This paper presents a theoretical analysis of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm. The authors derive performance bounds that demonstrate an improvement over known results for regularization based on the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality and Originality**

The paper's primary theoretical contribution is establishing novel performance guarantees for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm. A particularly noteworthy and elegant result is the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This finding appears to be a significant original contribution and holds independent interest beyond the immediate application in this work.

However, while the theoretical contributions are strong, the experimental evaluation is brief and lacks sufficient detail to fully assess the practical implications of the proposed method. The empirical section could be substantially strengthened by addressing the following points:

*   **Experimental Setup:** The paper does not specify the methodology used for selecting the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) in the synthetic examples. Providing this information is crucial for reproducibility and for understanding the conditions under which the method performs well.
*   **Comparison with Alternative Regularizers:** An empirical comparison with other regularization strategies would be valuable. For instance, it would be insightful to see how the proposed method compares to group regularization techniques, such as the l1,∞ norm, especially in scenarios where the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes.
*   **Benchmarking against Non-Convex Methods:** The evaluation would be more comprehensive if it included a comparison against established non-convex tensor decomposition algorithms. This would provide a clearer context for the performance and trade-offs of the proposed convex approach.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The theoretical arguments are presented logically, making the technical content easy to follow.

**Significance**

Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with wide-ranging applications. By improving upon existing theoretical guarantees for convex relaxation methods, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field. The theoretical results are interesting, and the work is well-motivated. The primary weakness is the limited scope of the experimental validation. Despite this limitation, the novelty of the theoretical findings and the importance of the problem domain make this a valuable submission.
