- Paper analyzes convex norm-regularized tensor factorization, comparing latent Schatten norm to overlapped norm and deriving mean-squared-error bounds under several noise models.
- Theoretical rates favor the latent norm and align with prior empirical findings; numerical simulations support the theory.
- Introduces a generalized class of structured tensor Schatten norms and proves a duality result (Lemma 1).
- Writing and technical development are clear and appear sound.
- Provides theoretical justification for the empirically observed advantage of the latent norm over the overlapped norm.
- Simulation results are consistent with the theoretical analysis.
- The duality result for the generalized norms is tidy and potentially useful.
- Limited ML impact: narrow, technical focus with no experiments or analyses on concrete ML tasks (e.g., tensor completion, recommendation, knowledge-graph tasks).
- Incremental contribution: strengthens a specific recent approach [25] but does not introduce surprising new insights or substantially novel techniques.
- No lower bounds or tightness guarantees are provided, weakening comparative claims about estimator optimality.
- Claim of a “new class of norms” is overstated: paper mainly defines the class and proves its dual, without algorithms, deeper analyses, or experiments exploiting it.
- Suggestion: add ML-relevant experiments, discuss how assumptions map to real data, or temper claims of novelty.
- Suggestion: provide or discuss lower bounds/tightness (minimax or instance-dependent) to strengthen comparisons.
- Suggestion: clarify what is new relative to prior work [25] and emphasize any novel technical ingredients.
- Presentation fixes: reposition or better explain Figure 1, soften abstract wording about the new norms, and correct minor editorial issues.
