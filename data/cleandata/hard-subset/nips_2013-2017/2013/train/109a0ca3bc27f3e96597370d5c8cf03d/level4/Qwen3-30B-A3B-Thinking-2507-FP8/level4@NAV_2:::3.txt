

This paper investigates a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, focusing on the "latent" approach to convex tensor decomposition. It builds upon recent work by [25, 26] which introduced the "overlapped" approach, and provides theoretical justification for the empirical observation that the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the true tensor is low-rank in a specific mode. The authors establish a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which generalizes results in structured sparsity literature [2, 13, 17, 21]. The paper also provides theoretical guarantees for the denoising performance of the latent approach, showing that the mean squared error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, rather than the average rank as in the overlapped approach.

Quality: The paper makes a strong theoretical contribution with rigorous proofs of the duality between the norms and the denoising performance guarantees. The theoretical analysis is sound and well-motivated. However, the experimental section is relatively brief and lacks sufficient detail about the experimental setup. For instance, the paper does not elaborate on how the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rK) were chosen for the examples, nor does it compare the performance of the latent approach to non-convex tensor decomposition methods. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more thorough analysis of how group regularization like the l1,∞-norm behaves when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank in all modes.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The technical content is presented in a logical flow, with definitions and proofs provided in the supplementary material as needed. The authors effectively communicate the relationship between their work and prior results in structured sparsity.

Originality: The main original contribution is the duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which is of independent interest beyond tensor decomposition. While the theoretical framework for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms builds on previous work [9, 15, 23, 25], the specific duality result and its application to explaining the empirical superiority of the latent approach represent a novel contribution.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with wide-ranging applications in neuroimaging, collaborative filtering, and other domains. By providing theoretical justification for the empirical superiority of the latent approach over the overlapped approach, this paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field. The result that the latent approach performs as well as knowing the mode with the smallest rank is particularly significant, as it provides a theoretical foundation for a practical improvement in tensor decomposition algorithms.

Overall, the paper makes a solid theoretical contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. While the theoretical results are strong, the experimental section would benefit from more detail and comparison to non-convex approaches. With these improvements, the paper would be an excellent contribution to the conference.
