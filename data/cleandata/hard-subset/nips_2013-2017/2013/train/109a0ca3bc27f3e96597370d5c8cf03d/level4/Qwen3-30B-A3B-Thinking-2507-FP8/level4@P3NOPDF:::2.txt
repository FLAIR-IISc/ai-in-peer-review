

The paper proposes a convex optimization approach for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, specifically the overlapped Schatten norm, and establishes recovery guarantees. The work is technically sound and makes a novel contribution by providing improved guarantees for Tucker decomposition. However, several clarifications are required to strengthen the paper’s accessibility, technical precision, and scholarly context.

**Major Revisions Required:**  
The primary weakness is the insufficient motivation for the problem within the machine learning context. To broaden accessibility, the authors must explicitly connect their framework to a latent variable model. Consider a graphical model where independent latent variables \(h_1, \dots, h_k\) (each of dimension \(d_i\)) map linearly to observed variables \(x_1, \dots, x_k\) (e.g., \(x_i = W_i h_i + \epsilon_i\)), with arbitrary dependencies among \(h_i\). The observed moment tensor then admits a Tucker decomposition with multilinear rank \((d_1, \dots, d_k)\). Crucially, the authors must clarify that their method’s advantage lies in handling cases where *only some* \(d_i\) are small (not all), which is central to their theoretical contribution. A graphical model diagram would significantly aid this exposition.  

**Technical Corrections:**  
- **Theorem 2**: The statement is inaccurate without noting that components are recovered *up to permutation*. This must be explicitly added.  
- **Theorem 2 Proof**: The claim that (12) implies (13) requires justification.  
- **Lemma 5**: The proof is missing but is essential for Theorem 2; it must be provided.  
- **Lemma 4**: The reference to another paper is insufficient; a concise self-contained explanation is needed.  

**Scholarly Context and Notation:**  
- **Related Work**: Expand discussion of HOSVD and tensor rank variants (e.g., multilinear rank vs. CP rank). Cite Kolda’s review (Section 4) to contextualize approximation bounds for existing methods.  
- **Rank Terminology**: Clarify that "mode-rank" is synonymous with "multilinear rank" (noting alternative tensor rank definitions) in the introduction.  
- **Incoherence**: The authors incorrectly equate their condition (energy spread across modes/components) with incoherence in sparse+low-rank recovery [1,3,10]. These are fundamentally distinct: the cited works require singular vectors to be incoherent with standard bases, while this paper addresses non-sparse noise. **Remove this comparison entirely.**  
- **Notation**:  
  - Replace confusing superscripts \(\hat{\mathcal{W}}^{(k)}\) with a clearer symbol (e.g., \(\hat{W}^{(k)}\)).  
  - Standardize rank notation: replace conflicting \(\bar{r}_k, \underline{r}_k\) with \(\text{mult\_rank}(\mathcal{T})\) for multilinear rank.  
  - Fix missing parentheses in Supplementary Material, p. 12.  
- **Computational Complexity**: Briefly discuss the complexity of the proposed method relative to existing approaches (e.g., HOSVD).  

**Minor Clarity Improvements:**  
- Abstract: Correct "result for structures Schatten" to "structured Schatten norm."  
- The phrase "the energy to be spread across different mixture components" (after (10)) should explicitly state that singular values must be bounded per mode (e.g., "no single mode or component dominates the singular spectrum").  

**Summary of Strengths and Weaknesses:**  
The paper’s core contribution—using overlapped Schatten norms for Tucker decomposition with provable guarantees—is novel and valuable. The technical approach is well-motivated mathematically, and the writing is generally clear. However, the lack of a concrete latent-variable model, the technical gaps in Theorem 2/Lemma 5, and the misaligned incoherence discussion undermine the paper’s impact. Addressing these points will significantly strengthen the work’s accessibility and rigor. The proposed revisions are essential for the paper to meet NIPS standards for clarity and technical completeness.  

**Recommendation:**  
**Accept with Major Revisions** (address all points above). The paper has strong technical merit but requires substantial refinement to ensure it is accessible to the ML community and free of technical inaccuracies. The authors should prioritize the latent-variable model explanation and Theorem 2 corrections, as these are foundational to the paper’s contribution.
