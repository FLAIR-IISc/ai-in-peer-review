Additional comments after the rebuttal:
The paper should better motivate the problem for a machine learning audience so it is accessible to a wider community. In particular, please present an explicit latent-variable model (with a graphical model) in which your tensor decomposition recovers parameters. For example, consider latent variables h1, h2, … where hi connects to observed variable xi (i = 1, 2, …). Assume a linear mapping from hidden to observed variables, while allowing arbitrary dependence among the hi’s. In this setting, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank equals dim(h1), dim(h2), …. Please add this discussion and, ideally, a figure illustrating the model. Also note that some hidden variables may be low dimensional while others are not; exploiting such heterogeneity is a key advantage of your new result and should be emphasized.

The paper studies convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured (overlapped) Schatten norms and derives improved reconstruction guarantees. The paper is generally well written and makes technically important contributions. Addressing the following technical and notational issues will further improve clarity and readability.

Clarifications regarding Theorem 2 and its proof:
- Theorem 2 as stated is not technically correct: components are recovered only up to a permutation. This is minor but should be explicitly stated.
- The implication from (12) to (13) is said to be immediate. Please provide a clear justification of this step.
- The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, yet it is central to Theorem 2. Please include it.
- It would be better to spell out the proof of Lemma 4 rather than referring to another paper, even if the argument is straightforward.

Minor issues:
- Related work: Please expand the discussion on HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods. In particular, note that higher-order SVD and its variants provide approximation bounds; see Kolda’s review (Section 4).
- Rank notions: What you call mode-rank is commonly referred to as multilinear rank. Please clarify this in the introduction and note that multiple, non-equivalent notions of tensor rank exist.
- Computational complexity: Briefly discuss the computational cost of the proposed methods (e.g., per-iteration complexity, dependence on tensor dimensions and ranks, and any practical bottlenecks).
- Incoherence (after equation (10)): You use “incoherence” to mean that energy is spread across mixture components and modes—i.e., no component or mode has an excessively large singular value. You claim this matches the incoherence conditions of [1,3,10]. I do not see this. In [1,3,10], incoherence is used in sparse+low-rank recovery with sparse noise, and the condition concerns singular vectors being incoherent with the standard basis. Your condition is different, and the noise here is not sparse. I suggest removing this comparison.

Typos and notational issues:
- The superscript notation for components, \hat{\mathcal{W}}^{(k)}, is confusing. Please use a different symbol or indexing scheme for components.
- Notation for k-mode rank is inconsistent: both \bar{r}_k and \underline{r}_k are used. Please choose one or introduce a clear function, e.g., mult_rank(tensor), to denote the multilinear rank.
- Supplementary material, last line of page 12: a parenthesis is missing.
- Abstract: “result for structures Schatten” should be “result for structured Schatten.”

Overall assessment:
The paper makes a novel and valuable contribution by showing that an overlapped Schatten norm can recover a Tucker-type low-multilinear-rank tensor with provable guarantees, and the empirical/theoretical reconstruction improvements are compelling. With stronger motivation for the ML audience, a clear latent-variable example and figure, explicit handling of permutation ambiguity in Theorem 2, completion of missing proofs (especially Lemma 5), clarification of the (12)→(13) step, a more careful discussion of incoherence, and the noted notational/typo fixes, the paper will be significantly strengthened.
