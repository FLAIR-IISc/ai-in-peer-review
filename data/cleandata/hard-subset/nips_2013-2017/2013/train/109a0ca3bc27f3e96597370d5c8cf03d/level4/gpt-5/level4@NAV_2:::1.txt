This paper investigates convex, norm-regularized methods for decomposing approximately low-rank tensors. It centers on the recently introduced “latent” Schatten-norm approach ([25]) and contrasts it with the earlier “overlapped” norm formulation. The authors derive mean-squared error bounds for the latent approach under various noise models, showing rates that improve upon existing analyses for the overlapped norm and align with previously reported empirical advantages. The theory is supported by numerical experiments. In addition, the paper introduces a broader family of structured Schatten norms and proves a duality result connecting the overlapped and latent constructions.

The manuscript is generally clear and appears technically sound (though I did not verify the supplementary proofs line by line). The results should be of interest to researchers working on optimization and theory for tensor decompositions. My primary concerns are:
(i) The scope is quite specialized and largely technical, with only indirect ties to mainstream machine learning, which may limit impact at NIPS. Although the authors reasonably argue that tensor factorizations arise in ML, the paper does not analyze or demonstrate the proposed approaches within concrete ML applications, either theoretically or empirically.
(ii) The contributions feel incremental and not especially surprising (acknowledging this is subjective). The work mainly strengthens the theoretical underpinnings of a specific recent method [25] that was already shown empirically to perform well, and the proof techniques themselves do not seem particularly novel.

A lesser concern is the absence of complementary lower bounds. Since the upper bounds are used to compare methods and argue superiority, the lack of lower bounds makes such claims potentially fragile.

Additional Comments
-----------------
- The abstract begins with “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...,” which is somewhat misleading. The body of the paper primarily analyzes two existing norms (overlapped and latent). The generalization appears mainly in Section 2.1, together with a duality statement (Lemma 1). There are no algorithms built around these generalized norms, no analysis of their algorithmic behavior, and no experimental evaluation based on them.
- Figure 1 is introduced in the opening section but is difficult to interpret prior to the background provided in Section 2 and beyond.
- Line 38: “Tensor is” → “Tensor modeling is” (?)
- Line 40: Add a comma after “Conventionally”
- Line 395: “also in such situations” → “in such situations as well”

The paper’s main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition method. While the work seems correct and carefully executed, it appears somewhat incremental and may have limited impact for NIPS.
