- Analyzes convex, norm-regularized methods for approximately low-rank tensor decomposition, focusing on the latent norm vs. the overlap norm.  
- Derives mean-squared error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models that favorably compare to overlap-norm analyses.  
- Provides numerical simulations that support the theoretical claims.  
- Presents a generalization that encompasses both norms and proves a corresponding duality result.  
- Paper is generally clear and appears technically sound (supplementary proofs not fully checked).  
- Contributions are narrow, technical, and only tangentially connected to machine learning, limiting likely impact for NIPS.  
- Work is somewhat incremental and unsurprising, mainly providing theoretical support for an approach already shown empirically effective.  
- Lacks lower bounds to complement the provided upper bounds, weakening algorithmic comparisons.  
- Abstract’s claim of proposing a “new class” of structured Schatten norms is misleading given limited development and evaluation of the generalization.  
- Figure 1 is introduced too early and isn’t interpretable without Section 2 background.  
- Minor writing/typo issues (examples: Line 38, Line 40, Line 395).  
- Main contribution: theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor decomposition method; technically sound but potentially limited NIPS impact.
