- Paper studies structured Schatten norms for convex tensor decomposition, comparing "overlapped" vs "latent" approaches and proving a novel duality between them.
- Shows theoretically that the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the true tensor is low-rank in a single mode.
- Proves latent error scales with the minimum mode-k rank while overlapped error depends on average rank, explaining prior empirical observations.
- Connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature; duality result is generalizable to that context.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorems 1–3: consistency, deterministic bounds, Gaussian noise performance).
- Numerical experiments validate the theoretical scaling of mean squared error and explain why latent succeeds when tensor is full-rank in most modes.
- Paper is well written and presents complex concepts accessibly.
- Motivation for machine learning applications is underdeveloped; needs a concrete latent-variable model or graphical example where the latent approach is relevant.
- Theorem 2 requires clarification (components recovered up to permutation; justification for transition from (12) to (13)); Lemma 5’s proof is missing but crucial.
- Notation is confusing (use of \bar{r}_k vs \underline{r}_k and superscript \hat{\cal W}^{(k)}); recommend consistent "multilinear rank" terminology.
- Related work omissions: should cite Kolda [14], discuss HOSVD and its approximation bounds, and clarify "mode-rank" terminology.
- Incorrect comparison of their incoherence condition to sparse+low-rank recovery papers (noise is not sparse); that comparison should be removed.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the theoretical clarifications, notation, motivation, and related-work/incoherence issues.
