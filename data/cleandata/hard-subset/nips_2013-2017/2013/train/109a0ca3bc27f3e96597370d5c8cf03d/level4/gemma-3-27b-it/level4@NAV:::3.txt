This paper establishes theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm utilizing latent Schatten norm regularization, achieving an improvement over existing bounds for regularization with the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality:** The work presents valuable theoretical findings concerning tensor decomposition with latent Schatten norm regularization. The dual characterization of both the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a particularly noteworthy contribution. However, the experimental evaluation feels somewhat limited and lacks detail; for example, the selection process for the Tucker ranks (r<sub>1</sub>,…r<sub>k</sub>) used in the examples is not explained. Furthermore, the behavior of group regularization techniques (like l<sub>1</sub>/∞ norms) when the tensor exhibits simultaneous low rank across all modes remains unclear. A comparison to non-convex methods would also be beneficial.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and readily understandable.

**Originality:** The core original contribution lies in the demonstrated duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, a result that appears to hold independent interest.

**Significance:** Given the broad applicability of tensor decomposition, and the improvement this paper offers over current state-of-the-art results, I consider it to be a significant contribution. The theoretical results are compelling and the manuscript is well-written. Despite these strengths, the experimental section could be strengthened with more thorough analysis and detail.
