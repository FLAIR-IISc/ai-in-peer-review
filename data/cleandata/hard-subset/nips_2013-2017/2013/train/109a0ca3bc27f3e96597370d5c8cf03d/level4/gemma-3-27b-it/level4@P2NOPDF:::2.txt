This paper presents a convex-optimization approach to tensor decomposition, leveraging structured Schatten norms, specifically an overlapped Schatten norm, to achieve improved reconstruction guarantees. The work is technically sound and represents a valuable contribution to the field. Addressing a few technical points and clarifications will further enhance the paper’s readability and impact.

**Major Comments (Following Rebuttal):**

To broaden the accessibility of this work to the machine learning community, the authors should more clearly motivate the problem within a probabilistic modeling framework. Specifically, I recommend explicitly outlining a latent variable model where the proposed tensor decomposition serves to learn model parameters. For example, consider a model where latent variables *h<sub>1</sub>*, *h<sub>2</sub>*, …, *h<sub>n</sub>* influence observed variables *x<sub>1</sub>*, *x<sub>2</sub>*, …, *x<sub>n</sub>* respectively, with a linear relationship from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among the *h<sub>i</sub>*. In this scenario, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank corresponds to the dimensions of the latent spaces (dim(*h<sub>1</sub>*), dim(*h<sub>2</sub>*), …). Including such a discussion, ideally with a corresponding graphical model illustration, would significantly strengthen the paper’s motivation. It’s also crucial to emphasize that the paper’s main advantage lies in the potential for only *some* latent variables to have small dimensions, while others do not – this should be explicitly highlighted.

**Specific Technical Clarifications:**

* **Theorem 2:** The statement of Theorem 2 is technically incomplete. While the components are recovered, they are recovered only up to a permutation. This limitation should be acknowledged. Furthermore, the derivation from equation (12) to (13) requires additional justification to be fully convincing.
* **Lemma 5:** The proof for Lemma 5 is currently missing. As Lemma 5 is central to the proof of Theorem 2, providing a complete proof is essential.
* **Lemma 4:** Although straightforward, explicitly detailing the proof of Lemma 4, rather than referring to another paper, would improve the paper’s self-containment.

**Minor Issues & Suggestions:**

* **Related Work:** The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of related work, particularly concerning Higher Order Singular Value Decomposition (HOSVD) and other existing tensor decomposition methods. Mentioning how HOSVD and its variants provide approximation bounds for tensor decomposition, as discussed in Kolda’s review (section 4), would be beneficial.
* **Tensor Rank Nomenclature:** The authors utilize the term "mode-rank," which is also commonly referred to as "multilinear rank.” Acknowledging this alternative terminology and briefly discussing the variety of tensor rank definitions in the introduction would improve clarity.
* **Computational Complexity:** A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods would provide valuable practical insight.
* **Incoherence Condition:** The authors’ interpretation of “incoherence” after equation (10) requires clarification. Their usage implies that the “energy” should be distributed across mixture components and modes, preventing any single component or mode from dominating. While the authors claim this aligns with incoherence conditions from [1, 3, 10], I disagree. The cited works typically employ incoherence to facilitate sparse-plus-low-rank recovery, whereas the noise model here is not sparse. The incoherence conditions in those papers hinge on the singular vectors being incoherent with the basis vectors, a condition distinct from the one presented in this work. Removing this comparison would be prudent.

**Notational & Typographical Issues:**

* **Component Notation:** The superscript notation for components,  \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}, is potentially confusing. I recommend employing a different symbol.
* **k-Mode Rank Notation:** The use of both \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k to denote the k-mode rank is ambiguous and should be standardized. Introducing a notation such as `mult_rank(tensor)` to represent the multilinear rank would be helpful.
* **Supplementary Material:** A parenthesis is missing in the last line of page 12 of the supplementary material.
* **Abstract:** Replace "result for structures Schatten" with "structured Schatten."



In summary, this is a strong paper with novel contributions in leveraging an overlapped Schatten norm for Tucker tensor decomposition and providing recovery guarantees. Addressing the technical clarifications and suggestions outlined above will significantly enhance the paper’s clarity, impact, and accessibility.
