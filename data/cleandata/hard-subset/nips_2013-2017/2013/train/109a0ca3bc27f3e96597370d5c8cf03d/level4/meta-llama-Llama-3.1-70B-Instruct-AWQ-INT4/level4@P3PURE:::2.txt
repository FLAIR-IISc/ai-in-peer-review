Additional comments after the rebuttal:

To increase the accessibility of the paper to a broader audience, the authors should provide a more thorough motivation for the problem in the context of machine learning. Furthermore, they should explicitly define the latent variable model, including a graphical representation, for which the tensor decomposition can learn the parameters. Consider a model where latent variable h1 connects to observed variable x1, h2 to x2, and so on, with a linear relationship between hidden and observed variables, and arbitrary dependence among the hi's. In this case, the observed moment tensor has a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank is dim(h1), dim(h2), etc. The authors should add this discussion and preferably provide a visual representation of this model. Additionally, they should highlight the possibility that only some hidden variables have small dimensions, which is a primary advantage of the new result in the paper.

The paper explores convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors employ the overlapped Schatten norm and demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving better reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well-written and makes significant technical contributions. Addressing a few technical and notational issues will enhance the paper's readability.

Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:

The statement of Theorem 2 is technically incorrect, as the components are only recovered up to a permutation. Although a minor issue, the authors should acknowledge this.

In Theorem 2, the claim that (12) immediately leads to (13) requires justification.

The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, which is a crucial component of Theorem 2.

It would be beneficial to explicitly clarify the proof of Lemma 4, rather than referring to another paper, despite its straightforwardness.

Minor issues:

The authors should include more related work on Higher-Order Singular Value Decomposition (HOSVD) and other methods for tensor decomposition. Specifically, they can mention how higher-order SVD and its variants yield approximation bounds for tensor decomposition, as discussed in Kolda's review on tensor decompositions (Section 4).

The authors should note that the notion of rank they use, referred to as mode-rank, is also known as multilinear rank. They can mention this in the introduction and acknowledge the existence of different notions of tensor rank.

The authors should briefly discuss the computational complexity of the employed methods.

The authors need to clarify their discussion about incoherence after (10). The term "incoherence" is used to imply that the "energy" should be spread across different mixture components and modes, meaning that no component or mode can have too high a singular value. However, this is not equivalent to the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10], which are used for sparse+low-rank recovery. The condition in [1,3,10] involves singular vectors being incoherent with basis vectors, differing from the condition in this paper. It is suggested to remove this comparison.

Typos/notational issues:

The superscript notation for components \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)} is confusing. Using a different symbol for components is recommended.

The notation \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k, both used for k-mode rank, should be changed. Introducing mult_rank(tensor) to denote the multilinear rank is a possible solution.

In the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material, some parentheses are missing.

The abstract should be corrected to read "result for structured Schatten" instead of "structures Schatten."

The paper is well-written and makes novel contributions to finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm, providing guarantees for recovery. However, some technical clarifications are needed, which will hopefully be addressed by the authors.
