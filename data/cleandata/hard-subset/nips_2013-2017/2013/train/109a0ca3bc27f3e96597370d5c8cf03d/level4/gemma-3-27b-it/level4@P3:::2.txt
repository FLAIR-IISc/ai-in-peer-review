## Review: Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization

This paper studies a class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, focusing on the “overlapped” and “latent” norms used in convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition. The authors theoretically analyze the “latent” approach, demonstrating its superior performance compared to the “overlapped” approach when the true tensor is low-rank in a specific, unknown mode – a phenomenon previously observed empirically. They also present a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms and validate their theory with numerical simulations.

The paper addresses an important problem in tensor decomposition – leveraging convex optimization for recovery and understanding the tradeoffs between different regularization strategies. Tensor decomposition has broad applications, ranging from neuroimaging and spatio-temporal data analysis to collaborative filtering, and the ability to efficiently and accurately decompose tensors is crucial. The work builds on recent advances in convex relaxation techniques for tensor decomposition [9, 15, 23, 25, 26] and connects these to the broader literature on structured sparsity [1, 2, 13, 17, 21]. 

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contributions:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical justification for the observed empirical success of the latent approach. Establishing bounds on the mean squared error and showing its scaling behavior based on the minimal mode-k rank is a significant contribution.
* **Duality Result:** The novel duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a valuable addition to the theoretical understanding of these regularization techniques.  This result has implications beyond tensor decomposition, relating to structured sparsity more generally.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The notation, while dense, is consistent, and the authors make an effort to explain the core ideas.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical results confirm the theoretical predictions, demonstrating the scaling behavior and further supporting the superior performance of the latent approach.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Motivation & Context for Machine Learning:** While the introduction mentions applications, the paper would benefit from a more concrete and accessible motivation for machine learning practitioners.  Specifically, illustrating the tensor decomposition problem within a latent variable model (with a graphical model representation) would significantly enhance understanding. For example, a model with latent variables *h<sub>i</sub>* connected to observed variables *x<sub>i</sub>* via a linear model, where the observed moment tensor can be linked to the multilinear rank of the latent variables, would be beneficial. Discussing scenarios where only some latent variables have low dimensions would highlight the advantage of the proposed approach.
* **Technical Nuances & Proof Details:** Several technical points require further clarification:
    * **Theorem 2:** The statement of Theorem 2 is not entirely precise; the recovered components are only guaranteed to be correct up to a permutation. This should be explicitly mentioned. The justification for the derivation of equation (13) from (12) requires more detailed explanation.
    * **Lemma 5:** The proof of Lemma 5, crucial for Theorem 2, is missing and must be included.
    * **Lemma 4:** While the proof is stated to be straightforward and referenced to another paper, providing a concise explanation within the paper itself would improve readability.
* **Related Work:**  The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work, particularly regarding Higher Order Singular Value Decomposition (HOSVD) and its variants, including their approximation bounds [5, 14].  Acknowledging different notions of tensor rank (e.g., multilinear rank) would also provide valuable context.
* **Incoherence Discussion:** The comparison to incoherence conditions in [1, 3, 10] appears misleading. The context of those works (sparse + low-rank recovery) differs significantly from the current setting (noisy tensor decomposition), and the definitions of incoherence are not directly comparable. Removing this comparison would strengthen the argument.
* **Notational Issues:**
    * The superscript notation for components (e.g.,  \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}) is potentially confusing. Alternative notation should be considered.
    * The use of both \bar{r}<sub>k</sub> and \underline{r}<sub>k</sub> for the k-mode rank is problematic. Introducing a more descriptive term like `mult_rank(tensor)` could improve clarity.
    * A missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material should be corrected.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound analysis of the latent approach to tensor decomposition, resolving an open question regarding its empirical performance.
* The duality result for structured Schatten norms is a valuable contribution to the field.
* The numerical results strongly support the theoretical findings.
* The paper is well-written and demonstrates a good understanding of the relevant literature.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a clearer connection to machine learning applications and a more intuitive explanation of the problem setting might limit its impact.
* The technical clarifications requested are important for ensuring the rigor and completeness of the work. 
* The potentially misleading discussion of incoherence and the notational issues detract from the overall clarity.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper has the potential to be a significant contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. However, addressing the weaknesses outlined above is crucial.  I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The authors should focus on strengthening the motivation for machine learning practitioners, clarifying the technical details, refining the related work discussion, and resolving the notational inconsistencies. Addressing these points will significantly enhance the paper’s clarity, impact, and overall quality.
