This paper analyzes convex norm-regularized optimization approaches for the factorization of approximately low-rank tensors, focusing on a recent “latent” norm approach [25] and its comparison to a previously used “overlap” norm. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared error behavior of the latent norm under several noise assumptions, demonstrating favorable performance compared to existing analysis of the overlap norm – results that support the empirically observed benefits of the latent norm reported in a prior work. These theoretical findings are validated through numerical simulations. Furthermore, the paper considers a generalization of both norms and proves a corresponding duality theorem.

The paper is generally well-written and appears technically sound, though I have not performed a detailed examination of the proofs in the supplementary material. The results are potentially useful for researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, my primary concerns are that:

(i) The results are narrow in scope and highly technical, with only a peripheral connection to machine learning, suggesting limited potential impact at NIPS. While the authors justifiably argue that tensor factorizations appear in machine learning contexts, the paper does not explore the proposed approaches within the framework of machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally.

(ii) The contributions feel incremental and not particularly surprising. The paper largely provides additional theoretical support for a recently proposed approach [25], which was already demonstrated to perform well empirically. Moreover, the techniques employed do not appear to be novel.

A minor weakness is the absence of lower bounds to complement the presented upper bounds. This is potentially problematic, as the upper bounds are used to compare different algorithms and justify the superiority of one approach over another.

Additional Comments:
-----------------
- The abstract states, “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors…” which is somewhat misleading. The paper primarily *analyzes* two existing approaches (overlap and latent). The generalization is only introduced in section 2.1, accompanied by a proof of its dual norm (Lemma 1). Critically, no new algorithms are proposed based on this generalized Schatten norm, and its behavior is neither analyzed nor experimentally tested.
- Figure 1 is presented in the introduction but requires background knowledge from section 2 onwards to be interpretable.
- Line 38: “Tensor is” should be revised to “Tensor modelling is”.
- Line 40: A comma is missing after “Conventionally”.
- Line 395: “also in such situations” should be replaced with “in such situations as well”. 

The paper’s main contribution lies in establishing theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition approach. While technically sound, the results are somewhat incremental and may have limited impact at NIPS.
