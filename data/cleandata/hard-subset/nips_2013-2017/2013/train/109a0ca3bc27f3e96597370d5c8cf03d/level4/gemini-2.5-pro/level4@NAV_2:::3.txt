This paper presents a theoretical analysis of a convex tensor decomposition method based on what the authors term the "latent Schatten norm." This approach, originally proposed in [25], decomposes a tensor into a sum of components, each of which is regularized to be low-rank in a specific mode. This work builds upon previous research into convex relaxations for tensor decomposition, such as [9, 15, 23, 26], which primarily focused on an "overlapped" Schatten norm that encourages all modes to be simultaneously low-rank. The main contribution of this paper is to provide a rigorous theoretical explanation for why the latent approach was observed empirically in [25] to outperform the overlapped approach, especially when the underlying tensor is low-rank in only one particular (and unknown) mode. A key component of the analysis is a novel duality result connecting the latent and overlapped Schatten norms, which also links this work to the broader literature on structured sparsity [2, 13, 17].

**Quality:**
The paper's theoretical contributions are strong and appear to be technically sound. The analysis rigorously demonstrates that the mean squared error of the latent approach scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank of the true tensor, whereas the overlapped approach depends on the *average* of the mode-k ranks. This provides a clear and compelling explanation for the method's effectiveness. The duality result between the two classes of structured Schatten norms (Lemma 1) is particularly elegant and may be of independent interest to researchers working on structured sparsity.

The primary weakness lies in the empirical validation. While the experiments in Section 4 confirm the predicted scaling behavior, they feel somewhat brief and could be expanded to make the paper's contributions more convincing. For instance:
*   The description of the data generation process is minimal. The paper would benefit from more detail on the experimental setup, for instance, the methodology for selecting the multilinear ranks (r1, r2, r3) for the synthetic tensors.
*   The comparison is limited to the two convex approaches (latent vs. overlapped). To better contextualize the results, a comparison against established non-convex tensor decomposition methods (e.g., alternate least squares) would be highly valuable.
*   The paper could explore the performance of other structured regularizers, such as the overlapped S1/∞-norm mentioned in the conclusion, especially in settings where the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes.

**Clarity:**
The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow. The authors do an excellent job of motivating the problem, defining the key concepts of overlapped and latent Schatten norms, and situating their work within the existing literature. The progression from the duality lemma to the main performance bounds in Section 3 is logical and well-explained.

**Originality:**
The core original contribution is the theoretical analysis that underpins the empirically observed advantages of the latent Schatten norm regularizer. While the algorithm itself was previously proposed, this paper is the first to provide performance guarantees that explain its behavior. The duality characterization of the overlapped and latent norms is a novel and significant result that connects this specific problem in tensor analysis to general principles of structured regularization.

**Significance:**
The work is significant as it addresses the fundamental problem of tensor decomposition, which has broad applications in many scientific and engineering domains. The paper provides clear theoretical guidance for practitioners, demonstrating that the latent approach is preferable, particularly in noisy settings, as it adapts to the unknown low-rank structure of the data without prior knowledge. The result that the latent approach performs as if it knew the mode with the minimum rank is a powerful insight that advances the state-of-the-art in the theoretical understanding of convex methods for tensor analysis.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Provides the first rigorous theoretical justification for the superior performance of the latent Schatten norm approach, answering an important open question from prior work [25].
*   Introduces a novel and elegant duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which is a contribution of broader interest to the structured sparsity community.
*   The theoretical results are significant, showing that the latent approach can perform as well as an oracle method that knows the lowest-rank mode.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presents its contributions.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
*   The empirical evaluation is limited and could be strengthened with more detailed experimental design, a broader set of comparisons including non-convex baselines, and a deeper analysis of different low-rank scenarios.
