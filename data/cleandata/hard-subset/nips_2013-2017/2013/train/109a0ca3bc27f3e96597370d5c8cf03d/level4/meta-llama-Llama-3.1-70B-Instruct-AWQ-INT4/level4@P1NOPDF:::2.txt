Additional comments after the rebuttal:

To make the paper more accessible to a broader audience, the authors need to provide a clearer motivation for the problem in the context of machine learning. A key aspect missing from the current discussion is a latent variable model that illustrates how tensor decomposition can learn the model parameters. Specifically, consider a model where latent variable $h_1$ connects to observed variable $x_1$, $h_2$ to $x_2$, and so on. Assuming a linear model from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependence among the $h_i$s, the observed moment tensor can be shown to have a Tucker decomposition, with its multilinear rank being $\text{dim}(h_1), \text{dim}(h_2), \ldots$. The authors should add this discussion and ideally provide a graphical representation of this model. Additionally, they should highlight that it is possible for only some hidden variables to have small dimensions, which is a main advantage of the new result in the paper.

The paper presents a convex-optimization-based approach to tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors employ the overlapped Schatten norm and demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving better reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well-written and makes significant technical contributions. Addressing a few technical and notational issues will further enhance its readability.

* Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:

The statement of Theorem 2 is technically incorrect, as the components are only recovered up to a permutation. Although a minor issue, the authors should acknowledge this.

In Theorem 2, the authors claim that equation (12) immediately leads to equation (13), but this requires justification.

The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, which is a crucial component of Theorem 2.

While the proof of Lemma 4 is straightforward, it would be better to clarify it explicitly rather than referring to another paper.

* Minor issues:

The authors should provide more related work on Higher-Order Singular Value Decomposition (HOSVD) and other methods for tensor decomposition. Specifically, they can mention how HOSVD and its variants yield approximation bounds for tensor decomposition, as discussed in Kolda's review on tensor decompositions (section 4).

The authors refer to the notion of rank as "mode-rank," which is also known as multilinear rank. They should mention this and note that there are different notions of tensor rank in the introduction.

A brief discussion on the computational complexity of the employed methods would be beneficial.

The authors' discussion about incoherence after equation (10) requires clarification. They use the term to imply that the "energy" needs to be spread across different mixture components and modes, i.e., no component or mode can have too high a singular value. However, this is different from the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10], which are used for sparse+low rank recovery. The conditions in [1,3,10] involve the singular vectors being incoherent with the basis vectors. It is suggested to remove this comparison.

* Typos/notational issues:

The superscript notation for components $\hat{\mathcal{W}}^{(k)}$ is confusing. Using a different symbol for components would be more intuitive.

The notation $\bar{r}_k$ and $\underline{r}_k$ for $k$-mode rank is inconsistent. It would be better to introduce a unified notation, such as $\text{mult\_rank}(\text{tensor})$, to denote the multilinear rank.

There is a missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material.

In the abstract, "result for structures Schatten" should be changed to "structured."

Overall, the paper is well-written and makes novel contributions in terms of finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm, providing guarantees for recovery. However, addressing the technical clarifications mentioned above will further strengthen the paper.
