- Paper analyzes convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing the latent norm to the overlap norm.  
- Main technical contribution: derived mean-squared-error upper bounds for the latent norm under various noise models, showing advantage over existing overlap-norm analyses.  
- Empirical numerical simulations corroborate the theoretical findings.  
- Introduces a generalization of the two norms and proves a duality theorem for it.  
- Manuscript is well-written and technical arguments appear sound (supplementary proofs not checked in detail).  
- Contribution is highly specialized and may be tangential to core ML interests, limiting NIPS audience impact.  
- Paper does not connect analyzed methods to specific ML applications via theory or experiments.  
- Work seems incremental: primarily provides theoretical support for method in [25], whose empirical strength was known.  
- Analytical techniques do not appear to represent a significant methodological advance.  
- Missing lower bounds for the derived upper bounds weakens the normative comparison between norms.  
- Claim of proposing a “new class of structured Schatten norms” is overstated given limited development and no algorithms/experiments for the generalization.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 is premature in the introduction; a few minor wording/grammar fixes suggested (specific line edits provided).
