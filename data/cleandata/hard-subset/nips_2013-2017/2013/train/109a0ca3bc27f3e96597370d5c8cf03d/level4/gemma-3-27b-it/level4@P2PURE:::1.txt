This paper investigates convex norm-regularized optimization techniques for the factorization of approximately low-rank tensors, focusing on a recent “latent” norm approach [25] and its comparison to a previously used “overlap” norm approach. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared-error performance of the latent norm under various noise assumptions, demonstrating improvements over existing analyses for the overlap norm and supporting its previously reported empirical advantages. These theoretical findings are validated through numerical simulations. The paper also explores a generalization of both norms and establishes a corresponding duality theorem.

The paper is generally well-written and technically appears sound, though a detailed verification of the proofs in the supplementary material was not conducted. The results may be of interest to researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, my primary concerns are that:

(i) The contributions are specialized and largely theoretical, with a tenuous connection to machine learning, and likely limited impact at NIPS. While the authors acknowledge the potential relevance of tensor factorizations to machine learning, the paper lacks any investigation of the proposed approaches within the context of actual machine learning problems, either theoretically or empirically.

(ii) The results feel incremental and not particularly surprising. They largely provide additional theoretical justification for a specific, recently proposed method [25] that had already demonstrated promising empirical performance. Furthermore, the techniques employed do not appear novel.

A minor point is the absence of lower bounds to complement the upper bounds presented. This is potentially problematic, as these upper bounds are used to compare algorithms and establish the superiority of one approach over another.

Additional Comments:
-----------------
- The abstract claims “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors…”, which is somewhat misleading. The paper primarily analyzes two existing approaches (overlap and latent). The generalization is introduced only in section 2.1, with Lemma 1 focusing on characterizing its dual norm. Consequently, no algorithms based on this generalized norm are proposed, analyzed, or experimentally evaluated.
- Figure 1, presented in the introduction, is difficult to interpret without the background information provided in section 2 onwards.
- Line 38: “Tensor is” should likely be “Tensor modelling is”.
- Line 40: A comma is missing after “Conventionally”.
- Line 395: “also in such situations” should be “in such situations as well”.

The paper’s core contribution lies in establishing theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition approach. While technically sound, the results are somewhat incremental and may have limited impact at NIPS.
