- Paper studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensors, comparing the “latent” norm to the “overlapped” norm.  
- Derives theoretical mean-squared error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models, showing favorable rates versus overlapped norm.  
- Supports theory with numerical simulations and introduces a generalization that encompasses both norms plus a duality result.  
- Paper is generally clear and appears technically sound.  
- Results may be useful to researchers on tensor decomposition optimization.  
- Main concern: contributions are narrow, largely technical, and only peripherally connected to machine learning, limiting potential NIPS impact.  
- Main concern: contributions feel incremental and not particularly surprising; mainly provide additional theoretical support for an existing approach ([25]).  
- Techniques do not seem especially novel.  
- Smaller concern: absence of lower bounds, which undermines comparisons based solely on upper bounds.  
- Abstract’s claim of proposing a “new class” of norms is misleading; the generalization is limited and not developed into algorithms or experiments.  
- Figure 1 appears too early and is not interpretable without later background; consider moving or adding explanation.  
- Minor editorial/typo suggestions: change “Tensor is” → “Tensor modeling is,” add comma after “Conventionally,” and replace “also in such situations” → “in such situations as well.”  
- Summary: main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor method; technically sound but incremental with limited perceived impact.
