- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms, defining overlapped and latent norms and proving a duality between them.
- Main statistical result: denoising error for the latent approach scales with the minimum mode rank, explaining its advantage when only one mode is low-rank.
- Experiments validate the theoretical scaling and show latent methods can outperform overlapped methods in imbalanced low-rank regimes.
- Connects prior convex tensor regularization work with structured sparsity and latent-group formulations, and builds on high-dimensional M-estimation frameworks.
- Would benefit from clearer discussion of classical tensor decompositions (HOSVD/Tucker) and their approximation guarantees.
- Strength: clean duality result between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms.
- Strength: new recovery bounds for the latent approach that match empirical behavior when one mode has much lower rank.
- Strength: sharp Gaussian-setting rates with a regularization choice independent of unknown ranks, and experiments aligned with theory.
- Weakness: limited ML motivation; needs explanation of practical scenarios and why latent helps when only some modes are low-rank.
- Weakness: several proof details and statements need clarification (permutation invariance, step from Eq. (12) to (13), missing lemma proof).
- Weakness: conflation of “incoherence” with sparse+low-rank matrix recovery conditions; the comparison is misleading and needs reframing.
- Weakness: notational inconsistencies and typos reduce readability.
- Clarify Theorem 2 to state component recovery is only identifiable up to permutation and provide the missing lemmas/proof sketches.
- Explain and justify spectral norm constraints (Eq. (11)) and their role in controlling cross-component interference.
- Improve exposition: add a latent-variable model example/figure, define mode-k (multilinear) ranks early, and summarize HOSVD/Tucker approximation bounds.
- Add computational complexity discussion: per-iteration ADMM cost (SVDs of mode unfoldings), memory footprint, and empirical scalability.
- Originality: duality result and latent-norm tensor analysis are novel and extend latent group-lasso ideas to tensors.
- Significance: provides practical guidance for choosing overlapped vs. latent convex tensor models in imbalanced rank settings.
- Arguments for acceptance: clear theoretical advance, strong theory-experiment alignment, and practical insights.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient ML use-cases, missing/unclear proofs, and presentation issues that impede accessibility.
- Specific fixes requested: state permutation ambiguity in Theorem 2; derive Eq. (12)→(13) transparently; include proof of Lemma 5 and sketch Lemma 4; reframe incoherence discussion; expand HOSVD/Tucker related work; add complexity notes.
- Typos/notation fixes: fix “structures Schatten” → “structured Schatten,” avoid confusing component superscripts, unify mode-rank notation, fix missing parenthesis in supplement, and ensure consistent unfolding/folding notation.
- Overall recommendation: lean accept pending clarifications, proof completions, improved motivation, and notation/presentation fixes.
