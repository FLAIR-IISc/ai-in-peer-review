This paper studies convex norm-regularized optimization methods for factorizing approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on a recently proposed “latent” norm approach ([25]) and compares it to the previously used “overlapped” norm. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared error of the latent norm under several noise models, showing rates that compare favorably to existing analyses of the overlapped norm and help explain previously reported empirical advantages. The theory is supported by numerical simulations. The paper also introduces a generalization encompassing both norms and proves a duality result for it.

The paper is generally clear and appears technically sound (though I did not carefully verify all proofs in the supplementary material). The results may be useful to researchers working on optimization for tensor decompositions. My main concerns are:

(i) The contributions are narrow and largely technical, with only peripheral connection to machine learning, which may limit their impact at NIPS. Although the authors reasonably argue that tensor factorizations arise in machine learning, the paper does not explore the proposed approaches within concrete machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally.

(ii) The contributions feel incremental and not particularly surprising (I acknowledge this is subjective). They mainly provide additional theoretical support for a specific, recently proposed approach [25] that has already shown strong empirical performance. The techniques themselves also do not seem especially novel.

A smaller concern is the absence of lower bounds. This is potentially problematic, since the provided upper bounds are used to compare algorithms and argue for the superiority of one over the other.

Additional comments:
- The abstract begins with “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...,” which I find somewhat misleading. The paper largely analyzes two existing approaches (overlapped and latent). Unless I missed something, the generalization is only introduced in Section 2.1 and accompanied by a dual norm characterization (Lemma 1). No specific algorithms based on these generalized norms are proposed, analyzed, or evaluated experimentally.
- Figure 1 appears in the introduction but is not interpretable without the background developed in Section 2 onward. Consider moving it or adding more explanation earlier.
- Line 38: “Tensor is” → “Tensor modeling is.”
- Line 40: Add a comma after “Conventionally,”
- Line 395: “also in such situations” → “in such situations as well.”

In summary, the main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition method. The paper appears technically sound, but the contribution seems incremental and its potential impact at NIPS may be limited.
