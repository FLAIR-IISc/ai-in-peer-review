- Paper studies convex-optimization tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms and shows overlapped Schatten norm gives improved reconstruction guarantees.  
- Well written and technically important contribution, but readability would improve with fixes to technical and notational issues.  
- Need clearer motivation for ML audience: provide a latent-variable model (with graphical model) showing how tensor decomposition recovers model parameters.  
- Illustrative example: hidden variables h1,h2,... linearly mapping to observed x1,x2,... gives a Tucker decomposition with multilinear ranks = dim(h_i); include a diagram.  
- Emphasize that only some hidden variables may have small dimension—key advantage of the method.  
- Theorem 2 is incomplete: recovery is only up to permutation and this must be stated.  
- The derivation from equation (12) to (13) in Theorem 2 needs explicit justification.  
- Proof of Lemma 5 is missing and is crucial for Theorem 2; also explicitly state (and optionally prove) Lemma 4 rather than just citing another paper.  
- Expand related work to include HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods; cite Kolda’s review (section 4) and discuss approximation bounds.  
- Clarify terminology: call mode-rank “multilinear rank” and briefly mention other tensor rank notions in the introduction.  
- Discuss computational complexity of the proposed methods.  
- The incoherence discussion after equation (10) is misleading: the paper’s “energy spread” condition is not the same as the incoherence notions in [1,3,10]; remove or correct this comparison.  
- Fix notation/typos: confusing superscript \hat{\cal W}^{(k)} (use different symbol), avoid both \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k for k-mode rank (use mult_rank(tensor)), supplement missing parenthesis on supp. p.12, and correct abstract “result for structures Schatten” → “structured Schatten”.
