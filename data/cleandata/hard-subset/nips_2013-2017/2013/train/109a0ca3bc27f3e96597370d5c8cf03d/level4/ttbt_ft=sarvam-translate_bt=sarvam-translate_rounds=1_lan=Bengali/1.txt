This paper considers convex norm-regularization optimization methods for analyzing producers of low-rank tensors. It focuses on a recently proposed "latent" norm method ([25]) and compares it with a previously used "overlap" norm method. Specifically, this paper establishes theoretical bounds on the "latent" norm mean-squared-error behavior under several perturbation assumptions, which favorably compares with existing analysis on the overlap norm, which supports its better empirical performance in a previous paper. The theory is also confirmed through some numerical simulations. Finally, this paper considers a generalization of both of these norms and proves a duality theorem for them.

The paper appears to be generally easy to follow and technically sound (although I did not carefully examine the proofs in the supporting materials). The results may also be useful for researchers focused on optimization methods for tensor decomposition. My main problems are:

(i) The results are narrow and technical in nature, only marginally related to machine learning, and may have limited impact on NIPS. Although the authors (justifiably) state that tensors may appear in machine learning, this paper does not attempt to test the proposed methods theoretically or experimentally in the context of machine learning problems.

(ii) The results seem somewhat incremental, and I did not find them particularly surprising (admittedly, this is a subjective notion). They seem to provide some additional theoretical support for a specific method recently proposed ([25]), which was already argued to work well empirically. The techniques used also do not seem particularly new.

A further minor flaw is that there is no lower bound along with the upper bounds. This is potentially dangerous, since the upper bounds are used to compare between different algorithms and argue for the superiority of one over the other.
