This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon the result known for regularization using the overlapped Schatten norm.

Quality: The paper obtains theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm. The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norm is particularly interesting. However, the experiments are briefly considered and not elaborated upon. For instance, the authors do not specify how the Tucker ranks (r1,...rk) of the examples were chosen. Additionally, it would be beneficial to discuss how group regularization, such as l_1,\infty, works when the tensor is simultaneously low rank in all modes. Furthermore, a comparison of the method's performance with non-convex approaches would be valuable.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clear to understand.

Originality: The main original result in the paper is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This result seems to be of independent interest.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is an important problem encountered in many applications. Since this paper improves upon existing results for this problem, I believe it is significant work. The theoretical results are interesting, and the paper is well-written. However, experiments are considered rather briefly towards the end of the paper.

Arguments For Acceptance:
- The paper provides new theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm.
- The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norm is an interesting contribution.
- The paper is well-organized and clear to understand.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
- The experiments are not thoroughly discussed and lack details.
- The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with existing methods, including non-convex approaches.
- The authors do not discuss the implications of their results in the context of real-world applications.

Recommendation: Accept, but the authors should be encouraged to address the mentioned concerns, particularly providing more detailed experiments and comparisons with existing methods.
