Summary
This paper studies convex tensor decomposition with latent Schatten norm regularization and derives performance bounds that improve upon those known for the overlapped Schatten norm. A key theoretical contribution is a dual characterization linking the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. The paper is clearly written and well organized, but the experimental section is brief and lacks important details.

Strengths
- The theoretical results are solid and carefully presented. Establishing bounds for tensor decomposition with the latent Schatten norm is valuable and appears to improve over prior results for the overlapped norm.
- The dual characterization between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is elegant and likely of independent interest, with potential implications beyond the specific estimation problem considered.
- The exposition is clear, and the structure of the paper makes the main ideas easy to follow.

Weaknesses and questions for the authors
- The experimental evaluation is limited and lacks detail.
  - How were the Tucker ranks (r1, …, rk) chosen for the examples? Were they known to the algorithm, estimated, or cross-validated? Please clarify whether the true ranks match the model’s assumptions and how sensitive performance is to mis-specification.
  - How does group regularization such as ℓ1,∞ behave when the tensor is simultaneously low rank in all modes? Is there a setting where ℓ1,∞ is preferable, or does it introduce bias toward sparsity patterns that conflict with multi-mode low-rankness?
  - How does the proposed convex approach compare empirically to common non-convex methods (e.g., Tucker/CP with alternating minimization, projected gradient on low-rank manifolds, or other factorization-based approaches)? Even if guarantees differ, runtime and estimation accuracy comparisons would contextualize the contribution.
- Practical guidance is limited.
  - How should the regularization parameter(s) be set in practice? Is there theory-backed scaling with noise level, tensor size, or ranks?
  - What noise model and structural assumptions (e.g., incoherence, mode-wise conditioning) are required for the bounds? Please state them clearly and discuss their plausibility in applications.
  - Does the dual characterization lead to computational advantages (proximal operators, efficient projections, or tighter certificates) for either norm in practice?
- Scope and clarity of improvements.
  - Please make explicit in what regimes the latent norm outperforms the overlapped norm (e.g., tensors low-rank in a subset of modes, or with unbalanced ranks across modes). A short intuitive explanation linking the dual relationship to these regimes would help practitioners.
  - It would be useful to highlight how the improved bounds scale with dimensions and ranks compared to prior overlapped-norm results, ideally with side-by-side expressions or a succinct comparison table.
- Scalability and complexity: The paper would benefit from a discussion of computational cost, memory requirements, and scalability to higher-order or larger-dimensional tensors, including any bottlenecks in the optimization procedure.

Suggestions for improvement
- Expand the empirical section to substantiate the theory:
  - Synthetic experiments that systematically vary Tucker ranks across modes, noise levels, and rank imbalance; include scenarios where only a subset of modes are low rank.
  - Comparisons among latent Schatten, overlapped Schatten, and group regularizers (e.g., ℓ1,∞), with sensitivity to hyperparameters and mis-specified ranks.
  - Baselines from non-convex factorization methods, with metrics including estimation error, test reconstruction error, and runtime.
- Provide practical guidance:
  - Recommendations for setting regularization based on problem size or noise.
  - Discussion of robustness to deviations from assumptions (e.g., mild incoherence violations).
- Add a concise intuition section:
  - A brief explanation of why the latent norm and its dual relationship to the overlapped norm yield better behavior in certain structures.
  - Sketch how the dual view informs proof techniques or may translate to algorithmic benefits (e.g., easier dual certificates or proximal computations).
- Reproducibility details: dataset descriptions, optimization parameters, stopping criteria, and code availability if possible.

Overall assessment
This is a strong theoretical paper with a clean and potentially impactful contribution: improved bounds for tensor decomposition under latent Schatten norm regularization and a compelling dual characterization with the overlapped norm. The writing is clear and the results are likely of interest to the NeurIPS community working on tensor methods and convex optimization. However, the empirical section is too brief to fully assess practical relevance and to demonstrate the claimed advantages across realistic settings. I would be inclined to recommend acceptance if the authors substantially strengthen the experiments and clarify the practical regimes where the latent norm excels, along with guidance on parameter selection and assumptions. Otherwise, the paper remains a valuable theoretical contribution whose impact would be enhanced by a more thorough empirical evaluation.
