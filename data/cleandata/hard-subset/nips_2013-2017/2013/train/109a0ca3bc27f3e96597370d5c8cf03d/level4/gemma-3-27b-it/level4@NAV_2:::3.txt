This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm. It improves upon existing results known for regularization using the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality:** The paper presents technically sound theoretical results for tensor decomposition utilizing the latent Schatten norm. The duality characterization between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly noteworthy and represents a contribution of independent interest. While the experimental validation is present, it feels somewhat limited in scope and detail. Specific questions arise regarding the selection of Tucker ranks (r1,…rk) for the test examples and a comparative analysis with non-convex approaches would strengthen the evaluation. Further exploration of how group regularization techniques, such as l<sub>1,∞</sub>, perform when the tensor exhibits simultaneous low rank across all modes would be valuable.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, facilitating understanding of the presented concepts and results. 

**Originality:** The primary original contribution lies in the established duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This result appears to be of broader significance beyond the immediate context of tensor decomposition. 

**Significance:** Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem with wide-ranging applications. As this paper advances the state-of-the-art by improving existing results for this problem, I consider it to be a significant contribution. The theoretical findings are compelling, and the paper is well-written. However, a more comprehensive experimental analysis would further enhance its impact.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical guarantees for the latent Schatten norm approach.
*   Novel and potentially impactful duality result for structured Schatten norms.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.
*   Addresses an important problem with broad applicability.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Experimental validation is somewhat limited and lacks detailed analysis.
*   Comparison to non-convex methods is missing.
*   Further exploration of related regularization techniques could be beneficial.
