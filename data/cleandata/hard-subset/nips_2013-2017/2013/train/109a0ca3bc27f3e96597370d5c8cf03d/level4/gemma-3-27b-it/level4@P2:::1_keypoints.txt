- Analyzes convex norm-regularized methods for approximate low-rank tensor factorization, comparing “latent” and “overlap” norms.
- Derives theoretical mean-squared error upper bounds for the latent norm under various noise models.
- Theoretical results align with and explain prior empirical observations and are validated by simulations.
- Generalizes both norms and proves a duality theorem (Lemma 1) for structured Schatten norms.
- Paper is well-written and appears technically sound, though supplementary proofs were not fully checked.
- Results are narrowly focused and have only peripheral connections to mainstream machine learning.
- Lacks exploration or demonstration of the methods on real machine learning problems, limiting practical relevance.
- Contributions feel incremental—mainly theoretical justification of an existing empirically successful method.
- Techniques used are not novel and findings are not particularly surprising.
- Absence of lower bounds is a limitation; upper bounds alone are insufficient for strong comparison claims.
- Claim of proposing a “new class of structured Schatten norms” is potentially misleading since the generalization serves mainly as a proof tool.
- No new algorithms are proposed based on the generalized norm, nor are such algorithms analyzed or tested.
- Figure 1 appears in the introduction but requires Section 2 background to interpret, hurting initial accessibility.
- Minor stylistic/typo issues (e.g., “Tensor is”, missing comma after “Conventionally”, awkward phrasing at Line 395).
- Overall assessment: technically solid but limited impact and incremental; reviewer leans toward reject but would not strongly oppose a borderline accept.
