This paper considers convex norm-regularized optimization approaches for factorization of approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on a recently proposed "latent" norm approach [25], and its comparison to a previously used "overlap" norm approach. Specifically, the paper derives theoretical bounds on the "latent" norm mean-squared-error behavior under several noise assumptions, which compare favorably to the existing analysis on the overlap norm, supporting its better empirically-observed performance in a previous paper. The theory is also confirmed via some numerical simulations. Finally, the paper considers a generalization of both these norms, and proves a duality theorem for it. 

The paper is generally easy to follow and appears to be technically sound (although I haven't checked the proofs in the supplementary material carefully). The results may be useful for researchers focusing on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. My main concerns are that 
(i) The results are narrow and technical in nature, only peripherally related to machine learning, and potentially of limited impact at NIPS. While the authors make the (justified) case that tensor factorizations can arise in machine learning, the paper does not attempt to examine the proposed approaches in the context of machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally. 
(ii) The results seem somewhat incremental, and I didn't find them to be particularly surprising (admittedly this is a subjective notion). They seem to provide additional theoretical backing to a specific recently proposed approach [25], which was already argued to work well empirically. The techniques used also do not seem particularly novel. 
A minor defect is that the upper bounds are not accompanied by any lower bounds. This is potentially problematic, since the upper bounds are used to compare different algorithms and argue for the superiority of one over the other. 

Additional Comments:

- The abstract begins with "We study a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...". However, this may be slightly misleading. The primary focus of the paper is analyzing two existing approaches (overlapped and latent). The generalization is introduced in section 2.1 and used to prove a duality theorem (Lemma 1). No particular algorithms are proposed based on this generalized Schatten norm, nor is their behavior analyzed or tested experimentally.

The paper’s main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition approach. The paper appears technically sound, but the results are somewhat incremental and may have limited impact at NIPS.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent tensor decomposition approach, providing insights into its performance.
*   The duality result for structured Schatten norms is a novel contribution that could be of independent interest.
*   The numerical simulations support the theoretical findings.
*   The analysis addresses a challenging problem in tensor decomposition and provides a clearer understanding of the strengths of the latent approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The paper's focus is narrow and the results are largely theoretical, with limited connection to broader machine learning applications.
*   The results are somewhat incremental, building on existing work and providing theoretical justification for previously observed empirical performance.
*   The lack of lower bounds on the error limits the strength of the performance comparisons.
*   The impact of the work at NIPS, a conference focused on broader machine learning advances, may be limited.
