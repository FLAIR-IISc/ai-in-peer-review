## Review of NIPS 2013 Submission

This paper considers convex-optimization based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, specifically employing an overlapped Schatten norm to achieve improved reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well-written and presents a technically important contribution to the field. Addressing a few technical points and clarifications will significantly enhance its readability and impact.

**Summary of Changes After Rebuttal:**

The authors should significantly strengthen the motivation of this work within the broader machine learning landscape to increase accessibility. Crucially, the paper would benefit from a clear articulation of the underlying latent variable model from which this tensor decomposition arises. For example, consider a model where latent variables *h<sub>1</sub>* through *h<sub>n</sub>* connect to observed variables *x<sub>1</sub>* through *x<sub>n</sub>*, respectively. Assuming a linear relationship from hidden to observed variables, and allowing arbitrary dependence amongst the *h<sub>i</sub>*’s, the observed moment tensor naturally admits a Tucker decomposition, where the multilinear rank corresponds to *dim(h<sub>1</sub>)*, *dim(h<sub>2</sub>)*, and so on. Including such a discussion, ideally accompanied by a graphical model representation, would significantly improve understanding. Furthermore, the authors should explicitly highlight the advantage of their approach – that only *some* latent dimensions may be low, a key aspect of their result.

**Detailed Comments:**

* **Theorem 2 & Proof:** The statement of Theorem 2 is technically incomplete. The recovery of tensor components is only guaranteed up to permutation, a nuance the authors should acknowledge.  The derivation claiming that equation (12) directly implies equation (13) requires justification; the authors should elaborate on this connection.  The proof of Lemma 5, which is a critical component of Theorem 2, is currently missing and must be provided. While the proof of Lemma 4 is relatively straightforward, explicitly outlining it rather than referring to another paper will improve self-containment.

* **Related Work:** The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive review of existing work on tensor decomposition, specifically mentioning Higher Order Singular Value Decomposition (HOSVD) and its variants. Including a discussion of the approximation bounds achievable with these methods, potentially referencing Kolda’s review on tensor decompositions (section 4), would be valuable. The authors should also clarify terminology; the “mode-rank” they employ is commonly referred to as “multilinear rank” and they should acknowledge the existence of different tensor rank definitions in the introduction.  A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods would also be beneficial.

* **Incoherence Discussion:** The authors’ discussion of incoherence following equation (10) is problematic. While they equate their condition to incoherence conditions found in [1, 3, 10], this comparison appears inaccurate. The cited works typically deal with sparse-plus-low-rank recovery scenarios, whereas the noise model here is not sparse.  Incoherence in those contexts often refers to the incoherence between singular vectors and basis vectors, a concept distinct from the condition presented in this paper, which relates to energy distribution across mixture components and modes. I recommend removing the comparison to [1, 3, 10].

* **Notational Issues:** The superscript notation for components (e.g.,  \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}) is visually confusing.  Adopting a different symbol for these components would enhance clarity. The use of both \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k to represent the k-mode rank is inconsistent and should be rectified. I suggest introducing a new notation, such as `mult_rank(tensor)`, to unambiguously denote the multilinear rank.  A minor typographical error exists on the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material, where a parenthesis is missing.

* **Abstract:**  The abstract contains a minor grammatical error: “result for structures Schatten” should be changed to “result for structured Schatten.”

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a novel and valuable contribution regarding Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm, coupled with recovery guarantees. While the technical contributions are significant, addressing the points raised above – particularly clarifying the latent variable interpretation and refining the technical details – will substantially improve the paper’s clarity, accessibility, and overall impact. I recommend acceptance contingent upon these revisions.
