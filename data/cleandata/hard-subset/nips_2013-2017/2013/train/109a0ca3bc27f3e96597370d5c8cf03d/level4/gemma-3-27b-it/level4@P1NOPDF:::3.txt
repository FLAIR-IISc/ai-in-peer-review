This paper presents theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm. It improves upon existing results achieved with regularization using the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality:** The paper derives theoretical results for tensor decomposition utilizing the latent Schatten norm. The dual characterization of both the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly interesting. However, the experiments are treated somewhat briefly and lack detailed explanation. For instance, how were the Tucker ranks (r<sub>1</sub>,…r<sub>k</sub>) selected for the examples used? How does group regularization, such as l<sub>1</sub>/l<sub>∞</sub> regularization, function when the tensor exhibits low rank simultaneously across all modes? Furthermore, how does the proposed method perform in comparison to non-convex approaches? 

**Clarity:** The paper is well organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the presented arguments.

**Originality:** The primary original contribution of this paper lies in the duality established between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This result appears to be of independent interest and significance.

**Significance:** Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem with applications across many fields. Given that this paper advances the state-of-the-art for this problem, I consider it to be a significant contribution. The theoretical results are compelling and the paper is generally well written. Nonetheless, the experiments are considered rather briefly towards the end, and further elaboration would be beneficial.
