This paper presents a convex optimization-based approach for Tucker tensor decomposition using a structured, overlapped Schatten norm. The authors provide a novel theoretical analysis demonstrating that this method offers improved reconstruction guarantees, particularly in scenarios with asymmetric ranks across different modes. The paper is well-written and makes a significant technical contribution to the field of tensor methods. However, several revisions are necessary to improve its clarity, accessibility, and technical completeness.

**Major Comments**

1.  **Motivation for Machine Learning Audience:** A primary weakness is the lack of clear motivation for the problem within a machine learning context. To make the paper more accessible and impactful for a wider audience, the authors should explicitly connect this decomposition method to a parameter learning problem. For example, consider a latent variable model (which could be illustrated with a graphical model) where an observed variable `x_i` is generated from a corresponding latent variable `h_i` (e.g., via a linear transformation), and the latent variables `h_i` have arbitrary dependencies among them. The observed moment tensor in such a model has a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank directly corresponds to the dimensions of the latent variables (`dim(h_1)`, `dim(h_2)`, etc.). Framing the problem this way would clearly establish the link to parameter estimation. This framing would also provide a natural way to highlight the main advantage of the proposed method: its ability to handle cases where only a subset of the latent variables is low-dimensional, while others are not.

2.  **Clarity and Correctness of Theoretical Results:** Several key components of the main theoretical results require clarification and correction to ensure the paper is technically sound and self-contained.
    *   **Theorem 2:** The statement of Theorem 2 is imprecise. The component tensors are recovered only up to permutation and scaling, a standard caveat in decomposition problems that should be explicitly mentioned for accuracy. Furthermore, the derivation of equation (13) from (12) is stated to be immediate but warrants a clear justification.
    *   **Missing and Abbreviated Proofs:** The proof for Lemma 5, a key component in the proof of Theorem 2, is missing from the manuscript. Additionally, while the proof of Lemma 4 may be straightforward, it should be provided explicitly in the paper rather than simply referencing another source, for the benefit of the reader and to make the work self-contained.

**Minor Comments**

1.  **Related Work and Terminology:**
    *   To better situate the work, the introduction should mention that the "mode-rank" used is also commonly referred to as "multilinear rank" and briefly note that other notions of tensor rank exist.
    *   The discussion of related work should be expanded to include the Higher-Order SVD (HOSVD) and its variants, along with their associated approximation bounds for tensor decomposition (e.g., as discussed in Kolda's 2009 review).

2.  **Incoherence Condition:** The discussion of the incoherence assumption after equation (10) is potentially confusing. The authors draw a parallel to the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10], which are used for sparse + low-rank matrix recovery where singular vectors must be incoherent with standard basis vectors. The condition here, however, seems to concern the "energy" being spread across different mixture components and modes, which is conceptually different as the noise model is not sparse. This comparison should be clarified or removed to avoid misleading the reader.

3.  **Computational Complexity:** A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed optimization algorithm would be a valuable addition for readers interested in practical applications.

**Notational Issues and Typos**

To improve readability, the authors should address the following:
*   The superscript notation for components, `\hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}`, is somewhat confusing. A different symbol for the estimated components might be clearer.
*   The notation for k-mode rank is inconsistent, with both `\bar{r}k` and `\underline{r}k` being used. Adopting a single, clear operator like `mult_rank(T)` would be preferable.
*   In the abstract, "result for structures Schatten" should be corrected to "structured Schatten."
*   In the supplementary material, the last line of page 12 is missing a parenthesis.

In summary, this paper introduces a novel and technically important result for Tucker tensor decomposition with strong recovery guarantees. Addressing the issues of motivation, technical clarity, and the minor points listed above will significantly strengthen the paper and broaden its impact.
