Additional comments after the rebuttal:

To enhance the paper's accessibility to a broader audience, the authors should provide a more thorough motivation for the problem in the context of machine learning. Furthermore, it would be beneficial to include a discussion on the latent variable model, accompanied by a graphical representation, to illustrate how the tensor decomposition can learn its parameters. Specifically, the authors could consider a model where each latent variable hi is connected to an observed variable xi, with a linear relationship between the hidden and observed variables, and arbitrary dependencies among the hi's. In this scenario, the observed moment tensor would exhibit a Tucker decomposition, with its multilinear rank corresponding to the dimensions of the hi's. A diagram depicting this model would be a valuable addition. The authors should also note that it is possible for only some hidden variables to have low dimensions, which is a key advantage of the new result presented in the paper.

The paper explores convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors successfully employ the overlapped Schatten norm, demonstrating its ability to yield improved reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well-written and makes significant technical contributions. Addressing a few technical and notational issues will further enhance its readability.

Regarding Theorem 2 and its proof:

A minor correction is needed in the statement of Theorem 2, as the components are only recovered up to a permutation. Although a minor issue, the authors should acknowledge this.

The transition from equation (12) to equation (13) in Theorem 2 requires justification.

The proof of Lemma 5, which plays a crucial role in Theorem 2, is missing and should be included.

While the proof of Lemma 4 is straightforward, it would be beneficial to provide an explicit clarification rather than referring to another paper.

Minor issues:

The authors should include additional related work on HOSVD and other methods for tensor decomposition, such as higher-order SVD and its variants, which provide approximation bounds for tensor decomposition. A relevant reference is Kolda's review on tensor decompositions (section 4).

The authors should note that the notion of rank they use, referred to as mode-rank, is also known as multilinear rank. They should mention this in the introduction and acknowledge the existence of different notions of tensor rank.

A brief discussion on the computational complexity of the employed methods would be a valuable addition.

The authors should clarify their discussion on incoherence after equation (10). The term incoherence is used to describe the need for the "energy" to be spread across different mixture components and modes, with no component or mode having too high a singular value. However, this is distinct from the incoherence conditions in references [1,3,10], which are used for sparse+low rank recovery. The authors should remove this comparison.

Typos/notational issues:

The superscript notation for components \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)} is confusing and could be replaced with a different symbol.

The notation \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k, both used for k-mode rank, should be changed. Introducing mult_rank(tensor) to denote the multilinear rank would be a more consistent approach.

A missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material should be corrected.

The abstract should be revised to read "result for structured Schatten" instead of "result for structures Schatten". 

The paper is well-written and makes novel contributions to finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm, providing guarantees for recovery. However, some technical clarifications are needed, which the authors will hopefully address.
