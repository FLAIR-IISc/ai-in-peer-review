- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximate low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the latent vs. overlap norms.  
- Derives mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under various noise models, favorably comparing to overlap-norm analyses.  
- Theoretical findings are supported by numerical experiments.  
- Generalizes both norms and proves a duality theorem for the extended framework.  
- Paper is well-structured and technically sound (supplementary proofs not fully verified by reviewer).  
- Main concern: work is narrowly technical and only tangentially relevant to ML, limiting NIPS impact due to lack of integration into ML problem contexts.  
- Secondary concern: contributions seem incremental, mainly providing additional theoretical support for an already empirically validated method [25] without novel techniques.  
- Minor issue: no lower bounds are provided, weakening claims of algorithmic superiority.  
- Abstract’s claim of a “new class of structured Schatten norms” is misleading; the generalization is used only to prove duality and no new algorithms are proposed or tested.  
- Figure 1 appears in the introduction but lacks interpretability without Section 2 context.  
- Minor typos/wording issues: Line 38, Line 40 (missing comma), Line 395.  
- Overall: core contribution is theoretical error bounds for a specific tensor decomposition method; technically solid but incremental and likely limited impact at NIPS.
