- Primary contribution: theoretical analysis of the latent structured Schatten-norm approach, deriving MSE bounds that scale with the minimum mode-k rank and explaining its advantage over the overlapped norm; validated by simulations.

- Introduces a generalized class of structured Schatten norms and proves a duality theorem linking these norms to structured sparsity.

- Strength: Provides rigorous theoretical justification for the latent method’s empirically observed superiority, especially when the tensor is low-rank in a single unknown mode.

- Strength: Technical formulation and Lemma 1 (duality) are elegant and unify different tensor-norm approaches.

- Strength: Paper is well-written and the numerical experiments support the theoretical error-scaling claims.

- Weakness: Significance is limited by focus on synthetic denoising tasks with no demonstration of impact on downstream ML problems.

- Weakness: Originality is incremental since the work analyzes a method recently proposed in [25] and relies on standard analytical techniques.

- Weakness: Analysis provides only upper bounds (no lower bounds), making it hard to assess tightness and fully compare algorithms.

- Comment: Abstract wording (“We study a new class…”) is slightly misleading because no new algorithms based on the generalization are proposed or evaluated.

- Comment: Figure 1 is hard to interpret in the introduction and should be moved or given more context in its caption.

- Minor writing fixes suggested: add comma after “Conventionally, tensor decomposition…” (Line 40) and rephrase “also in such situations” to “in such situations as well” (Line 395).
