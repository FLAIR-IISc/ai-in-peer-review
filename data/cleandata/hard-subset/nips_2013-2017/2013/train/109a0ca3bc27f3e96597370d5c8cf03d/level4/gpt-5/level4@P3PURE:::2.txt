Additional comments after the rebuttal:
To make the paper accessible to a broader machine learning audience, the motivation needs to be strengthened. Please provide an explicit latent variable model (with a graphical model diagram) in which the proposed tensor decomposition learns the underlying parameters. For example, consider a model where latent variable h1 connects to observed x1, h2 to x2, and so on, with a linear mapping from each hidden variable to its corresponding observed variable, and arbitrary dependence among the hi’s. In this setting, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank is dim(h1), dim(h2), …. Please add this discussion and, preferably, a figure depicting the model. Also note explicitly that it may be the case that only some hidden variables have small dimensions and others do not; highlighting this scenario is important because it is a main advantage of the new result in the paper.

Summary and overall assessment:
The paper studies convex-optimization–based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms and analyzes recovery properties of the overlapped Schatten norm, leading to improved reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well written and makes technically important contributions. Addressing the technical and notational issues below will improve clarity and readability.

Clarifications regarding Theorem 2 and its proof:
- The statement of Theorem 2 is not strictly correct: the components are only identifiable up to a permutation. Please state this explicitly.
- In Theorem 2, the step claiming that (12) immediately implies (13) requires justification. Please include a clear argument for this implication.
- The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, yet it is a key component of the proof of Theorem 2. Please provide the proof.
- It would be better to present an explicit proof of Lemma 4 rather than only referring to another paper, even if the argument is straightforward.

Minor issues and related work:
- Please add related work on HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods. In particular, discuss how higher-order SVD and its variants provide approximation bounds; see Kolda’s review on tensor decompositions (Section 4).
- The notion of rank you refer to as “mode-rank” is also known as “multilinear rank.” Please mention this and note in the introduction that there are multiple, non-equivalent notions of tensor rank.
- Include a brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods.

On incoherence (after equation (10)):
- The current use of “incoherence” appears to mean that energy is spread across mixture components and modes—i.e., no single component or mode has an excessively large singular value. The paper states this is the same as the incoherence conditions in [1, 3, 10], but this equivalence is not clear. In [1, 3, 10], incoherence is formulated for sparse + low-rank recovery, where the noise is sparse and the condition involves singular vectors being incoherent with the standard basis. That setting and condition differ from the one here. I suggest removing this comparison or clearly distinguishing the conditions.

Typos and notational issues:
- The superscript notation for components, e.g., \hat{𝒲}^{(k)}, is confusing. Please consider an alternative symbol or subscript-based convention for components.
- The notation \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k are both used for the k-mode rank; this is confusing and should be unified. Consider introducing a function such as mult_rank(tensor) to denote the multilinear rank.
- Supplementary material, last line of page 12: a parenthesis is missing.
- Abstract: “result for structures Schatten” should be “result for structured Schatten.”

Conclusion:
The paper makes a novel and valuable contribution by obtaining a strong Tucker tensor decomposition via an overlapped Schatten norm with recovery guarantees. With improved motivation for the machine learning audience, the requested clarifications (especially around Theorem 2 and its supporting lemmas), and the suggested notational and related-work refinements, the paper will be significantly strengthened.
