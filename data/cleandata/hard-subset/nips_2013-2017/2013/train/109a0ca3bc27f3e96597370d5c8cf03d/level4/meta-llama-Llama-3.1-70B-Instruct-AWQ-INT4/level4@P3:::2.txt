This paper introduces a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, which includes two recently proposed norms for convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition. The authors analyze the performance of the "latent" approach for tensor decomposition, which was empirically found to perform better than the "overlapped" approach in some settings. They show theoretically that this is indeed the case, particularly when the unknown true tensor is low-rank in a specific unknown mode. The authors also present a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms, which is interesting in the general context of structured sparsity.

The paper is well-written and makes technically important contributions. However, there are a few technical and notational issues that need to be addressed. The authors should clarify the statement of Theorem 2 and its proof, as the components are only recovered up to a permutation. Additionally, the proof of Lemma 5 is missing, and the authors should provide a justification for how (12) immediately leads to (13).

The authors should also add more related work on HOSVD and other methods for tensor decomposition, and clarify the notion of rank they use, which is also referred to as multilinear rank. They should briefly discuss the computational complexity of the methods employed and clarify their discussion about incoherence after (10).

There are some minor issues with notation, such as the superscript notation for components and the use of both \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k for k-mode rank. The authors should consider introducing mult_rank(tensor) to denote the multilinear rank.

The paper provides a good motivation for the problem, but the authors could do a better job of motivating the problem for machine learning and making it accessible to a wider audience. They should provide the latent variable model where this tensor decomposition can learn the parameters and point out that it is possible that only some hidden variables are of small dimensions and not the others.

Overall, the paper makes novel contributions in terms of finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm and provides guarantees for recovery. With some technical clarifications and minor revisions, the paper will be even more readable and impactful.
