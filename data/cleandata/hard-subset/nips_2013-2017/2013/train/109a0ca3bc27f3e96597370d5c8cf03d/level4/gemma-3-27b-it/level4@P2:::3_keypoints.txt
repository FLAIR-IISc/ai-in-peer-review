- Paper analyzes convex tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm and shows improvements over results with the overlapped Schatten norm.  
- Main contribution is establishing a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, of independent interest.  
- Theoretical results are sound and the dual characterization is particularly noteworthy.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and would benefit from expansion.  
- Missing details on selection of Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) weaken the empirical analysis.  
- Would be valuable to discuss performance of group regularization methods (e.g., l1,∞) when tensors are simultaneously low-rank across modes.  
- A comparison to non-convex decomposition approaches is needed for broader context.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and uses consistent notation.  
- The duality result is an original contribution that generalizes prior structured sparsity work.  
- Advances theoretical understanding of the latent Schatten-norm approach beyond prior convex tensor decomposition studies.  
- Results are significant due to tensor decomposition’s broad applications and the paper’s theoretical improvements.  
- Expanding the experimental section would increase the paper’s practical impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical analysis, valuable duality result, rigorous proofs, and relevance to an important problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: brief experiments lacking parameter details and comparisons, limited discussion of practical implications, and insufficient exploration of links to areas like compressed sensing or matrix completion.
