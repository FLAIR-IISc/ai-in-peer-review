

This paper investigates convex norm-regularized optimization methods for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, with a focus on the recently proposed "latent" norm approach [25] and its comparison to the established "overlap" norm. The authors derive theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under various noise assumptions, demonstrating favorable comparisons to existing overlap norm analysis and supporting the empirically observed superior performance of the latent norm in prior work. These theoretical findings are corroborated by numerical simulations. Additionally, the paper generalizes both norms and proves a duality theorem for this extension.  

The paper is well-written and technically sound (though I have not verified all supplementary proofs in detail). The results may interest researchers in tensor decomposition optimization. However, my primary concerns are:  
(i) The contributions are narrowly focused and technically specialized, tangentially related to machine learning. While the authors justify tensor factorization's relevance to ML, the work fails to evaluate the proposed approaches in actual ML contexts—neither theoretically nor experimentally—limiting its potential impact at NIPS.  
(ii) The results appear incremental, offering only supplementary theoretical support for a recently proposed method [25] that was already empirically validated. The techniques employed lack novelty, and the findings are not particularly surprising.  

A more significant limitation is the absence of lower bounds for the derived error upper bounds. This is problematic since the upper bounds are used to claim superiority of one algorithm over another, without establishing tightness.  

**Additional Comments**  
- The abstract states, "We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors," which is misleading. The paper centers on analyzing two existing approaches (overlap and latent norms). The generalization in Section 2.1 merely defines the extended norm and proves its dual (Lemma 1), without introducing new algorithms, analyzing their behavior, or providing experimental validation.  
- Figure 1 appears in the introduction but remains uninterpretable without the background provided in Section 2 onward.  
- Line 38: "Tensor is" → "Tensor modeling is"  
- Line 40: Missing comma after "Conventionally"  
- Line 395: "also in such situations" → "in such situations as well"  

The paper’s core contribution—theoretical error bounds for a specific tensor decomposition approach—is technically sound but incremental and lacks sufficient ML relevance to justify a NIPS publication.
