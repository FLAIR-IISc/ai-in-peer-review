- Compares overlapped vs latent structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition.  
- Theoretically shows latent approach outperforms when tensor is low-rank in a specific unknown mode; error scales with minimal mode-k rank instead of average rank.  
- Proves a novel duality between these structured norms, linking to structured sparsity literature.  
- Numerical experiments confirm theoretical predictions, showing precise MSE scaling behavior.  
- Technically important, rigorous analysis providing performance guarantees that explain empirical superiority; results are sound and well-motivated.  
- Theorem 2 must state that components are recovered only up to permutation.  
- The derivation from inequality (12) to (13) needs justification.  
- Proof of Lemma 5 is missing and is crucial for Theorem 2.  
- Proof of Lemma 4 should be given explicitly instead of relying on another paper.  
- Add a more detailed latent variable model discussion, including a graphical model showing hidden-to-observed linear relationships.  
- Explicitly address scenarios where only some hidden variables have small dimensions (a key advantage of the latent approach).  
- Expand related work discussion on HOSVD and tensor decomposition methods; cite Kolda’s review (Section 4).

- Overall: strong, significant contribution with practical implications, but requires the listed technical and notational clarifications before publication.
