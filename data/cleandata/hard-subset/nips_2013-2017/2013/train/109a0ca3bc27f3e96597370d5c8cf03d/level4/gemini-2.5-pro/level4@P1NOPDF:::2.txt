Additional comments after the rebuttal:

To make the paper accessible to a broader machine learning audience, the authors should provide stronger motivation for the problem. Specifically, the authors should introduce a latent variable model whose parameters can be learned via the proposed tensor decomposition. A graphical model representation would be beneficial. For instance, consider a model where latent variables h_i are connected to observed variables x_i, with a linear relationship from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among the h_i. In such a model, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank corresponds to the dimensions of the latent variables (dim(h_1), dim(h_2), ...). The paper would be strengthened by including this discussion, ideally accompanied by a figure illustrating the model. Furthermore, the authors should highlight that a key advantage of their proposed method is its ability to handle cases where only a subset of the latent variables have small dimensions, while others may be high-dimensional.

This paper presents a novel convex optimization-based approach for Tucker tensor decomposition. The authors utilize the overlapped Schatten norm and demonstrate that it yields improved theoretical recovery guarantees. The work is well-written and makes a significant technical contribution. However, several technical and notational issues, detailed below, should be addressed to improve the paper's clarity and impact.

**Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:**

*   The statement of Theorem 2 is not technically precise: the components are recovered only up to a permutation. While this is a minor point, it should be explicitly mentioned for correctness.
*   The claim in Theorem 2 that equation (13) immediately follows from (12) requires justification.
*   The proof of Lemma 5, which is a key component of the proof for Theorem 2, is missing.
*   For completeness, it would be beneficial to include the proof of Lemma 4 explicitly, rather than simply citing another paper, even if the proof is straightforward.

**Minor issues:**

*   **Related Work:** The related work section could be expanded to include more discussion on HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods. For example, the authors could discuss the approximation bounds provided by Higher-Order SVD (HOSVD) and its variants, referencing seminal works such as Kolda's review on tensor decompositions (Section 4).
*   **Rank Terminology:** The authors refer to the notion of rank as "mode-rank," which is also commonly known as "multilinear rank." It would be helpful to mention this synonym and briefly acknowledge in the introduction that several different definitions of tensor rank exist.
*   **Computational Complexity:** A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods should be included.
*   **Incoherence Condition:** The discussion of "incoherence" following equation (10) requires clarification. As I understand it, the authors use this term to describe a condition where the tensor's "energy" is well-distributed across its components and modes, meaning no single component or mode has a disproportionately large singular value. The authors claim this is analogous to the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10], but this connection is not clear. The conditions in [1,3,10] are typically used for sparse + low-rank matrix recovery and concern the incoherence between singular vectors and standard basis vectors. In this paper, the noise is not assumed to be sparse, and the condition appears different. I suggest removing this comparison to avoid potential confusion.

**Typos and Notational Issues:**

*   The superscript notation for components, \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}, is potentially confusing. I suggest using a different symbol to denote the components.
*   The notation for k-mode rank is inconsistent, with both \bar{r}_k and \underline{r}_k being used. This should be unified. Perhaps introducing a clearer operator, such as `mult_rank(tensor)`, would be preferable.
*   A parenthesis appears to be missing in the last line of page 12 of the supplementary material.
*   In the abstract, "result for structures Schatten" should be corrected to "result for structured Schatten."
