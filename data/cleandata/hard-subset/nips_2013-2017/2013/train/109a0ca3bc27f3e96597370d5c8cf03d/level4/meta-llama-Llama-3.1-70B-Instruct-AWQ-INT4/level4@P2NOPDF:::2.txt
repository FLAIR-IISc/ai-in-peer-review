Additional comments after the rebuttal:

To make the paper more accessible to a broader audience, the authors should provide a clearer motivation for the problem in the context of machine learning. A key addition would be the inclusion of a latent variable model, ideally with a graphical representation, to illustrate how tensor decomposition can learn model parameters. Specifically, the authors could consider a model where latent variables h1, h2, etc., connect to observed variables x1, x2, etc., assuming a linear relationship between hidden and observed variables and arbitrary dependence among the latent variables. This model would demonstrate how the observed moment tensor can be represented as a Tucker decomposition, with its multilinear rank corresponding to the dimensions of the latent variables (dim(h1), dim(h2), etc.). A visual representation of this model would be beneficial. Furthermore, the authors should highlight that their new result allows for some hidden variables to have small dimensions, which is a primary advantage of their approach.

The paper presents a well-written and technically significant contribution to convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors' employment of the overlapped Schatten norm leads to improved reconstruction guarantees. However, some technical and notational issues need to be addressed to enhance the paper's readability.

Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:

The statement of Theorem 2 is not entirely accurate, as the components are only recovered up to a permutation. Although a minor issue, the authors should acknowledge this.

In Theorem 2, the authors claim that equation (12) immediately leads to equation (13), but this requires justification.

The proof of Lemma 5 is missing and is a crucial component of Theorem 2. It is essential to include this proof.

While the proof of Lemma 4 is straightforward, it would be beneficial to provide a clear explanation rather than referring to another paper.

Minor issues:

The authors should expand their discussion of related work on Higher-Order Singular Value Decomposition (HOSVD) and other tensor decomposition methods. Specifically, they could mention how HOSVD and its variants provide approximation bounds for tensor decomposition, as discussed in Kolda's review on tensor decompositions (section 4).

The authors use the term "mode-rank" to refer to the rank of the tensor, which is also known as multilinear rank. They should acknowledge this alternative terminology in the introduction and note that there are different notions of tensor rank.

A brief discussion on the computational complexity of the employed methods would be beneficial.

The authors' discussion of incoherence after equation (10) requires clarification. They use the term to imply that the "energy" should be spread across different mixture components and modes, preventing any component or mode from having too high a singular value. However, this is distinct from the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10], which are used for sparse and low-rank recovery. The conditions in [1,3,10] involve the singular vectors being incoherent with the basis vectors, which is different from the condition presented in the paper. It is suggested that the authors remove this comparison.

Typos/notational issues:

The superscript notation for components \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)} is confusing and could be replaced with a different symbol.

The notation \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k, both used for k-mode rank, should be changed. Introducing a notation such as mult_rank(tensor) to denote the multilinear rank would be more consistent.

There is a missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material.

In the abstract, "result for structures Schatten" should be corrected to "structured."

Overall, the paper presents a novel contribution to finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm and provides guarantees for recovery. However, addressing the technical clarifications mentioned above will enhance the paper's quality.
