- Presents theoretical performance bounds for convex tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm, improving on bounds for the overlapped Schatten norm.  
- Dual characterization of overlapped and latent Schatten norms is an interesting, independently valuable theoretical contribution.  
- Solid theoretical results overall, but experimental evaluation is cursory and underdeveloped.  
- Experimental setup lacks explanation of how Tucker ranks (r1,…,rk) were chosen, hurting reproducibility.  
- No discussion of how group regularization (e.g., l1 or l∞) behaves when tensors are low-rank across all modes.  
- Crucial omission: no empirical comparison to state-of-the-art non-convex tensor decomposition methods.  
- Paper is well-organized; notation and derivations are clear and logical.  
- Theoretical improvements are significant for tensor decomposition, but practical impact is limited by weak experiments.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical advance (notably the duality result) but weakened by insufficient empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: expand experimental section to include comprehensive comparisons with non-convex methods.  
- Recommendation: detail and justify Tucker rank selection for test examples to improve reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: address group-regularization behavior and discuss practical limitations (scalability, parameter sensitivity).
