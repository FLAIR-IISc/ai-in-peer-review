- Presents a theoretical analysis of the convex "latent" low-rank tensor decomposition and introduces a structured Schatten-norm framework.
- Proves a novel duality connecting the latent and overlapped Schatten-norm classes.
- Main theoretical result: MSE for the latent method scales with the minimum mode-k rank, contrasting with overlapped methods where error scales with the average mode-k rank.
- Numerical experiments validate the predicted scaling behaviors for both latent and overlapped methods.
- High technical quality: rigorous derivations (Lemma 1, Theorems 2 & 3) building on established high-dimensional statistics techniques.
- Key insight: the latent norm's complexity is governed by the minimum of the mode-k ranks (Lemma 2), yielding a tight bound.
- Experiments are carefully designed to verify specific scaling laws using "TR complexity" and "LR complexity" plots.
- Authors explicitly note limitations and required conditions, such as an incoherence assumption.
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, with precise definitions and a clear progression from norms and duality to performance bounds.
- Minor clarity suggestion: explicitly state whether experiments used the constrained formulation (11) and how the incoherence parameter alpha was chosen.
- Originality: first rigorous performance analysis of the latent approach and a novel generalization of overlapped/latent norms to structured Schatten norms.
- The duality result appears new for tensors and connects the work to structured sparsity literature.
- Significance: provides actionable guidance to prefer the latent approach when a tensor is low-rank in only one mode and is likely influential for future research.
- Pros: first rigorous analysis, clean min(r_k) result, elegant unifying framework and duality, clear writing and supportive experiments.
- Cons: reliance on an incoherence assumption and constrained formulation; practical implications of selecting the constraint parameter alpha are not discussed.
