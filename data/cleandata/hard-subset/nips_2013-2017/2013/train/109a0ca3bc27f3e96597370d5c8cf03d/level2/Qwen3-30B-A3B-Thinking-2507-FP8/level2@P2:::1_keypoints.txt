- Paper studies structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition and the duality between overlapped and latent approaches.  
- Main theoretical result: dual of the overlapped Schatten norm is the latent Schatten norm (Lemma 1).  
- Proves error bounds showing latent approach error scales with minimum mode-k rank, overlapped with average mode-k rank (Theorems 1–3).  
- Theoretical analysis explains empirical superiority of latent approach when tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.  
- Numerical experiments on synthetic data confirm predicted scaling of mean squared error with dimensions and ranks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses appropriate notation and background.  
- Connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity/group-lasso literature, generalizing known results.  
- Novelty concern: duality result not clearly distinguished from prior structured sparsity work and previous introduction of structured Schatten norms.  
- Empirical limitation: experiments are synthetic only and do not explore scenarios where latent fails or overlapped may be preferable.  
- Practical limitation: paper omits discussion and comparison of computational complexity between approaches.  
- Significance: provides valuable theoretical explanation for latent approach but limited demonstration of practical advantage on real-world data.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify novelty relative to prior work, analyze conditions favoring each approach, add real-world experiments, and discuss computational trade-offs.  
- Overall: technically sound with solid theoretical contributions, but broader practical impact and novelty clarity could be improved.
