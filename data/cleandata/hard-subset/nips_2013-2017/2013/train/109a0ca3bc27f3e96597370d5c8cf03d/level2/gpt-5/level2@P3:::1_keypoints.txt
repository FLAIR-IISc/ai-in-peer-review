- Introduces a unified framework of structured tensor Schatten norms: overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* families.  
- Proves a duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm, generalizing overlapped/latent group sparsity dualities.  
- Analyzes latent convex tensor decomposition, providing consistency, deterministic, and high-probability Gaussian-noise error bounds.  
- Shows denoising error scales with the minimum mode rank of the true tensor, explaining latent approach advantages when one mode is predominantly low-rank.  
- Synthetic experiments validate predicted linear scaling with proposed complexity measures and improve over overlapped approach in [26].  
- Builds on convex tensor nuclear/trace-norm literature and connects to decomposable regularizers and structured sparsity frameworks.  
- Duality result extends overlapped/latent group-lasso dualities to tensor Schatten norms.  
- Statistical analysis adapts high-dimensional M-estimation techniques to tensor unfoldings and latent decompositions.  
- Technical development and proofs appear sound; bounds incorporate an incoherence-like constraint controlling cross-mode interactions.  
- Comparison to overlapped bounds is fair and highlights different complexity drivers (min vs average mode rank).  
- Experiments match theoretical predictions, including appropriate scaling of the regularization parameter.  
- Clarity is generally good with precise definitions; a small illustrative example and clearer statement about constrained formulation (11) would help.  
- Novelty: the duality and min-mode-rank scaling analysis are original and advance understanding of latent tensor methods.  
- Significance: provides theoretical and practical guidance favoring latent formulations in anisotropic low-rank noisy settings and informs broader structured tensor estimator design.  
- Pros: rigorous duality, meaningful statistical guarantees, error bounds depending on min mode rank, and experiments validating scaling and λ selection.  
- Cons: reliance on spectral incoherence constraints and constrained optimization (α choice/enforcement) is not fully clarified or shown active in experiments.  
- Cons: experiments limited to synthetic denoising of moderate size; no tensor completion or real-data evaluations.  
- Cons: focus mainly on S1/1 (and S∞/∞ dual); limited exploration of other Sp/q choices.  
- Cons: LR complexity plotting uses oracle-like rank estimates from noiseless latent runs, which may not be accessible in practice.  
- Cons: computational aspects (scaling, convergence, wall-clock comparisons between overlapped and latent solvers) are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Suggestions: clarify role and enforcement of spectral constraints in (11), report how α was chosen, and provide sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggestions: include at least one tensor completion experiment and a small real-data example.  
- Suggestions: add a brief proof sketch of the duality lemma in the main text and an illustrative rk vs r̄k example.  
- Suggestions: report computational costs and convergence behavior for overlapped and latent ADMM solvers.  
- Suggestions: discuss extending analysis to other Sp/q norms (e.g., overlapped S1/∞).  
- Minor comments: ensure consistent notation for unfoldings and ranks, restate rk vs r̄k near Theorem 3, and fix small editorial errors.  
- Overall: solid, timely contribution recommending acceptance pending clarifications on constrained formulation and modest additional experiments.
