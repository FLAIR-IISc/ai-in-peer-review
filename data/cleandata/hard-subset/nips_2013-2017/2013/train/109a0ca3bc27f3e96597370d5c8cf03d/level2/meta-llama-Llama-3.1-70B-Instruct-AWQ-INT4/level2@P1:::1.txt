This paper studies a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors and their application to convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition. The authors analyze the performance of the "latent" approach for tensor decomposition, which was empirically found to perform better than the "overlapped" approach in some settings. The main contribution of the paper is a theoretical analysis of the latent approach, which shows that it performs as well as knowing the mode with the smallest rank.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem of tensor decomposition and the limitations of existing approaches. They also provide a detailed explanation of the structured Schatten norms and their properties.

The theoretical analysis of the latent approach is rigorous and well-presented. The authors provide a generic consistency statement that does not take the low-rank-ness of the truth into account, as well as a tighter bound that takes the low-rank-ness into account with some incoherence assumption. They also compare the performance of the latent approach with the overlapped approach and provide an explanation for the empirically observed superior performance of the latent approach.

The numerical results presented in the paper confirm the theoretically obtained scaling behavior of the mean squared error as a function of the dimensionalities and the sum of ranks of the factors of the unknown tensor. The results show that the latent approach performs at least as well as the overlapped approach in many cases, and that the success of the latent approach relative to the overlapped approach is correlated with high TR complexity to LR complexity ratio.

The paper also discusses the relationship between the structured Schatten norms and structured sparsity, and shows that the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is closely related to the duality between the overlapped and latent group sparsity norms.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of tensor decomposition and provides a rigorous analysis of the latent approach. The results presented in the paper are well-supported by theoretical analysis and numerical experiments, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a rigorous analysis of the latent approach for tensor decomposition.
* The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem of tensor decomposition and the limitations of existing approaches.
* The paper presents a detailed explanation of the structured Schatten norms and their properties.
* The numerical results confirm the theoretically obtained scaling behavior of the mean squared error.
* The paper discusses the relationship between the structured Schatten norms and structured sparsity.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the reader is familiar with the basics of tensor decomposition and structured sparsity.
* Some of the proofs are presented in the supplementary material, which may make it difficult for readers to follow the paper.
* The paper could benefit from more discussion on the implications of the results and potential applications.
