This paper studies convex tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, specifically focusing on the "latent" approach proposed in recent work and demonstrating its advantages over the "overlapped" approach. The authors provide theoretical analysis, proving that the latent approach achieves error bounds scaling with the minimum mode-k rank, and a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms. They also provide empirical validation of their theoretical findings.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a crucial problem in tensor decomposition – the challenge of model selection and generalization. The connection to structured sparsity and group lasso is clearly articulated, providing valuable context. The theoretical contributions, especially the analysis of the latent approach and the duality result, are significant. The paper builds upon and provides theoretical justification for empirical observations made in prior work (specifically [25]), which is a strength. The numerical experiments support the theoretical results and demonstrate the practical benefits of the latent approach, even in cases where the overlapped approach *should* perform well. The writing is generally clear and organized, and the paper follows a logical structure. The connection to related work is well-established, with appropriate citations.

However, there are some weaknesses. While the paper establishes a theoretical advantage for the latent approach, the constant factors hidden in the asymptotic bounds are not fully explored.  The assumptions required to achieve these bounds (e.g., the incoherence assumption in Theorem 2) might be strong and their practical implications aren't thoroughly discussed. The practical considerations for choosing the regularization parameter λ are somewhat glossed over – the statement that it’s independent of rank is helpful but doesn't offer guidance on how to set it in practice beyond the theoretical upper bound. The discussion of the limitations of the approach is somewhat limited.  While the paper acknowledges a connection to tensor completion as future work, it doesn’t discuss how the results might generalize or break down in the tensor completion setting. The connection to existing work on low-rank matrix completion via nuclear norm minimization could be made more explicit, and discussing differences in the theoretical guarantees would be valuable.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Strong theoretical contributions:** The analysis of the latent approach and the duality result are novel and significant.
*   **Clear explanation of empirical success:** The paper provides a theoretical justification for the observed advantages of the latent approach.
*   **Well-written and organized:** The paper is easy to follow and logically structured.
*   **Relevance to NIPS audience:** The topic of convex optimization and tensor decomposition is highly relevant to the NIPS community.
*   **Empirical Validation:**  The numerical results support the theoretical findings.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Strong assumptions:** The theoretical bounds rely on potentially strong assumptions that might not hold in practice.
*   **Limited discussion of practical considerations:** Choosing the regularization parameter and the implications of the assumptions need further discussion.
*   **Missing links to related work:**  More explicit connections to the broader literature on low-rank matrix completion would be helpful.
*   **Constant factors:** The lack of discussion regarding constant factors in the asymptotic analysis.



**Overall assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field of tensor decomposition and warrants acceptance. The theoretical results are significant, the paper is well-written and organized, and the empirical validation supports the claims. While there are some areas for improvement, these do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. The strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses.

**Recommendation:** Accept. I would encourage the authors to address the points raised regarding assumptions, practical considerations, and connections to related work in a future version.
