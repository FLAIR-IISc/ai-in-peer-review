- Paper studies structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, comparing the "latent" and "overlapped" approaches.  
- Main theoretical result: latent approach error bounds scale with the minimum mode-k rank, improving over overlapped’s average-rank scaling.  
- Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis building on convex optimization and matrix completion literature.  
- Clear motivation and presentation, with a strong explanation of why the latent approach performs better.  
- Numerical experiments validate the theoretical scaling predictions.  
- Experimental validation is somewhat limited; needs more tensor sizes, rank configurations, and noise-level tests.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity and practical scalability of the latent approach.  
- Connection to broader structured sparsity literature should be woven more throughout the paper, not just Section 2.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution; accept with minor revisions to address experiments, complexity discussion, and literature linkage.
