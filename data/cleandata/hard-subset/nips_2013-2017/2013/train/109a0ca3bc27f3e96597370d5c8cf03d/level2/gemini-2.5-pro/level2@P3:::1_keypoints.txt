- Presents a theoretical analysis explaining why the latent convex tensor decomposition outperforms the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Introduces a general framework of structured Schatten norms that unifies overlapped and latent formulations.  
- Proves a duality result: the overlapped Sp/q-norm is the dual of the latent Sp*/q*-norm.  
- Derives noisy recovery bounds showing the latent estimator’s MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank (not the average).  
- Shows synthetic experiments that validate the predicted linear relationship between empirical error and the theoretical complexity measures.  
- High technical quality with rigorous proofs and well-supported theoretical claims.  
- Main theorems (2 and 3) give clear, interpretable error bounds linking performance to tensor rank structure.  
- Analysis uses standard incoherence assumptions and additional spectral-norm constraints to obtain sharper bounds.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well organized, with helpful comparisons between overlapped and latent approaches.  
- Original contribution: first rigorous performance analysis of the latent method and the minimal-rank insight.  
- The structured Schatten norm formulation and duality lemma are novel and connect to structured sparsity literature.  
- Significant impact: explains when latent is preferable and suggests it adapts to the simplest mode without oracle knowledge.  
- Suggest clarifying the practical handling of the incoherence parameter α and whether the spectral constraints were active in experiments.  
- Suggest adding discussion of computational complexity and whether the latent formulation’s K variables impose practical overhead (e.g., with ADMM).
