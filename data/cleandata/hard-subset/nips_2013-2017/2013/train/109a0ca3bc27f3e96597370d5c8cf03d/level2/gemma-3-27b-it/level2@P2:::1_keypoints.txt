- Provides theoretical justification that the latent convex tensor decomposition outperforms the overlapped approach, with error bounds scaling with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average rank.  
- Proves a useful duality result between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms, extending structured sparsity theory.  
- Paper is well-motivated, technically sound, and addresses an important problem in tensor decomposition.  
- Clear presentation with proper definitions and good connections to prior work (overlapped approach, group lasso).  
- Numerical experiments confirm the theoretical scaling and show latent approach superiority even in borderline cases.  
- Effectively connects results to literature in tensor decomposition, structured sparsity, and matrix completion.  
- Theoretical analysis depends on assumptions (specific incoherence condition, Gaussian noise) that may not hold in practice and need discussion.  
- Lacks discussion of practical implementation details, especially computational complexity and solver scalability.  
- Numerical evaluation is limited in scope; wider ranges of tensor sizes, ranks, noise levels, and violated-assumption cases should be tested.  
- Paper focuses mainly on latent vs. overlapped comparisons and should contrast with other tensor methods (non-convex, Tucker) regarding accuracy, speed, and robustness.  
- Strengths support acceptance: addresses an open problem, provides strong theory, introduces a novel duality, and validates results numerically.  
- Concerns against acceptance: reliance on potentially strong assumptions, limited empirical breadth, and insufficient practical/implementation discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to discuss assumption limitations, computational complexity/scalability, and to broaden empirical validation.
