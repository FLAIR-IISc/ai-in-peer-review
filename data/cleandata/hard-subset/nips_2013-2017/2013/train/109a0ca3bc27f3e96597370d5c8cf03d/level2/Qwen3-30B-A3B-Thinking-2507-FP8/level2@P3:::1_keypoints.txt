- Introduces a novel theoretical framework for structured Schatten norms in tensor decomposition, comparing "overlapped" and "latent" convex approaches.  
- Builds on recent tensor decomposition and structured sparsity literature, connecting prior proposals [9,15,23,25] and [2,13,17,21].  
- Main contribution: theoretical explanation for why the latent approach outperforms overlapped when the true tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.  
- Presents a novel duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms (Lemma 1), extending structured sparsity theory.  
- Theorem 2: latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, unlike overlapped which scales with average rank.  
- Provides a precise theoretical foundation explaining prior empirical observations [25,26].  
- Theoretical results are rigorously proven with proofs in supplementary material.  
- Numerical experiments confirm theoretical predictions, showing MSE scales linearly with predicted complexity measures.  
- Careful discussion of limitations: latent excels for mode-specific low-rankness but remains effective for simultaneously low-rank tensors.  
- Clarity and organization are excellent, with precise notation, clear norm definitions, and reproducible supplementary material.  
- Originality is high: first paper to theoretically justify empirical superiority of the latent approach and generalize duality to tensor norms.  
- Significance is substantial for applications (neuroimaging, collaborative filtering, environmental monitoring) and for guiding method choice.  
- Minor weakness: limited discussion/comparison of computational complexity between approaches.  
- Could further benefit from exploring concrete applications where the theoretical insights directly impact performance.  
- Meets NIPS quality criteria (soundness, clarity, originality, significance) and is recommended for acceptance.
