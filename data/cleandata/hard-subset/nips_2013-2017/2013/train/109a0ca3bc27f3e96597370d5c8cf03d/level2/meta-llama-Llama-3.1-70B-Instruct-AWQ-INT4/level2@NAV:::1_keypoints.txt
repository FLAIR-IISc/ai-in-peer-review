- Proposes a new framework for structured Schatten norms for tensors, covering overlapped and latent norms.  
- Claims the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in many cases.  
- Argues the latent Schatten-1 norm is a more suitable regularizer than the overlapped Schatten-1 norm.  
- Provides a duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms.  
- Offers theoretical analysis including a consistency statement, a deterministic bound, and a Gaussian noise model.  
- Supports theoretical claims with numerical simulations.  
- Presents ideas that are practically useful for tensor decomposition and completion (e.g., image/video processing, data mining, ML).  
- Demonstrates strong field knowledge and a comprehensive literature review.  
- Introduces a novel framework and new insights that are a significant contribution.  
- Provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including algorithmic and simulation explanations.  
- Limitation: assumes Gaussian noise, which may not hold in all applications.  
- Limitation: latent approach may not be suitable for all tensor decomposition problems.  
- Constructive criticism: lacks clear guidance on choosing the regularization constant in practice.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication due to its significant contribution and potential impact.
