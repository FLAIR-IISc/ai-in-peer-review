- Develops a unified framework of structured Schatten norms for tensors, unifying “overlapped” and “latent” variants.  
- Proves a duality between overlapped Sp/q norms and latent Sp*/q* norms as the central technical result.  
- Analyzes denoising performance of the latent approach, providing consistency, deterministic, and high-probability Gaussian-noise bounds.  
- Shows latent bounds scale with the minimum mode-k rank and recommends a λ independent of unknown ranks.  
- Simulations corroborate the predicted scaling and λ-independence of the latent approach.  
- Positions work against prior overlapped-trace-norm tensor methods and leverages decomposable-regularizer analysis tools.  
- Generalizes known overlapped/latent group-norm duality to Schatten norms on tensor unfoldings.  
- Comparative bound for overlapped norms draws on Tomioka et al.; discussion references tensor recovery lower bounds.  
- Strength: duality is elegant, novel, and tightens the analogy to group sparsity.  
- Strength: theoretical bounds align with empirical advantages of latent regularization when one mode is low-rank.  
- Strength: sound use of high-dimensional analysis yields transparent λ prescriptions under Gaussian noise.  
- Strength: empirical validation convincingly shows linear MSE trends with latent-rank complexity.  
- Weakness: stronger theorems rely on incoherence-type spectral constraints and a constrained problem involving parameter α.  
- Weakness: unclear whether experiments enforce the α constraints or how sensitive results are to α.  
- Weakness: α depends on unknown true decomposition; no guidance on selecting α from data.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to small synthetic denoising (no missing data), with no runtime or solver-complexity comparisons.  
- Weakness: constants (e.g., factor F, universal c) are opaque and tightness/lower bounds for latent norms are not discussed.  
- Weakness: limited comparison breadth—other tensor norms, mixed choices, and nonconvex baselines are not evaluated.  
- Clarity: generally well written but needs clarity on α, whether constraints were enforced, and how λ/σ were selected in practice.  
- Suggestions: state if/what α was enforced in experiments, vary/remove α in experiments, and report sensitivity.  
- Suggestions: include ADMM runtime/convergence data and discuss scalability in K and dimensions.  
- Suggestions: add at least one real-data or missing-data experiment to demonstrate broader utility.  
- Suggestions: provide a data-driven λ selection strategy (e.g., σ estimation) and discuss when overlapped norms may be preferable.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear theoretical duality, meaningful performance guarantees, and empirical validation of scaling.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on extra constraints/parameters not fully operationalized, limited experiments, and lack of computational/baseline breadth.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance, conditional on clarifying the role/implementation of spectral-norm constraints and strengthening empirical/ computational sections.
