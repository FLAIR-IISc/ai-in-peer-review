Summary and contributions
The paper introduces a unified framework of structured Schatten norms for tensors, encompassing two families: overlapped Sp/q norms (penalizing all mode-unfoldings of a single tensor) and latent Sp*/q* norms (penalizing a decomposition into mode-specific low-rank components). A core technical contribution is a duality result showing that the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm, generalizing well-known overlapped/latent group sparsity dualities. Building on this, the paper analyzes the “latent” convex tensor decomposition and provides: (i) a consistency bound in terms of the overlapped S∞/∞ norm of the noise; (ii) deterministic error bounds under an incoherence-type constraint linking cross-mode components; and (iii) high-probability bounds for Gaussian noise. A key finding is that denoising error scales with the minimum mode rank of the ground-truth tensor, explaining the empirical advantage of the latent approach when the tensor is low-rank predominantly in one mode. Synthetic experiments verify the predicted linear scaling with the proposed complexity measures and show improvements over the overlapped approach analyzed in [26].

Relation to prior work
The work is grounded in convex approaches to tensor estimation via nuclear/trace-type norms [9, 15, 23, 25, 26], and connects tightly to decomposable regularizers and structured sparsity frameworks [1, 12, 17, 20, 21]. The duality result mirrors the overlapped/latent group-lasso dualities and extends them to tensor Schatten norms. The statistical analysis follows the high-dimensional M-estimation program (e.g., [1, 20]) adapted to tensor unfoldings and the latent decomposition structure. The comparison with [26] is apt: the overlapped analysis depends on averages of mode ranks, while the present latent analysis depends on the minimum mode rank, aligning with empirical observations and with lower bound phenomena discussed in [19].

Quality and correctness
The technical development appears sound. The duality lemma is natural and valuable, and the derivations of consistency and error bounds are in line with contemporary techniques. The deterministic and Gaussian-noise bounds carefully incorporate an incoherence-like constraint on inter-mode spectral norms, which is a reasonable device to control cross-talk among latent components and is analogous to conditions used in related matrix problems (e.g., [1, 3, 11]). The comparison to overlapped bounds is fair and highlights distinct complexity drivers. The empirical section matches theoretical predictions, including the scale of the regularization parameter. That said, the practical necessity and enforcement of the spectral norm constraints in the optimization (11) are not fully clarified in the experiments, and constants in the bounds are not always made explicit.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-organized. Definitions of unfoldings, multilinear rank, and the two norm classes are precise. The relationship between multilinear ranks rk and latent component ranks r̄k is explained, though a small illustrative example could help intuition. It would be helpful to more explicitly state when the constrained formulation (11) is used in practice and how the constant α is chosen.

Originality
- The duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* tensor Schatten norms is novel and of independent interest.
- The statistical analysis of the latent approach for denoising, with bounds scaling with min mode rank, advances understanding beyond prior results focused on overlapped norms or noise-free completion.
- The unifying structured-norm view that connects tensor norms to structured sparsity is insightful.

Significance
The results provide both theoretical justification and practical guidance for preferring latent formulations in noisy tensor problems, especially in anisotropic low-rank settings. The duality result can inform algorithm design and complexity analyses for broader classes of structured tensor estimators. The work should be of interest to researchers in convex tensor methods and high-dimensional statistics.

Pros
- Clear, rigorous duality result; connects to a broad literature on structured sparsity.
- Meaningful statistical guarantees for the latent approach, including consistency and Gaussian-noise rates.
- Error bounds depend on min mode rank, aligning with empirical advantages over overlapped norms.
- Experiments verify scaling laws and provide guidance on λ selection that does not require oracle knowledge of ranks.

Cons and limitations
- The deterministic/Gaussian bounds rely on a spectral incoherence constraint and an optimization formulation with spectral norm constraints; practical enforcement and choice of α are not fully detailed. It is unclear whether these constraints were active in the reported experiments.
- Experiments are limited to synthetic denoising with moderate sizes; no tensor completion or real data results are provided.
- The study focuses primarily on S1/1 (and its S∞/∞ dual), although the framework introduces general Sp/q norms; empirical or theoretical exploration beyond p = q = 1 is not provided.
- The LR complexity used for plotting is obtained by running the latent procedure on noiseless data to estimate r̄k; this is reasonable for validating scaling, but it is an oracle-aided quantity that may not be directly accessible.
- Computational aspects (scaling, convergence, and wall-clock comparisons between overlapped and latent ADMM solvers) are not thoroughly discussed.

Suggestions for improvement
- Clarify the role of the spectral norm constraints in (11): describe whether they were enforced in experiments, how α was set, and provide sensitivity analyses when α is mis-specified. If constraints are not needed empirically, explain conditions under which the unconstrained latent formulation retains similar performance.
- Include at least one tensor completion experiment to complement the denoising results, even if theory focuses on denoising, and consider a small real-data example (e.g., neuroimaging or video data).
- Provide a brief proof sketch for the duality lemma in the main text to improve accessibility, and add an illustrative example distinguishing rk vs r̄k.
- Report computational costs and convergence behavior for both overlapped and latent ADMM solvers at the tested scales.
- Discuss prospects for extending the analysis to other Sp/q choices (e.g., overlapped S1/∞), hinted at in the conclusion.

Minor comments
- Ensure consistent notation for unfoldings and ranks; explicitly restate the distinction between rk and r̄k near Theorem 3.
- A few editorial fixes (e.g., “betters” → “better”, “prof.” → “Proc.”).

Overall assessment
A solid and timely contribution that advances theoretical understanding of convex tensor decomposition and provides actionable insights favoring latent formulations under noise. With clarifications on the constrained formulation and modest additional experiments, this would be a strong addition to the program. Recommended for acceptance.
