- Main claim: the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the true tensor is low-rank in a specific (unknown) mode, with latent MSE scaling with the minimum mode-k rank vs overlapped depending on the average rank.  
- Contribution 1: establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Contribution 2: provides rigorous theoretical analysis and consistency proofs showing latent error bounds depend on the minimum mode-k rank.  
- Contribution 3: numerical experiments confirm theoretical predictions and observed MSE scaling.  
- Proofs and experiments: theoretical results are well-supported by rigorous proofs (in supplement) and well-designed experiments.  
- Literature and positioning: demonstrates strong field knowledge with comprehensive references and a compelling connection to structured sparsity.  
- Limitation: analysis is restricted to noisy tensor decomposition, unlike prior noiseless tensor completion work.  
- Missing discussion: could more explicitly address computational complexity of the latent vs overlapped approaches for practical adoption.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant contribution that advances theoretical understanding; recommends acceptance.
