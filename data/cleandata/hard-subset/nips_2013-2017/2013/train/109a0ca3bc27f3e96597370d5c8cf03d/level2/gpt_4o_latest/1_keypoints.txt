- Introduces structured Schatten norms for tensors and analyzes two variants: "overlapped" and "latent" Schatten norms.
- Provides theoretical justification for why the latent approach can outperform the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.
- Establishes a novel duality result between overlapped and latent norms, linking the work to structured sparsity literature.
- Shows through analysis that latent denoising error scales with the minimal mode-k rank, unlike overlapped which depends on average ranks.
- Empirical experiments (synthetic) validate theoretical predictions, especially MSE scaling with dimensionalities and ranks.
- Demonstrates latent approach's superior denoising performance in simulations.
- Highlights practical relevance for applications (e.g., neuroimaging, collaborative filtering) where tensors are low-rank in specific modes.
- Clearly explains theoretical and empirical differences between overlapped and latent approaches, offering actionable guidance.
- Experiments are limited to synthetic data; real-world dataset evaluations are missing.
- Latent approach leads to more complex optimization; paper lacks discussion on computational efficiency and scalability for large tensors.
- Mentions potential extensions (other Schatten norms like S1/∞) but does not explore them in depth.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — add scalability discussion, real-world experiments, and elaboration on possible extensions.
