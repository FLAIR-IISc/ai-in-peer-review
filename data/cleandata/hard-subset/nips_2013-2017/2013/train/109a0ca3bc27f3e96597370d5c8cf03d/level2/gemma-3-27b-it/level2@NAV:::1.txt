## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, specifically analyzing the "latent" approach proposed in prior work which empirically outperforms the "overlapped" approach. The core contribution lies in providing a theoretical explanation for this empirical observation: the latent approach achieves error bounds scaling with the *minimum* mode-k rank, a significant improvement over the overlapped approach’s scaling with the average rank. The paper also establishes a novel duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing existing results in structured sparsity.

The paper is well-motivated, connecting tensor decomposition to various data analysis applications. The authors clearly define the problem and relevant background, including the nuanced differences between the overlapped and latent approaches. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, building upon recent work in convex optimization and matrix completion. The duality result is a valuable contribution in its own right, potentially applicable beyond tensor decomposition. The inclusion of numerical experiments to validate the theoretical scaling predictions strengthens the work. 

A key strength is the clear explanation of *why* the latent approach performs better – its scaling with the minimum rank is a crucial insight.  The paper acknowledges limitations, noting the focus on the noiseless setting in prior work and suggesting tensor completion as a future research direction.  

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the computational cost associated with the latent approach. While it provides theoretical advantages, practical considerations of scalability are important. The experimental section, while validating the theory, feels somewhat limited. Expanding the experiments to include different tensor sizes, rank configurations, and noise levels would provide a more comprehensive assessment. Furthermore, the connection to related work on structured sparsity could be made more explicit throughout the paper, rather than concentrated in Section 2.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical justification for the superior performance of the latent approach.
*   Novel duality result with broader implications for structured sparsity.
*   Clear presentation and rigorous analysis.
*   Validation of theoretical results via numerical experiments.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of computational complexity.
*   Somewhat limited scope of experimental validation.
*   Connection to broader structured sparsity literature could be more prominent.

**Overall:**

This is a solid theoretical contribution that addresses an important open question in tensor decomposition. While some aspects could be expanded upon, the clarity of the analysis and the significance of the results warrant acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
