- Introduces a structured Schatten-norm framework for tensors, unifying the "overlapped" and "latent" convex relaxations.  
- Main contribution: rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach in a denoising setting.  
- Shows latent MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, whereas overlapped scales with the average mode-k ranks.  
- Provides theoretical justification for latent’s empirical superiority when the tensor is low-rank in one unknown mode.  
- Supports results with a novel duality result linking the norms.  
- Validated findings with numerical simulations.  
- Strength: significant, technically sound theoretical contributions; the unification and duality result are elegant.  
- Strength: Theorem 3 is a novel, important performance bound clarifying the relationship between the two convex approaches.  
- Significance: practically and theoretically important—latent behaves as if it knew the lowest-rank mode, guiding practitioner choice.  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, with clear motivation, precise statements, and experiments that corroborate theory.  
- Primary weakness: limited discussion of assumptions—tighter bounds rely on an incoherence condition and extra optimization constraints.  
- Suggestion: include discussion on practical implications, guidance for setting parameter α, and sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept / strongly recommended for acceptance.
