- Studies convex tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, comparing the "latent" and "overlapped" approaches.
- Focuses on the latent approach and shows it has advantages over the overlapped approach.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving error bounds that scale with the minimum mode-k rank.
- Presents a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms.
- Empirically validates theoretical findings with numerical experiments.
- Well-motivated problem addressing model selection and generalization in tensor decomposition.
- Clearly connects to structured sparsity and group lasso, providing useful context.
- Builds on and theoretically justifies prior empirical observations (e.g., [25]).
- Numerical experiments support theory and show practical benefits of the latent approach even when overlapped should perform well.
- Writing is generally clear, organized, and logically structured with appropriate citations.
- Asymptotic bounds hide constant factors that are not explored.
- Theoretical results rely on potentially strong assumptions (e.g., incoherence) whose practical implications aren’t fully discussed.
- Guidance for choosing the regularization parameter λ is limited beyond a theoretical upper bound.
- Limited discussion of limitations and lack of analysis on generalization to tensor completion settings.
- Connections to low-rank matrix completion literature and differences in theoretical guarantees are not made explicit.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution warranting acceptance, with minor-to-moderate issues to address in a future revision.
