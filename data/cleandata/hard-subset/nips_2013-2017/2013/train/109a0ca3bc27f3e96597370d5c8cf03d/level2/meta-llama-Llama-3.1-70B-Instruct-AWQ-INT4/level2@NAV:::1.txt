**Paper Review**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a new framework for structured Schatten norms for tensors, which includes the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. The authors analyze the performance of the latent approach for tensor decomposition and show that it outperforms the overlapped approach in many cases. They provide theoretical and empirical evidence to support their claims.

**Claims**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The latent approach for tensor decomposition is more effective than the overlapped approach in many cases.
2. The latent Schatten 1-norm is a more suitable regularizer for tensor decomposition than the overlapped Schatten 1-norm.
3. The authors provide a duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the latent approach, including a consistency statement, a deterministic bound, and a Gaussian noise model. They also provide numerical simulations to support their claims.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful for tensor decomposition and completion problems. The latent approach can be used in various applications, such as image and video processing, data mining, and machine learning.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the field of tensor decomposition and completion. The authors provide a comprehensive review of the existing literature and clearly explain the relationships between different approaches.

**Novelty**

The paper presents a novel framework for structured Schatten norms for tensors, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors also provide new insights into the performance of the latent approach for tensor decomposition.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including a clear explanation of the algorithms and numerical simulations.

**Limitations**

The paper assumes that the noise is Gaussian, which may not be the case in all applications. The authors also acknowledge that the latent approach may not be suitable for all types of tensor decomposition problems.

**Constructive Criticism**

One potential criticism of the paper is that the authors do not provide a clear explanation of how to choose the regularization constant in practice. The authors may want to consider providing more guidance on this issue in future work.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper presents a novel framework for structured Schatten norms for tensors.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the latent approach, including theoretical and empirical evidence.
* The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful for tensor decomposition and completion problems.

Con:

* The paper assumes that the noise is Gaussian, which may not be the case in all applications.
* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how to choose the regularization constant in practice.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper for publication. The authors present a significant contribution to the field of tensor decomposition and completion, and their work has the potential to impact various applications in image and video processing, data mining, and machine learning.
