- Paper studies structured Schatten norms for tensors and analyzes a "latent" convex-optimization approach to tensor decomposition.
- Main theoretical contribution: latent approach performs as well as if one knew the mode with the smallest rank.
- Provides both a generic consistency result and a tighter bound exploiting low-rankness under an incoherence assumption.
- Compares latent vs overlapped approaches and explains why latent often outperforms overlapped empirically.
- Numerical experiments confirm theoretical scaling of MSE with dimensionalities and sum of ranks.
- Shows latent approach matches or exceeds overlapped performance in many cases; success correlates with high TR-to-LR complexity ratio.
- Discusses duality between overlapped/latent Schatten norms and the analogous duality in overlapped/latent group sparsity norms.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and accessible to readers with background in the area.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach.
- Strength: clear introduction and detailed explanation of structured Schatten norms.
- Strength: numerical results support theoretical claims.
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with tensor decomposition and structured sparsity.
- Weakness: some proofs relegated to supplementary material, which may hinder readability.
- Weakness: more discussion needed on implications and potential applications.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add broader context and more discussion of implications; overall rating 9/10.
