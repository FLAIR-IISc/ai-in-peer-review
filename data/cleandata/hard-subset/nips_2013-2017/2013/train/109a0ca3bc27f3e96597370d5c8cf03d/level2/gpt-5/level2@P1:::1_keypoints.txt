- Paper unifies overlapped and latent tensor Schatten-norm approaches under a structured Sp/q framework and proves the duality that overlapped Sp/q’s dual is latent Sp*/q*.
- Shows latent denoising error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, unlike overlapped bounds that scale with averaged mode ranks.
- Provides consistency result based on the dual overlapped S∞/∞ norm of the noise.
- Gives deterministic bounds under a spectral-norm incoherence constraint, yielding overall error ~ min mode-k rank and component-wise error ~ sum of component ranks.
- Derives a Gaussian-noise bound with an explicit λ independent of unknown ranks and rates proportional to σ^2 times a latent-rank complexity.
- Synthetic experiments corroborate theoretical scaling and show latent outperforms overlapped even for simultaneously low-rank tensors.
- Connects convex tensor methods and structured sparsity literature, generalizing overlapped/latent group-lasso duality to Schatten norms.
- Contrasts with prior overlapped-theory (averaged-rank scaling) and aligns with geometric analyses of convex regularizers.
- Duality lemma is natural, appears correct, and is central for optimization and complexity control.
- Consistency and deterministic analyses follow standard decomposable-regularizer techniques; λ requirement via noise dual norm is standard.
- The spectral-norm incoherence assumption and enforced constraints are reasonable but somewhat strong and explicitly acknowledged.
- Gaussian result gives explicit λ depending only on sizes, K, and α; the dimension factor F is mild but somewhat involved.
- Paper is generally clear, with precise definitions and good explanation of dual norms and experimental complexity proxies.
- Conceptually original: structured duality for Schatten norms is a useful generalization of known sparsity results.
- Theoretical novelty: min-rank-adaptive denoising bounds and S∞/∞-based consistency are new contributions.
- Empirically original: experiments clearly validate latent MSE scaling and improvements over overlapped.
- Significant for tensor methods: provides sharper understanding and practical guidance favoring latent when low-rankness concentrates in one mode.
- Strengths include a unifying framework, min-rank-adaptive guarantees, λ selection independent of ranks, and fair experiments testing predicted scaling.
- Main weaknesses/questions: reliance on spectral-norm incoherence and constraints—were these enforced in experiments, and can theory be extended without them?
- Practical concerns: guidance missing for choosing/estimating α and λ beyond Gaussian noise; LR complexity depends on unobservable latent component ranks.
- Experimental limitations: synthetic datasets only (K=3, moderate sizes); lacks real-data examples and scalability/runtime comparisons.
- Computational question: ADMM solver cited—discussion needed on per-iteration complexity, memory, and K>3 behavior due to extra latent variables.
- Suggest adding clarification about constraint use in experiments, guidance on α/λ selection, a duality proof sketch or illustration, discussion on identifiability of W(k), and runtime/memory reporting.
- Minor fixes: consistent notation for r̄k vs rk and restate F’s dependence near Eq. (14)–(15) for readability.
- Overall: technically sound, novel, and significant work recommended for acceptance after addressing incoherence-constraint clarifications and practical parameter-selection concerns.
