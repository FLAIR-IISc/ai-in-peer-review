- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the "latent" norm and comparing it to the "overlap" norm.  
- Derives theoretical MSE bounds for the latent norm under several noise models, showing favorable comparison to overlap norm analyses.  
- Confirms theoretical results with numerical simulations.  
- Proposes a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for it.  
- Paper is generally easy to follow and appears technically sound.  
- Results may be useful for researchers in optimization for tensor decompositions.  
- Main concern: results are narrow, technical, and only peripherally related to machine learning, limiting potential NIPS impact.  
- Paper does not examine the approaches in real machine learning contexts either theoretically or experimentally.  
- Results seem incremental and not particularly surprising; techniques lack novelty.  
- Upper bounds are provided without matching lower bounds, which weakens algorithmic comparisons.  
- Abstract wording is misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms since no algorithms or behavior analyses are presented for the generalization.  
- Minor presentation issues: Figure 1 appears too early to interpret, and a few typos/phrasing errors are noted.
