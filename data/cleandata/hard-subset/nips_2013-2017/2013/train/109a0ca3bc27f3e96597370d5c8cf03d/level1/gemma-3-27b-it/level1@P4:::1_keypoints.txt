- Studies convex tensor decomposition comparing overlapped vs latent structured Schatten norms.
- Main theoretical contribution: shows latent approach scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor.
- Presents a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms (Lemma 1).
- Numerical simulations validate the theoretical predictions.
- Well-motivated and clearly situates the work within tensor decomposition and structured sparsity literature.
- Precise definitions of overlapped and latent Schatten norms and consistent notation.
- Theorem 3 provides a strong scaling result explaining empirical success of the latent approach.
- Duality result is potentially broadly applicable beyond tensor decomposition.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and mathematically clear.
- Empirical validation increases confidence in the theoretical analysis.
- Theoretical results depend on assumptions (incoherence, noise distribution) that may not hold in practice.
- The bound in Theorem 3 is complex and may limit practical interpretability.
- Numerical experiments are limited—more datasets and noise regimes would strengthen validation.
- Paper omits discussion of computational cost comparing latent and overlapped approaches.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant problem, novel rigorous theory, helpful duality result, empirical support, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: strong/possibly unrealistic assumptions, complex bounds, limited experiments, missing computational-cost discussion.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify assumptions and limitations.
