- Presents a theoretical analysis comparing a "latent" convex tensor decomposition approach to the established "overlapped" approach using a new class of structured Schatten norms.  
- Formalizes overlapped and latent Sp/q-norms: overlapped penalizes sum of Schatten norms of unfoldings, latent models the tensor as a sum of mode-specific low-rank components.  
- Proves a novel duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q-norm is the latent Sp*/q*-norm, linking to structured sparsity (e.g., overlapped vs. latent group Lasso).  
- Provides the first rigorous performance analysis of the latent approach in tensor denoising, with MSE scaling with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor.  
- Contrasts with prior overlapped analysis where error scales with the average mode-k rank, explaining latent’s advantage when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Numerical simulations precisely validate the theoretical scaling laws for both overlapped and latent methods.  
- High technical quality: rigorous proofs, explicit error bounds and consistency results under Gaussian noise; assumptions (e.g., incoherence) are stated carefully.  
- Experiments are well-designed to directly verify theoretical complexity measures (TR vs LR complexity).  
- Exceptional clarity and organization: clear definitions, logical flow from theory to experiments, and helpful illustrations (e.g., Figure 1).  
- Original contributions: formalization of latent Schatten norm, first rigorous analysis showing adaptivity to minimum mode rank, and the duality result.  
- Significant impact: gives strong theoretical justification for preferring the latent approach when rank structure is unknown and deepens ties between tensor methods and structured sparsity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical insight, elegant unifying framework, clear practical implications, strong empirical validation, and high clarity/quality.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scope limited to denoising (tensor completion left for future work) and tighter bounds depend on potentially restrictive incoherence and additional optimization constraints.  
- Overall recommendation by reviewer: clear accept for NIPS.
