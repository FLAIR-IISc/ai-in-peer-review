## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, specifically analyzing the “latent” approach proposed in prior work, which empirically outperforms the “overlapped” approach in certain scenarios. The authors provide a theoretical justification for this empirical observation, demonstrating that the latent approach achieves performance comparable to knowing the mode with the smallest rank. A key contribution is a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms, generalizing existing results in structured sparsity. The theory is validated through numerical simulations confirming the predicted scaling behavior of the mean squared error.

The paper builds upon recent work in convex relaxation for tensor decomposition [9, 15, 23, 25, 26] and connects it to the broader field of structured sparsity [2, 13, 17, 21]. The authors clearly position their work within this context, referencing relevant literature. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, establishing consistency and a deterministic bound on the error, and demonstrating a clear advantage of the latent approach when the true tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The duality result is a valuable theoretical contribution in its own right, potentially applicable beyond tensor decomposition. The numerical experiments effectively support the theoretical findings, showcasing the scaling behavior predicted by the analysis.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a solid theoretical analysis of the latent approach, justifying its empirical success.
*   **Novel Duality Result:** The duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a significant contribution.
*   **Clear Connection to Existing Work:** The paper effectively situates itself within the broader literature on tensor decomposition and structured sparsity.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Numerical results confirm the theoretical predictions.
*   **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is generally clear and logically structured.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Assumptions:** The deterministic bound relies on an incoherence assumption (Equation 10) which might be strong in some applications. The practical implications of this assumption could be further discussed.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the numerical results are supportive, the experiments are relatively simple. Exploring more complex datasets or different noise distributions could strengthen the findings.
*   **Computational Cost:** The paper doesn’t discuss the computational complexity of the proposed approach compared to other tensor decomposition methods.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical advance in understanding convex tensor decomposition.
*   The duality result is a valuable contribution to the field of structured sparsity.
*   The empirical validation supports the theoretical findings.
*   The work addresses a relevant and challenging problem in machine learning.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The assumptions required for the deterministic bound might limit the applicability of the results.
*   The numerical experiments could be more comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept**. The theoretical contributions are significant, the analysis is rigorous, and the empirical results are supportive. While the assumptions and limited scope of experiments are minor weaknesses, they do not detract from the overall quality and importance of the work. This paper advances our understanding of convex tensor decomposition and provides valuable insights for practitioners and researchers in the field.
