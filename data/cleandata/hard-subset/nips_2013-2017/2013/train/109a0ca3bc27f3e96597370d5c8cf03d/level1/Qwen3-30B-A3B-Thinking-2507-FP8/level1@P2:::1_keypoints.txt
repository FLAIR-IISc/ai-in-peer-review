- Introduces a structured Schatten-norm framework and compares "overlapped" vs "latent" tensor regularization.  
- Proves a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms (Lemma 1), generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical performance bounds for the latent approach (Theorems 1–3) with proofs in the supplement.  
- Shows latent MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, while overlapped scales with the average (square-root) of mode-k ranks.  
- Theoretical results explain prior empirical observations (e.g., Figure 1) and are validated by numerical simulations (Section 4).  
- Demonstrates the latent approach performs at least as well in simultaneously low-rank cases and can match knowing the optimal mode.  
- Uses appropriate incoherence assumptions (eq.10) consistent with recent literature.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and connects clearly to structured sparsity literature.  
- Definitions and presentation of theorems and implications are precise and logical.  
- Experimental design (controlled tensor generation and noise) is sound and supports theoretical claims.  
- Minor weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity.  
- Weakness: does not address tensor completion (noted as future work).  
- Weakness: experimental section could include more comparisons with alternative methods.  
- Significance: provides a solid theoretical foundation for preferring the latent approach in noisy tensor decomposition.  
- Strengths: rigorous analysis, novel duality result, clear explanation of latent superiority, precise numerical validation, strong literature connections.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial theoretical contribution, resolves empirical questions from prior work, strong numerical confirmation, advances the field.  
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of tensor completion study, technical depth may limit accessibility, limited practical implementation details.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
