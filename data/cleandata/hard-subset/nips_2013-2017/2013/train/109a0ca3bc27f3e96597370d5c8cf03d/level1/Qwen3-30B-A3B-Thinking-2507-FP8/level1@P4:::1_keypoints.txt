- Investigates structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition and contrasts the "overlapped" and "latent" approaches.
- Establishes a novel duality: the dual of the overlapped Schatten norm is the latent Schatten norm.
- Shows theoretically that the latent approach performs as well as knowing the mode with the smallest rank, explaining empirical advantages.
- Provides theoretical results: consistency (Theorem 1), deterministic bounds (Theorem 2), and Gaussian noise analysis (Theorem 3) with proofs in the supplement.
- Gives denoising performance bounds and numerical experiments that confirm theoretical predictions.
- Carefully distinguishes multilinear rank of the true tensor from ranks of latent components and notes the optimal regularization constant is rank-independent.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and reproducible detail.
- Figures (notably Figure 2) effectively illustrate empirical results.
- Novel contribution: duality result generalizes structured sparsity literature and contextualizes tensor decomposition.
- First theoretical justification for the empirically proposed latent approach from prior work [25].
- Results are significant: provide theoretical foundation for when latent outperforms overlapped and may have broader applications.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and well-supported bounds for the latent approach.
- Strength: clear empirical validation aligning with theory.
- Strength: practical insight on choice of regularization constant.
- Weakness: limited experimental validation (only two tensor sizes and one noise level).
- Weakness: no detailed computational complexity comparison between approaches.
- Weakness: does not explore tensor completion applications, a major use case, though mentioned as future work.
- Builds on and extends prior work (Tomioka et al. [25], overlapped analyses [26]) and cites structured sparsity literature appropriately.
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution with empirical support; recommendation to accept despite limited experiments.
