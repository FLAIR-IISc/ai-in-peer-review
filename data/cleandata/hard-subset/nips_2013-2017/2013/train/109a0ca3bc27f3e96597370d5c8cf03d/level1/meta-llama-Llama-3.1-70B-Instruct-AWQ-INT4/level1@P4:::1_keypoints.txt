- Proposes a framework for structured Schatten norms that includes overlapped and latent Schatten-1 norms.  
- Proves a novel duality between the overlapped Schatten norm and the latent Schatten norm.  
- Theoretical result: error of the latent approach is upper-bounded by the minimal mode-k rank.  
- Empirical comparison shows the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach for noisy tensor decomposition.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides clear theoretical exposition.  
- Strength: novel unifying framework for structured Schatten norms.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach.  
- Strength: strong, well-presented empirical evidence supporting the latent approach.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with tensor decomposition and convex optimization, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison to other existing tensor decomposition methods.  
- Weakness: empirical results are limited to a specific setting; generalization is unclear.  
- Argument for acceptance: significant novel framework with rigorous theory and supporting experiments.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited accessibility, missing broader method comparisons, and narrow empirical scope.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
