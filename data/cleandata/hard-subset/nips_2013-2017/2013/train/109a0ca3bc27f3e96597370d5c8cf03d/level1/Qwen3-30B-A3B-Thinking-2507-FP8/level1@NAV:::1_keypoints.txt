- Compares structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, focusing on overlapped vs latent approaches.  
- Main contribution: novel duality result explaining why the latent approach outperforms overlapped when the tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.  
- Shows latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, unlike overlapped which scales with the average rank.  
- Presents rigorous theoretical analysis including consistency proofs and deterministic error bounds.  
- Duality framework underpins and supports the theoretical results.  
- Numerical experiments confirm predicted MSE scaling and compare the two approaches carefully.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.  
- Originality: extends structured sparsity theory to tensors and provides the first principled explanation for latent approach’s empirical success.  
- Significance: offers a principled basis for choosing tensor decomposition methods in applications (neuroimaging, collaborative filtering).  
- Strengths: strong theoretical contribution, comprehensive error analysis, well-designed empirical validation, and solid literature connection.  
- Weaknesses: relies on incoherence assumptions that may limit real-world applicability.  
- Weaknesses: empirical validation is limited to synthetic data and practical implementation details are under-discussed.  
- Overall assessment: excellent theoretical contribution that meets NIPS quality standards; recommendation to accept.
