- Introduces structured Schatten norms for tensors, encompassing two recent norms for convex tensor decomposition.  
- Analyzes the latent approach and shows it outperforms the overlapped approach in certain settings.  
- Shows latent approach superiority specifically when the tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.  
- Establishes a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms, relevant to structured sparsity.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with a clear introduction and literature review.  
- Theoretical results are technically sound and thoroughly analyzed.  
- Numerical simulations confirm the theoretical findings, showing latent > overlapped in many cases.  
- Quality: 9/10; Clarity: 9/10; Originality: 8/10; Significance: 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, thorough analysis, practical implications (neuroimaging, collaborative filtering), good presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concept of structured Schatten norms is not entirely new; reliance on a specific mode low-rank assumption may limit practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; address limitations and assumptions of the latent approach.
