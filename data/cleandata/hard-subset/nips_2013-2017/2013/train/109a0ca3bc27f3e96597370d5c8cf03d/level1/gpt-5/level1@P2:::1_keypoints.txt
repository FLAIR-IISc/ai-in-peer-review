- Paper develops structured Schatten Sp/q norms for tensors and analyzes two convex low-rank formulations: overlapped and latent.
- Proves duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is a latent Sp*/q* norm.
- Introduces and analyzes the latent approach using decomposable-regularizer techniques from high-dimensional M-estimation.
- Main theoretical results: consistency bound depending on overlapped S∞/∞ noise norm.
- Provides deterministic per-component error bounds under an incoherence-type spectral-norm constraint controlling cross-mode correlations.
- Shows Gaussian-noise MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank (not the average) for the latent method.
- Proves Lemma 2: latent S1/1 norm controlled by minimal sqrt(mode rank), explaining latent advantage when one mode is low-rank.
- Synthetic denoising experiments confirm predicted linear scaling and show latent often outperforms overlapped.
- Technical analysis appears sound; duality and bounds are plausible and align with experiments.
- Stronger bounds require additional spectral-norm (incoherence) constraints and a regularization λ that dominates S∞/∞ noise plus an unknown α(K−1), raising practical questions.
- Unclear whether the constrained optimization (11) and α constraints were enforced in experiments, creating a potential theory–practice gap.
- Experiments are limited to Gaussian denoising, moderate tensor sizes, no missing data, and lack nonconvex baselines (HOSVD/Tucker, CP-ALS).
- λ selection is only coarsely described (20 candidates, “three representative values”); fairness across methods could be clearer.
- Paper is well-organized with clear definitions, but proof details are deferred and some notation (ranks) is overloaded; a symbol table would help.
- Practical guidance on setting λ and α is limited; a prominent high-probability plug-in bound for λ would aid practitioners.
- Duality result and latent analysis are original and extend prior work by providing denoising guarantees and adaptivity to the best mode.
- Results are significant: principled rationale for preferring latent formulation in noisy tensor estimation and potential to influence applications.
- Strengths: unified framework, sharp adaptive bounds, experiments matching theory, and good literature positioning.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: clarify whether α constraints were used in experiments, expand empirical evaluation (completion, sensitivity, runtimes, nonconvex baselines), give guidance on λ/α and constants, and clarify rank notation with examples.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel duality, new performance guarantees, and corroborating experiments; likely influential.
- Arguments against acceptance: possible theory–practice gap on constraints and parameter choice, limited empirical scope, reliance on incoherence assumptions and hidden constants.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution favoring the latent approach; lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying constraints, parameter selection, and improving experiments.
