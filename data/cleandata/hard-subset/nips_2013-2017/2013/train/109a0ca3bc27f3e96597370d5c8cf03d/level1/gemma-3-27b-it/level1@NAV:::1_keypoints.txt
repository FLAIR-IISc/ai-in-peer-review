- Compares structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, finding the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Shows theoretically that the latent approach attains performance comparable to knowing the mode with the smallest rank.  
- Presents a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms (Lemma 1) that generalizes structured sparsity duality.  
- Theorems 1–3 establish consistency and performance bounds for the latent approach.  
- Numerical simulations validate theoretical predictions and the predicted MSE scaling behavior.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor that fills a gap in the literature.  
- Strength: duality result is a significant technical contribution with potential broader impact.  
- Strength: clear explanation linking latent approach advantage to minimum mode-k rank.  
- Strength: empirical results effectively support theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: deterministic bounds depend on a strong incoherence assumption (Eq. 10), limiting generality.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of practical computational challenges, notably spectral norm constraints in optimization (Eq. 11).  
- Weakness: numerical experiments are limited in scope and dataset diversity.  
- For acceptance: provides a novel, theoretically grounded explanation and closes a theoretical gap; duality result is notable.  
- Against acceptance: relies on strong assumptions and insufficiently addresses implementation/computational issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to discuss spectral-norm computational implications and expand numerical evaluation.
