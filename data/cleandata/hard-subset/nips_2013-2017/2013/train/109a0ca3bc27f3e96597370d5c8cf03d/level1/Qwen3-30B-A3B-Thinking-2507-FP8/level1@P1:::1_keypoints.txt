- Studies structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, comparing overlapped vs latent approaches and proving a novel duality with theoretical guarantees for latent.  
- Connects convex tensor decomposition literature to structured sparsity, mapping overlapped/latent Schatten norms to overlapped/latent group sparsity norms.  

Strengths:
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound; Lemma 1 gives a clear duality result generalizing structured sparsity.  
- Theorems 2 and 3 provide tight bounds showing latent performance depends on minimal mode-k rank (vs overlapped’s average rank).  
- Numerical experiments confirm theoretical predictions and MSE scaling behavior (Figure 2 effectively illustrates results).  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized with precise definitions, accessible to NIPS readers with optimization/ML background.  
- Original contribution: first theoretical explanation for latent approach’s empirical success and a novel duality result.  
- Significant practical implications: latent approach theoretically preferable and performs as well as knowing the smallest-rank mode.

Weaknesses:
- Paper omits discussion of computational complexity differences between latent and overlapped approaches.  
- Contribution is mainly theoretical; no new algorithms or substantial methodological extensions.  
- Scope limited to noisy tensor decomposition; does not address tensor completion with missing values (left to future work).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Provides first theoretical explanation for latent approach, establishes novel duality, offers rigorous guarantees, and empirically validates claims; paper is well-written.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited scope (only noisy decomposition), lacks complexity discussion, and the structured sparsity connection is not entirely surprising.

Overall recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance; suggests adding a brief discussion of computational complexity and exploring extensions to tensor completion.
