- Introduces structured Schatten norms unifying overlapped and latent convex tensor low-rank estimators and proves a duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms.  
- Main technical contribution: an elegant duality lemma generalizing overlapped vs latent relationships from structured sparsity to tensor Schatten norms.  
- Provides denoising error bounds for the latent S1/1 norm: consistency, deterministic bounds under incoherence, and high-probability Gaussian-noise bounds.  
- Shows MSE scales with the minimal mode rank (min-mode-rank) rather than an average across modes, improving over prior overlapped-norm results.  
- Experiments on synthetic data confirm the theoretical scaling and identify regimes where the latent approach outperforms overlapped.  
- Connects to prior convex tensor methods (Gandy–Recht–Yamada, Liu et al., Signoretto et al.) and decomposable-regularizer M-estimator theory (Negahban, Agarwal).  
- Analysis adapts modern high-dimensional tools (dual norms, decomposability) carefully and appears technically sound.  
- The duality result is broadly useful and conceptually unifying for structured tensor norms.  
- The deterministic and Gaussian bounds require additional spectral-norm cross-mode constraints (∥W(l)(k)∥S∞ ≤ α), which alter the estimator and introduce a tuning parameter α.  
- It is unclear how α should be chosen in practice, whether constraints were used in experiments, and how sensitive results are to α — clarification needed.  
- Constants (c and factor F) in bounds are unspecified; comments on their magnitude and practical benignness would help assess practicality.  
- Paper focuses on fully observed denoising; extending theory and experiments to tensor completion (missing data) would increase significance.  
- The latent decomposition is not unique; the paper should clarify identifiability/stability of components or state that only the sum W is targeted.  
- Clarity is generally good, but emphasize differences between multilinear rank and latent-component ranks earlier, add a Lemma 2 sketch, and intuitively explain how min-rank arises.  
- Suggestions: report whether α constraints were used, extend experiments to completion and real data, compare to nonconvex baselines, and analyze ADMM runtime/scaling.  
- Recommend exploring other Sp/q choices (e.g., S1/∞), provide practical guidance on selecting λ, and include sensitivity analyses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical advance, interpretable bounds showing adaptation to minimal mode rank, and experiments matching theory.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on extra constraints not tied to practical estimator, limited synthetic experiments without real-data/completion tests, and unquantified constants leaving practical tightness uncertain.
