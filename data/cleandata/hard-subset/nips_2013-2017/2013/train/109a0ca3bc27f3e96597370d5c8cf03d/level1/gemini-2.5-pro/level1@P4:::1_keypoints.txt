- Formalizes overlapped and latent tensor penalties as structured Sp/q-norms and establishes a novel duality between them.
- Provides rigorous performance analysis of the latent approach in tensor denoising with consistency and non-asymptotic Gaussian-noise error bounds.
- Main theoretical result: latent MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, unlike overlapped which scales with the average of sqrt of mode-k ranks.
- Explains why latent outperforms overlapped when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode but high-rank in others.
- Empirical experiments validate the theoretical scaling laws, showing linear MSE relationship with the predicted complexity measure (Figure 2).
- Technical soundness: rigorous theorems and proofs built on established high-dimensional statistics techniques.
- Weakness: tighter bound (Theorem 2) requires an incoherence assumption and an explicit constraint with parameter alpha that is hard to set in practice.
- Limitation: analysis is restricted to complete-observation denoising and does not address tensor completion with missing data.
- Writing and organization are clear and effective, with helpful figures and precise explanations of norms and results.
- Originality: first theoretical explanation for the latent approach’s performance and a new structured Schatten norm framework for tensors.
- Duality result generalizes structured sparsity concepts (e.g., overlapped vs. latent group Lasso) to the tensor setting.
- Significance: offers actionable guidance favoring the latent formulation and shows it can adapt to the simplest (lowest-rank) structure automatically.
- Impact caveat: immediate practical impact limited by focus on denoising rather than the more common completion problems.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical insights, duality framework, strong empirical support, clear presentation, and practical guidance.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on incoherence constraint and parameter, omission of tensor completion analysis, and techniques largely adapted from prior work.
