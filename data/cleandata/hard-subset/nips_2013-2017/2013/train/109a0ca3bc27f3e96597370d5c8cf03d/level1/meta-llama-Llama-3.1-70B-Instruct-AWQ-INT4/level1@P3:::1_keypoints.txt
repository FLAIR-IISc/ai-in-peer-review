- Introduces structured Schatten norms for tensors (overlapped and latent) for convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition.
- Analyzes the latent approach and shows it outperforms the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.
- Presents a novel duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing structured sparsity results.
- Builds on prior work in convex tensor decomposition and overlapped/latent group sparsity literature.
- Strength: novel duality result that advances structured sparsity theory.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach, including consistency and deterministic bounds.
- Strength: empirical evaluation that confirms theoretical predictions and shows latent superiority in many cases.
- Weakness: limited scope, focusing mainly on latent vs overlapped without exploring other approaches or extensions.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis depends on assumptions (e.g., incoherence) that may not always hold in practice.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the latent approach.
- Originality: duality result and theoretical treatment are original contributions.
- Significance: substantial contribution to tensor decomposition and structured sparsity with potential practical applications.
- Quality: well-written, rigorous proofs, and thorough empirical validation.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and easy to follow.
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel theory, empirical support, and overall significant contribution.
- Con-acceptance arguments: narrow focus, reliance on strong assumptions, and missing complexity analysis.
- Overall recommendation: accept—strengths outweigh weaknesses.
