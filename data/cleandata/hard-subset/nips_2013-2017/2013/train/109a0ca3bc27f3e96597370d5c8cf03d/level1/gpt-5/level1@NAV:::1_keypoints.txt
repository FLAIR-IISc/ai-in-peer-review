- Introduces a unified framework of structured Schatten norms for tensors and proves duality: overlapped Sp/q-norm duals to latent Sp*/q*-norm.
- Analyzes latent convex tensor decomposition and shows denoising MSE scales with the minimum mode rank, matching an oracle knowing the lowest-rank mode.
- Presents three main theoretical results: (i) consistency bound using overlapped S∞/∞ noise norm; (ii) deterministic error bounds under incoherence-style spectral-norm constraints; (iii) Gaussian-noise rates with error linear in min_k rank_k (up to mild dimension factors).
- Experiments on synthetic data confirm predicted scaling and show latent approach improves over overlapped even for jointly low-rank tensors.
- Theoretical development is careful, using decomposable-regularizer techniques to obtain sharp, interpretable rates.
- The duality result is clean and potentially useful beyond tensor problems.
- Some deterministic-bound assumptions (spectral-norm constraints and α) may be restrictive in practice.
- Dependence and interpretability of constants (c, F) in bounds are not always transparent.
- Paper is generally well written and organized; the derivation from duality to bounds is clear.
- Would benefit from added intuition for the spectral-norm constraints in equation (11) and guidance on estimating α.
- Computational complexity and scalability of the latent solver are not sufficiently discussed.
- The duality and adaptation-to-minimum-rank error rates appear novel, extending structured sparsity ideas to tensors.
- Results are significant: they explain empirical advantages of the latent approach and offer actionable regularization guidance for multiway denoising/imputation.
- Pros: elegant duality, sharp adaptive rates, empirical validation consistent with theory, and relevance to convex recovery and structured sparsity literature.
- Cons: practicality of deterministic constraints unclear, experiments limited to synthetic data (no completion/real-data), and limited discussion of computational costs and convergence versus nonconvex baselines.
- Suggestions: discuss how to estimate/tune α and σ, add real-data or tensor-completion experiments, report runtime and scaling, relate constants to problem dimensions, and clarify extension beyond S1/1 to general Sp/q choices.
