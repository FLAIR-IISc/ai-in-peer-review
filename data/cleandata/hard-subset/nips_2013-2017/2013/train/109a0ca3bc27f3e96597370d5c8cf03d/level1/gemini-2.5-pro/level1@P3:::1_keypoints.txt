- Paper provides a theoretical analysis of the latent convex tensor decomposition approach (decompose W = sum_k W(k) and penalize sum of Schatten-1 norms of mode-k unfoldings).
- Introduces a general "structured Schatten norms" framework and identifies latent S1/1 and overlapped S1/1 norms.
- Proves a novel duality between overlapped Sp/q-norms and latent Sp*/q*-norms, connecting to latent/overlapped group Lasso literature.
- Presents the first rigorous denoising performance guarantee for the latent approach: MSE scales with min_k r_k (minimum mode-k rank).
- Contrasts this with the overlapped approach whose error scales with a sum/average of mode-k ranks, explaining latent's advantage for asymmetric rank structures.
- Validates theoretical predictions with experiments showing MSE scales linearly with the proposed Latent Rank (LR) complexity metric.
- Strength: significant and novel theoretical contribution that guides when to prefer the latent model.
- Strength: elegant, unifying framework (generalized overlapped/latent Sp/q-norms) with a clear duality result.
- Strength: paper is well-written, clear, and logically organized with effective comparisons to prior bounds.
- Strength: strong experimental design that directly verifies derived scaling laws.
- Weakness: analysis depends on an incoherence assumption and a constrained optimization with a hyperparameter alpha whose practical selection is not discussed.
- Weakness: experiments use the constrained formulation but do not specify how alpha was chosen, hurting reproducibility.
- Weakness: analysis is limited to fully observed denoising and does not address tensor completion, an important application.
- Clarification needed: LR complexity in experiments is computed using an oracle decomposition of the true tensor, which is not practically computable—this should be emphasized.
- Arguments for acceptance: first theoretical analysis of latent approach, significant main result (error ~ min_k r_k), elegant duality framework, thorough experimental validation, and overall technical quality.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on incoherence/constrained formulation with unclear alpha selection and lack of coverage of tensor completion.
- Conclusion: paper is a strong, original contribution and the reviewer strongly recommends acceptance despite minor practical concerns.
