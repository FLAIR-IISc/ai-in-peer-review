- Presents a theoretical analysis of the latent convex tensor decomposition and contrasts it with the overlapped approach.  
- Shows latent approach error bound scales with the minimum mode-k rank, while overlapped depends on the average rank.  
- Provides theoretical justification for when latent outperforms overlapped (tensors low-rank in only one mode).  
- Introduces a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms, generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Connects tensor decomposition approaches to duality between overlapped and latent group lasso.  
- Theorems 2 and 3 are central, appear rigorous, and build on prior structured sparsity work.  
- Paper is well-motivated and explains tensor decomposition and rank-selection challenges clearly.  
- Numerical experiments support theoretical scaling predictions but are limited in scope.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor and clear, well-motivated bounds.  
- Strength: valuable novelty in the duality result and linkage to structured sparsity.  
- Strength: generally clear writing accessible to readers not expert in tensors.  
- Weakness: experiments need expansion (more sizes, noise levels, rank configs, and non-convex baselines).  
- Weakness: theoretical results rely on assumptions (e.g., incoherence, condition on λ) whose sensitivity is not fully explored.  
- Weakness: limited discussion/comparison with broader tensor decomposition literature and missing computational complexity comparison.  
- Weakness: some equations and proofs are dense and could be simplified for readability.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, original contribution with minor revisions suggested; recommend acceptance.
