- Paper introduces structured tensor Schatten norms unifying overlapped and latent families and proves duality between them (Sp/q ↔ Sp*/q*).  
- Special case p=q=1: latent S1/1 dual is overlapped S∞/∞ (max spectral norm over mode unfoldings).  
- Provides statistical guarantees for latent tensor denoising: basic consistency, deterministic bounds under an incoherence-like constraint, and Gaussian-noise bound with explicit λ.  
- Key deterministic result: estimation error scales with sum of component ranks and overall Frobenius error scales with the minimal mode-k rank.  
- Results contrast prior overlapped analysis where error scales with average of mode ranks.  
- Synthetic experiments confirm predicted scaling and show latent often outperforms overlapped even when tensor is simultaneously low-rank.  
- Duality result extends known overlapped/latent group norm dualities to tensor Schatten norms and appears novel.  
- Statistical analysis for latent tensor norms is new and connects to decomposable regularizer techniques.  
- Theory: proofs use standard convex duality/infimal convolution and decomposable-regularizer techniques; central inequality (latent norm bounded by min sqrt rank times Frobenius) is tight and explanatory.  
- Gaussian bound gives an explicit, rank-agnostic choice of λ dependent only on dimensions and noise.  
- Caveat: additional α-bound constraints on off-mode spectral norms (incoherence-like) may be strong and lack practical enforcement/selection guidance.  
- Experiments: well-designed for denoising and λ selection but limited to synthetic data, K=3, and no real data or completion tasks.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well organized, but r_k vs r̄_k distinction should be emphasized earlier and α’s role needs more discussion.  
- Suggest adding brief proof sketches in main text to improve readability and clarifying notation and norm definitions at first use.  
- Originality: novel duality and first denoising guarantees for latent approach with min-rank dependence; insightful connection to structured sparsity.  
- Significance: advances understanding of convex tensor methods, offers practical λ guidance, and could inform completion and other problems.  
- Weakness: practicality and tuning of α constraints, limited empirical scope, no comparisons to nonconvex baselines, and unclear impact of constants (factor F).  
- Suggestions: report whether constraints are enforced empirically, show robustness to α misspecification, add experiments for completion or missing data, and compare to ALS/Tucker-HOOI.  
- Notational/presentation fixes: unify r_k vs r̄_k, define norms at first use, discuss ADMM convergence and computational cost, and fix reference formatting.  
- Questions for authors: how to choose α in practice, sensitivity to violations of constraints (10), extension to completion, and whether min-rank dependence is minimax optimal.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clean duality result, theoretically justified advantage of latent norms, explicit λ calibration, and matching empirical scaling.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear practical constraints and tuning, limited empirical validation, missing comparisons to nonconvex methods, and presentation/proof placement issues.
