## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, focusing on a “latent” approach that empirically outperforms a previously proposed “overlapped” approach, particularly when the underlying tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The authors provide a theoretical analysis explaining this empirical success, demonstrating that the latent approach achieves performance comparable to knowing the mode with the smallest rank. A key contribution is a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms, generalizing existing results in structured sparsity. The findings are validated through numerical simulations confirming the predicted scaling behavior of mean squared error.

The paper builds upon recent work on convex relaxation for tensor decomposition [9, 15, 23, 25, 26], addressing a critical gap in theoretical understanding. It connects these techniques to broader work on structured sparsity [2, 13, 17, 21], providing a unifying perspective. The duality result presented (Lemma 1) is a significant technical contribution, potentially interesting beyond the specific context of tensor decomposition. The theoretical analysis (Theorems 1-3) rigorously establishes the consistency and performance bounds of the latent approach, and the numerical results effectively support these claims. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a strong theoretical foundation for the observed performance of the latent approach, filling a significant gap in the literature.
*   **Novel Duality Result:** The established duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a valuable contribution with potential wider applicability.
*   **Clear Explanation:** The paper clearly explains why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in specific scenarios, linking it to the minimum mode-k rank.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Numerical simulations confirm the theoretical predictions regarding the scaling behavior of the mean squared error.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Assumptions:** The deterministic bounds (Theorem 2) rely on the somewhat strong incoherence assumption (Equation 10), limiting its generalizability.
*   **Practicality:** While theoretically sound, the paper doesn’t delve into the practical computational challenges associated with solving the proposed optimization problem (11), specifically the spectral norm constraints.
*   **Limited Scope of Numerical Experiments:** The numerical experiments are fairly limited in scope, focusing primarily on demonstrating the scaling behavior. A more comprehensive evaluation on diverse datasets would strengthen the findings.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically grounded explanation for the superior performance of a recently proposed tensor decomposition method.
*   The duality result is a significant technical contribution.
*   The work addresses a crucial gap in the theoretical understanding of convex relaxation for tensor decomposition.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The strong assumptions required for the deterministic performance bounds.
*   The lack of discussion on the computational challenges of implementing the proposed method.



**Overall:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper’s theoretical contributions and clear explanation of the latent approach’s benefits outweigh its weaknesses. Addressing the points above – particularly discussing the practical implications of the spectral norm constraints and potentially expanding the numerical experiments – would further strengthen the manuscript.
