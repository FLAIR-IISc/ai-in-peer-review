This paper develops a unified framework of structured Schatten norms for tensors, showing that “overlapped” and “latent” tensor nuclear-norm regularizers are dual in the sense that the dual of the overlapped Sp/q-norm is a latent Sp*/q*-norm. Building on this, the authors analyze the latent approach to convex tensor decomposition and prove that, for denoising, its mean-squared error scales with the minimum mode rank of the ground-truth tensor—matching the behavior of an oracle that knows the lowest-rank mode. They provide three main results: (i) a consistency bound using the overlapped S∞/∞ norm of the noise; (ii) deterministic error bounds under an incoherence-style spectral-norm constraint controlling cross-mode interference; and (iii) Gaussian-noise rates where the error depends linearly on mink rk (up to mild dimension factors). Experiments on synthetic data confirm the predicted scaling and demonstrate improvements over the overlapped approach even when the tensor is jointly low-rank. The work is well-situated relative to prior NIPS and related literature on convex tensor methods [Tomioka et al.; Negahban et al.] and structured sparsity and dual norms [Bach, Jenatton, Obozinski], and contrasts with bounds for the overlapped norm [Tomioka et al.; Mu et al.].

Quality: The theoretical development is careful and leverages decomposable-regularizer techniques to obtain sharp, interpretable rates. The duality result is clean and broadly useful beyond tensors. Some assumptions in the deterministic bound (the spectral-norm constraints and α) may be restrictive operationally, and the dependence of constants (c, F) is not always transparent.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written and organized; the path from duality to bounds is clear. It would help to add intuition for the spectral-norm constraints in (11), clarify how α might be estimated, and discuss computational complexity and scalability of the latent solver.

Originality: The duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms and the adaptation-to-minimum-rank error rates appear novel and extend structured sparsity insights to tensors.

Significance: High. The results explain empirical advantages of the latent approach and provide actionable guidance on regularization, with potential impact on multiway denoising and imputation.

Pros for acceptance:
- Elegant duality connecting two families of tensor norms.
- Sharp rates adapting to the minimum mode rank; clear improvement over overlapped bounds.
- Empirical validation consistent with theory.
- Relevance to ongoing NIPS work on convex recovery and structured sparsity.

Cons for acceptance:
- Deterministic bounds require additional constraints whose practicality is unclear.
- Experiments are synthetic only; no tensor completion results.
- Limited discussion of computational costs and convergence vs. nonconvex baselines.

Suggestions: Discuss estimation/tuning of α and σ; add real-data or completion experiments; report runtime scaling; relate constants to problem dimensions; clarify whether results extend beyond S1/1 to general Sp/q choices.
