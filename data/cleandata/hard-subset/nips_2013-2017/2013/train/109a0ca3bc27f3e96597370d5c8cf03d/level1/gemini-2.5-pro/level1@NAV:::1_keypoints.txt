- Presents a theoretical analysis of the "latent" convex tensor decomposition approach within a class of structured Schatten norms.  
- Main contribution: first rigorous performance guarantee for the latent approach, explaining its empirical superiority over the overlapped method.  
- Shows estimation error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the tensor (vs. overlapped approach scaling with the sum of mode-k ranks).  
- Proves a novel duality between the latent and overlapped norm classes, linking to structured sparsity and overlapping group lasso.  
- Quality & significance: provides a clear, intuitive explanation of latent method robustness and adaptive behavior to the most compact tensor representation.  
- Originality: duality result is new and a unifying theoretical tool; rigorous analysis of the latent norm is novel.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and includes experiments designed to verify theoretical scaling laws.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 depends on an incoherence assumption and an added spectral-norm constraint; practical implications are not fully discussed.  
- Weakness: Analysis is restricted to tensor denoising; extension to tensor completion is left open.  
- Argument for acceptance: provides the first theoretical justification for a superior empirical method and offers a novel duality of independent interest.  
- Argument against acceptance: relies on technical assumptions that may not hold in all practical scenarios.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant, and original work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
