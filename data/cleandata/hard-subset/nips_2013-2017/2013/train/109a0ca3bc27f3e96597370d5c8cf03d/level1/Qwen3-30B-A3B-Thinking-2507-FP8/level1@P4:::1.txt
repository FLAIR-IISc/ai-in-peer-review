

# Review: Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization

This paper investigates structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, focusing on the relationship between two approaches: the "overlapped" approach (previously studied in [9, 15, 23, 25]) and the "latent" approach (proposed in [25]). The authors establish a novel duality between these approaches, demonstrating that the dual of the overlapped Schatten norm is the latent Schatten norm. They then provide theoretical analysis showing that the latent approach performs as well as knowing the mode with the smallest rank, which explains its empirically observed superiority over the overlapped approach in certain settings. The paper includes theoretical bounds for denoising performance and numerical experiments confirming these theoretical predictions.

**Quality**: The paper presents technically sound theoretical results. The authors establish a consistency theorem (Theorem 1), deterministic bounds (Theorem 2), and Gaussian noise analysis (Theorem 3) for the latent approach, with proofs provided in the supplementary material. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, with careful attention to the assumptions required for each result. The numerical experiments (Section 4) confirm the theoretical predictions, showing that the mean squared error scales linearly with the predicted complexity measures. The authors are careful to distinguish between the multilinear rank of the true tensor and the ranks of its latent components, and they acknowledge that the optimal regularization constant is independent of these ranks, which is an important practical insight.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and positions the work within the tensor decomposition literature. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-defined, with the key concepts (overlapped vs. latent norms) introduced early and clearly. The figures (particularly Figure 2) effectively illustrate the empirical results. The paper provides sufficient detail for an expert reader to understand and reproduce the results.

**Originality**: The paper makes a novel contribution with its duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which generalizes results from structured sparsity literature [2, 13, 17, 21]. While the latent approach was previously proposed in [25], this paper provides the first theoretical justification for its empirical success. The connection between tensor decomposition and structured sparsity is insightful and helps contextualize the work within a broader theoretical framework. The paper clearly distinguishes itself from previous NIPS papers on tensor decomposition [25, 26] by providing a rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach.

**Significance**: The results are significant because they provide a theoretical foundation for the empirical observation that the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach. This guidance is valuable for practitioners working on tensor decomposition problems. The structured Schatten norm framework proposed in the paper may have broader applications beyond tensor decomposition. The paper advances the state of the art in tensor decomposition by providing a more complete theoretical understanding of the latent approach, which was previously only empirically observed.

**Strengths**:
- Novel duality result for structured Schatten norms that generalizes structured sparsity literature
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with well-supported bounds for the latent approach
- Clear empirical validation of theoretical predictions
- Well-written paper with clear organization and sufficient detail
- Provides practical insight about the optimal choice of regularization constant

**Weaknesses**:
- Limited experimental validation (only two tensor sizes and one noise level)
- No detailed computational complexity analysis comparing the two approaches
- Does not address tensor completion applications, which is a major use case for tensor decomposition
- The paper mentions future work on tensor completion but doesn't explore this connection in depth

**Comparison to previous work**: The paper builds on the recent work by Tomioka et al. [25] which proposed the latent approach, but provides the first theoretical justification for its empirical success. It also extends the analysis of the overlapped approach from [26], showing that the latent approach's complexity depends on the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average rank. The paper correctly cites relevant prior work in structured sparsity [2, 13, 17, 21] and tensor decomposition [9, 14, 15, 23, 25].

**Overall assessment**: This paper makes a solid theoretical contribution to the field of tensor decomposition by providing rigorous justification for the empirical success of the latent approach. The duality result is novel and interesting, and the theoretical analysis is sound. The empirical results confirm the theoretical predictions, providing a complete picture of the performance of the latent approach. While the experimental validation could be more extensive, the paper's theoretical contribution is substantial and well-supported. The paper is clearly within the scope of NIPS, as tensor decomposition is a well-established topic in machine learning with many prior NIPS publications.

**Arguments for acceptance**: The paper presents a novel theoretical contribution with a clear duality result, provides rigorous theoretical analysis that advances our understanding of tensor decomposition, and confirms its theoretical predictions with empirical evidence. The work is well-written and makes a significant contribution to the field.

**Arguments against acceptance**: The paper lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis and does not address tensor completion applications, which are a major use case for tensor decomposition. However, these limitations do not significantly undermine the paper's theoretical contribution.

Given the strong theoretical contribution and clear empirical validation, this paper should be accepted.
