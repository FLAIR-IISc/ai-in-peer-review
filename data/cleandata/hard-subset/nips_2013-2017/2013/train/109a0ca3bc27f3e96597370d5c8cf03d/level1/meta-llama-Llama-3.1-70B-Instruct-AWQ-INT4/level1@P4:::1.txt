This paper proposes a framework for structured Schatten norms, which includes both the overlapped Schatten 1-norm and latent Schatten 1-norm recently proposed in the context of convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition. The authors show a duality between the two types of norms and rigorously study the performance of the latent approach for tensor decomposition. The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence that the latent approach is preferable to the overlapped approach for noisy tensor decomposition.

The main contributions of the paper are:

1. A novel duality result between the overlapped Schatten norm and the latent Schatten norm.
2. A theoretical analysis of the performance of the latent approach for tensor decomposition, which shows that the error of the latent approach is upper bounded by the minimal mode-k rank.
3. An empirical comparison between the overlapped approach and the latent approach, which confirms that the latent approach performs better in many cases.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed framework and the theoretical results. The empirical results are also well-presented and provide strong evidence for the superiority of the latent approach.
