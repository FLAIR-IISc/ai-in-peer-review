- Presents a comprehensive study of structured Schatten norms for tensors applied to convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition.
- Introduces a new class of norms, including "overlapped" and "latent" approaches, and analyzes their properties.
- Major technical contributions: establishes duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms.
- Derives consistency and deterministic bounds for the latent approach.
- Provides thorough theoretical and empirical comparison of overlapped vs. latent methods.
- Numerical experiments show the latent approach often outperforms overlapped, especially when the tensor is low-rank in one mode.
- Well-written and well-organized with a clear, concise introduction; proofs moved to supplementary material for readability.
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in tensor decomposition and convex optimization, reducing accessibility for non-experts.
- Weakness: some technical results (e.g., duality theorem) may be hard to follow without strong functional analysis background.
- Weakness: numerical experiments are limited in scope; more extensive experiments would strengthen the claims.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add background/explanations and expand experiments.
- Reviewer rating: 9/10; confidence: 8/10.
