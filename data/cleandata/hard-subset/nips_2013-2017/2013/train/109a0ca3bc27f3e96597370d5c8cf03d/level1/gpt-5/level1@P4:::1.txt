Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor estimation using structured Schatten norms. It formalizes two families:
- Overlapped Sp/q norms: sum or Lq-aggregation of Schatten p-norms of mode-k unfoldings, encouraging simultaneous low rank across modes (generalizing the commonly used overlapped Schatten-1 norm).
- Latent Sp*/q* norms: a decomposition-based norm defined via an infimum over sums of tensors, each regularized in one mode (generalizing the “latent” trace norm).

A main theoretical result is a duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm, extending known dualities from structured sparsity with overlaps to the tensor/Schatten setting. Building on high-dimensional M-estimation techniques for decomposable regularizers, the paper analyzes the latent approach in a denoising setup (no missing data). It proves (i) a basic consistency bound with a λ chosen by the dual norm of the noise; and, under an incoherence-style assumption controlled via spectral norm constraints, (ii) deterministic and Gaussian-noise error bounds. The key message is that the latent approach’s error scales with the minimal mode rank of the true tensor, contrasting with prior results for the overlapped approach where complexity scales with an average of mode ranks. Experiments corroborate the predicted scaling and show cases where the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach. The work relates and compares to prior tensor decomposition methods (ALS/HOOI), convex tensor completion (e.g., Liu et al., Signoretto et al.), analysis of overlapped norms (Tomioka et al. 2011/2012), and structured sparsity theory (Agarwal, Negahban et al., Maurer & Pontil, Jenatton et al.).

Strengths
- Clear and useful unification via structured Schatten norms and an elegant duality between overlapped and latent norms; this generalizes earlier overlapped/latent group-lasso insights to tensors.
- Technically substantive analysis of the latent approach in a noisy observation setting, with bounds that highlight dependence on min mode rank rather than averages, aligning with empirical behavior.
- Sound use of decomposable-regularizer techniques; conditions on λ are explicit and data-independent w.r.t. unknown ranks.
- Experiments designed to test scaling laws; empirical results align closely with theory and include comparisons to overlapped approaches.
- Paper is well-organized with precise definitions and statements, and appropriately situates contributions relative to NIPS and related literature.

Weaknesses and points needing clarification
- The deterministic and Gaussian-noise bounds rely on additional spectral norm constraints (parameter α) to enforce incoherence between components (equation (11)). The practical procedure for choosing α and whether these constraints are implemented in experiments are not clearly documented. This creates a gap between the analyzed estimator and the algorithm evaluated if the constraint is not enforced.
- The incoherence assumption (bounding ∥W∗(l)(k)∥S∞ for l ≠ k) may be restrictive; its typical size and realism for common data generating processes are not examined.
- Constants in the bounds are unspecified; although standard, discussing their magnitude would aid interpretation (especially the factor F in Theorem 3).
- The analysis is limited to the denoising setting without missing data; tensor completion is left as future work. Given the motivation, at least a discussion on whether the same techniques extend (and what obstacles arise) would be valuable.
- Experiments focus on 3-way synthetic tensors and 1/1 norms; the framework is more general (Sp/q). Some evidence that generalization beyond 1/1 is tractable (even small-scale) would strengthen the empirical section.
- Minor clarity: distinguish clearly between r̄k (ranks of latent components) and rk (multilinear ranks) upfront to avoid confusion; add brief intuition for the α(K−1) term in λ.

Quality
- The theoretical results are plausible and align with established techniques; the duality and bounds appear technically sound given the stated assumptions. The mismatch between the constrained estimator analyzed and the algorithmic instantiation used in experiments should be clarified.

Clarity
- Generally clear and well-structured. Definitions and notation are explicit. A few places would benefit from added guidance on α selection/implementation and the practical impact of the incoherence constraints.

Originality
- The duality result for structured Schatten norms and the latent-norm denoising guarantees that scale with min mode rank represent novel contributions relative to prior NIPS work on overlapped norms and tensor completion.

Significance
- Understanding when latent versus overlapped tensor norms are advantageous is important for practice. Showing min-rank dependence provides actionable guidance and advances theory. While limited to denoising, the framework and insights are likely to influence further developments in convex tensor methods.

Suggestions for improvement
- Explicitly state whether the spectral norm constraints were used in experiments; if not, discuss why empirical results still match theory and whether similar bounds can be proved without hard constraints (e.g., via approximate incoherence conditions).
- Provide guidance or heuristics for choosing α and assessing incoherence in data.
- Include a brief discussion on extension to tensor completion and challenges (e.g., restricted strong convexity, sample complexity with latent norms).
- Report sensitivity to λ and α (if used) and include confidence intervals or variability across trials.
- Add a small example or appendix illustrating the Sp/q generalization beyond 1/1.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel and clean duality connecting overlapped and latent tensor norms.
- New, interpretable error bounds demonstrating advantages of the latent approach under realistic conditions.
- Empirical validation aligns with theoretical scaling; practical relevance for tensor denoising.

Arguments against acceptance
- Analysis depends on additional constraints that may not be used in practice; unclear treatment in experiments.
- Scope limited to denoising; lacks results for the widely studied completion setting.
- Some assumptions (incoherence via spectral norm bounds) may be strong, with limited guidance on verification or parameter selection.

Overall: a solid theoretical and empirical contribution that clarifies the benefits of latent Schatten norms for tensor denoising. Addressing the role and implementation of the spectral norm constraints would further strengthen the paper.
