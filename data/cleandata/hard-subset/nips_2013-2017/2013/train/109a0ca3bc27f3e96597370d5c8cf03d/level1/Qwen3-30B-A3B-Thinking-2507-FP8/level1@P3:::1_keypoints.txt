- Problem: Compares overlapped vs latent convex tensor decomposition and explains why latent outperforms when the tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.  
- Contribution: Proposes a structured Schatten-norm framework that includes both overlapped and latent approaches.  
- Contribution: Proves a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms (Lemma 1).  
- Contribution: Theoretical analysis showing latent error scales with min_k √r_k while overlapped scales with (1/K)∑√r_k (Theorem 3).  
- Prior work: Builds on Tomioka et al. and connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature.  
- Strength — Theory: First rigorous explanation for empirical superiority of latent approach; clear scaling results.  
- Strength — Duality: Establishes a clean, general duality between the two norm types with deeper structural insight.  
- Strength — Experiments: Numerical results confirm theoretical predictions; MSE scales with the predicted complexity measures.  
- Strength — Exposition: Well-organized, precise mathematical presentation and motivated connections to structured sparsity.  
- Weakness — Scope: Focuses only on denoising (no missing data), limiting applicability to tensor completion tasks.  
- Weakness — Incoherence: Tighter bounds depend on an incoherence condition that may not hold or be easy to verify in practice.  
- Weakness — Computation: Does not compare computational complexity or practical optimization trade-offs between approaches.  
- Weakness — Related work: Insufficient discussion of how their bounds relate to Mu et al.'s lower bounds.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance — valuable theoretical advance with minor limitations.  
- Meta-evaluation: Paper meets criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Pros for acceptance: First rigorous explanation, novel duality, theory validated by experiments, strong literature connections.  
- Cons for rejection: Limited to denoising, reliance on incoherence assumption, lack of computational trade-off analysis.
