- Paper formalizes two structured Schatten-norm families: overlapped Sp/q (sum or Lq-aggregation of mode-k Schatten p-norms) and latent Sp*/q* (decomposition-based infimum over mode-regularized tensors).
- Main theoretical result: duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms, extending overlapped/latent group-lasso duality to tensors/Schatten setting.
- Analyzes the latent approach in a denoising (no missing data) setup using decomposable-regularizer techniques and M-estimation tools.
- Proves a consistency bound with λ chosen by the noise dual norm and, under an incoherence-style spectral constraint, deterministic and Gaussian-noise error bounds.
- Key message: latent approach error scales with the minimal mode rank of the true tensor, unlike overlapped approaches which scale with averaged mode ranks.
- Experiments on synthetic 3-way tensors corroborate theoretical scaling and show instances where latent outperforms overlapped norms.
- Relates and compares to prior tensor decomposition, convex tensor completion, overlapped-norm analyses, and structured sparsity literature.
- Strength: clear unification via structured Schatten norms and an elegant, general duality result.
- Strength: technically substantive analysis yielding interpretable min-rank-dependent error bounds consistent with empirical findings.
- Strength: rigorous use of decomposable-regularizer framework with explicit, data-independent λ conditions.
- Strength: experiments designed to test scaling laws and align closely with theory; paper is well-organized and well-situated in literature.
- Weakness: deterministic/Gaussian bounds rely on spectral-norm constraints (α) enforcing incoherence, but practical selection and experimental use of α are unclear.
- Weakness: incoherence assumption (bounding ∥W*(l)(k)∥S∞ for l ≠ k) may be restrictive; typical size and realism not investigated.
- Weakness: constants in bounds are unspecified; magnitudes (e.g., factor F in Theorem 3) are not discussed, limiting interpretability.
- Weakness: analysis limited to denoising without missing data; extension to tensor completion is left as future work without detailed discussion.
- Weakness: experiments focus on 3-way synthetic tensors and 1/1 norms; little empirical evidence for general Sp/q settings.
- Weakness (minor): notation could more clearly distinguish r̄k (latent component ranks) from rk (multilinear ranks) and explain the α(K−1) term in λ.
- Quality: theoretical results appear plausible and technically sound given assumptions, but mismatch between analyzed constrained estimator and implemented algorithm needs clarification.
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well-structured, but needs added guidance on α selection/implementation and practical impact of incoherence constraints.
- Originality: duality for structured Schatten norms and min-rank-dependent denoising guarantees offer novel contributions beyond prior overlapped-norm work.
- Significance: results clarify when latent vs. overlapped norms are advantageous, providing actionable insights for tensor denoising methods.
- Suggestion: state whether spectral-norm constraints were used in experiments and, if not, explain why empirical results match theory or derive bounds without hard constraints.
- Suggestion: provide heuristics for choosing α and assessing incoherence, discuss extension to tensor completion and related obstacles, and report sensitivity to λ/α with variability.
- Recommendation: overall a solid contribution; addressing implementation/role of spectral-norm constraints would strengthen the paper and resolve practical-theoretical gaps.
