- Unified framework: frames overlapped and latent tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms.  
- Overlapped approach: regularizes W by sum of Schatten-1 norms of unfoldings, encouraging low-rank in all modes.  
- Latent approach: models W as sum of mode-specific low-rank components; equivalent to a latent Schatten norm.  
- Contribution 1: introduces overlapped and latent Sp/q norms and proves a novel duality (Lemma 1), linking to structured sparsity.  
- Contribution 2: provides MSE bounds for latent method (Theorems 2–3) that scale with min_k r_k, unlike overlapped bounds scaling with (sum_k sqrt(r_k))^2.  
- Implication: latent method adapts to the “easiest” mode and excels when the tensor is low-rank in only one or a few modes.  
- Contribution 3: synthetic experiments validate theory, showing MSE scales with min_k r_k / n_K and latent outperforms overlapped as predicted.  
- Strength — Significance: first theoretical justification for latent approach and its adaptive behavior.  
- Strength — Originality & Quality: novel, rigorous analysis; structured Schatten norms and duality are elegant and technically sound.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and links theory to experiments clearly.  
- Strength — Completeness: includes theoretical framework, rigorous proofs, and empirical validation.  
- Weakness — Theory vs. practice: main theorem analyzes a constrained problem (||W(l)(k)||_S_inf ≤ alpha) but experiments use the unconstrained formulation, creating a disconnect.  
- Weakness — Incoherence assumption: role and choice of parameter alpha need clearer intuition and practical guidance; clarify trivial satisfaction in singleton decomposition if applicable.  
- Weakness — LR complexity computation: experimental procedure for latent-rank complexity is ad-hoc; discuss difficulty of finding optimal decomposition minimizing sum_k r̄_k.  
- Pro acceptance: important, novel theoretical contribution with clear practical implications and solid experiments.  
- Con acceptance: minor clarifications needed on constrained vs. unconstrained formulations and on the incoherence assumption.
