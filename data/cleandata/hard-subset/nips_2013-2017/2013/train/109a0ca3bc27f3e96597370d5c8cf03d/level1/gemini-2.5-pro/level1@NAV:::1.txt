This paper presents a theoretical analysis of the "latent" approach for convex tensor decomposition, framing it within a general class of structured Schatten norms. This work builds directly upon previous NIPS papers on convex relaxations for tensor decomposition, which primarily introduced and analyzed an "overlapped" approach (e.g., Tomioka et al., 2012). The main contribution is to provide the first rigorous performance guarantee for the latent approach, which was previously observed to outperform the overlapped method empirically. The authors prove that the latent approach's estimation error scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank of the underlying tensor, in contrast to the overlapped approach's dependence on the sum of mode-k ranks. A key component of this analysis is a novel duality result between the proposed "latent" and "overlapped" norm classes, which elegantly connects this line of work to the broader literature on structured sparsity and overlapping group lasso.

The paper has several significant strengths.
*   **Quality & Significance:** The theoretical contribution is substantial. It provides a clear and intuitive explanation for the robustness of the latent approach, demonstrating that it effectively adapts to the most compact representation of the tensor across its modes without prior knowledge. This is a significant step forward in understanding convex methods for tensor analysis.
*   **Originality:** The duality result is novel and provides a unifying theoretical tool. While the latent norm itself has been proposed before, its rigorous analysis is new and constitutes a solid contribution.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is very well-written and organized. It clearly introduces the problem, defines the relevant norms, presents the key duality lemma, and builds the main performance bounds upon this foundation. The experiments are particularly strong, as they are explicitly designed to verify the derived theoretical scaling laws rather than merely comparing methods.

The paper has a few minor weaknesses.
*   The tighter theoretical analysis (Theorem 2) relies on an incoherence assumption and an added spectral norm constraint in the optimization problem. While such assumptions are common in high-dimensional statistics, the paper could benefit from a brief discussion on their practical implications or necessity.
*   The analysis is currently focused on the tensor denoising setting. An extension to the tensor completion problem, a widely used application, remains an important open direction, as the authors note.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Provides the first theoretical justification for an empirically superior method.
*   The novel duality result is of independent interest and strengthens the connection between tensor methods and structured sparsity.
*   The analysis is technically sound and the results represent a clear advance for the field.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
*   The analysis relies on technical assumptions that may not hold in all practical scenarios.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant and original contribution to the theory of tensor methods. The strengths far outweigh the minor weaknesses, and I strongly recommend acceptance.
