- Introduces a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, including overlapped and latent variants.  
- Provides a novel duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, relevant to structured sparsity.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in a specific unknown mode.  
- Rigorous proofs are supplied via lemmas and theorems supporting the latent approach analysis.  
- Empirical experiments corroborate the theoretical findings.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough introduction to tensor decomposition issues.  
- Definitions of structured Schatten norms and the duality result are clearly explained.  
- Main contribution is the theoretical treatment of the latent approach and its superiority in the specified setting.  
- Strength: strong, rigorous theoretical analysis combined with supporting empirical evidence.  
- Strength: novel duality result that advances understanding in tensor methods and structured sparsity.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial prior knowledge in tensor decomposition and structured sparsity, which may hinder accessibility.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of practical implications and how results translate to real-world applications.
