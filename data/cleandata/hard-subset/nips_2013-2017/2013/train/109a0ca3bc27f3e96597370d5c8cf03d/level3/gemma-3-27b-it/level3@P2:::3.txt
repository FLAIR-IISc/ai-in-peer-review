This paper presents a theoretical analysis of convex tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, specifically focusing on the “latent” approach recently proposed by Tomioka et al. (2013). The authors establish consistency results and denoising performance bounds for the latent approach, demonstrating its superiority over the “overlapped” approach, particularly when the underlying tensor is low-rank in only one mode. A novel duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is also presented, with broader implications for structured sparsity.

The decomposition of tensors is a crucial problem in various fields, including neuroimaging, spatio-temporal data analysis, and collaborative filtering, as highlighted in the introduction. Traditionally tackled via non-convex methods (De Lathauwer et al., 2000; Kolda & Bader, 2009), recent work has explored convex relaxations (Gandy et al., 2014; Tomioka et al., 2013). The key contribution of this paper lies in providing a rigorous theoretical underpinning for the empirical success of the latent approach, a critical advance given the limited theoretical understanding of tensor decomposition algorithms. The connection to structured sparsity (Jenatton et al., 2010; Mairal et al., 2010) and the established duality result are valuable contributions in their own right.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Analysis:** The paper provides a rigorous mathematical analysis of the latent approach, including consistency and denoising bounds. The bounds are expressed in terms of the minimal mode-k rank, providing a clear explanation for the observed performance improvements.
*   **Novel Duality Result:** The duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a significant theoretical contribution, generalizing results in the structured sparsity literature (Maurer & Pontil, 2011).
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and organized, making the complex theoretical concepts accessible. Definitions and notation are clearly explained.
*   **Significance:** Addressing the theoretical limitations of convex tensor decomposition is important. The demonstrated performance improvements of the latent approach, backed by theory, represent a tangible advance.
*   **Connections to Prior Work:** The paper clearly situates its contributions within the broader context of tensor decomposition and structured sparsity, citing relevant literature.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Experimental Evaluation:** While the authors include numerical simulations, the experimental section is somewhat brief and lacks detail. The choice of regularization parameters and the method for determining Tucker ranks are not fully explained.
*   **Missing Comparison to Non-Convex Methods:** The paper focuses on comparing the overlapped and latent convex approaches, but lacks a comparison to traditional non-convex tensor decomposition methods (e.g., alternating least squares). This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach's benefits.
*   **Incomplete Exploration of Group Regularization:** The paper doesn't fully explore the behavior of group regularization approaches (e.g. l_{1,∞}) when the tensor *is* low-rank in all modes. Understanding this would further clarify the trade-offs between different regularization strategies.
*   **Reliance on Incoherence Assumption:** The deterministic bound (Theorem 2) relies on an incoherence assumption (Equation 10), which may not always hold in practice. The sensitivity of the results to this assumption should be further investigated.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important open problem in tensor decomposition: providing theoretical guarantees for convex approaches.
*   The theoretical results are strong and demonstrate a clear advantage of the latent approach.
*   The duality result is novel and of independent interest.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.
*   The numerical results support the theoretical findings, even if the experimental evaluation is not exhaustive.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The experimental evaluation is limited and lacks sufficient detail.
*   The absence of a comparison to non-convex methods weakens the assessment of the approach's practical advantages.
*   The reliance on an incoherence assumption is a potential limitation.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, the duality result is significant, and the paper is clearly written. While the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, the existing results support the theoretical claims. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.

**Rating:** Accept (Strongly Consider)

**Recommendation:** I recommend acceptance, contingent upon the authors adding more detail to the experimental section, specifically regarding the selection of regularization parameters and Tucker ranks. A brief discussion of the limitations of the incoherence assumption would also be beneficial.
