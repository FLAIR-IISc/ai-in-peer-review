- Topic: Studies convex tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, focusing on latent vs overlapped formulations.  
- Main contribution 1: Proves a duality—dual of overlapped Sp/q norm is latent Sp*/q*—generalizing overlapped vs latent group sparsity results.  
- Main contribution 2: Provides finite-sample denoising guarantees for the latent Schatten-1 norm, with consistency and bounds scaling with the minimum mode rank.  
- Theoretical refinement: Improves prior convex tensor analyses by replacing average (sqrt) rank dependence with min-rank scaling, matching empirical observations for single-mode low-rank tensors.  
- Experiments: Brief experiments illustrate the predicted scaling but lack depth and detail.  
- Quality: Theory is careful, technically sound, and assumptions/error bounds are clearly stated.  
- Duality value: The duality result is crisp and broadly useful for structured regularizers.  
- Weak experimental section: Missing key experimental details (e.g., how Tucker ranks were chosen) and limited comparisons.  
- Clarity: Paper is well organized and readable; proofs and assumptions are easy to follow.  
- Suggestion on clarity: Provide more intuition and practical guidance for enforcing the α-incoherence constraint.  
- Originality: High—dual characterization and min-rank latent-norm bounds appear novel and independently interesting.  
- Significance: Strong—the results advance convex tensor estimation and may guide new regularizer designs.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions for experiments: Expand to real datasets, report runtime/memory, test sensitivity to λ, ablate α-constraints, and compare to nonconvex methods (ALS/HOOI/Tucker/CP) with rank selection.  
- Additional suggestion: Clarify synthetic data rank selection and evaluate multiple regimes (global low-rank vs single-mode low-rank).  
- Consideration of variants: Discuss overlapped S1/∞ or ℓ1,∞ group-regularization behavior when tensor is low-rank in all modes.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Elegant, original duality; substantially improved theoretical bounds; clear exposition and reasonable assumptions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Thin empirical evaluation lacking strong nonconvex baselines and missing reproducibility details.
