This paper addresses the important problem of convex tensor decomposition, specifically comparing and theoretically analyzing the "overlapped" and "latent" approaches. The core contribution lies in providing theoretical guarantees for the latent approach, demonstrating its superior performance, particularly when the underlying tensor is low-rank in only one mode. A noteworthy and original result is the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, a contribution valuable beyond the immediate context of tensor decomposition.

**Summary:**

The authors investigate structured Schatten norms for tensors, focusing on the latent approach which has empirically shown better performance than the overlapped approach when a tensor is low-rank in a single mode. They provide theoretical justification for this empirical observation, proving the latent approach’s mean squared error scales with the minimum mode-k rank.  A key technical contribution is establishing a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms.  They support their theoretical results with numerical experiments confirming the predicted scaling behavior.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Analysis:** The paper presents a rigorous theoretical analysis, including consistency results and deterministic bounds. The proofs appear well-structured (although detailed in the supplementary material).
* **Novel Duality Result:** The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a significant contribution with potential broader applications in structured sparsity.
* **Clear and Well-Organized:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors’ line of reasoning. Definitions are clear, and the notation is consistent.
* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with applications in diverse areas, and improving the theoretical understanding of convex approaches is valuable.
* **Meaningful Improvement:** The paper demonstrably improves upon existing results by providing theoretical guarantees for the latent approach, explaining its empirical success.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Brief Experimental Evaluation:** While the experiments confirm the theoretical scaling, they are relatively brief and lack sufficient detail.
* **Tucker Rank Selection:** The paper does not detail how the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) were chosen for the experiments. This information is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the experimental setup. Were they chosen randomly? Based on some prior knowledge?
* **Missing Non-Convex Comparison:** The paper focuses solely on convex approaches. A comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition methods (e.g., alternating least squares) would strengthen the evaluation and provide a more complete picture of the latent approach’s performance.  It's not clear how competitive the convex approach is in practice against established non-convex methods.
* **Limited Group Regularization Analysis:** The paper briefly touches upon the behavior of group regularization when the tensor is low-rank in all modes but doesn't deeply explore it.  A more thorough analysis of this scenario could provide additional insights.  Specifically, investigating the interplay between the Schatten norm and different group regularization parameters would be beneficial.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The theoretical contributions are significant and advance the understanding of convex tensor decomposition.
* The dual characterization of the norms is a novel and potentially impactful result.
* The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
* The problem addressed is important, and the paper offers a meaningful improvement over existing work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The experimental evaluation is limited and lacks detail.
* The absence of a comparison to non-convex methods weakens the evaluation.
* The lack of in-depth analysis of group regularization is a missed opportunity.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the limitations in the experimental evaluation, this is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions. The clarity of presentation and the novelty of the duality result are particularly commendable. The paper meaningfully advances the state of the art in convex tensor decomposition and is worthy of acceptance. 

**Recommendations:**

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should:

1.  **Expand the experimental section:** Provide more details about the experimental setup, including how the Tucker ranks were chosen.
2.  **Add a comparison to non-convex methods:** Include a comparison to a standard non-convex tensor decomposition algorithm (e.g., alternating least squares).
3. **Clarify the role of the regularization constant:** Discuss the choice of the regularization constant λ in more detail and potentially provide guidance on how to select it in practice.
