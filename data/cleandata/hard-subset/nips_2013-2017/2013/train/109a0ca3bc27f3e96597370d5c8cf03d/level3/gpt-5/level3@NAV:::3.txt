This paper studies convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms and provides sharper theory for the “latent” formulation relative to the now-standard “overlapped” formulation. The main technical contributions are: (i) a dual characterization showing that the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm, generalizing results in structured sparsity (e.g., overlapped vs latent group lasso); and (ii) finite-sample guarantees for denoising with the latent Schatten-1 norm, including a consistency result and bounds whose dependence scales with the minimum mode rank rather than the average (square-root) dependence known for overlapped norms. This refines earlier convex analyses for tensor problems (e.g., NIPS work on decomposable regularizers and prior convex tensor recovery papers) and aligns with empirical observations that the latent approach excels when the tensor is low-rank in an unknown single mode. Brief experiments illustrate the predicted scaling.

Quality: The theory is careful and technically sound. The duality result is crisp and broadly useful. The main error bounds are clearly stated with transparent assumptions (including an incoherence-style condition) and improve upon prior overlapped-norm analyses. However, the experimental section is too brief to fully substantiate practical impact; key details (e.g., how Tucker ranks (r1,…,rK) were chosen for data generation) are missing, and comparisons are limited.

Clarity: The paper is well organized and clearly written. Definitions, assumptions, and proof roadmaps are easy to follow. Providing more intuition for the α-incoherence constraint and its practical enforcement would help.

Originality: High. The dual characterization for structured Schatten norms and the latent-norm bounds with min-rank scaling appear new and of independent interest.

Significance: Strong. The problem is important and the results advance the state of the art for convex tensor estimation. The duality may inform future designs of regularizers beyond tensors.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Expand experiments: include real datasets, runtime/memory, sensitivity to λ, and ablations on α-constraints.
- Compare against non-convex methods (ALS/HOOI/Tucker/CP with cross-validated ranks), reporting both accuracy and speed.
- Clarify how (r1,…,rK) were selected in synthetic data; report multiple regimes (simultaneously low-rank vs single-mode low-rank).
- Discuss/group-regularization variants (e.g., overlapped S1/∞ or ℓ1,∞ across modes) and their behavior when the tensor is low-rank in all modes.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Elegant, original duality with broad relevance.
- Substantially improved theoretical bounds (min-rank scaling).
- Clear exposition and well-scoped assumptions.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Empirical evaluation is thin and lacks comparisons to strong non-convex baselines.
- Missing experimental details hinder reproducibility and assessment of practical gains.
