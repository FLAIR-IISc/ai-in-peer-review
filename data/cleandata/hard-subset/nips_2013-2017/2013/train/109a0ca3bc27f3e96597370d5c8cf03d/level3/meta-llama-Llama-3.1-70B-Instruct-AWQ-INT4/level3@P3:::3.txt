This paper presents a novel framework for structured Schatten norms, which includes both the overlapped Schatten 1-norm and latent Schatten 1-norm, recently proposed in the context of convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition. The authors establish a duality between these two types of norms and provide a rigorous analysis of the performance of the latent approach for tensor decomposition. The paper is well-organized, and the theoretical analysis is strong.

The main contribution of this paper is the development of a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, which includes the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. The authors show that the dual norm of the overlapped Sp/q-norm is the latent Sp∗/q∗-norm, which is a novel result of independent interest. They also establish a generic consistency statement for the latent approach, which does not take into account the low-rank-ness of the truth. Additionally, they provide a tighter bound that takes the low-rank-ness into account with some incoherence assumption.

The authors also provide an empirical evaluation of the overlapped and latent approaches, which confirms their theoretical predictions. The results show that the latent approach performs better than the overlapped approach in many cases, especially when the unknown tensor is low-rank in one mode. The paper also provides a comparison with the overlapped approach, which shows that the complexity of the overlapped approach depends on the average (square root) of the mode-k ranks, whereas the complexity of the latent approach only grows linearly against the minimum mode-k rank.

The strengths of this paper include the development of a new framework for structured Schatten norms, the establishment of a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, and the rigorous analysis of the performance of the latent approach. The paper is well-organized, and the theoretical analysis is strong. The empirical evaluation also confirms the theoretical predictions, which provides additional evidence for the superiority of the latent approach.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. The experimental evaluation is brief and not sufficiently detailed. For example, the authors do not provide information on how the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) were chosen, which is an important detail in the experiment. Additionally, the paper does not provide a comparison with non-convex tensor decomposition approaches, which would be an interesting addition. Furthermore, the paper does not provide an analysis of group regularization (e.g., l_{1,∞}) behavior when the tensor is low-rank in all modes, which is an important aspect of the latent approach.

Overall, this paper provides an important contribution to the field of tensor decomposition and convex optimization. The development of a new framework for structured Schatten norms and the rigorous analysis of the performance of the latent approach make this paper a valuable addition to the literature. However, the paper could be improved by providing more details on the experimental evaluation and by including additional comparisons and analyses.
