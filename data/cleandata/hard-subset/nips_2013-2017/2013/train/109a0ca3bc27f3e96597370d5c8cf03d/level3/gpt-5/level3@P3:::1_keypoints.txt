- Studies convex, norm-regularized formulations for approximate low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on latent Schatten-1 norm vs overlapped Schatten-1 norm.
- Introduces a unified structured Schatten Sp/q framework and proves duality: dual of overlapped Sp/q is latent Sp*/q*.
- Analyzes latent norm for tensor denoising, providing consistency, deterministic error bounds under incoherence, and high-probability MSE bounds for Gaussian noise.
- Key message: latent regularization yields error scaling with the minimum mode rank, unlike overlapped norms that depend on average mode ranks.
- Numerical simulations corroborate theoretical scaling and show regimes where latent outperforms overlapped regularization.
- Connects and extends prior work on convex relaxations for low-rank tensors and structured sparsity, leveraging known Schatten inequalities and Gaussian concentration tools.
- Strength: clear, principled theoretical comparison explaining empirical advantages of latent regularization when one mode is low-rank.
- Strength: solid theoretical contributions (consistency, deterministic bounds, Gaussian-noise MSE) showing favorable latent scaling.
- Strength: clean, general duality theorem for structured Schatten norms.
- Strength: simulations closely match theory; paper is well organized and proofs appear careful.
- Weakness: only provides upper bounds—no minimax or matching lower bounds to assess optimality.
- Weakness: general Sp/q framework is mostly formal—no algorithms, complexity analysis, or behavioral results beyond the S1/1 case; abstract overstates contributions.
- Weakness: focuses only on fully observed denoising; lacks tensor completion theory and ML-relevant experiments or comparisons to nonconvex methods.
- Weakness: practical aspects under-discussed—choice/sensitivity of λ, selection and enforcement of α in constraints, and computational cost/scaling vs overlapped norms.
- Clarity issue: Figure 1 appears before key definitions and is hard to interpret; minor typos and reference inconsistencies need proofreading.
- Originality: duality result is neat but techniques are natural extensions of known group-norm dualities; contribution feels incremental.
- Significance: useful for convex tensor decomposition researchers but narrow scope (denoising fully observed) limits broader ML impact.
- Questions for authors: are latent-norm rates minimax optimal or matched by known lower bounds?
- Questions for authors: how should α be selected in practice, are constraints enforced empirically, and how sensitive are results to α?
- Questions for authors: do results extend to tensor completion, and what replaces the Gaussian noise term in λ?
- Pros for acceptance: technically sound analysis, clear takeaways favoring latent norms, and strong duality/unification.
- Cons for acceptance: narrow scope, limited ML relevance, lack of lower bounds, and moderate novelty.
- Recommendation: leaning toward weak reject unless strengthened with lower bounds, ML-relevant experiments/completion theory, and tempering of claims in the abstract.
