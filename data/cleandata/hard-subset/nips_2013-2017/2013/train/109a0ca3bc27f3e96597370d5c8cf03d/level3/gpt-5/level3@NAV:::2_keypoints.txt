- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms, unifying “overlapped” and “latent” formulations and proving a duality between them.  
- Main technical contribution: recovery bounds for the latent approach that scale with the minimum mode rank, explaining empirical advantages in noisy denoising.  
- Provides theoretical results (consistency; deterministic and Gaussian-noise bounds) and simulations that match predicted scaling.  
- Connects to structured sparsity (group lasso and latent variants) and classic tensor models (Tucker/HOSVD); timely for ML applications processing multiway data.  

Strengths:
- Technically solid and important: latent/overlapped duality and min-mode-rank error bounds sharpen understanding and align with practice.  
- Clear problem formulation with empirical validation that matches theory.  
- Broad relevance across ML areas using tensors (neuroimaging, recommender systems, etc.).  

Weaknesses / required clarifications:
- Motivation/accessibility: add a latent-variable graphical model, explain how observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, include figure, and note multilinear ranks = hidden-variable dimensions.  
- Emphasize advantage that only some hidden variables may be low-dimensional.  
- Theorem 2: state identifiability only up to permutation and precisely justify why Eq. (12) implies Eq. (13) (spell out singleton decomposition).  
- Proofs: provide the missing proof of Lemma 5 (pivotal for Theorem 2) and either include a self-contained proof of Lemma 4 or clearly delimit what is imported.  
- Related work: add HOSVD and variants, discuss approximation bounds, cite Kolda & Bader (SIAM Review, Sec. 4), and state explicitly that “mode-rank” = “multilinear rank” while noting multiple rank notions.  
- Complexity: briefly discuss computational complexity (per-iteration SVD costs, scaling with K and nk, and ADMM convergence).  
- Incoherence: post-(10) discussion is misleading relative to incoherence notions in cited works; carefully reconcile or remove the comparison.  
- Notation/typos: fix inconsistent superscripts for components, unify k-mode rank notation (e.g., mult_rank(·)), correct missing parenthesis (supp. p.12) and Abstract typo (“structured Schatten”).  

Pros for acceptance:
- New theoretical insight (duality; min-mode-rank guarantees) supported by empirical results.  
- Bridges structured sparsity and tensor decomposition literatures.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Several proof gaps and clarity/notation issues need addressing.  
- Motivation and broader accessibility require strengthening.  

Overall:
- Novelty is compelling—showing structured Schatten regularization yields Tucker-style decompositions with recovery guarantees and that the latent formulation adapts to the smallest mode rank; with the requested fixes this would be a strong contribution.
