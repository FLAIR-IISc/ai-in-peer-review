- Paper compares "latent" vs "overlapped" structured Schatten norms for convex tensor decomposition.  
- Derives MSE bounds showing latent approach outperforms overlapped when tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.  
- Establishes a duality theorem for structured Schatten norms, generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Numerical simulations confirm theoretical predictions.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and clearly written.  
- Duality result is an interesting and valuable contribution to structured sparsity literature.  
- Significance for NIPS is limited due to narrow, highly technical focus.  
- No experiments or theoretical analysis connecting methods to real ML applications.  
- Techniques appear incremental relative to recent convex tensor decomposition work (Tomioka et al., Agarwal et al.).  
- Absence of matching lower bounds weakens the theoretical contribution.  
- Abstract overstates novelty by claiming a new class of structured Schatten norms without providing algorithms or behavior analysis.  
- Minor issues: Figure 1 placed too early and some typos.  
- Evaluation on four criteria: Quality good, Clarity good, Originality limited, Significance low.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable for tensor-decomposition community but not compelling for NIPS — recommendation to reject.
