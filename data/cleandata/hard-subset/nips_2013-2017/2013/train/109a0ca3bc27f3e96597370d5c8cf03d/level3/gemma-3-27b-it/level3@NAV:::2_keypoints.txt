- Investigates convex relaxation for tensor decomposition using a “latent” structured Schatten-norm approach with theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Core contribution: shows the latent method attains reconstruction accuracy comparable to knowing the tensor’s smallest mode-rank, improving over existing convex methods.
- Presents a duality result for structured Schatten norms as a theoretical contribution.
- Numerical experiments support theoretical claims, showing superior performance of the latent approach in several cases.
- Timely topic with broader applicability beyond the neuroimaging motivation, which is well stated but narrowly framed.
- Introduction establishes context and highlights limitations of prior work, especially rank hyperparameter selection in Tucker decomposition.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and a key connection between the latent approach and minimal mode-rank.
- Strength: duality result for Schatten norms is valuable.
- Weakness: motivation could be strengthened for a machine-learning audience and connected to low-rank matrix factorization concepts.
- Suggestion: add a latent-variable graphical model illustrating decomposition and clarify how observed moment tensors admit Tucker decompositions.
- Technical issue: Theorem 2 needs clarification on permutation invariance of recovered components and justification of the step from equation (12) to (13).
- Missing pieces: proof of Lemma 5 absent; Lemma 4 lacks clear proof or justification beyond citation.
- Related work: expand discussion to include HOSVD variants, approximation bounds, and cite Kolda’s survey.
- Terminology: inconsistent use of “mode-rank” vs. “multilinear rank” — standardize terms.
- Remove or revise the incoherence comparison after (10) as it is misleading.
- Add a brief discussion of computational complexity.
- Notation errors: numerous inconsistencies (superscripts for components, k-mode rank) need correction.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, significant theoretical contribution, well written, and supported by numerical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: requires multiple technical, notational, and motivational revisions; related work needs expansion.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with potential; recommend acceptance contingent on major revisions.
