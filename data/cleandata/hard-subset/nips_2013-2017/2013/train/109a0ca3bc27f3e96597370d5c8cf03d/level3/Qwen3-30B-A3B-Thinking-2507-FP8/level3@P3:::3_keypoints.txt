- Studies convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms, comparing overlapped and latent approaches.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis showing the latent approach outperforms overlapped when the true tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.  
- Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing group sparsity results.  
- Proves the latent approach’s MSE scales with the minimal mode-k rank, unlike the overlapped approach which depends on the average mode-k rank.  
- Connects to prior convex tensor decomposition work and broader structured sparsity literature, and relates to Mu et al.’s lower bounds.  
- Mathematical exposition is clear, well-organized, and supported by comprehensive proofs in the supplement.  
- Experimental evaluation is brief and lacks sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Authors do not explain how Tucker ranks (r1,...,rK) were chosen in experiments.  
- Paper omits comparisons to dominant non-convex tensor methods (e.g., HOSVD, ALS), limiting practical evaluation.  
- Does not analyze group-regularization behavior (e.g., l_{1,∞}) when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank in all modes.  
- Significance is high: provides the first theoretical explanation for the empirical superiority of the latent approach.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — expand experiments, justify rank selection, add comparisons to non-convex methods, and analyze regularization behavior.  
- Pros: strong theoretical contribution, novel duality result, clear exposition; Cons: weak experimental section, missing rank-selection details, no non-convex comparisons, incomplete regularization analysis.
