This paper studies a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, specifically the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, and their application to convex tensor decomposition. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the performance of the latent approach, including a novel duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. The paper is well-organized, and the theoretical analysis is strong.

The main contribution of the paper is the provision of theoretical bounds for convex tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm, which improves on existing results for the overlapped Schatten norm. The authors show that the latent approach performs better than the overlapped approach, especially when the unknown tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The paper also provides a dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which is an interesting and original result of independent interest.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do a good job of explaining the technical details of their approach. The experimental evaluation is brief but provides some evidence for the effectiveness of the latent approach. However, there are some limitations to the experimental evaluation. For example, the authors do not provide sufficient detail on how the Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) were chosen, and there is no comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition approaches.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the authors demonstrate a good understanding of the technical aspects of their approach, and the paper is well-organized. However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved. For example, the experimental evaluation is brief and could be expanded to provide more detail on the performance of the latent approach. Additionally, the authors could provide more analysis of the behavior of the group regularization (e.g., l_{1,∞}) when the tensor is low-rank in all modes.

Overall, the paper addresses an important problem in the field of tensor decomposition, and the authors provide a meaningful improvement on existing results. The paper is well-written, and the theoretical analysis is strong. However, there are some limitations to the experimental evaluation, and the authors could provide more detail on the performance of the latent approach.
