

This paper investigates structured Schatten norms for convex tensor decomposition, comparing the "overlapped" and "latent" approaches. The authors establish theoretical guarantees showing that the latent approach achieves error scaling with the minimum mode-k rank, rather than the average rank as in the overlapped approach. The work contributes a novel duality between these structured norms and provides recovery guarantees for noisy tensor decomposition.

The paper's main strengths include a technically sound theoretical contribution with clear connections to structured sparsity literature, and empirical validation of the theoretical predictions. The authors successfully demonstrate that the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in a specific mode, which explains empirical observations.

However, several improvements are needed to strengthen the paper. The motivation for ML audiences could be enhanced by better connecting the problem to real-world applications. The paper should add a latent-variable graphical model figure illustrating the hidden→observed mapping and explicitly showing how observed moment tensors admit Tucker decomposition. The multilinear rank should be clearly defined as equal to the dimensions of hidden variables, and the advantage of having only some hidden variables with small dimension should be highlighted.

Several technical clarifications are required: Theorem 2 needs to clarify that components are recovered only up to permutation, and the step from (12) to (13) requires justification. The missing proof of Lemma 5 must be provided, and Lemma 4 should be properly proven rather than merely cited. The paper should add related work on HOSVD and variants, citing Kolda's survey (section 4), and clarify that "mode-rank" is synonymous with multilinear rank.

Notational inconsistencies need resolution (e.g., inconsistent k-mode rank notation), and the confusing superscript notation for components should be corrected. The incoherence discussion after (10) contains misleading comparisons that should be removed. A brief computational complexity analysis would strengthen the paper, and the abstract typo ("structures Schatten" → "structured Schatten") must be fixed.

Overall, the paper presents an important theoretical contribution with clear significance for tensor decomposition. With the suggested revisions addressing technical details and improving accessibility, this work would make a strong contribution to the field. The novelty of providing Tucker decomposition via overlapped Schatten norm with recovery guarantees is well established, pending the requested clarifications.
