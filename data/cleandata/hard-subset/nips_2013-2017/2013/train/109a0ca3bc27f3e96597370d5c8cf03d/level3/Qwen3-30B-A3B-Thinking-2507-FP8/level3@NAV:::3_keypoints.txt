- Introduces a novel framework for structured Schatten norms comparing "overlapped" and "latent" tensor decomposition approaches.  
- Theoretical bounds show the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the true tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.  
- Estimation error scales with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average rank.  
- Key contribution: a duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing structured sparsity (e.g., group lasso).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with clear proofs of consistency and performance bounds.  
- The dual characterization is original and of independent interest to the structured sparsity community.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and effectively connects tensor decomposition to broader sparsity literature.  
- Experimental evaluation is underdeveloped and needs substantial improvement.  
- Authors fail to specify how Tucker ranks (r₁,...,rₖ) were chosen, harming reproducibility.  
- Comparison is limited to the overlapped approach and omits standard non-convex tensor decomposition baselines.  
- Omits analysis of group-regularization behavior (e.g., ℓ_{1,∞}) for tensors low-rank across all modes.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant theoretical contributions and clarity; experimental gaps warrant minor revisions before final acceptance.
