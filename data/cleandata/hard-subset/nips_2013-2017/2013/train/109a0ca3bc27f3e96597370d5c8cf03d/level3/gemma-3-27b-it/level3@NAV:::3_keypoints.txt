- Paper studies convex relaxation for tensor decomposition, focusing on the latent Schatten-norm approach and its theoretical properties.
- Main contribution: theoretical bounds showing the latent approach can outperform the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in a single mode.
- Presents an original and valuable dual characterization of overlapped and latent Schatten norms, connecting to structured sparsity literature.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with key results in Lemma 1 and Theorems 2 and 3 and proofs in the supplement.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and situates itself appropriately within prior tensor and structured sparsity work.
- Experiments confirm theoretical scaling but are brief and lack crucial methodological details.
- Method for selecting Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) is not described, undermining reproducibility and interpretation of results.
- No comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition methods (e.g., alternating least squares), limiting assessment of practical performance.
- Missing analysis of group-regularization behavior (e.g., l_{1,∞}) when the tensor is low-rank in all modes, as suggested by the authors.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel duality result, improvements over existing convex approaches, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak experimental evaluation, missing comparisons to non-convex baselines, and lack of analysis on specific regularization effects.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing experimental details and providing more comprehensive comparisons/analyses.
