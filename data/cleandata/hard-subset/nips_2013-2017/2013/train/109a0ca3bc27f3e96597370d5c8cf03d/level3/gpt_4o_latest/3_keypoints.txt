- Introduces and analyzes structured Schatten norms, with a focus on the latent Schatten norm for tensor decomposition.
- Provides a novel dual characterization of overlapped and latent Schatten norms linking tensor decomposition to structured sparsity.
- Rigorously shows the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the true tensor is low-rank in a specific mode.
- Derives an error bound: latent approach error scales with minimal mode-k rank versus overlapped’s dependence on average rank.
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, leveraging duality and consistency arguments.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, accessible to readers familiar with tensor decomposition and convex optimization.
- Numerical experiments validate theoretical predictions and MSE scaling behavior.
- Experimental analysis is brief and lacks critical details (e.g., Tucker rank selection, role of group regularization).
- No comparisons to non-convex methods and limited real-world dataset evaluation, reducing practical applicability.
- Original contribution fills gaps in theoretical understanding and is well-situated in related work.
- Pros: strong theoretical contribution, novel duality results, improved bounds, clear presentation.
- Cons: insufficient experimental analysis and unanswered practical implementation questions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address experimental shortcomings and add practical insights.
