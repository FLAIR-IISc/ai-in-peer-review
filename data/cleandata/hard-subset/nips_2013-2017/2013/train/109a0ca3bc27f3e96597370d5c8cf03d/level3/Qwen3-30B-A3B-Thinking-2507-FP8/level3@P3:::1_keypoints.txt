- Paper compares overlapped vs latent structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, proves a duality theorem, shows latent MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, and confirms results with simulations.  
- Connects tensor decomposition literature to structured sparsity: latent approach ↔ latent group lasso; overlapped approach ↔ overlapped group lasso.  
- Builds on prior convex tensor completion and structured sparsity work (e.g., Kolda & Bader; Tomioka et al.; Jenatton et al.).  
- Strength: technically sound with rigorous proofs (duality lemma and MSE bounds) and clear, well-motivated theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: Numerical experiments effectively validate theoretical predictions, showing predicted MSE scaling.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and accessible to the target audience.  
- Weakness: Narrow scope and limited relevance to broader machine learning applications; no evaluations on typical ML tasks (e.g., collaborative filtering, image completion).  
- Weakness: Main empirical insight (latent better when low-rank in a specific mode) was already observed previously, so theoretical contribution is incremental.  
- Weakness: No lower bounds provided to complement the upper bounds, weakening algorithmic comparisons.  
- Weakness: Abstract’s claim of proposing a “new class” of structured Schatten norms is misleading given lack of algorithmic development or behavior analysis for the generalization.  
- Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance scores: 4/5, 5/5, 2/5, 2/5 respectively.  
- Pro: Provides rigorous theoretical foundation and a novel duality connecting tensor decomposition to structured sparsity; experiments confirm theory.  
- Con: Incremental contribution with narrow impact, lacking lower bounds and practical ML evaluations; abstract overstates novelty.  
- Recommendation: Reject for NIPS; suitable for a specialized optimization or signal-processing venue.
