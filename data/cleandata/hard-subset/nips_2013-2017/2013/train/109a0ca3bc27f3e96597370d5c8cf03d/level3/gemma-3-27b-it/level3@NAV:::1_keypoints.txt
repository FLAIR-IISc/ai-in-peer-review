- Studies convex optimization for approximate low-rank tensor factorization, comparing a “latent” norm to an “overlap” norm.  
- Main contribution: derives theoretical MSE bounds for the latent norm under noise, showing advantages over the overlap norm.  
- Generalizes both norms and proves a duality theorem.  
- Numerical simulations support the theoretical results.  
- Builds on prior convex relaxation and structured sparsity work; fills a gap in explaining why the latent approach can perform better.  
- Derivation of the duality theorem is a notable theoretical contribution.  
- Paper is generally clear with well-presented definitions and logical flow.  
- Scope is narrow and limits impact for a venue like NIPS.  
- No exploration of realistic ML applications—neither theoretical (e.g., generalization bounds) nor empirical (e.g., image/video experiments).  
- Results feel incremental and techniques lack substantial novelty relative to related literature.  
- Provides upper bounds but no lower bounds, weakening claims of optimality.  
- Claim of a “new class of structured Schatten norms” is misleading since no new algorithms or behavioral analysis are presented.  
- Minor issues: Figure 1 appears before relevant norms are defined and the manuscript contains a few typos.  
- Recommendation: reject, with encouragement to broaden scope and test real-world applications.
