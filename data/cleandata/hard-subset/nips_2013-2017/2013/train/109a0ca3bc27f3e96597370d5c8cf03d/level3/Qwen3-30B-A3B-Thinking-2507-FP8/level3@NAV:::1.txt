

This paper studies convex optimization approaches for tensor decomposition, focusing on the comparison between "latent" and "overlapped" structured Schatten norms. The authors derive theoretical mean squared error bounds for the latent approach under various noise models, demonstrating its superiority over the overlapped approach when the underlying tensor is low-rank in a specific mode. They establish a duality theorem for these structured norms, generalizing previous work on structured sparsity, and confirm their theoretical predictions with numerical simulations.

The paper is technically sound, well-organized, and clearly written, with a clean theoretical analysis supported by experiments. The duality result is an interesting contribution to the structured sparsity literature. However, the significance for NIPS is limited. The work is highly technical and narrow in scope, focusing on a specific aspect of tensor decomposition without connecting to broader machine learning applications. The paper does not examine the approaches in real ML contexts, either theoretically or experimentally, and the techniques appear incremental compared to recent work on convex tensor decomposition (e.g., Tomioka et al., NIPS 2010, 2013; Agarwal et al., NIPS 2010). The absence of matching lower bounds weakens the theoretical contribution, and the abstract misleadingly claims a new class of structured Schatten norms without presenting algorithms or behavior analysis for the generalization. Minor issues include Figure 1 appearing too early and some typos.

In terms of the four criteria: Quality is good (technically sound), Clarity is good (well-written), Originality is limited (incremental to existing tensor decomposition work), and Significance is low (narrow, not compelling for NIPS). The paper's contribution is valuable for the tensor decomposition community but lacks the broader impact expected for NIPS. While the theoretical results are clean, the work doesn't advance the state of the art in a demonstrable way for the broader machine learning community. The main argument for acceptance is the technical quality and duality result, but the main argument against is the limited significance for NIPS. Given the narrow focus and lack of connection to machine learning applications, I recommend rejection.
