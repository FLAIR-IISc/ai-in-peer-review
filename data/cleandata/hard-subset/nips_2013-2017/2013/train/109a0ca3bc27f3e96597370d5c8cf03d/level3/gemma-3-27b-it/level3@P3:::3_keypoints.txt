- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition, comparing and analyzing the overlapped and latent Schatten-norm approaches.  
- Main contribution: theoretical guarantees for the latent approach, showing superior performance when the tensor is low-rank in a single mode.  
- Novel technical result: dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, with broader implications.  
- Theoretical results: latent approach MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank; consistency and deterministic bounds provided.  
- Numerical experiments support theoretical scaling predictions.  
- Strength: strong, rigorous theoretical analysis with well-structured proofs.  
- Strength: novel duality result useful beyond tensor decomposition.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing with consistent notation and definitions.  
- Strength: addresses an important problem and meaningfully improves understanding of convex approaches.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is brief and lacks sufficient detail.  
- Weakness: unclear how Tucker ranks were chosen in experiments, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: no comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition methods (e.g., alternating least squares).  
- Weakness: limited analysis of group regularization effects when tensor is low-rank in all modes.  
- Argument for acceptance: significant theoretical contributions, novel duality, and clear presentation.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited experiments, missing non-convex comparisons, and shallow group-regularization analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with important theoretical contributions; recommended for acceptance despite experimental limitations.  
- Recommendation: expand experiments with rank-selection details, add a comparison to a standard non-convex method, and clarify guidance on selecting the regularization constant λ.
