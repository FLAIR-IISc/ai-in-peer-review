- Introduces structured Schatten norms for tensors, unifying overlapped and latent regularizers, and analyzes them for denoising.  
- Key novelty: duality result that the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm.  
- Uses decomposable-regularizer M-estimation tools to derive sharp error bounds for latent Schatten-1 denoising.  
- Theoretical highlights: consistency bound scaling with sqrt(min n_k).  
- Deterministic bound: reconstruction error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor (not the average).  
- Gaussian-noise bound: dominant term scales with min r_k (or sum of latent ranks under mild incoherence).  
- Results improve on prior overlapped-norm analyses that depended on averages of (sqrt) Tucker ranks.  
- Empirically, synthetic denoising confirms predicted scaling and shows latent typically outperforms overlapped, especially when one mode is low-rank.  
- Theory quality: strong, precise dual characterization and technically sound bounds; spectral-norm constraints are well motivated.  
- Assumptions caveat: incoherence-like condition and choice of α are reasonable but need more practical guidance and sensitivity analysis.  
- Empirics caveat: experiments are brief, synthetic-only, relatively small, and lack important implementation/detail disclosures.  
- Clarity: paper is very clear with consistent notation; suggest adding an intuition paragraph after Lemma 2 and precisely defining λ, α, and rank computations.  
- Originality: duality result is original and generalizes structured-sparsity dualities to tensor low-rank settings.  
- Significance: min-rank dependence is practically valuable and the duality may inform new algorithms/analyses.  
- Experimental weaknesses: missing details on Tucker rank sampling, λ/α selection, ADMM parameters, stopping criteria, runtimes, and convergence behavior.  
- LR complexity issue: computing latent ranks using noise-free ground truth introduces bias; provide alternative proxies from observed data.  
- Missing comparisons: no non-convex baselines (HOOI/Tucker-ALS, CP-ALS) or other convex variants to contextualize performance and runtime.  
- Missing group-norm analysis: lacks study of overlapped S1/∞ or mixed norms when tensor is jointly low-rank across modes.  
- Noise-model limitations: only Gaussian noise considered; robustness to heavy-tailed noise/outliers should be explored.  
- Real-data gap: absence of even a small real-data example (e.g., neuroimaging or video) reduces practical relevance.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong, original theory; clear exposition; experiments validate main scaling claims.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical evaluation, insufficient practical hyperparameter guidance, and missing comparisons/analyses.  
- Questions for authors: how were (r1,…,rK) sampled, how were λ and α chosen and how robust are results, can you compare to HOOI/CP-ALS, and can you assess overlapped S1/∞?  
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on expanded experimental detail, broader comparisons, and sensitivity analyses.
