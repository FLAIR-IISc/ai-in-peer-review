- Studies convex relaxations for tensor decomposition, comparing a “latent” structured Schatten norm to a previously proposed “overlapped” norm; derives MSE guarantees and validates them numerically.
- Proves a duality result between the latent and overlapped Schatten norms (Lemma 1), generalizing structured sparsity results.
- Properly situates the work in recent literature on convex relaxations for matrix/tensor factorization and highlights limitations of the overlapped approach when tensors are low-rank in only one mode.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and clearly connected to related structured sparsity work.
- Numerical experiments confirm the predicted scaling behavior and show the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach.
- Strengths: technical soundness, clear presentation, novel duality result, and convincing numerical validation.
- Primary weakness: narrow scope and limited relevance to broader machine learning applications due to lack of real-world experiments.
- Results feel incremental—the improved performance of the latent approach in single-mode low-rank cases is intuitive and not a major conceptual advance.
- Techniques lack significant novelty beyond formalizing known intuitions.
- Missing lower bounds weakens algorithmic comparisons; tightness of the provided upper bounds is not established.
- Abstract’s claim of proposing a “new class of structured Schatten norms” is misleading—the paper mainly analyzes existing norms rather than introducing new ones or algorithms.
- Minor issues: awkward placement of Figure 1, some typographical errors and stylistic awkwardness.
- Overall assessment: weak accept; technically solid but better suited to a more specialized venue unless expanded with applications, lower bounds, and clearer claims.
