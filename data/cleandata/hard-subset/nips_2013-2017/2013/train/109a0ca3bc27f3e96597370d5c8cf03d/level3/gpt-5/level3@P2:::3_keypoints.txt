- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition with structured Schatten norms, defining overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp/q and favoring the latent approach.  
- Central duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm, generalizing overlapped vs latent group lasso.  
- Main theoretical contribution: analysis of the latent S1/1 estimator with consistency bounds when λ exceeds the overlapped S∞/∞ noise norm.  
- Deterministic and Gaussian-noise error bounds depend on the minimal mode-k rank (or sum of latent ranks under incoherence), not on averages as in prior overlapped analyses.  
- Latent method achieves error scaling with min_k r_k, while overlapped approaches scale with the average of sqrt(r_k).  
- Analysis leverages decomposability techniques and spectral-norm tail bounds and connects to recent tensor recovery lower bounds.  
- Synthetic experiments confirm predicted scaling and show latent’s advantage, especially when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Strength: clear, principled definitions of structured Schatten norms and an elegant duality of independent interest.  
- Strength: technically solid analysis across regimes (consistency, incoherence-based deterministic bounds, Gaussian high-probability bounds).  
- Strength: concrete, interpretable improvement (min-mode-rank dependence) that explains empirical behavior and informs method choice.  
- Strength: well-organized, clearly written, with sound proofs and accessible main ideas.  
- Strength: experiments align with theory and illustrate scaling with problem size and ranks.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is brief and omits critical baselines (no non-convex methods like ALS/HOOI or CP/PARAFAC).  
- Weakness: missing experimental details on Tucker rank choices, selection of λ and α, and lack of sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: no real-data experiments and no tensor completion setups, limiting practical validation.  
- Weakness: discussion of S1/∞ and related norms is incomplete, lacking theory/experiments for tensors low-rank in all modes.  
- Weakness: limited practical guidance on setting α and understanding its impact on error.  
- Quality: technical results appear correct; duality lemma is clean; assumptions standard and constants/dimension dependence are explicit.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear; suggest sketching key proof ideas for main theorems, specifying α in experiments, and giving a practical recipe for λ.  
- Originality: novel dual characterization of overlapped vs latent Schatten norms and new min-rank performance guarantees.  
- Significance: meaningful advancement in convex tensor estimation; duality result likely reusable; theory substantial despite weaker experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong, original theory with clear improvement over prior bounds and a useful duality result; synthetic results corroborate theory.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical scope (missing baselines, real data, methodological detail) and insufficient exploration of alternative norms/regimes.  
- Questions for authors: how were (r1,…,rK) chosen; how were λ and α selected; can you compare to ALS/HOOI and CP and add a real dataset; do bounds favor overlapped S1/∞ when tensor is low-rank across modes?  
- Minor comments: fix reference typos and clarify ADMM solver complexity and runtime vs tensor size.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — strong theoretical contribution but experiments and methodological details need modest extension.
