- Studies convex optimization for approximately low-rank tensor decomposition, comparing a “latent” norm to an “overlap” norm.  
- Derives theoretical MSE upper bounds for the latent norm under several noise models and shows advantages over the overlap norm.  
- Proposes a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for structured Schatten norms.  
- Builds on recent convex relaxation and structured sparsity literature, motivated by empirical observations in prior work.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with careful derivations and consistency/performance bounds for the latent approach.  
- The duality result is novel and connects to broader structured sparsity theory.  
- Numerical simulations confirm the theoretical scaling behavior and support the paper’s claims.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear definitions and explanations.  
- Scope is narrow and the focus on specific norms feels disconnected from mainstream ML applications, limiting impact for NIPS.  
- No experiments or applications on real-world data; evaluation is limited to synthetic settings.  
- Contributions are somewhat incremental, largely building on existing convex optimization and structured sparsity techniques.  
- Provides upper MSE bounds but lacks corresponding lower bounds, weakening the assessment of optimality.  
- Abstract is misleading about introducing a “new class” of norms since no algorithms or behavior analysis for the generalization are provided.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 appears too early and some minor typos/inconsistent terminology exist.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous technical analysis, insightful theoretical results, and a novel duality theorem with potential broader use.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited relevance to broader ML, incremental novelty, missing lower bounds, and misleading presentation.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and valuable within tensor decomposition, but narrow significance and presentation issues lead to a Weak Reject recommendation.
