- Paper addresses a key limitation of existing Poisson graphical models by allowing both positive and negative conditional dependencies for count data.
- Authors propose three novel models: Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM).
- Theoretical contributions are strong: limitations of prior models are analyzed and sound proofs are provided.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the ideas easy to follow.
- Empirical evaluation includes simulation studies and a breast cancer microRNA network case study showing model effectiveness.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and would benefit from more comprehensive experiments.
- Lack of comparisons to other count-data methods (e.g., log-linear models, copula-based approaches) weakens empirical claims.
- Some notation and terminology choices (e.g., using "W" for variable domain) may be unclear to readers.
- The approaches are original and significant for the graphical models community.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but urges addressing experimental breadth and comparative baselines.
