- Paper studies convex norm-regularized tensor decomposition, introducing a unified Sp/q family that contains overlapped and latent Schatten norms.  
- Proves a duality theorem between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms, generalizing known structured-sparsity dualities.  
- Analyzes denoising performance of the latent Schatten-1 estimator, showing latent MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank.  
- Contrasts overlapped norms, whose MSE scales with an average of mode-wise ranks.  
- Provides consistency, deterministic (incoherence-type), and high-probability Gaussian-noise bounds with a rank-independent λ choice.  
- Numerical simulations confirm theoretical scaling and show latent can outperform overlapped even when tensor is low-rank across modes.  
- Relates work to prior convex overlapped Schatten-1 literature and to latent/dirty models in structured sparsity.  
- Duality result formalizes and extends overlapped/latent dualities to the tensor Schatten setting.  
- Connects to lower-bound results by Mu et al. though no matching latent lower bounds are provided.  
- Strength: clear theoretical advance giving sharp upper bounds depending on minimal mode rank.  
- Strength: useful, potentially reusable duality for structured Schatten norms.  
- Strength: clean statements and technically careful proofs.  
- Strength: empirical results align with theoretical predictions.  
- Weakness: narrow, technical contribution with limited demonstrated impact on ML tasks.  
- Weakness: techniques largely adapt existing decomposable-regularizer frameworks; latent formulation is known.  
- Weakness: only upper bounds for latent provided; no matching lower bounds to establish optimality.  
- Weakness: “new class of norms” claim is overstated without algorithmic/empirical exploration of Sp/q beyond S1/1.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to synthetic denoising; no tensor completion or real-data evaluations.  
- Weakness: practical aspects (incoherence constraints, choice/verification of α in (11), tuning λ/σ) are under-specified.  
- Weakness: minor presentation issues (figure placement, typos, phrasing).  
- Quality: technically sound with standard but realistic assumptions; lack of lower bounds weakens optimality claims.  
- Clarity: generally readable; suggests moving Figure 1, clarifying constraints, tempering “new class” language, and providing σ/λ guidance.  
- Originality: moderate—duality generalization and latent analysis are new for tensors, but methods follow established patterns.  
- Significance: moderate for theory/optimization communities; limited practical ML impact without real-data validation.  
- Suggestions: add real-data and tensor-completion experiments, runtime/baseline comparisons, and evaluation of another Sp/q member.  
- Suggestions: provide (partial) lower bounds or references, clarify α/constraint feasibility, and include proof sketches in main text.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clean theory resolving when latent outperforms overlapped, elegant duality, and empirical confirmation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, limited application validation, absence of lower bounds, and overstated generality.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution with clear insights into latent vs overlapped regularization, but narrow scope and limited empirical validation reduce practical significance.
