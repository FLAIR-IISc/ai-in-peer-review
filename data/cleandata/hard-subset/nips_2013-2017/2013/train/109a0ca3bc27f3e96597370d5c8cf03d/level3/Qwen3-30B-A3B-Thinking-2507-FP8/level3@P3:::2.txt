

# Review: Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization

This paper presents a theoretical analysis of two convex optimization approaches for tensor decomposition: the "overlapped" and "latent" Schatten norm methods. The authors establish a duality between these approaches and demonstrate that the latent method outperforms the overlapped method, particularly when the underlying tensor is low-rank in only one specific mode. The key theoretical contribution is showing that the error of the latent approach scales with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average rank as in the overlapped approach, explaining empirical observations.

## Strengths

The paper makes a significant contribution to the growing literature on convex tensor decomposition. The duality result between overlapped and latent norms is novel and connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, with clear connections to related work in matrix completion and tensor decomposition. The numerical experiments confirm the theoretical predictions, showing linear scaling of mean squared error with the predicted complexity measures.

## Key Improvements Needed

**Motivation and Accessibility:** The paper would benefit from a more accessible introduction to tensor decomposition for machine learning audiences. The current motivation focuses on neuroimaging applications but could better connect to broader ML contexts like collaborative filtering and multi-view learning. A brief explanation of why tensor decomposition matters for modern ML problems would strengthen the motivation.

**Latent-Variable Model:** The authors should add a latent-variable graphical model showing:
- Hidden variables h_i → observed variables x_i
- Linear mapping between hidden and observed spaces
- Arbitrary dependence among h_i
- Illustration that observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition

This graphical model would clarify the connection between the latent approach and Tucker decomposition. The multilinear ranks should be explicitly shown as the dimensions of the hidden variables (r_k = dim(h_k)), with a note that only some hidden variables (those corresponding to the smallest ranks) need to have small dimension - this is the key advantage of the paper's result.

**Technical Clarifications:**
- Theorem 2: Clarify that components are recovered only up to permutation
- Theorem 2: Justify the step from (12) to (13) by explicitly noting that the singleton decomposition (W^*(k) = W*, others zero) is allowed, enabling replacement of sum of ranks with min_k r_k
- Provide full proof of Lemma 5 (key to Theorem 2) instead of referencing another paper
- Clarify/proof Lemma 4 instead of only citing another paper
- Correct inconsistent notation for k-mode rank (use mult_rank(tensor) consistently)
- Fix superscript notation for components (use clearer symbols than \hat{\cal W}^{(k)})

**Related Work and Terminology:**
- Add related work on HOSVD and variants, citing Kolda's survey (section 4)
- State that "mode-rank" is also called "multilinear rank" and note that multiple tensor rank notions exist
- Clarify that the incoherence discussion after (10) is misleading compared to [1,3,10] - suggest removing this comparison
- Correct typo "structures Schatten" → "structured Schatten" in abstract
- Clarify computational complexity of proposed methods (mention that ADMM-based solvers require O(K n^3) per iteration)

## Overall Assessment

This is a technically strong paper with clear theoretical contributions to tensor decomposition. The main novelty is showing that the latent approach provides recovery guarantees that depend on the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average, explaining its empirical superiority. The paper is well-written overall but requires several technical clarifications to reach its full potential.

The authors should address the notation inconsistencies, provide missing proofs, and improve the accessibility of the problem statement. With these improvements, this paper would make a significant contribution to the tensor decomposition literature and provide valuable theoretical insights for machine learning practitioners.

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. The paper's technical contribution is significant, and the theoretical results provide important insights into tensor decomposition. The requested clarifications would substantially improve the paper's clarity and impact without requiring major rework. The connection between the latent approach and Tucker decomposition via the graphical model would make the paper more accessible to a wider ML audience.
