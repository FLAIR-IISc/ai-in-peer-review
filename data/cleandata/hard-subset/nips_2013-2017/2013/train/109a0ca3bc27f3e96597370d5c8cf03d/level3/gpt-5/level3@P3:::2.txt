Summary and relation to prior work
This paper introduces a unified class of structured Schatten norms for tensors that subsumes the “overlapped” and “latent” formulations used for convex tensor decomposition, proves a clean duality between them, and analyzes statistical recovery for the latent approach in a denoising setting. The main technical result shows error scaling with the minimum mode rank of the ground-truth tensor, explaining empirical advantages of the latent approach over the overlapped formulation whose error scales with an average of the mode ranks. The work connects to structured sparsity dualities (group lasso with overlaps vs latent group lasso), to prior convex tensor completion/decomposition (e.g., Tomioka et al.; Gandy–Recht–Yamada; Signoretto et al.), and to matrix denoising (Agarwal et al.; Negahban et al.). It is complementary to nonconvex Tucker/HOSVD methods (De Lathauwer et al.) and the survey by Kolda–Bader (SIAM Review 2009, Sec. 4), which report approximation bounds but not the denoising guarantees established here.

Motivation and accessibility for ML audiences
- Please add a simple latent-variable graphical model: hidden factors h1,…,hK with arbitrary dependence p(h1,…,hK); observed views xk = Ak hk + noise (linear maps). The cross-moment tensor T = E[x1 ⊗ … ⊗ xK] (or its empirical version) then has a Tucker decomposition with multilinear rank (r1,…,rK), where rk = dim(hk). This immediately motivates convex tensor regularization and explains why multilinear ranks equal the hidden dimensions. Crucially, only some hidden variables may have small dimension; the latent approach adapts to the smallest mode, which is the main advantage of your guarantees.
- Include a figure of this graphical model and a short paragraph explaining that the observed moment/parameter tensor admits a Tucker factorization under linear observation models, thereby making the problem and your norms accessible beyond the tensor community.

Strengths
- Clear, useful duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms, extending known structured sparsity dualities.
- Sharp statistical denoising guarantees for the latent formulation: errors scale with min mode rank, matching heuristics and experiments.
- Solid empirical confirmation of theory’s scaling predictions; practical ADMM-based solver.

Weaknesses and specific suggestions
Technical clarifications and fixes
- Theorem 2: state explicitly that the recovered decomposition {Ŵ(k)} is identifiable only up to permutation of components (and, in general, may be non-unique).
- Theorem 2: justify carefully how (12) implies (13). Spell out the singleton decomposition argument: choose k⋆ in argmink rk and set W∗(k⋆)=W∗ and W∗(k)=0 otherwise. Then ∑k r̄k ≤ rk⋆, which yields (13). Also align notation so r̄k denotes rank of W∗(k)(k) and rk denotes the mode-k rank of W∗.
- Provide the missing proof of Lemma 5 (it is pivotal to Theorem 2). A short, self-contained proof following the decomposability/dual-certificate approach of Agarwal et al. or Negahban et al. would suffice.
- Lemma 4: include a concise proof (rather than only citing), at least for the case used in your analysis, to keep the paper self-contained.
- Incoherence after (10): the comparison to incoherence in [1,3,10] is misleading because those assumptions are tailored to matrix RPCA or sparse-plus-low-rank; your cross-mode spectral constraints differ in role and scale. Suggest removing that comparison or carefully rephrasing to highlight the distinction.

Related work and terminology
- Add related work on HOSVD/Tucker and its approximation guarantees (De Lathauwer et al.; Kolda & Bader, Sec. 4), and briefly contrast with CP rank and other tensor rank notions. State clearly that “mode-rank” equals “multilinear rank,” and that multiple tensor rank notions exist.
- It would help to mention connections to multi-view latent variable models and spectral methods, which use Tucker-like moment decompositions, to broaden the audience.

Computational complexity
- Provide per-iteration complexity of your ADMM solver: each iteration requires K SVDs of unfolded matrices of size nk × (N/nk), costing roughly ∑k O(min{nk,N/nk}^2 max{nk,N/nk}). Discuss memory/layout costs due to unfoldings, and suggest using partial/randomized SVDs and warm starts. Comment on scalability with K and with unbalanced modes.

Notation and presentation
- Fix the confusing superscripts for components (currently Ŵ with superscripts resembling exponents). Suggest Ŵ_k or Ŵ[mode k] for clarity.
- Unify rank notation: avoid switching between r̄k, r̲k, and rk. For example, use rk(W) for mode-k rank of W, and r̄k for rank of W∗(k)(k), or define a helper like mult_rank_k(·).
- Correct the missing parenthesis in the last line of p.12 of the supplementary.
- Abstract typo: “structures Schatten” → “structured Schatten”.
- Throughout, clarify when norms are over unfoldings versus tensors, and keep the dual norm definition close to where it’s used.

Quality
Technically solid and careful overall. The duality is clean, the deterministic and probabilistic bounds are well-motivated, and experiments substantiate scaling laws. However, key proofs (Lemma 4/5) should be included, and the step from (12)→(13) needs explicit justification.

Clarity
Generally well written. The paper would benefit from the added latent-variable figure, consistent notation, and a brief complexity section. Removing/mending the incoherence comparison will avoid confusion.

Originality
The unifying structured Schatten norm framework and its duality, plus min-mode-rank guarantees for the latent approach, are original and timely relative to contemporaneous convex tensor work.

Significance
High. The results guide practitioners toward the latent approach for noisy tensor problems and clarify when it outperforms overlapped norms. The framework should be cited and built upon.

Pros for acceptance
- Important theoretical advance explaining empirical behavior of convex tensor norms.
- Clear duality and practical algorithm with evidence matching theory.
- Broad applicability; potential impact on multi-view/moment-based ML.

Cons for acceptance
- Missing proofs and some notational inconsistencies.
- Incoherence discussion is potentially misleading as written.
- Lacks an accessible motivating model/figure for a wider ML audience.

Recommendation
Weak accept, contingent on addressing the technical clarifications (proofs, (12)→(13), permutation caveat), adding the motivating latent-variable figure and discussion, unifying notation, and fixing minor issues. The paper’s core contributions are strong and likely to influence subsequent work.

Novelty (reiterated)
The paper shows that a Tucker-style low-multilinear-rank structure can be recovered via structured (overlapped/latent) Schatten norms, and establishes recovery guarantees that adapt to the smallest mode rank for the latent norm—provided the above clarifications are incorporated.
