- Introduces a novel framework for structured Schatten norms and analyzes tensor decomposition via convex optimization.
- Provides a duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms linking to structured sparsity literature.
- Offers theoretical consistency guarantees for the latent approach and improved recovery bounds based on minimal mode-k rank.
- Empirical experiments validate theory: latent approach outperforms overlapped in noisy settings and MSE scaling matches derived bounds.
- Paper needs clearer motivation and broader relevance to machine learning, including a graphical latent-variable model for clarity.
- Theorem 2 is incomplete (components recovered only up to permutation) and requires clarification.
- The derivation from equations (12) to (13) is unjustified and needs explanation.
- Proofs for Lemma 4 and Lemma 5 are missing or insufficiently detailed.
- Related-work discussion is limited; should incorporate HOSVD literature (e.g., Kolda’s review) and other tensor-decomposition references.
- Computational complexity of the proposed methods is not discussed, which is critical for practical use.
- Comparison of incoherence conditions with prior works ([1,3,10]) is inaccurate and should be removed or corrected.
- Notational inconsistencies and typographical errors (e.g., "structures Schatten") must be fixed.
- Minor formatting errors in supplementary material (missing parentheses) need correction.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on major revisions to address technical gaps, clarity, and minor issues.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical contributions, rigorous analysis, and strong empirical validation showing clear improvements.
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved technical proofs/incompleteness, limited related-work and complexity discussion, and numerous minor errors.
