- Addresses convex tensor decomposition and provides the first rigorous theoretical analysis of the "latent" structured Schatten norm approach versus the "overlapped" approach.

- Proposes decomposing a tensor into K component tensors with mode-specific low-rank penalties, contrasted with penalizing all unfoldings simultaneously.

- Key theoretical contribution: latent approach mean squared error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor, while the overlapped approach scales with the average/sum-related measure, explaining empirical advantages.

- Introduces a general structured Schatten Sp/q norm framework and proves a novel duality theorem between overlapped and latent norm families.

- Strength: Significant and novel—provides rigorous guarantees (Theorem 3, inequality 15) explaining when latent is preferable.

- Strength: Strong technical quality—sound analysis and a generalized duality result extending structured sparsity theory to tensors.

- Strength: Clear and well-organized exposition with good connections to structured sparsity and group lasso literature.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited and only compares to the overlapped convex approach, lacking comparisons to popular non-convex baselines (e.g., HOSVD, CP-ALS).

- Weakness: Missing experimental details necessary for full reproducibility, such as how multilinear ranks (r1,r2,r3) were chosen or sampled.

- Weakness: Scope of analysis is limited—focuses mainly on the S1/1 norm and lacks a theoretical characterization for cases where the overlapped approach may be preferable.

- Argument for acceptance: Provides the first rigorous performance guarantees for latent Schatten norm tensor decomposition, resolving a prior theoretical gap.

- Argument for acceptance: Main result (error ~ min_k r_k) is a strong, important finding with practical implications.

- Argument for acceptance: The duality result between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms is a significant theoretical contribution of independent interest.

- Argument against acceptance: Experimental work is insufficiently comprehensive and omits standard non-convex baselines and key reproducibility details.

- Argument against acceptance: Theoretical analysis could be expanded to more fully characterize regimes where overlapped methods outperform latent ones.
