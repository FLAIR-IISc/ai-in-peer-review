- Paper provides theoretical analysis showing the latent Schatten-norm approach outperforms the overlapped approach, especially when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Establishes a duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms.  
- Proves consistency and denoising guarantees for the latent approach.  
- Strong theoretical justification bridging empirical performance and rigorous analysis.  
- Novel duality result potentially useful beyond this work.  
- Clear exposition and helpful connection to structured sparsity literature.  
- Numerical experiments validate the theoretical scaling behavior.  
- Significant contribution given importance of tensor decomposition in applications.  
- Introduction lacks a broader ML-oriented motivation and accessible example use case.  
- Paper should include a latent-variable graphical model to clarify assumptions and mapping h_i → x_i.  
- Terminology: explicitly equate "mode-rank" with "multilinear rank" and acknowledge other tensor rank notions.  
- Theorem 2 needs clarification about permutation recovery and the jump from (12) to (13) using singleton decomposition.  
- Missing proofs (Lemma 5, Lemma 4) are crucial and must be included.  
- Related work section should cover HOSVD variants and Kolda’s survey with approximation bounds.  
- The incoherence comparison after (10) is misleading and should be removed or revised.  
- Notation is confusing (e.g., superscript Ŵ^(k), inconsistent symbols for k-mode rank) and should be standardized.  
- Minor errors: missing parenthesis in supplement and typo "structures Schatten" → "structured Schatten."  
- Paper omits discussion of computational complexity; add at least qualitative cost analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, clear writing, rigorous analysis, numerical support, practical significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: technical clarifications needed, missing proofs, limited complexity discussion, and weaker ML motivation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending major revisions to address proofs, notation, clarity, and complexity analysis; novelty is significant but contingent on fixes.
