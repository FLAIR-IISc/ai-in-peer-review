- Novel framework for structured Schatten norms (overlapped and latent) for convex tensor decomposition.  
- Provides a dual characterization: dual of overlapped Sp/q-norm is latent Sp*/q*-norm (1/p+1/p*=1, 1/q+1/q*=1).  
- Dual result is original and of independent interest; relates to overlapped/latent group sparsity duality.  
- Establishes theoretical bounds for the latent approach showing MSE scales no greater than the minimum mode-k rank.  
- Theoretical bounds improve on existing results for the overlapped approach and explain latent's superior performance in some settings.  
- Paper is well-organized, clear, and contains strong theoretical analysis and a thorough introduction.  
- Experimental evaluation is brief and not sufficiently detailed.  
- No thorough comparison to other approaches, including non-convex tensor decomposition methods.  
- Lack of detailed analysis on how Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) were chosen in experiments.  
- No analysis of group regularization behavior (e.g., l_{1,∞}) when the tensor is low-rank in all modes.  
- Contribution is important and applicable across tensor decomposition applications.  
- Originality: novel framework, dual characterization, and significant theoretical bounds.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem in tensor decomposition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, dual result, theoretical contributions, and problem importance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak experiments, missing comparisons, missing rank-selection and group-regularization analyses.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; confidence: 8/10.
