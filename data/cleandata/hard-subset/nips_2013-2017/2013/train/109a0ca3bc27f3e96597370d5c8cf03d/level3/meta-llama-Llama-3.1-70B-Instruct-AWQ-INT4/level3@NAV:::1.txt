This paper studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the "latent" norm and comparing it to the "overlap" norm. The authors derive theoretical MSE bounds for the latent norm under several noise models, showing favorable comparison to overlap norm analyses. They confirm these theoretical results with numerical simulations. The paper also proposes a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for it.

The paper is generally easy to follow and appears technically sound. The results may be useful for researchers in optimization for tensor decompositions. However, the main concern is that the results are narrow, technical, and only peripherally related to machine learning, limiting their potential NIPS impact. The paper does not examine the approaches in real machine learning contexts either theoretically or experimentally. The results seem incremental and not particularly surprising; the techniques lack novelty. Additionally, upper bounds are provided without matching lower bounds, which weakens algorithmic comparisons.

The abstract wording is misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms since no algorithms or behavior analyses are presented for the generalization. Minor presentation issues include Figure 1 appearing too early to interpret, and a few typos/phrasing errors are noted.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the latent norm and its comparison to the overlap norm.
* The authors derive theoretical MSE bounds for the latent norm under several noise models.
* The paper confirms these theoretical results with numerical simulations.
* The proposal of a generalization of both norms and the proof of a duality theorem for it are interesting contributions.
