- Investigates convex tensor decomposition using the latent approach modeling a tensor as a sum of mode-specific low-rank components.  
- Major theoretical contribution: error bounds showing latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor.  
- Provides formal explanation for latent's empirical advantage over overlapped approach, whose error depends on the average rank.  
- Novel dual characterization of overlapped and latent Schatten norms, of independent interest and connected to structured sparsity literature.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, technically sound, and advances understanding of convex tensor decomposition.  
- Originality: dual norm result is novel and potentially impactful beyond this application.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and clearly written, making complex arguments accessible.  
- Experimental evaluation is brief and lacks detail, reducing convincingness and reproducibility.  
- Missing specification of how Tucker ranks (r1,r2,r3) were chosen for synthetic data.  
- Empirical comparisons are limited to the overlapped approach; should include standard non-convex tensor decomposition baselines.  
- Would benefit from discussion of other group regularizers (e.g., l_{1,∞}), especially when tensor is low-rank in all modes.  
- Overall: strong and original theoretical contribution that outweighs experimental limitations, but addressing experimental and comparative gaps would strengthen the paper.
