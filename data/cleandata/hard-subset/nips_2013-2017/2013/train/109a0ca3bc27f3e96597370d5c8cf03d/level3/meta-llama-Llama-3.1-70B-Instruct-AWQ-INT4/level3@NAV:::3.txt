This paper proposes a novel framework for convex tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms. The authors provide theoretical bounds for the latent Schatten norm, improving upon existing results for the overlapped Schatten norm. The paper also presents a dual characterization of overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which is an interesting and original result of independent interest.

The theoretical analysis is strong, and the paper is well-organized and clear. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the structured Schatten norms, their properties, and their relationship to existing work on structured sparsity. The paper also includes a detailed analysis of the performance of the latent approach for tensor decomposition, including consistency and deterministic bounds.

However, the experimental evaluation is brief and not sufficiently detailed. The authors do not provide enough information about the experimental setup, including how the Tucker ranks were chosen. Additionally, the paper lacks a comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition approaches, which would be useful for understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method.

Furthermore, the paper does not provide an analysis of group regularization (e.g., l_{1,∞}) behavior when the tensor is low-rank in all modes. This is an important aspect of tensor decomposition, and understanding how the proposed method performs in this setting would be valuable.

Overall, the paper makes an important contribution to the field of tensor decomposition, and the results are likely to be of interest to researchers in this area. However, the paper could be improved with more detailed experimental evaluations and comparisons to existing methods.
