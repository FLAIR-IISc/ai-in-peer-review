- Presents a theoretical analysis of convex tensor decomposition focusing on the “latent” Schatten-norm approach (Tomioka et al., 2013).  
- Proves consistency and denoising performance bounds for the latent approach.  
- Shows latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach, especially when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Introduces a novel duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms with broader implications for structured sparsity.  
- Situates contributions within important application areas (neuroimaging, spatio-temporal data, collaborative filtering) and prior tensor-decomposition work.  
- Strong theoretical analysis: bounds expressed in terms of minimal mode-k rank that explain empirical performance.  
- Duality result generalizes existing structured-sparsity results and is of independent theoretical interest.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation with precise definitions and notation.  
- Significance: addresses theoretical gaps in convex tensor decomposition and provides rigorous backing for the latent approach.  
- Good integration and discussion of related work in tensor decomposition and structured sparsity.  
- Limited experimental evaluation: simulations are brief and lack detail on parameter selection and Tucker-rank determination.  
- No comparison to traditional non-convex tensor methods (e.g., alternating least squares), limiting practical assessment.  
- Incomplete exploration of group-regularization behavior (e.g., l_{1,∞}) when the tensor is low-rank in all modes.  
- Reliance on an incoherence assumption for deterministic bounds (Theorem 2); sensitivity and practicality of this assumption are unclear.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important open problem, provides strong theoretical results, and offers a novel duality; paper is well-written and numerically supported.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental shortcomings, missing non-convex comparisons, and dependence on a possibly restrictive incoherence assumption.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with rigorous theory and clear writing; recommended for acceptance.  
- Recommendation: accept contingent on expanded experimental details (regularization and Tucker-rank selection) and a brief discussion of incoherence assumption limitations.
