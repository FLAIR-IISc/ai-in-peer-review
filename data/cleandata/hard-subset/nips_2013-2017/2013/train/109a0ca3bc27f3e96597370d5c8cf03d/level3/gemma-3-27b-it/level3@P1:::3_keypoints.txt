- Studies convex tensor decomposition, comparing overlapped vs latent Schatten-norm regularization.
- Provides a novel duality result linking the overlapped and latent Schatten norms.
- Theoretically explains why the latent approach outperforms overlapped when the true tensor is low-rank in only a single mode.
- Proves consistency and gives MSE bounds that scale with the minimal mode-k rank, improving on prior work.
- Connects the tensor results to structured sparsity literature and generalizes duality results.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with consistent notation.
- Addresses an important, widely applicable problem (e.g., neuroimaging, collaborative filtering).
- Numerical simulations support theoretical scaling but the experimental section is brief.
- Experimental details (e.g., parameter selection, Tucker ranks) are insufficiently described.
- No empirical comparison to non-convex methods (e.g., alternating least squares).
- Does not explore other group regularization schemes (e.g., l_{1,∞}) for tensors low-rank in all modes.
- Theoretical results assume knowledge of noise level/parameters; sensitivity to these assumptions is not discussed.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical contribution resolving an open question, novel duality, clear exposition, meaningful improvement.
- Arguments for rejection: limited experiments and missing comparisons to non-convex and alternative regularizations.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper; recommend accept with minor revision to expand experiments, compare to non-convex methods, and clarify parameter selection.
