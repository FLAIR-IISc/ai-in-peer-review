

# Review for "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

## Summary
This paper studies structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, focusing on the comparison between two convex approaches: the "overlapped" and "latent" methods. The authors establish a duality relationship between these two norms, which generalizes results from structured sparsity literature. They provide theoretical analysis showing that the latent approach achieves error bounds proportional to the minimum mode-k rank, while the overlapped approach depends on the average mode-k rank. The paper confirms these theoretical predictions through numerical simulations. This work builds upon recent NIPS papers [9, 15, 23, 25, 26] on convex tensor decomposition and connects to structured sparsity literature [2, 13, 17, 21].

## Quality
The paper presents technically sound theoretical results. The authors establish a duality theorem for structured Schatten norms (Lemma 1) and derive meaningful error bounds (Theorems 2 and 3) that precisely characterize the performance of the latent approach. The numerical experiments in Section 4 successfully confirm the theoretical predictions. However, the paper lacks matching lower bounds for the upper bounds presented, which weakens the theoretical comparison between the two approaches. The authors are careful to acknowledge the limitations of their work, particularly noting that the latent approach's performance is analyzed in the denoising setting rather than tensor completion.

## Clarity
The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the main contributions are well-motivated. The paper would benefit from a clearer explanation of why the latent approach is preferable in the specific setting of the paper (the denoising problem), rather than claiming general superiority. The presentation could be improved by moving Figure 1 to after the explanation of the empirical observation it demonstrates. A few minor typos and phrasing issues (e.g., "low-rank" vs "low rank") are present, but these do not significantly impact readability.

## Originality
The core contribution is the theoretical analysis of the latent approach for tensor decomposition, which explains the empirical observation that it outperforms the overlapped approach in certain settings. While the duality result between the two norms is novel and interesting, the paper does not present a truly new class of structured Schatten norms as suggested in the abstract. The paper generalizes existing norms rather than introducing fundamentally new ones, and the techniques used are largely extensions of existing approaches in structured sparsity. The work is incremental to the recent NIPS literature on convex tensor decomposition, particularly [25, 26].

## Significance
The significance of this work is limited to the specific context of convex tensor decomposition for denoising. The paper does not connect to broader machine learning applications or demonstrate practical utility in real-world machine learning tasks. The theoretical results, while mathematically sound, do not appear to advance the field of machine learning in a meaningful way. The paper lacks discussion of how these results might be applied to actual machine learning problems, such as collaborative filtering or neuroimaging, which were mentioned in the introduction. The results are primarily of interest to researchers working on tensor decomposition algorithms, rather than the broader machine learning community.

## Arguments for and against acceptance

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- Provides a theoretically rigorous explanation for empirical observations in tensor decomposition
- Establishes a novel duality result between structured Schatten norms
- Numerical results confirm theoretical predictions
- Well-written and technically sound
- Contributes to the growing literature on convex tensor decomposition

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- Results are narrow in scope, focusing on a specific denoising setting without broader machine learning context
- Theoretical contribution is incremental rather than groundbreaking
- No lower bounds to match the upper bounds presented
- The claimed "new class of structured Schatten norms" is not fully realized as no novel norms or algorithms are introduced
- Limited significance to the broader machine learning community
- Does not demonstrate practical utility in real machine learning applications

## Overall Assessment
This paper makes a technically sound contribution to the specific area of convex tensor decomposition, providing theoretical justification for the empirical superiority of the latent approach. However, the significance of the work is limited to the tensor decomposition community, and it lacks the broader relevance to machine learning that would make it compelling for NIPS. The results are incremental rather than transformative, and the paper does not sufficiently demonstrate how these insights could be applied to solve real machine learning problems.

The paper would be strengthened by:
1. Providing lower bounds to match the upper bounds presented
2. Discussing practical applications of these results in machine learning contexts
3. Clarifying the relationship between the proposed "generalization" of norms and the actual contributions
4. Moving Figure 1 to a more appropriate position in the paper

Given the conference's focus on machine learning and the paper's limited connection to broader ML applications, I recommend rejection. While the theoretical work is sound, the significance to the field of machine learning is insufficient for acceptance at NIPS.
