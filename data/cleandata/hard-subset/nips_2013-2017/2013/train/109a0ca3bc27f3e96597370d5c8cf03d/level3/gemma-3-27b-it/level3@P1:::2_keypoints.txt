- Introduces structured Schatten norms for tensors, unifying “overlapped” and “latent” norms in a convex decomposition framework.  
- Provides a novel duality result linking the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, relevant to structured sparsity.  
- Theoretically analyzes the latent approach and explains its empirical superiority over the overlapped approach.  
- Shows latent estimator’s MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, matching performance as if the best mode were known.  
- Proves consistency of the latent estimator (Theorem 1) with an explicit Frobenius-norm bound.  
- Derives a deterministic bound under a low-rank mixture model and incoherence condition (Theorem 2).  
- Gives probabilistic bounds for Gaussian noise settings (Theorem 3).  
- Empirical simulations confirm the theoretical scaling of mean squared error and rank-independent regularization behavior.  
- Paper is technically sound, novel, and provides significant theoretical justification for the latent method.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-structured, though some notational inconsistencies and proof clarifications are needed.  
- Related work coverage should be expanded and certain technical details (e.g., Lemma 5 placement) clarified.  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity; addressing this and minor technical issues would strengthen the manuscript.
