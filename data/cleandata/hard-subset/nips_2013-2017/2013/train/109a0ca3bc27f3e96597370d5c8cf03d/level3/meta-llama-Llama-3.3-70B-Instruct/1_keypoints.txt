- Proposes three novel graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) for count data that capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Addresses a key limitation of existing Poisson graphical models, which only model negative dependencies.
- Paper is well-organized and provides a clear motivation and overview of prior limitations.
- Technical derivations appear sound and well-presented.
- Numerical experiments show the proposed models effectively recover network structure from count-valued data.
- Strength: introduces novel models that broaden applicability to real-world count data with mixed dependency signs.
- Strength: thorough analysis of limitations of prior Poisson graphical models and clear motivation for new approaches.
- Strength: empirical demonstration supports the methods’ effectiveness.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparisons to existing methods regarding computational efficiency and scalability.
- Weakness: some figures and tables need improved clarity and readability.
- Weakness: more interpretation of results and discussion of real-world implications would strengthen the paper.
- Meets conference criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance; overall recommendation is to accept with revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
