- Main contribution: first rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent convex tensor-decomposition approach, with MSE bounds showing performance scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor.
- Key theoretical contrast: latent MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, whereas overlapped methods’ errors scale with the sum/average of mode-k ranks, explaining latent’s empirical advantage when only one mode is low-rank.
- Framework contribution: introduces generalized overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms and proves a duality theorem that extends structured-sparsity duality results to tensors.
- Empirical validation: synthetic experiments confirm the theoretical MSE scaling predictions for the latent approach.
- Quality: technically sound and complete, with correct proofs and careful evaluation supporting the theoretical claims.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized; distinction between overlapped and latent norms is clear.
- Minor presentation issues: Figure 1 appears too early (before formal definitions) and the manuscript needs minor proofreading.
- Originality: fills an important gap by analyzing latent methods, but analytical techniques are largely straightforward applications of existing M-estimator/decomposable-regularizer frameworks.
- Scope of novelty: the duality generalization is elegant but mainly extends known vector-based results to tensors; the “new class” claim is overstated since analysis focuses on S1/1.
- Significance concern: results are technical and narrowly focused, likely of interest mainly to tensor-optimization specialists rather than the broader ML community.
- Application gap: no connection to practical ML tasks or experiments on real-world datasets, limiting demonstrated impact.
- Optimality gap: absence of matching lower bounds prevents assessment of whether the latent approach is information-theoretically optimal.
- Venue fit: work may be more appropriate for signal processing or optimization venues than a broad ML conference like NIPS.
- Arguments for acceptance: provides first rigorous explanation for latent method’s empirical gains, is technically solid, and offers an elegant duality result.
- Arguments for rejection: limited ML significance, incremental use of existing techniques, lack of lower bounds, and perceived venue misfit.
