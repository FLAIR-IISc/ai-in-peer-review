- Paper studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the "latent" norm and comparing it to the "overlap" norm.  
- Authors derive theoretical MSE bounds for the latent norm under several noise models, showing favorable comparison to overlap-norm analyses.  
- Numerical simulations confirm the theoretical results.  
- Paper is well-written with a thorough introduction explaining limitations of overlap approaches and benefits of the latent approach.  
- Technical contributions are sound and proofs are provided in the supplementary material.  
- Authors propose a generalization of both norms and prove a duality theorem for that generalization.  
- Main strengths: derivation of theoretical MSE bounds and empirical confirmation via simulations.  
- Results are narrow, highly technical, and only peripherally related to machine learning, limiting potential impact.  
- No examination of the approaches in real ML contexts (theoretically or experimentally).  
- Contributions seem incremental and techniques lack strong novelty.  
- Upper bounds are provided without matching lower bounds, weakening algorithmic comparisons.  
- Abstract wording is misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms because no algorithms or behavior analyses are presented for the generalization.  
- Minor presentation issues: Figure 1 appears too early to interpret, plus a few typos/phrasing errors.  
- Ratings: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 6/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
