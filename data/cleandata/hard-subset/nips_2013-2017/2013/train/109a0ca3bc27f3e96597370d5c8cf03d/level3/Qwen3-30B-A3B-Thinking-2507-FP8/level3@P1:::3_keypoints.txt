- Addresses tensor decomposition by providing rigorous analysis of the latent Schatten norm and linking it to prior convex tensor methods and structured sparsity literature.  
- Proves a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms (Lemma 1), of independent interest to structured sparsity theory.  
- Main theoretical contribution (Theorems 2–3): latent approach MSE scales with the minimal mode-k rank, while overlapped scales with the average of square roots of mode-k ranks.  
- Provides tight error bounds that cleanly separate noise-dependent and rank-dependent terms (Theorem 2).  
- Theoretical development is meticulous, with clear proofs and logical organization; supplementary material contains full proofs.  
- Writing is precise and accessible to the NIPS audience, making contributions easy to follow.  
- Empirical results support the theoretical scaling behavior (Figure 2) but are limited in scope.  
- Experiments use only two tensor sizes and do not specify how Tucker ranks were chosen, harming reproducibility.  
- Lacks comparison to standard non-convex tensor decomposition baselines (e.g., HOSVD, ALS), weakening practical relevance.  
- Does not analyze group regularization behavior when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes, despite theoretical interest.  
- Significance is high: provides the first theoretical justification for latent approach’s empirical success and enriches convex tensor decomposition and structured sparsity.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestion to add brief non-convex comparisons and clarify experimental rank-selection details for the camera-ready version.
