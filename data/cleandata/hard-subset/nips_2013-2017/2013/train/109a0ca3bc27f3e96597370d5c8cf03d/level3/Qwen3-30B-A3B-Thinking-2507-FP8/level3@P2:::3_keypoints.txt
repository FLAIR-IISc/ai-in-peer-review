- Problem: Compares convex tensor decomposition approaches—overlapped (joint low-rank penalty) vs latent (sum of mode-specific low-rank tensors).
- Main theoretical claim: Latent approach outperforms overlapped when the tensor is low-rank in a specific mode, with error scaling by the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average rank.
- Novel duality: Establishes a new duality between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms (Lemma 1).
- Theoretical results: Theorems 1–3 are rigorously proven and provide meaningful performance guarantees for the latent method.
- Key insight (Theorem 3): Latent approach performs as well as if it knew which mode has the smallest rank.
- Connection to literature: Builds on recent convex tensor decomposition work and links methods to structured sparsity (overlapped/latent group lasso).
- Experimental validation: Empirical results confirm theoretical MSE scaling but are limited in scope.
- Missing comparisons: No empirical comparison to popular non-convex tensor methods (e.g., Tucker decomposition, HOSVD).
- Experimental detail lacking: Paper does not specify how Tucker ranks (r1,...,rK) were chosen in simulations.
- Missing analysis: Does not analyze group-regularization behavior (e.g., l_{1,∞}) when the tensor is low-rank across all modes.
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized; notation consistent and supplementary proofs provided.
- Originality: Dual characterization of structured Schatten norms is novel and of independent interest.
- Significance: Theoretically grounded improvement with practical implications; likely influential for tensor-method researchers.
- Strengths: Strong theoretical contributions, novel duality, clear ties to structured sparsity, predictive MSE scaling, well-presented.
- Weaknesses: Limited and narrowly focused experiments, absent non-convex baselines, unclear experimental rank choices, incomplete analysis for all-mode low-rank case.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance based on substantial theoretical contributions, but suggest expanding empirical comparisons and experimental details.
