This paper studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the "latent" norm and comparing it to the "overlap" norm. The authors derive theoretical MSE bounds for the latent norm under several noise models, showing favorable comparison to overlap norm analyses. The results are confirmed with numerical simulations.

The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly presented. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem, discussing the limitations of the overlapped approach and the benefits of the latent approach. The technical contributions of the paper are sound, and the proofs are provided in the supplementary material.

The main strengths of the paper are the derivation of the theoretical MSE bounds and the confirmation of these results with numerical simulations. The authors also propose a generalization of both norms and prove a duality theorem for it. The results may be useful for researchers in optimization for tensor decompositions.

However, the paper has some limitations. The results are narrow, technical, and only peripherally related to machine learning, limiting their potential impact. The paper does not examine the approaches in real machine learning contexts either theoretically or experimentally. The results seem incremental and not particularly surprising; techniques lack novelty. Upper bounds are provided without matching lower bounds, which weakens algorithmic comparisons. The abstract wording is misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms since no algorithms or behavior analyses are presented for the generalization. Minor presentation issues include Figure 1 appearing too early to interpret, and a few typos/phrasing errors are noted.

Overall, the paper is technically sound, but its impact is limited due to its narrow focus and lack of connection to real machine learning problems. The results are incremental and not particularly surprising, and the techniques lack novelty. However, the paper may still be useful for researchers in optimization for tensor decompositions.
