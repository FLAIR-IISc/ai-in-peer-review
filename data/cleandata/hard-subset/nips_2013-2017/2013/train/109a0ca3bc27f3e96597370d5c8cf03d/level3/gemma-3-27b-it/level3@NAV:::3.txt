## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates convex relaxation techniques for tensor decomposition, specifically focusing on the "latent" approach and its theoretical properties. The core contribution lies in establishing theoretical bounds for the latent Schatten norm, demonstrating its advantage over the "overlapped" approach, particularly when the tensor is low-rank in a single mode. A significant and original result is the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which is a valuable contribution in its own right, relating to existing work on structured sparsity. 

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, building a strong theoretical foundation for its claims. The connection to existing literature on structured sparsity (group lasso, etc.) is well-established and appropriately referenced. The authors adeptly build upon prior work in tensor decomposition [5, 14, 26] and related fields, placing their work in a relevant context. The inclusion of a detailed duality result (Lemma 1) is a notable strength, providing a deeper understanding of the underlying mathematical structure. The theoretical analysis, culminating in Theorems 2 and 3, appears rigorous and sound, with proofs relegated to the supplementary material.

However, the experimental evaluation is a key weakness. While the authors confirm the scaling predicted by their theory, the experiments are brief and lack crucial details. Notably, the method for choosing the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) is not described, making it difficult to assess the impact of this parameter on the results. Furthermore, the paper lacks a comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition methods (e.g., alternating least squares [6]), hindering a comprehensive assessment of the proposed convex approach’s performance.  The analysis of the group regularization behavior (e.g., l_{1,∞}) when the tensor is low-rank in all modes, as suggested by the authors themselves, is also missing.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical foundation and rigorous analysis.
*   Original and valuable duality result for structured Schatten norms.
*   Addresses an important problem in tensor decomposition with meaningful improvements over existing convex approaches.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Insufficiently detailed experimental evaluation.
*   Missing comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition methods.
*   Lack of analysis on specific regularization behaviors.



**Overall:** Despite the limitations in the experimental section, this paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. The improvements to existing results and the novel duality result justify acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the concerns regarding the experimental details and providing a more comprehensive comparison to existing methods in a revised version. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
