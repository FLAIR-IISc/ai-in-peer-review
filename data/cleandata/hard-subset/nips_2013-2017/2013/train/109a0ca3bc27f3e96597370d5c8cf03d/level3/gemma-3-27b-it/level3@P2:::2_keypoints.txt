- Presents a theoretical analysis of convex tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, focusing on the latent approach and comparing it to the overlapped approach.
- Main theoretical contribution: shows latent approach performance scales with the minimum mode-k (multilinear) rank, explaining empirical advantages in some scenarios.
- Establishes a duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms.
- Provides numerical experiments that corroborate the theoretical predictions and compare latent vs. overlapped approaches.
- Situates work within convex tensor decomposition and structured sparsity literature, connecting to prior convex relaxations and group-Lasso–style methods.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor with consistency and deterministic reconstruction error bounds.
- Strength: duality result gives valuable mathematical insight into structured Schatten norms.
- Strength: empirical validation reinforces theoretical findings and practical benefit of latent approach.
- Strength: generally clear and well-organized presentation with standard mathematical notation.
- Weakness: introduction is not sufficiently accessible to a broad ML audience; recommend a latent-variable graphical model and explicit link between multilinear ranks and hidden-variable dimensions.
- Weakness: technical clarifications needed—transition from (12) to (13) in Theorem 2 is unclear and proof of Lemma 5 is missing but crucial; Lemma 4 discussion feels incomplete.
- Weakness: related work discussion should better cover HOSVD and approximation bounds (e.g., Kolda 2009) and define "mode-rank" as multilinear rank while acknowledging multiple tensor rank notions.
- Weakness: lacks any discussion of computational complexity of the proposed methods, a significant practical omission.
- Weakness: comparison to incoherence assumptions in cited works is misleading and should be removed.
- Weakness: notational inconsistencies (superscript component notation, multiple symbols for k-mode rank) should be resolved for clarity.
- Arguments for acceptance: fills a significant theoretical gap, strong results backed by experiments, and a valuable duality contribution.
- Arguments against acceptance: requires technical clarifications, a more accessible introduction, broader related-work coverage, and a complexity discussion.
- Overall assessment: technically strong and important paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing technical clarifications and complexity discussion.
