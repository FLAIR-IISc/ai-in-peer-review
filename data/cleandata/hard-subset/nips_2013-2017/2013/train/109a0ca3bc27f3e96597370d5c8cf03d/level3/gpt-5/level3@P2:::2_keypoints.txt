- Paper introduces overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms for tensors and proves a duality between them, generalizing group-sparsity analogues.
- Main theoretical result: latent approach achieves denoising error scaling with the minimum mode-rank when the tensor is low-rank in an unknown single mode.
- Provides consistency guarantees, Gaussian-noise risk bounds, empirical validation matching predicted scaling, and comparison to overlapped-norm bounds that depend on average mode-ranks.
- Relation to prior work is appropriate; suggest clearer positioning relative to HOSVD/Tucker approximation (cite Kolda & Bader) and emphasize that convex latent-Schatten gives explicit statistical rates and a novel duality.
- Suggest adding a simple latent-variable graphical-model motivation and a short tutorial tying multilinear ranks to hidden-block dimensions to improve accessibility.
- Duality lemma is elegant and broadly useful; deterministic and Gaussian-noise bounds are clear and align with experiments.
- Theorem 2 needs clarification: state component identifiability only up to permutation and clarify non-uniqueness of the induced decomposition.
- The transition from componentwise bound (12) to overall bound (13) is non-obvious; authors must rigorously justify the singleton decomposition argument and the min-k rank dependence.
- Lemma 5 is central but its proof is missing; the paper must include it.
- Lemma 4 currently only sketched/cited; provide a self-contained proof or detailed sketch because tensor-specific structure matters.
- The incoherence comparison after (10) may be misleading; either delineate differences to prior incoherence conditions precisely or remove the comparison.
- Algorithmic aspects are under-discussed: include per-iteration complexity, memory scaling (SVDs on mode-k unfoldings), practical sizes handled, and convergence/termination criteria for ADMM.
- Notation issues hinder readability: use clearer symbols for latent components (e.g., U(k)) instead of confusing superscripts like Ŵ(k).
- Make mode-k rank notation consistent (avoid r̄k vs r̲k); explicitly distinguish multilinear ranks of W* from ranks of latent factors in (9).
- State early that “mode-k rank” means Tucker/multilinear rank and note other tensor rank notions (e.g., CP rank).
- Fix minor editorial issues: abstract typo “structures Schatten” → “structured Schatten” and the missing parenthesis in the supplementary material.
- Ensure all theorem statements list required assumptions (on λ, α, dimensional ordering, etc.) inline where used.
- Originality: the overlapped/latent Schatten duality appears new and valuable; latent error bounds adapting to minimal mode-rank in a convex denoising setting are novel.
- Significance: results are practically useful—latent formulation can outperform overlapped when only some modes are low-rank—and the duality may inspire new algorithms.
- Pros: principled analysis adapting to min mode-rank, useful duality, solid empirical validation, and relevance to multiway ML applications.
- Cons: missing/insufficient proofs (Lemma 5, Lemma 4), unjustified step (12)→(13), unaddressed identifiability/permutation issues, potentially misleading incoherence discussion, notation inconsistencies, and limited computational scalability discussion.
- Requested revisions: add latent-variable motivation/tutorial; clarify Theorem 2 identifiability and (12)→(13) derivation; provide proofs for Lemmas 4 and 5; expand HOSVD-related discussion (cite Kolda & Bader); clarify/remove incoherence comparison; unify notation and fix typos; and discuss ADMM complexity and scalability.
- Overall recommendation: strong, timely paper with substantive theoretical and empirical contributions; recommend acceptance pending the requested technical clarifications and exposition improvements.
