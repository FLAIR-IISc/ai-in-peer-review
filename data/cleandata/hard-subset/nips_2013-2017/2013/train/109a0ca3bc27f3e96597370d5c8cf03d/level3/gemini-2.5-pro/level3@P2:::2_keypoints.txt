- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition with structured Schatten norms, comparing overlapped (sum of trace norms of unfoldings) vs latent (sum of mode-specific low-rank components).  
- Main theoretical contribution: latent method's mean-squared error scales with min_k r_k (minimum mode rank) versus overlapped scaling with average of mode ranks.  
- Provides a duality result linking overlapped Sp/q-norm and latent Sp*/q*-norm, of independent interest.  
- Numerical experiments validate the theoretical scaling predictions.  
- Strength: Theorems 2 and 3 showing adaptation to the smallest rank are significant and explain empirical superiority of the latent approach.  
- Strength: The duality lemma elegantly connects this work to structured sparsity literature.  
- Weakness: Paper is not fully self-contained; key technical steps and proofs (notably Lemma 5) are missing from the supplement.  
- Weakness: Transition in Theorem 2 from bounds on components (Eq.12) to bound on their sum (Eq.13) is insufficiently justified; permutation/identifiability of W*(k) needs clarification.  
- Clarity strength: Core idea contrasting sum-of-sqrt(ranks) vs min-of-sqrt(rank) is clearly presented and supported by experiments.  
- Clarity weakness: Motivation for low multilinear-rank tensors is weak; a simple latent-variable generative example would help.  
- Clarity weakness: Notation is confusing (e.g., W^(k) vs W(k); inconsistent r_k notation); suggest consistent operators for multilinear rank.  
- Clarity weakness: Discussion of the incoherence assumption and comparison to matrix completion literature is potentially misleading and should be clarified or removed.  
- Minor issues: Typos include "structures Schatten" → "structured Schatten" and a missing parenthesis in the supplement.  
- Originality strength: First rigorous theoretical analysis showing latent method's superior denoising performance and novel error bounds.  
- Originality weakness: Needs better positioning relative to classical tensor methods (e.g., HOSVD) and clarification of terminology (multilinear rank vs mode-rank).  
- Additional suggestion: Include a brief discussion of computational complexity for solving the proposed optimization problems.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel and significant theoretical result, resolution of open question comparing methods, elegant duality result, and supporting experiments.  
- Rejection/concern arguments: Presentation and motivation need improvement; proofs contain technical gaps (missing lemma, unjustified theorem step); assumptions/related work discussion imprecise.  
- Overall: Technically strong and important paper; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing proof gaps and improving clarity.
