- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the latent norm and comparing it to the overlap norm.
- Derives theoretical MSE bounds for the latent norm under several noise models, showing favorable comparisons to overlap-norm analyses.
- Confirms theoretical results with numerical simulations.
- Proposes a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for that generalization.
- Paper is generally easy to follow and appears technically sound.
- Results are narrow, technical, and only peripherally related to machine learning, limiting potential NIPS impact.
- Does not examine the approaches in real machine learning contexts, either theoretically or experimentally.
- Results appear incremental and techniques lack novelty; findings are not particularly surprising.
- Provides only upper bounds without matching lower bounds, weakening algorithmic comparisons.
- Abstract wording is misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms since no algorithms or behavioral analyses are presented.
- Minor presentation issues: Figure 1 appears too early to interpret, plus some typos and phrasing errors.
- Arguments for acceptance: thorough analysis of the latent norm, derived theoretical MSE bounds, and supporting simulations.
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow ML relevance, lack of real-world evaluation, incremental contributions, and missing lower bounds.
