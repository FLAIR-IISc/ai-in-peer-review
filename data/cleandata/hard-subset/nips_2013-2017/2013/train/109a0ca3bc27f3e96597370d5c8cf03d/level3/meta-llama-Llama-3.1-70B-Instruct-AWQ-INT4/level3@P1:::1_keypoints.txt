- Presents a framework for structured Schatten norms (overlapped and latent) and establishes duality between them.  
- Rigorously analyzes the latent Schatten-1 approach for tensor decomposition and bounds its MSE by the minimum mode-k rank.  
- Theoretical result explains why the latent approach can outperform the overlapped approach in some settings.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with strong explanations of theory and implications.  
- Numerical simulations strongly support the theoretical findings and provide informative comparisons with the overlapped approach.  
- Authors discuss limitations and suggest directions for future work.  
- Results are narrow, technical, and only peripherally related to machine learning, limiting potential impact at NIPS.  
- No examination of methods in real machine learning contexts (theoretically or experimentally), weakening practical relevance.  
- Techniques lack novelty and upper bounds are not matched with lower bounds, reducing strength of algorithmic comparisons.  
- Minor clarity issues: Figure 1 appears too early, plus typos and phrasing errors throughout.  
- Abstract is slightly misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms and should be rephrased.
