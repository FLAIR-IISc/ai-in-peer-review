- Studies convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms, focusing on the "latent" approach.  
- Latent approach regularizes a sum of tensors each low-rank in a specific mode; contrasted with the "overlapped" approach that penalizes all unfoldings.  
- Main theoretical contribution: recovery guarantees for the latent approach showing performance scales with the minimum mode-rank (vs. overlapped depending on average mode-rank).  
- Paper is technically significant, generally well-written; novel insight that the method adapts to the smallest-mode rank and a useful duality result between latent and overlapped norms.  
- Major revision: strengthen motivation and accessibility—add a latent-variable graphical model and figure to show how a linear hidden→observed mapping yields a Tucker decomposition and interprets multilinear ranks.  
- Major revision: fill technical gaps—provide missing proof of Lemma 5, include proof of Lemma 4 (not just citation), justify the step from Eq.(12) to (13), and clarify that components are recovered up to permutation in Theorem 2.  
- Minor revision: revise or remove potentially misleading incoherence discussion after Eq.(10) relative to prior work.  
- Minor revision: expand related work to discuss HOSVD and cite Kolda’s survey; add brief computational complexity discussion and note "mode-rank" = "multilinear rank" and other tensor rank definitions.  
- Minor revision: fix notation and typos—clean confusing superscripts for components, make k-mode rank notation consistent, correct abstract typo ("structures Schatten") and missing parenthesis in the supplement.  
- Overall verdict: important paper with a strong core contribution, but acceptance should be conditional on addressing the theoretical gaps and improving clarity/accessibility.
