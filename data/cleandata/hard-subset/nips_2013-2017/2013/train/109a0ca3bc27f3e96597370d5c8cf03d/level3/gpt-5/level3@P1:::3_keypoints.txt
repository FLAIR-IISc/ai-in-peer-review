- Introduces structured Schatten norms for tensors, unifying overlapped and latent formulations via a duality result.  
- Main conceptual contribution: dual of overlapped Sp/q is latent Sp*/q*, generalizing group-lasso dualities.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for latent tensor denoising using decomposable-regularizer techniques.  
- Establishes consistency under an S∞/∞ noise bound.  
- Gives deterministic error bounds per latent component under an incoherence-type condition controlling cross-mode correlations.  
- Derives probabilistic error bounds for Gaussian noise.  
- Key takeaway: latent approach error scales with the minimum mode rank (min_k r_k), unlike overlapped methods that scale with average mode ranks.  
- Empirical synthetic results confirm linear scaling with proposed complexities and show competitive or superior performance to overlapped methods.  
- Analysis is technically sound; duality lemma and norm inequality are central and well-motivated, and λ does not require rank knowledge.  
- Incoherence assumption is standard but may be conservative; constants and factor F in bounds are not optimized.  
- Paper is clearly written with precise definitions and a coherent narrative, but some empirical and algorithmic details are missing.  
- Originality: the overlapped/latent duality and the oracle-like min-rank scaling are novel contributions.  
- Significance: advances understanding of when latent outperforms overlapped regularization and offers practical guidance for λ selection.  
- Weaknesses: experiments lack details, real-data tests, and non-convex baselines; missing sensitivity analyses and runtime/convergence diagnostics.  
- Suggestions: expand experiments (rank sampling, λ/α tuning, runtime, non-convex baselines, real data), discuss simultaneous low-rank regimes, completion extensions, identifiability, and ADMM complexity/accelerations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theory, sharp guarantees, coherent writing, and empirical alignment with theory.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental evaluation, unexplored regimes where overlapped may be preferable, and potentially conservative assumptions.  
- Overall: strong theoretical paper that leans toward acceptance if empirical section and practical clarifications are strengthened.
