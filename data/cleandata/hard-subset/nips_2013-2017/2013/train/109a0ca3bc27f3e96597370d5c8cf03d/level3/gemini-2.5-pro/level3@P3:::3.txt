This paper addresses the problem of low-rank tensor recovery from noisy observations via convex optimization. This work builds upon recent research that formulates tensor decomposition as a convex problem by regularizing the Schatten norms of the tensor's matrix unfoldings. The authors focus on two such formulations: the "overlapped" approach, which penalizes all unfoldings simultaneously, and the "latent" approach, which models the tensor as a sum of components, each low-rank in a specific mode.

The main contributions are threefold. First, the paper introduces a novel and elegant dual characterization, proving that the "latent Schatten norm" used in the latent approach is the dual of the "overlapped Schatten norm." This result is of independent interest and connects tensor decomposition to the broader literature on structured sparsity and the latent group Lasso. Second, the authors provide the first rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach's denoising performance. They prove an error bound that scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank of the underlying tensor. This contrasts sharply with the bound for the overlapped approach, which depends on the *average* of the mode-k ranks. This theoretical result explains why the latent approach can significantly outperform the overlapped one, particularly when the tensor is low-rank in only one (unknown) mode. Finally, the paper presents numerical simulations that confirm these theoretical scaling predictions.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Quality:**
The paper is of high technical quality. The theoretical analysis is sound, rigorous, and provides a significant advancement over prior work. The proofs are detailed in the supplementary material, and the main results (Theorems 1-3) are clearly stated and well-motivated. Lemma 1, establishing the duality between the overlapped and latent norms, is a particularly strong and original contribution. The paper is well-structured and presents a complete piece of theoretical work.

However, the experimental evaluation, while sufficient to validate the main theoretical claims, is a significant weakness. The experiments are brief and lack the depth expected for a top-tier publication. Several key details and comparisons are missing:
1.  **Experimental Details:** The paper does not specify how the Tucker ranks (r1, r2, r3) were chosen for the synthetic data generation. This makes it difficult to assess the full range of conditions under which the method was tested and hinders reproducibility. A more systematic exploration, for instance, explicitly creating tensors that are low-rank in only one mode versus all modes, would have made the empirical results more compelling.
2.  **Comparison to Baselines:** The experiments are limited to a comparison between the proposed latent approach and the closely related overlapped approach. A comprehensive evaluation should include comparisons to standard, widely-used non-convex methods such as Alternating Least Squares (ALS) or Higher-Order SVD (HOSVD). While convex methods provide valuable theoretical guarantees, their practical performance relative to established (though non-convex) baselines is a crucial piece of information for researchers and practitioners.
3.  **Analysis of Different Scenarios:** The paper correctly identifies that the latent approach excels when the tensor is low-rank in a single mode. However, a deeper analysis of its behavior when the tensor is low-rank in *all* modes (a scenario favorable to the overlapped approach) is warranted. The experiments suggest the latent approach is still competitive in this setting, but a more thorough discussion of this regime, perhaps with theoretical insights, would strengthen the paper.

**Clarity:**
The paper is exceptionally clear and well-written. The authors do an excellent job of defining the problem, introducing the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, and motivating their analysis. The connection to the structured sparsity literature is well-articulated, providing useful context. The organization is logical, progressing from definitions and duality to the main theoretical results and finally to empirical validation.

**Originality:**
The paper is highly original. While building on the framework of convex tensor decomposition, it introduces two key novelties: the formalization and analysis of the latent Schatten norm for tensors, and the duality relationship between the latent and overlapped norms. The theoretical result showing that the latent approach automatically adapts to the lowest-rank mode is a significant and non-obvious finding that distinguishes this work from previous analyses.

**Significance:**
The paper addresses an important and active area of research. Tensor decomposition has broad applications, and the development of convex methods with strong theoretical guarantees is a critical direction. This work makes a meaningful contribution by providing a new convex regularizer (the latent Schatten norm) and proving that it leads to superior theoretical bounds and empirical performance in important scenarios. The finding that the latent approach can perform as well as if it knew the mode with the minimum rank is a powerful result that is likely to influence future research in this area. The duality result also provides a valuable tool for researchers working on structured sparsity more broadly.

### Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions. The novel duality result and the improved performance bounds for the latent approach represent a clear advance in the field of convex tensor decomposition. The paper is well-written and the theoretical claims are rigorously supported. The primary weakness is the limited scope of the experimental evaluation. However, the strength and importance of the theoretical results outweigh this limitation. The paper is a solid contribution to the field and is well-suited for publication.

To further improve the paper, I would recommend that the authors expand the experimental section in a future version to include more details on the experimental setup, a comparison with non-convex baselines, and a more thorough investigation of different low-rank structures.
