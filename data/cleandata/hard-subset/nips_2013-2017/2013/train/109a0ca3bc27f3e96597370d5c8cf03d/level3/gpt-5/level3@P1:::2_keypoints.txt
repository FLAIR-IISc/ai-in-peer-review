- Studies convex tensor decomposition using overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms, proves duality, and analyzes the latent S1/1 regularizer for denoising.
- Core result: latent approach achieves error scaling with the minimum mode-k rank, matching an oracle that knows the best mode.
- Provides non-asymptotic Gaussian-noise bounds and experiments that corroborate the predicted scaling.
- Contrasts with overlapped-norm bounds that scale with average mode ranks, explaining observed empirical performance gaps.
- Extends overlapped/latent duality from group norms to Schatten norms for tensors and situates work among convex and nonconvex tensor methods.
- Calls for fuller discussion of HOSVD/Tucker approximation bounds and clearer positioning relative to robust PCA, dirty models, and matrix/tensor completion literatures.
- Theoretical development is strong: interesting duality lemma, clean consistency bound, and carefully derived deterministic and Gaussian-noise guarantees.
- Several central proof elements are missing or need clarification, notably Lemmas 4–5 and the step from equation (12) to (13).
- The incoherence comparison is imprecise and should be clarified or removed to avoid misleading readers.
- Paper is generally well written but hampered by notational inconsistencies, missing proofs, and limited accessibility/motivation for a broad ML audience.
- Original contribution: novel extension of overlapped/latent duality to structured Schatten norms and formal adaptation to the minimum mode rank.
- Significance: practically useful for multiway data where few modes are low-rank and theoretically relevant for decomposable norms and convex tensor estimators.
- Recommend adding a latent-variable graphical-model motivation to explain Tucker decomposition and the setting where only some latent variables are low-dimensional.
- Theorem 2 needs explicit statements on identifiability (components recoverable only up to permutation/non-uniqueness) and that guarantees refer to the sum and an optimal latent decomposition.
- Require explicit justification of the singleton decomposition argument that turns a sum over component ranks into mink rk.
- Demand inclusion of proofs for Lemma 5 (and a self-contained Lemma 4 or precise mapping to cited results).
- Request clearer rank terminology: explicitly state “mode-rank” = multilinear (Tucker) rank and use consistent notation (e.g., r_k(W)).
- Advise notation cleanup (avoid conflicting superscripts for components, consistent hats/stars, clearer tensor notation).
- Ask for a brief computational-complexity discussion: per-iteration cost (K SVDs of mode-k unfoldings), scaling with K, partial SVDs, and impact of extra spectral-norm constraints.
- Recommend experiments that highlight the mink rk advantage (only-one-mode-low-rank setting), λ-sensitivity plots, and brief study of α’s effect.
- Lists typos/minor fixes (abstract wording, missing parenthesis, consistency for “Schatten” and norm notation).
- Pros: conceptually clean framework, sharp interpretable bounds, empirical validation, and technical relevance to the community.
- Cons: missing/sketchy proofs, confusing notation/exposition, and limited discussion of computational costs and classical HOSVD/Tucker methods.
- Overall: strong and timely contribution; recommend acceptance contingent on providing the requested clarifications, proofs, and improved exposition.
