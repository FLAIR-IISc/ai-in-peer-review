Summary and relation to prior work
This paper introduces and analyzes a class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, unifying two convex tensor regularizers: the overlapped Schatten norm (sum of nuclear norms of mode unfoldings) and the latent Schatten norm (infimal convolution over mode-specific low-rank tensors). The key technical novelty is a dual characterization: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm. Leveraging this duality and tools from high-dimensional M-estimation with decomposable regularizers, the authors establish sharp error bounds for denoising with the latent Schatten-1 norm.

Theoretical results show: (i) a consistency bound scaling with sqrt(min nk); (ii) deterministic bounds where the reconstruction error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor (rather than the average of mode ranks as in prior overlapped-norm analyses); and (iii) Gaussian-noise bounds whose dominant term scales with min rk (or with the sum of latent component ranks under a mild incoherence condition). These results improve on prior work on the overlapped norm (e.g., Tomioka et al. 2011/2012), which yielded dependencies on averages of (sqrt) Tucker ranks. The duality result also cleanly connects to the overlapped/latent group lasso literature (Bach; Jenatton et al.; Obozinski et al.).

Empirically, the paper compares the latent and overlapped approaches on synthetic denoising, showing that MSE follows the predicted scaling laws and that the latent approach typically outperforms, especially when only one mode is low rank. The paper is clearly written and well organized.

Quality
- Theory: Strong and careful. The dual characterization is precise and broadly useful. The bounds are technically sound, with clear conditions and comparisons. The use of spectral-norm constraints to control cross-mode interference is well motivated (though requires tuning).
- Assumptions: The incoherence-like condition and the need to set α in the constrained optimization are reasonable but their practical selection and sensitivity deserve more discussion/experiments.
- Empirics: The experiments confirm scaling and provide a head-to-head with the overlapped norm. However, the empirical section is brief and lacks several important details (see below) and comparisons. The datasets are synthetic only and relatively small.

Clarity
- The paper is very clear, with consistent notation and helpful structure. The connection to structured sparsity is well explained. I recommend adding a short “intuition” paragraph right after Lemma 2 to emphasize why the latent norm’s geometry leads to min-rank dependence (versus average-rank for the overlapped norm).
- Please define exactly how λ and α were set in all experiments, and how the latent factors’ ranks r̄k were computed for the LR complexity metric.

Originality
- The duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is original and of independent interest; it generalizes known dualities in structured sparsity to the tensor low-rank setting.
- The improved error bounds for the latent approach—min-mode-rank rather than average—are a meaningful theoretical advance that explains empirical behavior observed in prior work on the latent formulation.

Significance
- Addressing convex tensor decomposition with sharp, interpretable guarantees is important. The min-rank dependence indicates that the latent approach adapts to the easiest mode without knowing it, which is practically valuable.
- The duality result may inform new algorithms and analyses across structured low-rank and sparsity problems.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Experimental detail is insufficient:
  - Tucker ranks (r1,…,rK): Please specify how these were chosen across trials (ranges, distributions, and whether “simultaneously low-rank” cases were systematically explored).
  - Regularization and constraints: How were λ and α selected in practice? Your theory suggests λ depends on σ and dimensions; did you estimate σ from data? How sensitive are results to mis-specified α and to removing the α-constraints?
  - Optimization details: Report ADMM parameters, stopping criteria, runtime/iterations, and hardware. Include failure modes and any instances of non-convergence.
  - Computation of LR complexity: You run the latent solver on noise-free W* to estimate r̄k. This uses knowledge of the ground truth and a solver in the loop. Please discuss potential bias and provide an alternative proxy that could be computed from observed data.
- Missing comparisons:
  - Non-convex baselines widely used in practice (e.g., HOOI/Tucker-ALS, CP-ALS). Even if these lack guarantees, their empirical performance and runtime are highly relevant. A simple study under matched noise settings would strengthen the case for convex methods.
  - Other convex formulations (e.g., group-regularized variants, or using spectral norm max constraints without latent splitting) could help isolate where the gains come from.
- Missing analysis of group regularization behavior when the tensor is low-rank in all modes:
  - It would be informative to analyze and/or test overlapped S1/∞ or mixed norms that enforce joint low-rank across modes, which might be favorable when all mode ranks are small. A theoretical note comparing latent S1/1 to overlapped S1/∞ under jointly low-rank tensors, or a synthetic sweep where all rk are small and comparable, would complete the picture.
- Scope of noise models:
  - Results are for Gaussian noise; consider robustness to heavy-tailed noise or outliers and whether median-of-means or Huberized losses preserve the min-rank adaptation.
- Real data:
  - Even a small illustrative real-data example (e.g., neuroimaging or video tensor denoising with held-out entries) would increase practical relevance.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong, original theory: dual characterization and min-mode-rank bounds for latent Schatten regularization.
- Clear explanation of why latent outperforms overlapped, aligning with prior empirical observations.
- Well-written and well-organized; builds a useful bridge to structured sparsity.
- Experiments, while brief, do validate the main theoretical scaling claims.

Cons for acceptance
- Experimental section is too limited: missing key details, no non-convex baselines, small synthetic-only study.
- Practical aspects of choosing α and λ are underdeveloped; sensitivity not evaluated.
- No exploration of group-style regularization (e.g., l1,∞) when the tensor is low rank in all modes.

Questions for the authors
- How exactly were (r1,…,rK) sampled for the synthetic tensors? Did you include regimes with all rk small and comparable?
- How were λ and α chosen for each method and tensor size? Are results robust to these choices?
- Could you add comparisons to HOOI/Tucker-ALS and CP-ALS in the same denoising setting?
- Can you provide an experiment or analysis assessing overlapped S1/∞ or related group norms when the tensor is low rank in all modes?

Recommendation
Lean accept. The theoretical contributions and clarity are strong and of broad interest; the main weakness is the brevity of the empirical evaluation. With added experimental detail and broader comparisons, this would be a clear accept.
