- Paper introduces unified structured Schatten norms for tensors, proves duality between overlapped and latent forms, and analyzes latent denoising recovery.  
- Main technical result: latent error scales with the minimum mode rank, explaining its empirical advantage over overlapped norms (which scale with average mode ranks).  
- Connects to structured sparsity dualities, prior convex tensor decomposition/completion work, and matrix denoising literature; complementary to nonconvex Tucker/HOSVD methods.  
- Suggest adding a simple latent-variable graphical model (hidden factors hk, linear observations xk = Ak hk + noise) to motivate multilinear rank and latent regularization.  
- Recommend including a figure of the latent-variable model and a short paragraph linking observed moment tensors to Tucker factorizations.  
- Strength: clear, useful duality extending known structured sparsity results.  
- Strength: sharp statistical denoising guarantees for the latent formulation (min-mode-rank scaling).  
- Strength: empirical results confirm theory and a practical ADMM solver is provided.  
- Theorem 2 should state recovered decomposition is identifiable only up to permutation and may be non-unique.  
- Theorem 2: explicitly justify how (12) implies (13) via the singleton decomposition argument and align rank notation.  
- Provide the missing proof of Lemma 5, using a short decomposability/dual-certificate style proof.  
- Include a concise proof of Lemma 4 (at least the used case) to keep the paper self-contained.  
- Rephrase or remove the comparison to incoherence assumptions in prior RPCA/sparse+low-rank work, since roles and scalings differ.  
- Add related work on HOSVD/Tucker approximation guarantees and contrast mode/multilinear rank with other tensor rank notions.  
- Mention connections to multi-view latent variable models and spectral methods to broaden audience relevance.  
- Provide per-iteration complexity of the ADMM solver (K SVDs of unfoldings), discuss memory/layout costs, suggest partial/randomized SVDs and warm starts, and comment on scalability.  
- Fix confusing superscript notation for components (e.g., use Ŵ_k) and unify rank notation consistently.  
- Correct minor typos and presentation issues (missing parenthesis, "structures Schatten" → "structured Schatten"), and clarify where norms act on unfoldings vs tensors.  
- Overall technically solid and careful, but key proofs and the (12)→(13) step must be included.  
- Paper is generally well written but would benefit from the latent-variable figure, consistent notation, and a brief complexity section.  
- Originality: unifying structured Schatten framework, clean duality, and min-mode-rank guarantees are novel and timely.  
- Significance: high—guides practitioners toward latent approach and is likely to influence future work.  
- Pros for acceptance: important theory, clear duality, practical algorithm, and empirical support.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing proofs, notational inconsistencies, and potentially misleading incoherence discussion.  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on addressing proofs, permutation caveat, adding the motivating figure/discussion, unifying notation, and fixing minor issues.  
- Restated novelty: shows Tucker-style low-multilinear-rank recovery via structured Schatten norms with latent guarantees adapting to the smallest mode rank.
