- Tackles low-rank tensor recovery from noisy observations via convex optimization, comparing overlapped vs latent Schatten-norm formulations.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proves the latent Schatten norm is the dual of the overlapped Schatten norm.  
- Provides first rigorous denoising analysis for the latent approach with an error bound scaling with the minimum mode-k rank.  
- Shows this bound contrasts with the overlapped approach’s bound that depends on the average mode-k rank, explaining latent’s advantage when one mode is low-rank.  
- Numerical simulations corroborate the theoretical scaling predictions.  
- Technical quality is high: rigorous proofs, clear theorems (1–3), and strong original Lemma 1.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and places work in context with structured sparsity literature.  
- Originality is high: introduces and analyzes the latent Schatten norm and the latent/overlapped duality.  
- Significance is strong: advances convex tensor decomposition theory and offers tools likely to influence future work.  
- Primary weakness: experimental evaluation is brief and lacks depth.  
- Missing experimental detail: paper does not specify how Tucker ranks (r1,r2,r3) were chosen for synthetic data, hurting reproducibility.  
- Needs systematic empirical exploration, e.g., tensors low-rank in only one mode vs all modes.  
- Lacks comparisons to standard non-convex baselines (e.g., ALS, HOSVD) to assess practical performance.  
- Requires deeper analysis/discussion of the regime where tensors are low-rank in all modes, since overlapped may be favorable there.  
- Overall assessment: strong, publishable paper with significant theoretical contributions but would benefit from expanded experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: add experimental details, include non-convex baselines, and more thoroughly investigate different low-rank structures.
