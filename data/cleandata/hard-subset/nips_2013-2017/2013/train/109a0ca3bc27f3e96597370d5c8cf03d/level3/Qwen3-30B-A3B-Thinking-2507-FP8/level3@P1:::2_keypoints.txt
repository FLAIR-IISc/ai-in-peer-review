- Addresses tensor decomposition with structured Schatten norms, comparing overlapped vs. latent convex approaches.  
- Main theoretical result: latent approach has better MSE scaling when the true tensor is low-rank in a specific mode (scales with minimum mode-k rank vs. average rank).  
- Establishes a novel duality between the structured Schatten norms, linking tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature.  
- Provides rigorous recovery guarantees (Theorems 1–3) that explain empirical superiority of the latent approach.  
- Numerical experiments confirm theoretical scaling and show latent consistently outperforms overlapped when mode-specific low-rankness holds.  
- Paper is high quality overall with clear relation to prior convex tensor methods and structured sparsity work.  
- Recommendation to improve motivation for a broader ML audience by including a latent-variable graphical model showing how multilinear rank arises from hidden variables.  
- Request to explicitly define multilinear (mode-k) rank and state equivalence to “mode-rank,” noting multiple tensor rank definitions.  
- Theorem 2 needs clarification that components are recovered only up to permutation.  
- Transition from inequality (12) to (13) requires justification due to reliance on arbitrary decompositions (e.g., singleton decomposition).  
- Missing proof of Lemma 5 (critical for Theorem 2) must be provided.  
- Discussion of incoherence after eq.(10) is misleading and should be removed or substantially revised—current comparison to [1,3,10] is incorrect.  
- Notational issues: confusing superscript notation for components (e.g., \hat{W}^{(k)}) and inconsistent use of \bar{r}_k vs. \underline{r}_k; standardize notation (e.g., mult_rank()).  
- Minor fixes: abstract typo (“structures Schatten” → “structured Schatten”) and explicitly note “mode-rank” = “multilinear rank.”  
- Add brief discussion of computational complexity and expand related work on HOSVD/variants and approximation bounds (e.g., Kolda survey).  
- Overall assessment: valuable, significant advance with rigorous justification for latent approach; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing clarifications and notational/technical fixes.
