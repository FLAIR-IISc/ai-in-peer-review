- Studies structured Schatten norms for convex tensor decomposition, comparing overlapped vs latent methods.  
- Establishes a duality between the two norms, generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Theoretically shows latent approach error scales with minimum mode-k rank; overlapped scales with average mode-k rank.  
- Numerical experiments confirm the theoretical predictions.  
- Technical results are sound (Lemma 1, Theorems 2 and 3) but lack matching lower bounds.  
- Analysis is restricted to the denoising setting rather than tensor completion.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation; minor typos/phrasing issues exist.  
- Needs clearer explanation of why latent is preferable specifically for denoising and better placement of Figure 1.  
- Duality result is novel, but the paper does not introduce fundamentally new structured Schatten norms—more a generalization.  
- Contribution is incremental relative to recent NIPS work and mainly of interest to tensor decomposition researchers.  
- Limited significance to the broader ML community and lacks demonstration of practical utility in real ML tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theory, novel duality, experiments confirm theory, well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow scope, incremental, no lower bounds, overclaimed novelty, limited practical impact.  
- Recommendation: reject due to limited ML relevance; suggested improvements include adding lower bounds, discussing applications, clarifying novelty, and moving Figure 1.
