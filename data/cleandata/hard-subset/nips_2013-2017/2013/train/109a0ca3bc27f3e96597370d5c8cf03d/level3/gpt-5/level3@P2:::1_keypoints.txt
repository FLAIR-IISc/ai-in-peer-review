- Summary: Studies convex tensor decomposition with structured Schatten norms, focusing on latent S1/1 (sum of mode-low-rank components) vs overlapped S1/1, formalizing Sp/q norms, proving duality, deriving recovery/denoising guarantees, and showing simulations where latent often outperforms overlapped.
- Relation to prior work: Connects to convex low-rank recovery, decomposable regularizers, structured sparsity/group norms, convex tensor methods, and tensor recovery lower bounds.

Strengths
- Clear theoretical message: latent S1/1 rates depend on min mode rank versus overlapped’s average/sqrt rank.
- Useful duality result linking overlapped/latent Schatten norms to overlapped/latent group sparsity.
- Rigorous analysis: consistency, deterministic incoherence-based bounds, and Gaussian-noise bounds with practical λ.
- Empirical validation: simulations match predicted scaling and show latent advantage.
- Writing and organization: generally clear with careful proofs.

Weaknesses
- Narrow scope and indirect ML impact; no real-data applications demonstrated.
- Incremental novelty: techniques adapt known decomposable-regularizer and structured-sparsity analyses.
- No lower bounds for the latent approach to show tightness.
- Unclear practical relevance of deterministic incoherence/spectral-norm constraints and choice of α in experiments.
- New Sp/q class introduced but analysis/experiments focus only on S1/1; no p,q alternatives explored.
- Minor presentation issues: Figure 1 placed early and some typos/wording problems.

Quality
- Technically sound with correct application of standard proof techniques; informative upper bounds but missing latent lower bounds.

Clarity
- Mostly clear and well-structured; recommend moving figures later, clarifying α and experimental enforcement of constraints, and fixing typos.

Originality
- Moderate: solid duality and adaptation to tensors but builds on known frameworks; main novelty is the specific rates and implications.

Significance
- Moderate for tensor/statistics community; less direct for broader ML audience without real applications.

Questions and suggestions
- Clarify whether spectral-norm constraints in Theorem 2 are enforced in experiments and how α is set.
- Provide practical guidance for selecting λ beyond the Gaussian model and discuss sensitivity.
- Attempt lower bounds (even simplified) or empirical evidence for optimality of min-rank dependence.
- Add at least one real-data experiment (e.g., neuroimaging or recommendation) to show practical impact.
- Include or temper claims about the “new class” of norms by adding a non-1/1 example or empirical note.
- Move Figure 1 after norm definitions and fix minor typos (e.g., “betters” → “better”).

Pros for acceptance
- Clarifies when/why latent norms outperform overlapped norms with clear theoretical advance.
- General duality result of wider interest.
- Solid empirical support matching theory.
- Good fit for convex learning/structured estimation topics.

Cons for acceptance
- Incremental methods and proofs; limited novelty.
- Limited ML impact without real-data results.
- No latent lower bounds; comparison feels one-sided.
- Claims about a “new class” are underdeveloped experimentally/algorithmically.

Recommendation
- Borderline reject: technically sound and clarifying but too narrow and incremental for NIPS without added real-data validation or tighter theory/empirical tightness.
