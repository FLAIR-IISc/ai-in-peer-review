## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates convex optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, specifically focusing on a “latent” norm and its comparison to an “overlap” norm. The core contribution lies in deriving theoretical Mean Squared Error (MSE) bounds for the latent norm under various noise models, demonstrating its advantages over the overlap norm, a result previously observed empirically. The authors further generalize both norms and establish a duality theorem. Numerical simulations validate their theoretical findings.

The paper builds upon recent work in convex relaxation for tensor decomposition (e.g., [9, 15, 23, 25, 26]) and draws connections to structured sparsity literature ([2, 13, 17, 21]). The theoretical analysis, particularly the MSE bounds, represents a solid contribution, filling a gap in understanding *why* the latent approach performs better in certain scenarios. The derivation of the duality theorem is also a welcome addition, potentially opening avenues for future research. The clarity of the paper is generally good, with definitions clearly presented and the logical flow easy to follow. The inclusion of numerical experiments bolstering the theoretical findings is also a strength.

However, the paper suffers from a narrow focus that limits its potential impact at a venue like NIPS. While technically sound, the work remains largely confined to the theoretical analysis of these specific norms and their properties. The authors acknowledge this themselves, but the problem persists. Critically, there is no exploration of these approaches in realistic machine learning contexts - neither theoretically (e.g., analyzing generalization bounds for downstream tasks) nor experimentally (e.g., applying the methods to image/video data). 

The improvements presented feel incremental, and the techniques themselves lack substantial novelty. While the theoretical results are correct, they are not particularly surprising given related work in structured sparsity and low-rank matrix factorization. The paper provides upper bounds but lacks corresponding lower bounds, weakening the claims of optimality or superior performance. Moreover, the abstract’s claim of proposing a “new class of structured Schatten norms” feels misleading, as no new algorithms or behavioral analysis are presented for this generalization – it remains purely theoretical. 

Minor issues include Figure 1 appearing prematurely, before the relevant norms are fully defined, and a few minor typos throughout the manuscript.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Technically sound analysis with rigorous theoretical results.
*   Fills a gap in understanding the performance of the latent norm.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Narrow scope and limited relevance to mainstream machine learning.
*   Incremental results lacking significant novelty.
*   Absence of empirical evaluation in realistic applications.
*   Missing lower bounds.
*   Misleading abstract.



**Overall:** While a solid technical contribution, the paper’s limited scope and lack of connection to broader machine learning challenges make it a weak fit for NIPS. I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to broaden the scope and explore real-world applications.
