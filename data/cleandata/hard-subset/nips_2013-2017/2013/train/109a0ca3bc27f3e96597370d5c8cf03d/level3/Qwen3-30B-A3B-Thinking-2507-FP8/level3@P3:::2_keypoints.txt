- Paper analyzes overlapped vs. latent Schatten-norm convex tensor decomposition, proving a duality and that latent outperforms overlapped when tensor is low-rank in one mode.  
- Key theoretical contribution: latent error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, explaining empirical superiority over overlapped (which scales with average rank).  
- Strength: Novel duality linking tensor decomposition to structured sparsity with rigorous theoretical analysis and experiments matching predictions.  
- Improve motivation and accessibility: broaden application examples beyond neuroimaging (e.g., collaborative filtering, multi-view learning) and briefly explain tensor decomposition relevance to ML.  
- Add a latent-variable graphical model illustrating hidden h_i → observed x_i, linear mapping, arbitrary h_i dependence, and that observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition.  
- Explicitly relate multilinear ranks to hidden-variable dimensions (r_k = dim(h_k)) and note advantage when only some hidden variables have small dimension.  
- Theorem 2: clarify components recovered only up to permutation.  
- Theorem 2: justify step from (12) to (13) by noting singleton decomposition (W^*(k)=W*, others zero) is allowed, enabling min_k r_k replacement.  
- Provide full proofs for Lemma 5 (key to Theorem 2) and for Lemma 4 rather than only citing other work.  
- Fix inconsistent notation for k-mode rank (use mult_rank(tensor) consistently) and improve component superscript notation (avoid \hat{\cal W}^{(k)} ambiguity).  
- Related work: add HOSVD and variants (cite Kolda survey) and clarify that "mode-rank" = "multilinear rank" while acknowledging multiple tensor rank notions.  
- Correct misleading incoherence discussion after (10) versus prior refs [1,3,10] or remove the comparison.  
- Fix typographical error "structures Schatten" → "structured Schatten" in abstract.  
- Clarify computational complexity of methods (note ADMM-based solvers cost O(K n^3) per iteration).  
- Overall assessment: technically strong paper with important theoretical insight; recommend accept with minor revisions addressing notation, missing proofs, and accessibility improvements.
