## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates convex relaxation techniques for tensor decomposition, specifically focusing on the “latent” approach and its theoretical properties compared to the “overlapped” approach. The core idea is to leverage structured Schatten norms to induce low-rank solutions, and the authors demonstrate a theoretical advantage for the latent approach when the tensor is low-rank in only a few modes. The paper builds upon recent work in convex tensor decomposition and connects it to broader concepts in structured sparsity.

The strengths of this paper lie in its rigorous theoretical analysis and clear connection between the proposed method and existing literature. The duality result for structured Schatten norms is a valuable contribution, and the theoretical guarantees for the latent approach – showing it can perform as well as knowing the minimal rank – are significant. The numerical experiments provide supporting evidence for the theoretical findings. The paper is generally well-written and the authors clearly articulate the motivation and contributions.

However, several areas require improvement. The initial motivation could be strengthened for a machine learning audience; while the neuroimaging example is helpful, broadening the scope to more common ML applications (e.g., recommender systems, multi-modal learning) would increase accessibility. A visual representation of the latent-variable graphical model (as hinted at in the introduction) is crucial. Including a figure illustrating *hi* → *xi*, the linear hidden-to-observed mapping, and demonstrating how the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition would greatly enhance understanding.  The authors should explicitly state that “mode-rank” is also known as “multilinear rank” and acknowledge the existence of other tensor rank notions.

Several technical points need addressing. Theorem 2 requires clarification regarding the permutation of components. The step from (12) to (13) needs further justification. Critically, the proof of Lemma 5, central to Theorem 2, is missing and must be included.  Similarly, the proof of Lemma 4 should be provided, rather than just cited. The discussion of incoherence after (10) is misleading and should be removed or significantly revised. Notation inconsistencies (e.g., *r̄k* and *underline{r}k*) should be resolved, and a clearer notation for components *Ŵ^(k)* is needed. Finally, several minor typos and inconsistencies (as noted in the provided feedback) must be corrected.  A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods would also be beneficial.

**Arguments for acceptance:** The paper presents a technically sound and novel contribution to the field of tensor decomposition, offering theoretical guarantees for a promising convex relaxation approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:** The paper requires significant revisions to address technical gaps, improve clarity, and enhance accessibility for a broader audience.



**Overall Assessment:**  I recommend acceptance with major revisions. The core ideas are strong and the theoretical results are valuable, but the paper needs substantial polishing before it is ready for publication. Addressing the points raised above will significantly improve the quality and impact of this work.
