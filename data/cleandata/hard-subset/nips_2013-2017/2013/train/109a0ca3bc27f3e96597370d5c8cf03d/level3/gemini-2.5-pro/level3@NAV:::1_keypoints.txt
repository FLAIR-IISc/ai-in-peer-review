- Central contribution: theoretical analysis comparing latent Schatten norm to overlap Schatten norm for convex norm-regularized approximate low-rank tensor factorization.
- Main theoretical result: MSE upper bounds for the latent norm showing dependence on the minimum mode-k rank, versus the overlap norm’s dependence on the sum of mode-k ranks.
- Practical implication: explains why the latent approach can outperform overlap when a tensor is low-rank in only one mode.
- Empirical support: numerical simulations that confirm the predicted error scalings.
- Generalization: proposal of a family of structured Schatten norms that includes both latent and overlap norms as special cases.
- Novel theory: a new duality theorem for the generalized Schatten-norm family linking to structured sparsity literature.
- Writing and rigor: paper is well-written, technically sound, and derivations appear correct.
- Experiments quality: numerical experiments are well-designed and clearly support the theory.
- Scope limitation: narrow focus with limited connection to machine learning practice and no real-world evaluations.
- Novelty concern: results feel incremental—largely extensions of existing matrix/structured sparsity analyses.
- Theoretical gap: only provides MSE upper bounds without matching lower bounds, so tightness and true advantage of latent norm remain unclear.
- Overclaiming: abstract’s claim of a “new class” of structured Schatten norms is misleading since the class isn’t explored with new algorithms or behavioral analysis.
- Presentation issues: minor clarity problems (e.g., Figure 1 appears before concepts are introduced).
- Overall assessment: technically solid but limited novelty and practical impact, making it a borderline contribution for NIPS.
