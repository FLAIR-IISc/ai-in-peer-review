- Paper studies convex relaxations for low-rank tensor decomposition via "structured Schatten norms", focusing on overlapped (sum of nuclear norms of unfoldings) and latent (sum of mode-specific low-rank components) approaches.  
- Main theoretical contribution: a duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms and a denoising MSE bound showing the latent method scales with min_k r_k (vs. overlapped scaling with average ranks); experiments confirm predictions.

Strengths:
- First rigorous analysis of the latent approach, showing adaptation to the smallest mode rank without prior knowledge.  
- Novel, elegant duality framework linking structured Schatten norms to overlapped vs. latent sparsity literature.  
- Clear main message and well-designed experiments supporting theoretical claims.  
- Potentially high practical impact: latent approach recommended as a default for noisy tensor decomposition.

Weaknesses & suggestions:
- Motivation/accessibility: Add a simple latent-variable generative model and a small figure to make intuition clearer for a broad audience.  
- Theorem 2 / Eq. (13): The step from component error bound (12) to total error (13) is under-justified; provide a detailed argument connecting arbitrary true decompositions to the bound.  
- Missing proof: Lemma 5 is referenced but absent from the supplement; include the proof as it is critical for Theorem 2.  
- Proof of Lemma 4: Currently deferred to another paper; include at least a proof sketch to make the paper more self-contained.  
- Notation confusion: Distinguish component superscripts W^(k) from unfoldings W_(k) (e.g., use W_k and mat_k(W)); standardize mode-k rank notation (r_k vs. \bar{r}_k).  
- Clarify terminology: Explicitly state "mode-rank" = "multilinear rank" and note other tensor rank notions (e.g., CP rank).  
- Related work: Discuss classical non-convex methods (HOSVD and variants) and cite surveys like Kolda & Bader (2009) for context.  
- Computational complexity: Add a brief discussion of the cost of solving the convex programs (e.g., ADMM) for practitioners.  
- Incoherence assumption: Reconsider or better justify the comparison to matrix completion incoherence; state the assumption on its own terms or remove the comparison.  
- Minor editorial issues: fix "structures Schatten" → "structured Schatten" in the abstract, supply missing closing parenthesis in the supplement, and clarify that recovered components W^(k) are identifiable only up to permutation.

Overall recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance conditional on significant revisions: supply missing proofs, clarify the step from Eq. (12) to (13), improve notation and motivation, and address other presentation and technical gaps.
