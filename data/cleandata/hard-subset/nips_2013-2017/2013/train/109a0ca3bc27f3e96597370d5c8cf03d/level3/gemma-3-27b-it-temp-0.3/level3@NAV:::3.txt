## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates convex relaxation approaches to tensor decomposition, specifically focusing on the “latent” Schatten norm and its theoretical properties. The core contribution lies in providing theoretical bounds for the latent approach, demonstrating its performance relative to the previously studied “overlapped” approach, and establishing a novel duality result between the two structured Schatten norms. The work builds upon recent advances in convex tensor decomposition [9, 15, 23, 25, 26] and connects it to broader research on structured sparsity [2, 13, 17, 21]. 

The authors convincingly argue that the latent approach, which decomposes a tensor into a mixture of low-rank components along specific modes, performs better than the overlapped approach, particularly when the tensor is low-rank in only a few modes. This is supported by a rigorous theoretical analysis showing the error bound scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank, a significant improvement over the overlapped approach’s dependency on the average rank. The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a valuable contribution in its own right, potentially useful beyond tensor decomposition. The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and the theoretical analysis appears sound.

However, the experimental evaluation is a significant weakness. While the authors confirm the theoretical scaling behavior, the experiments are brief and lack crucial details. Specifically, the paper does not explain *how* the Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) were chosen for the generated tensors. This is critical for interpreting the results, as the performance is sensitive to rank selection. Furthermore, the paper lacks a comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition methods (e.g., alternating least squares), which remain the dominant approach in practice. Finally, the analysis of the group regularization behavior when the tensor is low-rank in all modes is missing; a deeper dive into this scenario would strengthen the conclusions.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses an important problem in tensor decomposition.
*   Provides meaningful theoretical improvements over existing results.
*   The duality result is a novel and potentially impactful contribution.
*   Strong theoretical analysis and clear presentation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Insufficiently detailed experimental evaluation.
*   Missing comparison to non-convex methods.
*   Lack of analysis of specific regularization behavior.



**Overall:** Despite the weaknesses in the experimental section, the theoretical contributions are significant and the paper is well-written. I recommend acceptance with the caveat that the authors address the experimental shortcomings in a revised version. Specifically, a more detailed description of the experimental setup, a comparison to non-convex methods, and a discussion of the behavior in the fully low-rank case are necessary.
