- Studies convex tensor decomposition with structured Schatten norms and provides a rigorous analysis of the "latent" approach (sum of components, each low-rank in a specific mode).  
- Proves a novel duality between the overlapped Sp/q-norm and the latent Sp*/q*-norm, linking tensor decomposition to structured sparsity.  
- Derives denoising MSE bounds for the latent estimator that scale with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor.  
- Shows theoretically why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped method when the tensor is low-rank in an unknown single mode.  
- Numerical simulations confirm the predicted error-scaling behavior.  
- Strength: technically sound with complete, rigorous proofs connecting the duality to performance bounds.  
- Strength: highly original duality result that provides a unifying perspective and is of independent interest.  
- Strength: significant advance—latent method adapts to the unknown lowest-rank mode and improves over prior convex methods.  
- Strength: paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and well-motivated.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to convex baselines and lack comparisons to standard non-convex methods (e.g., HOSVD, ALS).  
- Weakness: missing experimental details (e.g., how multilinear ranks r1,r2,r3 were chosen), harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: incomplete theoretical discussion of the regime where tensors are simultaneously low-rank across all modes and whether alternate regularizers might be preferable.  
- Overall: strong, original, and likely influential theoretical contribution, but experimental breadth and discussion could be strengthened.
