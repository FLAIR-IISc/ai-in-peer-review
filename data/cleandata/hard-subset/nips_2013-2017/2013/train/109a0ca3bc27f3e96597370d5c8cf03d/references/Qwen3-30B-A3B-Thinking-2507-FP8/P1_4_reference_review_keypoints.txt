- Introduces convex tensor decomposition framework comparing overlapped Schatten-1 norm and latent Schatten-1 norm approaches.  
- Proves a duality (Lemma 1): the dual of the overlapped Schatten norm is the latent Schatten norm.  
- Theorem 3: latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, overlapped scales with the average mode-k rank.  
- Provides theoretical explanation for empirical superiority of the latent approach in noisy tensor decomposition.  
- Connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature, broadening relevance to ML community.  
- Strong technical contribution with novel duality and tight error bounds; proofs are well-constructed.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized with precise definitions and logical development.  
- Original contribution: duality and minimum-rank-based analysis are novel relative to prior tensor work.  
- Significant practical implications: recommends latent approach for many noisy tensor problems.  
- Experiments validate theory but are limited to tensor completion with Gaussian noise and 3-way tensors.  
- Lacks experiments on missing data scenarios and higher-order tensors (K > 3), limiting empirical generality.  
- Could better relate to recent lower-bound work (e.g., [19]) and position results against those bounds.  
- Does not thoroughly address computational complexity or runtime comparisons; ADMM mentioned but not evaluated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, clear explanation of latent advantage, good experiments, and broader connections.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope, incomplete linkage to recent lower bounds, and missing computational analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-supported theoretical paper that advances understanding of convex tensor decomposition.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
