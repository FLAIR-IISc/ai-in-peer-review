- Paper analyzes convex tensor decomposition, comparing overlapped vs. latent Schatten norm regularization and shows latent performance scales with the tensor’s minimum rank.  
- Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, connecting tensor decomposition to structured sparsity.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees explaining why the latent approach excels when a tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Supports theory with numerical experiments that demonstrate the predicted scaling behavior.  
- Well-structured and clearly written, making complex arguments relatively accessible.  
- Main novelty lies in the analysis of the latent approach rather than the convex relaxation framework itself.  
- Deterministic bound (Theorem 2) depends on an incoherence assumption (Eq. 10) that may be hard to verify in practice.  
- Theorems are dense and rely on multiple assumptions; implications could be made more accessible.  
- Numerical experiments are somewhat limited in scope (tensor sizes, noise levels, and structures).  
- Quality rated 8/10 for technical soundness and rigorous proofs.  
- Clarity rated 7/10 due to complex notation and theorem presentation.  
- Originality rated 7/10: builds on known convex relaxation but contributes novel latent-analysis and duality.  
- Significance rated 8/10 for advancing understanding of convex tensor decomposition with potential practical impact.  
- Pros: gives theoretical justification for latent approach, introduces useful duality, and combines rigorous analysis with experiments.  
- Cons: relies on potentially restrictive assumptions, empirical evaluation could be broader, and core setup isn’t fundamentally new.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Requested revisions: clarify limitations/implications of the incoherence assumption, add more intuitive explanations of key theorems, and expand empirical evaluation across sizes, noise levels, and structures.
