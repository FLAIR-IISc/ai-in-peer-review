- Paper studies convex low-rank tensor estimation via overlapped vs. latent structured Schatten norms and proves a duality: the dual of overlapped Sp/q is the latent Sp*/q* (infimal convolution across modes).  
- Statistical analysis shows latent regularization yields error scaling with min_k r_k (minimum mode rank) rather than averaged ranks, explaining empirical advantages; synthetic experiments support the predicted scaling.  
- Theory: the duality lemma and deterministic/probabilistic error bounds are well-developed using standard convex-regularization techniques, clearly contrasting latent and overlapped rates.  
- Main sharp results require an incoherence-style condition and extra spectral-norm constraints on components, raising concerns about practical relevance and applicability.  
- Practical relevance concern: unclear if spectral-norm constraints (eq. 11) were enforced in experiments—cited code appears to solve the unconstrained latent problem; authors should clarify or provide theory for the unconstrained estimator.  
- Tightness concern: regularization λ scales like √(N/(nK)) and with α, which can be large for high-order tensors; bounds may be conservative and could be improved via Gaussian width/descent-cone analyses or mode-adaptive tuning.  
- Experiments: synthetic validation is clean and shows linear scaling, but LR complexity uses a decomposition obtained from the solver (peeking at ground truth), which should be acknowledged; comparisons to nonconvex Tucker/CP and tensor completion and real-data tests are missing.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written; suggest explicitly stating latent S1/1 as an infimal convolution, clearly separating constrained vs. unconstrained estimators, and giving guidance on choosing α.  
- Implementation: provide more details on the ADMM implementation, computational complexity, and scalability with tensor order K and dimensions.  
- Originality: the overlapped/latent Sp/q duality is novel and generalizes structured sparsity dualities; statistical theory for latent norms fills a gap.  
- Significance: the min-rank dependence is an important theoretical and practical insight that could influence convex tensor relaxation design.  
- Pro: clear duality between overlapped and latent tensor norms.  
- Pro: new statistical guarantees for the latent approach with favorable min-rank dependence.  
- Pro: insightful theoretical explanation for empirical advantages over overlapped norms.  
- Pro: clean synthetic experiments validating the predicted scaling.  
- Con: sharp bounds rely on added spectral-norm constraints whose use in experiments is unclear.  
- Con: λ and α dependence may yield conservative rates, with limited discussion on tightness.  
- Con: empirical evaluation limited to synthetic data and uses an oracle-like decomposition for LR complexity axis.  
- Con: limited comparison to strong nonconvex baselines and tensor-completion settings.  
- Questions for authors: did you enforce the spectral-norm constraints in experiments, and if not, can you provide bounds for the unconstrained estimator or justify constraints are inactive?  
- Question: how should α be chosen in practice; is there a data-driven selection method?  
- Question: comment on computational complexity and scalability relative to the overlapped approach.  
- Question: do you expect analogous min-rank dependence in tensor completion under standard sampling models?  
- Minor: clarify Sp/q definition (p on singular values, q across modes), fix typos/citation formatting, ensure venues in references, and consider a toy visual illustrating when overlapped fails and latent succeeds.  
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on clarifying the role/implementation of spectral-norm constraints and ideally adding theory or notes for the unconstrained case.
