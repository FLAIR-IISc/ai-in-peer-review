This paper investigates convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norm regularization, specifically comparing an overlapped approach to a latent approach. The authors provide theoretical analysis establishing consistency and denoising performance bounds for the latent approach, showing its superior scaling with respect to the minimal mode-k rank compared to the overlapped approach. Empirical results corroborate these findings.

The paper builds upon previous work in tensor decomposition [10, 14, 18] and convex relaxation techniques [9, 15, 23, 25, 26]. The core idea – leveraging Schatten norms for low-rank tensor decomposition – is well-established. The novelty lies in the rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach, demonstrating its advantages over the overlapped approach, and establishing a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. The connection to structured sparsity literature [2, 13, 17, 21] is also a valuable contribution.

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a strong theoretical foundation for the latent approach, offering consistency guarantees and performance bounds. The analysis is well-motivated and builds upon existing results in related fields.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The authors clearly articulate the limitations of the overlapped approach and motivate the investigation of the latent approach, especially in scenarios where the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.
*   **Duality Result:** The established duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a significant theoretical contribution, providing deeper insight into the properties of these regularization techniques.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments effectively demonstrate the theoretical findings, showcasing the improved performance of the latent approach, especially when the minimal rank is small. The systematic comparison with the overlapped approach is valuable.
*   **Connections to Related Work:** The authors appropriately situate their work within the existing literature, referencing relevant papers in tensor decomposition, convex optimization, and structured sparsity.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Assumptions:** The theoretical analysis relies on several assumptions (e.g., incoherence assumption in Theorem 2, Gaussian noise in Theorem 3). While not uncommon, the practical implications of these assumptions and their sensitivity could be discussed further.
*   **Complexity of Implementation:** Although the authors mention an available solver, the complexity of implementing and tuning the proposed algorithms (especially for large-scale tensors) is not explicitly addressed.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are focused on specific tensor sizes (50x50x20 and 80x80x40). Expanding the experimental evaluation to a wider range of tensor shapes and ranks would strengthen the empirical validation.
*   **Discussion of Practical Implications:** While the theoretical advantages are clear, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the practical implications of using the latent approach in real-world applications. For example, how does the choice of regularization parameter (lambda) affect performance?

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the field of convex tensor decomposition, providing rigorous analysis of the latent approach and establishing its advantages over existing methods.
*   The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is novel and insightful.
*   The empirical results corroborate the theoretical findings, demonstrating the practical benefits of the latent approach.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical analysis relies on several assumptions that may limit its applicability in real-world scenarios.
*   The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited in scope.
*   The paper does not fully address the practical challenges of implementing and tuning the proposed algorithms.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, the results are insightful, and the empirical validation is convincing. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the concerns regarding the assumptions, experimental scope, and practical implications. Specifically, adding a discussion section that addresses the sensitivity of the results to the assumptions made, and expanding the experimental section to include more varied scenarios would improve the paper significantly.
