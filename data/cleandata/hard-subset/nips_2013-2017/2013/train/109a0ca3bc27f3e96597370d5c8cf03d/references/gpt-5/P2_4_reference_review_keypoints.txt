- Paper studies convex low-rank tensor estimation using overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms and compares their performance for denoising.
- Main theoretical result: dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm, extending overlapped/latent group sparsity dualities to tensors.
- Authors provide statistical analysis of the latent approach: consistency bound, deterministic bound under incoherence-like constraints, and high-probability Gaussian-noise bounds.
- Key technical advantage: latent estimator error scales with the minimum mode-k rank (min_k rk) rather than averages of mode ranks, explaining better adaptation to favorable modes.
- Duality lemma and derived inequalities (e.g., Lemma 2) are technically sound and align with known sparsity-norm dualities.
- The deterministic analysis relies on additional spectral norm constraints ∥W(l)(k)∥S∞ ≤ α to control inter-component correlations.
- Practical enforceability and choice of the α constraints are not discussed, creating a potential theory–practice gap if experiments did not enforce them.
- Gaussian-noise bounds and λ tuning are standard but may be conservative in practice.
- Synthetic experiments validate predicted scaling laws and often show latent outperforming overlapped norms, even for tensors low-rank across modes.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to synthetic denoising; no tensor completion, no real-data experiments, no runtime or convergence comparisons.
- Computing LR complexity by running the latent method on noiseless W* is somewhat circular as a validation measure.
- Notational ambiguity: overlapped and latent S1/1 are denoted identically, which can confuse readers.
- Algorithmic and computational details are delegated to prior work; complexity and implementation choices are insufficiently summarized for reproducibility.
- Originality: clean, nontrivial extension of structured Schatten norm duality to tensors and a meaningful theoretical advance pinpointing min_k rk as key complexity.
- Significance: theoretical insights guide regularizer choice and can influence convex tensor methods, but lack of real-data/completion results limits immediate practical impact.
- Strengths: clear duality result, favorable error bounds for latent approach, consistency and Gaussian rates, and experiments aligned with theory.
- Weaknesses: reliance on α-constraints without practical discussion, limited experimental scope and comparisons, notational clarity issues, and missing scalability analysis.
- Suggested improvements: clarify whether α-constraints were used and how α is chosen, add completion and real-data experiments, include runtime and convergence metrics, compare to nonconvex and tubal-norm baselines, and disambiguate notation.
- Overall assessment: technically solid, original, and compelling explanation for latent norm benefits; clarity and empirical scope could be improved, reviewer leans toward acceptance.
