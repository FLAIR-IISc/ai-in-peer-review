

# Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

## Summary
This paper introduces a novel theoretical framework for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, specifically comparing the overlapped and latent approaches. The authors establish a duality between the overlapped Schatten norm (previously used in convex tensor decomposition) and the latent Schatten norm (proposed in [25]). They rigorously analyze the theoretical performance of the latent approach, showing that its error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor, unlike the overlapped approach which depends on the average mode-k rank. This explains the empirical superiority of the latent approach in cases where the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The work connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature, providing a broader theoretical foundation.

## Strengths

**Theoretical contribution:** The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by establishing the duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which generalizes results from structured sparsity literature. This duality is a key insight that wasn't previously recognized in tensor decomposition.

**Rigorous analysis:** The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis including consistency results, deterministic bounds, and Gaussian noise analysis. The proofs are technically sound and well-motivated, with clear connections to previous work like [26].

**Empirical validation:** The numerical experiments effectively confirm the theoretical predictions, showing linear scaling of MSE with the predicted complexity measures. The comparison between overlapped and latent approaches is well-designed and supports the theoretical claims.

**Clarity of core insights:** The paper clearly explains why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in certain scenarios, particularly when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. This addresses an important practical limitation of previous approaches.

## Weaknesses

**Limited discussion of computational aspects:** While the paper mentions using ADMM for optimization, it doesn't address the computational complexity or practical implementation challenges of the latent approach compared to the overlapped approach. This is a significant omission for a paper targeting the NIPS audience.

**Practical implementation guidance:** The paper states that the optimal regularization constant λ is theoretically independent of the tensor's rank, but doesn't provide practical guidance on how to select λ in real-world applications. This would be valuable for practitioners.

**Comparison with recent NIPS work:** The paper cites relevant work but could better contextualize its contributions relative to recent NIPS papers on tensor decomposition (e.g., [19, 26]). A more detailed comparison would strengthen the significance argument.

**Limited scope of experiments:** The experiments focus solely on comparing the two approaches without benchmarking against other state-of-the-art tensor decomposition methods. Including such comparisons would better demonstrate the practical significance of the latent approach.

## Recommendation

This paper makes a substantial theoretical contribution to the field of tensor decomposition with clear practical implications. The duality between the two Schatten norms is a novel and important insight that connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature. The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-supported by empirical results.

While the paper is strong in theoretical contribution and analysis, it would be significantly strengthened by addressing the computational aspects and providing more practical implementation guidance. The connection to recent NIPS work could also be expanded.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Pro acceptance:**
- Novel theoretical framework with duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with clear explanations for empirical observations
- Strong empirical validation of theoretical predictions
- Clear connection to structured sparsity literature
- Addresses a significant limitation in existing tensor decomposition approaches

**Con acceptance:**
- Limited discussion of computational complexity and practical implementation
- Insufficient comparison with other tensor decomposition methods
- Some theoretical results could be made more accessible to a broader audience
- Practical guidance for choosing regularization constant is lacking

## Overall

This is a high-quality theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of convex tensor decomposition. While it has some minor weaknesses in practical implementation discussion and comparative experiments, these are relatively minor given the strength of the theoretical core. The paper makes a clear and significant contribution to the field that would be valuable to both theoreticians and practitioners.

**Decision: Accept**

The paper presents a well-motivated theoretical contribution with strong empirical validation. The duality between the two structured Schatten norms is a significant insight that connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature. The theoretical analysis clearly explains why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in certain scenarios, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

To further strengthen the paper, the authors should address the computational aspects of the latent approach and provide more practical guidance for selecting the regularization parameter. Adding a brief comparison with other tensor decomposition methods would also enhance the paper's impact.
