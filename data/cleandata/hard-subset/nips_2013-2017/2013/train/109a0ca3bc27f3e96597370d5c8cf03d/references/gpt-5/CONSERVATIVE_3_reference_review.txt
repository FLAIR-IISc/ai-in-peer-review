Summary
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor denoising via structured Schatten norms. It contrasts two families: (i) the “overlapped” approach, which penalizes the nuclear norms of all mode unfoldings simultaneously, and (ii) the “latent” approach, which decomposes the tensor as a sum of K tensors, each low-rank in a specific mode. The authors formalize overlapped Sp/q norms and show that their duals are the latent Sp*/q* norms (an infimal convolution over mode-specific Schatten norms), extending known dualities from structured sparsity to the tensor/Schatten setting. They then analyze the latent Schatten-1 regularized estimator for denoising:

- Consistency: A basic bound in Frobenius norm given λ ≥ ||E||S∞/∞.
- Deterministic bound: Under an incoherence-type assumption and spectral norm constraints on cross-mode unfoldings of the latent components, they bound the sum of squared errors of the components by c λ^2 Σ r̄k and, for the aggregate, by c λ^2 mink rk.
- Gaussian noise: With λ chosen from Gaussian spectral norm concentration, they obtain high-probability error bounds that scale with mink rk (up to factors depending on dimensions and the incoherence parameter).

They compare these to prior bounds for the overlapped approach, which depend on averages of mode ranks, arguing this explains the latent approach’s better empirical behavior when only one mode is low-rank. Synthetic experiments (complete data, Gaussian noise) show linear scaling with their complexity proxies and favor latent over overlapped in many settings.

Strengths
- Clear, principled convex formulations and a unifying view of overlapped vs latent norms; the duality result is natural and useful for analysis.
- Theoretical guarantees that articulate when and why the latent approach can outperform overlapped penalties. The dependence on the minimal mode rank provides qualitative insight aligning with reported empirical trends.
- The denoising focus yields clean concentration-driven tuning for λ that does not depend on the (unknown) ranks.
- Experiments visualize predicted scaling behaviors and support the theory, at least on synthetic data.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Practicality of assumptions and constraints: The key deterministic/gaussian bounds rely on additional spectral norm constraints across modes (∥W^(l)(k)∥S∞ ≤ α for l ≠ k) and require a user-specified α. It is unclear how α should be set from data, whether it is enforced in experiments, and how sensitive results are to mis-specification. This undermines the practical applicability of the main bounds.
- Limited empirical validation: Experiments are small-scale, purely synthetic, and focus only on denoising with fully observed data. Many tensor applications involve missing data; the paper explicitly leaves completion to future work. There is no evaluation on real datasets, no ablations on α, and λ is effectively chosen via oracle model selection over a grid rather than a practical procedure (e.g., cross-validation). There is no runtime or scalability discussion.
- Baselines: Comparisons are restricted to the overlapped convex approach. Established nonconvex tensor methods (CP/ALS, Tucker/HOOI) are not evaluated, although they are commonly used and computationally competitive. Given the reviewer’s preference for established methods, stronger comparisons would be needed to justify the latent convex approach in practice.
- Novelty of the duality: The duality between overlapped and latent norms mirrors known results for overlapping group norms and latent group lasso. The tensor/Schatten instantiation is natural but seems a relatively direct extension; the paper should clarify what is technically new beyond adapting existing arguments.
- Bounds and decomposition flexibility: The step converting Σ r̄k to mink rk leverages the freedom to choose a singleton decomposition in analysis. While valid for the norm, it may overstate practical behavior if the true signal is simultaneously low-rank across modes and the method does not identify a singleton decomposition without loss. Some discussion of identifiability and bias toward singleton solutions would improve the narrative.
- Clarity/minor issues: Some notation is heavy; a brief proof sketch of the main theorems in the main text would aid readability. A few reference entries are incomplete. There appears to be a factor/typo in inequality (3) as written.

Quality
Technically careful overall, with standard techniques (decomposability, dual norms, spectral concentration) adapted to the tensor setting. However, the added spectral norm constraints and unknown α are a practical sticking point, and the empirical section does not stress-test these assumptions.

Clarity
Generally well-written and organized. Definitions are precise; the contributions are clearly separated. Please fix minor typos and unify notation for ranks (rk vs r̄k), and clarify whether constraints (10)/(11) are enforced in experiments and how α and λ are selected.

Originality
Moderate. The latent-vs-overlapped perspective and duality are known in structured sparsity; extending to Schatten/tensors is natural. The main novelty lies in the specific statistical bounds for latent tensor denoising and the comparison to prior overlapped bounds.

Significance
Potentially useful theoretically, providing a clearer story for when latent formulations are preferable. Practical impact is limited by the denoising-only focus, lack of real-data evidence, and the need for tuning/constraints that are not straightforward in practice.

Questions for authors
- How should α be set from data? Are constraints (10)/(11) enforced in your experiments? Sensitivity?
- Can you provide results without the cross-mode spectral norm constraints, or show they can be relaxed?
- How do results change under missing data (completion)? Any preliminary evidence?
- Provide comparisons to Tucker/CP with ALS/HOOI and report runtime and scaling.
- How to choose λ without knowledge of σ? Any robust data-driven heuristics?

Pros
- Clear convex framework; useful duality connection.
- Theoretical bounds highlight a favorable min-rank dependence for latent norms.
- Empirical scaling aligns with theory on synthetic data.

Cons
- Strong/inconvenient assumptions (α) and constraints; unclear practical tuning.
- Limited experiments, no real data, no established nonconvex baselines, oracle λ selection.
- Duality and conceptual framing are extensions of known ideas; novelty is incremental.

Recommendation: 5 (borderline). With stronger empirical validation (real datasets, practical tuning, broader baselines) and clarification on constraints/tuning, this could be a solid accept. Confidence: 0.7.
