Paper provides a theoretical analysis of convex tensor decomposition, showing latent Schatten regularization yields error bounds scaling with the tensor’s minimum mode-k rank.  
Authors prove a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing structured sparsity results.  
Theoretical results explain empirical advantages of the latent approach when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
Numerical experiments validate the theoretical scaling predictions and support the paper’s claims.  
Paper is well-written and logically organized, making the theory accessible despite dense notation.  
Strength: strong, rigorous theoretical foundation with a meaningful mean-squared-error bound.  
Strength: novel duality result that is insightful and broadly applicable.  
Strength: comprehensive combination of theory and experiments.  
Weakness: limited novelty in applying convex optimization to tensor decomposition; novelty mainly in analysis.  
Weakness: tighter bounds rely on strong assumptions (e.g., incoherence condition) whose practical validity is unclear.  
Weakness: noise model is simplistic (Gaussian), limiting robustness claims.  
Weakness: experimental scope is limited — needs more complex datasets, varied noise types, and comparisons to recent methods.  
Related-work coverage could be expanded to include more recent tensor decomposition advances beyond convex relaxations.  
Quality: technically sound with plausible proofs and well-supported claims.  
Clarity: generally clear but mathematical notation can be dense and challenging in places.  
Originality: main contributions are the theoretical analysis of the latent approach and the duality result.  
Significance: results are important for researchers choosing regularization strategies and guiding future work.  
Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing assumptions, related work, and limitations/future directions.  
Requested revisions: clarify assumptions for tighter bounds and their practical implications.  
Requested revisions: expand discussion of recent related work in tensor decomposition.  
Requested revisions: add a brief discussion of limitations and potential future research avenues.
