

# Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper addresses the theoretical analysis of two convex approaches to tensor decomposition: the overlapped approach (using overlapped Schatten 1-norm) and the latent approach (using latent Schatten 1-norm). The authors establish a duality between these two approaches, showing that the dual of the overlapped Schatten norm is the latent Schatten norm. They then provide theoretical guarantees for the latent approach, demonstrating that its error scales with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average mode-k rank as in the overlapped approach. The paper also includes numerical experiments confirming the theoretical predictions.

The paper builds upon previous work in convex tensor decomposition [9, 15, 23, 25, 26] and connects it to structured sparsity literature [2, 13, 17, 21], particularly the overlapped and latent group lasso. The connection to structured sparsity is well-motivated and provides a broader context for the tensor decomposition problem.

**Strengths:**

1. **Theoretical Contribution:** The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by establishing the duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms and providing a rigorous analysis of the latent approach's performance. The key insight that the latent approach's error scales with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average is both novel and well-supported by the theory.

2. **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The authors clearly define all necessary concepts, provide intuitive explanations, and present the theoretical results in a logical sequence. The connection to structured sparsity literature is particularly well-explained.

3. **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments effectively confirm the theoretical predictions. The authors demonstrate the linear scaling of MSE with the theoretically predicted complexity measures, providing concrete evidence for their claims.

4. **Broader Impact:** The framework of structured Schatten norms is general enough to potentially apply to other problems beyond tensor decomposition, and the insights about the relationship between the two approaches have practical implications for practitioners.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Contextualization of Contribution:** While the paper connects to structured sparsity literature, it could better clarify how this work differs from and extends previous theoretical results on tensor decomposition, particularly [26] which also analyzes the overlapped approach. The distinction between "convex tensor decomposition" and the specific approaches discussed is not sufficiently clarified.

2. **Comparison with Alternative Methods:** The paper only compares the overlapped and latent approaches, but doesn't benchmark against other tensor decomposition methods (e.g., non-convex approaches like Tucker decomposition or CP decomposition). Including such comparisons would strengthen the significance argument.

3. **Computational Considerations:** The paper focuses on theoretical performance but doesn't discuss the computational complexity of the latent approach versus the overlapped approach. For large-scale problems, computational efficiency is crucial, and this is a significant omission.

4. **Limitations of the Latent Approach:** The paper doesn't adequately address scenarios where the latent approach might be suboptimal (e.g., when the tensor isn't well-approximated by a sum of tensors each low-rank in a specific mode). A more balanced discussion of the approach's limitations would strengthen the paper.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper with significant theoretical contributions that advance our understanding of convex tensor decomposition. The duality between the two approaches and the theoretical analysis of the latent approach's performance are novel and well-executed. The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and supported by appropriate experiments.

The main weaknesses relate to contextualizing the contribution within the broader tensor decomposition literature and providing a more comprehensive comparison with alternative methods. The paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing these limitations.

**Recommendation:** Accept

The paper makes a clear theoretical contribution that advances the field of convex tensor decomposition. The insights provided about the relationship between the overlapped and latent approaches and the performance guarantees for the latent approach are valuable to both theoretical and applied researchers. While the paper could be strengthened by addressing the limitations mentioned above, the core contributions are strong and significant enough to warrant acceptance at NIPS. The work is well-positioned to influence future research in tensor decomposition and structured sparsity.
