Summary
This paper studies convex approaches to tensor decomposition based on structured Schatten norms. It contrasts two families: (i) the overlapped approach, which unfolds a tensor along each mode and enforces simultaneous low-rankness via a sum of Schatten-1 (nuclear) norms [9, 15, 23, 25], and (ii) the latent approach, which decomposes a tensor as a sum of components where each component is low-rank in a specific mode [25]. The authors:

- Define general overlapped Sp/q norms and their latent counterparts for tensors.
- Prove a duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is a latent Sp*/q* norm (generalizing dualities known for overlapped/latent group sparsity [1, 12, 17, 21]).
- Establish bounds for tensor denoising with latent S1/1 regularization. They give a consistency result, a deterministic bound under an incoherence-type constraint on cross-mode spectral norms, and a probabilistic bound for Gaussian noise. A key insight is that the latent approach’s error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, whereas the overlapped approach bound [26] scales with an average of square roots of the mode ranks. This theoretically explains empirical observations where latent regularization performs better when only one mode is low-rank.
- Provide experiments on synthetic denoising to verify predicted scaling laws and compare to the overlapped method, showing improved or at least competitive performance for the latent approach.

Relation to prior work: The paper connects tensor nuclear norm methods [9, 15, 23, 25, 26] with structured sparsity theory [2, 13, 17, 21] and builds on high-dimensional analysis for decomposable regularizers [1, 20] and random matrix tools [8, 28]. It complements [26], which focuses on the overlapped formulation (and primarily completion), by providing theory for the latent formulation in noisy denoising.

Strengths
- Clear conceptual framing of overlapped vs latent norms and their analogy to overlapped vs latent group lasso; the duality result for Sp/q norms on tensors is natural and useful for analysis and optimization.
- Technical results that sharpen understanding of when latent regularization is preferable: Lemma 2 shows latent S1/1 is bounded by sqrt(min rk) times the Frobenius norm; the Gaussian-noise risk bound scales with mink rk, contrasting with the average-rank dependence for the overlapped approach [26].
- The analysis adheres to modern high-dimensional techniques (dual norms, decomposability arguments), and the conditions on the regularization parameter are explicit and do not depend on unknown ranks.
- Experiments reflect the theory by examining scaling with ranks and dimensions and show that latent regularization remains competitive even when all modes are somewhat low-rank.

Weaknesses and limitations
- Practicality of constraints: The deterministic and Gaussian-noise bounds rely on additional cross-mode spectral norm constraints (equation (11)), controlled by α. The paper does not clearly explain how α is chosen in practice or whether these constraints are enforced in the experiments. If not enforced, there is a mismatch between the theoretical setting and empirical validation.
- Scope of experiments: Synthetic denoising with fully observed tensors is limited. The paper does not evaluate on tensor completion or real datasets, where convex tensor methods are often applied; prior work [25, 26] addressed completion, and a comparison in that setting would improve significance.
- Novelty of duality: While the duality for tensor Sp/q norms is a useful generalization, it closely parallels known overlapped/latent group norm dualities; the conceptual novelty is moderate.
- Tightness and optimality: The paper compares latent upper bounds to an existing overlapped upper bound [26] but does not provide lower bounds for the latent approach or discuss optimality of the dependence on mink rk. Citing [19], the discussion suggests consistency with a mink rk dependence, but a direct lower bound in the denoising setting would strengthen significance.
- Clarity on computational aspects: The ADMM solver is referenced, but algorithmic details for handling the latent decomposition and spectral constraints, computational cost vs overlapped, and convergence criteria are not discussed.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The definitions and duality are consistent with existing theory. The consistency and risk analyses follow established templates using dual norms and concentration. Assumptions (Gaussian noise, incoherence via spectral constraints) are standard but should be more explicitly connected to practice (choice/enforcement of α). The step from component-wise error to overall error via singleton decomposition is well-explained and hinges on properties of the latent norm.
- Empirical support aligns with the theoretical scaling; however, the setup is restricted, and reporting how α and λ were set would improve reproducibility.

Clarity
- Generally well-written and organized. Definitions and notations (unfoldings, ranks, norms) are precise. The role of the latent norm and its dual is clearly presented. Minor improvements: explicitly state whether constraints in (11) are enforced in experiments and how α is chosen; summarize the proof ideas for main theorems in the main text to aid intuition.

Originality
- Extends structured sparsity dualities to the tensor Sp/q setting and provides the first denoising analysis for the latent Schatten-1 tensor regularizer with bounds depending on mink rk. Given that the latent formulation itself was introduced earlier [25], the main novelty is theoretical.

Significance
- The result that latent regularization adapts to the lowest mode rank is practically important, explaining when one should prefer latent to overlapped norms. The analysis can guide method selection and parameterization in tensor denoising; extending to completion could broaden impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Clarify and justify the spectral norm constraints: how is α picked; are they enforced in experiments; what is the impact if they are removed or violated?
- Include tensor completion experiments and ideally at least one real dataset to illustrate practical relevance.
- Discuss computational complexity and provide more details on the solver and decomposition recovery (how Ŵ(k) are obtained).
- Explore robustness to non-Gaussian noise and model misspecification.
- Comment on potential lower bounds or optimality of mink rk dependence for latent denoising.

Arguments for acceptance
- Clear theoretical contribution explaining empirical advantages of latent tensor norms, with rigorous bounds and an elegant duality.
- Practical guidance on method choice grounded in rank dependence.
- Empirical results consistent with theory.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited empirical scope and unclear enforcement of key theoretical constraints.
- Duality and formulation are close to known structured sparsity analogs; novelty is primarily in extension and analysis rather than a fundamentally new method.
- No evaluation on completion or real data.

Overall, this is a solid and well-argued theoretical paper with clear insights into structured tensor norms; expanding empirical validation and clarifying constraints would further strengthen it.
