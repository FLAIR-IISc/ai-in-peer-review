Summary
This paper studies convex formulations for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms. Two families are considered: (i) “overlapped” norms that sum the Schatten norms of all mode-unfoldings, and (ii) “latent” norms that decompose a tensor into a sum of mode-specific low-rank components and sum the unfoldings’ Schatten norms of those components. The paper establishes a duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms, proves a consistency bound for denoising with the latent Schatten-1 norm, derives deterministic and probabilistic error bounds whose dominant term scales with the minimum mode-k rank (in contrast to prior results for overlapped norms that scale with an average of ranks), and provides small-scale experiments that qualitatively match the predicted scaling. The work connects tensor regularization to structured sparsity literature (e.g., latent group lasso), and argues that the latent approach is preferable for noisy tensor decomposition, particularly when only one mode is low-rank.

Strengths
- Clear conceptual contribution: the latent-vs-overlapped dichotomy for tensor norms and their duality generalize known results in structured sparsity to the tensor/Schatten setting.
- Useful theory: consistency bound; deterministic and Gaussian-noise bounds showing scaling with min mode rank; comparison against prior bound for overlapped norms explains empirical observations.
- Theoretical prescriptions for λ independent of unknown ranks are valuable.
- Experiments qualitatively support the scaling predicted by theory; code availability is noted.

Weaknesses and detailed concerns
Quality/technical correctness
- Notational ambiguity: The same symbol ||·||S1/1 is used for both overlapped and latent norms. In Sec. 2.1 eq. (1)/(2) denotes overlapped S1/1, while Sec. 2.2 eq. (6) redefines S1/1 as the latent norm. This is highly confusing and potentially incorrect. Distinct notation (e.g., ||·||over,S1/1 vs ||·||lat,S1/1) is needed throughout, including in all theorems.
- Possible typo in inequality (3): “≤ K ∑k √rk ||W||F” seems to have an extra factor K. Standard bounds give ∑k ||W(k)||S1 ≤ ∑k √rk ||W||F. Please verify constants.
- In Thm. 2, you define r̄k = rank(W* (k)(k)) but the bound (12) is written with ∑k rk (no bar). This appears inconsistent; please clarify which ranks are used.
- Eq. (11) objective missing 1/2 factor before ||Y−W||^2F (earlier Theorem and standard practice include 1/2). Several equations have missing parentheses and inconsistent notation (e.g., W (k)(k), W(k) (k)).
- The incoherence-type constraint ∥W*(l)(k)∥S∞ ≤ α (∀l≠k) and its enforcement in (11): It is unclear how α is chosen, whether it is known/estimable, and whether the constraint is enforced in experiments. Without specifying α, the practical relevance of Theorem 2/3 is reduced.
- The λ used in Theorem 3 includes a term “+ α(K−1)” and the factor F includes “α(K−1)2σ” (is this multiplied by 2σ or divided? Missing punctuation makes this ambiguous). Please check and fix dimensions and constants.
- The “equivalence” from (5) to minimizing L(W)+λ||W||S1/1 assumes the norm is the latent norm; yet due to the notational collision with the overlapped norm, the current presentation is misleading.

Clarity/presentation
- Numerous typos/grammar: “betters,” “prepint,” “Prof. ICCV,” random extra commas, inconsistent capitalization, “no missing entries,” “dimensionalities,” and awkward phrases. These detract from readability.
- Formatting and cross-referencing: A footnote marker “1” appears as in-text sentence; inconsistent equation numbering styles; missing or mismatched parentheses in multiple equations; superscripts/subscripts crowded (e.g., W (k)(k)).
- References are incomplete or malformed (missing years/venues; several listed as “Technical report,” with no identifiers; some conference names misspelled). Please provide full bibliographic details and verify all citations (e.g., Vershynin’s survey; Mu et al. “Square deal”; Tomioka et al. prior work).
- The duality lemma is central but relegated to the supplement; at least a brief proof sketch would help.

Originality
- The duality between overlapped/latent group norms is known in structured sparsity; extending it to tensor Schatten norms is natural but seems incremental. The main novelty lies in the denoising bounds that scale with min rank and the careful comparison to overlapped bounds. Please better position against Signoretto et al., Liu et al., and other tensor nuclear-norm formulations, and clarify what is genuinely new beyond [25,26].

Significance/experiments
- Experimental scope is narrow: Gaussian denoising only, no missing data/completion, no non-Gaussian noise, and limited sizes. Given claims about noisy tensor decomposition broadly, showing tensor completion or robustness would strengthen impact.
- Baselines: Only overlapped approach compared. Consider Tucker/HOSVD truncation, CP-based nonconvex methods, robust PCA-style baselines for tensors, or more recent convex relaxations.
- Hyperparameter selection: You “report three representative λ values,” and then discuss an “optimal” λ being independent of rank. How is “optimal” chosen without peeking at ground-truth MSE? Please specify selection protocol; cross-validation on held-out entries would be more convincing.
- LR complexity (18) is computed by running the latent approach on the noiseless true tensor to estimate a minimal sum of latent ranks. This uses oracle access and may bias the analysis; at least discuss this limitation.

Pros
- Clear, useful theoretical message: latent norms adapt to the smallest mode-rank.
- General duality result linking overlapped and latent tensor norms.
- Provides λ scaling guidance and finite-sample guarantees.

Cons (beyond theory)
- Severe notational ambiguity (overloaded S1/1), multiple equation/typo issues.
- Unclear constants/assumptions (α) and their role in experiments.
- Limited empirical validation and baseline comparisons.
- Poor reference formatting and copy-editing.

Questions for authors
1) Please disambiguate overlapped vs latent norms throughout and correct Eq. (3) and Eq. (11) constants.
2) How is α chosen in practice, and was the constraint enforced in the ADMM solver/experiments?
3) How is λ selected for the plots without using ground truth? Provide a principled procedure and report its performance.
4) Can you add tensor completion experiments and at least one nonconvex baseline?
5) Provide full, correct citations and fix typos/formatting.

Recommendation
Reject. While the core ideas are interesting and potentially impactful, the current manuscript has pervasive clarity/notation/formatting issues, several apparent inconsistencies in equations and assumptions, and limited experimental validation. With careful revision to fix notation, provide complete proofs/derivations or clearer sketches, clarify constants/assumptions, and strengthen experiments and baselines, this work could become a strong contribution.

Confidence: High.
