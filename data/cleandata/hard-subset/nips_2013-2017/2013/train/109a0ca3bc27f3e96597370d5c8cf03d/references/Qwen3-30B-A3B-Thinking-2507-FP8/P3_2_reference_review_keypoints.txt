- Paper's main contribution: establishes duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms and shows latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank (not the average).  
- Formal analysis of two convex tensor recovery methods: overlapped (penalize all unfoldings) and latent (sum of mode-specific low-rank tensors).  
- Theoretical results include consistency (Theorem 1), deterministic bounds (Theorem 2), and Gaussian-noise bounds (Theorem 3).  
- Lemma 1: novel and general duality result that generalizes overlapped/latent structured sparsity norms.  
- Provides a clear theoretical explanation for empirical superiority of the latent approach (Equation 15 vs. 16; Figure 1).  
- Connects tensor decomposition approaches to structured sparsity literature (overlapped vs. latent group sparsity).  
- Strong empirical validation: experiments show MSE scales linearly with the predicted complexity measures for both methods.  
- Analysis restricted to the denoising setting; does not address tensor completion with missing entries.  
- Incoherence assumption (Equation 10) is strong and may not hold in practical tensor data; no empirical justification given.  
- No practical guidance for choosing the regularization parameter λ; experiments use grid search which doesn't scale.  
- Terminology “latent approach” is not clearly explained or intuitively motivated in the paper.  
- Limited empirical comparison: only overlapped vs. latent convex methods; misses comparisons to non-convex methods (CP/Tucker) and key tensor completion work (e.g., Gandy et al. 2011).  
- Theoretical assumption that tensor decomposes into mode-specific low-rank components is restrictive; behavior when violated is not analyzed.  
- Numerical experiments use random tensors that may not capture worst-case scenarios where latent approach excels.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel theoretical contribution with strong empirical support, but limited by scope and assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Accept — valuable theoretical insight despite limitations.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly state denoising vs. completion limitation and discuss extensions to completion.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify “latent” terminology and give intuition; discuss computational complexity and ADMM costs.  
- Suggested improvements: provide λ-selection guidance or scalable tuning strategies; add comparisons to at least one non-convex method and include a one-mode-low-rank experiment like Figure 1.
