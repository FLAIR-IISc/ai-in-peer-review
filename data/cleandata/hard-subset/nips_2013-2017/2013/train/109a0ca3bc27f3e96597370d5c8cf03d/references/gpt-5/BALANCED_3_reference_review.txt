Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies convex approaches to tensor decomposition based on structured Schatten norms. It formalizes two families of tensor norms:
- Overlapped norms: penalize the sum (or ℓq-aggregate) of Schatten p-norms of all mode-unfoldings (e.g., the widely used sum of nuclear norms across modes).
- Latent norms: represent a tensor as a sum of K components, each low-rank in a specific mode, and penalize the nuclear norm of each component’s unfolding in its designated mode.

A central theoretical contribution is a duality result: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm (generalizing known dualities in overlapped/latent group sparsity). Building on this, the paper analyzes the statistical performance of the latent approach in a denoising setting (fully observed noisy tensor):
- A consistency bound using the latent S1/1 norm with λ ≥ ||E||S∞/∞.
- A deterministic error bound under an incoherence-type assumption across components: the error scales with the sum of component ranks and, importantly, the overall Frobenius error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor.
- A Gaussian noise bound that specifies a data-dependent λ and yields MSE scaling essentially with (min mode rank)/nK (up to dimension-dependent factors).

The theory contrasts with prior analysis of the overlapped approach (e.g., Tomioka et al. NIPS), which yields complexity scaling with the average of the square-roots of the mode ranks. Empirically, the authors demonstrate that the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach and that the latent approach’s MSE scales linearly with their latent-rank complexity measure. The work connects to: nuclear norm minimization for matrices (Fazel et al.; Recht et al.), decomposable regularizers (Negahban et al.), overlapped/latent group lasso (Obozinski et al.; Jenatton et al.), and prior tensor completion/decomposition (Gandy et al.; Liu et al.; Signoretto et al.; Tomioka et al.).

Strengths
- Clear, general duality between overlapped and latent Sp/q norms for tensors; conceptually unifies tensor nuclear-norm approaches with structured sparsity.
- New performance guarantees for the latent approach, including consistency and Gaussian denoising bounds with explicit λ, explaining empirical advantages when a tensor is low-rank in only one (or a few) modes.
- Insightful comparison to existing guarantees for the overlapped approach; the min-rank dependence is compelling and intuitively matches regimes where overlapped penalties are suboptimal.
- Empirical results corroborate the predicted scaling and show practical benefits of the latent approach across sizes and rank configurations.
- Discussion ties to a broader body of structured sparsity literature, reinforcing generality.

Weaknesses
- The deterministic/gaussian bounds rely on an additional incoherence-like constraint across components, enforced via spectral-norm constraints with a parameter α in the optimization (Eq. 11). In practice, α is unknown; it is unclear whether experiments enforced these constraints or how α was chosen. This creates a potential theory–practice gap and may limit applicability.
- Experiments focus on synthetic, fully observed denoising with relatively small tensors; no tensor completion or real-world datasets are shown. This limits evidence of practical impact and robustness.
- The choice of λ in theory includes α(K−1); the paper argues the optimal λ is rank-independent, but the dependence on α and dimensional factors F can be loose in practice. A sensitivity study for λ would strengthen claims.
- Some constants and dimension factors (e.g., √(N/nK) + √n1 as a bound on operator norms) appear conservative; discussion of tightness or lower bounds for the latent approach (analogous to Mu et al. for overlapped norms) would be valuable.
- Clarity issues: some references lack full bibliographic details; Figure references are described but not shown; minor typographical inconsistencies.

Quality
- Technical development appears sound: the duality lemma extends known results; the norm inequalities (Lemma 2) explain the min-rank dependence; the proofs (deferred to the supplement) seem to follow standard decomposable-regularizer tools. The gaussian bounds use typical random matrix spectral norm controls.
- The main caveat is the added spectral-norm constraints and the role of α. Clarifying whether these constraints are necessary (or can be replaced by a penalty) and how this aligns with the implemented solver is important.

Clarity
- Generally well-written and organized, with clear definitions and motivation. The relation to prior work is appropriate.
- Please improve exposition around Eq. (11): explain when/why the spectral norm constraints are needed, how α is set/estimated, and whether they are used in experiments.
- Add complete references and ensure figures/algorithms are sufficiently described for reproducibility (e.g., stopping criteria, runtime).

Originality
- The duality for tensor Sp/q norms and the statistical analysis of the latent tensor approach in denoising appear novel and timely. The connection to structured sparsity is insightful.

Significance
- The results offer a principled reason to prefer latent formulations in common regimes and may influence future practice in tensor denoising/completion. While the current empirical scope is limited, the theoretical insights are significant.

Arguments for acceptance
- Conceptually unifying framework with a clean duality.
- New, interpretable excess risk bounds favoring latent norms in important regimes.
- Empirical validation of predicted scaling.
- Builds a bridge to a well-established decomposable-regularizer theory.

Arguments against acceptance
- Reliance on additional incoherence constraints with an unknown α; unclear practical implementation.
- Limited empirical breadth (no completion, no real data), modest scales.
- Some looseness in bounds and missing discussion of lower bounds for latent norms.

Questions and suggestions
- Do your experiments enforce the spectral-norm constraints in Eq. (11)? If not, how do you reconcile the theory with the practice? Could α be estimated or replaced with a penalty term, and would the bounds adapt?
- Can you comment on tightness: are there lower bounds for latent approaches indicating optimal dependence on min mode rank?
- Provide a brief complexity/scalability discussion of the ADMM solver versus overlapped methods.
- Add a small real-world denoising or completion experiment to demonstrate practical utility.
- Clarify the choice and tuning of λ; include a sensitivity analysis and how δ is set.

Recommendation: Weak accept
The paper makes a solid theoretical contribution with a unifying perspective and practically relevant implications. Addressing the α-constraint/practicality gap and broadening experiments would strengthen it further.

Confidence: 4/5
