- Introduces a convex tensor decomposition framework comparing "overlapped" vs "latent" structured Schatten-norm regularization.  
- Establishes a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten-norm approaches.  
- Proves that the latent approach's error scales with the minimum mode-k rank (not the average), explaining its empirical advantages.  
- Connects overlapped/latent Schatten norms to overlapped/latent group sparsity literature, generalizing prior structured-sparsity results.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with clear proofs for key lemmas and Theorems 1–3.  
- Empirical simulations confirm theoretical predictions: MSE scales with TR complexity for overlapped and LR complexity for latent.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and helpful figures (e.g., Figure 2).  
- Original contributions include the duality result (Lemma 1) and the minimum-rank error scaling (Theorem 2).  
- Results have practical significance, guiding practitioners to prefer latent approach when rank structure is unknown.  
- The work advances understanding of convex tensor decomposition and may extend to tensor completion problems.  
- Limitations: no discussion of computational complexity of the latent vs overlapped approaches.  
- Limitations: experiments are limited to small synthetic tensors and lack real-world or large-scale validation.  
- Limitations: theoretical analysis assumes Gaussian noise and gives limited practical guidance on choosing regularization λ.  
- Overall assessment: strong, high-quality theoretical contribution that justifies acceptance (recommendation: accept).
