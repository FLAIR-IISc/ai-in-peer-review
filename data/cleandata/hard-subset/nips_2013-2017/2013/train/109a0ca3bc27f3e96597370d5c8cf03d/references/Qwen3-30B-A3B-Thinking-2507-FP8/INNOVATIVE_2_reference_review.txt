

# Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper introduces a novel perspective on convex tensor decomposition by formally analyzing the "latent approach" as an alternative to the previously studied "overlapped approach." The key contribution is establishing a duality between the two types of structured Schatten norms, and showing that the latent approach's error scales with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average rank, which explains its empirical superiority in many settings.

The paper makes several important theoretical contributions. First, it rigorously proves that the latent Schatten 1-norm (used in the latent approach) has a dual norm that is the overlapped Schatten ∞/∞-norm, establishing a clean duality relationship between the two approaches. Second, it provides a deterministic error bound showing that the latent approach's error scales with the minimal mode-k rank, which is a significant theoretical improvement over the overlapped approach's error bound that depends on the average rank. Third, it demonstrates through both theory and experiments that the latent approach consistently outperforms the overlapped approach, even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes.

The novelty of this work is substantial. While convex tensor decomposition has been studied before (e.g., [9, 15, 23, 25, 26]), the paper identifies a fundamental limitation in the overlapped approach and proposes a theoretically grounded alternative. The connection to structured sparsity literature ([2, 13, 17, 21]) is particularly insightful, as it shows the latent approach corresponds to "latent group sparsity" in the structured sparsity framework, whereas the overlapped approach corresponds to "overlapped group sparsity."

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with a logical progression from definitions to theoretical results to empirical validation. The theoretical results are sound, and the experimental section effectively confirms the theoretical predictions about the scaling behavior of the mean squared error with respect to the tensor dimensions and ranks.

The significance of this work is high. Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem in machine learning and data analysis, with applications in neuroimaging, collaborative filtering, and environmental monitoring. The paper provides a theoretically grounded approach that outperforms existing convex methods in a broader range of scenarios, particularly when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. This is a significant practical improvement over the overlapped approach, which has been the standard in convex tensor decomposition.

While the paper is strong overall, there are a few minor weaknesses. The empirical validation could be more comprehensive (e.g., testing on more diverse tensor sizes or real-world datasets), and the paper doesn't fully explore the computational complexity of the latent approach compared to the overlapped approach. However, these are relatively minor concerns given the paper's strong theoretical contributions.

As a reviewer who prioritizes novelty and bold theoretical insights, I find this paper highly compelling. The duality result between the two structured Schatten norms is elegant and generalizes existing results in structured sparsity. The theoretical explanation for why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in many cases is both novel and significant, and it provides a clear direction for future work in convex tensor decomposition.

The paper makes a strong contribution to the field by not only providing a new method but also by rigorously explaining its advantages through novel theoretical analysis. This is precisely the kind of work that advances the field beyond incremental improvements. The connection to structured sparsity literature is particularly valuable, as it places the tensor decomposition work in a broader theoretical context.

Compared to other papers in the convex tensor decomposition literature, this work is significantly more innovative. While [26] established theoretical guarantees for the overlapped approach, this paper fundamentally challenges the standard approach by proposing a new framework with superior theoretical properties. The paper also makes an important distinction between the multilinear rank of the tensor and the ranks of the latent factors, which is crucial for understanding the practical performance of the method.

Given the paper's strong theoretical contributions, clear explanation of the advantages of the latent approach, and empirical validation, I believe it makes a significant contribution to the field. The novelty of the duality result and the theoretical insight about the minimum mode-k rank scaling is particularly compelling.

I recommend acceptance. This paper introduces a significant new direction in convex tensor decomposition that has both theoretical and practical implications. It provides a clear explanation for empirical observations, establishes important theoretical connections to structured sparsity, and demonstrates consistent performance improvements over existing approaches. The work is well-executed, theoretically sound, and represents a meaningful advancement over previous work in the area.
