Summary
This paper studies convex formulations for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms. Two families of norms are considered: (i) overlapped Sp/q norms that penalize all mode-unfoldings simultaneously (sum or ℓq combination of Schatten p norms of unfoldings), and (ii) latent Sp*/q* norms that decompose the tensor into a sum of K components, each low-rank in one designated mode, and penalize the corresponding unfolding of each component. The authors establish a duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm, generalizing known dualities from structured sparsity with overlapping groups. They then analyze the “latent” convex estimator (sum of nuclear norms on per-mode components) for denoising, proving: (a) a basic consistency bound, (b) a deterministic error bound under an incoherence-type constraint among components, and (c) a Gaussian-noise bound showing the MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank (up to dimension factors), contrasting with prior analysis of the overlapped approach which scales with the average of the square-roots of mode ranks. Synthetic experiments corroborate the predicted scaling and show that the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach, including when the ground-truth tensor is low-rank in multiple modes.

Relation to prior work
The overlapped formulation (sum of nuclear norms of mode unfoldings) was proposed and analyzed in prior work (e.g., Liu et al. 2009; Gandy et al. 2011; Tomioka et al. 2011; Tomioka et al. 2013). The latent approach (mixture of mode-specific low-rank components) was proposed by Tomioka et al. (2011/2013), but lacked generalization/noisy recovery guarantees. This work fills that gap, leveraging techniques from decomposable regularizers and high-dimensional M-estimation (Agarwal et al. 2012; Negahban et al. 2010) and generalizing duality results from overlapped vs latent group lasso (Obozinski et al.; Bach et al.). The comparison to Mu et al. (2013) lower bounds is discussed. The paper does not cover alternative tensor nuclear norms based on t-SVD/tubal rank, which form a different line of work; acknowledging their distinct scope would improve positioning.

Quality
- Theory: The duality between overlapped and latent Sp/q norms appears correct and useful. The key inequalities (overlapped: norm ≤ sum_k sqrt(r_k) ||W||_F; latent: norm ≤ min_k sqrt(r_k) ||W||_F) cleanly explain the different statistical behaviors. The consistency result (Theorem 1) is standard but appropriate. The deterministic bound (Theorem 2) and Gaussian bound (Theorem 3) are sensible, with λ choices derived from spectral-norm concentration of unfoldings; the dependence on min mode rank is a compelling insight.
- Assumptions/estimator: The deterministic and probabilistic guarantees rely on an additional set of spectral-norm constraints coupling the components (parameterized by α) inside the estimator. This improves identifiability but raises practical issues: α must be known/tuned and, more critically, it is unclear whether these constraints were enforced in the experiments. If the empirical results used the unconstrained latent estimator, the theory-practice gap should be discussed and, ideally, a theorem covering the unconstrained case provided (perhaps by encoding the constraint implicitly via λ and incoherence).
- Proof presentation: Most proofs are deferred. A brief proof sketch for the duality lemma and Lemma 2 in the main text would help readers grasp the core ideas (infimal convolution and decomposability).
- Experiments: Synthetic denoising experiments match theory and include fair comparisons to the overlapped baseline across λ values. However, experiments are limited: medium-sized synthetic tensors, no missing data, no real data, and no comparison to nonconvex Tucker/CP baselines (e.g., HOOI/ALS). There is no runtime/scalability analysis of the ADMM solver. The procedure for selecting λ in practice should be clarified (theory-driven value vs cross-validation vs oracle MSE), as the narrative suggests both.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written, but several notational issues cause confusion:
- The same symbol ||·||_{S1/1} is used for both the overlapped and latent Schatten-1 norms; please adopt distinct notations (e.g., ||·||overlap,1/1 and ||·||latent,1/1) throughout.
- Clearly distinguish between multilinear ranks (r_k of W) and component ranks (r̄_k of W^(k)). Some statements switch without warning.
- Minor typos (e.g., “betters”→“better”, “prepint”→“preprint”, “There is no missing entries”).
Adding a concise intuitive explanation for Lemma 2 and the choice of λ (Theorem 3) would aid readability.

Originality
- The duality result for structured Schatten norms and the statistical analysis of the latent tensor estimator under noise appear novel and nontrivial extensions of group-sparsity theory to tensors. The insight that latent convex decomposition adapts to the minimum mode rank is new and important.

Significance
- The work advances the theoretical understanding of convex tensor methods, providing clear guidance on when latent vs overlapped formulations are preferable. The min-rank adaptivity is likely to influence practice in denoising and possibly completion. However, the practical impact would be stronger with real-data experiments and tensor completion results.

Pros
- Clear, general duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms.
- New statistical guarantees for the latent estimator; adaptation to min mode rank.
- Intuitive inequalities explaining empirical behavior.
- Experiments corroborate scaling predictions.

Cons
- Reliance on additional spectral-norm constraints (α) in the estimator; unclear if used empirically and how α is chosen.
- Limited experimental scope (no real data, no missing data, no nonconvex baselines, no scalability analysis).
- Notation conflates overlapped and latent norms.
- Some proofs entirely deferred; limited proof sketches in main text.

Suggestions for improvement
- Use distinct notation for overlapped vs latent norms; add a short proof sketch for the duality and Lemma 2.
- Clarify whether constraints ∥W^(l)(k)∥_{S∞} ≤ α are enforced in practice; if not, provide theory for the unconstrained estimator or discuss empirical sensitivity to α.
- Include experiments on tensor completion and at least one real dataset (e.g., video, EEG), plus comparisons to HOOI/Tucker/CP with rank selection.
- Report computational complexity, wall-clock times, and scaling to larger tensors.
- Discuss relation and differences to alternative tensor nuclear norms (t-SVD/tubal nuclear norm), even briefly.

Overall recommendation
This is a solid theoretical contribution with clear insights and supportive simulations. With clarifications on the estimator constraints and stronger empirical validation, it would be a strong NIPS paper. I lean toward acceptance, contingent on addressing clarity and experimental scope.
