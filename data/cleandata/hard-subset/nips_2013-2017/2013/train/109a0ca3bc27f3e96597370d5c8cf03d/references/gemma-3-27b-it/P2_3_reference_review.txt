## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a valuable theoretical analysis of the latent approach to convex tensor decomposition, providing insights into its superior performance over the overlapped approach, particularly when dealing with tensors low-rank in only one mode. The work is technically sound, well-written, and builds upon existing literature in a meaningful way. While the empirical results are somewhat limited, they support the theoretical findings. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

**Summary of the Paper:**

The paper investigates convex relaxation methods for tensor decomposition, specifically focusing on two approaches: the "overlapped" approach (penalizing all modes simultaneously with the Schatten 1-norm) and the "latent" approach (decomposing the tensor into a mixture of tensors, each low-rank in a specific mode). The core contribution is a rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating that the error bound for the latent approach scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank, which explains its empirical advantage in scenarios where the tensor is nearly full rank in all but one mode. The authors also establish a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms and provide empirical validation of their theoretical results.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a solid mathematical analysis of the latent approach, proving consistency and deriving error bounds. The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a significant theoretical contribution.
* **Clear Explanation of Performance Difference:** The theoretical results elegantly explain why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in specific scenarios, addressing a previously empirical observation.
* **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is clearly structured and easy to follow, with well-defined notation and logical flow.
* **Connection to Existing Literature:** The authors effectively connect their work to existing research in tensor decomposition, structured sparsity, and convex optimization. They cite relevant papers and position their contributions within the broader landscape of the field.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments, while not extensive, provide evidence supporting the theoretical predictions. The authors acknowledge limitations and clearly present their results.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Empirical Scope:** The experiments are relatively simple and focus primarily on verifying the scaling behavior. More comprehensive experiments, perhaps with real-world datasets or varying noise levels, would strengthen the empirical validation.
* **Assumptions in Theoretical Analysis:** The deterministic bound relies on some rather strong assumptions regarding the spectral norms of the latent factors (inequality 10). The impact of relaxing these assumptions could be explored.
* **Practical Considerations:** The paper doesn't delve into the practical challenges of implementing the latent approach, such as computational complexity or parameter tuning. A discussion of these aspects would be beneficial.
* **Minor Clarity Issue:** The connection between the duality established in Lemma 1 and its direct impact on improved performance could be more explicitly stated. While the authors discuss it, a more direct link would be helpful.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and well-supported by theoretical analysis. The derivations appear correct, and the claims are justified. It's a complete piece of work, presenting a clear and well-defined contribution. The authors are careful in their analysis and acknowledge limitations.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The notation is clear, and the concepts are explained in a manner accessible to readers familiar with tensor decomposition and convex optimization.
* **Originality:** The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms and the theoretical analysis of the latent approach's performance represent novel contributions. The paper builds on existing ideas but offers a significant advancement in understanding.
* **Significance:** The results are important for advancing the field of tensor decomposition. The improved understanding of the latent approach's performance has the potential to guide the development of more effective algorithms for real-world applications, such as neuroimaging and collaborative filtering.



**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Strong theoretical foundation and insightful analysis.
* Clear explanation of the latent approach's advantages.
* Well-written and organized presentation.
* Potential for significant impact in the field.

**Against:**

* Limited empirical scope.
* Strong assumptions in the theoretical analysis.




**Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, I suggest:

1. **Expand the empirical evaluation:** Include experiments with real-world datasets and explore the impact of varying noise levels.
2. **Discuss the impact of the assumptions:** Briefly discuss the sensitivity of the results to the spectral norm assumptions and potential directions for relaxing them.
3. **Clarify the link between duality and performance:** Make the connection between the duality result and the improved performance of the latent approach more explicit.




Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the understanding of convex tensor decomposition. The theoretical results are compelling, and the paper is well-written and presented. The suggested revisions would further strengthen the work and increase its impact.
