- Paper compares overlapped vs. latent Schatten norm regularization for convex tensor decomposition and analyzes the latent approach theoretically.  
- Main theoretical result: latent approach’s estimation error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, explaining its empirical advantages for tensors low-rank in few modes.  
- Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, linking to structured sparsity literature.  
- Provides numerical experiments that confirm the predicted scaling behavior and show latent’s empirical benefits.  
- Builds on prior convex tensor decomposition and Tucker decomposition work, and connects to group sparsity/structured sparsity results.  
- Strong theoretical analysis and rigorous framework for understanding the latent approach.  
- Duality result is novel and expands understanding of regularization techniques.  
- Theoretical results clarify previously observed empirical success of the latent method.  
- Numerical validation complements the theory and strengthens the paper’s claims.  
- Well-written and logically organized presentation, despite dense notation.  
- Key weakness: Theorem 2 depends on a strong incoherence assumption (Eq. 10) that may not hold in practice.  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity and scalability for large tensors.  
- Numerical experiments are somewhat limited in tensor sizes, rank configurations, and noise levels.  
- Missing discussion on adapting analysis to tensor completion, which is only mentioned as future work.  
- Equation (5) uses a generic loss L; the paper should clarify typical choices (e.g., squared error).  
- Overall assessment: high originality, significance, and quality; recommended as Strong Accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
