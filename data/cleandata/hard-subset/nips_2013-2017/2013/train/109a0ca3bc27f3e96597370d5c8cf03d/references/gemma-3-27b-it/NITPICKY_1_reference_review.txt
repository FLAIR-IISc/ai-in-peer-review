This paper investigates convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norm regularization, specifically contrasting an "overlapped" approach with a "latent" approach. The core contribution lies in establishing theoretical guarantees for the latent approach, demonstrating its performance scales with the minimum mode-k rank, explaining its empirical superiority when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. A duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is also presented, connected to existing work on structured sparsity.

While the paper tackles an important problem and presents some novel theoretical insights, its presentation suffers from a remarkable number of minor flaws and inconsistencies that, cumulatively, detract from its impact. The overall structure is logical, but the execution is excessively meticulous to a detrimental degree.

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Contribution:** Establishing a theoretical bound scaling with the minimum rank is a valuable contribution, providing justification for the observed benefits of the latent approach.
*   **Duality Result:** The identified duality between the norms is a non-trivial result and may have broader implications.
*   **Connection to Existing Work:** The paper effectively links tensor decomposition to the broader field of structured sparsity, providing context and relating the work to relevant literature.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments, while relatively simple, corroborate the theoretical findings.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Notational Inconsistencies:** The paper suffers from numerous notational inconsistencies. For example, W is used for both a general tensor *and* as the estimator in Section 3.1. Similarly, W(k) refers to both a tensor and an unfolding. While subtle, these inconsistencies make the paper harder to follow and suggest a lack of meticulous editing.
*   **Formatting Issues:** Repeatedly, equations are not properly broken across pages (e.g., equation (2)). The formatting of Lemma 1 is problematic; the definition of  Sp∗/q∗  is visually unclear due to the placement of the infimum.
*   **Overly Detailed/Verbose:** The introduction is excessively lengthy. Several sentences repeat concepts unnecessarily. The same holds true for Section 2; definitions are repeated multiple times, adding to the length without significantly improving understanding.
*   **Redundancy in Theorems:** Theorems 2 and 3 build upon Theorem 1, but the connection isn't clearly articulated. The conditions for the theorems feel arbitrary and lack clear justification, particularly the incoherence assumption in Theorem 2. Why is *that* particular constraint necessary?
*   **Lack of Discussion of Practical Implications:** While the theory is presented, the paper doesn’t sufficiently discuss the practical implications of the results. How does one estimate the minimal rank in practice? How sensitive is the performance to violations of the assumptions?
*   **Minor Mathematical Errors/Omissions:** The definition of the Frobenius norm uses a square root, but the subsequent use in (3) does not seem to require it, leading to confusion.
*   **Overuse of "Interestingly":** The word "Interestingly" appears multiple times and adds no substantial content.
*   **Reference Style:** The reference list isn't consistently formatted.

**Specific Points:**

*   Page 2, line 10: "unfold1" is awkward wording. Simply "unfold" is sufficient.
*   Page 3, Lemma 1: The definition of the latent norm is visually hard to parse.
*   Page 5, Theorem 2: The derivation of this theorem feels incomplete and relies heavily on citing [1] without providing sufficient detail.
*   Page 7, Equation (17) and (18): These definitions lack context. Explain *why* these complexities are relevant.
*   Throughout: The paper frequently uses phrases like "as in Figure 1" without adequately describing what the reader should be looking for in the figure.
*   The numbering of equations and lemmas is correct, but the referencing within the text could be more consistent.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

*   **Against:** The sheer number of minor flaws, despite the solid theoretical contribution, raises concerns about the overall rigor and attention to detail. The paper feels unfinished. The verbose writing style and inconsistent notation significantly impede readability.
*   **For:** The theoretical results regarding the latent approach are valuable and address an open question in the field. The connection to structured sparsity is well-established. The empirical validation supports the theoretical findings.

**Overall Assessment:**

Reject. While the core ideas are sound and the theoretical contributions are valuable, the pervasive issues with clarity, consistency, and presentation outweigh the strengths. The paper requires significant revision and polishing before it would be suitable for NIPS. The authors need to address the numerous minor flaws to demonstrate a level of meticulousness expected of a NIPS publication. A more concise and focused presentation of the results would also improve its impact. I would encourage the authors to resubmit after addressing these concerns.
