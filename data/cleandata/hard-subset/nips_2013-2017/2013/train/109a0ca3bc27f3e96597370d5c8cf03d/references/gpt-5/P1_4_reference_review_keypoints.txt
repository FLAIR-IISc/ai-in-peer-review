- Paper compares overlapped vs latent convex Schatten-norm regularizers for low-rank tensor estimation and proves a duality between them (overlapped Sp/q dual = latent Sp*/q*).  
- Provides denoising analysis for the latent approach: consistency, deterministic bound under an incoherence constraint, and high-probability Gaussian-noise bounds.  
- Key theoretical message: latent estimator error scales with the minimal mode-rank of the ground truth, explaining empirical advantages when rank is concentrated in one mode.  
- Synthetic experiments corroborate theoretical scaling predictions and show latent outperforming overlapped in relevant regimes.  
- Situates work within convex matrix/tensor recovery and structured sparsity literature, building on Tomioka et al. and connecting to latent/overlapped group norms.  
- Novelty: extension of overlapped/latent duality to tensor Schatten norms and new statistical analysis for the latent tensor norm.  
- Strength: clear conceptual contribution and thorough theoretical results (consistency, deterministic, probabilistic bounds).  
- Strength: careful comparison to prior theory that explains empirical behavior and unifies with structured sparsity perspectives.  
- Weakness: theoretical bounds rely on constrained problem (spectral norm constraints with parameter α) that may not match the unconstrained estimator used in experiments.  
- Weakness: practicality and selection of the incoherence parameter α are not addressed; α could be large in real settings.  
- Weakness: limited experiments—only synthetic Gaussian denoising, no real-data or completion tasks, and no sensitivity analyses for α or model misspecification.  
- Weakness: no lower bounds or minimax analysis for the latent estimator; factor F in Theorem 3 and dimensional dependence lack clarity on tightness.  
- Quality: technically sound proofs (in supplement) and reasonable spectral-norm concentration arguments, but main caveat is reliance on added constraints for key bounds.  
- Clarity: generally well written and organized, though some notation/footnote issues and deferred intuitions for key lemmas/theorems could be improved.  
- Suggestions: explicitly state if constraints in (11) are used in experiments, provide intuition sketches for Lemma 2/Theorem 2, and give guidance or heuristics for choosing α.  
- Questions for authors: were spectral-norm constraints enforced in experiments; how to set α in practice; extension to sub-Gaussian noise; any lower-bound results; can a small real-data study be added?  
- Pros: strong, novel theory with practical guidance favoring latent norms when rank concentrates in one mode.  
- Cons: mismatch between theory and empirical setup due to constraints, limited experimental scope, and insufficient practical guidance for α/λ.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful theoretical contribution but would benefit from clarifying constrained vs. unconstrained estimators and more practical analysis.
