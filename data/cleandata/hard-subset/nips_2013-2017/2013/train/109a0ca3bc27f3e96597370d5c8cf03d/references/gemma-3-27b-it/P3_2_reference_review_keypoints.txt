- Overall: Valuable theoretical and empirical contribution; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem focus: Compares overlapped vs latent Schatten-norm convex tensor decomposition, proving a duality and advantages of the latent approach when tensors are low-rank in only one mode.  
- Related work: Well-grounded in tensor decomposition, convex low-rank optimization, and structured sparsity literature.  
- Strength — theory: Rigorous analysis with Lemma 1 (duality) and Theorems 1 & 2 (consistency and deterministic bounds) that scale with the minimum rank.  
- Strength — explanation: Clear, principled explanation for why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach.  
- Strength — presentation: Exceptionally well-written, organized, and mathematically clear.  
- Strength — experiments: Numerical results align with theory and demonstrate latent approach benefits, though simple.  
- Strength — connections: Thoughtful linkage to prior work in convex optimization and structured sparsity.  
- Weakness — empirical scope: Experiments use small tensors and limited rank configurations; lacks real-world dataset tests.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Deterministic bound relies on a potentially restrictive incoherence assumption (eq. 10) with no sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — computational discussion: Paper omits analysis of computational complexity and runtime scaling for latent vs overlapped approaches.  
- Weakness — baseline comparisons: No comparison to more recent/state-of-the-art tensor decomposition methods (e.g., advanced ALS variants).  
- For acceptance: Strong theoretical justification, clear superiority explanation, good writing, and supportive empirical evidence.  
- Against acceptance: Limited experiments, restrictive theoretical assumptions, missing complexity discussion, and lacking comparisons.  
- Questions for authors: Provide computational complexity comparison, sensitivity to the incoherence assumption, plans/results on real-world datasets, and scaling behavior for K>3 tensors.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to broaden empirical validation, discuss assumption sensitivity, add computational complexity analysis, and include broader method comparisons.
