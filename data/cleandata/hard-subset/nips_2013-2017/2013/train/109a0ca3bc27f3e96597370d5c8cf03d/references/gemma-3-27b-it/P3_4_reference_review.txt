## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a valuable theoretical analysis of the latent Schatten norm approach to convex tensor decomposition, building on recent empirical observations. The authors convincingly demonstrate why the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach, particularly when the underlying tensor is low-rank in only a few modes. The work is technically sound, well-written, and addresses an important gap in the understanding of convex tensor decomposition methods. While the theoretical results build on existing work in structured sparsity, applying them to the tensor decomposition setting is a significant contribution. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper tackles the problem of decomposing tensors into low-rank components using convex optimization. Traditional tensor decomposition methods rely on non-convex optimization, lacking strong theoretical guarantees. Recent work has explored convex relaxations based on Schatten norm regularization, specifically the overlapped and latent approaches. The overlapped approach penalizes low rank across all modes simultaneously, while the latent approach encourages low rank in specific modes.  The authors build upon empirical evidence (citing Tomioka et al. [25]) suggesting the latent approach is superior in scenarios where the tensor is primarily low-rank along a single mode. This paper provides a theoretical justification for this observation, establishing consistency results and demonstrating a favorable scaling of the error bound with the minimal mode-k rank.  The work appropriately positions itself within the existing literature on structured sparsity (group lasso, etc.) and highlights the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The core contribution is a rigorous analysis of the latent Schatten norm approach. The consistency results (Theorem 1) and the deterministic bound (Theorem 2) provide valuable insights into the performance of the method.
* **Novel Duality Result:**  The identification of the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a significant theoretical contribution, connecting tensor decomposition to broader results in structured sparsity.
* **Clear Explanation of Performance Differences:** The paper effectively explains *why* the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach – its error bound scales with the *minimum* rank, while the overlapped approach depends on an average.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments confirm the theoretical predictions, demonstrating the scaling behavior and providing further evidence for the superiority of the latent approach, even in cases where the overlapped approach *should* perform well.
* **Well-Written & Organized:** The paper is clearly written and logically organized.  The authors explain the concepts and notations effectively.
* **Connection to Existing Literature:** The authors appropriately cite and build upon relevant work in tensor decomposition, convex optimization, and structured sparsity.

**3. Weaknesses & Areas for Improvement:**

* **Assumptions in Theorem 2 & 3:** The assumptions in Theorem 2 (incoherence) and Theorem 3 (Gaussian noise) are somewhat strong. While common in theoretical analysis, it would be valuable to briefly discuss the sensitivity of the results to violations of these assumptions, or point to directions for future work to relax them.
* **Practical Implications of Incoherence:** The meaning of the α parameter in Theorem 2 and its practical implications are not fully explored. How easy is it to verify or estimate this parameter in practice?
* **Computational Complexity:** While the paper focuses on theoretical guarantees, a brief discussion of the computational complexity of the latent approach compared to the overlapped approach would be useful.  Are there practical trade-offs beyond the error bound that might favor one approach over the other?
* **Limited Scope of Numerical Experiments:** The experimental setup, while effective in validating the theory, is relatively simple. Exploring more complex datasets or different noise distributions could further strengthen the empirical findings.

**4. Detailed Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality (4.5/5):** The paper is technically sound, with well-justified claims and a rigorous analysis. The proofs appear correct (though a thorough review of the supplementary material is needed). It's a complete piece of work addressing a specific research question.
* **Clarity (5/5):**  The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains complex concepts. The notation is consistent, and the structure is logical.
* **Originality (4/5):** While the individual techniques (Schatten norm regularization, structured sparsity analysis) are not entirely new, the application to the latent approach for tensor decomposition and the derivation of the duality result are original and significant.
* **Significance (4/5):**  The results are important for advancing the theoretical understanding of convex tensor decomposition.  The insights provided could guide the development of more effective algorithms and inform the choice of regularization strategies in practical applications.  The paper addresses a difficult problem and improves upon existing approaches.



**5. Pro/Con Arguments for Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Provides a theoretical justification for the observed superior performance of the latent approach.
* Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms.
* Rigorous analysis with clear and well-defined results.
* Empirically validated theoretical findings.
* Well-written and clear presentation.

**Con:**

* Strong assumptions in the theoretical bounds.
* Limited discussion of practical computational aspects.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I strongly recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.  I suggest the authors address the weaknesses mentioned above by:

1.  Discussing the sensitivity of the results to violations of the assumptions in Theorems 2 & 3.
2.  Providing some intuition about the practical implications of the incoherence parameter α.
3.  Adding a brief discussion of the computational complexity of the latent approach.
4.  (Optional) Expanding the numerical experiments to include more complex datasets.



This is a high-quality paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of tensor decomposition and convex optimization. It addresses an important open question and provides a solid theoretical foundation for future work.
