- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms, contrasting overlapped (sum of unfoldings) vs latent (sum of mode-specific low-rank components) approaches.
- Main contributions: duality linking overlapped Sp/q norms to latent Sp*/q* norms, denoising guarantees for latent approach (consistency, deterministic incoherence bound, Gaussian high-probability bound), and empirical evidence favoring latent scaling with min mode rank.
- Extends known structured-sparsity duality to Schatten norms over tensor unfoldings, and adapts decomposable-regularizer techniques to latent tensor denoising.
- Technical soundness: proofs are plausible and follow modern M-estimation templates; deterministic bound uses reasonable "dirty model"-style incoherence and spectral constraints.
- Key assumption: spectral incoherence constraints (involving α) are critical and may be strong or hard to set in practice.
- Completeness: theoretical denoising analysis is complete; tensor completion is left for future work.
- Experiments: synthetic denoising supports theoretical scaling and latent advantage but are limited to Gaussian noise, moderate sizes, no missing data, and require access to noiseless decomposition for LR complexity.
- Clarity: well organized and readable with precise definitions; notation is dense and a symbol table plus more discussion of α would help; minor copyediting issues.
- Originality: the overlapped–latent duality for tensor Schatten norms and denoising bounds scaling with min mode rank are novel and conceptually neat.
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding and explains when latent norms outperform overlapped ones, with practical relevance to multiway data problems.
- Strength: clear duality result and sharp latent denoising bounds with explicit λ independent of ranks.
- Strength: connects tensor methods to structured sparsity literature and provides experiments matching theory; code available.
- Weakness: practicality of spectral constraints and choice of α needs guidance or data-driven methods.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation should be broadened (tensor completion, real data, larger K, larger sizes) and sensitivity analyses added.
- Weakness: LR complexity plots rely on W* decomposition—practical estimation methods should be discussed.
- Suggestion: clarify constants and intuition in Theorem 3 (factor F) and provide sensitivity analyses for α and λ.
- Questions for authors: can bounds be proved without spectral constraints or under standard incoherence; how sensitive is performance to α and can it be tuned with λ; does duality extend to weighted/mixed norms; what's ADMM scalability/convergence behavior?
- Overall assessment: technically solid, original, and significant; lean toward acceptance contingent on stronger empirical validation and clearer guidance on α.
