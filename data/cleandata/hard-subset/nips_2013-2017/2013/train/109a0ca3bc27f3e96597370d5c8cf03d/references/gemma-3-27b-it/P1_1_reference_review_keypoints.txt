- Paper compares convex tensor decomposition methods, showing the latent Schatten-norm regularization outperforms the overlapped approach when tensors are low-rank in only one mode.  
- Proves consistency for the latent approach and shows denoising error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, a key theoretical improvement.  
- Identifies a novel duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, connecting tensor decomposition to structured sparsity.  
- Provides empirical validation that supports the theoretical predictions and demonstrates latent’s superior performance in many scenarios.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with rigorous analysis and proven bounds.  
- Clear explanation of why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach linking error scaling to minimum rank.  
- Well-written and well-organized presentation that situates the work within prior literature.  
- Numerical experiments are somewhat limited in range of tensor sizes, rank configurations, noise levels, and baseline comparisons.  
- Deterministic bounds rely on an incoherence assumption that may not hold in practice; robustness under violation is not fully discussed.  
- Theorem 3 is complex; practical implications and intuitive interpretation of the bound need clearer exposition.  
- Missing discussion of computational cost and potential practical limitations of the latent approach.  
- Technically sound and complete work with careful analysis and acknowledgement of limitations.  
- Original contribution by combining theoretical analysis, empirical validation, and demonstrating norm duality.  
- Significant impact potential for tensor decomposition applications in fields like neuroimaging, data mining, and ML.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, clear advantages of latent approach, novel duality, and supporting experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, reliance on restrictive incoherence assumption, and lack of computational-cost discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; expand experiments and clarify theoretical bounds for greater practical clarity.
