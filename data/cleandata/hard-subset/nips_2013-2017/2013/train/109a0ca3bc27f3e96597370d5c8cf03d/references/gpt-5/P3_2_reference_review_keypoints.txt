- Paper studies convex low-rank tensor estimation via two structured Schatten-norm families: overlapped (joint unfolding penalty) and latent (sum of mode-specific low-rank components).  
- Core technical contributions: duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms, analysis of latent S1/1 (consistency, deterministic and high-probability Gaussian-noise bounds), and comparison showing latent error scales with min mode-k rank vs overlapped with average rank.  
- Synthetic experiments support predicted scaling and show latent often outperforms overlapped, even for simultaneously low-rank tensors.  
- Mathematical development is largely sound; the duality lemma and the latent norm bound (sqrt(min rk)·Frobenius) are key insights.  
- Deterministic and probabilistic analyses appropriately use decomposability-style arguments and restricted-strong-convexity-like reasoning in Frobenius geometry.  
- Major caveat: introduction of spectral-norm constraints across non-native modes (Eq. 11 / condition (10)) changes the estimator and requires clarification about their necessity and use.  
- Need clarification on whether experiments enforce these spectral constraints, how α is set in practice, and whether guarantees can be obtained without explicit constraints or under milder assumptions.  
- Choice of Gaussian λ is reasonable but conservative; tighter characterization of maxk ∥E(k)∥S∞ could sharpen constants and dimensional dependencies.  
- Experiments convincingly show scaling trends but are limited to synthetic data, modest sizes, and do not test sensitivity to α/constraints or tensor completion scenarios.  
- LR complexity plotted is computed from a decomposition obtained by running the latent solver on noiseless truth, which is mildly circular but useful for proportionality checks.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear definitions; the structured-sparsity analogy aids intuition, though some notation is dense and a compact norms/duals table would help.  
- A few typos and incomplete references need fixing; footnotes and figure references should be clarified.  
- Originality: generalizing overlapped/latent duality to tensors and showing latent’s min-rank dependence are novel and unify several strands (tensor nuclear norms, structured sparsity).  
- Significance: provides practical insight on when latent vs overlapped formulations are preferable and gives theoretical guidance for λ selection, but broader impact would benefit from completion results, larger K, and real data.  
- Strengths: unifying framework and duality, sharp qualitative insight (latent scales with min mode rank), rigorous analysis, empirical validation, and code availability.  
- Weaknesses: theory–algorithm gap due to spectral constraints (α) seemingly absent in experiments, limited experimental scope, circular LR complexity computation, possibly conservative constants/dimensional dependencies, and minor clarity issues.  
- Questions for authors: do experiments enforce cross-mode spectral constraints; can guarantees be proved for the unconstrained latent formulation; how to choose α in practice; can the analysis and experiments be extended to tensor completion; what is ADMM complexity and convergence behavior for large-scale/higher-K tensors?  
- Minor suggestions: fix typos and incomplete references, add a compact norms/duals table, and provide an explicit algorithm description for the constrained latent problem (including ADMM integration and α selection).  
- Overall assessment: solid, insightful theoretical contribution with practical implications; addressing the constraint/α issue and broadening experiments would strengthen the paper — reviewer leans toward acceptance.
