## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates convex approaches to tensor decomposition, specifically comparing the “overlapped” and “latent” approaches based on structured Schatten norm regularization. It establishes a theoretical connection – a duality – between the norms used in these approaches and, crucially, provides a theoretical justification for the empirically observed superior performance of the latent approach, especially when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The authors demonstrate that the error bound for the latent approach scales with the *minimum* mode rank, whereas the overlapped approach’s bound depends on the average rank.  The paper complements previous work on tensor completion and provides both theoretical and empirical support for its claims.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Contribution:** The paper delivers a significant theoretical result establishing the performance bounds for the latent approach. The scaling with the *minimum* rank is a crucial insight and directly explains empirical observations. The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is also a valuable contribution.
* **Clear Connection to Existing Work:** The paper effectively situates its work within the broader contexts of tensor decomposition, convex optimization, and structured sparsity (group lasso). The references are comprehensive and demonstrate awareness of relevant literature.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments corroborate the theoretical findings, showing that the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach, even for simultaneously low-rank tensors. The analysis of the scaling behavior is well-presented.
* **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is generally clear and well-structured, with a logical flow from introduction to conclusion.  Definitions are provided and the mathematical notation is reasonably accessible.
* **Addresses a Significant Problem:** Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with applications in various fields, and improving its performance is highly valuable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Assumptions:** The theoretical results rely on certain assumptions (e.g., the incoherence assumption in Theorem 2). While reasonable, the impact of violating these assumptions isn’t fully discussed. More discussion of the practical implications of these assumptions would be beneficial.
* **Complexity of Implementation:** While the paper mentions the use of ADMM-based solvers, it doesn't delve into the computational complexity of these algorithms, especially for large-scale tensors.  A brief discussion of the practical challenges of implementing these approaches for high-dimensional data would be helpful.
* **Limited Scope of Numerical Results:**  The numerical experiments focus on relatively small tensors (50x50x20, 80x80x40). While sufficient to validate the scaling results, expanding the experiments to larger, more realistic datasets would strengthen the empirical evidence.
* **Discussion of Tensor Completion:** The paper mentions tensor completion as a future work. While appropriate, a brief discussion of the differences between the noisy tensor decomposition setting studied here and the tensor completion setting would be insightful.



**Assessment based on NIPS Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with rigorous theoretical analysis and well-supported claims. The analysis is complete and the authors are careful in their presentation. **Strong.**
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clearly written and well-organized. The mathematical notation is manageable, and the main ideas are presented in an accessible manner. **Strong.**
* **Originality:** The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is novel. The theoretical analysis providing bounds for the latent approach is also a significant contribution. The paper provides a clear differentiation from previous work. **Strong.**
* **Significance:** The results are important and address a fundamental problem in tensor decomposition. The improvement over existing approaches, demonstrated both theoretically and empirically, suggests that this work is likely to influence future research in this area. **Strong.**

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

*  Provides a crucial theoretical foundation for understanding the latent approach to tensor decomposition.
*  Establishes a clear advantage of the latent approach over the overlapped approach under certain conditions.
*  The duality result is a valuable contribution to the broader field of structured sparsity.
*  Well-written and thoroughly researched.

**Against:**

*  Relatively small-scale numerical experiments.
*  Assumptions in the theoretical results could be more thoroughly discussed.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I **strongly recommend accepting** this paper. The theoretical contributions are significant, the empirical validation is convincing, and the paper is well-written and clearly presented. While there are minor weaknesses, they do not detract from the overall quality and importance of the work. This paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of tensor decomposition and is well-suited for presentation at NIPS.
