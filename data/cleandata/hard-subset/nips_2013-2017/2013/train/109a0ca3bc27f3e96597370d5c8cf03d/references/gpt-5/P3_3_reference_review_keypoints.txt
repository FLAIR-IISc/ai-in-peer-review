- Paper compares two convex tensor regularizers: overlapped (sum of mode-unfolding Schatten norms) vs. latent (sum of mode-specific low-rank components).
- Main theoretical contribution: duality showing overlapped Sp/q norm dual is latent Sp*/q* norm, extending group-lasso dualities to Schatten norms.
- New statistical analysis for the latent estimator: basic consistency, deterministic error bounds scaling with min mode-rank, and Gaussian-noise rates with explicit λ independent of unknown ranks.
- Key inequality: latent Schatten-1 norm ≤ sqrt(min rank) · Frobenius norm, which explains adaptation to the easiest mode.
- Empirical results on synthetic denoising validate predicted MSE scaling and show latent often outperforms overlapped regularization.
- Places work relative to prior literature: extends analyses of overlapped nuclear-norm approaches and fills a gap for latent denoising analysis; connects to structured sparsity and recent lower bounds.
- Duality lemma appears correct and is a useful nontrivial generalization; proofs follow standard M-estimator/decomposability techniques adapted to tensors.
- Gaussian λ choice is plausible via concentration of spectral norms and union bounds over modes.
- Analysis requires incoherence-type spectral cross-mode constraints (eq. 10) which are added to the estimator (eq. 11); this is a strong modeling choice with practical issues.
- Unclear whether experiments enforced the incoherence constraints; paper should clarify and discuss the impact of omitting them.
- Latent bounds adapt to min_k r_k (or Σ r̄_k via a specific decomposition), while overlapped bounds scale with averages of sqrt r_k; comparison mixes multilinear and component ranks and needs more discussion.
- No new algorithmic contribution; optimization uses existing ADMM implementations—paper should discuss computational scaling, convergence, and tractability of added constraints.
- Notational ambiguity: same S1/1 symbol used for both overlapped and latent norms; distinct notation is needed to avoid confusion.
- Proof sketches for the duality and key inequality should be in the main text rather than deferred to the supplement to improve readability.
- Some references are incomplete and should be fixed.
- Novelty: duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* is new; min-rank adaptation analysis for latent denoising fills a literature gap.
- Significance: clarifies when latent vs. overlapped regularization is preferable and has practical implications for heterogeneous-mode rank tensors.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to synthetic denoising without missing data; stronger impact would require tensor completion, real data, and comparisons to baselines (e.g., single-unfolding CV, Tucker/CP, t-SVD norms).
- Strengths: clear conceptual duality, interpretable theoretical guarantees, and empirical trends consistent with theory.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: justify incoherence constraints, state whether used in experiments, discuss soft penalties or bounds without constraints, disambiguate notation, expand experiments (completion, real data, baselines, sensitivity analyses), clarify constants and guidance for λ, and discuss identifiability of component decompositions.
- Decision rationale: lean toward acceptance due to novel duality and solid analysis, conditional on clarifying role of constraints, improving notation, and strengthening or discussing experiments; main cons are practical gap between analyzed and commonly used estimators and limited empirical scope.
