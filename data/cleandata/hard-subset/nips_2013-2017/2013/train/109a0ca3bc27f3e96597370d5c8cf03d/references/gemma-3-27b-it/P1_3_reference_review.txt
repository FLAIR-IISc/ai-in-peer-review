## Peer Review for "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a valuable theoretical analysis of the latent approach to convex tensor decomposition, providing insights into its superior performance over the overlapped approach, particularly in scenarios where the tensor is low-rank in only a single mode. The work is technically sound, clearly written, and builds upon recent advances in convex relaxation for tensor decomposition. While the theoretical results rely on assumptions (incoherence, noise model), they provide a strong justification for the observed empirical success of the latent approach. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper investigates two convex approaches to tensor decomposition using Schatten norm regularization: the "overlapped" approach, penalizing low rank across all modes, and the "latent" approach, decomposing the tensor into a mixture of low-rank tensors, each focused on a specific mode. The authors establish a crucial duality between the norms used in these approaches and provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating that the mean squared error of the latent approach scales with the *minimum* rank across all modes, explaining its superior performance when only one mode exhibits low-rank structure. The work correctly positions itself within the growing body of research on convex tensor decomposition (citations [9, 15, 23, 25, 26]) and effectively connects it to broader concepts of structured sparsity and group lasso techniques ([1, 2, 13, 17, 21]).

**2. Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the latent approach, filling a significant gap in the existing literature.  The established duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a novel and important contribution.
* **Clear Explanation of Performance Difference:** The analysis clearly explains *why* the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in specific scenarios (single-mode low-rankness). The scaling result (MSE scales with the minimum rank) is a key finding.
* **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is well-structured, with a clear progression of ideas.  The notation is consistent, and the concepts are explained in a relatively accessible manner.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments confirm the theoretical predictions, demonstrating the scaling behavior and highlighting the practical benefits of the latent approach.
* **Connection to Existing Literature:** The paper effectively draws connections to related work in structured sparsity and convex optimization, broadening its impact.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Assumptions:**  The theoretical results rely on assumptions like the incoherence condition (Equation 10) and a specific noise model (Gaussian). While common in theoretical analysis, the practicality of these assumptions in real-world applications could be discussed further.  The sensitivity of the results to violations of these assumptions should be acknowledged.
* **Computational Cost:**  The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the latent approach compared to the overlapped approach. While the theoretical performance is superior, the practical efficiency of the optimization algorithm could be a limiting factor.
* **Limited Scope of Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments focus on a relatively simple setting (Gaussian noise, no missing data).  Exploring the performance of the latent approach with more realistic noise distributions and in the presence of missing data would strengthen the empirical validation.
* **Lack of Discussion of Practical Implications:** The paper would benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the practical implications of the results. For instance, how can practitioners choose the regularization parameter effectively, given the theoretical bounds?



**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound.  The proofs are outlined (with details in the supplementary material), and the theoretical results seem well-supported. The analysis appears complete for the stated goals of the paper. The authors are careful in stating their assumptions.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is generally well-written and clear. However, some sections (e.g., the definition of the norms) could benefit from more intuitive explanations.  The notation is consistent, which enhances readability.
* **Originality:** The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is novel. The theoretical analysis of the latent approach's performance is also a significant contribution.
* **Significance:** The results are likely to be significant to researchers working on tensor decomposition and convex optimization. The insights gained from this work could lead to more effective algorithms for various applications, such as neuroimaging, environmental monitoring, and collaborative filtering.




**5. Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro Acceptance:**

* Provides a crucial theoretical understanding of the latent approach, explaining its empirical success.
* Novel duality result between Schatten norms.
* Strong empirical validation supporting the theoretical findings.
* Addresses an important problem in tensor decomposition.

**Con Acceptance:**

* Relies on potentially restrictive assumptions.
* Does not address computational complexity.
* Limited scope of empirical evaluation.

**6. Recommendation:**

**Accept with minor revisions.**  I recommend acceptance of this paper.  The authors should address the following points in a revision:

* **Discuss the limitations of the assumptions:** Acknowledge the potential impact of violating the incoherence condition and Gaussian noise assumption.
* **Briefly discuss computational complexity:** Compare the computational cost of the latent and overlapped approaches, even if a full analysis is not provided.
* **Expand the empirical evaluation:** Consider including experiments with different noise distributions or missing data.
* **Discuss practical implications:**  Provide guidance on choosing the regularization parameter based on the theoretical bounds.




Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. Addressing the minor revisions outlined above will further enhance its impact.
