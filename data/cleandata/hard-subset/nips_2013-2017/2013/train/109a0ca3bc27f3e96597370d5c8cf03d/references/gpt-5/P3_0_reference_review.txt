Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor estimation, contrasting two families of structured Schatten norm regularizers: (i) an “overlapped” approach that sums Schatten p-norms of mode-unfoldings (promoting simultaneous low-rank across modes), and (ii) a “latent” approach that decomposes a tensor into a sum of K components, each regularized to be low-rank in a specific mode. The authors formalize both via overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms, establish a duality between them (generalizing known dualities for overlapped/latent group lasso), and provide statistical guarantees for the latent approach in a denoising setting. The key insight is that the latent Schatten-1 approach achieves mean-squared error scaling with the minimal mode-k rank of the true tensor, whereas prior analysis for the overlapped approach [Tomioka et al., NIPS’11] yields dependence on the average of the square roots of the mode ranks. The paper provides: (a) a general consistency bound (Theorem 1), (b) a sharper deterministic bound under an incoherence-style spectral norm constraint across components (Theorem 2), and (c) high-probability Gaussian noise bounds with an explicit regularization choice (Theorem 3). Numerically, they confirm the scaling predictions and show that the latent approach can outperform the overlapped one, even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank.

This connects convex tensor methods [e.g., Liu et al., ICCV’09; Gandy et al., Inverse Problems’11; Signoretto et al.] with structured sparsity literature (overlapped vs latent group lasso) [Jenatton et al.; Obozinski et al.; Bach et al.], and contrasts with Tucker/HOSVD and ALS-based nonconvex methods [De Lathauwer et al.; Kolda & Bader]. The lower bound by Mu et al. suggests min-mode-rank dependence is fundamental, aligning with the latent approach’s guarantees.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity: The overlapped/latent norm duality is clean and general, bridging structured sparsity and tensor nuclear norms. Lemma 1 and its extension are useful beyond this paper.
- Sharp statistical insight: The latent estimator’s error scaling with min mode rank is compelling and explains empirical observations where only one mode is low-rank. Lemma 2 neatly captures the difference from the overlapped bound.
- Practical guidance: The λ choice in Theorem 3 depends only on dimensions, noise level, and K (plus α), not unknown ranks, aligning with the experiments.
- Empirical corroboration: Simulations show linear scaling with the appropriate complexity proxies and illustrate when the latent approach outperforms the overlapped one.

Weaknesses and points for improvement
- Incoherence constraints and estimator mismatch: Theorems 2–3 hinge on solving a constrained latent problem (Eq. 11) with ∥W(l)(k)∥S∞ ≤ α for l ≠ k. It is unclear whether these constraints are enforced in the experiments (the ADMM implementation in [25] typically solves the unconstrained latent formulation). If the constraints are not used, the presented bounds do not technically apply to the experimental estimator. Please clarify: (i) how α is chosen, (ii) how constraints are enforced algorithmically, and (iii) whether analogous bounds can be proved without explicit constraints under standard incoherence assumptions on W*.
- Limited experimental scope: Only fully observed denoising with a single noise level and synthetic data are considered. Given the paper’s motivation (missing data; collaborative filtering), a tensor completion experiment would strengthen the work. Including nonconvex baselines (Tucker/HOSVD with rank selection, CP/Parafac) would contextualize practical performance.
- Use of LR complexity: To compute the latent complexity proxy, you decompose the true noiseless W* with the latent method and take the minimum with mink rk. This is reasonable for probing scaling, but somewhat circular and arguably more favorable than the TR complexity (which uses known Tucker ranks from construction). Consider reporting results using only the singleton mink rk proxy or providing a principled upper bound on LR complexity that can be computed from known construction parameters.
- Clarity/notation: The symbol S1/1 is used for both overlapped and latent norms; this is potentially confusing. Consider distinct notation, e.g., |||W|||overlap,1 and |||W|||latent,1. Explicitly state when Frobenius norms of unfoldings equal the tensor Frobenius norm (used in Lemma 2 via ∥W(k)∥F = ∥W∥F). A brief proof sketch of Lemma 1 in the main text would help readers not consulting the appendix.
- Quantitative constants: The factors and constants in Theorem 3 (F and c) are not fully unpacked; it would help to articulate typical scaling (e.g., dominant √(N/nK)+√n1 behavior for the overlapped spectral norm of Gaussian unfoldings) and provide practical guidance for choosing λ without knowledge of σ (e.g., via MAD or CV).
- Related work breadth: While coverage is good for the time frame, consider noting other convex tensor norms (e.g., tubal nuclear norm/transform-based norms) and low-rank tensor formats (TT/Tucker/CP) to better position scope and limitations, even if not directly comparable.

Quality
The technical development appears sound. The duality and norm inequalities are natural and well-motivated. The deterministic and probabilistic analyses are plausible, though their reliance on the spectral norm constraints deserves clearer justification and practical discussion. Experiments support the theory qualitatively but are limited.

Clarity
Generally well-written and organized. Strengthen notational disambiguation, explain the role of α and constraint handling, and provide a short intuition/proof sketch for the main duality.

Originality
The paper brings a novel and useful duality for structured Schatten norms and provides, to my knowledge, the first denoising guarantees for the latent tensor nuclear norm with min-mode-rank dependence, complementing earlier results for the overlapped norm.

Significance
The results are significant for convex tensor estimation, offering both conceptual clarity and practical guidance on when to prefer latent over overlapped formulations. The min-mode-rank dependence aligns with lower bounds, suggesting the approach is near-optimal in relevant regimes.

Arguments for acceptance
- Clear theoretical contribution (duality + sharp bounds) with practical implications.
- Addresses an important gap in understanding latent convex tensor decompositions.
- Empirical evidence, albeit limited, aligns with theory.

Arguments against acceptance
- Mismatch between analyzed constrained estimator and implemented method is not clarified.
- Experimental evaluation is narrow; lacks completion and real data, and broader baselines.
- Some notational confusion and missing constant guidance.

Recommendation
Lean toward acceptance, contingent on clarifying the estimator/constraints alignment and improving clarity. Adding a brief tensor completion experiment or stronger discussion on applicability would further strengthen the paper.
