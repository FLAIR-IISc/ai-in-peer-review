- Overall assessment: Valuable, technically sound theoretical analysis of the latent convex tensor decomposition approach; recommend accept with minor revisions.

- Main idea: The paper analyzes the latent approach to convex tensor decomposition, proving consistency and denoising guarantees and explaining its advantage over the overlapped approach when tensors are low-rank in only one mode.

- Novelty: Establishes a duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, extending prior structured-sparsity results to tensor decomposition.

- Theoretical contribution: Provides rigorous consistency results and a deterministic error bound that scales with the minimal mode-k rank.

- Empirical support: Numerical experiments corroborate theoretical scaling and show improved performance of the latent approach.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on convex Schatten-norm relaxations and connects to group lasso and structured sparsity literature.

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation with proofs and performance bounds previously lacking in the literature.

- Strength: Clear explanation of when and why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach.

- Strength: Novel duality result linking two regularization strategies.

- Strength: Well-written, organized presentation and thorough related-work discussion.

- Weakness: Deterministic bound requires a strong incoherence assumption (Eq. 10); sensitivity to violations is not fully explored.

- Weakness: Implementation details of the ADMM solver are sparse, limiting reproducibility.

- Weakness: Experiments are limited mainly to Gaussian noise; robustness to other noise types or missing data is not evaluated.

- Weakness: Notation is dense in places (notably Section 2.2) and could be clarified.

- NIPS criteria — Quality: 8/10; technically sound and empirically supported, but assumption sensitivity could be strengthened.

- NIPS criteria — Clarity: 7/10; generally clear but notation density hampers readability in parts.

- NIPS criteria — Originality: 8/10; extends existing work with novel theoretical analysis and duality.

- NIPS criteria — Significance: 9/10; provides important theoretical justification for an empirically successful approach.

- Pros for acceptance: Rigorous theory, novel duality, empirical validation, clear writing, addresses important problem.

- Cons against acceptance: Strong incoherence assumption, limited experimental scope, dense notation.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions including sensitivity discussion for the incoherence assumption, more ADMM implementation details, expanded experiments (other noise/missing data), and notation clarifications.
