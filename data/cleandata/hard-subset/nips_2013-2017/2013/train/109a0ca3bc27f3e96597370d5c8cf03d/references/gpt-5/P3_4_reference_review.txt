Summary
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor estimation via structured Schatten norms. Two families are considered: (i) overlapped norms that penalize all mode-wise matricizations simultaneously (sum or ℓq of Schatten-p norms across modes), and (ii) latent norms that represent a tensor as a sum of components, each low-rank in a designated mode, and regularize by the sum of nuclear norms of the corresponding unfoldings. The authors establish a duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q-norm is the latent Sp*/q*-norm (an infimal convolution across modes), generalizing analogous results in latent/overlapped group lasso. They analyze the statistical performance of the latent approach in a denoising setting. A basic consistency bound scales with √mink nk, while sharper deterministic and Gaussian bounds are derived under an incoherence-like assumption and additional spectral-norm constraints that control cross-mode correlations. The key insight is that latent regularization yields error dependence on mink rk (minimum mode-k rank) rather than on averages across modes, thereby explaining empirical advantages over the overlapped approach. Synthetic experiments corroborate the predicted scaling.

Quality
- Theory: The duality lemma is natural and well-argued, tying the tensor norms to known structured sparsity dualities. The deterministic and probabilistic error bounds are carefully developed using standard convex-regularization techniques (dual norm control, decomposability, tangent space arguments a la Negahban et al./Agarwal et al.). The contrast between latent and overlapped rates is clear and insightful.
- Assumptions and sharpness: The stronger results require (a) an incoherence-style condition (10) and (b) additional spectral norm constraints in the estimator (11) that bound off-mode unfoldings of each component. This raises two concerns:
  1) Practical relevance: It is not clear if these constraints are enforced in experiments; the code cited [25] appears to solve the unconstrained latent problem. The choice of α and whether constraints are active materially affect the λ condition and constants in Theorems 2–3. Please clarify if (11) was actually solved; if not, provide analogous theory for the unconstrained estimator or argue constraints are inactive with α → ∞.
  2) Tightness: λ scaling includes terms like √(N/nK), which can be very large for high-order tensors; while this matches operator-norm behavior of mode-k matricizations, it suggests bounds may be conservative. A discussion of potential improvements via Gaussian width of descent cones or mode-adaptive tuning could help.
- Experiments: The synthetic study is clean and demonstrates linear scaling versus the proposed complexity measures. However, LR complexity relies on a decomposition of the noiseless W* obtained by running the latent solver, which “peeks” at the ground truth to define the x-axis; this is acceptable for validating scaling but should be acknowledged as such (it is). Comparisons to nonconvex Tucker/CP baselines and to missing-data (tensor completion) would strengthen the practical message. Real-data examples (video, neuroimaging) would further increase impact.

Clarity
The paper is generally well-written and organized. Definitions of norms and unfoldings are clear. The intuition behind the latent formulation as infimal convolution across modes and its dual relation to max of spectral norms is helpful. A few suggestions:
- Make explicit early that the latent S1/1 norm is an infimal convolution of nuclear norms across modes, and connect to latent group lasso via a short illustrative example.
- Clearly separate the unconstrained latent estimator (5)/(6) from the constrained version (11), and state which is analyzed where, and which one is used in experiments. Provide guidance on setting α.
- Provide more detail on the ADMM implementation used and its computational complexity; briefly discuss scalability with K and dimensions.

Originality
The duality result for overlapped/latent Sp/q norms on tensors appears novel and crisply generalizes structured sparsity dualities. The statistical analysis of the latent tensor norm (with error depending on mink rk) fills a gap: prior work gave analysis for the overlapped norm [26] and introduced the latent approach [25] without theory. The link to lower bounds in Mu et al. is well-positioned.

Significance
Understanding when latent convex tensor decompositions outperform overlapped ones is important both theoretically and practically. The min-rank dependence is a compelling story that aligns with observed behavior and should influence the design of convex relaxations in tensor problems. If the mismatch between theory (constrained estimator) and experiments (likely unconstrained) is resolved, this will be a solid contribution.

Pros
- Clear and useful duality between overlapped and latent tensor norms.
- New statistical guarantees for the latent approach with favorable min-rank dependence.
- Insightful comparison to overlapped approach; theory explains empirical phenomena.
- Clean synthetic validations of predicted scaling.

Cons
- The main sharp bounds hinge on added spectral-norm constraints; unclear if used in practice/experiments.
- Regularization level λ scales with √(N/nK) and α, potentially yielding conservative rates; discussion on tightness is limited.
- Empirical evaluation is synthetic only; LR complexity requires oracle-like decomposition for axis construction.
- Limited comparison to strong nonconvex baselines and to completion settings where these methods are often applied.

Questions for authors
1) Did you enforce the spectral-norm constraints in (11) in experiments? If not, can you provide bounds for the unconstrained estimator or justify that constraints are inactive?
2) How should α be chosen in practice? Is there a data-driven way to estimate it?
3) Can you comment on computational complexity and scalability relative to the overlapped approach?
4) Do you anticipate analogous min-rank dependence in tensor completion under standard sampling models?

Minor comments
- Clarify the definition of Sp/q: p acts on singular values within each mode’s unfolding; q aggregates across modes.
- Fix small typos and improve citation formatting; ensure all references include venues.
- Consider visualizing a toy example illustrating when overlapped fails and latent succeeds.

Recommendation
Lean accept, contingent on clarifying the role and implementation of the spectral-norm constraints and, ideally, adding a note or appendix covering the unconstrained case. The conceptual clarity, duality, and min-rank guarantees are valuable.
