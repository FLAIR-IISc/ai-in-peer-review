Summary:
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor estimation, contrasting “overlapped” Schatten norms (sum of nuclear norms of mode unfoldings) with a “latent” formulation that decomposes the tensor into a sum of components, each low-rank in a specific mode. The authors:
- Formalize overlapped Sp/q norms and introduce the corresponding latent Sp*/q* norms via a duality result (Lemma 1).
- Establish a key inequality for the latent S1/1 norm that scales with the minimal mode rank (Lemma 2).
- Provide consistency (Theorem 1), a deterministic error bound under incoherence constraints (Theorem 2), and a Gaussian-noise bound with a prescribed λ (Theorem 3).
- Compare to prior bounds for overlapped norms [26], arguing the latent approach scales with min mode rank rather than an average of ranks, explaining empirical gains.
- Present synthetic experiments showing MSE scaling consistent with their theory and often favoring the latent approach.

Relation to prior work:
The work connects convex tensor priors [9,15,23,25,26] to structured sparsity (group lasso with overlaps vs latent group lasso) [1,12,13,17,21]. The duality result is a natural tensor analogue of known overlapped/latent group norm dualities. The analysis follows the decomposable regularizer framework of Agarwal/Negahban/Wainwright and related Gaussian width methods. The comparison to lower bounds in Mu et al. [19] is noted.

Strengths:
- Clear conceptual link between overlapped and latent formulations with a clean duality statement.
- The Lemma 2 inequality capturing dependence on min mode rank gives a simple, intuitive reason for when latent should win.
- Deterministic and stochastic error bounds that explicitly display rank and dimension dependencies.
- Empirical scaling plots roughly consistent with the theoretical predictions.
- Useful synthesis of tensor nuclear-norm regularization and structured sparsity theory.

Weaknesses and concerns:
Major clarity/notation issues:
- The same symbol ||·||S1/1 is used for both the overlapped norm (sum of nuclear norms of unfoldings; Eq. (1)) and the latent norm defined via an infimal convolution over decompositions (Eq. (6)). This is extremely confusing. Please use distinct notation, e.g., ||·||over,S1/1 and ||·||lat,S1/1.
- Eq. (3) appears incorrect: it states ||W||S1/1 ≤ K ∑k sqrt(rk) ||W||F. Standard bounds give ||W(k)||* ≤ sqrt(rk) ||W||F and hence ∑k ||W(k)||* ≤ ∑k sqrt(rk) ||W||F without the extra leading K. Please check constants carefully.
- Eq. (11) appears malformed (“( 1 … + λ … )”); likely 1/2 is missing, and parentheses don’t match.
- In Sec. 3.2, r̄k denotes the rank of the kth latent component, but Theorem 2’s inequality (12) sums “rk” (multilinear ranks?) not r̄k; notation seems inconsistent.
- Several typos, e.g., “betters,” “prepint,” “Prof. ICCV,” inconsistent “overlap/overlapped,” and inconsistent capitalization in references.

Methodological mismatches:
- The deterministic and Gaussian bounds (Theorems 2–3) hinge on additional spectral-norm incoherence constraints between components (Eq. (10), constraints in (11)) and on λ ≥ 2||E||S∞/∞ + α(K−1). However, the experimental section does not indicate these constraints were enforced (the referenced ADMM solver [25] typically solves the unconstrained latent formulation). This makes the comparison between theory and experiments questionable.
- The prescribed λ in Theorem 3 includes terms like 2σ(√(N/nK)+√n1+√(2 log(K/δ))) + α(K−1). The √(N/nK) term can be huge for moderate K and dimensions, making λ (and bounds) very conservative; guidance on practical tuning and the role/choice of α is lacking.

Experimental design and reporting:
- The LR complexity (Eq. (18)) depends on the ranks of latent factors r̄k, which are not known; the paper estimates them by running the latent method on the noiseless tensor and taking a minimum with min rk. This is a circular proxy and can bias the scaling check. Provide a principled estimator-independent surrogate, or assess robustness to this choice.
- Only synthetic denoising is considered; given the paper’s motivation (neuroimaging, environmental monitoring, CF), at least one real-data or missing-data (tensor completion) experiment would strengthen the case.
- Fairness of comparisons: only three representative λ per method are plotted; a more systematic λ-sweep with model selection (e.g., cross-validation) would be more convincing.
- No runtime or convergence details. ADMM parameter choices, stopping criteria, and complexity vs overlapped baselines are not discussed.

Scope and completeness:
- Many proofs are deferred to supplementary; in several places the main text relies on nontrivial steps (e.g., Lemma 1, Lemma 2). Given space constraints this is acceptable, but the main text should at least outline the core ideas (e.g., Fenchel dual derivation for Lemma 1; key decomposability steps).
- Discussion of related tensor norms is missing (e.g., weighted overlapped nuclear norms, tubal nuclear norm/TT/TNN formulations), and how the latent approach compares empirically/theoretically to those.

Policy violation:
- The paper includes a GitHub link to the solver, which is a clear breach of double-blind review. This must be removed.

Minor issues and suggestions:
- Provide explicit constants or at least clarify which are universal, which depend on α, and which on K.
- Clarify whether the singleton decomposition argument in (13)/(15) always yields the stated dependence on mink rk without worsening constants.
- Explain how to set α in practice and its effect on identifiability and recovery.
- Clean and complete references (years, venues), unify notation for unfoldings W(k) vs W(k)(k), and avoid ambiguous superscripts/subscripts.

Evaluation:
- Quality: The core ideas are sound and potentially impactful, but there are multiple mathematical typos/notation issues, a mismatch between the analyzed estimator and the implemented one, and very conservative bounds with limited practical guidance.
- Clarity: Weaker than NeurIPS standards due to notation reuse, typos, and missing details.
- Originality: Moderate; the duality is a natural extension; the latent-norm analysis for tensors with min-rank dependence is interesting.
- Significance: Potentially significant for tensor denoising, but empirical evidence is limited and theory-practice alignment needs fixes.

Pros:
- Conceptually neat duality between overlapped and latent tensor norms.
- Intuitive inequality (Lemma 2) and min-rank dependence.
- Some empirical support for the claimed scaling.

Cons:
- Notational confusion and mathematical/typographical errors (Eq. (3), (11), r vs r̄).
- Theoretical assumptions (constraints, λ) not matched in experiments; unclear choice of α.
- Conservative λ and bounds; limited practical guidance.
- Double-blind violation; incomplete references; limited experiments.

Recommendation: Reject. The contributions are promising, but the paper in its current form has too many issues (clarity, correctness/typos, theory-experiment mismatch, policy violation). I encourage the authors to fix notation and errors, align the estimator analyzed with the one implemented (or enforce the constraints), provide clearer guidance on α/λ, broaden experiments (including completion/real data), and resubmit.
