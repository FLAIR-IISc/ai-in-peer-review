- Paper studies convex low-rank tensor decompositions using overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms and proves duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms.
- Main technical result: latent Schatten-1 regularization yields denoising error bounds scaling with the minimum mode-k rank (min_k rk), improving over overlapped approaches.
- Provides three types of bounds: consistency via overlapped S∞/∞ noise norm, deterministic bound under an incoherence-like spectral constraint (α), and a high-probability Gaussian-noise bound with a chosen λ.
- Empirical synthetic denoising experiments confirm predicted scaling and show latent regularization outperforms overlapped norms, even when tensor is low-rank across modes.
- Relates to prior convex tensor regularization work (Liu, Signoretto, Tomioka) and extends latent/overlapped duality analogies from structured sparsity literature.
- Strength: clear conceptual contribution defining latent tensor Schatten norms and proving their duality to overlapped norms.
- Strength: theoretical advances including error bounds that explain empirical advantages and explicit λ choices for Gaussian noise.
- Strength: useful technical lemmas (e.g., latent norm ≤ min_k sqrt(rk) × Frobenius norm) supporting singleton decomposition intuition.
- Strength: experiments corroborate theory and show improved MSE for latent approach.
- Weakness: deterministic and probabilistic bounds require additional spectral-norm constraints (incoherence α) and λ ≥ 2||E||S∞/∞ + α(K−1), with unclear practical selection/implementation of α.
- Weakness: constants (F, c) unspecified and dimensional dependence in λ may be loose; tightness and lower bounds for latent norms are not discussed.
- Weakness: limited experimental scope — only fully observed denoising, modest sizes, no completion, outliers, real data, or nonconvex baselines (ALS, Tucker/CP) comparisons.
- Weakness: complexity plots use oracle decomposition from noiseless W*, which could bias interpretation and should be more explicitly caveated.
- Weakness: notation could be clearer by using distinct symbols for overlapped and latent S1/1 norms to avoid confusion.
- Quality: results appear technically sound and align with known convex-analytic constructions; proofs are in supplement but a proof sketch in main text would aid readability.
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized, but should state how constraints in (11) are implemented and how α is set in experiments.
- Originality: extension of overlapped/latent duality to Schatten norms and statistical analysis of latent tensor norm is novel and meaningful.
- Significance: min_k rk scaling is an interpretable, potentially influential result for multiway data and tensor regularization practice.
- Suggestion: discuss practical α selection and include ablations showing sensitivity to α.
- Suggestion: expand experiments to tensor completion, compare to ALS/Tucker/CP and weighted overlapped norms, and report runtime/memory.
- Suggestion: replace oracle LR complexity with a measurable proxy or show robustness to estimated LR values.
- Suggestion: consider adaptive/weighted latent penalties and discuss theoretical extension.
- Minor: fix typos, consistent references, footnote markers, and equation numbering for readability.
- Pros for acceptance: solid novel theory, elegant duality, and consistent empirical results.
- Cons for acceptance: limited experiments, under-explained spectral constraints/α dependence, and looseness in constants/dimensional dependence.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful theoretical contribution with promising empirical support, but needs broader experiments and clearer assumptions.
