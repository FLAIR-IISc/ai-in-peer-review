- Compares overlapped vs latent Schatten-norm approaches for convex tensor decomposition and establishes a duality between them.  
- Shows latent approach’s MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank (not the average), explaining its empirical superiority.  
- Provides Lemma 1 (duality) and Theorems 1–3 with rigorous, tight theoretical guarantees; proofs in the supplement.  
- Experiments confirm theoretical predictions, showing linear MSE scaling with the LR complexity and latent ≥ overlapped performance even when tensor is low-rank in all modes.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; precise mathematical definitions; some technical proofs in appendices could be more accessible.  
- Original contribution: generalizes structured sparsity duality to Schatten norms and reveals the minimum-rank dependence of latent error.  
- Significant practical implications: tighter guarantees for convex tensor decomposition and relevance to applications like neuroimaging and collaborative filtering.  
- Strengths: rigorous analysis, clear explanation of duality, theoretical results that match experiments, and connection to structured sparsity literature.  
- Weaknesses: limited experimental variety (only two tensor sizes), no computational complexity comparison, no comparison to non-convex methods, and limited guidance on choosing regularization parameters.  
- Recommendation: Accept — substantial theoretical contributions with supporting experiments despite limited empirical scope.
