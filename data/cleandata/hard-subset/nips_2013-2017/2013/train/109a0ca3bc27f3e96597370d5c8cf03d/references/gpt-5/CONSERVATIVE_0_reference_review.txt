Summary
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor decomposition based on structured Schatten norms. It contrasts two families: (i) overlapped norms that penalize all mode unfoldings simultaneously (sum of nuclear norms), and (ii) latent norms that decompose the tensor into a sum of components, each low-rank in one mode, and penalize the nuclear norm of each component’s matched unfolding. The authors establish a duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms (via an infimal convolution formulation), prove a basic consistency bound for latent S1/1-regularized denoising, and derive deterministic and Gaussian-noise error bounds under an incoherence-style assumption. A key qualitative message is that the latent approach achieves error scaling with the minimum mode rank of the ground truth, whereas the overlapped approach scales with an average of mode ranks. Experiments on synthetic 3-way tensors corroborate the predicted scaling and often show better MSE for the latent formulation.

Relation to prior work
This builds on convex tensor completion/denoising via overlapped nuclear norms [Gandy et al., Liu et al., Tomioka et al.], and connects to “latent group lasso” vs overlapped group lasso [Obozinski et al., Jenatton et al.]. The analysis borrows techniques for decomposable regularizers [Negahban et al.; Agarwal et al.]. The empirical comparison references the statistical bound for the overlapped norm from Tomioka et al. [NIPS’11] and a lower bound from Mu et al.

Strengths
- Clear conceptual link between overlapped and latent tensor norms, with an explicit duality that mirrors known structured sparsity dualities.
- Useful inequality (Lemma 2) showing latent S1/1 bounded by sqrt(min mode-rank) times Frobenius norm, aligning with the intuition that latent favors tensors low-rank in a single mode.
- Theoretical results (consistency, deterministic, and Gaussian bounds) indicate error rates that depend on the minimum mode-rank, offering a principled explanation for empirical behavior reported in previous work.
- Experiments illustrate linear scaling of MSE with their latent-rank complexity measure and show cases where latent outperforms overlapped.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Estimator mismatch in theory vs practice: The key deterministic and Gaussian bounds are for a constrained estimator (equation (11)) that enforces spectral norm constraints ∥W(l)(k)∥S∞ ≤ α for l ≠ k. This is a nonstandard addition not present in the original latent formulation (equation (5)). It is unclear if these constraints are used in experiments (likely not). Thus, the main bounds may not apply to the algorithm actually evaluated.
- Practicality of the α constraints: α is a property of the unknown components; selecting a valid α in practice is nontrivial. The theory depends on α and includes it in λ; experiments tune λ by grid search, not by the prescribed formula, reducing the practical guidance of the theory.
- Assumptions and scope: The deterministic result assumes W* decomposes into K components, each low-rank in a distinct mode. This latent structural assumption is much stronger than simply having low multilinear rank and may not hold generally.
- Empirical evaluation is limited: only synthetic Gaussian denoising, two small sizes, and only overlapped vs latent baselines. No comparisons to widely used nonconvex tensor decompositions (Tucker/HOSVD truncation, CP-ALS with model selection), no real data, and no missing data/tensor completion—where convex approaches are often motivated.
- Complexity measures used in plots require oracle information: LR complexity uses ranks of latent factors obtained by running the latent method on the noiseless tensor, which is not available in practice. This makes the scaling plots less predictive for new instances.
- Notation/clarity issues: The same symbol S1/1 appears to denote both overlapped (eq. 1) and latent (eq. 6) norms; this is confusing. The paper should use distinct notation (e.g., ∥·∥overlap,S1/1 and ∥·∥latent,S1/1).
- Novelty of the duality result seems incremental relative to established infimal convolution dualities in structured sparsity; the contribution here is mostly adapting known ideas to tensor Schatten norms.

Quality
The analysis appears technically careful at a high level, but its practical relevance is weakened by the added constraints and by lack of alignment with the evaluated algorithm. Bounds depend on unknown α and σ; the recommended λ is not used to choose hyperparameters. Proofs are deferred to the supplement; key ideas (e.g., Lemma 1’s duality) are plausible but deserve more detail in the main text.

Clarity
Generally well written and organized, but notation conflicts and the estimator mismatch should be clarified. Assumptions should be stated more transparently, and the role of α and whether constraints are used in experiments should be explicit.

Originality
Moderate. The latent formulation itself is from prior work; the duality and bounds are natural extensions of structured sparsity theory to tensor Schatten norms. The inequality tying latent norm to min mode-rank is a nice, albeit expected, technical piece.

Significance
Potentially useful theoretically, but the empirical evidence is too narrow, and the theoretical estimator deviates from practice. The impact would be stronger with experiments on completion, real datasets, and broader baselines, and with theory for the unconstrained latent estimator.

Questions for the authors
- Are the spectral norm constraints in (11) enforced in your experiments? If not, can you provide theory for the unconstrained latent estimator?
- How should practitioners choose α? How sensitive are results to α mis-specification?
- Can you compare against HOSVD/Tucker truncation and CP-ALS with rank selection, and on real-world tensors and completion tasks?
- Please disambiguate notation for overlapped vs latent S1/1.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear and useful theoretical perspective on latent vs overlapped tensor norms.
- Bounds that illuminate when latent can outperform overlapped.
- Inequalities and duality may interest researchers working on structured convex estimation.

Cons for acceptance
- Main bounds rely on additional constraints not reflected in experiments.
- Limited empirical validation and missing key baselines and tasks.
- Notation issues and incremental novelty of duality.

Recommendation
Weak reject. The paper has solid theoretical ingredients but needs clearer alignment between the estimator analyzed and the one used, stronger and broader empirical validation, and improved clarity. Strengthening these aspects could make it a strong submission in a future iteration.

Reviewer confidence
High on the methodological concerns; moderate on technical details deferred to the supplement.
