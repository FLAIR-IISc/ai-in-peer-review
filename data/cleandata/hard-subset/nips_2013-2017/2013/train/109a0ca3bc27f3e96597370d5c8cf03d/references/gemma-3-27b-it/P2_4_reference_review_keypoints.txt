- Paper compares convex tensor decomposition approaches (overlapped vs latent) using structured Schatten norm regularization.  
- Establishes a duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms.  
- Provides theoretical justification that the latent approach’s error bound scales with the minimum mode rank.  
- Shows overlapped approach’s error bound depends on the average mode rank.  
- Explains why latent approach empirically outperforms overlapped, especially when tensor is low-rank in one mode.  
- Numerical experiments corroborate theoretical findings and scaling behavior.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous analysis and novel duality result.  
- Clear connection to existing literature in tensor decomposition, convex optimization, and structured sparsity.  
- Well-written, well-organized, and accessible mathematical presentation.  
- Addresses an important problem with potential impact on future research.  
- Theoretical results rely on assumptions (e.g., incoherence) whose practical implications aren’t fully discussed.  
- Paper omits detailed discussion of computational complexity and practical ADMM challenges for large-scale tensors.  
- Numerical experiments are limited to relatively small tensors and lack larger, realistic datasets.  
- Tensor completion is only briefly mentioned; differences from noisy decomposition aren’t explored in depth.  
- Overall evaluation: quality, clarity, originality, and significance judged strong.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
