- Paper compares overlapped Schatten Sp/q norms (sum of mode unfoldings' nuclear norms) with latent Schatten norms (infimal convolution over mode-specific low-rank components) for tensor decomposition and denoising.  
- Establishes a duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms, generalizing latent/overlapped group sparsity dualities.  
- Derives norm inequalities relating these tensor norms to the Frobenius norm (Lemmas 1–2).  
- Provides statistical analysis for the latent S1/1-regularized estimator in Gaussian noise, proving consistency and denoising error bounds.  
- Key theoretical message: latent estimator error scales with min_k r_k (minimum mode rank) rather than average ranks, explaining empirical benefits when one mode is low-rank.  
- Synthetic experiments validate predicted linear scaling and often show lower MSE for the latent approach.  
- Situates work within convex tensor recovery literature and fills a gap by giving rigorous statistical analysis for the latent approach.  
- Novelty: duality result and statistical analysis for latent tensor norms are new and natural extensions of structured sparsity results.  
- Strength: clear conceptual framing and precise identification of latent norm as an infimal convolution.  
- Strength: theoretical contributions include explicit λ choice, dimension dependence, and useful inequalities.  
- Strength/insight: quantitatively explains when latent formulations outperform overlapped ones.  
- Empirics strength: synthetic validation across regimes demonstrates theoretical scaling.  
- Major weakness: main denoising bound requires additional spectral-norm constraints with unknown α, and paper lacks guidance on setting or impact of α misspecification.  
- Weakness: latent decomposition non-unique, limiting interpretability/identifiability; no discussion of whether algorithms produce singleton solutions.  
- Weakness: some bounds appear loose or with opaque constants (e.g., Theorem 1 and factor F in Theorem 3) and could be contextualized or tightened.  
- Weakness: fairness of comparisons limited—other overlapped weightings, S1/∞ variants, nonconvex methods, and recent tensor norms are not empirically compared.  
- Weakness: experiments restricted to synthetic full-observation denoising; no tensor completion or real-data evaluations and LR complexity uses unavailable ground-truth decomposition.  
- Clarity issues: reuse of S1/1 notation for both overlapped and latent norms is confusing; some typos and reference formatting problems.  
- Quality: technically sound, uses established convex-analytic tools; would benefit from brief main-text proof sketches and addressing incoherence constraints.  
- Suggestions: provide guidance or remove hard α-constraints, analyze unconstrained/penalized latent estimator, include sensitivity analysis for α and λ, add real-data/completion experiments and broader baselines, clarify notation, and discuss computational aspects.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear duality result, new latent theory, favorable min-rank dependence supported by experiments.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on unknown constraints (α), narrow empirical evaluation, and potentially limited/tightness of bounds.  
- Recommendation: weak accept—conceptual and theoretical contributions outweigh empirical limitations.
