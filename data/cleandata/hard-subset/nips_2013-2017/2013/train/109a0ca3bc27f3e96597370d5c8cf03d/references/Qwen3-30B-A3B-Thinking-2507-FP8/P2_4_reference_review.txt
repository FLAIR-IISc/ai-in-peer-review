

# Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper introduces a novel framework for convex tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, specifically comparing two approaches: the "overlapped" approach (penalizing all modes to be simultaneously low-rank) and the "latent" approach (decomposing the tensor into a mixture of components each low-rank in a specific mode). The authors establish a duality between these two approaches, prove theoretical guarantees for the latent approach showing its error scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank rather than the average mode-k rank, and demonstrate empirically that the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in many cases.

The paper builds on previous work by [25, 26] on convex tensor decomposition but provides crucial theoretical insights that were previously missing. The authors connect their work to structured sparsity literature [2, 13, 17, 21], showing that the overlapped/latent Schatten norms generalize the overlapped/latent group sparsity norms. This connection provides a broader theoretical foundation for their approach.

## Quality

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with rigorous theoretical analysis. The authors provide clear proofs for their key lemmas and theorems (Theorems 1-3), establishing the consistency and denoising performance of the latent approach. The theoretical results are well-motivated and build logically on previous work in tensor decomposition and structured sparsity. The experimental section confirms the theoretical predictions with numerical simulations on synthetic data, showing that the MSE scales linearly with the predicted complexity measures (TR complexity for overlapped, LR complexity for latent). 

The paper acknowledges limitations appropriately, noting that their incoherence assumptions (10) are similar to those in previous work [1, 3, 11]. The theoretical guarantees are carefully stated with precise conditions, and the authors are honest about the scenarios where the overlapped approach might still be preferable (e.g., when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes).

## Clarity

The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written. The introduction effectively sets up the problem, explains the limitations of previous work, and clearly states the contributions. Definitions of key concepts (overlapped vs. latent Schatten norms) are provided early and consistently used throughout. The paper's structure (sections on definitions, theoretical results, experiments) follows a logical flow that makes it easy to follow the arguments.

The figures (particularly Figure 2) are well-designed and effectively illustrate the theoretical predictions. The notation is consistent and well-explained. The supplementary material provides additional technical details for those interested in the proofs.

## Originality

The paper makes several significant original contributions. The most important is the duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms (Lemma 1), which generalizes results from structured sparsity literature. The key theoretical insight that the latent approach's error scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank (Theorem 2) rather than the average mode-k rank (as in the overlapped approach) is novel and explains the empirical superiority of the latent approach. This represents a meaningful advance over previous work [25, 26], which focused on noiseless tensor completion.

The paper clearly differentiates itself from prior work by explicitly connecting to structured sparsity literature and providing a unified framework for both approaches. The connection to the latent group lasso [1, 12] is particularly insightful.

## Significance

The results have significant practical implications for tensor decomposition applications. By providing theoretical justification for the empirical superiority of the latent approach, the paper helps guide practitioners toward more effective methods for tensor decomposition. The finding that the latent approach "performs as if it knows the mode with the minimum rank" is particularly valuable for real-world applications where the rank structure may be unknown.

The work advances the state of the art in convex tensor decomposition by resolving an open question about why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach. The theoretical insights could also be extended to other tensor-related problems, such as tensor completion with missing values.

## Weaknesses

While strong overall, the paper has a few limitations that could be addressed. The computational complexity of the latent approach compared to the overlapped approach is not discussed, which is important for practical implementation. The experiments are limited to small synthetic tensors (50×50×20 and 80×80×40) without testing on real-world datasets or larger tensors, which would strengthen the practical relevance. The paper assumes Gaussian noise in the theoretical analysis, which might not cover all practical scenarios. The paper also doesn't provide clear guidance for choosing the regularization constant λ in practice beyond the theoretical bounds.

## Overall Assessment

This is a high-quality paper that makes significant theoretical contributions to the field of convex tensor decomposition. The theoretical results are rigorous and well-motivated, and they provide a clear explanation for empirical observations that were previously unexplained. The paper is well-written, well-organized, and makes a meaningful contribution to the field.

The main weakness is the limited experimental validation on realistic datasets, but this doesn't undermine the strength of the theoretical contributions. The paper's theoretical insights are valuable regardless of the specific experimental setting.

## Recommendation

**Strengths for acceptance:**
- Strong theoretical contributions with clear duality result
- Rigorous proofs establishing the error scaling with minimum mode-k rank
- Clear explanation for empirical superiority of latent approach
- Connection to broader structured sparsity literature
- Well-written and well-organized paper

**Weaknesses against acceptance:**
- Limited experimental validation on real-world data
- No discussion of computational complexity
- Assumption of Gaussian noise in theoretical analysis

The paper makes a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of convex tensor decomposition. The insights provided will be valuable to both researchers and practitioners in the field. The theoretical results are compelling and well-supported, and the paper advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.
