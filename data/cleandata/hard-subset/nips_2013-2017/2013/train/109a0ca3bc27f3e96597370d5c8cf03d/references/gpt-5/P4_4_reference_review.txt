Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies convex formulations for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms. Two families are considered:

- Overlapped norms: penalize the sum (or Sp/q aggregation) of Schatten norms of all mode unfoldings, encouraging a tensor to be simultaneously low-rank across modes (prior works include Liu et al., Gandy et al., Tomioka et al. 2011–2012, and analysis by Tomioka et al. 2013).

- Latent norms: decompose the tensor into a sum of components, each regularized to be low-rank in a specific mode (proposed empirically by Tomioka et al. 2011/2012, but without generalization theory).

The paper’s main contributions are:
1) A duality result: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q-norm equals a latent Sp*/q*-norm (with p* and q* conjugates), generalizing dualities known in structured sparsity (e.g., latent/overlapping group lasso).
2) Statistical analysis for the latent approach in a denoising setting with full observations:
   - Consistency bound in Frobenius norm with λ chosen by the dual norm of noise.
   - Deterministic error bounds for each latent component under an incoherence-type constraint on cross-mode spectral norms, yielding an overall error scaling with the minimum mode-k rank of the ground truth.
   - Gaussian noise guarantees with λ depending only on noise level and dimensions (not on ranks), and MSE scaling with the sum of latent ranks or, via a singleton decomposition argument, with the minimum mode-k rank.
3) A comparison to the prior overlapped approach bound (Tomioka et al. 2013), which scales with the average of square roots of mode ranks; the latent approach scales with the minimum mode rank, explaining observed empirical advantages when one mode is significantly lower rank.
4) Experiments on synthetic tensors showing (i) linear MSE scaling with the predicted latent complexity measure and (ii) relative improvement of the latent approach that correlates with the ratio of overlapped to latent complexity.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The convex duality between overlapped and latent Sp/q norms appears correct and aligns with established structured sparsity dualities. The consistency and deterministic bounds follow standard decomposable-regularizer techniques (Agarwal/Negahban/Wainwright), adapted to tensor unfoldings and the latent decomposition. The Gaussian-noise bounds provide explicit dimension/noise dependencies and rank scalings.
- Assumptions: The deterministic and probabilistic bounds rely on additional spectral norm constraints on cross-mode unfoldings of each component (incoherence proxy). This modifies the estimator relative to the unconstrained latent formulation and raises questions about setting α and ensuring feasibility/practical implementation. The paper references similar constraints in “dirty models,” but practical guidance and empirical validation with the constrained solver are limited/unclear.
- Empirical evaluation: Synthetic experiments confirm scaling trends and dimension dependence, but are limited to denoising with Gaussian noise, moderate sizes (3-way tensors, two shapes), and no real data. The choice of λ is explored via a small grid; the claimed independence from ranks is qualitatively supported but not rigorously cross-validated. The latent complexity (sum of latent ranks) is estimated via decomposing the noiseless tensor, which is not available in practice (acceptable for validating theory, but it weakens the applied relevance of the metric).

Clarity
- The paper is generally well-organized: definitions of norms, duality lemma, main theorems, and experiments are clearly delineated. The relationship between overlapped and latent norms is explained and linked to structured sparsity analogs.
- Some clarity issues:
  - The introduction of spectral norm constraints (Eq. 11) is crucial for Theorems 2–3; however, the algorithmic implications and the value/selection of α are not specified, and it is unclear whether experiments enforce these constraints.
  - Minor typos and incomplete bibliographic details appear in references; a proofreading pass would help.
  - The factor F in Theorem 3 is termed “mild,” but its dependence on α and dimensions could be discussed more precisely.

Originality
- The duality between overlapped and latent Sp/q norms for tensors is a natural but nontrivial extension of known group-sparsity dualities; formalizing it for tensor unfoldings is of independent interest.
- The main novelty is the statistical analysis of the latent approach in denoising, leading to bounds scaling with the minimum mode rank, complementing prior results for the overlapped approach. This provides a theoretically grounded explanation of empirical observations from prior work.

Significance
- The results provide useful guidance on when latent decompositions should outperform overlapped regularization (e.g., when only one mode is low-rank), a common scenario in applications.
- The bounds are likely to influence method selection and inform future work on tensor recovery and completion. However, the current scope is limited to denoising with full observations; extending to tensor completion would increase impact.

Pros
- Clear, principled duality connecting overlapped/latent Schatten norms.
- New, interpretable generalization bounds for the latent approach with favorable dependence on minimum mode rank.
- Empirical evidence aligns with theoretical scaling predictions.
- Complements and clarifies prior NeurIPS work on convex tensor decomposition.

Cons
- Theoretical results for latent decomposition depend on additional spectral norm constraints; practical enforcement, α selection, and empirical validation of the constrained estimator are not fully addressed.
- Empirical evaluation is limited (synthetic, denoising only, K=3) and lacks real-data demonstrations.
- Some technical details are deferred to the supplement; a brief sketch of key proof ideas (e.g., decomposability, dual certificate) would aid readability.
- The novelty of the duality result is moderate given existing structured sparsity dualities; the main impact rests on the statistical analysis.

Suggestions for improvement
- Clarify whether Eq. (11)’s spectral constraints are used in experiments, how α is chosen, and provide sensitivity analyses.
- Discuss feasibility and computational handling of the constrained problem; include an algorithmic note or pointer.
- Add experiments on tensor completion or partially observed data, or explicitly scope the contribution as denoising-only.
- Expand discussion of the F factor and provide intuition for its scaling with dimensions and α.
- Improve references and fix typographical errors.

Overall, the paper provides a solid theoretical contribution that clarifies the advantages of latent Schatten-norm regularization for tensor denoising and connects convex tensor norms to structured sparsity dualities. Despite some practical gaps around constrained estimation and limited experiments, the work is technically sound, clearly written, and of interest to the NeurIPS community.
