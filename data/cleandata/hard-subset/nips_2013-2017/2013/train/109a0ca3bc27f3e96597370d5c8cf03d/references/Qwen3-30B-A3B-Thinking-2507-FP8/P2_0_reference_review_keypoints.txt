- Paper studies two convex tensor decomposition approaches: overlapped Schatten 1-norm and latent Schatten 1-norm.  
- Establishes duality: the dual of the overlapped norm is the latent norm and vice versa.  
- Shows latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor.  
- Shows overlapped approach error scales with the average of the square roots of mode-k ranks.  
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1–3) give consistency, deterministic bounds, and explain empirical superiority of latent approach.  
- Rigorous proofs provided in the appendix; strong technical quality.  
- Numerical experiments validate theoretical predictions; MSE scales with proposed complexity measures.  
- Connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature (e.g., group lasso) and generalizes prior results.  
- Original contribution: first theoretical explanation for latent approach’s empirical performance and duality result.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and reproducible for experts; notation and definitions are clear.  
- Significant practical implication: latent approach behaves as if it knows the minimum-rank mode, guiding practitioner choice.  
- Paper advances the state of the art in convex tensor decomposition and has broader relevance to structured sparsity.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with clear duality results.  
- Strength: explains prior empirical observations and matches experiments.  
- Strength: clear writing and important practical implications.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity differences between approaches.  
- Weakness: no comparison with non-convex tensor methods (Tucker, CP) which are standard.  
- Weakness: focuses on noisy decomposition and does not address tensor completion with missing values.  
- Weakness: incoherence assumption in Theorem 2 may be restrictive in practice.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, rigorously proven theoretical contribution validated experimentally.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited scope (no tensor completion, no non-convex comparisons) and limited computational discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance at NIPS.  
- Suggestion: add discussion of computational complexity of latent vs overlapped approaches.  
- Suggestion: mention relationship between latent approach and non-convex methods (CP/Tucker) in conclusion.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion on when the incoherence assumption might be violated in practice.  
- Suggestion: report computational times in experiments for a fuller comparison.
