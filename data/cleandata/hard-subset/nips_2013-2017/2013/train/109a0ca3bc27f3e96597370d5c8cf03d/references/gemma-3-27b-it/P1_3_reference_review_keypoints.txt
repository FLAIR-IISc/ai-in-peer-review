- Presents a theoretical analysis showing the latent Schatten-norm approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Establishes a novel duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms.  
- Proves the latent approach's MSE scales with the minimum rank across modes, explaining its superior performance in single-mode low-rank cases.  
- Positions the work within existing convex tensor decomposition literature and links it to structured sparsity and group lasso concepts.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical results with proofs (details in supplementary material).  
- Numerical experiments confirm the theoretical scaling behavior and practical benefits of the latent approach.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Relies on assumptions like incoherence and Gaussian noise; sensitivity to violations should be discussed.  
- Does not analyze or discuss computational complexity, leaving practical efficiency unclear.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (Gaussian noise, no missing data); needs broader, more realistic experiments.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical implications, such as guidance for choosing regularization parameters.  
- Work is original and significant for researchers in tensor decomposition and convex optimization.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: acknowledge assumption limitations, discuss computational cost, expand experiments (other noise/missing data), and provide guidance on regularization parameter selection.
