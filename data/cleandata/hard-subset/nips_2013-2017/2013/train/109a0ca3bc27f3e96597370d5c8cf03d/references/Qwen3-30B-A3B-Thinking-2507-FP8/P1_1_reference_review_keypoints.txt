- Paper analyzes two convex tensor decomposition approaches ("overlapped" and "latent") via structured Schatten norms and proves a duality between them (Lemma 1).
- Main theoretical result: latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, while overlapped error scales with the average (square root) of mode-k ranks.
- The theory explains empirical superiority of latent approach when tensor is low-rank in only one mode.
- Connects tensor decomposition approaches to structured sparsity literature (overlapped vs. latent group sparsity paradigms).
- Provides theoretical guarantees for the latent approach (Theorems 1–3) and validates scaling predictions empirically (Figure 2).
- Builds on recent convex tensor decomposition work but supplies the first rigorous explanation for latent approach performance.
- Theoretical rigor: complete analysis with well-structured proofs and an elegant, general duality result.
- Empirical validation: experiments confirm predicted linear scaling of MSE with complexity measures.
- Comprehensive analysis: distinguishes noiseless vs. noisy settings and consistency vs. deterministic bounds.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and focus (denoising rather than tensor completion).
- Paper is well-organized with clear notation and effective figures illustrating concepts and empirical results.
- Supplementary material contains detailed proofs, indicating technical completeness.
- Novelty: the duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a significant theoretical contribution.
- Provides the first rigorous theoretical explanation for prior empirical observations favoring the latent approach.
- Practical significance: offers guidance for practitioners to prefer the latent approach in many cases.
- Advances theoretical understanding of convex tensor decomposition and broadens connections to structured sparsity.
- Limited scope: analysis is restricted to denoising and does not directly address tensor completion.
- Computational complexity of the two approaches is not discussed, limiting practical guidance.
- The incoherence assumption in Theorem 2 may be restrictive in some applications.
- Some technical results may be challenging for readers without strong optimization backgrounds.
- Experimental comparisons are limited to overlapped vs. latent; other methods (Tucker, CP) are not compared.
- Novelty in broader tensor decomposition is somewhat limited since framework builds on prior work.
- Relevance may be narrower in practice because results are most powerful when tensors are low-rank in only one mode.
- Lacks practical guidance on choosing the regularization constant λ, despite theoretical recommendations.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, elegant duality, sound analysis, empirical support, and practical guidance.
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted focus on denoising vs. completion, missing computational complexity discussion, limited experimental comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper that rigorously explains latent approach success and is well-written and technically sound.
- Recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to discuss extensions to completion, include more method comparisons, and address computational complexity.
