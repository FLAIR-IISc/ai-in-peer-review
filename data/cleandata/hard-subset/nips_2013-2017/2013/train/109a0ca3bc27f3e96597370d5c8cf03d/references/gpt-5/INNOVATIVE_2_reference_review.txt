Summary:
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor decomposition based on structured Schatten norms. It formalizes two families of norms: (i) overlapped Sp/q norms obtained by unfolding a tensor along each mode and aggregating matrix Schatten norms, and (ii) latent Sp*/q* norms that decompose a tensor into a sum of mode-specific low-rank tensors. A key theoretical contribution is a general duality result showing that the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm (generalizing known dualities in structured sparsity). Leveraging this, the paper analyzes the “latent” tensor denoising estimator (sum of mode-specific low-rank tensors) and proves: a consistency bound; a deterministic bound under incoherence/spectral constraints, with error scaling with the sum of component ranks and, for the total error, with the minimum mode rank; and a high-probability Gaussian-noise bound with a rank-free choice of λ. This contrasts with prior analysis of the overlapped approach, where error scales with an average of square roots of mode ranks. Experiments on synthetic data corroborate the predicted scaling and typically show the latent approach outperforms the overlapped one, even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across modes.

Strengths:
- Conceptual clarity and unification: The duality between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms is elegant, connects tensor methods to the broader decomposable/structured sparsity literature, and provides a principled lens for comparing two widely used convex tensor models.
- Novel theoretical insight: The error scaling with the minimum mode rank is compelling and explains empirical behavior long observed but not previously justified. The rank-free prescription for λ in the Gaussian setting is also practically attractive.
- Technical soundness: The analysis follows modern frameworks (decomposable regularizers, dual norms, Gaussian widths/concentration) and appears careful. The deterministic and stochastic results are complementary to prior work that mainly focused on noiseless completion.
- Empirical support: Synthetic studies match the theory’s scaling predictions and show advantage of the latent formulation, especially when one mode is much lower rank.

Weaknesses and clarifications:
- Assumptions and constraints: The deterministic bound requires additional constraints (spectral norm incoherence across components) and the optimization (11) includes those constraints. It is not entirely clear whether the experiments enforce these constraints or use the unconstrained latent formulation from [25]. Please clarify whether the reported empirical results use (11) with α constraints; if not, discuss practical ways to estimate/enforce α and how sensitive performance is to this choice.
- Notation clarity: The same notation S1/1 appears to be used for both overlapped and latent norms in different places, which is confusing. Please disambiguate (e.g., S1/1^ov vs S1/1^lat) throughout.
- Tightness and optimality: While the min-mode-rank dependence is appealing, it would help to discuss tightness more explicitly. Are the rates minimax (up to constants/log factors) for the denoising setting considered? The comparison to the “Square Deal” lower bounds is in a different setting (linear inverse), and a direct lower bound here would strengthen the case.
- Practical scope: Results and experiments focus on denoising without missing entries. Given the strong interest in completion, even a preliminary extension or discussion of obstacles would add value. Also, only synthetic data are shown; a small real-data illustration (e.g., neuroimaging or video) would help demonstrate practical impact.
- Constants and dependencies: The factor F in Theorem 3 is somewhat opaque; a simplified bound and a short proof sketch (beyond the supplement) would improve readability. Similarly, the dependence on ordering n1 ≥ … ≥ nK in the λ choice could be clarified—does the choice adapt automatically or require preordering?
- Related work positioning: The duality result resembles infimal convolution/dual norm relations known in structured sparsity; it would be helpful to more explicitly tie the proof to these convex-analytic tools and to discuss whether any subtleties arise in the Schatten setting (e.g., unitary invariance, nonpolyhedral geometry).

Quality: Overall technically solid. The main claims are supported by theory with reasonable assumptions and matched by experiments. Some details (constraint enforcement, constants) should be clarified.

Clarity: Generally well written and well organized. Improving notation, briefly sketching key proof ideas in the main text, and clarifying the role of constraints would help.

Originality: Good. While overlapped/latent tensor norms and algorithms existed, the general duality for Schatten norms and the min-mode-rank statistical guarantees for the latent approach constitute a meaningful advance and a clear conceptual message.

Significance: High. The results guide practitioners toward the latent formulation in noisy settings, provide a unifying perspective for tensor regularization, and may influence future designs of convex tensor estimators.

Minor comments:
- Define vec and unfolding/folding just once and stick to consistent superscripts/subscripts; W(k)(k) is hard to parse.
- Provide a brief comment on computational complexity for the latent approach relative to the overlapped one (per-iteration costs, SVD sizes).
- Fix small typos (e.g., “betters” → “better”; spacing/parentheses in some equations).

Recommendation: Accept. The paper offers a clear, novel, and well-argued theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of convex tensor methods, with empirical validation and a unifying perspective likely to influence subsequent work. The minor issues are fixable.
