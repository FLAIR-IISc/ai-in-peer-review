## Peer Review: Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a valuable theoretical analysis of the latent approach to convex tensor decomposition, demonstrating why it often outperforms the overlapped approach, particularly when dealing with tensors low-rank in only one mode. The theoretical results are well-supported by empirical validation, and the connection to structured sparsity literature is clearly articulated. While the presentation could be improved for broader accessibility, the work makes a significant contribution to understanding the fundamental properties of these tensor decomposition methods. I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper investigates two convex relaxation approaches for tensor decomposition: the "overlapped" approach (penalizing all mode unfoldings with a Schatten norm) and the "latent" approach (decomposing the tensor into a mixture of low-rank tensors, each low-rank in a specific mode). While previous work ([9, 15, 23, 25, 26]) explored these methods empirically, this paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach. The key contribution lies in establishing a tighter error bound for the latent approach, scaling with the *minimum* rank across all modes, contrasting with the overlapped approach’s dependency on the average rank. The authors also highlight a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, linking these methods to the broader literature on structured sparsity (group lasso, etc.). The work builds upon and complements previous empirical studies [25, 26] by providing theoretical justification for observed performance differences.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper delivers a rigorous mathematical analysis of the latent approach, providing concrete bounds on its performance. The duality result is a particularly insightful contribution.
* **Clear Explanation of Performance Differences:** The analysis clearly explains why the latent approach excels when dealing with tensors low-rank in a single mode, a scenario where the overlapped approach struggles.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments validate the theoretical scaling results, demonstrating the practical benefits of the latent approach. The experiment design is well-thought-out, and the results support the theory.
* **Connection to Structured Sparsity:**  The authors successfully connect tensor decomposition to the well-established field of structured sparsity, highlighting the broader relevance of their findings.
* **Comprehensive Literature Review:** The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the existing literature on tensor decomposition and related optimization techniques.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Presentation & Accessibility:** The paper is mathematically dense and may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with tensor decomposition or structured sparsity to fully grasp. While the notation is standard, more intuitive explanations and illustrative examples would enhance accessibility. The introduction feels slightly rushed and doesn’t sufficiently motivate the problem for a broader audience.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments confirm the scaling results, they primarily focus on synthetic data and a specific noise model. Exploring the performance of the methods on real-world datasets would strengthen the findings.
* **Assumptions:** The theoretical analysis relies on certain assumptions (e.g., incoherence, specific noise distribution). A discussion of the sensitivity of the results to these assumptions would be valuable.
* **Practical Implementation Details:** The paper mentions using algorithms from a specific GitHub repository, but lacks details regarding implementation choices or computational cost considerations.

**4. Detailed Evaluation Based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and provides well-supported theoretical results. The proofs appear rigorous, and the experimental results corroborate the theory. The authors acknowledge the limitations of their assumptions and discuss potential avenues for future work.
* **Clarity:**  While mathematically precise, the paper suffers from a lack of intuitive explanations.  The definitions of the various Schatten norms could be presented more clearly with illustrative examples.  Streamlining the notation and adding more descriptive section headings could also improve clarity.
* **Originality:** The work is novel in its rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach to tensor decomposition. The duality result and the tighter error bound represent significant contributions. While the approaches themselves are not entirely new, the theoretical understanding provided is a substantial advance.
* **Significance:** The results are likely to be of interest to researchers working on tensor decomposition, low-rank modeling, and structured sparsity.  The insights gained from this work could lead to improved algorithms and a deeper understanding of the properties of these methods.  The practical implications for applications like neuroimaging and collaborative filtering are significant.



**5. Arguments for and against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Rigorous theoretical analysis providing insight into the behavior of tensor decomposition methods.
* Clear explanation for the superior performance of the latent approach in specific scenarios.
* Empirical validation supporting the theoretical results.
* Connection to broader literature on structured sparsity.

**Con:**

*  Mathematically dense and potentially inaccessible to a broader audience.
* Limited scope of experimental evaluation.
* Reliance on certain assumptions that may not hold in all practical scenarios.



**6. Recommendation:**

I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions. Specifically, I suggest the authors:

* **Improve Accessibility:**  Add more intuitive explanations and illustrative examples to make the paper more accessible to readers unfamiliar with tensor decomposition.
* **Expand Experimental Evaluation:** Consider including results on real-world datasets.
* **Discuss Assumption Sensitivity:**  Provide a discussion of the sensitivity of the results to the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis.
* **Provide Implementation Details:** Briefly describe the implementation details used in the experiments, including computational cost considerations.



This work is a valuable contribution to the field of tensor decomposition, and I believe it is well-suited for presentation at NIPS. The improvements suggested above will further enhance the clarity and impact of the paper.
