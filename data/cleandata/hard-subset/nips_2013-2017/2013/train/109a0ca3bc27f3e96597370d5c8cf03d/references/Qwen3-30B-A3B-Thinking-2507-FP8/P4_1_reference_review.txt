

# Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper studies two convex approaches to tensor decomposition: the "overlapped" approach (which penalizes all modes to be jointly low-rank) and the "latent" approach (which decomposes the tensor into a mixture of tensors each low-rank in a specific mode). The authors establish a duality between the structured Schatten norms used in these two approaches, showing that the dual of the overlapped Schatten norm is the latent Schatten norm and vice versa. This connection bridges tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature. The paper then provides theoretical analysis of the latent approach, showing that its error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the underlying tensor (as opposed to the average rank for the overlapped approach), which explains its empirical superiority in many cases. The authors empirically verify their theoretical predictions through numerical experiments.

**Quality:** The paper demonstrates strong technical quality. The theoretical results are rigorous and well-supported by proofs (referenced to supplementary material). The authors establish a clear duality between the two norm types, which is a significant theoretical contribution. The error bounds for both deterministic and Gaussian noise settings are properly derived, and the connection between the theoretical predictions and experimental results is well-established. The paper carefully considers both strengths and weaknesses of the approaches, particularly noting that the latent approach's theoretical advantage is most pronounced when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The only notable limitation is that the analysis focuses on the denoising setting rather than tensor completion (missing data), which is a common application of tensor decomposition.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-defined throughout. The connection between tensor decomposition and structured sparsity is clearly explained, making the paper accessible to researchers familiar with sparse regularization techniques. The figures (particularly Figure 1) effectively illustrate the key empirical findings. The experimental setup is described in sufficient detail to allow for reproducibility. The paper provides a clear roadmap of the contributions, making it easy for readers to follow the logical progression of the arguments.

**Originality:** The paper makes several novel contributions. The duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a new theoretical insight that generalizes results from structured sparsity literature. The theoretical analysis of the latent approach's performance, particularly the error bound scaling with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average, is a significant advancement over previous work. While the two approaches to convex tensor decomposition were previously proposed in [9, 15, 23, 25, 26], this paper provides the first rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach and explains its empirical superiority. The connection to structured sparsity literature [2, 13, 17, 21] is well-executed and provides a useful theoretical framework.

**Significance:** The results have clear practical significance for tensor decomposition applications, which are widespread in neuroimaging, collaborative filtering, and other multiway data analysis tasks. The paper provides both theoretical and empirical evidence that the latent approach is preferable for noisy tensor decomposition, which has direct implications for practitioners choosing between these methods. The insight that the latent approach's error scales with the minimum rank (rather than average rank) explains why it outperforms the overlapped approach in many cases, particularly when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The paper's framework for structured Schatten norms opens new avenues for research in tensor decomposition and structured regularization.

**Strengths:**
- Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach's performance
- Demonstrates clear error scaling with minimum mode-k rank (vs. average rank for overlapped approach)
- Empirically confirms theoretical predictions with well-designed experiments
- Well-written and clearly organized

**Weaknesses:**
- Focuses only on denoising setting, not tensor completion (a common application)
- Doesn't explore computational complexity differences between approaches
- Limited discussion of practical regularization parameter selection beyond theoretical optimal choice
- Theoretical advantage is most pronounced in extreme cases (low-rank in only one mode), which may not always be the case in practice

**Recommendation:** This paper makes a significant contribution to the theory of convex tensor decomposition. The duality between the two norm types is novel and well-supported, and the theoretical analysis of the latent approach's performance provides important insights that explain empirical observations. The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and addresses an important gap in the literature. While the focus on denoising rather than completion is a limitation, this is acknowledged by the authors and represents a natural direction for future work. The results have clear significance for both theoretical understanding and practical application of tensor decomposition methods. I recommend acceptance at NIPS.
