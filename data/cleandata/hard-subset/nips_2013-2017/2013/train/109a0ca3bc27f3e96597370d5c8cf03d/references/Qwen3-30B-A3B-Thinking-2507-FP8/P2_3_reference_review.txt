

# Review: Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization

## Summary
This paper addresses the problem of convex tensor decomposition, comparing two approaches: the "overlapped approach" (which penalizes all tensor modes to be jointly low-rank) and the "latent approach" (which decomposes the tensor into a mixture of components, each low-rank in a specific mode). The authors establish a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which generalizes results in structured sparsity literature. They provide rigorous theoretical guarantees showing that the latent approach's mean squared error scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the underlying tensor, rather than the average as in the overlapped approach. This explains the empirical superiority of the latent approach observed in previous work. The paper also includes numerical experiments confirming the theoretical predictions.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims. The theoretical contributions are significant: Lemma 1 establishes a novel duality between the two norms, and Theorems 1-3 provide tight theoretical guarantees for the latent approach. The analysis is rigorous, with proofs provided in the supplementary material. The experimental results align well with the theoretical predictions, confirming the linear scaling of MSE with the LR complexity (as defined in the paper). The paper carefully addresses the strengths and weaknesses of both approaches, particularly noting that the latent approach performs at least as well as the overlapped approach even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank.

## Clarity
The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and positions it within the broader context of tensor decomposition and structured sparsity. The mathematical definitions are precise, and the theoretical results are presented logically. The experimental section clearly demonstrates the theoretical predictions. The only minor weakness is that some of the more technical proofs (e.g., in Appendices) could be made more accessible to readers less familiar with tensor decompositions.

## Originality
The paper makes several original contributions. The duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms generalizes results in structured sparsity literature and provides a new theoretical framework for understanding tensor decomposition approaches. The key insight that the latent approach's error scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank (rather than the average) is novel and explains the empirical superiority of this approach. While the paper builds on previous work by [25, 26], it provides a more rigorous analysis that addresses a key theoretical gap in the literature.

## Significance
The results have significant implications for tensor decomposition in practice. The paper provides a theoretically grounded approach that outperforms previous convex methods, particularly in cases where the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The connection to structured sparsity literature broadens the applicability of the work. The results are relevant to numerous applications including neuroimaging, collaborative filtering, and environmental monitoring. The paper advances the state of the art in convex tensor decomposition by providing tighter theoretical guarantees and a better understanding of when to use which approach.

## Strengths
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with well-supported claims
- Clear explanation of the duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms
- Theoretical guarantees that explain empirical observations
- Experimental results confirming theoretical predictions
- Connection to broader structured sparsity literature

## Weaknesses
- Limited experimental evaluation (only two tensor sizes considered)
- Does not discuss computational complexity comparison between the two approaches
- Does not address how the latent approach compares to non-convex tensor decomposition methods, which are more commonly used in practice
- Could better discuss practical implications for choosing regularization parameters

## Recommendation
This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of convex tensor decomposition. The theoretical insights are novel and significant, and the empirical results confirm the theoretical predictions. While the experimental section could be expanded, the paper's theoretical contributions are substantial enough to warrant acceptance. The paper advances our understanding of convex tensor decomposition methods and provides a theoretically grounded approach that outperforms previous convex methods in many scenarios.

The paper meets all four NIPS criteria: it is of high quality with rigorous analysis, clearly written, original in its theoretical insights, and significant for the field of tensor decomposition and structured sparsity. The work is well-positioned to influence future research in convex optimization for tensor problems.

**Recommendation: Accept**
