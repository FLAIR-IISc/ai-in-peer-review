- Overall recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions — technically sound, significant theoretical contribution, and well-written.  
- Main contribution: rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent Schatten-norm approach for convex tensor decomposition.  
- Key theoretical results: consistency (Theorem 1) and deterministic error bounds (Theorem 2) showing favorable scaling with the minimal mode rank.  
- Novelty: identification of a duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms linking tensor decomposition to structured sparsity.  
- Explanation of advantage: latent approach outperforms overlapped because its error bound depends on the minimum rank across modes rather than an average.  
- Empirical validation: numerical experiments corroborate theoretical predictions and show latent approach superiority even in challenging cases.  
- Presentation: clear, well-organized, and effectively explained notation and concepts.  
- Related work: appropriately positioned within literature on Schatten norms, convex relaxations, and structured sparsity.  
- Weakness: assumptions in Theorems 2 & 3 (incoherence, Gaussian noise) are somewhat strong and their sensitivity is not discussed.  
- Weakness: practical meaning and estimability of the incoherence parameter α are not sufficiently explored.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity and practical trade-offs between latent and overlapped approaches.  
- Weakness: numerical experiments are relatively simple; broader datasets and noise models would strengthen empirical claims.  
- Suggested revisions: discuss sensitivity to assumption violations, provide intuition for α, add complexity discussion, and (optionally) extend experiments.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 4.5/5, Clarity 5/5, Originality 4/5, Significance 4/5.
