Summary and relation to prior work
This paper studies convex approaches to tensor decomposition/denoising and, in particular, formalizes and analyzes two structured Schatten-norm regularizers: (i) the “overlapped” norm, which penalizes the nuclear norms of all mode unfoldings simultaneously (as in prior work on sum-of-nuclear-norms for tensors [9, 15, 23, 25, 26]), and (ii) the “latent” norm, which decomposes the tensor into a sum of K tensors, each low-rank in a single mode, and penalizes the corresponding nuclear norms (proposed empirically in [25]). The paper’s main conceptual contribution is a duality result: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm, generalizing dualities in structured sparsity (e.g., group lasso with overlaps vs. latent group lasso [1, 12, 17, 21]). Building on this, the authors analyze the latent approach for denoising: they prove a consistency bound dependent on the overlapped spectral norm of the noise, and tighter deterministic and Gaussian-noise error bounds under an incoherence-type constraint that scale with the minimum mode-k rank, rather than the average of square roots of ranks as in the overlapped approach [26]. Synthetic experiments corroborate the predicted scaling and show the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach, even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank.

Strengths
- Clear formalization of “latent” vs. “overlapped” Schatten norms and a useful duality that connects tensor norms to known group-sparsity dualities. This unifies strands of the literature.
- Theoretical denoising guarantees for the latent approach that illuminate when and why it can outperform overlapped formulations: bounds scale with min mode-k rank and are independent of unknown ranks in the choice of λ.
- Comparison with prior guarantees for the overlapped approach [26], providing an interpretable explanation of empirical behavior seen in [25].
- Solid, reproducible experiments (code cited) verifying the scaling with dimensions and latent ranks; multiple sizes, noise level controlled.

Weaknesses and concerns
- The tighter bounds (Theorems 2–3) require additional spectral-norm constraints (parameter α) on cross-mode unfoldings of each latent component (Eq. 10), and the optimization (Eq. 11) enforces these constraints. It is unclear whether these constraints were enforced in experiments, how α is chosen in practice, and how sensitive results are to α. The latent approach in [25] did not include these constraints.
- The experimental evaluation is limited to synthetic denoising without missing data; no real datasets or tensor completion tasks are shown, and comparisons are restricted to overlapped vs. latent norms. Practitioners would benefit from benchmarks against Tucker/HoSVD with model selection, CP/CP-ALS, and robust/regularized matrix/tensor baselines.
- Some notational choices could confuse readers: both overlapped and latent versions are denoted S1/1; using distinct symbols (e.g., ||·||overlap and ||·||latent) would help. Also, the distinction between multilinear rank (rk) and component ranks (r̄k) merits more intuition earlier.
- The constant and rate factors in the Gaussian bound (F) are somewhat opaque; discussion of tightness and whether the min-rank dependence matches known lower bounds beyond [19] would be welcome.
- Minor: Inequality (3) seems to include an extra factor K in front of sum_k sqrt(rk) ||W||F; please verify constants.

Quality
- Generally technically sound. The duality lemma and denoising bounds appear well-grounded and are aligned with decomposable-regularizer analysis (e.g., [1, 20]). The added constraints (α) enable control of cross-mode interference but create a potential gap between theory and practice unless addressed empirically.

Clarity
- Well written and organized, with useful intuition (e.g., why min-rank vs. average sqrt-rank arises). The paper could improve clarity by: differentiating notations for the two norms; adding a brief proof sketch for the duality; giving a more accessible explanation of the incoherence/α condition and its practical selection; summarizing the ADMM steps for the latent norm.

Originality
- The duality result for structured tensor Schatten norms and the statistical analysis for latent tensor denoising appear novel and extend prior results in overlapped-norm tensor work [26] and structured sparsity.

Significance
- The results sharpen our conceptual understanding of convex tensor regularization and can influence algorithm choice in practice. While restricted to denoising, the insights are likely to extend to completion and other inverse problems. Broader impact would be strengthened by applications and comparisons beyond synthetic data.

Questions for the authors
1) Were the spectral-norm constraints with α in Eq. (11) enforced in the experiments? If not, can you comment on the practical necessity of these constraints and how to set α?
2) Can the analysis be extended to sub-Gaussian or bounded noise with similar scaling?
3) How sensitive is performance to λ in practice, and can you offer a data-driven selection rule (e.g., SURE or cross-validation)?
4) Is inequality (3) missing a constant? Please check the prefactor.

Suggestions for improvement
- Clarify and justify the α-constraint empirically: enforce it in experiments, provide a practical rule-of-thumb for α, and evaluate sensitivity.
- Add experiments on tensor completion (noisy missing data) and at least one real dataset (e.g., video, neuroimaging), plus comparisons to Tucker with rank selection and CP-based methods.
- Use distinct notation for overlapped vs. latent norms; add a short proof sketch and geometric interpretation of the duality.
- Discuss tightness of rates and relation to lower bounds more thoroughly.
- Provide implementation details for the proximal operators/ADMM steps and runtime scaling.

Pros for acceptance
- Conceptually unifying duality; clear, novel theoretical insight favoring latent norms.
- Rigorous denoising guarantees with interpretable rank dependence.
- Consistent empirical validation of scaling behavior.

Cons for acceptance
- Theory–practice gap around α-constraints and lack of guidance for α.
- Limited experimental scope (synthetic only; no completion or real data).
- Some clarity issues in notation and constants.

Recommendation: Weak Accept
The paper offers a meaningful theoretical advance with good clarity and promise for practical impact. Addressing the α-constraint and strengthening experiments would further solidify the contribution.
