

# Peer Review: Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization

This paper presents a rigorous theoretical analysis of two convex approaches to tensor decomposition: the "overlapped" and "latent" approaches. The authors establish a novel duality between the overlapped Schatten norm and latent Schatten norm, and demonstrate that the latent approach achieves error bounds scaling with the *minimum* mode-k rank rather than the average mode-k rank as in the overlapped approach. This theoretical insight explains the empirical superiority of the latent approach observed in prior work (Tomioka et al., 2013).

The paper makes several significant contributions. First, it provides the first theoretical analysis of the latent approach for tensor decomposition, connecting it to structured sparsity literature. The duality result (Lemma 1) generalizes the known duality between overlapped/latent group sparsity norms. Second, the main theoretical results (Theorems 2-3) establish that the latent approach's error bound depends on min_k √r_k rather than the average dependence in the overlapped approach (Theorem 1). This explains the empirical advantage shown in Figure 1 and in the authors' numerical experiments.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The mathematical definitions are precise, and the connection between tensor decomposition and structured sparsity is well-explained. The empirical results in Section 4 provide strong support for the theoretical claims, with MSE scaling linearly with the predicted complexity measures (TR complexity and LR complexity).

**Strengths:**
- The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides a principled explanation for the empirical success of the latent approach
- The duality result elegantly connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature
- The paper correctly identifies the key limitation of the overlapped approach (dependence on average rank) and shows how the latent approach overcomes it
- The empirical results confirm the theoretical predictions with clean experimental design
- The paper is well-positioned to advance the field of convex tensor decomposition by providing a more theoretically sound alternative to the overlapped approach

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper focuses exclusively on the S1/1-norm case without exploring how the results might extend to other Sp/q-norms. The duality result (Lemma 1) mentions this generalization but doesn't explore it in depth.
- The empirical section lacks comparison with non-convex tensor decomposition methods (e.g., higher-order SVD, ALS), which are the standard baselines in the field. This limits the practical significance of the comparison.
- The paper claims the optimal λ is independent of the true ranks, but doesn't provide practical guidance for choosing λ in real applications where the true ranks are unknown.
- The computational complexity of the latent approach versus the overlapped approach isn't discussed, which is an important practical consideration.
- The paper doesn't address how the latent approach handles missing data (tensor completion), which is a major application area for tensor decomposition.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical contribution to the field of convex tensor decomposition. The paper makes a clear and important theoretical advance by showing that the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in a principled way, with error bounds that depend on the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average. The duality result is particularly elegant and well-motivated.

The paper is well-written, technically sound, and makes a meaningful contribution to the field. While the empirical section could be strengthened by comparing with non-convex baselines and addressing computational considerations, the core theoretical contribution is significant and well-supported.

**Recommendation:**
Accept. This paper makes a clear theoretical advance that explains an empirical phenomenon in tensor decomposition and provides a more principled approach to convex tensor decomposition. The duality result connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature in a novel way, and the theoretical analysis is rigorous. While the empirical section could be expanded, the theoretical contribution is strong enough to warrant acceptance at NIPS.

**Pro and Con for Acceptance:**
- Pro: Theoretical explanation for empirical observations, novel duality result, rigorous error bounds
- Pro: Clear connection to structured sparsity literature, well-motivated contributions
- Con: Limited empirical comparison with non-convex baselines
- Con: No discussion of computational complexity or missing data applications

The paper advances the state of the art in convex tensor decomposition by providing a theoretically sound alternative to the overlapped approach, with clear advantages in both theory and practice. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field that will likely influence future work on convex tensor decomposition.
