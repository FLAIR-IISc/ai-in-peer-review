- Overall: Strong theoretical and empirical contribution showing advantages of latent Schatten-norm approach; recommends acceptance.
- Problem & focus: Studies convex tensor decomposition, contrasting overlapped vs latent Schatten-norm regularization.
- Main theoretical contribution: Provides rigorous analysis proving consistency and bounds for the latent approach, with error scaling by the minimum mode-k rank.
- Novelty: Establishes a duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, linking tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature.
- Empirical explanation: Theory explains prior empirical observations that the latent approach outperforms overlapped methods when low-rank in one mode.
- Experiments: Comprehensive numerical results on synthetic data that corroborate theoretical scaling and advantages of the latent approach.
- Presentation: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates itself well within related work.
- Strength: Strong mathematical rigor and insightful results that resolve an open question about the latent approach.
- Strength: Connections to existing literature are thorough and informative.
- Weakness: Theoretical analysis depends on assumptions (e.g., spectral norm bound/incoherence) whose violation effects are not fully explored.
- Weakness: Paper lacks discussion of computational complexity and practical cost of solving the latent optimization compared to overlapped methods.
- Weakness: Experiments are primarily synthetic; results on real-world datasets are missing.
- Weakness: Paper mentions Tucker decomposition but does not clarify the relationship or comparative advantages to the latent approach.
- Suggestion: Explicitly highlight the link from Lemma 2 to latent approach performance in the introduction.
- Suggestion: Provide more intuitive explanation for the factor F in Theorem 3 regarding dependence on dimensions and noise.
- Future work: Proposes extending the latent approach to tensor completion and other tensor problems with missing data.
- Final rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
