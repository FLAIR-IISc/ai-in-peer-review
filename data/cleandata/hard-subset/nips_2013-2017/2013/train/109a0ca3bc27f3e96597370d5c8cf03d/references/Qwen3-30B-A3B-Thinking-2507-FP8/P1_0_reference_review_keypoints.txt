- Introduces structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition with overlapped and latent approaches.
- Overlapped approach penalizes joint low-rank across modes via overlapped Schatten-1 norm.
- Latent approach decomposes tensor into mode-specific low-rank components using latent Schatten-1 norm.
- Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms (Lemma 1).
- Theoretical analysis shows latent approach error bounds scale with the minimum mode-k rank, not the average.
- Provides rigorous proofs for consistency (Theorem 1), deterministic bounds (Theorem 2), and Gaussian-noise results (Theorem 3).
- Numerical experiments validate theory: MSE scales linearly with predicted complexity measures.
- Empirically explains superior latent performance when tensor is low-rank in only one mode (Figure 1).
- Connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature (group lasso) and prior convex methods.
- Paper is technically sound and well-written with clear organization and effective figures.
- Limitations: lacks computational complexity comparison between overlapped and latent approaches.
- Limitation: incoherence assumption (inequality 10) is not discussed for practical restrictiveness.
- Limitation: doesn't explore tensors low-rank in multiple modes where latent may not be optimal.
- Lacks practical guidance on choosing regularization constant λ beyond theory.
- Original contribution: generalizes structured sparsity results via the duality result.
- Significant advancement: explains error scaling with minimum rank, improving prior work by Tomioka et al.
- Work is relevant to applications like neuroimaging, collaborative filtering, and environmental monitoring.
- Strength: empirical results strongly support theoretical claims.
- Strength: provides a rigorous theoretical foundation for preferring latent approach in noisy settings.
- Suggestion: discuss how restrictive incoherence assumption is with examples.
- Suggestion: include computational time comparisons between approaches in experiments.
- Suggestion: mention limitations of latent approach when no single-mode low-rankness holds.
- Suggestion: add brief explanation why minimum rank suffices for latent but not overlapped approach.
- Overall recommendation: accept—significant theoretical contribution, novel duality, strong empirical validation.
