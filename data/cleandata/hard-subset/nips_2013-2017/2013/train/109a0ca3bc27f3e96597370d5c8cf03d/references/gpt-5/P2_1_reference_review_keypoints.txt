- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition comparing overlapped (sum of nuclear norms) vs latent (sum of mode-specific low-rank components) Schatten-norm formulations.
- Main theoretical contribution: duality result showing the dual of overlapped Sp/q is latent Sp*/q*, e.g., latent S1/1 duals to overlapped S∞/∞.
- Proves consistency and denoising error bounds for the latent approach with rates depending on the minimal mode rank rather than the sum.
- Key inequality: latent S1/1 norm bounded by the minimal sqrt(mode-rank), yielding MSE scaling with min mode-k rank.
- Provides deterministic and Gaussian-noise error bounds under an incoherence-type spectral constraint (parameter α).
- Empirical synthetic denoising experiments corroborate theoretical scaling and show latent often outperforms overlapped even when tensor is low-rank across modes.
- Theoretically uses norm duality, decomposable-regularizer machinery, and Gaussian width/concentration arguments—technically sound at a high level.
- Relates to prior work on overlapped nuclear-norm methods (Liu, Tomioka, Signoretto, Recht) and to latent/dirty group-lasso models (Jacob, Jalali).
- Duality result is new for Schatten norms on tensors and mirrors known overlapped/latent sparsity dualities.
- Positions results against Tomioka et al. bounds and aligns with lower-bound insights from Mu et al.
- Strength: clear conceptual contribution extending structured sparsity insights to tensors with a clean duality proof.
- Strength: statistically meaningful advances showing when latent outperforms overlapped and practical adaptability to the easiest mode.
- Strength: empirical validation supports theoretical claims about scaling and regularization behavior.
- Weakness: deterministic bounds require additional spectral-norm constraints (α); unclear if these are enforced or how α is chosen in experiments.
- Weakness: experiments limited to Gaussian denoising with full observations; no tensor completion or nonconvex/Tucker/CANDECOMP baselines included.
- Weakness: practical selection of λ depends on unknown σ and α; experiments use grid search without guidance for real applications.
- Weakness: notation can be confusing (mode-k rank vs latent-factor ranks); a symbol table or clearer intuition is needed.
- Suggestion: discuss lower bounds for the denoising setting to assess tightness of rates.
- Overall evaluation: quality solid, clarity good but dense, originality high, significance moderate-to-high; recommendation: lean accept pending clarifications on α and λ and possibly a small completion experiment.
