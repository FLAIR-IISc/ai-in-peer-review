- Paper compares overlapped vs latent structured Schatten norms for low-rank tensor estimation and establishes a duality between them.  
- Main theoretical contribution: latent Schatten-1 estimator achieves MSE scaling with the minimal mode rank, improving over overlapped bounds.  
- Provides three main results: general consistency (Thm 1), deterministic bound with spectral incoherence (Thm 2), and high-probability Gaussian noise bounds with explicit λ (Thm 3).  
- Empirically confirms theoretical scaling and shows latent can outperform overlapped even for simultaneously low-rank tensors.  
- Connects convex tensor nuclear-norm methods to structured sparsity literature and contrasts with Tucker/HOSVD and nonconvex methods.  
- Strength: clean, general duality for structured Schatten norms (useful beyond this work).  
- Strength: sharp statistical insight explaining min-mode-rank dependence and empirical behavior (Lemma 2 highlights difference).  
- Strength: practical λ guidance in Theorem 3 that does not depend on unknown ranks.  
- Strength: simulations corroborate theoretical predictions and scaling behavior.  
- Weakness: Theorems 2–3 rely on constrained latent estimator with spectral ∞-norm bounds (α), but it's unclear if experiments enforce these constraints.  
- Weakness: Need clarification on how α is chosen, how constraints are enforced algorithmically, and whether bounds hold without explicit constraints under standard incoherence.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope limited to fully observed denoising with one noise level and synthetic data; missing tensor completion and nonconvex baselines.  
- Weakness: Latent complexity proxy may be circularly computed from decomposing W*; suggest reporting min-mode-rank only or providing principled upper bound.  
- Weakness: Notational confusion (same S1/1 used for overlapped and latent); request brief proof sketch of Lemma 1 and clearer statements about Frobenius norm equalities.  
- Weakness: Constants in Theorem 3 (F and c) not unpacked; need practical λ selection guidance (e.g., MAD or CV) and typical scaling discussion.  
- Weakness: Related work could be broadened to include other convex tensor norms (e.g., tubal nuclear norm) and low-rank formats (TT/CP).  
- Quality: Technical development appears sound but dependence on spectral constraints needs more justification and practical discussion.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written but needs notational disambiguation and explanation of α/constraint handling and duality intuition.  
- Originality: Novel duality and first denoising guarantees for latent tensor nuclear norm with min-mode-rank dependence.  
- Significance: Important contribution to convex tensor estimation with near-optimal guarantees in relevant regimes.  
- Argument for acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, fills gap on latent convex tensor decompositions, and empirical support.  
- Argument against acceptance: mismatch between analyzed constrained estimator and implemented method, narrow experiments, and notational/constant guidance issues.  
- Recommendation: Lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying estimator/constraint alignment, improving clarity, and ideally adding tensor completion experiment or stronger discussion on applicability.
