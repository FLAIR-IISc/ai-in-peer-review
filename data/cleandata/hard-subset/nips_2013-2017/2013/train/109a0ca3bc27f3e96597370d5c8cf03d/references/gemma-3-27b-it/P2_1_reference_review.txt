## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates convex approaches to tensor decomposition, specifically comparing the "overlapped" and "latent" Schatten norm regularization methods. It provides a theoretical analysis demonstrating why the latent approach, which decomposes a tensor into a mixture of low-rank components along specific modes, performs better than the overlapped approach, especially when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The authors establish a duality between the two norms and provide empirical evidence supporting their theoretical findings, confirming that the latent approach often outperforms the overlapped approach even when the tensor *is* simultaneously low-rank.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for understanding the performance differences between the two tensor decomposition approaches. The consistency and denoising performance results are well-defined and mathematically justified.
* **Novel Duality:** Establishing the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a significant contribution, connecting these tensor decomposition techniques to related work in structured sparsity.
* **Clear Explanation of Performance:** The analysis elegantly explains why the latent approach excels in scenarios where the tensor is low-rank in a single mode – its error bound scales with the *minimum* rank, while the overlapped approach depends on the *average* rank.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments convincingly demonstrate the theoretical predictions, reinforcing the practical advantage of the latent approach. The comparison against the overlapped approach is well-executed.
* **Connections to Existing Literature:** The paper successfully connects its work to relevant literature in matrix completion, sparse recovery, and tensor decomposition.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Numerical Experiments:** While the empirical results support the theory, they are somewhat limited in scope. More diverse datasets and tensor sizes could strengthen the conclusions.  The experiments primarily focus on randomly generated data, which might not fully represent real-world applications.
* **Assumptions in Theoretical Analysis:**  The theoretical results rely on certain assumptions (e.g., the incoherence assumption in Theorem 2) that may not always hold in practice. A discussion of the robustness of the results under violations of these assumptions would be beneficial.
* **Repetitive Discussion:** Some concepts, like the advantage of the latent approach, are reiterated several times throughout the paper. A more streamlined presentation could improve readability.
* **Minor Clarity Issues:**  The notation can be a bit dense at times, and certain definitions (like “Tucker rank complexity” and “Latent rank complexity”) are introduced quite late in the paper.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and provides strong theoretical support for its claims. The proofs appear rigorous (though a full verification is beyond the scope of this review). It represents a complete piece of work addressing a specific research question. The authors appropriately acknowledge limitations.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is generally well-written, but the density of notation and some repetitive discussions hinder clarity somewhat.  A more organized presentation with clearer definitions earlier in the text would be helpful.
* **Originality:** The duality result and the theoretical analysis of the latent approach are novel contributions. The paper builds upon existing work but offers a significant and unique perspective.  The authors demonstrate awareness of related work and provide appropriate citations.
* **Significance:**  The results are important because they provide a theoretical understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of different tensor decomposition methods. This knowledge can guide practitioners in choosing the appropriate approach for specific applications. The work advances the state of the art by providing a tighter performance bound for the latent approach and explaining its superior performance.



**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical foundation and rigorous analysis.
* Novel and significant contribution to the field of tensor decomposition.
* Clear and compelling empirical validation of theoretical results.
* Well-connected to existing literature.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Limited scope of numerical experiments.
* Reliance on potentially restrictive assumptions in the theoretical analysis.
* Some issues with clarity and organization.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, the duality result is novel, and the empirical results support the claims. While there are minor weaknesses regarding the scope of experiments and clarity of presentation, these do not significantly detract from the overall quality of the work.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**  I recommend accepting this paper, contingent upon the authors addressing the minor clarity issues and considering expanding the numerical experiments to include more diverse datasets. Addressing the robustness of the theoretical results to violations of the assumptions would also strengthen the paper.
