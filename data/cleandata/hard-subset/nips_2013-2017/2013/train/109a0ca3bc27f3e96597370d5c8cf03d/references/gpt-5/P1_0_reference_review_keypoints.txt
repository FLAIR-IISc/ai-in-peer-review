- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition using overlapped Sp/q norms and latent Sp*/q* norms, establishing a duality between them.  
- Latent estimator (sum of per-mode nuclear norms) is analyzed for denoising with consistency, deterministic, and Gaussian-noise error bounds.  
- Key theoretical insight: overlapped bounds scale with sum/average of mode ranks, while latent bounds scale with the minimum mode rank, explaining empirical advantages.  
- Duality result generalizes known overlapped vs latent group-lasso dualities to structured Schatten norms.  
- Work fills a gap by providing noisy-recovery guarantees for the latent formulation previously proposed without such theory.  
- Techniques leverage decomposable regularizers and high-dimensional M-estimation literature.  
- Does not address alternative tensor nuclear norms from t-SVD/tubal-rank literature; acknowledging this would improve positioning.  
- The duality and key inequalities appear correct and useful; main theorems (consistency, deterministic, Gaussian) are sensible.  
- The probabilistic bounds use spectral-norm concentration of unfoldings to choose λ, yielding min-rank dependence.  
- The estimator analysis requires additional spectral-norm constraints (parameter α) coupling components for identifiability.  
- Practical issues: α must be tuned or known, and it’s unclear if experiments enforced these constraints.  
- Theory-practice gap: if experiments used the unconstrained estimator, the mismatch should be discussed or theory extended to the unconstrained case.  
- Many proofs are deferred; brief proof sketches (duality, Lemma 2) in the main text would aid understanding.  
- Synthetic experiments corroborate theoretical scaling and show latent often outperforms overlapped methods.  
- Experimental limitations: medium-sized synthetic tensors only, no missing-data experiments, no real-data tests, and no comparisons to nonconvex Tucker/CP baselines.  
- No runtime or scalability analysis of the ADMM solver is reported.  
- Procedure for selecting λ in experiments is unclear (theory-driven vs CV vs oracle), and narrative seems inconsistent.  
- Notational issues cause confusion: same symbol used for overlapped and latent S1/1 norms and unclear distinction between multilinear ranks and component ranks.  
- Minor typos and clarity issues; adding intuitive explanations for Lemma 2 and λ choice would help readability.  
- Originality: duality for structured Schatten norms and statistical analysis of latent estimator are novel and nontrivial.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding and gives guidance on when to prefer latent vs overlapped formulations; practical impact would be stronger with real-data and completion experiments.  
- Pros: clear duality, new statistical guarantees, intuitive inequalities, and corroborating experiments.  
- Cons: reliance on spectral-norm constraint α, limited experiments, notational confusions, and deferred proofs.  
- Suggestions: use distinct notation for norms, add proof sketches, clarify enforcement and selection of α, include tensor completion and real-data experiments, compare to HOOI/Tucker/CP, and report computational costs.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution with supportive simulations; lean toward acceptance if estimator-constraint clarifications and stronger empirical validation are addressed.
