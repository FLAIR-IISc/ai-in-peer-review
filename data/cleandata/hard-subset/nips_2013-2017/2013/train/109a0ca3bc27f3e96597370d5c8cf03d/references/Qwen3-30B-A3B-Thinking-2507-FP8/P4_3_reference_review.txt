

# Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates convex approaches to tensor decomposition, specifically comparing the "overlapped" and "latent" approaches. The overlapped approach penalizes all modes to be jointly low-rank using the overlapped Schatten 1-norm, while the latent approach decomposes the tensor into a mixture of tensors each low-rank in a specific mode, using the latent Schatten 1-norm. The authors establish a duality between these two norms (Lemma 1), showing that the dual of the overlapped Sp/q-norm is the latent Sp*/q*-norm. They then prove theoretical results demonstrating that the latent approach has better performance guarantees, with error bounds depending on the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average mode-k rank as in the overlapped approach. The paper provides numerical experiments confirming their theoretical predictions.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper establishes a novel duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms (Lemma 1), which is a significant theoretical contribution. The proofs of the main theorems (1-3) are well-constructed and supported by rigorous mathematical analysis. The authors carefully connect their results to existing structured sparsity literature [2, 13, 17, 21], demonstrating how tensor decomposition fits into a broader theoretical framework.

2. **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical definitions are precise, the theorems are clearly stated, and the logical flow from definitions to theoretical results to empirical validation is coherent. The supplementary material provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

3. **Novelty:** The duality between the two norm types is a genuinely novel contribution that advances the theoretical understanding of convex tensor decomposition. The paper clearly differentiates itself from previous work [9, 15, 23, 25, 26] by providing a theoretical explanation for why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach, which was previously observed empirically but lacked theoretical justification.

4. **Significance:** The results provide a theoretically grounded explanation for the empirical superiority of the latent approach, which has practical implications for tensor decomposition applications. The paper establishes tighter error bounds that depend on the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average, which is particularly valuable for tensors that are low-rank in only one mode.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Scope of Empirical Validation:** While the numerical experiments (Section 4) confirm the theoretical scaling behavior, they primarily focus on cases where the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across modes. The paper acknowledges that "the improvements here are milder than that in Figure 1" because most randomly generated tensors were "simultaneously low-rank to some degree," suggesting the theoretical advantage might not be as pronounced in practice as the paper implies. The authors could have included more examples where the tensor is low-rank in only one mode to better demonstrate the theoretical advantage.

2. **Lack of Computational Complexity Analysis:** The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the latent approach compared to the overlapped approach. For practical applications, this is an important consideration that would strengthen the paper's contribution to the field.

3. **Limited Discussion of Limitations:** While the paper correctly notes that the latent approach performs at least as well as the overlapped approach in simultaneously low-rank cases, it does not thoroughly discuss scenarios where the overlapped approach might still be preferable (e.g., when the tensor has multiple modes with low rank but no single mode with very low rank).

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- Novel theoretical contribution establishing duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms
- Rigorous error bounds that explain empirical superiority of latent approach
- Clear connection to structured sparsity literature
- Strong empirical validation confirming theoretical predictions
- Well-written and technically sound paper

*Con:*
- Limited empirical validation in the most theoretically advantageous cases (tensor low-rank in only one mode)
- No computational complexity analysis
- Limited discussion of scenarios where overlapped approach might be preferable

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to the field of tensor decomposition by establishing a duality between two convex approaches and providing tighter error bounds for the latent approach. The theoretical results are sound, well-proven, and clearly presented. The paper is well-written and connects to relevant literature in structured sparsity. While the empirical validation could be strengthened by including more examples where the tensor is low-rank in only one mode, the paper provides sufficient evidence to support its claims. The theoretical results represent a meaningful advancement over previous work [25, 26], particularly in explaining the empirical success of the latent approach.

The paper meets all NIPS criteria: it is technically sound, clearly written, makes a novel contribution, and has significant potential impact for practitioners working with tensor data. The theoretical explanation provided for the empirical superiority of the latent approach is particularly valuable for the field. The paper is well-suited for NIPS publication.
