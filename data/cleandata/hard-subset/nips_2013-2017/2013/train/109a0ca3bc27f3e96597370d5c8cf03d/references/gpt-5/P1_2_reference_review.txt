Summary
The paper studies convex approaches to low-rank tensor estimation using structured Schatten norms. Two families of norms are considered: (i) overlapped Sp/q norms that penalize all mode-unfoldings jointly (recovering prior “overlapped nuclear norm” methods), and (ii) latent Sp*/q* norms that decompose a tensor into a sum of components, each low-rank in a specific mode (recovering the “latent nuclear norm” approach). The authors establish a duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm. For the key case p = q = 1, the dual is the overlapped S∞/∞ (max spectral norm across unfoldings).

They analyze the latent approach for tensor denoising (full observations with additive noise): (1) a basic consistency bound in Frobenius norm with λ ≥ ||E||S∞/∞; (2) a deterministic bound under an incoherence-style assumption enforced via additional spectral norm constraints that yields squared error scaling with the sum of latent component ranks and, by taking a singleton decomposition, with the minimum mode-k rank; and (3) a high-probability Gaussian noise bound with λ chosen as a function of tensor dimensions and σ, yielding MSE scaling ∝ min_k r_k (up to mild dimensional factors). This explains empirical observations that the latent approach performs substantially better than the overlapped one when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. Experiments on synthetic 3-way tensors corroborate the predicted scalings. A unified view ties these tensor norms to structured sparsity (overlapped vs latent group lasso).

Quality
- Technical soundness: The duality lemma is natural and carefully stated, generalizing known overlapped/latent group sparsity dualities. The key inequality for the latent S1/1 norm (bounded by min_k sqrt(r_k) times the Frobenius norm) cleanly explains the different statistical behavior relative to the overlapped norm (which depends on the sum of sqrt(r_k)). The denoising analyses follow the decomposable-regularizer framework and standard concentration for spectral norms of Gaussian matrices/tensors.
- Assumptions and scope: The strongest results (Theorems 2–3) rely on additional spectral norm constraints between components (parameter α) to model incoherence; this is sensible theoretically but raises practical questions (how to set α; whether the unconstrained latent program already enjoys the same guarantees under assumptions on W*). The Gaussian setting and full observation scenario are clearly stated; tensor completion is left for future work.
- Empirical support: The synthetic experiments align with the theory, and the solver is available. The plotting of MSE vs “TR” and “LR” complexities is informative.

Clarity
- The paper is mostly clear and well-organized, with definitions for unfoldings, ranks, and norms. The notation r_k (Tucker mode-k rank of W*) vs r̄_k (rank of the latent component unfolding) is easy to confuse; reiterating this distinction in Section 4 would help. Brief proof sketches (intuition) for Lemma 2 and Theorems 2–3 in the main text would improve readability.
- The role of α and the reason for adding constraints in (11) should be motivated earlier and tied to the incoherence condition (10). The recommended practical choice of λ and whether α is needed in practice should be discussed.

Originality
- The duality between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms is a clean and, to my knowledge, novel generalization of latent/overlapped group lasso duality to tensor Schatten norms.
- The error bounds that scale with the minimum mode-k rank for the latent approach, contrasted with the average (sqrt) rank dependence for the overlapped approach, provide new theoretical insight explaining previously reported empirical behavior.
- The work connects convex tensor norms to decomposable regularizer theory in a useful way.

Significance
- Tensor estimation is widely relevant. The results give a principled reason to favor latent nuclear norm formulations in noisy settings, particularly when only one mode is low-rank. The duality framework could guide new norm design and analysis. While the focus is denoising (not completion), the insights are likely to influence subsequent work.

Strengths
- Clear unifying framework and duality between overlapped and latent tensor norms.
- Simple inequality (Lemma 2) that crystallizes why latent can outperform overlapped.
- Deterministic and probabilistic bounds with interpretable dependencies (min mode rank).
- Empirical validation with scaling consistent across sizes; public solver.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Practicality of constraints: The optimization in (11) includes spectral norm constraints across modes controlled by α. How sensitive are results/solutions to α? Do the empirical results use these constraints? If not, can the theory be adapted to the unconstrained program using an incoherence assumption on W* instead?
- Parameter selection: λ depends on σ and dimensions. In practice, σ is unknown; a brief discussion of robust choices or cross-validation would help. Can the bounds be adapted with data-driven λ?
- Bound symmetry and dimensional factors: The Gaussian λ and error bound privilege the smallest mode n_K. Is this an artifact of the proof technique (max over unfoldings)? Could a symmetric bound in terms of min_k(n_k) or a tighter sum/average be obtained?
- Experimental scope: Only denoising with full observations and synthetic data is shown. Adding at least one real-world example (e.g., video or hyperspectral denoising) or an ablation where only one mode is low-rank (referenced as Figure 1) would strengthen the case. Sensitivity to α and λ, and scaling to larger tensors, would be informative.
- LR complexity definition uses an oracle decomposition (latent run on noiseless W* and taking the minimum with min_k r_k). While acceptable for analysis/visualization, it somewhat favors the latent method. Please clarify that this is only for plotting and not for tuning, and report results vs min_k r_k alone as a sanity check.
- Minor: unify notation r_k vs r̄_k; fix small typos/formatting (e.g., footnotes, equation numbering, spacing).

Questions for the authors
- Can the spectral norm constraints in (11) be removed while retaining Theorems 2–3 under a standard incoherence condition on W*? If not, can you provide guidance for setting α and show empirical sensitivity?
- Is the singleton decomposition always near-optimal in the regimes where latent excels? Any identifiability discussion for W^(k)?
- Could the analysis extend to tensor completion with missing entries (e.g., RIP- or sampling-based bounds) using your duality tools?

Reasons to accept
- Conceptually unifying and technically solid contribution that advances understanding of convex tensor decompositions.
- New duality and error bounds offer clear guidance on when to prefer latent norms.
- Empirical results are consistent with theory; code available.

Reasons to reject
- Limited empirical scope (no real data, no completion).
- Theoretical guarantees hinge on additional constraints and a parameter α not clearly justified in practice.

Recommendation
Accept. The paper provides a clear theoretical and empirical case for latent nuclear norm formulations in noisy tensor estimation, introduces a useful duality, and will likely influence follow-up work on tensor norms and completion. With minor clarifications and broader experiments, it would be even stronger.
