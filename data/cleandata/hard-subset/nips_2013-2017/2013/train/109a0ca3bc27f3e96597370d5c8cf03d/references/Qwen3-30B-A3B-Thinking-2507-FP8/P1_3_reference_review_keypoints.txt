- Paper compares overlapped vs. latent convex tensor decomposition and proves duality between their structured Schatten norms.  
- Main theoretical result: dual of overlapped Sp/q-norm is the latent Sp*/q*-norm (Lemma 1).  
- Latent approach MSE scales with the minimal mode-k rank, explaining empirical superiority when tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Extends connections to structured sparsity, generalizing overlapped/latent group sparsity norms.  
- Provides tight error bounds for the latent approach (Theorems 2 and 3) with rigorous proofs.  
- Numerical experiments confirm theory: latent MSE scales with LR complexity, overlapped with TR complexity.  
- Experiments are limited to Gaussian-noise tensor completion with no missing values.  
- No comparison with non-convex state-of-the-art tensor methods (e.g., CP, Tucker).  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity and scalability, despite using ADMM.  
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous, detailed proofs supporting claims.  
- Well-written and well-organized; supplementary material enables reproducibility for experts.  
- Novel contribution: first rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach and the duality result.  
- Results are significant for practical tensor applications and for guiding method choice.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Pros: novel theory, duality proof, tight bounds, empirical confirmation, broader applicability.  
- Cons: limited experiments (no missing data), missing non-convex comparisons, limited computational discussion.
