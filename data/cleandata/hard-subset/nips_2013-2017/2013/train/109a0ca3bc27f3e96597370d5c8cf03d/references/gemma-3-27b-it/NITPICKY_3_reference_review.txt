This paper investigates convex tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norm regularization, specifically comparing an "overlapped" and a "latent" approach. The core contribution lies in a theoretical analysis demonstrating the superior performance of the latent approach, particularly when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode, and establishing a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. While the ideas are interesting and build upon existing work in tensor decomposition and structured sparsity, the paper suffers from numerous, albeit minor, flaws in presentation, notation, and justification, leading to a negative recommendation.

**Summary:** The authors correctly identify a limitation of the standard convex tensor decomposition approach (overlapped Schatten norm) and propose a latent approach as a remedy. They provide a theoretical justification for this improvement, linking it to a duality between the norms and showing that the latent approach benefits from a tighter bound on the reconstruction error, scaling with the *minimum* mode-rank, versus the *sum* of mode ranks for the overlapped approach. Empirical results support these claims.

**Strengths:**

*   **Relevant Problem:** Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem with broad applications.
*   **Theoretical Contribution:** The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a novel and potentially useful connection. The theoretical analysis providing scaling guarantees is a significant effort.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The inclusion of numerical experiments, even if not extensive, provides supporting evidence for the theoretical findings.
*   **Connection to Existing Work:** The paper clearly links the proposed methods to related research in tensor decomposition, structured sparsity, and convex optimization.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Notational Inconsistencies:** The notation is frequently inconsistent and unnecessarily complex. For example, the meaning of W(k) shifts between representing the k-th unfolding and the k-th component in the latent decomposition without clear differentiation. The repeated use of both W(k) and W^(k) for different operations is confusing.
*   **Lack of Rigor in Definitions:** The definition of the overlapped Sp/q-norm (equation 2) is unnecessarily generalized. While mathematically valid, its inclusion feels extraneous without a clear justification or practical application demonstrated in the paper. The same applies to the generalization of Lemma 1.
*   **Missing Details in Proof Sketches:** Many claims, particularly in Section 3, are presented as theorems with proof sketches "in the supplementary material." While a lengthy proof isn’t necessary in the main paper, key steps and assumptions within those proofs should be summarized to ensure transparency and allow for independent verification. For example, the "incoherence assumption" in Theorem 2 feels arbitrary without a clear explanation of why it's needed and what it implies.
*   **Overstated Claims:** The claim that the latent approach "performs better in many cases" even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank (near the end of the introduction) is a weak statement supported by limited experimental evidence. The results in Figure 2 show only marginal improvements in some scenarios.
*   **Experimental Setup:** The experimental section lacks sufficient detail regarding parameter tuning, the choice of algorithms, and statistical significance. The comparison with the overlapped approach is not thoroughly explored. Why were only 20 regularization constants tested? How sensitive are the results to the choice of λ?
*   **Redundancy & Verbosity:** The paper contains significant redundancy, repeating the same ideas in different sections. The introduction, in particular, is overly long and could be more concise.
*   **Minor Technical Errors:** Equation (3) appears incorrect; the Frobenius norm cannot be bounded as stated. Similarly, the discussion of spectral norm constraints in Section 3.2 feels rushed and lacks sufficient justification.
*    **Formatting issues:** Several references are missing year information, while some are incomplete.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:** The theoretical analysis, particularly the duality result and the scaling guarantees for the latent approach, are valuable contributions to the field. The problem addressed is important, and the empirical results provide some support for the theory.

**Con:** The numerous flaws in presentation, notation, rigor, and justification undermine the overall quality of the paper. The experimental validation is weak, and the claims are often overstated. The inclusion of extraneous generalizations (e.g., the Sp/q-norm) adds unnecessary complexity without demonstrable benefit. The lack of detail in the proof sketches and the inconsistencies in notation make it difficult to assess the validity of the theoretical results.

**Overall Recommendation:**

**Reject.** While the core ideas have merit, the paper is marred by too many deficiencies to warrant acceptance. A significant revision addressing the issues outlined above – focusing on clarity, rigor, and justification – would be necessary for reconsideration. The current version lacks the polish and precision expected of a NIPS publication.
