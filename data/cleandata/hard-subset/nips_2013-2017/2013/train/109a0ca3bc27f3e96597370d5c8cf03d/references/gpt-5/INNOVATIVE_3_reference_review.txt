Summary
This paper studies convex formulations for tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms. It unifies two families: (i) overlapped Sp/q norms that penalize all mode-unfoldings simultaneously (e.g., sum of nuclear norms), and (ii) latent Sp/q norms that decompose the tensor into a sum of components, each low-rank in a specific mode. The key technical contribution is a general duality: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm. Building on this, the paper provides consistency and denoising error bounds for the latent approach, showing its error depends on the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor (rather than an average across modes as in prior analyses of the overlapped approach). Deterministic and Gaussian-noise bounds are given, along with an incoherence-style condition controlling cross-mode correlations. Experiments on synthetic denoising demonstrate scaling consistent with the theory and often favor the latent approach.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: The duality between overlapped and latent Sp/q norms for tensors is elegant and broadly useful, connecting convex tensor methods to structured sparsity (overlapped vs latent group lasso) in a principled way.
- Theoretical advance: New consistency and error bounds for the latent approach, with rates depending on min mode-rank, provide a clean explanation of when/why it can strictly outperform overlapped nuclear-norm penalties.
- Clear positioning: Relates to Agarwal/Negahban/Wainwright’s decomposable regularizers, Signoretto et al. on tensor nuclear norms, and prior overlapped analyses (Tomioka et al., Mu et al.).
- Empirical validation: Synthetic results match predicted scaling and suggest practical advantage of latent penalties even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across modes.
- Practical guidance: Regularization choices that do not depend on unknown ranks, and an explanation for empirical behavior when only one mode is low-rank.

Weaknesses
- Assumptions: The deterministic/Gaussian bounds for the latent method rely on extra spectral-norm constraints (α) across non-native modes. The necessity/tightness and how to set α in practice are not fully discussed; the comparison to overlapped may not be apples-to-apples if overlapped baselines do not get the same constraints.
- Experiments: Limited to moderate-sized synthetic denoising; no missing-data/completion tasks or real data; small range of K and dimensions; LR complexity is partially oracle-guided by running the latent method on the noiseless tensor.
- Clarity gaps: Some proofs are deferred; more intuition/examples for the duality and the geometry of the norms would help. Minor typos and presentation polish are needed.
- Scope: The empirical solver scalability and sensitivity to λ are not deeply analyzed; comparisons to nonconvex baselines (CP/Tucker-ALS) and recent relaxations (e.g., square norm, other tensor nuclear norms) are absent.

Quality
The theoretical contributions appear technically sound and build on well-established tools (dual norms, Gaussian width/Rademacher complexity, decomposable regularizers). The min-mode-rank dependence is compelling and consistent with recent lower bounds. However, the role of the α constraints should be examined more critically, and fairness of the comparison to overlapped methods clarified. Experiments are honest but limited.

Clarity
Generally well-organized with precise definitions and clear statements of main results. The paper would benefit from:
- A worked example illustrating overlapped vs latent decompositions and the duality (beyond formal definitions).
- A practical guide for setting α and λ, and discussion of computing the S∞/∞ norm in practice.
- Clean-up of minor typos and expanding the discussion of the generalized duality result in the supplement.

Originality
High. The duality result for structured Schatten norms on tensors and the error analysis for the latent approach with min-mode-rank dependence constitute a clear conceptual and technical step forward relative to prior work focused on overlapped sums of nuclear norms or noiseless completion.

Significance
Potentially high. Understanding when to prefer latent vs overlapped penalties matters for practitioners working with multiway data. The min-mode-rank phenomenon is both interpretable and actionable. The unifying perspective on structured Schatten norms could spur new designs and analyses.

Questions/clarifications for authors
- Can you discuss whether the α constraints are essential for the latent rates, and whether adding analogous constraints to the overlapped baseline changes the comparison?
- How sensitive are results to mis-specification of α? Can α be tuned from data, and is there a principled proxy?
- Can you provide experiments on tensor completion (with missingness) and at least one real dataset?
- How does the method scale to K>3 and larger modes? Any computational complexity/runtime comparisons?
- Could you compare against nonconvex CP/Tucker with rank selection, and recent relaxations such as the square norm?

Suggestions for improvement
- Add ablations varying α and λ, and report S∞/∞ estimates to guide practice.
- Include completion and real-data experiments; increase dimensionality/K.
- Provide a fairness check: overlapped with/without analogous constraints; report hyperparameter selection procedures uniformly.
- Expand the discussion on the geometry of latent vs overlapped norms; include a small visual/toy example.

Decision
Weak accept (7/10). The paper advances a novel and unifying theoretical framework with clear, interpretable results explaining the advantage of latent tensor norms, aligned with my preference for bold conceptual contributions. While the experiments are limited and some assumptions warrant more discussion, the core ideas are strong and likely to influence subsequent work.

Confidence
4/5
