## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates convex approaches to tensor decomposition, specifically comparing the “overlapped” and “latent” Schatten norm regularization techniques. It provides a theoretical analysis of the latent approach, demonstrating its performance scales with the *minimum* rank of the underlying tensor, explaining its empirical superiority when a tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  The authors also establish a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing previous results in structured sparsity. Finally, they provide empirical validation supporting their theoretical findings.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The core contribution – establishing a performance guarantee for the latent approach scaling with the minimum rank – is significant. This provides a solid theoretical foundation for the empirically observed benefits of this method, addressing a gap in understanding convex tensor decomposition.
* **Duality Result:** The demonstrated duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a novel and valuable connection, relating tensor decomposition to the broader field of structured sparsity. This generalization is well-motivated and could be useful for future research.
* **Clear Explanation of the Problem:** The introduction clearly articulates the challenges of traditional tensor decomposition (non-convexity, rank selection) and the motivations for exploring convex relaxations.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:**  The paper combines theoretical analysis with empirical validation, strengthening the overall conclusions. The numerical results thoughtfully compare the two approaches and demonstrate the scaling behavior predicted by the theory.
* **Well-written and Organized:** The paper is generally well-structured and clearly written, making it relatively easy to follow the complex arguments.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty in Setup:** The convex relaxation approach to tensor decomposition (using Schatten norms) is not entirely new, and the paper acknowledges this. The primary novelty lies in the analysis of the *latent* approach specifically, rather than introducing the overall framework.
* **Assumptions for Deterministic Bound:** The deterministic bound (Theorem 2) relies on an incoherence assumption (Equation 10) that may be difficult to verify in practical applications. The impact of violating this assumption isn't fully discussed.
* **Complexity of Theorems:** While the theorems are important, they are quite dense and rely on several assumptions. The discussion of their implications could be made more accessible to a broader audience.
* **Empirical Scope:** The numerical experiments are relatively limited in scope.  Exploring different tensor sizes, noise levels, and tensor structures would further strengthen the empirical validation.



**Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound, with rigorous proofs and a clear theoretical framework. The claims are well-supported, although the assumptions for the deterministic bound warrant caution. It's a complete and thorough piece of work.
* **Clarity (7/10):**  The paper is mostly clear, but the mathematical notation and the complexity of the theorems sometimes hinder readability. More intuitive explanations of the key results would be beneficial.
* **Originality (7/10):**  While the convex relaxation approach is established, the analysis of the latent approach and the duality result are novel contributions. The paper builds upon existing work in a significant way.
* **Significance (8/10):** The results are important for understanding the performance of convex tensor decomposition methods. The theoretical guarantees and empirical validation have the potential to influence future research and practical applications in areas like neuroimaging, spatio-temporal data analysis, and collaborative filtering.

**Arguments for/against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Provides a crucial theoretical justification for the empirically observed success of the latent approach.
* Introduces a valuable duality result linking tensor decomposition to structured sparsity.
* Offers a rigorous analysis of the performance guarantees.
* The combination of theory and experiments strengthens the paper’s impact.

**Con:**

* Relies on potentially restrictive assumptions for strong theoretical guarantees.
* The empirical evaluation, while solid, could be expanded.
* The core setup (convex relaxation) isn’t fundamentally novel.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of convex tensor decomposition. While the initial setup isn’t groundbreaking, the analysis of the latent approach and the duality result are significant advancements.  I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing the following minor revisions:

1.  **Clarify Assumptions:** Provide a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the incoherence assumption (Equation 10) and its practical implications.
2. **Improve Accessibility:**  Consider adding more intuitive explanations of the theorems and key results to broaden the paper’s reach.
3. **Expand Empirical Evaluation:** Explore the performance of the methods on a wider range of tensor sizes, noise levels, and tensor structures.
