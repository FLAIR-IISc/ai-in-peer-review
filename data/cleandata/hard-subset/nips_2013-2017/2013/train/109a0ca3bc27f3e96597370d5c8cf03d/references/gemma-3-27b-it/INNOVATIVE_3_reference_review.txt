This paper introduces a fascinating duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, and leverages this to provide a theoretical justification for the empirically observed superiority of the latent approach, particularly when dealing with tensors low-rank in only one mode. The core idea—connecting structured sparsity in tensor decomposition to a duality reminiscent of group Lasso variations—is genuinely novel and a significant contribution. The analysis leading to the bound scaling with the *minimum* rank is particularly compelling.

**Summary:** The authors investigate convex relaxation approaches to tensor decomposition, specifically contrasting an “overlapped” approach (penalizing all unfoldings) with a “latent” approach (decomposing into mode-specific low-rank components). They establish a duality between the corresponding Schatten norms and demonstrate, theoretically, that the latent approach achieves better performance, scaling with the minimum rank of the underlying tensor, which explains empirical results.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a key contribution. It provides a fresh perspective on existing tensor decomposition methods and opens avenues for further research.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the observed benefits of the latent approach, moving beyond purely empirical observations. The scaling result with the minimal rank is a strong point.
*   **Connection to Structured Sparsity:** Linking the tensor decomposition problem to the well-studied field of structured sparsity (group Lasso) provides valuable context and highlights the broader applicability of the results.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-organized and generally clearly written, with sufficient mathematical notation.
*   **Empirical Validation:**  The numerical experiments, while not extensive, provide supporting evidence for the theoretical findings, showing cases where the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental section feels somewhat cursory. While it confirms the theoretical scaling, a more comprehensive evaluation across different tensor shapes, rank configurations, and noise levels would be beneficial.  The experiments focused primarily on confirming the scaling; a broader exploration of practical performance in real-world applications would strengthen the paper.
*   **Assumptions in Theorem 2 & 3:**  The assumptions required for Theorems 2 and 3, particularly the incoherence assumption (10) and the specific noise model in Theorem 3, might be restrictive in practice. Discussing the robustness of the results to violations of these assumptions would be helpful.
*   **Discussion of Practical Implementation:** While the paper presents a theoretical framework, it lacks a detailed discussion of practical considerations for implementing the latent approach, such as solver selection and parameter tuning.
*   **Comparison to other Tensor Decomposition Methods:** The paper primarily focuses on comparing the latent approach to the overlapped approach. A brief discussion of how these methods compare to more traditional tensor decomposition techniques (e.g., Tucker decomposition, CP decomposition) would provide valuable context.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper tackles a fundamental problem in tensor decomposition with a novel and theoretically sound approach.
*   The duality result is a significant contribution to the field of structured sparsity and tensor analysis.
*   The theoretical guarantees, particularly the scaling with the minimal rank, provide valuable insights into the performance of the latent approach.
*   The paper demonstrates a clear understanding of the existing literature and positions its contribution effectively.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited and could be strengthened.
*   The theoretical results rely on specific assumptions that might not hold in all practical scenarios.
*   The paper does not provide detailed guidance on practical implementation.

**Overall Assessment:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper. Despite some minor weaknesses in the experimental section and assumptions made in the theoretical analysis, the novelty and theoretical depth of the work are exceptional. The paper introduces a significant new concept and direction in tensor decomposition, and the authors have convincingly demonstrated its potential. The fact that the theory successfully explains previously observed empirical results is a strong indicator of its validity. While further experimental validation and investigation of practical implementation details would be valuable, the current contribution is substantial enough to warrant acceptance. This paper has the potential to inspire further research and development in the field.
