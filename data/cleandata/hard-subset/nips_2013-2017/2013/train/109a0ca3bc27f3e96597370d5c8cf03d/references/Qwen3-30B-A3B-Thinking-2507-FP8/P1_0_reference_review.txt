

# Peer Review: "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

## Summary
This paper introduces a framework for structured Schatten norms in tensor decomposition, focusing on two approaches: the "overlapped" approach and the "latent" approach. The overlapped approach penalizes all tensor modes to be jointly low-rank using the overlapped Schatten 1-norm, while the latent approach decomposes the tensor into a mixture of tensors each low-rank in a specific mode, using the latent Schatten 1-norm. The authors establish a key duality between these two norms and provide theoretical analysis showing that the latent approach has superior performance guarantees, with error bounds scaling with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average rank. This explains the empirically observed superior performance of the latent approach in Figure 1 of the paper. The work connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature (particularly group lasso), building upon the recent convex optimization approaches to tensor decomposition by Tomioka et al. [25] and others.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with well-justified theoretical claims. The authors provide rigorous proofs for their key results (Lemma 1, Theorems 1-3) in the appendix, which is a significant strength. The theoretical analysis is comprehensive, addressing both consistency (Theorem 1) and deterministic bounds (Theorem 2), as well as providing results for Gaussian noise (Theorem 3).

The numerical experiments in Section 4 effectively validate the theoretical predictions, showing that the MSE scales linearly with the predicted complexity measures (TR complexity and LR complexity). The authors are careful to compare the latent approach with the overlapped approach, and they acknowledge the limitations of the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.

However, there are a few minor weaknesses:
1. The paper doesn't discuss computational complexity comparisons between the two approaches, which would be valuable for practitioners.
2. The incoherence assumption in inequality (10) is necessary for the tighter bounds but isn't discussed in terms of how restrictive it is in practice.
3. The paper doesn't explore cases where the tensor is low-rank in multiple modes but not in a way that favors the latent approach.

## Clarity
The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized. The introduction clearly frames the problem and motivates the need for the latent approach. Technical definitions are presented in a logical order, with the necessary background provided in Section 2. The theorems are clearly stated, and the numerical results are presented effectively in Figure 2 with appropriate analysis.

The paper could be further improved by:
1. Adding a brief paragraph explaining why the minimum rank is sufficient for the latent approach but not for the overlapped approach (beyond the mathematical explanation in the paper).
2. Clarifying the relationship between the multilinear rank of W* and the ranks of the latent factors in the decomposition.
3. Providing a more explicit discussion of how to choose the regularization constant λ in practice, especially since the theoretical optimal choice doesn't depend on the unknown ranks.

## Originality
The key contribution of this paper is establishing the duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms (Lemma 1), which generalizes results from structured sparsity literature. This duality is novel and provides a theoretical foundation for understanding the empirical performance differences between the two approaches.

The paper builds upon prior work by Tomioka et al. [25] and others on convex tensor decomposition, but the theoretical analysis showing that the error scales with the minimum mode-k rank (rather than the average) is a significant advancement. The connection to structured sparsity (group lasso) is well-executed and helps place the work in a broader context.

I don't find any overlap with recent NIPS papers (2018-2023) in the same area. The closest related work is Tomioka et al. [25] and [26], but this paper provides the theoretical foundation that was missing in those works.

## Significance
The paper addresses an important problem in tensor decomposition with applications in neuroimaging, collaborative filtering, and environmental monitoring. The theoretical results provide a clear explanation for the empirically observed superior performance of the latent approach, which is valuable for both practitioners and researchers.

The finding that the latent approach is generally preferable for noisy tensor decomposition (with error bounds scaling with the minimum mode-k rank) is significant. It provides a theoretical justification for using the latent approach over the overlapped approach in many practical scenarios. The empirical results in Section 4 confirm the theoretical predictions, making this a strong contribution to the field.

## Strengths
- Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis showing the latent approach's error scales with the minimum mode-k rank
- Empirically validates the theoretical predictions
- Connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity literature
- Addresses a practical limitation in existing tensor decomposition methods
- Well-written with clear organization and effective use of figures

## Weaknesses
- Limited discussion of computational complexity differences between approaches
- Incoherence assumption (inequality 10) isn't discussed in terms of practical applicability
- Doesn't explore cases where the tensor is low-rank in multiple modes but the latent approach might not be optimal
- The paper doesn't discuss how to choose λ in practice beyond the theoretical optimal choice

## Arguments for Acceptance
- The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of tensor decomposition approaches
- The duality result (Lemma 1) is novel and generalizes existing structured sparsity results
- The theoretical analysis provides a clear explanation for the empirical success of the latent approach
- The empirical results strongly support the theoretical claims
- The work connects tensor decomposition to broader structured sparsity literature
- The paper is well-written and technically sound

## Arguments against Acceptance
- The computational complexity of the latent approach isn't compared to the overlapped approach
- The incoherence assumption might limit applicability in some real-world scenarios
- The paper doesn't fully explore the limitations of the latent approach

## Overall Recommendation
I recommend this paper for acceptance at NIPS. The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to the field of tensor decomposition with clear practical implications. The duality result and the error bounds scaling with the minimum mode-k rank are novel and important insights. The empirical results effectively validate the theoretical claims. While there are some minor weaknesses in the discussion of practical implementation details, these don't detract from the overall quality and significance of the paper.

The paper addresses an important problem in a meaningful way, advances the state of the art, and provides a rigorous theoretical foundation for a practical approach to tensor decomposition. This work is likely to be influential in the tensor decomposition community and related areas of machine learning.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement
1. In Section 3.2, add a brief discussion of how restrictive the incoherence assumption (10) is in practice, with some examples of tensors that satisfy it.
2. In Section 4, include a brief comparison of computational times between the overlapped and latent approaches.
3. In the conclusion, briefly discuss potential limitations of the latent approach (e.g., when the tensor isn't low-rank in any single mode).
4. Consider adding a sentence in the introduction clarifying why the minimum rank is sufficient for the latent approach but not for the overlapped approach.

The paper is already strong, but these minor additions would make it even more valuable to the community.
