- Paper compares two convex tensor approaches (latent vs overlapped), proving a duality between their Schatten norms and showing latent error scales with the minimum mode rank while overlapped scales with the average mode ranks.  
- Connects tensor decomposition to structured sparsity, generalizing overlapped/latent group sparsity duality to tensors.  
- Lemma 1 establishes the duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms as a key theoretical contribution.  
- Theorems 2 and 3 show the latent approach’s error depends on the minimum mode-k rank, explaining empirical superiority in certain regimes.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, distinguishing noise regimes and providing tight bounds that incorporate low-rank structure.  
- Numerical experiments validate theory, demonstrating predicted linear MSE scaling and convincing comparisons across tensor sizes and ranks.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear motivation, accessible technical sections, and informative figures.  
- Provides practical guidance: latent approach is preferable when a tensor is low-rank in only one mode and performs at least as well when tensor is simultaneously low-rank.  
- Lacks concrete practical guidance for choosing the regularization parameter λ in real-world settings.  
- Does not address computational complexity or scalability differences between latent and overlapped convex formulations.  
- Assumes the true tensor is a sum of K tensors each low-rank in a specific mode (Eq. 9) without fully discussing how restrictive this is in practice.  
- Omits comparison to non-convex tensor methods (e.g., HOSVD, ALS), limiting context on performance and computational tradeoffs.  
- Mentions applications (neuroimaging, collaborative filtering) but provides no concrete case studies or domain examples.  
- Mathematical proofs appear sound with technical details in the supplement, and citations to prior work are appropriate.  
- The work is significant: it answers a fundamental question about convex tensor decomposition and may influence future convex methods by leveraging the minimum-rank insight.  
- Recommendation: accept (strong paper with minor revisions suggested).  
- Suggested revisions: add practical λ heuristics, discuss computational complexity/scalability, include a real-world example, analyze limitations of the Eq.9 model assumption, and compare to non-convex decomposition methods.
