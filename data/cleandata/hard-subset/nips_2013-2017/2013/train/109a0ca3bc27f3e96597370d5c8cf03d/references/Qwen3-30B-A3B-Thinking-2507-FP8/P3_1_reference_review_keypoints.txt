- Paper compares two convex tensor-decomposition approaches (overlapped vs latent) and introduces overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms.  
- Key theoretical contribution: duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Main result: latent approach MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, overlapped scales with the average (sqrt) of mode-k ranks.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: consistency (Thm 1), deterministic bounds (Thm 2), and Gaussian-noise analysis (Thm 3).  
- First theoretical analysis of the latent approach, building on empirical work by Tomioka et al.  
- Proofs are detailed and sound, with a solid connection to prior work (Agarwal et al.).  
- Empirical synthetic results validate theoretical scaling predictions.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and helpful figures (e.g., Fig. 1).  
- Supplementary material contains detailed proofs supporting the claims.  
- Lack of computational complexity analysis and no comparison of runtime or scalability between latent and overlapped approaches.  
- Incoherence assumption (Eq. 10) is restrictive and not justified or empirically evaluated for real data.  
- Experiments use only synthetic data; no real-world application to demonstrate practical utility.  
- No analysis or guidance on sensitivity to regularization parameter λ in practice.  
- Terminology conflates "mode-k rank" and "multilinear rank," which may confuse readers.  
- Connection to structured sparsity/group lasso is mentioned but not fully developed or mapped in detail.  
- Intuition for why latent outperforms overlapped in single-mode low-rank cases is technical and could be made more accessible.  
- Novelty centers on the duality result and latent-approach analysis; core approaches themselves were previously proposed.  
- Structured Schatten norms extend existing norms, with limited exploration of broader Sp/q families.  
- Paper focuses on denoising; results do not address tensor completion with missing entries.  
- No comparison to non-convex tensor-decomposition methods, leaving practical competitiveness unclear.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and novel theory, but practical limitations lead to recommendation "Weak Reject."  
- Suggested improvements: add real-world experiments, analyze computational complexity and λ selection, justify/verify incoherence, deepen group-lasso connections, compare to non-convex methods, and extend to tensor completion.
