Summary
This paper studies convex formulations for tensor decomposition based on structured Schatten norms. It contrasts two families: (i) overlapped norms that penalize the sum of nuclear norms of each matricization, and (ii) latent norms that model a tensor as a sum of components, each low-rank in one mode. The authors establish a duality between the overlapped Sp/q norm and a latent Sp*/q* norm via infimal convolution, generalizing known dualities in structured sparsity. They then analyze the statistical performance of the latent approach for denoising: a general consistency bound (no low-rank assumptions), deterministic error bounds under an incoherence-type condition with spectral-norm constraints on cross-mode unfoldings, and Gaussian noise bounds. A key inequality shows the latent Schatten-1 norm is controlled by the minimum mode rank, explaining why latent regularization adapts to the most compressible mode, unlike overlapped norms whose complexity depends on averages across modes. Synthetic experiments confirm linear scaling with their proposed complexity measure and show improvements over the overlapped approach, even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank.

Relation to prior work
Overlapped tensor nuclear norms and their analysis have been studied (e.g., Liu et al., Tomioka et al. 2011). The latent decomposition idea was proposed empirically (Tomioka et al. 2010/2011). The paper’s main novelty is a rigorous analysis of the latent approach’s denoising performance and a clear duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, paralleling results for overlapped/latent group lasso. The comparison with known bounds for overlapped norms (Tomioka et al. 2011/2013) is appropriate and highlights a qualitatively different dependence on rank structure.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The duality lemma follows cleanly from convex duality of sums vs infimal convolution, and the latent-norm/Frobenius inequality leveraging minimum mode rank is plausible and important. The consistency bound using λ ≥ ||E||S∞/∞ is standard. The sharper deterministic/Gaussian bounds rely on additional spectral-norm constraints and an incoherence-style assumption (10). These are reasonable but somewhat strong.
- Assumptions and estimator: The constrained estimator (11) introduces spectral norm constraints with parameter α to control cross-mode correlations. This strengthens identifiability but raises two concerns: (a) α is unknown; (b) it is unclear if these constraints were enforced in experiments (the text cites ADMM from [25], which did not include such constraints). If constraints were inactive (large α), the theoretical guarantees may not match the empirical setup; clarification is needed.
- Rates: The Gaussian noise choice of λ is data dimension dependent and matches the maximum spectral norm of unfoldings; the resulting MSE scales with σ^2 times min mode-rank (up to mild factors). This is a meaningful improvement over prior overlapped bounds that scale with averages of ranks.
- Experiments: Synthetic denoising on modest sizes supports the theory’s scaling and shows consistent improvements. However, experiments are limited: no tensor completion, no real datasets, and no runtime/scaling comparisons.

Clarity
The paper is generally well organized. Definitions of unfoldings, norms, and the latent/overlapped formulations are clear. Some notation is heavy (e.g., W(k)(k)), and a brief diagram or worked example of the decomposition could aid intuition. Several references are listed as “technical report”/typos; polishing bibliography would help. Please explicitly state whether the spectral-norm constraints in (11) were used in the experiments and how α was chosen.

Originality
- New: Duality between overlapped/latent Schatten norms for tensors; statistical denoising guarantees for latent Schatten-1 regularization with bounds depending on min mode rank; explanation of adaptation to the most compressible mode.
- Relation to known results: Extends structured sparsity dualities to tensor Schatten norms and complements prior analysis of overlapped norms.

Significance
The results sharpen our understanding of convex tensor regularization by showing that latent formulations can adapt to favorable mode-wise structure and outperform overlapped norms in common regimes. This is valuable theoretically and suggestive for practice. The constrained estimator and focus on denoising limit immediate applicability; extending to tensor completion and broader noise models would increase impact.

Pros
- Clear and useful duality result connecting overlapped and latent Schatten norms.
- New statistical error bounds for the latent approach with favorable dependence on min mode rank.
- Good comparison and positioning relative to prior overlapped analyses.
- Experiments that align with the theoretical scaling and show empirical gains.

Cons
- Strong additional constraints (spectral-norm bounds with α) in the main deterministic/Gaussian analysis; practicality and selection of α are unclear.
- Experimental scope is narrow (synthetic denoising only), with limited assessment of robustness and computational scalability.
- Some notation and bibliographic polishing needed.
- No discussion of sample complexity for missing data (tensor completion), which is a primary application.

Questions for authors
- Were the spectral-norm constraints in (11) enforced in your experiments? If so, how was α chosen; if not, how do you expect the bounds to change?
- Can your analysis be adapted to the unconstrained latent estimator (5), e.g., via decomposability and restricted strong convexity arguments?
- How does runtime scale with K and dimensions compared to the overlapped approach? Any empirical timing?
- Do results extend to tensor completion with random sampling and to non-Gaussian/heavy-tailed noise?

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper provides a solid theoretical contribution—duality and improved error bounds for latent tensor regularization—tied to clear empirical evidence. Addressing the role of α/constraints and broadening experiments would strengthen the work further.
