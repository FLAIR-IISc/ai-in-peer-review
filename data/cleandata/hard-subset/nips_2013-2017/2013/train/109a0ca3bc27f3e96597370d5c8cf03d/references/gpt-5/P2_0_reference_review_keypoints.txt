- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition using overlapped vs latent structured Schatten norms and analyzes their performance for denoising.  
- Main theoretical contribution: duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms via infimal convolution, generalizing structured-sparsity dualities.  
- Provides statistical analysis of the latent approach: consistency bound (no low-rank assumption), deterministic error bounds under an incoherence-style condition, and Gaussian-noise bounds.  
- Key inequality: the latent Schatten-1 norm is controlled by the minimum mode rank, explaining adaptation to the most compressible mode.  
- Shows latent regularization can outperform overlapped norms whose complexity depends on average ranks.  
- Synthetic experiments support the theoretical linear scaling with the proposed complexity measure and show empirical gains for latent method.  
- Prior work: builds on overlapped tensor nuclear norms and empirical latent decompositions (Tomioka et al.); novelty is rigorous analysis and duality for Schatten norms.  
- Technical soundness: duality and latent-norm bounds are plausible and well-derived; consistency bound is standard.  
- Main theoretical caveat: deterministic/Gaussian bounds rely on additional spectral-norm constraints and an incoherence-style assumption that are somewhat strong.  
- Practical concern: constrained estimator (11) uses spectral-norm bound parameter α, but α is unknown and unclear if constraints were used in experiments.  
- If constraints were inactive in experiments, theoretical guarantees may not match empirical setup — clarification needed.  
- Rates: Gaussian-noise choice of λ depends on data dimensions and yields MSE scaling with σ^2 times min mode-rank, an improvement over overlapped bounds.  
- Experiments are limited: only modest-size synthetic denoising, no tensor completion, no real datasets, and no runtime/scalability comparisons.  
- Clarity: generally well organized and definitions clear, but some heavy notation and bibliography/typos need polishing; a worked example would help.  
- Originality: new duality result and statistical denoising guarantees for latent Schatten-1; extends structured-sparsity dualities to tensors.  
- Significance: sharpens understanding of convex tensor regularization and suggests practical advantages for latent formulations, but focus on denoising and constrained estimator limits immediate applicability.  
- Pros: clear duality, favorable new error bounds tied to min mode-rank, good positioning vs prior work, experiments consistent with theory.  
- Cons: strong additional constraints and unclear α selection, narrow experimental scope, notation/bibliography issues, no sample-complexity results for tensor completion.  
- Questions for authors: were spectral-norm constraints used in experiments and how was α chosen; can analysis adapt to unconstrained estimator; runtime comparisons vs overlapped; extensions to tensor completion and non-Gaussian noise?  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid theoretical contribution with supporting experiments, but needs clarification on α/constraints and broader empirical validation.
