## Peer Review: Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a valuable theoretical analysis of the latent approach to convex tensor decomposition, providing insights into its superior performance compared to the overlapped approach, particularly when dealing with tensors low-rank in only one mode. The work is technically sound, well-written, and builds upon existing literature in convex optimization and tensor decomposition. While the core ideas have been empirically observed (as cited in the introduction), this paper provides a rigorous justification and a clearer understanding of the underlying mechanisms. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper tackles the problem of decomposing tensors into low-rank components using convex optimization.  Traditional methods rely on non-convex optimization, while recent work has explored convex relaxations based on Schatten norm regularization. Two main approaches have emerged: the “overlapped” approach, which penalizes all unfoldings of the tensor simultaneously, and the “latent” approach, which decomposes the tensor into a mixture of tensors, each low-rank in a specific mode.  This paper focuses on the latent approach, providing a theoretical analysis of its performance, proving consistency results, and establishing denoising guarantees. 

A key contribution is establishing a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing previous results in structured sparsity. The authors connect their work to related areas like group lasso and demonstrate how these concepts translate to tensor decomposition. The work builds upon prior contributions in convex relaxation for tensor decomposition ([9, 15, 23, 25, 26]) and extends them with theoretical justification and a performance bound linked to the minimal mode-k rank.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including consistency results and a deterministic bound on the error, based on the minimal mode-k rank. This is a significant contribution, as previous work lacked such guarantees.
* **Clear Explanation of Latent Approach's Superiority:**  The analysis effectively explains why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode, connecting it to the scaling of the error bound with the minimal rank.
* **Novel Duality Result:** The established duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a valuable theoretical insight, linking this work to broader concepts in structured sparsity.
* **Well-Written & Organized:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized, making it easy to follow the arguments and understand the contributions.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The numerical experiments support the theoretical findings, confirming the predicted scaling behavior and demonstrating the improved performance of the latent approach.
* **Thorough Related Work Discussion:** The authors provide a comprehensive overview of relevant prior work in tensor decomposition, convex optimization, and structured sparsity.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Assumptions for Deterministic Bound:** The deterministic bound (Theorem 2) requires a somewhat strong incoherence assumption (Equation 10).  The practical implications of this assumption could be discussed further. How sensitive are the results to violations of this assumption?
* **Complexity of Implementation:** While the paper provides a link to the solver, the implementation details of the ADMM algorithm used in the experiments are not fully described.  More details would enhance reproducibility.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments focus primarily on Gaussian noise. Investigating performance under different noise distributions or with missing data could provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
* **Minor Clarity Issue:** The notation can be dense at times, particularly in Section 2.2.  A more careful introduction of each symbol could improve readability.




**4. Detailed Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results. The mathematical derivations appear correct, and the empirical validation aligns with the theoretical predictions. The sensitivity analysis of the assumptions could be strengthened.
* **Clarity (7/10):** The paper is generally well-written, but the density of notation in Section 2.2 can be challenging.  More explicit definitions and explanations of symbols would improve clarity.
* **Originality (8/10):** While building upon existing work, the paper presents a novel theoretical analysis and a duality result that significantly advance the understanding of latent tensor decomposition.
* **Significance (9/10):** The results are important as they provide a theoretical justification for the empirically observed superior performance of the latent approach. The insights gained from this work are likely to be valuable to researchers and practitioners working on tensor decomposition and related problems.




**5. Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Rigorous theoretical analysis justifying the latent approach.
* Novel duality result linking overlapped and latent norms.
* Empirical validation supporting the theoretical findings.
* Clear and well-organized presentation.
* Addresses a significant problem in tensor decomposition.

**Con:**

* Deterministic bound relies on a potentially strong incoherence assumption.
* Limited scope of experiments (primarily Gaussian noise).
* Some density in notation requires careful reading.



**6. Recommendation:**

**Accept with minor revisions.** I recommend acceptance of this paper. The theoretical contributions are significant, the paper is well-written, and the empirical results support the claims.  I suggest the following minor revisions:

* **Discuss the sensitivity of the results to violations of the incoherence assumption (Equation 10).**
* **Provide more details on the implementation of the ADMM algorithm used in the experiments.**
* **Consider expanding the experiments to include different noise distributions or missing data scenarios.**
* **Review the notation for potential simplification and clarity, particularly in Section 2.2.**

These revisions would further strengthen the paper and enhance its impact on the research community.
