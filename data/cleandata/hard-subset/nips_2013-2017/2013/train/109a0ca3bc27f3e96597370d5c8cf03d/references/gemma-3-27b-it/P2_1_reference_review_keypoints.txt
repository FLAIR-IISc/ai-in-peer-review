- Paper compares convex tensor decomposition methods (overlapped vs. latent Schatten norm) and shows latent often performs better, especially when low-rank in a single mode.
- Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms linking to structured sparsity literature.
- Theoretical results: consistency and denoising bounds, with latent error scaling by minimum rank vs. overlapped by average rank.
- Empirical experiments support theory, showing latent typically outperforms overlapped even for simultaneously low-rank tensors.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and mathematically justified.
- Duality result is a significant and original contribution.
- Clear explanation of why latent excels in single-mode low-rank scenarios.
- Good connections and citations to matrix completion, sparse recovery, and tensor decomposition literature.
- Numerical experiments are limited in scope and mainly use random data, reducing real-world generality.
- Theoretical results rely on assumptions (e.g., incoherence) that may not hold in practice; robustness discussion is needed.
- Some repetitive discussion of the latent approach’s advantages harms conciseness.
- Notation and some definitions are introduced late, causing minor clarity issues.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable theoretical and empirical contributions, minor clarity and experiment-scope weaknesses.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand experiments, and discuss robustness to assumption violations.
