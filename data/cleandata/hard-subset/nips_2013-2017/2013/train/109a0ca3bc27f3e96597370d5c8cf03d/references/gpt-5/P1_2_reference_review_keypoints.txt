- Paper studies convex low-rank tensor estimation using overlapped Sp/q norms and latent Sp*/q* norms, proving a duality between them (for p=q=1 dual is overlapped S∞/∞).
- Latent approach analyzed for tensor denoising: consistency bound with λ ≥ ||E||S∞/∞.
- Deterministic bound under incoherence-style spectral constraints yields squared error scaling with sum of latent ranks and with min mode-k rank via singleton decomposition.
- High-probability Gaussian-noise bound with λ tuned to dimensions and σ gives MSE scaling ∝ min_k r_k (up to mild dimension factors).
- Theory explains why latent outperforms overlapped when tensor is low-rank in only one mode; synthetic experiments corroborate scalings.
- Duality lemma generalizes overlapped/latent group-sparsity dualities and is carefully stated.
- Key inequality (latent S1/1 bounded by min_k sqrt(r_k)·||·||F) clarifies differing statistical behavior from overlapped norm.
- Analyses use decomposable-regularizer framework and standard spectral-norm concentration arguments; technically sound.
- Strongest theorems require additional spectral-norm constraints (parameter α) modeling incoherence, raising practical questions about setting α and necessity.
- Gaussian/full-observation setting is clear; tensor completion is left for future work.
- Empirical support: synthetic experiments align with theory; solver is publicly available and plots of MSE vs complexities are informative.
- Clarity: paper mostly clear and organized, but notation r_k vs r̄_k can confuse and proof sketches for key lemmas/theorems should be added.
- Motivation for α and constraints (eq. 11) should appear earlier and practical guidance on λ and α is needed.
- Originality: novel generalization of overlapped/latent duality to tensor Schatten norms and new insight on error bounds scaling with min mode rank.
- Significance: results guide when to prefer latent nuclear norms and may influence norm design and analysis despite focus on denoising.
- Strengths: unifying framework, clean duality, simple explanatory inequality, interpretable deterministic/probabilistic bounds, empirical validation, public code.
- Weaknesses: practicality of α and constrained program unclear; λ depends on unknown σ and needs practical selection guidance.
- Weaknesses: Gaussian bound asymmetry favors smallest mode n_K—may be proof artifact and could be tightened for symmetry.
- Weaknesses: limited experiments (no real data, no completion), need sensitivity analyses for α and λ, and scaling to larger tensors.
- Weaknesses: LR complexity plotting uses an oracle decomposition which may favor latent method—clarify plotting procedure and report min_k r_k results.
- Minor issues: unify notation r_k vs r̄_k and fix small typos/formatting.
- Questions for authors: can spectral-norm constraints be removed under standard incoherence on W*, and guidance/empirical sensitivity for α?
- Questions for authors: is singleton decomposition near-optimal where latent excels, and any identifiability discussion for components W^(k)?
- Questions for authors: can analysis extend to tensor completion (RIP/sampling-based bounds) using the duality tools?
- Reasons to accept: unifying, technically solid contribution with novel duality and error bounds that clarify when to use latent norms; empirical consistency and available code.
- Reasons to reject: limited empirical scope and reliance on additional constraints/α not justified in practice.
- Recommendation: Accept, pending minor clarifications and broader experiments.
