Summary
This paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor estimation using structured Schatten-norm regularization. Two families are considered: (i) an “overlapped” approach that penalizes the sum of Schatten norms of mode-unfoldings (encouraging simultaneous low rank across modes), and (ii) a “latent” approach that decomposes the tensor into a sum of components, each low-rank in one mode, penalized by the sum of their mode-specific nuclear norms. The authors establish a duality between these two families: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is a latent Sp*/q* norm (generalizing known overlapped/latent group sparsity dualities). They then analyze the denoising performance of the latent approach: a consistency bound (for arbitrary tensors), a deterministic error bound under an incoherence-type constraint on cross-mode unfoldings, and high-probability bounds for Gaussian noise. A key message is that, unlike prior results for the overlapped approach (whose error depends on averages over mode ranks), the latent approach’s error scales with the minimal mode-k rank of the ground truth, explaining empirical advantages when the tensor is low-rank predominantly in a single mode. Synthetic experiments corroborate the scaling predictions.

Relation to prior work
The paper is well-situated relative to convex matrix/tensor recovery (Fazel et al.; Recht et al.; Signoretto et al.), structured sparsity and latent group lasso (Bach, Jenatton, Obozinski et al.), and recent convex tensor methods (Liu et al.; Tomioka et al.; Gandy et al.). It builds directly on [Tomioka et al. NIPS’11] which analyzed the overlapped Schatten-1 regularizer, and complements [Mu et al. 2013] who give lower bounds. The duality result connects neatly to the latent/overlapped group norms literature, and the denoising analysis for the latent tensor norm appears new.

Strengths
- Clear conceptual contribution: formal duality between overlapped and latent tensor Schatten norms (general Sp/q family).
- Theoretical advances: consistency, deterministic, and probabilistic denoising bounds for the latent approach. The dependence on mink rk is intuitively compelling and contrasts prior bounds for overlapped norms.
- Careful comparison with prior theory (explicit juxtaposition with [26]) that matches and explains empirical behavior observed previously.
- Experiments, though synthetic, align with the theory, including the predicted dependence on sizes and ranks and relative performance versus the overlapped approach.
- Connections to structured sparsity provide a unifying perspective.

Weaknesses
- Assumptions and estimator mismatch: The key deterministic and Gaussian bounds rely on problem (11), which adds spectral norm constraints on cross-mode unfoldings with parameter α. It is unclear whether these constraints are used in practice (and in the experiments); the ADMM solver referenced in [25] typically handles the unconstrained latent formulation. If constraints are not enforced, the theoretical guarantee may not apply to the implemented estimator.
- Incoherence condition (10): Its practicality is not fully discussed; α may be large in realistic scenarios, and choosing α in practice is not addressed.
- Scope of experiments: Only Gaussian denoising on synthetic tensors of moderate size; no real data or missing-data (completion) experiments. No sensitivity analyses for α or robustness to model misspecification.
- Tightness/optimality: The paper does not present lower bounds for the latent estimator or discuss minimax-optimality; the factor F in Theorem 3 is somewhat opaque, and it’s not fully clear how tight the dependence on dimensions is.
- Clarity details: Some notation and footnotes (e.g., “unfold1”) could be cleaned; proofs are deferred, which is standard, but some key intuition for Lemma 2 and Theorem 2 could be expanded in the main text.

Quality
Technically sound overall. The norm duality is plausible and consistent with known structured sparsity dualities; the proofs are in the supplement. The deterministic and Gaussian denoising bounds are reasonable and connect to standard spectral norm concentration. The main caveat is the reliance on additional constraints (α) for the key bounds and the lack of clarity on whether these constraints are used empirically.

Clarity
Generally well written and organized. The motivation is clear, definitions are precise, and the progression from norms to duality to statistical analysis is coherent. Suggestions:
- Explicitly state whether the constrained problem (11) is used in experiments; if not, discuss how analysis may extend to the unconstrained estimator.
- Provide brief intuition sketches in the main text for Lemma 2 (why min rank appears) and Theorem 2 (role of decomposability and projections).
- Clarify the choice and impact of α and give guidance for practice.
- Minor typos/formatting (footnotes; consistent notation for unfold/fold).

Originality
The extension of overlapped/latent duality to tensor Schatten norms and the statistical analysis for the latent tensor norm seem novel. The shift from average (sqrt) rank dependence to minimal rank dependence in the bounds is a meaningful conceptual advance.

Significance
Understanding when latent convex tensor models outperform overlapped ones is important for practical tensor learning. The results provide actionable guidance (prefer latent norms when rank is concentrated in one mode) and a theoretical foundation that researchers can build upon (e.g., extension to completion, other noise models). The work should be of interest to the NIPS community working on high-dimensional statistics, convex optimization, and tensor methods.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Do you actually enforce the spectral norm constraints in (11) in your experiments? If not, can you comment on extending the analysis to the unconstrained estimator, or empirically show little difference?
- How to set α in practice? Can you provide a heuristic and sensitivity analysis?
- Can your Gaussian noise analysis be extended to sub-Gaussian noise with similar λ?
- Any insights on lower bounds for the latent estimator to assess tightness?
- A small real-data case study (even denoising) would strengthen the practical message.

Pros
- Strong, clear theory with a novel duality and improved error dependence.
- Solid comparison to prior overlapped results, both theoretically and empirically.
- Unifying perspective connecting tensor norms and structured sparsity.

Cons
- Reliance on additional constraints not clearly reflected in experiments.
- Limited experimental scope; no real data or completion.
- Practical guidance for α and λ selection could be more concrete.

Recommendation: Weak accept. The paper makes a meaningful theoretical contribution with clear relevance to convex tensor learning, and the results are likely to influence practice. Strengthening clarity around the constrained estimator and adding discussion on α would further improve the work.
