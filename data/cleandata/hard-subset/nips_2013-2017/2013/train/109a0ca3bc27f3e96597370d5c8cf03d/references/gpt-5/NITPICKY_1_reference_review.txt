Summary
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor estimation and denoising. It contrasts two families of structured Schatten norms: (i) “overlapped” norms that sum Schatten norms of all mode-unfoldings, and (ii) “latent” norms that decompose a tensor into a sum of components, each low-rank in a specific mode, and penalize their unfoldings. The main theoretical contributions are: (a) a duality result showing the dual of the overlapped Sp/q-norm is a latent Sp*/q*-norm; (b) generic consistency for latent S1/1-regularized denoising; (c) deterministic and Gaussian-noise error bounds for the latent approach that scale with the minimum mode-k rank (or sum of latent component ranks), explaining empirical superiority over the overlapped approach whose complexity depends on the average mode ranks. Experiments on synthetic 3-way tensors illustrate scaling behavior consistent with theory.

Strengths
- Clear and relevant problem: low-rank tensor estimation is central in many NIPS areas.
- Conceptual unification: the duality between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms parallels known results for overlapped/latent group lasso and is a useful conceptual bridge.
- Nontrivial analysis: the latent approach is shown to achieve error scaling governed by mink rk (or sum of component ranks), matching intuitions and aligning with lower bound intuitions in recent work.
- Helpful inequalities: Lemma 2 (latent norm bounded by mink √rk ||W||F) clarifies why latent can outperform overlapped.
- Experiments qualitatively support the theory; code is referenced.

Weaknesses and concerns
Quality/technical
1) Estimator mismatch: The core analysis in Theorem 2 and Theorem 3 studies an estimator with additional spectral-norm incoherence constraints (problem (11): ∥W(l)(k)∥S∞ ≤ α for l ≠ k). The algorithm used in experiments (ADMM from [25]) appears to solve the unconstrained latent problem (5)/(6). This misalignment weakens the empirical validation of the theory and obscures practical guidance for choosing α. Please either analyze the unconstrained estimator or enforce/verify the constraints in experiments.

2) Regularization selection: The theory proposes a λ independent of unknown ranks; experiments nevertheless tune λ over 20 values and report “representative” choices per method, which is effectively oracle tuning. No hold-out, SURE, or theoretically motivated λ is used to evaluate performance. The claim that optimal λ depends only on size is not convincingly demonstrated.

3) Gaussian bound constants and dimensions: The expression for λ in Theorem 3 and the factor F mix terms (√(N/nK), √n1, √log(K/δ), α(K−1)) in a way that suggests typos or missing scaling by σ (e.g., “α(K − 1)2σ” in F looks malformed). Please check units and present a clean bound with explicit constants.

4) Consistency bound (Theorem 1) is very loose: ||Ŵ−W*||F ≤ 2λ √(mink nk) does not capture low-rank structure and can be vacuous. This is fine as a warm-up but should be clearly positioned as such.

5) Duality proof and assumptions: The duality Lemma 1 is central but deferred. Please state precise conditions (p,q ≥ 1, norm equivalence with appropriate weights, completeness of the latent decomposition space), and clarify whether the dual holds for all K-way unfoldings with the same infimum structure. Also clarify uniqueness/attainment issues of the latent decomposition.

6) Latent LR complexity used in experiments is computed by running the latent method on noise-free W* to find a decomposition with small sum-of-ranks; this uses algorithmic outcomes on ground truth and is not an inherent property accessible in practice. This introduces circularity in the complexity-versus-error plots.

7) No missing data or tensor completion experiments: Claims emphasize noisy tensor decomposition; however, the broader motivation includes completion and CF with contextual dimensions. Including completion experiments would substantively increase significance.

8) No runtime or scalability discussion: For large tensors, the latent approach involves K SVDs per iteration. Empirical complexity, convergence behavior, and wall-clock time are not reported.

Clarity/organization
9) Notational ambiguity: The paper reuses the same notation |||W|||S1/1 for both overlapped (eq. (1)/(2)) and latent (eq. (6)) norms, distinguished only by prose. This is a serious clarity issue. Use distinct symbols, e.g., |||W|||over,S1/1 versus |||W|||lat,S1/1, and maintain this consistently throughout (including lemmas, theorems, and experiments).

10) Inconsistent symbols: rk vs r̄k (component rank) are used; ensure consistent, prominent definitions where both appear (Section 3.2). Carefully distinguish multilinear ranks (rk of W*) from latent factor ranks (r̄k of W*(k)).

11) Presentation glitches: Equation references sometimes missing; a dangling footnote (“For a K-way tensor…”) appears mid-paragraph; some inequalities have mismatched parentheses.

Originality/related work
12) Positioning relative to Mu et al. (“Square Deal”) and other tensor nuclear norms (e.g., tubal nuclear norms, t-SVD) is brief; while outside your immediate scope, a short discussion would situate the contribution better.

13) The relation to Negahban et al. decomposable regularizers is alluded to but not made precise (which subspace pair, decomposability constants, RSC/RSS conditions). Stating these would strengthen the analysis.

Formatting and references
14) References are incomplete and inconsistent (missing years/venues; “prepint”, “Prof. ICCV”; capitalization and punctuation errors). This needs a thorough cleanup to meet NeurIPS standards.

15) Typos/grammar: multiple minor issues (“betters” → “performs better”, “noiseless”, “profint/Prof.”, subscript formatting “mink”), spacing around equations, and inconsistent hyphenation.

Questions for authors
- Can you align the analyzed estimator with the implemented one, or provide empirical evidence that the added spectral-norm constraints do not change solutions meaningfully?
- Can you provide results on tensor completion with varying sampling rates?
- Can you report runtime, iteration counts, and stop criteria?
- Can you clarify and fix the λ and F expressions (Theorem 3) and provide a simple corollary with order-sharp rates and clean constants?

Pros
- Solid theoretical insight connecting overlapped/latent norms via duality.
- Bounds that match intuition and highlight the advantage when only one mode is low-rank.
- Useful inequalities and a unifying framework across structured norms.

Cons
- Significant clarity and notation issues; many editorial errors.
- Estimator/theory mismatch and λ tuning concerns.
- Experiments are limited; no completion or real data; circularity in complexity metric.
- References and presentation below venue standards.

Overall recommendation: Weak reject
While the core ideas are interesting and potentially impactful, the accumulation of clarity issues, estimator mismatch, limited experiments, and reference/formatting problems prevent me from recommending acceptance at this time. With careful revision of notation, tighter and cleaner statements (with corrected constants), alignment between analysis and implementation, and stronger experimental validation (including completion and real data, principled λ selection, and runtime), this work could be competitive.
