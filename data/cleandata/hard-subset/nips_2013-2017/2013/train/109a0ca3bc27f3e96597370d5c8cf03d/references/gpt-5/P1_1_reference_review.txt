Summary
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor decomposition based on structured Schatten norms. Two families are considered: (i) overlapped norms, which penalize all mode unfoldings simultaneously (e.g., sum of nuclear norms of mode-k matricizations), and (ii) latent norms, which represent the tensor as a sum of K components, each penalized to be low-rank in a specific mode. The authors establish a duality between the overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms, generalizing known dualities in structured sparsity. The central technical result is that the latent Schatten-1 regularization leads to denoising error bounds scaling with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor, rather than the (average) dependence established previously for the overlapped approach. They provide: (a) a consistency bound depending on the overlapped S∞/∞ norm of the noise, (b) a deterministic bound under an incoherence-like spectral constraint across modes, and (c) a high-probability bound for Gaussian noise with a theoretically chosen λ. Experiments on synthetic denoising confirm the predicted scaling and show advantages over the overlapped approach, even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across modes.

Relation to prior work
Convex tensor regularization via overlapped nuclear norms has been explored (e.g., Liu et al., Signoretto et al., Tomioka et al. 2011/2013) with statistical analysis in Tomioka et al. (NIPS’11). The present paper addresses an empirical shortcoming of overlapped norms when low-rankness concentrates in one mode, providing theory for the latent approach previously proposed in Tomioka et al. (2013). The duality between overlapped/latent tensor norms parallels latent/overlapped group lasso dualities (Obozinski et al.; Jenatton et al.; Maurer & Pontil). The comparison with Mu et al. (lower bounds) is appropriate.

Strengths
- Clear conceptual contribution: identifies and formalizes latent tensor Schatten norms and proves their duality to overlapped norms.
- Theoretical advances: new error bounds for latent regularization that scale with mink rk, explaining empirical advantages; consistency and Gaussian-noise results with explicit λ choices.
- Technical lemmas (e.g., latent norm ≤ min_k sqrt(rk) × Frobenius norm) highlight the structural benefit of the latent formulation (singleton decomposition).
- Empirical validation: synthetic denoising experiments corroborate scaling with proposed complexity and show improved MSE over overlapped norms.
- Connections to structured sparsity are well made and broaden relevance.

Weaknesses and questions
- Assumptions and constraints: The deterministic and probabilistic bounds hinge on additional spectral-norm constraints across components (incoherence via α) and require λ ≥ 2||E||S∞/∞ + α(K−1). How α is chosen in practice, and how these constraints are enforced by the ADMM solver, are not fully described. Sensitivity to α and the feasibility of these constraints for realistic signals are unclear.
- Tightness and constants: The factor F and constants c are left unspecified, and the dimensional dependence in λ (√(N/nK)+√n1) may be loose relative to sharp random matrix concentration for rectangular unfoldings. A discussion of tightness or lower bounds for latent norms would help.
- Experimental scope: Only fully observed denoising is considered; no missing data, tensor completion, or outlier corruption scenarios. Sizes are modest, and evaluation is limited to overlapped vs latent; no comparison to nonconvex baselines (ALS for Tucker/CP), weighted/anisotropic overlapped norms, or alternative tensor nuclear norms.
- Oracle use in complexity plotting: The LR complexity axis uses a decomposition estimated on the noiseless W* to approximate ∑ r̄k, which grants oracle knowledge and could bias interpretation. While acceptable for scaling demonstration, this should be acknowledged more explicitly.
- Notational clarity: Both overlapped and latent norms are denoted S1/1; although context distinguishes them, distinct symbols would avoid confusion.

Quality
The results appear technically sound and well-motivated. Lemma 1 (duality) aligns with known convex-analytic constructions in structured sparsity; Lemma 2 and the resulting bounds are coherent with the singleton-decomposition intuition. The proofs are relegated to the supplement; within space limits this is understandable, but a sketch of key steps (e.g., how duality and decomposability drive the errors) would aid readability.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-organized. Definitions of unfolding, ranks, and norms are adequate. Suggestions:
- Use distinct notation for overlapped vs latent norms (e.g., O- and L-prefix).
- State more explicitly how constraints in (11) are implemented and how α is set in experiments.
- Provide a brief proof sketch for Theorem 3 to show where each term in λ comes from.

Originality
The extension of overlapped/latent duality to Schatten norms for tensors and the statistical analysis of the latent tensor norm are, to my knowledge, new and nontrivial. The work is a meaningful step beyond Tomioka et al. (NIPS’11) and Tomioka et al. (2013).

Significance
Understanding when and why latent tensor regularization outperforms overlapped norms matters for high-dimensional multiway data. The mink rk scaling is a sharp and interpretable message likely to influence practice and further theory (e.g., sample complexity for completion). The duality result may inspire new algorithmic developments and complexity analyses.

Suggestions for improvement
- Discuss practical selection of α and provide ablations varying α to illustrate its effect on performance and the validity of the bound.
- Expand experiments: include tensor completion with varying sampling rates; compare to ALS-based Tucker/CP and to weighted overlapped nuclear norms; report runtime and memory.
- Replace the oracle LR complexity with a proxy measurable from data, or at least add experiments showing robustness of the scaling w.r.t. approximate LR estimates.
- Consider adaptive/weighted latent penalties to further exploit mode imbalance; discuss whether the theory extends.
- Clarify whether the λ chosen per Theorem 3 is used in experiments or selected by validation; include sensitivity plots.

Minor comments
- Fix small typos and ensure consistent references (e.g., “preprint”; missing years).
- Footnote markers and equation numbering could be cleaned for readability.

Pros for acceptance
- Solid, novel theory with clear practical implication (mink rk scaling).
- Elegant convex-analytic duality connecting two important families of norms.
- Empirical results consistent with theory and relevant to NIPS audience.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited experimental scope; no completion or real data.
- Dependence on spectral-norm constraints and α is under-explained.
- Some looseness in constants and dimension dependence; no lower bounds for latent case.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper makes a meaningful theoretical contribution with clear insights and promising empirical support; expanding empirical validation and clarifying assumptions would strengthen it further.
