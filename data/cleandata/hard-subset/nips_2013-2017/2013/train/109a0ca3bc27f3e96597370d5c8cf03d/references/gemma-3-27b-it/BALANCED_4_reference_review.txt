This paper investigates convex relaxation for tensor decomposition, specifically comparing the “overlapped” and “latent” approaches based on structured Schatten norm regularization. The core contribution lies in a theoretical analysis demonstrating why the latent approach, which decomposes a tensor into a mixture of low-rank components along individual modes, outperforms the overlapped approach, which penalizes low rank across all modes simultaneously. The authors establish consistency results and derive error bounds, showing the latent approach’s error scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank, explaining its superior performance when the tensor is nearly full rank in all but one mode. A key technical contribution is the identification of a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing previous work on structured sparsity. Empirical results support the theoretical findings.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach, a significant improvement over prior work which often lacked theoretical guarantees. The consistency result and the error bound scaling with the minimum rank are valuable contributions.
*   **Novel Duality Result:**  Establishing the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a noteworthy technical result, connecting tensor decomposition to the broader field of structured sparsity. This connection potentially opens up avenues for further research and the application of existing techniques.
*   **Clear Explanation of Performance Difference:** The paper offers a compelling explanation for the empirically observed superior performance of the latent approach, grounding the observation in theoretical results concerning rank scaling.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments confirm the theoretical predictions and provide further evidence for the advantages of the latent approach. The comparison against the overlapped approach strengthens the claims.
*   **Well-written and Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The definitions and notation are clearly presented, and the connection between the different concepts is well-explained.
*   **Relevant References:** The authors demonstrate good awareness of the related literature, citing relevant papers in tensor decomposition, structured sparsity, and convex optimization.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Assumptions for Deterministic Bound:**  The tighter deterministic bound (Theorem 2) relies on the strong assumption of a specific factorization structure of the ground truth tensor (W* = ∑ W*(k)) and an incoherence condition (bounded spectral norms of off-diagonal components). While common in similar analyses, the practical applicability of these assumptions could be limited. It would be helpful to discuss the sensitivity of the results to violations of these assumptions.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments primarily focus on Gaussian noise. Exploring the performance of the proposed approach under different noise distributions or in more realistic scenarios (e.g., missing data, different data modalities) would strengthen the empirical validation.
*   **Computational Complexity:** While the theoretical analysis provides scaling results, the practical computational complexity of the latent approach is not thoroughly discussed. A comparison of computational costs with the overlapped approach would be valuable.
*   **Connection to Existing Work:** While the paper establishes a connection to group lasso, a deeper discussion of how this framework relates to other related approaches in tensor decomposition (e.g., Tucker decomposition with regularization, CP decomposition) would be beneficial.
* **Lack of discussion on parameter tuning**: The paper quickly glosses over the selection of the regularization parameter λ. A more thorough discussion of practical considerations for choosing λ, perhaps with a cross-validation strategy, would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical advancement in understanding the properties of convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norm regularization.
*   The duality result and the analysis of the latent approach are novel and contribute to the broader field of structured sparsity.
*   The empirical results support the theoretical findings and demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed approach.
*   The paper is well-written, organized, and addresses a relevant problem in tensor decomposition.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The strong assumptions required for the tighter theoretical bounds may limit the practical applicability of the results.
*   The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, exploring a wider range of scenarios and noise distributions.
*   A more detailed discussion of computational complexity and parameter tuning would strengthen the paper.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions to the field of tensor decomposition. While the assumptions for the tighter bounds and the limited scope of the experiments are weaknesses, the novelty of the results and the clear explanation of the performance differences between the overlapped and latent approaches outweigh these concerns.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.  Specifically, the authors should address the limitations of the assumptions in Theorem 2, discuss the computational complexity of the latent approach, and consider expanding the experimental evaluation. Adding a discussion about parameter selection (λ) would also be helpful.
