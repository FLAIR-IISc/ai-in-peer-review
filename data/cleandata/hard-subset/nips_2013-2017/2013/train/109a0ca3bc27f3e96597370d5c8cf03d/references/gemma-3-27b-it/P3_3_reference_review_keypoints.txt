- Overall assessment: valuable theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Core result: latent Schatten-norm approach has better reconstruction error, scaling with the tensor's minimum rank versus overlapped's average rank.  
- Duality: establishes a duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing existing results.  
- Theoretical rigor: analysis is thorough and provides a strong foundation explaining prior empirical successes.  
- Motivation: clearly motivates convex alternatives to non-convex tensor decomposition and connects to applications (neuroimaging, collaborative filtering).  
- Connections: situates work within structured sparsity and prior tensor decomposition literature.  
- Empirical validation: numerical experiments support theoretical scaling claims and show latent outperforms overlapped in tested scenarios.  
- Presentation: technically dense but well-written and organized for experts; standard notation and clear arguments.  
- Accessibility weakness: heavy mathematical notation may limit accessibility to a broader audience; needs more intuitive explanations.  
- Empirical scope weakness: experiments are limited; broader tensor sizes, rank settings, noise levels, and real datasets are needed.  
- Assumptions concern: theoretical results rely on assumptions (e.g., incoherence) that may not hold in practice; sensitivity analysis and relaxations are needed.  
- Computational cost omission: lacks discussion of computational complexity and practical runtime comparisons between latent and overlapped approaches.  
- Tensor completion omission: insufficient discussion of extensions or challenges for tensor completion applications.  
- Suggestions: add intuitive overviews, expand experiments (including real data), discuss computational costs, analyze assumption sensitivity, and clarify tensor completion links.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel significant theory, explains empirical results, provides useful duality, supported by experiments, and well-written.  
- Rejection concerns: limited accessibility, incomplete empirical evaluation, and potentially restrictive theoretical assumptions.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.
