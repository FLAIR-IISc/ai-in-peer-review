- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition using overlapped Sp/q norms and latent Sp*/q* norms, proving a duality between them.
- Shows the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm; S1/1 latent is convex dual of overlapped S1/1.
- Derives norm inequalities: latent S1/1 bounded by Frobenius norm times min mode-rank, improving over overlapped sum-of-ranks bounds.
- Provides consistency, deterministic, and Gaussian-noise error bounds for the latent approach with favorable scaling in the minimum mode rank.
- Gaussian bound yields an explicit λ independent of unknown ranks and MSE scaling with σ^2 times sum of latent ranks over smallest dimension.
- Theoretical analysis adapts decomposable-regularizer templates and appears technically sound; duality generalizes group-sparsity results to tensors.
- Deterministic bound requires an incoherence-like assumption and spectral-norm constraints across components, which are somewhat strong.
- Potential gap: analyzed estimator (with spectral constraints parameter α) may not match the practical solver/experiments; enforcement unclear.
- LR complexity used in experiments is oracle-based (from noiseless decomposition), limiting practical interpretability.
- Synthetic experiments validate predicted scaling and λ independence but are limited to moderate sizes and denoising only.
- Experiments lack comparisons to non-convex baselines (ALS, HOOI/CP), tensor completion tests, or stress tests for incoherence violations.
- Paper is well-organized with clear definitions, intuitive narrative, and appropriate figures illustrating scaling.
- Minor clarity issues: typos/formatting glitches, ambiguous notation (r̄k vs rk), and missing details on whether spectral constraints were enforced experimentally.
- Solver (ADMM) is referenced but computational complexity and convergence behavior are not discussed, impacting reproducibility.
- Novelty: latent/overlapped Schatten-norm duality in tensors and statistical analysis of latent S1/1 are new contributions.
- Key conceptual contribution: latent approach adapts to the minimum mode rank, explaining empirical superiority when one mode is low-rank.
- Significance: results guide regularizer choice in tensor problems and should spur follow-up work on completion and robustness.
- Strengths: clear theoretical advances, conceptual clarity, experiments aligned with theory, and connections to structured sparsity literature.
- Weaknesses: unclear enforcement/selection of spectral constraint α, narrow experimental scope, oracle-based metrics, and deferred proofs.
- Suggested improvements: clarify spectral constraint usage/sensitivity, compare to CP/Tucker baselines, add completion experiments, report solver costs/convergence, and discuss p≠1 or q≠1 cases.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, sound theory resolving why latent helps, practical λ guidance, and empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental evaluation and uninstantiated analytical assumptions in practice.
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept.
