- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of two convex tensor decomposition approaches (overlapped vs latent) and establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms.  
- Shows the latent approach’s error bound scales with min_k √r_k, improving over the overlapped approach’s dependence on average mode-k rank.  
- First theoretical analysis of the latent approach, connecting it to structured sparsity literature and generalizing known group-sparsity duality (Lemma 1).  
- Theorems 2–3 formalize the latent approach’s improved error scaling and explain prior empirical observations (Tomioka et al., 2013).  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and mathematically precise.  
- Empirical results in Section 4 support the theory: MSE scales linearly with the predicted TR and LR complexity measures.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical contribution that explains empirical success of the latent approach.  
- Strength: duality result elegantly links tensor decomposition to structured sparsity.  
- Weakness: analysis focuses only on the S1/1-norm; general Sp/q-norm extensions are mentioned but not explored.  
- Weakness: empirical section omits comparisons with standard non-convex baselines (e.g., HOSVD, ALS), limiting practical significance.  
- Weakness: no practical guidance for selecting the regularization parameter λ when true ranks are unknown.  
- Weakness: computational complexity trade-offs between latent and overlapped approaches are not discussed.  
- Weakness: does not address tensor completion / missing data scenarios, a major application of tensor decomposition.  
- Overall: strong, significant theoretical advance with solid empirical support; recommended for acceptance despite gaps in empirical baselines and practical considerations.
