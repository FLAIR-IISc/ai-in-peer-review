## Review of "Convex Tensor Decomposition via Structured Schatten Norm Regularization"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates convex approaches to tensor decomposition, specifically comparing the “overlapped” and “latent” Schatten norm regularization methods. It provides a theoretical analysis of the latent approach, demonstrating a performance guarantee scaling with the *minimum* mode-k rank of the underlying tensor – a key explanation for its empirical success. The authors also establish a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms and provide numerical validation of their theoretical findings.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach, a significant contribution given the predominantly empirical nature of previous work in this area. The bound on the mean squared error in terms of the minimum rank is a strong result.
* **Novel Duality:** The discovery and proof of the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms are insightful and generalize existing results in structured sparsity literature.
* **Clear Explanation of Empirical Observations:** The theory effectively explains why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped approach in scenarios where the tensor is low-rank in only one mode, as demonstrated in prior work [25].
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper combines theoretical analysis with numerical experiments, providing a well-rounded assessment of the proposed approach. The experimental setup directly addresses the scaling predicted by the theory.
* **Well-Written & Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured, making it relatively easy to follow the theoretical developments.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty in Application:** While the theoretical analysis is strong, the application of convex optimization to tensor decomposition isn’t entirely new. The novelty lies primarily in the analysis of the *latent* approach, rather than the approach itself.
* **Assumptions in Theoretical Results:** The tighter bounds (Theorem 2 & 3) rely on somewhat strong assumptions, such as the incoherence condition (Equation 10). The practical applicability of these assumptions warrants further discussion.  The noise model is also relatively simple (Gaussian).
* **Experimental Scope:**  The experiments, while validating the theory, are somewhat limited in scope.  Exploring more complex datasets or different noise distributions would strengthen the empirical evaluation.  It would also be valuable to compare against more recent tensor decomposition methods.
* **Connection to Existing Work:** While the related work section is reasonably comprehensive, it could benefit from a more detailed discussion of recent advances in tensor decomposition, particularly those beyond convex relaxation methods.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The proofs appear correct (although a thorough review of all proofs is beyond the scope of this review), and the claims are well-supported by the analysis and experiments. It presents a complete piece of work, focusing on a specific aspect (latent approach analysis) within the broader field of tensor decomposition.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clear, though the mathematical notation can be dense at times. The organization is logical, and the authors make a reasonable effort to explain complex concepts.
* **Originality:** The originality lies in the theoretical analysis of the latent approach and the discovery of the duality between the two Schatten norms.  The application of convex relaxation to tensor decomposition is not new, but this work provides a significant contribution to *understanding* those approaches.
* **Significance:**  The results are important for researchers working on tensor decomposition and convex optimization. The improved understanding of the latent approach and its theoretical guarantees are likely to influence future research directions. The paper offers valuable insights for choosing the appropriate regularization strategy in different tensor decomposition scenarios.


**Arguments Pro & Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Strong theoretical analysis with a clear connection to empirical observations.
* Novel duality result.
* Well-written and organized presentation.
* Significant contribution to understanding convex tensor decomposition.

**Con:**

* Limited novelty in the overall approach.
* Assumptions in theoretical results might limit applicability.
* Experiments could be more comprehensive.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. The theoretical analysis of the latent approach is a significant advance, and the duality result is insightful. While the paper isn't groundbreaking in terms of proposing a radically new approach, it provides a deeper understanding of existing methods and offers valuable guidance for their application.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**  I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the following minor revisions:

*   Clarify the assumptions required for the tighter theoretical bounds and discuss their practical implications.
*   Expand the discussion of related work to include more recent advances in tensor decomposition.
*   Consider adding a brief discussion of the limitations of the current approach and potential avenues for future research.
