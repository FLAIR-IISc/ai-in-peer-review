Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies convex formulations for tensor decomposition based on structured Schatten norms. It contrasts two families:

- Overlapped Sp/q norms: penalize the sum (or ℓq aggregation) of Schatten p-norms across all mode unfoldings, encouraging simultaneous low rank across modes. This includes the commonly used sum of nuclear norms across unfoldings [9, 15, 23, 25, 26].
- Latent Sp/q norms: represent the tensor as a sum of components, each regularized to be low-rank in a specific mode, encouraging a mixture where each component is low-rank in one mode [25].

Main contributions:
1) A duality result: the dual of the overlapped Sp/q norm is the latent Sp*/q* norm (Lemma 1), generalizing known dualities in structured sparsity [1, 12, 17, 21].
2) A key norm inequality for the latent S1/1 norm: it is bounded by min_k sqrt(r_k) times the Frobenius norm (Lemma 2), as opposed to the overlapped S1/1 bound which scales with the sum of sqrt(r_k) (inequality (3)).
3) Statistical analysis for denoising Y = W* + E using latent S1/1 regularization. Results include:
   - A consistency bound depending on dimensions only (Theorem 1).
   - Deterministic error bounds that incorporate low-rank structure under an incoherence-type assumption enforced via spectral norm constraints among components (Theorem 2), yielding overall error scaling with min_k r_k.
   - Gaussian noise bounds (Theorem 3) with a λ choice independent of unknown ranks, and MSE scaling with sum of component ranks or min mode-k rank.
4) Comparison to prior bounds for overlapped S1/1 [26], showing latent error depends on min mode-k rank while overlapped depends on an average of sqrt(r_k) (inequality (16)).
5) Synthetic experiments confirming predicted scaling vs. theory and demonstrating that latent often outperforms overlapped even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank.

The work connects tensor norms to structured sparsity (dirty models [12], multi-task with overlaps [13, 16, 21]), and contrasts with Tucker/HOSVD analyses [5] and tensor completion with sum-of-nuclear-norms [9, 15, 23, 25, 26].

Strengths
- Clear formalization of overlapped vs. latent Schatten norms and a general duality result that extends structured sparsity dualities to tensor norms.
- Sharp intuition via Lemma 2 explaining when latent should excel (when one mode is much lower rank).
- Statistical guarantees for the latent approach with explicit λ selection under Gaussian noise, showing favorable min-rank dependence; complements and contrasts prior results for overlapped norms [26].
- Empirical scaling consistent with theory across tensor sizes and ranks; analysis of the role of λ supports theory.
- The framework is broadly relevant to tensor denoising and potentially completion.

Weaknesses and points needing clarification
- Practicality of constraints: Theorem 2 and 3 rely on added spectral norm constraints ∥W(l)(k)∥S∞ ≤ α for l ≠ k in the estimator (11). It is unclear whether these constraints are enforced in experiments; the description suggests algorithms from [25] without such constraints. If not enforced, the empirical results do not directly validate the constrained-theory regime. Please clarify whether constraints were used, how α was set, and provide ablations with and without constraints.
- Incoherence assumption (10): The assumption is nontrivial and requires knowledge of α; more discussion is needed on when it holds for common tensor models, how sensitive bounds are to violations, and whether α can be estimated or controlled in practice.
- Dependence on decomposition: The deterministic and Gaussian bounds reference ranks r̄_k of latent components in an optimal decomposition; this raises questions of identifiability and whether different decompositions can change the bound. The singleton argument for obtaining min_k r_k is valid, but the role of r̄_k vs. multilinear ranks would benefit from more discussion.
- Constants and factor F: The factor F in Theorem 3 can be opaque. Some simplification or bounding under common regimes (balanced sizes) would aid interpretability. Also, constants c are universal but unspecified; empirical calibration would help.
- Empirical scope: Only synthetic, fully observed denoising is shown. Given application motivation (neuroimaging, environmental data), including at least one real dataset, and experiments with missing data would strengthen significance. Also, broaden baselines (e.g., CP/Tucker with model selection) and use principled model selection for λ (validation or SURE) for a fairer comparison.
- Fairness of complexity proxy: LR complexity uses r̄_k obtained by running the latent approach on W* (noise-free). Although used only for x-axis scaling, this “oracle” estimate may bias interpretation. Consider using ground-truth generating ranks or a decomposition-independent proxy.
- Related work completeness: Consider discussing tensor spectral norms and alternative tensor nuclear norms (e.g., t-SVD/tubal nuclear norms) as orthogonal approaches, and clarifying distinctions.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The duality and norm inequalities are consistent with known structured norms literature; the statistical analysis follows decomposable-regularizer frameworks [1, 20] adapted to tensors. The added constraints make the analysis cleaner, but reduce practicality; the gap between theory and the unconstrained estimator used in practice should be acknowledged and, if possible, narrowed.
- Empirical methodology is reasonable for scaling checks, but limited in scope; lack of real-data results and constrained-estimator tests are notable omissions.

Clarity
- Generally well organized with precise definitions. Proof sketches are deferred; the main text is clear enough to follow ideas. Some editorial issues (typos, grammar) remain; tightening Section 4’s description of how r̄_k is computed, and explicitly stating whether constraints are used, would improve clarity.

Originality
- The latent/overlapped duality for Schatten norms and the min-rank-based statistical guarantees for the latent approach appear novel and extend prior overlapped analyses. The conceptual bridge to structured sparsity is well articulated.

Significance
- The results provide actionable insight: in noisy settings, latent norms can outperform overlapped norms, especially with strong anisotropy in ranks. This is relevant to tensor learning in ML. Extending to completion and providing practical unconstrained analyses would further increase impact.

Arguments for acceptance
- General duality result and new norm inequality.
- Clear statistical advantage (min-rank dependence) with matching empirical scaling.
- Addresses a known weakness of overlapped formulations; provides guidance for practitioners.

Arguments against acceptance
- Reliance on additional constraints and incoherence without demonstrating practicality or empirical use.
- Limited empirical validation (synthetic only, no missing data, narrow baselines).
- Some bounds depend on decomposition-specific ranks r̄_k, which may be hard to interpret or compute.

Suggestions
- Clarify use of constraints in experiments; include results with the constrained estimator.
- Add at least one real-data experiment and a missing-data (completion) scenario.
- Provide guidance for choosing α and λ in practice; discuss robustness to assumption violations.
- Tighten presentation; fix typos and unify notation (r_k vs. r̄_k).

Overall recommendation: Weak accept
