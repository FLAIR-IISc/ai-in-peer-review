This paper investigates convex relaxation for tensor decomposition, specifically comparing the “overlapped” and “latent” approaches based on structured Schatten norm regularization. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the latent approach, demonstrating a performance bound scaling with the *minimum* mode-k rank, explaining its empirical superiority over the overlapped approach, particularly when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The paper also establishes a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing existing results in structured sparsity literature, and supports its theoretical findings with empirical validation.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Contribution:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach, a crucial aspect missing in previous work (as highlighted in the introduction). Establishing the scaling with the minimal rank offers a significant insight into why this approach outperforms the overlapped method in specific scenarios.
* **Novel Duality Result:** The demonstrated duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a valuable theoretical result, connecting tensor decomposition to the broader field of structured sparsity and providing a deeper understanding of the two approaches.
* **Clear Explanation of Empirical Observations:** The theoretical results directly explain previously observed empirical behavior (cite [25]), offering a principled understanding beyond just observation.
* **Comprehensive Comparison:** The paper doesn’t just promote the latent approach but also provides a nuanced comparison to the overlapped method, including conditions where the latter might still be suitable.
* **Well-Structured and Readable:** The paper is logically organized, clearly written, and uses appropriate mathematical notation.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments confirm the theoretical predictions, adding confidence to the analysis.
* **Connections to Existing Work:** The paper effectively positions its contribution within the existing literature on tensor decomposition, convex optimization, and structured sparsity, citing relevant work appropriately.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Assumptions for Deterministic Bound:** Theorem 2 relies on the “incoherence assumption” (Equation 10) which may not hold in all practical scenarios. While reasonable, its impact on the practical applicability of the result should be discussed further. How sensitive are the results to violations of this assumption?
* **Computational Complexity:** While the theory provides performance guarantees, it doesn’t address the computational complexity of solving the optimization problem involved in the latent approach. A discussion of the scalability of the method would be beneficial.
* **Limited Scope of Numerical Experiments:** The numerical experiments primarily focus on synthetic data. Evaluating the method’s performance on real-world datasets (e.g., neuroimaging as mentioned in the introduction) would strengthen the empirical validation.
* **Discussion of Tensor Completion:** The authors mention tensor completion as a future work, but a brief discussion of how the analysis might adapt to the completion setting (where some entries are missing) within the current paper could be valuable.
* **Tightness of Bounds:** While the theoretical bounds are valuable, a discussion of their tightness (i.e., how close they are to the actual performance) would be helpful. Are these bounds achievable in practice?



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant theoretical advance in understanding the latent approach for tensor decomposition.
* The duality result provides a new perspective on structured Schatten norms.
* The analysis addresses a crucial open question in the field, providing a theoretical justification for observed empirical behavior.
* The numerical results support the theoretical findings.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The assumptions required for the stronger deterministic bound (Theorem 2) might limit the applicability of the results.
* The lack of evaluation on real-world datasets weakens the empirical validation.
* The computational complexity of the method is not addressed.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. The theoretical analysis is strong, the duality result is novel, and the empirical results provide supporting evidence. While there are some weaknesses related to assumptions and computational considerations, they do not overshadow the significance of the work. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept**. However, I encourage the authors to address the following points in a revised version:

* **Discuss the sensitivity of the results to violations of the incoherence assumption in Theorem 2.**
* **Include a discussion of the computational complexity of the latent approach.**
* **Consider adding results from experiments on real-world datasets.**
* **Briefly discuss how the analysis might extend to the tensor completion setting.**
* **Comment on the tightness of the obtained bounds.**
