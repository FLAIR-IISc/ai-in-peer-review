Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies convex formulations for low-rank tensor estimation via structured Schatten norms. Two families are considered: (i) the “overlapped” Sp/q norm (sum/ℓq aggregation of Schatten-p norms of all mode-unfoldings) and (ii) the “latent” Sp*/q* norm defined as an infimal convolution over decompositions into K tensors, each penalized in a single unfolding. The authors establish a duality between these two classes (generalizing dualities known for overlapped vs latent group sparsity), and analyze the statistical performance of the latent S1/1 approach for tensor denoising. The main claims: (a) a general consistency bound scaling with λ√mink nk when λ exceeds the overlapped S∞/∞ norm of the noise; (b) deterministic and Gaussian-noise error bounds for a constrained latent formulation that scale with mink rk (multilinear Tucker ranks), explaining empirical superiority over the overlapped approach whose known bounds scale with averages of √rk [Tomioka et al., NIPS’11]. Numerical experiments on synthetic denoising demonstrate linear scaling with a proposed LR complexity and suggest favorable performance of the latent approach even when the truth is simultaneously low-rank.

Strengths
- Conceptually clean unification of overlapped and latent tensor norms and their duality; connects neatly to structured sparsity literature.
- Theoretical analysis for the latent formulation that highlights dependence on the minimum mode rank, which is intuitively appealing and complements prior results for the overlapped approach.
- The Gaussian noise bound includes an explicit choice of λ, independent of unknown ranks.
- Experiments aim to validate scaling predictions and compare overlapped vs latent approaches.

Weaknesses and concerns
Major technical and clarity issues:
1) Notational ambiguity and internal inconsistencies:
- The same triple-bar notation with the same subscript “S1/1” is used for both the overlapped and the latent norms (Sec. 2.1 vs 2.2). This is highly confusing and, as written, incorrect: (1)/(2) and (6) define different functionals. The paper must use distinct symbols (e.g., |||·|||over,S1/1 vs |||·|||lat,S1/1) throughout, including in Theorem statements.
- Eq. (3): It appears as “K∑k=1 √rk ||W||F”, which would incorrectly introduce an extra factor K. The intended bound is ∑k √rk ||W||F (each unfolding’s nuclear norm ≤ √rk||W||F). Please correct.
- Theorem 2: r̄k is defined as rank(W∗(k)(k)) (component ranks), but inequality (12) uses “∑k rk” (Tucker ranks of W∗). This is almost surely a typo; the LHS should be controlled by ∑k r̄k. Please disambiguate rk vs r̄k throughout.
- Eq. (11) objective has “( 1 ||Y−W||^2F + … )”; earlier formulations use (1/2)||·||^2F. Typesetting error or actual change?

2) Assumptions vs algorithms used:
- The deterministic and Gaussian bounds assume the constrained estimator (11) with spectral-norm incoherence constraints across non-native unfoldings and λ ≥ 2||E||S∞/∞ + α(K−1). It is unclear whether the experiments enforce these constraints (what α was used? how enforced in ADMM from [25]?). The description suggests using the unconstrained latent solver from [25]. If constraints are not enforced, theorems do not apply to the implemented estimator. Please clarify and, if needed, redo experiments with the constrained estimator or provide theory for the unconstrained variant.

3) Experimental methodology:
- Only denoising (no missing data), only K=3, two sizes, and a single noise level (σ=0.1). No real data. The claims about practical superiority would be stronger with tensor completion tests and varied SNR.
- Choice of λ: selected from 20 candidates (0.1–100) with “three representative values” reported; no precise selection criterion (e.g., oracle, CV), and no connection to the theoretically suggested λ that depends on σ, N, n1, nK, and α. The statement that the “optimal choice is independent of rank and only depends on the size” is weakly supported.
- Definition of LR complexity uses a decomposition obtained by running the latent algorithm on the noise-free truth—this is circular and not a quantity one could know in practice; thus, comparing MSE against this LR complexity is hard to interpret.

4) Bounds and scaling:
- In (14), the LHS is “N ∑k ||Ŵ(k)−W∗(k)||^2F”. Later, MSE is compared to LR complexity ∑k r̄k / nK without an N factor. The dimensional scaling between (14) and the plotted MSE should be made explicit; as written, there is a mismatch between theory (with a factor N) and the LR complexity used in plots.
- The factor F in (14)/(15) depends on α and log(K/δ); experiments ignore α and δ.

5) Novelty:
- The duality result extends known overlapped vs latent group-norm dualities; the tensor adaptation is interesting but incremental. The statistical analysis appears to adapt decomposable-regularizer techniques [Negahban et al., Jalali et al., Agarwal et al.]. The main novelty is the identification of min-mode-rank dependence, but this heavily relies on allowing singleton decompositions; the insight is valuable but its tightness vs lower bounds in the same denoising setting is not discussed.

6) Presentation and formatting:
- Numerous typos/grammatical errors (“betters”, “prepint”, “Prof. ICCV”), inconsistent notation (W(k), W( k ), W (k)(k)), missing spaces, footnotes in text, incomplete references (years/pages missing), and several equations are poorly typeset. The paper would benefit from thorough proofreading.

Questions for authors
- Did you enforce the spectral norm constraints in (11) in experiments? If so, how was α set, and how did it affect solutions? If not, can you provide theory for the unconstrained estimator?
- Please correct the notation so overlapped vs latent norms are unambiguously distinguished throughout; confirm/correct Eq. (3) and Theorem 2’s ranks.
- Can you extend the empirical study to tensor completion (with varying sampling rates) and multiple σ, and compare with non-convex baselines (ALS/HOOI) and recent relaxations (e.g., “square deal”)?
- Can you report wall-clock time and convergence diagnostics for the ADMM solver?
- Clarify the N factors in (14)/(15) and how they relate to the MSE plots; include error bars.

Minor comments/typos (non-exhaustive)
- Sec. 2.1: “∥W ∥Sp = (∑r j=1 …)” define r explicitly as rank.
- Lemma 2 explanation: “performs betters” → “performs better”.
- Several references lack years and venues; standardize formatting.
- “In Prof. ICCV” → “In Proc. ICCV”; “prepint” → “preprint”.
- Use consistent vectorization and unfolding notation; avoid W (k)(k).

Assessment
- Quality: Moderate; proofs are deferred; key estimator-theory mismatch (constraints) and notation errors reduce confidence.
- Clarity: Below NeurIPS bar in current form due to notation overload and formatting issues.
- Originality: Incremental-to-moderate; useful synthesis and bounds but largely builds on established frameworks.
- Significance: Potentially useful, but empirical support is limited and constrained assumptions may hinder practical adoption.

Recommendation: Reject (weak). The ideas are promising, but the paper in its current form has substantial notational, methodological, and experimental shortcomings. A revised version addressing the above issues and expanding experiments could be competitive.
