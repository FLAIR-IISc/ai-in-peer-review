This paper investigates convex tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norm regularization, specifically comparing an "overlapped" approach and a "latent" approach. The core contribution is a theoretical analysis demonstrating that the mean squared error of the latent approach scales with the *minimum* mode-k rank of the underlying tensor, providing a theoretical justification for its empirically observed superior performance, especially when the tensor is low-rank in only one mode. The paper also establishes a duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing existing results from structured sparsity literature.

The paper is well-structured, beginning with a clear introduction outlining the problem and motivation, followed by definitions and theoretical results, and concluding with empirical validation. The authors thoroughly relate their work to existing literature on tensor decomposition, convex optimization, and structured sparsity, citing relevant papers appropriately. The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, with key lemmas and theorems presented with clear statements and proofs relegated to the supplementary material. The numerical experiments corroborate the theoretical findings, demonstrating the scaling behavior and advantages of the latent approach.

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the latent approach, establishing consistency and denoising performance bounds.
*   **Novelty:** The duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a novel contribution, and the analysis explaining the superior performance of the latent approach based on the minimum rank is insightful.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The definitions are clear, and the motivation is well-explained.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments provide strong support for the theoretical claims.
*   **Connection to Related Work:** The authors effectively connect their work to broader areas of structured sparsity and convex optimization.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Assumptions:** The theoretical analysis relies on several assumptions, including incoherence (Assumption 10), which might not always hold in practice. The impact of violating these assumptions is not fully discussed.
*   **Complexity of the Constant:**  While the scaling with the minimum rank is clearly shown, the constant factor in the error bound (e.g., F in Theorem 3) could be quite large and might limit the practical applicability of the results. A more detailed discussion of the magnitude of this constant would be valuable.
*   **Limited Scope of Numerical Experiments:** The experiments focus primarily on synthetic data.  Evaluating the performance on real-world datasets would further strengthen the paper.
*   **Optimization Details:** The paper mentions using algorithms from [25] but lacks details about the implementation and parameter tuning. Providing more information on the optimization process would improve reproducibility.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of convex tensor decomposition.
*   The results provide a clear explanation for the observed advantages of the latent approach.
*   The paper is well-written, well-organized, and thoroughly grounded in existing literature.
*   The empirical results support the theoretical findings.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The theoretical analysis relies on assumptions that may not always be met in practice.
*   The complexity of the error bounds might limit the practical relevance of the results.
*   The lack of experiments on real-world datasets is a minor drawback.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. The theoretical analysis is rigorous and insightful, and the empirical results are compelling. While some assumptions are made, and the constants involved may be large, the overall quality of the work justifies acceptance.  I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions to address the comments regarding assumptions, constant factors, and experimental details.
