- Introduces a novel notion of tractability by defining fast-mixing Gibbs chains as tractable models for approximate inference in Ising models.  
- Core contribution: an algorithm to project arbitrary Ising-model parameters onto the set of fast-mixing models using a spectral-norm bound on the interaction matrix.  
- Explores projections under multiple divergences: Euclidean, a novel "piecewise" KL, and forward/reverse KL.  
- Derives a dual algorithm for Euclidean projection that preserves graph sparsity.  
- Uses stochastic projected gradient descent to minimize KL divergences—well-motivated optimization choices.  
- Provides clear, well-structured exposition of theory, algorithms, and experiments.  
- Experiments show sampling from projected parameters gives more accurate marginals within a fixed computational budget than mean-field, LBP, TRW, and Gibbs on original slow-mixing parameters.  
- Weakness: theoretical spectral-norm condition for fast mixing (<1) is much looser than empirical optimal values (~2.5), indicating a gap needing further analysis.  
- Weakness: the proposed piecewise KL performs poorly in practice and could be condensed or moved to an appendix.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound, practically useful paper that merits acceptance.
