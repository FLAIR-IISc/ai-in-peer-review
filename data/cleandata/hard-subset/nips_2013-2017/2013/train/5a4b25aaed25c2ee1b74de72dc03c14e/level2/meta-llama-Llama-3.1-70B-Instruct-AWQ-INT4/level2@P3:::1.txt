**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel approach to approximate inference in high-treewidth graphical models by projecting the intractable distribution onto a "fast-mixing" set, which guarantees rapid convergence of Gibbs sampling to the stationary distribution. The authors define a notion of "tractability" based on parameter-space conditions, rather than exact inference algorithms. They derive a dual algorithm to project the intractable distribution onto the fast-mixing set under several divergences, including Euclidean distance, piecewise KL-divergence, and zero-forcing KL-divergence. The proposed methods are compared experimentally to Gibbs sampling on the original parameters and several standard variational methods.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a new approach to approximate inference in high-treewidth graphical models, which is distinct from traditional variational methods.
2. **Theoretical foundations**: The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for their approach, including a bound on the dependency matrix for Ising models and a dual algorithm for projecting onto the fast-mixing set.
3. **Experimental evaluation**: The paper includes a thorough experimental evaluation of the proposed methods, comparing them to Gibbs sampling on the original parameters and several standard variational methods.
4. **Insights into mixing times**: The authors provide insights into the mixing times of Gibbs sampling on the original parameters and the projected distributions.
