- Proposes a new notion of "tractable" models based on fast-mixing properties that guarantee rapid Gibbs sampling convergence.  
- Develops an algorithm to project Ising model parameters onto a parameter set guaranteed to be fast-mixing under several divergences.  
- Considers four divergences for projection: Euclidean, zero-avoiding KL, a novel piecewise KL, and zero-forcing KL.  
- Provides experimental comparisons of projected models versus Gibbs on original parameters and standard variational methods.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Clear, concise introduction to inference in high-treewidth graphical models and the fast-mixing concept.  
- Projection algorithm is well-motivated with an explicit explanation of technical challenges.  
- Experimental results are well-presented and show clear comparisons.  
- Assumes substantial background in graphical models and MCMC, which may limit accessibility.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of limitations, especially implications of the potentially conservative spectral norm bound.  
- Experiments are limited in scope, raising questions about generalization to more complex models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, novel contribution with potential to improve accuracy and efficiency; experiments demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high assumed background, limited discussion of limitations, and narrow experimental evaluation.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
