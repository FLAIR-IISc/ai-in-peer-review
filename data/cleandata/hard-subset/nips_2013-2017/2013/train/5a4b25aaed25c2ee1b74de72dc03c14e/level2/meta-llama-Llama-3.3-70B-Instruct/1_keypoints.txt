- Proposes projecting intractable distributions onto a "fast-mixing" set so Gibbs sampling is guaranteed to converge rapidly.
- Derives a dual algorithm to compute the projection under multiple divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL).
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform variational approximations and Gibbs sampling on original parameters for finite-time inference.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction to the problem and approach.
- Technical contributions are significant and accompanied by thorough theoretical analysis.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and results are promising.
- Strength: novel approach to approximate inference in high-treewidth graphical models.
- Strength: derivation of a dual algorithm applicable under several divergences.
- Strength: strong empirical evidence of effectiveness.
- Weakness: spectral norm bound used is loose and may be overly conservative.
- Weakness: lacks a tighter or more precise bound characterizing when rapid mixing occurs.
- Weakness: current approach needs further development to extend to general Markov random fields.
- Overall: significant and promising contribution with practical potential but requires further work to tighten bounds and generalize the method.
- Pro-accept: novelty, strong technical analysis, and comprehensive experiments support acceptance.
- Con-accept: conservative bounds, missing tight mixing guarantees, and limited generality argue against immediate acceptance.
