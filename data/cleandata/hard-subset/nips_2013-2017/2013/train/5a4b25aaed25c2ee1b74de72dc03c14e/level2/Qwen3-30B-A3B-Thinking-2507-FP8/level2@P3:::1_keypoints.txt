- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a "fast-mixing" set so Gibbs sampling converges rapidly via spectral-norm constraints on the dependency matrix.  
- Shifts notion of tractability from graph structure (mean-field, LBP) to parameter-space constraints guaranteeing rapid mixing.  
- Core technical contribution: projection algorithm minimizing four divergences — Euclidean (SVD thresholding), piecewise KL, zero-avoiding KL, and zero-forcing KL.  
- Bridges prior mixing-time theory (e.g., Hayes, Dyer et al.) to a practical inference strategy.  
- Theorem 7 provides an efficient dual algorithm for Euclidean projection, giving a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Experiments comprehensively compare against LBP, TRW, mean-field, and Gibbs on original parameters.  
- Key empirical finding: projected parameters produce more accurate marginals than variational methods under finite sampling time.  
- Projected-Gibbs outperforms original-Gibbs when computation time is limited, addressing a practical MCMC bottleneck.  
- Piecewise KL and zero-forcing KL are well-motivated; zero-forcing KL enables scalable stochastic optimization.  
- Authors acknowledge and analyze the conservatism of the spectral norm bound (Section 7).  
- Weakness: spectral-norm bound (||R||2 < 1) is overly conservative for Ising models; empirical choice ||R||2 ≤ 2.5 lacks theoretical justification, weakening the "guaranteed fast-mixing" claim.  
- Weakness: piecewise KL underperforms empirically with unclear reasons—spanning trees may fail to capture structure compared to zero-forcing KL.  
- Weakness: no comparison to advanced MCMC samplers (e.g., Hamiltonian Monte Carlo, Swendsen-Wang), leaving competitiveness for strong interactions untested.  
- Recommendation: clarify and justify the spectral bound (e.g., empirical mixing-time curves, citations like Lubetzky & Sly) and discuss tighter bounds for Ising models.  
- Recommendation: re-evaluate piecewise KL using alternative subgraph choices (e.g., low-treewidth junction trees) and analyze why it underperforms.  
- Recommendation: expand experiments to include state-of-the-art MCMC baselines (HMC, Swendsen-Wang) to assess practical competitiveness.  
- Overall assessment: original, theoretically grounded, and practically validated idea redefining tractability via parameter constraints; advances inference for high-treewidth models.  
- Final recommendation: Accept — contributions are novel and well-supported; suggested revisions are reasonable and largely minor.
