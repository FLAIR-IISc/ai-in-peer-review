## Review of "Projecting Ising Model Parameters for Fast Mixing"

This paper addresses the challenge of slow mixing in Gibbs sampling for Ising models, particularly when interactions are strong. The authors propose projecting Ising model parameters onto a set guaranteed to be fast-mixing, leveraging the spectral norm of the dependency matrix as a mixing criterion. They explore several divergence measures for this projection – Euclidean, piecewise KL, and reversed KL – and demonstrate empirically that Gibbs sampling with projected parameters outperforms the original parameters when mixing is slow.

The core idea is novel and relevant to the NIPS community, building on established work in graphical models and MCMC methods (e.g., [6, 10, 18]). The connection to fast-mixing Markov chains and the use of spectral norm bounds (Theorem 4) are well-motivated. The exploration of different divergences for projection is a strength, offering a trade-off between computational cost and accuracy. The experimental evaluation, comparing against LBP, TRW, and naive mean-field, provides a reasonable benchmark.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. The theoretical justification for using a spectral norm bound of 2.5 (instead of the tighter 0.4407 from [11]) is not adequately explained, and the justification feels *ad hoc*. While the authors acknowledge limitations, the discussion feels somewhat cursory. The piecewise KL divergence, while interesting, doesn't perform well in experiments, and the reasons for this are not thoroughly investigated.  The stochastic gradient descent implementation for the reversed KL-divergence lacks detailed tuning information, making reproducibility challenging.  Clarity could be improved in Section 3, where the connection between the dependency matrix and fast mixing is presented; a more intuitive explanation would be beneficial.  Furthermore, the paper doesn’t fully explore the computational cost of the projection step itself, which could be significant for large models.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to accelerating Gibbs sampling by parameter projection.
*   Empirically demonstrates improved accuracy with limited sampling time.
*   Connects to established theory on fast-mixing Markov chains.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Loosely justified spectral norm bound.
*   Limited analysis of the piecewise KL divergence.
*   Lack of detailed implementation details for stochastic gradient descent.
*   Missing discussion of the computational cost of projection.

**Overall:**

This paper presents an interesting idea with promising initial results. However, the lack of rigorous justification for certain choices and the limited discussion of practical considerations prevent a strong recommendation for acceptance in its current form. I recommend **rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit**, focusing on tightening the theoretical analysis, providing more detailed implementation information, and thoroughly evaluating the computational cost of the proposed approach. Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the contribution and make it more appealing to the NIPS audience.
