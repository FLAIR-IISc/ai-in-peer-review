- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing set to improve Gibbs sampling mixing rates.
- Core idea is compelling: transform to a tractable parameter space while minimizing divergence from the original model.
- Explores multiple divergence measures (Euclidean, KL, piecewise KL) and practical projection algorithms.
- Theoretical contributions (Theorems 6 and 7) for Euclidean projection are solid and rigorously presented.
- Discusses computational tradeoffs among divergence measures and projection methods.
- Experiments show projected parameters can yield more accurate results than sampling the original model under limited sampling time.
- Empirical comparisons to relevant baselines are convincing and favorable.
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly connects to tractable approximations like mean-field and TRW.
- The link between the dependency matrix spectral norm and practical mixing behavior needs further elaboration.
- The spectral norm bound appears conservative compared to known critical values (e.g., 2D Ising), and causes are not fully explored.
- Piecewise KL divergence is theoretically interesting but underperforms empirically; reasons and potential subgraph selection improvements are not analyzed.
- Experimental results lack error bars or statistical significance testing, weakening conclusions.
- Description of the stochastic gradient algorithm for zero-forcing KL is concise; more details on parameter tuning and convergence are needed for reproducibility.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers a novel projection approach, provides solid theory, and shows empirical gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: conservative spectral norm bound, underwhelming piecewise KL performance, missing statistical tests, and insufficient SGD implementation details.
- Overall recommendation: strong and potentially impactful paper, but should add statistical testing and a deeper discussion of the spectral norm bound before acceptance.
