- Proposes projecting graphical model parameters onto a set that guarantees fast-mixing Gibbs sampling as a new inference strategy for high-treewidth models.
- Introduces a notion of "tractability" based on parameter-space conditions for rapid mixing rather than exact inference.
- Develops algorithms to project parameters onto the fast-mixing set using multiple divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL).
- Leverages a simple rapid-mixing condition for Ising models as the basis for the projection approach.
- Major technical contribution: a dual algorithm for Euclidean-distance projection onto the fast-mixing set.
- Provides a thorough analysis of mixing-time bounds and the dependency matrix for Ising models.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and compares proposed methods to standard variational methods and Gibbs sampling on original parameters.
- Empirical results show the proposed methods outperform variational approximations and can beat Gibbs sampling under finite-time budgets.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with a clear introduction and method presentation.
- Weakness: the spectral norm bound used may be loose/overly conservative in some settings.
- Weakness: extension to general Markov random fields is unresolved and requires new dependency-matrix bounds and projection algorithms.
- Recommendation: overall positive—strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance with future work suggested to tighten spectral norm bounds and generalize to broader MRFs.
