- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing set defined by ||R||2 < 1, using the tanh bound to relate R to |β|.
- Considers multiple projection objectives: Euclidean (dual with SVD thresholding), KL(q||p) (impractical), piecewise KL over tractable subgraphs, and KL(p||q) with a stochastic gradient.
- Euclidean dual projection (Theorem 7) is practically useful and preserves sparsity via masking constraints.
- Theorem 8 recovers the known gradient identity ∇ψ KL(ψ||θ) = Covψ[f]·(ψ−θ), supporting the KL(p||q) approach.
- The spectral-norm-based mixing condition and its specialization to Ising via tanh are standard but conservative.
- Stochastic optimization for KL(p||q) is plausible, but the gradient estimator and sampling schedule introduce bias and violate convergence conditions.
- Empirically, projected models with Gibbs sampling give better marginals than LBP, TRW, MF, and direct Gibbs under limited time on small grids and random graphs.
- Paper frames the approach as complementary to low-treewidth methods, offering a different tractability notion (fast mixing).
- The work relates to classical mixing-time theory (Dobrushin-type bounds, Hayes, Dyer-Goldberg-Jerrum).
- Theory section would benefit from more detail on convexity, duality gap, and uniqueness for the Euclidean projection.
- The reliance on the spectral norm of |β| is clear but conservative, potentially limiting practical tightness of guarantees.
- The stochastic gradient uses the same dependent MCMC samples for multiple estimates, yielding biased/low-quality updates under the reported schedule.
- Experimental reporting is inconsistent about whether true marginals come from junction tree or sampling, needing clarification.
- Comparisons do not equalize total computational effort and omit projection cost, making time-accuracy claims hard to interpret.
- The claimed “guarantee” is undermined by experiments using a spectral bound >1 (2.5), so mixing guarantees are not demonstrated.
- No direct empirical assessment of mixing (e.g., ESS, autocorrelation, convergence diagnostics) is provided to validate fast mixing.
- Piecewise KL uses a max over subgraphs but lacks explanation of subgradient handling and rationale for max vs average.
- The piecewise KL approximation is heuristic and its comparative benefit is not convincingly demonstrated.
- Original contributions include shifting to fast-mixing constrained families, a sparsity-preserving Euclidean projection, and a practical stochastic scheme for KL(p||q).
- The connection to mean-field as a zero-spectral-norm degenerate case is an insightful observation.
- The idea is potentially significant for practitioners relying on MCMC, but conservativeness and weak empirical validation limit immediate impact.
- Suggested fixes: run experiments using the ||R||2 < 1 bound or provide mixing diagnostics when relaxing it.
- Suggested fixes: clarify evaluation protocol, use junction tree ground truth consistently, and report variance across seeds/chains.
- Suggested fixes: equalize or report projection cost in time-accuracy comparisons, and provide time-accuracy curves including projection overhead.
- Suggested fixes: reduce bias in the stochastic gradient (independent samples, control variates, more MCMC per iteration, diminishing step sizes) and analyze convergence.
- Minor fixes: correct missing footnote marker, fix notation slips, and state the Gibbs update scheme (random vs systematic) used in experiments.
