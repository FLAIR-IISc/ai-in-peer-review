- Introduces a novel inference approach that projects model parameters onto a set guaranteeing fast-mixing Gibbs sampling.
- Proposes a new notion of "tractability" based on parameter-space conditions for rapid mixing rather than exact inference.
- Derives a dual algorithm to solve the projection problem under multiple divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, zero-forcing KL).
- Provides a thorough, technically sound analysis of mixing time bounds for Ising models.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing projected models to original Gibbs sampling and standard variational methods.
- Results show the proposed methods outperform variational approximations and can beat finite-time Gibbs sampling in accuracy.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains technical details accessibly with comprehensive references.
- Original contributions include the parameter-space tractability perspective and the novel piecewise and zero-forcing KL divergences.
- Significance is high, addressing a fundamental inference problem with broad applicability (CV, NLP, statistical physics).
- Limitation: the spectral norm bound used to guarantee rapid mixing is conservative and may be loose.
- Limitation: focus is primarily on Ising models, leaving generalization to more complex models unclear.
- Future work suggested: extend approach to general Markov random fields by bounding dependency matrices and develop projection algorithms for those cases.
- Future work suggested: explore using fast-mixing distributions for learning procedures.
- Overall assessment: significant, novel contribution with strong analysis and experiments, tempered by conservative bounds and limited scope to Ising models.
