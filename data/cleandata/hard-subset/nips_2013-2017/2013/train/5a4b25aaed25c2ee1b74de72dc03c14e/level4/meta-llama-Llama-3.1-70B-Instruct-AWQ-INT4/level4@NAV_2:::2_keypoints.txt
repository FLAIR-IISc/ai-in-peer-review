- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to a new setting that satisfies the Dobrushin criterion to improve Gibbs sampling mixing.  
- Formulates the projection as a constrained optimization problem and analyzes it under several distance/divergence measures.  
- Provides a clear, well-structured presentation including a detailed description and dual formulation of the projection problem.  
- Novel contribution by combining principles from stochastic and deterministic inference—an under-explored area.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and well-designed, showing practical benefits.  
- The role of the zij * dij = 0 constraints in ensuring the new parameters lie in the same space needs clearer explanation.  
- Missing comparisons of computational time for the projection operation versus Gibbs sampling to assess overall efficiency.  
- The choice to use a spectral norm bound of 2.5 instead of 1.0 is justified but its impact on performance should be further explored.  
- Strengths: novel method for improving mixing, insightful combination of inference paradigms, strong experimental work.  
- Weaknesses: methodological clarifications required (especially constraints), additional experimental comparisons needed, and deeper analysis of spectral norm effects.  
- Originality: introduces a novel projection-based approach and blends stochastic/deterministic ideas.  
- Significance: valuable contribution with potential applicability beyond Ising models.  
- Quality and clarity: generally well-written and easy to follow, though specific technical points (e.g., constraint role) need elaboration.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, under-explored combination of methods, and solid experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: outstanding clarifications and additional analyses/comparisons required to fully assess utility.
