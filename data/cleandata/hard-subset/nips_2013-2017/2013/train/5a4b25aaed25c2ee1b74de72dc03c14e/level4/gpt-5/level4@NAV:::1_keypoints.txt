- Paper proposes projecting strongly-coupled pairwise Ising models onto “fast-mixing” surrogates by weakening parameters subject to a spectral mixing criterion.
- Empirical results show improved marginal estimates under fixed compute in some regimes versus variational baselines and Gibbs on the original model.
- The idea of projecting onto the nearest rapidly-mixing model for approximate inference appears novel relative to prior work on mixing bounds.
- Technical development relies heavily on prior sufficient-condition results for rapid mixing [4,8].
- Main empirical reservation: improvements demonstrated only on small toy instances and may not scale to larger models where Gibbs mixing depends on size.
- The computational cost of the projection step is seemingly omitted from timing comparisons and may be significant.
- Unclear how the method extends beyond binary variables since general convergence guarantees are harder to obtain.
- Exposition is mostly clear but needs restructuring and more detail in places.
- Several “standard results” are stated as theorems without citations or proof sketches; Lemma 5 is unproved and its cited source covers only a special case.
- The dependency matrix R and its maximization properties are not sufficiently discussed; tractability is not explicitly stated.
- Sec. 4 is hard to follow: key symbols (g, M, Lambda) and notation used in Theorem 4 and before Theorem 7 are not properly defined.
- Experimental issues: projection time seems excluded from Figure 2 timings, no large-scale experiments or strong ground-truth benchmarks, and missing KL(θ||ψ) “best case” comparisons (junction tree viable on toy instances).
- Discrepancy between mean-field error and reverse-KL projection error merits discussion since MF is a degenerate reverse-KL case.
- Overall assessment: concept is appealing and elegant, but experimental concerns and limited scope of applicability reduce the paper’s impact.
