- Proposal: project slow-mixing pairwise Ising models onto nearby “fast-mixing” models using spectral bounds to accelerate Gibbs sampling and improve marginal estimates.

- Empirical claim: method yields improved marginals vs. variational methods and Gibbs on original model for certain regimes on toy Ising models.

- Novelty/technical basis: approach leverages prior rapid-mixing results [4,8]; projection-as-approximate-inference appears novel but depends heavily on existing bounds.

- Scalability concern: unclear how the method and observed mixing improvements scale to larger models.

- Projection cost: the computationally expensive projection step is not accounted for in timing comparisons, potentially biasing results.

- Generality concern: unclear whether approach extends beyond binary-state (Ising) models since convergence bounds are harder for general cases.

- Presentation issues: paper omits references/proof sketches for “standard results” (e.g., Theorem 6, unproven Lemma 5) making some claims hard to verify.

- Dependency matrix R: properties and tractability (maximization issues) of R need discussion and clarification.

- Section 4 clarity: latter half is hard to follow, introducing g, M, Λ and other notation before explanation; Theorem 4’s notation/optimization is inadequately explained.

- KL notation inconsistency: KL(q||p) used for both directions and q/p roles reversed relative to common literature; notation should be standardized.

- Experimental fairness: Figure 2 and related experiments omit projection computation time (not clearly stated), giving the method a potentially unfair advantage over standard Gibbs.

- Limited experiments: lack of larger-model experiments; suggested alternatives include symmetric models allowing exact marginals or using very long sampling runs as ground truth.

- Reverse KL suggestion: exploring KL(θ||ψ) (reverse KL) as a “best case” and using junction tree on toy models could be informative.

- Baseline analysis: large gap between mean-field error and reverse-KL projection needs discussion since mean field is a degenerate case of reverse KL.

- Overall: idea is elegant and promising, but experimental comparisons, scalability, applicability, and presentation must be clarified to strengthen the paper.
