- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a parameter set satisfying the Dobrushin criterion to speed Gibbs mixing.
- Novel idea combining stochastic and deterministic inference principles; under-explored and interesting.
- Technical description of the projection and dual problem is unclear and difficult to follow.
- Unclear how the constraints (e.g., zij * dij = 0) ensure the projected parameters preserve the original graphical support; could produce sparser models.
- Algorithmic procedure is ambiguous: unclear whether projection is done once or interleaved with sampling; no algorithm box provided.
- No proof or discussion of convergence guarantees for the projected gradient descent strategy.
- Experimental comparisons to deterministic methods use an arbitrary 30,000 Gibbs iterations rather than fair wall-clock time comparisons.
- Plots lack error bars or statistical significance analysis, preventing confidence in reported results.
- Comparison of samplers ignores the computational cost of the projection step; total time-to-accuracy should be reported.
- Missing comparisons to other Gibbs-improvement methods (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Gogate's lifted Gibbs sampler).
- Overall: promising idea but needs clearer technical exposition and far more rigorous, time-aware, and statistically-supported experiments.
