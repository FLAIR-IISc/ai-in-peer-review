- Addresses inference in Ising models by projecting parameters onto a space guaranteed to be fast-mixing for Gibbs sampling.
- Novel idea: define tractability by mixing time and develop projection algorithms into a fast-mixing parameter set.
- Explores multiple divergence measures (Euclidean, KL, approximations) and provides practical projection algorithms.
- Theoretical foundations are solid, leveraging spectral norm bounds and deriving an SVD-based Euclidean projection (Theorem 7).
- Experimental validation on small Ising models shows consistent improvements over LBP, TRW, MF and often improves Gibbs sampling under time constraints.
- Practical implementation details include stochastic gradient descent for reversed KL and discussion of computational trade-offs.
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and situates the work relative to existing approximate inference methods and Markov chain theory.
- Scalability is a major concern: experiments are limited to small graphs and projection (especially reversed KL) may be computationally expensive for larger models.
- Experimental reporting is ambiguous: Figure 2's x-axis labeled "number of samples" appears to reflect runtime; absolute runtimes for all methods should be reported.
- The spectral-norm-based fast-mixing set may be conservative; tighter bounds could improve projection effectiveness.
- Section 5.1 lacks details on stopping criteria for the inner LBFGS-B iterations, hindering reproducibility.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, promising empirical results, and useful analysis of divergence trade-offs.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability doubts, ambiguous experiment reporting, and possibly conservative spectral norm bounds.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor issues to fix; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
- Required revisions suggested: clarify Figure 2 and report runtimes, specify LBFGS-B stopping criteria, add larger-model experiments or discuss limitations, minor wording/terminology fixes.
