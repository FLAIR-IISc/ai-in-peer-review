- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to guarantee fast-mixing Gibbs sampling via a constrained optimization.  
- Combines stochastic and deterministic inference principles; focus on fast-mixing models is well-motivated.  
- Technical contribution: derives a projection algorithm and extends it to multiple divergences (Euclidean, KL, piecewise KL).  
- Uses the Dobrushin criterion soundly to establish fast-mixing conditions.  
- Experimental results are promising, showing improved Gibbs sampling accuracy.  
- The dual projection problem and the zij*dij = 0 constraint need clearer, more detailed explanation.  
- Lacks an algorithm box or step-by-step procedure to clarify the overall method.  
- Convergence analysis of the projected gradient/Ergodic Mirror Descent strategy is insufficient; rates and conditions are not fully discussed.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and could be more comprehensive and controlled.  
- Missing comparisons to advanced alternatives like blocked Gibbs samplers and other advanced Gibbs techniques.  
- Hyperparameter choices and computational resource details in experiments are not clearly reported.  
- Strengths: novel idea, thorough derivations, and promising empirical evidence.  
- Weaknesses: clarity and reproducibility issues, limited experimental scope, and incomplete convergence analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant technical contributions, promising results, and well-founded theoretical basis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient explanations and experimental rigor that may affect clarity and generalizability.
