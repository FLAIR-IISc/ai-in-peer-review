- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion to improve Gibbs sampling mixing rates.  
- Formulates this projection as a constrained optimization problem and analyzes it under several distance/divergence measures.  
- The combination of stochastic and deterministic inference principles is interesting and under-explored.  
- The dual formulation is unclear, particularly how the zij*dij = 0 constraints guarantee the new parameters remain over the same space (could they yield a smaller space?).  
- The overall procedure is ambiguous: do you fully run projected gradient updates then sample, or interleave sampling and updates? An algorithm box is needed.  
- It’s unclear whether the proposed projected gradient descent method has convergence guarantees.  
- Experimental choices are questionable (e.g., why run Gibbs for 30,000 iterations); time-based comparisons to deterministic methods would be fairer.  
- Plots lack error bars, so statistical significance of results is uncertain.  
- While projections show faster mixing than naive Gibbs, the time cost of computing the projection is not accounted for in comparisons.  
- Missing comparisons to blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s “Lifted Gibbs Sampler.”  
- Overall: promising idea but needs clearer development of the projection method and more comprehensive, time-aware experiments.
