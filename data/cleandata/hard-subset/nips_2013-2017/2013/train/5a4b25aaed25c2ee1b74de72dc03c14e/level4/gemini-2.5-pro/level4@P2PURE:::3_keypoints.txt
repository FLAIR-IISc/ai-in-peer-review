- Tackles slow mixing in Gibbs sampling for MRFs by projecting the original model onto a fast-mixing set and performing inference on the projection.  
- Defines fast-mixing models via a bound on the spectral norm of the matrix of absolute edge strengths, while preserving the original graph structure.  
- Formulates projection as minimizing a divergence between Gibbs distributions; Euclidean projection solved by dualizing and LBFGS-B.  
- KL, piecewise KL, and reverse KL projections optimized with a projected gradient algorithm.  
- Reverse KL projection requires running Gibbs sampling on the projected (fast-mixing) model during optimization.  
- Experiments on small random models compare approximated marginals to ground truth and benchmark against loopy BP, TRW, mean field, and original Gibbs.  
- Evaluation considers accuracy and runtime-accuracy trade-offs; proposed methods outperform TRW, MF, and loopy BP and beat standard Gibbs under a reasonable runtime budget.  
- Reverse KL projection consistently yields the best empirical performance among the variants.  
- Reviewer requests details on stopping criteria for inner LBFGS-B iterations in the projected gradient algorithm.

- Suggests evaluating larger models (e.g., Flach’s class with tractable partition function) for a more comprehensive benchmark.
