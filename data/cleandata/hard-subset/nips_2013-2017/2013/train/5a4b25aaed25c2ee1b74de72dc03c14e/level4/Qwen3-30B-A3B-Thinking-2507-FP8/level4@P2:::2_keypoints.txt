- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing parameter set using spectral norm constraints to accelerate Gibbs sampling.  
- Formulates constrained optimization under multiple divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL) and derives efficient algorithms.  
- Derives a dual Euclidean projection method using singular value thresholding (Theorem 7).  
- Core insight: guarantee rapid Gibbs mixing via parameter-space conditions rather than graph-structural constraints.  
- Novelty is strong and complements prior work on alternative tractability notions (e.g., Globerson & Jaakkola; Hazan & Shashua).  
- Technical derivations (especially the dual formulation) are generally sound and extend beyond Ising models.  
- Experiments show projected parameters can yield more accurate marginals than variational methods and naive Gibbs under limited sampling time.  
- Paper candidly acknowledges limitations (e.g., spectral norm conservatism for 2D Ising), which increases credibility.  
- Dual formulation lacks clarity: the role of the constraint Z_{ij} D_{ij} = 0 is not explicitly explained.  
- No algorithm box; unclear whether gradient updates are performed before sampling or interleaved.  
- Convergence guarantees for the projected gradient descent are not established and the citation to Ergodic Mirror Descent is not justified.  
- Experimental comparisons ignore the computational cost of projection (results reported by iterations, not runtime), undermining time-to-accuracy claims.  
- Figures lack error bars and statistical significance; comparisons omit important baselines like blocked Gibbs and lifted Gibbs.  
- Insufficient distinction from prior fast-mixing parameter work (Hayes; Dyer et al.); spectral-norm bound is conservative and not improved upon.  
- Recommended revisions: add an algorithm box, report accuracy vs. runtime with error bars, compare to blocked/lifted Gibbs, and clarify convergence conditions; as-is, reviewer recommends rejection.
