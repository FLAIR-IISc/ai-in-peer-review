- Proposes projecting slow-mixing two-label MRFs onto fast-mixing models preserving graph structure, using a spectral-norm constraint on absolute edge strengths.  
- Defines projection via divergences: Euclidean (dualization with LBFGS-B) and KL, piecewise KL, reversed KL (projected gradient), with reversed KL requiring Gibbs sampling on the fast-mixing model.  
- Experiments compare approximate marginals to ground truth on small random models against LBP, TRW, mean-field, and original-model Gibbs; assess accuracy and runtime-accuracy trade-offs.  
- Results show consistent superiority over TRW, MF, and LBP in accuracy; for practical runtimes also outperform original Gibbs; reversed KL gives the best performance among proposed methods.  
- Request clarification of the stopping criterion for the inner LBFGS-B iterations in the projected gradient algorithm (Section 5.1).  
- Figure 2’s x-axis labeled “number of samples” is misleading (sampling used only in reversed KL); recommend using runtime (seconds) and reporting absolute runtimes for LBP, TRW, and MF for fair comparisons.  
- Suggest extending experiments to larger models for scalability, citing Flach (2013, IEEE TPAMI) complete-graph random fields with tractable partition functions.
