- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin condition for rapid-mixing Gibbs sampling via a constrained optimization framed under multiple distance/divergence measures.  
- The core idea of augmenting parameters to improve mixing is promising and explores an underexamined link between stochastic and deterministic inference.  
- The dual formulation is unclear, especially how constraints like z_{ij}^* d_{ij} = 0 prevent the new parameterization from collapsing to a strict subset of the original space.  
- The paper lacks a precise algorithmic description (e.g., whether projected gradient updates are run to convergence before sampling or interleaved with Gibbs); a pseudocode box is needed.  
- Convergence guarantees for the proposed projected gradient descent procedure are not provided.  
- Experimental design is weak: choice of 30K Gibbs iterations seems arbitrary and a time-based comparison (samples/sec) would be more appropriate when comparing to deterministic methods.  
- Figures lack error bars, making the statistical significance of results ambiguous.  
- The computational cost of the projection step is not accounted for; projection overhead could negate practical benefits if expensive.  
- The work omits comparisons to established baselines such as blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.  
- Conclusion: idea is valuable but the dual formulation, algorithmic details, and experimental validation need substantial refinement before practical advantages are convincing.
