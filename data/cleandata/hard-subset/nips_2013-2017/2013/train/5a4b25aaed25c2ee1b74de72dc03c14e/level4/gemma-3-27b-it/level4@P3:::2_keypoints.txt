- Novel idea: project Ising model parameters onto a space guaranteeing faster Gibbs mixing to improve sampling efficiency.  
- Formulation: cast projection as constrained optimization under several divergence measures to stay close to original parameters.  
- Relevance: combines deterministic optimization with stochastic sampling, distinct from mean-field and loopy BP approaches.  
- Theoretical foundation: builds on established Markov chain mixing-time results and is generally sound.  
- Divergence exploration: examines Euclidean, piecewise KL, and reversed KL projections for nuanced analysis.  
- Algorithmic contribution: presents a dual algorithm for the Euclidean projection (Theorem 7).  
- Clarity issue: dual optimization description is unclear, particularly the role of the zij*dij = 0 constraints in preserving graph structure.  
- Presentation suggestion: include an explicit algorithm box to improve clarity of the method.  
- Convergence concern: no discussion of convergence guarantees or conditions for the projected gradient descent approach.  
- Experimental limitations: evaluation uses an arbitrary fixed 30,000 Gibbs iterations rather than time-based comparisons.  
- Statistical rigor: plots lack error bars, preventing assessment of significance.  
- Baselines missing: should compare to stronger MCMC methods (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Lifted Gibbs) beyond naive Gibbs.  
- Cost-benefit analysis missing: no quantitative assessment of projection time versus mixing-speed gains.  
- Theoretical limitation: spectral norm bound may be loose and this limitation needs more prominent discussion.  
- Strengths summary: novel approach, sound theory, multiple divergences explored, experimental potential shown.  
- Weaknesses summary: unclear dual description, unknown convergence, weak experiments, insufficient trade-off analysis, conservative spectral bound.  
- Recommendation: reviewer leans to rejection but encourages resubmission after addressing clarity, convergence analysis, experimental rigor, and trade-off evaluation.
