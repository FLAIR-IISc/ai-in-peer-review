- Proposes projecting input graphical models onto a set of fast-mixing models by bounding the spectral norm of the matrix of absolute Ising edge strengths.  
- Defines the fast-mixing model space and preserves graph structure during projection.  
- Explores multiple divergences for projection: Euclidean, KL, piecewise KL, and reversed KL.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of problem, method, and experiments.  
- Extensive experiments on small random models comparing to loopy BP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs sampling.  
- Proposed methods consistently outperform TRW, MF, and LBP in accuracy.  
- For reasonable runtimes, projected models also outperform Gibbs sampling on the original model; reversed KL performs best.  
- Lacks detail on the projected gradient algorithm, notably stopping criteria for inner iterations.  
- Plots (Figure 2 and supplement) are unclearly labeled; horizontal axis unclear; recommend reporting runtimes in seconds.  
- Recommends additional experiments on larger models (e.g., Boris Flach random fields on complete graphs).  
- Originality is high due to the novel projection approach and use of multiple divergences.  
- Overall quality and significance are strong with extensive comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the issues above.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10, Overall 8.5/10.
