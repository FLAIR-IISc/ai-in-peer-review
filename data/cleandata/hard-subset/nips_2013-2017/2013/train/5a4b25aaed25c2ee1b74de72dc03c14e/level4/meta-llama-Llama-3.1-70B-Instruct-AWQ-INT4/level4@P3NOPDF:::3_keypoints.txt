- Proposes projecting Ising MRFs onto a set of fast-mixing models (bounded spectral norm of absolute edge strengths) and performing inference there.  
- Defines projection via divergences between Gibbs distributions to preserve graph structure.  
- Uses dualization and LBFGS-B for Euclidean projection, and a projected-gradient algorithm for KL, piecewise KL, and reversed KL divergences.  
- Reversed KL projection requires Gibbs sampling to compute the projection, but sampling is done on the fast-mixing projected model.  
- Experiments on small random models compare approximated marginals to true marginals.  
- Baselines include loopy BP, TRW, mean field, and Gibbs sampling on the original model; runtime-vs-accuracy is evaluated.  
- Results: proposed methods consistently outperform TRW, MF, and LBP in accuracy.  
- For reasonable runtime ranges, proposed methods also outperform Gibbs sampling on the original model; reversed KL variant performs best.  
- Clarification needed on stopping criteria for the inner LBFGS-B iterations in the projected-gradient algorithm.  
- Figure 2’s horizontal axis labeled “number of samples” is unclear; recommend using runtime and reporting absolute running times (seconds) for fair comparisons.  
- Suggest extending experiments to larger models (e.g., datasets from Flach’s tractable complete-graph random fields) for stronger validation.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “onto the tree” to “onto graph T” (p.222) and “derivative” to “subgradient” (p.226); overall promising work but practical utility may be limited by runtime—needs rebuttal clarification.
