Success of Gibbs-sampling-based inference in MRFs (here restricted to two labels, i.e., the Ising model) depends heavily on the mixing rate of the underlying MCMC chain. The paper proposes the following inference strategy:
1) Project the input model (which may mix slowly) onto the class of models that mix rapidly.
2) Perform inference on the resulting fast-mixing model.
The set of fast-mixing models is specified by bounding the spectral norm of the matrix of absolute Ising edge weights. “Projection” is defined via divergences between Gibbs distributions and is constrained to preserve the graph structure. For Euclidean distance, the projection is obtained by dualizing the problem and applying L-BFGS-B. For other divergences (KL, piecewise KL, and reverse KL are implemented), a projected-gradient method is used. For reverse KL, Gibbs sampling (on the fast-mixing model) is required to compute the projection.
Extensive experiments on small random models compare approximate marginals to the true marginals. The evaluated methods include the proposed approach (with all the above divergences), loopy BP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs sampling on the original model. The study reports both accuracy and accuracy-versus-runtime. The results indicate that the proposed methods consistently outperform TRW, MF, and LBP in accuracy, and within a reasonable runtime range also outperform Gibbs sampling on the original model. Among the proposed variants, reverse KL performs best consistently.
