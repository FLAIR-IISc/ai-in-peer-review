- Paper proposes reparameterizing Ising models to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion, improving Gibbs mixing via a constrained projection optimization.  
- Combining stochastic and deterministic inference is an under-explored, interesting direction.  
- Explanation of the dual formulation is unclear, particularly how the constraint z_ij · d_ij = 0 guarantees the projected parameterization spans the original space.  
- Concern that the z_ij · d_ij = 0 constraint might restrict parameters to a smaller subspace rather than preserve the original space.  
- Procedure details are ambiguous: should projected gradient updates fully converge before sampling, or are updates and Gibbs sampling interleaved?  
- An algorithm box is needed to clearly describe the overall workflow.  
- No formal convergence guarantee is provided for the proposed projected gradient descent approach.  
- Experimental choices are unexplained (e.g., why run Gibbs for 30,000 iterations).  
- Performance comparisons omit runtime; sampling vs deterministic methods should be evaluated by wall-clock time.  
- Plots lack error bars, making statistical significance unclear.  
- Projection computation time is not accounted for; expensive projections could negate mixing gains.  
- Missing comparisons to alternative methods such as blocked Gibbs sampling or Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.  
- Overall: promising idea but projection formulation, method clarity, and empirical evaluation need strengthening.
