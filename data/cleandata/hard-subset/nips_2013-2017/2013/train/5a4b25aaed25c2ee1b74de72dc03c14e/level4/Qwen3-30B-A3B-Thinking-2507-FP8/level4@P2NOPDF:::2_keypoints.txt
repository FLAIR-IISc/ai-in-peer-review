- Positive/novel idea: projecting Ising-model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion for faster-mixing Gibbs is a promising and underexplored approach.  
- Unclear dual formulation: the justification that constraints z_{ij}^* d_{ij} = 0 preserve the original parameter space is not shown and may inadvertently restrict it.  
- Algorithmic ambiguity: the paper does not state whether projected gradient updates are applied before sampling or interleaved during sampling.  
- Missing pseudocode: a step-by-step algorithm box is needed to clarify the workflow.  
- No convergence guarantees: the projected gradient descent procedure lacks theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Inappropriate experimental metric: comparisons use iteration counts instead of wall-clock time, obscuring practical efficiency.  
- No error bars: absence of error bars prevents assessment of statistical significance.  
- Projection cost omitted: runtime of the projection step is not reported, so net benefit versus standard sampling is unclear.  
- Missing baselines: important alternatives (blocked Gibbs, Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs) are not included in comparisons.  
- Efficiency not demonstrated: experiments show improved mixing rates but do not establish time-per-effective-sample or overall efficiency gains.  
- Suggested reframe: report time-based metrics (samples/sec, time-to-convergence) and include projection overhead.  
- Recommended theory fixes: clarify the dual problem, prove parameter-space preservation, and provide convergence results for the optimization.  
- Recommended experimental fixes: add error bars, report projection runtimes, and compare to blocked/lifted Gibbs baselines.  
- Overall assessment: conceptually strong but currently lacks technical clarity, theoretical rigor, and empirical evidence of practical advantage.
