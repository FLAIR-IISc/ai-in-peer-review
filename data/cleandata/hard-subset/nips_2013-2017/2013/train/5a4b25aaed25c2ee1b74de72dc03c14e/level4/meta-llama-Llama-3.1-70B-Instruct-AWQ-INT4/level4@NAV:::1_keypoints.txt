- Proposes projecting a pairwise Ising model onto a “fast-mixing” model to improve Gibbs sampling mixing rates.  
- Shows improved marginal estimates in some regimes, outperforming variational methods and Gibbs on the original model.  
- Builds on prior theoretical conditions for rapid mixing; idea of nearest rapid-mixing projection is novel.  
- Scalability to larger models is unclear and not demonstrated by experiments.  
- Projection step is computationally expensive and its cost is not included in reported results.  
- Experiments comparing mixing times do not provide clear insight into how the method scales with problem size.  
- Unclear generalizability beyond binary-state models; convergence bounds for more complex cases are challenging.  
- Presentation needs reorganization and clearer explanations of “standard results.”  
- Theorem 6 is stated without proof or citation; Lemma 5 is unproven.  
- Properties and tractability of the dependency matrix R are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Second half of Section 4 is unclear and Theorem 7’s notation requires more explanation.  
- KL divergence notation is inconsistent across the paper.  
- Time comparison in Figure 2 omits projection computation, undermining fairness of comparisons.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; should include projection computation time and experiments on larger models.  
- Suggests exploring intractable KL(θ||ψ) as a potential best-case benchmark and discussing the large gap between mean field error and reverse-KL projection error.  
- Overall: elegant and novel idea but experimental, scalability, and presentation concerns need to be addressed.
