- Addresses slow mixing in Gibbs sampling by projecting MRFs onto a fast-mixing model space (bounded spectral norm of absolute edge weights) and performing inference there.  
- Projection is formulated via divergences between Gibbs distributions while preserving graph structure.  
- Implementations: Euclidean projection (dual + LBFGS-B) and KL, piecewise KL, reversed KL via projected gradient descent.  
- Reversed KL projection requires Gibbs sampling on the fast-mixing model to compute the projection.  
- Experiments on small random models compare approximate marginals to ground truth and analyze runtime-vs-accuracy trade-offs.  
- Proposed methods consistently outperform TRW, MF, and LBP, and beat standard Gibbs for comparable runtimes.  
- Reversed KL divergence yields the best empirical results among the proposed divergences.
