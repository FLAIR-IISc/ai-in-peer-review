- Paper proposes accelerating Gibbs mixing in Ising models by projecting parameters onto settings that satisfy the Dobrushin fast-mixing criterion.  
- The direction of combining stochastic (sampling) and deterministic (projection) inference is underexplored and interesting.  
- Presentation of the dual of the projection problem is unclear.  
- The constraint zij * dij = 0 is not justified: it may force the projected parameters into a strict subset rather than the same space.  
- The overall workflow is ambiguous: unclear whether one runs projected gradient descent to convergence before Gibbs sampling or interleaves sampling and updates.  
- An algorithm box is needed to clarify the procedure.  
- It is unclear whether the proposed projected gradient descent algorithm has convergence guarantees.  
- Experimental protocol issues: running Gibbs for 30K iterations is arbitrary; time-based comparisons would be fairer when contrasting sampling and deterministic baselines.  
- Plots lack error bars, so statistical significance of results is uncertain.  
- Computational cost of computing the projection is not accounted for, which may negate wall-clock gains in mixing.  
- Missing baselines: no comparison to blocked Gibbs samplers or Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.  
- Overall judgment: promising idea but requires clearer development of the projection method and a stronger, fairer experimental evaluation.
