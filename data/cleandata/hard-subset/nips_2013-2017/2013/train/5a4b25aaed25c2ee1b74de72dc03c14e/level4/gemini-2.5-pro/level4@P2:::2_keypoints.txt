- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to a "fast-mixing" set using a spectral (Dobrushin-like) condition to guarantee rapid Gibbs mixing.  
- Formulates this projection as a constrained optimization under four divergences: Euclidean, KL(q||p), a novel piecewise KL, and KL(p||q).  
- Derives a dual optimization algorithm for the Euclidean projection.  
- Uses stochastic projected gradient descent (requiring sampling) for the KL(p||q) projection.  
- Reframes tractability as guaranteed sampling efficiency rather than exact inference, bridging MCMC and variational methods.  
- Strength: highly original and potentially significant idea addressing slow MCMC mixing.  
- Strength: solid technical contributions (dual method and stochastic projected gradient application).  
- Weakness: unclear presentation of core projection algorithms (dual formulation and role of constraint ZijBij = 0 need more intuition).  
- Weakness: unclear whether projection and sampling are interleaved or sequential; needs an explicit algorithm box.  
- Weakness: claims about ergodic mirror descent convergence need discussion of whether chosen step-size schedule satisfies theory.  
- Experimental weakness: comparisons use fixed Gibbs iterations rather than wall-clock time, making fairness questionable.  
- Experimental weakness: projection computational cost is not accounted for, so net efficiency gains are uncertain.  
- Experimental weakness: missing relevant MCMC baselines (e.g., block Gibbs, Lifted Gibbs) for mixing improvement.  
- Experimental weakness: plots lack error bars/confidence intervals despite averaging over 50 trials.  
- For acceptance: idea is novel, well-motivated, and supported by concrete algorithms.  
- For rejection: lack of algorithmic clarity and an incomplete, potentially unfair experimental evaluation undermine practical impact.  
- Conditional recommendation: accept if authors improve clarity (algorithms, constraints, convergence) and strengthen experiments (time-based comparisons, projection cost, additional baselines, error bars).
