- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion to accelerate Gibbs sampling.  
- Frames projection as a constrained optimization problem preserving equivalence between original and projected parameter spaces, analyzed with various distances/divergences.  
- Tackles an important, underexplored synergy between stochastic and deterministic inference; idea is potentially valuable.  
- Explanation of the dual formulation is unclear, especially why the zij*dij = 0 constraints guarantee the projection remains in the original space.  
- Procedural flow is ambiguous: unclear whether projected gradient descent is completed before Gibbs sampling or interleaved with sampling; needs an explicit algorithm.  
- Convergence properties of the proposed projected gradient descent are not analyzed or justified.  
- Experimental choice of 30,000 iterations for the initial Gibbs sampler is not motivated.  
- Performance comparisons should be based on computational time, not just iterations, when comparing to deterministic methods.  
- Results lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance.  
- Evaluation ignores the computational cost of the projection itself, which could negate mixing gains.  
- Missing comparisons to established baselines such as blocked Gibbs sampling and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.  
- Overall: promising concept but needs clearer technical development and a more comprehensive, fair experimental evaluation.
