- Paper proposes projecting Ising-model parameters to satisfy Dobrushin criterion to speed Gibbs mixing via a constrained optimization formulation.  
- Commendable idea: combining stochastic (sampling) and deterministic (projection) inference principles.  
- Core concept is straightforward but the dual projection problem description is unclear.  
- Unclear how constraint z_{ij} d_{ij} = 0 ensures the projected parameters occupy the same space rather than a smaller subspace.  
- Overall procedure unclear: are projections done entirely before Gibbs sampling or interleaved with gradient updates?  
- An algorithm box/clear procedural description is needed for exposition.  
- No convergence guarantees provided for the projected gradient descent strategy.  
- Experiments are unconvincing: using fixed iterations (30k) vs time-based evaluation is inappropriate when comparing to deterministic methods.  
- Results lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance.  
- Projection runtime cost is not accounted for; a projection that is expensive could negate mixing gains.  
- Missing comparisons to relevant baselines such as blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.  
- Overall verdict: idea is promising but requires refinement of the projection development and stronger experimental validation.
