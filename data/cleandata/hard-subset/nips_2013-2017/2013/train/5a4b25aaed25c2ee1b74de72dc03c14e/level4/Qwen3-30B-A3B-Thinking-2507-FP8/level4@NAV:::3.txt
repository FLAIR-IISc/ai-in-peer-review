

The paper addresses the dependence of Gibbs sampling inference success in MRFs (specifically two-label Ising models) on the mixing rate of the underlying Markov chain. It proposes projecting a slow-mixing input model onto the set of fast-mixing models defined by bounding the spectral norm of the absolute edge strength matrix, while preserving the graph structure. Projection is achieved via divergences of Gibbs distributions: Euclidean distance uses dualization with LBFGS-B, while KL, piecewise KL, and reversed KL divergences employ projected gradient algorithms. Reversed KL projection requires Gibbs sampling on the fast-mixing model. Experiments on small random models compare approximated marginals against true marginals, evaluating the proposed methods (all divergences), loopy BP, TRW, MF, and original-model Gibbs sampling. Results show consistent accuracy superiority over TRW, MF, and LBP, and competitive performance against original-model Gibbs sampling within reasonable runtimes. The reversed KL method performs best across all proposed approaches.
