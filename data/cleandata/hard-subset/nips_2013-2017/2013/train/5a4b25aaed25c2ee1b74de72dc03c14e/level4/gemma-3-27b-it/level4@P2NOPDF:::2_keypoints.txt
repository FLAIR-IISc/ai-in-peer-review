- Introduces projecting Ising model parameters to a Dobrushin-satisfying parameterization to accelerate Gibbs sampling.  
- Combining stochastic and deterministic inference techniques is a promising and underexplored idea.  
- Conceptually sound approach: augmenting parameters to improve mixing time.  
- Derivation and explanation of the dual problem for the projection are unclear.  
- Rationale for the z_ij * d_ij = 0 constraints is not explained; unclear why they prevent collapse of the parameter space.  
- Overall procedure is ambiguous: unclear whether projected gradient descent is run to completion before Gibbs sampling or interleaved; an explicit algorithmic description is needed.  
- Convergence properties of the proposed projected gradient descent are not discussed.  
- Experimental choices are questionable (e.g., why run Gibbs for 30,000 iterations); recommend evaluating by computational time for fair comparison with deterministic methods.  
- Figures lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance.  
- Computational cost of the projection step is omitted from runtime comparisons; full runtime accounting is required.  
- No comparisons to established acceleration methods (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler), which are important baselines.  
- Overall: compelling idea but needs clearer mathematical development and a more comprehensive, statistically rigorous experimental evaluation including runtime and relevant baselines.
