- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin uniqueness condition to guarantee rapid Gibbs mixing.  
- Combines stochastic and deterministic inference principles, a promising and under-explored direction.  
- Technical exposition is unclear, particularly the dual formulation of the projection problem.  
- Unclear how zij * dij = 0 constraints preserve original support; may unintentionally restrict to a smaller subgraph.  
- Algorithmic procedure is ambiguous: unclear whether projection is done once or interleaved with sampling; pseudocode would help.  
- Unclear whether the projected gradient descent method is guaranteed to converge.  
- Experimental choice of a fixed 30,000 Gibbs iterations is not justified.  
- Comparisons should be based on wall-clock time rather than iteration count for fairness.  
- Plots lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance.  
- Experiments ignore computational overhead of the projection step, which may negate practical gains.  
- Missing comparisons to other advanced MCMC methods (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Gogate's Lifted Gibbs sampler).  
- Overall: idea is novel but needs clearer theoretical development and a more thorough, fair experimental evaluation.
