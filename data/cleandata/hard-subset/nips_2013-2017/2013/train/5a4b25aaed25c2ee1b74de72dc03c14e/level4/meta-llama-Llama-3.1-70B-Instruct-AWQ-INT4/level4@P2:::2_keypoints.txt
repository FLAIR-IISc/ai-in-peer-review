- Paper proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a setting that satisfies the Dobrushin criterion to improve Gibbs sampling mixing rates.  
- Projection is formulated as a constrained optimization problem under several distance/divergence measures.  
- Combining stochastic and deterministic inference principles is novel and under-explored.  
- The dual formulation needs clarification, especially how zij * dij = 0 ensures the projected parameters lie in the original parameter space.  
- Include an algorithm box to clarify the overall procedure (e.g., whether projection is completed before sampling or interleaved).  
- Clarify and/or prove convergence guarantees for the projected gradient descent strategy.  
- First experiment setup is questionable (e.g., why 30,000 Gibbs iterations) and should compare methods by runtime as well as iterations.  
- Experimental plots lack error bars, making significance of results unclear.  
- Second experiment shows projection speeds mixing, but does not account for the time required to compute the projection.  
- Important baseline comparisons are missing, such as blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.  
- Overall idea is promising but the projection problem and method require clearer development and refinement.  
- Experiments need to be more comprehensive and account for projection computation time to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Strengths: innovative approach to improving Gibbs mixing and interesting combination of inference paradigms.  
- Weaknesses: unclear dual description, unclear procedure, unconfirmed convergence, limited experiments, and omitted projection runtime in comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions — authors should clarify dual/algorithm, confirm convergence, expand experiments, and add relevant baseline comparisons.
