- Paper proposes reparameterizing Ising models to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion to speed Gibbs mixing via a constrained projection problem.  
- Integration of stochastic and deterministic inference is an interesting and underexplored idea.  
- The concept of augmenting parameters to improve mixing is straightforward but needs clearer motivation and framing.  
- The dual formulation of the projection problem is unclear and poorly explained.  
- It’s unclear how the zij * dij = 0 constraints ensure the new parameterization remains in the same parameter space rather than a smaller one.  
- The overall procedure is ambiguous: must projection complete before sampling, or can sampling and gradient updates be interleaved?  
- An algorithm box or explicit procedural description is needed to clarify steps and ordering.  
- It’s not shown whether the proposed projected gradient descent procedure is guaranteed to converge.  
- Experiments are unconvincing: choice of 30,000 Gibbs iterations is unexplained and arbitrary.  
- Comparisons should be time-based (wall-clock) rather than fixed-iteration to fairly compare sampling vs deterministic methods.  
- Plots lack error bars or significance measures, making results hard to interpret.  
- Projection runtime is not accounted for when comparing mixing speed, potentially negating practical gains.  
- Missing comparisons to other sampling improvements (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs) weaken empirical claims.  
- Overall: interesting idea but requires clearer development, theoretical guarantees, and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation.
