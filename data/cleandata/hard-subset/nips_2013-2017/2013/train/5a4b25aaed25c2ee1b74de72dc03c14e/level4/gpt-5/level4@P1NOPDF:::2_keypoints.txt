- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion to speed Gibbs mixing and explores several projection distance/divergence measures.  
- Interesting and underexplored idea: combining deterministic parameter modification with stochastic inference to accelerate mixing.  
- The dual formulation is unclear—specifically how the zij * dij = 0 constraints guarantee the projected parameters remain in the original parameter space and do not unduly restrict it.  
- The overall workflow is ambiguous: unclear whether projected-gradient updates are run to convergence before sampling or interleaved; a concise algorithm/pseudocode is needed.  
- Convergence guarantees for the projected gradient descent are not stated—specify conditions (smoothness, step-size) and whether convergence is to a local or global optimum.  
- Experiments fix Gibbs to 30K iterations, which is arbitrary; wall-clock time comparisons are more appropriate given deterministic baselines.  
- Plots lack error bars or variability estimates, making statistical significance and reproducibility unclear.  
- Projection computation cost is omitted; report total time-to-accuracy (projection plus sampling) to fairly assess benefits.  
- Missing comparisons to stronger baselines such as blocked Gibbs and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler to contextualize gains.  
- Recommendation: clarify dual and algorithmic details (with convergence), and strengthen empirical evaluation with time-based metrics, uncertainty estimates, projection cost accounting, and stronger baselines.
