- Paper proposes projecting Ising models onto a fast-mixing family (spectral-norm bound on absolute edge strengths) and performing inference on the projection to address slow Gibbs mixing.

- Projection minimizes a divergence between original and projected Gibbs distributions while preserving graph structure; divergences considered: Euclidean, KL, piecewise KL, reverse KL.

- Euclidean projection solved via dual optimization with LBFGS-B; other divergences use a projected gradient approach; reverse KL requires Gibbs sampling on the fast-mixing projected model.

- Experiments on small random models compare marginal estimates to ground truth and report accuracy-runtime trade-offs.

- Proposed methods consistently outperform TRW, MF, LBP, and standard Gibbs on the original model within a reasonable runtime budget; reverse KL projection performs best.

- Major concern: practical utility unclear due to potentially high runtimes—requires clarification in experiments.

- Major comment: Figure 2 x-axis ("number of samples") is unclear and should likely be wall-clock runtime (seconds) for fair accuracy-runtime comparisons.

- Major comment: Baseline runtimes (LBP, TRW, MF) are not reported—these should be shown (e.g., as vertical lines) to enable fair comparisons.

- Major comment: Clarify stopping criteria for inner LBFGS-B iterations in the projected gradient algorithm for reproducibility and complexity assessment.

- Suggestion: Demonstrate scalability by evaluating larger models (e.g., Flach’s class with tractable partition functions) rather than only small random graphs.

- Minor: Line 222 — "onto the tree" likely should read "onto graph T" for consistency.

- Minor: Line 226 — consider using "subgradient" instead of "derivative" given the optimization context.
