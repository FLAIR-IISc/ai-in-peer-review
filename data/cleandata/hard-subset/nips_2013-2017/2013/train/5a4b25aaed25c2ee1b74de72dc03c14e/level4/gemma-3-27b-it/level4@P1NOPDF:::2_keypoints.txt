- Paper proposes projecting Ising-model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion to accelerate Gibbs mixing via a constrained optimization formulation.
- Combining stochastic and deterministic inference methods is interesting and under-explored.
- Description of the dual problem is unclear, particularly how constraints like z_ij * d_ij = 0 ensure the new parameterization stays in the same parameter space (might restrict it).
- Unclear overall procedure: is projected gradient descent run to completion before sampling or interleaved with Gibbs updates—an algorithm box would clarify.
- No discussion or proof of convergence guarantees for the proposed projected gradient descent.
- Experiments are unconvincing: choice of 30,000 Gibbs iterations is unexplained and runtime comparisons would be more appropriate when comparing sampling to deterministic methods.
- Plots lack error bars, making statistical significance and variance unclear.
- The computational cost of computing the projection is not accounted for when comparing mixing improvements (could negate benefits).
- No comparisons to blocked Gibbs samplers or Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler, which are relevant baselines.
- Overall idea is promising but the projection formulation and empirical evaluation need refinement and broader experimental validation.
