- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a "fast-mixing" set to guarantee rapid Gibbs mixing via a spectral bound.  
- Method is novel and builds on recent rapid-mixing results.  
- Technical content is sound and well-grounded in prior work.  
- Small-model experiments show improved marginal estimates versus variational methods and Gibbs on the original model.  
- Major concern about scalability to larger models.  
- Projection step is computationally expensive and its cost is omitted from time comparisons.  
- Comparisons of mixing times are difficult to interpret and may not generalize to larger problem sizes.  
- Unclear generalizability beyond binary states; convergence bounds for more complex models are nontrivial.  
- Several “standard results” are cited without proofs or references (e.g., Theorem 6, Lemma 5), hindering accessibility for non-experts.  
- Properties and tractability of the dependency matrix R are not explicitly stated.  
- Second half of Section 4 has unclear notation and an opaque optimization procedure; needs reorganization and more explanation.  
- Inconsistent KL notation in Section 1 (q and p reversed relative to standard convention).  
- Figure 2 time comparison excludes projection time; authors should clarify and include projection overhead.  
- Experiments should include larger models and stronger baselines (e.g., junction tree, long sampling runs).  
- The large error gap between mean field and reverse KL projection warrants more discussion; exploring zero-avoiding KL as a best-case sampling baseline would be valuable.  
- Overall: elegant, potentially significant idea but requires revisions on scalability, presentation, and experimental evaluation.
