- Paper proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy Dobrushin criterion to improve Gibbs mixing.  
- Combining stochastic and deterministic inference is an interesting and under-explored direction.  
- Concept of augmenting parameters for faster mixing is straightforward and promising.  
- Derivation of the dual and the role of zij * dij = 0 constraints is unclear—uncertainty whether projection preserves the original parameter space or shrinks it.  
- Overall procedure is ambiguous: unclear if projected gradient is run to completion before sampling or interleaved with sampling; an algorithm box would clarify.  
- Unclear whether the projected gradient descent strategy has convergence guarantees.  
- First experiments are unconvincing: choice of 30,000 Gibbs iterations is unexplained and runtime-based comparisons would be fairer against deterministic methods.  
- Experimental plots lack error bars, making significance of results hard to judge.  
- Second experiments show faster mixing under projection but do not account for time/overhead of computing the projection.  
- No accounting for projection cost versus raw sampling throughput (e.g., samples generated during projection time).  
- Missing comparisons to relevant baselines like blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s "Lifted Gibbs Sampler."  
- Overall assessment: nice idea but needs clearer method development and more comprehensive, runtime-aware experiments to be convincing.
