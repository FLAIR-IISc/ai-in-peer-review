- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto the set of fast-mixing models by bounding the spectral norm of the matrix of absolute edge strengths.
- Defines projection via divergences between Gibbs distributions while preserving the graph structure.
- Derives algorithms for projection under Euclidean distance, KL-divergence, piecewise KL, and reversed KL-divergence.
- Evaluates on small random models, comparing approximated marginals to ground-truth and to LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs sampling.
- Proposed methods consistently outperform TRW, MF, and LBP in accuracy, and beat Gibbs sampling for a reasonable runtime range; reversed KL performs best.
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written.
- Missing detail: stopping criteria for the inner LBFGS-B iterations in the projected-gradient algorithm.
- Plot labeling issue: x-axis titled “number of samples” is misleading; should be labeled as algorithm runtime.
- Need to report runtimes for LBP, TRW, and MF to enable fair accuracy-runtime comparisons.
- Practical utility may be limited by high runtime; this should be clarified in the rebuttal.
- Original approach with interesting results, but more related-work context and comparisons are needed.
- Paper is significant and has potential impact in MRFs and related areas.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Strengths: novel approach, convincing empirical results, and clear presentation.
- Weaknesses: minor labeling/clarity issues and possible high computational cost.
- Suggested improvements: detail inner-iteration stopping, fix plot labels, report other algorithms’ runtimes, test larger models, and expand related-work comparisons.
