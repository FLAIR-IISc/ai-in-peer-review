- Problem and approach: addresses slow Gibbs mixing in Ising models by projecting parameters onto the nearest fast-mixing model defined by a spectral-norm constraint.  
- Strength — novelty: framing approximate inference as projection onto guaranteed fast-mixing models is a new and interesting perspective.  
- Strength — theory: leverages prior MCMC mixing-time bounds (Hayes [8], Dyer et al. [4]) to define the fast-mixing constraint.  
- Strength — experiments: provides a reasonable proof-of-concept comparison against strong baselines (MF, LBP, TRW, Gibbs) on small models.  
- Weakness — excluded projection cost: runtime comparisons omit the computational cost of projection (sometimes substantial, e.g., 30k Gibbs iterations for KL(ψ||θ)), biasing results.  
- Weakness — limited scale: experiments are confined to small graphs (8×8 grids, 10-node graphs) where exact inference is possible, leaving scalability and real-world applicability unclear.  
- Weakness — unsupported technical claims: key results (Theorem 6, Lemma 5) lack citations or proofs, making soundness hard to verify.  
- Weakness — opaque optimization section: latter half of Section 4 and Theorem 7 use symbols before definition and dense notation, reducing clarity and reproducibility.  
- Weakness — confusing notation/conventions: KL notation and the q/p convention deviate from common usage, which hinders readability.  
- Weakness — limited generality: method is developed for pairwise binary Ising models; extending to general discrete MRFs and deriving mixing bounds for general potentials is nontrivial.  
- Request — provide proofs/citations: supply citations for Theorem 6 and a proof or sketch for the generalization in Lemma 5.  
- Request — clarify dependency matrix R: explain properties and tractability of computing R and state limitations explicitly.  
- Request — rewrite Section 4: present the dual formulation (Theorem 7) more pedagogically with clear definitions and intuition.  
- Request — include projection time in experiments: report results that account for full computational budget (projection + sampling) for fair comparison.  
- Request — explain MF gap: give intuition for why Mean Field performs much worse than the KL(ψ||θ) projection.  
- Request — add oracle baseline: include KL(θ||ψ) projection using exact expectations (junction tree) as a best-case/oracle comparison in toy problems.
