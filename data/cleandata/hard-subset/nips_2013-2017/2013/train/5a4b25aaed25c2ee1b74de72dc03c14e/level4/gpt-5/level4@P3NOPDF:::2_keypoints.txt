- Proposal: project Ising-model parameters onto the Dobrushin set to guarantee fast-mixing, then run Gibbs sampling on the projected model.  
- Formulation: projection framed as constrained optimization under various distance/divergence measures to preserve model domain.  
- Strength: leveraging deterministic projection to force provable fast-mixing before stochastic sampling is interesting and underexplored.  
- Strength: framing projection as an optimization problem is natural and potentially broadly applicable.  
- Strength: exploring multiple divergences/distances offers flexibility for different downstream objectives.  
- Dual/constraints unclear: need better motivation and interpretation of dual formulation and constraints (e.g., role of z_ij * d_ij = 0).  
- Feasibility concern: unclear proof/argument that projection preserves the model’s support and doesn’t collapse parameter space; provide intuition/KKT-based argument.  
- Algorithmic clarity: unclear whether projection is run to convergence then sampling, or interleaved with sampling; include a concise algorithm box with inputs/outputs/stopping criteria and complexity.  
- Convergence guarantees: unclear when projected gradient descent converges (convexity/smoothness/step-size requirements) and whether projection onto the Dobrushin set is efficient.  
- Measurement protocol: reporting results after fixed 30k Gibbs iterations is hard to interpret—need wall-clock time comparisons or equal-time sample complexity and justify choice of 30k.  
- Statistical significance: report error bars/CI across seeds to assess variability and significance.  
- End-to-end cost: projection computation time must be included and amortized; compare equal-time performance versus naive Gibbs.  
- Baselines: compare to stronger MCMC methods (blocked Gibbs, lifted/blocked variants like Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs) to isolate benefits of projection.  
- Diagnostics: include mixing diagnostics (autocorrelation, ESS, PSRF) and report Dobrushin coefficient before/after projection and impacts on downstream metrics (marginals, log-likelihood).  
- Ablations/sensitivity: compare different divergences, regularization parameters, and stopping criteria to show robustness/sensitivity.  
- Practicality details: report model sizes, sparsity/density, coupling strengths, and quantify how much parameters change under projection (norms, coupling shrinkage, frequency of already-satisfying models).  
- Reproducibility: add clear algorithmic summary, optimization details (step-size rules, iterations, tolerances), and pseudocode or release code.  
- If projection is approximate: discuss its effect on guarantees and empirical behavior.  
- Overall: idea is compelling but requires stronger technical exposition, clarified algorithmic workflow/convergence, and substantially improved, time-aware, statistically sound experiments against stronger baselines.
