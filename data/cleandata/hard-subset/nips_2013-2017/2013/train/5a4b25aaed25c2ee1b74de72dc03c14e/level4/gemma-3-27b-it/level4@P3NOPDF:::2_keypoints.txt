- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion to accelerate Gibbs mixing via a constrained optimization formulation.
- Novel combination of stochastic and deterministic inference, with parameter augmentation to improve mixing time, is compelling and under-explored.
- Presentation of the dual and the z_ij d_ij = 0 constraints is unclear; need justification why these constraints keep the projection within the original parameter space.
- Overall procedure flow is ambiguous: unclear whether projected gradient descent (PGD) is run to full convergence before Gibbs sampling or interleaved with sampling.
- Recommend adding an algorithm box that outlines the complete procedure for clarity.
- Convergence properties of the proposed PGD are not addressed—need guarantees or conditions for convergence.
- Experimental comparisons are incomplete: use of 30,000-iteration runs should be complemented by wall-clock time comparisons between sampling and deterministic methods.
- Plots lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance.
- The computational cost of performing the projection is not included in runtime analysis and could negate practical benefits.
- Related work discussion is incomplete; should compare to baselines like blocked Gibbs and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.
- Overall: interesting and potentially valuable idea, but mathematical development and experiments require substantial revision; recommendation to reject with invitation to resubmit after addressing concerns.
