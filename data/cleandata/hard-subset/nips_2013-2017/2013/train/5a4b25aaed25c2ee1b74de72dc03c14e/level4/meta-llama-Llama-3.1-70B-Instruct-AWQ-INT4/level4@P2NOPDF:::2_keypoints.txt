Proposes projecting Ising-model parameters onto a setting that satisfies the Dobrushin criterion to improve Gibbs mixing, cast as a constrained optimization under several divergences.

Interesting and under-explored idea combining stochastic and deterministic inference principles.

Formulation details unclear: the dual of the projection problem is not well explained.

Unclear how zij * dij = 0 constraints ensure the projected parameters span the same space rather than a smaller one.

Ambiguity in the overall procedure: whether projected gradient steps are run to completion before sampling or interleaved with Gibbs; an algorithm box is needed.

No discussion or proof about convergence guarantees of the projected gradient descent strategy.

Experimental setup choices are questionable (e.g., why run Gibbs for 30,000 iterations).

Comparisons should be time-based when pitting sampling against deterministic methods rather than iteration-count based.

Plots lack error bars, making result significance unclear.

Mixing improvements shown ignore the time cost of computing the projection, which may negate benefits.

Missing comparisons to stronger baselines like blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.

Promising idea overall but needs clearer methodological development and a more comprehensive, fair experimental evaluation.
