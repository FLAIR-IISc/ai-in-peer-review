- Paper proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto settings satisfying the Dobrushin criterion to speed Gibbs mixing, via a constrained optimization analyzed under several divergences.  
- Idea of combining stochastic and deterministic inference is interesting and under-explored.  
- Dual projection description is unclear — specifically how constraints z_{ij} d_{ij} = 0 ensure the new parameters cover the same space rather than a smaller subspace.  
- Procedure unclear: are projected gradient updates run to completion before Gibbs sampling or interleaved with sampling? An explicit algorithm box is needed.  
- No discussion or proof of convergence guarantees for the proposed projected gradient descent.  
- Experimental setup weaknesses: choosing 30,000 Gibbs iterations seems arbitrary; runtime-based comparisons are more appropriate when comparing sampling to deterministic methods.  
- Results lack error bars/confidence intervals, so statistical significance of improvements is unknown.  
- Computational cost of performing the projection is not reported; a slow projection could negate practical benefits (e.g., if projection takes as long as sampling many naive samples).  
- Missing comparisons to stronger baselines such as blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.  
- Overall assessment: promising core idea but requires substantial clarification of the projection development, algorithmic procedure, convergence claims, and stronger, runtime-aware experimental validation.
