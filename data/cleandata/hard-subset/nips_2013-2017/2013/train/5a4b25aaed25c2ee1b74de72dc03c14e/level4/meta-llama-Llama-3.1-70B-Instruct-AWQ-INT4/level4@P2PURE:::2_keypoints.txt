- Paper proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a Dobrushin-criterion-satisfying space to improve Gibbs mixing, formulated as a constrained optimization under various distance/divergence measures.
- Combining stochastic and deterministic inference is interesting and under-explored, making the idea promising.
- The dual formulation of the projection problem is unclear and poorly explained.
- It is not apparent how the zij * dij = 0 constraints ensure the projected parameters lie in the same space as the original rather than a smaller subspace.
- The overall procedure is ill-defined: unclear whether projected gradient steps are run to completion before sampling or interleaved with Gibbs updates.
- An algorithm box or explicit pseudocode is needed to clarify the method.
- There is no discussion or guarantee of convergence for the proposed projected gradient descent strategy.
- Experiments are unconvincing: choosing 30,000 Gibbs iterations appears arbitrary.
- Comparisons should be based on runtime (wall-clock time) rather than fixed iteration counts, especially when comparing sampling to deterministic methods.
- Plots lack error bars, so statistical significance of results is unclear.
- Second experiment shows faster mixing after projection but does not account for the computational cost/time of performing the projection.
- No comparisons are made to relevant baselines such as blocked Gibbs samplers or Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.
- Overall, the projection development and method need refinement and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation to demonstrate utility.
