- Summary: Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a set satisfying the Dobrushin criterion to improve Gibbs mixing, via a constrained optimization studied under several divergences.
- Strength: Novel and promising combination of stochastic and deterministic inference.
- Strength: Intuitive and potentially practical idea to enforce a sufficient mixing condition by projection.
- Clarity: The dual formulation is unclear—how do constraints z_ij * d_ij = 0 ensure the projected parameters remain on the same space and not a strictly smaller one?
- Clarity: Overall procedure is underspecified—unclear whether projection runs to completion before Gibbs sampling or is interleaved; an explicit algorithm box is needed.
- Clarity: Convergence of the projected gradient descent is not established—conditions on step sizes, convexity, and feasibility are missing.
- Experiments: Running Gibbs for 30K iterations is unexplained; wall-clock time comparisons would be a fairer measure versus deterministic methods.
- Experiments: Plots lack error bars, so statistical significance and robustness are unclear.
- Experiments: Projection overhead time is omitted; end-to-end efficiency may be worse if projection is costly.
- Experiments: Important baselines are missing—no comparison to blocked Gibbs samplers or Gogate’s lifted Gibbs sampler.
- Overall: Interesting and promising contribution, but needs clearer exposition of the projection/dual, guaranteed convergence, and a stronger, time-aware empirical evaluation to demonstrate practical utility.
