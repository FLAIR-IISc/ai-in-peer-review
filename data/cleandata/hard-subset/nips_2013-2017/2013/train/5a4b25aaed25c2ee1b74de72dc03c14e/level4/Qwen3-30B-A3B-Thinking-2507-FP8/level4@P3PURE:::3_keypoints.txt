1. Proposes projecting two-label MRFs (Ising models) onto a set of fast-mixing models by bounding the spectral norm of the absolute edge-strength matrix, then performing inference on the projection.  
2. Projection is defined via divergences between Gibbs distributions, constrained to preserve the original graph structure.  
3. Uses Euclidean projection (dualized and solved with LBFGS-B) and other divergences (KL, piecewise KL, reversed KL) solved with a projected gradient method.  
4. Reversed KL projection uniquely requires Gibbs sampling on the fast-mixing model to compute the projection.  
5. Experiments on small random models compare proposed methods, LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs on the original model, measuring accuracy and runtime-vs-accuracy trade-offs.  
6. Results show the proposed methods consistently outperform TRW, MF, and LBP in accuracy, and for reasonable runtimes also outperform Gibbs on the original model.  
7. Among proposed divergences, reversed KL gives the most consistent best performance.
