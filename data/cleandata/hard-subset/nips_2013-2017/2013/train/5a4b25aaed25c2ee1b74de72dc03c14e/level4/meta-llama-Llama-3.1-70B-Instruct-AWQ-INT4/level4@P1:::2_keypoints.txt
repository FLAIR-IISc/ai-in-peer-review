- Proposal: project Ising model parameters onto a setting satisfying the Dobrushin criterion to speed Gibbs mixing via a constrained optimization under various divergences.  
- Novelty: combining stochastic and deterministic inference is interesting and under-explored.  
- Dual/constraints unclear: explanation for constraints (e.g., zij * Dij = 0) and why the new parameterization must lie in the same space is not justified.  
- Space question: unclear why the new parameterization cannot lie in a smaller subspace.  
- Procedure ill-defined: unclear whether projected gradient updates are run to completion before sampling or interleaved; an algorithm box is needed.  
- Convergence guarantee missing: connection to Ergodic Mirror Descent is stated but no convergence proof or guarantee is provided.  
- Experiment design issues: running Gibbs for 30,000 iterations seems arbitrary; comparisons should be time-based for fairness.  
- Missing uncertainty: charts lack error bars, so result significance is unknown.  
- Projection cost ignored: time to compute the projection is not accounted for and may negate mixing gains.  
- Missing baselines: no comparison to blocked Gibbs samplers or Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs, which are relevant.  
- Overall: promising idea but projection development, method clarity, and experiments need substantial improvement.  
- Acceptance vs rejection: could merit acceptance with clearer formulation and stronger experiments; as-is the unclear presentation and unconvincing experiments argue for rejection.
