- Proposes projecting pairwise Ising models onto nearest fast-mixing models using spectral bounds; experiments show improved marginal estimates on small toy models versus variational methods and original Gibbs.

- Novel idea but heavily reliant on prior rapid-mixing results [4,8].

- Main concern: uncertain scalability to larger models since experiments are limited to tiny Ising models and mixing times typically grow with problem size.

- The computational cost of the projection step appears omitted from time comparisons (Figure 2) and should be included.

- Unclear whether the approach generalizes beyond binary states, as establishing convergence bounds for broader domains is more challenging.

- Presentation issues: needs reorganization and clearer exposition in places; several “standard results” (e.g., Theorem 6) are referenced without proof or sketch.

- Lemma 5 is left unproven and cited reference only covers a special zero-field case.

- Dependency matrix R needs more discussion; computing the maximization for R may be intractable and this should be stated explicitly.

- Section 4 is hard to follow: symbols g, M, and Lambda are referenced before definition, and Theorem 4 uses poorly explained notation.

- Inconsistent KL divergence notation (KL(q||p) used in both directions) and reversed q/p convention relative to common literature; fix for consistency.

- Experimental suggestions: include larger models or alternatives (models with symmetries, very long sampling runs as ground truth) where junction tree is intractable.

- Suggest exploring reverse KL projection KL(θ||ψ) as a potential best-case comparison using junction tree on toy models.

- Need discussion of why mean field error differs greatly from reverse KL projection error, since mean field is a degenerate reverse KL case.

- Overall: elegant idea leveraging recent mixing bounds, but experimental comparisons and limited applicability raise concerns.
