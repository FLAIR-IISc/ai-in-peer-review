- Addresses slow-mixing MCMC for Ising models by projecting the model onto a space of guaranteed fast-mixing models using divergences that preserve graph structure.

- Proposes projection methods: Euclidean projection solved via dualization and LBFGS-B, and divergence-based projections (KL, piecewise KL, reversed KL) via projected gradient descent.

- Core idea is elegant and principled; operating on fast-mixing approximations can significantly improve inference.

- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing proposed methods to LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs, and focusing on accuracy and runtime-accuracy trade-offs.

- Results show consistent accuracy improvements over TRW, MF, and LBP; often outperform standard Gibbs within a reasonable runtime budget; reversed KL performs best.

- Missing implementation detail: stopping criterion for the inner LBFGS-B iterations in the projected gradient algorithm is not specified — needed for reproducibility and cost analysis.

- Plot/axis issue: Figure 2’s horizontal axis labeled “number of samples” is unclear (sampling used only for reversed KL); should be relabeled to runtime (seconds) and include absolute running times.

- Comparative runtime reporting: include runtime results for LBP, TRW, and MF to make runtime-accuracy trade-offs fair and interpretable.

- Scalability concern: experiments on small/random models only — recommend evaluating larger, more complex or tractable models (e.g., Flach’s complete-graph models) to assess scalability and accuracy.

- Minor edits: change “onto the tree” to “onto graph T” (line 222) and use “subgradient” instead of “derivative” in the projected gradient context (line 226).

- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on satisfactory responses about implementation details, runtime reporting, and scalability.
