- Problem addressed: slow mixing of Gibbs sampling in pairwise Ising models with strong interactions.
- Proposed method: project original parameters onto the nearest “fast-mixing” model that satisfies a spectral bound guaranteeing rapid mixing.
- Empirical claim: on small Ising models, projection yields more accurate marginal estimates under a fixed computational budget than Gibbs sampling and common variational methods.
- Novelty: using projection onto a rapidly mixing model for approximate inference appears to be a novel idea, though it builds on prior mixing-condition work [4,8].
- Reliance on prior work: technical framework depends heavily on existing rapid-mixing results, reducing the paper’s standalone technical contribution.
- Scalability concern: experiments are limited to small models, leaving unclear how performance and mixing times scale with problem size.
- Computational cost omission: the projection step appears expensive and seems omitted from experimental runtime comparisons, which is a major oversight.
- Generalization concern: paper does not address extension beyond binary variables; deriving convergence bounds for larger state spaces is likely challenging.
- Missing/unclear citations and proofs: “standard results” (e.g., Theorem 6) lack citations or proof sketches; Lemma 5 lacks a full proof and only covers a special case in the provided reference.
- Dependency matrix R issues: properties and tractability of R are insufficiently discussed—its maximization definition may be intractable and motivation for Lemma 5 should be clarified.
- Section 4 clarity problems: variables (g, M, Lambda) are introduced before definition, and Theorem 4 contains unexplained notation; optimization section needs clearer exposition.
- Notational inconsistencies: Section 1 mixes conventions for KL(q||p) and reverses standard notation by treating q as true distribution and p as approximation, risking reader confusion.
- Experimental shortcomings: time comparisons likely omit projection overhead; experiments are confined to small models where exact inference is feasible.
- Recommended additional experiments/baselines: evaluate on larger-scale problems (or symmetric models/long MCMC for ground truth), include junction-tree computed KL(θ||ψ) projection as a best-case baseline, and discuss surprising performance gap between mean field and reverse KL projection.
