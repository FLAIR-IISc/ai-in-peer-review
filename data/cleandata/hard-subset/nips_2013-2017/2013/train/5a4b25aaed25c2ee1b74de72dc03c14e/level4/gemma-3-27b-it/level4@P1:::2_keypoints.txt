- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing region (e.g., satisfying Dobrushin) to improve Gibbs mixing.  
- Formulates the projection as a constrained optimization under several distance/divergence measures.  
- Novel idea combining deterministic projection with stochastic sampling in an under-explored area.  
- Dual of the projection problem is described unclearly (e.g., role of z_ij d_ij = 0 constraints and space preservation).  
- Overall procedure is unclear: whether projection runs to completion before Gibbs sampling or is interleaved; an algorithm box would help.  
- Convergence guarantees for the projected gradient descent strategy are not discussed.  
- Experimental setup is unconvincing: choice of 30,000 Gibbs iterations is unexplained.  
- Comparisons should account for runtime (cost of projection vs. sample generation) to be equitable.  
- Plots lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance.  
- Computational cost of projection is not included in comparisons, which may negate practical gains.  
- Missing comparisons to relevant baselines such as blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs.  
- Theoretical justification for projection algorithms is provided, but spectral norm bound looseness is questionable.  
- Demonstrates improved accuracy versus standard variational methods in experiments.  
- Overall: promising and novel, but method development and experiments need refinement before acceptance.
