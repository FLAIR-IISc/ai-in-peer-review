- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin uniqueness condition to guarantee rapid Gibbs mixing.
- Leverages deterministic-inference principles to improve MCMC, a novel and under-explored idea.
- The preprocessing idea of modifying parameters to accelerate mixing is simple and elegant.
- Dual formulation is hard to follow; role of complementarity constraints (zij*dij = 0) is not well-explained and may allow sparser/altered graph structures.
- Overall algorithmic procedure is ambiguous—unclear if projection is a one-time pre-processing step or interleaved with sampling; needs clear pseudo-code.
- No discussion of convergence: properties of the optimization problem (convexity) and convergence guarantees for projected gradient descent are missing.
- Experimental comparisons are unfair: comparing to deterministic inference and using a fixed 30,000 iterations is arbitrary.
- Evaluation lacks error bars or confidence intervals, so statistical significance of results is unknown.
- Experiments ignore the computational cost of performing the projection, failing to report wall-clock time for projection plus sampling.
- Missing baseline comparisons to standard sampling-improvement methods (e.g., blocked Gibbs, lifted/advanced Gibbs techniques).
- Overall contribution is compelling but weakened by unclear technical exposition and insufficiently rigorous experiments.
- Reviewer requests clarification of the optimization formulation, a clear algorithm, and more thorough time-accounting and baseline experiments to reconsider the score.
