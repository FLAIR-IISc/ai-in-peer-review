- Contribution: Proposes projecting Ising-model parameters onto a set satisfying the Dobrushin condition to accelerate Gibbs mixing, posed as a constrained optimization under multiple divergences.
- Positives: Interesting and underexplored blend of stochastic and deterministic inference; straightforward, appealing idea.
- Clarity: Dual formulation and constraints (e.g., zij * dij = 0) are opaque — unclear how they guarantee the projected parameters remain in the original parameter space vs. a smaller one.
- Workflow: Unclear overall procedure — whether projected-gradient updates are run to convergence before Gibbs or interleaved with sampling; an algorithm box is needed.
- Convergence: No discussion/proof of whether the proposed projected gradient descent is guaranteed to converge.
- Experimental setup: Fixing Gibbs at 30K iterations is questionable; time-based comparisons are more appropriate when comparing sampling to deterministic methods.
- Statistical rigor: Plots lack error bars and uncertainty estimates, preventing assessment of significance.
- Cost accounting: Experiments omit the computational cost of computing the projection; projection time could negate sampling speedups.
- Baselines: Missing comparisons to reasonable alternatives like blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.
- Overall assessment: Promising idea but needs clearer formulation/exposition and a stronger, more careful experimental evaluation to demonstrate practical utility.
