- Paper introduces projecting arbitrary Ising model parameters onto a set guaranteed to be fast-mixing (spectral norm of absolute interaction matrix < 1) for approximate inference.  
- Core idea: redefine "tractable" models by dynamic properties (fast mixing) instead of structural ones (e.g., low treewidth).  
- Projection is explored under four divergences: Euclidean (L2), KL(q||p) ("zero-avoiding"), piecewise KL over low-treewidth subgraphs, and KL(p||q) ("zero-forcing").  
- Euclidean projection: efficient dual algorithm derived, using iterative SVD thresholding and preserving sparsity (Theorem 7).  
- KL(q||p) is theoretically sound but impractical because it requires expectations under the original slow-mixing distribution q.  
- Piecewise KL is a novel, tractable approximation computed over collections of low-treewidth subgraphs.  
- KL(p||q) is practical since it needs expectations only under the projected fast-mixing p and is optimized via stochastic projected gradient descent.  
- Experiments on grids and random graphs suggest sampling from projected parameters can give more accurate marginals than variational methods and Gibbs on original parameters under limited sampling budgets.  
- Strength: Highly original framing bridging MCMC and variational inference that could inspire further research.  
- Strength: Solid technical contribution for Euclidean projection with sparsity-preserving dual method (Theorem 7).  
- Strength: Thorough exploration of multiple divergence choices with clear discussion of trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Algorithmic clarity is lacking—practical details of stochastic KL minimization, ergodic mirror descent, and sample pool management are vague.  
- Weakness: Projection formulation needs clearer explanation of constraints like ZijBij = 0 to show preservation of graph sparsity.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is flawed—does not account for projection computational cost, so plots of accuracy vs. Gibbs samples are misleading.  
- Weakness: Missing error bars in plots despite averaging over 50 trials, preventing assessment of statistical significance.  
- Weakness: Missing key MCMC baselines (e.g., block Gibbs, Swendsen–Wang), which are essential given the paper's mixing-time motivation.  
- Summary: Conceptually strong and technically solid in parts, but current manuscript needs significant revisions—especially fair time-based experiments and clearer algorithms—before acceptance.
