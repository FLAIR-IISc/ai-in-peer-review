1. Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion to accelerate Gibbs sampling, formulated as a constrained optimization and analyzed via several distances/divergences.

2. Combining stochastic and deterministic inference is a promising and relatively unexplored direction; the work is an interesting contribution.

3. The dual formulation of the projection problem is unclearly described.

4. The justification for constraints zij * dij = 0 is unclear — they may unnecessarily restrict the parameter space.

5. The overall procedure is ambiguous: it’s unclear whether projected gradient iterations finish before Gibbs sampling or are interleaved; an algorithm box is needed.

6. Convergence guarantees for the proposed projected gradient descent are not provided.

7. Experimental setup is unpersuasive: no rationale for running Gibbs for 30,000 iterations and runtime-based comparisons to deterministic methods are missing.

8. Figures lack error bars, so statistical significance of results is unclear.

9. Computational cost of performing the projection is not accounted for and may offset mixing gains.

10. Missing comparisons to established alternatives such as blocked Gibbs samplers or Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs sampler.

11. Overall assessment: compelling idea but needs clearer methodological development and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation.
