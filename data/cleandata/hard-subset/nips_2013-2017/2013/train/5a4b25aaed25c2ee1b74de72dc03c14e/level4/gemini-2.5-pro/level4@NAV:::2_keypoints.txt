- Contribution: Proposes reparameterizing Ising models by projecting parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin condition, aiming to accelerate Gibbs sampling mixing.
- Novelty/Appeal: Combining stochastic and deterministic inference is valuable and under-explored; the idea of augmenting parameters to improve mixing is intuitive and promising.
- Projection formulation: The projection is cast as a constrained optimization intended to preserve the original model's support.
- Dual clarity: The dual formulation is unclearly presented; it's not obvious how constraints like zij * dij = 0 ensure the projected parameter space equals the original support rather than a smaller subspace.
- Algorithmic ambiguity: The overall procedure is ambiguous—unclear whether projected gradient descent is run to convergence before sampling or interleaved with sampling; a boxed formal algorithm is needed.
- Convergence guarantees: The paper does not address whether the projected gradient descent strategy is guaranteed to converge.
- Empirical setup: Running Gibbs for a fixed 30,000 iterations seems arbitrary; comparisons should use total runtime when juxtaposing sampling and deterministic methods.
- Statistical rigor: Plots lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance.
- Overhead omission: Experiments compare mixing speed but ignore the computational cost of computing the projection, which could negate sampling speedups.
- Baselines missing: The evaluation should include comparisons to other Gibbs-improvement methods (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Gogate's Lifted Gibbs) to be more convincing.
- Overall verdict: Nice central idea but requires clearer technical development and a more robust, runtime-aware experimental evaluation to demonstrate practical utility.
