- Proposes a new notion of tractability based on fast-mixing parameter conditions (spectral norm |||β|||₂ < 1) rather than structural constraints.  
- Defines fast-mixing models via a spectral norm bound and develops projection algorithms mapping arbitrary parameters into this set.  
- Implements projections under multiple divergences: Euclidean, piecewise KL, and reversed KL.  
- Euclidean projection uses a dual formulation solved with LBFGS-B; reversed KL uses Gibbs sampling on the fast-mixing target for stochastic gradient estimates.  
- Experiments on 8×8 grids and 10-node graphs show projected models outperform TRW, MF, and LBP in marginal accuracy for finite sampling budgets.  
- Reversed KL projection yields the strongest empirical results.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical foundation (e.g., Theorems 4 and 7) and discusses generalization to other MRFs (Section 7).  
- Novel contribution: shifts NIPS-era focus from tree-based tractability to parameter-space mixing guarantees; spectral-norm projection framework is original.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-structured, with precise definitions and algorithm descriptions.  
- Clarity issue: Figure 2 x-axis labeled "number of samples" is ambiguous and misrepresents sampling vs. runtime; recommend relabeling as runtime (seconds) and reporting absolute runtimes for all methods.  
- Quality issue: LBFGS-B inner-loop stopping criterion is unspecified; recommend stating convergence tolerance or max iterations.  
- Significance caveat: the spectral norm bound is conservative (e.g., β_c discrepancy for 2D Ising); recommend discussing potential tighter bounds (e.g., Lubetzky & Sly).  
- Originality caveat: piecewise KL approach lacks comparison to tractable-subgraph methods (Saul & Jordan 2002; Sutton & McCallum 2005); recommend contextualization.  
- Pro/Con summary: Pro — novel, theoretically grounded framework with strong time-constrained inference benefits; Con — ambiguous experimental metrics, unspecified optimizer details, and conservative bound limit immediate practical impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept, pending fixes to clarity (Figure 2 labeling, runtime reporting); conservative bound noted but not fatal to contribution.
