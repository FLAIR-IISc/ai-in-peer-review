- Paper tackles inference in Ising models with slow-mixing Gibbs by projecting onto fast-mixing models then performing inference on the projection.  
- Fast-mixing model class defined via a spectral-norm bound on the matrix of absolute edge weights; projection minimizes a divergence while preserving graph structure.  
- Euclidean projection solved by dualizing and using LBFGS-B; KL, piecewise KL, and reverse KL projections use a projected gradient algorithm.  
- Reverse KL projection requires Gibbs sampling, but sampling is performed on the fast-mixing projected model, not the original.  
- Experiments on small random models compare approximate marginals to ground truth and benchmark against LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs on the original model.  
- Results show the proposed methods consistently outperform TRW, MF, and LBP, and beat standard Gibbs within a reasonable runtime range; reverse KL variant performs best.  
- Reviewer finds the paper interesting with convincing empirical results.  
- Request 1: Provide stopping criteria/details for the inner LBFGS-B iterations in the projected gradient algorithm.  
- Request 2: Clarify the horizontal axis labeled "number of samples" (suggest using total runtime) and report absolute runtimes (seconds) for all algorithms to enable fair accuracy-vs-runtime comparisons.  
- Request 3: Experiments are limited to small models; include larger-scale benchmarks (e.g., Flach et al. models) to strengthen evaluation.  
- Request 4: Address concerns about practical utility due to high runtime, ideally alongside the clarified experimental plots.  
- Minor corrections: replace "onto the tree" with "onto graph T" and check if "derivative" should be "subgradient."
