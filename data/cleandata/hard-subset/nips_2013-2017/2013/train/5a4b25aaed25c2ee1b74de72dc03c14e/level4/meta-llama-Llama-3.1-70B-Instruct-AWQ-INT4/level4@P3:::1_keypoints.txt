- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a set that guarantees fast mixing to improve Gibbs sampling mixing rates.  
- Core idea: use nearest fast-mixing model under several divergences as an approximate inference method.  
- Technical analysis is sound and builds on prior rapid-mixing conditions.  
- The approach is innovative and theoretically well-motivated.  
- Experiments show regimes where projected models yield better marginal estimates for a given computation time.  
- Scalability to larger models is a major concern.  
- Projection step is computationally expensive and its cost is not included in reported timings.  
- Mixing-time comparisons across models are difficult to scale and may not generalize.  
- Paper refers to “standard results” without adequate citations or proof sketches.  
- Properties of the dependency matrix R are insufficiently discussed.  
- Second half of Section 4 is hard to follow due to new notation and unclear exposition.  
- Time comparison in Figure 2 is unclear and should include projection computation.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks experiments on larger models to demonstrate scalability.  
- Suggest investigating zero-avoiding KL as a potential best-case divergence for sampling.  
- The relationship between mean field and reverse KL projection (mean field as a degenerate case) needs discussion.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions addressing scalability, experimental completeness, timing transparency, and clarity of presentation.
