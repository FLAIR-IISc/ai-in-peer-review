- Goal: Improve Gibbs mixing in strongly interacting pairwise Ising models by projecting parameters to nearby “fast-mixing” models satisfying a spectral bound.
- Method/novelty: Proposes projecting onto the nearest rapidly-mixing model for approximate inference—a novel idea building on prior mixing bounds.
- Empirical claim: On small/tiny Ising models, the approach yields better marginal estimates per compute time than variational methods and Gibbs on the original model.
- Reliance on prior work: Technical results heavily build on earlier rapid-mixing conditions ([4,8]); the paper depends on those foundations.
- Scalability concern: Experiments are limited to small models, making extrapolation to larger problem sizes unclear given mixing-time dependence on size.
- Projection cost omission: Reported timings appear to exclude the computationally expensive projection step; this must be clarified and included.
- Generalizability concern: Unclear how the approach extends beyond binary-state models since convergence bounds are harder in more general settings.
- Missing proofs/references: Several “standard” results are presented as theorems without references or proof sketches (e.g., Theorem 6, Lemma 5).
- Dependency matrix R: Paper fails to discuss tractability of R’s construction or the maximization involved; reliance on Lemma 5 is unexplained.
- Presentation issues in Sec. 4: Symbols (g, M, Λ) are used before definition and Theorem 4 contains poorly explained notation; the section is hard to follow.
- KL notation inconsistency: KL(q||p) is used ambiguously for forward/reverse KL and q/p roles conflict with common literature conventions.
- Experimental coverage: Need experiments on larger/intractable regimes or models with symmetries (or using very long runs as ground truth) to better assess performance.
- Suggestion: Explore KL(θ||ψ) as a “best-case” projection (using junction tree on toy models) to benchmark sampling in approximate models.
- Mean-field discrepancy: The substantial error difference between mean field and the reverse-KL projection (despite mean field being a degenerate case) needs explanation.
