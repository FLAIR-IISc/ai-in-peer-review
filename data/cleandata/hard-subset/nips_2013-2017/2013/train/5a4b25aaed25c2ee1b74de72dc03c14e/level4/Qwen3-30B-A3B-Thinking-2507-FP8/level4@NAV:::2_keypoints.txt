- Introduces projection-based method to accelerate Gibbs sampling in Ising models by enforcing Dobrushin rapid-mixing criterion.
- Formulates projection as a constrained optimization problem and analyzes it under multiple distance/divergence metrics.
- Promising intersection of stochastic and deterministic inference; underexplored and compelling direction.
- Dual projection problem description is unclear, particularly how constraints z_{ij} d_{ij} = 0 ensure the new parameterization spans the same space rather than a subset.
- Procedural workflow ambiguous: unclear whether projected gradient updates are done before Gibbs sampling or interleaved; an explicit algorithm box is needed.
- No convergence guarantees provided for the proposed projected gradient descent strategy.
- Experimental setup weaknesses: choice of 30K Gibbs iterations appears arbitrary and not motivated.
- Evaluations lack error bars or measures of statistical significance.
- Computational cost of the projection step (e.g., ~1 minute) is not factored into net runtime gains, leaving practical benefit unclear.
- Missing comparisons to important baselines such as blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs sampler.
- Overall, idea is compelling but theoretical development and experimental validation need substantial strengthening.
