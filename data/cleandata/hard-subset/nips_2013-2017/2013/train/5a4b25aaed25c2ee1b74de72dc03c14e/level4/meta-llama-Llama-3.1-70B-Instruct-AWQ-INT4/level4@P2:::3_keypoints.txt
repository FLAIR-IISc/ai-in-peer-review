- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a set of fast-mixing models defined by a spectral-norm bound on absolute edge strengths, using various divergences to define the projection.
- Situates work within prior literature on mixing times and approximate inference (mean-field, loopy BP, TRW), framing the approach as a generalization of mean-field.
- Presents a dual algorithm for Euclidean-distance projection and projected-gradient algorithms for KL, piecewise KL, and reversed KL divergences.
- Provides extensive empirical comparisons against loopy BP, TRW, mean-field, and Gibbs sampling on the original model.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated approach that expands the approximating family beyond fully-factorized distributions.
- Strength: thorough theoretical development and flexible use of multiple divergences for projection.
- Strength: convincing empirical results demonstrating effectiveness of the approach.
- Weakness: spectral-norm bound may be loose and overly conservative in practice.
- Weakness: experimental choice of spectral norm 2.5 (vs. 1.0) is not fully justified.
- Weakness: experiments should include larger models and report more detailed runtime information.
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written but needs clearer notation, terminology, and exposition of some technical details.
- Recommendation: accept, but request authors address the spectral-bound justification, expand experiments, and clarify presentation; consider revision/resubmission.
