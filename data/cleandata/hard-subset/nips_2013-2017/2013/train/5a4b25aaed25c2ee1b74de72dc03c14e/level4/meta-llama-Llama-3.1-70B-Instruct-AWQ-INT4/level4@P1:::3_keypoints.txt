- Proposes projecting Ising MRFs onto a set of fast-mixing models by bounding the spectral norm of the matrix of absolute edge strengths.  
- Explores projections under several divergences: Euclidean, KL, piecewise KL, and reversed KL.  
- Derives a dual algorithm for Euclidean projection, solved with L-BFGS-B.  
- Uses a projected-gradient algorithm for the other divergences; reversed KL projection requires Gibbs sampling on the fast-mixing model.  
- Provides extensive experiments comparing the proposed methods to loopy BP, TRW, mean-field, and Gibbs sampling on the original model.  
- Empirical results show the proposed methods consistently outperform TRW, MF, and LBP in accuracy and, for reasonable runtimes, also outperform Gibbs on the original model.  
- Among the proposed methods, projection using reversed KL-divergence performs best.  
- Paper is well-written and makes a significant, convincing contribution to inference in MRFs.  
- Missing detail: stopping criteria for the inner L-BFGS-B iterations in the projected-gradient scheme (Section 5.1) are not specified.  
- Plot labeling issue: Figure 2’s horizontal axis "number of samples" is misleading since sampling is only used for reversed KL; should be "runtime of the algorithm"; also runtimes for LBP, TRW, and MF are not reported.  
- Suggests additional experiments on larger/tractable models (e.g., Flach’s complete-graph random fields) for a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Minor corrections: replace "onto the tree" with "onto graph T" (p.226) and prefer "subgradient" over "derivative" where gradient may be undefined (p.226).
