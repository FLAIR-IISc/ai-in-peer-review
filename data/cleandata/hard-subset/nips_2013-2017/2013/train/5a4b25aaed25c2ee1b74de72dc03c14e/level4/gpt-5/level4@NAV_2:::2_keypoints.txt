- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing regime (Dobrushin-style) via constrained optimization under various distances/divergences to accelerate Gibbs sampling.

- Interesting and under-explored integration of stochastic and deterministic inference ideas.

- Dual-formulation unclear: how do the zij * dij = 0 constraints ensure the projected parameters remain in the same parameter space rather than a strict subset?

- Workflow unclear: do you run projected gradient to convergence then run Gibbs, or interleave sampling and gradient updates? An algorithm box would clarify.

- Unclear whether the projected gradient descent scheme has a theoretical convergence guarantee.

- Experimental setup questionable: fixing Gibbs chain length to 30K is arbitrary; wall-clock or compute-normalized comparisons would be fairer.

- Plots lack error bars, making statistical significance and variability assessment difficult.

- Time to compute the projection is not accounted for and could negate sampling speedups (projection overhead vs samples drawn by naive Gibbs).

- Missing baseline comparisons to blocked Gibbs samplers and Gogate’s “Lifted Gibbs Sampler.”

- Overall: compelling idea but needs tightened exposition of the projection method and stronger, time-aware empirical evaluation with uncertainty quantification.
