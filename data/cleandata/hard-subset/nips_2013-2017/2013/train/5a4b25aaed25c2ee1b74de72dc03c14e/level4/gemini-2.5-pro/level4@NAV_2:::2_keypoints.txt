- Proposes a new notion of tractable distributions based on fast-mixing dynamics (Gibbs convergence), using spectral-norm < 1 for Ising models and projecting parameters into that set.  
- Develops an Euclidean projection algorithm and extends it to KL and piecewise KL divergences via projected gradient descent.  
- Novel and significant idea: defines tractability by dynamic mixing properties rather than static graph structure, combining MCMC and optimization.  
- Clarity: The dual formulation (Theorem 7) and how constraint Zij*Bij=0 enforces the same graph vs a subgraph is unclear.  
- Clarity: Description of the KL-divergence projection algorithm is hard to follow; unclear whether projection runs to completion or is interleaved with sampling—needs a formal algorithm block.  
- Theoretical/implementation gap: Claims of convergence via Ergodic Mirror Descent lack discussion of whether implementation choices (step-size schedule, MCMC steps) satisfy required conditions.  
- Experimental fairness: Comparisons should be based on total computation time, not a fixed number of Gibbs iterations.  
- Experimental reporting: Plots lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance.  
- Experimental omission: Time-accuracy plots do not account for the computational cost of the projection step, which could negate benefits.  
- Baselines: Should compare to advanced MCMC mixing-improvement methods (e.g., block Gibbs) rather than only deterministic approximations.  
- Summary: Promising and original contribution, but needs clearer exposition and a more rigorous, fair experimental evaluation.
