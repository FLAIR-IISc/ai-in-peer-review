- Proposes projecting parameters of slow-mixing Ising models into a fast-mixing set defined by the interaction matrix spectral norm < 1.  
- Main contribution: algorithms for projecting under four divergences — Euclidean, KL(original||approx), piecewise KL, and KL(approx||original).  
- KL(approx||original) is optimized via stochastic gradient descent using samples from the fast-mixing model, while KL(original||approx) requires intractable inference on the original model.  
- Experiments on small grids and random graphs show projected models give more accurate marginals than Mean Field, LBP, TRW and can outperform Gibbs on the original model under limited sampling budgets.  
- Novelty: defines a tractable approximation family by dynamic (mixing) properties rather than graphical structure.  
- Theoretical grounding: leverages established MCMC mixing-time results to justify the fast-mixing family.  
- Empirical implication: demonstrates a practical trade-off where a biased fast-mixing sampler may beat a slow but asymptotically exact sampler for fixed compute.  
- Concern: Time-accuracy comparisons appear unfair because the computational cost of the projection step (e.g., up to 30,000 Gibbs iterations) is excluded.  
- Concern: Scalability is unclear since experiments are limited to small models (e.g., 8x8 grids); behavior on larger models is unknown.  
- Concern: Method is restricted to pairwise binary Ising models; extension to higher-order or non-binary models is not addressed and may be difficult.  
- Clarify Section 4: symbols (g, M, Λ) are used before definition and Theorem 7’s notation (e.g., triple dot product) needs explanation.  
- Missing citations/proofs: Theorem 6 is uncited and Lemma 5 is presented without proof—add references or appendix sketches.  
- Explicitly state that computing the dependency matrix R is generally intractable, motivating the need for Lemma 5’s bound.  
- Clarify KL notation: consistently distinguish KL(p||q) vs. KL(q||p) and adopt p for true distribution, q for approximation.  
- Include "best-case" KL(θ||ψ) results for toy models (tractable via junction tree) as a benchmark of maximum achievable accuracy.  
- Discuss gap with Mean-Field: since Mean-Field is a degenerate reverse-KL case, explain what properties of the larger fast-mixing set yield substantially better accuracy.
