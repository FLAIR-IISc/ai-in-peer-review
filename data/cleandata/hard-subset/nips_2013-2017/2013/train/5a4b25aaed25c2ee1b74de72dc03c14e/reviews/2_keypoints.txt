- Paper proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy Dobrushin criterion for faster Gibbs mixing via a constrained optimization under various divergences.
- Combining stochastic and deterministic inference is interesting and under-explored.
- Description of the dual of the projection problem is unclear.
- Unclear how the zij * dij = 0 constraints guarantee the new parameterization spans the same space (could it be a smaller space?).
- Overall procedure is ambiguous: do you fully optimize the projection then sample, or interleave projection updates with sampling?
- An algorithm box is needed to clarify the procedure.
- Is the projected gradient descent strategy guaranteed to converge?
- Experiments are unconvincing: choice of 30K Gibbs iterations is arbitrary and iteration-based comparisons are unfair versus deterministic methods.
- No timing comparisons — projection runtime is not accounted for, so practical benefit is unclear.
- Plots lack error bars, so statistical significance of results is unknown.
- No comparisons to blocked Gibbs or Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.
- Overall: promising idea but needs clearer method development and stronger, fairer experimental evaluation.
