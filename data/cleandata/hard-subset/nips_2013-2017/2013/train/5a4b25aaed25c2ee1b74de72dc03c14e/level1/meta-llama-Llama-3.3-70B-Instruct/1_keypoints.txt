- Proposes projecting intractable distributions onto a "fast-mixing" set to guarantee rapid Gibbs convergence for high-treewidth graphical models.
- Derives a dual algorithm to solve the projection under multiple divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL).
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and an original approach.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and shows the proposed methods outperform standard variational methods and finite-time Gibbs sampling.
- Strength: novel approach targeting rapid-mixing guarantees for approximate inference.
- Strength: derivation of dual algorithms for several divergence measures.
- Strength: strong empirical results supporting effectiveness.
- Weakness: spectral norm bound is loose and may be overly conservative.
- Weakness: lacks a tighter theoretical bound on when rapid mixing occurs.
- Weakness: current method needs further work to extend to general Markov random fields.
- Recommendation: accept, but encourage addressing the looseness of the spectral bound and extending applicability/theoretical analysis.
