This paper presents an algorithm for projecting Ising model parameters onto a parameter set that is guaranteed to be fast mixing under several divergences. The authors explore the concept of "fast mixing" models, which are distributions that may be high-treewidth but have parameter-space conditions guaranteeing rapid convergence of Gibbs sampling to the stationary distribution.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough background on mixing times in Markov chains and establish a connection between the dependency matrix R and the spectral norm of the interaction strength matrix. The main contribution of the paper is the development of a dual algorithm for projecting Ising model parameters onto the fast-mixing set under the Euclidean norm.

The authors also consider other divergences, including the KL-divergence, piecewise KL-divergence, and reversed KL-divergence. They derive a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for minimizing the zero-forcing KL-divergence and demonstrate its effectiveness in experiments.

The experimental evaluation compares the proposed methods to Gibbs sampling on the original parameters and several standard variational methods. The results show that the proposed methods are more accurate than variational approximations and can provide better results than Gibbs sampling with finite time.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel approach to approximate inference in high-treewidth graphical models.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the mixing times in Markov chains and establish a connection between the dependency matrix R and the spectral norm of the interaction strength matrix.
* The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and compares the proposed methods to several standard approaches.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a specific form of the Ising model, which may not be applicable to more general Markov random fields.
* The spectral norm bound used in the paper is conservative, and a tighter bound would be more informative.
* The stochastic gradient descent algorithm for minimizing the zero-forcing KL-divergence requires careful tuning of parameters and may not be robust to different settings.
