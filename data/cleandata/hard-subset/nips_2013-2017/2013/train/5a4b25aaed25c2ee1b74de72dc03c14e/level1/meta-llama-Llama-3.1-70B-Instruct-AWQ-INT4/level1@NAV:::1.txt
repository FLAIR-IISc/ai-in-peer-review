This paper presents an algorithm to project Ising model parameters onto a parameter set that is guaranteed to be fast mixing, under several divergences. The authors aim to address the challenge of inference in high-treewidth graphical models, where exact inference is intractable. They propose a new notion of "tractability", which insists that a graph obeys parameter-space conditions ensuring that Gibbs sampling will converge rapidly to the stationary distribution.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough background on the topic, including the relevant literature on mixing times in Markov chains. The technical results are presented in a clear and concise manner, and the experiments are well-designed to evaluate the performance of the proposed methods.

* The authors provide a novel approach to address the challenge of inference in high-treewidth graphical models.
* The proposed algorithm is well-motivated and technically sound.
