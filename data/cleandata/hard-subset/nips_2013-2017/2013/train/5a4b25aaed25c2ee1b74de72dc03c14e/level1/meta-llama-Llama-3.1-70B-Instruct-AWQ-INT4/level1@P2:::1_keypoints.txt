- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing set and introduces a parameter-space notion of "tractability" for rapid Gibbs mixing.  
- Develops an algorithm to project parameters using multiple divergences and introduces a new "piecewise" divergence.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with proofs in the appendix; the method is technically sound.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, with consistent notation and reproducible experimental details.  
- Experiments show better accuracy than variational approximations and Gibbs on original parameters when interactions are strong and sampling time is limited.  
- Offers a new perspective on tractability in graphical models and addresses a difficult problem effectively.  
- Pro: Novel approach with solid theoretical and empirical support.  
- Pro: Thorough analysis and clear presentation enabling reproducibility.  
- Con: Relies on specific parameter-space conditions for rapid mixing that may not apply broadly.  
- Con: Lacks comprehensive comparisons to other approximate inference methods (e.g., loopy BP, tree-reweighted BP).  
- Con: Focus is limited to Ising models; extension to general MRFs is unclear.  
- Con: Computational complexity and runtime trade-offs versus other methods are not clearly discussed.  
- Recommendation: Accept, but require the authors to address comparisons with other methods and clarify computational complexity in the final version.
