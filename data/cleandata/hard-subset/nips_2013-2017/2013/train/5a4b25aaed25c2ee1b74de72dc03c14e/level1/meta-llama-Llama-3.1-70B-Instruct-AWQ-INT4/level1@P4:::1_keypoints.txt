- Paper proposes an algorithm to project Ising model parameters onto a parameter set guaranteed to be fast-mixing under several divergences.
- Introduces and explores the concept of "fast mixing" models with parameter-space conditions that guarantee rapid Gibbs sampling convergence even for high-treewidth distributions.
- Provides clear background on mixing times and links the dependency matrix R to the spectral norm of the interaction strength matrix.
- Main technical contribution is a dual algorithm for Euclidean-norm projection of Ising parameters onto the fast-mixing set.
- Also considers KL, piecewise KL, and reversed KL divergences and derives an SGD algorithm to minimize the zero-forcing KL-divergence.
- Experimental evaluation compares proposed methods to Gibbs sampling on original parameters and standard variational methods.
- Results show the proposed projections are more accurate than variational approximations and can outperform finite-time Gibbs sampling.
- Strength: novel approach for approximate inference in high-treewidth graphical models.
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis connecting mixing times, dependency matrix, and spectral norm bounds.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental comparison against several baseline approaches.
- Weakness: assumes a specific Ising model form, limiting applicability to more general Markov random fields.
- Weakness: spectral norm bound used is conservative; tighter bounds would be more informative.
- Weakness: SGD for zero-forcing KL requires careful tuning and may lack robustness across settings.
- Originality: novel projection-based approach and dual algorithm development for fast-mixing parameter sets.
- Significance: addresses challenging approximate inference in high-treewidth models and shows potential accuracy improvements.
- Pro-acceptance: novel method, solid theory, and comprehensive experiments support publication.
- Con-acceptance: limited model generality, conservative bounds, and potentially fragile SGD tuning weaken impact.
- Recommendations: extend approach beyond Ising models, seek tighter spectral-norm bounds, and provide more SGD tuning details.
