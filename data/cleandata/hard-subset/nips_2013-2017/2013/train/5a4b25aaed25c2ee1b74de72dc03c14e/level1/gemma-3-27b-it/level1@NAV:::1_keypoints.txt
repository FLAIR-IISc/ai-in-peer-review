- Goal: Improve slow mixing in Gibbs sampling for Ising models by projecting parameters to a space guaranteeing faster mixing using the dependency matrix spectral norm.  
- Methods: Explore projections under Euclidean, KL (zero-avoiding and reversed), and a piecewise KL approximation.  
- Novelty/Context: Builds on existing tractable approximation literature and frames fast mixing as a distinct tractability notion; proposing multiple divergences and piecewise KL is novel.  
- Theoretical grounding: Uses known results connecting the dependency matrix spectral norm to mixing time and leverages relevant theorems.  
- Strength — Motivation: Addresses an important practical bottleneck in MCMC convergence.  
- Strength — Theory: Method is grounded in appropriate Markov chain mixing-time theory.  
- Strength — Diversity of approaches: Comparing multiple divergences (including piecewise KL) yields nuanced trade-offs.  
- Strength — Empirical results: Projection often improves accuracy over LBP and naive mean-field, especially with limited sampling time.  
- Weakness — Computational cost: KL-based projections (especially zero-avoiding) are computationally expensive and evaluated only on small graphs.  
- Weakness — Loose bound: The spectral norm bound used is known to be loose, which may weaken the projection’s guarantees.  
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Reversed KL optimization via SGD requires careful tuning and sensitivity analysis is limited.  
- Weakness — Clarity: Relation to existing mean-field methods is stated but not clearly or fully elaborated.  
- For acceptance: Addresses a practical problem with solid theory, offers useful insights from multiple divergences, and shows promising empirical gains.  
- Against acceptance: Scalability concerns from computational cost, weakened guarantees from loose bounds, and limited experimental scope (small graphs).  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, acknowledge scalability and bound limitations, and address parameter-sensitivity of reversed KL.
