- Addresses intractable inference in Ising models by defining tractable models via parameter constraints that guarantee fast Gibbs mixing rather than graph structure.  
- Uses spectral-norm condition (||R||_2 < 1) and for Ising models enforces || |β| ||_2 < 1 to define the fast-mixing set Ψ.  
- Main contribution: algorithms to project arbitrary Ising parameters θ onto the fast-mixing set Ψ to find the closest ψ ∈ Ψ.  
- Explores four projection divergences: Euclidean distance, KL(θ||ψ) (“zero-avoiding”), piecewise KL (tractable approximation), and KL(ψ||θ) (“zero-forcing”).  
- Derives a dual algorithm with iterative SVD thresholding for Euclidean projection, with optional sparsity enforcement (Theorem 7).  
- Shows KL(θ||ψ) is theoretically natural but intractable because it requires expectations under the slow-mixing p(x;θ).  
- Proposes a novel piecewise KL-divergence that maximizes KL over low-treewidth subgraphs as a tractable approximation.  
- Uses stochastic projected gradient descent and Gibbs sampling for minimizing KL(ψ||θ), framing mean-field as an extreme case.  
- Empirically, sampling from projected parameters (especially via KL(ψ||θ)) yields more accurate marginals than LBP, TRW, and mean-field on grids and random graphs.  
- For limited time budgets, Gibbs sampling on projected parameters outperforms sampling on original slow-mixing parameters.  
- Quality: technically sound derivations and appropriate optimization methods, with thorough experimental comparisons.  
- Clarity: well-written, organized presentation with sufficient algorithmic details for implementation.  
- Originality: novel shift from structure-based to parameter-space tractability and introduction of piecewise KL.  
- Significance: practical approach that can improve MCMC efficiency and offers a new viewpoint connecting to mean-field.  
- Main theoretical weakness: the spectral-norm mixing bound is conservative; experiments use much looser empirical bounds (e.g., || |β| ||_2 < 2.5) lacking provable guarantees.  
- Computational concern: projection (especially KL(ψ||θ)) requires nontrivial optimization and iterative MCMC, adding overhead not fully accounted for.  
- Limited scope: methods are developed for Ising models with single-site Gibbs; extensions to general MRFs or other samplers remain nontrivial.  
- Pro acceptance: novel conceptual framework, strong empirical performance, practical relevance, and technical soundness.  
- Con acceptance: gap between theoretical guarantees and practical bounds, projection computational cost, and limited immediate applicability beyond Ising models.
