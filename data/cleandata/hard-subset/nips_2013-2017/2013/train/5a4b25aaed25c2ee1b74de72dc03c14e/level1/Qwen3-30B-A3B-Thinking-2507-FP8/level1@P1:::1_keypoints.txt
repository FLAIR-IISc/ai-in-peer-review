- Introduces projecting slow-mixing Ising parameters into a fast-mixing set to enable rapid Gibbs sampling-based inference.  
- Defines tractability by parameter-space conditions guaranteeing rapid mixing, not by graph structure.  
- Develops projection methods under four divergences, highlighting zero-forcing KL (KL(ψ||θ)) optimized via stochastic approximation as most practical.  
- Builds on prior work in mean-field, loopy BP, tree-reweighted methods, and mixing-time literature, properly citing related NIPS papers.  
- Theoretical contributions are sound; Theorem 7 gives an efficient Euclidean projection via singular value thresholding.  
- Authors acknowledge the conservativeness of the spectral-norm mixing bound and pragmatically use a looser constant (2.5) to improve approximations.  
- Experiments are thorough across graph types and interaction strengths, comparing to LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs on original parameters.  
- Empirical results show projected parameters yield more accurate marginals than variational methods when sampling time is limited.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written; background on mixing times and dependency matrices is clear and reproducible.  
- Originality: introduces "fast-mixing" as a new tractability notion and generalizes mean-field to fast-mixing approximating distributions.  
- The piecewise KL approach is novel but underperforms the true KL in experiments.  
- Limitation: focus on Ising models restricts immediate generality, noted as future work.  
- Strengths: novel concept, solid theory with practical implementation, clear experiments, and strong writing.  
- Weaknesses: conservative spectral-norm bound, underperforming piecewise KL, and limited to Ising models.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical and practical contribution addressing inference in high-treewidth models.
