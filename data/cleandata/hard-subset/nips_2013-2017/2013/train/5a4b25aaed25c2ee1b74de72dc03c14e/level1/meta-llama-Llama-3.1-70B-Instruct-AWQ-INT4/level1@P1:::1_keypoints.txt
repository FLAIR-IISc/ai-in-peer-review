- Algorithm to project Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing parameter set under several divergences.
- Targets inference in high-treewidth graphical models by enforcing parameter conditions that guarantee fast Gibbs mixing.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized with a thorough literature background on mixing times.
- Main technical contribution: a dual algorithm for projection under Euclidean, piecewise KL, and zero-forcing KL divergences.
- Provides a stochastic approximation method to project under the standard KL-divergence.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares to loopy BP, tree-reweighted BP, naive mean-field, and original Gibbs sampling.
- Results show better accuracy than variational approximations and improved performance over finite-time Gibbs sampling.
- Strength: addresses a challenging problem with strong theoretical grounding.
- Strength: algorithm is well-designed, efficient, and accompanied by a detailed analysis of properties.
- Strength: experiments are well-designed and discussed in detail.
- Weakness: relies heavily on a spectral norm bound that may be overly conservative.
- Weakness: approach may not generalize to arbitrary Markov random fields without significant technical extensions.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity, which is important in practice.
- Originality: introduces a new tractability notion based on fast mixing and a novel projection algorithm.
- Significance: potentially impactful and broadly applicable to graphical models and related ML areas.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; Rating 8/10; Confidence 8/10.
