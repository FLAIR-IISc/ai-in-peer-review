- Introduces parameter projection onto a fast-mixing set (via spectral-norm constraint) to make Gibbs sampling feasible in high-treewidth Ising models.  
- Proposes projection algorithms under Euclidean, piecewise KL, and forward/reverse KL divergences.  
- Derives an efficient dual algorithm for Euclidean projection that preserves graph sparsity (Theorem 7) and integrates projection into SGD for KL(ψ||θ).  
- Grounds fast-mixing guarantee in standard MCMC spectral-norm bounds.  
- Experiments thoroughly compare against LBP, TRW, MF, and time-limited Gibbs, showing projected-parameter sampling yields more accurate marginals under finite time budgets, especially in high-interaction regimes.  
- Strength: original, significant idea addressing MCMC convergence time with technically sound methods.  
- Weakness: spectral-norm bound is conservative; authors use an empirical bound (2.5) rather than the theoretical 1.0, weakening formal guarantees.  
- Weakness: reverse-KL stochastic optimization uses several hyperparameters with limited sensitivity analysis, reducing reproducibility.  
- Overall: compelling case for acceptance; valuable addition to approximate inference methods.
