- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a set guaranteed to be fast-mixing to improve Gibbs sampling when sampling time is limited.
- Core idea is compelling: trade approximation error for guaranteed faster convergence of MCMC.
- Builds on tractable approximation literature but reframes tractability as fast mixing—a novel and potentially fruitful perspective.
- Theorems for projection (notably Theorem 7) are well-defined and maintain graph structure; dependency matrix discussion correctly links to established mixing results.
- Experiments show consistent improvements over standard variational methods and illustrate the accuracy vs. sampling-time tradeoff.
- Spectral norm bound used to guarantee fast mixing is conservative/loose compared to known bounds (e.g., 2D Ising); needs deeper investigation or alternative criteria.
- Some divergence measures (zero-forcing KL, piecewise KL) lack intuitive/probabilistic explanation and could be better connected to prior methods.
- SGD hyperparameters in experiments (step size, pool size, iterations) appear somewhat arbitrary and should be justified.
- Original contributions include the projection framework, use of multiple divergences (piecewise KL), and reframing mean-field as a special case.
- Significance: addresses a practical problem (accelerating MCMC in Ising models); gains are modest but consistent and the approach could generalize to other graphical models.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framing, technically sound projection algorithms with guarantees, empirical improvements, clear presentation, broader applicability potential.
- Arguments against acceptance: conservative spectral bound, limited experimental gains, clarity issues on divergence explanations, limited discussion of computational complexity.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify divergence measures and tighten/discuss the spectral norm bound.
