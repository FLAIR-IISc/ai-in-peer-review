- Introduces a novel tractability criterion: project parameters onto fast-mixing sets instead of restricting graph structure.  
- For Ising models, shows a sufficient fast-mixing condition: spectral norm of the interaction matrix < 1.  
- Develops projection algorithms under multiple divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL).  
- Theorem 7 provides a technically interesting dual algorithm for Euclidean projection using singular value thresholding.  
- Builds on established mixing-time theory (e.g., Dyer, Hayes) and correctly applies it to Ising models.  
- Uses reversed KL with stochastic approximation and derives principled optimization methods.  
- Experimental evaluation compares methods to LBP, TRW, mean-field, and Gibbs on grids and random graphs across interaction strengths.  
- Empirically outperforms standard variational approaches in marginal accuracy and offers a practical fast-mixing alternative.  
- Acknowledges and quantifies the conservative spectral-norm bound (≈1.76 looser than known 2D Ising threshold).  
- Limited scope: analysis and experiments focus on Ising models, not general MRFs.  
- Missing detailed computational cost comparison, e.g., reporting "30,000 total Gibbs iterations" without baseline timing or complexity analysis.  
- Well-written, mathematically rigorous, reproducible experimental details, and overall recommended for acceptance.
