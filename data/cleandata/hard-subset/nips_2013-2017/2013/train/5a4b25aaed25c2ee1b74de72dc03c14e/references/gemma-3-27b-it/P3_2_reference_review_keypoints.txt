- Overall: Novel, technically sound approach projecting Ising model parameters to a fast-mixing set; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Core idea: Project parameters onto a set guaranteeing rapid Gibbs mixing by constraining the spectral norm of the dependency matrix.  
- Methods: Four projection divergences studied—Euclidean (SVD thresholding), KL(q||p), piecewise KL on low-treewidth subgraphs, and KL(p||q) via stochastic approximation.  
- Theoretical contribution: Solid derivation for the Euclidean projection (Theorem 7) and clear connection to mixing-time results.  
- Novelty: Fresh perspective focusing on parameter projection to enforce fast mixing rather than approximating the distribution directly.  
- Experimental evaluation: Well-designed experiments across graph topologies and interaction strengths, with comparisons to standard inference baselines.  
- Thoroughness: Good exploration of trade-offs between accuracy and computational cost across divergence choices.  
- Presentation: Clear, well-organized writing with useful background on mixing times.  
- Link to mean field: Correctly positions KL(p||q) projection as a generalization of mean-field inference.  
- Practical impact concern: Improvements over Gibbs on original parameters are often modest, limiting practical benefit beyond Euclidean projection.  
- Computational cost concern: More sophisticated divergences (piecewise KL, KL(p||q)) are computationally expensive and may not scale.  
- Spectral-norm conservatism: The bound of 1.0 is conservative; using 2.5 in experiments is arbitrary and lacks rigorous justification.  
- KL(q||p) limitation: Theoretically interesting but practically intractable, feeling disconnected from the rest of the paper.  
- Piecewise KL weakness: Piecewise KL consistently underperforms; causes (subgraph choice, approximation error) need investigation.  
- Lack of error analysis: No detailed bounds or analysis of information loss caused by projection or conditions for projection accuracy.  
- Scalability question: Need discussion of scaling to larger Ising models and potential optimizations for complex divergences.  
- Comparison to other methods: Should clarify whether approach is orthogonal or complementary to techniques like block Gibbs or tree sampling.  
- Suggested revisions: Justify spectral norm choice (possibly adaptively), analyze piecewise KL failures, provide error bounds, and address scalability.
