- Proposes a new tractable approximation family for high-treewidth Ising models: parameter settings where single-site Gibbs sampling is provably fast-mixing via spectral-norm constraints on |β|.  
- Projects a target Ising model θ onto the fast-mixing set Ψ by minimizing different divergences D(θ,ψ).  
- Implements four projection objectives: (i) Euclidean parameter projection with a dual algorithm preserving sparsity and operator-norm control (Theorem 7).  
- Considers KL(θ||ψ) (“zero-avoiding”) projection despite intractable expectations over θ.  
- Introduces a novel piecewise KL approximation computed on tractable subgraphs.  
- Optimizes KL(ψ||θ) (“zero-forcing”) via stochastic projected gradient descent using samples from fast-mixing ψ.  
- Experiments on 8×8 grids and small random graphs compare projections to LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs, using exact marginals for accuracy.  
- Conceptual novelty: reframing tractability via fast-mixing parameter sets is compelling and orthogonal to structure-based approximations.  
- Methodological contribution: convexification via reduction from B to D=|B| and a useful Euclidean projection that enforces operator-norm constraints while preserving sparsity.  
- Correct linkage to exponential-family calculus: gradient identity Covψ[f](ψ−θ) supports a practical sampler-based optimizer.  
- Piecewise KL divergence is a simple, tractable extension of piecewise likelihood ideas to divergence minimization.  
- Empirical evaluation is promising and compares against standard variational baselines.  
- Core guarantee weakened in practice: experiments use spectral bound c=2.5>1, undermining the provable fast-mixing claim.  
- Recommend reporting mixing diagnostics (autocorrelation, ESS, spectral gap proxies) and fraction of runs violating the norm bound.  
- Theorem 6 likely contains a typo (singular value clipping should be c, not c^2); correct and ensure consistency.  
- Theorem 7/optimization: objective is non-smooth at the projection boundary, so LBFGS-B may be inappropriate; subgradient handling and fuller derivation are needed.  
- Gradient derivations (Eq. 3–4) appear to omit expected factors from the 1/2||D−R||^2 term; provide more derivation detail for reproducibility.  
- The fast-mixing constraint could be tightened using tanh-based bounds, diagonal reweightings, or weighted norms to enlarge feasible regions and improve accuracy.  
- Experimental fairness issues: projection/optimization cost is omitted from timing, optimizer hyperparameter sensitivity is unexplored, and graph sizes are modest (8×8).  
- Piecewise KL uses a max over subgraphs, yielding a non-smooth objective that may overemphasize worst-case subgraphs; alternatives (averages, TRW-like combinations) should be considered.  
- Clarity/notation issues: inconsistent symbols, typos, unclear whether unary fields α are projected, and some cross-reference errors.  
- Overall quality: technically promising but needs clearer operator-norm projection derivation, non-smooth optimization handling, and adherence to theoretical guarantees in experiments.  
- Originality: high — novel framing of tractability via mixing-time constraints.  
- Significance: moderate — could influence approximate inference if theoretical and empirical issues are addressed.  
- Key suggestions: fix Theorem 6, supply rigorous Theorem 7 proof with subgradient/KKT treatment, report mixing and constraint activity, explore tighter feasible sets, include total compute time and optimizer traces, compare piecewise KL alternatives, and clarify unary treatment.  
- Acceptance pros: novel framing, useful Euclidean projection, sound KL(ψ||θ) gradient formulation, and promising empirical accuracy.  
- Acceptance cons: experiments violate main theoretical constraint without mixing evidence, theoretical derivations/notation need correction, limited scale and runtime accounting, and non-smooth optimization/convergence issues.  
- Recommendation: weak accept if derivations and experiments are strengthened as suggested; otherwise borderline. Confidence: high.
