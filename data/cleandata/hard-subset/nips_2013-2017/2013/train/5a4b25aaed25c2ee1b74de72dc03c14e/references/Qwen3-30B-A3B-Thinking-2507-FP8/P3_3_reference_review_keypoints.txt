- Paper proposes defining tractability via parameter-space conditions that guarantee fast mixing of Gibbs sampling, and projects Ising model parameters into this fast-mixing set.  
- For Ising models, spectral norm of the absolute interaction matrix < 1 guarantees rapid mixing; authors derive projection methods under several divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL).  
- Conceptual shift from structural tractability (tree-width) to parameter-space tractability, complementing prior variational methods.  
- Builds on Hayes and Dyer et al. mixing-time bounds and relates to prior approximations like planar graph methods.  
- Theorem 7: clear theoretical contribution and a dual algorithm for Euclidean projection.  
- Experiments are thorough, comparing against LBP, TRW, MF and Gibbs on various graphs and interaction strengths.  
- Empirical results show the proposed projections outperform variational approximations in marginal accuracy.  
- Method is practically relevant for producing rapidly mixing distributions when convergence diagnostics are hard.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and reproducible.  
- Main weakness: the spectral-norm bound (||R||₂ < 1) is overly conservative compared to known 2D Ising thresholds; authors use a looser factor (2.5) in practice without theoretical justification.  
- Computational cost of the projection step is not adequately analyzed or compared to Gibbs sampling on original parameters.  
- Scope is limited to Ising models; no demonstration or clear path for extending the dependency matrix bound to general MRFs.  
- Choice of "zero-forcing" KL over "zero-avoiding" KL is insufficiently justified; practical approximations for the latter are not explored.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel conceptual contribution with strong empirical results, but weakened by conservative theoretical bound and missing computational analysis.  
- Pro-accept reasons: new notion of tractability, theoretically grounded projection methods, strong empirical performance, and clear presentation.  
- Con-accept reasons: overly conservative theoretical bound, insufficient discussion of computational trade-offs, and limited generalizability beyond Ising models.  
- Recommended revisions: justify the practical looser spectral bound theoretically, provide detailed computational cost comparisons, and discuss/extensions to non-Ising MRFs.
