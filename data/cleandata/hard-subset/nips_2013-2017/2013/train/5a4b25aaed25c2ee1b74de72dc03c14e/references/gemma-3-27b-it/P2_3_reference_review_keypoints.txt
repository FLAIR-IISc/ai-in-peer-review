- Paper proposes projecting intractable Ising model parameters onto a “fast-mixing” parameter set to enable rapid Gibbs sampling and approximate inference.
- Investigates multiple projection methods: Euclidean (SVD thresholding), KL-divergence (intractable), piecewise KL on low-treewidth subgraphs, and reversed KL via stochastic approximation.
- Experiments on grid and random graphs show divergence-based projections generally yield more accurate marginals than loopy BP and mean-field, at higher computational cost.
- Theoretical foundation: provides relevant Markov chain mixing time definitions and supporting theorems.
- Writing and presentation are clear and logically structured.
- Novelty: explicitly targeting fast-mixing as an approximation criterion is a fresh and meaningful contribution.
- Multiple projection methods offer a useful comparison and trade-offs between efficiency and accuracy.
- Strong experimental evaluation includes accuracy and computational time comparisons across baselines.
- Weakness: practical looseness of the spectral norm bound (authors use 2.5 vs theoretical 1.0) weakens the strict fast-mixing guarantee.
- Weakness: computational complexity of projection methods, especially KL-based and reversed KL with SGD tuning, is insufficiently analyzed.
- Weakness: scope is limited to the Ising model and lacks explicit generalization to other graphical models.
- Weakness: piecewise KL performs worse than other approaches and lacks deeper analysis/explanation.
- Weakness: related work connections could be strengthened, particularly to spectral learning and parameter regularization literature.
- Quality score: 4/5 (technically sound but affected by spectral-bound looseness and complexity concerns).
- Clarity score: 5/5 (well-written and accessible notation).
- Originality score: 4/5 (novel framing and methods).
- Significance score: 3.5/5 (promising but limited by cost and scope).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach; strong experiments; solid theory; clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: spectral-bound looseness, computational cost, Ising-only scope, underperforming piecewise KL.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.
- Suggested revisions: examine trade-off between spectral norm bound and accuracy; provide detailed computational complexity analysis; discuss extensions to other graphical models; analyze why piecewise KL underperforms.
