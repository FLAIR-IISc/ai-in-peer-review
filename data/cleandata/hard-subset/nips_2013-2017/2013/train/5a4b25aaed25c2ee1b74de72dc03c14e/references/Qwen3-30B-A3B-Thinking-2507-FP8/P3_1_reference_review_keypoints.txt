- Novel approach: reframes tractability by enforcing parameter-space conditions for fast-mixing Gibbs rather than low-treewidth structures.
- Key theoretical result: for Ising models, spectral norm of the interaction matrix < 1 guarantees rapid mixing.
- Method: project arbitrary Ising parameters onto the fast-mixing set using four divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL, etc.).
- Theorem/Lemma strengths: Lemma 5 (spectral norm bound) and Theorem 7 (dual formulation for Euclidean projection) are sound and non-trivial.
- Practical method: dual approach for Euclidean projection is elegant; stochastic approximation for reversed KL leverages fast-mixing property.
- Experiments: thorough comparisons to LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs on grids and random graphs; projected methods show better marginal accuracy than variational baselines.
- Main weakness: spectral norm bound is overly conservative (e.g., predicts βc ≤ 0.25 vs true ~0.4407 for 2D Ising), severely limiting the projectable parameter set.
- Empirical hack: authors use threshold 2.5 instead of 1.0 without theoretical justification, undermining principled claims.
- Scalability concern: experiments limited to small graphs (8×8 grids, 10-node random graphs), so scalability to larger problems is untested.
- Computational cost unclear: paper lacks comparison of projection cost vs variational methods or running Gibbs on original parameters.
- Piecewise KL critique: piecewise KL approach is poorly justified, performs worse than true KL, and subgraph choices seem arbitrary.
- Missed opportunities: paper does not explore tighter/alternative mixing bounds (e.g., Dobrushin criterion) that might be more practical.
- Recommendation: weak accept—novel, technically sound idea with practical limitations that need addressing.
- Suggested improvements: justify spectral threshold, run larger-scale experiments, provide computational complexity comparisons, and explore tighter mixing-time bounds.
- Overall assessment: high quality and clarity, original perspective limited to Ising models, significant concept but practical impact reduced by conservative bound (scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10).
