- Introduces a novel tractability criterion: parameters guaranteeing rapid Gibbs mixing instead of low-treewidth structure.  
- Focuses on Ising models and uses spectral norm < 1 of the interaction matrix to guarantee rapid mixing.  
- Develops projection methods (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL) to map arbitrary parameters into the fast-mixing set.  
- Theoretical contributions are technically sound with clear proofs (Theorems 4, 7, 8) and a SVD-based projection (Theorem 7).  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs on grid and random graphs.  
- Major weakness: the spectral norm bound (〈1) is overly conservative and loose relative to known 2D Ising thresholds.  
- Authors use a practical bound of 2.5 without adequate justification or analysis of why it works.  
- Paper does not sufficiently explore the trade-off between the spectral norm bound and approximation quality.  
- Clarity is excellent: well-written, organized, consistent notation, and accessible to NIPS readers.  
- The dual optimization and technical details are complex but sufficiently explained for experts.  
- Originality is strong: reframing tractability via fast-mixing is novel and meaningful.  
- The piecewise KL divergence approach is creative and extends tractable-subgraph ideas.  
- Significance is moderate: promising alternative to existing methods but limited by loose theory and Ising-specific focus.  
- Practical impact is constrained because the approach isn’t yet generalized to broader MRFs.  
- Some empirical components (piecewise KL) underperform relative to expectations.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, contingent on addressing the looseness of the spectral norm bound and discussing trade-offs between theory and practical performance.
