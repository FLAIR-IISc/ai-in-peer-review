- Proposes projecting intractable Ising-model parameters onto the set of fast-mixing models (single-site Gibbs) using the spectral-norm condition ||R||2 < 1.  
- Uses dependency bound Rij ≤ tanh|βij| ≤ |βij| to relate model couplings β to the spectral condition.  
- Studies four projection objectives: Euclidean (Frobenius) parameter distance, inclusive KL KL(θ||ψ), piecewise KL over tractable subgraphs, and exclusive KL KL(ψ||θ).  
- Derives a dual algorithm for Euclidean projection enforcing sparsity and a spectral-norm constraint via iterative SVD thresholding (Theorem 7).  
- Optimizes exclusive KL by stochastic approximation using samples from ψ and projecting gradients onto the fast-mixing set.  
- Experiments on 8×8 grids and small random graphs compare against MF, LBP, TRW, and Gibbs, showing improved marginal error and favorable time-accuracy tradeoffs in many cases.  
- Builds on Dobrushin-type contraction and spectral-norm fast-mixing results (Dyer-Goldberg-Jerrum; Hayes), specialized to Ising models.  
- Connects reversed-KL gradient to exponential-family theory (Covψ[f] (ψ−θ)) and relates sampling-based optimization to Ergodic Mirror Descent.  
- Conceptual novelty: reframes tractability via rapid-mixing parameter regions rather than graph structure.  
- Technical contribution: a principled spectral-norm + sparsity Euclidean projection, useful beyond this paper.  
- Methodological breadth: evaluates multiple divergences and situates reversed KL as a continuum that includes MF as c=0.  
- Empirical signal: projections often outperform standard variational methods on small-to-moderate graphs.  
- Main concern: experiments use spectral bound c=2.5 (>1), violating the theoretical fast-mixing guarantee and undermining the core premise.  
- Authors should report achieved spectral norms and empirical mixing diagnostics (autocorrelation, Gelman-Rubin, coupling, drift/minorization) to substantiate fast mixing.  
- Tighter feasible set omission: paper uses |||β|||2 < 1 but ignores the potentially much larger valid set defined by ||tanh|β|||2 < 1.  
- Euclidean projection nonconvexity: the D=|B| constraint makes the problem nonconvex, and the implicit sign assumption sign(B)=sign(A) needs formal justification.  
- Notational and derivational clarity needed: dual derivation, notation (Λ′Z, Hadamard products), and equivalence claims (Theorem 7) are terse and unclear.  
- Likely typo in Theorem 6: singular values should be clipped at c (S′ii = min(Sii, c)), not “c 2”.  
- Piecewise KL critique: taking max_T KL(θ(T)||ψ(T)) is non-smooth, potentially overly conservative, and its relation to full-graph KL or marginal error is unclear.  
- Optimization concerns: reversed-KL is nonconvex, gradients are biased, and the projected-gradient procedure lacks convergence guarantees without standard MCMC/step-size schedules.  
- Scalability concerns: dual projection requires repeated dense SVDs and LBFGS-B solves; experiments are limited to n≤64 and runtime/SVD counts are not reported.  
- Experimental methodology issues: comparisons to Gibbs don’t control for total compute; need fair-time comparisons and plots of error vs coupling strength and post-projection norms.  
- Clarity issues: heavy notation, missing proofs (e.g., Lemma 5), and insufficient discussion of attractive vs mixed regimes and stronger baselines.  
- Questions for authors: formalize the sign-reduction argument, consider constraining ||tanh|β|||2 ≤ c, provide mixing diagnostics for c=2.5, and report average SVD counts and runtime scaling.  
- Pros summarized: novel perspective, useful projection operator, clear reversed-KL connection, and promising empirical gains.  
- Cons summarized: theoretical guarantees not upheld in experiments, nonconvexity and missing projection details, weak motivation for piecewise KL, limited scale and unfair comparisons.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject — interesting and potentially impactful but needs tighter guarantees, clearer theory/details, better diagnostics, and stronger/fairer experiments.
