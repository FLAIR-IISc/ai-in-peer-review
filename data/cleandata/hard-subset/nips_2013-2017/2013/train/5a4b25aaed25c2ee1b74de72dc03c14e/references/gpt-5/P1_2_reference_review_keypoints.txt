- Main contribution: propose projecting Ising-model parameters into a “fast-mixing” set (spectral-norm bound on dependency matrix) as an alternative tractable approximation family.
- Key sufficient condition: ensure || |β| ||2 < 1 (via Rij ≤ tanh|βij| ≤ |βij|) to guarantee rapid single-site Gibbs mixing.
- Studied projection objectives: Euclidean parameter-space projection, KL(θ||ψ) (inclusive), piecewise KL over tractable subgraphs, and KL(ψ||θ) (exclusive) optimized with samples from ψ.
- Euclidean projection: dual formulation (Theorem 7) yields an algorithm enforcing spectral-norm constraint on |ψ| while preserving sparsity via iterative SVD thresholding within LBFGS-B.
- KL(ψ||θ) gradient: correctly derived as ∇ψ KL(ψ||θ) = Covψ[f](ψ−θ) and implemented with a sampled “pool” and projected gradients, noting bias if same samples reused.
- Piecewise KL: novel max-over-subgraph KL objective using tractable subgraphs (trees/low-treewidth) as a compromise between tractability and fidelity.
- Experiments: 8×8 grids and 10-node random graphs show improved marginal accuracy over MF/LBP/TRW and better time–accuracy tradeoffs vs. Gibbs under finite budgets.
- Major caveat: experiments relax the spectral bound to 2.5 (vs. the theoretical <1), undermining the “guaranteed fast mixing” claim.
- Technical soundness: spectral-norm mixing conditions and many derivations are plausible, but Lemma 5 (general non-zero field) is only sketched and needs a full proof or citation.
- Theorem 7 and SVD-thresholding projection appear reasonable, but full derivations and convexity details are deferred/missing.
- Sampling-based KL(ψ||θ) estimator analysis is correct about unbiasedness only when using separate sample sets.
- Experimental methodology ambiguity: unclear whether marginals are estimated from samples or computed exactly via junction tree; ground truth and marginal computation need clarification.
- Time comparisons lack clarity on whether projection costs (repeated SVDs) are included in reported runtimes; SVD cost may scale as O(n^3) and threatens scalability.
- Notational clarity issues: role of D=|B| and Z in Eq.(1), meaning of Λ′Z and triple dot product, and whether Theorem 7 preserves sparsity need explicit explanation.
- Originality: novel idea of projecting into a fast-mixing set and the dual Euclidean projection respecting sparsity are conceptually distinct and original.
- Significance: could influence MCMC-based learning by constraining to fast-mixing regions, but current validation is limited to small graphs.
- Scalability concern: repeated SVDs and loose spectral-norm criterion near criticality raise doubts about applicability to large graphs.
- Pros: principled link between mixing-time theory and inference, concrete projection algorithm, practical stochastic KL routine, and empirical gains on tested settings.
- Cons: central guarantee not upheld experimentally, scalability and computational cost underexplored, piecewise KL is ad hoc/nonsmooth, and some proofs/citations missing.
- Suggested fixes: either enforce || |β| ||2<1 in experiments or provide mixing diagnostics/justifications for >1; clarify evaluation protocol and include projection time; ablate subgraph choices or smooth the piecewise KL; add proofs and discuss scalable SVD/alternative norms.
- Recommendation: weak reject due to unfulfilled guarantees, unclear scalability, and needed technical clarifications, but promising and could be strong with stronger empirical/theoretical support.
