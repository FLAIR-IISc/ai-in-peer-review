- Introduces a novel tractability notion based on fast mixing and projects arbitrary Ising model parameters onto the fast-mixing set using four divergence measures.  
- Theorem 7 provides an efficient Euclidean projection algorithm via singular value thresholding.  
- Extends projection to probabilistic divergences (piecewise KL, zero-forcing KL), with zero-forcing KL showing particular promise.  
- Empirical results on 8×8 grids and random graphs show projected models outperform LBP, TRW, and mean-field in marginal accuracy under time constraints.  
- Frames fast mixing as a constraint on parameter space, a novel perspective building on existing mixing-time theory (Theorem 4).  
- Experimental design is comprehensive across graph densities, interaction strengths, and interaction types.  
- Paper is technically rigorous and transparent about limitations, enhancing credibility.  
- Clarity issues: the link between the dependency matrix R and the spectral norm condition needs more intuitive explanation.  
- Practical choice of spectral norm bound (using 2.5 instead of 1.0) is mentioned but not deeply justified.  
- Theoretical mixing bound is conservative (e.g., β < 0.25 vs known β < 0.4407 for 2D Ising), limiting practical tightness.  
- Scope is currently restricted to Ising models and not demonstrated on larger or more general MRFs.  
- Promising implications for learning and time-constrained inference, but generalization requires further work.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, and practically useful contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept.
