- Proposal: define a tractable family of distributions whose parameters guarantee fast-mixing single-site Gibbs, using spectral-norm bounds on the absolute interaction matrix for Ising models.
- Methods: Euclidean projection onto an operator-norm-constrained sparse set via a dual SVD-thresholding procedure (Theorem 7, implemented with LBFGS-B).
- Methods: KL(θ||ψ) projection is theoretically attractive but intractable for high-treewidth targets because it requires expectations under θ.
- Methods: Piecewise KL approximation projects onto tractable subgraphs and minimizes maxT KL(θ(T)||ψ(T)).
- Methods: KL(ψ||θ) projection is optimized with projected stochastic gradients (expectations under ψ), with gradient form Covψ[f,f](ψ−θ) and sampling from the fast-mixing ψ.
- Experiments: on 8×8 grids and small random graphs, projected fast-mixing models—especially KL(ψ||θ)—outperform MF/LBP/TRW and finite-time Gibbs on original parameters in marginal accuracy.
- Relation to prior work: connects mixing-time theory (Dyer-Goldberg-Jerrum, Hayes) to variational inference and contrasts with MF, structured MF, TRW, BP, and tractable-substructure approaches.
- Strength: clear framing of fast-mixing as a tractable family bridging MCMC guarantees and variational design.
- Strength: technically interesting dual projection that preserves sparsity via SVD-thresholding.
- Strength: using KL(ψ||θ) with projected stochastic approximation generalizes MF while leveraging guaranteed mixing for sampling-based gradients.
- Strength: empirical gains over standard variational baselines and informative time–accuracy comparisons to Gibbs.
- Weakness: experiments use a looser spectral norm bound (2.5 > 1), undermining the main theoretical fast-mixing guarantee and requiring empirical mixing diagnostics.
- Weakness: inconsistent evaluation—unclear whether marginals are computed by exact junction-tree or estimated from Gibbs; need consistent ground truth and estimators across methods.
- Weakness: computational budget not fairly accounted for—projection/training cost (SVDs, Gibbs during optimization) is omitted from comparisons.
- Weakness: technical/notation issues in Euclidean projection (Theorem 6 typo, Hadamard product notation, Theorem 7 objective typo) and lack of discussion on non-smoothness and LBFGS-B convergence.
- Weakness: gradient estimation for KL(ψ||θ) should use covariance-form estimator to reduce variance/bias and requires clearer step-size/convergence discussion.
- Weakness: piecewise KL choice (max over pieces) is conservative and underperforms; alternative weightings or sums should be analyzed.
- Weakness: limited scale—experiments on small graphs only and no discussion of SVD/projection scaling for larger n.
- Weakness: missing discussion of tighter or alternative mixing bounds (correlation decay, Weitz-style, row-sum bounds, block updates) that may be less conservative.
- Quality: core ideas are sound but typos, deferred proofs, and experimental relaxation of constraints weaken rigor.
- Clarity: generally well written but needs notational fixes, pseudocode for projected SGD, and explicit convergence/complexity statements.
- Originality: novel idea of projecting onto a fast-mixing set and the dual projection preserving sparsity; insightful connection between constrained KL(ψ||θ) and MF.
- Recommendation: borderline accept—promising and novel but needs clearer experimental validation of guarantees, fairness in computational accounting, scalability analysis, and technical fixes.
