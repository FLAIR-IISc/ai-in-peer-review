- Proposes reframing tractability for high-treewidth graphical models by projecting parameters into a "fast-mixing" set rather than restricting graph structure.  
- Identifies a sufficient fast-mixing condition for Ising models: spectral norm of the interaction matrix < 1, and develops projection methods under several divergences.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves the fast-mixing condition (Theorem 4) and derives a dual algorithm for Euclidean projection (Theorem 7).  
- Experimental validation: compares against strong baselines (LBP, TRW, MF, Gibbs) on grids and random graphs, showing improved performance, especially for reversed KL projection.  
- Practical relevance: offers a new trade-off between accuracy and mixing time that addresses inference in high-treewidth models.  
- Clear presentation: well-organized paper with precise definitions and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Conservative spectral bound: the spectral norm ≤1 condition is acknowledged as conservative (vs. known βc ≈ 0.4407), and using larger bounds (e.g., 2.5) undermines theoretical guarantees.  
- Incomplete evaluation: the piecewise KL approach is introduced but not quantitatively evaluated, leaving its utility unclear.  
- Limited scalability discussion: experiments are on small graphs and the practical runtime/complexity of the projection algorithm is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weak relation to prior work: insufficient differentiation from existing fast-mixing approximations (e.g., work on planar graphs) weakens the novelty claim.  
- Limited practical guidance: paper lacks clear recommendations on choosing between Euclidean, reversed KL, and piecewise KL projections and their cost–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Overall assessment: original and valuable contribution with sound theory and promising experiments, but needs tighter bounds, fuller evaluation, and clearer practical guidance.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the spectral bound, evaluate piecewise KL more fully, and discuss scalability and trade-offs.
