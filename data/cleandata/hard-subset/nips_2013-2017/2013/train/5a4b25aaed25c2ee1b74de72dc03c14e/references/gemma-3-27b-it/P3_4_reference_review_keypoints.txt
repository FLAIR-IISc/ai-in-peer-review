- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing set so Gibbs sampling has guaranteed convergence instead of approximating distributions.  
- Investigates multiple divergence measures for projection: Euclidean (SVD thresholding), KL (direct and piecewise approximations), and reversed KL, with computational analyses.  
- Grounded in Markov chain mixing time theory with clear definitions and supporting theorems/lemmas.  
- Frames approach as a generalization of mean-field, expanding the set of approximations beyond fully-factorized forms.  
- Acknowledges and situates work relative to prior approximate inference and mixing-time literature.  
- Novel idea: manipulate parameters to ensure fast mixing rather than solely finding tractable distributions.  
- Thoughtful exploration of alternative divergences and practical algorithms (e.g., SGD for KL) to make projection tractable.  
- Experimental validation compares projected methods to LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs, showing promising trade-offs between accuracy and speed.  
- Main weakness: the spectral norm bound of 1.0 for fast mixing is conservative; known critical value (~0.44 for 2D Ising) undermines practicality.  
- Experiments are small-scale (8x8 grids, 10-node random graphs), leaving scalability to larger networks untested.  
- SGD for zero-forcing KL is sensitive to hyperparameters; more ablation studies are needed.  
- Piecewise KL subgraph choices (horizontal/vertical chains) seem arbitrary; a more systematic subgraph selection could help.  
- Reversed KL lacks strong theoretical justification and may be inferior to standard KL; motivation for including it is weak.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach, multiple divergences explored, practical algorithms proposed, and promising initial results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: conservative spectral norm limit, limited experimental scope, and weak justification for reversed KL.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with important weaknesses; novelty and theory outweigh concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: tighten spectral norm bounds, expand experiments to larger networks, perform detailed SGD parameter ablations, and better justify reversed KL inclusion.
