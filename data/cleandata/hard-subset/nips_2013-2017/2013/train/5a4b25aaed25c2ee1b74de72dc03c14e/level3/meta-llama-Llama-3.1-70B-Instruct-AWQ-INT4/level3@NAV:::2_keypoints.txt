- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a set guaranteeing fast mixing to accelerate Gibbs sampling, with projection under multiple divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL).  
- Well-motivated and clearly explains inference challenges in high-treewidth graphical models.  
- Novel formulation and thorough theoretical analysis, including relationship to the Dobrushin criterion.  
- Description of the dual of the projection problem is unclear.  
- Unclear explanation of how zij * dij = 0 constraints ensure the new parameterization spans the same space.  
- Overall procedure is ambiguous: not stated whether projection is fully optimized before sampling or interleaved with sampling.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and choices (e.g., 30K Gibbs iterations) appear arbitrary.  
- Iteration-based comparisons are unfair against deterministic methods, and plots lack error bars for statistical assessment.  
- Missing comparisons to relevant baselines such as blocked Gibbs and Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.  
- Originality: novel specific formulation and analysis but builds on existing projection/fast-mixing ideas.  
- Significance: potential to improve Gibbs efficiency in high-treewidth models, but limited by the sparse experimental validation.  
- Recommended fixes: clarify dual and zij*dij constraints, clarify optimization vs sampling workflow, expand experiments with proper baselines and error bars, and explain real-world applicability.
