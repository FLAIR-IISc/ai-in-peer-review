- Proposal: project slow-mixing Ising parameters θ to nearby ψ that satisfy a spectral-norm condition guaranteeing (or heuristically encouraging) rapid single-site Gibbs mixing.  
- Technical basis: use dependency bound Rij ≤ tanh|βij| and sufficient condition ||R||2 < 1 for rapid mixing.  
- Algorithms: derive Euclidean projection via singular-value clipping with sparsity mask, piecewise KL projection, theoretical inclusive KL, and stochastic reverse-KL projection using samples and Euclidean projection steps.  
- Empirical claim: on small grids/random graphs, projected models often give more accurate finite-time marginals than LBP, TRW, MF, and short-run Gibbs in strong-coupling regimes.  
- Soundness positive: use of Dyer–Goldberg–Jerrum/Hayes conditions and Rij ≤ tanh|βij| is appropriate for Ising.  
- Soundness concern: paper uses a spectral bound of 2.5 in experiments, far above the theoretical threshold <1, undermining the “guaranteed fast-mixing” claim and making the guarantee heuristic in practice.  
- Missing/unclear theory: Theorem 6 (operator-norm projection) treated as standard but needs precise citation; Lemma 5 lacks proof sketch/citation for non-zero-field cases.  
- Notational/definition gap: dependency matrix R defined as max over boundary configurations but the paper operates on the upper bound R ≤ tanh(|β|); this distinction and tractability implications should be stated up front.  
- Gradient bias: the reverse-KL gradient estimator reuses the same samples to estimate μ and the outer expectation, introducing bias not acknowledged or corrected.  
- Clarity issues: Section 4 (optimization, role of Z, dual variables Λ,M) is hard to follow and needs clearer derivation, intuition, consistent notation, and an algorithm box.  
- KL notation inconsistent: p/q roles flip in places; enforce a consistent KL convention throughout.  
- Missing references/recaps: add citation for operator-norm projection, proof sketch for Lemma 5 with fields, and a brief recap of mixing-time bounds for systematic vs random scan.  
- Symmetry constraint: paper should state whether β is enforced symmetric/zero-diagonal and how projection maintains symmetry.  
- Originality: projecting to a fast-mixing family is novel and complements existing tractable-family approximations; reverse-KL generalizes MF.  
- Significance limited by scope: promising if scalable, but current experiments are small and omit projection cost from timing comparisons.  
- Experimental issues: projection time excluded from runtimes; use of loose spectral bound contradicts theory and needs mixing diagnostics; experiments should include larger graphs and tractable-ground-truth comparisons.  
- Additional experiments requested: ablate performance vs spectral bound value; try inclusive KL on toy graphs via exact marginals; compare to block/tree samplers.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant idea linking mixing-time theory to inference, novel projection formulations, and empirical gains in challenging regimes.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on loose sufficient condition, exposition and citation gaps, limited scalability/fairness in comparisons, unclear generalization beyond binary Ising.  
- Recommendation: weak reject pending stronger empirical validation (including total runtime and larger models), transparent handling of theoretical guarantees, and improved exposition.
