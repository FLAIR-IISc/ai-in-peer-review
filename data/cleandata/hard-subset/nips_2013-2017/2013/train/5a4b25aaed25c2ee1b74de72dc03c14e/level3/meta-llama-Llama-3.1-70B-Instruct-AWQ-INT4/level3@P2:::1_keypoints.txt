- Proposes projecting Ising-model parameters onto a nearby "fast-mixing" model satisfying spectral bounds to speed up Gibbs mixing.  
- Introduces several projection methods that find parameters close to the original but weak enough to ensure rapid mixing.  
- Experiments on small Ising models show projected models can give better marginal estimates within fixed computation time than variational methods and original-model Gibbs.  
- Builds heavily on prior rapid-mixing results [4,8]; using projection as an inference tool appears novel.  
- Technical development is sound and the projection methods are clearly related to existing work.  
- Major concern: unclear whether accuracy gains and mixing-time improvements will generalize to larger models.  
- Computational cost of the projection step is high and not included in timing comparisons, biasing results in favor of the proposed method.  
- Needs more discussion on extending the approach beyond binary variables, since convergence bounds are harder for non-binary cases.  
- Presentation clarity issues: several "standard results" lack references or proof sketches (e.g., Theorem 6, Lemma 5 missing/partial proof).  
- Notation problems in Section 4; introduction of g, M, and Lambda requires reorganization and clearer explanation.  
- Dependency matrix R needs deeper discussion; maximizing it raises tractability concerns that are not addressed.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small models; authors should include larger or more realistic models or tractable alternatives/ground truth.  
- Missing exploration of KL(θ||ψ) projection as a "best case"; large error gap versus reverse-KL projection is unexplained.  
- Originality: elegant, novel idea leveraging recent mixing bounds.  
- Significance: potential for impact on inference algorithms but limited by scarce experiments and unclear scalability.  
- Overall recommendation: technically promising but requires a major revision to address scalability, computational-cost accounting, experimental breadth, and clarity issues.
