- Introduces projecting Ising model parameters onto a "fast-mixing" set (Dobrushin criterion ||R||₂ < 1) to guarantee rapid Gibbs sampling convergence.
- Shifts tractability focus from graph structure to parameter constraints, complementing variational methods like mean-field and LBP.
- Explores multiple divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, zero-forcing KL) to trade off approximation quality and computational feasibility.
- Experiments show projected parameters yield more accurate marginals than some variational methods under limited sampling time.
- The dual formulation in Theorem 7 is poorly explained and the constraint zijDij = 0 is ambiguous regarding dimensionality and parameter space preservation.
- Lacks an algorithm box clarifying whether projection is done before sampling or interleaved with sampling.
- Stochastic optimization approach lacks convergence guarantees.
- Using 30,000 Gibbs iterations for projection is arbitrary and makes timing comparisons with original-parameter Gibbs sampling unfair.
- The spectral norm bound (||R||₂ < 1) is overly conservative for 2D Ising models (paper uses β=0.25 vs known βc≈0.4407).
- Experiments lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance.
- Benchmarks omit advanced samplers (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Gogate's Lifted Gibbs), reducing the meaningfulness of comparisons.
- Practical benefit is unclear without runtime/timing data.
- While the core idea is novel and conceptually valuable, experimental evaluation does not convincingly demonstrate superiority over existing methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel fast-mixing perspective, principled trade-off between mixing time and approximation quality, and connection to mean-field.
- Arguments against acceptance: methodological ambiguities, unconvincing experiments, arbitrary choices, and failure to address looseness of spectral bound.
- Overall assessment: promising and potentially impactful idea but significant clarity and experimental validation issues.
- Recommendation: Major revision; Rating 4 (Weak Accept).
