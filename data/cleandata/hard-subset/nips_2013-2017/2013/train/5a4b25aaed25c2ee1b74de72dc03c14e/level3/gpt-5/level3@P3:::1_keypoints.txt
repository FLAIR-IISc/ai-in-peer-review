- Summary: Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a spectral-norm–bounded “fast-mixing” set for Gibbs sampling, with several projection methods and empirical gains on small graphs.
- Novelty: Framing the fast-mixing set as the tractable family and projecting onto it is a novel and elegant idea, though components (spectral bounds, SVD thresholding) are classical.
- Theory gap: Several technical results (Lemma 5, Theorem 6, Theorem 7) lack proofs, precise citations, or intuition; Section 4 needs clearer definitions and dual-variable explanation.
- Dual intuition: The Euclidean-projection dual (M, Λ), operator Πc[·], and triple-dot product require explanation and assumptions to ensure strong duality and convergence.
- Guarantees vs practice: Empirically using spectral bound c=2.5 (>1) breaks the proven rapid-mixing guarantee (||R||2<1); authors must justify c>1 theoretically or restrict main results to c≤1.
- Time accounting: Projection is computationally heavy (LBFGS-B, repeated SVDs); projection time was excluded from comparisons, invalidating time-accuracy claims.
- Complexity reporting: Need detailed per-iteration complexity (SVD/power-method cost, LBFGS-B steps), memory usage, and scalability discussion.
- Dependency matrix tractability: R is defined via a max over configurations; the paper should explicitly state the tanh|βij| upper bound, discuss tightness, and address non-binary-variable cases where bounds are weaker.
- Experiments scale: Results are limited to small graphs (8×8, 10-node ER); need larger grids (16×16, 32×32) and models with tractable ground truth to assess scalability.
- Ground truth: Recommend using planar zero-field Ising, very long chains, or coupling-from-the-past (CFTP) to establish true marginals and validate sampling.
- Inclusive-KL benchmark: Include best-case inclusive-KL (via junction tree on toy models) to bound achievable error and analyze why reverse-KL projection outperforms MF.
- Ablations: Provide ablations over spectral bound c, projection effort vs inference time, and how much gains come from allowing weak edges vs sampling improvements.
- Generality: Method is tailored to binary Ising; discuss required R bounds or alternatives for multi-state MRFs and feasibility of analogous projections.
- Clarity and notation: Section 4 is hard to follow; define all symbols (g, Πc, Z, triple-dot), fix KL notation and θ/ψ consistency, and clean theorem numbering/cross-references.
- Missing references: Several “standard results” are used without citation; provide sources (e.g., matrix analysis) or proof sketches in appendix.
- Pros: Elegant novel framing, sensible use of spectral mixing bounds, and promising empirical regimes where projected models beat variational methods and original Gibbs under time limits.
- Cons: Timing comparisons exclude projection cost; guarantees are relaxed in practice; experiments are small with no ground-truth checks; clarity and citation issues; unclear extension beyond binary Ising.
- Suggested fixes: Report full wall-clock including projection, add larger-model experiments and ground-truth baselines, run inclusive-KL on toy models, add complexity/memory analysis, clarify dependency bounds, and improve Section 4 presentation and citations.
- Significance: Potentially influential if validated at scale with fair timing and clearer theory, but current methodology and evidence leave practical impact uncertain.
- Recommendation: Appealing idea but needs substantial revision on theory, experiments, timing, and clarity; reviewer leans toward rejection in current form.
