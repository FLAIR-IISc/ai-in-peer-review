- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a “fast-mixing” set to guarantee rapid Gibbs-sampling convergence, using a novel parameter-space notion of tractability.  
- Theoretical framework and algorithmic developments are clear and technically sound.  
- Derives mixing-time bounds for Ising models and a Euclidean projection algorithm; extends approach to a piecewise divergence and reversed KL.  
- Experiments compare to standard variational methods and Gibbs on original parameters; methods are more accurate and can outperform finite-time Gibbs.  
- Strengths: novel parameter-space tractability, well-organized theory, convincing experiments, original contribution to approximate inference.  
- Weaknesses: spectral norm bound is conservative; approach may have high runtime and limited scalability to larger models.  
- Suggestions: include experiments on larger models, pursue tighter spectral-norm bounds for mixing, and clarify practical runtime utility.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address suggested improvements.
