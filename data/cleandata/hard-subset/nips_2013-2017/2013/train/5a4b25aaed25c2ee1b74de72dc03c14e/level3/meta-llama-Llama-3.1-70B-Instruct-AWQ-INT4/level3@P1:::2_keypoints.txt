- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a set that guarantees fast-mixing Gibbs sampling (Dobrushin criterion).  
- Explores multiple divergences for projection: Euclidean, KL-divergence, and piecewise KL-divergence.  
- Well-motivated overview of inference challenges in high-treewidth graphical models.  
- Combines stochastic and deterministic inference in a promising, under-explored direction.  
- Main idea: find parameters close to the original that satisfy fast-mixing conditions.  
- Description of the dual of the projection problem is unclear.  
- Constraints zij * dij = 0 are not well-explained and their span implications are unclear.  
- Overall procedure is ambiguous: unclear whether projection is fully optimized before sampling or interleaved with sampling.  
- Experimental evaluation is unconvincing and uses an arbitrary 30,000 Gibbs iterations.  
- Iteration-based comparisons are unfair against deterministic methods.  
- No timing comparisons and projection runtime is not accounted for, obscuring practical benefit.  
- Plots lack error bars, undermining assessment of statistical significance.  
- No comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs).  
- Paper is technically sound and theoretical claims are reasonably supported.  
- Clarity and implementation detail need improvement; authors should provide more algorithmic detail.  
- Recommendation: revise to clarify dual/constraints, provide an algorithm box, include timing and error bars, and compare to other methods.  
- With those revisions the approach has potential to make a significant contribution.
