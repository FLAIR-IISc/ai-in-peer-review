- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a set satisfying the Dobrushin criterion to guarantee fast mixing, via constrained optimization under multiple divergences (Euclidean, KL, piecewise KL).
- Uses iterative thresholding of the parameter matrix SVD to enforce fast-mixing constraints; develops a stochastic approximation for KL minimization.
- Strength: combines stochastic and deterministic inference (uses a sample pool to estimate gradients then projects in Euclidean norm), an under-explored hybrid approach.
- Weakness: derivation and description of the dual of the projection problem are unclear and need more detail.
- Weakness: unclear whether constraints zij * dij = 0 preserve the original parameter space span or shrink it; needs clarification.
- Weakness: overall procedure is ambiguous—unclear if projection is fully optimized before sampling or interleaved; an algorithm box is needed.
- Weakness: convergence properties of the projected gradient descent strategy are not discussed or guaranteed.
- Experimental concern: choice of 30K Gibbs iterations is arbitrary and iteration-based comparisons are unfair versus deterministic methods.
- Experimental concern: no timing or projection runtime reported, so practical speedup/overhead is unknown.
- Experimental concern: plots lack error bars, so statistical significance of results is unclear.
- Missing baselines: no comparisons to relevant methods like blocked Gibbs or Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler.
- Quality assessment: technically sound but claims lack theoretical support and experimental backing; evaluation is not thorough.
- Clarity assessment: generally well-written but specific parts (dual, algorithmic procedure) are unclear and need an algorithm box.
- Originality/significance: idea is novel but insufficiently distinguished from prior work and not shown to advance the state of the art convincingly.
