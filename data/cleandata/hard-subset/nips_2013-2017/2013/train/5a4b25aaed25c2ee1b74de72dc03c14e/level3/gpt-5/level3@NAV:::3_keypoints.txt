- Proposes projecting Ising MRFs onto fast-mixing models defined by a spectral-norm bound on absolute edge strengths while preserving sparsity.  
- Defines projections under multiple divergences: Euclidean, KL(θ||ψ), piecewise KL on tractable subgraphs, and reverse KL KL(ψ||θ).  
- Solves Euclidean projection via a dual formulation with sparsity constraints using L-BFGS-B; other divergences use projected-gradient with Euclidean proximal steps.  
- Reverse-KL projection nests Gibbs sampling on the projected (guaranteed fast-mixing) model.  
- Experiments on small grids and random graphs compare marginal errors to exact marginals and report accuracy–time tradeoffs versus LBP, TRW, mean-field, and Gibbs.  
- Results consistently favor the proposed projections—especially reverse KL—over TRW/MF/LBP and improve on original-parameter Gibbs at comparable runtimes.  
- Relates to variational approximations and mixing-time criteria; novelty is using “fast mixing” as the tractable family and explicitly projecting onto it while preserving graph structure.  
- Technical development is sound: clear dual for Euclidean projection and reasonable algorithms for other divergences.  
- Empirical claims are supported on small models, but runtime accounting and scalability need clarification.  
- Clarity is generally good with solid background on mixing bounds; some algorithmic details and figure axes need clarification.  
- Originality: novel idea to project onto fast-mixing sets under multiple divergences with graph-structure preservation and a workable reverse-KL scheme.  
- Significance: promising bridge between MCMC mixing theory and approximate inference; practical impact depends on scalability and runtime.

- Cons: runtime/overhead unclear, conservative mixing bound, limited scale, hyperparameter sensitivity for reverse KL.  
- Overall: interesting and empirically convincing, but needs clearer runtimes and scalability analysis to strengthen practical utility.
