The paper proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a "fast-mixing" set via the Dobrushin criterion to accelerate Gibbs sampling — a novel and potentially useful idea.

Authors explore multiple divergences for projection (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL) and combine stochastic and deterministic inference approaches.

Empirical claim: projected parameters give better sampling accuracy than LBP, TRW, and MF within limited sampling time.

Strength: conceptual contribution in defining "fast-mixing" as a tractability criterion.

Strength: investigation of combining reversed-KL (stochastic) and Euclidean/piecewise (deterministic) projections.

Theorem 7 dual formulation is poorly explained, making technical contribution unclear.

Constraint zij*dij=0 raises concerns that the projected space may be a strict subset (smaller subspace) of the original parameter space.

Experimental procedure is ambiguous — unclear whether projection is done once before sampling or interleaved with sampling; no algorithm box provided for replication.

Projected gradient descent convergence is not discussed or guaranteed.

Unfair comparison: authors use 30K Gibbs iterations for projection while deterministic baselines get none, biasing iteration-based comparisons.

No timing/runtime comparisons are provided, so total computational cost is unaccounted for.

Experiments lack error bars and comparisons to stronger samplers (blocked Gibbs, Gogate's lifted sampler).

Conclusion: idea is promising but needs clearer methodology, rigorous convergence guarantees, and fairer, runtime-aware experimental evaluation before being a substantial contribution.
