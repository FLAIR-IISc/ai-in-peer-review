- Core idea: projecting Ising model parameters to guarantee faster Gibbs mixing by combining deterministic projection with stochastic Gibbs sampling—a promising, under-explored direction.  
- Theoretical contributions: Theorems 6 and 7 on Euclidean projection are significant contributions to projection-based optimization.  
- Dual formulation unclear: Explanation and practical implementation of the dual formulation (Theorem 7) need clearer exposition.  
- Good contextualization: Strong overview of inference challenges, Dobrushin criterion, spectral norm bounds, and connection to mean-field inference.  
- Ambiguous procedure: Unclear whether projection is fully optimized before sampling or interleaved; an explicit algorithm box is needed.  
- Convergence justification lacking: Applicability and stability of projected gradient descent / Ergodic Mirror Descent are not adequately justified.  
- Experimental design issues: Using a fixed 30,000 Gibbs iterations is arbitrary and may obscure benefits of faster mixing.  
- Runtime not reported: The computational cost of the projection step is not measured, so practical utility of faster mixing is unknown.  
- Statistical significance missing: Plots lack error bars, preventing assessment of significance for observed improvements.  
- Limited baselines: No comparisons to more advanced sampling methods (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Lifted Gibbs), limiting evaluation of effectiveness.  
- Spectral norm conservatism: The spectral norm bound appears conservative and may need refinement relative to known Ising critical values.  
- Strengths summary: Novel approach, solid theoretical results, and good placement within approximate inference literature.  
- Weaknesses summary: Clarity issues, procedural ambiguity, insufficient experiments, arbitrary parameters, and weak convergence guarantees.  
- Recommendation: Interesting and theoretically solid but currently insufficiently clear and experimentally validated; reviewer recommends rejection with encouragement to resubmit after addressing issues.
