- Proposes a novel method: project pairwise interactions onto a "fast-mixing" Ising model to improve Gibbs sampling mixing rates.  
- Builds on prior rapid-mixing conditions but introduces the new idea of projecting to the nearest rapidly mixing model.  
- Experiments show improved marginal estimates in some regimes, especially with strong interactions and limited sampling time.  
- Paper is well-organized and generally clearly presented.  
- References "standard results" without sufficient proof or citation; needs clarification or references.  
- Experiments omit the computational cost of the projection step, potentially overstating practical gains.  
- Evaluation is limited to small models; scalability and applicability to larger models are untested.  
- Large error gap between the proposed method and mean-field baselines deserves deeper discussion.  
- The approach is elegant but may be applicable only to Ising models; extension to general MRFs is unclear.  
- Strengths: originality, technical soundness, and potential significance for Gibbs sampling efficiency.  
- Weaknesses: limited scope, lack of clarity in places, and concerns about real-world applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address clarity, experimental limitations, and broader applicability.
