- Introduces a novel approximation family for Ising models defined by parameter-space conditions guaranteeing fast-mixing Gibbs sampling (Dobrushin and spectral norm criteria).  
- Main contribution: projecting original Ising parameters onto a fast-mixing set via constrained optimization under Euclidean and KL-based divergences.  
- Re-frames variational inference: approximating with a dynamically simple (fast-sampling) family instead of structurally simple families like mean-field.  
- Strength: original and conceptually novel idea that could open a new direction for approximate inference.  
- Strength: interesting synthesis of deterministic projection optimization and stochastic sampling/gradient estimation.  
- Provides theoretical setup and derivations for Euclidean projection and KL gradient computations.  
- Clarity issue: core projection algorithm presentation is hard to follow and Theorem 7 derivation lacks intuition and is buried in the appendix.  
- Sparsity constraint Zij * Dij = 0 is not clearly justified to preserve the original parameter space versus overconstraining it.  
- Procedural ambiguity: unclear whether KL(ψ||θ) projection is fully optimized before sampling or interleaved with sampling; no algorithm box provided.  
- Technical mismatch: cites Ergodic Mirror Descent but experiments use constant step sizes, so theoretical convergence guarantees do not apply.  
- Experimental unfairness: comparisons count only Gibbs iterations after projection and ignore the large computational cost of the projection (e.g., 30,000 Gibbs iterations).  
- Missing wall-clock timing makes the "limited time" claim unverifiable.  
- No error bars or statistical significance analysis across trials, so reported differences may be due to variance.  
- Baselines incomplete: omits more advanced samplers (e.g., block Gibbs, lifted Gibbs) that could be stronger comparators.  
- Arbitrary experimental choices: 30,000 projection iterations and use of spectral norm bound 2.5 (vs. theoretical 1.0) lack justification and raise concerns about practicality.  
- Overall: highly original and promising idea but currently preliminary—requires clearer algorithmic description and a stronger, fairer experimental evaluation.
