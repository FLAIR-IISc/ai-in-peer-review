- Introduces parameter projection onto a "fast-mixing" set (Dobrushin criterion: spectral norm of interaction matrix < 1) to guarantee rapid Gibbs convergence.  
- Emphasizes parameter-space constraints for tractability rather than structural simplification, extending beyond mean-field and loopy BP.  
- Derives a dual optimization method for Euclidean projection (Theorem 7).  
- Explores KL-divergence-based projections (zero-avoiding, piecewise, zero-forcing), with zero-forcing generalizing mean-field.  
- Empirically, projected parameters yield more accurate marginals under limited sampling time compared to original Gibbs, LBP, TRW, and mean-field.  
- Frames contribution relative to prior mixing-time work (e.g., Hayes, Dyer et al.) but reframes tractability through parameter constraints.  
- Strength: Conceptually novel approach addressing MCMC limitations for strong interactions.  
- Strength: Technical soundness of Euclidean dual projection and practical applicability of zero-forcing KL.  
- Weakness: Constraint Z_{ij}D_{ij}=0 is not justified or clarified—unclear if projection preserves the original parameter space.  
- Weakness: Dual optimization derivation is opaque and lacks an algorithmic pseudocode, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Convergence claims for projected gradient descent cite general results but provide no proof or conditions specific to the paper’s constraints.  
- Weakness: Experimental design issues—using 30,000 Gibbs iterations for projection is arbitrary and excluded from comparisons, biasing results.  
- Weakness: Missing wall-clock timing comparisons, error bars, and statistical significance analysis.  
- Weakness: Important baselines are omitted (e.g., blocked Gibbs, Gogate’s Lifted Gibbs Sampler).  
- Overstated claim: The "limited time" superiority of projected Gibbs conflates projection cost with sampling time, making the comparison invalid.  
- Recommendation: The idea is promising but requires major revisions—clarify constraints, add pseudocode, prove convergence, include timing/error bars and additional baselines—currently not ready for acceptance.
