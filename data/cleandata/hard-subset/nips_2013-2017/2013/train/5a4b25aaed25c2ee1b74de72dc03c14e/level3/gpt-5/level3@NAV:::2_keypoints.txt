- Paper proposes projecting Ising parameters onto a fast-mixing subset using Dobrushin-style spectral constraints to enable rapid Gibbs sampling.  
- Projection formulated as constrained optimization under multiple divergences (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reverse KL) with a dual that enables SVD-thresholding and sparsity preservation.  
- Stochastic optimization for KL(ψ||θ) interleaves sampling and projection, linking MCMC and variational ideas.  
- Connects to classic mixing-time bounds and complements variational approximations by defining a tractable family via fast-mixing rather than low treewidth.  
- Clear motivation: use a fast-mixing surrogate when Gibbs on the target is slow.  
- Technical novelty: dual formulation handling sparsity plus spectral norm and SVD-thresholding.  
- Exploring multiple divergences gives useful perspective on design choices.  
- Potential impact: could improve time–accuracy tradeoffs in practice if validated.  
- Dual is under-specified: provide full Lagrangian derivation, handling of D=|B|, strong duality conditions, and roles/dimensions of Λ and M plus LBFGS-B convergence/termination criteria.  
- Clarify the claim ZijDij=0 with D=|B| preserves original edge parameters — show a proof or counterexample and state whether spectral projection changes feasible sign patterns or shrinks the space.  
- Procedure ambiguity: specify whether projections are solved fully per outer step or interleaved each iteration; include an explicit algorithm with initialization, gradient estimation, projection, step-size schedule, and stopping rules.  
- Convergence concerns: state guarantees for projected (stochastic) gradient descent under the constraints, cite required assumptions if invoking Ergodic Mirror Descent, and justify fixed step-size and single-sweep choices.  
- Experiments are unconvincing: report wall-clock budgets and projection time, runtime per method, error bars over many trials, justify iteration counts, and compare to stronger MCMC baselines (blocked Gibbs, Swendsen–Wang, tree sampling, Gogate).  
- Empirical claims need mixing diagnostics or effective sample sizes and justification for using spectral bound 2.5 versus the 1.0 theoretical guarantee (measure spectral gaps/R norms).  
- Ablations requested: accuracy vs spectral bound, piecewise subgraph choices, pool size/steps impact, and convergence plots.  
- Overall: conceptually sound and original but requires clearer theory, algorithmic detail, and fair, time-normalized experiments to demonstrate practical significance.
