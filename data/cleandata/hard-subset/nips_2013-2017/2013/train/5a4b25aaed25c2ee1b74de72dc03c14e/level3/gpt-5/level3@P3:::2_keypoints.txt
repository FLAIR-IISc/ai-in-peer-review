- Paper proposes projecting intractable Ising parameters onto a norm-bounded, fast-mixing set and compares several divergence-based projections plus a stochastic projected-gradient for KL(ψ||θ).

- Situates work at intersection of variational approximations, mixing-time analysis, and MCMC-based stochastic optimization; relates to blocked/advanced samplers.

- Strength: Clear motivation to trade controlled approximation for provable fast mixing to speed MCMC.

- Strength: Novel viewpoint of treating “fast mixing” as a projection family; shows MF as a degenerate zero-dependency case.

- Strength: Technical contribution: sparsity-preserving Euclidean projection via a dual with masked spectral-norm constraints.

- Strength: Promising hybrid algorithm that interleaves sampling and Euclidean projection for KL(ψ||θ).

- Weakness: Theorem 7 and the dual derivation are unclear—definitions, gradients, and atypical scaling factors need a clear primal/Lagrangian/KKT derivation.

- Weakness: Need explicit explanation why sparsity is preserved in B despite spectral projection on R+M−Λ∘Z, plus existence/uniqueness and L-BFGS-B complexity.

- Weakness: Clarify constraint Z∘D=0 and that the feasible set is smaller than all sparse Ising models; discuss possible loss of sign structure and reachability of sparse B under spectral constraint.

- Weakness: Algorithm description is ambiguous—state whether projection is solved fully then Gibbs is run, or sampling/projection are interleaved; provide a full algorithm box with inputs and per-iteration steps.

- Weakness: Convergence guarantees are missing for the practical settings used (fixed steps, fixed pool, one Gibbs sweep); provide theory under enforced conditions or frame method as a heuristic with diagnostics.

- Weakness: Using spectral bound c=2.5 contradicts ||R||2<1 sufficient condition; if relaxed, quantify actual mixing (autocorrelation, ESS, coupling).

- Weakness: Experimental comparisons by iteration count are unfair and ignore projection cost; report wall-clock times including projection overhead.

- Weakness: No timing comparisons—unclear whether projection+Gibbs is practically faster than well-tuned Gibbs or advanced samplers.

- Weakness: Missing error bars/statistical tests despite 50 trials; report means ± SE and significance.

- Weakness: Important MCMC baselines (blocked Gibbs, tree sampling, Swendsen–Wang, lifted samplers) are missing and should be included.

- Weakness: Missing additional diagnostics and ablations (compare stochastic KL(ψ||θ) to exact KL(θ||ψ) on small graphs, divergence ablations, objective/marginal error traces).

- Weakness: Reproducibility details absent—provide stopping criteria, step-size schedules, initialization, projection tolerances, and ideally release code.

- Quality: Technically promising but needs tightened dual derivation, clearer convergence story, and stronger empirical support.

- Clarity: Generally readable but key parts (dual derivation, algorithmic workflow) need clearer exposition and an algorithm box.

- Originality: Projection-to-fast-mixing and stochastic KL(ψ||θ) hybrid are original and under-explored relative to prior work.

- Significance: Potentially significant if it demonstrates better accuracy-time tradeoffs and scalable guarantees; currently limited by experimental evidence.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, useful projection machinery with sparsity preservation and a promising hybrid deterministic–stochastic inference idea.

- Arguments against acceptance: Ambiguous method description, lacking convergence guarantees under used schedules, relaxed spectral bound undermining theoretical guarantees, and weak empirical evaluation.

- Overall assessment: Promising direction but requires clearer derivations, explicit algorithm and convergence analysis, fairer and stronger experiments (timings, baselines, error bars); reviewer leans toward rejection now but would favor a revision addressing these issues.
