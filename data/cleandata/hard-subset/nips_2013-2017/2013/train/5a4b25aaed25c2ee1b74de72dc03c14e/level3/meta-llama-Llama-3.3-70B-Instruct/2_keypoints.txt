- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters to satisfy the Dobrushin criterion to improve Gibbs mixing rates (novel idea).
- Interesting combination of stochastic and deterministic inference with potential for future research.
- Description of the dual of the projection problem and the overall procedure is unclear and needs clarification.
- Technical soundness is questionable, especially the convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent strategy.
- No algorithm description box is provided, making the procedure hard to follow; add a clear algorithmic outline.
- Experiments are unconvincing: iteration choices are unclear and error bars are missing.
- Lacks comparisons to relevant baselines such as blocked Gibbs samplers and Lifted Gibbs Sampler.
- Development of the projection problem and the proposed method needs improvement and clearer exposition.
- Originality is acceptable but the paper must better situate itself relative to prior work on mixing times and fast-mixing models.
- Significance is unclear because experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.
- Overall recommendation: interesting ideas but requires substantial revisions in clarity, technical justification, and experimental evaluation.
