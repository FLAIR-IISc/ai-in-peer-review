- Paper proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing parameter set (spectral bounds) to speed Gibbs sampling.  
- Technical development builds on prior rapid-mixing results and is sound.  
- Several projection methods are proposed to find nearby parameters that ensure rapid mixing.  
- Experiments on small Ising models show projected models can give better marginals within fixed compute time than variational methods and original-model Gibbs.  
- Unclear whether mixing-time gains will scale to larger models; results may not generalize with problem size.  
- Projection computation appears expensive and is not included in timing comparisons, biasing experimental results.  
- Generalizability to non-binary variables is unclear since convergence bounds are harder beyond binary states.  
- Paper has clarity issues: "standard results" stated without references or proof sketches and notation is sometimes unclear.  
- The dependency matrix R needs discussion—maximization and tractability implications are not addressed.  
- Section 4 is hard to follow; notation (g, M, Lambda) and Theorem 4 are introduced unclearly and need reorganization.  
- Experimental flaws: projection computation omitted from Figure 2 timings and experiments lack larger/realistic models.  
- Authors did not explore the KL(θ||ψ) projection, which could represent a useful "best case" baseline.  
- Recommendation: paper has potential but requires significant revisions—add proofs/references, clarify notation, discuss R, reorganize Section 4, include projection cost, explore KL projection, and test larger/realistic models.
