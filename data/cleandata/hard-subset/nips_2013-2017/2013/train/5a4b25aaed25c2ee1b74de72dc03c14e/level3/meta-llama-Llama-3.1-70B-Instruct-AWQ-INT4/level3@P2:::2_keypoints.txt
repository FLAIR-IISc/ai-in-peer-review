- Paper proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto a fast-mixing set to enable faster Gibbs sampling.
- Authors explore multiple projection divergences: Euclidean, KL-divergence, and a novel piecewise KL on low-treewidth subgraphs.
- Main technical contribution: a constrained optimization dual formulation and a projected gradient descent algorithm for the projection.
- Novelty and potential significance: the projection-to-fast-mixing idea is original and could improve approximate inference accuracy.
- Technical soundness: dual formulation and projected gradient approach are generally well-executed.
- Description of the dual formulation is unclear; exposition needs clarification.
- Unclear how the zij * dij = 0 constraints ensure the projected parameterization spans the same space.
- Overall procedure is ambiguous: unclear whether projection is fully optimized before sampling or interleaved with sampling.
- Lack of an algorithm box or clear procedural pseudocode to clarify the method.
- Experimental setup issues: choice of 30K Gibbs iterations is arbitrary and iteration-based comparisons favor stochastic methods unfairly over deterministic ones.
- No timing comparisons, so practical runtime benefits are unquantified.
- Plots lack error bars, so statistical significance of results is unknown.
- Missing comparisons to relevant baselines like blocked Gibbs and Gogate's Lifted Gibbs Sampler.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify the dual, provide an algorithm box, and strengthen experimental evaluation (timing, baselines, error bars).
