- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters into a Dobrushin-safe space to accelerate Gibbs sampling—a novel and interesting idea.
- Combines stochastic and deterministic inference to improve sampling efficiency, addressing a significant challenge in probabilistic modeling.
- Explores multiple divergence measures for projection (Euclidean, piecewise KL, reversed KL via stochastic approximation).
- Builds on existing approximation methods (mean-field, tree-based) and connects to known results on mixing times, notably Theorem 4.
- Description of the dual problem in Theorem 7 is unclear; motivation for introducing matrices M and Λ is insufficiently explained.
- Unclear how the dual relates to the original minimization; presentation obscures rather than clarifies the derivation.
- Ambiguity about whether the constraint z_ij d_ij = 0 preserves the full parameter space or restricts it, potentially causing suboptimal solutions.
- Procedure is unclear: unclear if projection is fully optimized before sampling or interleaved with Gibbs iterations; an algorithm box is needed.
- Convergence guarantees for the projected gradient descent strategy are missing; Ergodic Mirror Descent is mentioned without formal justification in this setting.
- Experimental evaluation is unconvincing: using 30,000 Gibbs iterations as a benchmark seems arbitrary.
- Direct iteration-count comparisons to deterministic methods (LBP, TRW, MF) are unfair due to differing per-iteration costs; wall-clock or computational-cost comparisons are needed.
- Runtime of the projection step is not reported, so practical benefit of faster mixing cannot be assessed.
- Plots lack error bars, preventing assessment of statistical significance of reported differences.
- Missing comparisons to stronger Gibbs baselines like blocked Gibbs sampling and Gogate's Lifted Gibbs Sampler.
- Overall assessment: promising core idea but rejects due to lack of clarity, incomplete theory, and inadequate experiments; encouraged to revise and resubmit.
