- Novel approach: project Ising model parameters onto a space guaranteeing fast mixing of Gibbs sampling to improve sampling efficiency.
- Thorough exploration: investigates multiple projection divergences (Euclidean, KL, piecewise KL, reversed KL) with theoretical and empirical analysis.
- Builds on prior work: situates method relative to mean-field, loopy BP, TRW, and fast-mixing conditions via the dependency matrix spectral norm.
- Algorithmic contribution: develops projection algorithms, including a stochastic approximation for reversed KL.
- Empirical finding: reversed KL projection consistently yields the best performance; methods outperform TRW, MF, LBP and often match or beat Gibbs on original models given reasonable runtimes.
- Practical concerns: experiments are limited to small models and lack absolute runtime figures, leaving scalability and real-world utility uncertain.
- Reproducibility issue: stopping criteria for nested L-BFGS-B in the projected-gradient algorithm (Sec. 5.1) are unclear and should be specified.
- Recommended evaluation: expand experiments to larger, realistic models (e.g., tractable random-field models like Flach) to assess scalability.
- Minor edits: change “onto the tree” to “onto graph T” and use “subgradient” instead of “derivative.”
- Recommendation: reviewer leans toward acceptance, conditional on satisfactorily addressing runtime and scalability concerns in the rebuttal.
