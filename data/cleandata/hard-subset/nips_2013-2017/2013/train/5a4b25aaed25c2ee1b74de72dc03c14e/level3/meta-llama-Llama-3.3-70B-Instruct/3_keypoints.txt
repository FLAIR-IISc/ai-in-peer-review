- Proposes projecting an input MRF onto a set of fast-mixing models (bounded spectral norm of absolute Ising edge matrix) to accelerate Gibbs sampling.
- Projection defined via divergences between Gibbs distributions while preserving the original graph structure.
- Considers four divergences for projection: Euclidean, KL, piecewise KL, and reversed KL; provides algorithms for each.
- Uses a projected gradient algorithm to compute the projection (inner-iteration stopping criteria not fully detailed).
- Experimental results show the projected models outperform loopy BP, TRW, mean-field, and Gibbs on the original model in accuracy and runtime-vs-accuracy.
- Reversed KL projection consistently performs best in experiments.
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical foundations and concise explanations.
- Experiments are extensive but lack comparisons on larger models to demonstrate scalability.
- Some minor errors and omissions: missing reported runtime for some algorithms and insufficient details on stopping criteria.
- Strengths: originality, technical soundness, clarity, and strong empirical performance.
- Weaknesses: minor experimental and methodological reporting gaps and limited scalability analysis.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, solid theory, extensive experiments, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing runtimes, incomplete stopping-criterion details, and limited large-scale experiments.
