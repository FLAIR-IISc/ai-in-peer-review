- Proposes projecting Ising model parameters onto "fast-mixing" models (bounded spectral norm of absolute interactions) to guarantee rapid Gibbs convergence.
- Novel tractable model class that emphasizes MCMC convergence guarantees over structural simplicity.
- Develops projection algorithms for multiple divergences: Euclidean, KL, piecewise KL, and reversed KL.
- Empirical evaluation shows sampling from projected models produces more accurate marginals under a fixed time budget than LBP, TRW, MF, and Gibbs on the original model.
- Reversed KL projection performs consistently strongest.
- Major concern: computational cost of the projection itself is unclear and may limit practical utility.
- Need clarification of stopping criteria for the inner L-BFGS-B in the projected-gradient algorithm for reproducibility and complexity assessment.
- Figure 2’s x-axis ("number of samples") is unclear; authors should report absolute runtimes (including projection time) for all methods for fair comparison.
- Recommend experiments on larger, structured benchmarks (e.g., Flach UAI 2012 random-field models) to evaluate scalability.
- Minor textual fixes: replace "onto the tree" with "onto graph T" and use "subgradient" instead of "derivative" where appropriate.
- Overall verdict: promising technique with strong initial results, but acceptance depends on clarifying computational cost and demonstrating value beyond small-scale models.
