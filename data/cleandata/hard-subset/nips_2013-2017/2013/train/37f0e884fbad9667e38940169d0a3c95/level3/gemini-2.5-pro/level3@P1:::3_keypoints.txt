- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining one full gradient per epoch with stochastic updates to get O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic calls, with high-probability linear convergence.

- Novelty: the "mixed gradient" idea—anchoring stochastic steps with a static full gradient each epoch—is original and valuable.

- Technical quality: rigorous induction-based analysis using martingale concentration bounds supports the main claims.

- Practical relevance concern: the claimed advantage when κ ≤ n^(2/3) is not convincingly motivated with concrete ML examples, limiting perceived applicability.

- Trade-off not discussed: EMGD’s high-probability stochastic guarantee is weaker than deterministic guarantees of full-gradient methods and this sacrifice isn’t justified.
