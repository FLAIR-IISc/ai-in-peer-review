- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm using both full and stochastic gradients to reduce full-gradient computations.  
- Convergence: EMGD finds an ε-optimal solution with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Proof technique: convergence shown by induction using the Hoeffding-Azuma inequality to bound a martingale difference sequence (high-probability result).  
- Comparison: EMGD has lower computational complexity than Nesterov’s accelerated method when κ ≤ n^{2/3}.  
- Limitation: convergence guarantee is high-probability only, unlike deterministic batch gradient descent.  
- Limitation: analysis assumes the condition number κ is known a priori, which may be impractical.  
- Relevance concern: with regularization-driven strong convexity (μ ≈ C·N), EMGD outperforms batch GD only in the narrow regime N = O(C^3/L^3), questioning practical relevance.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear analysis and proofs.  
- Minor corrections needed: eq. (6) should read ||w^* − ŵ||^2; specify the function used in line 288 for eq. (7); remove the equality in line 286 for clarity.  
- Overall assessment: novel hybrid stochastic/batch SGD with theoretical convergence but unclear practical advantages for machine learning.  
- Strengths: novel method, potentially lower complexity than Nesterov in some regimes, clear analysis.  
- Weaknesses: high-probability proof, requirement of known κ, unclear ML relevance.  
- Reviewer scores: Originality 8/10; Significance 6/10; Quality 8/10; Clarity 8/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, theoretical convergence, complexity benefits in certain regimes.  
- Rejection arguments: non-deterministic convergence, impractical κ assumption, limited practical relevance.
