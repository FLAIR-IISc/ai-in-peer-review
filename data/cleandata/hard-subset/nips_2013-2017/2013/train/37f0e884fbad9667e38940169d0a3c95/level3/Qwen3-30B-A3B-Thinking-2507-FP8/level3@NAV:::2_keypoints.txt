- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm combining full and stochastic gradient oracles for smooth strongly convex optimization.
- Main theoretical claim: high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, removing √κ dependence in iteration complexity.
- Novel algorithm design using a "mixed gradient descent" update and a high-probability convergence guarantee (stronger than prior expected-rate results).
- Correctly identifies the practical issue that ill-conditioned problems demand excessive full gradient computations.
- Critical dependence on knowing the condition number κ a priori, with no analysis for unknown or misspecified κ.
- Step size/hyperparameter η depends on κ, but no estimation procedure or heuristic tuning guidance is provided.
- No experimental results to validate theory, study empirical behavior under misspecification, or compare against standard baselines (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.).
- Fails to review or compare to prior hybrid deterministic–stochastic work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).
- Neglects analysis or discussion of non-strongly convex settings that are common in practice.
- Problem setting and oracle assumptions are confusingly described (Section 3.1 misleading).
- Lacks discussion connecting the method to simple interleaving of deterministic and stochastic steps.
- Claims linear convergence but only provides high-probability rates rather than expected-rate results more relevant to practitioners.
- Overall: theoretically interesting but limited by impractical κ dependence, lack of empirical validation, and insufficient comparison to prior work; needs these addressed for publication.
