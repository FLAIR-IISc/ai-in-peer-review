The paper "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients" proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), that utilizes both full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The algorithm is designed to reduce the number of full gradients required, which can be computationally expensive for large data sets.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rate of EMGD, showing that it can achieve an ǫ-optimal solution by computing O(log 1/ǫ) full gradients and O(κ^2 log 1/ǫ) stochastic gradients. The proof of convergence is based on induction and utilizes the Hoeffding-Azuma inequality to bound the martingale difference sequence.

The paper also provides a comparison of EMGD with other optimization algorithms, including Nesterov's accelerated gradient descent, stochastic average gradient (SAG), and stochastic dual coordinate ascent (SDCA). The authors show that EMGD has a lower computational complexity than Nesterov's algorithm when the condition number κ is less than or equal to n^2/3, where n is the number of data points.

However, the paper has some limitations. The convergence proof is high-probability only, whereas batch gradient descent guarantees deterministic convergence. Additionally, the authors assume that the condition number κ is known beforehand, which may not be practical in many machine learning settings.

The relevance of the paper to machine learning is also a concern. The authors assume strong convexity from regularization, which gives a condition number μ ≈ C·N, where C is a constant and N is the number of data points. In this regime, the method is faster than batch gradient descent only if N = O(C^3/L^3), which may not be an interesting or practical regime.

In terms of clarity and presentation, the paper is well-written, but there are some minor mistakes and areas for improvement. For example, equation (6) should read ||w^* − ŵ||^2, and the authors should specify which function is used in line 288 for equation (7). Additionally, the authors should remove the equality in line 286 as it decreases clarity.

Overall, the paper proposes a novel hybrid stochastic/batch SGD with convergence using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients. However, the relevance to machine learning and the practical advantages over standard batch gradient descent are unclear.
