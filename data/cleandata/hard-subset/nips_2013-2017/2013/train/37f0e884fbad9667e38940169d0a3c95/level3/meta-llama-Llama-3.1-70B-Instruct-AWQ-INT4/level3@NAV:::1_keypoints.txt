- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): alternate epochs with one full gradient evaluation followed by multiple stochastic gradient steps.  
- Core idea: constant reduction in optimization error per epoch yields linear convergence for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis including a high-probability proof of linear convergence.  
- Claims reduction in required full gradients from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)), independent of condition number κ.  
- Includes comparisons to Nesterov’s accelerated gradient and the SAG algorithm.  
- Strength: novel and simple algorithm that mixes full and stochastic gradients.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical guarantees and high-probability convergence analysis.  
- Strength: situates work relative to existing accelerated and variance-reduced methods.  
- Weakness: requires knowledge of the condition number κ, which may be unavailable in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks experimental results to demonstrate empirical performance and practical behavior.  
- Weakness: potential computational inefficiency due to multiple stochastic gradient evaluations per epoch.  
- Suggested improvement: provide empirical experiments to validate performance and regimes of advantage.  
- Suggested improvement: investigate practical estimation or avoidance of needing κ.  
- Suggested improvement: add computational-cost comparisons with Nesterov and SAG.  
- Acceptance argument: theoretically solid, novel approach with potential efficiency benefits in some regimes.  
- Rejection argument: insufficient empirical validation and practical considerations (κ estimation, runtime analysis) limit acceptance.
