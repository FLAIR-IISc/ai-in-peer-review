- Proposes EMGD: interleaving full-gradient and stochastic-gradient steps to reduce condition-number dependence.  
- Claims EMGD achieves O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls, avoiding √κ.  
- Strength: simple, self-contained convergence proof with high-probability linear convergence (stronger than SAG’s in-expectation result).  
- Applicable to constrained problems and potentially better for memory/parallelization, though these advantages are not fully demonstrated.  
- Complexity table (Table 2) compares EMGD to Nesterov, SAG, SDCA and identifies regimes where EMGD may be preferable.  
- Major weakness: no experimental evaluation — practical performance and relevance remain unclear.  
- Discussion of limitations is brief; requires κ known a priori, unlike adaptive methods (SCDA, SAG).  
- Lacks automatic step-size tuning used in practical implementations of SAG/SCDA.  
- Needs a more detailed comparison with similar hybrid approaches (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).  
- Clarity could improve: add high-level intuition behind key proof choices and clarify assumptions in Table 1 (SCDA applicability, gradient expectation identity).  
- Minor edits needed for notation consistency (domain, norms, log/ln).  
- Recommend highlighting concrete scenarios where EMGD outperforms (e.g., regularized ML with k ≈ n/C') to strengthen impact.  
- Overall: technically strong and novel; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing experimental validation and practical limitations.
