- Proposes EMGD, a hybrid of full and stochastic gradients claiming O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient complexity to reduce expensive full-gradient evaluations.  
- Theoretical novelty: achieves linear convergence with condition-number-independent full-gradient accesses, building on SAG/SDCA and epoch methods.  
- Convergence proof is thorough and technically sound, using induction and martingale analysis, but contains minor notation/typo issues (e.g., eq.(6), eq.(4)).  
- Main strength is the theoretical contribution and elegant mixed-gradient algorithm design.  
- Practical relevance is limited: in typical ML with L2 regularization λ ∝ N, κ is small, so the ill-conditioned regime targeted by EMGD is rare.  
- Claimed speedup over batch GD for κ ≤ n^(2/3) holds in already well-conditioned regimes, undermining motivation.  
- Convergence guarantee is high-probability (1 − mδ) rather than deterministic, which may be less appealing to practitioners.  
- Lacks empirical evaluation or practical comparisons across datasets/condition numbers; Table 2 is only theoretical.  
- Clarity is good overall but needs fixes: define κ = L/λ, use norms in eq.(13), remove ambiguous equality at line 286, and refine related work distinctions.  
- Originality: conceptually similar to semi-stochastic methods but offers a significant technical contribution; practical impact limited by strong-convexity assumptions.  
- Recommendation: reject on significance grounds; suggest reframing as a theoretical contribution, add empirical validation, and address minor technical issues.
