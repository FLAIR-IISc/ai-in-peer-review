- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) combining full and stochastic gradients to reduce κ-dependence.  
- Claims O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient evaluations.  
- Builds on existing first-order methods and motivates hybrid deterministic–stochastic trade-offs.  
- Novelty: potentially valuable approach to mitigate ill-conditioning when full and stochastic gradients are available.  
- Theoretical rigor: presents a detailed convergence theorem and careful proofs for strongly convex functions.  
- Presentation: algorithm is clearly described with consistent notation.  
- Comparative analysis: contrasts EMGD with Nesterov, SAG, and SDCA under specific conditions.  
- Major weakness: requires a priori knowledge of condition number κ with no robustness analysis or estimation method.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation — no experiments to assess practical performance or sensitivity to κ.  
- Scope limitation: analysis restricted to strongly convex functions, not general convex problems.  
- Incomplete related work: omits relevant hybrid deterministic–stochastic literature (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).  
- Ambiguous problem setting: unclear whether the setting is finite-sum deterministic or stochastic approximation.  
- Limited algorithmic insight: unclear why this specific mixing strategy yields the claimed convergence versus simple interleaving.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: step size η depends on κ with no guidance or heuristics for misspecification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important theoretical claim (O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient complexity) and clear, rigorous writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: impractical κ assumption, lack of experiments, incomplete related work, and narrow scope.  
- Recommendation: reject pending additions — estimate κ, provide experiments (including robustness tests), extend analysis beyond strong convexity, and expand related work.
