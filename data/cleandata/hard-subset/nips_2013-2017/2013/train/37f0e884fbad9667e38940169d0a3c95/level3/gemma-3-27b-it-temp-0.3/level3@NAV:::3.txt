## Review of "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients"

This paper proposes a novel hybrid optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), designed to achieve linear convergence for strongly convex and smooth optimization problems while reducing the dependence on the condition number κ. The core idea is to interleave full gradient evaluations with stochastic gradients, aiming for O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients – a significant improvement over standard gradient descent’s O(√κ log(1/ε)) full gradient complexity when κ is large. 

The authors provide a convergence proof for EMGD, which appears technically sound, although minor corrections are noted (see below). The work builds upon existing research in epoch-based gradient descent and stochastic optimization methods like SAG and SDCA, appropriately referencing relevant literature. The paper clearly articulates the motivation – the computational cost of full gradient evaluations in ill-conditioned problems – and positions EMGD as a potential solution.

However, the central weakness lies in the practical relevance of the proposed method to modern machine learning. The authors demonstrate that EMGD offers an advantage over batch gradient descent *only* when N (the problem size) is O(C³/L³), where C is a constant related to regularization. This is a highly restrictive regime, and the authors fail to adequately justify why this scenario would commonly occur in real-world ML applications. Furthermore, the convergence guarantee is only high-probability, while batch gradient descent offers deterministic convergence, a potentially important consideration for sensitive applications. 

The paper lacks a thorough comparative discussion with batch gradient descent, specifically addressing the trade-offs between the potentially lower number of full gradients and the probabilistic convergence of EMGD. A convincing argument for practical superiority is missing. 

**Minor Comments:**

*   Equation (6) should read ||w^* − ŵ||².
*   Line 288: Specify which function is used in equation (7).
*   Line 286: Remove the equality sign for clarity.
*   Equation (13): Replace absolute values with norms.
*   Explicitly state/explain that L ≥ λ, even if obvious.
*   Equation (4): x^ should be ŵ, f should be F; the first term in the max is always larger by Lemma 1.
*   Precisely define the condition number as a function of λ and L.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a novel and theoretically interesting hybrid optimization method. However, its practical significance for machine learning is questionable due to the restrictive condition for outperforming batch gradient descent and the lack of deterministic convergence. While the technical contributions are valuable, the paper needs a more compelling justification of its relevance to real-world problems.

**Arguments for Acceptance:** Novelty of the hybrid approach and the convergence proof.
**Arguments against Acceptance:** Limited practical relevance, lack of a strong comparison to batch GD, restrictive conditions for improvement.

**Recommendation:** Borderline Reject. The authors should significantly strengthen the discussion of practical implications and address the concerns regarding the restrictive conditions for improvement before resubmission.
