- Proposal: Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm using one full gradient per epoch plus mixed stochastic updates to reduce expensive full gradient computations.
- Algorithmic idea: Each epoch computes a full gradient as an anchor, then uses a mixed gradient combining that full gradient with cheap stochastic corrections.
- Main theoretical claim: EMGD achieves ε-optimality with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients (κ = condition number).
- Significance: Number of full gradient evaluations is independent of κ, unlike AGD which needs O(√κ log(1/ε)) full gradients.
- Positioning: Frames EMGD between deterministic methods (e.g., AGD) and stochastic methods (e.g., SGD, SAG, SDCA) to combine linear convergence with fewer full-data passes.
- Strength — Originality: The mixed-gradient update and single full-gradient-per-epoch strategy are novel and theoretically interesting.
- Strength — Technical quality: Provides a rigorous inductive convergence proof establishing high-probability linear convergence.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and situates the contribution in the optimization literature.
- Weakness — Practical relevance: Cost comparison shows EMGD is advantageous only when κ < n^(2/3); for common ML settings with regularization λ_reg = O(1/n) (so κ = O(n)), EMGD is likely slower than AGD.
- Weakness — Applicability claim: Paper needs stronger justification or examples where κ is small relative to n, or to temper claims of practical superiority.
- Weakness — Guarantee type: EMGD’s convergence is high-probability rather than deterministic, making it a strictly weaker guarantee compared to batch GD.
- Suggestion — Comparative analysis: Include a more thorough comparison with batch GD addressing κ’s scaling with n and provide evidence/citations for claimed parameter regimes.
- Suggestion — Definitions/clarity: Define κ = L/λ more formally, note why L ≥ λ follows, and clarify the dependence of κ on smoothness and strong convexity.
- Suggestion — Presentation/notation: Fix minor notational issues and clarify proofs (check squared norms in Lemma 1/equation (6), norms vs absolute values in equation (13), clarity in lines ~286–288 and explicit function when invoking condition (7)).
- Overall assessment: The paper presents a solid and novel theoretical contribution, but its practical impact is unclear without better justification of regimes where EMGD outperforms standard AGD.
