- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), mixing full and stochastic gradients within epochs to reduce full-gradient calls.
- Claims reduction of full-gradient evaluations from O(√κ log 1/ε) to O(log 1/ε) while using O(κ² log 1/ε) stochastic gradients.
- Targets the condition-number bottleneck in ill-conditioned smooth strongly convex optimization.
- Strong theoretical contribution with a clear, self-contained convergence analysis.
- Theorem 1: EMGD achieves an ε-optimal solution with high probability, stronger than SAG’s in-expectation guarantee.
- Proof leverages the mixed gradient construction and a shrinking domain to bound stochastic error; novel for constrained optimization.
- Achieves linear convergence O(log 1/ε) with high-probability guarantees, unlike SAG/SDCA’s expectation-only results.
- Potential memory (Ω(d) vs. Ω(n)) and parallelization benefits noted but not fully developed.
- Comparison to prior work (SAG, SDCA, Nesterov) is useful but needs expansion and clearer context.
- Major weakness: no empirical validation, so practical relevance and performance are unclear.
- Requires prior knowledge of κ to set epoch length; lacks adaptivity compared to methods like SAG.
- Practical applicability questionable in typical ML regimes (κ ~ n/C), where EMGD may not outperform SAG or Nesterov.
- Clarity generally good but needs more intuition on the mixed gradient/shrinking domain, notation fixes, and relation to Friedlander & Schmidt (2012).
- Computational-complexity claims (Table 2) need better justification for the conditions when EMGD outperforms others.
- Overall: technically solid and elegant; recommend acceptance conditional on adding experiments and expanded discussion of practical limitations and comparisons.
