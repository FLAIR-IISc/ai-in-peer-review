- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- EMGD divides optimization into epochs: one full gradient evaluation followed by multiple stochastic updates.  
- Reduces required full gradients from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)), improving computational efficiency for large-scale problems.  
- Provides a thorough, well-organized theoretical analysis with a high-probability linear convergence theorem.  
- EMGD can handle constrained optimization problems, unlike some competing stochastic methods.  
- The proof is simpler than that of SAG, increasing accessibility and appeal.  
- Comprehensive comparison with Nesterov’s method, SAG, and SDCA, highlighting pros and cons.  
- Lacks empirical evaluations to validate practical performance on real-world datasets.  
- Requires knowledge of the condition number κ, which may not be available in practice.  
- Epoch length must be fixed a priori, potentially limiting adaptability across problems.  
- Limited discussion of other limitations and practical drawbacks.  
- Writing and exposition are clear overall, though some notation and terminology need clarification.  
- Overall positive assessment: originality 8/10, significance 8/10, quality 8/10, clarity 8/10.
