- Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid of full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems claiming O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient complexity independent of κ.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorously shows reduced dependency on the condition number κ for full gradients.  
- Novelty: interleaving deterministic and stochastic gradients is an interesting, potentially impactful idea.  
- Provides high-probability convergence guarantees rather than just expectation.  
- Flexibility: applicable to both constrained and unconstrained optimization.  
- Major weakness: assumes the condition number κ is known exactly and provides no practical estimation method.  
- No experimental validation or empirical comparisons to methods like SGD, averaged SGD, or SAG.  
- Analysis does not consider misspecified hyper-parameters, a common real-world issue.  
- Limited scope: only analyzes strongly convex problems, omitting non-strongly convex settings.  
- Insufficient related-work discussion and missing comparisons to prior hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).  
- Pro: strong theoretical novelty and potential relevance for large-scale optimization.  
- Con: impractical assumptions, lack of experiments, and incomplete literature comparison weaken the submission.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form; encourage adding empirical studies, κ-estimation strategies, robustness to misspecification, and a more comprehensive related-work discussion.
