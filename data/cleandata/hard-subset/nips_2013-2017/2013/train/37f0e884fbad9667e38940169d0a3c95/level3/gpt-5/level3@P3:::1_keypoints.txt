- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): one full-gradient per epoch plus many cheap mixed stochastic steps with an anchor and shrinking ball.  
- Main theoretical claim: high-probability linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Key insight: anchoring at epoch averages and subtracting ∇f(w̄k) tightly controls variance so epoch count is independent of κ.  
- Technically sound and clean convergence proof leveraging strong convexity, smoothness, shrinking domains, and Azuma–Hoeffding.  
- Important inconsistency: paper inconsistently states O(κ) vs O(κ2) stochastic complexity; theorem implies T = Θ(κ2) so fix throughout.  
- No experiments provided, limiting assessment of practical relevance, robustness to κ misspecification, and projection costs.  
- Clarity is generally good but needs a high-level proof plan, consistent notation (ln/log, norms, indices), and fixes for typos and acronym misuse.  
- Tables should state precise assumptions per method (finite-sum requirement, constrained vs. unconstrained, memory, guarantee type).  
- Make explicit unbiased-gradient identity E[∇f(w)] = ∇F(w), smoothness assumptions, and the function-oracle/finite-sum model.  
- Originality: simple, neat hybrid construction with self-contained analysis, but related semi-stochastic/hybrid work (especially Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) must be discussed and contrasted.  
- Significance (theory): notable condition-number–independent full-gradient count and high-probability guarantee improving on in-expectation results.  
- Significance (practice): O(d) memory and constrained-problem applicability are advantages, but practical impact is unclear without experiments and adaptive tuning.  
- Comparative complexity: EMGD can beat Nesterov when κ ≤ n2/3, matches SAG around κ ≈ n1/2, but SAG/SDCA often outperform EMGD; Section 3.4 needs a systematic regime diagram and wall-clock proxies.  
- Practical suggestions: add experiments on standard and constrained ML tasks, ablations over κ/n/projection cost, and report time as well as gradient calls.  
- Adaptivity concerns: EMGD requires κ to set T and η; suggest doubling schedules, variance-based tuning, or online L/λ estimation with safeguards.  
- Limitations to discuss: cost of projections onto W ∩ B(w̄k; Δk), sensitivity to anchor/averaging, and robustness to approximate smoothness.  
- Pros summarized: simple analysis, high-probability linear rate, κ-independent full-gradient count, constrained applicability, O(d) memory.  
- Cons summarized: no empirical validation, worse stochastic complexity O(κ2 log(1/ε)) vs. SAG/SDCA in many regimes, requires known κ and lacks adaptive mechanisms, thin related-work discussion.  
- Requested revisions: fix κ vs κ2 inconsistencies, unify notation/citations, expand comparisons (storage, guarantees, constrained use), add experiments and parallelization discussion, and address adaptivity/limitations.  
- Verdict: technically solid and theoretically meaningful; lean toward acceptance if authors commit to requested revisions (experiments, clearer comparisons, and fixes), otherwise borderline.
