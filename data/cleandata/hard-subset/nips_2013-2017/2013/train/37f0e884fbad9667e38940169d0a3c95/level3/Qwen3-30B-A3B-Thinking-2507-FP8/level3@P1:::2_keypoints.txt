- Proposes EMGD, a hybrid that uses full + stochastic gradients to get O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient accesses and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound under the assumption κ is known; Theorem 1 gives high-probability linear convergence.  
- Critical practical flaw: analysis and hyperparameter setting require κ known a priori; no guidance for unknown or misspecified κ.  
- No empirical validation: no experiments testing κ misspecification or comparisons to baselines (SGD, SAG, SDCA).  
- Claim of “computational efficiency” is unsupported because no evidence that stochastic gradients are significantly cheaper than full gradients.  
- Problem statement is misleadingly framed as general stochastic approximation, while the algorithm effectively relies on finite-sum structure.  
- Algorithm description is precise but motivation/intuition for the mixed-gradient estimator is underexplained despite rigorous derivation.  
- Prior work oversight: fails to cite or compare to similar hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012), overstating novelty.  
- Contribution is mainly a minor theoretical refinement (high-probability guarantee) rather than a significant new mechanism.  
- Significance is limited: unknown κ in practice, exclusion of non-strongly-convex problems, and unverified theoretical speedups.  
- Pros: rigorous high-probability guarantee, clear algorithm, useful for theoretical exploration of hybrid oracles.  
- Cons: unresolved κ assumption, no experiments, missing related work, misleading problem framing, no analysis of κ misspecification.  
- Recommendation: Reject — insufficient practical validation and unresolved κ dependency diminish the paper’s scientific value.
