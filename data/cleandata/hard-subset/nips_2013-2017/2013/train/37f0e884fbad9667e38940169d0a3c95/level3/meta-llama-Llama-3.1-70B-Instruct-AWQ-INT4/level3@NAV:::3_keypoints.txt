- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a novel hybrid stochastic/batch SGD algorithm to reduce full-gradient calls for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Algorithm divides optimization into fixed-length epochs and performs mixed updates combining full and stochastic gradients.  
- Authors prove linear convergence for EMGD with high probability.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a helpful introduction and explanations.  
- Strength: EMGD is novel and could improve optimization efficiency in ML settings.  
- Strength: Convergence proof is rigorous and clarifies algorithm behavior.  
- Strength: Provides useful comparisons with other optimization algorithms, noting pros and cons.  
- Weakness: Relevance to ML is unclear—assumption μ ≈ C·N from regularization may not hold generally.  
- Weakness: Convergence guarantee is high-probability only, unlike deterministic batch gradient descent.  
- Weakness: Lacks a careful discussion and justification comparing EMGD’s practical advantage to batch GD.  
- Weakness: Minor mistakes/typos present (e.g., incorrect equation (6), missing function at line 288).  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing concerns—add stronger ML relevance justification, more thorough comparison to batch GD, and fix errors/typos.
