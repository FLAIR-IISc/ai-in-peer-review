- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm interleaving full and stochastic gradient steps for strongly convex optimization.  
- Claims convergence: O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient evaluations and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient evaluations, with theoretical analysis supporting this.  
- Builds on prior deterministic and stochastic methods (e.g., Nesterov, SAG, SDCA) and adapts epoch gradient descent to include stochastic information.  
- Convergence proof appears technically sound and introduces a novel combination of techniques.  
- Minor technical corrections needed (e.g., Eq.(6), function spec on line 288, remove equality on line 286, use norms in (13), state L ≥ λ, correct (4) and condition number definition).  
- Major practical concern: EMGD outperforms batch GD only under the restrictive condition N = O(C³/L³), making gains unlikely for typical large-scale ML problems.  
- EMGD provides only high-probability convergence guarantees, whereas batch GD gives deterministic convergence.  
- Insufficient discussion and comparison of EMGD vs. batch GD across a wide range of condition numbers and problem sizes; more empirical/theoretical evaluation needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel hybrid approach, rigorous theoretical analysis, clear algorithm presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical relevance, probabilistic (not deterministic) guarantee, and inadequate discussion of real-world performance.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form; authors should expand comparison to batch GD, address the restrictive performance condition, and fix the noted technical issues.
