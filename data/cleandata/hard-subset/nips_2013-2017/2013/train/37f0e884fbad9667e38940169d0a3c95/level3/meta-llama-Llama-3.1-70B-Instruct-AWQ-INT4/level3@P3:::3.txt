This paper proposes a novel hybrid stochastic/batch stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithm, called Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), which achieves linear convergence with a condition number independent access of full gradients. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate, showing that EMGD can find an ε-optimal solution by computing O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The use of a hybrid approach, combining both full and stochastic gradients, is an interesting idea that could potentially lead to improvements in computational efficiency. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of related work in the field of convex optimization, which helps to situate their contribution within the broader context of the field.

One of the major strengths of the paper is its technical soundness. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate, using techniques from convex optimization and probability theory. The proof of Theorem 1, which establishes the convergence rate of EMGD, is particularly impressive, as it requires a careful analysis of the interplay between the full and stochastic gradients.

However, there are some concerns regarding the relevance of the algorithm to machine learning (ML) applications. The authors assume that the strong convexity of the objective function is given by regularization, which implies that the condition number μ is approximately equal to C \* N, where C is a constant and N is the number of data points. This means that the algorithm is only faster than batch gradient descent (GD) if N = O(C^3/L^3), which may not be a realistic or interesting regime for many ML applications.

Furthermore, the proposed convergence is high-probability only, whereas batch GD guarantees deterministic convergence. This may be a concern for some applications where deterministic convergence is required.

To address these concerns, the authors need to provide a more careful discussion comparing EMGD to batch GD, justifying its practical advantages and relevance to ML applications. They should also consider providing more experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of EMGD in practice.

There are also some minor issues with the paper. For example, equation (6) should read ||w^* - ŵ||^2, and the authors should specify which function is used in line 288 for equation (7). Additionally, the authors should remove the equality in line 286, as it decreases clarity.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel hybrid approach to stochastic gradient descent, which is a significant contribution to the field of convex optimization. However, the authors could have done a better job of highlighting the key insights and innovations of their approach, and explaining how it differs from previous work in the field.

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound, but it could benefit from a more careful discussion of its relevance to ML applications and a clearer explanation of its key insights and innovations.
