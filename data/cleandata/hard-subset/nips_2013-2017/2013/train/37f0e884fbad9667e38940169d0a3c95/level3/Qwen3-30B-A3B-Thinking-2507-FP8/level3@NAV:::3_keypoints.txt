- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm combining full and stochastic gradients for smooth strongly convex problems.  
- Claims linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, removing condition-number dependence from full-gradient counts.  
- Theoretical analysis proves high-probability convergence, contrasting with expected convergence guarantees of SAG and SDCA.  
- The convergence proof is technically sound but contains minor notational errors (e.g., eq. (6), eq. (4), eq. (13)).  
- Significant theoretical contribution by eliminating condition-number dependence for full-gradient accesses.  
- Practical relevance is not well justified: in typical ML settings κ ≈ 1/τ and the claimed advantage κ ≤ n^(2/3) (τ ≥ n^(-2/3)) is an uninteresting regime.  
- Paper lacks a thorough computational cost analysis in realistic ML scenarios to support practical advantages.  
- Fails to sufficiently contrast EMGD’s high-probability guarantee with batch GD’s deterministic convergence in terms of practical impact.  
- Overall: technically solid and novel, but practical significance is questionable; authors should substantially revise discussion and cost comparisons.
