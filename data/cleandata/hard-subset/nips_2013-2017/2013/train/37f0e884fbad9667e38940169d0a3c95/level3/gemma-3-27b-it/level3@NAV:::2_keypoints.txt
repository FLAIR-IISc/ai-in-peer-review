- Proposes EMGD, a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method interleaving full and stochastic gradients to attain linear convergence without κ-dependent full-gradient count.  
- Claims O(log(1/ε)) full gradient and O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient evaluations.  
- Positions itself among deterministic, stochastic, and semi-stochastic methods but fails to compare to Friedlander & Schmidt (2012).  
- Main strength: theoretical analysis proving the claimed convergence under the assumption that the condition number κ is known.  
- Major limitation: assumes known κ with no discussion of unknown or misestimated κ.  
- Hyperparameter η is tied to κ and no estimation procedure or heuristics for η are provided.  
- No experimental validation to support theoretical claims or to study behavior under κ misspecification.  
- No empirical or theoretical analysis of convergence when η (and thus κ) is set too large or too small.  
- Does not consider non-strongly convex settings, limiting broader applicability.  
- Section 3.1 misleadingly conflates finite-sum deterministic and stochastic approximation problem formulations.  
- Lacks deeper discussion of how the “mixed” update relates to simpler interleaving strategies.  
- Arguments for acceptance: provides a convergence guarantee (under a strong assumption) and targets reducing cost for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong known-κ assumption, lack of experiments, missing prior-work comparison, limited to strongly convex case, and potentially misleading problem description.  
- Overall recommendation: reject; suggests adding guidance for unknown κ and empirical results to improve the paper’s value.
