- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), an optimization algorithm mixing full and stochastic gradients in epochs.  
- Core idea: alternate mixed-gradient steps within epochs to leverage both full and stochastic information.  
- Theoretical claim: achieves high-probability linear convergence O(log(1/ε)) for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Analysis is thorough and clearly presented.  
- Crucial assumption: condition number κ (L and λ) is known a priori.  
- Requires hyperparameter η that depends on κ, with no estimation or tuning guidance.  
- No experiments are provided to validate theory or study empirical robustness to κ/η misspecification.  
- Does not address or extend to non-strongly convex (plain convex) settings.  
- Lacks clear comparison to prior hybrid deterministic–stochastic work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).  
- Missing empirical comparisons to standard baselines (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.).  
- Originality: algorithmic mixing is novel in this form, but the general idea of mixing deterministic and stochastic steps is not new and is under-discussed.  
- Strengths: novel algorithmic formulation, significant potential contribution, and solid theoretical analysis.  
- Weaknesses / reasons against acceptance: reliance on known κ, missing η estimation, no experiments or baseline comparisons, and limited scope to strongly convex problems.  
- Recommended improvements: provide methods to estimate/adapt κ, develop heuristics for η, add empirical comparisons to prior work and baselines, extend analysis to plain convex settings, and include experiments validating theory.
