- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Main idea: reduce number of full gradients and make iteration complexity independent of condition number.  
- Algorithm built on epoch gradient descent using mixed full and stochastic updates.  
- Provides a simple, self-contained convergence proof showing linear convergence with high-probability guarantees.  
- Theoretical novelty: mixed-gradient approach for constrained smooth strongly-convex optimization.  
- Complexity comparison: EMGD outperforms Nesterov when condition number ≤ n^(2/3).  
- SAG often outperforms EMGD and matches it near condition number ≈ n^(1/2).  
- Lacks empirical experiments or concrete examples to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Requires fixing epoch length a priori, which needs knowledge of the condition number — less adaptive than SAG/SDCA.  
- No automatic/adaptive step-size mechanism; step-size adaptivity is a practical concern.  
- Paper should compare and cite related hybrid deterministic–stochastic work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).  
- Needs more detailed comparisons to existing methods and discussion of EMGD’s limitations and drawbacks.  
- Clarity issues: add high-level intuition for proof choices, clarify Table 1 citations/assumptions, fix notation inconsistencies and typos.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and theoretically significant with an original, simple proof.  
- For acceptance: novelty and rigorous analysis are strong arguments; against acceptance: lack of experiments and practical adaptivity concerns.
