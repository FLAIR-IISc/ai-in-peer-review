- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), an algorithm interleaving full and stochastic gradient steps for smooth, strongly convex problems.
- Core claim: achieves linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, aiming to reduce cost for ill-conditioned problems.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly presented, and situates EMGD within relevant deterministic and stochastic optimization literature.
- Theoretical analysis is sound and reasonably rigorous, including a high-probability convergence guarantee.
- Key strength: novel algorithm bridging fast full-gradient rates and low per-iteration stochastic costs, with logarithmic dependence on 1/ε for full gradients.
- Critical weakness: limited practical relevance—EMGD outperforms batch GD only when κ ≤ n²/3, a restrictive regime for typical ML settings.
- Practical concern: in many ML problems μ (strong convexity) scales with data dimension (μ ≈ C·n), making EMGD advantageous only in narrow, uninteresting parameter ranges.
- Trade-off issue: EMGD provides high-probability (probabilistic) convergence versus batch GD’s deterministic convergence, which matters for high-precision applications.
- Authors should include a detailed discussion with concrete examples illustrating when EMGD is practically beneficial.
- Minor corrections needed: Eq.(6) should read ||w* − ŵ||²; clarify function in Eq.(7)/Line 288; remove equality sign in Line 286; use norms in Eq.(13); state L ≥ λ explicitly; fix symbols in Eq.(4) and note Lemma 1 implication; define κ = L/λ precisely.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel combined-gradient approach, strong theoretical foundation, high-probability guarantee, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted practical utility compared to batch GD, probabilistic vs deterministic trade-off, and insufficient empirical/practical comparison to batch GD.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and novel but practically questionable; recommend rejection unless authors expand practical discussion and provide empirical comparisons demonstrating real-world benefit.
