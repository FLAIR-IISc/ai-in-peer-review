- Proposes EMGD: an epoch-based hybrid using one full gradient plus stochastic steps per epoch, claiming O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients to reach ε-optimality.
- Addresses a relevant problem: reducing expensive full gradient evaluations for ill-conditioned problems.
- Theoretical analysis gives a clear high-probability linear convergence guarantee.
- Identifies an interesting trade-off between full and stochastic gradient use.
- Crucial assumption: requires knowing the condition number κ a priori with no practical estimation procedure provided.
- No empirical validation: the paper contains no experiments to support theoretical claims or show behavior under κ misspecification.
- Missing comparisons to prior hybrid methods and standard baselines (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012, SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA).
- Theoretical scope is limited to strongly convex problems, ignoring plain-convex or non-strongly convex settings common in practice.
- Lacks discussion relating EMGD to simple interleaving of deterministic and stochastic steps.
- Problem specification is confusing/misleading: oracle described as returning a random function f(·) but later treated as a finite-sum F(w)=(1/n)∑f_i(w).
- Technical analysis appears sound under the stated assumptions but practical limitations undermine impact.
- Paper is generally well-written but Section 3.1 and the algorithm context are misleading or under-explained.
- Originality is modest: the hybrid idea is somewhat novel but not properly situated with prior work.
- Significance is limited by reliance on known κ and lack of empirical support; claimed advantages over SAG/SDCA are unsubstantiated.
- Pro arguments for acceptance: addresses a meaningful problem, provides technically sound analysis, and proposes a novel mixing strategy.
- Con arguments against acceptance: impractical κ assumption, no experiments, no baseline/prior comparisons, restriction to strongly convex case, and confusing problem setup.
- Suggested fixes: provide κ estimation guidance, add experiments (including under κ misspecification), compare with prior hybrids and baselines, and clarify the problem/oracle setup.
