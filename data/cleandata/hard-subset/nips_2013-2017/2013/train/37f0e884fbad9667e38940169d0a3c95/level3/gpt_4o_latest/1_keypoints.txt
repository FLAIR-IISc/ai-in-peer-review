- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradient steps within epochs to cut full-gradient evaluations to O(log(1/ε)) independent of condition number κ.  
- Targets the high computational cost of traditional methods that need O(√κ log(1/ε)) full-gradient evaluations for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: self-contained, simple convergence proof showing constant suboptimality decrease independent of κ.  
- Offers high-probability convergence guarantees and handles constrained optimization, making it more general than SAG.  
- Uses less storage and has potential advantages for distributed computing.  
- Lacks adaptive step-size selection and may be slower in regimes with large κ.  
- Major drawback: no experimental results to validate practical performance.  
- Fixed number of steps per epoch limits adaptability compared to SAG or SDCA.  
- Paper is well-written but could expand high-level motivation and intuition behind the proof for broader accessibility.  
- Novel combination of existing techniques and a simpler proof framework, but practical significance is unclear without empirical evidence.  
- Suggested improvements: add experiments comparing to existing methods, discuss adaptive step-size and dynamic κ estimation, expand discussion of limitations/motivation, and fix minor typos/notation inconsistencies.  
- Conclusion: valuable theoretical advance but practical impact unproven; acceptance depends on conference emphasis on theory vs. experiments.
