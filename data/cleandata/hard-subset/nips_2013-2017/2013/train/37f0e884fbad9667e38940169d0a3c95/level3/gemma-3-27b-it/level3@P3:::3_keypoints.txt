- Proposes a novel hybrid stochastic/batch gradient algorithm (EMGD) that interleaves full and stochastic gradients to reduce dependence on condition number κ.  
- Claims convergence of O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical analysis is largely correct, with a reasonably rigorous, induction-based proof structure.  
- Algorithm 1 is clearly presented and easy to follow.  
- Provides comparisons of EMGD complexity against Nesterov, SAG, and SDCA.  
- Major practical concern: EMGD’s benefit requires the restrictive regime N = O(C^3/L^3), unlikely in typical ML problems.  
- Method seems only useful for extremely ill-conditioned problems with small datasets, a rare combination.  
- EMGD guarantees convergence only with high probability, unlike deterministic batch gradient descent.  
- Authors need to justify accepting probabilistic guarantees over deterministic ones.  
- Insufficient empirical/theoretical comparison and discussion of when EMGD is practically better than batch GD.  
- Paper omits discussion of overhead from managing stochastic gradient evaluations.  
- Minor technical issues to fix: Equation (6) norm, specify function at line 288, remove equality at line 286, use norms in Eq. (13), explicitly state L ≥ λ, correct notation in Eq. (4), and state Lemma 1 implication.  
- Condition number κ should be explicitly defined as κ = L/λ.  
- Paper is technically sound and original but its practical significance is unclear.  
- Recommendation: lean towards rejection unless authors strengthen practical relevance and fix minor technical errors; reconsider if realistic scenarios where EMGD outperforms batch GD are demonstrated.
