- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid full + stochastic gradient method for smooth, strongly convex problems.
- Main theoretical claim: with high probability an ε-optimal solution uses O(log(1/ε)) full gradient calls independent of condition number κ.
- Requires O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient calls to achieve that rate.
- Strength: novel objective of making expensive full-gradient calls κ-independent, valuable for ill-conditioned problems.
- Strength: provides a high-probability guarantee, stronger than typical in-expectation results.
- Critical weakness: assumes κ is known and sets key hyperparameter η using κ, with no estimation procedure or robustness analysis.
- Major omission: no empirical evaluation—no experiments to validate claims or study sensitivity to η.
- Needs empirical comparisons to baselines (SGD, SAG, SDCA, other hybrid methods) to demonstrate practical merit.
- Related work section is incomplete, notably omitting prior hybrid methods such as Friedlander & Schmidt (2012).
- Analysis is restricted to the strongly convex setting and is unclear about finite-sum vs general stochastic problem formulation.
- Overall: interesting theoretical direction but premature—must address κ estimation and add thorough experimental validation.
