- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) combining one full gradient per epoch with variance-reducing stochastic updates to reduce expensive full gradient calls.
- Theoretical claim: EMGD finds ε-optimal solution with O(log 1/ε) full gradients and O(κ² log 1/ε) stochastic gradients, making full-gradient count independent of κ.
- Strength — Significance & originality: Tackles costly full-gradient computations for ill-conditioned problems and achieves linear convergence in full-gradient calls.
- Strength — Theory: Provides a rigorous, high-probability convergence proof using an epoch-based scheme with a shrinking domain.
- Strength — Algorithm design: Introduces a “mixed gradient” g̃kt = ∇F(w̄k) + ∇fkt(wkt) − ∇fkt(w̄k) as an effective variance-reduction anchor per epoch.
- Weakness — Dependence on unknown parameters: Requires knowledge of κ (L and λ) for step size and inner iterations (T), with no guidance when κ is unknown.
- Weakness — No experiments: Paper contains no empirical results to validate theory, compare to baselines, or test robustness to hyperparameter misestimation.
- Weakness — Missing related work: Omits discussion of prior hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and similar literature.
- Weakness — Ambiguous problem setting: Mixes general stochastic approximation setup with comparisons to finite-sum methods (SAG/SDCA) without clarifying target domain.
- Weakness — Limited scope: Analysis is restricted to the strongly convex case with no discussion of extensions to non-strongly convex problems.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach to an important problem, strong theoretical result, and potentially useful epoch-based variance-reduction techniques.
- Arguments against acceptance: Reliance on known κ, absence of experiments, missing related work, and unclear problem framing undermine practical relevance.
- Overall: Interesting theoretical contribution but weakened by unrealistic assumptions and lack of empirical validation.
