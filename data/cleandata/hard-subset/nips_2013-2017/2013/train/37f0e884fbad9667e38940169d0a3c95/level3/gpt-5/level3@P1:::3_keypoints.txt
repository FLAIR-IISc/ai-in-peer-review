- Proposal: Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), alternating rare full-gradient evaluations with frequent stochastic-gradient updates within shrinking Euclidean balls.  
- Main theoretical result: Achieves linear convergence to ε-accuracy with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradients (κ = L/λ).  
- Mechanism: Mixed gradient g̃t = ∇F(w̄) + ∇f(wt) − ∇f(w̄) has bounded variance on shrinking domains, yielding constant reduction per epoch.  
- Relation to prior work: Differs from accelerated full-gradient (O(√κ log(1/ε))) and finite-sum variance-reduction (SAG/SDCA) by requiring fewer full gradients and handling constrained, general stochastic oracles.  
- Novelty: New mixed-gradient + epoch/shrinking-ball design, high-probability linear rate for general stochastic oracles, and condition-number-independent full-gradient count.  
- Quality of analysis: Careful and largely correct proof using strong convexity, smoothness, gradient mapping, and martingale concentration.  
- Key inconsistency to fix: Abstract/intro claim O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic steps conflicts with theorem implying O(κ^2 log(1/ε)); theorem’s κ^2 is correct.  
- Clarity issues to address: Define κ = L/λ early and state L ≥ λ; fix multiple notational errors (Eq.6 norm, Eq.13 norms, Eq.4 x̂→ŵ and f→F, line 286, specify function in Eq.7).  
- Practical impact concerns: High stochastic-gradient cost O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) may limit gains for ill-conditioned problems and typical ML settings where strong convexity comes from regularization.  
- Empirical/quantitative gaps: Needs concrete wall-clock trade-offs and experiments comparing to accelerated FG and SAG/SDCA under realistic parameterizations (e.g., logistic regression).  
- Implementation requirements: Requires knowing or estimating κ (and T, ∆1); discuss practical strategies for setting these.  
- Pros: Rigorous, novel approach; few full-gradients; high-probability guarantees; handles constraints and uses low memory.  
- Cons: Large stochastic complexity, need κ estimation, notation/inconsistency issues, limited discussion and no experiments.  
- Suggestions: Reconcile κ vs κ^2 everywhere, provide quantitative thresholds and experiments, discuss κ/∆1 estimation, and fix notational/clarity errors.  
- Overall recommendation: Theoretical and original but borderline due to practical significance and presentation issues; acceptance would be strengthened by fixing inconsistencies and adding empirical/quantitative comparisons.
