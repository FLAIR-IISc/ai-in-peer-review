- Contribution: Introduces EMGD, a hybrid algorithm combining full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex optimization to reduce cost on ill-conditioned problems.
- Theoretical result: EMGD achieves an ε-optimal solution with O(log 1/ε) full gradients and O(κ^2 log 1/ε) stochastic gradients.
- Analysis quality: Theoretical analysis is sound and builds on established stochastic and deterministic optimization results.
- Probabilistic guarantee: Provides a high-probability convergence guarantee rather than only expected convergence.
- Trade-off strength: Offers a potentially valuable trade-off between expensive full-gradient evaluations and cheap stochastic-gradient evaluations.
- Relation to prior work: Connects appropriately to related methods (SAG, SDCA, epoch GD) and includes a useful comparison table.
- Practical limitation: Applicability is limited because EMGD outperforms full gradient descent only when κ ≤ n^{2/3}, a restrictive condition.
- Regularization implication: Under typical regularization (κ ≈ C/λ), the required λ ≈ C^3/n^3 is unrealistic in common ML settings, reducing practical relevance.
- Baseline concern: Batch gradient descent’s deterministic linear convergence may be preferable in many applications, questioning EMGD’s advantage.
- Clarity issues (notation): Multiple notation and equation errors need fixing (e.g., eqs (4),(6),(7),(13); remove equality in line 286; use norms instead of absolute values).
- Clarity suggestions: Explicitly state L ≥ λ and precisely define condition number κ as a function of λ and L.
- Originality: Novel in its specific combination of full and stochastic gradients within an epoch framework despite building on prior techniques.
- Related work coverage: Related work section is comprehensive and well-situated.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel algorithm, rigorous theory, useful trade-off for specific problems, high-probability guarantee, comprehensive related work.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited practical relevance, weak justification for preferring EMGD over standard batch GD, unclear benefits relative to existing large-scale methods.
- Recommendation: Overall recommendation is rejection pending substantial revision and stronger empirical or analytic evidence showing practical scenarios where EMGD clearly outperforms alternatives.
