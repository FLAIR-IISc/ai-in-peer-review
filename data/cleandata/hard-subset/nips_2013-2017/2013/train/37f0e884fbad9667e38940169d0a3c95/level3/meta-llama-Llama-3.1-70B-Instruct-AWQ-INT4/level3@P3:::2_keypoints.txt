- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid method using full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Claims linear convergence with high probability while reducing full-gradient calls from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)).  
- Algorithm proceeds in epochs, each using one full gradient and O(κ^2) stochastic gradients per epoch.  
- Theoretical result: ε-optimal solution with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Main contribution: a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method achieving high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence.  
- Analysis crucially assumes the condition number κ is known a priori.  
- Requires hyperparameter η that depends on κ, with no estimation procedure or heuristics provided.  
- No experiments are included to validate theory or study empirical behavior under κ misspecification.  
- Strength: novel combination of full and stochastic gradients with rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Weakness: no treatment of unknown or misspecified κ, a common practical scenario.  
- Weakness: limited to strongly convex settings; non-strongly convex cases are not considered.  
- Weakness: no empirical comparisons to standard baselines (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.).  
- Originality: approach is novel but not a large departure from prior hybrid deterministic–stochastic methods.  
- Literature: paper could better review prior related work (e.g., Friedlander and Schmidt 2012).  
- Significance: results are important but practically limited by assumptions and lack of experiments.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel algorithm, rigorous theory, potential computational benefits.  
- Rejection arguments: lacks analysis for unknown κ, lacks experiments, limited problem scope, and missing baseline comparisons.  
- Recommended improvements: provide analysis for unknown/misspecified κ, include experiments, extend to non-strongly convex settings, compare to standard baselines, and expand literature review.
