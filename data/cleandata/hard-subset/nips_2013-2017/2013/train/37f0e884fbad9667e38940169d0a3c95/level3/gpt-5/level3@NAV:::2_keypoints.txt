- Proposal: EMGD mixes one full gradient per epoch with many stochastic gradients using g̃ = ∇F(w̄) + ∇f(w) − ∇f(w̄).
- Claim: For smooth, strongly convex objectives, shows high-probability linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and either O(κ·log(1/ε)) (abstract) or O(κ^2·log(1/ε)) (Theorem 1) stochastic gradients — inconsistency noted.
- Positioning: Sits between deterministic accelerated methods and stochastic variance-reduction approaches; related to Friedlander & Schmidt (2012), SAG, SDCA.
- Strength: Clear goal to reduce full-gradient calls while retaining linear convergence.
- Strength: Provides a high-probability guarantee and uses a simple, memory-light update that handles constraints.
- Strength: Epoch structure is conceptually clean and easy to implement.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on a priori knowledge of κ (L and λ); η and epoch length T depend on κ with no estimation or robustness analysis.
- Weakness: No experiments — no empirical validation, no misspecification study, and no comparisons to standard baselines (SGD variants, SAG/SVRG/SAGA, SDCA).
- Weakness: Ambiguous problem setting — mixes stochastic approximation and finite-sum contexts and gives a misleading oracle model.
- Weakness: Incomplete related work discussion; prior hybrid methods and modern variance-reduction literature not thoroughly positioned.
- Weakness: Noted inconsistency between abstract (O(κ)) and Theorem 1/T (O(κ^2)) stochastic-gradient complexity.
- Weakness: No comparison or ablation against simple interleaving of deterministic and stochastic steps.
- Weakness: Only treats the strongly convex case; no discussion of extensions to plain convex problems.
- Quality/Clarity: Mathematically promising but incomplete; some imprecise statements and unclear exposition around oracle model and κ-dependence.
- Originality: Moderately novel combination but lacks strong differentiation from existing hybrid/variance-reduction ideas.
- Suggestions: Add adaptive/parameter-free variants (doubling/backtracking, online L/λ estimation) and robustness analysis under κ misspecification.
- Suggestions: Provide comprehensive experiments on finite-sum problems versus SGD, averaged SGD, SAG/SVRG/SAGA, SDCA, and an interleaving baseline.
- Suggestions: Explore non-strongly convex extensions and fix the O(κ) vs O(κ^2) inconsistency.
- Arguments for acceptance: Clean idea with high-probability linear convergence and reduced full-gradient reliance; low-memory and applicable to constrained problems.
- Arguments against acceptance: No experiments, impractical dependence on known κ, incomplete related work, unclear setting, and inconsistency in complexity claims.
- Overall verdict: Lean reject in current form; could become competitive with adaptivity and solid empirical validation.
