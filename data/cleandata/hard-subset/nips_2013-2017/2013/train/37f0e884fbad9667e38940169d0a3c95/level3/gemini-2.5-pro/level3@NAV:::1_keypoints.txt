- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): one full gradient per epoch plus many cheap stochastic steps for constrained smooth strongly-convex optimization.  
- Aims for linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls independent of condition number κ, at cost of O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic calls.  
- Positions EMGD within hybrid/variance-reduced methods (e.g., SAG, SVRG, SCDA) seeking fast linear rates for finite-sum problems.  
- Main strength: novel algorithmic blend for constrained problems with a simple, self-contained convergence proof.  
- Provides a high-probability convergence guarantee, stronger than typical in-expectation results (e.g., SAG).  
- Claims practical advantages over SAG/SCDA: applicability to constrained settings, potentially lower memory use, and easier parallelization.  
- Major weakness: no experiments — prevents assessment of practical relevance and empirical complexity tradeoffs.  
- Theoretical performance claims (e.g., outperforming Nesterov when κ ≤ n^(2/3), matching SAG when κ ≈ n^(1/2)) are unvalidated empirically.  
- Practical limitation: epoch length T must be fixed a priori and requires knowledge of κ, unlike adaptive methods.  
- Literature omission: fails to discuss the relevant hybrid deterministic-stochastic method by Friedlander & Schmidt (2012).  
- Needs expanded comparisons in Section 3.4 and better motivation of real ML settings where EMGD is preferred.  
- Recommendation: technically sound and novel; suggest acceptance conditional on adding experiments and substantially revising discussion of limitations and prior work.
