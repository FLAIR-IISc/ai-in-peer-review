- Introduces EMGD, a hybrid algorithm combining stochastic and full gradients for smooth, strongly convex optimization.  
- Reduces full-gradient dependence from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)) by leveraging stochastic gradients.  
- Presents a rigorous convergence proof and shows linear convergence with high probability.  
- Applicable to constrained optimization problems, unlike SAG and SDCA.  
- Requires only O(d) storage, more practical for high-dimensional problems than O(n) methods.  
- High-probability convergence guarantee is stronger than some expectation-based competitors.  
- Relies on strong convexity, limiting relevance to many real-world machine learning problems.  
- Practical speedup is constrained to the regime κ ≤ n^{2/3}; outside this, other methods may be faster.  
- Probabilistic convergence lacks deterministic guarantees, potentially reducing robustness in practice.  
- Requires prior knowledge of the condition number κ, which may be difficult to obtain.  
- Some equations and notation are unclear and need minor corrections for readability.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss relevance to non-strongly convex ML problems, propose κ-estimation methods, compare probabilistic vs deterministic rates, and clarify notation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions if clarity and applicability limitations are addressed.
