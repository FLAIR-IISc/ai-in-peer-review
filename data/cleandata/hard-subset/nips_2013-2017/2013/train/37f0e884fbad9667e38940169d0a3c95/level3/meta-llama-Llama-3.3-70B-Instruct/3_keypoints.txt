- Proposes GREEDI, a novel distributed protocol for submodular maximization under cardinality constraints.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis showing GREEDI can achieve solutions competitive with centralized greedy under natural conditions.  
- Presents extensive experiments (exemplar-based clustering, active set selection) demonstrating effectiveness and scalability on large-scale datasets.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized, making the approach easy to follow.  
- Theoretical results are rigorous and offer valuable insights into algorithm performance.  
- Experimental results are impressive and validate scalability and effectiveness.  
- Assumes the submodular function is provided as a black box, which may not hold in practice.  
- Assumes monotonicity and submodularity of the function, limiting applicability to non-monotone/non-submodular cases.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison with existing distributed submodular maximization algorithms.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, significant, and effective approach that could make a valuable contribution with minor revisions.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
