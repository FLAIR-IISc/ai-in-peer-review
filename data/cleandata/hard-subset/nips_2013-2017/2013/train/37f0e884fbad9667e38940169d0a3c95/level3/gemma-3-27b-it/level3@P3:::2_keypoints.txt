- Proposal: EMGD, a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that interleaves full and stochastic gradient steps to achieve O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient evaluations independent of κ.
- Theoretical strength: rigorous analysis showing κ-independent full-gradient complexity under the paper’s assumptions.
- Motivation: addresses expensive full-gradient computations in ill-conditioned problems, relevant for ML applications.
- Critical flaw: requires the condition number κ to be known a priori, with step size η depending on κ, and provides no guidance for estimating κ.
- Sensitivity unaddressed: no analysis or experiments on algorithm behavior when η/κ are misspecified.
- No empirical validation: lacks simulations or real-world experiments and comparisons to baselines like SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA.
- Presentation issues: unclear problem setting (finite-sum vs. stochastic), misleading wording in Section 3.1, and limited discussion of the chosen mixing strategy.
- Justification of mixing unclear: paper doesn’t explain why its specific interleaving is necessary or superior to simple alternation.
- Related work omission: fails to adequately cite and compare to prior hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012), weakening novelty claims.
- Limited scope: analysis only for strongly convex problems, ignoring plain convex settings where ill-conditioning also matters.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical result (κ-independent full-gradient complexity), rigorous analysis, and relevant motivation.
- Arguments against acceptance: fatal known-κ assumption, no experiments, poor comparison to simpler strategies, missing related work, restricted setting, unclear exposition.
- Overall recommendation: reject; authors should develop κ-estimation/robustness heuristics and provide thorough empirical comparisons before resubmission.
