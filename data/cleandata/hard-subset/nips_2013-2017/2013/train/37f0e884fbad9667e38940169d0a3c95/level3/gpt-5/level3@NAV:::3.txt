Summary: The paper proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid first-order method that interleaves occasional full-gradient evaluations with frequent stochastic-gradient steps. EMGD operates in epochs, computing one full gradient per epoch and performing mixed-gradient updates over a shrinking ball, yielding linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients with high probability. This positions EMGD between deterministic accelerated methods (e.g., Nesterov) with O(√κ log(1/ε)) full gradients and variance-reduced/incremental methods such as SAG/SDCA that achieve linear rates under finite-sum structure. The mixed-gradient construction and shrinking domain are the main technical devices.

Quality: The convergence analysis appears sound and, to my knowledge, novel in how it couples a single full gradient per epoch with controlled stochastic corrections. The high-probability guarantee is clearly stated. That said, the paper needs a careful, quantitative comparison to batch GD/accelerated GD in regimes relevant to ML. Under the common ML scaling where F is a sum (not an average), the strong convexity parameter from regularization can scale as μ ≈ C·N; then EMGD is faster than batch GD only if N = O(C^3/L^3), which seems an uninteresting regime. Moreover, EMGD’s convergence is high-probability while batch GD’s is deterministic; this trade-off should be discussed. Please also reconcile the text stating O(κ log(1/ε)) vs Theorem 1’s O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.

Clarity: Generally well written but with several fixable issues: (i) Eq. (6) should be ‖w* − ŵ‖^2. (ii) Specify which function is used in line 288 for Eq. (7). (iii) Remove the equality in line 286 (it obscures the flow). (iv) In (13) replace absolute values with norms. (v) Explicitly state/explain that L ≥ λ (even if obvious). (vi) In (4) x̂ should be ŵ, f should be F; the first term in the max is always larger by Lemma 1. (vii) Precisely define κ = L/λ and use consistently throughout.

Originality: The hybrid update and proof technique are original relative to classical semi-stochastic schemes and epoch GD.

Significance: Potentially limited for mainstream ML unless the authors establish realistic regimes where wall-clock gains over batch GD/accelerated GD occur. Empirical validation and a nuanced complexity/cost model (full vs stochastic gradient cost, constants) would strengthen the case.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel, technically solid analysis; simple algorithm.
- High-probability linear convergence with few full gradients.
- Works with constraints and modest memory.

Cons against acceptance:
- Unclear practical advantage over batch GD in typical ML scaling.
- High-probability vs deterministic guarantees.
- Minor inconsistencies and clarity issues; no experiments.
