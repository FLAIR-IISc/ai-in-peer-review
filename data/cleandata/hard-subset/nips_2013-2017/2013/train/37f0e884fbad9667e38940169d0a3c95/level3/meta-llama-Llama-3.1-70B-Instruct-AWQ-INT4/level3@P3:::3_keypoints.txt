- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid stochastic/full-gradient SGD algorithm.  
- Proves linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients to reach ε-optimality.  
- Paper is well-written with clear algorithm description and thorough related-work review.  
- Hybrid approach combining full and stochastic gradients is interesting and potentially computationally beneficial.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous; proof of Theorem 1 is technically strong.  
- Concern: relevance to ML is questionable because strong convexity from regularization implies μ ≈ C·N, making speedup over batch GD only in restrictive regimes (N = O(C^3/L^3)).  
- Concern: convergence guarantee is high-probability rather than deterministic, unlike batch GD.  
- Recommendation: include a more careful comparison to batch GD and justify practical advantages for ML.  
- Recommendation: provide more experimental results to demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Minor corrections needed: eq.(6) should be ||w^* - ŵ||^2; specify which function in line 288 for eq.(7); remove equality in line 286 for clarity.  
- Originality: novel hybrid SGD approach is a significant contribution but key insights and differences from prior work should be highlighted more.  
- Overall: technically sound and clear, but needs stronger discussion of ML relevance and clearer emphasis on innovations.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel hybrid method and thorough convergence analysis.  
- Rejection arguments: limited ML relevance, high-probability only convergence, and insufficient discussion/experiments.
