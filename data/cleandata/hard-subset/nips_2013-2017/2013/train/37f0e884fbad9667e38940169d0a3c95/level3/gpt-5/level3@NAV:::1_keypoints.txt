- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): one full gradient per epoch plus many cheap stochastic steps, proving linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) epochs.
- Claims total stochastic-gradient calls are O(κ2 log(1/ε)) but paper inconsistently sometimes states O(κ log(1/ε)); this discrepancy must be reconciled.
- Main theoretical advantage: epoch count independent of condition number κ, reducing expensive full-gradient evaluations versus accelerated methods.
- Extends to constrained, strongly convex, smooth problems and provides a high-probability linear rate (vs. in-expectation for some competitors).
- Analysis is technically solid and streamlined but relies on shrinking-radius constrained sets and fixed per-epoch length.
- Practical evaluation is lacking: no experiments, no tuning guidance, and limited discussion on when the κ2 stochastic cost is advantageous.
- Needs explicit statement of assumptions (strong convexity, smoothness, solvability of projections, domain/ norm conventions).
- Section 3.4 requires expansion with apples-to-apples comparisons (cost models, storage, constraints) and clearer citations/assumptions in Table 1 (e.g., SDCA applicability).
- Must cite and contrast related hybrid/semi-stochastic work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and other relevant methods.
- Notation and presentation issues: fix typos, consistent domain/norm/log notation, and clarify use of the gradient-expectation identity.
- Novelty: simple mixed full/stochastic-gradient scheme for constrained strongly convex problems with a clean high-probability linear rate.
- Theoretical significance: decouples full-gradient calls from κ and offers a low-memory alternative.
- Empirical/significance unclear: no experiments; in finite-sum settings EMGD outperforms Nesterov when κ ≤ n^{2/3}, but SAG/SDCA often dominate and match near κ ≈ n^{1/2}.
- Recommend discussing realistic ML regimes (e.g., regularization ~ C/n) where EMGD is competitive.
- Pros for acceptance: strong/simple high-probability proof; κ-independent full-gradient count; handles constraints with low memory.
- Cons against acceptance: no experiments; missing related-work comparisons; fixed epoch length and non-adaptive step-size require knowledge of κ; κ vs κ2 inconsistency and clarity issues.
- Recommendation: technically sound and potentially impactful; accept conditional on adding experiments, resolving the κ discrepancy, expanding comparisons/citations, and fixing clarity/notation.
