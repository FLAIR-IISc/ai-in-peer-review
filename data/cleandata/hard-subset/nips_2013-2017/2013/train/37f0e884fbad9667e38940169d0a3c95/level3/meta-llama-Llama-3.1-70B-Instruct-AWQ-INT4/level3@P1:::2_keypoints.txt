- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a novel hybrid algorithm combining full and stochastic gradients with epoch-based mixed steps.  
- Theoretical guarantee: achieves an ǫ-optimal solution using O(log 1/ǫ) full gradients and O(κ^2 log 1/ǫ) stochastic gradients.  
- Main contribution: a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method with high-probability O(log(1/ε)) linear convergence for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Analysis requires knowing the condition number κ (L and λ) a priori, with no guidance for unknown or misspecified κ.  
- Algorithm needs a hyperparameter η that depends on κ, but no estimation procedure or practical heuristics are provided.  
- Paper contains no experiments to validate the theory or to assess empirical behavior under misspecification.  
- Does not address non-strongly convex settings, limiting practical applicability.  
- Well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the algorithm and theoretical analysis.  
- Originality limited: hybrid deterministic-stochastic idea is not entirely new and prior work comparisons are insufficient.  
- Significance is notable for providing O(log(1/ε)) convergence, but reduced by strong assumptions and lack of empirical validation.  
- Reviewer score: 7/10 — recognizes contribution but notes important limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution and clear exposition.  
- Arguments for rejection: reliance on known κ, no experiments, omission of non-strong convex cases, and inadequate comparison to prior hybrid methods.
