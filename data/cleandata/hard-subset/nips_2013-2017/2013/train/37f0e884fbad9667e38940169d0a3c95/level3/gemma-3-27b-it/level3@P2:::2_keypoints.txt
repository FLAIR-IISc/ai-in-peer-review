- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that interleaves full and stochastic gradients to reduce dependence on condition number κ, claiming O(log(1/ε)) iteration complexity with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients.  
- Addresses an important problem: ill-conditioned optimization is common and EMGD aims to balance iteration count and per-iteration cost.  
- Presentation and theoretical analysis are reasonably clear and provide a logarithmic convergence guarantee.  
- Connections to existing literature are made but some key related work is omitted.  
- Major practical limitation: algorithm assumes the condition number κ is known a priori.  
- No strategy or discussion for estimating κ or handling misspecification; lacks sensitivity analysis for η (dependent on κ).  
- Comparison to related work is incomplete—fails to discuss prior hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).  
- No experimental validation; lack of empirical comparisons to baselines like SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, and SDCA.  
- Fails to explore connection to simpler interleaving approaches or justify advantages of the proposed mixing strategy.  
- Problem setting is unclear: not specified whether F(w) is a finite-sum or general stochastic objective.  
- Scope is limited to strongly convex problems; no discussion or extension to plain convex settings.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel hybrid approach, clear theoretical results, tackles an important optimization issue.  
- Cons for acceptance: unrealistic κ assumption, no misspecification analysis, missing experiments, incomplete related work, limited applicability.  
- Overall assessment/recommendation: reject; authors should address κ estimation/misspecification and provide experimental validation.
