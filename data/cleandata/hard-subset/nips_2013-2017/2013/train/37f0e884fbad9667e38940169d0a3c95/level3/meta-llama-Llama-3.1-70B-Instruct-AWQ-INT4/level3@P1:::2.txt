The paper "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients" proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), which combines full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The algorithm divides the optimization process into epochs, with each epoch consisting of a mixed gradient descent step that uses both full and stochastic gradients. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the algorithm, showing that it can achieve an ǫ-optimal solution by computing O(log 1/ǫ) full gradients and O(κ^2 log 1/ǫ) stochastic gradients.

The authors also provide a comparison of their algorithm with other optimization methods, including Nesterov's algorithm, Stochastic Average Gradient (SAG), and Stochastic Dual Coordinate Ascent (SDCA).

Secondly, the algorithm requires a hyperparameter η that depends on κ, but no estimation procedure or heuristics are provided. Finally, the paper does not consider non-strongly convex settings, which are important in practice.
