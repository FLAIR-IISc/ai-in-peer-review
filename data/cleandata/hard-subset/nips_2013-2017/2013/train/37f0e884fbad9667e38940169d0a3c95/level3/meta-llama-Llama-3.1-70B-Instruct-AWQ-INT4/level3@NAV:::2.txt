This paper proposes a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method, called Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The main theoretical claim is that EMGD can achieve high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence with a known condition number and hybrid oracle. The paper provides a detailed analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate and shows that it can reduce the number of full gradients needed from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)).

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm that combines the strengths of deterministic and stochastic gradient methods.
* The analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate is thorough and provides insights into the trade-offs between the number of full gradients and stochastic gradients.
* The paper provides a detailed comparison with existing algorithms and shows that EMGD can be more efficient in certain scenarios.

Weaknesses:

* The analysis crucially assumes that the condition number κ is known a priori, which may not be the case in practice.
* The paper does not provide any analysis or guidance for cases where κ is unknown or misspecified.
* The algorithm requires a hyperparameter η that depends on κ, but no estimation procedure or heuristics are provided.
* The paper does not contain any experiments to validate the theory or study the empirical behavior under misspecification.
* The paper does not consider non-strongly convex settings, which are important in practice.
* The paper does not review and compare to prior hybrid deterministic-stochastic work.
* The paper does not provide a comparison to and experiments against standard baselines (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.).

Suggestions for improvement:

* Provide a more detailed analysis of the algorithm's behavior when κ is unknown or misspecified.
* Develop an estimation procedure or heuristics for the hyperparameter η.
* Include experiments to validate the theory and study the empirical behavior under misspecification.
* Consider non-strongly convex settings and provide a comparison to prior work in this area.
* Provide a more detailed comparison to and experiments against standard baselines.
