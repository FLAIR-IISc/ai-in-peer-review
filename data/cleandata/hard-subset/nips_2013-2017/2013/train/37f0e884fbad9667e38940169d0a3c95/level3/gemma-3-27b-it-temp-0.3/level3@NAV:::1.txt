## Review of "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients"

This paper introduces the Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) algorithm for smooth, strongly convex optimization, aiming to reduce the dependence on the condition number κ when full gradients are expensive. The core idea – interleaving full and stochastic gradient steps within each epoch – is a compelling approach to balance computational cost and convergence speed. The authors demonstrate that EMGD achieves linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls, a significant improvement over standard full-gradient methods when κ is large. 

The novelty lies in this specific mixing strategy for constrained smooth strongly-convex optimization, coupled with a relatively simple and self-contained convergence proof. The theoretical strength is indeed noteworthy, offering a high-probability guarantee of linear convergence, which is stronger than the in-expectation result provided by SAG. The advantage of EMGD working for constrained problems and potentially offering memory/parallelization benefits is also highlighted. 

However, the paper suffers from a significant lack of empirical validation. The absence of experiments or concrete examples severely limits the assessment of the algorithm’s practical relevance. While the theoretical analysis is strong, it's difficult to gauge whether the constant factors hidden within the complexity bounds render EMGD competitive in real-world scenarios.

Several areas require improvement. The discussion of limitations and drawbacks is missing; a more thorough comparison to existing methods (particularly Friedlander & Schmidt, 2012, which is a crucial omission) is needed in Section 3.4. The fixed epoch length requirement, unlike the adaptivity of SCDA and SAG, is a practical drawback that should be addressed. Furthermore, the paper lacks automatic/adaptive step-size mechanisms, a feature present in more practical algorithms. The authors should also clarify in what realistic machine learning settings (e.g., regularization strength) EMGD would demonstrably outperform SAG or Nesterov. 

Clarity could be improved by adding high-level intuition for key proof choices and clarifying citations/assumptions in Table 1 (e.g., SCDA applicability, gradient expectation identity). Minor manuscript fixes regarding notation consistency are also needed.

**Overall:** This paper presents a technically sound and theoretically significant contribution with a novel, simple proof. However, the lack of experimental validation and the missing discussion regarding limitations and practical considerations are substantial weaknesses. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing these concerns, ideally through experiments and a more comprehensive discussion in a rebuttal.
