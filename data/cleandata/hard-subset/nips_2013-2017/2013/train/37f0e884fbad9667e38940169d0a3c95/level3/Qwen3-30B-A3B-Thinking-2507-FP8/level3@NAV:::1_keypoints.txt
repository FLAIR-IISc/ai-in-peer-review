- Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm combining full and stochastic gradients for smooth strongly-convex optimization.  
- Core innovation: replace each full-gradient epoch with O(k²) mixed gradient steps to reduce full-gradient dependence.  
- Complexity: O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(k² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls.  
- Proves linear convergence with high-probability guarantees, stronger than SAG’s in-expectation result.  
- Provides a simple, self-contained convergence proof avoiding complex analyses in related work.  
- Extends stochastic optimization to constrained problems where SAG and SDCA do not apply, while retaining linear convergence.  
- High-probability guarantee is a meaningful theoretical improvement over existing methods.  
- Correctly analyzes regimes where EMGD outperforms Nesterov (κ ≤ n^(2/3)) and matches SAG (κ ≈ n^(1/2)).  
- Major limitation: no empirical validation—no experiments, benchmarks, or practical examples provided.  
- Fails to discuss practical limitations adequately, notably requiring κ known a priori and lacking adaptive step-size mechanisms.  
- Missing comparison to Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) and Section 3.4 needs substantial expansion for proper context.  
- Recommendation: add experiments, clarify realistic application scenarios (EMGD may not beat SAG with typical ML regularization), and expand limitations discussion.
