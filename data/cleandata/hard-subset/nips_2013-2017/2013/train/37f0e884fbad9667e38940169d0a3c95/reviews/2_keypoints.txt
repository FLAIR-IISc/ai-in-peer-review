- Proposes a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method mixing full and stochastic gradient oracles.  
- Main theoretical claim: with known condition number and hybrid oracle, one can achieve high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence.  
- Analysis crucially assumes the condition number κ (L and λ) is known a priori.  
- No analysis or guidance for cases where κ is unknown or misspecified.  
- The algorithm requires hyperparameter η that depends on κ, but no estimation procedure or heuristics are provided.  
- Paper contains no experiments to validate theory or study empirical behavior under misspecification.  
- Should analyze or empirically test convergence when η (κ) is set too large or too small.  
- Does not consider non-strongly convex settings (e.g., plain convex), which are important in practice.  
- Fails to review and compare to prior hybrid deterministic-stochastic work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).  
- Needs comparison to and experiments against standard baselines (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.).  
- Algorithm is presented as a “mixed” update but lacks discussion relating it to simple interleaving of deterministic and stochastic steps.  
- Optimization problem setting (finite-sum deterministic vs. stochastic approximation) is not clearly specified; Section 3.1 is misleading.
