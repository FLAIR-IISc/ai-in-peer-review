**Summary**

This paper presents a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), for optimizing a smooth and strongly convex function over a convex constraint set. The authors leverage a hybrid approach, combining expensive full gradient computations with cheaper stochastic gradient steps, to achieve a linear convergence rate. The algorithm requires O(log 1/eps) calls to the full gradient oracle and O(k^2 log(1/eps)) calls to the stochastic gradient oracle, where k is the condition number. The authors provide a self-contained proof of the convergence rate, which is independent of the condition number.

**Evaluation**

The paper is technically sound, and the idea of mixing full and stochastic gradient steps is novel and interesting. The algorithm is more general than the SAG algorithm, as it works for constrained optimization, and the proof is simpler. However, the paper lacks experimental results to demonstrate the practical relevance of the algorithm.

**Pros**

* Novel and interesting algorithmic idea
* Clean, simple, and solid theory
* More general than SAG, as it works for constrained optimization
* Simpler proof than SAG

**Cons**

* No experimental results to demonstrate practical relevance
* No discussion of limitations and drawbacks of the algorithm
* Proof lacks high-level comments to justify essential insights
* No concrete discussion of the sum of n functions example

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, but some experiments would have been appreciated. The authors should extend section 3.4 to discuss the limitations and drawbacks of the algorithm. They should also add a more concrete discussion of the sum of n functions example.

**Suggestions**

* Add high-level comments to the proof to justify essential insights
* Discuss the limitations and drawbacks of the algorithm, including the need to fix the number of steps within an epoch in advance
* Compare the algorithm to SAG and SCDA, highlighting the advantages and disadvantages of each
* Provide a more concrete discussion of the sum of n functions example
* Correct typos and notation inconsistencies

**Addendum**

* The authors should point out that the regularization parameter is often C/n in practice, which affects the condition number and the performance of the algorithm
* The authors should cite [Hybrid Deterministic-Stochastic Methods for Data Fitting. M. Friedlander, M. Schmidt. SISC, 2012], which presents a hybrid deterministic-stochastic algorithm with a linear rate of convergence.

**Clarity**

The paper is fairly clear, but the proof could be improved with high-level comments to justify essential insights. The notation should be consistent throughout the paper.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel combination of known techniques.

**Significance**

The practical relevance of the algorithm is not demonstrated yet, but the theoretical contribution could have impact. The simple proof of EMGD is a major contribution, especially in comparison to the difficult proof of convergence of SAG.
