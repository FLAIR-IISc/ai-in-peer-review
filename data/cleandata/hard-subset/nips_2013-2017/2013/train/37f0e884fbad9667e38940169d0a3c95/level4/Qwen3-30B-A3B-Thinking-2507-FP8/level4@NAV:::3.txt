

The paper introduces a novel hybrid stochastic/batch gradient descent strategy that combines both stochastic and batch gradients. The algorithm achieves convergence to an ε-accurate solution using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients. The convergence proof appears correct and novel, with minor errors noted below.  

My primary concern pertains to the algorithm's relevance for machine learning, the conference's focus area. In standard ML settings, strong convexity is induced by regularization, yielding μ = Θ(N) (i.e., μ = CN for constant C independent of N). Under this assumption, the proposed method outperforms batch gradient descent only when N = O(C³/L³), a regime unlikely to be practically meaningful. Additionally, the proposed method's convergence rate holds only with high probability, whereas batch gradient descent guarantees deterministic convergence. This critical point must be thoroughly addressed to demonstrate a genuine advantage over batch gradient descent and justify the paper's relevance to the ML community.
