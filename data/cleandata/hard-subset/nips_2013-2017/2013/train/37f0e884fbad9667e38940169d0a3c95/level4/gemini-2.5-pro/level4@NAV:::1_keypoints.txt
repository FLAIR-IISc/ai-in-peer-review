- Paper addresses optimizing smooth, strongly convex functions using a hybrid of occasional full gradients and many cheap stochastic gradients (Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent, EMGD).  
- EMGD requires only O(log(1/eps)) full-gradient calls (independent of condition number k) and O(k^2 log(1/eps)) stochastic calls by averaging O(k^2) mixed steps per epoch.  
- Theoretical proof is simple, self-contained, and yields high-probability linear convergence; no experimental validation is provided.  
- EMGD can be faster than Nesterov when k is not too large, but its stochastic cost scales as k^2.  
- Compared to SAG, EMGD is more general (applies to constrained problems), has a simpler proof, and gives high-probability guarantees, but is asymptotically slower in most regimes.  
- In the “sum of n functions” setting, EMGD is better than Nesterov for k ≤ n^(2/3) but worse than SAG for most practical parameter ranges (SAG dominates when k ≈ n/C).  
- Two main advantages over SAG: applicability to constrained problems and much simpler, more insightful convergence proof.  
- Major drawback: no experiments or concrete examples to demonstrate practical impact or regimes where EMGD is preferable.  
- Paper lacks thorough discussion of limitations and practical drawbacks (Section 3.4 needs expansion).  
- Practical limitation: EMGD requires pre-specifying the inner-loop length based on k, unlike SCDA and SAG which adapt and can exploit local conditioning.  
- Authors should add a concrete comparative analysis for the sum-of-n-functions example, detailing thresholds where EMGD, SAG, and Nesterov dominate.  
- Addendum: acknowledge common ML regime with regularization C/n (k ≈ n/C'), where SAG still outperforms Nesterov and EMGD; propose realistic settings where k < n^(2/3).  
- Include citation to Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods and clarify differences in condition-number dependence.  
- Proof would benefit from high-level commentary to motivate key quantities and make steps less “magical.”  
- Numerous minor editorial fixes and notation clarifications are recommended (specific line-by-line suggestions and typos listed).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel synthesis with potentially impactful theoretical contribution, contingent on adding limitations discussion and stronger comparative analysis; rebuttal was satisfactory pending revisions.
