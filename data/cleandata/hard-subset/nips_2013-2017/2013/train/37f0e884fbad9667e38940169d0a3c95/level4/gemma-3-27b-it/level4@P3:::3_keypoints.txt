- Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients to accelerate convex optimization.
- Claims EMGD finds an ε-optimal solution with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Theoretical analysis and convergence proof appear rigorous and sound.
- Paper is well-structured with clear motivation, algorithm details, and connections to prior work (e.g., Nesterov, SAG, SDCA).
- Inclusion of computational complexity comparison (Table 2) is helpful for context.
- Major practical weakness: limited relevance to large-scale ML since κ often scales with sample size N, requiring N to be small for EMGD to outperform batch methods.
- Authors should better explore scenarios where EMGD is beneficial (e.g., special data structure or very expensive full gradients).
- Probabilistic convergence guarantees are less desirable than deterministic batch-gradient guarantees, and the cost of high-probability assurances needs justification.
- Need clearer discussion of the trade-off between full and stochastic gradient costs in practical settings.
- Minor corrections: Eq. (6) should read ||w* - ŵ||²; clarify which function (7) refers to at line 288; remove equality at line 286 for readability.
- Minor corrections: in (13) replace absolute values with norms; explicitly state L ≥ λ; in (4) replace x^ with w^ and f with F and clarify the strong-convexity assumption behind the max-term claim.
- Condition number κ = L/λ should be defined upfront for clarity.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, theoretically well-supported, interesting full vs stochastic gradient trade-off, rigorous analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable practical impact for large-scale ML, probabilistic vs deterministic trade-off, insufficient discussion of outperforming scenarios.
- Overall assessment: technically solid but limited practical impact as presented; recommend weak reject and substantial expansion of practical implications and use-case demonstrations.
