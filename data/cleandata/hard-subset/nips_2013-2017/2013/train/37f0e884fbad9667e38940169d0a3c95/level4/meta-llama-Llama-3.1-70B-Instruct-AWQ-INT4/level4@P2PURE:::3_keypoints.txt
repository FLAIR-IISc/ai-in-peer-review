- Proposes a novel hybrid stochastic/batch SGD with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients to reach ε-accuracy.
- Convergence proof appears correct and novel, aside from minor errors noted below.
- Major concern: relevance to ML — with regularizer-based strong convexity (μ = C·N), the method only outperforms batch GD when N = O(C^3 / L^3), an impractical regime.
- Additional concern: proposed convergence is high-probability, whereas batch GD has a deterministic convergence rate.
- Paper must carefully discuss and justify any practical advantage over batch GD to establish ML relevance.
- Minor fix: Equation (6) should read ||w* − ŵ||^2.
- Clarify which function uses equation (7) (line 288).
- Remove the equality in line 286 as it reduces clarity.
- In equation (13) replace absolute values with norms.
- Explicitly state that L ≥ λ for clarity.
- In equation (4) x^ should be ŵ and f should be F; note the first term in the max is always larger by Lemma 1.
- Define the condition number precisely as a function of λ and L.
- Overall summary: advantage of the hybrid algorithm over standard batch GD in practical ML optimization is unclear.
