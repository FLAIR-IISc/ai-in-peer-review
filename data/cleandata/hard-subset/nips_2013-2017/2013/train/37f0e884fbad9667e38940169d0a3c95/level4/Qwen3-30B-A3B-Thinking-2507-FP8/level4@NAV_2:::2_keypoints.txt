- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence and reduces full-gradient calls from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)).  
- Strong theoretical contribution: elegant analysis showing elimination of condition-number dependence via a hybrid update and a clever mixed-gradient rule.  
- Assumes exact knowledge of the condition number κ (λ and L) with no method or analysis for estimation or misspecification—critical and unrealistic omission.  
- Completely lacks experimental validation, making it impossible to assess practical performance; needs empirical comparisons to SGD, SAG, SDCA.  
- Poor literature contextualization—fails to discuss related hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012 SISC) and how EMGD compares.  
- The problem setup is ambiguous (finite-sum deterministic vs stochastic expectation), undermining theoretical claims since methods like SAG/SDCA target finite-sum problems.  
- Does not consider or discuss extensions to non-strongly convex optimization, limiting applicability to many practical problems.  
- Conclusion: theoretical interest but practical relevance is questionable; authors should add experiments or stronger analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity for NIPS suitability.
