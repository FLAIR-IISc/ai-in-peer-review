- Problem & contribution: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid using few full gradients and many cheap stochastic gradients to minimize smooth strongly convex functions over convex sets.  
- Complexity: EMGD uses O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls (independent of condition number k) and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls.  
- Algorithm mechanics: Works in epochs—one full gradient per epoch plus O(k^2) mixed-gradient steps—with iterate averaging yielding constant suboptimality reduction per epoch (linear convergence).  
- Theoretical strength: Provides a clean, self-contained convergence proof with high-probability guarantees.  
- Applicability advantage: Handles constrained problems (via gradient-mapping) unlike SAG, and has a substantially simpler proof than SAG.  
- Practical/regime comparison: Assuming full gradient costs n stochastic gradients, EMGD beats Nesterov when k ≤ n^(2/3) but is slower than SAG in most regimes.  
- Detailed regime comparisons (requested): Reviewer asks authors to state that EMGD is worse than Nesterov for k > n^(2/3); EMGD matches SAG only for 1 ≤ k ≤ n^(1/2); SAG is best for n^(1/2) < k ≤ n/8; SAG worse than Nesterov for k > n/8.  
- Main pros: Novel hybrid idea, simple/solid analysis, broader applicability than SAG, high-probability bounds, clear presentation.  
- Main cons: No experiments or concrete practical scenarios; limited discussion of limitations/trade-offs versus alternatives; proof lacks higher-level motivation.  
- Practical drawback: EMGD requires fixing steps per epoch in advance (needs knowledge of k), unlike SDCA/SAG which do not require a fixed horizon and have adaptive step-size heuristics.  
- Addendum point (practical ML): With typical regularization scaling O(1/n) making k ≈ n/C′, SAG still improves over Nesterov but EMGD does not—authors should highlight this and point to regimes where k < n^(2/3).  
- Related work: Reviewer requests citation of Friedlander & Schmidt (SISC 2012) whose hybrid deterministic–stochastic method attains linear rate but depends on k.  
- Clarity suggestions: Keep Table 1, add high-level explanations for key constructions (mixed gradients, shrinking domain, averaging) and clarify several equations/derivations and notation inconsistencies.  
- Specific edits requested: Expand Section 3.4 on limitations/comparisons; clarify statements about SDCA/SAG, oracle properties, and identities like ∇F(w)=E[∇f(w)]; and make derivations and sums notation explicit.  
- Typos and notation fixes: Fix minor typos (e.g., “an convex” → “a convex”), use consistent domain symbol (D vs Ω), use norm notation, and prefer ln vs log consistently.  
- Recommendation/update: Reviewer is satisfied after rebuttal provided the requested clarifications, comparisons, and discussion of limitations/patterns are incorporated; experiments would strengthen the paper but are not required for acceptance.
