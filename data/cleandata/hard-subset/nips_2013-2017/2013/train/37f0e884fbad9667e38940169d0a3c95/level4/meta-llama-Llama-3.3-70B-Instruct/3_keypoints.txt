- Introduces a hybrid stochastic/batch gradient descent algorithm combining stochastic and full gradients.  
- Claims convergence to an ε-accurate solution using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Convergence proof is largely correct and innovative, aside from several minor errors.  
- Major concern: relevance to ML—strong convexity from regularizers gives μ = C·N, so μ scales with number of samples N.  
- Under μ = C·N, the proposed method beats batch GD only when N = O(C^3/L^3), which is not practically interesting.  
- Convergence guarantee holds only with high probability, whereas batch GD’s convergence is deterministic.  
- Paper needs a careful discussion demonstrating genuine advantage over batch GD and relevance to the ML community.
