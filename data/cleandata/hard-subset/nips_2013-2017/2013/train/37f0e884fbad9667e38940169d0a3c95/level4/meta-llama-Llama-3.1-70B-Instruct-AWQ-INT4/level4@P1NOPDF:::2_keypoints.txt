- Main contribution: proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that (when the condition number is known) attains O(log(1/ε)) convergence with high probability, removing kappa from the rate.  
- Theoretical result is interesting and striking under the paper’s assumptions.  
- Analysis assumes exact knowledge of the condition number κ (L and λ) and requires setting hyperparameter η accordingly.  
- No guidance or estimator for κ/η is provided, nor a heuristic tuning procedure.  
- No theoretical or empirical analysis of algorithm behavior under κ misspecification.  
- No experiments at all — a major omission given the paper proposes a new algorithm.  
- Only the smooth strongly convex case is considered; non-strongly-convex settings are not analyzed (e.g., Bach & Moulines).  
- Blending deterministic and stochastic steps is not novel; related hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) are not reviewed or compared.  
- The algorithm is written as a “mixed” update rather than explicit interleaving; the relation to simple alternation schemes is not discussed.  
- Lacks comparisons (theoretical or empirical) to standard baselines and modern stochastic methods (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.).  
- Optimization setting is ambiguous: unclear whether the target is finite-sum deterministic minimization or stochastic expectation; the theoretical setup is misleading.  
- Some claims (removal of κ dependence) should be tempered/clarified because they rely on the strong assumption that κ is known a priori.
