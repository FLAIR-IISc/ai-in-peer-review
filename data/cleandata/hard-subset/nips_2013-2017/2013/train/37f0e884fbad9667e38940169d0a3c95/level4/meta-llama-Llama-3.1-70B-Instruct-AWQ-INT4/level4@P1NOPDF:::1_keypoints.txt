- Paper proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) mixing one full gradient per epoch with O(k^2) stochastic steps to optimize smooth strongly-convex functions.  
- EMGD requires O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls (independent of condition number k) and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls.  
- By averaging O(k^2) iterates per epoch, the method achieves a constant decrease in suboptimality independent of k, yielding linear convergence.  
- Theoretical improvement over Nesterov occurs when k is not too large; EMGD is faster than Nesterov in theory for k ≤ n^(2/3) in the finite-sum setting.  
- EMGD is slower than SAG in all regimes and matches SAG only in certain scalings (e.g., k = n^1/2 both give O(n log(1/ε))).  
- Main advantages of EMGD vs SAG: works for constrained problems (with efficient gradient mapping) and has a much simpler convergence proof.  
- Claims about memory and parallelization advantages are unclear since SAG can be parallelized via mini-batches and reduce memory.  
- The paper provides a clean, self-contained, and technically sound proof but lacks high-level intuition motivating the proof quantities.  
- Major downside: no experimental results to demonstrate practical relevance.  
- Paper fails to sufficiently discuss limitations and drawbacks (Section 3.4 needs expansion), especially practical regimes where EMGD is inferior.  
- A drawback: epoch length must be fixed in advance using knowledge of k, unlike adaptive methods (SCDA, SAG) that can adapt step counts and step sizes.  
- The reviewer requests discussion of the finite-sum example differences with SAG and Nesterov and practical settings where k < n^(2/3).  
- Add citation request: include Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods and discuss differences.  
- Several suggested textual clarifications and phrasing fixes across the paper (e.g., Lines 050, 118–119, 132, 139, 224, 288, 304, 366).  
- Typographical corrections needed (e.g., "an convex" → "a convex", notation consistency for domain, use norm symbol, ln vs log).  
- Overall assessment: novel, clearly written, original combination of techniques with potential significance, but practical impact unproven without experiments and more discussion of limitations.  
- After rebuttal, reviewer is satisfied with responses but expects the authors to implement the suggested clarifications and extended discussion.
