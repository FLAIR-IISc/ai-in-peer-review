- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), mixing one full gradient per epoch with variance-reduced stochastic updates.  
- EMGD achieves O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, making full-gradient calls independent of κ.  
- Claims improvement over deterministic methods by leveraging stochastic information to bypass standard lower bounds.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound and yields linear convergence with high probability.  
- EMGD is applicable to constrained optimization (with efficient projection) unlike SAG/SDCA.  
- EMGD has low memory requirements O(d), much smaller than SAG’s O(n) storage.  
- Convergence guarantee is stronger (high-probability) than the in-expectation guarantees of SAG/SDCA.  
- Practical relevance is questioned: in many ML problems κ scales as O(n) or O(√n), limiting EMGD’s advantage.  
- EMGD outperforms accelerated full gradient only when κ ≤ n^(2/3), a regime that may be uncommon in practice.  
- Overall complexity O(n + κ²) can be worse than SDCA/SAG’s O(n + κ) in typical parameter regimes.  
- The paper should more thoroughly justify the practical regimes where κ ≤ n^(2/3) with concrete examples.  
- A major practical drawback is requiring prior knowledge of κ to set the inner iteration count T.  
- The proof in Section 4.2 is dense; transitions like (15)→(17) need clearer explanation for readability.  
- Suggest discussing adaptive or parameter-free strategies (estimates of κ) as potential extensions or heuristics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous, condition-number-independent full-gradient complexity, and favorable memory/constraint handling.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable practical impact, less favorable computational complexity than state-of-the-art in many regimes, and reliance on known κ.
