- Overall scores: quality 7/10, clarity 8, originality 8, significance 7.
- Problem/setting: optimize smooth, strongly convex F over convex set with expensive full gradients and cheap stochastic gradients.
- Main contribution: Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic calls.
- Key idea: each epoch uses one full gradient plus O(k^2) stochastic mixed-gradient steps and averaging to get k-independent epoch count.
- Theoretical properties: simple, self-contained convergence proof giving linear rates and high-probability guarantees.
- No empirical evaluation: paper contains no experiments to demonstrate practical impact.
- Comparison to Nesterov: EMGD can be faster than Nesterov when k is not too large (roughly k ≤ n^(2/3) in the sum-of-n setting).
- Comparison to SAG/SDCA: SAG outperforms EMGD in most regimes (matches EMGD only when k ≤ n^(1/2)); SAG/SDCA have practical advantages like adaptive step-sizes and not fixing epoch length.
- Advantages over SAG: handles constrained problems, much simpler proof, and high-probability (vs. expectation) results.
- Practical drawbacks: number of steps per epoch must be fixed a priori (requires knowledge of k); EMGD may be worse in common ML regimes where k ≈ n/C′.
- Request to expand Section 3.4: include fuller discussion of limitations, comparisons, and concrete regimes where EMGD vs. SAG vs. Nesterov win.
- Suggest adding high-level proof commentary to explain motivations and how components fit together.
- Cite related work: add Friedlander & Schmidt (SISC 2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods and clarify differences.
- Memory/parallelization claim unclear: note that SAG can be parallelized with mini-batches which also reduce memory.
- Minor fixes/typos: several wording, notation, and formatting corrections (e.g., "an convex" → "a convex", domain notation consistency, add parentheses, norm symbols, ln vs log).
