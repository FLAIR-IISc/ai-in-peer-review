- Proposes a hybrid first-order method that mixes full gradients and stochastic gradients to optimize strongly convex smooth objectives.  
- Main theoretical claim: with known condition number κ the method attains high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence that omits explicit κ dependence.  
- Strength: novel theoretical guarantee showing exponential rate without κ-dependence (when κ is given).  
- Strength: clear conceptual motivation leveraging complementary strengths of deterministic and stochastic updates.  
- Major concern: algorithm and analysis require knowing κ (λ and L) to set η and mixing schedule, limiting practical relevance.  
- Major concern: no analysis or guidance on estimating κ or robustness to κ misspecification.  
- Major concern: entirely lacks empirical evaluation to validate rates, constants, robustness, and compare baselines.  
- Major concern: unclear optimization setting—finite-sum vs. expectation—and ambiguous oracle/cost model and complexity measures.  
- Major concern: insufficient discussion and comparison to prior hybrid and variance-reduction methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt, SAG, SVRG, SDCA).  
- Suggestion: provide heuristics/estimation methods for L and λ, analyze misspecification effects, or propose adaptive schemes (doubling/halving, line-search, restarts).  
- Suggestion: explicitly state problem setting, define oracle cost model (full vs stochastic gradient counts), and discuss memory/pass complexity for finite-sum problems.  
- Suggestion: contrast estimator and schedule with hybrid/variance-reduced methods, clarify whether mixed gradient is a control variate and compare full-gradient frequency to SVRG.  
- Suggestion: discuss equivalence or differences to explicit alternation of deterministic and stochastic steps and whether alternation preserves the rate.  
- Suggestion: discuss extensions beyond strong convexity (e.g., regularization, homotopy/restarts) and cite related theory (e.g., Bach & Moulines).  
- Strongly recommended experiments: logistic/least-squares on synthetic and real data vs SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SVRG, SDCA; report full/stochastic gradient counts and runtime; test κ misspecification and ablations.  
- Minor issues: fix terminology (L = smoothness, λ = strong convexity), clarify “batch optimization” and “hybrid oracle,” and provide reproducible pseudocode with schedules and hyper-parameters.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically appealing but practically limited due to reliance on known κ, missing robustness analysis, weak relation to prior work, and no experiments; authors should address these to strengthen impact.
