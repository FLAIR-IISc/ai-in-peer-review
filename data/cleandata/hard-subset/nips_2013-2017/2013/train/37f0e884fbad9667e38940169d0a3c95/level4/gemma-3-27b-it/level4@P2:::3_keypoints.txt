- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid full+stochastic gradient algorithm for strongly convex optimization.  
- Claims iteration complexity O(log(1/ε)) for full gradients independent of condition number κ, and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical analysis and proofs supporting the stated convergence rates appear sound and logically consistent.  
- Core idea: strategically interleave full and stochastic gradients, building on epoch gradient descent and stochastic optimization prior work.  
- Correctly frames the trade-off between fast convergence of full gradients and low per-iteration cost of stochastic gradients.  
- Presentation is clear with a detailed algorithm description and convergence analysis, including relevant lemmas and inequalities.  
- Useful comparison to existing methods (Nesterov, SAG, SDCA) in Table 2.  
- Major weakness: limited discussion of practical relevance in machine learning, where κ often depends on sample size n.  
- Analysis indicates EMGD outperforms batch GD only in a narrow regime (κ ≤ n²/3), raising concerns about real-world benefit.  
- Deterministic convergence of batch gradient descent vs. probabilistic guarantee of EMGD weakens EMGD’s practical case.  
- Paper lacks exploration of specific problem/data settings where EMGD would clearly excel.  
- Does not address how practitioners should estimate condition number κ in practice, which is needed to apply EMGD.  
- Minor corrections: Equation (6) should be ||w* - ŵ||²; clarify function for eq. (7) at Line 288; remove unnecessary equality at Line 286; use norms in eq. (13) at Line 292; explicitly state L ≥ λ; in eq. (4) replace x^ with w^ and f with F and note first term in max exceeds second per Lemma 1.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel hybrid algorithm, theoretically sound convergence, addresses computational cost for ill-conditioned problems, clear exposition and comparisons.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical relevance for ML, narrow regime of improvement over batch GD, probabilistic vs deterministic guarantees, and lack of guidance on estimating κ.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid but practicality questionable; recommendation to reject pending major revisions that strengthen real-world applicability and address concerns.
