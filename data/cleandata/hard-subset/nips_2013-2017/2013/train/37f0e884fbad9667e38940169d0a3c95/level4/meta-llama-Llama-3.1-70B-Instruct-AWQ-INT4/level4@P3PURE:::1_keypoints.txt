- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
- Problem: Optimize smooth, strongly convex objective over convex set using both full and stochastic gradient oracles.
- Contribution: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm mixing full and stochastic gradients.
- Theoretical claim: EMGD uses O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls (condition-number independent) and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic calls.
- Key idea: Replace a full-gradient step with O(k^2) mixed-gradient steps and average iterates within epochs to get constant suboptimality decrease.
- Proof: Provides a self-contained convergence proof and linear convergence rate for regularized empirical loss minimization.
- Novelty: Combines known techniques in a novel way; more general than SAG (handles constraints) with a simpler proof.
- Practical gap: No experiments are included, so practical relevance is unverified.
- Limitation: Requires fixing the number of steps per epoch in advance, which may be impractical compared to SCDA and SAG.
- Practical comparison: SCDA offers automatic step-size selection and SAG has adaptive step-size heuristics, potentially yielding faster real-world convergence.
- Missing discussion: Incomplete treatment of limitations and comparisons to existing algorithms (SAG, SCDA, Nesterov).
- Suggestion for proof: Add high-level comments to motivate defined quantities and make the construction more intuitive.
- Specific paper edits: Clarify Table 1 references, SCDA applicability, domain notation, gradient expectation, summation parentheses, and validity conditions for inequalities.
- Corrections: Fix several typos and notation issues (e.g., "an convex" → "a convex", use norm symbol, use ln consistently).
