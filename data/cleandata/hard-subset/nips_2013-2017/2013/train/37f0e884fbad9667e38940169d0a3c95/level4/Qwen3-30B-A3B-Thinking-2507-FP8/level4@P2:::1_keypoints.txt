- Summary: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid full/stochastic-gradient method achieving O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient evaluations with linear convergence and condition-number-independent full-gradient access.

- Originality: Novel hybrid approach extending SAG/SDCA ideas to constrained problems with a simpler convergence proof.

- Theoretical quality: Analysis is technically sound, complete, self-contained, and shows linear convergence with high probability.

- Clarity: Well-written, logically organized, with a clear algorithm description and helpful summary table.

- Significance: Meaningful theoretical contribution for constrained optimization and likely useful as a basis for future extensions.

- Weakness — experiments: No empirical validation or runtime comparisons to show practical benefits on real datasets.

- Weakness — limitations discussion: Section 3.4 is insufficiently developed in comparing practical limitations versus SAG/SDCA.

- Weakness — knowledge of κ: Requiring the condition number κ in advance is a major practical drawback not adequately addressed.

- Weakness — theoretical regime comparisons: Paper should state EMGD is superior to Nesterov only when κ ≤ n^(2/3) (and note SAG is superior when κ ≤ n/8).

- Weakness — parallelization claim: Incorrectly claims SAG cannot be parallelized; both SAG and EMGD can use mini-batches for distributed implementations.

- Missing insight: Proof lacks higher-level intuition for choices like the specific mixed-gradient form.

- Suggestion: Add a concrete example showing when κ ≤ n^(2/3) occurs in practice (e.g., with specific regularization).

- Suggestion: Provide a comparison table clarifying computational complexity of EMGD, SAG, and Nesterov under different κ regimes.

- Suggestion: Correct the statement about SAG’s parallelizability and discuss mini-batch/distributed options for both methods.

- Suggestion: Discuss practical approaches to unknown κ (e.g., conservative estimates from early iterations) and explicitly note that κ = L/λ appears in rates but not in the count of full-gradient evaluations.

- Overall assessment: Technically strong and novel with clear presentation, but reduced practical impact due to missing experiments and incomplete discussion of limitations.

- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing comparisons, practical limitations, and empirical validation.

- Ratings: Quality 8/10; Clarity 9/10; Originality 8/10; Significance 7/10.
