SUMMARY: 
The authors propose a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), for optimizing a smooth and strongly convex function over a convex constraint set. The algorithm efficiently computes the gradient mapping update by combining expensive full gradient evaluations with cheap stochastic gradient oracles. EMGD achieves a linear convergence rate, requiring only O(log 1/eps) calls to the full gradient oracle and O(k^2 log(1/eps)) calls to the stochastic gradient oracle, where k is the condition number. This is an improvement over Nesterov's optimal first-order algorithm, whose convergence rate depends on the square root of the condition number. The authors provide a self-contained proof of their convergence rate, but lack experimental results.

I found the paper enjoyable to read, and the idea of mixing full gradient computations with stochastic gradient steps is novel and interesting. This work contributes to the recent line of research that aims to leverage cheaper stochastic oracles while maintaining linear convergence rates. A notable example is the SAG algorithm, which achieves a linear convergence rate for smooth strongly convex objectives that are the sum of n simple functions. In contrast, EMGD is more general, as it can handle constrained optimization problems, and its proof is simpler.

The main advantages of EMGD over SAG are its ability to handle constrained optimization and its simpler convergence proof, which could provide more insights and facilitate modifications with provable guarantees. Additionally, EMGD has a stronger result, as it holds with high probability, whereas SAG's result holds in expectation.
