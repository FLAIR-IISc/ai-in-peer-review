- Problem and contribution: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) for smooth, strongly convex optimization with a hybrid oracle, achieving O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls (independent of k) and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic calls.  
- Key algorithmic idea: Organize iterations into epochs, compute one full gradient per epoch, run O(k^2) mixed-gradient inner steps, and average iterates to get k-independent constant reduction.  
- Theoretical results: Simple, self-contained proof of linear convergence with high-probability guarantees.  
- Practical/experimental gap: No experiments to demonstrate empirical impact or where theoretical advantages matter.  
- Comparison to existing methods: EMGD can beat Nesterov when k ≤ n^(2/3) but is slower than SAG in most regimes; EMGD and SAG match when k ≤ n^(1/2); regime-specific comparisons with Nesterov and SAG provided.  
- Advantages over SAG: Handles constrained problems (with gradient mapping) and has a simpler proof yielding high-probability rather than in-expectation guarantees.  
- Limitations vs. competitors: Requires fixing the number of inner steps a priori based on k; lacks adaptive step-size and automatic tuning that SCDA/SAG offer; memory/parallelization advantages over SAG are unclear.  
- Requested expansions: Significantly expand Section 3.4 on limitations, give concrete discussion of the sum-of-n-functions setting and practical regimes where EMGD helps.  
- Addendum point: In common ML regularization (regularizer ≈ C/n), k ≈ n/C′ so EMGD often does not improve over Nesterov; authors should note at least one practical setting with k < n^(2/3).  
- Literature/citation: Add citation to Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) on hybrid deterministic–stochastic methods and clarify differences.  
- Clarity and exposition: Paper is generally clear with a helpful Table 1, but the proof needs more high-level motivation to avoid “magical” constructions.  
- Minor fixes: Numerous suggested phrasing clarifications, derivation clarifications, consistent notation (domain symbol), and typographical corrections (listed by line).  
- Overall recommendation: Technically sound and theoretically interesting; acceptability hinges on expanding discussion of limitations and incorporating the requested clarifications and citations.
