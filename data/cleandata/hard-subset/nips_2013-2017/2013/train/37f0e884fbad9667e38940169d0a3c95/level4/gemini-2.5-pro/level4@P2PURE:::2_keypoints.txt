- Paper proposes a hybrid deterministic/stochastic first-order method with a notable theoretical claim: O(log(1/ε)) convergence independent of condition number for smooth strongly convex objectives.
- Major flaw: the theoretical analysis requires knowing the exact condition number κ in advance.
- Algorithm EGD needs a hyperparameter η set from κ (Eq. 8), with no guidance when smoothness/strong-convexity constants are unknown.
- No analysis or empirical study of algorithm performance under misspecified κ.
- Major omission: no experiments to validate theory or study practical behavior and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Experiments should compare against baselines like SGD, averaged SGD, and recent methods such as SAG.
- Problem setting is unclear—whether the target is finite-sum (1/n ∑ F_i) or stochastic approximation (E[F(w)]), which affects relevant comparisons.
- Analysis is restricted to smooth, strongly convex objectives; non-strongly convex case is not addressed.
- Related work is insufficiently discussed—prior hybrid deterministic/stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) are missing.
- Paper does not compare the proposed mixed-update formulation to simpler interleaved full-gradient and stochastic-gradient strategies.
