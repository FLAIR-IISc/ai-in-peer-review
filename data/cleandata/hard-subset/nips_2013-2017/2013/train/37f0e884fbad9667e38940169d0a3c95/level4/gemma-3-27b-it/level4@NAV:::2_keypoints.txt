- Introduces a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method combining full-gradient and stochastic-gradient oracles.  
- Theoretical contribution: shows that with known condition number and a hybrid oracle, one can obtain high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence for batch optimization.  
- Analysis is limited to the case where the condition number κ is known a priori.  
- Does not analyze algorithm behavior under misspecified or unknown κ.  
- Lacks any experimental validation—no empirical section to support theoretical claims.  
- The algorithm requires tuning hyperparameter η tied to κ, but the paper gives no guidance or estimation procedure for κ.  
- Should examine convergence sensitivity to overly large or small η (impact of κ misspecification).  
- Restricts attention to smooth, strongly convex problems; does not explore non-strongly convex or broader settings.  
- Claim of novelty is weak because blending deterministic and stochastic steps is not new; the single “mixed” update differs from explicit interleaving but needs discussion.  
- Omits citation and comparison to relevant prior hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and lacks rate comparisons to existing algorithms.  
- Needs comprehensive empirical comparisons to SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, and other state-of-the-art methods.  
- The optimization setting (deterministic optimization with randomized algorithm vs. stochastic approximation) is ambiguous and not clearly defined.
