- Paper proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method claiming removal of condition-number dependence in convergence rate.  
- Main theoretical claim: with known condition number and a hybrid oracle, a suitable mix of deterministic and stochastic steps yields O(log(1/ε)) convergence with high probability.  
- Analysis assumes the condition number (κ) is perfectly known; no theory or discussion for κ misspecification.  
- No experiments are provided to validate theory or study sensitivity to κ estimation/misspecification.  
- The algorithm requires a hyperparameter η set from κ, but the paper gives no guidance or estimation procedure for κ.  
- If κ is assumed known for theory, the paper should still analyze or empirically study convergence when κ is set too large or too small.  
- Restrictive focus on smooth, strongly convex objectives; non-strongly convex cases are not treated though they are important (see Bach & Moulines 2011).  
- Blending deterministic and stochastic steps is not novel; the paper should compare its “mixed update” formulation to interleaving schemes and clarify differences.  
- Important prior work on hybrid deterministic–stochastic optimization (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) is not reviewed or compared.  
- The paper lacks experimental comparisons to baseline stochastic methods (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.).  
- Problem setting is unclear: authors do not clearly state whether they address finite-sum deterministic optimization or stochastic approximation, making theoretical claims ambiguous.
