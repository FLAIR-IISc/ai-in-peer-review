The paper introduces a novel hybrid stochastic gradient descent algorithm leveraging both stochastic and batch gradient evaluations. The authors demonstrate convergence to an ε-accurate solution with a complexity of O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients. The convergence analysis appears sound and represents a contribution to the field, aside from a few minor technical points detailed below.

However, a significant concern arises regarding the practical relevance of this algorithm within the typical machine learning context, which is the focus of this conference. In many machine learning applications, strong convexity is primarily introduced through regularization, resulting in a μ value proportional to the number of samples, N (μ = CN, where C is independent of N). Under this common assumption, the proposed method only offers a speedup over batch gradient descent when the number of samples is limited to O(C^3/L^3), a potentially restrictive scenario. Furthermore, the convergence rate presented is probabilistic, whereas batch gradient descent offers deterministic convergence. 

This distinction is crucial and requires thorough discussion to convincingly demonstrate a genuine advantage over existing batch gradient descent methods and to establish the paper’s relevance to the machine learning community.
