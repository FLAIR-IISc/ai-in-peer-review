- Main claim: A hybrid method mixing full and stochastic gradients yields a high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence rate that removes explicit dependence on the condition number, assuming κ is known.

- Major concern 1: Theory assumes known condition number κ (L/λ) and algorithm requires hyperparameter η tied to κ; no guidance on estimating κ or robustness to misspecification.

- Major concern 1 (needed fixes): Paper should provide κ/η estimation procedures, sensitivity analysis for over/underestimating κ, and clarify stability of the κ-free rate under misspecification.

- Major concern 2: No experimental validation; empirical results are essential to confirm theoretical rates and practical benefits of mixing oracles.

- Major concern 2 (experiments wanted): Experiments should validate rates, test robustness to κ misestimation and mix ratios, compare to strong baselines, and report both oracle counts and wall-clock time.

- Major concern 3: Optimization setting is unclear—finite-sum versus stochastic approximation—and Section 3.1 is ambiguous about the oracle model and performance metrics.

- Major concern 4: Missing discussion and comparison to prior hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012), including assumptions, oracle complexity, and conceptual differences.

- Major concern 5: Analysis is limited to smooth, strongly convex objectives; paper should discuss or extend to merely convex (non-strongly convex) settings.

- Detailed comment: Algorithm EGD’s mixed update requires correct terminology and clear definitions of L and λ; choosing η currently implies knowing κ and needs correction or justification.

- Detailed comment: Compare the proposed mixed update to simple interleaving of full and stochastic steps and clarify why the mixed update is preferable in oracle complexity or rates.

- Detailed comment: Clarify oracle and cost model—explicitly state counts of stochastic vs full-gradient calls for the rate and distinguish iteration complexity from oracle complexity.

- Detailed comment: State whether guarantees are in expectation or with high probability and specify variance assumptions on the stochastic oracle consistently across abstract and theorems.

- Detailed comment: If non-strongly convex extension is hard, discuss expected behavior and remedies like restarting, regularization, or adaptive schedules.

- Experimental suggestions: Benchmarks on standard finite-sum problems (logistic/ridge regression), baselines including SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, SVRG/SAGA, and metrics tracking optimization error vs oracle calls and wall-clock time.

- Experimental suggestions (ablations): Study sensitivity to η and κ misestimation, vary frequency of full-gradient steps, and ablate mixed-gradient construction.

- Overall assessment: Interesting and striking theoretical idea but requires clarifying problem setting/oracle model, relating to prior hybrid work, addressing known-κ dependence (estimation or robustness), and adding empirical evaluation to be publishable.
