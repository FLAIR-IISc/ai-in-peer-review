- Review scores: quality 7/10, clarity 8/10, originality 8/10, significance 7/10.
- Problem and setting: Studies first-order optimization for smooth, strongly convex objectives with access to an expensive full-gradient oracle and a cheaper stochastic-gradient oracle.
- Main contribution: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): one full gradient per epoch plus O(κ^2) mixed updates per epoch inside a shrinking ball, averaging iterates for linear convergence.
- Theoretical guarantees: EMGD achieves ε-accuracy with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, with high-probability linear convergence.
- Comparison to full-gradient methods: Reduces number of full gradients from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)), removing κ dependence for full-gradient calls.
- Comparison to stochastic and semi-stochastic methods: Improves over classic SGD’s polynomial 1/ε dependence; semi-stochastic methods still retain κ dependence in rates.
- Comparison to finite-sum methods: Relative to SAG/SDCA, EMGD is better when κ ≤ n^(2/3), comparable when κ ≤ n^(1/2), and worse otherwise; EMGD applies to constrained problems and uses O(d) memory.
- Novelty: A novel, simple combination of epochs, mixed gradients, and shrinking domains yielding cleaner analysis than SAG/SDCA.
- Pros: Clear algorithmic idea, clean self-contained analysis, high-probability linear rates, broader applicability (constrained problems), low memory.
- Cons: No experiments to demonstrate practical benefit or regimes of advantage.
- Cons: Limited discussion of limitations and trade-offs versus Nesterov/SAG/SDCA; Section 3.4 needs expansion.
- Cons: Requires knowing or tuning κ to set epoch length (O(κ^2)); less adaptive than SAG/SDCA which use practical adaptive step sizes.
- Cons: Some parallelization/distributed computing claims are overstated; SAG/SDCA can use mini-batches for speed/memory.
- Suggested additions: Add experiments, expand discussion of limitations/trade-offs, and provide concrete finite-sum cost-model comparisons and thresholds on κ vs. n.
- Suggested improvements: Discuss estimating κ or making EMGD adaptive (e.g., doubling tricks), clarify memory/parallelization, and add Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) citation.
- Proof and clarity suggestions: Expand high-level intuition for mixed-gradient and shrinking ball, make assumptions and indices explicit, and fix minor typos/notation inconsistencies.
- Final stance after rebuttal: Positive disposition provided camera-ready addresses limitations discussion, parallelization claims, added citation, and suggested clarifications.
