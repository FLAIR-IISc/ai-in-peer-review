- Introduces a novel hybrid optimization method mixing stochastic and full-batch gradients with provable convergence.
- Claims convergence to ε-accuracy using O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient computations.
- Convergence proof appears correct and novel, aside from minor issues.
- Major concern: practical relevance — in ML strong convexity often comes from regularization so μ typically depends on sample size N.
- If μ ≈ C·N, the method outperforms batch GD only when N = O(C^3/L^3), a very restrictive and likely impractical regime.
- Convergence guarantee is high-probability (probabilistic) while batch GD’s rate is deterministic — a critical distinction authors must address.
- Authors need a careful discussion showing clear practical advantage over batch gradient descent in typical ML settings.
