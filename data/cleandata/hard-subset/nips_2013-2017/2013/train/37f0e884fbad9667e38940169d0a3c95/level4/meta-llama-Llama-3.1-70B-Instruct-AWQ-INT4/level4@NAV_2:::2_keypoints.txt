- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a novel algorithm combining deterministic and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Theoretical claim: EMGD reduces full-gradient calls from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)), achieving linear convergence.  
- Result holds with high probability under the assumption that the condition number κ is known.  
- Analysis only covers the case of a perfectly known κ; misspecification behavior is not analyzed.  
- No guideline or estimation procedure is provided for choosing or estimating κ in practice.  
- The smooth and strongly convex setting is restrictive; non-strongly convex and other settings are not addressed.  
- The paper omits discussion and comparison with related hybrid deterministic–stochastic optimization literature.  
- Lacks a thorough experimental section and empirical comparisons (e.g., SGD, averaged SGD, recent stochastic methods).  
- The optimization setting (deterministic objective vs. stochastic approximation) is not clearly specified.  
- Strength: novel algorithmic idea that theoretically reduces full-gradient requirements.  
- Weakness: theoretical reliance on known κ and absence of empirical validation limit practical relevance.  
- Recommendations: add experiments, clarify the optimization setting, and analyze or provide guidelines for κ misspecification/estimation.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting theoretical contribution but needs empirical validation and robustness analysis (rating 7/10).
