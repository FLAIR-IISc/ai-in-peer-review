

The paper introduces a novel hybrid approach to gradient descent that combines both stochastic and full gradients. The proposed Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) algorithm achieves convergence to an ε-optimal solution using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients. The convergence proof appears technically sound and represents a novel contribution, with only minor issues that I'll detail below.

My primary concern relates to the algorithm's practical relevance for machine learning applications, which is the central focus of this conference. In typical machine learning problems, strong convexity is induced by a regularizer, making the strong convexity parameter λ proportional to the number of samples N (λ = Ω(N)). Under this common scenario, the proposed method only offers a computational advantage over standard batch gradient descent when the number of samples is bounded by O(C³/L³), where C is a constant independent of N. This regime does not appear to be practically relevant for most machine learning applications.

Furthermore, while the convergence rate of EMGD holds with high probability, standard batch gradient descent provides deterministic convergence guarantees. These points are crucial for establishing the paper's significance to the machine learning community and should be thoroughly addressed to demonstrate a clear practical advantage over batch gradient descent.

The paper presents a theoretically interesting algorithm, but its practical significance for machine learning optimization needs to be more convincingly established to justify its relevance to the conference's focus.
