- Proposes a hybrid optimization method interleaving stochastic and full-batch gradients for strongly convex smooth objectives with stated complexity bounds.  
- Convergence analysis appears largely correct and uses novel proof techniques, aside from minor fixes.  
- Strength: conceptually interesting strategy that aims to reduce expensive full-gradient evaluations while retaining linear convergence.  
- Strength: clear theoretical target of reducing full-gradient evaluations to O(log(1/ε)).  
- Main concern: unclear convention for strong convexity scaling—must state whether objective is a sum or an average over samples.  
- Need explicit definitions of μ, L, and condition number κ and how they depend on N (sum vs average convention).  
- Under the “sum” convention (μ ≈ C N), claimed speedups over batch GD hold only for impractically small N regimes; this should be clarified.  
- Translate complexity into ML-relevant units: passes over data, total stochastic gradient computations, and wall-clock time under a reasonable cost model.  
- Compare head-to-head with batch GD and variance-reduced methods (e.g., SVRG/SAGA) to identify regimes where the hybrid method is advantageous.  
- Clarify high-probability vs deterministic guarantees: dependence on confidence parameter δ, hidden constants, and whether expectation bounds match.  
- Presentation fixes: explicitly state sum vs average, define μ/L/κ formally, and explain why L ≥ λ.  
- Quantify and separate costs of full vs stochastic gradients (e.g., full gradient ≈ N stochastic gradients) and show total cost formula under that model.  
- Discuss constant factors and parameter choices (how k is set, step-size sensitivity, interactions with κ and N).  
- Minor corrections: Eq.(6) norm typo, Eq.(13) use vector norms, Eq.(4) notation fixes, remove unnecessary displayed equality (line 286), specify function for inequality (line 288), and provide precise condition-number definition.  
- Questions for authors: which scaling convention is used, how bounds translate to passes over data, what N/κ regimes give practical gains, empirical evidence on benchmarks, comparison with SVRG/SAGA, and dependence on confidence parameter.  
- Overall: novel and potentially impactful approach but practical relevance is not yet convincingly established without clearer scaling, explicit pass-level complexity, and empirical comparisons.
