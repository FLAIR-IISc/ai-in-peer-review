- Presents a novel hybrid optimization strategy mixing stochastic and full-batch gradients with convergence to ε-accuracy using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- The convergence proof is novel and largely sound, with only minor issues noted.
- Major concern: practical relevance is unclear for ML problems where strong convexity comes from regularization and μ may scale with sample size N.
- Under the assumption μ = O(N), the hybrid method is computationally better than batch GD only when N = O(C^3/L^3), a very restrictive condition limiting utility to small-scale problems.
- The algorithm’s convergence is high-probability (probabilistic) while batch GD’s guarantee is deterministic; this should factor into comparisons.
- The paper needs a clearer discussion of the ML regimes (relationships among N, L, μ) where the hybrid method outperforms batch or fully stochastic GD.
- Overall: theoretically interesting, but practical significance and regimes of advantage are not established.
