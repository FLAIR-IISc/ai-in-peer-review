- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): epochs that start with one full gradient followed by O(κ²) mixed gradient steps and averaging to reduce suboptimality.  
- Main theoretical result: O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls (κ-independent) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls.  
- Convergence proof is simple, self-contained, and yields high-probability (not just expectation) guarantees.  
- Extends hybrid fast methods to constrained optimization, a setting where SAG is not defined.  
- No experiments provided — a major omission that prevents assessing practical performance and the impact of the O(κ²) stochastic cost.  
- Comparisons to existing work (SAG, SCDA, Nesterov) are underdeveloped; authors should state precise regimes where EMGD wins or loses (e.g., κ ≲ n^(2/3), SAG faster when n^(1/2) ≲ κ ≤ n/8, common ML case κ ≈ n favors SAG).  
- Practical issues: EMGD requires knowing κ to set inner iterations and lacks adaptive step-size/inner-loop strategies that methods like SCDA/SAG offer.  
- Parallelization/memory advantage over SAG is not convincing since SAG supports mini-batching and reduced memory implementations.  
- Missing relevant citation and discussion: Friedlander & Schmidt (SISC 2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods.  
- Proof presentation needs more high-level commentary and intuition — some derivations feel "magical" and should be clarified.  
- Several minor typos, notation inconsistencies, and line-specific clarity suggestions were enumerated for correction.  
- Reviewer is satisfied with the rebuttal plans but expects revisions addressing limitations, deeper comparisons, the missing citation, and improved proof exposition.
