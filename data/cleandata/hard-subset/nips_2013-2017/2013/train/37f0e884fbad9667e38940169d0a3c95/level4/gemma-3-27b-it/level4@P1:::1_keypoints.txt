- Problem: optimize smooth, strongly convex function with efficient gradient-mapping updates using a cheap stochastic oracle and an expensive full-gradient oracle.  
- Contribution: propose Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that uses O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic calls.  
- Main idea: replace each full-gradient epoch by O(κ²) mixed steps combining one full gradient per epoch with many stochastic gradients and average iterates to get condition-number-independent epoch decrease.  
- Theory: provide a simple, self-contained convergence proof showing linear rate with number of full-gradient epochs independent of κ.  
- No experiments: paper lacks empirical validation or concrete practical examples.  
- Novelty: novel hybrid deterministic–stochastic idea situated among recent work using cheap stochastic oracles while retaining linear rates.  
- Comparison to SAG: EMGD is more general (handles constraints) and has a simpler, high-probability proof; but EMGD is slower than SAG in all regimes and matches SAG only for small κ.  
- Regime thresholds: EMGD outperforms Nesterov when κ ≤ n^{2/3}; SAG outperforms EMGD for many regimes and is better than Nesterov for κ ≤ n/8.  
- Example speed-up: if κ = n^{1/2}, EMGD and SAG are O(n log(1/ε)) while Nesterov is O(n^{5/4} log(1/ε)).  
- Advantages over SAG: works with constrained problems and has a much simpler, high-probability convergence proof.  
- Unclear practical benefits: memory/parallelization advantage over SAG is not clearly established.  
- Missing limitations: paper omits discussion of practical drawbacks and comparisons (section 3.4 should be expanded).  
- Epoch length issue: EMGD requires fixing the number of steps per epoch in advance based on κ, which is a practical drawback versus adaptive methods (SDCA, SAG).  
- Step-size adaptivity: SDCA has automatic step-size selection and SAG has effective adaptive heuristics; EMGD lacks such flexibility.  
- Practical regime remark: in ML with regularization λ ≈ C/n, κ ≈ n/C' often makes EMGD less favorable than SAG—this should be discussed.  
- Related work: should cite Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods and clarify differences in rates.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear; Table 1 is helpful, but the proof needs high-level motivation for some constructed quantities.  
- Suggested technical edits: specific line-by-line clarifications and notational fixes (lines 50, 118–139, 224, 288, 307, 366).  
- Typos: several minor grammatical and notation typos to correct (e.g., “an convex” → “a convex”, inconsistent domain symbol D vs Ω).  
- Overall evaluation: technically sound and theoretically interesting contribution with a simple proof, but needs experiments and a fuller discussion of limitations and comparisons.
