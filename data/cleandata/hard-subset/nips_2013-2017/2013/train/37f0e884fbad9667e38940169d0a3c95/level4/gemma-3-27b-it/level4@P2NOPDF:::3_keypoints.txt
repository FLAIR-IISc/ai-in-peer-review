- Paper proposes a novel hybrid SGD algorithm combining stochastic and batch updates with convergence guarantees.  
- Claims complexity: O(log(1/ε)) full gradient and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient evaluations to reach ε-accuracy.  
- Convergence proof appears largely correct but has a few minor technical issues to fix.  
- Primary practical concern: analysis depends on strong convexity μ which in ML often scales as μ = C N, making speedup over batch GD only for restrictive sample sizes O(C^3 / L^3).  
- Unclear practical relevance to ML; authors must discuss when the method is advantageous under typical ML regularization regimes.  
- Convergence is probabilistic versus deterministic for batch GD; the paper must justify the probabilistic guarantee’s value relative to deterministic convergence.  
- Minor correction: Equation (6) LHS should be ||w* − ŵ||^2 (squared norm).  
- Clarify in Line 288 which function equation (7) is applied to for readability.  
- Remove the unnecessary equality after the statement in Line 286.  
- Equation (13) should use vector norms instead of absolute values.  
- Explicitly state that L ≥ λ and correct Equation (4) notation: x* → w*, f → F; note Lemma 1 implies the first max term dominates.  
- Provide a precise definition of the condition number in terms of λ and L.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting contribution but practical advantages over batch GD are not convincingly demonstrated; a deeper discussion addressing ML-relevance is required.
