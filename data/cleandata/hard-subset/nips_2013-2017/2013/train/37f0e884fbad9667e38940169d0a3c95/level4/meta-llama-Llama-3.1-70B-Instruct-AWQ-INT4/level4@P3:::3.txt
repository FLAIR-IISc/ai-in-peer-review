This paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), that combines the benefits of both stochastic and batch gradients to achieve faster convergence rates for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The algorithm is shown to converge to an epsilon-accurate solution using O(log(1/eps)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/eps)) stochastic gradients.

The paper is well-written, and the convergence proof appears to be correct and novel. However, I have some concerns regarding the relevance of the proposed algorithm in the machine learning setting, which is the focus of this conference.

In typical machine learning algorithms, strong convexity is often introduced through regularization, which implies that the strong convexity parameter mu is of the order of the number of samples N, i.e., mu = C N, where C does not depend on N. Under this assumption, the proposed method is faster than batch gradient descent only if the number of samples is bounded by O(C^3/L^3), which may not be a practically interesting regime.

Furthermore, the convergence rate for the proposed algorithm holds only in high probability, whereas the convergence rate for batch gradient descent is deterministic. This difference in convergence guarantees may limit the applicability of the proposed algorithm in certain machine learning settings.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should carefully discuss the advantages and limitations of their algorithm in the context of machine learning optimization problems. Specifically, they should provide a more detailed analysis of the conditions under which their algorithm outperforms standard batch gradient descent and address the potential drawbacks of their algorithm, such as the high-probability convergence guarantee.

Weaknesses:

* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the conditions under which their algorithm outperforms standard batch gradient descent.

The authors should provide a more detailed analysis of the conditions under which their algorithm outperforms standard batch gradient descent and address the potential drawbacks of their algorithm.
