1. Proposes a novel hybrid stochastic/batch GD with provable convergence (O(log(1/ε)) full, O(k² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients); proof largely sound but contains minor errors.  
2. Major concern: relevance to ML—under standard regularization μ = Θ(N), so the algorithm only outperforms BGD in an unrealistic regime (N = O(C³/L³)).  
3. Concrete example shows the required regime is implausible (e.g., C=0.1, L=1 implies N < 0.001), undermining practical benefit.  
4. The claimed advantage over BGD lacks practical justification without empirical demonstration on realistic datasets.  
5. Convergence is high-probability while BGD’s is deterministic; the authors must address this trade-off and its practical implications (wall-clock time/generalization).  
6. Need to show that O(k² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient complexity yields tangible benefits over BGD’s O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient complexity in ML settings.  
7. Required experiments: quantify the practical regime where the hybrid method dominates BGD, e.g., empirical comparisons on standard ML tasks.  
8. Minor corrections: Eq.(6) use ||w*−ŵ||^2; Line 288 specify function in Eq.(7); Line 286 remove the unnecessary equality.  
9. Minor corrections (cont.): Eq.(13) replace absolute values with norms; explicitly state L ≥ λ; fix Eq.(4) (x^ → w^, f → F) and note first max term dominates per Lemma 1.  
10. Minor correction: precisely define condition number κ = L/λ.  
11. Overall: theoretical contribution non-trivial but currently a theoretical curiosity lacking empirical/ML relevance; needs clarifications and validation to be suitable for NIPS.
