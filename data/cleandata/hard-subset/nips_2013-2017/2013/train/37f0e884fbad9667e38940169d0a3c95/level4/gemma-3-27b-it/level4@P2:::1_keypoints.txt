- Problem: optimizing smooth, strongly convex functions with both expensive full-gradient and cheap stochastic-gradient oracles.  
- Contribution: proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) using O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls, with full-gradient calls independent of κ.  
- Core idea: replace each full-gradient epoch by O(κ²) mixed steps combining one full gradient per epoch with many stochastic gradients and average iterates to get κ-independent progress.  
- Theory: provides a self-contained convergence proof establishing linear convergence; no experimental results included.  
- Novelty/strength: interesting and novel hybrid algorithm with a clean, simple theoretical foundation and applicability to constrained optimization.  
- Comparison to SAG/Nesterov: EMGD outperforms Nesterov when κ ≤ n^{2/3} but is slower than SAG in all regimes; specific regime comparisons (e.g., κ = n^{1/2}) are discussed.  
- Advantages over SAG: works for constrained problems and has a simpler proof, potentially aiding modifications and insights.  
- Limitations noted: lacks experiments demonstrating practical impact and concrete examples of theoretical advantages.  
- Missing discussion: section 3.4 should be expanded to discuss limitations/drawbacks and comparisons (e.g., regime boundaries vs. SAG/Nesterov).  
- Practical drawback: requires pre-fixing epoch length based on κ (need to know κ), unlike SCDA/SAG which can adapt.  
- Proof presentation: technically sound but would benefit from more high-level commentary motivating key quantities and choices.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear; Table 1 is helpful, but some notation/phrasing inconsistencies should be fixed.  
- Editorial suggestions: reviewer lists specific line-by-line wording, notation, and equation clarifications to incorporate.  
- Typos: several minor typos and notation inconsistencies identified (e.g., "an convex" → "a convex", inconsistent domain notation, norm vs absolute value, log vs ln).
