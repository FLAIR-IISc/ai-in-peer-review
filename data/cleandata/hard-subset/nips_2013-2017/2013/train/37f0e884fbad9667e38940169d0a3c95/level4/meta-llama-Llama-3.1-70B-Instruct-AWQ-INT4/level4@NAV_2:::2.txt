The paper "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients" proposes a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), that combines deterministic and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The authors show that EMGD can reduce the number of full gradients needed from O(√κ log 1/ε) to O(log 1/ε), where κ is the condition number.

The paper presents an interesting and striking theoretical result. Assuming the condition number is known, EMGD can achieve a high-probability O(log 1/ε) convergence rate for batch optimization. However, there are several concerns with the current state of the paper. The theoretical analysis only covers the case where the condition number is perfectly known beforehand, and the behavior of the algorithm when this hyper-parameter is misspecified is not discussed.

The authors do not provide any guideline or theoretical argument for setting the condition number κ beforehand. Either this parameter has to be estimated, and the corresponding estimation procedure is missing, or it is assumed known, but the theoretical analysis/experimental section should cover the cases where this hyper-parameter is misspecified.

The smooth and strongly convex case can be too restrictive, and other settings, such as non-strongly convex cases, are also interesting. The authors do not review a related line of work, namely hybrid deterministic-stochastic optimization algorithms, and discussing and comparing the convergence rates would be valuable.

A thorough experimental study would be a valuable addition to the paper, including a detailed comparison with regular SGD, averaged SGD, and recent proposals for stochastic first-order optimization. The optimization setting considered in the paper is not clearly stated, and the authors should specify whether they intend to solve the deterministic optimization problem or the stochastic approximation problem.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel algorithm that combines deterministic and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence.
* The theoretical analysis shows that EMGD can reduce the number of full gradients needed from O(√κ log 1/ε) to O(log 1/ε).

Weaknesses:

* The theoretical analysis only covers the case where the condition number is perfectly known beforehand.
* The behavior of the algorithm when the condition number is misspecified is not discussed.
* The paper lacks an experimental section to assess the theoretical results and study the relevance of the algorithm on empirical data.
* The optimization setting considered in the paper is not clearly stated.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and interesting algorithm that achieves linear convergence for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems.
* The theoretical analysis is sound and shows that EMGD can reduce the number of full gradients needed.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks an experimental section to assess the theoretical results and study the relevance of the algorithm on empirical data.
* The optimization setting considered in the paper is not clearly stated.
* The behavior of the algorithm when the condition number is misspecified is not discussed.

Recommendation:

* The authors should add an experimental section to assess the theoretical results and study the relevance of the algorithm on empirical data.
* The authors should clarify the optimization setting considered in the paper and discuss the implications of their results.
* The authors should discuss the behavior of the algorithm when the condition number is misspecified and provide guidelines for setting the condition number κ beforehand.

Rating: 7/10
