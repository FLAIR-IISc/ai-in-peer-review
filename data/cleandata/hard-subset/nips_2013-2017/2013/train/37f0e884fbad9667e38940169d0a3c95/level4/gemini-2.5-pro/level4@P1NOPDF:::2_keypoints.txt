1. Proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that mixes full-gradient and stochastic-gradient oracles.  
2. Main theoretical claim: with a hybrid oracle and known condition number, the algorithm attains O(log(1/ε)) convergence with high probability.  
3. Major concern: the analysis critically requires knowing the condition number κ a priori.  
4. No analysis or empirical study of algorithm behavior under misspecified or unknown κ; robustness to κ-setting is unaddressed.  
5. Paper gives no practical guidance or procedure (even heuristic) for choosing the step-size/hyperparameter η that depends on κ.  
6. Complete absence of experiments — no empirical validation of theory, no sensitivity studies, and no comparisons to baselines.  
7. Problem setting is unclear: it’s ambiguous whether the method targets finite-sum minimization or stochastic approximation.  
8. Analysis is limited to smooth, strongly convex objectives; non-strongly convex cases are not treated.  
9. Novelty is questionable: blending deterministic and stochastic steps is not new, and the paper lacks comparison/discussion of related hybrid works (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and simpler interleaving schemes.  
10. Urgent recommendation: include experiments comparing to SGD, averaged SGD, SAG/SDCA and study sensitivity to κ and practical performance.
