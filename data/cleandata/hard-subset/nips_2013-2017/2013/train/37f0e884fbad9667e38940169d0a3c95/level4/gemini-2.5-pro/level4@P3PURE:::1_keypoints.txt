- Overall scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
- Problem addressed: optimize smooth, strongly convex function over a convex set assuming efficient projected gradients.
- Main contribution: Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid using rare full gradients and many cheap stochastic gradients.
- Complexity claim: EMGD uses O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls (independent of κ) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls.
- Algorithm structure: epochs with one full gradient followed by O(κ²) stochastic steps and averaging to reduce suboptimality by a constant factor.
- Proof: simple, self-contained convergence proof showing linear rate; analysis holds with high probability.
- Missing experiments: no empirical results to demonstrate practical utility or regimes where theory helps.
- Novelty and clarity: interesting, novel idea presented clearly with a solid theoretical analysis and helpful Table 1.
- Comparison to SAG: EMGD is more general (handles constraints), has a simpler proof, and gives high-probability guarantees vs. SAG’s expectation-based bound.
- Limitations omitted: paper lacks discussion of practical drawbacks and limitations—Section 3.4 needs expansion.
- Need for κ: EMGD requires knowing κ to fix epoch length, limiting adaptivity compared to SCDA and SAG.
- Step-size and adaptivity: EMGD lacks automatic/adaptive step-size heuristics that SCDA and SAG sometimes provide.
- Complexity regime comparisons: Nesterov better when κ > n^(2/3); EMGD better when κ < n^(2/3); SAG ties or wins in various κ ranges (noted thresholds n^(1/2), n/8).
- ML regime concern: for common regularization κ ≈ n/C', SAG often outperforms EMGD; authors should acknowledge practical settings where κ < n^(2/3).
- Missing citation: should cite and discuss Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods.
- Proof clarity: include higher-level intuition and commentary to motivate proof steps and constructions.
- Parallelization claim: incorrect to say SAG and SCDA can't be parallelized—both can use mini-batches to parallelize and reduce memory.
- Line-by-line corrections: suggest rewording line 050, specify SCDA scope at 118-119, clarify stochastic gradient condition at 132, state ∇F(w)=E[∇f(w)] at 139.
- Notation and clarity fixes: consistent domain notation (D vs Ω), use ||·|| for norms, add parentheses around (F(w_t)-F(w*)), clarify inequalities/conditions (e.g., δ ≤ exp(-1/2)), prefer ln vs log consistency.
- Typos: fix "an convex" → "a convex", standardize "strong convexity" and "condition number", and other minor typographical issues.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer satisfied with authors' response and expects suggested changes to be implemented in the revised paper.
