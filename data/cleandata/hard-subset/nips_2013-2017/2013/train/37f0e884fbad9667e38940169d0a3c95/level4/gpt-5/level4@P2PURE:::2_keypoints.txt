- Proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that mixes full-gradient and stochastic-gradient queries and proves an O(log(1/ε)) convergence rate when the condition number κ is known, removing explicit κ dependence.  
- Major theoretical concern: analysis assumes perfect prior knowledge of κ (strong convexity and smoothness), with no treatment of misspecification.  
- No empirical evaluation: the paper contains no experiments to corroborate theory or assess practical relevance.  
- Algorithm requires choosing hyper-parameter η which effectively depends on κ, but the paper gives no guidance or principled way to set κ/η.  
- Missing treatment of practical options: either describe/experiment with κ estimation heuristics or analyze robustness to κ/η misspecification.  
- Scope limited to smooth, strongly convex objectives; lacks discussion or extension to non-strongly convex settings.  
- The idea of blending deterministic and stochastic steps is not novel; the method should be related to and compared with simple alternation strategies.  
- Related-work gap: fails to review and compare to prior hybrid methods, notably Friedlander & Schmidt (SISC, 2012) and surveys therein.  
- Experimental comparisons are needed against baselines (SGD, averaged SGD) and recent methods for finite-sum problems (SAG, SDCA).  
- Problem setting unclear: paper does not explicitly state whether it targets finite-sum deterministic objectives or stochastic approximation, and Section 3.1 is misleading.
