- Proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that (theoretically) achieves an O(log(1/ε)) high-probability rate removing condition-number dependence for smooth, strongly convex functions.

- Core theoretical analysis crucially assumes the condition number κ is known and sets hyperparameter η using κ, with no treatment of unknown or misspecified κ.

- No empirical evaluation: the paper contains no experiments to validate theory, assess practical performance, or test sensitivity to κ.

- Problem setting is unclear—ambiguous whether the target is the finite-sum deterministic problem or the general stochastic approximation problem.

- Fails to discuss or compare to related hybrid deterministic–stochastic literature (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and missing convergence-rate comparisons.

- Novelty is limited: blending deterministic and stochastic gradients is not new; formulation as a single mixed-gradient update needs comparison to simpler interleaving strategies.

- Analysis is restricted to smooth, strongly convex objectives; the paper lacks discussion or extensions to non-strongly convex settings (e.g., references like Bach & Moulines).
