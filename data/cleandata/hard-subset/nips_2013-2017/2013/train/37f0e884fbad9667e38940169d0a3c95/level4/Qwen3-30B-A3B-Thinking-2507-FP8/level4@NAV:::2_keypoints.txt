- Introduces a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method combining full-gradient and stochastic-gradient queries.  
- Theoretical claim: with known condition number κ, a mixed sequence of steps yields high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence, removing κ dependence.  
- Major concern 1: Analysis assumes perfect a priori knowledge of κ and provides no theory or guidance for κ misspecification.  
- Major concern 2: No experimental section—empirical validation against SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, etc., is absent.  
- Hyperparameter η depends on κ, but the paper gives no method to set η or analyze the effect of η misspecification.  
- Setting is limited to smooth, strongly convex objectives; non-strongly convex cases (e.g., Bach & Moulines 2011) are not addressed.  
- The algorithm is presented as a “mixed” per-iteration update rather than explicitly as interleaved deterministic/stochastic steps; comparison to interleaving approaches is missing.  
- Related work on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) is omitted and convergence-rate comparisons are lacking.  
- The optimization problem setting is ambiguous—unclear whether the target is finite-sum deterministic optimization or stochastic approximation—Section 3.1 is misleading.  
- Overall: theoretical contribution is compelling but requires handling unknown κ and providing experimental validation to be convincing.
