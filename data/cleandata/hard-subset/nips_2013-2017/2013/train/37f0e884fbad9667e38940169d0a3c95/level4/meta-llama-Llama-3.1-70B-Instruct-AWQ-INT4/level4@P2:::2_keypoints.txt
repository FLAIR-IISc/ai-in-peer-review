- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a novel algorithm mixing deterministic and stochastic gradients to get linear convergence for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Claims reduction in required full gradient evaluations from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)).  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis and a formal convergence proof.  
- Discusses relationships between EMGD and algorithms like SGD and SDCA.  
- Theoretical results assume the condition number κ is known exactly; behavior under misspecification is not analyzed.  
- No experimental evaluation is provided to validate theoretical claims or show empirical behavior.  
- Limited comparison to other hybrid deterministic–stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012); convergence-rate comparisons are lacking.  
- Optimization problem setting is ambiguous—unclear whether the target is deterministic optimization or stochastic approximation.  
- Related-work review is incomplete; older hybrid-method literature is not sufficiently discussed.  
- Strengths: novel algorithmic idea, solid theoretical proof, and discussion of connections to existing methods.  
- Weaknesses: reliance on exact κ knowledge, absence of experiments, limited comparisons, and unclear problem formulation.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding experiments, analyzing κ misspecification, clarifying the optimization setting, and expanding comparisons and related work.
