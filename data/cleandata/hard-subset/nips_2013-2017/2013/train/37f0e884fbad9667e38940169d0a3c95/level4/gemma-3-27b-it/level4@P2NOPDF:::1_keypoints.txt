- Problem addressed: optimizing smooth, strongly convex functions over convex sets by combining expensive full gradients and cheap stochastic gradients.  
- Main contribution: EMGD algorithm requiring O(log(1/ε)) full gradient calls independent of condition number k and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic calls.  
- Core idea: replace each full-gradient epoch with O(k^2) mixed gradient steps and average iterates to achieve k-independent constant decrease in suboptimality.  
- Theoretical contribution: clear, self-contained proof showing linear convergence and high-probability guarantees.  
- Novelty: a new hybrid deterministic–stochastic approach that is simpler than SAG’s analysis and extends to constrained problems.  
- Comparison to Nesterov: EMGD can outperform Nesterov when k is sufficiently small (k ≤ n^{2/3} in the sum-of-n case).  
- Comparison to SAG: EMGD is generally slower than SAG, matches SAG’s big-O when k ≤ n^{1/2}, and is outperformed by SAG for many practical regimes.  
- Advantages over SAG: supports constrained optimization and gives high-probability (not only in-expectation) convergence with a simpler proof.  
- Practical limitations: requires pre-specifying steps per epoch (needs knowledge of k), unlike SCDA and SAG which avoid this.  
- Implementation concerns: lacks experiments to validate practical performance or provide concrete benefit examples.  
- Discussion gaps: section 3.4 is too brief on limitations and should be expanded to compare practical regimes and behaviors.  
- Step-size and adaptivity: SCDA offers automatic step-size selection and SAG has adaptive heuristics that EMGD lacks.  
- Regime warning: in common ML scaling (regularization ~ C/n), k ≈ n/C' often favors SAG over EMGD; this should be explicitly noted.  
- Prior work citation: should cite Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods and clarify differences.  
- Readability: paper is clear, Table 1 is helpful, but the proof would benefit from more high-level intuition and commentary on key definitions.  
- Detailed editorial fixes: suggest several line-specific clarifications, notation consistency (D vs Ω), and minor corrections (e.g., “an convex”→“a convex”, use norm symbols, replace “log” with “ln” where appropriate).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and theoretically valuable, but needs expanded discussion of limitations and experimental validation to demonstrate practical relevance.
