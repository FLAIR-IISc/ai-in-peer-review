The paper introduces a novel hybrid stochastic gradient descent algorithm, leveraging both stochastic and batch gradient evaluations. The authors demonstrate convergence to an ε-accurate solution with a complexity of O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients. 

The convergence analysis appears sound and presents a potentially valuable contribution, though a few minor corrections are needed (detailed below). 

My primary concern lies with the practical relevance of this algorithm within the typical machine learning paradigm, which is the focus of this conference. In many machine learning applications, strong convexity is implicitly enforced via regularization, leading to a parameter μ proportional to the number of samples, N (μ = C N, where C is independent of N). Under this common assumption, the proposed method only offers speedups over batch gradient descent when the number of samples is limited to O(C^3/L^3), a rather restrictive scenario. Furthermore, the stated convergence rate is probabilistic, whereas batch gradient descent offers deterministic convergence. 

This distinction is crucial and requires a thorough discussion. The authors need to clearly articulate a compelling advantage of their algorithm over standard batch gradient descent and justify the paper’s relevance to the machine learning community.

Minor comments:

- Equation (6) should read ||w^* - \hat{w}||^2.
- Line 288: Please clarify for which function equation (7) is being applied.
- Line 286: The equality sign is unnecessary and detracts from clarity.
- Equation (13): Replace absolute values with norms.
- Explicitly state the relationship L ≥ λ, even if it is intuitively obvious.
- In equation (4), replace x^ with w^ and f with F. Note that, based on Lemma 1, the first term in the maximum is always greater than the second.
- Provide a precise definition of the condition number in terms of λ and L. 

Overall, the algorithm's practical advantages over standard batch gradient descent in common machine learning optimization problems are currently unclear.
