- Paper proposes a novel hybrid SGD algorithm combining stochastic and full-batch gradient information with convergence guarantees.  
- Proven complexity: O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients to reach ε-accuracy.  
- Convergence proof appears largely correct and the contribution may be novel theoretically.  
- Main practical concern: analysis assumes strong convexity parameter μ often scales with sample size N (μ = C·N), which undermines practicality.  
- Under μ ∝ N, the method only outperforms batch GD when N = O(C^3 / L^3), a highly restrictive regime for large-scale ML.  
- Convergence guarantee is probabilistic, whereas batch GD gives deterministic convergence; no convincing argument why probabilistic guarantees are preferable.  
- Authors must articulate specific scenarios where the hybrid approach yields tangible benefits over standard batch or stochastic methods.  
- Need clearer discussion of trade-offs between probabilistic vs deterministic convergence for the ML community.  
- Equation (6) LHS should read ||w^* - ŵ||^2 (squared distance to optimum).  
- Line 288: specify which function equation (7) is applied to for clarity.  
- Line 286: remove the unnecessary equality sign to reduce clutter.  
- Equation (13): replace absolute values with norms to correctly represent vector quantities.  
- Explicitly state relationship L ≥ λ and define the condition number precisely as a function of λ and L.  
- Equation (4): use ŵ instead of x̂ and F instead of f for consistent notation; note based on Lemma 1 the first term in the max dominates the second.  
- Overall: theoretical contribution is interesting but the paper fails to demonstrate practical utility; address sample-size limitation and probabilistic vs deterministic issues to justify relevance to NIPS.
