- Main contribution: proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic optimizer with an O(log(1/ε)) convergence rate for batch problems assuming the condition number κ is known a priori.  
- Theoretical result is intriguing but only holds under the strong, unrealistic assumption of a known κ.  
- Analysis neglects κ misspecification: no theory or experiments on over/underestimating κ and its effect on convergence.  
- No experimental section at all, which is unacceptable for a paper proposing a new algorithm.  
- Algorithm requires setting η based on κ (η = c/κ) but the paper gives no practical guidance or heuristics for estimating κ.  
- Practical utility is unclear: without κ-estimation or robustness results the method is impractical.  
- Lack of novelty/context: similar hybrid approaches (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) are not adequately compared or distinguished.  
- Ambiguous problem setting: unclear whether the target is finite-sum optimization or stochastic approximation, impacting relevance to methods like SAG/SDCA vs SGD.  
- Overly restrictive assumptions: analysis limited to strongly convex, smooth objectives with no discussion of non-strongly convex cases (μ=0).  
- Required revisions: either provide theoretical/empirical analysis of κ misspecification or substantially revise claims to emphasize the known-κ limitation.  
- Overall verdict: interesting theoretical insight but limited practical impact due to reliance on known κ, missing experiments, unclear setting, and insufficient related-work comparison.
