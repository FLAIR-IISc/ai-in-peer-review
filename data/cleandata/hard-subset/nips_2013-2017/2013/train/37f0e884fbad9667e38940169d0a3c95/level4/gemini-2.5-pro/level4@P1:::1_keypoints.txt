- Problem setup: optimizing a smooth, strongly convex function over a convex set with access to an expensive full-gradient oracle and a cheap stochastic-gradient oracle.  
- Algorithm: Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) — epochs with one full gradient then many mixed stochastic steps and epoch-level averaging.  
- Complexity/result: EMGD uses O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls (independent of κ) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic calls, yielding linear convergence.  
- Practical regime: EMGD gives a theoretical speedup when the condition number κ is not too large.  
- Comparison to alternatives: In the finite-sum setting EMGD beats Nesterov when κ ≤ n^{2/3}; SAG is faster when κ ≤ n/8; EMGD and SAG match asymptotically when κ ≤ n^{1/2}.  
- Advantages: more general (handles constrained problems), simpler and self-contained convergence proof, and a high-probability (not just in-expectation) guarantee.  
- Claimed implementation benefits: authors suggest memory and parallelization advantages for EMGD, though this is unclear since SAG can use mini-batches.  
- Major cons: no experiments to assess practical impact; insufficient discussion of limitations (Section 3.4 needs improvement); proof lacks high-level intuition.  
- Practical drawback: EMGD requires pre-setting inner-loop length T based on κ, unlike SAG/SDCA which adapt and can exploit faster-than-expected convergence or local structure.  
- Common ML regime: with regularization ~1/n (so κ = O(n)), EMGD is not competitive and the authors should acknowledge this common scenario.  
- Related work missing: cite and discuss Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods, which is relevant despite differing dependence on κ.  
- Clarity request: provide higher-level motivation for key proof constructs (e.g., definitions around eq. 9) to make the analysis less "magical."  
- Minor editorial fixes: multiple typos, notation inconsistencies (W vs B), clarifications (e.g., ∇F(w)=E[∇f(w)]), and several line-specific corrections suggested.  
- Rebuttal outcome: reviewer is satisfied with the authors’ response and expects the suggested revisions in the final version.
