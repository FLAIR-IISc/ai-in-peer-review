- Proposes EMGD: a hybrid first-order method that computes one full gradient per epoch and uses variance-reduced mixed stochastic steps within a shrinking trust region to yield high-probability linear convergence.
- Main theoretical claim: ε-optimality with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls, where κ = L/λ.
- Positions itself between full-gradient accelerated methods (O(√κ log(1/ε))) and stochastic/finite-sum variance-reduced methods (SAG/SDCA), aiming to minimize full-gradient evaluations.
- Strength: Clear theoretical result showing decoupling of full-gradient call count from κ with high-probability guarantees.
- Strength: Intuitive “mixed gradient” construction that yields variance reduction within a shrinking domain explaining linear convergence.
- Strength: Applicable to constrained problems with only O(d) memory, unlike some finite-sum methods.
- Major weakness: Algorithm and analysis require knowing L and λ (κ); no guidance, heuristics, or robustness analysis for misspecified κ.
- Major weakness: No experiments to assess empirical performance, sensitivity to κ, or wall-clock trade-offs between full and stochastic gradients.
- Major weakness: Insufficient discussion and comparison with prior hybrid deterministic–stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).
- Major weakness: Unclear whether the per-iteration mixed-gradient design is essential versus simple alternation of deterministic and stochastic steps.
- Ambiguity: Inconsistent problem setting—paper switches between stochastic expectation F(w)=E[f(w)] and finite-sum F(w)= (1/n)∑ fi(w) without clarifying which is primary.
- Scope concern: Analysis limited to smooth, strongly convex objectives; unclear extension to non-strongly convex or nonsmooth cases.
- Suggestion: Provide practical rules or adaptive procedures for selecting η and T when L and λ are unknown and analyze mis-specification effects.
- Suggestion: Add empirical evaluation on logistic/least-squares tasks comparing to SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, and accelerated methods, reporting objective vs. full/stochastic evaluations and wall-clock time.
- Suggestion: Expand related-work discussion to position EMGD’s high-probability, constrained, and memory properties relative to existing hybrid and variance-reduced methods.
- Clarity fixes: Correct terminology (use “condition number”), define oracles and projection domain explicitly, and state whether comparisons hold with high probability or in expectation.
- Presentation: Ensure consistent naming (EMGD) and clearly state the optimization/oracle model in Section 3.1.
- Quality: Analysis appears technically sound with a clear inductive proof and high-probability bound, but practical relevance is limited by missing experiments and robustness study.
- Originality: The mixed per-iteration use of full and stochastic gradients with a shrinking domain is interesting but closely related to prior variance-reduction and hybrid approaches; deeper comparison needed.
- Significance: Potentially significant if empirically robust to κ misspecification; current lack of experiments and robustness analysis limits impact.
