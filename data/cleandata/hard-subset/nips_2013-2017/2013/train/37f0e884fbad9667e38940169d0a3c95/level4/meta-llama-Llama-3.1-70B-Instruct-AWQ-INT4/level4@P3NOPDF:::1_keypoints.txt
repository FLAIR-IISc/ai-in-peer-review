- Proposes a novel hybrid algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), for smooth strongly convex optimization using both full and stochastic gradients.  
- Claims EMGD attains linear convergence while requiring fewer expensive full-gradient oracle calls than existing methods.  
- Theoretical analysis is self-contained, clean, and technically sound; the simple convergence proof is a notable contribution.  
- EMGD is argued to be more applicable to constrained problems and simpler to analyze than methods like SAG.  
- Practical relevance is unclear because the paper lacks experimental results demonstrating empirical performance.  
- The paper should expand discussion of limitations, drawbacks, and comparisons to existing algorithms in practice.  
- Clarity is generally good, with a well-organized structure and helpful summary table, but high-level proof intuition is missing.  
- Originality: novel combination of known techniques, a valuable contribution to smooth convex optimization.  
- Significance: strong theoretical potential but needs empirical validation to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Detailed manuscript edits suggested (phrasing, explicit statements, correction of claims about distributed computing, clarification of derivation steps).  
- Specific technical corrections recommended (e.g., specify domains, state ∇F(w)=E[∇f(w], clarify conditions like “for all w”, inequality validity conditions).  
- Typographical and notation fixes required (several line-by-line typos and inconsistent symbols).  
- After rebuttal, reviewer is satisfied pending implementation of suggested changes, especially added experiments and fuller discussion of algorithm properties.
