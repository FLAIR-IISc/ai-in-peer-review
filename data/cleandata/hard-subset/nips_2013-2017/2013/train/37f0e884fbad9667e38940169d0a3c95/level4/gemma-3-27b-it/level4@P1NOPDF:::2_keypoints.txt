- Paper proposes a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method combining full-gradient and stochastic-gradient oracles and claims to remove condition-number dependence.

- Main theoretical claim: with known condition number κ and a hybrid oracle, a tuned mixture of steps achieves O(log(1/ε)) convergence with high probability.

- Analysis assumes κ is known a priori; behavior under unknown or misspecified κ is not analyzed.

- No procedure, heuristic, or experiments are provided for estimating κ or setting η (the step depending on κ).

- The paper lacks empirical evaluation entirely; no experiments validate theory or assess practical relevance.

- If κ is assumed known, the authors should either treat the work as purely theoretical or analyze/experiment with hyperparameter misspecification.

- Restrictive setting: analysis only covers smooth, strongly convex objectives and ignores non-strongly convex or other convexity regimes common in practice.

- Novelty concern: mixing deterministic and stochastic steps is not new; the paper does not clearly relate its “mixed” update to existing alternation/interleaving approaches.

- Related work review is incomplete—missing discussion of hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and comparisons of convergence rates.

- Experimental comparisons should include standard baselines (SGD, averaged SGD) and modern stochastic methods (SAG, SDCA, etc.).

- Minor issue: the optimization problem setting is unclear—finite-sum deterministic objective vs. stochastic expectation is not clearly specified and should be clarified.
