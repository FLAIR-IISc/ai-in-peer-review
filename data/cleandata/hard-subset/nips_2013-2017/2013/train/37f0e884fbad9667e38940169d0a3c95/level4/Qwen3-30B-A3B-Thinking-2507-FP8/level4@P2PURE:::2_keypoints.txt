- Main contribution: Proposes a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method that, assuming known condition number κ, removes κ from the convergence rate and achieves high-probability O(log(1/ε)) for finite-sum problems.

- Major flaw: Theoretical analysis requires exact knowledge of κ and gives no treatment of misspecification or adaptive estimation.

- Missing experiments: No empirical validation is provided to assess practical performance or robustness to κ misspecification.

- Detailed concern 1 (κ hyper-parameter): Algorithm depends on η set via κ with no guidance on estimating κ or handling incorrect values.

- Detailed concern 2 (assumptions): Restrictive smooth and strongly convex setting; non-strongly convex cases are common and unanalyzed.

- Detailed concern 3 (novelty/related work): Blending deterministic and stochastic steps is not novel; the paper omits comparisons to prior hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and simpler interleaving schemes.

- Detailed concern 4 (ambiguous setting): Unclear whether the target is finite-sum deterministic optimization or stochastic approximation; theoretical setup and claims are misleading.

- Detailed concern 5 (experiments needed): A thorough experimental comparison to baselines (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.) is essential, especially to validate κ robustness.

- Overall assessment: The theoretical result is interesting but its impact is undermined by missing analysis for unknown κ, absence of experiments, omission of related work, and an ambiguous problem statement.
