- Proposes EMGD, a novel hybrid deterministic–stochastic optimization method combining full and stochastic gradient evaluations.  
- Shows linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient evaluations independent of condition number κ, breaking the usual O(√κ log(1/ε)) barrier.  
- Claims an ε-optimal solution with O(log(1/ε)) full and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient evaluations, matching rates of SAG/SDCA and offering extra benefits.  
- Analysis assumes perfect prior knowledge of the condition number κ, which is often unrealistic in practice.  
- Paper fails to analyze or discuss robustness to κ misspecification or provide a method to estimate κ.  
- No empirical validation or experiments comparing EMGD to SGD, SAG, SDCA to assess practical performance.  
- Insufficient literature contextualization; does not adequately compare to prior hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt SISC 2012).  
- Theoretical setup is confusing—objective written as deterministic F(w)=(1/n)∑f_i(w) but analysis framed as solving E[F(w)], creating ambiguity.  
- Does not address the non-strongly convex case or discuss limitations for more general optimization settings.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution but weakened by practical concerns (κ requirement, lack of experiments) that should be addressed.
