- Introduces a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method combining full-gradient and stochastic queries.  
- Claims a high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence rate for batch optimization that removes dependence on the condition number κ.  
- Theoretical analysis assumes κ is known exactly a priori and provides no treatment of κ misspecification (over/underestimation).  
- No procedure or theoretical guidance is given for estimating κ, yet the update step-size η depends directly on κ, making the algorithm impractical as presented.  
- Authors acknowledge difficulty of unknown κ but offer no experimental evaluation of robustness to κ misspecification.  
- Paper contains no experiments; empirical validation and comparisons to standard baselines (SGD, SAG, averaged SGD) are missing.  
- Fails to situate the work within related literature on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and lacks rate comparisons to prior approaches.  
- Problem setting is ambiguous—unclear whether the target is deterministic finite-sum minimization or stochastic approximation—leading to misleading theoretical framing.  
- Restricts analysis to strongly convex/smooth settings and does not address non-strongly convex cases, limiting practical applicability.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical claim but undermined by unrealistic κ assumption, lack of experiments, missing related-work discussion, and unclear problem formulation; needs κ-sensitivity experiments, literature comparisons, and clarified setting.
