- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a first-order algorithm combining full and stochastic gradients.  
- Claims convergence rate O(log(1/ε)) independent of condition number κ, assuming κ is known.  
- Addresses important practical issue: mitigates ill-conditioning that makes full gradients costly in large-scale ML.  
- Theoretical analysis appears rigorous and the κ-independence claim is compelling.  
- Properly situates work relative to epoch gradient descent and existing iteration-complexity results (Table 1).  
- Paper is generally well-written with consistent notation and clear presentation.  
- Major weakness: no empirical evaluation to validate practical performance.  
- Strong assumption that κ is known a priori limits practical applicability.  
- No analysis or experiments on robustness to misspecified κ, a common real-world concern.  
- Insufficient comparison to other hybrid deterministic–stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012).  
- Ambiguity in the problem formulation: unclear whether the setting is deterministic optimization with randomness or stochastic approximation.  
- Focus on smooth, strongly convex functions restricts broader applicability; performance in weaker settings is unexplored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, potentially impactful theoretical result with rigorous analysis.  
- Arguments for rejection: lack of experiments, impractical strong assumption, and missing robustness discussion.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after experiments (including misspecified κ) and stronger comparisons to related methods.
