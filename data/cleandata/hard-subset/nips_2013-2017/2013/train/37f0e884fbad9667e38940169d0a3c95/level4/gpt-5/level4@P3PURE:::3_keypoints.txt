- Paper introduces a hybrid optimization method alternating stochastic and full gradients, with convergence to ε-accuracy using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Convergence analysis appears correct and novel aside from minor issues.
- Primary concern: practical relevance in ML, since strong convexity typically comes from regularization and μ scales with sample size N (μ = C N), which changes regime behavior.
- Under regularization-induced μ, the method is faster than batch GD only when N = O(C^3 / L^3), a restrictive and likely unrealistic regime for practical ML.
- Convergence guarantee holds only with high probability, whereas batch GD’s guarantee is deterministic—this weakens practical comparison.
- The paper should analyze and discuss realistic regimes (in N, L, μ/λ) where the hybrid method outperforms batch GD and justify relevance for ML under standard regularization scaling.
