- Proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that mixes full and stochastic gradients and claims removal of condition-number dependence in the convergence rate.  
- Main theoretical claim: with known condition number and a hybrid oracle, a suitable mix of steps yields O(log(1/ε)) convergence with high probability.  
- Major concern: analysis crucially assumes the condition number κ is exactly known a priori; no theory for κ misspecification.  
- No experiments section — the paper lacks empirical validation of the algorithm and the theoretical claims.  
- The algorithm (EGD) requires hyperparameter η (hence κ); authors give no guidance or estimation procedure for setting κ, nor study effects of over/underestimating it.  
- Paper omits consideration of non-strongly convex settings; results limited to smooth strongly convex case despite other important regimes (cite Bach & Moulines).  
- Blending deterministic and stochastic updates is not novel — related algorithmic forms (alternating/interleaving updates) are not discussed or compared.  
- The proposed “mixed” per-iteration update should be related to and compared with interleaving deterministic/stochastic-step schemes.  
- Important related work on hybrid deterministic–stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) is not reviewed or contrasted in terms of convergence rates.  
- A thorough experimental comparison with SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc., is recommended but missing.  
- The optimization setting is unclear: paper does not clearly state whether it targets finite-sum deterministic problems or stochastic expectation problems, making theoretical claims ambiguous.
