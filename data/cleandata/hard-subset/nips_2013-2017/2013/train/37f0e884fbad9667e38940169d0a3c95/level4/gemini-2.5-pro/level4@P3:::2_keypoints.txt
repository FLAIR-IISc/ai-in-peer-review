- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid epoch-based algorithm using one full gradient per epoch plus variance-reduced stochastic updates.
- Key theoretical claim: EMGD achieves ε-optimality with O(log 1/ε) full gradients and O(κ² log 1/ε) stochastic gradients, making full-gradient count independent of condition number κ.
- Central idea: store a full gradient as an anchor each epoch and use a "mixed gradient" combining that anchor with a stochastic component.
- Strength: Provides a significant theoretical result addressing the bottleneck of full-gradient evaluations on ill-conditioned problems.
- Strength: Novel mixing of techniques (mixed gradient + shrinking-domain epochs) that yields deterministic-style linear convergence using stochastic information.
- Strength: More general than SAG/SDCA—handles constrained problems and does not require finite-sum structure.
- Strength: Offers high-probability convergence guarantees, stronger than typical in-expectation results for many stochastic methods.
- Weakness: Requires prior knowledge of κ (or L and λ) to set step size and inner iterations; no analysis or practical scheme for misspecified or unknown κ.
- Weakness: No empirical validation—lacks experiments to confirm convergence rates, sensitivity to κ, or wall-clock comparisons to baselines.
- Weakness: Related-work discussion omits hybrid deterministic-stochastic literature (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and lacks comparison to those methods.
- Weakness: Ambiguous problem setting—unclear whether the method targets finite-sum problems or general stochastic expectations, complicating comparisons to SAG/SDCA.
- Detailed comment: The mixed-gradient resembles variance-reduction (e.g., SVRG); connections should be discussed.
- Detailed comment: EMGD’s κ² dependence in stochastic gradients can be prohibitive for large κ; empirical study needed to assess trade-offs.
- Detailed comment: Claim of better suitability for distributed computing is speculative and should be supported by algorithmic details or experiments.
