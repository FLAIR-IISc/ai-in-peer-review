- Introduces a novel hybrid stochastic/batch SGD with convergence using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Convergence proof appears correct and novel, with only a few minor errors noted.
- Main concern: relevance to ML is weak because strong convexity in ML is typically induced by regularization (μ = C·N), making the method better than batch GD only when N = O(C^3/L^3), an uninteresting regime.
- The algorithm’s convergence guarantee holds only with high probability, whereas batch gradient descent has deterministic rates — this weakens the practical appeal.
- The paper needs a thorough discussion comparing advantages over batch gradient descent under realistic ML assumptions.
