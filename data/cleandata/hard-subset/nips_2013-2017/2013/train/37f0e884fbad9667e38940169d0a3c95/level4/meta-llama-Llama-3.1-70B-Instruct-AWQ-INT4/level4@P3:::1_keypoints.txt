- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a novel algorithm using both full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex optimization.  
- Main theoretical claim: linear convergence with only O(log(1/ε)) full gradients (condition-number independent) and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Provides thorough, self-contained convergence analysis and comparison to existing methods, with a simpler proof and ability to handle constrained problems.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; Table 1 effectively summarizes iteration complexities.  
- Proof is clear but would benefit from additional high-level intuition about the authors’ approach.  
- Key technical innovation: mixing full-gradient and stochastic-gradient steps to reduce full-gradient dependence on κ.  
- Major weakness: no experiments — empirical comparisons with algorithms like SAG and SDCA on real datasets are needed.  
- Significance: valuable contribution to optimization/machine learning, but authors should avoid overclaiming applicability given potential dataset-dependent performance.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with potential to be influential if experiments and a more nuanced discussion of limitations are added.  
- Scores: Quality 8, Clarity 9, Originality 9, Significance 8.
