- Paper proposes a novel hybrid SGD combining full-batch and stochastic updates with a convergence proof.  
- Guarantees: O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient computations.  
- Convergence analysis is sound and appears novel, aside from minor issues.  
- Main concern: practical relevance for ML — in regularized ERM μ often scales with N, making the algorithm advantageous only for small N (N = O(C^3/L^3)).  
- Under realistic μ ≈ C·N, the method may be uninteresting for large-scale ML where N is huge.  
- The algorithm’s guarantee is high-probability (probabilistic) while batch GD’s guarantee is deterministic.  
- Authors must clearly discuss scenarios where their method outperforms standard batch gradient descent in ML settings.
