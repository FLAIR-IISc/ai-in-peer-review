- Proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that mixes full-gradient and stochastic-gradient oracle calls.  
- Main theoretical claim: with known condition number (λ and L), a suitable mixture achieves high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence, removing explicit κ-dependence.  
- Significant theoretical contribution under the strong-convexity + smoothness assumption.  
- Analysis assumes the condition number κ is known a priori; behavior under misspecified or unknown κ is unexplored.  
- The algorithm requires tuning η (via Eq. 8/7), but the paper gives no procedure or theoretical guidance for estimating κ or selecting η.  
- No empirical evaluation — major weakness given the paper’s algorithmic contribution; experiments could probe practical relevance and robustness to misspecification.  
- Suggests either providing an estimation/heurstic for κ or analyzing convergence when η is set too large/small.  
- Restrictive focus on smooth, strongly convex objectives; behavior in non-strongly convex settings is not addressed (see Bach & Moulines 2011).  
- Claim of “hybrid” approach is not novel; blending deterministic and stochastic steps has prior art and should be positioned against those methods.  
- The update structure (single “mixed” update then gradient-like step) differs from common interleaving schemes and lacks comparative discussion.  
- Missing citation and comparison to prior hybrid deterministic–stochastic work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt, SISC 2012) and to modern variance-reduced methods (SAG, SDCA, etc.).  
- No theoretical or empirical comparison of convergence rates to related algorithms; such comparisons would add value.  
- Optimization problem setting is unclear: deterministic finite-sum vs stochastic expectation is not explicitly defined, leading to ambiguous claims.  
- Recommend clearer problem statement, inclusion of experiments comparing to SGD, averaged SGD, SAG/SDCA, and analysis of hyperparameter misspecification.
