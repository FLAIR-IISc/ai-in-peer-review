- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm using one full gradient per epoch plus stochastic gradients.  
- EMGD achieves an ǫ-optimal solution with O(log(1/ǫ)) full gradients (independent of κ) and O(κ² log(1/ǫ)) stochastic gradients.  
- Algorithm runs in epochs: compute a full gradient, then perform mixed gradient steps combining full and stochastic information; averaging within an epoch yields constant suboptimality reduction.  
- Analysis gives linear convergence and decouples expensive full-gradient calls from the condition number κ.  
- Theoretical comparisons: EMGD can outperform Nesterov when κ ≤ n^(2/3), but is generally less favorable than SAG in many regimes.  
- Advantages over some alternatives: naturally handles constrained optimization (with projection) and has a simpler, high-probability convergence proof.  
- Strength: novel and interesting hybrid framework with a clean, self-contained convergence proof.  
- Strength: broader applicability to constrained problems compared to methods like SAG/SDCA.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Weakness: no empirical evaluation or experiments to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Weakness: inadequate discussion of limitations and comparative drawbacks versus state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis lacks high-level intuition to motivate key steps and definitions.  
- Quality: technically sound and theoretically supported, but practical potential unclear without experiments.  
- Major improvement needed: expand Section 3.4 to explicitly state regimes where EMGD is worse (e.g., κ > n^(2/3)) and address κ ∝ n common in ML where EMGD gives no theoretical improvement over Nesterov.  
- Practical drawback: EMGD requires prior knowledge of κ to set parameters, unlike more adaptive methods (SAG/SDCA).  
- Literature suggestion: cite and discuss Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods for better context.  
- Clarity: overall well-written; Table 1 effectively summarizes complexities but should be cross-referenced in the introduction.  
- Clarity/Proofs: proofs are complete but would benefit from more high-level commentary, explicit lemma references, and clearer summation notation.  
- Specific editorial suggestions: clarify SDCA’s applicability, state ∇F(w)=E[∇f(w)] for the oracle, soften claims about parallelizability of SAG/SDCA, and fix notation/typos (e.g., "an convex", log vs ln).  
- Minor technical suggestion: explicitly mention the condition δ ≤ e^(−1/2) when first used, use parentheses in summations, and ensure consistent domain notation.
