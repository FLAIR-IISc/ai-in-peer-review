- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): mix one full gradient per epoch with multiple stochastic updates centered at the previous epoch average and shrinking trust region.  
- Main theoretical claim: linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, where κ = L/λ.  
- Positions EMGD between full-gradient methods (e.g., Nesterov) and semi-/incremental methods (SAG/SDCA), claiming advantages for constrained problems and low memory.  
- Convergence proof is sound and novel in its mixed-gradient plus shrinking-constraint analysis, with careful high-probability arguments.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-structured but needs more explicit notation and assumptions (e.g., precise κ definition and scaling of L, λ).  
- The mixed-gradient construction plus shrinking ball and high-probability linear-rate appear original relative to prior hybrid schemes.  
- Practical relevance is unclear: under common ML scalings (strong convexity from regularizer), EMGD may only beat accelerated methods in a small-N regime.  
- Need clearer distinction between sum vs average objectives and how L and λ scale with sample size N when comparing complexities.  
- Pros: reduces full-gradient calls to O(log(1/ε)), gives high-probability linear convergence, handles constraints, uses O(d) memory, and is parallelizable for full-gradient step.  
- Cons: requires O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients which can be large, assumes knowledge/tuning of κ, and offers a high-probability (not deterministic) rate complicating comparisons.  
- Suggest providing empirical wall-clock and gradient-call comparisons to Nesterov, SAG, and SDCA across κ and N, including constrained settings.  
- Suggest analyzing or experimenting on reducing the κ^2 dependence (e.g., adaptive T or variance-reduction) and giving κ-estimation or κ-free schedules.  
- Minor fixes: correct notation in several equations (norm squares, vector norms), state L ≥ λ and κ = L/λ explicitly, and clarify certain proof references (Jensen, Hoeffding–Azuma).  
- Overall: theoretically interesting and novel but needs clearer practical motivation, empirical evidence, and fixes to notation/clarity; reviewer is on the fence about significance for the NIPS ML audience.
