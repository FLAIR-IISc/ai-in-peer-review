- Novel hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method with a theoretical O(log(1/ε)) rate under certain conditions.  
- Main theoretical strength: removes condition-number dependence under assumed known strong convexity and smoothness constants.  
- Analysis is limited to the case where the condition number is perfectly known; behavior under misspecification is not studied.  
- No experimental results — a major weakness preventing assessment of practical relevance and empirical performance.  
- Paper offers no guidance on how to estimate the condition number or choose the mixing parameter η in practice.  
- Theoretical analysis does not characterize convergence when η is set too high or too low.  
- Restriction to strongly convex and smooth objectives limits applicability; non-strongly convex settings are not considered.  
- No discussion of how the mixed-update approach compares to alternating deterministic/stochastic strategies.  
- Inadequate engagement with prior hybrid optimization work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and related literature.  
- The optimization setting (finite-sum vs. stochastic approximation) is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Recommends empirical comparisons to standard algorithms (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA) to validate claims.  
- Overall: promising theoretical contribution but limited impact without experiments, robustness analysis, literature comparison, and clearer problem formulation.
