- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a novel algorithm combining deterministic and stochastic gradient steps for smooth, strongly convex optimization.  
- Claims EMGD reduces full-gradient calls from O(√κ log 1/ε) to O(log 1/ε), where κ is the condition number.  
- The theoretical analysis is well-presented and gives clear insight into the algorithm's behavior.  
- Analysis assumes the condition number κ is perfectly known; behavior under misspecification is not addressed (significant limitation).  
- No experimental evaluation is provided to validate theoretical claims or assess real-world performance.  
- Lacks a detailed empirical and computational comparison with existing methods (e.g., SGD, SAG), leaving efficiency/accuracy tradeoffs unclear.  
- The optimization setting is not clearly stated; it appears to solve a deterministic problem using a stochastic algorithm without explicit clarification.  
- Insufficient guidance on algorithm hyperparameters (e.g., how to set step size η) and practical tuning.  
- With added experiments, clearer comparisons, and setting/hyperparameter clarification, the paper could be substantially strengthened.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, solid theory, and potential efficiency gains over SGD/SAG for certain problems.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on known κ, missing experiments, and inadequate comparisons/clarification of the setting.
