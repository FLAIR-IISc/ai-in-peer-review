- Problem: optimize smooth, strongly convex functions with access to expensive full gradients and cheap stochastic gradients.  
- Contribution: proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) mixing one full gradient per epoch with O(κ²) stochastic steps to reduce full-gradient calls.  
- Complexity claim: EMGD uses O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls (independent of κ) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls.  
- Core idea: replace each full-gradient epoch by O(κ²) mixed-gradient steps and average iterates to obtain constant suboptimality reduction.  
- Theoretical support: provides a simple, self-contained convergence proof showing linear convergence under stated conditions.  
- Empirics: no experimental evaluation is included, limiting assessment of practical performance.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on recent mixed-oracle methods; compares to SAG and Nesterov in various parameter regimes.  
- Comparative regimes: SAG beats Nesterov when κ ≤ n/8; EMGD beats Nesterov when κ ≤ n^{2/3}; SAG generally outperforms EMGD (e.g., for 1 ≤ κ ≤ n^{1/2}).  
- Example complexity comparison: for κ = n^{1/2}, SAG and EMGD are O(n log(1/ε)) while Nesterov is O(n^{5/4} log(1/ε)).  
- Advantages over SAG: applicable to constrained optimization (when gradient mapping is tractable) and has a much simpler proof that may ease extensions.  
- Claimed practical benefits: potential memory and parallelization advantages mentioned but not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Pros (summary): novel/interesting idea, clean/simple theory, generality to constrained problems, clear writing, self-contained proof, helpful summary table.  
- Cons (summary): no experiments, insufficient discussion of limitations vs. existing methods, proof lacks high-level intuition.  
- Major methodological drawback: requires fixing epoch length based on known κ, unlike SCDA and SAG which adapt and may perform better in practice.  
- Step-size and adaptation: SCDA offers automatic step-size; SAG uses an adaptive heuristic—EMGD lacks these adaptive features.  
- Recommendations: expand Section 3.4 on limitations/drawbacks and provide a more nuanced comparison with SAG and Nesterov across parameter regimes.  
- Clarity notes: generally clear; Table 1 is useful; proofs well-structured but need higher-level motivation for key definitions and steps.  
- Originality: novel combination of existing techniques with a simpler proof approach.  
- Significance: theoretical contribution is promising but practical relevance remains to be demonstrated via experiments.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: wording and clarity tweaks at multiple line numbers (e.g., replace “are summarized” phrasing, clarify SCDA scope, state conditions explicitly, notation fixes).  
- Typos: several small typos and notation inconsistencies identified (e.g., “an convex” → “a convex”, domain notation D vs Ω).  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer satisfied with revisions and believes core concerns were addressed, though experiments would still strengthen the paper.
