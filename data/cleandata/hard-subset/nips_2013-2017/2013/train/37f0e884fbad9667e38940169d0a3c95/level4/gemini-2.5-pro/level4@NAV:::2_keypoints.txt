- Proposes a hybrid optimization algorithm that interleaves deterministic full-gradient and stochastic gradient oracles.  
- Main theoretical claim: with a hybrid oracle and known condition number, the method attains O(log(1/ε)) convergence with high probability.  
- Theoretical guarantees rely on prior knowledge of the exact condition number κ, so the claim of κ-independence is conditional.  
- No analysis or empirical evaluation of algorithm robustness when κ (or η) is misspecified.  
- The update uses a hyperparameter η that requires knowing κ (λ and L), but the paper gives no practical guidance or estimation procedure.  
- Analysis is restricted to smooth, strongly convex problems; non-strongly convex cases are not addressed.  
- The idea of mixing deterministic and stochastic gradients is not novel and needs comparison to simpler interleaving strategies.  
- Fails to cite and discuss relevant hybrid optimization literature (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and lacks convergence-rate comparisons.  
- Completely lacks an experimental section and empirical comparisons to baselines like SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, etc.  
- The optimization problem setting is ambiguous (finite-sum vs. stochastic approximation); Section 3.1 is unclear and needs clarification.
