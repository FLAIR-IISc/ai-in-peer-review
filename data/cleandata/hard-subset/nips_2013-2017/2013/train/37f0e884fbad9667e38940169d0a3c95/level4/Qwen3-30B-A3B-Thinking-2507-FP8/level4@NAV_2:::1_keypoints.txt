- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) for smooth strongly convex optimization, mixing full and stochastic gradients.  
- Achieves O(log(1/ε)) full gradient calls (independent of κ) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient calls.  
- Replaces each full gradient step by O(κ²) mixed-gradient steps to obtain constant suboptimality reduction per epoch.  
- Provides high-probability linear convergence analysis; proof is simpler and self-contained.  
- Contrasts with SAG/SDCA which converge in expectation and require κ-dependent storage.  
- EMGD applies to constrained problems assuming efficient gradient mapping, unlike SAG/SDCA.  
- EMGD outperforms Nesterov when κ ≤ n^(2/3), but is dominated by SAG for n^(1/2) < κ ≤ n/8.  
- Key advantages over SAG/SDCA: applicability to constraints, simpler high-probability proof, and O(d) storage.  
- Main drawback: no experiments to demonstrate practical performance or convergence speed.  
- Section 3.4 insufficiently compares limitations vs. SAG/SDCA, notably EMGD’s need for prior κ knowledge.  
- Paper overstates SAG/SDCA parallelization limits; mini-batch/parallel variants (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt) exist.  
- Fails to discuss regimes where EMGD is impractical (e.g., typical ML regularization yields κ = Θ(n/C), favoring SAG).  
- Technical contribution is sound and theory correctly bounds κ dependencies; omission of experiments is a theoretical but notable weakness.  
- Clarity is good overall; proofs are logical but would benefit from higher-level intuition for key steps (e.g., mixed gradient design).  
- Table 1 effectively summarizes iteration complexities and aids readability.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of epoch-based mixed gradients decoupling full gradient cost from κ, building on EGD.  
- Significance: meaningful theoretical advance for constrained optimization, but limited immediate practical impact without empirical validation.  
- Critical revision requested: expand Section 3.4 to compare EMGD, SAG, and Nesterov across κ regimes and address prior-κ requirement.  
- Suggest citing Friedlander & Schmidt [5] as related hybrid/parallel work with κ-dependent rates.  
- Specific editorial suggestions: clarify Table 1 reference, state SDCA assumptions, explicit gradient oracle domain and expectation relation, fix SDCA typo, and add minor clarifications in proofs.  
- Typos to fix: "an convex"→"a convex"; standardize domain notation (Ω vs D); use ‖·‖ for norms; use "ln" consistently.
