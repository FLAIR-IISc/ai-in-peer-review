- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that combines stochastic and batch gradients for linear convergence on smooth, strongly convex problems.
- Convergence proof is novel and appears correct.
- Shows EMGD reaches ε-accuracy with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Provides a detailed comparison with existing algorithms and highlights scenarios where EMGD is advantageous.
- Reviewer questions relevance to machine learning because strong convexity from regularization makes μ ~ N, limiting practical speedups.
- Under typical ML assumptions, EMGD is faster than batch GD only if sample size N ≤ O(C^3 / L^3), which may be unrealistic.
- Convergence guarantees are high-probability only, whereas batch gradient descent has deterministic guarantees.
- This difference in probabilistic vs deterministic guarantees may reduce practical appeal.
- Authors should better justify relevance to the ML community and clarify practical advantages over batch GD.
- Minor issues to fix: correct typos, clarify notation, and provide more precise definitions.
- Strengths: novel/correct proof, thorough convergence analysis, and detailed comparisons.
- Weaknesses: unclear ML relevance, high-probability guarantees, and potentially unrealistic assumptions.
- Acceptance criteria: strengthen ML relevance arguments, address minor comments, and clarify practical advantages over batch GD.
