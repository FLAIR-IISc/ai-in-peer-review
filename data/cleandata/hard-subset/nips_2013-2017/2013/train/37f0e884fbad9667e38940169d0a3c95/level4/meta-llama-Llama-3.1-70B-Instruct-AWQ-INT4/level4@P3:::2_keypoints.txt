- Proposes EMGD, a novel algorithm combining deterministic and stochastic GD that attains linear convergence with high probability and reduces dependence on the condition number.  
- Addresses computational cost of full-gradient evaluations by mixing epoch-wise deterministic and stochastic updates.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear algorithmic description and rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Uses two oracles (gradient and function) to allow flexible and efficient optimization.  
- Provides thorough related-work discussion, highlighting advantages and limitations of existing methods.  
- Assumes the condition number is known a priori; needs analysis or experiments for misspecification/unknown cases.  
- Lacks an experiments section; requires empirical evaluation on real-world datasets and comparison to baselines.  
- Needs a more detailed comparison to other hybrid deterministic–stochastic algorithms (theory and experiments).  
- Optimization setting is unclear — should state whether the target is deterministic optimization or stochastic approximation.  
- Insufficient explanation and guidance for hyper-parameter choices (step size, number of iterations).  
- Strengths summarized: novel algorithm, theoretical linear convergence, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses summarized: reliance on known condition number, no experiments, limited comparisons, unclear setting, weak hyper-parameter discussion.  
- Review scores: Originality 8/10, Impact 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Significance 8/10.
