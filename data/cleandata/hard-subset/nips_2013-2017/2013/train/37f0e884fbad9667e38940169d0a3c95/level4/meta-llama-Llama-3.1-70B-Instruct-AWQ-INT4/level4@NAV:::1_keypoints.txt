- Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm mixing full-gradient and stochastic steps for smooth, strongly convex optimization over convex sets.  
- Claims linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient oracle calls (k = condition number).  
- Provides a self-contained convergence proof, reported to be independent of the condition number.  
- Novel and interesting algorithmic idea combining deterministic and stochastic updates.  
- Theoretical analysis is clean, simple, and technically sound.  
- More general than SAG because it handles constrained optimization.  
- Proof is simpler than SAG’s convergence proof.  
- Lacks experimental results to demonstrate practical performance and relevance.  
- No discussion of algorithmic limitations or practical drawbacks.  
- Proof presentation lacks high-level commentary to explain key insights.  
- Missing a concrete worked example for the “sum of n functions” application.  
- Suggests adding comparisons to SAG and SCDA to highlight trade-offs and benefits.  
- Recommends discussing the need to fix epoch lengths in advance as a limitation.  
- Notes the regularization parameter is often O(1/n), which affects condition number and performance.  
- Requests citation of Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) on hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods with linear convergence.  
- Paper is fairly clear overall but needs consistent notation and clearer proof exposition.  
- Originality: presents a novel combination of known techniques.  
- Significance: solid theoretical contribution but practical impact is unverified without experiments.
