- Proposal: hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method alternating full (deterministic) and stochastic gradient oracles, claiming high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence without κ-dependence when κ is known.

- Strength: conceptually appealing hybrid that aims to combine deterministic exponential convergence with stochastic data-scaling benefits.

- Strength: clean, surprising theoretical result showing O(log(1/ε)) convergence without explicit dependence on κ under the known-κ assumption.

- Major concern 1: Theory assumes exact knowledge of the condition number κ and gives no guidance on setting κ or η in practice.

- Major concern 1 (cont.): No analysis of algorithm robustness under κ misspecification—need either an estimator/heuristic plus experiments or formal robustness results for over/under-estimation.

- Major concern 2: No experimental evaluation; empirical validation and comparisons to SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, and ablations on κ/η and oracle frequency are missing.

- Major concern 3: Ambiguity about the optimization setting (finite-sum vs stochastic approximation); the paper must explicitly state the target problem and align assumptions/rates accordingly.

- Major concern 4: Inadequate discussion of related hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt, 2012) and unclear relation between the proposed mixed-update EGD and explicit alternation schemes and their oracle costs.

- Major concern 5: Restrictive assumptions (smoothness and strong convexity); the paper should discuss or extend to merely convex cases and relate to known convex/strongly-convex behavior.

- Detailed comment: EGD’s mixed-gradient update requires η that depends on κ, and the paper contains notation/terminology issues (confusing L and λ) with no tuning guidance.

- Detailed comment: Clarify how the per-iteration mixed update maps to interleaved deterministic/stochastic architectures and compare convergence and oracle complexity.

- Detailed comment: Revise Section 3.1 to unambiguously specify finite-sum vs stochastic approximation setting for correct interpretation of results.

- Conclusion: Interesting theoretical contribution but significant gaps—need κ estimation/robustness analysis, experiments, clearer problem setting, and positioning relative to prior hybrid work to strengthen the paper.
