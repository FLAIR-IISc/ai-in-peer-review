**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel hybrid algorithm, called Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), for optimizing smooth and strongly convex functions over convex constraint sets. The algorithm leverages both expensive full gradient and cheap stochastic gradient oracles to achieve linear convergence. The authors demonstrate that EMGD requires fewer calls to the full gradient oracle, making it potentially faster than existing algorithms, such as Nesterov's optimal first-order algorithm. The paper provides a self-contained proof of the convergence rate, but lacks experimental results.

**Evaluation**

The paper presents a novel and interesting algorithmic idea with a clean, simple, and solid theory. The authors successfully demonstrate the advantages of EMGD over existing algorithms, such as SAG, in terms of its applicability to constrained optimization problems and its simpler convergence proof. However, the paper has some limitations, including the lack of experimental results, discussion of limitations, and high-level comments on the proof.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the theoretical contribution could stand on its own. However, the authors should consider adding experimental results to demonstrate the practical relevance of the algorithm. Additionally, the discussion of limitations and drawbacks, particularly in comparison to existing algorithms, should be expanded.

**Clarity**

The paper is fairly clear, with a well-organized structure and a helpful summary table. However, some high-level comments on the proof would be beneficial to provide a deeper understanding of the algorithm's construction.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel combination of known techniques, making it a valuable contribution to the field of smooth convex optimization.

**Significance**

The practical relevance of the algorithm is not yet demonstrated, but the theoretical contribution has the potential to impact the field. The simple proof of EMGD is a significant contribution, especially in comparison to the complex proof of SAG.
