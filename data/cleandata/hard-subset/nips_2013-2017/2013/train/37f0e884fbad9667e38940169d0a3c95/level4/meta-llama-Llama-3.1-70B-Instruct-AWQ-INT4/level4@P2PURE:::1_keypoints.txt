- Proposes EMGD, a novel algorithm mixing occasional full-gradient evaluations with cheap stochastic gradient steps for smooth, strongly convex optimization over convex constraints.
- EMGD attains linear convergence, requiring O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls.
- Claims improvement over Nesterov’s method by avoiding sqrt(k) dependence in convergence.
- Provides a self-contained, simpler convergence proof compared to related work (e.g., SAG).
- Theoretical results hold with high probability, a stronger guarantee than SAG’s in-expectation result.
- EMGD is more general than SAG: it handles constrained problems and has a simpler proof that may ease modifications.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes a helpful summary table.
- Lacks experimental results to demonstrate practical performance and relevance.
- Fails to sufficiently discuss limitations and drawbacks, and to compare empirically or practically with existing algorithms like SAG.
- The proof would benefit from higher-level comments to motivate key quantities and insights.
- Technically sound overall, but would be strengthened by experiments and expanded discussion of properties.
