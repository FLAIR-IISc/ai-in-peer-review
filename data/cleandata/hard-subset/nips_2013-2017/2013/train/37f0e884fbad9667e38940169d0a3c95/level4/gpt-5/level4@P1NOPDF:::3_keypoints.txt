- Proposes a novel hybrid stochastic/batch gradient method that interleaves stochastic and full-gradient steps.  
- Proven complexity: ε-accuracy with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients; analysis appears correct and novel.  
- Major concern: relevance to ML—strong convexity usually comes from the regularizer so μ scales with sample size N (μ ≈ C N).  
- Under μ ≈ C N, the hybrid method outperforms batch GD only when N = O(C^3 / L^3), an uncommon/practically uninteresting regime.  
- The hybrid method’s convergence guarantee holds only with high probability, whereas batch gradient descent’s guarantee is deterministic.  
- Paper should discuss and quantify the regime where the hybrid method gives concrete advantages in realistic ML problems and clarify practical implications.
