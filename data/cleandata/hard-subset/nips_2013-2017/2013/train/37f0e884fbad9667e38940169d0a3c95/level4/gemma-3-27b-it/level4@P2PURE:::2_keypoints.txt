- Introduces a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method combining full and stochastic gradient queries.  
- Main theoretical claim: with known condition number and a hybrid oracle, a tuned mix of deterministic and stochastic steps yields high-probability O(log(1/ε)) batch convergence.  
- Analysis assumes the condition number κ is known a priori; behavior under κ misspecification is not analyzed.  
- The κ-independence in rates only holds when κ is known, limiting the practical applicability of the result.  
- No experimental evaluation is provided, which is a significant weakness for a new algorithmic contribution.  
- The update requires hyperparameter η tied directly to κ, but no guidance or method for determining κ (or η) is given.  
- The paper does not analyze or empirically test performance when η (hence κ) is set too high or too low.  
- The strong-convexity + smoothness setting is restrictive; extending to non-strongly convex cases (e.g., Bach & Moulines 2011) would broaden impact.  
- Per-iteration “mixed” updates are not entirely novel; the paper should compare to interleaved deterministic–stochastic strategies.  
- Related work on hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) is omitted; convergence-rate comparisons with existing methods are missing.  
- A thorough experimental comparison to SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, and other recent stochastic optimizers is recommended.  
- The optimization setting is ambiguous (finite-sum/deterministic vs stochastic approximation); Section 3.1 and subsequent claims need clarification.
