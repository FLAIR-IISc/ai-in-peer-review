- Contribution: Proposes a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method (EGD) achieving O(log(1/ε)) convergence with high probability under a known condition number and a first-order hybrid oracle.  
- Major assumption: Relies on perfect knowledge of the condition number κ (via η in Eq. 8).  
- Misspecification unaddressed: No theoretical analysis of algorithm behavior when κ is unknown or misspecified.  
- No experiments: Complete absence of empirical evaluation to show performance, robustness, or hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Practical guidance missing: Paper offers no procedure or guidance for setting or estimating κ in practice.  
- Limited scope: Analysis restricted to strongly convex, smooth problems, limiting broader applicability.  
- Extensions ignored: Does not explore or cite non-strongly convex scenarios (e.g., Bach & Moulines 2011).  
- Algorithmic design: EGD blends deterministic and stochastic gradients each iteration rather than interleaving; lacks discussion comparing these strategies.  
- Related work omission: Fails to compare/contrast with prior hybrid deterministic-stochastic algorithms (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and their convergence rates.  
- Ambiguous setting: Unclear whether the paper targets deterministic optimization with randomized algorithms or stochastic approximation; theoretical setup (Section 3.1) is ambiguous.  
- Overall: Strong theoretical result but practical relevance weakened by strong assumptions, missing experiments, limited discussion of robustness and related work.
