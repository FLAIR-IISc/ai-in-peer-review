The paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), that leverages both stochastic and batch gradients to achieve linear convergence. The algorithm is designed for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems, and its convergence proof appears to be correct and novel.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate, showing that it can achieve an epsilon-accurate solution using O(log(1/epsilon)) full gradients and O(kappa^2 log(1/epsilon)) stochastic gradients. The authors also provide a detailed comparison with existing algorithms, highlighting the advantages of EMGD in certain scenarios.

However, the reviewer raises concerns about the relevance of the proposed algorithm in the machine learning setting, which is the primary focus of the conference. Specifically, the reviewer notes that in typical machine learning algorithms, strong convexity is often induced by a regularizer, which implies that the value of mu is of the order of the number of samples N. Under this assumption, the proposed method is only faster than batch gradient descent if the number of samples is bounded by O(C^3/L^3), which may not be a realistic or interesting regime.

Furthermore, the reviewer points out that the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm holds only in high probability, whereas the convergence rate of batch gradient descent is deterministic. This difference in guarantees may limit the appeal of the proposed algorithm in practical machine learning optimization problems.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should carefully discuss the relevance of their algorithm to the machine learning community and provide more convincing arguments for its advantages over batch gradient descent. Additionally, the authors should address the minor comments raised by the reviewer, including correcting typos, clarifying notation, and providing more precise definitions.
