- Summary: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) combining occasional full gradients with stochastic mixed gradients to achieve linear convergence for smooth strongly convex problems, using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients (paper has inconsistency with O(κ) claim).

- Relation to prior work: Connects to full-gradient accelerated methods (Nesterov), stochastic SGD rates, finite-sum methods (SAG/SDCA), and variance-reduced/semi-stochastic estimators.

- Key algorithmic idea: Compute a full gradient at an epoch anchor and perform T mixed stochastic steps g̃t = ∇F(w̄k)+∇f(wt)−∇f(w̄k) with projection onto a shrinking ℓ2 ball.

- Strength — conceptual: Shows occasional full gradients can remove condition-number dependence from full-gradient counts while leveraging cheap stochastic updates.

- Strength — theory: Provides a high-probability linear convergence proof with explicit parameters and transparent epoch/shrinking-domain analysis.

- Strength — scope: Applies to constrained problems and requires only O(d) memory, unlike some finite-sum methods.

- Weakness — dependence on κ: Analysis and step-size schedule need L and λ, with no guidance on estimating them or sensitivity to misspecification.

- Weakness — lack of experiments: No empirical validation, robustness checks, or wall-clock comparisons to SGD/ASGD/SAG/SDCA.

- Weakness — unclear oracle/problem setting: Ambiguous whether primary focus is finite-sum or expectation model; needs an unambiguous statement and consistent claims.

- Weakness — rate inconsistency: Paper inconsistently states O(κ) vs O(κ^2) stochastic gradient complexity; this must be resolved.

- Weakness — technical errors: Strong convexity is misstated (uses norm difference instead of inner product) and there are notation/typo issues (e.g., “conditional number,” “epoches,” EMGD vs EGD).

- Weakness — relation to prior hybrid methods: Needs clearer positioning and direct comparison to hybrid/semi-stochastic approaches (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt) and variance-reduced estimators.

- Weakness — projection practicality: Projecting onto W ∩ B(w̄k; Δk) may be expensive; the paper does not discuss when this is tractable or alternatives.

- Quality: Main theorem is carefully proved but relies on exact L, λ and contains an incorrect strong convexity statement and rate inconsistencies, weakening presentation.

- Clarity: Algorithm and proof readable but manuscript needs consistent notation, corrected definitions, fixed typos, and explicit problem setting.

- Originality: Mixed estimator and epoch structure are interesting but closely related to existing hybrid/semi-stochastic ideas; high-probability linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients is a notable angle.

- Significance: Potentially useful if made adaptive and empirically validated (e.g., distributed settings with occasional full gradients), but current lack of adaptivity and experiments limits immediate impact.

- Suggested fixes — adaptivity: Provide methods for unknown L and λ (backtracking/line-search, doubling, regularization) and analyze robustness to misspecification.

- Suggested fixes — experiments: Add empirical comparisons on standard finite-sum tasks (logistic regression, least squares) vs AGD, SGD/ASGD, SAG, SDCA, including wall-clock time and sensitivity analyses.

- Suggested fixes — clarify setting: State upfront whether focus is finite-sum or expectation and map the function oracle accordingly.

- Suggested fixes — positioning: Compare explicitly to Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) and variance-reduced methods, detailing oracle complexity and assumptions.

- Suggested fixes — projection alternatives: Discuss when the shrinking-ball projection is efficient or propose alternatives if it is expensive.

- Arguments for acceptance: Theoretically interesting result showing occasional full gradients can remove condition-number dependence from full-gradient counts with high-probability linear convergence and constrained-problem applicability.

- Arguments against acceptance: Requires prior knowledge of L and λ, lacks adaptivity and experiments, has clarity/technical issues (strong convexity error, κ inconsistency, ambiguous setting), and unclear practical relevance versus SAG/SDCA.

- Overall recommendation: The paper is theoretically appealing but needs corrections, adaptive methods, clearer positioning, and empirical validation before being suitable for acceptance.
