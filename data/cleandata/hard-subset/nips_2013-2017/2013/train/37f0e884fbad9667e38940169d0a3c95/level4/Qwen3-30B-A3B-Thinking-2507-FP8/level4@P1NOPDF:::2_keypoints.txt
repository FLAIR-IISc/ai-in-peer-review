1. Proposes a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method claiming removal of condition-number dependence; main theorem: with known κ, mixed updates give O(log(1/ε)) high-probability convergence for finite-sum problems.

2. Analysis assumes exact knowledge of the condition number κ; behavior under κ misspecification (under/overestimate) is unaddressed.

3. If misspecification is theoretically intractable, the paper fails to acknowledge this and provides no empirical validation to compensate.

4. The paper contains no experiments—major omission preventing assessment of practical performance and verification of theoretical claims.

5. The algorithm depends on hyperparameter η tied to κ but gives no guidance or methods for estimating/setting η.

6. No robustness analysis for η/κ misspecification (e.g., η too large or small) is provided.

7. Restricts attention to strongly convex, smooth objectives and ignores non-strongly convex settings that are common and important.

8. The “hybrid” design is implemented as a single mixed update per iteration and lacks comparison to simpler interleaved deterministic/stochastic schemes to justify novelty.

9. Omits key related work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012 SISC) and fails to contextualize the contribution within prior hybrid methods.

10. The optimization problem setting is ambiguous (finite-sum vs. stochastic approximation); Section 3.1 is misleading and needs clarification.

11. Summary: interesting theoretical claim under restrictive known-κ assumption, but missing misspecification analysis, experiments, related-work engagement, and clear problem scope—needs substantial revisions before publication.
