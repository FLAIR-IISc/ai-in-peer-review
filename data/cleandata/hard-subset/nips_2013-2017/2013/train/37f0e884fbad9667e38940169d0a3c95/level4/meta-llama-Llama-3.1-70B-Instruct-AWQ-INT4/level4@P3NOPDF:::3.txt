The paper proposes a novel hybrid strategy for stochastic gradient descent that combines both stochastic and batch gradients. The algorithm is proven to converge to an epsilon-accurate solution using O(log(1/eps)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/eps)) stochastic gradients.

Upon reviewing the convergence proof, I found it to be correct and novel, with the exception of some minor errors detailed below. However, my primary concern lies in the relevance of the proposed algorithm in the context of machine learning, which is the focus of this conference.

In typical machine learning algorithms, strong convexity is often ensured by the regularizer. Consequently, the value of mu is of the order of the number of samples N, which can be approximated as mu = C N, where C is independent of N. Under this assumption, the proposed method is faster than batch gradient descent only when the number of samples is bounded by O(C^3/L^3), a regime that does not appear to be particularly interesting.

Furthermore, the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm holds only in high probability, whereas the convergence rate of batch gradient descent is deterministic. This distinction is crucial and warrants careful discussion to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed algorithm over batch gradient descent and to establish its relevance to the machine learning community.

Minor comments and corrections:

* Equation (6) should be ||w^*-\hat{w}||^2.
* Please specify the function used in equation (7) on page 288.
* The equality in equation (286) should be removed, as it does not contribute to comprehension and instead decreases clarity.
* In equation (13), absolute values should be replaced with norms.
* It would be beneficial to explicitly state that L ≥ lambda, even though it may seem obvious.
* In equation (4), x^ should be replaced with w^, and f should be replaced with F. Additionally, the first term in the max is always larger than the second term, as shown in Lemma 1.
* Please provide a precise definition of the condition number as a function of lambda and L.

In conclusion, while the paper presents a novel hybrid stochastic/batch gradient descent algorithm, it is unclear whether this algorithm offers any advantages over standard batch gradient descent in practical machine learning optimization problems.
