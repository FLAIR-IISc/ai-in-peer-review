- Introduces a novel hybrid stochastic/batch gradient descent algorithm with convergence to ε using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Convergence proof appears correct and novel.  
- Main concern: relevance to ML—strong convexity from regularization often gives μ = C·N, so the method outperforms batch GD only if N = O(C³/L³), which is not compelling.  
- The proposed convergence rate holds only with high probability, whereas batch gradient descent gives deterministic guarantees.  
- Authors must carefully discuss and demonstrate real advantage over batch gradient descent and analyze scenarios where the method outperforms existing methods in ML settings.
