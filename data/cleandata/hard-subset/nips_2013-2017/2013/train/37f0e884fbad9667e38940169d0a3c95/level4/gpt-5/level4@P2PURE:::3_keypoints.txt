- Proposes a hybrid stochastic/batch gradient method combining stochastic and full gradients.  
- Claims epsilon-accuracy with O(log(1/eps)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/eps)) stochastic gradients.  
- Convergence proof appears correct and (largely) novel.  
- Main concern: relevance to ML — strong convexity typically comes from regularization so mu scales with sample size N (mu = C N).  
- Under mu = C N, the method is faster than batch GD only if N = O(C^3 / L^3), an uninteresting regime.  
- Convergence guarantee holds only with high probability, whereas batch gradient descent guarantees are deterministic.  
- The paper must thoroughly discuss and demonstrate concrete advantages over batch gradient descent for ML problems.
