- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) combining stochastic and batch gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Claims convergence to an epsilon-accurate solution using O(log(1/eps)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/eps)) stochastic gradients.  
- Paper is well-written and the convergence proof appears correct and novel.  
- Concern: relevance to ML is unclear because strong convexity often scales as mu = C·N, making EMGD faster than batch GD only when N = O(C^3/L^3), a potentially impractical regime.  
- Concern: convergence guarantees for EMGD are high-probability, whereas batch gradient descent has deterministic guarantees, which may limit applicability.  
- Recommendation: authors should discuss advantages and limitations of EMGD specifically in ML contexts.  
- Recommendation: provide detailed analysis of conditions under which EMGD outperforms standard batch gradient descent.  
- Recommendation: address drawbacks such as the high-probability convergence guarantee and its practical implications.

- Minor: Provide a precise definition of the condition number as a function of lambda and L.  
- Strengths: novel algorithm, correct/novel convergence proof, and potential for faster convergence in some regimes.  
- Weaknesses: unclear ML relevance, high-probability rather than deterministic convergence, and insufficient analysis of when EMGD outperforms batch GD.  
- Decision: recommend acceptance conditional on addressing relevance, applicability, and providing the requested detailed analysis.
