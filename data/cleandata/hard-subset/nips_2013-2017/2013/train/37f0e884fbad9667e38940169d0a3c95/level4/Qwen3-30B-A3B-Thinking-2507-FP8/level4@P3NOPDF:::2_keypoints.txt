- Paper proposes a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method claiming O(log(1/ε)) batch convergence assuming known condition number κ.  
- Primary flaw: unrealistic assumption of perfect prior knowledge of κ with no method to estimate it or analyze misspecification.  
- No empirical evaluation; the theoretical claim remains unverified and practical relevance is unclear.  
- Authors must either provide a principled κ-estimation procedure with experiments or analyze convergence under κ misspecification.  
- Failure to cite or compare to relevant hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) obscures novelty and state-of-the-art positioning.  
- Ambiguous optimization setting: paper conflates empirical risk minimization (deterministic ERM) and stochastic approximation, leading to inconsistent oracle/model assumptions.  
- The “mixed gradient” update lacks discussion of how it compares to simpler alternatives like alternating deterministic and stochastic steps.  
- Analysis restricted to strongly convex, smooth objectives; no discussion of non-strongly convex cases or extensions.  
- Absence of experiments is especially problematic given dependence on κ; minimal baselines (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG) and datasets (e.g., logistic regression on MNIST/CIFAR) are missing.  
- Required revisions: add experiments (including κ misspecification), clarify problem setting, cite/compare related work, and provide κ estimation or sensitivity analysis.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: as written the claims are unsupported and overstated; recommend rejection pending substantial revision.
