- Overall scores: quality 7/10, clarity 8/10, originality 8/10, significance 7/10.
- Problem: optimizing smooth, strongly convex functions over convex constraint sets where gradient mapping is cheap to compute.
- Contribution: proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid that mixes occasional full gradients with many cheap stochastic gradients.
- Complexity/result: achieves linear convergence with O(log 1/ε) full-gradient calls and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls under moderate condition numbers.
- Method detail: each epoch uses one full gradient plus fixed-number mixed stochastic steps and averages iterates to get constant suboptimality reduction independent of condition number.
- Proof: provides a self-contained convergence proof that is simpler than those for methods like SAG, but lacks high-level motivational comments.
- Novelty: novel combination of known deterministic and stochastic techniques; more general applicability (constrained problems) than some competitors.
- Strengths: clean theory, simple algorithmic idea, linear rate without condition-number scaling with sample size, clearer proof than SAG.
- Major weakness: no experimental validation to demonstrate practical benefits.
- Important limitation: requires fixing the number of steps per epoch in advance, unlike adaptive methods (SCDA, SAG), which may hurt practical convergence.
- Missing comparisons: needs concrete analysis and discussion contrasting EMGD with SAG and Nesterov, especially for the sum-of-n-functions setting.
- Clarity issues: proof would benefit from additional high-level explanations; minor typos and notational inconsistencies should be fixed.
- Suggested additions: specify citations for rates in Table 1, clarify operating conditions for SCDA/SAG, and discuss the regime with regularization ~C/n (condition number ~n/C').
- Literature: recommend citing related hybrid deterministic–stochastic methods and prior work on the identified regimes.
