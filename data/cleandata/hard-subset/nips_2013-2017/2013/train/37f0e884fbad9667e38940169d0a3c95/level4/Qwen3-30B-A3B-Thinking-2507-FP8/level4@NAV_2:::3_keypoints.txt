- Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid of stochastic and full gradients, with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients; convergence proof is novel and technically sound.

- Primary concern: practical relevance — in ML strong convexity from regularization gives λ = Ω(N), so EMGD only outperforms batch GD when N = O(C³/L³), a regime unlikely in practice.

- EMGD’s convergence is high-probability while standard batch gradient descent offers deterministic guarantees, weakening practical comparability.

- Authors must more convincingly demonstrate a clear practical advantage of EMGD over batch gradient descent for ML tasks.
