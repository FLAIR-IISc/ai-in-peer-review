- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): epochs combining one full gradient step with multiple mixed full/stochastic steps for smooth strongly convex problems.  
- Claims O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls (condition-number independent) and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic calls.  
- Paper is technically sound with a simple, self-contained proof of linear convergence.  
- Novel and interesting algorithmic idea; clean theory and clear writing.  
- EMGD is more general than SAG (works for constrained problems) and has a much simpler proof.  
- Significance not fully demonstrated due to absence of experiments showing practical impact.  
- Lacks discussion of limitations and drawbacks, especially versus existing methods (SAG, SCDA, Nesterov).  
- Requires fixing the number of steps per epoch in advance, needing knowledge of κ — a practical disadvantage.  
- Does not discuss adaptive step-size or heuristic advantages of SCDA and SAG.  
- Needs comparison regimes: EMGD worse than Nesterov when κ ≳ n^(2/3); SAG comparisons across κ ranges should be detailed.  
- Should mention common ML regime (regularization ≈ C/n) where κ ≈ n/C and EMGD may not outperform SAG/Nesterov.  
- Should cite and contrast with Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) hybrid deterministic-stochastic method.  
- Proof would benefit from high-level intuition and motivation for key defined quantities.  
- Section 3.4 must be extended to discuss practical limitations and drawbacks.  
- Add experiments or concrete examples to illustrate when theoretical advantages are relevant in practice.  
- Table 1 is a clear and useful summary; keep and reference it appropriately.  
- Provide more explicit statements and clarifications in several places (notation, conditions, gradient identity, derivation steps).  
- Correct numerous typos and notation inconsistencies (domain symbol, norm vs absolute, log vs ln, grammar).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with revisions addressing the above points.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
