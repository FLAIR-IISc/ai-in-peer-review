- Proposes a novel hybrid stochastic/full-batch gradient descent reaching ε-accuracy with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Convergence analysis appears correct and largely novel, aside from minor issues.
- Major concern: practical relevance for ML where strong convexity µ scales with sample size N (µ = C N), making the method better than batch GD only for N = O(C^3 / L^3), which is not meaningful.
- Convergence guarantee holds only with high probability, whereas batch gradient descent has deterministic convergence.
- The practical-advantage issue is crucial and must be carefully discussed to justify relevance to the ML community.
