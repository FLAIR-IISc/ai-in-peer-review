- Problem: optimize smooth, strongly convex function over convex constraint set with efficient gradient-mapping updates, using expensive full gradients and cheap stochastic gradients.

- Contribution: proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that uses O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls.

- Key idea: replace each full-gradient epoch by O(κ^2) mixed-gradient steps combining one epoch full gradient and many stochastic gradients, then average iterates to get condition-number-independent epoch decrease.

- Theoretical result: linear convergence with number of full-gradient epochs independent of κ; proof is simple, self-contained, and holds with high probability.

- Empirical work: no experiments provided to demonstrate practical impact.

- Comparison to SAG: SAG is faster than Nesterov when κ < n/8; EMGD is faster than Nesterov when κ < n^(2/3); EMGD is slower than SAG in all regimes and matches SAG's big-O when κ ≈ n^(1/2).

- Example rates: if κ = n^(1/2), SAG and EMGD are O(n log(1/ε)) while Nesterov is O(n^(5/4) log(1/ε)).

- Advantages over SAG: EMGD handles constrained optimization and has a much simpler/high-probability proof, which may ease extensions and insights.

- Parallelization/memory claim: authors mention advantages but reviewer notes SAG can also be parallelized via mini-batches, reducing memory needs.

- Main cons: lack of experiments; insufficient discussion of limitations/drawbacks and comparisons to existing algorithms (section 3.4 needs expansion).

- Practical drawback: EMGD requires pre-fixing epoch length based on κ, unlike SCDA and SAG which adapt or don’t require fixing steps, limiting practical flexibility and step-size adaptation.

- Suggested additions: more discussion of sum-of-n-functions example to highlight regimes where EMGD/SAG/Nesterov dominate (specific κ thresholds provided).

- Suggested citation: include Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) hybrid deterministic-stochastic method and compare differences in theoretical guarantees.

- Clarity: paper is fairly clear; Table 1 is useful; proofs would benefit from more high-level motivation for chosen quantities.

- Quality: technically sound; theoretical contribution strong despite missing experiments.

- Originality: novel combination of known techniques.

- Significance: theoretical contribution could impact the field, particularly due to the simple convergence proof.

- Detailed editorial suggestions: multiple line-specific clarifications and fixes (e.g., specify SCDA domain, explicit gradient expectations, corrections in derivations, notation consistency).

- Typos and notation: several minor typos and inconsistent domain notation (D vs. Ω), and suggested notation fixes (norm symbols, ln vs log).

- Rebuttal update: reviewer satisfied with authors’ response contingent on implementing suggested changes in revised version.
