- Addresses smooth strongly convex optimization and proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes one full gradient per epoch with variance-reducing stochastic updates.  
- Main theoretical claim: EMGD needs O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, making full-gradient calls independent of κ.  
- Novel mixed-gradient update g̃ = ∇F(w̄) + ∇f(w) - ∇f(w̄) is an elegant variance-reduction idea.  
- Convergence analysis is rigorous, provides high-probability linear convergence using martingale concentration bounds.  
- EMGD applies to constrained problems and has low O(d) memory, unlike SAG/SDCA which target unconstrained finite-sum and may need O(nd) storage.  
- Practical relevance unclear: advantage over accelerated methods holds only when κ ≤ n^(2/3), but typical ML settings (small λ_reg) can make κ very large.  
- Reviewer requests concrete ML examples where κ ≤ n^(2/3) to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Convergence guarantee is probabilistic (high probability) vs. deterministic guarantees of some competitors, which should be emphasized in comparisons.  
- Algorithm requires prior knowledge of κ for setting epoch length T, which is impractical; need strategies to estimate or upper-bound κ.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: state L ≥ λ and define κ = L/λ explicitly in preliminaries.  
- Fix notation inconsistencies (use ∇F in strong convexity), correct Equation (13) to use norms not absolute values, and streamline/clarify derivations around lines 286–288.  
- Correct Lemma 1 to standard inequality form f(x) - f(x*) ≥ (λ/2)||x - x*||².  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution, but paper needs better motivation and discussion of practical utility and κ-dependence; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending revisions.
