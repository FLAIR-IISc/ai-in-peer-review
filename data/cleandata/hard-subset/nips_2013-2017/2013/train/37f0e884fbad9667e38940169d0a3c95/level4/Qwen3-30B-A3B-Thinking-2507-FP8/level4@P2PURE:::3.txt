

The paper introduces a novel hybrid stochastic/batch gradient descent strategy that combines stochastic and batch gradients. The algorithm guarantees convergence to an ε-accurate solution using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients. The convergence proof appears correct and novel, apart from the minor errors detailed below.  

My primary concern is the algorithm's relevance to machine learning, the conference's focus. In standard ML settings, strong convexity arises from regularization, so μ = Θ(N) (where C is a constant independent of N). Under this assumption, the proposed method only outperforms batch gradient descent when the number of samples N is bounded by O(C³/L³), which does not represent a practically relevant regime. Furthermore, the convergence rate for the proposed algorithm holds only with high probability, whereas batch gradient descent achieves deterministic convergence. This critical point must be thoroughly addressed to demonstrate a genuine advantage over batch gradient descent and establish the paper's significance for the ML community.
