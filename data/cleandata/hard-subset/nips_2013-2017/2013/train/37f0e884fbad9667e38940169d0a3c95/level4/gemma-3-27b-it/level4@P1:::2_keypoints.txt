- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a first-order method combining deterministic and stochastic gradient steps that attains O(log(1/ε)) convergence assuming known κ and access to full and stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical contribution: shows under specific conditions the dependence on the condition number κ can be removed from iteration complexity.  
- Major practical limitation: the method relies on exact knowledge of the condition number κ and provides no analysis of behavior under misspecification.  
- No procedure or guidance is given for estimating κ; the paper lacks even heuristic estimators or experiments testing estimation methods.  
- The analysis does not address convergence when κ is over- or underestimated, leaving robustness to misspecification unexamined.  
- The paper contains no empirical evaluation; experiments comparing EMGD to SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc., are needed to assess practical relevance.  
- The strong convexity assumption may be restrictive; extending or discussing non-strongly convex cases (e.g., Bach & Moulines 2011) would broaden applicability.  
- Mixing deterministic and stochastic steps is not entirely novel; the relation to interleaving/hybrid approaches and differences from those methods are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Related work review is insufficient; the manuscript should explicitly compare convergence rates and approaches to hybrid methods such as Friedlander & Schmidt (2012).  
- Problem setting is ambiguous: it is unclear whether the target is finite-sum deterministic optimization or stochastic approximation, and this distinction should be clarified.  
- Overall: promising and interesting theoretical advance but limited immediate impact without robustness analysis to κ misspecification, estimation strategies, clearer positioning, and empirical validation.
