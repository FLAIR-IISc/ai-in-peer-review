- Proposes a novel hybrid stochastic/batch gradient descent algorithm combining stochastic and batch gradients.  
- Shows convergence to an ε-accurate solution using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients; proof appears correct and novel aside from minor errors.  
- Main concern: relevance to ML — under typical ML strong convexity μ = Θ(N) (from regularization), the method outperforms batch GD only when N = O(C³/L³), a regime not realistic for large N.  
- Convergence guarantee holds only with high probability, while standard batch GD gives deterministic convergence; this weakens the claimed practical advantage.  
- The core contribution needs stronger justification of practical benefits over batch GD for ML optimization; otherwise relevance is questionable.
