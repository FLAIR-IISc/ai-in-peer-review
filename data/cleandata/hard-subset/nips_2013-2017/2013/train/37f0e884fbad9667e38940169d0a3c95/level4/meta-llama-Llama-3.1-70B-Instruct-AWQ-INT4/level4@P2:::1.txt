The paper "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients" proposes a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The algorithm utilizes both full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence, reducing the number of full gradients required from O(√κ log 1/ε) to O(log 1/ε), independent of the condition number κ.

The authors provide a clear and well-organized paper, summarizing the main ideas and relating them to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere. The paper is technically sound, and the proof of the convergence rate is self-contained and easy to follow.

The algorithm is novel and interesting, and the authors demonstrate its advantages over existing algorithms, such as SAG and SDCA. EMGD works for constrained optimization problems, whereas SAG is only defined for unconstrained problems. Additionally, the convergence proof for EMGD is simpler than the one for SAG, and the authors provide a more complete discussion of the properties of the algorithm.

However, the paper lacks an experimental section to illustrate the practical relevance of the algorithm. The authors should consider adding experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of EMGD in practice.

The authors should also extend Section 3.4 to include a more complete discussion of the limitations and drawbacks of their algorithm. For example, they should mention that the number of steps within an epoch needs to be fixed in advance, which can be a drawback in practice. Additionally, they should discuss the memory and parallelization advantages of EMGD compared to SAG and SDCA.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a solid theoretical contribution. With some additional discussion and experimentation, the paper has the potential to be even stronger.
