- Paper proposes a novel hybrid SGD strategy combining stochastic and batch gradients with theoretical convergence using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Convergence proof appears correct and is a novel contribution aside from minor issues.  
- Major concern: practicality in ML—strong convexity typically comes from regularization so μ ∝ N (μ = C N), making speedups over batch GD only when N = O(C^3 / L^3), an unconvincing regime.  
- The algorithm’s convergence is only shown with high probability, unlike batch GD’s deterministic guarantees; this crucial distinction needs discussion when claiming advantages.  
- Authors must carefully justify and discuss clear advantages over standard batch gradient descent for ML audiences.
