- Proposal: EMGD, a hybrid method interleaving full and stochastic gradients, achieves ε-accuracy with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient evaluations (κ = L/λ).  
- Analysis: Uses a mixed-gradient step (reference full gradient + variance-reduced stochastic differences) and shrinking trust regions to obtain linear (logarithmic in 1/ε) convergence in epochs.  
- Relation to prior work: Contrasts with accelerated full-gradient methods (O(√κ log(1/ε))) and finite-sum variance-reduced methods (e.g., SAG/SDCA), highlighting reduced full-gradient dependence and providing high-probability guarantees.  
- Novelty: Convergence proof appears sound and, to the reviewer’s knowledge, novel aside from minor issues.  
- Main practical concern: In typical ML, strong convexity scales with sample size N (μ ≈ C·N), so EMGD improves over batch methods only when N = O(C^3/L^3), an impractically small regime.  
- High-probability vs deterministic: EMGD’s linear rate is given with high probability, whereas batch gradient methods have deterministic convergence, weakening EMGD’s practical advantage.  
- Recommendation: Authors need to carefully address the practical relevance and provide clearer demonstration of advantage over full-gradient methods for ML audiences.
