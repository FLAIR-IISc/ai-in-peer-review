- Proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method (EGD) and shows it can remove condition-number dependence in convergence rates.  
- Striking theoretical result: with a tuned mix of deterministic and stochastic steps, high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence for batch optimization is possible.  
- Theory requires the condition number κ to be known exactly; analysis does not cover misspecification of this hyper-parameter.  
- No experimental section: paper lacks empirical validation of the algorithm or theoretical claims.  
- EGD’s update (Eq. 8) depends on η, which in turn requires κ; authors do not provide estimation strategies or heuristics for η.  
- Missing analysis/experiments on algorithm behavior when κ (or η) is unknown or incorrectly specified.  
- Problem setting is too restrictive—results assume smooth and strongly convex objectives; extensions to non-strongly convex or broader settings are not discussed.  
- Related work review is incomplete: authors do not compare or position EGD against prior hybrid deterministic–stochastic methods.  
- No empirical comparisons to baseline algorithms (regular SGD, averaged SGD, recent stochastic-first-order methods) to assess practical performance.  
- Optimization objective is unclear: authors should state whether they target deterministic optimization or stochastic approximation problems.
