- Paper proposes a novel hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method (EMGD) that mixes full and stochastic gradients.  
- Main theoretical claim: with known condition number κ and a hybrid oracle, a chosen mixture yields high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence for batch optimization.  
- Significant and rigorous theoretical result that removes condition-number dependence from the convergence rate under the known-κ assumption.  
- Major limitation: analysis assumes the condition number κ is known a priori; behavior under hyperparameter misspecification is not studied.  
- Step-size η depends directly on κ; the paper gives no guidance or procedure for estimating κ (theoretically or heuristically).  
- Needs analysis (theoretical or empirical) of overestimating/underestimating κ and robustness to misspecification.  
- No experimental section — absence of empirical validation is a major weakness for a new algorithm.  
- Restrictive setting: analysis only for smooth, strongly convex objectives; extension to non-strongly-convex cases is lacking.  
- Blending deterministic and stochastic steps is not entirely new; EMGD’s single “mixed” update per iteration differs from traditional interleaving and needs comparative discussion.  
- Missing citations and comparison to prior hybrid methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012); convergence-rate comparisons to existing hybrids are absent.  
- Paper should empirically compare EMGD to SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, and other stochastic first-order methods.  
- Ambiguity in the optimization setting: unclear whether the goal is deterministic optimization solved stochastically or stochastic approximation; theoretical setup needs clarification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, potentially impactful theoretical result and rigorous analysis addressing ill-conditioned problems.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of experiments, reliance on known κ, limited discussion of related work, and unclear practical applicability.
