- Paper proposes a hybrid SGD algorithm combining stochastic and batch gradient evaluations.  
- Claims convergence to ε-accurate solution with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Convergence analysis is generally sound but needs minor corrections.  
- Major practical concern: in ML settings with regularization-induced strong convexity (μ = C·N), the method speeds up batch GD only when N = O(C^3/L^3), a very restrictive regime.  
- Convergence is probabilistic, whereas batch gradient descent provides deterministic convergence—this distinction must be discussed.  
- Authors must clearly articulate a compelling advantage over standard batch gradient descent and justify ML relevance.  
- Minor fix: Equation (6) should read ||w^* - \hat{w}||^2.  
- Clarify Line 288: specify for which function equation (7) is applied.  
- Line 286: remove the unnecessary equality sign for clarity.  
- Equation (13): replace absolute values with norms.  
- Explicitly state the relationship L ≥ λ.  
- Equation (4): replace x^ with w^ and f with F; note from Lemma 1 the first term in the maximum always exceeds the second.  
- Provide a precise definition of the condition number in terms of λ and L.
