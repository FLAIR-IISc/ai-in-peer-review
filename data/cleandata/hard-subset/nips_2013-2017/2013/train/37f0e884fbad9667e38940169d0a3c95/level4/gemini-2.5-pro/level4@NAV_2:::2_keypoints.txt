- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), which mixes one full gradient per epoch with multiple stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Main theoretical claim: EMGD attains an ε-optimal solution with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients (independent of κ) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Strength: Significant theoretical reduction in expensive full-gradient evaluations for ill-conditioned problems, with a technically sound high-probability linear convergence proof.  
- Weakness: Algorithm and analysis require prior knowledge of the condition number κ (e.g., to set inner iterations T), limiting practical applicability and not analyzed under misspecification or estimation.  
- Weakness: No empirical evaluation—no experiments to validate convergence, compare to existing methods, or study hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Ambiguity in problem setting: unclear whether the target is general stochastic optimization F(w)=E[f(w)] or finite-sum problems F(w)=(1/n)Σf_i(w), affecting comparisons to SAG/SDCA.  
- Missing related work: should cite and compare to hybrid deterministic–stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and similar literature.  
- Practicality concerns: step size η and inner iteration count T depend on L and κ; paper needs guidance or heuristics for setting/estimating these in practice.  
- Suggestion: compare EMGD conceptually and empirically to simpler baselines that interleave full and stochastic gradient steps.  
- Scope limitation: analysis only covers the strongly convex case; discuss potential extensions or challenges for merely convex problems.  
- Overall verdict: theoretically elegant but reliance on known κ and lack of experiments are major issues that must be addressed before acceptance.
