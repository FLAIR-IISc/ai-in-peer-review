- Proposes a novel hybrid SGD algorithm that combines stochastic and full-batch gradients.  
- Theoretical result: convergence to ε-accurate solution with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient computations.  
- Convergence proof is sound and original, aside from a few minor errors.  
- Major concern: practical relevance—when μ ≈ C·N (common in regularized ML), the method outperforms batch GD only if N = O(C^3/L^3), a very restrictive regime.  
- Guarantee is high-probability (probabilistic) while batch GD’s guarantee is deterministic, weakening comparative appeal.  
- Authors must thoroughly discuss and establish the practical conditions under which the algorithm meaningfully improves over batch GD.
