- Proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that mixes full and stochastic gradients.
- Main theoretical claim: with known condition number and a hybrid oracle, one can get O(log(1/ε)) convergence (high probability).
- Analysis requires exact knowledge of the condition number; no analysis of hyper-parameter misspecification.
- No guidance or procedure is provided for selecting eta/kappa (no theory or heuristic).
- No experiments are provided to validate the algorithm or the theory.
- If extending theory to unknown kappa is hard, experiments should explore misspecification — this is missing.
- Assumes smooth strong convexity; non-strongly convex regimes are not discussed but are important.
- Combining full and stochastic gradients is not novel; the method’s novelty and relation to simple interleaving schemes are unclear.
- Omits relevant prior work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and lacks comparison of convergence rates.
- Needs empirical comparisons to standard baselines (SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.).
- The optimization setting (finite-sum vs. stochastic approximation) is ambiguous and should be clarified.
- Overall: interesting and notable theoretical result but significant theoretical, empirical, and clarity gaps remain.
