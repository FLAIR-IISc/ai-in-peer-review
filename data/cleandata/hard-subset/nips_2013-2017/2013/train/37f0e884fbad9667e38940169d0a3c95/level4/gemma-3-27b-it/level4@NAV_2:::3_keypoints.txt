- Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid stochastic/batch method with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients; convergence proof appears sound.
- Primary concern: practical relevance is questionable because analysis assumes strong convexity μ independent of dataset size n.
- In common ML settings where μ arises from regularization (μ = Cn), EMGD’s advantage over batch gradient descent only holds when n = O(C³/L³), which is not compelling.
- EMGD’s convergence guarantee is probabilistic, whereas batch gradient descent offers deterministic convergence; this distinction needs thorough discussion and justification.
- Paper lacks a detailed comparison to batch gradient descent and an explicit discussion of limitations in typical ML scenarios.

- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound algorithm and novel convergence analysis addressing reduced cost of full gradient evaluations in ill-conditioned optimization.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical applicability in standard ML, probabilistic vs deterministic guarantees, and insufficient discussion demonstrating real-world advantage.
- Overall: theoretical novelty is clear but limited practical relevance; paper should expand discussion and analysis to better justify its benefits to the ML community.
