- Proposes a hybrid stochastic/batch gradient method claiming O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Convergence proof is largely correct and novel, aside from minor mistakes.  
- Major concern: relevance to ML since strong convexity typically scales as μ = C·N (from regularization).  
- With μ = C·N, the method is faster than batch GD only when N = O(C^3 / L^3), which is not an interesting regime.  
- Proposed convergence is high-probability, while batch gradient descent has deterministic convergence.  
- The authors must address this crucial comparison to establish practical advantage and ML relevance.
