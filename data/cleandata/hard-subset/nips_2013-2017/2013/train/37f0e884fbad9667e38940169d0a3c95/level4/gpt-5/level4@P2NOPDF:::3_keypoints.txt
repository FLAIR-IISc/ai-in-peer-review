- Proposes a hybrid stochastic/full-gradient method achieving ε-accuracy with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient evaluations; convergence analysis is novel and largely correct aside from minor issues.  
- Primary concern: relevance to ML — in common regularized settings strong convexity μ scales with sample size N (μ = C·N), which undermines practical benefits.  
- Under μ = C·N, the method is faster than batch GD only when N = O(C^3 / L^3), a regime that seems impractically small.  
- The paper’s convergence guarantees hold only with high probability, whereas batch gradient descent provides deterministic rates.  
- Critical request: analyze and discuss the parameter regimes (N, L, μ/λ, variance) where the method clearly outperforms batch GD, and provide empirical evidence on representative ML problems.
