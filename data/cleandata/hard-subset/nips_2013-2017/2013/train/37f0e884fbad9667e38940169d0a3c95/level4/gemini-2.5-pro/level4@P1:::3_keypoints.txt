- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm to reduce expensive full gradient computations for smooth, strongly convex objectives.  
- EMGD runs in epochs using one full gradient plus mixed stochastic/full updates per epoch to achieve constant error reduction.  
- Main theoretical result: EMGD attains ε-accuracy with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, making full-gradient count independent of κ.  
- Strength: Addresses an important problem—mitigating gradient cost for ill-conditioned ML problems.  
- Strength: The hybrid mixed-gradient strategy is novel and yields linear convergence.  
- Strength: Theoretical analysis appears sound and provides a high-probability linear convergence guarantee.  
- Weakness: Practical relevance is unclear; conditions where EMGD outperforms standard methods are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Weakness: Claim of superiority when κ ≤ n^{2/3} lacks discussion/evidence on whether this regime occurs in common ML settings.  
- Weakness: EMGD’s high-probability guarantee contrasts with deterministic guarantees of batch methods; this trade-off is not discussed.  
- Suggestion: Authors should better contextualize results and show practical scenarios where EMGD is advantageous over batch methods.  
- Minor: Explicitly state L ≥ λ early in preliminaries for clarity.  
- Minor: Break down the dense derivations (especially (13)–(17)) with more intermediate steps to improve readability.  
- Minor: Proofread the manuscript for minor typos and notational inconsistencies in the analysis.  
- Conclusion: Strong theoretical contribution with solid proofs, but needs stronger justification of practical utility for the ML/NIPS audience.
