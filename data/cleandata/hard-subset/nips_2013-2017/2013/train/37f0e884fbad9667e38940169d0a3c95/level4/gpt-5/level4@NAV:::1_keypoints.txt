- Paper proposes EMGD, a hybrid method using O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic calls to obtain k-independent epoch complexity for smooth, strongly convex constrained problems.
- Core idea: replace each full-gradient epoch with O(k^2) mixed steps and average iterates to achieve k-free constant contraction per epoch.
- Theoretical contribution: clean, self-contained convergence proof with high-probability linear rates; analysis simpler than SAG’s.
- EMGD handles constrained problems (via efficient gradient-mapping updates), unlike SAG which is developed for unconstrained settings.
- EMGD can outperform Nesterov when k is moderate (theory shows advantage when k ≤ n^(2/3) in sum-of-n setting).
- Compared to SAG, EMGD is generally slower across regimes and only matches SAG in big-O for k ≤ n^(1/2); SAG often outperforms EMGD and Nesterov in many practical regimes.
- No experiments are provided to demonstrate practical impact or identify regimes where EMGD is advantageous.
- Paper lacks sufficient discussion of limitations and trade-offs versus existing methods; Section 3.4 should be expanded.
- EMGD requires fixing the number of inner steps per epoch in advance (needs knowledge of k), unlike SCDA and SAG which adapt step lengths and can exploit better local behavior.
- Practical ML regime with regularization ≈ C/n (k ≈ n/C′) makes EMGD less favorable; authors should acknowledge and highlight realistic scenarios where k < n^(2/3).
- Cite and compare to Friedlander & Schmidt (2012) hybrid methods, noting differences in condition-number dependence and ultimate rates.
- Provide a concrete treatment of the sum-of-n-functions case to clarify comparisons: (i) EMGD worse than Nesterov for k > n^(2/3); (ii) SAG matches EMGD for 1 ≤ k ≤ n^(1/2); (iii) SAG better than both for n^(1/2) < k ≤ n/8; (iv) SAG worse than Nesterov for k > n/8.
- Proof would benefit from more high-level intuition motivating key constructs, which currently feel ad hoc.
- Clarify several technical points and statements in the text (e.g., sources of rates in Table 1, SCDA applicability, condition statements, ∇F(w) = E[∇f(w], steps in proofs).
- Correct specific inaccuracies (e.g., claim that SAG/SCDA cannot leverage distributed computing via mini-batches).
- Fix typos and notation inconsistencies (e.g., “an convex” → “a convex”, domain notation D vs Ω, use norm symbols, unify log/ln).
- Reviewer finds the technical content solid and theory strong enough to stand without experiments, contingent on adding the requested discussions and comparisons.
- After rebuttal the reviewer is satisfied and expects camera-ready changes incorporating these clarifications, comparisons, citations, and an expanded discussion of limitations.
