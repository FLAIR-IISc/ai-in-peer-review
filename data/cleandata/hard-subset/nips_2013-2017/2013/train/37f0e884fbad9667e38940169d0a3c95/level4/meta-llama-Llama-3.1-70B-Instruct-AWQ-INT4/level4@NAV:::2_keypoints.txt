- Introduces a hybrid deterministic-stochastic first-order method that mixes deterministic and stochastic oracle queries to improve convergence and remove condition-number dependence.  
- Theoretical claim: with known condition number and a hybrid oracle, a suitable mix yields high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence for batch optimization.  
- Analysis assumes the condition number κ is perfectly known; the algorithm’s behavior under misspecified or unknown κ is not analyzed.  
- No experiments are provided — a major omission for a paper whose main contribution is a new algorithm.  
- The EGD algorithm requires setting η (hence κ) but the paper gives no guidance, estimation procedure, or heuristics for choosing it.  
- The paper should either include a κ-estimation method or analyze convergence when κ is set too large or too small.  
- The work ignores non-strongly convex settings; discussion or citation (e.g., Bach & Moulines 2011) about convex vs strongly convex cases is missing.  
- Mixing deterministic and stochastic steps is not new; the paper does not compare its “mixed update” to simple interleaving strategies or clarify relationships.  
- Important related literature on hybrid deterministic-stochastic optimization (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) is not reviewed or compared.  
- The paper needs empirical comparisons against baselines like SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, SDCA, etc.  
- The optimization setting is unclear (finite-sum deterministic vs stochastic approximation); Section 3.1 and later claims are misleading and should be clarified.  
- Summary: strong theoretical contribution but lacks empirical validation, practical guidance for hyper-parameters, broader setting coverage, and relation to prior work.
