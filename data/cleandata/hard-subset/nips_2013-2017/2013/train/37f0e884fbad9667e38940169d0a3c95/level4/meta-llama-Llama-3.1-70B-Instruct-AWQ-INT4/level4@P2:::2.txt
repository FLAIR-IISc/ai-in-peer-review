This paper presents a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), which combines deterministic and stochastic gradients to achieve a linear convergence rate for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The authors show that by leveraging both types of gradients, the algorithm can reduce the number of full gradients required from O(√κ log 1/ε) to O(log 1/ε), where κ is the condition number.

The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm, including a proof of its convergence rate. The authors also discuss the relationship between EMGD and other optimization algorithms, such as stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and stochastic dual coordinate ascent (SDCA).

However, there are several concerns with the current state of the paper. Firstly, the theoretical analysis only covers the case where the condition number is perfectly known beforehand. The behavior of the algorithm when this hyper-parameter is misspecified is not discussed. It would be valuable to analyze the convergence rate in cases where the hyper-parameter is set too large or too small.

Furthermore, the paper lacks an experimental section, which is a major concern. Since the main contribution of the paper is a new algorithm, it would be essential to perform experiments to assess the theoretical results and study their relevance to the actual behavior of the algorithm on empirical data.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other hybrid deterministic-stochastic optimization algorithms, such as the one presented in [Hybrid Deterministic-Stochastic Methods for Data Fitting, M. Friedlander, M. Schmidt, SISC, 2012]. Discussing and comparing the convergence rates would be valuable.

Other minor concerns include the lack of clarity in the optimization setting considered in the paper. The authors should specify whether they intend to solve the deterministic optimization problem or the stochastic approximation problem. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more thorough review of related work, including older works on hybrid deterministic-stochastic optimization algorithms.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel algorithm that combines deterministic and stochastic gradients to achieve a linear convergence rate.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough and provides a proof of the algorithm's convergence rate.
* The authors discuss the relationship between EMGD and other optimization algorithms.

Weaknesses:

* The theoretical analysis only covers the case where the condition number is perfectly known beforehand.
* The paper lacks an experimental section.
* The comparison with other hybrid deterministic-stochastic optimization algorithms is limited.
* The optimization setting considered in the paper is not clearly stated.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The theoretical analysis is thorough and provides a proof of the algorithm's convergence rate.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper does not discuss the behavior of the algorithm when the condition number is misspecified.

Recommendation:
