- Paper introduces a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method (EGD) using a "mixed gradient" update (Eq.7) and shows a high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence rate.  
- Main theoretical result: removes dependence on the condition number κ from the convergence rate, but only under the assumption that κ is known.  
- Analysis assumes κ (strong convexity λ and smoothness L) is perfectly known and provides no guidance for setting or estimating κ.  
- No theoretical or empirical analysis of algorithm behavior under κ misspecification (too high or too low).  
- The algorithm requires hyperparameter η that depends on κ, yet the paper lacks estimation procedures or heuristics for η.  
- The paper contains no experimental section to validate theory or demonstrate practical performance.  
- The strong-convexity assumption is restrictive; the paper does not treat non-strongly convex or more general settings.  
- The hybrid idea (mixing deterministic and stochastic gradients) is not novel, and the paper does not clearly compare to simple alternation schemes.  
- The paper omits discussion and comparison to related hybrid literature (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and earlier works.  
- Lacks empirical comparisons to standard baselines (SGD, averaged SGD) and modern finite-sum methods (SAG, SDCA).  
- The optimization problem setting is ambiguous: unclear whether the target is finite-sum minimization ∑Fi/n or a stochastic expectation E[F(w)].  
- Minor clarity issues in exposition (typos like "conditional number") and insufficiently explicit problem formulation.
