1. Main claim: Proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that, with known condition number κ, reportedly achieves O(log(1/ε)) convergence for batch optimization, removing explicit κ dependence.

2. Dependence on κ and robustness: Analysis assumes exact knowledge of κ (hence step-size η) with no guidance for estimating κ or theoretical/empirical study of misspecification effects.

3. Lack of experiments: No empirical validation; needs experiments to (i) confirm the O(log(1/ε)) behavior, (ii) test sensitivity to κ misspecification, and (iii) compare against baselines like SGD, averaged SGD, SAG, and SDCA.

4. Scope of assumptions: Analysis is limited to smooth, strongly convex objectives; extension or discussion of the non-strongly convex (general convex) case is missing.

5. Relation to prior work and algorithmic form: The paper insufficiently positions itself relative to existing hybrid/variance-reduction methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and should clarify whether the mixed update is equivalent to interleaving full and stochastic steps and how rates compare.

6. Problem-setting clarity: Ambiguity between finite-sum deterministic optimization and stochastic approximation—paper must explicitly state which setting(s) the guarantees target.

7. Overall recommendation: The result is interesting but incomplete; the paper should provide κ/η guidance or robustness analysis, empirical benchmarks, clearer problem scope, extensions beyond strong convexity, and stronger positioning against prior hybrid methods.
