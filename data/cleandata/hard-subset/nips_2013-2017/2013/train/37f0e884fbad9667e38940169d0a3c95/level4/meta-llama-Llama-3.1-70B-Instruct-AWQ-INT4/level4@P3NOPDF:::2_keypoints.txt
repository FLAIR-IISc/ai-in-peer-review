- Proposes a hybrid deterministic–stochastic first-order method that purports to remove condition-number dependence in convergence rates.  
- Strong theoretical claim: with known condition number and hybrid oracle, achieves O(log(1/ε)) convergence with high probability.  
- Theory assumes the condition number κ is perfectly known; no analysis of behavior under misspecification.  
- Major absence of experiments — empirical validation and practical relevance of the theoretical results are not demonstrated.  
- Algorithm (EGD) requires hyperparameter η (hence κ) but provides no guideline or theoretical method to set or estimate κ.  
- Analysis restricted to smooth, strongly convex objectives; non-strongly convex settings are not considered.  
- Lack of comparison to prior hybrid or interleaved deterministic–stochastic algorithms; related work review is incomplete.  
- No direct comparison of convergence rates with existing algorithms to contextualize the contribution.  
- Problem setting is unclear — not specified whether tackling deterministic optimization or stochastic approximation.  
- Recommended fixes: add experiments (including κ misspecification cases), discuss κ estimation, and compare/contrast with existing methods.
