- Presents Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining occasional full gradients with many cheap stochastic gradients to optimize smooth, strongly convex functions over convex sets.  
- Claims linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(k^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls (k = condition number).  
- Key idea: replace a full gradient step by a fixed number of mixed gradient steps and average iterates per epoch to get condition-number-independent constant suboptimality decrease.  
- Novel and interesting algorithmic idea with a clean, simple, and solid theoretical analysis.  
- EMGD applies to constrained optimization and has a simpler convergence proof than SAG.  
- Achieves linear convergence for regularized empirical loss minimization and is more general than SAG in some respects.  
- Lacks an experimental section to demonstrate practical performance and relevance.  
- Paper does not discuss limitations or drawbacks of the algorithm sufficiently.  
- Proofs lack high-level commentary to justify key insights and motivate defined quantities.  
- Major practical drawback: requires fixing the number of steps per epoch in advance, which may be impractical.  
- Technically sound overall, but should include experiments and a more concrete comparison with SAG and Nesterov.  
- Clarity: generally clear with a helpful Table 1, but proofs need more explanatory remarks.  
- Originality: novel combination of existing techniques.  
- Significance: theoretical contribution and simple proof could be impactful, but practical relevance is unproven.
