- Presents EMGD, a hybrid method using one full gradient per epoch and many stochastic updates, achieving linear convergence for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Main theoretical claim: only O(log(1/ε)) full gradient evaluations (independent of κ) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient evaluations.  
- Core algorithmic idea: “mixed gradient” updates within epochs where a single full gradient controls variance of subsequent stochastic steps.  
- Strength: novel and interesting theoretical result reducing expensive full gradient calls to logarithmic count, independent of condition number.  
- Major weakness: complete lack of empirical evaluation to validate theory, compare to baselines (AGN, SGD, SAG, SDCA), or study hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Major weakness: relies on knowing condition number κ to set step size and epoch length, an unrealistic assumption with no guidance or robustness analysis for unknown/misspecified κ.  
- Clarity issue: ambiguous optimization setup—paper oscillates between general stochastic expectation F(w)=E[f(w)] and finite-sum F(w)=(1/n)Σ f_i(w), needing explicit problem statement.  
- Insufficient comparison to related hybrid deterministic-stochastic methods (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and lacking discussion of how EMGD relates to simpler interleaved schemes.  
- Scope limitation: analysis is restricted to strongly convex problems; lacks discussion or extension to the non-strongly convex (merely convex) case.  
- Recommendation: add thorough experiments, analyze robustness to κ misspecification, clarify problem setting, and expand related-work comparisons to strengthen practical relevance.
