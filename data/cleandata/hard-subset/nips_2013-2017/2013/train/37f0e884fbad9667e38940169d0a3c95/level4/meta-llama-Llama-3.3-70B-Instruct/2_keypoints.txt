- Proposes a novel "hybrid" first-order optimization method that mixes deterministic and stochastic gradient queries.
- Claims theoretical result: with known condition number and a hybrid oracle, a mix of steps achieves high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence.
- Analysis critically depends on prior knowledge of the condition number κ (strong convexity λ and smoothness L).
- No treatment of algorithm behavior when κ is misspecified; robustness to hyper-parameter error is unexplored.
- Paper lacks experiments to evaluate performance or provide empirical insight into κ estimation or misspecification effects.
- EGD update uses a "mixed gradient" requiring hyper-parameter η set via κ; no guidance or estimation procedure is provided.
- Authors should either propose κ estimation (with experiments) or analyze misspecification theoretically/empirically.
- Restricting to smooth, strongly convex objectives may be too limiting; non-strongly convex settings are not considered.
- Presentation as a per-iteration "mixed" update raises questions about relation to alternating/interleaving deterministic-stochastic methods.
- Missing discussion and comparison to prior hybrid deterministic-stochastic work (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and convergence-rate comparisons.
- Needs thorough experimental comparisons with SGD, averaged SGD, and recent stochastic first-order methods.
- Optimization setting (deterministic objective solved with stochastic algorithm vs. stochastic approximation) is not clearly stated.
