- Central contribution: a hybrid deterministic-stochastic algorithm that, assuming the exact condition number is known, achieves a high-probability O(log(1/ε)) convergence rate removing explicit condition-number dependence.  
- Critical flaw: algorithm requires a priori exact knowledge of the condition number κ (from λ and L), and the theory depends entirely on this assumption.  
- No analysis of κ misspecification: paper gives no guidance, robustness analysis, or practical method to set or estimate κ.  
- No experiments: complete absence of empirical validation to confirm rates, practical performance, or sensitivity to κ.  
- Suggested experimental needs: validate theoretical rates on real/synthetic data, test sensitivity to κ and propose heuristics, compare against SGD, averaged SGD, and variance-reduced methods (e.g., SAG).  
- Ambiguous problem formulation: unclear whether target is finite-sum empirical risk minimization or general stochastic approximation, which affects applicability and comparisons.  
- Inadequate related-work discussion: misses prior hybrid deterministic-stochastic literature (e.g., Friedlander & Schmidt 2012) and simpler interleaving schemes for comparison.  
- Limited scope of analysis: restricted to smooth, strongly convex problems with no discussion or extension to non-strongly convex settings (e.g., connections to Bach & Moulines 2011).  
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting result but substantially weakened by the known-κ requirement and lack of empirical and comparative evaluation; needs sensitivity analysis and experiments to be convincing.
