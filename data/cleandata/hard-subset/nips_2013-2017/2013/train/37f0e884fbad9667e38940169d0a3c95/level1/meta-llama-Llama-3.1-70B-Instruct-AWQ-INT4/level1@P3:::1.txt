This paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), which combines full gradients and stochastic gradients to achieve a linear convergence rate for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The main contribution of this work is to reduce the number of full gradients required from O(√κ log 1/ǫ) to O(log 1/ǫ), independent of the condition number κ.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rate of EMGD, showing that it achieves a linear convergence rate with a high probability. The theoretical results are supported by a detailed proof, which is based on induction and uses techniques from convex optimization and stochastic approximation.

The paper also provides a comprehensive review of related work in convex optimization, including deterministic and stochastic optimization methods. The authors highlight the advantages of EMGD over existing methods, including its ability to handle both constrained and unconstrained optimization problems, its low storage requirements, and its potential for distributed computing.

One of the strengths of this paper is its clarity and organization. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, followed by a detailed description of the EMGD algorithm and its analysis. The paper is well-structured, making it easy to follow and understand.

However, there are some limitations and potential areas for improvement. One of the main limitations is that EMGD requires knowledge of the condition number κ, which may not always be available in practice. The authors acknowledge this limitation and propose potential solutions, such as estimating the condition number by sampling. However, more work is needed to develop practical and efficient methods for estimating κ.

Another potential area for improvement is the computational complexity of EMGD. While the authors show that EMGD has a lower computational complexity than some existing methods, such as Nesterov's algorithm, the complexity of EMGD is still O(κ^2 log 1/ǫ), which may be high for large values of κ.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel algorithm and analysis, which is a significant contribution to the field of convex optimization. The idea of combining full gradients and stochastic gradients to achieve a linear convergence rate is innovative and has the potential to impact the field.

Overall, this paper is well-written, well-organized, and presents a significant contribution to the field of convex optimization. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rate of EMGD and highlight its advantages over existing methods.
