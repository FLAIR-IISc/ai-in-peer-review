- Problem & goal: Reduce expensive full-gradient evaluations for smooth strongly convex optimization by using cheaper stochastic gradients so full-gradient count is independent of condition number κ.
- Algorithm: Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) runs epochs with one full gradient at the epoch start and many inner iterations using a variance-reduced "mixed gradient" estimator.
- Theoretical result: EMGD achieves linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients to reach ε-optimality.
- Positioning: EMGD aims to combine deterministic fast convergence (e.g., Nesterov AGD) and low per-iteration cost of stochastic methods, positioned between full-gradient and finite-sum variance-reduction methods.
- Strength — Originality & significance: Novel idea to make full-gradient complexity κ-independent by trading for stochastic gradients, providing an early variance-reduction mechanism.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, motivated, and presents the algorithm and inductive analysis clearly.
- Strength — Technical soundness: Proofs use standard convex optimization tools and concentration bounds; Theorem 1 appears correctly argued via epoch-wise induction.
- Strength — Generality: Requires only a stochastic gradient oracle (E[∇f]=∇F) and extends to constrained problems if projection onto W∩ball is efficient.
- Weakness — Stochastic gradient complexity: Stochastic cost scales as O(κ²), which can be prohibitive for ill-conditioned problems and may exceed O(√κ) full-gradient methods depending on costs.
- Weakness — Practicality & parameter dependence: Key hyperparameters (e.g., inner-loop length T) depend on κ (or L, λ), which are often unknown in practice and not addressed.
- Weakness — Lack of experiments: No empirical evaluation to validate practical performance, compare to AGD/SAG/SDCA, or test sensitivity to κ estimation.
- Weakness — Projection step complexity: Requires projections onto W∩B(w̄;Δk), which can be computationally expensive for general convex sets and may offset gains.
- Summary — Quality: Technically complete and sound theoretical contribution.
- Summary — Clarity: Clearly written and well-structured presentation.
- Summary — Originality: Highly original approach to mix oracle types for κ-independence of full gradients.
- Summary — Significance: Theoretical significance is high, but practical impact is limited by stochastic complexity and lack of experiments.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel and elegant theoretical result; achieves κ-independence for full gradients; sound analysis; more general than finite-sum-specific methods.
- Arguments for rejection: O(κ²) stochastic complexity is a major drawback for ill-conditioned problems; dependence on knowing κ limits practicality; absence of experimental validation.
