- Introduces EMGD: epoch-based mixed gradient using a full-gradient oracle Og and stochastic oracle Of with shrinking trust regions, proving high-probability linear convergence.  
- Complexity claim: O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients (analysis), but abstract incorrectly states O(κ log(1/ε)).  
- Update closely matches existing control-variate methods (SAG/SDCA) and is nearly identical to SVRG; paper omits SVRG citation and comparison.  
- Technical proof is coherent (variance control via smoothness, Azuma-Hoeffding martingale bound), yielding 1/2 reduction per epoch with η=1/(L√T) and T=Θ(κ2 log(1/δ)).  
- Important issue: inconsistency in reported stochastic-rate (κ vs κ2) must be reconciled across abstract, main text, and theorem.  
- Important issue: strong-convexity is misstated as gradient Lipschitz lower bound; should use standard strong-convexity definition and ensure proofs don’t rely on the stronger (incorrect) form.  
- Analysis lacks empirical evaluation; experiments vs AGD, SAG/SAGA, SDCA, and SVRG on standard datasets (including constrained cases) are needed.  
- Two-oracle model is reasonable for finite-sum problems, but trust-region projection onto W ∩ B(w̄k; Δk) may be more expensive and needs discussion of practicality.  
- Clarity is generally good and intuition clear, but there are typos (duplicate eq. numbers), grammar issues, and inconsistent complexity reporting.  
- Originality is limited: main idea is known (periodic full gradient as control variate); novelty claims should emphasize two-oracle formulation or constraint handling if justified.  
- Significance: independence of full-gradient count from κ and high-probability guarantee are attractive, but stochastic O(κ2) complexity is worse than state-of-the-art O((n+κ) log(1/ε)) methods.  
- Pros: simple/intuitive algorithm, high-probability linear convergence, O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient steps, low memory, accommodates constraints and distributed full-gradient computation.  
- Cons: stochastic complexity scales as κ2 (worse than SAG/SDCA/SVRG), inconsistency in rates, misstatement of strong convexity, missing SVRG comparison, no experiments.  
- Suggested fixes: correct rate inconsistency and strong-convexity statement; explicitly discuss and compare to SVRG; attempt to tighten κ2→κ if possible.  
- Suggested experiments and extensions: empirical comparisons (wall-clock and gradient counts), constrained problem experiments, proximal/composite extension (prox-EMGD), and adaptivity to unknown κ (doubling/adaptive T).  
- Discuss and clarify trust-region projection cost and when it is computationally feasible.
