- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients to cut expensive full-gradient calls for smooth, strongly convex problems.
- Theoretical claim: EMGD attains linear convergence and lowers full-gradient complexity from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)).
- Paper is well-organized with a clear related-work overview and rigorous algorithm description.
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and provides detailed insights into convergence rate and computational complexity.
- Comparisons with other optimization algorithms highlight EMGD’s computational and convergence advantages.
- Strength: Novel algorithm addressing limitations of existing optimization methods.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical treatment supporting the method’s benefits.
- Weakness: Requires prior knowledge of the condition number κ, which limits practical applicability.
- Weakness: Lacks experimental evaluations on real-world datasets to validate practical effectiveness.
- Pro-acceptance: Significant contribution with rigorous theory and demonstrated comparative advantages.
- Con-acceptance: Practical limitation of needing κ and absence of empirical validation.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; authors should address κ requirement and provide experimental evaluations.
