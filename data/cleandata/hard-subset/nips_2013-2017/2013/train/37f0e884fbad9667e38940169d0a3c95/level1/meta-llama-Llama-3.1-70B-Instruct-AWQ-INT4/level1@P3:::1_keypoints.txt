- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Main contribution: reduces required number of full gradients from O(√κ log 1/ε) to O(log 1/ε), independent of κ.  
- Provides a detailed convergence analysis showing high-probability linear convergence, with an inductive proof using convex optimization and stochastic approximation techniques.  
- Reviews related deterministic and stochastic optimization work and highlights EMGD advantages: works for constrained/unconstrained problems, low storage, and potential for distributed computing.  
- Paper is clear, well-structured, and easy to follow.  
- Limitation: EMGD requires knowledge of the condition number κ, which may not be available in practice; authors suggest sampling-based estimation but more practical methods are needed.  
- Limitation: overall computational complexity is O(κ^2 log 1/ε), which can be large for high κ.  
- Originality: presents a novel, potentially impactful idea of mixing full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, thorough theoretical analysis, and good presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on κ and potentially high computational complexity.  
- Recommendation: accept, but encourage authors to address κ estimation, provide practical estimation details, and investigate ways to reduce computational complexity.
