- Proposal: Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients to reduce dependence on condition number κ for smooth strongly convex problems.  
- Complexity claim: EMGD achieves O(log(1/ε)) full gradient evaluations (vs. O(√κ log(1/ε)) for standard methods) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, enabling linear convergence.  
- Prior work: Builds on Nesterov (2004, 2005), Rakhlin et al. (2012), and Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang (2013), addressing high cost for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Theoretical quality: Provides rigorous, technically sound convergence analysis using induction and martingale techniques.  
- Clarity: Well-structured presentation with clear pseudocode and consistent notation.  
- Originality: Novel mixed-gradient approach that differentiates EMGD from SAG, SDCA, and pure stochastic methods.  
- Significance: Substantially reduces full gradient evaluations, with potential computational benefits for large-scale problems.  
- Strengths: Careful theoretical analysis, clear differentiation from prior work, and discussion of practical limitations and distributed optimization applications.  
- Weaknesses: No empirical validation to support theoretical claims.  
- Practical limitation: Requires prior knowledge of κ, with no proposed remedy.  
- Modeling assumption: Assumes full gradients are n times more expensive than stochastic ones, which may not hold across applications.  
- Recommendation: Strong theoretical contribution warranting acceptance, but authors should add empirical validation in future work.
