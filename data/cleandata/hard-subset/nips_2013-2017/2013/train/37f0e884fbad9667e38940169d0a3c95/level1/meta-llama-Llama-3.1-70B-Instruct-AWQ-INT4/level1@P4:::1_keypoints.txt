- Presents EMGD, an algorithm that mixes full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex optimization.
- Claims linear convergence while substantially reducing required full gradients.
- Provable complexity: O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients for ε-optimality.
- Paper is well-organized with clear algorithm description and thorough analysis.
- Related work is comprehensive and includes detailed comparisons to existing methods.
- Strength: significantly reduces expensive full-gradient computations, enabling computational savings.
- Strength: provides a rigorous convergence analysis that clarifies algorithm performance.
- Strength: empirical/theoretical comparisons highlight EMGD’s advantages over prior methods.
- Weakness: requires knowledge of condition number κ, which may be unavailable or hard to estimate.
- Weakness: analysis assumes stochastic gradients can be computed efficiently, which may not always hold.
- Weakness: potential sensitivity to hyperparameters (step size η, iterations T).
- Suggestion: include methods or guidance for estimating κ or choosing a proxy.
- Suggestion: add a more detailed computational-cost analysis, including stochastic gradient cost.
- Suggestion: investigate hyperparameter sensitivity and provide tuning guidance.
- Overall: significant, broadly applicable contribution; recommendation to accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
