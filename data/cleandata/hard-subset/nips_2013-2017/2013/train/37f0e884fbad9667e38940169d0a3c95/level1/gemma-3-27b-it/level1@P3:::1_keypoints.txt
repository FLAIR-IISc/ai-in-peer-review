- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes full and stochastic gradients to achieve logarithmic iteration complexity independent of the condition number.  
- Well-motivated by the practical bottleneck of expensive full gradient computations in large-scale optimization.  
- Properly situates work within related literature (Nesterov, SAG, SDCA) and includes a helpful table comparing iteration complexities.  
- Theoretical analysis appears rigorous and Theorem 1 establishes the claimed convergence properties.  
- Thoughtful comparisons to related methods, highlighting tradeoffs and specific applicability conditions for EMGD.  
- Algorithm implementation is opaque; the constrained update (Step 8) is not well-justified or intuitively explained.  
- Proofs are dense and sketched rather than fully detailed, leaving gaps in the linkage between algorithm steps and theoretical bounds.  
- Reliance on Hoeffding-Azuma is appropriate but the paper should briefly explain its applicability in context.  
- Practical considerations are limited: condition number estimation suggestions are vague and step-size effects are not thoroughly investigated.  
- No numerical experiments; absence of empirical validation is a major weakness that limits assessment of practical relevance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel epoch-based mixing idea, theoretical rigor, important problem, and clear related-work positioning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing experiments, unclear algorithmic step, dense proofs, and insufficient practical discussion.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting and potentially valuable but undermined by lack of experiments and clarity, reducing impact.  
- Recommendation: Reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit after adding experiments, clarifying Algorithm 1, and expanding/clarifying the theoretical analysis and practical guidance.
