## Review of "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients"

This paper proposes a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), to address the computational cost of full gradient evaluations in strongly convex and smooth optimization problems, particularly when ill-conditioned (high κ). The core idea is to interleave full and stochastic gradient evaluations within epochs, achieving a linear convergence rate with O(log 1/ǫ) full gradient accesses – independent of the condition number κ. This is a significant improvement over standard methods requiring O(√κ log 1/ǫ) full gradients.

The paper clearly articulates the motivation and positions the work within the existing literature on both deterministic and stochastic optimization. It correctly highlights the trade-off between iteration complexity and per-iteration cost, and references relevant works like Nesterov’s accelerated gradient descent, SAG, and SDCA. The authors effectively summarize the limitations of existing approaches and clearly state their contribution. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of full and stochastic gradients within an epoch-based framework is a potentially valuable contribution. Achieving condition number independence in full gradient accesses is a strong result.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a theoretical analysis demonstrating linear convergence and explicitly quantifying the number of full and stochastic gradient evaluations.
* **Clear Presentation:** The algorithm is presented clearly, and the paper is generally well-written and organized.
* **Comparison to Related Work:** The authors provide a reasonable comparison to existing methods, highlighting the advantages of EMGD in specific scenarios.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Practical Considerations:** The paper lacks discussion of practical implementation details. The overhead of switching between full and stochastic gradients, and the choice of parameters (η, ∆) in practice are not addressed.
* **Assumptions:** The assumption of access to both full and stochastic gradients might be restrictive in some applications. The paper doesn't discuss scenarios where obtaining one type of gradient is significantly more expensive than the other.
* **Computational Complexity Analysis:** While the theoretical complexity is presented, a more detailed comparison of the *overall* computational cost (considering both gradient evaluations and per-iteration cost) against existing methods would be beneficial. The discussion in Section 3.4 feels somewhat incomplete.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The paper is purely theoretical and lacks any experimental validation. Demonstrating the performance of EMGD on benchmark problems would significantly strengthen the contribution.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The theoretical result of achieving condition number independent full gradient access is significant.
* The paper is well-written and provides a clear and rigorous analysis.
* The problem addressed is relevant and important in many machine learning applications.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The lack of empirical validation makes it difficult to assess the practical impact of the proposed algorithm.
* The discussion of practical considerations and implementation details is limited.




**Overall:**

This paper presents a theoretically sound and potentially impactful contribution to the field of optimization. However, the lack of empirical evaluation is a significant drawback. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**, contingent on the authors addressing the practical considerations and providing some preliminary experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of EMGD. Specifically, a discussion of parameter tuning and a comparison to existing methods on a few benchmark problems would greatly enhance the paper's value.
