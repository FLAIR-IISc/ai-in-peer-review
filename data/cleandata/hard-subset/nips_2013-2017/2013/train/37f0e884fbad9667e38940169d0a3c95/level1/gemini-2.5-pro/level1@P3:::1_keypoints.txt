- Problem addressed: reduce dependence on κ for expensive full-gradient evaluations in optimizing smooth, strongly convex functions.  
- Main contribution: Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining occasional full gradients with variance-reduced stochastic updates within epochs.  
- Theoretical result: EMGD achieves ε-optimality with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, giving linear convergence with κ-independent full-gradient count.  
- Strength — originality/significance: novel paradigm that breaks the sqrt(κ) barrier for full-gradient access by anchoring stochastic steps with few full gradients.  
- Strength — theoretical soundness: rigorous high-probability convergence proof using epoch induction and martingale concentration.  
- Strength — generality: works for general stochastic objectives F(w)=E[f(w)] and supports constrained optimization via projections.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written, motivated, and places results in context.  
- Weakness — κ^2 stochastic complexity: total stochastic gradient cost O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) may be large and worse than SDCA/SVRG for finite-sum problems.  
- Weakness — no experiments: purely theoretical work lacks empirical comparisons to assess practical performance and regimes of advantage.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter practicality: algorithm requires knowledge of L and λ (κ) to set inner iterations T, which is impractical when these constants are unknown.  
- Suggestion — clarify mixed-gradient variance reduction: emphasize and better explain the variance-reduction intuition of the estimator g̃_kt.  
- Suggestion — discuss regime κ ≤ n^(2/3): provide examples or problem classes where the proposed complexity comparison favors EMGD.  
- Suggestion — justify projection assumption: briefly argue that projected iterates remain in the assumed ball around the reference point.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novelty and theoretical contribution justify acceptance despite empirical and κ^2 concerns.
