- Introduces EMGD, interleaving full and stochastic gradients to reduce full-gradient dependence on κ in strongly convex optimization.  
- Achieves linear convergence with O(log 1/ε) full gradients and O(κ² log 1/ε) stochastic gradients.  
- Clear motivation: addresses computational bottleneck of full gradients when κ is large.  
- Well-structured paper with clear presentation of algorithm, theory, and comparisons.  
- Novelty: combined epoch-wise use of full and stochastic gradients is a logical extension of existing techniques.  
- Theoretical soundness: provides convergence theorem and proof sketch supporting stated rates.  
- Comprehensive comparison with related methods (EGD, SAG, SDCA) and discussion of trade-offs.  
- Well-written, concise, and easy to follow.  
- Requires knowing κ beforehand, which is a significant practical limitation.  
- O(κ² log 1/ε) stochastic-gradient requirement may still be prohibitive for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Purely theoretical work with no empirical evaluation to validate practical performance.  
- Proof sketch is too skeletal; key bounding steps (AT and BT) lack sufficient detail.  
- Insufficient exploration of connections to existing semi-stochastic methods and related references [12,13].  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, theoretically sound, comprehensive comparisons, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on known κ, large stochastic-gradient count, lack of experiments, and weak proof details.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, with requests to add empirical results, expand proof details, better discuss κ estimation costs, and clarify relation to semi-stochastic methods.
