- Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid optimizer using full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Reduces full gradient evaluations from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)).  
- Runs in epochs, mixing full-gradient updates with stochastic-gradient steps.  
- Proves linear convergence with high probability.  
- Requires O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient evaluations.  
- Handles constrained and unconstrained optimization, unlike SAG and SDCA.  
- Uses O(d) memory, lower than SAG/SDCA’s O(n) requirement.  
- Provides stronger (high-probability) convergence guarantees versus expectation-based guarantees of competitors.  
- Requires prior knowledge of the condition number κ, which may be hard to obtain.  
- High stochastic-gradient cost (O(κ^2 log(1/ε))) could be expensive for large κ.  
- Lacks empirical experiments to validate theoretical claims and assess practical trade-offs.  
- Comparative discussion is mainly theoretical; experiments would strengthen claims.  
- Some proofs and theoretical sections are dense and could be clarified or summarized.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing empirical validation and clarity.  
- Final rating: 7/10 (good paper, minor revisions needed).
