- Paper proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) using a mixed gradient g̃t = ∇F(w̄k) + ∇f(wt) − ∇f(w̄k) with epochal full-gradient references and shrinking-ball inner updates.  
- Main theoretical claim: linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and (paper text) O(κ2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, though abstract incorrectly states O(κ log(1/ε)).  
- The mixed-gradient estimator is the standard control-variate/variance-reduction form used in semi-stochastic methods (identical to SVRG’s estimator).  
- Strength: clear algorithm and epoch structure, low memory O(d), and handles constrained problems via projected/proximal updates.  
- Strength: self-contained, readable proof with explicit constants and a high-probability (Hoeffding–Azuma) martingale analysis rather than only expectation bounds.  
- Weakness: inconsistency between abstract and theorem about κ vs κ2 dependence; must be corrected.  
- Weakness: complexity dominated by κ2, substantially worse than contemporary variance-reduced methods (SAG, SDCA, SVRG/Prox-SVRG) that achieve O((n+κ) log(1/ε)).  
- Weakness: no comparison to SVRG-style methods despite using the same estimator; novelty appears limited.  
- Weakness: requires prior knowledge of κ to set T and epoch schedule; only brief mention of adaptivity in conclusion.  
- Weakness: no empirical evaluation to support practical claims (constrained settings, distributed full-gradient steps).  
- Technical quality: analysis follows standard templates, per-epoch contraction and union bounds are sound and consistent with assumptions.  
- Technical caveat: the T = Ω(κ2 log(1/δ)) requirement drives the κ2 bottleneck and no lower bound or tighter analysis is provided to justify/resolve it.  
- Clarity: generally well organized and readable, but contains typos (“epoches”) and must fix O(κ) vs O(κ2) inconsistencies; a complexity comparison table would help.  
- Originality: limited — estimator and epoch idea closely mirror SVRG/SAG/SDCA; novelty rests on shrinking-ball constraint handling and high-probability guarantees, which need stronger motivation or empirical support.  
- Significance: conceptually appealing to reduce full-gradient calls to O(log(1/ε)), but overall impact reduced by inferior κ dependence compared to state of the art.  
- Suggestions: fix the κ vs κ2 discrepancy, add rigorous comparison to SVRG/Prox-SVRG/SAGA, provide experiments (including constrained/distributed cases), and discuss adaptivity to unknown κ and whether high-probability bounds still hold.  
- Acceptance arguments: clean high-probability linear-convergence analysis in a mixed-oracle model, handles constraints with O(d) memory, and is implementable.  
- Rejection arguments: strong overlap with SVRG, materially worse κ2 dependence without justification, inconsistency in stated complexity, and lack of experiments.
