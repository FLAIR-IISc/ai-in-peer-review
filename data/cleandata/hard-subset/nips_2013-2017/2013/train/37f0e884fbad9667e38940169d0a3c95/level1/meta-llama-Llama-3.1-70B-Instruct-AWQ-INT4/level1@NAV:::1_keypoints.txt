- Main contribution: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that reduces full-gradient calls from O(√κ log 1/ε) to O(log 1/ε), independent of κ.  
- Problem setting: Targets smooth, strongly convex optimization using both full and stochastic gradients.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, related work, and detailed algorithm description.  
- Theoretical analysis: Provides rigorous convergence analysis and a proof of the main theorem.  
- Comparisons: Compares EMGD to existing methods (Nesterov, SAG, SDCA).  
- Strength — novelty: Introduces a novel algorithm combining full and stochastic gradients for linear convergence.  
- Strength — theory: Strong theoretical guarantees on convergence rate.  
- Strength — potential: Could be more efficient than existing methods in some scenarios.  
- Weakness — stochastic gradient cost: Assumes stochastic gradients can be computed efficiently, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness — condition number: Relies on estimating κ but offers no clear estimation method.  
- Weakness — practicality: Lacks experimental results and practical implementation details.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address assumptions, κ estimation, and add experiments.
