- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes full and stochastic gradients per epoch to reduce full-gradient cost in ill-conditioned strongly convex problems.  
- Claims linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradient evaluations independent of condition number κ.  
- Theoretical complexity: O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient evaluations.  
- Well-motivated problem statement emphasizing trade-off between iteration complexity and per-iteration cost.  
- Novelty: structured epoch-based combination of full and stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical rigor: convergence proof and complexity bounds are provided.  
- Good connection to prior work (e.g., Nesterov, SAG, SDCA) and related literature.  
- Lacks any experimental evaluation to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Requires knowledge/estimation of κ a priori, which may be limiting in practice.  
- Comparison to existing methods (SAG, SDCA) is superficial; needs deeper analysis of constants and practical implications.  
- Proofs in the appendix are lengthy and could be clearer and better organized.  
- Limited discussion of concrete machine-learning applications or scenarios where EMGD is especially advantageous.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions pending experimental validation, deeper practical discussion, and clarification on estimating κ.
