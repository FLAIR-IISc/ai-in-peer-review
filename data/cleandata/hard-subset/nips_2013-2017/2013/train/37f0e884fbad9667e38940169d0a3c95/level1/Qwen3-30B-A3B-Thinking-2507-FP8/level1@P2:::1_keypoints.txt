- Proposes EMGD, an algorithm combining full and stochastic gradients to reduce condition-number dependence for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Claims linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradient and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient evaluations, removing κ dependence from full-gradient count.  
- Builds on SAG/SDCA and semi-stochastic/epoch methods by introducing a "mixed gradient" and leveraging smoothness to bound stochastic error.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with a clear convergence proof (Theorem 1) and careful probabilistic bounds.  
- Proof methodology is standard but well-executed, with clear induction and trade-off handling between gradient types.  
- Compared to SAG/SDCA, EMGD uses Ω(d) storage, handles constrained problems, and provides high-probability guarantees rather than expectation.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; algorithm and notation are clear, but needs more intuitive explanation of the mixed gradient and shrinking domain role.  
- Lacks empirical validation of theoretical claims, a major omission for a NIPS submission.  
- Requires prior knowledge of the condition number κ, which limits practical applicability.  
- Unclear/insufficient explanation for why stochastic gradient count is O(κ²) rather than a lower order.  
- Computational-cost comparisons assume full gradient is n× more expensive than stochastic gradient without justification.  
- Claims the method is preferable for regimes like κ ≤ n^(2/3), but practical benefits are unverified without experiments.  
- Overall: technically interesting and novel but deemed insufficient for acceptance due to absence of experiments; recommendation: reject.
