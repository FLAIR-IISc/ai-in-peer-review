The paper "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients" presents a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The authors aim to reduce the number of full gradients required by exploiting the availability of stochastic gradients. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate and computational complexity.

The main idea of EMGD is to divide the optimization process into a sequence of epochs, where each epoch consists of a fixed number of iterations. At each iteration, the algorithm uses a combination of the full gradient and stochastic gradients to update the solution. The authors show that EMGD achieves a linear convergence rate, and the number of full gradients required is reduced from O( √ κ log 1/ǫ ) to O(log 1/ǫ ), independent of the condition number.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the algorithm and its analysis. The theoretical results are supported by a thorough analysis, and the authors provide a detailed comparison with existing algorithms.
