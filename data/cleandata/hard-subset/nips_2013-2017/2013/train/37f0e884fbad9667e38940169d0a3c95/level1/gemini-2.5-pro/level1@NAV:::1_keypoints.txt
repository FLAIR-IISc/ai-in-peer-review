- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), an epoch-based first-order method that mixes one full gradient per epoch with many stochastic updates to reduce full-gradient frequency.
- EMGD constructs a variance-reduced "mixed gradient" per epoch and runs O(κ²) stochastic updates in a shrinking domain.
- Theoretical guarantee: ǫ-optimal solution with O(log 1/ǫ) full gradients and O(κ² log 1/ǫ) stochastic gradients, high-probability convergence.
- Builds on epoch-based stochastic optimization and variance-reduction ideas (e.g., Hazan & Kale, SAG, SVRG).
- Strength: Novel and significant goal—making full-gradient count independent of κ is valuable for ill-conditioned, large-scale problems.
- Strength: Rigorous, clear theoretical analysis with high-probability guarantees and a transparent proof structure.
- Strength: Well-written and organized, with clear algorithm description and discussion of advantages (constrained problems, low memory).
- Weakness: O(κ²) stochastic-gradient cost per epoch can be prohibitive for large κ and may negate practical benefits versus methods like SDCA.
- Weakness: No empirical validation—lacks experiments comparing against Nesterov acceleration, SAG, and SDCA to assess real-world performance.
- Weakness: Requires prior knowledge of κ to set hyperparameters (T), limiting practical applicability.
