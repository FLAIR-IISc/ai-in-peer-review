The paper "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients" proposes a novel optimization algorithm called Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that utilizes both full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The authors' main contribution is to reduce the number of full gradients required from O(√κ log 1/ǫ) to O(log 1/ǫ), independent of the condition number κ.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, related work, and the proposed algorithm. The EMGD algorithm is described in detail, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of its convergence rate.

The strengths of the paper include:

1. Novel algorithm: The authors propose a new algorithm that combines the benefits of full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence.
2. Theoretical analysis: The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate, including a proof of the main theorem.
3. Comparison with existing methods: The authors compare their algorithm with existing methods, including Nesterov's algorithm, SAG, and SDCA.

However, there are some weaknesses and potential areas for improvement:

1. Assumptions: The authors assume that the stochastic gradients can be computed efficiently, which may not always be the case in practice.
2. Condition number estimation: The authors mention that estimating the condition number κ is crucial for the algorithm's performance, but they do not provide a clear method for doing so.
3. Practical implementation: The authors do not provide any experimental results or practical implementation details, which would be helpful in evaluating the algorithm's performance in real-world scenarios.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors make a significant contribution to the field of optimization. However, some of the assumptions and limitations of the algorithm need to be addressed in future work.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm that achieves linear convergence for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems.
* The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate.
* The algorithm has the potential to be more efficient than existing methods in certain scenarios.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The authors assume that stochastic gradients can be computed efficiently, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The algorithm requires estimation of the condition number κ, which can be challenging in practice.
* The paper lacks experimental results and practical implementation details.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the limitations and assumptions of the algorithm, provide more details on condition number estimation, and include experimental results or practical implementation details to demonstrate the algorithm's performance in real-world scenarios.
