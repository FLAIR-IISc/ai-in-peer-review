**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm called Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The algorithm aims to reduce the number of full gradients required by utilizing both full and stochastic gradients. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of EMGD, showing that it achieves a linear convergence rate and reduces the number of full gradients needed from O(√κ log 1/ε) to O(log 1/ε), independent of the condition number κ.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work in convex optimization, including the epoch gradient descent (EGD) algorithm and stochastic optimization methods. The authors also compare their work to other optimization algorithms, such as Nesterov's accelerated gradient descent and stochastic dual coordinate ascent (SDCA).

**Strengths**

1. **Novel algorithm**: The paper proposes a new optimization algorithm, EMGD, which combines the strengths of full and stochastic gradients to achieve a linear convergence rate.
2. **Theoretical analysis**: The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of EMGD, including a proof of its convergence rate and a bound on the number of full gradients required.
3. **Improved efficiency**: EMGD reduces the number of full gradients needed, making it more efficient than traditional full gradient methods for large-scale optimization problems.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumes knowledge of condition number**: The algorithm requires knowledge of the condition number κ, which may not always be available in practice.
2. **Limited applicability**: EMGD is designed for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems, which may limit its applicability to other types of problems.
3. **Computational complexity**: While EMGD reduces the number of full gradients needed, its computational complexity is still high, especially for large-scale problems.

**Originality**
