- Studies smooth, strongly convex optimization with both a full-gradient oracle and a stochastic oracle, proposing Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) with epochal anchors and mixed gradients.  
- EMGD uses a mixed-gradient estimator ∇F(w̄) + ∇f(w) − ∇f(w̄) and shrinking-ball constraints to obtain high-probability linear convergence.  
- Analysis shows O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and claims O(κ2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients (where κ = L/λ), with linear-rate proofs using standard concentration tools.  
- The mixed-gradient is a variance-reduction device closely related to SVRG/SAG/SDCA; the epoch/estimator structure is similar to SVRG.  
- The shrinking-ball constraint is a distinct design choice used to bound stochastic component norms and control variance.  
- There is a clear inconsistency: the abstract claims O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients while the theorem and body imply O(κ2 log(1/ε)); this must be corrected.  
- Algorithm requires knowing L, λ, and an initial radius Δ1 (derived from F(0) − F(w*)), which are nontrivial and may limit practicality.  
- The method relies on access to an exact full-gradient oracle, which is unrealistic in general expectation settings and equivalent to an n-pass in finite-sum problems.  
- Compared to contemporaneous variance-reduced methods, EMGD’s κ2 dependence is worse than the O(κ) or O(n+κ) rates achieved by SVRG/SAG/SDCA.  
- Proof and presentation of the algorithm are clear, and the role of the mixed gradient and domain shrinking is well explained.  
- Paper contains minor typos and presentation issues (e.g., “Epoches” → “epochs”), and needs clearer discussion on selecting Δ1 and robustness to mis-specification.  
- Reproducibility is feasible given L, λ, and Δ1, but the paper should discuss adaptive estimation strategies for these parameters.  
- No experiments are provided; empirical comparisons to SVRG/SAG/SDCA and accelerated methods are needed to validate wall-clock tradeoffs and sensitivity to parameter mis-specification.  
- Pros: intuitive variance reduction, O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls, O(d) memory, constrained-problem handling, and a careful high-probability analysis.  
- Cons: κ2 stochastic-gradient dependence, requirement of L/λ/Δ1 and full-gradient oracle, lack of experiments, and some inconsistencies/typos.  
- Suggested improvements: fix κ vs κ2 discrepancy, try to tighten analysis to O(κ) stochastic dependence, add empirical comparisons, and propose adaptive/robust methods to estimate L, λ, and Δ1 or use restarts/line searches.  
- Overall: interesting and careful work that reduces full-gradient calls, but currently incremental relative to SVRG-style methods due to worse κ dependence and lack of empirical validation.
