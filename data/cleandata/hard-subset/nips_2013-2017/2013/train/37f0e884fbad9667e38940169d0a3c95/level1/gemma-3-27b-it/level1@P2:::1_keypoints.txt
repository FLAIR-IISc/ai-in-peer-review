- Proposes EMGD: mixes full and stochastic gradients within epochs to reduce κ-dependence, achieving linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients.  
- Clear problem statement and motivation: full-gradient cost for ill-conditioned problems is significant.  
- Novelty: the specific epoch-based mixing strategy appears to be a genuine contribution.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees: Theorem 1 proves linear convergence and reduced full-gradient complexity.  
- Good contextualization and comparison with Nesterov, SAG, SDCA; includes discussion of limitations.  
- Well-written and well-organized presentation.  
- Practical consideration gap: estimating the condition number κ beforehand is difficult and not fully addressed.  
- Hidden constant factors in O() notation may hurt practical performance; no detailed analysis.  
- Limited empirical evaluation: no experiments on real-world datasets to validate practical gains.  
- Oracle assumption Of (random f) is abstract; connection to real stochastic gradient estimators is underexplained.  
- Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, novel method, rigorous theory, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing empirical validation, inadequate treatment of κ estimation and implementation details.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution worth accepting, but would be much stronger with experiments and practical discussion.  
- Rating: 7/10 (Strong Accept) with recommendation to add experimental results in follow-up.
