- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) to reduce full-gradient calls for smooth, strongly convex optimization.
- EMGD runs in epochs, combining one full gradient with stochastic gradients per iteration.
- Proves linear convergence and reduces full-gradient complexity from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)), independent of κ.
- Paper is well-written with clear algorithm description and consistent notation.
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of convergence rate and computational complexity.
- Includes detailed comparisons with existing algorithms to highlight pros and cons.
- Strength: novel algorithm targeting reduction of expensive full-gradient computations.
- Strength: rigorous and comprehensive theoretical analysis.
- Strength: helpful comparisons that contextualize EMGD’s advantages and limitations.
- Weakness: assumes condition number κ is known a priori, which may be impractical.
- Weakness: no experimental results or reported empirical validation despite implementation claim.
- Originality: new blend of full and stochastic gradients with a novel convergence analysis.
- Significance: tackles an important optimization bottleneck with potential ML and signal-processing applications.
- Clarity: exposition and mathematical derivations are clear and concise.
- Quality: analysis and comparisons are thorough and well-presented.
- Acceptance arguments: novel contribution, solid theoretical results, and broad applicability.
- Rejection arguments: reliance on known κ and lack of experimental validation.
- Overall rating/recommendation: 8/10 — accept with minor revisions to address κ estimation and provide experimental details.
