- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) to reduce full gradient evaluations for strongly convex, smooth problems by interleaving full and stochastic gradients within epochs.  
- Claims O(log(1/ε)) full gradients (independent of κ) and O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients to reach ε-optimality.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear motivation, related work, algorithm presentation, and rigorous theoretical analysis culminating in a convergence theorem.  
- Strength: Clearly defines the practical problem of costly full gradient computations in ill-conditioned optimization.  
- Strength: EMGD’s epoch-based mix of full and stochastic gradients is a novel algorithmic idea.  
- Strength: Provides theoretical convergence guarantees quantifying full and stochastic gradient counts.  
- Strength: Compares EMGD with existing methods (Nesterov, SAG, SDCA), giving useful context.  
- Strength: Generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion on practical matters like step-size/hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity to stochastic noise.  
- Weakness: Assumes knowledge of the condition number κ, a significant practical limitation with only brief suggestions for estimation.  
- Weakness: Entirely theoretical with no empirical experiments, limiting assessment of real-world performance.  
- Weakness: Improvements appear incremental; EMGD may not outperform alternatives in all settings.  
- Weakness: Proofs are rigorous but verbose with redundant intermediate steps that could be condensed.  
- Weakness: Oracle/of-function definition is unclear — the implication that sampled f(·) is L-smooth isn't well-justified.  
- Acceptance pro: Addresses an important problem, offers a novel method, and delivers rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Acceptance con: Practical applicability is weakened by lack of experiments, reliance on known κ, and only modest improvements over existing methods.  
- Overall: Theoretically sound and novel but practically under-validated; reviewer recommends weak accept and urges even limited synthetic experiments to strengthen the paper.
