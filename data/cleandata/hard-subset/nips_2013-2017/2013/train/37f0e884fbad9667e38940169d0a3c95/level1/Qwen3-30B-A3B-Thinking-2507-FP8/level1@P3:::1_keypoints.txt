- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients to reduce full-gradient evaluations for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Theoretical claim: EMGD finds an ε-optimal solution with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Builds on epoch gradient descent and introduces a novel "mixed gradient" within each epoch.  
- Situates work relative to semi-stochastic methods and recent linear-convergence results for sum-of-functions problems.  

Strengths:
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound, with a detailed inductive proof and careful martingale handling for stochastic components.  
- Novelty: achieves condition-number-independent full-gradient access without requiring full sum structure (unlike SAG/SDCA).  
- Algorithm is well-motivated and the mixed-gradient idea elegantly balances deterministic and stochastic updates.  
- Contributes to understanding trade-offs between full and stochastic gradients, relevant for ill-conditioned problems.  

Weaknesses:
- No empirical validation; absence of experiments undermines practical credibility of strong theoretical claims.  
- Computational-complexity comparison is confusing and hinges on the assumption that full gradients cost n times stochastic gradients without clear justification.  
- Omits a critical assumption: individual functions f_i being λ-strongly convex is not stated in the problem formulation.  
- Requires prior knowledge of the condition number κ, and the paper does not adequately address how to estimate or handle unknown κ in practice.  
- Overstates advantage over SAG/SDCA by claiming high-probability convergence for EMGD versus expectation-only for SAG/SDCA without sufficient verification.  

Comparative analysis:
- EMGD’s O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient cost can be worse than SAG (O(n log(1/ε))) and SDCA (O((n+κ) log(1/ε))) when κ is large.  
- Claim of lower computational cost than Nesterov’s method holds only under the paper’s cost model (κ ≤ n^(2/3)) and may not reflect practical settings.  

Recommendation:
- Technically interesting but practically weak due to missing experiments, unclear assumptions, and dependence on known κ; borderline but recommended for rejection pending empirical validation and clarification.
