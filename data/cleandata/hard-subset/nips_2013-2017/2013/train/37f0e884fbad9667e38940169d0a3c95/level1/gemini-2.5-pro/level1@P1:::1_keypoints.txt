- Summary: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that uses one full gradient per epoch plus stochastic corrections to achieve O(log(1/ε)) full gradient calls independent of condition number κ, with overall complexity O(log(1/ε)) full + O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic and linear convergence w.h.p.

- Key technique: Combines a fixed full gradient at a reference point with unbiased low-variance stochastic correction terms during inner iterations to reduce variance and guarantee constant error reduction per epoch.

- Theoretical guarantee: Provides a rigorous, high-probability linear convergence proof and clear complexity bounds.

- Generality: Framework applies to general expectation-form stochastic problems and constrained optimization (when projections are efficient), not just finite-sum objectives.

- Novelty/significance: Introduces a new tradeoff decoupling full-gradient complexity from κ, bypassing lower bounds for full-gradient-only methods.

- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and gives an honest, detailed comparison to related work.

- Practical validation missing: No experiments, so real-world wall-clock benefits and regime where EMGD is advantageous are untested.

- Heavy κ-dependence in stochastic cost: Inner iteration count T = O(κ²) yields potentially impractical O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients for ill-conditioned problems.

- Requires knowledge of problem parameters: Needs L and λ (hence κ) to set T, which are often unknown in practice.

- Argument for acceptance: Novel, significant theoretical idea with strong high-probability linear convergence, general applicability, and clear exposition.

- Argument against acceptance: Lack of empirical results, severe κ² stochastic cost, and reliance on known problem parameters limit practical impact.

- Conclusion: High-quality theoretical contribution likely to inspire follow-up work; recommended for acceptance despite practical limitations.
