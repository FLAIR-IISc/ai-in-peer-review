- Summary: Proposes EMGD, a mixed full/stochastic gradient method with linear convergence that cuts full-gradient calls to O(log(1/ε)), independent of κ.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on epoch gradient and stochastic methods and is compared to Nesterov acceleration and SDCA.
- Strength 1: Novel algorithm combining full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence.
- Strength 2: Thorough theoretical analysis with convergence proof and bound on full-gradient usage.
- Strength 3: Improved efficiency by reducing the number of costly full-gradient evaluations for large-scale problems.
- Weakness 1: Requires knowledge of the condition number κ, which may not be available in practice.
- Weakness 2: Applicable only to smooth, strongly convex problems, limiting broader applicability.
- Weakness 3: Computational complexity remains high for very large-scale problems despite fewer full gradients.
- Originality: Presents an original approach and new theoretical insights into mixed-gradient optimization.
- Significance: Potentially important for large-scale optimization due to reduced full-gradient requirements.
- Pro acceptance: Novel method with solid theory, fewer full-gradient calls, and practical relevance to large-scale problems.
- Con acceptance: Relies on κ, limited problem scope, and still considerable computational cost.
- Rating: Reviewer score 8/10.
