This paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), for smooth and strongly convex problems, aiming to reduce the number of full gradient evaluations to O(log 1/ǫ) by leveraging stochastic gradients. The core idea is to divide the optimization process into epochs, performing a mix of full and stochastic gradient evaluations within each epoch to achieve a constant reduction in error, ultimately leading to linear convergence.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the motivation, algorithm, theoretical analysis, and connections to related work. The introduction effectively highlights the trade-off between full and stochastic gradients and positions EMGD within this context. The related work section provides a solid overview of existing optimization methods, including deterministic, stochastic, and semi-stochastic approaches, appropriately citing relevant publications. The algorithm itself is clearly described in Algorithm 1, and the theoretical analysis, while detailed, appears sound. The comparisons with other methods (Nesterov, SAG, SDCA) in Table 2 are helpful and contextualize the contribution. 

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Problem Statement & Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of reducing full gradient evaluations for ill-conditioned problems and presents a compelling motivation for their approach.
* **Novelty:**  The combination of epoch-based descent with a mixed gradient approach is a novel contribution. The claim of achieving O(log 1/ǫ) full gradient evaluations, independent of the condition number, is significant.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, including a convergence theorem (Theorem 1) and supporting lemmas. The proof sketch, while condensed, seems logical and well-structured.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The related work section demonstrates a strong understanding of the literature in convex optimization and provides appropriate citations.
* **Detailed Algorithm Description:** Algorithm 1 is well-defined and easy to understand.
* **Comparative Analysis:** The comparison with existing algorithms (Nesterov, SAG, SDCA) provides a useful benchmark and highlights the potential advantages of EMGD.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Practical Considerations:** While theoretically appealing, the paper lacks a discussion of practical challenges and considerations. For instance, determining the optimal step size (η) and the initial domain size (∆1) in practice might be non-trivial.  The authors acknowledge the need for strategies to estimate the condition number κ in future work, but this is a crucial practical aspect.
* **Assumptions:** The algorithm relies on several assumptions, including access to both full and stochastic gradients and the L-smoothness and λ-strong convexity of the objective function. The impact of relaxing these assumptions is not discussed.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The paper lacks experimental results to validate the theoretical findings. Including simulations or experiments on benchmark datasets would significantly strengthen the contribution.
* **Proof Density:** The proof is somewhat dense and lacks detailed explanations for some steps. Expanding on the key arguments and providing more intuition would enhance readability.
* **Incremental Gradient Methods:** The relation to incremental gradient methods [2,5] could be strengthened. While mentioned in related work, a more detailed comparison of EMGD's specific characteristics (e.g. domain shrinking) against those methods would be valuable.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant problem in convex optimization – reducing the computational cost of full gradient evaluations.
* The proposed algorithm (EMGD) is novel and theoretically sound, with a rigorous convergence analysis.
* The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a comprehensive overview of related work.
* The potential for significant efficiency gains, particularly for large-scale ill-conditioned problems, justifies acceptance.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of empirical evaluation raises concerns about the practical applicability and performance of the algorithm.
* The theoretical analysis, while rigorous, is dense and may be difficult for some readers to follow.
* The discussion of practical considerations and potential limitations is limited.




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of convex optimization. The proposed algorithm, EMGD, is novel, theoretically sound, and has the potential to significantly improve the efficiency of optimization for large-scale problems. However, the lack of empirical validation is a significant weakness.  Despite this, the theoretical strength and potential impact of the work warrant acceptance, *contingent on the authors addressing the reviewer's concerns regarding practical considerations and potentially including preliminary experimental results in a future version*.

**Rating:**  Accept with Minor Revisions.  The primary revision should be to provide some empirical validation of the algorithm. Addressing the other weaknesses (proof clarity, discussion of practical issues) would further strengthen the paper.
