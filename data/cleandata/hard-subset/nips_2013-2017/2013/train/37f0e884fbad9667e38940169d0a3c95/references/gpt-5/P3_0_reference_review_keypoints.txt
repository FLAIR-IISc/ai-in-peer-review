- Paper proposes EMGD: epochal mixed-gradient method using one full gradient per epoch and T proximal steps with control-variate estimator, achieving O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients with high-probability linear convergence.
- The mixed gradient estimator is the standard control-variate used in variance-reduced methods (SVRG/SAGA); SVRG/SAGA and their proximal/distributed/high-probability variants are not cited or compared.
- Existing VR methods (SVRG/SAGA/SDCA) already achieve O(log(1/ε)) full gradients with better stochastic complexity O(κ log(1/ε)), so EMGD’s novelty is unclear.
- Strength: clear goal and simple algorithm with unbiased estimator and variance control via a shrinking trust region.
- Strength: provides a high-probability linear convergence proof and discusses constrained/low-memory settings.
- Main weakness: stochastic-gradient complexity is suboptimal (O(κ^2) vs O(κ)), likely due to analysis choices (η = 1/(L√T), shrinking-ball projections, and loose variance bounds).
- Weakness: related work is incomplete/outdated; claims about handling constraints and low memory ignore Prox-SVRG and memoryless VR variants.
- Weakness: the oracle model (exact full-gradient Og plus stochastic function oracle) is impractical outside finite-sum settings and, in finite-sum cases, is dominated by VR methods.
- Weakness: requires knowledge of κ and uses shrinking-ball projections (complicating projections and requiring awkward conditions like δ ≤ e−1/2), with large constants (e.g., T ≥ 1152κ^2 log(1/δ)).
- Weakness: no empirical evaluation to support computational claims.
- Technical: mixed gradient is unbiased but a standard VR-style variance analysis could likely remove the shrinking-ball and improve κ dependence.
- Technical: several presentation issues and possible errors — repeated equation labels, typo “epoches,” missing 1/(2η) factor in proximal update, and questionable use of strong-convexity inequality.
- Technical: distributed-computing and comparison table claims should be updated given distributed SVRG/SAGA literature.
- Significance: decoupling full-gradient count from κ is valuable but already achieved better by existing VR methods; EMGD does not advance theory or practice as presented.
- Clarity: paper is generally readable and idea is clear but needs better related work, corrected constants/assumptions, and careful editing.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Suggestions: tighten analysis to O(κ log(1/ε)) and remove shrinking-ball, properly situate and compare to SVRG/SAGA/SDCA and variants, add empirical comparisons, provide κ/Δ1-adaptive strategies, and fix typos and algorithmic constants.
