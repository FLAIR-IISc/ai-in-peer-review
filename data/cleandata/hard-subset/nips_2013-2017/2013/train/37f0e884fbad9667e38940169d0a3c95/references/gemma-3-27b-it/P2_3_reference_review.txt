## Review of "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), for smooth and strongly convex problems. The core idea is to interleave full gradient evaluations with stochastic gradient evaluations within epochs, achieving a linear convergence rate while reducing the number of full gradient accesses from the standard O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)), independent of the condition number κ.  The algorithm divides the optimization process into epochs and performs a mixed gradient descent step within each epoch, utilizing one full gradient and O(κ²) stochastic gradients.  The authors provide theoretical analysis and compare their method to existing approaches like SAG and SDCA, highlighting advantages in terms of applicability to constrained problems, storage requirements, and convergence guarantees.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a relevant problem:** Reducing the computational cost of full gradient evaluations, particularly for ill-conditioned problems, is a significant challenge in large-scale optimization.
* **Novel approach:** The combination of full and stochastic gradients within epochs to achieve condition number independent convergence is a creative idea.
* **Strong theoretical guarantees:** The paper provides a rigorous analysis demonstrating a linear convergence rate and explicitly quantifying the number of full and stochastic gradient evaluations.
* **Clear comparison to existing methods:** The authors appropriately situate their work within the broader landscape of optimization algorithms, providing a clear comparison to SAG, SDCA, and full-gradient methods.  The discussion of the trade-offs and advantages of EMGD is well-reasoned.
* **Well-written and organized:** The paper is generally clear, well-structured, and easy to follow. The algorithm is presented concisely and the notation is consistent.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Practical Considerations:** While the theoretical analysis is strong, the practical implications of the κ² factor in the number of stochastic gradient evaluations are not fully explored. For very large κ, this could become a bottleneck.  The paper acknowledges the need to estimate κ in practice, but doesn't delve into practical methods for doing so. This is a crucial point for real-world applicability.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:**  The paper lacks any experimental validation. Given the theoretical focus, this isn’t a fatal flaw, but empirical demonstration of the performance gains over existing methods, even on synthetic data, would significantly strengthen the contribution.
* **Assumptions:** The algorithm relies on the assumption of access to *both* full and stochastic gradients, which might not always be realistic in certain applications.  The paper does not discuss scenarios where obtaining full gradients is prohibitively expensive and how the algorithm performs in such cases.
* **Comparisons with other hybrid methods:** The related work section could benefit from a more thorough discussion of other hybrid full-gradient/stochastic gradient descent methods beyond the cited references, particularly those that explore similar concepts of epoch-based updates or mixing strategies.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:**  The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by the theoretical analysis. The proof appears rigorous, though a deeper dive would be required to verify every detail. The work seems complete within its defined scope.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the algorithm and its analysis.  The notation is consistent and the organization is logical.
* **Originality:** The approach of interleaving full and stochastic gradients within epochs with a guaranteed condition number independent convergence rate is novel and represents a meaningful contribution.
* **Significance:**  The potential to reduce the computational cost of optimization for ill-conditioned problems is significant. If the practical challenges can be addressed, this work could have a tangible impact on applications in machine learning and other fields.


**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant problem in large-scale optimization.
* Presents a novel and theoretically sound algorithm.
* Provides strong guarantees on convergence rate and gradient complexity.
* Offers advantages over existing methods in specific scenarios.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Lacks empirical validation to support the theoretical claims.
* The practical implications of the κ² factor are not fully discussed.
* Relies on assumptions that may not hold in all applications.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept** with the caveat that the authors should be encouraged to include empirical results in a future version. While the lack of experimentation is a weakness, the theoretical contribution is substantial, and the paper addresses an important problem in a novel and rigorous manner. The clarity of the presentation and the thorough comparison to existing methods further enhance the value of this work.  Addressing the practical considerations related to the κ² term and providing some empirical evidence would significantly strengthen the paper and solidify its impact.
