- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) using both a full-gradient oracle Og and a stochastic oracle Of with epochal variance-reduced updates and shrinking-ball constraints.  
- Claims linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) Og calls and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) Of calls for smooth, strongly convex objectives.  
- The mixed gradient estimator is the same core idea as variance-reduction methods.  
- The paper cites SAG/SDCA but omits SVRG, which is highly relevant and uses the same estimator with better complexity.  
- EMGD’s distinctive ingredient is the shrinking-ball constraint and its κ^2 inner-iteration scaling.  
- Strength: Clear motivation to reduce full-gradient evaluations in ill-conditioned problems.  
- Strength: Algorithm and analysis are conceptually simple; shrinking-domain yields high-probability linear convergence.  
- Strength: Results apply beyond finite-sum settings to general unbiased stochastic oracles.  
- Strength: Provides high-probability guarantees (Azuma–Hoeffding) rather than just in-expectation rates.  
- Weakness: Lack of citation and comparison to SVRG/SAGA/Prox-SVRG undermines novelty claims.  
- Weakness: Stochastic gradient complexity O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) is worse than O(κ log(1/ε)) from SVRG/SAGA/SDCA.  
- Weakness: The dual-oracle model (Og and Of) is restrictive or impractical in many large-scale/streaming settings.  
- Weakness: Requires knowing κ and a Δ1 depending on unknown optimum; not parameter-free.  
- Weakness: Projection onto W ∩ B(w̄; Δk) may be significantly more expensive than projection onto W alone.  
- Weakness: Manuscript has typos, formatting issues, nonstandard definitions, and missing experiments.  
- Technical: Proof technique (variance reduction + shrinking domain + martingale concentration) is standard and plausible, but the κ^2 rate seems loose.  
- Technical: Some nonstandard or unclear claims (e.g., gradient-norm form of strong convexity) should be stated in standard form.  
- Significance: Idea of reducing full-gradient dependence is important, but contribution is incremental given existing methods with better complexity.  
- To improve: Cite and compare to SVRG/SAGA, attempt to remove κ^2 factor, and justify the Og/Of model.  
- To improve: Add empirical comparisons across κ and Og/Of cost ratios, including constrained problems if claimed advantage.  
- To improve: Provide practical parameter-selection strategies (doubling tricks, adaptive epochs) and discuss projection costs.  
- Pros for acceptance: Clear goal, simple algorithm, high-probability linear convergence, low memory, potential general oracle model.  
- Cons for acceptance: Large overlap with SVRG, inferior κ^2 dependence, lack of experiments, parameter and projection issues, presentation flaws.  
- Verdict: Leaning to reject in current form; would be stronger with tighter rates, proper positioning vs SVRG/SAGA/SDCA, and experimental validation.
