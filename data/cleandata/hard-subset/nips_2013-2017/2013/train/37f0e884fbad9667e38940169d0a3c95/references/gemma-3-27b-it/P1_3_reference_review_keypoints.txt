- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), interleaving full and stochastic gradients within epochs to reduce full-gradient cost.  
- Claims linear convergence with only O(log(1/ε)) full gradients, independent of the condition number κ.  
- Builds on epoch gradient descent and situates work among deterministic, stochastic, and semi-stochastic methods (e.g., SAG, SDCA, EGD).  
- Compares complexity and practical trade-offs with SAG and SDCA (see Table 2).  
- Novel approach that addresses expensive full-gradient evaluations for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: a stated theorem and proof sketch showing linear convergence and κ-independence of full-gradient count.  
- Paper is generally well-written, with a clear algorithm (Algorithm 1) and mostly consistent notation.  
- Discusses practical considerations like estimating κ and compares costs with existing algorithms.  
- Focuses on strongly convex problems to enable aggressive convergence analysis.  
- Lacks practical implementation details (e.g., how to update domain size Δk, how to solve constrained subproblem efficiently).  
- Analysis depends on tuned parameters (η, Δ1, T) but does not discuss sensitivity or robust tuning strategies.  
- No empirical validation or runtime comparisons on real datasets or benchmarks.  
- Proof is only sketched; a full detailed proof is required for verification.  
- Assumes an oracle Of that returns a random smooth function, which is an unnatural/stilted assumption compared to standard stochastic gradient oracles.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important computational problem, offers strong theory, and is well-motivated and written.  
- Against acceptance: major concerns are missing experiments, implementation details, parameter sensitivity analysis, and a complete proof.  
- Overall: promising and theoretically compelling but currently incomplete in practical validation and exposition.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject with encouragement to add empirical evaluation, implementation guidance, parameter tuning discussion, and full proofs for resubmission.
