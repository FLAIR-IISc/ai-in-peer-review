- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), interleaving full and stochastic gradients to solve smooth strongly convex problems.
- EMGD achieves linear convergence while reducing full-gradient accesses to O(log(1/ε)), independent of condition number κ.
- Algorithm operates in epochs using one full gradient and O(κ²) stochastic gradients per epoch.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis quantifying full and stochastic gradient complexities and proving linear convergence.
- Compares EMGD to SAG, SDCA, and full-gradient methods, highlighting applicability to constrained problems and lower storage needs.
- Addresses a relevant problem for large-scale/ill-conditioned optimization.
- The mixing of full and stochastic gradients within epochs is a novel and creative approach.
- Strong theoretical guarantees and a clear, well-structured presentation with consistent notation.
- Practical implications of the O(κ²) stochastic gradient cost are not fully explored and could be a bottleneck for large κ.
- The paper lacks empirical validation—no experiments on synthetic or real data to demonstrate practical gains.
- Assumes availability of both full and stochastic gradients, which may be unrealistic in some applications and is not deeply discussed.
- Related work section could expand comparisons to other hybrid epoch- or mixing-based methods beyond cited references.
- Overall judged technically sound, original, and significant if practical challenges are addressed.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, novel theory, strong guarantees, useful trade-offs versus existing methods.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing experiments, unclear practical impact of κ² term, and possibly strong/limiting assumptions.
- Final verdict: strong accept conditional on encouraging empirical validation and discussion of practical κ estimation/κ² implications.
