- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes full and stochastic gradients to get linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients (κ-independent) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Theoretical analysis is strong: Theorem 1 and proofs use appropriate tools (martingale inequalities, strong convexity) and are well-supported.
- Assumes the condition number κ is known a priori, which is a significant practical limitation.
- No empirical validation or experiments are provided to demonstrate practical benefits.
- Computational complexity claims assume stochastic gradients are “efficiently computable” but do not clarify or compare this cost to full gradients.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with precise algorithm description and consistent notation.
- Explanation of why the mixed gradient approach works (shrinking domain bounds stochastic error) is clear and accessible.
- Contribution is novel in using a mixed gradient with a shrinking domain to achieve condition-number-independent full gradient access.
- Does not sufficiently distinguish EMGD from other semi-stochastic methods in the literature; needs clearer theoretical/practical contrasts.
- Advantages over SAG/SDCA highlighted: applicable to constrained/unconstrained problems, lower storage O(d) vs O(n), and high-probability convergence vs expectation.
- Significance is moderate: important theoretical advancement for ill-conditioned problems, but practical impact is unclear without experiments and runtime comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept — technically sound and valuable theoretically, but would be strengthened by empirical evaluation and addressing the κ-known assumption.
