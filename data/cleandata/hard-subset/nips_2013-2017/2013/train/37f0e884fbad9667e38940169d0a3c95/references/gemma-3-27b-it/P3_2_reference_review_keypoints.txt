- Overall assessment: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) with condition-number-independent full-gradient calls; theoretically sound but practical concerns lead to a Weak Reject.

- Summary & related work: EMGD mixes epoch-based full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence with full-gradient access independent of κ, but related-work comparisons (Table 2) may be cherry-picked.

- Novelty: Mixing epoch-based descent with combined full+stochastic gradients is a novel and potentially valuable idea.

- Theoretical analysis: Provides a clear (though dense) proof of linear convergence using Lemma 1 and Hoeffding-Azuma; main theoretical claim is significant.

- Practical relevance: Addresses the important problem of reducing costly full-gradient evaluations for large-scale optimization.

- Parallelization potential: Algorithm could leverage distributed computing to amortize full-gradient costs.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance depends on several hyperparameters (η, Δ1, T, m) with little guidance on practical selection, notably Δ1 tied to problem scaling.

- Incomplete cost analysis: Overall computational cost is inadequately analyzed—each epoch needs O(κ²) stochastic gradients and projection costs are not fully accounted for.

- Limited empirical/algorithmic comparison: Comparisons to SAG and SDCA are limited and ignore practical engineering optimizations that may favor those methods.

- Questionable assumptions: Assumes access to both full and stochastic gradients, which may be unrealistic in some settings and is not thoroughly discussed.

- Clarity issues: Algorithm 1’s notation and indexing are confusing and could be made more concise; several minor grammatical errors exist.

- Originality & significance: Core idea is original and theoretically significant, but practical impact is uncertain without more analysis.

- Pro: Novel algorithm, strong theoretical guarantee, relevant problem focus, parallelization potential.

- Con: Lacks practical guidance on hyperparameters, incomplete cost accounting, limited practical comparisons, strong assumptions, and unclear presentation.

- Recommendation: Weak Reject; request major revisions including detailed cost analysis, empirical comparisons to SAG/SDCA, hyperparameter selection strategies, discussion of the full/stochastic gradient assumption, and clearer algorithm presentation.
