- Paper proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes one full gradient per epoch with T stochastic inner steps using a control-variate estimator and shrinking trust regions to get O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.

- The mixed estimator is identical to the control-variate used in variance-reduced methods (e.g., SVRG/SAGA/Prox-SVRG).

- High-level proof (per-epoch contraction using smoothness, strong convexity, and martingale bounds) is standard and appears correct; shrinking ball plus Hoeffding–Azuma gives clean high-probability guarantees.

- The paper incorrectly states strong convexity as ‖∇F(w)−∇F(w′)‖ ≥ λ‖w−w′‖ and should correct to the standard inequality used later.

- The function-oracle cost model is ambiguous: inner steps effectively require two component gradient evaluations and this accounting must be made explicit.

- Algorithm requires knowledge of κ and an initial radius Δ1 dependent on F(w*), which is impractical; adaptive schemes should be proposed.

- Projection onto W ∩ B(w̄k; Δk) may be nontrivial for general constraint sets, undermining claimed advantages for constrained problems.

- Notation and typesetting need fixes (e.g., proximal update quadratic term, misspelling “epochs,” clarify smoothness/strong-convexity assumptions).

- The paper fails to cite and compare to SVRG/Prox-SVRG/SAGA, which is a significant omission given identical estimators and better complexities.

- EMGD’s stochastic-gradient complexity of O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) is worse than SVRG/Prox-SVRG’s O(κ log(1/ε)) or SAGA/SDCA’s O((n+κ) log(1/ε)).

- Claimed advantages over SAG/SDCA (memory, constraints, high-probability rates) also apply to SVRG/Prox-SVRG and should be acknowledged and discussed.

- No experiments are provided to demonstrate practical advantages (e.g., constrained or distributed settings), weakening the empirical claim.

- Pros: clear motivation to reduce full-gradient use, simple low-memory algorithm, applies to constrained settings if projections are efficient, and provides high-probability guarantees.

- Cons: substantial overlap with existing variance-reduction methods, worse κ-dependence, needs κ and Δ1, ambiguous oracle accounting, missing related work, and no empirical validation.

- Suggestions: add comparisons to SVRG/Prox-SVRG/SAGA, attempt tighter analysis to achieve T = O(κ), correct strong-convexity definition and update formatting, provide adaptive schemes for κ and Δ1, clarify oracle cost, and include experiments.

- Recommendation: Reject in current form; contributions are incremental and weaker than state of the art, but a revised version with tighter analysis, proper positioning, and experiments could be competitive.
