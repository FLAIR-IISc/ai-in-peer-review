- Paper proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) combining full and stochastic gradients to reduce full-gradient evaluations in smooth, strongly convex optimization.  
- Claims linear convergence O(log(1/ε)) with number of full-gradient evaluations independent of condition number κ; each epoch uses one full gradient and O(κ²) stochastic gradients.  
- Positions work relative to deterministic (Nesterov) and stochastic (SGD, SAG, SDCA) methods, highlighting iteration vs per-iteration cost trade-offs and links to epoch gradient descent.  
- Comparison table (Table 2) is informative and situates EMGD against related algorithms.  
- Strength: tackles a practical large-scale optimization problem with clear motivation.  
- Strength: EMGD is a novel, conceptually sound algorithm mixing epoch structure and gradient types.  
- Strength: Provides theoretical convergence guarantees showing linear rate and κ-independent full-gradient count.  
- Strength: Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest future work (e.g., condition number estimation).  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and organized.  
- Weakness: O(κ²) stochastic gradients per epoch may still be prohibitively expensive for large κ; mitigation strategies are not explored.  
- Weakness: No empirical evaluation or experiments to validate theoretical claims on real datasets.  
- Weakness: Requires access to both full and stochastic gradients, limiting applicability.  
- Weakness: Hidden constant factors in asymptotic bounds are not addressed and may harm practical performance.  
- Weakness: Some proof details are omitted or glossed over, reducing confidence in correctness.  
- Overall: Theoretical contribution is valuable and potentially impactful, but lack of experiments and practical analysis weakens the paper.  
- For acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, relevant problem, and clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: major lack of empirical validation, practical cost concerns for O(κ²) stochastic gradients, and incomplete proof details.  
- Rating/Recommendation: Weak Reject; recommend rejection pending experiments and addressing practical/proof concerns before resubmission.
