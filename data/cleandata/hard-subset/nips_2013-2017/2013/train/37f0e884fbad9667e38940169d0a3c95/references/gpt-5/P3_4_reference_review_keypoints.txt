- Studies smooth λ-strongly convex optimization with access to full gradient oracle and unbiased stochastic function oracle, aiming to decouple full-gradient calls from condition number κ.  
- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): one full gradient per epoch plus T inner stochastic updates using control-variate mixed gradients and shrinking ℓ2 trust regions.  
- Claims per-epoch constant contraction yielding O(log(1/ϵ)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ϵ)) stochastic gradients with high probability.  
- Related work section misses SVRG and SAGA, despite identical control-variate estimator and better κ dependence—this is a major omission.  
- Technical proof is clear and standard (reference-point rewrite, variance control via shrinking ball, Azuma-Hoeffding, induction/union bound) and is correct.  
- Key limitation: stochastic gradient complexity has suboptimal κ^2 dependence due to variance bound via L^2‖w−w̄‖^2 and shrinking ball; SVRG-style analyses obtain O(κ) per epoch.  
- EMGD requires knowledge of κ to set T and η; proposed heuristics are not sufficient for adaptivity claims.  
- Analysis needs an initial radius Δ1 containing w*, which is impractical in unconstrained settings unless user supplies F(0)−F(w*).  
- Per-iteration projection onto W∩B(w̄k;Δk) may be costly for complex constraint sets, affecting practical efficiency.  
- Minor presentation issues: typos/notation in Algorithm 1 (missing 1/2 in proximal term, “epoches”) and occasional imprecise assumptions.  
- Algorithmic idea (control variates with one full gradient per epoch) is not novel—closely matches SVRG—so originality is limited.  
- Shrinking trust region is a distinguishing feature but leads to worse κ dependence, reducing practical advantage.  
- Positive: decoupling full-gradient count from κ is meaningful and the method has low memory footprint and supports constrained domains.  
- Negative: κ^2 dependence makes EMGD less competitive than SVRG/SDCA in both finite-sum and oracle settings.  
- No empirical evaluation to show regimes where many cheap stochastic gradients vs. few expensive full gradients is advantageous.  
- Paper should directly compare theoretically and experimentally to SVRG/SAGA and clarify where EMGD outperforms them.  
- Suggest tightening analysis to achieve T=O(κ) without shrinking ball or making EMGD adaptive to unknown κ (e.g., doubling/line-search).  
- Ask for clarification on wall-clock trade-offs in finite-sum case when full gradients can be parallelized.  
- Request experiments illustrating regimes where EMGD excels (heavy regularization, very large n, constrained settings).  
- Recommendation: lean toward rejection due to incomplete related work and weaker theoretical guarantees; acceptance possible with thorough SVRG/SAGA comparison and improved analysis or strong empirical evidence.
