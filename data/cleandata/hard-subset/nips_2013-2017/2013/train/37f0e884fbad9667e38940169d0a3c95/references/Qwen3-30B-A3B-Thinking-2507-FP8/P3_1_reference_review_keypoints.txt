- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) combining full and stochastic gradients to reduce full gradient evaluations from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)).

- EMGD runs in epochs with one full gradient and O(κ²) stochastic gradients per epoch and is proven to converge linearly.

- Theoretical analysis (including Theorem 1) is rigorous and well-structured, using standard convex optimization techniques.

- No empirical validation: paper contains no experiments to validate theoretical claims or demonstrate practical benefits.

- Requires knowing the condition number κ in advance, a significant practical limitation not adequately addressed.

- Computational comparison focuses on gradient counts only and lacks wall-clock/time complexity analysis and justification of stochastic vs full gradient costs.

- Paper is well-written, organized, with consistent notation and clear pseudocode.

- Needs clearer intuition for the mixed gradient g̃kt = ∇F(w̄k) + ∇fkt(wkt) − ∇fkt(w̄k) and why it outperforms plain stochastic gradients.

- The mechanism behind achieving condition-number independence needs higher-level explanation for better understanding.

- Comparison with SAG and SDCA is useful but lacks context (e.g., why κ ≤ n^(2/3) is reasonable) and concrete practical examples.

- Contribution appears original: EMGD and its condition-number-independent full-gradient bound are novel and positioned within existing literature.

- Should better distinguish EMGD from other semi-stochastic methods [12,13] and clarify claimed advantages.

- Significance: theoretically important for large-scale ML by reducing expensive full gradient evaluations.

- Practical impact limited by the κ requirement and absence of empirical evidence.

- Strengths: rigorous theory, novel insight, clear writing, and contextualized comparisons to related work.

- Weaknesses: no experiments, need to estimate κ in practice, insufficient computational cost discussion, and weak motivation for mixed gradient.

- Recommendation: weak acceptance — solid theoretical contribution but needs empirical validation and better handling of κ.

- Suggested improvement: add experiments on standard ML tasks comparing EMGD, Nesterov, SAG, SDCA, measuring both full-gradient counts and wall-clock time.

- Suggested improvement: expand discussion and methods for estimating κ in practice, with analysis of trade-offs.

- Suggested improvement: provide clearer intuition for the mixed gradient and why it yields condition-number independence.

- Suggested improvement: include a detailed time-cost comparison between stochastic and full gradient evaluations.

- Suggested improvement: further clarify differences and advantages relative to existing semi-stochastic approaches.
