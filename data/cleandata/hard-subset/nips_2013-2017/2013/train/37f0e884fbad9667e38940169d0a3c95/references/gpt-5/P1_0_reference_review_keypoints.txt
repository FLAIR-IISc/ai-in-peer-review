- Summary: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) using periodic full gradients plus variance-reduced stochastic updates, claiming linear high-probability convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.

- Algorithm detail: Each epoch computes ∇F at a snapshot, then T inner mixed-gradient steps g̃ = ∇F(snapshot) + ∇f(w) − ∇f(snapshot) with projection to a shrinking ball.

- Main theorem: After m epochs, error halves each epoch with stepsize η = 1/(L√T) and T = Ω(κ^2 log(1/δ)), yielding the stated gradient complexities.

- Relation to SVRG: The mixed estimator and epoch strategy are essentially SVRG (Johnson & Zhang 2013); SVRG is highly relevant but not cited.

- Relation to SAG/SAGA/SDCA: Compares qualitatively but omits precise relation in complexity and convergence modes; EMGD uses O(d) memory unlike SAG/SAGA.

- Strength — goal: Clear objective to reduce expensive full-gradient evaluations while keeping linear convergence.

- Strength — simplicity: Algorithmic structure is simple and practical for finite-sum problems with snapshot gradients.

- Strength — high-probability guarantee: Provides Azuma-Hoeffding based linear convergence with high probability rather than only expectation.

- Strength — memory and constraints: Uses O(d) memory and can handle constrained problems if projections are tractable.

- Weakness — κ^2 dependence: Requires T = Ω(κ^2 log(1/δ)), leading to O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, which is worse than SVRG/SDCA (κ or n+κ dependence).

- Weakness — missing related work: Failure to cite SVRG/SAGA undermines novelty claims and obscures prior art.

- Weakness — oracle assumptions: Requires both full-gradient oracle and function oracle allowing multiple evaluations per sampled f, which is natural for finite-sum but nonstandard in general stochastic settings.

- Weakness — technical error: Strong convexity is misstated as a gradient-norm lower bound; needs correction to the standard quadratic form.

- Weakness — practicality issues: Needs knowledge of κ to set parameters, projection onto W ∩ ball may be hard, and there are no experiments to assess constants or empirical competitiveness.

- Weakness — claims/comparisons: Some assertions (e.g., distributed implementability, superiority to SAG/SDCA) are not rigorously justified.

- Quality: Analysis is standard variance-reduction with shrinking trust regions and martingale concentration; broadly sound but conservative and produces suboptimal κ^2 bound.

- Clarity: Readable with clear algorithms/theorems, but contains typos, formatting issues, omitted citations, and the incorrect strong convexity statement.

- Originality: Core idea closely mirrors SVRG; novelty mainly in shrinking-ball and high-probability framing, so originality is limited.

- Significance: The theoretical point on O(log(1/ε)) full gradients is interesting but not new for finite-sum problems; practical impact is limited given κ^2 cost and no experiments.

- Suggestions: Cite and compare to SVRG/SAGA/SDCA, fix strong convexity and related inequalities, attempt to improve κ^2 to κ, add experiments and cost-modeled comparisons, and clarify oracle/projection practicality.

- Recommendation: Lean reject due to limited novelty versus variance-reduced methods, worse stochastic complexity, missing key references, and lack of empirical validation.

- Confidence: Reviewer is highly confident in these assessments.
