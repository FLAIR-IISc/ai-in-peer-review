This paper introduces a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), designed to reduce the computational cost of full-gradient methods for smooth, strongly convex optimization problems, particularly when the condition number is large. The core idea is to strategically combine full and stochastic gradients within epochs to achieve a linear convergence rate with a number of full gradient evaluations independent of the condition number. The authors present theoretical analysis demonstrating this improvement over standard full-gradient methods, achieving O(log 1/ǫ) full gradient evaluations compared to O(√κ log 1/ǫ). 

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the algorithm, its theoretical foundations, and comparisons with related work. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and highlights the potential benefits of the proposed approach. The related work section provides a comprehensive overview of existing methods, including deterministic, stochastic, and semi-stochastic optimization techniques, and accurately positions the EMGD algorithm within the existing landscape. The algorithm description is concise and easy to follow. The theoretical analysis, while dense, appears rigorous and logically sound, culminating in a clear statement of the convergence rate in Theorem 1. Comparisons with SAG and SDCA are insightful, highlighting both the strengths and weaknesses of EMGD.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of full and stochastic gradients within an epoch-based framework to achieve condition number independence in the number of full gradient evaluations is a novel contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, with a well-defined convergence rate and supporting lemmas. The proof sketch provided gives confidence in the validity of the claims.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The algorithm is presented clearly, and the theoretical arguments are explained in a logical manner.
*   **Significance:** Reducing the computational cost of full gradient evaluations, especially for ill-conditioned problems, is a significant practical concern. The proposed algorithm offers a potential solution to this problem.
*   **Comprehensive Related Work:** The related work section provides a good overview of the current state of the art and correctly situates the paper's contribution.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Practical Considerations:** The paper acknowledges the need to estimate the condition number (κ) in practice, but the suggested approaches (sampling, leveraging regularizers) are mentioned briefly and lack detailed elaboration. This is a critical practical hurdle that could limit the algorithm's applicability.  More discussion and potentially preliminary experiments exploring these estimation techniques would be valuable.
*   **Computational Overhead:** While EMGD reduces the number of full gradient evaluations, the introduction of stochastic gradients and the epoch-based structure introduces additional computational overhead. A more detailed analysis of the overall computational complexity, taking into account both gradient evaluations and the cost of the epoch management, would strengthen the paper.  The comparison to SAG and SDCA doesn't fully account for this.
*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The paper lacks empirical evaluation. Demonstrating the performance of EMGD on real-world datasets and comparing it with existing methods would significantly strengthen the claims and demonstrate its practical effectiveness.
*   **Algorithm 1 Step 8:** The minimization step in Algorithm 1, `wkt+1 = argmin w∈W∩B(w̄k;∆k) η〈w −wkt , g̃kt 〉+ 1/‖w −wkt ‖2`, is a potentially expensive operation. The authors should elaborate on the computational cost and possible efficient solution strategies for this step.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound algorithm that addresses a significant practical problem in convex optimization.
*   The analysis demonstrates a clear improvement over existing methods in terms of the number of full gradient evaluations.
*   The paper is well-written, organized, and provides a comprehensive overview of related work.
*   The potential impact of reducing full gradient evaluations, especially in large-scale machine learning applications, is substantial.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of empirical evaluation limits the assessment of the algorithm’s practical effectiveness.
*   The discussion of practical considerations, particularly condition number estimation, is insufficient.
*   The computational overhead introduced by the algorithm is not fully analyzed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable theoretical contribution to the field of convex optimization. The proposed algorithm has the potential to improve the efficiency of optimization methods for large-scale problems. However, the lack of empirical evaluation and the limited discussion of practical considerations raise some concerns. Therefore, I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The authors should address the weaknesses by including:

1.  A more detailed discussion of practical methods for estimating the condition number.
2.  An analysis of the overall computational complexity, including the overhead introduced by the stochastic gradients and epoch management.
3.  Empirical results demonstrating the performance of EMGD on real-world datasets, compared to relevant baseline algorithms.
4. Further discussion on efficient implementation of the constrained optimization step in Algorithm 1.
