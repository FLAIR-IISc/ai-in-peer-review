- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), interleaving full and stochastic gradients to reduce full-gradient evaluations for smooth strongly convex problems.  
- Claims linear convergence with only O(log(1/ε)) full gradient evaluations, independent of the condition number κ.  
- Positions EMGD as bridging full-gradient methods’ speed and stochastic methods’ low per-iteration cost.  
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis (Theorem 1) and a proof outline in the appendix.  
- Connects to Epoch Gradient Descent (EGD) and frames EMGD as an extension using stochastic gradients to lower full-gradient dependence.  
- Compares computational complexity to methods like SAG, SDCA, and Nesterov, with a helpful Table 2.  
- Novel approach: mixing full and stochastic gradients in an epoch framework to target condition-number independence.  
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous analysis supporting linear convergence and reduced full-gradient count.  
- Clear motivation: addresses practical cost of full gradients when κ is large.  
- Potential practical impact for problems with cheap stochastic gradients and expensive full gradients.  
- Lacks experimental validation; unclear empirical performance and sensitivity to parameters (η, Δ1, T).  
- Parameter tuning concerns: the requirement T ≥ 1152 L^2/λ^2 ln(δ) seems restrictive and possibly impractical.  
- Relies on knowing the condition number κ a priori, which is a practical limitation.  
- The specific mixed-gradient form (g̃_kt) is not intuitively justified and needs clearer explanation.  
- Limited discussion on the shrinking domain constraint and its practical solvability across scenarios.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous, relevant to NIPS, and offers a useful comparative analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no experiments, restrictive parameter conditions, and dependence on known κ.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution but weakened by practical gaps; recommend rejection pending experimental and practical improvements.
