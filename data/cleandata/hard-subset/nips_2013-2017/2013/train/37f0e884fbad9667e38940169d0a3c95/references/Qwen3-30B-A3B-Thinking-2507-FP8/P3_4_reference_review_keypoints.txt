- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that uses one full gradient plus O(κ²) stochastic gradients per epoch to achieve linear convergence.  
- Claims EMGD attains ε-optimality with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and adapts standard convex optimization techniques to the mixed-gradient setting.  
- Proof carefully controls stochastic error via domain shrinking B(w̄ᵏ; Δᵏ); requirement T = O(κ²) is justified under smoothness assumptions.  
- Main practical limitation: algorithm requires knowledge of the condition number κ a priori.  
- Major omission: no empirical results to validate theoretical claims or practical computational benefits.  
- Computational-advantage claim (EMGD better when κ ≤ n^(2/3)) assumes full gradients cost n times stochastic ones, but actual savings are not quantified.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with consistent notation and logical presentation of algorithm and proofs.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical implementation issues, especially handling the domain constraint in constrained optimization.  
- The mixed full/stochastic gradient idea is novel and distinguishes itself from SAG/SDCA via O(d) storage, applicability to constrained problems, and high-probability guarantees.  
- Paper does not fully situate the idea in broader literature; related stochastic primal-dual or incremental-with-occasional-full-update methods may be similar.  
- Significance is high for large-scale ML, but O(κ²) per-epoch stochastic cost can offset benefits for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, clear comparisons, simple/elegant approach, and practical advantages over SAG/SDCA in certain respects.  
- Weaknesses: no experiments, needs κ estimation strategy, potentially prohibitive O(κ²) stochastic cost, and limited practical guidance.  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance contingent on adding empirical validation, proposing a practical κ-estimation method, and expanding discussion of trade-offs.
