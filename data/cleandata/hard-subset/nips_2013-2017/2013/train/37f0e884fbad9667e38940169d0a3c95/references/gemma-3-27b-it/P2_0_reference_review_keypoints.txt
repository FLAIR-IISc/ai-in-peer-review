- Proposal of Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that interleaves full and stochastic gradients to get linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients independent of condition number κ.
- Targets the practical bottleneck of expensive full gradient evaluations in large-scale, ill-conditioned problems.
- Novel epoch-based mixing of full and stochastic gradients; independence from κ in full-gradient complexity is notable.
- Strong theoretical analysis with lemmas and an inductive proof guaranteeing linear convergence under assumptions.
- Compares EMGD to methods like Nesterov acceleration, SAG, and SDCA to position its computational advantages.
- Discusses practical issues such as estimating the condition number and possible implementation avenues.
- Assumes ready access to both full and stochastic gradients, which may not hold in many settings and limits applicability.
- Hidden constant factors may be large (e.g., T ≥ 1152 L^2/λ^2 ln(δ)), so per-epoch cost could offset reduced full-gradient frequency.
- The specific form of the mixed gradient (∇F(w̄k) + ∇fkt(wkt) − ∇fkt(w̄k)) lacks clear intuitive justification.
- No empirical evaluation is provided; practical performance versus SGD or semi-stochastic methods is untested.
- Related work section is somewhat repetitive and could be streamlined.
- Overall technically sound and rigorous, but practical impact is unclear due to missing experiments.
- Quality score: 7/10 — solid theory but needs validation.
- Clarity score: 8/10 — well-written though mixed-gradient motivation could improve.
- Originality score: 8/10 — novel combination of epoching and mixed gradients.
- Significance score: 6/10 — potentially valuable if empirical results confirm theory.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, addresses an important computational problem, and gives κ-independent full-gradient guarantees.
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks experiments, relies on strong/possibly unrealistic gradient-access assumptions, and may suffer from large hidden constants.
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on adding thorough empirical evaluation and addressing constant factors and intuition for the mixed gradient.
