- Problem/goal: Reduce full-gradient calls in smooth strongly-convex optimization to O(log(1/ε)) by combining one full gradient per epoch with many stochastic gradients (EMGD).

- Method: EMGD uses a mixed-gradient estimator ∇F(w̄) + ∇f(w) − ∇f(w̄), shrinking projection balls per epoch, averaging iterates.

- Main theoretical claim: Linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient calls and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls (high probability).

- Relation to prior work: Estimator and epoch structure closely match SVRG; paper omits citation/comparison to SVRG and related SAGA/SVRG variants.

- Complexity critique: EMGD’s T = Θ(κ^2) inner iterations is substantially worse than Θ(κ) inner lengths in SVRG/SAGA analyses, and weaker than O((n+κ) log(1/ε)) finite-sum bounds.

- Strength: Clear objective and simple epoch-based, martingale-concentration proof yielding high-probability linear convergence.

- Strength: Works with a function oracle compatible with finite-sum settings and has low memory overhead; discusses constrained settings.

- Novelty concern: Core estimator is essentially identical to SVRG; claimed κ-independence of full-gradient count is already achieved by SVRG.

- Technical correctness issues: Nonstandard/incorrect strong convexity definition and a factor-of-2 error in inequalities that affect constants and rigor.

- Practicality issues: Requires knowing κ to set hyperparameters and an initial anchor radius upper-bounding distance to optimum; shrinking ball may be impractical.

- Empirical gap: No experiments provided to justify practical benefits or compare to established variance-reduction methods.

- Comparison gap: Key methods (SVRG, SAGA) are omitted from comparisons; claimed advantages over SAG/SDCA are partially shared by SVRG/SAGA.

- Analysis observation: κ^2 dependence likely comes from variance bounds and shrinking-domain device; tighter SVRG-style variance analysis could yield κ dependence.

- Clarity issues: Readable overall but has typos, nonstandard definitions, and some imprecise proof steps (e.g., averaging justification, function-oracle discussion).

- Originality assessment: Limited—shrinking projection is a minor twist that appears to worsen, not improve, dependence on κ.

- Significance assessment: Incremental contribution with weaker complexity than existing VR methods; limited impact without improved bounds, broader applicability, or experiments.

- Suggestions: Explicitly compare with SVRG/SAGA, attempt to reduce κ^2 to κ, remove need to know κ via adaptive schemes, auto-estimate anchor radius, and add experiments on constrained finite-sum problems.

- Pros for acceptance: Clean high-probability analysis and potential applicability to constrained problems.

- Cons for acceptance: Heavy overlap with VR literature, weaker complexity, missing related work, technical imprecision, and no empirical validation.

- Overall recommendation: Not recommended for acceptance in current form; revise to situate within VR literature, correct analysis, improve κ dependence, and add experiments.
