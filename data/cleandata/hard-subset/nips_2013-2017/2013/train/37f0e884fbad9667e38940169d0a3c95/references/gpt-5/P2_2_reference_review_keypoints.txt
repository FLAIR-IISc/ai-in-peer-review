- Contribution: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) using one full gradient per epoch plus mixed stochastic updates, claiming O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic-gradient calls (high probability).
- Estimator: Uses the standard control-variate mixed-gradient g̃ = ∇F(w̄) + ∇f(w) − ∇f(w̄) (as in SVRG-type methods).
- Analysis approach: Epoching, projection to a shrinking ball, and martingale concentration (Azuma–Hoeffding) yield high-probability linear convergence.
- Complexity gap: Dependence on the condition number is suboptimal—requires T = Ω(κ^2 log(1/δ)) stochastic steps per epoch versus known O(κ) or O(n+κ) results.
- Loose bounds: The analysis appears loose relative to existing variance-reduction benchmarks and yields large constants.
- Novelty: Limited novelty—main distinct element is the shrinking-domain projection to control variance; the estimator itself is standard.
- Missing related work: Fails to adequately cite and compare to closely related variance-reduction methods (e.g., SVRG/SAGA/SAG/SDCA) that achieve better complexity.
- Clarity/notation issues: Typos and inconsistencies (missing 1/2 in proximal term, duplicated equation labels, informal phrasing) reduce readability and confidence.
- Practical concerns: Projection onto W ∩ B may be nontrivial in practice, and the method requires knowledge of κ and Δ1.
- No empirical validation: Paper provides no experiments to demonstrate practical performance or computational trade-offs (wall-clock, cost of full vs. stochastic gradients).
- Strengths: Clear goal and simple algorithmic structure, applicability to constrained problems, O(d) memory, and a clean high-probability analysis template.
- Weaknesses: Suboptimal κ^2 stochastic cost, missing citations/comparisons, notation/proof details needing fixes, and unclear practicality of projections.
- Suggestions: Tighten analysis to target O(κ) steps, remove or justify shrinking-domain device, add experiments (constrained/unconstrained), fix notation, and address unknown-κ strategies.
- Recommendation: Weak reject—interesting and well-presented idea but re-derives known estimator with looser bounds, lacks positioning and experiments; needs tighter complexity and empirical evidence.
