- Overall: Proposes EMGD to reduce full gradient evaluations to O(log 1/ε) independent of κ; reviewer recommends Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Summary: Tackles the trade-off between per-iteration cost and convergence by mixing full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence with κ-independent full-gradient count.  
- Positioning: Paper correctly situates itself among gradient, accelerated, and stochastic methods and cites relevant prior work, including Epoch Gradient Descent.  
- Novelty: Systematic mixing of full and stochastic gradients is a nontrivial and valuable contribution.  
- Theoretical Rigor: Provides a solid convergence guarantee with a logical inductive proof sketch.  
- Clarity and Motivation: Problem statement and trade-offs are clearly articulated.  
- Practical Impact: Reducing expensive full-gradient computations is important for very large datasets.  
- Limitations Acknowledged: Authors note need for condition-number estimation and potential for distributed extensions.  
- κ Estimation Sensitivity: Algorithm depends on knowing or estimating κ; sensitivity to inaccuracies in κ is not analyzed.  
- Hyperparameter Sensitivity: Choice of step size η and epoch length T is crucial but unexplored, especially for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Comparisons Insufficient: Empirical and detailed theoretical comparisons with SAG and SDCA are superficial and need expansion; the n-times-cost assumption for full gradients may not hold.  
- Projection Cost: Projection onto W ∩ B(w̄k, Δk) in Algorithm 1 may be computationally expensive and is not discussed.  
- No Experiments: Paper lacks empirical validation on benchmark datasets to demonstrate practical advantage.  
- Notation Clarity: Some notation (e.g., fkt) should be defined directly in the algorithm for readability.  
- Statement Precision: Clarify that O(log 1/ε) refers to number of full gradient evaluations, not total iteration complexity.  
- Comparative Regime: The claim that EMGD is better than Nesterov only when κ ≤ n²/3 needs clearer illustration and justification.  
- Step 8 Feasibility: Need discussion or analysis of how to solve the constrained minimization in Algorithm 1 efficiently.  
- Proof Details: Provide fuller details in Section 4, specifically bounds on AT and BT, to increase confidence in correctness.  
- Adaptive Strategies: Suggest exploring adaptive step-size methods to avoid requiring precise κ knowledge.  
- Pros (accept): Novel algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, and clear motivation.  
- Cons (reject risks): Missing experiments, limited state-of-the-art comparison, and unresolved practical issues (κ estimation, projection).  
- Required Revisions: Add empirical results on benchmarks; provide detailed comparisons with SAG/SDCA including computational costs; analyze sensitivity to κ estimation; address projection computational cost; and improve notation clarity.
