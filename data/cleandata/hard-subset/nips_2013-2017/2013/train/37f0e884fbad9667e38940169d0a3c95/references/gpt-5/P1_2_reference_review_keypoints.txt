- Paper proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): per-epoch full gradient at anchor plus T projected mixed-gradient steps using control-variate estimator, with shrinking ℓ2-ball and averaging.  
- Claimed oracle complexity: O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient accesses and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic function-oracle accesses (κ = L/λ).  
- Method closely resembles SVRG/SAGA family — same control-variate estimator and one full gradient per epoch — but adds shrinking-ball constraint and high-probability analysis.  
- Technical correctness: high-level proof is standard and coherent, but contains errors that must be fixed (notably Eq. (4) on gradients and log notation).  
- Specific technical mistakes: incorrect strong-convexity norm inequality in Eq. (4) and sign/missing 1 in ln(1/δ) in condition (5).  
- The κ^2 dependence appears suboptimal compared to SVRG’s O(κ) behavior and may be an artifact of conservative step-size and concentration arguments.  
- Oracle assumption Of (returns random f with F = E[f]) is stronger than generic unbiased stochastic gradients and aligns with finite-sum settings.  
- Algorithm requires knowledge of κ to set T and shrinkage schedule; no adaptive procedure is provided.  
- Clarity issues: numerous typos/grammar, inconsistent notation (ln δ vs ln(1/δ)), algorithm formatting, and missing symbols — needs cleanup.  
- Practicality: constrained projection step assumes tractable projection onto W ∩ B(w̄k; Δk); this should be discussed.  
- Novelty is limited: main estimator/template already in SVRG/SAGA; EMGD’s distinct elements (shrinking-ball, high-probability bound) are incremental.  
- Significance is limited: reducing full-gradient calls to O(log(1/ε)) is useful but stochastic-gradient κ^2 cost and lack of experiments weaken practical impact.  
- Related work omissions: fails to cite/compare adequately to SVRG, SAGA, Katyusha, and modern proximal/SDCA analyses.  
- Pros: clear goal of decoupling full-gradient calls from κ, high-probability linear convergence, O(d) memory, works with constraints if projection is tractable.  
- Cons: limited novelty vs SVRG/SAGA, suboptimal κ^2 dependence, reliance on shrinking ball and known κ, technical inaccuracies, and no empirical validation.  
- Major suggestions: add thorough theoretical and empirical comparisons to SVRG/SAGA/Katyusha, fix technical errors, explore refined analysis to reduce κ^2 to κ, provide experiments (including constrained problems), and propose an adaptive scheme that avoids knowing κ.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — interesting goal and reasonable analysis but duplicates known variance-reduction ideas with weaker complexity, missing comparisons/experiments, and contains technical errors to fix.
