- Introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) to reduce costly full gradient evaluations by interleaving full and stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical result: O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients to reach ε-optimality.  
- Addresses a practical large-scale optimization problem relevant to machine learning.  
- Novel epoch-based mixed gradient approach achieving condition-number-independent full gradient access.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and linear convergence guarantees.  
- Clearly positions EMGD relative to methods like Nesterov, SAG, and SDCA with computational complexity comparisons.  
- Discusses practical considerations, including the need to know or estimate the condition number.  
- Well-written and well-organized presentation with consistent notation.  
- Full-gradient count is independent of κ but stochastic gradient count scales as κ², limiting practical gains if stochastic gradients are expensive.  
- Domain shrinkage per epoch may be impractical if the optimum lies outside the shrinking region and is not fully addressed.  
- Relies on strong assumptions (smoothness, strong convexity, unbiased oracle) with no robustness analysis for violations.  
- Lacks empirical evaluation; no experiments to validate theoretical claims or compare with baselines.  
- Algorithm appears more complex to implement than standard SGD, requiring stronger justification via performance gains.  
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on adding experiments comparing EMGD to SGD/SAG/SDCA, examining κ² impact, robustness to assumption violations, and domain shrinkage behavior.
