- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), a hybrid algorithm mixing full and stochastic gradients for smooth strongly convex problems.  
- Proves EMGD attains linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradient accesses independent of condition number κ.  
- EMGD uses one full gradient per epoch and O(κ²) stochastic gradients per epoch, totaling O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Contrasts with standard full-gradient methods (e.g., Nesterov) that need O(√κ log(1/ε)) full gradients.  
- Correctly positions work relative to prior methods (SGD, SAG, SDCA) and cites relevant optimal complexities.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-executed, with a detailed induction-based convergence proof (Theorem 1).  
- Notes practical limitation that κ must be known a priori; authors acknowledge this as future work.  
- Concern that O(κ²) stochastic-gradient dependence may be prohibitive for large κ and needs clearer discussion.  
- Shows EMGD can outperform Nesterov when κ ≤ n^(2/3) for finite-sum objectives, a practically relevant regime.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, with precise algorithm description and consistent notation.  
- Main clarity gap: computational cost per iteration not explicitly quantified (e.g., full vs. stochastic gradient cost).  
- Original and novel: mixes full and stochastic gradients to achieve condition-number-independent full-gradient access.  
- Distinguishes advantages over SAG/SDCA: works for constrained problems, O(d) storage, easier distribution, high-probability convergence.  
- Significance is moderate-to-high due to reduced expensive full-gradient evaluations, but practical impact limited for very ill-conditioned cases.  
- Strengths: novel algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, comprehensive related-work comparison, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: O(κ²) stochastic cost, requirement to know κ, unclear per-iteration computational cost, limited practical detail for large κ.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the O(κ²) issue, clarify computational cost, and discuss estimating κ in practice.  
- Final assessment: Quality—Good; Clarity—Good; Originality—High; Significance—Moderate to High.
