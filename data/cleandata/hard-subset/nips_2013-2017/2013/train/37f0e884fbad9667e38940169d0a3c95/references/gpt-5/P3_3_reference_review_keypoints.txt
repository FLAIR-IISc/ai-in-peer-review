- Proposes EMGD: alternates occasional full gradients at epoch anchors with per-iteration mixed gradients and shrinking-ball projections to minimize smooth, strongly convex F.
- Main theoretical claim: high-probability linear convergence using O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, where κ = L/λ.
- Mixed-gradient estimator is essentially the SVRG control-variate ∇F(w̄) + ∇f(w) − ∇f(w̄).
- Analysis uses standard tools (smoothness/strong convexity, martingale concentration, epoch averaging) and is technically sound under assumptions.
- The κ^2 stochastic-gradient complexity is loose compared to SVRG/SAGA/SDCA, which achieve O(κ log(1/ε)) or O((n+κ) log(1/ε)) in comparable models.
- A tighter analysis without shrinking-ball projections typically yields O(κ) dependence for the same estimator.
- The function-oracle model assumes multiple gradient queries on the same sampled f, which is stronger than the usual stochastic oracle and should be explicitly clarified.
- Projection onto W ∩ B(w̄k; Δk) may be harder than projection onto W alone; the paper assumes Step 8 is efficient without discussion of when this holds.
- The theorem requires knowing κ and an initial radius Δ1 to set T and η; guidance on estimating these or adaptive schemes is sketchy.
- Conceptual novelty is limited: control-variate mixing and epoch structure strongly overlap with SVRG; main differences are shrinking-ball projections and a high-probability analysis.
- Claimed advantages over SAG/SDCA (memory, constraints) are only partly valid given modern proximal/limited-memory variants.
- The number-of-full-gradients goal (O(log(1/ε))) is achieved and useful when full gradients are very costly relative to stochastic gradients.
- The κ^2 dependence likely makes EMGD unattractive in ill-conditioned problems where O(κ) methods dominate.
- No experiments are included, making practical wall-clock and tradeoff claims unsubstantiated.
- Presentation issues: typos, duplicate equation numbering, late introduction of assumptions (Δ1), and a missing comparison table.
- Paper should better position and cite SVRG/SAGA/MISO and clarify whether shrinking-ball projections yield real advantages or can be removed for tighter bounds.
- Suggested improvements: tighten analysis to O(κ), add empirical comparisons (AGD, SVRG/SAGA, SDCA) across κ and cost ratios, clarify oracle model and projection feasibility, and provide adaptive ways to set κ/Δ1.
- Overall assessment: moderate quality and clarity, limited originality, and limited practical significance as presented.
- Recommendation: Weak reject unless authors more thoroughly differentiate from SVRG/SAGA and provide empirical evidence or tighter κ dependence.
