- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining one full gradient per epoch with O(κ²) stochastic gradients per epoch to achieve linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradient evaluations independent of κ.

- Provides detailed theoretical analysis showing high-probability linear convergence (stronger than expectation-based results).

- Theoretical contribution: reduces full gradient evaluations from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)), independent of κ.

- Novel algorithm design: mixed gradient estimator g̃kt = ∇F(w̄k) + ∇fkt(wkt) - ∇fkt(w̄k) is well-motivated and justified.

- Stronger convergence guarantee: high-probability result (Theorem 1) is an advantage over SAG and SDCA.

- Practical advantages vs. SAG/SDCA: works for constrained/unconstrained problems, uses O(d) storage, and can exploit distributed full-gradient computation.

- Clear and careful theoretical proofs with detailed term bounds.

- Major omission: no comparison to SVRG (Johnson & Zhang 2013), a key baseline for variance-reduced methods.

- Algorithm assumes known condition number κ to set T, which is impractical and only vaguely addressed.

- Large constants (e.g., T ≥ 1152L²/λ² log(1/δ)) and O(κ²) stochastic gradients per epoch may be impractical for moderate κ.

- Lack of empirical validation: no experiments comparing EMGD to SVRG, SAG, Nesterov, etc.

- Limited comparison to more recent methods (e.g., SARAH/SPIDER) that are relevant to the problem.

- Insufficient practical implementation details for distributed full-gradient computation (communication costs, scalability).

- Suggest adding theoretical and empirical comparisons to SVRG to clarify trade-offs in full vs. stochastic gradient costs.

- Suggest providing a concrete method to estimate κ during optimization or showing robustness to κ overestimation.

- Recommend including experiments on standard ML problems (logistic/ridge regression) measuring convergence and runtime against SVRG, SAG, Nesterov.

- Suggest expanding related work to include SVRG, SARAH, SPIDER and discussing practical significance vs. these methods.

- Recommendation: accept with major revision — strong theory but must address SVRG omission and κ estimation and add empirical validation.

- Final assessment scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 7/10; overall important theoretical contribution needing stronger connection to state-of-the-art and practice.
