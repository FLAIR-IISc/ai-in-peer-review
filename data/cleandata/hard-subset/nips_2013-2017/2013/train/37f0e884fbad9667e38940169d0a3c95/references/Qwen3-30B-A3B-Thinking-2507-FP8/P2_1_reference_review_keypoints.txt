- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) to reduce expensive full gradient evaluations for smooth, strongly convex optimization.
- Claims linear convergence with only O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Shows EMGD outperforms full-gradient methods when κ ≤ n^(2/3) for finite-sum objectives.
- Provides rigorous convergence proof using standard convex optimization tools and martingale techniques.
- Induction proof of Theorem 1 is thorough and all intermediate steps are justified.
- Paper lacks any experimental results to validate theoretical claims.
- Requires knowing the condition number κ in advance, a major practical limitation.
- The O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradient requirement may be impractically large and its tightness is unexplored.
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and accessible to readers familiar with convex optimization.
- Algorithm is presented in pseudocode and analysis is detailed enough for expert verification.
- Novel contribution: making full-gradient access condition-number independent (O(log(1/ε))) is original and meaningful.
- Concept is similar to existing semi-stochastic methods, so novelty is somewhat incremental.
- EMGD offers advantages: constrained optimization support, lower storage needs, and high-probability convergence guarantees.
- Practical significance limited by κ knowledge requirement, high stochastic cost, and lack of empirical validation.
- Final recommendation: weak acceptance—strong theoretical contribution but needs more practical validation.
- Strengths: solid theoretical analysis, comprehensive comparisons, clear writing, and identified advantages over some existing methods.
- Weaknesses: dependence on known κ, no experiments, potentially high stochastic gradient cost, and limited novelty vs prior semi-stochastic work.
- Suggestions: discuss practical κ estimation methods, add experiments (even synthetic), better distinguish from prior semi-stochastic methods, and investigate improving the O(κ²) stochastic bound.
