- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) mixing one full gradient per epoch with stochastic gradients to reduce full gradient evaluations to O(log(1/ε)) while keeping linear convergence.
- Builds on epoch gradient descent and relates to variance-reduced methods (SAG, SDCA, SVRG), claiming condition-number-independent full gradient access.
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous analysis showing reduced full gradient evaluations with careful probability bounds and parameter dependencies.
- Novel algorithm design: mixed gradient estimator ∇F(w̄k)+∇fkt(wkt)−∇fkt(w̄k) with a shrinking domain constraint to ensure convergence.
- Practical considerations: works for constrained/unconstrained problems, uses O(d) storage (better than SAG/SDCA), and can be distributed to speed up full gradient computation.
- Major practical drawback: requires O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, which is significantly worse than SVRG’s O(κ log(1/ε)).
- Comparison weakness: claim of reduced full gradients is misleading because SVRG also uses one full gradient per epoch but with far fewer stochastic gradients.
- Requires knowledge of the condition number κ to set epoch length (T), which is impractical since κ is typically unknown; proposed estimation is vague.
- Theoretical limitations: restrictive probability condition (δ ≤ e^{-1/2}) and impractical tuning of δ across epochs for high-probability guarantees.
- Significance is limited: novelty is a minor variation on variance-reduction techniques and does not substantially improve over SVRG in practice.
- Recommendation: rejection — technically sound but not practically competitive due to O(κ²) stochastic cost and insufficient comparison to state-of-the-art.
- Suggested future directions: either achieve O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients with condition-number-independent full gradient access, or demonstrate practical gains for problem classes with small κ and large n.
