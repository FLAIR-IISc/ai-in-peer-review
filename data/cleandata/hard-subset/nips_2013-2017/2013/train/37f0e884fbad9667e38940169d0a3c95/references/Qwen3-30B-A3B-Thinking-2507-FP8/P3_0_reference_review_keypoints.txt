- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) combining full and stochastic gradients to get linear convergence with condition-number-independent full-gradient access.
- Claims EMGD reduces full gradient evaluations from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)) while requiring O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients and proves high-probability linear convergence.
- Strength: Solid theoretical contribution with rigorous, well-structured proofs using standard convex optimization tools.
- Strength: Clearly formulates the problem of reducing expensive full-gradient computations in ill-conditioned, large-scale settings.
- Strength: Provides thorough comparisons with related methods (Nesterov, SAG, SDCA) and useful complexity analysis (Table 2).
- Strength: Authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., need to know κ) and suggest future work directions.
- Strength: Discusses practical aspects like constrained problems and potential for distributed implementation.
- Weakness: No empirical validation — no experiments to confirm theoretical claims or practical efficiency.
- Weakness: Misleading claim that SAG and SDCA only have expectation-based convergence; high-probability guarantees exist for them under conditions.
- Weakness: Unclear practical trade-off between fewer full gradients and many more stochastic gradients; lacks guidance on when EMGD is advantageous.
- Weakness: Unjustified assumption that full gradient cost is exactly n times stochastic gradient cost; real cost ratios vary by problem.
- Weakness: Fails to analyze dependence on problem dimension d beyond mentioning O(d) storage.
- Weakness: Requires prior knowledge of κ, a significant practical limitation not resolved in the paper.
- Critical point: Concrete example (κ=1e4, ε=1e-6) shows EMGD can be far less efficient in wall-clock time despite fewer full gradients.
- Criticism: Insufficient evidence that the stated regime (κ ≤ n^(2/3)) yields practical improvements over standard methods.
- Comparison gap: Paper does not sufficiently distinguish itself from recent related NIPS work combining full and stochastic gradients.
- Recommendation: Reject due to lack of empirical validation and unproven practical significance despite sound theory.
- Suggestions: Add experiments on standard ML problems comparing EMGD to Nesterov, SAG, SDCA in convergence and wall-clock time.
- Suggestions: Justify or refine the full/stochastic gradient cost assumption and analyze dimension dependence.
- Suggestions: Correct claims about high-probability vs. expectation guarantees for competing methods and propose a practical κ-estimation method.
- Final assessment: Theory is sound and paper is clear, but originality and practical significance are limited; overall recommendation — reject.
