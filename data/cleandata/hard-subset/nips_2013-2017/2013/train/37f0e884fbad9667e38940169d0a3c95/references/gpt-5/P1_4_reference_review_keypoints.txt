- Paper proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD): interleaves infrequent full gradients with many stochastic steps using the SVRG-like mixed gradient and shrinking-ball constraints to get linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.

- Main claimed benefit: full-gradient evaluations independent of κ (O(log(1/ε))).

- Relation to prior work: method and estimator essentially match SVRG (Johnson & Zhang 2013), which already achieves O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.

- Major omission: paper does not cite or compare to SVRG or its proximal variants.

- Strength: clear goal of reducing costly full-gradient dependence and transparent proof structure with high-probability guarantees.

- Strength: explicit constants, low memory footprint claimed, and discussion of constrained and distributed settings.

- Weakness: stochastic-gradient complexity has worse κ dependence (κ^2 vs. κ) compared to SVRG/state of the art.

- Weakness: no empirical evaluation to support practical claims about memory, constraints, or distributed computation.

- Weakness: technical imprecision in assumptions—strong convexity is defined nonstandardly via gradient norms, then standard function-value inequalities are used inconsistently.

- Weakness: unusual shrinking-ball projection per epoch may be unnecessary and contributes to worse κ dependence.

- Clarity issues: notational inconsistencies, equation mislabels, typos, and missing practical guidance for solving the constrained subproblem.

- Originality limited: core estimator and epoch idea mirror SVRG; novelty is mainly shrinking-ball projection and high-probability analysis but with inferior complexity.

- Significance limited: claimed contribution is already achieved by SVRG; worse κ complexity and lack of experiments reduce impact.

- Suggestion: cite and explicitly compare to SVRG/Prox-SVRG, discussing storage, constraints, and expectation vs high-probability results.

- Suggestion: improve κ dependence—adopt SVRG-style analysis with constant step size and T = Θ(κ) to remove κ^2 factor and possibly drop shrinking-ball constraint.

- Suggestion: correct the strong-convexity definition and ensure consistent use of inequalities throughout proofs.

- Suggestion: add experiments on standard finite-sum problems (logistic/ridge regression) comparing EMGD to Nesterov, SAG, SDCA, SVRG/Prox-SVRG with wall-clock, passes, full-gradient counts, and memory.

- Suggestion: discuss practical implementation of projection onto W ∩ B(w̄k; Δk) and consider replacing ball constraint with proximal steps.

- Suggestion: address reliance on knowing κ—explore adaptive step sizes/inner-loop length (doubling) and extend high-probability analysis to adaptive schemes.

- Pros for acceptance: addresses variance-reduced stochastic methods, provides high-probability linear rate, and highlights practical aspects like low memory.

- Cons for acceptance: strong overlap with SVRG without citation, worse κ complexity, no experiments, and technical/clarity issues.

- Recommendation: Reject; work overlaps with SVRG, has inferior complexity and lacks empirical validation, but could become competitive after revising analysis, positioning, assumptions, and experiments.
