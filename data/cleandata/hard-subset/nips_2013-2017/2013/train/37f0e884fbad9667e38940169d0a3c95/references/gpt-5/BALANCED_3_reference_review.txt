Quality
- Positives: The analysis is careful under the stated oracle model and yields a high-probability linear rate. The result that the number of full gradients is O(log(1/őĶ)) is correct and practically appealing. The proof technique (martingale bounds on the estimator noise plus epoch averaging) is standard and sound.
- Concerns:
  - Missing core related work: SVRG and its proximal variants are directly relevant and essentially the same estimator/epoch pattern, but with stronger complexity guarantees (O(őļ) inner steps) and without shrinking domains. Not citing and comparing to SVRG is a major omission.

Significance
- Reducing the number of full gradients to O(log(1/őĶ)) is important, but this has already been achieved by SVRG and subsequent VR methods with stronger complexity bounds and broader applicability. Without a theoretical improvement (e.g., O(őļ) inner iterations, adaptive/no-őļ tuning, or stronger robustness) or compelling empirical evidence in regimes where EMGD excels, the contribution is limited.

Pros for acceptance
- Simple algorithm with a clean, high-probability linear convergence proof.
- Highlights the useful notion of decoupling full-gradient calls from őļ.
- Low memory footprint and a clear computational model.

Cons for acceptance
- Overlaps substantially with SVRG; key related work is missing.
- Theoretical guarantees are weaker (őļ^2 vs. őļ inner iterations).
- Practical issues: need for őļ and őĒ1, shrinking-ball projection, stronger oracle assumptions.
- No experiments to demonstrate advantages (e.g., constrained problems, distributed settings).

Suggestions for improvement
- Situate the work within the variance-reduction literature: compare and contrast carefully with SVRG (Johnson & Zhang, NIPS 2013), Prox-SVRG, SAGA (Defazio et al., NIPS 2014), and later refinements. Clarify what is new.
- Tighten the analysis to achieve O(őļ) inner iterations per epoch, leveraging the standard variance-reduction inequality bounding the estimator variance by suboptimality at the snapshot. This may obviate the shrinking-ball device.
- Replace W ‚ą© B(¬∑) projections with a proximal framework to handle general constraints/regularizers more practically; provide complexity for common sets (simplex, l1-ball).
- Remove the need for őļ via adaptive step sizes/epoch lengths (e.g., doubling tricks) and provide guidance for őĒ1 without oracle access to F(w*).
- Provide empirical studies comparing against AGD, SVRG/SAGA/SDCA on ill-conditioned and constrained problems, including wall-clock and gradient-call breakdowns.
- Fix typographical issues in Algorithm 1 and clarify the cost model (two component gradients per inner iteration).

Recommendation
Reject. The main idea and estimator coincide with established variance-reduction methods, and the theoretical and practical advantages are weaker than existing work. A revised version that positions the contribution correctly relative to SVRG/SAGA, improves the őļ-dependence, and includes empirical validation could be competitive.
