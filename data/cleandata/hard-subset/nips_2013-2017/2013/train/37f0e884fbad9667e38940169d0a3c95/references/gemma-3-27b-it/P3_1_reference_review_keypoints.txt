- Overall: Proposes EMGD to reduce condition-number dependence by mixing full and stochastic gradients; reviewer leans weak accept pending clarifications.  
- Summary: Uses epoch-based scheme with one full gradient per epoch plus many stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence in number of full-gradient evaluations.  
- Positioning: Correctly situates work among GD, AGD, SGD, SAG, SDCA and summarizes theoretical iteration complexities.  

- Strength — Novelty: Introduces a distinct epoch-based mix of full and stochastic gradients targeting condition-number independence of full-gradient calls.  
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: Convergence analysis is sound with linear convergence guarantees and a clear proof sketch.  
- Strength — Presentation: Algorithm and notation are clear and well-explained.  
- Strength — Practical focus: Directly addresses reducing costly full-gradient evaluations in large-scale optimization.  
- Strength — Limitations acknowledged: Authors note need to know κ and suggest future work to estimate it.

- Weakness — Practical impact unclear: O(κ^2) stochastic gradients per epoch may offset gains from fewer full-gradient evaluations for large κ.  
- Weakness — Limited comparisons: Table 2 lacks detailed constant-factor/overall-cost analysis and the assumption that a full gradient costs n times a stochastic one needs justification.  
- Weakness — Projection cost ignored: Projection onto W ∩ B(w̄k; ∆k) may be expensive in high dimensions but isn’t accounted for.  
- Weakness — No experiments: Lack of empirical validation weakens claims about practical applicability.  
- Weakness — Oracle assumption vague: The function oracle Of and its practical implementation are not discussed.

- Quality: Technically sound but undermined by missing empirical support.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized with consistent notation.  
- Originality: Novel combination of epoch descent with mixed gradients.  
- Significance: Potentially important for reducing full-gradient calls, but practical benefit is not demonstrated.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach, theoretical guarantees, tackles a relevant practical problem, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: No empirical validation, unclear practical significance, insufficient comparison to SDCA/SAG, and unaccounted costs of stochastic gradients and projection.

- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on revisions.  
- Requested revision 1: Add empirical results on benchmark datasets comparing EMGD to SDCA, SAG, etc.  
- Requested revision 2: Provide detailed computational cost analysis including stochastic gradient and projection costs and the role of κ.  
- Requested revision 3: Give a more nuanced, constant-factor-aware comparison to SDCA and SAG and clarify regimes where EMGD outperforms them.  
- Requested revision 4: Clarify how the function oracle Of would be implemented in practice.
