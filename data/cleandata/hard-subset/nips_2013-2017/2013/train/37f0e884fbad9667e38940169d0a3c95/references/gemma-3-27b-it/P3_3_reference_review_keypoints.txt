- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) to interleave full and stochastic gradients, aiming to reduce full gradient evaluations and achieve condition-number independent iteration complexity.  
- Novel epoch-based hybrid approach that is potentially impactful for large-scale, ill-conditioned problems.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound, claiming O(log(1/ε)) full gradient complexity and using Hoeffding-Azuma for stochastic bounds.  
- Clear motivation and well-positioned relative to epoch methods, SAG, and SDCA.  
- Algorithm 1 omits crucial implementation details—particularly how the projection/"argmin" in line 8 is solved and its computational cost.  
- Step size choice η = 1/[L√T] is presented without justification and may be suboptimal.  
- Comparisons to SAG/SDCA are incomplete and selective; lacks an apples-to-apples runtime, per-iteration cost, and memory comparison across a range of κ.  
- Table 2 is selective and should include broader κ scenarios to be persuasive.  
- Practical issues under-discussed: robustness to inaccurate κ estimates, assumptions on the function oracle, and distributed computing details.  
- Strong convexity and smoothness assumptions restrict applicability and need discussion or possible relaxations.  
- Notation is inconsistent and sometimes unclear (e.g., multiple uses of w with different indices/superscripts).  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on substantial revisions addressing implementation, comprehensive comparisons, practical considerations, and clearer presentation.
