- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes one full gradient per epoch with O(κ²) stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence.
- Claims full gradient evaluations drop from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)) and stochastic gradients are O(κ² log(1/ε)).
- Theorem 1 provides rigorous proof of linear convergence and condition-number-independent access to full gradients.
- Proof is technically sound and well-structured but somewhat complex and potentially hard to follow for some readers.
- Main theoretical weakness: EMGD may not be computationally optimal when κ > n^(1/2), and it assumes κ is known a priori.
- Major practical weakness: no empirical validation provided, making real-world performance and constant factors unclear.
- Clarity is good overall—well-organized, clear algorithm description, and helpful comparisons (Table 2).
- Paper lacks intuitive explanation for why the mixed-gradient approach yields O(κ²) stochastic gradients and discussion of constant factors.
- Novelty is high: condition-number-independent full gradient access with linear convergence is a meaningful advancement over related semi-stochastic methods.
- Significant potential impact for large-scale and constrained problems due to reduced storage and fewer full gradients, but practical significance limited by missing experiments and κ estimation issue.
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions to include empirical results and address practical concerns (especially κ estimation and experiments).
