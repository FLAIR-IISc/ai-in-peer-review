## Review of "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients"

This paper introduces Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), an optimization algorithm designed to reduce the number of full gradient evaluations required for strongly convex and smooth optimization problems, achieving a convergence rate independent of the condition number. The algorithm interleaves full gradient and stochastic gradient evaluations within epochs, aiming to trade off iteration count with per-iteration computational cost. 

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a practical concern in large-scale optimization where full gradient computations are expensive. The authors correctly identify the trade-off between iteration complexity and per-iteration cost when choosing between full and stochastic gradients.  The presented approach builds upon epoch gradient descent and aims to improve its efficiency by incorporating stochastic gradients. The paper clearly defines the problem, related work, the proposed algorithm, its theoretical convergence guarantees, and provides a comparison with existing methods. 

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Problem Formulation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of high computational cost associated with full gradient evaluations in ill-conditioned problems.
* **Novelty:** The proposed EMGD algorithm represents a novel combination of full and stochastic gradient approaches. The theoretical analysis demonstrating condition number independence in the number of full gradient evaluations is a noteworthy contribution.
* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a formal convergence analysis for the proposed algorithm, including a theorem specifying the convergence rate and relating it to the condition number.
* **Comparison to Related Work:** The paper offers a detailed comparison of EMGD with relevant algorithms like Nesterov's accelerated gradient descent, SAG, and SDCA, highlighting the trade-offs in terms of computational complexity.
* **Well-Organized and Clearly Written:** The paper is generally well-structured and the technical concepts are explained clearly. Algorithm 1 is presented in a standard, easy-to-follow format.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Practical Considerations:** The paper lacks discussion regarding practical challenges in implementing EMGD.  The need to know the condition number *a priori* is a significant limitation not fully addressed beyond suggesting estimation techniques in the conclusion. Estimating the condition number itself can be computationally expensive, potentially offsetting the benefits of reduced full gradient evaluations.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The paper is purely theoretical. The absence of experimental results significantly weakens the evaluation of the algorithm's practical performance.  It is unclear how EMGD performs in real-world scenarios compared to existing methods.
* **Assumptions:** The algorithm relies on the availability of both full gradients (via `Og`) and a function oracle (`Of`) providing stochastic gradients.  The practical implications of this dual oracle requirement aren't fully discussed.
* **Comparison Table Caveats:** The comparison with SAG and SDCA in Table 2 relies on the assumption that full gradient evaluation is 'n' times more expensive than stochastic gradient evaluation. This assumption might not hold true in all scenarios and could bias the comparison.
* **Proof Details:** While the proof outline is provided, details are limited, and readability suffers.  More detailed explanations would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel approach to address a significant challenge in large-scale optimization.
* The theoretical analysis provides a strong foundation for the algorithm’s claimed benefits.
* The paper demonstrates a clear understanding of the relevant literature and positions EMGD within the existing landscape of optimization algorithms.
* The focus on condition number independence is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of experimental validation prevents a comprehensive assessment of the algorithm’s practical performance.
* The requirement of knowing the condition number *a priori* limits the algorithm’s applicability.
* The limited discussion of practical implementation challenges and the dependence on a potentially unrealistic oracle setup raise concerns about real-world viability.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a theoretically sound and novel algorithm for reducing the computational cost of full gradient evaluations in convex optimization. The claim of condition number independence is a significant contribution. However, the absence of empirical validation and the practical limitations regarding condition number estimation are substantial drawbacks. 

**Recommendation:**

**Weak Reject.**  While the theoretical contribution is valuable, the lack of empirical evaluation and the practical limitations currently outweigh the benefits.  The authors should address these concerns by conducting experiments on real-world datasets and providing a more thorough discussion of the algorithm’s practical implementation and sensitivity to parameter choices before resubmission. A more detailed discussion on robust condition number estimation techniques would also be valuable.
