- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes one full gradient per epoch with multiple stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence.  
- Claims full gradient evaluations reduce from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)), with O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Core idea: use a "mixed gradient" and a shrinking domain constraint to avoid condition-number-dependent full gradient access.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, using standard strong-convexity/smoothness techniques and martingale concentration (Hoeffding-Azuma).  
- Assumptions (L-smoothness, λ-strong convexity) are standard and well-motivated.  
- No empirical validation is provided to support practical advantages.  
- Practical limitation: algorithm requires knowing the condition number κ in advance.  
- Computational advantage holds only when κ ≤ n^(2/3), limiting real-world applicability.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and notation/algorithm description are clear.  
- Minor clarity issue: insufficient justification for shrinking the domain by √2 each epoch.  
- Originality: novel combination of full and stochastic gradients and distinct from SAG/SDCA in storage and constraints.  
- Significance: meaningful theoretical advance for large-scale ML but tempered by practical constraints and lack of experiments.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, clear comparisons, novelty, and good exposition.  
- Weaknesses: no experiments, need to know κ, restrictive κ-dependent advantage, missing discussion of constant factors.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept — strong theoretical contribution but needs empirical validation and treatment of κ estimation.
