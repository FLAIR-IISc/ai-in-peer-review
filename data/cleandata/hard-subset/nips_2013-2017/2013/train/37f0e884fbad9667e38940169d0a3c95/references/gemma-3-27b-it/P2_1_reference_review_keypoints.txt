- Proposed EMGD mixes full and stochastic gradients within epochs to achieve linear convergence while reducing full-gradient calls to O(log(1/ε)) independent of condition number κ.
- Addresses a relevant bottleneck in large-scale optimization by reducing dependence on κ for full-gradient evaluations.
- Introduces a novel epoch-based mixed-gradient approach that leverages strengths of both full and stochastic gradients.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis showing linear convergence and reduced full-gradient evaluations.
- Compares EMGD to existing methods (full-gradient, stochastic, SAG, SDCA) with a helpful summary table.
- Discusses practical considerations, including condition-number estimation and applicability to regularized ML problems.
- Number of stochastic gradients scales as O(κ² log(1/ε)), which may negate benefits if stochastic gradients are costly.
- Algorithm requires careful tuning of multiple parameters (step size, domain size, epoch length), with little practical guidance.
- Lacks empirical validation, so practical performance and constant factors are untested.
- Some technical derivations and notation are dense; connections (e.g., Lemma 2 to BT bounds) need clearer exposition.
- Related-work discussion could be more comprehensive, especially on recent variance-reduction advances.
- Quality rated 8/10 for strong theory but penalized for missing experiments.
- Clarity rated 7/10 due to dense notation and insufficient explanatory detail.
- Originality rated 8/10 for the mixed-epoch gradient idea building on prior work.
- Significance rated 7/10 because stochastic-gradient dependence limits overall impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, tackles an important problem, and is well-motivated.
- Arguments against acceptance: no experiments, scalability limited by O(κ²) stochastic cost, difficult parameter tuning, and unclear technical details.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept pending significant revisions—add experiments, analyze full vs. stochastic trade-offs, clarify proofs, and provide tuning guidance.
