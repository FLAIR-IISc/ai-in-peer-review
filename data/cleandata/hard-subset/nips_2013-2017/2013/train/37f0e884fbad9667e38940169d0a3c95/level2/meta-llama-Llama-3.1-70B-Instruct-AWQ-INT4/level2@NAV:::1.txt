**Review of "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients"**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), which combines full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The authors demonstrate that EMGD can reduce the number of full gradients required from O(√κ log 1/ǫ) to O(log 1/ǫ), independent of the condition number κ.

**Claims**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. EMGD achieves linear convergence with a reduced number of full gradients.
2. The algorithm is more efficient than existing methods that rely solely on full or stochastic gradients.
3. EMGD can be applied to both constrained and unconstrained optimization problems.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of EMGD, including a proof of linear convergence and a bound on the number of full gradients required. They also compare the computational complexity of EMGD with existing methods, demonstrating its efficiency.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful, as they address a common problem in optimization: reducing the computational cost of full gradients. The algorithm's ability to combine full and stochastic gradients makes it a promising approach for large-scale optimization problems.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the field, citing relevant literature and building upon existing work. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the assumptions and limitations of their approach.

**Novelty**

The paper presents a novel algorithm that combines full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence. While the idea of using stochastic gradients to reduce computational cost is not new, the specific approach taken by the authors is innovative and contributes to the field.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including a clear description of the algorithm and its theoretical analysis. However, some minor clarifications on the implementation of the algorithm would be helpful.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge that the algorithm requires knowledge of the condition number κ, which may be difficult to estimate in practice. They also note that the algorithm may not be the most efficient approach in all cases, particularly when the condition number is large.

**Constructive Criticism**

To further improve the paper, the authors could consider providing more experimental results to demonstrate the practical effectiveness of EMGD. Additionally, they could explore ways to estimate the condition number κ in practice, making the algorithm more widely applicable.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and innovative algorithm that addresses a common problem in optimization.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough and well-supported.
* The algorithm has practical applications in large-scale optimization problems.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The algorithm requires knowledge of the condition number κ, which may be difficult to estimate in practice.
* The paper could benefit from more experimental results to demonstrate the practical effectiveness of EMGD.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization. With some minor revisions to address the limitations and provide more experimental results, the paper is ready for acceptance.
