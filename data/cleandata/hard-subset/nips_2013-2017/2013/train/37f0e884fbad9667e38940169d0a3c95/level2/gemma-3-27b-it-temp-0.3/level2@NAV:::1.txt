## Review of "Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients"

This paper addresses the challenge of ill-conditioning in convex optimization, specifically aiming to reduce the computational cost associated with evaluating full gradients. The authors propose a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), which leverages both full and stochastic gradients to achieve a convergence rate independent of the condition number κ. The core idea of mixing gradients within epochs, inspired by epoch gradient descent, is a promising approach.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the limitations of existing methods when dealing with ill-conditioned problems. The theoretical analysis demonstrates that EMGD achieves O(log 1/ǫ) full gradients and O(κ² log 1/ǫ) stochastic gradients, a significant improvement over the standard O(√κ log 1/ǫ) full gradient complexity. The authors appropriately position their work within the existing literature, referencing relevant papers on deterministic, stochastic, and semi-stochastic optimization methods (e.g., Nesterov’s accelerated gradient descent, SAG, SDCA). The comparisons to existing algorithms in Table 2 are helpful in contextualizing the proposed method's performance.

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses. While the theoretical results are strong, the practical implications and potential challenges of implementing EMGD are not thoroughly discussed. For example, the choice of the step size η and the initial domain size ∆₁ are crucial for performance, and more guidance on selecting these parameters in practice would be beneficial. The analysis relies on several assumptions (smoothness, strong convexity) which are standard, but a discussion of robustness to violations of these assumptions would strengthen the work. The comparison to other algorithms could be more comprehensive, particularly considering the overhead associated with switching between full and stochastic gradients.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to reducing the dependence on the condition number in gradient-based optimization.
*   Strong theoretical guarantees demonstrating linear convergence and reduced full gradient complexity.
*   Clear presentation and well-defined problem statement.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of practical implementation details and parameter tuning.
*   Lack of empirical evaluation to validate the theoretical results.
*   Comparisons to other algorithms could be more nuanced.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a theoretically sound and potentially impactful contribution to the field of convex optimization. While the lack of empirical validation and detailed implementation guidance are shortcomings, the novelty and theoretical strength of the proposed algorithm warrant consideration. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting the authors to address the practical considerations and potentially include preliminary experimental results.
