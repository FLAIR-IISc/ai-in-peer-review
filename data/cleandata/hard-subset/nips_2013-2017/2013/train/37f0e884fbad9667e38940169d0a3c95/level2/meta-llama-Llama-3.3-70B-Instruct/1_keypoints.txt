- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes full and stochastic gradients to reduce full-gradient calls from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)).
- Targets smooth, strongly convex optimization problems and claims improved gradient-efficiency.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and a proof of convergence rate.
- Uses martingale difference sequence and Hoeffding-Azuma inequality in the convergence proof.
- Algorithm description is clear, including the mixed gradient step and use of stochastic gradients.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and motivated by reducing full-gradient usage.
- Compares EMGD with Nesterov, SAG, and SDCA, showing computational advantages under certain conditions.
- Shows EMGD is cheaper than full-gradient methods when κ ≤ n^{2/3} (n = number of component functions).
- Strength: novel algorithm that meaningfully reduces full-gradient requirements.
- Strength: thorough and rigorous theoretical treatment.
- Strength: direct comparison with existing optimization algorithms.
- Weakness: requires prior knowledge of the condition number κ, which may be unavailable in practice.
- Weakness: lacks experimental results demonstrating practical effectiveness.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; add experiments and discuss limitations (notably the κ requirement).
