- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) to reduce computational costs in ill-conditioned convex optimization by mixing full and stochastic gradients.
- Claims convergence using only O(log(1/ε)) full gradients, removing dependence on condition number κ compared to standard methods.
- Well-motivated problem statement that clearly outlines limitations of existing approaches.
- Positions the work effectively within deterministic, stochastic, and semi-stochastic optimization literature.
- Provides a convergence theorem and theoretical analysis showing improved computational complexity.
- Compares EMGD to algorithms like SAG and SDCA and describes scenarios where EMGD is favorable.
- Presentation lacks clarity—Algorithm 1 is described but the intuition for the mixed gradient step (line 7) is insufficient.
- The analysis is dense and relies heavily on existing lemmas without clear explanation of their specific applicability here.
- Proof intuition is under-explained; a more accessible explanation would improve understanding.
- Discussion of future work and practical challenges (e.g., estimating κ) is brief and needs expansion.
- Assumes full gradients cost n times stochastic gradients without justifying this assumption.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid theoretical results, and clear motivation.
- Arguments against acceptance: dense/inaccessible analysis, insufficient intuition for core components, limited practical discussion.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify analysis, expand practical implications, and justify cost assumptions.
