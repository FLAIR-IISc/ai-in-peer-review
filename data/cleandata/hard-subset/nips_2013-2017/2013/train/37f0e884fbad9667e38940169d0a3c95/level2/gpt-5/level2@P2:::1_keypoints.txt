- Proposal: EMGD — epoch-based method using a full gradient at the epoch center plus stochastic gradient differences; proves linear convergence with m full gradients and mT stochastic gradients where T = O(κ^2 log(1/δ)), yielding O(log(1/ε)) full and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Mixed-gradient estimator is identical to the variance-reduced estimator used by SVRG (Johnson & Zhang, NIPS 2013), but SVRG is not cited or compared.
- Paper emphasizes a more general stochastic oracle and constrained domains, but requiring exact full gradients limits practicality outside the finite-sum setting and should be clarified.
- Analysis is careful (smoothness, strong convexity, martingale concentration) and uses shrinking-ball projections to control variance; epoch induction is sound under assumptions.
- Major inconsistency: abstract claims O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients while Theorem 1 and proof imply O(κ^2 log(1/ε)); this must be corrected.
- The κ^2 dependence is suboptimal compared to related methods (e.g., SVRG achieves O(κ) inner iterations), suggesting the analysis may be loose and shrinking-domain may be unnecessary.
- No empirical results are provided to validate practical behavior, sensitivity to κ, or robustness/overhead of the projection step.
- Clarity issues: typos, notational glitches, repeated equation labels, awkward constraints (e.g., δ ≤ e^{-1/2}), and insufficient motivation/cost discussion for the shrinking-ball projection.
- Originality is limited: main novelty is the oracle model and high-probability/shrinking-domain analysis, but overlap with SVRG significantly weakens the contribution.
- Significance is limited by suboptimal κ dependence and missing experiments, although constrained optimization handling and O(d) memory are positives.
- Pros for acceptance: simple algorithm, high-probability linear convergence proof, works with constrained domains, and reduces full-gradient calls to O(log(1/ε)).
- Cons against acceptance: strong overlap with SVRG and missing citation/comparison, suboptimal κ^2 complexity, abstract/theorem mismatch, presentation flaws, and no experiments.
- Recommended fixes: cite and compare to SVRG and related work; tighten analysis toward O(κ) inner iterations (potentially remove shrinking domain); correct abstract/notation; clarify full-gradient oracle practicality; add empirical comparisons and adaptive strategies for κ.
- Overall recommendation: Reject in current form due to overlap with existing variance-reduction methods, suboptimal complexity, and missing comparisons/experiments; could become acceptable after novelty clarification, improved theory, and empirical validation.
