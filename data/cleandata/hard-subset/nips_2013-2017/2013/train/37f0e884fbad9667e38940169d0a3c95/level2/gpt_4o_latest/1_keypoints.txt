- Main claim: EMGD reduces full gradient evaluations from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)) by mixing full and stochastic gradients across epochs.  
- Theoretical result: EMGD achieves O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients to reach ε-optimality.  
- Strength — Novelty: Proposes a novel hybrid full/stochastic gradient approach addressing ill-conditioned optimization.  
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: Provides detailed convergence proofs and complexity bounds, with comparisons to SAG and SDCA.  
- Strength — Practical applicability: Applicable to constrained/unconstrained problems, requires less storage, and suits distributed settings.  
- Strength — Clarity of contributions: Contributions and computational reductions are clearly stated.  
- Weakness — Known κ required: Algorithm assumes the condition number κ is known, limiting practicality.  
- Weakness — Complexity for large κ: For κ > n^{2/3}, EMGD can be less efficient than SAG and SDCA.  
- Weakness — Empirical validation missing: No experiments are provided to support the theoretical claims.  
- Weakness — Presentation density: Paper is dense and needs clearer, more intuitive explanations of algorithm steps.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add experiments and discuss κ estimation strategies.  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound method with potential impact and clear advantages over some existing methods.  
- Argument against acceptance: Lacks empirical validation, depends on known κ, and can be computationally inferior in some regimes.  
- Overall: Valuable theoretical contribution but would be stronger with experiments and practical κ-handling.
