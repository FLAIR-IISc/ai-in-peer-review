- Addresses expensive full gradient computations for smooth, strongly convex (ill-conditioned) problems by proposing Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) using both full and stochastic gradients.
- Core claim: EMGD finds an ε-optimal solution with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients (independent of κ) and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Novelty/significance: Decouples full-gradient count from condition number κ, trading full gradients for cheaper stochastic steps — a notable conceptual contribution.
- Theoretical quality: Analysis appears sound with high-probability linear convergence, clear assumptions, and a detailed proof sketch.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, with clear motivation, clean algorithm presentation, and upfront statement of main result.
- Lack of experiments: No empirical validation makes practical performance and comparisons to Nesterov, SAG, SDCA unclear.
- Practicality issue: Requires knowing κ to set epoch length T; estimation strategies are only briefly mentioned and not addressed in the main text.
- Missing comparison to SVRG: Relationship and differences to variance-reduction methods like SVRG are not sufficiently discussed.
- Summary recommendation: Theoretical contribution is valuable but incomplete for a top-tier venue without experiments and stronger practical/related-work treatment.
