- Studies smooth λ-strongly convex optimization with both full and stochastic gradients, proposing Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that uses one full gradient per epoch and mixed stochastic updates.  
- Algorithm uses control-variate estimator g̃ = ∇F(w̄k) + ∇f(w) − ∇f(w̄k) (identical to SVRG estimator) and projects onto a shrinking ball around the snapshot.  
- Claims linear convergence with high probability: m full gradients and mT stochastic gradients give geometric decrease, with T = Ω(κ^2 log(1/δ)); overall O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Abstract/body inconsistency: abstract states O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients while theorem and analysis indicate O(κ^2 log(1/ε)); needs reconciliation and corrections.  
- The κ^2 dependence is suboptimal compared to variance-reduced finite-sum methods (SVRG/SAG/SDCA) which obtain O((n+κ) log(1/ε)) or O(κ log(1/ε)) stochastic complexity; positioning relative to those is required.  
- Key novelty: shrinking projection/trust-region and high-probability analysis under a general stochastic oracle (not only finite-sum); but the estimator itself matches SVRG.  
- Strength: clear goal to reduce expensive full gradients while retaining linear rates, with a simple epoch structure and explicit constants in analysis.  
- Strength: works for constrained domains, uses O(d) memory, requires no duality, and discusses distributed computation considerations.  
- Main concern: κ^2 dependence — unclear if intrinsic to shrinking-ball approach or an artifact of the analysis; tighten proof or justify necessity.  
- Shrinking ball constraint helps bound variance but introduces extra projections and may be impractical for some constraint sets; consider removing or adapting it.  
- Paper lacks direct comparison to SVRG-style methods (theoretically and empirically), which is important given identical estimator in finite-sum cases.  
- Practicality issue: method requires knowledge of κ to set T; more concrete adaptive strategies (doubling/linesearch) are needed.  
- Minor clarity/typo issues: misplaced footnote about SDCA, inconsistent complexity table entries, and separation of assumptions vs remarks need fixing.  
- Analysis quality: technically sound, clear inductive structure and concentration bounds, with explicit constants; main technical worry is the κ^2 factor.  
- Clarity: generally well organized and readable; add intuition for how control variate plus shrinking ball leads to κ^2 to aid readers.  
- Originality: mixing snapshot full gradient with per-iteration corrections is not new (related to SVRG); novelty lies in shrinking projection and general/high-probability oracle treatment.  
- Significance: reducing full gradients to O(log(1/ε)) is valuable for expensive full-gradient settings, but overall impact depends on improving or justifying the κ^2 stochastic cost.  
- Suggested improvements: correct κ vs κ^2 claims, compare and contrast with SVRG/SAG/SDCA, investigate tightening analysis to O(κ), propose adaptive epoch lengths for unknown κ, and add experiments on standard problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear objective, simple algorithm, high-probability linear convergence, constrained applicability, and solid analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: suboptimal κ^2 dependence, inconsistency in complexity statements, insufficient differentiation/comparison with variance-reduction literature, and practicality concerns from shrinking projections and required κ knowledge.
