- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) alternating full and stochastic gradients to reduce dependence on condition number in strongly convex optimization.  
- Claims O(log(1/ε)) full-gradient complexity independent of condition number, supported by theoretical analysis.  
- Algorithm is precisely described and the convergence theorem is clearly stated.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and positions EMGD within existing optimization methods.  
- Comparative analysis with Nesterov, SAG, SDCA highlights trade-offs in cost and assumptions.  
- Authors honestly acknowledge needing an estimate of the condition number as a practical limitation.  
- Novelty: the epoch-based mix of full and stochastic gradients is a new approach for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Theoretical guarantees: provides convergence results showing logarithmic full-gradient dependence.  
- Clear presentation: writing and logical organization make the algorithm and analysis understandable.  
- Comparative analysis is valuable for contextualizing advantages and disadvantages.  
- Weakness — constant factors: hidden κ² dependence in stochastic-gradient count may be large and is not empirically examined.  
- Weakness — parameter selection: limited guidance on choosing epoch size T and initial domain Δ₁; T’s constraint appears strong.  
- Weakness — proof complexity: only a sketch is given; a more complete/rigorous proof would strengthen the paper.  
- Weakness — limited empirical evaluation: no experiments are provided to validate practical performance.  
- Weakness — incomplete connection to prior epoch-based methods; relationship to epoch gradient descent is not fully explored.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees addressing a relevant optimization problem.  
- Argument for acceptance: clear, seemingly sound technical analysis and useful comparisons to prior work.  
- Argument against acceptance: lack of empirical validation makes practical impact uncertain.  
- Argument against acceptance: large constant factors and strict parameter constraints may limit applicability.  
- Argument against acceptance: theoretical analysis could be strengthened with more detailed proofs.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution meriting acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: add small-scale empirical evaluation, analyze sensitivity to κ and mitigate κ² term, give guidance on T and Δ₁ selection, expand discussion of relation to epoch methods, and provide a more detailed proof sketch.
