This paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm called Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that combines the strengths of both full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The main idea is to divide the optimization process into a sequence of epochs, where each epoch consists of a mixed gradient descent step that utilizes both the full and stochastic gradients.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The EMGD algorithm is shown to achieve an optimal iteration complexity of O(log 1/ǫ) full gradients and O(κ^2 log 1/ǫ) stochastic gradients, which is a significant improvement over existing algorithms that rely solely on full or stochastic gradients.

The authors also provide a thorough comparison of EMGD with existing algorithms, including Nesterov's accelerated gradient descent, stochastic average gradient (SAG), and stochastic dual coordinate ascent (SDCA). The comparison highlights the advantages of EMGD in terms of its ability to handle both constrained and unconstrained optimization problems, its lower storage requirements, and its ability to achieve linear convergence with high probability.

The theoretical analysis of EMGD is based on a martingale difference sequence and the Hoeffding-Azuma inequality, which provides a rigorous bound on the convergence rate of the algorithm. The authors also provide a detailed proof of the convergence rate, which is helpful in understanding the underlying mathematics.

Overall, the paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The EMGD algorithm is a significant contribution to the field of optimization, and its ability to combine the strengths of both full and stochastic gradients makes it a promising approach for solving large-scale optimization problems.
