## Review of “Linear Convergence with Condition Number Independent Access of Full Gradients”

This paper proposes a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), aiming to reduce the computational cost associated with ill-conditioned convex optimization problems. The core idea is to leverage both full and stochastic gradients, achieving a convergence rate independent of the condition number κ, specifically O(log 1/ǫ) full gradients, which is a significant improvement over the standard O(√κ log 1/ǫ) for full gradient methods.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the limitations of existing approaches and highlighting the practical importance of reducing the dependence on full gradient evaluations. The authors effectively position their work within the context of related literature, referencing key papers in deterministic, stochastic, and semi-stochastic optimization. The theoretical analysis appears sound, providing a convergence theorem and demonstrating the proposed improvement in computational complexity.  The comparison to other algorithms like SAG and SDCA is a strength, outlining the trade-offs and specific scenarios where EMGD performs favorably. 

However, the presentation lacks some clarity. While the algorithm itself is described (Algorithm 1), the intuition behind the "mixed gradient descent" step (line 7) could be more thoroughly explained.  The analysis, while present, is dense and relies heavily on existing lemmas without fully elucidating *why* those lemmas are applicable in this specific context.  A more intuitive explanation of the proof would significantly enhance understanding.  Furthermore, the discussion of future work is brief – expanding on the practical challenges of estimating the condition number κ would add considerable value. The paper also states the comparison with other algorithms assumes full gradients are n times more expensive than stochastic gradients, but doesn't provide justification for that assumption. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   Novel approach to addressing the challenge of ill-conditioning in optimization.
*   Solid theoretical analysis demonstrating the desired convergence properties.
*   Clear motivation and positioning within the relevant literature.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
*   Dense and potentially inaccessible analysis.
*   Lack of intuitive explanation for the core algorithmic components.
*   Limited discussion of practical considerations.

**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of optimization. Despite some shortcomings in clarity and detail, the core idea and theoretical results are compelling. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically focusing on improving the clarity of the analysis and providing a more in-depth discussion of the practical implications and limitations of the proposed algorithm. The authors should also justify the assumption about the cost difference between full and stochastic gradient evaluations.
