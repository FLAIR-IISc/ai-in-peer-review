- Proposes EMGD, an algorithm combining full and stochastic gradients to reduce full-gradient computations.  
- Claims linear convergence for smooth, strongly convex optimization.  
- Complexity: O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients to reach ε-optimality.  
- Provides comparisons of computational complexity with Nesterov, SAG, and SDCA.  
- Strength: Novel approach targeting reduction of full gradients for large datasets.  
- Strength: Thorough, well-presented convergence analysis.  
- Strength: Informative comparisons clarifying EMGD’s advantages and disadvantages.  
- Weakness: Assumes condition number κ is known a priori, which may be impractical.  
- Weakness: Dense notation and mathematical derivations hinder accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness: Insufficient experimental results to demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing κ estimation and other noted weaknesses.
