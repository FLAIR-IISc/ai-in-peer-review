This paper proposes a novel algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), to reduce the dependence on the condition number in strongly convex optimization by leveraging both full and stochastic gradients. The core idea is to alternate between using a full gradient and a batch of stochastic gradients within each epoch, aiming for a logarithmic dependence on 1/ε in the number of full gradients required. The authors provide theoretical analysis demonstrating this result.

The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the motivation and contribution. The related work section appropriately positions EMGD within the context of existing optimization algorithms, contrasting it with full-gradient, stochastic-gradient, and semi-stochastic methods. The algorithm itself is described precisely, and the theorem stating the convergence rate is clearly stated. The comparisons to other algorithms like SAG and SDCA are valuable, highlighting the trade-offs in terms of computational cost and assumptions. The acknowledgment of limitations, specifically needing to estimate the condition number, demonstrates honesty and awareness of potential practical challenges.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of full and stochastic gradients in a specific epoch-based manner is a novel approach. It addresses a practical concern in ill-conditioned problems where full gradient evaluation is expensive.
* **Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper provides a theoretical convergence rate, demonstrating that EMGD achieves O(log 1/ε) full gradients, independent of the condition number. This is a significant contribution.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The algorithm is explained clearly, and the theoretical analysis is presented in a comprehensible manner.
* **Comparative Analysis:** The comparison with existing algorithms (Nesterov, SAG, SDCA) is valuable, providing context and demonstrating the advantages and disadvantages of EMGD.
* **Honest Discussion of Limitations:** Acknowledging the need for condition number estimation is crucial and shows a realistic assessment of the algorithm's applicability.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Constant Factors:** While the asymptotic complexity is appealing, the constant factors hidden within the O(log 1/ε) notation, particularly the κ² term in the number of stochastic gradients, could be significant in practice. The paper does not discuss the sensitivity of performance to κ, or potential strategies to mitigate this dependence.
* **Practical Considerations of Epoch Size:**  The optimal choice of epoch size (T) and the initial domain size (Δ₁) could be crucial for performance. The paper provides limited guidance on how to select these parameters in a practical setting.  The constraint on T (T ≥ 1152L²/λ² ln δ) seems quite strong and potentially limiting.
* **Proof Complexity:** The proof sketch is mentioned, but a complete and rigorous proof would significantly strengthen the paper. While a full proof isn’t always necessary for NIPS, more detail on the critical steps would be beneficial.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The paper focuses exclusively on theoretical analysis.  Even a small-scale empirical evaluation demonstrating the performance of EMGD on a few benchmark problems would significantly increase confidence in its practical viability.
* **Connection to Existing Methods:** The paper mentions epoch gradient descent [9], but doesn’t fully explore the connection. Is EMGD a generalization? A specific instance?  Further discussion could reveal additional insights.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees that addresses a relevant problem in optimization.
* The technical analysis appears sound, and the presentation is clear and concise.
* The paper contributes to the ongoing research on efficient optimization methods for machine learning and related applications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper lacks empirical validation, making it difficult to assess the practical performance of the algorithm.
* The constant factors and the strong constraints on parameters may limit the algorithm’s applicability in practice.
* The theoretical analysis, while present, could be strengthened by a more detailed proof sketch.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid theoretical contribution with the potential to be highly valuable. The novelty of the approach and the theoretical guarantees are compelling. However, the lack of empirical evaluation and the potential practical limitations raise some concerns. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The authors should be encouraged to:

1.  Include a small-scale empirical evaluation demonstrating the performance of EMGD on standard benchmark problems.
2.  Discuss the sensitivity of the algorithm's performance to the condition number κ, and explore potential strategies to mitigate the κ² term in the complexity of stochastic gradients.
3.  Provide more guidance on selecting appropriate values for the epoch size (T) and initial domain size (Δ₁).
4.  Expand the discussion of the relationship between EMGD and existing epoch-based methods.
5. Provide a more detailed sketch of the proof of Theorem 1, or at least highlight the key technical challenges.
