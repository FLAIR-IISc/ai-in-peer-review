This paper proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), which utilizes both full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems. The algorithm is designed to reduce the number of full gradients required, making it more computationally efficient for large data sets.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate, showing that EMGD can achieve an ǫ-optimal solution by computing O(log 1/ǫ) full gradients and O(κ^2 log 1/ǫ) stochastic gradients. The authors also compare the computational complexity of EMGD with other optimization algorithms, such as Nesterov's algorithm, Stochastic Average Gradient (SAG), and Stochastic Dual Coordinate Ascent (SDCA).

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed algorithm is novel and addresses a significant problem in optimization, namely reducing the number of full gradients required for smooth and strongly convex optimization problems.
* The analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate is thorough and well-presented.
* The comparisons with other optimization algorithms are informative and provide a clear understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of EMGD.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The paper assumes that the condition number κ is known beforehand, which may not be the case in practice. The authors mention that they will investigate how to estimate κ in future work, but this is an important consideration that should be addressed.
* The algorithm requires a significant amount of notation and mathematical derivations, which can make it difficult to follow for readers without a strong background in optimization.
* The paper could benefit from more experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of EMGD in practice.
