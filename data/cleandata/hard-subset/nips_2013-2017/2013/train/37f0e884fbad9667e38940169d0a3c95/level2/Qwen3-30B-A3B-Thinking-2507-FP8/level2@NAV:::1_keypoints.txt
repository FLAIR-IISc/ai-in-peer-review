- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients to reduce condition-number dependence in smooth, strongly convex optimization.  
- Claims EMGD achieves ε-optimality with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients, improving over standard methods' √κ dependence.  
- Positions EMGD as an advance relative to full-gradient methods (e.g., Nesterov) and purely stochastic methods (e.g., SAG, SDCA).  
- Theoretical analysis is strong: detailed proofs of linear convergence and clear complexity bounds.  
- Literature review is comprehensive and the comparison table (Table 2) clarifies computational trade-offs.  
- Practical advantages highlighted: applicability to constrained problems, lower storage O(d) vs Ω(n), and high-probability convergence guarantees.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation to support theoretical claims, limiting practical relevance for a conference paper.  
- Implementation issue: algorithm requires knowing κ, but the paper offers no practical method for estimating κ in real applications.  
- Insufficient discussion of how the O(κ²) stochastic gradient cost affects real-world performance.  
- Limited comparison with more recent variance-reduced methods like SVRG and SAGA.  
- Recommendation: theoretically interesting and novel, but recommend rejection unless experiments and a κ-estimation/implementation discussion are added.
