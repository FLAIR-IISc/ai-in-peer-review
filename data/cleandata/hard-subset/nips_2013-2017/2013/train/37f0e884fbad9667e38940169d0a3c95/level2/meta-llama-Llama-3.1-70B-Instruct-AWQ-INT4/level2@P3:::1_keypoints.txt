- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex problems.
- Claims linear convergence and reduces required full gradients from O(√κ log(1/ε)) to O(log(1/ε)).
- Theoretical analysis and proofs are thorough and technically sound.
- Presentation of the algorithm and derivations is clear and well-organized.
- Lacks sufficient numerical experiments to demonstrate practical performance.
- Provides limited guidance on hyperparameter selection (step size, iterations per epoch).
- Insufficient empirical comparisons with widely used optimization methods.
- Needs more intuitive explanations of mixed-gradient and epoch-based concepts.
- Would benefit from visualizations (convergence plots, optimization error curves).
- Original idea is novel in this formulation but builds on existing mixed full/stochastic gradient approaches.
- Potentially significant for reducing computational cost in ML optimization, but practical impact is underexplored.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions addressing experiments, hyperparameters, comparisons, intuition, and visual aids.
