- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), an algorithm that mixes full and stochastic gradients in epoch-based updates.  
- EMGD is designed for smooth, strongly convex problems and uses mixed gradient steps within each epoch.  
- Claims optimal iteration complexity: O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ^2 log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Shows linear convergence with high probability.  
- Theoretical analysis uses martingale difference sequences and the Hoeffding–Azuma inequality for rigorous convergence bounds.  
- Provides detailed convergence proofs to support the theoretical results.  
- Compares EMGD against Nesterov, SAG, and SDCA, highlighting advantages in various settings.  
- Argues EMGD handles both constrained and unconstrained problems and requires less storage than some alternatives.  
- Notes EMGD’s practical appeal for large-scale optimization due to combined gradient use.  
- Weakness: assumes the condition number κ is known a priori, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: no procedure or guidance is given for estimating κ in practice.  
- Weakness: paper lacks experimental results demonstrating empirical effectiveness.  
- Suggestion: include a practical method to estimate κ.  
- Suggestion: add experiments to validate EMGD empirically.  
- Suggestion: consider extending EMGD to non-smooth or non-strongly convex problems.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel algorithm with strong theoretical iteration complexity and favorable comparisons to existing methods.  
- Argument against acceptance: reliance on a known κ and absence of empirical validation.
