- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) to reduce full-gradient evaluations for smooth, strongly convex problems by combining full and stochastic gradients.  
- Claims iteration complexity O(log(1/ε)) in number of full gradients, independent of condition number κ.  
- Well-motivated for large-scale optimization where full gradients are expensive.  
- Presentation is clear, algorithm well-defined, and paper is easy to follow.  
- Theoretical analysis and convergence proof appear rigorous and significant.  
- Useful comparisons to existing methods (SAG, SDCA, Nesterov) that highlight EMGD’s potential benefits.  
- Novelty: epoch-based mixing of full and stochastic gradients to avoid κ-dependence in full-gradient count.  
- Practical concerns: lacks discussion on step-size sensitivity and effective tuning of η.  
- Major limitation: assumes knowledge of condition number κ without sufficient treatment of estimation methods or costs.  
- No empirical evaluation or experiments to demonstrate practical performance on real or synthetic datasets.  
- Notational overload in Algorithm 1 (confusing superscripts/subscripts) harming readability.  
- Some redundancy across sections (repeated discussion of trade-offs).  
- Abstractness of the function oracle Of; needs concrete examples to clarify assumptions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add experiments, address κ estimation and step-size guidance, and clean up notation/redundancy.
