- Summary: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) that mixes full and stochastic gradients to achieve linear convergence while reducing full gradient calls to O(log 1/ε), independent of κ.
- Main claim 1: EMGD achieves linear convergence with far fewer full-gradient evaluations.
- Main claim 2: EMGD is more efficient than methods using only full or only stochastic gradients.
- Main claim 3: EMGD applies to both constrained and unconstrained smooth strongly convex problems.
- Theoretical support: Provides a proof of linear convergence and bounds on full-gradient counts.
- Complexity comparison: Compares EMGD’s computational complexity favorably against existing methods.
- Usefulness: Practically valuable for large-scale optimization by lowering full-gradient costs via mixed updates.
- Field knowledge: Demonstrates strong familiarity with related work and clearly states assumptions and limitations.
- Novelty: Presents an innovative specific mix of full and stochastic gradients, though the general idea of using stochastic gradients for cost reduction is not new.
- Reproducibility: Sufficient algorithmic and theoretical detail for reproduction, with some minor implementation clarifications needed.
- Key limitation: Requires knowledge of the condition number κ, which can be hard to estimate and may hurt practicality.
- Efficiency caveat: May not be optimal when κ is very large; applicability depends on problem specifics.
- Suggested improvements: Add more experimental results and methods to estimate κ in practice.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel contribution, thorough theoretical analysis, and practical relevance to large-scale problems.
- Arguments against acceptance: Dependence on κ estimation and limited empirical validation.
