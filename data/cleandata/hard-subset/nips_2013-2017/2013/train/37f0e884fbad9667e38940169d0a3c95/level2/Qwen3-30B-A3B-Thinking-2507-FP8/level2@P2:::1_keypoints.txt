- Proposes EMGD reducing full-gradient calls to O(log(1/ε)) and stochastic gradients to O(κ² log(1/ε)).  
- Paper is well-organized with a technically sound theoretical analysis and clear linear-convergence proof.  
- Provides reasonable contextualization and comparison with related full-gradient and stochastic methods.  
- Strength: addresses computational cost of full gradients in ill-conditioned problems and gives thorough convergence analysis.  
- Weakness: novelty is overstated; paper does not adequately differentiate EMGD from existing semi-stochastic methods (e.g., [12,13]).  
- SAG already achieves linear convergence with O(n log(1/ε)), comparable to EMGD when κ is small.  
- Major gap: no empirical validation to demonstrate practical benefits over methods like SAG and SDCA.  
- Comparison with SAG/SDCA is superficial; claimed storage and high-probability advantages are not substantiated.  
- Incorrectly claims SAG and SDCA only have expectation guarantees; they also have high-probability results.  
- Practical limitation: EMGD requires knowing κ a priori and the paper offers no convincing estimation strategy.  
- Significance assessment: contribution appears incremental rather than transformative and may not hold advantage when κ is large.  
- For a venue like NIPS, lack of empirical results weakens the paper despite solid theory.  
- Suggested improvements: deeper comparison with semi-stochastic methods, empirical experiments, and a concrete κ-estimation method.  
- Overall: theoretically sound algorithm with limited novelty and insufficient evidence of practical significance.
