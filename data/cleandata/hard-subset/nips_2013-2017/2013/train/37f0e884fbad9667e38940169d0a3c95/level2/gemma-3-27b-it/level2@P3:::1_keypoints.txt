- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) to reduce dependence on condition number κ for smooth, strongly convex problems.  
- Core idea: combine full and stochastic gradients to achieve O(log(1/ε)) full gradients vs standard O(√κ log(1/ε)).  
- Motivated by high cost of full-gradient evaluations in large-scale problems.  

Strengths:
- Tackles a relevant, practical problem of reducing full-gradient computations for ill-conditioned optimization.  
- Theoretical improvement in the number of full gradients, addressing a key computational bottleneck.  
- Provides a reasonably thorough theoretical analysis, including a convergence theorem and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Related work is well-organized; comparisons to SAG and SDCA highlight trade-offs.  
- Includes discussion of practical considerations and potential future work (e.g., estimating κ).  

Weaknesses:
- Constant factors hidden in O(log(1/ε)) are not discussed and could negate practical gains.  
- Algorithm presentation lacks sufficient implementation detail (Δk updates, step size η selection).  
- Analysis depends strongly on smoothness and strong convexity; behavior under weaker conditions is unexplored.  
- Discussion of estimating κ is superficial and lacks concrete methods.  
- Experimental validation is insufficient or absent to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Distributed computing considerations for the gradient oracle (communication costs, implementation) are underdeveloped.  
- Justification for threshold T ≥ 1152L²/λ² ln(δ) is opaque and needs clearer rationale.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel approach reducing full gradient evaluations with strong theoretical guarantees and practical motivation.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Missing experiments, unclear constant factors, limited implementation guidance, and superficial κ estimation discussion.  

Overall assessment:
- The paper is theoretically sound and potentially valuable but limited by lack of experiments and implementation detail.  

Recommendations:
- Essential: add experimental results comparing EMGD to baselines on benchmark problems.  
- Essential: expand implementation details and practical parameter-selection guidance.  
- Major: analyze and discuss constant factors hidden in asymptotic bounds.  
- Minor: provide concrete techniques for estimating κ in practice.  
- Minor: explain intuitively the parameter T and the 1152L²/λ² ln(δ) threshold.
