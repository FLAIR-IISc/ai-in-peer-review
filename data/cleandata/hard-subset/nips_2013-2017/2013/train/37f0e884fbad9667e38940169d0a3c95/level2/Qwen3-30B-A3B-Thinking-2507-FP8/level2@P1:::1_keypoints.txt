- Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD), combining full and stochastic gradients for smooth, strongly convex optimization.  
- Claims EMGD finds an ε-optimal solution with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.  
- Well-motivated literature review that situates the method among recent linear-convergence algorithms (e.g., SAG, SDCA).  
- Correctly highlights the trade-off between full and stochastic gradient evaluations, important for ill-conditioned problems (large κ).  
- Main strength: novel mixed-gradient algorithm with clear, rigorous theoretical analysis and a solid proof of Theorem 1.  
- Comparison (Table 2) is insightful: EMGD handles constrained problems and requires less storage than SAG/SDCA.  
- Practical significance of reducing full gradient complexity to O(log(1/ε)) is not convincingly demonstrated with concrete examples or runtimes.  
- Comparison with SAG/SDCA is misleading because those methods target finite-sum problems (F(w)=1/n∑fi(w)) while EMGD is more general; settings should be made explicit.  
- Assumes condition number κ is known a priori, which is a significant practical limitation and not resolved in the paper.  
- The claim that stochastic gradients are “efficient” is vague; the paper lacks a clear computational cost analysis between full and stochastic gradients.  
- Writing, notation, and logical flow are clear and accessible.  
- Theoretical contributions are sound with careful probabilistic guarantees and proper citations.  
- Overall: technically strong and valuable to optimization theory but needs stronger practical validation and a solution for κ estimation.
