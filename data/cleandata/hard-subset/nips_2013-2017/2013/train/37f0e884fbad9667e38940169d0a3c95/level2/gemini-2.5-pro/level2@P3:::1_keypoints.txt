- Problem: Addresses optimizing smooth, strongly convex functions where first-order convergence depends on condition number κ, making full gradient calls costly for ill-conditioned problems.
- Contribution: Proposes Epoch Mixed Gradient Descent (EMGD) to make the number of full gradient evaluations independent of κ by combining full and cheaper stochastic gradients.
- Algorithm structure: Epoch-based method using one full gradient per epoch, mixed variance-reduced stochastic updates within epochs, and a shrinking search domain across epochs.
- Main theoretical result: EMGD achieves linear convergence with O(log(1/ε)) full gradients and O(κ² log(1/ε)) stochastic gradients.
- Originality/significance: Presents a novel trade-off between full and stochastic gradient calls, relevant for large-scale ill-conditioned optimization.
- Technical quality: Provides a rigorous, high-probability convergence analysis that appears technically sound and stronger than typical in-expectation results.
- Clarity: Well-written, precisely described algorithm and formal results, with clear positioning relative to prior deterministic and stochastic methods; notes practical advantages (constraints, memory).
- Parameter dependence issue: Requires knowledge of smoothness and strong-convexity constants (L, λ) to set inner-loop length T; sensitivity and adaptive strategies are not discussed.
- Complexity caveat: Total stochastic gradient complexity is O(κ²), which may make EMGD less competitive than SAG/SDCA when κ is large (e.g., on the order of n); a more nuanced regime analysis is needed.
- Lack of experiments: Entirely theoretical—empirical validation and comparisons to AGD, SAG, SDCA across κ regimes are missing and would strengthen the paper.
- Literature connection: The mixed-gradient update is identical to SVRG’s variance reduction; the paper should acknowledge SVRG and clarify distinctions (epoch/shrinking-domain use).
- Overall assessment: Strong and novel theoretical contribution with clear writing, but practical impact would be improved by addressing parameter-dependence, providing empirical results, and situating the method relative to SVRG and other baselines.
