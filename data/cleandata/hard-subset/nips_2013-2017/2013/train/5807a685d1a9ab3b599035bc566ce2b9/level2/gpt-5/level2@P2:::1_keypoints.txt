- Paper develops a minimax framework for local differential privacy (LDP) and gives sharp matching rates for two problems: multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Multinomial MSE scales as d/(nα^2), implying effective sample size n → nα^2/d; a variant of randomized response is shown minimax-optimal.  
- For Sobolev density classes Fβ, the rate changes from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} (non-private) to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP, with matching constructive estimators.  
- Lower bounds use contraction inequalities relating post-channel KL/mutual information to pre-channel total variation, combined with Le Cam/Fano techniques.  
- Upper bounds are achieved by explicit private mechanisms: privatized histograms (β=1) and privatized orthogonal-series estimators (β≥1) with tailored randomization.  
- Connects classical randomized response and minimax decision theory to modern differential privacy, complementing central-DP and prior local-DP work.  
- Uses deconvolution lower bounds to argue additive noise on raw samples is suboptimal for density estimation, motivating bespoke mechanisms.  
- Correctness: lower-bound techniques are sound and upper bounds appear constructive and plausible; randomized response optimality is well-supported.  
- Rigor/completeness: key inequalities (Proposition 1) have proofs deferred to a longer report—conference version should include proof sketches for delicate steps.  
- Scope: paper studies non-interactive channels only; discussion or results on whether interaction can improve rates would strengthen the work.  
- Clarity: exposition is generally clear and well-organized; multinomial mechanism and histogram estimator are easy to follow.  
- Minor clarity issues: possible typo/dimensional inconsistency in Equation (8) that needs correction.  
- Minor clarity issues: orthogonal-series mechanism uses constant ck and rejection sampling—acceptance rate, complexity, and bounds on ck should be made explicit.  
- Minor clarity issues: Sobolev class defined with periodic boundary conditions—brief comment on non-periodic or R extensions would help.  
- Originality: high—sharp minimax rates under LDP, change in nonparametric exponent, and optimality of randomized response are novel contributions.  
- Significance: significant—quantifies privacy–utility tradeoffs concretely, gives practical effective-sample-size guidance, and sets benchmarks for private estimators.  
- Pros: sharp matching rates, interpretable privacy penalties, clarifies randomized response optimality, and solid information-theoretic lower bounds with constructive mechanisms.  
- Cons: no empirical validation or simulations to illustrate constants and finite-sample behavior.  
- Cons: reliance on companion technical report for key proofs may limit accessibility.  
- Cons: focus on non-interactive protocols with limited discussion on interaction’s potential benefits.  
- Cons: minor typos and incomplete details on orthogonal-series mechanism’s constants and computational overhead.  
- Suggestions: fix the apparent typo in Theorem 1 and verify units/rates across norms.  
- Suggestions: include brief proof sketches of central information-contraction results in the conference version.  
- Suggestions: add a short synthetic empirical study comparing Laplace-on-samples, randomized response, and proposed estimators to show rates/constants.  
- Suggestions: discuss whether interactive LDP protocols can improve rates or state it as an open problem.  
- Suggestions: clarify constants, acceptance rates, and computational cost of the orthogonal-series mechanism and address adaptivity/data-driven choice of k.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
