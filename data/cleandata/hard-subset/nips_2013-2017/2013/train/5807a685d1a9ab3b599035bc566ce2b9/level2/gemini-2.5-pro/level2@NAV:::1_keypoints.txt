- Provides a rigorous decision-theoretic/minimax analysis of statistical estimation under local differential privacy.  
- Derives sharp convergence rates for d-dimensional multinomial estimation and β-smooth density estimation under α-local privacy.  
- Shows α-local privacy reduces effective sample size for multinomials from n to roughly nα²/d.  
- Shows privacy changes optimal density estimation rate from n⁻²β/(2β+1) to (nα²)⁻²β/(2β+2).  
- Proves Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal in this modern local-privacy setting.  
- Presents computationally efficient estimators that achieve the derived optimal rates, matching lower bounds.  
- Technical quality is excellent with tight matching lower and upper bounds and complete theoretical treatment.  
- Originality is high; among the first to apply full minimax analysis to local privacy and to characterize privacy’s fundamental impact.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and well-situated in the literature on privacy and statistical estimation.  
- Minor weakness: explanation of the orthogonal series sampling mechanism (Section 4.3) is dense and needs more intuition.  
- Minor suggestion: include a brief proof sketch for the main lower bounds to make the submission more self-contained.  
- Overall verdict: outstanding, significant, and influential work; strong recommendation for acceptance.
