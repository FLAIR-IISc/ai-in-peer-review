- Main contributions: derives sharp LDP minimax rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation; shows effective sample size reduction n → nα^2/d for multinomials and rate change to (α^2 n)−2β/(2β+2) for Sobolev densities, with mechanisms attaining these rates.  
- Randomized response (Warner) is shown optimal up to constants for multinomials; private histograms and orthogonal series achieve the density rates; naive additive Laplace on raw samples is provably suboptimal.  
- Lower bounds: built via Le Cam/Fano plus new information bounds for LDP channels; upper bounds: simple, implementable mechanisms.  
- Theoretical development is careful and technically sound, with matching rates and plausible information-theoretic lemmas.  
- Some proofs are deferred to a longer version; providing concise proof sketches would improve completeness.  
- No empirical evaluation provided; finite-sample constants or small simulations would help illustrate effects like n→nα^2/d.  
- Clarity: generally well organized but notation is dense; needs more intuition for key inequalities and a summary figure/table of rates.  
- Implementation clarity: orthogonal series sampling scheme would benefit from pseudocode and complexity discussion.  
- Originality: high — sharp LDP minimax rates, general optimality of randomized response, and suboptimality of Laplace perturbation are novel.  
- Significance/usefulness: high — quantifies privacy–utility trade-offs and gives practical mechanisms for LDP settings.  
- Limitation: scope restricted to non-interactive LDP; unclear whether interactivity can improve rates.  
- Limitation: no treatment of adaptivity to unknown smoothness β or private, data-driven bin/series selection.  
- Limitation: assumptions (e.g., bounded basis, α∈[0,1]) and robustness to model misspecification need clarification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: sharp matching bounds, practical efficient mechanisms, broad relevance to privacy-preserving statistics.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy reliance on an external longer version for proofs, limited discussion of interactivity/adaptivity, and lack of empirical illustrations.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept.
