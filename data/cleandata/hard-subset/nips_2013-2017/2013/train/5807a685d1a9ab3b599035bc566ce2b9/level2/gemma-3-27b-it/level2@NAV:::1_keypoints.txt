- Studies trade-offs between local differential privacy and statistical efficiency for probability estimation.  
- Provides sharp minimax lower and upper bounds for multinomial and density estimation under local privacy.  
- Shows randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Derives a novel polynomial convergence rate for density estimation that differs from the non-private case.  
- Uses minimax theory, information theory, and differential-privacy tools, and gives efficient algorithms achieving the upper bounds.  
- Clearly defines local privacy and connects results to Warner’s randomized response and recent differential privacy work.  
- Detailed proofs are provided (with full details available elsewhere), supporting the rigor of results.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical implications and real-world limitations of randomized response.  
- Weakness: density-estimation section could better address computational challenges and high-dimensional applicability.  
- Weakness: exposition is dense and assumes strong background in statistical decision and information theory, limiting accessibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel significant theoretical results, sharp rates, rigorous analysis, and connections to classical and recent work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dense presentation and insufficient practical discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance with a request to expand discussion of practical implications.
