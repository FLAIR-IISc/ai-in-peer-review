- Paper derives sharp minimax rates under α-local differential privacy for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation.  
- Multinomial MSE and L1 risks scale as Θ(min{1, d/(nα²)}), implying effective sample size n → nα²/d for small α.  
- Nonparametric Sobolev rate degrades from n−2β/(2β+1) to (nα²)−2β/(2β+2), changing the exponent under LDP.  
- Matching upper bounds achieved via privatized histograms (β=1) and a bounded, unbiased orthogonal-series mechanism (β≥1).  
- Lower bounds use Le Cam/Fano methods with specialized LDP information-contraction inequalities (KL/mutual information bounds).  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is minimax-optimal (up to constants) for multinomial estimation.  
- Connects to prior work on local privacy, differential privacy frameworks, private learning, and deconvolution lower bounds.  
- Technical development is sound; upper bounds are constructive and computationally reasonable.  
- Paper clearly states assumptions (non-interactive channels, α∈[0,1], bounded bases) and makes dependence on n, d, α, β explicit.  
- Some technical proofs are deferred to a longer version; a brief proof sketch of key information bounds would aid readability.  
- Novel contribution: first sharp minimax rates under LDP for these problems and a new orthogonal-series mechanism tailored to LDP.  
- Results are significant for practice and theory, clarifying privacy–accuracy trade-offs and influencing private estimation and mechanism design.  
- Pros: sharp rates with matching bounds, constructive simple mechanisms, resolution of randomized-response optimality.  
- Cons: restricted to non-interactive mechanisms, limited practical tuning/finite-sample guidance, assumes known β and bounded bases, deferred technical details.  
- Essential suggestions: clarify novelty relative to the cited arXiv version and include a brief proof sketch for the LDP information-contraction inequalities.  
- Optional suggestions: add simulations, discuss potential gains from interactive protocols, address adaptivity to unknown β, provide tuning guidance and robustness commentary.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and technically solid contribution that merits acceptance pending minor clarifications.
