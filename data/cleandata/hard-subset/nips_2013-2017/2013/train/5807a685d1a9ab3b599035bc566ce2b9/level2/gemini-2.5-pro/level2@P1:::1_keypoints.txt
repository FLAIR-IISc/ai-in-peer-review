- Establishes a rigorous connection between local differential privacy and minimax decision theory.  
- Analyzes two settings: d-dimensional multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation in a Sobolev class.  
- Shows α-local privacy reduces effective sample size for multinomial estimation to about nα^2/d.  
- Proves Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal for the multinomial problem.  
- Shows local privacy alters the nonparametric minimax rate exponent from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}.  
- Provides matching lower and upper bounds, including explicit private estimators (private histogram for β=1, private orthogonal series for general β).  
- Technical analysis is rigorous and high-quality, using Fano-style lower bounds and constructive upper bounds.  
- Correctly distinguishes stronger lower bounds for population-parameter estimation versus sample-quantity estimation.  
- Insightfully argues naive mechanisms (e.g., adding Laplace noise to samples) can be suboptimal.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and logically organized, with accessible exposition and connections to classic work.  
- Highly original: first to apply full minimax theory to local privacy and derive algorithm-independent fundamental limits.  
- Results are significant for ML and statistics, providing foundational benchmarks for local privacy trade-offs.  
- Pros for acceptance: fundamental characterization, sharp matching bounds, elegant link to randomized response, and novel rate-change insight.  
- Cons against acceptance: main proofs deferred to a companion report; optimal density-estimation mechanism is complex and needs more practical/implementation discussion.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, minor weaknesses, strong recommendation for acceptance.
