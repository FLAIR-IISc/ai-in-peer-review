- Studies probability estimation under local differential privacy, deriving sharp minimax convergence rates and privacy–utility trade-offs.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough introduction and literature review.
- Technical sections are clear and explain key concepts and results effectively.
- Main contribution: sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation under local DP.
- Demonstrates that local privacy can substantially reduce estimator quality and that optimal rates differ from classical settings.
- Uses minimax decision theory and differential privacy with a variational approach to obtain sharp lower bounds and provides algorithms that achieve them.
- Strength: gives a clear, intuitive characterization of the privacy–utility continuum and how to adjust procedures accordingly.
- Limitation: results depend on a specific definition of local differential privacy; robustness to other privacy definitions is unclear.
- Limitation: analysis is limited to specific statistical problems; generalization to other problems is not established.
- Overall assessment: significant, rigorous, and well-presented contribution to statistical inference under privacy constraints.
- Ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, rigorous results, and clear intuition about trade-offs.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on one privacy definition and limited problem scope.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the noted limitations.
