- Comprehensive study of estimating probability distributions under local differential privacy.  
- Theoretical framework combining differential privacy with minimax decision theory.  
- Focused analysis on multinomial estimation and density estimation.  
- Derives sharp minimax convergence rates that quantify privacy–utility trade-offs.  
- Shows local privacy degrades estimator quality and effectively reduces sample size.  
- Provides efficiently implementable algorithms (privatized histogram, orthogonal series) that attain sharp upper bounds.  
- Establishes matching lower bounds, giving a private analog of classical non-private results.  
- Framework offers a rigorous tool to analyze trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency.  
- Results have practical implications for designing privacy-preserving statistical procedures.  
- Limitation: analysis assumes a non-interactive setting, which may be unrealistic in some applications.  
- Limitation: scope restricted to two problem types, so generalization to other problems is unclear.  
- Limitation: reliance on DP and minimax theory may require substantial background for some readers.  
- Originality: novel integration of differential privacy and minimax decision theory with new minimax rates.  
- Significance: potential to influence development of methods that balance privacy and statistical efficiency.  
- Clarity and quality: well-written, organized, technically sound with rigorous proofs and supporting simulations.
