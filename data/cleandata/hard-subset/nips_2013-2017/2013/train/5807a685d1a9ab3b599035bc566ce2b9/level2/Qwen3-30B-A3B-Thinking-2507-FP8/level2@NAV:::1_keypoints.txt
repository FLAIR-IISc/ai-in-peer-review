- Rigorous theoretical analysis of probability estimation under α-local differential privacy, linking privacy and statistical inference.
- Establishes sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation under α-LDP, showing fundamental privacy–efficiency trade-offs.
- For multinomial estimation, effective sample size reduces to nα²/d, and Warner’s randomized response is shown to be optimal.
- For density estimation with β derivatives, rate degrades from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under α-LDP.
- Provides matching lower and upper bounds with constructive algorithms achieving the derived rates.
- Presents specific estimators: a privatized histogram for Lipschitz densities and an orthogonal series estimator for higher smoothness.
- Technical results are sound and thoroughly proved, with clear, well-organized writing and notation.
- Novelty lies in precise minimax characterization of the privacy–statistical efficiency trade-off, bridging classical decision theory and modern privacy.
- Results are significant, offering fundamental insights into the costs of privacy for practitioners and researchers.
- Minor limitation: focuses on theory without empirical validation and could discuss extensions to more complex models or implementation challenges.
- Overall assessment: high-quality theoretical contribution with strong novelty, technical quality, and impact; recommendation for acceptance.
