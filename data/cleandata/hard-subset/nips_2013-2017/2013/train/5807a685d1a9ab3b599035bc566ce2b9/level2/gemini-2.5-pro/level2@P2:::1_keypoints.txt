- Decision-theoretic analysis of trade-off between statistical utility and α-local differential privacy for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.

- Sharp minimax lower bounds on estimation error under local privacy, with matching computationally efficient upper bounds.

- Multinomial result: minimax squared error ∼ d/(nα^2), implying effective sample size nα^2/d; a modified Warner randomized response is minimax optimal.

- Nonparametric result: minimax integrated squared error ∼ (nα^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)), so local privacy changes the convergence exponent versus the non-private rate n^(-2β/(2β+1)).

- Proposes practical mechanisms achieving optimal rates: a privatized histogram for β=1 and a tailored orthogonal series estimator for β>1.

- Strength — significance/originality: provides a sharp, quantitative answer to the statistical cost of strong local privacy and bridges minimax theory with local differential privacy.

- Strength — technical quality: rigorous application of Le Cam and Fano, complete with tight lower bounds and matching constructive algorithms.

- Strength — clarity: well-written, organized, and effective comparison to classical non-private rates with good high-level intuition.

- Weakness — self-containedness: main proofs deferred to a longer manuscript; paper would benefit from proof sketches.

- Weakness — intuition: the sampling strategy for the orthogonal series estimator (Eq. 19) is sophisticated and needs more explanatory intuition.

- Suggestion: include an appendix with proof sketches for Theorems 1 and 2 to improve self-containedness.

- Suggestion: add brief interpretation of the `min` terms in rate expressions (8) and (9) to clarify different n, d, α regimes.

- Suggestion: comment on extension to large α and connection to non-private rates as α → ∞.

- Suggestion: more prominently highlight the elegant optimality connection to Warner’s 1965 randomized response (e.g., in abstract/introduction).

- Overall: outstanding, fundamental, technically strong paper with minor, easily addressable weaknesses and high potential influence.
