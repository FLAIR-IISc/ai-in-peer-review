- Paper studies estimation of probability distributions under local privacy, keeping data private even from the statistician.
- Main contribution: sharp minimax rates of convergence for locally private estimation.
- Shows fundamental trade-offs between privacy and convergence rate; local privacy can harm estimator quality.
- Provides tools to move along the privacy–statistical efficiency continuum.
- Strong theoretical support: minimax lower bounds and matching attainable upper bounds via efficient algorithms.
- Numerical experiments included to support theory, though more could help intuition.
- Well-written, well-organized, with clear notation and readable, well-structured proofs.
- Comprehensive and relevant related-work review with proper citations.
- High usefulness: provides a theoretical framework for balancing accuracy and privacy in statistical procedures.
- High novelty: original theoretical insights into limits of estimation under local privacy.
- Completeness: detailed technical analysis and discussion of implications.
- Limitations acknowledged; assumptions and scope discussed with suggestions for future work.
- Concerns: may be too technical for non-experts and results might be limited to specific settings.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add more numerical experiments and clarify assumptions/limitations.
- Overall rating: 9/10.
