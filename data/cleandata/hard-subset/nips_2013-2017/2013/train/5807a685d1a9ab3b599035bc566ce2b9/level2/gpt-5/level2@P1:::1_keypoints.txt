- Paper studies distribution estimation under α-local differential privacy (LDP) and derives sharp minimax rates for multinomial and Sobolev-class density estimation.
- For multinomial estimation, ℓ2-risk scales as min{1, d/(nα2)} and ℓ1-risk as min{1, d/√(nα2)}, showing effective sample-size reduction n → nα2/d.
- A variant of Warner’s randomized response (RR) and a simple Laplace scheme attain the optimal multinomial rates.
- For nonparametric density estimation over Sobolev classes Fβ, the minimax lower bound is Mn ≳ (nα2)−2β/(2β+2), worsening the exponent relative to non-private rates.
- Two mechanisms match the density lower bound: a privatized histogram (optimal for β=1) and a locally private orthonormal-series estimator (optimal for all β≥1).
- Direct additive noise on Xi cannot attain the optimal density rates due to deconvolution limits.
- Technical contributions combine Assouad/Fano/Le Cam lower bounds with LDP-specific information-contraction inequalities on KL and mutual information.
- The paper connects classic minimax testing reductions to modern privacy constraints, filling a gap relative to prior DP and RR work.
- Results are technically sound, with consistent rates and compelling optimality argument for RR in the multinomial case.
- The orthogonal-series mechanism is carefully designed for unbiasedness and LDP, with clear α-dependence in parameter scaling.
- Some core proofs are deferred to a companion source; including proof sketches in the supplement would improve self-containment.
- Efficiency claims for the series mechanism need brief complexity analysis and sampling pseudocode for clarity.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized; notation and background on minimax reductions are helpful.
- A few constants (e.g., ck) are referenced abstractly and would benefit from practical guidance for reproducibility.
- Originality is high: sharp minimax characterization under LDP, exponent degradation for densities, and RR optimality are novel.
- Significance is high: clarifies privacy–utility trade-offs, offers implementable mechanisms, and guides future algorithm design.
- Pros include matching bounds, effective sample-size identification, new rate-optimal private mechanisms, and methodological LDP–information links.
- Cons/questions: effect of interactivity, adaptivity to unknown β, computational/sampling details of the series mechanism, calibration of constants B and ck, communication-efficient RR variants, and robustness to model misspecification.
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theory, constructive algorithms, practical implications, and methodological advances.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on external proofs reduces self-containment and limited scope to local privacy without central DP comparison.
- Suggestions: add proof sketches in the supplement and include a short empirical illustration to show constants and practical performance.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance, contingent on improved self-containment and clarified implementation details.
