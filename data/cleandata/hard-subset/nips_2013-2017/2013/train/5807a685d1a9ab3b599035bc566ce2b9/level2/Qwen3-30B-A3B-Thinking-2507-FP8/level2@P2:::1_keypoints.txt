- Establishes sharp minimax rates for probability estimation under local differential privacy.  
- Connects Warner's randomized response to modern differential privacy, proving its optimality for multinomial estimation.  
- Treats two fundamental problems (multinomial and density estimation) under α-local DP with tight lower and upper bounds.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical framework using minimax decision theory; proofs are technically sound and supported by matching bounds (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- Focuses on population estimation θ(P) rather than sample estimation θ(x₁:n), yielding stronger lower bounds than prior work.  
- Supplies efficient algorithms that achieve the theoretical rates, showing practical relevance.  
- Original contribution: first sharp characterization of minimax rates under local privacy and novel synthesis of minimax theory with differential privacy.  
- Quantifies privacy–efficiency trade-offs precisely (e.g., effective sample size n → nα²/d for multinomial; altered density estimation rates).  
- Well-written and clearly organized with consistent notation and helpful examples.  
- Minor improvement: better contextualization relative to recent private estimation work (e.g., Chaudhuri & Hsu 2013; Smith 2011).  
- Minor improvement: provide more detail on computational complexity of the proposed algorithms despite claiming efficient computability.  
- Minor technical clarification: explicitly relate α-local differential privacy to ε-differential privacy and justify the chosen formulation.  
- Overall assessment: substantial, significant, and technically strong contribution appropriate for NIPS; recommend acceptance.
