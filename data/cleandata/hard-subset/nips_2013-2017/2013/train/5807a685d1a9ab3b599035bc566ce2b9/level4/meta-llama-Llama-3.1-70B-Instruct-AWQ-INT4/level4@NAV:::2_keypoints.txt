- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local (individual) privacy, focusing on multinomial and density estimation.  
- Authors derive sharp minimax rates of convergence for both multinomial (pmf) and density (pdf) estimation under local privacy.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy causes a quadratic reduction in effective sample size with respect to the privacy parameter α, often leaving effective sample size comparable to non-private when α is a small constant.  
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private rate is no longer attainable under local privacy.  
- Proofs appear correct and the density-estimation minimax bound under privacy seems novel.  
- Major concern: insufficient acknowledgement of prior work on noise complexity/lower bounds for differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) which studied related linear counting queries with L2 error.  
- Clarification requested on whether the local-privacy multinomial bound improves on previously known (central) differential privacy lower bounds, since central DP lower bounds apply a fortiori to local DP.  
- Ambiguity about density-estimation comparison: does the non-private lower bound in equation (13) hold for the general Sobolev space (Definition 1) or only for the trigonometric-basis case used in the paper?  
- Rebuttal partially clarified prior-work relation, but the reviewer also cites Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013) on mean-square-error as another relevant reference to include.  
- Overall: interesting, well-written, and correct results, but notable omission of prior noise-complexity/differential-privacy lower bounds that should be discussed and cited.
