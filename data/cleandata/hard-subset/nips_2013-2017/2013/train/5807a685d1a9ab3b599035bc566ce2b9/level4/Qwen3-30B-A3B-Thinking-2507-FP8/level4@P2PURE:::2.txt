

This paper establishes minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy constraints, addressing both multinomial estimation and density estimation. For multinomial estimation, the authors derive sharp minimax rates, showing that local privacy reduces the effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α. Given that α is typically small, the effective sample size remains comparable to the non-private case. For density estimation, the authors demonstrate that the optimal non-private minimax rate is unattainable under local privacy.  

The results are interesting and the proofs appear correct. To the best of my knowledge, this is the first work to establish minimax bounds for density estimation under privacy constraints.  

However, the multinomial estimation minimax bound is closely related to prior work on noise complexity in differential privacy (DP), which is not adequately cited. Specifically, Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012) establish worst-case lower bounds for linear query error under DP using the same L₂ metric. The probability estimation problem considered here is a special case of their linear counting query problem. Since local privacy imposes a stronger constraint than DP, their lower bounds also apply to local privacy. The paper should clarify whether its local privacy bound improves upon these existing DP lower bounds.  

For density estimation, the comparison is ambiguous. The authors claim the local privacy lower bound exceeds the non-private bound (eq. 13), but eq. 13 is derived for densities expandable in a trigonometric basis. It is unclear whether the non-private lower bound in eq. 13 holds generally for Sobolev spaces (as defined in Definition 1) or only for the trigonometric basis. A fair comparison requires the non-private bound to apply to the same basis.  

Additional note to rebuttal: While the authors partially address the relation to prior worst-case lower bounds, they should also cite Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which considers mean square error in the sparse and approximate DP settings.  

Overall, the paper proves sharp minimax bounds for PMF and PDF estimation with privacy guarantees. The results are significant, and the paper is well-written, but it fails to cite key prior work on noise complexity lower bounds for DP, which are directly relevant to the multinomial estimation results.
