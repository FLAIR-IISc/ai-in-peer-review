- Paper uses a Fano/LeCam-style argument to derive lower bounds for estimation with an untrusted algorithm; randomized response is an optimal strategy in this framework.  
- Reviewer did not evaluate the supplementary material.  
- Strength: characterizes fundamental limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed data.  
- Weakness: exposition contains imprecisions that could mislead casual readers.  
- Weakness: missing connections to existing differential privacy literature.  
- Local privacy model is distinct from standard differential privacy; authors should avoid broad generalizations about DP implications.  
- Question whether the restriction on α in Theorem 1 is necessary, since empirical limits (α ≈ 1) may reduce practical relevance.  
- Request more discussion of choices for the utility measure ρ to show how different objectives fit the analysis.  
- Related work missing: comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure (not cited).  
- Although randomized response achieves optimal rate for histograms, comparative analysis of other algorithms is needed.  
- Addendum: clarify population vs. sample statistics early in the paper.  
- Addendum: provide comparisons to related work (including Lei and other reviewers’ references).  
- Addendum: after reviewing [10], the paper’s main contribution is a clear exposition of the local privacy model (largely contained in [10]) and, secondarily, novel bounds for density estimation.
