- Studies probability estimation under local α-differential privacy in a min-max framework where an adversary picks distributions and the statistician designs local mechanisms.  
- Derives a min-max lower bound on the l2 estimation error for discrete distributions over d types.  
- Shows the bound is tight: classic randomized response matches the lower bound, proving its optimality in this setting.  
- Extends the analysis to density estimation, showing a simple local perturbation scheme achieves the optimal min-max rate.  
- Paper is well-executed, important, and merits acceptance.  
- Presentation/clarity could be improved: simplify the min-max exposition, reduce cumbersome notation, and state results directly in l2 norm.  
- Broaden discussion: compare to l1 norm and relate the l2 bounds to l1, and situate results relative to non-local differential privacy lower bounds.  
- Discuss generality: comment on whether the analytical approach extends to other privacy models; emphasize the key insight that per-user independent randomization is optimal.  
- Post-rebuttal: notes significant overlap with a FOCS 2013 paper, making parts (esp. Section 3) incremental; acceptance recommended with a reservation about novelty.  
- Overall: valuable, sharp results establishing optimality of randomized response, though impact is tempered by related prior work.
