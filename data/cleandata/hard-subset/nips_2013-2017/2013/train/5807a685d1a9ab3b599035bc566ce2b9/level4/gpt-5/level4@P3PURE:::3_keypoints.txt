- Studies local privacy (α-DP per user) using minimax expected error to analyze probability and density estimation.  
- Derives a minimax lower bound for l2 estimation when user types come from a d-category discrete distribution.  
- Shows a simple randomized response mechanism meets that lower bound, proving its optimality for the discrete case.  
- Analyzes density estimation and gives a minimax lower bound achieved by a scheme where each user perturbs only her own type.  
- Highlights the surprising result that per-user randomization (independent of others’ types/reports) is optimal despite the more general allowed dependence.  
- Overall assessment: important, timely work; recommends acceptance (but note weaker support below).  
- Suggests simplifying exposition and notation, and explicitly framing all bounds in l2 throughout.  
- Requests discussion of l1 error and connections to classical (non-local) differential privacy lower bounds.  
- Recommends broadening the scope of minimax bounds beyond the local setting to strengthen the paper.  
- New reservation: appears to partially extend/overlap with recently published FOCS 2013 work (especially Section 3), which reduces the strength of the recommendation.
