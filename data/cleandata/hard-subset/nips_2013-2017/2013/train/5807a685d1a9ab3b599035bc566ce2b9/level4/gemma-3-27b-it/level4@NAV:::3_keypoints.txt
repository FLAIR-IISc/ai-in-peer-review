- Analyzes local differential privacy with a min-max expected error framework and proves lower bounds for l₂ distance estimation over d-type discrete distributions.  
- Shows randomized response attains the lower bound, proving its optimality in this setting.  
- Extends results to density estimation, where perturbing individual types also meets the lower bound.  
- Strength: matching classic procedures to fundamental lower bounds is a significant and valuable contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Presentation concern: explanation of the min-max bounds needs greater clarity.  
- Notation concern: current notation is cumbersome; suggest consistently expressing all bounds in the l₂ norm.  
- Suggest adding discussion/comparison for the l₁ norm and relevant lower bounds from the broader differential privacy literature (noting differences with non-local settings).  
- Suggest explicitly state that schemes randomizing only individual types are optimal even when users could condition on others’ signals.  
- Suggest broadening scope: derive min-max lower bounds for additional settings beyond the local privacy model.  
- Novelty concern: substantial overlap/extension of FOCS 2013 results (Section 3) reduces the paper’s novelty/support.
