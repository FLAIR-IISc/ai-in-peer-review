- Paper studies minimax bounds under local differential privacy for multinomial probability estimation and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Authors derive sharp minimax convergence rates for both problems.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy effectively reduces sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α.  
- Since α is often a small constant, the effective sample size remains of the same order as in the non-private regime.  
- For density estimation, the classical (non-private) optimal rate is no longer attainable under local privacy.  
- Proofs appear correct and the lower bound for density estimation under privacy seems novel.  
- Main concern: multinomial minimax bound is closely related to prior noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) that are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Because local privacy is stronger than central DP, prior central-model lower bounds may already apply; authors should clarify whether their local bounds strictly improve on or merely recover these central-model bounds.  
- Presentation of the density-estimation comparison is unclear: does the non-private lower bound in Eq. (13) hold for the full Sobolev class or only the trigonometric subclass used in the private lower bound?  
- Fair comparison requires that the non-private lower bound apply to the same trigonometric-basis setting.  
- Rebuttal partially clarified relationships to worst-case bounds, but Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) — which analyzes mean squared error — should also be cited and discussed.  
- Overall: compelling, well-written paper with interesting results; main weakness is limited discussion of closely related noise-complexity lower bounds relevant to the multinomial results.
