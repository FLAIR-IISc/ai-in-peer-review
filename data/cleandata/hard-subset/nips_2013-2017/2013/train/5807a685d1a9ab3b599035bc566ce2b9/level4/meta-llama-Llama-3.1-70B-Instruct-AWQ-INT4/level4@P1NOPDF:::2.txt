This paper explores minimax bounds for probability estimation under the constraint of preserving individual data privacy, focusing on a privacy definition known as local privacy. The authors investigate two probability estimation problems: multinomial estimation and density estimation. In both cases, they establish sharp minimax rates of convergence. For the discrete multinomial estimation problem, they demonstrate that local privacy leads to a reduction in the effective sample size that is quadratic in the privacy parameter alpha. Since alpha is often considered a small constant, the effective sample size is of the same order as in the non-private case. In contrast, for the density estimation problem, the authors show that preserving local privacy makes the optimal rate for the non-private setting unattainable.

Overall, the results are very interesting. As far as I could verify, the proofs are correct. To the best of my knowledge, the minimax bound for density estimation under a privacy constraint has not been previously considered.

My main comment is that the minimax bound for multinomial estimation is closely related to previous works on noise complexity for differential privacy, but this connection is not adequately acknowledged. In particular, two papers address highly relevant problems: Hardt and Talwar, "On the geometry of differential privacy" (STOC, 2010), and De, "Lower bounds in differential privacy" (TCC, 2012). These papers study worst-case lower bounds for the error of linear queries under the constraint of differential privacy. The probability estimation problem considered in this paper is a special case of the linear counting query studied in those papers, and the error measures used are the same L2 metric. The primary difference lies in the fact that this paper considers local differential privacy, whereas Hardt and Talwar, and De consider differential privacy. Since local privacy imposes stronger constraints than differential privacy, lower bounds for differential privacy also serve as lower bounds for local privacy. I wonder if the bound for local privacy presented in this paper improves upon previous bounds for differential privacy.

Regarding the density estimation problem, the statement of the result is somewhat confusing. The authors claim that the lower bound in the local privacy setting is higher than in the non-private setting. However, the minimax bound in this paper is specific to the case where the density can be expanded with a trigonometric basis. I am unclear whether the lower bound for the non-private setting (Equation 13) holds for the general Sobolev space as defined in Definition 1 or only for the special case of a trigonometric basis. A fair comparison is only possible if the non-private lower bound holds for the trigonometric basis.

Additional comments for the rebuttal:
The feedback partially clarifies the relationship to previous worst-case lower bounds. However, note that the work of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang, "The Geometry of Differential Privacy: The Sparse and Approximate Cases" (STOC 2013), also considers Mean Square Error. I suggest that the authors incorporate their explanations and missing references into the paper.

This paper proves sharp minimax bounds for pmf and pdf estimation with a privacy guarantee. The results are interesting, and the paper is well-written. However, there is a lack of mention about known results on the noise complexity lower bound for differential privacy, which are highly relevant to this paper.
