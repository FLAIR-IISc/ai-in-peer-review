- Studies minimax estimation rates for distributions under α-local differential privacy (LDP), linking statistical decision theory with differential privacy.  
- Establishes sharp lower bounds for multinomial and density estimation under LDP, characterizing privacy–statistical efficiency trade-offs.  
- Shows Warner's randomized response achieves optimal rates for multinomial estimation in the considered LDP setting.  
- Derives new minimax rates for density estimation under LDP that differ from classical (non-private) results.  
- Technical quality: rigorous, well-structured proofs with matching upper bounds and efficiently implementable algorithms.  
- Main technical contribution: derivation of sharp minimax rates for both multinomial and density estimation under LDP, with correct population vs. sample estimation distinction.  
- Paper occasionally conflates local differential privacy with standard differential privacy and should clarify the distinction.  
- The restriction to α ≤ 1 in Theorem 1 is unexplained and needs justification given practical relevance around α ≈ 1.  
- Clarity: generally well-written and organized; should explicitly state LDP vs standard DP at the outset and qualify claims of optimality for the specific LDP setting.  
- Originality: LDP framework not entirely novel, but provides a more thorough minimax analysis; density estimation results are particularly novel.  
- Needs stronger comparison to concurrent work [10] and citation/discussion of Lei’s M-estimation [18], including comparison of estimators (orthogonal series vs Lei).  
- Significance: provides fundamental insights into privacy–utility trade-offs with practical implications for privacy-preserving survey design, but would benefit from concrete guidance for real-world parameter choices.  
- Pro-accept: fills theoretical gap, validates classic randomized response in modern LDP, presents new density results, and gives efficient algorithms matching bounds.  
- Con-accept: insufficient clarity between LDP and DP, inadequate related-work comparison, unexplained α ≤ 1 assumption, and limited practical implementation detail.  
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; accept conditional on clarifying LDP vs DP, justifying α restriction, and better contextualizing/contrasting related work.
