- Paper studies minimax bounds for multinomial and density estimation under local differential privacy and establishes sharp convergence rates.
- For multinomial estimation, privacy causes a quadratic reduction in effective sample size (scale by α^2), but this is often manageable when α is a small constant.
- For density estimation, local privacy prevents achieving the optimal non-private convergence rate.
- The paper is generally well-written, proofs appear correct, and this is, to the reviewer’s knowledge, the first work on minimax density estimation under a privacy constraint.
- Primary concern: limited engagement with prior worst-case noise-complexity/lower-bound literature for differential privacy (notably Hardt & Talwar 2010 and De 2012).
- The multinomial minimax bound is closely related to prior lower bounds for linear counting queries; since local DP is stronger than central DP, existing DP lower bounds may already imply similar results for local DP—clarification needed on novelty/improvement.
- Rebuttal only partially clarifies literature connections; the reviewer also recommends acknowledging Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which uses MSE.
- Ambiguity about density-estimation comparison: it’s unclear whether the non-private lower bound in Eq. (13) applies to the whole Sobolev class in Definition 1 or only to the trigonometric-basis subclass—fair comparison requires matching function classes.
- Recommendation: incorporate missing references and more thorough discussion of related differential-privacy noise-complexity/lower-bound work to strengthen the manuscript.
- Overall assessment: valuable, interesting results but needs deeper contextualization with existing DP lower-bound literature.
