- Paper studies estimation under local differential privacy, deriving sharp lower bounds and matching upper bounds for multinomial and density estimation problems.  
- Uses minimax decision theory with Le Cam’s method and Fano’s inequality to obtain information-theoretic bounds.  
- For multinomials, effective sample size reduces roughly from n to n α^2 / d for small α, and randomized response is optimal up to constants.  
- For density estimation over Sobolev classes of smoothness β, the non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades under local DP to (α^2 n)^{-2β/(2β+2)}.  
- Designs practical private estimators: modified randomized response for multinomials, privatized histogram optimal for Lipschitz densities, and a bounded orthogonal-series estimator achieving optimal rates.  
- Clarifies when common mechanisms (e.g., adding Laplace noise to raw data) are suboptimal in the local model.  
- Connects classical randomized response, minimax information techniques, and modern DP literature, focusing on population risk under the local model.  
- Strength: sharp theory with matching lower/upper bounds quantifying privacy–utility trade-offs in the local model.  
- Strength: clearly identifies qualitative differences between local and non-private convergence rates.  
- Strength: provides simple, practical mechanisms that achieve the theoretical bounds.  
- Strength: supplies reusable information-theoretic bounds and clarifies optimality of randomized response.  
- Weakness: presentation sometimes conflates “differential privacy” with “local differential privacy,” risking reader confusion.  
- Weakness: some claims are phrased broadly without emphasizing the local, non-interactive setting.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1’s restriction to small α is not fully discussed; regimes with α≈1 need more attention.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and empirical comparison to alternative mechanisms and related methods (e.g., Lei’s private M-estimators).  
- Weakness: overlap with existing arXiv versions may make incremental contribution appear narrower, especially for density estimation.  
- Suggestion: explicitly distinguish local DP from central DP early and consistently, replacing generic “differential privacy” with “local differential privacy.”  
- Suggestion: state upfront that bounds concern population estimation risk under privatized observations, contrasting with sample-statistic release results.  
- Suggestion: discuss necessity and implications of the small-α assumption, provide constants and crossover behavior for practical α values.  
- Suggestion: provide guidance on permissible loss metrics ρ (L1, L2, TV, ISE) and link analysis to common utilities.  
- Suggestion: expand related-work discussion, comparing to Lei and other local/central DP estimators and clarifying where improvements occur.  
- Suggestion: briefly compare randomized response to other local mechanisms (e.g., Laplace on one-hot, k-ary RR, RAPPOR) to contextualize optimality claims.  
- Suggestion: explain why orthogonal-series scheme avoids the deconvolution bottleneck that makes naive Laplace perturbation suboptimal.  
- Suggestion: add a “model summary” box collecting key definitions and improve notation/readability, with clear constants and dependencies.  
- Pro acceptance: technically strong results, practical mechanisms, and new optimal rates for density estimation under local DP.  
- Con acceptance: presentation/clarity issues, incomplete related-work and empirical context, and perceived overlap with prior arXiv work.  
- Addendum: emphasize population estimation vs. sample-statistic release, add direct comparisons to Lei and other methods, and highlight the paper’s main incremental contributions (minimax local DP exposition and new density bounds).
