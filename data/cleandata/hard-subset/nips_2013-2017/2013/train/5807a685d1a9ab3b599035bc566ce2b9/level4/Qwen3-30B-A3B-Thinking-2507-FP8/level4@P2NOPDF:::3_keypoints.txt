- Paper studies minimax estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) where each user perturbs only their own data.  
- Establishes ℓ₂-error minimax lower bounds for discrete distributions over d types.  
- Shows classic randomized response attains these bounds, proving its optimality in this LDP setting.  
- Extends analysis to density estimation, proving user-specific (non-coordinated) perturbation also achieves minimax rates.  
- Contribution: rigorously establishes sharp minimax rates for LDP and clarifies privacy-utility limits in decentralized collection.  
- Recommendation: accept, but with reservations about presentation and novelty.  
- Exposition criticism: heavy, complex notation obscures core insights; should simplify and consistently emphasize ℓ₂-error.  
- Missing analysis: no ℓ₁ results or comparison to known ℓ₁ lower bounds (e.g., centralized DP), which would contextualize findings.  
- Missing discussion: does not address whether rates extend to related settings (centralized DP or hybrid models).  
- Novelty concern: Section 3’s core lower bound technique appears to directly extend FOCS 2013 work, reducing novelty.  
- Still valuable: density estimation extension and explicit optimality of user-independent perturbation — authors should state "no coordination needed" clearly.
