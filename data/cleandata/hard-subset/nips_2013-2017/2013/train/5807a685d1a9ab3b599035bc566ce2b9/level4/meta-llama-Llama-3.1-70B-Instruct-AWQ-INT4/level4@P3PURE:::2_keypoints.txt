- Paper studies minimax bounds under local privacy for two problems (multinomial and density estimation) and establishes sharp minimax rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces the effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α; when α is small constant, effective sample size matches non-private order.  
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private convergence rate is no longer attainable under local privacy.  
- Results are interesting, proofs appear correct, and the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy seems novel.  
- Main critique: multinomial minimax bound is closely related to prior work on noise complexity for (central) differential privacy but this connection is not mentioned.  
- Specifically relevant prior works: Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012), which give worst-case lower bounds for linear queries (probability estimation is a special case) using the same L2 error metric.  
- Because local privacy is stronger than central DP, lower bounds for central DP also apply to local DP; the reviewer asks whether the paper’s local-DP bound improves on previous DP bounds.  
- For density estimation, the statement is unclear: the paper’s lower bound is derived for densities expandable in a trigonometric basis, so it’s unclear if the non-private lower bound (Eq.13) applies to the general Sobolev class or only the trigonometric case — fair comparison requires matching bases.  
- Rebuttal partially clarified prior-work relations, but the reviewer points out another relevant reference: Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which also considers mean square error.  
- Recommendation: authors should add explanations and the missing references on noise complexity / lower bounds in differential privacy to situate their contributions.  
- Overall: solid, well-written paper proving sharp minimax bounds for pmf and pdf estimation with privacy, but lacking discussion of closely related DP lower-bound literature.
