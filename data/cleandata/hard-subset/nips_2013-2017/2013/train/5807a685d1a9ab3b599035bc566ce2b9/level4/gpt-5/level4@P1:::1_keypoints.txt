- Paper studies minimax estimation under non-interactive α-local differential privacy, deriving sharp lower and matching upper bounds for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.
- For d-dimensional multinomial estimation, effective sample size scales as nα^2/d, giving minimax rates d/(nα^2) (squared error) and d/√(nα^2) (ℓ1); randomized response is optimal up to constants.
- For Sobolev density classes Fβ, local privacy changes the rate exponent: minimax rate becomes (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2), so nonprivate rate n−2β/(2β+1) is unattainable.
- Authors provide private histogram and orthogonal series estimators that are computationally simple and achieve the density estimation lower bounds for all β≥1.
- Simple additive-noise mechanisms (e.g., Laplace on raw samples) are shown to be suboptimal, linked to classical deconvolution lower bounds.
- Paper leverages Le Cam/Fano techniques and novel information-contraction inequalities that may be of independent interest.
- Strength: clear, rigorous minimax formulation under local privacy with tight lower and upper bounds.
- Strength: conceptually clean privacy–accuracy trade-off and demonstration of randomized response optimality for multinomials.
- Strength: novel, sharp nonparametric density estimation results with constructive, efficient mechanisms.
- Weakness: paper sometimes fails to emphasize that results are for the local (non-interactive) model, risking confusion with centralized DP.
- Weakness: Theorem 1 is stated for α∈[0,1] without discussion of whether this restriction is essential or how bounds behave for α≈1.
- Weakness: limited discussion of other loss metrics (Hellinger, TV, KL) and how they fit into the framework.
- Weakness: incomplete comparison to related estimators (e.g., Lei’s private M-estimators) and other practical privatization schemes for discrete data.
- Weakness: lack of discussion on constants, finite-sample regimes, and empirical trade-offs among alternative mechanisms (e.g., Laplace-perturbed counts, Hadamard transforms).
- Quality: technical development is sound; lower and upper bounds match up to constants, and proofs are convincing (supplement assumed complete).
- Clarity: generally clear and organized but should explicitly state population vs. sample targets and emphasize the local vs. centralized DP distinction.
- Originality: main novelty is density estimation rates and matching constructions; multinomial characterization more incremental given prior randomized response work.
- Significance: important contribution clarifying learnability under stringent local privacy and informing practical mechanism design.
- Requests for revision: clarify population vs. sample objectives and local DP model up front; discuss α∈[0,1] necessity and α≈1 behavior; add section on alternative loss choices; compare to Lei and other related methods; contextualize randomized response among other discrete mechanisms.
- Addendum: explicitly distinguish population vs. sample objectives, add comparisons to Lei and other work, and reframe contributions to emphasize new density results and exposition of the local privacy model.
