- Paper uses a LeCam-style argument in the local privacy model and shows randomized response is optimal for a specific task.  
- Main strength: characterizes fundamental limits of learning from locally privatized data.  
- Reviewer did not examine the supplementary material.  
- Commentary in the paper lacks precision and may confuse readers.  
- Paper insufficiently connects and compares its results to existing differential privacy literature.  
- Authors must emphasize the distinction between the local privacy model and the central DP model more clearly.  
- Avoid broad statements implying results transfer beyond the local model (e.g., the remark on page 4 about effects for large n).  
- Unclear whether the restriction on privacy parameter α in Theorem 1 is necessary; practical scenarios often have α ≈ 1.  
- Paper should discuss possible choices for the loss function ρ and their implications.  
- Missing citation/comparison to Lei’s M-estimation density estimator; related work discussion is incomplete.  
- Should compare randomized response’s performance to other discrete-data approaches for context.  
- Clarify the distinction between population and sample statistics early in the paper.  
- After rebuttal: related work (including Lei and recently appeared reference [10]) reduces the novelty—local-model exposition overlaps prior work, while new density-estimation bounds remain the primary novel contribution.
