- Paper studies local differential privacy via minimax risk, deriving sharp l2 minimax lower bounds for estimating discrete distributions and showing matching upper bounds.  
- Shows randomized response (RR) is optimal for the core probability estimation problem.  
- Extends results to density estimation, with per-user perturbation schemes (dependent only on individual types) matching lower bounds.  

- Strength: Clarifies statistical limits of learning under local privacy with sharp minimax rates rather than only order-level guarantees.  
- Strength: Identifies a simple, conceptually clean, and practically relevant optimal mechanism (randomized response).  
- Strength: Demonstrates that fully local, per-user perturbations suffice for density estimation optimality.  
- Strength: Results are timely and relevant at the intersection of statistics, privacy, and learning theory.  

- Weakness: Exposition is overly technical; a higher-level overview of minimax bounds and risk definitions is needed.  
- Weakness: Heavy notation throughout; many statements could be simplified by committing to l2 risk earlier.  
- Weakness: Focuses on l2 error without discussing l1/total-variation risk or how l2 results translate to l1.  
- Weakness: Scope seems tailored to the specific local-privacy model; unclear how techniques extend to shuffle models, communication-limited protocols, or central DP.  
- Weakness: The appealing optimality that users need only randomize based on their own type should be stated explicitly and prominently.  
- Weakness: Relation to recent FOCS 2013 work is unclear—need to delineate novelty (constants, classes, techniques, or framework).  

- Suggestion: Add an intuitive overview of the minimax formulation, the adversary’s role, and dependence of rates on n, d, and α.  
- Suggestion: Consolidate notation around l2 for main theorems and move generalizations to an appendix.  
- Suggestion: Include a short discussion on l1 risk, reductions between l2 and l1, and whether RR is rate-optimal for l1.  
- Suggestion: Make the independence-of-others optimality result explicit in the paper.  
- Suggestion: Expand related work to contrast with central DP lower bounds and contemporaneous FOCS 2013 papers, clarifying conceptual and technical novelties.  

- Overall: Strong paper that pins down achievable rates under local DP with simple mechanisms, but needs clearer exposition and novelty clarification.  
- Recommendation: Accept (borderline-strong).  
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
