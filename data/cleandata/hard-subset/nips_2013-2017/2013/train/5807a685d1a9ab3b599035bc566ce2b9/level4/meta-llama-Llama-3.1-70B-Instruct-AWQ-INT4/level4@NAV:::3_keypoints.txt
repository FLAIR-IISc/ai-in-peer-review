- Studies local privacy with α-differentially private signals and analyzes estimation under a min-max expected error framework.  
- Proves a lower bound for the min-max rate for l2 distance when users are drawn from a d-type discrete distribution.  
- Shows randomized response achieves that l2 lower bound, making it minimax-optimal.  
- For density estimation, proves the lower bound is met by a scheme where each individual perturbs only their own type.  
- Paper is well-written, significant, and of interest to the NIPS community; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Suggests simplifying the explanation of min-max bounds for clarity.  
- Criticizes cumbersome notation and recommends phrasing all bounds explicitly with respect to the l2 norm.  
- Requests discussion/comparison with l1 norm results and other (classical, non-local) differential-privacy lower bounds.  
- Wishes the authors had considered broader settings and provided min-max lower bounds for them.  
- Notes the important observation that although local randomization could depend on the other n−1 users, the simple scheme where each person randomizes only on their own type is optimal and should be stated explicitly.  
- After seeing FOCS 2013 acceptances, views the paper partly as an extension of prior work (especially Section 3), reducing enthusiasm but still recommending acceptance.
