- Paper studies estimation under local privacy, proving Fano/Le Cam lower bounds and showing randomized response is rate-optimal; proposes density estimators (supplement not reviewed).  
- Strength: clear minimax-style lower bounds quantifying the statistical cost of local privacy.  
- Strength: identifies randomized response as rate-optimal and sometimes optimal in a stronger sense.  
- Strength: provides seemingly new, concrete bounds for density estimation under local privacy.  
- Weakness: exposition sometimes conflates local privacy with central differential privacy, risking misleading general statements.  
- Weakness: connections to the broader differential privacy literature are underdeveloped; important related work and contrasts are missing.  
- Weakness: some implication statements are imprecise or too broad (e.g., claim at bottom of page 4).  
- Weakness: Theorem 1’s restriction on alpha is not well motivated and may not address practical regimes (e.g., alpha ~ 1).  
- Weakness: the role of the loss/utility functional rho is not explained clearly across different loss choices.  
- Weakness: density-estimation discussion lacks comparisons to other methods (M-estimation, histogram approaches) and practical considerations.  
- Request 1: Make the local privacy model explicit up front and avoid blanket statements about “differential privacy” unless qualified as local.  
- Request 1 (cont.): Rephrase the bottom-of-page-4 claim to be specific to the local model, problem class, and loss.  
- Request 2: Explicitly distinguish population parameters/risks from sample-level estimators/risks and state what is privatized.  
- Request 3: Add thorough comparison to prior work (e.g., Lei’s M-estimation) and clarify novelty relative to reference [10].  
- Request 3 (cont.): For density estimation, compare estimators to discrete histograms and other local randomizers, discussing constants and practical performance.  
- Request 4: Explain whether the alpha restriction is an artifact of the proof or a fundamental limitation, and discuss the alpha ≈ 1 regime.  
- Request 5: Clarify admissible choices of rho, give concrete examples (squared error, TV, Hellinger), and state any technical conditions required.  
- Request 6: Discuss alternative local mechanisms (k-ary randomizers, task-optimized maps) and when they might match or beat randomized response in constants or adaptivity.  
- Request 7: Provide a high-level outline of the Le Cam/Fano constructions (packing choice, contraction due to privacy, divergence control) and state assumptions near main theorems.  
- Addendum: Add an early clarification of population vs. sample statistics and the role of sample-level privatization.  
- Addendum: Provide a sharper comparison to Lei and other references; acknowledge overlap with [10] and frame contributions primarily as clear exposition of local privacy plus new density-estimation bounds.  
- Overall: Important and valuable work with clear minimax lower bounds and matching upper bounds via randomized response, but needs clearer local-DP framing, stronger engagement with related work, and discussion of practical alpha and rho choices.
