- Paper is a NIPS-formatted arXiv manuscript using a LeCam-style argument to derive lower bounds for estimation when algorithms are untrusted and data is privately held; randomized response is a corollary-optimal strategy under specific conditions.  
- Reviewer did not examine the supplementary material.  
- Pro: Provides a characterization of the limitations of learning from data perturbed for privacy.  
- Con: Exposition contains imprecisions that could mislead casual readers.  
- Con: Connections to existing differential privacy literature are missing.  
- The restriction to local privacy is essential and substantially different from standard differential privacy, and this distinction is not emphasized enough.  
- The paper’s broad statement about the asymptotic effect of providing differential privacy at level α (bottom of p.4) is potentially incorrect and should be avoided.  
- Question whether the restriction on α in Theorem 1 is necessary; practical relevance is unclear since α ≈ 1 often represents a practical limit.  
- The authors should discuss possible choices of the loss/utility measure ρ to clarify how different utilities are captured.  
- The proposed density estimator should be compared to Lei’s M-estimation procedure (not cited).  
- While randomized response attains optimal rates for histograms, the paper omits comparisons with other discrete-data histogram estimation methods.  
- Addendum: Clarify population vs. sample statistic distinctions early in the paper.  
- Addendum: Include comparative analysis with related work (including Lei and references raised by reviewers) for context.  
- Addendum: After examining [10] (now published), the reviewer finds the paper’s contribution narrower—local privacy exposition largely contained in [10], with novelty mainly in a careful exposition and new density estimation bounds.
