Paper establishes minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation.  
For multinomial estimation, authors derive sharp minimax rates showing local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in privacy parameter α (so for small α effective sample size remains comparable to non-private).  
For density estimation, authors show the optimal non-private minimax rate is unattainable under local privacy.  
Results are interesting, proofs appear correct, and this may be the first work to give minimax bounds for density estimation under privacy.  
Multinomial minimax bound is closely related to prior DP noise-complexity lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) using the same L2 metric, which are not adequately cited.  
Because local privacy is stronger than DP, existing DP lower bounds also apply; the paper should clarify whether its local-privacy bounds actually improve on those DP lower bounds.  
Density-estimation comparison is ambiguous: equation (13) is derived for a trigonometric basis, so it’s unclear if the non-private lower bound applies generally to Sobolev spaces as defined or only to that basis—fair comparison requires matching bases.  
Reviewers request citing additional relevant work, e.g., Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on mean-square error in sparse/approximate DP settings.  
Overall assessment: significant and well-written contributions but fails to cite key prior DP noise-complexity lower bounds directly relevant to the multinomial results.
