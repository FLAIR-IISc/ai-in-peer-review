- Paper studies local α-differential privacy with users reporting private signals and analyzes minimax expected ℓ2 estimation error.  
- Establishes tight lower bounds on the minimax rate for ℓ2 estimation over a discrete distribution of d types.  
- Shows randomized response attains the lower bound and is minimax-optimal for the discrete setting.  
- Extends analysis to density estimation, giving a perturbation scheme (perturb only one's own type) that meets the lower bound.  
- Key insight: optimal local privacy mechanisms require each user to randomize based only on their own type (not others' signals).  
- Paper is well-motivated, significant, and of interest to the NIPS community; overall recommendation for acceptance.  
- Suggest simplifying and clarifying minimax bounds notation by explicitly framing everything in the ℓ2 norm.  
- Recommends adding discussion/comparison with ℓ1 norms and existing non-local minimax results, since ℓ1 is more restrictive in DP.  
- Criticism: minimax analysis appears tailored to local privacy; extending to broader settings would increase impact.  
- New reservation: recent FOCS 2013 papers closely relate to Section 3, reducing novelty of that part.  
- Despite related prior work, the results remain valuable and the paper still merits acceptance, albeit less strongly.
