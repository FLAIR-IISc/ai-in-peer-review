- Paper (NeurIPS version of arXiv) uses Fano/Le Cam arguments to derive lower bounds for estimation under local (user-side) differential privacy; shows randomized response is optimal for certain problems.
- Results clearly characterize limits of learning from locally privatized data.
- Lower bounds and optimality of randomized response give useful guidance for algorithm design under local privacy.
- Some commentary is imprecise and may mislead readers about the scope of results.
- Connections to the broader differential privacy literature are underdeveloped.
- Clarify and emphasize the local privacy model and avoid language that conflates local and central differential privacy.
- Explain whether the restriction on privacy parameter α in Theorem 1 is technical or fundamental and discuss behavior around α ≈ 1.
- Provide guidance on choosing the utility parameter ρ and give examples of how different loss/utility measures are handled.
- Compare the proposed density estimator to Lei’s M-estimation approach (cite and contrast assumptions, rates, and implications).
- Discuss alternative algorithms for discrete histogram estimation and how they perform theoretically and empirically relative to the bounds.
- Explicitly distinguish population parameters from sample statistics early in the paper to avoid confusion about what is privatized and estimated.
- Expand the related work discussion to include Lei and additional references raised by reviewers, comparing assumptions, techniques, and results.
- Position the contribution relative to the newly appeared [10], noting overlap in exposition of the local model and novelty mainly in new density-estimation bounds.
