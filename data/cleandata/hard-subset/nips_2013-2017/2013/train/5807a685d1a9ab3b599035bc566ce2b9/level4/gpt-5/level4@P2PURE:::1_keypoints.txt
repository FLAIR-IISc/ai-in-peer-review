- Paper is a NIPS-formatted arXiv manuscript using a Fano/Le Cam-style argument to derive lower bounds for estimators on locally privatized data; randomized response shown optimal in a certain sense (supplement not read).  
- Pro: Results characterize limits of learning from data perturbed to guarantee privacy.  
- Con: Some commentary is imprecise and could mislead casual readers.  
- Con: Missing connections to existing differential privacy literature.  
- Major clarification needed: the paper restricts to local privacy, which is substantially different from central differential privacy and this distinction is not made sufficiently clear.  
- Question on Theorem 1: unclear if the restriction on alpha is necessary, and how the bound applies when alpha is around 1.  
- Request for more guidance on choosing rho to show how different utility measures are captured by the analysis.  
- Missing related work: comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure for density estimation is not discussed.  
- Missing empirical/contextual comparison: other histogram estimation methods for discrete data should be compared to randomized response despite its optimal rate.  
- Addendum: need clearer separation of population quantities versus sample statistics early in the paper.  
- Addendum: comparisons to Lei and other recently noted papers are important for context and are currently lacking.  
- Addendum: after examining [10], the paper’s novel contributions appear mainly to be a careful exposition of the local privacy model and new bounds for density estimation, with some overlap with prior work.
