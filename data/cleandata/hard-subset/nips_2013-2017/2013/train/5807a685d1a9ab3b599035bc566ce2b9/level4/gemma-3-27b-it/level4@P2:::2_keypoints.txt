- Studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation.
- Derives sharp minimax convergence rates for both problems.
- Shows multinomial estimation suffers an effective sample-size reduction quadratic in the privacy parameter α (may be manageable when α is small).
- Shows classical non-private convergence rates for density estimation are unattainable under local privacy.
- Proofs appear sound and the density-estimation minimax bound under local privacy is likely novel.
- Successfully connects ideas from statistical decision theory, differential privacy, and information theory.
- Reviewer requests explicit comparison to existing lower bounds for differential/privacy linear queries (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang 2013).
- Needs clarification whether local-privacy bounds improve upon or merely tighten known central differential privacy bounds.
- Density-estimation results require clarification: lower bound assumes a trigonometric basis—does the non-private rate compared (eq.13) hold under the same basis or only more general Sobolev assumptions?
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, significance, and generally clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing related-work discussion on DP lower bounds and unclear assumptions in the density-estimation comparison.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing related-work comparisons and clarifying density-class assumptions.
