- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation.  
- Establishes sharp minimax rates for both multinomial estimation and density estimation under local privacy.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α, making effective size comparable to non-private case when α is small.  
- For density estimation, local privacy prevents attaining the optimal non-private convergence rate.  
- Proofs appear correct and the density-estimation minimax bound under privacy is likely novel.  
- Main concern: multinomial minimax bound is closely related to existing noise-complexity/lower-bound results for differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) and this connection is not sufficiently acknowledged.  
- Key distinction is that this work uses local differential privacy (stronger constraint), so it should clarify whether the local-privacy bound improves on standard differential-privacy bounds.  
- Clarity issue for density estimation: the presented lower bound is derived for trigonometric basis expansions, so it is unclear if the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) holds for the whole Sobolev space in Definition 1 or only the trigonometric case.  
- Recommend ensuring fair comparisons by applying the non-private lower bound specifically to the trigonometric basis used.  
- Additional missing related work: include discussion of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on mean square error and other relevant worst-case lower bounds.  
- Overall: interesting, well-written, and technically sound work, but needs a more thorough discussion of prior differential-privacy lower bounds and incorporation of the missing references.
