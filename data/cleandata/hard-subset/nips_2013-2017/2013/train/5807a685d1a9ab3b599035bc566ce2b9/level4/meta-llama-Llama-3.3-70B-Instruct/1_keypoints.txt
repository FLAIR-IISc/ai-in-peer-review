- Adapts an ArXiv paper and uses a Fano/LeCam-style argument to derive lower bounds for estimation under local privacy, with randomized response optimal in some regimes.  
- Review based only on the main manuscript; supplementary materials were not considered.  
- Strength: provides a characterization of the limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed (local-private) data.  
- Weakness: manuscript commentary contains imprecisions that could confuse casual readers.  
- Weakness: lacks connections to the differential privacy literature, reducing contextual clarity and relevance.  
- Crucial point: the paper focuses on local privacy, which is materially different from differential privacy; statements implying general DP guarantees (e.g., bottom of p.4) may be misleading.  
- Question: necessity of the restriction on α in Theorem 1 is unclear, especially given experimental suggestions that α ≈ 1 may be a practical DP limit.  
- Suggestion: include discussion of possible choices of ρ to clarify how different utility measures are captured.  
- Suggestion: compare the method to Lei’s M-estimation procedure, which is relevant but currently unreferenced.  
- Suggestion: compare the proposed density/histogram estimator to other algorithms for discrete data in terms of convergence rates.  
- Addendum: clarify the distinction between population and sample statistics at the outset for reader comprehension.  
- Addendum: incorporate comparisons with related works (including Lei and others mentioned by reviewers) to better situate the contribution.  
- Addendum: after reviewing [10], the paper’s novel contribution is narrower—new density-estimation bounds and a careful exposition of local privacy, while much exposition overlaps prior work, making the overall contribution valuable but more limited than initially presented.
