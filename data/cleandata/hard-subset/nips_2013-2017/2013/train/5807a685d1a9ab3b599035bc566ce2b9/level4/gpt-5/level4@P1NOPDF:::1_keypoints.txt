- Paper: NIPS-formatted arXiv version using Fano/Le Cam arguments to derive lower bounds for estimators on privatized data with untrusted algorithms; shows randomized response is optimal in a certain sense.
- Reviewer did not read the supplementary material.

- Pro: Characterizes fundamental limitations of learning from data perturbed for privacy.

- Con: Some commentary is imprecise and may confuse readers.
- Con: Missing connections to existing differential privacy literature.

- Main methodological caveat: Results assume local privacy; the distinction from central differential privacy must be made explicit and broad statements avoiding conflation should be removed.

- Theorem 1: Reviewer questions whether the restriction on α is necessary and notes that α≈1 is common in practice, so implications are unclear for that regime.

- Suggestion: Provide discussion of possible choices of ρ to show how different utility measures are captured.

- Related work gap: Compare the proposed density estimator to Lei’s M-estimation (missing citation) and, if possible, empirical comparisons.

- Benchmarking: Although randomized response attains the optimal rate, compare other histogram/discrete-data estimation algorithms both theoretically and empirically.

- Addendum: Clarify the distinction between population quantities and sample-based statistics early in the paper.

- Addendum: Provide a more comprehensive related-work comparison including Lei and other papers raised by reviewers.

- Addendum conclusion: Contribution is twofold—(i) careful exposition of the local privacy model (largely contained in prior work [10]), and (ii) new bounds for density estimation (novel).
