- Studies local privacy where each user produces an α-DP signal (possibly depending on other users) and analyzes it via minimax expected error.  
- Proves a lower bound on the minimax rate for ℓ2-distance estimation with users from a discrete distribution over d types.  
- Shows randomized response achieves the ℓ2 lower bound, establishing its optimality.  
- For density estimation, a per-user perturbation scheme (each perturbs only their own type) meets the minimax lower bound.  
- Paper is well-written, important, and of interest to the community; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Suggests simplifying the explanation of minimax bounds for clarity.  
- Notes cumbersome notation; recommends phrasing bounds explicitly in the ℓ2 norm since all results use it.  
- Requests comment or comparison to ℓ1-norm bounds and to other differential-privacy lower bounds (typically non-local).  
- Wishes the authors had treated broader settings and provided minimax lower bounds beyond the local-privacy model.  
- Recommends explicitly stating that independent per-user randomization (ignoring other users) is optimal.  
- New reservation: perceives overlap/extension of a FOCS 2013 paper (especially Section 3), reducing enthusiasm though still supporting acceptance.
