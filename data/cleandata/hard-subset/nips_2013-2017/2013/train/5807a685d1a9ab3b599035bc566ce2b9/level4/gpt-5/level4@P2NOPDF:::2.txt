This paper investigates minimax risk for distribution estimation under a local privacy constraint. The authors consider two settings—multinomial probability mass function estimation and nonparametric density estimation—and establish sharp minimax rates in both. For the multinomial case, they show that local privacy effectively reduces the sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α; since α is often treated as a constant, the rate remains the same order as in the non-private case. For density estimation, they prove that the optimal non-private rate cannot be achieved under local privacy.

The results are interesting and, as far as I can tell, the proofs are sound. To my knowledge, the minimax bounds for density estimation under privacy constraints have not been previously established.

However, the connection to prior work on noise complexity and lower bounds in differential privacy is underdeveloped. In particular, Hardt and Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012) analyze worst-case mean-squared error for linear counting queries under (central) differential privacy. The multinomial estimation problem studied here is a special case of those linear queries, and the risk is measured in the same L2 metric. The key difference is the privacy model: this paper imposes local differential privacy, which is strictly stronger than central differential privacy. Consequently, lower bounds proved for central DP also apply to the local setting. It would be helpful to clearly state whether the multinomial lower bounds in this paper strictly improve upon, or simply recover, the dependence on problem parameters (dimension, sample size, α) known from those earlier works. If there is an improvement, please make it explicit; if the results are incomparable due to modeling differences, clarify the precise distinctions.

Regarding the density estimation results, the presentation could be clearer. The paper argues that the local-privacy lower bound exceeds the non-private benchmark, but the minimax analysis is carried out for densities expandable in a trigonometric basis. It is unclear whether the non-private lower bound in equation (13) is stated for the general Sobolev class in Definition 1 or specifically for the trigonometric subclass used in the local-privacy analysis. A fair comparison requires that the non-private lower bound be stated for the same function class (i.e., the trigonometric basis). Please clarify the scope of equation (13) and, if needed, provide the corresponding non-private bound for the trigonometric subclass.

Additional comment following the rebuttal: the feedback partially clarifies the relationship to worst-case lower bounds, but the paper should also reference and discuss the results of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang, “The Geometry of Differential Privacy: The Sparse and Approximate Cases” (STOC 2013), which also study mean-squared error. I recommend adding these citations and a more detailed comparison that positions the current contributions with respect to prior lower bounds for linear queries, highlighting what is new in the local model and in the nonparametric setting.

In summary, the paper provides sharp minimax bounds for pmf and pdf estimation under local privacy, with results that are both novel and technically solid. The exposition is generally clear. The main weakness is the limited discussion of closely related lower bounds in the differential privacy literature, especially for linear queries and L2 risk. Strengthening the related-work section and clarifying the basis-vs.-Sobolev comparison in the density results would significantly improve the paper.
