- Paper studies local differential privacy via minimax expected error for estimating distributions from α-DP user reports.  
- Proves a lower bound on minimax rate for l2 distance estimation over d-type discrete distributions.  
- Shows randomized response attains the lower bound, establishing its optimality in this setting.  
- Extends analysis to density estimation and presents a per-user perturbation scheme that also meets the minimax lower bound.  
- Key observation: although users may condition on others' signals, the optimal scheme has each user randomize independently based only on their own type.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting the results are valuable and of interest to the NIPS community.  
- Criticism: exposition of minimax bounds is not sufficiently accessible and notation is cumbersome.  
- Suggestion: frame bounds directly in the l2 norm (since all bounds use it) and explicitly discuss l1-norm comparisons or existing l1 lower bounds.  
- Suggestion: broaden scope by exploring minimax lower bounds in additional or more general privacy settings.  
- Concern: related/overlapping results from recent FOCS 2013 work (Section 3) make this paper more of an extension, reducing enthusiasm.
