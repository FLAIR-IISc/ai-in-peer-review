This paper derives minimax bounds for probability estimation under the constraint of local privacy. The authors address two fundamental problems: multinomial estimation and probability density estimation. For both settings, they establish sharp minimax convergence rates.

For the discrete multinomial estimation problem, they demonstrate that local privacy introduces a penalty equivalent to reducing the effective sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter, $\alpha$. The authors note that when $\alpha$ is treated as a small constant, the effective sample size remains of the same order as in the non-private setting. In contrast, for density estimation, they show that the optimal rate of the non-private setting is unattainable under local privacy constraints.

Overall, the paper presents interesting and significant results. The proofs appear to be correct, and to the best of my knowledge, the minimax analysis for density estimation under privacy constraints is novel.

My primary concern is the paper's lack of discussion regarding its connection to prior work on noise complexity in differential privacy, particularly concerning the multinomial estimation problem. Specifically, the results seem closely related to those in Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012). These papers establish worst-case lower bounds on the error of linear queries under the standard (central) model of differential privacy. The multinomial probability estimation problem is a special case of the linear counting queries analyzed in those works. Moreover, both this paper and the prior work use the same $L_2$ error metric. The key distinction is this paper's focus on *local* differential privacy, whereas the aforementioned papers address the *central* model. Since local privacy is a strictly stronger constraint than central differential privacy, any lower bound for the central model also applies to the local model. It would be valuable for the authors to clarify whether their new lower bound for the local model improves upon the existing bounds from the central model. A direct comparison and discussion of this relationship is essential for contextualizing the paper's contribution.

Regarding the density estimation problem, the statement of the main result is somewhat ambiguous. The authors state that the minimax rate under local privacy is strictly worse than in the non-private setting. However, their new bound is derived for the specific class of densities that can be expanded on a trigonometric basis. My question is whether the non-private lower bound cited in Equation (13) applies to the general Sobolev space (as per Definition 1) or if it is also specific to the class of functions with a trigonometric basis. This clarification is crucial, as the comparison is only fair if both the private and non-private bounds are established for the same function class.

**Additional Comments:**
The authors' rebuttal partially clarifies the relationship to previous worst-case lower bounds. However, I would encourage the authors to also consider the work of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which is highly relevant as it also analyzes Mean Squared Error. I strongly recommend that the authors incorporate their rebuttal's explanations and a discussion of these missing references into the final version of the paper.

In summary, this paper establishes sharp minimax bounds for probability mass function (pmf) and probability density function (pdf) estimation under local privacy guarantees. The results are novel and significant, and the paper is generally well-written. However, its primary weakness is the insufficient discussion of highly relevant prior work on lower bounds in differential privacy. This omission diminishes the paper's contribution by not clearly situating it within the existing literature. Addressing this point would substantially strengthen the paper.
