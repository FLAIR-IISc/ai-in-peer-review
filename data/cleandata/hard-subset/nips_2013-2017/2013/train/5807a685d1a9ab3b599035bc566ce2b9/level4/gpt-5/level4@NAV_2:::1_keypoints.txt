- Paper is a NIPS-formatted arXiv submission using Fano/Le Cam arguments to derive lower bounds for estimators under local privacy.  
- Randomized response is shown to be an optimal privatization strategy in a precise sense.  
- Reviewer did not inspect the supplementary material in detail.  
- Strength: illuminates fundamental limits of learning from locally privatized data.  
- Weakness: narrative contains imprecise statements that may mislead casual readers.  
- Weakness: connections to established differential privacy literature are underdeveloped.  
- Important: the local privacy model differs notably from central/standard DP and this distinction should be made explicit to avoid confusion.  
- Authors should avoid broad claims attributing effects to “differential privacy” without qualifying the local model.  
- Question: is the restriction on α in Theorem 1 necessary, since practical regimes often have α ≈ 1?  
- Suggestion: discuss possible choices of the loss/metric ρ to show how different utility criteria fit the analysis.  
- Missing comparison/citation: relate the proposed density estimator to Lei’s private M-estimation approach.  
- Suggestion: briefly compare randomized response to alternative discrete/histogram estimation strategies even if RR attains optimal rate.  
- Addendum: explicitly distinguish population vs. sample statistics early in the paper.  
- Addendum: provide a more thorough related-work discussion, including Lei and other papers raised by reviewers.  
- Addendum: in light of a recent [10], the paper’s novelty is narrower—main contributions are careful exposition of the local privacy model (overlapping with [10]) and new density-estimation bounds (the substantive new contribution).
