- Paper studies local differential privacy in a minimax framework, where users send α-DP signals and the curator estimates underlying distributions.
- Main problems analyzed are multinomial (discrete d-type) estimation and density estimation under local privacy constraints, with l2 risk focus.
- Authors derive sharp lower bounds on minimax rates for multinomial estimation and show randomized response attains these bounds (optimality).
- For density estimation, they derive matching lower bounds and show a per-user perturbation scheme meets the bounds.
- Optimal estimators identified: privatized histogram for Lipschitz densities and orthogonal series estimator for higher smoothness.
- Strength: thorough, rigorous analysis of minimax rates and their relation to local privacy, producing sharp bounds.
- Strength: novel and significant result proving the optimality of randomized response for multinomial estimation.
- Weakness: notation is cumbersome and can hinder readability and comprehension.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other differential privacy lower bounds, especially non-local settings.
- Weakness: exclusive focus on local privacy may limit applicability to other privacy models and settings.
- Recommendation: accept, given originality and significance of results.
- Review score: 8/10, reviewer confidence: 8/10.
