- Studies minimax lower bounds for probability estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), focusing on worst-case expected ℓ2 error.  
- Proves tight minimax rates for estimating discrete distributions over d types.  
- Shows the classic randomized response mechanism attains the lower bound, proving its information-theoretic optimality for ℓ2 estimation.  
- Extends results to density estimation, showing non-coordinated per-user perturbation remains optimal.  
- Frames a clear minimax characterization of the privacy–accuracy trade-off and resolves an open question about optimality of standard LDP techniques.  
- Concern: exposition is dense and notation-heavy (e.g., P, Q, R); could be streamlined by framing results in ℓ2 from the outset (e.g., Θ(√(d/n) + √(d/(n²α²)))).  
- Concern: omission of ℓ1 analysis—authors should discuss implications for ℓ1 (norm equivalence or direct bounds) to better connect with DP literature.  
- Concern: insufficiently explicit connection to prior FOCS 2013 work (Duchi et al.); clarify how this minimax approach differs or generalizes that result.  
- Suggest explicitly stating the practical takeaway that user coordination is unnecessary for optimal ℓ2 estimation under LDP.  
- Overall: technically strong and significant; recommendation to accept with minor clarifications.
