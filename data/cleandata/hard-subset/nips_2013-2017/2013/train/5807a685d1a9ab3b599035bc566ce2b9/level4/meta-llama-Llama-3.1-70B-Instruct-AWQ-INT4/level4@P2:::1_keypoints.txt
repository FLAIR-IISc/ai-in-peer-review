- NIPS-formatted ArXiv manuscript using a LeCam-style argument to derive lower bounds for estimators on privately held data.
- Shows randomized response is optimal in a certain formal sense for private survey sampling.
- Provides detailed study of discrete and continuous distribution estimation under local privacy where the statistician is untrusted.
- Derives sharp minimax convergence rates, quantifying trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency.
- Supplies tools to move along the privacy–efficiency continuum for practitioners.
- Proves lower bounds on convergence rates for any estimator that guarantees local differential privacy.
- Complements lower bounds with matching, computationally efficient upper bounds (achievable algorithms).
- Demonstrates local privacy can substantially degrade estimator quality compared to the nonprivate case.
- Shows classical nonprivate rates are unattainable under local differential privacy for multinomial estimation.
- For density estimation, local privacy causes qualitatively worse convergence rates than a mere multiplicative sample-size loss.
- Achieves optimal density-estimation rates using histogram and orthogonal series estimators, with sharp minimax lower bounds.
- Concludes with discussion of implications and directions for future work.
- Pro: Characterizes fundamental limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed data.
- Pro: Integrates differential-privacy disclosure risk with minimax decision theory for probability estimation.
- Con: Imprecise commentary in places could confuse casual readers.
- Con: Missing connections to existing differential-privacy literature and related results.
- Con: Restriction to local privacy differs substantially from standard differential privacy and may be underemphasized.
- Con: Paper needs clearer explanation of population vs. sample statistics distinction.
- Con: Important comparisons to related work (e.g., Lei and others) are absent.
- Question: Is the restriction on alpha in Theorem 1 necessary, especially since alpha≈1 is relevant in some DP settings?
- Suggestion: Provide commentary on choices of utility measure rho to illustrate captured utility trade-offs.
- Question: How does the proposed density estimator compare to Lei’s M-estimation procedure (not cited)?
- Question: While randomized response is optimal rate-wise, how do other histogram algorithms for discrete data compare in practice?
