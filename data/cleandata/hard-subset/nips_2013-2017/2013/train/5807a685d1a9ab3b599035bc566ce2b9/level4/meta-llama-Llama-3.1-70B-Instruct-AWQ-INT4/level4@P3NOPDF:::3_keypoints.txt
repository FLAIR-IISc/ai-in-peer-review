- Paper studies local α-differential privacy where each user reports a private signal possibly depending on the other n−1 users, analyzed via a min-max expected error framework.

- Provides a lower bound on the min-max rate for l2 distance estimation with users drawn from a discrete distribution over d types.

- Shows randomized response attains that lower bound, proving its optimality for the l2 setting.

- For density estimation, presents a lower bound met by a perturbation scheme where each user perturbs only their own type.

- Paper is well-written, important, and likely of interest to the NIPS community; reviewer recommends acceptance.

- Suggests simplifying the explanation of min-max bounds and streamlining cumbersome notation, emphasizing that bounds apply to the l2 norm.

- Requests commentary or comparison to the l1 norm (more restrictive) and to other differential-privacy lower bounds from classical (non-local) settings.

- Wishes the authors had considered broader settings beyond the local-privacy model and provided corresponding min-max lower bounds.

- Recommends explicitly stating the (perhaps surprising) result that per-user randomization based solely on one’s own type is optimal.

- Notes overlap with a recently published FOCS 2013 paper (especially Section 3); this reduces enthusiasm but reviewer still recommends acceptance.

- Overall: thorough analysis showing matching lower and upper bounds (randomized response); with suggested revisions the paper could be more impactful.
