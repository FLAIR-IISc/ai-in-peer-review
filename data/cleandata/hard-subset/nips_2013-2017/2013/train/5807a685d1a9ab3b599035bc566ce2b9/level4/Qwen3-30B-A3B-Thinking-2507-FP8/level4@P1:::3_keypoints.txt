- Paper derives sharp minimax convergence rates for probability estimation under local differential privacy for multinomial and density settings.  
- Connects Warner's randomized response to modern differential privacy and shows it attains optimal sample complexity for multinomial estimation.  
- Demonstrates density estimation rates degrade from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (n α^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy, indicating a fundamental difference from classical settings.  
- Introduces a formal minimax framework for local privacy, showing effective sample size in multinomial estimation drops from n to n α^2 / d.  
- Proves Warner’s randomized response mechanism achieves the optimal multinomial rate, reviving a classical method as optimal for private surveys.  
- Shows local privacy necessitates different estimation strategies for density estimation and provides matching upper and lower bounds.  
- Proposes histogram and orthogonal series estimators that achieve the optimal private density-estimation rates.  
- Technical contributions are rigorous with thorough proofs and careful treatment of strengths and limitations.  
- Explains why naive Laplace perturbation fails to attain optimal density-estimation rates.  
- Clarity is generally good, but the density section is technically dense and could use more intuitive explanations.  
- Suggests expanding discussion contrasting L2 results with L1 considerations, which are relevant for probability estimation.  
- Originality is strong, notably the novel combination of local privacy minimax rates and the historical link to Warner’s method.  
- Significance is substantial, quantifying privacy–statistical-efficiency trade-offs and offering practical guidance for private estimation.  
- Minor weakness: limited explicit comparison to non-local differential privacy literature and classical non-private density-estimation results.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality theoretical contribution with strong practical implications; recommendation for acceptance.
