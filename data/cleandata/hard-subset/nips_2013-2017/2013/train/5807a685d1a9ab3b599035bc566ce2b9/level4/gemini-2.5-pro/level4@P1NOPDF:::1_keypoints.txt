- Paper derives minimax lower bounds for estimation under local differential privacy using Fano/LeCam arguments and shows randomized response is rate-optimal.
- Review ignores the supplementary material.
- Pro: Provides valuable characterization of fundamental limits of learning with locally privatized data.
- Con: Explanatory text contains imprecisions that may confuse readers.
- Con: Lacks discussion connecting results to broader differential privacy literature.
- The distinction between local and centralized privacy models must be stated explicitly and early to avoid reader confusion.
- The necessity and practical relevance of the restriction on α in Theorem 1 is unclear, since real applications often have α ≈ 1.
- The paper should discuss which loss functions ρ are permissible and how different utility measures fit the analysis.
- The proposed density estimator needs comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure, which is highly relevant but not cited.
- Although randomized response is rate-optimal, comparisons to alternative histogram estimation methods for discrete data are missing.
- After rebuttal: the paper should more clearly distinguish population versus sample statistics upfront.
- After rebuttal: comparison to related work (e.g., Lei and other cited papers) is essential to contextualize contributions.
- After rebuttal: the paper’s main contributions are narrowed to (1) a careful exposition of the local privacy model and (2) novel bounds for density estimation, while the exposition overlaps significantly with reference [10].
