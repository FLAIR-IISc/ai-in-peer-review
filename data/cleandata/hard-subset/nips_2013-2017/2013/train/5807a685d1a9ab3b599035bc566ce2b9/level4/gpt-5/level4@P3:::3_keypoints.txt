- Studies population-level statistical estimation under non-interactive α-local differential privacy (LDP), deriving minimax lower bounds and matching algorithms.
- Shows for multinomial estimation (θ ∈ Δd) under α-LDP the effective sample size drops to about nα^2/d, with sharp ℓ2 and ℓ1 rates (Theorem 1).
- Establishes a variant of Warner’s randomized response as minimax-optimal for multinomial estimation under LDP.
- Shows density estimation over Sobolev class Fβ on [0,1] degrades classical rate n^−2β/(2β+1) to (α^2 n)^−2β/(2β+2) under LDP.
- Constructs private histograms (β=1) and private orthogonal-series estimators (β≥1) that achieve the optimal private nonparametric rates.
- Argues naive additive-noise mechanisms (e.g., Laplace) are suboptimal, linking to deconvolution lower bounds.
- Key technical tool: information-contraction bounds under LDP that control mutual information/KL, used with Le Cam/Fano reductions for sharp lower bounds.
- Situates work within DP, randomized response, and nonparametric minimax literatures, noting overlap with authors’ prior FOCS 2013/arXiv results.
- Technical soundness is strong: standard testing techniques plus privacy-induced information bounds give convincing matched upper and lower bounds.
- Completeness: both ℓ2 and ℓ1 multinomial results and tailored density mechanisms are treated with appropriate proofs and references to longer version.
- Clarity is generally good but notation-heavy; paper would benefit from earlier high-level intuition and streamlined background.
- Originality: sharp integration of LDP with population minimax analysis and novel private orthogonal-series mechanism; however parts overlap prior work and need clearer delineation of new contributions.
- Significance: quantifies privacy–utility trade-offs sharply, validates randomized response, and shows privacy can change convergence exponents in nonparametric settings.
- Pros: sharp bounds with efficient mechanisms, clear interpretation (effective sample size), novel practical density mechanisms, strong theoretical framing.
- Cons/suggestions: clarify novelty vs FOCS 2013, discuss potential gains from interactive mechanisms, reduce notation and add high-level proof outlines, and optionally provide practical guidance or small simulations.
- Recommendation: Accept, subject to clarifying novelty and improving exposition.
