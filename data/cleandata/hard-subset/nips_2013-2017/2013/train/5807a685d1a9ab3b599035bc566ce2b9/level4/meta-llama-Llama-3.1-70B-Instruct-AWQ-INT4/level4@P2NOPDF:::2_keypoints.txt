- Paper studies sharp minimax rates for probability estimation under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation problems.  
- Shows local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α for multinomial estimation.  
- Notes that because α is typically small, the effective sample size remains the same order as the non-private case in multinomial estimation.  
- Shows the non-private optimal rate is unattainable for density estimation under local privacy.  
- Results are interesting, proofs appear correct, and the density-estimation privacy minimax bound seems novel.  
- Reviewer requests connection to prior differential-privacy “noise complexity” lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012), since the multinomial problem is a special case of linear counting queries with L2 error.  
- Asserts lower bounds for (central) differential privacy also apply to local privacy and asks whether the paper’s local-privacy bounds improve upon those previous DP bounds.  
- Points out additional relevant prior work (Nikolov, Talwar, Zhang 2013) that considers mean square error and should be cited/discussed.  
- For the density result, reviewer finds the statement confusing and asks whether the non-private lower bound (equation 13) holds for the general Sobolev space or only for the trigonometric-basis case.  
- Requests the authors clarify basis-specific assumptions so comparisons between private and non-private rates are fair.  
- Recommends the authors add the missing references and a discussion of how their local-privacy bounds relate to existing worst-case lower bounds in the differential-privacy literature.
