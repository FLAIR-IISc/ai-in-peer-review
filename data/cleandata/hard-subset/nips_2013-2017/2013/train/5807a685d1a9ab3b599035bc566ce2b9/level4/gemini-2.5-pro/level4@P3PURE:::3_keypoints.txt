- Studies estimation under local differential privacy using a minimax-risk framework where each user's α-DP signal may depend on all users' types.  
- Main result: a sharp lower bound on the minimax rate for discrete probability estimation under l2 loss, matched by randomized response, proving its optimality.  
- Provides a similar optimality result for density estimation via a local perturbation scheme.  
- Key insight: although reports can depend on other users, the optimal scheme randomizes only on each user's own type — this should be emphasized.  
- Reviewer verdict: valuable theoretical contribution and recommended for acceptance.  
- Clarity/presentation concern: minimax explanation and notation are cumbersome; suggest simplifying and focusing exposition on the l2 norm.  
- Scope/prior-work concern: analysis restricted to l2 — recommend discussion of l1 norms and connections to other DP lower bounds (including non-local settings).  
- Generality concern: authors should discuss whether their techniques extend to broader privacy settings and more general minimax bounds.  
- Related work note: a related FOCS 2013 paper makes parts of the submission (notably Section 3) seem like an extension, slightly reducing perceived novelty.
