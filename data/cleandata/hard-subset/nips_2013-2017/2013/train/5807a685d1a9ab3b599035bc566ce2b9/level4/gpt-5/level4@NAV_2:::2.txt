This paper investigates minimax bounds for estimating probability distributions when individual data must remain private from the analyst. The authors adopt the local differential privacy model and analyze two problems: multinomial probability estimation and nonparametric density estimation. In both cases, they establish sharp minimax convergence rates. For the discrete multinomial setting, they show that local privacy effectively reduces the sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter alpha. Since alpha is often treated as a small constant, the effective sample size remains of the same order as in the non-private regime. For density estimation, they demonstrate that the classical optimal rate without privacy is no longer attainable once local privacy is imposed.

Overall, the results are compelling. The proofs appear correct to the extent I checked. To my knowledge, the minimax lower bound for density estimation under privacy constraints has not previously been established.

My main concern is that the multinomial minimax bound is closely related to prior work on noise complexity for differential privacy, which is not sufficiently discussed. In particular, Hardt and Talwar (On the Geometry of Differential Privacy, STOC 2010) and De (Lower Bounds in Differential Privacy, TCC 2012) analyze worst-case lower bounds for the error of linear queries under differential privacy. The probability estimation task here is a special case of the linear counting queries studied in those papers, and the error is measured in the same L2 norm. The key difference is that this paper assumes local differential privacy, whereas Hardt–Talwar and De consider the central model. Because local privacy imposes a stronger constraint than central differential privacy, their lower bounds automatically apply here as well. It would be helpful if the authors clarified whether their local privacy bounds strictly improve on, or merely recover, the previous central-model bounds in this special case.

Regarding density estimation, the presentation of the result could be clearer. The paper states that the local-privacy lower bound is larger than the non-private bound, but the minimax bound here is proved for densities expandable in the trigonometric basis. Does the non-private lower bound in equation (13) hold for the full Sobolev class in Definition 1 or only for the trigonometric subclass? The comparison is fully fair only if the non-private lower bound also applies to the trigonometric-basis setting used for the private lower bound.

Additional comments to the rebuttal:
The response partially clarified the relationship to worst-case lower bounds. However, Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (The Geometry of Differential Privacy: The Sparse and Approximate Cases, STOC 2013) also analyze mean squared error and should be cited and discussed. I recommend the authors incorporate these explanations and the missing references into the paper.

In summary, the paper establishes sharp minimax bounds for pmf and pdf estimation under local privacy, presenting interesting results in a well-written manuscript. The main weakness is the limited discussion of closely related noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy that are highly relevant to the multinomial results.
