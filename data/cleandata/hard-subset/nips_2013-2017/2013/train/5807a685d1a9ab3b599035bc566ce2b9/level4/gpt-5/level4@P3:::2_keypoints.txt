- Paper studies probability estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) and derives α-private minimax risks for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation.  
- Uses Le Cam/Fano lower bounds with novel LDP-tailored divergence contractions and gives matching computationally efficient mechanisms.  
- Multinomial results: MSE ~ min{1, d/(nα^2)} and ℓ1 error ~ min{1, d/√(nα^2)}, implying effective sample size n → nα^2/d under LDP.  
- Shows a variant of randomized response with projection to the simplex is minimax-optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Density estimation results: non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} is unattainable under LDP; optimal LDP rate is (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}.  
- Provides matching mechanisms for density estimation: privatized histogram (β=1) and orthogonal series estimator with tailored privatization for general β≥1.  
- Demonstrates additive Laplace noise to raw data is suboptimal for LDP via deconvolution arguments.  
- Connects classical randomized response to modern DP and minimax decision theory, complementing global DP and LDP literature.  
- Technical soundness: lower and upper bounds are clean, implementable, and rate-sharp according to proofs.  
- Completeness: matching bounds and implementability discussed, though some constants and regime transitions need more explicit treatment.  
- Evaluation: theory-backed proofs are provided; practical constants and regime-switching thresholds could be clarified for practitioners.  
- Clarity issues: need to explicitly align the Sobolev class used in lower bounds (periodic/trigonometric) with the non-private baseline in Eq. (13).  
- Clarity issues: explicitly state dependence on d and α for multinomial results and discuss common regimes (fixed d, growing d, small α).  
- Prior-work positioning: paper should better relate multinomial LDP lower bounds to global DP linear query lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov et al. 2013).  
- Originality: density estimation under LDP and the changed polynomial exponent with matching mechanisms are novel; multinomial LDP minimaxization and randomized response optimality are valuable.  
- Significance: provides clear privacy–utility tradeoffs, simple optimal mechanisms, and shows limits of naive additive-noise approaches under LDP.  
- Specific suggestions: clarify whether LDP multinomial bounds strictly strengthen or are incomparable to global DP bounds and explain where gains arise.  
- Specific suggestions: add citations/discussion for Hardt & Talwar (STOC’10), De (TCC’12), Nikolov/Talwar/Zhang (STOC’13), Kasiviswanathan et al., and Smith (STOC’11).  
- Specific suggestions: explicitly state the Sobolev/trigonometric periodicity in Section 4 and align Eq. (13) with that class (cite Tsybakov).  
- Specific suggestions: give an intuitive explanation of the privatization scheme in Section 4.3 (geometry of randomized sign mechanism) and summarize constants and computational costs.  
- Pros: sharp minimax bounds, simple optimal mechanisms, identification of changed nonparametric rates, and strong, broadly applicable proof techniques.  
- Cons: limited discussion of related global DP lower bounds and minor clarity issues about the function class and practical regimes.  
- Recommendation: strong accept, conditional on adding requested references and clarifications.
