- Paper studies sharp minimax rates for probability estimation under local (individual) privacy for multinomial and density estimation problems.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces the effective sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α; if α is small constant, sample-size order remains similar to non-private case.  
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private convergence rate is no longer achievable under local privacy.  
- Proofs appear correct and the density-estimation minimax bound under privacy is novel to the reviewer’s knowledge.  
- Primary omission: close relation of the multinomial minimax bound to prior noise-complexity / lower-bound work in differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) is not acknowledged.  
- The multinomial probability estimation is a special case of linear counting queries studied in DP literature, with the same L2 error metric.  
- Because local privacy is stronger than (central) differential privacy, DP lower bounds are also relevant and should be discussed; question whether the paper’s local-privacy bounds improve on known DP bounds.  
- Missing reference: Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on geometry of DP and mean square error should be cited and discussed.  
- Density-estimation claim is confusing: the lower bound proven is for densities expanded in a trigonometric basis, so it’s unclear if the non-private lower bound (eq.13) applies to the general Sobolev class or only that basis.  
- Fair comparison requires showing the non-private lower bound holds for the same (trigonometric) basis used in the private setting.  
- Overall: paper is interesting and well-written but needs to incorporate discussion and citations of related DP noise-complexity / worst-case lower-bound results.
