- Paper analyzes minimax bounds under local privacy for multinomial (pmf) and density (pdf) estimation, establishing sharp minimax convergence rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy effectively reduces sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α; with α treated as a small constant, effective sample size remains same order as non-private.  
- For density estimation, enforcing local privacy prevents achieving the optimal non-private convergence rate.  
- Proofs appear correct and the density-estimation minimax bound under privacy is novel to the reviewer’s knowledge.  
- Main concern: insufficient discussion of related work on noise complexity in (central) differential privacy—Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012)—which study worst-case lower bounds for linear queries measured in L2 and are closely related.  
- Clarify whether the paper’s local-privacy lower bounds improve upon or relate to prior central-DP lower bounds, since central-DP bounds also apply (a fortiori) to local privacy.  
- Presentation confusion in density section: unclear if the non-private lower bound in eq. (13) holds for the full Sobolev space or only the trigonometric-basis subclass; fair comparison requires matching settings.  
- Rebuttal partially clarifies the prior-work connection but omits Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which also analyzes mean-squared error and should be cited and discussed.  
- Overall: interesting and well-exposed results proving sharp minimax bounds for pmf and pdf under privacy, but needs stronger acknowledgement and integration of existing DP lower-bound literature.
