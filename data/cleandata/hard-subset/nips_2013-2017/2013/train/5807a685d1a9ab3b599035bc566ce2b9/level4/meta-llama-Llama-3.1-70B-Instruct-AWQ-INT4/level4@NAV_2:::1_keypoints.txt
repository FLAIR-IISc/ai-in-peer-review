- Paper derives Fano/LeCam-style lower bounds for estimation under local (untrusted-algorithm) privacy and shows randomized response is optimal in some settings.
- Provides sharp minimax convergence rates for discrete and continuous distribution estimation under local privacy, highlighting privacy–efficiency trade-offs.
- Main contributions: characterization of learning limitations with perturbed data and demonstration of randomized response optimality.
- The manuscript sometimes conflates local privacy with (central) differential privacy, a distinction that may confuse readers.
- Overbroad statements (e.g., bottom of p.4 about "providing differential privacy") should be avoided or qualified.
- The restriction on the privacy parameter α in Theorem 1 may be unnecessary or unclear, especially given empirical suggestions that α ≈ 1 is realistic.
- The authors should give guidance on choices of the utility measure ρ to illustrate how different utilities are captured by the analysis.
- A comparison to related work (notably Lei’s M-estimation procedure) is missing and would provide useful context.
- The proposed density estimator reportedly compares favorably to Lei’s M-estimator and this comparison should be explicitly discussed.
- For discrete histogram estimation, randomized response attains optimal rates, but comparisons to other histogram algorithms are needed.
- Overall the paper is a valuable contribution but needs clarifications and additional comparisons to related work.
- Pros: characterizes limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed data.
- Cons: imprecise commentary that may mislead readers; missing connections to existing differential-privacy results.
- Additional requested clarifications: explicit population vs. sample statistic distinction early in the paper.
- Addendum: after rebuttal, the reviewer notes overlap with [10]; the paper’s distinct contributions are careful exposition of the local privacy model and new bounds for density estimation.
