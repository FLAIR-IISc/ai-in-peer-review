- Paper studies minimax bounds under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation and proves sharp minimax convergence rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces the effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α, so for small constant α the effective sample size is same order as non-private.  
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private convergence rate is no longer attainable under local privacy.  
- Results are interesting, paper is well-written, proofs appear correct, and the density-estimation minimax bound under privacy appears novel.  
- Related-work concern: multinomial minimax bounds closely relate to noise-complexity / linear-query lower bounds in Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012), which study L2 error for (central) differential privacy.  
- Clarification requested whether the paper’s local-privacy bounds improve on or differ from existing (central) DP lower bounds, since DP lower bounds also apply to the stronger local-privacy model.  
- Density-estimation concern: the presented lower bound is for densities expanded in a trigonometric basis—need clarification whether the non-private lower bound (eq.13) holds for the general Sobolev space in Definition 1 or only for the trigonometric case to ensure a fair comparison.  
- Suggest adding missing references and explanations, notably Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on geometry of differential privacy and mean square error.  
- Overall positive recommendation but requests clearer relation to prior DP work and additional contextualization of results.
