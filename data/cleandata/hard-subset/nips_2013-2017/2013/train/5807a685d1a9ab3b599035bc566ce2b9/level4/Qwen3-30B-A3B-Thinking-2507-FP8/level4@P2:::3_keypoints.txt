- Establishes sharp minimax convergence rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation under α-local differential privacy.  
- Shows Warner's 1965 randomized response is minimax-optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Demonstrates effective sample size for multinomial estimation shrinks from n to nα²/d under α-local privacy.  
- Proves density estimation rate degrades from n⁻²β/(2β+1) (classical) to (nα²)⁻²β/(2β+2) under local privacy.  
- Proposes practical estimators (histogram and orthogonal series) that achieve the new minimax rates for densities.  
- Provides tight, well-structured upper and lower bounds with technically sound proofs.  
- Shows simple mechanisms (randomized response) suffice; naive Laplace perturbation of samples is suboptimal.  
- Connects classical randomized response literature to modern differential privacy and minimax theory (strong originality).  
- Results are significant and practically relevant for privacy-sensitive data collection (surveys, health, etc.).  
- Clarity is generally good, but some technical details are dense and could be made more accessible.  
- Lacks sufficient intuition for why the density exponent changes from 2β/(2β+1) to 2β/(2β+2).  
- Limited discussion of ℓ₁ loss, which is often more relevant for probability estimation.  
- Could more explicitly contrast local privacy with the more commonly studied centralized privacy setting.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify density-rate intuition and briefly address ℓ₁ loss.
