- Paper establishes minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation.  
- For multinomial estimation, authors derive sharp minimax rates showing local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in α, yet for small α the effective sample size is comparable to non-private.  
- For density estimation, authors show the optimal non-private rate is unattainable under local privacy; the minimax bound is a novel contribution.  
- Proofs appear correct and results are significant.  
- Primary concern: multinomial lower bound closely relates to prior noise-complexity/linear-query lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012); authors must clarify whether their local-privacy bound improves on or simply inherits these DP bounds.  
- Since local differential privacy is stronger, standard DP lower bounds may apply, so the novelty/strength of the multinomial contribution needs explicit differentiation.  
- Density-estimation presentation unclear: unclear if the non-private lower bound (eq.13) applies to the general Sobolev space of Definition 1 or only to the trigonometric-basis case used in the privacy analysis.  
- A fair comparison requires the non-private bound to be established in the same trigonometric-basis framework as the private analysis.  
- Rebuttal missed discussing Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013); authors should explicitly address this related work and its relevance.  
- Overall: interesting and well-written work, but omission of key prior DP noise-complexity results and basis-specific clarification for density comparison weaken the novelty claim; add references and clarifications.
