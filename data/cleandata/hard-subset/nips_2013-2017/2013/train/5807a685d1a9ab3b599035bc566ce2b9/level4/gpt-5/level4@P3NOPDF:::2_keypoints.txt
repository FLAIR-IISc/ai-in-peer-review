- Paper studies minimax bounds under local privacy for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation, establishing sharp convergence rates in both settings.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α; for constant α, rates match non-private order up to constants.  
- For density estimation, the classical non-private optimal rate is unattainable under local privacy; the paper gives a clean characterization of the degraded optimal rate.  
- Strength: questions are fundamental and timely, of interest to statistics and privacy communities.  
- Strength: density-estimation results appear novel and technically sound; proofs and presentation are generally clear.  
- Concern: multinomial result closely relates to central-DP lower bounds for linear counting queries (Hardt–Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov–Talwar–Zhang 2013), so prior work must be explicitly compared.  
- Request: explicitly map multinomial estimation to linear counting queries and align the L2 error metrics.  
- Request: compare dependence on α (ε), dimension, and n to the bounds in the cited central-DP papers and state whether new lower bounds are strictly stronger in the local model.  
- Request: clarify whether the novelty is stronger bounds in the local model or primarily statistical framing/sharp constants and matching upper bounds.  
- Clarify density-estimation function class: specify whether non-private lower bound (Eq.13) holds for the exact trigonometric (Fourier) subclass used in the private lower bound or only for the broader Sobolev class.  
- Request: state/prove the non-private lower bound for the same trigonometric/Sobolev subclass or add a citation showing equivalence for fair comparison.  
- Requests for manuscript improvements: include Nikolov–Talwar–Zhang (STOC 2013) in related work and explain its MSE relation; make relationship between α and ε explicit and use consistent notation.  
- Request: for multinomial estimation, discuss optimal mechanisms (e.g., randomized response variants) achieving upper bounds and how they match lower bounds up to constants/log factors.  
- Request: for density estimation, explicitly state (i) smoothness assumptions, (ii) precise basis and truncation scheme used, and (iii) whether adaptivity to unknown smoothness is possible under local privacy.  
- Request: comment on a possible privacy–smoothness trade-off: whether smoother classes reduce the gap to non-private rates or if the qualitative separation persists.  
- Overall evaluation: strong, interesting, technically solid paper with novel density results; main weakness is incomplete positioning relative to prior DP lower bounds for linear queries, which should be addressed for acceptance.
