- Paper adapts an ArXiv manuscript and uses Fano/Le Cam arguments to derive lower bounds for estimation when the algorithm is untrusted; randomized response emerges as optimal in some cases.  
- Provides a detailed study of discrete and continuous distribution estimation under local privacy, giving sharp minimax rates and explicit privacy–statistical-efficiency trade-offs.  
- Integrates differential-privacy formalism with minimax decision theory to produce lower bounds and matching, computationally efficient upper bounds.  
- Well-organized and clearly written with a thorough introduction to local privacy, the minimax framework, and clear presentation of technical results and implications.  
- Contains imprecise, overly broad statements (e.g., about effects “for suitably large n”) that could mislead casual readers and should be tightened.  
- The local-privacy restriction makes the model materially different from standard differential privacy; the paper should better clarify these connections and distinctions.  
- Needs more discussion of possible choices of the loss/utility measure ρ to show how different utilities are captured by the analysis.  
- Should compare the proposed density estimator to Lei’s M-estimation procedure (Lei not cited).  
- Should compare results to other histogram-estimation approaches for discrete data.  
- Should clarify the distinction between population vs. sample statistics and how that affects the results.
