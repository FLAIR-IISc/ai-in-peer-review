- Paper studies minimax bounds under α-local differential privacy for multinomial probability estimation and nonparametric density estimation, giving matching lower and upper bounds.  
- Multinomial result: local privacy effectively reduces sample size to nα^2/d (for small α); mechanisms (including randomized response variants) achieve the rates.  
- Density result: non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} is unattainable under local privacy; optimal private rate is (α^2 n)^{-2β/(2β+2)}, achieved by histogram and orthogonal series estimators.  
- Proofs appear correct and the density minimax bounds under local privacy are novel.  
- Strength: Clear, rigorous minimax analysis with tight lower and upper bounds under local privacy.  
- Strength: Conceptually clean information-theoretic framework (Le Cam/Fano) adapted to the local privacy setting.  
- Strength: Identification and demonstration of optimal mechanisms, notably randomized response for multinomial estimation.  
- Strength: Shows a genuine change in minimax exponent for nonparametric density estimation and supplies matching procedures.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to prior global-DP lower bounds for linear/counting queries (Hardt–Talwar 2010, De 2012, Nikolov–Talwar–Zhang 2013), which are relevant to multinomial/L2 error.  
- Weakness: Need to clarify whether the non-private rate (Eq. 13) is stated for the same Sobolev class used in the private lower bound (trigonometric basis vs. general elliptical Sobolev).  
- Weakness: Multinomial effective-sample-size statement should more clearly emphasize dependence on d (nα^2/d) to avoid misinterpretation.  
- Quality: Technical soundness high; lower bounds use controlled information-theoretic arguments and upper bounds are constructive and efficient.  
- Clarity: Generally well written; density section needs clearer alignment of function class statements (trigonometric vs general Sobolev).  
- Originality: Density estimation results and changed minimax exponent under local privacy are new; multinomial analysis extends known DP ideas to local setting with sharp rates.  
- Significance: High — provides quantitative privacy–accuracy trade-offs and identifies optimal mechanisms, relevant to theory and practice.  
- Suggestion: Add a dedicated subsection comparing multinomial bounds to Hardt–Talwar (2010), De (2012), and Nikolov–Talwar–Zhang (2013), and state whether local-privacy bounds are strictly stronger.  
- Suggestion: In Section 4, explicitly state that the non-private rate (13) applies to the same Sobolev class induced by the trigonometric basis or adjust the comparison.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly discuss the role of d in the multinomial rate to show the potential severity of degradation.  
- Reasons to accept: New sharp minimax results under local privacy, changed nonparametric exponent, conceptual clarity, and practical relevance.  
- Reason to reject: Incomplete discussion of relevant DP lower-bound literature for linear queries; needs to be addressed.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on adding the missing related work and clarifying the function-class alignment in the nonparametric comparison.
