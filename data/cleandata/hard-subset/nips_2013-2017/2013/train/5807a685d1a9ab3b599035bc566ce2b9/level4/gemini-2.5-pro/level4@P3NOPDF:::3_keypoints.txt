- Studies minimax bounds for estimation under local differential privacy for n users with d discrete types and α-LDP constraints.  
- Contribution 1: derives a minimax lower bound for squared l2-error in estimating the empirical distribution and proves Randomized Response is optimal.  
- Contribution 2: establishes minimax lower bounds for nonparametric density estimation and shows a simple local perturbation scheme attains them.  
- Strength: provides tight matching lower and upper bounds, offering a definitive characterization of optimal accuracy under local privacy.  
- Strength: shows simple, non-interactive mechanisms are optimal, an important implication that should be emphasized.  
- Strength: technically solid and directly relevant to the ML and privacy communities.  
- Weakness: heavy notation and presentation; minimax framework and l2-results could be stated more clearly and accessibly.  
- Weakness: narrow focus on l2-error; lacks discussion of other metrics (e.g., l1) and comparisons to central DP results.  
- Weakness: limited discussion on whether the analytical approach generalizes to other privacy models or broader statistical problems.  
- Weakness: novelty substantially reduced by overlapping results published at FOCS 2013.  
- Recommendation: Accept (downgraded from strong accept) — valuable contribution but less novel due to recent related work.
