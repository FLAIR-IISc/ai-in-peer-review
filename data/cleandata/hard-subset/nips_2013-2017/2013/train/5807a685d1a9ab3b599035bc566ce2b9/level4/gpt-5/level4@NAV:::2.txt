This paper investigates minimax bounds for probability estimation under the requirement that individual data remain private. The authors adopt the local privacy framework and analyze two estimation tasks: multinomial probability estimation and density estimation. In both settings, they establish sharp minimax convergence rates. For the discrete multinomial problem, they show that local privacy reduces the effective sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter alpha; since alpha is often a small constant, the effective sample size remains of the same order as in the non-private case. For density estimation, they demonstrate that the optimal non-private rate is no longer achievable when local privacy must be enforced.

Overall, the results are very interesting, and the proofs appear correct based on my reading. To the best of my knowledge, the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy constraints has not been addressed previously.

My main comment is that the multinomial minimax bound is closely related to prior work on noise complexity for differential privacy, which is insufficiently referenced. In particular, two highly relevant papers are Hardt & Talwar, On the Geometry of Differential Privacy (STOC 2010), and De, Lower Bounds in Differential Privacy (TCC 2012). These works analyze worst-case lower bounds for the error of linear queries under differential privacy. The probability estimation problem here is a special case of the linear counting queries studied in those papers, and the error is measured in the same L2 metric. The primary difference is that this paper considers local differential privacy, whereas Hardt–Talwar and De treat (central) differential privacy. Since local privacy is a stronger constraint, lower bounds for differential privacy also apply to local privacy. I would like to know whether the local privacy bounds in this paper improve upon the prior differential privacy lower bounds.

For the density estimation problem, the presentation of the result is somewhat confusing. The authors claim that the local-privacy lower bound exceeds the non-private bound. However, the minimax bound in this paper is derived for the special case where the density admits a trigonometric basis expansion. I am unsure whether the non-private lower bound in equation (13) holds over the general Sobolev class defined in Definition 1 or only for the trigonometric subclass. The comparison is only fair if the non-private lower bound applies to the trigonometric basis as well.

Additional comments to the rebuttal:
The response partially clarifies the relationship to prior worst-case lower bounds, but note that Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang, The Geometry of Differential Privacy: The Sparse and Approximate Cases (STOC 2013), also studies mean squared error. I suggest incorporating these explanations and the missing references into the paper.

This paper establishes sharp minimax bounds for pmf and pdf estimation under privacy guarantees. The results are compelling and the paper is well written. However, it under-cites existing noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy that are highly pertinent to this work.
