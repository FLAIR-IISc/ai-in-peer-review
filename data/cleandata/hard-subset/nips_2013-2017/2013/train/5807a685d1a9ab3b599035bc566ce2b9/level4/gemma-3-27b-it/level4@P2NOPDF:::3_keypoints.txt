- Paper studies local differential privacy with a min-max expected error framework for estimating distributions from privatized user signals.  
- Presents a lower bound on the minimax l2-rate for discrete distributions over d types.  
- Shows randomized response achieves the lower bound, proving its optimality in this setting.  
- Extends results to density estimation and shows single-user perturbation attains the derived lower bound.  
- Important contribution: practical, well-known procedures are optimal under the established minimax bounds.  
- Criticism: the min-max framework explanation is dense and would benefit from clearer, simpler exposition.  
- Criticism: notation is heavy; consistently expressing bounds in the l2 norm would improve readability.  
- Suggestion: include discussion and comparison with l1-norm bounds and existing differential privacy lower bounds.  
- Suggestion: broaden scope by deriving minimax lower bounds for additional privacy settings beyond the local model.  
- Positive note: emphasize the surprising insight that optimal schemes randomize only on an individual’s own type, not on others’ signals.  
- Concern: related FOCS 2013 work overlaps with Section 3, reducing reviewer confidence somewhat, though acceptance is recommended.
