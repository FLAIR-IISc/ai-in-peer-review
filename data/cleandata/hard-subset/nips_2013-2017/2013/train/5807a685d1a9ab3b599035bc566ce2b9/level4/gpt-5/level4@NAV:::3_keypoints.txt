- Paper studies local differential privacy in a minimax expected-error framework for n users emitting α-DP signals.  
- Main result: lower bound on minimax l2-rate for estimating discrete distributions over d types.  
- Randomized response is shown to achieve the lower bound and is therefore optimal for the discrete case.  
- For density estimation, a matching lower bound is given and achieved by a per-user perturbation scheme.  
- Clear and significant work; reviewer recommends acceptance (interest to the NIPS community).  
- Exposition and notation are heavy; minimax bounds could be simplified and stated directly in l2 norm.  
- Reviewer requests discussion of l1 guarantees and relation to other differential privacy lower bounds.  
- Minimax bounds seem tailored to the local-privacy model; broader families of settings should be considered.  
- Reviewer suggests authors explicitly state that per-user-only randomization is optimal despite allowing dependence on other users.  
- New concern: possible overlap/extension of a FOCS 2013 paper (Section 3), weakening the recommendation.
