- Uses a LeCam-style argument to derive lower bounds in the local privacy model and shows randomized response is optimal in a specific sense.  
- Effectively characterizes fundamental trade-offs for learning from privacy-preserving perturbed data.  
- Exposition and discussion lack precision at times, risking confusion for non-expert readers.  
- Fails to adequately connect and compare findings to existing differential privacy literature.  
- Does not clearly distinguish the local privacy model from the central model; some contextual statements (e.g., bottom of p.4) are potentially misleading.  
- Questions whether the constraint on α in Theorem 1 is necessary, since α ≈ 1 is a practically important regime.  
- Lacks discussion of choices for the loss function ρ and how different utility measures affect the analysis.  
- Omits comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure and does not explain how the proposed density estimator relates to that work.  
- Beyond randomized response, the paper doesn't discuss alternative histogram estimation algorithms or their empirical/theoretical performance.  
- After rebuttal: the paper should more clearly separate population versus sample statistics early on.  
- After rebuttal: a thorough comparison with related work (including Lei and others) is essential for proper context.  
- After reviewing reference [10], the manuscript’s contributions appear to be (1) detailed presentation of the local privacy model (largely present in [10]) and (2) novel bounds for density estimation, narrowing its originality.
