- Paper derives minimax lower bounds under the local privacy model via a Fano/LeCam-style argument and shows randomized response achieves the bound.
- Pro: Characterizes fundamental limits of learning from locally privatized data.
- Con: Exposition contains imprecise language that could confuse readers about privacy-model nuances.
- Con: Insufficient discussion of connections to the broader differential privacy literature.
- Important to emphasize and clarify the distinction between local and central differential privacy early in the paper.
- Unclear whether the restriction on the privacy parameter α in Theorem 1 is necessary, especially since practical work often uses α ≈ 1.
- The paper should discuss choices for the loss function ρ to show applicability to different utility measures.
- Missing comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure; relevant prior work is not cited.
- Although randomized response attains the optimal rate, the paper should compare to other histogram estimators for discrete data to provide context.
- Need clearer explanation of population vs. sample statistics at the paper’s start, as noted in rebuttal.
- Must compare and contextualize results with related work (including Lei and other methods raised in reviews).
- Published reference [10] reduces some novelty: the paper’s exposition of local privacy overlaps with [10], while the density estimation bounds are the primary new contribution and should be acknowledged as such.
