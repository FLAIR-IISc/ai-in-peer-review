- Paper derives sharp minimax convergence rates under local privacy for two problems: discrete multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy introduces a factor depending on the privacy parameter α, while the dependence on sample size n matches the non-private rate.  
- For density estimation, the paper shows the optimal non-private rate cannot be achieved under local privacy.  
- Results are novel and significant; proofs appear correct and the density minimax analysis under local privacy may be the first of its kind.  
- Major concern: insufficient discussion of prior lower-bound literature in differential privacy that is highly relevant.  
- Specifically, Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012) give worst-case lower bounds for linear queries (including multinomial estimation) with the same L2 error metric.  
- Because central DP lower bounds also apply to local DP, the authors must clarify whether their local DP bounds improve upon or relate to those central DP lower bounds.  
- Also missing discussion/citation of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on MSE for related problems.  
- For density estimation, the private vs. non-private rate comparison may be misleading: the private lower bound is derived for densities with a trigonometric basis expansion, so the non-private lower bound in Eq. (13) must be shown to apply to that same class rather than the broader Sobolev space.  
- Summary: paper is interesting, well-written, and well-supported, but needs a thorough discussion of relevant DP lower-bound literature to properly contextualize contributions.
