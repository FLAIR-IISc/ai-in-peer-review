- Provides sharp minimax rates for probability estimation under local privacy in both multinomial and nonparametric density settings.  
- Shows Warner's randomized response is sample-complexity optimal for d-dimensional multinomial estimation under α-local differential privacy.  
- Establishes effective sample size reduction from n to nα²/d for d-dimensional multinomial estimation under local privacy.  
- Derives novel density-estimation rate (α²n)⁻²β/(2β+2) for β-smooth densities, differing from the classical n⁻²β/(2β+1) rate.  
- Presents matching lower and upper bounds, with practical estimators (histogram and orthogonal series) that achieve the rates.  
- Rigorous minimax framework with precise local privacy definitions and solid, well-motivated proofs.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation accessible to readers with statistical learning background.  
- Original contribution quantifying the statistical cost of local privacy and linking Warner’s survey-sampling idea to modern privacy frameworks.  
- Significant impact: provides practical guidance and fundamental understanding of the privacy–efficiency trade-off.  
- Minor issue: occasional conflation of “local privacy” and centralized “differential privacy”; should consistently use “local privacy.”  
- Minor issue: connection to Lei’s private M-estimators and comparisons to Laplace-perturbed density estimators need strengthening.  
- Minor issue: restriction to α ∈ [0,1] in Theorem 1 lacks full justification and should be clarified regarding practical implications.  
- Minor suggestion: include discussion of alternative loss functions (e.g., KL divergence) and their effect on results.  
- Overall judgment: strong theoretical contribution with minor addressable issues; recommendation to accept.
