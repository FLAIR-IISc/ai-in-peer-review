- Studies local privacy where each user produces an α-DP signal and analyzes estimation error in a min-max expected error framework.
- Provides a lower bound on the min-max rate for l2 distance when users drawn from a discrete distribution over d types.
- Shows randomized response achieves the l2 lower bound, proving its optimality in this setting.
- For density estimation, the lower bound is met by a perturbation scheme where each person perturbs only their own type.
- Paper is well-written, significant, and likely of interest to the NIPS community; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Criticism: explanation of min-max bounds could be simplified and notation is cumbersome—suggest phrasing bounds directly in l2 norm.
- Suggestion: add comments or comparisons to l1 norm (more restrictive) and to other DP lower bounds from classical/non-local settings.
- Desire for broader scope: reviewer wishes for min-max lower bounds in settings beyond local privacy.
- Recommend explicitly stating that per-user-only randomization is optimal in the local setting.
- New concern: appears to extend an existing FOCS 2013 paper (notably Section 3), which reduces the strength of the recommendation.
- Overall conclusion: effectively analyzes local privacy with sharp min-max rates and demonstrates classic procedures (randomized response) are optimal.
