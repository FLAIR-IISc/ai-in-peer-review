1. Studies local α-differential privacy with min-max expected error for estimating distributions from n users.  
2. Establishes l2 minimax lower bounds for discrete distributions over d types.  
3. Proves randomized response achieves the lower bound and is optimal.  
4. Extends results to density estimation, showing per-user perturbation attains the bound.  
5. Significant contribution: classic mechanism matches theoretical lower bounds; recommends acceptance.  
6. Presentation issue: explanation of min-max bounds could be clearer and more accessible.  
7. Notation is cumbersome; bounds should be stated explicitly in terms of the l2 norm.  
8. Suggests discussing or comparing results to l1-norm bounds, since l1 is stricter.  
9. Recommends relating results to other differential privacy lower bounds (mainly non-local).  
10. Limitation: bounds appear specific to the local privacy setting; broader settings would be valuable.  
11. Important point to emphasize: allowing dependence on other users' signals does not improve over per-user randomization.  
12. Concern about overlap: appears to extend an existing FOCS 2013 paper (esp. Section 3), tempering enthusiasm.
