- Paper studies trade-offs between statistical estimation accuracy and local differential privacy, deriving sharp minimax lower bounds (via Le Cam/Fano) for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation; shows randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomials and local privacy changes density estimation rates.

- Reviewer did not read the supplementary material.

- Strength: Rigorous, quantitative characterization of the costs of local privacy, providing sharp minimax rates and tying classical randomized response to modern DP.

- Weakness 1: Need clearer distinction between the local privacy model used and the central differential privacy model; avoid unqualified statements that generalize to “differential privacy.”

- Weakness 2: Positioning and originality unclear relative to a longer arXiv manuscript—authors should explicitly state which contributions are unique to this NIPS submission (likely the density estimation analysis).

- Weakness 3: Related-work comparisons are incomplete—should discuss Lei [19] (DP M-estimation) for density results and compare private histogram methods to the minimax bounds for multinomials.

- Weakness 4: The distinction that the paper estimates population parameters (not sample statistics) is important and should be emphasized earlier and more prominently.

- Question 1: Is the restriction α ∈ [0,1] in Theorem 1 essential, or can the bounds be extended to larger α values relevant in practice?

- Question 2: Provide guidance on the choice of metric ρ (e.g., L1 vs L2) and discuss how different metrics reflect different analyst utility goals.
