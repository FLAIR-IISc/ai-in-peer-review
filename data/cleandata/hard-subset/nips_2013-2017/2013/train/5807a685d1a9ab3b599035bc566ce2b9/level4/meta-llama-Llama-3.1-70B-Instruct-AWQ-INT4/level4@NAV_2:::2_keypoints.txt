- Studies minimax bounds under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation, proving sharp minimax rates.
- Shows for multinomial estimation local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter alpha.
- Notes that if alpha is a small constant, the effective sample size remains of the same order as the non-private case.
- Shows for density estimation the optimal non-private rate is no longer attainable under local privacy.
- Results are interesting, proofs appear correct, and the density-estimation privacy minimax bound is novel to the reviewer’s knowledge.
- Main critique: missing discussion and citations of related differential-privacy noise-complexity lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar STOC 2010; De TCC 2012).
- Those prior works study worst-case lower bounds for linear counting queries with L2 error, which include the multinomial problem as a special case.
- Because local privacy is stronger than (central) differential privacy, existing DP lower bounds also apply—unclear if this paper’s local-privacy bounds improve on those prior results.
- Also missing citation: Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang, The Geometry of Differential Privacy: The Sparse and Approximate Cases (STOC 2013), which considers mean squared error.
- Density-estimation claim is confusing: the lower bound is proved for densities with a trigonometric basis expansion.
- Need clarification whether the non-private lower bound (eq.13) holds for the general Sobolev space in Definition 1 or only for the trigonometric-basis case; fair comparison requires matching function classes.
- Recommend authors add the missing references, clarify relation to prior worst-case lower bounds, and explain whether their local-privacy bounds constitute an improvement.
- Overall: technically sound and well written; recommendation — accept with minor revisions to address references and clarifications.
