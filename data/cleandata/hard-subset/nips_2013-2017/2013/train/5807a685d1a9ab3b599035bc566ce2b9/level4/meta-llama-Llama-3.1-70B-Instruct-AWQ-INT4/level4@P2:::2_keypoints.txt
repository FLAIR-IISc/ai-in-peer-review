- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation, proving sharp rates of convergence.
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α; when α is small constant, effective sample size is same order as non-private.
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private rate is no longer attainable under local privacy.
- Results are interesting and proofs appear correct; density-estimation minimax bound under privacy seems novel.
- Main concern: lack of discussion of related work on noise complexity/differential privacy lower bounds.
- Specifically missing references: Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012), which study worst-case lower bounds for linear queries using the same L2 error metric.
- Probability estimation here is a special case of linear counting queries studied in those papers; need to clarify relation and whether this paper’s local-privacy bounds improve on prior (global DP) bounds.
- Clarify that local differential privacy is a stronger constraint than standard differential privacy, so DP lower bounds also apply, but comparison/implications should be stated.
- Confusion about density-estimation statement: is the non-private lower bound (eq.13) proved for general Sobolev spaces or only for trigonometric-basis densities?
- Fair comparison requires the non-private lower bound to hold for the same trigonometric basis case used in the private setting.
- Rebuttal note: also cite Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on geometry of differential privacy and mean square error; authors should add explanations and these missing references.
