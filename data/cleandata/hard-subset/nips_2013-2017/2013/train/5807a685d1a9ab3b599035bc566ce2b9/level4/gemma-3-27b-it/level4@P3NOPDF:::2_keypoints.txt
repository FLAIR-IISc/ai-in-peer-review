- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation, establishing sharp rates of convergence.
- For multinomial estimation, authors show a quadratic reduction in effective sample size due to the privacy constraint, with small α making the reduction modest and approaching non-private performance.
- For density estimation, the paper proves that the optimal non-private rate cannot be achieved under local privacy.
- Proofs appear sound and the density minimax bound under privacy is a novel contribution to my knowledge.
- Main concern: the paper omits discussion of related worst-case lower bounds for differential privacy (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) which are directly relevant.
- Because local privacy is stronger than differential privacy, existing DP lower bounds (linear queries / L2 error) may imply comparable lower bounds here; the authors must clarify whether their bounds improve on DP results.
- The rebuttal partially clarified connections, but the authors should also cite Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on mean square error and expand the literature discussion.
- The density lower-bound presentation is unclear: the result is stated for densities expressible in a trigonometric basis, so it’s unclear if the cited non-private minimax bound (eq.13) applies generally to the Sobolev class or only the trigonometric-restricted class.
- A fair comparison requires showing the non-private lower bound holds under the same trigonometric-function restriction, or otherwise clarifying the scope of the bounds.
- Overall: significant and well-written work, but needs a more thorough comparison to noise-complexity/DP literature and clarification of assumptions in the density lower bound.
