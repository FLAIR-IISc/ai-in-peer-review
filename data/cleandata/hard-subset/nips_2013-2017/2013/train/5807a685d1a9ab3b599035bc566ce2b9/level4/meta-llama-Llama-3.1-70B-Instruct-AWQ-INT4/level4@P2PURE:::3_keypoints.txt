- Paper studies local α-differential privacy in a min-max expected error framework where an adversary picks a distribution and an α-DP local scheme minimizes estimation error.  
- Derives sharp lower bounds for the minimax l2-rate for probability estimation over d discrete types.  
- Shows randomized response achieves the lower bound, proving its optimality for the discrete setting.  
- Extends results to density estimation and shows a per-user perturbation scheme attains the minimax lower bound.  
- Well-written, important results that the NIPS community will find interesting; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Suggests simplification of the min-max bound exposition and less cumbersome notation—state bounds directly in the l2 norm.  
- Requests discussion/comparison with l1-norm results and other classical (non-local) differential privacy lower bounds.  
- Wishes the authors had considered broader settings beyond the local-privacy model and provided minimax lower bounds for them.  
- Recommends explicitly stating that per-user-only randomization (ignoring other users’ signals) is optimal in the local setting.  
- Notes overlap/extension with recently accepted FOCS 2013 work (esp. Section 3), which weakens the endorsement somewhat though acceptance is still recommended.
