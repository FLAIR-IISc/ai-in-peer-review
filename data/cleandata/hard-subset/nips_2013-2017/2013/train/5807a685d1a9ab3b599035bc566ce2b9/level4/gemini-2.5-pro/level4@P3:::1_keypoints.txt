- Paper studies the trade-off between statistical accuracy and local differential privacy, deriving sharp minimax rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Technical approach: uses Fano’s inequality and Le Cam’s method for lower bounds and constructs efficient estimators that achieve those bounds.  
- Main contribution 1: For d-dimensional multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size to about nα²/d, and Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal.  
- Main contribution 2: For β-smooth density estimation, the rate worsens from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)}; private histogram and orthogonal series estimators achieve this rate.  
- Strength: Addresses a fundamental, timely problem by quantifying the statistical cost of strong (local) privacy with sharp minimax characterizations.  
- Strength: Technically rigorous—derives matching upper and lower bounds giving a complete picture for the studied problems.  
- Strength: Significant insights—connects randomized response to modern DP optimality and identifies a novel change in polynomial convergence for density estimation.  
- Strength: Generally clear and well-organized exposition.  
- Weakness: Privacy model clarity—paper risks confusing local vs central differential privacy and should explicitly define and contrast them early.  
- Weakness: Positioning—fails to discuss related work sufficiently (e.g., Lei 2011) and should compare alternative private histogram methods (e.g., Laplace-noise approaches).  
- Weakness: Scope of theoretical results—Theorem 1’s restriction of α to [0,1] is not motivated; authors should clarify generality and discuss effects of different error semi-metrics ρ.  
- Weakness: Novelty vs arXiv preprint—conference version appears close to the authors’ arXiv manuscript; the paper should clearly state what is new (density estimation seems the main addition).  
- Recommendation: Strong paper with addressable clarity/positioning issues; recommend acceptance.
