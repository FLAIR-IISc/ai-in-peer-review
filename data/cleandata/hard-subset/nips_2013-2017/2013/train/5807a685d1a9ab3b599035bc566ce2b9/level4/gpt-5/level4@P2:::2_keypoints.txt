- Paper analyzes probability estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) in two settings—multinomial over simplex and nonparametric density in Sobolev classes—and derives sharp minimax rates with matching private mechanisms.  
- Multinomial result: LDP reduces effective sample size from n to about nα^2/d (small α), with tight lower/upper bounds in ℓ2 and ℓ1; randomized response is optimal up to constants.  
- Density result: For Sobolev smoothness β, classical rate n−2β/(2β+1) becomes (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2) under LDP; histogram (β=1) and orthogonal series (β≥1) mechanisms achieve these rates.  
- Technical quality: Results are technically sound with matching bounds and careful use of information-theoretic tools (Fano/Le Cam, mutual information tailored to LDP).  
- Mechanisms are simple, computationally efficient, and implementable.  
- Clarity suggestions: explicitly state regimes where multinomial risk is only a constant-factor degradation (fixed d, constant α) versus severe degradation (growing d).  
- Clarity suggestions: clarify exactly which function class (e.g., trigonometric-periodic Sobolev) is used for the density lower bound so the nonprivate baseline comparison is fair.  
- Originality: Sharp minimax rates for density estimation under LDP and tight multinomial characterization (optimal randomized response variant) appear novel.  
- Significance: Provides fundamental, tight privacy–accuracy tradeoffs and constructive mechanisms likely to influence theory and practice.  
- Relation to prior work: Missing citations and discussion of central-DP lower bounds for linear queries (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov–Talwar–Zhang 2013) and how those bounds relate to or contrast with the LDP results.  
- Request: explain precisely how the multinomial lower bound relates to central-DP linear-query MSE bounds and whether d,α dependence improves or is incomparable due to model differences (local vs central, non-interactive).  
- Multinomial specificity: paper should explicitly note that for fixed d and constant α the ℓ2 risk degrades by only a constant factor compared to the nonprivate MLE.  
- Density specificity: ensure the nonprivate baseline (eq. (13)) is proved for the same trigonometric Sobolev class used in the private lower bound.  
- Observation: simple additive Laplace perturbation of Xi is suboptimal for the density problem; the deconvolution-based argument is appropriate.  
- Pros summarized: sharp minimax rates with matching mechanisms; clear privacy–efficiency tradeoffs and optimality of randomized response; solid technical contributions.  
- Cons summarized: incomplete citation/discussion of relevant central-DP lower bounds; need to clarify function class in density comparisons; focus limited to non-interactive local model without discussion of interactive/central extensions.  
- Rebuttal suggestion: add discussion and cite Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) specifically, and clarify how your α- and d-dependence compares in the multinomial setting.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance after minor revisions to add the missing references and clarify the density-comparison scope and multinomial-rate interpretation.
