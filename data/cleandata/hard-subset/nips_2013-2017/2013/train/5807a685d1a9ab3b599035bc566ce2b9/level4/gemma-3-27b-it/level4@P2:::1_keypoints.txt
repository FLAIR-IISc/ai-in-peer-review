- Presents a theoretical analysis of probability estimation under local privacy, using minimax decision theory and differential privacy concepts.  
- Main contribution: establishes sharp minimax rates of convergence for discrete (multinomial) and continuous (density) estimation under local privacy.  
- Quantifies the trade-off between privacy and statistical efficiency, with matching lower and upper bounds.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is optimal for survey sampling with privacy guarantees.  
- Builds on Warner [23] and connects classical randomized response to modern differential privacy frameworks.  
- Uses information-theoretic tools (KL-divergence, mutual information) to derive lower bounds and achievable rates with efficient algorithms.  
- Strength: provides a rigorous mathematical foundation combining minimax theory and differential privacy.  
- Strength: derives sharp, tight minimax rates that clarify performance limits under privacy.  
- Strength: has practical relevance by reaffirming an optimal, implementable privacy mechanism (randomized response).  
- Strength: clearly formulates the local privacy model and its relation to differential privacy.  
- Strength: situates contributions within existing literature and cites key related work.  
- Weakness: statements linking local privacy to broader differential privacy need more precise interpretation to avoid misreading.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of sensitivity to the privacy parameter α, especially when α is near 1, where bounds may be less applicable.  
- Weakness: analysis focuses on specific distance metrics; discussion of other utility measures is limited.  
- Weakness: should engage more thoroughly with recent work (e.g., Lei [19] on differentially private M-estimators).  
- Weakness: lacks discussion/comparison of alternative estimation algorithms beyond randomized response and proposed density estimators.  
- Overall: strong, rigorous paper with valuable theoretical contributions and minor clarifications needed.  
- For acceptance: novel rigorous analysis, insightful minimax rates, practical confirmation of randomized response, and clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: needs clearer interpretation of implications and fuller engagement with recent related work.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing minor weaknesses (clarity of interpretation and expanded related-work discussion).
