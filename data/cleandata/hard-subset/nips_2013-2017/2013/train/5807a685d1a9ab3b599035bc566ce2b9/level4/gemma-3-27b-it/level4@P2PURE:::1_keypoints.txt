- Paper presents a NIPS-formatted arXiv work using Fano/LeCam arguments to derive lower bounds for estimation with an untrusted algorithm and shows randomized response is optimal under certain conditions.  
- Reviewer did not evaluate the supplementary material.  
- Strength: characterizes fundamental limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed data.  
- Weakness: accompanying commentary contains imprecisions that could mislead casual readers.  
- Weakness: paper lacks discussion of connections to existing differential privacy literature.  
- The focus on local privacy differs from standard differential privacy and this distinction is not made sufficiently clear, risking reader confusion.  
- Some broad statements (e.g., page 4) are potentially inaccurate given the local-privacy context and should be avoided or qualified.  
- The requirement on α in Theorem 1 needs closer scrutiny; for practical cases (α ≈ 1) the bound’s relevance is unclear.  
- The paper should discuss choices for the utility measure ρ to illustrate applicability to different settings.  
- Missing comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure; relevant prior work should be addressed.  
- It would be valuable to compare randomized response to other histogram estimation algorithms for discrete data.  
- Addendum: the distinction between population and sample statistics must be clarified early in the paper.  
- Addendum: a comprehensive comparison to related work (including Lei and others) is essential for proper context.  
- Addendum: after [10], the contribution is narrower—local-privacy exposition largely overlaps [10], with novelty mainly in density-estimation bounds.
