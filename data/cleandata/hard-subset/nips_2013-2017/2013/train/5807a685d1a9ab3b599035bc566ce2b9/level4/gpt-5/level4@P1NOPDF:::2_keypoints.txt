- Studies minimax bounds under local differential privacy for multinomial (pmf) and density (pdf) estimation, proving sharp minimax rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α; if α is a small constant, sample-size order matches non-private.  
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private rate is no longer achievable under local privacy.  
- Proofs appear correct and density minimax bounds under privacy are novel to the reviewer’s knowledge.  
- Main critique: insufficient discussion of related noise-complexity/lower-bound work in (central) differential privacy, notably Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012).  
- Clarify whether the local-model lower bounds strictly improve upon or merely match the best known central-model lower bounds for the same linear-query/probability-estimation setting, and whether techniques are comparable.  
- Density-estimation comparison is unclear because the private lower bound is for a trigonometric-subclass, while the cited non-private lower bound may be for the full Sobolev class; the comparison should be made basis-subclass-specific or justified.  
- Rebuttal partially clarified relationships, but also consider Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013) which analyzes MSE and is directly relevant.  
- Recommend incorporating the missing references and expanding the discussion contrasting these prior linear-query DP lower bounds with the paper’s local-model results, explicitly stating what is new (constants, regimes, or model differences).  
- Overall: interesting and well-written work with a key shortcoming in contextualization against prior DP lower bounds and a need for a precise basis-dependent comparison in the density section.
