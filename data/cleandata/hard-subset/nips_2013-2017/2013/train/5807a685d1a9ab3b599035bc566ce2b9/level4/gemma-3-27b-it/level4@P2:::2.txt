This paper investigates minimax bounds for probability estimation under the privacy constraint of local differential privacy. The authors consider two fundamental problems: multinomial estimation and density estimation, deriving sharp minimax rates of convergence for both. For multinomial estimation, they demonstrate a reduction in effective sample size quadratic in the privacy parameter α, though note this reduction may be manageable when α is small. For density estimation, they show the classical non-private convergence rate is unattainable under local privacy.

Overall, the results are compelling and the paper is well-written. The proofs appear sound, and to the best of my knowledge, the minimax bound for density estimation under a local privacy constraint is a novel contribution. The work successfully connects concepts from statistical decision theory, differential privacy, and information theory.

A key point for discussion concerns the relationship to existing work on differential privacy, specifically lower bounds on the complexity of estimating linear queries. While the paper focuses on *local* differential privacy, which is a stronger constraint than standard (central) differential privacy, relevant results exist for the latter, such as those by Hardt and Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012). These papers study worst-case lower bounds for the error of linear queries under differential privacy, and the probability estimation problem addressed here can be viewed as a special case of linear counting queries.  The authors use the same error metrics (L2 distance). It would strengthen the paper to explicitly discuss how their bounds compare to these existing results for differential privacy. Specifically, it would be valuable to understand if the bounds achieved under local privacy improve upon those known for differential privacy, or whether they simply provide a tighter characterization under the stronger privacy constraint. It is worth noting the work of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) which also considers Mean Squared Error and might be relevant.

Regarding density estimation, the presentation of the results could be clarified. The authors state their lower bound applies in a setting where the density is expressible using a trigonometric basis. It’s crucial to clarify whether the classical minimax rate (eq. 13) cited for comparison also holds specifically for densities with a trigonometric basis representation, or if it’s more general (e.g., holding for broader Sobolev spaces as described in Definition 1). A fair comparison hinges on this point.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The derivation of sharp minimax bounds for density estimation under local privacy is a significant contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper appears technically sound and the proofs are likely correct.
*   **Significance:** The work provides a fundamental understanding of the trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency, which is crucial for developing privacy-preserving statistical methods.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and the concepts are presented in a clear manner.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Missing Related Work:** The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of relevant work on lower bounds for differential privacy, specifically concerning linear queries.
*   **Clarification Needed:** The comparison between the new lower bound for density estimation and existing non-private bounds requires clarification regarding the assumptions on the density class.



I recommend acceptance contingent upon the authors addressing the points raised regarding related work and clarifying the assumptions underlying the comparison of the density estimation bounds. Addressing these comments would further strengthen an already compelling contribution.
