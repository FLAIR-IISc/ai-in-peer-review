- Main contribution: rigorous theoretical analysis of probability estimation under α-local differential privacy with sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation.  
- Shows fundamental trade-off between privacy and statistical efficiency, providing quantitative minimax convergence rates.  
- Builds on Warner's randomized response and connects it to modern differential privacy, proving randomized response is minimax optimal for discrete estimation.  
- Density estimation result: privacy changes convergence exponents from 2β/(2β+1) to 2β/(2β+2) for Sobolev-smooth densities.  
- Provides matching lower and upper bounds, with computationally efficient algorithms achieving the upper bounds.  
- Technical approach: well-structured proofs using mutual information to relate privacy constraints to statistical rates.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and reproducible for experts; notation is dense but consistent.  
- Suggested clarity improvement: introduction should better summarize key techniques and explicitly distinguish local vs central differential privacy.  
- Originality: first to establish sharp minimax rates under local privacy and to show classical randomized response is optimal in this setting.  
- Significance: results have substantial theoretical and practical impact for privacy-preserving surveys and data collection.  
- Weakness: focus limited to multinomial and density estimation; needs more discussion on extending framework to other problems (e.g., regression, classification).  
- Weakness: should more thoroughly compare with non-local differential privacy lower bounds and temper broad claims about prior optimality.  
- Recommendation: strong, technically sound paper that meets NIPS criteria; recommendation for acceptance.
