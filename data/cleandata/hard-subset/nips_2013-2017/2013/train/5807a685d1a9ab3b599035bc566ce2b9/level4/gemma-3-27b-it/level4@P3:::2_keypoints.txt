- Paper derives sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation under local (individual) privacy.  
- Multinomial estimation: local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α (manageable if α is small).  
- Density estimation: classical non-private optimal convergence rate is unattainable under local privacy.  
- Technical contributions appear novel, especially the minimax bounds for density estimation under privacy.  
- Proofs are technically sound and the paper integrates differential privacy with minimax theory effectively.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Related-work discussion is limited; connections to differential-privacy noise-complexity and linear-query lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010, De 2012) are underdeveloped.  
- Should acknowledge and relate to Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on mean square error.  
- Need to clarify whether the non-private minimax lower bound (eq. 13) applies to the same Sobolev function class or only to the trigonometric-basis case.  
- Potential exists to refine bounds by leveraging connections to existing differential-privacy lower bounds.  
- Strengths: novelty, technical soundness, theoretical insight, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: limited related-work discussion, ambiguity in density-estimation comparison, possible room for improved bounds.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand related-work discussion and clarify the scope of the non-private lower bound.
