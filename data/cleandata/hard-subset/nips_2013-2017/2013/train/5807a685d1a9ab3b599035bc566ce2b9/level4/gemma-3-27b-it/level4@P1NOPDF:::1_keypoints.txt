- Uses a Fano/LeCam-style argument to derive lower bounds for estimation under a local privacy model where the data holder doesn't trust the algorithm; shows randomized response is optimal under certain conditions.
- Reviewer did not examine the supplementary material.
- Pro: Characterizes fundamental limitations of learning from perturbed/private data.
- Con: Explanatory commentary contains imprecisions that may confuse casual readers.
- Con: Paper lacks discussion of connections to existing differential privacy literature.
- The restriction to local privacy is crucial and distinct from differential privacy; authors should avoid broad/general statements implying equivalence (e.g., bottom of p.4).
- Question whether the restriction on α in Theorem 1 is necessary, since experiments suggest α ≈ 1 may be the practical upper bound for some problems.
- Recommend adding discussion of possible choices for ρ to clarify how different utility measures are captured.
- Missing citation and comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure for density estimation.
- Suggest comparing randomized response to other histogram/discrete-data estimation algorithms despite its optimal rate.
- Addendum: Clarify population vs. sample statistic distinction explicitly at the paper's start.
- Addendum: Provide comparisons to related work (including Lei and other papers raised by reviewers).
- Addendum: After reviewing [10], the contribution is narrower—careful exposition of local privacy largely overlaps with [10], while the density estimation bounds remain novel.
