- Paper is a NIPS-formatted reworking of prior arXiv work using a Fano/LeCam argument to derive lower bounds for estimation under a distrustful algorithm designer; randomized response is shown optimal in some regimes.

- Reviewer did not evaluate the supplementary material.

- Provides useful insights into fundamental limits of learning from privacy-perturbed data.

- Presentation lacks precision in places and may mislead readers unfamiliar with the nuances.

- Missing connections to standard differential privacy literature and related work.

- The focus on local privacy should be clearly distinguished from standard (central) differential privacy to avoid confusion.

- Statements suggesting general implications for differential privacy at large sample sizes (e.g., page 4) are overly general and should be toned down.

- Unclear whether the constraint on α in Theorem 1 is necessary; empirical evidence suggests α ≈ 1 may be an upper limit in some problems, questioning applicability.

- Paper should discuss practical implications of different choices for the utility measure ρ and how varying ρ affects results.

- Needs comparison to Lei’s M-estimation work and discussion of other histogram estimation methods for discrete data versus randomized response.

- Post-rebuttal: clarify population vs. sample statistics early, expand related-work comparisons (including Lei and works pointed out by other reviewers), and acknowledge that the local privacy exposition overlaps with recent [10], leaving novel contribution primarily in new density estimation bounds.
