- Studies local α-differential privacy with minimax expected-error framework for probability and density estimation.
- Proves sharp minimax lower bounds for l2 estimation over d discrete types.
- Shows randomized response attains the lower bound, proving optimality.
- Demonstrates for density estimation that schemes where each user perturbs only her own type attain the minimax bound.
- Paper is clear, important, and of interest to the community; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Reservation: exposition and notation are cumbersome; minimax explanation could be simplified and focused on l2 norm.
- Reservation: lacks discussion of l1 guarantees and connections to classical (non-local) DP lower bounds.
- Reservation: minimax results are tailored to the local-privacy model; broader settings would strengthen the work.
- Positive suggestion: explicitly state the optimality of per-user-only randomization versus using other users’ signals.
- New reservation: appears to extend an existing FOCS 2013 paper (notably Section 3), which weakens the recommendation.
