- Studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation problems.  
- Establishes sharp minimax convergence rates showing local privacy induces a fundamental trade-off between privacy and statistical efficiency.  
- Multinomial result: effective sample size reduces from n to nα²/d for small α, implying Warner’s randomized response attains optimal sample complexity.  
- Density result: non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy, and the rate is attainable with suitable estimators.  
- Formalizes the privacy–efficiency trade-off using minimax decision theory, connecting classical privacy concepts with modern differential privacy frameworks.  
- Technical correctness: proofs are sound, results properly connected to literature, and presentation permits reproduction.  
- Writing and organization are clear and detailed.  
- Originality: provides novel minimax bounds under local privacy but lacks sufficient engagement with prior differential privacy noise-complexity results.  
- Needs explicit comparison of multinomial bounds to worst-case lower bounds for linear queries (Hardt & Talwar STOC 2010; De TCC 2012), noting local privacy is stronger than standard DP.  
- Should cite and relate to Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on mean square error under differential privacy.  
- Density-estimation clarification required: confirm whether non-private lower bound (equation 13) holds for the general Sobolev class in Definition 1 or only for the trigonometric basis.  
- Overall assessment: significant and important contribution with practical implications, but would be strengthened by more comprehensive engagement with prior DP literature.
