- Uses a minimax decision-theoretic framework (Le Cam, Fano) to derive sharp lower bounds for estimation under local differential privacy.
- Precisely characterizes the trade-off between privacy level α and statistical accuracy.
- Studies two problems: multinomial parameter estimation and nonparametric density estimation.
- Shows for multinomials local privacy effectively reduces sample size from n to nα²/d.
- Proves Warner's randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation under local privacy.
- Shows for Sobolev density estimation the convergence exponent degrades from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy.
- Provides computationally efficient estimators that achieve the lower bounds, confirming sharpness.
- Strength: rigorous, timely treatment quantifying fundamental costs of enforcing local privacy.
- Strength: technically strong results that give clear quantitative privacy-utility insights.
- Strength: novel finding that local privacy can change polynomial convergence rates, not just constants.
- Strength: complete contribution with matching upper bounds via practical algorithms.
- Weakness: needs clearer, prominent distinction between the local and central differential privacy models.
- Weakness: related work discussion should be expanded, including comparisons to other discrete-distribution mechanisms and citing Lei (NIPS 2011).
- Weakness: clarify the restriction α ∈ [0,1] in Theorem 1 and behavior as α approaches 1.
- Weakness: discuss how the analysis adapts to other choices of the semi-metric ρ and different utility measures.
