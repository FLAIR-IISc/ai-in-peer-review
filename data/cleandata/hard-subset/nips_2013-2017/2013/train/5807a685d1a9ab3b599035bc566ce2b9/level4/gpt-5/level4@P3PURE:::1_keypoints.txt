- Paper is a NIPS-formatted arXiv manuscript deriving Fano/Le Cam–style lower bounds for estimation under local (untrusted analyst) differential privacy.  
- Corollary shows randomized response is (conditionally) optimal for some problems.  
- Reviewer did not read the supplementary material (caveat).  
- Strength: Clearly characterizes limitations of learning from locally privatized data.  
- Strength: Clean lower-bound framework yielding interpretable corollaries.  
- Weakness: Exposition is imprecise about the distinction between local and central (global) differential privacy.  
- Weakness: Connections to existing differential-privacy results are thin or missing, risking overgeneralization beyond the local model.  
- Detailed: Must be explicit throughout about which privacy model (local vs central) each statement pertains to.  
- Detailed: Clarify whether the alpha restriction in Theorem 1 is necessary and whether the bounds cover the practically relevant regime alpha ≈ 1.  
- Detailed: Provide commentary on choices of the utility measure rho and sensitivity of bounds to this choice.  
- Detailed: Compare the proposed density estimator to related work (e.g., Lei’s M-estimation) and other histogram/estimation methods, theoretically or empirically.  
- Addendum: Make the distinction between population parameters and sample statistics explicit early in the paper.  
- Addendum: Include direct comparisons to related work raised by reviewers to properly situate contributions.  
- Addendum: Explicitly position contributions relative to [10], noting the paper’s new density-estimation bounds and that exposition of the local model overlaps with [10].  
- Overall: Presents a useful local-privacy lower-bound framework and optimality result for randomized response, but needs sharper exposition, clearer treatment of alpha≈1, and more complete related-work/estimator comparisons.
