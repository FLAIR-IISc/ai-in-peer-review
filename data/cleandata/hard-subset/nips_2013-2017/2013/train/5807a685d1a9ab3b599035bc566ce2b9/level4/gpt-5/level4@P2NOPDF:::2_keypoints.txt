- Studies minimax risk under local differential privacy for multinomial pmf and nonparametric density estimation, proving sharp minimax rates in both settings.
- Shows for multinomial estimation that local privacy reduces effective sample size by a factor quadratic in α, so rates remain same order as non-private when α is constant.
- Shows for density estimation that the optimal non-private rate cannot be achieved under local privacy.
- Results are interesting, proofs appear sound, and density-estimation minimax bounds under privacy are novel.
- Connection to prior work on noise complexity and DP lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) is underdeveloped and needs clearer discussion.
- The multinomial problem is a special case of linear counting queries; the paper should state whether its multinomial lower bounds strictly improve on or simply recover known central-DP dependencies (dimension, n, α).
- Clarify modeling distinctions between central and local DP if results are incomparable, and make explicit any improvements due to the local model.
- Presentation of density results needs clarification: specify whether the non-private lower bound in eq. (13) is for the full Sobolev class or only the trigonometric subclass used in the local-privacy analysis.
- If eq. (13) is not for the trigonometric subclass, provide the corresponding non-private bound for that subclass to enable a fair comparison.
- After rebuttal, relationship to worst-case lower bounds is partially clarified, but the paper should also cite and compare to Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on MSE for linear queries.
- Recommendation: strengthen the related-work section and explicitly position the contributions relative to prior lower bounds for linear queries and L2 risk, highlighting what is new for the local model and the nonparametric setting.
- Overall: technically solid and novel contributions, with the main weakness being limited discussion of related DP lower bounds and the need for clearer basis-vs.-Sobolev comparisons.
