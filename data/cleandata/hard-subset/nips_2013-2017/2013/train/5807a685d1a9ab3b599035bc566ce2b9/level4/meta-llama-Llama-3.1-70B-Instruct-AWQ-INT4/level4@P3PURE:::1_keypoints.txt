- Paper derives lower bounds for estimation under local (untrusted-algorithm) privacy using Fano/LeCam arguments and shows randomized response is optimal in some senses.
- Reviewer did not assess the supplementary material.
- Strength: provides insights into limitations of learning from perturbed/private data.
- Weakness: some imprecise commentary that may confuse casual readers.
- Weakness: missing connections and comparisons to existing differential privacy literature.
- Key issue: restriction to local privacy is crucial and substantially different from (central) differential privacy; the paper understates this distinction.
- Recommendation: avoid broad/possibly incorrect statements equating local-privacy results with differential privacy claims (e.g., bottom of p.4).
- The necessity of the alpha restriction in Theorem 1 is unclear, especially given experiments suggesting alpha ≈ 1 in some DP settings.
- Suggestion: discuss choices of the utility measure rho to clarify captured utility tradeoffs.
- Missing comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure and other relevant related work.
- Suggestion: compare randomized response to other histogram-estimation algorithms for discrete data, despite its optimal rate.
- Post-rebuttal: need an explicit early distinction between population and sample statistics.
- Post-rebuttal: essential to compare to related work (Lei and others raised by reviewers).
- Post-rebuttal: after inspecting [10], the paper’s novel contribution is narrower—mainly careful exposition of local privacy (largely in [10]) and new bounds for density estimation.
