- Paper adapts an arXiv manuscript to NeurIPS format and proves lower bounds for estimation under the local privacy model, showing randomized response is optimal in certain settings.  
- Strength: provides a rigorous characterization of fundamental limits for learning from locally perturbed data.  
- Major concern: the paper conflates local privacy with (central) differential privacy and must explicitly distinguish the two throughout.  
- Major concern: fails to contextualize results with existing differential privacy utility bounds, weakening its placement in the broader privacy literature.  
- Major concern: exposition of the local privacy model largely overlaps with Lei et al. (JMLR 2016), so the novel contribution is mainly the density estimation bounds and needs direct comparison to [10].  
- Major concern: theoretical gaps—unclear necessity of the α restriction in Theorem 1 and inadequate discussion of the choice and implications of the utility parameter ρ.  
- Major concern: comparative omissions—no comparison to Lei’s M-estimator or to other histogram methods (e.g., Laplace perturbation, non-private baselines) despite claiming optimality of randomized response.  
- Minor suggestions: explicitly define population vs. sample statistics early and add a concise comparison to related work (including Lei’s method and other histogram estimators).  
- Overall: technical results are valuable but impact is undermined by conflation with DP, lack of contextualization with [10] and DP literature, and insufficient practical discussion (ρ, α regimes).  
- Decision: Reject—major revisions required to resolve conceptual confusions and contextual gaps.
