- Studies the local privacy (α-LDP) setting via a minimax expected-error framework over n users' types.  
- Proves lower bounds on the minimax ℓ2 estimation error for discrete distributions over d categories.  
- Shows a slight variant of randomized response matches the ℓ2 lower bound, proving optimality.  
- Derives lower bounds for density estimation and provides per-user perturbation mechanisms that attain those rates.  
- Main contribution: classical randomized response attains sharp minimax rates—clean and impactful result.  
- Recommendation: accept; results likely of significant interest to the NeurIPS community.  
- Stylistic critique: notation and abstract Φ ∘ ρ presentation is heavy; recommend stating bounds directly in ℓ2 where possible.  
- Suggest adding discussion of ℓ1 error, since ℓ1 is often a stricter metric for probability estimation and ties to central-model results.  
- Suggest evaluating whether comparable lower bounds hold in broader privacy models beyond the local model.  
- Note to emphasize: although the model allows dependence on others' reports, per-user independent randomization is optimal and should be made explicit.  
- Concern: parts of the submission (notably Section 3) overlap or extend prior FOCS 2013 work, tempering the endorsement.
