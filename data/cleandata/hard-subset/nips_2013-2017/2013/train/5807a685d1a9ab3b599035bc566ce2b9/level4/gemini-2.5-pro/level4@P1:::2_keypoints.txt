- Paper studies minimax lower bounds for probability estimation under α-local differential privacy for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size from n to nα^2/d (d = number of categories).  
- For density estimation over Sobolev class of smoothness β, privacy changes the rate from n^(-2β/(2β+1)) to (nα^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)).  
- Authors provide matching upper bounds via specific efficient algorithms, yielding sharp minimax rates.  
- Results are novel and significant; appears to be the first formal minimax analysis for density estimation under privacy.  
- Technical arguments are sound and connect minimax decision theory with differential privacy guarantees.  
- Main weakness: insufficient discussion of related lower-bound work in the central differential privacy literature (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang 2013).  
- Authors should clarify how their local-privacy lower bounds compare to existing central-privacy bounds and add the missing references.  
- Clarify the density-estimation comparison: confirm that the non-private rate n^(-2β/(2β+1)) applies to the same trigonometric-subclass used for the private lower bound.  
- Overall: well-written, valuable contribution if contextualization and the density-comparison clarification are addressed.
