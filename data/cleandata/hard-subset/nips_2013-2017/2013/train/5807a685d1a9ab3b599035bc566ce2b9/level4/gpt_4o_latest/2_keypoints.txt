- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy.  
- Analyzes two problems: multinomial (pmf) estimation and density (pdf) estimation, deriving sharp minimax rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α; for small α the effective sample size stays comparable to non-private.  
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private convergence rate is unattainable under local privacy.  
- Proofs appear correct and the density-estimation minimax bound under privacy seems novel.  
- Main concern: multinomial minimax bound is closely related to prior noise-complexity/worst-case lower bounds in differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012), but these connections are not discussed.  
- Those prior works study worst-case lower bounds for linear counting queries with L2 error; the probability estimation problem here is a special case, and differential privacy lower bounds should be relevant since local DP is stricter.  
- Question remains whether the paper’s local privacy bounds strictly improve on existing differential privacy lower bounds.  
- Density-estimation presentation unclear: lower bound is given for trigonometric-basis expansions, and it’s unclear if the non-private lower bound (Eq.13) applies to the full Sobolev space or only the trigonometric case; comparisons should be made on the same function class.  
- Rebuttal comment: also include Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which considers MSE and is relevant.  
- Overall: interesting, well-written results but notable omission of discussion and citations to existing noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy.
