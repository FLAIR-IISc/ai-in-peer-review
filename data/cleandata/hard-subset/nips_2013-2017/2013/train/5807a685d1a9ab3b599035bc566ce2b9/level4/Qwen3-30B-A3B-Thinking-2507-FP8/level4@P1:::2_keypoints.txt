- Establishes sharp minimax rates under local differential privacy for multinomial (discrete) and density (continuous) estimation, quantifying privacy–efficiency trade-offs.  
- Shows local privacy reduces effective sample size for multinomial estimation by a factor α²/d.  
- Proves density estimation rate degrades from n⁻²ᵝ/(2ᵝ⁺¹) (non-private) to n⁻²ᵝ/(2ᵝ⁺²) under local privacy for β-smooth densities.  
- Demonstrates Warner’s randomized response is minimax-optimal for multinomial estimation, a practically significant and surprising result.  
- Provides technically sound proofs and matching upper bounds via efficient algorithms.  
- Strength: resolves a key gap on privacy–statistics trade-offs with rigorous minimax analysis and clear writing.  
- Weakness: insufficient contextualization with prior differential privacy lower bounds for linear queries (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang 2013).  
- Concern: those DP lower bounds may imply related local privacy bounds; authors should clarify whether their multinomial bounds improve on or differ from these works.  
- Issue: comparison of private vs non-private density rates hinges on whether the non-private rate (Eq.13) applies to the trigonometric basis used in Theorem 2.  
- Request: explicitly confirm/cite that the non-private minimax rate holds for the trigonometric basis (e.g., Tsybakov on Sobolev classes).  
- Suggestion: add cited DP lower-bound literature to the introduction and explain relations to the local privacy setting.  
- Suggestion: refine discussion of Warner’s optimality, emphasizing why it remains optimal under local privacy and contrasting with non-local mechanisms.
