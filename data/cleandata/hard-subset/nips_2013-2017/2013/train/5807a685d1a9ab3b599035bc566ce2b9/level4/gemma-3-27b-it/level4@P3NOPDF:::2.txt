This paper investigates minimax bounds for probability estimation under the constraint of local privacy, considering both multinomial and density estimation problems. The authors establish sharp rates of convergence for both, demonstrating a quadratic reduction in effective sample size due to the privacy constraint in the multinomial case. Notably, they show that, when the privacy parameter α is small, this reduction may be modest, approaching the performance of the non-private setting. For density estimation, the authors demonstrate that achieving the optimal non-private rate is impossible under local privacy. Overall, the results are compelling, and the proofs appear sound based on my reading. To the best of my knowledge, the minimax bound for density estimation with a privacy constraint is a novel contribution.

My primary concern relates to the connection between the multinomial estimation bound presented here and existing work on noise complexity for differential privacy. While the paper does not explicitly acknowledge this connection, it’s highly relevant. Specifically, the work of Hardt and Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012) addresses worst-case lower bounds for the error of linear queries under differential privacy. The probability estimation problem studied in this paper can be viewed as a specific instance of the linear counting queries analyzed in those papers, using the same L₂ error metric. Given that local privacy implies a stronger constraint than differential privacy, lower bounds for differential privacy also hold for local privacy. Therefore, it’s crucial to understand whether the local privacy bound presented here improves upon existing bounds for differential privacy. The authors should address this comparison and include these relevant citations.

Regarding the density estimation result, the presentation of the lower bound is somewhat unclear. The authors state that the local privacy lower bound exceeds the non-private bound, but this comparison seems limited by the fact that the reported minimax bound holds only for densities expressible with a trigonometric basis. I question whether the non-private minimax bound cited as equation (13) generally holds within the Sobolev space defined in Definition 1, or specifically for the trigonometric basis case. A fair comparison requires establishing that the non-private lower bound also applies when restricting the function class to trigonometric basis functions.

**Additional Comments for the Rebuttal:**

The authors’ response partially clarifies the relationship to previous worst-case lower bounds. However, the work of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which also considers Mean Square Error, is also pertinent and should be acknowledged. I recommend the authors expand their discussion and incorporate these missing references into the paper.

In summary, this paper presents significant results on minimax bounds for both probability mass function and probability density function estimation under privacy guarantees. The paper is generally well-written, but a more thorough discussion of relevant work on noise complexity in differential privacy would strengthen the contribution and provide valuable context for the reader.
