- Studies local privacy where each user releases an α-differentially private signal and analyzes estimation under a minimax expected-error framework.
- Provides a thorough treatment of probability distribution estimation (discrete and continuous) when data is private even from the statistician.
- Derives sharp minimax rates of convergence under local privacy, highlighting trade-offs between privacy (α) and statistical efficiency.
- Shows that imposing local differential privacy reduces the effective sample size and can harm estimator quality.
- Proves Warner's randomized response is rate-optimal for multinomial estimation under local privacy.
- Shows histogram and orthogonal series estimators achieve optimal rates for density estimation in the local privacy setting.
- Presents tools to navigate the privacy–statistical-efficiency continuum.
- Paper is technically sound, original, and well-written with necessary mathematical rigor and comprehensive references.
- Heavy use of notation and technical content may limit accessibility for a broader audience.
- Suggests simplifying the explanation of minimax bounds to improve clarity for non-experts.
- Recommends adding discussion of implications for other norms (e.g., ℓ1) and considering broader settings with additional minimax lower bounds.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, conditional on addressing clarity and broader-scope suggestions.
