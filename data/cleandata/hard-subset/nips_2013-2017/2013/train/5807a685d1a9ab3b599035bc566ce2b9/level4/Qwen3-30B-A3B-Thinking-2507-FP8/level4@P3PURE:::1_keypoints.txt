- Paper derives a LeCam-style lower bound for estimation with privately perturbed data when the algorithm is untrusted; shows randomized response is optimal under certain conditions.  
- Results characterize fundamental limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed data.  
- Commentary contains imprecisions that could confuse readers unfamiliar with the area.  
- Connections to established differential privacy results are underdeveloped.  
- Analysis is restricted to local privacy, which is substantially different from standard differential privacy and this distinction is understated.  
- Broad claims about effects for large sample sizes n at privacy level α (e.g., bottom of p.4) are potentially incorrect and should be avoided.  
- Necessity of the restriction on α in Theorem 1 needs clarification; experiments suggest α ≈ 1 may be a practical limit.  
- Paper should discuss possible choices for ρ to clarify how different utility measures are captured.  
- Omits comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure for density estimation (relevant and uncited).  
- Does not address how alternative histogram estimation approaches for discrete data compare to randomized response.  
- Distinction between population and sample statistics needs clearer, earlier exposition.  
- A comparative discussion of related work (including Lei and other reviewer-suggested contributions) is essential for context.  
- After rebuttal, scope appears narrower: careful exposition of the local privacy model (largely covered by [10]) and novel density estimation bounds.
