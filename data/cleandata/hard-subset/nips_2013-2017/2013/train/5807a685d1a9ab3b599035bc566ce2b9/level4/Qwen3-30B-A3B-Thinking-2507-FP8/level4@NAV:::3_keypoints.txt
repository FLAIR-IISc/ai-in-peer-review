- Studies local differential privacy where each user reports an α-DP signal potentially based on their type and others’ signals, analyzed via minimax expected error.  
- Proves a lower bound on the minimax ℓ₂ estimation rate for users from a discrete distribution over d types.  
- Shows randomized response achieves the lower bound, establishing its minimax optimality for ℓ₂ estimation.  
- For density estimation, presents a scheme where each user perturbs only their own type and meets the lower bound.  
- Well-motivated and significant result: classic procedures attain optimal rates under local privacy; appropriate for NIPS.  
- Reservation 1: Minimax bounds and notation are overly cumbersome; simplification (e.g., focus on ℓ₂) would help clarity.  
- Reservation 2: Paper should discuss ℓ₁ norms (more restrictive) and relate results to existing ℓ₁ lower bounds in non-local DP.  
- Reservation 3: Framework appears tailored to local privacy; extending to broader settings would increase impact.  
- Important point to emphasize: optimal performance is achieved when users randomize solely based on their own type (ignore others’ signals).  
- New reservation: Section 3 overlaps related work recently accepted at FOCS 2013, reducing novelty and slightly tempering enthusiasm.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, but enthusiasm tempered by notation issues, limited scope, and overlap with recent work.
