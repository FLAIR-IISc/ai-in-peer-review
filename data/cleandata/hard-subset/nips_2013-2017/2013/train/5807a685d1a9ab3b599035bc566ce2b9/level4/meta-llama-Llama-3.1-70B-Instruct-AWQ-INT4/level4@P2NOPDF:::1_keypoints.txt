- Paper is a NIPS-formatted ArXiv manuscript deriving lower bounds for estimation under untrusted algorithms (local privacy) using Fano/LeCam arguments; shows randomized response is optimal in some cases.  
- Reviewer did not examine the supplementary material.  
- Strength: provides useful insights into limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed data.  
- Weakness: some imprecise commentary that could confuse casual readers.  
- Weakness: missing connections and comparisons with existing differential privacy literature.  
- Important clarification: restriction to local privacy makes the model distinct from (central) differential privacy; some author statements blur this distinction.  
- The necessity of the alpha restriction in Theorem 1 is unclear; empirical evidence suggests alpha ≈ 1 may be the realistic regime, so the bound’s relevance is uncertain.  
- Authors should avoid overly broad claims about the effect of providing differential privacy for large sample sizes n.  
- Suggestion: provide commentary on possible choices of the utility measure ρ to illustrate captured utility trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: compare to Lei’s M-estimation procedure (paper not cited but likely relevant).  
- Suggestion: discuss how other histogram-estimation algorithms for discrete data compare to randomized response, beyond rate-optimality.  
- After rebuttal: clarify and explicitly distinguish population vs. sample statistics at the start of the paper.  
- After rebuttal: include comparisons to related work (including Lei and other relevant papers) for proper context.  
- Additional assessment: closer inspection of [10] indicates the paper’s novel contribution is narrower—careful exposition of local privacy (largely in [10]) plus new bounds for density estimation.
