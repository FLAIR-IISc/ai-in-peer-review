- Paper studies local differential privacy and derives minimax expected-error lower bounds for probability estimation under l₂ norm.  
- Shows randomized response attains the l₂ minimax lower bound for discrete distributions over d types, proving its optimality.  
- Extends analysis to density estimation and shows a scheme where each user perturbs only their own type meets the minimax lower bound.  
- Contribution is significant and of interest to the NIPS community; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Presentation issues: minimax bounds are presented in a complex, hard-to-access manner and notation is cumbersome.  
- Suggests consistently expressing all bounds in the l₂ norm (since results are in l₂) for clarity.  
- Recommends adding discussion/comparison to l₁-norm results or bounds achievable under l₁.  
- Recommends relating results to existing differential-privacy lower bounds in the non-local (central) setting to contextualize differences.  
- Urges authors to explicitly state the important finding that individual randomization (ignoring others’ signals) is optimal despite the local model allowing more general strategies.  
- Suggests extending or discussing minimax lower bounds for a wider range of settings beyond the specific local privacy model analyzed.  
- Notes overlap concern: recent FOCS 2013 work (Section 3) appears to cover related results, making this more of an extension; lowers enthusiasm but still supports acceptance.
