- Studies local privacy where each user outputs an α-DP signal based only on their own type and analyzes minimax expected l₂ estimation error.  
- Proves a tight minimax lower bound for l₂ estimation with users from a discrete distribution over d types.  
- Shows randomized response achieves the lower bound, establishing its minimax optimality in this setting.  
- Extends analysis to density estimation and shows per-user perturbation schemes meet the minimax lower bound.  
- Key insight: optimal local privacy schemes need not exploit correlations across users—only per-user perturbation is required.  
- Recommends acceptance; results are theoretically important and of interest to the NIPS community.  
- Reservation 1: Minimax exposition is needlessly complex and notation cumbersome; state bounds explicitly in l₂ to improve clarity.  
- Reservation 2: Lacks discussion/comparison with l₁ norms and related classical (non-local) DP literature; adding l₁ analysis would strengthen the work.  
- Reservation 3: Minimax bounds focus narrowly on local privacy; deriving bounds for other settings (e.g., centralized DP) would better contextualize results.  
- Critical reservation: Section 3 closely overlaps with a FOCS 2013 paper, making the contribution more of an extension than wholly novel and reducing enthusiasm.  
- Overall: Establishes tight minimax rates for local privacy via randomized response; core technical arguments are sound and well-motivated.
