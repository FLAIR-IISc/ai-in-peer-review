- Problem: Studies probability estimation under local differential privacy where each user sends an α-DP randomized signal, possibly depending on others' types.  
- Objective: Analyzes minimax estimation error for estimating the underlying distribution measured by squared l2 distance.  
- Main contribution (discrete): Proves sharp minimax lower bound for discrete distributions over d types and shows randomized response attains this bound.  
- Main contribution (continuous): Gives a matching lower bound for density estimation and shows a simple non-interactive perturbation scheme is optimal.  
- Significance: Demonstrates classic randomized response is information-theoretically optimal, a valuable and practical result.  
- Presentation suggestion: Simplify the minimax framework explanation and streamline cumbersome notation, possibly state results directly in l2 terms.  
- Scope suggestion: Discuss l1 norm implications or compare l2 bounds to l1 and to other (non-local) DP lower bounds for broader context.  
- Generality concern: Clarify whether proof techniques extend to other settings or privacy models beyond local privacy.  
- Non-interactivity insight: Emphasize the important finding that interaction is unnecessary—optimal schemes only require per-user randomization based on their own type.  
- Update/overlap: Notes significant overlap with a FOCS 2013 paper (esp. Section 3), which may temper novelty but not the paper’s overall value.  
- Recommendation: Recommends acceptance, citing rigorous minimax analysis and sharp lower bounds matched by simple mechanisms.
