- Studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation.
- Establishes sharp minimax rates in both multinomial and density settings.
- Multinomial: local privacy reduces the effective sample size quadratically in α; for small α the effective sample size is same order as non-private.
- Density estimation: the optimal non-private rate is no longer achievable under local privacy.
- Proofs appear correct and minimax bounds for density estimation under privacy are novel.
- Main critique: inadequate discussion of prior noise-complexity lower bounds for differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).
- Those prior works give worst-case L2 error lower bounds for linear queries, and probability estimation is a special case; central-DP lower bounds thus apply to local privacy.
- Reviewer asks whether the paper’s local-privacy bounds improve on existing central-DP bounds.
- Density-estimation comparison is confusing: the privacy lower bound is proved for densities in a trigonometric basis subclass, so it’s unclear if the non-private lower bound in Eq.(13) holds for the full Sobolev space or only the subclass.
- A fair comparison requires the non-private lower bound to also hold for the trigonometric-basis subclass.
- Rebuttal partially clarified the DP relation; reviewer also suggests adding Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013) and other missing references and explanations.
- Overall: interesting, well-written paper with sharp results, but lacking discussion of relevant differential-privacy noise-complexity lower bounds.
