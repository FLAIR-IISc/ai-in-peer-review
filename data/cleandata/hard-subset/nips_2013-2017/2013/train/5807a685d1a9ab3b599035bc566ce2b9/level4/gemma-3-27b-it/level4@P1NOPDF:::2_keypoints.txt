- Paper studies minimax bounds under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation and establishes sharp minimax rates.

- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces the effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α, so for small constant α the effective sample size remains comparable to non-private.

- For density estimation, the optimal non-private convergence rate is unattainable under local privacy.

- Results are interesting and proofs appear correct; the minimax bound for density estimation is novel.

- Main concern: the multinomial minimax bound is closely related to prior noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012), but this connection is not discussed.

- Those prior works study worst-case lower bounds for linear queries under global differential privacy; the probability estimation problem here is a special case with the same L2 metric, so global DP lower bounds should apply and need comparison to the local DP bound.

- For density estimation, clarity is needed: the lower bound is proved for densities expanded in a trigonometric basis, so it is unclear whether the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) holds for the general Sobolev space in Definition 1 or only for the trigonometric basis; a fair comparison requires matching bases.

- Additional suggestion: include and discuss related work such as Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which also considers mean square error, and add the missing references.

- Summary: paper proves sharp minimax bounds for pmf and pdf estimation with privacy and is well-written, but should acknowledge and discuss known noise-complexity lower bounds for differential privacy.
