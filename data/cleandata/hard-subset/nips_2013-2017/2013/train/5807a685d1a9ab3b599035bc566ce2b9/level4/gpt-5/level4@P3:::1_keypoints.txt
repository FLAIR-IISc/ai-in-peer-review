- Paper studies probability estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), using minimax decision theory and information-theoretic tools to derive sharp lower bounds with matching algorithms.
- Multinomial result: under α-LDP the effective sample size is ~ nα^2/d, giving minimax squared-error ~ min{1, d/(nα^2)} and ℓ1-error ~ min{1, d/√(nα^2)}; Warner’s randomized response is rate-optimal.
- Density estimation result: for Sobolev class Fβ the nonprivate rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} deteriorates under LDP to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}; privatized histogram (β=1) and privatized orthogonal series (β≥1) achieve these rates.
- Paper focuses on local (not central) DP and on population estimation risk rather than accuracy of released sample statistics.
- Strength: technically solid—lower bounds use Fano/Le Cam plus LDP-tailored contraction bounds and are matched by efficient estimators.
- Strength: original sharp minimax characterization under LDP, especially for nonparametric density estimation, and formal identification of randomized response as optimal.
- Strength: significant practical relevance for crowdsourced/distributed analytics; provides clear benchmarks and simple mechanisms.
- Clarity (partial): problem formulation and core results are clearly stated and constructions are implementable.
- Weakness: exposition sometimes conflates “differential privacy” with the local model; needs early explicit clarification that results are for LDP.
- Weakness: must emphasize population versus sample targets and discuss implications and relationships between population-level and sample-level guarantees.
- Weakness: Theorem 1 restricts α∈[0,1]; unclear if this is essential and how conclusions extend to α>1 or to moderate α regimes—request more guidance.
- Weakness: limited discussion of other loss metrics (e.g., total variation, Hellinger) and whether LDP changes rates under those metrics.
- Weakness: related work coverage should better position against Lei (central DP), Wasserman & Zhou, Smith/De, and prior categorical-data mechanisms (Laplace counts, unary encoding, k-ary RR).
- Weakness: some claims overgeneralize (e.g., “effective sample size reduction”); suggestions to condition such statements on the multinomial/LDP setting and note incremental contributions vs prior arXiv versions.
- Quality: proofs and constructions are sound and matching; ask to state whether constants depend on d or β and to clarify the necessity of α-bounded assumptions.
- Clarity suggestions: add an explicit early section contrasting LDP vs central DP and population vs sample targets, and a short subsection on admissible loss functions and mappings to standard utilities.
- Originality: strong contribution in the LDP setting, with valuable formalization of multinomial and nonparametric optimal mechanisms.
- Significance: high for the LDP literature; provides definitive benchmarks and simple optimal procedures, with indirect relevance to central DP.
- Pros for acceptance: sharp matching minimax bounds, simple efficient mechanisms (randomized response; privatized histogram/orthogonal series), and clear privacy–statistical trade-offs.
- Cons for acceptance: exposition could confuse local vs central DP, limited α discussion and metric choices, and related work comparison needs expansion.
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifications about privacy model, estimation target, role of α, and expanded related work.
