- Paper provides a decision-theoretic analysis of local differential privacy, quantifying trade-offs between privacy level α and statistical accuracy.  
- For d-dimensional multinomial estimation, sharp minimax rates show local privacy reduces effective sample size to n * α^2 / d, and randomized response is minimax optimal.  
- For nonparametric density estimation in Sobolev class Fβ, minimax rate changes from n^(-2β/(2β+1)) to (n * α^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)), indicating a fundamental change in problem difficulty.  
- Authors provide matching, computationally efficient algorithms that achieve the derived lower bounds, proving the rates are sharp.  
- Significance: foundational contribution linking minimax theory and local privacy, offering principled quantification of privacy-accuracy trade-offs.  
- Originality: novel insight that randomized response is optimal and that local privacy alters convergence exponents for density estimation.  
- Technical quality: rigorous use of Le Cam, Fano, and information-theoretic tools with well-supported proofs and tight bounds.  
- Clarity: well-organized and precise but technically dense and assumes strong familiarity with minimax theory; notation can be cumbersome.  
- Suggestion — Accessibility: add a high-level intuitive walkthrough of minimax lower bounds to broaden readership.  
- Suggestion — Contextualization: include comparison of derived rates to central differential privacy results to highlight costs of the local model.  
- Suggestion — Practical implications: emphasize that simple non-interactive mechanisms are optimal, underscoring feasibility of local data collection.  
- Suggestion — Concurrent work: clearly situate contributions relative to very recent literature (e.g., FOCS 2013) to distinguish novelty.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — foundational, technically deep, and significant results justify acceptance despite presentation issues.
