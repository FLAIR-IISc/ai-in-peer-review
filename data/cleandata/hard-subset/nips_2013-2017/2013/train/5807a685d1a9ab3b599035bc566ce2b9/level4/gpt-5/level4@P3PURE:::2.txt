This paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under the constraint that the estimator must preserve the privacy of individual data. The authors adopt a local privacy definition and analyze two problems: multinomial estimation and density estimation. In both settings, they establish sharp minimax rates of convergence. For the discrete multinomial problem, they show that local privacy reduces the effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α; since α is often treated as a small constant, the effective sample size remains of the same order as in the non-private case. For density estimation, they demonstrate that the optimal non-private rate is no longer achievable when local privacy must be respected.

Overall, the results are very interesting. As far as I could check, the proofs are correct. To the best of my knowledge, minimax bounds for density estimation under privacy constraints have not been previously considered.

My main comment concerns the connection between the multinomial minimax bounds and prior work on noise complexity for differential privacy, which is not adequately discussed. In particular, two highly relevant papers are:
- Hardt and Talwar, On the Geometry of Differential Privacy, STOC 2010
- De, Lower Bounds in Differential Privacy, TCC 2012

These works study worst-case lower bounds on the error of linear queries subject to differential privacy, using the same L2 error metric. The probability estimation problem here is a special case of the linear counting queries considered in those papers. Moreover, local privacy imposes a stronger constraint than (central) differential privacy, so lower bounds for differential privacy also apply to local privacy. I would like to know whether the local privacy bounds in this paper improve over the existing bounds for differential privacy.

For the density estimation problem, the statement of results is somewhat confusing. The paper claims that the lower bound under local privacy exceeds the non-private lower bound. However, the minimax bound here is proved for the special case of densities expandable in a trigonometric basis. Does the non-private lower bound in equation (13) hold for the general Sobolev space defined in Definition 1, or only for the trigonometric-basis subclass? The comparison is only fair if the non-private lower bound also holds for the trigonometric basis.

Additional comments to the rebuttal:
The feedback partially clarifies the relation to previous worst-case lower bounds. Note also the work of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang, The Geometry of Differential Privacy: The Sparse and Approximate Cases (STOC 2013), which considers mean squared error. This paper proves sharp minimax bounds for pmf and pdf estimation with privacy guarantees. The results are interesting and the paper is well written. However, there is a lack of discussion of known noise-complexity lower bounds for differential privacy that are highly relevant to this work.
