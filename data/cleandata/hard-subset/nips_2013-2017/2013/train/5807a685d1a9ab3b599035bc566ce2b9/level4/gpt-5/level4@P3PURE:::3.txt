Local Privacy and Minimax Bounds: Sharp Rates for Probability Estimations

This paper studies local privacy, a setting in which each user outputs an α-differentially private signal based on her own type and the signals of the other n−1 users, and reports that signal to the data curator. The analysis is framed via minimax expected error: an adversary chooses a distribution over inputs (the users’ types), and we choose an α-DP local scheme to minimize the distance between an estimate derived from the original data and a corresponding estimate derived from the privatized reports (typically sums, aggregations, or averages). The authors provide a lower bound on the minimax rate for l2 estimation when user types are drawn from a discrete distribution over d categories. They then show that a simple randomized response mechanism meets this bound and is therefore optimal. The paper further analyzes density estimation, again establishing a minimax lower bound that is achieved by a scheme in which each person perturbs only her own type.

Overall, the paper is important and timely: it identifies sharp lower bounds and shows that a classic, simple procedure attains them. I expect the NIPS community to find these results interesting, and I recommend acceptance.

Reservations and suggestions:
- Presentation and notation: The exposition of minimax bounds could be simplified. The notation is often cumbersome; since all the reported bounds concern the l2 norm, it would help to phrase them explicitly with respect to l2 throughout.
- Relation to l1: I would appreciate a discussion of l1 error, which is more restrictive, or at least comments relating the given l2 bounds to l1 guarantees. It would also be useful to connect these results to known lower bounds in differential privacy from the classical (non-local) setting.
- Scope of minimax bounds: The minimax bounds developed here seem tailored to the local privacy setting. It would strengthen the paper to consider a broader set of settings and provide corresponding minimax lower bounds.
- Optimality of simple, per-user randomization: Although the local setting allows users to randomize their reports based on the remaining n−1 users, it is noteworthy that the simple scheme in which each person randomizes solely based on her own type is optimal. Please state this explicitly in the text.

New reservation: Since the list of FOCS 2013 accepted papers has now been published, it appears that this paper is, in part, an extension of existing work—especially the results in Section 3. I still recommend acceptance, but less strongly than before.

In sum, the paper analyzes local privacy via minimax rate bounds, provides lower bounds on the rate, and shows that randomized response attains the corresponding upper bounds. A nice result.
