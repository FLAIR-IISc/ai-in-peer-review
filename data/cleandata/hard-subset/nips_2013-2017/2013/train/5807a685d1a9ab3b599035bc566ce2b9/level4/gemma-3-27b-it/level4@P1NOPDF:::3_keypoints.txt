- Paper studies local privacy where each user sends an α-DP signal possibly depending on others’ signals, analyzed via a min-max expected error framework.
- Provides a lower bound on the minimax rate for l₂ distance estimation over discrete distributions on d types.
- Shows randomized response achieves the lower bound, proving its optimality in this setting.
- Extends results to density estimation and demonstrates a per-user perturbation scheme meets the minimax lower bound.
- Key surprising finding: allowing dependence on others’ signals does not improve over simple per-user randomization; authors should state this explicitly.
- Paper is strong, important, and of interest to the community; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Presentation concerns: min-max bounds could be made more accessible and notation is cumbersome.
- Suggestion to consistently express all bounds in terms of the l₂ norm for clarity.
- Request to discuss l₁ norm (more restrictive) and compare to l₁-based bounds and other DP lower bounds.
- Desire for broader scope: consider additional settings beyond the specific local-privacy model and provide minimax bounds for them.
- Reviewer notes overlap/extension relative to recent FOCS 2013 results (especially Section 3), reducing enthusiasm though still recommending acceptance.
