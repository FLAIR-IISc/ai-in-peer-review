- Rigorous theoretical analysis establishing sharp minimax convergence rates for multinomial and density estimation under α-local differential privacy.  
- Technically sound: sharp lower bounds with matching efficient algorithms and clear minimax framework; but Theorem 1's restriction to α ∈ [0,1] needs more practical justification.  
- Well-written and organized mathematically, but the paper sometimes conflates "local privacy" and "differential privacy" and should clarify this distinction early.  
- Density estimation presentation could use more intuition explaining rate differences between classical and private settings.  
- Original contributions: (1) thorough theoretical exposition linking local privacy to Warner’s randomized response and (2) novel sharp minimax bounds for density estimation under local privacy.  
- Should more explicitly acknowledge and compare to related work (e.g., Smith [24], Lei [19]) and contrast with prior local-privacy papers [9,13].  
- Significant: clarifies fundamental privacy–statistical efficiency trade-offs, shows randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation, and advances privacy-preserving minimax theory.  
- Strength: bridges classical statistics with privacy via sharp minimax bounds.  
- Strength: delivers optimal rate analysis for discrete and continuous estimation.  
- Strength: demonstrates Warner’s randomized response achieves optimal sample complexity.  
- Strength: provides computationally efficient algorithms attaining theoretical bounds.  
- Strength: clearly distinguishes population vs. sample estimation focus.  
- Weakness: conflation of local and (global) differential privacy in abstract/introduction.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of practical relevance of α ∈ [0,1] restriction in Theorem 1.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to related work (Lei [19], Smith [24]).  
- Weakness: needs clearer exposition of the privacy–utility trade-off continuum.
