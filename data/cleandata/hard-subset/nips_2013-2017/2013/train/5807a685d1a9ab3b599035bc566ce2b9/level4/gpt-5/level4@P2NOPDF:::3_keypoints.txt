- Paper studies estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) using a minimax expected-error framework for interactive and non-interactive protocols.  
- Main contribution: sharp minimax L2 lower bound for estimating discrete distributions over d categories.  
- Shows classical randomized response achieves the lower bound, proving its optimality in this LDP setting.  
- Extends results to density estimation with matching lower and upper bounds via per-user perturbation mechanisms.  
- Demonstrates that simple per-user (non-interactive) mechanisms suffice to attain optimal rates; recommend stating this explicitly.  
- Overall assessment: strong, timely results clarifying fundamental limits of locally private learning; recommendation to accept.  
- Suggest simplifying exposition by standardizing on L2 notation and moving general-norm formalism to an appendix.  
- Recommend adding discussion of L1 risk and relating L2 bounds to possible L1 analogs and central (non-local) DP bounds.  
- Advise discussing whether proof techniques extend beyond the local model (e.g., communication-constrained or hybrid models) and which ingredients are inherently local.  
- Raises concern about overlap with recent FOCS 2013 results (notably Section 3), reducing enthusiasm despite recommending acceptance.
