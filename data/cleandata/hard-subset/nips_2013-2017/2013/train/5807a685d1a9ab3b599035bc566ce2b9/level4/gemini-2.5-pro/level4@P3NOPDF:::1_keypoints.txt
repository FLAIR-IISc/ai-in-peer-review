- Paper derives information-theoretic lower bounds for estimation under the local privacy model using Fano’s inequality and Le Cam’s method.  
- Key corollary: randomized response is minimax optimal for discrete distribution estimation under local privacy.  
- Strength: rigorous, information-theoretic characterization of the statistical cost of local privacy, providing benchmarks for algorithms.  
- Strength: elegant result showing randomized response’s optimality for certain tasks.  
- Weakness: insufficiently clear distinction between local and central differential privacy; some statements are overly broad or potentially misleading.  
- Weakness: needs to explicitly contrast population vs. sample statistics early to frame contributions.  
- Weakness: missing engagement with related work (e.g., Lei on private M-estimation) and comparisons to other histogram/discrete-data methods.  
- Weakness: overlap with reference [10] reduces novelty; authors should delineate what is new (density estimation lower bounds).  
- Weakness: unclear necessity of the privacy-parameter restriction (α) in Theorem 1 and its relevance to practically used regimes (α ≈ 1).  
- Weakness: general loss ρ is useful but paper should discuss concrete losses (L1, L2, KL) to improve accessibility.  
- Recommendation: explicitly distinguish local vs. central DP throughout, incorporate related-work comparisons, and clarify novelty relative to [10].
