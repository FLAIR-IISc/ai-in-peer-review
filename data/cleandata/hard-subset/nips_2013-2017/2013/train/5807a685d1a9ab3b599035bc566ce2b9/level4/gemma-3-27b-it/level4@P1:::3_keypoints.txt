- Studies probability estimation under local privacy and establishes sharp minimax convergence rates, characterizing privacy–statistical efficiency trade-offs.  
- Demonstrates Warner’s randomized response is optimal for survey sampling in the local privacy setting.  
- Rigorous analysis linking classical privacy techniques with modern statistical decision theory; valuable for the NIPS community.  
- Recommends acceptance.  
- Presentation could be clearer: the minimax framework needs more accessible explanation for non-experts.  
- Paper uses heavy notation; following arguments is sometimes cumbersome—suggest consistently framing results in l2 norm.  
- Requests discussion of l1-norm implications or comparison with existing lower bounds in centralized differential privacy.  
- Minimax bounds appear specific to local privacy; recommend exploring broader settings and providing corresponding minimax lower bounds for greater generality.  
- Important observation: allowing users to randomize based on others’ signals does not outperform per-user randomized response—should be stated explicitly.  
- Notes overlap with recent FOCS 2013 accepted papers (especially Section 3), which reduces enthusiasm somewhat.  
- Concludes the work establishes matching lower bounds and shows randomized response attains the upper bound—significant and well-executed result.
