- Studies local privacy via a minimax expected-error framework where an adversary picks distributions and a designer picks DP schemes to minimize estimation error.  
- Proves a lower bound for minimax l2-rate for discrete distributions over d types.  
- Shows randomized response attains this lower bound, proving its optimality.  
- Extends results to density estimation; a scheme where users perturb only their own type also achieves the lower bound.  
- Contribution is valuable: establishes fundamental limits and provides theoretical justification for randomized response.  
- Recommends acceptance to NIPS based on the core results.  
- Exposition around the minimax bounds is technically sound but lacks intuitive accessibility and clarity.  
- Notation is cumbersome; since results are l2-specific, present bounds directly in l2 to improve readability.  
- Requests discussion/comparison with l1-norm bounds or at least known l1 lower bounds.  
- Wants broader contextualization with the wider differential-privacy literature, especially classical (non-local) bounds.  
- Notes minimax bounds appear specialized to the local setting; exploring broader scenarios would increase generality, or the paper should emphasize the surprising optimality of individual-only randomization despite possible conditioning on others’ reports.  
- Points out overlap with a FOCS 2013 paper (Section 3), which reduces novelty and weakens the acceptance recommendation, though the paper remains worthwhile.
