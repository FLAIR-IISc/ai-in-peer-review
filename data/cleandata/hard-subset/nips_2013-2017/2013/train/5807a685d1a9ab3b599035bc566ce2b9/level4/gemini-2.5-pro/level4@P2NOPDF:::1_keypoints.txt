- Paper studies minimax lower bounds for estimation under the local privacy model and shows randomized response is minimax-optimal for density estimation.  
- Strength: provides formal characterization of the privacy–utility trade-off and a theoretical benchmark via lower bounds.  
- Major concern: the paper does not clearly distinguish the local privacy model from the central differential privacy model, risking misleading generalizations.  
- Major concern: insufficient comparison to related work—fails to cite/compare Lei's M-estimation for private density estimation and lacks discussion of other private histogram algorithms.  
- Major concern: novelty is unclear relative to reference [10], which already develops the local privacy framework; the paper should explicitly limit its new contribution to the minimax bounds.  
- Suggestion: make the distinction between population and sample statistics explicit and early in the exposition.  
- Question: clarify whether the restriction on the privacy parameter α in Theorem 1 is necessary and how results apply when α ≈ 1.  
- Suggestion: provide interpretation and concrete examples of the loss function ρ to show the framework’s versatility across estimation tasks.
