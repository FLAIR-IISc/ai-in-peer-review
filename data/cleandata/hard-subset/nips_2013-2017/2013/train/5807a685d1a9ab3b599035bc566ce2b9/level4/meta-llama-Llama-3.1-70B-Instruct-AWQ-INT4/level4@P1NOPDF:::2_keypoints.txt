- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy, covering multinomial and density estimation, and establishes sharp minimax rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces the effective sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α; for small constant α this matches the non-private order.  
- For density estimation, local privacy makes the non-private optimal rate unattainable.  
- Results are interesting, proofs appear correct, and minimax bounds for density estimation under privacy are novel.  
- Main concern: insufficient acknowledgment of related work on noise-complexity/differential-privacy lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).  
- Those prior works study worst-case lower bounds for linear queries using L2 error; multinomial estimation is a special case and differential-privacy lower bounds also apply to local privacy—unclear if this paper improves on them.  
- Density-estimation statement is confusing: unclear whether the non-private lower bound (Eq.13) applies to the general Sobolev space or only to the trigonometric basis; fair comparison requires matching bases.  
- Rebuttal suggestion: cite and discuss Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which also considers mean-square error, and incorporate missing references/explanations.  
- Overall verdict: sharp, well-written contribution but lacking discussion of known noise-complexity lower bounds for differential privacy.
