- Paper studies minimax lower bounds for probability estimation under local differential privacy (LDP).  
- Analysis covers two problems: multinomial parameter estimation and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Authors derive sharp minimax convergence rates for both settings.  
- For multinomial estimation, LDP induces an effective-sample-size reduction quadratic in the privacy parameter α, implying similar order sample size to non-private when α is small.  
- For density estimation, LDP prevents achieving the optimal non-private convergence rate.  
- Proofs appear sound and the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy is a novel contribution.  
- Main concern: omission of discussion linking the multinomial minimax bound to prior noise-complexity/central-DP lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012), which consider the same L2 error for linear queries.  
- It is unclear whether the new LDP bound quantitatively improves over existing central-DP lower bounds, since central-DP bounds apply a fortiori.  
- Clarity issue for density result: the private lower bound is proved for densities represented in a trigonometric basis, so comparison to the cited non-private bound (Eq. 13) is ambiguous unless both bounds refer to the same function class.  
- Rebuttal partially clarified connections, but the authors should add missing citations (notably Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang 2013) and integrate these discussions into the revised manuscript.  
- Overall: significant, well-written paper with important results, but weakened by failure to acknowledge and discuss closely related prior work on DP lower bounds.
