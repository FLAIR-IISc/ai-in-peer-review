- Problem & model: studies probability estimation under non-interactive α-local differential privacy, deriving sharp minimax rates for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation.  
- Multinomial result: effective sample size reduces from n to nα^2/d (small α); randomized response is minimax optimal with matching ℓ2 and ℓ1 bounds.  
- Density result: non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades to (α^2 n)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP; private histogram (β=1) and privatized orthogonal series (β≥1) attain the rate; naïve Laplace-on-samples is suboptimal.  
- Techniques: develops information-contraction inequalities for the local model and uses Le Cam/Fano for lower bounds with matching constructive upper-bound mechanisms.  
- Relation to prior work: connects classical randomized response to modern DP optimality and complements DP literature, but overlaps with the authors’ earlier arXiv/FOCS work (notably for multinomial).  
- Quality: technically strong—matching lower/upper bounds and simple, efficient mechanisms with carefully derived information bounds.  
- Clarity: solid exposition but notation-heavy; should introduce the non-interactive LDP setup and intuition earlier.  
- Originality: impactful characterization of LDP minimax rates and density-estimator constructions, though parts (Section 3) overlap prior FOCS2013 results and need clearer novelty claims.  
- Significance: provides important theoretical and practical guidance on sample complexity and mechanism choice under local privacy.  
- Strengths: sharp minimax bounds, optimality of randomized response, elegant contraction bounds, and practical message about sample-size reduction and rate shifts.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: simplify notation, highlight ℓ1 guarantees, compare to central DP rates, clarify novelty vs FOCS2013, give tuning advice and include simulations.  
- Recommendation: Accept, conditional on clarifying novelty and improving exposition; main concerns are overlap with prior work and lack of empirical illustrations.
