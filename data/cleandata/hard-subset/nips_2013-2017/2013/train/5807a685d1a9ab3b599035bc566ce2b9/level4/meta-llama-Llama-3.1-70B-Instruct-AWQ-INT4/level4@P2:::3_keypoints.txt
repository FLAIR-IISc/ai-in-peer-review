- Studies local privacy where each user outputs an α-differentially private signal and analyzes estimation quality via a minimax expected-error framework.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly explains local privacy, its relation to differential privacy, and the min-max analysis, with good ties to prior work.  
- Main technical contribution: sharp lower bounds for the minimax rate for l2 distance estimation with users from a discrete distribution over d types.  
- Shows randomized response attains these bounds, proving it optimal for the l2 discrete setting.  
- Provides a lower bound for density estimation and matches it with a scheme where each user perturbs only their own type.  
- Results have significant implications for designing privacy-preserving statistical procedures and offer a foundation for future research.  
- Presentation issues: some notation is unnecessarily complex and could be simplified.  
- Exposition issues: proofs would benefit from more intuition to aid reader understanding.  
- Missing a comparison to other differential-privacy lower bounds to contextualize the results.  
- Would be strengthened by a discussion of implications for other settings beyond the ones analyzed.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance: strengths and significance outweigh the noted weaknesses.
