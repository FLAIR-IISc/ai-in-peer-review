- Paper presents a Fano/LeCam-style lower bound for estimation with privately perturbed data and shows randomized response is optimal in a specific sense.
- Supplementary material was not reviewed.
- Pros: characterizes fundamental limits of learning from privacy-perturbed data.
- Cons: commentary contains imprecisions that could mislead casual readers.
- Cons: lacks connections to established differential privacy literature.
- The paper assumes local privacy, which significantly differs from central differential privacy and is not emphasized enough.
- Phrases suggesting the results extend to differential privacy for large n are overly broad and potentially misleading.
- The necessity of the α restriction in Theorem 1 is unclear given practical α≈1 limits in learning under DP.
- The analysis would benefit from discussion of plausible choices for the utility measure ρ.
- The density estimator should be compared to Lei’s M-estimation procedure, which is unreferenced.
- Though randomized response attains optimal rates, other histogram estimators for discrete data are not evaluated.
- After rebuttal: the population vs. sample statistic distinction needs clearer upfront explanation.
- After rebuttal: comparison to related work (including Lei and recent published [10]) is essential for context.
- After rebuttal: main novelty appears to be density estimation bounds; the exposition of the local privacy model is largely derivative of prior work.
