- Derives sharp minimax lower bounds under local privacy for multinomial parameter estimation and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Shows local privacy induces an effective sample-size penalty for multinomial estimation that scales quadratically with the privacy parameter α.  
- Notes that for small α the effective sample size is of the same order as in the non-private setting.  
- Proves that under local privacy the optimal non-private density-estimation rate is no longer attainable.  
- Results are novel and significant; proofs appear correct and the minimax density-analysis under privacy is a new contribution.  
- Paper is well-written overall.  
- Major weakness: omits discussion of highly relevant prior work on lower bounds for differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov, Talwar, & Zhang 2013).  
- Multinomial bounds relate to prior worst-case L2 lower bounds for linear counting queries under central DP; the paper should clarify whether its local-privacy bounds quantitatively improve on those central-DP bounds.  
- Authors should add the missing references and thoroughly situate their results within the existing literature.  
- Density-estimation comparison needs clarification: ensure the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) and the paper’s private lower bound are derived for the same trigonometric-expansion function class (not just the broader Sobolev class) for a fair comparison.
