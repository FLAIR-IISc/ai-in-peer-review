- Paper applies Fano/LeCam arguments to derive lower bounds for estimation under local (untrusted-algorithm) privacy; shows randomized response is optimal in some sense.
- Reviewer did not examine the supplementary material.
- Pro: provides insight into limitations of learning from data perturbed for privacy.
- Con: some imprecise commentary that could confuse casual readers.
- Con: missing connections to existing differential privacy results.
- The restriction to local privacy is crucial and makes the model significantly different from differential privacy; authors should avoid broad/incorrect comparisons (e.g., bottom of p.4).
- Question whether the restriction on α in Theorem 1 is necessary, since practical DP settings may have α ≈ 1 and implications are unclear.
- Recommend adding discussion of choices for the utility measure ρ to illustrate what different measures capture.
- Ask for comparison between the paper’s density estimator and Lei’s M-estimation procedure (Lei not cited).
- Suggest comparing randomized response to other histogram-estimation algorithms despite its optimal rate, to show practical tradeoffs.
- Addendum: clarify the distinction between population and sample statistics early in the paper.
- Addendum: include comparisons to related work (Lei and other papers raised by reviewers).
- Addendum: after inspecting [10], the paper’s contribution is seen as twofold—careful exposition of the local privacy model (largely in [10]) and new bounds for density estimation (the latter is novel).
