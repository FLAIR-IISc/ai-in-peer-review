- Paper uses a Fano/LeCam argument to give lower bounds for estimation under local (untrusted-algorithm) privacy; randomized response is an optimal strategy in some settings.
- Reviewer did not examine the supplementary material.
- Strength: characterizes limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed data.
- Weakness: some imprecise commentary that could mislead casual readers.
- Missing connections and comparisons to existing differential privacy results.
- Important distinction: the paper assumes local privacy, which is different from (central) differential privacy and should not be conflated.
- Question on necessity of the restriction on α in Theorem 1; unclear relevance when α ≈ 1 as seen in some DP settings.
- Recommend adding discussion of possible choices of ρ to clarify captured utility measures.
- Paper should compare to Lei’s M-estimation procedure (missing citation) and related work.
- Need comparisons to other histogram estimation methods for discrete data, not just randomized response.
- Clarify population vs. sample statistics early in the paper.
- Related work [10] overlaps: this paper’s primary contributions are careful exposition of local privacy and new density-estimation bounds, but the exposition is largely contained in [10].
- Overall: bounds for density estimation are novel, but contextualization and clarity (comparisons, model distinctions, and precise statements) need improvement.
