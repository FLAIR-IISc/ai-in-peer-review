- Studies local differential privacy with an adversarial minimax expected-error framework for estimating aggregates (sums/averages) from alpha-DP user reports.  
- Proves a lower bound on the minimax l2 estimation rate for users drawn from a discrete distribution over d types.  
- Shows simple randomized response attains this lower bound, establishing its optimality for the discrete probability estimation task.  
- Analyzes density estimation and proves a matching lower bound achieved by a mechanism in which each user perturbs only their own type.  
- Key insight: per-user randomization independent of others’ signals is optimal; reviewer suggests stating this explicitly.  
- Exposition critique: notation is cumbersome; recommend simplifying and presenting bounds directly and consistently in l2.  
- Suggests adding discussion/analysis for the l1 norm or relating l2 bounds to l1 guarantees and to known (central) differential privacy lower bounds.  
- Generality concern: current minimax bounds are tailored to the local model; recommend discussing broader problem classes and providing more general lower bounds where possible.  
- Novelty reservation: potential overlap/extension of results from recently published FOCS 2013 papers (notably Section 3); weakens recommendation slightly.  
- Overall assessment: solid, noteworthy contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance (though somewhat less strongly due to overlap).
