- Paper adapts arXiv work into NIPS format, using a Fano/LeCam-style argument to derive lower bounds for estimation with private data under a distrustful algorithm designer.  
- Key consequence: randomized response is identified as an optimal strategy in this local-privacy framework.  
- Strength: characterizes fundamental limits of learning from privacy-perturbed data.  
- Weakness: some imprecise statements risk misleading readers about the results.  
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with existing differential privacy literature.  
- Major clarification needed: the paper focuses on local privacy, which is substantially different from standard differential privacy, and this distinction must be clearly communicated.  
- Specific misleading phrasing (e.g., page 4) should be reworded to avoid incorrect generalizations about differential privacy effects.  
- Authors should explicitly state the exact privacy guarantees provided by their local privacy model.  
- Question about technical assumptions: unclear if the restriction on α in Theorem 1 is necessary, especially since α ≈ 1 is common in practice.  
- Practical guidance lacking: the paper should discuss how to choose ρ and illustrate how different utility measures fit the analysis.  
- Missing citation/connection: relate the proposed density estimator to Lei’s M-estimation procedure and cite that work.  
- Contextualization needed: compare randomized response’s convergence-rate optimality to other histogram-estimation algorithms for discrete data under privacy constraints.  
- Post-rebuttal concerns: clarify population vs. sample statistics up front in the introduction.  
- Post-rebuttal concern: provide a more comprehensive comparison to related work (including Lei and other reviewers’ suggestions).  
- Contribution reassessment: while bounds for density estimation are new, the exposition of the local privacy model overlaps substantially with reference [10], reducing novelty.
