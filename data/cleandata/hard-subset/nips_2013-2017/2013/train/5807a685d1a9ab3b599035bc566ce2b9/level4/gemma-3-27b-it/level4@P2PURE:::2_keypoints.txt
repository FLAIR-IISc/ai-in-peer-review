- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy, focusing on multinomial and density estimation.  
- Establishes sharp minimax rates for both multinomial and density problems.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α.  
- Since α is often a small constant, the effective sample size can remain comparable to the non-private case.  
- For density estimation, achieving the non-private optimal rate is impossible under local privacy.  
- Proofs appear correct and the work is claimed to be the first to study minimax bounds for density estimation under privacy.  
- Main concern: close relationship between the multinomial minimax bound and prior noise-complexity/differential-privacy lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) is not adequately acknowledged.  
- The probability estimation problem is a special case of linear counting queries with L2 error, so central DP lower bounds may apply or inform the local DP case.  
- Need clarification whether the new local-privacy bound improves upon or is implied by existing central-DP lower bounds.  
- Density result is unclear: the lower bound is shown for densities with a trigonometric basis—uncertain if the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) holds for the full Sobolev space or only the trigonometric case.  
- Rebuttal should also acknowledge related work by Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on mean square error and incorporate missing references/explanations.  
- Overall: results are interesting and paper is well-written, but it lacks sufficient acknowledgement and comparison to relevant existing differential-privacy lower bounds.
