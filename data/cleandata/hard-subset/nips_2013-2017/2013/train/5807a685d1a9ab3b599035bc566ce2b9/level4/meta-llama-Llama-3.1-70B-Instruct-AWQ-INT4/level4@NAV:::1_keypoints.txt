- Reformatted ArXiv manuscript using a Fano/LeCam approach to derive lower bounds for estimation when the learner is untrusted by the data holder; randomized response is optimal under certain conditions.
- Results give valuable insights into limits of learning from privacy-preserving perturbed data.
- Some imprecise commentary risks confusing casual readers and should be tightened.
- Paper lacks clear connections and comparisons to existing differential privacy results.
- The distinction between local privacy (the paper’s focus) and differential privacy is crucial and under-emphasized; some statements overreach.
- Necessity of the alpha restriction in Theorem 1 is unclear given experiments suggesting alpha ≈ 1 may be attainable in some DP settings.
- Authors should discuss choices of the utility parameter ρ to show how different utility measures are captured.
- Missing citation and comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure; a direct comparison would be valuable.
- Unclear how other histogram/estimation methods for discrete data compare to randomized response despite its optimal rate.
- Need a clearer explanation of population vs. sample statistics early in the paper.
- Overall contribution may be limited: detailed exposition of local privacy largely overlaps with [10], while the novel part is the density-estimation bounds.
