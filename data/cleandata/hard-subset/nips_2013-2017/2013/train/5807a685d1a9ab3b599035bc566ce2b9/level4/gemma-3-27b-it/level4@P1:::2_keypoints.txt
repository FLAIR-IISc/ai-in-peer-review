- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local (individual) privacy for multinomial and density estimation, showing reduced effective sample size and different convergence rates versus non-private case.  
- Results are technically sound and the density estimation minimax bound under privacy appears novel.  
- Main critique: insufficient discussion of related differential privacy (central DP) literature, especially worst-case lower bounds for linear queries (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).  
- The multinomial estimation can be seen as a special case of linear counting queries; need to clarify whether the paper’s bounds improve or relate to known DP bounds given local privacy is stronger.  
- Also should cite and relate to Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (2013), who study mean square error under DP.  
- Density estimation presentation needs precision: Theorem 2 applies to densities expandable in a trigonometric basis, so clarify whether the non-private lower bound (eq.13) holds for the full Sobolev space or only the trigonometric case.  
- Clarify conditions under which both private and non-private density lower bounds are valid for a fair comparison.  
- Arguments for acceptance: sharp minimax bounds under local privacy; clear privacy–efficiency trade-off; technically sound and well-written; novel study of density estimation under privacy.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate acknowledgment of broader DP literature; unclear scope/assumptions for the density estimation result.
