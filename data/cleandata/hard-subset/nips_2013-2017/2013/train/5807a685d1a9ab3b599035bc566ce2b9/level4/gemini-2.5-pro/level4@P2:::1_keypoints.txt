- Paper studies privacy–accuracy trade-offs for probability estimation under α-local differential privacy using a minimax decision-theoretic framework.  
- For multinomial estimation, derives sharp minimax L2 and L1 rates and shows local privacy reduces effective sample size to nα²/d.  
- Shows Warner's randomized response is rate-optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- For Sobolev density estimation, proves local privacy changes the convergence exponent from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)}.  
- Provides computationally efficient private histogram and orthogonal series estimators that attain the optimal rates.  
- Strength: tackles a fundamental, significant problem quantifying the statistical cost of privacy.  
- Strength: uses rigorous minimax analysis (Le Cam, Fano) to obtain general lower bounds.  
- Strength: results are sharp and insightful, linking classical randomized response to modern DP and revealing a novel nonparametric penalty.  
- Strength: gives constructive, practical algorithms matching the theoretical limits.  
- Weakness: insufficient clarity differentiating local versus central differential privacy; some wording overgeneralizes to “differential privacy” without the local qualifier.  
- Suggestion: explicitly define and contrast local and central DP in the introduction and clarify focus on population-parameter estimation versus sample statistics.  
- Weakness: related work coverage is incomplete (e.g., missing discussion/citation of Lei on DP M-estimators and comparisons to other histogram mechanisms).  
- Suggestion: cite and compare to Lei [19], discuss differences in privacy models and rates, and compare other private histogram mechanisms to randomized response.  
- Weakness: scope questions—Theorem 1 restricts α ∈ [0,1] and the treatment of general semi-metric errors could be expanded.  
- Suggestion: comment on whether the α-range is technical or essential and discuss how other utility measures fit into the framework.  
- Weakness: originality concerns due to overlap with a longer arXiv preprint; main new contribution for this submission may be the density estimation bounds.  
- Suggestion: clearly state the relationship to the preprint and specify which results are new in this submission.  
- Conclusion: technically strong and influential work; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing clarity, related-work positioning, and novelty/contextualization.
