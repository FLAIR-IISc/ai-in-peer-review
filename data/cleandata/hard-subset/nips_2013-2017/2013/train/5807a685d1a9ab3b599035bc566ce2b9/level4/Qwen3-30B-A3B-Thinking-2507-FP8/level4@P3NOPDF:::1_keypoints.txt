1. Paper adapts an arXiv manuscript to NIPS format and proves a LeCam-style lower bound showing randomized response is optimal under a local privacy model.  
2. Strength: provides a rigorous theoretical treatment of limits on learning from locally privatized (untrusted) data.  
3. Major concern: repeatedly conflates local privacy with differential privacy, causing misleading statements and confusion about applicability.  
4. Major concern: insufficient engagement with existing DP and local-privacy literature, especially failing to clearly relate to or distinguish from recent work [10] that subsumes core results.  
5. Major concern: Theorem 1’s restriction on α (e.g., α < 1/2) appears arbitrary and its relevance to practical settings (α ≈ 1) is unclear.  
6. Minor concern: Need discussion of how different choices of utility metric ρ (KL, Hellinger, etc.) affect the privacy-utility trade-off.  
7. Minor concern: Claims of randomized response optimality lack comparison to other estimators (e.g., Lei’s M-estimation) and local-privacy techniques.  
8. Minor concern: Paper must explicitly clarify population vs. sample statistics early to avoid confusion about estimation error definitions.  
9. Clarification requested: Replace "differential privacy" with "local privacy" throughout to align terminology with the model used.  
10. Clarification requested: State precisely under what conditions (α, choice of ρ) randomized response is declared "optimal."  
11. Clarification requested: Ensure key details from the supplementary material (e.g., about ρ and comparisons) are included in the main text.  
12. Rebuttal note: Authors promised fixes (population/sample clarification and comparisons), but [10] already covers the local privacy framework, making this mostly a rederivation and narrowing novelty.  
13. Overall assessment: Valid technical lower bound but limited novelty and undermined credibility due to model mischaracterization and missing literature comparisons.  
14. Recommendation: Reject unless authors explicitly distinguish local privacy from DP, compare rigorously to [10] and Lei, justify the α restriction, and precisely scope randomized response optimality.
