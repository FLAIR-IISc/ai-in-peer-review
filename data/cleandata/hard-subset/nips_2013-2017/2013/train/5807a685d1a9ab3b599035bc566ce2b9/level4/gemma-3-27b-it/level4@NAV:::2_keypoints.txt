- Paper studies minimax bounds under local (individual) privacy for multinomial and density estimation, deriving precise rates of convergence.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size by a factor proportional to alpha^2, often manageable when alpha is small.  
- For density estimation, local privacy prevents achieving the non-private optimal convergence rate.  
- Results are compelling, proofs appear sound, and a minimax bound for density estimation under privacy seems novel.  
- Main concern: multinomial analysis closely relates to prior noise-complexity/lower-bound work for differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) that is not acknowledged.  
- Since local privacy is stronger than differential privacy, differential-privacy lower bounds apply; authors should clarify whether their local-privacy bounds improve on existing DP bounds.  
- Density-estimation lower bound presentation is unclear: the minimax bound is shown for trigonometric-basis densities, but it must be clarified if the non-private bound (eq.13) holds for the full Sobolev space or only the trigonometric case.  
- Rebuttal partially clarified prior-work connections, but Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) — which also considers MSE — is still missing and should be cited.  
- Recommendation: incorporate and discuss the missing differential-privacy lower-bound literature and clarify the scope of density results (trigonometric vs. full Sobolev) in a revision.
