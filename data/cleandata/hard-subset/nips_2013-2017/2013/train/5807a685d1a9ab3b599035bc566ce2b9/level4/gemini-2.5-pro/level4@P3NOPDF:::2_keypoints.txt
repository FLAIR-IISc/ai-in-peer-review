- Paper derives sharp minimax rates under local differential privacy (LDP) for multinomial and density estimation.  
- For multinomial estimation, LDP reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α, so for small α the convergence rate matches the non-private order.  
- For density estimation, LDP prevents achieving the optimal non-private rate, indicating a fundamental statistical cost to privacy.  
- Results are theoretically significant, proofs appear correct, and the minimax bound for density estimation under LDP is likely novel.  
- Main weakness: insufficient discussion of connections to prior work on differential privacy lower bounds, especially noise complexity for linear queries.  
- Multinomial estimation is a special case of linear counting queries studied by Hardt & Talwar (2010), De (2012), and related work; these should be explicitly compared.  
- Since LDP is stronger than central DP (CDP), CDP lower bounds apply to LDP; authors must clarify whether their LDP lower bound is strictly stronger than existing CDP bounds.  
- Recommend citing Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) as directly relevant to MSE comparisons.  
- Clarify the density-estimation comparison: the paper’s lower bound uses a trigonometric basis while the cited non-private bound (eq.13) is for a general Sobolev class—ensure an apples-to-apples comparison.  
- If the non-private bound holds for the trigonometric basis, the claim is valid; otherwise, the claim that the non-private optimal rate is unattainable under LDP should be qualified.  
- Overall: strengthen literature positioning and clarify comparison details to increase the paper’s impact.
