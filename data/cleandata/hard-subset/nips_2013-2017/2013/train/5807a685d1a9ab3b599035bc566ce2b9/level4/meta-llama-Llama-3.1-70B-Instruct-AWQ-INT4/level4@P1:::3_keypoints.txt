- Studies local differential privacy (α-DP per user) using a minimax expected-error framework for estimating aggregates.  
- Derives a minimax lower bound for l2-distance estimations when users come from a discrete distribution over d types.  
- Shows randomized response attains that lower bound, proving its optimality for the l2 setting.  
- Extends results to density estimation and shows a per-user perturbation scheme meets the minimax lower bound.  
- Concludes that simple per-user-only randomization is optimal in this local-privacy model (authors should state this explicitly).  
- Paper is important and of likely interest to the NIPS community; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Exposition of minimax bounds is overly complex and could be simplified.  
- Notation is cumbersome; bounds could be presented directly in l2 notation for clarity.  
- Lacks discussion of l1-norm results or connections to other differential-privacy lower bounds (classical/non-local setting).  
- Minimax bounds appear tailored to the specific local-privacy setting; broader settings are not analyzed.  
- New concern: overlap with recently published FOCS 2013 work, especially Section 3, making the paper seem like an extension.  
- Because of the overlap reservation, the recommendation for acceptance is weaker than initially stated.  
- Overall summary: solid analysis showing randomized response meets fundamental lower bounds — a nice and important result.
