- Paper is a NIPS-formatted arXiv manuscript using Fano/Le Cam arguments to give lower bounds for estimators on privatized data.  
- Shows randomized response is optimal in a specific sense when the analyst is untrusted.  
- Reviewer did not read the supplementary material (caveat on completeness of review).  
- Results characterize limits of learning from perturbed (private) data.  
- Exposition contains imprecision that may confuse casual readers.  
- Missing connections and comparisons to existing differential privacy literature.  
- Crucial distinction between local privacy (used here) and standard global differential privacy is not emphasized enough.  
- Sweeping claims about the effect of providing differential privacy for large n are potentially incorrect and should be avoided.  
- Unclear if the restriction on alpha in Theorem 1 is necessary; experiments suggest alpha ≈ 1 may be relevant.  
- Need discussion of choices of the utility measure rho to show how different utilities fit the analysis.  
- Missing comparisons to related methods: Lei’s M-estimation and other histogram estimators for discrete data.  
- After rebuttal: clarify population vs sample statistics early and add comparisons to related work (including [10]); contribution viewed as mainly exposition of local privacy plus new density-estimation bounds, with narrower novelty than initially implied.
