- Paper studies local α-differential privacy with users generating private signals possibly depending on other users, analyzed via minimax expected-error framework.  
- Establishes tight minimax lower bounds for ℓ2-distance probability estimation with d discrete types.  
- Shows randomized response achieves the lower bound, proving its optimality for the ℓ2 setting.  
- For density estimation, gives a perturbation scheme where each user perturbs only their own type that meets the minimax lower bound.  
- Well-written and important result; reviewer recommends acceptance and expects NIPS community interest.  
- Suggest simplifying the exposition of minimax bounds for clarity.  
- Notation is cumbersome; since results are for ℓ2 norm, rephrasing bounds directly in ℓ2 would be more intuitive.  
- Reviewer requests discussion/comparison with ℓ1-norm results and other (non-local) differential-privacy lower bounds.  
- Wishes authors had considered broader settings beyond the local-privacy model and provided minimax lower bounds for them.  
- Suggests explicitly stating that per-user randomization based only on one’s own type is optimal, despite allowing dependence on the other n−1 users.  
- New reservation: overlap/extension relative to FOCS 2013 accepted papers (particularly Section 3) weakens endorsement somewhat.
