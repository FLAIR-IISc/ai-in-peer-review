- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation, deriving sharp convergence rates.
- For multinomial estimation, authors show local privacy reduces effective sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α, leaving the order of the sample size similar to non-private case when α is small.
- For density estimation, authors show the optimal non-private rate is unattainable under local privacy.
- Proofs appear correct and the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy seems novel.
- Main concern: multinomial minimax bound is closely related to prior noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy and insufficiently referenced.
- Missing relevant citations include Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012), which analyze worst-case lower bounds for linear queries in L2 error.
- Since local privacy is stronger than central DP, central DP lower bounds apply and the reviewer requests clarification whether the paper’s local privacy bounds improve on these prior DP lower bounds.
- Density estimation presentation is confusing: it’s unclear if the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) applies to the full Sobolev class or only the trigonometric-subclass used in the paper, and comparison is fair only if both use the same class.
- Rebuttal should incorporate explanations and missing references (also Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang, STOC 2013) and clarify the relationship to prior worst-case MSE lower bounds.
