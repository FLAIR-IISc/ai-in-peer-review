- Paper studies minimax probability estimation under α-local differential privacy for two problems: d-dimensional multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.
- For d-dim multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size to n * α^2 / d.
- Warner’s randomized response (1965) is shown to be minimax optimal for the multinomial problem.
- For Sobolev density class F_β, the private minimax rate is (n * α^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)), strictly slower than the nonprivate n^(-2β/(2β+1)) rate.
- The authors provide matching lower bounds and computationally efficient estimators that achieve the optimal private rates.
- Strength: forges a rigorous connection between minimax decision theory and differential privacy, quantifying the utility–privacy trade-off.
- Strength: technically strong results with sharp (matching) upper and lower bounds.
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly organized.
- Strength: impactfully links a classic technique (randomized response) to modern differential privacy.
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with prior TCS literature on lower bounds for answering linear queries (e.g., Hardt & Talwar, De, Nikolov/Talwar/Zhang).
- Question: authors should clarify how their multinomial lower bound compares to central-DP linear-query lower bounds and whether local privacy yields a provably stronger bound (by what factor).
- Weakness/clarification needed: explicitly confirm the classical nonprivate density rate applies to the same Sobolev subclass (trigonometric basis) used in Theorem 2 for a fair comparison.
- Overall recommendation: positive paper with important contributions, but novelty of the multinomial bound and positioning require addressing missing references and clearer comparisons to prior lower bounds.
