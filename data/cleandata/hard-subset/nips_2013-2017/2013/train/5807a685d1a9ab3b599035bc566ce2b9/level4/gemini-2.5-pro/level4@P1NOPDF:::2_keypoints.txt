- Paper derives sharp minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy for two problems: multinomial estimation and density estimation.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy imposes a penalty equivalent to reducing the effective sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α; if α is a small constant, effective sample size is same order as non-private.  
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private convergence rate is unattainable under local privacy.  
- Proofs appear correct, and the minimax analysis for density estimation under privacy is novel.  
- Major concern: insufficient discussion of related work on noise complexity in differential privacy, particularly Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012), which give related worst-case lower bounds in the central model.  
- Authors should clarify whether their local-model lower bounds improve on or are implied by existing central-model bounds and explicitly compare the results.  
- Ambiguity in the density-estimation comparison: it’s unclear whether the non-private lower bound in Eq.(13) applies to the full Sobolev class or only to densities representable in a trigonometric basis; this must be clarified for a fair comparison.  
- Rebuttal partially clarified connections; reviewer urges adding discussion of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) (MSE analysis) and incorporating those references and explanations into the final paper.  
- Overall: novel, significant, and well-written work, but weakened by lack of situating results within prior DP lower-bound literature; addressing this would substantially strengthen the contribution.
