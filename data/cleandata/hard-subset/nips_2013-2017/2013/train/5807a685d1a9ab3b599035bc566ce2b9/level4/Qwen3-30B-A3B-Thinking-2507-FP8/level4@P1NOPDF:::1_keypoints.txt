- Paper establishes a lower bound on estimation accuracy under a local privacy model using a Fano–LeCam-style argument and shows randomized response is optimal in some settings.  
- Strength: Clearly characterizes fundamental limitations of learning from privately perturbed data, offering a valuable theoretical foundation.  
- Weakness: Conflates local privacy with differential privacy (DP), producing confusing and potentially incorrect statements (e.g., bottom of p.4) and risking reader misinterpretation.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 imposes restrictions on α; unclear if these are essential or merely technical, and whether the bounds remain meaningful for practical α≈1.  
- Weakness: Missing contextualization—fails to cite Lei’s M-estimation for density estimation under privacy and omits comparisons to other discrete-data estimators (beyond randomized response/histograms).  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of how different utility measures (ρ) might affect the analysis and interpretation of results.  
- Additional context: Contribution appears twofold (local-privacy exposition and density-estimation bounds), but [10] already covers much of the local-privacy exposition, narrowing novelty.  
- Rebuttal addendum: Must clarify population vs. sample statistic distinction early and provide a thorough comparison to Lei and related approaches.  
- Conclusion: Technical results are sound but presentation and literature positioning need improvement (clear local-privacy exposition, explicit acknowledgment of Lei, and clarification of α) to preserve the contribution’s significance.
