- Paper presents a NIPS-formatted arXiv manuscript using Fano/LeCam arguments to derive lower bounds for estimation with untrusted algorithms on private data.  
- Randomized response appears as an optimal strategy in some settings.  
- Strength: provides valuable insights into limitations of learning from perturbed/private data.  
- Weakness: commentary on results is imprecise and may confuse casual readers.  
- Weakness: connections to differential privacy literature are not explicitly established.  
- Important: the paper restricts to local privacy, which is distinct from differential privacy and this distinction needs clearer emphasis.  
- Problematic statement: claims equating effects of differential privacy at level α to their results (bottom of p.4) are overly broad or potentially incorrect and should be removed.  
- Unclear necessity of the restriction on α in Theorem 1; experiments suggest α ≈ 1 may be the realistic limit for some problems.  
- The paper should provide commentary on choices of ρ to clarify what utility measures are captured by the analysis.  
- Missing comparison and citation: Lei’s M-estimation work is relevant and should be discussed.  
- Missing comparisons: other histogram and discrete-data estimators should be compared to the proposed density estimator, especially on convergence rates.  
- Recommend clearer distinction between population and sample statistics at the start and explicit comparison to related work, acknowledging overlap with [10].  
- Contribution claim: paper’s new parts are careful exposition of local privacy (largely in [10]) and novel bounds for density estimation; the overlap and unique contributions must be clearly articulated.
