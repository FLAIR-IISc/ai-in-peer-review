- Studies accuracy–privacy trade-offs under local differential privacy using minimax decision theory.  
- Derives sharp minimax rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation under α-local privacy.  
- Shows effective sample size for d-dimensional multinomial reduces to ~ n * α^2 / d.  
- Proves Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Shows density estimation rate exponent changes from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (n α^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}, a more severe penalty.  
- Provides computationally efficient algorithms matching the lower bounds, so the rates are tight.  
- Strength: Establishes a rigorous connection between differential privacy and classical statistical decision theory, giving quantitative privacy–utility trade-offs.  
- Strength: Strong technical quality—matching upper and lower bounds with explicit dependence on α and d.  
- Strength: Key insights include optimality of simple/non-interactive mechanisms and the novel exponent change for nonparametric problems.  
- Weakness: Presentation is dense; background/minimax material needs more intuitive exposition for a general audience.  
- Weakness: L1 (total variation) results are stated but under-discussed relative to the focus on L2 error.  
- Weakness: Possible substantial overlap with the authors’ FOCS 2013 paper; novelty relative to that work must be clarified.  
- Weakness: Important distinctions (population vs. sample estimation) and the non-interactive optimality result should be emphasized more in the introduction.  
- Recommendation: High-quality, influential work recommended for acceptance, subject to clarification of novel contributions beyond the FOCS version.
