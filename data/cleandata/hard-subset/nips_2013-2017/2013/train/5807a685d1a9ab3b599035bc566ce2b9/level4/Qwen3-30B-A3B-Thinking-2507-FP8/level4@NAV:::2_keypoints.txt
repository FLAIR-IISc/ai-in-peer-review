- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy, covering multinomial and density estimation, and establishes sharp minimax rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces the effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α, leaving it comparable to non-private when α is small.  
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private convergence rate is unattainable under local privacy.  
- Results are technically sound with correct proofs and possibly the first minimax bounds for density estimation under privacy.  
- Major concern: omission of relevant differential privacy (DP) noise-complexity literature that parallels the multinomial bound.  
- The multinomial result closely parallels Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012), which give worst-case DP lower bounds for linear queries under the same L2 error metric.  
- Because probability estimation is a special case of linear counting queries and local privacy is stronger than DP, DP lower bounds also apply; the authors should state whether their local privacy bound improves on existing DP bounds.  
- The density-estimation comparison needs clarification: eq. (13) applies only to densities with trigonometric bases, so the claimed gap vs. the non-private bound is valid only when comparing the same trigonometric-basis setting, not general Sobolev spaces.  
- Rebuttal note: authors partially addressed DP connections but omitted Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on MSE under DP; this and related references should be incorporated and discussed.  
- Overall: interesting and sharp contributions, but the related-work section must be expanded to acknowledge foundational DP lower bounds; omission of key references weakens the novelty claim for the multinomial result.
