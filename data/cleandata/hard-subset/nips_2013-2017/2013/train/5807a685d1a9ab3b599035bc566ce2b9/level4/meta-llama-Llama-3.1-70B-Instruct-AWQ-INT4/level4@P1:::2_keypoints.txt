- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation, proving sharp convergence rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter alpha.  
- For density estimation, local privacy prevents achieving the non-private optimal rate.  
- Proofs appear correct and the density estimation minimax bound under privacy seems novel.  
- Main critique: lack of discussion of related work on noise complexity/lower bounds for (central) differential privacy.  
- Specifically missing citations: Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012), which study worst-case lower bounds for linear queries with L2 error.  
- Pointed out that multinomial estimation is a special case of linear counting queries, so prior bounds for differential privacy are relevant.  
- Question whether the paper’s local-privacy bounds improve over previous (central DP) bounds, since local DP is stronger and central DP lower bounds also apply.  
- Confusion about density estimation statement: unclear if the non-private lower bound (eq.13) applies to general Sobolev spaces or only to the trigonometric-basis special case.  
- Suggestion to clarify comparisons and ensure non-private bounds are stated for the same trigonometric basis to be fair.  
- Additional missing reference: Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on mean square error in differential privacy.  
- Strengths listed: sharp minimax bounds for pmf/pdf under privacy, interesting results, well written.  
- Weaknesses listed: omission of known DP noise-complexity results and unclear relation to prior DP bounds.  
- Pro/con acceptance: Pro—strong results and writing; Con—significant omission regarding prior DP lower bounds and unclear relation.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10, Overall score 8/10.
