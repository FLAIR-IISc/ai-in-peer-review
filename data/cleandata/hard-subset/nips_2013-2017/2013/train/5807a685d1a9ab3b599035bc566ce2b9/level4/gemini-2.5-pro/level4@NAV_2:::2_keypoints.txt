- Presents sharp minimax analysis of probability estimation under local differential privacy for multinomial and nonparametric density settings.  
- Shows local privacy reduces effective sample size for d-dimensional multinomial estimation from n to about nα^2/d.  
- Proves a variant of Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Shows for β-smooth density estimation the convergence exponent degrades from 2β/(2β+1) to 2β/(2β+2) under local privacy.  
- Provides matching lower bounds and computationally efficient estimators that achieve the private minimax rates.  
- Strength: Addresses a fundamental problem, giving universal, algorithm-independent privacy–utility trade-offs.  
- Strength: Novel application of minimax decision theory; surprising finding that local privacy changes the polynomial exponent for density estimation.  
- Strength: Technically rigorous, combining information-theoretic tools with matching upper bounds achieved by practical algorithms.  
- Weakness: Needs to acknowledge and cite related lower-bound work on linear queries (e.g., Hardt & Talwar, De) and clarify connections.  
- Weakness: Should discuss differences between their average-case minimax risk and the worst-case error bounds studied in private query release literature.  
- Weakness: Must explicitly compare the derived local-privacy lower bound to existing central-privacy bounds to highlight novelty.  
- Weakness: For density estimation, explicitly state that the classical non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} applies to the trigonometric Sobolev class used, to make the comparison fully convincing.
