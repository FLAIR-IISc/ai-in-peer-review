- Paper studies local differential privacy with users producing α-DP signals and analyzes minimax expected error for estimators (sums/averages).
- Establishes lower bounds on minimax rates for l2 distance with discrete distribution over d types.
- Shows randomized response achieves the l2 lower bound and is therefore optimal.
- For density estimation, a perturbation scheme where each user perturbs only their own type meets the lower bound.
- Paper is well-written, significant, and of interest to the NIPS community; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Reviewer finds min-max explanation could be simplified and notation is cumbersome, especially since results all use the l2 norm.
- Suggests restating bounds directly in l2 norm and adding comments or comparisons for the more restrictive l1 norm and other DP lower bounds.
- Wishes the authors had considered broader settings beyond the local-privacy model and provided minimax lower bounds for those.
- Notes the important observation that allowing users to randomize based on the other n−1 users does not improve over per-user randomization; asks authors to state this explicitly.
- Mentions recent FOCS 2013 papers make this work feel like an extension of existing results (notably Section 3), weakening the endorsement somewhat.
