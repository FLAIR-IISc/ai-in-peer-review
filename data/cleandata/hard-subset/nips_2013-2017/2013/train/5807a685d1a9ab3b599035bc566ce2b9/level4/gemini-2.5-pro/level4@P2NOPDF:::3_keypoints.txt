- Investigates minimax utility limits in the local differential privacy model for probability estimation.
- Main result: sharp minimax lower bound on expected squared ℓ2 error for discrete distributions.
- Shows randomized response achieves that bound, proving its optimality for the discrete case.
- Extends analysis to nonparametric density estimation with a matching minimax lower bound met by a simple non-interactive perturbation.
- Strength: formally establishes optimality of a classic, simple mechanism (randomized response); technically solid and relevant to NIPS.
- Suggestion: improve clarity—add intuitive explanations and streamline dense notation, possibly by specializing to squared ℓ2 earlier.
- Suggestion: discuss other error metrics (particularly ℓ1) and challenges in extending the bounds to those metrics.
- Suggestion: connect derived local-privacy lower bounds to existing centralized-DP lower bounds to highlight the cost of local privacy.
- Suggestion: comment on whether the minimax analytical techniques generalize to other settings or privacy models.
- Key implication to emphasize: optimal protocol is simple and non-interactive—each user randomizes independently rather than coordinating.
- Concern: apparent significant overlap with a FOCS 2013 paper (noted after submission), reducing the work’s novelty.
- Conclusion: valuable, high-quality theoretical contribution and recommended for acceptance, but with reduced conviction due to novelty concerns.
