- Studies sharp minimax rates under local differential privacy for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation; density-analysis is novel.  
- Multinomial estimation: local privacy reduces effective sample size by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α; for fixed α the convergence order matches non-private.  
- Density estimation: local privacy prevents achieving the non-private optimal rate, showing a fundamental cost to privacy.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct.  
- Main weakness: insufficient engagement with prior lower-bound work in (central) differential privacy.  
- Multinomial problem closely relates to linear counting query lower bounds in Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010), De (TCC 2012), and Nikolov/Talwar/Zhang (STOC 2013) and should cite/discuss them.  
- Since local DP is stronger, central DP lower bounds apply; the paper must state whether its local-DP minimax bounds improve upon or are implied by existing central-DP bounds.  
- Authors should explicitly compare their minimax bounds to the central-DP literature and add the missing analysis/discussion.  
- Density-estimation comparison is unclear because the new lower bound is derived for densities with a trigonometric basis, but the scope of the non-private bound in eq. (13) is not clarified.  
- The authors must clarify whether the non-private bound applies to the full Sobolev class or only the trigonometric subclass to ensure an apples-to-apples comparison.  
- Recommendation: address the relation to central-DP lower bounds and clarify the function-class assumptions in the density section to strengthen the paper.
