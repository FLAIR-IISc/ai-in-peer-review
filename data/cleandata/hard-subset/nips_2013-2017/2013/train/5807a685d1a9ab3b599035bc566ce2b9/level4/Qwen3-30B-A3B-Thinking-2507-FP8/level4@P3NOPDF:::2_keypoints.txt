- Establishes minimax bounds under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation, showing sharp convergence rates and clarifying privacy's impact on efficiency.
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size by a factor Ω(α²), so with small constant α the practical efficiency loss is minimal.
- For density estimation, proves the optimal non-private rate is unattainable under local privacy, yielding a strictly slower minimax rate; claimed to be the first such result.
- Technical contributions are novel and proofs appear correct.
- Major issue 1: The multinomial minimax bound omits comparison to prior central-DP noise-complexity lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang 2013); paper must cite and clarify whether it improves on central-DP results.
- Major issue 2: The density-estimation comparison is ambiguous—Eq.(13)’s non-private lower bound is for a trigonometric basis while Definition 1 uses Sobolev spaces; authors must clarify/apply matching basis or derive the appropriate non-private bound.
- Recommendation: Accept pending revisions that integrate the missing literature context and resolve the basis mismatch in the density analysis.
