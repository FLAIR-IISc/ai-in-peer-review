This paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under the constraint that the estimation must preserve the privacy of individual data, using a privacy definition called local privacy. The authors consider two probability estimation problems: multinomial estimation and density estimation. In both problems, they establish sharp minimax rates of convergence. For the discrete multinomial estimation problem, they demonstrate that local privacy results in a reduction in the effective sample size that is quadratic in the privacy parameter alpha. Since alpha can often be viewed as a small constant, the effective sample size is of the same order as the non-private case. In contrast, for the density estimation problem, the authors show that the optimal rate for the non-private setting is no longer attainable when local privacy must be preserved.

Overall, the results are very interesting, and the proofs appear to be correct. To the best of my knowledge, the minimax bound for density estimation under a privacy constraint has not been considered before.

My main comment is that the minimax bound for multinomial estimation is closely related to previous works on the noise complexity for differential privacy, but this connection is not explicitly mentioned. In particular, two papers consider highly relevant problems: Hardt and Talwar, "On the geometry of differential privacy," STOC, 2010; and De, "Lower bounds in differential privacy," TCC, 2012. These two papers study worst-case lower bounds for the error of linear queries under the constraint of differential privacy. The probability estimation problem considered in this paper is actually a special case of the linear counting query studied in those two papers. Moreover, the measures used for the error are the same L2 metric. The only difference is that this paper considers local differential privacy, while Hardt and Talwar and De consider differential privacy. Since local privacy imposes stronger constraints than differential privacy, lower bounds for differential privacy are also lower bounds for local privacy. I would like to know whether the bound for local privacy given in this paper improves upon previous bounds for differential privacy.

For the density estimation problem, the statement of the result is somewhat confusing. The authors claim that the lower bound in the local privacy setting is higher than the non-private setting. However, the minimax bound in this paper is for the special case where the density can be expanded with a trigonometric basis. I am unclear whether the lower bound for the non-private setting (Equation 13) holds for the general Sobolev space as given in Definition 1 or for the special case of a trigonometric basis. A fair comparison can only be made if the non-private lower bound holds for the trigonometric basis.

In response to the rebuttal, while the feedback partially clarifies the relation to previous worst-case lower bounds, I would like to point out that the work of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang, "The Geometry of Differential Privacy: The Sparse and Approximate Cases" (STOC 2013), also considers Mean Square Error. I suggest that the authors add their explanations and missing references to the paper.

In summary, this paper proves sharp minimax bounds for pmf and pdf estimation with a privacy guarantee. The results are interesting, and the paper is well-written. However, there is a lack of mention about known results on the noise complexity lower bound for differential privacy, which are highly relevant to this paper.
