- Studies minimax bounds under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation, deriving sharp convergence rates.  
- Shows local privacy causes a quadratic reduction in effective sample size for multinomial estimation (in alpha), though alpha often small so practical impact may be limited.  
- Finds that under local privacy the optimal non-private rate for density estimation is unattainable.  
- Results are interesting and proofs appear correct; the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy seems novel.  
- Notes a close relationship between the multinomial minimax bound and prior worst-case noise-complexity lower bounds for differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012), both using L2 error.  
- Emphasizes the difference in privacy model (local DP here vs. global DP in prior work) and asks whether the new local-DP bounds improve on existing DP lower bounds.  
- Requests clarification on density-estimation comparisons: whether the non-private lower bound (eq.13) applies to the full Sobolev space or only the trigonometric basis; fair comparisons should use the trigonometric case.  
- Notes the rebuttal partially addressed prior-work connections but recommends citing Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013), which also treats mean-squared error.  
- Overall: well-structured and valuable results, but needs a more comprehensive discussion and integration of existing differential-privacy noise-complexity lower bounds.
