- Establishes sharp minimax bounds under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation, showing multinomial LDP effectively reduces sample size quadratically in α and density estimation suffers a strictly worse private rate than non-private.  
- Results are significant, technically sound, novel for LDP density estimation, and clearly written.  
- Major omission: fails to relate multinomial results to prior DP lower bounds for linear counting queries (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012); authors must clarify if LDP bounds improve on or merely match those DP bounds.  
- Density-estimation comparison needs clarification: Eq. (13) is derived for a trigonometric basis, so the non-private lower bound must be shown for the same basis (or the private bound should be compared to the corresponding non-private trigonometric-case bound).  
- Missing citation and discussion of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which studies mean square error under DP and is relevant to the paper’s noise-complexity results.  
- Recommendation: explicitly address connections to Hardt & Talwar, De, and Nikolov et al., and verify/apply the non-private lower bound within the trigonometric basis framework to solidify the paper’s claims.
