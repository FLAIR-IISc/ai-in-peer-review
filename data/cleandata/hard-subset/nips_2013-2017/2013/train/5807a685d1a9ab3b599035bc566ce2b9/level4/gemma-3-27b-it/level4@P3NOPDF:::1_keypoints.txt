- Presents Fano/LeCam-style lower bounds for estimation under local privacy and identifies randomized response as optimal in certain regimes.  
- Provides a rigorous, fundamental characterization of limits on learning from privacy-perturbed data.  
- The discussion is sometimes imprecise and may mislead readers unfamiliar with model nuances.  
- Connection to standard differential privacy is underdeveloped, limiting broader impact.  
- The restriction to the local privacy model is not emphasized enough, risking misinterpretation as standard differential privacy.  
- A specific claim (page 4) about differential privacy’s effect should be rephrased or qualified to reflect the local setting.  
- Questioned necessity of the α restriction in Theorem 1; practical relevance when α ≈ 1 is unclear.  
- Requests discussion of how different choices of the utility measure ρ affect the analysis and captured utilities.  
- Missing citation and comparison to Lei’s M-estimation procedure; paper should explain differences.  
- Lacks comparative analysis of other histogram estimation techniques versus randomized response.  
- Needs earlier and clearer distinction between population and sample statistics in the exposition.  
- Post-rebuttal: still requires clearer population/sample distinction and a more comprehensive related-work comparison.  
- Contribution clarified: novel density estimation bounds and clear exposition of the local privacy framework, though core local-privacy analysis overlaps with [10].  
- Overall: technically solid and interesting but requires presentation and contextualization improvements (especially regarding differential privacy and related work).
