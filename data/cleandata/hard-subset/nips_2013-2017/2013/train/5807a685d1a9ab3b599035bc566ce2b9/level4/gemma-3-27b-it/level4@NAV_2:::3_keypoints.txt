Paper studies discrete and continuous probability estimation under local privacy constraints.  
Establishes sharp minimax rates of convergence and identifies procedures that achieve optimal performance.  
Confirms Warner’s randomized response is an optimal strategy for privacy-preserving survey sampling.  
Well-motivated connection of Warner’s work to modern differential privacy and minimax decision theory.  
Provides rigorous characterization of the trade-off between privacy and statistical efficiency.  
Technical analysis appears sound with clear derivations of upper and lower bounds.  
Effectively combines established statistical decision theory techniques with recent privacy advances.  
Identifies that local privacy effectively reduces the usable sample size.  
Results cover both multinomial estimation and density estimation with tight achievability results.  
Strengths: mathematical rigor, clear presentation of core concepts, and high relevance to the field.  
Weaknesses: initial minimax presentation could be more accessible; notation is somewhat dense.  
Recommendation to streamline notation and possibly frame bounds consistently in a single norm (e.g., ℓ2).  
Suggests expanding discussion to other norms (e.g., ℓ1) and broader applicability to other privacy models.  
Notes should explicitly state that the optimal strategy uses per-user randomization independent of others’ reports.  
Mentions related FOCS 2013 work reduces novelty somewhat but does not invalidate the contribution; recommends acceptance.
