- Paper provides a comprehensive study of minimax convergence rates for probability estimation under α-local privacy.  
- Establishes sharp minimax bounds for multinomial (discrete) and density (continuous) estimation.  
- Shows multinomial effective sample size reduces from n to nα²/d under local privacy.  
- Demonstrates Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Derives a new density estimation rate (α²n)^(-2β/(2β+2)), differing from the classical n^(-2β/(2β+1)).  
- Main contribution: quantifies fundamental trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency.  
- Paper is well-structured, technically rigorous, and provides matching lower and upper bounds.  
- Algorithms achieving the minimax rates are efficient and practically implementable.  
- Historical connection to Warner’s work adds context and shows long-known optimal strategies for survey sampling.  
- Significance: provides a theoretical foundation for the cost of privacy with direct practical implications.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of relationship to prior differential privacy lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar 2010, De 2012).  
- Weakness: needs explicit clarification whether local-privacy bounds improve upon or match previous differential-privacy results.  
- Weakness: unclear whether the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) for the trigonometric basis applies to the general Sobolev class used.  
- Weakness: should more fully address connection to Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) regarding MSE under differential privacy.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen literature comparisons and clarify non-private vs. locally private rate comparisons.
