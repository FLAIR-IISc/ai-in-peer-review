1. Paper studies estimation under local differential privacy and allows interactive protocols.  
2. Establishes a minimax lower bound on l2 error for estimating a d-type discrete distribution.  
3. Shows the bound is tight and achieved by randomized response, proving its optimality.  
4. Extends analysis to nonparametric density estimation with a matching lower bound met by a simple non-interactive scheme.  
5. Contribution is strong and significant; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
6. Suggests clarifying presentation and simplifying notation, e.g., state results directly in l2.  
7. Recommends discussing l1 norms and comparing to non-local differential privacy bounds.  
8. Advises expanding discussion on generalizability to other privacy settings.  
9. Notes the important insight that although interaction is allowed, the optimal mechanism is non-interactive and this should be highlighted.  
10. Points out overlap with concurrent FOCS 2013 work (Section 3), reducing novelty and tempering endorsement.
