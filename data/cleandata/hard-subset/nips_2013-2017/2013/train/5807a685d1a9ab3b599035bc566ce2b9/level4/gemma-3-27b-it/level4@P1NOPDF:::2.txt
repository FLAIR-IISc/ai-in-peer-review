This paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under the constraint of preserving individual data privacy. The authors consider a privacy definition known as local privacy and investigate two probability estimation problems: multinomial estimation and density estimation. For both problems, they establish sharp minimax rates of convergence. Specifically, for the discrete multinomial estimation problem, they demonstrate that local privacy results in a reduction of the effective sample size that is quadratic in the privacy parameter α. Given that α is often a small constant, this reduction means the effective sample size remains comparable to the non-private case. For density estimation, the authors show that the optimal rate achievable in the non-private setting is unattainable when local privacy is enforced. 

Overall, the results are very interesting and, to the best of my understanding, the proofs are correct. To my knowledge, the minimax bound for density estimation under a privacy constraint is a novel contribution.

My main comment concerns the minimax bound for multinomial estimation, which is closely related to prior work on noise complexity for differential privacy. However, this connection is not discussed in the paper. In particular, the papers by Hardt & Talwar (“On the geometry of differential privacy,” STOC, 2010) and De (“Lower bounds in differential privacy,” TCC, 2012) address highly relevant problems. These works study worst-case lower bounds for the error of linear queries under differential privacy. The probability estimation problem considered here is, in fact, a special case of the linear counting query studied in those papers, and the error metric used (the L₂ metric) is the same. The primary difference is that this paper considers local differential privacy, while Hardt & Talwar and De consider global differential privacy. Since local privacy imposes a stronger constraint than global differential privacy, lower bounds for global differential privacy also apply to local privacy. I am curious whether the bound presented in this paper for local privacy improves upon existing bounds for global differential privacy.

Regarding the density estimation problem, the statement of the result is somewhat unclear. The authors state that the lower bound in the local privacy setting is higher than in the non-private setting. However, the minimax bound in this paper is established for the specific case where the density can be expanded using a trigonometric basis. I question whether the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) holds generally for the Sobolev space specified in Definition 1, or only for the special case of the trigonometric basis. A fair comparison requires the non-private lower bound to hold for the trigonometric basis as well.

Additional comments for the rebuttal:

The feedback partially clarifies the relationship to previous worst-case lower bounds. However, the work of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (“The Geometry of Differential Privacy: The Sparse and Approximate Cases,” STOC 2013) also considers Mean Square Error. I recommend that the authors include their explanations and the missing references in the paper.

In summary, this paper proves sharp minimax bounds for probability mass function (pmf) and probability density function (pdf) estimation with privacy guarantees. The results are interesting, and the paper is well-written. However, it would benefit from acknowledging and discussing known results on the noise complexity lower bounds for differential privacy, which are highly relevant to the presented work.
