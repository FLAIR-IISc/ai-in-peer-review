- Paper studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local differential privacy.  
- Considers two problems: multinomial (pmf) estimation and density (pdf) estimation.  
- Establishes sharp minimax rates for both problems.  
- Shows local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α for multinomial estimation.  
- Notes that because α is often a small constant, the effective sample size remains same order as non-private in practice for multinomial case.  
- Demonstrates that the optimal non-private rate is unattainable under local privacy for the density estimation problem.  
- Results are interesting, proofs appear correct, and the density-estimation minimax bound is novel.  
- Major critique: insufficient acknowledgment of related noise-complexity and worst-case lower-bound work in differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).  
- Multinomial estimation is a special case of linear counting queries studied in those prior works, using the same L2 error metric.  
- Authors should compare their local-privacy bounds to existing differential-privacy bounds to assess improvements.  
- Density-estimation result is unclear: the lower bound is proved for a trigonometric basis—uncertain whether the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) holds for the full Sobolev space or only that basis.  
- Rebuttal partially clarifies literature relation, but authors should also cite Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) regarding MSE and include these explanations/references.  
- Overall: paper is well-written and makes significant contributions but must address relevant prior work and clarify the density-estimation comparisons.
