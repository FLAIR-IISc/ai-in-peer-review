- Studies minimax bounds under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation, providing sharp rates of convergence.  
- Shows for multinomial estimation local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α; for small α this is same order as non-private.  
- Shows for density estimation the non-private optimal rate is no longer attainable under local privacy.  
- Proofs appear correct and the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy is novel.  
- Main critique: insufficient discussion of connections to prior noise-complexity / worst-case lower bounds for differential privacy (e.g., Hardt & Talwar, De).  
- Notes multinomial estimation is a special case of linear counting queries; since local DP is stronger than global DP, global-DP lower bounds are relevant and should be compared.  
- Asks whether the local-privacy bound here improves on existing global-DP bounds.  
- Density-estimation presentation unclear: the lower bound is shown for a trigonometric basis—need clarification whether the non-private lower bound (eq.13) holds for the general Sobolev space or only that basis.  
- Recommends ensuring the non-private lower bound is established for the trigonometric basis for a fair comparison.  
- Rebuttal suggestion: acknowledge and cite additional relevant work on MSE/noise complexity (Nikolov, Talwar, & Zhang 2013) and expand discussion of related literature.  
- Overall verdict: interesting, well-written paper but missing some key references and clarifications on comparisons to known DP lower bounds.
