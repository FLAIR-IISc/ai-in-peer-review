- Paper studies minimax bounds under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation, deriving sharp minimax rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces the effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter ε.  
- For density estimation, the optimal non-private convergence rate is unattainable under local differential privacy.  
- Results are theoretically significant, proofs appear sound, and this is likely the first work deriving minimax bounds for density estimation under local privacy.  
- Primary concern: insufficient engagement with prior work on noise complexity for differential privacy, which directly relates to the multinomial results.  
- The multinomial problem is a special case of linear counting queries with L2 error like Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012); the authors must clarify the relation.  
- Need explicit clarification whether the local privacy lower bound improves on known central DP lower bounds or simply inherits them, since central DP bounds apply a fortiori.  
- Density estimation comparison needs basis clarity: Theorem 3 assumes a trigonometric basis, but the non-private lower bound (eq.13) is stated for the general Sobolev space—confirm eq.13 holds for the trigonometric case.  
- Rebuttal omitted Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013); that reference and its relation to the paper’s local-privacy bounds should be discussed.  
- Summary: technically compelling and well-written, but novelty claim weakened by lack of literature context; clarifying connections to Hardt & Talwar, De, Nikolov et al., and the basis assumption will strengthen the paper.
