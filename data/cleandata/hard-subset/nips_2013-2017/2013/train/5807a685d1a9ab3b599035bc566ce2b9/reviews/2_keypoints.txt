- Studies minimax bounds under local privacy for multinomial and density estimation, proving sharp minimax rates.  
- For multinomial estimation, local privacy reduces effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α; for small α the effective sample size is same order as non-private.  
- For density estimation, local privacy prevents attaining the non-private optimal rate.  
- Proofs appear correct and the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy is novel.  
- Main critique: lacks citation/discussion of related noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).  
- Those prior works give worst-case lower bounds for linear queries with L2 error; multinomial estimation is a special case, so authors should clarify whether their local-DP bounds improve on differential-DP bounds.  
- The density-estimation lower bound is derived for trigonometric-basis expansions; unclear if the non-private lower bound (eq.13) holds for general Sobolev spaces or only that basis—comparison must be on the same function class.  
- Rebuttal partially clarified prior-work relations, but the authors should also cite Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013), which addresses mean-squared error.  
- Overall: interesting, well-written paper with important results, but missing discussion of known noise-complexity/differential-privacy lower bounds.
