- Paper studies local differential privacy with minimax expected-error rates for estimating probabilities and densities.  
- Provides lower bounds on minimax rates for l2 distance with discrete distributions over d types.  
- Shows randomized response (each user perturbs only their own type) meets the lower bound, proving optimality.  
- Extends results to density estimation with a simple per-user perturbation scheme achieving the lower bound.  
- Positive evaluation: paper is important, shows classic procedures are optimal, and is of interest to the community; recommends acceptance.  
- Criticism: exposition of minimax bounds is overly complex and could be simplified.  
- Criticism: notation is cumbersome; bounds could be stated directly in l2 norm since all results use it.  
- Suggestion: include discussion or comparison to l1 norm results and other differential-privacy lower bounds (mostly non-local settings).  
- Suggestion: consider broader settings beyond the tailored local-privacy model and provide minimax lower bounds for them.  
- Request: explicitly state that per-user independent randomization is optimal despite availability of schemes that condition on other users.  
- New reservation: results overlap or extend a recently published FOCS 2013 paper (Section 3), weakening novelty; still recommends acceptance but less strongly.
