- Paper (NIPS-formatted ArXiv) uses Fano/LeCam-style arguments to derive lower bounds for estimation when the algorithm is untrusted by the data holder; randomized response is a corollary and is optimal in some sense.  
- Reviewer did not examine the supplementary material.  
- Strength: characterizes limitations of learning from data perturbed to guarantee privacy.  
- Weakness: imprecise commentary in places that could confuse casual readers.  
- Missing connection to existing differential privacy results and literature.  
- The restriction to local privacy makes the model different from differential privacy and the paper sometimes overstates or blurs that distinction (example: statement at bottom of p.4).  
- Question whether the restriction on alpha in Theorem 1 is necessary; unclear relevance given experimental regimes where alpha ≈ 1 under differential privacy.  
- Request for commentary on choices of rho (utility measures) to show how different utilities fit the analysis.  
- Missing citation/comparison to Lei’s M-estimation density estimator; Lei’s work should be discussed.  
- Need comparison to other histogram/discrete-data estimation methods to show how they perform relative to randomized response.  
- After rebuttal: population vs. sample statistics must be explained more clearly and earlier in the paper.  
- After rebuttal: a comparison to related work (Lei and others cited by reviewers) is important for context.  
- Final assessment: contribution is twofold—clear exposition of the local privacy model (largely contained in [10]) and new bounds for density estimation.
