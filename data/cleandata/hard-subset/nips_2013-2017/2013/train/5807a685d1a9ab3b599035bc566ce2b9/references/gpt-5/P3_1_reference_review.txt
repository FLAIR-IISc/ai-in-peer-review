Summary and contributions
- Problem and model: The paper studies statistical estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), where each user privatizes their datum via a memoryless channel Q satisfying α-LDP before any aggregation. The goal is to characterize minimax risks for population estimation tasks under this privacy constraint.
- Methodology: The lower bounds reduce estimation to private hypothesis testing, leveraging Le Cam/Fano, together with information-contraction inequalities under LDP. In particular, Proposition 1 bounds KL and mutual information between privatized marginals as a function of total variation between the underlying distributions, with explicit α-dependence. Upper bounds are attained by concrete, efficiently implementable non-interactive mechanisms.
- Main results:
  1) Multinomial estimation: For θ ∈ Δd, the α-private minimax risks satisfy E||θ̂−θ||_2^2 ≍ min{1, d/(nα^2)} and E||θ̂−θ||_1 ≍ min{1, d/√(nα^2)}, up to constants for α ≤ 1. Thus the effective sample size degrades from n to nα^2/d. A variant of Warner’s randomized response achieves the upper bounds, showing its optimality.
  2) Density estimation over Sobolev classes F_β: The private minimax L2 risk scales as (nα^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)), changing the classical exponent from 2β/(2β+1). For β=1 (Lipschitz), a privatized histogram with projection is optimal; for general β≥1, a privatized orthogonal series estimator with a carefully designed LDP mechanism achieves the lower bound. The paper also argues that naive Laplace perturbation of raw samples (deconvolution setting) is suboptimal due to characteristic function decay.

Relation to prior work
- Connects the classical randomized response (Warner) with modern differential privacy. Builds on and restates core information bounds from Duchi–Jordan–Wainwright (arXiv:1302.3203), and relates to foundational DP work (Dwork et al.), the geometry of DP (Hardt–Talwar), and statistical DP frameworks (Wasserman–Zhou).
- In the LDP model, there are prior learnability/sample-complexity results (Kasiviswanathan et al.), but this paper provides sharp minimax rates for concrete estimation problems and identifies optimal mechanisms. The deconvolution limitation for Laplace noise is tied to Carroll–Hall.
- Compared to earlier central-DP rate results, the paper provides population minimax lower bounds specific to LDP, which are strictly stronger than sample-based accuracy guarantees common in DP releases.

Strengths
- Technical depth and sharpness: The lower bounds are clean, with transparent dependence on α, n, and d/β; the matching upper bounds are constructive, non-interactive, and computationally efficient (simple projection or series truncation).
- Conceptual contribution: Demonstrates that LDP can fundamentally alter nonparametric rates (exponent change), not just constants or effective n, and pinpoints when classical mechanisms (randomized response) are optimal.
- Clarity of the high-level story: The paper convincingly ties privacy guarantees to statistical efficiency through minimax theory and information contraction.

Weaknesses and questions
- Sampling mechanism for orthogonal series (Sec. 4.3): The “halfspace-uniform on {−B,B}^k” sampler (19) is asserted to be α-LDP and efficiently computable (e.g., by rejection sampling). However:
  - Uniform sampling over {z ∈ {−B,B}^k : ⟨z,τ̃⟩>0} is nontrivial. Rejection from the full cube yields acceptance ~1/2 but not necessarily uniformity if you flip a sign post hoc. Please provide an explicit algorithm that is provably uniform and runs in O(k) time, or bound the expected runtime of exact rejection sampling.
  - The constant c_k in Eq. (20) is said to be O(1) and bounded away from 0. Please specify its exact dependence on k (or prove uniform boundedness) because the choice B ∝ √k/(α c_k) and the variance of Z scale with k; the final rates rely on controlling these constants.
- Histogram mechanism (Sec. 4.2): The guarantee that adding i.i.d. Laplace(α/2) to one-hot vectors and then projecting onto kΔ_k yields α-LDP is plausible via L1 sensitivity, but a short proof sketch would help. The bound (17) includes an extra term √α n^(−3/4) that becomes negligible asymptotically; can it be removed by a refined variance-control argument, or at least be explained more transparently?
- Multinomial randomized response (Sec. 3.2): The per-coordinate randomized mechanism (10) is elegant. Since x ∈ {e_j} but the privacy argument is shown for x,x′ ∈ Δ_d, please clarify the domain over which α-LDP is guaranteed and confirm the triangle inequality step that yields ||x−x′||_1 ≤ 2 is the worst case relevant for one-hot inputs. For the ℓ1-risk upper bound (9), a short self-contained calculation (beyond “similar argument”) would improve completeness.
- Assumptions and scope:
  - The analysis focuses on α ∈ [0,1]. Briefly discuss behavior for larger α and the transition to classical rates.
  - Only non-interactive channels are considered; a sentence on whether interactivity can improve rates in these problems would be valuable (my expectation: no asymptotic improvement for these estimation tasks, but worth clarifying).
  - Beyond L2 risk for densities, is there anything known for L1 or sup-norm risks under LDP?
- Empirical illustration: Although the paper is primarily theoretical, a small synthetic experiment (e.g., multinomial MSE vs n,α,d; Lipschitz density L2 error vs n,α) would make the implications more concrete.

Quality
- The results are technically sound, with correct use of minimax testing reductions and information bounds. Some algorithmic details (uniform sampling in 4.3, constants) should be tightened to fully justify the achievability statements.

Clarity
- Generally well written and organized. Adding brief proof sketches for the privacy guarantees and the ℓ1 upper bound, and clarifying the sampler and constants in Sec. 4.3, would aid reproducibility.

Originality
- High in the LDP setting: sharp minimax rates and identification of optimal mechanisms for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation appear novel and impactful relative to prior DP and LDP literature.

Significance
- Strong. The work sets benchmarks for private estimation under LDP, including the notable change in nonparametric exponents, and should stimulate further research on optimal mechanisms and rates in private statistics.

Arguments for acceptance
- Sharp, conceptually important minimax results under LDP with matching mechanisms.
- Clear implications for practice (randomized response optimality; pitfalls of naive Laplace on raw data for densities).
- Likely to be influential in both theory and applications of privacy-preserving statistics.

Arguments against acceptance
- Some implementational details of the orthogonal-series mechanism are underspecified.
- No empirical demonstrations; a few proofs and constants are deferred/implicit.

Overall recommendation
- Accept after minor revisions to clarify the orthogonal-series sampling algorithm and constants, and to add brief proof sketches for key privacy and risk claims.
