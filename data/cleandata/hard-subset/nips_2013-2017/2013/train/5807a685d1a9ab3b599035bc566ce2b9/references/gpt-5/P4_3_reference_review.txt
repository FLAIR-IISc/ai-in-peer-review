Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies statistical estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), focusing on minimax rates for (i) multinomial probability estimation and (ii) nonparametric density estimation over Sobolev classes. The authors adopt a non-interactive local privacy model: each user maps Xi to Zi via a fixed channel Q that satisfies α-local DP (Evfimievski et al. [13]). They integrate this privacy model with standard minimax testing reductions (Le Cam/Fano [26, 25, 22]) and recent information-theoretic bounds that control divergences/mutual information under LDP (from their prior work [9]). This enables lower bounds on private minimax risk and matching upper bounds via efficient mechanisms.

For d-dimensional multinomial estimation, the paper shows squared ℓ2 risk scales as Θ(min{1, d/(nα2)}), and ℓ1 risk as Θ(min{1, d/√(nα2)}), quantifying an effective sample size reduction from n to nα2/d for α in a small-constant regime. A variant of Warner’s randomized response [23] is shown to achieve the optimal rates, implying that this classical scheme is sample-complexity optimal under LDP.

For density estimation over Sobolev classes Fβ on [0,1], the nonprivate minimax L2 risk n−2β/(2β+1) deteriorates under LDP to (nα2)−2β/(2β+2), changing the polynomial exponent. The paper provides two achievability results: (a) a privatized histogram estimator that is minimax-optimal for Lipschitz densities (β=1) with k≈(nα2)1/4 bins, and (b) a locally private orthogonal-series estimator that attains the optimal rate for all β≥1. This latter mechanism uses a carefully designed unbiased privatization of bounded basis coefficients that scales noise as B≈B0√k/α and samples Z in {−B,B}k conditional on randomized signs, differing from simple additive Laplace noise; the paper notes (via deconvolution limits [4]) that direct additive noise to X cannot achieve the optimal rate.

This work connects to a growing literature on DP in statistics and learning, including Dwork et al. [11], Hardt–Talwar [16], Kasiviswanathan et al. [17], Smith [20], and Wasserman–Zhou [24]. The primary novelty is the sharp private minimax characterization under local DP with matching algorithms, and the identification of a rate-exponent change for nonparametric density estimation. The separation from sample-estimation-focused DP lower bounds (e.g., [2, 8, 16]) is emphasized: the bounds here target population estimation.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The lower bounds follow a standard and appropriate path—packing, Le Cam/Fano, plus LDP-specific divergence/mutual information control (Proposition 1 from [9]). The upper bounds are constructive and computationally efficient: a projection-based estimator with a randomized response channel for multinomials, a projected privatized histogram, and an orthogonal series estimator with an LDP mechanism ensuring unbiasedness. The scaling laws are internally consistent; the impossibility for Laplace-perturbed samples is supported by deconvolution theory [4].
- Completeness: Key information bounds are cited from [9], and several proofs are deferred there. For NeurIPS, it would be preferable to include full proofs (or a complete supplement) so the submission is self-contained. Some constants (e.g., ck in Section 4.3) are not fully specified; providing explicit formulas or bounds would aid reproducibility.
- Evaluation of strengths/limitations: The paper appropriately contrasts local vs non-private rates and discusses why naive noise-addition to the raw data is suboptimal.

Clarity
- The paper is well-organized: setting/definitions, minimax framework, information bounds, then applications. Notation is standard. The privacy definition and minimax criterion are clear.
- Suggestions:
  - Make explicit which results are new beyond [9] and which rely entirely on [9].
  - Provide pseudocode for the orthogonal-series privatization (Section 4.3) and clarify the role and computability of ck and the rejection sampling procedure.
  - State assumptions consistently (e.g., bounds on α, boundedness B0 of the basis, and in which regimes the “effective sample size” interpretation applies).
  - For multinomial ℓ2 risk, the lower-bound expression includes an intermediate term; clarifying the exact regimes where each term dominates would improve readability.

Originality
- High. The work delivers sharp, matching minimax characterizations for local privacy in both finite-dimensional multinomials and infinite-dimensional Sobolev classes, and it identifies a change in the convergence exponent in the nonparametric case. The orthogonal-series LDP mechanism appears novel and essential for optimality, beyond more standard Laplace/exponential mechanisms.

Significance
- High. The results provide clear guidance on the statistical price of local privacy: a precise degradation in effective sample size for multinomials and a fundamental rate-exponent shift for density estimation. The identification of optimal mechanisms (including the optimality of randomized response) is practically relevant for survey design and distributed data collection. The methodology (information bounds under LDP + minimax testing) is likely to be reusable for other inference problems.

Pros
- Sharp private minimax rates with matching algorithms in both parametric and nonparametric settings.
- Clear demonstration that naive additive noise to X is suboptimal for density estimation.
- Simple, explainable, and optimal mechanism (randomized response) for multinomials.
- Solid theoretical integration of differential privacy and decision-theoretic minimax analysis.

Cons
- Reliance on external results from [9] for core information bounds and some proofs; the submission would benefit from a self-contained appendix.
- The orthogonal-series privatization includes unspecified constants and sampling details that may hinder straightforward replication; clearer algorithmic specification would help.
- Restriction to non-interactive local channels; a brief discussion of whether interaction could improve rates would contextualize the limits.

Suggestions for improvement
- Include full proofs (or a detailed supplement) for the main theorems, especially the information bounds and the orthogonal-series mechanism’s variance control.
- Provide explicit constants or constructive bounds for ck and B in Section 4.3, and a precise, efficient sampling algorithm.
- Add a short comparison to interactive local DP and to central DP to situate the rates.
- Clarify the precise novelty relative to [9], and add pointers for reproducibility (e.g., code or detailed pseudo-code).
