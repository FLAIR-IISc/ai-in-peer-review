- Derives sharp minimax rates under α-local differential privacy for multinomial probability estimation and β-smooth density estimation.  
- Shows LDP reduces effective sample size: for multinomial estimation n → nα²/d, with Warner's randomized response achieving optimality.  
- Proves density estimation rate degrades from n^(-2β/(2β+1)) to (nα²)^(-2β/(2β+2)), and provides histogram and orthogonal series estimators that match these rates.  
- Theoretical rigor and novelty: provides both tight lower and upper minimax bounds, improving on prior work that gave mostly upper bounds.  
- Historical connection: connects Warner's 1965 randomized response to modern LDP, showing its optimality for multinomial estimation.  
- Practical relevance: supplies efficiently computable, projection-based estimators achieving the optimal rates.  
- Clear exposition: well-written, organized development of information bounds and minimax risk analysis.  
- Limited scope: focuses only on multinomial and density estimation, not on regression, classification, or high-dimensional settings.  
- Lack of empirical validation: no experiments on synthetic or real data to confirm theoretical rates.  
- Limited comparison with alternative mechanisms: does not thoroughly investigate other practical privacy mechanisms for density estimation.  
- Strong privacy assumption: exclusively analyzes α-local privacy without exploring relaxed or central-model DP variants.  
- Practical implementation concerns: does not address large-scale implementation issues such as communication constraints in distributed LDP.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant theoretical contribution with sharp rates and historical insight; recommendation: accept.
