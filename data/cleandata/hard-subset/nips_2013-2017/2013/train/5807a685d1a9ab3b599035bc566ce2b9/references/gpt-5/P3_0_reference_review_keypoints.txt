- Develops a decision-theoretic framework for local differential privacy (LDP) and derives sharp minimax rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation using information-theoretic lower bounds and new divergence/mutual-information contractions.  
- Multinomial result: minimax MSE scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)} and ℓ1 risk as min{1, d/√(nα^2)}, yielding an “effective sample size” n → nα^2/d for small α; (modified) randomized response and perturb-then-project estimators achieve these rates.  
- Density estimation result: for Sobolev class Fβ, nonprivate rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades under LDP to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}; matching upper bounds via privatized histogram (β=1) and an orthogonal-series mechanism for general β.  
- Key mechanism insight: privatizing coefficients (orthogonal series) rather than adding noise to raw samples avoids deconvolution penalties and attains optimal rates.  
- Places work in context of randomized response, LDP, differential-privacy decision theory, strong data-processing-like contractions, and deconvolution literature, unifying these strands.  
- Strength: technically deep with sharp matching upper and lower bounds and explicit dependence on α, n, d, β.  
- Strength: conceptually clear focus on population minimax risk under LDP versus prior sample-statistic accuracy notions.  
- Strength: mechanism-design contributions showing randomized response is rate-optimal for multinomials and providing simple, computationally efficient private estimators.  
- Strength: develops general mutual-information and KL contraction tools under LDP that are broadly useful.  
- Weakness: likely typo in randomized response Eq.(10) (bit-flip vs negation) — needs correction and recheck of privacy-ratio derivation.  
- Weakness: several key technical results and constants are deferred to a companion paper [9]; more self-contained proofs or sketches and explicit constants would aid reproducibility.  
- Weakness: orthogonal-series mechanism lacks practical implementation details (acceptance rates for rejection sampling, runtime, explicit ck, guidance for choosing k and B, numerical stability/clipping).  
- Weakness: procedures assume known smoothness β; adaptivity to unknown β under LDP is not addressed and should be discussed.  
- Weakness: scope restricted to non-interactive LDP; discussion needed on whether interaction could improve rates or why lower bounds extend.  
- Weakness: no empirical validation; a small simulation study comparing naive Laplace perturbation vs proposed mechanisms and illustrating α^2 dependence is recommended.  
- Quality: overall technically strong and appears sound, with well-crafted information-contraction-based lower bounds and constructive upper bounds.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but some notation and constant definitions need tightening; include proof outlines in main/supplement or appendix.  
- Originality: high — precise minimax characterization under local privacy, altered density-estimation exponent, and novel orthogonal-series privatization mechanisms.  
- Significance: high — results will influence understanding of LDP’s statistical cost, guide private mechanism design, and seed future private nonparametric work.  
- Suggestions: fix Eq.(10), add proof sketches and explicit constants/computational guarantees, discuss adaptivity and interactive LDP, and include brief experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
