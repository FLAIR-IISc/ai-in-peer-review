Summary
The paper studies statistical estimation under local (non-interactive) differential privacy, combining minimax decision theory with local DP constraints. Two main problems are treated:

- Multinomial estimation: For a d-dimensional multinomial, the paper proves sharp minimax rates under α-local DP. The mean-squared error scales like min{1, d/(n α^2)}; the ℓ1 risk scales like min{1, d/√(n α^2)}. The authors show (a slightly modified) randomized response achieves these rates, implying an effective sample size reduction roughly n → n α^2 / d for small α.

- Density estimation: For Sobolev smoothness β, the classical non-private L2 risk scales as n^{-2β/(2β+1)}. Under local DP, the authors show the rate degrades to (n α^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}. They give matching upper bounds via (i) a privatized histogram for β=1 and (ii) a locally private orthogonal series estimator for general β, using a nontrivial privatization mechanism that is not simple additive noise on raw samples.

The lower bounds leverage information-theoretic inequalities (Le Cam/Fano) with new DP-aware contraction bounds on divergences/mutual information. The upper bounds are constructive and computationally efficient.

Relation to prior work
The work sits squarely within the literature on differential privacy and statistical inference. It builds on local DP (Evfimievski et al.), minimax lower bound techniques (Yang–Barron, Tsybakov), and recent DP lower bounds/geometry (Hardt–Talwar, Smith) and learnability under local DP (Kasiviswanathan et al.). The information bounds and some proofs are deferred to the authors’ longer paper [9]. The multinomial setting connects back to Warner’s randomized response, which is shown to be rate-optimal. For density estimation, the paper contrasts with deconvolution limits (Carroll–Hall), explaining why naive additive-noise mechanisms are suboptimal under local DP.

Strengths
- Clear and significant theoretical contribution: sharp minimax lower bounds with matching upper bounds for two canonical problems under local DP.
- Methodologically sound: Uses established minimax techniques and DP definitions; arguments are careful and plausible, and the constructions for achievability are efficient.
- Insightful findings: The change in exponent for nonparametric density estimation under local DP is important and non-obvious; identifying optimal mechanisms beyond simple additive noise is valuable.
- Good contextualization: Links classical randomized response to modern DP with optimality guarantees.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Completeness: Many technical details and proofs are deferred to [9]. While acceptable to some extent, the NeurIPS paper would benefit from at least detailed proof sketches for the main theorems, and self-contained statements of key lemmas with assumptions.
- Clarity/minor issues:
  - The ℓ2 lower bound statement in (8) includes a term sqrt(n α^2) in the min, which appears dimensionally odd and likely unnecessary; please clarify or correct.
  - Constants (e.g., cℓ, cu < 5) and the role of ck in the orthogonal-series mechanism are underspecified; readers would benefit from explicit bounds and their dependence on α, d, β.
  - Some notation and assumptions (e.g., bounds B0, choice of B, and the exact privacy proof for the series mechanism) are sketched; a concise but rigorous justification would improve readability.
  - References are incomplete (missing years/pages in several entries). Please fix.
- Scope limitations: The analysis is restricted to non-interactive (memoryless) channels. Some problems admit gains with interaction; a discussion of whether interaction could change the rates here would strengthen the contribution.
- Practicality/Adaptivity: No discussion of adaptation to unknown smoothness β under privacy constraints (e.g., private model selection or cross-validation costs). Also, there is no empirical validation or simulations to illustrate constants and finite-sample behavior.
- Assumption regime: Results emphasize α ∈ [0,1]. It would be useful to discuss behavior for moderate/large α and the transition to the non-private rates.

Questions for the authors
1. Can you provide a proof sketch (in the main text) for Theorem 2 that highlights the construction of the packing and how the DP-aware information bounds are applied?
2. Is the sqrt(n α^2) term in (8) a typo, or does it arise from a technical artifact? If the latter, please explain.
3. For the orthogonal-series mechanism, can you give explicit bounds on ck and a concise proof of α-local privacy for the sampling strategy (19)?
4. Do interactive local mechanisms improve rates for either multinomial estimation or Sobolev density estimation? If known, a brief discussion or a negative/positive result would be valuable.
5. Can your estimators be made adaptive to unknown β under a fixed privacy budget, and what rate penalty would arise?

Minor comments
- Improve exposition in Section 3.2: briefly justify the privacy of (10) and the projection step’s effect on risk.
- In Section 4.2, clarify the dominance of the first term in (17) and the precise regime of α, n where it holds.
- Clean up and complete the bibliography.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong, rigorous theory with matching lower/upper bounds.
- Important and timely topic; clarifies fundamental trade-offs under local DP.
- Uses established techniques thoughtfully; results are likely to be influential.

Cons for acceptance
- Heavy reliance on an external long version for proofs.
- Some clarity and presentation issues; a few statements need correction/precision.
- No experiments; limited discussion of adaptivity or interaction.

Recommendation: Weak Accept
