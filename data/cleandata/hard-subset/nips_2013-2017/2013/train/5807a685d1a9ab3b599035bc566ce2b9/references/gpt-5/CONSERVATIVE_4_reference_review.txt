Summary
The paper studies statistical estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), combining minimax decision theory with privacy constraints. The setting is non-interactive/memoryless channels Qi producing privatized views Zi of i.i.d. data Xi with α-LDP. The authors leverage information-contraction bounds (via KL and mutual information) under LDP, coupled with Le Cam/Fano methods, to derive lower bounds on private minimax risks and provide matching algorithms.

Two main problems are treated:
- Multinomial estimation: For θ ∈ Δd, the minimax ℓ2 and ℓ1 risks under α-LDP scale as d/(nα^2) and d/√(nα^2), respectively (up to constants and truncation at 1), i.e., effective sample size degrades n → nα^2/d. A variant of randomized response attains the bounds, as does simple coordinate-wise Laplace perturbation (for ℓ2).
- Density estimation over Sobolev classes Fβ on [0,1]: The classical non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades under LDP to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}. For β=1, a privatized histogram with k≈(nα^2)^{1/4} bins is minimax; for β≥1, an orthogonal-series estimator using a tailored locally private mechanism for basis coefficients achieves the lower bound. The paper also argues that directly adding Laplace noise to samples leads to suboptimal deconvolution rates.

Relation to prior work: The paper builds on differential privacy foundations and nonparametric minimax theory, and specifically reuses information bounds from the authors’ prior work [9], which are restated here. The contribution relative to [9] is the derivation of sharp rates for multinomial and density estimation under LDP with matching mechanisms.

Strengths
- Addresses an important and timely question: precise statistical cost of LDP in classical estimation problems.
- Technically principled: uses standard minimax testing reductions with careful information-contraction bounds under LDP.
- Sharpness: Provides matching upper and lower bounds (up to constants) for two canonical problems, with simple mechanisms (randomized response; privatized histogram; privatized orthogonal series).
- Conceptual clarity: clearly contrasts local vs. non-private rates; explains why naive Laplace perturbation of samples is suboptimal for density estimation (deconvolution argument).

Weaknesses and concerns
- Novelty relative to [9]: The central information bounds and much of the framework are taken from the authors’ arXiv/[9]. The submission explicitly defers all proofs to [9], and it is not fully clear which theorems are new beyond that manuscript. NeurIPS expects a self-contained contribution or a clear delineation of what is new. As written, the novelty seems incremental relative to [9].
- Missing proofs and limited detail: Key theorems (minimax lower bounds and achievability) are stated without proof, with only brief sketches for mechanisms. For a theory paper, at least proof sketches and a complete supplementary document are expected so the community can verify the arguments.
- Clarity/technical inconsistencies:
  - Eq. (8): The lower bound includes a term sqrt(nα^2), which increases with n and cannot be a risk; likely a typo (perhaps √(d/(nα^2)) or similar). This needs correction.
  - Randomized response mechanism in (10) is confusing: Z is claimed in {0,1}^d, yet the rule uses “−xj,” which would produce negative entries if xj=1. The subsequent expectation/variance calculations are hard to follow given this inconsistency.
  - Proposition 2 bound includes a term √(α n^{-3/4}), which has unusual scaling and units; at least justify or correct it.
  - Several notational slips (e.g., σ(Z), σ(X), dependence of Qi on i) and minor typos make some parts hard to reproduce.
- Scope limitations not discussed: Results are for non-interactive LDP; no discussion whether interaction could improve rates. Density estimation is 1D; implications for higher dimensions (where non-private rates already degrade) are not addressed.
- Empirical illustration absent: While not strictly required, a small synthetic study could illustrate constants, the magnitude of trade-offs, and the behavior of the proposed privatization schemes.

Quality
The overall approach is theoretically sound and uses established tools appropriately. However, the paper relies heavily on [9] for proofs, and there are several presentation issues and likely typos in key statements. The mechanisms for orthogonal-series privatization are interesting but would benefit from a more explicit algorithmic description and computational analysis.

Clarity
Generally well organized, with a clear problem setup and connection to classical minimax theory. That said, the noted inconsistencies/typos and the lack of proofs reduce readability and reproducibility. The paper would also benefit from a clearer statement of assumptions (e.g., bounded basis functions, range of α, constants).

Originality
Moderate. The general information-theoretic framework appears largely from [9]; this paper’s main added value is specializing to multinomial and Sobolev density estimation with matching constructions. Novel mechanisms for orthogonal-series LDP are plausible contributions but need clearer differentiation from prior LDP estimators and from [24].

Significance
High potential significance: pinning down the statistical price of LDP for classical tasks is impactful, and the “effective sample size” interpretation is valuable. If the results are indeed sharp and the presentation is tightened, this work is likely to be influential.

Pros
- Important problem with compelling, sharp results.
- Solid theoretical framework; matching upper/lower bounds.
- Simple, implementable mechanisms in the multinomial and β=1 cases.

Cons
- Overlap with [9]; unclear novelty and no proofs in this submission.
- Technical inconsistencies/typos in key formulas.
- Limited scope discussion (non-interactive only; 1D density; no interactivity or high-dim analysis).
- No empirical validation.

Suggestions for improvement
- Precisely delineate the contributions beyond [9]; include full proofs or detailed sketches and a complete supplement.
- Fix Eq. (8), clarify (10) to ensure Z’s support and α-LDP verification are consistent, and re-derive the stated expectations/variances.
- Provide explicit pseudocode and complexity for the orthogonal-series privatization; define ck and B0 clearly and bound them.
- Discuss interactivity: can interactive LDP improve the rates? If not, state known lower bounds; if unknown, say so.
- Add a brief synthetic experiment to illustrate constants and compare mechanisms (randomized response vs Laplace vs proposed series mechanism).
- Discuss extension to higher-dimensional density estimation and the impact of dimensionality.

Recommendation: Weak reject (borderline). The results are promising and likely important, but the submission in its current form lacks clarity, appears to overlap significantly with [9], and omits proofs. With tightened exposition, explicit novelty relative to [9], and corrected technical details, this would be a strong NeurIPS theory paper. Confidence: high.
