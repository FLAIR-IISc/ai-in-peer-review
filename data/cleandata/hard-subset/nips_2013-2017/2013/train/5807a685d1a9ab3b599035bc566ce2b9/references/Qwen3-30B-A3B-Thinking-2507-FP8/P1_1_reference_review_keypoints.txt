- Summary: Establishes sharp minimax rates under α-local differential privacy for multinomial and β-smooth density estimation, showing privacy reduces effective sample size and alters convergence rates.  
- Multinomial result: Effective sample size drops from n to nα²/d and Warner's randomized response is rate-optimal.  
- Density result: For β-smooth densities, rate shifts from n⁻²β/(2β+1) to (nα²)⁻²β/(2β+2), showing privacy fundamentally changes convergence.  
- Technical contribution: Provides tight lower bounds and efficient estimators achieving the bounds with complete theoretical proofs.  
- Quality: Paper is technically rigorous with sound, well-structured proofs and careful treatment of limitations.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized with consistent notation and clear background exposition.  
- Originality: Novelly proves optimality of randomized response for multinomials and identifies the new density estimation rate under local privacy.  
- Significance: Results give precise quantitative privacy-utility trade-offs with practical implications for private data collection.  
- Weakness — empirical validation: No simulations or numerical examples to illustrate practical sample-size implications.  
- Weakness — practical parameter choices: Lacks guidance on choosing the privacy parameter α in practice.  
- Weakness — scope of density estimation: Focuses on [0,1] with trigonometric basis; extensions to higher dimensions or other bases not discussed.  
- Weakness — literature positioning: Could better compare/position against recent NIPS work on privacy and learning.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, novel, and rigorously proven theoretical contribution.  
- Suggested improvement: Add discussion/examples on choosing α and the privacy-utility trade-off.  
- Suggested improvement: Include a short section comparing theoretical rates to practical sample-size requirements for given accuracy.  
- Suggested improvement: Briefly discuss extensions of density results to higher dimensions or other bases.  
- Suggested improvement: Explicitly connect and compare results to recent NIPS papers on privacy and statistical learning.
