- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of statistical estimation under local differential privacy, deriving sharp minimax convergence rates.  
- Establishes sharp rates for multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation, showing matching lower and upper bounds.  
- Demonstrates a concrete privacy–efficiency trade-off: privacy parameter α reduces effective sample size and degrades convergence rates.  
- Bridges classical randomized response (Warner) with modern differential privacy, showing randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Builds on and properly situates the work within prior literature (Warner, Dwork et al., Chaudhuri et al., and Duchi et al.).  
- Focuses specifically on local privacy and provides tight minimax optimality results using Le Cam/Fano-style arguments.  
- Theoretical framework and proofs are rigorous and well-structured (technical report supports details).  
- Novel insight: local privacy affects density estimation differently than multinomial estimation, yielding a different rate exponent.  
- Offers practical algorithmic implementations (orthogonal series for density estimation) that are computationally feasible.  
- Presentation is mostly clear with standard, well-defined notation and a motivating introduction.  
- Density estimation algorithm is somewhat impractical in form; choice of basis and tuning parameter k need fuller discussion.  
- Paper lacks empirical validation or simulations to illustrate performance degradation with varying α.  
- Early sections are technically dense; more intuitive explanations alongside formal definitions would aid accessibility.  
- Limited discussion of broader limitations of local privacy, such as sensitivity to the choice of privacy channel Q.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, sharp minimax rates, and strong connections across privacy and classical estimation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing empirical results, practical concerns about the density estimator, and potentially inaccessible technical exposition.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions to add simulations and clarify practical considerations.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
