- Bridges minimax decision theory and α-local differential privacy to characterize privacy–utility trade-offs in probability estimation.
- Provides sharp lower bounds and matching upper bounds for multinomial and density estimation under local privacy.
- Shows for multinomial estimation privacy reduces effective sample size from n to nα²/d; Warner’s randomized response is rate-optimal.
- Shows for density estimation privacy changes the convergence rate from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (α² n)^{-2β/(2β+2)}, a different exponent than the classical case.
- Technical quality is high: rigorous, complete proofs and careful treatment of previous approach limitations (e.g., Laplace perturbation fails).
- Original contribution: novel application of minimax theory to local privacy with surprising, insightful characterizations.
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, consistent notation, clear theorems, and good connection to practical mechanisms.
- Significance: first complete theoretical characterization of privacy–utility trade-offs in these problems with direct practical implications.
- Limited scope: focuses on multinomial and Sobolev classes without discussing broader generalizations to other models.
- Algorithmic details underexplored: computational complexity (e.g., orthogonal series sampling) may be burdensome for large k.
- Comparison to related work could be deeper (e.g., more discussion of Chaudhuri and Hsu and other recent results).
- Recommendation: accept — strong, novel, technically sound paper with minor, addressable weaknesses.
