- Paper studies estimation under non-interactive α-local differential privacy (LDP), unifying minimax decision theory with LDP guarantees.  
- Authors develop information-contraction bounds (KL/TV and mutual information) to derive sharp minimax lower bounds under LDP.  
- They provide matching, computationally efficient privatization and estimation schemes achieving the lower bounds.  
- Multinomial estimation: MSE and ℓ1 minimax risks scale as min{1, d/(nα2)} and min{1, d/√(nα2)}, so effective sample size becomes nα2/d.  
- Randomized response (and simple Laplace-perturbed counts) is shown to be minimax-optimal for the multinomial problem.  
- Density estimation in Sobolev class Fβ: nonprivate rate n−2β/(2β+1) degrades under LDP to (α2 n)−2β/(2β+2), a genuine worsening of the polynomial exponent.  
- They give implementable optimal estimators: privatized histograms for β=1 and orthogonal series with tailored privatization for β≥1.  
- Paper connects classic randomized response to modern LDP literature and builds on the authors’ prior information bounds [9].  
- Lower bounds use standard minimax reductions with LDP-specific information contraction; upper bounds are constructive and efficient.  
- Many proofs are deferred to [9]; including brief sketches (especially for mutual information variational bounds and key propositions) would improve completeness.  
- Minor technical issues: Eq. (10) likely uses “1−xj” instead of “−xj”; variance bound for Z is terse; constant c_k in (20) needs clearer dependence on k.  
- Clarity is generally good; suggestions include adding proof sketches, intuition for the rate exponent change under LDP, and explicit discussion of non-interactive vs interactive LDP.  
- Originality: provides new sharp minimax characterizations and identifies optimal mechanisms; the change in nonparametric exponent is notable.  
- Significance: quantifies privacy–utility tradeoffs, guides mechanism design, and sets benchmarks for private estimation.  
- Pros: sharp matching bounds, clear optimal mechanisms, conceptual insight on rate changes, and practical/efficient methods.  
- Cons: reliance on external report for proofs, focus only on non-interactive LDP, minor clarity/typo issues, and no empirical validation.  
- Questions for authors: can interactivity improve exponents or only constants; is adaptive selection of k under LDP possible; robustness to misspecification of α or heavy-tailed bases?  
- Minor comments/typos reiterated: correct Eq. (10), define Πk and kΔk at first use, and clarify Proposition 2’s second term and domination regimes.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, rigorous, and relevant contribution despite minor clarity and exposition issues.
