- Problem and model: Studies minimax estimation under α-local differential privacy (LDP) where each user privatizes data via a memoryless channel before aggregation.  
- Methodology: Lower bounds via reduction to private hypothesis testing (Le Cam/Fano) plus information-contraction inequalities; matching upper bounds from explicit non-interactive mechanisms.  
- Main result — Multinomial: α-private minimax risks scale as ||·||_2^2 ≍ min{1, d/(nα^2)} and ||·||_1 ≍ min{1, d/√(nα^2)}, achieved by a randomized-response variant.  
- Main result — Density estimation: For Sobolev class F_β, L2 minimax risk scales as (nα^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)), altering the classical exponent; optimal privatized histogram/orthogonal-series mechanisms given.  
- Methodological insight: Naive Laplace perturbation of raw samples is suboptimal for density estimation due to characteristic-function decay (deconvolution limits).  
- Relation to prior work: Connects classical randomized response to DP, builds on Duchi–Jordan–Wainwright info bounds, and sharpens prior learnability/sample-complexity results in LDP with concrete minimax rates.  
- Strengths: Technical depth and sharp α,n,d,β dependence; constructive, efficient non-interactive mechanisms; conceptual result that LDP can change nonparametric rates.  
- Weakness — sampler in Sec.4.3: The proposed halfspace-uniform sampler over {−B,B}^k lacks an explicit O(k)-time uniform algorithm or runtime bound for rejection sampling.  
- Weakness — constant c_k: The constant c_k in Eq.(20) is claimed O(1) but its dependence on k (or uniform boundedness) is not specified, yet affects B and variance scaling.  
- Weakness — histogram mechanism: The α-LDP claim for Laplace noise + projection needs a short proof sketch and the extra √α n^(−3/4) term should be clarified or tightened.  
- Weakness — randomized response: Clarify domain of α-LDP guarantee (one-hot vs Δ_d) and provide the omitted ℓ1-risk calculation for completeness.  
- Scope questions: Analysis is for α∈[0,1] only — discuss larger α and transition to classical rates; state whether interactivity can improve rates; note lack of results for L1 or sup-norm density risks under LDP.  
- Empirics: Paper would benefit from small synthetic experiments illustrating MSE/error vs n,α,d for the proposed mechanisms.  
- Quality: Results are technically sound but some algorithmic/constant details need tightening to fully justify achievability.  
- Clarity: Generally well written; recommend adding brief proof sketches for privacy guarantees and the ℓ1 upper bound and clarifying sampler/constants.  
- Originality: High — provides novel, sharp minimax rates and optimal mechanisms in the LDP setting.  
- Significance: Strong — sets benchmarks for private estimation under LDP and shows notable changes in nonparametric exponents.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Sharp, constructive minimax results with clear practical implications (randomized response optimality, Laplace pitfalls).  
- Arguments against acceptance: Some implementational details underspecified and no empirical demonstrations; a few proofs/constants deferred.  
- Recommendation: Accept after minor revisions to clarify orthogonal-series sampling, constants, and include brief proof sketches for key claims.
