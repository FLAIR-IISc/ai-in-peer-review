Summary
The paper studies minimax rates for probability estimation under local (α-)differential privacy. It formalizes the private minimax risk by restricting estimators to those operating on privatized samples Zi obtained via a non-interactive channel Q satisfying α-local DP. Two main problems are analyzed:

- Multinomial estimation (θ ∈ Δd): Lower and upper bounds show MSE scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)}, and ℓ1-risk as min{1, d/√(nα^2)}. The authors argue the effective sample size degrades from n to nα^2/d for small α, and that a variant of randomized response achieves the rate.

- Nonparametric density estimation on [0,1] in Sobolev classes Fβ[C]: The minimax L2-risk under local DP scales as (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2), worsening the non-private exponent 2β/(2β+1). Achievability is claimed via (i) privatized histograms for β=1 and (ii) a bounded orthonormal-series scheme for general β, both with computationally efficient sampling/estimation.

Lower bounds are obtained via Le Cam/Fano reductions combined with information-contraction bounds under local DP (citing the authors’ prior work [9]).

Relation to prior work
This line of work connects local DP to statistical decision theory and minimax lower bounds. The multinomial result relates to Warner randomized response and k-ary RR; density estimation relates to classical nonprivate minimax theory (e.g., Tsybakov/Efromovich) and deconvolution limitations (Carroll–Hall). Information-theoretic techniques (Fano/Le Cam, mutual information bounds) are standard; the local-DP contraction inequalities are taken from [9].

Strengths
- Conceptually valuable unification of local DP and minimax decision theory, with clean rates illustrating privacy–utility tradeoffs.
- Sharp lower bounds with matching (claimed) upper bounds for both multinomial and Sobolev density estimation; the change in nonparametric exponent is interesting and important.
- Practical mechanisms (histogram; orthogonal series with bounded outputs) that are computationally simple and respect local DP.

Weaknesses and concerns
1) Incompleteness: proofs deferred to [9]. The submission states “we provide proofs of all results in [9]” and “we restate two theorems from [9].” As a NeurIPS submission, the paper should be self-contained at least for the main theorems, or provide detailed sketches enabling verification. As written, core technical contributions rely on an external arXiv preprint.

2) Multinomial mechanism (Sec. 3.2) is unclear/inconsistent:
- Z is said to lie in {0,1}^d, but (10) assigns [Z]j = −xj with some probability, which would produce −1 entries when xj=1.
- The mechanism independently perturbs each coordinate of a one-hot x, potentially producing multiple ones; this is not the standard k-ary randomized response. The text claims optimality of “randomized response,” but the mechanism differs from classical RR and needs precise specification and justification.
- The α-LDP proof sketch uses a likelihood ratio e^(α/2)(‖z−x‖1−‖z−x′‖1); this seems to skip over the actual product-of-Bernoulli likelihood and constants. Please provide a correct derivation or adopt a standard k-ary RR mechanism with p = e^α/(e^α + d − 1), q = 1/(e^α + d − 1).
- The variance bound E[‖Z − E Z‖^2] < d/4 + 1 < d is puzzling; for Bernoulli coordinates one expects ≤ d/4. Clarify the extra “+1” or correct it.
- The estimator (11) has notational ambiguities: the subtraction “1/(1+e^{α/2})” should be a ones-vector scaled by that constant; parentheses and vector operations should be made explicit.

3) Definition 1 (Sobolev class) appears incorrect: the constraint is written as ∑ j j^{2β} ϕ_j^2 ≤ C^2; it should involve coefficients θ_j (∑ j j^{2β} θ_j^2 ≤ C^2). This is a substantive typo.

4) References are incomplete/inconsistent:
- Many entries lack years/venues/pages; several have placeholder commas only; “Fourty” should be “Forty.”
- In-text citation numbers do not match the reference list size (e.g., repeated [26,25,22] but only 24 entries are present). Please fix numbering and ensure every citation maps correctly.
- The algorithmic citation for simplex projection “[3]” points to “An O(n) algorithm for quadratic knapsack” (Brucker), which is not the right reference. Standard references include: Duchi et al. (ICML 2008) for ℓ1-ball projection, Michelot (1986), or Condat (2016)/Wang & Carreira-Perpiñán (2013) for simplex projection.

5) Local DP claims for mechanisms:
- Histogram scheme (15): adding i.i.d. Laplace(α/2) noise to each coordinate indeed yields a density proportional to exp(−(α/2)‖z−e_k‖1), which is α-LDP due to triangle inequality. This should be stated and proved carefully.
- Orthogonal-series scheme (19): The α-LDP claim is asserted; a precise proof sketch would help. Also, clarify the role and bounds of c_k, and ensure B selection B = Θ(B0√k/α) is consistent with unbiasedness and variance bounds. It would be useful to bound c_k uniformly (e.g., show c_k ∈ [c_min, c_max]) and to detail the rejection sampling complexity.

6) Lower-bound statements rely critically on Proposition 1 from [9]. Given that these are central to the contribution, at least a self-contained statement with proof sketch (and conditions under which the sup over S is tight) should be included. Also, clarify the notation in (7): P^i is defined but the bound uses P(S) without superscript.

7) Minor technical issues and clarity:
- The notation “an . bn” for O(·) is nonstandard; consider using an = O(bn).
- Lemma 1 (5b): ensure correct form 1 − (I + log 2)/log |V|.
- State clearly the dependence and ranges of universal constants c_ℓ, c_u, and when the “sufficiently small” α regime is assumed for claims like n → nα^2/d.
- In Sec. 4.2, choose k explicitly (k = (nα^2)^{1/4}) and show the bias–variance trade-off leading to (17); the extra √α n^{−3/4} term should be derived or referenced precisely.
- Consistency in notation for basis functions (ϕ), coefficients (θ), and densities f̂.

Pros
- Sharp, impactful rates illuminating the local DP price for both finite- and infinite-dimensional problems.
- Clear high-level roadmap and plausible mechanisms with efficient computation.
- Timely and relevant to NeurIPS.

Cons
- Heavy reliance on external arXiv for proofs; not self-contained.
- Multiple technical/notation errors (some substantive), unclear mechanism in multinomial section, and incorrect citations.
- Reference list and citation mapping are in poor shape.

Recommendation: Reject (as currently written)
The core ideas are strong and likely worthy of publication after substantial revision, but the paper in its current form has too many technical and presentation issues for NeurIPS. I recommend a thorough rewrite to (i) include self-contained proofs or detailed sketches of the main results, (ii) correct the multinomial mechanism and its DP/variance analysis, (iii) fix Definition 1 and all notational inconsistencies, (iv) repair the reference list and citation mapping, and (v) provide precise privacy and risk analyses for the proposed mechanisms. I would be enthusiastic to reconsider a polished, corrected version.
