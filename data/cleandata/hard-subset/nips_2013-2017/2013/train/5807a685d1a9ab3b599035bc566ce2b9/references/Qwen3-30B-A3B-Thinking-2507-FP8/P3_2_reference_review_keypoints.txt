- Establishes sharp minimax rates for probability estimation under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation.  
- Multinomial: effective sample size drops from n to nα²/d; Warner's randomized response is rate-optimal.  
- Density (β-smooth): classical rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} becomes (α²n)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy.  
- Provides matching lower and upper bounds and efficient algorithms that achieve the derived rates.  
- Technical analysis is rigorous and builds on established differential privacy and minimax theory.  
- Density estimation results are particularly strong and reveal a fundamentally different convergence structure.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses clear notation and logical exposition.  
- The explanation of randomized response and its optimality is clear and accessible.  
- Novel contribution: focuses on local privacy minimax rates and connects modern results to Warner (1965).  
- Results are significant for understanding fundamental privacy–utility trade-offs and guiding practitioners.  
- Limitation: analysis restricted to the non-interactive (memoryless) setting; interactive mechanisms not addressed.  
- Practical guidance is limited on choosing α and scaling to large d or high-dimensional data.  
- Computational complexity of the proposed algorithms is not fully analyzed in terms of d or basis size k.  
- Could more explicitly connect to recent related work in differentially private statistical estimation (e.g., Bassily et al., Wang et al.).  
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS) — strong theoretical contribution with some practical and scope limitations.
