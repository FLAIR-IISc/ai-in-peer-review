- Paper studies minimax estimation under non-interactive local differential privacy (LDP), combining Le Cam/Fano with information-contraction bounds to derive sharp rates and matching algorithms.

- Main contribution (multinomial): α-private minimax ℓ2 risk scales as min{1, d/(nα2)} and ℓ1 as min{1, d/√(nα2)}, with randomized response shown optimal.

- Main contribution (density estimation): For Sobolev class Fβ, private rate degrades from n−2β/(2β+1) to (nα2)−2β/(2β+2); achieved by privatized histogram (β=1) and a bounded orthogonal-series scheme.

- Shows that naive Laplace perturbation of Xi yields worse deconvolution rates and cannot attain the optimal private rate.

- Builds on authors’ prior technical work [9], restating key KL and mutual-information contraction bounds under LDP.

- Technical soundness: Lower bounds use standard Le Cam/Fano with sharp LDP information bounds; upper bounds are constructive and computationally feasible.

- Completeness: Covers lower and upper bounds and implementable algorithms, though some practical choices (k, ck, rejection sampling costs) need elaboration.

- Clarity: Generally clear and well-organized; mechanism descriptions are reproducible.

- Minor clarity issues: ambiguity in bit-flipping notation (Eq. 10) and insufficient details on ck, B, acceptance rates, and parameter ranges for the n → nα2/d heuristic.

- Originality: Novel precise minimax rates under local privacy and optimality of randomized response for multinomial estimation; orthogonal-series mechanism is technically interesting.

- Concern: Significant reliance on the authors’ arXiv [9] for proofs; novelty versus [9] should be clearly stated.

- Significance: High — establishes fundamental utility/privacy trade-offs under LDP and influences mechanism choice in practice and theory.

- Strengths: Sharp matching bounds, practical mechanisms, unified minimax-information framework, and clear exposition.

- Weaknesses: Dependent on [9] for proofs, focused only on non-interactive LDP, lacks empirical validation, and doesn’t address adaptivity to unknown smoothness β.

- Suggestions: Clarify novelty relative to [9], discuss potential gains from interactive LDP, provide guidance for adaptive k and mechanism constants, and add experiments illustrating rates and Laplace suboptimality.

- Pros for acceptance: Foundational, well-written, and practically relevant theoretical contributions.

- Cons for acceptance: Overlap with arXiv manuscript and limited scope (non-interactive only) with no empirical results.

- Overall recommendation: Accept, subject to clarifying novelty vs. [9] and improving a few presentation details.
