Summary
This paper studies statistical estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) constraints via a minimax lens. The authors analyze two canonical problems: multinomial probability estimation and nonparametric density estimation over Sobolev classes. They derive sharp lower bounds under α-LDP using information-contraction inequalities tailored to local channels and match them with computationally efficient upper bounds.

Key results:
- Multinomial estimation: Under α-LDP, the minimax mean-squared error (MSE) degrades from the nonprivate d/n rate to d/(n α^2) (up to constants), i.e., the effective sample size becomes nα^2/d. They show a variant of randomized response (RR) achieves this rate, as do simple Laplace-perturbation schemes followed by projection.
- Density estimation: For Sobolev smoothness β, the nonprivate rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} deteriorates under α-LDP to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}. They provide two optimal private estimators: a privatized histogram for β=1, and a privatized orthogonal series estimator for general β≥1, using a carefully designed locally private channel that is not simple additive noise.

Relation to prior work:
The paper connects Warner’s randomized response (local privacy) with recent formalizations of differential privacy. The lower bounds leverage information inequalities from Duchi, Jordan, and Wainwright (arXiv:1302.3203), and the density estimation side relates to classical nonparametrics (Tsybakov, Efromovich) and deconvolution barriers (Carroll and Hall). Compared to earlier DP optimality works focused on sample quantities or central-DP (e.g., Hardt-Talwar, Smith, Wasserman-Zhou, Chaudhuri et al.), this work cleanly characterizes population-level minimax rates in the local model and identifies optimal channels.

Quality
- The technical core is strong: lower bounds via mutual information contraction specialized to LDP, and matching upper bounds via explicit privatization mechanisms and estimators. The density result’s change in exponent is both technically and conceptually interesting.
- The orthogonal-series mechanism is carefully constructed to satisfy α-LDP while preserving unbiasedness (up to scaling), and the choice of B ~ B0√k/α is well motivated.
- A few issues need correction/clarification:
  - Theorem 1 appears to contain a typo in the lower bound: the term “√(n α^2)” in the min does not make sense dimensionally as a lower bound on MSE and likely should be “1/√(n α^2)” or omitted; the intended rate is d/(n α^2).
  - The randomized response mechanism (Eq. 10) uses “−xj,” which would produce entries −1 and contradicts later claims that coordinates are Bernoulli. This likely should be “1 − xj” (bit-flip). The privacy ratio derivation also implicitly assumes z ∈ {0,1}^d (Hamming metric), which should be stated consistently.
  - Definition 1 of the Sobolev class has a notational error: the constraint should be on the coefficients θj (∑ j^{2β} θ_j^2 ≤ C^2), not on basis functions ϕj.
  - The text says proofs are in [9]; this submission would benefit from sketches of the main arguments (at least proof outlines for Theorems 1–2 and Propositions 2–3) to make it more self-contained.
  - The constant ck in Eq. (20) is asserted to be O(1). A brief derivation or citation would help.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written and organized, with helpful reminders of information-theoretic quantities and the minimax testing reduction. The mechanisms for the upper bounds are clearly described, and the histogram/series constructions are intuitive. However, several typos and notational inconsistencies (noted above) should be fixed. Including a short discussion on sampling efficiency of the halfspace-uniform mechanism (acceptance probability, computational cost) would aid reproducibility.

Originality
The combination of sharp minimax lower bounds and matching constructive upper bounds under LDP for both multinomial and Sobolev density estimation appears novel and impactful. The strong claim that classical RR is optimal for multinomial estimation under LDP is particularly nice. The paper relies on divergence bounds developed by the same authors in [9]; the novelty here lies in the instantiation to concrete problems with tight rates and optimal mechanisms. The authors should clearly delineate what is new here versus [9].

Significance
Understanding the precise privacy-utility tradeoff in the local model is important both theoretically and practically (e.g., survey design, telemetry, federated analytics). The results will likely serve as benchmarks and guide mechanism design. The demonstration that naive additive noise cannot achieve optimal density estimation rates underscores the importance of mechanism design beyond Laplace perturbation.

Pros
- Sharp, matching lower and upper bounds under local DP for two central estimation problems.
- Clear identification of effective sample size degradation and change in nonparametric rates.
- Elegant and implementable mechanisms (RR variant; histogram; orthogonal series with private channel).
- Good integration of information-theoretic tools with statistical minimax methodology.

Cons
- Some technical typos/inconsistencies (RR mechanism, Sobolev definition, Theorem 1 bound).
- Heavy reliance on an external arXiv for proofs; limited proof sketches in the submission.
- No empirical illustrations to complement theory (optional but could strengthen impact).
- Related work could better contrast with local model learning results (e.g., SQ model connections).

Suggestions for improvement
- Fix the noted typos and clarify the randomized response mechanism and privacy proof.
- Provide proof sketches for Theorems 1–2 and at least the key steps for Propositions 2–3.
- Clarify novelty relative to [9] and what is contributed in this submission.
- Briefly discuss computational aspects (sampling efficiency, constants) of the vector privatization mechanism.
- Optionally add a small simulation to visualize the rates and constants.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper makes significant, well-motivated theoretical contributions with clear implications for private data analysis in the local model. Addressing the clarity issues and better self-containment would make it a strong accept.
