- Connects local differential privacy and minimax decision theory, giving sharp convergence rates for multinomial and density estimation.
- Builds on Warner’s randomized response and shows it is optimal for multinomial estimation; privacy alters density estimation rates.
- Provides tight minimax bounds with matching, efficiently implementable algorithms and rigorous proofs.
- Demonstrates that privacy changes the density estimation exponent from 2β/(2β+1) to 2β/(2β+2).
- Shows practical relevance by theoretically justifying Warner’s randomized response for multinomial problems.
- Combines information-theoretic tools (Fano, Le Cam) with privacy constraints in technically sophisticated, well-structured proofs.
- Gives clear algorithmic constructions (privatized histogram, orthogonal series estimator) that achieve the optimal rates.
- Limited comparison to other privacy mechanisms (e.g., perturbation approaches) weakens practical contextualization.
- Orthogonal series estimator’s sampling strategy is non-intuitive and needs clearer motivation and comparison to simpler alternatives.
- Insufficient discussion of practical trade-offs between privacy level α and statistical efficiency in applied settings.
- Related-work coverage could better engage recent advances in private statistical estimation (e.g., Chaudhuri & Hsu, Smith).
- Overall: technically strong, important theoretical contribution; recommendation: Accept.
