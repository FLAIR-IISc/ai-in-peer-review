Summary
The paper studies statistical estimation under local (non-interactive) differential privacy (LDP). It adopts a minimax decision-theoretic framework and derives sharp lower and upper rates for two core problems:
- Multinomial estimation: For θ ∈ Δd, it shows α-LDP reduces the effective sample size to roughly nα2/d. Theorems give matching (up to constants) rates in ℓ2 and ℓ1 risks and show a (modified) randomized response mechanism and a Laplace-perturbation-of-counts mechanism are optimal.
- Density estimation over Sobolev classes Fβ: Under α-LDP, the minimax L2 risk deteriorates from n−2β/(2β+1) (non-private) to (nα2)−2β/(2β+2). The bounds are shown tight via private histogram (β=1) and an orthogonal series mechanism (β≥1) that uses a nontrivial privatization strategy not reducible to simple additive noise on raw X.

The work leverages testing reductions (Le Cam, Fano) and information bounds specialized to LDP.

Relation to prior work
The paper connects Warner’s randomized response to modern LDP and formalizes utility–privacy tradeoffs via minimax theory. It relies on information-theoretic tools previously developed by the authors ([9]) and contrasts with central DP works that target sample statistics rather than population parameters. The density estimation result clarifies why simple Laplace noise on X is suboptimal (deconvolution lower bounds, Carroll-Hall). The topic is timely and relevant to NeurIPS.

Strengths
- Clear, principled minimax framework for LDP with tight lower and upper bounds.
- Conceptual contribution: precise “effective sample size” degradation for multinomial; polynomial-exponent change for nonparametric density estimation.
- Mechanism-design insights: randomized response is optimal; additive noise on sufficient statistics (histogram counts) can be optimal; need for nontrivial privatization for series estimators.
- Results are likely to be influential and widely used.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and completeness
- Theorem 1 (eq. 8): The lower bound includes min{1, sqrt(nα2), d/(nα2)}. The term sqrt(nα2) increases with n and seems dimensionally inconsistent for a risk; likely a typo (perhaps 1/√(nα2)?). As written, it is confusing and undermines confidence in the bound.
- Section 3.2 randomized response channel (eq. 10): “[Z]j = xj with prob … ; −xj with prob …”. “−xj” cannot be a Bernoulli output in {0,1}. It seems the intent is to output 1−xj; please correct and re-derive the DP ratio carefully.
- Variance bound for Z: “E[||Z−E[Z]||2^2] < d/4 + 1 < d.” The +1 is unexplained and likely unnecessary if coordinates are independent (sum of Bernoulli variances ≤ d/4). Please justify or fix.
- Fano inequality (eq. 5b): The expression “≥ [1 − I + log 2 / log |V|]” is missing parentheses; standard form is P_e ≥ 1 − (I + log 2)/log |V|.
- Sobolev class definition (Def. 1): The constraint is written as ∑ j2β ϕ2j ≤ C2; this should be ∑ j2β θ2j ≤ C2 (weights on coefficient magnitudes), not basis functions.
- Orthogonal series mechanism: The key constant ck is left unspecified (“may depend on k but is O(1) and bounded away from 0”). The analysis depends on ck; the paper should define it, show its value/range, and how it arises. As is, the unbiasedness/scaling and variance calculations are not fully reproducible.
- Mechanism (eq. 19) “by inspection” LDP claim is too terse. A formal proof of α-LDP (or a precise reference) is needed since the distribution is a constrained uniform over 2^k vertices conditioned on a half-space; it is not obvious that likelihood ratios are bounded uniformly over all τ, τ′ in [−B0, B0]^k.
- Computational feasibility: Drawing a vector uniformly from {−B,B}^k subject to 〈z, τ̃〉>0 (or ≤0) at scale k ≈ (nα2)1/(2β+2) needs a concrete sampling algorithm and complexity guarantees. “By rejection sampling” is vague; naive rejection from the hypercube accepts with probability ≈ 1/2, but implementing exact conditional uniformity efficiently without exponential preprocessing is nontrivial. Please specify a practical, polynomial-time sampler.
- Several core proofs (e.g., Theorem 2) are deferred to [9]. A NeurIPS paper should be more self-contained. At least provide full proof sketches and all key lemmas in the appendix.

Clarity and presentation
- Numerous typos and notational inconsistencies make parts hard to follow:
  - Eq. (3) uses Mnν(A) and later M n ν; unify notation.
  - ΠΔd vs Πk and kΔk not clearly introduced; define kΔk explicitly when first used.
  - Boldface for 1 vs scalar 1 is inconsistent; in (11) the vector of ones is missing.
  - “Fourty-Second/Fourty-Third” in references; should be “Forty-…”.
  - “we provide proofs of all results in [9]” reads incorrectly (you mean “we provide proofs in [9]” but then this paper is not self-contained).
- Reference list is incomplete/inconsistent (missing years, venues, pages, capitalization, dangling commas). Several entries lack publication details.

Significance/originality
- The problem and results are important; however, substantial overlap with [9] needs to be clarified explicitly. What is new here beyond restating bounds and providing attainability for multinomial/density? If the key results first appeared in [9], this submission must delineate the incremental novelty.

Specific suggestions/questions
- Fix Theorem 1 and all typos noted above; provide a careful, self-contained derivation of the RR estimator’s bias/variance and the variance bound.
- Provide a full proof (or detailed sketch) of Theorem 2 in the appendix, and rigorously derive Propositions 2 and 3, including constants and dependence on α, β, d, k.
- Give a formal α-LDP proof for mechanism (19) and an efficient exact sampling algorithm with complexity analysis.
- Clarify why Laplace noise on ek(X) achieves the histogram rate, and contrast more explicitly with Laplace noise directly on X via deconvolution rates.
- Unify notation and add a table of symbols; fix equation numbering and missing parentheses.
- Clean up and complete the references.

Pros
- Sharp, conceptually clean minimax rates under LDP for two fundamental problems.
- Matching mechanisms and clear insight on why some natural mechanisms are suboptimal.
- High potential impact.

Cons
- Multiple technical and editorial errors (some possibly substantive, e.g., Theorem 1, Def. 1, Fano form).
- Insufficient self-containment; heavy reliance on [9].
- Orthogonal series mechanism’s LDP and sampling efficiency are under-specified.
- Reference list and notation are sloppy.

Recommendation
Weak reject. The results are significant, but the accumulation of technical inaccuracies/typos, missing proofs/details, and presentation issues is too large for NeurIPS in the current form. With careful correction, fuller proofs, and clarified novelty relative to [9], this could be a strong paper.
