Summary and relation to prior work
This paper studies statistical estimation under local differential privacy (LDP). It considers the non-interactive (memoryless channel) setting where each Xi is privatized independently via an α-LDP channel Qi, and focuses on population risk. The authors bring together minimax decision theory and LDP: (i) they restate and use information-contraction bounds under LDP (KL and mutual information variational bounds) to derive sharp lower bounds on private minimax risk, and (ii) they develop matching, computationally efficient mechanisms achieving these rates.

Two canonical problems are treated in depth:
- Multinomial estimation (θ ∈ Δd): The paper shows the squared L2 and L1 risks scale as d/(nα2) up to constants when α ≤ 1, implying an effective sample size loss n → nα2/d. They show a slight modification of Warner’s randomized response (RR) achieves the optimal rate, providing a clean optimality statement for a 50-year-old mechanism within the LDP-minimax framework.
- Density estimation on [0,1] with Sobolev smoothness β: In the nonprivate setting the minimax L2 risk scales as n−2β/(2β+1). Under LDP, the paper proves a fundamental change in the exponent: (nα2)−2β/(2β+2). They give matching upper bounds using (a) privatized histograms for β=1 and (b) a bounded orthonormal series estimator with a novel privatization scheme for β≥1 that avoids simple additive noise and is tailored to achieve the sharp rate.

The work is situated relative to DP and statistics: Warner’s RR, Wasserman & Zhou on statistical DP, deconvolution rates (Carroll & Hall) to show the suboptimality of direct additive noise, ERM under DP (Chaudhuri et al.), geometric lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar), learning under DP (Kasiviswanathan et al.), and earlier lower bounds in DP. A key ingredient is the information-contraction bounds under α-LDP from the authors’ prior work [9], which enable sharp minimax lower bounds.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The lower bounds leverage Le Cam/Fano with LDP-specific divergence contractions; the upper bounds are constructive and efficient (projection onto simplex in O(d), privatized histogram, orthogonal series with bounded basis). The departure in nonparametric rates is compelling and well-justified (including the deconvolution barrier for Laplace noise).
- Completeness: Many proofs are deferred to [9]. For NeurIPS, at least proof sketches of the main theorems would improve self-containedness. The privatization scheme for orthogonal series (thresholded randomized signs with a Bernoulli switch) is interesting; a clearer privacy proof and complexity analysis (e.g., expected trials in rejection sampling) would help.
- Correctness/clarity issues to check:
  - Theorem 1 lower bound expression in L2 seems to include a term √(nα2), which grows with n; likely a typographical error (dimensionally inconsistent). Please clarify the exact lower bound form.
  - In the d-dimensional RR description, equation (10) says “[Z]j = xj with probability ... and −xj otherwise,” but Z∈{0,1}d and xj∈{0,1}, so “−xj” is not in {0,1}. This looks like a typo; perhaps it should be “1−xj.” Please fix and re-derive the expectation accordingly.
  - Some references are incomplete.

Clarity
The paper is well-organized, with a clear formalization of the LDP minimax risk and a coherent development from information bounds to applications. Notation is standard. However, to be self-contained and more readable:
- Include at least proof sketches for Theorems 1 and 2 and the key propositions, or move essential lemmas from [9] into the appendix.
- Improve the description and intuition for the orthogonal-series mechanism (19) and its privacy guarantee; quantify constants like ck and the choice of B, and discuss implementation details.
- Fix typos (mentioned above) and complete references.

Originality
The conceptual synthesis—minimax decision theory under LDP with tight rates for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation—is strong. The finding that LDP changes nonparametric exponents is novel and important. The optimality of RR for multinomials under LDP is crisp. While the information bounds are from the authors’ prior work [9], the application to these canonical problems with matching mechanisms is a substantive and original contribution. Please clarify how this submission advances beyond [9] (e.g., which results are new, which proofs are new, and what is only restated).

Significance
High. The results provide principled guidance on the cost of local privacy in two foundational problems, with clean prescriptions for optimal mechanisms. The change in rate exponents for nonparametrics under LDP is likely to influence future work in private estimation and algorithm design. The results are of broad interest to theory of machine learning, statistics, and privacy.

Pros
- Sharp, matching upper/lower bounds under LDP for multinomial and density estimation.
- Clear identification of effective sample size loss and a fundamental change in nonparametric rates.
- Simple, efficient mechanisms; RR shown optimal; thoughtful mechanism design for orthogonal series.
- Solid information-theoretic lower bound technique specialized to LDP.

Cons
- Heavy reliance on a companion paper [9] for proofs; limited proof detail here.
- Some technical/typographical issues (Theorem 1 form; RR mechanism description).
- No empirical illustration or finite-sample evaluation.
- Scope restricted to non-interactive LDP; adaptivity to unknown β and multivariate extensions not discussed.
- Some references incomplete.

Questions and suggestions
- Clarify the exact statements and constants in Theorem 1 (and fix the RR typo).
- Provide proof sketches for Theorem 2 and Propositions 2–3 to make the submission self-contained.
- Discuss adaptivity to unknown smoothness (e.g., private model selection or Lepski under LDP) and the privacy cost of tuning k.
- Comment on the computational complexity and acceptance rate of the sampling scheme (19); can it be implemented without rejection sampling? Any connection to extremal LDP mechanisms?
- Extend the discussion to multivariate densities and to interactive protocols; are the lower bounds tight there?
- Complete and standardize references.

Recommendation: Weak accept
This is a strong theoretical paper with clear, sharp, and practically meaningful results on LDP minimax rates and mechanisms. Addressing the presentation issues and clarifying novelty vis-à-vis [9] would strengthen it further.
