- Paper derives sharp private minimax rates under local differential privacy (LDP) for two problems: multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Multinomial result: private minimax risk scales as d/(n α^2), implying effective sample size n → n α^2/d; Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal.  
- Density estimation result: classical rate n−2β/(2β+1) degrades under LDP to (n α^2)−2β/(2β+2), with constructive mechanisms achieving the rate.  
- Upper bounds: privatized histogram (β=1) and a tailored privatized orthogonal-series estimator (β≥1) are computationally efficient in the non-interactive model.  
- Lower bounds: obtained via packing reductions and information-theoretic inequalities (Le Cam/Fano) combined with privacy-constrained divergence/mutual information bounds.  
- Builds on prior work: randomized response, differential privacy foundations, local model learning, minimax theory, and recent DP information bounds.  
- Advances beyond prior work by characterizing population risk under LDP and improving over deconvolution-based noisy releases for nonparametrics.  
- Technical soundness: theorems give matching upper/lower bounds; mechanisms are explicitly constructed and privacy verified.  
- Completeness: some technical proofs are deferred to an arXiv version, reducing self-containment of the submission.  
- Assumptions: non-interactive, i.i.d. sampling, bounded α (often ≤1), and bounded basis functions — assumptions are reasonable and stated.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-structured exposition, though notation is heavy and some constants and conditions need clearer explanation.  
- Originality: novel sharp private minimax rates and a new orthogonal-series privatization mechanism that is different from Laplace perturbation.  
- Significance: clarifies privacy–utility tradeoffs, validates randomized response for categorical data, and provides implementable optimal mechanisms.  
- Strengths: sharp matching bounds, unification of minimax theory with LDP, optimal randomized response result, and rate-optimal nonparametric mechanism.  
- Weaknesses/questions: reliance on arXiv for proofs, focus on non-interactive LDP (interaction gap not fully discussed), and limited detail on orthogonal-series computational constants.  
- Practicality concerns: tuning (choice of k) requires knowledge of β and adaptivity/privacy-cost issues are not fully addressed.  
- Suggestions: add appendix sketches of lower bounds, discuss interaction effects, elaborate on rejection-sampling efficiency/constants, address private adaptivity, and update simplex-projection citations.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — high-quality, novel theoretical contributions with minor concerns about self-containment and implementation details.
