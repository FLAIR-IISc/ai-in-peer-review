- Paper establishes sharp minimax rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation under local differential privacy.  
- Shows privacy reduces effective sample size for multinomial estimation from n to nα²/d.  
- Derives altered convergence rate for density estimation from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)} for β-smooth densities.  
- Demonstrates Warner's randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Provides efficient estimators that achieve the derived lower bounds for both problems.  
- Technically rigorous with tight lower and upper bounds and precise conditions.  
- Original contribution linking classical randomized response to modern differential privacy and minimax theory.  
- Significant theoretical insight into privacy–utility trade-offs that can guide design of private procedures.  
- Well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation.  
- Lacks empirical validation of proposed estimators despite claiming computational efficiency.  
- Scope is somewhat narrow, focusing only on multinomial and density estimation without extension to other models.  
- Could better distinguish itself from recent related work in differentially private estimation (e.g., Chaudhuri & Hsu, Smith).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance (NIPS) given the strong theoretical contribution.  
- Pros: sharp rates, surprising optimality of randomized response, rigorous analysis, clear presentation.  
- Cons: missing empirical results, limited problem scope, and incomplete comparison to some recent literature.
