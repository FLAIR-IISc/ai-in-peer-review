Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies statistical estimation under local (non-interactive) differential privacy constraints. It formalizes the α-local privacy model via likelihood ratio bound (Evfimievski et al., 2003) and defines a private minimax risk over all α-private channels and estimators. Using information-theoretic tools (Le Cam/Fano and new bounds on divergences under local DP), the authors derive sharp minimax rates for two problems:

- Multinomial estimation (θ ∈ Δd): The private minimax risk in ℓ2 scales as min{1, d/(nα2)} and in ℓ1 as min{1, d/√(nα2)} for α ∈ [0,1], implying an effective sample size reduction n → nα2/d relative to the non-private 1/n rate. They provide matching upper bounds via simple procedures, including a slight variant of Warner’s randomized response (1965) and Laplace perturbation, showing RR is minimax optimal for this task.

- Density estimation over Sobolev classes Fβ on [0,1]: The non-private rate n−2β/(2β+1) degrades under local DP to (nα2)−2β/(2β+2). For β=1 (Lipschitz), a privatized histogram with k∝(nα2)1/4 bins achieves the lower bound. For β≥1, they propose a locally private orthogonal-series mechanism (not simple additive noise on X) that attains the optimal rate. They also argue that direct perturbation of samples (e.g., Laplace noise) cannot achieve the optimal rate due to deconvolution barriers (Carroll & Hall, 1988).

The analysis builds on general information bounds for locally private channels (restated from Duchi et al., 2013 arXiv), connecting α-local privacy to reduced mutual information and KL between privatized distributions. The work relates to DP foundations (Dwork et al., 2006; Hardt & Talwar, 2010), statistical DP frameworks (Wasserman & Zhou, 2010), and local-model learnability (Kasiviswanathan et al., 2011), and complements prior optimality results in global DP (e.g., Smith, 2011) by focusing on population risk under local constraints.

Strengths
- Sharp characterization with matching upper and lower bounds for two canonical problems; clear quantitative privacy–accuracy trade-offs.
- Identification of RR as minimax optimal for multinomial estimation under local DP provides a clean and practically relevant message.
- Novel rate phenomenon in nonparametric density estimation: change in exponent due to local privacy, with tailored mechanisms that achieve optimality.
- Methodologically sound use of information-theoretic inequalities to derive general lower bounds under local privacy constraints.
- Algorithms are conceptually simple (histograms; series estimators) and respect non-negativity and normalization for densities.

Weaknesses and limitations
- Scope restricted to local, non-interactive channels; potential gains from interaction are not analyzed.
- Several proofs are deferred to an external long version [9], limiting self-containment. Formal privacy and accuracy guarantees for the orthogonal-series mechanism (including constants ck) are only sketched.
- Computational aspects of the series mechanism (sampling uniformly over a high-dimensional halfspace within {±B}k) are not fully detailed; efficiency guarantees beyond “rejection sampling” are not quantified.
- Assumptions such as uniformly bounded bases and knowledge of β for tuning k are standard but limit adaptivity; no discussion of private model selection or adaptation to unknown smoothness.
- Constants and finite-sample behavior are not explored; empirical demonstrations are absent.

Assessment by review criteria
- Quality: The theoretical results are technically sound, with lower bounds derived via standard testing reductions and specialized information bounds for local DP, and upper bounds provided by explicit mechanisms. The claim that simple noise addition to X is suboptimal is supported by deconvolution theory. Completeness would be improved by including full proofs and complexity analysis in the submission or supplement.
- Clarity: The paper is generally well organized, with clear problem setup, definitions, and statements of main theorems and propositions. Some mechanisms (notably the orthogonal-series privatization) would benefit from more detailed explanations of privacy proofs, constants, and sampling procedures. Explicit statements of parameter regimes (e.g., α small vs. large) and discussion of constants in the bounds would aid readers.
- Originality: Combining local differential privacy with minimax decision theory to obtain sharp population-risk rates, and proving optimality of RR for multinomial estimation under local DP, are novel contributions. The identification of a different exponent for nonparametric density estimation under local DP and the design of matching mechanisms appear new relative to prior DP literature focused on global DP or sample estimation.
- Significance: The results provide foundational guidance on the statistical cost of local privacy and inform mechanism design. The optimality of RR and the nonparametric rate shift are likely to influence subsequent work in private learning and estimation, especially in decentralized settings.

Questions and suggestions for improvement
- Can interaction in the local model improve the rates for multinomial or density estimation? A brief discussion or negative/positive results would strengthen scope.
- Provide full proofs (or detailed sketches) of privacy and unbiasedness for the orthogonal-series mechanism, including bounds on ck and an explicit, efficient sampling algorithm with complexity analysis.
- Discuss adaptivity to unknown β (e.g., private selection of k) and whether the optimal rate can be maintained up to logarithmic factors.
- Compare with global (central) DP rates for the same problems to contextualize the gap between privacy models.
- Clarify the dependence on α beyond α∈[0,1]; do rates transition smoothly for larger α?

Arguments pro acceptance
- Sharp, matching minimax bounds for important problems under local DP.
- Clear conceptual contributions (optimality of RR; exponent change in density estimation).
- Technically solid framework with broadly reusable tools.

Arguments contra acceptance
- Not fully self-contained; key proofs deferred.
- Limited discussion of computational efficiency for the series mechanism.
- No empirical validation or finite-sample evaluation.
- Focused on non-interactive local privacy; broader applicability not explored.

Recommendation: Accept, contingent on adding detailed proofs and algorithmic details in the final version or supplement.
