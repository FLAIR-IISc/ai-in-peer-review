- Bridges minimax decision theory and local differential privacy to establish sharp rates for probability estimation.  
- Multinomial result: effective sample size reduces from n to nα²/d under α-local privacy; Warner's randomized response is optimal.  
- Density result: convergence rate changes from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy, with algorithms achieving these rates.  
- Tight minimax lower bounds derived using Le Cam's two-point method and Fano's inequality, matched by constructive upper bounds.  
- Proofs are detailed, technically rigorous, and clearly connect information-theoretic bounds to statistical estimation.  
- Authors note limitations such as failure of Laplace perturbation for density estimation.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with consistent formalism and notation.  
- Effectively connects historical work (Warner's randomized response) to modern differential privacy theory.  
- Original contributions include the new theoretical framework and the novel density-estimation rate change under local privacy.  
- Methodological innovation: new sampling strategies enabling optimal rates where standard approaches fail.  
- Significant practical and theoretical implications, especially for survey design and private nonparametric statistics.  
- Strengths highlighted: tight bounds, optimality results, novel insights, efficient algorithms, and foundational connections.  
- Main weakness: exclusive focus on the local privacy model; could discuss extensions to the central model.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance as a significant, well-executed contribution to privacy-preserving statistics.
