- Decision-theoretic foundation for estimation under local differential privacy (LDP); defines α-private minimax risk and derives sharp lower and matching upper bounds for canonical problems.  
- Multinomial estimation: MSE Θ(min{1, d/(nα^2)}); ℓ1-risk Θ(min{1, d/√(nα^2)}); effective sample size reduced to nα^2/d; modified randomized response attains bounds.  
- Density estimation over Sobolev Fβ on [0,1]: rate degrades from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}; privatized histogram (β=1) and orthogonal-series mechanism achieve optimal rates.  
- Lower bounds use information-contraction (KL and mutual information) for LDP channels combined with Le Cam/Fano reductions; upper bounds via explicit mechanisms matching bias-variance tradeoffs.  
- Positions work relative to randomized response, centralized DP, and prior private estimation literature; distinct focus on LDP and minimax population risk with sharp rates.  
- Shows naive Laplace perturbation is suboptimal for density estimation (deconvolution lower bounds).  
- Theoretical soundness: minimax framework and contraction lemmas are appropriate; rates are sharp and constructions carefully calibrated.  
- Completeness concern: many proof details deferred to prior work; main text lacks full proofs for new density lower bound and some variance calculations.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well organized; orthogonal-series encoder construction and calibration are insightful.  
- Minor clarity/typo issues: probable typo in Eq. (10) ([Z]j = −xj vs 1−xj); periodic/bounded-basis assumptions should be stated more prominently; summarize choice of k/constants for bias-variance tradeoff.  
- Originality: novel framing of LDP estimation in minimax decision theory; new nonparametric exponent change and optimality of modified randomized response are important contributions.  
- Significance: high — tight quantification of privacy-induced sample-complexity penalties and implementable mechanisms with practical implications.  
- Scope limitation: focuses on non-interactive (memoryless) LDP; lacks discussion on whether interaction can improve rates.  
- Practicality gap: no empirical validation; recommends simulations comparing proposed mechanisms to Laplace perturbation and standard randomized response.  
- Generality limitation: analysis restricted to 1D periodic Sobolev classes and L2 loss; extensions to higher dimensions, other smoothness classes, and other losses are not addressed.  
- Parameter selection issue: procedures depend on α and β (choice of k); no practical/ private methods (e.g., private cross-validation) discussed for tuning.  
- Technical question: multinomial ℓ1-rate d/√(nα^2) is much worse than non-private √(d/n); discussion on tightness under other losses or structured settings (e.g., sparsity) would be useful.  
- Pros: sharp minimax LDP bounds with matching efficient algorithms; conceptual unification of privacy and decision theory; practical relevance of randomized response optimality.  
- Cons: deferred proofs and limited self-contained technical depth, no experiments, limited discussion on adaptivity/higher dimensions/other losses, and some presentation typos.  
- Recommendation: strong accept; suggests adding a supplementary with full proofs and a small empirical study to further strengthen the submission.
