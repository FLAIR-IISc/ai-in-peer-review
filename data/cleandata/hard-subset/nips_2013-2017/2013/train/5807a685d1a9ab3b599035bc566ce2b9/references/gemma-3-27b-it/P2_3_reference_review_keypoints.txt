- Paper studies trade-offs between local differential privacy and statistical efficiency, deriving sharp minimax rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation with local privacy constraints.  
- Shows effective sample size reduction for multinomial estimation and modified convergence rates for density estimation, with matching upper bounds and achievability.  
- Demonstrates optimality of Warner’s randomized response and provides efficient algorithms to achieve upper bounds.  
- Strong theoretical foundation using divergence bounds and hypothesis testing, with clear, rigorous analysis.  
- Results are sharp and precisely quantify the privacy-utility trade-off.  
- Practical relevance: validates a classical randomized response method in the modern private estimation setting.  
- Clear connections to prior work (e.g., Duchi et al., Warner) and standard, well-defined notation.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, though some background is dense.  
- Density estimation section has unclear practical implications; perturbation-based estimators appear suboptimal.  
- Important rate for perturbation-based estimators ((nα^2)^{-2β/(β+3)}) is buried and needs emphasis to motivate orthogonal series approach.  
- Background material is dense and may be hard for readers unfamiliar with minimax theory and differential privacy.  
- Limited empirical evaluation; a small simulation study (e.g., showing effect of α on error) would strengthen the paper.  
- Missing discussion of related work, e.g., private density estimation via kernel density estimation (KDE).  
- Assessment: Quality—Very Strong; Clarity—Good; Originality—Strong; Significance—Strong.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous, insightful, sharp theoretical contributions that extend existing research and have practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: density results lack clear practical impact and background is dense for some readers.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggestions to add small-scale simulations and more intuitive background explanations.
