Summary
The paper studies statistical estimation under local (non-interactive) differential privacy. It combines minimax decision-theoretic tools with information-contraction inequalities tailored to locally private channels to derive sharp lower and upper bounds. Two settings are treated: (i) multinomial probability estimation over the simplex, where the effective sample size degrades from n to roughly n α^2 / d; (ii) nonparametric density estimation over Sobolev classes, where the convergence rate degrades from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} (non-private) to (n α^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy. The paper presents matching, computationally efficient mechanisms: a variant of randomized response for multinomials and histogram/orthogonal series estimators for densities, with carefully designed privatization to avoid the deconvolution bottleneck of additive noise.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on and extends lines of research on differential privacy, especially local DP (Evfimievski et al., Kasiviswanathan et al.) and information-theoretic lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar; Smith). It emphasizes population risk (not just sample quantities), and relates local DP to classical minimax lower-bounding tools (Le Cam/Fano; Yang & Barron; Tsybakov). The deconvolution limitation for Laplace noise (Carroll & Hall) is used to justify the need for different mechanisms.

Strengths
- Clear, compelling formulation of locally private minimax risk and sharp information bounds (KL and mutual information) tailored to local channels.
- Identification of optimal rates in both finite-dimensional multinomial and nonparametric Sobolev density estimation.
- Elegant and practically relevant mechanisms: an optimal variant of randomized response for multinomials, and a non-additive orthogonal-series mechanism for densities that avoids deconvolution barriers.
- Results are conceptually significant: they quantify the precise privacy–utility tradeoff in important models and show new exponents appear under local DP.

Weaknesses and concerns
Major issues:
- Anonymity and self-referencing: The submission explicitly cites a non-anonymized “our work [9]” with author names and includes an Acknowledgments section identifying authors and funding. This violates double-blind policy and NeurIPS submission guidelines.
- Reliance on an external “longer paper [9]” for proofs. Many central proofs are deferred; Proposition 1 (information bounds) and Theorem 2 lower bound are not proved here. NeurIPS expects a self-contained submission or a supplement with full proofs.
- Incorrect/implausible statements and typos in key theorems and mechanisms:
  - Theorem 1 (Eq. 8): The lower bound contains terms min{1, sqrt(n α^2), d/(n α^2)}. A minimax risk cannot increase with n; the √(n α^2) term is dimensionally wrong. The intended bound seems to be Θ(min{1, d/(n α^2)}).
  - Randomized response mechanism (Eq. 10): The text claims Z ∈ {0,1}^d but defines “[Z]_j = x_j with prob … and −x_j with prob …”, which would produce negative entries if x_j=1. The subsequent expectation calculation matches flipping to 1−x_j, not −x_j. This inconsistency needs correction.
  - Variance bound after Eq. (10): E[||Z − E Z||^2] < d/4 + 1 < d. For Bernoulli coordinates the variance sum is ≤ d/4; the +1 term is unexplained.
  - Definition 1 (Sobolev): the constraint uses j^{2β} ϕ_j^2 instead of θ_j^2; likely a typo (coefficients should be θ_j).
  - Proposition 2 bound: the additive term √α n^{-3/4} increases with α, which is counterintuitive for a privacy parameter; although α∈[0,1] mitigates this, the dependence and its provenance should be explained.
- Mechanism correctness/DP guarantees:
  - The α-LDP guarantee for the multinomial mechanism is asserted via a triangle inequality argument; given the earlier inconsistency in Eq. (10), this needs a careful, explicit proof for the full vector mechanism.
  - The orthogonal-series privatization (Sec. 4.3) is claimed α-LDP “by inspection.” A rigorous proof or a citation with constants is needed, especially as the mechanism samples from the hypercube conditioned on a random sign constraint and rescales B = O(B0 √k / α).
- Projection reference: The claim that simplex projection can be computed in linear time cites [3] (“O(n) algorithm for quadratic knapsack problems”), which is an inappropriate reference for simplex projection. Standard, correct references include Duchi et al. (2008) for l1-ball projection and later O(d) simplex projection methods (e.g., Condat 2016). Please correct.
- Presentation/clarity:
  - Several equations have typographical inconsistencies (notation for measures/σ-fields, spacing, brackets in Fano bound).
  - The mutual information variational bound (Eq. 7) should be contextualized with assumptions ensuring bounded sup over S and the role of mixture P^i.
  - The histogram construction uses θ ∈ kΔ_k and projection Π_k onto kΔ_k; while correct, the choice of scaling and its bias/variance decomposition could be more explicit.

Formatting and references
- Numerous incomplete or malformed citations (missing years/venues/pages), misspellings (“Fourty”), placeholders with commas only, and inconsistent formatting. This is below NeurIPS standards and must be fixed.
- Acknowledgments must be removed for anonymity.

Pros for acceptance
- Important and timely problem; clear conceptual contribution linking local DP and minimax theory.
- Sharp, apparently optimal rates with constructive mechanisms.
- Likely to be influential and citable in both ML and statistics/DP communities.

Cons for acceptance
- Violates anonymity; relies on a non-anonymized prior with proofs; submission not self-contained.
- Multiple technical typos in key statements/mechanisms (some affecting correctness/interpretability).
- Missing or incorrect references and insufficient rigor in DP guarantees for proposed mechanisms.

Recommendation
Reject in current form due to anonymity violation, reliance on external non-anonymized manuscript for proofs, and several technical and presentation issues (including an apparent error in Theorem 1 and inconsistencies in the multinomial mechanism). The core ideas are strong and significant; I encourage resubmission with:
- Full, self-contained proofs (or detailed appendix).
- Corrected statements (Theorem 1, Eq. 10, Definition 1) and rigorous DP proofs for mechanisms.
- Proper references and polished formatting; remove acknowledgments for review. My confidence is high in the technical direction but low in the current submission quality.
