- Paper bridges minimax decision theory and local differential privacy, proving sharp convergence rates for probability and density estimation.  
- Shows Warner's randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation, resolving a longstanding question.  
- Derives new minimax rates for density estimation under local differential privacy, with a different exponent than classical results.  
- Theoretical rigor and novelty: sharp lower and upper bounds established for multinomial and density estimation.  
- Clear separation of privacy and statistical trade-offs, demonstrating privacy fundamentally alters convergence rates.  
- Practical relevance: connects classical randomized response with modern differential privacy, informing survey design.  
- Comprehensive treatment: provides both lower bounds and computable estimators (histogram and orthogonal series) that achieve the bounds.  
- Limited discussion of computational complexity and scalability (e.g., projection onto simplex for very large d).  
- Analysis restricted to α ∈ [0,1], not addressing behavior for larger α values (α > 1).  
- Paper is purely theoretical with no empirical or synthetic validation to illustrate practical trade-offs.  
- Ambiguity about whether density estimation rates depend on the chosen trigonometric basis or hold for other bases (e.g., wavelets).  
- Originality/significance: establishes fundamental price of local privacy and connects historical methods to modern theory.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong theoretical contribution despite noted limitations.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss high-dimensional computational costs, extend analysis to α > 1, clarify basis-dependence of Theorem 2, and comment on practical choices of α.
