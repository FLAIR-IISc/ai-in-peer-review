- Studies private minimax risk under local differential privacy (LDP) and derives sharp rates for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation.  
- For multinomial α-LDP (non-interactive), MSE scales as d/(n α^2) (ℓ1 rates match); effective sample size reduced to n α^2 / d; variant of randomized response attains the rate.  
- For Sobolev density estimation, non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades to (n α^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP; privatized histograms and orthogonal series achieve matching upper bounds.  
- Shows naive Laplace/noise-addition on raw samples is suboptimal for density estimation (deconvolution limits).  
- Builds on decision-theoretic minimax framework and the authors’ prior LDP information bounds, connecting to randomized response and deconvolution literature.  
- Lower bounds use Le Cam/Fano reductions with LDP-constrained information bounds; upper bounds are constructive and computationally efficient.  
- Matching rates for both problems strongly support the paper’s claims; key negative result is convincingly argued and cited.  
- Some proofs are deferred to a longer version; including key proofs or full supplement details would improve self-containment.  
- Focuses on non-interactive LDP and α ∈ [0,1]; authors should clarify whether interactivity affects rates (likely not for multinomial).  
- Minor technical issues: probable typo in Eq. (8) (term ∝ sqrt(n α^2)), randomized response sampling rule likely uses “1 − x_j” not “− x_j.”  
- Histogram LDP guarantee (Sec. 4.2) needs explicit calibration argument for Laplace noise to ensure per-sample α-LDP.  
- Generally well written, organized, and precise; definitions, setup, and results are clear and parameters/tuning are stated explicitly.  
- Recommend summarizing dependence on (n, d, α, β) for all cases (ℓ2/ℓ1, β=1, β>1) in one place for quick reference.  
- Original and substantial: first sharp private minimax rates under local DP for these tasks with matching achievability and new mechanism insights.  
- Connects classical randomized response to modern LDP optimality and shows qualitative change in nonparametric rate exponents under LDP.  
- Significant for practice: quantifies statistical cost of LDP, identifies optimal/simple mechanisms, and guides mechanism design for learning/statistics.  
- Pros: sharp matching bounds, clear mechanisms, unification of minimax theory and LDP, and strong literature connections.  
- Cons: deferred proofs, minor typos/notation bugs, limited discussion of interactivity and adaptivity (unknown β), and some abstractly referenced constants hindering reproducibility.  
- Requests: clarify interactivity’s effect, provide private adaptive selection of k for unknown β, correct Eq. (8) and randomized response rule, and include proof sketches or supplement pointers for key bounds and α-LDP calibration.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept, pending minor clarifications and improved self-containment.
