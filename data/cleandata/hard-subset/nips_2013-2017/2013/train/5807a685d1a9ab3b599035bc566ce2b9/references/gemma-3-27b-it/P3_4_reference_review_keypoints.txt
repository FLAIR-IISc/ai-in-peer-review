- Strong theoretical contribution: provides sharp minimax rates for probability estimation under α-local differential privacy.  
- Bridges fields: connects differential privacy, minimax estimation, and classical statistical inference.  
- Establishes matching lower and upper bounds for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation under local DP.  
- Quantifies the statistical penalty imposed by privacy in terms of sample size and convergence rate.  
- Reaffirms the optimality of Warner’s randomized response in this formal framework.  
- The mathematical analysis is rigorous with solid proofs and use of minimax theory.  
- Derived minimax rates are sharp, showing tight upper and lower bounds.  
- Clear motivation and strong connection to classical work and prior differential privacy literature.  
- Practical implications: gives concrete guidance for privacy-preserving estimators (e.g., randomized response, histogram estimator).  
- Well-written and organized manuscript with consistent notation and a clear introduction.  
- Limited empirical evaluation; simulations comparing estimators across privacy levels and non-private benchmarks are lacking.  
- Density estimation section is less polished and needs clearer intuition on basis choice and parameter B.  
- Real-world constraints (high dimensionality, non-i.i.d. data, implementation issues) are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Heavy reliance on a separate paper ([9]) for core technical results reduces self-containedness.  
- Specific clarifications requested: expand on likelihood-ratio comment, clarify the “min” in Theorem 1, and specify the orthonormal basis in Proposition 3.  
- Suggest discussing why Dwork et al.’s perturbation approach is not optimal in this setting and giving intuition for why privacy changes classical density estimation rates.  
- For acceptance: significant theoretical advance, sharp results, well-motivated and timely.  
- Against acceptance: lack of empirical validation, less polished density section, and dependence on [9] for core proofs.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add simulations, provide more intuition on density results, and include key lemmas/proofs from [9] to improve self-containedness.
