- Paper formulates a minimax decision-theoretic framework for estimation under α-local differential privacy (LDP) and studies population parameter estimation from privatized samples.

- Main results: multinomial estimation MSE scales as min{1, d/(nα2)} and ℓ1 error as min{1, d/√(nα2)}, achieved by tuned randomized response and Laplace-on-one-hot schemes.

- Main results: Sobolev density estimation rate degrades from n−2β/(2β+1) to (nα2)−2β/(2β+2) under LDP, with Laplace perturbation shown suboptimal.

- Proposes two optimal mechanisms for density estimation: a privatized histogram for β=1 and a locally private orthogonal-series estimator that attains the lower bound for all β≥1.

- Develops information-contraction bounds and adapts Le Cam/Fano-style minimax lower bound techniques to the LDP setting.

- Distinguishes clearly between local vs global DP and population vs sample estimation; connects deconvolution limits to explain suboptimality of naive perturbation.

- Strength: sharp matching upper and lower bounds giving interpretable dependence on n, α, d, β.

- Strength: conceptual contribution quantifying the price of LDP, revealing new polynomial rates in nonparametric problems.

- Strength: mechanism design identifies and implements computationally efficient, unbiased α-LDP randomization schemes that are rate-optimal.

- Weakness: many key proofs (density lower bound, orthogonal-series mechanism) deferred to the long version—needs more proof sketches for self-containment.

- Weakness: limited practical guidance on data-driven tuning (choice of k, handling unknown β) and robustness of procedures under LDP.

- Weakness: mechanism sampling strategy (19) using rejection/constrained sampling over {−B,B}k needs clarification of complexity, constants, and an explicit sampling algorithm.

- Weakness: paper restricts to non-interactive channels; discussion on whether interaction could improve rates is missing and would be valuable.

- Weakness: bounds presented up to constants for α∈[0,1]; paper should clarify regimes where min terms are active and behavior for larger α (α→∞).

- Quality and clarity: technically sound and well-organized, but minor notation/typo issues (Definition 1 θ/ϕ typo, variance bound explanation) and could add intuition for orthogonal-series privatization.

- Originality and significance: high—novel sharp LDP minimax rates and a new local orthogonal-series mechanism with strong implications for private analytics.

- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, matching constructions, and clear framing within privacy literature.

- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on the long version for proofs, under-explained implementation/tuning details, and limited scope to non-interactive LDP.

- Minor comments: fix typos (Def.1), clarify equation formatting around (10), and cite deconvolution rate results more precisely; add appendix with proof sketches.

- Recommendation: Accept.
