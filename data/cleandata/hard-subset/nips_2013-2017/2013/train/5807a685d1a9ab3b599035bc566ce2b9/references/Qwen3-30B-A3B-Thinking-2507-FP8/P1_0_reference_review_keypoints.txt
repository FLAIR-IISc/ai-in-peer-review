- Bridges classical privacy concepts with differential privacy and minimax decision theory, providing sharp theoretical bounds for privacy-constrained estimation.
- Establishes minimax rates for multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation under local differential privacy.
- Multinomial result: effective sample size reduces from n to nα²/d under α-local DP, and Warner's randomized response is optimal.
- Density result: classical rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} changes to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)} for β-smooth densities, with algorithms achieving these rates.
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Duchi et al. 2013) but delivers sharp minimax rates rather than only upper bounds.
- Technical rigor: carefully derived lower bounds using information-theoretic tools and Le Cam’s method, plus matching upper bounds and efficient algorithms.
- Originality: connects Warner’s 1965 randomized response to modern DP and introduces a new theoretical framework for privacy-utility trade-offs.
- Significance: provides foundational, quantitative insights for survey design, data collection, and privacy-preserving machine learning.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, self-contained, with clear notation and helpful examples illustrating results.
- Weakness: restricted to the non-interactive (memoryless) setting; interactive mechanisms not analyzed.
- Weakness: lacks empirical validation or numerical experiments to illustrate theoretical rates.
- Weakness: computational complexity of proposed algorithms (O(d) projection, O(k) steps) not thoroughly analyzed for high-dimensional settings.
- Weakness: does not consider heterogeneous or data-dependent privacy levels (varying α across data points).
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, and significant theoretical contribution.
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS.
