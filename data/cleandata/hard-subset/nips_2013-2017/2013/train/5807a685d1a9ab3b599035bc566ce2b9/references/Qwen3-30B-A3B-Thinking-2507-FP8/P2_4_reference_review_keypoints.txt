- Provides sharp minimax rates for probability estimation under α-local differential privacy, linking minimax decision theory with differential privacy.  
- Shows multinomial estimation effective sample size degrades from n to nα²/d under α-local privacy.  
- Establishes density estimation rate for β-smooth densities as (α²n)^(-2β/(2β+2)), differing from classical n^(-2β/(2β+1)).  
- Proves minimax lower bounds and gives matching upper bounds with computationally efficient algorithms.  
- Revisits and proves Warner’s randomized response is optimal for privatized multinomial estimation.  
- Uses rigorous information-theoretic tools (KL divergence, mutual information) for lower bounds.  
- Strong technical quality with well-structured proofs and appropriate theoretical machinery.  
- Original contribution by connecting minimax estimation and differential privacy and revealing new fundamental rates.  
- Significant practical implications quantifying the privacy–utility trade-off and required sample-size increases.  
- Clear writing, consistent notation, and logically organized presentation; main results precisely stated.  
- Some proofs are deferred and rely on a longer paper (Duchi et al. [9]) limiting self-containment.  
- Purely theoretical work lacking experimental validation or simulations to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Focuses exclusively on local differential privacy without discussing extensions to central or other privacy models.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution with broad interest in privacy and statistical theory.
