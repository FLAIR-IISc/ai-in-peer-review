Summary
The paper studies parameter estimation under local (non-interactive) differential privacy constraints using a minimax decision-theoretic framework. The authors formalize local privacy via α-local DP channels and define an α-private minimax risk. They derive lower bounds by reducing estimation to multi-hypothesis testing and controlling KL/mutual information after private channels, invoking Le Cam and Fano. Key technical ingredients are information-contraction bounds under locally private channels (KL and a variational mutual information bound), restated from [9].

Two main application domains are treated:

- Multinomial estimation: For θ ∈ Δd, they show the effective sample size degrades from n to nα^2/d. Specifically, the mean-squared error minimax rate scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)} up to constants (with a matching upper bound), and the ℓ1 risk scales as min{1, d/√(nα^2)}. They further show that a version of Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal and provide a simple estimator (projection onto the simplex) achieving the upper bound. They also note Laplace perturbation of the one-hot vector achieves the same order.

- Nonparametric density estimation: For Sobolev classes Fβ on [0,1] (β≥1), the non-private minimax L2 risk is n^{-2β/(2β+1)}. Under α-local DP, they prove a lower bound of order (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}, i.e., the exponent degrades from 2β/(2β+1) to 2β/(2β+2). They provide matching upper bounds via (i) privatized histograms for β=1 and (ii) a new locally private orthogonal series mechanism for β≥1 that randomizes bounded basis coefficient vectors in a way that preserves unbiasedness and achieves the lower bound. They argue that simple Laplace perturbation of samples or coefficients is suboptimal (consistent with deconvolution lower bounds, e.g., Carroll & Hall).

Relation to prior work: The paper connects classical randomized response (Warner), information-theoretic minimax tools (Le Cam, Fano, Tsybakov, Yang & Barron), and differential privacy (Dwork et al.). The contraction bounds enabling private minimax lower bounds are from the authors’ prior work [9]. The density results relate to private estimation frameworks (Wasserman & Zhou; Lei) but improve rates under local privacy by designing mechanisms beyond naive Laplace noise.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The framework and reductions are standard and appropriate. The lower bounds rely on clear information bounds (KL and mutual information), and the mechanisms proposed for achievability are plausible and efficiently implementable in principle. The multinomial upper bound derivation using randomized response is convincing. For density estimation, the privatized histogram analysis for β=1 and orthogonal series estimator for β≥1 are in line with classical bias-variance tradeoffs adapted to the variance induced by the locally private channel.

- Completeness: Many proofs are deferred to [9]; the paper restates key lemmas but leaves important privacy and variance calculations (e.g., for the orthogonal series mechanism) at the level of “by inspection.” Providing at least proof sketches for Theorems 1–2 and a concrete bound on acceptance probabilities/variance constants in the sampling strategy (19) would improve self-containment and reproducibility.

- Potential technical issues to clarify:
  - Equation (8) includes a term √(nα^2) inside a min for an ℓ2-squared risk lower bound; dimensionally and behaviorally this seems odd and may be a typo. The natural form is c·min{1, d/(nα^2)} (as the upper bound shows).
  - For the orthogonal series mechanism, the existence and magnitude of ck and the choice B = Θ(B0√k/α) should be accompanied by explicit bounds on variance, acceptance probability in rejection sampling, and runtime as functions of k and α.
  - Proposition 2 includes a term √α n^{-3/4}; its origin and whether it can be removed/tightened should be explained.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written, with clear setup, notation, and motivation. The link between DP and minimax is well articulated. The presentation of histogram/series estimators is accessible. Suggestions:
- Provide intuitive explanation for the variational mutual information bound (7) and guidance on choosing the set S.
- Explicitly discuss the range of α (small-α regime) underlying the approximations e^α−1 ≈ α.
- Standardize references (several entries lack years/details, e.g., [17], [20]; “Fourty” should be “Forty”).
- Clarify whether the series estimator enforces nonnegativity/integration constraints (it optimizes L2 risk, but may not be a bona fide density).

Originality
The paper’s core contribution is the unification of local DP with minimax theory and derivation of sharp rates and mechanisms under local privacy for both finite-dimensional and nonparametric problems. The multinomial rates and optimality of randomized response, as well as the density estimation rates and locally private orthogonal series mechanism, appear novel relative to prior DP works focusing on central DP or naive perturbations. The results, while overlapping with the authors’ arXiv tech report [9], represent substantial original contributions at the time, provided [9] has not appeared in a peer-reviewed venue with the same content.

Significance
The results sharpen our understanding of the cost of local privacy: sample size penalties in parametric models and altered exponents in nonparametric settings. Showing classical randomized response is optimal is impactful for practice. The orthogonal series mechanism illustrates that privacy mechanism design is as crucial as estimator choice. The work is likely to influence subsequent research on private learning/statistics and is well within NeurIPS scope.

Pros
- Clear, principled framework combining DP and minimax theory.
- Sharp lower bounds with matching upper bounds in both multinomial and Sobolev density settings.
- Identifies optimal mechanisms (randomized response; tailored series mechanism) and shows suboptimality of naive Laplace noise.
- Broad relevance to privacy-preserving data analysis.

Cons
- Heavy reliance on an external tech report for proofs; limited proof detail in the main text.
- Minor potential typo/inconsistency in Theorem 1 (Eq. 8).
- Orthogonal series mechanism needs more explicit analysis of constants, acceptance probability, and computational complexity.
- No empirical validation or discussion of constants/practicality; references formatting needs attention.

Recommendation
Accept. The paper provides technically sound, conceptually clean, and significant advances on an important problem, with sharp rates and constructive mechanisms. Minor revisions to fix typos, bolster self-containment with proof sketches, and clarify the orthogonal series sampling details would further strengthen the submission.
