- Paper studies minimax estimation under α-local differential privacy (LDP) for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation, providing sharp lower and matching upper bounds.  
- Multinomial result: MSE degrades from d/n to d/(n α^2); randomized response and Laplace-perturbation plus projection achieve this rate.  
- Density result: For Sobolev smoothness β, rate changes from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}, with optimal privatized histogram (β=1) and orthogonal-series estimators (β≥1).  
- Lower bounds use mutual-information/contraction inequalities tailored to local channels; upper bounds give explicit, computationally efficient private mechanisms.  
- Connects classical randomized response to modern DP formalism and relates density results to classical nonparametrics and deconvolution barriers.  
- Technical core is strong; the change in exponent for density estimation is both technically and conceptually interesting.  
- Orthogonal-series mechanism is carefully constructed to satisfy α-LDP and preserve unbiasedness up to scaling, with well-motivated parameter choices.  
- Issues to correct: Theorem 1 contains a typo (√(n α^2) term likely wrong); intended multinomial rate is d/(n α^2).  
- Issues to correct: Randomized response equation uses “−xj” and conflicts with Bernoulli claims; should clarify bit-flip and privacy ratio assumptions.  
- Issues to correct: Sobolev class definition has a notational error—the constraint should be on coefficients θ_j, not basis functions φ_j.  
- Paper over-relies on external arXiv [9] for proofs; include proof sketches for main theorems and key propositions for self-containment.  
- Provide brief derivation or citation for the claim that constant c_k in Eq. (20) is O(1).  
- Improve clarity by fixing typos/notation and discussing sampling efficiency and computational cost of the halfspace-uniform/vector privatization mechanism.  
- Originality: Novel instantiation of divergence bounds to concrete LDP problems with tight rates and optimal mechanisms; should clarify what is new versus [9].  
- Significance: Results provide important benchmarks for privacy-utility tradeoffs in the local model and show naive additive noise can be suboptimal.  
- Pros: Sharp matching bounds, clear identification of effective sample-size degradation, and implementable mechanisms.  
- Cons: Technical typos, limited in-paper proofs, no empirical illustrations, and related work could better contrast local-model/SQ connections.  
- Suggestions: Fix typos, add proof sketches for Theorems 1–2 and key propositions, clarify novelty relative to [9], discuss computational aspects, and optionally include a small simulation.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — significant theoretical contributions but needs improved clarity and self-containment.
