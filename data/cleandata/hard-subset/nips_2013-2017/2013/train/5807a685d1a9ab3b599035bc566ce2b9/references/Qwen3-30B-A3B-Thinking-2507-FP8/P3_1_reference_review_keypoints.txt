- Main contribution: Characterizes minimax trade-offs between local differential privacy and statistical efficiency for multinomial and density estimation, giving sharp rates.
- Multinomial result: Effective sample size reduces from n to nα²/d; Warner's randomized response is minimax-optimal.
- Density result: For β-smooth densities, rate is (nα²)^(-2β/(2β+2)), differing from the classical n^(-2β/(2β+1)).
- Strength — theoretical rigor: Provides tight lower and upper minimax bounds using Le Cam and information-theoretic methods.
- Strength — historical insight: Connects modern local differential privacy optimality to Warner’s 1965 randomized response.
- Strength — novel density insight: Demonstrates a fundamental finite- vs infinite-dimensional difference under privacy constraints.
- Strength — practical algorithms: Presents histogram and orthogonal-series estimators that achieve the lower bounds and are computationally feasible.
- Weakness — practical discussion: Lacks concrete interpretation of α in real applications and comparison of computational costs to non-private methods.
- Weakness — Laplace explanation: Claim that Laplace perturbation is suboptimal for density estimation is under-explained; needs clearer link to characteristic function decay.
- Weakness — privacy model scope: Focuses only on α-local differential privacy without discussing extensions to other privacy definitions.
- Weakness — literature positioning: Does not fully compare or position results against more recent private density-estimation work (e.g., Lei 2011, Chaudhuri & Hsu 2012).
- Critical: The minimax analysis and proofs are technically sound and clearly presented.
- Critical: The paper’s impact would increase with clearer practical implications (e.g., what α=0.1 means for estimate quality in applications).
- Critical: Needs stronger justification for why orthogonal-series estimators are necessary and why Laplace-based estimators cannot attain the rate.
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, sharp theoretical contribution relevant to NIPS.
- Pro acceptance: Sharp minimax rates, optimality of randomized response, highlights finite/infinite-dimensional privacy differences, and provides matching computable estimators.
- Con acceptance: Limited practical guidance, incomplete positioning against recent related work, and insufficient explanation of Laplace perturbation failure.
