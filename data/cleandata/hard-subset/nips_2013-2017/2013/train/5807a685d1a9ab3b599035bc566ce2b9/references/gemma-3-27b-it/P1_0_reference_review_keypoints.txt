- Summary: Provides tight minimax lower and upper bounds for multinomial and density estimation under α-local differential privacy, showing sample-complexity penalties (n → nα²/d) and modified density rates, and proving Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal with efficient algorithms.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on Duchi et al. (2013) and classical minimax theory (e.g., Tsybakov), connecting differential privacy, information-theoretic bounds, and Warner’s randomized response.

- Strength — Rigorous theoretical framework: Uses differential privacy and minimax decision theory to analyze privacy-utility trade-offs formally.

- Strength — Sharp bounds: Derives tight minimax rates that clarify fundamental limits of private probability estimation.

- Strength — Practical implications: Shows a classical randomized response mechanism is optimal and provides efficiently implementable algorithms.

- Strength — Novel combination of techniques: Integrates differential privacy, minimax theory, and information-theoretic methods in a new way.

- Strength — Clear exposition: Generally well written and explains complex theoretical concepts clearly (with some assumed background).

- Strength — Important problem: Addresses a foundational and timely question about the costs of privacy.

- Weakness — Limited density-estimation scope: Focus on smoothness β and the trigonometric basis may limit broader applicability.

- Weakness — Practicality of histogram estimator: Optimal histogram may require many bins (k = (nα²)^(1/(2β+2))), causing issues in high dimensions or low-data regimes.

- Weakness — Basis-function assumptions: Orthogonal series estimation assumes bounded basis functions, which may be restrictive.

- Weakness — No empirical validation: Lacks simulations or experiments to demonstrate algorithm performance and validate theoretical bounds.

- Weakness — Notational density: Heavy notation can make derivations occasionally hard to follow.

- Arguments pro acceptance: Fundamental contribution, valuable sharp bounds, significant optimality result for Warner’s method, and overall clear presentation.

- Arguments con acceptance: Limited scope of density results, practical concerns about estimators, and absence of empirical validation.

- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical paper that substantially advances understanding of private probability estimation; recommended for acceptance.

- Confidence & recommendation: Reviewer confidence 8/10; recommendation — Accept with minor revisions (discuss assumption limitations and add simulations).
