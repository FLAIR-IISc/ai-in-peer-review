- Main result (multinomial): Minimax ℓ2^2 and ℓ1 risks scale as d/(nα^2) and d/√(nα^2) respectively, showing effective sample size n → nα^2/d.
- Main result (density): For Sobolev class Fβ, L2 risk changes from n−2β/(2β+1) to (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2), i.e., privacy alters the polynomial exponent.
- Achievability: Practical mechanisms (randomized response variants, privatized histogram, bounded sign-based series privatization) match lower bounds.
- Methodology: Uses LDP-adapted information-theoretic KL and mutual information bounds plus Le Cam/Fano reductions to derive lower bounds.
- Novel technical tool: A mutual-information variational bound for LDP (from authors’ prior work) enables the reductions.
- Relation to prior work: Connects and shows optimality of randomized response for multinomials and complements central-DP and classical minimax literature.
- Quality — soundness: Lower bounds and achievability are technically convincing; density mechanism argued to be optimal.
- Quality — concern: Possible typo in Equation (8) with an unexpected sqrt(nα^2) term; needs clarification.
- Completeness: Many proofs are deferred to a technical report [9], reducing self-containment; brief in-paper proof sketches would help.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized, though small notational glitches and missing specifics (constants ck, B setting) should be fixed.
- Intuition gap: A short, clear explanation of why privacy “adds one” to the smoothness denominator would improve accessibility.
- Originality: Establishes sharp minimax LDP rates and optimal channels for both finite- and infinite-dimensional problems; combination of bounds and channel design is novel.
- Novelty/overlap concern: The paper should explicitly delineate which results are new versus restated from the authors’ technical report [9].
- Significance: Provides fundamental guidance on the privacy-utility tradeoff under LDP and yields practically implementable optimal mechanisms.
- Pros: Sharp matching bounds, clear effective-sample-size interpretation, simple efficient mechanisms, and reusable information bounds.
- Cons: Heavy reliance on external report for proofs; restricted to non-interactive channels; no empirical validation; orthogonal-series estimator assumes known β (no adaptivity).
- Suggested clarifications: State novelty relative to [9], fix/explain (8), clarify constants and B, and add brief lower-bound proof sketches.
- Suggested extensions/discussion: Address adaptivity to unknown β, potential gains from interactive channels, and provide intuition on deconvolution/loss of exponent.
- Recommendation: Strong accept.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
