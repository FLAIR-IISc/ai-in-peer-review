- Paper studies minimax estimation under non-interactive local differential privacy (LDP), deriving lower bounds via Le Cam/Fano plus LDP-specific information-contraction and matching simple privatization schemes for upper bounds.  
- Multinomial estimation: minimax MSE scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)} (ℓ1-risk min{1, d/√(nα^2)}), implying effective sample-size reduction n → nα^2/d; a variant of randomized response (and Laplace one-hot) attains optimal rate.  
- Density estimation on Sobolev class F_β: non-private rate n−2β/(2β+1) degrades under LDP to (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2); histogram (β=1) and orthogonal-series with bounded private basis coefficients (β≥1) achieve the optimal private rates.  
- Directly adding Laplace noise to raw Xi cannot achieve optimal nonparametric rates due to deconvolution limits.  
- Builds on prior DP and minimax literature and introduces LDP-specific KL/mutual-information contraction results (Proposition 1) linking LDP to decision-theoretic lower bounds.  
- Technical soundness: lower bounds use standard reductions with tight LDP contractions; upper bounds provide explicit mechanisms and variance control; change in polynomial exponent is well-justified.  
- Completeness: many proofs deferred to the long version but key lemmas are stated; mechanisms are implementable (randomized response, noisy histogram, series privatization with rejection sampling).  
- Correctness caveats: series mechanism uses constant c_k and bounded basis B0 with B=Θ(√k/α); privacy claim for mechanism (19) is asserted “by inspection” and would benefit from a short formal proof.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well-structured; exposition of minimax framework and information bounds is crisp and transparent.  
- Suggested clarifications: provide a brief proof that (19) is α-LDP and compute E[Z|X]; clarify role and bounds on c_k; state computational complexity of series mechanism explicitly; tidy variance bound for Z and detail the projection step.  
- Originality: combining sharp LDP information contraction with minimax lower bounds and identifying exact rate changes under LDP for multinomial and Sobolev estimation is original and impactful.  
- Significance: provides quantitative privacy-utility trade-offs under LDP with simple, deployable mechanisms and fundamental insights (effective sample-size reduction and exponent change) likely to influence future work.  
- Pros: sharp matching bounds, clean conceptual message, practical mechanisms, strong ties to information theory and classical statistics.  
- Cons/weaknesses: reliance on long version for proofs; restriction to non-interactive mechanisms without discussion of interaction benefits; no empirical results; adaptivity to unknown β not addressed under privacy.  
- Questions/suggestions for authors: clarify whether interaction helps for rates; give explicit α-LDP proof and quantify c_k for orthogonal series; discuss extensions to other losses (L1/Hellinger); consider communication-efficient report schemes; discuss private adaptivity/model selection.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — high-quality theoretical contribution with precise results, practical mechanisms, and strong relevance to the NIPS community.
