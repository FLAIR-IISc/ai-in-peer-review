Summary
The paper studies statistical estimation under α-local differential privacy (LDP) using a minimax decision-theoretic lens. It establishes lower bounds on private minimax risk via information-contraction inequalities and matches them with computationally efficient mechanisms. Two main application domains are treated: (i) multinomial probability estimation over the simplex, where the private MSE scales like d/(nα^2), implying an effective sample size reduction n → nα^2/d and showing randomized response is optimal; (ii) nonparametric density estimation over Sobolev classes Fβ on [0,1], where LDP changes the minimax rate from n−2β/(2β+1) to (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2), with histogram (β=1) and orthogonal series mechanisms achieving the bounds.

Relation to prior work
The technical backbone (divergence and mutual information bounds under LDP) is imported from the authors’ prior work [9], and classical minimax tools (Le Cam, Fano, Assouad) are standard [22,25,26]. The paper positions itself against classical DP mechanism analyses that target sample statistics rather than population risk, and against deconvolution limits (Carroll–Hall [4]). It also connects to randomized response (Warner [23]) and private ERM/geometry of DP literature. The positioning is appropriate, but the degree of novelty over [9] is unclear because some core results and proofs are deferred there.

Strengths
- Clear identification of private minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation, with matching upper bounds.
- Conceptually insightful: LDP can change the convergence exponent in nonparametrics, not just induce a constant-factor sample loss.
- Mechanism design is constructive: a simple RR-like channel for multinomials and a bounded orthogonal-series privatization scheme for density estimation.
- Discussion links privacy-utility tradeoffs to classical decision theory in a clean way.

Weaknesses and methodological concerns
- Dependence on [9] for key proofs: The information bounds (Prop. 1) and the lower bound machinery are central, yet multiple main proofs, including Theorem 2, are said to be in [9]. For a NeurIPS paper, the current submission is not self-contained enough to assess technical soundness fully.
- Potential error in Theorem 1 lower bound (Eq. 8): The lower bound includes min{1, sqrt(nα^2), d/(nα^2)} for an ℓ2-squared risk, which is dimensionally and conceptually odd (risk decreasing in sqrt(nα^2)). I suspect a typo; the standard scaling should be min{1, d/(nα^2)} up to constants. This needs correction/clarification.
- Ambiguity/typo in randomized response mechanism (Eq. 10): “with probability … − xj with probability …” appears to intend “1 − xj” (complement), not “−xj” (which would yield −1). This is important for correctness and for the claim Z ∈ {0,1}d.
- Estimator formula (Eq. 11): θ̂part uses (Zi − 1/(1+eα/2)) times a scalar factor. This seems to be missing an explicit vector of ones; otherwise there is a dimensional inconsistency. Please write (Zi − (1/(1+eα/2))·1)×((eα/2+1)/(eα/2−1)).
- Projection citation: The claim that projection onto the simplex can be done in linear time cites Brucker (quadratic knapsack). The standard projection reference is not Brucker; O(d log d) (sorting-based) or linear-time selection-based projections exist but should be cited appropriately (e.g., Condat 2016; Duchi et al. 2008 for ℓ1-ball/simplex).
- Privacy proof details: For per-coordinate perturbations of one-hot vectors with Laplace(α/2) noise, the paper should explicitly show the likelihood ratio bound with respect to the L1 sensitivity (which is 2 here), to avoid confusion with central DP calibrations and to clarify why the scale corresponds to α/2 in their Laplace parametrization.
- Orthogonal-series mechanism (Sec. 4.3): The definition of ck and the computation of B and the constant factors for unbiasedness are opaque. The paper asserts ck=O(1), bounded away from 0, but does not quantify or prove this within the submission; the computational feasibility of sampling uniformly from a half-hypercube by rejection for large k is also not discussed (expected number of trials, fallback algorithms).
- Histogram result (Prop. 2): The bound has two terms, 5(α^2n)−1/2 and √α n−3/4; the second term’s scaling is unusual and not motivated. A standard bias-variance decomposition under Lipschitz smoothness would typically yield a single dominant term after optimizing k; this needs a clearer derivation or better presentation.
- Claims about Laplace suboptimality: The negative result on Laplace perturbation of Xi via deconvolution is persuasive, but the paper should more precisely distinguish “adding Laplace to raw sample Xi” from “adding Laplace to per-sample one-hot features ek(Xi) and averaging,” since both are additive but live in different spaces. As written, this could confuse readers.
- Scope restrictions: The paper focuses on non-interactive (memoryless) channels; it would help to clarify whether interaction could improve rates (even qualitatively) or whether the lower bounds extend to interactive LDP protocols.
- Notational and presentation issues:
  - Overloading X as sample space and random variable; later the partition sets are also denoted Xj; consider disambiguating.
  - Several small grammar issues (e.g., “when are kept differentially private”).
  - Inconsistent boldface and vector notation (1 vs 1).
  - Mutual information bound (Eq. 7): constants seem nonstandard; a brief derivation or clearer reference would help.

References and formatting
- Numerous entries are incomplete (missing years, venues, page numbers) and several in-text citations do not correspond to entries (e.g., repeated use of [26] though the list ends at 25; “[17]” is referenced but not listed).
- Please normalize the bibliography to NeurIPS style and ensure all in-text numerals match entries. Provide full citations for key algorithms (simplex projection), and for lower bound tools (Le Cam, Fano, Assouad) as they are used.

Pros
- Important problem; insightful change in minimax exponents under LDP in nonparametrics.
- Matching lower/upper bounds and concrete mechanisms.
- Conceptually unifies DP and minimax risk.

Cons
- Heavy reliance on prior work [9] for core proofs; not self-contained.
- Multiple technical ambiguities/typos in key equations (8, 10, 11).
- Bibliographic and formatting issues pervasive.
- Some algorithmic details (constants, sampling complexity) are hand-wavy.

Questions for authors
1) Please correct Eq. (8) and clarify whether the sqrt(nα^2) term is a typo.
2) In Eq. (10), do you mean 1 − xj? Please fix and re-derive E[Z|x] explicitly.
3) Can you provide a precise bound and derivation for ck and the expected runtime of the sampler in Sec. 4.3?
4) Do your lower bounds extend to interactive LDP mechanisms? If not, can you comment on potential gaps?
5) Can you integrate key proofs (e.g., Theorem 2) into the paper, at least in an appendix?

Recommendation
Weak reject. While the results are significant and likely correct, the submission has too many technical and presentation issues, relies on another paper for core arguments, and contains ambiguous formulae in central mechanisms. With careful revision (self-contained proofs, corrected equations, complete references, and clarified algorithms), this could become a strong contribution.
