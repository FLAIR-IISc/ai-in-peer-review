Summary
The paper studies statistical estimation under local differential privacy (LDP). It combines minimax decision-theoretic tools with privacy channel constraints to (i) derive sharp lower bounds for α-LDP estimation, and (ii) construct matching upper bounds via efficient mechanisms. Two settings are analyzed:

- Multinomial estimation: For θ ∈ Δd, the minimax MSE under α-LDP scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)} (up to constants); ℓ1 error scales as min{1, d/√(nα^2)}. This implies an effective sample size reduction from n to nα^2/d. A variant of Warner’s randomized response is shown (claimed) to be optimal.

- Density estimation on [0,1]: For Sobolev smoothness β, the non-private L2 risk rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades under α-LDP to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}. The authors provide two mechanisms achieving the rate: privatized histograms for β=1, and a nontrivial orthogonal-series scheme (bounded basis, cleverly designed privatization) for general β, arguing Laplace perturbation of raw samples is suboptimal.

A key technical ingredient is an information-contraction bound under LDP (KL and MI bounds) to feed into Le Cam/Fano lower bounds.

Strengths
- Conceptually clean and important: rigorous minimax characterization under LDP for two canonical problems. The shift in density estimation exponent is notable.
- Strong lower-bound technique via information contraction tailored to LDP; the results appear broadly reusable.
- Constructive mechanisms with performance matching lower bounds (histogram for β=1; orthogonal series for β≥1) and discussion of why naive Laplace noise on samples is suboptimal.
- Good connection to classical randomized response and nonparametric estimation.

Weaknesses / issues (many are small but numerous; some affect correctness/clarity)
Technical/notation:
- Definition 1 (Sobolev class) appears incorrect: the constraint uses ∑ j^{2β} φ_j^2 rather than the coefficient sequence ∑ j^{2β} θ_j^2 ≤ C^2. This undermines the formal definition of the model class.
- Theorem 1 lower bound (8) includes a term √(nα^2) inside min{1, √(nα^2), d/(nα^2)} for an MSE risk, which is dimensionally and conceptually suspect (the term grows with n). Likely a typo intended as 1/√(nα^2) or it should be absent.
- Randomized response mechanism (10): “[Z]j = xj with prob … and −xj with prob …” conflicts with Z ∈ {0,1}^d; −xj ∈ {0,−1}. Later expectation uses (1 − x), suggesting the intended mapping was 1 − xj, not −xj. Needs correction and consistent notation (bold 1 vector vs scalar 1).
- Privacy proof after (10) says x,x′ ∈ Δd, but the data domain is {e1,…,ed}. The bound is true but the domain mismatch is confusing; please specify domain consistently.
- Estimator (11): missing bold 1 vector and parentheses; as written, dimensions are ambiguous. The projection claim should cite a standard simplex projection ref; citation [3] (Brucker, quadratic knapsack) is not the common reference (e.g., Duchi et al., 2008; or Wang & Carreira-Perpiñán, 2013).
- Proposition 2 bound 5(α^2 n)^{-1/2} + √α n^{-3/4}: the √α term is unusual for LDP rates depending on α^2; suggest re-deriving to eliminate looseness or justify the scaling.
- Section 4.2: The α-LDP claim for additive Laplace(α/2) noise per coordinate on a one-hot vector should be accompanied by a short proof or a clear sensitivity argument (and a comment on independence across coordinates and composition). Given only two coordinates differ between bins, the L1 sensitivity is 2; make this precise.
- Section 4.3 sampling scheme (19): “Uniform on {z ∈ {−B,B}^k : ⟨z, τ̃⟩ > 0}” needs a concrete, efficient sampler; rejection sampling is fine but please state expected trials (=2) and ensure uniformity. Also, the constant ck in (20) is left opaque; specify its value or tight bounds and how it depends on k.
- Reliance on external tech report [9] for essential proofs, rather than an on-paper appendix/supplement, reduces reproducibility for a NeurIPS submission.

Clarity/presentation:
- Numerous typos and formatting issues: “Fourty-Second,” inconsistent spacing (e.g., nα 2), missing boldface for vectors, inconsistent notation Πk vs ΠkΔk, switching between Fβ and Fβ[C] without clear convention.
- References are incomplete/inconsistent (missing years/volumes/pages, stray commas, placeholders), and some attributions are off (e.g., simplex projection).
- The paper says “we restate two theorems” but presents one proposition; label and cross-referencing inconsistencies recur.

Relation to prior work:
- The relationship to central DP optimality results (e.g., Smith; Hardt-Talwar; De) and to LDP learning (Kasiviswanathan et al.) is noted but could be sharpened: explicitly contrast contraction bounds here with privacy funnel results, and clarify what is genuinely new in the bounds and mechanisms beyond [9]/arXiv.
- Discussion of interactive LDP protocols is brief; either justify non-interactive focus via lower bounds or discuss potential gaps to interactive rates.

Quality
- The core ideas and main results are technically strong and significant. However, several presentation/notation errors (some affecting correctness of statements) and reliance on external proofs hinder verification. The multinomial upper/lower bounds and density rates align with known results, but precision and self-contained proofs need attention.

Clarity
- Currently below NeurIPS standards due to pervasive typos, ambiguous notation, and reference issues. The paper would benefit from rigorous proofreading and a self-contained supplement with proofs.

Originality
- High. The unified minimax framework under LDP with sharp rates and optimal mechanisms is impactful.

Significance
- High. Results will influence theory and practice in privacy-preserving statistics.

Pros
- Important problem with clear, sharp results.
- Novel information-contraction lemmas tailored to LDP.
- Optimal mechanisms for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation; insightful negative result for Laplace-perturbed samples.

Cons
- Several technical/notation errors (Definition 1, Theorem 1 term, mechanism (10)).
- Missing or opaque constants/derivations (ck, sampling efficiency).
- Incomplete references and presentation issues.
- Proofs deferred to external source rather than an appendix/supplement.

Recommendation
Weak reject. The contribution is significant, but the accumulation of technical typos/inconsistencies, missing details, and reference/presentation issues impedes verification. I would enthusiastically support acceptance after a careful revision that fixes the noted issues, clarifies mechanisms and proofs (in supplementary), and cleans up references and notation.
