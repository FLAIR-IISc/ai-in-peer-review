- Paper studies statistical estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), defines a private minimax risk, and develops KL/mutual-information tools to derive sharp lower bounds.

- Multinomial estimation: minimax MSE scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)} (and analogous ℓ1 bounds); randomized response (and Laplace-perturbed counts) attain the optimal rate.

- Density estimation on [0,1] over Sobolev classes Fβ: non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades under LDP to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}; lower bounds via packing/Fano + LDP information bounds; achieved by privatized histograms (β=1) and a private orthogonal-series estimator.

- Naively adding Laplace noise to raw samples is suboptimal for density estimation due to deconvolution limitations.

- Technical soundness: precise statements, appropriate techniques, and sharp matching upper/lower bounds; constructive and computationally efficient estimators.

- Completeness concern: proofs are deferred to a longer version; brief proof sketches for main results would improve self-containment.

- Mechanism correctness: multinomial privatization, projection estimator, and orthogonal-series privatization are correctly designed (unbiased and α-LDP with optimal variance scaling).

- Clarity: generally well written and organized; definitions of local privacy, private minimax risk, and information bounds are helpful.

- Notation issues to fix: ambiguous “− x_j” in (10); check the “+1” in the variance bound for Z; discuss regimes/transitions in Theorem 1; explicitly justify α-LDP for Z = e_k(X)+Lap(α/2) via ℓ1 sensitivity of 2.

- Reference formatting is inconsistent and should be standardized (years, venues, full titles).

- Originality: formalizes and sharpens known randomized-response intuition for multinomials; density-rate exponent change under LDP and tailored information inequalities are novel.

- Significance: quantifies sample-complexity penalty of local privacy and demonstrates a qualitative shift in nonparametric rates; practical focus on non-interactive channels.

- Strengths: sharp matching rates, clean implementable mechanisms, reusable LDP information bounds, and clear articulation of privacy–utility trade-offs.

- Weaknesses: omission of proofs in the paper, restriction to non-interactive protocols and 1D densities, lack of empirical validation, and minor notational errors.

- Suggested improvements: include concise proof sketches for Theorems 1–2 and Propositions 2–3, discuss interactivity and higher-dimensional extensions, add simple simulations comparing mechanisms, and fix notational/reference issues.

- Pros for acceptance: strong theoretical contribution with optimal mechanisms, relevance to ML/privacy communities, and clear practical takeaways (e.g., optimality of randomized response).

- Cons for acceptance: reliance on an external long version for proofs, limited discussion of interactivity and higher-dimensional cases, and absence of experiments.

- Recommendation: Accept (or strong weak accept) — a high-quality theoretical paper with significant contributions.

- Minor technical comments: clarify constants and α ranges where rates saturate, double-check the unusual dependence in Prop.2 (√(α n^{-3/4})), and state computational cost of the orthogonal-series privatization (e.g., expected trials for rejection sampling).
