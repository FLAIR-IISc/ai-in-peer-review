- Problem: Studies statistical estimation under non-interactive local differential privacy (LDP), observing Zi ~ Q(·|Xi) with α-LDP and only Z1:n available.  
- Technical tool: Develops information-contraction bounds (KL / mutual information upper bounds via TV) used with Le Cam/Fano to derive sharp minimax lower bounds.  
- Multinomial result: Private minimax risk (ℓ2, ℓ1) scales like d / (n alpha^2) (up to constants/truncation), so effective sample size reduces to n alpha^2 / d.  
- Multinomial achievability: A modified randomized-response mechanism attains the lower bound, proving its optimality.  
- Density estimation result: For Sobolev class F_beta, non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades under LDP to (n alpha^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} — privacy worsens the exponent.  
- Density estimators: Proposes a privatized histogram (β=1) and a novel orthogonal-series mechanism (general β) randomizing signs over a hypercube to preserve unbiased basis coefficients.  
- Suboptimality of naive noise: Shows adding Laplace noise to samples is information-theoretically suboptimal (deconvolution lower bounds).  
- Relation to prior work: Thoroughly situates results with randomized response, LDP literature, global DP geometry, population vs sample risk, and classical minimax theory.  
- Technical soundness: Lower bounds follow standard but nontrivial packing+information arguments; contraction inequalities are appropriate and powerful; upper bounds are constructive and order-optimal.  
- Completeness caveat: Many proofs are deferred to a companion paper—key steps (unbiasedness/variance and privacy of orthogonal-series mechanism) need brief in-paper sketches or lemma pointers.  
- Correctness/typos: Minor notational errors (e.g., constraint should be sum_j j^{2β} θ_j^2 ≤ C^2; Eq.10 “− xj”→“1 − xj”); clarify constants and complexity claims.  
- Clarity: Well written and organized overall; density section is technical and would benefit from a short privacy/unbiasedness proof sketch, pseudocode, and computational-cost discussion.  
- Originality: Novel, sharp minimax bounds under LDP, identification of randomized response optimality, and a new locally private orthogonal-series mechanism with a changed nonparametric exponent.  
- Significance: Highly significant — quantifies LDP’s statistical cost, gives optimal mechanisms, and the exponent change for nonparametrics is notable.  
- Pros: Sharp matching bounds; clear effective-sample-size interpretation; novel mechanism and explanation of Laplace-suboptimality; strong info-theoretic integration.  
- Cons: Heavy reliance on companion paper; restricted to non-interactive LDP (interaction not addressed); no empirical validation; minor typos/notation issues.  
- Suggestions/questions: Extend to interactive channels? Adaptation to unknown β under LDP? Provide concise LDP proof/pseudocode and variance bounds for sign-aggregation; clarify constants (ck), choice of B, and sampling complexity; add small simulations.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — high-quality, original, impactful with minor clarifications and typos to fix.
