- Summary: Provides sharp minimax lower and upper bounds for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation under local differential privacy, showing privacy reduces effective sample size and alters density convergence rates; gives optimal mechanisms and implementable algorithms.  
- Relation to prior work: Connects Warner’s randomized response, differential privacy literature, and minimax theory, building on Duchi et al. (2013) divergence bounds.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis with well-justified use of Le Cam/Fano and prior propositions to derive sharp bounds.  
- Strength: Clear practical relevance by focusing on probability estimation, tying classical survey methods to modern privacy needs.  
- Strength: Demonstrates optimality of Warner’s randomized response for multinomial estimation.  
- Strength: Provides constructive algorithms that achieve matching upper bounds, validating theoretical sharpness.  
- Strength: Well-written, well-organized, and mathematically sound presentation.  
- Strength: Important contribution establishing fundamental privacy–accuracy trade-offs and benchmarks for private inference.  
- Weakness: Density estimation scope limited to smooth densities and L2 metric; other classes/metrics not explored.  
- Weakness: Histogram estimator may be computationally expensive in high dimensions or large datasets; discussion is limited.  
- Weakness: Orthogonal series estimator and sampling scheme (19) are complex and may be hard to implement in practice.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of real-world applicability and the realism of α-local differential privacy assumptions.  
- Argument for acceptance: Addresses a critical intersection of statistics and privacy with rigorous, novel, and impactful results and matching algorithms.  
- Argument against acceptance: Density scope narrow and some algorithms may be practically complex; real-world limitations need more discussion.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, significant, and well-presented contribution with minor weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to discuss extensions, computational complexity, and practical limitations.  
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10.
