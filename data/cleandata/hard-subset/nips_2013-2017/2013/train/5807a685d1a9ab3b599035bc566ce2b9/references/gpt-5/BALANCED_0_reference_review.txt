Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper studies statistical estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) constraints using a minimax decision-theoretic framework. The setup considers non-interactive channels Qi that privatize individual samples Xi into Zi with α-LDP guarantees. The authors develop information-contraction bounds under LDP and use Le Cam/Fano techniques to derive sharp minimax lower bounds, together with matching upper bounds via efficiently implementable mechanisms.

Two main problems are treated:
- Multinomial estimation: For θ ∈ Δd, the paper establishes that the α-private minimax mean-squared error scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)} (up to constants), and in ℓ1 loss as min{1, d/√(nα^2)}. The effective sample size degrades from n to roughly nα^2/d for small α. A slightly modified randomized response mechanism and a Laplace-perturbation of one-hot vectors achieve these rates, showing randomized response (Warner [23]) is minimax-optimal for this task under LDP.
- Density estimation over Sobolev classes Fβ on [0,1] under integrated squared error: The non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} becomes (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP. The lower bound is shown via novel information bounds (adapted from Duchi et al. [9]). Upper bounds are achieved by: (i) a privatized histogram estimator for β=1 (Lipschitz), and (ii) a new locally private orthogonal-series mechanism that releases privatized basis coefficients and attains the optimal rate for all β≥1. The paper also argues that direct additive noise on Xi (e.g., Laplace) cannot attain the optimal rate due to deconvolution limits (Carroll and Hall [4]).

This work connects classical privacy notions (Warner [23]) with modern DP literature (Dwork et al. [11], Kasiviswanathan et al. [17], Wasserman & Zhou [24], Smith [21]) and nonparametric minimax theory (Tsybakov [22], Yang & Barron [25]). The information bounds (KL and mutual information contraction under LDP) are restated from the authors’ longer paper [9] and are key to the lower bounds.

Strengths:
- Clear, sharp characterization of the privacy–utility tradeoff for two canonical problems, with matching lower and upper bounds and explicit mechanisms.
- Conceptual contribution: information contraction bounds under LDP that neatly plug into Fano/Le Cam, yielding broadly applicable lower-bounding tools.
- Identifies when classical randomized response is optimal, giving crisp guidance for practitioners in categorical data collection.
- Nontrivial and novel mechanism design for nonparametric estimation under LDP (the orthogonal-series scheme), and a careful argument showing why naïve perturbation of Xi is suboptimal.
- Results are computationally grounded: estimators are efficiently implementable (simple histograms, projections onto the simplex, and bounded-coordinate randomized mechanisms).

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Reliance on [9] for proofs: Many key proofs (including the main lower bounds and Proposition 1) are deferred to the longer version. For a conference paper, adding proof sketches (beyond statements) would improve self-containment.
- Clarity of regimes in the multinomial bounds: Eq. (8) contains a somewhat puzzling lower-bound term min{1, √(nα^2), d/(nα^2)}. A clearer partition of regimes (in n,d,α) and alignment with the upper bound would help readers parse when each term dominates. Similarly, explicitly stating the constants’ dependence and the small-α regime assumptions would improve readability.
- Efficiency/communication of the orthogonal-series mechanism: The sampling strategy that selects Z uniformly over a half-hypercube raises practicality questions. Please quantify computational complexity, acceptance rates/rejection sampling overhead, and communication cost in k and α. Provide bounds on the variance inflation introduced by B = Θ(B0√k/α) and the constant ck.
- Adaptivity and robustness: The procedures assume known smoothness β and bounded basis (B0). Discuss adaptivity to unknown β (e.g., Lepski–type schemes or cross-validation under LDP), sensitivity to basis choice, and robustness to model misspecification.
- Interactive vs non-interactive: The work focuses on non-interactive (memoryless) channels. A discussion of whether interaction can improve rates (or a lower-bound argument ruling out improvements) would strengthen the conceptual message.
- Approximate privacy: Results are for pure α-LDP. Briefly comment on the (ε,δ)-LDP setting and whether the rates or mechanisms extend, especially since (ε,δ)-LDP can be practically relevant.
- Empirical illustration: While the contributions are theoretical, small synthetic experiments demonstrating the behavior of the proposed estimators and the sharpness of constants would broaden impact and aid practitioner understanding.
- Minor exposition points: Define all constants (e.g., ck) where used; unify the treatment of d-ary randomized response with standard k-ary RR literature; clarify the uniform boundedness assumption for the trigonometric basis (it holds, but mention the bound explicitly).

Quality:
- The results appear technically sound and carefully argued, with appropriate use of information-theoretic tools. The lower/upper bounds match in rate, and the mechanisms are plausible and efficient. Some proofs are deferred; adding sketches would help.

Clarity:
- Generally well-organized and readable, but dense. The paper could benefit from clearer regime statements, more intuition around the contraction inequalities, and a brief overview of the mechanisms before the technical details.

Originality:
- High. Sharp minimax rates under LDP for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation, and the novel series-based privatization mechanism, represent a meaningful advance over prior DP work that focused on sample statistics or central DP, and over earlier locally private analyses.

Significance:
- High. LDP is widely used in practice; knowing optimal rates and mechanisms for fundamental estimation tasks is valuable and likely to influence both theory and applications.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Sharp, matching minimax results with constructive mechanisms.
- Novel information-contraction techniques under LDP enabling general lower bounds.
- Practical relevance (randomized response optimality; simple histogram estimator for β=1).
- Advances understanding of nonparametric estimation under privacy.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Heavy reliance on the longer version for proofs; limited proof content in the submission.
- Practical aspects of the orthogonal-series mechanism (complexity and communication) need more elucidation.
- No empirical validation; limited discussion of interaction and (ε,δ)-LDP.

Recommendation: Accept. The paper makes substantial and timely theoretical contributions with clear implications for practice. Addressing the suggested clarifications and adding brief proof sketches would further strengthen the submission.
