- Establishes sharp minimax rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation under local differential privacy.  
- Shows local privacy reduces effective sample size to nα²/d for multinomial estimation.  
- Shows density estimation rate changes from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy.  
- Proves Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Provides efficient algorithms (histogram and orthogonal series estimators) that achieve the lower bounds.  
- Connects classical randomized response (Warner, 1965) with modern differential privacy and statistical decision theory.  
- Theoretical results are rigorous with matching lower and upper bounds and careful proofs.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and reproducible for experts.  
- Novel extension of prior local privacy work (e.g., Duchi et al.) by deriving sharp rates and new trade-offs.  
- Results have significant implications for privacy-preserving inference and survey design.  
- The change in the density estimation exponent is counterintuitive and needs a more intuitive explanation.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical implementation challenges (large d/k complexity, parameter sensitivity).  
- Density lower bound relies on a trigonometric basis; unclear if rates generalize to other bases.  
- Overall assessment: technically excellent, complete theoretical contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept for NeurIPS.  
- Pro-accept arguments: sharp bounds for fundamental problems, RR optimality, precise privacy-utility trade-offs, efficient algorithms, historical-modern connection.  
- Con-reject arguments: need clearer intuition for density rate change, limited practical discussion, possible basis-dependence concerns.
