- Formalizes private minimax risk under α-LDP and derives sharp rates for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation.  
- Lower bounds use information-contraction inequalities plus Le Cam/Fano; dependence ∝(e^α−1)^2 (≈α^2 for small α) and the packing constructions are standard and plausible.  
- Shows the nonparametric rate exponent shifts from 2β/(2β+1) to 2β/(2β+2) under LDP; the technical argument appears correct.  
- Provides matching upper bounds: a modified randomized response and projection estimator for multinomials achieving ℓ2 O(d/(nα^2)) and ℓ1 O(d/√(nα^2)).  
- For density estimation, uses histogram (β=1) and orthogonal-series (β≥1) mechanisms with bounded-output privatizers whose variance scales as B^2∝k/α^2, yielding k≈(nα^2)^{1/(2β+2)} and optimal bias–variance tradeoff.  
- Argues simple additive Laplace noise on raw data is provably suboptimal, supported by classical deconvolution limits.  
- Proof sketches are convincing and algorithms are implementable, though full proofs are deferred to a longer manuscript.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, and well situated within minimax decision theory and DP literature.  
- Typos/clarity fixes requested: Eq. (10) likely “1 − xj” instead of “− xj”; check dimensional consistency in Theorem 1’s √(nα^2) term.  
- Reproducibility requests: give an explicit bound or citation for ck=O(1) in the orthogonal-series sampler and justify the histogram’s α-LDP Laplace calibration (sensitivity 2 one-hot vector).  
- Conceptual novelty: formalizes private minimax risk under LDP and pinpoints the striking change in nonparametric rates.  
- Methodological novelty: leverages mutual information/KL contraction under LDP and designs a tailored LDP series mechanism (uniform over half-hypercube with randomized sign).  
- Significance: quantifies definitive sample-complexity penalties for LDP (effective sample size nα^2/d for multinomials; loss of one in smoothness denominator for Sobolev), and shows randomized response is optimal for discrete distributions.  
- Emphasizes that mechanism design matters: suitable privatization of feature maps attains optimality while naive Laplace perturbation does not.  
- Main weaknesses/questions: restriction to non-interactive channels—need discussion whether interactivity can improve rates or if bounds extend; provide quantitative constants/constructive sampler for orthogonal mechanism.  
- Suggests adding comparisons to other multinomial LDP schemes (k-ary RR, Optimal Unary Encoding, Local Hashing) or argue optimality within one-shot locally private mechanisms.  
- Recommends optional small simulations to illustrate rate changes and Laplace suboptimality.  
- Pros: sharp, principled minimax characterization; matching, efficient mechanisms; elegant information-contraction tools.  
- Cons: minor presentation/typo issues, limited discussion of interactivity, and full proofs deferred to another manuscript.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept, contingent on fixing minor clarity issues and adding a brief discussion on interactivity.
