- Studies population estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), develops a minimax decision-theoretic framework, and derives sharp rates with matching algorithms for multinomial and density estimation.

- For d-dimensional multinomials, minimax MSE ~ min{1, d/(nα^2)}, implying effective sample size n → nα^2/d; randomized response (with slight modification) and simple Laplace perturbation achieve the rate.

- For Sobolev densities Fβ on [0,1], non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} becomes (α^2 n)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP; matching upper bounds via privatized histograms (β=1) and a novel orthogonal-series mechanism for all β≥1.

- Core technical tool is an information-contraction bound (KL/mutual information controlled by total variation) combined with Le Cam/Fano to get uniform lower bounds over all α-LDP channels.

- Achievability results are constructive and computationally reasonable: randomized response for multinomials and a series privatization scaling B = O(√k/α) to maintain unbiasedness and control variance.

- Demonstrates that naive additive Laplace noise on samples or series coefficients is suboptimal for density estimation due to deconvolution limitations.

- Technical soundness is strong, with bounds tracking α and problem geometry; main proofs rely on standard testing reductions and careful packing constructions.

- Completeness concern: main proofs deferred to a longer version; more proof sketches (especially for the mutual information variational bound and series mechanism) would improve accessibility.

- Scope/assumptions: analysis restricted to non-interactive (memoryless) LDP channels; density results assume bounded orthonormal (trigonometric) basis and periodic boundary conditions; series estimator requires known smoothness β.

- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized; suggested improvements include intuition for the variational mutual information bound, a rates comparison table, pseudocode/runtime for the series mechanism, and discussion of α regimes and constants.

- Originality: high—first to give sharp population minimax rates under LDP, identify exponent degradation for nonparametric density estimation, and design optimal nontrivial series-privatization mechanisms.

- Significance: high—provides foundational privacy–utility tradeoffs, benchmarks for future work, and implementable optimal mechanisms.

- Pros: sharp minimax rates with matching algorithms; clear sample complexity characterization (nα^2/d and exponent shift); novel mechanisms beyond additive noise linking randomized response to modern DP.

- Cons: no empirical demonstrations; non-interactive restriction with no discussion on interaction benefits; adaptivity for β not addressed; some technical and implementation details (series sampling efficiency) not fully spelled out.

- Questions for authors: can interaction in the local model improve rates or are bounds interaction-robust; is private/adaptive selection of k possible (e.g., private Lepski/cross-validation) while preserving rates; what is the computational complexity and sampling efficiency of the orthogonal-series mechanism for large k?

- Minor comments: clarify constants cℓ, cu and α ranges where e^α−1 ≈ α is used; state acceptance probability or provide an efficient sampler for the constrained hypercube in the series mechanism; discuss boundary effects for densities on [0,1].

- Recommendation: Strong accept — high-quality, original, and significant theoretical contribution with high confidence.
