- Investigates trade-offs between α-local differential privacy and minimax statistical efficiency, giving sharp minimax lower and upper bounds for multinomial and density estimation.  
- Demonstrates an effective sample size reduction for multinomial estimation and altered convergence rates for density estimation under local DP.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Develops efficient, implementable algorithms that attain the derived optimal rates.  
- Builds on and extends prior work in differential privacy and minimax theory, applying divergence bounds from Duchi et al.  
- Connects modern DP theory to classical randomized response literature.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: rigorous combination of local DP and minimax decision theory.  
- Sharp results that clearly characterize the privacy–utility trade-offs and optimal procedures.  
- Practical implications for designing privacy-preserving statistical systems and allocating privacy budgets.  
- Well-written, logically organized, with clear notation and sufficient background.  
- Comprehensive coverage of two key problems: multinomial and density estimation.  
- Analysis is mathematically dense and may be inaccessible to readers without strong statistical/DP background.  
- Density estimation results are limited to specific Sobolev classes; broader classes not explored.  
- Heavy reliance on properties of the chosen orthonormal basis; sensitivity to basis choice is under-discussed.  
- Lacks empirical evaluation or simulations to validate practical performance of proposed estimators.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fundamental problem, rigorous/sharp results, strong literature connection, and practical algorithms.  
- Arguments against acceptance: technical density limiting accessibility and absence of empirical validation.  
- Overall: high-quality theoretical contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; reviewer recommends Strong Accept.  
- Review scores: Quality 5, Clarity 4, Originality 5, Significance 5.
