- Studies local differential privacy for probability and density estimation, deriving minimax expected-error lower bounds for l2 distance on discrete distributions and proving randomized response is optimal.  
- Extends results to density estimation with a per-user perturbation scheme that attains the lower bound.  
- Well-motivated work that combines differential privacy and minimax decision theory.  
- Provides sharp minimax convergence rates that expose fundamental privacy–utility trade-offs.  
- Lower bounds derived via Le Cam’s two-point method and Fano’s inequality, with matching computationally efficient upper bounds.  
- Detailed treatment of discrete and continuous distribution estimation under stringent local privacy (statistician does not see raw data).  
- Exposition of minimax bounds is overly complex and could be simplified.  
- Notation is cumbersome; bounds could be stated directly in l2 norm since all results use it.  
- Missing discussion/comparison to l1-norm results and to other (mostly non-local) differential-privacy lower bounds.  
- Would benefit from considering broader privacy models beyond the tailored local-privacy setting and providing minimax bounds for them.  
- New reservation: overlap/extension of a recent FOCS 2013 paper (Section 3) weakens novelty.  
- Technically sound, claims well-supported, well-organized and clearly written overall.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested improvements to exposition and notation.
