- Study of probability estimation under local differential privacy that derives sharp minimax convergence rates and formalizes privacy–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Main technical result: effective sample size drops from n to n·α^2/d for small constant α, and Warner’s randomized response achieves optimal sample complexity.  
- Strength: important theoretical contribution to differential privacy, clarifying fundamental trade-offs.  
- Strength: provides sharp minimax rates of convergence for private estimation problems.  
- Strength: establishes optimality of classical procedures (e.g., randomized response) in private settings.  
- Strength: results extend to other problems (e.g., density estimation) and have broad implications for statistical inference under privacy.  
- Weakness: exposition of minimax bounds is overly complex and could be simplified for accessibility.  
- Weakness: notation is cumbersome; bounds could be stated directly in ℓ2 norm for clarity.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and comparisons (e.g., ℓ1 results and other DP lower bounds) reduce contextual clarity.  
- Weakness: overlap/extension of a recent FOCS 2013 paper weakens perceived novelty.  
- For acceptance: significant, broadly relevant results and a comprehensive, well-written treatment.  
- Against acceptance: complexity of exposition, cumbersome notation, limited discussion, and overlap with prior work.  
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on revisions to improve clarity, notation, and discussion of related work.
