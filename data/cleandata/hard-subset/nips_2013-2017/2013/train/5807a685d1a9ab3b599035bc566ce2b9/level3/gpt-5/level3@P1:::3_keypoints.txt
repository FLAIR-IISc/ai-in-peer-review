- Problem/setup: studies non-interactive α-LDP where each user privatizes Xi via Zi∼Q(·|Xi) and defines the α-private minimax risk using Le Cam/Fano with new LDP-specific divergence bounds.  
- Multinomial estimation: minimax MSE scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)} and ℓ1 risk as min{1, d/√(nα^2)}; a variant of Warner’s randomized response (RR) is optimal.  
- Density estimation rates: for Sobolev smoothness β, nonprivate rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}, i.e., the polynomial exponent worsens by one.  
- Density achievability: matched by a privatized histogram for β=1 and a locally private orthogonal-series estimator for general β≥1 with unbiased per-user mappings.  
- Laplace noise is provably suboptimal for density estimation due to classical deconvolution limits.  
- Relation to prior work: bridges DP and statistical decision theory, extending local-learning and geometric lower-bound results and improving on global-DP and Laplace-perturb-then-estimate approaches.  
- Technical soundness: lower bounds reduce to testing with new KL/mutual information controls under LDP; upper bounds are constructive, efficient, and match the lower bounds.  
- Completeness: contrasts with nonprivate baselines and explains failure of naive noise-addition methods.  
- Clarity issues: exposition is dense; minimax framework, channel notation, and measure-theoretic setup could be streamlined.  
- Notation critique: heavy notation — suggest focusing on primary L2/ℓ1 losses and moving general Φ∘ρ and σ-field details to the supplement.  
- Originality: novel combination of LDP-specific information bounds, sharp population minimax rates, and matching mechanisms; some overlap with authors’ FOCS 2013 work.  
- Novelty positioning: paper should better delineate what is new relative to FOCS and arXiv versions (population risk, density achievability, constructions).  
- Significance: quantifies accuracy loss under local privacy and identifies optimal mechanisms; likely impactful for theory and practice.  
- Strengths: sharp matching bounds, clear operational guidance (RR optimal for multinomials), and conceptual link between DP and minimax decision theory.  
- Exposition suggestions: simplify minimax setup, add a compact table of rates (multinomial and Sobolev), and give intuition for the α^2 penalty and exponent degradation.  
- Scope suggestions: clarify whether lower bounds extend to interactive local protocols and whether interaction can improve rates.  
- Comparison suggestions: expand discussion of ℓ1/Hellinger/TV risks for densities and compare more explicitly with other DP lower bounds (e.g., Smith; Hardt & Talwar) and FOCS results.  
- Practicality suggestions: give constants and computational costs for the orthogonal-series mechanism, detail implementation of sign-restricted sampling, and discuss adaptivity to unknown β and robustness to α>1.  
- Key open questions for authors: do lower bounds change under interactive protocols; can interaction recover nonprivate exponents; do bounds extend to TV/Hellinger losses; is there an adaptive β procedure; extensions to structured simplices?  
- Pros for acceptance: rigorous sharp results with matching constructions, clarifies RR optimality, and broad relevance to privacy-preserving statistics.  
- Cons for acceptance: heavy exposition, overlap with FOCS raising novelty concerns, and limited treatment of interactive or broader privacy models.  
- Recommendation: Accept — important and rigorous contribution despite presentation and positioning issues.
