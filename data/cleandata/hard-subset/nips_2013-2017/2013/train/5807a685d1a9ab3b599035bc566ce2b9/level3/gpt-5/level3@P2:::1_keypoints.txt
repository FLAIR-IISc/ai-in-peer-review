- Studies non-interactive α-local differential privacy (LDP) for statistical estimation; develops a minimax framework with information-theoretic lower bounds (Le Cam/Fano + new KL/MI contraction) and matching constructive upper bounds.  
- Main result (multinomial): MSE scales like d/(n α^2), implying effective sample size n → n α^2/d; randomized response is minimax optimal for this task.  
- Main result (Sobolev density): Nonprivate rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades to (n α^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP; provides privatized histogram (β=1) and orthogonal-series estimators (β≥1).  
- Strength: Clear, principled minimax treatment with sharp lower and matching upper bounds for two canonical estimation problems.  
- Strength: Precisely characterizes privacy–accuracy tradeoffs, including change of exponent in nonparametric density estimation.  
- Strength: Conceptually valuable proof of randomized response optimality and provision of constructive, efficient mechanisms.  
- Strength: Technically solid use of information-contraction bounds under LDP combined with Le Cam/Fano methods.  
- Strength: Practical insight showing naive per-sample Laplace perturbation is suboptimal and providing improved mechanisms.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent terminology/model framing—must explicitly and early state all results are in the non-interactive LDP model.  
- Weakness: Related-work positioning is weak; needs clearer contrast with central DP estimation and prior LDP/interactive learning results.  
- Weakness: Needs clearer early explanation that lower bounds target population parameters θ(P) (not sample-based statistics θ(x1:n)).  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 restricts α∈[0,1]; unclear if rates/constants extend to α>1 or how results transition to the non-private regime.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of other utility metrics (TV, Hellinger, sup-norm); add comments on how the framework handles them.  
- Weakness: Lacks concrete comparisons (and possible empirical illustrations) to other discrete mechanisms (e.g., Laplace on one-hot, count sketches) with explicit constants.  
- Weakness: Comparison to Lei (2012) is cursory—clarify Lei studies central DP, compare rates/smoothness, and explain why two-stage histogram/M-estimation is suboptimal under LDP.  
- Weakness: Editorial clarity needs tightening around privacy definitions; add a figure summarizing main rates vs α, n, d, β and state computational costs for the orthogonal-series mechanism.  
- Quality: Results appear technically sound with sharp bounds and efficient achievability constructions; some proofs deferred to a longer version.  
- Clarity: Generally well written but needs earlier emphasis on LDP, clearer population vs. sample distinction, and expanded related-work comparisons.  
- Originality: High for the LDP setting—density estimation rates/mechanisms under LDP and the tight multinomial characterization are novel.  
- Significance: Significant and timely; quantifies fundamental limits under local privacy and supplies practical mechanisms likely to influence future work.  
- Pros for acceptance: Sharp matched minimax bounds, clear link between information contraction and statistical lower bounds, practical tuning guidance, and randomized response optimality result.  
- Cons for acceptance: Needs clearer model framing (LDP vs central DP), stronger related-work comparisons, and broader discussion of utility metrics and mechanism alternatives.  
- Minor comments: State whether constants in Theorem 1 depend on d, discuss impact of basis sup-norm B0 and rejection-sampling practicality as k grows, fix typos, and define notation (Πk/ΠΔd, Φ∘ρ) on first use.  
- Overall: Strong, novel paper deserving acceptance after clarifications and expanded related-work and exposition improvements.
