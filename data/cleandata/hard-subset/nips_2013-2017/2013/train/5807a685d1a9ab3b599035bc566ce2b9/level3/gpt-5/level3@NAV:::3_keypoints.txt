- Studies estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), deriving sharp minimax expected-error rates for multinomials and nonparametric densities.  
- Shows classic randomized response is ℓ2-minimax optimal for multinomials, matching the lower bound.  
- For Sobolev density estimation, privacy changes the polynomial rate from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (α^2 n)^{-2β/(2β+2)}, with per-user mechanisms achieving the lower bound.  
- Analysis uses Le Cam/Fano techniques with LDP-specific information contraction bounds and connects to prior DP and randomized-response work.  
- Technically strong and careful, with convincing lower bounds and matching constructive, efficient algorithms.  
- Privacy–utility trade-offs are quantified cleanly.  
- Exposition is generally clear but minimax bounds presentation and heavy notation could be streamlined; recommend stating main ℓ2 rates upfront.  
- Original contributions: proving randomized response optimality under LDP and deriving density-estimation rates with simple per-user mechanisms; but overlap with FOCS’13 weakens novelty.  
- Significance is high: sharp LDP rates and practical optimal mechanisms are broadly interesting.  
- Suggestion: simplify presentation and consolidate notation, stating main ℓ2 results early.  
- Suggestion: include discussion/comparisons for ℓ1 risk and relate to central/interactive DP lower bounds.  
- Suggestion: consider broader settings (interactive LDP, shuffle/secure aggregation, communication constraints) and provide minimax bounds or discussion.  
- Suggestion: explicitly state that per-user independent randomization is optimal within the non-interactive local model.  
- Pros: sharp lower/upper bounds, simple implementable mechanisms, and strong theoretical foundations with practical relevance.  
- Cons: exposition/notation could be improved, limited scope beyond non-interactive LDP, and overlap with FOCS’13 reduces novelty.  
- Recommendation: Accept (with reservations about novelty).
