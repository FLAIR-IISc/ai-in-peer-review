- Paper studies estimation of probability distributions under local differential privacy, providing a rigorous minimax analysis of privacy–efficiency trade-offs.
- Derives sharp lower bounds on convergence rates using Fano/LeCam-style arguments and provides matching upper bounds.
- Shows randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation under certain conditions.
- Extends analysis to density estimation, finding local privacy imposes a stronger penalty on convergence rates than in the classical setting.
- Proposes practical, privacy-preserving estimators: randomized response for multinomials and orthogonal series estimators for densities that achieve the minimax rates.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor with detailed proofs and a comprehensive minimax framework.
- Strength: practical relevance—proposed mechanisms are computationally efficient and applicable in practice.
- Strength: novel connection between minimax decision theory and local differential privacy, especially the density estimation extension.
- Strength: clear and rigorous mathematical derivations that bolster credibility.
- Weakness: discussion lacks precision, notably about the necessity and scope of the restriction on α in Theorem 1.
- Weakness: insufficient connection to related work (e.g., Lei’s M-estimation and histogram-based approaches), weakening contextualization.
- Weakness: some statements about differential privacy are overly broad and risk misinterpretation given the local privacy focus.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of how different utility measures ρ affect results and practitioner guidance.
- Weakness: unclear upfront distinction between population and sample statistics, which may confuse readers.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contributions, practical optimal strategies, and high mathematical rigor.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor contextualization and imprecise commentary could limit accessibility and lead to misinterpretation.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revisions to improve related-work discussion, clarify results (including α restriction), and refine broad claims about differential privacy.
