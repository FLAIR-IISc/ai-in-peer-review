- Main contribution: Derives sharp minimax lower bounds under local differential privacy for multinomial and β-smooth density estimation using Le Cam and Fano methods.  
- Multinomial result: Local privacy reduces effective sample size to ~nα²/d and shows Warner's randomized response is minimax optimal.  
- Density estimation result: Local privacy changes the polynomial minimax rate from n⁻²β/(2β+1) to (nα²)⁻²β/(2β+2), a fundamentally worse exponent.  
- Complements lower bounds with matching, computationally efficient upper bounds (private histogram and orthogonal series estimators).  
- Strength: Provides a rigorous, quantitative characterization of the statistical cost of local privacy with sharp minimax rates.  
- Strength: Technically solid application of classical minimax theory and information-theoretic tools from the authors' companion work.  
- Strength: Establishes an important link between Warner's randomized response and modern differential privacy, proving its optimality.  
- Strength: Novel insight that local privacy can alter polynomial convergence rates in nonparametric problems.  
- Weakness: Exposition could better and earlier emphasize the crucial distinction between local and centralized privacy models to avoid confusion.  
- Weakness: Needs clearer, more detailed comparisons to related work—e.g., other simple mechanisms for multinomial estimation and Lei’s private M-estimators.  
- Weakness: Clarify scope of theoretical results—explain restriction to α ∈ [0,1] and discuss behavior for α>1 and extensions to other error metrics beyond L1/L2.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, original, and technically sound paper with significant theoretical contributions; recommend acceptance.
