- Studies minimax bounds for multinomial and density estimation under local differential privacy, characterizing sharp convergence rates and privacy-efficiency trade-offs.  
- Shows a quadratic reduction in effective sample size for multinomial estimation at small α and that local privacy prevents achieving the non-private optimal rate for density estimation.  
- Provides rigorous analysis, novel minimax lower bound for density estimation under local privacy, and matching efficient algorithms achieving the upper bounds.  
- Technically sound, well-written, and significant for understanding fundamental limits of privacy-preserving statistical inference.  
- Major omission: lacks thorough discussion of noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012), which give worst-case L2 lower bounds for linear queries and relate to multinomial estimation.  
- Authors should clarify whether their local-DP results improve upon or complement existing differential-DP bounds and include a comprehensive comparison in the main text.  
- Density estimation lower bound is derived using a trigonometric basis; it is unclear whether the non-private lower bound (eq.13) holds for general Sobolev spaces or is basis-specific.  
- Need an explicit statement of the function class (e.g., which Sobolev space) and justification or comparison with known lower bounds for general Sobolev classes.  
- Missing citation: Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013) addresses MSE and is relevant to the paper’s metric—should be incorporated and discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important open problem, strong/correct technical results, valuable insights on privacy vs. accuracy, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of relevant prior work weakens positioning; basis-specific nature of density lower bound limits generality.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing related-work omissions, clarifying the scope/generalization of the density lower bound, and adding the Nikolov et al. citation.
