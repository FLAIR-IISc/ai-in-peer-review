- Provides detailed study of discrete and continuous probability estimation under local differential privacy (LDP).  
- Establishes sharp minimax rates of convergence, characterizing privacy–statistical efficiency trade-offs.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is optimal for private survey sampling.  
- Integrates statistical decision theory, differential privacy, and information theory to analyze disclosure risk.  
- Quantifies privacy cost in terms of statistical accuracy rather than merely achieving privacy.  
- Extends results to density estimation with associated minimax bounds.  
- Rigorous technical analysis with clear implications for effective sample size and convergence rates.  
- Exposition is sometimes overly complex; Lemma 1 could be simplified to emphasize intuition.  
- Notation is cumbersome; framing results consistently in l2 norm would improve clarity.  
- Should include more discussion of related work (especially l1 results and non-local DP lower bounds) for context.  
- Could discuss broader settings beyond the tailored local-privacy model and provide minimax lower bounds there.  
- Authors should explicitly state optimality of per-user independent randomization (no advantage from conditioning on others’ data).  
- Notes potential overlap with a FOCS 2013 paper, which somewhat reduces novelty.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, rigorous analysis, compelling randomized-response optimality, useful density estimation extension, well-motivated.  
- Arguments against acceptance: complex exposition, cumbersome notation, potential prior overlap.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; strong paper with significant and lasting theoretical contributions.
