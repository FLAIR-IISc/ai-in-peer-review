This paper provides a comprehensive study of the estimation of probability distributions under local privacy constraints. The authors prove sharp minimax rates of convergence for both multinomial and density estimation problems, demonstrating fundamental trade-offs between privacy and convergence rate. The results show that local privacy can significantly impact the quality of statistical estimators, particularly in the case of density estimation.

The paper is well-written and technically sound. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the relevant background material. The main results are presented in a logical and easy-to-follow manner, and the proofs are thorough and well-explained. The paper also includes a discussion of related work and provides a comprehensive list of references.

One of the main strengths of the paper is its ability to provide sharp minimax rates of convergence for both multinomial and density estimation problems. The authors' use of Le Cam's two-point method and Fano's inequality to derive lower bounds on the minimax risk is particularly noteworthy. The paper also provides a novel sampling strategy for achieving the optimal rate of convergence in the case of density estimation.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results for practitioners. While the authors provide some insight into the trade-offs between privacy and convergence rate, more could be done to explain the practical significance of the results.

In terms of originality, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of statistical inference under local privacy constraints. The authors' use of minimax decision theory to analyze the trade-offs between privacy and convergence rate is novel and provides a new perspective on the problem.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication. The results are significant, and the paper is well-written and technically sound. However, I would suggest that the authors provide more discussion of the practical implications of the results and consider adding more examples or simulations to illustrate the trade-offs between privacy and convergence rate.
