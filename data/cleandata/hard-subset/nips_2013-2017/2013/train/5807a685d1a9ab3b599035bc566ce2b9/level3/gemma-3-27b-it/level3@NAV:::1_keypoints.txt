- Studies minimax estimation rates under local differential privacy where data are privatized before sharing.  
- Derives lower bounds for density and multinomial estimation, showing privacy–efficiency trade-offs.  
- Demonstrates optimality of Warner’s randomized response mechanism in certain survey scenarios.  
- Connects recent differential privacy advances to classical minimax theory, formally characterizing limits in the local model.  
- Highlights that local privacy yields different convergence rates than standard (central) differential privacy.  
- Covers both multinomial and density estimation, demonstrating generality of results.  
- Core ideas are sound but the exposition has clarity issues and occasional imprecision.  
- The paper’s linkage to existing differential privacy literature is underdeveloped and needs expansion.  
- Claim that the provided lower bounds are “stronger” than prior work lacks direct comparative evidence.  
- The choice and practical justification for the local privacy model are insufficiently motivated.  
- The necessity of the α restriction in Theorem 1 is unclear and requires discussion of relevant scenarios.  
- Should compare to Lei’s M‑estimation and other histogram/discrete-data estimators for context.  
- The distinction between population and sample statistics is not made clear up front and obscures implications.  
- Missing empirical validation or comparisons to alternative estimators.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on improving clarity and contextualization.
