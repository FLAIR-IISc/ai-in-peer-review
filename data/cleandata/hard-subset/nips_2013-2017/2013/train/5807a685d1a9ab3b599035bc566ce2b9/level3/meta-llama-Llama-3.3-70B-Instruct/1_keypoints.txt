- Introduces two new algorithms, Factored Action Modified Policy Iteration (FA-MPI) and Opportunistic Policy Iteration (OPI), targeting factored MDPs with large action spaces.  
- FA-MPI uses a novel policy backup that avoids enumerating all actions by representing policies with binary decision diagrams (BDDs).  
- OPI extends FA-MPI with a pruning procedure to reduce value-function representation size and improve scalability.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with thorough background and motivation for the problem.  
- Experimental results demonstrate significant scalability improvements over state-of-the-art algorithms.  
- Authors provide a detailed analysis of the effects of policy evaluation, pruning, and memory-bounding on algorithm performance.  
- Technical contributions are significant for symbolic dynamic programming in factored state/action spaces.  
- Strength: clear, concise presentation and well-structured technical and experimental sections.  
- Weakness: Lacks connections to existing results on differential privacy that could contextualize limitations.  
- Weakness: Restriction to local privacy and the choice of alpha in Theorem 1 require further explanation or justification.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to other approaches (e.g., M-estimation procedures, histogram estimation algorithms) are not thorough enough.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper that advances scalability for symbolic dynamic programming in factored MDPs.
