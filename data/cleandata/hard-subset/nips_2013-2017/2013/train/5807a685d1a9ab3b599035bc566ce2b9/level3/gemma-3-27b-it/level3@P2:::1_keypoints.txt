- Provides a detailed theoretical study of probability estimation under local privacy, deriving sharp minimax rates for discrete (multinomial) and continuous (density) problems.
- Leverages tools from differential privacy and minimax decision theory to characterize trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency.
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is optimal for privately sampling surveys.
- Well-motivated with historical context connecting Warner (1965) to modern differential privacy.
- Clearly defines local privacy and relates it to disclosure risk, bridging classical decision theory with privacy.
- Extends prior work (Duchi et al., 2013) by covering density estimation under local privacy.
- Delivers sharp minimax rates with matching lower and upper bounds, demonstrating achievability.
- Identifies a departure from standard minimax rates for the density estimation problem due to privacy constraints.
- Sometimes blurs the distinction between local and global differential privacy; the restriction to local privacy needs clearer justification and implications.
- Occasional imprecise language and statements that would benefit from clearer phrasing for a broader audience.
- Related-work discussion is limited; missing comparisons to differential private M-estimators (Lei 2017) and other private estimation techniques.
- Lacks analysis comparing histogram/discrete-data methods to randomized response in the local privacy setting.
- Would benefit from exploring different utility functions ρ and how they affect results to show framework flexibility.
- Needs clearer emphasis earlier on the distinction between population and sample statistics and its implications.
- After rebuttal, clarifying population/sample distinction and adding comparisons to Lei (2017) would improve contextualization.
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theoretical analysis, sharp minimax rates, practical optimality result for randomized response, and a bridge between privacy and minimax theory.
- Arguments against acceptance: blurred local/global privacy distinction, limited related-work discussion, and occasional lack of precision.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity, contextualization, and related-work gaps.
