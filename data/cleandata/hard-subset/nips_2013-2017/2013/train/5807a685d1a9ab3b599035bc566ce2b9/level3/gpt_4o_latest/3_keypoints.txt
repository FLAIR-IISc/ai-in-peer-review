- Explores local privacy for probability and density estimation using a minimax expected error framework, deriving fundamental lower bounds for l2 distance with d discrete types.  
- Proves classical randomized response attains these lower bounds, showing its optimality for l2 norm estimation.  
- Extends results to density estimation, showing a simple user-specific perturbation scheme also meets the lower bound.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with sharp minimax convergence rates, linking statistical decision theory and differential privacy.  
- Reaffirms the practical relevance of classical privacy mechanisms and has significant implications for privacy-preserving statistical inference.  
- Presentation is dense and notation-heavy; needs simpler, more intuitive explanations to improve accessibility.  
- Scope is limited to l2 norm; the paper does not investigate l1 bounds or their relation to l2 results.  
- Analysis is specific to local privacy; extending to other privacy models (e.g., central DP) would increase generality.  
- Section 3 partially overlaps with a FOCS 2013 paper, reducing novelty in the multinomial estimation results.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and address noted limitations.  
- For acceptance: rigorous bounds, demonstration of classical-method optimality, and strong impact on the field.  
- Against acceptance: complex presentation, limited scope, and partial prior-work overlap.
