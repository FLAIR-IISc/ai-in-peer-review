This paper studies minimax estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) for two canonical problems: multinomial probability estimation and nonparametric density estimation. Using information-contraction bounds specialized to the LDP setting, the authors establish sharp lower bounds that are matched by computationally simple mechanisms. For multinomial estimation, they show that the effective sample size is reduced by a factor quadratic in the privacy parameter α, yielding mean-squared error of order d/(nα^2) (up to constants). Randomized response is shown to be minimax optimal, linking Warner’s classic mechanism to modern minimax decision theory. For density estimation over Sobolev-type classes, they prove that LDP fundamentally changes the rate: the classical n−2β/(2β+1) becomes (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2), and they give matching upper bounds via privatized histograms (β=1) and orthogonal series estimators (β≥1). The results relate naturally to prior NIPS/TCS work on DP and sample complexity/noise complexity, and extend the minimax perspective in a way that will interest both statistics and privacy communities.

Quality: The technical development appears careful and correct; the proofs of the lower and matching upper bounds are convincing, and the density-estimation lower bound under LDP seems novel. The achievability mechanisms are simple and implementable.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well organized. Some definitions (e.g., function classes for density estimation) are precise and the privacy model is stated unambiguously.

Originality: The specific sharp minimax rates under LDP, especially for density estimation, and the optimality of randomized response in the multinomial case, are original contributions.

Significance: The results are important: they quantify privacy–utility trade-offs in core estimation problems, highlight inherent losses under LDP, and provide optimal mechanisms.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- The paper lacks discussion of known noise-complexity lower bounds under (central) differential privacy for linear queries: Hardt & Talwar (STOC’10), De (TCC’12), and also Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC’13) for mean-squared error. Since multinomial estimation can be cast as linear queries, the authors should clarify whether their LDP bounds strengthen or are incomparable to these central-DP worst-case bounds, and under what regimes.
- The density lower bound is developed using a trigonometric basis. Please clarify whether the non-private benchmark (eq. 13) and the private lower bound are stated for the same function class (general Sobolev spaces vs. basis-dependent elliptical classes). The comparison should be apples-to-apples.
- A brief discussion of whether interaction could improve rates under LDP would be valuable.

Pros:
- Sharp, matched minimax rates under LDP
- Novel density-estimation lower bound
- Simple, optimal mechanisms (randomized response; privatized series)

Cons:
- Missing related-work discussion on DP noise complexity
- Basis-specific presentation for density classes without a fully general statement
