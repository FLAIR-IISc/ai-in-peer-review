- Paper gives a decision-theoretic, minimax analysis of estimation under local differential privacy with sharp lower bounds and matching achievable procedures.
- Key result (multinomial): local privacy effectively reduces sample size from n to n * α^2 / d, and randomized response is minimax optimal.
- Key result (density): for Sobolev smoothness β, rate changes from n^(-2β/(2β+1)) to (n * α^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)), a heavier penalty than in parametric settings.
- Strength: rigorous, quantitative characterization of the "price of privacy" under the local model, giving clear benchmarks.
- Strength: elegant connection showing Warner’s randomized response is optimal in this formal framework.
- Weakness: paper sometimes blurs the crucial distinction between local and central DP and overstates comparisons to central-model results.
- Weakness: claims like “our lower bounds are stronger than most prior work” need qualification regarding population vs. sample statistics and model differences.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to related literature, notably a too-brief dismissal of Lei [19] without detailed model/assumption/result comparison.
- Weakness: multinomial section should compare other discrete-data mechanisms beyond randomized response to justify practical optimality.
- Clarity issue: some commentary is vague (e.g., about choosing noise mechanisms) and should specify which mechanisms are optimal and why.
- Technical clarity issue: Theorem 1’s restriction α ∈ [0,1] is not justified — authors should state if it is technical or fundamental.
- Question for authors: How does the choice of utility measure ρ beyond L1/L2 affect the analysis and results?
- Question for authors: Why is the α ≤ 1 constraint necessary in Theorem 1?
- Final assessment: technically sound, significant contributions to local privacy theory, but needs clearer positioning relative to central DP and more detailed related-work comparisons.
