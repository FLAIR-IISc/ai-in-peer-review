- Paper studies minimax risk under local differential privacy (LDP) for multinomial probability estimation and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Develops a decision-theoretic framework combining Le Cam/Fano reductions with new information-contraction inequalities for LDP channels.  
- Proves sharp lower bounds and gives matching, efficiently implementable estimators.  
- Multinomial result: LDP induces an effective sample-size reduction to roughly nα^2/d (for α ≤ 1), yielding MSE ~ min{1, d/(nα^2)} and ℓ1 error ~ min{1, d/√(nα^2)}.  
- Shows randomized response (with slight modification) is optimal for locally private multinomial estimation.  
- Density estimation result: LDP changes the minimax exponent from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} (non-private) to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}.  
- Provides matching upper bounds via privatized histograms (β=1) and a novel locally private orthogonal-series estimator (β ≥ 1).  
- Explains why simple additive Laplace noise on raw data cannot achieve the optimal private density-estimation rate.  
- Techniques and proofs are technically sound; assumptions (non-interactive LDP, α∈[0,1], Sobolev classes via basis) are explicit and algorithms are implementable.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; minimax setup, role of channel Q, and core information bounds are clearly presented.  
- Requested clarifications: refine “effective sample size” phrasing to separate dependence on n from α and d, and avoid misleading implications for small α.  
- Requested clarifications: ensure Eq. (13) (non-private rate) is for the identical trigonometric-basis Sobolev class used in the private lower bound and state whether results extend to other Sobolev/Besov classes or bases.  
- Missing related-work discussion: compare LDP minimax lower bounds to central-DP noise lower bounds for linear queries (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012) and cite Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang 2013.  
- Pros: sharp matching minimax bounds, novel finding that LDP changes density-estimation rate exponent, clear optimal mechanisms identified, reusable information-contraction framework.  
- Cons: lacks discussion connecting to central-DP lower bounds, and has ambiguity about the function-class alignment and “effective sample size” narrative.  
- Suggested improvements: add a subsection contrasting LDP vs central-DP lower bounds for linear queries, explicitly align function classes in Eq. (13) and Theorem 2, and tighten the effective-sample-size discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting, well-executed, and important paper; recommend acceptance after addressing related-work and clarification points.
