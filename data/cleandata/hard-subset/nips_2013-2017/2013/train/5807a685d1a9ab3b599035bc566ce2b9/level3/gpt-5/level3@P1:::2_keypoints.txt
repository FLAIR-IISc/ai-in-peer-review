- Paper develops sharp minimax rates for population estimation under non-interactive α-local differential privacy (LDP) using information-theoretic bounds on KL and mutual information.  
- Key technical tool: Le Cam/Fano reductions combined with variational bounds to control divergences after privatization, yielding lower bounds on private minimax risk.  
- Multinomial estimation: privacy reduces effective sample size to roughly n α^2 / d (for small α), with matching upper and lower bounds.  
- Multinomial rates: minimax ℓ2 error ≍ min{1, d/(n α^2)} and ℓ1 error ≍ min{1, d/√(n α^2)}, sharp up to constants for α ∈ [0,1].  
- Optimal mechanism for multinomial: a simple variant of Warner’s randomized response achieves the minimax rates, proving its optimality under LDP.  
- Density estimation (Sobolev Fβ, trigonometric/periodic basis): LDP alters the polynomial rate from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (n α^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}.  
- Intuition for density result: privacy effectively adds one degree in the bias–variance trade-off denominator.  
- Constructions: privatized histograms are optimal for β=1; a tailored locally private orthogonal-series estimator attains lower bounds for all β≥1.  
- Naïve noise addition (Laplace/exponential) is suboptimal for density estimation due to deconvolution limits.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on DP and decision-theory literature; focuses on population estimation under LDP (stricter than central DP) rather than empirical-query error.  
- Novelty: the LDP density-estimation lower bound and sharp multinomial optimality of randomized response are new and significant.  
- Quality: proofs are technically sound; lower bounds and constructive, computationally efficient upper bounds are well supported (some proofs deferred to arXiv [9]).  
- Clarity: paper is well organized and explains local vs central DP, non-interactivity, and likelihood-ratio implications clearly.  
- Weakness: limited discussion comparing LDP population lower bounds to central-DP “noise complexity” lower bounds and missing a citation to Nikolov–Talwar–Zhang (2013).  
- Weakness: density results are proved for periodic Sobolev classes (trigonometric basis); need explicit statement about boundary/basis assumptions and extension to non-periodic spaces.  
- Weakness: mechanism constants and practical details (ck, acceptance probabilities, rejection rates, choice of k when β unknown) need quantification and guidance.  
- Weakness: paper restricts to non-interactive channels; should discuss whether interaction could improve rates or cite results on non-interactivity optimality.  
- Recommendation: strong accept candidate—interesting, original, and impactful work conditional on addressing clarifications, citations, and practical-constant details.
