- Studies minimax estimation rates under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation, providing sharp rates and matching algorithms.  
- Establishes a quadratic reduction in effective sample size for multinomial estimation under local privacy.  
- Shows a fundamentally different convergence rate for density estimation compared to the non-private case, with a novel minimax lower-bound proof.  
- Connects results to Warner’s randomized response and frames privacy results within minimax decision theory.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and a well-defined theoretical framework.  
- Major weakness: inadequate discussion of related work in noise-complexity and differential privacy, omitting key papers (e.g., Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang 2013; Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).  
- Need clearer comparison to existing DP bounds to determine whether the local-DP results improve on established DP/linear-query bounds, especially for multinomial case.  
- Density-estimation lower bound is derived using a trigonometric basis; authors must clarify if the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) holds for general Sobolev spaces or is basis-specific.  
- Require comparisons on the same function class to meaningfully contrast private vs non-private rates.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address related-work gaps and clarify the scope of the density-estimation result.
