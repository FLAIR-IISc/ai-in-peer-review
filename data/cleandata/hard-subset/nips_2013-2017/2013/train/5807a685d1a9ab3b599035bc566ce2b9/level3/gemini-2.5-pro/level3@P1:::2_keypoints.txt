- Paper studies trade-offs between statistical efficiency and local differential privacy using minimax decision theory for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.
- For multinomial estimation over d categories, α-local privacy reduces effective sample size from n to about nα^2/d for small α.
- Warner’s randomized response is shown to be minimax optimal for the multinomial problem.
- For Sobolev-class density estimation (smoothness β), local privacy changes the integrated squared error rate from n^(-2β/(2β+1)) to (nα^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)), a stricter penalty.
- The paper provides computationally efficient algorithms that achieve the derived minimax rates.
- Strength: Significant and original connection between minimax theory and local differential privacy, yielding sharp, non-asymptotic bounds.
- Strength: High technical quality and sound proofs; results are important for privacy-statistics research.
- Strength: Clear writing and organization, with precise theorem statements and good contextualization versus classical rates.
- Weakness: Fails to engage important related work on central differential privacy lower bounds for linear queries (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang 2013).
- Weakness: Needs to clarify that the non-private density estimation rate compared is for the exact same Sobolev function class (trigonometric basis) to avoid ambiguity.
- Pro-accept: Novel, technically strong results that quantify the statistical cost of local privacy and unify major theoretical frameworks.
- Con-accept: Omission of comparisons to established central-DP lower bounds makes it hard to position the contribution relative to prior work.
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing the related-work gap and clarifying the density-class comparison.
