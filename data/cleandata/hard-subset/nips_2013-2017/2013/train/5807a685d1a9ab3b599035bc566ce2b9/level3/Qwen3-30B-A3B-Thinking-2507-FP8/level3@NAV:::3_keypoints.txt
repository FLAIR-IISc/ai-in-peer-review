- Comprehensive study of minimax rates for probability and density estimation under local differential privacy.  
- Establishes tight l2 lower bounds for discrete (multinomial) and continuous density estimation.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is rate-optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Shows a histogram-based method achieves the optimal rate for density estimation.  
- Bridges classical survey sampling techniques with modern differential privacy theory.  
- Precisely characterizes fundamental privacy–utility trade-offs.  
- Strong technical depth and rigorous minimax analysis with complete proofs.  
- Provides efficiently computable estimators that match the lower bounds, enhancing practical relevance.  
- Exposition and minimax derivations are unnecessarily complex and could be simplified.  
- Notation is cumbersome; results should be stated directly in l2 norm since analysis uses it.  
- Recommend explicitly stating that per-user independent randomization is optimal.  
- Suggest comparing to l1-norm settings and discussing connections to non-local DP lower bounds.  
- Some overlap with Duchi et al. FOCS 2013 (Section 3), slightly weakening novelty.  
- Overall assessment: strong/significant work, moderate clarity, good originality (partly overlapping); recommend acceptance with revisions to clarity/notation.
