- Studies local differential privacy with minimax expected-error rates for probability and density estimation.  
- Derives lower bounds on minimax l2 rates for discrete distributions and proves randomized response meets the lower bound (optimality).  
- Extends results to density estimation with a simple per-user perturbation scheme that achieves the lower bound.  
- Provides sharp minimax rates that quantify fundamental trade-offs between privacy and convergence rate.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is optimal for private survey sampling.  
- Main contributions: minimax lower bounds for probability and density estimation under local DP and matching achievable (upper) bounds.  
- Offers computationally efficient algorithms that attain the derived rates.  
- Paper is technically sound, clear, original, and well-organized.  
- Exposition of minimax bounds is overly complex and notation is cumbersome.  
- Results could be stated directly in l2 norm since all analyses use it.  
- Suggests comparing to l1-norm results and other (mostly non-local) DP lower bounds.  
- Recommends extending analysis to broader settings beyond the tailored local-privacy model.  
- Some overlap/extension of a recent FOCS 2013 paper (Section 3) may weaken novelty.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, arguing contributions outweigh the weaknesses.
