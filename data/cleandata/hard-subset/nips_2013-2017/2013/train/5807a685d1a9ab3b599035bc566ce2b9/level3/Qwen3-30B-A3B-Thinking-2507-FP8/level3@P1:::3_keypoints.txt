- Paper derives sharp minimax rates for local differential privacy in probability estimation, covering multinomial and nonparametric density problems.  
- For multinomial estimation, shows ℓ2-error rate scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)}, reducing effective sample size to nα^2/d.  
- Proves Warner's randomized response (1965) is optimal for the multinomial problem, resolving a long-standing question.  
- For β-smooth density estimation on [0,1], minimax rate degrades from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy.  
- Achievable rates obtained via private histogram and orthogonal series estimators that match the lower bounds.  
- Technical strength: tight and elegant minimax lower bounds using information-theoretic tools, not just upper bounds.  
- Integrates survey methodology and modern differential privacy, quantifying privacy–utility trade-offs missing in prior work.  
- Density estimation extension is a notable and valuable contribution beyond prior multinomial-focused results.  
- Exposition weaknesses: excessive notational complexity, especially in Sections 2–3, obscuring core results.  
- Definitions and reductions (e.g., minimax risk Eq.2, Lemma 1) are more convoluted than necessary; focus on ℓ2 could simplify presentation.  
- Novelty concern: partial overlap with Duchi et al. (FOCS 2013), which treated the same local model and solved the multinomial case.  
- Paper does not sufficiently contrast results with non-local differential privacy settings or discuss ℓ1-error commonly used in surveys.  
- Does not address broader settings like adaptive privacy mechanisms, limiting scope of applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, significant, and fills an important gap; clarity and related-work comparisons should be improved in revision.  
- Suggested revisions: streamline notation, explicitly compare with non-local privacy results, and clarify the relationship to the FOCS 2013 paper.
