- Studies minimax estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation, using information-contraction bounds to get sharp lower bounds matched by simple mechanisms.  
- Multinomial estimation: effective sample size reduced by a factor α^2, yielding MSE ≍ d/(nα^2); randomized response is minimax optimal.  
- Density estimation (Sobolev-type): LDP changes rate from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} with matching upper bounds via privatized histograms (β=1) and orthogonal series (β≥1).  
- Technical development is careful and correct; proofs of lower and matching upper bounds are convincing.  
- The density-estimation lower bound under LDP appears novel.  
- Achievability mechanisms are simple and implementable.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, with precise definitions and an unambiguous privacy model.  
- Originality: provides specific sharp minimax rates under LDP and establishes optimality of randomized response for multinomial estimation.  
- Significance: quantifies privacy–utility trade-offs in core estimation problems and provides optimal mechanisms, of interest to statistics and privacy communities.  
- Weakness: missing discussion of central-DP noise-complexity lower bounds (Hardt & Talwar ’10, De ’12, Nikolov et al. ’13) and clarification of relation to those results.  
- Weakness: density lower bound developed using a trigonometric basis—unclear if non-private benchmark and private lower bound refer to the same function class (Sobolev vs. basis-dependent elliptical classes).  
- Weakness: lacks discussion on whether interaction could improve rates under LDP.  
- Pros summary: sharp matched minimax rates, novel density lower bound, and simple optimal mechanisms.  
- Cons summary: incomplete related-work discussion on DP noise complexity and basis-specific presentation without a fully general statement.
