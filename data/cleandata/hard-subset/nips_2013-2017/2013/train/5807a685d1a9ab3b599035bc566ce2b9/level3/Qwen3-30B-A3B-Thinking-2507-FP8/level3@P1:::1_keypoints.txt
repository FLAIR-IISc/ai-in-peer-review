- Studies minimax estimation rates under local differential privacy, giving sharp bounds for multinomial and density estimation.
- Derives lower bounds using Fano's and Le Cam's inequalities to characterize the trade-off between privacy (α) and statistical efficiency.
- Shows Warner's randomized response mechanism attains optimal sample complexity for multinomial estimation.
- Establishes a novel density estimation rate of (α² n)^(-2β/(2β+2)), vs. classical n^(-2β/(2β+1)), showing privacy changes nonparametric convergence rates.
- Formalizes the statistical cost of local privacy through minimax analysis, filling a gap in DP literature.
- Technical development is sound with rigorous lower bounds and matching efficient upper-bound algorithms.
- Demonstrates that privacy constraints fundamentally alter convergence rates in nonparametric settings — a novel and important finding.
- Confuses local differential privacy with standard differential privacy in places, weakening technical precision.
- Fails to sufficiently compare to recent related work (notably Lei [19]) limiting assessment of novelty.
- Does not adequately discuss how different utility measures (ρ) affect the minimax results and generality.
- Restriction to α ∈ [0,1] in Theorem 1 is problematic and needs clarification or extension to larger α.
- Exposition on the relationship between population and sample estimation is unclear and should be explained earlier.
- Overstates novelty of the local privacy model exposition, given prior work (e.g., Duchi et al.).
- Practical implications are underexplored; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing privacy-model distinctions, literature comparisons, α-range clarification, and exposition improvements.
