- Studies minimax estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) for discrete distributions and Sobolev-class density estimation, giving sharp expected-error rates.  
- Derives information-theoretic lower bounds under LDP via new contraction inequalities controlling divergences and mutual information after privatization.  
- Shows for discrete distributions ℓ2 risk degrades effectively from n to about nα^2/d when α is small.  
- Proves Warner’s randomized response (with minor calibration) is minimax-optimal for the discrete case.  
- Shows LDP changes the nonprivate density rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} for Sobolev smoothness β.  
- Provides two simple, non-interactive per-user mechanisms (Laplace-perturbed histogram for β=1 and orthogonal series with coordinate-wise privatization for β≥1) that achieve the density rates.  
- Connects classical minimax theory and nonparametric estimation to differential privacy, relating to prior DP and deconvolution literature.  
- Expands on the authors’ FOCS 2013 work by extending achievability and density applications, though some technical overlap exists.  
- Technically strong: careful lower-bound analysis and constructive, computationally simple upper bounds.  
- Discrete-case optimality and tailored density schemes are crisply established; proofs appear sound and complete.  
- Exposition of the general minimax framework is longer and more notationally heavy than necessary for the ℓ2-focused results.  
- Paper is otherwise well-organized and mechanisms/estimators are described concretely and reproducibly.  
- Contraction bounds and sharp local-model rates are important contributions, but novelty is somewhat reduced by overlap with prior FOCS 2013 material.  
- High significance: settles exact local-privacy rates and clarifies the statistical cost of local privacy versus central DP.  
- Strengths: sharp local-model lower bounds, simple matching mechanisms, clear privacy–accuracy trade-offs, and optimality of randomized response.  
- Weaknesses: overly complex minimax exposition, limited discussion of ℓ1 risk and relation to central-DP bounds, and confinement to a non-interactive local model without clarity on interaction/shared randomness.  
- Suggested improvements: simplify lower-bound presentation in ℓ2, streamline notation, add ℓ1/Hellinger discussion for densities, explicitly contrast with central-DP dependence on α/d/n, and discuss interaction/shared randomness/heterogeneous α.  
- Also suggest explicitly stating whether lower bounds cover interactive protocols and acknowledging overlap with FOCS 2013 while highlighting new components.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with enthusiasm moderated by overlap with prior work.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
