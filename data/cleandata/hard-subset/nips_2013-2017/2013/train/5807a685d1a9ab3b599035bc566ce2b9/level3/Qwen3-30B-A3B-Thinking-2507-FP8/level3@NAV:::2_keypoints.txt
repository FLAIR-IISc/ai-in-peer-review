- Rigorous study of minimax estimation under local privacy, establishing sharp rates for multinomial and density estimation.
- Local privacy fundamentally reduces statistical efficiency: for multinomial estimation effective sample size scales as n → nα²/d.
- For density estimation with β smoothness, convergence degrades from n⁻²β/(2β+1) to (nα²)⁻²β/(2β+2).
- Proofs appear technically sound; the density-estimation lower bound is a novel contribution.
- Clear exposition linking Warner’s randomized response to optimal privacy-preserving estimation.
- Development of efficiently implementable algorithms that match the derived lower bounds.
- Theoretical framework successfully connects statistical decision theory with differential privacy to analyze the privacy–efficiency trade-off.
- Literature discussion is incomplete: authors should compare to noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy (Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).
- Clarify whether the local-DP bounds presented improve upon or relate to global differential-privacy lower bounds for linear queries (multinomial as a special case).
- Density-estimation lower bound is derived for trigonometric-basis expansions; unclear if the non-private bound (eq. 13) holds for general Sobolev spaces—specify the function class.
- Rebuttal partially addressed prior-work relations, but paper should also cite Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013) for relevant MSE results.
- Missing discussion of established noise-complexity results is a significant gap in contextualizing novelty.
- Overall: interesting, well-written paper with important, original theoretical results; high quality and significance, but needs improved contextualization relative to existing DP lower bounds.
