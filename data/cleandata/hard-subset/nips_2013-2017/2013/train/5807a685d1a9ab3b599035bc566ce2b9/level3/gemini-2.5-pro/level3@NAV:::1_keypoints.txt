- Paper studies trade-offs between statistical efficiency and local (pre-data) privacy using minimax decision theory.  
- Uses Fano’s and Le Cam’s methods to derive sharp lower bounds for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Key result: Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation under local privacy.  
- Key result: Local privacy slows density-estimation rates from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (α^2 n)^{-2β/(2β+2)}.  
- Main strength: Rigorous, sharp characterization of the inherent statistical cost of local privacy for discrete and continuous problems.  
- Main strength: Establishes theoretical optimality for a practical, long-standing mechanism (randomized response).  
- Weakness: Insufficient contextual framing—distinction between local and central differential privacy is sometimes blurred.  
- Weakness: Population vs. sample statistic distinction needs clearer, earlier explanation to frame results.  
- Related-work gap: Should cite and discuss Lei’s private M-estimators for density estimation and other private histogram/discrete estimation methods.  
- Technical clarifications needed: Explain necessity of the alpha restriction in Theorem 1 and its relevance in practical regimes.  
- Technical clarifications needed: Provide more discussion on the choice of utility measure rho and how alternative utility functions fit the analysis.  
- Overall judgment: High originality and significance; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing clarifications and related-work comparisons.
