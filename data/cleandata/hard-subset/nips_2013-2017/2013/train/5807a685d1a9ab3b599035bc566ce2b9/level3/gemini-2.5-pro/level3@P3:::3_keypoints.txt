Summary: Rigorous decision-theoretic analysis of estimation under local differential privacy deriving sharp minimax l2-error bounds for multinomial and density estimation, showing a privacy-accuracy trade-off and achievable rates with simple mechanisms.

Strength — Importance: Establishes fundamental, widely relevant minimax rates quantifying the price of local privacy for basic estimation tasks.

Strength — Optimality of Simple Mechanisms: Proves randomized response is (nearly) minimax optimal for multinomial estimation, giving strong practical justification for simple methods.

Strength — Technical Quality: Sound, complete proofs using information-theoretic tools (Le Cam, Fano) and matching estimators for upper bounds.

Weakness — Clarity of Exposition: Notation and general Φ o ρ formulation are overly dense; presentation could be simplified by focusing on the l2 norm.

Weakness — Limited Scope/Contextualization: Lacks comparisons to l1/total-variation rates, central DP results, and discussion of extensions beyond the non-interactive, memoryless local model.

Weakness — Understated Implication: Should explicitly emphasize that independent per-user randomization is minimax optimal, ruling out gains from interactive schemes.

Originality/Novelty: Density estimation results are novel and substantial, but multinomial results overlap with the authors' recent FOCS 2013 work, weakening overall novelty—clarification needed.

Recommendation: Accept with reservations about novelty; technically strong and important, especially for the new density results.
