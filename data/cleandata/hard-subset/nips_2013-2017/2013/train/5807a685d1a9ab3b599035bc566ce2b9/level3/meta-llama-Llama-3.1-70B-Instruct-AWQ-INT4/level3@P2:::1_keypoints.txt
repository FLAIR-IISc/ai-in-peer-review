- Studies probability estimation under local privacy, linking differential privacy notions with minimax decision theory.
- Provides clear motivation and literature review, connecting local privacy to Warner’s randomized response.
- Well-written and well-organized, making arguments easy to follow.
- Main technical contribution: sharp minimax convergence rates for estimation under local privacy.
- Supplies lower bounds and matching upper bounds achievable by computationally efficient algorithms.
- Applies results to multinomial and density estimation, yielding new insights on privacy–efficiency trade-offs.
- Characterizes fundamental limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed data and formalizes the local privacy model.
- New density-estimation bounds are significant and compared against related work (e.g., Lei’s M-estimator).
- Some commentary could be more precise; certain statements (e.g., bottom of page 4) need clarification.
- Connection to existing differential-privacy results and literature should be explained more clearly.
- Paper sometimes blurs the distinction between local privacy and (central) differential privacy.
- Relevance of the restriction on alpha in Theorem 1 is unclear, especially when alpha ≈ 1 in DP regimes.
- Authors should give more commentary on choices of rho (utility measures) and how different utilities fit the analysis.
- More empirical or theoretical comparisons to other histogram/discrete-data estimation methods are needed.
- Clarify the distinction between population and sample statistics in the local-privacy context.
- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the listed weaknesses.
