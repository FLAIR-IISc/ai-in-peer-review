- Establishes sharp minimax rates for probability estimation under local differential privacy for multinomial and density problems.  
- Connects Warner's randomized response to modern differential privacy, proving it minimax optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Shows density-estimation convergence rate shifts from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (α² n)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy for β-smooth densities.  
- Proves local privacy reduces effective sample size from n to nα²/d for multinomial estimation; Warner's RR achieves this rate.  
- Derives a novel lower bound for density estimation and shows naive perturbation (e.g., Laplace noise) is suboptimal.  
- Proposes histogram and orthogonal-series estimators that match the density-estimation lower bound.  
- Technical contributions are rigorous: matching lower and upper bounds with solid proofs.  
- Clear connections to prior work and statistical decision theory, though relation to existing DP lower bounds needs expansion.  
- Strengths: fundamental problem, sharp/matching minimax bounds, practical algorithms, clear writing, and insightful historical connection.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of noise-complexity lower bounds in DP (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010, De 2012) and relation to linear-query worst-case bounds.  
- Weakness: density lower bound is derived for trigonometric basis—unclear if it generalizes to all Sobolev spaces.  
- Missing/needed citations: Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013) and related MSE/privacy literature.  
- Quality and clarity: exceptionally well-written and reproducible by experts.  
- Originality: significant advance in characterizing local-DP minimax rates and linking classical randomized response to modern DP.  
- Significance: important theoretical and practical implications; demonstrates optimality of a classical mechanism under modern privacy.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address related-work and generality questions.
