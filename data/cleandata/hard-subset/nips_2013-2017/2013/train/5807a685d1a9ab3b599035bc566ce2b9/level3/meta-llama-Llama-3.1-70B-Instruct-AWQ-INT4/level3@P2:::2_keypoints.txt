- Paper studies probability estimation under local differential privacy and derives sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation.  
- Combines differential-privacy formalism with minimax decision theory using Le Cam’s two-point method and Fano’s inequality.  
- Reviews prior information bounds from the authors’ earlier work relating error to privacy level.  
- Multinomial result: local privacy can severely hurt accuracy, effectively reducing sample size from n to nα^2/d for small constant α.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response achieves optimal sample complexity for multinomial problems under local privacy.  
- Density result: local differential privacy changes the convergence rate from the classical (non-private) rate.  
- Proves a lower bound for private density estimation and shows sample-perturbation methods (Laplace/exponential) cannot attain this rate.  
- Constructs optimal estimators: a privatized histogram for Lipschitz densities and an orthogonal-series estimator achieving optimal rates for smoothness β ≥ 1.  
- Paper frames a clear privacy–utility tradeoff and offers ways to adjust procedures to meet privacy or utility needs.  
- Major omission: lacks citation/discussion of related noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy (important prior work).  
- Specific unclear point: density lower bound derived for trigonometric bases — unclear if it extends to general Sobolev spaces or only that basis.  
- Authors should clarify how their local-DP bounds compare to central (differential) DP bounds and cite Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013) on MSE.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and well-written (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Pros: sharp minimax rates, strong theoretical synthesis of privacy and minimax theory, important contributions to private inference.  
- Cons: missing literature discussion/citations on DP noise-complexity lower bounds, and limited clarity on generality of density lower bounds and local vs central DP improvements.
