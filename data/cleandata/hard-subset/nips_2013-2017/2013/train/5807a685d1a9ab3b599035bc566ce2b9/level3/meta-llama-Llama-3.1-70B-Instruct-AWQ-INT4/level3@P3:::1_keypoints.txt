- Studies estimation of probability distributions under local privacy and provides sharp minimax convergence rates.
- Exhibits fundamental trade-offs between privacy and statistical convergence rate and tools to traverse the privacy–efficiency continuum.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly explained, and well-organized with concise presentation of results.
- Technical results are sound with detailed analysis of estimation under local privacy constraints.
- Strength: characterizes limitations of learning from privacy-perturbed data and gives sharp minimax bounds.
- Strength: provides clear explanation of privacy vs. convergence-rate trade-offs.
- Weakness: some imprecise commentary (e.g., bottom of page 4) that may confuse casual readers.
- Weakness: unclear connection to existing differential privacy literature and prior results.
- Authors sometimes overstate or blur the distinction between local privacy and differential (central) privacy.
- Request: provide a clearer, more explicit explanation of differences between local and differential privacy.
- Request: expand discussion of the choice of rho (utility measures) and how different utilities affect results.
- Missing citation/comparison: Lei’s M-estimation density estimator should be discussed and compared.
- Missing empirical/theoretical comparison: contrast histogram/discrete-data estimators with randomized response performance.
- After rebuttal, authors should clarify population vs. sample statistics and compare to related works cited by reviewers.
- Overall assessment: significant and original contribution to privacy-preserving statistical estimation with important implications.
- Acceptance pros: strong technical contributions, sharp minimax rates, and relevance to privacy-aware estimation.
- Acceptance cons: imprecise exposition in places, weak literature connections, and missing method comparisons.
