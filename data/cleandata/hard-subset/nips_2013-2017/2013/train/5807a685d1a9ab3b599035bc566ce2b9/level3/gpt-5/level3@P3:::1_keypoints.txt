- Paper derives minimax lower and matching upper bounds for estimation under non-interactive α-local differential privacy for multinomial and Sobolev density classes.  
- Warner’s randomized response is shown to be minimax-optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Under local privacy the Sobolev density-rate changes from n−2β/(2β+1) to (nα2)−2β/(2β+2), with histogram and orthogonal-series procedures achieving lower bounds.  
- Introduces “information contraction” inequalities (KL/mutual information bounds) to transfer classical Le Cam/Fano techniques to the local privacy setting.  
- Technical arguments are sound: carefully constructed packings, mutual-information bounds, and constructive, simple algorithms matching lower bounds.  
- Multinomial rates are tight in ℓ2 and ℓ1; density results include matching mechanisms.  
- Query about α-regime: need clarification whether the α≤1 restriction is essential and how rates/constants behave for α≈1–3 common in practice.  
- Request to discuss scaling of bounds outside the small-α regime to aid applicability.  
- Suggestion to expand utility/loss discussion: map other risks (TV, Hellinger, L1) to the framework and state whether rates/mechanisms change.  
- Requested direct theoretical and empirical comparison between randomized response and alternative local mechanisms (e.g., Laplace on one-hot encodings or counts) under ℓ1/ℓ2.  
- Clarity concerns: distinguish population vs sample targets early, as current wording can mislead about strength relative to prior DP lower bounds.  
- Clarity concerns: consistently and explicitly distinguish local DP from central DP to avoid implying bounds transfer.  
- Suggest adding a consolidated discussion of ρ, Φ and how choice of utility affects bounds for readability.  
- Originality: clear exposition of local model with minimax testing (largely exposition) plus novel, sharp density-estimation bounds under local privacy.  
- Significance: precisely characterizes privacy–utility trade-offs in the local model and shows a consequential change in minimax exponents for densities.  
- Missing connections: strengthen ties to Kasiviswanathan et al., Wasserman–Zhou, Smith, Hardt–Talwar, De, and Lei, and better position population-risk results relative to central DP lower bounds.  
- Lei-related work: expand discussion comparing assumptions (central vs local), rates, and practicality, and explain inapplicability or suboptimality in the local model.  
- Pros: clean general technique, sharp interpretable rates, optimal/simple randomized response for discrete data, and constructive matching upper bounds.  
- Cons: occasional imprecision about local vs central DP and population vs sample targets; limited discussion of alternative utilities; insufficient α-regime/constants discussion; related-work engagement needs depth.  
- Questions for authors: is α≤1 essential? How do rates change for α≈1–3? Do TV/Hellinger losses alter multinomial rates or RR optimality? What are density rates under L1/Hellinger? Can you compare RR vs Laplace-to-counts for histograms?  
- After rebuttal requests: clarify population vs sample targets early and expand related-work discussion (Lei and others).  
- Recommendation: strong paper, lean toward acceptance contingent on clarifying modeling distinctions, α-regimes/utilities discussion, and strengthening related-work comparisons.
