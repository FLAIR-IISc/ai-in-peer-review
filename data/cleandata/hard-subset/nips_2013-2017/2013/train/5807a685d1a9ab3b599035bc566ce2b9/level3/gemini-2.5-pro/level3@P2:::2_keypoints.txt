- Provides a rigorous decision-theoretic analysis of the statistical cost of local differential privacy (LDP).  
- Studies minimax rates for multinomial parameter estimation and nonparametric density estimation under LDP.  
- Main contribution: sharp matching upper and lower bounds characterizing the trade-off between privacy level (α) and statistical efficiency.  
- For multinomial estimation: LDP reduces effective sample size by ≈ α²/d and Warner's randomized response is shown to be minimax optimal.  
- For Sobolev nonparametric density estimation: LDP worsens the polynomial convergence rate exponent from 2β/(2β+1) to 2β/(2β+2).  
- Connects classical randomized-response motivation (Warner, 1965) with modern differential privacy and minimax decision theory.  
- Technical quality: proofs are sound, build on prior information-theoretic bounds, and produce sharp, matching bounds.  
- Constructive: provides computationally efficient algorithms that achieve the stated upper bounds.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and makes the minimax and LDP settings accessible.  
- Minor clarity issue: need to explicitly state whether the classical non-private rate in Eq. (13) applies to the identical Sobolev function class used for the lower bound.  
- Originality: highly original—one of the first sharp information-theoretic characterizations of minimax risk under stringent local privacy.  
- Novel and surprising finding: local privacy can fundamentally change convergence rates (not just reduce sample size).  
- Main weakness: fails to cite and discuss relevant TCS literature on noise-complexity lower bounds (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012; Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang 2013).  
- Needs explicit discussion clarifying how the LDP bounds relate to or strengthen existing central-DP linear-query lower bounds.  
- Significance: high impact for ML and statistics; provides fundamental benchmarks and practical guidance for private statistical procedure design.
