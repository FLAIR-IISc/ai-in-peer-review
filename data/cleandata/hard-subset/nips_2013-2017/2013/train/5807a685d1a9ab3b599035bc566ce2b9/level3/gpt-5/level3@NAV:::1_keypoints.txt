- Studies estimation under local differential privacy, deriving minimax lower bounds via Fano/Le Cam–style information inequalities.
- Shows an “effective sample size” reduction n → n α^2 / d for multinomial estimation under local privacy.
- Shows density-estimation rates change from n^{−2β/(2β+1)} to (n α^2)^{−2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy for Sobolev classes.
- Provides mechanisms that attain the derived rates and shows Warner’s randomized response is optimal in a precise sense.
- Contributes a clear exposition of the local privacy model and new sharp bounds for density estimation.
- Technical approach appears solid: reductions to testing, divergence control under privacy, and tightness via matching constructions are convincing.
- Theorem 1’s restriction on α needs discussion: is it necessary for the lower bound and how relevant is it when practical deployments use α ≈ 1?
- The paper should more explicitly and earlier state whether targets are population parameters θ(P) or sample statistics θ(x1:n).
- Clarity concern: the manuscript sometimes blurs local versus central differential privacy; it must state the assumed model clearly and discuss translations (or not).
- Provide guidance and concrete examples for choices of utility/semi-metrics ρ to show how different utilities fit the analysis.
- Originality: well-executed adaptation of classical information-theoretic lower bounds to local privacy; novel and important density estimation rates.
- Significance: crisply characterizes limitations of learning from privatized data and supplies optimal mechanisms, justifying randomized response.
- Related work: should better situate itself in the NIPS privacy literature and compare directly to Lei’s private M-estimation and other histogram/discrete-data methods.
- Pros: sharp, tight minimax bounds with matching mechanisms; clear privacy–statistical efficiency trade-offs; conceptual importance of randomized response optimality.
- Cons: missing comparisons to relevant DP estimation methods; imprecise local vs central DP distinctions; unclear necessity of α restriction; limited guidance on ρ; population vs sample targets not explained early.
