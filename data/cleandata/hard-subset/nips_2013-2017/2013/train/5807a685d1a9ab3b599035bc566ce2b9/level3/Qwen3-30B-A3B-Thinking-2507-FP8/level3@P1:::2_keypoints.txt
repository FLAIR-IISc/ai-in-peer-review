- Comprehensive minimax analysis of probability estimation under local differential privacy linking classical decision theory and modern privacy frameworks.  
- Establishes sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation, revealing privacy–efficiency trade-offs.  
- Multinomial result: local privacy α reduces effective sample size from n to nα²/d; Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal.  
- Density-estimation result: for β-smooth densities, convergence rate (α² n)^(-2β/(2β+2)), different from the classical n^(-2β/(2β+1)).  
- Bounds are tight and matched by efficiently computable estimators.  
- Strong technical contribution: rigorous lower bounds via information-theoretic tools and matching upper bounds.  
- Paper is very clear and well organized, with consistent notation and a good exposition of the survey-sampling connection.  
- Significant practical implications for survey design and privacy-preserving statistics.  
- Novelty: connects modern DP analysis to Warner’s 1965 randomized response and provides first sharp minimax characterization under local DP.  
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with prior DP literature on noise-complexity lower bounds for linear queries (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).  
- Weakness: unclear whether the local-DP bounds improve upon or are consistent with existing differential-privacy bounds.  
- Weakness: density-estimation lower bound is derived using a trigonometric basis—unclear if it extends to general Sobolev spaces.  
- Missing citation/discussion of related work (Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang, STOC 2013) on MSE in differential privacy.  
- Overall: technically sound, novel, and significant work; recommended for acceptance.
