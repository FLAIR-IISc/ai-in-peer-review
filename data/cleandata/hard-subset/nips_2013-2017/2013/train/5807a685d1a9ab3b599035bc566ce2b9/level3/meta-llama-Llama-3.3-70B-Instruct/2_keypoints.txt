- Introduces two novel algorithms, Factored Action Modified Policy Iteration (FA-MPI) and Opportunistic Policy Iteration (OPI), targeting MDPs with factored state and action spaces to improve scalability.
- FA-MPI uses a new policy backup that employs a BDD representation of the policy to avoid enumerating all actions.
- OPI extends FA-MPI with a pruning procedure to reduce the size of the value-function representation and increase practical efficiency.
- Provides formal analysis including convergence proofs and bounds on the size of the value-function representation.
- Experimental results show OPI outperforms existing algorithms (symbolic value iteration, factored action regression) with significant speedups and better scalability on benchmarks.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a thorough introduction and clear explanation of BDDs/ADDs and key technical concepts.
- Technical contributions are significant and well-motivated, with clear descriptions of algorithms and methods.
- Lacks a detailed comparison with related work such as asynchronous policy iteration, beyond a brief mention.
- Discussion of limitations and challenges is limited, particularly regarding how value and policy diagrams grow with problem complexity.
- Needs more analysis or experiments addressing scalability limits and diagram growth behavior.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with impressive experiments and clarity, likely to have strong impact after minor revisions.
- Pro/Con summary: Pros — significant contribution, well-explained algorithms, strong experiments, clear writing; Cons — insufficient related-work comparison, limited discussion of limitations, unaddressed diagram growth.
