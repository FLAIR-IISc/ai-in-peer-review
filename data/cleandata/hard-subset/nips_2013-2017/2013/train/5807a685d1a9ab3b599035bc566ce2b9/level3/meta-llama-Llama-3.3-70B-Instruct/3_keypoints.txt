- Introduces two novel algorithms (FA-MPI and OPI) for symbolic dynamic programming in factored state/action MDPs that improve scalability.
- FA-MPI performs exact policy evaluation by treating the policy as a constraint on standard Bellman backups.
- OPI is a conservative, opportunistic policy backup that enforces the policy constraint only when it does not increase value-function representation size.
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of OPI, including convergence guarantees and its relationship to asynchronous policy iteration.
- Empirically evaluated on Inventory Control, SysAdmin, and Elevator Control benchmark domains.
- OPI significantly outperforms FA-MPI and prior state-of-the-art methods on scalability; memory-bounding further boosts OPI’s performance.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides strong background and motivation.
- Notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to some readers and would benefit from more explanations.
- Some sections (e.g., related work) feel rushed and could be expanded.
- Would benefit from additional examples and illustrations to clarify concepts and algorithms.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contributions with thorough evaluation — recommended for acceptance.
