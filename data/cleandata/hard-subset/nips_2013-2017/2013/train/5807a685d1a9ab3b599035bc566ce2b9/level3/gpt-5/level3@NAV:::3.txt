Summary and relation to prior work: The paper studies estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), establishing sharp minimax expected-error rates for both discrete distributions and nonparametric densities. For multinomials over d categories, it derives lower bounds in ℓ2 and shows that classic randomized response—each user perturbing only their own type—matches the lower bound, proving optimality. For density estimation over Sobolev classes, it proves a privacy-induced change in the polynomial rate from n−2β/(2β+1) to (α2n)−2β/(2β+2), and presents simple per-user mechanisms (private histograms for β=1 and orthogonal-series sampling for β≥1) that achieve the lower bound. The analysis builds on Le Cam/Fano methods with information contraction bounds tailored to LDP, connecting to Warner’s randomized response and to differential privacy literature (e.g., Wasserman–Zhou; Hardt–Talwar; Smith; De; Kasiviswanathan et al.).

Quality: Technically strong and careful. Lower bounds and matching constructive upper bounds are convincingly argued, with efficient algorithms. The privacy–utility trade-offs are quantified cleanly.

Clarity: The high-level narrative is clear, but the exposition of the minimax bounds is overly complex. The notation is heavy; since all results are in ℓ2, the main rates could be stated upfront directly in ℓ2 with a streamlined notation, deferring generalities to the appendix.

Originality: The contribution that randomized response is minimax-optimal under LDP and the density-estimation rates under simple per-user mechanisms are noteworthy. However, several results overlap or extend a recently published FOCS’13 paper (notably Section 3), which weakens novelty.

Significance: High. Establishing sharp LDP rates and the optimality of classic procedures is of broad interest, and the per-user mechanisms are practical.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Simplify the presentation of minimax bounds and consolidate notation; state all main rates in ℓ2 early.
- Include a discussion and, if possible, comparisons for ℓ1 risk, and relate to central/interactive DP lower bounds (e.g., Hardt–Talwar; Smith; De) to situate LDP-specific penalties.
- Consider broader settings beyond the non-interactive local model (interactive LDP, shuffle/secure aggregation, communication constraints) and provide corresponding minimax lower bounds or discussion.
- Explicitly state that per-user independent randomization is optimal even when schemes that condition on other users’ messages are allowed (within the non-interactive local model).

Pros:
- Sharp lower/upper bounds under LDP; clear privacy–utility scalings (effective n→nα2/d; exponent shift for densities).
- Simple, implementable mechanisms match lower bounds.
- Strong theoretical foundations and practical relevance.

Cons:
- Exposition and notation could be streamlined.
- Limited scope beyond non-interactive LDP.
- Overlap with FOCS’13 reduces novelty.

Recommendation: Accept (with reservations on novelty; still clearly valuable to the community).
