- Studies minimax bounds for probability estimation under local privacy, focusing on multinomial and density estimation.  
- Establishes sharp minimax rates revealing trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency.  
- Shows local privacy causes a quadratic reduction in effective sample size for multinomial estimation.  
- Shows optimal non-private density-estimation rates are unattainable under local privacy; provides novel minimax bounds.  
- Proofs are correct, well-constructed, and supported by both lower and upper bounds.  
- Provides efficient algorithms that achieve the stated minimax rates.  
- Demonstrates the optimality of Warner’s randomized response for multinomial estimation under local privacy.  
- Uses orthogonal series (trigonometric basis) estimators to attain optimal density-estimation rates.  
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical exposition and detailed proofs.  
- Lacks sufficient contextualization and comparison to prior work in differential privacy and noise-complexity lower bounds.  
- Misses important references (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010, De 2012, Nikolov–Talwar–Zhang 2013) and related results on linear counting queries.  
- Unclear whether multinomial bounds improve on existing differential-privacy results and whether non-private lower-bound comparisons via the trigonometric basis are valid.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing related-work gaps and clarifying the scope of contributions.
