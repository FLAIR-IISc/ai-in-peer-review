- Main contribution: derives sharp minimax convergence rates for probability estimation under local differential privacy, with lower bounds and matching, computationally efficient upper bounds.
- Strength: clear exposition of the local privacy model and new bounds for density estimation.
- Strength: thorough analysis of fundamental privacy–statistical efficiency trade-offs.
- Weakness: some commentary is imprecise and may confuse casual readers (e.g., overstatement at bottom of page 4).
- Weakness: the paper sometimes blurs the distinction between local privacy and (central) differential privacy.
- Weakness: unclear whether the restriction on alpha in Theorem 1 is necessary, given regimes where alpha ≈ 1 under DP.
- Weakness: insufficient explicit connection to existing differential privacy results and literature.
- Weakness: needs more detailed discussion of the choice of rho (utility measures) and how different utilities fit the analysis.
- Weakness: should compare performance to other histogram/discrete-data estimation methods (e.g., randomized response).
- Related work: should explicitly compare to Lei’s M‑estimation density estimator and clarify differences from prior contributions.
- Clarification needed after rebuttal: clearly distinguish population vs. sample statistics in the paper.
- Quality: technically sound; theoretical claims and experiments support conclusions.
- Clarity: generally well-written but contains imprecise statements that require tightening.
- Originality: novel synthesis of differential-privacy disclosure risk concepts with minimax decision-theory tools.
- Significance & recommendation: important implications for privacy-preserving inference; recommend acceptance pending revisions to address the above weaknesses.
