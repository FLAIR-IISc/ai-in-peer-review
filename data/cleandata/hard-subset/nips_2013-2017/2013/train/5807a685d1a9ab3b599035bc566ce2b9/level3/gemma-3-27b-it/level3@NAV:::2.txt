## Review of “Local Privacy and Minimax Bounds: Sharp Rates for Probability Estimation”

This paper investigates minimax estimation rates under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation problems. The authors establish sharp minimax rates, demonstrating the trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency, and provide algorithms achieving these rates. The work builds on the formal treatment of privacy provided by differential privacy and connects it with minimax decision theory.

The contribution is significant. The authors demonstrate a quadratic reduction in effective sample size for multinomial estimation with local privacy, and a fundamentally different convergence rate for density estimation compared to the non-private case. The proof of the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy is novel and a key strength. The connections made to Warner’s randomized response as an optimal survey sampling method are also insightful. The paper is well-written and clearly presents the theoretical framework and results. 

However, a major weakness is the lack of sufficient discussion of related work in noise-complexity and differential privacy. While the authors acknowledge some foundational papers, they omit key contributions like Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013) which addresses mean-squared error, and crucially, fail to adequately contextualize their findings with respect to the established noise-complexity lower bounds from Hardt & Talwar (2010) and De (2012). The authors’ rebuttal partially addresses this, but a more thorough comparison to the differential privacy literature, clarifying whether their local-DP bounds improve upon existing DP bounds (especially for the multinomial case, a special case of linear query estimation), is essential. 

Furthermore, the density estimation lower bound is derived for trigonometric-basis expansions. The authors need to clarify whether the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) holds for general Sobolev spaces or is specific to this basis. A comparison with existing results needs to be performed on the same function class to be meaningful. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and mathematically rigorous results on minimax rates under local privacy.
*   Clear exposition and well-defined theoretical framework.
*   Demonstrates fundamental trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency.
*   Provides algorithms achieving the derived bounds.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Insufficient discussion and comparison to related work in differential privacy and noise-complexity.
*   Unclear scope of the density estimation lower bound (basis dependence).



**Overall:** This is a strong paper with important contributions. Addressing the criticisms regarding the related work and clarifying the scope of the density estimation result would significantly strengthen it. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
