- Studies estimation of probability distributions under local privacy and provides sharp minimax rates for discrete and continuous settings.  
- Integrates differential privacy formalism with minimax decision theory to quantify privacy–statistical efficiency trade-offs.  
- Main technical contributions: sharp minimax rates for multinomial estimation and for density estimation under local privacy.  
- Shows for multinomial estimation that local privacy can reduce effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α.  
- Shows that for density estimation local privacy prevents attaining the non-private optimal rate and provides a novel minimax lower bound.  
- Proofs appear correct, and the density-estimation minimax lower bound under local privacy is novel.  
- Lacks discussion of related noise-complexity/differential-privacy lower bounds (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).  
- Unclear whether the local-DP bounds here improve upon or relate to worst-case differential-DP lower bounds for linear queries with L2 error.  
- Density-estimation lower bound is derived for trigonometric-basis expansions; unclear if it extends to general Sobolev spaces—needs comparison on the same function class.  
- Authors partially clarified in rebuttal but should additionally cite Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013) on mean-squared error.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, clearly organized, and uses standard notation.  
- Contributions are original and significant, likely to influence future work, but the novelty should be clarified via better related-work discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address related-work gaps and clarify novelty relative to prior differential-privacy bounds.
