- Studies minimax estimation under local differential privacy using Fano/Le Cam to derive sharp rates for probability estimation.
- Shows Warner's randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation under local privacy.
- Derives new minimax rates for density estimation showing local privacy degrades rates from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to n^{-2β/(2β+2)}.
- Provides computationally efficient algorithms that attain the derived lower bounds.
- Clear theoretical characterization of the privacy–utility tradeoff, including the novel density-rate degradation result.
- Technical minimax bounds are rigorous and the algorithms demonstrate practical relevance.
- Fails to properly distinguish local privacy from standard differential privacy, with at least one misleading statement (bottom of p.4).
- Restriction to the local privacy model is under-justified given its central role in the paper.
- Treatment of the parameter α in Theorem 1 is unclear and its necessity is not well motivated for typical α≈1 settings.
- Omits crucial comparisons to Lei’s M-estimation work and other histogram-based methods, weakening assessment of novelty and practicality.
- Imprecise commentary and delayed clarification of population vs. sample statistics hamper readability and may confuse casual readers.
- Connection to broader differential privacy literature and existing DP results is underdeveloped.
- Significance is limited by narrow focus on local privacy; contribution reads more like careful exposition of local privacy bounds than a major advance.
- Revisions needed to clarify the relationship to standard DP, better situate results in existing literature, and improve presentation.
