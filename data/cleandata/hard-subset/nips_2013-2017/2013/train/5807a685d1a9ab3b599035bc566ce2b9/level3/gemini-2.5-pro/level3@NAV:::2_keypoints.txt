- Derives sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation under local differential privacy, characterizing privacy–efficiency trade-offs.  
- Shows local privacy effectively reduces sample size by a factor ~α² for multinomial estimation, so for small α the rate is comparable to non-private.  
- Proves local privacy fundamentally degrades the optimal convergence rate in nonparametric density estimation, changing the minimax exponent.  
- Connects Warner’s randomized response to modern differential privacy/statistical decision theory and shows a modified randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Strength: Novel minimax lower bound for density estimation under local privacy that quantifies a previously unquantified fundamental limitation.  
- Strength: Provides sharp, matching upper and lower bounds with sound proofs and clear exposition.  
- Weakness: Insufficient engagement with centralized-DP noise-complexity lower bounds—should cite/discuss Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (2012), since multinomial estimation is a linear-queries special case.  
- Weakness: Needs to clarify whether the local-DP bounds are novel improvements over centralized-DP bounds or reflect model-specific trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Density lower bound uses a trigonometric basis; authors should state whether the non-private comparison rate (eq.13) applies to that class or to general Sobolev spaces.  
- Weakness: Prior work on mean-squared error discussion should include Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013).  
- Overall: Interesting, high-quality theoretical contributions that would be strengthened by better situating results within existing differential-privacy lower-bound literature.
