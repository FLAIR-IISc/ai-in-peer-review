- Paper studies estimation under non-interactive local differential privacy (LDP) and develops a minimax decision-theoretic framework quantifying the statistical cost of LDP.  
- Multinomial estimation: α-private minimax MSE scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)}, so effective sample size ≈ nα^2/d for small α; Warner’s randomized response and calibrated Laplace perturbation are rate-optimal (with projection).  
- Nonparametric density estimation (Sobolev Fβ, L2 risk): rate degrades from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP; lower bounds via Le Cam/Fano with new information-contraction inequalities and matching upper bounds via private histogram (β=1) and private orthogonal series (β≥1).  
- Naive Laplace-perturbation of raw data is suboptimal for density estimation (shown via deconvolution limits).  
- Connects classical randomized-response/survey methods with modern DP and nonparametric minimax theory, and relates to prior LDP and DP geometry/statistical DP work.  
- Technical soundness: lower bounds use tailored packings and information-contraction; upper bounds are simple, implementable, and match lower bounds for the non-interactive model.  
- Completeness: covers both parametric (multinomial) and nonparametric (Sobolev) regimes with tight rates and constructive mechanisms; negative deconvolution argument strengthens claims.  
- Empirical aspects: not central — appropriate for a theory paper; results are self-contained and reproducible from descriptions.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written but dense; notation and some derivations (e.g., variational information bound) could be streamlined and main rates stated earlier.  
- Originality: synthesis of minimax theory with local DP and density results is impactful; discrete-estimation part overlaps somewhat with recent FOCS’13 work, so novelty there is incremental.  
- Significance: clean quantification of privacy–utility tradeoffs under LDP, identification of simple optimal mechanisms, and extension of LDP theory to nonparametric density estimation.  
- Pros: sharp matching minimax rates; elegant information-contraction tools; simple implementable mechanisms (randomized response, private histogram, orthogonal series); clarifies limits of perturb-then-estimate.  
- Cons: heavy exposition and notation; overlap with FOCS’13 for discrete estimation reduces novelty there; restricted to non-interactive, independent per-user channels without deep discussion of interactive/correlated mechanisms.  
- Suggestions: simplify notation and highlight main rates upfront; move technical lemmas to appendix; add concise algorithmic summaries of mechanisms; discuss L1 metrics for densities or justify L2 focus; compare to database/central DP lower bounds and clarify scope regarding interaction.  
- Minor comments: remind readers that e^α−1 ≈ α for small α; provide constants’ dependence where feasible; include a figure/table summarizing rates across regimes.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — significant, rigorous contributions with sharp rates and constructive procedures despite some overlap in the discrete case.
