- Paper studies estimation under local privacy, deriving sharp minimax convergence rates and tools to trade off privacy and statistical efficiency.
- Two main results: minimax rate for multinomial PMF estimation and new, surprising bounds for density estimation under local privacy.
- Strength: clearly characterizes limitations of learning from data perturbed for local privacy and derives lower bounds when the algorithm is untrusted.
- Strength: provides new bounds for density estimation in the locally private, nonparametric setting.
- Weakness: some commentary connecting to differential privacy literature is imprecise and may confuse readers.
- Weakness: exclusive focus on local privacy sometimes blurs distinctions from (central) differential privacy.
- Weakness: the restriction on alpha in Theorem 1 is unclear and its practical relevance (e.g., alpha ≈ 1) is not explained.
- Weakness: lacks empirical or theoretical comparisons to other histogram/discrete-data estimation methods versus randomized response.
- Weakness: missing citation/discussion of Lei’s M-estimation density estimator and related comparisons.
- Suggestion: clarify and more precisely situate results within existing differential privacy literature.
- Suggestion: explain the relevance and implications of the alpha restriction in Theorem 1.
- Suggestion: include comparisons to other histogram/discrete-data estimators to benchmark performance.
- Suggestion: discuss Lei’s work on M-estimation density estimators and contrast results.
- Suggestion: explain population vs. sample statistics more clearly and earlier in the paper.
- Originality: novel application of Fano/LeCam-style arguments to derive lower bounds in the local privacy setting.
- Significance: important contribution with meaningful implications for privacy-preserving statistical inference.
- Rating: overall score 8/10, with potential to be a strong contribution after revisions.
