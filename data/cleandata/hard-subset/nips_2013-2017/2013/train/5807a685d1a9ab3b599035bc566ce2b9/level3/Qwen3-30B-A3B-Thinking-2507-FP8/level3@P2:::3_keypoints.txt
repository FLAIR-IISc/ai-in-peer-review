- Studies trade-offs between local differential privacy and statistical efficiency, giving sharp minimax bounds for multinomial and density estimation and algorithms that attain them.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation under local privacy.  
- Provides matching upper and lower bounds with rigorous, comprehensive proofs establishing sharp convergence rates.  
- Quantifies the privacy-utility trade-off concretely, extending results to density estimation beyond prior work.  
- Novelly connects classical randomized-response literature (Warner) with modern differential privacy and minimax decision theory.  
- Exposition is overly complex with cumbersome and inconsistent notation, hindering readability.  
- Significant overlap with Duchi et al. (FOCS 2013) on multinomial estimation weakens the novelty claim.  
- Focuses only on per-user independent perturbation without justifying why interactive or dependent schemes are not better.  
- Suggestion: simplify notation and present results directly in terms of the L2 norm.  
- Suggestion: explicitly address and contrast the relationship with the FOCS 2013 paper and clarify distinct contributions.  
- Suggestion: include a brief comparison of L1 vs L2 results and relate findings to non-local differential privacy lower bounds.  
- Suggestion: explicitly argue why per-user independent randomization is optimal despite more complex schemes.  
- Pro-accept: fills a literature gap with sharp minimax rates, shows practical optimality of randomized response, and is technically sound.  
- Con-accept: novelty overlap, cumbersome exposition, lack of justification for independent perturbation, and limited contextualization in broader DP literature.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with valuable density-estimation extension; recommend acceptance pending clarifications on novelty and exposition.
