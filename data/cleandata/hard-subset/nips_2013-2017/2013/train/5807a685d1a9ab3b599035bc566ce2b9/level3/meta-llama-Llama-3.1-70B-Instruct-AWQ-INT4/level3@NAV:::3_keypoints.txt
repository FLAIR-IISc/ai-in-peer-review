- Studies local differential privacy, deriving minimax expected-error rates for probability and density estimation under l2 loss.  
- Provides lower bounds on minimax l2 rates for discrete distributions over d types.  
- Shows randomized response is optimal by matching the lower bound for discrete estimation.  
- Extends results to density estimation with a simple per-user perturbation scheme achieving the lower bound.  
- Gives sharp minimax rates and characterizes privacy–convergence trade-offs in the locally private setting.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Importance/significance is high: shows classic procedures are optimal and is of community interest.  
- Exposition of minimax bounds is overly complex and notation is cumbersome; results could be stated directly in l2 norm.  
- Lacks discussion/comparison to l1 norm results and other (mostly non-local) differential-privacy lower bounds.  
- Could consider and provide minimax lower bounds for broader settings beyond the tailored local-privacy model.  
- Originality is somewhat weakened by overlap/extension of a recent FOCS 2013 paper (Section 3).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; simplify exposition and consider broader settings.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 9/10.
