## Review of "Local Privacy and Minimax Bounds: Sharp Rates for Probability Estimation"

This paper investigates minimax estimation rates under local differential privacy for multinomial and density estimation problems. The authors establish sharp minimax rates, demonstrating fundamental trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency. A key finding is that local privacy reduces the effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α for multinomial estimation, while preventing the attainment of the non-private optimal rate for density estimation. The work is well-motivated, building on Warner’s classical randomized response and connecting it to modern differential privacy frameworks. 

The paper’s strengths lie in its rigorous theoretical analysis and novel results, particularly the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy. The proofs appear correct, and the authors successfully demonstrate the optimality of Warner’s randomized response procedure. The clarity of the presentation is also commendable, with a logical flow and clear definitions of key concepts. The contribution is significant, providing a foundational understanding of the limitations imposed by local privacy on statistical estimation.

However, a significant weakness is the limited discussion of related work in noise-complexity and differential privacy. While the authors acknowledge prior work (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010, Dwork et al. 2011), they don’t fully contextualize their local-DP bounds within the broader landscape of differential privacy lower bounds, particularly those concerning linear queries with L2 error. Clarifying whether their bounds improve upon existing differential-DP results for the multinomial case is crucial. The rebuttal partially addressed this, but the authors should also cite Nikolov, Talwar & Zhang (STOC 2013) which addresses mean-squared error. 

Furthermore, the density estimation lower bound is derived for trigonometric basis expansions. The authors need to clarify whether the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) holds generally for Sobolev spaces or is specific to this basis. A comparison on the same function class is essential for a valid assessment.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound results on minimax rates under local privacy.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Demonstrates the optimality of a classical privacy-preserving mechanism.
*   Addresses an important and increasingly relevant problem.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Insufficient discussion and comparison with existing differential privacy lower bounds.
*   Lack of clarity regarding the generality of the density estimation lower bound.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper with important contributions to the field. Addressing the concerns regarding related work and the scope of the density estimation lower bound would significantly strengthen the manuscript and warrant a clear acceptance. I recommend acceptance contingent on addressing these points.
