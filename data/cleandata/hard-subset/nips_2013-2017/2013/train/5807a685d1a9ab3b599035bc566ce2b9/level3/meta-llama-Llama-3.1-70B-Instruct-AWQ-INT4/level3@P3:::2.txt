This paper presents a detailed study of the estimation of probability distributions under local privacy constraints, providing sharp minimax rates of convergence for both discrete and continuous settings. The authors bring together the formal treatment of disclosure risk provided by differential privacy with the tools of minimax decision theory, obtaining quantitative trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency.

The paper's main contribution is the derivation of sharp minimax rates for multinomial estimation and density estimation under local privacy constraints. For multinomial estimation, the authors show that providing local privacy can reduce the effective sample size quadratically in the privacy parameter α. In contrast, for density estimation, local privacy prevents attaining the non-private optimal rate, and the authors obtain a novel minimax lower bound.

The proofs appear correct, and the minimax bound for density estimation under privacy is novel. However, the paper lacks a discussion of related noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy, such as those established by Hardt and Talwar (2010) and De (2012). These prior works give worst-case lower bounds for linear queries with L2 error, and it is unclear whether the local-DP bounds in this paper improve upon these differential-DP bounds.

Furthermore, the density-estimation lower bound is derived for trigonometric-basis expansions, and it is unclear whether the non-private lower bound holds for general Sobolev spaces or only for this specific basis. A comparison on the same function class is necessary to establish the novelty of the result.

The authors partially clarify the relationship with prior work in their rebuttal, but they should also cite Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which addresses mean-squared error. Overall, the paper is well-written and presents interesting and important results, but it is missing a discussion of known noise-complexity/differential-privacy lower bounds.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. The authors are careful about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader. The notation is standard, and the definitions are clear.

Originality: The problems and approaches are new, and the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques. However, the authors could improve the discussion of related work and clarify the novelty of their results.

Significance: The results are important, and other researchers are likely to use these ideas or build upon them. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents novel and important results on the estimation of probability distributions under local privacy constraints.
* The authors provide sharp minimax rates of convergence for both discrete and continuous settings.
* The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear correct.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks a discussion of related noise-complexity lower bounds in differential privacy.
* The authors do not clarify whether the local-DP bounds improve upon differential-DP bounds.
* The density-estimation lower bound is derived for a specific basis, and it is unclear whether the result holds for general Sobolev spaces.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the issues mentioned above, including a discussion of related work and clarification of the novelty of their results.
