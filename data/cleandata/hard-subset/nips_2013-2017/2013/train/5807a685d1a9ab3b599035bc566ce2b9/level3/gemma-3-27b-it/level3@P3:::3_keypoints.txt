- Studies fundamental limits of local differential privacy for probability and density estimation and provides sharp minimax rates of convergence.  
- Connects differential privacy with minimax decision theory to rigorously characterize privacy–utility tradeoffs.  
- Proves randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation and extends analysis to density estimation with a different private convergence rate.  
- Establishing optimality of randomized response is a significant and valuable theoretical contribution.  
- Results are likely of high interest to the NIPS community and rest on a strong minimax theoretical foundation.  
- Exposition is overly complex (especially Section 2) and the heavy notation creates a barrier for non-experts.  
- Recommends stating bounds directly in the l2 norm to improve readability since results rely on it.  
- Suggests future work: compare to l1-norm results, contrast with other (non-local) DP lower bounds, and derive minimax bounds for more general privacy models.  
- Urges explicitly stating that per-user independent randomization is optimal despite schemes that condition on other users’ data.  
- Notes overlap with a FOCS 2013 paper, which reduces novelty somewhat though authors acknowledge it.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous technical analysis, significance to privacy-preserving inference, and (largely) original, comprehensive treatment.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unnecessarily complex exposition, cumbersome notation, and reduced novelty due to overlap.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with caveats — valuable contribution but should improve clarity and expand related-work comparisons.
