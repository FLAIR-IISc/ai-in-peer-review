- Studies trade-off between local differential privacy and statistical accuracy, deriving minimax estimation rates for probabilities and densities.  
- Provides tight lower bounds for multinomial and density estimation under local privacy constraints.  
- Shows classic randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Proposes a per-user perturbation scheme achieving the lower bound for density estimation.  
- Connects Warner’s randomized response to modern privacy frameworks and decision-theoretic minimax analysis.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor and clear optimality proof for a practical mechanism.  
- Weakness: exposition is overly complex; KL-divergence/mutual-information proofs and heavy notation obscure core ideas.  
- Suggestion: consistently state bounds in the l2 norm to improve readability.  
- Suggestion: compare results to l1-norm settings and broader (non-local) differential privacy lower bounds.  
- Suggestion: explore more general privacy models and corresponding minimax bounds to broaden impact.  
- Suggestion: explicitly state that per-user independent randomization is optimal despite schemes that condition on others’ data.  
- Concern: significant overlap with a referenced FOCS 2013 paper, weakening novelty.  
- Recommendation: accept, but with slightly reduced enthusiasm due to overlap.
