- Presents decision-theoretic analysis of estimation under local differential privacy, deriving minimax rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Proves sharp lower bounds on squared L2 error for any estimator using locally privatized data and gives efficient estimators that achieve these bounds.  
- Shows multinomial estimation suffers effective sample size reduction from n to ~nα²/d and that Warner's randomized response is minimax optimal.  
- Shows local privacy is costlier for density estimation, changing the convergence exponent from 2β/(2β+1) to 2β/(2β+2).  
- Connects classical minimax theory (Le Cam, Fano, Tsybakov) with modern differential privacy, focusing on the stringent local model.  
- Significance: provides sharp, quantitative trade-offs between privacy parameter α and minimax estimation error, valuable to researchers and practitioners.  
- Originality/impact: establishes randomized response as fundamentally optimal and reveals a novel, deeper privacy cost in density estimation.  
- Quality: technical analysis is sound, using Fano/Le Cam methods, with matching constructive and efficient algorithms demonstrating sharpness.  
- Clarity: exposition is dense and could be simplified (e.g., avoid generic Φ ◦ ρ formalism since analysis focuses on standard norms).  
- Originality concern: multinomial results overlap with the authors' prior FOCS 2013 work; needs explicit clarification of novel contributions.  
- Scope: should compare to central-model lower bounds and expand discussion of l1 vs l2 losses and potential l1 results for density estimation.  
- Key takeaway to emphasize: non-interactive (per-user independent) privacy mechanisms suffice for optimal rates, so interactivity gives no asymptotic benefit.  
- Recommendation: strong technical paper worth acceptance, but revise for clarity and explicitly delineate novelty from prior work.
