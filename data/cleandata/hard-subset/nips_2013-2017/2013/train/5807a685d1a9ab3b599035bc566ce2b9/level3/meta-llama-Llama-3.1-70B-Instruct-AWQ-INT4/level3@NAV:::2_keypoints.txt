- Comprehensive study of estimating probability distributions under local privacy constraints, covering multinomial and density estimation.
- Proves sharp minimax rates of convergence, demonstrating fundamental trade-offs between privacy and convergence rate.
- Shows local privacy can significantly degrade estimator quality, especially for density estimation.
- Well-written, technically sound paper with clear introduction, background, and logically presented main results.
- Thorough, well-explained proofs and comprehensive related-work discussion with extensive references.
- Uses Le Cam's two-point method and Fano's inequality to derive lower bounds on minimax risk.
- Proposes a novel sampling strategy that achieves the optimal convergence rate for density estimation.
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical implications for practitioners and real-world applicability.
- Could benefit from more examples or simulations to illustrate the privacy–convergence trade-offs.
- Assumes high technical expertise, which may limit accessibility to a broader audience.
- Overall recommendation: accept; significant and original contribution to statistical inference under local privacy.
- Reviewer rating: 8/10, confidence: 9/10.
