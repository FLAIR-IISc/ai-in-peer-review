1. Studies minimax rates for estimation under local differential privacy for multinomial and nonparametric density problems.  
2. Derives sharp lower bounds and gives computationally efficient estimators that achieve these bounds.  
3. Finds that α-local privacy effectively reduces sample size by a factor ∝ α²/d for d-dimensional multinomial estimation.  
4. Shows Warner's randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation under local DP.  
5. Shows a stronger privacy penalty for β-smooth density estimation: non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} is unattainable and private rate is (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)}.  
6. Strength: rigorous, technically sound analysis with novel density-estimation lower bound and seemingly correct proofs.  
7. Strength: results give a precise quantitative characterization of the privacy–accuracy trade-off and connect classic randomized response to modern DP theory.  
8. Strength: well-written and organized with clear problem setup and precisely stated main theorems.  
9. Weakness: insufficient engagement with and citation of central-DP noise-complexity lower bounds literature.  
10. Weakness: missing explicit comparison of multinomial lower bounds to Hardt & Talwar (STOC 2010) and De (TCC 2012), which give central-DP L2 lower bounds.  
11. Weakness: density lower bound is proved for a trigonometric Sobolev class—authors should clarify whether the non-private rate and their private bound apply to the same function class.  
12. Weakness: missing citation/discussion of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), relevant to private MSE for query answers.  
13. Summary: important and novel contributions that advance understanding of local DP limits, but the paper would be substantially strengthened by explicit comparisons and citations to central-DP lower-bound literature.
