- Provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of minimax estimation under local differential privacy, giving sharp rates for discrete probability and nonparametric density estimation.
- Demonstrates Warner’s randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation under local privacy, yielding practical significance.
- For d-type discrete distributions, shows effective sample size reduces from n to nα²/d and proves tight lower bounds.
- Shows a minor modification of randomized response achieves the optimal multinomial rate (upper bound).
- For β-smooth densities, proves minimax rate degrades from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy.
- Provides simple, efficiently computable estimators (histogram and orthogonal series) that attain the density estimation rates.
- Bridges classical statistical decision theory with differential privacy, clarifying privacy–efficiency trade-offs relevant to applications.
- Technical results are strong with matching lower and upper bounds and a clear focus on population (not sample) estimation.
- Exposition is overly complex: Sections 2–3 use convoluted notation that obscures main insights and could be simplified using l2 directly.
- Paper neglects discussion/extension to l1 norm, which would be more meaningful for many probability estimation tasks.
- Could better relate to broader privacy literature and compare to non-local DP lower bounds and other privacy models.
- Significant overlap with a FOCS 2013 paper (Duchi et al.) on multinomial minimax bounds, weakening novelty.
- Nonetheless, extension to density estimation and the unified minimax framework remain novel and valuable contributions.
- Should more explicitly argue why per-user independent randomization cannot be improved by conditioning on other users’ data.
- Overall technically sound and significant; recommended for acceptance though novelty is somewhat diminished by prior overlap.
