- Paper formalizes private minimax risk under α-local differential privacy (LDP) and derives sharp convergence rates for multinomial and Sobolev-class density estimation.
- For multinomial estimation, minimax MSE = Θ(min{1, d/(nα^2)}) and ℓ1 rate = Θ(min{1, d/√(nα^2)}), implying effective sample size n → nα^2/d.
- A (slightly modified) Warner randomized response mechanism is shown to be optimal for multinomial estimation under LDP.
- For density estimation over Sobolev balls Fβ, the nonprivate rate n−2β/(2β+1) is unattainable under LDP; the optimal LDP rate is (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2).
- Matching upper bounds for density estimation are achieved by privatized histograms (β=1) and a locally private orthogonal-series scheme using a randomized sign-and-threshold mechanism.
- The paper explains why naive Laplace perturbation of samples cannot attain the optimal density rate via deconvolution-based limitations.
- New LDP-specific information-theoretic lower bounds and testing reductions are developed and matched by efficient constructions.
- Links to classical statistical decision theory and practical implications for LDP mechanism design are well drawn.
- Results build on but differ from prior DP lower bounds for linear queries / noise-complexity trade-offs by focusing on population risk and the stronger local model.
- Reviewer requests expanded discussion comparing multinomial rates to Hardt & Talwar (2010), De (2012), and STOC’13 linear-query results to clarify strengths/incomparabilities.
- Density lower bound is proved for Sobolev classes via the trigonometric basis; reviewer asks explicit statement that comparisons use the same function class and extension to equivalent Sobolev definitions/boundary conditions.
- Suggestion to discuss small-α regimes and constants so practitioners know when asymptotics become relevant.
- Paper focuses on noninteractive (memoryless) channels; reviewer asks to comment on whether interaction can improve rates or cite known separations.
- Request for more details on orthogonal-series mechanism: explicit expected runtime bound, rejection-sampling cost, and guidance for choosing truncation parameter k (possibly data-driven).
- Minor suggestion: add a short, self-contained sketch explaining why Laplace perturbation fails for readers unfamiliar with deconvolution references.
- Technical quality: sound proofs, clear minimax reductions, and matching constructions; some proofs deferred but outlines are clear.
- Clarity: overall well written with explicit definitions; ask to clarify basis dependence of Fβ and include a concise comparison table of nonprivate vs LDP rates/mechanisms.
- Originality: high — sharp minimax characterization under LDP and the exponent change for density estimation are novel and important.
- Significance: high — results likely to influence private estimation theory and practical LDP data collection; tools appear broadly applicable.
- Arguments for acceptance: sharp, conceptually clear results with matching bounds and practical mechanisms; strong writing and impact potential.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete discussion of related DP noise-complexity lower bounds and basis-specific presentation for density lower bounds could be broadened.
- Overall recommendation: Accept, with suggested expansions on related-work comparisons and clarification of Sobolev-class generality.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
