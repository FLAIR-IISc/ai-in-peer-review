- Studies trade-offs between statistical efficiency and local differential privacy, connecting differential privacy with classical minimax theory.  
- For d-dimensional multinomial estimation, proves minimax squared l2 error scales as d/(nα²), implying effective sample size nα²/d.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is (nearly) minimax optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- For β-smooth density estimation, proves the non-private rate n^(-2β/(2β+1)) degrades to (nα²)^(-2β/(2β+2)) under local privacy.  
- Provides matching constructive, computationally efficient procedures: privatized histogram for β=1 and orthogonal series estimator for β>1.  
- Technical strength: rigorous, complete analysis with matching lower and upper bounds (gold standard in minimax theory).  
- Clarity concern: Section 2’s abstract minimax framework is overly general and could be simplified to focus on squared l2 (and l1) risks.  
- Originality: novel connection of local DP and minimax theory and the density estimation rate change; multinomial results overlap with recent FOCS 2013 work and should be acknowledged.  
- Significance: gives a quantitative foundation for the “price of privacy,” with practical implications for choosing privacy mechanisms.  
- Important implication: simple non-interactive, memoryless schemes are minimax optimal for these problems, reducing need for interactive protocols.  
- Suggested improvements: simplify exposition, discuss l1 vs l2 comparisons, compare local vs central DP lower bounds, and note extensions to more general local mechanisms.  
- Acceptance view: recommend acceptance—strong, significant contributions—though enthusiasm is tempered by overlap on discrete estimation.
