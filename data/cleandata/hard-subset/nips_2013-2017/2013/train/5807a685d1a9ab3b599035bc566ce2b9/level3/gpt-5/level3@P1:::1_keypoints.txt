- Paper provides sharp minimax lower and upper bounds for probability estimation under α-local differential privacy (LDP) in discrete and nonparametric settings.  
- Uses Le Cam/Fano-style reductions with LDP-tailored information-contraction bounds to quantify privacy–utility trade-offs.  
- Multinomial result: minimax MSE scales as min{1, d/(nα^2)}, implying effective sample size n → nα^2/d for small α.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response (RR) is minimax optimal for multinomials with an explicit private mechanism and estimator.  
- Density estimation over Sobolev class Fβ: classical rate n−2β/(2β+1) changes to (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2) under LDP (exponent shift).  
- Provides matching constructive upper bounds: privatized histogram (optimal for β=1) and orthogonal-series estimator with binary privatization (optimal for β≥1).  
- Conceptual contribution: decision-theoretic privacy–efficiency trade-off for local DP distinct from central DP.  
- Algorithmic contributions are simple and implementable (RR/projections, histogram, orthogonal series).  
- Relation to prior work: builds on and extends Warner/Evfimievski, DP literature (Dwork, Hardt–Talwar), private learning, and nonprivate minimax theory; extends recent LDP-minimax analyses [Duchi–Jordan–Wainwright].  
- Strength: clear, principled characterization with tight matching bounds and implementable mechanisms.  
- Strength: identification and proof of RR optimality for multinomials is valuable and informative.  
- Weakness: paper sometimes blurs local vs. central DP—needs clearer, early distinction and consistent framing of adversary/utility models.  
- Weakness: related-work comparisons are light—should more directly compare to Lei (NIPS 2012), Smith (STOC 2011), and Chaudhuri–Hsu (ICML 2012) emphasizing local vs central assumptions and rates.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of other loss functions (Hellinger, TV, KL) and whether contraction bounds extend to them—brief guidance requested.  
- Weakness: unclear restriction α ∈ [0,1] in Theorem 1—need to clarify technical vs. essential and behavior for α>1 (and how constants vary).  
- Weakness: should compare RR/Laplace counts to other discrete local mechanisms (one-hot perturbation, k-ary RR) to aid practitioners.  
- Clarity issues: be explicit early that lower bounds concern population parameters (not sample statistics) and specify the metric/regime when claiming RR optimality.  
- Presentation suggestion: summarize key proof ideas (information-contraction with Fano/Le Cam) in the main text rather than deferring to supplement/[9].  
- Quality: technically sound with careful minimax constructions; some proofs rely on prior arXiv work—ensure completeness.  
- Originality: exponent change in nonparametric density estimation and the optimal orthogonal-series mechanism are new and interesting; multinomial RR optimality strengthens folklore.  
- Significance: high—results influence local data-collection mechanism design and private learning under LDP, with practical message about RR.  
- Questions for authors: is α ≤ 1 necessary in Theorem 1; can information bounds handle Hellinger/other distances; provide guidance on constants and practical choice of k for density estimation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: sharp, conceptually clean minimax characterization, matching simple mechanisms, and novel exponent change in nonparametrics.  
- Arguments against acceptance: blurred positioning between local and central DP, light related-work comparison, and deferred technical details affecting clarity.  
- Overall assessment: strong, timely contribution; lean toward acceptance pending clearer positioning, expanded loss discussion, and more thorough related-work comparison.
