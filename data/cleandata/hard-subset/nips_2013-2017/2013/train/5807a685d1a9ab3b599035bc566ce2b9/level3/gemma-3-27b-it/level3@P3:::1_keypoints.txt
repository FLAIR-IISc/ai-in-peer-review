- Addresses estimation under local differential privacy, deriving minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation.  
- Uses Fano/Le Cam-style arguments to characterize the privacy–statistical efficiency trade-off.  
- Demonstrates optimality of Warner’s randomized response in a specific survey sampling context.  
- Well-motivated, tying differential privacy to classical minimax theory.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and clear characterization of limitations imposed by local privacy.  
- Significant contribution: derivation of minimax rates for density estimation.  
- Strength: establishes enduring relevance and optimality of randomized response.  
- Exposition of the local privacy model is sufficiently self-contained (building on prior work).  
- Weakness: occasional imprecise commentary and ambiguous statements that may hinder readers.  
- Major omission: insufficient connection to broader differential privacy literature and unclear distinction between local and global DP.  
- Unclear necessity and relevance of the restriction on alpha in Theorem 1, especially when alpha ≈ 1 in some regimes.  
- Request: more detailed discussion of chosen rho (utility) measures and how different utilities affect bounds.  
- Request: comparison to Lei’s M-estimation density estimator [19] and other histogram/discrete-data estimation methods.  
- Authors pledged to clarify population vs. sample statistics and add relevant comparisons in revision — an important fix.  
- Summary: main contributions are exposition of the local privacy model and new minimax density bounds, but clarity and contextualization need improvement.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous analysis, optimality result for randomized response, addresses a fundamental problem with potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: imprecise commentary, weak situating within DP literature, unclear alpha restriction, missing comparisons to related work.
