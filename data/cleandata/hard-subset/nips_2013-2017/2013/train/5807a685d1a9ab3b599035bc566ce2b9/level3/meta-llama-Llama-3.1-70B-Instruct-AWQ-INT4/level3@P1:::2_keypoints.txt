- Presents sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation under local differential privacy constraints.  
- Revisits Warner’s randomized response and builds a theoretical framework for optimal estimation under local privacy.  
- Shows local privacy can greatly degrade estimator quality; effective sample size decreases quadratically with the privacy parameter α for multinomial estimation.  
- Proves a novel minimax lower bound for density estimation under local differential privacy, showing local DP can preclude attaining the non-private optimal rate.  
- Combines minimax decision theory and differential privacy, bringing a new perspective to privacy–efficiency trade-offs.  
- Technical analysis is sound, claims are well-supported, and results are reproducible for expert readers.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for replication.  
- Results are original and significant; likely to influence future research and practice.  
- Missing discussion of related noise-complexity/differential-privacy lower bounds (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).  
- Unclear whether the non-private lower bound (eq. 13) for density estimation holds for general Sobolev spaces or only for the trigonometric basis used.  
- Rebuttal partly clarifies prior-work relations but does not fully address connections to worst-case DP bounds.  
- Should additionally cite Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (STOC 2013), which studies mean-squared error under privacy.  
- Pros: novel significant results, comprehensive study, well-written, reproducible.  
- Cons: omits discussion of known DP lower bounds, basis-specific density lower bound concerns, and a missing relevant citation.
