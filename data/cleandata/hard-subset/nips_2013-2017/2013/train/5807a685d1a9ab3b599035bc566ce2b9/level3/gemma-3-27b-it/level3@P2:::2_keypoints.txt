- Studies trade-offs between privacy and statistical accuracy in probability estimation under local differential privacy.
- Derives sharp minimax convergence rates for multinomial and density estimation under local privacy, quantifying privacy’s impact on rates.
- Shows for multinomial estimation local privacy effectively reduces the sample size by a factor depending on the privacy parameter α.
- Shows for density estimation local privacy alters the polynomial convergence rate compared to the non-private case.
- Provides efficiently implementable algorithms that achieve the derived minimax rates.
- Recovers and proves the optimality of Warner’s randomized response for survey sampling under local DP.
- Positions the work within classical statistical decision theory and differential privacy, using standard likelihood-ratio formalization of local privacy.
- Analysis is rigorous with matching lower and upper bounds; proofs appear sound.
- Strength: clear, novel results with practical implications and solid theoretical foundation.
- Weakness: limited engagement with related DP literature on noise-complexity lower bounds (e.g., Hardt & Talwar 2010; De 2012).
- Unclear whether the local-DP bounds strictly improve upon or relate to existing central-DP lower bounds for multinomial estimation; rebuttal only partially addressed this.
- Missing citation/discussion of Nikolov, Talwar, and Zhang (2013) regarding MSE bounds in differential privacy.
- Density-estimation lower bound is tied to trigonometric-basis expansions; the connection to general Sobolev-space minimax rates needs clarification.
- Request for clearer comparison of rates under different function-class assumptions and whether the non-private lower bound (Eq. 13) holds universally for Sobolev spaces.
- Recommendation: accept — novel, rigorous, significant, and well-written, but should expand related-work discussion and clarify function-class assumptions.
