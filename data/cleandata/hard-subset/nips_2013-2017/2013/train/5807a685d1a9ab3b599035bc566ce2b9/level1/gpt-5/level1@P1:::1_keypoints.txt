- Study of nonparametric and multinomial estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), combining minimax lower bounds with privacy-constrained mechanism design.  
- Multinomial estimation: private minimax risks scale as min{1, d/(nα^2)} (ℓ2) and min{1, d/√(nα^2)} (ℓ1), implying effective sample size n→nα^2/d; a variant of randomized response is optimal.  
- Density estimation (Sobolev Fβ): non-private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP; privatized histogram (β=1) and a novel privatized orthogonal series (all β≥1) achieve these rates.  
- Direct Laplace noise on raw samples is shown to be suboptimal for density estimation via deconvolution lower bounds.  
- Builds on DP and minimax literature; key technical tool is an information-contraction (KL/mutual information) inequality under LDP from the authors’ companion work.  
- Lower bounds use packing + Fano/Le Cam with new LDP-specific contraction inequalities; upper bounds are constructive and computationally efficient.  
- Mechanisms proposed: modified randomized response for multinomials, Laplace-perturbed histograms with projection, and a privatized orthogonal series using randomized sign/halfspace selection.  
- Proofs are rigorous but many details are deferred to a companion paper, reducing self-containment.  
- Assumptions (non-interactive channels, α∈[0,1], bounded bases) are clear; constants are tracked but practical magnitude guidance is limited.  
- No empirical evaluation; contributions are theoretical and claims are analytically supported.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized; minimax setup, notation, and mechanisms are described concretely.  
- Some exposition could be improved (intuition for orthogonal series geometry, role of ck) and complexity of rejection sampling should be analyzed.  
- Originality: new minimax characterization under LDP, sharp change in nonparametric exponent, and proof that randomized response is minimax-optimal for multinomials.  
- The privatized orthogonal series mechanism is a novel, LDP-tailored contribution.  
- Significance: high — quantifies fundamental privacy–accuracy trade-offs relevant to practical LDP applications.  
- Strengths: sharp matching bounds, interpretable rates, simple optimal mechanisms, and strong connections to prior DP/minimax work.  
- Weaknesses: focus limited to non-interactive LDP; substantial reliance on companion paper; lack of simulations or experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss potential benefits of interaction, include more proof sketches or appendix material, add simple simulations, clarify ck and sampling efficiency, address bounded-basis assumption and extensions, and contrast with global DP rates.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong, original theoretical advances with implementable and insightful mechanisms.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy dependence on companion paper for proofs, no empirical illustrations, limited discussion of interactive protocols.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, with minor clarifications and ideally some empirical illustrations.
