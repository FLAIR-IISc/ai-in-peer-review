**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper studies the estimation of probability distributions under local differential privacy constraints. The authors provide sharp minimax rates of convergence for estimation in locally private settings, exhibiting fundamental trade-offs between privacy and convergence rate. They apply their theoretical tools to two problems: multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation. The results show that providing local privacy can significantly impact the quality of statistical estimators, and the authors demonstrate that some classical methods, such as Warner's randomized response, achieve optimal convergence rates under local privacy.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds on the work of Warner [23] on randomized response and the concept of local differential privacy introduced by Evfimievski et al. [13]. The authors also draw on the literature on differential privacy [11, 1] and minimax decision theory [26, 25]. The paper's focus on the trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency is novel and provides a new perspective on the field of privacy-preserving statistical inference.
