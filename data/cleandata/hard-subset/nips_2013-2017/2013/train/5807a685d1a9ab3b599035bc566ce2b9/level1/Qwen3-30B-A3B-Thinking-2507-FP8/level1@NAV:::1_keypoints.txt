- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of probability estimation under α-local differential privacy, deriving sharp minimax rates for discrete (multinomial) and continuous (density) problems.
- Shows Warner's randomized response is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation, connecting classical survey methodology to modern differential privacy and decision theory.
- For multinomial estimation, effective sample size drops from n to nα²/d under α-local privacy.
- For density estimation with smoothness β, convergence rate changes from n⁻²β/(2β+1) to (nα²)⁻²β/(2β+2) under α-local privacy.
- Presents matching lower and upper bounds and efficient algorithms that achieve the theoretical rates.
- Technical quality is high: rigorous minimax analysis, careful lower/upper bound proofs, and builds on recent differential privacy literature.
- Results are novel for the venue and represent a significant theoretical advancement in understanding privacy–statistical efficiency trade-offs.
- Strengths: clear exposition of fundamental trade-offs, elegant treatment of discrete and continuous cases, and practical insight about randomized response optimality.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of practical implementation challenges and real-world applicability.
- Weaknesses (cont.): could better situate results relative to more recent NIPS privacy work (e.g., Chaudhuri et al., Lei).
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, original, significant theoretical contribution that meets NIPS criteria and is suitable for acceptance.
