- The paper studies probability estimation under local differential privacy, deriving sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation.  
- It connects Warner’s randomized response to modern differential privacy and minimax decision theory, showing optimality in the multinomial case.  
- The work is well-motivated, filling a gap by formalizing privacy–utility trade-offs in a decision-theoretic framework.  
- The notation is generally defined but is dense and may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with the field.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 deliver significant, rigorously proven lower and upper bounds on estimation accuracy under privacy constraints.  
- The authors successfully leverage classical minimax theory (e.g., Tsybakov) combined with privacy-specific bounds to obtain new conclusions.  
- Results have practical relevance, clarifying the trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency for privacy-preserving data analysis.  
- The paper is well-organized with clear definitions, theorem statements, and explanations.  
- There is limited discussion of computational cost and practical feasibility of the proposed estimators, notably the orthogonal series estimator and high-dimensional settings.  
- Density estimation results focus on the trigonometric basis; the paper lacks discussion on generalization to other bases or the basis’s impact on rates.  
- Related-work coverage could be more exhaustive, particularly regarding private density estimation methods (e.g., kernel-based approaches).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve accessibility, add computational-complexity discussion, and expand related-work coverage.
