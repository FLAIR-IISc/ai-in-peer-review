- Study of distribution estimation under local differential privacy, characterizing privacy–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Main theoretical contribution: framework for optimal estimation under α-local privacy and the notion of an "α-private minimax rate."  
- Derivation of lower bounds on convergence rates for any estimator under α-local privacy.  
- Application to multinomial estimation: Warner's randomized response shown to be optimal.  
- Application to density estimation: new minimax lower bound with a different polynomial convergence rate than classical (non-private) results.  
- Strength: high technical rigor with detailed, well-organized proofs.  
- Strength: clear exposition and thorough literature review.  
- Strength: results are significant for understanding privacy vs. statistical efficiency trade-offs.  
- Weakness: results are primarily theoretical with unclear practical applicability.  
- Weakness: paper assumes substantial technical background, potentially limiting accessibility.  
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; arguments for acceptance (clear exposition, sharp minimax rates, significant implications), arguments against (theoretical focus, high technical barrier).
