- Studies distribution estimation under local differential privacy (LDP) and derives sharp minimax rates for multinomial and Sobolev density estimation, showing LDP fundamentally changes sample complexity and nonparametric rate exponents.  
- Combines classical minimax lower bounds (Le Cam, Fano) with LDP-specific information-contraction bounds to obtain tight lower bounds.  
- Key insight: for multinomials effective sample size scales like nα^2/d (small α), and for Sobolev densities the rate exponent changes from 2β/(2β+1) to 2β/(2β+2).  
- Provides matching upper bounds using simple, implementable mechanisms: a modified randomized response (RR) for multinomials and privatized histograms/orthogonal-series estimators for densities.  
- Demonstrates naive Laplace/noise-addition to samples is suboptimal for these problems.  
- Builds on DP/LDP and minimax literature but distinguishes itself by focusing on population estimation under LDP in a minimax framework and leveraging LDP channel information bounds.  
- Lower-bound results: multinomial Mn(Δd, ||·||2^2, α) ≍ min{1, d/(nα^2)} and Mn(Δd, ||·||1, α) ≍ min{1, d/√(nα^2)}; Sobolev Mn(Fβ, ||·||2^2, α) ≥ cβ (nα^2)−2β/(2β+2).  
- Upper-bound results: modified RR is α-LDP and unbiased with variance giving the minimax rate; privatized histogram achieves β=1 and orthogonal-series mechanism achieves optimal rates for β≥1.  
- Technical arguments appear consistent and well referenced, but several key proofs are deferred to a companion longer version, reducing self-containedness.  
- Paper is generally clear with concrete mechanism descriptions and parameter choices, though minor notational typos and some unexplained constants appear.  
- Reproducibility could be improved by adding pseudocode for orthogonal-series sampling and clarifying expected rejection-sampling costs and constant calibration.  
- Original contributions: sharp minimax LDP rates, identification of exponent degradation, proving RR optimality for multinomials, and new locally private orthogonal-series mechanisms.  
- Significance: clarifies sample complexity costs of LDP, proposes implementable optimal mechanisms, and provides broadly relevant theory for private data collection.  
- Weaknesses: restricted to non-interactive channels, reliance on companion paper for proofs, assumptions on support/basis and boundary handling, lack of empirical evaluation, and limited implementation detail for mechanisms.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid minimax theory under LDP with matching bounds and practical mechanisms and clear privacy–utility trade-offs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy dependence on a companion paper, limited scope (non-interactive only), absence of experiments, and some presentation/notation issues.
