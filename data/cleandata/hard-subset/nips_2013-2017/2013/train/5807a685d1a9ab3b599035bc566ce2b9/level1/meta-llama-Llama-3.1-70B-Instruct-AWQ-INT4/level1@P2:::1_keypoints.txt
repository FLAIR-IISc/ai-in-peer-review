- Paper studies estimation of probability distributions under local privacy, keeping data private from the statistician.  
- Develops a theoretical framework characterizing optimal estimation under privacy constraints.  
- Applies tools to multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, with a thorough literature review.  
- Significant technical contributions, providing sharp minimax rates of convergence for locally private estimation.  
- Unified framework elucidates trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency.  
- Shows local privacy can degrade statistical estimator quality and provides tools to navigate the privacy-efficiency continuum.  
- Main weakness: heavy theoretical focus with few practical examples or applications.  
- Additional weakness: several technical proofs are deferred to the appendix, hindering readability of main arguments.  
- Quality score: 8/10 (technically sound, needs more practical examples).  
- Clarity score: 9/10.  
- Originality score: 9/10.  
- Significance score: 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, unified privacy-efficiency framework, important practical implications.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited practical illustrations and key proofs placed in appendix.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to add practical examples/applications and move some proofs into the main text.
