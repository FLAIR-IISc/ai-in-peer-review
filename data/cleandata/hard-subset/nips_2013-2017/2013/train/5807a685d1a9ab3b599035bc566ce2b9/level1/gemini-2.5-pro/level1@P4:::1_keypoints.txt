- Studies trade-offs between statistical efficiency and α-local differential privacy using minimax decision theory, giving sharp non-asymptotic bounds for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.

- Characterizes the minimax risk under α-local differential privacy, connecting Warner’s randomized response to modern differential privacy and quantifying the statistical cost of privacy.

- Multinomial estimation (d dimensions): minimax MSE scales as d/(n α^2), meaning effective sample size reduces to n α^2 / d, and randomized response is minimax optimal.

- Density estimation over Sobolev class Fβ: classical rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades to (n α^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)}, changing the convergence exponent and indicating a more severe privacy penalty.

- Provides matching upper bounds with computationally efficient mechanisms, including a private histogram for β=1 and a private orthogonal series estimator for β>1.

- Technical rigor: combines Fano’s inequality, KL-divergence bounds, and Le Cam’s method to derive tight lower bounds and presents constructive algorithms for upper bounds.

- Originality/significance: first sharp minimax characterization under strong local privacy, theoretically justifies randomized response, and reveals fundamental changes in difficulty for density estimation.

- Offers practical quantitative guidance on the “price of privacy,” likely to inspire substantial follow-up research.

- Presentation: exceptionally clear, well-organized, with precise theorem statements and lucid discussion of implications.

- Weakness: main proofs are deferred to an external arXiv paper, so the conference submission is not fully self-contained for verification.

- Weakness: implementation details and computational cost for the orthogonal series estimator (complex sampling/rejection sampling) are under-specified, leaving practical feasibility unclear.

- Overall: substantial strengths far outweigh minor weaknesses; the paper is a strong, foundational contribution to privacy-aware statistical estimation.
