- Studies trade-off between statistical accuracy and α-local differential privacy for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation within a minimax framework.  
- Main contributions: sharp minimax lower bounds and computationally efficient estimators that achieve these bounds.  
- Multinomial result: minimax risk scales as d / (n * α^2), implying effective sample size n * α^2 / d.  
- Classic randomized response (Warner 1965) is minimax optimal for multinomial estimation under local privacy.  
- Density estimation result: rate degrades from n^(-2β/(2β+1)) to (n * α^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)), changing the polynomial exponent.  
- Privacy imposes more than a constant-factor penalty for nonparametric problems.  
- Proposed optimal mechanisms: privatized histogram for Lipschitz densities and a novel orthogonal series estimator for smoother densities.  
- Connects and modernizes Warner’s survey-privacy work within differential privacy and shifts focus to local privacy.  
- Provides sharp population-level minimax bounds, extending beyond prior sample-level results.  
- Methodological advance: applies minimax and information-theoretic tools to quantify the inherent statistical cost of privacy.  
- Technical quality: rigorous proofs using information-theoretic arguments (Fano) and matching constructive upper bounds.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, formulation, and lucid presentation of key takeaways.  
- Originality and significance: novel synthesis of minimax theory and local differential privacy with likely broad impact.  
- Weakness: key proofs (e.g., Proposition 1) are deferred to an arXiv version, reducing self-containedness.  
- Weakness: description of the orthogonal series sampling strategy is dense; more intuition for its two-stage design would help.  
- Suggestions: include a brief high-level proof sketch for core bounds and clarify motivation for the orthogonal series mechanism.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — foundational, technically flawless, and highly likely to inspire follow-up work; minor con is deferred proofs.
