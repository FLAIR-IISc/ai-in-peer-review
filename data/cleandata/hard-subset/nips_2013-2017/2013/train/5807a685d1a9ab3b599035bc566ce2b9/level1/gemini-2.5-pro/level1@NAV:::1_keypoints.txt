- Rigorous decision-theoretic analysis of probability estimation under local differential privacy (LDP), connecting differential privacy with classical minimax theory.
- Main contribution: sharp minimax convergence rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation under LDP.
- For d-dimensional multinomial, LDP reduces effective sample size from n to approximately nα²/d.
- For density estimation with β derivatives, convergence rate changes from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα²)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP.
- Warner’s randomized response mechanism is shown to be minimax optimal.
- Significance & originality: foundational bridge between fields, providing sharp non-asymptotic characterizations that will guide future algorithm design.
- Technical quality: excellent—tight lower bounds paired with constructive, efficient algorithms that achieve the bounds.
- Presentation: exceptionally well-written and organized, making sophisticated arguments clear.
- Primary weakness: purely theoretical focus with no numerical experiments or simulations.
- Overall verdict: landmark theoretical paper with deep, original contributions; enthusiastic accept.
