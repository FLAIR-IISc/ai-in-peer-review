- Studies minimax rates under local differential privacy (LDP) for multinomial and Sobolev-smooth density estimation, giving sharp rates and matching mechanisms.  
- Develops private versions of Le Cam and Fano by bounding divergences through the privacy parameter α.  
- Shows d-dimensional multinomial risk scales as d/(nα^2), i.e., effective sample size n → nα^2/d; a Warner randomized-response variant is minimax-optimal.  
- Shows Sobolev density rates change from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} (non-private) to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP.  
- Provides optimal mechanisms: privatized histograms for β=1 and an orthogonal-series mechanism with a tailored private sampling scheme for β≥1.  
- Introduces novel mutual-information/variational bounds under LDP that are broadly useful.  
- Technical development is strong and lower bounds are precise in α, n, d, and β, with efficient procedures achieving them up to constants.  
- Some proofs are deferred and finite-sample constants (e.g., ck, choice of B) and tuning guidance (e.g., selecting k) are opaque.  
- Notation is heavy; suggestions include adding pseudocode for mechanisms, intuition for the variational MI bound, and summaries of regimes and constants.  
- No empirical demonstrations or finite-sample validation are provided.  
- Originality is high: establishes sharp minimax rates under non-interactive LDP and identifies an exponent shift for density estimation.  
- Significance is high: clarifies fundamental privacy–utility trade-offs and offers tools likely to influence private learning design.  
- Pros: sharp matching bounds, simple/efficient mechanisms, clear conceptual contributions, strong links to prior DP literature.  
- Cons: restricted to non-interactive LDP and 1D densities, lacks empirical/finite-sample guidance, some technical assumptions and constants under-specified.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
