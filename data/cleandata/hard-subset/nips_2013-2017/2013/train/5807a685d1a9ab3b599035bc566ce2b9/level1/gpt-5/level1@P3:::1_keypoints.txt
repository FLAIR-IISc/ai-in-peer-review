- Paper studies non-interactive local differential privacy (LDP) for d-dimensional multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation over Sobolev classes in a minimax framework, deriving sharp lower bounds and matching upper bounds.  
- Lower bounds use Le Cam/Fano plus LDP-specific information-contraction, yielding an (e^α−1)^2 ≈ α^2 dependence that degrades effective sample size.  
- Upper bounds are simple, efficient procedures: a modified randomized response for multinomials and privatized histograms / α-LDP orthogonal-series estimators for densities.  
- Key takeaway: privacy induces an explicit privacy–efficiency trade-off — multinomial effective sample size reduced by ≈ d/α^2; Sobolev density rate changes from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (α^2 n)^{-2β/(2β+2)}.  
- Results unify and sharpen prior work (randomized response, classical minimax theory, and DP literature) in the stricter local model.  
- Technical soundness: α^2 scaling is natural; constructed mechanisms match lower bounds in rate; avoidance of naive Laplace noise is justified by deconvolution limitations.  
- Completeness: proofs deferred to a longer version but core constructions and rate calculations are described and plausible; series mechanism sampling is implementable.  
- Minor technical issues/typos to fix: Eq. (8) contains an incorrect √(nα^2) term; Eq. (10) should have “1−xj” instead of “−xj”; Sobolev constraint uses φ_j^2 instead of θ_j^2; clarify constants c_k and their dependence on k.  
- Assumptions: non-interactive (memoryless) local model and α∈[0,1] are clear; behavior for larger α is not discussed; series estimator requires known B0 and β (no adaptivity).  
- Clarity: well-structured and intuitive narrative, but dense notation and omitted constants/conditions impede immediate reproducibility for some readers.  
- Originality: high — provides sharp minimax rates under LDP and shows privacy can change polynomial exponents for nonparametric rates; optimal randomized response characterization is notable.  
- Significance: substantial practical and theoretical impact for survey sampling and distributed data collection; methods and contraction bounds are reusable for further lower-bound work.  
- Pros: sharp matching bounds, clear effective-sample-size and exponent characterization, implementable mechanisms, solid theoretical integration, and strong relevance to the venue.  
- Cons: typographical and notation errors; no adaptive procedure for unknown smoothness β; focus limited to non-interactive channels with little discussion of interactivity’s effect; limited treatment of (α,δ)-LDP and alternative losses (e.g., L1/TV); some constants and computational costs for large k not explicit.  
- Suggestions/questions: correct stated typos and provide an appendix with exact constants and α ranges; address (α,δ)-LDP applicability; discuss whether interactivity alters rates; discuss projection to the density cone and its risk effect; explore adaptive selection of k under LDP.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept.
