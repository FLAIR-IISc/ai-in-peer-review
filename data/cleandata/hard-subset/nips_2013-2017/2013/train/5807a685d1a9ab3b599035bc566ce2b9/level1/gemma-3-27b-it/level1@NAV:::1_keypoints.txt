- Studies trade-offs between local differential privacy and statistical efficiency for probability estimation.  
- Derives sharp minimax convergence rates for multinomial and density estimation under local privacy.  
- Multinomial result: effective sample size reduced to n·α² / d.  
- Density result: rate degrades from n⁻²β/(2β+1) to (n·α²)⁻²β/(2β+2).  
- Provides matching lower and upper bounds and exhibits computationally efficient algorithms achieving the bounds.  
- Refines Warner’s randomized response and introduces a novel orthogonal series estimator.  
- Technically rigorous with clear connections to minimax theory and prior work (Dwork et al., Chaudhuri et al., Duchi et al.).  
- Presentation is generally clear with well-explained definitions and notation.  
- Paper is dense and technical, which may limit accessibility for readers unfamiliar with minimax theory or differential privacy.  
- Several proofs are deferred to an external technical report, potentially hindering reproducibility.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical implications for specific applications.  
- Computational cost analysis is brief; more detail beyond linear-time projection claims would help.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fundamental, sharp results; practical algorithms; interdisciplinary relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high technicality limits accessibility; deferred proofs may affect reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
