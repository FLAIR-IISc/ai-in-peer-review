- Rigorous decision-theoretic analysis of the trade-off between statistical accuracy and α-local differential privacy (LDP).  
- Sharp, non-asymptotic minimax bounds for multinomial parameter estimation and nonparametric density estimation under LDP.  
- For d-dimensional multinomial estimation, privacy reduces effective sample size from n to nα^2/d.  
- For Sobolev density estimation of smoothness β, the minimax rate changes from n^(-2β/(2β+1)) to (nα^2)^(-2β/(2β+2)), a fundamental exponent change.  
- Warner’s randomized response is minimax-optimal (up to constants) for multinomial estimation, providing modern justification for a classic mechanism.  
- The authors provide practical, computationally efficient algorithms (privatized histogram and orthogonal series estimator) that achieve the new optimal rates.  
- Core lower bounds leverage information-theoretic inequalities (Proposition 1) drawing on the authors’ companion arXiv work.  
- Technical arguments are sophisticated, rigorous, and the derivations of matching upper bounds make the results complete.  
- Presentation is exceptionally clear, well-motivated, and carefully situates the work within statistics and privacy literature.  
- Novel contributions include: formulation of α-private minimax rates, sharp multinomial characterization, optimality of randomized response, changed nonparametric rates, and new optimal mechanisms.  
- Significant and lasting impact: provides concrete formulas for privacy cost, influences subsequent theory and deployed local-privacy systems.  
- Minor weakness: reliance on an external arXiv paper [9] for key proofs makes the conference submission less self-contained.
