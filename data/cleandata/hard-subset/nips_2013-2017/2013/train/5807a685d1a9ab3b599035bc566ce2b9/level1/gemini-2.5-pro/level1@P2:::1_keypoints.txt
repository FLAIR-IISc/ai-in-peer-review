- Rigorous decision-theoretic analysis of the accuracy–privacy trade-off in the local differential privacy model.  
- Connects minimax theory with differential privacy to derive sharp fundamental limits on estimation error.  
- For d-dimensional multinomial estimation, minimax squared error scales as d / (n * α^2).  
- Warner’s randomized response is shown to be minimax optimal for the multinomial problem.  
- For β‑smooth density estimation, the non‑private rate n^{-2β/(2β+1)} degrades to (n α^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy.  
- Local privacy can change the polynomial convergence rate, not just the effective sample size.  
- Provides matching lower bounds and computationally efficient estimators that achieve the optimal rates.  
- Develops a privatized histogram (β=1) and a novel privatized orthogonal series estimator (β>1).  
- Shows naive perturbation schemes (e.g., adding Laplace noise to data points) are suboptimal.  
- Technical analysis uses Le Cam and Fano methods; proofs are rigorous but largely deferred to a companion paper.  
- Paper is exceptionally well written, clearly motivated, and carefully formulated.  
- Highly original synthesis of survey privacy, minimax theory, and modern differential privacy.  
- Significant and lasting impact: provides benchmarks, methodology for other problems, and practical guidance on the price of privacy.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fundamental contribution, high originality, significant impact, technical excellence, and clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on external proofs and absence of empirical results (minor concerns given the theoretical focus).
