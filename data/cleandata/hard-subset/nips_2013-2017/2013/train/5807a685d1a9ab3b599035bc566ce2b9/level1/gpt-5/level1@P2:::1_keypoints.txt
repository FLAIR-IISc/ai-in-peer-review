- Studies statistical estimation under non-interactive local differential privacy (LDP), combining minimax decision theory with LDP to derive sharp rates for multinomial and nonparametric density estimation.
- Develops information-contraction inequalities for LDP channels (KL and mutual information bounds) and uses Le Cam/Fano reductions to obtain minimax lower bounds.
- Designs simple, computationally efficient privatization mechanisms and estimators that match lower bounds up to constants, giving tight minimax characterizations.
- Multinomial result: under α-LDP the effective sample size degrades to ~nα^2/d for small α, with Mn(∥·∥2^2) ≍ min{1, d/(nα^2)} and Mn(∥·∥1) ≍ min{1, d/√(nα^2)}.
- Shows (modified) Warner’s randomized response is minimax-optimal for multinomial estimation under LDP.
- Density estimation result: for Sobolev class Fβ the minimax rate exponent changes from n^{-2β/(2β+1)} to (nα^2)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under LDP.
- Provides two estimators attaining the private density rates: a privatized histogram for β=1 and a privatized orthogonal series estimator for β≥1 using bounded unbiased privatization of basis evaluations.
- Proves simple Laplace perturbation of raw samples cannot attain optimal density-estimation rates (via deconvolution limits).
- Situates work with relevant DP and minimax literature (e.g., Warner; Dwork; Wasserman & Zhou; Le Cam; Fano; Tsybakov).
- Technical soundness: lower bounds from tailored information bounds and standard reductions; upper bounds via explicit channels, variance control, and bias correction.
- Completeness: thorough treatment of non-interactive LDP with both lower/upper bounds and implementable procedures; proofs deferred to an extended version.
- Caveats: results limited to the non-interactive local model; interactive protocols may be more powerful in some cases.
- Additional caveats: density estimators assume uniformly bounded bases and known smoothness constants (B0, β) for tuning k; adaptivity to unknown β is not addressed.
- Practical limitations: no empirical evaluation to assess finite-sample constants or performance; some constants (e.g., c_k) not fully explicit in main text.
- Clarity: well written with a helpful minimax background, clear flow from information bounds to lower bounds, and concrete mechanism descriptions.
- Suggested clarifications: give geometric intuition for privatization in Eq. (19), clarify role/magnitude of c_k and choice of B, discuss computational cost of rejection sampling, and guidance for selecting k when β is unknown.
- Originality: novel, substantial contributions—sharp minimax rates under local DP, exponent change for density estimation, optimality of randomized response, and new information-contraction lemmas.
- Significance: high—quantifies privacy–efficiency trade-offs, offers optimal constructive mechanisms, and provides benchmarks likely to motivate further research.
- Pros for acceptance: sharp matching bounds, unification of minimax theory with local DP, practical simple mechanisms, and clarification of limits of naive perturbation.
- Cons for acceptance: restricted to non-interactive LDP, requires known smoothness for tuning, lacks adaptive/private model selection, no empirical studies, and some technical details deferred.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically solid, original, significant, with clear exposition and constructive algorithms; minor clarifications suggested but not critical.
