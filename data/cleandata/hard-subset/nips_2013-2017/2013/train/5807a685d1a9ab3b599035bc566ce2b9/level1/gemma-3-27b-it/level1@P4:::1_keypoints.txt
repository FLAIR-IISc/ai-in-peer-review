- Tackles probability estimation under local differential privacy, deriving sharp minimax rates for multinomial and density estimation.  
- Connects Warner’s randomized response to modern differential privacy and minimax theory, quantifying privacy–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Paper is well-structured with a clear introduction and concise formalization of local privacy.  
- Presents a thorough minimax framework and restates necessary background results for self-containment.  
- Multinomial estimation results are strong, proving optimality of Warner’s randomized response.  
- Density estimation results characterize rates under privacy constraints, though they are more complex.  
- Novelty: provides rigorous, quantitative trade-offs between privacy and statistical efficiency.  
- Technical soundness: convincing lower bounds via Fano’s inequality and careful divergence control.  
- Achievability: randomized response and histogram/orthogonal series estimators are well-analyzed and compelling.  
- Clarity: well-written, consistent notation, and accessible mathematical derivations.  
- Significance: results inform theoretical limits and guide design of optimal privacy-preserving estimators.  
- Weakness: practical implementation of the orthogonal series estimator (basis choice, tuning k) needs more detail.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability for higher dimensions or smoothness parameters are not fully discussed.  
- Weakness: scope limited to densities with bounded derivatives; behavior for heavy tails or singularities is unaddressed.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to the privacy parameter α and other practical deployment considerations are not thoroughly explored.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational complexity and practical considerations.
