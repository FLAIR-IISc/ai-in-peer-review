- Paper studies estimation of probability distributions under local differential privacy, characterizing privacy-utility trade-offs.  
- Provides sharp minimax rates of convergence for locally private estimation.  
- Main result (multinomial): effective sample size shrinks from n to n·α^2/d for small constant α.  
- Main result (density estimation): convergence rate becomes (α^2 n)^{-2β/(2β+2)} under local privacy.  
- Presents efficient algorithms that achieve the sharp upper bounds.  
- Shows Warner’s randomized response is optimal for multinomial estimation.  
- Gives a private histogram estimator that attains the optimal rate for Lipschitz densities.  
- Formalizes disclosure risk to enable decision-theoretic analysis of privacy vs. inferential utility.  
- Integrates differential privacy with minimax decision theory for probability estimation under privacy constraints.  
- Proves sharp lower bounds on convergence rates that are achievable with computationally efficient methods.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, well-organized, and technically strong.  
- Results are original, significant, well-related to prior work, and supported by theoretical and empirical evidence.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with a score of 9/10 and lists no objections.
