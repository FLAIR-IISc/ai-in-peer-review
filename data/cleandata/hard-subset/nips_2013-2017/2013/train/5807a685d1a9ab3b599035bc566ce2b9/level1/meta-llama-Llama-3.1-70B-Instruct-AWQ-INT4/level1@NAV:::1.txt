**Summary**

This paper provides a detailed study of the estimation of probability distributions in a locally private setting, where data is kept private even from the statistician. The authors develop a theoretical framework that allows them to characterize optimal estimation under constraints on privacy. They apply their theoretical tools to two probability estimation problems: multinomial estimation and nonparametric density estimation. The results show that providing local privacy can sometimes be detrimental to the quality of statistical estimators, and the authors identify a fundamental continuum along which privacy may be traded for utility in the form of accurate statistical estimates.
