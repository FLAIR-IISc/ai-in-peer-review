- Proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters approximately using BIC rather than factorized posteriors.
- Method is a reasonable, plausibly asymptotic extension of existing FAB work, yielding an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties.
- Main advantage is computational efficiency: much faster than sampling or variational Bayes approaches.
- Empirically achieves higher held-out/predictive likelihoods and faster runtime than several IBP implementations (Table 1).
- Novelty is incremental because the work mainly extends prior FAB papers [3,4].
- Exposition and mathematical formulations are imprecise and confusing in places.
- Equation (3) is problematic: log-sum over z can be infinite if all z are zero, which has non-zero probability under non-degenerate q — why FIC is not always infinite is unclear.
- Theorem 2 lacks clarity about the asymptotic regime and assumptions, specifically how K scales with N.
- Applying BIC per component appears to require fixed finite K, which contradicts the IBP’s infinite-component motivation.
- Minor technical errors: pk(X | z{.,k}) should condition only on data assigned to component k, and equation (4) is missing an expectation on the LHS.
- Stopping criterion for FAB is unspecified, making runtime comparisons and claims of orders-of-magnitude speedups hard to interpret.
- Improvements in predictive likelihood may be due to algorithmic factors (local optima, initialization) rather than the model selection criterion; Gibbs sampler learning too many components suggests implementation/local-optima issues.
- Conclusion: approach is reasonable and computationally attractive but incremental, with imprecise math and insufficient analysis to fully explain experimental gains.
