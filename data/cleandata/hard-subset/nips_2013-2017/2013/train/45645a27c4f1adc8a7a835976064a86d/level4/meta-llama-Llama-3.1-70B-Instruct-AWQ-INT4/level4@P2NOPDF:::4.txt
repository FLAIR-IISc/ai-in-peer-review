This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models, building upon the success of FAB in mixture models and hidden Markov models. FAB is a model selection method that maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. However, the applicability of FAB has been limited to models where the Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood is block diagonal. The authors overcome this limitation by deriving a lower bound of the FIC for latent feature models, demonstrating that it shares the same representation as the FIC for mixture models.

The authors evaluate their proposed method, FAB/LFM, on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing its performance to other methods such as fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet process, variational Bayes, and maximum-expectation IBP. The results show that FAB/LFM outperforms these methods not only in prediction tasks but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well-written, with a clear and thorough description of the derivation steps of the proposed method. While the extension of FAB from mixture models to latent feature models can be considered incremental, it is an interesting and efficient contribution. The results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, and its relevance to the NIPS community is evident. Overall, this is a nice paper that presents an interesting idea, and I support its acceptance.

Minor suggestions for improvement:

* Consider adding a brief introduction to the FAB method and its significance in the context of model selection.
* Provide more details about the experimental setup and the datasets used for evaluation.
* Discuss the implications of the results and the potential applications of the proposed method in various fields.
* Consider adding a conclusion section to summarize the main contributions and findings of the paper.
