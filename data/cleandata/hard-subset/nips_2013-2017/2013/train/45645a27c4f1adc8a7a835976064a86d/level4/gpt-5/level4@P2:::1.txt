Summary and relation to prior work
The paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features. The core idea is to derive a factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs by analyzing the asymptotics of the Hessian of the complete log-likelihood. Despite LFMs violating the block-diagonal/Hessian assumptions previously used for mixture models (MMs), the authors show that the resulting FIC for LFMs has the same functional form as in MMs, producing automatic feature shrinkage via log sum_n z_nk terms. This yields an EM-like algorithm that alternates between a mean-field E-step (interpreted as CCCP updates) and closed-form M-steps, with an acceleration scheme for early shrinkage and a post-hoc merge step motivated by an identifiability analysis. Empirically, the method (FAB/LFM) is compared to several IBP-based baselines (Gibbs, VB, MEIBP) and a variational EM LFM, showing improvements in wall-clock time and predictive log-likelihood in several datasets.

The work builds directly on prior FAB results for MMs and HMMs, and connects to nonparametric Bayesian LFMs such as the IBP and its scalable variants (accelerated Gibbs, VB, MEIBP), as well as related asymptotic perspectives (e.g., MAD-Bayes).

Strengths
- Conceptual: Clear extension of the FAB/FIC machinery to a broader class (LFMs) where the standard block-diagonal Hessian argument does not hold, supported by an asymptotic determinant result (Lemma 1).
- Practical: An EM-like optimization that avoids the computational burden of MCMC or full variational treatments, plus useful engineering refinements (CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration; merge step).
- Empirical: Competitive or superior predictive likelihood with substantially lower runtime in several settings; automatic selection of K without hand-tuned hyperparameters; improved robustness to overfitting indicated by small TLL–PLL gaps.

Weaknesses and questions
- Asymptotic regime and assumptions: Theorem 2 states that the marginal likelihood “can be asymptotically approximated as” a specific criterion, but the precise asymptotic regime, regularity conditions, and treatment of K are not fully specified. Does K remain fixed as N grows, or can K = K(N) increase? The argument appears to require that the number of activations for each feature diverges, which suggests fixed finite K. This tension should be reconciled explicitly with the IBP context, where the number of features can grow with N.
- Behavior when features are unused: The FIC includes terms like log(sum_n z_nk); if a feature is never used (sum_n z_nk = 0), this term is −∞. Since a non-degenerate q assigns nonzero probability to such events, why is Eq[log(sum_n z_nk)] finite? The paper later removes always-off/always-on features and uses linearization around π̃k > 0, but the precise conditions ensuring finiteness of the bound (and monotone increases) should be stated clearly.
- Clarity/precision of derivations:
  - In the MM discussion, pk(X | z·,k) should depend only on data assigned to component k (i.e., the subset with z_nk = 1), not the entire dataset. Clarifying the notation would help.
  - In Eq. (4), the left-hand side appears to be missing the expectation operator (it should bound Eq[log(sum_n z_nk)]).
  - Please state all assumptions required for Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 in the main text (or give a concise summary) so that the scope of the results is clear without consulting the supplement.
- Experimental protocol and fairness:
  - Stopping criteria: The FAB stopping rule is described (relative improvement threshold), but it is unclear how many CCCP inner steps are used per outer iteration in practice (beyond Tshrink), and how stopping was set for each baseline (especially the Gibbs sampler). Without standardized stopping rules (e.g., fixed wall-clock budgets, ESS targets, or convergence diagnostics), runtime comparisons are hard to interpret.
  - Sensitivity to initialization and local optima: Predictive gains might reflect better local optima rather than the model selection criterion per se. It would strengthen the claims to report sensitivity to initialization, multiple restarts, and ablations that hold K fixed across methods (or give each method the same K) to disentangle selection vs. optimization effects.
  - Parameter learning: Some baselines use fixed noise variance while FAB learns it. This could advantage FAB. Please provide runs where all methods use the same fixed noise level, or where all learn it, to isolate the contribution of the selection criterion.

Quality
Technically sound overall, with a plausible and interesting asymptotic argument; however, key assumptions and regimes (growth of K, conditions ensuring finiteness of the FIC bound) should be made explicit. The algorithmic details are clear, but some derivations and notational choices could be tightened.

Clarity
The paper is generally well organized. The CCCP view is helpful. That said, several equations and assumptions need clearer statements (see points above), and some notational ambiguities (e.g., pk(X | z·,k)) should be corrected. Summarizing the main assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 in the main text would improve readability.

Originality
The work is a natural but nontrivial extension of prior FAB results to LFMs, notably without the block-diagonal Hessian condition. The CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability-driven merge step are useful refinements. Overall, the contribution feels incremental but meaningful.

Significance
If widely applicable, the result could broaden the use of FAB-style criteria to other latent variable models and offer a practical alternative to IBP-based inference in LFMs. The empirical gains are promising, but the interpretation would be stronger with the suggested ablations and clearer stopping protocols.

Suggestions for improvement
- State the asymptotic regime precisely: whether K is fixed, the growth rates of sum_n z_nk, and the conditions under which Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 hold.
- Clarify how the FIC avoids −∞ when features are unused under q, and how the shrinkage/removal steps interact with the bound’s validity and monotonicity.
- Fix notational issues in Section 2 and Eq. (4) and add a brief summary of the assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 in the main text.
- Standardize stopping criteria across methods (or report performance under fixed time budgets) and add ablations controlling for K, initialization, and noise variance learning.

Arguments for acceptance
- Extends FAB/FIC to LFMs with a principled asymptotic argument.
- Practical, scalable EM-like algorithm with effective shrinkage and competitive predictive performance.
- Potentially broad implications for model selection in other latent variable models.

Arguments against acceptance
- Asymptotic assumptions and regimes (especially the behavior of K) are not clearly articulated.
- Some mathematical details and notational choices are imprecise.
- Experimental comparisons would benefit from stronger controls and ablations to isolate the source of improvements.

Overall assessment
A sensible and practically valuable extension of FAB to LFMs with promising empirical results. The paper would be significantly strengthened by clarifying the asymptotic setup, tightening the derivations, and refining the experimental protocol to address fairness and interpretability.
