Summary:
This paper introduces an algorithmic approach to efficient model selection for latent feature models, leveraging factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). Notably, the authors extend FAB and FIC, previously applied to mixture models, to latent feature models by employing a mean field approximation for latent inference and accelerating the shrinkage of globally selected features. This method offers increased automation in model selection, minimizing manual intervention.

Quality:
The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of their method, benchmarking it against a diverse range of competing methods across multiple datasets. The references provided are adequate and sufficient.

Clarity:
The paper is well-structured and clearly written, facilitating easy comprehension of the proposed method and its underlying concepts.

Originality/Significance:
The work represents a logical and solid extension of existing research in model selection for latent feature models (LFMs) using FAB inference and FIC. The novelty lies in combining multiple approximations to create a cohesive method that yields promising results. However, it would be beneficial to investigate the limitations of these approximations, specifically how they might contribute to suboptimal performance when pushed to their limits, given the interplay of several approximations (FIC, mean field, and shrinkage) in the proposed method.
