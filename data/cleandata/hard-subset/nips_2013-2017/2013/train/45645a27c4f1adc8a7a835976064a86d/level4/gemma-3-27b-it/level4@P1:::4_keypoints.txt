Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).  
Addresses a key limitation of FAB by removing the Hessian block-diagonal requirement that previously restricted applicability.  
Derives a lower bound for the factorized information criterion (FIC) in LFMs and shows it has the same representation as for mixture models.  
Provides theoretical justification for applying FAB to LFMs despite violated original assumptions.  
Presents empirical comparisons on synthetic and real datasets against IBP Gibbs, variational Bayes, and MEIBP.  
Demonstrates superior predictive performance and computational efficiency for FAB/LFM in experiments.  
Paper is well-written with a thorough, accessible derivation of the method.  
Contribution is incremental (builds on existing FAB work) but removes an important restriction, making it a valuable step.  
Reviewer recommends acceptance based on convincing theoretical and empirical results.
