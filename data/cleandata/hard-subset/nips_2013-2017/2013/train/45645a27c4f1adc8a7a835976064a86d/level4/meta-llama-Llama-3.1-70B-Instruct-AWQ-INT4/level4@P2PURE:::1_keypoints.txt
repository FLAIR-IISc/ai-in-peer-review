- Proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models that extends variational Bayes by integrating out parameters with a BIC-style approximation.  
- Demonstrates improved held-out/predictive likelihoods and faster runtime compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Method is motivated by a plausible asymptotic argument and yields an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties, making it computationally more efficient than sampling or full VB.  
- Technical contributions are novel but incremental—essentially an extension of prior work ([3], [4]).  
- Exposition and math are sometimes imprecise (e.g., equation (3) suggests a log-sum divergence when all z values are zero, raising why FIC is not always infinite).  
- Theorem 2 lacks clarity about the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K scales with N; it appears to require fixed finite K, which conflicts with the infinite-component IBP motivation.  
- Minor technical issues: pk(X | z·,k) should only include data assigned to component k, and equation (4) is missing an expectation on the LHS.  
- Quantitative results (Table 1) show higher predictive likelihoods in less time, but the Gibbs sampler stopping criterion is not specified.  
- It’s unclear whether improvements come from the model-selection criterion or from algorithms avoiding worse local optima; implementation/initialization details may influence results.  
- Gibbs appears to learn excessively many components on some datasets, suggesting possible local-optima or implementation issues rather than a ground-truth likelihood advantage.  
- Overall assessment: reasonable and computationally appealing approach but somewhat incremental, with imprecise math and experimental analysis that needs further clarification.
