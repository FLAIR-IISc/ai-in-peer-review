- Proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models using BIC-style parameter integration instead of factorized variational posteriors.  
- Implements the criterion as a computationally efficient EM algorithm with complexity penalties, avoiding costly sampling or full variational inference.  
- Empirically reports improved held-out/predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes compared to existing IBP implementations.  
- Approach is well-motivated by an asymptotic argument and is a sensible research direction.  
- Technical contributions are incremental, building directly on the authors’ prior FAB work.  
- Presentation has ambiguous/imprecise notation that needs clarification.  
- Equation (3) contains a log-sum that becomes infinite when all z are zero; the paper does not explain why this does not yield an infinite FIC score.  
- Theorem 2 lacks details on the asymptotic regime and assumptions, specifically how K (number of components) scales with N.  
- Applying BIC per component implies a fixed finite K (requiring infinite activations per feature as N grows), which conflicts with the IBP motivation of unbounded feature discovery.  
- Minor technical issues: p_k(X | z·,k) should be computed only on data assigned to component k, and equation (4) appears to be missing an expectation operator.  
- Table 1 shows better predictive likelihoods and faster completion times, but interpretation is limited by an unspecified Gibbs sampler stopping criterion.  
- Unclear whether performance gains stem from the new model selection criterion or from differences in convergence/local optima; Gibbs often finds too many components, suggesting sensitivity to initialization/convergence.  
- Overall: conceptually sound with computational benefits, but incremental, with presentation ambiguities and insufficient experimental analysis to conclusively attribute improvements to the proposed criterion.
