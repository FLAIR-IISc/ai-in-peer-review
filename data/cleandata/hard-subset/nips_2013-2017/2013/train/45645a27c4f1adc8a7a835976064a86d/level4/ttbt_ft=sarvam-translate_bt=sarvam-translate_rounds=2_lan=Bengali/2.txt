Abstract:

The authors propose an algorithm/estimation method for efficient model selection for latent feature models using Factorized Aymptotic Bayesian (FAB) estimation and Factorized Information Criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC are well-known hybrid models, where the latter estimates the log likelihood of a model through factorization and Laplace approximation, which is useful for easy model selection. One of the key contributions is the generalization of FAB for latent feature models, which uses a min-field approximation and rapid shrinking of the global number of selected features to estimate the latent variables.

A feature of this method is the automation of the model selection process, which requires minimal manual intervention. The results are shown in synthetic and real data and show significant improvements in computational efficiency.

Quality:

The presented results include thorough analysis and the method is evaluated by comparing it with various competing methods in multiple data sets. Adequate references are provided.

Clarity:

The paper is clearly written and well-structured.

Originality/Importance:

It seems to be a logical extension and rigorous generalization of previous work for model selection for LFM using FAB estimation and FIC. In particular, a new method has been created through several approximate combinations, which works well.

It is important to understand when/how these approximations can lead to poor performance, i.e., the method "falls" until it breaks down, as several approximations (FIC, min-field, shrinking) play a role at the same time.
