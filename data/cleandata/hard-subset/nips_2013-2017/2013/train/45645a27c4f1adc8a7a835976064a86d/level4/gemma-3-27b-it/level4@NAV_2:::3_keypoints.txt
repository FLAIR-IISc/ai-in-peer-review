- Presents an EM-based inference algorithm using Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) shrinkage for binary latent feature models to learn features z and infer K.
- Novelty: application of the FAB framework to latent feature models is new, distinct from IBP-based nonparametrics.
- Empirically outperforms Gibbs, variational, and MAP methods on some large datasets in runtime and inference quality.
- Technical soundness and algorithmic details appear reasonable and correct.
- Method scales to large datasets and jointly infers the number of latent features, which is appealing.
- Paper is reasonably clear methodologically but several experimental details are unclear or under-specified.
- No comprehensive discussion of algorithmic limitations or scenarios where it may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling.
- Convergence issues of the EM-like procedure are not addressed; no guarantees or sensitivity analyses to initialization are provided.
- Method is limited to binary LFMs and cannot be directly applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA; potential for generalization is not discussed.
- Experimental evaluation lacks thoroughness: missing standard deviations across runs and more rigorous controls.
- Behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotic arguments may fail) is unclear and not explored.
- Several recent works on efficient inference for LFMs are omitted from the related work discussion.
- MEIBP baseline is inappropriately used on datasets where its non-negativity constraint on W is violated; synthetic data should respect MEIBP’s assumptions.
- Baseline comparisons may be unfair (e.g., IBP Gibbs noise setting) and some VB-IBP experiments used finite truncation rather than infinite variational versions; experimental settings lack detail (e.g., restarts).
- Reported ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data is suspicious and likely due to poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance.
- Reference [21] is incorrectly described as Gibbs sampling when it actually describes MAP inference; must be corrected.
- Paper should cite and discuss related work such as MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) on small-variance asymptotics for nonparametric LFMs.
- Minor comment: suggested noise std-dev setting for real data should follow prior recommendations (0.25× data std) rather than 0.75 fixed value.
- After author feedback: some clarifications provided, but experimental methodology, baseline settings, and key references still need substantial improvement.
- Overall assessment: promising core idea but reject with request for major revisions due to insufficient experimental rigor and clarity.
