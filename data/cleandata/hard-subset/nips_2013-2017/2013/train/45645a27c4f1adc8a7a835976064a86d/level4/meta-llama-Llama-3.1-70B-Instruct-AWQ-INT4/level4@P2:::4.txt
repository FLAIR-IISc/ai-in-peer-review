This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), a significant advancement of the FAB method, which has shown promising results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. FAB is a model selection method that maximizes a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood.

The authors effectively address the limitation of FAB, which has only been applicable to models satisfying a specific condition on the Hessian matrix of a complete log-likelihood. They derive a lower bound of FIC for LFMs and demonstrate that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs. This achievement enables the application of FAB to a broader range of models.

The paper provides a thorough description of the derivation steps and presents results on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The comparison of FAB/LFM to other methods, such as fast Gibbs sampling in models using the Indian Buffet process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP), illustrates the superiority of FAB/LFM. The proposed method achieves better performance not only in the prediction task but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method. While the extension of FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs may be considered incremental, it is a significant contribution to the field. The results are convincing, and the idea is of great interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a nice paper that presents an interesting idea, and I support its acceptance.
