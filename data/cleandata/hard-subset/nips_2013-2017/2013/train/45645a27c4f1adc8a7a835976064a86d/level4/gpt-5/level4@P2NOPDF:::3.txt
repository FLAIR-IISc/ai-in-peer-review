Summary:
The paper proposes an EM-style inference algorithm for binary latent feature models (LFMs), where each observation xn is associated with a binary vector zn ∈ {0,1}^K indicating the presence of K latent features. The goal is to infer the feature assignments z for all observations, along with other model parameters, while also determining the appropriate number of features K. The method employs Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference as a shrinkage-based model selection criterion, rather than using Bayesian nonparametric priors such as the Indian Buffet Process (IBP). FAB approximates the log marginal likelihood as the expected complete log-likelihood plus a model selection penalty that encourages pruning of unnecessary features, starting from an upper bound on K and shrinking during optimization. On several large datasets, the proposed approach reportedly outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and IBP MAP baselines in both runtime and inference quality.

Quality:
The technical development appears sound, and the algorithmic details are coherent. The ability to scale to large datasets while automatically selecting K is a strong practical advantage.

Clarity:
The methodological presentation is generally clear. However, aspects of the experimental setup and baseline configurations lack sufficient detail (see below).

Originality:
FAB has been previously developed for mixture models and HMMs. Applying FAB to LFMs is, to my knowledge, novel and of independent interest.

Significance:
Efficient inference in LFMs with automatic determination of K is an important problem, and the proposed method is potentially impactful if empirically validated more thoroughly.

Weak Points:
- Lack of discussion of limitations: Under what conditions might the method underperform compared to standard Gibbs sampling or variational IBP (e.g., highly multimodal posteriors, strong feature correlations)?
- Convergence issues are not addressed: As an EM-like procedure with nonconvex objectives, the algorithm may be sensitive to initialization and local optima. Practical guidance or diagnostics would help.
- Scope restriction: The method targets binary LFMs and does not directly extend to continuous latent factor models (e.g., factor analysis, probabilistic PCA).
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete and, in places, inappropriate (details below). More careful comparisons and ablations are needed.
- Small-sample behavior is unclear: Since FAB relies on asymptotic arguments, performance in low-data regimes should be discussed or empirically examined.
- Missing related work: Recent advances on efficient inference in LFMs are not adequately covered (see below).
- MEIBP baseline misuse: The MEIBP algorithm in [22] assumes a nonnegative linear-Gaussian model (entries of W must be nonnegative). The synthetic and block image experiments appear invalid for MEIBP unless the data-generating W is constrained to be nonnegative. Comparisons under violated assumptions are not meaningful.

Other Comments:
- Block-image experiments: The FAB method is given the true noise variance. For a fair comparison, the IBP Gibbs sampler should also use the same noise variance rather than a 0.75× std-dev heuristic.
- VB-IBP baseline [2]: Consider the infinite variational formulation rather than a finite truncation. Also report experimental settings comprehensively (e.g., truncation level if used, initialization, number of random restarts, stopping criteria).
- Sonar dataset: It is surprising that accelerated Gibbs sampling discovers ~70 latent features in 49 dimensions. This likely reflects suboptimal initialization and/or mis-specified noise variance. Please investigate and report sensitivities.
- Line 70: Reference [21] performs MAP inference for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling. Please correct the text.
- Related work: Please discuss small-variance asymptotics for nonparametric LFMs, e.g., MAD-Bayes: MAP-based Asymptotic Derivations from Bayes (ICML 2013), and clarify conceptual and practical differences with FAB.

Minor Comments:
- Line 349: For real data, [1,22] recommend setting the noise std-dev to 0.25 times the empirical std-dev of examples across dimensions, not 0.75.

Comments after author feedback:
The feedback addressed several questions, but important issues remain before acceptance:
- Experimental methodology needs clearer, justified configurations for both the proposed method and baselines. Explain why certain baselines behave as observed (e.g., fixed alpha in Gibbs sampling leading to overestimation of K, or mis-specified noise variance/hyperparameters).
- Correct the description of [21] as MAP inference, not sampling.
- Include and discuss the MAD-Bayes reference for IBP-related inference.
Overall, the proposed FAB-based LFM inference is interesting, scales well, and learns K automatically. With a more rigorous and fair experimental evaluation, clearer baseline configurations, and discussion of limitations and convergence behavior, the paper would be substantially strengthened.
