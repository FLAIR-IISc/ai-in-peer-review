- Introduces an EM-based FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that uses shrinkage to infer feature assignments and automatically prune the number of active features K.
- FAB avoids sparsity-inducing priors (unlike IBP) by approximating log marginal likelihood with an expected complete log-likelihood plus a parsimony penalty, enabling starting from a large K and automatic pruning.
- Experiments claim FAB outperforms IBP methods (Gibbs, VB, MAP) in runtime and inference quality on large datasets.
- Technical exposition is sound and clear; applying FAB to latent feature models is a novel and practically significant contribution, especially for scalability.
- Comparison to MEIBP is invalid because MEIBP assumes non-negative loading matrices while the experiments used real-valued W; synthetic data should have non-negative W for a fair comparison.
- Baseline settings are unfair: FAB was given the true noise variance while the Gibbs sampler used a heuristic; all methods should be given equal information or equally tuned hyperparameters.
- Experimental details for VB-IBP are insufficient—paper must state whether finite or infinite variational version was used and report critical settings like number of restarts.
- Some baseline results are suspicious (e.g., Gibbs discovering ~70 features on 49-d Sonar), suggesting potential implementation or hyperparameter issues that need investigation and explanation.
- Paper lacks analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and reasons why baseline algorithms performed poorly in experiments.
- Missing discussion of the proposed method’s limitations: cases where it may underperform relative to Gibbs, potential convergence issues of the EM-like procedure, and behavior in small-data regimes given FAB’s asymptotic basis.
- The method’s restriction to binary latent features (vs. more general factor analysis/PCA models) is not sufficiently acknowledged or discussed.
- Incorrect citation: reference [21] is misdescribed—it performs MAP estimation for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling.
- Missing related work: should discuss small-variance asymptotic approaches such as MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013).
- Minor correction: the heuristic for setting real-data noise std in [1,22] should be 0.25×data std across dimensions, not a fixed 0.75.
- Overall conclusion: method is interesting and scalable but the experimental evaluation contains significant flaws that must be addressed before acceptance.
