This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection method demonstrating strong performance with mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models by maximizing a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of existing FAB methods is applicability restricted to models where the Hessian matrix of the complete log-likelihood is block diagonal. This work overcomes this limitation by extending FAB to LFMs, despite the violation of this Hessian condition. The authors successfully derive a lower bound on the FIC for LFMs, demonstrating its identical representation to the FIC for MMs. Empirical evaluation on both synthetic and real-world datasets compares FAB/LFM against competing methods including Indian Buffet Process implementations utilizing fast Gibbs sampling, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). Results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, with the proposed method achieving improved performance in prediction and exhibiting favorable computational efficiency.

The paper is well-written and provides a thorough description of the method's derivation. The extension of FAB from MMs to LFMs is incremental but significant, and the experimental results support the efficacy of the proposed approach. While my expertise does not lie directly within this specific domain, I believe this work would be of substantial interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a compelling idea with convincing results, and I recommend its acceptance.
