- Proposes an efficient model-selection algorithm for latent feature models by generalizing Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC).  
- Uses a mean-field approximation for latent feature inference combined with accelerated shrinkage of the global number of features.  
- High degree of automation reduces the need for manual tuning.  
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Evaluation is thorough and rigorous, comparing against a wide range of competing approaches and providing sufficient references.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, making contributions easy to follow.  
- Novelty lies in combining several approximation techniques into a new, effective generalization of prior FAB/FIC work.  
- Recommends analyzing potential failure modes and limits arising from the interplay of FIC, mean-field approximation, and shrinkage.
