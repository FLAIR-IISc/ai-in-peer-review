- Proposal: Extends FAB and FIC to latent feature models using mean-field latent approximations, Laplace-approximated FIC, and a global shrinkage mechanism to prune features automatically and efficiently.  
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments on synthetic and real data showing substantial computational speedups, minimal tuning, and competitive accuracy against many baselines.  
- Clarity: Manuscript is clearly written and well organized; FAB, FIC, mean-field inference, and shrinkage are explained coherently.  
- Originality/significance: A logical yet nontrivial generalization of FAB to LFMs; combining FIC, mean-field inference, and accelerated shrinkage is technically sound and practically valuable.  
- Concern — approximation limits: Need clarification on regimes where Laplace/FIC approximations may fail (e.g., small samples, highly correlated features, non-Gaussian posteriors).  
- Concern — mean-field bias: Request analysis of how the mean-field assumption affects posterior bias and feature recovery when latent-feature dependencies are strong.  
- Concern — shrinkage failure modes: Ask for discussion of when shrinkage might over-prune (underfit) or under-prune (overfit) and for diagnostics/safeguards.  
- Suggestion — ablations/sensitivity: Include ablation studies isolating FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage contributions and sensitivity analyses to initializations and hyperparameters.  
- Suggestion — computational details: Provide per-iteration cost, memory footprint, convergence behavior, and matched wall-clock comparisons to clarify resource use.  
- Suggestion — stress tests: Recommend stress-testing on known challenging cases to identify when and how performance degrades.  
- Overall verdict: A clear, well-executed, and useful extension of FAB to LFMs with strong empirical support; addressing the above analyses would further strengthen the paper.
