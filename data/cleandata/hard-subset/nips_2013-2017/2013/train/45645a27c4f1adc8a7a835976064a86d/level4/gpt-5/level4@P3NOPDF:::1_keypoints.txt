- Proposes a BIC-style model selection criterion for binary latent feature models, yielding an EM-like algorithm with added complexity penalties.
- Empirically outperforms several IBP baselines in held-out likelihood and is faster than sampling/variational approaches.
- Strength: clear motivation to replace expensive parameter posteriors with a BIC-like approximation and simple EM-like procedure.
- Strength: shows empirical promise with higher predictive likelihood and substantial speedups in some cases.
- Strength: practical and straightforward to implement and scale.
- Concern: Theorem 2 lacks a clear asymptotic regime—what tends to infinity (N, Nk, both) must be specified.
- Concern: Unclear how K behaves with N; proof seems to require fixed finite K with Nk → ∞, but IBP implies K grows with N.
- Concern: Need justification for applying the per-feature BIC penalty when K grows like O(α log N) under IBP and when Nk are small or heterogeneous.
- Concern: Equation (3) and log(Nk)-type penalties are undefined for Nk=0; formalize handling of zero-activation features (pruning or ε).
- Concern: Clarify treatment and higher-order corrections for very small Nk and whether the approximation remains valid.
- Concern: Technical novelty appears incremental relative to prior FAB work; specify exact extensions for binary features/IBP and where it reduces to or diverges from FAB.
- Empirical issue: Gains may stem from optimization/implementation, not the criterion—stopping criteria and matched budgets are not clearly reported.
- Empirical issue: Provide predictive-likelihood vs wall-clock curves and matched stopping rules across methods.
- Empirical issue: Run ablations (EM with/without penalty), multiple restarts, and report mean/variance to assess local optima effects.
- Empirical issue: Use synthetic data with known ground truth to evaluate feature recovery, K estimation, and scaling with N.
- Empirical issue: Compare to collapsed samplers/advanced variational methods under equal compute budgets and check Gibbs mixing/hyperparameter effects.
- Empirical issue: Report learned K and activation histograms; correlate predictive gains with K and sparsity differences.
- Empirical issue: State how α and other priors are set/learned and ensure fairness across methods.
- Clarity: Section 2’s factorization is unclear—p_k should condition only on data assigned to feature k if no coupling is intended.
- Clarity: Equation (4) likely needs an expectation operator; define q precisely and all optimized quantities in E/M steps.
- Notation: Define Nk, K+, z·,k and other symbols at first use; fix typos and inconsistencies.
- Suggestions: Provide exact asymptotic statement for Theorem 2 with remainder rates and conditions for growing K.
- Suggestions: Explicitly formalize pruning/threshold rules and handling of zero/rarely-activated features.
- Suggestions: Add ablation and sensitivity studies, convergence/time plots, and matched compute budgets for baselines.
- Minor: Fix divergence by adding small constant inside logs or defining domain; correct typos (“quantiative” → “quantitative”).
- Overall: Promising, practical contribution but theoretical assumptions, edge-case definitions, and empirical controls are insufficient; current form reads as incremental FAB extension and warrants weak reject unless revised.
