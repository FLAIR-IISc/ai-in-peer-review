This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models. FAB is a recent model selection method that has demonstrated strong performance on mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models by maximizing a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of the original FAB method is its applicability only to models where the Hessian of the complete likelihood is block diagonal. The authors address this limitation by successfully extending FAB to latent feature models (LFMs), despite this condition not being met. They derive a lower bound on the FIC for LFMs and demonstrate that it shares the same form as the FIC for MMs. The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, with comparisons to alternative approaches including fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet Process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, showing improved performance not only in prediction accuracy but also in computational efficiency. 

The paper is clearly written, and the authors provide a comprehensive description of the derivation. This work represents an incremental extension of FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs, but it is a compelling contribution, and the results validate its effectiveness. While I am not an expert in this specific area, I believe this work would be of considerable interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a novel and interesting idea, supported by convincing results, and I recommend its acceptance.
