- Summary: Extends FAB inference from mixture/HMMs to latent feature models, showing FIC for LFMs matches MMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessian, and derives a FAB/LFM algorithm with automatic feature selection and CCCP-based shrinkage-acceleration.  
- Strength: Clear theoretical contribution proving FIC equivalence between LFMs and MMs without the prior Hessian condition.  
- Strength: Practical algorithm that automatically determines the number of latent features and enforces parameter identifiability.  
- Strength: CCCP interpretation yields an effective shrinkage-acceleration scheme that reduces computational cost.  
- Strength: Empirical results on synthetic and real data show strong model selection, predictive performance, and competitive or superior runtime versus IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Strength: Paper is well written with thorough, transparent derivations.  
- Weakness: Contribution is incremental—an extension of FAB to LFMs rather than a fundamentally new modeling framework.  
- Quality: Technically sound asymptotic analysis and carefully derived algorithm supported by comprehensive experiments.  
- Clarity: Manuscript is clearly written and well organized, with clear descriptions of algorithmic components.  
- Originality: Incremental but meaningful; the Hessian/FIC insight is noteworthy.  
- Significance: Automated model selection for LFMs with improved efficiency and accuracy is valuable to the community.  
- Pros for acceptance: Strong theory, automatic feature selection/identifiability, effective acceleration, convincing experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Cons for acceptance: Primarily an extension of existing methods, so limited novelty.  
- Recommendation: Support acceptance—well-executed, convincing theoretical and practical contributions.
