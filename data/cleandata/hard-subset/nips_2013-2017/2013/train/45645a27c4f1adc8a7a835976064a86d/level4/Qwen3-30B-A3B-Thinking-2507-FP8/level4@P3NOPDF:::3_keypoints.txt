- Proposes an EM-based FAB inference algorithm for binary LFMs that infers latent features and K without explicit priors, claiming improved scalability and inference quality.  
- Technical approach is sound and practically significant due to automatic K-selection and large-scale applicability.  
- Methodology is well-explained, but experimental details lack clarity.  
- Original contribution: applying FAB to LFMs is novel as an extension of the FAB framework.  
- Missing limitations discussion: no analysis of scenarios where FAB underperforms (e.g., small-n regimes or violated asymptotic assumptions).  
- Convergence concerns: no discussion of convergence properties or initialization sensitivity; anomalous results (70 features on 49-dim Sonar) suggest instability.  
- Scope limitation: method is restricted to binary LFMs and does not generalize to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Flawed experiment — MEIBP baseline: MEIBP requires positive W but experiments used real-valued W, invalidating the comparison.  
- Flawed experiment — IBP Gibbs fairness: Gibbs used heuristic σ while FAB used true σ, making comparisons unfair.  
- Flawed experiment — VB-IBP implementation: used finite variational variant instead of infinite; missing details on restarts, hyperparameters, and convergence criteria.  
- Omitted related work: fails to cite or compare to MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) on small-variance asymptotics for IBP.  
- Other comments: mischaracterizes reference [21] (MAP vs Gibbs) and did not follow or justify suggested noise-estimation heuristics on real data.  
- Required revisions: rerun or remove MEIBP baseline with positive W; ensure equal noise parameterization; provide full experimental settings and convergence info; discuss limitations/convergence; cite MAD-Bayes and correct references.  
- Recommendation: technically valid but experimental flaws are critical—currently a weak reject until the methodological and evaluation issues are resolved.
