Summary:
The authors propose an algorithm/approximation method for efficient model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) using factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC are well known for mixture models, where FIC approximates a model’s log marginal likelihood via factorization and a Laplace approximation to enable tractable model selection. The main contribution is the generalization of FAB to LFMs, employing a mean-field approximation for latent-variable inference and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism on the global number of selected features. A key advantage is increased automation of model selection with minimal hand-tuning. Results on synthetic and real datasets show significant gains in computational efficiency.

Quality:
The experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing the proposed method against a wide range of competing approaches across multiple datasets. The analysis is careful, and the references are sufficient.

Clarity:
The paper is clearly written and well structured.

Originality/Significance:
This work is a logical extension and solid generalization of prior efforts on model selection in LFMs using FAB and FIC, achieved by combining several approximations (FIC, mean field, and shrinkage) into a single, effective method.

Suggestions/Concerns:
It would be valuable to understand when and how these approximations might lead to poor performance—i.e., to “push” the method until it breaks—given that several approximations (FIC, mean field, and shrinkage) operate simultaneously.
