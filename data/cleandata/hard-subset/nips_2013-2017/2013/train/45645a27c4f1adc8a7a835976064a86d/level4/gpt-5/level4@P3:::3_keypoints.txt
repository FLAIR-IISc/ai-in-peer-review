- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models (LFMs), deriving an FIC objective for LFMs despite coupled parameters.  
- Main theoretical contribution: asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) showing the same FIC form arises as in mixtures, with shrinkage-inducing log-sum term and identifiability results.  
- Algorithm: EM-like procedure with mean-field E-step, CCCP interpretation motivating shrinkage-acceleration, and a post-hoc merge step justified by identifiability.  
- Compared to IBP/Gibbs, VB, and MEIBP, FAB is prior-free, selects K automatically, and is designed for scalability.  
- Empirics: FAB is competitive or superior in predictive performance and model selection on synthetic, image, and real datasets, often faster thanks to shrinkage acceleration.  
- Strength — Theory: Clear, nontrivial asymptotic derivations and corollary establishing FIC tightness; identifiability/merge criterion well-motivated.  
- Strength — Algorithm: Monotone lower-bound optimization, interpretable CCCP view, practical shrinkage routine that removes irrelevant features early; natural stopping criteria.  
- Strength — Empirics: Demonstrates scalability and efficiency; shrinkage mechanism yields substantial speedups.  
- Limitation — Scope: Method targets binary LFMs with Gaussian noise and diagonal precision; does not handle real-valued latents, non-Gaussian likelihoods, or structured priors — discuss/extensions needed.  
- Limitation — Small-N behavior: Asymptotic reliance raises concerns for small datasets; paper lacks systematic analysis or experiments showing when FAB underperforms IBP/VB.  
- Limitation — Convergence & initialization: Nonconvex procedure sensitive to initialization and Tshrink; discuss sensitivity, heuristics (restarts/annealing), and whether merges always increase the bound.  
- Missing detail — Assumptions: Key “mild assumptions” enabling determinant decomposition in Lemma 1 are only in supplement; state them in main text.  
- Baseline fairness — MEIBP: Some comparisons violate MEIBP’s nonnegativity assumptions; either use nonnegative W for MEIBP or omit those comparisons and explain.  
- Baseline fairness — Noise variance & hyperparams: FAB sometimes uses true/tuned noise variance while baselines use heuristics; align noise settings and document hyperparameter tuning for fairness.  
- Baseline fairness — VB-IBP: Clarify why infinite variational IBP was not used, and report VB restarts/sensitivity and all prior/hyperparameter settings.  
- Related work: Discuss small-variance asymptotics for LFMs (MAD-Bayes, Broderick et al. 2013) and clarify similarities/differences to FAB.  
- Citation/error: Correct description of MEIBP as a MAP/submodular method (not sampling) and fix the noise-setting heuristic citations.  
- Quality: Derivations are sound and interesting, but some experimental controls (noise, priors, MEIBP applicability) must be tightened to support claims.  
- Clarity: Method is mostly clear, but main text should surface key assumptions, per-iteration complexity (dependence on N, D, K), and full experimental hyperparameter/initialization details.  
- Originality: Novel and nontrivial extension of FAB to LFMs; CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability-driven merging are valuable.  
- Significance: Automatic K selection with a scalable, prior-free objective is valuable; tightened experiments and clarified limitations would increase practical impact.  
- Acceptance pros: Strong theoretical extension, scalable automatic feature selection, and competitive empirical performance with speedups.  
- Acceptance cons: Some unfair baseline comparisons, insufficient discussion of small-N/convergence issues, and limited applicability beyond binary Gaussian LFMs.  
- Minor fixes: Clarify preprocessing and noise protocols, use consistent notation, provide code/appendix for reproducibility, and include MAD-Bayes reference.  
- Post-rebuttal recommendations: Correct/omit invalid MEIBP comparisons, align noise/hyperparameter settings across methods and explain anomalies, add brief assumptions and small-N discussion, and fix MEIBP citation/description.
