In this paper, the authors extend the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference method to latent feature models (LFMs), building on its successful application to mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. FAB is a recently developed model selection approach that maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. However, the original FAB method has a limitation: it is only applicable to models where the Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood is block diagonal. The authors overcome this limitation by effectively deriving a lower bound of the FIC for LFMs, showing that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs.

The authors evaluate their proposed FAB/LFM method using both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing its performance to other approaches such as fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet Process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, with the proposed method achieving better performance in both prediction tasks and computational cost.

The paper is well-written, with the authors providing a thorough description of the derivation steps for their method. While the proposed model can be considered an incremental extension of FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs, it is an interesting and efficient contribution. Although I am not an expert in this area, I believe that this work would be of great interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a nice paper that presents a compelling idea, and the results are convincing. I support its acceptance.
