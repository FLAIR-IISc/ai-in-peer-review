- Proposes a model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models that approximates parameter integration with BIC instead of a factorized variational posterior.  
- Reports improved held-out likelihoods compared to existing IBP implementations.  
- Primary computational advantage is an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties, avoiding expensive sampling or variational inference.  
- Technical contribution is incremental, mainly extending the existing FAB framework.  
- Equation (3) is problematic: the log-sum-exp over all-zero z would diverge, implying the FIC score should be infinite—this is not addressed.  
- Theorem 2 omits key details: asymptotic regime, assumptions, and how K scales with N; it implicitly assumes fixed K, conflicting with the IBP’s infinite-component motivation.  
- Minor errors: p_k(X | z_{·,k}) should condition only on data assigned to component k, and Equation (4) lacks an expectation on the LHS.  
- Quantitative results show faster convergence and higher predictive likelihoods, but runtime comparisons are ambiguous due to no standardized Gibbs stopping criterion.  
- The source of the performance gap is unclear: improvements may stem from the criterion or from Gibbs sampling getting trapped in poor local optima (e.g., excessive component counts), rather than a true superiority of the method.  
- Overall assessment: conceptually reasonable but incremental, with imprecise mathematics and insufficient theoretical/empirical analysis; requires stronger grounding to justify novelty.
