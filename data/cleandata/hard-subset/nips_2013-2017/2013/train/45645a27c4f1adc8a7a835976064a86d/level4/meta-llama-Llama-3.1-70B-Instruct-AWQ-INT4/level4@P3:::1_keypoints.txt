- Proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models as an extension of the FAB framework, showing improved held-out likelihood compared to several IBP implementations.
- Approach is motivated by an asymptotic argument and yields a computationally efficient EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties instead of costly sampling/variational methods.
- Technical contributions are novel but incremental, building on prior FAB work.
- Paper gives a clear, concise introduction to FAB and a thorough analysis of the method and its theoretical properties.
- Exposition issues: equation (3)’s log-sum over z can be infinite when all values are zero, and the paper should clarify why the FIC score is not always infinite.
- Theorem 2 is underspecified: the asymptotic regime and assumptions (e.g., how K scales with N) are not stated, and assuming finite K conflicts with the IBP motivation.
- Minor errors: in Section 2, pk(X|z·k) should sum only over points assigned to component k; equation (4) should include an expectation operator.
- Experimental results (Table 1) show higher predictive likelihoods in less time, but the Gibbs sampler stopping criterion is not specified, making timing and comparisons unclear.
- Unclear source of predictive gains: need evidence that improvements come from the model-selection criterion rather than algorithmic issues or implementation/initialization differences.
- Strengths: computational efficiency, improved held-out likelihood, clear exposition of FAB, and thorough theoretical/related-work analysis.
- Weaknesses: clarity/exposition problems, unclear asymptotic assumptions in Theorem 2, unspecified Gibbs stopping criterion, and unclear origin of performance improvements.
- For acceptance: method is novel and empirically better, with solid analysis; against acceptance: unresolved clarity and theoretical-assumption issues and missing experimental details.
