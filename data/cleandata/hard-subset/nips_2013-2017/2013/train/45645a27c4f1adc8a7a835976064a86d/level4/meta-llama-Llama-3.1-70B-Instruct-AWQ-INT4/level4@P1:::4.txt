This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently-developed model selection method that has achieved good results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. In essence, this method maximizes a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood. The limitation of FAB is that it has only been applicable to models satisfying the condition that the Hessian matrix of a complete likelihood should be block diagonal. The authors extend FAB to LFMs, despite the fact that this condition is not satisfied.

They effectively derive a lower bound of FIC for LFMs and show that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs. They provide results on both synthetic and real-world datasets and compare FAB/LFM to other methods, such as fast Gibbs sampling in models that use the Indian Buffet process, models that use variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results illustrate the superiority of FAB/LFM; the proposed method claims better performance not only in the prediction task but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well-written, and the authors thoroughly describe the derivation steps of their method. The proposed model extends FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs and could be considered incremental. It is quite interesting, and the results underline its efficiency. Although I am not an expert in this area, I think that it would be of great interest to the NIPS community. All in all, it is a nice paper that presents a really interesting idea. The results are convincing, and I support its acceptance.
