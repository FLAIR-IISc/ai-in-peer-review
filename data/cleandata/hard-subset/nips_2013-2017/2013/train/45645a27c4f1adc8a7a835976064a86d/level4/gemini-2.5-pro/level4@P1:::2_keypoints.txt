- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), removing the block-diagonal Hessian requirement.  
- Provides a novel asymptotic analysis showing the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same tractable form as for mixture models.  
- Proposes the FAB/LFM algorithm that uses a mean-field approximation for latent features and an effective shrinkage acceleration technique.  
- Automatically infers the number of latent features with minimal hyperparameter tuning.  
- Theoretical support provided by Lemma 1 and Theorems 2 and 4, including an analysis of parameter identifiability and a principled post-processing merge step.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate major computational efficiency gains and competitive performance versus Indian Buffet Process–based methods.  
- Method is technically sound and thoroughly evaluated against a wide range of competing approaches.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and describes the algorithm with sufficient implementation detail.  
- Core technical contribution—the Hessian-based derivation of FIC for LFMs—is original, while combining known techniques (Laplace, mean-field, CCCP) effectively.  
- Significant practical impact: a fast, automated tool for model selection in LFMs likely useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- Area for improvement: discuss limitations and potential failure modes arising from combined approximations (asymptotic FIC, mean-field factorization, shrinkage heuristic).  
- Suggest investigating performance degradation in low-data regimes and on problems with strong posterior dependencies that violate mean-field assumptions.
