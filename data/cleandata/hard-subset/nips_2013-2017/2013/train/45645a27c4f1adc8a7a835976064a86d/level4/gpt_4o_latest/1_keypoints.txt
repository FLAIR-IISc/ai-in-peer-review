- Introduces a new model selection criterion for binary latent feature models, similar to variational Bayes but integrating parameters approximately via BIC.
- Empirically achieves improved held-out likelihoods compared to several IBP implementations.
- Method is supported by a plausible asymptotic argument.
- Main advantage is computational efficiency: effectively an EM algorithm with complexity penalties, avoiding costly sampling or full VB.
- Technical contributions are novel but incremental, building on the FAB framework from prior work.
- Exposition has imprecise mathematical formulations that reduce clarity.
- Equation (3) is problematic: log-sum over z can be infinite if all z are zero, which has nonzero probability under nondegenerate q.
- Theorem 2 lacks explicit asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K is treated as N grows.
- Assuming fixed finite K conflicts with the IBP’s infinite-K motivation, undermining theoretical justification.
- Minor errors: pk(X | z·,k) should condition only on data assigned to component k; equation (4) should include an expectation on the LHS.
- Table 1 shows higher predictive likelihoods and faster runtime for FAB, but stopping criteria and convergence rules are not specified.
- Unclear whether predictive gains come from the model selection criterion itself or from differences in convergence/local optima; Gibbs sometimes learns too many components.
- Overall: reasonable but incremental; mathematical precision and deeper experimental analysis are needed to substantiate claims.
