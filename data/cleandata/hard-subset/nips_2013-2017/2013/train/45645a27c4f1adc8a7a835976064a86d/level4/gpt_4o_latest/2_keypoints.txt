- Introduces an algorithm extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) to latent feature models using mean-field approximation and accelerated shrinkage for efficient model selection.  
- Primary contribution: generalizes FAB/FIC to latent feature models, integrating multiple approximations into a cohesive method.  
- Uses mean-field latent inference plus an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to reduce the global number of selected features.  
- Offers increased automation in model selection with minimal manual intervention.  
- Demonstrates computational efficiency and effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Results are well-supported, benchmarked against diverse competing approaches, and adequately referenced.  
- Paper is well-written and logically organized.  
- Suggestion/limitation: should test scenarios where combined approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage) may produce suboptimal or degraded performance to assess robustness.
