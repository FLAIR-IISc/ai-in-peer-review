In this paper, the authors extend factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models. FAB is a recently developed model selection method that has demonstrated strong performance with mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. Briefly, this method maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of FAB has been its applicability only to models where the Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood is block diagonal. The authors address this limitation by extending FAB to latent feature models (LFMs), despite the fact that this condition is not generally satisfied. They effectively derive a lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and demonstrate that it possesses the same representation as the FIC for MMs. The authors present results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM to alternative methods such as fast Gibbs sampling with Indian Buffet Process priors, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, with the proposed method achieving better performance not only in prediction tasks but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well-written, and the authors thoroughly describe the derivation of their method. The extension from FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs represents an incremental but valuable contribution. The idea is interesting, and the results support its efficiency. While I am not an expert in this specific area, I believe this work would be of significant interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a compelling idea. The results are convincing, and I support its acceptance.
