- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models and HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs).
- Addresses a key limitation of original FAB that required block-diagonal Hessian of the complete-data log-likelihood.
- Derives a lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.
- Shows the FIC lower bound for LFMs has the same functional form as the FIC for mixture models.
- FAB/LFM maximizes this FIC lower bound, which converges to the marginal log-likelihood.
- Provides comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Compares against relevant baselines: Fast Gibbs IBP, Variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP.
- Empirical results show better predictive performance and lower computational cost for FAB/LFM.
- Paper is well-written with thorough methodological derivations.
- Contribution may be incremental but is interesting, significant, and likely of high interest to the NIPS community; reviewer recommends acceptance.
