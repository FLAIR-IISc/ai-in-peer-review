- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), removing a previous block-diagonal Hessian requirement.  
- Shows LFMs exhibit a factorized information criterion (FIC) structure similar to mixture models, enabling FAB application.  
- Provides theoretical analysis and derives a lower bound for the FIC in LFMs, with Lemma 1 linking to FAB for MMs.  
- Proposes a CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration method to speed convergence.  
- Analyzes parameter identifiability to strengthen theoretical foundations.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show competitive model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency versus IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Positions the work within related LFM inference literature and contrasts with IBP and MAD-Bayes approaches.  
- Theoretical derivations appear rigorous and well-justified.  
- Extending FAB to LFMs is a novel and significant contribution.  
- Shrinkage acceleration yields practical computational improvements.  
- Experiments convincingly validate effectiveness and efficiency.  
- Paper is clearly written and explains complex concepts accessibly.  
- Computational cost comparisons lack hardware details and per-method runtime breakdowns.  
- Using fixed noise standard deviation for some competitors may bias comparisons; sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Merge post-processing lacks detail on overlap thresholds and their performance impact.  
- “Mild assumptions” in Lemma 1 are not explicitly stated or discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound extension with strong empirical support and practical acceleration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete computational comparisons, potential bias from fixed competitor parameters, and insufficient merge/post-processing detail.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommendation for acceptance.
