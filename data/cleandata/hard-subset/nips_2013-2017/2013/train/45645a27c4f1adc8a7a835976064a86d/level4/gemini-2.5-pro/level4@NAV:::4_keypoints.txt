- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs).
- FAB approximates marginal log-likelihood by maximizing a lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC).
- Original FAB required the complete-data likelihood Hessian to be block-diagonal, a key limitation.
- Main contribution: overcomes that limitation by deriving a novel FIC lower bound for LFMs.
- New bound yields a representation similar to FAB for Mixture Models.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets against IBP Gibbs, Variational Bayes, and MEIBP.
- Results show better predictive performance and lower computational cost than baselines.
- Paper is clearly written with thorough theoretical derivations.
- Contribution is incremental but non-trivial and of interest to the NeurIPS community.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
