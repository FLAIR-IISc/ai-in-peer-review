- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).
- FAB maximizes a lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC), which asymptotically converges to the marginal log-likelihood.
- Prior FAB required block-diagonal Hessian of complete-data likelihood; authors handle cases where this condition does not hold for LFMs.
- Derive a valid lower bound of the FIC for LFMs that has the same functional form as the FIC used for mixture models.
- Empirical comparisons (synthetic and real data) against IBP Gibbs, variational Bayes, and MEIBP show superior predictive performance and lower computational cost.
- Paper is clearly written with thorough, coherent derivations.
- Contribution is incremental (FAB/MMs → FAB/LFMs) but interesting and practically useful.
- Reviewer is not a primary expert in the area but believes the work will interest the NIPS community and supports acceptance.
