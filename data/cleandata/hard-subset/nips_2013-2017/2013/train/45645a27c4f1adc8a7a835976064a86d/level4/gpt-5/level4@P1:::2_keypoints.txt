- Extends FAB/FIC from mixtures/HMMs to latent feature models via asymptotic Hessian analysis showing the same FIC form as mixtures.  
- The derived FIC yields the same regularizer that enables automatic pruning of irrelevant features and supports parameter identifiability.  
- Interprets the FAB E-step as a CCCP, motivating a shrinkage-acceleration scheme that removes irrelevant features early to reduce computation.  
- Proposes an identifiability-guided merge post-processing step to handle redundant features.  
- Empirically compares to strong baselines (IBP Gibbs/VB, MEIBP, variational EM) on synthetic and real data.  
- Reports competitive or superior model selection and predictive performance with substantial computational speedups.  
- Method is automated with minimal hyperparameter tuning required.  
- Theoretical contributions (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) are central and well-motivated; proofs are in the supplement.  
- Optimization uses a tractable lower bound that increases monotonically; the CCCP interpretation is clear and useful.  
- Experiments are broad, references are sufficient, and figures/algorithm descriptions are clear.  
- The generalization of FAB/FIC to LFMs and the equivalence to the mixture FIC form is a noteworthy theoretical insight.  
- Practical contributions (CCCP-based acceleration and merge) complement the theory and improve scalability.  
- Weakness: multiple interacting approximations (FIC asymptotics, mean-field, shrinkage, merging) need ablation and stress tests to identify failure regimes (small N, collinearity, high noise, heavy tails).  
- Weakness: key assumptions behind the asymptotic results are mainly in the supplement; summarizing their practical scope and diagnostics in the main text is needed.  
- Weakness: guidance on behavior in small-sample regimes and how priors compare to the FAB regularizer is lacking.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear theoretical advance, practical monotone algorithm with acceleration, strong empirical gains, minimal tuning, good clarity.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited exploration of failure modes and deferred discussion of asymptotic assumption scope.
