- Proposes an efficient model-selection algorithm for latent feature models by extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) to LFMs.  
- Uses mean-field approximation for latent inference combined with an accelerated global feature-shrinkage mechanism to prune features.  
- Automates model selection with minimal manual tuning required.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show substantial computational speedups.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation with comparisons to a broad set of baselines across multiple datasets.  
- Citations are adequate and the manuscript is clearly written and well organized.  
- The work is a natural yet solid generalization of prior FAB/FIC approaches to LFMs, combining multiple approximations into an effective method.  
- Reviewer requests clearer delineation of when and how the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) may degrade performance and stress-test the method’s limits.
