- Proposes an EM-like FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that uses a shrinkage term to learn Z and infer the effective number of features K.  
- Reports that FAB outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP methods in runtime and inference quality on large datasets.  
- Technical development appears sound and consistent with an EM augmented by a model-selection shrinkage term.  
- Methodological exposition is generally clear but several experimental details are insufficiently explained.  
- Novelty: applying FAB to latent feature models is, to the reviewer’s knowledge, new.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem—efficient inference in LFMs with automated K selection.  
- No discussion of limitations or regimes where the method might underperform vanilla Gibbs sampling.  
- Limited discussion of convergence challenges given the EM-like procedure.  
- Method is restricted to binary LFMs and does not generalize to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains apparent flaws.  
- Behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotic approximations may fail) is unclear and needs discussion/experiments.  
- Some recent relevant work on efficient LFM inference and small-variance asymptotics (e.g., MAD-Bayes) is missing from the discussion.  
- MEIBP baseline is an invalid comparison for experiments where W is not constrained nonnegative; synthetic/block-image data should match MEIBP assumptions if included.  
- Baseline experimental setups need correction: e.g., provide IBP Gibbs the same known noise variance, consider infinite VB-IBP, and report details like restarts.  
- Surprising results (e.g., accelerated Gibbs finding ~70 features on Sonar) suggest mis-specified initialization or noise hyperparameters and need explanation.  
- Paper should correct factual errors: reference [21] performs MAP inference, not Gibbs sampling.  
- After author feedback, the reviewer requests clearer experimental methodology, corrected description of [21], and inclusion/discussion of MAD-Bayes.  
- Overall: method is interesting and scalable with strong contributions on K inference, but acceptance requires stronger, fairer experiments and clearer reporting.
