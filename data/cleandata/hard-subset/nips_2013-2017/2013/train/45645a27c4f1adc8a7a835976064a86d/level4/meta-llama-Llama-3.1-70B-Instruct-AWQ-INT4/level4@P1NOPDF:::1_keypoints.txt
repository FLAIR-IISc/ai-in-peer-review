- Proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters using BIC rather than factorizing the posterior as in variational Bayes.  
- Empirically shows improved held-out likelihoods compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Approach is reasonable and motivated by a plausible asymptotic argument.  
- Main practical advantage: corresponds to an EM algorithm with complexity penalties, avoiding more expensive sampling or variational methods.  
- Technical contributions are novel but incremental, essentially extending prior work ([3], [4]).  
- Exposition is confusing and some math is imprecise (e.g., equation (3) implies log-sum is infinite if all z are zero).  
- The paper does not explain why the FIC score would not be infinite given non-degenerate variational distributions.  
- Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K scales with N.  
- Theorem appears to require fixed finite K, which conflicts with the IBP’s infinite-component motivation.  
- Minor errors: pk(X | z{·,k}) should use only data assigned to component k, and equation (4) is missing an expectation on the LHS.  
- Table 1 shows higher predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes, but stopping criteria for algorithms are unspecified, making comparisons unclear.  
- Faster convergence of FAB vs. Gibbs may be arbitrary; stopping the sampler and implementation/initialization details are unclear.  
- Unclear whether improvements come from the model selection criterion or from algorithms avoiding worse local optima; Gibbs learning too many components suggests local-optima/implementation issues.  
- Overall: reasonable but somewhat incremental; math needs precision and experiments require further analysis to be convincing.
