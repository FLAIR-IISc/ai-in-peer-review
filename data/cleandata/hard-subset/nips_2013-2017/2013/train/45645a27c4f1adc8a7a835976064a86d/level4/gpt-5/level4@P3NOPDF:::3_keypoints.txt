- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm for binary LFMs with FAB-derived shrinkage to jointly infer latent assignments and K.  
- FAB uses an asymptotic marginal likelihood approximation to add a principled model selection penalty that prunes features.  
- Method reportedly outperforms Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP methods in speed and estimation on large datasets.  
- Technical development is sound and the integration of shrinkage into EM is elegant and scalable.  
- Experimental methodology and baseline configurations are insufficiently documented and in some cases potentially incorrect.  
- Presentation is generally clear but hyperparameter choices and experimental settings need more detail for reproducibility.  
- Applying FAB to LFMs is a novel and worthwhile extension of prior FAB work.  
- The approach could be significant for large-scale LFM inference if validated with a more rigorous empirical study.  
- Paper lacks discussion of limitations, e.g., failure modes versus Gibbs in multimodal posteriors or local optima.  
- Convergence and sensitivity to initialization are not adequately discussed; practical diagnostics or guidance are missing.  
- Method is limited to binary LFMs and does not address continuous factor models like FA or PPCA.  
- Small-sample behavior is unclear due to FAB’s reliance on asymptotic approximations and needs analysis or experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation has multiple issues that undermine conclusions and require correction.  
- MEIBP comparisons are invalid when data violate its non-negativity assumptions; either constrain data or remove comparisons.  
- For fairness, the IBP Gibbs baseline should be given the same noise variance used by FAB rather than a heuristic value.  
- Variational IBP comparisons need full details (infinite vs truncated formulation, truncation level, priors, restarts, convergence).  
- The Sonar result (≈70 features for 49-dim data) suggests problematic initialization or hyperparameter settings and needs investigation.  
- Analyze the effect of fixing α on K inflation or infer α and report sensitivity analyses.  
- Report consistent runtime measurements with hardware, stopping criteria, and variance across runs.  
- Clarify evaluation metrics for “inference quality” and include uncertainty estimates.  
- Missing related work: discuss MAD-Bayes small-variance MAP approaches and contrast them with FAB.  
- Correct Ref. [21] description: it performs MAP inference for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling.  
- Provide pseudocode/algorithm summary, stopping criteria, pruning schedule, and curves showing K evolution during training.  
- After author feedback, several experimental and reference corrections remain required before acceptance.
