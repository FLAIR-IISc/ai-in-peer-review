- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).
- FAB normally maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), converging to marginal log-likelihood.
- Prior FAB applicability was limited to models with block-diagonal Hessian of the complete-data likelihood.
- Authors derive a valid lower bound of FIC for LFMs even though the block-diagonal Hessian condition fails.
- The derived bound has the same form as the FIC for mixture models.
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets compare FAB/LFM to IBP Gibbs, variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP.
- Results show FAB/LFM outperforms alternatives in prediction accuracy and computational cost.
- Paper is well written with clear and thorough derivations.
- Contribution is incremental but interesting and relevant to the NIPS community.
- Reviewer views the paper as solid with convincing results and supports acceptance.
