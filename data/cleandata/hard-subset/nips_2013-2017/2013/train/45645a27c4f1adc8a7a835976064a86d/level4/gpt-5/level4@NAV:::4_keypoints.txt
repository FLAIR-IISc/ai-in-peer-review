- Paper generalizes factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- FAB maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood.  
- FAB was previously limited to models whose complete-data likelihood has a block-diagonal Hessian; LFMs do not meet this condition.  
- Authors derive a valid lower bound of the FIC for LFMs despite the Hessian condition not holding.  
- The derived lower bound for LFMs has the same form as the FIC for mixture models.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets compare FAB/LFM to IBP Gibbs, variational Bayes, and MEIBP.  
- Results show FAB/LFM achieves better predictive performance and lower computational cost than alternatives.  
- Paper is clearly written with careful, thorough derivations.  
- Contribution is incremental but interesting and appears computationally efficient.  
- Reviewer (not a specialist) believes the work is of interest to the NIPS community and recommends acceptance.
