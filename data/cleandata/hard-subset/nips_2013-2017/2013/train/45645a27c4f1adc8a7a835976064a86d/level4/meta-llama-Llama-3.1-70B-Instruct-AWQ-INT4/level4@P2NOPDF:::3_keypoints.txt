- Paper proposes an EM-based inference algorithm for binary latent feature models using a FAB shrinkage mechanism to infer the number of latent features (K), novel application of FAB to LFMs.  
- Technical approach is sound, scales to large datasets, and methodology is clearly explained.  
- Experimental details are unclear and some experiments appear flawed.  
- Paper lacks discussion of limitations, including cases where it may perform worse than Gibbs sampling.  
- No discussion of potential convergence issues for the EM-like procedure.  
- Method is limited to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Missing comparison/discussion of recent efficient inference work for LFMs, e.g., MEIBP [22] and MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013).  
- VB-IBP baseline settings are insufficiently described; using a fixed alpha in Gibbs may have led to overestimated K.  
- Noise variance hyperparameters were not properly set and may have biased results.  
- MEIBP was applied to invalid datasets (it requires non-negative linear Gaussian models); synthetic data should have positive loading matrix W for valid comparison.  
- For block images, IBP Gibbs should use the same noise value as FAB for fair comparison; infinite variational VB-IBP could be used instead of finite.  
- Accelerated Gibbs finding 70 features on 49-dim Sonar data is suspicious and may indicate bad initialization or mis-specified noise variance.  
- Reference [21] is mischaracterized—it reports MAP estimation for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling; citation/text should be corrected.  
- Suggest adding discussion of small-variance asymptotic methods (MAD-Bayes) for nonparametric LFMs.  
- Minor correction: suggested std setting in [1,22] should be 0.25× (not 0.75×) the examples' std across dimensions.  
- After author feedback, experimental methodology still needs clearer explanation and justifications for observed algorithm behavior.  
- Overall: proposed inference method is interesting but the experimental evaluation needs to be more careful and better documented.
