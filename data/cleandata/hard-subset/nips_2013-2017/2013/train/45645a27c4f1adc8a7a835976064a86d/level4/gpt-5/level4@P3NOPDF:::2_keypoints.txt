- Extends FAB/FIC to latent feature models using mean-field variational inference plus accelerated shrinkage for automatic, efficient feature selection with strong computational gains.

- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing to strong baselines across multiple datasets with careful analyses and adequate references.

- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and presents motivation and algorithms coherently.

- A logical and valuable extension of FAB/FIC; the unified FIC + mean-field + shrinkage pipeline emphasizes automation and practical impact.

- Concern about validity of combined approximations: accelerated pruning might remove features that would become useful later, especially under non-convex objectives.

- Request ablation studies isolating contributions of (i) FIC vs alternatives, (ii) shrinkage schedule/strength, and (iii) mean-field vs structured variational families, plus sensitivity to hyperparameters and initialization.

- Ask for theoretical clarification on the asymptotic regime and assumptions for FIC accuracy, with bounds on approximation error or bias and dependence on sparsity, sample size, and priors.

- Need discussion of convergence behavior, susceptibility to local optima, interaction of shrinkage with restarts/annealing, and empirical diagnostics like variability across random seeds.

- Recommend robustness tests under model misspecification (heavy-tailed noise, correlated/redundant features, very sparse vs dense latent structures).

- Request scalability details: time/memory complexity in N, D, K and how asymptotic behavior yields the observed gains.

- Recommend reporting predictive performance (held-out likelihood/reconstruction error), uncertainty calibration, interpretability of features, and statistical significance/confidence intervals.

- Ask for comparison to Bayesian nonparametric baselines (IBP/Beta process with MCMC/variational) to clarify trade-offs between accuracy, speed, and simplicity.

- Practical guidance desired: recommended default priors and shrinkage parameters, failure signals, and remedial steps for over-pruning.

- Minor suggestions: clarify FAB vs FIC early and maintain consistent notation; provide concise pseudocode including shrinkage; release code or implementation details for reproducibility.

- Overall: strong, practical contribution with clear writing and compelling efficiency gains; addressing ablations, sensitivity analyses, and approximation validity would further strengthen the paper.
