In this paper, the authors generalize factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models. FAB is a recently developed model selection framework that has shown strong performance for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models (HMMs). In brief, the approach maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of FAB has been its applicability only to models whose complete-data likelihood has a block-diagonal Hessian. The authors extend FAB to latent feature models (LFMs) even though this condition is not met. They derive a valid lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and demonstrate that it takes the same form as the FIC for MMs. They present experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM to alternatives including fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results indicate that FAB/LFM is superior, achieving better predictive performance and lower computational cost.

The paper is clearly written, and the derivations are carefully and thoroughly explained.

The proposed method extends FAB from MMs to LFMs and, while incremental, is interesting and appears efficient. Although I am not a specialist in this area, I believe it would be of significant interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting a compelling idea, the empirical results are persuasive, and I recommend acceptance.
