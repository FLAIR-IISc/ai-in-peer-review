- Proposes extension of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMMs to latent feature models.  
- FAB maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood.  
- Overcomes prior limitation requiring block-diagonal Hessian by deriving a lower bound of the FIC suitable for latent feature models.  
- Shows the derived bound has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models.  
- Empirically evaluates on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Compares against fast Gibbs sampling with IBP, variational Bayes, and max-expectation IBP.  
- Demonstrates superior predictive performance and computational efficiency of FAB/LFM.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the derivation and method.  
- Extension is somewhat incremental but presents an interesting and efficient approach.  
- Reviewer, though not an expert, believes the work is of interest to the NIPS community and supports acceptance.
