- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMM settings to latent feature models (LFMs).
- Overcomes the prior FAB limitation requiring block-diagonal Hessian by handling Hessian condition violations in LFMs.
- Derives a lower bound on the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs, showing an identical representation to the FIC for mixture models.
- FAB/LFM maximizes a lower bound that converges to the marginal log-likelihood, as in prior FAB work.
- Empirical comparison on synthetic and real datasets against IBP (fast Gibbs), variational Bayes, and MEIBP.
- Results show FAB/LFM improves predictive performance and offers favorable computational efficiency.
- Paper is well-written with thorough methodological derivation.
- Extension is incremental relative to prior FAB work but still significant.
- Reviewer believes the work is of substantial interest to the community and recommends acceptance.
