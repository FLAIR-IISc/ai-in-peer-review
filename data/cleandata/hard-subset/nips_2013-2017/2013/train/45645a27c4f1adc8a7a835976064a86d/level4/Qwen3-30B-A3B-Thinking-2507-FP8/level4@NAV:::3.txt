

Review Summary:  
The paper introduces an inference algorithm for the binary latent feature model (LFM), where each observation \( \mathbf{x}_n \) is represented by a \( K \)-dimensional binary vector \( \mathbf{z}_n \) with \( z_{nk} = 1 \) indicating the presence of the \( k \)-th latent feature. The algorithm learns all \( \mathbf{z}_n \) (and model parameters) via an EM-based approach incorporating a shrinkage mechanism to infer both the latent features and the number of features \( K \). While conceptually related to nonparametric Bayesian models like the Indian Buffet Process (IBP), the method avoids prior-induced parsimony, instead employing the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference (FAB) criterion—previously applied to mixture models and HMMs—to balance the expected complete log-likelihood with a model selection term that enforces shrinkage (via an upper-bound on \( K \)). On large datasets, FAB outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP estimation for IBP in terms of speed and inference quality.  

Quality: The technical approach appears sound, with correct algorithmic details. The capacity to handle large datasets and infer \( K \) is highly appealing.  

Clarity: The methodology is reasonably clear, but experimental details lack sufficient clarity (see below).  

Originality: While FAB originates from recent work on mixture models and HMMs, its application to latent feature models constitutes a novel contribution.  

Significance: The paper addresses a critical challenge: efficient inference in LFMs with automatic \( K \)-estimation.  

Weak Points:  
- No discussion of limitations (e.g., scenarios where the algorithm underperforms compared to vanilla Gibbs sampling).  
- Absence of convergence analysis for the EM-like procedure.  
- Restricted to binary LFMs (inapplicable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA).  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and contains flaws (see below).  
- Unclear behavior in small-data regimes (asymptotic arguments may not hold; requires discussion/experiments).  
- Omission of relevant recent works on efficient LFM inference (see below).  
- The MEIBP baseline [22] requires a positive loading matrix \( \mathbf{W} \); synthetic and block data experiments are invalid for MEIBP unless \( \mathbf{W} \) is constrained to be positive.  

Other Comments:  
- For block images, IBP Gibbs should use the known noise value (not the 0.75 heuristic) for fairness.  
- VB-IBP [2] should use the infinite variational version (experiments used finite version); details on restarts are missing.  
- The Gibbs sampler’s 70 latent features on 49D Sonar data likely stems from poor initialization or incorrect noise variance.  
- Line 70: Reference [21] describes MAP inference (not Gibbs sampling); correct this.  
- Recent work on small-variance asymptotic inference for nonparametric LFMs (e.g., "MAD-Bayes" from ICML 2013) should be cited.  

Minor Comments:  
- Line 349: For real data, [1,22] recommends setting noise std-dev to 0.25×the data std-dev (not 0.75).  

Comments after Author Feedback:  
The authors addressed some points, but further revisions are required:  
- Experimental methodology (for both proposed and baseline methods) must be clarified, including justification for observed behaviors (e.g., Gibbs using fixed \( \alpha \) leading to overestimated \( K \), or mis-specified noise hyperparameters).  
- Correct Line 70: Reference [21] is MAP, not sampling.  
- Include the MAD-Bayes reference.  
The method’s appeal (scalability to large datasets + \( K \)-inference) is strong, but the experimental evaluation requires rigorous revision.
