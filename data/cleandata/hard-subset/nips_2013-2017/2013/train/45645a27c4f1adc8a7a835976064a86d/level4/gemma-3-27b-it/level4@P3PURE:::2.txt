Summary:
The authors propose an algorithm and approximation method for efficient model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) using factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC are well-established techniques for mixture models, where FIC approximates a model’s log likelihood via factorization and Laplace approximation to enable tractable model selection. This work extends FAB to LFMs, employing a mean field approximation for latent variable inference and accelerated shrinkage of the global number of selected features. A key feature of the approach is its relative automation, requiring minimal manual parameter tuning. The method is evaluated on both synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating significant gains in computational efficiency.

Quality:
The presented results include thorough analysis, and the method is evaluated comparatively against a wide range of competing methods across multiple datasets. The references are sufficient.

Clarity:
The paper is clearly written and well-structured.

Originality/Significance:
This work represents a logical extension and solid generalization of previous work on model selection in LFMs utilizing FAB inference and FIC. The authors effectively combine several approximations into a novel method that performs well in practice. 

However, it would be valuable to understand the conditions under which these approximations might lead to suboptimal performance, or how to identify scenarios where the method “breaks down.” Given the concurrent application of FIC, mean field approximation, and shrinkage, a discussion of potential failure modes and the interplay between these approximations would strengthen the paper.
