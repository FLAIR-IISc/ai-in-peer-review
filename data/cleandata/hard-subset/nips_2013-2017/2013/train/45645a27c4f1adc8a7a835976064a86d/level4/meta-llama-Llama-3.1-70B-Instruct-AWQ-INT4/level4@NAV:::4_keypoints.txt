- Paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).
- FAB previously showed promise for mixture models and HMMs by maximizing a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC).
- FAB’s applicability was previously limited to models with block-diagonal Hessian of the complete likelihood.
- Authors derive a lower bound of FIC for LFMs that has the same representation as FIC for mixture models.
- Method converges to the marginal log-likelihood by maximizing the derived lower bound.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets compares FAB/LFM to fast Gibbs IBP, variational Bayes, and ME-IBP.
- Results show FAB/LFM outperforms alternatives in prediction accuracy and reduces computational cost.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the derivation of the proposed method.
- Extension is somewhat incremental but conceptually interesting and demonstrably efficient.
- Work has potential impact for the NIPS community and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
