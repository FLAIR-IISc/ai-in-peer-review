Summary:
The authors propose an efficient model selection algorithm for latent feature models, leveraging factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). Notably, FAB and FIC are well-established methods for mixture models, where the latter approximates a model's log likelihood via factorization and Laplace approximation, facilitating tractable model selection. The key contribution of this work is the generalization of FAB to latent feature models, which employs a mean field approximation for latent variable inference and accelerated shrinkage of the global number of features selected. A distinctive feature of this approach is its increased automaticity, requiring minimal manual tuning. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their method on both synthetic and real-world data, showcasing significant gains in computational efficiency.

Quality:
The presented results are thorough and well-supported, with the proposed method being evaluated against a diverse range of competing approaches on multiple datasets. The references provided are sufficient and relevant.

Clarity:
The paper is clearly written, well-structured, and easy to follow.

Originality/Significance:
This work appears to be a logical extension and solid generalization of previous research in model selection for latent feature models using FAB inference and FIC. Specifically, the authors' novel combination of several approximations (FAB inference, FIC, mean field, and shrinkage) yields a method that performs well in practice. However, it would be beneficial to understand the limitations of these approximations and how they might lead to poor performance under certain conditions, i.e., when the method is "pushed to its limits." Investigating the robustness of the proposed approach in such scenarios would provide valuable insights into its applicability and reliability.
