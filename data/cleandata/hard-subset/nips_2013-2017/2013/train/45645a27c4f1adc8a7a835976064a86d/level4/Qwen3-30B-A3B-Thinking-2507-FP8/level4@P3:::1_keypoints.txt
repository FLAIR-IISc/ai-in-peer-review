- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), targeting binary-feature models like the IBP.  
- Shows LFMs admit the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as mixture models despite violating the block-diagonal Hessian assumption (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
- Enables automatic feature selection via model-induced regularization without hyperparameters or priors while preserving FAB guarantees.  
- Introduces a practical FAB algorithm for LFMs with a novel shrinkage-acceleration method based on a CCCP interpretation (Algorithm 2).  
- Proves parameter identifiability properties and proposes a merge post-processing step (Theorem 4).  
- Properly situates the work relative to variational Bayes, MAP, and sampling-based IBP approaches, and links the acceleration to CCCP theory.  
- Empirical results show consistent predictive-likelihood improvements and substantial computational savings, especially on large datasets (Table 1, Figure 1).  
- Shrinkage acceleration effectively removes irrelevant features early, reducing computational complexity.  
- Mathematical exposition contains imprecisions: the asymptotic regime is unspecified (how K scales with N is unclear).  
- Analysis conflicts with IBP’s infinite-K motivation because the theory appears to assume finite K without clarifying alternatives like K = O(N^α).  
- Equation (3) has a problematic log(∑_n z_nk) term that is undefined when all z_nk = 0, and the paper does not explain handling under non-degenerate q.  
- Experimental comparisons are weak: stopping criterion (δ = 1e-4) is unjustified and the Gibbs-sampler comparison may be affected by initialization/implementation differences.  
- Authors should provide controlled experiments (same initialization/optimization) to isolate the effect of FIC-based model selection from implementation factors.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental, largely building on prior FAB work; gains are mainly computational rather than fundamentally methodological.  
- Claim of automatic K selection without priors is not unique, as nonparametric methods like IBP also select K via posterior inference.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing theoretical imprecisions (asymptotics and zero-feature cases) and strengthening experimental controls to isolate the method’s advantages.
