In this paper, the authors extend factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models. FAB is a recently developed model selection method that has achieved strong results with mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. Briefly, the method maximizes a lower bound on a factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of FAB has been its applicability only to models where the Hessian of the complete likelihood is block diagonal. The authors address this limitation by extending FAB to latent feature models (LFMs), despite the fact that this condition is not satisfied. They effectively derive a lower bound on the FIC for LFMs and demonstrate that it shares the same form as the FIC for MMs. They evaluate the proposed method on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM to other approaches such as fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet Process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM; the proposed method achieves better performance not only in prediction but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well-written and the authors provide a thorough derivation of their method. 

Extending FAB from MMs to LFMs represents incremental progress, but it is a valuable and interesting contribution, and the results clearly support its effectiveness. While I am not an expert in this specific area, I believe this work would be of considerable interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a compelling idea. The results are convincing, and I recommend accepting it.
