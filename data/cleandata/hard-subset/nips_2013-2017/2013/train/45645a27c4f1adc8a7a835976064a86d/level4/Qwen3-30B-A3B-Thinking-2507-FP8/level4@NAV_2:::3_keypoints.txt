- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), showing FAB applies despite LFMs violating block-diagonal Hessian assumptions.  
- Lemma 1 proves the log-determinant of the Hessian for LFMs asymptotically yields the same regularization term (∑ₙ zₙₖ) as in mixture models.  
- This yields inherited FAB properties: automatic feature selection via the −D/(2Nπ̃ₖ) term, parameter identifiability (Theorem 4), and convergence guarantees.  
- Proposes Algorithm 1 with a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) that eliminates irrelevant features early to reduce computation.  
- Empirical results show FAB/LFMs outperform IBP (Gibbs), VB, and MEIBP on model selection (K), prediction (PLL), computational efficiency, and generalization (smaller TLL–PLL gap) across synthetic and real datasets (block, EEG, USPS).  
- Builds on Fujimaki et al. (NIPS 2012, 2013) FAB work and addresses gaps noted by Griffiths & Ghahramani and Doshi-Velez; should also cite MAD-Bayes for related asymptotic LFM approximations.  
- Technical derivations are sound with rigorous asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) and correct algorithmic implementation; CCCP and shrinkage are well-motivated.  
- Methodology and notation are clear, but experimental details need refinement (fixed noise variance rationale, which VB-IBP variant was used, and explicit noise settings for block data).  
- The application of FAB to LFMs is novel and the theoretical insight that LFMs share the same FIC form despite Hessian differences is significant.  
- The work is significant for scalable latent feature inference, offering automatic K selection and speed that enable large-scale applications and address identifiability issues.  
- Strengths: elegant theoretical extension (Lemma 1), practical CCCP acceleration, consistent empirical superiority, and clear connection to prior FAB and LFM literature.  
- Weakness — MEIBP comparison may be invalid because MEIBP requires non-negative W, while some experiments used arbitrary (possibly negative) W; authors should re-run or remove MEIBP comparisons.  
- Weakness — Experimental gaps: no small-N analysis where asymptotics may fail; insufficient detail on VB-IBP settings (restarts, K range); no comparison to infinite VB-IBP.  
- Weakness — Specific empirical results (e.g., 70 features for Sonar) need justification regarding sensitivity to noise variance and initialization.  
- Weakness — Theoretical scope is limited to binary LFMs (Equation 5) and does not cover factor analysis/PCA; broader applicability should be discussed.  
- Minor correction: reference [21] is misattributed as Gibbs sampling but is MAP inference (Reed & Ghahramani, ICML 2012).  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution with compelling empirical validation; weaknesses are addressable via minor revisions.
