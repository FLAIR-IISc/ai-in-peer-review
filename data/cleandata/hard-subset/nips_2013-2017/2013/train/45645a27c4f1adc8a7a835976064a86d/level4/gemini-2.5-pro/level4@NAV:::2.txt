**Summary:**
This work presents an efficient algorithm for model selection in latent feature models, leveraging factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC). The authors build upon FAB and FIC, established techniques for mixture models where FIC enables tractable model selection by approximating the log-likelihood through factorization and the Laplace method. The primary contribution is the novel generalization of FAB to the latent feature model domain. This is achieved by employing a mean-field approximation for latent variable inference and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to determine the global number of features. A key advantage of this approach is its high degree of automation, reducing the need for manual parameter tuning. The method's efficacy is demonstrated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, where it achieves substantial gains in computational efficiency.

**Quality:**
The presented results are supported by a thorough analysis. The proposed method is comprehensively evaluated against a broad set of competing approaches across multiple datasets. The paper is well-referenced.

**Clarity:**
The manuscript is well-written and logically organized, making it easy to follow.

**Originality/Significance:**
The work represents a solid and logical generalization of prior research in model selection for LFMs using FAB inference and FIC. Its novelty lies in the successful integration of multiple approximation techniques—specifically FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage—into a single, effective framework.

A valuable addition would be an investigation into the method's failure modes. Given the interplay of several approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage), an analysis of the conditions under which these combined approximations might degrade performance would strengthen the paper. Characterizing the method's breaking points would provide a clearer understanding of its practical limitations.
