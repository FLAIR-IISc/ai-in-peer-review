

This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection method that achieves strong empirical performance for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models, maximizing a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of FAB is its requirement that the Hessian of the complete likelihood be block diagonal, a condition not satisfied by LFMs. The authors successfully extend FAB to LFMs by deriving a valid lower bound for the FIC under this relaxed setting, demonstrating that it shares the same representation as the FIC for MMs. They evaluate the proposed FAB/LFM approach on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing it against fast Gibbs sampling in Indian buffet process (IBP) models, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). Results demonstrate FAB/LFM's superiority in prediction accuracy and computational efficiency. The paper is clearly written, with thorough derivation of the method. While representing an incremental advance over FAB/MMs, the extension to LFMs is conceptually interesting and the results convincingly support its efficiency. Although not a specialist in this niche, I believe this work would be of significant interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a novel and well-executed idea, with compelling results that warrant acceptance.
