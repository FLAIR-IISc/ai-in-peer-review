- Proposes Feature Inclusion Criterion (FIC) for binary latent feature models, approximating parameter integration with BIC instead of a fully factorized variational posterior.  
- Method corresponds to an EM algorithm with complexity penalties, offering substantially better computational efficiency than Gibbs sampling or standard variational Bayes.  
- Empirical results report higher held-out predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Theoretical exposition is imprecise: Eq.(3)’s log(sum_i z_ik) is -∞ if a feature has zero activations under q, which needs clarification.  
- Theorem 2 is underspecified: the asymptotic regime and assumptions (how K behaves as N grows) are not stated, calling into question the per-component BIC use.  
- Runtime comparisons are inconclusive because stopping criteria and iteration counts for competing algorithms (e.g., Gibbs) are not specified.  
- Improved predictive likelihoods may be due to optimization/initialization or avoidance of local optima rather than the FIC criterion itself; evidence tying gains specifically to FIC is lacking.  
- Gibbs sampler learning too many components on some datasets suggests issues with local optima and weakens comparative validity.  
- Technical novelty appears incremental, mainly extending the existing FAB framework from prior work.  
- Minor issues: Section 2’s p_k(X | z_{·,k}) should condition only on data assigned to component k; Eq.(4) appears to be missing an expectation over q.  
- Overall: sensible and computationally attractive approach, but theoretical imprecision and ambiguous experimental analysis are significant concerns.
