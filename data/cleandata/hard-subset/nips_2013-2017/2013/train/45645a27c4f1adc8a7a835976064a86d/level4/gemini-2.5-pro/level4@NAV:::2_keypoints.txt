- Proposes an efficient model selection algorithm for latent feature models using FAB inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC).
- Generalizes FAB/FIC to latent feature models by combining mean-field latent inference with an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to determine the global number of features.
- Method emphasizes automation and reduces the need for manual parameter tuning.
- Demonstrates substantial computational-efficiency gains on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Provides thorough analysis and comprehensive empirical evaluation against a broad set of competing approaches.
- Paper is well-written and logically organized.
- Novelty is the integration of FIC, mean-field approximation, and shrinkage into a single effective framework.
- Recommends investigating failure modes and characterizing conditions where the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) might degrade performance.
