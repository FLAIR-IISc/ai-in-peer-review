- Summary: Proposes a novel algorithm for efficient model selection in latent feature models using FAB/FIC, mean-field latent inference, and accelerated shrinkage, showing automation and large computational gains.  
- Quality: Evaluation is comprehensive with rigorous experiments, appropriate statistical testing, and a thorough literature review.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured, clearly written, with accessible technical exposition and supportive figures.  
- Originality/Significance: Non-trivial extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs that unifies mean-field inference and accelerated shrinkage, enabling scalable model selection.  
- Major strength — Computational efficiency: Reports orders-of-magnitude speedups (e.g., 10–100×) on real datasets.  
- Major strength — Automation: Reduces manual hyperparameter tuning compared to existing approaches.  
- Major strength — Empirical validation: Strong results across image, text, and biological datasets without loss of accuracy.  
- Critical concern — Approximation sensitivity: Needs analysis of when FIC, mean-field, or shrinkage approximations fail (e.g., high feature correlation, sparse regimes, extreme complexity).  
- Critical concern — FIC justification: Lacks theoretical or empirical justification that FIC’s factorization assumptions hold for LFMs.  
- Critical concern — Comparison scope: Missing comparisons to recent scalable/variational LFM alternatives, leaving relative advantages unclear.  
- Minor suggestion: Clarify the accelerated shrinkage mechanism and its interaction with mean-field updates (add brief pseudocode).  
- Minor suggestion: In Table 2, indicate whether reported computational gains are absolute times or relative speedups.  
- Minor suggestion: Discuss potential interpretability limitations—whether shrinkage biases feature selection.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed contribution that meaningfully advances scalable LFM model selection; concerns are addressable with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).
