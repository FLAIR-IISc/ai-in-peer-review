- Introduces a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that approximates parameter integration via BIC rather than a factorized variational posterior.  
- Demonstrates superior held-out likelihoods compared to existing IBP implementations in experiments.  
- Motivated by a plausible asymptotic argument and computationally reduces to an EM algorithm with complexity penalties, avoiding costly sampling/variational methods.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, essentially extending the FAB framework from prior work.  
- Equation (3) is ambiguous: it includes a log-sum over zero-valued z variables which would be infinite under a nondegenerate variational q, yet the FIC score is not addressed as infinite.  
- Theorem 2 is vague about the asymptotic regime, assumptions, and how K scales with N, implicitly assuming fixed finite K contrary to IBP’s infinite-component motivation.  
- This fixed-K assumption contradicts the expectation that new features emerge as N grows in the IBP setting.  
- Minor notational/technical issues: Section 2’s pk(X | z·,k) should be restricted to data assigned to component k, and Equation (4) needs an expectation on its left-hand side.  
- Table 1 shows faster and higher predictive likelihoods for the proposed method, with FAB sometimes orders of magnitude quicker.  
- Experimental results lack interpretability due to unspecified Gibbs sampler stopping criteria, introducing arbitrariness in comparisons.  
- Unclear whether likelihood improvements come from the proposed model selection criterion or from implementation-dependent factors/local optima (e.g., Gibbs overfitting by learning too many components).  
- Overall: method is sound but incremental, with mathematical imprecision and insufficient analysis to attribute empirical gains definitively.
