- Proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters with a BIC-style approximation, improving held-out likelihoods.  
- Method is like variational Bayes but computationally cheaper, behaving like an EM algorithm with additional complexity penalties.  
- Empirical results show higher predictive likelihood and faster runtime than several IBP baselines.  
- Technical contributions are novel but incremental, mainly extending prior FAB work.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized.  
- Equation (3) is problematic: log-sum over z is infinite if all z are zero, which can occur under nondegenerate q, so the FIC score may be ill-defined.  
- Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions (e.g., how K scales with N); it appears to require finite fixed K, which conflicts with IBP motivations.  
- Section 2 has a notation error: pk(X | z·,k) should condition only on data assigned to component k.  
- Equation (4) is missing an expectation on the left-hand side.  
- Experimental stopping criteria and convergence details (especially for the Gibbs sampler) are not specified, making runtime comparisons unclear.  
- It is unclear whether predictive gains stem from the model-selection criterion or from algorithmic/initialization differences and local optima; more analysis is needed.  
- More analysis is required to interpret results, assess robustness, and clarify strengths and limitations.  
- Overall assessment: sensible and significant empirical gains but needs corrections/clarifications; scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: plausible idea, computational efficiency, strong empirical improvements; arguments for rejection: exposition/math imprecision, incremental novelty, insufficient analysis of results.
