This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection method that has demonstrated strong performance for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. The method operates by maximizing a lower bound on a factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of FAB, however, is that its applicability has been restricted to models where the Hessian of the complete-data log-likelihood is block-diagonal.

The authors overcome this limitation by extending FAB to LFMs, for which this condition does not hold. They derive a valid lower bound on the FIC for LFMs and show that it takes an analogous form to the FIC for MMs. The paper presents results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing the proposed FAB/LFM method against several alternatives, including fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM; the proposed method achieves better performance on prediction tasks while also being more computationally efficient.

The paper is well-written, and the authors thoroughly describe the derivation steps of their method. The proposed work extends the FAB framework from MMs to LFMs, and could therefore be considered an incremental contribution. Nevertheless, the extension is interesting and non-trivial, and the results effectively highlight its efficiency. Although I am not an expert on this topic, I believe the work will be of great interest to the NIPS community. In summary, this is a strong paper that presents a valuable idea with convincing results, and I support its acceptance.
