- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs).  
- Overcomes FAB's prior requirement of a block-diagonal Hessian, which LFMs do not satisfy.  
- Derives a tractable lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs analogous to the mixture model case.  
- Enables efficient application of FAB to a broader class of models formerly precluded.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with thorough, verifiable derivations.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets compares against fast Gibbs IBP, Variational IBP, and MEIBP.  
- FAB/LFM achieves superior predictive accuracy and greater computational efficiency than strong baselines.  
- Theoretical extension is non-trivial despite being a direct FAB extension, and is well justified by practical results.  
- Strong, convincing paper recommended for acceptance.
