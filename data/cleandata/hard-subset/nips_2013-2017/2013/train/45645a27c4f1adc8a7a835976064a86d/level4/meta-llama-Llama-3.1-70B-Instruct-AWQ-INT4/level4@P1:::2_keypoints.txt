- Proposes a novel algorithm generalizing factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC) to latent feature models using mean-field inference and accelerated feature shrinkage.

- Uses mean-field approximation for latent inference and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to reduce the global number of selected features.

- Offers more automatic model selection with minimal manual tuning required.

- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains.

- Evaluated thoroughly on synthetic and real datasets and compared against a wide range of competing methods.

- Writing is clear and the manuscript is well structured.

- Represents a logical and solid generalization of prior FAB/FIC work by combining several approximations into a practical method.

- Main concern: multiple approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage) are used simultaneously, and it is unclear when/how they may cause poor performance.

- Potential robustness issues to certain data types or models; paper lacks detailed analysis of limitations and failure modes.
