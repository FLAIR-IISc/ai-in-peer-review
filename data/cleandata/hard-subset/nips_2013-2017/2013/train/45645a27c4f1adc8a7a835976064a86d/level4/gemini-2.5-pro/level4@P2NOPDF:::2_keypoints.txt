- Proposes an efficient approximation method for model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) by generalizing Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC).  
- Uses a mean-field approximation for latent variable inference and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to prune global features.  
- Core contribution is integrating FIC, mean-field inference, and accelerated shrinkage into a single scalable framework for LFMs.  
- Method is largely automated and requires minimal manual parameter tuning for model selection.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show significant computational efficiency gains over existing approaches.  
- Evaluation is thorough, comparing against a comprehensive set of competing methods across multiple datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and well-situated within the existing literature with appropriate references.  
- Novelty is a solid, logical generalization of prior work rather than a radical new paradigm, with practical empirical success.  
- Main weakness: lacks an in-depth analysis of limitations and failure modes—needs exploration of interactions between approximations, breaking points, and data conditions that violate assumptions.
