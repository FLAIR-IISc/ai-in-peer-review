- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), removing the block-diagonal Hessian requirement.  
- Derives a new lower bound for the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) specifically for LFMs.  
- Shows the new bound shares the same functional form as the FIC for mixture models, connecting the model classes.  
- Conducts thorough empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Compares against strong baselines: fast Gibbs IBP, VB-IBP, and ME-IBP.  
- FAB-LFM outperforms baselines in predictive accuracy and computational efficiency.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents theoretical derivations.  
- Technical contribution is significant, broadening FAB’s applicability to models with more complex dependencies.  
- Comprehensive experiments convincingly demonstrate practical advantages.  
- Weakness: contribution may be viewed as an incremental extension of the existing FAB framework.  
- Overall: high-quality, well-motivated work; reviewer supports acceptance.
