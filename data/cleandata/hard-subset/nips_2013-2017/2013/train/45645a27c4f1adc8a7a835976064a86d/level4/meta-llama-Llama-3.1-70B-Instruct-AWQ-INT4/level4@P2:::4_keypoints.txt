- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), broadening FAB's applicability.  
- Derives a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs, addressing previous Hessian-condition limitations.  
- Shows the derived lower bound has the same representation as FIC for mixture models, enabling similar FAB treatment.  
- Provides thorough, clear derivation steps and exposition of the method.  
- Presents empirical results on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Compares against fast Gibbs IBP, variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP, demonstrating FAB/LFM’s superiority.  
- Demonstrates improved prediction performance and lower computational cost.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Contribution could be considered incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Results are convincing and relevant to the NIPS community.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance.
