

Summary:  
The authors present an algorithm for efficient model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) through the generalization of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). While FAB and FIC are established for mixture models—where FIC approximates model log likelihood via factorization and Laplace approximation for tractable selection—their contribution extends FAB to LFMs using mean field approximation for latent inference and accelerated shrinkage of the global feature count. The approach achieves greater automation in model selection with minimal manual hyperparameter tuning. Computational efficiency gains are substantial, demonstrated on synthetic and real datasets.  

Quality:  
Results feature rigorous analysis, comprehensive comparisons against diverse baselines across multiple datasets, and sufficient references.  

Clarity:  
The paper is well-written, logically structured, and clearly presented.  

Originality/Significance:  
This constitutes a logical extension and robust generalization of prior FAB/FIC work for LFM model selection. The novel integration of multiple approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage) yields a highly effective method. To further strengthen the contribution, the authors should consider providing a deeper analysis of failure modes—specifically, identifying scenarios where the interplay of these approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage) may degrade performance, thereby testing the method's robustness.
