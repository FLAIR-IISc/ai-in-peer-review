- Introduces a new model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models that approximates parameter integration via a BIC-like method and yields better held-out likelihoods than several IBP implementations.  
- Method resembles variational Bayes but integrates out parameters, effectively reducing to an EM algorithm with complexity penalties and lower computational cost than sampling or standard VB.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, mainly extending the existing FAB framework.  
- Some mathematical statements are imprecise: equation (3)’s log-sum term diverges when all z entries are zero, which has nonzero probability under a nondegenerate q, so FIC might be infinite.  
- Theorem 2’s asymptotic approximation lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, especially how K scales with N; applying BIC component-wise seems to require fixed finite K.  
- Assuming finite K undermines the IBP motivation because IBP allows infinitely many features that grow with data.  
- Minor correctness issues: p_k(X | z_{·,k}) should involve only datapoints assigned to component k, and equation (4)’s left-hand side should be an expectation.  
- Experimental results show higher predictive likelihoods and faster runtime for FAB, but stopping criteria (especially for Gibbs) are unspecified, complicating runtime comparisons.  
- It’s unclear whether predictive gains come from the model-selection criterion itself or from algorithmic/local-optima differences and implementation details (e.g., initialization).  
- The Gibbs sampler sometimes learns too many components, suggesting local-optima or tuning issues rather than definitive superiority of FAB’s approximation.  
- Overall assessment: sensible method with computational benefits, but incremental novelty, some mathematical imprecision, and insufficient analysis of experimental results.
