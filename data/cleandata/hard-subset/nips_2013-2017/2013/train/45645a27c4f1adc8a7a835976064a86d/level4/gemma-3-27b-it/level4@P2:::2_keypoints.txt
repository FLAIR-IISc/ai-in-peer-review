- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC) from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Shows FICs for LFMs have a similar form to mixture models, enabling the extension despite prior Hessian restrictions.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method to reduce computational cost.  
- Analyzes parameter identifiability and proposes merging post-processing for latent features.  
- Empirical results show improved computational efficiency and competitive performance versus methods like Indian Buffet Processes.  
- Presents a clear, rigorous derivation of the FIC for LFMs and a well-defined algorithm.  
- Thorough experimental comparisons across synthetic and real-world datasets with comprehensive references.  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and uses consistent notation with clear mathematical derivations.  
- Organizes analysis and refinements effectively, including CCCP interpretation and detailed algorithmic explanations.  
- Addresses a significant practical problem: scaling LFM model selection and reducing manual hyperparameter tuning.  
- Novelty: successful generalization of FAB/FIC to LFMs, overcoming previous applicability limits.  
- Computational efficiency: demonstrates significant speedups, improving practicality for large datasets.  
- Theoretical soundness: provides Hessian analysis and establishes identifiability.  
- Limitation: relies on multiple approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) with limited discussion of combined failure modes.  
- Limitation: lacks focused investigation of scenarios where approximations lead to suboptimal performance; sensitivity analysis recommended.  
- Overall verdict: strong contribution with sound theory and compelling empirical results; recommendation for acceptance.
