- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), removing a key limitation of the original method.  
- Overcomes the Hessian block-diagonal requirement by deriving a valid lower bound for the factorized information criterion (FIC) applicable to LFMs.  
- Proves the new lower bound shares the same representation as FIC for mixture models.  
- Technical derivation is well-motivated, clearly presented, and mathematically rigorous.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets compare FAB-LFM to IBP Gibbs, variational Bayes, and MEIBP.  
- Empirical results show FAB-LFM achieves higher predictive accuracy and substantially lower computational cost, especially at large scale.  
- FAB-LFM inherits FAB’s computational efficiency and model-selection advantages, broadening FAB’s applicability to widely used LFMs.  
- Writing is clear, comparisons are fair, results are statistically rigorous, and claims are appropriately grounded.  
- No major flaws identified; the paper is a meaningful incremental advance and is recommended for acceptance at NIPS.
