- Introduces Feature Information Criterion (FIC) for binary latent feature models, integrating out parameters via BIC while keeping a variational posterior over latent features.  
- Claims computational efficiency and an EM-like optimization that scales better than Gibbs or fully-variational IBP methods, with improved held-out likelihood and competitive runtimes.  
- Strength: Pragmatic, well-motivated approach that replaces difficult parameter integration with a tractable BIC approximation.  
- Strength: Intuitive connection to EM and clear optimization path; empirical results show consistent predictive-likelihood gains.  
- Weakness: Theoretical foundations lack clarity; key assumptions are unstated and mathematical precision is missing.  
- Major concern: Theorem 2’s asymptotic regime is undefined—applicability of BIC seems to assume fixed finite K while IBP allows K to grow with N.  
- Major concern: Equation (3) includes log(sum_i z_ik), which is undefined if a feature is unused; a non-degenerate q can assign positive mass to that event.  
- Weakness: Novelty appears incremental, mainly extending prior FAB variational-Bayes work with BIC applied to parameters.  
- Experimental concern: Improvement source is ambiguous—could be due to optimization/local optima or baseline implementation issues rather than the model-selection criterion.  
- Experimental concern: Runtime comparisons are potentially unfair—Gibbs stopping criteria/convergence diagnostics are unspecified, and FAB’s much faster time but worse likelihood suggests premature convergence.  
- Minor: Clarify whether p_k(X | z_{·,k}) should condition only on data assigned to feature k rather than the whole dataset.  
- Minor: Equation (4) likely should be an expectation under q (i.e., E_q[...]).  
- Conclusion: Promising and efficient approach but needs clearer asymptotic assumptions, rigorous justification for FIC, and more controlled experiments to isolate the true source of improvements.
