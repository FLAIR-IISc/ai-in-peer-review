- Proposal: An efficient model selection algorithm for latent feature models using FAB inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).  
- Contribution: Generalization of FAB/FIC from mixture models to latent feature models via mean-field latent inference and accelerated feature-size shrinkage.  
- Method highlight: Combines FAB, FIC, mean-field approximation, and shrinkage to enable tractable, automatic model selection with minimal tuning.  
- Empirical results: Demonstrated significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real-world datasets against diverse baselines.  
- Evaluation quality: Results are thorough, well-supported, and compared against multiple competing approaches; references are adequate.  
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and easy to follow.  
- Originality/significance: Logical and solid extension of prior work; novel combination of approximations yields practical performance.  
- Limitation/concern: Lacks analysis of failure modes—need investigation of robustness and scenarios where the approximations break down or perform poorly.
