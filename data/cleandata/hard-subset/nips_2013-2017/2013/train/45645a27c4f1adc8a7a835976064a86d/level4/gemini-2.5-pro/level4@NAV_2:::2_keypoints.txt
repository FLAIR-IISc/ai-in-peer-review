- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to Latent Feature Models (LFMs).
- Derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition.
- Shows the model complexity term for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models.
- Proposes the FAB/LFM algorithm combining the asymptotic FIC, mean-field variational inference, and a novel shrinkage-acceleration method to auto-select latent feature count.
- Positions FAB/LFM as a computationally efficient alternative to nonparametric Bayesian methods like the Indian Buffet Process (IBP).
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous derivations (Lemma 1, Theorems 2 and 4) and comprehensive experiments.
- Empirics: robust evaluation against IBP, VB, MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets showing strong model selection, prediction, and computational performance.
- Originality: novel and significant generalization of FAB to LFMs; key insight enabling FIC extension is original.
- Novel practical contributions: CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration and merge post-processing informed by identifiability analysis.
- Significance: offers a highly automated, scalable alternative for discovering latent dimensionality, valuable for large-scale applications.
- Clarity: well-organized and clearly written with clear theoretical motivation and algorithm presentation.
- Weakness: limited discussion and testing of failure modes—reliance on multiple approximations (asymptotic FIC, mean-field, iterative linearization) and insufficient analysis of limits (e.g., small-N regimes like Libras).
