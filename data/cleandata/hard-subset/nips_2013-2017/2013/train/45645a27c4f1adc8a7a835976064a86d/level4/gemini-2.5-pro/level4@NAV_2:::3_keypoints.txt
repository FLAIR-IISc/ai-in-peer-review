- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models, learning binary features and inferring K via an EM-like algorithm.  
- Uses a factorized information criterion (FIC) to approximate log marginal likelihood and encourage shrinkage of unused features.  
- Derivation and algorithm are well-presented and technically sound, enabling scalability to large datasets.  
- Novel application: FAB justified for LFMs despite prior block-diagonal Hessian assumptions not holding.  
- Empirical results claim better performance and efficiency than IBP baselines (Gibbs, variational, MAP) on large datasets.  
- Experimental section lacks sufficient detail, harming reproducibility and validity assessment.  
- Paper omits discussion of failure modes, convergence properties, and sensitivity to local optima of EM-like methods.  
- Method is limited to binary LFMs and does not apply to continuous models (e.g., factor analysis, PPCA).  
- Comparison to MEIBP appears invalid because MEIBP assumes non-negativity of W, not satisfied by used synthetic data.  
- Small-data regime performance is not evaluated, despite FAB’s asymptotic justification potentially failing there.  
- Missing comparisons to relevant asymptotic approaches, notably MAD-Bayes (Broderick et al., ICML 2013).  
- Baseline comparisons need fairness: e.g., IBP Gibbs should be given true noise value as for FAB.  
- Variational IBP setup is under-specified (finite vs infinite, number of restarts) and must be clarified.  
- The accelerated Gibbs result reporting K=70 on Sonar seems suspicious and may indicate poor baseline tuning or initialization.  
- Reference [22] is mischaracterized; Reed & Ghahramani propose an efficient MAP method, not a sampling-based one.  
- Minor correction: noise std-dev setting should be relative to data scale (e.g., fraction of data std-dev), not a fixed value.
