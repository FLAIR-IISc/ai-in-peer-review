- Addresses gap by extending factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) that violate standard Hessian assumptions.  
- Theoretically shows LFMs yield factorized information criteria (FICs) identical in form to mixture models despite non-block-diagonal Hessians (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
- Introduces a practical FAB/LFM algorithm using a mean-field approximation for latent variables.  
- Proposes a novel shrinkage acceleration mechanism (Algorithm 2) that removes irrelevant features early, reducing computation.  
- Proves parameter identifiability for FAB/LFMs (Theorem 4) and uses it to justify a merge post-processing step for compact models.  
- Comprehensive experiments compare FAB/LFMs to IBP, variational Bayesian, and MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Empirical results show improved model selection accuracy, predictive performance, and computational efficiency, especially on larger datasets.  
- Shrinkage acceleration substantially lowers runtime without hurting model quality (Figure 1).  
- Empirical validation supports identifiability claims and benefits of the merge post-processing (Section 3).  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis is supported by extensive experimental validation across multiple metrics.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and accessible to the NIPS audience.  
- Original contribution: non-trivial extension of FAB to LFMs with novel theoretical insights beyond prior FAB work.  
- Significance: enables automated model selection for LFMs with reduced computational cost, useful for large-scale applications.  
- Limited discussion of theoretical limitations under non-asymptotic settings (e.g., small samples or non-Gaussian data).  
- Explanation of relationship between FAB-induced shrinkage and prior-based regularization is brief and could be expanded.  
- Some experimental details (e.g., parameter settings) could be more thoroughly explained.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically useful, and recommended for acceptance at NIPS.
