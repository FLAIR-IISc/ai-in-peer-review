This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection framework with strong empirical results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models (HMMs). In brief, FAB maximizes a lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC), which asymptotically converges to the marginal log-likelihood.

A known limitation of prior FAB applications is the requirement that the Hessian of the complete-data likelihood be block diagonal. The authors show how to apply FAB to LFMs even when this condition does not hold. They derive a valid lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and demonstrate that it takes the same functional form as the FIC used for MMs.

Empirical results on both synthetic and real datasets compare FAB/LFM against competing approaches, including fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The proposed method achieves superior predictive performance while also reducing computational cost.

The paper is clearly written, and the derivations are presented thoroughly and coherently. The contribution can be viewed as an incremental extension from FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs, but it is nevertheless interesting and practically useful. While this is not my primary area of expertise, I believe the work will be of interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a solid paper with a compelling idea and convincing results. I support acceptance.
