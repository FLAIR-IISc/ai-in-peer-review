Summary:
The authors propose an efficient model selection approach for latent feature models (LFMs) based on Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC are well established for mixture models, with FIC approximating the model’s log-likelihood via factorization and a Laplace approximation to enable tractable model selection. The main contribution is to generalize FAB to LFMs, using a mean-field approximation for latent-variable inference and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism on the global number of selected features. A notable strength is the increased automation of model selection with minimal manual tuning. Experiments on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate significant computational efficiency gains.

Quality:
The empirical evaluation is thorough. The method is compared against a wide range of competing approaches across multiple datasets, and the analyses are careful and comprehensive. The references are appropriate and sufficient.

Clarity:
The paper is clearly written and well structured.

Originality/Significance:
This work is a logical extension and solid generalization of prior FAB/FIC-based model selection methods to LFMs. It effectively combines several approximations into a single, practical algorithm that performs well in experiments.

That said, it would be valuable to clarify when and how these approximations might lead to degraded performance. In particular, it would help to “push” the method to its limits and discuss failure modes, given that several approximations (FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage) operate simultaneously.
