- Proposes an algorithm for efficient model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) by generalizing factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).  
- Generalization uses a mean-field approximation for latent inference and accelerated shrinkage of the global feature count.  
- Integrates FIC, mean-field approximation, and accelerated shrinkage into a single automated model-selection method requiring minimal manual tuning.  
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Results are thoroughly analyzed with comprehensive comparisons against a wide range of competing methods.  
- Literature review is adequate and sufficiently referenced.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured, making the technical contributions easy to follow.  
- Novelty lies in extending FAB-based model selection to LFMs and combining multiple approximations effectively.  
- Reviewer recommends stronger evaluation of failure modes by rigorously testing scenarios where the combined approximations may degrade performance.
