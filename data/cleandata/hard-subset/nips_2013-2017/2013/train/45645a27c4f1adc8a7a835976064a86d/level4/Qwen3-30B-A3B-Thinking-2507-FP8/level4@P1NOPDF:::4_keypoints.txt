- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming the Hessian block-diagonal requirement.  
- Derives a valid lower bound for the factorized information criterion (FIC) under LFMs that matches the FIC representation for mixture models.  
- Evaluates on synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM to fast Gibbs (IBP), variational Bayes, and MEIBP.  
- Shows consistent superiority of FAB/LFM in prediction accuracy and computational efficiency.  
- Provides compelling empirical evidence of practical advantages, especially for larger-scale problems.  
- Technical derivations are rigorous and the paper is well-structured and clearly presented.  
- Contribution is incremental but meaningful, filling a clear gap in the literature.  
- Methodology and arguments are sound; writing is clear though minor phrasing improvements are suggested.  
- Overall assessment: results are convincing, the approach is novel within its scope, and the paper merits acceptance.
