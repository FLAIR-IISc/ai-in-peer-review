In this paper, the authors extend the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) approximation for latent feature models. FAB is a recently developed model selection method that yields genuinely good results for mixed models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. In brief, this method maximizes the lower bound of a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-probability. The limitation of FAB is that it is only applicable to models that meet the condition that the Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood must be block diagonal. The authors extend FAB for latent feature models (LFMs) even though this condition is not met. They effectively derive a lower bound of FIC for LFMs and show that it has the same representation as FIC for MMs. They provide results in both synthetic and real-world datasets and compare FAB/LFM with other methods, such as models using the Indian buffet process, rapid Gibbs sampling, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectant IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM; the proposed method claims to perform well not only in prediction tasks but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well-written, and the authors thoroughly describe the steps involved in deriving their method.

The proposed model extends from FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs and will be considered incremental. It is quite interesting and highlights the effectiveness of the results. Although I am not an expert in this field, I believe it will be of great interest to the NIPS community. Overall, it is a beautiful paper that presents a truly interesting idea. The results are convincing, and I support its acceptability.
