- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) by showing LFMs meet a Hessian-like condition required for FAB.
- Provides theoretical results on parameter identifiability supporting FAB’s applicability to LFMs.
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method that significantly reduces computational cost.
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art IBP methods on model selection, predictive performance, and runtime.
- Builds on the FAB framework to offer a tractable marginal log-likelihood approximation for a broader class of models.
- Connects the optimization procedure to the convex-concave procedure (CCCP) for interpretability.
- Strong motivation, solid theoretical grounding, and thorough empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets.
- Comprehensive comparisons to relevant baselines, including IBP and variational Bayes methods.
- Exposition occasionally lacks precision, leading to confusion (e.g., discussion around Eq. (3) and infinite values).
- The asymptotic regime underlying Theorem 2 and how K scales with N are not explicitly stated.
- The relationship between the derived FIC and the IBP’s infinite-component motivation is underexplored.
- Possible mathematical presentation errors: missing expectation in Eq. (4) and ambiguous summation scope in Section 2.
- Empirical evaluation weaknesses: unclear Gibbs sampler stopping criterion and less convincing runtime comparison.
- Need to disentangle improvements due to model selection criterion from those due to optimization convergence/local optima.
- Gibbs sampler sometimes learns an excessive number of components, suggesting convergence or implementation issues.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension, theoretical justification, empirical and computational improvements, and useful shrinkage method.
- Arguments against acceptance: imprecise exposition, unclear asymptotic assumptions, insufficient empirical analysis, and somewhat incremental contribution.
- Recommended revisions: clarify Theorem 2 assumptions (K vs N), discuss FIC limitations for infinite models, specify Gibbs stopping criterion, and fix ambiguous notation.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
