- Proposes a model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters with a BIC-style penalty, similar to variational Bayes but avoids factorized posteriors.  
- Empirically achieves improved held-out likelihood and faster runtime compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Main practical advantage is computational: reduces to an EM algorithm with complexity penalties, avoiding expensive sampling or full VB.  
- Technical novelty is incremental—largely an extension of the existing FAB framework.  
- Equation (3) appears problematic: log of sum over z can diverge to −∞ when all entries are zero, yet q assigns nonzero probability to this event.  
- Theorem 2 lacks a specified asymptotic regime or assumptions, particularly about how K behaves as N grows; BIC-style penalties seem to require fixed finite K.  
- Assuming finite K undermines the motivation for using the IBP, which allows infinitely many components to appear with more data.  
- Minor correction: in Section 2, pk(X | z·,k) should be defined using only data points assigned to component k, not the entire dataset.  
- Minor correction: equation (4) is missing an expectation on the left-hand side.  
- Experimental results show substantial gains in predictive likelihood and speed, but the stopping criterion for FAB and competitors is unspecified, complicating comparisons.  
- Unclear if performance gains are due to the proposed model selection criterion or differences in optimization dynamics (local optima, initialization, implementation).  
- Gibbs sampler learning too many components on some datasets suggests issues with convergence or premature stopping; given enough time Gibbs should target the true posterior that FAB approximates.  
- Overall: approach is sensible and computationally attractive but incremental; math needs clarification and experiments need clearer protocols, convergence diagnostics, sensitivity analysis, and ablation studies to attribute gains to the criterion.
