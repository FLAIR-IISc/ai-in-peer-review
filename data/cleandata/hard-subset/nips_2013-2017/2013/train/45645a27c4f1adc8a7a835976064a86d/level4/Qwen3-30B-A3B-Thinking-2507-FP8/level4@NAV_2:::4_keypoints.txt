- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models by removing the block-diagonal Hessian requirement and proving the FIC for LFMs matches the MM form (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
- Establishes parameter identifiability for FAB/LFMs (Theorem 4).  
- Shows FAB/LFMs inherit automatic dimensionality selection via the log sum_n z_nk penalty.  
- Demonstrates computational efficiency and scalability relative to prior FAB work.  
- Introduces shrinkage acceleration via a CCCP interpretation (Section 3) that significantly reduces runtime while maintaining performance.  
- Provides merge post-processing and Algorithm 2 that yield tangible speedups without accuracy loss.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show superior model selection accuracy compared to IBP, VB, and MEIBP (Fig. 2).  
- Achieves better predictive performance (PLL) and lower overfitting (smaller PLL–TLL gap) than baselines.  
- Reports up to 3.7× speedup over MEIBP, demonstrating substantial computational gains (Table 1, Figure 1).  
- Technical novelty: resolves a key theoretical barrier extending FAB beyond mixture models.  
- Rigor: clear asymptotic analysis and identifiability proofs underpin the method.  
- Evaluation: comprehensive experiments with strong baselines and clear metrics support claims.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-structured derivations and algorithmic details (Section 2.2).  
- Limitation: analysis assumes Gaussian noise (Eq. 5); non-Gaussian LFMs (e.g., Poisson) are not addressed.  
- Limitation: comparisons omit some recent LFM methods (e.g., [10, 17]) that could be stronger baselines.  
- Limitation: lacks ablation on hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., delta in Algorithm 1) beyond a fixed value.  
- Overall judgment: significant theoretical and practical contribution to scalable latent feature modeling; recommendation to accept.
