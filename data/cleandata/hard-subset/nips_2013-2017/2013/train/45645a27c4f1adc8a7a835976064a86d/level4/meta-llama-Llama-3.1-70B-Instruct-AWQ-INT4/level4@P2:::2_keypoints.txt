- Summary: Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) using mean-field approximation and accelerated shrinkage for automated model selection.
- Quality: Thorough analysis and empirical evaluation against multiple baselines and datasets, showing effective performance.
- Clarity: Well-written and structured with clear technical explanations and background.
- Originality/Significance: Logical and useful generalization of FAB to LFMs, but needs investigation of approximation limits and failure cases.
- Strength — Efficient model selection: Enables automatic model selection with minimal manual tuning.
- Strength — FAB generalization: Successfully expands FAB inference applicability to LFMs.
- Strength — Accelerated shrinkage: Shrinkage technique reduces computational cost significantly.
- Weakness — Approximation limitations: Reliance on FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage approximations may harm performance in some scenarios.
- Weakness — Lack of sensitivity analysis: Paper does not analyze how performance varies with parameters or data characteristics.
- For acceptance — Computational gains: Provides substantial computational efficiency while maintaining strong performance.
- For acceptance — Strong evaluation: Thorough analysis and comparisons support the method’s effectiveness.
- For acceptance — Field contribution: Valuable advancement in model selection for LFMs.
- Against acceptance — Approximation risks: Multiple approximations could lead to poor results in certain cases.
- Against acceptance — Insufficient investigation: Authors did not thoroughly probe approximation limitations or sensitivity.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focusing on exploring approximation limitations, sensitivity analysis, and potential failure-mode mitigation.
