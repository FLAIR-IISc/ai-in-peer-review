- Introduces an EM-based FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that includes a shrinkage mechanism to infer the number of features K.
- Uses the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework instead of sparsity-inducing priors to encourage smaller K via a penalty in the approximate marginal likelihood.
- Algorithm starts from an upper bound on K and learns the appropriate K during inference.
- Experiments claim FAB outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP methods in runtime and inference quality on large datasets.
- Technical approach appears sound and algorithmic details seem correct.
- Method can scale to large datasets while simultaneously learning model complexity, which is appealing.
- Methodology section is reasonably clear but several experimental details are ambiguous or under-described.
- Application of FAB to latent feature models is novel (FAB previously used for mixtures and HMMs).
- Paper addresses an important problem: efficient inference in LFMs with automatic model selection.
- Lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations and scenarios where it may perform worse than standard methods like Gibbs sampling.
- No discussion of potential convergence issues inherent to EM-like procedures.
- Restricted to binary latent features; cannot be directly applied to continuous latent models like factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains flaws that weaken comparisons.
- Unclear performance behavior in small-data regimes where asymptotic justification may fail; experiments/discussion on this are missing.
- Fails to cite or discuss some relevant recent work on efficient LFM inference (e.g., MAD-Bayes).
- Comparison with MEIBP is invalid for some experiments because MEIBP assumes non-negative W while synthetic data used real-valued W.
- For block images experiment FAB was given true noise variance while IBP Gibbs was not—unfair experimental setup.
- Used finite variational VB-IBP rather than infinite version and did not detail VB experimental settings (e.g., restarts).
- Suspects Gibbs sampler’s overestimation of K (e.g., Sonar dataset) may be due to poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance.
- Mischaracterizes reference [22] (MEIBP) as a Gibbs sampling method; it is a MAP method and should be corrected.
- Minor mistake: noise standard deviation recommendation from prior work was misinterpreted (should be 0.25× data std, not fixed 0.75).
- After author feedback some questions were answered, but major issues remain: clearer experimental methodology, justification for baseline behaviors, correction of [22] description, and discussion of MAD-Bayes.
- Overall: proposed method is interesting and scalable, but experimental evaluation must be redone and better described before acceptance.
