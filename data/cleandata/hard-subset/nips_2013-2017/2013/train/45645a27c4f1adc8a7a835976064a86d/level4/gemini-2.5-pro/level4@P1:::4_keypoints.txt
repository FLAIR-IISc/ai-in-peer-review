- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming the block-diagonal Hessian limitation.  
- Shows (Lemma 1) that the FIC for LFMs has the same functional form as for mixture models, enabling FAB application to LFMs.  
- Proposes a complete FAB/LFM algorithm with a CCCP-derived shrinkage acceleration technique.  
- Analyzes parameter identifiability and includes a principled post-processing step to merge redundant features.  
- Theoretical claims (Lemma 1, Theorems 2 and 4) are technically sound and supported by supplementary proofs.  
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real data comparing to IBP-based baselines (fast Gibbs, VB, MEIBP).  
- Empirical results show superior or competitive model selection and predictive performance, often with lower computational cost.  
- Produces more compact models (fewer features K) and smaller train–test likelihood gaps, suggesting reduced overfitting.  
- CCCP-based acceleration substantially reduces computation time for feature shrinkage (Figure 1).  
- Manuscript is clear, well-organized, and derivations are thoroughly explained.  
- Original contribution: novel asymptotic analysis for LFMs and the insight that log(sum_n z_nk) emerges despite shared parameters.  
- Significance: provides a practical, automated, and efficient alternative to nonparametric Bayesian methods for LFMs.  
- Weakness: could be viewed as an incremental extension of FAB rather than a completely new paradigm.  
- Evaluation is honest and complete, noting a small-sample failure case (Libras dataset).  
- Overall recommendation: strong support for acceptance.
