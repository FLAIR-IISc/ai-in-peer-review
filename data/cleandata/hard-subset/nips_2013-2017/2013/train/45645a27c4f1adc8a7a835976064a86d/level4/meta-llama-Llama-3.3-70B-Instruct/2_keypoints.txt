- Proposes a novel algorithm for efficient model selection in latent feature models using factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).
- Extends FAB/FIC from mixture models to latent feature models with a mean-field approximation for latent inference.
- Adds an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to reduce the global number of selected features.
- Method improves automaticity of model selection, minimizing manual tuning.
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate substantial computational efficiency gains.
- Evaluation is comprehensive across many competing approaches and references are adequate.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- Represents a significant, logical extension by combining multiple approximations into one method.
- Reviewer suggests studying limitations and failure modes of the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) to assess robustness.
