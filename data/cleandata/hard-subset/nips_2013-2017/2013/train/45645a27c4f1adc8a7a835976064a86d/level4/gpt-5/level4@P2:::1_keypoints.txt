- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features, deriving a factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs.  
- Shows FIC for LFMs has the same functional form as for mixture models, producing automatic feature shrinkage via log sum_n z_nk terms.  
- Proposes an EM-like algorithm alternating a mean-field E-step (interpreted as CCCP updates) and closed-form M-steps, plus shrinkage acceleration and a post-hoc merge step.  
- Empirically outperforms several IBP-based baselines and a variational EM LFM in wall-clock time and predictive log-likelihood on multiple datasets.  
- Builds on prior FAB results for MMs/HMMs and connects to IBP-based and asymptotic approaches (e.g., MAD-Bayes).  
- Conceptual strength: nontrivial extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs despite lack of block-diagonal Hessian, supported by an asymptotic determinant result (Lemma 1).  
- Practical strength: scalable EM-like optimization avoiding MCMC/complex variational methods, with engineering refinements (CCCP acceleration; merge step).  
- Empirical strength: competitive or superior predictive likelihood, faster runtimes, automatic K selection without hand-tuned hyperparameters, reduced overfitting.  
- Key weakness: asymptotic regime and assumptions unclear — unclear whether K is fixed or can grow with N and what regularity conditions are required.  
- Concern that the argument seems to require each feature’s activation count to diverge, potentially conflicting with IBP’s growing-K setting.  
- Issue with unused features: FIC contains log(sum_n z_nk), which is −∞ if a feature is never used; need explanation why Eq[log(sum_n z_nk)] remains finite under non-degenerate q.  
- Paper removes always-off/always-on features and linearizes around π̃k > 0, but precise conditions ensuring finiteness and monotonicity of the bound are not stated.  
- Clarity/precision issues: notation in MM discussion (pk should depend only on assigned data), missing expectation in Eq. (4), and missing explicit assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 in main text.  
- Experimental protocol concerns: stopping criteria and number of CCCP inner steps unclear; fairness of runtime comparisons questionable without standardized stopping or fixed time budgets.  
- Sensitivity to initialization and local optima not fully explored; need restarts, ablations holding K fixed, or matched initializations to separate selection vs optimization effects.  
- Parameter-learning discrepancy: some baselines fix noise variance while FAB learns it, which may bias comparisons; run controls where noise variance settings are matched.  
- Technical quality: generally sound and plausible asymptotic argument, but key assumptions and regimes should be explicit.  
- Clarity: well organized overall and useful CCCP perspective, but several equations, assumptions, and notational ambiguities need correction and summary in main text.  
- Originality: natural but meaningful extension of prior FAB results to LFMs; CCCP acceleration and identifiability-driven merge are useful refinements.  
- Significance: could broaden FAB applicability to other latent models and offer a practical alternative to IBP inference, but empirical claims need stronger ablations and clearer protocols.  
- Suggested improvements: precisely state asymptotic regime (K behavior, growth rates of sum_n z_nk) and assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify how FIC avoids −∞ for unused features and how shrinkage/removal steps affect bound validity and monotonicity.  
- Suggested improvements: fix notation issues (Section 2, Eq. 4) and add a concise summary of assumptions for main theoretical results in the main text.  
- Suggested improvements: standardize stopping criteria across methods, report fixed time-budget comparisons, and add ablations controlling for K, initialization, and noise-variance learning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs, practical scalable algorithm with effective shrinkage, and promising empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear asymptotic assumptions (especially K growth), imprecise math/notation in places, and experimental comparisons need stronger controls.  
- Overall: sensible and practically valuable extension with promising results, but would be significantly strengthened by clarifying asymptotic setup, tightening derivations, and improving experimental fairness.
