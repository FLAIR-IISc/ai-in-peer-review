- Proposes an EM-based FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that learns feature assignments zn and infers K via shrinkage.
- Uses a model-selection penalty (FAB) instead of sparsity priors, setting an upper bound on K and shrinking unused features.
- Claims superior runtime and inference quality on large datasets compared to IBP Gibbs, variational, and MAP methods.
- Technical development and algorithmic details appear sound and correct.
- Method is scalable and can automatically determine the number of latent features.
- Presentation is generally clear but some experimental details are insufficiently explained.
- Novel application of the FAB framework to latent feature models.
- Addresses a significant problem: efficient inference in LFMs with automatic K selection.
- Paper fails to discuss limitations or settings where it may underperform relative to Gibbs sampling.
- Does not address convergence challenges inherent to EM-like procedures.
- Method is limited to binary LFMs and not applicable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains apparent flaws.
- Behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotics may not hold) is unclear and unevaluated.
- Missing discussion of recent small-variance asymptotics work (e.g., MAD-Bayes).
- MEIBP was inappropriately used as a baseline since it requires nonnegative W; synthetic and block experiments are invalid for MEIBP comparison.
- FAB was given true noise in block image experiments; IBP Gibbs should be given the same noise for a fair comparison.
- VB-IBP experiments used a finite truncation and under-specified VB settings (e.g., restarts).
- Unusual result of ~70 features from accelerated Gibbs on Sonar likely due to poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance.
- Reference [21] is mischaracterized (MAP inference, not Gibbs) and should be corrected.
- Minor: recommended noise std-dev for real data is 0.25× empirical std-dev, not a fixed 0.75.
- After author feedback: experimental methodology needs clearer description/justification, include MAD-Bayes reference; method is promising but experiments must be improved before acceptance.
