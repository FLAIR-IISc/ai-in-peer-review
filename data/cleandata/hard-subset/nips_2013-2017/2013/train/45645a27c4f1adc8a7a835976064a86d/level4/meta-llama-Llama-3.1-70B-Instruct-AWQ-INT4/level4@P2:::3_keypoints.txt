- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm using FAB shrinkage for binary latent feature models to learn latent codes z and infer number of features K, analogous to IBP but using model selection instead of priors.  
- Technical approach appears sound and algorithmic details are correct.  
- Algorithm scales to large datasets and can infer K, which is appealing.  
- Paper lacks discussion of limitations and cases where it may perform worse than vanilla Gibbs sampling.  
- No discussion of convergence difficulties despite being an EM-like procedure.  
- Method is limited to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and contains potential flaws.  
- Behaviour in the small-data (non-asymptotic) regime is unclear and not evaluated.  
- Some relevant recent works on efficient inference in LFMs are missing from the related work.  
- The MEIBP baseline is inapplicable to the authors’ simulated and block-data experiments (MEIBP requires non-negative W).  
- For block images, the IBP Gibbs baseline was not given the same known noise value, creating an unfair comparison.  
- VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version and experimental settings (e.g., restarts) are not well described.  
- The accelerated Gibbs result finding ~70 features on 49-D Sonar data is suspicious and likely due to bad initialization or mis-specified noise variance.  
- Reference [21] is mischaracterized — it does MAP inference for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling.  
- Paper should cite and discuss MAD-Bayes / small-variance asymptotic work for nonparametric LFMs.  
- Minor correction: suggested std-dev setting in prior work is 0.25 of example std-dev across dimensions, not 0.75.  
- After author feedback: authors addressed some questions, but experimental methodology, baseline settings, and explanations for algorithm behaviours still need improvement before acceptance.
