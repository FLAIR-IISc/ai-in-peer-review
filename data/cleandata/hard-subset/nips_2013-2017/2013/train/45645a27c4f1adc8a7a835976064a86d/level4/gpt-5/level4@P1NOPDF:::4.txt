This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection method with strong results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. In brief, it maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A known limitation of FAB is that it has been applicable only to models where the Hessian of the complete-data likelihood is block-diagonal. The authors extend FAB to LFMs even though this condition is not satisfied. They derive a valid lower bound of FIC for LFMs and show that it has the same form as the FIC for MMs.

The paper provides results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM to alternatives such as fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results illustrate the superiority of FAB/LFM: the proposed method reports better performance not only on prediction tasks but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well written, and the derivations are presented clearly and thoroughly. The proposed approach extends FAB from MMs to LFMs; I would consider this an incremental contribution. Nevertheless, it is interesting, and the results underscore its efficiency. Although I am not an expert in this area, I believe it would be of great interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a compelling idea with convincing results, and I support its acceptance.
