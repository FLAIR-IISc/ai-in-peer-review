The paper proposes a FAB-based inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that uses shrinkage to infer the number of latent features and scales to large datasets.

Technically sound algorithmic details and attractive ability to handle large datasets and infer K.

Paper lacks discussion of limitations, e.g., cases where it may underperform vanilla Gibbs sampling.

No discussion of potential convergence difficulties of the EM-like procedure.

Method is restricted to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.

Experimental evaluation is not thorough and contains potentially flawed comparisons.

Behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotic FAB arguments break down) is unclear.

Some recent efficient-inference works for LFMs are not cited or discussed.

MEIBP baseline is inappropriately used since it applies only to non-negative linear Gaussian models, invalidating some experiments.

For block images, IBP Gibbs should be given known noise for a fair comparison.

VB-IBP experiments should consider the infinite variational version and provide more experimental details.

Accelerated Gibbs finding ~70 features on 49-dim Sonar data is suspicious and may reflect bad initialization or mispecified noise variance.

Reference [21] is mischaracterized: it does MAP inference for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling.

MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and other small-variance asymptotics work for nonparametric LFMs should be cited and discussed.

Minor correction: suggested real-data noise setting in [1,22] is 0.25 times the examples' standard deviation, not 0.75.

Post-feedback required fixes: clarify experimental methodology and baseline settings and explain anomalous algorithm behaviors.

Post-feedback required fix: correct reference [21] to indicate MAP inference.

Post-feedback required fix: include reference/discussion of MAD-Bayes inference for the IBP.
