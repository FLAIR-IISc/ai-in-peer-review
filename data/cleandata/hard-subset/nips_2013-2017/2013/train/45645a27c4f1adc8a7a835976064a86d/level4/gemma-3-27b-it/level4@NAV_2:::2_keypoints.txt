- Proposes efficient model selection for latent feature models by generalizing factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).
- Uses mean field approximation for latent variable inference and an accelerated shrinkage technique to promote feature selection.
- Method is largely automated and requires minimal manual parameter tuning.
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets compared to competing methods.
- Thorough experimental evaluation against a diverse set of baselines, with comprehensive referencing.
- Clear, well-structured writing that facilitates understanding of the method and principles.
- Theoretical analysis provides insights on parameter identifiability and principled feature merging.
- Novel contribution is the combination of multiple approximations into a practical, effective method for LFMs.
- Suggested future work: investigate limitations and failure modes where FIC, mean field, and shrinkage interactions may be suboptimal.
- Argument for acceptance: addresses an important problem with a theoretically grounded, novel approach and strong empirical efficiency gains.
- Argument against acceptance: reliance on several approximations raises robustness concerns across different conditions.
- Argument against acceptance: would benefit from deeper analysis of sensitivity to varying data characteristics.
