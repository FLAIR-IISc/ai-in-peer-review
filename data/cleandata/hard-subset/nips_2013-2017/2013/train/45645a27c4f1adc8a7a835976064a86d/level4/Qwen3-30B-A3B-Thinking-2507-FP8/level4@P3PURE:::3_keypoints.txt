- Paper introduces an EM-based inference algorithm using FAB shrinkage for binary latent feature models to infer binary feature vectors and automatically select K, claiming better scalability and inference quality than Gibbs, VB, and MAP IBP.  
- Technical approach is sound and correctly detailed; significant strength is scalability and automatic K inference on large data.  
- Methodology is clearly presented, but experimental details are insufficiently described.  
- Novelty: adapting FAB (previously for mixtures/HMMs) to LFMs is a novel contribution.  
- Significance: addresses efficient inference in LFMs with automatic selection of the number of features K.  
- Missing discussion of failure modes and cases where the algorithm may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling (e.g., sensitivity to initialization or hyperparameters).  
- No analysis of convergence behavior for the EM-like procedure.  
- Scope limited to binary LFMs; not applicable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental comparison flawed: MEIBP baseline requires non-negative loading matrix W, but experiments use general W, invalidating that comparison.  
- For block-image experiments, the IBP Gibbs sampler was unfairly given a heuristic noise std (0.75) instead of the true value provided to FAB.  
- VB-IBP comparison used the finite variational version instead of the infinite variant; experimental settings like restarts and hyperparameter tuning are underreported.  
- The Gibbs sampler finding 70 features on 49-dimensional Sonar data is suspicious and likely due to poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance; needs investigation.  
- No experiments or discussion for small-data regimes where FAB’s asymptotic approximation may break down.  
- Missing literature: small-variance asymptotic inference for nonparametric LFMs (e.g., MAD-Bayes, ICML 2013) is not cited or discussed.  
- Reference error: [21] is mischaracterized as Gibbs sampling (it describes MAP inference); this remains uncorrected after author feedback.  
- Minor correction: recommended noise std in cited refs is 0.25 * std(x), not 0.75.  
- Authors addressed some concerns but critical issues remain: experimental methodology, reference [21], and inclusion/discussion of MAD-Bayes.  
- Final recommendation: promising core idea but requires revision—correct experimental methodology and comparisons, address cited weaknesses (including references and small-data behavior) before acceptance.
