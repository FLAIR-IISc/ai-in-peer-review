- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) despite LFMs not meeting the block-diagonal Hessian condition, using asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1) to show FIC has same form as mixture models.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical proofs (supplementary materials) supporting the extension.  
- Introduces a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration that removes irrelevant features early and significantly reduces computational cost.  
- Establishes parameter identifiability for FAB/LFMs, enabling a merge post-processing step that improves compactness and performance.  
- FAB/LFMs inherit desirable FAB/MM properties: automatic hidden-state selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic increase of the lower FIC bound during optimization.  
- Carefully positions the work relative to IBP, VB, MEIBP, and distinguishes it from MAD-Bayes (different asymptotic regime).  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real data shows FAB/LFMs outperform IBP, VB, and MEIBP in model selection accuracy, computational cost, and predictive performance.  
- Analysis demonstrates FAB/LFM regularization reduces overfitting compared to competing methods.  
- Paper is clearly written, with precise algorithm descriptions, reproducible experimental setup, and a well-explained FIC derivation.  
- Original theoretical contribution: broadens applicability of FAB by showing LFMs can achieve FIC equivalence to MMs.  
- Practical contribution: CCCP-based shrinkage substantially improves computational efficiency for large-scale problems.  
- Significant impact: addresses a core FAB limitation, offers hyperparameter-free automatic model selection, and scales better than current state-of-the-art methods.  
- Limitation: assumes a Gaussian noise model, restricting applicability to non-Gaussian settings.  
- Limitation: identifiability theorem depends on technical conditions that may not hold universally in practice.  
- Limitation: merge post-processing, while theoretically sound, needs more detailed empirical analysis across different data types.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, well-motivated, and suitable for NIPS; recommend acceptance.
