- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), broadening FAB’s applicability beyond mixture models.  
- Shows the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has a similar form to that for mixture models, enabling FAB use in LFMs.  
- FAB/LFMs provide automatic hidden-state dimensionality selection without manual hyperparameter tuning.  
- Demonstrates parameter identifiability for LFMs and proposes a merging post-processing step to improve interpretability.  
- Relates the FAB E-step to a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and introduces a shrinkage acceleration method to reduce computational cost.  
- Empirically outperforms Indian Buffet Process (IBP) baselines and competes strongly with VB and MEIBP in model selection, prediction, and speed.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous derivation of FIC for LFMs under different assumptions than prior FAB work.  
- Computational efficiency: proposed shrinkage acceleration substantially improves runtime compared to existing LFM methods.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation on synthetic and real datasets, comparing to multiple competing methods.  
- Weakness — relies on multiple approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage); cumulative failure modes are not fully explored.  
- Weakness — limited analysis of sensitivity to initialization and implementation details.  
- Weakness — paper lacks discussion of negative results or scenarios where the method performs poorly.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded extension with strong empirical and computational benefits.  
- Argument against acceptance: dependence on stacked approximations and insufficient exploration of robustness and failure cases.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with valuable contributions; acceptance recommended, subject to further robustness analysis.
