- Proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that approximates parameter marginal likelihood using BIC instead of a factorized posterior.
- Shows improved held-out likelihoods compared to several IBP implementations.
- Method is well-motivated by an asymptotic argument and is computationally more efficient, resembling an EM algorithm with complexity penalties.
- Technical contribution is novel but largely an incremental extension of the FAB framework.
- Exposition has mathematical imprecision: Equation (3)’s log(sum_k z_nk) is undefined (−∞) if all z_nk are zero, which can occur under variational q.
- Theorem 2 is unclear about the asymptotic regime and assumptions, notably how K (number of features) scales with N.
- Applying BIC per feature seems to implicitly assume fixed finite K, conflicting with the IBP motivation of a growing/infinite feature set.
- Minor formulation issues: p_k(X | z_{.,k}) appears defined over full X instead of only data assigned to feature k, and Eq. (4) seems to miss an expectation under q.
- Quantitative results report higher predictive likelihoods in less time, but stopping criteria (e.g., Gibbs sampler termination) are unspecified, making runtimes and comparisons hard to interpret.
- It’s unclear whether performance gains come from the model-selection criterion or from optimization/implementation differences (local optima, initialization).
- Evidence of Gibbs learning excessively many features on some datasets suggests poor mixing or optimization confounds rather than a clear superiority of the criterion.
- Overall: well-motivated and efficient but incremental; mathematical precision and experimental analysis are insufficient to convincingly attribute gains to the proposed method.
