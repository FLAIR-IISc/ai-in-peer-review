- Novel extension of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Theoretical finding that LFMs yield the same factorized information criterion (FIC) form as mixture models despite not meeting the block-diagonal Hessian condition.  
- FAB/LFM automatically selects the number of latent features via regularization without priors or hyperparameters.  
- Empirical claims: FAB/LFM outperforms variational inference, Gibbs sampling, and MEIBP in model selection, prediction accuracy, and runtime.  
- Technical development is sound with rigorous asymptotic analysis and supplementary proofs.  
- Algorithmic implementation appears correct and introduces a novel shrinkage mechanism for automatic feature selection.  
- Computational efficiency claims are supported by experiments.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and clearly written; methodology and algorithmic details are well explained.  
- Experimental descriptions lack sufficient detail for reproducibility (initializations, parameter settings for baselines).  
- Noise variance selection in real-world experiments is insufficiently justified.  
- Originality: meaningful and novel adaptation of FAB to LFMs, expanding FIC applicability.  
- Significance: addresses efficient inference and hyperparameter-free model selection, valuable for large-scale applications.  
- Strength: demonstrates rigorous theoretical analysis showing FIC equivalence with mixture models.  
- Strength: provides automatic feature selection without priors or hyperparameters.  
- Strength: shows clear empirical comparison with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of algorithm limitations, especially in small-data regimes where asymptotic assumptions may fail.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of convergence behavior for the EM-like procedure.  
- Weakness: scope restricted to binary latent feature models, excluding models like probabilistic PCA.  
- Weakness: experimental issues — MEIBP comparison invalid on synthetic data because MEIBP requires non-negative W while synthetic W is real-valued.  
- Weakness: IBP Gibbs sampler in block data experiment used a heuristic noise variance instead of the same 0.3 as FAB.  
- Weakness: VB-IBP comparison used a finite-K variant rather than the infinite variational approach.  
- Weakness: accelerated Gibbs sampling result (70 features on Sonar) suggests problems with initialization or noise variance.  
- Weakness: reference [21] mislabeled as Gibbs sampling when it describes MAP inference.  
- Suggestions: clarify why FAB works well in small-data regimes despite asymptotic assumptions.  
- Suggestions: discuss related work such as MAD-Bayes that applies asymptotic analysis to nonparametric LFMs.  
- Suggestions: fix MEIBP applicability issues in experiments and align noise variance choices across methods.  
- Suggestions: provide detailed experimental methodology (parameter settings, initializations) for reproducibility.  
- Suggestions: correct the reference to [21] (it is MAP inference, not Gibbs sampling).
