- Introduces a BIC-style model selection criterion for binary latent feature models implemented as an EM algorithm that approximately integrates out parameters and reports improved held-out likelihoods.
- Strength: Method is reasonable and supported by a plausible asymptotic motivation.
- Strength: Computational advantage—reduces to an EM-like procedure with complexity penalties, avoiding costly sampling or full variational inference.
- Strength: Empirical results show improved predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes in several cases.
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental, mainly extending the existing FAB framework rather than introducing a fundamentally new principle.
- Weakness: Equation (3) is problematic—log of sum of z can diverge to −∞ if all z are zero, which has nonzero probability under nondegenerate q, so why wouldn’t the FIC score be −∞ almost surely?
- Weakness: Theorem 2 is imprecise about the asymptotic regime and assumptions—unclear what happens to K as N grows; derivation seems to assume fixed finite K, conflicting with the IBP’s infinite-component behavior.
- Experimental concern: Table 1 comparisons are ambiguous because stopping criteria (especially for the Gibbs sampler) are unspecified, making runtime and likelihood comparisons unclear.
- Experimental concern: Unclear whether predictive gains come from the model selection criterion itself or from optimization artifacts/local optima and implementation details (e.g., initialization); Gibbs learning too many components hints at local optima issues.
- Minor: In Section 2, p_k(X | z_{·,k}) should depend only on data assigned to component k, not the entire dataset.
- Minor: Equation (4) appears to be missing an expectation on the left-hand side.
- Minor: Paper contains typos and inconsistent notation; some definitions and conditioning sets need clarification.
- Overall: Sensible and practically appealing approach but feels incremental; needs clearer asymptotic assumptions (K vs N), tightened derivations (e.g., Eq. 3), and standardized experimental protocols (explicit stopping rules and ablations) to strengthen the paper.
