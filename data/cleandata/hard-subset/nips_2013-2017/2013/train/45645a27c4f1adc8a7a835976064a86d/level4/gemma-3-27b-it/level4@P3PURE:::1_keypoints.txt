- Proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters using a BIC-like approximation instead of factorized variational posteriors.  
- Demonstrates improved held-out likelihoods versus several IBP implementations.  
- Method is well-motivated asymptotically and corresponds to an EM algorithm with complexity penalties, offering computational efficiency over sampling or variational Bayes.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, extending previous FAB work [3],[4].  
- Mathematical notation and exposition lack precision, requiring clarification.  
- Equation (3) is problematic: the log-sum term can be infinite if all z values are zero, and the authors do not explain why FIC would not diverge under nondegenerate q.  
- Theorem 2’s asymptotic approximation is vague: the asymptotic regime and assumptions (particularly how K behaves as N→∞) are not specified.  
- Applying BIC per component seems to assume fixed finite K, which contradicts the IBP’s allowance for unbounded K and ongoing feature discovery.  
- Minor issues: pk(X | z·,k) should condition only on data assigned to component k, and an expectation symbol is missing on the LHS of equation (4).  
- Quantitative results (Table 1) show higher predictive likelihoods and lower runtimes for FAB, but the stopping criterion for competing methods is unspecified, complicating comparisons.  
- The Gibbs sampler’s large number of learned components and potential local optima raise questions, since Gibbs should compute the likelihood more exactly than FAB’s approximation.  
- Unclear whether predictive gains stem from the proposed model selection criterion or from differences in optimization/initialization; additional experiments are needed to attribute improvements.  
- Overall assessment: sensible and computationally attractive approach but incremental, with required clarifications in theory, notation, and deeper experimental analysis.
