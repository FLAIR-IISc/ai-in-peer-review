- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- FAB maximizes a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood.  
- Prior FAB required block-diagonal Hessian of the complete-data likelihood, a limitation for many models.  
- Authors remove this limitation by deriving a lower bound of the FIC applicable to LFMs.  
- The derived FIC lower bound for LFMs has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models.  
- Empirical evaluation includes synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Compared against fast Gibbs for IBP, variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP baselines.  
- Results show superior predictive performance and lower computational cost for FAB/LFM.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough derivations.  
- Extension is incremental but non-trivial and demonstrates practical efficiency.  
- Contribution is relevant and of interest to the NIPS community; reviewer recommends acceptance.
