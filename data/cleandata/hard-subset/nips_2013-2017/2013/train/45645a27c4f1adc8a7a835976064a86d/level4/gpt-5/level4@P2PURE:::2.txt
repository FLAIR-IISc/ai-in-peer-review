Summary:
The authors propose an algorithm/approximation method for efficient model selection in latent feature models using factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC are well known in the context of mixture models, where FIC approximates a model’s log-likelihood via factorization and a Laplace approximation to enable tractable model selection. The main contribution is to generalize FAB to latent feature models, employing a mean-field approximation for latent inference and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to reduce the global number of selected features. A key advantage is more automated model selection with minimal manual tuning. Results on both synthetic and real data demonstrate significant gains in computational efficiency.

Quality:
The results are thoroughly analyzed, and the method is compared against a broad set of competing approaches across multiple data sets. The references are adequate.

Clarity:
The paper is clearly written and well structured.

Originality/Significance:
This appears to be a logical extension and solid generalization of prior work on model selection in latent feature models using FAB and FIC, notably by combining several approximations into a single, effective method.

A valuable addition would be a deeper analysis of failure modes: when and how these approximations might lead to poor performance. In particular, it would help to “push” the method until it breaks, given that multiple approximations (FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage) are operating simultaneously.
