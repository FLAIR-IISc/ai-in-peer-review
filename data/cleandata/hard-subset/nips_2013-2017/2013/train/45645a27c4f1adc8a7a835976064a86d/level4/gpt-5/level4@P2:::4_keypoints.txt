- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs) via asymptotic Hessian analysis.
- Shows the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models despite lacking a block-diagonal Hessian.
- Proposes a FAB/LFM algorithm that optimizes a tractable lower bound with alternating updates and a mean-field approximation for binary features.
- Introduces a CCCP-based acceleration that enforces automatic shrinkage of irrelevant features and a merge post-processing step motivated by identifiability analysis.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show accurate model selection, competitive or superior predictive likelihood, and favorable runtime versus IBP-based methods and VB.
- Builds on prior FAB successes in MMs/HMMs and addresses computational limits of IBP/MEIBP/VB for inferring feature count.
- Main novelty is Lemma 1: asymptotic Hessian analysis enabling FAB in LFMs where prior FAB assumptions fail.
- Theoretical contribution includes asymptotic consistency of FIC with marginal log-likelihood and discussion of identifiability.
- Strength: principled derivation that nontrivially extends FAB to a new model class.
- Strength: automatic, prior-free model selection producing compact models without hand tuning.
- Strength: practical algorithmic refinements (CCCP shrinkage, merge) that improve speed and model compactness.
- Weakness: largely an incremental extension of existing FAB methodology rather than a wholly new paradigm.
- Weakness: key results rely on “mild” asymptotic assumptions deferred to the supplement; robustness under weakened assumptions is underexplored.
- Weakness: use of mean-field factorization over Z may limit posterior fidelity; impact on model selection not deeply analyzed.
- Weakness: baseline comparisons have coverage/fairness issues (fixed noise variance, missing VB on larger datasets, MEIBP inapplicability).
- Weakness: limited sensitivity analyses (initialization, Tshrink, stopping criteria) and robustness checks.
- Clarity: paper is well written and organized, but main-text statement of Lemma 1 assumptions would improve readability.
- Originality: moderately original — nontrivial technical analysis but builds on existing FAB ideas.
- Significance: useful for practitioners needing scalable, automatic model selection in LFMs; merging heuristic is practically valuable.
- Suggested improvements: state Lemma 1 assumptions in the main text, add sensitivity/ablation studies, include a medium-scale VB comparison or clarify trade-offs, and discuss extensions beyond linear-Gaussian LFMs.
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical advance enabling FAB for LFMs, practical automatic feature selection, strong empirical performance, and community relevance.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental nature, reliance on asymptotic/mean-field assumptions with limited robustness analysis, and some baseline comparison gaps.
- Overall assessment: well-executed, nontrivial extension of FAB to LFMs with theoretical and practical value; recommended for acceptance.
