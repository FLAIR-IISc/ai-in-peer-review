- Proposes an algorithm to improve model selection in latent feature models using factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).
- Extends FIC to LFMs by applying a mean-field approximation for latent inference and accelerated shrinkage to select the number of features.
- Builds on factorization and Laplace approximation techniques adapted from mixture-model literature.
- Method minimizes manual parameter tuning by automating model selection.
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets show substantial computational efficiency gains.
- Rigorous evaluation against a diverse set of competing techniques across multiple datasets.
- Analysis is comprehensive, references are adequate, and the paper is well written and organized.
- Represents a meaningful generalization of FAB-/FIC-based model selection and an effective integration of multiple approximation techniques.
- Lacks a detailed discussion of limitations; should examine scenarios where combining FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage might produce suboptimal results or violate assumptions.
