- Proposal: Generalizes Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) to latent feature models (LFMs) using mean-field latent inference and accelerated shrinkage on global feature count.
- Contribution: Combines FIC, mean-field approximation, and a shrinkage mechanism into a practical algorithm for automated model selection in LFMs.
- Strength: Increased automation with minimal manual tuning for model selection.
- Empirical results: Thorough evaluation on synthetic and real datasets showing significant computational efficiency gains and strong comparisons to baselines.
- Quality: Experiments are comprehensive and references are appropriate and sufficient.
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written and well structured.
- Originality/significance: Logical and solid extension of prior FAB/FIC methods to LFMs that performs well in practice.
- Limitation/concern: Needs discussion of failure modes and conditions where combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) may degrade performance; suggest stress-testing the method.
