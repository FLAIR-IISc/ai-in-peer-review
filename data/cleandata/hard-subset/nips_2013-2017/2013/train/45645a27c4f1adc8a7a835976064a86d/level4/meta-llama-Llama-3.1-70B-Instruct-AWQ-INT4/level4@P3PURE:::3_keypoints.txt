- Presents an EM-based FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that shrinks features and infers K without explicit priors, claiming better speed and quality on large datasets.  
- Technical approach appears sound and scales to large datasets while learning the number of latent features.  
- Methodology section is reasonably clear, but experimental details are under-specified.  
- Applying FAB to latent feature models is novel relative to prior FAB uses in mixtures/HMMs.  
- Addresses an important problem: efficient inference in LFMs with automatic model selection.  
- Paper lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations and failure modes compared to Gibbs sampling.  
- No discussion of potential convergence difficulties of the EM-like procedure.  
- Method is restricted to binary LFMs and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and contains flaws.  
- Behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotics fail) is unexplored and untested.  
- Paper omits discussion of recent efficient-inference works in LFMs (e.g., MAD-Bayes).  
- MEIBP baseline comparison is invalid because MEIBP requires non-negative W, making some synthetic/block experiments inappropriate.  
- For block images, FAB was given known noise; IBP Gibbs should be given the same value for fair comparison.  
- VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version; the infinite variational option and experiment settings (e.g., restarts) are not detailed.  
- Accelerated Gibbs finding ~70 features on 49-d Sonar is suspicious and potentially due to bad initialization or mis-specified noise variance.  
- Reference [21] is mislabeled as Gibbs sampling but actually reports MAP inference; this and related citations must be corrected.  
- Reviewer requests inclusion/discussion of MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and other small-variance asymptotics work.  
- Minor correction: line 349 advice on standard deviation should be 0.25 times data SD, not 0.75.  
- After author feedback: authors addressed some points but must improve experimental methodology explanations, fix reference errors, include MAD-Bayes, and correct/strengthen empirical evaluation.
