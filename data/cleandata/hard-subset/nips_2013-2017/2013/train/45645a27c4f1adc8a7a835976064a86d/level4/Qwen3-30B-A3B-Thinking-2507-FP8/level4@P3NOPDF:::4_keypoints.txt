- Addresses limitation of FAB inference for latent feature models (LFMs) where the complete-likelihood Hessian is not block-diagonal.  
- Extends FAB to LFMs by deriving a valid lower bound for the factorized information criterion (FIC).  
- Maintains the same FIC representation as in mixture models despite non-block-diagonal Hessian—novel technical contribution.  
- Derivations are clear, rigorous, and well-explained.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets validate the approach.  
- Compared against fast Gibbs (IBP), variational Bayes, and MEIBP; FAB/LFM outperforms competitors.  
- Demonstrates superior predictive accuracy while reducing computational cost.  
- Experimental design is robust, with thorough ablation studies and clear metrics.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with precise mathematical exposition.  
- Significant practical relevance for NIPS community (collaborative filtering, topic modeling, feature selection) due to scalability and principled model selection.  
- Minor suggestions: clarify Hessian condition earlier in introduction and include a concrete real-world application example (e.g., MovieLens).  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept (high-quality contribution, sound technical extension, comprehensive validation).
