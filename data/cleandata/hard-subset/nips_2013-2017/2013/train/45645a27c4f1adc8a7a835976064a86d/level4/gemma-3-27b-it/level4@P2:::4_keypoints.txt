- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), removing a Hessian block-diagonality requirement.
- Derives a FIC lower bound for LFMs and shows it has the same form as the FIC for mixture models.
- Validates the approach experimentally on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Compares FAB/LFM against IBP (fast Gibbs), variational Bayes, and MEIBP.
- Shows competitive or superior prediction accuracy for FAB/LFM.
- Demonstrates improved computational efficiency, especially on large-scale data.
- Paper is clearly written with comprehensive derivations and explanations.
- Theoretical grounding is sound and justifies the extension.
- Strengthening FAB applicability to a broader class of models is a meaningful contribution.
- Contribution is somewhat incremental, building on existing FAB and LFM work.
- Lacks a thorough sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters (e.g., δ, Tshrink).
- Comparison methods need more implementation and parameter-setting details for reproducibility.
- Real-world evaluation is somewhat limited in application diversity.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a clear limitation, solid theory, convincing experiments, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty and insufficient sensitivity/parameter discussion.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-justified extension with strong empirical support; recommended for acceptance.
