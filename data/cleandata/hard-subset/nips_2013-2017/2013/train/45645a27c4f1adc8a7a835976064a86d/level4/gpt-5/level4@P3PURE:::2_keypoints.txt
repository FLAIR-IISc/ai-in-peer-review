- Proposed method: Generalizes FAB/FIC to latent feature models using a mean-field latent-variable approximation and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism for feature selection.
- Main advantage: Increases automation of model selection with minimal hand-tuning and yields substantial computational efficiency gains.
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments comparing against many competing methods on synthetic and real datasets with careful analysis and sufficient references.
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written and well structured.
- Originality/significance: A logical, solid generalization of prior FAB/FIC work by combining FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage approximations into one method.
- Concern/suggestion: Missing analysis of failure modes—need to characterize when and how the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) can lead to poor performance.
