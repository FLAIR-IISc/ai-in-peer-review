This paper presents an extension of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recent model selection technique demonstrating strong performance with mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models by maximizing a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of the original FAB method is its requirement for a block-diagonal Hessian of the complete likelihood – a condition not met by LFMs. The authors successfully circumvent this limitation, deriving a novel FIC lower bound for LFMs that maintains the same mathematical form as the FIC for MMs. The efficacy of FAB/LFM is demonstrated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, with comparisons to established methods including fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet Process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). Results consistently show FAB/LFM achieving superior performance in prediction accuracy and computational efficiency. 

The paper is clearly written, with a detailed and well-explained derivation of the proposed method. While incremental in extending FAB from MMs to LFMs, the contribution is valuable and the experimental results strongly support its effectiveness. Though not my specific area of expertise, I believe this work would be of significant interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a compelling and well-supported approach; I recommend acceptance.
