- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), filling a prior gap where FAB required specific Hessian conditions.  
- Shows LFMs yield the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) as mixtures via novel asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1).  
- Provides rigorous theoretical foundation (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) proving FIC equivalence and enabling FAB for LFMs.  
- Introduces a practical FAB/LFM algorithm (Algorithm 1) with monotonic convergence guarantees.  
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2) that speeds runtime significantly without harming accuracy.  
- Demonstrates empirical advantages: better model selection (K estimation), improved predictive performance (PLL), and lower computational cost on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Shows FAB produces more compact models (smaller K) and reduced overfitting (smaller PLL–TLL gaps).  
- Establishes parameter identifiability for LFMs (Theorem 4), addressing a key theoretical limitation.  
- Novelty: extends FAB beyond mixtures/HMMs to a broader class of latent variable models, distinct from IBP-based prior approaches.  
- Clarity: methods and theory are well-structured with supportive supplement and CCCP interpretation.  
- Experimental issue: MEIBP comparisons are invalid on synthetic/block data because MEIBP requires non-negative W but experiments use arbitrary W.  
- Fairness concern: noise variance settings (e.g., σ=0.75 for real data) lack justification; comparisons should use consistent noise assumptions.  
- Scope limitation: asymptotic assumptions may fail in small-N regimes (e.g., Libras); paper omits discussion of small-sample behavior.  
- Related-work omission: fails to cite/discuss MAD-Bayes (small-variance asymptotics for LFMs), weakening contextualization.  
- Algorithmic limitation: method is restricted to binary LFMs and does not cover standard factor analysis/PCA; authors should clarify implications.  
- Meta recommendation: promising, theoretically grounded, and empirically strong work, but requires minor revisions (fix MEIBP comparisons, justify noise settings, discuss small-N, cite MAD-Bayes) — recommend accept with minor revisions.
