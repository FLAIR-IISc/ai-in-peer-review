- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm using FAB shrinkage for binary latent feature models to learn z_n and infer K automatically.
- Method parallels nonparametric IBP approaches but replaces priors with a Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian model-selection term to encourage feature shrinkage.
- Empirical results claim better running time and inference quality than Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP baselines on large datasets.
- Technical implementation appears sound and scalable, with correct algorithmic details.
- Methodology is reasonably clear, but several experimental details are under-specified and need clarification.
- Novelty: applying FAB inference to latent feature models is a new contribution building on earlier FAB work for mixtures/HMMs.
- Significance: addresses important problem of efficient inference and automatic determination of number of latent features.
- Paper lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations and scenarios where it might underperform compared to standard methods.
- Convergence concerns of the EM-like procedure are not addressed.
- Method is limited to binary latent feature models and not readily extensible to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains apparent flaws that undermine some comparisons.
- Unclear behavior in small-data regimes where asymptotic FAB arguments may not hold; requires discussion or experiments.
- Relevant recent literature on efficient inference for LFMs (e.g., small-variance asymptotics / MAD-Bayes) is not discussed or cited.
- The MEIBP baseline is an invalid comparison for some experiments because it requires a nonnegative loading matrix W, which the experiments did not ensure.
- Experimental fairness issues: known noise levels were given to FAB but not to IBP Gibbs; VB-IBP infinite variational version and restart details were not considered.
- Surprising results (e.g., ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data) suggest poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance and need investigation.
- Citation error: Reference [21] describes MAP estimation for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling — this should be corrected.
- Minor correction: suggested standard deviation in prior work was 0.25 of data std, not 0.75 as stated.
- After author feedback, some concerns were addressed but key issues remain: experimental methodology, baseline settings, hyperparameter effects, and missing citations.
- Overall: the inference idea is interesting and scalable, but acceptance requires more careful and corrected experimental evaluation and related-work discussion.
