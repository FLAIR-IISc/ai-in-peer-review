- Paper proposes an EM-like inference algorithm using FAB shrinkage to learn binary latent feature assignments and automatically select K.  
- Method is analogous to nonparametric IBP but uses a model selection term (FAB) instead of priors for parsimony.  
- Authors claim superior runtime and inference quality on large datasets compared to Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP methods.  
- Technical foundation and algorithmic details appear sound.  
- Method’s ability to scale to large datasets and infer K is a notable strength.  
- Methodology is reasonably clear, but some experimental details are under-explained.  
- Applying FAB to latent feature models is novel and a meaningful contribution.  
- Problem addressed (efficient inference in LFMs with automatic K selection) is significant.  
- Paper lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations and scenarios where it may underperform vs. Gibbs sampling.  
- No discussion of potential convergence challenges inherent to EM-like algorithms.  
- Method is restricted to binary LFMs and cannot be applied to models like factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains flaws.  
- Behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotic FAB assumptions may fail) is unclear and needs evaluation.  
- Several recent works on efficient LFM inference are not cited or discussed (e.g., MAD-Bayes).  
- MEIBP baseline comparisons are invalid because MEIBP requires a non-negative loading matrix W; synthetic and block data experiments did not respect this.  
- For fairness, noise variance provided to FAB should also be provided to IBP Gibbs in the block images experiment.  
- VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version; the infinite variational version and experimental settings (e.g., restarts) were not adequately detailed.  
- Accelerated Gibbs reporting ~70 features on 49-d Sonar dataset is suspicious and may reflect poor initialization or incorrect noise variance.  
- Reference [21] is mischaracterized in the text—it addresses MAP inference, not Gibbs sampling.  
- Reviewer recommends discussing MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and other related asymptotic inference works for IBP.  
- Minor: recommended noise-std setting for real datasets was misstated; correct heuristic is 0.25× data std, not 0.75.  
- After author feedback, remaining issues include unclear experimental methodology, need to justify observed baseline behaviors, correcting reference [21], and adding MAD-Bayes discussion.  
- Overall: proposed method is compelling for scalability and K inference, but experimental rigor and execution must be improved before acceptance.
