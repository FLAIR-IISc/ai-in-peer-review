This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recent Bayesian model selection method that has demonstrated strong performance with mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models by maximizing a tractable lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of existing FAB implementations is a requirement for the Hessian matrix of the complete log-likelihood to be block diagonal – a condition not met by many models, including LFMs. This work addresses this limitation by deriving a FIC lower bound for LFMs, demonstrating its equivalence in form to the FIC for MMs. The authors validate their approach through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM to established methods such as Indian Buffet Process (IBP) with fast Gibbs sampling, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate competitive or superior performance of FAB/LFM, particularly concerning computational efficiency and prediction accuracy.

The paper is clearly written and provides a comprehensive explanation of the method’s derivation. The extension of FAB from MMs to LFMs represents an incremental but significant contribution. The authors effectively demonstrate the method's efficiency through experimental results, and the work is likely to be of interest to the NIPS community, even for researchers not specializing in latent feature models. The inclusion of comparisons against a range of relevant methods strengthens the paper's evaluation. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses a limitation of FAB:** The paper successfully extends the applicability of FAB inference to a broader class of models (LFMs) by overcoming the Hessian matrix constraint.
*   **Theoretical grounding:** The derivation of the FIC lower bound for LFMs and the demonstration of its equivalence to the MMs case provide a solid theoretical foundation.
*   **Empirical validation:** The experimental results on both synthetic and real-world datasets support the method's effectiveness, demonstrating competitive or superior performance compared to existing approaches.
*   **Computational efficiency:** The reported improvements in computational cost are particularly valuable, especially for large-scale datasets.
*   **Clear presentation:** The paper is well-organized and clearly explains the key concepts and mathematical details.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Incremental contribution:** While valuable, the extension of FAB to LFMs can be considered incremental, building upon existing work on FAB and LFM inference. It might benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the specific challenges posed by LFMs and how this work uniquely addresses them beyond simply adapting the FAB framework.
*   **Parameter sensitivity:** While the authors mention parameter settings, a more thorough sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters (e.g., δ, Tshrink) could strengthen the robustness of the findings.
*   **Comparison details:** Providing more details regarding the specific implementations and parameter settings of the comparison methods (IBP, VB, MEIBP) would further enhance the reproducibility and comparability of the results.
*   **Limited scope of real-world data:** While several datasets are used, the diversity of real-world applications explored is somewhat limited. Expanding the evaluation to more diverse data domains could broaden the impact of the work.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a well-defined limitation of an existing, promising method (FAB).
*   The theoretical derivations are sound and justified.
*   The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed approach.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.
*   The work aligns with the NIPS focus on innovative machine learning methods and their applications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The contribution, while valuable, is relatively incremental.
*   A more thorough sensitivity analysis and discussion of parameter settings would be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that presents a valuable extension to the FAB inference framework. The authors have successfully addressed a key limitation and demonstrated the practical benefits of their approach through rigorous experimentation. While the contribution is incremental, the clear presentation, strong theoretical grounding, and compelling empirical results make this a worthwhile addition to the NIPS conference. I recommend acceptance.
