**Summary**

The authors propose an algorithm for efficient model selection in latent feature models, leveraging Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC). While FAB and FIC are established techniques for mixture models—where FIC approximates the model's log-likelihood via factorization and a Laplace approximation for tractable selection—the primary contribution here is the generalization of FAB to latent feature models. This generalization is achieved through a mean-field approximation for inferring latent variables, combined with an accelerated shrinkage of the global number of selected features. A key benefit of this approach is that it largely automates the model selection process, requiring minimal manual tuning. Results on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate a significant improvement in computational efficiency.

**Quality**

The paper presents a thorough analysis, and the method is comprehensively evaluated against a wide range of competing approaches on multiple datasets. The references are sufficient.

**Clarity**

The paper is clearly written and well-structured.

**Originality/Significance**

This work is a logical extension and solid generalization of prior research on model selection in latent feature models using FAB inference and FIC. Specifically, it combines several existing approximation techniques into a novel and effective method.

A potential weakness is the lack of analysis on the method's failure modes. Given that the approach relies on the interplay of several approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage), the analysis would be stronger if it characterized the conditions under which these combined approximations might lead to poor performance.
