- Proposes an efficient model selection algorithm for latent feature models by generalizing FAB and FIC.  
- Generalization uses mean-field inference for latent variables and accelerated shrinkage of the global feature count.  
- Achieves greater automaticity in model selection, requiring minimal manual tuning.  
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Provides thorough empirical evaluation with rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art methods across diverse datasets.  
- Literature review is adequate.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Original contribution: cohesive combination of FIC, mean-field inference, and accelerated shrinkage to extend FAB to latent feature models.  
- Noted gap: lacks analysis of failure modes and scenarios where the approximations may degrade performance, especially under extreme conditions.
