This paper presents a valuable extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to the domain of Latent Feature Models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed and effective technique for model selection, which has previously demonstrated strong performance for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. The core of the FAB method involves maximizing a lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood.

A key limitation of the original FAB framework was its reliance on the assumption that the Hessian matrix of the complete-data log-likelihood is block-diagonal. This paper's main contribution is to overcome this limitation, successfully applying FAB to LFMs where this condition is not met. The authors skillfully derive a new lower bound for the FIC specifically for LFMs. Notably, they show that this bound shares the same functional representation as the FIC for MMs, thus providing an elegant connection between these model classes within the FAB framework.

The authors conduct a thorough empirical evaluation on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The proposed FAB-LFM method is compared against several strong and relevant baselines, including fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models, variational Bayes IBP (VB-IBP), and maximum-expectation IBP (ME-IBP). The experimental results are convincing, illustrating that FAB-LFM achieves superior performance not only in predictive accuracy but also in computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is well-written and logically structured. The authors do an excellent job of describing the theoretical background and the derivation of their method, making the technical contributions easy to follow.
*   **Significant Technical Contribution:** The extension of FAB to models without a block-diagonal Hessian is a non-trivial achievement. It significantly broadens the applicability of a promising inference methodology to a widely used class of models.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The empirical study is robust and the chosen baselines are appropriate. The results convincingly demonstrate the practical advantages of the proposed method in terms of both model accuracy and computational cost, which is a compelling combination.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The contribution could be viewed as an incremental extension of the existing FAB framework rather than the introduction of a completely new paradigm. However, the technical work required to adapt the method to the more complex dependency structure of LFMs is significant and should not be understated. The paper successfully demonstrates that this is a non-trivial and highly effective extension.

**Conclusion:**

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that presents a well-motivated and technically sound contribution. The idea is interesting, the execution is thorough, and the results clearly underline the method's efficiency and effectiveness. While the work builds upon an existing framework, it is a valuable and important step forward that expands the utility of FAB inference. The paper will be of great interest to researchers working on Bayesian inference, model selection, and latent variable models. The convincing results and clear presentation make a strong case for its inclusion in the conference. I support its acceptance.
