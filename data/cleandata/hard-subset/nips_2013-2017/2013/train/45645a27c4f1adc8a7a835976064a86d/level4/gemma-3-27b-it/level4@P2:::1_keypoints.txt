- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), addressing costly model selection for latent dimensionality.  
- Derives a factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs and shows its equivalence to the mixture-model FIC despite different Hessian structure.  
- Proposes an efficient FAB algorithm with EM-like optimization and a shrinkage acceleration technique.  
- Provides identifiability analysis and links FAB E-steps to a convex-concave procedure (CCCP).  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art IBP methods on model selection, predictive likelihood, and runtime in reported experiments.  
- Frames the method as a tractable lower bound on marginal likelihood, akin to a BIC-style criterion.  
- Builds on prior FAB work; technical contributions are novel but incremental within the FAB framework.  
- CCCP interpretation and shrinkage acceleration are particularly valuable algorithmic contributions.  
- Identifiability results give theoretical support for robustness of the approach.  
- Potential mathematical issue: Equation (3)’s log-sum term may become infinite if all z values are zero under a non-degenerate variational distribution.  
- Theorem 2 lacks clarity on the asymptotic regime and assumptions, especially how K (features) scales with N (samples).  
- Applying a BIC-like criterion per component implicitly assumes finite K, conflicting with the IBP’s infinite-feature perspective.  
- Minor error: section 2’s pk(X | z⋅, k) appears to use the entire dataset instead of only points assigned to component k.  
- Minor typo/omission: equation (4) seems to miss an expectation on the left-hand side.  
- Experimental concerns: Table 1 shows better predictive likelihood and lower time for FAB, but stopping criteria are not clearly defined.  
- Performance gains might arise from differences in optimization landscapes or local optima, not solely from the model selection criterion.  
- Gibbs sampler overestimation of components on some datasets suggests optimization/regularization issues that need further analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve mathematical clarity and provide deeper analysis attributing empirical improvements.
