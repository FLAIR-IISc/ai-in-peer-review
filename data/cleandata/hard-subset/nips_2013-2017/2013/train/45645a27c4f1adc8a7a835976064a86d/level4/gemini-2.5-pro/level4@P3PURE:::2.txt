**Summary:**
The authors propose an efficient algorithm for model selection in latent feature models by extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC). While FAB and FIC are established techniques for mixture models—where FIC approximates the model's log-likelihood via factorization and a Laplace approximation for tractable model selection—the primary contribution of this work is their generalization to latent feature models. The proposed method achieves this by using a mean-field approximation for inferring the latent features and an accelerated shrinkage of the global number of selected features. A key advantage of this approach is its high degree of automation, which minimizes the need for manual tuning. The paper demonstrates significant gains in computational efficiency on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Quality:**
The paper presents a thorough analysis of its results. The proposed method is rigorously evaluated against a wide range of competing approaches across multiple datasets. The authors provide sufficient references to related work.

**Clarity:**
The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making the contributions easy to follow.

**Originality/Significance:**
This work is a logical extension and solid generalization of previous research on model selection in latent feature models using FAB inference and FIC. The novelty lies in combining several approximation techniques into a new, effective method.

A valuable addition to the paper would be an analysis of the method's potential failure modes. Given that the approach relies on a combination of approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage), it would be insightful to understand the conditions under which their interplay might lead to poor performance. Investigating the limits of the proposed method would further strengthen the contribution.
