- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs).
- Addresses FAB’s key limitation by handling cases where the complete-likelihood Hessian is not block-diagonal.
- Derives a lower bound on the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs that has the same form as for mixture models.
- Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Compared against fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet Process (IBP), variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP.
- Shows superior prediction accuracy and better computational efficiency than alternatives.
- Paper is clearly written with a comprehensive derivation.
- Work is an incremental but compelling extension of FAB, validated by convincing results.
- Likely of considerable interest to the NIPS community.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
