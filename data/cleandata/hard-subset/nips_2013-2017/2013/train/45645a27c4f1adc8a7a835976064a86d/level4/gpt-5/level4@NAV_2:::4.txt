This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed approach to model selection that has shown strong performance for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models, maximizing a tractable lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A known limitation of FAB is its reliance on the complete-data Hessian being block diagonal. The authors overcome this for LFMs, where the condition does not hold, by deriving an FIC lower bound for LFMs and showing it has the same form as for MMs. They present experiments on synthetic and real datasets comparing FAB/LFM to fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results indicate that FAB/LFM yields superior performance in prediction as well as computational efficiency.

The paper is clearly written, and the derivations are described thoroughly.

The contribution extends FAB from MMs to LFMs and is, in my view, incremental. Nevertheless, it is interesting, and the empirical results highlight its efficiency. Although I am not a specialist in this precise topic, I believe it will be of interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting a compelling idea; the results are convincing, and I support acceptance.
