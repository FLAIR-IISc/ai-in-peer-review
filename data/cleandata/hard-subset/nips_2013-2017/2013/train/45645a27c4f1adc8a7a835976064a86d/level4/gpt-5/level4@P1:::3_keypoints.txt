- Proposes an EM-like FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that jointly infers Z, parameters, and effective K via a factorized information criterion and shrinkage.  
- Claims improved runtime and inference quality over Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP-style IBP methods on larger datasets.  
- Technical development appears sound and algorithmic details are coherent.  
- Scales to large datasets and jointly infers K, which is attractive if empirical gains hold.  
- Methodological exposition is generally clear, but some experimental details are underspecified or inconsistent.  
- Extends the FAB framework from mixture models/HMMs to LFMs; this application appears novel.  
- Important problem with potential broad interest if comparisons are fair and controlled.  
- Paper lacks discussion of limitations and regimes where the algorithm underperforms relative to Gibbs or IBP methods.  
- Convergence behavior (local optima, initialization sensitivity) is not examined in depth despite the EM-like procedure.  
- Approach is restricted to binary LFMs and does not apply to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains apparent flaws.  
- Unclear behavior in small-sample regimes where FIC asymptotics may not hold; needs discussion or targeted experiments.  
- Some recent related work on efficient LFM inference is omitted and should be discussed.  
- MEIBP baseline comparisons are invalid in places because MEIBP assumes W ≥ 0 but synthetic/block data do not enforce this.  
- For the block images experiment, baselines (e.g., IBP Gibbs) should be given the same known noise level provided to FAB.  
- VB-IBP settings are unclear; the infinite variational formulation could be used but appears not to be, and VB experimental settings (restarts, K) should be detailed.  
- Accelerated Gibbs reporting ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data is suspicious and likely due to poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance.  
- Incorrectly describes [21] as Gibbs sampling; it is MAP inference and should be corrected.  
- Should discuss MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and its relation to small-variance asymptotics for nonparametric LFMs.  
- Minor: recommended noise sd setting for real data is 0.25 times empirical sd across dimensions, not a fixed 0.75.  
- After author feedback, remaining required fixes: clarify and justify all experimental settings, correct [21] description, and include/discuss MAD-Bayes.  
- Overall: FAB for LFMs is interesting and scalable, but empirical evaluation must be redesigned and reported more carefully to substantiate comparative claims.
