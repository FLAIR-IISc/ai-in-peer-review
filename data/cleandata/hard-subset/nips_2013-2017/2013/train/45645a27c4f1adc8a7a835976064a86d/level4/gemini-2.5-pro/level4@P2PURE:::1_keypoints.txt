- Proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models that uses BIC to integrate out parameters instead of a factorized variational posterior.

- Method is computationally efficient, corresponding to an EM algorithm with extra complexity penalties, and is faster than sampling or standard variational Bayes.

- Empirical results show improved held-out predictive likelihoods compared to several IBP implementations.

- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, essentially extending the existing FAB framework from prior work.

- Exposition lacks mathematical precision in key places, making some claims unclear.

- Equation (3) is problematic: the log-sum over z is −∞ when a feature has zero activations, yet such configurations have nonzero probability under non-degenerate q, so the FIC score’s finiteness is unclear.

- Theorem 2 claims an asymptotic marginal likelihood approximation but fails to specify the asymptotic regime or assumptions, especially the behavior of K as N grows.

- The analysis appears to implicitly require fixed, finite K so each feature has infinitely many activations, which contradicts the IBP motivation of a potentially growing/infinite number of components.

- Minor technical issues: in Section 2, the likelihood pk(X | z·,k) should likely condition only on data assigned to component k; Equation (4) seems to be missing an expectation on the left-hand side.

- Experimental comparisons are hard to interpret because stopping criteria for algorithms are unspecified, making runtime comparisons inconclusive and Gibbs stopping arbitrary.

- It is unclear whether predictive gains come from the proposed model-selection criterion or from other factors (e.g., algorithms getting stuck in worse local optima or initialization/implementation differences).

- Gibbs sampler learning too many components on some datasets suggests possible local-optima/implementation issues rather than a fair baseline, undermining confidence in attributing improvements to the new method.
