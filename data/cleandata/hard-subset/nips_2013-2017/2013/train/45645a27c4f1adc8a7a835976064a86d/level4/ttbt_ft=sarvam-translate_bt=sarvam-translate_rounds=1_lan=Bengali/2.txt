Summary:
The authors propose an algorithm/approximation method for efficient model selection for latent feature models using Factorized Aymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and Factorized Information Criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC are well-known mixed models, where the latter estimates the log likelihood of a model through factorization and Laplace approximation, which is useful for easily selecting models. One of the key contributions is the generalization of FAB for latent feature models, which uses a min-field approximation and rapid shrinking of the global number of selected features for estimating latent variables.

A feature of this method is the automation of the model selection process, which requires minimal manual intervention. Results have been shown in synthetic and real data, and significant improvements in computational efficiency have been observed.
