- Paper introduces a FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that jointly learns features and their number K.  
- Uses model selection via FAB instead of nonparametric priors like the IBP to induce parsimony.  
- Demonstrates competitive runtime and inference quality on large datasets compared to Gibbs, VI, and MAP methods.  
- Technical approach is logically presented and implementation appears correct.  
- Strength: automatically determines latent dimensionality and scales to large datasets.  
- Novel application of the FAB framework to latent feature models.  
- Paper is generally clear methodologically but experimental details need clarification.  
- Lacks discussion of method limitations and failure modes (e.g., when it may underperform versus Gibbs).  
- Does not address convergence issues inherent to EM-like procedures.  
- Restriction to binary latent features limits applicability to continuous-factor models like factor analysis or PPCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive but not sufficiently rigorous, especially for small-data regimes where asymptotics may fail.  
- Comparison with MEIBP [22] is flawed because MEIBP requires non-negative data and experiments do not satisfy this.  
- Specific experimental concerns: inconsistent noise variance in block image experiments affecting fairness.  
- VB-IBP comparisons should use the infinite variational version rather than a finite approximation.  
- Experimental settings lack details (e.g., number of restarts); some reported results (e.g., accelerated Gibbs on Sonar) seem suspicious and need investigation.  
- Reference [21] is mischaracterized as Gibbs sampling when it is actually a MAP estimation method.  
- Missing citation and discussion of related work: "MAD-Bayes: MAP-based Asymptotic Derivations from Bayes" (ICML 2013).  
- After author feedback some issues were addressed, but substantial experimental and presentation improvements remain necessary.  
- Required fixes: clarify experimental methodology, justify observed behaviors (overestimation of K, hyperparameter tuning), correct references, and include MAD-Bayes discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: interesting, scalable, and useful approach for efficient LFM inference with automatic model selection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficiently rigorous experiments, flawed baseline comparisons, omitted related work, and missing limitations/convergence analysis.  
- Overall assessment: Reject with request for major revisions; core idea promising but experimental validation and presentation require substantial improvement.
