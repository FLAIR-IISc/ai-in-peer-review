

Summary:  
The authors introduce a novel algorithm for efficient model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) by extending factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). While FAB and FIC are established for mixture models—where FIC approximates log-likelihood via factorization and Laplace approximation to enable tractable model selection—their key contribution is generalizing this framework to LFMs. This is achieved through a mean-field approximation for latent inference and accelerated shrinkage of the global feature count. The method reduces the need for manual tuning, with significant computational gains demonstrated on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  

Quality:  
The results are rigorously evaluated against diverse baseline methods across multiple datasets, with comprehensive analysis supporting the claims. The literature review is sufficiently thorough.  

Clarity:  
The paper is clearly written and well-organized, with a logical flow from problem statement to evaluation.  

Originality/Significance:  
This work represents a natural extension of prior FAB/FIC approaches to LFMs, integrating multiple approximations (mean-field inference, FIC, accelerated shrinkage) into a cohesive, effective framework. The generalization is well-motivated and demonstrates strong empirical performance.  

Critical Comment:  
While the method shows clear advantages in efficiency and automation, it would be valuable to identify scenarios where the approximations (mean-field, FIC, shrinkage) may degrade performance. Specifically, a systematic exploration of failure modes—such as when data violates assumptions of the mean-field approximation or when feature correlations challenge the factorization—would strengthen the analysis. The authors should discuss how these approximations interact under challenging conditions to provide a more complete picture of the method’s robustness.
