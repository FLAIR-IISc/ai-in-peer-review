- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming prior inapplicability due to block-diagonal Hessian requirements.  
- Proves LFMs share the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) structure as mixture models, enabling FAB's derivation for LFMs.  
- Introduces an efficient FAB/LFM algorithm with shrinkage acceleration that automatically selects the number of latent features.  
- Demonstrates computational advantages over state-of-the-art methods and provides shrinkage-based speedups without loss of accuracy.  
- Builds on prior FAB work for mixture models, representing a novel theoretical extension to a broader class of latent variable models.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with detailed proofs in the supplement, supporting the main claims.  
- Empirically validates FAB/LFM across multiple datasets, showing superior model selection accuracy, predictive performance, and efficiency.  
- Shows automatic feature selection and parameter identifiability as key advantages over IBP and VB-style methods.  
- Lacks thorough investigation of method limitations—no sensitivity analysis for failure modes (e.g., highly correlated features, varying noise levels).  
- Shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) is empirically effective but lacks theoretical justification regarding CCCP convergence rate and why multiple iterations accelerate convergence.  
- Experimental comparisons could better explain why competing methods (e.g., MEIBP) perform worse despite speed advantages.  
- Needs clearer discussion of when FAB/LFM is preferable to prior-informed Bayesian approaches (e.g., IBP) given FAB’s asymptotic prior-ignorance.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, significant contribution, well-written — recommend acceptance.
