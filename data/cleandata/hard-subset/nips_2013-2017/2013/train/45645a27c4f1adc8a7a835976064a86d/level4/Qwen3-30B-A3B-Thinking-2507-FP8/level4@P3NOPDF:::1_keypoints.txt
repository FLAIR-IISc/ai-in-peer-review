- Paper proposes a BIC-style modification of variational Bayes for binary latent feature models with an EM-like algorithm and complexity penalties.  
- Empirical claims: improved held-out likelihood and faster runtime compared to IBP implementations (Gibbs, FAB).  
- Major mathematical flaw: Equation (3)’s log-sum over binary assignments is −∞ when a feature has all z_nk = 0, which occurs with nonzero probability under any nondegenerate q, making the FIC score undefined.  
- Theorem 2 is under-specified: it omits asymptotic regime/assumptions and its proof requires fixed finite K, contradicting IBP’s goal of K → ∞ as N → ∞.  
- Finite-K assumption undermines the theoretical relevance to IBP and the claimed asymptotic marginal likelihood approximation.  
- Expository/mathematical inconsistencies: p_k(X | z_{·,k}) is defined over the entire dataset instead of only assigned data points.  
- Notational error: Equation (4) omits the expectation operator typical in variational objectives.  
- Experimental runtime comparisons are misleading because Gibbs’s stopping criterion is unspecified, preventing fair timing comparisons.  
- Reported likelihood gains are unexplained; improvements may result from algorithmic stability (avoiding poor local optima) rather than the model selection criterion.  
- Lack of controls: no experiments fixing initialization or comparing local optima across methods to isolate the criterion’s effect.  
- Contribution is incremental—an extension of prior FAB work—so computational efficiency alone is insufficient for strong novelty.  
- Recommendations for improvement: handle zero-assignment cases, revise asymptotic analysis for K → ∞, and run ablation/controlled experiments to separate criterion effects from algorithmic artifacts.  
- Overall verdict: fundamental mathematical errors, inconsistent theory, and unconvincing experiments justify rejection.
