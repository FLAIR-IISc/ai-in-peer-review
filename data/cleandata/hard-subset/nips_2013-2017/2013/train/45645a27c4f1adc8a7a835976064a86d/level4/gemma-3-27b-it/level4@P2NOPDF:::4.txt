This paper presents an extension of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recent model selection technique demonstrating strong performance in mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models, operating by maximizing a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of the original FAB method is its applicability only to models where the Hessian of the complete likelihood is block diagonal – a condition not generally met by LFMs. This work addresses this limitation by successfully deriving a FIC lower bound specifically for LFMs, demonstrating its equivalence in form to the FIC used for MMs. 

The authors validate FAB/LFM through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing its performance against established methods including Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet Process (IBP), variational Bayes (VB), and maximum expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, showcasing improvements not only in predictive accuracy but also in computational efficiency. 

The paper is clearly written and the methodological derivations are thoroughly explained, allowing for ease of understanding. The extension from FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs represents a valuable, incremental contribution. The observed efficiency, supported by the experimental results, is compelling. While my expertise does not lie directly within this specific area, I believe the work will be of significant interest to the NIPS community. 

Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a novel and promising approach. The results are convincing and I recommend acceptance.
