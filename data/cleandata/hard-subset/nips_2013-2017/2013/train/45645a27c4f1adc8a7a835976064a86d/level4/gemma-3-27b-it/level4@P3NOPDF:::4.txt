This paper presents an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recent model selection technique demonstrating strong performance on mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models, operating by maximizing a lower bound on the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of the original FAB approach is its reliance on a block-diagonal Hessian matrix for the complete likelihood. This work successfully overcomes this limitation, extending FAB's applicability to LFMs where this condition does not hold. 

The authors effectively derive a lower bound on the FIC specifically for LFMs, demonstrating that it shares the same functional form as the FIC used in the FAB-MM framework. This is a significant theoretical contribution. They validate their approach through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM against competing methods including Fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet Process (IBP), variational Bayes (VB), and Maximum Expectation IBP (MEIBP). The empirical results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, not only in predictive performance but also in computational efficiency.

The paper is clearly written and the mathematical derivations are presented thoroughly, allowing for easy understanding of the proposed method. The extension from FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs represents a valuable, incremental advance. While not a radical departure, the approach is demonstrably effective, as supported by the presented results. Although my expertise does not lie specifically in this subfield, I believe this work would be of considerable interest to the NIPS community due to the practical benefits of improved efficiency in latent feature modeling.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a limitation of FAB:** The work successfully extends FAB to a broader class of models (LFMs) by overcoming the block-diagonal Hessian requirement.
* **Strong theoretical justification:** The derivation of the LFM-specific FIC lower bound and its connection to the FAB-MM formulation are well-explained.
* **Compelling empirical results:** The comparison to several established methods on both synthetic and real datasets clearly highlights the advantages of FAB/LFM in terms of both accuracy and computational cost.
* **Clear writing and presentation:** The paper is well-organized and the technical details are presented in a readily understandable manner.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Incremental nature:** While the contribution is valuable, it's important to acknowledge its incremental character. The paper builds upon existing work rather than proposing a fundamentally new paradigm. (This is noted in the original draft and retained here). 

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a well-executed and impactful paper that presents a practical and theoretically sound extension to a promising inference technique. The results are convincing, and the authors have clearly demonstrated the benefits of applying FAB to latent feature models. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2013.
