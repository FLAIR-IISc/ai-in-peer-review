- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm using the FAB framework to learn binary latent feature assignments and infer the number of active features K via shrinkage.  
- Claims to achieve model parsimony without a nonparametric prior and reports better runtime and inference quality than several IBP methods on large-scale datasets.

- Strength: Novel application of FAB to latent feature models, offering a new alternative to IBP-style nonparametric approaches.  
- Strength: Addresses the important problem of efficient inference while determining model complexity.  
- Strength: Demonstrates scalability to large datasets compared to standard IBP Gibbs sampling.  
- Strength: Technical derivation appears sound and is presented clearly.

- Major weakness: Experimental comparisons are flawed, undermining empirical claims.  
- Flaw: MEIBP baseline misused—MEIBP requires non-negative W but experiments use real-valued W, invalidating that comparison.  
- Flaw: Unfair hyperparameter treatment—FAB given true noise while IBP Gibbs got a heuristic value, biasing results.  
- Flaw: Insufficient details for VB-IBP baseline (finite vs infinite version, number of restarts, etc.).  
- Flaw: Questionable results (e.g., ~70 features on 49-d Sonar) likely due to poor initialization or hyperparameter settings and need investigation.

- Weakness: Lack of discussion on algorithm limitations—no analysis of convergence or susceptibility to local optima.  
- Weakness: No discussion of FAB’s asymptotic assumptions and potential poor performance in small-data regimes.  
- Weakness: Method scope limited to binary LFMs and not directly applicable to continuous latent-variable models; this is not discussed.

- Missing/incorrect citations: Omits relevant work (e.g., MAD-Bayes ICML 2013) and mislabels reference [21] as Gibbs sampling when it is a MAP method.

- Recommendation: Revise experiments to correct/remove the MEIBP comparison, ensure fair, fully specified baseline settings, and investigate surprising outcomes.  
- Recommendation: Add a limitations section covering convergence, small-data behavior, and model scope.  
- Recommendation: Correct reference descriptions and include discussion of related work like MAD-Bayes.
