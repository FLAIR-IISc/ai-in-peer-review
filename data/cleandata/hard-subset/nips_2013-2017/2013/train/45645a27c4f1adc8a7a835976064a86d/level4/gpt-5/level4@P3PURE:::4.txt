This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection framework that has demonstrated strong performance for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. In brief, FAB maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood.

A key limitation of prior FAB work is that it applies only to models whose complete-data log-likelihood has a Hessian that is block-diagonal. The authors extend FAB to LFMs even though this condition does not hold. They derive an effective lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and show that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs.

The empirical evaluation includes both synthetic and real-world datasets. The authors compare FAB/LFM against fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results indicate that FAB/LFM outperforms these alternatives not only in prediction accuracy but also in computational cost.

The paper is well written, and the derivations are presented clearly and thoroughly.

While the contribution extends FAB from MMs to LFMs and is therefore somewhat incremental, the approach is interesting and the experiments highlight its efficiency. Although I am not an expert in this specific area, I believe the work will be of significant interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting a compelling idea, with convincing results. I support acceptance.
