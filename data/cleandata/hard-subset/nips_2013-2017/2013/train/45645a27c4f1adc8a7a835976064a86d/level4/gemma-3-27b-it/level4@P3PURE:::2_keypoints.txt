- Proposes an algorithm and approximation method for efficient model selection in latent feature models using FAB inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).  
- Extends FAB and FIC—previously used for mixture models—to latent feature models.  
- Uses a mean-field approximation for latent variables and accelerated shrinkage to reduce the global number of selected features.  
- Method is relatively automated and requires minimal manual parameter tuning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, showing significant computational efficiency gains.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares against a wide range of competing methods; references are sufficient.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Represents a logical, solid generalization of prior work and combines several approximations into a practically effective method.  
- Lacks discussion of conditions or scenarios where the combined approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage) might fail or produce suboptimal results; needs analysis of potential failure modes and their interplay.
