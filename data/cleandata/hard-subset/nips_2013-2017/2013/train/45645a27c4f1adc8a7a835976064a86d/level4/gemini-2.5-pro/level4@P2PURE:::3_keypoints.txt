- Introduces an EM-based FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that shrinks and infers the number of features K automatically.
- FAB approximates the log marginal likelihood with a model-complexity penalty to reduce active features from a specified upper bound.
- Experiments claim FAB outperforms IBP methods (Gibbs, VB, MAP) in runtime and inference quality on large datasets.
- Technical quality: algorithmic details appear sound and scalable.
- Clarity: methodology is reasonably clear but several experimental details are unclear.
- Originality: novel application of the FAB framework to latent feature models.
- Significance: addresses efficient inference and automatic dimensionality selection in LFMs.
- No discussion of the algorithm’s limitations or scenarios where it may underperform standard methods.
- Convergence issues common to EM-like procedures are not discussed.
- Method is restricted to binary latent features and not applicable to real-valued factor models (e.g., factor analysis, PPCA).
- Experimental evaluation lacks thoroughness and contains apparent flaws.
- No analysis or experiments for small-data regimes where FAB’s asymptotic justification may not hold.
- Several recent works on efficient inference for LFMs are not cited.
- Comparison to MEIBP [22] is invalid because MEIBP requires a non-negative loading matrix W while experiments used real-valued W.
- For block images, the IBP Gibbs baseline should have been given the known noise value for fair comparison.
- VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version; the infinite version and key details (e.g., number of restarts) were not specified.
- The accelerated Gibbs sampler finding ~70 features on 49-D Sonar is surprising and may indicate initialization or noise-variance mis-specification.
- Reference [21] is incorrectly described; it concerns MAP estimation for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling.
- The MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) small-variance asymptotic work should be cited and discussed.
- Minor: recommendation in [1,22] was to set noise std to 0.25×data std across dimensions, not a fixed 0.75.
- After author feedback: experimental methodology and baseline protocols still need more detail; citation correction and MAD-Bayes inclusion remain necessary.
- Overall: method is compelling and scalable, but its impact is undermined by inadequate and flawed experimental evaluation.
