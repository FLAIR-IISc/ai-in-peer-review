- Proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models that approximates parameter integration using BIC instead of a fully factorized variational posterior.  
- Demonstrates improved held-out (predictive) likelihoods compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Motivated by a plausible asymptotic argument and offers computational efficiency by reducing to an EM algorithm with complexity penalties.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, extending prior FAB work.  
- Exposition has mathematical imprecision: equation (3)’s log-sum over z can be infinite if all z=0, raising why FIC doesn’t become infinite under non-degenerate q.  
- Theorem 2 lacks clarity about the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K behaves as N grows.  
- Applying BIC per component appears to require fixed, finite K, which conflicts with the IBP’s infinite-component motivation.  
- Minor notation/definition issues: p_k(X | z_·,k) should condition on only data assigned to component k, and equation (4) should include an expectation on the LHS.  
- Table 1 shows faster runtimes for FAB and higher predictive likelihoods, but runtime comparisons are hard to interpret without a clear stopping criterion.  
- The Gibbs sampler’s stopping point seems arbitrary, complicating runtime and performance comparisons.  
- Unclear whether predictive improvements come from the model-selection criterion itself or from differences in convergence/local optima and implementation/initialization choices.  
- Observation that Gibbs learns too many components on some datasets suggests local-optima or implementation issues rather than a definitive superiority of FAB’s criterion.  
- Overall: approach is sound but somewhat incremental; mathematical presentation needs tightening and experiments require deeper analysis to attribute gains.
