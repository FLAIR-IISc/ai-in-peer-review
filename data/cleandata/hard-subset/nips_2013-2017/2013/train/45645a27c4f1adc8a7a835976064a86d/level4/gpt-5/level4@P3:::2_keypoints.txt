- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) via asymptotic Hessian analysis producing an FIC for LFMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Presents a practical FAB-LFM algorithm: mean-field E-step, alternating E/M maximizing a tractable FIC lower bound, CCCP-based E-step acceleration for shrinkage, and a merge post-processing step.  
- Main theoretical claim (Lemma): log-determinant of per-dimension Hessian ≈ sum_k log(sum_n znk)/N + O_p(1), yielding an FIC with the same form as mixtures.  
- Theorem 2 approximates the marginal complete log-likelihood; alternating updates provably increase the lower bound.  
- Identifiability result (Theorem 4) shows FAB favors balanced, smaller-K ML solutions and motivates the merging heuristic.  
- Key proofs and regularity conditions are deferred to the supplement; the paper should state precise assumptions (growth rates, boundedness, nonsingularity) in the main text.  
- Algorithmic specifics: closed-form mean-field fixed-point E-updates and PPCA-style M-updates; CCCP interpretation justifies multiple inner E-steps to accelerate shrinkage.  
- Merge post-processing uses a bound-increase check as a safeguard and is theoretically motivated by identifiability analysis.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show favorable model selection accuracy, predictive performance, and runtime versus IBP methods, MEIBP, and EM.  
- Ablation on shrinkage acceleration is informative, but sensitivity to baseline implementation choices (fixed noise variance, IBP α, MEIBP constraints) may bias comparisons.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, with explicit algorithms and updates, but needs unified notation, minor typo fixes, and clearer statement of assumptions and FIC limitations.  
- Original contributions: novel derivation of LFM FIC without block-diagonal Hessian assumption, CCCP-based acceleration, and identifiability-driven merge.  
- Significance: offers automated model selection for LFMs without hyperparameter tuning, scalable alternative to IBP, and theoretical ideas potentially extendable to other discrete latent models.  
- Pros: solid theory, principled monotonic algorithm, empirical gains with lower K and faster runtimes, CCCP acceleration, and theory-grounded merge heuristic.  
- Cons: missing explicit asymptotic assumptions in main text, possible unfairness in baseline setups, limited stress-testing (small N, model mismatch), and unclear impact of mean-field approximation.  
- Recommended additional analyses: sensitivity to noise variance and IBP α (or inferring α), initialization effects, Tshrink and number of CCCP loops, small-N experiments, and per-iteration complexity/wall-clock normalized comparisons.  
- Requests to authors: explicitly list Lemma 1/Theorem 2 assumptions, discuss finite-sample O_p(1) impacts, report merge frequency/effect, and contrast with MAD-Bayes asymptotics.  
- Overall assessment: solid and timely contribution with meaningful theoretical and practical advances; recommend acceptance subject to clearer assumptions and expanded empirical robustness checks.
