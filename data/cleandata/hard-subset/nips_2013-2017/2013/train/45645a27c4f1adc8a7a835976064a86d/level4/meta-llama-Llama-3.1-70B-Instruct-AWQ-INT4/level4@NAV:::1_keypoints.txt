- Introduces a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models, extending variational Bayes with a BIC-based parameter integration approximation.  
- Demonstrates superior held-out likelihoods compared to several existing IBP implementations.  
- Main advantage is computational efficiency: reduces to an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties instead of costly sampling/variational methods.  
- Technically novel but largely an incremental extension of prior FAB work [3,4].  
- Equation (3) is unclear: log-sum of z can be infinite if all z are zero, which has nonzero probability under q, questioning why the FIC score isn’t always infinite.  
- Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions (notably K’s behavior as N grows), seeming to require fixed finite K contrary to the IBP’s infinite-component assumption.  
- Minor technical issues: pk(X | z·,k) should likely include only data assigned to component k, and equation (4) omits an expectation on the LHS.  
- Table 1 shows higher predictive likelihoods and faster runtime for the proposed method.  
- Missing stopping criterion for the Gibbs sampler makes runtime and result comparisons hard to interpret.  
- Improvements could stem from model selection or from algorithms getting stuck in local optima; sensitivity to initialization and implementation details is unclear.  
- Overall: reasonable and computationally attractive approach but somewhat incremental with imprecise math and requiring further analysis to clarify theoretical and experimental claims.
