- Contribution: Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs) by deriving a tractable lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC).  
- Technical novelty: Overcomes the prior requirement of a block-diagonal complete-data Hessian and yields an FIC lower bound with the same functional form as in mixture models.  
- Practical benefit: Enables efficient optimization and automatic feature pruning for LFMs, preserving FAB’s model selection advantages.  
- Empirical results: FAB/LFM outperforms strong baselines (IBP Gibbs, VB, MEIBP) on synthetic and real datasets in predictive accuracy and computational cost.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written with a transparent, careful derivation of the extension from MMs to LFMs.  
- Theoretical coherence: The derived FIC lower bound mirrors the MM case, supporting the transfer of FAB benefits to LFMs.  
- Practical relevance: Reduces computation while improving or maintaining predictive performance; automatic feature selection is valuable for high-dimensional LFMs.  
- Incremental novelty: Extension to LFMs is technically nontrivial but conceptually incremental rather than a fundamentally new criterion.  
- Assumptions/tightness: Needs clearer statement of assumptions for the lower bound and discussion of its tightness relative to true FIC/marginal likelihood.  
- Robustness/sensitivity: Requires more analysis of sensitivity to initialization, hyperparameters, stopping criteria, and risks of premature pruning.  
- Scalability details: Would benefit from a detailed complexity analysis (N, D, inferred features), wall-clock breakdowns, implementation specifics (batching/parallelization), and released code.  
- Baseline breadth: Could discuss relationships to other contemporary nonparametric feature-learning methods to contextualize results.  
- Minor issues: Fix typos (e.g., “LFMS,” “ad,” “Indian Bufffet,” “GIbbs,” “it’s efficiency”) and briefly summarize intuition for why the MM-style FIC form remains useful despite non-block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed, and convincing extension likely of interest to the community; recommendation to accept.
