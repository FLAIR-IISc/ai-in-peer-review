- The paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models (LFMs) via an asymptotic Hessian analysis showing the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs matches the mixture-model form despite non-block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Authors derive an EM-like algorithm that maximizes a tractable lower bound to the FIC with a mean-field approximation on Z.  
- The algorithm includes a principled shrinkage term that prunes irrelevant features and automatically selects K.  
- E-step updates are related to a convex–concave procedure (CCCP), motivating a shrinkage-acceleration heuristic.  
- Paper presents an identifiability analysis and a merge post-processing step to handle redundant features.  
- Empirical comparisons claim advantages over IBP Gibbs sampling, variational IBP (VB), and MEIBP in model selection, prediction, and runtime.  
- Technical derivations are consistent with prior FAB work; the Hessian→FIC link and CCCP interpretation are interesting and credible.  
- Algorithm is simple to implement and the shrinkage mechanism is well-motivated.  
- Paper lacks sufficient discussion of failure modes and convergence behavior, especially for small-sample (small N) regimes where asymptotics are weak.  
- Several experimental details are underspecified (VB settings, hyperparameters, restarts, initialization, stopping criteria).  
- Some baselines are not fairly evaluated or violate their assumptions (e.g., MEIBP used when W is real-valued though MEIBP assumes W ≥ 0).  
- Noise-variance handling is inconsistent across methods (FAB uses known noise for block images; IBP Gibbs should be given same noise for fairness).  
- Surprising empirical behaviors (e.g., accelerated Gibbs finding ~70 features) likely stem from hyperparameter/initialization choices and need justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- The paper should include experiments or discussion on small-N behavior to show limits of asymptotic approximations.  
- Related-work gap: discuss small-variance asymptotics approaches for LFMs (e.g., MAD-Bayes) and relate them to FAB.  
- There is a citation/description error: reference [21] performs MAP inference, not Gibbs sampling.  
- Strengths: novel theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs, principled automatic feature selection, CCCP-driven acceleration, identifiability+merge analysis, and promising empirical speed–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of convergence/local optima, scope restricted to binary LFMs (no real-valued factor extensions), experimental fairness/details issues, and inappropriate baseline usage.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, well-motivated theory and scalable algorithm with automatic feature selection and good empirical performance on large datasets.  
- Cons for acceptance: experimental methodology needs tightening, convergence/failure-mode analysis is lacking, and broader applicability beyond binary LFMs is not demonstrated.  
- Required fixes post-rebuttal: provide clearer experimental methodology and hyperparameter justification (e.g., α, noise variance), correct [21] description, and discuss/compare to MAD-Bayes.
