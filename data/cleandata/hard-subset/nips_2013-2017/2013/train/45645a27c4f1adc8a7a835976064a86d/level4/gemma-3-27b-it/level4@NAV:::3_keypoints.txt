- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm using Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework for binary Latent Feature Models that shrinks and infers the number of features K.  
- Claims empirical speed and quality improvements over Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP on large datasets.  
- Technical approach appears sound and able to scale to large datasets while learning K.  
- Methodology is clearly presented, but experimental setup details are insufficient.  
- Applying FAB to latent feature models is a novel contribution.  
- Tackles the important problem of efficient inference and automatic determination of K.  
- Paper lacks discussion of potential limitations and failure scenarios compared to standard Gibbs sampling.  
- Convergence properties of the EM-like procedure are not addressed and need discussion.  
- Method is limited to binary LFMs and cannot be directly extended to factor analysis or PPCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains methodological flaws.  
- Behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotics may fail) is unclear and should be examined.  
- Recent relevant work on efficient LFM inference is not adequately cited or discussed.  
- Comparison with MEIBP is invalid because MEIBP requires non-negative linear Gaussian models with positive W.  
- For block image experiments, the IBP Gibbs baseline should be given the known noise level for fair comparison.  
- VB-IBP should use the infinite variational version and report experimental settings such as number of restarts.  
- The discovery of 70 features on 49-d Sonar data suggests poor initialization or incorrect noise variance for baselines.  
- Reference [21] is incorrectly described as Gibbs sampling; it actually covers MAP estimation and must be corrected.  
- The paper should cite MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and other small-variance asymptotics work for nonparametric LFMs.  
- Correction: suggested standard deviation setting in prior work is 0.25× data std, not 0.75.  
- After author feedback, methodological and experimental explanations remain insufficient and require more justification (e.g., hyperparameter effects on Gibbs sampler).  
- Overall, the method is promising for scalability and inferring K, but acceptance requires a more rigorous and carefully conducted experimental evaluation.
