Summary:
The paper proposes an efficient, largely automatic approach to model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) by extending factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC) beyond mixture models. The method uses a mean-field approximation for latent variables and a global shrinkage mechanism to prune features, while FIC approximates the marginal log-likelihood via factorization and a Laplace approximation, enabling tractable selection. Experiments on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate substantial computational gains with minimal manual tuning.

Quality:
The empirical evaluation is thorough, with comparisons against a broad set of baselines across multiple datasets. The analyses appear careful, and the citations are adequate for situating the work within the literature. Overall, the experimental section supports the paper’s claims of efficiency and effectiveness.

Clarity:
The manuscript is clearly written and well organized. The methodological components (FAB, FIC, mean-field inference, and shrinkage) are described in a coherent way that makes the overall procedure easy to follow.

Originality/Significance:
The contribution is a logical yet nontrivial generalization of FAB to LFMs. The integration of FIC, mean-field inference, and accelerated shrinkage into a single algorithm is technically sound and practically useful, reducing reliance on hand-tuned model selection. The demonstrated speedups and competitive performance make the approach significant for practitioners working with LFMs.

Suggestions/Concerns:
- Please clarify the conditions under which the approximations may degrade performance. In particular, discuss regimes where the Laplace/FIC approximation may be inaccurate (e.g., small sample sizes, highly correlated features, or strongly non-Gaussian posteriors).
- Analyze the impact of the mean-field assumption on posterior bias and feature recovery, especially when dependencies among latent features are strong.
- Provide insight into the shrinkage mechanism’s failure modes: when might it over-prune (underfit) or under-prune (overfit)? Any safeguards, diagnostics, or heuristics to detect and mitigate these issues would be valuable.
- Include ablations to isolate the contribution of each component (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage schedule), and sensitivity analyses to initializations and any hyperparameters.
- Elaborate on computational complexity and resource usage: per-iteration cost, memory footprint, convergence behavior, and wall-clock comparisons under matched conditions.
- If possible, “stress-test” the method by pushing it toward known challenging cases and report where performance starts to break down. This would help practitioners judge when the method is reliable.

Overall, this is a clear, well-executed paper presenting a solid and useful extension of FAB to latent feature models, with strong empirical evidence of efficiency and competitive accuracy. Addressing the suggested analyses of approximation limits and sensitivity would further strengthen the contribution.
