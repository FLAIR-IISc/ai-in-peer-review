- Introduces an efficient method for model selection in latent feature models by generalizing FAB inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) to LFMs.  
- Combines a mean-field variational approximation with an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to infer latent variables and determine the global number of features.  
- High degree of automation that substantially reduces the need for manual parameter tuning.  
- Demonstrated strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets with significant computational efficiency gains over existing methods.  
- Technical quality is high, supported by rigorous empirical evaluation and thorough analysis of results.  
- Benchmarked against a wide range of competing techniques across multiple datasets and well-contextualized with appropriate references.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly structured, with a logical exposition from theory to final algorithm.  
- Originality stems from the novel and effective combination of existing components (FIC, mean-field VI, shrinkage priors) rather than new individual components.  
- The resulting algorithm is computationally efficient and practically valuable for model selection in LFMs.  
- Lacks an in-depth discussion of limitations; reliance on multiple approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) could produce failure modes.  
- Recommends stress-testing the method to identify conditions under which the approximations interact poorly and delineate applicability boundaries.
