- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).
- FAB maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) converging to the marginal log-likelihood.
- Original FAB required a block-diagonal Hessian, a condition not met by LFMs.
- Authors derive a valid lower bound for the FIC under the relaxed Hessian condition.
- The derived bound shares the same representation as the FIC for mixture models.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world data compares FAB/LFM to IBP Gibbs, variational Bayes, and MEIBP.
- Results show FAB/LFM outperforms baselines in prediction accuracy and computational efficiency.
- Paper is clearly written with thorough derivations of the method.
- Extension is incremental but conceptually interesting and convincingly demonstrates efficiency.
- Reviewer (not an LFM specialist) considers the work of significant interest to the NIPS community and recommends acceptance.
