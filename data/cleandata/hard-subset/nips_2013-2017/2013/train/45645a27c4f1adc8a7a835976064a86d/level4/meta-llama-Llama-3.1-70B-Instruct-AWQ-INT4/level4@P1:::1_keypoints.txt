1. Proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models by extending factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and using BIC to integrate out parameters.

2. Method resembles variational Bayes but integrates parameters approximately rather than assuming a fully factorized posterior.

3. Computationally efficient: corresponds to an EM algorithm with additional complexity penalties, cheaper than sampling or variational Bayes.

4. Demonstrates improved held-out/predictive likelihoods and much faster runtimes compared to several IBP implementations.

5. Technical contribution is novel but incremental, building on prior FAB work [5,6].

6. Exposition needs clarification—e.g., equation (3)’s log-sum over z can be infinite when all z are zero and should be discussed.

7. Theorem 2 lacks a clear specification of asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K scales with N.

8. Applying BIC per component seems to require fixed finite K, conflicting with IBP’s infinite-component motivation.

9. Minor fixes: clarify that pk(X | z·k) uses only data assigned to component k (section 2) and add an expectation in equation (4).

10. Experimental results are promising but would be stronger with a specified stopping criterion and analysis separating model-selection effects from local-optima differences.

11. Strengths: plausible asymptotic motivation, computational efficiency, and empirical gains in predictive likelihood.

12. Weaknesses: incremental novelty, unclear theoretical assumptions, and some unclear math/exposition.

13. Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify math, assumptions, and experimental details.
