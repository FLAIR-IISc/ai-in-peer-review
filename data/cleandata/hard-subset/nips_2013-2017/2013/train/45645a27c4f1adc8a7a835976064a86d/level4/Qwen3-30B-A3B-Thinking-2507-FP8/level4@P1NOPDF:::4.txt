

This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recent model selection method that achieves strong performance for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models by maximizing a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of FAB is its requirement that the Hessian of the complete likelihood be block diagonal—a condition not satisfied by LFMs. The authors successfully overcome this limitation by deriving a valid lower bound for FIC under LFMs, demonstrating that this bound shares the same representation as FIC for MMs. They evaluate their approach on synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM against fast Gibbs sampling (with Indian Buffet Process), variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). Results show consistent superiority of FAB/LFM across both prediction accuracy and computational efficiency, with the authors providing compelling evidence for its practical advantages.  

The paper is well-structured and clearly articulates the technical derivation. The extension from FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs is incremental but meaningful, addressing a well-identified gap in the literature. The empirical validation is thorough and aligns with the theoretical claims. While the contribution builds on prior work, the authors convincingly demonstrate its applicability to a broader class of models. The results are persuasive and support the proposed method’s efficacy, particularly given the computational benefits observed in larger-scale settings.  

As a reviewer, I note that the technical arguments are sound and the methodology is rigorously presented. The paper would be of significant interest to the NIPS community, especially given the growing relevance of latent feature models in modern machine learning. The writing is generally clear, though minor refinements to phrasing could enhance readability (e.g., "the condition is not satisfied" → "the condition does not hold"). Overall, this is a well-executed study that advances the state of model selection for non-trivial latent structure. The results are convincing, the approach is novel within its scope, and the work merits acceptance.
