- Paper extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), aiming to infer features and select K efficiently.  
- Derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs showing a form similar to mixture models despite shared parameters and non block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Proposes an EM-style algorithm with a model-induced shrinkage regularizer that automatically prunes irrelevant features.  
- Introduces a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) based acceleration for faster shrinkage and convergence.  
- Provides an analysis of parameter identifiability for the proposed method.  
- Empirical results show the method is computationally efficient and achieves strong predictive and model-selection performance versus several IBP-based baselines.  
- Strength: novel and significant extension of FAB to LFMs with an elegant theoretical insight (Lemma 1) broadening FAB’s applicability.  
- Strength: technically sound derivation, clear algorithm, CCCP motivation, identifiability discussion, and monotonic objective improvement guarantee.  
- Strength: methodology and automatic shrinkage mechanism are clearly explained.  
- Weakness: Invalid comparison to MEIBP because MEIBP assumes non-negative W but experiments use real-valued W; baseline results here are unfair.  
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons are unfair/incomplete — FAB given true noise while IBP used heuristic, finite VB-IBP used without justification, and baseline hyperparameter/initialization details (restarts, alpha) are insufficient.  
- Weakness: Missing discussion of limitations — reliance on large-sample asymptotics (behavior in small-N), vulnerability to EM local optima and initialization, and limited model scope (binary LFMs with Gaussian likelihood).  
- Weakness: Omits discussion of closely related work “MAD-Bayes” (Broderick et al., ICML 2013) which also applies asymptotic MAP-based inference to LFMs.  
- Minor issues: possible misquotation of noise-setting heuristics from cited papers and potential mischaracterization of a referenced method.  
- Summary: solid, principled, and promising contribution but requires thorough experimental revision for fair baselines, added limitations discussion, and proper related-work placement before acceptance.
