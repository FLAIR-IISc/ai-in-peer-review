- Proposes an EM-based FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that jointly infers latent features z_n and model complexity K without explicit priors.  
- FAB incorporates a model-selection term into the marginal likelihood to encourage parsimony and scales well to large datasets.  
- Empirically outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP in speed and inference quality on reported benchmarks.  
- Technical approach is sound and algorithmic details are correctly specified.  
- Methodology presentation is clear, but experimental details are insufficiently described.  
- Adapting FAB to LFMs is novel relative to prior FAB work on mixtures and HMMs.  
- Addresses an important problem—efficient LFM inference with automatic K selection—relevant for high-dimensional data.  
- Missing discussion of limitations and failure modes (e.g., when EM-like convergence issues or Gibbs might outperform FAB).  
- Scope limited to binary LFMs; does not extend to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA variants.  
- Experimental comparisons lack rigor: MEIBP comparison invalid because MEIBP requires W ≥ 0 but experiments used real-valued W.  
- Other experimental issues: incorrect noise sigma used for IBP Gibbs on block images, finite VB used instead of infinite VB-IBP variant, and unclear restart/initialization protocols.  
- Implausible result flagged: Gibbs sampler reporting 70 features for 49-dim Sonar data likely due to poor initialization or wrong noise hyperparameters.  
- No analysis/experiments on small-data regimes where FAB’s asymptotic assumptions may fail; efficacy depends on asymptotics.  
- Related-work omission: does not cite MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013), which is relevant for small-variance asymptotics in nonparametric LFMs.  
- Reference correction needed: [21] is MAP inference for IBP, not Gibbs sampling.  
- Other recommended fixes: use 0.25×sample SD as noise std for real data per prior work, and fully document experimental protocols and hyperparameter settings.  
- After author feedback, reviewers still require clarifications on baseline behaviors, explicit ref correction, inclusion of MAD-Bayes, and complete methodological transparency.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and scalable contribution, but accept only after requested experimental and discussion revisions.
