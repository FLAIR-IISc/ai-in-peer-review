- Extension of FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Removes previously required block-diagonal Hessian condition for applicability.  
- Asymptotic analysis shows the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models.  
- Enables automatic hidden-state/feature selection and parameter identifiability.  
- Empirical results show superior model selection, predictive performance, and computational efficiency versus IBP-based methods.  
- Introduces a mean-field approximation for latent-variable inference.  
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration technique that early-eliminates irrelevant features and reduces computation.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical support (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) establishing asymptotic equivalence results.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation against multiple baselines (IBP, VB, MEIBP, EM) on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Supplementary materials include detailed proofs and stated assumptions.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and algorithms/pseudocode enable reproducibility.  
- Novel and significant: broadens FIC applicability and advances model selection for LFMs with minimal hyperparameter tuning.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of when the approximations or asymptotic assumptions may fail and would benefit from systematic failure-case exploration.
