- Central contribution: Extends FAB inference to Latent Feature Models by asymptotically analyzing the LFM Hessian and showing the FIC penalty matches that for mixture models, enabling FAB/LFM.  
- Algorithm: Derives a practical FAB/LFM method with automatic latent-feature selection, parameter identifiability, and a CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration.  
- Theoretical novelty: Lemma 1’s derivation is elegant and non-trivial, suggesting FIC is applicable beyond block-diagonal-Hessian models.  
- Practical impact: The proposed acceleration reduces computation time and the method includes a principled feature-merging post-processing step.  
- Empirical evaluation: Comprehensive comparisons to IBP baselines (fast Gibbs, VB, MEIBP) show superior or competitive model selection, predictive performance, and speed.  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Weakness — asymptotic guarantees: Theoretical results are asymptotic; FIC approximation may be inaccurate in small-N regimes and needs clearer characterization.  
- Weakness — perceived incrementality: The work extends an existing framework (FAB), which may be viewed as incremental despite overcoming a significant theoretical barrier.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel theoretical extension, practical and efficient algorithm with acceleration, strong experimental support, and relevance to model selection in LFMs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Dependence on asymptotic assumptions for performance in small-data settings and reliance on a pre-existing framework (incrementality concern).  
- Overall verdict: Technically sound, significant contribution with strong empirical results — reviewer strongly supports acceptance.
