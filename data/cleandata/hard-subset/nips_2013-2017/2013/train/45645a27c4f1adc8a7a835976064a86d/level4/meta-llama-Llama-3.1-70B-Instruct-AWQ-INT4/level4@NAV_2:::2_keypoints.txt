- Proposal: Generalizes factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and factorized information criterion (FIC) from mixture models to latent feature models using mean-field inference and accelerated feature shrinkage.  
- Methodology: Uses mean-field approximation for latent inference and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to reduce the global number of features.  
- Evaluation: Thorough empirical comparison against a wide range of competing methods across multiple datasets.  
- Results: Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains.  
- Originality/significance: Logical and solid generalization of prior FAB/FIC work; novel combination of several approximations into a working method.  
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written, well structured, with sufficient references.  
- Main concern: Lacks analysis of failure modes—when and how the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) can lead to poor performance.  
- Acceptance pros: solid extension of prior work, efficiency improvements, clear presentation.  
- Acceptance cons: insufficient exploration of the limits/robustness of the approximations.
