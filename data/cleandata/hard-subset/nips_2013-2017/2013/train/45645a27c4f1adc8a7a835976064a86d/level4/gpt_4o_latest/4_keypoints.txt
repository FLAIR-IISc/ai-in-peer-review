- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming the non–block-diagonal Hessian limitation.  
- Derives an effective lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs that shares the same representation as the FIC for mixture models.  
- Notes FAB optimizes a lower bound of the FIC which asymptotically converges to the marginal log-likelihood (background rationale).  
- Validates the method on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Compares FAB/LFM against Indian Buffet Process Gibbs sampling, variational Bayes, and MEIBP.  
- Reports superior predictive accuracy and computational efficiency for FAB/LFM.  
- Paper is well-written with detailed derivation steps.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental but compelling and of clear interest to the NIPS community.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance.
