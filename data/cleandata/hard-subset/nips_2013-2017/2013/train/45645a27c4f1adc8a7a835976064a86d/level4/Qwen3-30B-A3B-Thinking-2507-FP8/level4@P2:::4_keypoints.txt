- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming prior limitations.  
- Proves via Lemma 1 that LFMs share the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as mixture models despite non-block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Derives a novel asymptotic result log det |F^(d)| = ∑_k log(∑_n z_nk)/N + O_p(1) enabling the theoretical extension.  
- Enables FAB/LFMs to inherit FAB properties: automatic feature selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic increase in the FIC lower bound.  
- Introduces CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) to eliminate irrelevant features early and reduce computation.  
- Adds a merge post-processing step to address identifiability issues empirically.  
- Builds on Fujimaki et al.’s FAB framework and fills a gap in scalable model selection for LFMs.  
- Outperforms prior methods (VB, MEIBP, IBP variants) in efficiency and removes need to tune latent dimension K.  
- Empirical results: 3–5× faster than VB, better model selection accuracy (e.g., 95% vs. 82% on EEG), and stronger predictive performance with less overfitting.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees (Theorems 2–4) and detailed algorithmic descriptions with supplementary proofs.  
- Strength: significant theoretical advance (Hessian analysis) extending FIC applicability beyond block-diagonal models.  
- Strength: practical and impactful CCCP acceleration and comprehensive empirical validation across datasets.  
- Weakness: assumption of Gaussian noise limits applicability to non-Gaussian LFMs.  
- Weakness: merge post-processing lacks full theoretical guarantees and could use further justification; discussion of non-Gaussian extensions is limited.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution with minor limitations — Accept.
