- Extends FAB inference to binary latent feature models and proposes the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) with a Laplace approximation and EM-like optimization, showing competitive predictive performance and lower computational cost.
- Main strength: a practical, computationally efficient algorithm for model selection in LFMs that offers a principled alternative to IBP-based nonparametrics.
- Key advantage: algorithm resembles EM with complexity penalties and is significantly faster than MCMC sampling or standard variational inference.
- Major weakness: theoretical exposition is confusing and mathematically imprecise in places.
- FIC definition issue: penalty term log(sum_n z_nk) is undefined when a feature is unused, and the paper does not explain how this edge case is handled.
- Asymptotic assumptions unclear: Theorem 2 lacks a clear statement of the asymptotic regime and whether K is fixed or grows with N, which conflicts with IBP motivations.
- Notation problems: ambiguous expression pk(X|z·k) and a missing expectation operator in Equation (4).
- Runtime comparisons may be misleading because MCMC baselines’ stopping criteria and convergence diagnostics are not specified.
- Source of predictive gains unclear: improvements could stem from optimization/inference differences or initialization rather than the FIC itself, and IBP baselines may suffer from slow mixing or hyperparameter choices.
- Overall: promising and efficient extension but feels incremental; needs clearer theory and deeper, more controlled empirical analysis to substantiate superiority.
