- Introduces a new model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that uses BIC to integrate out parameters instead of a fully factorized variational posterior.  
- Reports superior held-out likelihoods compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Method is well-motivated asymptotically and is computationally efficient, implemented as an EM algorithm with complexity penalties.  
- Technical novelty is incremental, largely extending the existing FAB framework.  
- Mathematical exposition lacks precision: Equation (3)’s log-sum term appears infinite if all z values are zero, which is problematic under non-degenerate q.  
- Theorem 2 is vague about the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly the behavior of K as N increases.  
- Applying BIC per component seems to assume a fixed finite K, which conflicts with the IBP’s motivation of an unbounded, data-dependent K.  
- Minor notation/derivation issues: pk(X | z·,k) should be conditioned only on data assigned to component k, and Equation (4) seems to miss an expectation operator.  
- Empirical results (Table 1) show better predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes, but stopping criteria for algorithms are not specified, complicating runtime comparisons.  
- The source of predictive improvements is not investigated; gains might arise from avoiding poor local optima or implementation/initialization differences rather than the criterion itself.  
- Evidence that the Gibbs sampler learns too many components suggests possible convergence/local-optima issues with competing methods.  
- Overall: conceptually sensible but incremental, with some imprecise derivations and insufficient analysis to fully interpret experimental gains.
