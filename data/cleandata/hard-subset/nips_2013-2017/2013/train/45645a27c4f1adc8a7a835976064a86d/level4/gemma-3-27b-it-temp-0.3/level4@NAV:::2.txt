## Review:

This paper presents a novel algorithm and approximation method designed to enable efficient model selection within the context of latent feature models. The approach leverages factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference alongside the factorized information criterion (FIC) – a technique already established for mixture models, where it approximates model log-likelihood through factorization and Laplace approximation to facilitate tractable model selection. The core contribution lies in generalizing FAB inference to latent feature models, employing a mean field approximation for latent variable inference and incorporating accelerated shrinkage to refine the selection of the global number of features. 

A key benefit of this method is its increased automation in model selection, minimizing the need for manual parameter tuning. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their approach through comprehensive results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, highlighting substantial gains in computational efficiency.

The presented results are supported by thorough analysis, and the method is rigorously evaluated against a diverse set of competing techniques across multiple datasets. The referencing is comprehensive and appropriate. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and logically structured, enhancing its clarity.

This work represents a sound and logical extension of existing research in latent feature model selection utilizing FAB inference and FIC. Specifically, the successful combination of several approximation techniques into a cohesive and effective method is noteworthy. 

However, a deeper understanding of the limitations of this approach would be valuable. Investigating scenarios where the interplay of these approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage) might lead to suboptimal performance – essentially identifying the boundaries of its robustness – would strengthen the paper. Despite this, the authors propose an algorithm/approximation method for efficient model selection for latent feature models by using factorized asymptotic bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). Results are shown on synthetic and real data, and the gain in computational efficiency is significant.
