Summary:
The paper introduces an approximation/algorithmic framework for efficient model selection in latent feature models using factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference together with the factorized information criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC have been established for mixture models, where FIC approximates the marginal log-likelihood via factorization and Laplace’s method to enable tractable model selection. The main contribution is extending FAB to latent feature models by employing a mean-field approximation for the latent variables and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism for the global number of features. A key characteristic of the approach is its automation of model selection with minimal hand tuning. Experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate substantial computational speedups.

Quality:
The work presents careful analysis and evaluates the method against a broad set of baselines across multiple datasets. The citations appear appropriate and sufficient.

Clarity:
The manuscript is clearly written and well organized.

Originality/Significance:
This appears to be a natural yet meaningful extension that generalizes prior FAB/FIC-based model selection to latent feature models, effectively combining several approximations into a single, novel procedure that performs well empirically. It would be helpful to delineate regimes where the approximations (FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage) might degrade performance—i.e., stress-test the method to clarify its failure modes—since multiple approximations are applied in concert. The paper proposes an approximation/algorithmic approach for efficient model selection in latent feature models via FAB and FIC, with results on synthetic and real data showing notable gains in computational efficiency.
