- Proposes generalizing FAB/FIC to latent feature models using a mean-field latent inference and an accelerated shrinkage mechanism for efficient model selection.
- Leverages factorized asymptotic Bayesian inference and factorized information criterion (with Laplace-like approximations) to enable tractable selection.
- Claims automated model selection with minimal manual tuning.
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough with broad comparisons across multiple datasets; references are adequate.
- Paper is clearly written and well structured.
- Originality: a logical and solid generalization of prior FAB/FIC work that unifies several approximations into one method.
- Limitation: lacks deeper analysis of failure modes—needs experiments that push the method until approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) break.
