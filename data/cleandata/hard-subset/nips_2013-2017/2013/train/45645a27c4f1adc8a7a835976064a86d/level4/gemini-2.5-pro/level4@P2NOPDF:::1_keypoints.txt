- Introduces Feature-specific Information Criterion (FIC) for IBP latent feature models, combining variational Bayes with BIC-based parameter integration.
- Claims FIC optimizes a marginal likelihood lower bound while analytically integrating out parameters, improving held-out likelihoods over existing IBP methods.
- Presents a practical trade-off between computational efficiency and model complexity via an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties.
- Empirically achieves higher predictive likelihoods in less time compared to several baselines.
- Contribution appears incremental relative to prior Factorial Asymptotic Bayes (FAB) work.
- Equation (3) may yield a negative-infinite FIC when all z_ik for a datapoint are zero; handling of this case is not explained.
- Theorem 2 lacks a defined asymptotic regime and assumptions, making the BIC-based justification questionable for nonparametric IBP where K can grow with N.
- Using BIC per feature implies a fixed-K regime, conflicting with IBP’s unbounded-feature premise.
- Minor technical errors: p_k(X | z_{.,k}) seems incorrectly conditioned on full dataset X rather than the feature’s subset.
- Equation (4) appears to be missing an expectation over the variational distribution q.
- Experimental methodology lacks specified stopping/convergence criteria for baselines, undermining runtime comparisons.
- Improvements in predictive likelihood may be due to optimization/initialization differences or baselines not converging, not necessarily the FIC criterion itself.
- Gibbs sampler producing many features on some datasets suggests baselines may not have been run to convergence.
- Recommendation: substantially revise theoretical exposition for rigor and provide more controlled, diagnostic experiments to attribute gains to FIC.
