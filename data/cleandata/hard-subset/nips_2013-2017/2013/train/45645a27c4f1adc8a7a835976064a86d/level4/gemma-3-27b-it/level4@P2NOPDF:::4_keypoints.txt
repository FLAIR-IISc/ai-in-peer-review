- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).
- FAB maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood.
- Original FAB was limited to models with block-diagonal Hessian of the complete likelihood, a condition not satisfied by LFMs.
- This paper derives an FIC lower bound for LFMs and shows it is equivalent in form to the FIC used for mixture models.
- Validates FAB/LFM on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Compares against Gibbs sampling with IBP, variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP.
- Demonstrates superior predictive accuracy and improved computational efficiency for FAB/LFM.
- Paper is clearly written with thorough methodological derivations.
- The extension is a valuable incremental contribution likely of interest to the NIPS community.
- Reviewer judges the paper strong and recommends acceptance.
