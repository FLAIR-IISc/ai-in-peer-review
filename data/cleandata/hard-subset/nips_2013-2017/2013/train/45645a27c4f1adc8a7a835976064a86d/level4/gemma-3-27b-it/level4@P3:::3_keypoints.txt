- Proposes a novel FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models to learn feature assignments and latent dimensionality efficiently.  
- Extends FAB to LFMs via a derived FIC (Lemma 1), overcoming previous applicability limitations.  
- Uses FAB model selection to induce sparsity without explicit priors, offering a computationally attractive alternative to IBP.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration mechanism connected to the convex-concave procedure (CCCP).  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets and joint learning of model dimensionality.  
- Reports better runtime and model selection performance than Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP-IBP in experiments.  
- Technical derivations appear mathematically rigorous and the algorithmic implementation seems correct.  
- Methodology section is well-written and clear, though notation may be hard for readers unfamiliar with FAB.  
- Applying FAB to LFMs and the proposed shrinkage acceleration are original contributions.  
- Addresses an important problem in LFMs: efficient inference and automatic model selection, reducing hyperparameter tuning.  
- Lacks thorough discussion of algorithmic limitations and conditions where it may underperform versus alternatives like Gibbs sampling.  
- Convergence properties of the EM-like procedure are under-discussed; initialization and convergence strategies need attention.  
- Algorithm is currently restricted to binary latent features; extensions to continuous-valued features are not addressed.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks sufficient detail and justification for baseline parameter settings (e.g., IBP α, VB noise variance).  
- Does not evaluate performance in small-sample regimes where FAB’s asymptotic approximations may fail.  
- Omits relevant related work, notably MAD-Bayes (Broderick et al., ICML 2013).  
- Comparison with MEIBP is flawed because MEIBP assumes non-negative loadings; experimental comparisons are invalid or need adjustment.  
- Reference [21] is mischaracterized as Gibbs sampling work when it actually proposes a MAP framework.  
- Specific experimental concerns: inconsistent noise variance across methods, VB-IBP setup could be improved, and the Sonar latent-feature count seems suspect.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound extension of FAB to LFMs; scalable and efficient; competitive empirical results; useful shrinkage acceleration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental detail, missing discussion of limitations and small-sample behavior, flawed comparisons, and missing related work.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions to fix experimental methodology, discuss limitations and convergence, include missing related work, and correct inaccuracies.
