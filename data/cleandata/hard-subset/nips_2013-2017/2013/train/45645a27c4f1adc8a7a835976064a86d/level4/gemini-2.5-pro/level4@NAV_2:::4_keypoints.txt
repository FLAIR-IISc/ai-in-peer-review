- Central contribution: Asymptotic analysis shows the FIC for latent feature models has the same form as for mixture models, despite LFMs not meeting the block-diagonal Hessian condition.  
- Practical outcome: This insight enables a complete FAB algorithm for LFMs (FAB/LFM) that performs automatic selection of the number of latent features.  
- Theoretical soundness: Key results (Lemma 1 and Theorem 2) appear correct and form a solid basis for the extension.  
- Empirical validation: Thorough experiments on synthetic and real datasets show superior model selection, predictive performance, and computational efficiency versus IBP-based baselines.  
- Algorithmic refinements: Authors introduce a CCCP-based acceleration (shrinkage acceleration) and a post-processing merge step informed by identifiability analysis.  
- Clarity: The paper is well-written and clearly motivates and derives the FIC for LFMs, with detailed algorithm description.  
- Originality: Novel and non-trivial extension of FAB to LFMs by bypassing the restrictive Hessian assumption; acceleration and merge steps are original refinements.  
- Significance: Provides a competitive, practical alternative to nonparametric Bayesian methods with automatic model selection and fewer tunable hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: Contribution is primarily an extension of existing FAB framework (conceptual foundation inherited), though the technical advances are meaningful.  
- Overall judgment: Strong, technically sound work with convincing empirical results; recommendation to accept.
