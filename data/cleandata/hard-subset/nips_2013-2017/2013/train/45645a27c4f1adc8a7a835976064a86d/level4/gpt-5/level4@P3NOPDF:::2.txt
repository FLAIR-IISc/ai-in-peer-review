Summary:
The paper presents a generalized factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework for model selection in latent feature models (LFMs), extending the factorized information criterion (FIC) and FAB inference beyond mixture models. The method combines a mean-field variational approximation for latent variables with an accelerated shrinkage mechanism that prunes unnecessary global features, yielding an automatic and computationally efficient approach to selecting the number of latent features. Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate substantial computational gains.

Quality:
The experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against a broad set of strong baselines on multiple datasets. The analyses appear careful, and the related work and references are appropriate and sufficient.

Clarity:
The paper is clearly written, well organized, and easy to follow. The motivation for extending FAB to LFMs is well articulated, and the algorithmic components are described coherently.

Originality and Significance:
The work is a logical and solid extension of FAB/FIC ideas from mixture models to latent feature models. The combination of FIC-based approximations, mean-field inference, and feature shrinkage into a unified, practically effective procedure is valuable. The emphasis on automation with minimal manual tuning is particularly appealing for practitioners. Given the computational savings shown, the contribution is likely to be impactful.

Main comments and questions:
- Validity of combined approximations: The approach layers several approximations (FIC asymptotics, mean-field factorization, and aggressive shrinkage). It would be helpful to analyze conditions under which these approximations interact poorly. For instance, does early, accelerated pruning ever remove features that would have become useful later, especially under non-convex objectives?
- Sensitivity and ablations: If not already included, please provide ablation studies isolating the contribution of (i) FIC versus alternative criteria, (ii) the shrinkage schedule/strength, and (iii) mean-field versus more structured variational families. Understanding sensitivity to hyperparameters and initialization would strengthen the empirical case.
- Theoretical guidance: Clarify the asymptotic regime and assumptions under which the FIC extension to LFMs is accurate. Are there bounds on the approximation error or bias relative to exact marginal likelihoods, and do these depend on feature sparsity, sample size, or parameter priors?
- Convergence behavior: Discuss convergence properties of the proposed updates. How susceptible is the method to local optima, and how does the shrinkage interact with potential restarts or annealing? Empirical diagnostics (e.g., variability across random seeds) would be useful.
- Robustness: Please examine performance under model misspecification, such as heavy-tailed noise, correlated or highly redundant features, and very sparse versus dense true latent structures. These are common stress tests for LFMs and may expose failure modes of the approximations.
- Scalability details: Provide complexity in terms of N (samples), D (dimensions), and K (features), and report memory usage. Wall-clock comparisons are valuable, but asymptotic complexity clarifies where the gains come from and how they scale.
- Evaluation metrics: In addition to runtime, report predictive performance (e.g., held-out likelihood or reconstruction error), calibration of uncertainty (when applicable), and, where relevant, interpretability of the learned features. Statistical significance or confidence intervals for improvements would increase confidence.
- Comparison to nonparametric baselines: Situate the method against Bayesian nonparametric alternatives (e.g., IBP/Beta process with MCMC or variational inference). How does the proposed approach trade off accuracy in feature recovery/model selection versus speed and simplicity?
- Practical guidance: Since a key selling point is reduced hand-tuning, please include recommended default settings for priors and shrinkage parameters, and describe failure signals and remedial steps (e.g., detect over-pruning and adjust schedules).

Minor suggestions:
- Clarify the distinction between FAB and FIC early on and maintain consistent notation throughout.
- Provide a concise pseudocode summary of the full algorithm, including the shrinkage mechanism.
- If possible, release code or sufficient implementation details to support reproducibility.

Overall assessment:
This is a strong and practically useful extension of FAB/FIC to latent feature models. The paper is clear, the experiments are comprehensive, and the computational efficiency gains are compelling. Addressing the questions above—especially ablations, sensitivity, and a sharper discussion of approximation validity and failure modes—would further solidify the contribution.
