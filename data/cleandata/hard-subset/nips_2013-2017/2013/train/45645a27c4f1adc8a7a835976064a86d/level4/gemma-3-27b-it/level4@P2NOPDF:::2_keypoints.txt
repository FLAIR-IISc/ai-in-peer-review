- Proposes an efficient algorithm for model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) using factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).
- Extends FAB/FIC to LFMs by combining mean-field latent inference with accelerated shrinkage to select the number of features automatically.
- Reduces reliance on manual parameter tuning during model selection.
- Demonstrates substantial computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and rigorous, comparing favorably against diverse competitive methods.
- Related work is adequately cited.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.
- Original contribution is a logical, well-executed integration of FIC, mean-field approximation, and shrinkage into a novel model-selection algorithm.
- Lacks detailed discussion of limitations and failure modes given the reliance on multiple approximations.
- Recommends studying when the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) break down and mapping the method’s applicability boundaries.
