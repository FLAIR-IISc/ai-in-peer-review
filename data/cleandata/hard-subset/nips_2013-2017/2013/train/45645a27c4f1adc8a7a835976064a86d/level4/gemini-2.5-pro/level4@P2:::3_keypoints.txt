- Paper extends FAB inference to Latent Feature Models, offering an EM-like algorithm that jointly learns parameters and selects the number of binary features via an FIC lower bound.  
- Novel theoretical result: despite LFMs lacking block-diagonal Hessian, the FIC for LFMs takes a similar tractable form as in prior FAB work.  
- Method is computationally efficient and scales to large datasets, avoiding costly sampling-based IBP methods.  
- Practical algorithmic improvements (shrinkage acceleration, CCCP view) reduce computation time and a post-processing merge addresses identifiability/redundancy.  
- Experimental evaluation has major issues that undermine claims of superiority.  
- Invalid baseline: MEIBP comparisons are inappropriate because MEIBP assumes non-negative loadings but was applied to real-valued W data.  
- Baseline configurations are under-specified and may be suboptimally tuned (e.g., VB-IBP details, IBP Gibbs hyperparameters leading to implausible K on Sonar).  
- Unfair hyperparameter treatment: FAB/LFM was given true noise in one experiment while IBP baselines were not.  
- Paper lacks discussion of method limitations, notably reliance on large-sample asymptotics and unknown behavior in small-data regimes.  
- No discussion of convergence/local-optima risks for the EM-like algorithm or recommended initialization strategies.  
- Scope is limited to binary latent features; potential extensions to real-valued latent-variable models (Factor Analysis/PPCA) are not discussed.  
- Important related work ("MAD-Bayes", Broderick et al. 2013) is omitted and should be compared.  
- Miscellaneous issues: possible mischaracterization of a cited IBP method (sampling vs MAP) and an incorrect/noisy-stdev citation—these should be corrected.  
- Recommended fixes: revise experiments for fair/reproducible baselines, add limitations/discussion, include MAD-Bayes, correct citations and minor technical details.  
- Overall: promising and novel contribution but currently unconvincing empirical support; addressing experimental and discussion shortcomings could make it a strong paper.
