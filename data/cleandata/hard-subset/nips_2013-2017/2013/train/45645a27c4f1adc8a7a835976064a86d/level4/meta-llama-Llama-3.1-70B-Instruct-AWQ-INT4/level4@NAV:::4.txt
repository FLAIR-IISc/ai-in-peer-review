The authors of this paper have successfully expanded the scope of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to accommodate latent feature models (LFMs). FAB, a novel model selection technique, has already demonstrated promising results in the context of mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. By maximizing a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), FAB converges to the marginal log-likelihood. However, its applicability has been limited to models where the Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood is block diagonal. The authors have bridged this gap by deriving a lower bound of FIC for LFMs, which shares the same representation as the FIC for MMs. The paper presents empirical results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing the performance of FAB/LFM to alternative methods such as fast Gibbs sampling with Indian Buffet process, variational Bayes, and maximum-expectation IBP. The results unequivocally demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, showcasing its enhanced performance in prediction tasks and reduced computational costs.

The paper is well-structured and clearly articulates the derivation of the proposed method. Although the extension of FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs can be considered incremental, the idea is intriguing, and the results underscore its efficiency. Given its potential impact on the field, I believe this work will resonate with the NIPS community. Overall, the paper presents a compelling concept, supported by convincing results, which warrants its acceptance.
