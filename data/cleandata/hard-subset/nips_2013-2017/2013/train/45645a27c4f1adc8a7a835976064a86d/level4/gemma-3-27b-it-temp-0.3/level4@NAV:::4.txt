This paper presents an extension of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recent model selection technique demonstrating strong performance with mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models, operating by maximizing a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) – an approximation of the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of the original FAB method is its requirement for a block-diagonal Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood, a condition not met by LFMs. The authors successfully circumvent this limitation, deriving a novel FIC lower bound for LFMs that maintains the same functional form as that for MMs. The efficacy of the proposed FAB/LFM approach is demonstrated through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, with comparisons to alternative methods including fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet Process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). These results showcase the advantages of FAB/LFM, indicating improved performance in both predictive accuracy and computational efficiency.

The paper is clearly written, with a detailed and well-explained derivation of the method. While incremental in extending FAB from MMs to LFMs, this work represents a valuable contribution and the experimental results strongly support its effectiveness. Though not my area of expertise, I believe this research would be of significant interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a compelling idea, supported by convincing results, and I recommend it for acceptance.
