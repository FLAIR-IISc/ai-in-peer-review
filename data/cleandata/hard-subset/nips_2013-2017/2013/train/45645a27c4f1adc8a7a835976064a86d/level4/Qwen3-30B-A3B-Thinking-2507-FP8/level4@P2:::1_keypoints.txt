- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), removing the previous block-diagonal Hessian requirement.  
- Shows via asymptotic analysis that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models despite different model structures.  
- Proposes an FAB algorithm for LFMs that achieves automatic feature selection through model-induced regularization.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2) that eliminates irrelevant features early, dramatically reducing computation while preserving performance.  
- Well-motivated alternative to computationally expensive IBP implementations, and clearly related to prior FAB, variational inference, and CCCP approaches.  
- Theoretical strength: clear derivation demonstrating FIC’s broader applicability beyond mixture models.  
- Practical strength: empirical validation that shrinkage acceleration yields large speedups without degrading results.  
- Theoretical strength: proves parameter identifiability for FAB/LFMs (Theorem 4), addressing non-identifiability in standard EM and enabling feature merging.  
- Empirical strength: comprehensive experiments showing better predictive performance and lower computational cost versus IBP, variational EM, and MEIBP on synthetic and real data.  
- Weakness: some theoretical statements lack precision—Theorem 2 omits clear asymptotic regime and assumptions about how K scales with N, potentially assuming fixed K.  
- Weakness: misleading statement that log ∑n znk is infinite when all znk=0; expectations of log sums are finite under any non-degenerate variational q.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons do not clearly separate gains from the model selection criterion versus implementation/optimization details; Gibbs sampler behavior may reflect local optima.  
- Weakness: stopping criterion (δ = 10−4) used in runtime comparisons is not fully justified, leaving open whether speedups stem from algorithmic efficiency or early stopping.  
- Recommendation: clarify asymptotic assumptions (particularly K vs N) and provide additional analysis isolating the source of predictive improvements (model selection vs optimization/initialization).
