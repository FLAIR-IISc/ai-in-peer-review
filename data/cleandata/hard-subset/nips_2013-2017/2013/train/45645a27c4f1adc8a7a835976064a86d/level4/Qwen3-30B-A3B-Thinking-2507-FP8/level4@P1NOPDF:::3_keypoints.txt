- Introduces an EM-style inference algorithm using Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference with shrinkage for binary latent feature models (LFMs), inferring latent features z_n and model dimension K.  
- Method is conceptually related to IBP but replaces prior-driven inference with a FAB-based model selection criterion.  
- FAB frames the log marginal likelihood as expected complete log-likelihood plus a regularization term that encourages feature shrinkage and bounds K.  
- Authors report FAB outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP on large datasets in runtime and inference quality.  
- Technical foundation appears sound and the method can handle large-scale data while automatically selecting K.  
- Methodology is clearly presented, but experimental details lack sufficient precision.  
- Novelty: applying FAB to LFMs is a new contribution beyond its prior use in mixture models and HMMs.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem—efficient LFM inference with automatic K selection—for scalable latent feature learning.  
- Missing analysis of failure modes or scenarios where FAB underperforms compared to Gibbs (e.g., particular data structures or noise regimes).  
- Convergence behavior of the EM-like procedure is not discussed, raising potential stability concerns.  
- Method is restricted to binary LFMs and does not apply to continuous latent-factor models like factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental flaw: MEIBP baseline requires positive loadings W, but synthetic and block experiments used non-positive W, invalidating that comparison.  
- Experimental flaw: IBP Gibbs sampler for block images should use the known noise value instead of a heuristic 0.75×std, to match FAB’s setup.  
- Experimental flaw: VB-IBP used a finite variational approach; the infinite variant would be more appropriate and experimental parameters (e.g., restarts) are underspecified.  
- Lacks analysis/experiments for small-data regimes where FAB’s asymptotic assumptions may not hold.  
- Related work omission: fails to cite and discuss MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013), relevant for nonparametric LFMs.  
- Reported anomaly: Gibbs sampler finding 70 features on 49-D Sonar data likely due to poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance.  
- Incorrect description: Line 70 mischaracterizes reference [21] as Gibbs sampling when it uses MAP inference.  
- Reference/parameter mismatch: paper cites guidance that noise std should be 0.25×empirical std (not 0.75), per cited refs.  
- Post-author feedback still requires clearer experimental justification (e.g., Gibbs α setting, noise hyperparameters), correction of ref [21], and addition of MAD-Bayes citation.  
- Overall: FAB for LFMs is promising for scalability and automatic K selection, but the experimental evaluation needs substantial strengthening to validate the claims.
