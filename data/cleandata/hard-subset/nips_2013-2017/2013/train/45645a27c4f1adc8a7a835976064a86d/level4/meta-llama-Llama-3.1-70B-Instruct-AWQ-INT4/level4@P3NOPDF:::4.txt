This paper presents an extension of the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference method to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection technique that has shown promising results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. The core idea behind FAB is to maximize a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. However, the applicability of FAB has been limited to models that satisfy the condition that the Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood is block diagonal.

The authors successfully extend FAB to LFMs, despite the fact that this condition is not met. They derive a lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and demonstrate that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs. The proposed method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, and its performance is compared to other methods, including fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results show that FAB/LFM outperforms these methods not only in terms of prediction accuracy but also in computational efficiency.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough description of the derivation steps of their method. The proposed model extends FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs, which can be considered an incremental contribution. Nevertheless, the idea is interesting, and the results highlight its efficiency. Although I am not an expert in this area, I believe that this work would be of great interest to the NIPS community.

Overall, this is a nice paper that presents a compelling idea. The results are convincing, and I support its acceptance.
