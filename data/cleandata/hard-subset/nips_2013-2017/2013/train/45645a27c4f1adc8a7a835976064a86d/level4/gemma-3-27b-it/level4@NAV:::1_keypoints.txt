- Proposes a new model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that approximates parameter integration via BIC as an alternative to variational Bayes.  
- Claims improved held-out (predictive) likelihood compared to existing IBP implementations.  
- Method is well-motivated by asymptotic reasoning and offers a compelling computational advantage, resembling an EM algorithm with added complexity penalties.  
- Cheaper computationally than sampling or variational Bayes methods.  
- Technical contributions are novel but appear incremental, building directly on prior FAB work [3,4].  
- Exposition has ambiguities and imprecise mathematical formulations (e.g., eq. (3) log-sum singularity when inputs are all zero).  
- Theorem 2 lacks a clear statement of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K behaves as N grows.  
- Applying BIC per component implies a fixed finite K, which may conflict with the IBP’s ability to generate an unbounded number of components.  
- Minor corrections needed: p_k(X | z_{·,k}) should likely use only data assigned to component k.  
- Equation (4) is missing an expectation symbol on its left-hand side.  
- Table 1 shows higher predictive likelihoods and shorter runtimes for the proposed method; FAB is substantially faster in some cases.  
- Comparison is weakened by an unspecified/stopping criterion for the Gibbs sampler, making runtimes and convergence unclear.  
- The source of predictive gains is unclear—could be the selection criterion itself or differences in convergence to local optima influenced by initialization/implementation.  
- Gibbs sampler sometimes learns too many components on some datasets, suggesting possible suboptimal convergence despite its theoretical exactness.  
- Overall assessment: sensible and potentially valuable approach but somewhat incremental; requires clearer mathematical presentation and deeper analysis to interpret experimental results.
