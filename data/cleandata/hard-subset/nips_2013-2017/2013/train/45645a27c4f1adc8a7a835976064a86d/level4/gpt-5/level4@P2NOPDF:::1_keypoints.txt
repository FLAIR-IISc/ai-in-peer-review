- Proposes a new model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters with a BIC-style penalty and shows higher held-out likelihoods than several IBP implementations.

- Approach is sensible with a plausible asymptotic argument and a key practical advantage: reduces to an EM procedure with complexity penalties, avoiding costly sampling or variational inference.

- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, largely extending existing FAB work.

- Equation (3) is problematic: log(sum z) → −∞ when all z are zero; the paper must clarify handling of zero-activation cases (conventions, smoothing, or conditioning).

- Theorem 2 lacks a clear asymptotic regime and assumptions; it must state how K behaves with N and reconcile a finite-K BIC-like approximation with the IBP’s potentially unbounded K.

- Minor correction: in Section 2, pk(X | z·,k) should condition only on data assigned to component k, not the entire dataset.

- Minor correction: the left-hand side of equation (4) should be an expectation.

- Empirical results show higher predictive likelihood and faster runtime, but interpretation is limited by missing experimental details.

- Stopping criteria for compared methods (especially Gibbs) are unclear; arbitrary stopping rules make wall-clock comparisons unreliable.

- Observed gains may come from optimization differences rather than the model selection criterion; the paper needs controlled ablations (same initialization, update ordering, matched stopping) and optimization diagnostics.

- Recommend multiple restarts, objective traces, and experiments that toggle the model selection penalty while keeping the optimizer fixed to disentangle effects.

- Gibbs’s tendency to learn many components hints at mixing or local-optima issues; comparisons should address whether Gibbs is converged and theoretically aligned with FAB’s approximation.

- Overall: reasonable and practically attractive idea, but needs tighter mathematical statements and more rigorous, controlled experiments to substantiate claimed advantages.
