- Introduces an EM-based inference algorithm for binary latent feature models using FAB to infer features and feature count K.  
- Uses Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference (FAB) to impose shrinkage and upper-bound K instead of prior-induced parsimony like IBP.  
- Claims faster and higher-quality inference than Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP on large datasets.  
- Technical approach appears sound with correct algorithmic details.  
- Algorithm’s scalability and automatic K estimation are highly appealing and significant.  
- Methodology is reasonably clear but experimental details are insufficiently described.  
- Novel application of FAB to latent feature models, extending prior FAB uses in mixtures/HMMs.  
- No discussion of limitations or scenarios where the method underperforms relative to Gibbs sampling.  
- Lacks convergence analysis for the EM-like procedure.  
- Restricted to binary LFMs; not applicable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and contains flaws that undermine conclusions.  
- Unclear behavior in small-data regimes; asymptotic arguments may not hold and need experiments/discussion.  
- Omission of relevant recent works on efficient LFM inference (e.g., MAD-Bayes ICML 2013) and other references.  
- MEIBP baseline experiments are invalid unless the loading matrix W is constrained to be positive.  
- For block images, IBP Gibbs should use known noise value instead of a 0.75 heuristic for fairness.  
- VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version; should use infinite variational version and report restart details.  
- The Gibbs sampler’s 70 latent features on 49D Sonar likely due to poor initialization or incorrect noise variance — needs investigation.  
- Correct reference: [21] describes MAP inference, not Gibbs sampling (Line 70).  
- Minor correction: for real data, recommended noise std-dev is 0.25×data std-dev (not 0.75) (Line 349).  
- After author feedback: some issues addressed but experimental methodology needs further clarification and justification.  
- After feedback: must correct reference [21] and include MAD-Bayes citation; overall scalability claim stands but experiments require rigorous revision.
