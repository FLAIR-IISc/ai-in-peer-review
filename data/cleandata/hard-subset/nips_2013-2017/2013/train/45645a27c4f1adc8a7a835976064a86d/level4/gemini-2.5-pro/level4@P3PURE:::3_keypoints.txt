- Paper proposes an EM-like, FAB-based inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that infers feature assignments and model complexity from an upper bound K.  
- Method is novel in applying the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework to latent feature models and shows promising scalability.  
- Major concern: Comparison with MEIBP is invalid because MEIBP assumes a non-negative loading matrix W, but experiments used real-valued W.  
- Major concern: Experimental setup is unfair/inconsistent — e.g., FAB was given true noise values while IBP Gibbs was not, and VB-IBP experimental details are sparse.  
- Major concern: Reported baseline performance looks questionable (e.g., Gibbs finding ~70 features in 49-D Sonar), suggesting poor tuning or implementation issues.  
- Major concern: Paper lacks discussion of limitations and failure modes, including scenarios where EM-like procedure may perform worse than alternatives.  
- Major concern: Scope is limited to binary features, not continuous factor models, and asymptotic assumptions raise questions about small-data performance.  
- Major concern: Important related work (e.g., MAD-Bayes ICML 2013) is omitted and should be discussed.  
- Minor point: Incorrectly cites [21] as a Gibbs sampler when it is a MAP inference method; citation must be corrected.  
- Minor point: Misstates heuristic for setting noise SD — it should be 0.25 times data SD across dimensions, not 0.75.  
- After author feedback: Authors need to clarify experimental methodology, justify hyperparameter choices, and investigate poor baseline behavior (e.g., fixed alpha, noise variance).  
- Acceptance contingent on addressing experimental flaws, correcting textual errors, and adding missing related-work discussion.
