- Proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters via BIC instead of factorized posteriors, showing improved held-out likelihoods.  
- Main advantage is computational efficiency: the method reduces to an EM algorithm with complexity penalties, avoiding costly sampling or full variational inference.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, essentially extending prior FAB work ([3],[4]).  
- Mathematical imprecision: Equation (3)’s log-sum is infinite if all z are zero — unclear why the FIC score would not be infinite given nonzero probability of that event under q.  
- Theorem 2 omits the asymptotic regime and key assumptions; it does not state how K (number of features) scales with N, making the approximation regime unclear.  
- Applying BIC per component implies fixed finite K, which conflicts with the IBP’s infinite-component model where new features appear as N grows.  
- Fixing K undermines the main motivation for using an IBP prior.  
- Minor technical errors: Section 2’s p_k should condition only on data assigned to component k, and Equation (4) is missing an expectation on its LHS.  
- Experimental results: Table 1 reports higher predictive likelihoods and much lower runtime for FAB, but Gibbs sampler stopping criteria are unspecified, making runtime and convergence comparisons ambiguous.  
- Unclear source of predictive gains: could be the model-selection criterion or algorithmic artifacts (e.g., Gibbs sampling stuck in local optima due to initialization/implementation).  
- Overall: approach is reasonable but incremental, with mathematical imprecision and insufficient experimental analysis preventing clear interpretation of the reported improvements.
