This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models. FAB is a recently developed model selection method that has shown promising results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. The method maximizes a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood. However, FAB has been limited to models that satisfy the condition that the Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood is block diagonal. The authors successfully extend FAB to latent feature models (LFMs), which do not satisfy this condition, by deriving a lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and showing that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs.

The authors evaluate their method, FAB/LFM, using both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing its performance to other methods such as fast Gibbs sampling with Indian Buffet process models, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation Indian Buffet process (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, with the proposed method achieving better performance in both prediction tasks and computational cost.

The paper is well-written, with the authors providing a thorough description of the derivation steps of their method. Although the proposed model extends FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs, which could be considered an incremental contribution, the results highlighting its efficiency make it an interesting and valuable contribution. As a non-expert in this area, I believe that this work would be of great interest to the NIPS community. Overall, the paper presents a compelling idea, and the results are convincing. I support its acceptance.
