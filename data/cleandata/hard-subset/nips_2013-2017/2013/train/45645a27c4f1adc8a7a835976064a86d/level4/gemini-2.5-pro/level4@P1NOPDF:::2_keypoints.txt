- Proposes an efficient model-selection method for latent feature models by generalizing factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).

- Generalization employs a mean-field approximation over latent features and introduces an accelerated shrinkage mechanism to determine the global number of features.

- Leverages factorized Laplace/FIC approximations to make model-selection tractable.

- Key advantages are high automation (minimal manual tuning) and substantial computational efficiency, demonstrated on synthetic and real datasets.

- Evaluation is thorough, comparing against a wide range of competing approaches and supported by sufficient references.

- Paper is clearly written and well structured.

- Novelty lies in combining multiple approximation techniques into a single, effective generalization of prior FAB/FIC work.

- Major shortcoming: lacks analysis of limitations and failure modes—needs experiments probing when interactions among FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage produce poor performance.
