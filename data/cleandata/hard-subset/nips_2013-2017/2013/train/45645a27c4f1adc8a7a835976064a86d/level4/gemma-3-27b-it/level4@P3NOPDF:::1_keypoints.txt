- Introduces a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models using BIC instead of variational Bayes.  
- Claims improved held-out likelihoods over existing IBP implementations.  
- Approach is intuitively motivated by asymptotic arguments and is computationally efficient, resembling an EM algorithm with complexity penalties.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, building on prior FAB work.  
- Mathematical notation and presentation lack precision in places.  
- Equation (3) appears problematic: log-sum over z can be infinite when all zeros, and non-degenerate q assigns non-zero probability to that configuration, raising FIC consistency concerns.  
- Theorem 2 is underspecified: the asymptotic regime and assumptions (especially behavior of K vs N) are not defined.  
- Implicit assumption of fixed, finite K contradicts the IBP’s infinite-component motivation and may invalidate theoretical claims.  
- Minor technical issues: p_k(X | z_•,k) seems computed over the whole dataset instead of only assigned data points.  
- Equation (4) appears to be missing an expectation operator on the left-hand side.  
- Empirical results show higher predictive likelihood and much faster runtime for FAB, but Gibbs sampler stopping criteria are unspecified, undermining comparisons.  
- Unclear whether likelihood gains stem from the proposed criterion or from differences in convergence/local optima and initialization.  
- Gibbs sampler learning too many components on some datasets suggests possible convergence or tuning issues rather than a clear superiority of the method.  
- Overall: promising, computationally attractive method but needs stronger mathematical justification, clearer exposition, and experiments isolating the model selection contribution; contribution is incremental.
