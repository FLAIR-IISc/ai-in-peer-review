

This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming a key limitation of prior FAB work that required the Hessian of the complete log-likelihood to be block diagonal. The authors demonstrate that LFMs, despite not satisfying this condition, yield an FIC with identical form to mixture models (MMs) through rigorous asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1 and Theorem 2). This enables FAB/LFMs to inherit desirable properties: automatic dimensionality selection (via the $\log \sum_n z_{nk}$ term), parameter identifiability (Theorem 4), and computational efficiency. The proposed shrinkage acceleration via CCCP interpretation (Section 3) significantly reduces runtime while maintaining performance, as validated on synthetic and real-world datasets (Table 1, Figure 1). FAB/LFMs outperform state-of-the-art methods (IBP, VB, MEIBP) in model selection accuracy (Fig. 2), predictive performance (PLL), and computational cost (up to 3.7× faster than MEIBP), with superior regularization mitigating overfitting (smaller $\text{PLL}-\text{TLL}$ gap).

**Strengths:**  
- *Technical novelty:* Correctly identifies and resolves the Hessian condition barrier for LFMs, extending FAB's applicability beyond MMs.  
- *Rigor:* Clear asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1–Theorem 2) and identifiability proof (Theorem 4) establish theoretical foundations.  
- *Practical impact:* Shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) and merge post-processing (Section 3) deliver tangible computational gains without sacrificing accuracy.  
- *Evaluation:* Comprehensive experiments (synthetic, real-world) with strong baselines (IBP, VB, MEIBP) and clear metrics (K selection, PLL, TLL).  
- *Clarity:* Well-structured derivation of FIC/LFMs and algorithmic details (Section 2.2) facilitate reproducibility.  

**Weaknesses:**  
- *Scope limitation:* Analysis assumes Gaussian noise (Eq. 5); extension to non-Gaussian LFMs (e.g., Poisson) would broaden applicability.  
- *Comparison depth:* Limited to IBP/VB/MEIBP; recent LFM methods (e.g., [10, 17]) could provide stronger baselines.  
- *Empirical gap:* No ablation on hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., $\delta$ in Algorithm 1) beyond the fixed $\delta=10^{-4}$.  

**Pro/Con Acceptance Summary:**  
*Pros:* Theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs is significant, with clear novelty over prior work (Fujimaki et al., 2014 for FAB/MMs). Results demonstrate state-of-the-art efficiency and accuracy, directly addressing scalability bottlenecks in IBP.  
*Cons:* Scope constrained to Gaussian LFMs; additional baselines would strengthen claims.  

The paper aligns with NIPS’ focus on principled, scalable methods. It advances the state of the art in latent feature modeling by unifying FAB’s automation with LFM flexibility, with clear significance for large-scale applications (e.g., link prediction). The technical contribution is substantial, well-executed, and thoroughly evaluated. I recommend acceptance.
