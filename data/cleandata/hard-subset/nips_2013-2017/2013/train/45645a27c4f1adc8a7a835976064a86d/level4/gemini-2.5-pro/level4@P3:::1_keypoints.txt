- Extends the FAB inference framework to Latent Feature Models and derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs that includes a sparsity-inducing complexity penalty.  
- Proposes an EM-like iterative FAB/LFM algorithm that is computationally efficient.  
- Empirically outperforms several IBP implementations in predictive log-likelihood and model selection accuracy, often at much lower computational cost.  
- Strength — Computational efficiency: FAB/LFM is faster than MCMC or complex variational IBP methods, supported by experimental runtimes.  
- Strength — Strong empirical performance: achieves competitive or superior predictive scores and typically selects more compact models, reducing overfitting.  
- Strength — Theoretical extension: key technical result (Lemma 1) shows the Hessian log-determinant yields a penalty term sum_k log(sum_n z_nk), extending FAB applicability.  
- Strength — Practical refinements: introduces shrinkage acceleration (via CCCP) and a post-processing merge step to improve robustness and efficiency.  
- Weakness — Unclear asymptotic assumptions: unclear how K scales with N and whether per-feature activation counts must grow to infinity, which conflicts with IBP-style motivations.  
- Weakness — Experimental interpretation issues: runtime comparisons are hard to interpret (MCMC stopping unclear) and it’s unclear whether gains come from FIC model selection or better optimization/mixing.  
- Weakness — Incremental originality: primarily an extension of the authors’ prior FAB work, limiting novelty.  
- Minor — Clarify handling of log(sum_n z_nk) when a feature’s usage count is zero under q, especially during optimization.  
- Minor — Verify that p_k(X|z_·k) in Section 1.1 should be defined over the subset assigned to component k, not the entire dataset.  
- Minor — Possible typo in Equation (4): expectation placement appears incorrect; should confirm whether the log applies inside or outside Eq[·].  
- Conclusion: method is practically useful and empirically strong but needs clearer asymptotic justification and deeper analysis of experimental advantages; novelty is somewhat incremental.
