This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference for latent feature models (LFMs), building upon the success of FAB for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. FAB maximizes a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood. However, the original FAB method is limited to models satisfying a specific condition on the Hessian matrix of the complete log-likelihood, which is not met by LFMs. The authors address this limitation and derive a lower bound of FIC for LFMs, showing that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs.

The authors provide a thorough derivation of their method, and the paper is well-written. The results on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM compared to other methods, including fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). FAB/LFM achieves better performance in prediction tasks and lower computational costs.

The proposed model is an extension of FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs, which can be considered an incremental contribution. However, the results highlight its efficiency, and it would be of great interest to the NIPS community. Overall, the paper presents a compelling idea, and the results are convincing. I support its acceptance.

Strengths:

* The paper extends FAB inference to LFMs, addressing a limitation of the original method.
* The authors provide a thorough derivation of their method and present well-written results.
* The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM compared to other methods.

Weaknesses:

* The contribution can be considered incremental, as it builds upon existing FAB/MMs.
* The paper assumes a certain level of background knowledge in Bayesian inference and LFMs.
