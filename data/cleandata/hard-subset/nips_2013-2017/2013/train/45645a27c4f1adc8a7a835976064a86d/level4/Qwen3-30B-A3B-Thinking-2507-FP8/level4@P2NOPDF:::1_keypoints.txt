- Introduces a model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models (IBP), extending variational Bayes with a BIC-style parameter integration and an EM-like algorithm for faster inference.  
- Novel but incremental contribution, building directly on the FAB framework.  
- Major theoretical flaw: Equation (3) contains a log-sum-exp that diverges when all z_{n,k}=0, making the FIC score undefined and requiring clarification.  
- Theorem 2 is underspecified—missing the asymptotic regime, key assumptions, and how K behaves as N grows; applying BIC per component assumes fixed K, contradicting IBP’s growing/infinite K.  
- Minor technical errors: p_k(X | z_{·,k}) should be restricted to data assigned to component k, and Equation (4) needs an expectation operator on the LHS.  
- Empirical results show higher predictive likelihoods and faster runtime, but runtime comparisons are unfair because Gibbs sampler stopping criteria are unspecified.  
- Reported likelihood gains may reflect Gibbs sampler implementation/optimization issues (e.g., overestimating components) rather than superiority of the FAB criterion; results are ambiguous without controlled comparisons.  
- Required fixes: resolve the divergence in Eq. (3), specify the asymptotic assumptions for Theorem 2 (fixed vs. growing K), and perform ablation/controlled experiments to isolate the criterion’s effect.  
- Overall assessment: computationally efficient with modest empirical gains, but theoretical gaps and experimental weaknesses prevent robust support of the methodological claims; strongest validated claim is speed.
