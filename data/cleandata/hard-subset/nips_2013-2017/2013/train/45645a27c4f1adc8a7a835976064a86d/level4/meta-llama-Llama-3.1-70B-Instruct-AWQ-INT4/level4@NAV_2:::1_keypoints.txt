- Proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters with BIC instead of assuming factorized posteriors.
- Empirically shows improved held-out likelihoods and faster runtime versus several IBP methods.
- Computationally appealing: reduces to an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties instead of costly sampling/variational methods.
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, essentially extending prior FAB work.
- Exposition has imprecise math; e.g., equation (3)’s log-sum over z can be infinite when all z are zero, which has nonzero variational probability.
- Theorem 2 lacks a clear statement of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K scales with N.
- Applying BIC per component seems to require fixed finite K, conflicting with the IBP’s infinite-component motivation.
- Minor errors: pk(X | z·,k) should restrict to data assigned to component k; equation (4) needs an expectation on the LHS.
- Table 1 reports faster and better predictive likelihoods, but stopping criteria (especially for Gibbs) are unspecified, complicating comparisons.
- Unclear whether likelihood improvements stem from the model selection criterion or from differences in optimization/local optima and implementation details.
- Gibbs sampler learning too many components on some datasets suggests local-optima/implementation issues rather than a clear method advantage.
- Overall assessment: sensible and efficient approach with promising results, but somewhat incremental, mathematically imprecise in places, and requiring deeper experimental analysis.
