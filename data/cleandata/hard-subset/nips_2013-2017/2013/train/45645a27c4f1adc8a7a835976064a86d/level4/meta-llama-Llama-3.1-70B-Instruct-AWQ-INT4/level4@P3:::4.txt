In this paper, the authors extend factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference for latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently-developed model selection method that has shown promising results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. In essence, this method maximizes a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood. The limitation of FAB has been that it has only been applicable to models satisfying a specific condition on the Hessian matrix of a complete log-likelihood. The authors extend FAB to LFMs, despite the fact that this condition is not satisfied.

The authors provide a thorough derivation of the FIC for LFMs and show that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs. This is a significant contribution, as it demonstrates the wide applicability of FAB inference to various latent variable models. The authors also propose a shrinkage acceleration method that reduces the computational cost of the algorithm. They provide experimental results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM to other methods such as fast Gibbs sampling in models that use the Indian Buffet process, models that use variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP).

The results illustrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, with the proposed method achieving better performance not only in the prediction task but also in terms of computational cost. The authors also provide a CCCP interpretation of the FAB optimization and propose a merge post-processing step to improve the model's identifiability.

The paper is well-written, and the authors thoroughly describe the derivation steps of their method. The proposed model extends FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs and is considered incremental. However, the results underline its efficiency, and it would be of great interest to the NIPS community. The authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their work, making it easy to follow and understand.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian inference by extending FAB to LFMs.
* The authors provide a thorough derivation of the FIC for LFMs and demonstrate its wide applicability.
* The proposed shrinkage acceleration method reduces the computational cost of the algorithm.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM in terms of prediction performance and computational cost.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with FAB inference and LFMs, which may make it challenging for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more discussion on the limitations of their method and potential future directions.
