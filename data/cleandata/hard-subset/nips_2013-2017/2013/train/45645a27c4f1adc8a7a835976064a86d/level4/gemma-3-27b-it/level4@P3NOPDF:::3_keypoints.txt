- Presents an EM-based Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that infers the number of features K via a shrinkage mechanism.  
- Claims FAB outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP in runtime and inference quality on large datasets.  
- Technical approach appears sound and algorithmic details are clearly presented.  
- Method scales to large datasets and jointly infers K, a significant strength.  
- Paper is generally clear, but the experimental setup and results need further clarification.  
- Applying FAB to latent feature models is a novel contribution building on prior FAB work.  
- Solves an important problem—efficient inference when K is unknown—with potential broad impact.  
- Lacks discussion of limitations and scenarios where the method may underperform (e.g., versus Gibbs).  
- Needs discussion/analysis of convergence properties given the EM-like procedure.  
- Method is restricted to binary LFMs and cannot be directly extended to continuous latent-variable models; this limitation should be stated.  
- Experimental evaluation is not comprehensive; behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotic assumptions may fail) is not addressed.  
- Several recent works on efficient inference in LFMs are missing from the related work.  
- Comparison to MEIBP appears flawed because MEIBP requires a non-negative loading matrix; current synthetic/block experiments are invalid for that baseline.  
- Specific experiment fixes suggested: give IBP Gibbs the same known noise level in block data; consider infinite variational VB-IBP and report restart details.  
- Surprising results (e.g., accelerated Gibbs finding 70 features on 49-d Sonar) suggest issues with initialization or noise-variance settings.  
- Correct reference error: [21] is MAP estimation for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling.  
- Recommend adding discussion of MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) as related work on small-variance asymptotics in LFMs.  
- Minor: set real-data noise stddev to 0.25× data stddev (not 0.75) as per prior work.  
- After author feedback: some concerns addressed, but experimental methodology still needs stronger justification, corrections (e.g., [21] misattribution), and inclusion of MAD-Bayes.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and scalable approach; recommend acceptance pending stronger experimental rigor and documentation revisions.
