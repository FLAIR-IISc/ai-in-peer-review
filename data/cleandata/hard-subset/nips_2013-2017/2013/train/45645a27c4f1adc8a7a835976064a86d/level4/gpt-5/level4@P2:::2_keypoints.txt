- Extends FAB to latent feature models by deriving an FIC for LFMs and showing the log(sum_n z_nk) regularizer arises despite non-block-diagonal Hessians (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
- Proposes an efficient FAB algorithm using a mean-field lower bound, an E-step interpreted as CCCP, and an accelerated shrinkage scheme to remove irrelevant features early.  
- Enables automatic feature-number selection, monotonic improvement of the lower bound, and parameter identifiability, with a merge post-processing step.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show improved model selection accuracy, competitive or better predictive performance, and lower computational cost versus IBP variants and EM.  
- Technical soundness: asymptotic derivations are clearly stated, FIC mirroring mixture models is a nontrivial insight, and optimization guarantees monotonic improvement.  
- CCCP interpretation clarifies the shrinkage term’s role and motivates the accelerated removal and identifiability analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive (simulations, real datasets, runtime, model selection, predictive log-likelihoods) and shows the acceleration strategy reduces cost without harming outcomes.  
- Caveats: core “mild assumptions” behind Lemma 1 are not summarized in the main text and should be explained for applicability.  
- Limitations: FIC accuracy may degrade for small N; mean-field factorization and linearization for bounding E[log sum z] may introduce bias and need ablation.  
- Experimental fairness concerns: some baselines were time-capped or manually initialized; convergence criteria and sensitivity analyses should be clarified.  
- Clarity: generally clear with consistent notation and helpful algorithm summary, but add an intuitive sketch of the log det→log(sum_n z_nk) step and summarize assumptions; minor editorial polish needed.  
- Originality/significance: technically novel extension of FAB to LFMs, with useful CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability-guided merging; valuable for automatic model selection on medium-to-large datasets.  
- Suggested additions: report ablations for Tshrink/merge/mean-field, clarify baseline settings and convergence, and include stress tests to illustrate failure modes (e.g., small N).
