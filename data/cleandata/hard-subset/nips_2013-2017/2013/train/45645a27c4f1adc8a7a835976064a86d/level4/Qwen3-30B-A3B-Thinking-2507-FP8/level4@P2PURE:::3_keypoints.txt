- Proposes an EM-based FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that shrinks features and infers K without explicit priors.  
- Shows superior runtime and inference quality versus Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP on large datasets.  
- Technical approach is sound and scalable; algorithmic details are correctly specified.  
- Methodology is clearly presented but some experimental details lack clarity.  
- Novel contribution: extending FAB inference from mixture models/HMMs to latent feature models.  
- Addresses the important problem of efficient inference while simultaneously inferring the number of latent features.  
- No discussion of scenarios where the algorithm may underperform compared to vanilla Gibbs sampling.  
- Lacks convergence analysis for the EM-like procedure and potential convergence challenges.  
- Scope limited to binary LFMs; not applicable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains flaws.  
- No analysis of small-data behavior where asymptotic assumptions may fail.  
- Omits citation/discussion of recent related work (e.g., MAD-Bayes).  
- MEIBP baseline comparisons are invalid because MEIBP requires non-negative W but experiments used negative entries.  
- IBP Gibbs baseline used an inappropriate noise heuristic; should use known noise for fair comparison.  
- Should use the infinite variational VB-IBP (not the finite version used) and provide experimental details like restarts.  
- Accelerated Gibbs finding 70 features on 49-d Sonar data indicates possible initialization or noise-variance mis-specification.  
- Incorrect references: [21] and [22] describe MAP inference, not Gibbs sampling—text must be corrected.  
- Minor: recommended noise std-dev for real data is 0.25× example std-dev (not 0.75).  
- After author feedback: some concerns addressed, but experimental methodology and justification (e.g., Gibbs alpha, noise settings) still need clearer revision and added rigor.
