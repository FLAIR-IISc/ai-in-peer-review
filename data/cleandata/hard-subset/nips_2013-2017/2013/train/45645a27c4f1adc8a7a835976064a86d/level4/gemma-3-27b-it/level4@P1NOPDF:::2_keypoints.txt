- Proposes an algorithm and approximation method for efficient model selection in latent feature models using FAB inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).
- Generalizes FAB to latent feature models by applying a mean field approximation for latent inference and accelerated shrinkage of the global number of selected features.
- Increased automation of model selection, requiring minimal manual tuning.
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Evaluation is thorough, with comprehensive comparisons against a wide range of competing approaches across multiple datasets.
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.
- Represents a logical and robust generalization of prior FAB/FIC work, combining multiple approximations into a novel, practically effective method.
- Lacks analysis of failure modes—needs investigation of conditions under which the combined approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage) lead to suboptimal performance.
