- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that uses FAB shrinkage to learn latent features and infer K.  
- FAB replaces nonparametric priors (e.g., IBP) with a model-selection term encouraging feature shrinkage.  
- Empirically outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP methods on some large datasets.  
- Technical approach appears sound and scalable to large datasets.  
- Paper is generally clear but several experimental details are unclear and need clarification.  
- Novel application of the recently introduced FAB framework to latent feature models.  
- Addresses an important problem: efficient inference while inferring the number of latent features.  
- Missing discussion of algorithm limitations, including scenarios where it may underperform compared to vanilla Gibbs.  
- No discussion of potential convergence difficulties for the EM-like procedure.  
- Method is limited to binary latent feature models; not applicable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and contains possible flaws.  
- Unclear behavior in small-data regimes where asymptotic FAB arguments may not hold; needs experiments/discussion.  
- Recent related works on efficient LFM inference, including MAD-Bayes for the IBP, are not discussed.  
- MEIBP [22] is inapplicable to the paper’s experiments because it targets non-negative linear Gaussian models.  
- Experimental fairness issues: IBP Gibbs should use same noise setting for block images; VB-IBP infinite variational version and VB settings should be detailed.  
- Accelerated Gibbs results on Sonar look suspicious, possibly due to bad initialization or incorrect noise variance.  
- Reference [21] is incorrectly described and should be cited as MAP estimation for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling.  
- Minor correction: line 349 recommendation was sigma = 0.25 * data std, not 0.75.  
- After author feedback, remaining open items: clarify experimental methodology, correct reference [21], and include MAD-Bayes/other recent references.
