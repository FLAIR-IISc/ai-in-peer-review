**Summary**

The authors propose an efficient algorithm for model selection in latent feature models, leveraging factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). Notably, this work extends the application of FAB and FIC, previously well-established for mixture models, to latent feature models. The key contribution lies in the generalization of FAB to latent feature models, employing a mean field approximation for latent variable inference and introducing accelerated shrinkage to efficiently determine the optimal number of global features. A significant advantage of this approach is its ability to automate model selection with minimal manual intervention. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their method using synthetic and real-world datasets, showcasing substantial computational efficiency gains.

**Quality**

The paper presents a thorough analysis of the proposed method, evaluating its performance against a diverse range of competing approaches on multiple datasets. The authors provide sufficient references to support their claims, ensuring the work is well-grounded in existing literature.

**Clarity**

The paper is clearly written and well-structured, facilitating a smooth understanding of the proposed method and its underlying principles. The authors effectively convey the technical details of their approach, making the content accessible to readers familiar with the field.

**Originality/Significance**

The work represents a logical extension and solid generalization of previous research in model selection for latent feature models using FAB inference and FIC. The authors successfully combine multiple approximations (FAB, FIC, mean field, and shrinkage) to create a novel method that demonstrates promising performance. However, it would be beneficial to explore the limitations of these approximations, investigating scenarios where their combined use may lead to suboptimal performance. Understanding the potential failure modes of the method, particularly when "pushed to its limits," would provide valuable insights into its robustness and applicability.

**Additional Comments**

To further strengthen the paper, the authors may consider discussing the potential drawbacks and limitations of their approach, as well as providing guidance on when and how to apply the method effectively. Additionally, exploring the theoretical foundations of the proposed approximations and their interplay could provide deeper insights into the method's behavior and performance.
