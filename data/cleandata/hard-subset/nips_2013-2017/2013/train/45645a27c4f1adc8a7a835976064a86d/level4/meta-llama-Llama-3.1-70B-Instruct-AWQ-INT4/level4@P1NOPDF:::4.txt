This paper presents an extension of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models. FAB is a recently developed model selection method that has shown promising results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. In essence, this method maximizes a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood. However, FAB has a limitation: it has only been applicable to models that satisfy the condition that the Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood is block diagonal. The authors successfully extend FAB to latent feature models (LFMs), despite the fact that this condition is not met. They derive a lower bound of FIC for LFMs and demonstrate that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs. The authors provide experimental results on both synthetic and real-world datasets and compare FAB/LFM to other methods, including fast Gibbs sampling with the Indian Buffet process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results highlight the superiority of FAB/LFM, with the proposed method claiming better performance not only in prediction tasks but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough description of the derivation steps of their method. The proposed model extends FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs, which I would consider an incremental contribution. Nevertheless, it is quite interesting, and the results underscore its efficiency. Although I am not an expert in this area, I believe that this work would be of great interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a nice paper that presents a really interesting idea, with convincing results. I support its acceptance.
