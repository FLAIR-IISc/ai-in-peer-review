- Proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters via BIC, analogous to variational Bayes.  
- Demonstrates improved held-out likelihoods compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Main advantage is computational efficiency: corresponds to an EM algorithm with complexity penalties rather than costly sampling/variational methods.  
- Technical contribution is novel but largely an incremental extension of prior FAB work ([3], [4]).  
- Exposition contains imprecise math (e.g., Eq. (3) where log-sum of z can be infinite under nondegenerate q), raising correctness questions.  
- Theorem 2 lacks clarity on the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K scales with N.  
- Applying BIC per component appears to require fixed finite K, which contradicts the IBP’s infinite-component motivation.  
- Minor errors: missing expectation in Eq. (4) and mis-specified pk(X | z{.,k}) that should only involve data assigned to component k.  
- Experimental results show better predictive likelihood and runtime, but lack of stopping criteria and possible algorithmic/local-optima effects make the source of improvements unclear and warrant further analysis.
