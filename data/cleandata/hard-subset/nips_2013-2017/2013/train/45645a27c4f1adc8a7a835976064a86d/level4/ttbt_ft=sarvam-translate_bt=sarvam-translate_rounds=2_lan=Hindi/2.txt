Summary:

The author proposes an algorithm/estimation method for efficient model selection for implicit feature models using Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) estimation and Factorized Information Criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC are known for mixed models, where the latter estimates the log likelihood of the model through factors and Laplace approximations, making model selection easier. The contribution is a generalization of FAB for implicit feature models, which uses a mean field approximation to estimate implicit features and rapidly reduces the global number of selected features.

A feature of this approach is the greater automation of model selection, requiring minimal manual correction. Results are shown on synthetic and real data, and the benefit in computational efficiency is significant.

Quality:

The presented results include in-depth analysis and the method has been evaluated against a wide range of competing methods on several data sets. References are sufficient.

Clarity:

The paper is clearly written and well-structured.

Originality/Importance:

This appears to be a logical extension and solid generalization of previous work in the field of model selection in LFMs using FAB estimation and FIC. That is, using a combination of several approximations that work well in a new method.

It would be good to understand when/how these approximations can cause poor performance, i.e., "pushing" the method until it breaks, given that several approximations (FIC, mean field, shrinkage) are playing a role at the same time.
