- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).
- FAB maximizes a tractable lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood.
- Known FAB limitation: requires the complete-data Hessian to be block diagonal, which does not hold for LFMs.
- Authors derive an FIC lower bound for LFMs and show it has the same form as for mixture models, overcoming the limitation.
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets compare FAB/LFM to fast Gibbs for IBP, variational Bayes, and MEIBP.
- Results indicate FAB/LFM yields superior predictive performance and computational efficiency.
- Paper is clearly written and derivations are thorough.
- Contribution is incremental but interesting, with empirical results highlighting efficiency.
- Reviewer (not a specialist) believes the work will interest the NIPS community and supports acceptance.
