- Proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models: a variational-Bayes extension that approximately integrates out parameters using BIC.  
- Demonstrates improved held-out/predictive likelihoods compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Motivation rests on a plausible asymptotic argument; method is computationally efficient, equivalent to an EM with complexity penalties rather than expensive sampling or full variational inference.  
- Technical contributions are novel but essentially incremental, building on prior work [3] and [4].  
- Exposition is generally clear but some mathematical derivations need clarification (e.g., Eq. (3) log-sum-of-z can be infinite under non-degenerate q, calling FIC into question).  
- Theorem 2’s asymptotic regime and assumptions are unclear—particularly how K scales with N; per-component BIC appears to require fixed finite K, conflicting with the IBP’s infinite-component nature.  
- Minor fixes needed: pk(X | z·,k) should only include data assigned to component k; Eq. (4) is missing an expectation on the LHS.  
- Table 1 shows better predictive likelihoods and run-time, but results are weakened by the absence of a specified stopping criterion for the Gibbs sampler.  
- Unclear whether predictive improvements stem from the model selection criterion or from other algorithmic factors (e.g., algorithms getting stuck in worse local optima or initialization differences).  
- Evidence is needed that performance gains are due to the criterion; the Gibbs sampler’s tendency to learn too many components on some datasets suggests possible local-optima issues affecting comparisons.  
- Overall: the approach is reasonable and yields runtime and likelihood improvements, but further analysis is required to fully interpret results and validate the claimed contributions.
