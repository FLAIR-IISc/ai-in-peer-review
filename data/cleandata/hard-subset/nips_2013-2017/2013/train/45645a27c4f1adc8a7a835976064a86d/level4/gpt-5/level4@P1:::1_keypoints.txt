- Contribution: Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to binary latent feature models (LFMs) by deriving a factorized information criterion (FIC) and proposing a practical FAB algorithm.  
- Method: Derives FIC for LFMs via Hessian analysis, showing asymptotic FIC has same form as in mixture models, and implements EM-like inference with mean-field E-step, M-step, and automatic feature pruning.  
- Algorithmic novelties: Connects E-step updates to CCCP, introduces a shrinkage-acceleration heuristic, and claims automatic feature selection and parameter identifiability.  
- Empirical claim: Reports improved predictive likelihoods and substantially lower runtimes compared to IBP baselines (Gibbs, VB, MEIBP) on several datasets.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on prior FAB analyses for mixtures/HMMs; technical novelty is extending FIC to LFMs that lack block-diagonal Hessian structure.  
- Strength: Provides a plausible asymptotic argument yielding familiar log∑n znk regularizers for LFMs.  
- Strength: Proposes a simple, scalable EM-like algorithm with an effective pruning mechanism and practical acceleration.  
- Strength: Empirical results often show better predictive performance and large speedups over IBP implementations.  
- Weakness: Asymptotic precision unclear—paper does not state regime/assumptions (e.g., whether K is fixed or grows with N) required for Lemma 1 and Theorem 2.  
- Weakness: Potential divergence—FIC contains −(Dk/2) log∑n znk which is −∞ when ∑n znk = 0; paper does not clarify how this is handled or how the bound remains finite under nondegenerate q.  
- Weakness: Some exposition errors and missing expectations/elements in equations (Section 2 and Eq. 4), reducing clarity.  
- Weakness: Experimental protocol under-specified—stopping criteria for Gibbs, VB, and other methods are not detailed, making runtime and convergence comparisons potentially unfair.  
- Weakness: Unclear source of predictive gains—could be due to optimization/local optima differences rather than principled model selection; more controlled baselines needed.  
- Requested additional experiments: Compare to variational EM/EM with fixed K and identical initialization, report variability across random restarts, and include ablations (no CCCP acceleration, no merges) to isolate contributions.  
- Quality assessment: High-level derivation is plausible and algorithm practically motivated, but key assumptions and edge cases (e.g., log 0) lack rigorous treatment.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally organized but several equations and asymptotic assumptions need clarification to improve rigor and readability.  
- Originality: Novel but incremental—extension of FAB to LFMs with useful heuristics rather than a fundamentally new framework.  
- Significance: Practically appealing with promising empirical gains; impact would increase with clarified theory and more controlled experiments.  
- Pros summary: Scalable EM-like inference, sensible asymptotic justification, CCCP view, and strong empirical runtime/performance.  
- Cons summary: Insufficiently specified asymptotic regime, potential FIC divergence for inactive features, imprecise notation/equations, and unclear experimental comparisons.  
- Overall: Useful and promising extension of FAB to LFMs with practical benefits, but needs tighter mathematical assumptions and more carefully controlled experiments to substantiate claimed advantages.
