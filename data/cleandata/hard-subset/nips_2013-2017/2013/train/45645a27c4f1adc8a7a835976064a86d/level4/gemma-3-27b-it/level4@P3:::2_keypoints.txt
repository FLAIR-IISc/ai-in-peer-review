- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), enabling model selection via a FIC lower bound approximating marginal log-likelihood.  
- Demonstrates LFMs satisfy a condition analogous to a block-diagonal Hessian, allowing FAB application despite prior limitations.  
- Introduces a CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration method to speed up inference.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including parameter identifiability and convergence properties within the FAB/LFM framework.  
- Empirical evaluations show improved model selection, prediction accuracy, and computational efficiency over Indian Buffet Process (IBP) variants.  
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets comparing against IBP variants, VB, and EM.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the theoretical foundations and methodological adaptations.  
- Computational efficiency is a key strength due to the proposed shrinkage acceleration.  
- Addresses the important problem of automated and efficient model selection for LFMs.  
- Weakness: method stacks multiple approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage); potential cumulative errors and failure scenarios are not fully explored.  
- Weakness: limited discussion/analysis of sensitivity to initialization and minor parameter settings.  
- Weakness: could strengthen evaluation by comparing to more recent or highly optimized IBP implementations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound extension with demonstrable efficiency and empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need deeper investigation of approximation effects and failure-mode/sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall: strong, valuable contribution that merits acceptance despite noted limitations.
