Summary: 
The authors propose an efficient algorithm for model selection in latent feature models, leveraging factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC are established methods for mixture models, where they approximate a model's log likelihood through factorization and Laplace approximation, enabling tractable model selection. The key contribution of this work is the extension of FAB to latent feature models, which employs a mean field approximation for latent inference and accelerated shrinkage to determine the global number of selected features. 
A notable feature of this approach is its increased automaticity, requiring minimal manual tuning. The authors demonstrate the method's effectiveness on both synthetic and real-world data, showcasing significant computational efficiency gains. 

Quality: 
The presented results exhibit thorough analysis, with the proposed method evaluated against a diverse range of competing methods across multiple datasets. The references provided are sufficient. 

Clarity: 
The paper is well-written and structured, facilitating easy comprehension. 

Originality/Significance: 
This work appears to be a logical and solid extension of previous research on model selection in latent feature models using FAB inference and FIC. Specifically, the authors successfully combine multiple approximations into a novel method that yields promising results. 
However, it would be beneficial to investigate the scenarios in which these approximations may lead to suboptimal performance, effectively "stress-testing" the method given the interplay of multiple approximations (FIC, mean field, and shrinkage).
