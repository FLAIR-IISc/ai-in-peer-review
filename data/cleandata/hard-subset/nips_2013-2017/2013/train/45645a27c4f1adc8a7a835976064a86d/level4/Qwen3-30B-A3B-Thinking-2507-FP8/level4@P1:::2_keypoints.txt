- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming the previous Hessian condition limitation.  
- Shows the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models despite the Hessian condition not holding.  
- Uses a mean-field approximation for latent variable inference.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method (CCCP-based) that dramatically reduces computational cost.  
- Demonstrates automatic hidden-state/feature selection and parameter identifiability (Theorem 4).  
- Empirically outperforms or matches state-of-the-art methods (IBP, VB, MEIBP) in model selection, prediction, and speed.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with proofs in supplementary material.  
- Conducts comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets using appropriate metrics (time, selected K, predictive and training log-likelihood).  
- Paper is very clear, well-structured, and reproducible; figures (e.g., Fig. 1) effectively illustrate computational gains.  
- Original contributions: non-trivial theoretical generalization of FAB to LFMs and novel shrinkage acceleration technique.  
- Significant practical impact by enabling automatic K selection, valuable for large-scale applications.  
- Strengths: theoretical novelty, rigorous proofs, computational improvements, comprehensive evaluation, no hyperparameter tuning for model selection, identifiability result.  
- Weaknesses: FIC approximation may be less accurate for small N (noted in Libras); needs more analysis of failure modes and assumptions behind the Hessian approximation in Lemma 1.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
