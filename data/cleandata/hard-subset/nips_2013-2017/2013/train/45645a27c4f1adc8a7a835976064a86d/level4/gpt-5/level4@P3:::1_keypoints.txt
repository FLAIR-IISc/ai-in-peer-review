- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (binary features), deriving a FIC penalty −(D/2)∑k log(∑n znk) and an EM-like algorithm.  
- Provides Hessian-based asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1 / Theorem 2) showing the same complexity form as mixtures despite LFMs lacking block-diagonal Hessians.  
- Relates the FAB E-step to a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and introduces an acceleration that speeds shrinkage of irrelevant features.  
- Proves an identifiability property for LFMs and proposes a merge post-processing step motivated by that result.  
- Empirically outperforms several IBP-based methods (Gibbs, variational IBP, MEIBP) on model selection, predictive likelihood, often runtime, and typically finds more compact models.  
- Asymptotic regime is underspecified: need clarity on N→∞ behavior of K (fixed, slowly growing, or data-dependent) and implications for feature activation counts.  
- Lemma 1’s technical conditions (identifiability/regularity, boundedness, eigenvalue/separation) are relegated to supplements and should appear in the main text.  
- Handling of zero or always-on features is unclear: E[log ∑n znk] can be problematic; algorithm uses a lower bound and boundary removal but this should be surfaced and justified early.  
- Minor mathematical imprecision: missing expectation in Eq.(4) and incorrect description of pk(X|z·k) in Sec.1.1 that needs correction.  
- Clarify prior usage: specify where priors (η(πk) term) matter and when they are dominated by the −(D/2) log activation penalty.  
- Notation and indexing need tightening (consistent boldface, explicit definitions of H(q), Op(1)), plus state monotonicity guarantee and stopping criterion δ.  
- CCCP explanation should state why multiple inner CCCP steps accelerate shrinkage and how the Tshrink parameter affects runtime and convergence.  
- Originality: nontrivial extension of FAB via the Hessian argument, practical CCCP acceleration, and useful identifiability/merge insight.  
- Significance: offers a practical alternative to IBP inference with fewer tunables if assumptions hold, but stronger evidence is needed to separate criterion benefits from optimization effects.  
- Experimental methodology needs tighter controls: equalize stopping rules/compute budgets across methods and report performance as a function of time.  
- Initialization sensitivity: report variability across multiple restarts and provide means/variances; Gibbs behavior may reflect convergence/stopping rather than model failings.  
- Ablation suggestion: compare FAB to EM with fixed K (matched and oracle) to isolate gains due to the FIC penalty vs optimization.  
- Baseline parity: match settings like learned vs fixed noise variance, test sensitivity to IBP α, and clarify how MEIBP was adapted for nonnegative data.  
- Pros: conceptually clean extension of FAB, simple hyperparameter-free algorithm with automatic feature selection, and strong empirical results.  
- Cons: asymptotic assumptions and regime are underspecified, some mathematical imprecision, and experimental protocol requires tighter controls.  
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on clarifying asymptotic assumptions, fixing mathematical imprecision, and strengthening experimental validation.
