Summary:
The authors present an algorithm and approximation method for efficient model selection in latent feature models, leveraging factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). FAB and FIC are established techniques for mixture models, where FIC approximates model log-likelihood via factorization and Laplace approximation to enable tractable model selection. This work extends FAB to latent feature models, employing a mean field approximation for latent variable inference and accelerated shrinkage for global feature selection. A key benefit of this approach is increased automation in model selection, requiring minimal manual tuning. The authors demonstrate significant computational efficiency gains on both synthetic and real datasets.

Quality:
The presented results are supported by thorough analysis, and the method is rigorously evaluated against a diverse set of competing approaches across multiple datasets. The references provided are adequate.

Clarity:
The paper is well-written and logically structured. 

Originality/Significance:
This work represents a natural and robust generalization of existing techniques for model selection in latent feature models utilizing FAB inference and FIC. Specifically, the authors effectively combine several approximations into a novel method that performs well in practice. 

However, it would be valuable to understand the conditions under which these approximations might lead to suboptimal performance, or the limits of the method’s robustness. Given the interplay of FIC, the mean field approximation, and shrinkage, it's important to consider how these combined approximations might “break down” under certain circumstances. The authors propose an algorithm/approximation method for efficient model selection for latent feature models by using factorized asymptotic bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). Results are shown on synthetic and real data, and the gain in computational efficiency is significant.
