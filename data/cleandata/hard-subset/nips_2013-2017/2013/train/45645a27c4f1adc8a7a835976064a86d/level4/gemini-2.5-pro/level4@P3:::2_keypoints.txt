- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), enabling automated model selection for LFMs.  
- Main theoretical result (Lemma 1): the LFM Hessian log-determinant has the same asymptotic form as in mixture models, permitting an FIC for LFMs.  
- Proposes a practical FAB/LFM algorithm using a mean-field approximation for latent variables.  
- Introduces a CCCP-motivated shrinkage acceleration technique to speed up feature pruning.  
- Provides parameter identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) and a principled post-processing step to merge redundant features.  
- Empirical evaluation against IBP-based baselines shows more compact models, competitive or better predictive performance, and much lower computational cost.  
- Experiments indicate better regularization (smaller train–test log-likelihood gaps) and strong scalability on larger datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and logically organized, with insightful links between E-step and CCCP.  
- Combines asymptotic analysis, variational inference, and optimization for a novel, practical alternative to nonparametric Bayesian LFMs.  
- Primary weakness: limited discussion of potential failure modes from cascading approximations (FIC asymptotics, convex relaxation, mean-field).  
- Unclear behavior in small-N regimes (e.g., Libras dataset) where asymptotic approximations may fail.  
- Unclear robustness to model mismatch such as non-Gaussian noise or strong latent-feature dependencies violating mean-field.  
- Recommends stress-test experiments or deeper analysis to delineate the method’s applicability limits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: non-trivial theoretical extension, superior empirical performance and speed, and valuable contributions (CCCP insight, identifiability); main con is insufficient limitation analysis.
