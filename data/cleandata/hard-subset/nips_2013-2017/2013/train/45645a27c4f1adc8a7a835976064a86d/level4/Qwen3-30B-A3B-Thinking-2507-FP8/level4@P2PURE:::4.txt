

The authors extend factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection method that achieves strong results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models by maximizing a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. The limitation of FAB is its applicability only to models where the Hessian of the complete likelihood is block diagonal—a condition not satisfied by LFMs. The authors successfully extend FAB to LFMs despite this limitation. They derive a lower bound for the FIC applicable to LFMs and demonstrate that it shares the same representation as the FIC for MMs. The paper presents results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM with competing methods including fast Gibbs sampling (using the Indian Buffet Process), variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate FAB/LFM's superiority, with the authors claiming improved performance in both prediction tasks and computational cost. The paper is well-written, with thorough derivation steps. The extension of FAB from MMs to LFMs is incremental but interesting, and the results convincingly highlight its efficiency. Although not an expert in this specific area, I believe this work would be of significant interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a novel idea with convincing results, and I support its acceptance.
