- Introduces a novel algorithm extending FAB/FIC to latent feature models using mean-field latent inference and accelerated global feature shrinkage for efficient model selection.  
- Reduces manual tuning and shows significant computational gains on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Results are rigorously evaluated against diverse baselines across multiple datasets with comprehensive analysis.  
- Literature review is sufficiently thorough.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized with a logical flow.  
- Original contribution: a well-motivated generalization of FAB/FIC to LFMs that combines multiple approximations into an effective framework with strong empirical performance.  
- Critical concern: lacks systematic exploration of failure modes where mean-field, FIC, or shrinkage approximations may degrade performance (e.g., violated mean-field assumptions or correlated features).  
- Authors should discuss how the approximations interact under challenging conditions to better characterize robustness.
