

Summary:  
The paper introduces an inference algorithm for the binary latent feature model (LFM), where each observation $x_n$ is represented by a $K$-dimensional binary vector $z_n$ with $z_{nk} = 1$ indicating the presence of the $k$-th latent feature. The goal is to infer $z_n$ for all observations (alongside other model parameters). The proposed EM-based algorithm incorporates a shrinkage mechanism to learn $z_n$ and infer the number of latent features $K$. While conceptually related to nonparametric Bayesian latent feature models like the Indian Buffet Process (IBP), the approach does not employ prior distributions to induce model parsimony. Instead, it leverages Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference—a model selection criterion recently developed for mixture models and HMMs. FAB expresses the log marginal likelihood as the expected complete log-likelihood plus a penalty term that encourages shrinkage of $K$ (by imposing an upper bound and learning the optimal $K$ during inference). The method demonstrates superior performance over Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP estimation for the IBP on large datasets, in terms of both runtime and inference quality.  

Quality:  
The technical approach appears sound, with algorithmic details correctly specified. The ability to handle large datasets while inferring $K$ is a significant strength.  

Clarity:  
The methodology is presented clearly. However, certain experimental details require clarification (addressed in the Weak Points section).  

Originality:  
While FAB inference was previously applied to mixture models and HMMs, its extension to latent feature models constitutes a novel contribution.  

Significance:  
The paper addresses a critical problem: efficient inference in latent feature models that simultaneously infers the number of latent features.  

Weak Points:  
- **Limitations**: No discussion of scenarios where the algorithm may underperform relative to vanilla Gibbs sampling.  
- **Convergence**: Absence of analysis on potential convergence challenges inherent to EM-like procedures.  
- **Scope**: Restricted to binary LFMs; not applicable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- **Experimental rigor**: Evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains flaws (detailed below).  
- **Small-data behavior**: Unclear how the algorithm performs when asymptotic assumptions fail (no discussion or experiments).  
- **Related work**: Key recent advances in efficient LFM inference (e.g., MAD-Bayes) are omitted.  
- **MEIBP baseline validity**: The MEIBP method in [22] requires a non-negative loading matrix $W$ (positive entries only), yet the synthetic and block data experiments use $W$ with negative entries, invalidating comparisons. Synthetic data must be generated with positive $W$ for fair MEIBP comparison.  

Other Comments:  
- **Block images**: For fairness, the IBP Gibbs sampler should use the known noise value (not the $0.75$ std-dev heuristic).  
- **VB-IBP [2]**: The infinite variational version (not the finite one used) should be applied; experimental settings (e.g., restarts) lack sufficient detail.  
- **Gibbs on Sonar**: The accelerated Gibbs sampler identifying $70$ latent features on $49$-dimensional Sonar data suggests potential issues with initialization or noise variance specification.  
- **Line 70**: Reference [21] describes MAP inference for IBP (not Gibbs sampling), as does [22]. The text must be corrected.  
- **MAD-Bayes**: The ICML 2013 work "MAD-Bayes: MAP-based Asymptotic Derivations from Bayes" should be cited and discussed.  

Minor Comments:  
- **Line 349**: For real data, [1,22] recommend setting the noise std-dev to $0.25$ times the std-dev of examples across all dimensions (not $0.75$).  

Comments after Author Feedback:  
The feedback addressed some concerns, but further revisions are required:  
- Experimental methodology (for both the proposed method and baselines) needs clearer justification (e.g., why Gibbs used a fixed $\alpha$ leading to overestimated $K$, or suboptimal noise variance settings).  
- Reference [21] describes MAP inference, not sampling—this must be corrected.  
- The MAD-Bayes reference must be included.  
While the proposed inference method is appealing for its scalability and $K$-inference capability, the experimental evaluation requires greater rigor.
