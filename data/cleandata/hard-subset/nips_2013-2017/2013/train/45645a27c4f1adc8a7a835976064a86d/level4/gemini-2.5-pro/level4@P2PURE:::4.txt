This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection method that has demonstrated strong results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. The method works by maximizing a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of FAB has been its applicability only to models where the Hessian matrix of the complete-data likelihood is block-diagonal. The authors address this by extending FAB to LFMs, a model class where this condition is not met. They successfully derive a lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and show that it has the same representation as the FIC for MMs.

The proposed FAB/LFM method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets. It is compared against several alternative approaches, including fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process models, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, with the proposed method achieving better performance in both prediction tasks and computational cost.

The paper is well-written, and the derivations of the method are described thoroughly. While the work extends the existing FAB framework from MMs to LFMs and could thus be viewed as incremental, the extension is non-trivial and the results highlight its efficiency. This contribution should be of great interest to the NIPS community. In summary, this is a solid paper presenting an interesting idea with convincing results. I support its acceptance.
