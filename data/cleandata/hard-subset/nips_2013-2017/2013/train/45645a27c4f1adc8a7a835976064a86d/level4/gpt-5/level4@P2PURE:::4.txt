In this paper, the authors extend factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model-selection method with strong results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models. In brief, it maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A limitation of FAB has been its applicability only to models where the Hessian of the complete-data likelihood is block-diagonal. The authors extend FAB to LFMs even though this condition is not met. They derive a lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and show that it has the same form as the FIC for MMs. They present experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM with alternatives such as fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models, variational Bayes (VB), and the Maximum-Expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, showing better predictive performance and lower computational cost.

The paper is well written, and the derivations are thoroughly explained.

The contribution extends FAB from MMs to LFMs and is, in that sense, incremental. Nonetheless, it is interesting, and the results highlight its efficiency. Although I am not an expert on this topic, I believe it would be of significant interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a solid paper with a compelling idea and convincing results, and I support its acceptance.
