- Introduces a new BIC-like model selection criterion for binary latent feature models, reporting improved held-out likelihood versus IBP-based methods.  
- Method is sensible with a plausible asymptotic justification and offers computational advantage by reducing to an EM-style routine with explicit complexity penalties.  
- Technical contribution is original but incremental, essentially extending existing FAB methodology.  
- Presentation has imprecise mathematics; Eq. (3) appears to diverge when all entries are zero, raising why the FIC objective remains finite given nonzero probability of that event under q.  
- Theorem 2 lacks clear asymptotic regime and assumptions (e.g., how K scales with N); the BIC-like per-component treatment seems to require fixed finite K, conflicting with IBP’s unbounded feature growth.  
- Minor technical issues: in Section 2, pk(X | z·,k) should use only data assigned to component k, and Eq. (4) appears to be missing an expectation on the left-hand side.  
- Quantitative results show higher predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes, but stopping criteria (especially for the Gibbs sampler) are unclear, making runtime comparisons hard to interpret.  
- It’s unclear whether predictive gains stem from the model selection criterion or from different local optima and implementation/initialization choices; stronger evidence is needed to disentangle these factors.  
- The Gibbs sampler sometimes learns too many components, suggesting local optima issues and questioning whether it is estimating the same marginal likelihood FAB approximates.  
- Overall assessment: reasonable but somewhat incremental work with imprecise analysis in parts; experiments show advantages but require additional analysis to clearly attribute improvements.
