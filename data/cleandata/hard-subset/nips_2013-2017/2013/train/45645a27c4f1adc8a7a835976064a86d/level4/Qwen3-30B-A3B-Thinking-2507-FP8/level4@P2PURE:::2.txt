

Summary:  
The authors propose an efficient model selection algorithm for latent feature models via factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). While FAB and FIC are established for mixture models—where FIC approximates log likelihood through factorization and Laplace approximation—their key contribution is generalizing FAB to latent feature models. This generalization employs mean-field inference for latent variables and accelerated shrinkage of the global feature count. The approach achieves greater automaticity in model selection, requiring minimal manual tuning. The authors demonstrate significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets, with thorough empirical evaluation against multiple competing methods across diverse datasets.  

Quality:  
The results include comprehensive analysis, with rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art methods on multiple datasets. The literature review is adequate.  

Clarity:  
The paper is clearly written and well-structured.  

Originality/Significance:  
This work presents a well-motivated extension of prior FAB-based model selection for latent feature models, effectively combining multiple approximations (FIC, mean-field inference, accelerated shrinkage) into a cohesive and effective method. A notable gap remains in understanding failure modes: it would be valuable to identify scenarios where the interplay of these approximations leads to degraded performance, particularly under extreme conditions.
