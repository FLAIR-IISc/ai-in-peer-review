- Proposes a new model selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters with a BIC-based approach and reports improved held-out likelihoods versus IBP methods.
- Main practical advantage is computational: reduces to an EM-style algorithm with complexity penalties, avoiding costly sampling or standard variational Bayes.
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental—primarily an extension of the FAB framework from prior work.
- Mathematical imprecision: Eq. (3) seems problematic because log(sum z) → −∞ if all entries are zero, yet that event has nonzero probability under any nondegenerate q, so the FIC objective may blow up.
- Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions (notably how K scales with N); applying BIC per component appears to require fixed finite K, conflicting with the IBP’s infinite-component nature.
- Minor corrections: in Section 2, pk(X | z·,k) should include only data assigned to component k; Eq. (4) is missing an expectation on the LHS.
- Experimental claims: Table 1 shows higher predictive likelihoods and faster convergence, but stopping criteria (especially for the Gibbs sampler) are unspecified, making runtime comparisons unclear.
- Unclear source of gains: improvements might stem from differences in optimization/local optima or initialization/implementation rather than the model selection criterion itself.
- Evidence of local optima concerns: Gibbs sometimes learns too many components, suggesting optimizer/initialization issues since it nominally targets the exact likelihood FAB approximates.
- Overall: a sensible but somewhat incremental contribution with imprecise mathematics and insufficient analysis to clearly interpret empirical improvements.
