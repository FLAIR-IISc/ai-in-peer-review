- Proposes an efficient algorithm for model selection in latent feature models using factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC).  
- Notes FAB and FIC are established for mixture models; FIC approximates log likelihood via factorization and Laplace approximation to enable tractable selection.  
- Main contribution: generalizes FAB to latent feature models using a mean-field approximation for latent inference.  
- Method accelerates shrinkage of the global number of features selected, improving efficiency.  
- Automates model selection with minimal manual tuning required.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets shows significant computational efficiency gains.  
- Results are thorough, well-analyzed, and compared against diverse competing approaches; references are adequate.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Originality: logical and solid generalization combining FAB, FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage; recommends studying scenarios and failure modes where the approximations may be suboptimal to assess robustness.
