- Proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models by extending factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference.  
- Uses a BIC-like marginal likelihood approximation to enable efficient model selection and parameter estimation as an alternative to VB and IBP sampling.  
- Leverages an EM-style algorithm with complexity penalties, trading off computational efficiency and selection accuracy versus sampling/variational methods.  
- Technical contribution is novel as an extension of prior FAB work but is somewhat incremental.  
- Empirical results show improved held-out/predictive likelihoods over several IBP implementations.  
- Reports substantially reduced computational time and faster convergence in many cases compared to alternatives.  
- Raises concern that equation (3)’s log-sum of z could be infinite if all z are zero, questioning FIC score robustness under nondegenerate q.  
- Theorem 2’s marginal likelihood approximation lacks a clear specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, especially how K scales with N.  
- Implicit assumption of fixed finite K contradicts IBP’s infinite-dimensional motivation and may undermine theoretical justification.  
- Minor exposition issues: p_k(X | z_·,k) should use only data assigned to component k, and equation (4) should explicitly show an expectation on the LHS.  
- Experimental interpretation requires caution because stopping criteria are not specified and Gibbs sampler termination appears arbitrary.  
- Unclear whether predictive gains arise from the model selection criterion itself or from differing susceptibility to local optima; Gibbs often finds too many components.  
- Overall: worthwhile contribution with promising performance; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify mathematical assumptions and exposition.
