- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMMs to latent feature models, overcoming previous applicability limits.  
- Addresses the Hessian block-diagonal requirement by deriving a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) for latent feature models.  
- Shows the derived lower bound has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models.  
- Proposes the FAB/LFM algorithm for latent feature model selection.  
- Evaluates FAB/LFM on synthetic and real-world datasets against fast Gibbs-IBP, variational Bayes, and ME-IBP.  
- Reports that FAB/LFM outperforms baselines in prediction accuracy and computational cost.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, thorough derivation steps.  
- Extension is somewhat incremental but interesting, efficient, and relevant to the NIPS community.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance.  
- Minor suggestions: add a brief FAB introduction/significance, provide more experimental/setup/dataset details, discuss implications and applications, and add a concise conclusion.
