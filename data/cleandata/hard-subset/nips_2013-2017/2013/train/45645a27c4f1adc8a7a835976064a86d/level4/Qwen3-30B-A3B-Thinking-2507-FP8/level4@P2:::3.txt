

Summary: This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), a class of models that represent observations as linear combinations of binary latent features. While FAB inference has previously been applied to mixture models and hidden Markov models, its extension to LFMs is novel. The authors demonstrate that LFMs, despite not satisfying the standard block-diagonal Hessian condition required for FAB, can still utilize the same factorized information criterion (FIC) form as mixture models. The proposed FAB/LFM framework automatically selects the number of latent features through model regularization, without requiring prior distributions or hyperparameters. Empirical results show FAB/LFM achieves better model selection, prediction accuracy, and computational efficiency compared to state-of-the-art methods including variational inference, Gibbs sampling, and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP).

Quality: The technical development is sound and well-justified. The authors provide rigorous asymptotic analysis showing that FIC for LFMs takes the same form as for mixture models despite LFMs not satisfying the standard Hessian condition. The theoretical foundation is solid, with clear derivations and supplementary proofs. The algorithmic implementation appears correct, and the authors demonstrate how FAB/LFM achieves automatic feature selection through a novel shrinkage mechanism. The computational efficiency claims are supported by experiments.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written. The methodology section effectively explains the theoretical contributions and algorithmic details. However, some experimental descriptions lack sufficient detail for reproducibility. For instance, the initialization procedures for different algorithms and specific parameter settings for baseline methods require more precise explanation. The justification for noise variance selection in real-world experiments could be strengthened.

Originality: The application of FAB inference to LFMs represents a meaningful extension of a promising framework previously applied to mixture models and HMMs. While the core FAB approach has been established, its adaptation to LFMs is novel and addresses an important gap in the literature. The authors demonstrate how LFMs, which do not satisfy the standard Hessian condition, still yield the same FIC form as mixture models, revealing broader applicability of the FIC framework.

Significance: The paper addresses a significant challenge in latent feature modeling: efficient inference with automatic model selection. The ability to simultaneously learn latent features and select the appropriate number of features without hyperparameters is highly valuable for practical applications. The demonstrated computational advantages over existing methods, particularly for large-scale datasets, make this work relevant to both theoretical and applied machine learning communities.

Strengths:
- Novel application of FAB framework to LFMs
- Rigorous theoretical analysis showing FIC equivalence with mixture models
- Automatic feature selection without priors or hyperparameters
- Demonstrated computational efficiency on large datasets
- Clear empirical comparison with state-of-the-art methods

Weaknesses:
- The paper lacks discussion of the algorithm's limitations, particularly in small-data regimes where asymptotic assumptions may not hold
- Insufficient analysis of convergence behavior for the EM-like procedure
- Limited scope to binary latent feature models, excluding applications like probabilistic PCA
- Experimental evaluation has several issues:
  * MEIBP comparison is invalid for synthetic data as it requires non-negative W, while the authors' synthetic data generates real-valued W
  * For the block data experiment, the IBP Gibbs sampler should have used the same noise variance as FAB (0.3) rather than a heuristic value
  * The comparison with VB-IBP used a finite K version rather than the infinite variational approach
  * The accelerated Gibbs sampling result of 70 features for Sonar data (49 dimensions) suggests potential issues with initialization or noise variance
  * The reference to [21] as Gibbs sampling is incorrect; it describes MAP inference

Additional suggestions:
- Clarify why FAB outperforms other methods in small-data regimes where asymptotic assumptions are less valid
- Include a discussion of related work, particularly MAD-Bayes [1], which also uses asymptotic analysis for nonparametric LFMs
- Address the issue with MEIBP's applicability to the block data and synthetic experiments
- Provide more detailed experimental methodology for reproducibility, including parameter settings and initialization procedures
- Correct the reference to [21] (it's MAP inference, not Gibbs sampling)

The paper presents a technically sound and novel extension of FAB inference to LFMs with clear theoretical contributions and empirical benefits. While the experimental evaluation requires more careful execution and justification, the core idea is promising and addresses an important problem in latent feature modeling. With improved experimental details and discussion of limitations, this work would make a strong contribution to the field.
