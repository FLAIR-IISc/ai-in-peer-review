- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm using FAB shrinkage to learn binary latent feature models and infer the number of features K.  
- Method is similar in spirit to IBP/nonparametric approaches but replaces priors with a FAB model-selection term.  
- FAB expresses log marginal likelihood as expected complete log-likelihood plus a shrinkage term, allowing an upper bound on K and automatic pruning.  
- Reported results show FAB outperforming Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP for IBP on large datasets in runtime and inference quality.  
- Technical and algorithmic details appear sound and well analyzed.  
- Strength: scales to large datasets and can learn the number of latent features automatically.  
- Major omission: paper does not discuss scenarios where the algorithm may perform worse than alternatives like Gibbs sampling.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient, with methodological flaws and unclear experimental details.  
- Comparison set is incomplete—recent efficient inference methods for LFMs are not included.  
- MEIBP baseline is inappropriately used for some experiments because it applies only to non-negative linear Gaussian models.  
- Algorithm is restricted to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Paper needs clearer experimental methodology and explanations for why certain algorithms behaved as reported.  
- Scores: high originality and significance, but lower clarity and overall quality due to experimental and limitation issues.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel application of FAB to LFMs, scalability, and solid theoretical analysis.  
- Rejection arguments: missing limitation discussion, flawed/insufficient experiments, and limited applicability to only binary LFMs.
