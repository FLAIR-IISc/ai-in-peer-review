- Paper proposes an EM-based inference algorithm for binary latent feature models using FAB to induce shrinkage and infer K.  
- Claims improved runtime and inference quality over Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP methods on large datasets.  
- Technical approach appears sound and handles large datasets while inferring the number of latent features.  
- Paper is generally clear, but some experimental details are under-specified.  
- Applying FAB to latent feature models is a novel contribution building on prior FAB work for mixtures/HMMs.  
- The problem of efficient inference in LFMs and inferring K is important and well-motivated.  

Weak points:
- No discussion of scenarios where the algorithm may perform worse than Gibbs sampling.  
- Convergence issues of the EM-like procedure are not addressed.  
- Method is restricted to binary LFMs and not easily extendable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and contains potential flaws.  
- Behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotic approximations may fail) is unclear and not explored.  
- Several recent works on efficient inference in LFMs are missing from the discussion.  
- Comparison to MEIBP is invalid because MEIBP requires a positive definite (non-negative) W; synthetic/block data comparisons are therefore inappropriate unless datasets are generated with PD W.  

Other experimental/comments:
- For fairness, the IBP Gibbs sampler should be given the known noise value used by FAB in block image experiments.  
- VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version; the infinite variational version and full VB experimental settings (e.g., restarts) should be considered and reported.  
- The accelerated Gibbs sampler finding ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data suggests poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance.  
- Reference [21] is MAP estimation, not Gibbs sampling; the text should be corrected.  
- Recent small-variance asymptotics work (e.g., MAD-Bayes ICML 2013) for nonparametric LFMs should be cited and discussed.  

Minor:
- Suggested standard deviation heuristic in prior work is 0.25 of example stddev, not 0.75 as cited.  

After author feedback:
- Authors addressed some questions, but experimental methodology for both proposed method and baselines still needs clearer explanation and justification.  
- Must correct reference [21] (MAP vs. sampling) and include a citation to MAD-Bayes for IBP.  

Overall:
- The inference method is interesting and scalable with automatic K selection, but the experimental evaluation needs to be more rigorous and carefully revised before acceptance.
