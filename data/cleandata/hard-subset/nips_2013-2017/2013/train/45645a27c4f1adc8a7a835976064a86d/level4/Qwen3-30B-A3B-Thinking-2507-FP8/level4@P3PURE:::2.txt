

Summary:  
The authors propose an algorithm for efficient model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) via factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). While FAB and FIC are established for mixture models—where FIC approximates model log-likelihoods through factorization and Laplace approximation to enable tractable model selection—their key contribution is generalizing FAB to LFMs. This generalization employs a mean-field approximation for latent inference and accelerated shrinkage of the global feature count. The method achieves greater automation in model selection, requiring minimal manual tuning. Results on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate significant computational efficiency gains.  

Quality:  
The results are thoroughly analyzed, with comprehensive comparisons against a wide range of competing methods across multiple datasets. The literature review is adequate and sufficiently referenced.  

Clarity:  
The paper is clearly written and well-structured, facilitating easy comprehension of the technical contributions.  

Originality/Significance:  
This work represents a logical extension and solid generalization of prior FAB-based model selection for LFMs using FIC. The novelty lies in the integrated use of multiple approximations (FIC, mean-field, accelerated shrinkage) into a single effective method. To strengthen the evaluation, it would be valuable to identify scenarios where these approximations may degrade performance—i.e., to rigorously test the method’s limits by "pushing" it until failure, given the interplay of multiple approximation components.
