- Proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models approximating parameter integration via BIC-like terms, yielding an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties and computational advantages over sampling/variational methods.  
- Empirically shows improved held-out likelihoods and faster runtimes compared to existing IBP implementations.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, essentially extending prior FAB work ([3], [4]).  
- Equation (3) appears problematic: log-sum-exp over z can be infinite if all z_{ik}=0 under nondegenerate q, so why isn’t the FIC score always infinite?  
- Theorem 2 is underspecified: it omits the asymptotic regime, key assumptions, and how K behaves as N→∞, conflicting with the IBP’s infinite-component nature.  
- Minor exposition errors: p_k(X | z_{·,k}) should condition only on data assigned to component k, not the entire dataset.  
- Equation (4) is missing an expectation over the variational distribution on its left-hand side.  
- Runtime comparisons are hard to interpret because the Gibbs sampler’s stopping criteria are unspecified, making FAB’s reported speedups arbitrary.  
- Predictive-likelihood gains are not convincingly attributed to the proposed criterion; Gibbs’s tendency to overestimate component counts and possible local-optima/initialization effects could explain results.  
- Overall assessment: conceptually sound but undermined by mathematical imprecision, incremental novelty, and insufficient experimental analysis linking improvements directly to the criterion.
