This paper presents an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). FAB is a recent and highly effective model selection technique, previously demonstrated on Mixture Models (MMs) and Hidden Markov Models. The method works by maximizing a lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC), which approximates the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of the original FAB formulation is its reliance on the complete-data likelihood's Hessian matrix being block diagonal.

The primary contribution of this work is to overcome this limitation, successfully applying FAB to LFMs where this condition is not met. The authors derive a novel lower bound on the FIC specifically for LFMs and show that it shares a similar representation to the one for MMs. The proposed FAB/LFM is empirically evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets, with its performance benchmarked against methods like fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process models, Variational Bayes (VB), and Maximum-Expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach, showing improved performance in prediction tasks and a lower computational cost.

The paper is clearly written, and the theoretical derivations of the proposed method are presented with commendable thoroughness.

While the proposed model is an extension of FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs and could be considered an incremental contribution, it is a non-trivial and interesting one whose efficiency is underlined by the results. Although this is not my primary area of expertise, I believe the work will be of significant interest to the NeurIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a compelling idea with convincing results, and I support its acceptance.
