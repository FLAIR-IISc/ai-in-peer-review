- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), removing the block-diagonal Hessian requirement.
- Derives a lower bound on the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs that shares the same functional form as the FAB-MM FIC.
- Theoretical contribution broadens FAB’s applicability to models where the original Hessian assumption fails.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets shows FAB/LFM outperforms Fast Gibbs (IBP), variational Bayes, and MEIBP.
- Demonstrates both better predictive performance and improved computational efficiency.
- Paper is clearly written with thorough mathematical derivations.
- Contribution is valuable but incremental, building on existing FAB methodology rather than introducing a new paradigm.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS 2013 and considers the work of interest to the community despite limited subfield expertise.
