- Summary: Proposes an EM-style FAB inference algorithm for binary latent feature models that shrinks features to infer K from an upper bound, reportedly outperforming Gibbs, VB, and IBP MAP baselines on large datasets.

- Quality: Technical development is sound and coherent; method scales well and automatically selects K, a strong practical advantage.

- Clarity: Method is generally clear, but experimental setup and baseline configurations lack sufficient detail.

- Originality: Applying FAB to LFMs appears novel and of independent interest, though FAB itself is known for mixtures/HMMs.

- Significance: Efficient, automatic K selection in LFMs is important and the method could be impactful if validated more thoroughly.

- Weak point — limitations missing: Paper lacks discussion of scenarios where FAB may underperform (e.g., multimodal posteriors, strong feature correlations).

- Weak point — convergence: EM-like nonconvex optimization may be sensitive to initialization/local optima; no convergence analysis or practical diagnostics provided.

- Weak point — scope restriction: Method targets binary LFMs only and does not extend directly to continuous latent factor models.

- Weak point — experiments incomplete: Experimental evaluation needs more careful comparisons, ablations, and justified baseline settings.

- Weak point — small-sample behavior: FAB relies on asymptotics; performance in low-data regimes is unclear and unexamined.

- Weak point — missing related work: Recent efficient inference methods for LFMs (e.g., MAD-Bayes small-variance asymptotics) are not adequately discussed.

- Weak point — MEIBP misuse: MEIBP requires nonnegative W; some experiments appear invalid for MEIBP if W was not constrained nonnegative, making comparisons meaningless.

- Other comment — block-image experiments: FAB was given true noise variance; IBP Gibbs should be run with the same noise variance for fair comparison.

- Other comment — VB-IBP baseline: Use infinite variational formulation or at least report truncation level, initialization, restarts, and stopping criteria.

- Other comment — Sonar dataset anomaly: Gibbs discovering ~70 features in 49 dims suggests bad initialization or mis-specified noise variance; sensitivity analysis needed.

- Other comment — citation correction: Reference [21] performs IBP MAP inference, not Gibbs sampling; text should be corrected.

- Other comment — relate MAD-Bayes: Discuss MAP-based small-variance asymptotics for nonparametric LFMs and clarify differences with FAB.

- Minor comment: Correct recommended noise std-dev setting from 0.75 to 0.25 of empirical std-dev per references [1,22].

- Post-feedback issues: Authors addressed some points but must clarify experimental methodology, justify baseline behaviors (e.g., fixed alpha, noise hyperparameters), correct [21] description, and include MAD-Bayes discussion before acceptance.
