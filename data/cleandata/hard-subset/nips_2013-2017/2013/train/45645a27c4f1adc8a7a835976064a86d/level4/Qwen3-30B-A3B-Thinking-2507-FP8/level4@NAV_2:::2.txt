

The authors extend factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), demonstrating that FAB/LFMs maintain the desirable properties of FAB for mixture models while overcoming the previously required condition on the Hessian matrix of the complete log-likelihood. Their asymptotic analysis reveals that the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs shares the same form as for mixture models, despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition. This enables FAB/LFMs to achieve automatic hidden state selection and parameter identifiability, with empirical results showing superior model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency compared to state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process methods. The paper introduces a mean-field approximation for latent variable inference and a shrinkage acceleration technique that significantly reduces computational cost through early elimination of irrelevant features.

Quality: The paper provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including Lemma 1 and Theorem 2, which establish the asymptotic equivalence between FIC for LFMs and mixture models. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing FAB/LFMs against multiple baselines (IBP, VB, MEIBP, EM) across synthetic and real-world datasets, with thorough analysis of computational efficiency, model selection accuracy, and predictive performance. The supplementary materials contain detailed proofs and assumptions supporting the main results.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a logical progression from theoretical foundations to algorithmic implementation and experimental validation. The technical details are sufficiently explained to allow reproduction of the results, and the algorithms are clearly presented with pseudocode.

Originality/Significance: The work represents a significant extension of FAB inference to LFMs, demonstrating that the FIC framework applies more broadly than previously thought. The key insight that LFMs can utilize the same FIC form as mixture models despite lacking the block-diagonal Hessian condition is novel and demonstrates the general applicability of the FIC framework. The proposed shrinkage acceleration technique and the identifiability analysis provide additional theoretical and practical value. This work advances the state of the art in model selection for latent feature models with minimal hyperparameter tuning.

Pro: The paper makes a theoretically sound extension of FAB inference to LFMs, demonstrates rigorous theoretical properties (identifiability, automatic feature selection), provides significant computational advantages through shrinkage acceleration, and shows clear empirical superiority over existing methods.

Con: The paper could benefit from more detailed analysis of the conditions under which the approximations might fail, particularly regarding the asymptotic assumptions. While the paper demonstrates the method's effectiveness, a more systematic exploration of failure cases would strengthen the contribution.
