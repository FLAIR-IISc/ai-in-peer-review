- Proposes an efficient algorithm for model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) by extending FAB inference and FIC with mean-field latent inference and accelerated shrinkage to automate feature-count selection.  
- Reports significant computational efficiency gains on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, compares to many competing methods across multiple datasets, and presents convincing results.  
- Related work is comprehensive and appropriately cited.  
- Paper is very well written, logically structured, and uses accessible mathematical notation.  
- Original contribution: a meaningful generalization of FAB/FIC to LFMs and a notable combination of FIC, mean-field approximation, and shrinkage.  
- Missing a detailed discussion of limitations and scenarios where the multiple approximations might lead to suboptimal performance.  
- Lacks sensitivity analysis to hyperparameters despite claims of minimal tuning.  
- Needs a breakdown of computational costs for each algorithm component (e.g., mean-field vs. FIC) and clearer runtime/memory scaling comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Overall: a strong, high-quality submission addressing an important problem and deserving of acceptance.
