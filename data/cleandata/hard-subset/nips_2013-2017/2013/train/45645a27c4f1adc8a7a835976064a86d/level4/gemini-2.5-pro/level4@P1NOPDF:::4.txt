This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference, a recently developed and effective model selection method, from mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models to the broader class of latent feature models (LFMs). The FAB method works by maximizing a lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood. A key limitation of the original FAB framework was its restriction to models where the Hessian of the complete-data log-likelihood is block diagonal.

The main contribution of this work is to extend FAB to LFMs, a model class that does not satisfy this block-diagonal condition. The authors successfully derive a lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and, notably, show that it takes the same functional form as the FIC for MMs.

The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation on both synthetic and real-world datasets. They compare their proposed FAB/LFM method against several relevant baselines, including Fast Gibbs Sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models, Variational Bayes (VB), and Maximum-Expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, as the proposed method achieves better performance not only in prediction tasks but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well-written, and the authors thoroughly describe the derivation of their method. While the extension of FAB from MMs to LFMs might be considered an incremental step, the contribution is nonetheless interesting and significant. The empirical results are convincing and effectively underline the efficiency of the proposed approach. Although this is not my primary area of expertise, I believe this work will be of great interest to the NIPS community.

In summary, this is a solid paper that presents a valuable extension to an important inference framework. The results are compelling, and I support its acceptance.
