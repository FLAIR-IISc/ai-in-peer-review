- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming prior applicability limits.  
- Derives a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs and shows it matches the FIC form for mixture models.  
- Addresses the Hessian block-diagonality condition violation and adapts FAB to models where it does not hold.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate FAB/LFM outperforms alternatives.  
- Shows better prediction accuracy compared to Indian Buffet Process (IBP) Gibbs sampling, variational Bayes, and MEIBP.  
- Reports computational efficiency gains over competing methods.  
- Thorough, clear derivation of the method and detailed presentation of steps.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and accessible to readers with sufficient background.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental—extension of an existing technique to a new model class.  
- Results are convincing and likely of interest to the NIPS community.  
- Assumes reader familiarity with FAB and LFMs, which may hinder non-experts.  
- Lacks deeper discussion of method limitations and future research directions.  
- Novel application of FAB to LFMs, providing new theoretical and empirical insights.  
- Potentially significant impact on model selection and prediction in latent feature modeling.  
- Experiments are well-designed and presented clearly.  
- Overall recommendation: support acceptance based on novelty, empirical performance, and clarity.
