- Paper proposes a new model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models using a BIC-like approximation to integrate out parameters, similar to variational Bayes.  
- Empirical results show improved held-out likelihoods and faster runtime compared to existing IBP implementations.  
- Method is plausibly motivated by an asymptotic argument and yields computational advantages by reducing inference to an EM algorithm with penalties.  
- Technical novelty appears incremental, building on prior work [3] and [4].  
- Equation (3) is unclear: log-sum of z could be -infinity if all z are zero, which has non-zero probability under non-degenerate variational distributions, raising questions about the FIC score.  
- Theorem 2 lacks clarity on its asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K behaves as N grows.  
- Applying BIC component-wise seems to require a fixed finite K, conflicting with the IBP’s infinite-component formulation.  
- Minor technical issues: definition of pk(X | z·,k) should restrict to data assigned to component k, and equation (4) is missing an expectation on the LHS.  
- Table 1 shows better predictive likelihoods and less computation time, but the Gibbs sampler’s stopping criterion is unspecified, complicating comparisons.  
- It’s unclear whether performance gains stem from the model-selection criterion or from algorithms becoming stuck in local optima or implementation differences.  
- Evidence that the Gibbs sampler learns an excessively large number of components on some datasets suggests potential local-optimum issues.  
- Overall assessment: approach is reasonable and computationally beneficial but incremental, with imprecise mathematics and insufficient analysis to attribute empirical gains to the proposed criterion.
