- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models (LFMs) via asymptotic Hessian analysis and derives a FIC for LFMs plus an EM-like algorithm.  
- Main technical claim: despite LFMs lacking block-diagonal Hessian structure, the resulting FIC has a similar form to that for mixture models.  
- Proposed algorithm maximizes a lower bound on the FIC and is computationally efficient, akin to EM with an extra penalty encouraging compact models.  
- Empirically, FAB/LFM shows strong held-out predictive log-likelihoods and often much faster runtimes than several IBP baselines.  
- FAB yields a smaller gap between training and predictive likelihoods, suggesting effective regularization and reduced overfitting.  
- Originality concern: contribution seen as an incremental but non-trivial extension of prior FAB work.  
- Clarity issue: Equation (3) contains Eq[log(sum_n z_nk)]; if q(Z) allows sum_n z_nk = 0 this gives -∞ — need clarification or constraints on q.  
- The asymptotic regime in Theorem 2 is unclear: how K scales with N is not specified, and the derivation seems to assume fixed K, conflicting with nonparametric IBP motivation.  
- Minor technical clarifications needed: in FIC for MMs, log p_k(X|z_.k) appears to use full X instead of only points assigned to k; Equation (4) seems to be missing an expectation E_q[...].  
- Experimental interpretation concern: timing comparisons may be unfair without specifying stopping criteria, especially for Gibbs samplers vs tolerance-based FAB.  
- Source of predictive gains is ambiguous: unclear whether improvements come from a better model selection objective (FIC) or from superior optimization that avoids poor local optima; more evidence is needed to disentangle these factors.
