- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) by showing an asymptotic Hessian analysis yields the same FIC regularizer as mixture models, enabling automated model selection without hand-tuned priors.
- Lemma 1 proves log det|F(d)| contributes a log(sum_n z_nk) shrinkage term in LFMs despite parameter sharing; Theorem 2 gives an FIC for LFMs with the MM form.
- Proposes a FAB/LFM algorithm using mean-field variational approximation on Z, closed-form M-steps for W, Λ, b, and fixed-point updates for feature inclusion probabilities with FIC-induced shrinkage.
- Reinterprets E-step updates as a convex–concave procedure (CCCP) and introduces a shrinkage-acceleration routine to prune irrelevant features early and reduce computation.
- Provides identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) showing FAB favors balanced, more identifiable solutions and motivates a post-hoc merge step for overlapping features.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show superior model selection, competitive or better predictive performance, and favorable computational cost, benefiting from shrinkage acceleration.
- Strength: elegant theoretical generalization of FAB to LFMs that removes the block-diagonal Hessian requirement.
- Strength: algorithmic clarity with monotonic lower-bound improvement, automatic feature selection, and practical CCCP-based speedups.
- Strength: identifiability theorem gives principled motivation for feature merging.
- Strength: strong empirical evidence of model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency.
- Weakness: “mild assumptions” of Lemma 1 are deferred to the supplement and should be listed and discussed in the main text (e.g., Λ diagonal, bounds on W, moments of X, conditioning on π).
- Weakness: theory is asymptotic; finite-sample behavior and the gap between FIC and marginal log-likelihood need empirical quantification, especially when N is small relative to D,K.
- Weakness: mean-field factorization on Z may be restrictive; impact should be discussed and compared to structured approximations where feasible.
- Weakness: experimental fairness concerns—clarify whether FAB learns noise variance σ and provide controlled comparisons (e.g., fixed σ for parity); report sensitivity to IBP hyperparameter α.
- Weakness: need explicit per-iteration and total complexity in N, D, K and quantified empirical speedup from shrinkage acceleration; detail Tshrink and stopping tolerance δ.
- Weakness: address convergence/local optima issues—initialization strategies, robustness across random starts, and variance reporting (medians/quartiles) for K and PLL/TLL.
- Weakness: merge heuristic not fully specified—provide finite-sample merging criterion (e.g., thresholds) and ablation showing bound improvement after merging.
- Weakness: clarify relation to other asymptotic frameworks (MAD-Bayes, MDL/BIC for singular models) and why the ∑_k log ∑_n z_nk scale is appropriate for LFMs.
- Evaluation: technically sound with clear derivations and identifiability argument but key assumptions should be moved to main text for rigor and reproducibility.
- Evaluation: generally clear writing; tighten exposition around Lemma 1/Theorem 2 and add a complexity section; fix minor typos.
- Evaluation: original extension of FAB to LFMs and thoughtful CCCP-based acceleration and merging ideas.
- Evaluation: significant contribution broadening FAB to widely used models with practical benefits for automated structure learning.
- Pros for acceptance: strong theoretical insight, practical monotonic algorithm with automatic selection and acceleration, solid empirical performance, and identifiability-guided post-processing.
- Cons against acceptance: omitted main-text assumptions for Lemma 1, experimental fairness/sensitivity questions, limited finite-sample/initialization analysis, and underspecified merge criterion.
- Questions for authors: list precise assumptions for Lemma 1 and their scope (non-diagonal Λ, non-Gaussian likelihoods)?
- Questions for authors: did FAB learn noise variance in real-data experiments; if so, provide results with σ fixed for parity?
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to initialization and Tshrink; provide variance across runs?
- Questions for authors: what are per-iteration time complexity and memory footprint; how does acceleration change them empirically?
- Questions for authors: can the method extend to count or binary observations (Poisson/Bernoulli LFMs)?
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance contingent on addressing assumption clarity, experimental fairness, and finite-sample/merge details.
