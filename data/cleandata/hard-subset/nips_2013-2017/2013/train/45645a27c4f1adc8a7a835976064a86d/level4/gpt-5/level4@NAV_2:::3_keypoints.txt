- Summary: Proposes an EM-style FAB inference for binary LFMs that uses a shrinkage complexity penalty to auto-select K, reporting favorable runtime and quality vs. Gibbs, VB, and MAP IBP baselines.
- Quality: Technical development appears sound and algorithm components are coherent.
- Clarity: Methodology is generally clear but experimental setup and certain choices need better specification.
- Originality: Novel and nontrivial extension of FAB ideas from mixture/HMMs to LFMs.
- Significance: Tackles an important problem—scalable LFM inference with automatic determination of feature count.
- Limitation not stated: Paper fails to articulate scenarios where the method would underperform compared to standard Gibbs sampling.
- Convergence concerns: Convergence behavior and possible pathologies of the EM-like procedure are not discussed.
- Scope limitation: Method targets binary LFMs and does not directly apply to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
- Empirical evaluation: Experiments are not fully comprehensive and contain methodological issues that undermine some comparisons.
- Small-sample behavior: Asymptotic arguments may not hold in small-sample regimes; this is insufficiently explored or discussed.
- Missing citations: Some recent efficient LFM inference work is omitted and should be discussed.
- Invalid baseline: MEIBP [22] only applies when W≥0, so certain artificial and block-data comparisons using unconstrained W are not valid baselines.
- Experimental fairness: FAB was given true noise level on block image data; IBP Gibbs should be given the same noise level for fair comparison.
- VB-IBP details: Authors used a finite-K VB instead of infinite variational IBP; settings (e.g., restarts) need to be detailed.
- Suspect results: Accelerated Gibbs finding ~70 features on 49-dim Sonar data seems implausible and likely due to poor initialization or mis-specified noise hyperparameters.
- Mischaracterization: Line 70 incorrectly describes [21] as Gibbs sampling; it is a MAP/submodular method and should be corrected.
- Missing discussion: Paper should discuss MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and relate small-variance asymptotic approaches to their method.
- Minor comment: Line 349—recommended noise std-dev setting for real data per [1,22] is 0.25× empirical std-dev, not fixed 0.75.
- Post-rebuttal revisions needed: Clarify and justify experimental methodology and atypical baseline behaviors, correct [21]’s description, add MAD-Bayes reference, and improve empirical design and reporting.
