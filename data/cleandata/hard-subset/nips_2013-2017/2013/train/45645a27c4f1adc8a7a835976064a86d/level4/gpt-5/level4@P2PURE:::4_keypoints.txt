- Paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs).
- FAB maximizes a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC), which converges to the marginal log-likelihood.
- Prior FAB applicability required the complete-data likelihood Hessian to be block-diagonal; LFMs do not meet this condition.
- Authors derive a lower bound of the FIC for LFMs and show it has the same form as the FIC for mixture models.
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets compare FAB/LFM to fast Gibbs IBP, variational Bayes, and MEIBP.
- Empirical results show FAB/LFM achieves better predictive performance and lower computational cost than alternatives.
- Paper is well written with thorough derivations.
- Contribution is incremental but interesting and of likely interest to the NIPS community; reviewer supports acceptance.
