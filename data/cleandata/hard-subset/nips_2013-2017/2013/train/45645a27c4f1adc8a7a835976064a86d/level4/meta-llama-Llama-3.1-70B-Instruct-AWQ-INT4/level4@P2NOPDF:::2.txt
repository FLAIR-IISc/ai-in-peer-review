The authors propose an efficient algorithm for model selection in latent feature models, leveraging factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC). Notably, FAB and FIC are well-established methods for mixture models, where the latter approximates a model's log likelihood through factorization and Laplace approximation, facilitating tractable model selection. The primary contribution of this work lies in the generalization of FAB to latent feature models, which employs a mean field approximation for latent inference and accelerated shrinkage of the global number of features selected.

A key feature of this approach is its ability to automate model selection with minimal manual intervention. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their method on both synthetic and real-world data, showcasing significant gains in computational efficiency.

The quality of the presented results is high, with a thorough analysis and a comprehensive evaluation of the method against a range of competing approaches on multiple datasets. The references provided are sufficient and relevant.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow. The authors have done an excellent job in presenting their work in a logical and coherent manner.

The originality and significance of this work lie in its logical extension and solid generalization of previous research in model selection for latent feature models using FAB inference and FIC. The authors have successfully combined several approximations to create a novel method that performs well. However, it would be beneficial to understand the limitations of these approximations and how they may impact the method's performance in certain scenarios. Specifically, it would be valuable to investigate when and how these approximations may lead to poor performance, given the interplay between FIC, mean field approximation, and shrinkage.

Overall, the authors have made a valuable contribution to the field, and their work has the potential to significantly impact model selection in latent feature models.
