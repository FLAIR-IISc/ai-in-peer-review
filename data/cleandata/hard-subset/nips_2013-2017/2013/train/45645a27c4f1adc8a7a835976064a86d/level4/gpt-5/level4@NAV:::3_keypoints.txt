- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm for binary latent feature models with a FAB shrinkage mechanism to infer z’s and the number of latent features K.
- FAB reformulates log marginal likelihood as expected complete log-likelihood plus a model selection term to shrink K, avoiding Bayesian nonparametric priors.
- Experiments show FAB outperforms IBP Gibbs, variational inference, and MAP methods on runtime and inference quality on several large datasets.
- Technical development and algorithmic details appear correct; method scales well and automatically selects K.
- Presentation is largely clear, but several experimental setup details are insufficiently explained.
- Application of FAB to latent feature models is a novel extension of previous FAB work for mixtures/HMMs.
- Tackles an important problem: efficient scalable LFM inference with automatic determination of K.
- No discussion of algorithm limitations or scenarios where it may underperform vanilla Gibbs sampling.
- No treatment of potential convergence challenges inherent to EM-like procedures.
- Method is limited to binary latent feature models and does not extend to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and contains flaws that affect baseline comparisons.
- Behavior in small-data regimes (where asymptotic arguments may fail) is unclear and not evaluated.
- Some recent relevant work on efficient inference for LFMs (e.g., MAD-Bayes small-variance asymptotics) is not discussed.
- MEIBP baseline is an invalid comparison for some experiments because MEIBP requires non-negative loading matrices; synthetic data should enforce non-negativity if used.
- For block image data, IBP Gibbs should be given the known noise level for fair comparison rather than a heuristic.
- VB-IBP experiments appear to use a finite truncation; infinite variational formulation and VB hyperparameter/restart settings are under-specified.
- Accelerated Gibbs finding ~70 features on 49-dim Sonar dataset is suspicious and suggests poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance.
- Reference [21] is incorrectly described as Gibbs sampling; it performs MAP inference and must be corrected.
- Minor correction: recommended noise std-dev for real data is 0.25 of empirical std-dev (not 0.75) per cited works.
- After author feedback, experiments still need clearer methodology, justification for baseline behaviors, correction of reference [21], and inclusion/discussion of MAD-Bayes before acceptance.
