- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm with a shrinkage mechanism for binary latent feature models that infers binary vectors and the number of latent features K.  
- Uses Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference (FAB) to encourage feature shrinkage instead of nonparametric priors like the IBP.  
- Claims scalability and superior running time and inference quality compared to Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP IBP on large datasets.  
- Technical details and algorithm appear sound, and scalability/automatic K inference are appealing strengths.  
- Methodology section is reasonably clear but some experimental details are under-specified.  
- Applying FAB to latent feature models is novel, building on prior FAB work for mixtures and HMMs.  
- Addresses an important problem: efficient, scalable inference in LFMs with automatic determination of K.  
- Paper fails to discuss limitations and scenarios where it may underperform relative to Gibbs sampling.  
- No discussion of potential convergence difficulties arising from the EM-like procedure.  
- Restricted to binary latent feature models; cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and contains flaws that undermine some comparisons.  
- Unclear behavior in small-data regimes where asymptotic FAB arguments may not hold; needs discussion or experiments.  
- Overlooks recent relevant works on efficient LFM inference (e.g., small-variance asymptotics / MAD-Bayes) and should discuss them.  
- Comparison with MEIBP is invalid because experimental simulations do not meet MEIBP’s non-negative linear Gaussian assumptions.  
- Specific experimental concerns: IBP Gibbs sampler not given known noise value, VB-IBP settings not detailed, and MEIBP/infinite variational IBP not fairly compared.  
- Result of ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data by accelerated Gibbs is suspect—may reflect bad initialization or incorrect noise variance.  
- Incorrect citation: reference [21] pertains to MAP inference for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling, and should be corrected.  
- After author feedback, reviewer reiterates need for clearer experimental methodology, corrected references, and inclusion of MAD-Bayes/related work; overall method is interesting but experiments need more careful execution.
