- Proposes an EM-based inference algorithm with a shrinkage mechanism for binary latent feature models, using FAB to infer the number of features K.
- FAB expresses log marginal likelihood via expected complete log-likelihood plus a shrinkage model selection term.
- Empirical results claim FAB outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP methods on large datasets.
- Technical approach appears sound and algorithmic details are correct.
- Algorithm scales to large datasets and can infer the number of latent features automatically.
- Methodology section is reasonably clear but several experimental details are unclear or under-explained.
- Applying FAB to latent feature models is novel, building on recent FAB work for mixtures and HMMs.
- Addresses an important problem: efficient inference and model selection in latent feature models.
- Paper lacks discussion of limitations and cases where the method may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling.
- No analysis or discussion of convergence properties of the EM-like procedure.
- Method is restricted to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete and contains apparent flaws.
- Behavior in small-data regimes is unclear and requires additional experiments or discussion.
- Recent related work on efficient inference in LFMs is not discussed or cited.
- Comparison with MEIBP is invalid because the synthetic dataset does not meet MEIBP’s requirements.
- IBP Gibbs baseline should use the same noise value as FAB for fair comparison.
- The infinite variational VB-IBP variant could be used; VB experimental settings are insufficiently described.
- Surprising accelerated Gibbs results on Sonar may stem from poor initialization or mis-specified noise variance.
- A referenced paper was mischaracterized (it concerns MAP estimation for IBP, not Gibbs sampling) and needs correction.
- A recent small-variance asymptotic approach for nonparametric LFMs should be cited and discussed.
- Minor suggestion: clarify how standard deviation was set for real-data experiments.
- After author feedback, several issues remain: experimental methodology/justification, reference corrections, and inclusion of MAD-Bayes/related references.
