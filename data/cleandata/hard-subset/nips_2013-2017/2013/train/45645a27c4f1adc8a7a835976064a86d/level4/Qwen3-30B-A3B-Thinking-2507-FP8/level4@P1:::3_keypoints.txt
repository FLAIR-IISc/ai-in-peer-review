- Introduces Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference for latent feature models (LFMs), extending FAB beyond mixtures/HMMs.  
- Shows LFMs share the same asymptotic behavior as mixture models with respect to the factorized information criterion (FIC) despite not meeting previous Hessian conditions.  
- FAB/LFM automatically selects the number of latent features without priors or hand-tuned hyperparameters.  
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration technique that significantly reduces computational cost.  
- Derives theoretical results (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) establishing FIC equivalence and provides rigorous asymptotic analysis.  
- Presents a CCCP interpretation and a detailed FAB/LFM algorithm (Algorithm 1) with reproducible implementation details.  
- Supplementary materials include thorough proofs supporting the main theoretical claims.  
- Empirical results show superior model selection accuracy, predictive performance, and runtime compared to competing methods on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Formalizes novel insights into parameter identifiability and a natural merge post-processing procedure.  
- Theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs is original and well-contextualized within related NIPS literature.  
- Shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) is a novel and practically useful contribution.  
- Paper is generally well-written and logically structured, with clear mathematical derivations.  
- Comparison with MEIBP is flawed because synthetic datasets contain negative values incompatible with MEIBP’s non-negative model assumptions.  
- Paper does not adequately discuss limitations in small-sample regimes where asymptotic assumptions may fail.  
- Missing comparison with MAD-Bayes, another asymptotic approach for LFMs.  
- Experimental details for VB-IBP (e.g., number of restarts, initialization) are insufficiently documented.  
- Handling and justification of noise variance settings for baseline methods (e.g., fixed 0.75) is inadequate.  
- Some author feedback addressed concerns, but key experimental methodology and contextualization issues remain.  
- Reference [21] is incorrectly described as Gibbs sampling when it implements MAP inference.
