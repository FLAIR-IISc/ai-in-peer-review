Review- Summary:
The paper presents an algorithmic approximation for efficient model selection in latent feature models (LFMs) based on Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC). While FAB and FIC are established for mixture models—where FIC approximates a model’s marginal log-likelihood via factorization and a Laplace approximation to enable tractable selection—the key contribution here is extending FAB to LFMs. The method employs a mean-field approximation for latent variable inference and an accelerated global feature-shrinkage mechanism to prune features. A notable advantage is greater automation of model selection with minimal manual tuning. Experiments on synthetic and real datasets show substantial gains in computational efficiency.

Quality:
The empirical study is thorough, with comparisons against a broad set of baselines across multiple datasets, and the analysis is careful. The citations are adequate.

Clarity:
The manuscript is clearly written and well organized.

Originality/Significance:
This work is a natural yet solid generalization of prior FAB/FIC-based model selection approaches to LFMs, combining multiple approximations into a single, effective method.

It would be helpful to delineate when and how these approximations might degrade performance—i.e., stress-test the method to its limits—given the simultaneous roles of FIC, mean-field, and shrinkage. The authors propose an approximation/algorithm for efficient LFM model selection using FAB and FIC; results on synthetic and real data indicate significant computational speedups.
