**Summary**

This paper proposes an Expectation-Maximization (EM) based inference algorithm for binary latent feature models (LFMs), where each observation is represented as a binary vector of latent features. The algorithm uses a shrinkage mechanism to learn the latent features and infer the number of features (K) using Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference, a model selection criterion recently introduced for mixture models and Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). The FAB approach expresses the log marginal likelihood in terms of the expected complete log-likelihood and a model selection term that encourages shrinkage of the number of latent features. Experimental results on large datasets show that FAB outperforms other inference methods, including Gibbs sampling, variational inference, and MAP estimation methods for the Indian Buffet Process (IBP).

**Quality**

The technical quality of the paper appears sound, and the algorithmic details are correct. The ability to handle large datasets and learn the number of latent features is a significant advantage.

**Clarity**

The methodology section is reasonably clear, but some experimental details are unclear and require further explanation.

**Originality**

The application of FAB inference to latent feature models is novel, although the underlying framework is based on recently proposed work on FAB inference for mixture models and HMMs.

**Significance**

The paper addresses an important problem in efficient inference for latent feature models, including the inference of the number of latent features.

**Weak Points**

Several limitations and areas for improvement are noted:

* The paper lacks a discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm, including potential cases where it may perform worse than vanilla Gibbs sampling.
* There is no discussion on the convergence properties of the EM-like procedure.
* The algorithm is limited to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
* The experimental evaluation is not thorough and appears flawed in some cases.
* The behavior of the algorithm in the small-data regime is unclear, and further discussion or experiments are needed.
* Recent works on efficient inference in LFMs are not discussed in the paper.
* The comparison with the MEIBP algorithm is invalid, as the synthetic dataset does not meet the requirements for the MEIBP baseline.

**Other Comments**

Several specific comments are made on the experimental evaluation, including:

* The IBP Gibbs sampler should be given the same noise value as the FAB algorithm for fairness.
* The infinite variational version of VB-IBP could be used, and experimental settings for VB are not described in sufficient detail.
* The results for accelerated Gibbs sampling on the Sonar data are surprising and may be due to bad initialization or poorly specified noise variance.
* A reference is corrected, as it pertains to MAP estimation for the IBP, not Gibbs sampling.
* A recent work on efficient inference using small-variance asymptotic in nonparametric LFMs is noted and should be discussed.

**Minor Comments**

A minor comment is made on the suggestion for setting the standard deviation for real-data experiments.

**Comments after Author Feedback**

The author feedback addresses some questions, but several issues remain to be fixed or improved before the paper can be accepted, including:

* Experimental methodology and justifications for certain algorithmic choices need to be better explained.
* Reference corrections are needed, including the correction of the MAP inference reference.
* The reference on MAD-Bayes inference for the IBP should be included.
