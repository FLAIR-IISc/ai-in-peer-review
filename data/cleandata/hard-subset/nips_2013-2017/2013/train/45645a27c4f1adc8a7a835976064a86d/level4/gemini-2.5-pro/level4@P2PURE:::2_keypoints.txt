- Proposes a generalization of FAB inference and FIC from mixture models to latent feature models using a mean-field approximation and accelerated global feature shrinkage.  
- Main benefit: largely automated model selection with minimal manual tuning.  
- Empirical claim: significant computational-efficiency improvements on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Thorough evaluation against many competitive baselines across multiple datasets.  
- Writing and organization are clear and easy to follow.  
- Conceptually a solid, logical extension that combines existing approximation techniques into an effective method.  
- References are sufficient.  
- Main weakness: no analysis of failure modes or conditions under which the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) may perform poorly.
