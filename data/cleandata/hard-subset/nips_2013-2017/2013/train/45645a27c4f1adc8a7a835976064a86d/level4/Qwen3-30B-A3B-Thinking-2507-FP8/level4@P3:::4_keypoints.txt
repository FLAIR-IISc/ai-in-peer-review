- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), removing the prior block-diagonal Hessian restriction.  
- Theoretical core: shows FIC for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models via a novel asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1).  
- Consequence: FAB/LFMs inherit automatic hidden-feature selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic convergence properties.  
- Connects FAB/LFMs to convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and uses this to derive a practical shrinkage acceleration method.  
- Provides an identifiability theorem that justifies a merge post-processing step for redundant features.  
- Builds on Fujimaki et al.'s FAB work and positions FAB/LFMs as an alternative to IBP-based NPB and VB methods.  
- Key practical advantage: no hyperparameter/prior tuning required; model complexity is selected automatically via FIC regularization.  
- Rigorous, well-motivated theoretical development with proofs and supplementary material for reproducibility.  
- Comprehensive experiments (simulations, block data, real datasets) comparing to IBP (Gibbs), finite-K VB, and MEIBP.  
- Empirical results: superior model-selection accuracy (especially large N), higher predictive log-likelihoods, and better computational efficiency.  
- Shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) empirically cuts computation by removing irrelevant features early.  
- Weakness: FIC approximation may be inaccurate for small N; practical impact needs clearer discussion.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to VB methods due to omitted VB runs for computational reasons.  
- Weakness: connection between Theorem 4 (identifiability) and the practical merge post-processing should be clarified in the main text.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, significant, and the reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
