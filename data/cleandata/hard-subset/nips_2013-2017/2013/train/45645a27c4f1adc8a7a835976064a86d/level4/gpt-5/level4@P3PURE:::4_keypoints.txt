- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs).
- Addresses prior FAB limitation that required block-diagonal Hessians by handling LFMs where this condition fails.
- Derives an effective lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs.
- Shows the derived FIC lower bound for LFMs has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models.
- Empirical evaluation uses both synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Compares FAB/LFM to fast Gibbs sampling for IBP, variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP.
- Results indicate FAB/LFM outperforms alternatives in prediction accuracy and computational cost.
- Paper is well written with clear and thorough derivations.
- Contribution is somewhat incremental but interesting and practically efficient.
- Reviewer believes the work is of significant interest to the NIPS community and supports acceptance.
