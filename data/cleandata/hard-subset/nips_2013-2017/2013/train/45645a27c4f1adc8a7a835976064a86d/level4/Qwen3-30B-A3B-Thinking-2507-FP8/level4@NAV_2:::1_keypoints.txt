- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), showing applicability despite LFMs not meeting prior block-diagonal Hessian conditions.  
- Asymptotic analysis and Theorem 2 claim that LFMs' Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) has the same form as for Mixture Models (MMs), enabling FAB inference for LFMs.  
- Claims desirable properties: automatic hidden-state selection and parameter identifiability for LFMs under FAB.  
- Empirical results report improved model selection, predictive performance, and computational efficiency vs state-of-the-art NPB and VB methods.  
- Technical quality: generally sound asymptotic analysis and proofs, but some mathematical details are imprecise.  
- Major math concern: Equation (3) includes a log-sum over z that would be infinite if all z are zero (nonzero probability under nondegenerate q), raising doubts about FIC finiteness.  
- Theorem 2 lacks clarity about the asymptotic regime and how the number of features K scales with sample size N, important given IBP’s infinite-feature assumption.  
- Clarity issues: equation (4) appears to miss an expectation, and notation in section 2 (pk(X|z·k)) should restrict to data assigned to component k.  
- Originality: meaningful technical contribution by applying FAB to LFMs, but somewhat incremental since it extends existing FAB framework rather than introducing fundamentally new theory.  
- Significance: offers notable computational advantages via an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties instead of costly sampling/variational methods.  
- Empirical caveat: improvements in predictive likelihood may stem from model-selection criterion, implementation differences, or local optima; Gibbs sampler’s tendency to learn too many components suggests optimizer issues.  
- Strengths summarized: rigorous asymptotic analysis, automatic state selection, identifiability, computational efficiency, and clear experimental gains.  
- Weaknesses summarized: mathematical imprecision in key equations, unclear asymptotic assumptions about K, potential conflation of criterion vs optimization effects, and incremental novelty.  
- Overall: valuable extension of FAB to LFMs with promising empirical results, but requires clearer mathematical precision and justification of asymptotic assumptions.
