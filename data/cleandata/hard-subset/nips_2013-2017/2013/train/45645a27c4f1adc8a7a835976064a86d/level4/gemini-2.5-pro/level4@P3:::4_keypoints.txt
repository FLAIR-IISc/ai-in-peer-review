- Extends the FAB framework to Latent Feature Models by asymptotic analysis of the Hessian, showing the FIC for LFMs matches the MM form and enabling a FAB/LFM algorithm for automatic feature selection.  
- Introduces a CCCP-inspired "accelerateShrinkage" procedure and a post-processing merge step to handle parameter identifiability and prune redundant features.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show FAB/LFM is competitive or superior to IBP-based baselines (fast Gibbs, VB, MEIBP) in predictive log-likelihood, model compactness, and runtime.  
- Theoretical contribution is non-trivial and may allow FAB to be applied to broader classes of latent-variable models with shared parameters.  
- Practical algorithmic refinements (CCCP interpretation and early pruning) yield significant speed-ups and make the method more usable in practice.  
- The paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and logically organized for readers familiar with Bayesian latent-variable models.  
- Scope of originality is somewhat incremental—primarily an extension of existing FAB work—and the paper would benefit from discussion on applying the Hessian analysis to other models that violate block-diagonality.  
- Experimental setup concern: baselines used a fixed noise variance while FAB/LFM appears to learn it, which could unfairly advantage the proposed method; baselines should be allowed to learn noise variance for a fair comparison.  
- Limitation of the asymptotic approximation: FIC relies on N → ∞ and can be inaccurate for small datasets (as observed on Libras); more guidance on when the asymptotic regime is reliable would help practitioners.  
- Overall: technically sound, strong empirical support, practical improvements, minor weaknesses do not outweigh merits—recommend acceptance.
