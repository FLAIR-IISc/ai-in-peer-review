- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- FAB's prior limitation: requires block-diagonal Hessian of the complete likelihood, which LFMs do not satisfy.  
- Authors derive a new lower bound for the factorized information criterion (FIC) that applies to LFMs.  
- The derived lower bound shares the same representation as the FIC for mixture models (MMs).  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets compare FAB/LFM to fast Gibbs (IBP), variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP.  
- Results show FAB/LFM outperforms competitors in prediction accuracy and computational cost.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough derivation steps.  
- The extension is incremental but interesting and demonstrates convincing efficiency.  
- Reviewer views the work as of significant interest to the NIPS community.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
