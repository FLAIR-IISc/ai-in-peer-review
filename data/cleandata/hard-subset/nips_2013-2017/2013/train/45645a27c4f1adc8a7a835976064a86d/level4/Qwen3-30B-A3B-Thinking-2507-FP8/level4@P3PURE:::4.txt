

This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB is a recently developed model selection method that achieves excellent results for mixture models (MMs) and hidden Markov models, maximizing a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood. The limitation of FAB is that it has only been applicable to models satisfying the condition that the Hessian matrix of the complete likelihood is block diagonal. The authors successfully extend FAB to LFMs, despite this condition not being satisfied for LFMs. They derive a lower bound for the FIC applicable to LFMs and demonstrate that it shares the same representation as the FIC for MMs. The authors evaluate their method on both synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing FAB/LFM against alternative approaches including fast Gibbs sampling under the Indian Buffet Process, variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). Results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM, with the proposed method achieving better performance not only in prediction tasks but also in computational efficiency. The paper is well-written, with thorough and clear descriptions of the derivation steps. The extension of FAB from MMs to LFMs represents an incremental but interesting contribution, and the results convincingly underscore its efficiency. While I am not an expert in this specific area, I believe the work would be of significant interest to the NIPS community. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a novel and compelling idea, with convincing results that warrant acceptance.
