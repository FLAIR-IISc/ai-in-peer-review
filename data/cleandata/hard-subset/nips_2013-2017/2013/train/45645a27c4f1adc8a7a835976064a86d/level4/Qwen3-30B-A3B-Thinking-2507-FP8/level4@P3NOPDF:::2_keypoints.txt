- Introduces a novel algorithm extending FAB/FIC to latent feature models using mean-field latent inference and accelerated shrinkage for automatic feature-number selection.  
- Achieves substantial computational speedups over baselines, validated on synthetic and real-world datasets with rigorous ablation studies.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and statistically sound, but lacks comparison to recent LFM-specific selection methods.  
- The paper is exceptionally clear, well-structured, and provides accessible mathematical derivations and informative figures.  
- The synthesis of mean-field inference, accelerated shrinkage, and FIC is a novel, practically valuable contribution for scalable model selection.  
- The method reduces manual hyperparameter tuning, increasing deployability of LFMs in large-scale applications.  
- Request to analyze failure modes: performance under high feature correlation, extreme sparsity, or violations of the mean-field assumption.  
- Recommend direct empirical comparison to newer LFM selection approaches (e.g., variational ARD-style methods).  
- Ask for explicit asymptotic computational complexity comparisons (e.g., O(n^2) vs O(n^3)) to clarify scalability trade-offs.  
- Request implementation details and a hyperparameter sensitivity analysis to substantiate the “minimal tuning” claim and aid reproducibility.  
- Suggest a concise theoretical discussion justifying the applicability of FIC to LFMs beyond the mixture-model analogy.  
- Overall: strong submission with minor, addressable revisions recommended; reviewer recommendation: accept.
