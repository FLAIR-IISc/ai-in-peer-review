In this paper, the authors extend the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) estimation to Hidden Feature Models. FAB is a recently developed model selection method that yields genuinely good results for mixture models (MMs) and Hidden Markov models. In brief, this method maximizes the lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) that converges to the limiting log-probability. The limitation of FAB is that it only applies to models that meet the condition that the Hessian matrix of a complete likelihood must be block diagonal. The authors extend FAB to Hidden Feature Models (LFMs), despite the fact that the condition is not met. They effectively derive the lower bound of FIC for LFMs and show that its representation is similar to that of FIC for MMs. They provide results on both synthetic and real-world datasets and compare FAB/LFM to other methods, such as the Indian buffet process, fast Gibbs sampling, variational Bayes (VB), and models using maximum-expected IBP (MEIBP). The results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM; the proposed method claimed better performance not only in the prediction task but also in terms of computational cost.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have described the derivation steps of their method well. The proposed model extends FAB/MMs to FAB/LFMs and will be considered incremental. It is quite interesting, and the results underline its efficiency. Although I am not an expert on this, I think it will be of great interest to the NIPS community. Overall, it is a good paper that presents a truly interesting idea. The results are reassuring, and I support its acceptance.
