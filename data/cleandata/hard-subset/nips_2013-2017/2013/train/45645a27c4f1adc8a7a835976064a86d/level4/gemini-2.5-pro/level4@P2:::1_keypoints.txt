- Computational efficiency: EM-like, penalized formulation yields a scalable algorithm much faster than MCMC/standard variational IBP.  
- Well-motivated approach: Novel asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1) extends FAB inference to LFMs and relates the LFM complexity term to mixture models.  
- Strong empirical performance: Consistently high predictive log-likelihoods and more compact models with smaller train–test gaps across real datasets.  
- Algorithmic refinements: Shrinkage acceleration (CCCP view) and identifiability-based post-processing reduce runtime and merge redundant features.  
- Asymptotic analysis clarity: Theorem 2 does not specify the asymptotic regime—unclear how K scales with N; derivation appears to assume fixed K, contradicting IBP motivations.  
- Running-time comparison ambiguity: Speed claims lack standardized stopping criteria for competing methods (e.g., IBP Gibbs sampler), so wall-clock comparisons may be misleading.  
- Source of performance gains unclear: Hard to disentangle whether improvements come from the FIC model-selection criterion or from superior optimization/initialization avoiding poor local optima.  
- FIC formulation edge case: The term log(sum_n z_nk) is undefined when a feature is unused (sum_n z_nk = 0); needs clarification to avoid instability.  
- Minor: Equation (4) seems to be missing an E_q expectation on the left-hand side.  
- Minor: Notation p_k(X|z_{·,k}) is confusing—implies component likelihood over full dataset rather than assigned subset.  
- Summary: Valuable, computationally efficient extension of FAB to LFMs with strong empirical results but somewhat incremental novelty and insufficiently precise theoretical and experimental claims.
