This paper proposes a novel model selection criterion for binary latent feature models, which is an extension of the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework. The proposed approach is similar to variational Bayes, but instead of assuming a factorized posterior over latent variables and parameters, it approximately integrates out the parameters using the Bayesian information criterion (BIC). The authors demonstrate improved held-out likelihood scores compared to several existing Indian Buffet Process (IBP) implementations.

The proposed approach seems like a reasonable thing to do, and is motivated by a plausible asymptotic argument. The main advantage relative to other methods for IBP inference is that computationally, it corresponds to an EM algorithm with some additional complexity penalties, rather than the more expensive sampling or variational Bayes algorithms.

The technical contributions seem novel but incremental, as they are essentially an extension of the FAB work of [5] and [6]. However, some parts of the exposition could be improved for clarity. For example, in equation (3), the log sum of the z values is infinite if all of the values are zero. Since this has nonzero probability under any nondegenerate variational distribution q, it would be helpful to discuss why the FIC score is not always infinite.

Theorem 2 states that the marginal likelihood "can be asymptotically approximated as" something, but it would be beneficial to specify the asymptotic regime and assumptions. In particular, how is K assumed to behave as N gets larger? Since the BIC is applied to each component individually, the theorem seems to require a fixed finite K, so that the number of activations of every feature would approach infinity. Under the IBP model, the number of components is infinite, and new components would continue to be observed as the amount of data increases, so assuming finite K removes one of the motivations for using the IBP.

Some minor points: in section 2, it would be more accurate to state that pk(X | z·k) includes only the data points assigned to mixture component k, rather than the entire dataset. In equation (4), there should be an expectation on the left-hand side.

The quantitative results in Table 1 show that the proposed method achieves higher predictive likelihood scores in less time compared to alternative methods. While FAB finishes faster by orders of magnitude in some cases, it would be helpful to specify the stopping criterion to facilitate interpretation of this result. The improvements in predictive likelihood are significant, but it would be more convincing to provide evidence that the difference is due to the model selection criterion rather than which algorithms get stuck in worse local optima.

Overall, the proposed approach seems to make sense, but is somewhat incremental. Some of the math could be clarified, and more analysis would be beneficial to interpret the experimental results.

Strengths:

* The proposed approach is motivated by a plausible asymptotic argument.
* The method is computationally efficient, corresponding to an EM algorithm with additional complexity penalties.
* The authors demonstrate improved held-out likelihood scores compared to several existing IBP implementations.

Weaknesses:

* The technical contributions seem incremental, building on previous FAB work.
* Some parts of the exposition could be improved for clarity.
* The asymptotic regime and assumptions in Theorem 2 are not clearly specified.
* The improvements in predictive likelihood may be due to differences in local optima rather than the model selection criterion.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The proposed approach is a reasonable extension of the FAB framework.
* The method is computationally efficient and demonstrates improved held-out likelihood scores.

Arguments for rejection:

* The technical contributions seem incremental rather than novel.
* Some parts of the exposition could be improved for clarity.
* The asymptotic regime and assumptions in Theorem 2 are not clearly specified.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above.
