- Extends FAB inference from mixture/HMM settings to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and Gaussian observations.  
- Key technical result: asymptotic analysis shows log det F(d) yields D/2 sum_k log sum_n z_nk (up to Op(1)), matching FIC form for mixture models.  
- This FIC gives the same model-induced regularization ∝ sum_k log(sum_n z_nk), enabling automatic feature shrinkage and identifiability without priors.  
- Derives a practical FAB algorithm for LFMs using a mean-field q(Z) and a CCCP interpretation of the E-step.  
- Introduces a CCCP-motivated shrinkage-acceleration routine and a merge post-processing step based on identifiability analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation vs IBP (fast Gibbs), VB-IBP, and MEIBP on synthetic, blocks, and real datasets shows strong model selection, predictive performance, and competitive or improved efficiency.  
- Positions work relative to prior FAB for MMs/HMMs, IBP-family methods, and MAP-asymptotic approaches (MAD-Bayes).  
- CCCP interpretation links to convex-concave procedures and mean-field variational updates, providing algorithmic insight.  
- Core Lemma/Theorem appears well-motivated; formal proofs are provided in the supplement.  
- FAB lower bound derivation follows established methodology with an added mean-field approximation for tractability.  
- CCCP-based acceleration yields a principled algorithm with monotonic bound increase and natural stopping criterion.  
- Experiments show consistent gains in selecting smaller/more accurate K, better predictive log-likelihood, and reduced computational cost in many settings.  
- Caveat: some baselines fix noise variance while FAB learns it, potentially biasing likelihood comparisons.  
- Caveat: MEIBP settings were sensitive and required special initialization; variance across runs is not reported.  
- Caveat: asymptotic approximation may be less accurate at small N (acknowledged).  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized; progression from theory to algorithm to refinements is easy to follow.  
- Suggest clarifying Lemma 1 assumptions in the main text, and reporting per-iteration complexity with/without acceleration.  
- Suggest control experiments for fairness (e.g., FAB with fixed noise variance) and reporting reconstruction errors (RMSE/MAE).  
- Suggest ablations on sensitivity to initialization and hyperparameters, scalability analysis, and variance across seeds/statistical significance.  
- Originality: nontrivial extension showing FIC for LFMs matches MMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessian; practical CCCP acceleration and merge heuristic are valuable.  
- Significance: automatic feature selection/identifiability without priors and a monotonic objective is practically useful for LFMs.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid theoretical extension, principled monotonic algorithm, strong empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Cons against acceptance: some experimental choices may favor FAB, asymptotic claims less persuasive at small N, and incremental for readers already familiar with FAB.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — substantive, well-supported extension of FAB to LFMs with useful algorithmic and empirical contributions.
