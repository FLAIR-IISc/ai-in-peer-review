- Proposes a novel BIC-based model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models, yielding an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties.  
- Approach is reasonable and motivated by a plausible asymptotic argument.  
- Authors report improved held-out/predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, essentially extending prior FAB work.  
- Mathematical exposition is imprecise in places (e.g., eq. (3) log-sum of z may be infinite), raising concerns about the Factorized Information Criterion.  
- Theorem 2 omits specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, especially how K scales with N; BIC approximation appears to assume fixed finite K, conflicting with IBP’s infinite-K motivation.  
- Experimental comparisons (Table 1) show faster convergence for FAB but stopping criteria are unspecified, making runtime and truncation comparisons arbitrary.  
- It is unclear whether predictive gains derive from the model-selection criterion itself or from algorithmic avoidance of poor local optima; Gibbs may be learning too many components due to implementation/local-optima issues.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally, provided authors fix imprecise math, clarify stopping criteria, and strengthen the experimental analysis and comparisons.
