- Proposes generalizing FAB inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC) to latent feature models using mean-field inference and accelerated feature shrinkage.  
- Combines several approximations into a novel, effective approach that is a logical extension of prior FAB/FIC work.  
- Demonstrates automatic model selection that eliminates extensive hand-tuning by automatically selecting the number of latent features.  
- Shows significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets, often by orders of magnitude.  
- Provides a thorough empirical evaluation against state-of-the-art baselines (IBP, variational Bayes, ME-IBP).  
- Presents clear, well-structured exposition with detailed derivation of FIC for LFMs.  
- Analyzes method properties, including claimed robustness to overfitting and accelerated feature shrinkage behavior.  
- Novelty lies in the unique combination of FAB, mean-field approximation, and accelerated shrinkage applied to LFMs.  
- Significant contribution: addresses a difficult problem more effectively than prior work and offers practical scalability.  
- Major weakness: lacks clear analysis of when and how the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) may fail.  
- Missing comprehensive discussion of limitations and potential failure modes and limits of the approximations.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding a more comprehensive analysis of limitations, failure modes, and robustness.
