- Extends FAB/FIC from mixtures/HMMs to binary latent feature models with linear-Gaussian likelihood via asymptotic Hessian analysis.
- Shows LFMs yield the same FIC penalty structure: global O(DK log N) and per-feature log sum_n z_nk terms.
- Derives a variational lower bound for the FIC objective and optimizes it with a mean-field approximation over Z.
- Proposes fixed-point E-step updates including a model-induced shrinkage term −D/(2N π̃_k).
- Interprets optimization via CCCP and introduces accelerateShrinkage (inner CCCP steps) to prune irrelevant features early.
- Proves an identifiability property encouraging balanced activation of duplicate features and adds a merge post-processing heuristic.
- Empirically compares FAB/LFM to Gibbs IBP, variational IBP, MEIBP, and EM on synthetic and real datasets.
- Reports competitive or superior model selection and predictive performance with substantially reduced compute and little tuning.
- Clear, well-structured paper with precise derivations and readable algorithmic details, especially the CCCP view.
- The lemma establishing the log sum_n z_nk term is insightful and broadens FIC applicability.
- Automatic model selection is largely parameter-free, as shrinkage is induced by the model rather than priors.
- CCCP-based acceleration yields large computational savings without harming final solution quality.
- Broad and fair-seeming empirical evaluation covering synthetic recovery, diverse real datasets, and major baselines.
- Identifiability analysis provides useful theoretical justification for the merge heuristic.
- Combined approximations (asymptotic FIC, mean-field, log-count linearization, CCCP acceleration) are stacked and their failure modes are not fully characterized.
- Lemma 1’s assumptions are deferred to the supplement, leaving limited intuition for regimes where they break (small N, collinearity, near-singular Λ, imbalanced activations).
- Failure modes (small-N, extreme sparsity/density, misspecified noise, outliers, overlapping features) are only briefly acknowledged.
- Experimental choices may affect fairness: FAB learns noise precision while some baselines fix it; VB omitted on real data for runtime; MEIBP required parameter tuning and special initialization.
- Sensitivity to initialization (K, Tshrink) and merge thresholds is not reported.
- Derivations and monotonic lower-bound improvements are plausible and align with past FAB work, but proof assumptions should be more visible.
- Recommend additional ablations (no acceleration, no merge, no mean-field, fixed vs. learned noise) and sensitivity analyses.
- Suggest stress tests: small-N vs large-N, correlated/duplicate features, π near 0/1, non-diagonal/heavy-tailed noise, missing-not-at-random.
- Ask authors to make Lemma assumptions explicit in the main text and discuss when FIC may be inaccurate.
- Recommend ensuring experimental fairness (align noise treatment) and releasing code/scripts for reproducibility.
- Reasons to accept: meaningful extension of FAB/FIC, solid theory, practical algorithms, automation, and efficiency with consistent empirical benefits.
- Reasons to reject: insufficient analysis of approximation limits and some evaluation choices that could bias comparisons.
- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on strengthening discussion and experiments around approximation limits and ablations.
