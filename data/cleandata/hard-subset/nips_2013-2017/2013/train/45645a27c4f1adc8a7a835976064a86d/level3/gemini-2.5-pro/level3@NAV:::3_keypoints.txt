- Paper proposes an EM-based inference algorithm for binary latent feature models using Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to automatically infer K.
- Core novelty is applying a FAB-derived shrinkage criterion as a model-selection penalty to LFMs, offering a scalable alternative to IBP-based nonparametric methods.
- Extends FAB methods (previously for mixtures/HMMs) to the more complex dependency structure of LFMs.
- Strength: technically sound and a novel, valuable contribution to efficient LFM inference with simultaneous model complexity selection.
- Strength: algorithm claims scalability to large datasets and shows promising experimental runtime and quality compared to IBP-based methods.
- Major flaw: experimental evaluation contains critical mistakes that invalidate some comparisons and reduce confidence in results.
- Specific flaw: MEIBP baseline was inappropriately applied to data containing negative values.
- Specific flaw: IBP Gibbs baseline was not given the known noise variance in a key experiment, biasing comparisons.
- Specific flaw: variational baseline lacks essential methodological and implementation details for reproducibility.
- Missing analysis: paper fails to discuss algorithm limitations such as possible EM convergence issues, restriction to binary LFMs, and asymptotic assumptions failing in small-data regimes.
- Related work is incomplete—omits recent small-variance asymptotics work for nonparametric LFMs (e.g., MAD-Bayes, ICML 2013).
- Citation error: reference [21] is incorrectly described as Gibbs sampling instead of MAP inference.
- Recommendation: not ready for acceptance until authors correct experimental errors, provide reproducible baseline details, expand related work and limitation discussion.
