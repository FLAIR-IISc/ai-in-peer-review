## Review of “Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models”

This paper proposes a novel extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors address the limitation of prior FAB implementations requiring a specific block-diagonal Hessian condition, demonstrating that this condition holds for LFMs through asymptotic analysis. This allows for the application of a BIC-based model selection criterion, resulting in an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties. The paper claims improved performance in terms of predictive likelihood and runtime compared to existing IBP implementations.

The core idea is sound and builds logically upon previous FAB work (Fujimaki et al., [6]). The motivation for extending FAB to LFMs is clear, given the computational challenges associated with traditional IBP methods. The theoretical analysis, while aiming to justify the approach, reveals some imprecision. Specifically, Equation (3) raises concerns about the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) potentially being infinite, and the asymptotic regime specified in Theorem 2 lacks sufficient detail, particularly regarding the scaling of K (number of latent features) with N (sample size). The reliance on a fixed finite K in the BIC approximation seems at odds with the infinite-K motivation of IBP.

Several minor technical issues detract from the clarity of the presentation. The conditioning in section 2’s pk(X | z·,k) is incorrect, and Equation (4) is missing an expectation term. The experimental evaluation, while demonstrating faster convergence for FAB, suffers from a lack of specified stopping criteria, making runtime comparisons somewhat arbitrary. Furthermore, it’s unclear whether the observed predictive improvements stem from the model selection criterion itself or from the algorithm avoiding suboptimal local optima, a common issue with Gibbs sampling. The tendency of Gibbs sampling to learn too many components suggests potential implementation or local-optima related problems.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of FAB inference to a new model class (LFMs).
*   Reasonable theoretical motivation based on asymptotic analysis.
*   Empirically demonstrates improved performance in some cases.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Imprecise mathematical exposition and missing details in the asymptotic analysis.
*   Insufficient experimental analysis to definitively attribute performance gains.
*   Incremental contribution building directly on prior FAB work.
*   Minor technical errors in the presentation.



**Overall:** This paper presents a sensible, though somewhat incremental, method. The mathematical foundations require further refinement, and a more thorough experimental analysis is needed to convincingly demonstrate the benefits of the proposed approach. I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to address the identified weaknesses before resubmission. The authors should clarify the asymptotic assumptions, correct the technical errors, and provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results.
