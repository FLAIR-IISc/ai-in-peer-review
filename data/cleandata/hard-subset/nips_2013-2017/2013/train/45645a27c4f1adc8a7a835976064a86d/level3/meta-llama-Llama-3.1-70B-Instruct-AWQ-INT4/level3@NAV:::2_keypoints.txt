- Proposes a novel extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models using mean-field inference and accelerated feature shrinkage.  
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets while preserving model selection accuracy and predictive performance.  
- Well-written and clearly structured, making technical details and arguments easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough empirical evaluation against a wide range of competing methods, including state-of-the-art IBP approaches.  
- References and related-work discussion are sufficient and appropriately contextualize the contribution.  
- Method is a logical, solid extension of prior FAB work that combines several approximations into an effective approach.  
- Use of mean-field inference and accelerated feature shrinkage enables more automatic model selection with little hand-tuning.  
- Major concern: unclear when or how the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) may fail; failure modes and limits are not examined.  
- Technically sound: theoretical analysis and experimental results support the authors’ claims.  
- Clarity is excellent: concise explanations and well-organized presentation accessible to readers familiar with Bayesian inference.  
- Originality is high: a new combination of techniques and a clear difference from prior work.  
- Significance is high: addresses a difficult problem and improves on previous approaches for model selection in LFMs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/effective method, thorough experiments, clear writing, and logical extension of FAB.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of failure modes and limitations, and limited discussion on potential improvements.
