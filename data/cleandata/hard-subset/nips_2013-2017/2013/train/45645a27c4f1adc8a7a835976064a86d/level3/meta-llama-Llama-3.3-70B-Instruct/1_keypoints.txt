- Proposes "decision jungles": ensembles of rooted decision DAGs as an alternative to decision trees.  
- Motivates approach by noting traditional trees grow exponentially with depth, problematic for memory-constrained hardware.  
- Method jointly optimizes features and DAG structure using a local-search algorithm to minimize an objective.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of the approach, optimization algorithms, and experimental setup.  
- Empirical results show decision jungles can achieve higher predictive accuracy and lower memory consumption than traditional decision forests and variants.  
- Technical contributions are novel and significant: DAGs allow more flexible, efficient modeling and joint feature/structure optimization is a key innovation.  
- Needs more detailed experimental analysis, including statistical significance of improvements and broader comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Lacks insight into trade-offs between different optimization algorithms and hyperparameter choices.  
- Minor issues: mathematical precision (e.g., add expectations in Eq. 4) and clarify the Gibbs sampler stopping criterion.  
- Requests more details on computational resources for training/testing and discussion of real-world application scenarios.  
- Overall assessment: promising, significant contribution with effective results on challenging classification tasks, but some analyses and clarifications are needed.
