- Presents a novel extension of the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework to latent feature models (LFMs) via an asymptotic Hessian analysis.
- Shows the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models, enabling FAB-style model selection.
- Develops a FAB/LFM algorithm with automatic hidden-state selection and improved parameter identifiability.
- Relates the method to prior FAB, mixture-model, and LFM work and contrasts it with nonparametric Bayesian and variational-Bayesian approaches.
- Paper is well-organized with clear notation.
- Technical derivations and algorithmic details appear sound and correctly presented.
- Experimental comparisons include Gibbs sampling, variational EM, and MEIBP, claiming better runtime and inference quality on some large datasets.
- Experimental details are insufficiently described (missing parameter initialization, convergence criteria, and number of restarts for VB).
- Lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations, cases where it may underperform vs. Gibbs sampling, and potential convergence difficulties of the EM-like procedure.
- Method is restricted to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA; extensions are not discussed.
- Experimental design has flaws (MEIBP misapplied when W is not non-negative; VB-IBP used in a finite variational form with unspecified restarts).
- Does not analyze behavior in small-data regimes where asymptotic arguments may break down.
- Some recent related work on efficient inference for LFMs is missing from the discussion.
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension of FAB to LFMs, correct algorithmic development, and promising experimental performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing discussion of limitations and convergence, insufficient/flawed experimental evaluation, and limited applicability to other latent-variable models.
