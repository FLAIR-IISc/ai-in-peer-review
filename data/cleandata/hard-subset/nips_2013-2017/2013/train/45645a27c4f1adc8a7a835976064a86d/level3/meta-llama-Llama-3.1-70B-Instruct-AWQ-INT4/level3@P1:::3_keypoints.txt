- Novel extension of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Derivation of a new factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs, analogous to the mixture-model FIC despite violated original FAB conditions.  
- Proposed FAB/LFM algorithm offers automatic hidden-state (latent feature) selection.  
- Algorithm ensures parameter identifiability for the model.  
- Iterative optimization yields monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound.  
- Presents a CCCP interpretation of FAB optimization that accelerates the algorithm.  
- Scales to large datasets and can automatically infer the number of latent features.  
- Experiments show competitive performance versus Gibbs sampling, variational Bayes, and MEIBP.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear and concise problem introduction and sound technical derivations.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough and has design/presentation flaws.  
- MEIBP baseline was inappropriately used; IBP Gibbs should have been given known noise in the block images experiment.  
- Behavior in small-data regimes is not explored or discussed.  
- Lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations, cases where it may underperform relative to Gibbs sampling, and potential convergence difficulties of the EM-like procedure.  
- Method is restricted to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Missing citations/discussion of recent efficient-inference work for LFMs, including MAD-Bayes and other small-variance asymptotic approaches.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective method, scales well, automatic feature inference, CCCP-based acceleration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak/Flawed experimental evaluation, missing discussion of limitations and convergence, limited applicability to binary LFMs.
