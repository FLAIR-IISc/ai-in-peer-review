- Extends FAB inference to Latent Feature Models, removing the block-diagonal Hessian requirement and showing the FIC for LFMs matches that for mixture models.  
- Provides key theoretical results (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) enabling FAB application to LFMs and analyzes parameter identifiability (Theorem 4).  
- Presents rigorous derivations and a logical CCCP interpretation with a practical shrinkage acceleration technique.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized with consistent notation and clear mathematical exposition.  
- Novelty: a non-trivial extension of FAB to a broader class of models and an original acceleration method.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show superior predictive performance and computational efficiency vs Gibbs sampling, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Highlights automated model selection and improved parameter identifiability as valuable features.  
- Claims significant practical impact due to speedups and scalability improvements for LFMs.  
- Contribution may be viewed as incremental since it extends existing FAB framework rather than introducing a wholly new approach.  
- Missing analysis of parameter sensitivity beyond δ, especially for shrinkage parameters (Tshrink) and initialization choices.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to synthetic and relatively small real datasets; needs experiments on larger, more complex datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, support for acceptance; suggest minor revisions to address parameter sensitivity and broaden evaluation.
