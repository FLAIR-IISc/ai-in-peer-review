- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), removing a prior restriction on Hessian block-diagonality.  
- Shows LFMs admit a similar factorized information criterion (FIC), enabling FAB application.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical justification (Lemma 1 and Theorem 2) for applying FAB to LFMs.  
- Broadens FAB’s applicability beyond mixture models to LFMs used in link prediction and dimensionality reduction.  
- Introduces shrinkage acceleration (inspired by CCCP) to improve computational efficiency.  
- Proposes a merging post-processing step that leverages parameter identifiability.  
- Claims automated model selection (latent feature dimensionality) and improved parameter identifiability.  
- Empirical results reportedly outperform IBP-based methods in model selection, prediction, and runtime.  
- Emphasizes parsimony and practical algorithmic improvements for automated selection.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks sufficient detail and contains inconsistencies, undermining fairness of comparisons.  
- Use of MEIBP (assumes non-negative weights) on datasets with negative values is a confounding factor.  
- The claim of accelerated Gibbs finding ~70 features on Sonar is questionable and may indicate initialization or noise-variance issues.  
- Reference [21] is inaccurately cited and needs correction.  
- Paper does not adequately discuss convergence difficulties of the EM-like optimization and other algorithmic limitations (e.g., binary latent features).  
- Omits evaluation in small-data regimes where asymptotic assumptions may fail.  
- Related work is incomplete; missing recent small-variance asymptotic analyses like MAD-Bayes and others.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension to LFMs, sound theory, practical algorithmic gains, promising empirical performance, addresses automated model selection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: flawed/insufficient experiments, missing discussion of limitations and failure modes, omitted related work, inaccurate references, unaddressed convergence concerns.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical and algorithmic contribution but empirical and exposition weaknesses require addressing.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance contingent on improved experiments, corrected references, inclusion of missing related work, clearer hyperparameter/baseline justification, and discussion of limitations.
