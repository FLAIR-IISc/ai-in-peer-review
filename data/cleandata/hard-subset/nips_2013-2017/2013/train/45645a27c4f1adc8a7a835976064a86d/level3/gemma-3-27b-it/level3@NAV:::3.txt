## Review of “Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models”

This paper presents a Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference algorithm tailored for binary Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors extend the applicability of FAB, previously limited by Hessian matrix conditions, to LFMs by demonstrating that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs shares a similar form to that of mixture models. This allows for automatic latent feature selection and parameter identifiability without relying on priors, offering a compelling alternative to traditional nonparametric Bayesian methods like IBP. The core contribution lies in applying and adapting FAB inference to the LFM context, enabling efficient inference and automatic dimensionality estimation – a significant challenge in this domain.

The work is technically sound, building on existing FAB theory and providing a clear derivation of the FIC for LFMs. The proposed shrinkage acceleration method and discussion of parameter identifiability add further depth.  Empirical results demonstrate that FAB outperforms Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP methods on certain datasets in terms of both runtime and inference quality. The method's scalability and automated K-selection are also positive attributes, particularly appealing for large-scale data analysis. The novelty of applying FAB to LFMs, extending it beyond mixtures and HMMs, is commendable.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. While the technical approach is robust, the experimental evaluation is insufficient. Details regarding experimental setup, hyperparameter tuning, and baseline configurations are lacking, hindering reproducibility and comprehensive assessment. Several issues with the baseline comparisons exist: the MEIBP baseline is improperly used due to its non-negativity constraint, and the IBP Gibbs sampler wasn’t given a fair advantage in the block image experiment. Furthermore, the accelerated Gibbs result on the Sonar dataset appears suspect. 

The authors also fail to adequately discuss limitations. The method’s restriction to binary LFMs and potential convergence issues of the EM-like procedure are not addressed. Missing from the discussion are related works like MAD-Bayes and other recent advancements in efficient LFM inference. Finally, minor errors, such as an incorrect description of a referenced paper and a misstated standard deviation setting, require correction.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel application of FAB to LFMs, addressing an important problem.
*   Sound theoretical basis and clear algorithmic details.
*   Empirical results showing performance gains on some datasets.
*   Scalability and automatic K selection.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Insufficient experimental details and flawed baseline comparisons.
*   Lack of discussion regarding limitations and potential failure cases.
*   Missing related work.
*   Minor errors requiring correction.

**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution with a promising approach. However, significant revisions are needed to address the experimental shortcomings, clarify limitations, and ensure a rigorous and fair evaluation. I recommend **rejection with request for major revisions**, contingent on addressing the points raised above. Specifically, the authors *must* fix reference descriptions, include MAD-Bayes, and substantially improve the experimental evaluation and explanation.
