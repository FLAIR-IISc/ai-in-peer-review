- Extends FAB to latent feature models, deriving a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) and an EM-like algorithm with a complexity penalty for automatic feature selection.
- Key technical contribution (Lemma 1) shows the FIC penalty for LFMs matches that for mixture models despite shared parameters.
- Uses a Laplace approximation to the complete-data likelihood to approximate marginal log-likelihood.
- Connects the modified E-step to the convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and proposes a practical shrinkage acceleration.
- Discusses parameter identifiability and introduces a merge post-processing step.
- Strong empirical results: outperforms several IBP-based methods in predictive likelihood, runtime, and learns more compact models with less overfitting.
- Well-motivated approach: nontrivial extension of FAB to a model class without block-diagonal Hessian, producing an elegant, principled algorithm.
- Useful algorithmic refinements: CCCP view, acceleration technique, and identifiability handling improve practicality.
- Incremental originality: primarily an extension of prior FAB work rather than a fundamentally new inference paradigm.
- Lack of theoretical precision: asymptotic regime and how K may scale with N are not specified; Theorem 2 assumptions need clarification.
- Formal/mathematical issues: imprecise notation and minor errors (e.g., log(sum_n z_nk) when sum is zero, missing expectations, conditioning mistakes).
- Experimental ambiguities: unclear stopping criteria for competitors (esp. MCMC), making runtime comparisons potentially unfair.
- Unclear source of empirical gains: need ablation to determine whether improvements arise from the FIC objective or better optimization/hyperparameter choices.
- Overall: practical and effective method with strong empirical support but requires clearer theory and fairer, more detailed experimental analysis; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
