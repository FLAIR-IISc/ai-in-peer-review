- Proposes extending FAB/FIC to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) despite violated block-diagonal Hessian assumption.  
- Key technical result (Lemma 1) recovers the FIC regularizer including the crucial log sum(z_nk) shrinkage term.  
- Derives a tractable lower bound on the FIC using mean-field approximation and linearization, yielding the FAB/LFM algorithm.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method motivated by a CCCP interpretation to speed feature pruning.  
- Provides an automated model selection procedure for LFMs that requires less hand-tuning than IBP-based methods.  
- Positioned as a computationally efficient alternative to nonparametric Bayesian approaches like the IBP.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and connects effectively to prior work.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing to strong baselines (IBP samplers, VB-IBP, MEIBP) on synthetic and real data, showing better efficiency and compact models.  
- Main weakness: insufficient analysis of failure modes and interaction of multiple approximations (FIC asymptotics, mean-field q(Z), linearized log-sum).  
- Open questions: How performance degrades with small N and when nonparametric priors may be preferable.  
- Open questions: Sensitivity to data violating mean-field assumptions (strong posterior dependencies between features).  
- Suggestion: add targeted experiments or discussion exploring these limitations to guide practitioners.
