This paper proposes a Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference method for latent feature models (LFMs), extending prior FAB work to a setting where the standard condition on the Hessian matrix of the complete log-likelihood does not hold. The authors demonstrate that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has a form similar to that of mixture models, enabling an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties. They report improved predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes compared to several IBP implementations, attributing these gains to the model-selection criterion and avoidance of local optima.

The work builds upon the existing literature on FAB inference [5, 6] and extends it to LFMs [9, 17], a significant area within Bayesian nonparametrics. The authors appropriately position their contribution within the context of related methods like Indian Buffet Processes (IBP) [10, 11] and variational inference [3]. The connection to prior work on MAD-Bayes [1] is acknowledged, highlighting the different asymptotic assumptions.

**Strengths:**

* **Sensible Approach:** The extension of FAB inference to LFMs is a reasonable and well-motivated endeavor. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs, despite not satisfying the initial FAB conditions, is a key contribution.
* **Theoretical Motivation:** The approach is grounded in an asymptotic argument, providing a theoretical basis for the proposed method.
* **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results demonstrate improved predictive performance and faster runtimes compared to several existing methods, including IBP variants.
* **Identifiability Analysis:** The discussion of parameter identifiability and the proposed merge post-processing step are valuable additions, addressing potential issues with non-uniqueness of solutions.
* **Shrinkage Acceleration:** The CCCP interpretation and proposed shrinkage acceleration method demonstrate a thoughtful attempt to improve computational efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Imprecise Mathematical Exposition:** The mathematical exposition suffers from imprecision in places. Specifically, equation (3) raises concerns about the FIC’s validity when all *z* values are zero, potentially leading to an infinite log-sum.
* **Asymptotic Regime Specification:** Theorem 2 lacks a clear specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly regarding how *K* (the number of latent features) scales with *N* (the number of data points). The BIC-based approximation seems to require a fixed, finite *K*, which conflicts with the IBP's inherent motivation of an infinite number of features.
* **Minor Formal Errors:** There are minor formal errors, such as the conditioning in section 2’s *p<sub>k</sub>(X | z·,<sub>k</sub>)*, which should only condition on data assigned to component *k*. Equation (4) is also missing an expectation.
* **Insufficient Experimental Analysis:** While the reported results are promising, the experimental analysis is somewhat insufficient. The stopping criteria for the FAB algorithm are unspecified, making runtime comparisons and the truncation level for the Gibbs sampler (used in IBP comparisons) arbitrary.
* **Attribution of Improvements:** It remains unclear whether the observed predictive improvements stem from the model-selection criterion itself or from the algorithms (FAB/EM) avoiding worse local optima compared to the Gibbs sampler (which might get stuck). The Gibbs sampler's tendency to learn too many components suggests potential implementation issues or difficulties in escaping local optima.
* **Incremental Contribution:** While technically sound, the contribution is incremental. It represents an extension of existing FAB work rather than a fundamentally new approach.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, although the mathematical presentation could be more precise. Claims are supported by experimental results, but further analysis is needed to fully interpret the gains. It presents a complete piece of work, though the experimental section could benefit from more rigorous analysis. The authors are reasonably careful about evaluating the strengths and weaknesses, though the discussion surrounding the source of performance gains could be more nuanced.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. However, the mathematical derivations could be presented more clearly and with greater attention to detail. Providing more information regarding the implementation details and hyperparameter settings would improve reproducibility.

**Originality:** The work combines existing techniques (FAB, EM, LFM) in a novel way and extends the applicability of FAB inference. However, the core ideas are not entirely new, and the contribution is largely an extension of prior work.

**Significance:** The results are potentially significant, particularly if the reported improvements in computational efficiency and predictive performance hold up under more rigorous analysis. The method could be useful for applications involving high-dimensional data and latent feature discovery. However, the significance is somewhat limited by the incremental nature of the contribution.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Presents a reasonable extension of FAB inference to LFMs.
*   Demonstrates empirical improvements over existing methods.
*   Offers a theoretical justification for the proposed approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Mathematical exposition contains imprecisions.
*   Asymptotic assumptions are not fully specified.
*   Experimental analysis lacks sufficient detail and rigor.
*   Incremental contribution compared to existing work.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a sensible and well-motivated method with some imprecise math and insufficient experimental analysis to fully interpret the reported gains. While the paper’s contribution is incremental, it represents a solid addition to the literature on Bayesian inference for latent feature models. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the mathematical imprecisions and providing a more detailed discussion of the experimental setup and results. Specifically, clarifying the asymptotic assumptions, specifying the stopping criteria, and performing a more thorough analysis of the source of performance gains would significantly strengthen the paper.
