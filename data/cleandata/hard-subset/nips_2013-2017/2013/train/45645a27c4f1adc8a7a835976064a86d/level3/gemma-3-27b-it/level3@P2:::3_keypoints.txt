- Contribution: Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), a setting where prior FAB work didn't apply.  
- Theoretical result: Shows the Fisher Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models, justifying FAB's use.  
- Benefits: Enables automatic hidden-state selection, parameter identifiability, and computational efficiency in LFMs.  
- Empirical claim: Demonstrates competitive performance vs IBP and variational methods, notably in runtime and model selection.  
- Novelty/context: Provides a deterministic, computationally cheaper alternative to Bayesian nonparametrics like the IBP, extending FAB beyond mixture models and HMMs.  
- Practical contribution: Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method (inspired by CCCP) that reduces computational time.  
- Additional theory/tools: Discusses parameter identifiability and includes a post-processing merge step to strengthen results.  
- Strengths summary: Technically sound, clearly presented theory, combined theoretical and empirical validation, and clear practical advantages for scaling and feature selection.  
- Weakness — experiments: Experimental details are insufficiently described, making comparisons hard to assess and baseline settings unjustified.  
- Specific flaw: MEIBP was used inappropriately (it requires non-negative weights), invalidating some comparisons.  
- Specific flaw: IBP Gibbs comparison is unfair—IBP was not given known noise, which would level the playing field.  
- Specific flaw: VB-IBP experiments lack key details (e.g., number of restarts).  
- Suspicious result: The claim of ~70 features found by accelerated Gibbs on the Sonar dataset is questionable and needs investigation.  
- Missing related work: Omits discussion of MAD-Bayes (Broderick et al., ICML 2013) and other small-variance asymptotics literature.  
- Incorrect citation: Reference [21] is misdescribed as Gibbs sampling when it is MAP inference.  
- Missing limitations: Paper acknowledges binary-LFM restriction but does not explore implications or extensions to other data types.  
- Missing limitations: Does not discuss potential convergence difficulties of the EM-like procedure or behavior in small-data regimes where asymptotics may fail.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel extension, solid theoretical foundation (FIC equivalence), competitive runtime performance, practical shrinkage acceleration, and relevance to efficient inference/model selection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Inadequate experimental methodology, flawed baseline comparisons, incomplete discussion of limitations, missing related work, and lack of small-data analysis.  
- Recommendation: Conditional acceptance — authors must fix MEIBP use, clarify all parameter settings, add missing related work (e.g., MAD-Bayes), correct reference [21], and more fully discuss limitations, failure modes, and experimental justification.
