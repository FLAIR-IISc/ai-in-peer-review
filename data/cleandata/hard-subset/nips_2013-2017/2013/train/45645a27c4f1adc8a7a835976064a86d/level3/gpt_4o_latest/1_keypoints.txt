- Main contribution: Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models (LFMs) by deriving a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.  
- Complexity term result: Shows the model complexity term for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models, enabling automatic feature selection and parameter identifiability.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Proposes a FAB/LFM algorithm resembling EM with complexity penalties and a shrinkage acceleration mechanism to reduce runtime.  
- Empirical claim: FAB/LFM outperforms state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process (IBP) implementations on predictive likelihood and computational efficiency, producing more compact models.  
- Strength — Novelty: Generalizes FAB inference to a broader, practically important class of models (LFMs).  
- Strength — Computational efficiency: Shrinkage acceleration is a practical contribution that significantly lowers computational cost on large datasets.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Experiments convincingly show advantages over IBP in likelihood and runtime while reducing overfitting.  
- Strength — Identifiability: Provides theoretical insight that FAB resolves parameter non-identifiability in LFMs.  
- Weakness — Incremental: Technical contributions are largely adaptations of prior FAB work rather than fundamentally new concepts.  
- Weakness — Theoretical assumption: Uses a fixed finite number of features K, which conflicts with the infinite-feature IBP framework and raises consistency questions.  
- Weakness — Mathematical clarity: Parts of the exposition (notably equation (3) and Theorem 2) are imprecise and may hinder reproducibility.  
- Weakness — Experimental interpretation: Stopping criteria, sensitivity to local optima, and potential implementation-dependent effects are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance conditional on addressing mathematical exposition issues and clarifying theoretical assumptions.  
- For acceptance: Broadens FAB applicability, shows strong empirical and computational benefits, and offers theoretical identifiability insights.  
- Against acceptance: Contributions are incremental, rest on assumptions that conflict with IBP, and have clarity issues in derivations.
