- Proposes an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) enabling model selection by maximizing a tractable lower bound on a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC).  
- Addresses a prior FAB limitation by proving (Lemma 1) that the log determinant of the Hessian in LFMs decomposes to yield an FIC with the same form as mixture models.  
- Introduces an EM-like algorithm (FAB/LFM) that automatically infers the number of latent features K via a model-induced regularization penalty.  
- Positions FAB/LFM as a scalable, deterministic alternative to nonparametric Bayesian methods like the Indian Buffet Process (IBP).  

Strengths:
- Novel and significant extension of FAB to LFMs, broadening applicability for large-scale latent feature inference.  
- Core technical derivation appears sound and provides the key insight enabling the method.  
- Provides useful analysis including a CCCP interpretation of the E-step and a shrinkage acceleration that improves runtime.  
- Discusses parameter identifiability (Theorem 4) and introduces a practical merge post-processing step.  
- Experiments show competitive or superior predictive performance and often much lower computational cost with smaller inferred K on several real-world datasets.  

Weaknesses — Experimental methodology:
- Inappropriate comparison to MEIBP on datasets with negative values despite MEIBP requiring non-negative data.  
- VB-IBP baseline uses finite truncation; an infinite variational IBP would be a fairer comparator and experiment details (e.g., restarts) are missing.  
- IBP Gibbs sampler was unfairly disadvantaged by not being given the true noise variance in one experiment.  
- Some baseline results appear suspicious (e.g., ~70 features for accelerated Gibbs on Sonar), suggesting poor initialization or parameter settings.  
- Minor factual error: recommended noise SD for real data was misstated (0.25× data SD commonly, not 0.75×).  

Weaknesses — Method limitations and presentation:
- Relies on asymptotic arguments but lacks discussion or experiments on small-data regimes where FIC may be a poor approximation.  
- Does not discuss potential convergence/local-optima issues inherent to EM-like procedures.  
- Scope is limited to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to real-valued latent-variable models (e.g., factor analysis, PPCA); this limitation is not explicitly stated.  
- Missing discussion of related asymptotic/nonparametric work, notably MAD-Bayes (Broderick et al., ICML 2013).  

Recommendations:
- Substantially revise the experimental section to ensure fair, appropriate baseline comparisons and full reproducibility (correct baselines, fair parameter settings, report restarts).  
- Add empirical evaluation or discussion of small-sample behavior and convergence properties.  
- Explicitly state limitations (binary-latent restriction, asymptotic dependence, local optima).  
- Update related work to include contemporary asymptotic analyses for nonparametric models (e.g., MAD-Bayes).
