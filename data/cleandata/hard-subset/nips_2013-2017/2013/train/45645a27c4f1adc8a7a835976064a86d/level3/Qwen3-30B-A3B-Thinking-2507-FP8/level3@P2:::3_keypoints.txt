- The paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) despite LFMs not meeting the original Hessian condition, showing FICs for LFMs match those for mixture models.  
- The theoretical extension is novel, significant, and well-motivated, building on prior FAB work for mixtures and HMMs.  
- FAB/LFM provides automatic selection of the number of latent features K without priors or hand-tuned hyperparameters.  
- The proposed algorithm is theoretically grounded and includes useful analysis on parameter identifiability and merge post-processing.  
- Experiments claim FAB/LFM is more computationally efficient than IBP methods while maintaining competitive model selection and prediction performance.  
- The shrinkage acceleration technique effectively reduces computation by removing irrelevant features early.  
- A major experimental flaw: MEIBP comparisons are invalid on block/artificial data because MEIBP requires non-negative features while those datasets contain negative values.  
- Baseline implementation details are insufficient (e.g., VB-IBP restarts and K selection are unclear), weakening comparative claims.  
- The paper fails to discuss key limitations: applicability is restricted to binary latent feature models (not factor analysis or PPCA).  
- Potential convergence issues of the EM-like optimization procedure are not discussed.  
- Reliance on asymptotic analysis raises concerns about behavior in small-data regimes, which are not empirically explored.  
- Some results appear questionable (e.g., FAB finding ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data), suggesting problems with initialization or hyperparameters.  
- Important related work is omitted, notably MAD-Bayes (small-variance asymptotics for nonparametric LFMs).  
- A cited reference [21] is mischaracterized as Gibbs sampling when it actually performs MAP inference.  
- Pro arguments: novel theoretical contribution, practical automatic model selection, computational advantages, and useful theoretical insights.  
- Con arguments: flawed and under-detailed experimental evaluation, invalid MEIBP comparisons, unacknowledged related work, and insufficient discussion of method limitations.  
- Recommendation: Reject pending substantial revisions — fix MEIBP comparisons, provide detailed baseline implementations, discuss limitations (binary restriction and convergence), include MAD-Bayes and correct references, and investigate suspect results (e.g., Sonar).
