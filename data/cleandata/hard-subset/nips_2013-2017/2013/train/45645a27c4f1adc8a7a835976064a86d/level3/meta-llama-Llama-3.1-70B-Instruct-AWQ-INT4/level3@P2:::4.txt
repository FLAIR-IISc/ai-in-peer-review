This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models and hidden Markov models to latent feature models (LFMs). FAB inference maximizes a computationally tractable lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to a marginal log-likelihood for a large sample limit. The authors address a limitation of previous FAB inference methods, which required a block-diagonal Hessian of the complete likelihood, a condition that LFMs do not satisfy.

The authors derive a lower bound of FIC applicable to LFMs despite the Hessian condition violation. They show that the derived FIC for LFMs has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models. This result indicates the wide applicability of FICs and suggests that FIC representation of approximated marginal log-likelihoods is feasible not only for mixture models but also for more general (discrete) latent variable models.

The paper provides a thorough derivation of the FIC for LFMs, including a lemma that plays a crucial role in the derivation. The authors also propose a shrinkage acceleration method that drastically reduces computational cost in practice. They conduct an empirical evaluation of FAB/LFMs on synthetic and real datasets, comparing their method against fast Gibbs sampling for the Indian Buffet Process (IBP), variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP).

The results indicate superior predictive performance and lower computational cost for FAB/LFMs. The paper is well-written, with thorough derivation steps and a clear explanation of the proposed method. The contribution is somewhat incremental but interesting and relevant to the NIPS community.

Originality: The paper extends FAB inference to LFMs, which is a novel combination of familiar techniques. The authors provide a new perspective on FAB inference and its application to LFMs. The proposed shrinkage acceleration method is also original and contributes to the efficiency of the FAB/LFM algorithm.

Significance: The results are important, and the proposed method has the potential to be widely used in practice. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research, and the authors provide a unique theoretical and pragmatic approach. The paper advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way, and the proposed method provides unique data and conclusions on existing data.
