- Proposes "decision jungles": ensembles of rooted decision DAGs as a compact extension of decision trees.  
- Targets the exponential growth-with-depth limitation of trees to reduce memory use on constrained hardware.  
- Allows multiple root-to-leaf paths, yielding more compact and potentially more discriminative models.  
- Introduces two local-search learning algorithms, LSearch and ClusterSearch; LSearch is simpler and more effective.  
- Provides thorough empirical comparison to competing methods across multiple datasets.  
- Reports reduced memory consumption and improved generalization; node merging appears to regularize the model.  
- Paper is well-written, well-referenced, and positions the approach as a logical extension of prior work.  
- Use of multiple approximations improves efficiency but invites questions about limitations and failure modes.  
- Weakness: limited exploration/discussion of potential risks, limitations, and failure cases.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the mechanism linking node merging to the observed regularization.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novel, efficient method with clear algorithms and strong empirical results; clarity of presentation.  
- Con-accept arguments: lack of deeper analysis on failure cases and the merging→regularization relationship.
