- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), filling a gap in the FAB framework.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves LFMs have the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as mixture models (Theorem 2) despite not having block-diagonal Hessians.  
- Builds naturally on prior FAB work for mixture models and prior LFM literature.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) gives a solid foundation for the FIC derivation.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing to IBP, VB, MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Demonstrates substantial computational efficiency gains and improved model selection accuracy without harming predictive performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized; algorithms and proofs are clearly presented with useful supplement material.  
- Clear explanation of the connection between FAB and CCCP, offering valuable theoretical insight.  
- Original insight that broadens FAB applicability to LFMs; introduces a practical shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2).  
- Significant practical impact: automatic model selection with minimal hyperparameters, valuable for large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure modes and limits of the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage), especially for small samples.  
- Weakness: needs stronger connection and intuition between theoretical results and practical implementation (e.g., why multiple CCCP steps speed shrinkage).  
- Pro acceptance points: novel theoretical extension, rigorous proofs, practical and faster algorithm, comprehensive experiments, clear presentation.  
- Con acceptance points: limited failure-mode analysis, some superiority claims lack detailed statistical significance testing, theoretical-practical links could be clearer.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong theoretical novelty and convincing empirical results; minor limitations do not outweigh the contribution.
