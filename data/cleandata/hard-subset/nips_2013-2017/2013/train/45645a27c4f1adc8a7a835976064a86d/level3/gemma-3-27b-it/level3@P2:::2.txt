This paper presents a significant extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing a limitation of the original FAB framework that required a block diagonal Hessian matrix. The authors demonstrate that LFMs, despite not initially satisfying this condition, exhibit a similar form in their Factorized Information Criterion (FIC), enabling the application of FAB. They further introduce an acceleration method based on a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) and establish parameter identifiability, culminating in an efficient and automated model selection approach.

The core idea – extending FAB to LFMs – is a logical and valuable contribution. Previous work [5, 6] established FAB’s effectiveness with Mixture Models (MMs), and LFMs represent a natural and important generalization. The authors rightly point out the computational bottlenecks of existing LFM inference methods, such as Indian Buffet Processes (IBP) [10, 11] and Variational Bayes (VB) [3], making the potential for a more efficient approach highly relevant. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs (Theorem 2) is a key result, demonstrating the broad applicability of the FIC framework. The addition of the shrinkage acceleration, inspired by the CCCP, and the identifiability analysis further strengthens the contribution. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a rigorous mathematical derivation of the FIC for LFMs, building upon the foundations of previous FAB work. Lemma 1 is central to this extension and appears well justified. The analysis of parameter identifiability (Theorem 4) is a valuable addition.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The authors clearly explain the motivation, methodology, and experimental setup. The notation is consistent, and the key equations are well-explained.
*   **Originality:** The extension of FAB inference to LFMs is a novel contribution. The combination of the FIC derivation, the CCCP-inspired acceleration, and the identifiability analysis offers a unique approach to LFM inference.
*   **Significance:** The proposed method addresses a significant practical challenge – efficient model selection for LFMs. The empirical results demonstrate that FAB/LFMs achieve competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods, while often offering significant computational advantages.  The automatic model selection (dimensionality selection) is a particularly compelling feature.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Approximation Limitations:** While the authors thoroughly present their methodology, a critical discussion of the potential failure modes and limitations of the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) is lacking. Specifically, the impact of the mean-field approximation on the accuracy of the FIC is not fully explored. When might these approximations break down, and what are the consequences? A deeper examination of conditions under which the assumptions hold is needed.
*   **Sensitivity Analysis:** While the authors state that the choice of δ=10<sup>-4</sup> was not sensitive, a more systematic sensitivity analysis of key hyperparameters (e.g., Tshrink) would be beneficial.
*   **Comparison Depth:**  While a wide range of methods are compared, some comparisons seem somewhat cursory. For instance, the choice of K=2,...D for VB seems arbitrarily broad; a more refined hyperparameter search for each method could provide a fairer comparison.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of a promising inference method (FAB) to a practically relevant problem (LFM model selection).
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in terms of both computational efficiency and predictive performance.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the complex methodology.
*   The contribution is significant and likely to be of interest to researchers and practitioners in the field.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of a thorough discussion of the limitations of the approximations employed, particularly the mean-field approximation, is a concern.
*   A more detailed sensitivity analysis of key hyperparameters would strengthen the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent variable models. The authors have successfully extended FAB inference to LFMs, resulting in a computationally efficient and automated model selection method. While the paper would benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the limitations of the approximations used and a more thorough sensitivity analysis, the strengths of the work clearly outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the concerns regarding approximation limitations.  Specifically, the authors should add a section discussing scenarios where the approximations are likely to fail and the potential consequences, as well as a more systematic hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
