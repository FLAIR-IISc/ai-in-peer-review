- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), filling an important gap in FAB applicability.
- Authors show FIC for LFMs has same form as mixtures, enabling FAB to automatically select number of latent features K without priors.
- Presents an EM-based FAB inference algorithm with shrinkage and a shrinkage-acceleration technique; claims better runtime and inference quality than Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP on large datasets.
- Theoretical contributions are sound with Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 supporting the FAB/LFM approach and proofs in the supplement.
- Algorithmic details are correct and the method demonstrates scalability to large datasets.
- Empirical strengths claimed: automatic feature selection via FAB shrinkage and strong performance on large-scale real-world data.
- Major experimental flaws: MEIBP baseline was inappropriately applied to data with negative values (MEIBP requires non-negative W).
- Major experimental flaws: IBP Gibbs baseline was not given the known noise value in block images experiments (used a heuristic instead).
- Major experimental flaws: VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version without reporting critical details (e.g., number of restarts).
- Major experimental flaws: Accelerated Gibbs result finding ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data appears suspicious and likely due to bad initialization.
- Major experimental flaws: Reference [21] is mischaracterized as Gibbs sampling when it performs MAP inference.
- Paper lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations and cases where FAB may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling.
- Paper omits analysis of potential convergence difficulties for the EM-like procedure and behavior in small-data regimes where asymptotics may fail.
- Method is limited to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA as presented.
- Related work is incomplete: missing discussion/citation of MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and other small-variance asymptotic approaches for nonparametric LFMs, plus some recent efficient LFM inference methods.
- Experimental methodology is inadequately reported: insufficient baseline settings, incomplete methodology reporting, and no parameter sensitivity analysis.
- Paper contains an incorrect description of a recommended standard deviation setting (stated as 0.75× std instead of 0.25× std).
- Recommended fixes: correct reference [21], add MAD-Bayes citation/discussion, fix standard deviation description, clarify experimental methodology, address MEIBP baseline/noise-value issues, and discuss limitations/small-data behavior.
- Overall conclusion: technically novel and sound, but serious experimental evaluation flaws undermine claims of superiority and require correction before acceptance.
