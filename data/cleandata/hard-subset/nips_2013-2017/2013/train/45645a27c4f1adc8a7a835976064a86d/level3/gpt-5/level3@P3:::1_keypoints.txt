- Contribution: Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to linear-Gaussian latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features, deriving an FIC with per-feature log-occupancy penalties that enable automatic shrinkage and pruning.

- Technical core: Asymptotic Hessian analysis yields the same FIC structure as FAB for mixture models, including a global O(DK log N) term and per-feature penalties (D/2) log(∑n znk).

- Algorithm: Proposes an EM-like optimization of a tractable lower bound with closed-form M-steps, an E-step interpreted as CCCP, a shrinkage-acceleration heuristic, and a post-hoc merge to handle non-identifiability.

- Empirics: Compares FAB to IBP implementations and variational EM on synthetic and real data, reporting often better held-out likelihoods, smaller K, and faster runtimes.

- Relation to prior work: Builds directly on prior FAB for MMs/HMMs and IBP LFM inference; main novelty is showing FIC structure holds despite parameter sharing across features.

- Strength — motivation: Provides a simple, automatic model-selection mechanism for LFMs without relying on priors or hand-tuning.

- Strength — technical insight: Lemma/Theorem argue that per-feature log-occupancy penalties drive principled shrinkage; CCCP reinterpretation is insightful and practically useful.

- Strength — practicality: EM-like updates, monotone lower-bound ascent, built-in pruning/merging, and a clear stopping criterion increase usability.

- Strength — empirical quality: FAB often achieves competitive or better predictive log-likelihoods with smaller K and lower runtime; qualitatively cleaner features in some studies.

- Strength — asymptotic plausibility: FIC approximation and shrinkage behavior are plausibly justified in large-N regimes.

- Weakness — asymptotic regime unclear: Theorem 2 lacks explicit assumptions about scaling of D and K with N, and whether K may grow with N as in IBP.

- Weakness — empty/full features: FIC contains log counts that are ill-defined when ∑n znk=0 (or N); reliance on ad hoc pruning needs principled domain definition and effects on guarantees.

- Weakness — lower-bound notation: Equation (4) and related lower-bound equations are ambiguous/missing expectations and need clearer notation and specification of conditioning.

- Weakness — Lemma/Theorem assumptions hidden: Key equality log det|F(d)| = ∑k log(∑n znk)/N + Op(1) has assumptions relegated to supplement and needs summarized main-text conditions and failure modes (collinearity, near-zero noise).

- Weakness — Op(1) uniformity: It’s unclear whether the Op(1) remainder is uniform over Z and K, which matters for model comparisons and selection.

- Weakness — attribution of gains unclear: Improvements may stem from FIC-based model selection or from algorithmic advantages (better local-optima avoidance); ablation studies are needed.

- Clarity — writing imprecision: Several notational/typo issues (e.g., Eq. (3)/(4), “mean-filed”); main assumptions for theorems should be moved to the main text.

- Clarity — CCCP vs Laplace: Consolidate derivations and clarify which results are guaranteed (monotonicity) versus heuristic (acceleration, merge).

- Originality: Novel but incremental — extends existing FAB machinery to LFMs with practical refinements; meaningful but builds heavily on prior FAB work.

- Significance: Potentially high practical impact as a simple, fast method for choosing K in LFMs, but theoretical assumptions and controlled experiments need strengthening to establish robustness.

- Experiments — fairness concerns: Stopping criteria, truncations, initial Ks, and fixed noise variances for baselines are not fully specified, complicating runtime and performance comparisons.

- Experiments — variability and stats: Need confidence intervals, multiple seeds, and statistical tests for PLL/TLL/K to assess significance and robustness.

- Experiments — diagnostics and ablations: Should report K vs iteration/time, training vs validation likelihood, and ablations isolating FIC, CCCP acceleration, and merging contributions.

- Questions for authors: Specify the exact asymptotic regime for Theorem 2, whether Op(1) is uniform over Z/K, principled safeguards for ∑n znk extremes, and provide ablations and sensitivity analyses (noise variance, Tshrink, δ).

- Pros summary: Sensible extension of FAB to LFMs, simple and fast algorithm, competitive empirical performance with automatic model selection.

- Cons summary: Insufficiently specified asymptotic assumptions, some mathematical/notation imprecision, and experimental setup lacks controls to fully attribute gains.

- Overall assessment: Promising and practically appealing but incremental; requires clearer assumptions, tightened exposition, and stronger controlled experiments to be fully convincing.
