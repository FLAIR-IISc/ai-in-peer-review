- Main contribution: Extends FAB inference to binary-feature linear-Gaussian LFMs, showing Hessian asymptotics yield an FIC with a D/2 · sum_k log(sum_n z_nk) complexity penalty.
- Algorithmic contribution: Derives an EM-like FAB algorithm using mean-field variational Z, optimizing a tractable lower bound and enabling automatic feature shrinkage.
- E-step interpretation: Presents a CCCP view of the E-step and introduces a shrinkage acceleration technique.
- Identifiability: Analyzes identifiability issues and proposes a merging post-processing heuristic to handle redundant features.
- Empirical claim: Reports better predictive likelihoods, faster runtimes, and more compact models (smaller K) than IBP baselines (Gibbs, VB-IBP, MEIBP) on synthetic and real data.
- Relation to prior work: Direct extension of FAB/FIC from MMs/HMMs; relates to Laplace/BIC approximations, MAD-Bayes asymptotics, and variational/exponentiated-gradient updates.
- Key theoretical lemma: Shows log det Hessian per-dimension scales like sum_k log(sum_n z_nk) up to Op(1), motivating the same FIC form as for mixture models.
- Main theoretical concern: Asymptotic regime is underspecified—unclear whether K is fixed, grows with N, or required growth rates for the approximation to hold.
- Edge-case issue: Treatment of pathological features with sum_n z_nk ∈ {0,1} is handled algorithmically but not formally excluded from the Laplace approximation.
- Precision errors: Minor inaccuracies in the main text (conditioning in pk, missing expectation in Eq.4, domain issues for log-sum terms) need correction and clarification.
- Assumption omission: Regularity conditions (fixed D, eigenvalue bounds, bounded moments, identifiability) are not stated clearly in the main manuscript where used.
- Algorithmic quality: Updates and lower bound are sensible and practical; CCCP and shrinkage acceleration are helpful in practice.
- Merge heuristic caveat: Merge conditions and bounds on loss increase are not formally specified—need tolerances and guarantees for “safe” merging.
- Experimental methodology concern: Stopping criteria, truncations, initializations, and fairness of runtime comparisons are insufficiently standardized and reported.
- Ablation gap: No ablations isolating the D/2 log-sum penalty, CCCP acceleration, merging, or prior-only regularization effects.
- Scope limitation: Method currently limited to linear-Gaussian likelihoods with binary features; generalization to other likelihoods not demonstrated.
- Significance if valid: Offers a practical, hyperparameter-free alternative to IBP for LFMs with automatic K selection and good empirical performance.
- Originality assessment: Novel within FAB literature to extend Hessian analysis to LFMs, but conceptually an incremental generalization of Laplace/FIC-based selection plus EM updates.
- Pros summary: Clear asymptotic motivation, deterministic algorithm with automatic feature selection, identifiability discussion, compact models, and implementable algorithmic details.
- Cons summary: Vague asymptotic assumptions, minor mathematical imprecision, experimental standardization and ablation gaps, and limited scope.
- Suggested improvements: State asymptotic assumptions explicitly, fix derivational inaccuracies, add ablations and matched-K comparisons, standardize stopping criteria, discuss MAD-Bayes/BIC relations, and include sensitivity analyses for K and Tshrink.
- Recommendation: Weak reject—promising and useful but needs stronger theoretical clarity and tighter experimental validation.
