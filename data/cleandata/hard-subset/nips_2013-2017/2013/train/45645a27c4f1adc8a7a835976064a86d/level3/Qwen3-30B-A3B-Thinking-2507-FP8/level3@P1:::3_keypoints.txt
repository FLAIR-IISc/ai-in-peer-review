- Significant theoretical contribution: extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs), filling a gap in FAB applicability.  
- Novel asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) shows LFMs yield FICs with the same form as mixture models despite violating previous Hessian assumptions.  
- Theorem 4 provides parameter identifiability analysis, justifying post-processing (feature merging) to improve compactness.  
- CCCP interpretation of the FAB algorithm leads to a practical shrinkage acceleration method that reduces computation (Algorithm 2, Figure 1).  
- Empirical results claim FAB/LFMs outperform IBP (Gibbs), VB, and MEIBP on model selection, prediction, and runtime across synthetic and real datasets.  
- FAB achieves better predictive log-likelihoods with smaller K and lower computational cost in reported experiments (Figure 2, Table 1).  
- Experimental weaknesses: inappropriate MEIBP baseline comparisons on datasets with negative values (MEIBP requires non-negative W).  
- Experimental weaknesses: questionable noise variance choices for real data (used 0.75× std vs. recommended 0.25×) and heuristic noise settings in block data.  
- Suspicious result: ~70 features inferred on 49-d Sonar data suggests possible issues with initialization or noise variance needing further investigation.  
- Missing evaluation of small-data regimes where asymptotic theory may break down.  
- Related-work issues: omission of MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and incorrect description of reference [21] as Gibbs sampling when it performs MAP inference.  
- Algorithmic concerns under-discussed: potential convergence difficulties of the EM-like procedure and limitations beyond binary LFMs (not applicable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA).  
- Insufficient experimental detail: unclear number of restarts for VB-IBP and initialization procedures.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical novelty and practical ideas, but experimental and related-work shortcomings lead to recommendation for rejection pending major revisions.
