Summary and relation to prior work
This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the associated factorized information criterion (FIC) from mixture and HMM settings to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian likelihoods. The key technical contribution is an asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1) showing that, despite the lack of block-diagonal structure in the complete-data Hessian for LFMs, the log-determinant of per-dimension Hessians contributes the same regularization term as in mixtures: a D/2 sum over features of log usage counts, log(∑n znk). This yields an FIC for LFMs (Eq. 9) of the same form as for mixture models (Eq. 3), implying automatic shrinkage of unused features, asymptotic equivalence to marginal log-likelihood, and tractable lower bounds for optimization (with a mean-field q(Z)). Building on this, the paper derives a FAB algorithm for LFMs with (i) a mean-field E-step update (Eq. 11) that includes the FAB-induced −D/(2Nπ̃k) shrinkage term; (ii) closed-form M-steps (as in PPCA) including π updates (Eq. 12); (iii) a CCCP view (Eq. 13–14) leading to a practical shrinkage-acceleration routine (Algorithm 2); and (iv) a merge post-processing heuristic motivated by an identifiability analysis (Theorem 4).

The work is well-situated within the literature on FIC/FAB [Fujimaki & Morinaga; Fujimaki & Hayashi], singular learning theory [Watanabe], IBP-based LFMs (Gibbs, accelerated Gibbs, VB, MEIBP), and related asymptotics (MAD-Bayes). Empirically, FAB/LFM is compared against IBP sampling, finite VB, MEIBP, and a variational EM baseline, on synthetic (including the “blocks” data) and real datasets (EEG, Piano, YaleB, USPS, etc.), showing strong model selection and predictive performance with lower or comparable runtime.

Strengths
- Clear theoretical insight: Lemma 1 is non-trivial and shows that FIC regularization emerges for LFMs even without block-diagonal Hessians. This meaningfully broadens the scope of FAB/FIC beyond mixture-like models.
- Practical algorithm: The mean-field FAB updates are simple, and the CCCP perspective is insightful. The shrinkage-acceleration is an effective engineering contribution that reduces runtime substantially.
- Automatic model selection: The −(D/2) log usage term induces principled shrinkage independent of priors, in line with the FAB philosophy.
- Identifiability discussion: Theorem 4 clarifies that FAB favors balanced usage among duplicated features and motivates a merge operation; this addresses well-known non-identifiability issues in LFMs.
- Empirical evaluation: Comparisons are broad and fair overall, with competitive or superior predictive log-likelihoods and compact models (smaller K), and strong performance on standard synthetic tests.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Assumptions and failure modes: Lemma 1 relies on “mild assumptions,” and Theorem 2 assumes bounded joint marginal log-likelihood; these are deferred to the supplement. The paper would benefit from enumerating the key conditions in the main text with intuition and guidance on when they break (e.g., near-singular W, extreme sparsity/density of Z, highly collinear features, very small N).
- Multiple approximations: The approach layers FIC asymptotics, a mean-field factorization for q(Z), CCCP-based linearization, shrinkage feature pruning, and merging heuristics. It is not fully clear which of these most affects performance or when they may fail. An ablation study (no CCCP acceleration; no merge; no feature pruning; prior-only regularization) would help.
- Monotonicity and bound tracking: While the E- and M-steps increase the lower bound L, the shrinkage and merge steps are justified heuristically (“increases L” or accept merge if L increases). Please clarify whether the monotonicity guarantee strictly holds with acceleration and post-processing, and report bound traces showing no drops.
- Mean-field limitations: The factorized q(zn) may underestimate posterior correlations among features. A brief discussion or experiment on cases where feature co-occurrence is strong (e.g., structured IBP priors) would be useful.
- Experimental details: Some fairness points need clarification. In several baselines the observation noise variance is fixed (1 or 0.75) per prior code, whereas FAB appears to learn Λ in the M-step; does FAB also fix Λ in those comparisons? Please make this consistent or report results under both settings. Also include per-iteration complexity and memory as a function of N, D, K, and explore sensitivity to initialization (K0 = min(N,D)), δ, and Tshrink.

Quality
- Technical soundness appears solid; however, the core asymptotics (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) and identifiability (Theorem 4) are central and should have proof sketches and explicit assumptions in the main paper. The CCCP link is correct and useful.
- Empirical methodology is largely careful; adding ablations, variability across seeds, and clearer runtime breakdowns would strengthen the case.

Clarity
- The paper is well written and organized, with helpful equations and algorithms. Suggested improvements:
  - Move the key assumptions behind Lemma 1/Theorem 2 into the main text with intuition and examples.
  - Provide a short pseudocode or clearer description of the merge() routine and its complexity.
  - Explicitly state when the bound L is guaranteed to increase (and when not).

Originality
- The extension of FIC/FAB to LFMs via a new Hessian analysis is novel and interesting. The CCCP reinterpretation and shrinkage-acceleration are also fresh and practically meaningful.

Significance
- The result broadens FAB applicability, provides an automated alternative to IBP-based methods, and appears computationally attractive. This should be of interest to both theory and practice communities working on latent feature discovery.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel theoretical insight enabling FAB for LFMs.
- Simple, effective algorithm with strong automatic model selection.
- Clear empirical gains in predictive performance and compactness across diverse datasets.
- Good writing and clear relation to prior work.

Arguments against acceptance
- Core assumptions and potential failure modes are not sufficiently exposed in the main text; reliance on the supplement limits accessibility.
- Limited ablation and sensitivity analyses; unclear contribution of each approximation step.
- Some fairness ambiguities in experimental setup (noise variance learning).

Suggestions for the authors
- Detail the assumptions behind Lemma 1 and provide intuition/diagnostics for when they hold or fail; include a finite-sample discussion.
- Add ablation studies (no CCCP, no merging, no pruning, prior-only regularization) and sensitivity to initialization and Tshrink.
- Clarify bound monotonicity with shrink/merge; report bound trajectories and acceptance criteria.
- Ensure fair comparisons regarding noise variance; report both fixed and learned Λ settings.
- Briefly discuss extensions beyond diagonal Λ and to non-Gaussian likelihoods, and whether the FIC form persists.

Recommendation: Accept (borderline to clear accept). The theoretical extension and practical algorithm are compelling, and the empirical results are strong. Addressing the clarity and ablation concerns would make this contribution even more impactful.

Reviewer confidence: High.
