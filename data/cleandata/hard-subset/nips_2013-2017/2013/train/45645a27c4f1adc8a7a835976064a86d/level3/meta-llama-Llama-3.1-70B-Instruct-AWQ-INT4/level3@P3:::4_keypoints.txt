- Proposes extending the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Derives a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to the marginal log-likelihood for LFMs.  
- Overcomes the previous FAB requirement of a block-diagonal Hessian, which LFMs do not satisfy.  
- Introduces the FAB/LFM algorithm with automatic hidden-state selection.  
- Shows parameter identifiability for the proposed method.  
- Demonstrates monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound through iterative optimization.  
- Paper is well-written and provides a thorough, clear derivation of FIC for LFMs.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets shows superior predictive performance and lower computational cost versus fast Gibbs for IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental but interesting and relevant to the NIPS community.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments adequately support the claims; paper quality is high.  
- Originality is moderate—builds on prior FAB and LFM work but combines them in a novel way.  
- Significance is high due to addressing a difficult problem more effectively than prior research.  
- Arguments for acceptance: thorough derivation, strong empirical results, relevance and organization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution and limited appeal to readers unfamiliar with FAB or LFMs.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication at NIPS.
