- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by showing FICs for LFMs have a form similar to mixture models, enabling FAB application.  
- Introduces a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration technique and provides identifiability results with a principled feature-merging post-processing.  
- Theoretical contributions (e.g., Lemma 1, Theorem 2) are rigorous, well-motivated, and supported by proofs in the supplement.  
- Novelty: significant extension of FAB to a previously excluded class of models and an original acceleration method.  
- Clear exposition and consistent notation make the paper accessible to readers familiar with Bayesian inference.  
- Empirical validation is comprehensive, comparing against strong baselines (IBP, VB, MEIBP) on synthetic and real datasets and demonstrating improvements in speed, model selection, and prediction.  
- Practical significance: provides an automated, computationally efficient solution for model selection in LFMs with potential broader applicability.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of approximation limitations and potential failure modes of combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage).  
- Weakness: lacks a sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (e.g., Tshrink) and baseline parameter choices.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, significant theoretical and empirical contributions, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about the robustness of approximations and missing sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper whose strengths outweigh weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance contingent on addressing approximation analysis and parameter sensitivity.
