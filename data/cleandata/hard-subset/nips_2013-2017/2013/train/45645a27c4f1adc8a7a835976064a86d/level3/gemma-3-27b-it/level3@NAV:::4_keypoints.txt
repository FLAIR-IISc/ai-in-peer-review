- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixtures/HMMs to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by removing the block-diagonal Hessian requirement.  
- Asymptotic analysis shows the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same representation as for mixture models, enabling FAB application.  
- Method provides automatic hidden-state selection and improves parameter identifiability.  
- Introduces a shrinkage-acceleration technique inspired by convex-concave procedure (CCCP).  
- Demonstrates superior predictive performance and computational efficiency compared to Gibbs sampling, Variational Bayes, and MEIBP.  
- Empirical validation is strong, with consistent results on both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Derivations are rigorous and clearly presented, making theoretical contributions accessible.  
- Connects clearly to prior work on FIC and FAB, situating the contribution well.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental—extends existing framework rather than introducing a paradigm shift.  
- Comparisons may depend on specific parameter settings (e.g., IBP α), so sensitivity to those settings should be discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, given technical soundness, practical relevance, clarity, and empirical support.
