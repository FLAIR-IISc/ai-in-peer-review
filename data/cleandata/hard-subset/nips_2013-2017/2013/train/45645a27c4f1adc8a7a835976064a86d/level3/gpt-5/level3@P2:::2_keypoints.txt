- Extends FAB/FIC from mixtures/HMMs to binary latent feature models (LFMs) with linear-Gaussian likelihoods via a new asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1).  
- Shows FIC regularizer for LFMs is same form as mixtures: (D/2) sum_k log usage_k, enabling automatic shrinkage and tractable lower bounds.  
- Derives a practical FAB algorithm for LFMs: mean-field E-step with FAB shrinkage, closed-form M-steps, CCCP reinterpretation, shrinkage-acceleration, and a merge heuristic.  
- Situates work within FIC/FAB, singular learning theory, IBP-LFM literature, and MAD-Bayes asymptotics.  
- Empirically compares against IBP sampling, VB, MEIBP, and variational EM on synthetic and real datasets, showing strong selection, predictive performance, and runtime.  
- Lemma 1 is a significant theoretical contribution, broadening FAB applicability beyond block-diagonal Hessian cases.  
- The algorithm is simple, practically effective, and the CCCP perspective provides useful insight and acceleration.  
- The (−D/2) log usage term yields principled, prior-independent automatic model shrinkage.  
- Theorem 4 and identifiability discussion motivate a merge operation and address feature non-identifiability issues.  
- Empirical evaluation is broad and generally fair, showing competitive predictive log-likelihoods and more compact models.  
- Key assumptions behind Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 are deferred to the supplement; these should be highlighted and their failure modes explained.  
- The method stacks multiple approximations (FIC asymptotics, mean-field q(Z), CCCP linearization, pruning, merging), leaving unclear which components drive performance.  
- No ablation study to isolate effects of CCCP acceleration, merging, pruning, or regularization choice.  
- Monotonicity claims during shrinkage and merge steps are heuristic; need clear guarantees or bound traces to show no decreases.  
- Mean-field factorization may underestimate feature correlations; limited discussion or experiments on strong co-occurrence settings.  
- Some experimental fairness issues: baselines sometimes fix noise variance while FAB learns Λ; comparisons should be consistent or include both settings.  
- Lacks sensitivity analysis: per-iteration complexity/memory (N,D,K), initialization sensitivity (K0), and hyperparameters (δ, Tshrink).  
- Technical soundness is promising but central asymptotic proofs and identifiability results should have proof sketches and explicit assumptions in main text.  
- Writing and organization are good; moving key assumptions and a clear merge pseudocode into main text would improve clarity.  
- Originality: novel Hessian analysis enabling FAB for LFMs and practical CCCP/shrinkage-acceleration contributions.  
- Significance: broadens FAB applicability, offers an automated alternative to IBP methods, and appears computationally attractive.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theory, simple effective algorithm, strong empirical gains, and clear relation to prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient exposure of core assumptions, limited ablation/sensitivity analyses, and some experimental fairness ambiguities.  
- Suggested fixes: state assumptions and intuition for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 in main text, add ablations and sensitivity experiments, clarify monotonicity with bound traces, and ensure fair noise-variance comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept (borderline to clear accept).  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
