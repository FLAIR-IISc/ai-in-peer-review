## Review of “Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models”

This paper presents a compelling extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing a limitation of previous work that required a specific block-diagonal Hessian condition. The authors demonstrate that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs takes the same form as that for Mixture Models, enabling the application of FAB’s benefits – automatic model selection and computational efficiency – to a broader class of models. The core contribution lies in showing this theoretical equivalence and leveraging it to develop a practical algorithm with significant speedups through accelerated feature shrinkage. 

The work is well-motivated, clearly written, and logically structured. It effectively builds upon prior FAB/FIC research [5, 6] and connects it to the existing literature on LFMs and non-parametric Bayesian methods like the Indian Buffet Process (IBP) [10, 11]. The empirical evaluation is thorough, encompassing both synthetic and real-world datasets, and demonstrates substantial computational gains and competitive performance against a range of competing approaches [2, 3, 22]. The inclusion of detailed experimental setup and comparisons to established methods strengthens the validity of the findings. The analysis relating FAB E-steps to a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) [29] and the subsequent shrinkage acceleration is a clever addition, further enhancing the method’s practicality. The discussion of parameter identifiability and the proposed merge post-processing step are insightful and contribute to a more robust and interpretable model.

However, a significant concern remains regarding the limitations of the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage). While the paper demonstrates strong performance in many scenarios, it lacks a detailed examination of potential failure modes. Specifically, it’s unclear under what conditions these approximations might break down, leading to inaccurate model selection or poor performance. A more in-depth analysis of the assumptions underlying the derivations and a discussion of scenarios where these assumptions are violated would significantly strengthen the paper. Exploring the sensitivity of the method to noise levels or specific data characteristics would also be valuable. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and effective extension of FAB inference to LFMs.
*   Significant computational efficiency gains.
*   Thorough empirical validation against a diverse set of methods.
*   Clear writing and logical structure.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of detailed analysis of potential failure modes and limitations of the approximations.



**Overall:** Despite the concern regarding failure modes, this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field. The theoretical insights, algorithmic innovations, and strong empirical results warrant acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the limitations outlined above, perhaps through additional analysis or discussion in a revised version.
