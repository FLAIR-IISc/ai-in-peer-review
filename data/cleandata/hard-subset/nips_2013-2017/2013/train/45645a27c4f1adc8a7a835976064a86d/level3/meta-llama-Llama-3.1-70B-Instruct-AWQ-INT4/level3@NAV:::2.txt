This paper proposes a novel extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) using mean-field inference and accelerated feature shrinkage. The authors demonstrate that their approach, FAB/LFM, achieves significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets while maintaining good model selection accuracy and predictive performance.

The paper is well-written and clearly structured, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the technical details. The authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation of their method against a wide range of competing methods, including state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process (IBP) methods. The references are sufficient, and the authors adequately discuss related work in the field.

The method itself is a logical and solid extension of prior FAB work, combining several approximations into a novel and effective approach. The authors' use of mean-field inference and accelerated feature shrinkage allows for a more automatic model-selection procedure that requires little hand-tuning.

One major concern with the paper is that it is unclear when or how the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) may fail. The authors should examine failure modes and limits of the approximations to provide a more comprehensive understanding of their method's strengths and weaknesses.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

The paper's clarity is excellent, with clear explanations of the technical details and a well-organized structure. The writing is concise and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers with a basic understanding of Bayesian inference and latent variable models.

The originality of the paper is high, as it proposes a novel extension of FAB inference to LFMs. The authors' approach is a new combination of familiar techniques, and it is clear how their work differs from previous contributions in the field.

The significance of the paper is also high, as it addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The authors' method provides a unique solution to the problem of model selection in LFMs, and their experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness.

In conclusion, this paper is a strong contribution to the field of Bayesian inference and latent variable models. While there are some concerns about the limits of the approximations used, the authors' method is a significant improvement over existing approaches, and their experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective extension of FAB inference to LFMs.
* The authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation of their method against a wide range of competing methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly structured, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the technical details.
* The method is a logical and solid extension of prior FAB work, combining several approximations into a novel approach.

Arguments against acceptance:
