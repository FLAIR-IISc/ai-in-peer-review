- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models by deriving a lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.  
- Shows FIC for LFMs has a similar representation to MMs despite LFMs not meeting the block-diagonal Hessian condition.  
- Enables automatic hidden-state selection and ensures parameter identifiability in LFMs.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including asymptotic Hessian analysis and proofs (in supplementary materials).  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration mechanism to improve computational efficiency.  
- Empirical results (synthetic and real data) demonstrate superior prediction accuracy and runtime versus IBP and VB, especially at large scale.  
- Paper is clear and well organized with thorough explanation of FAB framework and algorithmic details.  
- Addresses identifiability and proposes a merging post-processing step for model refinement.  
- Limitation: FAB/FIC approximation may degrade performance in small-sample scenarios and needs further exploration.  
- Limitation: Experiments could include a wider variety of real-world datasets to strengthen generalizability.  
- Limitation: Reliance on supplementary materials for proofs and some algorithmic details may hinder accessibility for some readers.  
- Overall assessment: incremental but meaningful contribution; recommended for acceptance.
