- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) by showing the FIC for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models, enabling FAB application to LFMs.  
- Key theoretical contribution: asymptotic analysis of the LFM Hessian yields the same regularization term as in mixture models, enabling automatic hidden-state selection without priors.  
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2) based on viewing FAB updates as a CCCP, which removes irrelevant features early and reduces computation.  
- Provides an identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) showing FAB’s regularization helps resolve non-identifiability in LFMs.  
- Empirical results claim superior model selection accuracy, predictive performance, and computational efficiency, especially at large scale.  
- Mathematical imprecision: Equation (3) omits the expectation over q(Z) and the log-sum ∑n z_nk term is problematic if all z_nk = 0.  
- Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime—no clear statement of how latent feature count K scales with sample size N.  
- Theoretical claim of automatic model selection is unclear without specifying K’s behavior as N increases (IBP/ infinite-K context not addressed).  
- Experimental issues: stopping criteria for FAB and baselines are unspecified, making runtime comparisons arbitrary.  
- Unclear whether faster convergence arises from the model-selection criterion itself or from avoiding poor local optima (versus Gibbs sampling issues).  
- Comparison with IBP methods is potentially misleading because concentration parameter α was fixed (α=3) and may bias K estimates.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison with variational EM for LFMs to isolate whether gains come from FAB or algorithmic choices.  
- Contribution is novel but incremental—meaningful extension of FAB to LFMs but not a major theoretical leap.  
- Paper is well-written and structured, but technical/asymptotic details are glossed over and need careful revision to strengthen theoretical validity.
