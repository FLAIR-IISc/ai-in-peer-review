- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), removing the need for a block-diagonal Hessian condition.  
- Derives a lower bound of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs and shows FIC has the same representation as for mixture models despite the Hessian violation.  
- Proves theoretically that the FIC for LFMs converges to the marginal log-likelihood.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method that substantially reduces computation while preserving predictive performance.  
- Demonstrates high technical quality with rigorous asymptotic analysis and complete derivations.  
- Addresses both theory and practice, including parameter identifiability, CCCP interpretation, and merge post-processing.  
- Empirically outperforms fast Gibbs sampling, variational Bayes, and MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets in predictive accuracy and computational cost.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, notation, and logical flow enabling replication.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental but practically important for expanding FAB to LFMs.  
- Significant impact: enables automated, hyperparameter-free model selection for LFMs, tackling expensive dimensionality selection.  
- Minor weakness: incremental novelty, mitigated by the elegant theoretical insight about FIC representation.  
- Related work (e.g., Indian Buffet Process, prior FAB work) is properly acknowledged.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, meaningful contribution with strong support for acceptance.
