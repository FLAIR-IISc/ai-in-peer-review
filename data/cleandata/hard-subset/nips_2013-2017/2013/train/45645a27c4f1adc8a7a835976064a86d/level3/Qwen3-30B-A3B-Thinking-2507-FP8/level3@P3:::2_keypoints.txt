- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), filling a gap in FAB applicability.
- Proves (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) that LFMs yield Factorized Information Criteria (FICs) with the same form as mixture models despite different Hessian structure.
- Enables a fully automated model selection procedure (FAB/LFM) that performs automatic latent-dimension selection with minimal hand-tuning.
- Introduces a novel FAB/LFM algorithm that combines mean-field inference and accelerated feature shrinkage for practical estimation.
- Provides theoretical analysis of algorithm properties, including parameter identifiability (Theorem 4) and a CCCP interpretation for computational acceleration.
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, comparing FAB/LFM to IBP, VB, EM, and MEIBP on synthetic and real data.
- Shows consistent superiority in computational efficiency, model selection accuracy, and predictive performance (higher PLL) across datasets, especially large ones.
- Demonstrates substantial speedups from the proposed acceleration and validates shrinkage and merge post-processing effectiveness.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-structured, and reproducible; algorithms and proofs are precisely documented with supplementary materials.
- Strength: Significant theoretical contribution showing LFMs share FIC form with mixture models.
- Strength: Truly automatic method removing need for hand-tuned hyperparameters in model selection.
- Strength: Strong computational efficiency gains making the method practical for large-scale use.
- Weakness: Does not sufficiently analyze when or why the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) might fail or provide concrete failure-mode examples.
- Weakness: Sensitivity to initialization is not explored, despite using a large initial feature count (min(N,D)).
- Weakness: Theoretical assumptions (e.g., bounded joint marginal log-likelihood) lack practical guidance on when they may be violated.
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, original, and significant contribution with strong empirical support; recommendation for acceptance, with suggestion to address failure-mode analysis in the final version.
