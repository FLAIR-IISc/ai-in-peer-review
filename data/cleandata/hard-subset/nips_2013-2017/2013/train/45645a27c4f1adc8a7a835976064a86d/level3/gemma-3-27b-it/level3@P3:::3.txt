This paper presents a Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference algorithm for binary latent feature models (LFMs), extending previous FAB work to a new model class. The key contribution lies in demonstrating that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has a form consistent with that of mixture models, enabling the application of FAB despite the LFM not strictly meeting the original FAB applicability conditions. The authors also introduce a shrinkage acceleration method and discuss parameter identifiability. Experimental results suggest FAB outperforms Gibbs sampling, variational Bayes, and MAP IBP methods on some large datasets in terms of runtime and inference quality.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Application of FAB:** Applying FAB to LFMs is genuinely novel and extends the scope of the method beyond mixtures and HMMs.
* **Sound Technical Approach:** The theoretical analysis regarding the FIC for LFMs appears solid, and the algorithmic details are correctly presented. Lemma 1 is a crucial result.
* **Scalability:** The method scales well to large datasets, a significant advantage for practical applications.
* **Automatic K Inference:** The ability to automatically infer the number of latent features (K) is valuable and removes the need for manual hyperparameter tuning.
* **Identifiability Analysis:** The discussion of parameter identifiability and the proposed merging post-processing step are insightful.
* **Shrinkage Acceleration:** The proposed shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2) offers a practical speedup in computation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Insufficient Experimental Detail:** The experimental methodology lacks sufficient detail. Specific parameter settings for each baseline method, hardware specifications, and multiple run statistics are missing.
* **Limited Discussion of Limitations:** The paper doesn’t adequately discuss the limitations of the FAB approach, specifically scenarios where it might underperform compared to Gibbs sampling (e.g., small sample sizes or highly structured data).
* **Convergence Concerns:** The EM-like procedure’s potential convergence difficulties are not addressed. While a monotonic increase in the lower bound is guaranteed, this doesn't guarantee convergence to a global optimum.
* **Model Restriction:** The method is limited to binary latent feature models and cannot be easily extended to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
* **Flawed Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental evaluation has several flaws, including an inappropriate baseline (MEIBP) and unfair comparisons (IBP Gibbs).
* **Missing Related Work:** Recent related work on efficient inference for LFMs is not discussed, particularly MAD-Bayes.
* **Incorrect Reference:** Reference [21] is mischaracterized as Gibbs sampling when it actually performs MAP inference.
* **Behavior in Small Data Regimes:** The behavior of the method in small-data regimes is not explored or discussed. The asymptotic arguments may not hold in these cases.



**Specific Concerns & Suggestions:**

* **MEIBP Usage:** The use of MEIBP is inappropriate for some comparisons since it requires non-negative data.  Comparisons using it on datasets with negative values are invalid.
* **IBP Gibbs Baseline:** IBP Gibbs should have been provided with the known noise value in the block images experiment, instead of relying on a heuristic.  This creates an unfair comparison.
* **VB-IBP Details:** The VB-IBP experiments lack crucial details, such as the number of restarts used.  The authors should also consider using an infinite variational version of VB-IBP.
* **Suspicious Gibbs Result:** The accelerated Gibbs result finding ~70 features on the 49-d Sonar data is questionable and potentially due to poor initialization or an incorrect noise variance setting. Further investigation is necessary.
* **Reference Correction:** Correct the description of reference [21].
* **MAD-Bayes Inclusion:** Add a discussion of MAD-Bayes [1] and other small-variance asymptotic work for nonparametric LFMs.
* **Std-Dev Setting:** Correct the recommended standard deviation setting for real data to 0.25 times the example’s standard deviation, as opposed to 0.75.
* **Post-Review Comments:** The authors need to address the concerns raised in the post-review comments regarding clearer explanations and justifications for experimental methodology and baseline settings.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some significant flaws in the experimental evaluation and a lack of thorough discussion of limitations, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field. The extension of FAB to LFMs is novel and theoretically sound. The proposed acceleration method is promising. However, the experimental results need to be more carefully scrutinized and presented with greater detail, and the authors must address the criticisms raised in the post-review comments.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and theoretically sound extension of FAB inference.
* Demonstrated scalability and automatic K selection.
* Potential for improved runtime and inference quality compared to existing methods.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Flawed experimental evaluation and insufficient detail.
* Lack of discussion of limitations and convergence concerns.
* Incorrect reference and omission of relevant related work.



**Recommendation:**

**Major Revision.** I recommend a major revision. The authors need to address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly regarding the experimental evaluation and discussion of limitations. Fixing the reference, including MAD-Bayes, and providing more details on the experimental setup are crucial.  Only with these revisions will the paper be suitable for publication in NIPS.
