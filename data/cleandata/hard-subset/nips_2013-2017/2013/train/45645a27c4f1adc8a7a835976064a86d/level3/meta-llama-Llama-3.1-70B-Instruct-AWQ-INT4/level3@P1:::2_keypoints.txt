- Proposes extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Derives a new factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs via asymptotic Hessian analysis.  
- Develops an accelerated feature shrinkage method based on a CCCP interpretation of FAB optimization.  
- Presents a more automatic model-selection procedure that requires little hand tuning.  
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and technically sound.  
- Provides a thorough empirical evaluation against a wide range of competing methods.  
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Method is a logical and original extension of prior FAB/FIC work.  
- References are sufficient and the explanation is clear and concise.  
- Major concern: unclear when or how the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) may fail.  
- Authors should analyze failure modes and limits of the approximations to clarify strengths and weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the concerns about failure modes and approximation limits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension to LFMs, new accelerated shrinkage method, strong empirical gains, thorough evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of analysis on conditions under which the combined approximations break down.
