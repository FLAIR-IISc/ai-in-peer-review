- Presents a method extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) to latent feature models (LFMs).
- Theoretical contribution: derives an FIC-like criterion for LFMs even though they do not satisfy the block-diagonal Hessian condition.
- Integrates this criterion with a mean-field variational approximation and a novel shrinkage-acceleration heuristic.
- Produces an efficient algorithm for model selection in LFMs as an alternative to nonparametric Bayesian approaches (e.g., Indian Buffet Process).
- Major significance: delivers a more automatic model-selection procedure requiring little hand-tuning, valuable to practitioners.
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and claims are supported by thorough empirical evaluation.
- Empirical results show strong computational efficiency gains and competitive model-selection performance on synthetic and real datasets.
- Major weakness: lacks critical discussion of method limitations and failure modes arising from the cascade of approximations (asymptotic FIC, mean-field factorization, shrinkage heuristic).
- Specific concerns: unclear when mean-field is too restrictive and how FIC accuracy degrades in small-N scenarios; addressing these would strengthen the paper.
