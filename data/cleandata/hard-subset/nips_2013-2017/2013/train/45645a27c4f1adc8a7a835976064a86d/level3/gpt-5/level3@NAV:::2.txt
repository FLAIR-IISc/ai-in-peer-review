This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC) from mixture/HMM settings to latent feature models (LFMs). The core technical claim is an asymptotic analysis of the complete-data Hessian showing that, despite LFMs violating prior block-diagonal assumptions, the resulting FIC has the same form as for mixtures, yielding the characteristic log sum_n z_nk penalty. Building on this, the authors propose a practical algorithm combining a tractable lower bound, mean-field inference over Z, a CCCP interpretation of the E-step, and an accelerated feature-shrinkage routine. They further analyze identifiability and introduce a merge post-processing. Empirically, the method outperforms or matches IBP, VB, and MEIBP baselines in model selection, prediction, and often in speed on synthetic and several real datasets. This work is a logical extension of prior FAB/FIC contributions and connects to nonparametric IBP, VB, and MAP/MEIBP alternatives, as well as “MAD-Bayes.”

Quality: The technical development appears sound, with clear derivations and monotone bound optimization. The CCCP view nicely justifies the shrinkage acceleration. Experiments are thorough, include synthetic and diverse real data, and compare to strong baselines with sensible protocols. However, the paper largely assumes the “mild conditions” behind Lemma 1; it would benefit from a more explicit statement, diagnostics for violations, and guidance on finite-sample behavior.

Clarity: The paper is well written and structured; algorithms are specified and the relation to prior FAB/MM work is clear. Minor suggestions: explicitly list all assumptions for Lemma 1 in the main text, clarify whether accelerateShrinkage preserves bound monotonicity, and report sensitivity to Tshrink and initialization.

Originality: While the building blocks (FIC/FAB, mean-field, CCCP) are known, their combination for LFMs, the Hessian asymptotics establishing FIC’s form, and the shrinkage acceleration/merge step represent a novel and effective synthesis.

Significance: Automatic model selection with little tuning and strong computational gains are practically valuable. The approach should interest researchers working on scalable latent variable modeling beyond IBP-based methods.

Pros for acceptance:
- Solid, principled extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs with clear theory and algorithms.
- Automatic model selection and identifiable solutions; minimal hand-tuning.
- Strong empirical results and notable speedups; broad comparisons and adequate references.
- Clear writing and reproducible presentation.

Cons / concerns:
- Limited analysis of failure modes when approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) interact.
- Assumptions for Lemma 1 not foregrounded; small-N regimes and highly correlated features may degrade accuracy.
- Limited ablations on acceleration and merging; convergence guarantees under acceleration could be clarified.

Overall: A strong, well-executed paper; I recommend acceptance after addressing the failure-mode analysis and assumption clarity.
