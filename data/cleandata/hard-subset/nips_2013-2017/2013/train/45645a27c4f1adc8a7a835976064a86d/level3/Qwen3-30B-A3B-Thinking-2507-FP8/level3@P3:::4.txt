

# Review: Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models

This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture and HMM models to latent feature models (LFMs), addressing a significant limitation in previous FAB work. Previous FAB inference required a block-diagonal Hessian of the complete likelihood, a condition not satisfied by LFMs. The authors demonstrate through rigorous asymptotic analysis that LFMs, despite violating this condition, yield FIC (Factorized Information Criterion) with the same form as mixture models. This is achieved through a key lemma showing that log det |F(d)| = ∑ₖ log(∑ₙ zₙₖ)/N + Oₚ(1), which naturally introduces the log(∑ₙ zₙₖ) term required for FIC without needing the block-diagonal Hessian assumption.

The paper makes several important contributions: (1) Deriving a FIC applicable to LFMs despite the Hessian condition violation; (2) Showing FIC for LFMs has the same representation as for mixture models; (3) Proposing a shrinkage acceleration method based on CCCP interpretation; (4) Demonstrating parameter identifiability in FAB/LFMs and providing a merge post-processing algorithm. The empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets compares FAB/LFMs against fast Gibbs sampling for IBP, variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP, showing superior predictive performance and lower computational cost.

The paper is well-written with thorough derivation steps, making the technical content accessible. The connection between FAB E-steps and CCCP provides an important theoretical insight that leads to the shrinkage acceleration method, which significantly reduces computational cost in practice. The authors carefully address the theoretical properties of FAB/LFMs, including the automatic feature selection capability and parameter identifiability, which are crucial for practical applications.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with rigorous asymptotic analysis and thorough empirical validation. The theoretical derivations are well-supported with proofs in the supplementary materials. The experimental results clearly demonstrate FAB/LFMs' superiority in predictive performance and computational efficiency across multiple datasets, with appropriate comparisons against state-of-the-art methods.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear, with well-organized presentation of the key ideas. The derivation steps are carefully explained, and the algorithms are clearly presented. The connection between FAB and CCCP is particularly well-explained, providing valuable intuition for the shrinkage acceleration method.

**Originality:** While building upon previous FAB work (Fujimaki et al., NIPS 2011, ICML 2012), this extension to LFMs represents a meaningful technical contribution. The key insight that LFMs yield the same FIC form as mixture models despite violating the Hessian condition is non-trivial and expands the applicability of FAB inference. The paper clearly differentiates itself from related work, including MAD-Bayes and other NBP methods.

**Significance:** The work addresses an important problem in latent feature modeling—automatic model selection without requiring prior knowledge of feature dimensionality. The demonstrated improvements in computational efficiency and predictive performance make this work highly relevant to the NIPS community, particularly for applications involving large-scale data where computational cost is prohibitive for existing methods. The automatic feature selection capability also addresses a practical challenge in LFM applications.

**Weaknesses:** The paper's contribution is somewhat incremental, extending previous FAB work rather than introducing a fundamentally new framework. The asymptotic approximation may not be accurate for small N cases, though the authors appropriately acknowledge this limitation in the experimental section. The paper does not explore the sensitivity of the method to the choice of shrinkage acceleration parameters, though the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in practice.

**Pro and Con Arguments for Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Successfully extends FAB inference to a broader class of models (LFMs) that previously could not utilize FAB
- Demonstrates theoretically and empirically that FAB/LFMs achieve superior predictive performance and computational efficiency
- Provides valuable theoretical insights (CCCP interpretation, parameter identifiability) that enhance understanding of FAB
- Well-written with thorough derivations and clear experimental validation

*Con:*
- Contribution is somewhat incremental (extending previous FAB work)
- Limited exploration of the method's sensitivity to hyperparameters

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent variable models. The extension of FAB to LFMs is non-trivial and well-executed, with compelling theoretical and empirical results. The paper's clarity, thoroughness, and relevance to the NIPS community strongly support its acceptance. While the contribution is incremental, it is a meaningful extension that addresses a significant limitation in previous work and opens the door for applying FAB to a wider range of models. The empirical results consistently demonstrate FAB/LFMs' superiority in both predictive performance and computational efficiency, making this an important contribution to the field.
