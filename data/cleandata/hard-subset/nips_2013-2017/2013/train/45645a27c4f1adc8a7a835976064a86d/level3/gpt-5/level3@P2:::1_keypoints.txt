- Paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian likelihoods via an asymptotic Hessian analysis.  
- Lemma 1 shows the Hessian determinant yields a log feature-usage penalty, giving an FIC for LFMs with the same form as for mixtures.  
- Presents an EM-like algorithm that maximizes a tractable lower bound to the FIC, enabling automatic feature shrinkage and selection.  
- Reinterprets the FAB E-step as a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and proposes “shrinkage acceleration” performing multiple CCCP iterations per E-step to prune features earlier.  
- Analyzes parameter identifiability, argues FAB balances redundant feature usage, and suggests a post-hoc merge heuristic.  
- Empirical comparisons to IBP-based methods and variational EM show better model selection, competitive/improved held-out likelihoods, and faster runtimes for FAB/LFM.  
- Strength: clear motivation to broaden FAB applicability beyond block-diagonal Hessian models.  
- Strength: plausible and potentially impactful technical idea that the Hessian asymptotics reproduce log-usage penalties despite parameter sharing.  
- Strength: practical algorithmic contributions (CCCP view, shrinkage acceleration, merge heuristic) that reduce wall-clock time.  
- Strength: empirical evidence of consistent model selection and speedups from shrinkage acceleration.  
- Weakness: asymptotic regime and assumptions are underspecified—unclear whether K is fixed or can grow with N and what regularity/singularity conditions are required.  
- Weakness: edge cases where log ∑n znk is undefined (unused or always-on features) are not rigorously handled during optimization.  
- Weakness: several mathematical imprecisions and notation issues (conditioning in mixture discussion, missing expectations in Eq.(4), ambiguous “asymptotically ignores priors” statement).  
- Weakness: originality is incremental—extension of prior FAB work with new Hessian analysis but needs clearer differentiation from related asymptotic criteria.  
- Weakness: experimental methodology issues—runtime/stopping rules, fairness of likelihood comparisons (fixed vs learned noise), sensitivity to IBP α, and lack of ablations.  
- Weakness: merge step is heuristic with unclear guarantees about likelihood improvement and identifiability conditions.  
- Quality: technically plausible but important details and assumptions are relegated to the supplement, limiting validation.  
- Clarity: generally well-written with helpful intuition, but suffers from notational ambiguities, missing assumptions, and minor typos.  
- Originality: moderately original; main novelty is extending FAB to LFMs and CCCP-based acceleration.  
- Significance: useful to practitioners for automated model selection in LFMs if asymptotic results are made precise.  
- Questions for authors: clarify asymptotic regime for K, state explicit assumptions for Lemma 1, show how boundary cases for feature usage are avoided, allow baselines to learn noise variance, report sensitivity to IBP α, and provide ablations isolating algorithmic effects.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled extension to LFMs, practical fast algorithm with automatic selection, and useful CCCP interpretation/acceleration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: under-specified asymptotic theory, mathematical imprecision, heuristic edge-case handling, and incomplete/possibly unfair experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline leaning reject pending clearer asymptotic assumptions, tighter math, and more careful empirical validation.
