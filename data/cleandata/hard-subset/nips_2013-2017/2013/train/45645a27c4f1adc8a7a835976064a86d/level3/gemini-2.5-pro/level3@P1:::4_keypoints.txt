- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming a key limitation.
- Shows via asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1) that the LFM Hessian yields an FIC penalty of the same form as mixture models: sum of log(sum_n z_nk) terms.
- Derives a valid FIC and a corresponding FAB algorithm for LFMs enabling model selection.
- Algorithm supports automatic feature selection, includes a shrinkage acceleration step (CCCP-inspired), and a post-processing merge to handle identifiability.
- Theoretical derivations are sound; Lemma 1 is the core technical contribution enabling the framework.
- Provides convergence arguments linking FIC to marginal log-likelihood and includes identifiability analysis.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing to IBP Gibbs, VB-IBP, and MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets.
- Results show strong predictive performance and accurate model selection with substantially lower computational cost than competitors.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized; theoretical development and algorithm description are clear and reproducible.
- Original contribution: non-trivial extension of FAB to LFMs and the novel asymptotic Hessian insight that may generalize further.
- Practical significance: fast, scalable, automated alternative to nonparametric Bayesian methods for LFMs.
- Minor weaknesses: work is an extension of existing FAB framework rather than an entirely new paradigm.
- Minor weakness: key assumptions for Lemma 1 are in the supplementary material, so core results rely on those details.
- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths substantially outweigh minor weaknesses.
