This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian likelihoods. The core theoretical claim is that, despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition used in prior FAB for mixture models (MMs), the asymptotics of the Hessian still induce the same factorized information criterion (FIC) form: a complexity penalty that includes D/2 times the sum over features of log sum_n z_nk. Building on this, the authors derive an EM-like FAB algorithm that optimizes a tractable lower bound on the FIC via mean-field variational distributions over Z, yielding automatic feature selection through a model-induced regularizer and a CCCP interpretation of the E-step. They also analyze identifiability and propose a merging post-processing step. Empirically, they report better predictive likelihoods and lower runtime than several Indian Buffet Process (IBP) implementations (Gibbs, VB-IBP, MEIBP) on synthetic and real datasets, with particularly compact models (smaller K).

Relation to prior work: This paper is a direct extension of FAB for MMs/HMMs (Fujimaki and colleagues) to LFMs, and it compares against nonparametric alternatives including IBP and MEIBP, as well as variational EM for finite K. It is also related to Laplace/BIC-type marginal likelihood approximations and to MAD-Bayes asymptotics. The CCCP view of the E-step connects to existing variational updates and exponentiated gradient interpretations.

Quality
- Theory: The key Lemma/Theorem argue that log det Hessian w.r.t. per-dimension parameters scales like sum_k log sum_n z_nk (up to Op(1)), leading to an FIC that matches the MM case. The idea is plausible and appealing, but the asymptotic regime is underspecified. In particular, Theorem 2 does not clearly state whether K is fixed, grows with N, or under what growth rates the approximation holds. A BIC-style Laplace approximation typically assumes fixed finite parameter dimension; this seems in tension with the IBP framing (potentially unbounded K). The paper uses a finite K with shrinkage, but the conditions under which the FIC remains a valid approximation as K changes are not made explicit. Relatedly, the treatment of pathological cases where sum_n z_nk ∈ {0,1} is ad hoc; while the algorithm shrinks/removes such features, the approximation’s validity in their neighborhood should be stated formally.
- Correctness and precision: There are minor inaccuracies and imprecisions. For example, in Section 1.1, pk(X|z·k) should condition only on data assigned to feature/component k; equation (4) appears to omit an expectation operator; Eq. (3)’s log sum z term can diverge if the sum is zero unless one explicitly excludes unused features—this is later handled algorithmically, but the criterion’s domain should be stated. The supplement may contain details, but important assumptions (regularity, boundedness, identifiability conditions) should be summarized in the main text where invoked (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).
- Algorithms: The lower bound and EM-like updates are standard and sensible; the CCCP view is helpful and the shrinkage acceleration is practically motivated. The identifiability analysis and merge heuristic are reasonable, though the conditions for safe merging could be spelled out with a tolerance and bound on the loss increase.

Clarity
- The manuscript is generally readable, but the main theoretical assumptions are too terse in the body. Clarify the asymptotic setting (fixed D, fixed or slowly growing K, bounded moments, eigenvalue conditions on the Fisher information), the role of priors (asymptotic irrelevance), and the handling of degenerate features. Improve precision in the exposition around Eqs. (2)–(4) and the FAB lower bound.
- Experimental methodology would benefit from more explicit stopping criteria and fair-comparison protocols.

Originality
- The extension of FAB/FIC from MMs to LFMs via a new Hessian analysis is novel within the FAB literature, and the CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration is a nice practical twist. That said, the conceptual arc—Laplace/FIC-based selection plus EM-like updates—is an incremental generalization of earlier FAB work. The identifiability observation and merging heuristic are useful but not fundamentally new.

Significance
- If the asymptotics hold in the intended regime, the method offers a compelling alternative to IBP-based model selection for linear-Gaussian LFMs: no hyperparameter tuning, automatic K selection, and competitive or better predictive performance with good speed. This could be of practical interest to researchers using LFMs for matrix factorization-like tasks. The scope is currently limited to linear Gaussian likelihoods with binary features; demonstrating generalization beyond this setting would increase impact.

Pros
- Clear, plausible asymptotic motivation; unifies LFM FIC with MM FIC.
- Practical, deterministic algorithm with automatic feature selection and CCCP-based acceleration.
- Parameter identifiability discussion and merging post-processing address an important issue.
- Competitive predictive performance with compact models; favorable runtimes vs VB-IBP and Gibbs.
- Code-like algorithmic details that others can implement.

Cons
- Asymptotic regime and assumptions are not stated with sufficient precision; unclear how K scales with N.
- Some mathematical imprecision and minor errors in the main text; edge cases for the log-sum penalty inadequately formalized.
- Experimental comparisons leave room for ambiguity: stopping criteria, truncations, initializations, and fairness of runtime measurements are not fully standardized; limited ablations to disentangle effects of the FIC penalty from improved optimization.
- Scope limited to linear-Gaussian LFMs; relation to alternative asymptotics (e.g., MAD-Bayes) could be deepened.

Suggestions for improvement
- State the asymptotic assumptions explicitly in the main text: fixed D, constraints on K (fixed, o(N), or conditions on effective active features), regularity conditions for Lemma 1 (eigenvalue bounds, concentration), and how degenerate features (sum_n z_nk ∈ {0,1}) are excluded from the Laplace term.
- Fix minor derivational inaccuracies (e.g., include expectations where needed, correct conditioning in pk, define the domain of the FIC clearly).
- Provide ablations: FAB without the D/2 log sum penalty (i.e., variational EM), FAB without CCCP acceleration, without merging, and with prior-only η regularization. Also, compare at matched K (force baselines to use the K selected by FAB) to isolate the effect of model selection vs optimization.
- Standardize stopping criteria across methods (fixed number of epochs/passes, wall-clock budget, or tolerance on objective) and report variability over multiple runs with identical initializations when applicable.
- Discuss the relationship to MAD-Bayes and to classical BIC for LFMs; clarify when each approximation is appropriate.
- Include sensitivity analyses w.r.t. initial K and Tshrink; report per-iteration costs and total passes through data.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The paper presents a sensible and potentially useful extension of FAB to LFMs with promising empirical results. However, the theoretical assumptions underlying the FIC in the LFM setting are not sufficiently explicit, and the experimental analysis does not fully disentangle model selection from optimization effects or standardize comparisons. Strengthening the theoretical statements, fixing minor errors, and tightening the experimental methodology would make this a strong submission.
