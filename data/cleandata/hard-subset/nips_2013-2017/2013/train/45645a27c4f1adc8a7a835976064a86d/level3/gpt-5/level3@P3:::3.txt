Summary and relation to prior work
This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features. The central theoretical contribution is an asymptotic analysis of the Hessian of the complete log-likelihood that yields an FIC for LFMs with the same form as for mixture models: a complexity penalty of the form (D/2)∑k log ∑n znk plus O(K log N) terms. This leads to an EM-like algorithm that optimizes a tractable lower bound, includes a CCCP interpretation of the E-step, and introduces a shrinkage acceleration that aggressively prunes features. The method automatically infers the number of latent features K without priors, similar in spirit to nonparametric IBP approaches but using a model-selection criterion instead of prior-based parsimony. The paper also discusses an identifiability result and a merge post-processing step.

Relative to previous FAB work (mixtures, HMMs), applying FAB to LFMs is novel, since LFMs violate the block-diagonal Hessian structure previously required. Relative to nonparametric LFM methods (IBP Gibbs, variational IBP, and MEIBP), the contribution is a prior-free, scalable alternative with automatic feature shrinkage. The CCCP view is useful and practically motivated.

Quality
– Technical soundness: The asymptotic derivation appears careful and yields the expected log-count penalty, supporting automatic shrinkage. The EM-like updates, CCCP interpretation, and acceleration are correct and well-motivated. The identifiability discussion is insightful.
– Empirical validation: Results suggest FAB can be faster and yield better model selection/prediction than several IBP baselines on some datasets. However, several experimental design and baseline-setup issues diminish the strength of the claims (details below). Important limitations and convergence behavior are not discussed.

Clarity
The methodology sections are mostly clear and well-organized; the derivations and algorithmic details are accessible. However, the experimental section lacks sufficient details (e.g., initialization protocols, number of restarts, noise settings per method), and some baseline configurations are inappropriate or undocumented, making it hard to assess fairness and reproducibility.

Originality
High. Extending FAB to LFMs and deriving an FIC of the same form as mixtures despite shared parameters is new. The CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration for LFMs is also a useful contribution.

Significance
Potentially high. Efficient inference with automatic K selection in LFMs is an important problem. If empirically validated with fair baselines, this approach could be adopted by practitioners as a scalable alternative to IBP variants. Current empirical issues limit the strength of the evidence.

Strengths
- Clear asymptotic analysis leading to an FIC for LFMs; technically sound and elegant.
- EM-like algorithm with monotone lower-bound improvement; CCCP interpretation and shrinkage acceleration are practical and effective.
- Automatic inference of K without priors or tunable hyperparameters.
- Scalability: algorithms appear to handle large datasets, and shrinkage reduces computation.
- Novel application of FAB to LFMs; addresses a relevant and challenging problem.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Scope: Limited to binary latent feature models; not applicable to Gaussian factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
- Convergence: No discussion of potential convergence difficulties, sensitivity to initialization, or local optima in the EM/CCCP procedure. The impact of acceleration (multiple CCCP steps) on monotonicity and stability deserves discussion.
- Missing small-data analysis: Behavior in small-sample regimes (where asymptotics may be weak) is not explored.
- Experimental evaluation issues:
  • MEIBP baseline misuse: MEIBP assumes nonnegative W; using it on data violating this (e.g., block data and some simulations) makes those comparisons invalid.
  • IBP Gibbs noise setting: In block image experiments, fairness would require giving Gibbs the true noise std rather than a heuristic.
  • Variational IBP: Only a finite-K variational version is used; the infinite variational formulation exists and should be included. Number of restarts and truncation settings are not reported.
  • Accelerated Gibbs anomaly: Reporting ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data is suspicious—likely due to poor initialization or an incorrect noise variance; needs diagnosis or removal.
  • Runtime fairness: Some baselines do not learn noise variance; mixing fixed vs learned noise across methods can bias outcomes.
  • Restarts/initializations: Not specified across baselines; FAB is run once from random µ, but how many runs for others?
- Related work: Missing discussion of MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and broader small-variance-asymptotic approaches for nonparametric LFMs. Also other recent efficient LFM inference methods are not covered.
- Reference issue: Ref [21] (Reed & Ghahramani) is incorrectly described as Gibbs; it performs MAP via submodular maximization and should be clearly labeled as MEIBP/MAP.
- Minor correction: The recommended real-data noise std setting in some prior work is 0.25× data std (not 0.75×).

Specific suggestions for improvement
- Fix reference [21] to correctly describe it as MAP (MEIBP), not Gibbs.
- Add discussion and citations to MAD-Bayes and other small-variance asymptotics for LFMs; clarify how FAB differs (asymptotic in N vs σ2→0) and when each might be preferable.
- Provide a convergence and limitations section: local optima, sensitivity to initialization, CCCP step scheduling, when shrinkage may over-prune, and cases where Gibbs may outperform (e.g., highly multimodal posteriors).
- Small-N analysis: synthetic experiments varying N (very small regimes) to show when FIC-based approximation may underperform and to quantify gaps vs IBP.
- Baseline corrections:
  • Re-run MEIBP only on nonnegative data or with appropriate preprocessing; otherwise, omit.
  • Give IBP Gibbs the true noise in block experiments; report performance with/without known noise.
  • Include infinite variational IBP; report truncation level, restarts, and initialization.
  • Report number of restarts and selection criterion (best held-out likelihood) for all methods.
  • Investigate the Sonar anomaly; ensure fair noise settings and sensible initializations.
- Report convergence criteria, wall-clock per-iteration breakdown, and effect of Tshrink; confirm lower-bound monotonicity with acceleration.
- Clarify the recommended noise std for real data (use 0.25× per prior refs) and justify any deviations.
- Discuss applicability limits: binary Z only; not directly applicable to factor analysis/PPCA; outline how FAB might extend to real-valued features.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong and novel theory; extends FAB to a new model class.
- Practical, scalable algorithm with automatic K inference.
- Clear algorithmic details; identifiability insight and merging are valuable.

Cons against acceptance
- Experimental methodology and baselines contain flaws that undermine empirical claims.
- Missing related work and incorrect reference description.
- No discussion of limitations or convergence.
- Limited scope (binary LFMs only) without broader applicability discussion.

Recommendation
Promising paper with solid theory and an appealing algorithm, but the experimental evaluation and related-work treatment need correction before acceptance. I recommend rejection in the current form, with encouragement to resubmit after:
- Fixing ref [21], adding MAD-Bayes and related citations,
- Revising experiments with fair, appropriate baselines and detailed methodology,
- Adding discussion of limitations and convergence behavior.
