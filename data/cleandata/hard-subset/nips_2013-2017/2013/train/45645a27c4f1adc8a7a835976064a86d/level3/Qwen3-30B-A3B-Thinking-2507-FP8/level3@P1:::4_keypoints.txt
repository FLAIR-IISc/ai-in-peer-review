- Main contribution: Extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) by proving LFMs admit the same factorized information criterion (FIC) as mixture models despite violating the block-diagonal Hessian condition.  
- Key theoretical result: Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 show the log-determinant of the Hessian asymptotically depends on ∑ₙ zₙₖ, enabling FIC and automatic feature selection/identifiability.  
- Algorithmic advances: Proposes FAB/LFM algorithm with CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration and an identifiability-driven merge post-processing (Theorem 4), with monotonic convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical performance: Comprehensive experiments show FAB/LFM outperforms IBP, VB, and MEIBP in predictive likelihood, model selection (smaller K), and computational efficiency (e.g., ~50x faster than MEIBP on USPS).  
- Quality of work: Theoretical contributions are rigorous and well-justified, proofs and convergence claims are provided, and experiments evaluate speed, K-selection, and PLL/TLL thoroughly.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Paper is well-organized with precise notation, clear algorithm descriptions, and supplementary materials containing full proofs.  
- Originality: Significant extension of prior FAB work by removing the block-diagonal Hessian requirement; CCCP shrinkage and merge steps are novel practical refinements.  
- Significance: Provides a scalable, hyperparameter-free alternative for Bayesian feature selection in LFMs with clear real-world applicability and large runtime reductions.  
- Weaknesses: Omits VB in large-scale comparisons due to runtime, performs worse on small-N datasets (Libras) where FIC approximation weakens, and offers limited analysis of small-N approximation error and initialization sensitivity.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong, well-executed contribution that fills a theoretical gap and delivers practical gains — accept.
