- Proposal: Extend Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models (LFMs) using a Laplace approximation to derive a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) that penalizes complexity and enables automatic selection of K, with an EM-like optimization algorithm.

- Relation to prior work: Direct extension of existing FAB literature to a model class lacking the block-diagonal Hessian assumption, positioned as a computationally efficient alternative to IBP-based methods.

- Strength — Novelty & practicality: Non-trivial adaptation of FAB to LFMs, producing a practical EM-like algorithm with an integrated sparsity-inducing penalty and an effective shrinkage acceleration technique.

- Strength — Empirical performance: Demonstrates superior or competitive held-out predictive log-likelihoods versus state-of-the-art IBP implementations, often with much faster runtimes.

- Strength — Motivation: Principled asymptotic justification via Laplace approximation linking the criterion to BIC-style reasoning.

- Weakness — Incremental contribution: Core technical machinery mostly extends prior FAB work and does not introduce a fundamentally new inference principle.

- Weakness — Mathematical imprecision: Objective includes log(sum_n z_nk), which is undefined if a feature is unused; the paper does not clarify handling of this edge case.

- Weakness — Theorem limitations: Theorem 2 omits the asymptotic regime and the relation between K and N, raising doubts about validity when K grows with N (contrasting with IBP motivations).

- Weakness — Minor formal errors: Some conditioning and notation errors (e.g., mixture model conditioning, clarity around the expectation and lower-bounding in Eq. 4).

- Weakness — Experimental shortcomings: Runtime comparisons to IBP Gibbs lack specified stopping criteria, making convergence-time claims ambiguous.

- Weakness — Ambiguous source of gains: Unclear whether better predictive performance arises from FIC as a model selection criterion or from improved optimization/avoidance of poor local optima; hyperparameter and mixing issues for competing methods are not disentangled.

- Overall assessment: Promising, practically useful method with strong empirical results but somewhat incremental, requiring clearer mathematical treatment and more rigorous, detailed experimental comparisons to fully substantiate claims.
