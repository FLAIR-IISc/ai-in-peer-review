- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the FIC from mixtures/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Key theoretical result (Lemma 1): per-dimension Hessian yields the same log sum_n z_nk penalty as mixtures, enabling an FIC of the same form for LFMs.  
- Theorem and corollary show asymptotic equivalence of the derived FIC to the marginal log-likelihood.  
- Practical FAB algorithm: mean-field lower bound on FIC, linearized log penalty, alternating E/M updates.  
- E-step admits a CCCP interpretation and motivates a shrinkage-acceleration scheme that prunes features early.  
- Identifiability analysis shows FAB regularization favors balanced/merged representations and motivates a merging post-processing step.  
- Empirics: competitive or superior predictive log-likelihoods and lower runtime versus strong IBP baselines (fast Gibbs, VB) and MEIBP on synthetic and real data.  
- Block-data experiment demonstrates effective feature recovery and pruning; real-data studies show more compact K and plausible PLL gains.  
- Main caveat: asymptotic guarantees rely on large N; finite-sample behavior depends on the lower bound and initialization.  
- Mean-field q(Z) may underestimate posterior dependencies; structured variational forms are not explored.  
- Some experimental choices may bias results toward FAB (e.g., FAB learns noise variance while some baselines had it fixed; MEIBP constrained to non-negative data).  
- Reviewer suggests summarizing the “mild assumptions” for Lemma 1 in the main text (e.g., bounds on Λ,W, moments of X, non-degenerate features).  
- Suggests additional robustness checks: small-N stress tests, sensitivity to initialization, Tshrink, tolerance δ, and multiple random seeds.  
- Requests fairness in comparisons (allow baselines to estimate noise variance) and reporting of per-iteration complexity and memory costs.  
- Clarify the generality of the analysis beyond Gaussian likelihoods (e.g., Poisson/Bernoulli LFMs).  
- Paper is well written, organized, with explicit derivations and consistent notation; main text could briefly state assumptions.  
- Originality: nontrivial and useful extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs despite prior Hessian factorization issues; incremental within FAB literature but novel for LFMs.  
- Significance: offers automated, prior-insensitive model selection for LFMs with provable asymptotics and practical efficiency, valuable to practitioners.  
- Strengths: solid asymptotic analysis, practical monotonic algorithm with automatic shrinkage and acceleration, identifiability/merge handling, strong empirical performance, clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: asymptotic dependence on large N, mean-field approximation limits, some experimental fairness issues, and need for complexity/memory analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound extension, elegant generalizable result, practical algorithmic innovations, strong empirical evidence, and relevance to community.  
- Arguments against acceptance: asymptotic nature with finite-N concerns, incremental within FAB program, and experimental setup could be more balanced and robustly analyzed.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
