- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs).
- FAB maximizes a tractable lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to marginal log-likelihood in the large-sample limit.
- Addresses a limitation of prior FAB methods that required a block-diagonal Hessian, which LFMs do not satisfy.
- Derives a lower bound of FIC applicable to LFMs despite the Hessian-condition violation.
- Shows the derived FIC for LFMs has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models, indicating wider applicability to discrete latent-variable models.
- Provides a thorough derivation of the FIC for LFMs, including a crucial lemma used in the proof.
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method that drastically reduces computational cost in practice.
- Empirically evaluates FAB/LFMs on synthetic and real datasets, comparing to fast Gibbs sampling for IBP, variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP.
- Results indicate superior predictive performance and lower computational cost for FAB/LFMs versus competitors.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations, logical organization, and reproducible technical details.
- Claims are technically sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Contribution is somewhat incremental but presents a novel combination and perspective on FAB and LFMs.
- The proposed shrinkage acceleration is an original contribution improving algorithmic efficiency.
- The method is significant and has potential for wide practical use, advancing the state of the art.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension, strong empirical results, clear exposition, and community relevance.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental over prior FAB work and would benefit from more diverse experimental validation.
- Overall recommendation: reviewer supports acceptance.
