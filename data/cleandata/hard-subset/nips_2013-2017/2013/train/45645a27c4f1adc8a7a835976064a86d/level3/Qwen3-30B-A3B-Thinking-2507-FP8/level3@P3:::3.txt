

# Review: Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models

This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), addressing an important gap in the FAB framework that previously only applied to models with specific Hessian conditions (e.g., mixture models). The authors demonstrate that despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition, their FIC (Factorized Information Criterion) has the same form as for mixture models, enabling FAB inference to automatically select the number of latent features K without requiring priors or hyperparameters. The paper presents an EM-based inference algorithm using FAB shrinkage, which the authors claim outperforms Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP methods on large datasets in runtime and inference quality.

The paper makes a novel contribution by applying FAB to LFMs, which represents an important extension beyond previous work on mixtures and HMMs. The theoretical analysis is sound, with Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 providing solid justification for the FAB/LFM approach. The algorithmic details are correct, and the paper demonstrates good scalability to large datasets. The authors also introduce a shrinkage acceleration method that significantly reduces computational cost, and they provide a theoretical justification through CCCP interpretation.

The paper's strengths include:
- Novel application of FAB to LFMs, addressing an important problem in efficient inference while automatically inferring K
- Sound theoretical foundation with rigorous proofs provided in supplementary materials
- Demonstrated scalability to large datasets
- Automatic feature selection capability through FAB shrinkage
- Good empirical performance on large-scale real-world data

However, the paper has significant weaknesses that need addressing before acceptance:

1. Experimental evaluation contains critical flaws:
   - MEIBP baseline was inappropriately used on datasets containing negative values (MEIBP requires non-negative W)
   - IBP Gibbs was not given the known noise value in block images experiments (used heuristic instead)
   - VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version without details (number of restarts, etc.)
   - The accelerated Gibbs result finding ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data appears suspicious and likely due to bad initialization
   - Reference [21] is incorrectly described as Gibbs sampling (it performs MAP inference)

2. The paper lacks important discussion:
   - No discussion of algorithmic limitations or cases where FAB may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling
   - No analysis of potential convergence difficulties for the EM-like procedure
   - No exploration of behavior in small-data regimes where asymptotic arguments may fail
   - Limited to binary latent feature models (cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA)

3. Related work is insufficiently addressed:
   - Missing discussion of MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and other small-variance asymptotic work for nonparametric LFMs
   - Some recent efficient inference methods for LFMs are omitted

4. Experimental details are inadequate:
   - Insufficient description of baseline settings and justification
   - Incomplete reporting of experimental methodology
   - No discussion of parameter sensitivity (e.g., the recommended standard deviation setting is incorrectly described as 0.75 times examples' std-dev instead of 0.25)

While the paper presents a technically sound and novel extension of FAB to LFMs with promising empirical results, the experimental evaluation contains serious flaws that undermine the claims of superiority over other methods. The inappropriate use of MEIBP on non-negative data, the questionable Sonar experiment result, and the omission of key related work (like MAD-Bayes) significantly weaken the paper's contribution.

The paper would be significantly improved by:
1) Correcting the reference [21] description
2) Including MAD-Bayes citation and discussion
3) Fixing the standard deviation description (0.25 instead of 0.75)
4) Providing clearer experimental methodology and justification
5) Addressing the MEIBP baseline issue and using appropriate noise values
6) Discussing limitations and small-data behavior

Overall, while the core idea is novel and the technical approach is sound, the experimental evaluation's flaws prevent the paper from making a convincing case for its superiority. The authors need to address these critical issues before the paper can be recommended for acceptance. The method has potential significance for large-scale latent feature modeling, but the current presentation fails to adequately demonstrate this due to experimental shortcomings.
