The paper "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models" proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters via BIC, yielding an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties. The authors demonstrate improved held-out/predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes compared to several Indian Buffet Process (IBP) implementations.

The approach is reasonable and motivated by a plausible asymptotic argument, and the technical contribution is novel but incremental, essentially an extension of prior FAB work. The mathematical exposition is mostly clear, but there are some imprecise points, such as equation (3), where the log-sum of z may be infinite if all zeros, raising questions about FIC.

The paper's main contribution is the derivation of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for Latent Feature Models (LFMs), which has the same form as the FIC for Mixture Models (MMs). The authors show that the FIC for LFMs can be approximated using a similar approach to the FIC for MMs, despite LFMs not satisfying the conditions required for the FIC for MMs.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, FAB/LFMs, in terms of computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance. FAB/LFMs consistently achieved good predictive performance with low computational cost, and the method's unique regularization worked well for mitigating over-fitting.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K scales with N. The BIC-based approximation seems to require a fixed finite K, conflicting with the IBP's infinite-K motivation. Additionally, the experimental results report faster convergence for FAB, but the stopping criteria are unspecified, making runtime comparisons and Gibbs sampler truncation arbitrary.

Furthermore, it is unclear whether the predictive improvements come from the model-selection criterion or from algorithms avoiding worse local optima. The Gibbs sampler's tendency to learn too many components suggests implementation/local-optima issues.

Overall, the paper presents a sensible but somewhat incremental method, with some imprecise math and insufficient experimental analysis to fully interpret the reported gains. However, the proposed method shows promise, and with further refinement and clarification, it could be a valuable contribution to the field.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, but there are some imprecise points in the mathematical exposition. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the paper could benefit from more detailed analysis and clarification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide sufficient background information and explanations of the proposed method. However, some of the mathematical derivations could be clarified, and the notation could be more consistent throughout the paper.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel extension of the FAB framework to LFMs. While the technical contribution is incremental, the application of the FAB framework to LFMs is new and shows promise.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The proposed method has the potential to be widely applicable and could advance the state of the art in latent feature modeling.
