This paper presents an extension of the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to latent feature models (LFMs), which have not been applicable to FAB inference due to the lack of a specific condition on the Hessian matrix of the complete log-likelihood. The authors provide an asymptotic analysis of the Hessian matrix of LFMs, showing that the factorized information criterion (FIC) of LFMs has the same form as those of mixture models. This leads to the development of a FAB/LFM algorithm that offers several desirable properties, including automatic hidden state selection and parameter identifiability.

The paper relates the proposed method to previous work on FAB inference, mixture models, and latent feature models. The authors also discuss the differences between their approach and other methods, such as non-parametric Bayesian (NPB) methods and variational Bayesian (VB) methods. The paper is well-organized, and the notation is clearly defined.

The technical approach appears sound, and the algorithmic details are correct. The authors provide a thorough derivation of the FIC for LFMs and show that it has the same form as the FIC for mixture models. The FAB/LFM algorithm is also well-described, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the optimization procedure.

The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the authors compare their method to several baselines, including Gibbs sampling, variational EM, and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results show that FAB/LFM outperforms the baselines in terms of runtime and inference quality on some large datasets.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The methodology sections are reasonably clear, but the experimental details are insufficiently described. For example, the authors do not provide enough information about the initialization of the parameters, the convergence criteria, and the number of restarts used in the variational EM algorithm.

Additionally, the paper lacks a discussion of algorithmic limitations and cases where the method may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling. The authors also do not discuss potential convergence difficulties for the EM-like procedure.

Another limitation of the paper is that the method is limited to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA. The authors should discuss the potential extensions of their method to other types of latent variable models.

The experimental evaluation is not thorough, and there are some flaws in the design of the experiments. For example, the MEIBP baseline was inappropriately used, as it requires non-negative W, which is not the case in some of the simulations and block data comparisons. Additionally, the VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version, and the number of restarts is not specified.

The authors should also discuss the behavior of their method in small-data regimes, where the asymptotic arguments may fail. Furthermore, some recent related work on efficient inference for LFMs is missing from the discussion.
