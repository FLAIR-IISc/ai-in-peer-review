Summary and relation to prior work
The paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMM settings to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features. The main theoretical contribution is an asymptotic analysis of the Hessian of the complete log-likelihood for LFMs (Lemma 1), showing that log det terms contribute the same log sum_n z_nk complexity penalty as in mixtures. This yields an FIC for LFMs (Theorem 2) with the same functional form as in mixtures, enabling an EM-like FAB algorithm that jointly estimates parameters and automatically prunes features without explicit priors. The authors relate the E-step updates to a convex-concave procedure (CCCP), proposing a shrinkage-acceleration routine and a merge post-processing, and show an identifiability result (Theorem 4) that favors balanced/merged features at stationary points. Experiments compare FAB against IBP (Gibbs, variational, and the MEIBP MAP method), claiming advantages in model selection, prediction, and compute.

This work connects to: FAB for mixtures/HMMs (Fujimaki et al., AISTATS/ICML), variational and sampling IBP methods (Doshi-Velez et al., AISTATS; Doshi-Velez & Ghahramani accelerated sampling), and MAP/submodular IBP (Reed & Ghahramani). It is also closely related to the small-variance asymptotics line (e.g., MAD-Bayes, Broderick et al., ICML 2013), which derives prior-induced regularizers in the σ→0 limit for mixtures and LFMs; your FIC-based regularization differs in origin (large-N asymptotics that asymptotically ignore priors) but is comparable in spirit and deserves a more explicit comparison.

Quality
- Theory: The central asymptotic claim (Lemma 1 leading to Theorem 2) appears plausible and important, but it is stated under “mild assumptions” deferred to the supplement. For a NIPS paper, the main assumptions and their scope should be summarized clearly in the main text: asymptotic regime (N→∞ with D,K fixed or slowly growing?), conditions on Z (e.g., feature activation rates bounded away from 0/1), regularity of Λ and W, and whether Op(1) constants depend on K,D. It would help to add intuition for why log det F(d) decomposes into ∑k log ∑n z_nk.
- Algorithm: The lower bound, mean-field factorization on Z, and CCCP interpretation are technically sound. The shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃_k) is clearly derived from the FIC and leads to automatic feature pruning. The merge post-processing is reasonable given Theorem 4, though its behavior under near-collinearity (not exact equality) is not analyzed.
- Empirics: The experiments suggest potential for speed and model selection. However, several methodological issues limit the strength of the evidence (see below), including fairness/validity of baselines and incomplete reporting.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well written and organized, and the derivations are clear. The CCCP perspective and algorithm boxes help.
- Please:
  1) Move a concise statement of Lemma 1 assumptions and asymptotic regime into the main text, and discuss limitations when N is small.
  2) Clarify convergence properties and practical guidance: number of CCCP inner steps; need for restarts; sensitivity to initialization; stopping criteria for merge().
  3) Provide computational complexity per iteration and empirical scaling in N, D, K.
  4) Expand experimental details: number of restarts per method, truncation levels for VB-IBP, priors/hyperparameters, noise handling, and how K is initialized/updated across methods.

Originality
- Novelty is strong: extending FAB/FIC to LFMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessians and showing the same FIC form as mixtures is a nontrivial and insightful contribution. The CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability/merge analysis are useful refinements.
- Related work coverage is good but should be broadened and corrected in places:
  - Provide a substantive discussion comparing FIC-based regularization to small-variance asymptotics/MAD-Bayes for LFMs, and clarify when each approximation is appropriate (large-N vs. σ→0).
  - Include additional recent efficient inference for LFMs (e.g., scalable variational truncations, structured mean-field, or additional MAP formulations) if relevant.
  - Correct the description of Reed & Ghahramani (MEIBP) as a MAP/submodular optimization method, not a Gibbs sampler.

Significance
- If the asymptotic result is correct and robust, the implication—that FIC with automatic feature pruning extends beyond mixtures to LFMs—has practical and theoretical impact. The algorithm is simple, hyperparameter-free, and fast, with potential adoption by practitioners.
- The significance is tempered by empirical issues that currently prevent a confident assessment of superiority over strong IBP baselines.

Detailed concerns and suggestions
- Baselines and fairness:
  - MEIBP assumes nonnegativity; applying it to data that violate this (or with negative W) is inappropriate. Restrict those comparisons to valid settings or impose matching constraints on FAB/EM for fairness.
  - In block-image experiments, if the true noise std is known (synthetic), use the true value across all methods, including Gibbs IBP; do not use different heuristics.
  - For VB-IBP, specify truncation level, number of restarts, and convergence criteria; consider stronger infinite-IBP variational baselines or more competitive truncations.
  - The accelerated Gibbs result producing ~70 features on a 49-d Sonar dataset seems suspicious; investigate initialization, noise variance, and convergence diagnostics.
- Small-N behavior: Since FIC relies on large-N asymptotics, add experiments and discussion in small-data regimes (your Libras note hints at this). Report where FAB underperforms and why.
- Convergence: Discuss local optima, whether multiple restarts are needed, and any annealing/tempering that improves robustness.
- Identifiability: Theorem 4 treats exact equality w_k = w_l. Analyze near-duplicate features and the merge criterion’s stability; show empirical effects on real data.
- Minor corrections:
  - Reference [21] (MEIBP) performs MAP inference via submodular maximization; correct any misstatements.
  - Include and discuss MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and related small-variance asymptotic work for LFMs more thoroughly.
  - The recommended real-data noise setting in the cited works is 0.25× the data std, not 0.75; please verify and correct.
  - Tighten the description of assumptions for Lemma 1 in the main text.

Pros
- Conceptually novel asymptotic result extending FIC to LFMs.
- Clean, hyperparameter-free algorithm with automatic feature selection.
- CCCP interpretation and shrinkage acceleration are practical and well motivated.
- Empirical results show promise on several datasets.

Cons
- Incomplete/misaligned baseline setups (MEIBP constraints, noise settings) and missing experimental details.
- Limited analysis of small-N regimes, convergence, and limitations.
- Some related work and citations need correction/expansion.
- Theory relies on unstated assumptions in the main text; scope/limitations not fully clear.

Recommendation
Weak reject. The theoretical contribution and algorithm are compelling, but the paper needs stronger, cleaner empirical validation, clearer articulation of assumptions/limitations, and corrected/expanded related work. With these addressed, this could become a strong submission.
