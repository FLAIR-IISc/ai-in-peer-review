- Paper extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs), filling a gap where previous FAB required Hessian block-diagonality.
- Shows LFMs share the same FIC structure as mixture models despite not satisfying the block-diagonal condition.
- Derives a novel FAB/LFM algorithm that provides automatic feature selection and improved parameter identifiability.
- Builds on prior FAB work for mixture models and demonstrates empirical gains in predictive likelihood and computational efficiency versus IBP implementations.
- Theoretical strengths: asymptotic argument for FIC derivation (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) is sound in intent.
- Practical strengths: automatic model selection without hyperparameters and strong empirical performance with faster convergence.
- Shrinkage acceleration technique (Algorithm 2) is a valuable contribution for large-scale applications.
- Mathematical imprecisions: Equation (3) problematic when all z·k = 0, raising validity concerns for FIC in that case.
- Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime, particularly how K scales with N, conflicting with IBP’s infinite-K motivation.
- Exposition errors: Section 2’s pk(X|z·,k) should condition only on data assigned to component k, and Equation (4) appears to omit an expectation.
- Experimental issues: stopping criteria are unspecified (Table 1), making runtime comparisons arbitrary and potentially unfair.
- Unclear whether predictive improvements come from the model-selection criterion or from avoiding poor local optima (possible Gibbs implementation issues).
- Overall assessment: reasonable, practically useful, but technically incremental with insufficient mathematical precision and theoretical justification.
- Recommendation: weak reject due to limited novelty and insufficient technical rigor; paper needs clearer assumptions, tighter exposition, and better experimental controls.
