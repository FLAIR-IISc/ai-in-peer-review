- Paper extends FAB inference to linear-Gaussian latent feature models (LFMs) with Bernoulli features and derives the same FIC form as for mixtures via Hessian asymptotics.
- Main theoretical contribution: Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 show a complexity term (D/2)∑k log ∑n znk arises despite non-block-diagonal Hessian.
- Algorithmic contributions: EM-like FAB with mean-field E-step giving a shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃k) that prunes features, standard M-step, CCCP interpretation for shrinkage acceleration, and post-hoc merging for identifiability.
- Empirical claim: FAB matches or outperforms accelerated Gibbs IBP, variational IBP, and MEIBP on model selection, predictive likelihood, and runtime on several datasets.
- Relation to prior work: first FIC derivation for LFMs via Hessian; should compare and distinguish from small-variance/mode-seeking asymptotics (e.g., MAD-Bayes).
- Strength: Novel and nontrivial theoretical extension recovering FIC form without previous Hessian assumptions.
- Strength: Practical algorithm with provable monotonic lower-bound ascent and an elegant CCCP-based acceleration.
- Strength: Automatic model selection and principled merging heuristic improving identifiability.
- Strength: Promising empirical results with compact models and favorable runtimes on medium/large datasets.
- Weakness (scope): Method limited to binary-feature LFMs with linear-Gaussian likelihoods; not applicable to continuous features or non-Gaussian likelihoods.
- Weakness (convergence): EM/CCCP can get stuck; paper lacks discussion on initialization, restarts, and convergence diagnostics.
- Weakness (small-N): Asymptotic guarantees rely on large N; behavior and failure modes on small datasets are not analyzed.
- Experimental fairness issue: MEIBP requires W ≥ 0; several comparisons are invalid without enforcing nonnegativity.
- Experimental fairness issue: Baselines should use the same noise model; some experiments used mismatched or heuristic noise settings and incorrect recommended settings.
- Experimental fairness issue: Variational IBP used a finite-K variant without reporting K range, restarts, or priors; infinite variational approach should be considered.
- Experimental issue: Reported accelerated Gibbs results (e.g., ~70 features on 49-d Sonar) are suspicious and may result from poor initialization or wrong noise settings; needs multi-seed runs and variance reporting.
- Reference/description error: MEIBP was misdescribed as Gibbs; it is a MAP/submodular method and should be corrected with matched hyperparameters and constraints.
- Clarity: Writing is clear but experiments need more detail—preprocessing, splits, hyperparameters, initialization, stopping criteria, restarts, wall-clock times, and per-iteration complexity.
- Theoretical clarity: State all assumptions for Lemma 1 in the main text and discuss their practical reasonableness.
- Originality: Novel relative to FAB on mixtures/HMMs, but should explicitly contrast with small-variance asymptotics literature.
- Significance: If corrected baselines hold, the method is a meaningful automated, scalable alternative to nonparametric LFMs with useful identifiability insights.
- Requested fix: Correct MEIBP description and rerun or remove invalid comparisons respecting MEIBP constraints.
- Requested fix: Provide detailed experimental methodology, multiple restarts, variance across runs, and matched/known noise settings for baselines.
- Requested fix: Add discussion and citation of small-variance asymptotics (e.g., MAD-Bayes) and clarify differences in assumptions/outcomes.
- Requested fix: Analyze small-N regimes and convergence behavior; include at least one case where FAB underperforms and explain why.
- Requested fix: Clarify limitations to binary-feature LFMs and discuss possible extensions or why they are nontrivial.
- Pros for acceptance: Strong theory, simple fast algorithm with automatic selection, good empirical performance, and useful identifiability analysis.
- Cons for acceptance: Experimental fairness issues and missing details, limited model scope, lack of small-N/convergence analysis, and incorrect/missing related-work descriptions.
- Overall recommendation: Promising and solid theory with attractive algorithm; accept if experimental flaws and related-work/analysis gaps are fixed, otherwise recommend rejection.
