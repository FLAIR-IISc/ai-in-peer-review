

# Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), including Indian Buffet Process (IBP) models. The authors demonstrate that despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition required for FAB in previous work (e.g., for mixture models), their Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) takes the same form as for mixture models. This leads to an FAB/LFM algorithm that automatically selects the number of latent features while providing computational efficiency and improved predictive performance compared to existing IBP implementations.

The paper builds on Fujimaki and Morinaga's FAB work for mixture models [5,6] and extends it to LFMs. The authors show that the log-determinant of the Hessian matrix for LFMs contains a log-sum term (log ∑n znk) that enables FIC to serve as a model selection criterion, similar to mixture models. They derive an FAB/LFM algorithm with a shrinkage acceleration technique and prove parameter identifiability for the model.

**Quality**: The paper makes a technically sound contribution by extending FAB inference to LFMs. The asymptotic analysis in Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 provides a reasonable justification for the FIC form, though Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime (particularly how K scales with N). The requirement for fixed finite K conflicts with IBP's motivation for infinite latent features, creating a conceptual tension that the authors do not adequately resolve. The mathematical exposition contains imprecisions: equation (3) is problematic as log(∑n znk) may be infinite if all znk=0, and equation (4) is missing an expectation. The experimental results (Table 1) report faster convergence but lack specification of stopping criteria, making runtime comparisons arbitrary.

**Clarity**: The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written. The connection between FAB and CCCP is insightful, and the shrinkage acceleration technique is well-explained. However, the paper would benefit from more detailed discussion of the asymptotic regime in Theorem 2 and clarification of the relationship between the finite-K FAB approach and IBP's infinite-K motivation.

**Originality**: The technical contribution is novel but incremental, extending prior FAB work [5,6] to LFMs. The key insight—that LFMs, despite not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition, yield the same FIC form as mixture models—is reasonable and well-supported. The paper adequately references related work, though it could better clarify how this work differs from the authors' previous FAB publications [4,5].

**Significance**: The method shows promising empirical results with improved predictive performance and computational efficiency compared to IBP implementations. The automatic model selection capability is valuable for practitioners, and the shrinkage acceleration technique provides practical benefits. However, the significance is somewhat limited by the incremental nature of the contribution. The paper does not fully resolve whether the predictive improvements stem from the model-selection criterion or from the algorithm avoiding worse local optima (as suggested by Gibbs sampling's tendency to learn too many components).

**Strengths**: The paper provides a theoretically motivated extension of FAB inference to LFMs, demonstrates practical computational benefits, and offers a new shrinkage acceleration technique. The empirical results (Table 1) show consistent improvements in predictive likelihood and computational efficiency, particularly for larger datasets.

**Weaknesses**: The asymptotic justification is incomplete without specifying the scaling of K with N. The mathematical exposition contains several imprecisions that could confuse readers. The experimental section lacks sufficient detail about stopping criteria, making runtime comparisons questionable. The paper does not sufficiently address whether the observed improvements are due to the model selection criterion or algorithmic advantages in avoiding local optima.

**Recommendation**: This is a reasonable but incremental contribution that extends prior FAB work to LFMs. While the approach is well-motivated and the results are promising, the paper would benefit from a more rigorous treatment of the asymptotic regime and clearer experimental reporting. The technical contribution is solid but not transformative for the field.

**Pro Acceptance**: Well-motivated extension of FAB to LFMs; practical computational improvements; theoretically sound (with caveats); promising empirical results.

**Con Against Acceptance**: Incremental contribution; incomplete asymptotic justification; mathematical imprecisions; insufficient experimental detail; unclear whether improvements stem from model selection or algorithmic advantages.
