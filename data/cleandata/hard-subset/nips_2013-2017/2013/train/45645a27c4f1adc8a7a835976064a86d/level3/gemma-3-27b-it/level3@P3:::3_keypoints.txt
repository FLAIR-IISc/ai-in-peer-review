- Novel application of FAB to binary latent feature models, extending FAB beyond mixtures/HMMs.  
- Theoretical analysis showing FIC for LFMs matches mixture-model form, enabling FAB use despite nonstandard applicability.  
- Lemma 1 and related technical results are sound and algorithmic details are correctly presented.  
- Proposed shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) provides practical runtime speedups.  
- Method scales well to large datasets.  
- Automatic inference of the number of latent features (K) removes manual tuning.  
- Discussion of parameter identifiability and a merging post-processing step is insightful.  
- Experimental results claim FAB outperforms Gibbs, VB, and MAP IBP on some large datasets in runtime and quality.  
- Experimental methodology lacks sufficient detail (baseline parameters, hardware, multiple runs).  
- Paper insufficiently discusses limitations and failure modes (e.g., small samples, highly structured data).  
- EM-like procedure may face convergence issues; monotonic bound increase doesn’t ensure global optimum.  
- Approach is restricted to binary LFMs and not easily extendable to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Experimental evaluation contains flaws: inappropriate baseline choices (MEIBP) and unfair IBP Gibbs comparisons.  
- Missing discussion of recent related work on efficient LFM inference, notably MAD-Bayes and small-variance asymptotics.  
- Reference [21] is mischaracterized as Gibbs sampling while it performs MAP inference.  
- Behavior in small-data regimes is unexplored; asymptotic arguments may not hold there.  
- MEIBP was inappropriately used on datasets with negative values, invalidating those comparisons.  
- IBP Gibbs baseline should have been given known noise values in block images experiment for fairness.  
- VB-IBP experiments lack details (e.g., number of restarts); consider using an infinite variational VB-IBP.  
- Suspicious accelerated Gibbs result (~70 features on 49-d Sonar) likely due to poor initialization or wrong noise variance.  
- Correct recommended std-dev setting for real data to 0.25× example SD (not 0.75).  
- Authors should correct reference descriptions, include MAD-Bayes and related work, and address post-review methodological concerns.  
- Overall: novel and theoretically solid contribution but undermined by flawed/insufficient experiments and missing discussions.  
- Recommendation: Major Revision — address experimental details, limitations, references, and include missing related work before acceptance.
