This paper presents a novel extension of the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to latent feature models (LFMs). The authors derive a new form of the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs, which is similar to the one for mixture models, despite the fact that LFMs do not satisfy the conditions required for the original FAB inference. The proposed FAB/LFM algorithm is shown to have several desirable properties, including automatic hidden state selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound through iterative optimization.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of model selection in LFMs. The technical approach appears sound, and the algorithmic details are correct. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in comparison to other state-of-the-art methods, including Gibbs sampling, variational Bayesian methods, and maximum-expectation Indian Buffet process (MEIBP).

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to scale to large datasets and automatically infer the number of latent features. The authors also provide a novel interpretation of the FAB optimization as a convex concave procedure (CCCP), which leads to an acceleration of the algorithm. The paper is well-written, and the methodology sections are reasonably clear.

However, there are some limitations and areas for improvement. The experimental evaluation is not thorough, and there are some flaws in the design and presentation of the experiments. For example, the MEIBP baseline was inappropriately used, and the IBP Gibbs method should have been given the known noise value in the block images experiments. Additionally, the behavior of the algorithm in small-data regimes is not explored or discussed.

Furthermore, the paper lacks a discussion of algorithmic limitations and cases where it may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling. There is also no discussion of potential convergence difficulties for the EM-like procedure. The method is limited to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.

In terms of related work, the paper is missing some recent studies on efficient inference for LFMs. The authors should also consider discussing the MAD-Bayes method (ICML 2013) and other small-variance asymptotic work for nonparametric LFMs.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective method for model selection in LFMs. While there are some limitations and areas for improvement, the paper is well-written and provides a valuable contribution to the field.
