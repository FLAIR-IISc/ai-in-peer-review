- Extends the FAB inference framework to latent feature models (LFMs) by showing the FIC retains the mixture-model form asymptotically.  
- Overcomes prior requirement of a block-diagonal Hessian for FAB by providing an asymptotic analysis applicable to LFMs.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method derived from a CCCP interpretation of FAB updates.  
- Establishes parameter identifiability for FAB/LFMs and proposes a merge post-processing step.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: derivation of the FIC for LFMs and rigorous arguments.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrating computational efficiency and competitive predictive performance.  
- Clear connections to prior work (FAB, IBP, variational Bayes) and motivation addressing computational challenges in LFM model selection.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparison against strong baselines (fast Gibbs IBP, VB, MEIBP).  
- Writing is clear and well-organized; supplementary material needed for full derivations.  
- Technical soundness is high; derivations appear rigorous and logically presented.  
- Contribution feels somewhat incremental since FIC framework itself is not novel.  
- Limited insight into why the FIC form is preserved beyond the asymptotic Hessian analysis.  
- Performance gains in experiments are positive but not consistently large.  
- Ablation studies insufficient—impact of shrinkage acceleration and merge steps should be isolated.  
- Sensitivity to the fixed noise standard deviation (0.75) in real-data experiments needs discussion.  
- Assumptions underlying Lemma 1 should be discussed for their impact on applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs, efficient CCCP-based acceleration, identifiability treatment, strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, uneven performance gains, and incomplete ablation/sensitivity analyses.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, well-executed work suitable for acceptance with minor revisions addressing the noted points.
