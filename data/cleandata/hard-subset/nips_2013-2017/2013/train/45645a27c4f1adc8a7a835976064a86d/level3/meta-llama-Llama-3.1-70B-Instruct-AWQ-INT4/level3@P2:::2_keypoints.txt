- Proposes extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by deriving a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.  
- Achieves more automatic model selection for LFMs with minimal hand tuning.  
- Derives an FIC with the same form as for mixture models despite LFMs not meeting FAB’s usual conditions.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured and builds logically on prior FAB/FIC work.  
- Provides thorough empirical evaluation against many competing methods, including state-of-the-art IBP approaches.  
- Demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Method combines several approximations: FIC, mean-field inference, and accelerated feature shrinkage.  
- Major concern: lacks discussion of when and how the combined approximations may fail (missing failure modes).  
- Weakness: no theoretical analysis of robustness to model misspecification or non-standard data distributions.  
- Suggested additional evaluations: model misspecification tests (true model not an LFM), non-Gaussian/outlier-prone data, and very high-dimensional feature settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective automatic model selection and strong empirical and computational results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis/discussion of limitations and robustness; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing these concerns.
