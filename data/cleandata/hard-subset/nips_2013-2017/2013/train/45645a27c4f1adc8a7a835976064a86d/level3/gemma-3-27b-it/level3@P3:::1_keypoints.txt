- Proposes a Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference method for latent feature models (LFMs), extending FAB to cases where the Hessian condition fails.  
- Derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs resembling the mixture-model form, enabling an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties.  
- Empirically reports improved predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes versus several IBP implementations, attributed to the model-selection criterion and avoidance of local optima.  
- Positions the work within existing FAB, LFM, IBP, variational inference, and MAD-Bayes literature, acknowledging differences in asymptotic assumptions.  
- Strength: Sensible, well-motivated extension of FAB to LFMs with a key derivation of FIC despite violated FAB conditions.  
- Strength: Provides theoretical motivation via asymptotic arguments supporting the proposed method.  
- Strength: Demonstrates empirical gains in predictive performance and computational speed over existing methods.  
- Strength: Discusses parameter identifiability and introduces a merge post-processing step to address non-uniqueness.  
- Strength: Proposes a CCCP interpretation and shrinkage acceleration to improve computational efficiency.  
- Weakness: Mathematical exposition is imprecise in places, raising concerns about validity (e.g., equation (3) when all z are zero).  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 lacks clear specification of the asymptotic regime and how K scales with N, conflicting with IBP’s infinite-feature motivation.  
- Weakness: Minor formal errors (conditioning in section 2, missing expectation in equation (4)) reduce clarity and correctness.  
- Weakness: Experimental analysis is insufficiently detailed—stopping criteria for FAB, truncation level for Gibbs sampler, and other implementation details are unspecified.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether improvements arise from the model-selection criterion or from algorithms avoiding poor local optima; Gibbs sampler behavior suggests possible implementation/escaping issues.  
- Weakness: Contribution is incremental—an extension of existing FAB work rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Quality: Technically sound overall but mathematical presentation needs greater precision; claims are supported but require deeper analysis to interpret gains.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized, but derivations and implementation/hyperparameter details need clearer presentation for reproducibility.  
- Originality: Novel combination and extension of FAB, EM, and LFM ideas, but core concepts are not entirely new.  
- Significance: Potentially significant if computational and predictive improvements hold under rigorous analysis; applicability to high-dimensional latent feature discovery is promising.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Reasonable FAB-to-LFM extension, empirical improvements, and theoretical justification.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Imprecise mathematics, unspecified asymptotic assumptions, insufficient experimental rigor, and an incremental contribution.  
- Overall assessment: Sensible, well-motivated, technically solid work with some imprecisions and limited experimental analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify math, asymptotics, stopping criteria, and source-of-gain analysis.
