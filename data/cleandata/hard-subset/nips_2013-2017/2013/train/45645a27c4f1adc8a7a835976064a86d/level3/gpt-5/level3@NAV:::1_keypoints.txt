- Main contribution: Extends FAB inference to binary latent feature models, deriving a factorized information criterion (FIC) and an EM-like algorithm with CCCP-inspired shrinkage.  
- Key idea: Uses BIC-style Laplace integration to produce explicit complexity penalties of the form (D/2) log sum_n z_nk that drive automatic feature selection.  
- Empirical claim: Reports improved held-out/predictive likelihoods and faster convergence versus several IBP implementations.  
- Relation to prior work: Positioned as an extension of FAB for mixtures/HMMs and related to IBP/VB and MAD-Bayes asymptotics.  
- Theoretical underspecification: Theorem 2 lacks a stated asymptotic regime/assumptions (e.g., K fixed vs. scaling with N, behavior of D), conflicting with IBP’s infinite-K motivation.  
- Finite-ness concern: Equation (3)’s log sum_n z_nk can diverge when a feature is never or always active; derivation should state conditions ensuring finiteness.  
- Lemma 1 unclear: “Mild assumptions” not specified and the domain for the log det F(d) claim needs precise statement.  
- Minor formal errors: Conditioning in Sec.2 p_k(X | z·,k) is incorrect and Eq.(4) seems to miss an expectation.  
- Clarity issues: Mathematical exposition is imprecise in places—need exact asymptotic regime, spelled-out lemma assumptions, and consistent notation/conditioning.  
- Originality: Novel but incremental—mainly an application of existing FAB techniques to LFMs with Hessian analysis and CCCP-based acceleration.  
- Significance/experimental issues: Experimental protocols ambiguous—no explicit stopping criteria or matched runtime budgets; Gibbs truncation choices seem arbitrary.  
- Ambiguity in source of gains: Unclear if improvements come from the model-selection penalty or better avoidance of local optima.  
- Missing ablations: Experiments should include FAB-penalty ablation, CCCP on/off, multiple restarts, and time-matched comparisons to strong IBP/VB baselines.  
- Pros: Principled automatic feature selection, practical EM-like algorithm with shrinkage acceleration, and useful identifiability/merging discussion.  
- Cons: Asymptotic assumptions underspecified, derivation gaps/minor formal errors, and unclear/insufficiently controlled experiments.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject — promising work but needs tighter theory and more rigorous, controlled experiments.
