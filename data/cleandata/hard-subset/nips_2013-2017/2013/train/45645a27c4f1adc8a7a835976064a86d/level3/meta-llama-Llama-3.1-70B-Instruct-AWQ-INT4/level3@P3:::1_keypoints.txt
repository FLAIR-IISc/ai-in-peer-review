- Proposes a novel model-selection criterion (FAB/LFMs) for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters via a BIC-like Factorized Information Criterion (FIC).
- Derives an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties that yields improved held-out/predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes vs several IBP implementations.
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, essentially extending prior FAB work to latent feature models.
- Mathematical exposition is mostly clear but contains imprecise points (e.g., equation (3) may be undefined if z is all zeros, raising FIC validity questions).
- Main theoretical claim: the FIC for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models and can be approximated similarly despite LFMs not meeting original FIC conditions.
- Experimental results show FAB/LFMs achieves strong predictive performance, good model-selection, and low computational cost; unique regularization reduces overfitting.
- Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, notably how K (number of features) scales with N.
- BIC-based approximation appears to require fixed finite K, conflicting with the infinite-K motivation of the IBP.
- Runtime comparisons are undermined by unspecified stopping criteria and arbitrary choices for Gibbs sampler truncation.
- Unclear whether predictive gains come from the model-selection criterion itself or from algorithms avoiding poorer local optima.
- The Gibbs sampler tends to learn too many components, suggesting implementation or local-optima issues in baselines.
- Paper is technically sound overall but would benefit from clearer, more precise mathematical derivations and consistent notation.
- Experimental analysis is promising but insufficiently comprehensive to fully interpret reported gains.
- Originality: the extension of FAB to LFMs is new, though incremental relative to prior FAB literature.
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem and could be widely applicable if clarified and validated further.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel FAB extension, promising speed/selection/prediction improvements, addresses a challenging problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: imprecise mathematics, unclear asymptotic assumptions, and incomplete experimental validation.
- Recommendations: clarify derivations and notation, specify asymptotic regime and K-scaling assumptions, and run more thorough experiments with transparent stopping/truncation criteria.
