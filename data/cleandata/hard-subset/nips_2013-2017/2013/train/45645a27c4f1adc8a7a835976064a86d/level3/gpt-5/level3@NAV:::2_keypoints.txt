- Extends FAB/FIC from mixture/HMM settings to latent feature models (LFMs) with an asymptotic Hessian analysis showing FIC retains the same log sum_n z_nk penalty despite prior block-diagonal violations.

- Proposes a practical algorithm combining a tractable lower bound, mean-field inference over Z, a CCCP interpretation of the E-step, accelerated feature-shrinkage, and a merge post-processing step.

- Provides an identifiability analysis for the proposed model and introduces a merge procedure to handle redundancy.

- Empirically outperforms or matches IBP, VB, and MEIBP baselines on model selection and prediction, and often yields notable speedups on synthetic and multiple real datasets.

- Technical development appears sound with clear derivations and monotone bound optimization; CCCP view justifies shrinkage acceleration.

- Experiments are thorough, compare to strong baselines, and follow sensible evaluation protocols.

- Main weakness: Lemma 1 depends on “mild conditions” that are not explicitly listed; needs clearer assumptions, diagnostics, and guidance on finite-sample behavior.

- Clarity is good overall; minor requests include explicitly listing Lemma 1 assumptions, clarifying whether accelerateShrinkage preserves monotonicity, and reporting sensitivity to Tshrink and initialization.

- Originality: novel and effective synthesis of known components (FIC/FAB, mean-field, CCCP) plus new Hessian asymptotics, shrinkage acceleration, and merge step.

- Significance: provides practical, low-tuning automatic model selection with computational benefits relevant beyond IBP-based methods.

- Pros for acceptance: principled theoretical extension, automatic model selection, identifiable solutions, strong empirical results and speedups, clear writing.

- Cons/concerns: limited analysis of failure modes when approximations interact, potential degradation in small-N or highly correlated-feature regimes, and limited ablations/convergence guarantees for acceleration.

- Overall recommendation: strong, well-executed paper; recommend acceptance after addressing failure-mode analysis and clarifying assumptions.
