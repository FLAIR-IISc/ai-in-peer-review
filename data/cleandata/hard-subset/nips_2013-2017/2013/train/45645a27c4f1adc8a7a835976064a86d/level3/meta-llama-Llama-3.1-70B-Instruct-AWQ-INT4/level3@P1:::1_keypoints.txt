- Proposes extending the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) for automatic model selection and parameter estimation.  
- Derives a new Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs, showing it has the same form as the FIC for mixture models.  
- Shows this FIC result holds despite LFMs not meeting FAB’s usual conditions, extending FAB’s applicability.  
- Presents an EM-based optimization algorithm for the FIC tailored to LFMs.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method that removes irrelevant features early to reduce computation.  
- Empirically compares the method to state-of-the-art approaches (IBP, VB) across multiple datasets.  
- Experimental results claim better model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency than IBP and VB.  
- Main contribution is the theoretical derivation of FIC for LFMs, enabling FAB use in a broader class of models.  
- Mathematical exposition is imprecise in places and some assumptions (e.g., asymptotic regime, scaling of K with N) are not clearly specified.  
- Lacks clarity on whether predictive gains stem from the model-selection criterion itself or from the algorithm avoiding poor local optima.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, building on prior FAB inference work.  
- Paper is technically sound overall but needs clearer mathematical details (Quality: 7/10).  
- Well-organized and mostly clear presentation, though some derivations are hard to follow (Clarity: 8/10).  
- Originality rated moderate due to incremental nature (Originality: 6/10); significance considered high because of practical impact on model selection in LFMs (Significance: 8/10).  
- Acceptance arguments: novel FAB-to-LFM extension, strong empirical performance, and clear explanation of approach.  
- Rejection arguments: imprecise math/assumptions, unclear source of predictive improvements, and incremental novelty.
