- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming prior applicability issues.  
- Shows LFMs yield the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as mixture models, enabling FAB for LFMs.  
- Proposes an FAB/LFM algorithm with a shrinkage-acceleration technique.  
- Provides theoretical analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) including parameter identifiability results.  
- Empirical results show improved model selection accuracy and computational efficiency versus IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Theoretical contribution is interesting but mathematically imprecise in places.  
- Equation (3) may be problematic since log(∑n znk) can be −∞ if all znk are zero, raising FIC validity concerns.  
- Theorem 2 does not specify the asymptotic regime or how K scales with N, conflicting with IBP’s infinite-K motivation.  
- Authors should clarify asymptotic assumptions and K-scaling in their theory.  
- Experimental runtime comparisons lack sufficient detail about stopping criteria, reducing their credibility.  
- Minor formal errors: Section 2’s conditioning statement is incorrect and equation (4) is missing an expectation.  
- Supplementary materials appear to contain rigorous proofs that address many theoretical concerns.  
- Contribution is novel in applying FAB to LFMs but is incremental relative to prior FAB work.  
- Shrinkage acceleration is a useful practical contribution, though its CCCP interpretation is not entirely new.  
- Significance is practical for large-scale LFMs—better predictive likelihood and efficiency—but it’s unclear if gains come from the criterion or algorithmic avoidance of bad local optima.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify theoretical assumptions, fix formal errors, and provide more experimental details.
