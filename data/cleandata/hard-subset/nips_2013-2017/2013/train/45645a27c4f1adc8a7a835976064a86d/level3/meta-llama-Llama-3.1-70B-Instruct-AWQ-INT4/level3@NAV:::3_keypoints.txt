- Summary: Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), auto-inferring feature count and compared to IBP methods.

- Strength: Novel extension of FAB to LFMs, a significant methodological contribution.

- Strength: Efficient inference that automatically determines the number of latent features and scales to large datasets.

- Strength: Empirical results show FAB/LFM outperforms IBP baselines on synthetic, block, and real-world data.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited and includes invalid comparisons (e.g., MEIBP with non-negative W).

- Weakness: Paper lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations, such as convergence issues or cases where Gibbs sampling may perform better.

- Weakness: Method is restricted to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.

- Weakness: Insufficient experimental detail (e.g., number of restarts for variational Bayesian methods).

- Suggestion: Perform more thorough experiments and compare to additional baselines (e.g., MAD-Bayes).

- Suggestion: Add discussion of algorithmic limitations and potential failure modes versus Gibbs sampling.

- Suggestion: Explore extending the method to other models like factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.

- Suggestion: Provide full experimental setup details, including VB restarts and other hyperparameter choices.

- Acceptance: Recommend acceptance conditional on addressing the listed weaknesses and suggestions.
