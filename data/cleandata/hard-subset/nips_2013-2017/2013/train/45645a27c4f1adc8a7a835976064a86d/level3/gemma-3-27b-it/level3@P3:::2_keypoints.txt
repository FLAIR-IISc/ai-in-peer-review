- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), addressing a prior limitation.
- Shows FIC can be applied to LFMs via asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).
- Provides a principled automated model selection mechanism for latent feature dimensionality.
- Introduces shrinkage acceleration (CCCP-inspired) that yields substantial computational speedups.
- Thorough empirical evaluation against IBP variants and variational methods on synthetic and real data.
- Demonstrates strong performance in model selection accuracy and predictive performance versus strong baselines.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and builds naturally on prior FAB/FIC work.
- Includes identifiability analysis and a useful merging post-processing step to address parameter non-uniqueness.
- Theoretical contributions appear technically sound and convincingly presented.
- Major weakness: insufficient discussion of potential failure modes of combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage).
- Unclear sensitivity to violations of Hessian analysis assumptions and when the log-determinant approximation fails.
- Some parameters (e.g., δ, Tshrink) still require tuning; sensitivity analysis is lacking.
- Lacks a detailed breakdown of computational costs per algorithmic step (E-step, M-step, shrinkage).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid extension; computational gains; automated model selection; thorough experiments; clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of approximation failure modes; remaining parameter tuning needed.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, accept with minor revisions to add limitations/failure-mode discussion and mitigation suggestions.
