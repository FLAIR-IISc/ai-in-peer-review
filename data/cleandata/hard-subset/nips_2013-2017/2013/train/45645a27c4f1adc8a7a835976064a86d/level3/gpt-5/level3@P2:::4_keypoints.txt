- Extends FAB inference from mixtures/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs) via an asymptotic Hessian analysis that yields Lemma 1 and an FIC with the same form as mixtures.  
- Main theoretical result: log det|F(d)| gives the same log sum_n z_nk complexity term despite non-block-diagonal Hessian, enabling asymptotic identifiability and automatic feature shrinkage.  
- Algorithm: mean-field FAB lower bound with E-step fixed-point update containing a D/(2Nπ̃_k) shrinkage term, closed-form M-step updates, and a shrinkage step that removes always-on/off features.  
- E-step reinterpretation as CCCP permits multiple inner iterations to accelerate shrinkage; a post-hoc merge step is motivated by an identifiability theorem to combine redundant features.  
- Empirics: compares to IBP (Gibbs), IBP-VB, and MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets; FAB is strong in model selection and predictive log-likelihood and competitive in runtime.  
- Theory quality: Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 are the main contributions, appear sound, and proofs are provided in the supplement.  
- Optimization quality: lower-bound optimization is carefully constructed with monotonic improvement for stated updates; CCCP interpretation is appropriate.  
- Caveat: simultaneous updates of π and π̃ during accelerated E-step need a brief convergence argument (empirical improvements asserted).  
- Empirics quality: comparisons are broad and fair, with discussion of small-N regimes where FIC may be less accurate.  
- Concern: baseline configurations may be unfair because FAB learns noise variance Λ while some baselines fix noise; sensitivity analysis or allowing baselines to learn/tune noise is recommended.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized and clear, with core ideas, derivations, and algorithmic steps explained.  
- Readability suggestions: state all assumptions for Lemma 1 in the main text and clarify dependence of Op(1) terms.  
- Complexity suggestion: provide per-iteration computational complexity and memory usage in terms of N, D, K, and Tshrink.  
- Hyperparameter guidance: offer advice on choosing Tshrink and δ, and clarify merge decision thresholds and their effect on the lower bound.  
- Monotonicity: explicitly state that multiple CCCP E-step updates maintain monotonicity of the lower bound under the proposed schedule.  
- Originality: novel and insightful that FIC for LFMs matches mixtures despite non-block-diagonal Hessian; CCCP acceleration and merge steps are useful if incremental.  
- Significance: advances automated model selection for LFMs with a theoretically grounded, efficient method; broader generalization beyond linear-Gaussian/Bernoulli would increase impact.  
- Pros: strong theoretical result, practical monotone algorithm, automatic feature selection, principled merge step, solid empirical performance, and clear writing.  
- Cons: key assumptions and accelerated-step convergence mainly relegated to supplement, potential unfairness in baseline noise treatment, limited exploration beyond linear-Gaussian models, and small-N behavior not deeply characterized.  
- Requests for authors: summarize Lemma 1 assumptions in main text; provide per-iteration/total complexity and wall-clock vs K during shrinkage; run sensitivity analyses for Tshrink, δ, and initialization; let baselines learn or tune noise variance; clarify merge-step guarantees and tolerances.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (confidence 4/5).
