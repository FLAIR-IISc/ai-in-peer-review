This paper presents a novel extension of the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to latent feature models (LFMs). The authors derive a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) that is applicable to LFMs, despite the fact that LFMs do not satisfy the block-diagonal Hessian condition required by previous FAB frameworks. The derived FIC for LFMs has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough derivation of the FIC for LFMs. The empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrates the superior predictive performance and lower computational cost of FAB/LFM compared to state-of-the-art methods such as fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet processes, variational Bayes, and maximum-expectation Indian Buffet processes.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The derivation of the FIC for LFMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The empirical evaluation, which demonstrates the superior performance of FAB/LFM.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The contribution is somewhat incremental, as it builds upon previous work on FAB inference.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research.

Overall, I believe that the paper is a strong contribution to the field and deserves to be accepted. The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of FAB/LFM in a range of experiments, and the derivation of the FIC for LFMs is a significant contribution to the field.
