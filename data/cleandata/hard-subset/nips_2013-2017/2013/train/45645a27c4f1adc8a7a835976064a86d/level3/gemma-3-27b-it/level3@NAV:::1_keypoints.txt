- Proposes extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing the block-diagonal Hessian limitation.  
- Derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs and an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties.  
- Empirically shows faster convergence and better predictive likelihoods versus several IBP implementations on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Theoretical approach builds on existing FAB theory and provides a plausible asymptotic argument for LFMs.  
- Motivation to automate latent dimensionality selection and avoid manual hyperparameter tuning is clearly stated.  
- Connection to BIC offers rationale for complexity penalties; CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration is a valuable technique.  
- Mathematical exposition lacks precision; Equation (3) may be invalid when all elements of z are zero (potential infinite log-sum).  
- Theorem 2’s asymptotic regime and scaling assumptions are underspecified—unclear how K (latent features) scales with N, conflicting with BIC vs IBP frameworks.  
- Minor technical errors noted (conditioning issue in section 2; missing expectation in Equation (4)).  
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete: FAB stopping criteria are undefined, making runtime and truncation comparisons arbitrary.  
- Unclear whether predictive gains stem from the model selection criterion or from FAB avoiding Gibbs sampler local optima.  
- Gibbs sampling’s tendency to learn too many components raises concerns about implementation or local-optima issues.  
- Overall assessment: sensible, incremental, and promising work but hampered by mathematical rigor gaps and insufficient experimental detail.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel FAB-to-LFM extension, improved empirical performance, and sound theoretical motivation.  
- Cons against acceptance: imprecise mathematics, underspecified asymptotics, and incomplete/unclear experimental analysis.
