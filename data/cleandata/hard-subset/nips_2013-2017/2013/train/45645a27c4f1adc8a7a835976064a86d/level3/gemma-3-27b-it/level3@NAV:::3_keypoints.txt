- Introduces a Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference algorithm for binary Latent Feature Models (LFMs).  
- Extends FAB applicability by showing the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has a form similar to mixture models.  
- Enables automatic latent feature selection and parameter identifiability without priors, offering an alternative to IBP.  
- Core technical contribution: adapting FAB to LFMs to allow efficient inference and automatic dimensionality estimation.  
- Sound theoretical derivation, plus a proposed shrinkage-acceleration method and discussion of identifiability.  
- Empirical claims: FAB outperforms Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP on some datasets in runtime and inference quality; scalable and auto-selects K.  
- Novelty: first application/extension of FAB beyond mixtures/HMMs to LFMs.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently detailed — missing setup, hyperparameter tuning, and baseline configurations hindering reproducibility.  
- Baseline comparisons are flawed: MEIBP misapplied (non-negativity constraint), IBP Gibbs disadvantaged in one experiment, and the accelerated Gibbs Sonar result looks suspect.  
- Paper omits discussion of limitations such as restriction to binary LFMs and potential convergence issues of the EM-like algorithm.  
- Related work is incompletely cited — missing MAD-Bayes and other recent efficient LFM inference methods.  
- Contains minor errors (incorrect paper description, misstated standard deviation) that need correction.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel application, solid theory, promising empirical gains, scalability, and automatic K selection.  
- Cons against acceptance: weak/partial experiments, missing limitations and related work, and minor but important errors.  
- Recommendation: reject with request for major revisions; authors must fix references, include MAD-Bayes, and substantially improve experiments and explanations.
