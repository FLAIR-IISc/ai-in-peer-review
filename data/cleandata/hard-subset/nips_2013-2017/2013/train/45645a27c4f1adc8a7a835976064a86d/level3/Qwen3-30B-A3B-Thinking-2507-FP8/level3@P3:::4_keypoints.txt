- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming a prior limitation.  
- Proves LFMs yield the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as mixture models despite violating the block-diagonal Hessian assumption.  
- Key lemma: log det |F(d)| = sum_k log(∑_n z_nk)/N + O_p(1), which introduces the necessary log(∑_n z_nk) term for FIC.  
- Contribution 1: Derives an FIC applicable to LFMs even when the Hessian condition is violated.  
- Contribution 2: Shows FIC for LFMs has the same representation as for mixture models.  
- Contribution 3: Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method motivated by the CCCP interpretation of FAB E-steps.  
- Contribution 4: Demonstrates parameter identifiability in FAB/LFMs and introduces a merge post-processing algorithm.  
- Empirical results: FAB/LFMs outperform fast Gibbs IBP, variational Bayes, and MEIBP in predictive performance and computational cost on synthetic and real data.  
- Theoretical rigor: Asymptotic analysis is thorough and supported by proofs in the supplement.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear derivations and a well-explained FAB–CCCP connection.  
- Originality: Meaningful technical extension of prior FAB work; non-trivial insight that LFMs share FIC form with mixtures.  
- Significance: Enables automatic feature selection and scalable Bayesian inference for LFMs, useful for large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: Contribution is somewhat incremental—an extension rather than a fundamentally new framework.  
- Weakness: Asymptotic approximations may be inaccurate for small N, acknowledged but still a limitation.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of sensitivity to shrinkage-acceleration/hyperparameter choices.  
- Pro arguments: Broadens FAB applicability, offers theoretical insights (CCCP, identifiability), and shows empirical and computational advantages.  
- Con arguments: Incremental advance and limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
