- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models, introducing a model-selection criterion.  
- Derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) by integrating out parameters with a BIC-like approximation.  
- Produces an EM-like deterministic algorithm with an inherent complexity penalty that automatically prunes inactive latent features.  
- Frames a deterministic alternative to nonparametric stochastic methods such as the Indian Buffet Process (IBP).  
- Empirically shows improved held-out predictive likelihoods and substantially faster runtimes versus several IBP implementations.  
- Practical strength: well-motivated, principled model selection and effective automatic feature pruning.  
- Contribution is incremental — a fairly direct application of existing FAB methodology to a new model class.  
- Mathematical exposition has imprecisions (e.g., FIC in Eq. (3) may be ill-defined if a feature is never used).  
- Theorem 2 omits the asymptotic regime specification; unclear how the number of features K scales with data size N.  
- BIC-like derivation assumes fixed K, conflicting with the infinite-feature motivation of IBP baselines.  
- Experimental validation is inconclusive: baseline stopping criteria and Gibbs truncation are unspecified, and gains may reflect avoidance of local optima rather than superiority of FIC.
