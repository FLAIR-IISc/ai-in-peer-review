## Review of “Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models”

This paper presents an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference, originally developed for mixture and hidden Markov models, to the realm of Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The core contribution lies in overcoming a previous limitation of FAB – the requirement of a block-diagonal Hessian of the complete likelihood – which LFMs do not naturally satisfy. The authors demonstrate, through asymptotic analysis, that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs maintains the same representation as that for mixture models, enabling the application of FAB. They further showcase the method’s benefits, including automatic hidden state selection and parameter identifiability, alongside improved performance compared to existing methods like Gibbs sampling, Variational Bayes, and MEIBP, both in terms of predictive accuracy and computational efficiency.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and technically sound. The derivation steps are thoroughly presented, making the theoretical contributions readily accessible. The connection to existing work on FIC and its application to mixture models is clearly articulated, providing valuable context. The inclusion of a shrinkage acceleration method, inspired by a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) analysis, and the discussion of parameter identifiability further strengthens the contribution. The empirical validation on both synthetic and real datasets provides compelling evidence of FAB/LFM’s effectiveness. The results consistently demonstrate superior predictive performance and computational advantages. 

While the contribution is somewhat incremental – building upon the established FAB framework – it is nonetheless interesting and relevant to the NIPS community. Extending the applicability of FAB to LFMs addresses a practical limitation and opens avenues for its use in a broader range of modeling scenarios. The paper effectively bridges theoretical advancements with empirical validation, solidifying its contribution. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** Rigorous derivation and theoretical justification.
*   **Practical Relevance:** Extends FAB to a relevant model class (LFMs) with demonstrated performance gains.
*   **Clarity:** Exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Strong results on both synthetic and real datasets.

**Minor Considerations:**

*   The incremental nature of the contribution should be acknowledged. While valuable, it doesn't represent a paradigm shift.
*   The reliance on specific parameter settings for comparison methods (e.g., α for IBP) should be carefully discussed, acknowledging potential sensitivity.



Overall, I recommend acceptance. This paper presents a well-executed and valuable extension to the FAB framework, addressing a practical limitation and demonstrating significant benefits for Latent Feature Modeling.
