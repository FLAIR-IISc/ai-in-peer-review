- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixtures/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Derives a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) applicable to LFMs despite non–block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Shows the FIC for LFMs has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models.  
- Empirically compares FAB/LFM to fast Gibbs IBP, variational Bayes, and MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Reports superior predictive performance and lower computational cost for FAB/LFM relative to baselines.  
- Provides thorough derivation steps, clear methodology, and comprehensive experimental design.  
- Presents theoretical results including FIC derivation and proof of parameter identifiability.  
- Notes FAB/LFM can automatically select the number of latent features and has a regularizing effect.  
- Strength: significant and well-executed extension of FAB inference to a broader model class.  
- Strength: rigorous derivations, solid theoretical insights, and comprehensive empirical evaluation.  
- Weakness: contribution is somewhat incremental, building on prior FAB work.  
- Weakness: paper could discuss limitations and future research directions in more detail.  
- Originality: novel and original derivation of FIC for LFMs.  
- Significance: broadens FAB applicability and demonstrates practical efficiency and accuracy gains.  
- Clarity and related work: well-written, consistent notation, and good review/motivation of prior work.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication at NIPS.
