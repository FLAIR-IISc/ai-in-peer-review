- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming prior limitations.
- Identifies the problem: LFMs violate the FAB requirement of block-diagonal Hessian due to shared parameters across features.
- Provides novel asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) showing the log-determinant yields the same complexity term as in mixture models.
- Derives an FIC for LFMs with the same form as mixture-model FIC, enabling FAB applicability to LFMs.
- Results in automatic model selection that prunes irrelevant latent features via a model-induced regularizer (log(sum z_nk)).
- Demonstrates parameter identifiability and proposes a practical merging post-processing step.
- Introduces a shrinkage-acceleration technique from a CCCP interpretation of the E-step to speed convergence.
- Thorough empirical validation against strong baselines (fast Gibbs IBP, VB-IBP, MEIBP) across synthetic and real datasets.
- Shows superior predictive log-likelihood, more compact selected K, and substantially lower computational cost than baselines.
- Ablation study confirms the practical benefit of the shrinkage acceleration (Figure 1).
- High technical quality: non-trivial, sound theoretical derivations and well-supported claims.
- Exceptionally clear writing, logical presentation of derivations, and transparent experimental setup.
- Novel and significant: solves an open problem extending FAB beyond block-diagonal Hessian models and may inspire broader asymptotic Bayesian applications.
- Limitation: asymptotic approach can degrade in small-N scenarios, acknowledged by the authors.
- Possible counterargument: contribution could be viewed as incremental extension of FAB, though non-trivial and impactful.
