- Paper extends FAB/FIC to latent feature models (binary features), deriving an FIC with the same form as mixtures and an EM-like FAB algorithm that prunes features automatically.
- Main theoretical contribution: asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1) leading to Theorem 2; log det yields ∑k log ∑n z_nk complexity penalties.
- E-step related to a convex-concave procedure (CCCP); authors propose shrinkage-acceleration and a merge post-processing; Theorem 4 gives an identifiability result favoring balanced/merged features.
- Experiments compare FAB to IBP baselines (Gibbs, variational, MEIBP) and claim advantages in model selection, prediction, and runtime.
- Theory is plausible and important but relies on “mild assumptions” relegated to the supplement; these assumptions and asymptotic regime must be summarized in the main text (N→∞, behavior of Z, regularity of Λ/W, dependence on K,D).
- Provide intuition for why log det F(d) decomposes into ∑k log ∑n z_nk and clarify whether Op(1) constants depend on K,D.
- Algorithmic derivation (mean-field on Z, CCCP) is sound; the FIC-derived shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃_k) explains automatic pruning.
- Merge post-processing is reasonable under Theorem 4 but its behavior for near-collinearity (not exact equality) is unanalyzed.
- Empirical results are promising but weakened by methodological issues: unfair baselines, incomplete reporting, and suspicious experimental outcomes.
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, but should: state Lemma 1 assumptions/main asymptotic regime in the main text and discuss small-N limitations.
- Need practical guidance on convergence: number of CCCP inner steps, restarts, initialization sensitivity, and stopping criteria for merge().
- Report computational complexity per iteration and empirical scaling in N, D, K.
- Expand experimental details: restarts per method, VB-IBP truncation levels, priors/hyperparameters, noise handling, and how K is initialized/updated across methods.
- Novelty is strong: nontrivial extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessians; CCCP acceleration and identifiability/merge analysis are useful.
- Related work coverage should explicitly compare FIC-based regularization to small-variance asymptotics/MAD-Bayes and include recent scalable LFM inference and MAP methods; correct characterization of MEIBP as MAP/submodular.
- Significance is high if asymptotic results are robust: a simple, hyperparameter-free, fast algorithm with automatic feature selection could be widely adopted.
- Major methodological concerns: MEIBP comparisons invalid when nonnegativity is violated; ensure fairness by matching constraints or restricting comparisons.
- For synthetic experiments, use true noise std across methods; for VB-IBP give truncation and restart details; investigate strange Gibbs results (e.g., too many features on low-D data).
- Add experiments/discussion for small-N regimes to show where FAB underperforms and why.
- Discuss convergence to local optima, need for multiple restarts, and any annealing/tempering to improve robustness.
- Analyze stability of merge criterion for near-duplicate features and provide empirical evidence on real data.
- Minor corrections: fix MEIBP description, cite MAD-Bayes and small-variance LFM work, verify recommended real-data noise settings, and tighten Lemma 1 assumptions in the main text.
- Pros: novel asymptotic extension, hyperparameter-free automatic feature selection, practical CCCP/shrinkage acceleration, promising empirical performance.
- Cons: incomplete/misaligned baselines, limited analysis of small-N regimes and convergence, missing related-work citations and unstated theory assumptions.
- Recommendation: Weak reject — compelling theory and algorithm but requires stronger/cleaner empirical validation, clearer assumptions/limitations, and corrected/expanded related work.
