- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by generalizing the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) and removing restrictive Hessian assumptions.  
- Introduces a mean-field approximation and accelerated feature-shrinkage for automated, computationally efficient model selection.  
- Empirically outperforms Indian Buffet Process (IBP) and Variational Bayesian (VB) baselines on synthetic and real datasets in model selection, prediction, and runtime.  
- Major theoretical contribution: derives FIC for LFMs and shows equivalence to mixture-model FIC despite LFMs violating original FAB assumptions.  
- Practical utility: removes need for manual hyperparameter tuning and enables automatic latent feature selection.  
- Computational efficiency: accelerated shrinkage mechanism substantially reduces computation in experiments.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation across multiple datasets, demonstrating superior performance and efficiency.  
- Clear writing, logical structure, and adequate referencing increase accessibility of the work.  
- Additional theoretical analysis: discusses parameter identifiability and proposes post-processing (e.g., merging latent features) to improve robustness.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of when mean-field and other approximations fail, particularly for small samples or complex distributions.  
- Weakness: scalability to extremely large datasets and very high-dimensional feature spaces is not thoroughly evaluated.  
- Weakness: lacks sensitivity analysis for key parameters (e.g., latent-feature initialization) and their effects on convergence/performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a challenging problem, offers a novel theoretically supported extension, and shows strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of approximation limitations and unclear scalability may hinder reproducibility and applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept, but include a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential failure cases.
