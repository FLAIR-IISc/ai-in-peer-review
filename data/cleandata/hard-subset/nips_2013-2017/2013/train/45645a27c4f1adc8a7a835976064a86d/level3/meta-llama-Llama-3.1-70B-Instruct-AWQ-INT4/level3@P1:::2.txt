The paper "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models" proposes an extension of the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference method to latent feature models (LFMs). The authors derive a new form of the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs, which is similar to the FIC for mixture models. They also develop an accelerated feature shrinkage method to improve computational efficiency.

The paper is well-written and well-structured, and the authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation of their method against a wide range of competing methods. The references are sufficient, and the method is a logical, solid extension of prior FAB/FIC work.

The key contributions of the paper are:

* A new form of the FIC for LFMs, which is derived using asymptotic analysis of the Hessian matrix of the log-likelihood.
* An accelerated feature shrinkage method, which is based on a convex concave procedure (CCCP) interpretation of the FAB optimization.
* A more automatic model-selection procedure, which requires little hand tuning.

The paper demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method against competing methods.

However, there are some concerns about the paper. The most significant concern is that it is unclear when or how the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) may fail. The authors should examine the failure modes and limits of the approximations to provide a more complete understanding of the method's strengths and weaknesses.
