This paper proposes a generalization of factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference and the factorized information criterion (FIC) to latent feature models (LFMs) using mean-field inference and accelerated feature shrinkage. The authors demonstrate that their approach produces a more automatic model-selection procedure requiring little hand-tuning, resulting in significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets.

The paper is well-written and well-structured, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning. The method is a logical, solid extension of prior FAB/FIC work, combining several approximations into a novel, effective approach. The authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation against a wide range of competing methods, including state-of-the-art Indian Buffet processes, variational Bayesian methods, and maximum-expectation IBP.

One of the key strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a more automatic model-selection procedure. The authors show that their approach can automatically select the number of latent features, eliminating the need for hand-tuning. This is a significant advantage over other methods, which often require careful tuning of hyperparameters to achieve good performance.

The paper also demonstrates significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets. The authors show that their approach can reduce computational time by several orders of magnitude compared to other methods, making it a more practical choice for large-scale datasets.

The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method, including a detailed derivation of the FIC for LFMs. They also provide a thorough analysis of the method's properties, including its ability to automatically select the number of latent features and its robustness to over-fitting.

One potential concern with the paper is the lack of a clear explanation of when and how the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage) may fail. The authors should examine failure modes and limits of the approximations to provide a more complete understanding of the method's strengths and weaknesses.

In terms of originality, the paper provides a novel combination of familiar techniques, including FAB inference, mean-field approximation, and accelerated feature shrinkage. The authors demonstrate that their approach is a significant improvement over existing methods, including state-of-the-art Indian Buffet processes and variational Bayesian methods.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The authors provide a more automatic model-selection procedure, resulting in significant computational efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets. The paper also provides a unique theoretical and pragmatic approach to latent feature models, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Overall, the paper is well-written, well-structured, and provides a significant contribution to the field of latent feature models. The authors demonstrate a clear understanding of the problem and provide a novel, effective approach to solving it.
