- Proposes an extension of the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Shows FAB can be applied to LFMs even though LFMs do not meet the condition required in mixture models.  
- Derives that the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models.  
- Claims desirable properties: automatic hidden-state selection and parameter identifiability.  
- Presentation is clear, well-structured, and includes a thorough related-work review.  
- Provides a detailed description of the proposed method and a theoretical analysis with a proof of the asymptotic approximation of the marginal complete log-likelihood.  
- Experimental results demonstrate improved computational speed, accurate model selection, and strong prediction performance.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental, building on prior FAB work.  
- Mathematical exposition has minor errors and imprecisions.  
- Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and underlying assumptions.  
- Experimental section is missing details on stopping criteria and implementation of baseline methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending fixes to mathematical precision and additional experimental implementation details.
