- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), removing a prior Hessian condition barrier.  
- Rigorous asymptotic analysis shows FIC for LFMs matches the form for mixture models, enabling automatic model selection without hand-tuning.  
- Combines mean-field inference with accelerated feature shrinkage to produce a computationally efficient algorithm.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods (IBP, VB, MEIBP) on synthetic and real datasets in model selection accuracy, prediction, and runtime.  
- Paper is clear and well-structured with well-motivated theoretical development and thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Provides comprehensive comparisons demonstrating significant computational advantages while maintaining or improving predictive performance.  
- Theoretical contributions (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) support identifiability and automatic feature selection, extending FAB’s applicability.  
- Highly original combination of FAB, mean-field approximation, and shrinkage acceleration for LFMs with appropriate related-work context.  
- Primary weakness: lacks systematic analysis of failure modes for the combined approximations (e.g., highly correlated features, unusual data distributions, extreme noise).  
- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to address the missing failure-mode analysis.
