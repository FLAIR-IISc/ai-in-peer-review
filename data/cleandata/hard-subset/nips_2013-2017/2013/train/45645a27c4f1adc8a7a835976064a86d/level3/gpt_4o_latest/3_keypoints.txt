- Novel extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models enabling automatic latent feature selection and dimensionality determination.  
- Derivation of a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs that mirrors the form used in mixture models, adapting FAB’s asymptotic properties.  
- Development of an EM-based algorithm with shrinkage acceleration and post-processing to improve computational efficiency and parameter identifiability.  
- Empirical claims that the method outperforms Gibbs sampling and variational inference in model selection accuracy, prediction performance, and runtime.  
- Explicit handling of parameter non-identifiability via a merging post-processing step.  
- Significant theoretical novelty by extending FAB beyond simpler models (MMs, HMMs) to LFMs.  
- Experimental comparisons appear unfair due to differences in initialization and noise-variance settings versus baselines.  
- Insufficient reporting of hyperparameter tuning and convergence criteria for baseline methods, hindering reproducibility and validity of comparisons.  
- Omission of recent related work (e.g., MAD-Bayes), weakening contextualization and benchmarking.  
- Method restricted to binary LFMs; limited analysis of performance in small-data regimes.  
- Minor presentation errors (mislabelled references, incorrect experimental settings) that reduce clarity.  
- Paper is overall well-organized with clear exposition of methodology and theoretical contributions.  
- Experimental section lacks sufficient detail and justification for design choices, reducing credibility.  
- High originality but needs better discussion of related work to contextualize contributions.  
- Promising significance: scalable automatic feature selection without priors/hyperparameter tuning, but scope and experimental issues limit broader applicability.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept contingent on fixing experimental fairness, adding missing references, and providing more comprehensive methodological justifications.
