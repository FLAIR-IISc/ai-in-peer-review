This paper proposes an extension of the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to latent feature models (LFMs), which have been previously addressed using non-parametric Bayesian (NPB) and variational Bayesian (VB) methods. The FAB framework is a recently-developed Bayesian approximation inference method for model selection of latent variable models. The authors demonstrate that FAB inference can be applied to LFMs, which do not satisfy the specific condition required for FAB inference in mixture models. They show that the FIC for LFMs has the same form as those for mixture models, and that FAB/LFMs offer several desirable properties, including automatic hidden states selection and parameter identifiability.

The authors provide a clear and well-structured presentation of their work, including a thorough review of related work and a detailed description of their proposed method. They also provide a theoretical analysis of their approach, including a proof of the asymptotic approximation of the marginal complete log-likelihood. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in terms of computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance.

The technical contribution of this paper is novel but incremental, building on previous work on FAB inference. The mathematical exposition is generally clear, but there are some minor errors and imprecisions, such as the lack of specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions in Theorem 2. Additionally, the experimental results could be improved by providing more details on the stopping criteria and the implementation of the compared methods.

Overall, this paper presents a sensible and well-motivated extension of the FAB framework to LFMs. While the technical contribution is incremental, the paper provides a clear and well-structured presentation of the work, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
