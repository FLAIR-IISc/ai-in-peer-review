- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming FAB’s previous block-diagonal Hessian requirement.  
- Shows LFMs admit a similar Factorized Information Criterion (FIC), enabling FAB application (Theorem 2; Lemma 1 central).  
- Introduces a CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration to speed inference.  
- Proves parameter identifiability for the proposed LFM formulation (Theorem 4).  
- Offers an efficient, automated model (dimensionality) selection method for LFMs.  
- Demonstrates competitive predictive performance and significant computational advantages over IBP and VB in experiments.  
- Paper is technically rigorous, well-written, and clearly organized with consistent notation.  
- Original combination of FIC derivation, CCCP acceleration, and identifiability analysis is novel and valuable.  
- Lacks a thorough discussion of potential failure modes and limitations of combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage).  
- Insufficient systematic sensitivity analysis of key hyperparameters (e.g., δ, Tshrink).  
- Some empirical comparisons (e.g., VB hyperparameter search) appear cursory and could be more thorough.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address approximation limitations and add systematic hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
