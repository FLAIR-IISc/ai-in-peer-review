This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models (LFMs), deriving a factorized information criterion (FIC) that mirrors the mixture-model case and yields an EM-like algorithm with a CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration. The key idea is to integrate out parameters via a BIC-style Laplace approximation, producing explicit complexity penalties of the form (D/2) log sum_n z_nk that drive automatic feature selection. Empirically, the method reportedly improves held-out/predictive likelihoods and convergence speed relative to several IBP implementations. The work is positioned as an extension of prior FAB for mixtures/HMMs and relates to IBP/VB and MAD-Bayes asymptotics.

Quality: The approach is reasonable and the asymptotic motivation is plausible, but several theoretical and empirical details are underspecified. Theorem 2 does not specify the asymptotic regime or assumptions (e.g., whether K is fixed, how K scales with N, behavior of D), despite relying on a BIC-like approximation that typically assumes fixed finite K—seemingly at odds with IBP’s infinite-K motivation. Equation (3)’s log sum_n z_nk can diverge when a feature is never or always active; while the algorithm prunes such features, the derivation should state the conditions ensuring finiteness. Lemma 1’s “mild assumptions” and the domain over which log det F(d) holds need to be spelled out. Minor formal issues: in Sec. 2, p_k(X | z·,k) should condition only on data assigned to component k; Eq. (4) appears to miss an expectation.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written, but the mathematical exposition is imprecise in places. Please specify the exact asymptotic regime, assumptions of Lemma 1, and ensure consistency of notation and conditioning.

Originality: The contribution is novel but incremental—largely an extension of prior FAB work to LFMs with a careful Hessian analysis and CCCP-based shrinkage.

Significance: Potentially useful, especially for automated model selection and identifiability, but the experimental evidence is not fully conclusive. Runtime comparisons lack explicit stopping criteria and matched budgets; Gibbs truncation choices appear arbitrary. It remains unclear whether predictive gains stem from the model-selection criterion or simply better avoidance of local optima. Ablations (e.g., FAB penalty on/off; CCCP on/off), multiple restarts, and time-matched comparisons to strong IBP/VB baselines would strengthen claims.

Pros:
- Sensible, principled criterion with automatic feature selection
- EM-like algorithm with practical shrinkage acceleration
- Identifiability discussion and merging heuristic

Cons:
- Asymptotic regime and assumptions under-specified
- Derivation gaps and minor formal errors
- Ambiguous experimental protocols; unclear source of gains

Recommendation: Borderline reject; promising but needs tighter theory and more rigorous, controlled experiments.
