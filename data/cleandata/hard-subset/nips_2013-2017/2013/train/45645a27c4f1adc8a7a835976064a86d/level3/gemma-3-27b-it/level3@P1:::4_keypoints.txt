- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming a prior applicability limitation.  
- Shows FICs for LFMs have the same form as for mixture models via asymptotic Hessian analysis.  
- Proves FAB/LFMs retain automatic hidden state selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic FIC-bound convergence.  
- Introduces CCCP-based analysis of FAB E-steps and a “shrinkage acceleration” method to reduce computation.  
- Presents identifiability-driven post-processing for merging latent features.  
- Provides theoretical derivations that are rigorous and clearly explained.  
- Novelly broadens FAB’s applicability from mixture/HMMs to LFMs.  
- Practical algorithmic improvements that demonstrably speed up inference.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real data shows strong model selection, accuracy, and speed versus IBP, VB, MEIBP.  
- Well-written, well-organized paper with consistent notation and logical derivations.  
- Contribution may be viewed as incremental—extension rather than a major new paradigm.  
- Scalability concerns remain: some competing methods timed out and broader complexity analysis is lacking.  
- Limited discussion of parameter sensitivity and tuning effects across methods.  
- Shrinkage acceleration is useful but not entirely novel relative to existing LFM shrinkage techniques.  
- Strong case for acceptance: relevant problem, solid theory, empirical support, practical improvements, and clarity.  
- Case against acceptance: perceived incremental novelty and remaining scalability questions.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed work recommended for acceptance, with suggested future attention to scalability and parameter-sensitivity discussion.
