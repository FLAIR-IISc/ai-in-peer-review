This paper presents an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing a limitation of previous FAB work that required a block-diagonal Hessian of the complete log-likelihood. The authors demonstrate that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same representation as that for mixture models, despite the Hessian condition violation. They provide theoretical analysis and empirical validation on both synthetic and real datasets, showing superior predictive performance and computational efficiency compared to existing methods like Gibbs sampling, Variational Bayes, and MEIBP.

The paper builds upon recent work in FAB inference [5, 6] and extends its applicability to a broader class of models. The authors correctly position their work within the landscape of Bayesian non-parametric methods for LFMs, referencing the Indian Buffet Process [10, 11] and related approaches [2, 3, 22].  The connection to the broader field of asymptotic Bayesian methods, including MAD-Bayes [1], is also appropriately acknowledged. The detailed review of FIC for mixture models in the introduction provides valuable context and clearly explains the motivation for extending FAB to LFMs.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical derivations are rigorous and well-presented. Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 are crucial contributions, enabling the application of FAB to LFMs. The analysis regarding parameter identifiability (Theorem 4) is insightful and strengthens the theoretical foundation.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The authors clearly articulate the problem, their approach, and the results. The notation is consistent, and the mathematical derivations are easy to follow. The inclusion of the CCCP interpretation and shrinkage acceleration is presented logically.
* **Originality:** While building on existing FAB work, extending the framework to LFMs, which previously did not satisfy the necessary Hessian condition, represents a novel and valuable contribution.  The derivation showing the FIC for LFMs has the same form as for MMs is a non-trivial result.  The acceleration technique based on the CCCP interpretation further enhances the originality.
* **Significance:**  The potential impact of this work is significant. FAB/LFMs offer a compelling alternative to computationally expensive methods like IBP and MEIBP, while achieving competitive or superior performance. The automated model selection capability and parameter identifiability are particularly attractive features. The demonstrated speedups in computation, especially with the shrinkage acceleration, are crucial for applying these models to large-scale datasets.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Incremental Contribution:** The work could be viewed as somewhat incremental. It extends an existing framework rather than presenting a fundamentally new approach. However, the extension is non-trivial and addresses a significant limitation of the original FAB method.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** While the paper mentions the insensitivity of δ, it would be beneficial to discuss the sensitivity of other parameters, particularly those used in the shrinkage acceleration step (Tshrink) and the initializations.
* **Comparison Scope:** The comparison to existing methods is thorough, but primarily focuses on synthetic and relatively small real-world datasets. Expanding the evaluation to larger, more complex datasets would further strengthen the claims of scalability and efficiency.



**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

* The theoretical contribution of extending FAB to LFMs and deriving the corresponding FIC is significant.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of FAB/LFMs in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency.
* The automated model selection and parameter identifiability are valuable features.
* The inclusion of the CCCP-based acceleration technique is a clever and practical addition.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

* The contribution, while valuable, is relatively incremental.
* The evaluation could be expanded to larger and more complex datasets.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent feature models. The technical soundness, clarity, and empirical validation are all excellent. While the contribution is somewhat incremental, it addresses a significant limitation of existing methods and offers a compelling alternative. The reviewer supports acceptance.  Minor revisions addressing the parameter sensitivity and suggestions for expanding the evaluation would further strengthen the paper.
