- Extends FAB inference from mixture/HMM models to latent feature models (LFMs), addressing parameter-sharing that breaks block-diagonal Hessian assumptions.
- Main technical result (Lemma 1): asymptotic decomposition of the Hessian determinant yields the same log-sum znk term as mixtures, producing an FIC that matches the marginal log-likelihood.
- Proposes an FAB algorithm for LFMs using a mean-field E-step and provides a lower-bound with monotonic improvement guarantees.
- Interprets the E-/M-updates as a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and introduces a shrinkage-acceleration routine for faster feature pruning.
- Proves an identifiability result that justifies a practical merge post-processing step for features.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real data compares FAB/LFM to Gibbs IBP, VB, and MEIBP, showing superior predictive performance and competitive or lower computational cost.
- Theoretical development is careful and technically sound, with clear derivations and credible central asymptotic claims.
- Important assumptions for Lemma 1 are deferred to the supplement; the reviewer recommends surfacing these “mild” conditions and adding finite-sample discussion in the main text.
- Clarity is good overall; derivations and algorithms are well presented, but a brief summary of key assumptions in the main text would improve readability.
- Potential evaluation bias: FAB learns noise variance while competing methods had fixed noise variance; a matched setting or sensitivity analysis is suggested for fairness.
- Runtime scaling with dataset size (N) and dimensionality (D) is not systematically characterized and should be reported more thoroughly.
- Originality: meaningful extension of FAB beyond block-diagonal Hessian regimes; CCCP view, acceleration, and merging are useful refinements though somewhat incremental relative to prior FAB work.
- Significance: makes FAB applicable to widely used LFMs, enabling automated model selection without priors and showing practical gains; reviewer supports acceptance.
