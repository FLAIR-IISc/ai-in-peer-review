**Paper Review: Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models**

**Summary**

This paper presents an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors derive a novel FAB algorithm for LFMs, which can automatically infer the number of latent features and achieve efficient inference. The proposed method is compared with state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process (IBP) methods, including Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP, on various datasets.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel application of FAB**: The authors successfully extend FAB inference to LFMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Efficient inference**: The proposed FAB algorithm can automatically infer the number of latent features and achieve efficient inference, making it suitable for large datasets.
3. **Good experimental results**: The authors demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM over existing methods on various datasets, including artificial simulations, block data, and real-world data.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited experimental evaluation**: The experimental evaluation is not thorough, and some comparisons are invalid due to the use of inappropriate baselines (e.g., MEIBP with non-negative W).
2. **Lack of discussion on algorithmic limitations**: The authors do not discuss the limitations of their algorithm, such as potential convergence difficulties or cases where it may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling.
3. **Limited applicability**: The method is limited to binary latent feature models and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
4. **Insufficient description of experimental details**: The authors do not provide sufficient details about the experimental setup, such as the number of restarts for variational Bayesian methods.

**Suggestions for improvement**

1. **Conduct more thorough experimental evaluations**: Compare the proposed method with other state-of-the-art methods, including MAD-Bayes, and provide more detailed experimental results.
2. **Discuss algorithmic limitations**: Provide a discussion on the limitations of the proposed algorithm, including potential convergence difficulties or cases where it may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling.
3. **Extend the method to other models**: Explore the possibility of extending the method to other models, such as factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.
4. **Provide more details about experimental setup**: Provide more details about the experimental setup, including the number of restarts for variational Bayesian methods.

**Acceptance**

Based on the review, I would recommend acceptance of the paper, but with the condition that the authors address the weaknesses and suggestions mentioned above. The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field, and with some revisions, it has the potential to be a high-quality paper.
