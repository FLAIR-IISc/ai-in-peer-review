- Extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) and derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) with a feature-wise complexity penalty (log of assigned counts).
- Provides a tractable lower bound and an EM-like algorithm that automatically prunes inactive features for model selection.
- Introduces an acceleration technique based on a CCCP interpretation of the updates.
- Empirically compares FAB/LFM to IBP methods, reporting better predictive likelihood, more compact models, and faster computation on several datasets.
- Main technical contribution (derivation of FIC for LFMs) is interesting and appears reasonable but has presentation weaknesses.
- Central asymptotic argument (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) lacks sufficient rigor in the main text and relies on supplementary assumptions whose implications are crucial.
- It is unclear whether the derivation holds when model complexity K grows with N; the asymptotic regime should be specified.
- Mathematical imprecision: the FIC includes Eq[log(sum_n z_nk)] which is ill-defined if a feature is unused (log(0)), and this needs clarification.
- Experimental concerns: runtime comparisons are hard to interpret without clear, comparable convergence criteria and details on MCMC truncation versus optimization convergence.
- It is unclear whether performance gains arise from the FIC criterion itself or from the EM-like optimizer avoiding poor local optima compared to the MCMC samplers.
- IBP baseline behavior (finding too many features) could be due to hyperparameter or implementation choices rather than a fundamental issue.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear motivation and algorithmic exposition.
- Clarity issues remain: main theoretical statements need precise assumptions in the main text, and there are minor notational inconsistencies (e.g., conditioning in mixture examples, missing expectations).
- Contribution is novel in extending FAB to a model class that violates prior assumptions, with useful practical additions (shrinkage acceleration, identifiability analysis).
- The work is somewhat incremental over the authors' prior FAB papers but technically meaningful in broadening applicability.
- Addresses an important problem (automatic model selection for LFMs) and offers a deterministic, efficient, hyperparameter-free alternative to IBP-based methods.
- If robust, the method could be widely used and inspire further asymptotic model-selection research as a practical alternative to nonparametric Bayes.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles significant problem, technically non-trivial extension, practical and efficient algorithm, offers a deterministic alternative to MCMC.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental over prior work, theoretical asymptotics lack precision (especially K vs N scaling), and experimental comparisons (runtimes and cause of gains) are not fully rigorous.
- Overall: solid and useful contribution with strong empirical results, but needs stronger mathematical rigor and more careful experimental validation.
