- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), removing a prior Hessian condition.
- Shows the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models, enabling FAB properties for LFMs.
- Combines mean-field inference with accelerated feature shrinkage to overcome LFM-specific challenges.
- Lemma 1 provides theoretical justification for applying FIC to LFMs.
- Introduces a novel merge post-processing step to address parameter identifiability.
- Proposes shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) as a practical enhancement for efficiency.
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written and clearly structured, easing followability of theory and algorithms.
- Appropriately situates the work relative to IBP and Variational Bayes with relevant citations.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, using synthetic and real datasets and diverse competing methods.
- Demonstrates computational speedups and improved predictive performance, including reduced overfitting.
- Major concern: limited exploration of potential failure modes and limitations of the combined approximations (FIC, mean-field, shrinkage).
- Specifically needs analysis of effects from strong latent-feature dependencies and highly non-Gaussian noise on FIC accuracy and algorithm stability.
- Requires further study of sensitivity to initialization and data characteristics.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound extension; computational gains; thorough validation; clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of limitations and sensitivity analyses.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted limitations.
