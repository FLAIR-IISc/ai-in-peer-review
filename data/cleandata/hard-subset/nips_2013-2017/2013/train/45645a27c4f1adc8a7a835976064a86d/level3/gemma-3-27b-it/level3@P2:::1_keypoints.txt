- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming a prior limitation requiring block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Shows LFMs exhibit properties allowing FAB application and derives a factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs.  
- Proposes an efficient algorithm with a shrinkage acceleration step and a merging post-processing strategy.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show competitive model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency versus state-of-the-art IBP methods.  
- Builds on recent FAB advances and leverages asymptotic analysis to derive a tractable model selection criterion; connection to BIC is clearly discussed.  
- Algorithm provides automatic hidden-state selection and parameter identifiability, extending FAB’s applicability.  
- Technical contribution includes analysis of the Hessian and derivation of FIC for a broader class of models.  
- Acceleration and merging enhancements improve practical utility and runtime.  
- Mathematical exposition contains imprecisions that need correction.  
- Equation (3) problematic: log-sum term may be undefined if all z_nk are zero, challenging FIC validity.  
- Theorem 2 lacks key details on the asymptotic regime, assumptions, and how K scales with N.  
- BIC-style approximation may be more appropriate for fixed finite K, potentially conflicting with IBP’s infinite-K spirit.  
- Minor formal errors: conditioning in p_k(X | z_{.,k}) should restrict to data assigned to component k, and equation (4) misses an expectation symbol.  
- Experimental issues: stopping criteria are unspecified, making runtime comparisons (especially with truncated Gibbs) ambiguous.  
- Unclear whether predictive improvements come from the model selection criterion or from better optimization escaping local optima; convergence and initialization sensitivity are not analyzed.  
- IBP baseline used α=3, which may bias comparisons and overestimate K; baselines need more careful parameter tuning.  
- Discussion comparing FAB to MEIBP [22] is limited and would benefit from deeper analysis.  
- Strengths summarized: novel FAB→LFM extension, tractable FIC, efficient algorithm, competitive empirical performance, and asymptotic theoretical justification.  
- Weaknesses summarized: imprecise math, incomplete asymptotic assumptions, ambiguous experimental protocol, unclear source of predictive gains, and potentially biased baseline settings.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify asymptotic assumptions, fix equation (3) issue, and provide detailed experimental setup including stopping criteria and baseline tuning.
