- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models/HMMs to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming prior applicability limits.
- Resolves the Hessian block-diagonal requirement by showing asymptotically LFMs yield FICs of the same form as mixture models.
- Proposes FAB/LFM that provides automatic feature selection, parameter identifiability, and computational benefits.
- Builds on Fujimaki’s FAB work and positions itself against IBP-based nonparametric Bayes and variational Bayes approaches.
- Key theoretical contributions are Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 establishing the asymptotic results.
- Introduces a practical acceleration technique using a CCCP interpretation and shrinkage acceleration to reduce computation.
- Empirical results show superior model selection accuracy, predictive performance, and computational efficiency on synthetic and real datasets.
- Theoretical derivations are thorough and accompanied by rigorous proofs in supplementary material.
- Experimental setup, implementation details, and parameter settings are well-documented for reproducibility.
- Acknowledges limitations: FIC approximation may be less accurate for small N and comparisons omit some state-of-the-art methods (e.g., VB).
- Originality is moderate but includes a novel theoretical insight and useful practical algorithmic contributions.
- Significance is substantial for scalable Bayesian inference in LFMs (e.g., link prediction) and for automatic complexity selection.
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, well-written, and practically valuable.
