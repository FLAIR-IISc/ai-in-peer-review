- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), including IBP models, enabling automatic feature selection.  
- Shows FIC for LFMs takes the same form as for mixture models despite LFMs not having block-diagonal Hessian structure.  
- Derives an FAB/LFM algorithm with a shrinkage acceleration technique and proves parameter identifiability.  
- Theoretical analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) justifies the FIC form but lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and how K scales with N.  
- Fixed finite-K requirement conflicts with IBP’s motivation for infinite latent features, a conceptual tension not resolved.  
- Mathematical imprecisions: Eq. (3) problematic since log(∑n znk) can be infinite if all znk=0; Eq. (4) missing an expectation.  
- Experimental results claim faster convergence and improved predictive performance, especially on larger datasets.  
- Runtime comparisons are questionable because stopping criteria are not specified.  
- Clarity is generally good; presentation, FAB–CCCP connection, and shrinkage technique are well-explained.  
- Paper would benefit from clearer discussion of the asymptotic regime in Theorem 2 and relation between finite-K FAB and infinite-K IBP.  
- Originality is novel but incremental—extension of prior FAB work to LFMs rather than a transformative advance.  
- Significance is practical: automatic model selection and computational efficiency are useful, but impact limited by incremental nature and unresolved theoretical points.  
- Strengths: theoretically motivated extension, practical shrinkage acceleration, empirical gains in predictive likelihood and speed.  
- Weaknesses: incomplete asymptotic justification, mathematical imprecision, insufficient experimental detail, and unclear whether gains come from model selection or avoiding poor local optima.  
- Overall recommendation: reasonable but incremental contribution; acceptable with revisions to asymptotic treatment, mathematical clarity, and experimental reporting.
