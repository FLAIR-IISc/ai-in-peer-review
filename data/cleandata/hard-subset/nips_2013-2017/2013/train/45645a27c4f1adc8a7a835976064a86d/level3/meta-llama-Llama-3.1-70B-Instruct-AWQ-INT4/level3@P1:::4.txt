This paper presents an extension of the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference method from mixture models and hidden Markov models to latent feature models (LFMs). The authors successfully derive a lower bound of the factorized information criterion (FIC) that is applicable to LFMs, despite the fact that LFMs do not satisfy the block-diagonal Hessian condition required by previous FAB methods.

The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of the FIC for LFMs, which has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models. This is a significant achievement, as it enables the application of FAB inference to a wider range of models. The authors also provide a thorough empirical evaluation of their method on both synthetic and real datasets, comparing it against other state-of-the-art methods such as fast Gibbs sampling for Indian Buffet Process (IBP), variational Bayes (VB), and maximum-expectation IBP (MEIBP).

The results indicate that the FAB/LFM method achieves superior predictive performance and lower computational cost compared to the other methods. The paper is well-written, with thorough derivation steps and clear explanations of the methodology and results. The contribution is somewhat incremental, but it is interesting and relevant to the NIPS community.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the authors provide a clear and concise abstract that effectively summarizes the main contributions and results of the paper. The introduction provides a good background on the FAB inference method and its limitations, and clearly motivates the need for extending it to LFMs. The methodology section is thorough and well-explained, and the empirical evaluation is comprehensive and well-designed.

The paper also has some nice theoretical results, such as the derivation of the FIC for LFMs and the proof of the identifiability of the model parameters. The authors also provide some interesting insights into the behavior of the FAB/LFM method, such as the fact that it can automatically select the number of latent features and that it has a regularization effect that helps to prevent overfitting.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication at NIPS. The contribution is significant, the paper is well-written, and the results are thorough and well-evaluated.
