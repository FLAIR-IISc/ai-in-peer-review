- Paper extends FAB inference to binary latent feature models (LFMs) and derives an FIC with a (D/2)∑k log ∑n znk penalty, enabling automatic K selection without priors.  
- Presents an EM-like algorithm optimizing a tractable lower bound, with a CCCP interpretation of the E-step and a shrinkage acceleration that prunes features.  
- Provides an identifiability discussion and a merge post-processing step.  
- Novelty: applying FAB to LFMs (violating prior block-diagonal Hessian assumptions) is new and the CCCP-based shrinkage is a useful contribution.  
- Technical soundness: asymptotic Hessian analysis and resulting log-count penalty appear careful and correct; algorithmic updates are well-motivated.  
- Empirical validation: shows faster inference and better model selection/prediction than some IBP baselines on some datasets, but experimental design issues weaken claims.  
- Clarity: methodology and derivations are clear; experimental details (initializations, restarts, noise settings) are insufficient for reproducibility.  
- Significance: potentially high if empirical evaluation is made fair and robust; could be a scalable alternative to IBP methods.  
- Strength: clear, elegant asymptotic analysis giving FIC for LFMs.  
- Strength: monotone lower-bound EM-like algorithm with practical CCCP interpretation and effective shrinkage acceleration.  
- Strength: automatic inference of K without priors or tunable hyperparameters.  
- Strength: scalable algorithms that prune features to reduce computation.  
- Weakness: limited scope to binary Z; not directly applicable to Gaussian factor analysis or PPCA.  
- Weakness: no discussion of convergence issues, sensitivity to initialization, or potential local optima in EM/CCCP.  
- Weakness: lack of small-N analysis to show where asymptotic FIC may fail.  
- Experimental issue: MEIBP was misused on data with negative weights despite assuming nonnegative W, invalidating those comparisons.  
- Experimental issue: IBP Gibbs was not given the true noise std in block image experiments, biasing comparisons.  
- Experimental issue: only finite-K variational IBP used—should include infinite variational formulation and report truncation and restarts.  
- Experimental issue: suspicious accelerated Gibbs result (~70 features on 49-d Sonar) likely due to poor initialization or incorrect noise variance; needs diagnosis.  
- Experimental issue: runtime comparisons mix methods that learn noise variance with those that fix it, creating unfairness.  
- Experimental issue: number of restarts/initializations not reported across baselines; FAB run once only.  
- Related-work gap: missing citations/discussion of MAD-Bayes and small-variance-asymptotic approaches for nonparametric LFMs.  
- Reference error: Ref [21] (Reed & Ghahramani) is incorrectly described as Gibbs; it is MAP/MEIBP via submodular maximization.  
- Minor factual correction: recommended real-data noise std in prior work is 0.25× data std (not 0.75×).  
- Suggested fixes: correct Ref [21], add MAD-Bayes and related citations, and clarify differences between FAB (N→∞ asymptotics) and σ2→0 approaches.  
- Suggested fixes: add convergence/limitations section covering local optima, initialization sensitivity, CCCP scheduling, and shrinkage failure modes.  
- Suggested fixes: run small-N synthetic experiments to evaluate FIC performance vs IBP in non-asymptotic regimes.  
- Suggested fixes: re-run and correct baselines — restrict MEIBP to nonnegative data or omit, give Gibbs true noise where appropriate, include infinite variational IBP, and report restarts/truncation.  
- Suggested fixes: investigate and fix the Sonar anomaly, report per-iteration runtimes and confirm lower-bound monotonicity with acceleration.  
- Recommendation: promising paper with solid theory but experimental and related-work flaws; recommend rejection in current form with encouragement to resubmit after corrections and improved experiments.
