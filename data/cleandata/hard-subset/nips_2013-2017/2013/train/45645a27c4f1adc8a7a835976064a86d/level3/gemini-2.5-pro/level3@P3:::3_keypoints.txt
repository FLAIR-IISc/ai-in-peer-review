- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models (LFMs) with an EM-like algorithm optimizing a tractable FIC lower bound.  
- Key theoretical contribution: Lemma 1 shows the Hessian determinant yields a complexity term for LFMs similar to mixture models.  
- Algorithm includes a shrinkage penalty that automatically infers the number of latent features K during optimization.  
- Method is deterministic, scalable, and presented as an alternative to nonparametric Bayesian approaches like IBP.  
- Monotonicity of the objective guarantees convergence to a local optimum; analysis links updates to a convex-concave procedure (CCCP).  
- Experimental claims of empirical superiority are undermined by a superficial and flawed experimental evaluation.  
- Baseline misuse: MEIBP comparisons are invalid where its non-negative W assumption is violated.  
- Unfair comparisons: IBP Gibbs sampler was not given fair settings (e.g., true noise variance) in some experiments.  
- Suspicious baseline results (e.g., accelerated Gibbs finding ~70 features on 49-d Sonar) suggest flawed baseline implementation or settings.  
- Experiments lack crucial methodological details (e.g., VB-IBP configuration, number of restarts, justification for finite variational version) hindering reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-written and clear on methodology, but the experimental section is poorly explained and needs clarification.  
- Novelty/significance: novel application of FAB to LFMs, offering scalable automatic model selection and pruning of irrelevant features.  
- Missed opportunity to connect to related asymptotic work (e.g., MAD-Bayes) and broader literature on small-variance asymptotics.  
- Missing/incorrect references: Reed & Ghahramani mischaracterized as Gibbs sampling instead of MAP via submodular optimization.  
- Lack of discussion on limitations: sensitivity to initialization, local optima, small-data regimes where asymptotics may fail, and restriction to binary LFMs with Gaussian noise.  
- Required fixes: major overhaul of experiments for fair, reproducible comparisons; correct baseline usage and references; expand discussion of limitations and related asymptotic methods.  
- Conclusion: core technical ideas are strong and promising, but the paper is undermined by experimental flaws and missing contextual discussion, requiring substantial revision.
