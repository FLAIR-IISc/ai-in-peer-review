- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with an EM-based algorithm that automatically infers the number of latent features K via FAB shrinkage.  
- Demonstrates automatic feature selection and claimed computational efficiency advantages over existing methods, with experiments showing superior performance on large datasets.  
- Novel extension of FAB from mixtures/HMMs to LFMs despite LFMs not satisfying the standard Hessian block-diagonal condition.  
- Theoretical contribution: analysis showing FIC equivalence between LFMs and mixtures.  
- Strength: technically sound approach with correct algorithmic details and clear methodology.  
- Strength: addresses an important problem in scalable Bayesian inference for LFMs.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation contains critical flaws (MEIBP misapplied to non-negative data).  
- Weakness: IBP Gibbs baseline was not given the known noise value in block data experiments, biasing comparisons.  
- Weakness: accelerated Gibbs result on Sonar data appears suspicious and may be unreliable.  
- Weakness: paper lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations, convergence difficulties, and behavior in small-data regimes where asymptotic arguments may fail.  
- Weakness: method limited to binary LFMs (excludes factor analysis/PCA) but this restriction is not adequately addressed.  
- Weakness: experimental details are insufficient and key related work is missing (e.g., MAD-Bayes ICML 2013 and recent efficient LFM inference); reference [21] is mischaracterized (it performs MAP, not Gibbs).  
- Recommendation: clarify and fix baseline settings, correct reference descriptions, include missing related work, and provide more thorough analysis of limitations and small-data behavior before acceptance.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical/technical contribution but requires substantial revision to strengthen experimental rigor and discussion of limitations.
