- Technical contribution: Extends FAB inference to latent feature models with an asymptotic Hessian analysis showing FIC has the same form as in mixtures, enabling an EM-like FAB with a CCCP E-step and shrinkage that auto-inferrs K.  
- Relation to prior work: Conceptually related to IBP nonparametrics but replaces prior-driven parsimony with a model-selection criterion; should discuss small-variance asymptotics work (e.g., MAD-Bayes ICML 2013).  
- Identifiability: Authors claim parameter identifiability improvements and propose a merging post-process to address redundancy.  
- Empirical claims: FAB is reported to outperform Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP methods in runtime and inference quality across datasets.  
- Theoretical quality: The asymptotic derivation and algorithmic details appear technically sound, and the CCCP interpretation is insightful.  
- Experimental methodology problems: Experiments are under-specified and contain fairness/errors (MEIBP used despite W allowing negatives, wrong noise settings for some comparisons, suspicious Sonar result suggesting bad initialization or noise variance).  
- Baseline issues: VB-IBP baseline uses an unspecified finite truncation (no truncation level/restarts); an infinite variational IBP should also be evaluated.  
- Incorrect citation/description: Reference [21] is MAP (submodular MEIBP), not Gibbs; this needs correction.  
- Reproducibility/clartiy gaps: Paper lacks crucial experimental details (preprocessing, hyperparameters, noise handling, initialization, number of restarts, truncation K, stopping criteria) for reproducibility.  
- Originality assessment: Applying FAB to LFMs is novel beyond prior FAB work; shrinkage acceleration and identifiability analysis add value, but related small-variance LFM work should be cited.  
- Significance: Efficient inference with automatic K for LFMs is important and of clear interest; the method scales and is promising.  
- Missing analyses/limitations: Paper lacks analysis of small-N behavior, convergence behavior/limitations, and circumstances where Gibbs might outperform FAB.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled novel FAB extension to LFMs, automatic K inference, scalability, practical algorithm, and clear CCCP/identifiability connections.  
- Cons against acceptance: Flawed/insufficient experimental methodology, missing related work and mischaracterization, no discussion of convergence/limitations, and limited scope (binary-only).  
- Recommendation: Promising contribution but requires corrected baselines, added related work (including MAD-Bayes), detailed experimental clarification, and discussion of limitations/convergence before acceptance.
