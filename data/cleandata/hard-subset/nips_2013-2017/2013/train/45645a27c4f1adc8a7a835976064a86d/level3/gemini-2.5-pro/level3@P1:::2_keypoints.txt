- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) via a derived Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.
- Key theoretical result (Lemma 1): log-determinant of the Hessian yields a penalty term sum_k log(sum_n z_nk), analogous to FAB for Mixture Models, enabling feature shrinkage.
- Derives a tractable lower bound on the FIC and develops an iterative FAB/LFM algorithm alternating E-step (variational latent features) and M-step (parameters).
- Positions FAB/LFM as a computationally efficient, automated alternative to nonparametric methods like the IBP for selecting the number of latent features.
- Originality/technical strength: non-trivial generalization of FAB to models with shared parameters; CCCP interpretation of the E-step and a shrinkage acceleration technique.
- Practical significance: single self-contained procedure for joint parameter learning and model selection, avoiding IBP hyperparameter sensitivity and repeated VB runs.
- Empirical evaluation: thorough comparisons to IBP, VB, MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets; shows competitive or superior predictive likelihood, more compact models, and large speedups.
- Ablation/analysis: inclusion of shrinkage acceleration experiments (Figure 1) convincingly validates that refinement.
- Clarity: well-written, logically organized, consistent notation, clear connection to prior work.
- Main weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations and potential failure modes arising from three stacked approximations (asymptotic Laplace, Jensen lower bound, mean-field variational).
- Specific open concerns: performance in small-N regimes, robustness to non-Gaussian noise, and behavior with highly correlated latent features where mean-field may fail.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound extension with practical utility and strong empirical support.
- Argument against acceptance: needs a more critical analysis of approximation-induced limitations and when the method may break down.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, impactful work with strengths outweighing the noted weakness; recommend acceptance.
