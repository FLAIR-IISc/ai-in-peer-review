- Proposes extending factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to binary latent feature models (LFMs) with an EM-based algorithm that uses FAB shrinkage to learn Z and infer K.  
- Algorithm similar in spirit to IBP nonparametrics but uses a FAB model-selection criterion instead of priors for parsimony.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound; algorithmic details appear correct.  
- Method scales to large datasets and can automatically infer the number of latent features.  
- Application of FAB to LFMs is novel relative to prior FAB work on mixtures and HMMs.  
- Experimental methodology and details are insufficiently described and unclear in places.  
- Experimental evaluation is not thorough and contains flaws; small-data regimes are not explored where asymptotic arguments may fail.  
- Lacks discussion of algorithmic limitations and cases where it may underperform compared to Gibbs sampling.  
- No discussion of potential convergence difficulties for the EM-like procedure.  
- Method is limited to binary LFMs and cannot be applied to factor analysis or probabilistic PCA.  
- Some baselines were used inappropriately (e.g., MEIBP requires non-negative W), invalidating certain comparisons.  
- IBP Gibbs baseline was not given a fair advantage (known noise value) in block images experiments.  
- VB-IBP experiments used a finite variational version without sufficient details (e.g., restarts); an infinite variational version could be used.  
- Accelerated Gibbs result finding ~70 features on 49-d Sonar data is suspicious and likely due to bad initialization or wrong noise variance.  
- Reference [21] is mischaracterized as Gibbs sampling but actually performs MAP inference and must be corrected.  
- Authors should cite and discuss MAD-Bayes (ICML 2013) and other small-variance asymptotic work for nonparametric LFMs.  
- Minor correction: recommended std-dev setting for real data is 0.25× examples' std-dev (refs [1,22]), not 0.75.  
- After author feedback, experimental methodology and baseline settings still need clearer explanation and justification.  
- Required fixes before acceptance: correct reference description for [21], include MAD-Bayes citation, and improve experimental evaluation and discussion of limitations.  
- Overall: novel and efficient method with good empirical promise, but requires more thorough experiments and clearer discussion of limitations.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
