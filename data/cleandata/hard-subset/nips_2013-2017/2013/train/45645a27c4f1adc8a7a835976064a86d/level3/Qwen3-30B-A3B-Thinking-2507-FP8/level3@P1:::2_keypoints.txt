- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), filling a gap in applicability to more complex latent-variable models.  
- Theoretical contribution: shows via asymptotic analysis that LFMs share the same factorized information criterion (FIC) form as mixture models (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) despite lacking block-diagonal Hessian structure.  
- Proposes a novel FAB/LFM algorithm that automatically selects the number of latent features K without hand-tuned hyperparameters.  
- Achieves automatic feature selection through a model-induced regularization effect that shrinks irrelevant features to zero.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration technique using a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) interpretation to remove irrelevant features earlier (Algorithm 2), reducing computation.  
- Provides parameter identifiability analysis for LFMs (Theorem 4) and a merge post-processing step to improve model compactness.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing against strong baselines (IBP, VB, MEIBP) on synthetic and real data.  
- Results show clear computational advantages, better model-selection accuracy (especially for larger N), competitive predictive performance, and consistently more compact models.  
- Demonstrates that merge post-processing improves model representation.  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and situates contributions properly within related NIPS literature.  
- Connects FAB/LFM with the CCCP framework, offering theoretical insight into algorithm behavior.  
- Main weakness: lacks systematic analysis of failure modes and limitations, including regimes where asymptotic assumptions may break (e.g., small N, strong feature correlations, non-Gaussian noise).  
- Practical sensitivity to initialization (initial K = min(N,D)) is not analyzed, leaving unclear robustness of final model selection.  
- Significance: advances model selection for LFMs by providing an efficient, minimally tuned method; recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address asymptotic limitations.
