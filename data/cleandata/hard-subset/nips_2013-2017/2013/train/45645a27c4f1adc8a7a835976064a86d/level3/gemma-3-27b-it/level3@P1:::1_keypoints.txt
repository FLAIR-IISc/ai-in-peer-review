- Contribution: Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by showing LFMs’ FIC has the same form as for mixture models, enabling FAB application despite missing block-diagonal Hessian structure.

- Theoretical result: Proves Lemma 1/Theorem 2 that FIC for LFMs mirrors that for MMs, and connects the E-step to a convex–concave procedure (CCCP).

- Algorithmic refinements: Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method and a merge post-processing step leveraging parameter identifiability.

- Identifiability: Analyzes parameter identifiability in LFMs and uses it to justify merging latent features.

- Empirical performance: Demonstrates competitive accuracy and computational efficiency on synthetic and real datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art IBP methods in model selection and prediction.

- Novelty: Broadens FAB’s applicability beyond mixture models to a more general class (LFMs), a meaningful methodological extension.

- Motivation: Clearly motivated by scalability limitations of existing LFM model-selection methods and positions FAB as a scalable alternative.

- Weakness — mathematical clarity: Some derivations lack rigor/clarity (e.g., log-sum in Eq.(3) may be infinite; asymptotic regime/assumptions for Theorem 2 not fully specified).

- Weakness — scaling with K: Relying on a BIC-like approximation implies fixed finite K, raising concerns about compatibility with the infinite-dimensional IBP and unclear scaling of K with N.

- Weakness — minor errors: Several typographical/notation issues (e.g., conditioning in pk(X|z·,k), missing expectation in Eq.(4)) reduce polish.

- Weakness — experimental detail: Experimental setup omits key details (e.g., stopping criteria); arbitrary truncation of Gibbs runtime undermines runtime comparisons.

- Weakness — ambiguous gains: It’s unclear whether improvements derive from the model-selection criterion or from the FAB algorithm better avoiding poor local optima compared to IBP implementations.

- Weakness — limited VB comparison: Comparisons with modern variational Bayes approaches are limited; a more thorough VB comparison would strengthen the evaluation.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important, scalable model-selection problem for LFMs with solid theoretical grounding, empirical gains, and practical algorithmic improvements.

- Arguments against acceptance: Needs clearer mathematical rigor, justification of K scaling, more experimental detail, and stronger differentiation/ comparison with VB methods.

- Overall recommendation: Sensible and promising contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to fix mathematical imprecision, clarify K vs N scaling, add experimental details (including stopping criteria), and expand VB comparisons.
