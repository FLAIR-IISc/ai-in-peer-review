- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian observations.  
- Main theoretical contribution: asymptotic analysis of the complete-data Hessian shows FIC for LFMs has same form as mixtures despite non-block-diagonal Hessian (Lemma 1 → Theorem 2).  
- Key penalty: each feature is penalized by (D/2) log(sum_n z_nk) plus an O(K log N) term in the FIC.  
- Derives FICLFM by substituting the FIC into the variational identity and optimizing a tractable lower bound.  
- Optimization uses (i) linearization of the convex penalty, (ii) joint optimization over parameters P instead of MLE, and (iii) a mean-field q(Z) over binary indicators.  
- E-step reduces to coordinate-wise logistic regressions with a FAB-specific shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃_k) that drives automatic pruning of unused features.  
- M-step is closed-form as in PPCA, with π_k and π̃_k set to empirical usage.  
- Reinterprets E-step as a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and proposes accelerateShrinkage: multiple CCCP inner updates per iteration to remove irrelevant features early and speed runtime.  
- Proves a form of parameter identifiability under FAB regularization; motivates a merge post-processing step for near-duplicate features when it increases the lower bound.  
- Positions work relative to FAB for mixtures/HMMs, IBP models and variants, MEIBP, MAD-Bayes, BIC, and singular learning theory.  
- Empirically evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, reporting runtime, selected K, and predictive log-likelihoods.  
- Results: FAB typically selects compact models with competitive or superior predictive performance and is substantially faster than most NPB/VB baselines; MEIBP sometimes faster but often worse in prediction.  
- Technical development is sound, proofs in supplement, optimization bound well-motivated, and monotone ascent is maintained.  
- CCCP view and acceleration are correct and justified; identifiability and merge heuristic are reasonable and useful in practice.  
- Main caveat: multiple approximations are combined (asymptotics/O_p(1), mean-field, linearization, early shrinkage/merging) without detailed failure-mode analysis.  
- Requests clearer discussion of when approximations break (small N, dense/highly correlated features, extreme noise) and ablations quantifying each component’s contribution.  
- Clarity is strong; suggestions include stating Lemma 1 assumptions in main text, detailing per-iteration complexity and memory, clarifying merge thresholds and monotonicity, and diagnostics for FIC vs log-evidence reliability.  
- Originality: nontrivial extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs without block-diagonal Hessian; CCCP reinterpretation and shrinkage-acceleration are simple yet effective innovations.  
- Significance: important practical method for automatic K selection with minimal tuning; likely transferable to other discrete latent-variable models, but currently limited to linear-Gaussian setting and asymptotics.  
- Specific actionable requests: summarize Lemma 1 assumptions, provide ablations for FAB penalty / accelerateShrinkage / merge, report sensitivity to initialization and Tshrink, run fair baselines with matched noise settings, clarify runtime scaling and pruning effects, add diagnostics for small N, and explore generalization to non-Gaussian likelihoods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical advance, practical algorithm with good empirical performance, useful CCCP acceleration and merge analysis, clear writing and thorough comparisons.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of detailed failure-mode analysis for combined approximations, some unfair baseline choices, and limited scope to linear-Gaussian LFMs.  
- Overall stance: strong contribution; lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying assumptions, adding ablations, and tightening empirical comparisons.
