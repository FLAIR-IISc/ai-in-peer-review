This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs). The main technical contribution is an asymptotic analysis of the Hessian showing that the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as in mixtures, enabling an EM-like FAB procedure with a CCCP-based E-step and a shrinkage mechanism that automatically infers the number of features K. The approach is conceptually related to nonparametric IBP models but substitutes a model-selection criterion for prior-driven parsimony. The authors also argue parameter identifiability benefits and propose a merging post-process. Empirically, FAB is claimed to outperform Gibbs, variational, and MAP IBP methods in runtime and inference quality on several datasets.

Quality: The asymptotic derivation and algorithmic details appear technically sound, and the CCCP interpretation is insightful. However, the experimental methodology is under-specified and contains fairness issues and likely errors: MEIBP was used in settings where W can be negative (violating MEIBP’s non-negativity requirement), some block-image comparisons did not give the IBP Gibbs sampler the known noise level, and the Sonar result (~70 features in 49D) suggests bad initialization or incorrect noise variance. The VB-IBP baseline uses a finite truncation without details (e.g., truncation level, restarts), while an infinite variational IBP should also be evaluated. The recommended real-data noise setting in prior work is 0.25× data std-dev (not 0.75). Reference [21] is MAP (submodular MEIBP), not Gibbs; this needs correction.

Clarity: The methodology is generally clear, but experimental details (preprocessing, hyperparameters, noise handling, initialization, number of restarts, truncation K, stopping criteria per method) need substantial elaboration to ensure reproducibility.

Originality: Applying FAB to LFMs is novel beyond prior FAB work on mixtures/HMMs. The shrinkage acceleration and identifiability analysis add value. Some recent related work on small-variance asymptotics for LFMs (e.g., MAD-Bayes, ICML 2013) should be discussed.

Significance: Efficient inference with automatic K for LFMs is important and of clear interest to the NeurIPS community. The method scales and is promising, but conclusions are weakened by baseline issues and missing analyses (small-N regimes, convergence behavior/limitations, cases where Gibbs may outperform).

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel FAB extension to LFMs with principled derivation and practical algorithm.
- Automatic K inference, scalability, and strong potential impact.
- Clear connection to CCCP and identifiability insights.

Cons against acceptance:
- Flawed/insufficient experimental methodology and unfair baselines.
- Missing related work (MAD-Bayes) and mischaracterization of [21].
- No discussion of convergence difficulties or limitations; binary-only scope (not FA/PPCA).
- Small-data behavior not analyzed.

Recommendation: Promising contribution, but requires corrected baselines, added related work (including MAD-Bayes), clarification of experimental details, and discussion of limitations/convergence before acceptance.
