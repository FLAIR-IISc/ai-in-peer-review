- Proposes extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), deriving an FIC for LFMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessians.  
- Develops an EM-like algorithm that updates parameters and the posterior over binary latent features.  
- Introduces a model-induced regularization term that automatically shrinks the number of active latent features for model selection.  
- Proposes a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration to speed up feature elimination.  
- Experiments claim superior computational efficiency and predictive performance versus IBP variants, especially on larger datasets.  
- Theoretical derivations (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) appear sound and constitute the core technical contribution.  
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation has serious flaws that undermine empirical claims.  
- MEIBP baseline was inappropriately applied to non-negative-data settings, invalidating that comparison.  
- IBP Gibbs baseline was not given the ground-truth noise variance in the block experiment, compromising fairness.  
- VB-IBP baseline lacks important details (truncation level, restarts) and an infinite variational alternative was not considered.  
- Some reported results look suspicious (e.g., ~70 features on 49-dimensional Sonar), likely due to baseline misconfiguration or initialization.  
- Paper is generally well-written and method sections are clear with consistent notation.  
- Experimental section lacks reproducibility details and fails to discuss limitations like local optima and small-sample behavior.  
- Limitation: method restricted to binary latent features and may not apply to continuous-factor models (e.g., PPCA, factor analysis).  
- Limitation: relies on asymptotic arguments, so performance for small N is unclear and should be tested or discussed.  
- Limitation: objective is non-convex, raising convergence/local optima concerns that are not addressed.  
- Related work omission: should discuss MAD-Bayes (Broderick et al., ICML 2013) for context on asymptotic approaches to nonparametric LFMs.  
- Minor corrections requested: clarify noise standard-deviation heuristic (0.75 vs. 0.25× data std) and verify accurate characterization of baseline methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel non-trivial extension of FAB to LFMs, scalable EM-like algorithm, and useful CCCP acceleration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: flawed empirical evaluation, missing discussion of limitations, and omission of relevant related work — revision required.
