This paper introduces an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors address the limitation of the original FAB framework, which required a block-diagonal Hessian of the complete log-likelihood, a condition not met by LFMs. They demonstrate, through asymptotic analysis, that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs takes the same form as that for mixture models, enabling the application of FAB. Furthermore, they propose a shrinkage acceleration method based on a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and demonstrate parameter identifiability. The work is well-motivated, tackling a practical limitation of FAB inference and extending its applicability to a broader class of models.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a rigorous asymptotic analysis of the Hessian matrix for LFMs, convincingly demonstrating the applicability of FAB. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs appears well-justified, and the theoretical results (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) are clearly stated.
* **Novelty:** Extending FAB inference to LFMs is a significant contribution, as it expands the applicability of this promising Bayesian approximation technique. The analysis addressing the Hessian condition is original and insightful.
* **Practical Improvements:** The proposed shrinkage acceleration method, leveraging a CCCP interpretation, offers a substantial computational improvement, as demonstrated in the experiments. The discussion of parameter identifiability and the merge post-processing step adds practical value.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of FAB/LFMs. The comparisons against established methods (IBP, VB, MEIBP) are comprehensive and highlight the advantages of the proposed approach in terms of model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency. The ablation study with and without shrinkage acceleration is particularly informative.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical derivations are clearly presented, and the algorithms are described in a straightforward manner.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Discussion of Prior Influence:** While the authors emphasize the asymptotic ignoring of priors, a more nuanced discussion of the impact of the prior (even weak) on the results could be beneficial.  How sensitive are the results to different prior settings (even if only in the supplementary material)?
* **Convergence Analysis:** The paper states that the FAB algorithm guarantees a monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound. However, a more detailed discussion of convergence properties (e.g., convergence rate, stopping criteria) would be valuable. The influence of the tolerance δ on the results is briefly mentioned, but further investigation is warranted.
* **Computational Complexity Analysis:**  While the empirical results demonstrate the computational efficiency of the proposed method, a theoretical analysis of the computational complexity of the FAB/LFM algorithm compared to alternative methods would strengthen the paper.
* **Merge Post-processing Details:** The merge post-processing step is mentioned as improving results, but the specific criteria for merging latent features could be more detailed.  How sensitive are the results to the threshold used for merging? 
* **Comparison with Recent Advances in VB for LFMs:** The related work section briefly mentions VB methods for LFMs.  However, more recent advancements in scalable VB inference techniques for LFMs (beyond those cited) might warrant discussion and comparison.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant limitation of the FAB framework, extending its applicability to a wider range of models.
* The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-supported, demonstrating the validity of the approach.
* The proposed shrinkage acceleration method offers a practical improvement in computational efficiency.
* The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating the effectiveness of FAB/LFMs on a variety of datasets.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The lack of detailed analysis regarding prior influence, convergence properties, and computational complexity are minor weaknesses.
* The description of the merge post-processing step could be more thorough.
* While not critical, a more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art VB methods for LFMs would further strengthen the paper.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent feature models. The authors address a practical limitation of FAB inference and propose a novel and effective solution. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the empirical results are compelling.  I recommend acceptance, with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, I suggest adding a section in the supplementary material discussing prior sensitivity and providing more details on the merge post-processing criteria.
