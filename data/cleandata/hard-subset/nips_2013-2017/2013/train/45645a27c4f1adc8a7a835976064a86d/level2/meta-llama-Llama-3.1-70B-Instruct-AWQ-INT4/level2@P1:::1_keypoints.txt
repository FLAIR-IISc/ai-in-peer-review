- Proposal: Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) and derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) and FAB algorithm for LFMs.  
- Theoretical contribution: Thorough Hessian analysis showing LFMs' log-likelihood Hessian has the same form as mixture models, enabling FIC despite not meeting FAB's usual conditions.  
- Writing: Paper is well-written with clear explanations and defined notation.  
- Experiments: Demonstrates improved computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance on synthetic, block, and real-world datasets.  
- Strength: Significant contribution by extending FAB to a new model class (LFMs).  
- Strength: Detailed and crucial Hessian analysis underpins the method.  
- Strength: Empirical results support effectiveness across scenarios.  
- Weakness: Assumes substantial background in Bayesian inference and LFMs, reducing accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets; broader benchmarking is needed.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Originality: Novel and substantial extension of FAB to LFMs with rigorous theoretical work.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for model selection and prediction across domains (e.g., imaging, NLP, bioinformatics).  
- Quality: High-quality writing, well-designed experiments, and rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Clarity: Notation, terminology, method, and experiments are clearly presented.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novelty, solid theoretical analysis, and supportive empirical results favor acceptance.  
- Rejection arguments: Limited datasets, missing comparative baselines, and accessibility issues argue for further revisions.  
- Recommendation: Rated 8/10 — accept with minor revisions (expand experiments, add comparisons, improve accessibility).
