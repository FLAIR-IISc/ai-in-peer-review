- Proposal: Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) and derives a factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs that matches the mixture-model form despite LFMs not meeting FAB conditions.  
- Algorithm properties: The proposed FAB/LFM yields automatic hidden-state selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound.  
- Theoretical rigor: Derivation of the FIC for LFMs is presented as rigorous and well-supported by theoretical analysis.  
- Empirical performance: Experiments show FAB/LFM outperforms existing methods, including state-of-the-art IBP approaches, in computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction.  
- Writing and clarity: The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains the method and theory clearly.  
- Strength — theoretical contribution: Significant contribution by extending FIC/FAB theory to LFMs.  
- Strength — empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments that convincingly demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Weakness — scope: Focus on LFMs may limit applicability to other model classes.  
- Weakness — computational cost: Even with an acceleration method, the approach remains computationally demanding.  
- Suggested improvement: Provide more implementation details and empirical evaluation of the acceleration method’s effectiveness.  
- Suggested improvement: Compare against other state-of-the-art approaches (e.g., variational inference, MCMC).  
- Suggested improvement: Give deeper insights into the theoretical implications of deriving FIC for LFMs.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theoretical contributions, empirical superiority, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited applicability beyond LFMs and significant computational resource requirements.
