- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), deriving the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.
- Shows FIC for LFMs takes the same form as for Mixture Models despite initial theoretical mismatch.
- Derives a FAB algorithm for LFMs and introduces a CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration method.
- Demonstrates parameter identifiability and proposes a merge post-processing step for practical use.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets shows competitive performance vs. IBP and VB.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and logically organized.
- Lemma 1 and the asymptotic Hessian analysis are key technical contributions and appear mathematically sound.
- Novelty lies in extending FAB to a broader class of models and revealing the surprising FIC similarity.
- Shrinkage acceleration improves computational efficiency, aiding scalability.
- Comprehensive experiments include multiple datasets and missing-value prediction tasks.
- Identifiability discussion and merge step add theoretical and practical value.
- Important assumptions in Lemma 1 are only in the supplement and should be stated in the main text for clarity.
- Discussion of prior work on FIC/FAB and the role of the prior is limited and could be expanded.
- Lacks detailed theoretical or empirical time-complexity analysis comparing computational cost to baselines.
- Insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., stopping criterion, Tshrink) to support robustness claims.
- Merge post-processing is briefly described; criteria and effects (and reliance on Euclidean distance) need more detail.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant extension of FAB, solid technical derivation, practical acceleration, and strong empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of assumptions and related work, limited computational and hyperparameter analyses, and brief merge description.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance if authors address assumptions, related-work context, and computational/hyperparameter details.
