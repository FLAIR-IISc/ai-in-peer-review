- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with Bernoulli features and linear-Gaussian observations, deriving an FIC with the same functional form as for mixture models.
- Key technical contribution: asymptotic analysis of the complete-data Hessian (Lemma 1) yields a ∑k log(∑n znk) penalty that drives automatic feature shrinkage.
- Derives a tractable lower bound and an EM-like FAB algorithm using mean-field q(Z), with E-step interpretable via CCCP and an implemented shrinkage acceleration scheme.
- Proposes a merge post-processing step justified by an identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) that explains preference for balanced representations.
- Empirical results: FAB selects compact models and often achieves better predictive log-likelihoods than IBP variants, with competitive or improved runtime due to shrinkage acceleration.
- Builds on prior FAB work for mixtures/HMMs and connects to IBP-based nonparametric latent feature models and MAD-Bayes asymptotics.
- Strength: conceptual advance showing FIC emerges without block-diagonal Hessian—broadens FAB applicability.
- Strength: clear algorithmic specification and CCCP perspective that explains and motivates shrinkage/acceleration.
- Strength: automated model selection via a data-driven −(D/2)∑k log(∑n znk) regularizer without hyperparameter tuning.
- Strength: insightful identifiability analysis and principled merge strategy.
- Strength: favorable empirical performance and substantial speedups from shrinkage acceleration.
- Weakness: pivotal Lemma 1 rests on “mild assumptions” hidden in the supplement; key conditions should be summarized in main text.
- Weakness: mean-field factorization on Z weakens the FIC–marginal likelihood link; induced approximation gap not analyzed or empirically assessed.
- Weakness: fairness of experimental comparisons is questionable—baselines often use fixed noise/prior settings while FAB learns noise precision.
- Weakness: scope limited to linear-Gaussian LFMs with diagonal noise; unclear extension to non-diagonal Λ or non-Gaussian likelihoods.
- Weakness: missing explicit per-iteration complexity, iteration counts, sensitivity to Tshrink, and clarity on monotonicity preservation under acceleration and merge procedures.
- Weakness: potential poor performance for small N; no controlled study varying N to quantify regime of FIC reliability.
- Quality: technically plausible and algorithmically clear, but core asymptotic result needs key steps/conditions presented in main text to boost confidence.
- Clarity: generally well organized; should emphasize role of π/π̃, clarify η(πk) connection to shrinkage, and state Lemma 1 assumptions and proof outline.
- Originality: novel extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs without block-diagonal Hessian and useful CCCP/merge contributions.
- Significance: important contribution to automated model selection for LFMs if Lemma 1 assumptions hold broadly; empirical gains are promising.
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical extension, practical automated algorithm with acceleration, identifiability-backed merge, and strong empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: overreliance on supplementary proofs, fairness concerns in baselines, limited discussion of generality, and unaddressed mean-field FIC gap.
- Suggestions: state Lemma 1 assumptions and sketch derivation in main text; have baselines learn/tune noise variance and α; report complexity and iteration stats; summarize merge criteria and effects; study sensitivity to Tshrink and N.
- Overall: strong submission, borderline accept leaning to accept pending clearer presentation of assumptions and fairer empirical comparisons.
