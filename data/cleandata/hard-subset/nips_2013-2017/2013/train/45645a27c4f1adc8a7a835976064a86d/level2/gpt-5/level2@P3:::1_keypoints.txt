- Extends FAB to latent feature models (LFMs) via asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1) showing log det F(d) = sum_k log sum_n z_nk + O_p(1), yielding an FIC identical in form to mixture models (MMs).  
- Derives a tractable FAB lower bound with mean-field q(Z) and E-step fixed-point updates that include a data-driven shrinkage term proportional to D/(2N π̃_k).  
- Provides a CCCP interpretation of the E-step and introduces a shrinkage-acceleration routine that removes irrelevant features earlier, reducing runtime.  
- Offers an identifiability analysis proving FAB prefers balanced allocations among duplicate features and motivates a principled post-hoc merge operation.  
- Empirical comparisons (synthetic and real) against IBP (Gibbs), VB-IBP, and MEIBP show improved model selection (K), competitive or better predictive likelihood, and favorable computational efficiency with acceleration.  
- Positions work relative to prior art: extends FAB from MMs/HMMs to LFMs, contrasts with IBP-based inference and MAD-Bayes, and connects to singular learning theory; notes BIC would over-penalize here.  
- Theoretical quality: Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 are plausible and yield the expected log-sum regularizer; CCCP and identifiability results are sound and valuable.  
- Algorithmic quality: Lower bound and updates are well specified; acceleration is straightforward and monotone under stated coordinate updates; merge heuristic is principled.  
- Empirical quality: Results support claims on model selection, prediction, and runtime, though some experimental choices raise fairness questions.  
- Clarity: Clear, well-organized exposition, but key technical assumptions are deferred to the supplement—main text should state essential regularity conditions.  
- Originality: Novel asymptotic result for LFMs, CCCP-based acceleration, and identifiability-driven merge represent substantive, new contributions.  
- Significance: Enables automated model selection for LFMs without priors and suggests broader applicability of FAB beyond MMs/HMMs.  
- Main strengths: Rigorous asymptotic analysis, automatic feature selection via model-induced regularization, monotone optimization with effective acceleration, identifiability treatment, and strong empirical evidence.  
- Essential weaknesses: Missing succinct statement of assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 in main text; potential experimental unfairness (noise handling, hyperparameters, initialization); lack of per-iteration complexity analysis and empirical scaling/feature-pruning statistics.  
- Optional improvements: Add sensitivity analyses (Tshrink, µ init, δ), explicit CCCP convergence details, robustness tests for small-N regimes, and release code for reproducibility.  
- Clarification questions requested: precise conditions for the log-det decomposition (especially as π_k → 0 or 1); whether accelerateShrinkage can decrease the lower bound; sensitivity and lower-bound effect of the merge threshold; practical setting and influence of π priors.  
- Pros for acceptance: Clear theoretical advance enabling FAB for LFMs, simple effective algorithm with acceleration, and strong empirical performance.  
- Cons for acceptance: Reliance on supplemental assumptions not summarized in main text, experimental fairness/sensitivity concerns, limited analysis of small-N failure modes.  
- Overall verdict: Strong, consequential contribution extending FAB to an important model class with practical benefits.
