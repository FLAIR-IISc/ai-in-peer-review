- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming the non-block-diagonal Hessian issue.  
- Proves via asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) that the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models.  
- Enables FAB/LFMs that automatically select the number of latent features without hyperparameters, ensuring parameter identifiability and computational efficiency.  
- Strong theoretical contribution that bridges FAB inference to a broader class of models.  
- Introduces a novel shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2) that dramatically reduces computational cost.  
- Provides comprehensive empirical validation on simulations, block data, and real-world datasets.  
- Demonstrates superior model selection accuracy, predictive performance, and computational efficiency compared to IBP, VB, and MEIBP, especially on larger datasets.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, and includes sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and experimental design effectively compares relevant baselines.  
- Appropriately acknowledges limitations: FAB's asymptotic approximation may be less accurate for very small sample sizes.  
- Would benefit from more discussion on scenarios where FAB/LFMs are unsuitable and comparisons with more recent LFM methods.  
- Overall: significant, high-quality contribution meeting standards for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; recommend acceptance.
