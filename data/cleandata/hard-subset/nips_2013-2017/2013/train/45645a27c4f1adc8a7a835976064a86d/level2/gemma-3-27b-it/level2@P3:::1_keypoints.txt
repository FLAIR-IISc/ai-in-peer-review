- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs).
- Shows the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for Mixture Models despite LFMs not meeting FAB’s standard conditions.
- Proposes a shrinkage-acceleration method based on a Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP) interpretation of the FAB E-step.
- Establishes parameter identifiability results for FAB applied to LFMs.
- Empirical results show competitive performance vs. Indian Buffet Process (IBP) methods in model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency.
- Theoretical derivation of the FIC is rigorous and connections to mixture models are clearly articulated.
- Analysis of the Hessian matrix and Lemma 1 are key technical contributions justifying the extension.
- CCCP-based acceleration is a practical addition that improves computational efficiency.
- Discussion of parameter identifiability provides valuable theoretical insights.
- Experiments are comprehensive, covering synthetic and real datasets and relevant baselines.
- Ablation study demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed shrinkage acceleration.
- Presentation is clear: mathematical derivations, algorithmic details, and logical organization are well done.
- Authors provide sufficient details for reproducibility and thorough related-work citations.
- Lacks deeper intuition for why the FIC takes the same form for LFMs and mixture models.
- Discussion of the mean-field approximation is brief and needs stronger justification and analysis of its accuracy impact.
- Sensitivity analysis to different noise (standard deviation) levels is missing and should be added.
- Ablation studies should include analysis of different Tshrink values and other parameter effects.
- More detailed comparison of model characteristics (e.g., sparsity of latent features) between FAB and competitors would be beneficial.
- Notation inconsistencies (e.g., boldface for vectors) should be cleaned up.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; minor improvements suggested; score 8/10.
