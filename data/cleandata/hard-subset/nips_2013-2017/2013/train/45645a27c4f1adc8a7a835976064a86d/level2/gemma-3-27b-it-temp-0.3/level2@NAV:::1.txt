## Review of “Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models”

This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing a limitation of the original method which required a block-diagonal Hessian matrix of the complete log-likelihood. The authors demonstrate that LFMs, despite not initially satisfying this condition, exhibit a similar factorized information criterion (FIC) structure as Mixture Models, enabling the application of FAB. They present theoretical analysis, including a connection to the Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP) leading to a shrinkage acceleration method, and prove parameter identifiability. Empirical evaluation on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrates competitive performance in model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency compared to existing methods like Indian Buffet Processes (IBP) and Variational Bayes (VB).

The core contribution of this work lies in extending the applicability of FAB inference to a broader class of models – LFMs – by demonstrating the equivalence of their FIC structure to that of MMs. This is a significant step towards establishing FAB as a general-purpose Bayesian model selection technique. The theoretical analysis is well-developed, particularly the connection to CCCP which provides a practical acceleration. The experimental results are comprehensive, showcasing improvements in speed and accuracy over competing methods, especially for larger datasets. The acknowledgement of limitations, such as the reliance on a mean-field approximation, adds to the paper’s credibility.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the assumptions underlying Lemma 1. While mentioned, a clearer articulation of these assumptions and their potential impact on the applicability of the method would be valuable. Additionally, the comparison to existing methods could be strengthened by including a more thorough analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity for those methods. The presentation of results in the tables could be improved by including standard deviations or confidence intervals to better assess the statistical significance of the observed differences.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of FAB inference to LFMs.
*   Strong theoretical justification and analysis.
*   Competitive empirical performance on diverse datasets.
*   Practical acceleration method based on CCCP.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Assumptions underlying Lemma 1 could be more thoroughly discussed.
*   Hyperparameter sensitivity analysis of competing methods is limited.
*   Presentation of results could be improved with statistical measures.

**Overall:** This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian model selection. Despite minor areas for improvement, the theoretical soundness, practical acceleration, and compelling experimental results warrant acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
