- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs).  
- Shows the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for Mixture Models despite LFMs not meeting standard FAB assumptions.  
- Enables FAB properties (automatic model selection, parameter identifiability) within the LFM framework.  
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method based on a CCCP interpretation of the FAB E-step to improve computational efficiency.  
- Demonstrates parameter identifiability and discusses implications for feature merging.  
- Theoretical analysis (notably Lemma 1 and Theorem 2) is sound and convincingly establishes FIC applicability to LFMs.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using synthetic and real datasets, comparing against IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Empirical results show competitive or superior model selection accuracy and prediction performance.  
- Ablation study strongly supports the benefit of the proposed shrinkage acceleration.  
- Weakness: practical intuition about why the derived FIC form is beneficial is underexplained.  
- Weakness: computational complexity analysis is insufficient—needs finer-grained timing breakdowns across dataset sizes.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify FIC’s practical implications and expand computational performance analysis.
