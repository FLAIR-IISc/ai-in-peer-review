- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by overcoming the block-diagonal Hessian requirement.  
- Asymptotic analysis shows the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models, enabling FAB application.  
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method based on a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) to speed inference.  
- Demonstrates parameter identifiability and adds a merge post-processing step to improve results.  
- Technical derivations (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) are rigorous and clearly presented.  
- Novel contribution: original analysis addressing the Hessian condition and extending FAB to LFMs.  
- Practical improvements: substantial computational gains from shrinkage acceleration and useful merge/post-processing.  
- Empirical validation: strong experiments on synthetic and real datasets with comparisons to IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Ablation study effectively shows the impact of shrinkage acceleration.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; mathematical derivations and algorithms are clear.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of prior influence and sensitivity to prior settings.  
- Weakness: convergence properties (rate, stopping criteria, influence of tolerance δ) not fully analyzed.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical computational complexity analysis comparing FAB/LFM to alternatives.  
- Weakness: merge post-processing criteria and sensitivity to merging threshold need more detail.  
- Weakness: related work could better cover recent scalable VB advances for LFMs and comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated paper with rigorous theory and compelling experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
