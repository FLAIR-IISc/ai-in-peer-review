This paper proposes an extension of the factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to latent feature models (LFMs). The authors derive a factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs, which has the same form as that for mixture models, despite LFMs not satisfying the conditions required for FAB inference. The proposed FAB/LFM algorithm is shown to have several desirable properties, including automatic hidden state selection, parameter identifiability, and a monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed method. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs is rigorous and well-supported by theoretical analysis. The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM over existing methods, including state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process (IBP) methods, in terms of computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance.

The strengths of the paper include:

1. Theoretical contributions: The authors provide a rigorous derivation of the FIC for LFMs, which is a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian inference.
2. Empirical evaluations: The authors conduct thorough experimental evaluations of the proposed method, demonstrating its superiority over existing methods.
3. Clarity and organization: The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

1. Limited scope: The paper focuses on LFMs, which may limit its applicability to other types of models.
2. Computational complexity: While the authors propose an acceleration method to reduce computational cost, the method still requires significant computational resources.

To improve the paper, the authors could consider the following suggestions:

1. Provide more details on the acceleration method: While the authors propose an acceleration method, they do not provide sufficient details on its implementation and effectiveness.
2. Compare with other methods: The authors could compare their method with other state-of-the-art methods, such as variational inference and Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods.
3. Provide more insights on the theoretical contributions: The authors could provide more insights on the theoretical implications of their derivation of the FIC for LFMs.

In conclusion, the paper is well-written and provides significant contributions to the field of Bayesian inference. The authors demonstrate the superiority of their proposed method over existing methods, and the paper is a valuable addition to the literature.
