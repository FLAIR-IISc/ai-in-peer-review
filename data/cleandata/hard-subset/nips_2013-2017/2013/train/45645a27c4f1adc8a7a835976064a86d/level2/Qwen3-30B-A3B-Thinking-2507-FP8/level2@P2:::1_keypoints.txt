- Proposes extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), removing a previous Hessian-condition limitation.  
- Shows LFMs share the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as Mixture Models (MMs), enabling FAB application to LFMs.  
- Lemma 1: the log determinant of the Hessian for LFMs contains the key log ∑n znk term.  
- Theorem 4: establishes parameter identifiability for FAB/LFMs.  
- Introduces a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2) that significantly reduces computational cost.  
- Theoretical derivations are rigorous with proofs in supplementary material.  
- Empirical results: FAB/LFMs outperform IBP, VB, and MEIBP on model selection accuracy, prediction, and efficiency across simulations and five real datasets.  
- Shrinkage acceleration is well-motivated and effective, reducing cost without losing performance.  
- Empirical claims are under-supported: experiments are limited to five datasets and VB was omitted due to computational cost.  
- Theoretical insight that LFMs share FIC form with MMs is opaque and needs more intuitive explanation.  
- Limited analysis of "automatic hidden states selection"—insufficient evaluation of model selection behavior, especially near boundary cases.  
- Insufficient distinction from related methods (MAD-Bayes, MEIBP); relation to prior work should be clarified.  
- Clarity issues: dense theoretical sections may be inaccessible; clearer, more intuitive exposition needed.  
- Significance: addresses an important model-selection problem for LFMs but impact limited by specialized scope and narrow empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand empirical evaluation, clarify theoretical insights, and better compare to related methods.
