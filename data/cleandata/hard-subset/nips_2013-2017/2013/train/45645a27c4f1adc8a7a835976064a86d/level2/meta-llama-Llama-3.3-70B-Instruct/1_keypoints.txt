- Proposes extending factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Provides asymptotic analysis of the Hessian for LFMs, enabling a factorized information criterion (FIC) with the same form as for mixture models.  
- Derives FAB inference for LFMs with properties like automatic hidden-state selection and parameter identifiability.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains technical details.  
- Thorough analysis of the FAB algorithm, including a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) interpretation.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method for the algorithm.  
- Experiments show improved computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance.  
- Strength: novel and significant contribution to Bayesian inference for LFMs.  
- Strength: detailed theoretical and algorithmic analysis.  
- Strength: empirical demonstration of effectiveness.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in Bayesian inference and LFMs, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of method limitations and future research directions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, thorough analysis, and strong experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns, limited experiments, and insufficient discussion of limitations.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
