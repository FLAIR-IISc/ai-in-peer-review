- Paper extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) and shows the same FIC penalty ∑k log(∑n znk) arises asymptotically despite non-block-diagonal Hessians (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
- Authors derive a practical FAB/LFM algorithm with mean-field q(Z), CCCP interpretation, shrinkage-acceleration, and a merge post-processing motivated by identifiability.

Strengths:
- Novel asymptotic analysis broadening FAB beyond prior block-diagonal assumptions and clarifying LFM complexity as O(∑k log Nk).  
- Monotone lower-bound optimization with principled CCCP-based shrinkage that enables automatic feature selection.  
- Identifiability results are useful and justify a sensible merge heuristic.  
- Empirical results show compact models, improved predictive performance, reduced overfitting (PLL vs TLL), and effective ablation of shrinkage acceleration.

Weaknesses:
- Key assumptions for Lemma 1 (e.g., linear-Gaussian likelihood, diagonal precision, boundedness/regularity) are deferred to the supplement and not clearly stated in the main text.  
- Generality beyond Gaussian LFMs is unclear; finite-sample behavior is not discussed.  
- Mean-field factorization may weaken the bound; no comparisons to richer variational families or structured/tempered inference.  
- Fairness of baseline comparisons is questionable (fixed noise variance, default hyperparameters, limited tuning and run variance reported).  
- Scalability claims lack per-iteration complexity and memory analysis; experiments are Matlab-based and MEIBP can be faster in wall-clock time.  
- Limited evaluation on non-Gaussian likelihoods and canonical IBP tasks (e.g., link prediction).

Constructive suggestions:
- Move and state all assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 into the main text and discuss finite-sample limitations.  
- Add sensitivity analyses for initial K, Tshrink, merge thresholds; report mean±std over multiple runs and retune baselines (including noise variance).  
- Provide complexity analysis, larger-scale experiments, and release code for reproducibility.  
- Clarify applicability to non-Gaussian likelihoods or add related experiments/discussion and compare to MAD-Bayes under matched settings.

Meta assessment:
- Quality: Technically sound overall, though some proofs and assumptions are deferred.  
- Clarity: Good exposition but assumptions and complexity should be foregrounded.  
- Originality: Strong contribution.  
- Significance: High for automated model selection in LFMs.

Recommendation:
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, practical algorithm, solid empirical gains, and impactful scope.  
- Arguments against acceptance: buried assumptions, fairness and scalability concerns.  
- Overall verdict: lean accept.
