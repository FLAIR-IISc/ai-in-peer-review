- Proposes an extension of the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), previously inapplicable to FAB.  
- Derives a new Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs that has the same representation as for Mixture Models despite LFMs not satisfying the original Hessian condition.  
- Introduces the FAB/LFM algorithm with automatic hidden-state selection, parameter identifiability, and guaranteed monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound during optimization.  
- Experimental results show FAB/LFM outperforms state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process (IBP) methods in computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a clear, thorough explanation and derivation of the proposed method.  
- Novel contribution: first extension of FAB to LFMs and a new FIC derivation for LFMs.  
- Demonstrates potential wide applicability (e.g., computer vision, NLP, recommender systems).  
- Assumes strong background in Bayesian inference and latent variable models, which may limit accessibility for non-experts.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison with other existing methods such as Variational Bayesian (VB) approaches.  
- Does not clearly explain the computational complexity, raising concerns for large-scale datasets.  
- Overall assessment: strong, high-quality work rated 9/10 by the reviewer.
