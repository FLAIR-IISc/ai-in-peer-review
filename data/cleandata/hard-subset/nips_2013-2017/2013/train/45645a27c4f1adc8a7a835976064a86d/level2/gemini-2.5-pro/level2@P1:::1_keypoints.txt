- Proposes an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by asymptotically analyzing the complete-data log-likelihood Hessian.
- Lemma 1 shows the log determinant of the Hessian decomposes into terms dependent on per-feature data counts, enabling a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.
- The derived FIC matches the mixture-model form, allowing application of the FAB framework to latent feature selection.
- Develops an efficient iterative algorithm that automatically shrinks irrelevant features and selects the number of latent features.
- Interprets the optimization as a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and uses this insight to propose a practical shrinkage-acceleration technique.
- Proves an identifiability result preventing redundant features and proposes a feature-merging post-processing step motivated by this result.
- Empirically compares FAB/LFM to IBP-based methods (Gibbs, VB-IBP, MEIBP) on synthetic and real data, showing competitive or superior model selection, predictive accuracy, and runtime.
- Strength: technically sound and novel theoretical contribution (Hessian asymptotics in Lemma 1) that logically yields the FIC and algorithm.
- Strength: highly original extension of FAB to LFMs and novel practical contributions (shrinkage acceleration, merging).
- Strength: significant practical impact—automated model selection, computational efficiency, and strong empirical results likely valuable to practitioners.
- Weakness/Question: Lemma 1 depends on "mild assumptions" relegated to the supplement; the main text should summarize these assumptions and potential practical violations.
- Weakness/Question: The algorithm compounds approximations (Laplace, log-sum linearization, mean-field q(Z)); authors should discuss cumulative effects and when FIC is reliable, especially for small N.
- Weakness/Question: Initialization sensitivity—starting K = min(N,D) may be costly; clarify sensitivity of convergence and final result to initial K and choice of T_shrink for acceleration.
- Weakness/Question: Shrinkage-acceleration intuition could be clearer—explicitly state that inner µ/π iterations drive feature usage counts to zero faster to enable earlier pruning.
- Summary evaluation: strong, well-written, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated work; minor clarifications requested do not detract from overall quality.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theory, practical automated algorithm for LFMs, strong empirical performance, and useful theoretical insights (identifiability, CCCP).
- Arguments against acceptance: exposition could better address key assumptions and discuss impacts of multiple approximations.
