- Proposes extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) via a derived Factorized Information Criterion (FIC).  
- Shows the FIC for LFMs has the same form as for Mixture Models despite LFMs not meeting standard FAB conditions.  
- Introduces the FAB/LFM algorithm with desirable properties: automatic hidden state selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound.  
- Derivation of the FIC for LFMs is rigorous and well-explained, with a thorough analysis of algorithmic properties.  
- Experimental results demonstrate FAB/LFM outperforms existing methods, including state-of-the-art IBP approaches.  
- Provides a unified FAB framework applicable to both Mixture Models and more general discrete latent variable models.  
- Identifies a model-induced regularization effect from Bayesian marginalization that aids robustness to overfitting and feature selection.  
- Empirical results show good predictive performance and low computational cost, with automatic selection of feature dimensionality.  
- Paper is well-written and clear; authors give a concise introduction and motivate the algorithm effectively.  
- Major strength: significant contribution to Bayesian inference with a novel, generalizable algorithmic framework.  
- Potential weakness: assumes a specific form for the prior on latent variables, leaving extensibility to more complex priors unclear.  
- Potential weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, valuable algorithmic addition, strong empirical performance, and thorough property analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive prior assumption and insufficient computational complexity/scalability analysis.
