- Extends FAB to latent feature models (binary features, linear-Gaussian observations); Lemma 1 shows log-det Hessian ≈ ∑k log(∑n z_nk), yielding an FIC for LFMs like mixtures.
- Enables an FAB lower-bound algorithm with automatic feature shrinkage, a CCCP-based acceleration for faster elimination, and a post-hoc merge step from identifiability analysis.
- Experiments (synthetic, block patterns, real data) show FAB often selects more compact K, achieves stronger predictive log-likelihood, and is computationally competitive; MEIBP can be faster but worse predictively.
- Novelty: large-sample Laplace asymptotic gives FIC despite non-block-diagonal Hessian; CCCP E-step view and identifiability/merge analysis appear new.
- Strength: Theoretical lemma is insightful and, under stated conditions, justifies the same log(∑n z_nk) complexity term that drives automatic shrinkage.
- Strength: Algorithmic design (mean-field q(Z), CCCP acceleration, monotonic lower-bound guarantee) is clean, practical, and well-motivated.
- Strength: Empirically finds compact models with strong predictive performance and benefits from early-shrinkage acceleration.
- Strength: Clear discussion of the regularizer D/(2Nπ̃_k) and its role in shrinkage.
- Concern: Key assumptions for Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 are deferred to the supplement; they should be explicit in the main text with a proof sketch.
- Concern: Unclear how degeneracies (∑n z_nk ≈ 0 or N) and conditions on Λ, W, π affect the asymptotic approximation and validity.
- Concern: The cumulative approximation gap after mean-field factorization and convex linearization is not quantified or discussed for finite N.
- Concern: Experimental fairness issues — FAB infers noise precision λ while baselines used fixed variance; IBP α fixed; MEIBP initial K chosen ad hoc, potentially biasing comparisons.
- Concern: Limited task scope focused on matrix reconstruction; lacks link-prediction/network latent feature evaluations common for IBP LFMs.
- Concern: Missing computational analysis — no per-iteration complexity, convergence plots across N/D/K, or ablation of Tshrink vs total runtime and final K.
- Concern: Merge step theoretical guarantee is narrow (exact equality); practical thresholding lacks principled criterion or guarantees and needs reporting of empirical impact.
- Suggestion: State exact assumptions and give a brief proof outline for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 in the main paper; clarify handling of degeneracies.
- Suggestion: Empirically study bound tightness by comparing FIC, the lower bound L, and approximate marginal log-likelihood on synthetic data.
- Suggestion: Re-run key experiments with λ fixed across methods, infer or cross-validate IBP α and variance where possible, and run sensitivity analyses for MEIBP initial K; standardize hardware/time reporting.
- Suggestion: Include at least one link-prediction/network latent feature task or justify the focus on matrix data.
- Suggestion: Add per-iteration complexity discussion, convergence diagnostics, and ablations for Tshrink and merging behavior.
- Suggestion: Clarify the merge heuristic (criterion, thresholding) and report how often and how much merges improve the objective L.
- Minor: Add an intuitive explanation for why log det F(d) decomposes into ∑k log(∑n z_nk), fix typos, and emphasize monotonicity applies to the lower-bound L, not the true FIC.
- For acceptance: Novel, nontrivial theoretical extension with a practical algorithm, strong automatic model selection, and useful acceleration — potentially impactful alternative to IBP inference.
- Against acceptance: Opaque theoretical assumptions, experimental fairness and scope issues, and insufficient complexity/ablation analysis.
- Overall: Solid and promising contribution; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing theoretical clarity and experimental fairness concerns.
