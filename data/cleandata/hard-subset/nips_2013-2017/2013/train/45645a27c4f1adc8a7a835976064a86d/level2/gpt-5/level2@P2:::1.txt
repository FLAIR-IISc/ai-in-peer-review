Summary
This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian observation models. The key theoretical result (Lemma 1) shows that the log-determinant of the per-dimension Hessian of the complete-data log-likelihood scales as the sum over features of log of effective sample sizes (sum_n z_nk), yielding an FIC for LFMs that has the same form as that for mixture models. This enables an FAB lower-bound algorithm with automatic feature shrinkage, a CCCP-based acceleration for faster elimination of irrelevant features, and a post-hoc merge step motivated by an identifiability analysis. Experiments on synthetic data, block patterns, and several real datasets compare FAB/LFM to IBP (Gibbs), variational IBP, and MEIBP, showing advantages in model selection, predictive log-likelihood, and often computational cost (especially vs VB), with MEIBP being faster in some regimes but with worse predictive performance.

Relation to prior work
FAB was previously developed for mixtures and HMMs (Fujimaki et al.). LFMs have been primarily addressed by nonparametric Bayesian approaches such as the IBP (Griffiths & Ghahramani), with accelerated Gibbs sampling and variational methods (Doshi-Velez et al.) and MAP/submodular approaches (MEIBP; Reed & Ghahramani). MAD-Bayes (Broderick et al.) derives MAP-regularizers from a small-noise limit. This work differs by providing a large-sample Laplace-based asymptotic that yields an FIC for LFMs despite the lack of block-diagonal Hessian structure, enabling FAB-like automation without priors. The CCCP view of the E-step and the identifiability/merge analysis appear novel in this context.

Strengths
- Theoretical contribution: The asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1) is insightful and, if correct under stated conditions, justifies an FIC for LFMs with the same key log(∑n z_nk) complexity term known to drive automatic shrinkage and identifiability in FAB for mixtures.
- Algorithmic design: The FAB lower bound with mean-field q(Z), the CCCP interpretation and acceleration, and a monotonic increase guarantee are clean and practical. The identifiability analysis motivates a principled merge post-processing step.
- Empirical performance: Across synthetic and real datasets, FAB often selects more compact K with strong predictive log-likelihood, and is computationally competitive; the acceleration effectively reduces runtime by early shrinkage.
- Clarity of main ideas: The derivation is well motivated; the role of the regularization term D/(2Nπ̃_k) in shrinkage is transparently discussed.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Assumptions behind Lemma 1 and Theorem 2: The main paper defers the “mild assumptions” and proofs to the supplement. Given the centrality of log det F(d) ≈ ∑k log(∑n z_nk), the paper should state the assumptions explicitly in the main text and give a proof sketch. It is important to know conditions on Λ, W, π, and the occupancy counts (e.g., 0 < ∑n z_nk < N) and how violations (all-zero or all-one features) are handled asymptotically.
- Bound tightness after mean-field and linearization: Once the mean-field approximation on q(Z) and convex linearization are applied, the objective L is a tractable lower bound to FIC, which itself approximates the marginal log-likelihood. The paper would benefit from a clearer discussion of the cumulative approximation gap and when it remains small (e.g., large-N regimes), beyond the asymptotic equivalence of FIC to log evidence.
- Experimental fairness: FAB learns noise precision λ, while competing codes (IBP, VB, MEIBP) did not, and were run with fixed noise SD (1.0 or 0.75). This likely advantages FAB in predictive likelihood. Similarly, IBP’s α was fixed to 3 (potentially inflating K), and MEIBP initial K was set ad hoc (e.g., 100). More balanced baselines (inferring variance where possible; sensitivity analysis for α and initial K; or fixing λ across all methods) are needed to support the strength of claims.
- Scope of tasks: Evaluations are mostly reconstruction/prediction on matrix data. Since IBP LFMs are widely used in link prediction and network models, a task in that regime (e.g., latent attribute models) would strengthen significance. VB was omitted due to runtime; at least a subset of datasets or a tighter implementation would help triangulate performance.
- Computational analysis: While acceleration helps, the paper does not quantify per-iteration complexity or show convergence plots across N, D, K. A complexity discussion and ablation (number of CCCP steps Tshrink vs total runtime and final K) would improve the empirical section.
- Merge step: The identifiability theorem is interesting but narrow (exact equality w_k = w_l). In practice, a threshold is used; a more principled criterion (e.g., bound improvement guarantee or statistical test) and reporting of how often and how much the merge improves L would be useful.

Suggestions for improvement
- State the exact assumptions and a brief proof outline for Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 in the main paper; clarify how degeneracies (∑n z_nk near 0 or N) affect the approximation and how the shrinkage step preserves the validity of the asymptotics.
- Provide a discussion on the tightness of the bound with mean-field and linearization; if possible, include a small study comparing FIC, its lower bound, and (approximate) marginal log-likelihood on synthetic data.
- Re-run key experiments with: (a) λ fixed across all methods (to isolate the effect of model selection), and (b) IBP with inferred α (or cross-validated α) and variance (if available), plus sensitivity analyses for MEIBP initial K. Report wall-clock with standardized hardware and iterations to convergence.
- Include at least one link-prediction/network latent feature task, or justify the focus on matrix data.
- Add per-iteration complexity and convergence diagnostics; ablate Tshrink and merging.
- Clarify the merge heuristic: criterion, thresholding, and monotonicity of L after merges; report quantitative impact.

Minor comments
- A brief intuitive explanation of why log det F(d) decomposes into ∑k log(∑n z_nk) would aid readers.
- Some typos and minor grammar issues exist; a careful proofreading would help.
- Make explicit that the monotonicity guarantee applies to the lower bound L, not the true FIC.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel and nontrivial theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs with a clean, practically useful algorithm.
- Strong automatic model selection behavior, good empirical performance, and a compelling CCCP-based acceleration.
- Potentially impactful alternative to IBP-style inference for LFMs.

Arguments against acceptance
- Key theoretical assumptions are not transparent in the main text; proofs are deferred.
- Experimental comparisons have fairness issues (noise variance, α, initial K), and the task scope is limited.
- Limited complexity analysis and ablations.

Overall evaluation
This is a solid and promising contribution. With clearer theoretical assumptions and more balanced experiments, it would be a strong paper. I lean toward acceptance, contingent on addressing the fairness and clarity concerns.
