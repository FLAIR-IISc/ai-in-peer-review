This paper proposes an extension of the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), which have not been applicable to FAB due to the lack of a specific condition on the Hessian matrix of the complete log-likelihood. The authors derive a new form of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs, which has the same representation as that for Mixture Models (MMs), despite the fact that LFMs do not satisfy the required condition. The proposed FAB/LFM algorithm is shown to have several desirable properties, including automatic hidden state selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound through iterative optimization.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the proposed method. The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM over existing methods, including state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process (IBP) methods, in terms of computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a new and innovative extension of the FAB inference framework to LFMs, which has not been applicable to FAB previously.
* The authors provide a thorough and clear explanation of the proposed method, including the derivation of the new FIC form for LFMs.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM over existing methods, including state-of-the-art IBP methods.
* The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise summary of the proposed method and its contributions.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a strong background in Bayesian inference and latent variable models, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison of FAB/LFM with other existing methods, such as Variational Bayesian (VB) methods, which may be considered as a limitation.
* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which may be an important consideration for large-scale datasets.
