- Main contribution: Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by showing the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for Mixture Models via asymptotic Hessian analysis.
- Key claims: Enables FAB for LFMs and yields automatic hidden-state selection, parameter identifiability, and improved model selection/prediction/computational efficiency versus IBP methods.
- Strength — Novelty: Overcomes a prior limitation of FAB, providing a theoretically significant generalization to LFMs.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Extensive experiments on real-world datasets show superior performance to IBP, VB, and MEIBP in selection, prediction, and speed.
- Strength — Theoretical insight: Provides rigorous proofs of identifiability and introduces a shrinkage-acceleration mechanism that reduces computational cost.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-organized with clear algorithmic exposition and supplementary materials for detailed proofs.
- Weakness — Limited limitations discussion: Insufficient analysis of other failure modes (e.g., sensitivity to initialization, assumptions in Lemma 1) beyond small-sample FIC accuracy.
- Weakness — Comparative analysis gap: Does not fully explore scenarios where MEIBP has computational advantages or trade-offs between efficiency and predictive performance.
- Weakness — Reproducibility concerns: Some implementation details and parameter-tuning choices for FAB and baselines are not fully specified.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address limitations and reproducibility concerns.
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, strong empirical results, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: Need for a deeper discussion of limitations/trade-offs and clearer reproducibility/parameter-tuning details.
