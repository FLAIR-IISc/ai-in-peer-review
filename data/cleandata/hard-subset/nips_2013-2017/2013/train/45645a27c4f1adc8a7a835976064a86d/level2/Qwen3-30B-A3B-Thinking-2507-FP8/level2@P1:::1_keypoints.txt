- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), filling a significant gap.  
- Shows LFMs yield the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as mixture models despite not satisfying block-diagonal Hessian assumptions.  
- Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 provide rigorous theoretical foundation for FIC equivalence between LFMs and mixture models.  
- Introduces a novel shrinkage acceleration method based on a CCCP interpretation that eliminates irrelevant features early and reduces computation.  
- Theorem 4 identifiability analysis motivates a merge post-processing procedure that improves model compactness.  
- Empirical results: better model selection accuracy (especially for large N), competitive predictive performance, and substantially reduced runtime vs. IBP, MEIBP, and other baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with consistent notation and detailed supplementary proofs.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and include artificial, block, and real-world datasets with appropriate baselines and metrics.  
- Main strengths: theoretical novelty, practical computational improvements, and automatic hyperparameter-free model selection beneficial for practitioners.  
- Weakness 1: Claim of “automatic hidden states selection” needs clearer differentiation from IBP/variational methods that also select complexity.  
- Weakness 2: Omission of VB from experiments is justified by runtime but lacks a detailed complexity comparison explaining FAB’s efficiency advantage.  
- Weakness 3: Identifiability theory is sound but could use more discussion of its practical impact beyond theoretical guarantees.  
- Significance: Substantial — enables a theoretically grounded, efficient approach to model selection for LFMs, valuable for large-scale data.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
