- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), removing a prior block-diagonal Hessian requirement.  
- Core technical contribution: Lemma 1 proving LFMs asymptotically yield the same FIC form as mixture models despite violating block-diagonal condition.  
- Enables FAB/LFMs to inherit automatic dimensionality selection and parameter identifiability properties.  
- Develops shrinkage acceleration method interpreted via convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and implemented as Algorithm 2.  
- Provides a merge post-processing step with an identifiability proof (Theorem 4) to handle redundant features.  
- Empirical results show superior model selection accuracy and computational efficiency versus IBP, VB, and MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Rigorous asymptotic analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) addresses a fundamental gap in FAB applicability.  
- Derivation of FIC for LFMs is technically sound and clearly connected to prior mixture-model work (e.g., FICMM).  
- Shrinkage acceleration is well-motivated and empirically reduces computational cost without harming objective value (Figure 1).  
- Identifiability proof offers a theoretically grounded post-processing strategy for latent feature merging.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive: model selection, feature extraction, and predictive performance favor FAB/LFMs and produce compact models with less overfitting.  
- Weakness: limited experimental analysis of the merge post-processing—no ablation quantifying its impact.  
- Weakness: CCCP interpretation (Section 3.1) and link between Eq. (14) and FAB update (11) need clearer exposition or an illustrative example.  
- Weakness: omission of VB runtimes leaves the computational-efficiency claim without full context; supplementary-dependence could be more explicitly cited in the main text.  
- Overall: significant, well-justified contribution advancing Bayesian model selection for latent-variable models; minor clarifications would improve reproducibility and impact.
