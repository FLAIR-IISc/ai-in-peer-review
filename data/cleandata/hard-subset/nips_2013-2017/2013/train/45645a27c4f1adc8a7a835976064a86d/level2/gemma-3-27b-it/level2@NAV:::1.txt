## Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference – a promising Bayesian approximation method – to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The core contribution lies in demonstrating that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs takes the same form as that for Mixture Models, despite LFMs not initially satisfying the conditions required for standard FAB application. This allows leveraging the desirable properties of FAB (automatic model selection, parameter identifiability) within the LFM framework. The authors further propose a shrinkage acceleration method based on a CCCP interpretation of the FAB E-step and show parameter identifiability within their framework.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly explaining the limitations of existing FAB applications and the potential benefits of extending it to LFMs. The theoretical analysis, particularly Lemma 1 and Theorem 2, appears sound and convincingly demonstrates the applicability of FIC to LFMs. The introduction of the shrinkage acceleration method, inspired by the CCCP analysis, is a valuable contribution aimed at improving computational efficiency. The discussion of parameter identifiability and its implications for feature merging is also insightful. 

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, utilizing both synthetic and real-world datasets. The results demonstrate that FAB/LFMs achieve competitive or superior performance compared to existing methods like IBP, VB, and MEIBP, particularly in terms of model selection accuracy and prediction performance. The ablation study highlighting the benefits of the shrinkage acceleration is particularly strong.

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses. While the theoretical justification is solid, the explanation of the practical impact of the derived FIC is sometimes lacking. A more intuitive explanation of why this particular form of the FIC is beneficial could enhance understanding. The experimental section, while thorough, could benefit from more detailed analysis of the computational complexity. Specifically, a more granular breakdown of the timings for each method across different dataset sizes would be helpful.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of FAB inference to LFMs, expanding the applicability of this promising technique.
*   Solid theoretical foundation with clear and convincing mathematical derivations.
*   Demonstrated empirical performance competitive with or exceeding state-of-the-art methods.
*   Practical refinement through the shrinkage acceleration method.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Explanation of the practical benefits of the derived FIC could be improved.
*   More detailed analysis of computational complexity is needed.

**Overall:** This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent variable models. Despite minor weaknesses, the novelty, theoretical soundness, and empirical results justify acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify the practical implications of the FIC and provide a more detailed analysis of computational performance.
