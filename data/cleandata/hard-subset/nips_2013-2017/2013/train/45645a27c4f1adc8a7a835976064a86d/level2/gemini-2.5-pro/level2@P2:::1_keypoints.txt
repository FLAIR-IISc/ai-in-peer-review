1. Extension of FAB to Latent Feature Models via asymptotic Hessian analysis, yielding an FIC for LFMs and the FAB/LFM algorithm that auto-selects feature count.  
2. CCCP interpretation of the update rule enabling an effective acceleration technique.  
3. Identifiability analysis motivates a feature-merging post-processing step.  
4. Empirical comparison to IBP variants and variational EM shows FAB/LFM is often superior or competitive in model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency.  
5. Significant and novel theoretical contribution: moves FAB from block-diagonal parameter models to shared-parameter LFMs, with Lemma 1 recovering the key sum_k log(sum_n z_nk) term.  
6. Sound theoretical support: derivation of FIC for LFMs, CCCP connection, and identifiability theorem (proofs in supplement).  
7. Strong empirical evaluation: well-chosen baselines, multiple metrics (speed, feature recovery, predictive likelihood), and demonstration of shrinkage acceleration effectiveness.  
8. Clear writing and organization: logical flow and clear distinction from variational EM updates.  
9. Weakness: key assumptions underlying Lemma 1 are deferred to the supplementary material and not summarized in the main text.  
10. Weakness: could strengthen the contrast to BIC by explicitly showing BIC vs. FIC penalties and explaining why FIC is more appropriate.  
11. Weakness: experimental choices (learning vs. fixing noise parameter lambda, IBP hyperparameter alpha) may affect fairness of comparisons and need discussion of impact and sensitivity.  
12. Overall verdict: high-quality, impactful paper with minor presentation and experimental-setup issues; recommendation to accept.
