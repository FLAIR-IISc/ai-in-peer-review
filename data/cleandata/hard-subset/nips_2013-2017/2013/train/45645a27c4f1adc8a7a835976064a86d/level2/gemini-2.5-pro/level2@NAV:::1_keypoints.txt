- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming the non-block-diagonal Hessian issue.  
- Proves a novel asymptotic result (Lemma 1) showing the FIC for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models.  
- Develops an efficient FAB/LFM algorithm for simultaneous parameter estimation and automatic model selection (number of latent features).  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration technique via a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) interpretation.  
- Proves the method resolves parameter identifiability issues inherent in LFMs.  
- Originality/significance: meaningfully broadens FAB applicability; core theoretical insight is non-trivial and elegant.  
- Quality: theoretical claims are well-motivated and supported by comprehensive synthetic and real-data experiments.  
- Empirical results: outperforms IBP and variants in model selection accuracy, predictive performance on missing data, and computational efficiency; shows less overfitting.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written; algorithmic contributions (acceleration, merging) are clearly explained and justified.  
- Completeness concern: "mild assumptions" and rigorous proofs for Lemma 1 are deferred to the supplement and should be briefly stated in the main text.  
- Limitation: asymptotic FIC can be less accurate in small-sample regimes (e.g., Libras dataset); this trade-off versus full Bayesian methods should be discussed more prominently.  
- Overall: high-quality contribution combining novel theory, practical algorithms, and convincing empirical validation.
