- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMM models to latent feature models (LFMs).
- FAB maximizes a lower bound of a factorized information criterion (FIC) that converges to marginal log-likelihood.
- Previous FAB limitation: required block-diagonal Hessian of complete likelihood, which LFMs do not satisfy.
- Authors derive a lower bound of FIC applicable to LFMs despite the Hessian condition violation.
- Show the derived FIC for LFMs has the same representation as the FIC for mixture models.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets.
- Compared against fast Gibbs sampling for IBP, variational Bayes (VB), and MEIBP.
- Results indicate superior predictive performance and lower computational cost for FAB/LFM.
- Paper is well written with thorough derivation steps.
- Contribution is somewhat incremental but interesting and relevant to the NIPS community; reviewer supports acceptance.
