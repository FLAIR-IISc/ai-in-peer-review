- Proposes a novel model-selection criterion for binary latent feature models that integrates out parameters via BIC, yielding an EM-like algorithm with complexity penalties.  
- Shows improved held-out/predictive likelihoods and faster runtimes compared to several IBP implementations.  
- Approach is reasonable and motivated by a plausible asymptotic argument.  
- Technical contribution is novel but incremental—essentially an extension of prior FAB work ([3],[4]).  
- Mathematical exposition is imprecise in places (e.g., equation (3) — log-sum of z may be infinite if all zeros, raising questions about FIC).  
- Theorem 2 lacks specification of the asymptotic regime and assumptions, particularly how K scales with N; BIC-based approximation seems to require fixed finite K, conflicting with IBP’s infinite-K motivation.  
- Minor formal errors: section 2’s pk(X | z·,k) should condition only on data assigned to component k; equation (4) is missing an expectation.  
- Experimental results (Table 1) report faster convergence for FAB, but stopping criteria are unspecified, making runtime comparisons and Gibbs sampler truncation arbitrary.  
- Unclear whether predictive improvements come from the model-selection criterion or from algorithms avoiding worse local optima; Gibbs’s tendency to learn too many components suggests implementation/local-optima issues.  
- Overall assessment: sensible but somewhat incremental method, with some imprecise math and insufficient experimental analysis to fully interpret the reported gains.
