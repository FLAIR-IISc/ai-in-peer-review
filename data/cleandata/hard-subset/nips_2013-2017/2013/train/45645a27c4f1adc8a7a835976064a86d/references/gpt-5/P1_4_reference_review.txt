Summary
The paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMM settings to linear-Gaussian latent feature models (LFMs). The main theoretical contribution is Lemma 1, showing that the log-determinant of the per-dimension Hessian of the complete-data negative log-likelihood behaves as sum_k log sum_n z_nk (up to Op(1)), even though LFMs do not satisfy the block-diagonal Hessian condition that underpinned prior FAB results for MMs. This yields an FIC for LFMs with the same form as for MMs, hence enabling an FAB algorithm that automatically selects the number of features without hand-tuned hyperparameters. The algorithm optimizes a tractable lower bound via mean-field q(z), offers a monotone increase guarantee, and introduces a CCCP interpretation of the E-step that motivates a shrinkage-acceleration routine. The paper also proves an identifiability result implying FAB favors identifiable solutions and suggests a principled merge post-processing when columns of W coincide. Experiments on synthetic, block data, and several real datasets compare FAB to EM (variational mean-field without the FAB term), IBP (Gibbs), VB (finite K), and MEIBP (submodular MAP), showing faster convergence than VB, competitive or better model selection accuracy than IBP/MEIBP, and strong predictive log-likelihood with compact K.

Relation to prior work
- Builds on FAB for mixtures/HMMs (Fujimaki and co-authors), and on FIC as an asymptotic approximation to marginal likelihood.
- Connects to IBP-based Bayesian nonparametrics (Gibbs, VB, MEIBP), highlighting differences: FAB is prior-agnostic asymptotically and avoids hyperparameters; MEIBP is MAP with submodular structure; MAD-Bayes derives asymptotics in a small-noise limit.
- Uses CCCP (Yuille and Rangarajan) to reinterpret and accelerate E-steps.
- Discusses identifiability in the context of Watanabe’s singular learning theory and EM degeneracies.

Strengths
- Novel and nontrivial theoretical extension: deriving an FIC for LFMs despite lack of block diagonal structure, with the same regularization term log sum_n z_nk that is key to automatic shrinkage.
- Clean algorithmic design: mean-field lower bound with closed-form M-step, CCCP-based E-step that is intuitive and provably monotone on the bound, and a practical shrinkage acceleration that demonstrably reduces runtime.
- Useful identifiability analysis; the merge heuristic is well motivated by the theory.
- Empirical results are broad: synthetic recovery and scaling, block-structure discovery, and real datasets with predictive evaluation. FAB selects compact K and reduces overfitting (PLL–TLL gaps).

Weaknesses and concerns
- The central Lemma 1 is deferred to the supplement with “mild assumptions.” For a NeurIPS audience, at least a precise statement of assumptions (e.g., diagonal noise, centering, conditions on Z usage frequencies, regularity bounds on W, Λ, scaling in N and K) and a proof sketch in the main text would strengthen confidence. It is unclear how robust the result is beyond diagonal Λ, or under correlated features/collinearity.
- The mean-field factorization q(zn)=∏k q̃(znk) breaks the exact FIC interpretation (Eq. 1). While common in VB, this adds an additional approximation whose effect on model selection consistency is not analyzed.
- Comparisons may be somewhat unfair: IBP/VB/MEIBP do not learn noise variance in the provided implementations, whereas FAB/EM estimate Λ; the paper fixes σ to 1 or 0.75 for baselines, which could disadvantage them in PLL. MEIBP is omitted on negative-valued datasets; for yaleB/USPS it required manual K initialization. Please discuss the sensitivity of FAB to Λ estimation and provide baselines that also learn noise where feasible.
- The CCCP-based accelerateShrinkage uses multiple inner iterations (Tshrink=100), yet complexity is not analyzed. A per-iteration cost and wall-clock scaling w.r.t. N, D, K (with/without acceleration) would help practitioners.
- The identifiability result addresses exactly equal columns; in practice columns are near-duplicate. The merge procedure relies on Euclidean distance heuristics—guidance for thresholds and a guarantee that merging increases the bound would be valuable.
- Scope: the method targets linear-Gaussian LFMs with diagonal Λ. How does the FIC term and algorithm extend to non-Gaussian likelihoods (e.g., Bernoulli/Poisson) or non-diagonal Λ?

Quality
Overall technically sound and carefully linked to prior FAB work. The monotonicity of the lower bound updates and CCCP equivalence are convincing. The main theoretical novelty (Lemma 1 and Theorem 2) is pivotal but under-detailed in the main text. Experiments are solid but could be made more balanced by harmonizing likelihood models (noise estimation) across methods and reporting more sensitivity analyses (e.g., rare features with small ∑n znk).

Clarity
Generally clear and well organized. The algorithm boxes are helpful. However:
- State all assumptions for Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 in the main paper and add a proof sketch.
- Provide computational complexity and implementation details for accelerateShrinkage and merge (include threshold selection and stopping conditions).
- Clarify the role of π: it is estimated by MLE (π=∑n μnk/N), not with a Beta prior; connect this to the “asymptotically ignores priors” claim.

Originality
High. Showing that the FIC for LFMs matches the MM case without block-diagonal Hessians is new and interesting. The CCCP interpretation and identifiability-guided merging are also novel in this context.

Significance
Meaningful for scalable automated model selection in LFMs. The approach removes the need for nonparametric priors and hyperparameters while retaining strong performance. If the theoretical claims hold broadly, the framework could influence work beyond LFMs.

Questions for the authors
- Please state the precise assumptions for Lemma 1 (on Λ, W, scaling of K with N, bounds on ∑n znk, etc.) and provide a sketch of the proof.
- How sensitive is FAB to rare-but-true features (small ∑n znk), given the −(D/2) log ∑n znk penalty? Can it miss rare features?
- Can you provide results where IBP/VB/MEIBP also estimate noise variance (or ablate FAB to fixed Λ) to equalize comparisons?
- What are the time/space complexities per iteration with and without accelerateShrinkage, and typical values of Tshrink that balance speed and convergence?
- Any insights or preliminary results for non-Gaussian likelihoods?

Pros
- Elegant theory bridging FAB and LFMs; automatic K selection.
- Monotone bound optimization; practical acceleration; identifiability-aware merging.
- Strong empirical performance and compact models.

Cons
- Key lemma’s assumptions/proof not visible in main text.
- Some comparison choices may favor FAB (noise learning).
- Limited discussion of limitations (non-diagonal Λ, non-Gaussian data) and complexity.

Recommendation
Lean accept, contingent on clarifying Lemma 1 assumptions/proof sketch and improving experimental fairness and complexity reporting. The paper offers a substantial theoretical and practical advance likely to interest the NeurIPS community.

Confidence
High on algorithmic/empirical assessment; medium on the full generality of the asymptotic theory pending main-text assumptions.
