

# Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

This paper proposes extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), claiming it achieves automatic model selection without hyperparameters. The authors demonstrate that FAB/LFMs have the same FIC representation as FAB/MMs despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition required by previous FAB work. The paper introduces a shrinkage acceleration technique and proves parameter identifiability, with experiments showing competitive performance.

**Strengths:** The theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs is non-trivial and addresses an important gap in the literature. The shrinkage acceleration technique is well-motivated and empirically validated. The identifiability theorem provides a valuable theoretical foundation for the algorithm's behavior. The experimental results on synthetic and real data demonstrate practical advantages over competing methods.

**Weaknesses:** Despite the promising contributions, the paper contains several minor but significant methodological inconsistencies and presentation issues that undermine confidence in the technical claims:

1. **Notation inconsistencies:** The paper uses inconsistent notation for the same concepts (e.g., "logN" vs. "log(N)" in equations (3) and (8), "D/2Nπ̃k" vs. "-D/2Nπ̃k" in equations (4) and (11)). These inconsistencies create confusion about the mathematical formulation.

2. **Lemma 1 formulation:** Lemma 1 states "log det |F(d)| = ∑k log ∑n znk N +Op(1)" which appears to be missing parentheses. The correct form should be "log det |F(d)| = ∑k log(∑n znk)/N + Op(1)" to avoid ambiguity.

3. **Theorem 4 ambiguity:** Theorem 4 states "w∗·k = w∗·l is a sufficient condition of ∑n µ∗nk/N = ∑n µ∗nl/N" but this is mathematically incorrect. If w·k = w·l, then the condition ∑n µnk/N = ∑n µnl/N is necessary for identifiability, not sufficient. This represents a significant theoretical error.

4. **Algorithm 1 and 2 details:** Algorithm 1 calls "merge({µn},W)" on line 10 without explaining what this function does, despite being critical to understanding the algorithm. Algorithm 2 uses "Tshrink = 100" without justification or sensitivity analysis.

5. **Experimental justification:** The paper claims "we omitted VB because of its long computational time" but provides no empirical evidence to support this claim, making the comparison unfair.

6. **Citation issues:** The paper cites "MAD-Bayes" [1] as a related method but doesn't clarify how it differs from the FAB approach, which is important for context.

7. **Supplementary material references:** The paper repeatedly refers to "supplementary materials" for rigorous proofs but doesn't provide any details about what's included, making it difficult for readers to verify claims.

8. **Terminology confusion:** The paper states "FAB inference places more importance on automation" but provides no quantitative comparison to other methods that prioritize automation, making this claim unsubstantiated.

The paper's most serious issue is the theoretical error in Theorem 4, which fundamentally undermines the identifiability claim. While the paper presents a novel extension of FAB to LFMs, this theoretical flaw, combined with the notational inconsistencies and lack of experimental justification for key claims, significantly weakens the contribution.

**Recommendation:** Reject. While the paper addresses an interesting problem and makes some valuable contributions, the methodological inconsistencies and theoretical errors are too severe to overlook. The paper would require substantial revision to address the mathematical errors and presentation issues before it could be considered for publication. The minor flaws identified here collectively undermine the paper's technical credibility, and a perfectionist reviewer must reject papers with such fundamental issues, regardless of the potential significance of the work.
