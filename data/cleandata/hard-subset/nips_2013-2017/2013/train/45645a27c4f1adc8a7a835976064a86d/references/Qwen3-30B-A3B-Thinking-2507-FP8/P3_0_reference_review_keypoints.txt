- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), broadening FAB's applicability.  
- Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 show the Hessian log-determinant for LFMs asymptotically contains the same log(∑_n z_nk) term as mixture models.  
- Derives the Factorized Information Criterion (Equation 9) for LFMs and presents a corresponding algorithm (Algorithm 1) with rigorous proofs.  
- CCCP interpretation (Section 3.1) provides a principled theoretical foundation for the shrinkage acceleration method.  
- Theorem 4 establishes identifiability results for LFMs and motivates a practical merge post-processing step.  
- Experiments show FAB/LFMs outperform IBP, VB, and MEIBP in model selection accuracy (especially for large N) and computational efficiency.  
- The acceleration method effectively reduces computational cost without harming model selection accuracy (supported by simulations).  
- Block data experiments demonstrate FAB's ability to extract true patterns and avoid irrelevant features.  
- Real-world experiments are limited to four datasets and omit VB comparisons without justification.  
- Claims of consistently good predictive performance lack statistical significance tests or rigorous evidence.  
- MEIBP comparison may be unfair because different initial K values were used for some datasets, potentially biasing results.  
- Paper fails to clearly differentiate FAB's automatic model selection from IBP and VB in theory or practice.  
- Insufficient discussion of how this approach compares to prior nonparametric Bayesian LFM methods (e.g., Miller et al., NIPS 2009).  
- Some claims are overstated given the limited experimental support, and trade-offs (e.g., asymptotic behavior on small datasets) are under-discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments, add statistical tests, clarify differences from existing methods, and discuss trade-offs.
