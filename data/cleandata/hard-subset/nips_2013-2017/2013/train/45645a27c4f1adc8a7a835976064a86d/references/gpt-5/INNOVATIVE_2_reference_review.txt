Summary
The paper extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework from mixture and HMM settings to latent feature models (LFMs). The key theoretical result (Lemma 1) shows that, despite LFMs not meeting the block-diagonal Hessian assumptions previously required by FAB, the determinant of the per-dimension Hessian contributes an asymptotic term of the form sum_k log sum_n z_nk, yielding an FIC for LFMs that matches the form previously derived for mixture models. This leads to an FIC lower bound (FICLFM) and a practical FAB algorithm with mean-field q(Z), closed-form M-step updates, and an E-step that admits a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) interpretation. Leveraging this, the authors introduce a shrinkage acceleration strategy that quickly eliminates irrelevant features, and provide an identifiability analysis that motivates a merging post-processing. Experiments on synthetic and real datasets compare FAB to IBP (Gibbs and VB variants), MEIBP, and EM, showing fast model selection, compact representations, and competitive or better predictive log-likelihoods at substantially reduced cost.

Relation to prior work
- Connects to FAB for MMs and HMMs (Fujimaki et al.), demonstrating that FIC-style asymptotics apply more broadly than previously thought.
- Contrasts with IBP-based Bayesian nonparametrics (Gibbs, VB, MEIBP), emphasizing automation and asymptotic prior-ignorance vs posterior/hyperparameter-driven model selection.
- Relates to Watanabe’s singular learning theory and “model-induced regularization,” and to MAD-Bayes asymptotics, but with a different asymptotic regime (large N vs small noise).
- The CCCP interpretation links to optimization literature and provides a neat bridge to accelerated shrinkage.

Strengths
- Novel and bold theoretical insight: Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 show that LFMs inherit the same FIC structure as MMs without block-diagonal Hessian assumptions. This is a nontrivial and potentially impactful generalization.
- Clean algorithmic instantiation: mean-field E-step with a simple fixed-point form, closed-form M-step, and automatic feature shrinkage without hand-tuned hyperparameters.
- CCCP view is elegant and practically useful; the accelerateShrinkage routine is simple and effective.
- Identifiability analysis is valuable in a setting known to suffer from non-identifiability; the merge heuristic is principled and practical.
- Empirical results indicate strong model selection and predictive performance with good runtime, often yielding compact K and reduced overfitting (PLL close to TLL).

Weaknesses and questions
- Heavy reliance on supplemental proofs. Lemma 1 is pivotal; the “mild assumptions” should be stated more explicitly in the main text, with at least an intuition/proof sketch (e.g., structure induced by Z, scaling of Fisher information with ∑n z_nk).
- Fairness of comparisons: IBP/VB/MEIBP use fixed noise variance while FAB learns Λ. This likely advantages FAB in likelihood-based metrics. At minimum, a control experiment where Λ is fixed for FAB (or learned across all methods equivalently) would clarify.
- The CCCP-based acceleration updates π and π̃ inside the inner loop “for further acceleration.” Does this preserve the global monotonicity of the lower bound L? The text asserts monotonic increase of L “in every single step,” but the modified inner loop could break standard EM-style guarantees; please clarify conditions or provide empirical monotonicity plots.
- Sensitivity to Tshrink and δ is briefly discussed as “not sensitive,” but an ablation on Tshrink (e.g., 0, 10, 50, 100) and its effect on runtime and final K would strengthen the case.
- Scope: Gaussian noise with diagonal Λ and Bernoulli features. How does Lemma 1 extend to non-diagonal Λ, non-Gaussian likelihoods (e.g., logistic/probit LFMs), or real-valued features? A discussion would help position the generality of the result.
- Missing data handling is central to PLL reporting, but the method for handling missingness in the E/M steps isn’t described in the main text; please detail how X̄, sufficient statistics, and updates are adapted to missing entries.
- Initial K is set to min(N, D), which can be very large; while shrinkage handles overcompleteness, runtime comparisons to methods initialized closer to true K may be skewed. A small study varying initial K would be fairer to MEIBP and others.
- Omission of VB due to runtime is understandable, but perhaps include VB on a subset/smaller datasets to provide at least one head-to-head where all methods complete.
- Clarity: some notational overload (π̃ vs π) and asymptotic terms (constants in Op(1)) could be streamlined; a short complexity analysis per iteration (in N, D, K) would improve reproducibility.

Quality
Technically sound overall; the asymptotic derivations are plausible and consistent with singular learning literature. However, because the main theoretical step is deferred to the supplement, the paper would benefit from a clearer statement of assumptions and a proof sketch. Empirics are solid but could improve in fairness (noise variance) and ablations.

Clarity
Generally well organized. The algorithmic presentation is clear, and the CCCP insight is nicely explained. Improve by: (i) stating assumptions for Lemma 1 in the main text; (ii) detailing missing-data handling; (iii) adding iteration complexity; (iv) clarifying monotonicity under acceleration.

Originality
High. Showing that the FIC form carries over to LFMs without previous structural assumptions is a substantial conceptual advance; the CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability/merge analysis are also original and useful.

Significance
High. The work broadens FAB to an important model class, provides an automated, prior-agnostic alternative to IBP, and offers practical speedups. Likely to be used and extended.

Pros
- Strong, general theoretical idea (FIC for LFMs) with practical consequences.
- Automated model selection with compelling empirical evidence.
- Elegant CCCP interpretation and effective acceleration.
- Identifiability analysis and principled merge post-processing.

Cons
- Key assumptions/proofs deferred; need more visibility in main text.
- Potential evaluation bias due to Λ treatment and large initial K.
- Monotonicity guarantees under accelerated updates not fully justified.
- Limited discussion of generality beyond Gaussian/diagonal Λ.

Suggestions for improvement
- State and briefly justify the assumptions of Lemma 1 in the main paper; add an intuition/proof sketch.
- Add experiments equalizing noise treatment across methods or fixing Λ in FAB for fairness.
- Provide ablations on Tshrink and initial K; show L monotonicity curves with acceleration.
- Clarify missing-data handling and per-iteration complexity.
- Discuss extensions to non-Gaussian likelihoods and non-diagonal Λ.
