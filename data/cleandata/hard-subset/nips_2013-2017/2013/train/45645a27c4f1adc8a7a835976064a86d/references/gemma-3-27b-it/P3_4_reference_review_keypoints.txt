- Extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) and shows FIC has the same form as for mixture models despite different Hessian structure.  
- Provides asymptotic analysis and Lemma 1 that justifies applying FAB to LFMs.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method based on a connection to the Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP), reducing computational cost.  
- Analyzes parameter identifiability in LFMs and proposes a merge post-processing step to improve robustness and interpretability.  
- Empirically validates the method on synthetic and real datasets, comparing favorably to IBP, VB, and MEIBP in model selection accuracy and efficiency.  
- Writing and organization are clear, with consistent notation and accessible derivations.  
- Main novelty is an application/extension of FAB to a new domain rather than a fundamentally new inference framework.  
- Relies on strong asymptotic assumptions (e.g., log-priors constant with N) without fully exploring implications of relaxing them.  
- Experimental details are insufficient for full reproducibility (missing hyperparameter settings, computational infrastructure).  
- Choice of fixed noise standard deviation (1 for synthetic, 0.75 for real) appears ad-hoc and under-justified.  
- Merge post-processing is described briefly and needs more detail on criteria and impact.  
- Paper lacks discussion of failure cases or scenarios where FAB/LFMs may underperform and possible remedies.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical justification, effective algorithmic improvements, comprehensive empirical results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited core novelty, dependence on strong asymptotic assumptions, and incomplete experimental detail.  
- Overall assessment: a strong contribution that extends FAB to LFMs with useful algorithmic enhancements; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: discuss assumptions and their impacts, expand merge post-processing description, provide full experimental/hyperparameter and infrastructure details, discuss failure cases, and analyze sensitivity to fixed noise standard deviation.
