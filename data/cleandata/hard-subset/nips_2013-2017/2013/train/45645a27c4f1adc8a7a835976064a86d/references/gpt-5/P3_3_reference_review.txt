Summary
The paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs) with Gaussian likelihoods and binary features. The core theoretical claim is Lemma 1: the log-determinant of the per-dimension negative Hessian of the complete log-likelihood scales as a sum of log feature-usage counts, leading to an FIC for LFMs whose form matches that for MMs. Building on this, the authors derive a practical FAB algorithm by lower-bounding the FIC via linearization and a mean-field q(Z), yielding an EM-like scheme with a distinctive shrinkage term that prunes unused features without hand-tuned priors. They further interpret the E-step as a convex-concave procedure (CCCP), proposing a multi-step “accelerateShrinkage” routine. Finally, they provide an identifiability result suggesting FAB favors balanced, parsimonious representations, which motivates a merge post-processing. Experiments compare against IBP sampling, VB-IBP, MEIBP (MAP via submodularity), and a variational EM baseline, reporting competitive or better model selection and predictive log-likelihoods at relatively low computational cost.

Strengths
- Conceptual advance: Showing that the FIC for LFMs retains the same log-∑n znk structure as for MMs despite shared parameters is insightful and, if correct under mild conditions, broadens FAB’s scope.
- Practical algorithm: The FAB updates are simple, and the CCCP view is elegant; the accelerateShrinkage heuristic is sensible and appears effective.
- Automatic model selection: The −(D/2) log usage penalty yields data-driven pruning, with empirical evidence of compact K and reduced overfitting (PLL−TLL gap).
- Identifiability discussion: Theorem 4 and the merge heuristic address a real pathology in LFMs (non-identifiability of duplicated columns), providing a pragmatic remedy.
- Monotonic bound optimization: Clear lower-bound objective with alternating maximization and practical stopping criterion.

Weaknesses and detailed comments
Quality/technical soundness
- Assumptions behind Lemma 1: The paper repeatedly says “mild assumptions” but does not state them in the main text. Given Lemma 1 is pivotal, a concise statement and intuition are needed: requirements on Λ (diagonal vs full), boundedness of x̄n and W, non-degeneracy of feature counts (no columns with o(N) usage), and conditions preventing near-collinearity among columns of Z that could make F(d) ill-conditioned. The scope (Gaussian likelihood, binary features) should be explicit.
- Potential inconsistency in CCCP update: Eq. (11) contains the shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃k), which with π̃k = (1/N)∑n µnk equals −D/(2∑n µnk). In Eq. (14), derived via CCCP, the term appears as −D/(∑n µt−1nk) (missing the factor 1/2). Unless I’m misreading, this is a factor-of-two discrepancy that should be corrected or explained (e.g., if constants were absorbed elsewhere). This is important since it changes shrinkage strength.
- The −|P| term in J(Z,P) (Eq. 8) is unconventional. Typically Laplace yields −(dim(P)/2) log N plus a constant (Op(1)) including (dim/2) log 2π and log det Fisher. Why is there a standalone −|P| retained but other constants dropped? Please clarify the exact derivation and constants retained/omitted.
- Monotonicity with heuristics: While alternating updates of q, P, and π̃ increase L, the shrinkage and merge steps could, in principle, break monotonicity. You state shrinkage increases L because reconstruction is unchanged and complexity drops; a short proof sketch would help. For merge, you do check L, which is good; note the potential for local maxima issues and discuss whether multiple merges per iteration are safe.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well-written, but a few places hinder reproducibility: (i) provide an explicit proof sketch or intuition for Lemma 1 in the main text; (ii) specify all assumptions; (iii) give the exact M-step updates (even if standard PPCA-like) in the supplement; (iv) correct minor typos (“mean-filed”, “this this update”, “AIS-TATS”).
- Complexity analysis: Please report per-iteration complexity and memory (in N, D, K) and how accelerateShrinkage changes the total cost. A plot of K vs iterations with/without acceleration is helpful (you partly provide this).

Originality
- The extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs under shared parameters is novel and non-trivial; the CCCP interpretation is a nice addition. The identifiability angle and merge heuristic are practical contributions. Related work coverage is adequate (IBP, VB-IBP, MEIBP, MAD-Bayes), though a tighter comparison to “MAD-Bayes” (asymptotics but different regime) would be useful.

Significance
- If Lemma 1 holds broadly, this opens FAB to a wider class of latent variable models with shared parameters, which is impactful. The algorithm is simple and could be adopted by practitioners needing automatic model selection in Gaussian LFMs. The results on real datasets show promise, though broader applicability (e.g., non-Gaussian likelihoods, full covariance, structured sparsity) remains to be explored.

Experimental evaluation
- Fairness concerns: You note IBP/VB/MEIBP codebases do not learn noise variance and fix it to 1 or 0.75, but it is unclear whether FAB/EM also fixed λ or learned it. If FAB learns Λ while baselines cannot, PLL/TLL comparisons may be biased. Please clarify and, if needed, report results with Λ fixed across methods.
- Hyperparameters and initialization: MEIBP performance can be sensitive to K initialization; using min(N,D) vs a smaller K (100) for some datasets raises fairness questions. Similarly, IBP α=3 may overestimate K; consider a sensitivity analysis or cross-validated α.
- Omission of VB due to runtime is understandable, but then claims about state-of-the-art should be tempered; at least, provide one medium-scale VB result.
- Ablations: Show sensitivity to Tshrink, δ, and merge threshold; report how many features were merged and their effect on L/PLL.
- Scale: Include a larger-scale experiment (e.g., N>10^4) to substantiate scalability claims.

Pros for acceptance
- Solid, potentially generalizable theoretical insight (FIC for LFMs).
- Practical, automated model selection with an interpretable penalty.
- Elegant CCCP view and effective acceleration.
- Useful identifiability perspective and merge heuristic.
- Competitive empirical results with compact models and good PLL.

Cons for acceptance
- Key assumptions and a critical factor (1/2) in CCCP update unclear; derivation details need tightening.
- Some experimental fairness and completeness issues (noise variance, hyperparameters, VB results).
- Scope limited to Gaussian likelihood with diagonal precision; generality not demonstrated.

Recommendation
Weak accept, contingent on clarifying Lemma 1’s assumptions, fixing the CCCP shrinkage factor (or explaining it), and addressing experimental fairness (noise variance and baseline settings). The conceptual contribution and practical algorithm are worthwhile for the NeurIPS community, and the issues seem addressable in revision.

Questions for authors
- Please state the precise assumptions for Lemma 1 and provide a proof sketch.
- Is Eq. (14) missing a factor 1/2 relative to Eq. (11)?
- Why retain −|P| in Eq. (8)? Which constants are kept vs absorbed into Op(1)?
- Did FAB/EM learn Λ while others fixed it? If so, can you provide results with Λ fixed for FAB/EM?
- Does accelerateShrinkage guarantee non-decreasing L? If not, how often does it decrease in practice?
- How sensitive are results to Tshrink and the merge threshold?
