- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) and proves LFMs yield the same FIC form as Mixture Models despite violating the Hessian block-diagonal condition (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
- Authors derive that log det |F(d)| for LFMs contains the same ∑ₙ zₙₖ/N term enabling FAB's automatic feature selection.  
- Introduces a CCCP-based interpretation of the FAB E-step and a shrinkage acceleration technique (Algorithm 2) that reduces computational cost without losing accuracy.  
- Proves parameter identifiability for LFMs (Theorem 4), addressing non-identifiability issues in latent variable models.  
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic, block, and real-world data show improved model selection accuracy and lower computational cost compared to IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Theoretical contribution significantly broadens the applicability of FAB and demonstrates generalizability of the FIC framework.  
- CCCP/shrinkage acceleration is a practical algorithmic innovation with convincing empirical speedups (Figure 1).  
- Identification theorem is an important theoretical insight often overlooked in latent feature model literature.  
- Experimental setup has limitations: simulations use fixed λₖ=1 and πₖ=0.5, which may not reflect realistic scenarios.  
- Real-data experiments omit VB due to computational cost without providing comparative complexity analysis, weakening scalability claims.  
- Paper does not clearly distinguish FAB’s “asymptotic ignorance of priors” from other Bayesian methods that also ignore priors in large samples.  
- Connection to prior work on LFMs (e.g., IBP) and the theoretical relationship between FAB’s automatic feature selection and infinite-feature priors is insufficiently contrasted.  
- Merge post-processing is mentioned but not thoroughly explained; effects on model selection and when merging is beneficial are unclear.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and novel with solid empirical validation, but would benefit from clearer theoretical distinctions and more comprehensive experimental comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
