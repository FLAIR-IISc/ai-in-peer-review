

# Peer Review: "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

## Summary
This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference, previously applicable only to specific models like mixture models (MMs), to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors prove that despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition required for prior FAB applications, the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs takes the same form as for MMs. This enables FAB to automatically select the number of latent features without hyperparameters or priors. The paper makes three key contributions: 1) theoretical derivation of FIC for LFMs via Lemma 1 and Theorem 2; 2) a novel CCCP interpretation of the FAB E-step with a shrinkage acceleration method; and 3) a proof of parameter identifiability and merge post-processing for LFMs. The authors demonstrate FAB/LFMs' superiority over IBP, VB, and MEIBP in model selection accuracy and computational efficiency across synthetic and real-world datasets.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Novelty and Rigor**: The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by showing that FAB can be extended to LFMs despite their failure to satisfy the block-diagonal Hessian condition. Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 are the core theoretical advances, with the paper providing rigorous proofs in the supplementary materials. This theoretical insight is valuable for understanding the broader applicability of FIC.

2. **Practical Algorithmic Improvements**: The CCCP interpretation of the FAB E-step (Section 3) leads to the novel shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2), which drastically reduces computational cost by eliminating irrelevant features early. The empirical results in Figure 1 clearly demonstrate this efficiency gain without compromising objective value.

3. **Theoretical Insights on Identifiability**: The identifiability proof (Theorem 4) and the proposed merge post-processing are important theoretical contributions that address a critical issue in latent variable models. This provides a natural guide for handling overlapping features that is missing in previous approaches.

4. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation**: The experiments cover artificial simulations, block data, and real-world datasets with missing values. The results consistently show FAB/LFMs outperforming state-of-the-art methods in model selection accuracy while being significantly faster (Table 1), especially for large-scale problems.

## Weaknesses

1. **Clarity of Theoretical Connection**: The connection between Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 could be clearer in the main paper. The authors state that Lemma 1 "induces" Theorem 2, but the exact logical pathway from the Hessian analysis to the marginal log-likelihood approximation would benefit from more explicit explanation.

2. **Comparison with Recent Work**: While the paper compares with IBP, VB, and MEIBP, it doesn't include recent advances in latent feature models such as the work by [15, 16] on nonparametric latent feature models for link prediction. A more thorough comparison with the most recent state-of-the-art would strengthen the significance claim.

3. **Limitations Discussion**: The paper acknowledges that FIC approximation may not be accurate for small N, but doesn't explore the practical implications for real-world applications where N might be moderate. The authors should discuss how their method performs when N is not sufficiently large.

4. **Implementation Details**: The paper mentions using "random and uniform initialization" but doesn't specify how this affects convergence or final model selection. For a method that claims to be fully automated, more details on implementation robustness would be valuable.

## Arguments for and Against Acceptance

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- The theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs is significant and well-supported.
- The shrinkage acceleration method is novel and provides substantial computational benefits.
- The identifiability proof and merge post-processing address important theoretical gaps.
- The empirical results consistently demonstrate advantages over state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper is well-written, well-organized, and makes a clear contribution to Bayesian model selection.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- The theoretical connection between Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 could be clearer.
- The comparison with the most recent methods in the field is somewhat limited.
- The paper doesn't fully address the limitations of FAB for moderate N scenarios.
- Some implementation details could be better explained for reproducibility.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a significant theoretical contribution to Bayesian model selection with clear practical benefits. The extension of FAB to LFMs is novel and well-justified, and the accompanying algorithmic improvements (shrinkage acceleration and merge post-processing) are valuable for real-world applications. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches.

The paper meets all NIPS quality criteria: it's technically sound with rigorous theoretical analysis, well-written with clear organization, original in its extension of FAB to LFMs, and significant in both theoretical contribution and practical application. The minor weaknesses in clarity and comparison with recent work don't detract from the paper's overall contribution.

I recommend acceptance for NIPS. The paper advances the understanding of Bayesian inference for latent variable models and provides a practical, efficient method for model selection that will likely be adopted by researchers and practitioners working with latent feature models.

## Specific Suggestions
1. Clarify the logical connection between Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 in the main text.
2. Include a brief comparison with the most recent state-of-the-art methods in latent feature modeling.
3. Add a short discussion of the practical implications when N is moderate (not large).
4. Provide more details about the initialization procedure for the latent features.
