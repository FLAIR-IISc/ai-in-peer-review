- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming a core limitation.  
- Proves FAB applicability to LFMs despite the complete log-likelihood lacking a block-diagonal Hessian (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
- Positions work relative to IBP, VB, and MAD-Bayes, emphasizing computational advantages of FAB for large-scale data.  
- Demonstrates strong computational efficiency and a shrinkage acceleration technique that speeds inference versus BNP and VB methods.  
- Retains automatic model selection property, removing need for manual hyperparameter tuning.  
- Provides identifiability analysis and a merge post-processing step to address redundant latent features.  
- Paper is clearly written, mathematically rigorous, and well-organized.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets are comprehensive and show competitive performance.  
- Performance gains are most pronounced for large N; low-data regime behavior is underexplored.  
- Method may be sensitive to initialization; analysis of initialization effects is limited.  
- Merge post-processing uses heuristic criteria (Euclidean distance of W and L increase) without rigorous justification.  
- VB comparison may be unfairly limited by a narrow K range; broader/prior-based VB could be more competitive.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss low-data limitations and remedies, analyze initialization strategies, justify or improve merging criterion, and expand VB comparison over a wider K/prior range.
