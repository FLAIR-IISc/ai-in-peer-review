## Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a significant extension of the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors successfully demonstrate that, despite LFMs not initially satisfying the conditions required for FAB, a theoretical argument (Lemma 1) allows for its application.  The work is technically sound, well-written, and addresses a relevant problem in scalable Bayesian modeling. While the empirical evaluation is promising, it lacks a more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art methods on larger, more complex datasets. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

**1. Summary of Ideas and Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds upon the recent success of FAB inference [Fujimaki et al., 2017, 2018] – a method for automated model selection in latent variable models (like mixture models) by maximizing a tractable lower bound on the marginal log-likelihood.  FAB's key advantage is its automation – avoiding the need for hyperparameter tuning common in VB or NPB methods.  The authors identify a limitation of existing FAB: its applicability only to models with a block-diagonal Hessian of the complete log-likelihood.  They address this by showing that LFMs, despite *not* strictly satisfying this condition, still exhibit the necessary properties for FAB to be applied effectively.  

The work connects to a rich body of literature on LFMs, including infinite LFMs and the Indian Buffet Process (IBP) [Griffiths & Ghahramani, 2005], variational inference for IBP [Doshi-Velez et al., 2011], and MAP estimation via submodular optimization [Reed & Ghahramani, 2012].  The authors clearly position their work within this context, highlighting the computational advantages of FAB over existing NPB and VB approaches. They also relate their work to “MAD-Bayes” [Broderick et al., 2014], noting the different asymptotic assumptions.

**2. Strengths and Weaknesses:**

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The central theoretical result (Lemma 1) is a significant contribution, extending the applicability of FAB to a broader class of models. The derivation appears rigorous and well-motivated.
* **Methodological Innovation:**  The proposed acceleration technique based on a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) interpretation of the FAB E-step is insightful and demonstrably effective in reducing computational cost.
* **Identifiability Analysis:** The discussion of parameter identifiability and the proposed merge post-processing step addresses a crucial issue in latent feature modeling and provides a practical improvement.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and relatively easy to follow.  The mathematical derivations are presented in a logical manner.
* **Automatic Model Selection:**  The core benefit of FAB—automatic dimensionality selection—is clearly articulated and demonstrated.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** While the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, they are somewhat limited in scope. The synthetic experiments are helpful, but the real-world datasets are relatively small. The comparison to other methods could be more comprehensive, particularly in terms of scalability on very large datasets.
* **Comparison to Strong Baselines:** The comparison against IBP, VB, and MEIBP is a good start, but a comparison to more recent and sophisticated methods for LFM inference (e.g., stochastic variational inference) would strengthen the evaluation.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity of Baselines:** The authors acknowledge fixing the noise standard deviation for IBP/VB/MEIBP. It would be valuable to briefly discuss how the results might change with different settings, or to justify the chosen values more explicitly.
* **Merge Post-Processing Detail:**  The explanation of the merge post-processing is somewhat concise. Providing more details on the criteria for merging features (beyond simply a lower bound increase) would be helpful.



**3. Evaluation Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the derivations appear correct. The experimental results support the claims made in the paper. The focus on theoretical guarantees (Lemma 1) and identifiability analysis adds to the quality.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors effectively explain the core concepts and mathematical details.
* **Originality:** The extension of FAB to LFMs and the development of the acceleration technique are novel contributions. The identifiability analysis and merge post-processing are also valuable additions.
* **Significance:**  The work has the potential to be significant. FAB offers a computationally efficient alternative to existing methods for LFM inference, especially in large-scale settings.  The automated model selection aspect is particularly appealing.



**4. Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Extends the applicability of the promising FAB framework to a wider class of models.
* Introduces a computationally efficient acceleration technique.
* Addresses the important issue of parameter identifiability.
* Well-written and theoretically sound.

**Con:**

* Limited empirical evaluation, particularly on large-scale datasets.
* Comparison to baselines could be more comprehensive.
*  Details on the merge post-processing could be expanded.




**5. Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, I suggest the following:

* **Expand the empirical evaluation:**  Include experiments on larger, more challenging datasets.
* **Strengthen the baseline comparison:** Consider including more recent and sophisticated LFM inference methods.
* **Provide more details on the merge post-processing step:**  Explain the criteria for merging features more thoroughly.
* **Address potential sensitivity of baseline hyperparameters:** Discuss the impact of different hyperparameter settings on the results of the baseline methods.



This is a strong paper with the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian modeling. Addressing the minor revisions outlined above will further enhance its impact.
