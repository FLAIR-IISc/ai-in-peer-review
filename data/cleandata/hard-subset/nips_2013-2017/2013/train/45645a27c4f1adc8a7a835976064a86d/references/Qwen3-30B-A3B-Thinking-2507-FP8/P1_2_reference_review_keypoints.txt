- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), deriving an FIC with the same form as for mixture models.  
- Key theoretical contributions: Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 proving FIC derivation for LFMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Algorithmic contribution: CCCP interpretation of the FAB E-step and a shrinkage acceleration method that prunes irrelevant features early.  
- Identifiability: Theorem 4 proves parameter identifiability and authors propose a merge post-processing to handle overlapping features.  
- Empirical results: FAB/LFMs outperform IBP, VB, and MEIBP in model selection accuracy and are computationally faster across synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical novelty and rigorous proofs supporting broader applicability of FIC.  
- Strength: Practical speedups from shrinkage acceleration shown empirically without degrading objective values.  
- Strength: Identifiability result and merge step address a key latent-variable modeling issue.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments including missing-data scenarios and large-scale problems.  
- Weakness: The logical connection between Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 is not sufficiently clear in the main text.  
- Weakness: Comparisons omit some recent nonparametric latent feature work (e.g., refs [15,16]) and other SOTA methods.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of FIC accuracy and practical performance when sample size N is moderate or small.  
- Weakness: Insufficient implementation details, particularly about initialization and its effect on convergence/model selection.  
- Acceptance arguments: Significant theoretical extension, novel and effective speedups, identifiability proof, and strong empirical gains.  
- Rejection concerns: Clarity of key theoretical link, limited recent-method comparisons, moderate-N limitations, and missing implementation specifics.  
- Suggested revisions: Clarify Lemma→Theorem logic, compare to recent SOTA LFMs, discuss practical behavior for moderate N, and detail initialization procedures.
