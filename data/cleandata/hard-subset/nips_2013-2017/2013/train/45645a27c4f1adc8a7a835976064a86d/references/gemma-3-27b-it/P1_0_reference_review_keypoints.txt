- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to Latent Feature Models (LFMs).
- Shows the Hessian structure of LFM log-likelihood permits applying the FAB framework despite initial condition mismatches.
- Derives FIC for LFMs (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) with proofs sketched and detailed in supplement.
- Provides theoretical identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) and a principled merging approach for redundant features.
- Proposes a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration technique to reduce FAB computational cost.
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real datasets, comparing competitively to IBP, VB, and MEIBP.
- Positions FAB relative to BNP methods (IBP) and VB, emphasizing fully automated model selection without priors/hyperparameters.
- Clarifies difference from MAD-Bayes in the asymptotic limit considered.
- Strength: automated dimensionality selection without explicit priors or tuning.
- Strength: computational efficiency improvements demonstrated (Figure 1).
- Strength: generally clear writing, organization, and accessible mathematical derivations.
- Weakness: underlying LFM formulation is standard; novelty mainly in inference method.
- Weakness: reliance on Laplace approximation, which can be problematic in high dimensions or complex posteriors.
- Weakness: potential sensitivity to initialization for EM-based FAB; variance across runs not reported.
- Weakness: merge post-processing uses a Euclidean-distance heuristic that may feel ad hoc.
- Weakness: missing implementation details (optimization choices, stopping criteria) hurt reproducibility.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify initialization sensitivity, discuss Laplace approximation limitations, note merging heuristic limits, and add implementation details.
