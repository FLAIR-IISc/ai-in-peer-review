- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Lemma 1 shows LFMs yield the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) term as MMs despite not having block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Develops an FAB/LFM algorithm with shrinkage-accelerated E-step (CCCP interpretation) and a merge post-processing for identifiability.  
- Theoretical results are rigorous (Theorem 2 for asymptotics, Theorem 4 for identifiability) and well-justified.  
- Empirical results (simulations, block data, real datasets) show better model selection and predictive performance with lower computational cost than competitors.  
- Experiments demonstrate strong scalability advantages on large datasets (EEG, Piano, USPS) and automatic feature selection without hyperparameters.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and clear in definitions, notation, and algorithm descriptions.  
- The CCCP interpretation of the FAB E-step and identifiability analysis are novel and theoretically insightful.  
- Practical contributions include effective shrinkage acceleration and a merge step that improves identifiability in LFMs.  
- Limitation: paper could offer a more intuitive explanation for why LFMs yield the same FIC form despite lacking block-diagonal structure.  
- Limitation: omission of variational Bayes (VB) baseline—small-dataset comparisons would give a fuller picture.  
- Limitation: merge post-processing lacks quantification and deeper analysis of when merges occur.  
- Limitation: theoretical connection to MAD-Bayes is mentioned but could be elaborated for additional context.  
- Overall assessment: strong, publishable contribution that meets NIPS criteria; recommendation for acceptance.  
- Pros: novel extension of FAB to LFMs, computational efficiency, automatic model selection, strong experiments, theoretical insights.  
- Cons: needs clearer intuition for key lemma, missing VB comparisons, and more thorough explanation/quantification of merging.
