- Summary: Extends FAB inference from mixture models to Gaussian latent feature models (binary features), deriving an FIC with log feature-usage counts and a practical EM-like FAB algorithm with shrinkage and merge heuristics.  
- Key theoretical claim: Lemma 1 shows the log-determinant of the per-dimension Hessian scales as a sum of log feature-usage counts, yielding an FIC for LFMs matching the MM form.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Derives a lower-bound FIC with mean-field q(Z) and CCCP interpretation, producing simple FAB updates and an accelerateShrinkage routine to prune features automatically.  
- Identifiability/merge: Theorem 4 and a merge post-processing address duplicated-column non-identifiability and encourage parsimonious, balanced representations.  
- Empirics: Reports competitive or better model selection and predictive log-likelihoods compared to IBP sampling, VB-IBP, MEIBP, and a variational EM baseline at relatively low cost.  
- Strength — conceptual advance: Extending FIC to LFMs despite shared parameters is insightful and broadens FAB’s applicability.  
- Strength — practical algorithm: FAB updates are simple; CCCP view and accelerateShrinkage heuristic are sensible and empirically effective.  
- Strength — automatic model selection: The −(D/2) log usage penalty enables data-driven pruning and reduces overfitting (smaller PLL−TLL gap).  
- Strength — identifiability discussion: Theorem and merge heuristic provide a practical remedy for duplicated-column pathology.  
- Strength — monotonic bound: Presents a clear lower-bound objective with alternating maximization and a practical stopping criterion.  
- Weakness — Lemma 1 assumptions unclear: “Mild assumptions” are not stated; need explicit conditions on Λ, boundedness, non-degenerate feature counts, and Z collinearity to validate Lemma 1’s scope.  
- Weakness — CCCP shrinkage factor discrepancy: Eq. (11) has −D/(2∑n µnk) but Eq. (14) appears as −D/(∑n µt−1nk), a factor-of-two mismatch that must be fixed or explained.  
- Weakness — −|P| term in J(Z,P): Unconventional retention of −|P| instead of standard −(dim/2) log N plus constants; clarify which Laplace constants are kept vs absorbed.  
- Weakness — monotonicity and heuristics: Shrinkage and merge steps could break monotonicity; provide a sketch/proof that shrinkage increases L and note merge-related local maxima issues.  
- Clarity issues: Include a proof sketch and explicit assumptions for Lemma 1 in main text, supply exact M-step updates in supplement, and fix minor typos.  
- Complexity: Missing per-iteration complexity and memory analysis (N,D,K) and how accelerateShrinkage affects total cost; a K vs iterations plot with/without acceleration would help.  
- Originality: Novel extension of FAB/FIC to LFMs and useful CCCP/merge additions; related work coverage is adequate but tighter comparison to MAD-Bayes suggested.  
- Significance: If Lemma 1 holds broadly, this opens FAB to more latent models; algorithm is practical though limited to Gaussian diagonal-precision LFMs for now.  
- Experimental fairness concerns: Unclear whether FAB/EM learned Λ while baselines fixed it — this could bias PLL/TLL comparisons and needs clarification or fixed-Λ results.  
- Hyperparameter/init sensitivity: MEIBP and IBP settings (K initialization, α) may bias comparisons; suggest sensitivity analysis or cross-validation.  
- Missing baselines/scale: VB omitted for runtime—provide at least one medium-scale VB result and include larger-scale (N>10^4) experiments to support scalability claims.  
- Ablations requested: Report sensitivity to Tshrink, δ, merge thresholds, number of merged features, and their effects on L/PLL.  
- Pros for acceptance: Strong theoretical insight, practical automated model selection, elegant CCCP and acceleration, useful identifiability fix, and competitive empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: Unstated key assumptions, the shrinkage factor discrepancy, and experimental fairness/completeness issues limit confidence until revised.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on clarifying Lemma 1 assumptions, fixing/explaining the 1/2 factor in CCCP update, and addressing experimental fairness (noise variance and baseline settings).  
- Questions for authors: State precise Lemma 1 assumptions and proof sketch; confirm if Eq. (14) misses 1/2; justify −|P| retention; clarify whether FAB/EM learned Λ vs baselines; does accelerateShrinkage guarantee non-decreasing L; and report sensitivity to Tshrink/merge threshold.
