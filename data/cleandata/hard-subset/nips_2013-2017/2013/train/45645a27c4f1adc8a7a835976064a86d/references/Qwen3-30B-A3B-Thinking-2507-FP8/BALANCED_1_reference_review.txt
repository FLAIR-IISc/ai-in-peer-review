

# Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

## Summary
This paper extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), which have traditionally been handled by nonparametric Bayesian (NPB) or variational Bayesian (VB) methods. The key insight is that despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition required for FAB in previous applications (e.g., Mixture Models), the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for MMs. The authors prove Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 establishing this equivalence, derive an FAB/LFM algorithm with a shrinkage acceleration method based on Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP) interpretation, and analyze parameter identifiability with a merge post-processing step. The paper makes a significant contribution by demonstrating that FAB can be applied to a broader class of models while maintaining the desirable properties of automatic model selection and computational efficiency.

## Strengths

**Theoretical Contribution (Originality & Quality):** The paper's most significant strength is its theoretical insight that LFMs, despite not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition, still yield the same FIC form as MMs. Lemma 1 is particularly novel, showing that the log ∑n znk term naturally appears in log det |F(d)| without requiring assumptions A1 and A2. This represents a meaningful generalization of the FAB framework beyond MMs. The CCCP interpretation of FAB updates provides valuable theoretical insight that leads to the practical shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2), which significantly reduces computational cost while maintaining model selection accuracy.

**Experimental Rigor (Quality & Significance):** The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering artificial simulations, synthetic block data, and multiple real-world datasets. The comparison with state-of-the-art methods (IBP, VB, MEIBP, EM) is fair and thorough, with clear metrics including computational time, model selection accuracy, and predictive performance. The paper demonstrates that FAB/LFM achieves better model selection accuracy than alternatives while being significantly more computationally efficient, especially with the shrinkage acceleration. The real-world data results are particularly convincing, showing FAB's superior predictive performance and better mitigation of overfitting (smaller gap between training and predictive log-likelihood).

**Clarity and Organization:** The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the theoretical contributions and algorithmic details. The connection between FAB and CCCP is particularly well-explained, and the pseudocode for the algorithm is clear. The supplementary materials provide necessary theoretical details for reproducibility.

## Weaknesses

**Theoretical Intuition (Quality & Clarity):** While the paper provides rigorous proofs, it lacks sufficient intuition about *why* LFMs satisfy the FIC equivalence despite not meeting the block-diagonal condition. The key Lemma 1 is stated without sufficient geometric or probabilistic intuition, making it difficult for readers to grasp why the log ∑n znk term appears naturally in LFMs. This is a missed opportunity to deepen the theoretical understanding.

**Experimental Details (Quality & Significance):** The real-world dataset descriptions are insufficiently detailed (Table 1 lacks critical information about dataset sizes, dimensionality, and characteristics). The paper claims "FAB consistently achieved good predictive performance" but doesn't provide statistical significance tests for the comparisons across datasets. For the small N scenario where FAB might underperform (Libras dataset), the paper mentions this limitation but doesn't characterize the threshold at which FAB becomes superior to NPB methods.

**Connection to Prior Work (Originality & Significance):** The paper could better articulate how this work extends previous FAB frameworks. While it references [5,6], it doesn't explicitly state how the LFMs extension differs conceptually from the MMs application. The relationship to "MAD-Bayes" [1] is mentioned but not thoroughly contrasted, despite MAD-Bayes also addressing asymptotic model selection.

## Recommendation

This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by extending the FAB framework to LFMs, demonstrating that the FIC form is preserved despite LFMs not satisfying the previously required Hessian condition. The theoretical results are rigorous, the algorithmic improvements are practical and well-motivated, and the experimental results convincingly demonstrate FAB/LFM's advantages in model selection accuracy and computational efficiency.

The main weaknesses are the lack of intuitive explanation for the key theoretical insight and insufficient experimental details for the real-world datasets. However, these are fixable with minor revisions. The paper is well-written, makes a clear contribution to Bayesian model selection, and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

**Pro arguments for acceptance:**
1. The theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs is novel and well-supported by rigorous proofs.
2. The CCCP interpretation provides a valuable theoretical insight leading to a practical algorithmic improvement.
3. The experimental results demonstrate clear advantages over state-of-the-art methods in both model selection accuracy and computational efficiency.
4. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the problem of automatic model selection for latent variable models.

**Con arguments for acceptance:**
1. The paper lacks sufficient intuitive explanation for why LFMs satisfy the FIC equivalence without the block-diagonal condition.
2. Some experimental details for real-world datasets are insufficient for full reproducibility.
3. The connection to prior FAB work could be more explicitly articulated.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian model selection. The theoretical insights are valuable, and the practical benefits of the method are clearly demonstrated. With minor revisions to address the clarity issues noted above, this paper would be an excellent fit for NeurIPS. I recommend acceptance.
