- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming prior block-diagonal Hessian restrictions.  
- Shows LFMs yield the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as Mixture Models (MMs), enabling automatic feature selection without hyperparameters.  
- Introduces a CCCP interpretation of FAB and a shrinkage acceleration technique that reduces computation while preserving accuracy.  
- Provides identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) and a merge post-processing procedure to address latent feature identifiability.  
- Lemma 1 is a key theoretical result: log det |F(d)| = ∑k log ∑n znk/N + Op(1) for LFMs, supported by rigorous proofs.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing FAB/LFMs to IBP, VB, and MEIBP on synthetic, block, and real-world datasets.  
- Empirical results show superior computational efficiency (up to 10× faster), better model selection accuracy for large N, and improved predictive performance with less overfitting.  
- Robustness checks include handling missing data and multiple random seeds.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear notation, precise algorithms (Algorithms 1–2), and strong linkage between theory and algorithms.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of when FAB may fail (e.g., small-N scenarios) and lack of guidance on method selection.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit some recent latent feature modeling advances (e.g., 2022 deep/nonparametric methods), which could better contextualize results.  
- Weakness: main paper lacks an intuitive heuristic for why Lemma 1 holds despite non-block-diagonal structure; heavy technical detail relegated to supplement.  
- Recommendation for acceptance: novel theoretical extension, practical acceleration, comprehensive experiments, strong theoretical grounding, and useful hyperparameter-free model selection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of small-N limitations, incomplete positioning against the latest literature, and potentially dense theoretical presentation for a broad audience.
