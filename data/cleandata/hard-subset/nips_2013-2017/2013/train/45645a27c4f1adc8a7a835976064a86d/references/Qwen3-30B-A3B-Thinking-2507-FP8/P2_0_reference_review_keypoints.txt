- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), removing the prior block-diagonal Hessian requirement.  
- Shows via Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 that LFMs yield the same FIC form as mixture models, a significant theoretical generalization.  
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method based on the Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP) to speed inference.  
- Provides identifiability analysis and a merge post-processing step (Theorem 4) to handle latent feature merging.  
- Situates work relative to FAB for mixture models and Bayesian nonparametrics (IBP) and variational Bayesian approaches.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous with appropriate assumptions and careful relation between FIC and marginal log-likelihood.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: artificial simulations, synthetic block data, and real datasets with comparisons to IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Shrinkage acceleration yields substantial computational speedups while maintaining model selection accuracy (Figures 1–2).  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: FIC approximation may be less accurate for small sample sizes (empirical evidence on Libras dataset).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with reproducible experiment details for expert readers.  
- The intuition behind Lemma 1 (form of log det Hessian) could be clearer to enhance accessibility.  
- Algorithm descriptions are clear, but the "merge()" subroutine is relegated to supplementary materials.  
- Original contributions include the theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs, CCCP-based shrinkage, and identifiability/merge analysis.  
- High practical significance: scalable automatic feature selection without hyperparameters, improved predictive performance and efficiency.  
- Demonstrated ability to avoid overfitting (smaller TLL–PLL differences) and better predictive log-likelihood (PLL) than alternatives.  
- Strengths: theoretical extension, rigorous proofs, practical speedup, comprehensive experiments, superior model selection and efficiency.  
- Weaknesses: limited intuition for key lemma, important details in supplementary material, and unclear failure modes for small N.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, practically significant, and a strong contribution to Bayesian model selection for latent variable models.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
