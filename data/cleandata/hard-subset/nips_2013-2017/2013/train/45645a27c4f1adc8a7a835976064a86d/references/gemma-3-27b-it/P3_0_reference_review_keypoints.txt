- Overall: Strong paper extending FAB inference to latent feature models with sound theory, useful practical methods, thorough experiments; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Problem & positioning: Addresses model selection for LFMs, extending FAB to a broader class and contrasting with NPB and VB methods.  
- Theoretical contribution: Proves FAB applicability to LFMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessian (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
- Acceleration & identifiability: Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method and a merge post-processing step to improve speed and handle non-identifiability.  
- Experiments: Comprehensive comparisons to IBP, VB, MEIBP on synthetic and real data show competitive predictive accuracy and faster computation.  
- Automated model selection: FAB provides inherent model selection without manual hyperparameter tuning.  
- Connection to prior work: Clear linkage to authors’ previous FAB work and relevant related methods (FIC, MAD-Bayes).  
- Writing: Generally clear but sometimes dense; could be simplified for readability.  
- Algorithm complexity: FAB remains complex and may be hard to implement; a high-level algorithm overview would help.  
- Assumptions: “Mild assumptions” in Lemma 1 are not fully stated in the main text and should be briefly discussed there.  
- Implementation dependence: Comparisons rely on existing implementations, so performance differences may reflect implementation details.  
- Lemma clarity request: Reviewer asks for a more intuitive/visual explanation of why log det |F(d)| simplifies in LFMs.  
- Tshrink sensitivity: Request for sensitivity analysis on the Tshrink parameter and its impact on performance.  
- Merge criteria justification: Questions theoretical basis for using Euclidean distance of W as merge criterion.  
- Experimental details: asks for more dataset specifics (dimensionality, sample sizes) in main text or supplement.  
- Computational cost: Requests a detailed breakdown of computational cost per FAB step.  
- For acceptance: Significant extension, sound theory, practical speedups, automated selection, and strong experiments.  
- Against acceptance: Needs clearer writing, algorithm complexity may hinder adoption, and comparisons may be implementation-dependent.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing assumptions clarity, intuitive explanations, Tshrink sensitivity, and other suggested clarifications.
