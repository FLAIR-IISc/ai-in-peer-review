This paper proposes extending Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The core contribution lies in demonstrating that, despite LFMs not initially satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition required for standard FAB, a similar FIC can be derived, enabling automated model selection (dimensionality of latent features) with potentially reduced computational cost. The authors further analyze the FAB E-step, relating it to a Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP) and leveraging this to propose a shrinkage acceleration method. Identifiability is also addressed, guiding a post-processing step to merge redundant latent features.

While the idea of extending FAB to LFMs is potentially valuable, and the theoretical analysis appears rigorous (though relegated to the supplementary materials, which is a concern – see below), the paper suffers from a multitude of minor, yet cumulatively substantial, flaws that severely hinder its impact and readability. The writing style is often dense and repetitive, and a concerning number of technical details are glossed over or stated without sufficient justification. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Contribution:** The derivation of the FIC for LFMs, and the demonstration of its similarity to the MM case, is a genuinely interesting result. Lemma 1 is central to this and appears key.
*   **Potential for Efficiency:** The proposed shrinkage acceleration, stemming from the CCCP interpretation, is a promising direction to address the computational burden of FAB.
*   **Identifiability Analysis:** Addressing parameter identifiability is a valuable contribution, providing a theoretical justification for the merging post-processing step.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity and Readability:** The paper is exceedingly difficult to follow. The mathematical notation is often introduced without adequate explanation, and the flow of arguments is frequently disjointed. The introduction, while aiming to contextualize FAB, feels unnecessarily verbose and lacks a clear motivation for *why* extending FAB to LFMs is important beyond simply being possible.
*   **Insufficient Detail:** Many critical aspects are underdeveloped. The “mild assumptions” mentioned in Lemma 1 are not explicitly stated in the main paper and need to be clarified. The derivation of the FIC itself feels rushed and lacks intermediate steps that would aid understanding. The M-step solution being "exactly the same as PPCA" is stated without providing the relevant equation; readers are expected to find it elsewhere.
*   **Experimental Rigor:** The experimental setup is inadequate. The choice of parameters (e.g., α for IBP) is not justified, and the rationale for using a fixed noise standard deviation in simulations is weak. The comparison against MEIBP is also problematic, given the stated limitations on its applicability to non-negative data and the initial K setting in the case of USPS.
*   **Supplementary Material Reliance:**  Crucially, "Rigorous proofs and assumptions… are given in the supplementary materials."  This is unacceptable for a NIPS submission. Key theoretical justifications should be presented *in the main paper* or at least summarized comprehensively. Placing core arguments in supplementary material effectively hides them from casual reviewers and limits the impact of the work.
*   **Minor Errors/Inconsistencies:** Numerous small errors and inconsistencies detract from the paper's credibility. For example, the notation `aij` for the (i,j)-th element of a matrix A is standard, but using `a·j` for the j-th column vector is unconventional and confusing.  The explanation of the acceleration step and the merge algorithm are also vague.
* **Overstated Claims:** The statement that FAB "achieves as good or even better model selection accuracy as state-of-the-art non-parametric Bayesian (NPB) methods" in the introduction needs significant justification, and is not supported by the provided experiments.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   The theoretical result regarding the FIC for LFMs is novel and potentially significant.
*   The analysis of the E-step and the development of the shrinkage acceleration are promising.

**Con:**

*   The paper is extremely difficult to understand due to poor writing and insufficient detail.
*   The experimental validation is weak and does not convincingly demonstrate the benefits of FAB/LFMs.
*   The reliance on supplementary material for key theoretical arguments is unacceptable.
*   The numerous minor errors and inconsistencies erode confidence in the authors’ attention to detail.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potential novelty of the ideas presented, the numerous flaws in this paper, particularly the lack of clarity, insufficient detail, and over-reliance on supplementary material, outweigh its strengths. While the theoretical contribution regarding the FIC is interesting, its impact is diminished by the poor presentation.  The experimental validation is unconvincing, and the writing style hinders understanding.  I recommend **rejection**. The authors should significantly revise the paper, addressing the clarity and detail issues, providing a more thorough experimental evaluation, and *incorporating the essential theoretical arguments from the supplementary material into the main text*.  A substantial rewrite is required to bring this paper up to NIPS standards.
