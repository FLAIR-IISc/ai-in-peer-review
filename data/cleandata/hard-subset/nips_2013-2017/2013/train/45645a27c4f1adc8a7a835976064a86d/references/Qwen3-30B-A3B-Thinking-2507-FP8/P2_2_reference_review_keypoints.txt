- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), removing the prior Hessian block-diagonal requirement.  
- Lemma 1: the log determinant of the Hessian for LFMs naturally yields the log(∑ₙ zₙₖ) term, giving the same FIC form as mixture models.  
- Theorem 2 and Corollary 3: establish convergence of FAB/LFMs to marginal log-likelihoods, providing strong theoretical justification.  
- Introduces CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) to speed feature pruning.  
- Proposes a merge post-processing step and proves parameter identifiability under mild conditions (Theorem 4).  
- Empirical results across simulations and real data show superior model selection accuracy and computational efficiency versus IBP, MEIBP (and claimed improvements over VB).  
- Presentation is clear, with precise notation, accessible derivations, and well-motivated pseudocode.  
- Limited experimental baselines: omits some relevant prior work (e.g., Miller et al. 2009) and downplays VB due to runtime, weakening comparative claims.  
- Theoretical assumptions are only in the supplement and not fully discussed; identifiability condition (0 < ∑ₙ μₙₖ/N < 1) may be restrictive in practice.  
- Focuses heavily on model selection (K) and less on how automatic selection improves predictive performance.  
- Overall: technically sound, original, and significant for automated model selection in LFMs, especially for large-scale problems.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
