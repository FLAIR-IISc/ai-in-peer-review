Summary
The paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and Gaussian likelihood. The key technical claim (Lemma 1) asserts that the log-determinant of the per-dimension Hessian of the complete log-likelihood contributes a term of the form sum_k log(sum_n z_nk), leading to an FIC for LFMs (Eq. 9) that has the same structure as for mixtures (Eq. 3). Based on this, the authors derive a practical FAB algorithm using: (i) a linearization bound for the log-count term, (ii) replacing the intractable MLE by optimizing parameters jointly, and (iii) a mean-field approximation over Z. They relate the E-step updates to CCCP, propose an “accelerated shrinkage” routine to eliminate irrelevant features early, and discuss identifiability, including a merge post-processing of redundant features. Experiments on synthetic “block” data and several real datasets compare FAB to IBP Gibbs, variational EM, and MEIBP, demonstrating competitive or better model selection and predictive log-likelihood, with lower or comparable computation.

Strengths
- Novel theoretical extension: If correct, the result that FIC for LFMs has the same effective complexity term as for mixtures is elegant and of broad interest; it suggests wider applicability of FAB beyond block-diagonal Hessian cases (A1–A2).
- Practical algorithmic contributions: The CCCP interpretation clarifies the fixed-point updates and motivates an inner-loop “accelerated shrinkage” that appears to reduce wall-clock time in practice.
- Automatic model selection: The D/(2Nπ̃k) shrinkage term yields a principled, prior-agnostic mechanism to prune features, addressing a common pain point in LFMs.
- Identifiability discussion: Theorem 4 and the merging heuristic address non-identifiability issues in LFMs and connect them to model complexity, which is useful in practice.
- Empirical results suggest good model selection and predictive performance across several datasets, with consistent trends that FAB avoids overfitting (PLL vs TLL gap).

Weaknesses and detailed concerns
Technical derivations
- Critical dependence on Lemma 1 with only “mild assumptions” deferred to the supplement. As the entire FIC form hinges on this scaling, at least a sketch of conditions (e.g., moments, rank conditions on Z, behavior of Z^T Z/N, handling of λd and b_d) is needed in the main text. The statement log det|F(d)| = sum_k log(sum_n z_nk/N) + Op(1) is unusual; clarify dimensionality, constants, and whether the result holds uniformly in K, D, and for random Z with dependencies.
- The term −|P| − (DK/2) log N in J(Z,P) (Eq. 8) is opaque. Conventionally, Laplace yields −(dim P/2) log N; the split between |P| and DK/2 log N should be derived carefully. As written, the mix of constants and log N terms is hard to verify.
- Mean-field approximation: The corollary (Eq. 9 and following) states asymptotic closeness of FIC to the log evidence, but the algorithm optimizes an additional lower bound due to mean-field and linearization. Please separate the asymptotics for the ideal FIC from the tractable lower bound actually optimized.
- CCCP update: Eq. (14) appears inconsistent with Eq. (11). From L(q) in Eq. (13), linearizing −(D/2) log(sum_n µ_nk) yields a gradient term −D/(2 sum_n µ^{t−1}_nk), giving µ^t_nk = g(c_nk + η(πk) − D/(2 sum_n µ^{t−1}_nk)). Eq. (14) omits the factor 1/2. The text claims equivalence to Eq. (11) by setting Nπ̃k = sum_n µ^{t−1}_nk, which would also demand the 1/2 factor. This discrepancy is nontrivial and should be corrected; otherwise the accelerated update is mis-scaled.
- M-step: The claim that the solutions for W, Λ, b are “exactly the same as those of PPCA” is misleading. PPCA assumes continuous Gaussian latent variables; LFMs have binary Z with mean-field moments. The correct M-step solves ridge-like linear systems using E[zz^T] = µµ^T + diag(µ − µ^2); please provide the actual formulas or a citation specific to LFMs.

Algorithmic and convergence issues
- Monotonicity: While alternating maximization of the stated lower bound L is claimed to be monotone, the shrinkage removal and merge post-processing change model dimensionality. The paper states that removal “increases L due to a decrease in model complexity,” but this needs a formal argument for both removal (π̃k → 0 or 1) and merging (when w·k ≈ w·l) ensuring no decrease in L. The practical merge step relies on a Euclidean threshold and re-optimization; guarantees are unclear.
- Complexity: There is no explicit per-iteration complexity analysis. The accelerated shrinkage introduces Tshrink inner loops per feature; the asymptotic and practical cost scaling in N, D, K (before and after shrinkage) should be quantified.

Empirical evaluation
- Fairness: FAB/EM appear to estimate Λ (noise precision), while “other software did not learn the standard deviation,” so it was fixed to a presumed ground truth or 0.75. This mismatch likely affects TLL/PLL and K; please either fix noise across all methods (including FAB) or use variants that learn noise for all baselines. Otherwise the comparison is biased.
- Baselines: VB is “totally omitted” due to runtime; MEIBP is omitted on block data “since its parameter setting was very sensitive.” These choices reduce the strength of the empirical case. Consider including at least one VB configuration on a subset, and ensure MEIBP settings are tuned comparably to FAB’s. For MEIBP on yaleB/USPS, the initial K was changed to 100 ad hoc due to runtime; again, this compromises comparability.
- Reporting: Many key figures/tables are in the supplement; the main text lacks error bars/variability. Some results are reported as “median over 10 trials,” others over 5 splits; this is inconsistent. Hardware and stopping criteria per method should be detailed. Statistical significance or at least variability should be reported.
- Scope: All experiments use diagonal Λ and Gaussian likelihood. Discuss robustness to correlated noise or other likelihoods.

Presentation
- Notational inconsistencies and typos: “mean-filed,” “NBP” vs NPB, “MEIPB,” “this this update,” inconsistent use of w·k vs wd, missing parentheses (Eq. 1), unclear constants in Eq. (10) (“− 2D + K log N”), and minor LaTeX errors. These hinder reproducibility.
- Many core assumptions and proofs are deferred; at least summarize the assumptions for Lemma 1 and Theorem 4 in the main text.

Pros
- Potentially impactful theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs.
- Clear mechanism for automatic feature selection via a principled term.
- Practical acceleration and an identifiability-aware post-processing idea.
- Promising empirical trends on several datasets.

Cons
- A critical derivation error (factor 1/2) in the CCCP update; unclear constants in the FIC/J expression.
- Heavy reliance on unpresented “mild” assumptions; insufficient detail in main text.
- Questionable fairness in baseline setups (fixed noise for competitors, omitted baselines), limited statistical rigor.
- Convergence/monotonicity under shrink/merge not formally established.
- Notational and editorial issues reduce clarity.

Recommendation: Reject (weak)
The central idea is interesting and potentially significant, but the current manuscript has technical inconsistencies (notably Eq. 14), missing/opaque derivations, and fairness issues in experiments that undermine confidence. With corrected derivations, clearer assumptions, stronger and fairer empirical evaluation, and improved exposition, this work could become a solid contribution.

Confidence: 0.8
