- Core contribution: extends FAB to latent feature models (binary features, linear-Gaussian) with Lemma 1 showing the Hessian determinant yields the log ∑n znk penalty, producing an FIC for LFMs matching mixtures and asymptotic equivalence to marginal likelihood.

- Algorithmic contribution: tractable mean-field lower bound, fixed-point E-step with FAB shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃k), standard M-step, CCCP interpretation of updates, accelerateShrinkage (multiple CCCP steps) and a post-hoc merge routine.

- Empirical claim: FAB is competitive or superior in model selection and predictive log-likelihood, uses fewer features than EM, and is faster than IBP/VB; MEIBP can be faster but often has worse predictive performance.

- Theoretical strength: Lemma 1 is pivotal and, if its assumptions hold broadly, is an elegant result recovering the FAB penalty for LFMs; FICLFM and monotonic ascent under basic alternating updates are consistent.

- Theoretical concerns: key assumptions and regularity conditions for Lemma 1, neglect of O_p(1) terms, and dropping log-determinant Laplace factors are deferred to the supplement and need explicit statement/justification.

- Monotonicity concerns: unclear which algorithmic variants (accelerateShrinkage, simultaneous π/π̃ updates, pruning/merging) preserve the lower-bound ascent guarantee versus being heuristic.

- Identifiability: Theorem 4 shows FAB biases toward balanced activations for duplicated columns; needs clearer intuition, precise conditions (exclude degenerate activation sums), and empirical frequency of successful merges.

- Experimental fairness concerns: FAB learns noise precision while many baselines use fixed noise; IBP α is fixed at 3; MEIBP initialization limited — these choices may bias comparisons in FAB’s favor.

- Requested experimental ablations: match or allow noise learning across methods, vary or infer α, remove/ablate shrinkage acceleration and merge, report wall-clock vs iterations and per-iteration complexity, and evaluate larger-scale settings.

- Clarity issues: main text omits key assumptions for Lemma 1 and high-level intuition about shared θd producing additive log ∑n znk terms; per-iteration complexity (N, D, K) should be stated.

- Minor presentation fixes: fix typos (“mean-filed”, duplicated words), clarify footnote on regular vs singular models and invertibility conditions for FZ, Fk, and formalize conditions for safe feature elimination (∑n µnk/N ∈ {0,1}).

- Merge procedure under-specified: provide a concrete criterion (e.g., threshold on ||w_k − w_l||) and a proof sketch or empirical evidence that merging increases the lower bound.

- Originality: novel extension of FAB to LFMs that break the block-diagonal Hessian assumption, novel asymptotic Hessian determinant calculation, and useful CCCP viewpoint and identifiability/merge ideas.

- Significance: potentially broad impact for automatic model selection in LFMs if assumptions hold; promising empirical results but stronger, fairer experiments needed to confirm.

- Pros summarized: elegant theoretical extension, practical algorithm with monotonic basic updates and pruning, CCCP acceleration, identifiability-aware analysis, and compact models in practice.

- Cons summarized: heavy reliance on supplemental assumptions/proofs, heuristic accelerated/post-processing steps with unclear guarantees, and experimental fairness and limited ablations.

- Suggestions for authors: state Lemma 1 assumptions and intuition in main text, specify which steps preserve monotonicity and guard against violations, provide complexity/runtime and shrinkage trajectories, perform fairness/sensitivity experiments, and compare to related factor models (BPFA, ARD-based methods).

- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid theoretical and practical contribution contingent on addressing clarity and experimental fairness issues.
