1. Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs), enabling automatic feature-number selection without priors.  
2. Shows theoretically that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models despite non-block-diagonal Hessians (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
3. Proposes a practical FAB/LFM algorithm with a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration technique.  
4. Proves parameter identifiability for LFMs, enabling a justified merge post-processing step (Theorem 4).  
5. Connects the work to Bayesian nonparametrics (e.g., IBP) and prior FAB literature, addressing a gap in FAB applicability.  
6. Strong theoretical contributions overall, with precise derivations and useful CCCP interpretation.  
7. Some theoretical assumptions (used in Lemma 1) are not fully specified or explored for sensitivity to violations.  
8. Asymptotic claims (FIC converging to marginal log-likelihood) are sound but not thoroughly validated experimentally for large N.  
9. Experimental evaluation is comprehensive but has limitations: MEIBP applied to non-negative-data models without adaptation, and only moderate N (≤2000).  
10. Computational cost comparisons may be misleading due to differing implementation efficiencies (Matlab FAB vs optimized baselines).  
11. Lack of thorough statistical testing across seeds (only 5 for real data) weakens empirical claims slightly.  
12. Paper is generally clear and well-structured, with precise math and a well-explained CCCP section.  
13. Some key theoretical motivations and implementation details (e.g., baseline parameter settings, code versions) are under-explained, affecting reproducibility.  
14. Novelty: core insight that LFMs share FIC form with MMs is significant; CCCP acceleration and identifiability-based merging are valuable additions.  
15. Paper could better distinguish itself from existing IBP/variational methods and clarify the practical role of linearization parameter π̃k.  
16. High significance: offers scalable, theoretically grounded model selection for LFMs with demonstrated empirical advantages.  
17. Recommended for acceptance: strong theoretical extension and practical algorithm despite minor empirical and exposition weaknesses.
