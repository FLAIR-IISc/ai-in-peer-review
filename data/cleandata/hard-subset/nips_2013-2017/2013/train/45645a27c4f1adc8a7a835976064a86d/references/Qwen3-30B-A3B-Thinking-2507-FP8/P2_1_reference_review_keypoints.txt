- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Key theoretical insight: LFMs have the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as mixture models despite not satisfying block-diagonal Hessian conditions.  
- Proves Lemma 1 and Theorem 2, establishing convergence to the marginal log-likelihood under stated assumptions.  
- Introduces a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2) to eliminate irrelevant features early and speed optimization.  
- Presents identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) and a merge post-processing step to address parameter identifiability.  
- Empirical results across synthetic and real datasets show better model-selection accuracy and predictive performance (higher PLL) with lower computational cost than IBP, VB, MEIBP, and EM.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and comparisons to baselines are fair; algorithm descriptions are precise and reproducible.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-connected to prior FAB work, with assumptions detailed in supplementary material.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; mathematical derivations and CCCP connection are clearly explained.  
- Clarity could be improved with more intuitive explanations for why the Hessian analysis holds for LFMs and with concrete examples.  
- Original contributions include the novel extension of FAB to LFMs, the CCCP interpretation, shrinkage acceleration, and identifiability results.  
- Significance: provides an automated, hyperparameter-free method for selecting latent feature count, practical for large-scale problems.  
- Limitation: FIC is asymptotic and may be inaccurate for small N; practical threshold for N is not specified.  
- Weakness: could better specify scenarios favoring FAB/LFM and expand comparisons to more recent scalable VB methods.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically sound, novel, practically valuable, and meets NIPS standards.
