- Summary: Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), showing FIC structure persists and introducing a CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration and identifiability analysis.

- Strength — Theoretical contribution: Demonstrates surprising preservation of FIC structure for LFMs; Lemma 1 is key and well-justified.

- Strength — Computational efficiency: Proposes shrinkage acceleration based on CCCP, empirically shown to speed FAB (Figure 1).

- Strength — Identifiability analysis: Analyzes parameter identifiability, explains MLE issues, and motivates merge post-processing.

- Strength — Empirical validation: Shows competitive performance vs. IBP, VB, MEIBP on synthetic and real data with accuracy and runtime benefits.

- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and derivations (including CCCP connection) are clear.

- Weakness — Novelty limited: Applying FAB to LFMs is mainly an inference-method contribution, not new modeling capability.

- Weakness — Experimental scope: Comparisons are uneven (e.g., VB omitted on real data); broader, consistent experiments needed.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter bias: Fixing noise std for comparisons may bias results; sensitivity to these choices needs more discussion.

- Weakness — Merge post-processing: Empirical impact of merging is limited and not convincingly demonstrated.

- Quality (8/10): Technically sound derivations and convincing theory; would benefit from deeper analysis of FIC limits for small N.

- Clarity (9/10): Clear exposition and standard notation; CCCP-acceleration link well-explained.

- Originality (7/10): Novel extension of FAB to LFMs with useful insight, but builds heavily on prior FAB work.

- Significance (7/10): Advances efficient Bayesian inference for LFMs and offers practical speedups, though impact is somewhat specialized.

- Pro: Provides sound theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs with practical acceleration and regularization justification.

- Pro: Competitive empirical performance and computational advantages make it a useful tool for LFM inference.

- Con: Contribution is mainly methodological rather than modeling-innovative; experiments and post-processing impact need strengthening.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — address experimental scope and discuss fixed-noise comparisons more thoroughly.
