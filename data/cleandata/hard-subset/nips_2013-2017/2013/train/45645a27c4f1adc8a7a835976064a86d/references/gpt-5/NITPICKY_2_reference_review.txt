Summary
The paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian observations. The key technical claim is Lemma 1: the log-determinant of the per-dimension negative Hessian scales as sum_k log(∑n znk/N) + Op(1), which leads to an FIC for LFMs of exactly the same functional form as for MMs. Building on this, the authors propose a tractable lower bound and an EM-like FAB algorithm with: (i) a mean-field E-step that has a CCCP interpretation, (ii) an “accelerateShrinkage” routine to prune features early, and (iii) an identifiability analysis motivating a merge post-processing. Experiments compare FAB to IBP (Gibbs), VB (finite K), MEIBP, and a variational EM baseline.

Strengths
- Conceptual contribution: a nontrivial extension of the FAB/FIC framework to LFMs via asymptotic analysis that removes the block-diagonal Hessian assumption used in prior work on MMs.
- Interesting insight: relating the FAB E-step to CCCP and using it to accelerate shrinkage.
- Practical algorithm that produces compact models (small K) and competitive or better predictive log-likelihood on several datasets, with empirical speedups from early pruning.
- Discussion of identifiability and a principled merge post-processing is valuable.

Weaknesses and detailed concerns
Technical correctness and rigor
- Central lemma deferred: Lemma 1 (log det F(d) ≈ ∑k log(∑n znk/N)) is the linchpin. The main paper provides neither proof sketch nor clear assumptions. Given Z^T Z contains off-diagonal terms ∑n znkznℓ, it is nontrivial that det(F(d)) factorizes into counts ∑n znk up to Op(1). Please state precise assumptions (e.g., independence of features, sparsity, incoherence conditions on Z, concentration results) and at least a proof outline in the main text.
- Equation (2) signs/inverses: Laplace approximations typically yield factors (2π)^{|θ|/2} det(H)^{-1/2}. The expression as written appears to use det(·)^{+1/2}, which looks inverted. This matters for deriving the penalties. Please double-check.
- Theorem 2/J(Z, P): J includes −|P| and −(DK/2) log N terms. The −|P| term is unusual (in BIC-like criteria one gets (|P|/2) log N, plus O(1) constants). If you are absorbing constants, be explicit; otherwise this looks dimensionally inconsistent. Also, |P| for this model is DK + D + D + K; the paper does not clearly state what |P| refers to later (Eq. 10 uses “− 2D + K log N”, which seems inconsistent with J).
- Eq. (10) constants: L(q,P, π̃) shows “− 2D + K log N”. This seems at odds with J’s −|P| − (DK/2) log N. Please reconcile and present a clean, correct expression.
- CCCP factor inconsistency: Eq. (11) uses − D/(2Nπ̃k), while Eq. (14) (the CCCP form) uses − D/(∑n μt−1 nk) without the factor 1/2. Since Nπ̃k = ∑n μt−1 nk by Eq. (12), Eq. (14) is missing 1/2 and is not equivalent to Eq. (11) as claimed. This is a critical inconsistency.
- M-step “exactly the same as PPCA”: For binary latent features under mean-field, the M-step for W is a linear least-squares with E[Z], E[ZZ^T], not PPCA (which assumes Gaussian latent variables and a specific isotropic prior). The closed forms are similar in shape but not “exactly the same.” Please correct.
- Monotonicity guarantees: The text claims “monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound through iterative optimization,” yet accelerateShrinkage performs multiple inner CCCP steps and updates π, π̃ between E/M, and the shrinkage/merge steps change model dimension. A formal monotonicity guarantee is not provided for this composite procedure. Please clarify which steps are provably monotonic and when only heuristic.
- Merge step errors/typos: The text states “w∗·k = 2w∗·k and μ∗·k = μ∗·k + μ∗·ℓ” when merging; this is clearly a typo and also ignores scaling/normalization issues. Please provide the exact, justified merge rule and show that L increases.
- Assumptions such as “joint marginal log-likelihood is bounded for sufficiently large N” are unclear; log-likelihood typically scales with N. If you mean per-sample quantities, please say so.

Empirical evaluation and fairness
- Noise handling fairness: FAB/EM learn Λ (precision), whereas baselines had noise std fixed (to the true value for synthetic, and 0.75 for real). This is an unfair advantage. Either allow baselines to estimate noise (or fix Λ for FAB/EM) or present sensitivity analyses.
- Baseline coverage: VB is omitted on real data for runtime; MEIBP is omitted on some data and reinitialized with K=100 on others “to finish,” which introduces bias. The conclusions about superiority would be more convincing with: (i) consistent initialization policies for all methods; (ii) reporting early-stopped baselines with wall-clock budgets; (iii) including stronger recent baselines (e.g., beta-process factor analysis, ARD-based sparse factor models).
- Reproducibility details are sparse: exact preprocessing (centering with b or global mean?), stopping criteria for all baselines, number of CCCP inner steps, pruning thresholds when ∑n μnk/N is near 0 or 1, and merge thresholds are not fully specified. Some algorithm lines are garbled (Algorithm 2’s “− 121w>·k”).

Clarity and presentation
- Numerous typos and notation issues impede readability: “mean-filed,” “this this update,” “NBP vs NPB,” “on early learning phase,” etc. Some equations are malformed or ambiguously typeset (e.g., Algorithm 2 line 2). Please thoroughly proofread and standardize notation (e.g., consistent use of vectors/rows/columns; define |P| once).
- Lemma/Theorem statements should include assumptions and brief intuition in the main paper. As it stands, the most novel technical result (Lemma 1) is opaque.

Originality and significance
- If correct, the result that FIC for LFMs matches the MM form is interesting and could broaden applicability of FAB. The CCCP perspective is a nice touch. However, given the central proof is absent, inconsistencies in key equations, and fairness issues in experiments, the current version does not yet meet the bar.

Pros
- Ambitious and potentially impactful theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs.
- Practical algorithm with sensible heuristics (early shrinkage, merging).
- CCCP connection is insightful.
- Promising empirical results and compact models.

Cons
- Central theoretical claim not substantiated in the main text; key assumptions unclear.
- Equation inconsistencies (notably missing 1/2 factor in CCCP update), questionable constants, and likely Laplace sign errors.
- Unclear/incorrect statements (PPCA equivalence), merge rule typos.
- Experimental fairness and completeness concerns; reproducibility details missing.
- Many presentation/formatting issues.

Questions for authors
1) State and justify all assumptions for Lemma 1; provide at least a proof sketch. Under what Z regimes does the determinant reduce to counts?
2) Fix the CCCP update inconsistency and clarify the exact lower bound being optimized; which steps guarantee monotonicity?
3) Provide the correct M-step derivations and clarify the relationship to PPCA.
4) Re-run key experiments with fair noise handling and standardized budgets/initializations; include ablations on accelerateShrinkage/merge.
5) Correct the merge procedure and show it increases L.
6) Clean up equations/constants and provide a clear expression for the FIC lower bound used in practice.

Recommendation: Weak reject
The idea is promising, but the accumulation of technical inconsistencies, missing proofs for the central lemma, and fairness/presentation issues outweigh the contributions in the current form. With corrected theory, clarified algorithms, and fairer experiments, this work could be much stronger.
