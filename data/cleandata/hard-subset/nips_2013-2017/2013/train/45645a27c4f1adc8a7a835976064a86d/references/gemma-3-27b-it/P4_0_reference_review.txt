This paper presents Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference extended to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The core contribution is demonstrating that the FIC for LFMs has the same form as for Mixture Models (MMs), despite LFMs not initially satisfying the condition required for prior FAB applications. This allows applying the FAB framework to a broader class of models. The authors further analyze the FAB E-step through a Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP) lens, leading to a shrinkage acceleration method and insights into parameter identifiability and feature merging.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the extension of FAB to LFMs. The authors effectively relate their work to existing literature on FAB, Non-Parametric Bayesian (NPB) methods, Variational Bayesian (VB) methods, and Infinite LFMs. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs is a significant step, and the Lemma 1 establishing the crucial log-det identity is clearly stated. The connection to CCCP and the resulting acceleration method are insightful. The discussion on parameter identifiability and the proposed merging post-processing are valuable additions.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Extending FAB inference to LFMs is a novel contribution, expanding the applicability of this promising Bayesian approximation method. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs is a key achievement.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical derivations, particularly Lemma 1 and Theorem 2, appear to be solid, with references to relevant asymptotic analysis techniques. The CCCP analysis and its application to acceleration are also well-reasoned.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is standard, and the explanations are clear. The authors diligently relate the concepts to prior work, making it easier to understand the contributions.
*   **Significance:** The method addresses the limitations of existing approaches like NPB and VB methods for large-scale data by offering potentially lower computational cost. The automated model selection aspect of FAB is also valuable. The empirical evaluation on both synthetic and real data supports the claims.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Assumptions:** While the paper states the need for “some mild assumptions” for Lemma 1, these assumptions are relegated to the supplementary materials. A more explicit discussion of these assumptions within the main body would strengthen the presentation. The limitations imposed by these assumptions should be clearly articulated.
*   **Experimental Scope:** While the experimental section is comprehensive, a deeper analysis of the ablation study for the acceleration method (Algorithm 2) would be valuable. Specifically, how much computational speedup is achieved for different data sizes, and what are the tradeoffs in terms of accuracy?
*   **Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The comparison to MEIBP feels somewhat incomplete. The statement about requiring an appropriately set initial K for MEIBP suggests sensitivity that requires further investigation.  A more standardized setup or a more careful tuning of MEIBP parameters would provide a fairer comparison.
*   **Convergence Discussion:** The stopping criterion (δ = 10−4) is mentioned but not justified. A discussion on the sensitivity of the results to this parameter would be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of FAB inference to LFMs.
*   The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-supported.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on both synthetic and real data.
*   The paper addresses a significant problem in latent feature modeling by offering a computationally efficient alternative to existing methods.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The assumptions underlying Lemma 1 are not sufficiently discussed in the main body of the paper.
*   The experimental evaluation, while extensive, could benefit from a more in-depth ablation study and a fairer comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
*   A more detailed discussion of the convergence criterion and its sensitivity would strengthen the work.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper represents a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent feature models. The extension of FAB to LFMs is a valuable addition, and the theoretical and experimental results are convincing. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the points raised above regarding assumptions, experimental analysis, and convergence discussion. The potential impact of this work on the scalability and automation of Bayesian model selection justifies its inclusion in NIPS.
