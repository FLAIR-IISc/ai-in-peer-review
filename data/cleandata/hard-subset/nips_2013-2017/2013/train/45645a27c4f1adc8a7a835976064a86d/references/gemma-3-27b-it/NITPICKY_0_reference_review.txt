This paper presents an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing a limitation of previous FAB work requiring a block-diagonal Hessian. The authors demonstrate FIC convergence, parameter identifiability, and propose a shrinkage acceleration method based on a Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP) interpretation. While the core idea of extending FAB to LFMs is promising, and the theoretical analysis appears sound, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor, yet cumulatively significant, issues related to clarity, presentation, and a lack of thorough experimental validation.

**Summary of Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Contribution:** The derivation of FICs for LFMs and the demonstration of their equivalence to those for MMs is a valuable theoretical result, broadening the applicability of FAB inference.
*   **CCCP-based Acceleration:**  The connection to CCCP and the resulting shrinkage acceleration method offer a practical improvement in computational efficiency.
*   **Identifiability Analysis:** Addressing parameter identifiability is crucial for the reliability of learned models and the analysis presented is insightful.
*   **Automated Model Selection:** Maintaining the key benefit of FAB – automatic model selection without manual hyperparameter tuning – is a significant advantage.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is frequently difficult to follow due to dense mathematical notation, abrupt transitions between ideas, and a lack of intuitive explanation for key concepts. The introduction, while outlining the motivation, feels rushed and fails to adequately contextualize the problem for readers unfamiliar with FAB.
*   **Minor Technical Inconsistencies:** Numerous minor errors and inconsistencies plague the mathematical derivations and notations. For example, the definition of notation (aij, ai, a·j) appears redundant and inconsistent with standard linear algebra conventions. The usage of “Op(1)” is not clearly defined in the context of the theorems.
*   **Insufficient Experimental Detail:** The experimental section lacks crucial details regarding parameter settings, baselines, and statistical significance. The claims of improved performance are not fully substantiated. The choice of fixing the noise standard deviation to 0.75 for the real datasets appears arbitrary.
*   **Limited Novelty of Experiments:** While the synthetic experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the acceleration method, they are relatively simple. The block data experiment is more compelling but lacks a detailed comparison with alternative approaches.
*   **Merge Post-processing:** The description of the merge post-processing step is vague and its practical impact is not adequately demonstrated.  The claim that “a few merging operations were likely to occur” lacks quantitative support.
*   **Overly Strong Claims:** The authors repeatedly state “superiority” without sufficient justification. The comparisons, while showing competitive results, do not definitively establish superiority across all tested scenarios.
* **Formatting issues:** There are many formatting issues that distract from the content, e.g. inconsistent equation numbering, and a non-standard definition of notation.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   **Introduction:** The motivation for extending FAB to LFMs is clear, but the limitations of existing methods (NBP and VB) are presented without sufficient quantitative support.
*   **Section 1.1 (Related Work):** Equation (1) lacks clear explanation for ‘P’.  The discussion of FICMM feels unnecessarily detailed and repetitive.
*   **Section 2.1 (FICs for LFMs):** Lemma 1 is a crucial result, but the phrase “under some mild assumptions” is vague. These assumptions should be explicitly stated and justified. The meaning of ‘Op(1)’ is unclear; a formal definition or reference is required.
*   **Section 2.2 (FAB/LFM Algorithm):**  The derivation of the FAB update equations is not sufficiently detailed. The justification for the mean-field approximation is weak. The discussion of the shrinkage effect is repetitive.
*   **Section 3 (Analysis and Refinements):** The CCCP interpretation is interesting but lacks a clear explanation of why it leads to acceleration. The identifiability analysis is well-presented but could be more concise.
*   **Section 4 (Experiments):** The experimental setup lacks sufficient detail. The choice of evaluation metrics is not fully justified.  The results are presented in a disorganized manner.  The claim that MEIBP is faster but less accurate is not supported by clear evidence. The discussion of the difference between TLL and PLL needs to be more nuanced.
*   **Appendix/Supplementary Material:** The paper relies heavily on the supplementary material, but the quality of the writing and presentation in the supplement is also suboptimal.

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**Against:**

*   Numerous minor technical errors and inconsistencies.
*   Lack of clarity and organization.
*   Insufficient experimental detail and validation.
*   Overly strong claims and a lack of nuance.
*   Formatting Issues
*   The contributions, while theoretically sound, don’t represent a breakthrough significant enough to warrant acceptance *given* the deficiencies in presentation.

**For:**

*   The theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs is a valuable contribution.
*   The CCCP-based acceleration method is a practical improvement.
*   The identifiability analysis is insightful.
*   The core idea addresses an important problem in latent feature modeling.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potential significance of the theoretical contributions, I recommend **rejection**. The paper is hampered by a multitude of issues relating to clarity, presentation, and experimental validation. While the ideas presented are interesting, the execution is flawed and the paper, in its current form, does not meet the standards of quality expected for NIPS. Significant revisions addressing the points raised above would be necessary for reconsideration. Specifically, a complete rewrite focusing on clarity and a more thorough experimental evaluation are essential.
