- Extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), enabling automated model selection without priors or tuning.  
- Derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs despite the absence of the block-diagonal Hessian assumption (supported by Lemma 1 and Theorem 2).  
- Provides theoretical analyses of parameter identifiability and proposes a merge post-processing step to handle non-uniqueness.  
- Introduces a shrinkage-acceleration technique based on a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) to speed up FAB inference.  
- Empirically demonstrates competitive performance on synthetic and real-world datasets versus IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Situates the work relative to prior FAB, Bayesian nonparametrics (IBP), MAD-Bayes, and CCCP literature, highlighting an asymptotic (rather than prior-based) approach.  
- Strength: Broadens FAB applicability with solid theoretical derivation for LFMs.  
- Strength: Rigorous identifiability treatment and practical merge strategy.  
- Strength: Notable computational efficiency gains via the proposed acceleration and clear presentation.  
- Weakness: The FIC derivation is complex and may be hard to follow; transition from Lemma 1 to Theorem 2 feels abrupt.  
- Weakness: Assumptions underlying Lemma 1 are not thoroughly discussed, limiting clarity on theoretical scope.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity of the acceleration hyperparameter (Tshrink) is not systematically studied.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to strong deep-learning-based feature-learning baselines (e.g., autoencoders, sparse coding).  
- Weakness: Merge post-processing is described briefly without quantitative merging criteria or thresholds.  
- For acceptance: Addresses an important limitation of FAB, provides novel theoretical results, shows strong empirical performance, and offers a useful automated model-selection tool.  
- Against acceptance: Derivations may be inaccessible to a broad audience, theoretical guarantees depend on restrictive assumptions, and acceleration hyperparameter sensitivity needs more analysis.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with significant contribution; reviewer recommends Strong Accept.
