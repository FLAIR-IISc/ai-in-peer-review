- Theoretical advance: extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) via Lemma 1, enabling FIC to take the same form despite non-block-diagonal Hessians.  
- Lemma 1 novelty: shows the log Σn znk term appears in log det |F(d)| without block-diagonal assumptions, a counterintuitive and broadly relevant insight.  
- Algorithmic innovation: shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) based on CCCP drastically reduces computation and enables early elimination of irrelevant features.  
- Method development: presents an FAB/LFM algorithm with merge post-processing and proves parameter identifiability and FIC convergence.  
- Empirical performance: outperforms IBP, VB, and MEIBP in model selection accuracy and predictive performance, especially for large N.  
- Practical benefits: automatically selects compact models with less overfitting (smaller TLL–PLL gaps) and lower computational cost.  
- Rigor: strong theoretical proofs and comprehensive experiments; supplementary materials support key claims.  
- Clarity concern: Lemma 1’s derivation and intuition need clearer, more accessible explanation for non-expert readers.  
- Comparative context lacking: paper should better explain how FAB/LFM’s identifiability and other properties differ practically from competing methods.  
- Limitations insufficiently discussed: missing analysis for high-dimensional settings (D >> N), non-Gaussian noise, and complex feature interactions that may violate assumptions.  
- Presentation issues: occasional inconsistent notation (e.g., use of “N”) and Algorithm 1 needs more detailed stepwise commentary.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance due to substantial theoretical, algorithmic, and empirical contributions.  
- Suggested improvements: add intuitive explanation for Lemma 1, clarify identifiability implications, discuss limitations (high-dim/non-Gaussian), and clarify Algorithm 1.
