- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by showing shared Hessian structure enables FAB’s application.  
- Demonstrates theoretical foundation with Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 validating the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.  
- Relates FAB E-steps to the Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP), enabling a CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2).  
- Provides identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) and guidelines for merging latent features in post-processing.  
- Empirically competitive with IBP and VB on synthetic and real datasets, showing good predictive performance and automatic dimensionality selection.  
- Ablation study confirms shrinkage acceleration reduces computational cost.  
- Well-written and organized with clear theoretical derivations and algorithm descriptions.  
- Experimental comparisons sometimes use limited parameter settings (e.g., fixed noise std) for baselines, reducing thoroughness.  
- FAB remains computationally heavy on larger datasets (e.g., USPS) compared to faster methods like MEIBP.  
- Related work discussion could better contrast asymptotic assumptions (noise variance vs. prior) with MAD-Bayes.  
- Merge post-processing is theoretically justified but underdeveloped and its practical impact is unclear.  
- Quality rated 8/10: technically sound with rigorous proofs and solid experiments.  
- Clarity rated 8/10: mostly clear but theoretical sections are dense and could use better visualizations of shrinkage/merging effects.  
- Originality rated 8/10: novel extension of FAB to LFMs with CCCP connection and identifiability insights.  
- Significance rated 8/10: valuable automated model selection and potential for faster LFM inference.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: broaden parameter sweeps for baselines, analyze merge post-processing in more detail, and clarify comparisons to MAD-Bayes’ asymptotic assumptions.
