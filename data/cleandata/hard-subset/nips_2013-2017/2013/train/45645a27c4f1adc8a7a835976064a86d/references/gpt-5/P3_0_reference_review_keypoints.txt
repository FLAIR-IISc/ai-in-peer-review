- Core contribution: extends FAB inference from mixtures to binary-feature linear-Gaussian LFMs, deriving an FIC that penalizes by −(DK/2) log N − (D/2)∑k log ∑n znk and yields an EM-like algorithm with automatic shrinkage.  
- Theoretical claim (Lemma 1): log-det of per-dimension Hessian decomposes into a sum of log feature-usage counts, giving the same FIC form as mixtures.  
- Algorithmic work: mean-field E-step fixed-point updates include a shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃k); authors reinterpret E-step as CCCP and add an accelerateShrinkage routine plus a merge post-processing step.  
- Empirical results: synthetic, block, and real-data experiments show strong model selection, predictive performance, and competitive runtimes, especially with acceleration.  
- Strength — novelty: non-obvious extension showing FIC form holds for LFMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessians.  
- Strength — hyperparameter-free: FAB selects K/parameters asymptotically without hand-tuned priors; shrinkage arises from theory.  
- Strength — algorithms: CCCP viewpoint, acceleration, and merge heuristic are well-motivated and empirically useful; merge preserves bound by checking L.  
- Weakness — key theory deferred: Lemma 1’s proof sketch and explicit assumptions are missing, raising doubts about required incoherence or bounded-correlation conditions on Z.  
- Weakness — notation and bound clarity: typos and unclear constants (e.g., Eq. (10)) obscure how complexity terms arise from det F(d); dimensionalities are not well defined.  
- Weakness — monotonicity under acceleration: accelerateShrinkage and early feature elimination lack a formal guarantee that the lower bound L never decreases.  
- Weakness — experimental fairness: FAB appears to learn noise precision λ while baselines use fixed σ; IBP hyperparameters (α) are fixed without tuning; some relevant baselines (ARD/PPCA/spike-and-slab) are omitted.  
- Weakness — reporting gaps: need runtime breakdowns, sensitivity analyses for Tshrink/initialization/δ, and partial results for methods omitted due to runtime.  
- Weakness — merging criterion: Theorem 4 addresses exact duplicates only; a principled, quantitative merge criterion for near-duplicates and complexity of merge for large K are lacking.  
- Quality concern: reliance on supplement for critical proofs reduces rigor; adding a proof sketch and tightened assumptions in main text is necessary.  
- Clarity concern: overall readable but marred by typos, inconsistent notation, and missing definitions; recommend a concise summary table of objectives, updates, and guarantees.  
- Originality: idea is original and interesting — extending FAB to LFMs and CCCP acceleration is a useful contribution.  
- Significance: potentially practical, hyperparameter-light alternative to IBP variants if theoretical claims hold broadly.  
- Key author action items: state Lemma 1 assumptions and sketch proof; clarify whether FAB learns λ and fix or ablate accordingly; provide monotonicity proof or safeguards for accelerateShrinkage; report sensitivity to Tshrink/initialization/merge threshold; characterize FIC reliability for small N.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos/equation inconsistencies (Eq. 6, 8, 10), define |P| and dimensionalities, clarify per-iteration complexity and savings from early shrinkage, and cite ARD/variational FA/PPCA baselines.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept pending clarification of Lemma 1 assumptions/proof and addressing experimental fairness issues.
