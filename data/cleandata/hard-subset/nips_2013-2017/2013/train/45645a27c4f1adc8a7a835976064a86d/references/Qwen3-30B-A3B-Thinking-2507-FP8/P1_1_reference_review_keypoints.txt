- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), enabling automated model selection without hyperparameters.  
- Shows the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models despite LFMs violating block-diagonal Hessian assumptions.  
- Introduces a CCCP interpretation of FAB optimization and a shrinkage acceleration method that greatly reduces computational cost.  
- Provides theoretical identifiability analysis and a merge post-processing step for latent features.  
- Key theoretical contributions include Lemma 1 and Theorem 2, addressing Laplace-method challenges when parameters are shared across features.  
- Empirical results on synthetic, block, and real datasets show superior computational efficiency and model-selection accuracy versus IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Novelty: first rigorous extension of FAB to LFMs, CCCP perspective, shrinkage acceleration, and identifiability insights.  
- Paper quality: technically sound, well-organized, clear notation, and supplementary proofs support main claims.  
- Significance: addresses scalable, hyperparameter-free model selection for widely used latent-variable models with practical impact.  
- Minor theoretical weakness: the link between Hessian analysis and the FIC derivation could be made more explicit.  
- Experimental weakness: lacks specific runtime/speedup numbers and comparisons; MEIBP comparisons limited by its failure on some datasets.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — substantial theoretical and practical contributions with only minor, addressable issues.
