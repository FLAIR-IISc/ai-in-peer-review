- Summary: Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), proving (Lemma 1) that LFMs yield the same FIC form as mixture models and introducing an FAB algorithm with CCCP-based shrinkage and a merge post-processing step.

- Strength: Theoretical contribution — Lemma 1 (with Theorem 2, Corollary 3) rigorously shows the FIC form holds for LFMs despite Hessian differences.

- Strength: Algorithmic innovation — derives a clear FAB/LFM algorithm and a clever CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration that improves model-selection efficiency.

- Strength: Empirical validation — experiments show superior computational efficiency and model-selection accuracy versus IBP, VB, and MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets.

- Strength: Theoretical depth — provides identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) and a merge post-processing step that link theory to practical implementation.

- Weakness: Limited theoretical intuition — main text lacks clear intuition and explicit assumptions behind Lemma 1 (left to supplementary material).

- Weakness: Comparison with MEIBP — evaluations do not fully clarify how to compare a MAP-based method (MEIBP) to the Bayesian approximation (FAB).

- Weakness: Missing formal complexity analysis — claims of "drastically reduced computational cost" lack scaling analysis in N, D, and K.

- Weakness: Implementation detail gaps — choices like Tshrink = 100 are undocumented and lack sensitivity analysis.

- Evaluation (Quality & Clarity): Technically sound and well-organized overall, though some asymptotic-statistics details are dense for non-experts.

- Evaluation (Originality & Significance): Novel and significant extension of FAB to LFMs with practical advantages in automatic model selection and computational efficiency.

- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS; pros — novel theory, effective algorithm and acceleration, strong empirical results, identifiability analysis; cons — incomplete assumptions discussion for Lemma 1, limited MEIBP comparison, no formal complexity analysis, minor clarity issues.
