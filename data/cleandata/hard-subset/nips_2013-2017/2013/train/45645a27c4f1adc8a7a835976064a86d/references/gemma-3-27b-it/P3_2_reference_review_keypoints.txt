- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) via a key theoretical result (Lemma 1) showing FAB applicability despite LFMs not meeting original conditions.  
- Builds on prior work (FAB, IBP, variational IBP, MAD-Bayes) and positions method as a computationally efficient alternative for automated model selection.  
- Theoretical contribution is rigorous and significant, enabling FAB for a broader model class.  
- Introduces an acceleration technique interpreting the FAB E-step via Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP), reducing computational cost.  
- Addresses parameter identifiability and proposes a merge post-processing step to improve learned representations.  
- Demonstrates automatic dimensionality selection as a core benefit of the approach.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents mathematical derivations logically.  
- Experimental results are promising but limited in scope, with synthetic tests and relatively small real-world datasets.  
- Comparisons to baselines (IBP, VB, MEIBP) are useful but not comprehensive; lacks evaluation against more recent or scalable methods (e.g., stochastic variational inference).  
- Baseline hyperparameter choices (fixed noise std) may affect comparisons and need discussion or justification.  
- Merge post-processing is under-specified; more detail on merging criteria beyond lower bound increases is needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand empirical evaluation to larger datasets, strengthen baseline comparisons, detail merge procedure, and address baseline hyperparameter sensitivity.
