

# Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

This paper proposes an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), claiming to achieve fully automated model selection without hyper-parameters. The authors derive FIC (Factorized Information Criterion) for LFMs, showing it has the same form as for mixture models despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition required for previous FAB applications. They also propose a shrinkage acceleration method and analyze parameter identifiability.

**Strengths:** The paper makes a theoretically interesting contribution by showing FAB can be extended to LFMs despite their different structure. The experimental results demonstrate that FAB achieves better model selection accuracy than competing methods with comparable or lower computational cost. The shrinkage acceleration method is well-motivated and empirically effective. The paper's technical claims about FIC convergence and identifiability are generally well-supported.

**Weaknesses:** The paper contains numerous technical and formatting inconsistencies that undermine its credibility as a rigorous NIPS submission. 

First, there are multiple notational inconsistencies: "logN" should be "log N" (e.g., Eq. 3, 4, 8), "DK" should be "D_K" (Eq. 8), and "D 2 log" should be "D/2 log" (Eq. 8, 13). In Lemma 1, the expression "log ∑ n znk N" is mathematically incorrect and should be "log(∑_n z_{nk}/N)". The paper claims FAB "does not have a hand-tunable hyper-parameter," yet uses δ = 10^{-4} and Tshrink = 100 in experiments, contradicting this claim.

Second, there are technical inaccuracies: The description of the shrinkage step when "∑_n µ_{nk}/N = 1" incorrectly states that w_k can be seen as a bias and added to b. This is incorrect because the bias term b is already present in the model, and adding w_k to b would distort the model structure. The paper also claims "automatic hidden states selection" (Introduction), but should say "automatic feature selection" since it's about latent feature models.

Third, the paper has formatting issues: Equation (11) has inconsistent spacing around operators ("η(πk)−D/2Nπ̃k" should be "η(π_k) - D/(2N\tilde{π}_k)"), and the bibliography entry for [1] is incomplete ("MAD-Bayes: MAP-based Asymptotic Derivations from Bayes, T. Broderick, B. Kulis, M.I. Jordan, In ICML,, , ,"). Reference [29] is also incomplete.

Fourth, the paper makes claims that aren't sufficiently supported in the main text: Theorem 4 on identifiability is referenced to supplementary materials, but the main paper doesn't provide sufficient justification for its importance to the algorithm's operation. The claim that "FAB/LFMs have parameter identifiability" is made without explaining how this differs from standard LFMs or why it's significant for the application.

Finally, there's a logical inconsistency: The paper states "FAB inference... does not have a hand-tunable hyper-parameter," but then uses δ = 10^{-4} and Tshrink = 100, which are clearly hyper-parameters. The paper should either clarify this point or acknowledge that these are indeed hyper-parameters.

**Conclusion:** While the paper presents an interesting theoretical extension of FAB inference to LFMs, the numerous technical inconsistencies, notational errors, and unsupported claims significantly undermine its scientific rigor. The paper would benefit from careful revision to address these issues before consideration for NIPS. The claims about hyper-parameters, the shrinkage step description, and the notational inconsistencies are particularly problematic for a venue like NIPS that values technical precision.

**Recommendation:** Reject. The paper's technical flaws are too numerous and fundamental to overlook, especially given NIPS' emphasis on rigorous mathematical presentation. While the core idea is potentially interesting, the current presentation fails to meet the technical standards expected at NIPS.
