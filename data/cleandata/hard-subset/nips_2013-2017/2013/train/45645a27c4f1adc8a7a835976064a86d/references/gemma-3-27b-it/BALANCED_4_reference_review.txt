This paper introduces a factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework extended to latent feature models (LFMs), addressing a limitation of previous FAB work that required a block-diagonal Hessian of the complete log-likelihood. The authors demonstrate that LFMs, despite not initially satisfying this condition, exhibit equivalent behavior in terms of FIC representation. They further analyze the FAB/LFM algorithm, relating E-steps to a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and introducing a shrinkage acceleration method, and establishing parameter identifiability with a merging post-processing step. The empirical evaluation demonstrates competitive performance in terms of both computational efficiency and model selection accuracy.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The extension of FAB inference to LFMs is a significant theoretical contribution, broadening the applicability of this promising Bayesian approximation method. The demonstration of FIC equivalence despite the block-diagonal condition being absent is insightful and well-motivated.
* **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of FICs for LFMs appears rigorous, supported by Lemma 1 and Theorem 2. The connection to the CCCP and the subsequent acceleration method are logically presented and mathematically grounded.
* **Identifiability Analysis:** The discussion of parameter identifiability and the proposed merging post-processing step address a crucial aspect of LFM learning and offer a practical solution to non-identifiable scenarios.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments showcase the effectiveness of the proposed FAB/LFM algorithm in both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating competitive performance against existing methods like IBP and VB. The ablation study with the shrinkage acceleration is particularly convincing.
* **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The notation is clearly defined, and the mathematical derivations are presented in a relatively accessible manner.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty in Application:** While the theoretical extension is significant, the application to LFMs themselves feels somewhat standard. The choice of datasets (e.g., block data) is also conventional, and the benefits over existing LFM methods beyond computational efficiency aren’t always dramatically highlighted.
* **Computational Complexity Discussion:** While shrinkage acceleration is presented, a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of FAB/LFM compared to other methods (IBP, VB, MEIBP) would strengthen the paper. For example, a table summarizing the time complexity of each step would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper states that FAB has no hand-tunable hyperparameters, which is a strength. However, the experiments mention setting parameters for the comparison methods. A brief discussion of the sensitivity of *those* hyperparameters and how they were chosen would be helpful for readers wishing to reproduce or adapt the experiments.
* **Merge Post-Processing Detail:** The merge post-processing step feels somewhat ad-hoc. While theoretically justified, a more detailed explanation of the criteria for merging (e.g., the threshold on the Euclidean distance) and its impact on performance would be appreciated.
* **Missing Related Work Discussion:** While the related work section touches upon relevant areas, the connection to recent advances in scalable Bayesian inference, beyond those explicitly mentioned, could be strengthened. For example, mentioning stochastic variational inference (SVI) methods might be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel theoretical extension of FAB inference to a broader class of latent variable models.
* The technical analysis is rigorous and the derivations are well-supported.
* The empirical results demonstrate competitive performance and highlight the computational advantages of the proposed method.
* The inclusion of identifiability analysis and a merging post-processing step adds significant value.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The application to LFMs, while solid, lacks significant novelty in terms of the problem itself.
* The computational complexity analysis could be more detailed.
* The merging post-processing step could benefit from more thorough explanation and justification.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions. The extension of FAB inference to LFMs is a valuable addition to the field, and the accompanying analysis and experimental validation are convincing. While there are a few areas for improvement, particularly regarding computational complexity analysis and the merging post-processing step, the overall quality of the work is high. I believe the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses, and I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. 

**Recommendations for Revision:**

1. **Elaborate on computational complexity:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the time complexity of FAB/LFM and compare it to other methods.
2. **Expand on merge post-processing:** Provide a more detailed explanation of the merging criteria and its impact on performance, potentially including an ablation study.
3. **Address hyperparameter sensitivity:** Briefly discuss the sensitivity of hyperparameters used in the comparison methods.
4. **Strengthen related work:** Include a discussion of relevant recent advances in scalable Bayesian inference (e.g., SVI).
