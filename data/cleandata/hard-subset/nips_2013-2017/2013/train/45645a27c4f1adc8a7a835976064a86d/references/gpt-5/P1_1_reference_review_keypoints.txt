- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models (binary Z, linear-Gaussian) and derives an asymptotic Lemma showing log-det Hessian yields a sum_k log sum_n z_nk term despite non-block-diagonal Hessian.
- Theorem 2: produces an FIC for LFMs with the same functional form as mixtures, giving an automatic feature-selection penalty O(∑_k log ∑_n z_nk).
- Practical algorithm: optimize a lower bound with mean-field q(Z), linearized concave penalty, and standard M-steps; E-step includes an FAB shrinkage term D/(2Nπ̃_k) that prunes features without priors.
- E-step viewed as a convex-concave procedure (CCCP); propose accelerateShrinkage that runs multiple CCCP steps per iteration to speed up pruning.
- Identifiability analysis: FAB helps resolve duplicate-feature non-identifiability and motivates a merge post-processing; a merge heuristic is proposed.
- Empirical results: on synthetic, block images, and real datasets FAB shows stronger model selection and predictive performance than IBP, VB, MEIBP, with competitive or lower runtime.
- Situates work relative to FAB for mixtures/HMMs, IBP and variational IBP literature, MEIBP, CCCP, and singular learning theory; distinguishes from MAD-Bayes asymptotics.
- Strength: novel, rigorous asymptotic analysis showing the preservation of the log-activation penalty in LFMs despite shared parameters.
- Strength: CCCP interpretation yields a practical acceleration (accelerateShrinkage) and a monotone, bound-increasing algorithm with justified pruning/merging.
- Strength: identifiability result and simple merge heuristic are useful practical contributions.
- Significance: offers deterministic, hyperparameter-free model selection for LFMs and principled regularization leading to compact models.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with clear algorithms and update equations; proofs are in the supplement.
- Weakness: core assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 are relegated to the supplement and not stated in the main text; scope and regularity conditions need exposition.
- Weakness: unclear monotonicity guarantees when accelerateShrinkage updates π and π̃ inside multiple CCCP steps—need to state whether each step provably increases the bound.
- Weakness: mean-field q(Z) restriction may weaken the FIC approximation; empirical impact (vs structured q) is not evaluated.
- Weakness: merge operation as written seems inconsistent with preserving expected reconstruction; exact merge rule and bound-change calculation are unclear.
- Experimental concern: possible unfairness—competitors fix noise variance while FAB may learn λ; need consistent treatment or reporting both settings.
- Experimental concern: initialization (K = min(N,D)) may disadvantage baselines; sensitivity to initial K for FAB and MEIBP should be tested.
- Experimental concern: unclear which VB variant was used (finite-K vs IBP variational); justify choice and comparisons.
- Experimental concern: missing complexity analysis — provide per-iteration complexity, wall-clock scaling with N,D,K, and an ablation of Tshrink to quantify speed vs accuracy.
- Scope limit: asymptotic approximations may falter in small-N regimes (noted poorer performance on Libras); characterize regimes (N/D, SNR) where FAB is preferable.
- Suggestions: move core assumptions and an intuition/proof sketch for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 into main text, clarify merge rule with bound-change calc, add complexity and sensitivity studies, and consider small-N and non-Gaussian experiments; release code.
- Recommendation: overall technically solid and original; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending clarifications on assumptions, merging, monotonicity, experimental fairness, and complexity.
