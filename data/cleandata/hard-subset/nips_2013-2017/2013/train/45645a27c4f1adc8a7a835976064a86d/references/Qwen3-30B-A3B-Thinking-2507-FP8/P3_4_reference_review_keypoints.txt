- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), filling an important applicability gap.  
- Key theoretical result (Lemma 1): log det |F(d)| for LFMs has form ∑_k log(∑_n z_nk)/N + O_p(1), enabling an FIC like for mixture models.  
- Derives an FAB/LFM algorithm that automatically selects feature complexity without hyperparameters.  
- Provides CCCP interpretation of optimization and a shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) that cuts computation while preserving performance.  
- Strong identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) justifies a merge post-processing step to resolve non-identifiability in LFMs.  
- Empirical evaluation across artificial, synthetic, and real datasets supports theoretical claims and demonstrates computational advantages.  
- Strength: theoretical novelty showing wider applicability of FIC beyond previous FAB assumptions.  
- Strength: practical acceleration via shrinkage mechanism with empirical validation.  
- Strength: automatic model selection without hyperparameters is demonstrated.  
- Weakness: Lemma 1 is said to hold under “mild assumptions” but the paper insufficiently spells out or justifies these assumptions.  
- Weakness: Comparison with MEIBP is methodologically questionable because MEIBP assumes non-negative data and may have been applied to negative-valued datasets (e.g., EEG) without clarification.  
- Weakness: Omitted comparisons to recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., submodular IBP approaches and other scalable Bayesian LFM methods).  
- Weakness: The paper does not fully explain why the O_p(1) terms in Theorem 2 do not affect asymptotic model-selection behavior.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and practical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify assumptions and experimental comparisons.
