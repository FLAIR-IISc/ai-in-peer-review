- Paper extends FAB inference from mixture/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs) with linear-Gaussian emissions and Bernoulli features.  
- Main technical result (Lemma 1): log-determinant of per-dimension Hessian asymptotically decomposes into sum of log ∑n znk terms despite non–block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Theorem 2: derives an FIC for LFMs matching the MM form — data-fit minus DK/2 log N plus (D/2)∑k log ∑n znk — yielding principled, prior-free shrinkage.  
- Algorithm: mean-field variational E-step, closed-form M-step for W,b,Λ (as in PPCA), and a shrinkage step that removes features with average activation 0 or 1.  
- CCCP interpretation: E-step seen as CCCP iteration; accelerateShrinkage runs multiple CCCP steps per FAB E-step to speed convergence.  
- Theorem 4: partial identifiability result suggesting FAB favors small-K ML solutions and motivates a merge post-processing for near-duplicate features.  
- Empirics: across synthetic, block-pattern, and real datasets, FAB often selects more compact models with competitive or better predictive log-likelihood and reduced compute; acceleration further cuts runtime.  
- Strength: surprising and useful conceptual contribution showing FAB/FIC form carries to LFMs without block-diagonal Hessians.  
- Strength: clear practical algorithm and CCCP-based acceleration that empirically reduces computational cost.  
- Strength: theoretical support — asymptotic equivalence of FIC to marginal log-likelihood (Op(1) gap) and identifiability insight.  
- Strength: empirical evidence of compact models, good predictive performance, and runtime gains.  
- Weakness: Lemma 1 is deferred to supplement with only “mild assumptions”; main text should state substantive conditions and uniformity of the Op(1) remainder over K,D,parameters.  
- Weakness: fairness of comparisons — baselines fix noise variance while FAB learns Λ, likely advantaging FAB; suggest matching noise treatment.  
- Weakness: shrinkage/merge practicalities unclear — exact pruning thresholds (∑n µnk/N ≈ 0 or 1), sensitivity to thresholds and Tshrink not reported.  
- Weakness: identifiability theorem is limited (only when wk = wl); lacks broader characterization (e.g., near-collinearity) or finite-N robustness bounds.  
- Weakness: computational complexity not quantified (per-iteration time/memory in N,D,K), and tradeoff due to Tshrink inner loop is unexplored.  
- Clarity: need to clarify which priors are asymptotically ignored (priors on P but not Z), and make monotonicity guarantees for accelerateShrinkage/merge explicit.  
- Clarity: explain how CCCP steps interact with π and π̃ updates and relate equations (11) and (14) inline; fix minor typos/grammar.  
- Originality: novel non-trivial asymptotic extension of FAB to LFMs; CCCP interpretation and acceleration are modest but useful; identifiability/merge heuristic pragmatic.  
- Significance: automatic K selection for widely used LFMs is valuable; FIC regularizer is a theoretically grounded alternative to IBP/ARD if assumptions hold.  
- Significance caveat: empirical claims would be strengthened by fairer baselines (fixed/learned noise) and additional competitors (e.g., sBIC/WBIC, scalable variational IBP).  
- Questions for authors: state precise Lemma 1 assumptions and discuss failure modes; is Op(1) uniform in K,D?  
- Question: sensitivity to mean-field approximation on Z — would structured/correlated posteriors change FIC/N materially?  
- Question: provide results with FAB using fixed noise variance or enable noise learning in baselines to ensure fair comparison.  
- Question: report numerical thresholds used for pruning and merging, ablation on Tshrink and merge frequency.  
- Question: provide per-iteration complexity and measured wall-time vs K evolution to illustrate speedups.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid asymptotic theory, practical monotone lower-bound algorithm with acceleration, good empirical compact models and prediction, clear CCCP/identifiability connections.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing main-text assumptions/proofs, fairness issues with noise variance in experiments, limited sensitivity analysis for thresholds/Tshrink, narrow identifiability claim.  
- Recommendation: leaning toward acceptance contingent on clarifying Lemma 1/Theorem 2 assumptions, fixing experimental fairness on noise variance, and adding practical details (thresholds, complexity).
