- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixtures/HMMs to linear-Gaussian latent feature models (LFMs) with Bernoulli features, deriving an FIC with an automatic complexity penalty.  
- Lemma 1 (central theoretical claim): per-dimension log-determinant of the Fisher/Hessian scales as ∑_k log(∑_n z_nk) up to Op(1), yielding an MM-like FIC for LFMs (Theorem 2).  
- Practical algorithm: mean-field q(Z) lower bound, linearized convex term, EM-like updates with shrinkage −D/(2Nπ̃_k), closed-form M-step, feature pruning/merge, and CCCP-motivated inner updates to accelerate shrinkage.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on FAB for MMs/HMMs, connects to singular learning/MAD-Bayes, contrasts marginalization-derived regularizer vs ARD/IBP priors, and relates to IBP inference and PCA/FA updates.  
- Strength: nontrivial theoretical result broadening FAB applicability despite non-block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Strength: interpretable shrinkage term and practical heuristics (pruning, merging) that enable automatic K selection.  
- Strength: CCCP perspective gives principled acceleration with empirical benefits; experiments show improved K recovery and predictive performance vs strong baselines.  
- Theoretical weakness: Lemma 1’s “mild assumptions” are deferred; these should be stated explicitly in the main text (conditions on Λ, moments, activation rates, rank).  
- Theoretical weakness: reliance on Op(1) accuracy — authors must clarify scaling so Op(1)/N vanishing and whether K may grow with N.  
- Identifiability weakness: Theorem 4 is weak (only matches average activations when columns equal); needs characterization of stability, local curvature, and guarantees for merge operations.  
- Variational approximation concern: using mean-field q(Z) breaks exact variational representation—tightness and whether FIC consistency still holds under mean-field must be discussed.  
- Algorithmic discrepancy: factor-of-2 mismatch between Eqs.(11) and (14) in the shrinkage term (−D/(2Nπ̃_k) vs −D/(∑_n μ_{nk})); needs correction/explanation.  
- Monotonicity concern: inner CCCP updates, π/π̃ changes, and pruning may break ascent guarantees—provide proof/conditions for monotonic increase of the bound.  
- Complexity concern: provide per-iteration time/memory complexity (N, D, K), wall-clock scaling, and empirical justification that acceleration reduces total time across regimes (guidance on Tshrink).  
- Pruning/bias update question: clarify whether replacing a fully active feature with bias update preserves the bound and affects regularization given Λ structure.  
- Empirical fairness: baselines (IBP/VB/MEIBP) do not learn noise variance while FAB does—either allow baselines to learn Λ or fix Λ for FAB and analyze sensitivity.  
- Empirical robustness: report sensitivity to IBP α and MEIBP initialization, include VB where feasible, provide variance across seeds, and ablation studies (with/without acceleration, merge, etc.).  
- Presentation issues: clarify constants/signs in derivations (e.g., −2D+K log N), summarize key assumptions and proof sketches in the main text, and fix typos/notation.  
- Pros summary: novel theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs, practical algorithm for automatic selection with acceleration, and strong empirical signals.  
- Cons summary: opaque critical assumptions, probable factor-of-2 error in CCCP update, unclear monotonicity/complexity guarantees, and fairness/quantitative issues in experiments.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on clarifying Lemma 1/Theorem 2 assumptions/proofs, fixing the CCCP discrepancy, and strengthening empirical evaluation and fairness.
