Summary
The paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs). The core theoretical contribution is Lemma 1/Theorem 2, arguing that, despite LFMs not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition used in previous FAB derivations, the asymptotic model complexity term retains the familiar ∑k log(∑n znk) form. This yields an FIC for LFMs identical in structure to that for MMs, enabling automated feature selection via an FAB algorithm. The authors further: (i) interpret the E-step updates as a CCCP procedure and propose a “shrinkage acceleration” heuristic; (ii) provide an identifiability analysis and a merge post-processing step. Experiments compare FAB/LFM (with and without acceleration) to variational EM, several IBP-based methods (Gibbs, VB, MEIBP), and report speed, model selection accuracy, and predictive log-likelihood on synthetic and real datasets.

Strengths
- Ambitious theoretical extension: Establishing that FIC carries over to LFMs without the MM block-Hessian assumptions would be a substantial contribution if the technical conditions indeed hold broadly.
- Practical algorithmic implications: The CCCP view is insightful and the shrinkage acceleration is simple and effective in experiments.
- Automation and hyperparameter-free emphasis is attractive; the method selects K without elaborate priors or tuning.
- Empirical results show promising predictive performance and compact models; the shrinkage seems to mitigate overfitting relative to some baselines.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Heavy reliance on asymptotics with key technical assumptions deferred to the supplement and described as “mild.” The main text provides no concrete conditions for Lemma 1. Given that this lemma is pivotal (log det Fisher ≈ ∑k log ∑n znk), I am uneasy endorsing the generality without a clearer statement and discussion of when it fails (e.g., collinearity in W, near-singular Λ, feature usage degeneracies).
- Mean-field approximation on Z is introduced for tractability, but its effect on the FIC lower bound tightness and on the asymptotic claims is not quantified. The claim of “monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound” does not, by itself, ensure closeness to the true marginal evidence.
- Identifiability claims are overstated. Theorem 4 shows a balancing property when w·k = w·l, but this is far from full identifiability. LFMs remain non-identifiable up to permutations, and near-duplicates can persist; the merge heuristic is ad hoc and depends on thresholds. Please temper the language and clarify the precise identifiability notion proved.
- Experimental fairness and scope:
  - Noise variance: FAB/EM estimate Λ while several baselines either do not or are fixed to certain values (1.0/0.75). This can materially affect both PLL and model selection and may favor FAB. A fairer comparison would fix Λ across methods or equip all with learned noise models.
  - Hyperparameters: IBP α fixed to 3 may bias K upward; MEIBP requires nonnegativity and is omitted for negative data. Several runs are stopped at 50 hours and some initial K values are hand-set for MEIBP. Overall, the experimental protocol appears to advantage FAB; sensitivity analyses to α, initial K, and time caps are needed.
  - Small-N behavior: The paper acknowledges FIC may be inaccurate at small N (Libras). More systematic characterization of the N at which FAB becomes reliable would help.
  - Evaluation breadth: No comparison to singular BIC/WAIC-style criteria or cross-validation for model selection; no ablations isolating the effect of shrinkage term D/(2Nπ̃k) vs prior η(πk).
- Algorithmic details: Feature removal conditions use exact ∑n µnk/N ∈ {0,1}; with mean-field updates, exact 0/1 is rare. In practice, do you use thresholds? Early elimination risks local minima; what safeguards exist? Similarly, the merge step relies on W distances; how are thresholds chosen and how often does it change L?
- The CCCP acceleration uses Tshrink = 100 without justification; no convergence analysis is provided for the alternating CCCP/FAB updates.

Quality
Technically promising but the core theorem relies on unstated conditions in the main text. The empirical section is substantial but has fairness issues and limited sensitivity analyses. The identifiability result is narrow; claims should be made precise.

Clarity
Generally well-written and organized, with helpful connections to prior FAB work and CCCP. However, the main text needs to clearly state the assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2, and to more carefully discuss the scope/limitations of the identifiability claim and the merge heuristic. A complexity analysis would also aid clarity.

Originality
Extending FAB to LFMs and deriving the same FIC form is novel as far as I know. The CCCP interpretation and shrinkage acceleration are incremental but useful.

Significance
If the theoretical result is correct under practically relevant conditions, this is impactful: it broadens FAB beyond MMs/HMMs and offers an efficient alternative to IBP/VB for feature selection. However, the practical significance is clouded by the asymptotic nature of the justification and the fairness concerns in experiments.

Questions and suggestions
- State the precise assumptions for Lemma 1 (regularity, boundedness, conditions on Z usage patterns, Λ, W) in the main paper. Provide a discussion of failure modes and diagnostic checks.
- Provide a controlled experiment where all methods share the same noise model (either fixed Λ or all learning Λ) to isolate the effect of FAB vs IBP/VB.
- Add sensitivity analyses: α in IBP, Tshrink, initialization, and pruning/merge thresholds. Report variance over multiple seeds.
- Evaluate small-N regimes more systematically; show how FAB’s K selection and PLL evolve with N, compared to IBP/VB/MEIBP.
- Compare to sBIC/WAIC or cross-validated likelihood for model selection, at least on smaller datasets.
- Clarify the feature removal and merge criteria (thresholds, stopping) and demonstrate that these steps improve the bound L consistently without hurting PLL.
- Temper “parameter identifiability” claims; reframe as “regularization resolves a specific degeneracy” and quantify empirically.

Pros
- Novel extension of FAB to LFMs with a clean FIC form.
- Insightful CCCP connection; practical acceleration works.
- Automated K selection; promising PLL and compact models.

Cons
- Key theoretical assumption not clearly stated; asymptotic nature largely unchecked.
- Experimental fairness issues (noise variance, hyperparameters, time caps).
- Overstated identifiability claim; heuristic merge/prune steps insufficiently justified.
- Limited analysis of sensitivity and small-N regimes.

Recommendation: Borderline reject. The direction is interesting and potentially impactful, but I need clearer theoretical conditions in the main paper and a fairer, more thorough experimental evaluation before endorsing publication.
