- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing a challenging model-selection problem.
- Key technical hurdle — lack of block-diagonal Hessian in LFMs — is overcome by showing the FIC effectively behaves as if the condition holds.
- Theoretical contributions are strong and supported by formal results (Lemma 1 and Theorem 2) showing asymptotic marginal likelihood convergence.
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method (linked to CCCP) that improves computational efficiency.
- Provides identifiability analysis and a feature-merging post-processing step to refine learned representations.
- FAB/LFM offers automated model selection without explicit priors or hyperparameter tuning.
- Experimental results show competitive accuracy and faster runtime compared to several BNP, VB, and MAP approaches.
- Paper is well written and clearly organized, with accessible mathematical notation and explanations.
- Weakness: ablation studies are limited — need quantitative analysis of shrinkage acceleration speedups across scenarios.
- Weakness: comparisons to state-of-the-art, highly optimized IBP implementations are incomplete and under-detailed.
- Weakness: experiments could include deeper analyses on a smaller set of relevant real-world datasets.
- Weakness: relies on Hessian-related assumptions (in supplement) that need clearer discussion of practical implications and limitations.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Final recommendation: Accept — solid theoretical and practical contribution with minor experimental and discussion improvements suggested.
