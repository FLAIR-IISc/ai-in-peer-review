

# Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), claiming it achieves automatic model selection without hyperparameters. The authors argue that FAB/LFMs maintain the desirable properties of FAB/MMs, including convergence to marginal log-likelihood, automatic feature selection, and monotonic optimization. The key contribution is Lemma 1, which shows LFMs share the same FIC form as mixture models despite not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition.

**Strengths:** The paper presents a theoretically interesting extension of FAB to LFMs. The connection to CCCP for acceleration (Algorithm 2) is novel and well-motivated. The empirical results demonstrate computational advantages over competing methods, particularly for large datasets, and show good model selection performance.

**Weaknesses:** The paper contains multiple technical inconsistencies that undermine its theoretical claims:

1. **Notational and Typographical Errors:** In equation (6), the claim log det |F(d)| = ∑k log ∑n znk/N + Op(1) is mathematically problematic. The left side is a logarithm of a determinant (a sum of log eigenvalues), while the right side is a sum of logarithms. The paper fails to clarify how these can be asymptotically equivalent.

2. **Inconsistent Notation:** The paper uses inconsistent notation for the log term (e.g., "log ∑n znk" vs "log(∑n znk)") and for model complexity terms (e.g., "−2D +K logN" in equation 10, which should likely be −(2D + K)/2 logN).

3. **Incomplete Theoretical Justification:** Lemma 1 states a key claim without providing the derivation. The paper claims "the log ∑n znk term naturally appears in log det |F(d)| without A1 and A2" but offers no mathematical justification for this surprising result.

4. **Misleading Claims:** The paper states FAB "does not have a hand-tunable hyper-parameter" but π̃k is a linearization parameter that must be set (though it's not a hyperparameter in the traditional sense). The paper also claims "FAB/LFMs have parameter identifiability" but Theorem 4 only provides a sufficient condition (w*·k = w*·l implies ∑n μ*·nk/N = ∑n μ*·nl/N), not the necessary condition for identifiability.

5. **Insufficient Experimental Justification:** The paper claims "the latter shrinking effect [from η(πk)] is too weak to mitigate over-fitting" but provides no evidence for this claim beyond a vague reference to Section 4. The comparison with MEIBP is limited to a few datasets without statistical significance testing.

6. **Inconsistent Terminology:** The paper alternates between "FAB/LFMs" and "FAB/LFM" without explanation, creating confusion about whether the framework is singular or plural.

**Minor Issues:** The paper contains several minor but distracting errors: "log ∑n znk" vs "log(∑n znk)" inconsistencies, the ambiguous reference to "Op(1)" in Theorem 2, and the unexplained reference to "Figure 1" in the abstract (which is not actually referenced in the main text).

**Overall Assessment:** While the paper presents an interesting extension of FAB to LFMs with promising empirical results, the theoretical foundation is undermined by multiple notational inconsistencies, incomplete mathematical justifications, and misleading claims. The paper would benefit from a more careful mathematical derivation of Lemma 1, consistent notation throughout, and clearer statements about the role of π̃k. These issues prevent the paper from meeting NIPS's standards for technical rigor.

**Recommendation:** Reject. The paper has a promising direction but contains too many technical flaws that compromise its theoretical contribution. The authors should address these fundamental issues before resubmission. The minor flaws identified are substantial enough to warrant rejection, as they undermine the paper's core theoretical claims about the FIC for LFMs.
