- Paper extends FAB inference from mixtures/HMMs to linear-Gaussian latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features, deriving an FIC via a new Hessian determinant result (Lemma 1).
- Lemma 1 shows the per-dimension Hessian determinant induces the same ∑k log ∑n z_nk penalty as in FAB for mixtures, despite LFMs lacking block-diagonal Hessian structure.
- Theorem 2 gives an FIC for LFMs matching the mixture-model FIC; Corollary 3 implies asymptotic closeness to the marginal log-likelihood.
- Algorithmically, a linearization plus mean-field q(Z) yields an EM-like FAB procedure with a shrinkage term -D/(2Nπ̃_k) in the E-step that automatically prunes features.
- The updates are interpreted as CCCP, and an accelerateShrinkage variant (multiple CCCP steps) is proposed to speed up shrinkage.
- Identifiability analysis shows the regularizer favors balanced activations; a merge post-processing increases the bound for duplicate features.
- Experiments on synthetic, block, and real datasets show competitive or superior model selection and predictive performance with lower or comparable compute in many cases.
- Core theoretical novelty (Lemma 1) is important but its assumptions and proofs are deferred to the supplement and need clearer statement and intuition in the main text.
- The transition to the practical lower bound and monotonicity relies on standard linearization/mean-field and CCCP; authors should state/verify monotonicity when using accelerateShrinkage.
- E-step, M-step, and role of η(πk) and the FAB shrinkage term are well explained; merge heuristic is reasonable though guarantees are limited to exact duplicates.
- Experimental concerns: baselines fix noise variance while FAB learns it, VB omitted on many real datasets, MEIBP tuning/initialization sensitivity not fully detailed.
- Suggested additional experiments: baseline sensitivity or learning λ, include VB on small/medium datasets, per-iteration time and convergence plots, and a network/link-prediction task.
- Clarity is generally good but the main text should include explicit assumptions and a derivation sketch for how the determinant yields ∑k log ∑n z_nk.
- Originality: novel theoretical extension of FAB beyond block-diagonal Hessian models; CCCP acceleration and merge post-processing are useful methodological contributions.
- Significance: automated model selection for LFMs without tuning priors is valuable and practical if Lemma 1’s assumptions hold broadly.
- Pros: elegant theoretical extension, same clean FIC form, interpretable shrinkage, CCCP-based acceleration, identifiability insight, and good empirical results.
- Cons: deferred critical assumptions/proofs, experimental fairness and sensitivity issues, limited evaluation breadth, and merge step lacks guarantees beyond exact duplicates.
- Recommendations to authors: state Lemma 1 assumptions and intuition, verify monotonicity with accelerateShrinkage, report timing/convergence and ablations, test sensitivity to η(πk) and D/N scaling, and align baseline settings.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — theoretically elegant and practically promising but needs clearer assumptions and stronger empirical rigor.
