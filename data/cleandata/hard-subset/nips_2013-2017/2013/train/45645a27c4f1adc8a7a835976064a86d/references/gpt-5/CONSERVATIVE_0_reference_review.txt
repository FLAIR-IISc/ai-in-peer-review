Summary
The paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs). The main theoretical claim is that, despite the lack of block-diagonal Hessian structure in LFMs, an FIC-style asymptotic approximation still yields the same regularization term as in mixtures: a sum over features of log(sum_n z_nk). This is formalized via Lemma 1 (asymptotics of log det of the Hessian w.r.t. per-dimension parameters), leading to Theorem 2 and an FIC for LFMs. Building on this, the authors propose a practical FAB algorithm using a mean-field approximation and a CCCP-based “shrinkage acceleration” to remove irrelevant features early. They also analyze identifiability under FAB and propose a merge post-processing step. Experiments on synthetic and real datasets compare FAB to IBP-based methods, variational EM, and MEIBP, showing improved predictive log-likelihoods and more compact K, with competitive runtime (MEIBP sometimes faster).

Strengths
- Ambitious theoretical extension: If correct, Lemma 1/Theorem 2 are interesting, showing that FAB-style penalties emerge for LFMs even without the block-diagonal Hessian assumption used in prior FAB work.
- Practical algorithmic contributions: The CCCP interpretation and the associated shrinkage acceleration are sensible and appear to help wall-clock time. The monotone lower-bound optimization is attractive in principle.
- Empirical results: FAB tends to select smaller K with good predictive likelihoods, and the shrinkage mechanism works well in the block-data example. The identifiability discussion and merging heuristic address a known issue in LFMs.
- Connections to related lines (IBP, VB for IBP, MEIBP, MAD-Bayes, singular learning theory) are mentioned.

Weaknesses
- Theoretical transparency: The key result (Lemma 1) is deferred to the supplement and stated under “mild assumptions” that are not made explicit in the main text. The Op(1) terms and the conditions under which log det terms of Fisher matrices can be ignored are crucial to the validity of the FIC, yet are largely hand-waved. Given that the Hessian is not block-diagonal in LFMs, the result is surprising and needs at least a sketch and concrete assumptions in the paper.
- Guarantees with acceleration: The algorithm’s selling point is monotonic increase of the FIC lower bound. However, the accelerateShrinkage() routine performs multiple CCCP steps and updates π and π̃ jointly. It is unclear whether monotonicity of the same lower bound is still guaranteed under this schedule. The paper suggests empirical gains but no proof or safeguard.
- Shrinkage and pruning: The paper proposes removing features when ∑n µ_nk/N equals 0 or 1. Under mean-field with logistic updates, exact 0/1 is rare; if thresholds are used, they are not stated, and the impact on the bound is not analyzed. Claims that L increases upon removal assume Eq[log p(X|Z,P)] is unchanged; with fractional µ, this is delicate.
- Identifiability/merge: Theorem 4 assumes stationary points with 0<∑n µ_nk/N<1 and boundedness conditions. The proposed merge operation sets w’=2w and µ’=µ1+µ2. This can push µ’ outside [0,1], and while µ’s are variational means, the generative model still uses Bernoulli Z. The consistency of the merged model (and its effect on the lower bound) needs a clearer derivation, not a heuristic plus “empirically found”.
- Experimental fairness and breadth:
  - Noise variance: FAB/EM learn λ; competing codes “did not learn” variance, so the authors fix it (1 on synthetic, 0.75 on real). This likely advantages FAB/EM. A fairer comparison would either learn noise for all methods (e.g., using variants that do) or fix λ for FAB/EM to match baselines.
  - Hyperparameters and tuning: IBP’s α is fixed at 3 (acknowledged to overestimate K); MEIBP is reported sensitive and sometimes initialized with K=100. This suggests limited tuning for baselines.
  - Omitted baselines: VB is omitted due to runtime; MEIBP omitted on negative data; link-prediction tasks (where IBP shines) are not included. For claims of “superiority,” broader and better-controlled comparisons are needed.
  - Scale and complexity: No clear per-iteration complexity analysis, memory footprint, or scaling study versus N, D, K is provided. Figures show qualitative time reductions, but quantitative complexity would help.
- Clarity: Many essential details are moved to the supplement (proofs, PPCA-form M-step, merge algorithm). The main text would benefit from at least one worked derivation (e.g., a sketch for Lemma 1), explicit thresholds for pruning/merging, and a clear statement of assumptions.

Questions for the authors
1. State explicitly the assumptions needed for Lemma 1 in the main text. Under what conditions on Z, W, Λ, and π do the results hold? How sensitive is the approximation to violations?
2. Does accelerateShrinkage() preserve monotonic increases of the same lower bound L? If not, can you provide a safeguarded variant or empirical evidence of no degradation?
3. How do you prune in practice (exact 0/1 or thresholds)? Prove that the pruning step does not decrease L under the practical rule you use.
4. In the merge operation, how do you ensure µ’ remains in [0,1]? Provide a derivation that L increases under the merge, or specify conditions under which it does.
5. Can you equalize the noise variance setting across methods (e.g., fixing λ for FAB/EM) and provide sensitivity to misspecification?
6. Please compare against at least one IBP/VB or MAP variant that learns noise, and include a link-prediction or binary matrix completion benchmark.

Minor comments
- Provide per-iteration computational complexity for E/M steps and for accelerateShrinkage versus standard updates.
- Clarify whether the FIC result extends beyond Gaussian LFMs (non-diagonal Λ? non-Gaussian likelihoods?).
- Some typos and formatting issues (e.g., missing or misplaced parentheses, superscripts) should be cleaned.

Overall assessment
- Quality: Promising, but the core theoretical contribution is under-specified in the main text, and some algorithmic steps are heuristic without guarantees. Experimental comparisons raise fairness concerns.
- Clarity: Adequate but relies too heavily on the supplement. Key assumptions and procedures should be explicit.
- Originality: Moderate. The extension of FAB to LFMs and the CCCP view are novel within this line, but closely related to prior FAB and CCCP literature.
- Significance: Potentially useful if the asymptotics are correct and the method is robust, but current evidence is not fully convincing.

Recommendation: Reject (borderline). With clearer, in-paper theoretical assumptions/proofs, stronger and fairer empirical validation (especially controlling for noise variance and including more appropriate baselines), and formal guarantees for the acceleration/pruning/merging steps, I would be inclined to reconsider.

Confidence: 4/5
