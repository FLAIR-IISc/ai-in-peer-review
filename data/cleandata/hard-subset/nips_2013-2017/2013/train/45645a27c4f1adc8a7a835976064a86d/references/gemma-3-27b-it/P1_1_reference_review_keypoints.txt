- Paper extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework from mixture models to Latent Feature Models (LFMs).  
- Shows via asymptotic analysis that LFMs share the same FIC structure as MMs despite non-block-diagonal Hessians.  
- Introduces a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration to make FAB for LFMs computationally efficient.  
- Proves parameter identifiability for the proposed LFM formulation and motivates a feature merging post-processing step.  
- Provides theoretical lemmas and theorems approximating the marginal log-likelihood for LFMs.  
- Empirically compares FAB/LFM to IBP, VB, and MEIBP on synthetic, block, and real-world datasets, showing competitive model selection and prediction.  
- Demonstrates reduced computational cost thanks to the proposed shrinkage acceleration.  
- Paper is generally well-written, logically organized, and uses clear notation.  
- Builds on prior FAB work (Fujimaki et al.) and situates itself relative to BNP/IBP, variational, and MAP approaches.  
- Differentiates from MAD-Bayes by asymptotic regime and regularization approach.  
- Mathematical derivations are complex and require substantial background to fully follow.  
- Key assumptions (e.g., “mild assumptions” in Lemma 1) are left to supplementary material rather than summarized in the main text.  
- Experimental comparisons may be sensitive to baseline parameter settings (e.g., IBP’s α) and lack sufficient detail on tuning.  
- The limitation that MEIBP cannot handle negative values is mentioned but not discussed in terms of fairness in comparisons.  
- The merge post-processing is described briefly and lacks justification for why it helps or how often it’s applied.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical extension, practical acceleration, strong experiments, automated model selection, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility limited by mathematical complexity, insufficient discussion of assumptions, and minor experimental detail gaps.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, asking authors to add intuitive explanations, summarize Lemma 1 assumptions, detail baseline parameter tuning, and expand on merge post-processing.
