- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models with Bernoulli activations and linear-Gaussian likelihood, deriving an FIC for LFMs.  
- Lemma 1: the per-dimension Hessian log-determinant asymptotically equals (D/2)∑k log ∑n znk — the central theoretical claim.  
- Theorem 2: the resulting FIC for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models despite violating prior block-diagonal Hessian assumptions.  
- Presents an EM-like optimization: mean-field E-step with sigmoid update plus automatic shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃k), closed-form M-step (PPCA-like), and a shrinkage pruning step.  
- Reinterprets the E-step via CCCP and introduces accelerateShrinkage (multiple CCCP updates per E-step) to remove irrelevant features earlier.  
- Proves an identifiability property: identical columns of W yield equal expected usages at stationary points, motivating a merge post-processing.  
- Empirically, FAB selects more compact models, attains competitive or better predictive log-likelihoods, and is much faster than IBP/VB while slightly slower but more accurate than MEIBP.  
- Theory concerns: Lemma 1 is the lynchpin but its assumptions and proofs are deferred to the supplement; main text should state key conditions.  
- Monotonicity concern: overall monotonicity holds for alternating maximization, but accelerateShrinkage’s inner steps lack an explicit non-decreasing L guarantee.  
- Algorithm design: E-step shrinkage is well-motivated and prior-agnostic; the merge heuristic is theoretically justified and tested against the objective.  
- Experimental fairness caveat: FAB learns noise variance while some baselines fix it—an ablation where FAB also fixes variance is needed.  
- Experimental coverage: VB is omitted on large datasets for time reasons; running VB on at least one medium-scale case would improve comparisons.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: IBP’s α is fixed to default (3); a sensitivity analysis is needed to assess bias in K.  
- Clarity improvements needed: state key assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 in the main text and clarify how K may scale with N.  
- Clarify computational aspects: provide per-iteration complexity, impact of Tshrink on wall-clock time, and whether L always increases after accelerateShrinkage.  
- Clarify merge details: specify search criterion, complexity, and ensure reproducibility (even if pseudocode is in supplement).  
- Originality: novel and nontrivial extension of FAB to models without block-diagonal Hessians and unexpected emergence of the same FIC penalty.  
- Significance: broadens FAB theory and gives a practical, scalable automated method for latent feature number selection between costly IBP and faster EM/VB.  
- Pros: elegant theoretical extension, automated prior-agnostic shrinkage, CCCP acceleration, identifiability-driven merge, strong empirical compactness and performance.  
- Cons/questions: key assumptions/proofs in supplement, monotonicity of accelerateShrinkage, fairness of baseline comparisons, limitations to diagonal Gaussian/mean-field Z and scaling behavior.  
- Suggested additions: include a brief proof sketch or assumptions in main text; ablations turning off −D/(2Nπ̃k) and varying Tshrink; time-vs-accuracy curves; sensitivity studies for IBP α and FAB initialization; compare to ARD-style baselines.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original, and practically useful work pending minor clarifications and additional ablations; recommend accept with minor revisions.
