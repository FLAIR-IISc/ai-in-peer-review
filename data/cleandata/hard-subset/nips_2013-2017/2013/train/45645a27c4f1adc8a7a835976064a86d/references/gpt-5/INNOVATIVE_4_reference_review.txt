Summary
This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs). The core theoretical result (Lemma 1) shows that the Hessian determinant of the complete-data log-likelihood for each observed dimension d contributes a penalty term of the form sum_k log(sum_n z_nk), leading to an FIC for LFMs that is algebraically identical to the FIC for mixture models despite the lack of block-diagonal structure in the Hessian. Building on this, the authors derive a practical FAB algorithm with a mean-field q(Z), yielding an E-step update with an automatic shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃_k) that prunes irrelevant features without tuning hyperparameters or priors. They relate the E-step to a convex-concave procedure (CCCP), which motivates a shrinkage acceleration routine. They also analyze identifiability, proving that FAB favors balanced solutions and motivates a merge post-processing step. Experiments on synthetic and real datasets compare FAB to IBP (Gibbs), VB, and MEIBP, showing competitive or better model selection and predictive performance with favorable runtimes, especially with the proposed acceleration.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on FAB/FIC for mixtures and HMMs, IBP-based nonparametrics for LFMs (Gibbs, VB, and submodular/MEIBP), and asymptotic approximations (BIC, MAD-Bayes). The main novelty is removing the previous FAB requirement that the complete-data Hessian be block-diagonal, yet recovering the same FIC form for LFMs via a new asymptotic argument. The CCCP view and merge heuristic are also interesting additions. The paper is well-situated relative to IBP/VB/MEIBP and Watanabe’s singular learning perspective.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: A bold and elegant extension of FAB to LFMs, with the surprising result that the FIC penalty structurally matches that of mixtures even without the earlier Hessian factorization assumptions.
- Theory: Clear large-sample rationale, with FIC converging to the marginal log-likelihood up to Op(1), asymptotic prior-independence, and automatic model selection via the log count penalty.
- Algorithmic contributions: Practical mean-field FAB for LFMs, a CCCP interpretation that motivates an effective shrinkage acceleration, and a principled merge post-processing justified by identifiability analysis.
- Practical impact: No hand-tunable hyperparameters; implicit regularization combats overfitting and selects K automatically. Empirically compelling in several settings, often yielding more compact models with strong predictive performance.
- Clarity of the key message: The derivation and resulting implications for shrinkage and identifiability are well explained.

Weaknesses and questions
- Assumptions behind Lemma 1: The main theorem relies critically on Lemma 1 and “mild assumptions,” but these are relegated to the supplement. Given the paper’s hinge on this result, a concise statement of assumptions and a proof sketch should be in the main text. For instance, what conditions on Z, π, and Λ guarantee the log det decomposition and control the Op(1) term?
- Monotonicity and acceleration: The core FAB alternation guarantees monotone increase of the lower bound L. The accelerateShrinkage routine performs multiple CCCP steps and updates π along with π̃; please clarify whether every substep preserves monotonicity of L and under what conditions. A small ablation showing L per iteration with and without acceleration would be reassuring.
- Fairness in comparisons: Competing methods sometimes fix noise variance (per released code), while FAB learns Λ; this could advantage FAB. Consider reporting results with matched noise settings or with FAB’s noise fixed similarly. Also, IBP’s α was set to 3 (may cause overestimation). Please discuss sensitivity to α and include error bars or multiple seeds for all methods.
- Scale and robustness: Results are promising, but more evidence on scalability (e.g., N and D sweeps with wall-clock vs. performance) and sensitivity (e.g., to Tshrink, initialization, and the merge threshold) would strengthen the empirical case.
- Clarity: Some derivations are terse (e.g., connection from Theorem 2 to the precise E-step update). A brief derivation sketch for Eq. (11) and an explicit statement of when prior-based shrinkage is negligible compared to FAB-induced shrinkage would help. Minor language issues and typos exist.

Quality
- Theoretical claims are interesting and mostly well supported; however, the central assumptions should be made explicit in the main text. The algorithmic steps are standard and careful, with reasonable convergence arguments. Empirical evaluation is solid but could be fairer and broader.

Clarity
- Generally well organized and readable. Please move assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 into the main paper with a concise proof sketch; clarify the monotonicity of the accelerated updates; and better document the merge routine and its complexity.

Originality
- High. Extending FAB to LFMs without the previous Hessian factorization and recovering the same FIC penalty is a fresh and insightful contribution. The CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability-based merging are also novel and useful.

Significance
- Significant. The work advances automated model selection for LFMs with strong asymptotic justification and practical algorithms. It can influence future work on FAB for broader latent variable models and may provide a competitive alternative to IBP/VB in practice.

Pros
- Bold theoretical extension with a clean and surprising result.
- Practical, hyperparameter-free algorithm with automatic shrinkage.
- Useful CCCP acceleration and identifiability insights leading to merging.
- Strong performance and compact models in experiments.

Cons
- Main assumptions/proof details for Lemma 1 left to supplement.
- Some fairness concerns in experiments (noise learning, α).
- Limited scalability/sensitivity analyses; monotonicity of acceleration not fully established in main text.

Recommendation
Accept (leaning toward accept due to novelty and potential impact), contingent on moving core assumptions/proof sketches into the main paper and clarifying experimental fairness and acceleration monotonicity.

Confidence
High in the assessment of novelty and potential impact; moderate in detailed technical conditions pending review of the supplement.
