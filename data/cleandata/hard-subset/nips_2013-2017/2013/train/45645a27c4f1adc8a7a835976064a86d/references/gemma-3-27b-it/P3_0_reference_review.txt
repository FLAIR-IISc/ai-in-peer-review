## Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a significant extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). It successfully overcomes a key limitation of previous FAB work, expanding its applicability to a broader class of models. The theoretical analysis demonstrating the applicability of the original FAB framework to LFMs is sound, and the proposed acceleration method and identifiability analysis are valuable contributions. The experimental validation is thorough, demonstrating competitive performance against established methods. While the writing could be slightly more polished in places, the paper is generally clear and makes a strong case for its contributions. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary and Related Work:**

The paper addresses the problem of model selection (determining the dimensionality of latent features) in LFMs. Existing methods, particularly non-parametric Bayesian (NPB) and variational Bayesian (VB) approaches, suffer from high computational cost, limiting their scalability. The authors build upon their previous work on FAB inference, which offers automation and computational efficiency, by extending it to the more general LFM setting. 

The paper effectively positions itself within the landscape of existing work, correctly highlighting the advantages of FAB (automation, computational efficiency) and contrasting it with NPB and VB methods. The discussion of FIC, its connection to marginal log-likelihood approximation, and the derivation for MMs are well-explained and provide necessary context. The introduction of MAD-Bayes and its differences from FAB are appropriately noted. The related work section is comprehensive and demonstrates the authors’ awareness of the field.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The core contribution – demonstrating the applicability of the FAB framework to LFMs despite the lack of block-diagonal Hessian conditions – is solid and well-justified through Lemma 1 and Theorem 2. This significantly expands the scope of FAB inference.
* **Practical Improvement: Acceleration & Identifiability:** The proposed shrinkage acceleration method, inspired by the CCCP interpretation of the FAB E-step, offers a substantial improvement in computational efficiency (demonstrated in the experiments). The analysis of parameter identifiability and the proposed merging post-processing step offer a practical way to further refine the model and address potential non-identifiability issues.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing FAB/LFMs against strong baselines (IBP, VB, MEIBP) on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The results consistently show competitive performance, particularly in terms of predictive accuracy and computational speed.  The ablation study showing the effectiveness of the shrinkage acceleration is particularly valuable.
* **Automated Model Selection:**  The inherent automated model selection capability of FAB is a significant advantage, eliminating the need for manual hyperparameter tuning.
* **Clear Connection to Existing Work:** The paper builds logically on the authors' previous work and clearly articulates the connections between FAB and related techniques.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Writing Style:** While generally clear, the writing occasionally suffers from dense phrasing and could benefit from simplification. For instance, some sentences in the introduction and the description of the algorithm could be more concise.
* **Complexity of the Algorithm:** Despite the advantages, the FAB algorithm remains complex and may require significant implementation effort. The paper could benefit from a more accessible high-level overview of the algorithm's steps.
* **Limited Discussion of Assumptions:** The paper mentions "mild assumptions" in Lemma 1 but does not fully articulate them within the main text. While the supplementary material provides details, a brief discussion of these assumptions in the main body would enhance clarity.
* **Dependence on Existing Implementations:** The comparison relies heavily on existing implementations of other methods. Any performance differences could be partially attributed to implementation details rather than inherent algorithmic differences.

**4. Detailed Comments & Questions:**

* **Lemma 1:** Could the authors provide a more intuitive explanation of why the log det |F(d)| term simplifies in the LFM case?  A visual aid might be helpful.
* **Shrinkage Acceleration:**  The impact of the `Tshrink` parameter on performance is not thoroughly investigated. Was any sensitivity analysis performed on this parameter?
* **Merge Post-Processing:** The criteria for merging latent features (Euclidean distance of W) seem somewhat arbitrary. Is there a theoretical justification for this choice?
* **Experimental Details:** It would be helpful to have more details about the datasets used (e.g., the dimensionality of the real datasets, the number of samples) in the main text or a more detailed supplementary material.
* **Computational Cost Breakdown:** A more detailed breakdown of the computational cost of each step in the FAB algorithm would be valuable.

**5. Arguments for and against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Significant extension of FAB inference to a wider class of models.
* Sound theoretical analysis and justification of the approach.
* Practical improvements through the proposed acceleration method and identifiability analysis.
* Thorough experimental validation demonstrating competitive performance.
* Automated model selection capability.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Writing style could be more polished.
* Algorithm complexity may limit its adoption.
* Dependence on existing implementations for comparison.



**6. Final Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The authors should address the suggestions outlined in the “Weaknesses” and “Detailed Comments” sections to improve the clarity and accessibility of the paper. Specifically, clarifying the assumptions in Lemma 1, providing a more intuitive explanation, and offering a sensitivity analysis for the Tshrink parameter would strengthen the paper.
