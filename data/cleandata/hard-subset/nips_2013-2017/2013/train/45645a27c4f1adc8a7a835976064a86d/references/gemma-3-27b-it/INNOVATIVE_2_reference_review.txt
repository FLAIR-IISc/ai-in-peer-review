This paper presents a significant extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), a domain where it previously lacked applicability due to Hessian matrix constraints. The authors convincingly demonstrate that, despite these constraints not being met by LFMs, the resulting Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) retains a similar form to that of Mixture Models, allowing for the application of FAB’s desirable properties – automatic model selection, asymptotic prior-independence, and computational efficiency. The inclusion of a shrinkage acceleration method based on a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) interpretation and the analysis of parameter identifiability further strengthen the contribution.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** Extending FAB inference to LFMs is a genuinely novel contribution. The theoretical analysis showing the FIC's conserved form despite the broken assumptions is impressive. This opens up a new avenue for applying FAB to a broader class of latent variable models.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the FIC for LFMs appears rigorous, with key lemmas and theorems presented. The connection to existing work on asymptotic Bayesian inference is well-established.
* **Practical Relevance:** The proposed shrinkage acceleration method directly addresses a potential computational bottleneck, making the method more practical for large-scale datasets. The identifiability analysis provides a theoretical justification for merging latent features, a potentially valuable post-processing step.
* **Strong Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real-world data demonstrate the effectiveness of FAB/LFMs, particularly in terms of model selection and predictive performance. The comparison with established methods like IBP, VB, and MEIBP is thorough and highlights the advantages of the proposed approach.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The mathematical derivations are presented clearly, and the explanations are concise and informative.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experimental evaluation is solid, it primarily focuses on datasets commonly used in the LFM literature. Expanding the evaluation to a wider range of datasets and application domains would further strengthen the paper’s claims.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** Although the authors claim robustness to parameter settings (e.g., δ), a more detailed sensitivity analysis of key hyperparameters would be beneficial.
* **Merge Post-processing Detail:** The description of the merging post-processing step lacks some detail. It would be helpful to provide a more precise criterion for determining when to merge features and to quantify the impact of this step on performance.
* **Convergence Analysis:** The stopping criterion based on the change in the lower bound (δ) is reasonable, but a more in-depth analysis of convergence properties would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant limitation of FAB inference and extends its applicability to a relevant class of models.
* The theoretical contributions are substantial and provide a solid foundation for the proposed method.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of FAB/LFMs.
* The inclusion of the acceleration method and identifiability analysis adds further value to the work.
* The paper exhibits a high level of technical rigor and clarity.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The experimental evaluation could be broadened to increase confidence in the generalizability of the results.
* The parameter sensitivity analysis is limited.
* The description of the merge post-processing step is somewhat lacking in detail.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and significant contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent variable models. While there are some minor areas for improvement, the strengths of the work far outweigh its weaknesses. The paper introduces a conceptually elegant and practically useful method that has the potential to impact research in a variety of areas. I lean strongly towards acceptance, despite the minor flaws. The willingness to overlook some incomplete validation given the boldness and novelty of the approach is justified.



**Recommendation:** Accept.
