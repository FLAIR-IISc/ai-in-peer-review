- Summary: Extends FAB inference to binary-feature linear-Gaussian LFMs, proving Lemma 1 that yields the same FIC regularizer as mixtures and deriving a practical FAB algorithm with a CCCP-based shrinkage-acceleration routine and merge post-processing.
- Theory concern: Lemma 1 → Theorem 2 is plausible and interesting but depends on “mild” assumptions relegated to the supplement; these should be stated and sketched in the main paper.
- Approximation gap: The move from equation (7) to the practical lower bound (10) mixes Laplace, linearization, and mean-field approximations; the effect on tightness and convergence needs clearer discussion.
- Algorithm: E-step updates are standard mean-field with an added FAB shrinkage term; CCCP interpretation explains acceleration, and the identifiability theorem plus merge heuristic are useful but rely on deferred conditions.
- Experiments fairness: Baselines fix noise variance while FAB learns it, potentially biasing PLL comparisons; all methods should harmonize σ treatment or use σ-insensitive metrics (e.g., RMSE).
- Experiments scope: Synthetic results show shrinkage and K recovery; real-data results often favor FAB with smaller K, but VB is omitted on large sets and runtime/scaling claims need per-iteration complexity and scaling plots.
- Clarity request: State assumptions of Lemma 1 and give intuition (effective sample sizes per feature) in the main text.
- Clarity request: Clarify Corollary 3’s regime (how K grows with N) and what the Op(1) term hides.
- Clarity request: Specify the merge criterion, distance threshold, and prove monotonic improvement of the bound when merging identical columns.
- Clarity request: Discuss initialization sensitivity (K0, µ), Tshrink, and stopping criteria in more detail.
- Originality: Novel extension of FAB beyond block-diagonal Hessian settings, retaining FIC form for LFMs; CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability+merge are original contributions.
- Significance: If asymptotics hold and experiments are fair, the method offers valuable automated model selection for scalable binary LFMs with practical impact.
- Pros: Conceptually neat asymptotic result showing FIC persistence; practical closed-form updates with effective acceleration; identifiability analysis and merge post-processing; promising empirical performance with compact K.
- Cons: Critical assumptions and proof details not in main paper; experimental fairness issues regarding σ; limited VB comparisons and lacking scalability characterization; merge step is heuristic with unclear conditions.
- Suggestion 1: Put precise assumptions for Lemma 1 in the main text and include a short proof sketch explaining when Op(1) stays bounded.
- Suggestion 2: Clarify whether FIC requires nontrivial feature usage (∑n znk not near 0 or N) and how approximation behaves as π̃k → 0 or 1 during shrinkage.
- Suggestion 3: Harmonize noise-variance treatment across methods or re-run experiments fixing σ for FAB to match baselines and report PLL/RMSE.
- Suggestion 4: Report per-iteration complexity and wall-clock scaling vs. N, D, K, and include ablations for Tshrink and merge.
- Suggestion 5: Provide sensitivity analysis to initialization (K0, µ) and include a small-data regime where FIC accuracy may degrade.
- Overall assessment: Strong, promising paper with novel theory and practical algorithm; lean toward acceptance if assumptions/proofs are clarified in the main text and experimental fairness issues are addressed.
