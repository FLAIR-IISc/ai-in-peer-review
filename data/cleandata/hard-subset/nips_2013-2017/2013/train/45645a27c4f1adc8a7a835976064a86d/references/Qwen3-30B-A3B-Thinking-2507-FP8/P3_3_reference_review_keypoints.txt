- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), showing LFMs share the same FIC form as MMs despite different Hessian structure.
- Lemma 1: Novel insight that LFMs admit the same FIC form as MMs even without block-diagonal Hessian, expanding FAB applicability.
- Practical contribution: introduces a CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) that reduces computation while preserving accuracy.
- Theorem 4: Provides identifiability results explaining why FAB avoids overfitting and enables a natural post-processing merge of redundant features.
- Empirical results: FAB/LFMs outperform state-of-the-art methods in model selection accuracy, computational efficiency, and reduced overfitting on synthetic and real datasets.
- Strength: Strong theoretical contribution linking FAB and LFMs, with rigorous proofs in supplementary material.
- Strength: Valuable practical innovation that convincingly speeds up inference (empirical support in Figure 1).
- Weakness: Novelty overstated—claims like “asymptotically ignores priors” are standard for marginal-likelihood/asymptotic Bayesian methods and need clearer distinction from similar approaches (e.g., MAD-Bayes).
- Weakness: Related work is insufficiently comprehensive—misses key Bayesian nonparametric LFM work (e.g., Griffiths & Ghahramani 2005; Miller et al. 2010) and mischaracterizes comparisons (MEIBP MAP vs. FAB marginal-likelihood).
- Weakness: Limited theoretical exposition in main text—key assumptions (e.g., enabling log det |F(d)| approximation in Lemma 1) lack intuitive explanation for non-specialists.
- Weakness: Experimental comparisons omit variational methods (VB) for small-scale cases and recent scalable Bayesian LFM methods, weakening empirical claims.
- Critical evaluation: Paper makes a meaningful, technically sound extension but overstates novelty and needs better contextualization among asymptotic Bayesian approaches.
- Critical evaluation: Experiments are strong but should include fairer comparisons and discuss extensions to more complex LFMs (e.g., non-Gaussian observations, deep Bayesian models).
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify relationship to other asymptotic Bayesian methods, expand related work, better explain asymptotic prior-ignorance, and include fairer comparisons with scalable Bayesian LFMs.
