Summary and relation to prior work
The paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models (MMs) and HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian likelihood. The central technical result (Lemma 1) asserts that the log-determinant of the Hessian of the complete-data negative log-likelihood with respect to per-dimension parameters θd contributes a regularization term ∑k log ∑n znk (up to Op(1)), even though LFMs do not satisfy the block-diagonal Hessian structure previously required for FAB in MMs. This yields an asymptotic approximation to the marginal complete log-likelihood (Theorem 2) and an FIC for LFMs that matches the MM case, leading to an implementable FAB lower bound and an EM-like algorithm with mean-field q(Z), a distinctive shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃k) in the E-step, and updates for π̃ and π. The authors interpret the E-step as a convex-concave procedure (CCCP), propose a shrinkage-acceleration routine (multiple CCCP iterations), and analyze identifiability, showing FAB prefers balanced solutions and motivating a post-hoc feature merge.

Empirically, FAB/LFM is compared to EM (variational EM with Bernoulli priors), IBP sampling, variational IBP (VB), and MEIBP on synthetic, block data, and several real datasets with missing entries. FAB generally selects more compact models and achieves competitive or better predictive log-likelihood at lower cost than most baselines (MEIBP is faster in some settings but with worse predictive performance).

Quality
- Technical soundness: The asymptotic derivation hinges on Lemma 1 and the regularity of the complete-data likelihood. The main proofs are in the supplement; in the main text, the assumptions are labeled “mild” but not stated. The reduction of log det|F(d)| to ∑k log ∑n znk/N + Op(1) is the key step; providing a precise statement of conditions (growth regimes of N, D, K; invertibility; boundedness) is essential to judge correctness. The use of Laplace on complete-data likelihood is standard when that model is regular; for LFMs this seems plausible, yet the non-identifiability issues are acknowledged and partly addressed.
- Algorithmic details: The lower bound L(q, P, π̃) is optimized by alternating steps; the mean-field restriction on q(Z) makes the E-step tractable and monotone in L. The CCCP view is sound and motivates the acceleration routine. The shrink/merge heuristics are sensible; the merge is only applied if it increases L, preserving monotonicity. One subtlety: Algorithm 2 runs Tshrink inner CCCP updates before the per-feature updates; clarity on how this maintains monotonicity of the global L at each sub-step would help (though CCCP guarantees monotonicity per inner iteration).
- Experiments: The comparisons are informative but have caveats. Noise variance is learned by FAB/EM but fixed for IBP/MEIBP/VB (per released code), making PLL comparisons potentially unfair. VB is largely omitted due to runtime. Some MEIBP runs required manual K initialization. Still, the synthetic K-recovery and block-data visualizations support the claims; real-data PLL/TLL gaps suggest reduced overfitting.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized, with helpful intuition (CCCP interpretation, identifiability). However:
- The exact assumptions for Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 should be summarized in the main text (invertibility, boundedness, whether K and D are fixed vs. allowed to grow with N, treatment of diagonal Λ).
- The handling of missing data in training and PLL computation should be specified.
- The shrinkage/merge conditions (what threshold for ∑n µnk/N equals 0 or 1 in practice?) should be clarified.

Originality
- Conceptual novelty: Showing that the FAB regularizer emerges for LFMs without block-diagonal Hessian assumptions is a substantial and nontrivial contribution.
- Algorithmic novelty: The CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability-guided merging are incremental but useful extensions. Relating FAB’s regularization to model-induced regularization in singular models provides context.

Significance
If the asymptotic result holds under broadly applicable conditions, this extends the reach of FAB beyond mixture-like models and provides a practical, prior-light alternative to IBP/VB for selecting latent feature dimensionality. Given the ongoing interest in LFMs (e.g., IBP variants) at NeurIPS, the contribution is relevant. The empirical advantages in compactness and training stability are attractive, though stronger, more controlled baselines would solidify impact.

Pros
- Novel asymptotic result yielding FIC for LFMs; aligns with prior FAB work yet breaks earlier structural assumptions.
- Practical algorithm with provable lower-bound monotonicity (under stated steps) and automatic feature shrinkage.
- CCCP interpretation enables effective acceleration.
- Identifiability analysis and merge post-processing are thoughtful and address real issues in LFMs.
- Empirical results show competitive or superior predictive performance with compact models and reasonable runtime.

Cons
- Key assumptions and proof conditions are not presented in the main text; validity and scope of Lemma 1 remain opaque without the supplement.
- Comparisons are not fully apples-to-apples (noise variance fixed for competitors; VB omitted; MEIBP sensitivity noted but not systematically tuned).
- Some algorithmic details need tightening: monotonicity with accelerateShrinkage, numerical criteria for feature removal (µ averages exactly 0/1 are rare), and missing-data handling.
- Limited exploration beyond diagonal noise; unclear robustness when D is large or when K grows with N.

Questions for the authors
- Please state the exact conditions under which Lemma 1 holds (growth of K and D with N, bounds on Λ, W, π; identifiability constraints), and whether K is allowed to scale with N.
- How are missing values handled in training and in computing PLL/TLL? Is inference modified, or are missing entries imputed?
- In practice, do you threshold ∑n µnk/N to decide pruning (e.g., < ε or > 1 − ε)? If so, how does ε affect monotonicity of L?
- Does accelerateShrinkage guarantee a non-decrease in L at each inner iteration? If not, do you checkpoint and revert?
- Can the method handle full-covariance noise? Would Lemma 1 and FIC form change?

Suggestions for improvement
- Move a concise statement of the main assumptions and a short proof sketch of Lemma 1/Theorem 2 into the main paper.
- Provide a controlled experiment where all methods learn noise variance (or all use the same fixed variance) to isolate the effect of model selection.
- Report sensitivity to Tshrink and δ; ablate merge post-processing.
- Clarify missing-data treatment and ensure predictive likelihoods are computed comparably across methods.
- Consider an experiment with K growing with N to probe asymptotic behavior.

Recommendation: Weak accept
The paper presents a strong and interesting theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs with a practical algorithm and supportive experiments. The main reservation is the opacity of the key asymptotic assumptions and some experimental fairness issues. With clarifications and modest revisions, this would be a valuable contribution.

Confidence: 0.7
