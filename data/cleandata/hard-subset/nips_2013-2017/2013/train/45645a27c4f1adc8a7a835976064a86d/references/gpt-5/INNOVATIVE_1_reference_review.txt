Summary
This paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMM settings to latent feature models (LFMs). The central theoretical contribution is Lemma 1, showing that, despite the non-block-diagonal Hessian structure of LFMs, the log-determinant of the per-dimension likelihood Hessian contributes the same model-complexity term as in mixtures, namely a sum over features of log(∑n znk). This leads to an FIC for LFMs that matches the MM form and yields an FAB algorithm with automatic feature shrinkage, asymptotic prior-ignorance, and a provably monotonic lower bound optimization. The paper further (i) interprets the FAB E-step as a convex-concave procedure (CCCP), motivating a practical shrinkage acceleration that removes irrelevant features early, and (ii) analyzes identifiability, showing FAB prefers balanced solutions and suggesting a principled post-hoc feature merging. Experiments on synthetic, block data, and several real datasets compare FAB/LFM (with acceleration) to IBP sampling, VB, and MEIBP, reporting improved model selection, competitive or better predictive log-likelihood, and good speed, especially compared to IBP/VB.

Relation to prior work
FAB has been successful for MMs/HMMs (Fujimaki et al.). LFMs are often approached via IBP (Teh, Griffiths & Ghahramani), MEIBP (Reed & Ghahramani), and VB. MAD-Bayes considers asymptotics in a small-noise limit; here the asymptotics are sample-size driven and produce model-induced regularization linked to singular learning theory (Watanabe). The key novelty is extending FIC/FAB beyond settings where the complete-data Hessian block-diagonalizes, with a rigorous asymptotic accounting of complexity for LFMs, and the CCCP view enabling a new acceleration.

Strengths
- Novel theory: The identification that log det|F(d)| contributes ∑k log(∑n znk) without block-diagonal assumptions is elegant and important. It suggests FIC-type approximations apply more broadly than previously believed.
- Clean algorithmic translation: The resulting FAB/LFM mirrors FAB for mixtures, with a simple mean-field E-step and closed-form M-step, retaining a monotonic lower-bound improvement and automatic feature shrinkage.
- CCCP interpretation and acceleration: The CCCP view clarifies dynamics of the shrinkage term and motivates a practical multi-step update that significantly cuts runtime by eliminating features earlier.
- Identifiability insight: The analysis that FAB selects balanced solutions and provides a merging rule addresses a real pain point in LFMs (non-identifiability of linear-Gaussian feature models).
- Empirical evidence: Synthetic and real-data results indicate accurate model selection, competitive to superior predictive likelihoods, and strong practical automation (no hand-tuned hyperparameters), with much lower cost than IBP/VB, and often better PLL than MEIBP.

Weaknesses and questions
- Assumptions and scope of Lemma 1: The “mild assumptions” are deferred to the supplement. It is important to state in the main text the key conditions (e.g., scaling of Z’Z, boundedness, feature co-occurrence structure) under which the log det decomposition holds, and whether correlations among features degrade the approximation. A brief intuition sketch would help.
- Mean-field approximation: The move to factorized q(Z) breaks the exact equality in Eq. (1). While common in VB, it weakens the FIC interpretation. Clarify whether the monotone ascent guarantees refer to the variational lower bound L only, and discuss any observed gaps between FIC and log-marginal empirically.
- Model class and generality: The analysis and algorithm are presented for linear-Gaussian LFMs with diagonal noise. Please discuss how far the FIC extension and CCCP updates generalize to non-Gaussian likelihoods, non-diagonal Λ, or logistic likelihoods (e.g., binary observations).
- Experimental fairness and scale: Some comparisons fix noise std for competitors and initialize K at min(N, D), which may disadvantage them (MEIBP sensitivity is acknowledged). A more balanced setup (e.g., allowing competitors to learn noise or a sweep over K/alpha) would strengthen conclusions. Also, results stop after 50h for some methods; reporting progress curves and wall-clock parity via optimized/compiled implementations would be ideal.
- Merging: The merging rule is heuristic (based on W distances and gain in L). Provide the thresholding protocol and an ablation showing its effect on runtime and PLL/TLL.
- Complexity analysis: An explicit per-iteration complexity and memory footprint would help practitioners gauge scalability in N, D, K.

Quality
- Generally solid, with nontrivial asymptotic analysis and careful algorithm design. Proof details are supplemental; bringing conditions forward would improve rigor. Empirical results are convincing overall, though some fairness/scale clarifications are desirable.

Clarity
- Well organized and readable, but some heavy steps (Lemma 1 assumptions; derivation of Eq. 6 and Theorem 2) should be summarized in the main text. A brief cost analysis and a clearer description of merge/accelerateShrinkage would help.

Originality
- High. Extending FIC/FAB beyond block-diagonal Hessians to LFMs is a meaningful conceptual step. The CCCP view and identifiability-guided merging are also original and useful.

Significance
- High. Automated, prior-free model selection with strong shrinkage in LFMs is valuable, providing a practical alternative to IBP variants. The result may stimulate broader application of FIC/FAB to other latent-variable models.

Suggestions for improvement
- State the key assumptions for Lemma 1 in the main paper and provide intuition.
- Provide per-iteration complexity and memory analysis; include wall-clock vs iteration plots.
- Add ablations: with/without acceleration, with/without merge, effect of Tshrink.
- Clarify fairness in experimental settings (noise learning, initialization, hyperparameters).
- Discuss extension beyond linear-Gaussian LFMs.

Pros
- Conceptually bold extension of FAB to LFMs with new asymptotic insight.
- Simple, efficient algorithm with monotonic lower-bound ascent.
- Practical CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability-aware merging.
- Strong empirical model selection and predictive performance.

Cons
- Key theoretical assumptions relegated to the supplement.
- Experimental setup could be more balanced across baselines.
- Limited discussion of generality beyond linear-Gaussian, diagonal noise.

Recommendation
- Accept (8/10). The paper introduces a significant new direction by broadening FAB’s applicability and offering practical, automated model selection for LFMs. Minor theoretical and experimental clarifications would further strengthen it, but the novelty and potential impact justify acceptance.

Confidence
- 0.7 (read paper carefully; did not verify supplemental proofs).
