This paper presents an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing a limitation of previous FAB work that required a block-diagonal Hessian. The authors demonstrate that LFMs, despite not initially satisfying this condition, can still be analyzed within the FAB framework. They derive FICs for LFMs, propose a shrinkage acceleration method based on a connection to the CCCP algorithm, and analyze parameter identifiability.

The paper is well-structured and the mathematical derivations appear generally sound, though a deeper dive into the supplementary materials would be required to fully verify all claims. The motivation for extending FAB inference is clear – it offers a computationally efficient alternative to NPB and VB methods for model selection in LFMs. The core contribution, demonstrating the applicability of FAB to LFMs despite the Hessian non-block-diagonal property, is potentially valuable. The connection to CCCP and the resulting acceleration method are interesting and practically relevant, as evidenced by the experimental results. The discussion of parameter identifiability and its connection to a merging post-processing step adds further depth.

However, numerous minor flaws and a concerning lack of detail in experimental methodology significantly detract from the overall quality. My assessment leans towards rejection, not due to a lack of interesting ideas, but due to a lack of thoroughness and precision.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Extending FAB inference to LFMs is a non-trivial contribution.
*   **Theoretical Soundness (appears so):** The derivations of the FICs and the analysis of the Hessian seem mathematically reasonable, pending verification of supplementary material.
*   **Practical Improvement:** The CCCP-based acceleration method demonstrably improves computational efficiency.
*   **Identifiability Analysis:** The discussion of parameter identifiability and the merging post-processing provide valuable insights.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Insufficient Experimental Detail:** The experimental setup lacks crucial details. For instance, the choice of noise standard deviation (0.75 for real data) is stated without justification. What motivated this value? The use of software default parameters is also concerning; were any parameter sweeps performed? The statement "we fixed it to 1 for artificial simulations, which is the true standard deviation of toy data" reads oddly; why wasn't this information presented *before* describing the setup?
*   **Minor Notational Inconsistencies:** Throughout the paper, there are minor inconsistencies in notation. For example, the definition of *P* changes slightly in different contexts. While not critical, they detract from clarity. The subscript notation (e.g., *p<sub>k</sub>*) is sometimes omitted, making the equations harder to follow.
*   **Overly Assertive Language:** The authors frequently use strong, unqualified statements. For example, “FAB consistently achieved good predictive performance.” This needs to be tempered with more nuanced discussion of the results. Similarly, claiming “FAB achieved the most accurate estimation of K” requires robust statistical support, which isn’t presented.
*   **Missing Error Bars/Statistical Significance:** The results presented in the figures lack error bars or any indication of statistical significance. This makes it impossible to assess the reliability of the comparisons.
*   **Limited Ablation Study:** While the shrinkage acceleration is presented, a more detailed ablation study exploring the impact of *T<sub>shrink</sub>* would be beneficial.
*   **Lack of Comparison to Simpler Baselines:** Comparing FAB/LFMs to simpler LFM inference methods (e.g., standard EM) would provide a more complete picture of its performance gains.
*   **Unclear Justification for Hyperparameter Choices:** The initial number of latent features being set to min(N,D) is not justified. Why this choice?
*   **Merge Post-processing Detail:** The description of the "merge()" algorithm is relegated to the supplementary materials. This is a crucial part of the method, and more detail should be included in the main text.
*   **Vague statements**: The statement “FAB typically achieved a more compact and better model representation than the others (smaller K)” is vague. What metric constitutes "better"?

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The extension of FAB inference to LFMs is a novel and potentially impactful contribution.
*   The CCCP-based acceleration method is a practical improvement.
*   The theoretical analysis appears sound.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of experimental detail and statistical rigor undermines the credibility of the results.
*   The numerous minor flaws and inconsistencies detract from the overall quality of the paper.
*   The overly assertive language and lack of nuanced discussion weaken the arguments.
*   The limited ablation study and comparison to simpler baselines leave unanswered questions about the method's performance.



**Overall Assessment:**

While the paper presents interesting ideas, the lack of rigor and attention to detail raises serious concerns. The authors need to significantly improve the experimental methodology, provide more detailed explanations, and temper their claims. As it stands, the paper does not meet the quality standards expected for NIPS acceptance. I recommend **rejection**. A revised version, addressing the aforementioned concerns, could be reconsidered.
