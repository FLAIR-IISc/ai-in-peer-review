- Main contribution: extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), deriving an FIC identical in form to mixture models despite violated Hessian assumptions.  
- Core theoretical result: Lemma 1 shows each output-dimension Hessian contributes O(∑k log ∑n znk), leading to Theorem 2/Corollary 3 that the FIC asymptotically approximates the marginal log-likelihood.  
- Algorithmic contributions: a tractable lower bound via convex linearization and mean-field on Z, a fixed-point E-step, CCCP interpretation, and a shrinkage-acceleration routine encouraging early feature elimination.  
- Identifiability analysis: Theorem 4 shows FAB favors balanced solutions and motivates a distance-based merge post-processing step.  
- Empirical results: compares FAB/LFM to IBP variants, MEIBP, and EM on synthetic, structured-image, and real datasets, showing competitive or better model selection and predictive performance with lower/comparable compute.  
- Novelty: first extension of FAB beyond block-diagonal complete-data Hessian models to LFMs, plus new CCCP/shrinkage insights and identifiability results.  
- Strength: clear high-level theory showing why the same FIC penalty arises and asymptotic equivalence to marginal likelihood.  
- Strength: sound lower-bound optimization and elegant CCCP view that justifies shrinkage-acceleration and runtime reductions from early feature elimination.  
- Strength: identifiability theorem addresses LFM pathologies and supports principled merging.  
- Concern: key assumptions for Lemma 1 are relegated to the supplement and described only as “mild”; main text should state conditions (e.g., on Λ, π, W, eigenvalue growth).  
- Concern: Theorem 2 ignores O(1) constants and assumes bounded joint marginal log-likelihood; clarification of the valid regime is needed.  
- Concern: mean-field restriction changes the bound—monotonic increase claims need explicit statements about which steps (mean-field updates, CCCP sub-steps, accelerateShrinkage) guarantee L increases.  
- Concern: merge step is heuristic and should clarify conditions under which merges strictly increase L beyond degenerate equality cases.  
- Concern: fairness of empirical comparisons—FAB learns noise variance while competitors use fixed variance—requires sensitivity analysis to avoid advantaging FAB.  
- Concern: missing per-iteration time/memory complexity and only anecdotal sensitivity to Tshrink; include wall-clock scaling and ablations on Tshrink and merge threshold.  
- Clarity requests: move concise Lemma 1 assumptions and a proof sketch into main text, explicitly list monotonicity guarantees under mean-field/CCCP, and add a brief complexity discussion and ablations.  
- Pros: strong theoretical advance, practical algorithm with effective shrinkage, identifiability handling, and good empirical automation/performance.  
- Cons: essential proof details hidden, empirical fairness/complexity reporting could be improved, limited discussion beyond Gaussian diagonal-precision and Bernoulli priors.  
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on addressing clarity and evaluation concerns.
