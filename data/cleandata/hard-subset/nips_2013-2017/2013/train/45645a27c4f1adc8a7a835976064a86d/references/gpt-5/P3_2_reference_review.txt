Summary
The paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture/HMM settings to linear-Gaussian latent feature models (LFMs) with Bernoulli features. The central theoretical claim (Lemma 1) is that, despite the Hessian of the complete log-likelihood not being block diagonal in LFMs, the log-determinant of the per-dimension Fisher/Hessian scales as sum_k log(sum_n z_nk) (up to Op(1)). This yields an FIC for LFMs (Theorem 2) that matches the known FAB/FIC form for mixture models, with an automatic complexity penalty −(D/2)∑_k log(∑_n z_nk). Based on this, the authors derive a practical lower bound using mean-field q(Z), linearize the convex term as in prior FAB, and propose an EM-like algorithm with an E-step update that contains a shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃_k), an M-step with closed forms, a feature-pruning step, and an acceleration motivated by a CCCP interpretation that performs multiple inner updates to shrink irrelevant features early. They also analyze identifiability, arguing FAB prefers low-K ML solutions and propose a post-hoc merge operation when columns of W coincide. Experiments on synthetic and several real datasets compare against IBP sampling, VB, MEIBP, and EM, reporting better K selection and predictive log-likelihood at competitive cost, especially with the shrinkage acceleration.

Relation to prior work
- Builds on FAB for MMs/HMMs (Fujimaki et al.) by extending the asymptotic analysis to LFMs.
- Connects to singular learning theory/MAD-Bayes (Watanabe; Broderick et al.) in that the regularizer emerges from marginalization rather than explicit priors, contrasting with ARD or IBP’s Beta-Bernoulli priors.
- Related to IBP-based inference (Gibbs/VB/MEIBP), and probabilistic PCA/FA for the M-step updates.
- The CCCP perspective is standard for difference-of-convex decompositions; using it to accelerate FAB’s shrinkage is an interesting engineering contribution.

Strengths
- A nontrivial theoretical result (Lemma 1) showing the same FIC-form emerges for LFMs as for MMs, despite lack of block-diagonal Hessian. If correct, this broadens the applicability of FAB beyond mixture-like structures.
- Clear algorithmic instantiation with an interpretable shrinkage term that automatically selects K, plus practical heuristics (feature pruning, merging).
- The CCCP interpretation provides a principled view of the inner updates and motivates an effective acceleration strategy, with empirical gains.
- Experiments suggest improved K recovery and predictive performance relative to strong baselines on several datasets, with competitive runtime.
- Discussion of identifiability and post-hoc merging addresses a real issue in LFMs (non-uniqueness from redundant columns).

Weaknesses and questions
Theoretical
- Lemma 1 is pivotal but its “mild assumptions” are deferred to the supplement. Please state them precisely in the main text (e.g., conditions on Λ, boundedness of moments, feature activation rates bounded away from 0/1, rank conditions), and outline the proof idea. When do Op(1) terms depend on K, D, π?
- Theorem 2 uses Op(1) accuracy on log p(X,Z|M); the claim that FIC/N approximates log p(X|M)/N hinges on the Op(1)/N term vanishing. Please make this scaling explicit and clarify whether K may grow with N.
- Identifiability (Theorem 4) is weak: it shows that if w_k = w_l, then average activations match at a stationary point. This does not establish identifiability or uniqueness, nor how close-but-not-equal columns behave. Can you characterize stability (e.g., local curvature) and provide guarantees for the merge operation increasing the bound when columns are near-duplicated?
- The use of mean-field q(Z) breaks the exact variational representation (Eq. 1). Please discuss the tightness of the bound and whether the FIC consistency (Corollary 3) still holds under this restriction.

Algorithmic
- There appears to be a discrepancy between Eqs. (11) and (14): Eq. (11) uses −D/(2Nπ̃_k) while Eq. (14) uses −D/(∑_n μ_{nk}^{t-1}). Given Nπ̃_k = ∑_n μ_{nk}, Eq. (14) seems to miss a factor 1/2. Please correct or explain.
- Monotonicity: FAB is said to monotonically increase the lower bound, but Algorithm 2 updates π and π̃ within inner CCCP loops and prunes features. Do these steps preserve ascent guarantees? A brief proof or conditions under which monotonicity holds would strengthen the paper.
- Complexity: Please provide per-iteration time/memory complexity in terms of N, D, K (with and without acceleration), and wall-clock scaling plots. The accelerateShrinkage loops over k and Tshrink; show empirically that total time is reduced across regimes and provide guidance on Tshrink.
- Feature pruning and bias updates: When ∑_n μ_{nk}/N = 1 and b is updated by adding w_k, does this preserve the bound exactly given the Λ-diagonal noise? Any effect on regularization terms? Clarify.

Empirical
- Fairness: IBP/VB/MEIBP do not learn noise variance in your setup; you fix it to 1/0.75, while FAB learns Λ. This could advantage FAB in PLL/TLL. Please either allow noise learning for baselines (where possible) or control for this via fixing Λ in FAB, and discuss sensitivity.
- Hyperparameters: IBP’s α fixed to 3 may bias K upward. Report sensitivity to α and to MEIBP’s initialization/parameters.
- Missing VB due to long runtime undermines completeness on some datasets. Consider at least a subset with VB and report time/accuracy tradeoffs.
- Report variance across seeds for K and PLL/TLL, and show ablations: with/without acceleration; with/without merge; using only prior-based η(π_k) shrinkage.
- Provide quantitative tables/figures (in the main text) with actual numbers; currently many results are described qualitatively.

Clarity and presentation
- Some constants/signs are unclear (e.g., “−2D+K log N” in Eq. 10). Please check derivations and present all constants that affect model selection.
- Summarize the key assumptions and the main proof steps of Lemma 1/Theorem 2 in the main text; currently too much is deferred.
- Minor: fix typos and improve notational consistency; clearly define Op(1), DZ, Dk, |P|, etc.

Pros
- Novel extension of FAB theory to LFMs with a neat, MM-like FIC form.
- Practical algorithm with automatic model selection and effective acceleration.
- Generally strong empirical results and insightful connections (CCCP, identifiability/merging).

Cons
- Critical theoretical assumptions and some derivation details are opaque in the main paper.
- A likely factor-of-2 error in the CCCP update.
- Monotonicity and complexity claims need clearer guarantees.
- Some fairness issues in the experimental setup and missing quantitative details.

Recommendation
Borderline accept. The core idea—establishing an FIC for LFMs and leveraging it for efficient, automatic model selection—is interesting and potentially impactful. However, acceptance should be contingent on clarifying assumptions/proofs for Lemma 1/Theorem 2, fixing the CCCP discrepancy, and tightening the empirical evaluation and fairness. If adequately addressed, this work would be a valuable addition to the literature on scalable Bayesian model selection for latent feature models.
