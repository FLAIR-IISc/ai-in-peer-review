Summary
The paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian likelihoods. The key theoretical step is Lemma 1, which asserts that the log-determinant of the per-dimension Hessian contributes a sum_k log ∑n znk term asymptotically, despite the Hessian not being block-diagonal as in mixtures. This leads to an LFM FIC with the same structure as for mixtures, enabling FAB-style lower-bound optimization, automatic feature shrinkage, and a CCCP-based acceleration. The authors also claim a form of identifiability that motivates a post-hoc merging of redundant features. Experiments on synthetic, block data, and several real datasets compare FAB to IBP variants, VB, and a simple EM baseline, reporting favorable K-selection, predictive log-likelihoods, and runtime with the proposed acceleration.

Relation to prior work
FAB/FIC for mixtures and HMMs exists (Fujimaki et al.), and latent feature selection is typically addressed by IBP (sampling, VB, MEIBP) or MAP/MAD-Bayes asymptotics. The paper’s main claim is that a mixture-style FIC emerges for LFMs via a different asymptotic analysis, allowing prior-agnostic automatic shrinkage. The CCCP view parallels known convex-concave decompositions of variational objectives. The identifiability discussion connects to Watanabe’s singular learning theory.

Strengths
- Ambitious and potentially impactful extension of FAB to LFMs, if the asymptotics are correct.
- Clear algorithmic template with a computationally attractive CCCP acceleration and a simple shrinkage rule.
- Claims of automatic model selection and prior-agnostic regularization are interesting in practice.
- Empirical results suggest competitive K-selection and predictive performance on some datasets; acceleration appears to reduce runtime substantially.

Weaknesses and concerns
Theoretical clarity/correctness:
- Lemma 1 is central but underspecified. The statement “log det|F(d)| = ∑k log ∑n znk/N + Op(1)” is ambiguous (is it log(∑/N) or log(∑) − log N?). In Theorem 2, this becomes penalties −(DK/2) log N − (D/2)∑k log∑n znk. The mapping from Lemma 1 to (8) is not made precise, constants are inconsistently treated (e.g., a bare “−|P|” term in J), and the exact regularity assumptions are deferred. Given that this underpins the whole FIC, more detail is essential.
- The “asymptotic ignoring” of log det|FZ| and other terms is invoked but not justified for LFMs; conditions for Laplace applicability in the presence of latent binary variables and singularities need to be stated.
- The CCCP derivation (Eq. 13–14): signs and convexity/concavity assertions depend critically on constants. The form in Eq. (10): “− 2D +K log N” appears dimensionally incorrect (likely −(2D+K)/2 log N or similar). Several such inconsistencies make it hard to verify the derivations.

Algorithmic correctness:
- Merge post-processing appears mathematically invalid as written. If w·k = w·l, the proposal “w′·k = 2w·k and µ′·k = µ·k + µ·l” does not preserve the reconstruction (it doubles it) and violates the Bernoulli mean constraint when µ′ > 1. No reparameterization that preserves both the likelihood and the Bernoulli family is provided. This is a substantive flaw.
- Shrinkage step: removing features with ∑n µnk/N = 0 is harmless; treating always-active features (∑n µnk/N = 1) as bias by updating b ← b + wk is only valid if µnk = 1 exactly for all n. With sigmoid updates, exact 0/1 is measure-zero; the algorithm offers no thresholding policy nor bound monotonicity guarantees under approximate 0/1.
- Monotonicity: while E/M updates increase the lower bound, accelerateShrinkage (multiple CCCP steps per E) and the shrink/merge heuristics can break monotonicity. The paper partially acknowledges this by checking L after merging, but guarantees are not established.

Experimental methodology:
- Comparisons are not fair: FAB/EM learn noise precision λ, whereas IBP/VB/MEIBP are run with fixed noise (chosen by the authors), which can strongly affect K-selection and PLL. For a fair comparison, either fix λ for all methods or use implementations that learn λ for the baselines.
- VB is omitted on real data due to runtime; MEIBP is omitted on some datasets due to non-negativity or runtime. These choices bias the comparison and limit conclusions about state of the art.
- Initialization and hyperparameter choices (IBP α=3, initial K=min(N,D) for FAB/EM, special-casing MEIBP) can materially affect performance; ablations are missing.
- Missing quantitative details: no complexity analysis of accelerateShrinkage; Tshrink=100 is arbitrary. No sensitivity analysis for δ, Tshrink, or priors η(πk).

Clarity/Presentation:
- Numerous typos and inconsistencies: “mean-filed,” “NBP” vs “NPB,” “MEIPB,” “we here omit,” etc. Equations miss parentheses; constants are off; footnote markers are misplaced; notation D, DZ, Dk is conflated with data dimension D. Algorithm pseudocode uses undefined variables (e.g., cn in Eq. 13) and lacks missing-data handling despite using missingness in experiments.
- References are incomplete (missing years/venues) and inconsistently formatted.

Questions for the authors
1. Provide the precise statement and proof sketch of Lemma 1 in the main text, including assumptions and the exact constants, and show explicitly how it yields Eq. (8).
2. Clarify Eq. (10) constants and the CCCP derivation; ensure convex/concave parts and signs are correct.
3. How do you handle missing values in the E/M updates? This is not described.
4. Fix the merge operation: how can you merge two identical columns while preserving the model class (Bernoulli latent features) without violating µ ∈ [0,1]? If no exact merge exists, justify any heuristic and its effect on the bound.
5. Provide fair comparisons with learned noise precision for baselines, or fix λ for FAB as well, and report sensitivity to α and initialization. Include VB where feasible and report truncated-K settings.

Pros
- Interesting and potentially broad theoretical claim (FIC for LFMs without block-diagonal Hessian).
- Simple, practical algorithm with an acceleration idea.
- Promising empirical results in some regimes.

Cons
- Central theory insufficiently specified; constants/assumptions unclear.
- Merge step is incorrect as written; shrinkage heuristics can break guarantees.
- Unfair/limited experimental comparisons; missing ablations.
- Many clarity/formatting issues that impede verification and reproduction.

Recommendation
Reject. The contribution could be significant, but the current manuscript has too many technical, methodological, and presentation flaws. A revised version should (i) present the core asymptotics clearly and correctly, (ii) fix or remove the merge heuristic, (iii) ensure fair and thorough empirical evaluation, and (iv) clean up notation, equations, and references.
