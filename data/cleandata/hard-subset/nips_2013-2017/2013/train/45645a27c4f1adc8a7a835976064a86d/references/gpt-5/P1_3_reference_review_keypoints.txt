- Paper extends FAB/FIC from mixture models to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian emissions, deriving a factorized information criterion for LFMs.
- Lemma 1 shows each dimension contributes a term sum_k log(sum_n z_nk / N) to the log-determinant of the Hessian, despite LFMs lacking block-diagonal Hessian structure.
- Theorem 2 yields an FIC for LFMs matching MM model-complexity terms, enabling automatic feature pruning and asymptotic consistency to the marginal log-likelihood.
- Authors derive mean-field E-step updates with a novel shrinkage term D/(2 N π̃_k) that drives unused features to extinction.
- M-step updates match PPCA for W, b, Λ, and π updates equal average posterior feature usage.
- FAB E-step is reinterpreted as a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP); multiple inner CCCP steps accelerate early shrinkage.
- A merge post-processing heuristic is proposed, motivated by an identifiability theorem favoring balanced feature usage and merging identical W columns.
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets compare FAB against IBP, VB-IBP, MEIBP, and EM, showing competitive or superior model selection and predictive log-likelihood with lower or comparable runtime.
- Strength: Novel asymptotic analysis broadens FAB/FIC applicability beyond mixtures.
- Strength: FIC provides principled, prior-free automatic feature pruning and helps resolve non-identifiability.
- Strength: Practical algorithmic contributions (CCCP interpretation, accelerateShrinkage, merge heuristic) are simple and effective.
- Strength: Empirical validation demonstrates compact K, reduced overfitting, and clear speedups from shrinkage acceleration.
- Weakness: Key assumptions and proofs for Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 are deferred to the supplement; main text lacks explicit assumptions and proof sketch.
- Weakness: Experimental fairness unclear—baselines reportedly have fixed noise while it’s unclear whether FAB learns Λ, potentially biasing PLL/TLL comparisons.
- Weakness: Sensitivity to Tshrink, initialization K0, and merge thresholds not analyzed; robustness claims need sensitivity studies.
- Weakness: Missing per-iteration complexity and memory analysis; unclear when acceleration is most beneficial and how runtime scales with N, D, K.
- Weakness: Merge step is heuristic (Euclidean distance + bound check); needs more details and ablation studies on merge frequency and effect.
- Weakness: Limited comparisons—VB/MEIBP omitted on some datasets due to runtime or constraints, reducing completeness.
- Quality: Technical development appears sound but central asymptotic arguments in supplement make verification harder; empirical results solid but need more sensitivity/fairness checks.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written; should explicitly state assumptions, clarify whether Λ is learned in experiments, add complexity analysis, and fix minor notation/typo issues.
- Originality: High—extending FAB/FIC to LFMs without MM Hessian condition, CCCP reinterpretation, and identifiability/merge analysis are substantial novel contributions.
- Significance: High—provides a scalable, hyperparameter-free alternative to IBP for model selection in LFMs with theoretical grounding and good empirical performance.
- Questions for authors: list exact assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2, clarify whether Λ is learned and provide matching experiments, report per-iteration complexity and Tshrink effects, state if accelerateShrinkage preserves monotonic bounds, and discuss sensitivity to initialization.
- Minor comments: fix small typos, formatting glitches, and add discussion contrasting FIC/FAB with MAD-Bayes beyond the zero-noise limit.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical extension, practical algorithm with automatic feature selection and acceleration, strong empirical performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing key theoretical assumptions in main text, experimental fairness/sensitivity issues, and heuristic merge/acceleration needing more ablations.
- Overall recommendation: Accept (weak to moderate) pending clarifications and additional experiments addressing theoretical assumptions and experimental fairness.
