- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by deriving an FIC despite non-block-diagonal Hessians.
- Shows LFMs share FIC form with mixture models via Hessian structure analysis.
- Relates FAB E-steps to Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP) and introduces a shrinkage acceleration technique.
- Proves parameter identifiability and proposes merge post-processing to handle redundant features.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show competitive accuracy and notably faster computation versus IBP, VB, MEIBP.
- Theoretical derivations are significant but dense and may be hard to follow without more intuition in the main text.
- Experimental evaluation is limited in dataset variety, scale, and depth of baseline comparisons.
- Assumptions (e.g., Lemma 1) are insufficiently discussed; robustness to assumption violations is unclear.
- Merge post-processing is promising but lacks thorough empirical evidence and ablation studies.
- No formal computational complexity analysis comparing FAB to other methods is provided.
- Overall: original and practically useful contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: expand experiments (larger/more baselines), clarify derivations, discuss assumptions, evaluate merge step, and add complexity analysis.
