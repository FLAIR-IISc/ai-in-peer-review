- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from Mixture Models to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by showing analogous FIC form despite non-block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Lemma 1 is the central theoretical result justifying FAB’s applicability to LFMs.  
- Connects the FAB E-step to the Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) and derives a shrinkage acceleration method.  
- Provides parameter identifiability analysis and a merging post-processing step for latent features.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic, benchmark, and real datasets comparing to IBP, VB, MEIBP, and EM.  
- Demonstrates competitive accuracy, scalability, and benefits from shrinkage acceleration (ablation study).  
- Algorithm offers automated model selection, monotonic improvement, and computational efficiency.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically precise.  
- “Mild assumptions” around Lemma 1 are not fully discussed; implications and failure cases are unclear.  
- Sensitivity to initialization is insufficiently explored.  
- Lacks a formal computational-complexity analysis of FAB (with and without acceleration) versus baselines.  
- Merge post-processing is described only in supplementary material; main-text criteria and impact are opaque.  
- Some experiments fix noise standard deviations, undermining the automation claim; automatic noise estimation not addressed.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and experiments support claims, but assumption discussion should be expanded.  
- The CCCP connection, shrinkage acceleration, and identifiability/merge steps are original contributions.  
- The method is significant for scalable, automated model selection in high-dimensional LFMs.  
- For acceptance: strong theory, novel extension, thorough experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: incomplete assumption discussion, insufficient merge details, and missing formal complexity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept, with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
