- Extends FAB inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with central claim (Lemma 1) that the log-determinant Hessian term asymptotically reduces to ∑k log ∑n znk, yielding an FIC for LFMs matching mixtures.  
- Derives a tractable lower bound using linearization of the concave complexity term and a mean-field approximation on Z, leading to an alternating FAB E/M algorithm.  
- Interprets the E-step as a CCCP update and proposes “shrinkage acceleration” (multiple CCCP steps per iteration) to eliminate irrelevant features faster.  
- Proves an identifiability result that biases toward balanced feature usage and proposes a merge postprocessing heuristic.  
- Empirical results on synthetic, block, and real datasets show competitive or better model selection and predictive log-likelihood, with lower or comparable runtime, especially using acceleration.  
- Theoretical soundness: Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 are central but key assumptions and proofs are deferred to the supplement, making scope hard to assess.  
- Theoretical limitations: results rely on diagonal noise precision and a linear-Gaussian likelihood; extensions to full covariance or non-Gaussian models are not shown.  
- Optimization: lower bound relies on linearization and mean-field q(Z); authors claim asymptotically constant distortion but this departs from exact FIC.  
- Monotonicity concerns: need clarification that each inner CCCP step in accelerateShrinkage provably increases the bound and that simultaneous updates of π and π̃ preserve monotonicity.  
- Shrink/merge operations are said to increase the objective, but merge is only applied if it empirically improves L — practical but requires precise conditions.  
- Empirical fairness concerns: baselines fix noise variance and IBP hyperparameters, VB omitted on large datasets, and MEIBP excluded on negative data, potentially biasing comparisons.  
- Suggested empirical fixes: tune baselines (e.g., grid over noise variance and IBP α), include VB on smaller datasets, and report wall-clock vs accuracy trade-offs.  
- Writing/clarty: generally well organized; recommend adding Lemma 1 assumptions and a proof sketch in the main paper for intuition.  
- Clarity improvements: explicitly state model scope (linear-Gaussian with diagonal precision), discuss generalization, and specify thresholds/conditions for shrink/merge provable increases.  
- Originality: conceptually novel asymptotic result showing FIC for LFMs matches mixtures despite lack of block-diagonal Hessian; methodologically solid FAB formulation and CCCP-based acceleration.  
- Significance: valuable automatic, hyperparameter-free feature selection for LFMs with principled asymptotic justification and practical speed/accuracy benefits.  
- Pros: novel asymptotic result, simple practical algorithm with shrinkage and acceleration, automatic model selection, identifiability analysis, favorable empirical results.  
- Cons: critical assumptions/proofs relegated to supplement, ambiguity about monotonicity under acceleration, potentially unfair baselines, limited discussion of failure modes and generality beyond diagonal Gaussian LFMs.  
- Requests for authors: state exact assumptions of Lemma 1 and a brief proof sketch (does it require diagonal Λ and independent dimensions?).  
- Requests for authors: clarify whether Theorem 2/FIC extends to non-Gaussian observations or full-covariance noise.  
- Requests for authors: provide monotonicity guarantees for accelerateShrinkage when updating π and π̃ inside the inner loop.  
- Requests for authors: report sensitivity to Tshrink and µ initialization via an ablation.  
- Requests for authors: include tuned IBP (α) and a small-scale VB comparison, and consider grids over noise variance for fairness.  
- Overall assessment: compelling and potentially strong contribution; leaning toward acceptance pending clearer theoretical conditions and tighter, fairer empirical comparisons.
