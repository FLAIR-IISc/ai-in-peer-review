## Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

**Summary:**

This paper extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework, previously successful with mixture models, to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The key contribution is demonstrating that despite LFMs not initially satisfying the conditions required for standard FAB application, their Hessian structure allows for a similar FIC derivation. The authors also present analysis relating FAB E-steps to a Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP), leading to a practical shrinkage acceleration technique, and prove parameter identifiability offering a guide for post-processing. Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrates competitive performance in model selection and prediction, with a focus on computational efficiency.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The derivation of the FIC for LFMs is a significant theoretical extension of the FAB framework, expanding its applicability beyond models with block-diagonal Hessians.  Demonstrating the shared form of FICs between MMs and LFMs is insightful.
* **Practical Improvement:** The identification of the connection to CCCP and the resulting shrinkage acceleration are valuable practical contributions. The experiments clearly show a significant speed-up without sacrificing performance.
* **Identifiability Analysis:** Establishing parameter identifiability is a strong point, addressing a common concern in latent variable models and providing a principled approach for merging redundant features.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments, while not exhaustive, provide evidence of competitive performance in terms of accuracy and, importantly, computational speed. The comparison to IBP, VB, and MEIBP is relevant.
* **Clear Writing:**  The paper is generally well-written and organized, clearly explaining the theoretical derivations and algorithmic steps.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the results are promising, the experimental evaluation feels somewhat limited.  The number of datasets is small, and the comparison to strong baselines (especially IBP variants) isn’t consistently comprehensive. The reliance on default parameter settings for comparison methods is also a concern.  A deeper dive into different data modalities and larger datasets would strengthen the claims.
* **Complexity of the derivation:** The theoretical derivations are dense and may be difficult for readers not already familiar with FAB and asymptotic Bayesian inference. While the authors provide details in the supplementary material, greater clarity in the main paper would be beneficial.
* **Lack of Discussion on Assumptions:**  The paper mentions assumptions (e.g. Lemma 1), but a more detailed discussion of their impact and potential limitations would be helpful.  How robust is the method to violations of these assumptions in real-world data?
* **Merge Post-processing – Limited Results:**  The merge post-processing step seems promising, but the paper reports little empirical evidence of its effectiveness. Further investigation is needed to demonstrate its practical impact.
* **Computational Complexity Analysis:** While speed is highlighted as an advantage, a formal computational complexity analysis comparing FAB to other methods would strengthen the claims.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The derivations appear mathematically rigorous (though dense), and the experimental results support the claims. However, the lack of thorough robustness checks and detailed assumption discussion slightly weakens this aspect.
* **Clarity:** The paper is mostly clear, but the theoretical sections could benefit from more intuitive explanations and examples. The organization is logical, but the density of information can be overwhelming.
* **Originality:**  Extending FAB to LFMs is a novel contribution. The connection to CCCP and the identifiability analysis also represent original insights. The work is well-referenced, though a more thorough survey of recent LFM literature might be beneficial.
* **Significance:** The potential for a computationally efficient Bayesian inference method for LFMs is significant.  If the scalability and accuracy benefits hold up under more extensive testing, this work could have a real impact on applications involving large-scale latent feature modeling.



**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Extends a promising Bayesian inference framework to a new class of models.
* Offers a practical acceleration technique with demonstrated benefits.
* Addresses the important issue of parameter identifiability.
* Demonstrates competitive performance on a variety of datasets.

**Con:**

* Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and depth.
* Theoretical derivations are dense and potentially inaccessible to some readers.
* Assumptions need more careful discussion.
* Lack of formal computational complexity analysis.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent variable models. The theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs is significant, and the proposed acceleration technique is a practical improvement.  While the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive and the theoretical presentation clearer, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the following:

1. **Expand the experimental evaluation:** Include more datasets, particularly larger-scale ones, and provide a more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., more recent IBP variants, optimized VB implementations).
2. **Clarify the theoretical derivations:**  Add more intuitive explanations and examples to make the derivations more accessible.
3. **Discuss assumptions in greater detail:**  Elaborate on the assumptions made in the derivations and discuss their potential impact on the results.
4. **Provide a more detailed analysis of the merge post-processing step.** Show the performance of the algorithm with and without merging.
5. **Consider including a brief computational complexity analysis.**
