- Paper extends FAB inference from mixture models to latent feature models (binary features, linear-Gaussian observations).
- Main theoretical claim (Lemma 1): per-dimension Hessian log-determinant yields same “log count” regularizer as FAB for mixtures despite non-block-diagonal structure.
- Derives an FIC approximation for LFMs with a mean-field EM-like lower bound that induces automatic feature shrinkage and asymptotically ignores priors.
- E-step/π̃-updates are interpreted as CCCP, used to accelerate shrinkage.
- Identifiability analysis argues FAB favors parsimonious solutions and proposes a merge post-processing step.
- Experiments compare FAB-LFM to variational EM, IBP (Gibbs), VB, and MEIBP, showing competitive/superior model selection and predictive performance and lower cost than IBP/VB.
- Technical soundness: asymptotic argument is plausible but Lemma 1’s “mild assumptions” are deferred and several equations appear inconsistent or unexplained.
- Specific equation concerns: unusual “−|P|” term in J(Z,P) and unclear constants like “−2D + K log N” that conflict with standard Laplace terms.
- Mean-field approximation departs from exact FIC; paper should clarify that asymptotic tightness no longer strictly holds and when the gap is small.
- M-step claim of being “exactly the same as PPCA” is doubtful; need explicit closed-form updates and clarity on noise assumptions (diagonal vs isotropic Λ).
- CCCP acceleration view is reasonable; need to state if multiple inner CCCP steps preserve monotonicity and how pruning/merge steps affect the lower bound.
- Theorem 4 and merge heuristic are sensible; more detail needed on acceptance criteria (ΔL > 0) and sensitivity to distance thresholds.
- Clarity issues: key derivations compressed, missing explicit assumptions for Lemma 1, and several symbols are undefined on first use.
- Request to fix/justify constants in Eqs. (7)–(10), provide exact M-step formulas, and define notation (Θ vs P, DZ, Dk, |P|).
- Originality: novel and nontrivial extension of FAB to LFMs; CCCP use is practical though not new; identifiability and merge are useful contributions.
- Significance: a prior-free automated model-selection method for LFMs is valuable if asymptotic link to marginal likelihood holds under stated assumptions.
- Empirical concerns: fairness questions about noise variance handling, IBP α setting, omitted VB baselines, lack of per-iteration cost and confidence intervals.
- Pros: strong conceptual contribution, clean regularizer for automatic pruning, practical acceleration, and empirical evidence of good selection and runtimes.
- Cons: insufficient justification for Lemma 1 and FIC constants, ambiguous M-step claims, potential experimental fairness issues, limited sensitivity and scalability analysis.
- Suggestions: bring Lemma 1 assumptions into main text, correct equations, provide explicit M-step and noise-model details, add sensitivity analyses and small-scale VB, clarify monotonicity with acceleration/merge, and report complexity/memory.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending clearer derivations, fairer experiments, and more explicit algorithmic details; reviewer confidence medium.
