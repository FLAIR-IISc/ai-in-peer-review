- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixture models to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), deriving a comparable FIC despite LFMs not meeting original Hessian conditions.  
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method by relating the FAB E-step to a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP), improving computational efficiency.  
- Proves parameter identifiability and justifies a post-processing feature-merge step to remove redundant latent features.  
- Positions FAB/LFM as a competitive, automated alternative to BNP and VB methods (e.g., IBP, VB, MAP-IBP) without requiring priors or hand-tuned hyperparameters.  
- Theoretical contribution: key Lemma 1 and derivation of FIC for LFMs are significant and well-motivated.  
- Computational contribution: empirical speedups from the accelerateShrinkage routine address a common bottleneck.  
- Practical benefit: automated model selection simplifies application by eliminating hyperparameter tuning.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on synthetic and real data show competitive predictive performance and scalability versus IBP, VB, and EM.  
- Presentation: generally clear mathematical derivations and a well-defined algorithm, though some parts assume familiarity with CCCP.  
- Weakness: underlying LFM formulation is standard; main novelty is in inference rather than model design.  
- Weakness: reliance on a mean-field factorized variational approximation which may limit performance; more discussion needed.  
- Weakness: experimental hyperparameter choices for baselines (e.g., IBP α=3) need stronger justification.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 is complex (nested loops and subroutines); pseudocode or simplification would aid readability.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of failure cases or scenarios where FAB/LFMs may be outperformed.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant and practical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity, experimental justification, and failure-mode discussion.
