- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), despite LFMs not having block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Proves LFMs yield the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as mixture models (Lemma 1, Theorem 2).  
- Develops an FAB/LFM algorithm with a novel shrinkage-acceleration technique motivated by a CCCP interpretation.  
- Provides a formal identifiability analysis for LFMs and proposes a merge post-processing step.  
- Presents rigorous theoretical proofs (detailed in supplementary material) supporting the main claims.  
- Comprehensive experiments (synthetic, block, real-world) showing FAB/LFM achieves better model selection accuracy and much lower computational cost than IBP, VB, and MEIBP.  
- Clear exposition and well-organized presentation, though math is dense and may require prior FAB knowledge.  
- Original contributions: theoretical extension of FAB to LFMs, CCCP-based acceleration, identifiability proof.  
- Demonstrates practical significance for large-scale latent feature model selection and real-world tasks (link prediction, missing-value prediction).  
- Strengths: novel theory, rigorous proofs, practical algorithm with acceleration, strong empirical results, close theory–practice alignment.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on when asymptotic FIC assumptions break down for small N; lacks comparison to some recent LFM methods; deeper analysis of shrinkage-acceleration effects on convergence would help.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant theoretical and practical contribution suitable for NIPS.
