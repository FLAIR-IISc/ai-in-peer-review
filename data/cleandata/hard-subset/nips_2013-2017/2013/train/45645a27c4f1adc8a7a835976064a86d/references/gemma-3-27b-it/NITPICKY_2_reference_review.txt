This paper presents an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), addressing a limitation of previous FAB work requiring a block-diagonal Hessian. The authors demonstrate through asymptotic analysis that LFMs exhibit similar properties to mixture models allowing for application of FAB. They further propose a shrinkage acceleration method based on a connection to the convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and analyze parameter identifiability, offering a post-processing step to merge redundant latent features.

While the core idea of extending FAB to LFMs is conceptually sound and potentially valuable, the paper suffers from a substantial number of minor flaws, methodological omissions, and a lack of rigorous experimental validation that, cumulatively, raise serious concerns. The writing quality, while generally understandable, is often imprecise and lacks sufficient detail, hindering reproducibility and critical evaluation. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Contribution:** The derivation showing the applicability of FAB to LFMs despite the lack of a block-diagonal Hessian is a noteworthy theoretical result. Lemma 1 is the central contribution.
*   **CCCP Connection & Acceleration:**  Relating FAB E-steps to CCCP and deriving the subsequent shrinkage acceleration is a clever optimization and potentially impactful in practice.
*   **Identifiability Analysis:** The discussion of parameter identifiability and the merging post-processing step are valuable additions, addressing a common concern in latent variable modeling.
*   **Automated Model Selection:** Maintaining the automation and lack of hyperparameter tuning characteristic of FAB is a positive aspect.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Insufficient Detail in Derivations:** The proofs and assumptions referenced in the supplementary materials are crucial, yet the paper lacks sufficient detail *within* the main text to fully understand the derivation of the FICs for LFMs.  For example, the "mild assumptions" required for Lemma 1 are not adequately specified, hindering verification.
*   **Vague Notation:** While the paper defines notation, the frequent use of  'P' to represent both the parameter set and a probability distribution is confusing. Similarly, the definition of 'Θ' is introduced late in the paper without explaining its connection to 'P'.
*   **Incomplete Algorithm Description:** The description of the merge post-processing algorithm is extremely brief. The criterion for determining "overlapping features" (Euclidean distance of W) feels arbitrary and lacks justification. What threshold is used? Is it sensitive to the threshold value?
*   **Experimental Setup Concerns:** The experimental evaluation is deeply flawed. The choice of fixing the noise standard deviation in the simulations to a value used in *toy data* then applying it to real-world datasets is problematic. The authors acknowledge this in a footnote but fail to justify it adequately.  The comparison to other methods (IBP, VB, MEIBP) is uneven; different software packages were used, and parameter settings were not consistently optimized. The explanation for omitting VB due to long computation time is acceptable, but doesn't address why certain methods weren't run on larger datasets.
*   **Limited Baselines:** While the inclusion of IBP and VB is good, comparing against a standard EM implementation *without* the FAB regularization would have provided a clearer baseline.
*   **Overstated Claims:** The statement that FAB "achieves as good or even better model selection accuracy as state-of-the-art non-parametric Bayesian (NPB) methods" in the introduction is not convincingly supported by the experimental results presented. The results are mixed, with FAB performing well on some datasets but not others.
*   **Minor Errors & Inconsistencies:** Numerous minor errors exist:  Equation (2) contains a subscript "̂" on P that appears inconsistent with the surrounding text. The notation in Equation (4) is unclear regarding the summation indices. The term "RHS" in Equation (10) is not defined until later.

**Pro & Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   The theoretical contribution of extending FAB to LFMs is novel and potentially significant.
*   The proposed acceleration method could improve the practical applicability of FAB.
*   The identifiability analysis and merging post-processing address important practical concerns.

**Con:**

*   The lack of detail in derivations and algorithm descriptions hinders reproducibility and evaluation.
*   The experimental setup is flawed and does not provide strong evidence to support the claims made.
*   The numerous minor errors and inconsistencies detract from the paper's credibility.
*   The significance of the work is overstated based on the presented results.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potentially interesting theoretical contribution, the paper’s numerous shortcomings significantly outweigh its strengths. The lack of rigor in the derivations, the flawed experimental setup, and the numerous minor errors make it difficult to assess the true value of the proposed method.  The paper feels incomplete and lacks the polish expected of a NIPS publication.  

**Recommendation:** **Reject.** 

The authors should address the issues raised in this review by providing more detailed derivations, conducting a more rigorous experimental evaluation with appropriate baselines and justified parameter settings, and carefully proofreading the manuscript to eliminate errors and inconsistencies.  A significantly revised version of this paper might be suitable for future consideration.
