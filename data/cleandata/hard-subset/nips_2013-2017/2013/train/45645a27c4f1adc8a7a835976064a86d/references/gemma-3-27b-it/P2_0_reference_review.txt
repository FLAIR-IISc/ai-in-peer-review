## Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

**Summary:**

This paper extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework, previously successful with Mixture Models (MMs), to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). FAB is a fully automated Bayesian inference method that avoids explicit priors and hyperparameter tuning. The authors demonstrate that despite LFMs not initially satisfying the conditions for FAB’s application, a theoretical analysis reveals a shared structure in the Hessian of the log-likelihood. They further contribute by relating FAB E-steps to a Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP), leveraging this connection for a shrinkage acceleration method, and proving parameter identifiability, offering a guide for post-processing.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The core contribution – extending FAB to LFMs by demonstrating the applicability of the underlying FIC despite the lack of initial condition satisfaction – is significant. The Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 provide a solid theoretical foundation.
* **Practical Algorithm Improvements:** The CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) is a valuable addition, demonstrably reducing computational cost in experiments.
* **Identifiability Analysis:**  The identifiability result (Theorem 4) is important for understanding the behavior of the algorithm and provides a principled approach to merging latent features, leading to more interpretable models.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments, including both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrate the competitive performance of FAB/LFMs compared to existing methods like IBP and VB, particularly in terms of predictive performance and model selection (dimensionality estimation).  The ablation study showing the benefit of the shrinkage acceleration is also strong.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the theoretical derivations and algorithmic steps.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments are thorough, the comparison is sometimes limited to specific parameter settings for the competing methods (e.g., fixing the noise standard deviation).  A more comprehensive parameter sweep for alternatives would further strengthen the comparison.
* **Computational Cost in Larger Datasets:** While accelerated, FAB still seems to struggle with some of the larger real-world datasets (e.g., USPS) compared to faster methods like MEIBP.  Further optimization of the algorithm is warranted.
* **Connection to Existing Work:**  While the related work section touches on MAD-Bayes, a more detailed discussion comparing the nuances of the asymptotic assumptions (noise variance vs. prior) would be beneficial.
* **Merge Post-processing:**  The discussion of the merge post-processing feels somewhat underdeveloped.  While the theoretical justification is present, it’s not clear how robust or critical this step is to overall performance. The paper notes no observed merging in the small example.



**Assessment of Review Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound, with strong theoretical derivations and supporting experimental results. The proofs appear rigorous (although a complete verification is beyond the scope of this review). It presents a complete, well-considered piece of work, with honest discussion of potential limitations.
* **Clarity (8/10):**  The paper is mostly clearly written. Some sections (particularly the theoretical derivations) are dense and require careful reading, but the key ideas are conveyed effectively.  Better visualization of the effects of the shrinkage acceleration and merging could be useful.
* **Originality (8/10):** Extending FAB to LFMs is a novel contribution. The connection to CCCP and the identifiability analysis further enhance the originality. While building on previous FAB work, it presents a significant advance.
* **Significance (8/10):** The automated model selection capability, coupled with good performance, makes this work significant.  The potential for faster inference in LFM applications is valuable. The insights into parameter identifiability are also impactful.




**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

*  Presents a novel and theoretically grounded extension of a promising Bayesian inference framework.
*  Demonstrates strong empirical performance, competitive with state-of-the-art methods.
*  Introduces valuable algorithmic improvements (shrinkage acceleration).
*  Addresses a fundamental issue in LFM inference – automated dimensionality selection.

**Against Acceptance:**

*  Computational cost remains a concern for very large datasets.
*  Some aspects of the experimental comparison could be more comprehensive.
* The practical impact of the merge post-processing isn't fully established.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** 

This is a strong paper with significant contributions. The minor revisions suggested would further strengthen the work and address the few remaining concerns. Specifically, I recommend:

1.  Expanding the experimental evaluation to include a broader parameter sweep for competing methods.
2.  Providing a more detailed analysis of the merge post-processing step.
3. Addressing the comparisons to MAD-Bayes with regards to the different asymptotic assumptions.
