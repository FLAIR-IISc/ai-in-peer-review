This paper presents an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing a limitation of previous FAB implementations requiring a block-diagonal Hessian. The authors demonstrate that LFMs, despite not strictly adhering to this condition, exhibit equivalent behavior in terms of the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC). They further propose a shrinkage acceleration method based on a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) interpretation of the FAB E-step and establish parameter identifiability, leading to a merging post-processing step.

The central idea of extending FAB to LFMs is indeed a worthwhile contribution, potentially offering a computationally efficient alternative to existing nonparametric and variational Bayesian methods. The theoretical justification, demonstrating the equivalence of FIC forms despite the absence of the block-diagonal Hessian condition, is a key strength. However, the paper suffers from a significant number of shortcomings, ranging from minor notational inconsistencies to potentially misleading claims and a lack of rigorous comparison.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Extension:** Applying FAB inference to LFMs represents a novel direction for this approximation technique.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The demonstration of FIC equivalence provides a sound basis for the extension.
*   **Practical Acceleration:** The proposed shrinkage acceleration method, rooted in CCCP, offers a tangible improvement in computational efficiency.
*   **Identifiability Analysis:** The identifiability proof and resulting merge post-processing are valuable theoretical contributions.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Notational Inconsistency:** The notation is inconsistent. For instance, the definition of `P` changes throughout the paper (parameter set vs. a single parameter). Similarly, `D` is used to denote both dimensionality and parameter dimensionality, creating ambiguity. `a_{ij}` is defined only in the introduction, but is never used.
*   **Lack of Clarity in Lemma 1:** The statement of Lemma 1 and its “mild assumptions” are vague. A precise statement of the assumptions is crucial, as they fundamentally underpin the entire derivation. Referencing “supplementary materials” is insufficient – core assumptions need to be clearly stated in the main text.
*   **Overselling Convergence:** The statement that FIC "converges to a marginal log-likelihood" requires careful qualification. While asymptotically true, the rate of convergence and the practical implications for finite sample sizes are not discussed.
*   **Weak Experimental Validation:** The experiments are insufficiently rigorous.
    *   **Artificial Simulations:** While demonstrating speedup with shrinkage, the results don't convincingly show *superiority* in terms of accuracy over established methods. A more comprehensive comparison with different noise levels and dimensionality would be beneficial.
    *   **Block Data:** The comparison only shows the *early* learning phase *and* after convergence, but doesn't track the learning trajectory. This makes it hard to assess the practical benefits of FAB.
    *   **Real-World Data:** The justification for fixing the noise standard deviation to 0.75 for the real datasets is weak. The fact that MEIBP couldn’t complete a single iteration for some datasets raises concerns about its fair comparison. The table lacks standard deviations for PLL and TLL.
*   **Merge Post-Processing:** The description of the merge post-processing is superficial. The criteria for merging are not clearly defined (Euclidean distance alone may be insufficient).
*   **Repetitive Explanations:** Certain concepts (e.g., the regularization effect) are repeated unnecessarily throughout the paper.
*   **Minor Technical Issues:** Equation (14) seems to use `µt-1nk` when the previous equation uses `µtnk`. This inconsistency is jarring.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   Novel application of FAB to LFMs.
*   Solid theoretical foundation for the extension.
*   Potential for computational efficiency.

**Con:**

*   Numerous notational inconsistencies and lack of clarity.
*   Insufficiently rigorous experimental evaluation.
*   Overselling of convergence properties.
*   Vague assumptions and missing details in key theoretical derivations.
*   Repetitive explanations and overall lack of conciseness.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potentially valuable contribution of extending FAB inference to LFMs, the paper is currently unacceptable for NIPS. The numerous minor flaws, combined with the lack of a truly compelling empirical validation, undermine the credibility of the work. The authors need to address the notational inconsistencies, clarify the theoretical assumptions, provide a more rigorous experimental evaluation (including proper error bars and comparisons to a wider range of methods), and streamline the presentation. A significantly revised version with these improvements could be reconsidered. The current state lacks the polish and rigor expected of a NIPS publication.



**Rating:** Reject.
