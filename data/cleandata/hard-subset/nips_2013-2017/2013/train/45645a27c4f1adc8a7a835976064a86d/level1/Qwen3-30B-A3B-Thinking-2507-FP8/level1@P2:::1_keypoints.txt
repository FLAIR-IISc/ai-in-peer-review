- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs), overcoming previous applicability limits.  
- Shows LFMs, despite non-block-diagonal Hessian, yield the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) form as mixture models.  
- Enables practical FAB-based model selection for LFMs, avoiding costly nonparametric Bayesian or variational methods.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with key Lemma 1 and Theorem 2, proofs in supplementary material.  
- Demonstrates FAB performs especially well for large N, consistent with its asymptotic basis.  
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets (EEG, Piano, yaleB, USPS, Libras) comparing to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Proposes a well-designed FAB/LFM algorithm with shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) and merge post-processing for efficiency.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and uses consistent, clear mathematical notation.  
- Includes reproducible implementation details and pseudocode (Algorithms 1 and 2).  
- CCCP interpretation (Section 3.1) is clearly explained and helps contextualize optimization.  
- Original insight: the log ∑n znk term arises naturally in LFMs, enabling FAB extension — a novel theoretical contribution.  
- Empirically achieves better model selection, predictive performance (higher PLL), lower computational cost, and more compact models.  
- Strengths: theoretical novelty, rigorous proofs, novel shrinkage acceleration, extensive experiments, reproducibility, clear relation to prior work.  
- Weaknesses: reduced accuracy for small N and limited analysis of exact conditions causing this limitation.  
- Weaknesses: merge post-processing is described but not deeply analyzed for frequency or necessity in practice.  
- Weaknesses: theoretical limits of FAB for LFMs compared to other asymptotic methods are not fully explored.  
- Pro-accept: significant theoretical and empirical advance, clear writing, reproducible, and computationally efficient.  
- Con-accept: asymptotic nature may limit small-dataset applicability and comparison to other asymptotic approaches is incomplete.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and recommended for acceptance.
