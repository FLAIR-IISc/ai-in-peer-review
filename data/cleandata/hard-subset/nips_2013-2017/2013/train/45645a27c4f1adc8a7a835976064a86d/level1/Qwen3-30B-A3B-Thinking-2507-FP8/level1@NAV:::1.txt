

This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming a key limitation that previously prevented FAB's application to LFMs. The authors demonstrate that LFMs, despite not satisfying the block-diagonal Hessian condition required for standard FAB, share the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) structure as mixture models through their Lemma 1 and Theorem 2. This theoretical insight enables FAB/LFMs to achieve automatic hidden state selection, parameter identifiability, and computational efficiency, as empirically validated against state-of-the-art methods including Indian Buffet Process (IBP) and variational Bayesian approaches.

The paper excels in theoretical quality with rigorous asymptotic analysis and clear proofs in supplementary materials. The authors successfully address a significant gap in the FAB framework, building upon previous NIPS work by Fujimaki et al. (2013, 2014) on FAB for mixture models and latent variable models. The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating FAB/LFMs' superiority in model selection accuracy, prediction performance, and computational efficiency across artificial and real-world datasets. The proposed shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2) effectively reduces computational costs by eliminating irrelevant features early.

Strengths: The theoretical contribution is sound and significant, the empirical evaluation is thorough and well-designed, and the paper clearly articulates the connection between FAB and LFMs. The automatic model selection capability represents a meaningful advance over existing methods.

Weaknesses: The paper could better contextualize how this work differs from the recently published "Scaling the Indian Buffet Process via Submodular Maximization" (Reed & Ghahramani, ICML 2015), which also addresses model selection for IBPs. Some technical details about the Hessian analysis could be clarified for broader accessibility.

Pro: Strong theoretical foundation, clear demonstration of practical advantages, significant extension of FAB framework. Con: Some contextualization with very recent IBP work could be improved.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a substantial contribution to the field of Bayesian model selection for latent variable models. The significance is evident in both theoretical advancement and practical utility, with clear potential for adoption by practitioners. The paper meets all NIPS quality standards and should be accepted.
