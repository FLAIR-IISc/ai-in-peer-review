- Theoretical extension: shows LFMs satisfy asymptotic conditions allowing FIC derivation despite lacking a block-diagonal Hessian.  
- Key insight: the log determinant of the LFM Hessian produces the log(∑_n z_nk) term, yielding an identical FIC form to mixture models.  
- Enables FAB to automatically select feature dimensionality without hyperparameters, offering an alternative to IBP methods.  
- Practical contribution: shrinkage acceleration via CCCP (Algorithm 2) significantly reduces computational cost without sacrificing performance.  
- Identifiability analysis (Theorem 4) provides theoretical understanding and a merge post-processing step that improves model compactness.  
- Experiments: comprehensive comparisons on synthetic and real data show FAB achieves smaller K, higher predictive log-likelihood (PLL), and lower compute than competitors.  
- Strength: rigorous derivations (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) and solid asymptotic analysis.  
- Strength: novel practical methods (shrinkage acceleration and merge post-processing) with demonstrated empirical benefits.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of when the asymptotic approximation fails for small N (no concrete thresholds or guidance).  
- Weakness: incomplete comparison with variational baselines—VB omitted despite being a standard LFM baseline.  
- Weakness: connection to singular model theory (Watanabe) and broader literature is underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of trade-offs; FAB may not uniformly outperform others on prediction for small datasets (e.g., Libras).  
- Significance: a non-trivial, novel theoretical advance with practical applicability and empirical gains.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong theoretical and practical contributions despite asymptotic and comparative limitations.
