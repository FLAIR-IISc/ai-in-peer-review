- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing shared-parameter challenges.
- Key theoretical result (Lemma 1): despite a non-block-diagonal Hessian, the log-determinant asymptotically decomposes to yield a penalty sum_k log(sum_n z_nk).
- New FIC for LFMs enables automatic model selection by shrinking the effective number of features.
- Derives an efficient alternating optimization algorithm (FAB/LFM) from the LFM-specific FIC.
- Interprets the variational update as a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) and introduces an accelerateShrinkage scheme.
- Proves parameter identifiability under the FAB framework and proposes a principled merge post-processing for redundant features.
- Positions FAB/LFM as a computationally efficient, accurate alternative to IBP-based nonparametric methods.
- Strong theoretical contribution that broadens FAB applicability and suggests a unifying principle for discrete latent models.
- High technical quality: clear derivation, sound analyses (CCCP, identifiability), and practical algorithmic improvements.
- Comprehensive experiments: faster than sampling/VB, more accurate feature recovery in synthetic tests, and strong predictive performance on real data.
- Paper is very well written, clear, and well-organized, with good relation to prior work.
- Main theoretical proof (Lemma 1) is relegated to the supplement and would benefit from more intuition or a proof sketch in the main text.
- Critical assumptions for Lemma 1 are not stated in the main paper and should be clarified to define method scope.
- Suggested deeper connection to singular learning theory (RLCT) and two experimental clarifications: sensitivity analysis for T_shrink and consistent treatment of noise variance across baselines.
