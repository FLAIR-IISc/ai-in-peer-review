- Contribution: Extends FAB inference to Latent Feature Models by proving (Lemma 1) that LFMs yield a complexity term of sum_k log(sum_n z_nk), enabling a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC).
- Algorithm: Derives an EM-like algorithm maximizing a tractable FIC lower bound with a unique regularization in the responsibility updates that drives automatic feature shrinkage.
- Refinements: Interprets updates as a Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) enabling an accelerateShrinkage heuristic, and analyzes identifiability to motivate a merge post-processing step.
- Positioning: Presents a computationally efficient, frequentist-flavored alternative to nonparametric Bayesian methods like the IBP with fewer hyperparameters.
- Originality/significance: Non-trivial generalization of FAB to models without block-diagonal Hessian; Lemma 1 is an elegant, impactful insight.
- Technical quality: Asymptotic analysis and derivations are sound; Section 3 and Theorem 4 strengthen practical and theoretical grounding.
- Clarity/organization: Well-written and logically presented, linking theory to the algorithm’s shrinkage behavior.
- Empirical validation: Thorough comparisons to IBP baselines show faster runtime, comparable or better predictive performance, and more parsimonious models with reduced overfitting.
- Weakness — asymptotics: Reliance on N → ∞ may limit small-sample applicability; more discussion on failure modes for small N would help.
- Weakness — hidden assumptions: Key “mild assumptions” for Lemma 1 are deferred to the supplement; briefly stating them in the main text would improve self-containedness.
- Weakness — baselines: Missing a simple baseline comparison (e.g., EM+ BIC) to directly contrast the FIC penalty with BIC’s K log N.
- Weakness — implementation details: accelerateShrinkage introduces Tshrink (set to 100) without sensitivity analysis, and merge() details are relegated to the supplement.
- Acceptance — pro: Strong theoretical novelty, practical and efficient model-selection method, comprehensive empirical support, and high-quality writing.
- Acceptance — con: Dependence on asymptotic theory and some important details placed in the supplement reduce immediate applicability and self-containedness.
