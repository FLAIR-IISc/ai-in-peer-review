The paper proposes a factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework extended to latent feature models (LFMs). The core contribution lies in demonstrating that the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as that for mixture models, despite LFMs not satisfying the initial conditions required for FAB inference. This allows the application of FAB to LFMs, offering benefits like automatic hidden state selection and parameter identifiability. The authors also present a shrinkage acceleration method based on a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) and analyze parameter identifiability, leading to a merging post-processing step for latent features.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the theoretical extensions of FAB to LFMs. The motivation is clearly stated, and the connection to prior work on FAB, LFMs, and related methods like Indian Buffet Processes (IBP) is thoroughly established. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs is a significant technical contribution, and the CCCP-based acceleration method appears promising. The inclusion of identifiability analysis and the corresponding merging post-processing adds further value. The experimental section includes comparisons with several relevant methods on both synthetic and real-world datasets, providing a comprehensive evaluation.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Extending FAB inference to LFMs is a novel contribution, addressing a limitation of the original framework. The demonstration of FIC equivalence between LFMs and MMs is insightful.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis, particularly the derivation of the FIC for LFMs (Lemma 1 and Theorem 2), appears rigorous and well-motivated. The CCCP interpretation and identifiability analysis are also technically sound.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical derivations are presented clearly, and the explanations of the algorithm and its components are easy to follow.
*   **Completeness:** The paper provides a complete description of the FAB/LFM algorithm, including the E-step, M-step, acceleration method, and post-processing steps.
*   **Empirical Evaluation:** The experimental section is comprehensive, including comparisons on both synthetic and real-world data, using various evaluation metrics.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Assumptions:** The paper mentions "some mild assumptions" in Lemma 1 without fully detailing them in the main body (referencing supplementary materials). This slightly hinders a full assessment of the derivation's generality.
*   **Computational Complexity:** While the shrinkage acceleration is claimed to reduce computational cost, a more detailed complexity analysis comparing FAB/LFM with other methods (e.g., IBP, VB) would be beneficial. The authors state MEIBP is faster, but lack a detailed comparison of complexity.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The sensitivity of FAB/LFM to hyperparameter settings (e.g., the tolerance δ for convergence) is not thoroughly investigated.
*   **Limited Novelty in Experiments:** The experimental setup closely follows existing literature. While the results are positive, introducing a novel experimental paradigm or dataset could further strengthen the contribution.
*   **Merge Post-processing:** The paper mentions that the merge post-processing only had limited effects. The benefit of including it, in the first place, then becomes questionable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of FAB inference to a new class of models (LFMs).
*   The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides valuable insights into the properties of FAB/LFMs.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach compared to existing methods.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to understand and follow.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The assumptions underlying the key technical results (Lemma 1) are not fully detailed in the main body.
*   A more thorough computational complexity analysis is needed to fully assess the scalability of the proposed approach.
*   The limited impact of the merge post-processing reduces the significance of that specific contribution.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent feature models. The novelty of extending FAB to LFMs, the technical soundness of the derivation, and the comprehensive experimental evaluation outweigh the minor weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the details of the assumptions in Lemma 1 and providing a more detailed analysis of computational complexity. A discussion on parameter sensitivity would also strengthen the paper.
