- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), overcoming prior applicability limitations.  
- Proves via Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 that LFMs share the same Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) structure as mixture models despite Hessian differences.  
- Enables automatic hidden-state selection, parameter identifiability, and computational efficiency for FAB/LFMs.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods (IBP, variational Bayes) in model selection accuracy, prediction performance, and speed on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Proposes a shrinkage acceleration method (Algorithm 2) that reduces computation by eliminating irrelevant features early.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous with clear asymptotic analysis and supplementary proofs.  
- Builds on and substantially extends prior FAB work (Fujimaki et al. 2013, 2014) by filling a significant gap.  
- Weakness: needs better contextualization relative to recent ICML 2015 work (Reed & Ghahramani) on scaling the IBP.  
- Weakness: some technical details of the Hessian analysis could be clarified for broader accessibility.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality, significant contribution to Bayesian model selection for latent-variable models; recommended for acceptance.
