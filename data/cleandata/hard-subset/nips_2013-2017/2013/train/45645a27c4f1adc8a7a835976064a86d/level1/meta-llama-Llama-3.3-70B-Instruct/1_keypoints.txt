- Extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) by resolving Hessian-related challenges.  
- Provides asymptotic analysis of the LFM Hessian and shows the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs matches the form for mixture models.  
- Derives FAB inference for LFMs enabling automatic hidden-state selection and parameter identifiability.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes thorough background and related work.  
- Presents a detailed derivation of FIC for LFMs and proposes a shrinkage acceleration method to reduce computational cost.  
- Experiments show FAB/LFM outperforms state-of-the-art IBP and variational Bayesian methods in model selection, prediction, and computational efficiency.  
- Strength: rigorous asymptotic Hessian analysis is a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Strength: the FAB/LFM framework offers fully automated model selection.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate superiority in selection, prediction, and speed.  
- Weakness: assumes a specific prior form for latent variables, which may limit applicability.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art (e.g., nonparametric Bayesian) methods.  
- Weakness: could include more discussion on limitations and potential extensions.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
