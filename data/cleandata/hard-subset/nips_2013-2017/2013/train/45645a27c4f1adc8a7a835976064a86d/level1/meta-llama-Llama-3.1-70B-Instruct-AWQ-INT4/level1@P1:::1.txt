This paper proposes an extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), which have been a challenging class of models for FAB inference due to the lack of a specific condition on the Hessian matrix of a complete log-likelihood. The authors provide an asymptotic analysis of the Hessian matrix of LFMs, showing that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as those for mixture models, despite the fact that LFMs do not satisfy the condition.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, with a clear introduction to the background and motivation of the work. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, including FAB inference for mixture models and infinite LFMs, and Indian Buffet Process. The technical contribution of the paper is sound, with a rigorous derivation of the FIC for LFMs and a clear explanation of the FAB algorithm for LFMs.

The paper also provides an analysis of the FAB algorithm, including a CCCP interpretation and a shrinkage acceleration method, which significantly reduces computational cost in practice. The authors also show that FAB inference resolves non-identifiability in LFMs, which is an important theoretical aspect in learning algorithms for latent variable models.

The experimental evaluation of the paper is thorough, with a comparison of FAB inference with existing methods, including state-of-the-art IBP methods, on both artificial and real-world data sets. The results show that FAB inference achieves good predictive performance with low computational cost, and that it is able to select a more compact and better model representation than the other methods.

Overall, the paper is a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian inference and latent variable models, and it has the potential to be a valuable resource for researchers and practitioners in the field.
