Summary and relation to prior work
The paper extends factorized asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and linear-Gaussian likelihood. The core technical contribution is an asymptotic analysis of the Hessian of the complete-data log-likelihood with respect to per-dimension parameters θd = (wd, bd, λd). Lemma 1 shows that log det F(d) = Σk log(Σn znk/N) + Op(1), implying that the marginal complete log-likelihood can be approximated by J(Z,P) = log p(X,Z|P) − |P| − (DK/2) log N − (D/2) Σk log Σn znk, leading to an FIC for LFMs identical in form to that for mixture models (MMs). Based on this FIC, the authors derive a tractable lower bound with a mean-field variational family q(Z) and give an EM-like FAB algorithm with a characteristic shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃k) in the E-step that prunes irrelevant features. They further reinterpret the E-step updates via a convex-concave procedure (CCCP), using multiple CCCP inner updates to accelerate shrinkage, and propose an identifiability result that suggests a merging post-processing when columns of W coincide.

Related work includes FAB for MMs and HMMs [Fujimaki & Morinaga, AISTATS’12; Fujimaki & Hayashi, ICML’12], the Indian Buffet Process (IBP) and its accelerations (Gibbs and VB) [Griffiths & Ghahramani; Doshi-Velez et al. AISTATS’09], and recent MAP/submodular approximations (MEIBP) [Reed & Ghahramani, ICML’13]. The paper also situates its analysis within Watanabe’s theory of singular models and Laplace approximations.

Quality (technical soundness)
- The main asymptotic result (Lemma 1) is plausible: for the LFM likelihood p(x·d|Z,θd), the Hessian in θd depends on Z through Z^T Z, and the determinant’s leading term should scale like Πk Σn znk up to constants if off-diagonals are controlled. However, the paper defers all assumptions and proof details to the supplement. For a result that enables the entire FIC, a statement of the key conditions in the main text is needed (e.g., bounds on cross-feature co-activations Σn znkznℓ relative to Σn znk, ℓ≠k, regularity of λd, absence of degeneracy in Z). As written, “mild assumptions” is too vague to judge generality.
- The transition from (7) to an FIC lower bound follows prior FAB work and is careful about monotonicity. The additional mean-field restriction on q(Z) makes the bound looser; the paper asserts limited degradation and cites VB practice, but an empirical ablation (with/without MF) would strengthen the case.
- The CCCP view is correct: L(q) is a sum of a convex −(D/2) Σk log Σn µnk term and concave terms; linearizing the convex part reproduces the E-step fixed-point update. The shrinkage acceleration is sensible and appears effective empirically.
- The identifiability theorem asserts that equal columns w·k = w·ℓ imply equal expected usages, supporting a merging heuristic. This is a useful property, but practical near-collinearity (w·k ≈ w·ℓ) is common; providing a perturbation analysis or a principled merging criterion with guarantees would improve robustness.

Clarity
- The exposition is generally clear, with a nice connection from the Hessian analysis to the FIC form and to algorithmic consequences (automatic pruning). However, crucial assumptions for Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 should be summarized in the main text. Some algorithmic steps (accelerateShrinkage, merge) are under-specified in the main paper; a brief pseudo-code and stopping criteria are helpful but thresholds and safeguards against premature pruning/merging need clarification.
- Minor issues: a few typos and formatting artifacts; ensure consistent notation for vectors (e.g., x̄n vs X̄).

Originality
- Extending FAB beyond models with block-diagonal Hessians to LFMs is a substantive conceptual advance. Showing that FIC for LFMs has the same penalty structure as for MMs despite feature sharing is novel and insightful. The CCCP interpretation is not new per se, but its use to accelerate FAB shrinkage is an interesting contribution. The identifiability perspective and merge post-processing are also useful, though partially heuristic.

Significance
- LFMs and IBP-based methods are widely used for feature discovery and link prediction. A fast, prior-insensitive, automatically shrinking alternative is valuable. If the Hessian asymptotics hold under broad conditions, this work could impact model selection in a range of latent feature models.
- Empirically, the method appears competitive or superior in K selection and predictive log-likelihood with favorable runtime on several datasets. However, some comparisons are potentially confounded by differing noise variance treatment and sensitive initialization. A more controlled experimental setup would strengthen claims.

Pros
- Clear theoretical insight: the LFM FIC penalty matches MM form via Hessian asymptotics.
- Practical, monotone, and simple algorithm with automatic feature pruning and no hand-tuned hyperparameters.
- Effective CCCP-based acceleration with demonstrated runtime benefits.
- Useful identifiability analysis leading to merge post-processing.
- Strong performance on several datasets, often with more compact K and less overfitting.

Cons
- Key assumptions and proofs are relegated to the supplement; scope of applicability is unclear in the main text.
- Mean-field restriction may weaken the FIC approximation; no ablation is shown.
- Experimental fairness: FAB learns noise variance while IBP/VB/MEIBP are run with fixed variance; hyperparameters for IBP may bias K upward; MEIBP sensitivity and non-negativity constraints complicate comparison.
- Potential over-aggressive shrinkage for high-D due to penalty scaling with D; no analysis of this scaling or sensitivity to Tshrink and δ.
- Merge heuristic lacks principled thresholds or guarantees for near-collinear features.

Questions/suggestions for authors
- State the precise conditions for Lemma 1 in the main text and discuss when they may fail (e.g., highly co-occurring features).
- Provide a proof sketch or intuition bounding the effect of off-diagonal entries of Z^T Z on log det F(d).
- Analyze the impact of dimensionality D on the shrinkage penalty and show sensitivity experiments (vary D while holding N, K fixed).
- Report results where all methods either learn or share a common fixed noise variance to ensure fairness; include IBP with learned variance if possible.
- Add an ablation on mean-field vs a richer q(Z) and on the number of CCCP inner steps Tshrink.
- Specify the merge criterion (distance threshold, acceptance by bound increase) and evaluate on synthetic near-collinear features.

Recommendation
This is a solid and interesting extension of FAB to LFMs with both theoretical and practical contributions. Conditional on clarifying assumptions and tightening the experimental comparisons, I lean toward acceptance.
