This paper proposes an extension of the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors provide a thorough analysis of the Hessian matrix of the log-likelihood of LFMs, which leads to the derivation of a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs. This FIC has the same form as that of Mixture Models (MMs), despite the fact that LFMs do not satisfy the conditions required for FAB inference in MMs.

The authors also propose a shrinkage acceleration method, which significantly reduces the computational cost of the FAB algorithm. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on artificial and real-world data sets, showing that FAB/LFMs achieve better model selection accuracy and predictive performance than state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process (IBP) methods.

Strengths of the paper:

1. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the Hessian matrix of the log-likelihood of LFMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs is a major achievement, as it allows for the application of FAB inference to a wider range of models.
3. The proposed shrinkage acceleration method is a significant improvement over the original FAB algorithm, making it more efficient and practical for large-scale data sets.
4. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of FAB/LFMs in achieving better model selection accuracy and predictive performance than state-of-the-art IBP methods.

Weaknesses of the paper:

1. The paper assumes a significant amount of background knowledge in Bayesian inference, latent variable models, and FAB inference, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
2. The notation and terminology used in the paper are not always clearly defined, which may lead to confusion.
3. The experimental results are mostly focused on the performance of FAB/LFMs, with limited comparison to other methods.
4. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential applications of FAB/LFMs.
