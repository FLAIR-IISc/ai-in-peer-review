- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing prior challenges.  
- Provides asymptotic analysis of the Hessian showing the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs matches the form for mixture models.  
- Delivers a rigorous derivation of the FIC and a clear explanation of the FAB algorithm for LFMs.  
- Presents algorithmic analysis including a CCCP interpretation and a shrinkage acceleration method that reduces computational cost.  
- Demonstrates that FAB inference resolves non-identifiability issues in LFMs.  
- Offers thorough experimental comparisons with state-of-the-art IBP methods on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Empirical results show good predictive performance, lower computational cost, and more compact/better model representations.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized with a clear introduction and motivation.  
- Provides a comprehensive related-work review (FAB for mixtures, infinite LFMs, Indian Buffet Process).  
- Strength: significant technical contribution in deriving FIC for LFMs.  
- Strength: FAB algorithm presentation is clear and easy to follow.  
- Strength: in-depth algorithm analysis and practical acceleration technique.  
- Strength: comprehensive and well-designed experimental evaluation.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in Bayesian inference and latent variable models, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: notation is sometimes confusing with multiple symbols for the same quantity.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of the FAB algorithm’s limitations and comparative weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel technical contribution, clear algorithm, strong experiments, good writing.  
- Arguments for rejection: accessibility issues, confusing notation, lack of limitations discussion.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
