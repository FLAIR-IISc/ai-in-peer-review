- Extends FAB to binary LFMs with linear-Gaussian likelihood via asymptotic Hessian analysis, yielding an FIC that matches mixture-model form.  
- Lemma 1: log det F(d) ≈ Σk log(Σn znk / N) + Op(1), leading to J(Z,P) and the stated FIC penalty.  
- Derives a tractable lower bound with mean-field q(Z) and an EM-like FAB algorithm featuring an E-step shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃k) that prunes features.  
- Reinterprets the E-step with CCCP; multiple CCCP inner updates accelerate shrinkage and improve runtime.  
- Proposes an identifiability result and a merging post-processing when columns of W coincide.  
- Situates work relative to FAB for MMs/HMMs, IBP methods (Gibbs/VB), MEIBP, and Watanabe’s singular-model theory.  
- Lemma 1 is plausible but main-text lacks explicit assumptions and proof details (bounds on cross-feature co-activations, λd regularity, Z non-degeneracy).  
- Mean-field restriction loosens the FIC bound; no ablation is provided to quantify this effect.  
- CCCP interpretation and shrinkage acceleration are correct and appear effective empirically.  
- Identifiability theorem supports merging but lacks perturbation analysis or principled criteria for near-collinear columns.  
- Exposition is generally clear, but key assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 and some algorithmic steps (accelerateShrinkage, merge) should be summarized in the main text.  
- Minor issues: typos, formatting artifacts, and inconsistent vector notation.  
- Originality: substantive advance extending FAB to non–block-diagonal Hessians in LFMs; CCCP use is an interesting practical contribution.  
- Significance: potentially impactful as a fast, prior-insensitive, automatic-pruning alternative for LFMs if Hessian asymptotics hold broadly.  
- Empirical results competitive, but comparisons may be confounded by differing noise-variance treatment, initialization sensitivity, and MEIBP constraints.  
- Pros: clear theoretical insight, simple monotone algorithm with automatic pruning, effective CCCP acceleration, useful identifiability analysis, strong empirical performance.  
- Cons: key assumptions/proofs relegated to supplement, possible weakening from mean-field, experimental fairness concerns, potential over-aggressive D-scaled shrinkage, and heuristic merge lacking guarantees.  
- Suggestions: state precise conditions for Lemma 1 in main text, give a proof sketch bounding off-diagonal effects, analyze shrinkage scaling with D, run controlled experiments with shared/learned noise variance, ablate mean-field vs richer q(Z) and Tshrink, and specify/evaluate a principled merge criterion.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying assumptions and tightening experimental comparisons.
