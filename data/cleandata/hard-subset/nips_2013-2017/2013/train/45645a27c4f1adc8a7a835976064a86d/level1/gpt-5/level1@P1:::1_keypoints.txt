- Extends FAB inference from mixtures/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs) via asymptotic Hessian analysis yielding a factorized information criterion (FIC).  
- Theorem 2: asymptotic expansion shows a penalty −(D/2)∑_k log ∑_n z_nk, producing an FIC identical in form to the mixture case.  
- Uses linearization to lower-bound the intractable expectation and derives an EM-like algorithm: mean-field E-step, closed-form M-step (W, Λ, b, π), and feature shrinkage/removal.  
- Provides a CCCP interpretation of µ and π̃ updates and an accelerateShrinkage routine that runs multiple CCCP steps per M-step to speed elimination.  
- Presents an identifiability analysis resolving duplicated W columns and proposes a merge post-processing that can increase the bound when features are redundant.  
- Empirical comparisons to IBP sampling, variational IBP, and MEIBP show strong model selection, competitive predictive performance, lower runtime than IBP/VB, and more compact models.  
- Central asymptotic result is plausible and important, but the paper defers proofs and does not list explicit assumptions in the main text (e.g., on Λ, Z moments, MLE stability, Fisher regularity).  
- CCCP reinterpretation is correct and the monotonic increase of the lower bound follows from the linearization and coordinate updates.  
- The identifiability theorem is useful but would be strengthened by more conditions and a proof sketch in the main text.  
- Empirical results show benefits from accelerated shrinkage, but comparisons may be biased (fixed noise variance for baselines, fixed IBP α, MEIBP initialization choices).  
- Recommends more careful tuning or time-budgeted comparisons and reporting per-iteration complexity and wall-clock breakdowns (FAB vs MEIBP).  
- Paper is generally well-organized and clear; derivations of FIC, algorithm, and CCCP view are succinct.  
- Suggests stating exact assumptions for Lemma 1 in the main text and giving intuition for why log det Hessian decomposes into ∑_k log ∑_n z_nk.  
- Requests explicit computational complexity per iteration in N, D, K for E-step, M-step, and accelerateShrinkage.  
- Recommends clarifying difference between prior-induced shrinkage via η(π_k) and model-induced shrinkage via the FIC term, ideally with an ablation.  
- Asks for detailed merging criteria (distance thresholds, effect on L) and pseudocode or code for merge(), plus reporting number of merges per dataset.  
- Originality: novel and nontrivial extension of FIC/FAB to LFMs without the block-diagonal Hessian condition; CCCP and merge heuristics are practical additions.  
- Significance: impactful for automated, prior-free model selection in LFMs, though currently restricted to linear-Gaussian models with diagonal noise; extensions would broaden impact.  
- Pros: principled asymptotic justification, simple scalable algorithm with monotonic bound improvement and automatic feature selection, useful CCCP acceleration, identifiability insight, solid empirical evidence.  
- Cons: main assumptions not detailed, potential bias in baseline comparisons, limited to diagonal linear-Gaussian LFMs, some implementation details (merge thresholds, complexity, T_shrink) under-specified.  
- Minor comments: clarify π vs π̃ updates and why accelerateShrinkage updates both; discuss behavior for always-on/off features and its impact on the FIC approximation; provide merge implementation details and merge counts.  
- Recommendation: reviewer leans toward acceptance, contingent on making assumptions explicit and ensuring fairer baseline tuning and comparisons.
