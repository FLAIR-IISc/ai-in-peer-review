This paper proposes an extension of the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors provide a thorough analysis of the Hessian matrix of the log-likelihood of LFMs, which allows them to derive a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs. This is a significant contribution, as FAB inference was previously limited to models that satisfy a specific condition on the Hessian matrix.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their methodology and theoretical results. The use of Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 to derive the FIC for LFMs is a notable achievement, as it shows that the FIC for LFMs has the same form as the FIC for mixture models. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the FAB algorithm for LFMs, including the E-step, M-step, and shrinkage step.

The experimental results presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of the FAB/LFM framework in terms of computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance. The comparison with other methods, such as Indian Buffet Process (IBP) and Variational EM (VB), shows that FAB/LFM achieves better performance in many cases.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified framework for FAB inference in LFMs, which is a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian inference. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the theoretical properties of the FAB/LFM framework, including the identifiability of the model parameters.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One potential weakness is that the paper assumes a specific form for the prior distribution on the model parameters, which may not be realistic in all cases. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the FAB/LFM algorithm, as well as a comparison with other methods in terms of computational efficiency.

* Quality: 9/10 (the paper is well-written and provides a thorough analysis of the FAB/LFM framework)
