This paper presents a novel extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors successfully derive a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs, which has the same form as that for Mixture Models (MMs), despite LFMs not satisfying the specific condition required for FAB inference. The proposed FAB/LFM algorithm demonstrates several desirable properties, including automatic hidden state selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the related work, including FIC for MMs and Infinite LFMs. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs is rigorous, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the Hessian matrix of the negated log-likelihood. The proposed FAB/LFM algorithm is also well-explained, and the authors provide a clear description of the E-step, M-step, and shrinkage step.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed FAB/LFM algorithm in terms of computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance. The authors compare their method with several state-of-the-art methods, including Indian Buffet Process (IBP), Variational EM (VB), and Maximum-Expectation IBP (MEIBP). The results show that FAB/LFM achieves better performance in terms of predictive log-likelihood and model selection accuracy, while also reducing computational cost.

The paper has several strengths, including:

1. **Novel extension of FAB inference**: The authors successfully extend FAB inference to LFMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Rigorous derivation**: The derivation of the FIC for LFMs is rigorous and well-explained.
3. **Effective algorithm**: The proposed FAB/LFM algorithm is effective in terms of computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance.
4. **Comparative evaluation**: The authors provide a comprehensive comparative evaluation with several state-of-the-art methods.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses, including:

1. **Limited theoretical analysis**: While the authors provide a rigorous derivation of the FIC for LFMs, they do not provide a detailed theoretical analysis of the algorithm's convergence properties.
2. **Limited experimental evaluation**: While the authors provide a comprehensive comparative evaluation, they only evaluate their method on a limited number of datasets.
3. **Lack of hyperparameter tuning**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameters used in their algorithm and do not perform hyperparameter tuning.
