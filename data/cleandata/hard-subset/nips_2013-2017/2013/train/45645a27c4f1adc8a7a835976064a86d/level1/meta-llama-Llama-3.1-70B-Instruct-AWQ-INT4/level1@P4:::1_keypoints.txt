- Extends the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) and derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.  
- The derived FIC for LFMs has the same form as the FIC for Mixture Models despite LFMs not meeting standard FAB conditions.  
- Proposes a FAB/LFM algorithm that achieves automatic hidden state selection and parameter identifiability.  
- The algorithm guarantees a monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound through iterative optimization.  
- The FIC derivation is rigorous and original, offering new theoretical insights into LFMs.  
- Experimental results show FAB/LFM outperforms existing methods (including IBP) in speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations of the FAB framework, extension to LFMs, and consistent notation.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of the algorithm’s theoretical properties.  
- Experimental comparisons are clearly presented and effectively demonstrate performance differences.  
- Assumes a strong background in Bayesian inference and LFMs, which reduces accessibility for non-experts.  
- The paper could give more intuition connecting the FIC derivation for LFMs to the FIC for MMs.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets; broader empirical validation would strengthen the work.  
- High potential significance for Bayesian inference and LFMs, with practical adoption possible given the performance gains.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous derivation, useful algorithmic properties, and strong empirical results; arguments against: accessibility, lack of intuition, and limited experiments.
