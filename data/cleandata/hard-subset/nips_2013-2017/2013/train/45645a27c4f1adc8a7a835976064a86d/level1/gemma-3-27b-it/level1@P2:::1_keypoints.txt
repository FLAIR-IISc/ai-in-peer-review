- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), removing the block-diagonal Hessian requirement.
- Shows LFMs share a similar Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) structure with Mixture Models, enabling FAB application.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including derivation of FIC for LFMs and proofs of identifiability.
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method, inspired by CCCP, to improve computational efficiency.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets shows competitive model selection and faster computation versus IBP, VB, and MEIBP.
- Paper is technically sound with well-motivated connections (e.g., to CCCP) and effective algorithmic contributions.
- Mathematical derivations are complex and dense, requiring careful reading and reliance on supplementary material.
- Discussion of limitations and validity conditions of the Laplace approximation is insufficiently detailed.
- Notation is mostly consistent but some variables should be explicitly defined earlier for clarity.
- Would benefit from a more intuitive explanation of the FIC and its relationship to marginal log-likelihood for non-expert readers.
- Novel contribution: first extension of FAB to LFMs and original analysis linking FIC structures between LFMs and MMs.
- Significant practical impact: more scalable, automated hidden-state selection and improved computational performance for large-scale LFMs.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical rigor, computational efficiency, strong empirical validation, and overall significance.
- Arguments against acceptance: dense theoretical complexity, limited discussion of Laplace approximation limits, and insufficient exploration of parameter sensitivity (e.g., δ, Tshrink).
- Overall recommendation: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and address approximation/parameter discussion.
