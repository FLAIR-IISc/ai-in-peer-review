## Review of "Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian Inference for Latent Feature Models"

This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference, a recently developed Bayesian approximation method, to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The core contribution lies in demonstrating that the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs takes the same form as that for Mixture Models, despite LFMs not initially satisfying the conditions required for standard FAB application. This allows the authors to leverage the desirable properties of FAB – automatic hidden state selection, parameter identifiability, and computational efficiency – to LFM model selection. They further present a shrinkage acceleration method based on a Convex Concave Procedure (CCCP) interpretation of the FAB E-step and analyze parameter identifiability, offering a guide for merging latent features.

The paper builds upon prior work in FAB inference for MMs [5, 6] and extends it to a more general model class. It correctly positions itself within the landscape of LFM inference techniques, acknowledging the computational limitations of existing methods like Indian Buffet Processes (IBP) [10, 11] and Variational Bayes [3]. The connection to existing work on marginal likelihood approximations like MAD-Bayes [1] is also noted. 

**Strengths:** The theoretical analysis demonstrating the applicability of FAB to LFMs is a significant contribution. The derivation of the FIC and the proof of Lemma 1 are well-presented. The proposed shrinkage acceleration method, motivated by a CCCP interpretation, is a clever optimization. The discussion of parameter identifiability and the resulting merging post-processing step add valuable insights. The experimental evaluation, including comparisons with IBP, VB, and MEIBP, demonstrates the competitive performance of FAB/LFMs in terms of both accuracy and computational efficiency.

**Weaknesses:** While the paper provides a strong theoretical foundation, the experimental section could be more extensive. The choice of datasets is somewhat limited, and the ablation study focusing solely on the shrinkage acceleration could be expanded. The discussion of the limitations of the FIC approximation for small N could be further elaborated. The notation, while generally clear, could be improved for readability (e.g., consistently defining variables). The connection to existing work on regularization in Bayesian models could be more thoroughly explored.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of FAB inference to a broader class of models (LFMs).
*   Strong theoretical justification for the proposed approach.
*   Demonstrated competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods.
*   Practical improvements through the shrinkage acceleration technique.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.
*   Limited discussion of the limitations of the method.
*   Some areas of the presentation could be improved for clarity.



**Overall:** This is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for latent variable models. The theoretical novelty, combined with the practical improvements and competitive experimental results, makes this paper worthy of acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
