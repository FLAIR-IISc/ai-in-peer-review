- Novel contribution: extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) and derives a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.
- Key theoretical finding: FIC for LFMs has the same form as for Mixture Models despite LFMs not meeting standard FAB conditions.
- Algorithm properties: FAB/LFM enables automatic hidden-state selection, parameter identifiability, and monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound.
- Presentation: paper is well-organized with clear explanation of related work (FIC for MMs, Infinite LFMs).
- Derivation quality: rigorous FIC derivation with detailed Hessian analysis of the negated log-likelihood.
- Algorithm description: E-step, M-step, and shrinkage step are clearly and thoroughly explained.
- Empirical performance: shows improved computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance.
- Baseline comparisons: outperforms IBP, Variational EM (VB), and MEIBP on predictive log-likelihood and model selection while reducing computational cost.
- Strengths summary: novel extension, rigorous derivation, effective algorithm, and comprehensive comparative evaluation.
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of the algorithm’s convergence properties.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to a small number of datasets.
- Weakness: lacks detailed hyperparameter analysis and tuning.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical, experimental, and hyperparameter-analysis gaps.
- Pro-accept arguments: novelty, demonstrated effectiveness, and comparative evaluation support acceptance.
- Con-accept arguments: insufficient convergence theory, limited experiments, and missing hyperparameter tuning argue caution.
