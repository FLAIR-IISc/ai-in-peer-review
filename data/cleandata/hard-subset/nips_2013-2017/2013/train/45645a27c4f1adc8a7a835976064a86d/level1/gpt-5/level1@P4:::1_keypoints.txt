- Paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixtures/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs).  
- Core technical contribution: asymptotic Hessian analysis yields a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs with the same log-count penalty form as mixtures.  
- Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 formally recover the ∑k log(∑n znk) term despite parameter sharing, and FIC is asymptotically equivalent to marginal log-likelihood up to Op(1).  
- Practical FAB algorithm derived by linearizing the concave log-count term and using a mean-field q(Z)=∏n∏k Bern(µnk).  
- E-step fixed-point updates include a shrinkage term −D/(2Nπ̃k) that drives automatic feature pruning; M-step updates are closed-form and PPCA-like.  
- E-step reinterpreted as a convex-concave procedure (CCCP); multiple inner CCCP iterations are used to accelerate shrinkage.  
- Post-hoc merge step motivated by identifiability analysis (Theorem 4), showing FAB favors balanced solutions when W has duplicated columns.  
- Empirical comparisons vs IBP (Gibbs), variational IBP, MEIBP, and variational EM on synthetic, block images, and real datasets.  
- Results: FAB often achieves competitive or better predictive performance with more compact K and favorable runtime; MEIBP can be faster but often worse in prediction.  
- Lemma 1’s decomposition (log det|F(d)| → ∑k log counts) is plausible but main assumptions and proof are deferred to the supplement.  
- Lower-bound optimization and coordinate-ascent µ updates appear standard and correct; CCCP interpretation is consistent.  
- Accelerated shrinkage uses multiple CCCP steps, but a formal monotonicity guarantee for the original lower bound under the accelerated routine is not provided.  
- Merge heuristic is coherent but depends on thresholds; formal guarantees that merging increases the bound hold only for exactly equal W columns, which is rare.  
- Experiments have confounding factors: unequal treatment of noise variance (FAB learns λ), fixed IBP hyperparameters (α), and omissions of some baselines on larger data.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized; derivation flow and algorithms are easy to follow.  
- Clarity could be improved by summarizing assumptions for Lemma 1/Theorem 2 in the main text and adding per-iteration complexity/memory analysis.  
- Merge post-processing needs a clearer description of the distance criterion and its effect on the bound.  
- Originality: novel and insightful extension of FAB to LFMs, plus CCCP acceleration and identifiability-driven merge—distinct from MAD-Bayes and MEIBP.  
- Significance: provides principled, scalable automated model selection for LFMs and suggests FIC-like penalties extend beyond mixtures.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, practical algorithm with automatic feature selection, CCCP acceleration, identifiability analysis, and competitive empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: critical assumptions/proofs deferred, experimental fairness caveats, lack of formal monotonicity for accelerated updates, and limited merge guarantees.  
- Suggested improvements: summarize precise assumptions in main text, add per-iteration complexity and scaling plots, include experiments where all methods estimate noise variance, report sensitivity to IBP α and initial K, ablate accelerateShrinkage and merge, and clarify/quantify merge criterion and its effect on the bound.
