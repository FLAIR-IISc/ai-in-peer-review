- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixtures/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs) via asymptotic Hessian analysis.  
- Lemma 1: log det|F(d)| ≈ ∑k log(∑n znk) up to Op(1), despite non-block-diagonal Hessian structure in LFMs.  
- Theorem 2: derives an FIC for LFMs with the same penalty form as mixtures: global O(DK log N) and feature-wise (D/2)∑k log(∑n znk).  
- Method inherits automatic feature-shrinkage, asymptotic prior-ignorance, and monotone lower-bound optimization from FAB.  
- CCCP interpretation motivates a shrinkage-acceleration routine that enables earlier pruning and faster convergence.  
- Theorem 4: provides an identifiability result justifying merging nearly duplicate features post hoc.  
- Experiments vs IBP (Gibbs), variational IBP, MEIBP, and variational EM show improved model selection, competitive or better prediction, and much lower runtime than IBP/VB.  
- MEIBP is sometimes faster but often less accurate; FAB/LFM can underperform nonparametric baselines for small N.  
- Theoretical contribution is nontrivial; main results are clearly stated but proofs/assumptions are in the supplement.  
- Concerns: key assumptions (e.g., nonsingularity near MLE, behavior for rare features) are not listed in the main text and should be stated.  
- Algorithmic approach (mean-field over Z, alternation with M-steps) is standard and tractable; CCCP acceleration is well motivated.  
- Monotone lower-bound increase is justified by standard variational/coordinate-ascent arguments; merge step provably improves the bound when features coincide.  
- Empirical suite is broad (synthetic, benchmarks, real datasets) and shows FAB finds compact K and strong predictive likelihoods; acceleration markedly reduces runtime.  
- Experimental fairness issues: unclear whether noise variance was fixed or learned across methods, IBP’s α left at default without sensitivity analysis, and inconsistent MEIBP setup.  
- Clarity is generally good, but recommend stating Lemma 1 assumptions in the main text, adding complexity/memory costs, and specifying the merge() routine/criteria.  
- Originality: novel determinant asymptotics for LFMs, CCCP-based E-step view, and identifiability/merge heuristic are valuable contributions.  
- Significance: provides a principled, fast alternative to IBP/VB for automated feature discovery without priors/hyperparameters.  
- Pros: principled extension of FAB to LFMs, same FIC form as mixtures, CCCP acceleration, identifiability analysis, and strong empirical results.  
- Cons: missing main-text assumptions, experimental fairness and sensitivity issues, and limited finite-sample analysis (FIC less accurate for small N).  
- Suggestions: state Lemma 1 assumptions and edge cases in the main text, clarify noise-variance handling and harmonize baselines, add runtime/space complexity and merge details, and include sensitivity plots for IBP α and Tshrink.  
- Recommendation: overall strong paper with substantive theory and practical algorithm; reviewer leans toward acceptance despite minor clarity and fairness issues.
