Summary and relation to prior work
The paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixtures/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs). The key technical contribution is an asymptotic analysis of the Hessian of the complete-data log-likelihood in linear-Gaussian LFMs with Bernoulli features, showing that the determinant of the per-dimension Hessian scales as the product over features of the effective sample sizes (sum_n z_nk), yielding a factorized information criterion (FIC) with the same form as for mixtures. Concretely, Theorem 2 shows log p(X,Z|M) ≈ log p(X,Z|P̂) − |P|−(DK/2) log N − (D/2)∑_k log ∑_n z_nk + O_p(1), which implies an FIC whose dominant regularization term is −(D/2)∑_k E_q[log ∑_n z_nk], identical to the mixture case [5,6]. As in prior FAB work, the intractable expectation is lower-bounded via linearization, yielding a tractable objective and an EM-like algorithm that alternates: (i) a mean-field E-step for µ_nk = E_q[z_nk], (ii) an M-step with closed-form updates for W, Λ, b (as in PPCA [24]) and π, and (iii) a shrinkage/removal step for always-on/off features.

Two additional contributions are: (a) a convex-concave procedure (CCCP) interpretation of the µ,π̃ updates, leading to a practical “accelerateShrinkage” routine that takes multiple CCCP steps per M-step to hasten feature elimination; and (b) an identifiability analysis showing that FAB resolves non-identifiability due to duplicated columns in W and suggesting a merge post-processing that increases the bound when two features are redundant.

Empirically, the authors compare to Indian Buffet Process (IBP) sampling [2,10,11], truncated variational IBP [3], and the MEIBP MAP approach [22] across synthetic and real datasets. FAB reportedly achieves strong model selection and predictive performance with lower runtime than IBP/VB and competitive runtimes relative to MEIBP, while selecting more compact models.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The central asymptotic result (Lemma 1 → Theorem 2) is plausible and important: it explains why log ∑_n z_nk arises as a complexity term in LFMs without the block-diagonal Hessian condition used for mixtures. However, the main text refers to “mild assumptions” and defers proofs to the supplement. For NIPS readers, it would be helpful to list these assumptions explicitly (e.g., conditions on Λ, bounds on moments of Z, stability of MLE, regularity of Fisher information) and to discuss whether the result extends beyond diagonal Λ or to non-Gaussian likelihoods. The CCCP reinterpretation is correct and neatly unifies the µ/π̃ alternation. The monotonic increase of the lower bound is standard given the linearization and coordinate updates. The identifiability result (Theorem 4) is intuitively appealing and useful for practice; further conditions and proof sketches in the main text would strengthen confidence.
- Empirical support: The experiments demonstrate computational gains from accelerated shrinkage and reasonable model selection and predictive performance across several datasets. That said, some choices may bias comparisons: the IBP/MEIBP baselines do not learn the noise variance (fixed), while FAB learns Λ; IBP hyperparameter α is fixed to 3; MEIBP initial K is set to min(N,D) or manually chosen when initialization is too slow. These factors should be discussed more carefully, and ideally tuned for best performance of each method. The omission of VB on large datasets due to runtime is understandable, but a time-budgeted comparison (e.g., report performance vs time) would be fairer. Finally, per-iteration complexity and wall-clock breakdowns (FAB vs MEIBP) would help contextualize speed claims.

Clarity
The paper is generally well-organized and clear. The derivation of the FIC, the algorithm, and the CCCP view are presented succinctly. Suggestions:
- State the exact assumptions for Lemma 1 in the main text and give a brief intuition for why log det Hessian decomposes into ∑_k log ∑_n z_nk.
- Explicitly state computational complexity per iteration in N, D, K for E-step, M-step, and accelerateShrinkage.
- Clarify the difference between prior-induced shrinkage via η(π_k) and model-induced shrinkage via the FIC term, perhaps with a small ablation.
- Detail merging criteria (distance thresholds, effect on L) in the main text or appendix.

Originality
Extending FAB/FIC to LFMs where the block-diagonal Hessian condition fails is novel and nontrivial. The identification that the same FIC penalty emerges for LFMs is interesting and broadens the scope of FIC beyond mixtures/HMMs [5,6]. The CCCP interpretation and the ensuing acceleration are simple but useful insights; the identifiability analysis and merge heuristic are practical contributions. The work is clearly differentiated from IBP, VB, MEIBP, and MAD-Bayes [1], and related work is appropriately cited.

Significance
LFMs are widely used; automated, prior-free model selection with provable asymptotic justification is impactful. The proposed algorithm is simple, scalable, and competitive with state-of-the-art nonparametric alternatives, which should interest both practitioners and theorists. The identifiability discussion and merge step address a real pain point in LFMs. The approach is presently restricted to linear-Gaussian LFMs with diagonal noise; discussing extensions (e.g., non-diagonal Λ, exponential family likelihoods) would broaden significance.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel and elegant extension of FIC/FAB to LFMs with a clear asymptotic justification.
- Practical, simple algorithm with monotonic bound improvement and automatic feature selection.
- Useful CCCP interpretation leading to significant runtime savings.
- Identifiability insight and merging improve model compactness.
- Solid empirical results on synthetic and real data; favorable trade-off vs IBP/VB/MEIBP.

Cons/weaknesses
- Assumptions for the main asymptotic lemma are not detailed in the main text; scope and limitations are unclear.
- Comparisons may be biased by fixing noise variance and hyperparameters for baselines; more careful tuning/time-budgeted comparisons are desirable.
- Method is limited to linear-Gaussian LFMs with diagonal noise; generality to other likelihoods is not demonstrated.
- Some implementation details (merge thresholds, complexity, convergence behavior with T_shrink) are under-specified.

Minor comments
- Clarify the role of π vs π̃ in updates; Eq. (12) sets π=π̃=∑_n µ_nk/N, but accelerateShrinkage updates both—explain why this helps.
- Discuss behavior when features are always-on/off and the impact on FIC approximation; your shrinkage step addresses this, but a brief derivation would help.
- Provide code or pseudocode for merge() and report the number of merges across datasets.

Recommendation
I lean toward acceptance. The paper offers a principled and practically effective extension of FAB to an important model class, with clear algorithmic contributions and promising empirical results. Strengthening the presentation of assumptions and ensuring fair baseline tuning would further improve the paper.
