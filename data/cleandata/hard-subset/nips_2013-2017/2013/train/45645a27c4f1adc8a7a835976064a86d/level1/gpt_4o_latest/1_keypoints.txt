- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by deriving FIC for LFMs and showing it matches the form for mixture models, enabling FAB application to LFMs.  
- Enables automatic feature selection, improves parameter identifiability, and offers better computational efficiency for LFMs.  
- Introduces a CCCP-inspired shrinkage acceleration method to substantially reduce computational costs.  
- Proposes a merging post-processing step to address remaining parameter identifiability issues.  
- Empirical results show FAB/LFMs outperform IBP and Variational Bayesian methods in model selection, predictive accuracy, and runtime on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Novelty and theoretical rigor: significant and non-trivial extension of FAB with well-supported derivations.  
- Practical impact: provides a fully automated model selection mechanism that reduces manual tuning.  
- Computational advantage: shrinkage acceleration is a practical innovation validated experimentally.  
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments and fair comparisons support the claims.  
- Clarity of experimental results: results are well-organized and clearly presented.  
- Presentation weakness: some mathematical sections (e.g., Lemma 1, Theorem 2) lack clear intuition and could be clearer for non-experts.  
- Limited discussion of limitations: small-sample performance concerns and mitigation strategies are not fully explored.  
- Experimental scope: additional comparisons with other modern nonparametric Bayesian methods would strengthen claims.  
- Accessibility issue: key proofs and algorithmic details are relegated to supplementary materials, hindering readability.  
- Acceptance argument: theoretically sound, empirically validated, addresses a major limitation, and is relevant to the community.  
- Rejection argument: presentation and limited robustness discussion leave open questions about accessibility and performance in certain regimes.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance despite minor clarity and scope issues.
