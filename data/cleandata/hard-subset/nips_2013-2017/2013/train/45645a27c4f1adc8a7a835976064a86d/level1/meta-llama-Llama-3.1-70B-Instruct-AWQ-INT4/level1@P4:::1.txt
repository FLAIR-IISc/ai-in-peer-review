This paper proposes an extension of the Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference framework to Latent Feature Models (LFMs). The authors derive a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs, which has the same form as the FIC for Mixture Models (MMs), despite LFMs not satisfying the conditions required for FAB inference. The proposed FAB/LFM algorithm offers several desirable properties, including automatic hidden state selection, parameter identifiability, and a monotonic increase in the lower FIC bound through iterative optimization.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the FAB inference framework and its extension to LFMs. The derivation of the FIC for LFMs is rigorous, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's properties. The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM over existing methods, including state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process (IBP) methods, in terms of computational speed, model selection accuracy, and prediction performance.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a rigorous derivation of the FIC for LFMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The proposed FAB/LFM algorithm offers several desirable properties, including automatic hidden state selection and parameter identifiability.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of FAB/LFM over existing methods.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a strong background in Bayesian inference and LFMs, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more intuition behind the derivation of the FIC for LFMs and its relationship to the FIC for MMs.
* The experimental results are limited to a few datasets, and it would be beneficial to see more extensive evaluations.
