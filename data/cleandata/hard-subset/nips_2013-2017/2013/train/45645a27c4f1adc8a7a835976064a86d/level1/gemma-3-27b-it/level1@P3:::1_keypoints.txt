- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing a key limitation of prior FAB applicability.
- Shows LFMs share a Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) structure similar to mixture models, enabling FAB application.
- Develops a shrinkage acceleration method based on convex-concave procedure (CCCP) analysis that speeds up computation.
- Proves parameter identifiability and uses this result to justify a latent feature merging post-processing step.
- Theoretical analysis establishing the FIC form for LFMs is rigorous and well-motivated (details in supplement).
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrates competitive performance versus IBP methods and VB.
- Needs clearer discussion of scalability to very high-dimensional data, where Hessian computation may be a bottleneck.
- The claim that priors are “asymptotically ignored” is imprecise; the role of priors in early learning should be acknowledged.
- Comparisons to existing methods are uneven; parameter tuning procedures for all methods (including FAB hyperparameters) are underreported.
- Choice of noise standard deviation (0.75) on real data seems arbitrary and lacks justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Reliance on predictive log-likelihood (PLL) is limited; additional metrics (e.g., AUC, error rate) would better assess classification performance.
- Omission of VB experiments is noted but needs a clearer explanation for why VB is substantially slower than FAB/LFMs.
- Presentation has redundancy (e.g., repeated FIC description) and occasionally dense notation that could be streamlined.
- Merge post-processing lacks detail on how the Euclidean distance threshold is chosen; a visual illustration of merging would help.
- Strength: novel extension of FAB to a broader model class (LFMs).
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor supporting FAB/LFM applicability.
- Strength: practical algorithmic improvement via CCCP-based shrinkage acceleration.
- Strength: identifiability proof that underpins feature merging.
- Strength: thorough empirical validation showing competitive results.
- Weakness: limited discussion of scalability to extreme dimensions.
- Weakness: imprecise language regarding priors’ influence.
- Weakness: insufficiently transparent and uniform parameter tuning/comparison across methods.
- Weakness: evaluation metrics are narrow, focusing mainly on PLL.
- Weakness: presentation could be clearer and less redundant.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant limitation, offers solid theory, practical improvements, and strong empirical results; likely to be influential.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of limitations/scalability, uneven comparisons, and presentation issues that need clarification.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address limitations, comparisons, and presentation clarity.
