- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), addressing the block-diagonal Hessian limitation.  
- Shows LFMs exhibit a factorized information criterion (FIC) structure similar to mixture models.  
- Proposes a FAB algorithm for LFMs with a CCCP-based acceleration step and proves parameter identifiability.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: Hessian analysis (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) and identifiability guarantees (Theorem 4).  
- Provides a practical acceleration technique (Algorithm 2) that significantly reduces computational cost.  
- Experimental results indicate competitive performance versus state-of-the-art on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Experimental section lacks sufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning across methods.  
- Needs a more thorough ablation study to isolate the benefits of the shrinkage/acceleration step.  
- Comparison to MEIBP is limited by MEIBP’s parameter sensitivity and computational cost, which may understate its advantages.  
- FIC approximation’s validity in low-sample (small N) regimes is acknowledged but not fully explored for its performance impact.  
- Merging post-processing is briefly discussed and lacks concrete examples demonstrating practical effect.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen experimental details and the merging discussion.
