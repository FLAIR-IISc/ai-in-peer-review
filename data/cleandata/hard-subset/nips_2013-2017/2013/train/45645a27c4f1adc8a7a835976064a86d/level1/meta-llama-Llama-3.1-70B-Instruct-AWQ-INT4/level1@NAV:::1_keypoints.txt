- Proposes extension of Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) with a derived Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) for LFMs.
- Provides a rigorous analysis of the Hessian matrix of the LFM log-likelihood that enables the FIC derivation.
- Finds the FIC for LFMs has the same form as for Mixture Models despite LFMs not meeting MM/FAB conditions.
- Introduces a shrinkage acceleration method that substantially reduces the computational cost of FAB.
- Demonstrates through experiments on synthetic and real data that FAB/LFMs outperform state-of-the-art Indian Buffet Process (IBP) methods in model selection and predictive performance.
- Strength: rigorous Hessian analysis is a significant theoretical contribution.
- Strength: derivation of FIC extends FAB applicability to a broader class of models.
- Strength: shrinkage acceleration makes the method more efficient and practical for large-scale data.
- Strength: experimental results convincingly show improved accuracy and prediction versus IBP baselines.
- Weakness: paper assumes substantial background in Bayesian inference and FAB, making it hard for non-experts.
- Weakness: some notation and terminology are not clearly defined, risking reader confusion.
- Weakness: limited comparisons—experiments focus mainly on FAB/LFMs with fewer alternative baselines.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of limitations and potential application scenarios.
- Originality: novel extension of FAB to LFMs and a new FIC derivation for LFMs.
- Significance: approach improves model selection and predictive performance with potential application across domains (vision, NLP, recommender systems).
- Clarity/quality: overall well-organized and technically sound, though minor clarifications in notation would help.
