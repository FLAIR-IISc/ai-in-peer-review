- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs), showing the Factorized Information Criterion (FIC) retains the same functional form even when LFMs violate the usual Hessian block-diagonal condition.  
- Derives a FAB algorithm for LFMs that performs automatic model selection (chooses number of features) without relying on priors.  
- Central theoretical contribution is Lemma 1, which asymptotically justifies applying FIC to LFMs.  
- Interprets the E-step as a convex-concave procedure and develops a practical shrinkage acceleration technique.  
- Proves a parameter identifiability result motivating a feature-merging post-processing step.  
- The proposed algorithm is fully automated and does not require hyperparameter tuning.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing against IBP, VB-IBP, and MEIBP on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Empirically achieves a strong balance of model selection accuracy, predictive performance, and model compactness.  
- Work is technically sound, well-motivated, and the algorithmic refinements are well-grounded.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Weakness: the "mild assumptions" underlying Lemma 1 are only presented in the supplement and should be briefly summarized in the main text.  
- Weakness: computational speed is not uniformly superior—MEIBP is faster in some cases; FAB/LFM’s main advantages are accuracy and compactness rather than universal speed-up.
