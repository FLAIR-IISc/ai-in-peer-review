This paper extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference from mixtures/HMMs to latent feature models (LFMs) with binary features and Gaussian observation models. The core technical contribution is an asymptotic analysis of the Hessian of the complete log-likelihood showing that the factorized information criterion (FIC) for LFMs has the same form as for mixture models, yielding the characteristic log(sum_n z_nk) complexity penalty without the block-diagonal Hessian assumption used in prior FAB work (Fujimaki & Morinaga; Fujimaki & Hayashi). Building on this, the authors derive an implementable lower bound with a mean-field q(Z), provide E/M updates, and interpret the E-step as a CCCP, which motivates a shrinkage-acceleration routine. They also prove a form of parameter identifiability that favors compact representations and propose a merge post-processing step. Empirically, FAB/LFM is compared to IBP (Griffiths & Ghahramani), VB-IBP (Doshi-Velez et al.), and MEIBP (Reed & Ghahramani), reporting better model selection, prediction, and often lower runtime. Connections to singular learning theory (Watanabe) and related asymptotics (MAD-Bayes; Broderick et al.) are discussed.

Quality: The derivations are plausible and consistent with Laplace/CCCP machinery, and the monotonic increase of the lower bound is clear. However, Lemma 1 and Theorem 2 rely on “mild assumptions” deferred to the supplement; the precise regularity conditions and finite-sample remainder control Op(1) deserve more visibility in the main text. The mean-field approximation and ignoring log-determinant constants are standard but could bias model selection for small N; the authors partially acknowledge this. Experiments are thorough across synthetic and several real datasets, though fairness could be improved by aligning noise learning across baselines and reporting sensitivity to initialization and Tshrink.

Clarity: Generally well organized; notation is heavy and the role of each assumption (where Lemma 1 applies) should be summarized in the main paper. Algorithmic steps are clear, and the CCCP view is helpful.

Originality: Extending FAB to LFMs that violate the classical block-diagonal Hessian condition is novel and insightful. The CCCP-based acceleration and identifiability/merging analysis are incremental but useful.

Significance: If the asymptotics hold broadly, FAB/LFM is a compelling alternative to IBP-style nonparametrics: automated model selection without priors/hyperparameters, competitive accuracy, and strong shrinkage-based regularization. Potentially impactful for scalable feature learning.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel asymptotic result yielding an LFM FIC identical in form to mixtures.
- Practical, monotone algorithm with acceleration and identifiability insights.
- Strong empirical model selection and predictive performance; computationally efficient.

Cons against acceptance:
- Key assumptions and finite-sample behavior insufficiently exposed in the main text.
- Evaluation fairness (fixed noise for baselines) and sensitivity analyses could be strengthened.
- Scope limited to Gaussian LFMs with diagonal noise and binary features; generality not demonstrated.
