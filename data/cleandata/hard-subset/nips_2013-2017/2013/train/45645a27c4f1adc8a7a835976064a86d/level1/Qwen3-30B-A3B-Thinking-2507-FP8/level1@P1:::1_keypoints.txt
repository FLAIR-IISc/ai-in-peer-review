- Extends Factorized Asymptotic Bayesian (FAB) inference to Latent Feature Models (LFMs) by showing LFMs share the same FIC form as mixture models via asymptotic Hessian analysis (Lemma 1).  
- Resolves previous block-diagonal Hessian limitation, enabling FAB applicability to a broader class of latent variable models.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) and provide a solid foundation for the extension.  
- Introduces FAB/LFMs that offer automatic model (dimensionality) selection, parameter identifiability, and computational efficiency.  
- Empirical validation is comprehensive (simulations, block data, real datasets) and shows better model selection accuracy, prediction performance, and runtime vs. baselines.  
- Shrinkage acceleration (Algorithm 2) based on a CCCP interpretation is well-motivated and empirically effective.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: FIC approximation may be less accurate for small N and this is discussed.  
- Paper is generally well-written with precise, reproducible algorithm descriptions, though some Hessian technicalities and Theorem 4 need clearer exposition.  
- Originality is high: novel theoretical insight extending FAB to LFMs and practical innovations (shrinkage acceleration, merge post-processing).  
- Significance: addresses practical need for efficient, automatic feature selection in LFMs and scales to large datasets where IBP/variational methods are costly.  
- Pro-accept: novel theory, practical speedups, strong empirical validation, and clear relevance to large-scale latent feature modeling.  
- Con-accept: some theoretical parts could be made more accessible, relation to recent non-IBP advances could be clearer, and experiments could compare to more recent state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall verdict: strong contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
