- Tackles mismatch between undirected graphical models and multivariate count data, extending Poisson graphical models to allow mixed-sign dependencies.  
- Theorem 1: Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals cannot correspond to any joint MRF (impossibility result).  
- Theorem 2: Introduces Truncated PGM (TPGM) via a valid exponential-family truncation with finite support and no sign constraints on edges.  
- Theorem 3: Shows linear sufficient statistics require base measures with at least quadratic growth to support mixed-sign edges, motivating Quadratic PGM (QPGM).  
- Proposition 2: QPGM normalizability condition resembles negative-definiteness of a quadratic form.  
- Theorem 4: Proposes Sublinear PGM (SPGM) using piecewise sublinear sufficient statistics to allow arbitrary edge signs while keeping a Poisson-like base measure.  
- Learning approach: ℓ1-penalized nodewise neighborhood selection with variational upper bounds (Gaussian bound for QPGM, Poisson bound for SPGM).  
- Empirical results: TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian and nonparanormal baselines on simulated hub/scale-free graphs and TCGA miRNA data, recovering plausible biological hubs.  
- Theory strength: Impossibility and necessity results are valuable and well positioned; proofs could use more intuition and concise proof sketches.  
- Methodology request: Clarify convexity/log-concavity of nodewise objectives for QPGM/SPGM and give computational complexity in p, node degree, and max count.  
- Experimental suggestions: Add held-out predictive log-likelihood, calibration diagnostics, sensitivity analyses over R and (R0,R), runtime and convergence details, and compare to Poisson log-normal/latent-factor baselines.  
- Clarity notes: Paper is well organized; define “sparsistent” on first use and resolve notation clash (use ℝ for reals, different symbol for truncation R).  
- Minor edits: Fix small wording issues (e.g., “graphical mode distributions” → “graphical model distributions”).  
- Practical guidance: Provide a short guide for choosing among TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM based on tail behavior and count ranges.  
- Originality: Multiple nontrivial contributions (negative result, TPGM, necessity result, QPGM, SPGM) advancing count-valued graphical modeling.  
- Significance: Enables modeling of both positive and negative conditional dependencies with broad applications (genomics, text, spatial incidence, recommender systems).  
- Questions/suggestions: Explain why truncation boundary indicator breaks joint consistency, advise on selecting R and (R0,R) (e.g., via quantiles/CV), and detail assumptions needed for claimed sparsistency.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theoretical advances, three concrete model classes addressing core limitations, and compelling empirical/biological findings.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited hyperparameter/model selection guidance, missing predictive baselines/metrics, and some notational/expository rough edges.  
- Overall: A well-written, original, and significant paper that needs minor clarifications and more practical guidance to be a strong contribution.
