- Paper proposes three alternatives to standard Poisson graphical models: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).
- Paper correctly identifies and addresses the limitation that standard PGMs only model negative conditional dependencies.
- Authors prove a previously suggested truncation method is invalid.
- TPGM is a natural truncation approach; QPGM and SPGM are novel by altering the exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics.
- Much of the theoretical development follows directly from Yang et al., limiting theoretical novelty.
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and appears biased toward the proposed models.
- Synthetic experiments seem tailored to the methods rather than impartial benchmarks.
- Real-data comparisons omit important copula-based alternatives.
- Results are mainly qualitative/anecdotal; objective metrics (e.g., test log-probability) are missing.
- Copula competitors are used in a black-box way with inappropriate marginals; better marginals like KDE should be considered.
- Identical performance of proposed models and Glasso is suspicious and suggests baseline or implementation issues.
- Paper is generally well-written and including proofs in the supplement is good practice.
- Presentation could improve: bring the key figure for Section 3.2 into the main text and shorten/clarify the overly detailed Section 3.1 (remove an unnecessary bound).
- Overall: theoretically appealing approaches but insufficient experimental evidence; more comprehensive, objective experiments and copula comparisons are needed.
- Reviewer rating/recommendation: 6/10 — interesting ideas but requires stronger empirical validation before acceptance.
