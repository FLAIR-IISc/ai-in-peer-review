- Proposes QPGM and SPGM to overcome PGMs’ limitation of only modeling negative conditional dependencies.
- QPGM modifies the Poisson base measure with a quadratic term; SPGM uses a sub-linear sufficient statistic.
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear overview of existing PGM limitations.
- Models are technically sound and well-derived with analysis of normalizability and tail behavior.
- Key strength: can capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Empirical results show effective network-structure learning on simulated and real data.
- QPGM has a Gaussian-like thin tail, which may be undesirable in some applications.
- SPGM has heavier tails but its log-partition function lacks a closed form, complicating use.
- Literature review is incomplete, missing spatial statistics and high-dimensional count methods; contains misunderstandings about copulas and prior work.
- Practical motivation is questionable; claim that sequencing reads replace microarrays is overstated.
- Method details are unclear, especially the role and selection of constants R and R0; guidance needed.
- Authors must better justify why practitioners should prefer these models over simpler alternatives (e.g., Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement).
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address literature review, method clarity, and practitioner justification; overall rating 7/10.
