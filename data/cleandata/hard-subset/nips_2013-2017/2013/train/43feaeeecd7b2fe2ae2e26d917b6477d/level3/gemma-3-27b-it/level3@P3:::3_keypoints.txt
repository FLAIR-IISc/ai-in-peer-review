- Addresses multivariate count graphical modeling and proposes TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM by modifying the Poisson base measure and sufficient statistics to allow positive and negative dependencies.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving normalizability of the proposed models and shows naive truncation is flawed (Theorem 1).
- Introduces the sublinear sufficient statistic in SPGM, a particularly innovative idea.
- Derivations of QPGM and SPGM are mathematically sound and give insight into conditions needed for count PGMs.
- Empirical validation on simulations and microRNA sequencing data shows SPGM can recover network structure and is promising.
- Situates work within prior PGM literature (Besag; Yang et al.) and links to Gaussian graphical models and copula approaches, but could expand these connections.
- Explanations for key constants R (TPGM) and R0 (SPGM) are insufficient; authors need guidance and sensitivity analysis for selecting them.
- Claim that sequencing reads replace microarrays uses an outdated reference and needs more current citations.
- QPGM lacks compelling practical motivation: it produces thinner tails and may worsen empirical fit compared to simpler alternatives (e.g., Gaussian RF + Poisson measurement).
- Suggestion: include illustrative plots of SPGM PMF vs Poisson and Gaussian to clarify properties and advantages.
- Literature review is incomplete and at times inaccurate (misunderstands copulas, vague/mischaracterized citations [8] and [11]).
- Neglects related areas such as spatial statistics and other high-dimensional count methods that address correlated counts.
- Overlooks approaches (e.g., Gaussian copulas) that can implicitly capture dependence without imposing MRF independence in observable space.
- Lacks investigation of important theoretical properties like sparsistency; discussion or preliminary analysis of extending sparsistency results is needed.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and novel but practical motivation, parameter guidance, literature gaps, and missing theoretical analysis justify rejection with encouragement to revise.
