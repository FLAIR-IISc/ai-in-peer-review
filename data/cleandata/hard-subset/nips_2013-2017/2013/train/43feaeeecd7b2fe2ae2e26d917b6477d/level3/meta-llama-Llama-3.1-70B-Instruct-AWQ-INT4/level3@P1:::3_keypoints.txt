- Proposes three new Poisson-like graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) for multivariate unbounded counts respecting an undirected graph's Markov structure.  
- Derives theoretical properties and shows models can learn network structure from iid high-dimensional count data via neighborhood selection.  
- Empirical results (simulations + TCGA data) indicate the proposed models outperform Gaussian graphical models and the original Poisson graphical model.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with accessible derivations.  
- Literature review is thorough in places but contains omissions and some inaccuracies.  
- Empirical evaluations are convincing and real-world data use adds credibility.  
- QPGM produces marginally thinner tails than desired, limiting suitability for heavy-tailed count data.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification motivation and its claim to be truly "Poisson."  
- Practical motivation for using Poisson distributions needs stronger discussion.  
- Paper should provide guidance on selecting constants R and R0 for practitioners.  
- Authors misstate or misunderstand some existing work (e.g., copulas) and omit relevant references.  
- Neglects spatial statistics and other high-dimensional count methods that are relevant to the problem.  
- Theoretical contribution is valid and interesting, but practitioners may need stronger justification to prefer these models over simpler alternatives (e.g., Gaussian random field + Poisson measurements).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel solution to a long-standing problem, thorough theoretical analysis, convincing empirical results, and overall significant contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: QPGM thin tails, SPGM lack of closed-form conditionals, incomplete/misstated literature, and omission of related statistical approaches.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss practical motivation and limitations of Poisson choice, address QPGM/SPGM shortcomings, correct and expand literature review, and offer guidance on choosing R and R0.
