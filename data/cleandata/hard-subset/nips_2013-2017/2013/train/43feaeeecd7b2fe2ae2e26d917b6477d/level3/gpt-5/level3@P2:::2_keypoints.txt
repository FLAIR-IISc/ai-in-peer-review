- Revisits Poisson graphical models and proves Winsorized (hard-truncated) node-conditionals cannot yield a consistent joint MRF (Theorem 1).
- Proposes Truncated PGM (TPGM): an exponential-family-preserving truncation with finite partition function for any parameters.
- Proposes Quadratic PGM (QPGM): uses a quadratic base measure to allow positive pairwise edges while keeping linear sufficient statistics.
- Proposes Sublinear PGM (SPGM): retains Poisson base measure but uses sublinear sufficient statistics to allow all real pairwise parameters and heavier tails.
- Provides normalizability analyses, including necessity of at-least-quadratic base measure for linear sufficient statistics (Theorem 3).
- Performs structure learning via l1-penalized neighborhood selection within the exponential-family GLM framework.
- Strength: clarifies a long-standing misconception by proving Winsorization impossibility.
- Strength: offers three concrete, well-motivated model families permitting positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Strength: node-conditional likelihoods enable scalable estimation and the paper is clearly presented.
- Weakness: theoretical novelty is incremental beyond prior exponential-family MRF work (Yang et al.); main theoretical gains are limited.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is biased toward the proposed models and lacks rigorous predictive metrics (held-out log-likelihood, PSIS-LOO, etc.).
- Weakness: comparisons to copula-based and latent-variable count models are incomplete; stronger baselines (Poisson log-normal, semiparametric copulas, NB MRFs) are missing.
- Weakness: suspicious identical performance to Glasso in some plots suggests possible implementation or tuning errors for baselines.
- Weakness: practical guidance is thin—sensitivity to hyperparameters (R, R0), optimization details, runtime, and scalability are not systematically studied.
- Theory quality: statements appear plausible but full proofs and clearer conditions should be provided in the supplement.
- Experimental quality: study design and metrics are insufficient; need ablations, hyperparameter sensitivity, and stress tests for zero-inflation/overdispersion.
- Implementation quality: preprocessing, optimization hyperparameters, and tuning procedures need clearer documentation and reproducibility details.
- Clarity: generally well written; recommend moving key SPGM figure to main text, trimming section 3.1, and relocating proofs/algorithms to supplement.
- Originality: moderate—Winsorization impossibility and base-measure growth condition are novel, but model variants are natural extensions.
- Significance: potentially high if empirically validated, but current limited evidence reduces immediate impact.
- Specific suggestions: add held-out predictive metrics, include stronger baselines, perform sensitivity analyses for R/R0, address zero-inflation/overdispersion, and report computational cost.
- Pros for acceptance: corrects literature error and proposes principled alternatives that integrate with existing GLM-MRF estimation.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient empirical evidence, missing baselines and diagnostics, and limited practical guidance on hyperparameters.
- Overall recommendation: promising and theoretically appealing but lean toward rejection or weak accept unless experiments, hyperparameter guidance, baselines, and complete proofs are substantially strengthened.
