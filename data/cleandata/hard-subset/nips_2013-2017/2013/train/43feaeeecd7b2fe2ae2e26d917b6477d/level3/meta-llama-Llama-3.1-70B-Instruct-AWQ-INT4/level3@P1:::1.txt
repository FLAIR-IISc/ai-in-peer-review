This paper proposes novel extensions to the Poisson graphical model (PGM) for modeling count data, addressing the limitation of the original PGM in only capturing negative conditional dependencies. The authors introduce three new approaches: the Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), the Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and the Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM). These models aim to capture both positive and negative dependencies between count-valued variables.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the proposed models and their theoretical foundations. The experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed models in capturing complex dependencies in count data.

The reviewer finds the paper original and its results significant, as it addresses a long-standing limitation of the PGM and provides new tools for modeling count data. However, there are some minor concerns and suggestions for improvement.

Firstly, the reviewer questions the critique of the Yang et al. default model, which is argued to be unsatisfactory due to its limitation in only allowing negative correlations to normalize. While this limitation is acknowledged, it is unclear whether this is a significant drawback in all domains, particularly those with low counts.

Secondly, the reviewer is unconvinced by the "big negatives" claimed for the truncation model. The truncation model may be suitable for domains with low counts, where the truncation level is not too restrictive. In such cases, the truncation model may provide a reasonable approximation to the original PGM.

Thirdly, the reviewer questions the critique of the quadratic model, which is argued to have Gaussian-like thin tails. However, in some settings, such tails may be appropriate, particularly when the data exhibits a strong central tendency.

Finally, the reviewer is unclear on the term "sparsistent" despite its established usage. A brief explanation or reference to previous work would be helpful in clarifying this concept.

Minor editorial suggestions include changing "graphical mode distributions" to "model" (p.3) and clarifying "Note that the log..." (p.5) to something like "Note that although the log...".

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes novel and significant extensions to the Poisson graphical model, addressing a long-standing limitation.
* The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the proposed models and their theoretical foundations.
* The experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed models in capturing complex dependencies in count data.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The critique of the Yang et al. default model may be overstated, and the limitation may not be significant in all domains.
* The truncation model may be suitable for domains with low counts, and the "big negatives" claimed may not be universally applicable.
* The critique of the quadratic model may be overly harsh, and Gaussian-like thin tails may be appropriate in some settings.
* The paper could benefit from minor editorial clarifications and explanations.

Overall, the reviewer recommends acceptance of the paper, subject to minor revisions addressing the concerns and suggestions outlined above.
