- Paper proposes three novel Poisson multivariate models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to allow positive and negative conditional dependencies.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous with clear normalizability conditions for each model.  
- Models permit marginal count distributions and enable simple neighborhood-selection estimation.  
- Numerical experiments show TPGM and SPGM can recover network structure and outperform Gaussian methods on count data.  
- Breast cancer microRNA case study is a useful application but needs stronger data visualization.  
- QPGM produces marginally thinner tails, limiting usefulness for heavy-tailed count data (common in genomics).  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, weakening the conditional-specification motivation.  
- Practical motivation is weak because the authors do not empirically demonstrate that Poisson is an appropriate fit for their data.  
- No visualizations of the SPGM PMF on real data are provided.  
- Methodological choices for constants (R, R0) are unclear and lack practitioner guidance.  
- The paper misrepresents parts of the literature (e.g., Dobra & Lenkoski 2011, Banerjee et al. 2008, possibly Yahav & Shmueli 2007).  
- Important related work is omitted, notably spatial statistics (e.g., Cressie 1993) and some high-dimensional count/Gaussian copula approaches.  
- The authors fail to justify preferring their models over simpler alternatives like Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement models.  
- A cited claim about sequencing replacing microarrays is supported by an outdated reference and needs more current citations.  
- Overall contribution is a valuable theoretical advance but practical impact is limited by empirical, methodological, and literature-review weaknesses.  
- Recommended revisions: strengthen empirical validation, provide guidance for choosing constants, add SPGM PMF visualizations, and correct/expand the literature review.
