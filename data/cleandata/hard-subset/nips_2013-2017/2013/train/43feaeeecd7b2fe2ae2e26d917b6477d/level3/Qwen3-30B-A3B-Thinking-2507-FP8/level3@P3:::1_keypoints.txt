- Problem: standard Poisson graphical models cannot model positive conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints.  
- Prior work: builds on Yang et al.'s results showing PGMs only allow negative edge weights.  
- Contribution 1: TPGM — a novel truncation method yielding a valid joint Poisson graphical model.  
- Contribution 2: QPGM — shows a quadratic base measure is necessary for normalizability with linear sufficient statistics.  
- Contribution 3: SPGM — a sub-linear sufficient statistic variant that allows a broader parameter space and heavier tails.  
- Theoretical rigor: careful, correct analysis of normalizability; refutes Winsorized Poisson and provides mathematically sound alternatives (Theorems/Propositions).  
- Experiments: synthetic and breast cancer miRNA data show TPGM/SPGM outperform Gaussian approaches; SPGM identifies biologically relevant hub miRNAs.  
- Strengths: significant theoretical advances, clear logical development, strong empirical validation, excellent writing and organization.  
- Weaknesses: TPGM’s applicability to “low counts” is inadequately supported due to fixed truncation R; critique of QPGM’s Gaussian-like tails may be overly dismissive.  
- Presentation issues: the term “sparsistent” is unexplained for some readers; minor editorial corrections recommended.  
- Application relevance: provides the first general framework for PGMs capturing both positive and negative conditional dependencies, useful in genomics, spatial stats, and big-data domains.  
- Recommendation summary: high-quality, original, and significant work likely to influence future research; acceptance favored pending clarification on truncation applicability and discussion of quadratic-tail use cases.
