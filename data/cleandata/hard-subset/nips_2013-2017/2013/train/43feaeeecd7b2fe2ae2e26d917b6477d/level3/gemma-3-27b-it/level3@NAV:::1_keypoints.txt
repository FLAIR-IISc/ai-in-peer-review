- Addresses modeling multivariate count data by extending Poisson graphical models to handle richer dependencies.
- Demonstrates existing PGMs cannot represent negative correlations due to normalizability constraints.
- Proposes three solutions: a novel truncation method, a quadratic base-measure, and a sub-linear sufficient statistic to overcome these limits.
- Presents clear mathematical derivations and motivations for each proposed model.
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic and microRNA sequencing data shows the proposed methods outperform standard approaches like Gaussian graphical models.
- Original and theoretically sound contributions, with clever introductions of quadratic and sub-linear models.
- Critique of the Winsorized Poisson truncation may be overstated—truncation can be practical for inherently low-count domains.
- Criticism of the quadratic model’s Gaussian-like thin tails may be dismissive, since thin tails can be appropriate in some settings.
- The term “sparsistent” is used without intuitive explanation and should be briefly clarified.
- Minor editorial fixes: replace “graphical mode distributions” with “model” (p.3) and clarify the phrasing starting “Note that the log…” (p.5).
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing discussion of truncation and quadratic models and adding a brief explanation of “sparsistent.”
