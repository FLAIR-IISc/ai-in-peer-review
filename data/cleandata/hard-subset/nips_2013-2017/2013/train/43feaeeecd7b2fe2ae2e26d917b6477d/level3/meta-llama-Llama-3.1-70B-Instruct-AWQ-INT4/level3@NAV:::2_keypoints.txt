- Proposes novel extensions to Poisson graphical models that capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies for count data.  
- Authors thoroughly analyze limitations of existing methods; paper is well-written and shows strong theoretical understanding.  
- Main contributions: three models—Truncated Poisson GM (TPGM), Quadratic Poisson GM (QPGM), and Sublinear Poisson GM (SPGM).  
- TPGM uses truncation to yield a proper MRF; QPGM and SPGM modify the exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- QPGM and SPGM permit both positive and negative conditional dependencies and produce heavier-tailed marginals than Poisson.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and clearly explains advantages over existing approaches.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only qualitative results and no objective metrics (e.g., test log-probability).  
- Synthetic experiments appear tailored to the proposed methods and real-data comparisons omit copula-based baselines.  
- Reported results are anecdotal and do not offer comprehensive comparisons to existing alternatives.  
- Paper lacks discussion of practical implications, including performance in high-dimensional settings and comparison to Gaussian/non-paranormal models.  
- Meets criteria for quality, clarity, and originality, but significance is limited by insufficient experiments and practical analysis.  
- Recommendations: add comprehensive objective evaluations and baseline comparisons, discuss practical/high-dimensional behavior, and include more derivational/theoretical details.
