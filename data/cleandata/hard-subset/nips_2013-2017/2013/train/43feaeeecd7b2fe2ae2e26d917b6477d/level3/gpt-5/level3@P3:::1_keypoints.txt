- Paper addresses inability of classical Poisson graphical models to represent positive conditional dependencies and proposes three alternative constructions (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM).  
- TPGM: principled truncation of Poisson node-conditionals yielding a valid finite-domain MRF and proving prior “Winsorized” truncation is inconsistent.  
- QPGM: necessity result shows linear sufficient statistics require base measure growing ≥ quadratic, motivating a quadratic-base-measure model normalizable under negative-definite quadratic form.  
- SPGM: replaces linear sufficient statistic with a sub-linear piecewise function to keep Poisson base measure, admits all real pairwise parameters, and yields heavier tails than QPGM.  
- Provides variational upper bounds for nodewise normalization to enable neighborhood selection in SPGM.  
- Relates constructions to generalized exponential-family graphical models and connects to multivariate Poisson and copula-based alternatives.  
- Empirically, TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian-based baselines and original PGM in simulations and a TCGA microRNA sequencing case study.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong: impossibility of Winsorized conditionals, TPGM validity, necessity of quadratic base measure, and SPGM normalizability.  
- QPGM normalizability via negative-definite quadratic form is clear and interpretable.  
- Methods use nodewise penalized conditional likelihood with proximal gradients, consistent with prior exponential-family GM work.  
- Simulations are standard and informative; real-data analysis is compelling and uses stability selection.  
- Missing empirical evaluations: held-out likelihood, predictive calibration, runtime/memory scaling, and sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters R and R0.  
- Clarity is generally good, but “sparsistent” is used without definition and should be explicitly defined and cited.  
- Critique of truncation and quadratic tails should be more balanced and offer guidance for practical regimes where they may be appropriate.  
- Suggest providing practical defaults or data-driven schemes for SPGM hyperparameters R0 and R (e.g., quantiles, stability selection).  
- Recommend including additional baselines (negative-binomial graphical models or copula-based methods) to position approaches versus heavy-tailed marginals.  
- Learning guarantees (sparsistency) are not provided; sketching assumptions and sample-size scaling would strengthen the paper.  
- Minor editorial fixes: correct “graphical mode distributions,” clarify phrasing about log-partition, and ensure consistent domain notation (W vs X).  
- Pros: solves central PGM limitation, strong theory, three complementary model classes, solid empirical demos, clear writing and prior-work connections.  
- Cons: no formal sparsistency proof, limited hyperparameter discussion, lacking predictive metrics and broader baselines, and critique tone could be more balanced.  
- Overall: original, significant, and well-written contribution that warrants acceptance with minor clarifications and additional guidance on hyperparameters and evaluations.
