- Addresses a key limitation of Poisson graphical models by proposing three extensions (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) that allow positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Rigorously proves the Winsorized Poisson approach cannot yield a valid joint distribution (Theorem 1), correcting prior literature.
- Introduces TPGM (truncated domain) as a valid joint model (Theorem 2).
- Introduces QPGM by modifying the base measure to quadratic sufficient statistics, enabling normalizability with positive edges (Theorem 3).
- Introduces SPGM using sub-linear sufficient statistics to achieve heavier tails while allowing positive dependencies (Theorem 4).
- Strong theoretical development and proofs that advance understanding of exponential-family graphical models.
- Provides empirical demonstrations on simulated data and a breast cancer microRNA dataset, and a foundation for structure learning via neighborhood selection.
- QPGM has Gaussian-like thin tails, which may be inappropriate for heavy-tailed count data.
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification motivation and practical usability.
- Practical motivation for Poisson-based models is weak; no evidence is provided that Poisson fits the breast cancer data (suggested need for PMF vs empirical counts).
- Literature review is incomplete and contains inaccuracies (mischaracterizes copula methods and omits relevant spatial/high-dimensional count work).
- Methodological details are unclear—no guidance or sensitivity analysis for selecting truncation constants R and R0 for TPGM/SPGM.
- Labeling SPGM as "Poisson-like" is potentially misleading because sub-linear sufficient statistics change distributional behavior.
- Practical significance is moderate: theoretical advance clear but not convincingly superior to simpler alternatives (e.g., Gaussian RF + Poisson measurement).
- Quality and clarity are good for theory and exposition, but implementation and empirical justification need strengthening.
- Overall recommendation: borderline—strong accept only if authors fix literature issues, add parameter-selection guidance, and present empirical evidence of Poisson fit in real data.
