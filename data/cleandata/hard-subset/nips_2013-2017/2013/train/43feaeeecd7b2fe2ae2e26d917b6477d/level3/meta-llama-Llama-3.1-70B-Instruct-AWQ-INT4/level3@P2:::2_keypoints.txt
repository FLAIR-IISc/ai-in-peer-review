- Identifies limitation of existing Poisson graphical models (PGMs): requirement of non-positive edge weights prevents modeling positive conditional dependencies.
- Shows only one of two natural Poisson truncation approaches yields a valid joint distribution, introducing the Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM).
- Proposes Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) by altering the exponential-family base measure to allow positive and negative dependencies.
- Proposes Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) by modifying sufficient statistics to control linear Poisson effects and enable richer dependence.
- Proves the proposed QPGM and SPGM are normalizable and can capture richer dependence structures between count variables.
- Empirical evaluation compares TPGM and SPGM to Gaussian graphical models and other methods on simulated networks and a microRNA dataset.
- Results claim the proposed models outperform competitors, particularly in capturing positive conditional dependencies.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks more objective metrics (e.g., test log-probability) to validate practical performance.
- Comparison with copula-based alternatives is insufficient, and questionable marginal choices may have biased results.
- Paper is well-written and explanations are clear, but Section 3.1 is overly detailed and should be condensed.
- A key figure for Section 3.2 is missing from the main text, reducing clarity of the SPGM presentation.
- Theoretical contributions are novel and significant, but experimental evidence is insufficient to establish practical merit.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically appealing methods with clear exposition and important contribution to multivariate count modeling.
- Arguments for rejection: limited empirical validation, weak comparison to copula methods, and insufficient discussion of practical implications.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
