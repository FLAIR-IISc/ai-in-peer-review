- Addresses a major limitation of Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs): inability to model positive conditional dependencies in multivariate count data.
- Proves a negative result showing the previously proposed "Winsorized" Poisson conditional (Kaiser & Cressie, 2000) cannot yield a valid joint distribution.
- Proposes Truncated PGM (TPGM), a truncation strategy that preserves exponential-family form and permits arbitrary-signed edge weights with a normalizable joint.
- Introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) motivated by a theorem showing the base measure must scale at least quadratically to retain linear sufficient statistics and allow positive dependencies.
- Shows QPGM is normalizable under conditions analogous to GGMs (e.g., negative-definite parameter matrix), making it "Gaussian-esque" on the integer lattice.
- Proposes Sub-linear PGM (SPGM), which keeps the Poisson base measure but uses sub-linear sufficient statistics to allow any real-valued pairwise parameters and heavier tails.
- Provides theoretical rigor with proofs (including Theorem 3) and clear derivations supporting model validity and normalizability conditions.
- Develops a neighborhood-selection-based learning framework for parameter estimation consistent with established methods.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic (hub, scale-free) and real microRNA sequencing data shows TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian-based methods and original PGM in network recovery.
- Strength: highly significant and practical contributions applicable to many domains with count data (genomics, neuroscience, social networks).
- Strength: original contributions including the Winsorized disproof and the three new model classes with a logical motivation progression.
- Strength: technically sound, rigorous proofs, and solid experimental validation demonstrating biological relevance.
- Strength: exceptionally well-written and well-organized, with clear motivation and narrative flow.
- Weakness: framing of TPGM and QPGM limitations could be more nuanced—TPGM may suffice for low/bounded counts and QPGM’s thin tails can be desirable.
- Weakness: should more explicitly state QPGM’s connection to Gaussian graphical models and compare to alternatives like non-paranormal transformations.
- Weakness: uses the term "sparsistent" without definition or citation; a brief parenthetical definition would aid broader accessibility.
