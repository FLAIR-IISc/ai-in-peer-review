- Addresses an important gap: existing Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) can only model negative conditional dependencies.  
- Proposes three novel approaches enabling both positive and negative conditional dependencies for count data.  
- Contribution 1: Proves Kaiser and Cressie’s Winsorized Poisson approach cannot yield a valid joint distribution.  
- Contribution 2: Introduces Truncated PGM (TPGM) on {0,...,R}, which permits positive and negative dependencies but is limited for unbounded counts.  
- Contribution 3: Introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) with quadratic base measure (necessary for normalizability with linear sufficient statistics).  
- Contribution 4: Introduces Sub-linear PGM (SPGM) with sub-linear sufficient statistics, enabling heavier tails and broader parameter ranges for unbounded counts.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: clear proofs of normalizability conditions (Theorems 1–4) and generalization of PGM framework.  
- Synthetic experiments: TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian-based methods for count data; SPGM excels for networks with mixed positive/negative edges.  
- Real-data application: miRNA breast cancer sequencing shows authors’ methods recover biologically meaningful networks (20+ miRNAs) missed by original PGM (only 14 negative relationships).  
- Paper quality: exceptionally well-written and organized with careful mathematical exposition; minor editorial fixes suggested.  
- Limitations noted: critique of QPGM’s thin tails may be overstated; TPGM’s drawbacks for low-count domains may be exaggerated.  
- Minor clarity issue: uses the term “sparsistent” without definition.  
- Practicality: models can be learned via neighborhood selection, making them suitable for high-dimensional data.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound, significant advance in graphical modeling for count data, recommend acceptance.
