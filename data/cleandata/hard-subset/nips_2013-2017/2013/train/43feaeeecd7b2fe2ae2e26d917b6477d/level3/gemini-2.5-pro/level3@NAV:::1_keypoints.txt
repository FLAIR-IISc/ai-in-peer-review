- Addresses limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs) that only model negative conditional dependencies due to normalizability over an infinite domain.  
- Proposes three novel models to allow positive dependencies: Truncated PGM (TPGM) with a valid truncation scheme.  
- Proposes Quadratic PGM (QPGM) that modifies the base measure to be quadratic, with conditions proving necessity.  
- Proposes Sub-linear PGM (SPGM) using sub-linear sufficient statistics to permit heavier tails.  
- Provides theoretical justification for each model (normalizability, necessity results, etc.).  
- Demonstrates utility in network recovery via experiments on synthetic data and real microRNA sequencing data.  
- Strength: significant contribution enabling modeling of both positive and negative dependencies in count graphical models.  
- Strength: highly original—three distinct solutions and proof that a prior "Winsorized" approach is invalid.  
- Strength: technical quality—sound theory and solid experimental evaluation against relevant baselines.  
- Strength: manuscript is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized.  
- Concern: critique of TPGM’s bounded domain and QPGM’s thin tails may be overstated—these can be pragmatic choices in many applications.  
- Suggestion: briefly define the term “sparsistent” for broader audience clarity.  
- Recommendation: strong accept.  
- Minor editorial fixes: correct “graphical mode distributions” → “graphical model distributions” (p.3); clarify sentence on log-partition function A(θ) (p.5).
