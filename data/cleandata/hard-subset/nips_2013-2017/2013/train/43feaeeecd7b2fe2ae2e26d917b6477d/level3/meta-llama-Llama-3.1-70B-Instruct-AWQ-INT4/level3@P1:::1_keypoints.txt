- Proposes three novel extensions to Poisson graphical models: TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM to capture positive and negative dependencies.  
- Well-written paper with clear, thorough explanations and theoretical foundations.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data show the proposed models effectively capture complex dependencies in count data.  
- Original and significant contribution addressing a long-standing limitation of the PGM.  
- Reviewer questions whether Yang et al.'s default model critique is universally significant, especially in low-count domains.  
- Reviewer doubts the claimed "big negatives" of the truncation model; truncation may be adequate when counts are low.  
- Reviewer challenges the harsh critique of the quadratic model; Gaussian-like thin tails can be appropriate in some settings.  
- Term "sparsistent" is unclear to the reviewer; a brief explanation or citation is requested.  
- Minor editorial fixes suggested (e.g., "graphical mode distributions" → "model"; clarify phrasing around "Note that the log...").  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel extensions, solid theory, convincing experiments, and clear writing.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: some critiques may be overstated and need qualification; minor clarifications required.  
- Overall recommendation: accept subject to minor revisions addressing the above concerns.
