- Problem and contribution: Addresses building multivariate distributions over unbounded counts respecting undirected Markov structure and proposes three models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to allow positive and negative dependencies.  
- Main strength: Novel and valid theoretical constructions that relax traditional PGM parameter constraints and yield simple neighborhood-selection estimation suitable for high-dimensional settings.  
- QPGM limitation: Quadratic base measure produces marginally thinner tails, making it behave more like a Gaussian and potentially unsuitable for heavy-tailed count data.  
- SPGM limitation: Lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification motivation and raising doubts about its status as a meaningful “Poisson” model.  
- Weak empirical support: Practical motivation and validation are weak—insufficient evidence that the Poisson assumption fits real data (e.g., breast cancer dataset) and no empirical vs. fitted PMF comparisons.  
- Comparative justification missing: Paper lacks clear guidance on when to prefer these models over simpler alternatives (e.g., latent Gaussian random field with Poisson measurement layer).  
- Methodological clarity lacking: Role, selection, and practical guidance for constants R and R0 are not explained.  
- Literature review gaps: Important related work in spatial statistics and high-dimensional count methods is neglected; copula role is misunderstood and contributions of cited works [8,11] are misrepresented.  
- Minor claim issue: The statement that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays needs a more current reference.
