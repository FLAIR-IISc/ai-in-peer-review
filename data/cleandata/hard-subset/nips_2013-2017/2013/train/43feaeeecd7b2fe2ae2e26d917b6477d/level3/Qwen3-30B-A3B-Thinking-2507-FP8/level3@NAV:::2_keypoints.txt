- Addresses an important gap: existing Poisson graphical models can only capture negative conditional dependencies.  
- Proposes three methods (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to enable both positive and negative dependencies by modifying exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Builds on Yang et al.'s framework, extending it via novel modifications.  
- Strengths: clear problem statement, rigorous proofs on truncation limitations (Theorem 1), well-justified TPGM, sound theoretical framework, and good writing/notation.  
- Theoretical novelty is limited because much of the framework follows directly from Yang et al.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: synthetic experiments appear tailored to proposed methods and real-data comparisons omit or mishandle copula-based alternatives.  
- Results lack objective metrics (e.g., test log-probability) and identical performance to Glasso is suspicious, indicating possible baseline or evaluation issues.  
- Paper overemphasizes Section 3.1 detail and omits key Figure 3.2 from the main text.  
- Recommendations: move proofs to supplementary material, perform more comprehensive validation with proper baselines and objective metrics, and address copula competitors using appropriate marginals (e.g., KDE).  
- Overall: theoretically interesting contribution but practical significance is limited by insufficient and potentially flawed experimental validation.
