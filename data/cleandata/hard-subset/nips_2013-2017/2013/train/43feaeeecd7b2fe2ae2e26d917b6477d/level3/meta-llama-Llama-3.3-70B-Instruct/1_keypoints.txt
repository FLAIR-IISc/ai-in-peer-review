- Introduces three novel models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) for joint distributions over count variables that capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies.  
- Paper is well-written with a thorough, clear introduction and organized technical sections.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data demonstrate effectiveness in recovering network structures.  
- TPGM truncates the Poisson to allow positive and negative dependencies but is limited by domain restrictions and types of dependencies it can model.  
- QPGM modifies the Poisson base measure to scale quadratically, permitting positive and negative edge-parameters but yielding Gaussian-like thin tails.  
- SPGM uses sub-linear sufficient statistics, allowing heavier tails and a broader set of feasible pairwise parameters.  
- The models are well-motivated and theoretically sound, addressing a key limitation of existing Poisson graphical models.  
- Concerns: TPGM may be unsuitable for some count data types; QPGM may not handle heavy-tailed data well.  
- Minor errors: typo ("graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model distributions") and some sentences that need rephrasing (e.g., page 5).  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; technically sound.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
