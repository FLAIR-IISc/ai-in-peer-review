The paper "On Poisson Graphical Models" proposes a novel approach to modeling multivariate count data using Poisson graphical models (PGMs). The authors address the major drawback of traditional PGMs, which can only model negative conditional dependencies, by introducing three new variants of PGMs: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their proposed models. The TPGM is a natural extension of the traditional PGM, where the domain of the variables is truncated to a finite set. The QPGM and SPGM models are more fundamental modifications of the Poisson distribution, where the base measure and sufficient statistics are modified to allow for both positive and negative conditional dependencies.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed models and their relationships to previous work. The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed models in recovering the true network structure from multivariate count data.

However, there are some limitations and potential drawbacks to the proposed models. For example, the TPGM has a bounded domain, which may not be suitable for modeling infinite count-valued data. The QPGM has a Gaussian-esque thin tail, which may not be desirable in some applications. The SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, which may make it less practical for some applications.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes new variants of PGMs that address the limitations of traditional PGMs. The QPGM and SPGM models are novel and provide a new perspective on modeling multivariate count data.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to provide a more accurate and flexible framework for modeling multivariate count data. The proposed models have the potential to be used in a wide range of applications, including genomics, spatial statistics, and social network analysis.

However, the paper could be improved by providing more practical motivation and examples of how the proposed models can be used in real-world applications. The authors could also provide more details on the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed models.

Overall, I would recommend accepting the paper for publication, but with some revisions to address the limitations and potential drawbacks mentioned above.
