- Paper addresses the limitation that Poisson MRFs (auto-Poisson) only allow non-positive pairwise parameters, preventing positive conditional dependencies.  
- Theorem 1: Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals (mass pooled at R) cannot come from any compatible joint MRF.  
- Proposal: TPGM — an exponential-family truncation on {0,…,R} yielding a valid joint and removing edge sign constraints.  
- Theorem 3: With linear sufficient statistics B(x)=x, normalizability with positive edges requires the base measure to grow at least quadratically.  
- Proposal: QPGM — quadratic base/terms enabling positive and negative edges on unbounded support, but inducing thin (Gaussian-like) tails.  
- Proposal: SPGM — uses sublinear sufficient statistics to allow positive/negative dependencies with heavier tails, at the cost of no closed-form partition term.  
- Estimation approach: penalized neighborhood selection using node-conditional likelihoods (GLM-style where possible).  
- Empirics: simulations and a microRNA-seq case study show improved edge recovery vs Gaussian and semiparametric Gaussian copula baselines and recover biologically plausible hubs.  
- Strength: Corrects a prior incorrect claim in spatial literature about Winsorized conditionals and proves incompatibility rigorously.  
- Strength: Offers three constructive model families with distinct trade-offs (TPGM simple finite support, QPGM unbounded quadratic, SPGM heavy-tail feasibility).  
- Strength: Theorem 3 provides a useful conceptual link between tail behavior/base-measure growth and feasibility of positive dependencies.  
- Weakness: QPGM’s quadratic base induces thin tails that may misfit overdispersed or zero-inflated count data; tail behavior should be quantified and compared to Poisson-lognormal/NB.  
- Weakness: SPGM tractability concerns — node-conditional log-partition D(θ,R0,R) lacks closed form and requires infinite sums/approximations; practical computation, error control, and complexity are insufficiently detailed.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameters R and R0 are underdeveloped; paper lacks principled guidance, sensitivity analyses, and diagnostics for misspecification.  
- Clarity issue: Literature review is thin/misleading — need clearer distinction that copula methods model conditional independence in latent space and fuller discussion of Poisson-lognormal/log-Gaussian Cox models.  
- Clarity issue: References around contingency-table log-linear models are inconsistent/misattributed and require correction.  
- Practical-motivation issue: Poisson often poorly fits RNA-seq; paper should show marginal goodness-of-fit and fitted PMFs for SPGM/QPGM on real data.  
- Originality: Theorems and QPGM/SPGM constructions are novel and interesting; TPGM is a useful exponential-family truncation.  
- Significance conditional: Practical impact depends on tractable SPGM estimation, hyperparameter guidance, and comparisons to latent-Gaussian/Poisson-lognormal alternatives.  
- Requests: Provide details on SPGM computation (evaluation/truncation of D and gradients, error bounds, effect on consistency).  
- Requests: Give data-driven recipes and sensitivity studies for choosing R and R0, and explain effects on identifiability/sparsistency.  
- Requests: Show fitted marginal distributions and overdispersion metrics for QPGM/SPGM vs empirical histograms on RNA-seq data.  
- Requests: Compare to Poisson-lognormal/NB graphical models and latent-Gaussian + Poisson measurement baselines; include posterior predictive checks.  
- Requests: Soften/clarify the claim that sequencing is “replacing microarrays” and update the reference.  
- Requests: Provide optimization details (step sizes, convergence criteria, runtimes) and scalability analysis.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel constructions overcoming auto-Poisson limitations, correction of an influential error, and promising empirical results.  
- Cons against acceptance: SPGM’s lack of closed-form normalization undermines practical advantage, QPGM’s thin tails may misfit data, incomplete related-work comparisons, and insufficient guidance for R/R0.  
- Overall: Theoretically solid and creative with potential impact, but needs stronger empirical/computational evidence (SPGM tractability, R/R0 guidance, tail-fit diagnostics) and expanded comparisons to be compelling for practitioners.
