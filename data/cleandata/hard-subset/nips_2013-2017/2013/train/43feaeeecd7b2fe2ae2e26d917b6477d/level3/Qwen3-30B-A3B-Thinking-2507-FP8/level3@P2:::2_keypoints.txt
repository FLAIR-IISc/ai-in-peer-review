- Addresses gap in graphical models for count data and notes existing Poisson graphical models can only represent negative conditional dependencies.  
- Correctly identifies and proves the Winsorized Poisson approach is invalid for constructing joint graphical models (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes three novel variants — Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) — that can model both positive and negative conditional dependencies.  
- Theoretical contributions are clear and well-justified; TPGM preserves exponential family structure and QPGM/SPGM modify base measure and sufficient statistics novelly.  
- Much of the theoretical framework follows Yang et al. (2013), so theoretical novelty is somewhat limited and contributions are not sufficiently distinguished from prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: synthetic experiments appear tailored to favor proposed methods and important copula-based competitors are omitted.  
- Results are qualitative and anecdotal, lacking objective metrics such as test log-probability to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Methodological issues include inappropriate marginal choices for copula competitors (Gaussian marginals for count data) instead of better options like KDE-based marginals.  
- Identical performance to Glasso in some settings is suspicious and may indicate baseline or methodological problems rather than superiority.  
- Paper should include objective evaluation metrics, broader/appropriate competitors, and more detailed experimental analysis.  
- Overemphasis on Section 3.1 (an arguably unnecessary bound) at the expense of explaining Section 3.2; the sublinear-statistic figure should be in the main text.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting but empirical evidence is insufficient; recommendation — weak reject.
