- Addresses limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs) that only allow negative conditional dependencies; aims to model both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Analyzes truncation-based strategies and formally proves the previously proposed Winsorized Poisson method is invalid.  
- Proposes a valid Truncated PGM (TPGM) in the exponential family that permits arbitrary dependencies.  
- Shows that for linear sufficient-statistic models to allow positive dependencies, the base measure must scale at least quadratically.  
- Introduces a Quadratic PGM (QPGM) derived from this necessity to enable positive dependencies.  
- Proposes a Sub-linear PGM (SPGM) that uses sub-linear sufficient statistics to retain the original Poisson base measure while allowing heavier tails.  
- Empirical evaluation on simulated data and microRNA sequencing data demonstrates the proposed models outperform relevant baselines.  
- High technical quality with rigorous theoretical proofs (including invalidity of prior approach and normalizability results).  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized with clear motivation and precise mathematical presentation.  
- Minor clarity issues: some critiques of authors’ own models may be overstated; "sparsistent" is used without definition; a small typo ("graphical mode distributions").  
- Highly original contributions, including the disproof of a prior method and three novel model classes (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM).  
- Significant impact for modeling high-dimensional count data across domains (e.g., genomics), with a practical demonstration on microRNA networks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a key problem, introduces novel sound models, provides a valuable negative result, strong empirical validation, and high-quality presentation.  
- Argument against acceptance: only a minor presentation nuance—reframe model drawbacks as characteristics rather than inherent weaknesses.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper with a strong recommendation for acceptance.
