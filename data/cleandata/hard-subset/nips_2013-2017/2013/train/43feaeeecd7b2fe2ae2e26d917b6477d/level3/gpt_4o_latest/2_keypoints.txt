- Tackles limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs) that cannot capture positive conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints.  
- Proposes three new models: Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM).  
- Theoretically proves infeasibility of prior truncation methods (e.g., Winsorized Poisson) for this problem.  
- Introduces alternative exponential-family formulations that enable both positive and negative conditional dependencies.  
- Rigorously derives normalizability conditions, extending PGMs’ applicability to broader count-data scenarios.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, but Sections 3.1 and 3.2 (QPGM and SPGM derivations) are dense and could be clearer.  
- Recommends adding detailed proofs in supplementary material to improve accessibility and reproducibility.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: synthetic experiments appear biased toward the proposed models.  
- Real-data comparisons lack rigor and omit objective metrics such as log-probability evaluations.  
- Fails to adequately compare to copula-based alternatives and uses copula models in a questionable “black-box” manner.  
- Some empirical findings are suspicious, including similar performance to Glasso, raising concerns about novelty of results.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but practical utility unclear; recommendation is acceptance conditional on a major revision to strengthen experiments and comparisons.
