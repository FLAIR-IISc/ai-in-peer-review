- Proposes three new Poisson graphical model variants (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to model multivariate count data.  
- Addresses limitation of traditional PGMs that only model negative conditional dependencies.  
- TPGM truncates variable domains to a finite set as a straightforward extension of PGM.  
- QPGM modifies base measure/sufficient statistics, allowing positive and negative dependencies but yields thin, Gaussian-like tails.  
- SPGM similarly modifies the Poisson structure to allow mixed dependencies but lacks closed-form conditional distributions.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and the paper is technically sound.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and links to prior work.  
- Numerical experiments show the proposed models can recover true network structure from count data.  
- TPGM’s bounded domain may be unsuitable for modeling unbounded count data.  
- QPGM’s thin tail can be undesirable depending on application needs.  
- SPGM’s lack of closed-form conditionals may reduce practical usability.  
- Lacks sufficient practical motivation, real-world examples, and discussion of computational complexity/scalability.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address limitations and provide more application/methodological details.
