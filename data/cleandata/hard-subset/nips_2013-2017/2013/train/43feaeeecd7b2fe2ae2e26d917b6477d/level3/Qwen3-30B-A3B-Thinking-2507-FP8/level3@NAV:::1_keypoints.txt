- Paper addresses a key limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs): inability to model positive conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints.  
- Proposes three approaches—Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM)—to enable both positive and negative dependencies.  
- TPGM uses a novel truncation method that preserves exponential family properties.  
- QPGM and SPGM achieve normalizability with flexible dependency structures by modifying the base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Strong theoretical rigor with clear proofs of normalizability conditions and model properties.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic data and breast cancer microRNA sequencing demonstrates practical utility and outperforms Gaussian-based methods for count data.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and appropriately connected to prior graphical-model work.  
- Major strengths: resolves a fundamental limitation in count-data modeling, significant theoretical contributions, thorough empirical validation, and clear exposition.  
- Concern 1: Claim that TPGM’s “big negatives” are problematic may be overstated; TPGM could be suitable for low-count domains common in applications.  
- Concern 2: Critique of QPGM’s Gaussian-like thin tails is possibly dismissive—such tail behavior may be appropriate in some settings.  
- Concern 3: The term “sparsistent” remains unclear despite established usage.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “graphical mode distributions” to “model” (p.3) and clarify the note on p.5 (“Note that although the log...” ).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original contribution with significant potential impact for practitioners working with count data; recommend acceptance.
