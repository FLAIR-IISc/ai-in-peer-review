- Addresses the important problem of modeling multivariate count data and limitations of existing Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs).
- Identifies normalizability constraints of standard PGMs that prevent modeling negative correlations.
- Proposes three alternatives: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).
- Provides theoretical justification, showing invalidity of a prior truncation method and motivating modifications to base measure and sufficient statistics.
- TPGM is a well-motivated natural extension; QPGM and SPGM are substantive departures enabling richer dependencies.
- Much of the theoretical development parallels prior work (Yang et al. 2013, 2014), reducing perceived novelty.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: simulations appear tailored to favor proposed models.
- Real-data experiments lack comparisons to established copula-based methods.
- Copula competitors use overly simplistic marginal distributions, making comparisons unfair.
- Results are largely qualitative/anecdotal; rigorous quantitative metrics (e.g., test log-probability) are missing.
- Identical performance versus a Glasso baseline is suspicious and suggests possible baseline implementation or experimental issues.
- Writing is generally clear, but proofs should be moved to the supplement and Section 3.1 shortened.
- Key findings from Section 3.2 and its illustrative figure should be presented and explained in the main text.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, introduces potentially useful PGM variants, and is well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty, biased/weak experiments, missing comparisons, and insufficient quantitative analysis.
- Overall recommendation: reject now but encourage resubmission after substantially improving experimental rigor and comparisons.
