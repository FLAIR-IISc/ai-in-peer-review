- Paper identifies a fundamental limitation of standard Poisson graphical models (PGMs): they can only model negative conditional dependencies due to normalizability over an infinite domain.
- Authors propose three novel solutions: a valid Truncated PGM (TPGM), a Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and a Sub-linear PGM (SPGM) to allow positive and richer dependencies.
- They show a previously proposed "Winsorized" Poisson conditional is inconsistent and correct this with the TPGM for finite domains.
- For infinite domains, they introduce QPGM (quadratic base measure necessary for linear statistics) and SPGM (sub-linear sufficient statistic) with theoretical justification.
- Theoretical contributions include proofs of Winsorized inconsistency (Theorem 1), TPGM validity (Theorem 2), necessity of quadratic base measure (Theorem 3), and SPGM validity (Theorem 4).
- Experimental results on synthetic and real-world microRNA sequencing data demonstrate improved network-structure learning and biologically meaningful findings.
- The work is significant and original given the prevalence of count data and the practical drawback of standard PGMs.
- Technical quality is high: arguments are rigorous and the experimental evaluation is convincing versus standard baselines.
- The paper is clear, well-written, and logically organized.
- Critique: the paper sometimes overstates drawbacks of TPGM—fixed truncation R may be acceptable in domains with naturally bounded or low counts.
- Critique: labeling QPGM’s thin (Gaussian-esque) tails as a drawback should be nuanced since thin tails may be appropriate in many settings.
- Suggestion: define or cite “sparsistent” briefly for broader readability.
- Minor editorial fixes recommended: correct “graphical mode distributions” → “graphical model distributions” and rephrase the awkward log-partition sentence on page 5.
