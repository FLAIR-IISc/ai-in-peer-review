This paper tackles a long-standing challenge: constructing multivariate distributions for unbounded counts that respect an undirected graph’s Markov structure. Building on Besag’s autopoisson and the exponential-family graphical-model framework of Yang et al., the authors (i) prove that “Winsorized” node-conditionals cannot yield a valid joint MRF, (ii) propose a truncated MRF (TPGM), and (iii) introduce two unbounded variants—the Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM)—that allow both positive and negative conditional dependencies. Estimation proceeds via penalized neighborhood selection; simulations and a breast cancer miRNA case study are provided. This connects to prior work on multivariate Poisson (Holgate; Karlis), copula-based models, and spatial/hierarchical count models, while aiming to retain observable-space Markov structure.

Strengths:
- Clear theoretical contributions: an impossibility result for Winsorization; conditions showing base measures must be Ω(Z^2) for linear sufficient statistics; normalizability of SPGM without sign constraints.
- Constructions preserve marginal count interpretations and enable relatively simple nodewise estimators.
- Empirical results suggest advantages over Gaussian-based structure learning on count data.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- QPGM appears to have marginally thin (Gaussian-esque) tails; please quantify tail behavior and its impact on edge recovery versus overdispersed data.
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditionals; relying on variational bounds undermines the conditional-specification motivation and raises whether the model is meaningfully “Poisson.” Clarify the inferential implications and approximation error.
- Practical motivation: Poisson is often a poor empirical fit for sequencing counts (overdispersion, zero-inflation). Please show data (e.g., the breast cancer set), provide dispersion diagnostics, and overlay empirical PMFs with SPGM/QPGM/TPGM/Poisson/NegBin fits; include a plot of the SPGM PMF.
- Method details are underspecified: explain the role of R and R0, how to select them (e.g., by cross-validation, empirical quantiles, tail-moment matching), and provide practitioner guidance and sensitivity analyses.
- Related work is incomplete: clarify the discussion of copulas (e.g., Gaussian copula graphical models encode conditional independencies in a latent space, not an observable-space MRF); check the representation of [8] and possible misstatement of [11]. Engage with spatial and high-dimensional count literature (CAR/SAR, Poisson lognormal/log-Gaussian Cox, latent Gaussian + Poisson measurement) and compare empirically. Practitioners need justification for preferring the proposed models over these simpler, widely used alternatives.
- Update the claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays with recent references (e.g., reviews circa 2016–2019 and TCGA-era practice).

Evaluation:
- Quality: The theory is sound and interesting; empirical evaluation is promising but comparative scope is limited.
- Clarity: Generally clear, but estimation for SPGM and the roles of R, R0 need much more detail.
- Originality: High; the constructions and impossibility result are novel.
- Significance: Potentially high if practical guidance and stronger empirical comparisons are added.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel, principled constructions with solid theory.
- Addresses an important modeling gap for count-valued graphs.

Cons:
- Practical motivation and empirical validation against strong baselines are insufficient.
- Methodological guidance (R, R0; SPGM inference) is unclear.
- Literature coverage needs correction and expansion.
