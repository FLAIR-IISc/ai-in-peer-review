This paper proposes novel extensions to Poisson graphical models (PGMs) to capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies between count-valued variables. The authors correctly identify the limitations of current approaches and provide a thorough analysis of the existing methods. The paper is well-written, and the authors demonstrate a good understanding of the underlying theory.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of three novel approaches: the Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), the Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and the Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM). The TPGM is a natural truncation approach that yields a proper MRF distribution, while the QPGM and SPGM are novel in altering the exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics.

The theoretical analysis of the paper is sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of the existing methods and the advantages of their proposed approaches. The QPGM and SPGM are particularly interesting, as they allow for both positive and negative conditional dependencies and have heavier tails than the traditional Poisson distribution.

However, the experimental evaluation of the paper is disappointing. The authors only provide qualitative results and do not report any objective evaluation metrics, such as test log-probability. The synthetic experiments are tailored to the proposed methods, and the real-data comparisons omit copula-based alternatives. The reported results are anecdotal and do not provide a comprehensive comparison of the proposed methods with existing approaches.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the practical implications of the proposed methods. For example, how do the TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM perform in high-dimensional settings? How do they compare to other methods, such as Gaussian graphical models or non-paranormal models?

In terms of the reviewer guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, clarity, and originality. However, the significance of the paper is limited by the lack of comprehensive experimental evaluation and practical implications.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper proposes novel extensions to Poisson graphical models that capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies between count-valued variables.
* The theoretical analysis is sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of existing methods and the advantages of their proposed approaches.
* The QPGM and SPGM are particularly interesting and have the potential to be widely applicable.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The experimental evaluation is disappointing and does not provide a comprehensive comparison of the proposed methods with existing approaches.
* The paper lacks a detailed discussion of the practical implications of the proposed methods.
* The reported results are anecdotal and do not provide a clear understanding of the performance of the proposed methods.

**Recommendations:**

* The authors should provide a more comprehensive experimental evaluation, including objective evaluation metrics and a comparison with existing approaches.
* The authors should discuss the practical implications of the proposed methods, including their performance in high-dimensional settings and their comparison to other methods.
* The authors should consider adding more details to the paper, such as the derivation of the QPGM and SPGM and a more detailed discussion of the theoretical analysis.
