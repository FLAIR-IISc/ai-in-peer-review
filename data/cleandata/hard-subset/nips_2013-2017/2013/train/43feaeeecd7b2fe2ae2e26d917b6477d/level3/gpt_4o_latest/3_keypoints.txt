- Introduces a novel approach for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect Markov structures on undirected graphs.  
- Proposes three new models: Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM).  
- Addresses a longstanding challenge by allowing modeling of both positive and negative dependencies in count data.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical foundations, including proofs of normalizability and tractability.  
- Demonstrates practical performance via simulation studies and a breast cancer microRNA case study.  
- Empirically outperforms Gaussian graphical models and traditional PGMs in network recovery from counts.  
- Identifies biologically relevant hubs in the microRNA network, indicating real-world impact.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, raising questions about its status as a Poisson-like model.  
- QPGM has Gaussian-like thin tails, potentially limiting use on heavy-tailed count data.  
- Motivation for using Poisson models is under-justified given common departures from Poisson assumptions in empirical data.  
- Paper lacks visualizations (e.g., SPGM PMF plots) that would clarify model behavior.  
- Literature review is incomplete, omitting relevant spatial statistics and copula-based work.  
- Practical utility versus simpler alternatives (e.g., structured Gaussian random fields with Poisson measurements) is not convincingly shown.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revisions—improve literature engagement, provide practitioner guidance and visualizations, address model limitations, and update references.
