- Proposes three novel models for joint distributions over count variables: truncation, quadratic base-measure statistic, and sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Argues existing models (e.g., Poisson graphical model) are unsatisfactory because they only allow negative correlations for normalization.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, thorough introduction and literature review.  
- Contributions are original and significant to the field.  
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic and real datasets provides strong evidence for model effectiveness.  
- Clearly articulates limitations of existing models and motivates the proposed solutions.  
- Provides thorough analysis of proposed models’ properties, including normalizability and sparsistency.  
- Reviewer is unconvinced by the claimed "big negatives" of the truncation model and suggests it may be suitable for low-count domains; more evidence needed.  
- Critique of the quadratic model may be overstated; Gaussian-like thin tails could be desirable in some settings and warrant more balanced discussion.  
- The term “sparsistent” is unclear to the reviewer and should be better explained despite its established usage.  
- Minor editorial fixes suggested: change “graphical mode distributions” to “model” (p.3) and clarify the phrasing of “Note that the log...” (p.5).  
- Scores highly on originality, significance, and clarity in the Qualitative Evaluation.  
- Paper could improve critique of existing models and provide more discussion of benefits and drawbacks of the proposed approaches.  
- Authors should provide more evidence to support claims and clarify relevance to real-world applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel solutions, strong empirical support, clear writing, and thorough property analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: critiques need strengthening, benefits/drawbacks discussion incomplete, more supporting evidence required, and clearer real-world relevance needed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address the issues above.
