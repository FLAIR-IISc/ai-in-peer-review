The paper "On Poisson Graphical Models" proposes novel approaches to modeling multivariate count data using Poisson graphical models (PGMs). The authors correctly identify the limitations of current approaches, particularly the restriction of PGMs to modeling negative conditional dependencies. The paper proves that a previously suggested truncation method is invalid and proposes three alternative approaches: the Truncated PGM (TPGM), the Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and the Sublinear PGM (SPGM).

The TPGM is a natural and well-justified truncation approach, while the QPGM and SPGM are novel in altering the exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics. However, much of the theory follows directly from Yang et al., limiting the theoretical novelty of the paper.

The experimental evaluation is disappointing and appears biased towards the proposed models. The synthetic experiments are tailored to the methods, and real-data comparisons omit copula-based alternatives. The reported results are qualitative/anecdotal, and objective evaluation metrics, such as test log-probability, are needed. Additionally, the copula competitors are used in an overly black-box way with inappropriate marginal choices; better marginals, such as kernel density estimation (KDE), should be used.

The identical performance of the proposed models to Glasso is suspicious and suggests baseline/method issues. However, the paper is generally well-written, and including all proofs in the supplementary material is a good practice.

The paper could be improved by presenting and better explaining the key figure for Section 3.2 in the main text. Additionally, Section 3.1 is overly detailed, and a bound seems unnecessary.

Overall, the paper proposes theoretically appealing approaches, but the experimental evidence is insufficient to establish their practical merit. The authors should provide more comprehensive experiments, including comparisons with copula-based models and objective evaluation metrics, to demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed approaches.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes novel approaches to modeling multivariate count data using PGMs.
* The authors correctly identify the limitations of current approaches and provide a clear motivation for their work.
* The paper is generally well-written, and the inclusion of all proofs in the supplementary material is a good practice.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The experimental evaluation is limited and appears biased towards the proposed models.
* The paper lacks objective evaluation metrics, such as test log-probability.
* The copula competitors are used in an overly black-box way with inappropriate marginal choices.
* The identical performance of the proposed models to Glasso is suspicious and suggests baseline/method issues.

Rating: 6/10

Recommendation: The paper has some interesting ideas, but the experimental evaluation is limited, and the authors should provide more comprehensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed approaches. With additional experiments and a more thorough evaluation, the paper could be a good fit for NIPS.
