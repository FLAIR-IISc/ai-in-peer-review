- Addresses modeling flexible undirected graphical models for multivariate count data and the limitation of PGMs to negative dependencies.  
- Proves the previously proposed "Winsorized" truncation approach is invalid for a joint distribution.  
- Proposes a valid Truncated PGM (TPGM) as a natural fix to truncation issues.  
- Introduces two novel model classes, Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM), that modify base measure or sufficient statistics to allow positive and negative dependencies.  
- Builds on the Yang et al. framework for constructing joint models from node-conditionals.  
- Problem significance: the topic is important for applications like genomics and social sciences.  
- Theoretical contribution: clarifies a misconception by formally invalidating the Winsorized approach.  
- Conceptual novelty: QPGM and SPGM are creative, fundamental alterations to the exponential family specification.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written with clear motivations and derivations.  
- Limited theoretical novelty: joint construction framework is largely taken from prior work, so novelty mainly in model specification.  
- Biased/incomplete experiments: synthetic data is generated from models favoring the proposed methods, limiting robustness assessment.  
- Missing baselines: omits copula-based competitors and better-fitted marginals for fair comparison.  
- Lack of objective metrics: no quantitative model-fit metrics (e.g., test log-probability) reported, only ROC and qualitative visualizations.  
- Suspicious baseline choices: comparisons to GGM and PGM are weak, and near-identical TPGM/SPGM performance raises differentiation concerns.  
- Presentation issues: proofs should be in supplement, Section 3.1 over-detailed on variational bound, and SPGM visualization should be moved to main text.  
- Overall: theoretically appealing and promising models but empirical validation is insufficient to demonstrate practical superiority.
