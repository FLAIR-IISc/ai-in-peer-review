- Addresses an important problem: constructing multivariate count distributions for applications like genomics and neuroimaging.  
- Correctly identifies limitations of existing Poisson graphical models (only negative dependencies).  
- Proposes three novel models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) that can capture both positive and negative dependencies.  
- TPGM is a natural, well-justified truncation of the Poisson distribution.  
- QPGM and SPGM are more innovative, modifying the exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Theoretical development is solid and clearly presented.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized.  
- Synthetic experiments appear tailored to the proposed methods, reducing their objectivity.  
- Real-data comparisons omit copula-based alternatives, weakening empirical claims.  
- Reported results are qualitative/anecdotal; objective metrics (e.g., test log-probability) are missing.  
- Copula competitors were used in a black-box manner with inappropriate marginal choices; better marginals (e.g., KDE) should be used.  
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and includes an unnecessary bound.  
- Key figure for Section 3.2 should be shown and better explained in the main text.  
- Conclusion: methods are theoretically appealing but lack sufficient experimental evidence to establish practical merit.  
- Recommended improvements: more comprehensive experiments, include copula baselines with better marginals, report objective evaluations, simplify Section 3.1, and present/explain the key figure for Section 3.2.
