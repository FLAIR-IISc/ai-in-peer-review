- Proposes a novel low-rank factorization approach to reduce DNN parameter count by representing weight matrices as products of two smaller matrices.  
- Key idea: carefully construct one factor to predict the remaining weights, enabling parameter reduction.  
- Well-written paper with clear mathematical formulation and thorough explanation of the technique.  
- Strong experimental evaluation on benchmarks (MNIST, TIMIT, CIFAR-10).  
- Results show large parameter reductions often with little or no performance loss.  
- Thorough related-work discussion and suggested future research directions.  
- Main limitation: lacks thorough comparison to other parameter-reduction methods (e.g., pruning, quantization).  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational requirements and overhead of constructing the low-rank approximation.  
- Could include more discussion of potential limitations and practical challenges of the method.  
- Scores well on quality, clarity, and significance per conference criteria.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, effective experiments, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons, missing computational analysis, and limited discussion of limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address the above limitations.
