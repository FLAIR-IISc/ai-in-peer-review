- Paper addresses limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs) that force negative pairwise parameters and proposes three contributions to allow positive and negative dependencies.

- Contribution 1 (TPGM): Shows Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals cannot form a consistent MRF (Theorem 1) and proposes a redistributed truncation yielding a valid Truncated PGM without sign restrictions (Theorem 2).

- Contribution 2 (QPGM): Proves any linear-sufficient-statistics model allowing both signs requires base measure C(z)=Ω(z^2) (Theorem 3) and introduces Quadratic PGM with quadratic base measure, normalizable under negative-definite quadratic form and with Gaussian variational bounds.

- Contribution 3 (SPGM): Introduces sub-linear sufficient statistics that saturate tails to retain log-factorial base measure, yielding Sub-linear PGM that is normalizable without sign constraints and has a Poisson variational bound (Theorem 4, Proposition 3).

- Estimation: Uses ℓ1-penalized neighborhood selection with variational upper bounds for nodewise partition functions for QPGM/SPGM.

- Experiments: Synthetic (hub and scale-free; TPGM / sums-of-Poissons data) and TCGA miRNA RNA-seq real-data study; SPGM/TPGM outperform Gaussian-based methods and original PGM, recovering plausible biological hubs.

- Theory quality: Clear constructions and necessity results; correct and important impossibility result for Winsorized construction; suggestion to clarify why R>3 in Theorem 1 and boundary cases R≤3.

- Algorithms quality: Penalized neighborhood selection is appropriate; variational bounds sensible but paper should discuss approximation error and its effect on edge-sign recovery and thresholds.

- Experiments quality: Results are appropriate and convincing, but need sensitivity analyses over R and R0 and comparisons to alternative count models (e.g., Poisson log-normal, latent Gaussian copula).

- Clarity: Paper is well written and organized; define “sparsistent” explicitly on first use and fix minor editorial issues/phrasing suggestions provided.

- Originality: High — provides rigorous impossibility, necessity result for base-measure growth, and three new model classes addressing a key limitation.

- Significance: High — relevant for sequencing, spatial incidence, clicks, ratings; provides practical toolkit spanning tail behaviors and parameter constraints.

- Pros for acceptance: Corrects literature misconception, principled tractable alternatives allowing both dependency signs, solid theory, practical estimation, promising empirical results.

- Cons/points to address: Tone on TPGM “big negatives” may be overstated; reframe QPGM “thin tails” as trade-off; discuss tuning of R/R0 and impact on identifiability/sample complexity; add comparisons to Poisson log-normal/copula models; provide optimization details and robustness of variational bounds.

- Question 1: Explain intuition for why R>3 is required in Theorem 1, behavior for R≤3, and whether restricted graph classes could allow Winsorized construction.

- Question 2: Clarify assumptions for Theorem 3 (pairwise-only, linear B, unbounded support) and whether higher-order cliques alter the C(Z)=Ω(Z^2) necessity.

- Question 3: For QPGM, characterize admissible positive edge weights given diagonal terms and give guidance/regularization to ensure negative-definite quadratic form during estimation.

- Question 4: For SPGM, report sensitivity to (R0,R) and whether these can be estimated (e.g., via profile likelihood or CV) without biasing sparsistency claims.

- Question 5: In experiments, show results when TPGM R is not set to max observed count and report runtime and convergence diagnostics.

- Recommendation: Accept — well-executed, timely contribution with both theoretical insight and practical methods, subject to clarifications and modest additional comparisons.
