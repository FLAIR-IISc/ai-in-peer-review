- Problem: Poisson graphical model (PGM) can only model negative conditional dependencies, which limits its applicability.
- Contribution: Proposes three novel models to overcome PGM limitations: TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM.
- TPGM (Truncated Poisson Graphical Model): Models positive and negative dependencies via truncation but has bounded domain and issues with large counts.
- QPGM (Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model): Changes sufficient statistic and base measure to allow both dependency signs, with Gaussian-like thin tails.
- SPGM (Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model): Uses sublinear sufficient statistic and milder base measure, yielding heavier tails and a broader set of feasible pairwise parameters.
- Experiments: Synthetic and real-data evaluations show TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian graphical models and the original PGM in structure recovery.
- Strength: Addresses a significant limitation of PGM and offers well-motivated, theoretically sound solutions.
- Strength: Experimental evaluations are thorough and demonstrate effectiveness.
- Weakness: Reviewer unconvinced that truncation’s drawbacks are “big negatives” since truncation may suit low-count domains.
- Weakness: Critique of the quadratic model is questionable because thin tails may be appropriate in some settings.
- Weakness: The term “sparsistent” is unclear and needs clarification.
- Minor edits: Fix typo "graphical mode distributions"→"model" (p.3) and clarify phrasing "Note that the log..." (p.5).
- Recommendation: Paper is well-written, original, significant, and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
