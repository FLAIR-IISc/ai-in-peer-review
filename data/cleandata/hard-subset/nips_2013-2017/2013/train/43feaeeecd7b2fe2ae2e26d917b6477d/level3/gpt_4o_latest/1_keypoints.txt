- Introduces three novel Poisson graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) that enable modeling both positive and negative dependencies in count data.  
- Critiques existing PGM (Yang et al.) for only allowing negative conditional dependencies due to normalization constraints.  
- Provides thorough theoretical foundations, proving normalizability and flexible parameterization of the proposed models.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real datasets show proposed methods outperform Gaussian graphical models and the original PGM in network recovery.  
- Real-world application to breast cancer microRNA networks identifies biologically relevant hubs and cliques missed by other methods.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and makes a substantial contribution to count data modeling.  
- Strengths highlighted: originality, significance, clarity, and strong theoretical and experimental quality.  
- Criticism of TPGM (bounded domain) may be unconvincing since many datasets have naturally bounded counts.  
- Criticism of QPGM (Gaussian-like behavior) may be overstated because Gaussian-like smoothness could be desirable in some contexts.  
- Authors should provide more nuanced discussion on when each model is preferable.  
- The term "sparsistent" is unclear and should be explicitly defined.  
- Minor textual corrections needed on pages 3 and 5.  
- Recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh minor weaknesses and the work is likely to inspire further research.
