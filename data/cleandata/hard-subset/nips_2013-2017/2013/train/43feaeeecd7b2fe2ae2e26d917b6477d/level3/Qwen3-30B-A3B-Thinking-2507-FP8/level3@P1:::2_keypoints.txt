- Addresses an important gap: modeling multivariate count data in graphical models, relevant to genomics and spatial stats.  
- Correctly identifies limitation of existing Poisson graphical models (only negative conditional dependencies due to normalizability).  
- Three main contributions: (1) proves Winsorized Poisson approach is invalid for graphical models.  
- (2) Proposes Truncated PGM (TPGM) to allow both positive and negative dependencies via natural truncation.  
- (3) Introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) by modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and clear presentation of theoretical development.  
- Important technical result: Theorem 1 shows Winsorized Poisson cannot yield a valid joint distribution.  
- TPGM is well-justified for bounded-count domains; QPGM/SPGM are novel in altering exponential-family structure to permit positive dependencies.  
- Weakness: theoretical novelty limited because framework largely follows Yang et al.  
- Major experimental concerns: synthetic experiments are biased toward proposed methods (data generated to favor TPGM/SPGM).  
- Real-data evaluation incomplete: omits proper copula-based baselines (e.g., nonparanormal with appropriate marginal modeling).  
- Competitor usage criticized: copula baselines used with inappropriate marginals instead of better choices like KDE.  
- Lacks objective evaluation metrics: no test log-probabilities or quantitative model-fit comparisons, only qualitative hub claims.  
- Results suspicious: claimed superiority appears similar to Gaussian graphical models, raising baseline/method validity concerns.  
- Paper structure issues: overly detailed/unnecessary bound in Sec.3.1; key figure for sublinear sufficient statistics buried in appendix.  
- Empirical claims about SPGM (heavier tail, milder base measures) are not adequately supported.  
- Significance questionable: theoretical interest exists but practical advantage not convincingly demonstrated on standard benchmarks.  
- Recommended fixes: include proper baselines with correct marginal modeling, add quantitative metrics (e.g., test log-prob), and test across diverse data-generating processes.  
- Overall verdict: technically sound theory but insufficient experimental evidence to support practical significance.
