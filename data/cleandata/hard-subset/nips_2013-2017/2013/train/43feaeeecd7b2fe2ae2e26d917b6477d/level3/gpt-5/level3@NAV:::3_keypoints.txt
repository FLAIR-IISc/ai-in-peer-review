- Paper addresses constructing multivariate distributions for unbounded counts respecting an undirected graph’s Markov structure and builds on Besag and Yang et al.’s frameworks.  
- Main contributions: impossibility proof for “Winsorized” node-conditionals, proposal of a truncated MRF (TPGM), and two unbounded variants (QPGM, SPGM) allowing positive and negative conditional dependencies.  
- Estimation via penalized neighborhood selection; demonstrations include simulations and a breast cancer miRNA case study.  
- Strength: clear theoretical results (impossibility of Winsorization; base measures must be Ω(Z^2) for linear sufficient statistics; SPGM normalizable without sign constraints).  
- Strength: constructions preserve marginal count interpretations and enable relatively simple nodewise estimators.  
- Strength: empirical results indicate advantages over Gaussian-based structure learning on count data.  
- Weakness: QPGM appears to have thin (Gaussian-like) tails; need quantification of tail behavior and effect on edge recovery with overdispersed data.  
- Weakness: SPGM lacks closed-form conditionals and relies on variational bounds, undermining the conditional-specification motivation; clarify inferential implications and approximation error.  
- Weakness: practical motivation weak because Poisson often poorly fits sequencing counts; request dispersion diagnostics and overlays of empirical PMFs with SPGM/QPGM/TPGM/Poisson/NegBin fits and a plot of the SPGM PMF.  
- Weakness: method details underspecified—clarify role and selection of R and R0 (e.g., cross-validation, empirical quantiles, tail-moment matching) and provide practitioner guidance and sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: related work coverage incomplete or misstated—clarify copula discussion (latent vs observable-space MRF), check representation of cited works ([8], [11]), and engage with CAR/SAR, Poisson lognormal/log-Gaussian Cox, and latent Gaussian + Poisson models.  
- Suggestion: provide empirical comparisons to simpler, widely used alternatives and justify when to prefer the proposed models.  
- Suggestion: update claims that sequencing reads replace microarrays with recent references (circa 2016–2019, TCGA-era).  
- Evaluation—quality: theory is sound and interesting; empirical evaluation promising but limited in comparative scope.  
- Evaluation—clarity: generally clear, but SPGM estimation and the roles of R and R0 need much more detail.  
- Evaluation—originality: high; constructions and impossibility result are novel.  
- Evaluation—significance: potentially high if practical guidance and stronger empirical comparisons are added.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, principled constructions with solid theory addressing an important modeling gap for count-valued graphs.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient practical motivation and empirical validation against strong baselines; unclear methodological guidance (R, R0; SPGM inference); literature coverage needs correction and expansion.
