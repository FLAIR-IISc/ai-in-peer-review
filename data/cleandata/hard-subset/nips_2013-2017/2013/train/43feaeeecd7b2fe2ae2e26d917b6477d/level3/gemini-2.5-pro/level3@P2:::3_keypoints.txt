- Paper addresses constructing undirected graphical models for multivariate count data and the Poisson Graphical Model's limitation to negative dependencies.  
- Main contribution: three novel Poisson-like graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) that allow both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Demonstrates Winsorized PGM is invalid and proposes a valid truncation (TPGM).  
- For unbounded counts, introduces QPGM (quadratic base measure) and SPGM (modified sufficient statistics).  
- Models are learned via penalized neighborhood selection and evaluated on simulations and a microRNA sequencing dataset.  

Strengths:
- Tackles an important, well-motivated problem with wide applications (genomics, social sciences).  
- Presents theoretically grounded, novel model constructions and clarifies prior invalid attempts.  
- Estimation is tractable through neighborhood selection, making high-dimensional learning feasible.  

Weaknesses / Areas for improvement:
- Practical fit questionable: real-world count data are often overdispersed; empirical goodness-of-fit evidence is missing.  
- QPGM has Gaussian-like thin tails, limiting use on heavy-tailed count data; need justification versus latent-Gaussian alternatives.  
- SPGM lacks a closed-form conditional and relies on variational bounds, weakening the Besag construction motivation and raising questions about its "Poisson-like" nature.  
- Insufficient guidance on selecting hyperparameters R and R0 (ad-hoc choice R = max(count)); needs sensitivity analysis and practical rules.  
- Related-work review is incomplete/misleading: copula methods, sparse log-linear models, and spatial/econometric hierarchical/latent approaches are not adequately discussed.  

Questions / Suggestions:
- Provide a detailed comparison with latent Gaussian models (e.g., Gaussian copulas) clarifying specific advantages of the proposed models.  
- Clarify theoretical and practical implications of choosing R and R0; justify or replace the R = max(count) heuristic.  
- Update dated citations (e.g., sequencing reads claim) with more recent references.  
- Discuss computational cost of SPGM's proximal gradient ascent and line-search, given no closed-form conditional.  

Overall summary:
- The paper offers valid and interesting theoretical contributions but needs stronger empirical validation, clearer hyperparameter guidance, and a more complete comparison to established alternatives to demonstrate practical significance.
