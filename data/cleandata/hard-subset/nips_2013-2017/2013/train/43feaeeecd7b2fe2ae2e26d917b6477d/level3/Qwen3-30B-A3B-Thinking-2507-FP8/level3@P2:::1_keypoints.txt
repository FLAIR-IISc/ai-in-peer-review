- Addresses limitation of Yang et al.'s Poisson graphical model (PGM) which can only model negative conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints.  
- Proposes three novel approaches that enable modeling both positive and negative dependencies for count data.  
- Rigorously analyzes prior methods (Winsorized and truncated Poisson) and shows only one truncation yields a valid joint distribution (TPGM).  
- Introduces Truncated PGM (TPGM) as a valid bounded-domain model for counts.  
- Proposes Quadratic PGM (QPGM) using quadratic base measures to allow both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Proposes Sublinear PGM (SPGM) with sub-linear sufficient statistics as another valid alternative.  
- Key theoretical result: a quadratic base measure is necessary to permit both positive and negative conditional dependencies while maintaining normalizability.  
- Provides rigorous proofs for normalizability conditions and theoretical properties of each proposed model.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic data shows superior performance versus Gaussian-based methods.  
- Real-world application to breast cancer microRNA sequencing data demonstrates practical utility and uncovers biologically relevant hubs (e.g., mir-375, mir-10b) missed by Gaussian models.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, logically organized, and carefully acknowledges limitations.  
- Minor clarity/editorial fixes suggested (e.g., "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model"; rephrase note on log-partition function).  
- Weaknesses: QPGM’s "thin tail" critique may be overstated, explanation of "sparsistent" could be clearer, and more discussion of when TPGM’s bounded domain is inappropriate would help.  
- Overall recommendation: substantial, original contribution with strong theoretical and practical significance; recommend acceptance.
