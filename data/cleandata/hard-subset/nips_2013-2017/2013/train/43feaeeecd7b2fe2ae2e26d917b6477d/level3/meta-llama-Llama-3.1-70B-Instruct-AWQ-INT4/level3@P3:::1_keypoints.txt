- Proposes several new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables to address Poisson graphical model limitations.  
- Argues Yang et al.'s default model is unsatisfactory because it only allows negative correlations to normalize.  
- Introduces fixes: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic, and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and the results are significant.  
- Provides a thorough review of existing Poisson graphical model literature and clearly explains their limitations.  
- Proposed models are well-motivated and theoretically sound.  
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic and real datasets is convincing and shows superiority over existing approaches.  
- Weakness: critique of Yang et al. lacks a clear explanation of why the negative-correlation normalization limitation matters in practice.  
- Weakness: claim that the truncation model is unsuitable for low-count domains may be overstated; truncation could still be useful in some cases.  
- Reviewer unclear on the term "sparsistent"; authors should define it and explain its relevance.  
- Minor editorial fixes suggested: change "graphical mode distributions" to "model" and clarify the phrasing of "Note that the log...".  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; technically sound and well-organized.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel models, strong theory, thorough review, and convincing experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially overstated critiques and unclear terminology.
