- Problem: constructs flexible graphical models for multivariate count data and addresses the limitation that standard Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) can only model negative conditional dependencies.

- Contribution: proves a previously proposed truncation/Winsorized PGM method is invalid.

- Contribution: introduces three new models (Truncated PGM, Quadratic PGM, Sublinear PGM) that permit both positive and negative dependencies.

- Methodology: develops models within the Yang et al. exponential-family graphical model framework.

- Strength: tackles a well-motivated problem and corrects a misconception in the literature with a solid formal proof.

- Strength: TPGM is a natural alternative; QPGM and SPGM are novel and theoretically appealing by modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.

- Strength: manuscript is well-written and presents theoretical derivations clearly.

- Weakness: experimental evaluation is insufficient and appears biased toward the proposed methods.

- Weakness: synthetic experiments seem tailored to the proposed data-generating processes and comparisons omit relevant competitors (e.g., copula-based graphical models).

- Weakness: reported results are mainly qualitative (ROC curves, visualizations) and lack rigorous metrics such as held-out test log-probability.

- Weakness: theoretical similarity of QPGM to Gaussian graphical models raises questions about practical distinctiveness that experiments do not resolve.

- Recommendation: major revision of the experimental section is needed to convincingly demonstrate practical utility over existing methods.
