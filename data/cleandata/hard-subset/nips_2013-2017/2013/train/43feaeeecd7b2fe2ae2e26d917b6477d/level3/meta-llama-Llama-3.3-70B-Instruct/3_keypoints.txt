- Proposes reducing parameters by representing weight matrices as low-rank products of two smaller matrices and predicting weights via kernel ridge regression.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results, claiming >95% of weights can be predicted without accuracy loss on several benchmarks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and includes a thorough review of related work.  
- Provides clear, intuitive explanations with examples and illustrations of feature prediction.  
- Analyzes trade-offs across different kernels and choices of index set α.  
- Approach is orthogonal to other parameter-reduction methods (e.g., dropout, rectified units).  
- Novel contribution but builds on existing work in the field.  
- Unclear how well the method generalizes to deeper or more complex network architectures.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and runtime costs.  
- Does not provide a clear empirical comparison to other parameter-reduction methods.  
- Claims significant significance and contribution to deep learning, but generalization concerns remain.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel method, strong benchmark performance, clear exposition, complementary to other techniques.
