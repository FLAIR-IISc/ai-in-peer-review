- Identifies limitation of existing Poisson graphical models (PGMs): inability to represent positive correlations due to normalizability constraints.  
- Proposes three novel variants—Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM)—to expand modeled dependencies.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly presented, and demonstrates strong knowledge of graphical models and count data literature.  
- Comprehensive related-work review situates contributions among log-linear models, copulas, and multivariate Poisson approaches.  
- Rigorous mathematical derivations, especially for normalizability conditions and the quadratic base measure justification.  
- Convincing argument that a quadratic base measure can allow both positive and negative dependencies under certain conditions.  
- Thorough exploration of multiple approaches; authors analyze trade-offs rather than presenting a single solution.  
- TPGM is a useful starting point but has clear limitations compared to more advanced variants.  
- SPGM (sub-linear sufficient statistics) appears particularly promising for balancing flexibility and interpretability.  
- Empirical validation includes simulated experiments and a real-world microRNA expression dataset.  
- Criticism of the Winsorized Poisson truncation (Kaiser & Cressie) is arguably overstated; truncation can be relevant for limited-range datasets.  
- Concern about QPGM’s Gaussian-like thin tails may be application-specific and not universally problematic.  
- The term “sparsistent” is used without definition; a brief explanation or citation is needed for clarity.  
- Minor editorial fixes recommended (e.g., "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical models"; rephrase a sentence on page 5).  
- Acceptance arguments: originality, significance for applications, technical quality, and overall clarity.  
- Rejection/concern arguments: overly critical view of truncation methods and potentially overstated universal drawbacks of QPGM.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points above.
