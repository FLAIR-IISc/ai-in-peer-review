- Tackles construction of multivariate count distributions, focusing on Poisson graphical models (PGMs) and their inability to model negative conditional dependencies.
- Rigorously proves that the previously proposed Winsorization/truncation approach does not yield a consistent joint distribution.
- Proposes three alternative models: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).
- Theoretical development is strong and mathematically well-founded, with clear connections to prior work (e.g., Yang et al.).
- Some theoretical novelty is limited by reliance on existing frameworks, reducing perceived originality in parts.
- Experimental validation is weak and tailored to the proposed methods, undermining claims of practical merit.
- Real-data comparisons omit relevant alternatives (e.g., copula-based models) and use copula competitors in a black-box manner with inappropriate marginal choices.
- Results are largely qualitative and anecdotal; objective metrics like test log-probability are missing.
- Recommendation to use better marginal estimators (e.g., KDE) for fairer copula comparisons.
- The identical performance to Glasso is suspicious and suggests possible implementation or experimental setup issues needing investigation.
- Paper is generally well-written, but all proofs should be included in the supplement for transparency.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and contains an apparently unnecessary bound.
- The key figure for the sublinear statistic (Section 3.2) should be moved to the main text and more thoroughly explained.
- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage revision and resubmission after stronger, more comprehensive experimental evaluation and investigation of baseline results.
