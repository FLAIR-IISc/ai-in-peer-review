- Paper proposes novel multivariate Poisson graphical models to capture rich dependencies in count data.  
- Motivation: existing Poisson graphical models can only model negative conditional dependencies.  
- Authors analyze two truncation strategies and identify one valid joint distribution (TPGM).  
- Introduce Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM) that expands allowable conditional dependencies.  
- Propose Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) with quadratic base measure allowing positive and negative dependencies.  
- QPGM has a Gaussian-like thin tail, which may be undesirable in some applications.  
- Propose Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) with heavier tails and a broader feasible parameter set.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, weakening its conditional-specification motivation.  
- Empirical results: TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian graphical models and some existing methods on simulated and microRNA-seq data.  
- Strength: novel multivariate Poisson distributions addressing dependency limitations of prior models.  
- Strength: clear motivation and explanation of limitations of prior Poisson graphical models.  
- Strength: methods demonstrated on both simulated and real genomic data.  
- Weakness: literature review incomplete; apparent misunderstandings/misrepresentations of copulas and related work.  
- Weakness: practical motivation for Poisson modeling questioned—Poisson may be a poor empirical fit in many settings.  
- Weakness: selection and role of constants R and R0 are unclear and need practitioner guidance.  
- Originality: QPGM and SPGM are new contributions not previously proposed.  
- Significance: contributes meaningfully to graphical models and multivariate count modeling with potential applications in genomics and spatial stats.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear notation and well-formatted equations.  
- Quality: technically sound theory and well-designed experiments with clear results.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—address SPGM conditional-closure, incomplete literature review, guidance on R/R0, and practical Poisson motivation.
