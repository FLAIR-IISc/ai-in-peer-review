- Proposes three novel Poisson graphical model extensions (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to allow positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Shows the Winsorized Poisson approach (Kaiser & Cressie [20]) is invalid for graphical models.
- Provides theoretical normalizability guarantees for the proposed models.
- Offers simple estimation via neighborhood selection.
- Evaluations on simulated networks and a breast cancer miRNA dataset show improvements for TPGM and SPGM over existing methods.
- QPGM produces marginally thinner tails than desired, contradicting the Poisson-like motivation.
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the paper’s conditional-specification motivation.
- Fails to show SPGM PMFs or data plots on real counts, weakening empirical justification for practitioners.
- Role and selection of constants R and R0 in TPGM/SPGM are unclear and lack practitioner guidance.
- Literature review is incomplete/misleading (mischaracterizes copulas [8], possibly misstates [11]) and omits spatial statistics and other high-dimensional count approaches.
- Does not convincingly justify why practitioners should prefer these models over simpler alternatives (e.g., Gaussian random fields with Poisson measurement models).
- Contains an outdated claim about sequencing reads replacing microarrays and overall lacks sufficient empirical significance to warrant acceptance.
