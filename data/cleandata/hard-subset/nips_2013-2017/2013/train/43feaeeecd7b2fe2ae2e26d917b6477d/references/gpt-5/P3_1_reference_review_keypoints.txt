- Paper addresses normalizability limitations of Poisson MRFs and proposes new valid models for multivariate counts that allow positive and negative edges.
- Authors prove Winsorized (truncated-at-R) Poisson node-conditionals are incompatible with any joint MRF, correcting prior literature.
- They introduce an exponential-family truncation (TPGM) with finite support and tractable normalization that yields a valid joint.
- Theorem: with linear sufficient statistic B(x)=x, normalizability allowing positive pairwise interactions requires a base measure that grows at least quadratically.
- They propose Quadratic PGM (QPGM): replace Poisson base measure with quadratic energy, yielding Gaussian-like tails and normalizability under negative curvature.
- They propose Sublinear PGM (SPGM): keep Poisson base measure but use a piecewise-linear saturating sufficient statistic to allow positive edges and heavy tails.
- Nodewise neighborhood selection is used for structure learning; simulations and a miRNA case study are provided.
- Theorem 1 (Winsorized incompatibility) is important but needs clarified assumptions and a proof sketch (strict positivity, class of allowable E(X_{V\s}), role of indicator terms).
- TPGM construction is technically clean via Yang–Ravikumar exponential-family joint construction and tractable normalization.
- Theorem 3 (necessity of quadratic growth) is plausible and insightful but needs clarity on per-node vs uniform in p growth conditions and tightness.
- QPGM analysis via Gaussian variational upper bound is sensible, but identifiability between θ_ss and θ_st and implied mean–variance/dispersion issues should be discussed.
- SPGM bounding of pairwise energies is appealing; however, nodewise log-partition D(θ,R0,R) is intractable and treatment of negative θ is unclear.
- Need algorithmic details for SPGM when θ<0: how to compute/approximate log-partition and preserve convexity for optimization.
- Exposition could be clearer about “base measure vs sufficient statistics,” especially that QPGM’s quadratic term is part of the energy, not a base measure.
- Explicit computational complexity per nodewise fit should be stated (TPGM O(R) per gradient eval; QPGM/SPGM strategies).
- Novelty: correcting Winsorization literature and the quadratic-growth necessity theorem are original contributions.
- QPGM and SPGM are new in the MRF count context; QPGM relates to discrete Gaussian models and SPGM is an elegant heavy-tail positive-edge solution.
- Related work coverage is good but should more directly compare to Poisson log-normal/latent Gaussian count models in tail behavior, interpretability, and tractability.
- Significance: provides tractable MRF families admitting both positive and negative dependencies and theoretical results likely to influence future work.
- Simulations are appropriate but should include Poisson log-normal and auto-Poisson baselines, and report held-out likelihood or pseudo-likelihood for calibration.
- Case study findings are interesting but need more quantitative validation (stability, overlap with other methods, enrichment) and robustness checks for chosen R and R0.
- Pros: clear problem statement, corrects misconception, offers three complementary models, solid theory, and practical estimation approach.
- Cons: key proofs need clearer statements; SPGM estimation details for θ<0 and convexity are missing; hyperparameter selection and sensitivity unaddressed; limited baselines; QPGM may poorly model counts in practice.
- Specific author questions: provide a concise Theorem 1 proof sketch; explain how to choose/tune R and (R0,R); detail SPGM handling of θ<0 and surrogate tightness; add Poisson log-normal baselines and held-out likelihoods; report QPGM mean–variance/dispersion behavior.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — significant conceptual and theoretical contributions but needs clearer proofs and more computational/empirical detail to be a strong accept.
