- Paper addresses Poisson MRF limitation: auto-Poisson only normalizable with non-positive pairwise parameters, preventing positive conditional dependencies.  
- Contribution 1: Shows Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals (Kaiser & Cressie) cannot form a consistent joint MRF (Theorem 1), clarifying spatial-statistics confusion.  
- Contribution 2: Proposes Truncated PGM (TPGM) as a valid exponential-family truncation with finite partition function and no sign restrictions on edges (Theorem 2), noting R→∞ recovers negativity constraint.  
- Contribution 3: Introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) with quadratic base measure and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) with saturating sufficient statistics; proves necessity of quadratic growth for linear stats to allow positive edges (Theorem 3) and SPGM normalizability for all θst (Theorem 4).  
- Provides variational upper bounds for nodewise neighborhood selection and empirical evaluation on simulations and a breast cancer miRNA dataset.  
- Builds on Besag’s auto-models and exponential-family graphical model framework; clarifies incorrect spatial-statistics claims and complements alternative multivariate count approaches.  
- Theory quality: TPGM construction and Theorem 1 are sound and nontrivial; Theorem 3 is insightful but needs precise quantifiers and scope statements.  
- QPGM: normalizability condition via negative-definite quadratic form is clear; variational Gaussian bound for node-conditionals is reasonable.  
- SPGM: plausibility of normalizability via saturated B(·) and factorial decay is noted and useful.  
- Algorithms: Practical optimization details are sparse—unclear how partition functions/gradients are computed (truncation, variational surrogates, adaptive cutoffs) and whether true likelihoods or surrogates are optimized.  
- Algorithmic concerns: Convergence, bias, and scalability of proposed optimization not discussed.  
- Experiments: Simulations show gains over Gaussian/nonparanormal baselines, but key count-model baselines (Poisson lognormal, negative binomial, copula models) are missing.  
- Experimental design: No SPGM-generated simulations shown; TPGM included but additional matched settings would strengthen evaluation.  
- Case study: miRNA results are promising but preprocessing and hyperparameter choices (how counts yield max R=11, selection of R,R0, per-node vs global) are unclear, harming reproducibility.  
- Clarity: Generally well written and organized, but some definitions (B(x;R0,R) piecewise form, Proposition 2 conditions, Theorem 3 quantifiers) need more precision in main text.  
- Recommend adding an algorithm box detailing nodewise optimization and how partition functions/gradients are computed.  
- Originality: Theorem 1 and necessity of quadratic base measure are novel; QPGM and SPGM are new and expand feasible dependency structures for count MRFs.  
- Significance: Addresses important modeling gap for positive and negative conditional dependencies in count graphs, but adoption needs clearer training procedures, hyperparameter guidance, and stronger baselines.  
- Pros: solid theoretical contributions, three principled model classes, important impossibility/necessity results, empirical evidence of advantages.  
- Cons: missing algorithmic/training details, no sparsistency/finite-sample theory, limited baselines, ad hoc hyperparameter selection, unclear case-study preprocessing.  
- Suggestions: provide partition-function/gradient computation details, compare to Poisson lognormal and copula-based models, include sensitivity analyses for R/R0, tighten/main-text proofs of Theorems 1 and 3, and clarify miRNA preprocessing.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — substantial and timely theoretical contributions but needs stronger empirical and algorithmic details.
