- Addresses modeling multivariate count data and limitations of existing Poisson graphical models.
- Proposes three novel extensions: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).
- Demonstrates necessity of quadratic or sub-linear base measures for richer dependency modeling.
- Provides theoretical results (Theorems 1–4, Propositions 1–3) underpinning model development.
- Empirical validation via simulations and an miRNA expression case study showing network recovery potential.
- Clear motivation and thorough literature review linking to prior work (Besag, Yang et al., Gaussian GMs).
- Models are original and advance the ability to model both positive and negative dependencies in counts.
- SPGM is theoretically appealing but appears complex and hard to implement in practice.
- Computational scalability and detailed inference/parameter-estimation methods are insufficiently addressed.
- Simulation studies are limited; more networks and varying sparsity levels should be tested.
- Parameter selection (R, R0, c) and sensitivity analysis are not thoroughly discussed or guided.
- Lacks comparisons to other recent count-data methods (e.g., negative binomial, copula-based approaches).
- Paper assumes count data without discussing robustness to departures from that assumption.
- Some sections, especially SPGM description, need clearer exposition and simplification.
- Overall judged a strong, significant, and original contribution with minor revisions suggested.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand simulations, clarify computation/parameter tuning, and broaden comparisons.
