- Paper addresses PGMs' inability to model negative dependencies in count data and shows Winsorization fails.  
- Introduces three extensions: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) to allow negative and positive dependencies.  
- Provides theoretical development with normalizability conditions, theorems, and proofs for the proposed models.  
- Demonstrates neighborhood-selection-based network learning and evaluates models on simulations and a breast cancer miRNA dataset.  
- Positions work relative to Besag and Yang et al., and connects to log-linear, copula, hierarchical, and Gaussian graphical model literature.  
- Highlights novelty: quadratic and sublinear base measures as original ways to relax PGM constraints.  
- Empirical validation is comprehensive and includes a relevant biological case study.  
- Writing is clear and the paper is well-organized.  
- Computational complexity concerns: parameter estimation for QPGM and SPGM, especially high-dimensional, is not deeply addressed.  
- SPGM analysis is limited compared to TPGM and QPGM; needs deeper theoretical and empirical exploration.  
- Practical choice of truncation parameter R in TPGM requires sensitivity analysis and discussion.  
- Lacks explicit comparison to other count-data approaches (e.g., hurdle, zero-inflated models).  
- Missing theoretical guarantees (e.g., sparsistency) — deferred to future work but would strengthen the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a fundamental problem, novel and theoretically sound methods, promising empirical results, broad impact potential.  
- Arguments for rejection: practical computational challenges, limited SPGM exploration, and missing theoretical guarantees.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand computational discussion and optimization strategies; provide deeper SPGM evaluation and sensitivity analysis; compare trade-offs among TPGM/QPGM/SPGM; relate to other count models; add discussion of theoretical guarantees.
