- Addresses gap in multivariate count-data graphical models by allowing both positive and negative conditional dependencies.  
- Identifies flaw in Kaiser & Cressie's Winsorized Poisson and proves it cannot yield a consistent joint distribution.  
- Proposes three novel models: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).  
- Theoretically rigorous: multiple theorems show normalizability with positive and negative edge weights; Theorem 3 links base-measure scaling to positivity.  
- TPGM: simple truncation preserving exponential-family structure.  
- QPGM: modifies base measure to quadratic form enabling positive edge weights.  
- SPGM: alters sufficient statistics for more flexible/heavy-tailed behavior.  
- Experiments: simulations and a breast cancer microRNA case study show TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian methods and recover biologically relevant hubs.  
- Weakness — limited baselines: lacks comparison to other count-data models (e.g., generalized Poisson graphical models, multivariate Poisson regression).  
- Weakness — implementation: omits details on computational complexity and scalability for high-dimensional settings.  
- Weakness — SPGM tuning: no guidance on selecting R₀ and R or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — theory clarification: needs clearer explanation of why quadratic base measure yields Gaussian-like tails and implications for heavy-tailed real data.  
- Weakness — related work: insufficient contrast with recent count-data methods (e.g., Allen & Liu on RNA-seq).  
- Overall: strong, original theoretical contribution with supporting experiments but requires stronger empirical comparisons and practical details.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
