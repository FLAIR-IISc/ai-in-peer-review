- Addresses modeling multivariate count data and identifies limitations of existing Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) in representing negative dependencies.  
- Demonstrates inconsistency of the Winsorized Poisson approach previously proposed as a fix.  
- Proposes three novel PGMs: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) to allow both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including normalizability conditions and base measure requirements for the proposed models.  
- Shows SPGM as a particularly flexible approach that balances normalizability and modeling power.  
- Briefly explores structure learning and uses proximal gradient ascent for parameter estimation.  
- Presents illustrative empirical results on simulated data and breast cancer microRNA expression, suggesting improved performance over alternatives.  
- Builds on prior work by Besag and Yang and directly critiques Kaiser & Cressie and Griffith on Winsorized Poisson methods.  
- Connects to broader non-Gaussian graphical model literature, including copula-based and nonparametric methods.  
- Strength: clear problem statement and comprehensive theoretical development.  
- Strength: novelty of TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM and their theoretical justification.  
- Strength: practical relevance demonstrated via real-world microRNA application.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation—needs more parameter exploration and broader baselines.  
- Weakness: increased complexity of QPGM and SPGM with unclear practical/computational implications and unclear comparative benefits.  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation details (regularization, convergence criteria) hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: variational Gaussian bound for SPGM lacks thorough justification and accuracy analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, theoretically sound paper with practical promise.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on expanded empirical evaluation, analysis of computational complexity and parameter tuning, fuller implementation details, and stronger justification of the variational bound.
