- Identifies limitation of existing Poisson graphical models (only negative conditional dependencies) and proposes TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM to fix it.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis proving limitations of prior approaches and establishing normalizability conditions for positive and negative edge weights.
- Refutes the Winsorized Poisson method as insufficient.
- Demonstrates practical utility via simulations and a breast cancer microRNA network case study.
- Technically sound with well-constructed proofs (Theorems 1–4) and careful treatment of base measures, sufficient statistics, and normalizability.
- Experimental results show proposed models outperform Gaussian-based approaches on count data, with SPGM particularly promising.
- Lacks detailed learning/optimization algorithms, computational complexity analysis, and full development of sparsistency (structure learning) guarantees.
- Well-organized and clearly written overall, with consistent notation and helpful figures.
- Needs more intuitive explanation of SPGM's sublinear sufficient statistics and expanded discussion of QPGM’s thin-tail implications.
- Original contribution: multiple novel approaches, especially SPGM allowing heavy tails and arbitrary edge signs; clearly distinguishes from prior work.
- Significant impact potential across domains with count data (genomics, spatial stats, climate), with biologically meaningful miRNA findings supporting utility.
- Recommendation: accept — substantial theoretical novelty and convincing empirical validation.
- Specific improvements suggested: detail computational complexity and scalability, develop structure-learning guarantees, make SPGM intuition clearer, compare to more recent count-data models, and discuss practical approximations for SPGM’s log-partition function.
