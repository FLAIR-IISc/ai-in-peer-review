- Paper addresses limitation of Poisson graphical models that disallow positive conditional dependencies and proposes solutions.
- Main contributions: (1) impossibility of Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals admitting a Besag/Yang joint MRF (Theorem 1).
- (2) Proposal of Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM) with finite state-space node-conditionals and a valid joint MRF (Theorem 2), allowing mixed-sign dependencies.
- (3) Analysis of required base-measure growth for mixed-sign edges, proving quadratic growth is necessary for linear sufficient statistics (Theorem 3).
- Introduction of Quadratic PGM (QPGM) with negative-definite quadratic form (Proposition 2) to ensure normalizability.
- Introduction of Sublinear PGM (SPGM) using a piecewise-linear saturating sufficient statistic to retain heavy tails while permitting all pairwise parameters (Theorem 4).
- Methodology: nodewise ℓ1-penalized neighborhood selection with proximal gradient and variational bounds for learning (Gaussian bound for QPGM, Poisson bound for SPGM when θ≥0).
- Theoretical strengths: important impossibility result (Theorem 1), clear TPGM consistency, insightful necessity of quadratic base-measure growth (Theorem 3), and convincing SPGM normalizability argument.
- Requested theoretical clarifications: include proof sketches for main theorems, and state formal assumptions (e.g., mixed-sign edges, unbounded support) precisely.
- Algorithmic weaknesses: sparse computational details (step sizes, convergence, handling intractable log-partition for SPGM, screening, runtimes).
- Experimental issues: missing comparisons to count-specific baselines (e.g., COM-Poisson, Poisson log-normal, negative binomial copula models).
- TPGM/SPGM tuning concerns: choice of truncation R (and R0) set to observed max risks overfitting; sensitivity analyses and heuristics for selection needed.
- Reporting gaps: insufficient details on simulation graph generation, edge weights, hyperparameters, runtime, calibration, and lack of quantitative validation in the TCGA case study.
- Clarity suggestions: provide intuition/proof sketches in main text, clarify notation (W vs N0, base measure C vs log Z!), and give practical heuristics for R/R0 selection.
- Originality: novel and substantive contributions, especially Theorem 3 and SPGM; TPGM is straightforward but useful.
- Significance: important for modeling multivariate counts in genomics, neuroscience, spatial stats; potential to become standard with stronger empirical validation.
- Pros: corrects a widely cited error on Winsorization, strong theory, introduces practical models enabling mixed-sign dependencies, and shows empirical advantages over Gaussian/copula baselines.
- Cons: limited optimization/algorithmic detail (SPGM when θ<0), sensitivity of truncation parameters, missing modern count-model baselines, and incomplete experimental/benchmark reporting.
- Recommendation: borderline-accept to accept contingent on improved experiments, clearer optimization details (especially for SPGM), and guidance on hyperparameter selection.
