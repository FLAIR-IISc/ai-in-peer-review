- Paper addresses normalization limitation of Poisson graphical models that forbids positive edges and proposes solutions enabling positive/negative dependencies.
- Theorem 1: Winsorized (hard-truncated) Poisson node-conditionals cannot be consistent with any joint MRF, correcting prior literature.
- TPGM: Introduces an exponential-family truncation yielding a valid joint MRF on {0,…,R}^p that allows positive edges.
- Theorem 3: Shows necessity of base-measure growth Ω(z^2) for linear sufficient statistics if positive interactions are allowed on the infinite count domain.
- QPGM: Proposes a Quadratic PGM with quadratic base measure and joint density; normalizability holds under negative-definiteness conditions (analogy to Gaussian GMRFs).
- SPGM: Proposes a Sublinear PGM that keeps Poisson base measure but uses a saturating/sublinear sufficient statistic, enabling normalizability for arbitrary pairwise parameters and heavier tails.
- Estimation: Describes neighborhood selection using node-conditional likelihoods and variational bounds (Gaussian for QPGM, Poisson for SPGM) to approximate partition functions.
- Empirics: Simulations show improved ROC/AUC over Gaussian/nonparanormal baselines; TPGM best when well-specified, SPGM competitive.
- Case study: Breast cancer miRNA data yields biologically plausible hubs (miR-375, miR-10b) and a chr19 cluster, but lacks ground truth for quantitative validation.
- Theoretical quality: Core claims are well-motivated and plausible; key results (Theorems 1–4, Proposition 2) are insightful and connect to prior work.
- Missing theory details: Need precise assumptions and proof sketches for Theorem 3, regularity/identifiability for QPGM/SPGM, and computational guarantees for proximal schemes with variational approximations.
- Experimental gaps: No comparisons to Poisson log-normal or copula-Poisson network methods; sensitivity analyses for R, R0, and misspecification are missing.
- Clarity suggestions: Spell out Theorem 3 conditions with a simple two-node example, provide intuition and selection methods for (R0,R), and clarify when/how variational bounds are used in SPGM optimization.
- Originality: Contributions are novel—correcting Winsorization misconceptions, introducing TPGM/QPGM/SPGM, and the growth-condition theorem are significant.
- Significance: Advances count-valued graphical modeling allowing positive/negative edges without latent copulas, with likely impact in genomics and spatial epidemiology.
- Pros: Clear theoretical motivation, important negative result, three complementary models, simple node-wise learning strategies, and promising empirical results.
- Cons: Some theoretical exposition incomplete, reliance on variational bounds without formal guarantees, limited empirical baselines, and unexplored hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Questions/suggestions for authors: Provide a constructive example for Theorem 1 with R>3; formalize Theorem 3’s dependence on p/graph structure; recommend practical selection of R and (R0,R); quantify approximation error from Poisson bounds; add Poisson log-normal/copula comparisons.
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on tightening theoretical exposition and clarifying estimation details.
