Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles a long-standing limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs) for multivariate count data: Besag’s auto-Poisson (and its EF-MRF generalization) requires non-positive pairwise parameters for normalizability, so it only models negative conditional dependencies. The authors (i) prove that the “Winsorized” (hard-truncated-at-R) Poisson node-conditional proposal of Kaiser & Cressie cannot be made consistent with any joint MRF (Theorem 1); (ii) propose a tractable truncated Poisson EF that preserves exponential-family structure at the node conditionals and yields a proper joint MRF on {0,…,R}p with no sign constraints (TPGM; Theorem 2); (iii) show that with linear sufficient statistics B(x)=x, any joint MRF that admits positive conditional dependencies must use a base measure that grows at least quadratically, C(x)=Ω(x^2) (Theorem 3); (iv) instantiate this insight as a Quadratic PGM (QPGM) by adding quadratic unary terms (and allowing the quadratic form to be negative definite), yielding a model that supports both positive and negative edges and admits a Gaussian variational upper bound for nodewise fitting; and (v) propose a Sublinear PGM (SPGM) that retains the Poisson base measure but replaces B(x) with a piecewise sublinear, saturating statistic B(x;R0,R), ensuring normalizability for all pairwise parameters while producing heavier tails than QPGM. They illustrate neighborhood selection for structure learning and present simulations and an RNA-seq microRNA case study, showing improvements over Gaussian and nonparanormal baselines.

Quality
- Theory: The high-level arguments are compelling. Theorem 1 (non-existence of a joint for Winsorized node-conditionals) is plausible—introducing an indicator at the truncation boundary breaks the EF structure required by the Brook/Hammersley-Clifford construction. Theorem 2 for TPGM is standard EF-MRF consistency once support is finite. The necessity claim in Theorem 3 is insightful: with linear sufficient stats and infinite support, any positive pairwise interaction implies quadratic growth in the exponent that will dominate the Poisson base measure log x!, hence a quadratic (or stronger) base measure is necessary. QPGM normalizability via a negative-definite quadratic form is sound. SPGM normalizability follows from bounded sufficient stats. The Gaussian variational upper bound for QPGM node conditionals is reasonable (integral upper bounds the sum under θss<0), but the paper should state any conditions (e.g., log-concavity) under which the inequality strictly holds.
- Methodology/algorithms: The paper outlines nodewise penalized likelihood with proximal methods. However, details are sparse for SPGM (how D is computed/approximated for θ<0?) and for enforcing symmetry/consensus across neighborhoods. For QPGM, the variational surrogate is described but optimization details and guarantees are not.
- Experiments: Simulations demonstrate ROC improvements over Gaussian/nonparanormal baselines on count data. The case study is suggestive and highlights the inadequacy of the original PGM. However, comparisons lack more specialized count-network baselines (e.g., Poisson log-normal graphical models, copula-based count networks, negative-binomial GLM-based neighborhood selection), and quantitative metrics (AUCs, CIs) are not reported.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well written and places the contributions in context. A few points need clarification:
  - In Sec. 3.1, the text initially emphasizes “same sufficient statistics as Poisson” but then introduces QPGM with an additional quadratic unary term. Please reconcile this with the EF-MRF construction (node conditionals are then a 2D EF with {x, x^2}).
  - Precisely state assumptions for Theorem 3 (e.g., existence of any θst>0 with the original Poisson base measure) and give a proof sketch.
  - For SPGM, clearly define how R0 and R are chosen in practice, whether they are global or per-variable, and sensitivity to these choices.
  - The microRNA section reports R=11 as the maximum count; given typical RNA-seq scales, please explain preprocessing/normalization.

Originality
- Demonstrating the inconsistency of Winsorized node-conditionals (Theorem 1) is new and useful to the community. TPGM is a natural, EF-preserving truncation that seems to have been overlooked. The Ω(x^2) necessity result is the key conceptual contribution that cleanly explains the negative-dependence pathology and motivates QPGM. SPGM’s saturating sufficient statistic is an original, practical workaround to obtain rich dependencies with mild tails and easy normalizability. Overall, the conceptual advances over prior EF-MRF work on PGMs are significant.

Significance
- Count-valued networks arise in genomics, neuro, and social data. Providing MRFs that admit positive and negative dependencies without resorting to intractable multivariate Poisson constructions is important. The theoretical diagnosis (Theorem 3) will likely influence future model design. That said, broader empirical validation and comparisons to state-of-the-art count network models (Poisson log-normal, copula-based count GGMs, negative-binomial variants) would strengthen the practical impact.

Pros
- Sharp theoretical insight (Ω(x^2) requirement) that clarifies limitations of PGMs.
- Clean constructions: TPGM (finite support), QPGM (negative-definite quadratic), SPGM (saturating sufficient stats).
- Demonstrates that Winsorized conditionals cannot yield a consistent joint.
- Practical nodewise estimation strategies and promising empirical results on simulated and real data.

Cons
- Limited methodological details for optimization (SPGM partition, QPGM surrogate, symmetry).
- Experimental comparisons omit strong count-specific baselines; quantitative reporting is thin.
- Hyperparameter choices (R, R0) and their selection are underexplained; R tied to max observed count is questionable for generalization.
- Some theoretical statements (Theorem 1 scope; QPGM conditional EF form; variational bound conditions) could be tightened.

Questions/suggestions for the authors
- Provide a proof sketch for Theorem 1 and clarify why R>3 is required; what happens for small R?
- For QPGM, explicitly write the node-conditional EF (with stats {x, x^2}) and relate it to the joint construction in [18,19].
- Detail the SPGM learning algorithm when θ can be negative; how is D(θ,R0,R) approximated, and what accuracy/complexity trade-offs arise?
- Discuss principled selection of R and R0 (e.g., via cross-validation, stability) and perform a sensitivity analysis.
- Add comparisons to Poisson log-normal network models and other count-network baselines; report AUCs with confidence intervals.
- Clarify preprocessing for the TCGA miRNA data leading to R=11.

Overall recommendation
This paper makes a clear and novel theoretical contribution and proposes practically motivated models that address a real limitation of PGMs for count data. With improved clarity on certain theoretical points, richer experimental comparisons, and more algorithmic detail, it would be a strong NeurIPS paper. I lean toward accept, contingent on addressing the noted issues.
