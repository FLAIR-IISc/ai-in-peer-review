- Paper addresses Poisson MRF normalizability that forces nonpositive edges and proposes fixes allowing positive dependencies.
- Theorem 1: proves Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals cannot correspond to a consistent joint MRF.
- Introduces TPGM: truncated-exponential-family MRF on {0,…,R} that permits arbitrary-sign edges.
- Theorem 3: shows linear sufficient-statistic EF-MRFs need base measures with at least quadratic growth to allow mixed-sign edges.
- Proposes QPGM: linear sufficient statistics with a quadratic base measure and negative-definite normalizability condition.
- Proposes SPGM: retains Poisson base measure but uses piecewise sublinear (saturating) sufficient statistics to allow positive/negative edges.
- Uses neighborhood selection via conditional likelihood for structure learning; applies variational bound for SPGM nodewise inference.
- Simulation and breast cancer microRNA data show TPGM/SPGM outperform Gaussian-based competitors and recover plausible hubs.
- Theoretical contributions are the paper’s main strength and clarify longstanding misconceptions in the literature.
- TPGM and SPGM normalizability results and the quadratic-base necessity theorem are insightful and well motivated.
- Empirics lack quantitative comparison to the original PGM baseline (results omitted), weakening claims about PGM deficiencies.
- Missing comparisons to common multivariate/count models (Poisson log-normal, negative binomial, COM-Poisson, copula models).
- No quantification of the variational approximation error for SPGM or ablations comparing to exact truncated computations.
- No runtime or scalability analysis despite potentially heavier SPGM neighborhood selection computations.
- Proof-level details and explicit assumptions for key theorems are sometimes deferred, reducing confidence.
- Notation and condition clarity needs tightening (e.g., relation of Θ to quadratic/diagonal terms, domains, definition of B(·)).
- Reproducibility details missing: choice of R in TPGM, stability selection parameters, regularization paths.
- Paper is original and significant, providing new EF-MRF constructions with normalizability guarantees.
- Adoption depends on practical guidance (hyperparameter selection, computational performance) and theoretical guarantees (sparsistency, approximation quality).
- Pros: rigorous identification of Poisson MRF limitation, three normalizable model classes, and strong theoretical insights.
- Cons: limited baselines, lack of computational/approximation analysis, sensitivity/interpretability issues for SPGM hyperparameters, deferred technical details.
- Recommendations: add PGM and Poisson log-normal/negative-binomial baselines, include proof sketches/assumptions, analyze SPGM (R0,R) selection and sensitivity, report computational costs and variational error, provide goodness-of-fit/moment statements.
- Overall: high originality and potential impact; lean toward acceptance if empirical baselines and proof/assumption clarifications are strengthened, otherwise borderline.
