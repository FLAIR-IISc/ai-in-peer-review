- Paper tackles modeling multivariate count data and proposes TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM to overcome PGMs' inability to represent negative dependencies, validated on simulated and real miRNA data.
- Identifies the core limitation of standard PGMs: the non-negativity constraint on edge weights preventing negative dependencies.
- Demonstrates that a natural truncation (Winsorization) approach fails to resolve the negativity constraint.
- Introduces TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM with modifications to base measures and sufficient statistics to allow richer dependency structures.
- QPGM and SPGM are particularly novel and interesting for bypassing the negativity constraint.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with well-stated normalizability results (Theorems 1–4) clarifying feasibility conditions.
- Empirical results show promising performance, especially for SPGM, on both simulations and a breast cancer miRNA dataset.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
- TPGM has limited novelty and its practical value is restricted by acknowledged limitations.
- Computational challenges remain insufficiently addressed, notably normalization intractability and sparse details on optimization (proximal gradient ascent).
- Empirical evaluation is limited; broader comparisons to other count-data models and discussion of regularization choices are needed.
- Paper could better connect to existing non-Gaussian graphical-model literature (e.g., copulas, nonparametric methods).
- Review scores: Quality 8/10 (sound but scalability concerns), Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10 (TPGM less novel), Significance 8/10.
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically sound, and original work that should address computational and empirical gaps.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, proposes novel theoretically justified models, shows promising empirical results, and is clearly written.
- Arguments against acceptance: computational complexity concerns, limited empirical breadth, and limited novelty of TPGM.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
