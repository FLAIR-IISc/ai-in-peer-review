- Addresses a key gap: proposes flexible graphical models for multivariate count data (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to allow positive and negative dependencies.
- Identifies limitations of existing approaches (log-linear, copula, original PGM's negative-edge constraint).
- Strength: tackles an important and under-served problem for count data.
- Strength: provides a thorough literature review and clear articulation of prior shortcomings.
- Strength: introduces novel model extensions (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) with insightful base-measure modifications.
- Strength: theoretical rigor—propositions/theorems and proofs establish properties and limitations.
- Strength: includes simulation studies and a real-world microRNA application for empirical validation.
- Strength: generally clear and well-organized writing with strong motivation.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited in scope (few network structures, single real dataset).
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational complexity, scalability, and inference for QPGM/SPGM.
- Weakness: SPGM’s sub-linear statistic is complex and less well-motivated/interpretable than QPGM.
- Weakness: could better connect to related models (e.g., GLMMs and other exponential-family approaches).
- Quality assessment: strong theoretical analysis and overall quality.
- Clarity assessment: good, but some sections (SPGM) need simpler intuition.
- Originality assessment: high—novel contributions to Poisson graphical modeling.
- Significance assessment: high—broad potential impact across genomics, epidemiology, social science.
- Arguments for acceptance: fills an important gap, novel/theoretically sound models, promising results, well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical scope, missing computational discussion, SPGM less compelling.
- Overall assessment: strong paper, worthy of acceptance with recommended addressing of empirical and computational weaknesses.
