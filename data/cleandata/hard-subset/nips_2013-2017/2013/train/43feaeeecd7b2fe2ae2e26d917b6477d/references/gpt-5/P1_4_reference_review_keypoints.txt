- Paper addresses multivariate count MRFs that live on nonnegative integers, have tractable node-conditionals, and can represent both positive and negative dependencies, building on Poisson graphical models and node-conditional exponential families.

- Contribution 1: Shows Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals cannot form a consistent joint MRF (Theorem 1).

- Contribution 2: Proposes Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM) with exponential-family-consistent truncation yielding a finite-domain joint and normalizability without sign constraints (Theorem 2).

- Contribution 3: Proves that with linear sufficient statistics the base measure must scale at least quadratically to permit positive dependencies, motivating the Quadratic PGM (QPGM) with negative-definite Θ for normalizability (Theorem 3, Proposition 2).

- Contribution 4: Proposes Sublinear PGM (SPGM) using a saturating statistic B(·; R0,R) to bound pairwise interactions and allow normalizability for any edge weights, with variational bounds linking QPGM to Gaussian and SPGM to Poisson (Theorem 4).

- Empirics: Use ℓ1-penalized node-conditional likelihoods for TPGM and SPGM, showing they outperform Gaussian-based baselines on simulated graphs and a TCGA miRNA dataset; standard PGM recovers only negative edges.

- Theory quality: Impossibility result for Winsorization, existence/uniqueness for TPGM, necessity of quadratic base measure, and normalizability conditions for QPGM/SPGM are insightful and valuable.

- Empirics quality: Simulations are reasonable and illustrative; real-data case study is qualitative; learning procedure lacks algorithmic detail and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well-structured but has notational slips, formatting issues, and should state theorem assumptions more explicitly; more optimization and complexity details needed for reproducibility.

- Originality: High — impossibility and necessity results and the SPGM construction appear novel; QPGM–Gaussian connection is well-motivated.

- Significance: Significant for applications needing multivariate count models with mixed dependencies; models and proofs likely to spur further work.

- Main strengths: Clear problem identification and resolution; elegant theoretical results; practical model constructions with empirical support; connections to Gaussian methods aiding adoption.

- Main weaknesses / suggestions: Hyperparameter selection (R, R0) is ad hoc and needs data-driven guidance and sensitivity analysis to avoid saturation bias.

- Main weaknesses / suggestions: Provide explicit learning details — node-conditional objectives, gradients/Hessians, convexity discussion, optimization settings, and how infinite sums (e.g., D(θ,R0,R)) are approximated with error control.

- Main weaknesses / suggestions: Compare against stronger count-model baselines (e.g., Poisson log-normal, multivariate Poisson constructions) and report precision/recall or AUPRC in addition to ROC.

- Main weaknesses / suggestions: Strengthen real-data evaluation with quantitative metrics (stability selection, edge stability, held-out predictive/perplexity) and deeper biological validation.

- Main weaknesses / suggestions: Discuss practical interpretability and restrictions of negative-definite Θ in QPGM and provide examples of learned Θ patterns.

- Main weaknesses / suggestions: Make assumptions in Theorem 3 explicit and discuss tightness or possible relaxations of the quadratic growth requirement.

- Minor: Fix reference formatting, clarify notation (W, X) and constraints (e.g., R>3), and provide code or pseudo-code for Gibbs sampling and node-wise estimation.

- Decision: Accept (weak accept).

- Pros: Novel principled models for mixed-sign dependencies in counts; strong theoretical contributions; practical neighborhood selection framework with promising results.

- Cons: Hyperparameter selection and sensitivity underexplored; limited comparisons to alternative multivariate count models; insufficient learning/optimization detail.

- Reviewer confidence: High.
