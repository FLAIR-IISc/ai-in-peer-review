- Paper addresses normalizability limits of Poisson graphical models and allows mixed-sign dependencies in MRFs for count data.
- Main contribution 1: Theorem 1 shows Winsorized/trimmed Poisson node-conditionals cannot form a consistent joint MRF.
- Main contribution 2: Proposes Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM) with finite support {0,…,R} that permits positive and negative dependencies and a finite partition function.
- Main contribution 3: Shows linear sufficient statistics require quadratic base measures (Theorem 3) and introduces QPGM and SPGM as viable alternatives.
- Theorem 3: Necessity result that base measure must grow at least quadratically, C(Z)=Ω(Z^2), for mixed-sign θ with linear B(X)=X.
- QPGM: Adds quadratic base/terms (X^2) yielding normalizability if aggregate quadratic form is negative-definite (Proposition 2), enabling positive off-diagonal interactions.
- QPGM supports Gaussian-style variational upper bounds for nodewise likelihoods, aiding tractable approximations.
- SPGM: Uses sublinear sufficient statistics that saturate to ensure normalizability for all real pairwise parameters (Theorem 4) with a Poisson log-partition upper bound for approximations.
- Learning approach: ℓ1-penalized nodewise conditional likelihood is used for structure learning across models.
- Simulations (n=50–200, p=50–100) show TPGM/SPGM outperform Gaussian-based baselines (GGM, nonparanormal, SKEPTIC) in ROC.
- Real data: TCGA miRNA analysis (n=544, p=262) yields plausible hubs and clusters; original PGM recovers mostly negative edges as expected.
- Relation to prior work: Engages with Besag/Yang auto-models and alternatives like log-linear, copulas, sums-of-Poissons, and hierarchical spatial models.
- Theory quality: Theorems are well-motivated and insightful, especially the impossibility and Ω(Z^2) necessity results.
- Algorithmic concerns: Variational bounds proposed, but more details on optimization, convexity, gradients, and convergence are needed.
- Experimental concerns: Good simulations but missing key comparisons to Poisson log-normal/latent Gaussian copula and negative-binomial models.
- Clarity issues: Need precise statement and constants for Theorem 3, domain/identifiability clarifications for QPGM, and practical guidance for choosing (R0,R) in SPGM.
- Originality: High — novel impossibility proof, necessity result, and constructive QPGM/SPGM models.
- Significance: Important advance for multivariate count MRFs; potential impact in genomics, neuroscience, and spatial statistics.
- Strength: Rigorous identification of PGM limitation and principled alternative model designs with normalizability guarantees.
- Weakness: Limited empirical baselines, lack of hyperparameter selection guidance, no sparsistency guarantees, and incomplete computational/scalability analysis.
- Additional weakness: SPGM partition approximations need more detail and QPGM’s thin tails may be a practical liability.
- Suggested improvements: Add Poisson log-normal/negative-binomial baselines, sensitivity analyses for (R0,R), optimization details, robustness experiments, and clarify Theorem 3 and QPGM identifiability.
- Case study improvements: Provide quantitative validation (stability, subsampling, enrichment) for biological findings.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on stronger empirical comparisons and clearer theoretical/algorithmic exposition.
