- Addresses gap: flexible multivariate count graphical models that allow both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Shows standard Poisson graphical model requires non-positive edges and Winsorized Poisson is inconsistent (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes three solutions: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).  
- Strong theoretical contributions with clear proofs for Theorems 1–4.  
- Theorem 3: quadratic base measure is necessary for normalizability with positive edge weights (key novel insight).  
- TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM are theoretically sound and address different trade-offs.  
- Experiments show TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian-based methods on count data.  
- Evaluation could be improved by analyzing parameter sensitivity (e.g., R in TPGM), computational complexity, and convergence rates.  
- Authors acknowledge model limitations (TPGM bounded domain, QPGM Gaussian-like tails), demonstrating integrity.  
- Paper is very well written and organized; mathematical derivations and model distinctions are clear.  
- Practical implementation details for SPGM (selecting R0 and R) need more discussion.  
- Original contributions include critique of Winsorized Poisson and the novel QPGM/SPGM approaches.  
- Work is significant for applications (genomics, epidemiology) and finds biologically relevant miRNAs missed by Gaussian models.  
- Strengths: identifies fundamental limitation, rigorous theory, three motivated models, appropriate experiments, honest limitations.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of scalability/computational complexity, SPGM parameter selection, biological interpretation depth, and performance on mixed small/large counts.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — substantial theoretical and practical advance despite needing more computational exploration.  
- Specific suggested additions: discuss computational complexity, add biological context for identified miRNAs, provide practical guidance for choosing R/R0, and compare to recent multivariate Poisson literature.
