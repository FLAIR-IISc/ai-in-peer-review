- Paper identifies a fundamental limitation of existing Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs): normalizability forces only negative dependencies.  
- Authors propose novel extensions (Quadratic PGM and Sublinear PGM) that enable modeling both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with insightful proofs (notably Theorems 1 and 3) and well-motivated derivations.  
- The work is novel, using modified base measures and sufficient statistics to extend PGMs.  
- The paper is self-contained, clearly written, and places the contributions well within related literature.  
- Proposed models have clear practical relevance for genomics, epidemiology, and spatial statistics.  
- Empirical validation is preliminary and limited; more datasets and baselines (beyond hub and scale-free simulations) are needed.  
- Computational complexity and scalability of inference/estimation (partition function computation, optimization) are not fully addressed.  
- SPGM’s intuition and motivation are less clear than the QPGM’s quadratic modification and need expansion.  
- The variational upper bound is presented but its tightness and practical impact on performance are not discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant gap, theoretically sound, novel, well-written, and promising experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical evaluation, incomplete discussion of computational challenges, and weaker motivation for SPGM.  
- Qualitative verdict: excellent quality and originality, very good clarity, and high significance.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on expanded empirical evaluation and deeper discussion of computational issues.
