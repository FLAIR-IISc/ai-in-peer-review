- Paper contributions: proves Winsorized node-conditionals cannot form a joint (Theorem 1), proposes truncated Poisson EF (TPGM, Theorem 2), shows Ω(x^2) base-measure necessity for positive edges (Theorem 3), introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM), and demonstrates neighborhood selection with simulations and an RNA-seq case study.

- Theoretical assessment: Theorem 1 is plausible; Theorem 2 follows from finite support EF-MRF theory; Theorem 3 gives an insightful necessity result explaining PGM limitations.

- QPGM/SPGM validity: QPGM normalizability via negative-definite quadratic is sound and admits a Gaussian variational upper bound; SPGM normalizability follows from bounded sufficient statistics.

- Methodological gaps: optimization details are sparse—SPGM’s D(θ,R0,R) computation for θ<0 is unclear, QPGM surrogate optimization and symmetry/consensus enforcement lack specifics.

- Experimental strengths and weaknesses: simulations show ROC gains over Gaussian/nonparanormal baselines and a suggestive case study, but comparisons miss strong count-specific baselines and quantitative metrics (AUCs, CIs) are not reported.

- Clarity issues: reconcile the claim of “same sufficient statistics as Poisson” with QPGM’s quadratic term; state assumptions for Theorem 3 explicitly; clarify R0/R selection and TCGA preprocessing giving R=11.

- Originality: novel demonstration of Winsorized inconsistency, overlooked TPGM construction, key Ω(x^2) insight, and original SPGM saturating statistic.

- Significance: addresses an important limitation for count-valued networks and offers theory likely to influence future models, but broader empirical validation is needed.

- Pros: sharp theoretical contribution, clean model constructions (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM), shows Winsorized inconsistency, and proposes practical nodewise estimation.

- Cons: insufficient optimization/algorithmic detail, missing comparisons to state-of-the-art count-network methods, underexplained hyperparameter choices, and some theoretical statements need tightening.

- Actionable reviewer requests: provide proof sketch for Theorem 1 and R>3 requirement, explicitly write QPGM node-conditionals, detail SPGM learning and D approximation for negative θ, give principled R/R0 selection and sensitivity analysis, add Poisson log-normal and other count baselines with AUCs/CIs, and clarify TCGA preprocessing.

- Overall recommendation: lean toward accept conditional on resolving theoretical clarifications, expanding experimental comparisons, and adding algorithmic details.
