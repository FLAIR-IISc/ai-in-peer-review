- Paper addresses missing tools for undirected graphical models on count data, revisits Poisson graphical models (PGMs) and their negative-only edge limitation, and proves Winsorized node-conditionals cannot form a joint MRF (Thm 1).
- Introduces TPGM: a valid truncated exponential-family MRF over {0,…,R}^p that permits mixed-sign dependencies.
- Proves necessity (Thm 3) that with linear sufficient statistics B(X)=X a base measure must grow at least quadratically to allow positive edges and preserve normalizability.
- Proposes QPGM: adds quadratic self-potentials to ensure normalizability and allow mixed signs under a global negative-definiteness (Θ ≺ 0) coercivity condition.
- Proposes SPGM: keeps the Poisson base log(X!), uses a sublinear/saturating B(·) to allow arbitrary pairwise parameters and heavier tails, with a variational partition bound for θ≥0.
- Theoretical contributions are strong (impossibility result, constructive TPGM, necessity result), but some assumptions and proofs need clearer statements and caveats (e.g., growth conditions, presence of self-quadratic terms).
- QPGM normalizability condition is clear but enforces a hard global constraint that is nontrivial to impose during estimation.
- Estimation methods are only sketched (penalized node-conditional likelihoods, variational bounds) and do not enforce normalizability constraints, risking non-normalizable fitted models; no sparsistency/consistency guarantees are provided.
- Experiments show ROC gains for TPGM/SPGM on synthetic and RNA-seq data and plausible biological findings, but QPGM is not evaluated and predictive/held-out likelihoods are missing.
- Empirical evaluation omissions: no sensitivity analysis for truncation hyperparameters R/R0, no comparisons to Poisson log-normal, negative-binomial, or copula-based count models, and no predictive metrics reported.
- Clarity issues: ambiguous notation (W, Wp), unclear Θ entries (self vs cross-terms, symmetry), theorems lacking explicit assumptions/quantifiers, SPGM B(·) definition deferred to appendix, and incomplete references.
- Originality: novel and timely—winsorizing impossibility, quadratic-base necessity, and QPGM/SPGM families are valuable advances; TPGM is a useful constructive baseline.
- Significance: solving Poisson MRF sign limitations is important for many applications, but practical impact depends on tractable, constrained estimation, hyperparameter guidance, and stronger empirical validation.
- Suggested improvements: include main-text proof sketches for Thm 1/3, propose constrained estimators enforcing Θ≺0 (projected gradient or parametrizations), discuss SPGM hyperparameter selection and sensitivity, clarify preprocessing (R choice), report computational costs and variational tightness (especially for θ<0), add strong count-model baselines, and include predictive metrics.
- Pros: addresses a major deficiency of Poisson MRFs, introduces novel and interpretable model classes, provides an important negative result, and presents promising empirical findings.
- Cons: underdeveloped estimation procedures without enforced normalizability, limited empirical comparisons and diagnostics, and some theoretical results need clearer assumptions and proof outlines.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept conditional on strengthening empirical evaluation (include QPGM and stronger baselines), clarifying and justifying key theorems, and enforcing or discussing normalizability constraints in estimation.
