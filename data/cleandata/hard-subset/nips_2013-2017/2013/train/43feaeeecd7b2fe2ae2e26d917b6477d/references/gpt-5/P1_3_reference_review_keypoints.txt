- Paper shows Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals cannot form a consistent joint MRF (Theorem 1).
- Proposes Truncated PGM (TPGM) that preserves exponential-family form and is normalizable on {0,…,R}^p, allowing positive and negative edges (Theorem 2).
- Shows Poisson-like models with linear sufficient statistic require base-measure growth at least quadratic to allow positive edges (Theorem 3).
- Introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) with quadratic base measure and Θ ≺ 0 normalizability condition (Proposition 2).
- Proposes Sublinear PGM (SPGM) using factorial base measure and sub-linear sufficient statistics to ensure normalizability for all pairwise parameters (Theorem 4).
- Provides neighborhood-selection learning with ℓ1 penalties and proximal-gradient optimization.
- Derives variational upper bounds to node-conditional partition functions (Gaussian bound for QPGM; Poisson bound for SPGM when θ ≥ 0).
- Empirical results: simulations and miRNA RNA-seq case study show improved network recovery versus Gaussian/nonparanormal baselines.
- Relates work to Besag’s auto-models, Yang et al. GLM-to-MRF construction, multivariate Poisson, copula/GGM, and spatial auto-Poisson literature, and corrects Winsorization misconception.
- Theoretical results are well-motivated and appear technically sound, but some theorem statements need clearer assumptions and precise asymptotics (e.g., Theorem 3’s Ω(·)).
- Request for clearer proof sketches or intuition for key theorems (especially Theorem 1).
- Optimization concerns: need clarification on concavity/Lipschitz of node-conditional objectives and whether variational bounds are optimized as surrogates (potential bias).
- Practical computation questions: how node-conditional infinite sums are truncated/approximated and error controls for D(θ,R0,R).
- Experiments lack quantitative detail: request AUCs with variability, runtimes, sensitivity to R and (R0,R), and recovery when data are generated from SPGM/QPGM.
- Missing comparisons to overdispersed-count baselines (e.g., negative binomial, copula-Poisson) which are relevant for RNA-seq data.
- Clarity issues: notational inconsistencies (C(Z), D(θ)/A(θ)/D̄), and theorems’ assumptions/domains should be explicitly stated.
- Practical guidance missing for choosing truncation/sublinearity parameters (R, R0); suggest heuristics like quantiles or stability selection.
- Originality: corrects prior literature, provides necessary growth-rate condition, and introduces three new model families—SPGM particularly elegant.
- Significance: fills an important gap by enabling positive and negative conditional dependencies for count data; potential impact if scalable and sparsistent.
- Pros summary: addresses Poisson MRF limitation, strong theoretical contributions, complementary model families, practical learning strategy, and correction of Winsorization.
- Cons summary: no formal sparsistency guarantees, limited hyperparameter guidance, light computational detail, omitted overdispersion baselines, and some theorem precision issues.
- Suggested improvements: add proof sketches/appendix pointers, clarify Theorem 3 rigor, provide hyperparameter selection procedures and sensitivity analyses, report AUCs/confidence intervals and runtimes, add overdispersed comparisons, and clarify surrogate vs exact optimization and convexity.
- Recommendation: weak accept — substantial advances but needs clarity and stronger empirical evaluation.
