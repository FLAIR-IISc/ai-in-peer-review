

# Review of "On Poisson Graphical Models"

This paper addresses a significant limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs) that can only model negative dependencies due to constraints on edge weights. The authors propose three novel extensions: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM), which can model both positive and negative dependencies in count data. The work builds on the foundational work of [17, 18, 19] on graphical models via exponential families.

## Strengths

The paper makes a clear and important contribution by identifying a fundamental limitation of existing Poisson graphical models. The theoretical analysis is generally sound, with the authors correctly identifying that the Winsorized Poisson approach [20, 21] cannot yield a valid joint distribution (Theorem 1). The proposed TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM models represent meaningful extensions that overcome this limitation. The numerical experiments demonstrate the practical utility of these models, particularly on real-world microRNA expression data where the proposed models outperform Gaussian graphical models and the original PGM.

## Weaknesses

### Theoretical Concerns

1. **Incomplete Theoretical Justification**: Theorem 3 states "C(Z) = Ω(Z²)" for normalizability but provides no precise mathematical formulation of this asymptotic condition. The paper claims "the base measure terms ∑_s∈V C(X_s) = ∑_s∈V log(X_s!) scale more slowly than the quadratic pairwise terms X_sX_t" but fails to provide the precise asymptotic comparison needed to support this claim. Without this, the theoretical foundation of the QPGM is incomplete.

2. **Ambiguous Terminology**: The paper repeatedly claims the SPGM has "heavier tails" than QPGM without providing a clear mathematical definition of "heavier tails" in this context. In statistics, this term has precise meaning related to tail decay rates, but the paper doesn't establish this rigorously.

3. **Inconsistent Notation**: The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout (e.g., "Xs" vs "X_s" in different sections). In Section 2.1.1, the authors write "λ_s = exp(η_s) = exp(θ_s + ∑_{t∈N(s)} θ_stX_t)" but in Equation (2), they use "η_s" without defining it as a function of θ. This inconsistency could confuse readers.

4. **Missing Proof Details**: The paper references "Proposition 2 in [18]" without providing the full statement or proof, making it difficult to verify the claims about the joint distribution's form. The proof of Theorem 1 is also not provided in the main text.

### Methodological Concerns

1. **Truncation Parameter R**: The paper claims "R is a fixed positive constant" but provides no guidance on how to select R in practice. In the case study, R is set to "the maximum count value" without justification or discussion of sensitivity to this choice.

2. **QPGM's "Gaussian-esque thin tail"**: The paper states that QPGM has a "Gaussian-esque thin tail" but doesn't quantify this. Without a clear mathematical characterization, this claim is vague and unverifiable.

3. **Simulation Design**: The paper compares methods using "data of dimension n = 200 samples and p = 50 variables" generated via TPGM and "via the sums of independent Poissons method of [15]" for positive-weight edges. This is problematic because the sums-of-independent-Poissons method cannot generate negative-weight edges, making the comparison unfair to the proposed models.

### Presentation and Writing Issues

1. **Incomplete References**: The paper cites "Yahav and Shmueli [11]" but the reference list shows "I. Yahav, G. Shmueli, Arxiv preprint arXiv:0710.5670" without the full citation. This is inconsistent with the other references.

2. **Figure Reference**: The paper mentions "Figure 3 in the appendix" but the provided text doesn't include the figure, making it impossible to verify the claim about the sublinear statistic.

3. **Inconsistent Terminology**: The paper alternates between "Poisson graphical model" (PGM) and "Poisson graphical model distribution" without clear distinction, which could confuse readers.

4. **Formatting Inconsistencies**: In Section 2.1.1, the paper writes "I(Z = R) ( 1 − ∑R−1 i=0 λi i! exp(−λ) )" but the summation index should be "i" not "R-1 i=0".

## Recommendation

While the paper identifies an important limitation in existing Poisson graphical models and proposes theoretically sound extensions, the paper contains multiple significant theoretical and methodological gaps that undermine the rigor of the claims. The incomplete theoretical justification, ambiguous terminology, and inconsistent notation are particularly concerning for a NIPS submission that should be a complete piece of work.

The paper would benefit from:
1. Providing complete proofs for key theorems
2. Clarifying the asymptotic conditions for Theorem 3
3. Defining "heavier tails" precisely
4. Adding guidance for selecting truncation parameter R
5. Fixing inconsistent notation and formatting issues

Given the paper's technical shortcomings despite its potentially significant contribution, I recommend rejection. The theoretical gaps are too substantial to be overlooked in a top-tier conference like NIPS, where rigor and completeness are expected. While the proposed models are promising, the paper as written does not sufficiently establish their theoretical foundation or practical utility.

The paper's contributions would be significantly stronger with a more rigorous theoretical foundation and careful attention to the details that define a high-quality NIPS submission.
