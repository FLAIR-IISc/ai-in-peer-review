- Problem: Existing Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) are restricted to modeling negative dependencies due to normalizability constraints.  
- Goal: Systematically explore modifications to PGMs to allow positive dependencies for multivariate count data.  
- Prior fix failure: Demonstrates the truncation approach of Kaiser & Cressie (2020) fails.  
- New models: Proposes three alternatives — Truncated PGM (TPGM) with a novel truncation, Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).  
- Methodology: Modifies the Poisson base measure and sufficient statistics to achieve normalizable models permitting positive dependencies.  
- Theory: Provides rigorous mathematical proofs (Theorems 1–4) establishing normalizability and base measure requirements.  
- Empirics: Shows illustrative results on simulated networks and a breast cancer miRNA expression dataset.  
- Comparisons: Benchmarks against Gaussian GGM and Nonparanormal methods; draws parallels to Gaussian graphical model literature.  
- Strength — Motivation: Clearly articulates the limitation of existing PGMs and motivates alternative approaches.  
- Strength — Novelty: Introduces novel model families (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) with different trade-offs.  
- Strength — Theory-Practice link: Connects QPGM to Gaussian variational bounds, aiding interpretability and potential inference reuse.  
- Weakness — Accessibility: Theoretical analysis is dense and may be inaccessible without strong statistical background.  
- Weakness — Empirical scope: Limited empirical evaluation (only two simulated network structures; sparse biological validation).  
- Weakness — Computation: Insufficient discussion of computational challenges, log-partition function computation, and scalability.  
- Weakness — SPGM intuition: The motivation for the sublinear sufficient statistic is less compelling than the quadratic modification.  
- Weakness — TPGM tuning: Lacks guidance on selecting the truncation parameter R, which is likely crucial for performance.  
- For acceptance: Addresses an important limitation, presents novel and theoretically grounded models, and provides initial empirical support.  
- Against acceptance: Paper’s theoretical complexity, limited experiments, and under-addressed computational/parameter-selection issues weaken its case.  
- Overall: Strong, valuable contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to improve accessibility and empirical/computational treatment.  
- Rating: Accept with Minor Revisions.
