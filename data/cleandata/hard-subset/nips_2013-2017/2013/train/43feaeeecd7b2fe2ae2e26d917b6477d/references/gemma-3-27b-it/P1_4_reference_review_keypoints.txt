- Problem: Modeling high-dimensional multivariate count data where existing Poisson-based approaches are limited (e.g., only negative dependencies or intractable scaling).

- Key contribution: Identifies a flaw in Winsorized Poisson approaches and introduces TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM that modify the base measure/sufficient statistics to permit both positive and negative dependencies.

- Theoretical contributions: Provides theorems/propositions establishing normalizability and sufficient conditions for parameter estimation for the proposed models.

- Empirical validation: Simulation studies and a breast cancer miRNA case study show improved performance over baseline methods, especially for count data.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on Besag and Yang’s Poisson Graphical Models, critiques Winsorized approaches, and situates itself relative to Gaussian and non-paranormal graphical models.

- Strength — clarity: Clearly states the problem and motivations.

- Strength — novelty: Proposes three novel models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) that address known limitations.

- Strength — rigor: Presents formal theoretical analysis and links to existing frameworks for potential extensions.

- Strength — empirical: Demonstrates practical utility with simulations and a real-world dataset.

- Strength — exposition: Provides detailed mathematical explanations for model construction.

- Weakness — complexity: QPGM and SPGM are mathematically complex, raising the barrier for practitioners.

- Weakness — practical implementation: Scaling and computational cost, particularly for SPGM, are not fully addressed.

- Weakness — scalability discussion: Lacks detailed treatment of performance for extremely high-dimensional settings (p >> n).

- Weakness — parameter selection: No guidance or cross-validation strategies for choosing truncation/sublinearity parameters R and R0.

- Weakness — theoretical gap: Needs a fuller exposition on extending consistency results from prior works to the new models.

- For acceptance: Addresses a significant problem with novel, theoretically sound models and compelling empirical evidence.

- Against acceptance: Model complexity, limited scalability discussion, and unresolved parameter-selection issues may hinder practical adoption.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with significant contributions; weaknesses are primarily practical and suitable for revision.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Requested revisions: expand discussion of SPGM computational scalability/optimizations; provide guidance/methods for selecting R and R0; elaborate on extending prior consistency results; add limitations and future work section.
