- Addresses modeling multivariate count data and limitations of existing scalable or restrictive approaches.  
- Identifies classic Poisson Graphical Model (PGM) cannot model positive dependencies and proves Winsorized Poisson fix is incorrect (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes three novel extensions: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) with normalizability theorems.  
- Explores structure learning algorithms and demonstrates methods on simulations and a breast cancer microRNA dataset.  
- Positions work relative to prior PGM literature (Besag, Yang) and compares to copula, hierarchical, and other graphical model approaches.  
- Strength: clear problem definition and strong motivation for improved count-data graphical models.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis, including proof against Winsorized Poisson and normalizability results for new models.  
- Strength: novelty in introducing TPGM/QPGM/SPGM and using alternative base measures and sufficient statistics.  
- Strength: practical demonstrations via simulations and real data; paper is well-written and organized.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited—needs more varied network structures, parameter settings, and comparisons to microRNA-specific methods.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability of inference and parameter estimation are not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: SPGM practicality is questionable due to sensitive tuning of R0 and R; needs parameter selection guidance.  
- Weakness: justification for using a Gaussian log-partition variational bound in QPGM is weak and needs rigor or alternatives.  
- Weakness: experimental details (e.g., regularization parameter selection) are missing, hindering reproducibility.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, provides strong theoretical contributions, introduces promising models, and shows initial empirical promise.  
- Against acceptance: empirical, computational, and parameter-selection issues require further work.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution and likely acceptable pending minor revisions to expand experiments, computational discussion, and SPGM parameter clarification.  
- Final rating: Accept (with minor revisions).
