- Identifies a fundamental limitation: existing Poisson graphical models only model negative dependencies due to normalizability constraints.
- Contributions: (1) proves the Winsorized Poisson approach is invalid for graphical models. 
- Contributions: (2) proposes Truncated PGM (TPGM) allowing positive and negative dependencies on a bounded domain.
- Contributions: (3) introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) to permit richer dependencies while maintaining normalizability.
- Theoretical results are rigorous: Theorem 1 corrects prior work; Theorems 2–4 establish normalizability for the new models.
- Experimental evaluation is solid, comparing against appropriate baselines on both synthetic data and a breast cancer microRNA dataset.
- Authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., TPGM bounded domain, QPGM tail behavior) and connect theory to practice.
- Paper is exceptionally well written, with clear motivation, consistent notation, and reproducible mathematical detail.
- Numerical experiments are clearly described and supported by appropriate visualizations (ROC curves, adjacency matrices).
- Contributions are novel: correcting the literature and introducing three distinct model variants with new base measures and sufficient statistics.
- The work is significant for practitioners working with multivariate count data across domains like genomics and spatial statistics.
- Real-world impact demonstrated: methods identify biologically relevant miRNA hubs missed by Gaussian approaches and outperform Gaussian-based methods on counts.
- Strength: rigorous identification and proof of a fundamental limitation in existing PGMs.
- Strength: three theoretically sound and distinct model variants addressing the core limitation.
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical analysis and empirical validation on synthetic and real data.
- Weakness: paper could better detail computational complexity and scalability for large networks.
- Weakness: SPGM’s non-closed-form log-partition function and related inference/learning challenges are not fully addressed.
- Weakness: stronger discussion connecting theoretical properties (e.g., quadratic base) to practical performance would be helpful.
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS) — technically sound, novel, significant, and well-presented despite some computational aspects needing more detail.
