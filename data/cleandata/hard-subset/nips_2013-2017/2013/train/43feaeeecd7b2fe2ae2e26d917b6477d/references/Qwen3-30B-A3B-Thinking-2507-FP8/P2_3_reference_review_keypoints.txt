- Addresses the lack of multivariate count-data graphical models that can model both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Identifies that existing Poisson graphical models (PGMs) only permit negative edge weights due to normalizability constraints.  
- Disproves the previously proposed Winsorized Poisson approach (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes three novel models—Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM)—that overcome previous limitations.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees of normalizability for the proposed models and proves key theorems (Theorems 1–4).  
- Establishes a new insight about quadratic base-measure requirements for graphical models of count data (Theorem 3).  
- Acknowledges limitations of each model (TPGM’s bounded domain, QPGM’s thin tails) and discusses trade-offs honestly.  
- Empirical validation on simulations and a breast cancer microRNA dataset shows clear advantages over Gaussian graphical models and original PGM.  
- SPGM particularly effective at capturing heavier tails and identifying biologically relevant hub miRNAs that other methods miss.  
- Experimental design is solid with appropriate baselines, metrics (ROC, adjacency matrices), and clear figures.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and reproducible for experts.  
- Novelty is strong: three distinct approaches and clear positioning relative to prior work.  
- Significance is high given widespread need for count-data models in genomics, epidemiology, and social sciences.  
- Weaknesses: incomplete learning-algorithm details and complexity analysis; hyperparameter selection (R, R0) under-addressed; SPGM log-partition analysis could use better approximations; connections to spatial-statistics methods could be expanded.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant work with strong recommendation for acceptance.
