- Paper addresses Poisson MRF limitation of only negative pairwise parameters and proposes valid alternatives enabling positive interactions.  
- Theorem 1: Shows Winsorized (hard-truncated) Poisson node-conditionals cannot yield a consistent joint distribution.  
- Theorem 2: Proposes TPGM, a finite-support exponential-family MRF on {0,…,R}^p that yields a valid joint distribution.  
- Theorem 3: Proves necessity that with linear sufficient statistics B(x)=x, the base measure must scale at least quadratically, C(x)=Ω(x^2), to allow both signs of interactions.  
- QPGM: Introduces a Quadratic PGM using a quadratic base measure that permits positive and negative interactions under a negative-definiteness condition.  
- SPGM: Introduces Sublinear PGM that keeps the Poisson base log(x!) but uses a piecewise-linear saturating B(x) to ensure normalizability for all pairwise parameters.  
- Builds on Besag’s auto-models and Yang et al.’s exponential-family MRF framework and critiques prior Winsorized fixes.  
- QPGM is a discrete analogue to Gaussian MRFs; SPGM relates to prior saturating sufficient-statistic ideas.  
- Lacks empirical comparison to overdispersed count models (e.g., Poisson-lognormal, negative-binomial) and copula-based discrete networks.  
- Theorem 1 is a strong, nontrivial negative result but needs a proof sketch and clarification of the R>3 role.  
- Theorem 2 is sound but depends on R and reintroduces negativity constraints as R→∞; sensitivity when counts near R is a drawback.  
- Theorem 3 is insightful but requires clearer assumptions and a sketch of the divergence argument.  
- QPGM: negative-definiteness condition is clean, but identifiability and estimation trade-offs between diagonal and pairwise quadratic terms need discussion and regularization.  
- QPGM variational/Gaussian bounds for nodewise conditionals may blur discrete model interpretation and affect consistency/edge selection.  
- SPGM: practical estimation challenges include intractable node-conditional partition functions, gradient/likelihood approximation, handling θ<0 beyond the provided θ≥0 variational bound, and sensitivity to (R0,R) choice.  
- Estimation relies on ℓ1-penalized nodewise methods but defers sparsistency analysis; new sufficient statistics/base measures and variational approximations warrant at least high-level theoretical conditions.  
- Simulations: reasonable baselines (Gaussian/nonparanormal) but should include count-specific competitors, precision-recall and sample-complexity curves, symmetrization/tuning details, and runtime.  
- Case study (TCGA miRNA): promising biological findings; should add held-out predictive metrics and ablation comparing TPGM/SPGM/Gaussian under identical thresholds.  
- Clarity: overall readable; streamline notation (C, A, D, D̄), include proof sketches for main theorems, define Wp, and fully specify nodewise fitting algorithm/objectives/complexity.  
- Originality: high — novel impossibility, necessity, and new model constructions.  
- Significance: significant for enabling positive-dependence MRFs on counts, with impact for genomics and other count data.  
- Suggested improvements: add proofs/sketches for Theorems 1 & 3; practical guidance for selecting R and (R0,R); detailed estimation procedures for SPGM/QPGM including θ<0 handling and gradients; add at least one count-model baseline; address QPGM identifiability and regularization; report computational scaling.  
- Minor: fix typos and complete/consistent references.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.  
- Pros: strong theoretical contributions, practical models enabling positive dependencies, demonstrated empirical gains.  
- Cons: some proofs and estimation details are sparse, limited count-specific baselines, and hyperparameter sensitivity (R, R0) not fully addressed.
