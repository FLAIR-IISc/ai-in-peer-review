- Paper fills a gap in graphical models for multivariate count data by analyzing limitations of existing PGMs and proposing new approaches.  
- Authors show existing PGMs only capture negative dependencies due to normalizability (Proposition 1).  
- Truncated Poisson (Winsorized) approach is invalid (Theorem 1); modifying base measure/sufficient statistics yields three new models: TPGM, QPGM, SPGM.  
- Rigorous theoretical foundations provided (Theorems 1–4) addressing normalizability, tail behavior, and parameter constraints.  
- Experiments and a breast cancer miRNA network analysis demonstrate practical utility and meaningful biological findings.  
- Exceptional technical quality with comprehensive proofs and transparent discussion of limitations.  
- Novel insight that the base measure must scale quadratically to allow positive dependencies (Theorem 3); proposed models are original and well-justified.  
- High significance: addresses a widespread need for modeling both positive and negative dependencies in count data, with SPGM offering a practical solution.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized, with helpful figures and accessible presentation of theory and experiments.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of inference algorithms and computational complexity, especially for SPGM where log-partition lacks closed form.  
- Weakness: TPGM’s bounded domain limits real-world applicability and the paper lacks guidance for choosing truncation R.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to recent count-data methods (e.g., Poisson-lognormal, negative binomial) beyond Gaussian/non-paranormal baselines.  
- Final recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, original, significant, and practically valuable despite minor weaknesses.
