- Paper identifies a flaw in a recent truncation-based Poisson approach and proposes three new Poisson-based graphical models: TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM.  
- TPGM uses a novel truncation scheme to relax negativity constraints of traditional PGMs.  
- QPGM and SPGM modify the Poisson base measure (quadratic and sublinear) to permit both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Theoretical contributions include proofs invalidating the Winsorized Poisson approach and theorems on normalizability conditions.  
- Empirical validation via simulations and a breast cancer miRNA case study provides initial support and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Strength: addresses an important, timely problem in multivariate count modeling with clear motivation.  
- Strength: rigorous mathematical development and novel model formulations (quadratic and sublinear bases).  
- Strength: connects well to existing graphical model literature and extends PGM capabilities.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability of parameter estimation for QPGM/SPGM are not deeply addressed.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited; more diverse networks and data-generating processes are needed.  
- Weakness: practical selection and impact of truncation level R in TPGM require more systematic study.  
- Weakness: the chosen sublinear function in SPGM appears somewhat ad hoc and needs further justification or alternatives.  
- Weakness: some theoretical notation and presentation are dense and could be made more accessible.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contributions; recommended as Strong Accept, pending minor revisions to address weaknesses.
