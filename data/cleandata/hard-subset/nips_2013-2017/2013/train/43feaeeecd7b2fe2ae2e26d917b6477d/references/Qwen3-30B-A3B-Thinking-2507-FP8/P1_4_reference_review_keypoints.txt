- Paper identifies lack of multivariate count graphical models that can capture both positive and negative dependencies and shows existing PGMs are limited by normalizability to negative dependencies only.  
- Authors prove the Winsorized Poisson approach (Kaiser & Cressie) cannot yield a consistent joint distribution.  
- Introduces three new models—Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM)—to address the limitations of previous approaches.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorems 1–4) establishing limitations of prior methods and normalizability conditions for the proposed models.  
- QPGM and SPGM are mathematically novel constructions; the three models are genuine new contributions for multivariate count data.  
- Empirical validation via simulations and a breast cancer microRNA network shows clear advantages over Gaussian GMs and original PGM, with SPGM and TPGM finding biologically meaningful networks.  
- Authors carefully discuss limitations of each model (e.g., TPGM's bounded domain, QPGM's thin tail).  
- Paper lacks a thorough discussion of computational complexity and scalability for high-dimensional networks.  
- Insufficient comparison with more recent count-data graphical models (e.g., Allen & Liu 2013) limits contextual positioning.  
- Limited practical intuition and guidance for interpreting parameters, especially in QPGM and SPGM.  
- No sensitivity analysis exploring performance dependence on key tuning parameters (e.g., R in TPGM, R0 and R in SPGM).  
- Recommendation: accept—strong, well-motivated theoretical contributions and convincing empirical results despite presentation/discussion weaknesses.  
- Pro acceptance: solves an important problem, offers rigorous guarantees, demonstrates empirical advantages, and adds three new model classes.  
- Con acceptance: limited complexity/scalability discussion, incomplete recent-work comparisons, weak parameter interpretation guidance, and little analysis of model-choice effects.
