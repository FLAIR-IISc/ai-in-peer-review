- The problem of constructing multivariate count distributions is important and the authors correctly identify limitations of current approaches.  
- The paper proves previously suggested truncation does not yield a valid model and proposes alternatives.  
- Introducing TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM is natural and conceptually novel by modifying the base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- The model modifications are well motivated and theoretically appealing.  
- Much of the theory follows directly from Yang et al., making the contribution incremental.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and biased toward the proposed models.  
- Synthetic experiments are tailored to favor the new models, limiting generality.  
- Real-data comparisons omit copula-based alternatives, a glaring omission.  
- Results are qualitative and anecdotal; objective log-probability evaluations on test data are needed.  
- It is unclear whether the new models are necessary given poorly handled copula competitors.  
- The use of the non-paranormal marginal (which has no density) is questionable; better marginal baselines (e.g., KDE) should be considered.  
- The near-identical performance to Glasso is suspicious and suggests stronger baselines are needed.  
- The paper is generally well written and the results appear correct, but all proofs should be included in the supplement.  
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and contains a bound that doesn't aid the Gaussian-like argument.  
- Section 3.2 is central and should be explained more slowly with its figure moved into the main text for better intuition.  
- Overall: theoretically appealing and novel ideas, but limited and biased experiments leave the practical merit unclear.
