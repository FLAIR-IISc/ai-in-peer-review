- Paper addresses a key limitation of Poisson graphical models (only negative conditional correlations) and proposes three solutions: truncation, quadratic base measure, and sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Contributions are original, technically sound, and clearly motivated, with a systematic exploration of the solution space.  
- Theoretical results are valuable, notably the proof that a quadratic base measure is necessary for modeling positive correlations under certain constraints.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world data convincingly demonstrate the proposed methods' utility.  
- The truncation model’s limitation (choosing K) is noted, but truncation can be practical and effective in domains with naturally bounded or rarely large counts.  
- The critique of the quadratic model’s Gaussian-like thin tails could be nuanced: thin tails may be appropriate for data where extreme counts are genuinely rare.  
- Suggest adding brief discussion of use cases where truncation is appropriate to balance the assessment of that model.  
- Suggest commenting on applications where quadratic-tail behavior is desirable rather than strictly a limitation.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: define or cite “sparsistent” for accessibility.  
- Minor corrections: fix typo “graphical mode distributions” → “graphical model distributions,” and rephrase the sentence about the (intractable) log partition function for clarity.
