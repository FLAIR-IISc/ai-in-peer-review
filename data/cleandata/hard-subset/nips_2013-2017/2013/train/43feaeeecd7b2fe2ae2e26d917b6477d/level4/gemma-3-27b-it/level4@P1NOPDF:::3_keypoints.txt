- Proposes a construction for multivariate count distributions that respect an undirected graph’s Markov structure.  
- Enables marginal distributions over counts and yields relatively straightforward estimation algorithms.  
- QPGM exhibits marginally thinner tails than desirable.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, which undermines its role as a conditionally specified model.  
- Doubt whether SPGM can be accurately described as Poisson; QPGM may only alter the base measure.  
- Questions the motivation for using Poisson given empirical mismatch; requests a plot of the breast cancer data to assess Poisson fit.  
- Requests a plot or PMF illustration for the SPGM because its formulation appears complex.  
- Asks how R and R0 are treated/selected (assumed constants) and requests practitioner guidance.  
- Acknowledges the theoretical difficulty and interest of constructing Markov multivariate count models.  
- Criticizes the literature review as incomplete, especially the authors’ limited understanding of copulas.  
- Asserts the authors misinterpret reference [8], which addresses multivariate discrete distributions and provides MCMC/approximate inference methods.  
- Suggests the misinterpretation of [8] undermines the discussion of [11], whose key contribution is building discrete models with log-linear parameters without exponential growth.  
- Notes the paper omits extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data.  
- Observes that existing approaches (e.g., sparse precision Gaussian copula) don’t enforce MRF independence in the observable space; questions why practitioners should prefer this model over a Gaussian random field with Poisson measurements.  
- Requests a more recent reference than [20] for the claim that sequencing read counts are replacing microarrays.  
- Overall: the method offers meaningful theoretical contributions with pros and cons, but the literature review and practical motivation need strengthening.
