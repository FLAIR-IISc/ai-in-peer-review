- Problem is important and authors correctly identify limitations of current multivariate count models.  
- Theoretical contributions include proving a previously suggested truncation is invalid and proposing alternatives.  
- TPGM truncation is justified by natural interpretation; QPGM and SPGM are novel by altering base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Changes are well-motivated, but much theory follows from Yang et al., leaving practical value of models uncertain.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and biased toward the proposed models.  
- Synthetic experiments are tailored to the new models, limiting generality.  
- Real-data comparisons omit copula-based alternatives, a glaring omission.  
- Reported results are qualitative/anecdotal; need objective log-probability evaluation on test data.  
- Competing methods are used in an overly black-box way; marginal modeling choices (e.g., non-paranormal) are questionable.  
- Similar performance to Glasso is suspicious; simple baselines like Gaussian KDE should be tested.  
- Choices like R=11 could inform copula marginals and should be considered as reasonable baselines.  
- Paper is well-written; include all proofs in supplementary material.  
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and a bound seems unnecessary; expand and better explain Section 3.2 and its figure.  
- Overall: approach is appealing with some novelty, but limited/biased experiments leave the model's practical merit unclear.
