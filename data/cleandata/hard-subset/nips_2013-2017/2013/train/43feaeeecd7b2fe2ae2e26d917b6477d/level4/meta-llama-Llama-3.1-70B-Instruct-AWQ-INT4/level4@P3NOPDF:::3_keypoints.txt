- Proposes multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect the Markov structure of an undirected network.  
- Problem is long-standing and well-studied (e.g., Besag; Arnold et al.'s conditional specification literature).  
- Strength: constructions permit marginal count distributions and relatively simple estimation algorithms.  
- QPGM drawback: yields marginally thinner tails.  
- SPGM drawback: lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining conditional-specification intent.  
- SPGM may not truly be “Poisson” (doesn’t share Poisson’s defining traits); QPGM is more a base-measure modification.  
- Questions the motivation for using Poisson given its poor empirical fit for many count datasets (e.g., sequencing, breast cancer data).  
- Recommends showing the SPGM probability mass function plot because its form appears convoluted.  
- R and R0 appear treated as constants (to preserve exponential-family form), but selection/choice guidance is missing.  
- Despite limitations, the construction is theoretically interesting and commendable for the community.  
- Literature review is incomplete: misrepresents copulas, apparently inaccuracies about reference [8], and overlooks extensive spatial-statistics work on high-dimensional counts.  
- The sparse-precision Gaussian copula (ref [8]) and Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement models are relevant alternatives that deserve discussion.  
- Requests a more recent reference than [20] to support the claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays.
