- Multivariate count distribution construction is important and the authors correctly identify limitations of existing approaches, though some reservations remain.
- The paper’s theoretical contributions include disproving the validity of previously proposed truncation methods.
- Authors propose alternative constructions: TPGM truncation (intuitively justified), and QPGM/SPGM which modify base measure and sufficient statistics.
- The proposed theory closely follows Yang et al., yielding multiple model variants that require practical evaluation to determine usefulness.
- Experimental evaluation is underwhelming; synthetic experiments appear tailored to the proposed models.
- Real-world experiments omit comparisons with copula-based alternatives and present mainly anecdotal results.
- Objective evaluation metrics (e.g., test log-probability) are needed because all methods construct joint distributions.
- Copula competitors are treated too simplistically; using a non-paranormal marginal (which lacks a density) for Gaussian copula is inappropriate.
- The similar performance to Glasso is suspicious; basic baselines like Gaussian kernel density estimates might perform better.
- Choices like R=11 could implicitly correspond to marginal choices for copulas; a reasonable baseline exploration is warranted.
- Paper is generally well-written; all proofs should be included in the supplementary material.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and the presented bound adds little to the Gaussian-like argument.
- Section 3.2 is central to the method and should be explored more thoroughly; its figure should be presented and explained in the main text.
- Overall: the conditional exponential alternatives offer theoretical novelty and flexibility for mixed dependencies, but limited and biased experiments leave the practical merit unclear.
