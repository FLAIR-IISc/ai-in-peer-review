- Paper addresses limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs) that only allow negative conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints.  
- Builds on Yang et al. (2013) formalization of PGMs with univariate Poisson node-conditionals.  
- Demonstrates standard PGM requires non-positive edge weights for normalizability, limiting practical use.  
- Proposes three approaches to allow both positive and negative dependencies: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).  
- Shows Winsorized Poisson (Kaiser & Cressie, 2007) cannot yield a consistent joint distribution (Theorem 1).  
- TPGM uses natural truncation, preserves exponential-family properties, and permits positive/negative dependencies on a bounded domain.  
- QPGM employs a quadratic base measure and proves quadratic scaling is necessary for normalizability with positive edges (Theorem 3).  
- SPGM uses sub-linear sufficient statistics to keep a Poisson-like base while expanding feasible parameters and addressing tail behavior.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous with careful proofs of normalizability and distribution properties.  
- Learning via neighborhood selection is presented and empirically validated.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real microRNA-seq data show promising performance in high-dimensional settings.  
- Breast cancer miRNA case study identifies biologically meaningful hubs (e.g., mir-375, mir-10b) missed by prior methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes complex material accessible.  
- Thorough comparisons to related work and recent advances in count-data graphical models are provided.  
- Main practical limitations: TPGM requires choosing truncation level R, which lacks clear guidance.  
- QPGM may have undesirable thin (Gaussian-like) tails for heavy-tailed data applications.  
- SPGM mitigates tail issues but needs careful selection of sub-linear statistic parameters R0 and R.  
- Terminology issue: "sparsistent" used without definition and should be clarified.  
- Minor typo noted: "graphical mode distributions" should be "graphical model distributions."  
- Overall assessment: significant, original contribution that resolves a key PGM limitation and is recommended for acceptance.
