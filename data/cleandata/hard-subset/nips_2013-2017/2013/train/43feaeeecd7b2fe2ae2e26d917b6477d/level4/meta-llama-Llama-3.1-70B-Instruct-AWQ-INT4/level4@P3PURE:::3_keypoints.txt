- Addresses the challenging problem of building multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect an undirected Markov structure, but the literature review is incomplete (e.g., Besag, Arnold et al.).

- Positive: constructions allow marginal distributions over counts and yield relatively simple estimation algorithms.

- QPGM drawback: yields marginally thinner tails than desirable.

- SPGM drawback: lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the goal of a conditionally-specified model.

- Conceptual concern: unclear in what sense SPGM is “Poisson”; Poisson often poorly fits real-world count data (e.g., cancer read counts).

- Suggestion: include a plot of the SPGM probability mass function and clarify treatment/choice of parameters R and R0 (assumed constant for exponential-family status).

- Literature critique: apparent misunderstanding of copulas and misrepresentation of reference [8], which targets multivariate discrete distributions including counts.

- Literature critique: unclear discussion of reference [11], whose main point is efficient log-linear discrete model construction without exponential growth.

- Omission: neglects extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data and related approaches (e.g., sparse precision Gaussian copula models).

- Practitioner concern: unclear advantage of this approach over the longstanding structured Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement model.

- Minor: citation [20] is outdated for the claim about sequencing reads replacing microarrays; a more recent reference is needed.
