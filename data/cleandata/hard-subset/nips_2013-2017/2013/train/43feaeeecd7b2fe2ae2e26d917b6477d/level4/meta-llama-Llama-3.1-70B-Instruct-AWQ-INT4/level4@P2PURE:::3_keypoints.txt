- Paper proposes multivariate count distributions obeying an undirected Markov structure, a problem with long-standing literature (Besag; Arnold et al.).
- Positives: constructions permit marginal count distributions and yield relatively simple estimation algorithms.
- QPGM limitation: marginally thinner tails than desirable.
- SPGM limitation: lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification goal.
- Questionable label: SPGM does not exhibit clear Poisson properties; QPGM may only modify the base measure.
- Motivation concerns: Poisson is often a poor empirical fit for counts; need justification and illustrative PMF plots (e.g., for SPGM).
- Practitioner guidance missing: R and R0 are treated as constants but selection/estimation procedures are unclear.
- Literature review issues: incomplete and sometimes incorrect—misunderstands copulas and misrepresents refs [8] and [11].
- Major omission of spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data, and insufficient comparison to Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement models.
- While theoretically interesting, practical advantages over existing, simpler models are unclear.
- Request for update: include a more recent reference than [20] to support the claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays.
