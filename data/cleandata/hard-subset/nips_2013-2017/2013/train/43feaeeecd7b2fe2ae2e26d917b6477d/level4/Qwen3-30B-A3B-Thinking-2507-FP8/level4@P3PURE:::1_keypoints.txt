- Introduces novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Identifies limitation in Yang et al.'s "default" model: requires negative correlations for normalization.  
- Proposes several refinements: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary under fixed conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Provides experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets showing meaningful advances.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and makes significant contributions; technical arguments are sound.  
- Reviewer finds the claimed "major drawbacks" of the truncation model unconvincing; truncation may be useful in many low-count domains.  
- Reviewer questions the claim that the quadratic model is inherently unsuitable, noting Gaussian-like thin tails may be appropriate in some contexts and stronger justification is needed.  
- Minor correction: change "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model" (p.3, above 2.1.1).  
- Minor correction: change "Note that the log..." → "Note that although the log..." (p.5, above 3.1).  
- Note: the term "sparsistent" is unfamiliar to the reviewer but appears established in the literature.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality paper with strong technical merit.
