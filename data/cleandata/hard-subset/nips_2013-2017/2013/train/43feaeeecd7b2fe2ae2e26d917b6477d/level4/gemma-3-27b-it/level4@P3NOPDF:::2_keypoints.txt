- Addresses an important problem: constructing multivariate distributions for count data and identifies limitations of existing approaches (with a noted reservation).
- Provides a proof that previously suggested truncation methods are invalid.
- Proposes alternatives: TPGM truncation (natural interpretation), and novel QPGM and SPGM via modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.
- Modifications are well-motivated and intuitive, but much of the theory follows Yang et al., leaving practical value uncertain.
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and appears biased toward the proposed models in synthetic experiments.
- Real-data experiments omit comparisons to copula-based alternatives.
- Reported results are mostly qualitative/anecdotal; objective metrics like held-out log-probability are needed.
- Copula baselines are handled in a “black-box” way; better to use well-chosen marginals than the non-paranormal.
- Using non-paranormal marginals seems driven by theoretical convenience rather than practical performance.
- Near-identical performance to Glasso is suspicious; simpler methods (e.g., Gaussian kernel density estimation) might do better.
- Choices like R=11 on real data could inform sensible copula marginal selection; baselines require more justification.
- Paper is generally well-written; include all proofs in the supplementary material.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and its bound adds little; section 3.2 should be presented more clearly with the supplementary figure moved to the main text.
- Overall: conditional exponential constructions are appealing and novel theoretically, but limited/biased experiments leave their practical advantage unclear.
