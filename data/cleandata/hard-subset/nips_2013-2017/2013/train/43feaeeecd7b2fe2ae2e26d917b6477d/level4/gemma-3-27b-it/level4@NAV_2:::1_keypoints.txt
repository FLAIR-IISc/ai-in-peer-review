- Proposes new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables to allow both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Argues the standard Yang et al. model is limited to negative correlations for normalizability.  
- Introduces three modifications: a novel truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic with theoretical justification, and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Provides experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and makes a significant contribution to modeling multivariate count data.  
- Problem importance: modeling positive and negative dependencies in count data has broad applications and the approaches are compelling.  
- Reviewer finds discussion of truncation and quadratic model limitations somewhat unconvincing; truncation may work when counts are inherently bounded and quadratic’s thin tails can be appropriate.  
- Reviewer is unfamiliar with the term “sparsistent” but accepts its established use.  
- Minor editorial corrections: "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model distributions"; "Note that the log..." → "Note that although the log...".  
- Strengths: originality, significance, technical soundness, clarity, and empirical validation.  
- Weaknesses: need more in-depth discussion of truncation/quadratic limitations and additional analysis of statistical properties (e.g., consistency, convergence rates).
