- Paper introduces new models for joint distributions over integer-valued count variables.
- Argues Yang et al.’s default model is inadequate because normalization forces it to capture only negative correlations.
- Proposes several remedies: a new truncation scheme, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary when other components fixed), and a sublinear sufficient statistic.
- Provides empirical results on both synthetic and real datasets.
- Paper is clearly written, original, and presents significant results.
- Reviewer is not fully convinced that the “big negatives” of truncation and quadratic models are decisive.
- Notes truncation may work well when counts rarely become large.
- Raises possibility that Gaussian-like thin tails from the quadratic model might be appropriate in some cases.
- Reviewer unfamiliar with the term “sparsistent,” though acknowledges it has prior usage.
- Two minor editorial suggestions: change “graphical mode distributions” to “model,” and clarify “Note that the log...” to something like “Note that although the log.”
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-written paper on modeling joint distributions over count variables.
