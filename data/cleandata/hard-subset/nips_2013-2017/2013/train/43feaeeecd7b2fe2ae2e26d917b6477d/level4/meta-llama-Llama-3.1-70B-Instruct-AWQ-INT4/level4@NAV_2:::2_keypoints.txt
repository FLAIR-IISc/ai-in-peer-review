- Problem importance: constructing multivariate distributions for count data is important and current approaches have limitations.  
- Theoretical contribution: paper proves previously suggested truncation is invalid and proposes alternatives.  
- TPGM: truncation of the Poisson graphical model is justified by its natural interpretation.  
- QPGM and SPGM: novel ideas by modifying the base measure and sufficient statistics of the exponential-family Poisson.  
- Motivation: proposed modifications are well motivated and appealing.  
- Novelty caveat: much of the theory directly follows Yang et al., leaving multiple model variants whose practical merit is unclear.  
- Related work: paper situates ideas well with prior NIPS work and properly acknowledges Yang et al.  
- Strength — exposition: clear, well-motivated introduction and good overview of main ideas.  
- Strength — originality: proposed models are novel and have theoretical novelty.  
- Weakness — experiments: experimental evaluation is limited, potentially biased, and anecdotal.  
- Missing comparisons: no comparison to copula-based alternatives and marginal model choices are unjustified.  
- Evaluation metrics: lacks objective evaluation (e.g., test log-probability) and reasonable baselines.  
- Clarity score: paper is less clear on experiments and result summaries.  
- Significance score: significance is reduced by limited empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: revise to include comprehensive, objective experiments (including copula baselines) and clearer model explanations.  
- Pro-acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers novel models, and is well written.  
- Con-acceptance: weak experimental validation, missing copula comparison, and anecdotal results.
