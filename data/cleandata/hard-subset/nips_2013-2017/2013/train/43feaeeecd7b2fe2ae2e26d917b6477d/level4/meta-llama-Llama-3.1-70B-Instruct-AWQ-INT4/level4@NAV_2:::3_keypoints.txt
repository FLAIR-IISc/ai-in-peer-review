- Paper constructs multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect the Markov structure of an undirected graph.  
- Building such multivariate count distributions is a long-standing hard problem; the paper makes a nontrivial contribution.  
- Authors propose three novel models: Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM).  
- Constructions allow for marginal distributions over counts and lead to relatively simple estimation algorithms (neighborhood selection).  
- TPGM and SPGM empirically outperform Gaussian graphical models and other baselines on simulated networks and a breast cancer microRNA-seq dataset.  
- QPGM exhibits marginally thinner tails, which may be a modeling drawback.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, complicating analysis and use.  
- Literature review is incomplete: missing discussion of copulas, log-linear models for contingency tables, and extensive spatial statistics work on high-dimensional counts.  
- Authors should provide guidance for choosing SPGM tuning parameters R and R0, which are crucial for performance.  
- A probability mass function plot for the SPGM would help readers understand its behavior.  
- The approach is theoretically interesting and contributes to the field, justifying publication consideration.  
- Practical limitations (tail behavior, conditional form, parameter guidance) may hinder applicability and argue against acceptance without revision.  
- Empirical evaluation uses iid samples and neighborhood selection to recover network structure; results support the proposed methods but could be broadened.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution but needs better contextualization in prior work and more practical guidance for SPGM.
