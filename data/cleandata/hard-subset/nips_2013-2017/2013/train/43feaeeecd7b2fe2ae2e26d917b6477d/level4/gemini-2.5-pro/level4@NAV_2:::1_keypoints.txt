- Identifies a core limitation of standard Poisson graphical models (PGMs): they can only model negative conditional dependencies due to normalizability on an infinite domain.
- Refutes a previous "Winsorized" Poisson conditional as not yielding a valid joint distribution (Theorem 1).
- Proposes a valid Truncated PGM (TPGM) based on an exponential family formulation to handle bounded counts (Theorem 2).
- Shows that linear sufficient statistics require a quadratic base measure to permit positive dependencies, leading to the Quadratic PGM (QPGM) (Theorem 3).
- Introduces the Sublinear PGM (SPGM) that uses sub-linear sufficient statistics to ensure normalizability for any real pairwise parameters and supports heavier tails (Theorem 4).
- Demonstrates empirical performance of TPGM/QPGM/SPGM on simulated data and a real microRNA sequencing dataset, outperforming Gaussian-based and standard PGM baselines for relevant settings.
- Strength: Significant contribution addressing an important open problem in multivariate count modeling with practical relevance to genomics.
- Strength: Original work, including an important negative result and three novel model classes motivated by theoretical analysis.
- Strength: Technically rigorous with proofs for main claims and a clear experimental evaluation.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with a logical progression among models.
- Weakness: The critique dismissing TPGM and QPGM may be overstated; truncation or Gaussian-like tails may be acceptable in many real-world settings and should be qualified.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis for structure recovery (sparsistency); a brief discussion on how existing sparsistency analyses might extend would strengthen the paper.
