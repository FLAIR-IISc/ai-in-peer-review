- Proposes new undirected graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to allow both positive and negative conditional dependencies for multivariate count data.
- Shows Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals cannot produce a consistent joint distribution (Theorem 1).
- Introduces a truncation that preserves exponential-family node form and yields a valid Truncated PGM (TPGM).
- Proves necessity of at least quadratic base-measure growth for linear sufficient statistics to permit both sign edges (Theorem 3).
- Proposes Quadratic PGM (QPGM) using quadratic base measures, normalizable under negative-definite/pointwise-negative quadratic forms.
- Proposes Sublinear PGM (SPGM) using saturating sufficient statistics to allow arbitrary pairwise parameters while keeping Poisson base measure.
- Uses ℓ1-penalized neighborhood selection for estimation; provides a Gaussian variational bound for QPGM node-conditionals.
- Theoretical contributions clarify misconceptions in spatial stats and provide structural insights into exponential-family graphical models.
- Empirical results on simulated hub/scale-free graphs and TCGA miRNA-seq data show improved structure recovery over Gaussian-based baselines.
- Real-data networks recover biologically plausible hubs (e.g., miR-375, miR-10b).
- Suggestion: give intuition or sketch for Theorem 1 and discuss the R > 3 assumption and small-R behavior.
- Suggestion: clarify whether the Gaussian variational bound for QPGM biases estimation or affects sparsistency.
- Suggestion: report additional evaluation metrics (precision at fixed recall, sign accuracy, parameter error) and computational time.
- Suggestion: provide practical guidance and sensitivity analyses or selection procedures for hyperparameters R (TPGM) and R0, R (SPGM).
- Clarity note: define “sparsistent” on first use and fix minor wording/typos.
- Suggestion: add a brief contrast with other multivariate Poisson constructions (sum-of-Poissons, Poisson-lognormal, copulas).
- Originality: presents a rigorous negative result, a new truncation scheme, and novel QPGM/SPGM models addressing thin-tail issues.
- Significance: fills an important gap by enabling positive and negative conditional dependencies for count data in a principled MRF framework.
- Pros: strong theory, practical new models, tractable estimation, and convincing real-data application.
- Cons: limited practical guidance on hyperparameters, reliance on a variational bound for QPGM without clarified impacts, missing count-specific baselines and diagnostics.
- Concern: computational complexity and scalability (p, n) not thoroughly discussed.
- Overall: well-written, original, and significant paper; recommended for acceptance pending clarifications on Theorem 1, hyperparameter selection, and expanded empirical evaluation.
