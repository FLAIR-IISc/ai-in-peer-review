- Addresses an important problem: constructing multivariate distributions for count data and limits of existing approaches.
- Proposes novel modifications to Poisson graphical models (Truncated PGM, Quadratic PGM, Sublinear PGM) to allow positive as well as negative dependencies.
- Provides a valuable proof correcting the invalidity of a previously proposed truncation approach [20].
- Theoretical contributions are significant and well-motivated, building on exponential family and prior work (Yang et al.).
- Much of the core theory relies on prior work, so practical merit depends on empirical validation.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: synthetic data may be tailored to favor the authors’ models.
- Real-data experiments omit important copula-based baselines and rely on qualitative, anecdotal results.
- Lacks objective quantitative evaluation (e.g., held-out log-probabilities) for joint distributions.
- Comparisons to existing methods seem unfair—marginal choices for copula methods are suboptimal and treated as “black boxes.”
- Specific baseline choices (e.g., truncation R=11) are questionable and could be reframed within copula marginal selection.
- Paper is well-written and correct, but proofs should be moved to supplementary material for readability.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and its bound weakly supports the “Gaussian-esque” claim.
- Section 3.2 (SPGM) is the core innovation and needs clearer presentation and inclusion of the sublinear-statistic figure in the main text.
- Strengths: theoretical novelty, sound derivations, clear motivation, and correction of prior error.
- Weaknesses: limited empirical validation, biased/insufficient comparisons, qualitative real-data results, and some overly detailed exposition.
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on addressing evaluation concerns and improving presentation of SPGM and key figures.
