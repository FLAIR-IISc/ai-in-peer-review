- Introduces several new models for joint distributions over integer count variables.
- Argues Yang et al.’s “default” model is unsatisfactory because it must enforce negative correlations to be normalizable.
- Proposes three remedies: a truncation approach, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proven necessary), and a sublinear sufficient statistic model.
- Presents experiments on both synthetic and real datasets.
- Paper is well-written, appears original, and makes significant contributions to multivariate count modeling.
- Reviewer questions the claimed negatives: truncation may be fine in domains where counts are naturally small.
- Reviewer questions the quadratic-model critique: Gaussian-like thin tails can be appropriate in some cases.
- Reviewer is unfamiliar with the term “sparsistent.”
- Minor typos: “graphical mode distributions” → “graphical model distributions”; clarify “Note that the log …” wording. 
- Overall summary: high-quality paper with thoughtful methodological advances and reasonable empirical support.
