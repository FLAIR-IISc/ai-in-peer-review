

The paper introduces novel models for representing joint distributions over integer-count variables. The authors contend that the "default" model derived from Yang et al. is unsatisfactory, as it can only model negative correlations to ensure distribution normalization. They propose several refinements, including a new truncation method, a quadratic base measure statistic (which they prove is necessary when other components are fixed), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic. Experimental results are presented on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  

This is a well-written paper presenting original and significant contributions to count data modeling.  

However, I find the "major drawbacks" of the truncation and quadratic models somewhat unconvincing. For truncation, domains with typically low counts might yield excellent performance, making the limitation less universal. Regarding the quadratic model, "Gaussian-like thin tails" could be appropriate in certain applications.  

I am unfamiliar with the term "sparsistent," though I note its prior usage in the literature.
