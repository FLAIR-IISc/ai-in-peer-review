- Novel method for multivariate count distributions respecting MRF independence structure.  
- Achieves valid marginal distributions and relatively simple estimation algorithms.  
- QPGM has marginally thinner tails than Poisson, limiting use with overdispersed data.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining conditional-specification motivation.  
- The term "Poisson" for SPGM is questionable given its construction.  
- Practical motivation is weak because Poisson marginals often poorly fit real count data.  
- Authors should provide empirical evidence (e.g., histogram of breast cancer data) and a PMF plot for SPGM.  
- Need clear guidance on choosing/practical treatment of parameters R and R0.  
- Related-work discussion is superficial and contains inaccuracies (misunderstands copulas; misrepresents refs [8] and [11]).  
- The paper neglects extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional counts and alternative models.  
- The authors must better contextualize benefits versus standard alternatives (e.g., Gaussian random fields with Poisson observations).  
- Minor: update or replace the decade-old reference about sequencing reads replacing microarrays.
