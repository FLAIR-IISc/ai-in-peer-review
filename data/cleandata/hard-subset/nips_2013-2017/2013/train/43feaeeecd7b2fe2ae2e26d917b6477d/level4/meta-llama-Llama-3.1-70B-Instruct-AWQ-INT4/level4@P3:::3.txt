This paper proposes a novel construction for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that obey the Markov structure of an undirected network. Building a multivariate distribution over unbounded counts is a challenging problem, and the authors' proposal succeeds in some relevant ways. The main contributions of the paper are the development of three novel approaches to Poisson graphical models, namely, the Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), the Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and the Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM).

The TPGM approach involves truncating the Poisson distribution and using it as the node-conditional distributions in the construction of a graphical model. This approach allows for both positive and negative dependencies among variables, but it has some limitations, such as the domain of the variables being bounded a priori by the distribution specification.

The QPGM approach modifies the Poisson distribution by changing its base measure to a quadratic function. This allows for both positive and negative edge-parameters, but the resulting distribution has a Gaussian-esque thin tail.

The SPGM approach modifies the Poisson distribution by changing its sufficient statistics to a sublinear function. This approach allows for a broader set of feasible pairwise parameters and has a heavier tail than the QPGM.

The authors demonstrate the performance of their methods using simulated data and a real-world dataset of breast cancer microRNA expression. The results show that the proposed methods outperform existing methods, such as Gaussian graphical models and the original Poisson graphical model.
