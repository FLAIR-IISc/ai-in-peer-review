- Addresses a key limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs): inability to model positive conditional dependencies, proposing three novel extensions (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM).
- Situates work in prior literature (Besag 1974; Yang et al. 2012) showing node-conditional Poissons yield valid joint distributions but only negative edges.
- Proves the Winsorized Poisson approach (Kaiser & Cressie 2006) cannot produce a valid joint distribution (Theorem 1), correcting a misconception.
- Introduces Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) and derives normalizability conditions with rigorous proofs (Theorems 3–4).
- Provides the first count-data graphical model classes capable of both positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Empirical results show the proposed models outperform Gaussian-based approaches on simulated data and a real miRNA sequencing dataset.
- Compelling case study: breast cancer miRNA networks where proposed models find biologically meaningful hubs (mir-375, mir-10b) missed by original PGM and Gaussian models.
- Weak linkage to broader literature: insufficient discussion of spatial count models (e.g., Cressie & Wikle, Gelfand et al.) and related work in spatial statistics.
- Misleading dismissal of copula-based methods; nonparanormal copula models can capture conditional independence in transformed spaces and deserve nuanced comparison.
- Practical concerns about SPGM: lack of closed-form conditional distributions undermines the conditional-specification approach.
- Insufficient guidance on choosing hyperparameters (R, R0) and missing parameter-sensitivity experiments that are critical for practitioners.
- Not enough justification for preferring these models over simpler, established alternatives (Gaussian copula or latent Gaussian Poisson models commonly used in genomics).
- Unsupported claim that count data are "replacing microarrays"—needs more recent citations and evidence.
- Overall: technically rigorous, original, and significant; empirical validation could be stronger.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on strengthening literature connections and providing clearer practical guidance on model selection and hyperparameters.
