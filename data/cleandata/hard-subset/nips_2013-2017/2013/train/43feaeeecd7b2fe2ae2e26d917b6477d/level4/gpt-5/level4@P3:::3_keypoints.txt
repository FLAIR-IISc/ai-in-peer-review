- Paper addresses why standard Poisson MRFs forbid positive interactions and proposes three compatible joint models allowing both signs of dependency (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM).
- Theorem 1: Winsorized (hard-truncated) node-conditionals cannot form a compatible joint MRF.
- Introduces TPGM: an exponential-family truncation that redistributes tail mass to obtain a normalizable joint without sign constraints.
- Theorem 3: With linear sufficient statistic B(x)=x, any normalizable joint allowing positive pairwise interactions requires a base measure growing at least quadratically.
- Introduces QPGM: replaces log x! base measure with a quadratic term to permit positive and negative interactions under a negative-definite quadratic form.
- Introduces SPGM: retains Poisson base measure but uses a sublinear B(x) that flattens beyond a threshold to obtain heavier tails than QPGM.
- Uses ℓ1-penalized neighborhood selection and variational upper bounds for SPGM node-conditional normalizers for structure learning.
- Empirical results on simulations and TCGA microRNA-seq show improved edge recovery over Gaussian/nonparanormal baselines and original PGM.
- Strength: Clear diagnosis of the canonical PGM limitation and a rigorous impossibility result for the Winsorized approach.
- Strength: Presents three principled, compatible model classes expanding feasible dependency structures for counts.
- Strength: Estimation via penalized node-conditional likelihood fits naturally into exponential-family MRF framework, suggesting scalability.
- Weakness: SPGM computation relies on an upper bound for the node-conditional normalizer—unclear what is optimized, how bounds are used in proximal updates, and whether estimators are consistent.
- Weakness: Choice and role of SPGM thresholds R and R0 are underexplained—unclear if they’re global or node-specific and how they’re selected in practice.
- Weakness: TPGM truncation level R set to observed maximum in simulations; needs discussion of bias–variance trade-offs and guidance when counts are unbounded.
- Weakness: Theorem 1 is important and warrants a proof sketch in the main text for intuition.
- Clarity: Naming SPGM as “Poisson-like” is misleading since univariate marginals are not Poisson; recommend clearer terminology.
- Clarity: Recommend plotting univariate PMFs and B(x; R0,R) to visualize tail behavior and provide optimization/experimental details (step sizes, stopping criteria, stability selection).
- Originality: Novel necessity result on base measure growth and impossibility of Winsorized approach meaningfully delimit design space for count MRFs.
- Significance: Modeling both positive and negative conditional dependencies for counts is important, but comparisons to Poisson log-normal and copula-based discrete GGMs are missing.
- Significance: For RNA-seq case study, recommend marginal histograms/overdispersion diagnostics and updated references to justify count-modeling assumptions.
- Pros for acceptance: Strong theoretical contributions, practical model classes, and demonstrated empirical improvements on relevant datasets.
- Cons for acceptance: Incomplete empirical comparisons to prominent alternatives, limited hyperparameter selection guidance, and unclear computational guarantees for SPGM bounds.
- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance after addressing clarity, empirical comparisons, hyperparameter guidance, and computational exposition.
