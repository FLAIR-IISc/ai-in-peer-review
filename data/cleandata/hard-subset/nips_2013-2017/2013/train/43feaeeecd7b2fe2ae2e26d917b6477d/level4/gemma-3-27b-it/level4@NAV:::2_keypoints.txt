- Addresses an important problem: constructing multivariate distributions for count data, with only a minor reservation about scope.  
- Provides a corrective proof invalidating a previously proposed truncation and introduces alternative approaches.  
- TPGM truncation is simple and well-justified due to clear interpretability.  
- QPGM and SPGM are novel in how they modify the Poisson base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- The proposed modifications are well-motivated and intuitively appealing.  
- Much of the underlying theory follows Yang et al., so the contributions need practical validation.  
- Experimental evaluation is underwhelming and appears biased toward the proposed models.  
- Synthetic experiments seem tailored to favor the new models rather than being broadly convincing.  
- Real-data experiments lack comparisons to copula-based alternatives and rely on qualitative/anecdotal results.  
- A quantitative evaluation metric (e.g., test log-probability) is necessary for assessing joint-distribution performance.  
- Copula-based competitors were used opaquely and with questionable marginal choices (non-paranormal), undermining baseline strength.  
- The near-identical performance to Glasso is concerning; simpler alternatives (e.g., Gaussian KDE) might outperform the chosen baselines.  
- Practical choices (e.g., using R=11) could inform better copula marginal selection; more sensible baselines are warranted.  
- Paper is well-written; recommend moving proofs to the supplement and reducing detail in section 3.1’s bound.  
- Section 3.2 is central and needs more emphasis: include the figure in the main text and expand its intuition.  
- Overall: theoretically appealing and innovative but limited and somewhat biased experiments leave practical value uncertain.
