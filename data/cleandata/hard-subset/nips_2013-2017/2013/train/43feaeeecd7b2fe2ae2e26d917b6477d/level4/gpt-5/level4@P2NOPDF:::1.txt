The paper proposes several new models for joint distributions over integer-valued (count) variables. The authors argue that the default construction from Yang et al. is unsatisfactory because normalization forces the model to capture only negative correlations. They then explore a sequence of remedies: a new truncation approach, the use of a quadratic base measure statistic (which they prove is necessary given the rest of the setup), and finally a sublinear sufficient statistic. They report experiments on both synthetic and real datasets.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that offers thoughtful solutions for modeling count data. The contributions appear original, and the results are significant.

That said, I find the stated “major drawbacks” of the truncation and quadratic models less convincing. For truncation, there are many practical settings where counts are naturally small or effectively bounded, in which case truncation may be a perfectly reasonable modeling choice. For the quadratic model, thin-tailed (Gaussian-like) behavior can often be appropriate, depending on the application. It would help to more clearly delineate when these drawbacks become problematic in practice and to provide additional empirical evidence or guidance on choosing among the proposed alternatives.

I am unfamiliar with the term “sparsistent,” though I recognize it has been used in the literature. Please define it at first use to aid readers who may not know the term.

Minor comments and editorial suggestions:
- Page 3, above Section 2.1.1: “graphical mode distributions” should be “graphical model distributions.”
- Page 5, above Section 3.1: The sentence beginning “Note that the log...” reads ambiguously; do you mean “Note that although the log ...”? Please clarify the intended contrast or qualification.

In summary, this is a high-quality, clearly written paper on modeling joint distributions over count variables, with compelling ideas and solid experiments. I recommend acceptance, with the caveats above addressed to strengthen the exposition and practical guidance.
