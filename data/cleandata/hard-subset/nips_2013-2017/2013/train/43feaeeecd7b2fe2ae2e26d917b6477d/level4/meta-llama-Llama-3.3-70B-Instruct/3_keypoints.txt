- Paper proposes a method to construct multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect the Markov structure of an undirected graph.  
- Strength: can define marginal distributions over counts and yields relatively simple estimation algorithms.  
- Weakness: QPGM exhibits thinner tails, which may misrepresent count data tail behavior.  
- Weakness: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the goal of a conditionally specified model.  
- Critique: Characterizing SPGM as Poisson is questionable and its relation to classical Poisson distributions is unclear.  
- Critique: Poisson models may be inappropriate for many empirical count datasets that exhibit overdispersion or other deviations.  
- Suggestion: Include a plot of the SPGM probability mass function to clarify its properties.  
- Request: Clarify the role and selection of parameters R and R0 (assumed constants for exponential-family form) and provide practitioner guidance.  
- Literature critique: Review is superficial and overlooks copula-based approaches and specific prior work (e.g., [8]) with MCMC/approximate inference for discrete/count data.  
- Misplaced/mischaracterized discussion of [11], which focuses on discrete models with log-linear parameters rather than exponential complexity growth.  
- Significant omission: neglect of extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data and related modeling approaches.  
- Practical concern: Although theoretically valid, the advantage of this model class over simpler alternatives (e.g., structured Gaussian random fields with Poisson measurement models) is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Minor request: Update application context (gene expression analysis) with more recent references post-microarray era (RNA-seq/count-based analyses).
