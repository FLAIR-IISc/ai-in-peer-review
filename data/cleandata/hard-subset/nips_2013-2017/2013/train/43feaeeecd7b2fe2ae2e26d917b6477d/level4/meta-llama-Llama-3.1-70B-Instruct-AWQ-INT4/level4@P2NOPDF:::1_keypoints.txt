- Proposes novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables addressing limitations of existing approaches.  
- Argues the standard model (Yang et al.) is insufficient because it can only capture negative correlations for normalization.  
- Introduces a new truncation method to mitigate the standard model's limitations.  
- Introduces a quadratic base-measure statistic and proves its necessity within the existing framework.  
- Proposes a sub-linear sufficient statistic as an additional modeling modification.  
- Provides experimental validation on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and presents a significant contribution.  
- Reviewer finds the authors' emphasis on the “big negatives” of truncation and quadratic models unconvincing — truncation may work when counts are low and quadratic (Gaussian-like thin tails) may be appropriate in some contexts.  
- Notes unfamiliarity with the term “sparsistent,” but acknowledges it is established in the field.  
- Lists minor editorial suggestions: correct “graphical mode distributions” → “graphical model distributions,” and rephrase a sentence on page 5 for clarity.
