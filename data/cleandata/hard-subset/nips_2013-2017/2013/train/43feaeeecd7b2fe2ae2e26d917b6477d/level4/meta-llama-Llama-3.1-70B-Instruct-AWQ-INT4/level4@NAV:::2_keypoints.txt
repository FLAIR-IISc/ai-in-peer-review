- Problem importance noted: constructing multivariate count distributions is crucial and authors correctly identify limits of existing methods.  
- Theoretical contribution: proof that prior truncation methods do not yield valid models and introduction of alternatives.  
- TPGM truncation: simple but justified by a natural interpretation.  
- QPGM and SPGM: novel in focusing on the base measure and sufficient statistics to modify the Poisson exponential-family form.  
- Theory dependency: builds substantially on Yang et al., producing multiple model variants that require empirical validation.  
- Experiments weak: evaluation is underwhelming given the paper’s practical aims.  
- Synthetic experiment: promising but designed to favor the proposed models.  
- Real-data evaluation: lacks comparison to copula-based alternatives and is mainly qualitative/anecdotal.  
- Missing metric: need objective evaluation (e.g., test log-probability) since methods construct joint distributions.  
- Copula baselines criticized: used too simplistically and the non-paranormal marginal choice is questionable for practical comparisons.  
- Suspicious baselines: comparable performance to Glasso is dubious; simpler methods (e.g., Gaussian KDE) might do better.  
- Hyperparameter choices (e.g., R=11) could equivalently tune copula marginals; more reasonable baselines needed.  
- Presentation: paper is generally well-written; include all proofs in the supplement.  
- Revision suggestions: shorten section 3.1 (remove excessive detail and the bound) and expand/explain section 3.2 and its figure.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically appealing and allows mixed dependencies, but experimental bias and limited evaluation leave practical merit unclear.
