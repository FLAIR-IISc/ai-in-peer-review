The paper provides a construction for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that obey the Markov structure of an undirected network. Building a multivariate distribution over unbounded counts is in general a hard problem, as studied at length by Besag and others for the past 30 years. 

The authors' proposal succeeds in some relevant ways. The constructions allow for marginal distributions over counts and lead to relatively simple estimation algorithms. However, the Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) has marginally thinner tails, and the Sub-Linear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) does not have closed-form conditional distributions. 

It is unclear what the main motivation for modeling counts is, as the Poisson itself is not a good distribution to fit empirical data. A plot of the probability mass function of the SPGM would be useful, as it feels somewhat convoluted at first sight. The choice of R and R0 as constants is also unclear, and advice for practitioners on how to choose these parameters would be helpful.

The paper's effort to construct a multivariate distribution over counts obeying the independence model of a Markov random field is appreciated, and the results are of theoretical interest to NIPS. However, the literature review feels incomplete, with some references presented in the wrong way or omitted altogether. For instance, the authors seem not to understand the concept of copulas, and the discussion of certain references is unclear.

The paper's contribution is valid, but as a practitioner, it is unclear why one should choose this model space over simpler alternatives, such as a structured Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement model. A reference newer than 2011 claiming that counts of sequencing reads are replacing microarrays would also be appreciated.

Strengths:
- The paper provides a construction for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that obey the Markov structure of an undirected network.
- The constructions allow for marginal distributions over counts and lead to relatively simple estimation algorithms.

Weaknesses:
- The QPGM has marginally thinner tails.
- The SPGM does not have closed-form conditional distributions.
- The literature review feels incomplete.
- It is unclear what the main motivation for modeling counts is.
- The choice of R and R0 as constants is unclear.

Acceptance:
- The paper's contribution is valid, but its practical implications are unclear.
- The paper's results are of theoretical interest to NIPS.
- However, the literature review and presentation of certain references are unclear.

Recommendation:
- The paper could be improved by providing a more complete literature review and clarifying the presentation of certain references.
- The authors could also provide more guidance on how to choose the parameters R and R0.
- A plot of the probability mass function of the SPGM would be useful.
- The authors could also discuss the practical implications of their model and why it is preferable to simpler alternatives.
