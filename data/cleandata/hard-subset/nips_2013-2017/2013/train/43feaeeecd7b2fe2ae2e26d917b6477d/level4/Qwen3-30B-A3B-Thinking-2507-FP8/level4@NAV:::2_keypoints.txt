- Problem is important and authors correctly identify shortcomings in existing multivariate count models (with a noted caveat).  
- The paper has theoretical strengths: shows prior truncation approaches are invalid and proposes viable alternatives.  
- TPGM truncation is conceptually straightforward and well-justified by interpretation.  
- QPGM and SPGM are novel in explicitly specifying base measure and sufficient statistics to modify the Poisson within an exponential-family framework.  
- Much of the underlying theory builds on Yang et al.; several proposed models lack empirical validation of practical utility.  
- Experimental evaluation is underwhelming and biased: synthetic data are tailored to proposed models and real-data comparisons with copula methods are missing.  
- Results are qualitative/anecdotal; objective metrics (e.g., test log-probability) are necessary for joint-distribution assessment.  
- Evaluation of copula competitors is opaque and suboptimal (marginal choices questionable; Gaussian-kernel or other sensible baselines likely better; R=11 choice unexplored).  
- Paper is well-written and appears valid; include all proofs in the supplement.  
- Section 3.1 contains excessive detail (omit the bound); Section 3.2 is central and should be paced slower with the key figure and intuition in the main text.  
- Overall: conditional-exponential constructions are promising alternatives to Poisson graphical models, but limited/biased experiments leave practical merit unclear.
