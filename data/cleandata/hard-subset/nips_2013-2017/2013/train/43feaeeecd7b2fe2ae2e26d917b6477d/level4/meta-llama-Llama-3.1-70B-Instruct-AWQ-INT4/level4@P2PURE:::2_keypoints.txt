- Problem significance: Constructing multivariate distributions for count data is important and current approaches have notable limitations.  
- Theoretical contribution: Paper proves previously suggested truncation is invalid and proposes alternatives.  
- Proposed models: TPGM is justified by interpretation; QPGM and SPGM are novel by modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Novelty caveat: Underlying theory largely follows Yang et al., yielding multiple model options that require practical evaluation.  
- Experimental shortcomings: Evaluation is disappointing and synthetic experiments are tailored to favor the proposed models.  
- Missing baselines: Real-data experiments omit copula-based alternatives and rely on qualitative anecdotes rather than objective metrics.  
- Evaluation metric: Need objective evaluation using test-data log-probabilities since all methods build joint distributions.  
- Copula usage concerns: Copula competitors are used in a black-box way with questionable marginal choices (non-paranormal), undermining the need for the new model.  
- Baseline suggestion: Simpler marginals (e.g., Gaussian kernel density estimates) or tuning choices like R=11 for marginals should be tried as reasonable baselines.  
- Writing and presentation: Paper is generally well-written; include all proofs in supplement.  
- Section-level suggestions: Shorten overly detailed end of Section 3.1; expand Section 3.2 (core method) and move its explanatory figure into the main text.  
- Overall assessment: Methodologically appealing with some novelty, but limited and biased experiments leave the practical merit unclear.
