- Problem is important and authors correctly identify limitations of current multivariate count models (with a noted reservation).  
- The paper provides a proof that previous truncation approaches do not yield valid models.  
- Authors propose alternatives (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM); TPGM is interpretable, QPGM and SPGM are novel by modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- The theoretical development is well-motivated but largely follows Yang et al., so practical value of new models is uncertain.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing: synthetic data tailored to favor proposed models.  
- Real-data experiments omit crucial comparisons to copula-based methods.  
- Reported results are largely qualitative/anecdotal; need objective metrics like test log-probability.  
- Copula baselines are used in a black-box manner and employ inappropriate marginals (non-paranormal lacks a density); better marginal choices or simple KDEs should be tried.  
- The choice of R=11 in analyses could inform marginal selection; reasonable baseline exploration is warranted.  
- Paper is generally well written; include all proofs in supplementary material.  
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and the bound adds little; expand section 3.2 and move its figure into the main text with clearer intuition.  
- Overall: approach is appealing and somewhat novel, but limited and biased experiments leave the contribution unclear.
