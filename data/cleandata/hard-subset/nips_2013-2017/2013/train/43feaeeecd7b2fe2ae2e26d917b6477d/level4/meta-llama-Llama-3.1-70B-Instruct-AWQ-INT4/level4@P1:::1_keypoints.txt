- Paper proposes several new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.
- Argues Yang et al.'s "default" model is unsatisfactory because it can only model negative correlations if normalized.
- Proposes fixes: a truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary under fixed conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.
- Presents experiments on synthetic and real datasets demonstrating model performance.
- Paper is well-written, original, and yields significant results.
- Reviewer finds the drawbacks of the truncation and quadratic models not fully convincing.
- Truncation may be appropriate in many domains where counts are naturally bounded.
- Quadratic model’s thin Gaussian-like tails might be acceptable in some applications.
- Reviewer is unsure of the meaning of "sparsistent" and suggests clarifying its use and relevance.
- Minor typos: p.3 above 2.1.1 ("graphical mode distributions" → "model"); p.5 above 3.1 ("Note that the log..." → "Note that although the log...").
- Arguments for acceptance: originality, clear exposition of limitations of existing models, and convincing experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficiently convincing negatives for truncation/quadratic models and lack of clarity on "sparsistent."
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
