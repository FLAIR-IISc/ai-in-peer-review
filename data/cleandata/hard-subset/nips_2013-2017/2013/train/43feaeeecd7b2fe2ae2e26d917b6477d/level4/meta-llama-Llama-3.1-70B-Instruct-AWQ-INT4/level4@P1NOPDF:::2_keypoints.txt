- Problem importance: constructing multivariate distributions for count data is important and current approaches have limitations.  
- Theoretical contribution: authors prove previously suggested truncation is invalid and propose alternatives.  
- Model proposals: TPGM truncation is natural; QPGM and SPGM are novel by modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Motivation: changes to exponential-family representation are well-motivated and appealing.  
- Novelty caveat: much of the theory follows directly from Yang et al., making contributions incremental.  
- Experiments weak: experimental evaluation is disappointing given the paper's practical goals.  
- Synthetic data bias: synthetic experiments appear tailored to the proposed models.  
- Missing baselines: comparison to copula-based alternatives is glaringly absent.  
- Evaluation metric issue: results are qualitative/anecdotal; need objective evaluation using test log-probabilities.  
- Copula usage concern: copula competitors are used black-box; marginal choices (e.g., non-paranormal) are inappropriate (no density).  
- Suspicious results: near-identical performance to Glasso is questionable; simple alternatives (e.g., Gaussian KDE) might perform better.  
- Experimental choices: parameter choices like R=11 could inform sensible copula marginals and should be considered as baselines.  
- Presentation suggestions: include all proofs in the supplement; shorten overly detailed parts of §3.1.  
- Core exposition: §3.2 is central and should be presented more slowly with its explanatory figure in the main paper.  
- Overall verdict: approach is appealing with some theoretical novelty, but limited/biased experiments leave its practical merit unclear.
