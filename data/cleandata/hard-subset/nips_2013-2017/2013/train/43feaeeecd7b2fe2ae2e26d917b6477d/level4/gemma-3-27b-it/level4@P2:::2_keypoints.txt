- Addresses an important problem: constructing multivariate distributions for count data and identifying limitations of existing approaches.
- Provides a proof that a previously proposed truncation method is invalid, and proposes alternative truncation strategies.
- Introduces TPGM (intuitive truncation), QPGM, and SPGM, with QPGM/SPGM novel for modifying the base measure and sufficient statistics.
- Theoretical contributions are well-motivated but largely extend prior work by Yang et al., limiting novelty.
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and biased: synthetic data is tailored to favor the proposed models.
- Real-data analysis lacks comparison to relevant copula-based baselines and is presented mainly qualitatively.
- Needs objective evaluation metrics (e.g., test log-probability) rather than mainly qualitative results.
- Competitor modeling is overly “black-box”; choice of non-paranormal marginal for Gaussian copula is inappropriate and lacks a proper density.
- The similar performance to Glasso is suspicious; simpler methods (e.g., Gaussian KDE) might outperform the used baselines.
- The choice of R=11 for marginals could be reinterpreted as marginal selection in a copula framework and should be justified or compared.
- Presentation issues: move proofs to supplementary material; shorten overly detailed section 3.1.
- Suggest expanding section 3.2 (core method), include the figure in main text, and improve its intuition/explanation.
- Overall: approach is appealing with some theoretical novelty, but practical benefits over simpler alternatives remain unconvincing.
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, theoretical justification, clear motivation, promising synthetic results.
- Cons against acceptance: limited/biased experiments, missing strong baselines, theory largely builds on existing work, unclear practical advantages.
