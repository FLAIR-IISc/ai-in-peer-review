- Paper proposes a non-trivial method for multivariate count distributions that are Markov w.r.t. an undirected graph; theoretically interesting.

- Important prior work (Besag, Arnold et al. 1999, and the general theory of conditionally specified models) is highly relevant but not sufficiently engaged.

- Advantage: proposed construction permits marginal distributions over counts and yields relatively simple estimation algorithms.

- QPGM drawback: marginal tails are thinner than a standard Poisson.

- SPGM drawback: lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the point of a conditionally specified model and calling into question its label as “Poisson.”

- Broader concern: motivation for sticking closely to Poisson form is unclear given Poisson’s frequent poor fit to empirical count data.

- Empirical evidence missing: unclear if the breast cancer data are Poisson-like; authors should show marginal histograms.

- Request to visualize model: a plot of the SPGM probability mass function is needed because its formulation appears convoluted.

- Model assumptions unclear: paper seems to treat R and R0 as constants (needed for exponential-family status) but gives no guidance on how to choose them.

- Literature review is superficial and sometimes inaccurate, notably a misinterpretation of reference [8].

- Mischaracterization of [11]: that work focuses on parsimonious log-linear discrete models, which the paper does not properly acknowledge.

- Major omission: failure to engage with the extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data.

- Concession: many alternative approaches do not model MRF-style conditional independencies in observed counts, so the theoretical contribution retains validity.

- Practical impact weak: the paper does not make a compelling case for preferring this model over simpler alternatives like structured Gaussian random fields with Poisson measurement models.

- Minor request: replace or supplement reference [20] with a more recent citation to support the claim about sequencing reads replacing microarrays.
