- Proposes novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables, addressing Yang et al.'s negative-correlation normalization limitation.  
- Introduces three contributions: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proven necessary when others are fixed), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real-world datasets is thorough and supports the authors' claims.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and makes significant, original technical contributions with clear potential impact.  
- Limitation 1: Critique of truncation's "big negatives" is somewhat overstated; needs a nuanced discussion of when truncation is advantageous (domain examples or theoretical bounds).  
- Limitation 2: The quadratic model’s "Gaussian-esque thin tails" may be acceptable in some applications; authors should justify why thin tails are inherently problematic or explore relevant cases.  
- Terminology issue: "sparsistent" is used without definition; add a one-sentence clarification.  
- Minor editorial fixes: correct "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model distributions" and clarify the incomplete sentence "Note that the log..." above Section 3.1.  
- Overall: High-quality work with manageable issues; recommend acceptance after minor revisions.
