- Proposes three new multivariate count models: (i) an exponential-family-preserving truncation scheme, (ii) a quadratic base-measure variant allowing positive/negative dependencies, and (iii) sublinear sufficient statistics with Poisson base measure.  
- Shows learning via penalized neighborhood selection and evaluates on simulations plus a microRNA sequencing dataset.  
- Paper is clearly written, technically solid, and presents original contributions with potential impact.  
- Reviewer unconvinced by critiques of truncation: truncation can be effective when counts rarely exceed moderate values.  
- Reviewer unconvinced by critique of quadratic base measure: Gaussian-like thin tails may be appropriate in some applications.  
- Suggests defining the term “sparsistent” at first mention for clarity.  
- Minor typos to fix: “graphical mode distributions” → “graphical model distributions”; “Note that the log ...” → “Note that although the log ...”.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper on joint modeling of count variables.
