- Proposes a novel construction for multivariate unbounded-count distributions that respect an undirected graphical (MRF) structure.  
- Resulting models yield tractable marginal distributions and relatively straightforward estimation procedures.  
- The Quasi-Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) produces marginally thinner tails than a standard Poisson.  
- The Sparse Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) lacks closed-form conditional distributions, weakening its claim as a conditionally specified model.  
- Labeling SPGM as a Poisson variant is questionable—the connection to Poisson is unclear and a PMF visualization would help.  
- Unclear treatment of parameters R and R0: are they fixed, how chosen, and what practical guidance is provided?  
- Literature review is limited: copula discussion is superficial and understanding of reference [8] appears incomplete.  
- Overlooks substantial spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data; omission is significant.  
- Discussion of reference [11] is questionable given its focus on efficient discrete model parameterizations to avoid exponential complexity.  
- Theoretical contribution is interesting, but the practical complexity may not justify replacing simpler Gaussian latent-field + Poisson measurement approaches.  
- The claim that sequencing read counts are “replacing microarrays” needs a more recent citation.
