- Addresses an important problem (multivariate count distributions) and correctly identifies limitations in existing approaches.  
- Valuable proof showing invalidity of a previously proposed truncation method.  
- Introduces three alternative truncation schemes (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM), each with distinct strengths.  
- TPGM truncation is intuitively justified; QPGM and SPGM are novel by manipulating base measure and sufficient statistics in an exponential form.  
- Conceptual and theoretical contributions are appealing but largely build on Yang et al., leaving practical novelty uncertain.  
- Empirical validation is weak and disappointing relative to the paper’s practical aims.  
- Synthetic experiment appears tailored to favor the proposed models.  
- Real-data experiments omit comparisons to relevant copula-based alternatives.  
- Reported results are mostly qualitative and lack rigorous quantitative metrics (e.g., test log-probability).  
- Application of competing methods seems arbitrary—use of non-paranormal copula with poor marginal modeling is questionable.  
- A simple Gaussian marginal or Gaussian KDE might be a more sensible baseline; parity with Glasso is suspect.  
- Choice of R=11 in real-data analysis is unexplained and may affect marginal selection; baselines should be more reasonable.  
- Writing is generally good, but all proofs should be in the supplement given the paper’s motivation.  
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and its bound doesn't strengthen the Gaussian approximation; Section 3.2 (core method) needs more thorough exposition.  
- Move the illustrative figure from the supplement to the main paper and clarify the underlying intuition.  
- Overall: theoretically sound and conceptually appealing, but limited and potentially biased experiments leave the practical contribution unclear.
