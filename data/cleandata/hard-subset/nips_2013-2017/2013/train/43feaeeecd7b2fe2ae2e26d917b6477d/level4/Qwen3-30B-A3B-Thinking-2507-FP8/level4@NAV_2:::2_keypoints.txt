- Problem is important and authors correctly identify limitations of existing count-data multivariate models.  
- Theoretical contribution: proof that previous truncation methods are invalid.  
- TPGM truncation is conceptually straightforward and well-justified.  
- QPGM and SPGM are novel, technically simple modifications within the exponential-family framework.  
- Theoretical development heavily follows Yang et al., so practical value of multiple proposals requires empirical validation.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and not convincing relative to the paper’s practical aims.  
- Synthetic experiments are tailored to the proposed models, limiting their generality.  
- Real-data experiments omit comparisons to copula-based alternatives and report only qualitative results.  
- Evaluation should include objective metrics like test log-probability for joint distributions.  
- Copula competitors are used in a black-box way; using non-paranormal marginals (no density) is inappropriate.  
- Identical performance to Glasso is suspicious; simple Gaussian KDEs or better marginal choices (e.g., informed R=11) should be baselineed.  
- Paper is generally well-written and correct; all proofs should be included in the supplement.  
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed with an unhelpful bound; section 3.2 needs fuller treatment and its figure should be in the main text.  
- Overall: the conditional exponential construction is promising but empirical limitations and potential bias leave its practical merit unclear.
