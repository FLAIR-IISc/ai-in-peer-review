This paper introduces several novel models for representing joint distributions of integer-count variables. The authors identify a key limitation in the standard model from Yang et al., namely its inability to capture positive correlations while remaining a normalized distribution. To address this, the paper explores a sequence of remedies, including a new truncation method, the use of a quadratic base measure statistic (which is proven to be necessary while holding other components fixed), and finally, a sub-linear sufficient statistic. The proposed models are evaluated empirically on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

This is a well-written paper presenting elegant solutions for modeling count data. The work appears to be original and its results are significant.

However, I found the arguments for the "big negatives" of the truncation and quadratic models to be somewhat unpersuasive. Regarding the truncation model, it seems plausible that it would perform well in numerous domains where high counts are rare. Similarly, for the quadratic model, the criticism of "Gaussian-esque thin tails" may be overly strong, as such tail behavior could be appropriate for certain data distributions.
