- Proposes novel models for joint distributions of count variables, overcoming Yang et al.'s limitation to negative correlations.  
- Introduces modifications: a truncation approach, a quadratic base-measure statistic (argued necessary), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Well-structured, original work with significant results.  
- Criticism: objections to truncation and quadratic models are unconvincing—truncation may suit low-count domains and quadratic (Gaussian-like thin tails) may be appropriate in some contexts.  
- Notes unfamiliarity with the term "sparsistent," though it appears used previously in the field.  
- Minor suggested edits: replace "graphical mode distributions" with "model" (p.3 above §2.1.1); rephrase "Note that the log..." to "Note that although the log..." (p.5 above §3.1).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper effectively addressing joint count-variable modeling.
