- Introduces novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables and identifies Yang et al.'s normalization limitation requiring negative correlations.  
- Proposes solutions: a truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proven necessary under fixed conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic, with experiments on synthetic and real-world data.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and shows clear empirical advantages over existing approaches.  
- Major concern: Critique of truncation’s “big negatives” is weak—many real-world counts have natural upper bounds (e.g., word counts, event frequencies) where truncation is appropriate.  
- Major concern: Dismissal of the quadratic model’s “Gaussian-esque thin tails” is premature—Gaussian-like tails can be suitable for distributions with strong central tendencies and rapid decay.  
- Terminology issue: “sparsistent” is unexplained; add a brief clarification or citation for readers unfamiliar with the term.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “graphical mode distributions” → “graphical model” and “Note that the log...” → “Note that although the log...”.
