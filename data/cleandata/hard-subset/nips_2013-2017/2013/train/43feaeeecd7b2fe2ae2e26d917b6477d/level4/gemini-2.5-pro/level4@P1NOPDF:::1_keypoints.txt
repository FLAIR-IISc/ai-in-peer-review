- Proposes several new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.
- Argues Yang et al.’s default model is unsatisfactory because its normalization permits only negative correlations.
- Introduces three remedies: a truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary), and a sub-linear sufficient-statistic model.
- Validates the proposed approaches with experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Paper is well-written, with original, significant, and well-motivated contributions.
- Reviewer finds the criticisms of truncation and quadratic models unconvincing—truncation can work when counts are naturally bounded and quadratic’s Gaussian-like thin tails may be desirable in some domains.
- Minor suggestion: define or cite the term “sparsistent” for broader accessibility.
- Minor corrections: change “graphical mode distributions” to “graphical model distributions” and consider rephrasing to “Note that although the log…” for clarity.
