- Presents a method for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts respecting an undirected graphical model’s Markov structure.  
- Construction of such distributions is challenging and has extensive prior work (e.g., Besag; Arnold et al., 1999).  
- Contributions: enables marginal distributions over counts and provides relatively straightforward estimation procedures.  
- Limitation: QPGM yields thinner tails than expected for count data.  
- Limitation: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, a major drawback for conditionally-specified models.  
- Questionable: labeling SPGM as a Poisson distribution is problematic; QPGM’s base-measure modification is more defensible.  
- Suggestion: authors should visually compare empirical data (e.g., breast cancer example) to a Poisson and plot the SPGM PMF to illustrate fit and complexity.  
- Clarification needed on whether R and R0 are constants and practical guidance on how to choose them.  
- Commendation: tackling multivariate count MRFs is hard and the theoretical results are noteworthy.  
- Major criticism: literature review is incomplete and engagement with existing work is cursory.  
- Specific error: misunderstanding and misrepresentation of copula literature and reference [8], which addresses multivariate discrete/count constructions with established inference methods.  
- Omission: fails to cite substantial spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data and important discrete modeling approaches (e.g., log-linear scalable models related to [11]).  
- Practical relevance: unclear advantages over established approaches like structured Gaussian random fields with a Poisson measurement model.  
- Request: provide a more recent citation than [20] to support the claim about the shift from microarrays to sequencing read counts.
