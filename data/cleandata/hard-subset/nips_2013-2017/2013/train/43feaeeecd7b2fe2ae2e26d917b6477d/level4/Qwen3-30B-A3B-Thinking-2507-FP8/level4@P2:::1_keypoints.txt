- Addresses a major gap: existing Poisson graphical models only allow negative conditional dependencies.  
- Proposes three novel models: TPGM (domain truncated to {0,...,R}), QPGM (quadratic base measure), and SPGM (sub-linear sufficient statistics).  
- Collectively enable modeling of both positive and negative conditional dependencies for count data.  
- Theoretical rigor: proves necessary conditions for normalizability and validates constructions (Theorems 1–4, Theorem 3).  
- Compares trade-offs: TPGM has bounded domain, QPGM yields Gaussian-like thin tails, SPGM yields heavier tails.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic data shows clear advantages over existing methods.  
- Real-world application: breast cancer microRNA-sequencing analysis identifies biologically relevant hub miRNAs missed by Gaussian models (e.g., mir-375, mir-10b).  
- Well-written and well-organized with precise mathematical formulations and connections to prior work.  
- Thorough experimental comparisons with Poisson graphical model, Gaussian graphical models, and non-paranormal approaches.  
- Original contribution: significant departure from prior work enabling positive dependencies in count graphical models.  
- High practical significance for genomics, spatial statistics, and other domains with count data.  
- Strength: rigorous theory, three well-motivated approaches, comprehensive experiments, and clear discussion of trade-offs.  
- Limitation: practical guidance for choosing truncation parameter R in TPGM is insufficient.  
- Limitation: more specific guidance needed on when QPGM’s thin tails are desirable or problematic.  
- Limitation: connection between theoretical analysis and computational complexity/learning efficiency could be expanded.  
- Limitation: term "sparsistent" is used without definition and may confuse readers.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, significant, and recommended for acceptance.
