- Paper introduces several novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Authors critique Yang et al.'s "default" model, arguing it can only capture negative correlations to remain normalized.  
- Proposed improvements: a new truncation method to handle counts.  
- Proposed a quadratic base measure statistic and prove its necessity under fixed conditions.  
- Introduce a sub-linear sufficient statistic as a final model refinement.  
- Experimental results presented on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Reviewer finds the paper well-written, original, and its results meaningful and significant.  
- Reviewer is unconvinced by the arguments against the truncation method, noting many practical cases where counts stay small.  
- Reviewer questions dismissal of the quadratic model, suggesting Gaussian-like thin tails may be appropriate in some settings.  
- Reviewer is unfamiliar with the term "sparsistent" but notes prior literature uses it.  
- Minor editorial comments: replace "graphical mode distributions" with "model" (p.3) and clarify phrasing "Note that the log..." (p.5).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper on modeling joint count distributions.
