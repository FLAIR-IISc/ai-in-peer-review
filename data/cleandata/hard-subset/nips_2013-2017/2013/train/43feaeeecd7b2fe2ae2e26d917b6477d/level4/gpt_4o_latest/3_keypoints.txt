- Introduces a method to construct multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect the Markov structure of an undirected graph.  
- Developing such multivariate count models is hard and has a long literature (Besag, Arnold et al. 1999); the paper’s engagement with prior work is superficial.  
- Positive: the construction supports marginal distributions over counts and enables relatively straightforward estimation.  
- Limitation: QPGM exhibits marginally thinner tails than desirable.  
- Limitation: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, which undermines a conditionally-specified model and raises doubts about calling it “Poisson.”  
- Practical concern: Poisson may be a poor fit for empirical counts—authors should plot the breast cancer data against Poisson assumptions and show the SPGM PMF.  
- Model specification concern: R and R0 appear treated as constants (else not exponential family); authors should explain how these parameters are chosen and provide practitioner guidance.  
- Literature issues: apparent misinterpretation of copula literature and of reference [8], which already addresses multivariate discrete constructions and provides MCMC/approximate inference methods.  
- Omission: important spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data is largely ignored.  
- Practical relevance: although the theoretical contribution is valid, it’s unclear the model offers advantages over simpler structured Gaussian random field + Poisson observation models (e.g., sparse precision Gaussian copula).  
- Minor: update the gene expression citation [20] to a more recent reference supporting the claim about sequencing reads replacing microarrays.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting with strengths and weaknesses, but the literature review and practical motivation need significant improvement.
