- Addresses modeling multivariate count data and limitations of existing PGMs that only allow negative conditional dependencies.
- Proves Winsorized Poisson truncation cannot yield a consistent joint model.
- Proposes a truncated PGM (TPGM) that stays in the exponential family and removes sign constraints on edges.
- Introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) using a quadratic base measure to permit mixed-sign dependencies.
- Introduces Sublinear PGM (SPGM) modifying sufficient statistics for heavier tails and broader feasibility.
- Recommends neighborhood selection for structure learning and demonstrates methods in simulations and a miRNA sequencing case study.
- Builds on Besag and Yang et al.’s conditional-to-joint exponential-family construction.
- Main theoretical contributions are the Winsorization impossibility result and the base-measure/sufficient-statistic tradeoff requiring quadratic base measures for mixed signs.
- Clear motivation: enabling both positive and negative conditional dependencies among counts is practically important.
- The Winsorization impossibility proof clarifies a misinterpretation in prior work.
- TPGM is a natural, tractable truncation that preserves exponential-family form and normalizability.
- QPGM and SPGM are simple, well-motivated extensions with transparent normalizability conditions and reasonable variational arguments for nodewise learning.
- Framework is compatible with standard neighborhood selection, implying potential scalability.
- Theoretical novelty is incremental since much follows from Yang et al.; empirical validation therefore is crucial.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and at times biased toward the proposed models; synthetic data align with TPGM/SPGM assumptions and real-data results are mainly qualitative.
- Missing comparisons to copula-based methods; recommend including Gaussian copula with sensible marginals instead of nonparanormal transforms.
- Evaluation should include held-out log-likelihood or pseudo-likelihood, not just ROC curves.
- Choices like R (and R0 for SPGM) in the miRNA study need justification via sensitivity analysis or data-driven selection.
- Provide computational details: timing, convergence behavior of proximal-gradient implementations, and sample-size requirements for TPGM/QPGM/SPGM.
- Improve presentation by including all proofs in the supplement, condensing Section 3.1, and expanding Section 3.2 with the sublinear statistic figure and clearer intuition.
- Pros for acceptance: fills a real gap, offers simple constructions with sound normalizability, and corrects an error in the literature.
- Cons for acceptance: empirical evidence is not compelling and novelty over prior conditional-exponential constructions is modest.
- Overall: conceptually clean and potentially useful models, but acceptance hinges on stronger empirical comparisons (copula baselines, likelihood evaluation, sensitivity analyses, and computational benchmarks).
