- Introduces novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Argues Yang et al.'s "default" model is limited to modeling negative correlations for normalization.  
- Proposes modifications: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary given other fixed components), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Provides experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and presents significant results.  
- Reviewer unconvinced by the paper's emphasis on the "big negatives" of truncation and quadratic models (truncation may suit domains with natural count thresholds; quadratic thin tails may be appropriate in some cases).  
- Reviewer unfamiliar with the term "sparsistent" despite its prior literature usage.  
- Notes minor errors: "graphical mode distributions" should be "graphical model distributions" (p.3); rephrase "Note that the log..." to "Note that although the log..." (p.5).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper on modeling joint distributions of count variables.
