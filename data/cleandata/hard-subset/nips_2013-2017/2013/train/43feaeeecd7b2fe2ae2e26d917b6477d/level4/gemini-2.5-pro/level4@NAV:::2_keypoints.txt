- Paper addresses multivariate count distributions and clearly identifies shortcomings in existing approaches (with one caveat noted).

- Proves a previously proposed truncation method yields an invalid model and proposes several alternatives.

- Includes TPGM for interpretability and introduces QPGM and SPGM, which modify base measure and sufficient statistics in a conceptually sound way.

- The theoretical development is largely a direct extension of Yang et al., so the contribution is mainly plausible model proposals rather than radically new theory.

- Experimental evaluation is underwhelming and appears tailored to favor the proposed models.

- Synthetic experiments seem biased by the data generation process.

- Real-data experiments omit comparisons to copula-based alternatives.

- Reported results are mostly qualitative/anecdotal; objective metrics like test log-probability are needed since joint densities are constructed.

- Copula baselines are implemented superficially (poor marginal choices like non-paranormal); better marginal estimators (e.g., Gaussian KDE or R=11) could substantially improve baselines.

- Similar performance to Glasso is suspicious, indicating a need for more careful baseline implementation.

- Paper is well-written; recommend including all proofs in the supplement given the correction of prior work.

- Section 3.1 contains an unnecessary detailed bound; Section 3.2 should be expanded and the key explanatory figure moved to the main paper.

- Overall: approach is theoretically appealing and novel, but limited and potentially biased empirical validation leaves practical value uncertain.
