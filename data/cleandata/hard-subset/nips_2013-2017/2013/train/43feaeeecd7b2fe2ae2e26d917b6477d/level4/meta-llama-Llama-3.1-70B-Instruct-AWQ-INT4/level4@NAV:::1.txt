This paper presents novel models for representing joint distributions over integer-count variables, addressing the limitations of the default model proposed by Yang et al. The authors contend that this model is inadequate due to its inability to capture positive correlations while maintaining normalization. To rectify this, they explore several alternatives, including a new truncation approach, the incorporation of a quadratic base measure statistic, and the utilization of a sub-linear sufficient statistic. The authors provide experimental results on both synthetic and real-world data.

Overall, this is a well-crafted paper that offers innovative solutions for modeling count data, demonstrating originality and significant results. However, I found the criticisms of the truncation and quadratic models to be somewhat unconvincing. The truncation method may be effective in domains where counts are typically low, while the quadratic model's "Gaussian-esque thin tails" could be suitable in certain contexts.

Additionally, I am unfamiliar with the term "sparsistent," although it appears to have been used previously in the literature.

Minor corrections are needed: on page 3, "graphical mode distributions" should be replaced with "model," and on page 5, "Note that the log" could be rephrased as "Note that although the log" for clarity.
