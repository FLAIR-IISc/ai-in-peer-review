- Introduces novel models for representing joint distributions over integer-count variables.
- Argues the standard Yang et al. model is limited because it can only model negative correlations to ensure normalization.
- Proposes modifications: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary in their framework), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.
- Provides experimental results on synthetic and real data demonstrating the proposed models’ effectiveness.
- Paper is well-written, original, and makes significant, substantial contributions to modeling count data.
- Reviewer finds some criticisms of the truncation and quadratic models unconvincing: truncation can be appropriate when counts have natural bounds, and quadratic (Gaussian-like thin) tails may be acceptable in some settings.
- Notes a minor typo: "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model distributions" (page 3, above Sec. 2.1.1).
- Suggests rephrasing "Note that the log..." to "Note that although the log..." (page 5, above Sec. 3.1).
- Mentions the term "sparsistent" was unclear but acknowledges it is established in the literature.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper with minor revisions needed; likely to have substantial impact.
