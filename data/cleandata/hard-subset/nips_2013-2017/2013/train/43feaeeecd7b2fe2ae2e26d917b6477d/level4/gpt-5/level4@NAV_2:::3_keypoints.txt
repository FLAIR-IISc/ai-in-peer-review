- Constructs multivariate count distributions that respect undirected-graph MRF conditional independences — a notable theoretical contribution.  
- Proposed models yield joint distributions with count marginals and enable relatively straightforward estimation.  
- QPGM induces noticeably lighter tails, which may limit fit to heavy-tailed count data.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form node-conditionals, weakening the rationale for a conditionally specified construction.  
- It is unclear in what sense SPGM remains “Poisson,” unlike QPGM where the sufficient statistic/base measure story is clearer.  
- Broader question: forcing Poisson-like forms for observed counts is questionable since Poisson often misfits empirical count data.  
- Empirical diagnostics needed: plot breast-cancer counts vs a Poisson fit and show the SPGM probability mass function.  
- Practical detail missing: R and R0 appear treated as fixed—need guidance on how these are set in practice.  
- Literature coverage is incomplete, omitting relevant copula-based multivariate discrete methods and extensive spatial statistics work.  
- Specific concerns about mischaracterizing prior work: misunderstanding of copula methods ([8]) and an incomplete treatment of discrete graphical-model literature ([11]).  
- From a practitioner’s standpoint, unclear why accept MRF-consistent count-model constraints versus using Gaussian random fields with Poisson observations.  
- Minor: update citation for gene-expression claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays.  
- Overall: the problem is hard and the paper has theoretical interest, but practical motivation and literature/contextual treatment need strengthening.
