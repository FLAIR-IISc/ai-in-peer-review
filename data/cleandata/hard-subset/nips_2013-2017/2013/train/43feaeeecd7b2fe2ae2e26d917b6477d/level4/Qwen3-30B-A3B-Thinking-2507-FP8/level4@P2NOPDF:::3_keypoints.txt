- Proposes two models (QPGM and SPGM) for multivariate count distributions respecting undirected graph Markov structure.  
- QPGM: yields tractable marginals and efficient estimation but has marginal tails thinner than Poisson.  
- SPGM: lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining conditional-specification premise and is not reasonably called “Poisson.”  
- Authors should provide an SPGM probability mass function plot and clarify whether parameters (R, R0) are constants and how to choose them.  
- The theoretical construction is of interest but the literature review is notably incomplete.  
- Copula-based methods (e.g., sparse precision Gaussian copula) are treated superficially and misrepresented relative to existing discrete/count work.  
- The paper fails to engage relevant log-linear parameterization work (e.g., [11]) and its implications for Poisson-like constructions.  
- Critically omits spatial statistics literature, which has long-studied high-dimensional count models.  
- The paper does not demonstrate clear advantages over the standard structured Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement approach in flexibility, accuracy, or scalability.  
- Lacks empirical validation of model fit and practical motivation, given Poisson often poorly fits real count data.  
- Minor issue: citation [20] is outdated for sequencing vs microarray claims; a more recent reference is needed.  
- Overall: plausible theoretical contribution but weak contextualization, practical justification, and engagement with key prior work; major drawbacks include SPGM’s lack of closed-form conditionals and superficial treatment of copulas and spatial methods.
