- Paper constructs multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect an undirected Markov network structure.
- Construction allows marginal distributions over counts and yields relatively simple estimation algorithms.
- QPGM has marginally thinner tails, which may limit modeling of heavy-tailed count data.
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the purpose of a conditionally-specified model.
- It is unclear whether SPGM can legitimately be called a Poisson distribution; only the base measure of QPGM is clearly modified.
- The motivation for modeling counts using Poisson-based constructions is questioned because Poisson may poorly fit empirical data.
- A plot of the SPGM probability mass function is needed because the formulation appears convoluted.
- The authors assume R and R0 are constants (else not exponential families), but do not explain how these parameters should be chosen in practice.
- Literature review is incomplete—discussion of copulas and their application to discrete/count data is lacking and appears to misunderstand reference [8].
- Discussion of reference [11] is unclear; the reviewer notes [11] focuses on discrete log-linear models without exponential variable growth.
- The manuscript neglects extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data.
- Practical benefits of the proposed models are unclear compared to standard approaches like structured Gaussian random fields with Poisson measurements.
- A more recent reference than [20] is requested, and the claim about sequencing reads replacing microarrays may be outdated.
