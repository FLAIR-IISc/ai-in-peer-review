- Proposes a novel graphical-model approach for multivariate count data with clear motivation.  
- Proves a previously suggested truncation approach is invalid.  
- Introduces TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM, with QPGM and SPGM modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- The proposed modifications are well-motivated and conceptually appealing.  
- Much of the theory follows directly from Yang et al., limiting theoretical novelty.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and biased toward the proposed models.  
- Synthetic experiments are tailored to the models and real-data comparisons lack rigor.  
- Missing comparisons to copula-based alternatives and no objective log-probability test evaluation.  
- Copula competitors are used as black-boxes and marginals are not chosen sensibly.  
- Nearly identical performance to Glasso is suspicious; simple baselines like Gaussian KDE might outperform.  
- Paper is generally well-written and results likely true, but many proofs should be moved to supplementary material.  
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and includes a non-contributory bound; Section 3.2 needs clearer exposition and its figure in the main text.  
- Overall appealing theoretical ideas but practical merit remains unclear due to limited experiments.
