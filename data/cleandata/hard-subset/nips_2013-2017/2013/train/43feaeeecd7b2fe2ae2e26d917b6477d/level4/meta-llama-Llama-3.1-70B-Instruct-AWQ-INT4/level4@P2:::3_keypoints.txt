- Proposes constructions for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect the Markov structure of an undirected network.  
- Constructions permit specified marginal count distributions and yield relatively simple estimation algorithms.  
- Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) produces marginally thinner tails than desired.  
- Sub-Linear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) does not admit closed-form conditional distributions.  
- Main motivation for modeling counts is unclear, since Poisson often poorly fits empirical data.  
- SPGM presentation feels convoluted; a probability mass function (PMF) plot would clarify its behavior.  
- Choice of constants R and R0 is unexplained; authors should give guidance for practitioner selection.  
- Literature review is incomplete and contains misinterpretations (e.g., copulas) and missing or unclearly presented references.  
- Paper does not justify why to prefer this model class over simpler alternatives (e.g., structured Gaussian RF + Poisson observations).  
- Request for a more recent reference supporting the claim that sequencing reads replace microarrays.  
- Contribution is theoretically interesting and valid for NIPS, but practical implications and applicability remain unclear.  
- Recommended fixes: complete and correct the literature review, clarify references, add SPGM PMF plot, explain R/R0 choice, and discuss practical advantages.
