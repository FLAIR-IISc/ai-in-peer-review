- Paper proposes multivariate count distributions that respect Markov structure on undirected networks, a challenging problem with notable progress.  
- Strength: yields valid marginal count distributions.  
- Strength: provides computationally efficient estimation algorithms.  
- Limitation: QPGM produces marginally thinner tails than desirable.  
- Limitation: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining its conditional-specification claim.  
- Claim that SPGM is a “Poisson distribution” is questionable; QPGM only modifies the base measure.  
- Practical motivation weak: Poisson models often fail for real count data (e.g., breast cancer read counts).  
- Empirical behavior unclear — a plot of the SPGM probability mass function is needed.  
- Practitioner guidance missing for selecting parameters R and R0 (assumed constant for exponential-family validity).  
- Theoretical contribution is valid for constructing count distributions under MRF independence constraints.  
- Literature review is deficient: misrepresents copula-based approaches that handle arbitrary discrete data with existing inference methods.  
- Literature review overlooks spatial statistics and established high-dimensional count analysis.  
- Practical justification is weak compared to simpler, established alternatives (e.g., Gaussian random field with Poisson observations).  
- Reference [20] is outdated and should be replaced with a more recent source.
