- Proposes three novel Poisson graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) for count data.
- Motivates need: Gaussian and Ising models are ill-suited for increasingly common count data.
- TPGM: truncated Poisson extension that can model positive and negative dependencies but requires an a priori bounded domain.
- QPGM: introduces a quadratic base measure; proven necessary for permitting both positive/negative conditional dependencies and ensuring normalizability.
- QPGM drawback: Gaussian-like thin tail that may be undesirable in some applications.
- SPGM: uses a sub-linear sufficient statistic, yields heavier tails than QPGM and a larger feasible set of pairwise parameters.
- Theoretical contributions include proofs about normalizability and necessity of the quadratic modification.
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data demonstrate effectiveness of the proposed models.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated models with solid theoretical grounding.
- Strength: clear exposition of limitations of existing models and advantages of the new models.
- Weakness: major limitations (bounded domain for TPGM, thin tails for QPGM) may be insufficiently mitigated.
- Weakness: paper assumes background in graphical models and Poisson distributions, reducing accessibility.
- Writing and organization: well-written, clearly organized, with proper citations and formatting.
- Acceptance arguments: for — originality, theoretical soundness, convincing experiments; against — unresolved practical drawbacks of some models.
