- Proposes multivariate count distributions that are Markov w.r.t. an undirected graph, addressing a hard problem with theoretical interest.  
- Construction yields proper marginal count distributions and relatively simple estimation algorithms.  
- QPGM has marginally thinner tails, which may be a practical downside.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification rationale and casting doubt on calling it a Poisson model.  
- Raises question of motivation: Poisson often fits empirical count data poorly, so why explicitly model counts here?  
- Requests empirical checks: plot breast cancer data against a Poisson and include a plot of the SPGM probability mass function.  
- Asks how constants R and R0 are chosen in practice and requests practitioner guidance on their selection.  
- Literature review is overly light and sometimes misleading, notably under-engaging with copula methods and spatial statistics.  
- Claims reference [8] is misunderstood/misrepresented; [8] already constructs multivariate discrete models and offers MCMC/approximate inference options.  
- Questions adequacy of discussion around reference [11], which addresses discrete log-linear models and avoids exponential growth in variables.  
- Acknowledges the paper’s theoretical contribution (enforcing MRF structure for counts) but doubts practical benefit relative to Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement models.  
- Requests a more recent reference than [20] to support the claim that sequencing read counts are replacing microarrays.  
- Overall: interesting and nontrivial contribution with clear advantages and shortcomings, but literature engagement is incomplete and at times misleading.
