The paper introduces several new models for representing joint distributions over integer-count variables. The authors motivate their work by arguing that the default model proposed by Yang et al. is limited, as it can only represent negative correlations while maintaining a normalized distribution. They then explore a series of improvements, including a novel truncation method, the use of a quadratic base measure statistic (proven necessary under certain conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic. The authors support their contributions with experimental results on both synthetic and real datasets.

This is a well-written paper that describes elegant solutions for modeling count data. I believe the work is original and the results are significant. 

I found the discussion of limitations for the truncation and quadratic models somewhat unconvincing. Regarding the truncation method, I can easily envision domains where counts are naturally bounded, and this model would perform well. For the quadratic model, couldn’t “Gaussian-esque thin tails” be appropriate in some situations? 

I am unfamiliar with the term "sparsistent," but I note that it appears to be established in the literature.

On page 3, above section 2.1.1, “graphical mode distributions” should be changed to “model.” 

On page 5, above section 3.1, the phrase “Note that the log…” is unclear. Do the authors mean “Note that although the log…”? Overall, this is a high-quality paper on modeling joint distributions over count variables.
