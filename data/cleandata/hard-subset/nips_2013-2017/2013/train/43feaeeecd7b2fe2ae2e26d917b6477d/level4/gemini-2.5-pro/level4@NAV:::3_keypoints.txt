- Presents a novel construction for multivariate unbounded-count distributions that respect Markov independence, addressing a historically challenging problem.  
- Proposed method yields valid marginal distributions and relatively straightforward estimation algorithms.  
- QPGM exhibits marginally thinner tails than typically desirable for count data.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the motivation for a conditionally-specified model.  
- The justification for calling SPGM “Poisson” is unclear; QPGM’s changes may be confined to the base measure.  
- Broader motivation is unclear because the standard Poisson is often a poor direct fit; need demonstration of real-world fit beyond Poisson-like assumptions.  
- Recommend plotting the SPGM probability mass function because its formulation is not immediately intuitive.  
- Clarify how R and R0 are chosen; if treated as constants for exponential-family structure, give practitioner guidance.  
- The theoretical effort to construct MRF-respecting multivariate count distributions is commendable and distinct.  
- Major weakness: limited engagement with existing literature, especially underdeveloped discussion of copulas.  
- Misrepresents reference [8], which actually builds multivariate distributions for arbitrary discrete data with MCMC/approximate inference methods.  
- Discussion of [11] appears misguided; that work focused on discrete log-linear models that avoid exponential parameter growth.  
- Striking omission of the extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data.  
- Practically, the paper does not yet justify adopting these models over established alternatives like structured Gaussian random fields with Poisson observation models.  
- Minor: the claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays should cite a more recent reference than [20].
