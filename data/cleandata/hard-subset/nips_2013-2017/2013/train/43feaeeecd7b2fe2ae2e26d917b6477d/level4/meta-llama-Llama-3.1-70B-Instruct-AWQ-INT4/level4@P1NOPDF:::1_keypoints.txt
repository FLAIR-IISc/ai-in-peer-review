- Proposes several new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Argues the default model (Yang et al.) is unsatisfactory because it can only capture negative correlations to ensure normalization.  
- Introduces fixes including a novel truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic, and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Proves the quadratic base-measure statistic is necessary (assuming other components are fixed).  
- Presents experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Reviewer finds the paper well-written, original, and the results significant.  
- Reviewer is unconvinced by the criticisms of the truncation and quadratic models—truncation may be fine when counts aren’t large and Gaussian-like tails can be appropriate.
