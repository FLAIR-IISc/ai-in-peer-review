- Problem importance: constructing multivariate distributions for count data is important and authors identify limitations of current approaches.  
- Theoretical contribution: authors prove previous truncation approach is invalid and propose alternatives.  
- Model proposals: TPGM is natural and interpretable; QPGM and SPGM are novel for explicitly modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Novelty caveat: underlying theory largely follows Yang et al., so practical value of the suggested models remains to be shown.  
- Experimental weakness: synthetic experiments are biased toward the proposed models.  
- Missing baselines: real-data experiments omit comparisons to copula-based alternatives.  
- Evaluation metric: reported results are anecdotal; need objective log-probability evaluation on test data.  
- Copula critique: copula competitors are used black-box; non-paranormal marginals lack densities and are a poor practical choice.  
- Alternative marginals: simple Gaussian kernel density estimates or marginals informed by R=11 could provide stronger baselines.  
- Presentation suggestion: include all proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Sectioning critique: section 3.1 is overly detailed with an unhelpful bound.  
- Core method exposition: section 3.2 needs clearer, more deliberate presentation and the key figure should be in the main text.  
- Writing quality and verdict: paper is generally well written and theoretically appealing, but limited and biased experiments leave overall merit unclear.
