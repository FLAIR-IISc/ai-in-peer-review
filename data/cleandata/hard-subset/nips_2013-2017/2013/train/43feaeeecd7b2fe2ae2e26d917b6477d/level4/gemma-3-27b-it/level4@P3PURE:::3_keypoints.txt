- Paper proposes constructions for multivariate count distributions that respect an undirected graph’s Markov structure.  
- Method succeeds in allowing marginal count distributions and yields relatively straightforward estimation algorithms.  
- QPGM exhibits marginally thinner tails than desired, potentially mismatching real count data.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the advantage of a conditionally-specified model.  
- Doubt whether SPGM should be legitimately called a Poisson distribution; QPGM may only alter the base measure.  
- Raises broader question of motivation since the Poisson is often a poor empirical fit for counts.  
- Suggestion to plot breast cancer data (and SPGM PMF) to assess fit and clarify the model’s behavior.  
- Assumes R and R0 are treated as constants; reviewer asks how these parameters are chosen and requests practical guidance.  
- Literature review is superficial and misses important prior work (copula literature and [8] in particular).  
- Authors appear to misrepresent the contribution of work on copulas and multivariate discrete constructions.  
- Neglects extensive spatial statistics literature that addresses high-dimensional count data.  
- Notes that some existing approaches (e.g., sparse precision Gaussian copula) do not impose MRF-style independence in the observed space.  
- Theoretical contribution is valid and of interest, but practical advantages over simpler Gaussian random field + Poisson observation models are unconvincing.  
- Claim that sequencing reads are “replacing microarrays” should cite a more recent reference.
