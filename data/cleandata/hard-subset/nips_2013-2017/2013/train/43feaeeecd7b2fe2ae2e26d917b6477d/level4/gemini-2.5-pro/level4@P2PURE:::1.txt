This paper presents several new models for representing joint distributions over integer-count variables. The authors motivate their work by highlighting a key limitation of the model from Yang et al., which can only represent negative correlations while ensuring a normalizable distribution. To address this, they propose a series of solutions: a novel truncation method, the use of a quadratic base measure statistic (which they prove is a necessary modification under certain conditions), and finally a sub-linear sufficient statistic. The proposed methods are evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

This is a well-written paper presenting original and significant contributions to the problem of modeling count data.

My main reservations concern the arguments presented against the truncation and quadratic models. The stated limitations felt somewhat unconvincing. For instance, the truncation method seems well-suited for many interesting domains where counts are naturally bounded or do not typically reach high values. Similarly, the "Gaussian-esque thin tails" produced by the quadratic model might be an appropriate and desirable property for certain types of data, rather than a universal drawback.
