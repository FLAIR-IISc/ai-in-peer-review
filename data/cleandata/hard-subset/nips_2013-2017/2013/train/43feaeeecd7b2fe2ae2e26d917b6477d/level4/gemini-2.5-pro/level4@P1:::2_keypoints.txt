- Addresses constructing flexible undirected graphical models for multivariate count data to capture both positive and negative dependencies.
- Correctly identifies limitation of standard Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) to negative conditional dependencies.
- Proves that a previously proposed "Winsorized" Poisson truncation method does not produce a valid joint distribution.
- Proposes three new models: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).
- TPGM uses a valid truncation scheme but is sensitive to the chosen truncation level.
- QPGM and SPGM modify the base measure and sufficient statistics to ensure normalizability and richer dependency modeling.
- Novel and principled theoretical approach building on exponential family representation and Yang et al.'s framework.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear motivations for each model.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient to convincingly show practical advantages over alternatives.
- Missing comparisons to modern copula-based baselines and stronger copula implementations with better marginals.
- Real-data evaluation is anecdotal; lacks quantitative metrics like held-out test log-likelihoods for objective comparison.
- Synthetic experiments are biased toward models related to the proposed methods, limiting assessment of robustness.
- The theoretical contribution is somewhat incremental since joint construction follows Yang et al.; main novelty is new model classes whose utility remains to be proven.
- Suggestions: expand experiments (include Gaussian copula baseline, use truncation-informed marginals, report test-set log-likelihoods).
- Suggestions: improve presentation by trimming Section 3.1, expanding Section 3.2, and moving SPGM figure to main text for intuition.
- Suggestions: include full proofs for all theorems and propositions in the supplementary material for rigor and reproducibility.
