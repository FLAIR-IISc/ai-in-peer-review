- Addresses modeling multivariate count data and overcomes Poisson graphical models’ inability to represent positive correlations.  
- Proposes three approaches: a refined truncation method (TPGM), a Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and a Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM).  
- Proves a theoretical necessity of a quadratic base measure for modeling positive correlations and provides rigorous analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation on simulations and real microRNA sequencing data shows SPGM (and others) outperform Gaussian and original Poisson graphical models.  
- Paper is well-written, builds clearly on prior work (Besag, Yang et al.), and motivates each modification effectively.  
- Comprehensive exploration of multiple solutions with thoughtful trade-off analysis rather than a single fix.  
- Acknowledges limitations of QPGM (thin tails) and TPGM, but these limitations need more detailed elaboration.  
- Recommend more in-depth analysis of TPGM performance across different truncation levels R.  
- The term “sparsistent” is used without definition and should be defined or cited.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “graphical mode distributions” → “graphical model” (p.3) and clarify “Note that the log...” (p.5).  
- Suggest expanding practical guidance for choosing between TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM based on data characteristics.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; originality, significance, technical soundness, and clarity justify acceptance.
