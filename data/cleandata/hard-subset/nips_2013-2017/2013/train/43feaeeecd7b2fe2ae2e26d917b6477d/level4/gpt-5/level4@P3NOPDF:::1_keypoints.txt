- Summary: Proposes models for joint distributions over count variables, arguing the default model is limited to negative correlations and exploring truncation, a quadratic base measure (with a necessity proof), and sub-linear sufficient statistics; evaluated on synthetic and real data.

- Strength: Clear motivation identifying a concrete limitation of an established model.

- Strength: Methodological contributions include a necessity result for quadratic base measures and use of sub-linear sufficient statistics to expand feasible correlations.

- Strength: Empirical evaluation shows the proposed models can work well in practice.

- Strength: Paper is generally well written and easy to follow.

- Concern (truncation): Critique of truncation is not fully convincing; recommend quantifying truncation error/bounds, sensitivity analyses across thresholds and tail heaviness, and discussing computational trade-offs.

- Concern (quadratic base measure): Claim of undesirably thin tails is context-dependent; ask for empirical tail-fit comparisons, clarification of what is held fixed in the necessity result, intuition for why quadratic terms are necessary, and discussion of alternatives that retain heavier tails.

- Concern (correlation structure): Request an analytic example or proposition showing when positive correlations are permitted under sub-linear statistics and empirical diagnostics comparing learned vs. true pairwise correlations on synthetic positive-dependence data.

- Concern (relation to alternatives): Ask for clearer comparison to other multivariate count models (Poisson log-normal, negative binomial variants, copula-based, COM-Poisson) with predictive or correlation-recovery baselines.

- Concern (scalability/computation): Request details on computational complexity, behavior in high dimensions (time/memory, convergence), and effects of any approximations (e.g., pseudo-likelihood) on estimation bias/variance.

- Concern (theory clarity): Suggest adding an intuitive explanation of the necessity proof mechanism and stating whether the result is tight or has corner cases.

- Minor comments: Define “sparsistent” at first use, fix typos (“graphical model distributions”, “Note that although the log...”), and give guidance on choosing between quadratic vs. sub-linear approaches in practice.

- Overall assessment: Well written and original work with meaningful theoretical and practical contributions; recommend acceptance contingent on clarifying necessity scope, expanding comparisons to alternative models, and adding empirical analyses on truncation sensitivity, achievable correlations, and tail fit.
