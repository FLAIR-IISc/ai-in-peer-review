- Problem is important and authors correctly identify limitations of current multivariate count models.
- Theoretical contributions include correcting prior truncation error and proposing alternatives (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM).
- TPGM truncation is natural; QPGM and SPGM are novel by modifying base measure and sufficient statistics in exponential family form.
- Much of the underlying theory closely follows Yang et al., so novelty is limited to model suggestions whose practical value is untested.
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing: synthetic data tailored to proposed models, real-data experiments lack rigor.
- Key baseline comparisons are missing — notably copula-based methods are not included.
- Evaluation is qualitative and anecdotal; objective assessment via test-set log-probabilities is essential but absent.
- Paper fails to justify the need for a new model, treating copula competitors as black boxes rather than constructing reasonable marginal baselines.
- The choice of marginals used for copula baselines is questionable; simple alternatives (e.g., Gaussian KDE) could perform much better.
- Specific real-data choices (e.g., R=11) could translate to copula marginals; reasonable baseline exploration is warranted.
- Writing is generally good and results appear correct; recommend including all proofs in the supplement.
- Section 3.1 contains an unnecessary detailed bound; Section 3.2 (core method) needs clearer presentation and its figure should be in the main text.
- Overall, the conditional exponential construction is theoretically appealing but experiments are limited and biased, leaving practical merit unclear.
