- Problem motivation: constructing multivariate count distributions is important and authors correctly identify limitations of current approaches.
- Theoretical contribution: authors prove previous truncation is invalid and propose alternatives.
- TPGM is justified by natural interpretation; QPGM and SPGM are novel by modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.
- Much of the theoretical development follows Yang et al., leaving multiple model proposals that require empirical validation.
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing: synthetic data is biased toward proposed models and real-data experiments omit copula-based comparisons.
- Reported results are anecdotal; need objective evaluation (e.g., test-set log-probabilities) since all methods define joint distributions.
- Copula baselines are used simplistically: Gaussian copula marginals (non-paranormal) lack densities and are a poor practical choice.
- Identical performance to Glasso is suspicious; simple alternatives (e.g., Gaussian KDE marginals) would likely be stronger baselines.
- Choice of R=11 for marginals can be translated into copula marginal choices; a reasonable baseline exploration is warranted.
- Paper is generally well-written; include all proofs in the supplement for completeness.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and the bound is unnecessary for the Gaussian-like argument.
- Section 3.2 is the methodological core and should be presented more slowly with its figure and clearer intuition in the main text.
- Overall: appealing theoretical ideas and some novelty, but limited and biased experiments leave the practical merit unclear.
