- Introduces several new models for representing joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Motivates work by arguing Yang et al.’s default model is limited (only represents negative correlations while normalized).  
- Proposes improvements: a novel truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proven necessary under certain conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Supports contributions with experiments on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and presents elegant solutions for modeling count data.  
- Work appears original and results significant.  
- Reviewer finds the discussion of limitations for the truncation and quadratic models somewhat unconvincing.  
- Truncation method could perform well in domains where counts are naturally bounded.  
- Quadratic model’s “Gaussian-esque thin tails” might be appropriate in some applications.  
- Reviewer unfamiliar with the term “sparsistent” but notes it appears established in the literature.  
- Minor editorial issue: change “graphical mode distributions” to “model” (p.3).  
- Clarification requested on p.5 above Sec. 3.1: “Note that the log…” likely should read “Note that although the log…”.  
- Overall assessment: a high-quality paper on modeling joint distributions over count variables.
