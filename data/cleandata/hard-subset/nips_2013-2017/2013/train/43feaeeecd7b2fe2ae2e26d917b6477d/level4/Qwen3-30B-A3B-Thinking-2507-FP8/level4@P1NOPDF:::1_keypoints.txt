- Introduces novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Identifies a limitation in Yang et al.’s default model that forces negative correlations for normalization.  
- Proposes improvements: a new truncation method, a quadratic base measure statistic (proved necessary), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets support the proposed approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, original, and makes significant contributions to modeling count data.  
- Main concern: the critique of the truncation method’s “big negatives” is unconvincing—truncation may be fine when counts rarely exceed moderate values.  
- Main concern: the quadratic model’s Gaussian-like tail behavior might be appropriate in some contexts, questioning its general suitability.  
- The term “sparsistent” appears without definition and should be clarified for broader accessibility.  
- Minor typo: “graphical mode distributions” should be “graphical model distributions” (page 3, above 2.1.1).  
- Minor wording: “Note that the log...” likely intended as “Note that the log...” without “although” (page 5, above 3.1).  
- Overall assessment: well-executed paper with practical improvements and thorough experimental validation; concerns are minor relative to strengths.
