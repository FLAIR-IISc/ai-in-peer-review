

This paper introduces novel models for representing joint distributions over integer-count variables. The authors identify a limitation in the default model from Yang et al., which requires negative correlations for normalization. They propose several improvements: a new truncation method, a quadratic base measure statistic (which they prove necessary under their constraints), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic. Experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets support their approach.

The paper is well-written, presents a technically sound solution, and appears original. The contributions are significant for modeling count data.

While the paper makes strong contributions, I have two main concerns. First, the criticism of the truncation method's "big negatives" is somewhat unconvincing. I can envision domains where counts rarely exceed moderate values (e.g., certain biological or text applications), where the truncation would perform well. Second, for the quadratic model, I question whether "Gaussian-like tail behavior" would be inappropriate in some contexts—this seems plausible for certain data distributions.

I note the term "sparsistent" appears without definition; while it has been used in prior literature, its specific meaning should be clarified for broader accessibility.

Minor corrections:  
- Page 3, above 2.1.1: "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model distributions"  
- Page 5, above 3.1: "Note that the log..." likely intended as "Note that the log..." (no need for "although" here).

Overall, this is a well-executed paper with clear technical contributions. The proposed models offer practical improvements for count data modeling, and the experimental validation is thorough. The concerns raised are minor relative to the paper's strengths.
