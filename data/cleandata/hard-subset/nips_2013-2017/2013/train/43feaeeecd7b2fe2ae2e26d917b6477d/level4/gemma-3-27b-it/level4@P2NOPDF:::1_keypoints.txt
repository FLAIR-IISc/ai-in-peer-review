- Introduces novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Identifies limitation of Yang et al.’s default model: cannot represent positive correlations while remaining normalized.  
- Proposes improvements: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic, and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Demonstrates that the quadratic statistic is a necessary component for certain behaviors.  
- Shows effectiveness through experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and reports significant results.  
- Reviewer questions the authors’ dismissive stance on truncation—bounded counts are common and truncation may perform well in practice.  
- Reviewer doubts the critique of the quadratic model’s “Gaussian-esque thin tails,” noting such tails can be appropriate in some scenarios.  
- Notes unfamiliar term “sparsistent,” though it seems established in the literature.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: change “graphical mode distributions” to “model” (p.3) and clarify “Note that the log…” to “Note that although the log…” (p.5).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper offering a substantial contribution to modeling joint count distributions.
