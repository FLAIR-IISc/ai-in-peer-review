- Presents a novel construction for multivariate count distributions that respect an undirected graphical (MRF) structure.  
- Proposal yields appealing features, including relatively tractable estimation algorithms.  
- Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) produces marginally thinner tails than a standard Poisson.  
- Sparse Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining conditional-specification benefits.  
- Labeling SPGM as “Poisson” is questionable because its modifications are more substantial than QPGM’s base-measure changes.  
- Raises the broader question of whether explicitly modeling counts is well-motivated given common Poisson misfit to empirical data.  
- Recommends visualizing SPGM’s probability mass function since its formulation appears complex and unintuitive.  
- Assumes R and R0 are constants via exponential-family arguments, but needs clarification and practical guidance on selecting these parameters.  
- Theoretical contribution is valid and commendable, and of interest to the NIPS community.  
- Engagement with prior literature is incomplete: copula discussion seems superficial and reference [8] is misrepresented.  
- Omits substantial spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data, which is a notable gap.  
- Discussion around [11] may be irrelevant given [8]’s core contribution of multivariate discrete constructions with established inference methods.  
- Practitioner relevance is unclear; paper lacks strong justification that the model’s complexity outperforms simpler approaches (e.g., Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement).  
- Suggests updating citation [20] to a more recent reference for the claim about sequencing read counts replacing microarrays.
