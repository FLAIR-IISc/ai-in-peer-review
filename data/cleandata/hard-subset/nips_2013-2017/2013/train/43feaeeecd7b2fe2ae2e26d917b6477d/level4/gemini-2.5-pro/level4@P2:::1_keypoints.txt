- Problem: Standard Poisson graphical models are limited to non-positive edge parameters, preventing modeling of positive conditional dependencies.
- Contribution: Proposes three new count-data graphical models that allow both positive and negative dependencies.
- TPGM: Introduces a valid truncation-based PGM on a finite domain to permit arbitrary dependencies, correcting prior Winsorized-Poisson approaches.
- QPGM: Shows linear-statistic models need quadratic base measures for normalizability with positive edges, yielding a quadratic-base PGM with Gaussian-like normalizability.
- SPGM: Proposes a sub-linear sufficient statistic model that keeps the Poisson base measure while allowing all real-valued edge parameters.
- Theory: Provides rigorous proofs (e.g., necessity of quadratic base measure, normalizability) and logical progression from flawed prior ideas to valid models.
- Experiments: Demonstrates practical benefits via structure recovery on simulated networks and a microRNA sequencing dataset, where TPGM and SPGM outperform continuous-data methods.
- Originality/significance: Addresses an important limitation in multivariate count modeling with novel, principled solutions likely to influence future work.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and explains modeling intuition clearly.
- Technical quality: Theoretical contributions and proofs are sound and well-presented.
- Weakness: The paper frames TPGM and QPGM drawbacks too strongly and should more evenly discuss scenarios where truncation or thin tails are acceptable or desirable.
- Suggestion: Add a brief theoretical argument for why neighborhood-selection sparsistency results (e.g., Yang et al.) should extend to the new models.
- Minor issues: Typo "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model distributions" and clarify a dense sentence about tractability of log-partition functions.
- Recommendation: Strongly recommend acceptance.
