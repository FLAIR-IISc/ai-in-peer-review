- Paper proposes several new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables, addressing limitations of the Yang et al. default model (only negative correlations).  
- Proposed fixes include a truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proven necessary under fixed conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Experimental evaluation includes both synthetic and real-data experiments.  
- Reviewer finds the paper well written, original, and presenting significant results.  
- Reviewer is unconvinced by the "big negatives" claimed for the truncation model (may work well when counts are low) and the quadratic model (Gaussian-like thin tails can be appropriate).  
- Reviewer notes unfamiliarity with the term "sparsistent," though acknowledges it has prior usage.  
- Minor typos/edits: change "graphical mode distributions" → "model" (p.3) and clarify "Note that the log..." to "Note that although the log" (p.5).  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear writing, novel and significant contributions, and experiments on varied data; Arguments against: concerns about criticisms of proposed fixes, unclear terminology, and minor grammatical errors.
