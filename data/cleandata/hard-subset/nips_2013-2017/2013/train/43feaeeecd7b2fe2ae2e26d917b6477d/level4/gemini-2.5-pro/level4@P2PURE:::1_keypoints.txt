- Paper proposes multiple new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables, addressing limitations of prior work.
- Motivation: prior model (Yang et al.) can only represent negative correlations while remaining normalizable.
- Proposed solutions: a truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary under some conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.
- Methods are evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Paper is well-written and presents original, significant contributions.
- Main reservation: criticisms of the truncation method are unconvincing — truncation is suitable when counts are naturally bounded or small.
- Main reservation: criticisms of the quadratic model are weak — its Gaussian-like thin tails may be desirable for some data.
- Minor comments/typos: define or cite "sparsistent"; correct "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model distributions"; rephrase the sentence starting "Note that the log..." on page 5 for clarity.
