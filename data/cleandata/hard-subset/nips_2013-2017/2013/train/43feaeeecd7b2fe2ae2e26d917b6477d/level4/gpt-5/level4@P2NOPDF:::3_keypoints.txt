- Strength: Proposes valid multivariate count models that preserve graph-structured dependence.  
- Strength: Estimation procedures are relatively simple and computationally tractable.  
- Strength: Addresses a difficult theoretical problem of interest to the community.

- Concern: QPGM produces tails thinner than Poisson, which may be inappropriate for overdispersed or heavy-tailed count data.  
- Concern: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining efficient local computation and inference.  
- Concern: The label “Poisson” for SPGM is unclear—need justification of nomenclature and which Poisson properties are retained.  
- Concern: Need empirical motivation for Poisson-type families in observable space; many real-world counts are overdispersed or zero-inflated.  
- Concern: For the breast cancer example, compare empirical distribution to a Poisson fit (plot requested).  
- Concern: A pmf plot for SPGM is needed to build intuition because its form is convoluted.  
- Concern: R and R0 appear treated as constants to preserve exponential-family form—practical guidance needed on selection, defaults, tuning, or data-driven choice.

- Related-work: Literature review is incomplete and sometimes inaccurate, notably around copulas.  
- Related-work: Must clarify differences and improvements over discrete copula-based methods (conditional independence, scalability, identifiability, tail behavior).  
- Related-work: Treatment of classic log-linear MRF literature ([11]) is unclear; clarify what is inherited or alternative.  
- Related-work: Omission of spatial statistics and latent-Gaussian-plus-Poisson models is substantial; position the observed-space MRF relative to these established approaches.

- Practical trade-off: Need concrete price–performance comparisons versus structured Gaussian random fields with Poisson measurements (predictive accuracy, calibration, tail fit, robustness, computational cost).  
- Practical trade-off: Clarify inference and learning consequences of SPGM’s lack of closed-form conditionals (algorithmic complexity, mixing, convergence, scalability).

- Empirical suggestions: Provide SPGM pmf plots and tail comparisons with Poisson and QPGM.  
- Empirical suggestions: Provide sensitivity analyses for R and R0.  
- Empirical suggestions: Fit datasets known to be overdispersed or zero-inflated and report results.  
- Empirical suggestions: Compare to latent-Gaussian count models and discrete copula-based baselines.  
- Empirical suggestion: Update or soften the genomics citation claim with a more recent reference.

- Overall: Paper tackles an important hard problem with interesting theoretical contributions and simple estimation, but needs clearer nomenclature, a more careful literature review/positioning, hyperparameter guidance, and stronger empirical evidence to address practical concerns.
