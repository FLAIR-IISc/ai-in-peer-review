This paper presents a novel construction for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts, constrained by the Markov structure of an undirected network. Constructing such distributions is a known challenge, extensively studied for decades – as highlighted in works like Arnold et al. (1999) and the broader literature on conditional specification.

The proposed approach offers valuable contributions, enabling marginal distributions over counts and facilitating relatively straightforward estimation. However, it also presents limitations. The Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) exhibits marginally thinner tails, while the Sparse Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) lacks closed-form conditional distributions, potentially undermining the benefits of a conditionally specified model. Indeed, the classification of SPGM as a Poisson distribution is questionable, though the modification to the base measure in QPGM is more readily defensible. This raises questions about the core motivation for count modeling, given the known limitations of the Poisson distribution itself for empirical data fitting. Visualizing the data (e.g., breast cancer counts) alongside the proposed distributions, and a plot of the SPGM probability mass function, would be beneficial for understanding its behavior. Clarification on the selection of parameters R and R0, and practical guidance for their use, would also be helpful.

Despite these points, constructing multivariate count distributions respecting Markov Random Field (MRF) independence is a difficult problem, and the authors’ effort is commendable. The results hold theoretical interest for the NIPS audience. However, the paper’s engagement with existing literature feels somewhat superficial. The authors’ understanding of concepts like copulas and related work (e.g., [8]) appears incomplete, and the discussion of [11] is potentially misaligned with the core principles of log-linear modeling for discrete data. Critically, the paper overlooks the extensive literature in spatial statistics, which has long addressed high-dimensional count data analysis. While some existing approaches don’t directly model MRF-style constraints in the observable space, their omission is significant.

The theoretical contribution is valid, but as a practitioner, the advantages of this model over established methods – such as a structured Gaussian random field combined with a Poisson measurement model – are not immediately clear. Finally, a more recent citation than [20] supporting the claim regarding the replacement of microarrays with sequencing read counts would strengthen the paper. 

In summary, this is a method for constructing multivariate count distributions with Markovian properties. It presents both advantages and disadvantages, but the literature review could be more comprehensive.
