- Proposes a construction for multivariate count distributions that respect the Markov structure of an undirected graphical model.  
- Construction offers benefits: allows marginal count distributions and relatively straightforward estimation procedures.  
- Quadratic Pair Gaussian Model (QPGM) has marginally thinner tails, limiting fit for heavy-tailed count data.  
- Sparse Pair Gaussian Model (SPGM) lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining a conditionally-specified model’s advantages.  
- Unclear whether SPGM can legitimately be considered a Poisson distribution; for QPGM the base measure may be the only modification.  
- Questions the core motivation given the frequent inadequacy of the Poisson distribution for empirical count data.  
- Requests empirical diagnostics: plot of breast cancer data to assess Poisson similarity and plot of SPGM’s probability mass function.  
- Assumes R and R0 are treated as constants; asks how these are chosen and what practical guidance is provided.  
- Appreciates the theoretical difficulty and contribution of constructing Markov-compatible multivariate count distributions.  
- Major concern: limited and sometimes inaccurate engagement with relevant literature, especially on copulas.  
- Criticizes mischaracterization of reference [8], which builds multivariate discrete distributions and offers MCMC/approximate inference methods.  
- Questions discussion of [11] and points to existing discrete log-linear constructions that scale efficiently.  
- Notes a significant omission of the spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data.  
- Practical skepticism: simpler models (structured Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement) may be preferable to the proposed complex approach.  
- Requests an updated reference to support the claim that sequencing read counts are replacing microarrays.
