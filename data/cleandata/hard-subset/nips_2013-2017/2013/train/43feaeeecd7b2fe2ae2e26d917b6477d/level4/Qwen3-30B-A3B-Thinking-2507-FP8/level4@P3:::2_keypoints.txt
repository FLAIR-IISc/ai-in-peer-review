- Addresses an important gap by extending Poisson graphical models to allow positive and negative conditional dependencies.  
- Correctly identifies and proves the flaw in Kaiser & Cressie [20] (Winsorized Poisson cannot yield a valid joint distribution; Proposition 1 and Theorem 1).  
- Proposes three novel models: Truncated PGM (TPGM) using domain truncation, Quadratic PGM (QPGM) modifying the base measure, and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) altering sufficient statistics.  
- Theoretical development is well-motivated and builds on Yang et al., explicitly addressing the constraints from Proposition 1.  
- Strong theoretical quality with normalizability results (Theorems 3 and 4) and solid exponential-family justification; trade-offs between models are identified.  
- Clarity is generally good; TPGM exposition is clear, but SPGM’s sufficient-statistics explanation needs more intuitive motivation (move Figure 3 into main text).  
- Originality is high: meaningful advancement over prior work with thorough related-work positioning.  
- Significance is limited by weak experimental evaluation—synthetic experiments mainly use data generated by the proposed models, reducing generality.  
- Real-data analysis (breast cancer miRNA) is promising but lacks critical comparisons to appropriate baselines, especially Gaussian copula models with sensible marginals.  
- Main experimental weakness: failure to properly evaluate copula-based methods and reliance on the non-paranormal baseline with problematic marginal assumptions.  
- Recommendation to strengthen experiments: include comparisons to Gaussian copula models with appropriate marginals, log-probability test comparisons, and more comprehensive baseline analyses.  
- Writing is clear overall; suggest shortening tangential discussion of QPGM’s Gaussian-like tails and improving practical-discussion of truncation parameter choices.
