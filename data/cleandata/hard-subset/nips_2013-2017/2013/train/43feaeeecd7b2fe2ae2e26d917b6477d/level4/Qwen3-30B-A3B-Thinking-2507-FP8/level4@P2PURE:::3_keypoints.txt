- Paper proposes a construction for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect Markov structure of an undirected graph.  
- Proposal enables marginal count distributions and yields relatively straightforward estimation algorithms.  
- QPGM exhibits marginally thinner tails, which may be a drawback.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the rationale for a conditionally-specified model.  
- It is unclear whether SPGM should be called a Poisson distribution (QPGM may only alter the base measure).  
- Fundamental motivation is questioned because the Poisson rarely fits empirical count data well; authors need to justify modeling counts this way.  
- Request to plot the SPGM probability mass function, since it appears complex.  
- If R and R₀ must be constants for exponential-family properties, authors should explain how to choose them and give practical guidance.  
- Constructing MRF-consistent multivariate count distributions is hard; the theoretical contribution is of interest.  
- Literature review is incomplete and underappreciates relevant work (notably copulas and sparse precision Gaussian copula approaches).  
- Discussion of prior work [11] seems questionable regarding log-linear structures and treatment of Poisson distributions.  
- Paper neglects extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data.  
- Practitioner concern: unclear why to prefer this model over simpler alternatives like a structured Gaussian random field with a Poisson measurement model.  
- Request for a more recent reference than [20] to support the claim that sequencing read counts are replacing microarrays.
