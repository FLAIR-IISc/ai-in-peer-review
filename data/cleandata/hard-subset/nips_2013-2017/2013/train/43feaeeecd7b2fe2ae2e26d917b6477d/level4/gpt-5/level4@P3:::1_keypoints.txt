- Addresses limitation of Poisson graphical models requiring non-positive edges; proposes TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM to allow mixed-sign dependencies for counts.
- TPGM: shows commonly suggested Winsorized truncation is invalid and proposes a new truncation that yields a valid exponential-family MRF on {0,…,R}^p with no sign constraints.
- QPGM: proves that mixed-sign edges with linear sufficient statistics require a quadratic-scaling base measure and introduces a quadratic base model permitting mixed signs under a negative-definite quadratic form.
- SPGM: introduces sub-linear sufficient statistics with a Poisson base measure to allow mixed-sign edges while avoiding Gaussian-like thin tails; provides a variational bound for node-wise log-partition.
- Estimation is performed via penalized neighborhood selection with proximal gradient; simulations and an RNA-seq miRNA network are presented.
- Theoretical contributions are sound: clear theorems on invalidity of Winsorized conditionals, necessity of quadratic base growth, and SPGM normalizability.
- Normalizability conditions for QPGM (negative-definite quadratic form) are reasonable and well justified.
- Variational upper bounds for node-wise estimation (Gaussian for QPGM, Poisson for SPGM when θ ≥ 0) are sensible.
- Empirical evaluation: TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian/nonparanormal baselines in simulations and produce biologically plausible hubs in the miRNA case study.
- Missing empirical baselines: Poisson log-normal, negative-binomial correlated models, and copula-based count models should be included or discussed.
- Practical/methodological details are light: guidance for choosing R and (R0,R), sensitivity analyses, computational complexity, convergence criteria, and formal graph-recovery guarantees are needed.
- Clarity is generally good, but define “sparsistency” on first use and provide intuition for Theorem 3.
- For SPGM, clarify how the infinite-sum partition function is handled numerically and when the Poisson variational bound is tight.
- Originality: novel mix of results—invalidating Winsorized conditionals, necessity proof for quadratic bases, and sub-linear sufficient-statistics construction.
- Significance: enabling mixed-sign conditional dependencies in tractable MRFs for counts is important for many applications (e.g., RNA-seq); SPGM is particularly practical.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a key limitation, provides three concrete learnable model families, shows empirical benefits, and is well written and connected to prior work.
- Cons/suggestions: add heuristics/data-driven selection for truncation, reframe QPGM tail issue as a trade-off and demonstrate where it excels, detail optimization settings and numerical stability, discuss variational bias, provide or cite recovery guarantees, broaden baselines, and release code.
- Minor comments: define sparsistency, fix typos (e.g., “graphical mode”→“graphical model”), correct wording in a few places, ensure consistent capitalization/hyphenation, and complete reference bibliographies.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original contribution that advances count-valued MRFs; recommend acceptance pending improved practical guidance and broader comparisons.
