- Addresses multivariate count distributions by proposing modifications to Poisson graphical models to handle mixed positive and negative dependencies.  
- Proves a previously proposed truncation method for Poisson graphical models is not a valid probability distribution.  
- Introduces TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM; novelty comes from modifying the base measure and sufficient statistics in the exponential-family representation.  
- Theoretical contributions are sound and well-motivated but heavily rely on Yang et al. (2012).  
- Paper primarily offers new model proposals whose practical advantages are not established empirically.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient, leaving the practical utility and overall merit unclear.  
- Synthetic experiments appear designed to favor the proposed models, limiting generalizability.  
- Real-data experiments lack rigor and omit comparisons to modern copula-based alternatives.  
- Results are presented qualitatively; objective quantitative metrics (e.g., held-out log-probability) are needed.  
- Baseline implementations (e.g., Gaussian copula with rank-based marginals) appear suboptimal and treated as black boxes.  
- More competitive baseline choices (e.g., kernel density marginals, better discrete marginal selection) should be used and tuned.  
- Specific parameter choices (e.g., R=11 for discrete marginals) may be inappropriate and should inform marginal modeling.  
- Include all proofs in the supplement for transparency and reproducibility, especially after correcting prior work.  
- Structural/clarity suggestions: Section 3.1 is overly detailed and non-essential; Section 3.2 needs clearer pacing and the key illustrative figure moved to the main text.
