- Proposes two constructions (QPGM and SPGM) for multivariate, unbounded-count distributions that are Markov w.r.t. an undirected graph.  
- Addresses a difficult and important problem and offers concrete constructions with tractable estimation.  
- Theoretical contribution is relevant and of independent interest for the conference.  
- Concern: QPGM appears to have thinner tails than Poisson, raising questions about modeling overdispersion and zero-inflation; request empirical tail diagnostics.  
- Concern: SPGM lacks closed-form conditionals, which may complicate inference (e.g., Gibbs sampling); ask for clarification of conditional/pseudo-conditional inference, cost, and accuracy.  
- Concern: The term “Poisson” for SPGM is unclear if base measure/kernel deviate from Poisson; request justification or rebranding.  
- Concern: Role and selection of R and R0 are unclear—how are they chosen, are they fixed, and what about identifiability, regularization, and robustness?  
- Request: Provide intuition and visualization (e.g., PMFs) for SPGM and comparisons across SPGM, QPGM, and Poisson (mean/variance, tail, zero mass).  
- Request: Clarify when a direct count MRF is preferable to latent-Gaussian approaches and quantify the trade-offs in complexity, flexibility, and fit.  
- Relation to prior work: Need deeper connection to classical conditional specification and log-linear discrete graphical models, especially parameter-scaling issues.  
- Relation to prior work: Copula-based discrete/count models (including Gaussian copulas with sparse precision) are insufficiently discussed and should be compared fairly.  
- Relation to prior work: Overlooks spatial-statistics literature (Poisson-lognormal, CAR/ICAR, hierarchical models) that provides competitive baselines and tools.  
- Empirical evaluation: Require fit and predictive comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., Poisson-lognormal/latent Gaussian) and diagnostics for marginal and dependence fit.  
- Computational evaluation: Provide runtime/memory scaling with node count and graph density, and report convexity/convergence guarantees for estimation.  
- Practical guidance: Explain when to choose QPGM vs SPGM based on data features (dispersion, graph sparsity).  
- Minor: Update or soften the claim about sequencing reads replacing microarrays with a more recent reference.  
- Minor: Clarify whether constructions admit consistent structure learning procedures (e.g., penalized neighborhood selection) despite tail/conditional issues.  
- Overall: Promising theoretical work but needs stronger literature placement, clarification of R/R0 and “Poisson” nomenclature, and richer empirical diagnostics/comparisons to be compelling.
