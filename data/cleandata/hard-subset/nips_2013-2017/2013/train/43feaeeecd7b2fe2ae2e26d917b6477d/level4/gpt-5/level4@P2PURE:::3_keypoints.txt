- Proposes constructions for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect an undirected MRF structure.  
- Problem context: building multivariate count distributions is difficult and has long been studied (e.g., Besag; Arnold et al., 1999).  
- Positive: constructions yield valid marginal distributions over counts.  
- Positive: lead to relatively simple estimation procedures.  
- Negative: QPGM appears to produce marginally thinner tails.  
- Negative: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining its conditional-specification rationale.  
- Terminology concern: unclear why SPGM is described as “Poisson”; only QPGM plausibly changes the base measure.  
- Modeling concern: Poisson may be a poor empirical fit for many count data sets; motivation for Poisson-like models needs justification.  
- Empirical requests: show a plot of the breast cancer data to assess Poisson fit and a plot of SPGM’s probability mass function.  
- Clarification needed: are R and R0 treated as constants (necessary for exponential-family status)? How are they chosen in practice — guidance for practitioners required.  
- Literature engagement is light: apparent confusion/misrepresentation of copula methods and of reference [8].  
- Questionable discussion of [11]; omission of extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional counts.  
- Practical relevance: many alternative approaches (e.g., sparse-precision Gaussian copula, Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement) do not enforce MRF observables; benefits of restricting to this model class are not clearly demonstrated.  
- Final minor point: supply a more recent reference than [20] to support the claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays.
