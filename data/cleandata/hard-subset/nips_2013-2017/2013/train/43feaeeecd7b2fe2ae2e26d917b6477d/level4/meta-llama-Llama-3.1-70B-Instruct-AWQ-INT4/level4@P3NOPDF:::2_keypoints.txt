- Paper tackles an important problem and correctly identifies limits of current multivariate count models, though a reservation is noted.  
- Theoretical contributions: proves prior truncation invalid and proposes TPGM, QPGM, SPGM; TPGM is natural, QPGM/SPGM are novel in modifying base measure/sufficient statistics.  
- Much of the theory closely follows Yang et al., so practical merit of multiple model variants remains to be demonstrated.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and biased: synthetic data favors proposed models, real-data experiments omit copula-based baselines, and results are only qualitative.  
- Need objective evaluation using test-set log-probabilities since methods produce joint distributions.  
- Copula baselines are used in a black-box/inappropriate way (non-paranormal marginals have no density); simpler marginals or Gaussian KDE would be a more reasonable baseline.  
- Presentation: paper is well written but should include full proofs in the supplement; trim overly detailed parts of Sec.3.1 and better present/explain Sec.3.2 and its figure in the main text.  
- Overall: approach is appealing with some novelty, but limited and biased experiments leave the practical value of the new models unclear.
