- Introduces several new models for joint distributions over integer-valued count variables.  
- Argues Yang et al.’s default model is unsatisfactory because normalization forces only negative correlations.  
- Proposes three remedies: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary when other components fixed), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Presents experimental results on both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Paper is well-written; contributions appear original and results significant.  
- Reviewer not fully convinced the claimed “big negatives” of truncation and quadratic models hold in practice (truncation okay when counts small; quadratic may suit thin-tailed data).  
- Reviewer unclear on the meaning of “sparsistent.”  
- Minor edits suggested: “graphical mode distributions” → “model”; clarify “Note that the log...” to “Note that although the log...”.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed paper on modeling joint distributions over count variables.
