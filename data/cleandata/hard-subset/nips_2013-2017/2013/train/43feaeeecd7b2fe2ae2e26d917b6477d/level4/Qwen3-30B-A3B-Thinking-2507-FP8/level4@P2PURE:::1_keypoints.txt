- Introduces novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Claims the "default" Yang et al. model is unsatisfactory because it only models negative correlations to ensure normalization.  
- Proposes refinements: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary when others fixed), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Presents experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Reviewer states the paper is well-written and makes original, significant contributions.  
- Reviewer finds the criticisms of the truncation and quadratic models unconvincing: truncation may work well in low-count domains, and Gaussian-like thin tails from the quadratic model can be appropriate in some applications.  
- Reviewer is unfamiliar with the term "sparsistent" but notes it has prior usage in the literature.  
- Minor suggested wording corrections: "graphical mode distributions" → "graphical model"; "Note that the log..." → "Note that although the log...".
