- Proposed method constructs multivariate count distributions respecting an undirected MRF and offers relatively simple estimation procedures.  
- The paper makes a theoretically interesting contribution to building multivariate count models with MRF independence.  
- QPGM appears to have marginally thinner tails, raising concerns about tail behavior.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the goal of conditionally specified models and calling into question calling it “Poisson.”  
- Primary motivation for modeling counts is unclear given Poisson’s poor empirical fit; empirical justification (e.g., plot of breast cancer data vs. Poisson) is requested.  
- A plot of the SPGM probability mass function is needed because its form is convoluted and hard to interpret.  
- Clarify treatment and practical selection of constants R and R0 (assumed fixed for exponential-family structure) and provide guidance for practitioners.  
- Literature review is light and sometimes misleading, suggesting unfamiliarity with copulas and misrepresenting reference [8].  
- Discussion of reference [11] is questionable; its core contribution on constructing discrete log-linear models without exponential complexity is overlooked.  
- The paper largely omits relevant spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data, which is important for comparison.  
- Practical concern: practitioners may prefer a structured Gaussian random field plus Poisson measurement model over staying within this complex model class.  
- Minor request: provide a more recent reference than [20] to support the claim that sequencing read counts are replacing microarrays.  
- Overall: the method is a valid nontrivial multivariate Markov count construction with advantages and shortcomings, but the literature review and practical motivation are incomplete.
