- Addresses a significant challenge in constructing multivariate count distributions and correctly identifies limitations of existing approaches.
- Theoretical contributions are largely sound and well-motivated, proving prior truncation methods fail to yield valid models.
- Proposes three alternatives: TPGM (intuitive truncated Poisson), QPGM, and SPGM, with QPGM/SPGM novel in treating base measure and sufficient statistics as design parameters.
- The theoretical development closely follows Yang et al., leaving practical merit unproven without empirical validation.
- Synthetic experiments are overly tailored to the proposed models and do not convincingly demonstrate general practical value.
- Real-data evaluation omits comparisons to standard copula-based alternatives, undermining claims of practical improvement.
- Results rely on qualitative anecdotes rather than objective metrics; log-probability on test data (and similar metrics) is essential.
- Copula competitors are treated as black boxes and use inappropriate marginal choices, invalidating comparisons; identical performance to Glasso is suspicious.
- Simpler marginal estimators (e.g., Gaussian KDE) or translating the chosen R=11 to copula marginals were not tried but likely would improve copula baselines.
- Paper is well written and mathematical claims appear valid; authors correctly fixed a prior error and should include all proofs in the supplement.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and Section 3.2 needs clearer presentation; the key figure belongs in the main text with more intuitive explanation.
- Summary: technically sound and novel framework, but empirical shortcomings and non-standard baselines prevent demonstrating practical advantages; stronger, fairer experiments needed.
