- Addresses an important problem: constructing multivariate count models and notes limits of existing Poisson graphical models (with one reservation).
- Strength: proves a previously proposed truncation is invalid and presents alternative models.
- Proposes TPGM (intuitive truncation), and novel QPGM and SPGM that modify base measure and sufficient statistics in an exponential-family framework.
- Design choices are well motivated, but much theory closely follows Yang et al., leaving many variants whose merits need empirical comparison.
- Experimental evaluation is underwhelming and appears tailored to favor the proposed models.
- Missing direct comparison to copula-based methods on real data.
- Results are largely qualitative; quantitative evaluation (e.g., held-out log probability) is necessary since models define joint distributions.
- Copula competitors are treated as black boxes; the Gaussian copula should be paired with sensible marginal models, not the nonparanormal transform.
- The similarity to Glasso is hard to interpret; simple marginal estimators (e.g., Gaussian kernel density) might yield better copula baselines.
- Choice of R=11 in real-data experiments could be mirrored by informed copula marginals; a fair copula baseline is required.
- Paper is generally well written; include all proofs in the supplement for completeness given the correction of prior work.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and the bound seems unhelpful; Section 3.2 needs clearer exposition and its key figure should be in the main text.
- Overall: conceptually attractive and novel, but empirical evaluation is limited/biases remain, so practical benefits are unclear.
