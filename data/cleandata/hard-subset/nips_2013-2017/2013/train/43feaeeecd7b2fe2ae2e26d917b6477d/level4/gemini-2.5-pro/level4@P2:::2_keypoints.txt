1. Paper addresses limitation of Poisson Graphical Models and aims to model both positive and negative dependencies in count data.  
2. Main contributions: proof that a "Winsorized" PGM is invalid, a valid Truncated PGM (TPGM), and two new models QPGM and SPGM.  
3. Builds on node-conditional graphical model construction framework (Besag, 1974; Yang et al., 2012).  
4. Strength: tackles an important, widely applicable problem in modeling multivariate count data.  
5. Strength: novel, elegant idea of modifying base measure/sufficient statistics to avoid PGM parameter restrictions.  
6. Strength: valuable correction to prior work by proving Winsorized PGM is not a valid joint distribution.  
7. Strength: paper is generally well-written and organized with clear motivation.  
8. Weakness: empirical evaluation is insufficient to demonstrate practical advantages over strong baselines.  
9. Weakness: lacks objective metrics—no held-out log-likelihood comparisons to assess generative model fit.  
10. Weakness: comparisons to copula-based baselines are superficial and likely use inappropriate marginal transformations.  
11. Weakness: theoretical framework is incremental, mainly a new specification within existing node-conditional construction.  
12. Suggestion: include held-out log-likelihood comparisons on synthetic and real data for all models and baselines.  
13. Suggestion: perform more careful copula comparisons using appropriate marginal transformations (empirical CDF, KDE, etc.).  
14. Suggestion: condense or move variational-bound details to appendix and expand SPGM exposition and its illustrative figure in the main text.  
15. Suggestion: provide full, detailed proofs in the supplement to ensure reproducibility and verification.  
16. Recommendation summary: conceptually appealing and well-motivated, but requires stronger empirical validation to confirm practical utility.
