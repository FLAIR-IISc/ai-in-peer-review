- Proposes three novel count-valued graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) that can model both positive and negative conditional dependencies.  
- Builds on Besag and Yang et al., addressing a major gap in count-data graphical models.  
- Proves Kaiser and Cressie’s Winsorized Poisson construction is invalid (Theorem 1).  
- Introduces TPGM: a valid truncated model that relaxes negative edge-weight constraints but has bounded-domain limitations.  
- Introduces QPGM: uses a quadratic base measure (C(Z)=Z²) to allow both positive and negative edges while remaining normalizable (Proposition 2).  
- Introduces SPGM: uses sublinear sufficient statistics to achieve broader parameter flexibility (Theorem 4).  
- Shows learning via neighborhood selection and validates models on synthetic networks and TCGA microRNA data.  
- Technical quality is strong with sound theoretical proofs and clear mathematical presentation.  
- Some technical details (e.g., choice of R and R₀ for SPGM) need better motivation.  
- SPGM conditional distributions lack closed forms (Proposition 3), reducing practicality for direct inference.  
- Motivation for Poisson-like modeling is weak—paper doesn’t convincingly show TCGA counts deviate from Poisson and should include empirical distribution plots.  
- Literature review is incomplete or misleading: insufficient comparison to copula methods, inaccurate dismissal of sparse log-linear models, and underappreciation of spatial statistics work.  
- Practical advantages over alternatives (Gaussian copulas, hierarchical Poisson models) are not clearly justified.  
- Some citations/claims (e.g., sequencing replacing microarrays) are outdated and need revision.  
- Overall: significant theoretical contribution suitable for NIPS, but would benefit from stronger practical motivation, fuller literature context, and addressing SPGM inference issues.
