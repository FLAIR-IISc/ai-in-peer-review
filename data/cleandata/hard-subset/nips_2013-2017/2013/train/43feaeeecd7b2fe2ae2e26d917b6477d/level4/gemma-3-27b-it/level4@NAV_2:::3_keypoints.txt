- Paper constructs multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect MRF independence structure.
- Contribution addresses a challenging problem and offers theoretically interesting results for the community.
- QPGM yields marginal distributions with thinner tails than desired.
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the purpose of a conditionally-specified model.
- Reviewer questions whether SPGM can legitimately be called a Poisson distribution.
- Authors provide relatively simple estimation algorithms and marginal count distributions as practical upsides.
- No plot of the SPGM probability mass function is provided, which hinders intuition and evaluation.
- It’s unclear whether R and R0 are constants and how practitioners should choose them—guidance is missing.
- Literature review is incomplete, notably overlooking copula models and related multivariate discrete constructions.
- Presentation of reference [8] is inadequate given its core contribution to multivariate discrete models and inference methods.
- Relevance of reference [11] in the context of Poisson models is questioned and needs clarification.
- Extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data is ignored, a significant omission.
- Practical advantages over standard Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement models are not convincingly demonstrated.
- Theoretical contribution stands, but the paper needs stronger empirical/practical motivation and more comprehensive related-work coverage.
- Request to replace or supplement an outdated reference [20] about sequencing reads replacing microarrays.
