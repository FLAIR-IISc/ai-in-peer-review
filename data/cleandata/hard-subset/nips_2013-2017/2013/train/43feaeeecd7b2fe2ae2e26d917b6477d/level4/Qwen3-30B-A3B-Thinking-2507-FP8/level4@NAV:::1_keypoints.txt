- Introduces several new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Claims the "default" Yang et al. model is inadequate because it forces negative correlations to normalize.  
- Proposes refinements: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary when other components fixed), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Provides experimental validation on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Reviewer considers the paper well-written, original, and its results significant.  
- Reviewer questions the strength of the claimed "major drawbacks" for truncation and quadratic models — truncation may work when counts are moderate and quadratic’s thin tails may be acceptable in some applications.  
- Reviewer notes unfamiliarity with the term "sparsistent" but acknowledges its established use in the literature.  
- Minor suggested edits: change "graphical mode distributions" to "model" (p.3) and "Note that the log..." to "Note that although the log..." (p.5).  
- Overall assessment: a well-written, high-quality paper on modeling joint count distributions.
