- Addresses important problem: defining multivariate count distributions that respect undirected graphical conditional independence.  
- Contribution: proposes three models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to allow both positive and negative dependencies while enabling neighborhood-based estimation.  
- Strength: direct, thoughtful constructions of joint distributions from conditional specifications, of clear theoretical interest.  
- Strength: leads to relatively simple neighborhood-selection estimation algorithms suitable for high-dimensional settings.  
- Weakness: QPGM’s quadratic base gives Gaussian-like thin tails, possibly inappropriate for heavy-tailed count data.  
- Weakness: SPGM’s conditionals lack closed-form expressions, undermining the motivation for conditional specification and raising practical estimation/sampling concerns.  
- Weakness: QPGM and SPGM alter the Poisson base and sufficient statistics, weakening the connection to a Poisson framework and raising motivation questions.  
- Suggestion: include data-driven analyses (e.g., marginal PMF plots) and a plot of SPGM’s PMF to justify functional forms and show fit to real data; provide guidance on choosing SPGM hyperparameters R and R0.  
- Major concern: incomplete engagement with related literature, especially spatial statistics on high-dimensional lattice count modeling.  
- Concern: mischaracterizes copula-based methods; works like Dobra & Lenkoski use latent Gaussian structures for discrete/count data and deserve fuller comparison.  
- Concern: oversimplifies Lauritzen’s log-linear model results—his work shows graphical constraints can prevent exponential parameter growth.  
- Open question: why prefer direct observable-space models over standard latent-variable approaches (e.g., latent Gaussian MRF with Poisson observations)?  
- Overall: theoretically interesting and novel, but weakened by limited literature engagement and insufficient justification of modeling choices relative to established alternatives.
