- The problem of building multivariate distributions for count data is important and the authors correctly identify limitations of current approaches (with one reservation).  
- The paper proves a previously suggested truncation is invalid and proposes several alternative constructions.  
- The TPGM truncation is justified by a clear, natural interpretation.  
- The QPGM and SPGM are simple yet novel in using base measures and sufficient statistics of exponential families to modify the Poisson.  
- Much of the theory closely follows Yang et al., leaving multiple candidate models whose relative merits are not established.  
- The experimental evaluation is disappointing and appears biased toward the proposed models.  
- Synthetic experiments look promising but seem tailored to the new methods.  
- Real-data comparisons omit copula-based alternatives and are mainly qualitative/anecdotal.  
- A more objective evaluation (held-out test log-likelihood) is needed since all methods produce joint distributions.  
- Copula competitors are used in a black-box way; marginals should be chosen sensibly rather than using the nonparanormal transform.  
- The surprising parity with Glasso suggests better marginal choices (e.g., Gaussian KDE) would likely improve copula baselines.  
- The paper is well written and correct; include all proofs in the supplement.  
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and its bound adds little; Section 3.2 (the method’s core) needs clearer exposition and its figure should be moved into the main text.  
- Overall, the approach is theoretically appealing but practical value is unclear due to limited and biased experiments.
