- Presents a novel method for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect an undirected network Markov structure.  
- Strength: allows marginal distributions over counts with relatively simple estimation algorithms.  
- Weakness: QPGM exhibits thinner tails than expected.  
- Weakness: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification intent.  
- Questionable: SPGM may not exhibit characteristic Poisson properties, so calling it “Poisson” is unclear.  
- General concern: Poisson is often a poor fit for empirical count data and may be an unsuitable base model.  
- Suggestion: Include a plot of the SPGM probability mass function to clarify its properties.  
- Clarification needed: treatment of R and R0 as constants is unexplained; guidance on selecting these parameters is required.  
- Literature review is incomplete and misses important prior work (e.g., copulas, spatial statistics).  
- Claim: Discussion of reference [8] appears inaccurate.  
- Practicality concern: unclear why practitioners should prefer this model over simpler alternatives like structured Gaussian random fields with Poisson measurements.  
- Positive: theoretical contribution is valid and of interest to the community.  
- Minor request: cite a more recent reference than [20] to support the claim about sequencing reads replacing microarrays.
