- Introduces and analyzes new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables, addressing limitations of prior work.  
- Identifies Yang et al. (2012) model’s key limitation: inability to model positive correlations while ensuring normalizability.  
- Proposes and systematically explores solutions: variable-support truncation, a quadratic base-measure statistic, and a sub-linear sufficient statistic model.  
- Proves the quadratic base-measure statistic is a necessary condition given other model components.  
- Validates the proposed models empirically on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear motivation, original technical contributions, and a logical, compelling progression.  
- Primary critique: characterization of truncation and quadratic models as major limitations is unconvincing.  
- Truncation model drawback (pre-specifying max count) may be acceptable in domains with naturally bounded or rarely large counts.  
- Quadratic model critique (Gaussian-esque thin tails) may be inappropriate when thin tails are a reasonable modeling assumption.  
- Recommendation: acknowledge potential utility of truncation and quadratic models in specific scenarios rather than dismissing them.  
- Minor typos: change "graphical mode distributions" to "graphical model distributions" (p.3), and rephrase the awkward sentence before Sec. 3.1 (p.5).
