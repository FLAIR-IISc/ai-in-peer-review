The paper "On Poisson Graphical Models" proposes a novel approach to modeling count data using Poisson graphical models. The authors argue that existing models, such as Gaussian graphical models and Ising models, are not well-suited for count data, which are increasingly common in big-data settings. They introduce three new models: the Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), the Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and the Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM).

The TPGM is a natural extension of the univariate Poisson distribution, which truncates the domain of the Poisson random variables. The authors show that this model can capture both positive and negative dependencies among variables, but it has some limitations, such as the domain of the variables being bounded a priori by the distribution specification.

The QPGM is a more fundamental modification of the Poisson distribution, which uses a quadratic base measure statistic. The authors prove that this is necessary to permit both positive and negative conditional dependencies and to ensure normalizability. The QPGM has a Gaussian-esque thin tail, which may be a drawback in some cases.

The SPGM is a further modification of the QPGM, which uses a sub-linear sufficient statistic. This model has a heavier tail than the QPGM and allows a broader set of feasible pairwise parameters.

The authors present experimental results on both synthetic and real data, demonstrating the effectiveness of their models. They also discuss the limitations of their models and potential avenues for future research.
