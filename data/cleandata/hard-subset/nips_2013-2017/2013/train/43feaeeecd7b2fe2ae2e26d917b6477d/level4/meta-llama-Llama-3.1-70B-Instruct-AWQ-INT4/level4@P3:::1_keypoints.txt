- Proposes extensions enabling modeling of both positive and negative dependencies in Poisson graphical models, addressing normalizability limits of the original model.
- Reviews existing undirected graphical models and argues they are ill-suited for count data; critiques log-linear, copula, and multivariate Poisson approaches.
- Introduces three new approaches: truncated Poisson, Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and Sub-Linear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM).
- Truncated Poisson can capture positive and negative dependencies but has a bounded domain and struggles with large counts.
- QPGM changes the base measure and sufficient statistics, permits both dependency signs, and exhibits a Gaussian-like thin tail.
- SPGM uses a sub-linear sufficient statistic, produces heavier tails, and allows a broader feasible set of pairwise parameters.
- Simulation studies and a breast cancer microRNA case study show the proposed models outperform Gaussian and original Poisson graphical models.
- Strength: clear, concise review of prior work and limitations for count data.
- Strength: proposed models are well-motivated and directly address key drawbacks of the original Poisson graphical model.
- Strength: experimental evidence supports the models’ ability to capture both positive and negative dependencies.
- Weakness: truncation approach’s bounded domain and large-count issues limit practicality.
- Weakness: QPGM’s thin Gaussian-esque tail may be inappropriate for some applications.
- Weakness: SPGM’s log-partition function lacks a closed form, which may hinder applicability and inference.
- Recommendations: address truncation limitations, clarify application contexts and limitations of QPGM/SPGM, and provide additional experiments/comparisons.
