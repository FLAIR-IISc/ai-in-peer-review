- Addresses the hard problem of constructing multivariate distributions for unbounded count data respecting Markov structure.  
- Proposes three novel models: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).  
- Main contribution: relaxes Poisson graphical model restrictions to allow both positive and negative dependencies.  
- TPGM uses a novel truncation approach to produce a valid graphical model.  
- QPGM and SPGM modify the Poisson base measure and sufficient statistics to extend modeling flexibility.  
- Provides penalized neighborhood selection estimation and empirical results on simulated networks and microRNA data.  
- Strong theoretical grounding with clear derivations and discussion of normalizability and truncation limits.  
- Empirical validation demonstrates initial applicability and performance on real and simulated datasets.  
- Clear and rigorous mathematical presentation.  
- Incomplete literature review: superficial treatment of copulas and neglects extensive spatial statistics and nuanced log-linear model literature.  
- SPGM’s labeling as “Poisson” is tenuous given its modified sufficient statistics; PMF visualization would help.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical implementation challenges and computational costs, especially for SPGM.  
- No guidance on choosing TPGM/SPGM parameters (R, R0) for practitioners.  
- Insufficient justification for choosing these complex models over simpler alternatives like Gaussian graphical models with Poisson measurements.  
- Contains an outdated citation regarding sequencing replacing microarrays.  
- Overall assessment: valuable theoretical contribution but weakened by incomplete literature review and missing practical details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, introduces novel theoretically sound models, and shows promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: key related work omitted, practical/implementation concerns not addressed, and model complexity justification is unconvincing.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—address literature gaps, add practical/computational discussion and parameter guidance, and include SPGM PMF visualization.
