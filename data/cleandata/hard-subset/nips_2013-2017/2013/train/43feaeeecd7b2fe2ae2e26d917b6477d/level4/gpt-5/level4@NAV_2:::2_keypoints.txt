- Goal is important: designing multivariate models for count data is a valuable objective, though caveat noted about existing-method limitations.
- The paper proves a previously proposed truncation is not a valid joint model.
- Authors introduce alternatives: TPGM (natural interpretation), QPGM and SPGM (novel use of exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics).
- Theoretical modifications are well motivated and appealing.
- Much of the theoretical development largely follows Yang et al., limiting the novelty to a menu of plausible model classes.
- Empirical evaluation is underwhelming and biased toward the proposed models in synthetic studies.
- Real-data experiments omit comparisons to copula-based alternatives and rely mainly on qualitative outcomes.
- A more objective assessment (held-out log-likelihood or proper scoring rules) is needed since all methods define joint distributions.
- Copula baselines are used in an overly black-box manner (nonparanormal marginals are inappropriate/practical mismatch).
- Simpler marginal choices (e.g., Gaussian kernel density) would likely improve copula baselines and provide a fairer comparison.
- The chosen truncation parameter (R = 11) in TPGM/SPGM could be mirrored by informed marginal choices in copulas; a reasonable baseline is warranted.
- Paper is generally well written; results appear correct and the identification of prior error is valuable.
- Recommend including full proofs in the supplement.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and the bound adds little; Section 3.2 is the core contribution and needs a clearer, more intuitive presentation with its key figure moved to the main text.
- Overall: proposes appealing Poisson-like multivariate families enabling mixed dependencies, but practical advantage remains uncertain due to limited/biased empirical evaluation.
