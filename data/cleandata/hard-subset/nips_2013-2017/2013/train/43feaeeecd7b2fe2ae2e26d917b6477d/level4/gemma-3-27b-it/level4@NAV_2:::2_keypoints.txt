- Task is important and authors correctly identify limitations of current multivariate count models.  
- Theoretical contribution: proof that a previously suggested truncation is invalid, and several alternative constructions are proposed.  
- TPGM truncation is straightforward and well-motivated by natural interpretation.  
- QPGM and SPGM are novel in modifying the Poisson via base measure and sufficient statistics in the exponential family.  
- Much of the theory follows Yang et al., so many proposed model variants remain to be empirically evaluated.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing: synthetic data is biased toward proposed models and real-data experiments omit key copula-based comparisons.  
- Reported results are mainly qualitative/anecdotal; objective log-probability evaluation on test data is needed.  
- Copula competitors are used in a black-box way; Gaussian copula should use sensible marginals (non-paranormal is inappropriate as it lacks a density).  
- The identical performance to Glasso is suspect; simple alternatives (e.g., Gaussian KDE marginals) might perform better.  
- Hyperparameter/marginal choices (e.g., R=11) for the proposed model should translate to copula baselines—reasonable baseline tuning is warranted.  
- Paper is generally well written; include all proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Presentation issues: end of Section 3.1 is overly detailed and the bound is of limited value; Section 3.2 should be expanded with its figure and clearer intuition.  
- Overall: approach is appealing and has theoretical novelty, but limited and biased experiments leave the practical merit unclear.
