- Proposes several new models for joint distributions over integer-valued (count) variables.  
- Criticizes Yang et al.’s default construction for forcing models to capture only negative correlations.  
- Introduces remedies: a new truncation approach, a quadratic base measure statistic (proven necessary), and a sublinear sufficient statistic.  
- Reports experiments on synthetic and real datasets demonstrating the methods.  
- Paper is well-written, contributions are original, and results are significant.  
- Finds claimed “major drawbacks” of truncation and quadratic models not fully convincing.  
- Requests clearer delineation of when truncation or quadratic models become problematic and more empirical guidance for choosing among alternatives.  
- Asks to define the term “sparsistent” at first use for reader clarity.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “graphical mode distributions” to “graphical model distributions” and clarify an ambiguous sentence on page 5 above Section 3.1.  
- Recommends acceptance, contingent on addressing the exposition and practical guidance caveats.
