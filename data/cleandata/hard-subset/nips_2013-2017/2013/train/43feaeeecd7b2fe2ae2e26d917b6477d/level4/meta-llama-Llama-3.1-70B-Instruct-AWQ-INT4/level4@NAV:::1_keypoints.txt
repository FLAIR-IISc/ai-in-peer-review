- Paper introduces novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables, improving on Yang et al.'s default model.  
- Claims Yang et al.'s model cannot capture positive correlations while maintaining normalization.  
- Proposes several alternatives: a new truncation approach, a quadratic base-measure statistic, and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Provides experimental validation on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Reviewer judges the paper well-crafted, original, and yielding significant results.  
- Criticism: the reviewer finds the negative assessment of the truncation and quadratic models unconvincing.  
- Notes that truncation can be effective when counts are typically low, and quadratic models’ thin (Gaussian-like) tails may be appropriate in some contexts.  
- Reviewer is unfamiliar with the term "sparsistent" despite prior literature use.  
- Minor edits suggested: replace "graphical mode distributions" with "model" (page 3) and rephrase "Note that the log" to "Note that although the log" (page 5).
