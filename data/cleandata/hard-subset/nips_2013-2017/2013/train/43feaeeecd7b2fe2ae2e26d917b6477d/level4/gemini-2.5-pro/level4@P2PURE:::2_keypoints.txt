- Addresses constructing multivariate count distributions and correctly identifies limitations in existing approaches (with one reservation).  
- Proves a previously proposed truncation method is invalid and proposes several alternatives.  
- Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM) is a straightforward, naturally interpretable correction.  
- Quadratic and Submodular Poisson Graphical Models (QPGM, SPGM) are novel, modifying base measure and sufficient statistics in an intuitive way.  
- Much of the underlying theory largely follows Yang et al., so contributions are mainly new model proposals.  
- Practical utility of the proposed models is uncertain due to limited empirical validation.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing: synthetic data appear tailored to favor the new models.  
- Real-data experiments omit comparisons to copula-based alternatives and lack objective metrics.  
- Results are qualitative/anecdotal; need quantitative evaluation such as test log-probability.  
- Copula baselines are treated as black boxes and use a non-paranormal marginal (which lacks a density); better-chosen marginals could yield stronger baselines.  
- Reviewer suggests using Gaussian kernel density marginals and discrete marginals informed by R=11 for fairer copula comparisons.  
- Recommend including all proofs in the supplement for verification and improving exposition (shorten end of Sec.3.1, expand Sec.3.2, move key illustrative figure into main text).  
- Overall: theoretically appealing models enabling mixed dependencies, but empirical evidence is limited and potentially biased, leaving practical advantages unclear.
