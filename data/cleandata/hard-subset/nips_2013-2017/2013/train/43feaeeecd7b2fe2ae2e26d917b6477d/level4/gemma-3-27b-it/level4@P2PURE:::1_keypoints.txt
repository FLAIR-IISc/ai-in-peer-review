- Introduces several new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Motivates work by highlighting limitation of Yang et al.'s model (only normalized distributions with negative correlations).  
- Proposes refinements: a novel truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic with a necessity proof, and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via experiments on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and presents significant results.  
- Arguments against the truncation method are unpersuasive—truncation may suit domains with naturally bounded counts.  
- Critique of the quadratic model is weak—Gaussian-like thin tails may be appropriate in some scenarios.  
- The term “sparsistent” is unfamiliar to the reviewer, though it is acknowledged as established in the literature.  
- Minor edits suggested: correct “graphical mode distributions” to “model” (p.3) and clarify “Note that the log…” (p.5).
