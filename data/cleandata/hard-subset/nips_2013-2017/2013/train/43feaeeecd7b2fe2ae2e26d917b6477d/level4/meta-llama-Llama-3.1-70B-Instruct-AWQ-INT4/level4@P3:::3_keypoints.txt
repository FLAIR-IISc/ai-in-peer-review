- Main contribution: Proposes three novel Poisson graphical model constructions (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) for multivariate unbounded counts that respect undirected Markov structure.  
- TPGM: Uses truncated Poisson node-conditionals allowing both positive and negative dependencies but imposes a priori bounded domains.  
- QPGM: Alters the base measure to a quadratic form, permitting signed edge parameters but producing thin, Gaussian-like tails.  
- SPGM: Replaces sufficient statistics with a sublinear function, enabling a broader parameter range and heavier tails than QPGM.  
- Empirical evaluation: Shows improved performance over Gaussian graphical models and original Poisson graphical models on simulations and a breast cancer microRNA dataset.  
- Strength: Introduces a novel, well-motivated construction for multivariate count distributions with Markov properties.  
- Strength: Develops three distinct model variants, each addressing different dependency and tail-behavior trade-offs.  
- Strength: Demonstrates empirical gains on both simulated and real-world data.  
- Weakness: Literature review is incomplete and misses relevant related work and approaches.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of limitations and concrete potential applications for each approach.  
- Weakness: Lacks practical guidance for selecting SPGM tuning parameters (R and R0).  
- Originality: The model constructions are novel contributions to Poisson graphical modeling and count distributions.  
- Significance: Methods have potential impact across genomics, neuroimaging, spatial statistics, and other fields dealing with counts.  
- Clarity and quality: Paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly presents methods and results.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to address the literature review, expand discussion of limitations/applications, and provide guidance for SPGM parameter selection.
