1. Problem importance acknowledged: constructing multivariate count distributions is important and current approaches have limitations.  
2. Paper proposes alternatives to Poisson graphical models via a conditional exponential construction to allow mixed positive/negative dependencies.  
3. Theoretical contributions include proving a previous truncation is invalid and proposing TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM as alternatives.  
4. QPGM and SPGM are novel in modifying the exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics.  
5. Much of the theory closely follows Yang et al., so many proposed models still require empirical validation.  
6. Experimental evaluation is disappointing and biased: synthetic data favors proposed models and real-data comparisons omit copula-based methods.  
7. Reported real-data results are anecdotal; objective evaluation using held-out log-probability is needed.  
8. Copula baselines were used in a black-box way; sensible marginal models (e.g., KDE) should replace the nonparanormal and simple choices could improve performance.  
9. Choice tuning (e.g., R=11) for the proposed models could be translated to copula marginals—reasonable baselines are warranted.  
10. Paper is generally well written and results appear correct; include all proofs in the supplement.  
11. Presentation issues: end of Section 3.1 is overly detailed and an unhelpful bound; Section 3.2 needs slower exposition and its figure should be in the main text with clearer intuition.  
12. Overall verdict: approach is appealing with some theoretical novelty, but limited and biased experiments leave practical merit unclear.
