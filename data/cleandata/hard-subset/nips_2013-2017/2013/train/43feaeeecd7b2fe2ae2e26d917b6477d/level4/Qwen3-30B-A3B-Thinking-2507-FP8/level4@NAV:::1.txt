

The paper introduces several novel models for representing joint distributions over integer-count variables. The authors contend that the "default" model derived from Yang et al. is inadequate, as it requires negative correlations to ensure distribution normalization. They propose a sequence of refinements, including a new truncation method, a quadratic base measure statistic (which they establish as necessary when other components are fixed), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic. Experimental validation is provided on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  

This is a well-written paper presenting elegant solutions for count data modeling. I consider the work original and its results significant.  

However, I find the "major drawbacks" of the truncation and quadratic models somewhat unpersuasive. For the truncation approach, I can envision scenarios where counts rarely exceed moderate values, rendering the model highly effective. Regarding the quadratic model, "Gaussian-esque thin tails" might be suitable in certain applications.  

I am unfamiliar with the term "sparsistent," though I note its established usage in the literature.
