- Paper proposes multivariate count constructions respecting Markov properties for undirected graphs, making nontrivial theoretical progress.  
- Constructions allow marginal count distributions and relatively straightforward estimation algorithms—practical advantages.  
- QPGM produces marginally thinner tails than standard Poisson, raising fit concerns.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, a critical drawback for conditionally specified models.  
- Terminology is questionable: calling SPGM a “Poisson distribution” and QPGM a mere base-measure modification needs justification.  
- Authors should display SPGM’s probability mass function to clarify its behavior and complexity.  
- Selection and role of parameters (R and R₀) are unclear—are they fixed or estimated, and what guidance is provided?  
- Theoretical contribution is valid but the literature review is incomplete and undermines credibility.  
- The paper misrepresents copula-based work ([8]) and appears to misunderstand copulas’ applicability to discrete/count data.  
- Reference [11] is possibly mischaracterized; key related work on log-linear discrete models is not properly situated.  
- The manuscript neglects extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data.  
- The paper fails to justify advantages over the simpler, well-established structured Gaussian random field + Poisson observation model.  
- The claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays is outdated and should cite more recent literature.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting but practical relevance is weakened by SPGM issues, unclear parameter choices, incomplete review, and insufficient comparison to established alternatives.
