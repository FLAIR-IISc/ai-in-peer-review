- Paper proposes novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables, addressing limitations of existing approaches.  
- Authors argue Yang et al.'s conventional model is limited because it can only model negative correlations to ensure normalization.  
- Proposed modifications include a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary under fixed conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Experiments are performed on both synthetic and real datasets, showing significant results.  
- Reviewer finds overall writing and originality strong and the contribution high-quality.  
- Reviewer is unconvinced by the discussion of limitations for the truncation method, noting many domains have naturally bounded counts.  
- Reviewer is also unconvinced by dismissal of the quadratic model, noting Gaussian-like thin tails may be appropriate in some settings.  
- Reviewer notes unfamiliarity with the term "sparsistent" (though acknowledges it is used in the field).  
- Minor errors: replace "graphical mode distributions" with "model" (p.3) and consider adding "although" to "Note that the log..." (p.5).
