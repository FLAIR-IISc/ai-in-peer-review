The paper proposes novel models for representing a joint distribution over integer-count variables. The authors argue that the conventional model, as presented by Yang et al., is not satisfactory due to its limitation in modeling only negative correlations to ensure normalization. To address this limitation, the authors explore several modifications, including a new truncation method, a quadratic base measure statistic (which they prove to be necessary under fixed conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic. Experimental results are presented for both synthetic and real data.

This is a well-written paper that presents innovative solutions for representing count data. The work appears to be original, and the results are significant. However, I found the authors' discussion on the limitations of the truncation and quadratic models to be somewhat unconvincing. Specifically, for the truncation method, it is possible to envision various domains where counts typically do not exceed high values, and the model would perform adequately. Similarly, for the quadratic model, it is conceivable that "Gaussian-esque thin tails" could be suitable in certain situations.

Additionally, I am unfamiliar with the term "sparsistent," although it appears to be a term that has been used in the field for some time.

I also noted a few minor errors: on page 3, above section 2.1.1, the phrase "graphical mode distributions" should be replaced with "model." On page 5, above section 3.1, the sentence "Note that the log..." would be improved by adding "although" to read "Note that although the log..." Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a high-quality contribution to the field of modeling joint distributions over count variables.
