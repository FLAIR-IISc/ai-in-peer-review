- Problem importance: Building multivariate distributions for count data is important and current methods have shortcomings (with caveat noted).  
- Theoretical contribution: Authors prove a prior truncation is invalid and propose alternatives.  
- Model proposals: TPGM is straightforward and naturally interpretable.  
- Novelty: QPGM and SPGM are novel by modifying the base measure and sufficient statistics of the exponential-family Poisson.  
- Motivation: Modifications are well motivated and attractive, but much theory follows directly from Yang et al.  
- Empirical validation gap: Multiple candidate models need empirical validation to establish their value.  
- Experiments weak: Experimental evaluation is underwhelming and biased toward the proposed models.  
- Synthetic data issue: Synthetic study is promising but tailored to the proposed models.  
- Missing baselines: Real-data experiments omit copula-based comparisons and treat copula competitors as black boxes.  
- Evaluation metric: Results are largely qualitative; objective assessment via test log-likelihood is needed.  
- Copula implementation critique: Using nonparanormal marginals (which lack a density) is inappropriate; sensible marginals (e.g., KDE or matched choices like R=11) should be used.  
- Presentation suggestions: Include all proofs in the supplement; shorten the overly detailed bound in Section 3.1.  
- Clarity improvements: Rework Section 3.2 with clearer exposition and move its figure into the main text.  
- Overall judgement: Approach is appealing with theoretical novelty, but empirical evaluation is limited and practical value remains unclear.
