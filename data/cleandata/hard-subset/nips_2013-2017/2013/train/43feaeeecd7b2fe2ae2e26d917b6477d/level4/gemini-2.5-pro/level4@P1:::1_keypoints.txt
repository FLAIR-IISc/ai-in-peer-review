- Introduces novel graphical models to overcome Poisson graphical model (PGM) limitation of only capturing negative conditional dependencies.  
- Proposes three solutions: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sub-linear PGM (SPGM) in logical progression.  
- TPGM: uses truncation to model both positive and negative dependencies.  
- QPGM: proves linear sufficient-statistic models require a quadratic-scaling base measure to permit positive dependencies.  
- SPGM: uses sub-linear sufficient statistics to allow heavier tails than QPGM.  
- Strong theoretical contributions, including showing the Winsorized PGM is invalid and deriving necessity of quadratic base measure.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and microRNA expression networks demonstrate advantages over existing methods.  
- Paper is well-written and significant, but discussions of TPGM and QPGM drawbacks should be more balanced (TPGM may be fine when counts are naturally bounded; QPGM’s thin tails can be appropriate).
