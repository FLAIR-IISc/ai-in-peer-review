- Paper proposes several novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables and argues Yang et al.'s default model is limited (only allows negative correlations for normalization).  
- Authors introduce refinements: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proven necessary under certain conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Experimental validation is presented on both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Reviewer considers the paper well-written, original, and its results significant.  
- Reviewer finds the critique of the truncation model unconvincing, noting many domains have naturally bounded counts where truncation would work well.  
- Reviewer finds the critique of the quadratic model unconvincing, suggesting Gaussian-like thin tails may be appropriate in some scenarios.  
- Reviewer is unfamiliar with the term “sparsistent,” though acknowledges it appears in prior literature.  
- Minor edits: on page 3 change “graphical mode distributions” to “model”; on page 5 clarify the phrase “Note that the log…” (perhaps “Note that although the log…”).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper on modeling joint distributions over count variables.
