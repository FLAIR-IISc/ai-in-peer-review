- Problem importance: Constructing multivariate count distributions is timely and important, and the paper correctly identifies shortcomings of current approaches.
- Theoretical contribution: Authors prove a previously proposed truncation is invalid and propose alternatives (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM).
- Model novelty: TPGM has a natural interpretation; QPGM and SPGM are technically simple and novel by leveraging the base measure and sufficient statistics.
- Limited theoretical originality: Much of the underlying theory closely follows Yang et al., leaving novelty somewhat incremental.
- Experiments (synthetic): Synthetic experiments are promising but appear tailored to the proposed models, limiting generality.
- Experiments (real data): Real-data evaluation is disappointing—comparisons to copula-based alternatives are missing and results are largely qualitative/anecdotal.
- Evaluation metric: An objective evaluation (e.g., held-out log-likelihood) is needed since all methods build joint distributions.
- Copula baselines mishandled: Copula methods are treated as black-box; using nonparanormal marginals (which lack densities) is inappropriate—better marginal choices (or simple KDEs) and principled settings (e.g., using R=11) are warranted.
- Presentation and reproducibility: Include all proofs in the supplement; Section 3.1 is overly detailed, while Section 3.2 needs clearer exposition and its figure should appear in the main text.
- Overall verdict: Approach is appealing with some theoretical novelty, but limited and biased empirical validation leaves practical merit unclear.
