- Paper proposes constructions for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect the Markov structure of undirected graphs.  
- Advantages: constructions permit marginal count distributions and lead to relatively simple estimation algorithms.  
- Disadvantage (QPGM): Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model yields marginally thinner tails than desired.  
- Disadvantage (SPGM): Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification goal.  
- Conceptual concern: unclear whether SPGM should be called “Poisson” (unlike QPGM, which mainly changes the base measure).  
- Practical concern: Poisson may be a poor fit for real data; authors should show data (e.g., breast cancer counts) and justify Poisson modeling.  
- Suggestion: include a plot of the SPGM probability mass function and clarify interpretation.  
- Clarification needed: are R and R0 treated as constants (to preserve exponential-family form)? How are they chosen in practice?  
- Positive note: constructing MRF-consistent multivariate count distributions is hard; the theoretical contribution is valuable.  
- Literature critique: the literature review is incomplete and sometimes inaccurate, especially regarding copulas and reference [8].  
- Omission: neglects extensive spatial statistics and other high-dimensional count methods that are relevant.  
- Practitioner question: unclear why one would prefer this model over simpler structured Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement models.  
- Minor request: provide a more recent reference than [20] to support the claim about sequencing reads replacing microarrays.  
- Overall: method has meaningful advantages and shortcomings; despite review gaps, the contribution merits consideration.
