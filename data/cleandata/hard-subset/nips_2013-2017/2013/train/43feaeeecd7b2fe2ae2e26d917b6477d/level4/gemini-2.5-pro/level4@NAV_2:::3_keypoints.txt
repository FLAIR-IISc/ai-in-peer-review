- Addresses constructing multivariate count distributions with Markov properties and overcomes Poisson graphical models' restriction to negative conditional dependencies.  
- Proposes three novel models: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) to allow both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Builds on conditionally specified model literature (e.g., Besag) to define valid joint distributions from conditionals.  
- Main strength: direct, novel constructions that are normalizable and allow rich dependency structure for unbounded discrete variables.  
- Practical advantage: models admit relatively straightforward estimation via penalized neighborhood selection.  
- Trade-off: QPGM’s quadratic base yields thin, Gaussian-like tails unsuitable for overdispersed count data.  
- Trade-off: SPGM is more flexible but lacks a closed-form conditional distribution and has a tenuous connection to the Poisson family.  
- Suggestion: authors should justify the SPGM sufficient statistic choice and provide a plot of its probability mass function for intuition.  
- Practical concern: TPGM and SPGM treat truncation parameters (R, R0) as fixed—need guidance on how practitioners should choose them.  
- Suggestion: strengthen motivation for Poisson-based models with exploratory analyses (e.g., marginal plots of real data like the breast cancer miRNA set).  
- Literature review is incomplete: needs more nuanced comparison to copula models, correct framing of log-linear models, inclusion of spatial statistics literature, and comparison to latent-Gaussian + Poisson hierarchical baselines.  
- Minor correction: claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays should cite more recent literature than 2008.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting and valid contribution, but significance limited by narrow literature view, missing comparisons to strong baselines, and inadequate discussion of limitations and practical use.
