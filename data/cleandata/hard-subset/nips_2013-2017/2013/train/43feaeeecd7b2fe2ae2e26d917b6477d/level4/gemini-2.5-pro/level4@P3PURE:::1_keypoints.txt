- Paper introduces several new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables and argues Yang et al.’s default model is limited because normalizability forces only negative correlations.
- Proposes and analyzes solutions: a truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary under certain conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient-statistic model.
- Empirical validation provided on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Overall assessment: well-written, novel, significant contributions with compelling results.
- Main criticism: objections to the truncation approach are unconvincing since many domains have naturally bounded or low-count data where truncation is appropriate.
- Main criticism: objections to the quadratic model’s “Gaussian-esque thin tails” are unconvincing because such tail behavior can be suitable for some count data.
- Minor: reviewer is unfamiliar with the term “sparsistent” despite its prior use in literature.
- Minor: typo—“graphical mode distributions” should be “graphical model distributions.”
- Minor: sentence on page 5 (beginning “Note that the log...”) is unclear and should be rephrased (e.g., “Note that although the log...” ).
