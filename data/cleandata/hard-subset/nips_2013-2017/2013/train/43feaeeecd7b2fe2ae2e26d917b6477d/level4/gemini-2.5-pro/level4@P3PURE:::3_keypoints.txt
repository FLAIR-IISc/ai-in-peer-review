- Strength: Tackles the difficult problem of multivariate distributions for unbounded counts with MRF structure; constructions permit marginal count distributions and relatively simple estimation, a valuable theoretical contribution.

- QPGM limitation: QPGM produces marginally thinner tails, potentially limiting applicability to real count data.

- SPGM limitation: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, which undermines the advantage of a conditionally specified model.

- Poisson connection unclear: The justification for calling SPGM a “Poisson” graphical model is not well-established; QPGM can be seen as changing the base measure but SPGM’s link to Poisson is ambiguous.

- Motivation weak: Poisson is often a poor fit for empirical counts; the paper needs stronger practical motivation, e.g., empirical fit diagnostics (a plot for the breast cancer data).

- Literature engagement poor: Related work discussion is cursory and sometimes misleading, failing to accurately represent prior contributions.

- Copula treatment inadequate: The paper underdevelops copula approaches and mischaracterizes reference [8], which offers a general framework and inference methods for discrete data.

- Misrepresentation of [11]: The paper’s discussion of the seminal book [11] is questionable given its focus on constructing discrete log-linear models without exponential parameter growth.

- Spatial statistics omission: The paper neglects extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count modeling, a significant oversight.

- Practical justification lacking: The paper does not convincingly show why practitioners should prefer these models over latent-variable approaches (e.g., Gaussian random fields with Poisson observations).

- Clarify SPGM form: Include a plot of the SPGM probability mass function for intuition, since its form is not intuitive.

- Parameter guidance needed: Explain how to choose R and R0 in practice and offer guidance for users.

- Update citation: Support the claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays with a more recent reference than [20].
