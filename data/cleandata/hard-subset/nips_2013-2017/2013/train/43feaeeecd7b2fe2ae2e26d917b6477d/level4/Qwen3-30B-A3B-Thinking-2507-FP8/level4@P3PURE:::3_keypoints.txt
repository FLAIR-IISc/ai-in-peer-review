- Paper proposes a multivariate distribution for unbounded counts that respects the Markov structure of an undirected network.  
- Strength: constructions yield marginal count distributions.  
- Strength: estimation algorithms are relatively simple.  
- Limitation: QPGM has marginally thinner tails than Poisson, which may be unrealistic for count data.  
- Limitation: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining its claim as a conditionally specified model.  
- Questioning: calling SPGM a “Poisson distribution” is questionable when it deviates from Poisson behavior.  
- Missing practical evaluation: no PMF plot of SPGM to assess empirical relevance (e.g., for sequencing read counts).  
- Implementation gap: authors treat R and R0 as constants but provide no guidance on their selection or practical implementation.  
- Literature review inadequate: misrepresents prior work [8] and shows misunderstanding of copulas.  
- Critique of [11] is context-free and ignores its contributions on scalable log-linear parameterizations for discrete models.  
- Major omission: ignores extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count analysis.  
- Practical relevance uncertain: a structured Gaussian random field with Poisson observations may be simpler and more interpretable than the proposed model.  
- Minor correction: claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays needs a more recent reference than [20].  
- Overall: theoretically interesting contribution but lacking in literature engagement, model justification, and practical motivation.
