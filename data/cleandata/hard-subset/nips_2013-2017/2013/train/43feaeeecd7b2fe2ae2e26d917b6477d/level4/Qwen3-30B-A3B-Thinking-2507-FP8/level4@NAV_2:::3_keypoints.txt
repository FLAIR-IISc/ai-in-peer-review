- Presents novel construction for multivariate distributions over unbounded count variables that respect Markov properties of undirected graphs.  
- Develops three models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) enabling modeling of positive and negative conditional dependencies in count data.  
- Strength: can model marginal count distributions while maintaining graphical structure.  
- Strength: estimation is relatively straightforward via neighborhood selection.  
- Strength: empirical validation provided on simulated and real microRNA-sequencing data.  
- Limitation: QPGM exhibits thinner tails than typical for count data.  
- Limitation: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the advantage of conditional specification.  
- Questionable claim: labeling SPGM as a "Poisson" variant is dubious because base measure and sufficient statistics were substantially modified.  
- Practical gap: motivation for Poisson-style modeling of counts needs clarification since empirical counts rarely follow Poisson; need guidance on choosing R and R0 and visualizing SPGM PMF.  
- Literature issues: review is incomplete and misrepresents [8] (Nonparanormal) and overlooks extensive spatial statistics work on high-dimensional count data.  
- Insufficient comparison/positioning relative to established methods like sparse precision Gaussian copula models and Gaussian random fields with Poisson measurements.  
- Minor: claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays needs more recent supporting references.  
- Overall: theoretical contribution is sound and interesting for NIPS, but practical relevance and literature context require significant strengthening.
