- Addresses important problem of multivariate count distributions and correctly identifies limits of existing approaches.
- Proves a previously suggested truncation method is invalid and proposes alternative models.
- Introduces Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM) as a natural extension with clear interpretation.
- Proposes Quadratic and Submodular Poisson Graphical Models (QPGM, SPGM) that modify base measure/sufficient statistics—novel but technically simple.
- Much of the theory follows directly from Yang et al. (2012), so novelty is incremental and requires empirical validation.
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and biased toward the proposed models.
- Synthetic experiments appear designed to favor the new methods.
- Real-data experiments omit important copula-based baselines and report results qualitatively rather than via test log-probabilities.
- Treatment of copula competitors is simplistic: non-paranormal marginals lack a density and are not appropriate for counts.
- Suspects simple copula baselines (e.g., Gaussian copula with kernel-estimated count marginals) would outperform reported baselines.
- Choice of model hyperparameter (R=11) could inform better copula marginals; a more reasonable baseline is warranted.
- Paper is generally well-written but should include all proofs in supplementary material for verification.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and its bound is not useful; Section 3.2 is central and should be paced slower with the illustrative figure moved into the main paper.
- Overall: theoretically appealing but empirical support is limited and practical merit remains unclear.
