- Problem significance: Developing multivariate count distributions is important and the paper correctly highlights limitations of existing methods.
- Theoretical contribution: Authors show a previously proposed truncation is invalid and propose several alternative constructions.
- Proposed models: TPGM is straightforward with intuitive justification; QPGM and SPGM innovatively modify Poisson via exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics.
- Novelty caveat: Much of the underlying theory builds on Yang et al., so contributions are extensions/suggestions rather than a wholly new theory.
- Practical utility concern: The practical benefits of the proposed models remain unproven.
- Experiments—synthetic: Synthetic experiments seem tailored to favor the new models and only show limited promise.
- Experiments—real data: Real-data evaluation is weak—missing comparisons to copula-based alternatives and relying on qualitative/anecdotal results.
- Evaluation metrics: Need rigorous quantitative evaluation (e.g., test log-probabilities) since all methods produce joint distributions.
- Copula baselines critique: Copula competitors are treated as black boxes; inappropriate marginal choices (e.g., non-paranormal) undermine fair comparison.
- Baseline recommendation: Simple alternatives (e.g., Gaussian KDE marginals) or reasonable copula marginals should be included; choices like R = 11 could guide marginal selection.
- Presentation quality: Paper is generally well-written and results seem correct; include all proofs in the supplement for completeness.
- Exposition suggestions: Section 3.1 is overly detailed and adds little; Section 3.2 needs clearer, slower exposition and the supplementary figure should be moved to the main text.
- Overall assessment: Conceptually appealing and partly novel, but limited and biased experimental evaluation leaves the overall value of another multivariate Poisson-like model unclear.
