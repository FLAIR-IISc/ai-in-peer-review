- Strong theoretical contribution: identifies limitations of prior truncation approaches and proposes three alternatives (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM).  
- TPGM has a natural interpretation; QPGM and SPGM introduce novel modifications to Poisson base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- The theoretical development largely follows Yang et al., so practical merits require empirical validation.  
- Synthetic experiments are overly tailored to the proposed models and lack generality.  
- Real-data evaluation omits critical baselines, notably copula-based methods.  
- Comparison to the non-paranormal method is flawed: it uses a marginal model with no density and is inappropriate for count data.  
- Black-box treatment of copulas is unacceptable; sensible marginal models (e.g., Poisson or negative binomial) should be used.  
- Reported performance matching Glasso is suspicious; simpler methods like Gaussian KDEs might outperform the misapplied baseline.  
- Hyperparameter choice (R=11 for copula bandwidth) is arbitrary and its effect on marginal models is not addressed.  
- Results lack objective metrics (e.g., test log-probability), making conclusions qualitative and unconvincing.  
- Authors must provide rigorous comparisons against well-configured, established baselines to justify the models.  
- Writing is generally good but proofs should be included in the supplement and Section 3.2 needs clearer exposition.  
- Condense tangential bounds in Section 3.1 and move the key figure into the main text with a clearer intuitive explanation.  
- Overall: conceptually sound theory but severe experimental shortcomings; empirical gaps must be closed to demonstrate practical utility.
