- Proposes three Poisson-based multivariate count models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) via conditional exponential-family modifications to allow positive and negative dependencies.
- Proves a previously suggested truncation yields an invalid model, correcting prior literature.
- Addresses an important problem and offers a clear, useful perspective by treating base measure and sufficient statistics as modification levers.
- TPGM is straightforward and interpretable; QPGM and SPGM are simple, novel, and well-motivated extensions.
- Theoretical contributions feel incremental and closely follow prior work, lacking clear criteria for preferring one variant over another.
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient and appears biased toward the proposed models.
- Synthetic experiments seem tailored to favor the authors’ methods and lack broader scenarios (e.g., varying overdispersion, zero-inflation, mixed dependency strengths).
- Real-data comparisons omit key competitors, notably copula-based methods with appropriate count marginals.
- Reported results are largely qualitative; objective metrics like held-out log-likelihood and calibration/posterior predictive checks are missing.
- Copula baselines are unfairly implemented (nonparanormal transformation is inappropriate for counts) and likely underperform due to ill-specified marginals.
- Authors should include fair copula baselines (Gaussian copula with negative binomial/ZI marginals, discretized lognormal, or kernel-smoothed CDFs) and match model capacity/hyperparameter choices.
- Experimental design should systematically vary dispersion, zero-inflation, sparsity, dependency sign/magnitude, include ablations isolating truncation vs. base-measure changes, and report hyperparameter sensitivity and runtimes.
- Clarify theoretically or empirically when each variant is preferred (e.g., when SPGM captures stronger negative dependence than QPGM or when TPGM bias arises).
- Presentation needs polishing: include all proofs in supplement, tighten/shorten Section 3.1, and expand Section 3.2 with the deferred figure and more intuition.
- Overall: conceptually promising and corrects prior errors, but current empirical evidence is weak; reviewer leans to a weak reject pending stronger, fairer evaluations.
