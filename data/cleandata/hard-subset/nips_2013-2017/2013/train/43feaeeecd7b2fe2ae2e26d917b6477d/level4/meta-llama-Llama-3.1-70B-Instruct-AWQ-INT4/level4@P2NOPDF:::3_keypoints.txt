- Paper proposes a construction for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect an undirected network’s Markov structure.  
- Building multivariate count distributions is hard and has extensive prior work (e.g., Besag; Arnold et al. 1999).  
- Positive: the construction allows marginal count distributions and yields relatively simple estimation algorithms.  
- Negative: the QPGM exhibits marginally thinner tails than desirable.  
- Negative: the SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification aim.  
- Negative: the SPGM does not appear to satisfy the traditional definition of a Poisson distribution, calling the Poisson motivation into question.  
- Suggestion: include a plot of the SPGM probability mass function, since its form appears convoluted.  
- Technical note: R and R0 must be treated as constants for exponential-family structure; authors should advise how to choose them in practice.  
- Contribution: construction is of theoretical interest for multivariate count MRFs and is appreciated despite drawbacks.  
- Criticism: literature review is incomplete and underestimates existing related work.  
- Criticism: apparent misunderstanding of copulas and of reference [8], which targets multivariate discrete distributions including counts.  
- Criticism: discussion of [11] is misplaced; that work deals with discrete log-linear models without exponential growth in variables.  
- Omission: neglects the extensive spatial statistics literature on high-dimensional count data.  
- Practical concern: unclear why one should prefer this model class over simpler alternatives (e.g., structured Gaussian random fields with Poisson observations).  
- Minor: ask for a more recent citation than [20] to support the claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays in gene expression analysis.
