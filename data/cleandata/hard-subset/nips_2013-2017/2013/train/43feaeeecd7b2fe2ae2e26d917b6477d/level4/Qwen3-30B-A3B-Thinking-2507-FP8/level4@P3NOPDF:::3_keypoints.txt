- Addresses the hard problem of constructing multivariate Markov distributions for unbounded counts with two new constructions (QPGM and SPGM).  
- Theoretical contribution is non-trivial: provides valid Markov-preserving frameworks and avoids exponential parameter blow-up.  
- Presents relatively tractable estimation algorithms suitable for high-dimensional settings.  
- QPGM yields Poisson-like marginals but with thinner tails than true Poisson, limiting applicability to heavy-tailed count data.  
- SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining its use as a conditionally specified model.  
- Claiming SPGM is a “Poisson distribution” is misleading: it does not satisfy Poisson properties (e.g., mean-variance equality) nor reduce to Poisson marginals.  
- Paper fails to justify practical need: many real count data show overdispersion/zero-inflation, so the Poisson-based constructions may be inappropriate.  
- No empirical demonstration that the models fit real data (e.g., PMF comparisons for cited datasets) or outperform standard alternatives.  
- Neglects relevant literature on copula-based multivariate count models (e.g., Joe 2014) and mischaracterizes prior work (Arnold et al., 1999).  
- Overlooks spatial statistics literature and established workflows (latent Gaussian/Poisson models, CAR priors, Gaussian copulas) that handle MRF constraints in practice.  
- Provides no comparative empirical evidence, hyperparameter guidance (e.g., selection of R and R0), or practitioner-oriented recommendations.  
- Uses an outdated gene expression reference, suggesting a weak connection to current practice.  
- Required revisions: clarify SPGM’s relation to Poisson, provide empirical comparisons to standard methods, engage with copula and spatial-statistics literature, and specify hyperparameter selection.  
- Overall assessment: technically interesting but limited and practically unconvincing; recommend rejection due to insufficient novelty, mischaracterizations, and lack of empirical support.
