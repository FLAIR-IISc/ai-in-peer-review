- Identifies key limitation: standard Poisson graphical models (Yang et al., 2013) only allow negative conditional dependencies due to normalizability (Proposition 1).  
- Paper’s central contribution: proposes three Poisson graphical model variants—TPGM, QPGM, SPGM—that permit both positive and negative edge weights.  
- Theoretical grounding: proves necessary normalizability conditions for each model variant, addressing prior gaps.  
- Corrects prior work: shows Winsorized Poisson approaches lacked valid joint distributions (Theorem 1).  
- Methodology: systematic modifications—truncation, base-measure alteration, sublinear sufficient statistics—extend Poisson framework.  
- Experimental validation: synthetic experiments show TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian graphical models and original PGM on count data.  
- Case study impact: SPGM recovers biologically meaningful miRNA network edges (e.g., mir-375, mir-10b) missed by Gaussian models.  
- Quality and clarity: technically strong, well-written, clear proofs, and reproducible experimental design.  
- Originality: first classes of Poisson-like graphical models that capture both positive and negative dependencies, advancing prior literature.  
- Significance: important for genomics, spatial stats, and other domains with count data requiring both dependency types.  
- Limitation: TPGM requires a bounded domain, which may limit applicability when counts can be arbitrarily large.  
- Limitation: QPGM exhibits Gaussian-like thin tails, which may be unsuitable for some applications.  
- Suggested improvement: paper should more explicitly discuss when each model variant is most appropriate and model-selection trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: reviewer endorses acceptance—significant, original, technically sound work with compelling experiments.
