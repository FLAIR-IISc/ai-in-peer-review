- Introduces a method to construct multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect an undirected-graph MRF.  
- Positive: constructions yield valid marginal count distributions and enable fairly straightforward estimation.  
- Negative: QPGM exhibits thinner tails than desired.  
- Negative: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification motivation and its “Poisson” interpretation.  
- Request empirical checks: plot the breast-cancer count data to assess Poisson fit and include a plot of the SPGM probability mass function.  
- Clarify modeling choices: are R and R0 treated as constants, and how are they selected in practice—provide guidance for practitioners.  
- Literature coverage is light/incomplete; missing extensive prior work by Besag, Arnold et al., and spatial statistics on high-dimensional counts.  
- Possible misunderstanding or misstatement of copula methods and reference [8]; copula approaches and sparse-precision Gaussian copula alternatives should be discussed accurately.  
- Question appropriateness of discussion of [11]; verify its treatment of Poisson/log-linear constructions.  
- Note that several alternatives do not impose observed-space MRF independencies, so the paper’s theoretical contribution remains valid but practical advantages are not clearly shown.  
- From a practitioner perspective, it’s unclear why one would accept the costs of this MRF model class versus the established Gaussian random field + Poisson observation model.  
- Suggest updating the gene-expression citation with a more recent reference showing RNA-seq replacing microarrays.
