- Introduces new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables, critiquing Yang et al.'s default model for permitting only negative correlations for normalization.  
- Proposes fixes: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proved necessary under fixed conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic; includes synthetic and real-data experiments.  
- Well written and clearly presented.  
- Proposes thoughtful, original solutions with significant contributions to joint count modeling.  
- Empirical evaluation covers both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Concern: Criticism of truncation is unconvincing—many practical domains have naturally bounded counts where truncation is adequate and computationally attractive.  
- Concern: Criticism of the quadratic model’s “Gaussian-esque” thin tails is overstated; tail behavior may be acceptable or desirable in some applications and needs qualification.  
- Request to define or cite the term “sparsistent” to make the paper self-contained.  
- Minor edit: change “graphical mode distributions” to “graphical model distributions” (or simply “model”).  
- Minor edit: revise the sentence above 3.1 on page 5 to “Note that although the log ...” to signal the intended contrast.  
- Overall: High-quality, original paper; recommend tempering blanket negatives about truncation and quadratic models and providing guidance on when each approach is appropriate.
