- Introduces novel models for joint distributions of integer-count variables and targets a key limitation of Yang et al.’s model (inability to capture positive correlations while remaining normalized).  
- Proposes a sequence of remedies: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proven necessary when other components fixed), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Evaluates the proposed models empirically on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, presents elegant solutions, appears original, and reports significant results.  
- Reviewer finds arguments that truncation and quadratic models are major negatives unpersuasive—truncation may work well when high counts are rare.  
- Reviewer also finds the criticism of the quadratic model’s “Gaussian-esque thin tails” possibly too strong, since such tails may suit some data.  
- Minor: “sparsistent” was unfamiliar to reviewer but is used in prior literature.  
- Minor typo: “graphical mode distributions” should be “graphical model distributions.”  
- Minor clarity suggestion: rephrase “Note that the log...” to “Note that although the log...”.
