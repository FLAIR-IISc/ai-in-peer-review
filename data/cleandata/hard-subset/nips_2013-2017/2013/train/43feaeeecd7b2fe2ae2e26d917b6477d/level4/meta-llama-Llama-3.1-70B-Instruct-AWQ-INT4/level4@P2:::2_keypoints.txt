- Goal: constructing multivariate distributions for count data is important and current approaches have limitations.  
- Theory merit: authors prove previously suggested truncation is invalid and propose alternatives.  
- Models proposed: TPGM truncation is well-motivated; QPGM and SPGM are novel by modifying base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Theoretical novelty is limited: much follows directly from Yang et al., leaving practical merit uncertain.  
- Experiments disappointing: synthetic data favors proposed models and real-data evaluation is weak.  
- Missing comparisons: copula-based alternatives are not compared against, a major omission.  
- Evaluation metrics: results are qualitative and anecdotal; need objective log-probability on test data.  
- Copula baselines used as black-boxes: marginal models for copulas are poorly chosen and unrealistic.  
- Baseline improvements: sensible marginals (e.g., KDE) or translating model choices into copula marginals would be reasonable.  
- Presentation: include all proofs in the supplementary material to support claims.  
- Exposition: section 3.2 needs clearer, slower explanation and the illustrative figure should be in the main text.  
- Writing: paper is generally well written and results appear correct, but some parts (end of 3.1) are overly detailed and add little.  
- Overall: approach is appealing with some theoretical novelty, but practical evaluation is limited so the model's real-world benefit remains unclear.
