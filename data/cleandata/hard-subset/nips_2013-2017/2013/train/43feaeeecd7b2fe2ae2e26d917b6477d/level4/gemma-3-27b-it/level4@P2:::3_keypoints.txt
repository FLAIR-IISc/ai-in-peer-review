- Introduces a novel construction for multivariate count distributions that respect Markov structure in undirected graphical models.  
- Provides approaches that allow count-valued marginals and relatively straightforward estimation algorithms.  
- Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) produces marginally thinner tails than desired.  
- Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) lacks closed-form conditional distributions, reducing benefits of a conditionally specified model.  
- SPGM deviates from the standard Poisson base measure, calling into question its label as a “Poisson” model despite a reasonable rationale.  
- Motivation for modeling counts directly is unclear given Poisson’s imperfect fit to empirical data.  
- Recommends visualizing empirical data against the proposed PMFs (especially SPGM) to clarify fit and complexity.  
- Clarify selection and practical guidance for SPGM parameters R and R0 for practitioners.  
- Theoretical contribution is valuable and extends graphical model frameworks to count data.  
- Literature review is incomplete, understating copula-based approaches for discrete/count multivariate modeling.  
- Discussion of prior work ([11]) lacks nuance; log-linear models and scalable parameterizations are not adequately acknowledged.  
- Significant omission of spatial statistics literature, which has long addressed high-dimensional count data.  
- Methods like sparse precision Gaussian copula deserve explicit acknowledgement despite differences in observable-space Markov properties.  
- Unclear whether model complexity justifies deviation from simpler alternatives (e.g., structured Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement).  
- Empirical results demonstrated on simulated and real data, supporting the approach’s practical performance.  
- Suggest updating citation [20] with a more recent reference to support claims about sequencing read counts replacing microarrays.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address literature coverage, practitioner guidance, and model justification.
