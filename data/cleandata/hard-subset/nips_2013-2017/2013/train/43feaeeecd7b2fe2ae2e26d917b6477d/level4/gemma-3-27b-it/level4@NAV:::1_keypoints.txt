- Introduces novel models for joint distributions of integer-count variables.  
- Identifies limitation of Yang et al.’s model: cannot represent positive correlations while maintaining normalization.  
- Proposes and investigates improvements: a new truncation approach.  
- Proposes and demonstrates necessity of a quadratic base measure statistic.  
- Introduces a sub-linear sufficient statistic for count modeling.  
- Evaluates model effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written, original, and has impactful findings.  
- Reviewer questions the argument against truncation as potentially overstated for naturally bounded count data.  
- Reviewer questions the criticism of the quadratic model’s “Gaussian-esque thin tails” since thin tails may be appropriate in some contexts.  
- Notes unfamiliarity with the term “sparsistent,” but acknowledges its field usage.  
- Minor corrections: change “graphical mode distributions” to “model” (p.3) and clarify “Note that the log…” (p.5) to something like “Note that although the log…”.
