- Introduces novel models for joint distributions over integer-count variables.  
- Identifies limitation of Yang et al. (2013): cannot represent positive correlations while maintaining normalization.  
- Proposes refinements: a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (necessary under some conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.  
- Empirically validates models on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, original, and reports significant, convincing results.  
- Reviewer has reservations about the justification for truncation and quadratic variants.  
- Argues the paper overstates negatives of truncation since many domains have natural upper bounds.  
- Notes quadratic model may be appropriate for thin-tailed, Gaussian-like distributions and its limitations shouldn’t be overemphasized.  
- Suggests a more nuanced discussion acknowledging potential use cases for truncation and quadratic models.  
- Requests brief definition or citation for the term “sparsistent” for reader accessibility.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “graphical mode distributions” to “model” (p.3) and revise “Note that the log...” to “Note that although the log...” (p.5).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution to modeling count data; recommends acceptance.
