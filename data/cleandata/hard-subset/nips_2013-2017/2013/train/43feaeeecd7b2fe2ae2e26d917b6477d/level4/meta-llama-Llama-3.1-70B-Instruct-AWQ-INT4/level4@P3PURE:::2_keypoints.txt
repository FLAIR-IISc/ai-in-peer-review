- Problem is important and authors correctly identify limitations of existing multivariate count models.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves previous truncation approach is invalid and proposes alternatives.  
- TPGM truncation is natural and warranted; QPGM and SPGM are novel by altering base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Much of the theory follows directly from Yang et al., so practical merit of proposed models remains to be shown.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and biased toward the proposed models.  
- Real-data comparisons omit copula-based alternatives and lack objective metrics.  
- Reported results are qualitative/anecdotal; need log-probability on test data for fair joint-distribution evaluation.  
- Copula competitors were used as black boxes with poor marginal choices (non-paranormal); better marginals (e.g., Gaussian KDE) should be tried.  
- Identical performance to Glasso is suspicious and suggests copula baselines were not tuned sensibly.  
- Hyperparameter/margin choices (e.g., R=11) for real data should inform copula marginal selection; at least reasonable baselines are warranted.  
- Paper is generally well written; include all proofs in the supplement.  
- Presentation: end of Sec.3.1 is overly detailed/unnecessary; Sec.3.2 is central and should be expanded with its figure in the main text.  
- Overall: approach is appealing with some novelty, but limited and biased experiments leave its practical value unclear.
