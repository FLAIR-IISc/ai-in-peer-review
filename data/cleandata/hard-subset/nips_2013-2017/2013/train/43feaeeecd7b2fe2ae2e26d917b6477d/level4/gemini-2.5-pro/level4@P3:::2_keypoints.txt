- Problem: standard Poisson graphical models (PGMs) cannot model positive conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints; paper seeks Poisson-like PGMs that allow mixed dependencies.

- Contribution 1: proves the previously proposed Winsorized/truncated Poisson is not a valid Besag joint distribution, correcting the literature.

- Contribution 2: proposes a renormalized Truncated PGM (TPGM) that permits mixed dependencies but requires a pre-specified truncation level R.

- Contribution 3: introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) by modifying the base measure to quadratic, enabling mixed-sign parameters but yielding thin Gaussian-like tails.

- Contribution 4: introduces Sublinear PGM (SPGM) by using a sub-linear sufficient statistic, retaining the Poisson base measure while allowing both positive and negative dependencies.

- Evaluation: experiments on simulations and a microRNA sequencing dataset, but experimental setup is limited and potentially biased.

- Strength: addresses an important, well-motivated problem in modeling multivariate count data.

- Strength: offers original, conceptually principled model designs and a valuable technical correction to prior work.

- Strength: paper is generally clear and well organized.

- Weakness: experimental validation is insufficient to establish practical utility of proposed models.

- Weakness: missing and weak baselines—copula-based alternatives (e.g., Gaussian copula with appropriate count marginals) are not properly compared; GLasso/non-paranormal baseline configuration is suspicious.

- Weakness: evaluation lacks objective metrics (e.g., held-out test log-likelihood); relies on ROC plots and visual inspection.

- Weakness: simulations are biased toward TPGM-like data-generating processes, reducing generality of conclusions.

- Weakness: theoretical novelty is limited; learning framework largely follows Yang et al. (2012, 2013), so main contribution is model specification rather than new theory.

- Suggestion: include a properly constructed Gaussian copula graphical model baseline with appropriate count marginals and report test set log-likelihoods.

- Suggestion: provide full proofs for all theorems in the supplementary material.

- Suggestion: condense the detailed variational upper-bound discussion on QPGM partition function.

- Suggestion: expand Section 3.2 on SPGM and move the sub-linear sufficient-statistic figure into the main paper to improve intuition.

- Summary: novel and appealing model proposals (especially QPGM and SPGM) and a useful correction, but insufficient and biased experiments fail to convincingly demonstrate practical advantages.

- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, corrects prior error, proposes novel and well-motivated models, and is clearly presented.

- Arguments against acceptance: weak experimental evidence, missing key baselines and quantitative metrics, and limited theoretical novelty.
