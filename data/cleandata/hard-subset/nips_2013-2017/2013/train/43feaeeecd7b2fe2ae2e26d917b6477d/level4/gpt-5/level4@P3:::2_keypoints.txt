- Paper addresses Poisson graphical model normalizability that forces nonpositive edges and proves Winsorized node-conditionals cannot yield a valid Besag/Yang joint (Theorem 1).

- Proposes three alternatives: TPGM (truncated support, remapped mass, allows positive edges but bounded domain), QPGM (quadratic base-measure, Gaussian-like tails, requires negative-definite quadratic form), and SPGM (sublinear sufficient statistic, heavier tails, variational bounds for partition functions).

- Models are estimated via l1-penalized neighborhood selection in the GLM framework; evaluated on simulations and TCGA microRNA counts.

- Theory: negative result on Winsorization is useful; normalizability and variational conditions are stated, but several central claims need full proofs and clearer assumptions in the supplement.

- Estimation: no sparsistency or sample-complexity guarantees; limited computational details on SPGM approximations, enforcing QPGM constraints, and convergence diagnostics.

- Experiments: synthetic simulations favor proposed models (possibly by design); real-data analysis is qualitative (network plots) and lacks quantitative held-out evaluation.

- Clarity: manuscript is generally well written but should move the Gaussian variational bound to the supplement, expand Section 3.2 with the B(x;R0,R) figure, unify domain notation, and fix minor copyedits.

- Originality: conceptually novel to modify base measure and sufficient statistics to permit positive dependencies; constructions are simple but well motivated and the Winsorization correction adds value.

- Significance: models could be useful for multivariate counts if supported by stronger empirical evidence and practical estimation procedures; current practical significance is limited by evaluation gaps.

- Major request — baselines: include copula-based competitors with Poisson/NegBin marginals (e.g., Gaussian copula with fitted count marginals) rather than nonparanormal.

- Major request — quantitative evaluation: report held-out test log-likelihood/pseudo-likelihood, node-conditional calibration, parameter recovery error, and ablation studies comparing linear vs sublinear statistics.

- Major request — sensitivity and robustness: study sensitivity to R and R0, justify R=11 in TCGA, provide data-driven selection strategies and stability across subsamples; clarify QPGM negative-definiteness enforcement.

- Major request — computation: detail SPGM infinite-sum approximations and error control, provide runtimes and proximal method convergence diagnostics.

- Major request — theory: include full proofs, state regularity assumptions, and, if possible, sketch sparsistency guarantees adapted from GLM analyses.

- Pros: corrects prior mistake, elegant framework modifications, three concrete model classes, potentially broad applicability.

- Cons: missing strong baselines, biased/limited empirical evaluation, no sparsistency theory or computational treatment, unexplored sensitivity to truncation parameters.

- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger baselines, quantitative held-out assessments, sensitivity analyses, and fuller theoretical and computational supplements.
