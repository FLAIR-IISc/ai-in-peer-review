- Paper introduces new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables, addressing limits of existing Poisson graphical models (PGMs).
- Shows standard PGMs (Yang et al.) can only model negative correlations due to normalizability constraints.
- Proposes three solutions: a truncation approach, a quadratic Poisson graphical model (QPGM) with a quadratic base measure, and a sub-linear Poisson graphical model (SPGM) with a modified sufficient statistic.
- Provides theoretical justification, including necessity of a quadratic base measure for modeling positive correlations.
- Evaluates models via simulations and microRNA sequencing data, showing improved performance over Gaussian graphical models and original PGM estimator.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and presents original, significant contributions to count-data graphical models.
- Experimental results support effectiveness of proposed approaches on synthetic and real datasets.
- Some arguments about limitations of truncation and quadratic models felt underexplored and could use more discussion or justification.
- Truncated model may be applicable to naturally bounded counts; QPGM’s Gaussian-like thin tails might suit some datasets—these use cases need elaboration.
- The term “sparsistent” is used without initial definition, which could confuse non-expert readers.
- Specific editorial fixes: change "graphical mode distributions" to "graphical model" (Page 3) and clarify wording "Note that the log..." to "Note that although the log..." (Page 5).
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing minor clarity and discussion points.
