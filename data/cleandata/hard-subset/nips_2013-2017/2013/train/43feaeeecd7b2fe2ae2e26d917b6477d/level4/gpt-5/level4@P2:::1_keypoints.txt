- Paper introduces three new undirected count graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to allow both positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Shows standard Poisson graphical model (PGM) is limited to non-positive pairwise parameters due to normalizability.
- Proves Winsorized truncation cannot produce a consistent joint distribution under the Besag/Yang construction.
- TPGM: exponential-family form on bounded counts permitting positive/negative edges.
- QPGM: retains linear sufficient statistics with a quadratic base measure, necessary to allow positive dependencies while normalizable.
- SPGM: uses sublinear sufficient statistics to get heavier tails and broad feasibility of pairwise parameters.
- Structure learning via ℓ1-penalized neighborhood selection; variational bounds used for node-wise likelihoods.
- Empirical results on simulations and TCGA miRNA data show improved edge recovery and biologically plausible networks versus Gaussian baselines and PGM.
- Theoretical contributions are solid and clearly stated, including impossibility and necessity results.
- Paper is generally well written, but some terms (e.g., “sparsistent”) and notation should be defined on first use.
- Work is original in proving Winsorization inconsistency and proposing three tractable model families with different tail behaviors.
- Significant contribution for applications requiring modeling of positive/negative dependencies in counts.
- Strength: rigorous diagnosis of PGM shortcomings and principled alternatives with complementary trade-offs.
- Strength: practical estimation approach and sensible empirical validation including biological interpretation.
- Weakness: claim that truncation/QPGM tails are “big negatives” lacks convincing evidence; need guidance on when each model is preferable.
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity (R, R0) is underexplored; recommend sensitivity analyses and selection strategies.
- Weakness: SPGM node-conditional partition function handling is unclear—clarify exact vs. approximate computation and provide runtime/convergence diagnostics.
- Weakness: missing formal sparsistency/structure recovery guarantees; sketching conditions would increase confidence.
- Weakness: suggest additional baselines (e.g., copula-based multivariate Poisson), calibration checks (PIT), and reporting of computational costs/scalability.
- Weakness: case study preprocessing (miRNA counts, choice R=11) needs clarification on normalization, filtering, rescaling, or capping.
- Minor editorial: define “sparsistent,” fix typos (“graphical mode”→“model”), add intuition after Theorem 1, and standardize notation (e.g., N0, W/X).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a known PGM limitation with principled, original, and practically useful alternatives likely to impact applications.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient exploration of when proposed tails are problematic in practice, missing theoretical recovery guarantees, and scarce implementation/scalability details.
- Overall verdict: high-quality, original, and relevant work that would be strong after modest clarifications and additional empirical/theoretical support.
