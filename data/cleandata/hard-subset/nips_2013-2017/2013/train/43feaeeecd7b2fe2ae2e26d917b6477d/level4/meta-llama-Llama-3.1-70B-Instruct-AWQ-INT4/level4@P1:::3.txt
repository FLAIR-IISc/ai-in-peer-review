The paper provides a construction for multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that obey the Markov structure of an undirected network. Building a multivariate distribution over unbounded counts is in general a hard problem, as studied at length by Besag and others for the past 30 years. 

The authors' proposal succeeds in some relevant ways. The upsides are constructions that do allow for marginal distributions over counts and which lead to relatively simple estimation algorithms. The downsides are that the Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) has marginally thinner tails and the Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) does not have closed-form conditional distributions. 

One wonders what the main motivation for modeling counts is, since the Poisson itself is not a good distribution to fit empirical data. It would be very useful to have a plot of the probability mass function of the SPGM, which feels somewhat convoluted at first sight. The authors should clarify how the constants R and R0 are chosen and provide advice to practitioners on their selection.

To construct a multivariate distribution over counts obeying the independence model of MRF is hard. The results in this paper are of theoretical interest to NIPS. However, the literature review feels incomplete, and the authors seem to overlook some relevant works. For instance, the discussion of copula models and the spatial statistics literature is lacking. 

The authors should address these omissions and provide a more thorough comparison of their approach with existing methods. They should also clarify why their model is preferable to simpler alternatives, such as a structured Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement model. 

Finally, the authors should provide a more recent reference to support their claim that counts of sequencing reads are replacing microarrays in gene expression analysis.
