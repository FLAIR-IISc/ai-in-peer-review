- Paper proposes new models for joint distributions over integer-count variables to overcome Yang et al.’s limitation of modeling only negative correlations.
- Proposed modifications include truncation, a quadratic base-measure statistic (with formal necessity proof under certain conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.
- Experimental results are reported on both simulated and real datasets.
- Paper is well-written, original, and presents significant results.
- Reviewer finds the authors’ dismissal of truncation and quadratic model limitations unconvincing.
- Truncation may be acceptable in domains where counts rarely reach high values.
- Quadratic model’s Gaussian-like thin tails could be appropriate in some applications.
- Reviewer is unfamiliar with the term “sparsistent,” though notes it appears in the literature.
- Typo: “graphical mode distributions” should be “graphical model distributions.”
- Typo: “Note that the log…” should be “Note that although the log…”.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of approaches, theoretical soundness with proofs, potential significance for applications (e.g., genomics, spatial statistics), experimental validation, and overall clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: practical limitations (truncation constraints, thin tails) may affect real-world performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: discussion of limitations is not sufficiently compelling.
- Arguments against acceptance: models are complex and may demand significant computational resources.
