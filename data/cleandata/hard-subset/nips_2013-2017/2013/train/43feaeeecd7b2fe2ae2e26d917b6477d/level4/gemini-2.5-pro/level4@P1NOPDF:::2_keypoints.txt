- Problem importance acknowledged; authors correctly identify limits of current multivariate count models (one reservation noted).
- Valuable theoretical contribution: proof that a previously suggested truncation method for Poisson graphical models is invalid.
- Proposed alternatives (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) are well-motivated and novel in modifying base measure/sufficient statistics.
- Theoretical development leans heavily on Yang et al.’s framework, so main novelty is proposing new models rather than new theory.
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and does not convincingly demonstrate practical benefits.
- Synthetic experiments appear tailored to favor the proposed models.
- Real-data experiments omit comparisons to modern copula-based alternatives.
- Results are reported anecdotal/qualitative; objective evaluation (e.g., held-out log-probability) is needed for generative model comparison.
- Copula baselines are treated as black boxes; non-paranormal transformation is a poor choice for count marginals.
- Reported parity with Glasso is suspicious and suggests suboptimal baselines; a Gaussian copula with KDE-fitted marginals would likely be more competitive.
- Discretizing real data (R=11) could have justified discrete marginals for copula baselines but was not leveraged.
- Overall, better-configured and more thorough baseline comparisons are required to justify the new models.
- Paper is generally well-written, but full proofs of theoretical claims should be included in the supplement.
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and presents an unhelpful bound; Section 3.2 (core method) feels rushed and should be expanded with its key figure moved into the main paper.
- Summary: approach is appealing with some theoretical novelty, but limited/weak experiments leave the practical utility of the proposed models unclear.
