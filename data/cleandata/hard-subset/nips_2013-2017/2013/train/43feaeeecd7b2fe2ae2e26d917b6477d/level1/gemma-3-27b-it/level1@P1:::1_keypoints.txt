- Addresses modeling multivariate count data and limits of Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) in capturing negative conditional dependencies.  
- Shows a previous truncation method (Kaiser and Cressie) is invalid and introduces a Truncated PGM (TPGM).  
- Proposes two further variants—Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM)—by modifying the base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including proofs about truncation and base measure requirements (Theorems 1 and 3).  
- Demonstrates empirical performance on simulated data and real microRNA sequencing data.  
- Novel contributions: TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM expand the toolkit for count-valued graphical models.  
- Theoretical soundness is a major strength, with careful derivations and literature connections.  
- Experimental results are convincing but limited in scope (more network structures and dataset sizes needed).  
- Explanation of the intuition behind SPGM’s modified sufficient statistic is lacking and could be clearer.  
- Appendix visualizations help but the main text should better explain SPGM intuition.  
- Computational challenges of SPGM—especially the intractable log-partition function and reliance on Poisson approximation—are under-discussed.  
- Stability selection is used, but sensitivity analysis for regularization parameters is insufficient.  
- The discussion of QPGM’s relation to Gaussian Graphical Models (GGMs) is superficial and could benefit from deeper/asymptotic analysis.  
- Lacks quantitative comparisons such as likelihoods or information criteria against original PGM and alternatives.  
- Paper length constrains deeper exploration of SPGM implications and applications beyond the provided cancer example.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, and theoretically solid work meriting acceptance with minor revisions to address computational, experimental, and exposition concerns.
