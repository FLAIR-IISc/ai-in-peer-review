- Addresses limitation of existing Poisson graphical models by enabling positive and negative conditional dependencies for count data.  
- Proposes three new models: Truncated Poisson Graphical Models (TPGM), Quadratic Poisson Graphical Models (QPGM), and Sublinear Poisson Graphical Models (SPGM).  
- Theoretically derives normalizability conditions that relax restrictions of traditional PGMs.  
- Introduces sublinear sufficient statistics in SPGM as a notable theoretical contribution.  
- Demonstrates applicability to real-world count data (e.g., genomic sequencing, spatial incidence).  
- Case study on breast cancer microRNA networks identifies biologically meaningful hubs (mir-375, mir-10b).  
- Empirical evaluation (simulations and ROC analyses) shows TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian and traditional PGMs.  
- Provides clear visualizations (adjacency matrices, inferred networks) to compare methods.  
- Presentation could be clearer—needs more accessible explanations and an explicit transition from theory to implementation (e.g., neighborhood selection).  
- Scalability and computational complexity for high-dimensional data are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Limited benchmarking against other modern multivariate count methods (e.g., copula-based or hierarchical models).  
- Sensitivity analysis for truncation and related parameters (R, R0) is lacking.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical/practical contribution, novelty, and demonstrated empirical advantages.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity/accessibility issues and insufficient scalability/benchmarking analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing clarity and scalability concerns in the final version.
