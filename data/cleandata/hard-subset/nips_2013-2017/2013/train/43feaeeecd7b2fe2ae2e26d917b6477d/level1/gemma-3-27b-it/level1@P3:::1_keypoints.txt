- Addresses an important problem: extending graphical models to multivariate count data, overcoming Poisson graphical models' inability to model negative correlations.  
- Proposes three novel models: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).  
- Proves impossibility of simple truncation (Winsorization) and shows the need for at least quadratic base measures to allow both positive and negative correlations.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis and clear motivation for the proposed distributional modifications.  
- Explores multiple sufficient-statistic modifications in SPGM, adding valuable model variants.  
- Empirically validated on simulations and a real miRNA dataset, comparing against Gaussian GGM, nonparanormal, and original PGM.  
- Comparison benchmarks are useful and show the proposed methods outperform existing alternatives.  
- Justification for the specific sublinear sufficient statistic in SPGM is insufficient and appears somewhat arbitrary.  
- The relationship between the variational upper bound used in QPGM learning and actual model performance is not fully explained.  
- Numerical experiments lack rigorous statistical testing (e.g., p-values) and sensitivity analyses for regularization and truncation levels (R, R0).  
- Choice of R0 in SPGM is mentioned without justification; effects of varying R0 are not studied.  
- Computational complexity and scalability (especially for high-dimensional data like single-cell RNA-seq) are not discussed despite using proximal gradient ascent.  
- Limited discussion of alternative count-data modeling approaches (e.g., generalized additive models, copula-based methods) and their relationship to the proposed work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, empirical validation, and practical significance across domains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient model-specification detail, missing scalability analysis, and incomplete empirical/statistical comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated contribution recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing model justification, scalability, and experimental rigor (reviewer rates as strong accept).
