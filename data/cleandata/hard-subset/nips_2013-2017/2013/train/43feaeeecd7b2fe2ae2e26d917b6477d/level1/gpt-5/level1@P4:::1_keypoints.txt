- Paper identifies the normalization limitation of classical Poisson MRFs (only negative conditional dependencies) and analyzes truncation fixes.
- Theorem 1: Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals cannot yield a consistent joint MRF (negative result for a common folk-construction).
- Introduces TPGM: an exponential-family truncation that yields a valid finite-state MRF without sign constraints on edges (Theorem 2), with practical limitations as truncation R grows.
- Theorem 3: With linear sufficient statistic B(x)=x, allowing both sign interactions requires a base measure that grows at least quadratically (Ω(Z^2)).
- Proposes QPGM: adds quadratic node terms to permit positive/negative interactions under a negative-definiteness-type condition (Proposition 2); has Gaussian-like tails.
- Derives a Gaussian variational upper bound on node-conditional log-partitions to enable tractable QPGM estimation.
- Proposes SPGM: uses a saturating sublinear sufficient statistic (linear up to R0, then plateaus) to retain Poisson base measure and ensure normalizability for all pairwise parameters (Theorem 4).
- Provides a Poisson-based variational upper bound for SPGM when canonical parameters are nonnegative (Proposition 3).
- Learning approach: l1-penalized neighborhood selection applied to all proposed models (node-wise regression).
- Simulations: TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian/nonparanormal baselines on ROC recovery; TPGM best on TPGM-generated data.
- Real-data case study (TCGA miRNA-seq): Poisson-like models yield biologically plausible hubs, but no gold-standard network for quantitative validation.
- Theory strengths: clear negative result on Winsorization, novel necessity result for quadratic base, and explicit normalizability conditions; some proofs/assumptions could be clearer in main text.
- Algorithmic weaknesses: variational bounds are sketched but lack detail on tightness, gradient bias, convergence, and handling of negative θ in SPGM.
- Missing theoretical guarantees: no sparsistency or finite-sample results for structure learning with new models.
- Practical gaps: limited guidance on selecting truncation/saturation parameters (R, R0), no sensitivity analyses, and no runtime/scalability discussion.
- Comparison gaps: omitted some alternative count approaches (e.g., copula-based counts, negative binomial variants) and no discussion of enforcing global symmetry/coherence from node-wise fits.
- Suggested improvements: add proof sketches for key theorems, detail optimization/variational implementation, address θ<0 handling in SPGM, provide parameter-selection guidance and sensitivity analyses, discuss symmetry enforcement and runtime/comparisons.
