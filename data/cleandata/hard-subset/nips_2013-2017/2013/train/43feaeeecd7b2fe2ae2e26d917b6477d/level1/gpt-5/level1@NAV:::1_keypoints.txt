- Corrects literature: shows Winsorized (hard-truncated) Poisson node-conditionals do not admit a valid joint MRF.  
- Proposes Truncated PGM (TPGM) using exponential-family truncation that permits both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Proves necessity of at least quadratic base measure for any normalizable joint model with linear sufficient statistics and mixed-sign interactions, leading to Quadratic PGM (QPGM).  
- Introduces Sublinear PGM (SPGM) that uses a sublinear transformation of the sufficient statistic to allow heavy tails and arbitrary pairwise parameters.  
- Implements ℓ1-penalized neighborhood selection with variational bounds for practical estimation.  
- Empirical results show improved ROC performance over Gaussian, nonparanormal, and SKEPTIC baselines and biologically plausible hubs in a TCGA microRNA network.  
- Theoretical contributions are substantive: clear impossibility result for Winsorization and precise normalizability condition C(z)=Ω(z^2).  
- New model families (TPGM/QPGM/SPGM) are clearly defined with precise normalizability conditions.  
- Estimation approach is practical, but finite-sample/statistical guarantees are not provided.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and readable with helpful contrasts among model classes.  
- Requests clarification on computational complexity and convergence for neighborhood selection under SPGM (partition function approximation).  
- Asks for guidance or heuristics for choosing truncation/tuning parameters R and R0.  
- Originality is high, with novel negative result on Winsorization and novel SPGM construction.  
- Significance is strong given prevalence of high-dimensional count data and need for models allowing positive dependencies.  
- Pros: clear theoretical corrections, three practical model classes, and empirical gains with interpretable biological findings.  
- Cons: lacks sparsistency/consistency theory for QPGM/SPGM, sensitivity to truncation/tuning not systematically studied, and omitted comparisons to Poisson log-normal or negative-binomial graphical models.  
- Recommendation: Accept (weak) with suggested additions of experiments vs. Poisson log-normal/NB models and heuristics for choosing R and R0.
