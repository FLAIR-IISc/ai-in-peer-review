- Addresses modeling multivariate count data and a key limitation of Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs): inability to capture negative dependencies.  
- Proposes three novel models to overcome this: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).  
- Provides rigorous theoretical justification for each proposed model with clear theorems and proofs.  
- Clearly written and well-organized presentation, with logical progression of ideas and derivations.  
- Demonstrates empirical performance on simulations and real microRNA sequencing data, showing improvements over existing methods.  
- Novel contribution: offers new tools for modeling count data with both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Practical relevance: application to microRNA sequencing shows potential real-world utility.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., QPGM’s thin tail, SPGM computational challenges) and discuss them.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational cost and scalability, especially for QPGM and SPGM in high dimensions.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; needs broader comparisons and more extensive parameter tuning for robustness.  
- SPGM parameter choices (R0 and R) are not fully justified; sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Reliance on a variational upper bound for SPGM raises concerns about approximation accuracy and lacks error analysis.  
- Could better situate work within related literature (e.g., copula-based graphical models and other non-Gaussian approaches).  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, presents novel and theoretically sound models, and shows encouraging empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about computational complexity, limited empirical validation, and insufficient justification for some model specifications.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance.
