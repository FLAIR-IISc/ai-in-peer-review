**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper "On Poisson Graphical Models" proposes novel extensions to Poisson graphical models (PGMs) to capture rich dependence structures between count-valued variables. The authors first discuss the limitations of existing PGMs, which can only model negative conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints. They then introduce three new approaches: Truncated Poisson Graphical Models (TPGMs), Quadratic Poisson Graphical Models (QPGMs), and Sublinear Poisson Graphical Models (SPGMs). These models aim to overcome the limitations of traditional PGMs by modifying the Poisson distribution's sufficient statistic and base measure.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on PGMs, including the construction of PGMs as the joint distribution that corresponds to Poisson distributed node-conditional distributions [17, 18, 19]. The authors also discuss related work on modeling count data, such as log-linear models [11], indirect copula transforms [12], and multivariate Poisson distributions [13, 14, 15]. The paper's focus on modifying the Poisson distribution's sufficient statistic and base measure is a novel contribution to the field.

**Strengths**

1. The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the limitations of existing PGMs and the need for new approaches.
2. The authors propose three novel extensions to PGMs, each with its own strengths and weaknesses.
3. The paper includes a thorough analysis of the normalizability conditions for each model, which is essential for ensuring the validity of the proposed distributions.
4. The authors demonstrate the performance of their models using simulation studies and a case study on breast cancer microRNA networks.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes a strong background in graphical models and exponential families, which may make it challenging for non-experts to follow.
2. The authors could have provided more discussion on the interpretability of the proposed models, particularly in the context of real-world applications.
3. Some of the mathematical derivations, such as the proof of Theorem 3, are not fully presented in the paper, which may make it difficult for readers to verify the results.
4. The paper could have benefited from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, such as Gaussian graphical models, to highlight the advantages of the proposed approaches.
