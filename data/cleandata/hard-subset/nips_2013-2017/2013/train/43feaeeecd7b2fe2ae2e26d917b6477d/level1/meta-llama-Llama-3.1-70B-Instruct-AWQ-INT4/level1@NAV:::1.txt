**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a new class of graphical models, called Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs), designed to model count-valued data. The authors propose three novel approaches to extend the traditional Poisson distribution to capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies between variables. The first approach, called Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), truncates the Poisson distribution and shows that it can model both positive and negative dependencies. However, this approach has limitations, such as a bounded domain and problems with large count values. The second approach, called Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), modifies the base measure of the Poisson distribution to allow for positive and negative edge-parameters. The third approach, called Sub-Linear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM), uses a sub-linear sufficient statistic to control the linear Poisson sufficient statistics.
