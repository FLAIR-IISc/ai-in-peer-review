This paper proposes novel extensions to the Poisson graphical model (PGM) to capture rich dependence structures between count-valued variables. The authors' objective is to address the major drawback of the PGM, which only models negative conditional dependencies due to the normalizability constraint. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the proposed solutions.

The authors first consider two strategies for truncating the Poisson distribution and show that only one of these leads to a valid joint distribution. They then propose two novel variants of the Poisson distribution and their corresponding joint graphical model distributions. The three novel approaches provide classes of Poisson-like graphical models that can capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies between count-valued variables.

The strengths of the paper are:

1. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the proposed solutions.
2. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a logical flow of ideas.
3. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed models, including their normalizability and the conditions under which they can capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies.
4. The authors provide numerical experiments to demonstrate the performance of their proposed models.

The weaknesses of the paper are:
