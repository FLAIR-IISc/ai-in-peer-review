- Identifies a fundamental limitation of existing Poisson graphical models (PGMs): normalizability forces non-positive edge weights, preventing positive conditional dependencies.
- Proposes three novel models to overcome this: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).
- Rigorously proves normalizability for the proposed models, allowing both positive and negative dependencies.
- Shows the Winsorized Poisson approach (Kaiser and Cressie) is invalid (Theorem 1) and provides a valid truncation alternative (Theorem 2).
- Carefully analyzes two truncation strategies and their implications for model validity.
- Develops QPGM by modifying the base measure to be quadratic and SPGM by using sub-linear sufficient statistics.
- Theoretical development is thorough, with clear propositions, theorems, and references to related work.
- Experimental evaluation demonstrates the proposed models outperform existing approaches on simulated networks (hub and scale-free) and a breast cancer microRNA-sequencing case study.
- Case study highlights biological relevance: new models identify meaningful hubs (e.g., mir-375, mir-10b) missed by Gaussian graphical models and original PGM.
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and accessible to readers familiar with graphical models.
- Main limitations: limited experimental scope (only two simulation scenarios and one case study).
- Computational efficiency and learning complexity of the proposed methods are not thoroughly explored.
- The choice and motivation for SPGM's specific sub-linear sufficient statistics could be better justified.
- Highly original contribution: first Poisson-like graphical models that can capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies and refutation of prior Winsorized approach.
- Overall: technically sound, significant potential impact for count-data applications, and recommended for acceptance despite limited experimental breadth.
