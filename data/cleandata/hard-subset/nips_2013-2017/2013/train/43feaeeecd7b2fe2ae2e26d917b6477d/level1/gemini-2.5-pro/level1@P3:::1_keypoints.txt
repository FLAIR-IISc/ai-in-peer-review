- Standard Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) can only model negative conditional dependencies, limiting their applicability to multivariate count data.
- The paper proposes three "Poisson-like" graphical models that allow both positive and negative dependencies: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).
- TPGM: valid truncation scheme yielding exponential-family conditionals and a proper joint MRF, allowing arbitrary edge weights but requiring a pre-specified count upper bound R.
- QPGM: adds a quadratic term (-c X_s^2) to the log-probability, showing the base measure must grow at least quadratically to permit positive edges with linear sufficient statistics.
- SPGM: uses a sub-linear sufficient statistic B(x) that is linear for small counts and constant for large counts, retaining the Poisson base measure while allowing arbitrary edge weights and heavier tails.
- Structure learning is performed via l1-penalized neighborhood selection using proximal gradient ascent on node-wise regressions.
- Empirical results on simulations and miRNA sequencing data show substantial improvements over GGMs and standard PGMs, uncovering biologically relevant structures.
- Strength: tackles an important open problem and offers three principled, well-motivated solutions expanding tools for high-dimensional count data.
- Strength: strong technical contributions, including Theorem 1 (refuting Winsorized Poisson MRF) and Theorem 3 (linking base-measure growth to permissible interactions).
- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition that systematically critiques prior work and develops each model logically.
- Strength: convincing empirical validation demonstrating superiority of proposed methods on synthetic and real data.
- Weakness: TPGM and SPGM depend on hyperparameters (R, R0) chosen heuristically from observed maxima without principled selection or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: QPGM is not evaluated empirically, leaving its practical performance unclear despite strong theoretical motivation.
- Weakness: sparsistency/theoretical guarantees for neighborhood selection are left to future work; a high-level argument for extension of existing guarantees would help.
- Weakness: the piecewise form of the SPGM sublinear statistic B(x) seems somewhat ad hoc; justification for this specific choice over alternatives is limited.
- Detailed: Theorem 1 corrects prior literature on Winsorized Poisson conditionals and highlights MRF construction subtleties.
- Detailed: the insight connecting tail behavior of the base measure to allowed parameter space is broadly useful for graphical model construction.
- Detailed: fitting uses proximal gradient and a variational upper bound for the SPGM conditional log-partition function; analysis of bound tightness would be beneficial.
