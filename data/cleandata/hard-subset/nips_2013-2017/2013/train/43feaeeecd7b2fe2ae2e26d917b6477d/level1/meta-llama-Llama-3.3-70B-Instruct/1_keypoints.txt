- Proposes three novel Poisson-based graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) that capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies for count data.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, thorough introduction and motivation.  
- Significant technical contributions with detailed analysis of model properties (normalizability, scalability, interpretability).  
- Experimental results on simulated and real-world data demonstrate effective network structure recovery.  
- Strength: directly addresses a major limitation of existing Poisson graphical models.  
- Strength: thorough comparative analysis of each proposed model’s advantages.  
- Strength: empirical validation supports practical utility.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with graphical models and Poisson distributions, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: many technical proofs and derivations are placed in the appendix, reducing immediate accessibility of technical contributions.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with other count-data methods (e.g., log-linear models, copula approaches).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add more background/explanations for non-experts, expand technical proofs/derivations, and include more detailed comparisons with existing methods.
