- Paper proposes novel modifications to Poisson graphical models to allow both positive and negative conditional dependencies, addressing the non-positive edge-weight limitation.
- Introduces Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM): truncates Poisson at R to ensure a valid joint distribution and capture both dependency signs.
- TPGM drawbacks: bounded variable domain and parameters become increasingly negative or near zero as truncation R grows.
- Introduces Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM): uses a quadratic base measure to allow both dependency signs while ensuring normalizability.
- QPGM drawback: Gaussian-like thin tails that may be undesirable in some applications.
- Introduces Sub-Linear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM): uses sub-linear sufficient statistics to permit both dependency signs and broader feasible pairwise parameters.
- SPGM drawback: heavier tails than QPGM and a more complex form that may complicate use.
- Numerical experiments show TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian and original Poisson graphical models in recovering true networks from multivariate count data.
- Case study on breast cancer microRNA networks demonstrates the models learn biologically meaningful networks.
- Strengths: addresses a significant limitation, provides thorough theoretical analysis, and supports claims with experiments and a real-data case study.
- Weaknesses: TPGM domain and parameter issues, QPGM tail behavior, and SPGM increased complexity.
- Originality and significance: novel theoretical contributions with potential applications in genomics, neuroimaging, and spatial statistics.
- Clarity and quality: well-written, accessible, technically sound, and reproducible presentation.
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance despite noted weaknesses.
