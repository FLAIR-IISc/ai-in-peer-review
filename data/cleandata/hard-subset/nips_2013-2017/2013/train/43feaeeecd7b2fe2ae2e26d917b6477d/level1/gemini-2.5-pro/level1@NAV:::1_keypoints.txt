- Addresses critical limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs): inability to model positive conditional dependencies.
- Proves the previously proposed "Winsorized" Poisson approach is invalid for constructing a joint distribution.
- Introduces three valid graphical models for counts that allow positive and negative dependencies: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).
- Demonstrates model performance via structure recovery on simulated networks and a real microRNA sequencing dataset.
- Significance: substantially increases practical applicability of PGMs for real-world count data in domains like genomics and social sciences.
- Originality and quality: contributions are novel, technically sound, and the disproof of Winsorized PGM corrects the literature.
- The authors analyze trade-offs among models (e.g., QPGM’s thin tails vs SPGM’s heavier tails).
- Empirical results convincingly show superiority over Gaussian-based approaches and the original PGM.
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and explains deficiencies before presenting solutions.
- Weakness: TPGM and SPGM introduce hyperparameters (R and R0) with insufficient guidance and sensitivity analysis for selection.
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees for the proposed neighborhood selection learning procedure (e.g., sparsistency) are deferred to future work.
- Argument for acceptance: clear, significant solution with novel, theory-backed models and strong empirical validation.
- Counterpoint against acceptance: brief hyperparameter discussion and missing theoretical learning guarantees.
- Overall: strengths far outweigh weaknesses; a high-quality paper making a substantial contribution to count-data graphical models.
