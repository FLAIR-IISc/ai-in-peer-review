- Identifies a major limitation of standard Poisson Graphical Models: inability to model positive conditional dependencies.  
- Proposes three novel variants to address this: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM).  
- Argues convincingly that Poisson node-conditionals impose competitive relationships due to normalizability constraints.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical justification, showing quadratic base measures are required to allow both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Demonstrates that the truncated Poisson approach fails to yield a valid joint distribution.  
- Offers breadth by exploring truncation, quadratic, and sublinear modifications to the Poisson model.  
- Includes simulation studies and a microRNA sequencing case study to demonstrate empirical performance.  
- Compares proposed models to Gaussian graphical models and existing PGM estimators, strengthening empirical claims.  
- SPGM’s link between its sublinear statistic and resulting graphical properties is not explained as intuitively as QPGM.  
- Numerical experiments rely on ROC curves and lack detailed statistical comparisons or significance testing.  
- Computational complexity and practical cost of estimating the models, especially SPGM, are not discussed.  
- Sparsistency analysis is deferred to future work; discussion of expected challenges and related theory would be helpful.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing the above clarity, experiments, and complexity concerns.
