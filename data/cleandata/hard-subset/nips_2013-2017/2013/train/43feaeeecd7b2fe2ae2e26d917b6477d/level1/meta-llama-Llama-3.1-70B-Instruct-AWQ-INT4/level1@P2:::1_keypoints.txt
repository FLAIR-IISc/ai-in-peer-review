- Proposes novel extensions to the Poisson graphical model to capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies among counts.
- Analyzes two truncation strategies for the Poisson and shows only one yields a valid joint distribution.
- Introduces two new Poisson variants and their joint graphical models, providing three Poisson-like models overall.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and logical flow.
- Provides thorough analysis of model normalizability and conditions for capturing dependency signs.
- Includes numerical experiments demonstrating the proposed models' performance.
- Assumes substantial background in graphical models and exponential families, which may limit accessibility.
- Lacks more intuitive explanations of the proposed models and their relation to existing work.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to simulations and a single real-world dataset; broader evaluations are needed.
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous analysis.
- Clarity: clear writing but may be hard for non-experts.
- Originality: presents significant, novel extensions to PGM.
- Significance: addresses a major limitation of PGM and generalizes count modeling.
- Recommended improvements: add intuitive explanations and expand empirical comparisons and datasets.
- Acceptance arguments: novel contribution, rigorous analysis, and experimental support.
- Rejection arguments: high background assumptions, weak intuition/interpretability, and limited empirical validation.
- Overall recommendation: accept with suggestions to improve intuition and broaden evaluations.
