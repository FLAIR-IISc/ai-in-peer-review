- Proposes three extensions to Poisson graphical models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to capture positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Shows only one truncation strategy yields a valid joint distribution, naming it the Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM).
- Introduces Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) with a quadratic base measure.
- Introduces Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) using a sublinear sufficient statistic.
- Provides derivations and proofs of normalizability for QPGM and SPGM.
- Empirically evaluates methods on simulated networks and a breast cancer microRNA dataset, outperforming Gaussian graphical models.
- Clearly explains limitations of traditional PGM and motivates the need for extensions.
- Each proposed method has distinct strengths and weaknesses discussed by the authors.
- Paper includes detailed mathematical derivations that aid technical understanding.
- Demonstrates practical relevance by applying methods to a real-world genomics dataset.
- Assumes high background knowledge in graphical models and Poisson theory, limiting accessibility to non-experts.
- Lacks a detailed comparison of computational complexity across the proposed methods.
- Does not discuss interpretability of the estimated networks, an important practical concern.
- QPGM and SPGM are novel contributions not previously proposed in the literature.
- Addressing PGM’s limitation is significant and relevant to fields like genomics, neuroscience, and social network analysis.
- Emphasizes the importance of modeling data distribution when estimating graphical models.
- Paper is well-written with logical structure, clear notation, and helpful figures/tables.
- Technically sound work with thorough proofs and empirical validation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel methods, theoretical contributions, and real-data demonstration.
- Arguments against acceptance: steep technical barrier for readers, missing computational/interpretability analyses.
