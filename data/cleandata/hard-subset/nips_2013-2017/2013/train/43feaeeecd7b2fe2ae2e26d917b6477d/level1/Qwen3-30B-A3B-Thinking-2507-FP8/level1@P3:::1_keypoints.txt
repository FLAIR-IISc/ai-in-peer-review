- Paper addresses Poisson graphical models' inability to model positive conditional dependencies and builds on Besag (1974) and Yang et al. (2012).
- Main contributions: (1) proves Winsorized Poisson approach is invalid for graphical models (Theorem 1), (2) proposes a valid truncated Poisson graphical model (TPGM), (3) introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) that allow both positive and negative dependencies while ensuring normalizability.
- Theoretical strengths: rigorous mathematical proofs for key claims and normalizability conditions (Theorems 3–4).
- Conceptual significance: fills a critical gap limiting PGMs' applicability to count data with mixed dependencies.
- Experimental strengths: thorough simulations showing advantages over Gaussian approaches, especially in high dimensions.
- Real-world validation: breast cancer miRNA network case study identifies biologically meaningful interactions missed by original PGM.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational complexity and scalability of proposed methods vs. Gaussian/PGM approaches.
- Weakness: SPGM section lacks practical implementation details and concrete comparisons of tail behavior with QPGM and Poisson.
- Weakness: comparisons to other count-data models (e.g., negative binomial graphical models, copula methods) are limited.
- Overall assessment: novel, technically sound, well-written work that advances graphical models for count data with convincing empirical validation.
- Recommendation: accept — meets criteria for technical soundness, clarity, originality, and significance.
- Pro: resolves a long-standing PGM limitation, offers three novel theoretically sound approaches, and provides rigorous proofs plus strong experiments.
- Con: needs expanded computational/scalability discussion, clearer SPGM intuition/implementation, and broader comparisons to other count-data models.
