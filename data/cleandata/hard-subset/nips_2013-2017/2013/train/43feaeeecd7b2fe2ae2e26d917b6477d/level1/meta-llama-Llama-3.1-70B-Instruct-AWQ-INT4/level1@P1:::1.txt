**Summary**

This paper proposes three novel approaches to extend the Poisson graphical model (PGM) to capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies between count-valued variables. The authors first discuss the limitations of the traditional PGM, which can only model negative dependencies due to the normalizability constraint. They then investigate two strategies for truncating the Poisson distribution and show that only one of these leads to a valid joint distribution, which they call the Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM). However, the TPGM still has limitations, and the authors propose two more fundamental modifications to the Poisson distribution: the Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) and the Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM). The QPGM uses a quadratic base measure, while the SPGM uses a sublinear sufficient statistic. The authors demonstrate the performance of their methods on simulated networks and a real-world dataset of breast cancer microRNA expression.
