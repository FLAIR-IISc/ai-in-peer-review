- Tackles limitation of Poisson graphical models that force nonpositive pairwise parameters, preventing positive conditional dependencies.  
- Theorem 1: formally refutes Winsorized (point-mass-at-R) Poisson node-conditionals as compatible with any joint MRF.  
- Proposes Truncated PGM (TPGM) using Poisson conditioned on {0,…,R} that permits both positive and negative edges (Theorem 2).  
- Theorem 3: proves base measure must scale at least quadratically (C(z)=Ω(z^2)) for Poisson-like EF-MRFs with linear sufficient statistics to allow positive edges while remaining normalizable.  
- Develops Quadratic PGM (QPGM) with linear sufficient statistics and quadratic base measure, normalizable under a negative-definiteness condition (Proposition 2), enabling both sign dependencies.  
- Introduces Sublinear PGM (SPGM) with bounded sublinear sufficient statistics B(·;R0,R), preserving Poisson base measure and achieving normalizability for all real pairwise parameters (Theorem 4).  
- Outlines nodewise ℓ1-penalized conditional likelihood estimation using proximal gradient and provides variational upper bounds (Gaussian for QPGM, Poisson for SPGM when θ≥0).  
- Empirical results: simulated data and TCGA miRNA network show TPGM/SPGM outperform Gaussian-based baselines and the original PGM in ROC performance.  
- Request for full proofs or constructive counterexample for Theorem 1, and clarification of regularity assumptions on E(·), since the claim hinges on R>3.  
- Theorem 3 needs a more precise, self-contained statement about the set of θ where A(θ)<∞ and whether sufficiency holds when C(z)≈z^2.  
- QPGM normalizability condition XTΘX ≤ −c||X||^2 requires clarification whether Θ includes diagonal and off-diagonal entries and whether negative-definiteness suffices versus elementwise negativity.  
- Practical concern: Θ≺0/negative-definiteness may be restrictive in high-degree graphs; need examples where positive edges are feasible under this constraint.  
- Computational issue: node-conditional log-partitions are infinite sums; need specifics on how they are computed/approximated, impact of variational bounds on bias and consistency, and complexity.  
- For SPGM, the provided Poisson variational upper bound applies only when θ≥0—paper must explain handling of general θ (positive and negative).  
- Empirical comparisons omit strong count-data baselines (Poisson log-normal, negative binomial); suggest adding these even with approximate inference.  
- Missing experiments: recovery when data are generated from SPGM, sensitivity analyses for R and R0, calibration for edge sign recovery and parameter error, and reporting computational cost.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation (W vs Wp), unclear definition of Θ (diagonal vs off-diagonal), and need for intuition/plot for B(·;R0,R).  
- Biological data concern: reported R=11 for miRNA counts seems inconsistent with typical RNA-seq counts—clarify preprocessing (normalization, filtering, binning) and justification for R,R0 choices.  
- Originality: novel combination of refuting Winsorized conditionals, valid truncated EF construction, necessary quadratic-growth insight, and two new models (QPGM, SPGM); SPGM construction is especially creative.  
- Significance: enabling positive/negative conditional dependencies in count MRFs is important for genomics and spatial data; theoretical insights can guide future EF-MRF design.  
- Pros: strong theoretical contributions (Theorems 1–4), three tractable model classes enabling positive dependencies, and improved graph recovery with biologically plausible results.  
- Cons: some theoretical claims lack full proofs/conditions, computational details and log-partition evaluation are under-specified, experiments miss key baselines and sensitivity analyses, and QPGM practical constraints may limit applicability.  
- Suggested improvements: include full proofs/sketches for key theorems, detail optimization and log-partition approximations with error control, add comparisons to Poisson log-normal and NB models, include SPGM-generated data and sensitivity studies, provide guidance for selecting R and R0, and discuss identifiability and convexity conditions for nodewise problems.  
- Recommendation: strong and original work with a favorable lean toward acceptance contingent on addressing proofs, computational details, and expanded empirical validation.
