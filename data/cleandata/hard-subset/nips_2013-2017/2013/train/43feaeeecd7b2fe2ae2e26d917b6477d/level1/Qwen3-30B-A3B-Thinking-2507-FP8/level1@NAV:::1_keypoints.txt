- Identifies a core limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs): they can only model negative conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints.  
- Positions the work within prior literature (Yang et al. 2012 PGMs; nonparanormal and Gaussian copula approaches) and highlights the gap addressed.  
- Contribution 1: Proves the Winsorized Poisson approach (Kaiser & Cressie 2009) cannot produce a valid joint distribution.  
- Contribution 2: Introduces the Truncated PGM (TPGM) that permits both positive and negative dependencies within a bounded domain.  
- Contribution 3: Proposes Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) variants that remove domain limitations and allow richer dependence structures.  
- Technical quality: Provides rigorous theoretical proofs of normalizability conditions and clear model specifications.  
- Experimental validation: Shows strong empirical performance on simulated networks and real microRNA sequencing data, capturing biologically meaningful networks.  
- Clarity: Well-organized mathematical derivations and effective use of theorems for exposition.  
- Originality: Strong—addresses a longstanding limitation of PGMs and advances theoretical understanding for count-data graphical models.  
- Significance: Substantial impact for count-data modeling across domains like genomics and spatial statistics.  
- Limitation: SPGM’s log-partition function lacks a closed form, but a useful variational upper bound is provided.  
- Overall recommendation: High-quality contribution with convincing results; recommend acceptance.
