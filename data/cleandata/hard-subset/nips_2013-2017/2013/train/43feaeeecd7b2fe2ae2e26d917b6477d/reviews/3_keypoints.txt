- Paper constructs multivariate distributions over unbounded counts that respect an undirected graph’s Markov structure.  
- Building multivariate count distributions is hard and has a long history (Besag, Arnold et al.).  
- Positive: proposed constructions allow marginal count distributions and lead to relatively simple estimation algorithms.  
- Negative: QPGM yields marginally thinner tails than desired.  
- Negative: SPGM lacks closed-form conditional distributions, undermining the conditional-specification motivation and raising whether it is truly “Poisson.”  
- Questioned practical motivation: Poisson is often a poor empirical fit; authors should show data (e.g., breast cancer) and plot the SPGM PMF.  
- Method details unclear: role and selection of constants R and R0 need explanation and practitioner guidance.  
- Literature review is incomplete/light—authors seem to misunderstand copulas, misrepresent [8], and possibly misstate [11].  
- Neglect of spatial statistics and other high-dimensional count methods; many existing approaches (e.g., Gaussian copula) do not impose MRF independence in observable space.  
- Theoretical contribution is valid and interesting, but practitioners need stronger justification to prefer this model over simpler Gaussian random field + Poisson measurement models.  
- Request an updated reference for the claim that sequencing reads are replacing microarrays.
