- Paper proposes several new models for representing joint distributions over integer-count variables.
- Authors argue the Yang et al. default model is unsatisfactory because it only allows negative correlations to normalize.
- Proposed fixes include a new truncation method, a quadratic base-measure statistic (proven necessary under fixed conditions), and a sub-linear sufficient statistic.
- Experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real datasets is presented.
- Reviewer finds the paper well written, original, and its results significant.
- Reviewer is unconvinced by the "big negatives" claimed for the truncation model (may suit domains with low counts).
- Reviewer questions the critique of the quadratic model—Gaussian-like thin tails could be appropriate in some settings.
- Reviewer is unclear on the term "sparsistent" despite its established usage.
- Minor editorial suggestions: change "graphical mode distributions" to "model" (p.3) and clarify "Note that the log..." (p.5) to something like "Note that although the log...".
