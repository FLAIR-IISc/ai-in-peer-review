- Constructing multivariate count distributions is important and authors correctly identify limitations of current approaches.  
- The paper proves previously suggested truncation is invalid and proposes alternatives (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM).  
- TPGM truncation is natural and well-justified.  
- QPGM and SPGM are novel in altering the exponential-family base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Much of the theory follows directly from Yang et al., limiting theoretical novelty and leaving practical merit to be demonstrated.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and appears biased toward the proposed models.  
- Synthetic experiments are tailored to the methods; real-data comparisons omit copula-based alternatives.  
- Reported results are qualitative/anecdotal; objective evaluation (e.g., test log-probability) is needed.  
- Copula competitors are used in an overly black-box way with inappropriate marginal choices; better marginals (e.g., KDE) should be used.  
- Identical performance to Glasso is suspicious and suggests baseline/method issues.  
- Paper is generally well written; include all proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Section 3.1 is overly detailed and a bound seems unnecessary; present and better explain the key figure for Section 3.2 in the main text.  
- Overall: theoretically appealing approaches but experimental evidence is insufficient to establish their practical merit.
