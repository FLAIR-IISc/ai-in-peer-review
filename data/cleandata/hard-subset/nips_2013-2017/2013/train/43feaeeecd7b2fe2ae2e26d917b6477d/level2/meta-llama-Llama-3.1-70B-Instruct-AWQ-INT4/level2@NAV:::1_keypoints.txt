- Main drawback identified: existing Poisson graphical models can only model negative conditional dependencies.
- Contribution: three new models proposed — Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM).
- Claim: TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM can capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Claim: the proposed distributions are normalizable and can represent rich dependence structures.
- Support: theoretical analysis and numerical experiments validate normalizability and dependency capture.
- Empirical evaluation: simulations and a real-world dataset demonstrate model performance.
- Usefulness: models are relevant for high-dimensional multivariate count data in applications like genomics, neuroimaging, and spatial statistics.
- Literature: paper shows good understanding and clear review of existing Poisson graphical models and limitations.
- Originality: QPGM and SPGM are novel contributions; TPGM provides an additional approach.
- Reproducibility: paper is largely complete with sufficient detail, though some proofs are only in the appendix.
- Limitations: TPGM requires a priori bounded variable domain; QPGM exhibits a Gaussian-like thin tail that may be undesirable.
- Suggested improvements: include more detailed proofs and derivations in the main text.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with novel methods and potential impact.
- Recommendation and score: Accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
