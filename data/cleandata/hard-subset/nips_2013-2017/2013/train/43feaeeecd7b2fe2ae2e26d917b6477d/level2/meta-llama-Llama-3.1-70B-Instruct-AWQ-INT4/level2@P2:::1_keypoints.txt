- Proposes novel Poisson-like graphical models that capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies for count-valued variables.
- Identifies limitation of existing Poisson graphical models: normalizability constraints restrict them to negative dependencies.
- Explores two truncation strategies for the Poisson; only one yields a valid joint distribution (TPGM).
- TPGM expands the range of dependencies but still has limitations.
- Introduces two new variants: Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) with Gaussian-like thin tails and Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) with heavier tails.
- QPGM and SPGM both allow modeling of positive and negative conditional dependencies.
- Empirical results on simulated networks and microRNA-seq data show TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian-based methods for count data.
- Strength: thorough analysis of existing limitations and truncation strategies.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation.
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with graphical models and Poisson distributions, reducing accessibility for non-experts.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity.
- Weakness: does not sufficiently discuss potential real-world application scenarios.
- Originality: QPGM and SPGM are novel, distinct approaches for modeling count data.
- Significance: addresses a major gap in Poisson graphical modeling and has potential relevance to genomics, neuroimaging, and spatial statistics.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting more detail on computational complexity and discussion of real-world applications.
