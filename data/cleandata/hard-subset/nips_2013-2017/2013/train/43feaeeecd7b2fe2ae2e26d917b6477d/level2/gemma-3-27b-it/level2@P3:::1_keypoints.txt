- Addresses modeling multivariate count data and key limitation of existing Poisson graphical models: inability to capture negative dependencies.  
- Identifies flaws in a previously suggested truncation approach and derives a valid Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM).  
- Proposes Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) by modifying the Poisson base measure and sufficient statistics.  
- Provides mathematical derivations and clear justifications for TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM.  
- Offers significant theoretical contributions: theorems on normalizability and conditions for modeling both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Empirically validates methods on simulated data and real microRNA sequencing data, demonstrating efficacy.  
- Well-motivated problem and clear presentation of motivation and contributions.  
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous but dense, potentially limiting accessibility.  
- Presentation of sublinear Poisson sufficient statistics is especially hard to follow; a visual of B(x) would help.  
- Experimental section lacks details on parameter tuning and sensitivity analyses, hurting reproducibility.  
- Real-data evaluation relies mainly on visual network comparisons; needs quantitative metrics (e.g., precision/recall) and biological significance assessment.  
- Discussion of computational complexity and learning cost is limited; implementation details of proximal gradient ascent are sparse.  
- Practical applicability concerns: QPGM and SPGM introduce complexities that may hinder adoption; limitations and remedies need more discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, mathematically sound extensions addressing a key gap, with comprehensive theory and experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dense math, limited computation/tuning discussion, insufficient quantitative real-data evaluation, and practical adoption concerns.  
- Overall assessment: significant, high-quality contribution whose impact would increase after revisions addressing accessibility, tuning, evaluation, and computational details.
