- Paper addresses normalizability limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs) that forces nonpositive pairwise parameters.  
- Shows Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals cannot correspond to any joint MRF in the Besag/Yang framework (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes a truncation-based exponential-family TPGM whose node-conditionals yield a valid joint MRF, allowing mixed-sign dependencies on a finite domain (Theorem 2).  
- Proves that for exponential-family count MRFs with linear sufficient statistics, the base measure must grow at least quadratically to admit positive edges (Theorem 3).  
- Introduces QPGM: retains linear sufficient statistics but adds quadratic node terms; normalizability requires negative-definite quadratic matrix, permitting mixed-sign edges (Proposition 2).  
- Proposes SPGM: keeps Poisson base measure but replaces linear sufficient statistic with a sublinear, saturating B(x; R0, R), yielding heavier tails and normalizability for all pairwise parameters (Theorem 4).  
- Sketches neighborhood-selection with ℓ1 regularization using variational bounds for intractable nodewise log-partitions.  
- Empirically compares TPGM and SPGM to Gaussian, nonparanormal, and SKEPTIC on simulated graphs and TCGA miRNA-seq data, showing improved ROC on counts and richer biological networks.  
- Strength: Clearly identifies and formally resolves a longstanding PGM issue; Theorem 1 is novel and corrects prior practice.  
- Strength: Provides constructive alternatives (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) and a principled necessity result motivating QPGM.  
- Strength: Uses sound Besag/Yang conditional-to-joint construction; proposed families are interpretable.  
- Strength: Empirical evidence supports count-aware models outperforming Gaussian/copula baselines on count data.  
- Weakness: Theoretical gaps in learning and computation—convexity/concavity of penalized node-conditional problems for QPGM/SPGM is not discussed.  
- Weakness: No guarantees provided for proximal gradient ascent if nodewise conditional log-likelihoods are nonconvex.  
- Weakness: Sparsistency is deferred; extending Yang et al.’s conditions to QPGM/SPGM requires nontrivial work and a theorem sketch would help.  
- Practicality concern: Choice and selection of SPGM saturation parameters (R0, R) is unexplained; fixed values lack justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Practicality concern: If many counts exceed R0, B(x; R0, R) may lose information and harm identifiability.  
- Practicality concern: SPGM nodewise log-partition lacks closed form; Poisson upper bound applies only when θ≥0—handling θ<0 and the bound’s looseness are unclear.  
- Empirical limitation: TPGM limitations (boundary effects when R set to observed max) are under-explored; boundary-induced spurious hubs need sensitivity analysis.  
- Comparison gaps: Original PGM results are omitted; including its ROC would be informative despite known negatives.  
- Comparison gaps: Other count-network methods (Poisson lognormal, negative binomial/PLN-based, log-linear models) are not compared empirically; Allen & Liu (2012) deserves direct comparison.  
- Clarity gap: Theorem 3 needs a precise statement (conditions, tail regime, graph-size role) and a proof sketch explaining why quadratic growth is necessary.  
- Clarity gap: Proposition 2 should clarify mapping from θst/θss to Θ and give concrete examples of mixed-sign edge patterns under Θ≺0.  
- Clarity gap: Computational details (gradients, proximal steps, step-size, convergence, runtime) are minimal and hurt reproducibility.  
- Quality assessment: Modeling contributions and negative/necessity results are significant and technically sound, but learning methodology needs more detail.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally well written with good intuition, but some proofs, algorithmic details, and notational definitions (e.g., Wp) are missing or unclear.  
- Originality: High—Winsorized impossibility, necessity of quadratic base measure, and SPGM are novel to the reviewer’s knowledge.  
- Significance: High potential—enables mixed-sign dependencies for count MRFs and could become a standard toolkit with stronger learning guarantees and comparisons.  
- Suggestions: Provide precise statement and proof sketch for Theorem 3 and intuition about competition between interactions and base-measure growth.  
- Suggestions: Analyze convexity of nodewise problems or state conditions for unique optima and discuss optimization of variational surrogates and consistency impact.  
- Suggestions: Detail SPGM estimation when θ can be negative; propose tighter bounds or truncation-sum approximations with error control.  
- Suggestions: Include sensitivity analyses for R and (R0, R), report boundary frequencies, and examine effects on degree distribution.  
- Suggestions: Add comparisons to Poisson lognormal/negative binomial/PLN-based methods and prior log-linear sequencing models on TCGA with quantitative metrics.  
- Suggestions: Report runtimes, convergence behavior, and include PGM baseline curves even if limited to negative-edge recovery.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important limitation with principled new families and significant theoretical insights; negative-result corrects prior practice.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Incomplete estimation theory and optimization details; unclear practical guidance for SPGM hyperparameters and limited empirical comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: Lean toward acceptance if authors strengthen learning/optimization details and broaden empirical evaluation as suggested.
