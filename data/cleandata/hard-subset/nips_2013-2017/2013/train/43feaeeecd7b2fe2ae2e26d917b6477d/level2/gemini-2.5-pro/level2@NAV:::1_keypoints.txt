- Addresses Poisson Graphical Models' key limitation: inability to model positive conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints.  
- Rigorously analyzes truncated Poisson distributions and formally proves the previously proposed "Winsorized" approach is invalid for constructing a joint distribution.  
- Introduces a valid Truncated PGM (TPGM) as a correct truncation-based alternative.  
- Proposes two fundamental model modifications: Quadratic PGM (QPGM) altering the base measure and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) using sub-linear sufficient statistics to allow positive and negative dependencies.  
- Provides theoretical analysis linking the base measure to normalizability (Theorem 3), offering insight into when PGMs are valid.  
- Builds on Besag (1974) and Yang et al. (2012), extending foundational frameworks to model richer dependency structures in count data.  
- Significance and originality: first general framework for PGMs that can model both positive and negative dependencies, with substantial applicability (e.g., genomics).  
- Quality: sound, clearly presented theoretical contributions and a strong proof discrediting the Winsorized approach.  
- Clarity and completeness: well-written, logical progression from truncation to model modifications, with convincing experiments on simulated and real miRNA sequencing data.  
- Weakness: defers formal theoretical analysis of structure recovery (sparsistency), lacking discussion on extending existing guarantees to the new models.  
- Weakness: limited practical guidance on hyperparameter selection (especially R0 and R for SPGM) or data-driven selection methods.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — significant, well-supported contribution with strong empirical evidence.
