- Addresses limitation of standard Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) that cannot model positive dependencies due to normalizability constraints.
- Demonstrates the fundamental normalizability issue in standard PGM formulation preventing positive correlations.
- Analyzes truncation strategies and shows the previously proposed Winsorization approach is inadequate.
- Introduces three novel extensions: Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM), each increasing modeling flexibility.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and proofs regarding normalizability of the proposed models.
- Shows why existing truncation methods fail through formal arguments.
- Presents QPGM and SPGM as significant advances for flexible graphical modeling of count data.
- Develops variational bounds for SPGM to enable practical inference.
- Empirical evaluation on simulated data and a breast cancer miRNA dataset shows encouraging performance over standard approaches.
- Simulation studies are limited in scope and should explore more scenarios.
- Discussion of computational challenges, especially the SPGM log-partition function and scalable inference, is brief and lacks concrete techniques.
- Biological interpretation of the inferred networks in the case study is insufficiently detailed.
- High density of mathematical notation in sections 3–4 reduces clarity and would benefit from more intuitive explanations.
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (expanded simulations, more on computational scalability, and clearer presentation).
