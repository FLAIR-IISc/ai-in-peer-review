## Review of "On Poisson Graphical Models"

This paper addresses the limitations of existing Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) – specifically their inability to model positive dependencies – and proposes several novel extensions. The authors correctly identify a fundamental issue with the standard PGM formulation stemming from normalizability constraints, preventing the modeling of positive correlations crucial for many real-world applications. The paper systematically investigates truncation strategies, ultimately demonstrating the inadequacy of a Winsorization approach proposed previously. They then introduce the Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM), each offering increasingly flexible modeling capabilities and addressing the limitations of the preceding approaches. 

The strengths of this work lie in its rigorous theoretical analysis. The proofs regarding the normalizability of the proposed models, and the demonstration of why existing truncation methods fail, are particularly strong. The introduction of the QPGM and SPGM represents a significant step forward in developing flexible graphical models for count data. The exploration of variational bounds for the SPGM provides a practical avenue for inference. The empirical evaluation, while limited, provides encouraging results on simulated data and a breast cancer miRNA dataset, demonstrating the potential of the proposed models to outperform standard approaches.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. The simulation studies could be more extensive and explore a wider range of scenarios. The discussion of computational challenges, particularly regarding the SPGM's log-partition function, is brief and lacks concrete details on scalable inference techniques beyond mentioning the variational bound. While the case study provides a promising application, a more detailed biological interpretation of the inferred networks would strengthen the findings. The clarity suffers somewhat from a high density of mathematical notation, particularly in sections 3 and 4, which could benefit from more intuitive explanations. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant limitation of existing PGMs.
*   Presents rigorous theoretical justification for the proposed models.
*   Demonstrates potential for improved performance in both simulated and real datasets.
*   Offers novel and potentially impactful extensions to the class of graphical models for count data.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited empirical evaluation.
*   Lack of detailed discussion on computational scalability.
*   High mathematical density impacting clarity.



**Overall:** This is a strong theoretical contribution that pushes the boundaries of Poisson graphical modeling. While the empirical validation is limited, the rigorous analysis and novel model formulations are compelling. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting expanded simulation studies and a more detailed discussion of computational aspects. The authors should also consider revising the presentation to improve clarity and accessibility.
