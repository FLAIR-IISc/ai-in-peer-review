- Proposes three novel extensions of the Poisson graphical model (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) to capture richer dependencies for count data.  
- Evaluates two truncation strategies and finds only one yields a valid Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM).  
- Notes TPGM limitations: bounded domain and poor handling of large counts.  
- QPGM changes the Poisson base measure to scale quadratically, enabling positive and negative conditional dependencies.  
- SPGM modifies the Poisson sufficient statistic to be sub-linear, also allowing both positive and negative dependencies.  
- Shows QPGM and SPGM are normalizable and capture a wider range of dependencies than the original PGM.  
- Technical derivations are thorough, well-explained, and balanced with practical applications.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and a significant contribution to the field.  
- Weakness: assumes high technical expertise, making it less accessible to non-experts.  
- Weakness: some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to readers outside the field.  
- Weakness: needs more discussion of practical implications and real-world application guidance.  
- Originality: offers three novel approaches that address the PGM’s inability to model positive dependencies.
