## Review of "On Poisson Graphical Models"

This paper addresses the limitations of existing Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) – specifically their inability to model positive dependencies – and proposes several novel extensions to overcome this issue. The authors correctly identify the normalizability constraint as the root cause of this limitation and explore strategies to circumvent it. They begin by analyzing truncation approaches, demonstrating the inadequacy of a previously proposed method and introducing a valid Truncated PGM (TPGM). Recognizing limitations of the TPGM, they then delve into more fundamental modifications of the Poisson distribution, leading to the Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM). 

The paper is well-motivated and tackles a significant problem in modeling count data. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, with clear proofs for the proposed models’ normalizability and limitations. The introduction of the QPGM and SPGM, offering potential for both positive and negative dependencies, is a valuable contribution. The exploration of different base measures and sufficient statistics provides a nuanced understanding of the challenges in extending PGMs. 

However, the paper’s strength in theory isn’t fully matched by the empirical evaluation. While simulation studies and a case study with miRNA expression data are presented, the experimental setup feels somewhat cursory. The comparison to existing methods is limited, and the justification for parameter choices (e.g., R in TPGM, R0 in SPGM) isn’t always clear.  A more thorough ablation study, exploring the sensitivity of performance to these parameters, would strengthen the results. Furthermore, the computational complexity of the proposed models isn’t discussed, which is crucial for practical applicability.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant limitation of existing PGMs.
*   Rigorous theoretical analysis and clear proofs.
*   Novel model extensions (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) with potential for broader applicability.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks depth.
*   Computational complexity is not discussed.
*   Parameter selection justification is weak.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a valuable theoretical contribution to the field of graphical models for count data. While the empirical evaluation could be strengthened, the novelty and rigor of the proposed methods warrant acceptance, with a strong recommendation for the authors to expand the experimental validation and address the computational considerations in a revised version. I rate this as a **weak accept**.
