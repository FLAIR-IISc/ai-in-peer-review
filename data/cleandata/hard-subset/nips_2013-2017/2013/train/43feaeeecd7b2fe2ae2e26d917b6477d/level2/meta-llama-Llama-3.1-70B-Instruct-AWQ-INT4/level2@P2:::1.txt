This paper proposes novel classes of Poisson-like graphical models that can capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies between count-valued variables. The authors first discuss the limitations of existing Poisson graphical models, which can only model negative dependencies due to normalizability constraints. They then explore two strategies for truncating the Poisson distribution and show that only one of these leads to a valid joint distribution. This truncated Poisson graphical model (TPGM) can accommodate a wider range of conditional dependencies, but still has some limitations.

To address these limitations, the authors investigate two additional novel variants of the Poisson distribution and their corresponding joint graphical model distributions. The Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM) and the Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM) provide classes of Poisson-like graphical models that can capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies between count-valued variables. The QPGM has a Gaussian-esque thin tail, while the SPGM has a heavier tail with milder base measures.

The authors demonstrate the performance of their methods by learning simulated networks and a network from microRNA-sequencing data. The results show that the TPGM and SPGM methods have superior performance for count-valued data compared to Gaussian-based methods.
