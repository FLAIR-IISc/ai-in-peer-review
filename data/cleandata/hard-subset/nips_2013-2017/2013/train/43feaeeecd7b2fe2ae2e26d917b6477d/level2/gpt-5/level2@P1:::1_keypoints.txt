- Addresses limitation of Poisson graphical models (PGMs) that only allow negative conditional dependencies under standard constructions.  
- Proves Winsorized Poisson node-conditionals cannot yield a consistent joint MRF (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes Truncated PGM (TPGM) on finite support that admits consistent joint MRFs with mixed-sign interactions (Theorem 2).  
- Shows linear sufficient statistics require a base measure with at least quadratic growth for mixed-sign edges on unbounded domains (Theorem 3).  
- Introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM) with quadratic base measure and negative-definite quadratic form to allow mixed-sign interactions.  
- Introduces Sublinear PGM (SPGM) that uses a sublinear/saturating sufficient statistic to keep Poisson base and allow all real pairwise parameters (Theorem 4).  
- Provides variational bounds relating QPGM to Gaussian and SPGM to Poisson for analysis and computation.  
- Uses penalized neighborhood selection (node-conditional likelihood) for structure learning.  
- Simulations show TPGM/SPGM outperform Gaussian/nonparanormal baselines on count data across hub and scale-free graphs.  
- Real-data miRNA-seq case study is compelling and includes biological interpretation and stability selection.  
- Negative result for Winsorized conditionals and necessity result for base-measure growth are novel and important contributions.  
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1–4) are valuable and clarify confusions in the literature.  
- SPGM normalizability result is useful for constructing heavy-tailed count graphical models.  
- Estimation for SPGM is unclear: node-conditional log-partition involves an infinite sum; unclear whether bounds, truncation, or surrogates are used in optimization and their bias/consistency impact.  
- For QPGM, unclear whether Gaussian variational bound is used in optimization or only for analysis; need characterization of any approximation gap.  
- Hyperparameter selection for R and R0 is underspecified; no principled selection method or sensitivity analysis reported.  
- Global parameter coherence: unclear symmetrization rule for node-wise estimates and conditions ensuring global negative-definite Θ (for QPGM) or joint consistency.  
- Comparisons are limited: missing baselines like Poisson lognormal, hierarchical overdispersed count models, and GLM-based Poisson graphical model (Yang et al.).  
- Evaluation metrics lack uncertainty: AUCs should include confidence intervals and calibration/sparsity at operating points.  
- Case study lacks preprocessing details (normalization, filtering), rationale for R=11/R0=5, and basic count-distribution reporting.  
- Paper is overall well-written but needs added precision on assumptions (e.g., formal definition of Ω(·) and asymptotic vs pointwise statements).  
- Recommend including exact expression or approximation strategy for D(θ,R0,R) used in SPGM optimization.  
- Strengths: clear articulation of limitation, important negative result, new theoretically grounded model families, practical learning strategies, and solid empirical comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: under-specified estimation details, no sparsistency/statistical guarantees provided, limited comparative baselines, and missing sensitivity/ablation analyses.  
- Recommendation: strong theoretical and practical contribution; lean toward acceptance if authors add implementation specifics, sensitivity analyses, and expanded comparisons.
