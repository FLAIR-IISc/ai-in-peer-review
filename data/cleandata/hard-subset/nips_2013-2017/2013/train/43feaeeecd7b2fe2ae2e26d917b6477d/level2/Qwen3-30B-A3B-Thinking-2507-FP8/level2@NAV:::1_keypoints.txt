- Problem: Standard Poisson graphical models (PGMs) can only model negative conditional dependencies; paper addresses this limitation.  
- Contribution: Proposes three new models—Truncated PGM (TPGM), Quadratic PGM (QPGM), and Sublinear PGM (SPGM)—that capture both positive and negative dependencies for count data.  
- Theory: Proves normalizability of the proposed models under appropriate conditions (Theorems 1–4).  
- Experiments: Simulations demonstrate superior performance on count data compared to Gaussian-based methods.  
- Application: Real-world breast cancer microRNA network analysis shows practical utility and identifies biologically relevant miRNA clusters.  
- Strength: Clearly identifies a significant gap in the literature and addresses it directly.  
- Strength: Provides elegant theoretical derivations that properly handle normalizability constraints.  
- Strength: Well-designed experiments and meaningful biological application bolster empirical claims.  
- Related work: Demonstrates strong understanding and proper positioning relative to foundational PGM work (Besag, Yang et al.).  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of computational complexity for the proposed methods, notably SPGM.  
- Weakness: Insufficient implementation details on handling SPGM’s infinite summations and use of the Poisson log-partition variational upper bound.  
- Weakness: Lacks clear articulation of trade-offs between TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM for different application scenarios.  
- Significance: Substantial—enables modeling of both positive and negative conditional dependencies in count-data domains (genomics, spatial statistics, etc.).  
- Recommendation: High-quality contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address computational complexity discussion.
