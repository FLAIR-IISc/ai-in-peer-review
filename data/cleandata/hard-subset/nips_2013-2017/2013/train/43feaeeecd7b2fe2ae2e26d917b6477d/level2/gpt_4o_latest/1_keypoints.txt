- Introduces three novel models (TPGM, QPGM, SPGM) that allow both positive and negative conditional dependencies for multivariate count data.
- Provides rigorous theoretical development with proofs of model normalizability.
- Addresses a practical limitation of existing PGMs and demonstrates relevance in applications like breast cancer microRNA networks.
- Comprehensive evaluation via simulations and a case study showing superiority over Gaussian graphical models and traditional PGMs (ROC curves, adjacency matrices).
- Authors clearly state model-specific limitations: TPGM’s bounded domain and QPGM’s Gaussian-like thin tails; SPGM offered for heavier tails and wider parameter ranges.
- Presentation is mathematically dense and may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with graphical models or exponential families.
- Paper lacks extensive discussion of computational scalability for very high-dimensional datasets.
- Reproducibility concerns due to insufficient implementation details (hyperparameter tuning, runtimes).
- Arguments for acceptance: fills a critical gap, well-founded theory, clear empirical advantages, and high impact potential in fields using count data.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility from dense exposition and missing computational/implementation details.
- Recommendation: accept, with revisions to improve clarity and add computational/implementation details.
