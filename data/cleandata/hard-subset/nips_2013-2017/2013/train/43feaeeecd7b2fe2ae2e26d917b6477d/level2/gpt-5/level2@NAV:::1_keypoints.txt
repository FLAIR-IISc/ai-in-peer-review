- Addresses Poisson graphical models' limitation requiring non-positive edges; proves Winsorized node-conditionals cannot yield a consistent joint MRF (Theorem 1).
- Introduces a valid exponential-family truncation (TPGM) that supports both positive and negative dependencies.
- Proves normalizability with linear sufficient statistics requires a base measure growing at least quadratically (Theorem 3).
- Proposes Quadratic PGM (QPGM) and Sublinear PGM (SPGM) with piecewise sublinear sufficient statistics to allow mixed-sign edges.
- Derives normalizability conditions and variational bounds enabling neighborhood selection for the new models.
- Empirical results show TPGM/SPGM outperform Gaussian-based methods and the original PGM on simulations and a TCGA microRNA case study.
- Theoretical contributions are substantive and mostly sound, notably the impossibility of Winsorization and necessity of quadratic base measures.
- Learning via penalized node-conditional likelihood is standard and appropriate, but sparsistency (structure recovery guarantees) is deferred.
- Inference and computational complexity for SPGM (involving infinite sums) are not fully characterized.
- Empirical evaluation lacks statistical significance tests and omits broader baselines (e.g., Poisson log-normal, negative-binomial graphical models, copula-based count models).
- Clarity is generally good, but SPGM’s piecewise statistic, choice of R0/R, and hyperparameter selection need more intuition and computational detail.
- Originality is high, with novel negative results and model constructions (Winsorization impossibility, Ω(Z^2) requirement, SPGM).
- Significance is strong: enabling positive and negative dependencies for counts fills an important gap for genomics and other domains.
- Pros: clear theoretical advances (Theorems 1–4), three concrete model classes with learning recipes, and relevance to graphical-model/high-dimensional inference communities.
- Cons: missing sparsistency/finite-sample guarantees, limited comparisons and hyperparameter ablations, unclear scalability/runtime, and case study lacks objective ground truth.
- Suggested improvements: add theoretical structure-recovery guarantees; compare to Poisson log-normal and negative-binomial models; provide runtime/memory benchmarks and sensitivity analyses for R0/R; clarify SPGM partition-function estimation/bounds; release code and describe stability-selection settings.
