- Standard Poisson Graphical Models (PGMs) cannot model positive conditional dependencies due to normalizability constraints, limiting real-world applicability.
- The paper rigorously proves the previously proposed Winsorized Poisson approach is not a valid joint distribution (Theorem 1), correcting the literature.
- Introduces Truncated PGM (TPGM): valid exponential-family joint distribution allowing positive/negative dependencies but requires a pre-specified truncation level R.
- Introduces Quadratic PGM (QPGM): modifies the Poisson base measure to be quadratic, yielding normalizability under negative-definite parameters and Gaussian-like thin tails.
- Introduces Sub-linear PGM (SPGM): uses sub-linear sufficient statistics to bound interactions, allowing heavy tails and arbitrary real-valued pairwise parameters while ensuring normalizability.
- Empirical results show TPGM and SPGM outperform Gaussian-based methods and classical PGM, including an application to learning a microRNA expression network.
- Strength: tackles a significant open problem for multivariate count data with clear practical applications (genomics, neuroscience, social sciences).
- Strength: highly original theoretical contributions, including the Winsorized PGM disproof and elegant, well-motivated model constructions.
- Strength: paper is clear, well-organized, and candid about trade-offs (bounded domain for TPGM, thin tails for QPGM, etc.).
- Weakness: hyperparameter selection (R for TPGM; R0 and R for SPGM) relies on heuristics (max observed count) without sensitivity analysis or data-driven selection guidance.
- Weakness: learning algorithm details are insufficiently specified—unclear whether the variational bound for SPGM was used or how optimization was performed in experiments.
- Weakness: QPGM is not evaluated empirically; including it in experiments would complete the comparison and validate its properties.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees for learning (e.g., sparsistency); a sketch of how existing proofs could adapt to these models would strengthen the paper.
- Overall assessment: excellent, novel, and theoretically sound work with minor implementation and exposition gaps; strong candidate for acceptance.
