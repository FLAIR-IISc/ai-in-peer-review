**Summary**

This paper introduces novel graphical models for count-valued data, addressing the limitations of existing Poisson graphical models. The authors propose three new approaches: the Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), the Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and the Sub-Linear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM). These models can capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies between count-valued variables, overcoming the restrictive assumptions of traditional Poisson graphical models.

**Strengths**

1. The paper provides a thorough review of existing graphical models for count data, highlighting their limitations and the need for new approaches.
2. The proposed models (TPGM, QPGM, and SPGM) are well-motivated and mathematically sound, with a clear derivation and justification for each.
3. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the properties and advantages of each model, including their ability to capture both positive and negative dependencies.
4. The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed models through simulation studies and a case study on breast cancer microRNA networks.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes a high level of mathematical background, which may limit its accessibility to non-experts in the field.
2. Some of the derivations and proofs are relegated to the appendix, which may make it difficult for readers to follow the arguments without additional effort.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed models, particularly in high-dimensional settings.
4. The case study on breast cancer microRNA networks, while informative, is relatively brief and could be expanded to provide more insights into the practical applications of the proposed models.

**Originality and Significance**

1. The paper introduces novel graphical models for count-valued data, which is a significant contribution to the field of statistical modeling.
2. The proposed models address the limitations of existing Poisson graphical models, providing a more flexible and robust framework for modeling count data.
3. The paper's focus on count-valued data is timely and relevant, given the increasing importance of this type of data in many fields, including genomics and social network analysis.
