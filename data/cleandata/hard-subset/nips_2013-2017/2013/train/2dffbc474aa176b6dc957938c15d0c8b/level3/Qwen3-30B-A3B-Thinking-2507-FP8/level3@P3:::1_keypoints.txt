- Presents a fully Bayesian method for GP state-space models by marginalizing transition dynamics using Particle MCMC with ancestor sampling.  
- Enables joint state and parameter estimation without parametric approximations of the transition function.  
- Builds on GP dynamical systems literature but adds fully Bayesian learning of GP dynamics rather than assuming pre-learned dynamics.  
- Leverages Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to handle the resulting non-Markovian latent-variable model.  
- Technically sound with clear derivations and efficient computational methods (sparse GP constructions, Cholesky updates).  
- Empirical results show ability to capture complex dynamics and multimodal smoothing even with misspecified mean, but experiments are brief.  
- Well-written and organized; mathematical derivations are clear; minor issue: CPF-AS used before definition.  
- Novel contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics via ancestor sampling preserving GP expressivity.  
- Significant: fills an important gap for fully Bayesian learning of dynamics with applications in robotics/control and uncertainty quantification.  
- Suggestion: add discussion comparing parametric transition models versus using them as GP mean for forecasting/out-of-sample performance.  
- Strengths: novel approach, strong computational considerations, clear theoretical foundations, handles multimodal smoothing.  
- Weaknesses: limited experimental exploration (hyperparameter sensitivity), unclear RMSE reporting (in-sample vs out-of-sample), minor abbreviation placement.  
- Recommendation: accept — important problem addressed with a technically sound, novel, and well-presented solution.
