- Paper proposes a fully Bayesian GP state-space model using PMCMC to marginalize transition dynamics, building on prior GP-SSM and SMC work.  
- Marginalizing the dynamics function is a significant theoretical contribution enabling nonparametric learning.  
- Derivation of the marginal prior and a tailored PMCMC algorithm are presented with reasonable clarity and literature grounding.  
- Inclusion of a sparse GP (FIC) variant shows awareness of computational scalability issues.  
- Experiments (nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole) demonstrate initial feasibility of the approach.  
- The paper glosses over practical computational challenges and complexity details.  
- Convergence of the PMCMC scheme, especially in higher-dimensional state spaces, is not adequately addressed.  
- Choices of hyperparameters (particle count, MCMC iterations) lack justification and no sensitivity analysis is provided.  
- Inducing point selection for the sparse GP is acknowledged as problematic but no guidance or best practices are given.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: limited baselines and missing robust comparisons to relevant methods.  
- Results lack statistical analysis to assess significance of improvements.  
- Cart-pole example is largely qualitative and lacks quantitative evaluation metrics.  
- Claim of “very good mixing” is unsubstantiated by diagnostic plots or convergence tests.  
- For acceptance: approach fills a gap by enabling fully nonparametric learning in GP-SSMs and is technically clear.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient critical evaluation of limitations and an incomplete experimental validation; overall reads as a proof-of-concept.
