- Presents a fully Bayesian inference and learning approach for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using Gaussian process priors.  
- Uses particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) to sample from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing the state transition function.  
- Preserves full nonparametric expressivity and supports sparse Gaussian processes to reduce computational cost.  
- Clear explanation of marginalizing the transition function and the application of PMCMC.  
- Well-written, well-organized paper with a thorough introduction and literature review.  
- Lacks a detailed, critical evaluation of the method’s strengths and weaknesses.  
- Experimental section demonstrates the approach but is not a rigorous empirical evaluation.  
- Does not adequately discuss practical challenges of making the PMCMC scheme work (hyper-parameter choices, particle numbers, computational complexity).  
- Missing comparisons with existing methods and a fuller discussion of limitations.  
- Insufficient implementation details (choice of proposal distributions, hyper-parameter tuning).  
- Methodology applies existing PMCMC techniques to a new problem but introduces no novel algorithms.  
- Addresses an important problem and has potential practical utility, but provides limited evidence of real-world impact.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, potentially useful approach, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical evaluation, lack of critical analysis, no new technical contributions.
