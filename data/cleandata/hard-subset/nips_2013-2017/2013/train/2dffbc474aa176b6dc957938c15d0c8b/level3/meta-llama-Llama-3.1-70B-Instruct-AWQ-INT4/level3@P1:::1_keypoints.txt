- Proposes a novel PMCMC algorithm with ancestor sampling for GP state-space models, marginalizing the transition function — technically sound and well-presented.  
- Main contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics via ancestor sampling enabling efficient inference in non‑Markovian SSMs.  
- Addresses computational efficiency via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates to reduce complexity.  
- Empirical results are brief but indicate effectiveness; recommend expanding experiments and comparisons.  
- Needs a detailed comparison of using a parametric transition model a priori versus using it as the GP mean and implications for forecasting.  
- Clarify evaluation metrics in Table 1—unclear if RMSEs are in-sample or out-of-sample; add out-of-sample/forecast RMSE.  
- Minor clarity issue: CPF‑AS abbreviation used before being defined.  
- Overall: strong, novel contribution likely to stimulate further research; accessible presentation for the target audience.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, technical soundness, clear exposition, empirical support, attention to computational concerns.  
- Rejection/weakness arguments: insufficient empirical comparisons and scenario evaluation, unclear RMSE reporting, minor terminology clarity issues.
