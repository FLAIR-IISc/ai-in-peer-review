## Review of “Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC”

This paper presents a compelling application of Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) to Bayesian inference in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs). The core idea – leveraging PMCMC, specifically Lindsten et al.’s ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs, to marginalize over the transition dynamics and enable efficient inference – is both interesting and original. The development of a sparse GP-SSM variant to address computational complexity is a valuable methodological contribution. The paper is clearly written and well-organized, effectively explaining the technical details and motivations behind the proposed approach. It builds upon existing work in GP-SSMs (Deisenroth et al., Turner et al., Ghahramani & Roweis) by offering a fully Bayesian, nonparametric solution that avoids the limitations of parametric approximations.

A significant weakness, however, is the lack of comparative analysis against other established particle MCMC schemes. While the authors demonstrate the efficacy of their approach, a direct comparison to Particle Marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.) is crucial. As highlighted in the reviewer guidelines, such a comparison is relatively straightforward to implement and would provide essential insights into the relative performance and scalability of the proposed method. The reviewer’s belief that PMMH’s particle count scales quadratically with the time horizon *T*, while particle Gibbs variants exhibit sublinear growth, underscores the importance of this evaluation. 

Furthermore, the results section lacks sufficient diagnostic information. The paper would be significantly strengthened by including graphs illustrating performance metrics – such as Autocorrelation Function (ACF) or Effective Sample Size (ESS) – as functions of both the particle count *N* and the time horizon *T*. These visualizations are necessary to assess the scaling behavior of the algorithm and validate the theoretical expectations regarding computational complexity. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel application of PMCMC to GP-SSMs, offering a fully Bayesian and nonparametric solution.
*   Clear and well-written presentation of the methodology.
*   Development of a sparse GP-SSM variant for improved computational efficiency.
*   Demonstration of the approach on benchmark problems, showcasing its ability to learn complex dynamics.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of comparison to other particle MCMC schemes (PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling).
*   Insufficient diagnostic information to assess scaling behavior and algorithm performance.



Overall, this is a promising paper with a strong technical foundation. Addressing the identified weaknesses – particularly the comparative analysis and the inclusion of performance diagnostics – would substantially enhance its contribution and justify acceptance. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing these points.
