- Proposes a fully Bayesian, nonparametric state-space model using GPs and online marginalization of the transition via a tailored PMCMC sampler with ancestor sampling.  
- Novelty lies in marginalizing the transition dynamics online, avoiding parametric approximations common in prior work.  
- Builds on and clarifies relations to prior GP-SSM work (Deisenroth et al., Turner et al., Ghahramani & Roweis, Wang et al.).  
- Technical presentation is rigorous, mathematically sound, and clearly written.  
- Logical organization from model and inference to implementation and experiments.  
- Uses FIC sparse GP prior and low-rank Cholesky updates for computational efficiency.  
- Provides Algorithm 1 (PGAS) which aids clarity and reproducibility.  
- Method handles non-Markovian latent-variable models and can incorporate prior knowledge via GP mean functions.  
- Experiments show practical utility on a synthetic nonlinear system and a cart-pole example.  
- Discussion of uncertainty quantification and implications for control (cart-pole) is insightful.  
- Experimental scope is limited; needs more diverse datasets and stronger comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- RMSE in Table 1 is ambiguous about in-sample vs out-of-sample; an explicit forecast/out-of-sample RMSE is recommended.  
- Should expand discussion on using a parametric model as the GP mean and its effects on forecasting/generalization.  
- Minor clarity issue: abbreviation CPF-AS used before being defined.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant novel contribution, solid technical work, practical and computationally mindful solution, promising results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental validation and a few minor presentation issues.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify RMSE, discuss parametric priors, and fix abbreviation usage.
