- Proposes applying particle MCMC (specifically particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling, PGAS) for Bayesian inference and learning in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs).  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents an original application of PMCMC to GP-SSMs.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant that reduces computational complexity, a significant methodological contribution.  
- Major omission: no empirical comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes (e.g., PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling).  
- Results lack informative diagnostics (e.g., ACF, ESS) and plots showing performance versus particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Paper does not verify algorithmic scaling; reviewer suspects PMMH may need particle count to grow quadratically with T while PG variants require sublinear growth.  
- Recommendation: include comparisons to other PMCMC methods and scaling diagnostics in future work to strengthen claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: original application of PMCMC, sparse GP-SSM contribution, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing comparisons, insufficient diagnostics, unverified scaling behavior.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
