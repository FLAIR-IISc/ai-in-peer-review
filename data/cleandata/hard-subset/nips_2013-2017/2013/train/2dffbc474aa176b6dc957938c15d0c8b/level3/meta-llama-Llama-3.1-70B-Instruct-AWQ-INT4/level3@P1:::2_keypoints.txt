- Proposes applying particle MCMC to inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs), focusing on particle Gibbs with ancestral sampling.  
- Develops a sparse GP-SSM variant that reduces computational complexity.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on two nonlinear systems, showing the model can capture complex dynamics.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents an original contribution.  
- Omits comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, specifically particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- Results lack informative diagnostics (e.g., ACF or ESS) and plots as functions of particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Reviewer suggests PMMH likely requires particle count to grow quadratically with T, while particle Gibbs variants require sublinear growth—highlighting the need for comparative analysis.  
- Including comparisons to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would be straightforward and important for assessing relative performance.  
- Quality: technically sound but the lack of comparisons and diagnostics is a significant omission.  
- Clarity: well-organized and easy to follow.  
- Originality: presents a novel contribution via the sparse GP-SSM variant.  
- Significance: has potential for strong impact if supplemented with comparative evaluations and richer diagnostics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, meaningful sparse-variant contribution, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing comparisons to other particle MCMC methods, insufficient diagnostic evaluation, needs more comprehensive empirical assessment.
