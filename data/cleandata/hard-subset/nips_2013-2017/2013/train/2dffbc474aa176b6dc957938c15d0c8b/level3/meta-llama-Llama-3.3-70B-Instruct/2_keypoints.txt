- Proposes a novel technique treating tightly balanced network dynamics as an algorithm for optimizing signal representation.  
- Develops a quadratic programming (QP) approach that relates firing rates to network connectivity and inputs.  
- QP framework allows investigation of the computational role of tuning-curve shapes and the network mechanisms that produce them.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, facilitating understanding of methods and arguments.  
- Using quadratic programming to compute firing rates is a significant, novel contribution to the literature.  
- Successfully explains function and mechanisms for diverse tuning curves (monotonic and bump-shaped) and analyzes tuning-curve inhomogeneity effects.  
- Does not compare the QP method to other firing-rate calculation approaches (e.g., linear approximations or alternative optimization methods).  
- Lacks detailed analysis of the robustness of the QP approach to various noise types and perturbations.  
- Work is original and potentially high-impact for neuroscience, data analysis, and computational neurodegeneration.  
- Recommends acceptance, contingent on addressing comparisons to other methods and robustness analyses.
