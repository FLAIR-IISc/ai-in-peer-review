- Contribution: Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the state transition function and uses a tailored PMCMC sampler for smoothing inference.
- Scalability: Introduces a sparse GP formulation to reduce computation and scale the method to larger datasets.
- Experiments: Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and real systems (e.g., cart-and-pole), including handling multimodal smoothing and limited training data.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on GP-SSM and EM-based GP approaches (e.g., Deisenroth et al., Turner et al.) but focuses on marginalizing transitions to avoid parametric assumptions.
- Strength — Technical: Tailored PMCMC sampler is a notable advancement enabling efficient sampling while retaining nonparametric expressivity.
- Strength — Scalability: Sparse GP priors significantly reduce computational complexity, improving practicality.
- Strength — Empirical: Experiments show the method can learn complex dynamics and manage multimodal posteriors.
- Strength — Presentation: Paper is well-organized with clear methodological explanations and derivations.
- Weakness — Implementation details: Lacks detailed discussion of practical implementation challenges like hyperparameter tuning and computational bottlenecks.
- Weakness — Critical assessment: Missing thorough evaluation of limitations and failure modes (e.g., extreme data sparsity).
- Weakness — Experimental rigor: Experiments prioritize capability demonstration over rigorous evaluation—missing runtime, hyperparameter sensitivity, and robustness analyses.
- Weakness — Comparative significance: Does not quantify how much it advances state of the art relative to existing approaches.
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, technically sound approach addressing a challenging problem with demonstrated potential and improved scalability.
- Argument against acceptance: Insufficient critical analysis and incomplete experimental benchmarking reduce impact.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address critical evaluation and experimental rigor.
