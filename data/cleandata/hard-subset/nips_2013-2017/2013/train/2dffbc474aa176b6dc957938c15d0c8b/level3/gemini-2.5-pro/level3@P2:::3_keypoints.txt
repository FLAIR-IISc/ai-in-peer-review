- Proposes a fully Bayesian, nonparametric GP-SSM by marginalizing the GP transition and using PGAS to sample joint posterior of states and hyperparameters.
- Collapsing out the GP retains full nonparametric flexibility and targets the smoothing distribution without committing to a point-estimated function.
- Produces posterior predictive dynamics as a mixture-of-Gaussians, enabling rich uncertainty quantification and multimodal predictive behavior.
- Paper is well-written with clear derivations (marginalized prior) and a clear PGAS description; includes a sparse GP variant for computational tractability.
- Limited originality: primarily a straightforward combination of existing GP-SSM and PMCMC techniques with PGAS applied largely off-the-shelf.
- Lacks critical evaluation of the PMCMC sampler’s practical performance; claims about good mixing and sufficient particle counts are unsubstantiated.
- Missing empirical analysis of sampler mixing (trace plots, ACFs, effective sample sizes) for both hyperparameters and latent states.
- No sensitivity study on number of particles N or analysis of how inference quality degrades as N is reduced.
- No discussion of potential failure modes or robustness issues (e.g., computing predictive probabilities for ancestor sampling in high-dimensional/long sequences).
- Experimental validation is weak: first experiment compares only to simple parametric models rather than advanced GP-SSM methods (variational/EM).
- Cart-pole experiment is qualitative and lacks quantitative metrics or baseline comparisons, making results hard to assess.
- Paper does not evaluate scalability with state dimensionality, a critical concern for both GPs and particle methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled and elegant fully Bayesian framework, strong uncertainty quantification, and clear presentation with speed-up options.
- Arguments for rejection: primarily an integration of known methods rather than a fundamental advance, missing practical evaluation of PMCMC, and weak empirical evidence.
