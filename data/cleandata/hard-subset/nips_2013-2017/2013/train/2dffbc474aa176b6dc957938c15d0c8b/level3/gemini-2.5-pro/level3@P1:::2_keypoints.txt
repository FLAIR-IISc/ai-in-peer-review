- Proposes a fully Bayesian approach for GP-SSMs by marginalizing the unknown dynamics, producing a non-Markovian latent process.  
- Uses Particle MCMC (specifically Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling, PGAS) to jointly sample latent state trajectories and hyperparameters.  
- Reconstructs the posterior predictive dynamics as a mixture of Gaussian Processes from posterior state samples.  
- Introduces a sparse FIC GP-SSM variant reducing computational cost from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and tackles an important, challenging problem in nonparametric time-series modeling.  
- Novel and principled combination of marginalizing GP dynamics with modern PMCMC techniques.  
- Sparse variant increases practical scalability and potential impact.  
- Experiments demonstrate the model can capture complex, multi-modal posterior distributions over states.  
- Major omission: no comparison to other PMCMC samplers (e.g., PMMH or Particle Gibbs with backward sampling) to justify the choice of PGAS.  
- Lacks justification or empirical evidence that PGAS outperforms alternatives in mixing, variance, or particle/accuracy trade-offs.  
- Experiments do not include standard MCMC diagnostics (e.g., ACF, ESS) to assess sampler efficiency.  
- No empirical study of how sampler performance scales with number of particles (N) or time-series length (T).  
- Conclusion: strong central idea and contributions, but incomplete experimental validation and missing diagnostics weaken claims about efficiency and scalability.
