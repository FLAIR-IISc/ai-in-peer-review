

# Review for "Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC"

This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models (SSMs) by placing Gaussian process (GP) priors over the state transition dynamics. The authors address a key limitation in previous GP-SSM approaches that typically require parametric approximations of the transition dynamics. Their core contribution is marginalizing out the transition dynamics function and using a tailored Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) sampler with ancestor sampling (PGAS) to infer the joint smoothing distribution directly. The paper demonstrates how this approach preserves the full nonparametric expressivity of the model while enabling efficient inference.

The work builds on recent advances in PMCMC methods [3, 4] and extends them to non-Markovian state-space models, which is a significant departure from conventional approaches that require fixed transition models. The authors relate their work to prior efforts by Deisenroth et al. [5, 6] who developed inference methods for already-learned GP dynamics, and Turner et al. [2] who used EM to learn parametric GP models. The paper effectively situates its contribution as providing the crucial component needed for these previous approaches - learning the GP model itself in a fully Bayesian manner.

**Strengths:**

*Quality:* The paper is technically sound with rigorous theoretical development. The marginalization of the transition dynamics function is well-justified, and the authors provide a clear derivation of the non-Gaussian prior over state trajectories. The use of PGAS with ancestor sampling to handle the non-Markovian structure is well-motivated and effectively addresses the path degeneracy problem in standard SMC methods. The computational complexity analysis and sparse GP implementation details demonstrate careful consideration of practical scalability.

*Clarity:* The paper is exceptionally well-written with clear explanations of complex concepts. The mathematical derivations are presented accessibly, and the authors effectively use figures to illustrate key concepts. The connection between the GP prior and the state transition dynamics is particularly well-explained.

*Originality:* The online marginalization of transition dynamics via ancestor sampling represents a significant novelty in the field. Previous approaches to GP-SSMs either treated the dynamics as parametric [2, 8] or required fixed transition models [5, 6]. This work's ability to marginalize the dynamics while maintaining full nonparametric expressivity is a clear advancement.

*Significance:* The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in Bayesian inference for nonlinear dynamical systems. The ability to jointly learn the dynamics and states without parametric constraints has broad implications for applications in robotics, control, and time series analysis. The empirical results on both synthetic and cart-pole systems demonstrate the method's practical utility.

**Weaknesses:**

*The paper could benefit from more thorough empirical evaluation. While the experiments demonstrate the approach's capability, they lack out-of-sample forecasting performance metrics. The RMSE in Table 1 is not clearly defined as in-sample or out-of-sample, which is critical for assessing predictive performance. Adding an out-of-sample column would strengthen the empirical validation.

*The discussion of the parametric model as the GP mean (e.g., "model B" in Section 6.1) could be expanded. The paper shows the GP can correct for a poor parametric baseline, but a more detailed comparison of using a parametric model a priori versus as the GP mean would provide valuable insights for practitioners.

*Minor notational issue: The abbreviation "CPF-AS" is used before being defined in Section 3.3.1, which slightly disrupts the flow of the argument.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pros:*
- Novel approach to marginalizing transition dynamics in GP-SSMs
- Rigorous theoretical development with clear mathematical exposition
- Efficient implementation with careful computational complexity analysis
- Demonstrated applicability on both synthetic and real-world benchmark
- Strong connection to recent PMCMC literature [3, 4]

*Cons:*
- Limited out-of-sample forecasting evaluation
- Some details about the parametric vs. GP mean comparison could be expanded
- Minor notational inconsistency with CPF-AS abbreviation

**Overall Recommendation:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian inference for state-space models. The technical novelty is clear, the approach is well-motivated, and the implementation is carefully considered. While the empirical evaluation could be expanded with out-of-sample performance metrics, the current results are sufficient to demonstrate the method's viability. The paper addresses a fundamental problem in the field and would likely stimulate further research on inference for non-Markovian state-space models. The writing is clear and accessible, making the complex material understandable to a broad NIPS audience.

I recommend acceptance as a strong contribution to the conference. The paper is technically sound, novel, and significant, with only minor weaknesses that could be addressed in the camera-ready version. The authors have made a clear step toward enabling fully Bayesian learning of nonlinear dynamical systems with nonparametric flexibility.
