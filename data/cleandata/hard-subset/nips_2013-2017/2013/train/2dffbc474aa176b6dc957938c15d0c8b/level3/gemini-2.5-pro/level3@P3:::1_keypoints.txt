- Novel fully Bayesian method for GP-SSMs that marginalizes the transition function, yielding a non-Markovian state prior and using PGAS for inference; extended with sparse GPs for scalability.

- Original and significant: marginalizing the GP avoids parametric approximations and enables principled inference for nonparametric, non-Markovian temporal models.

- Technically sound: correct derivation of the marginal prior over trajectories and well-justified integration into a PMCMC sampler.

- Clear presentation: manuscript is well-written, logically developed, and provides sufficient methodological detail.

- Strong computational treatment: reduces naive O(T^4) complexity to O(T^3) via rank-one Cholesky updates/downdates and achieves O(M^2 T) with sparse GP priors.

- Empirical evaluation is brief and partly ambiguous—Table 1’s RMSE is unclear (in-sample smoothing vs out-of-sample prediction).

- Recommendation to authors: clarify RMSE calculation and add out-of-sample multi-step forecasting RMSE to better assess generalization.

- GP mean-function discussion is underdeveloped—need comparison to using the parametric model alone and discussion of extrapolation/bias trade-offs.

- Minor presentation issue: "CPF-AS" is introduced in Algorithm 1 before being defined; define it at first use.

- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and technically sophisticated paper with minor addressable weaknesses; recommendation: Accept.
