- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by collapsing the GP transition and using PMCMC to sample x0:T while marginalizing f.  
- Key technical move: analytically integrate out the GP to obtain a non-Markovian prior expressed as GP one-step predictions.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) for trajectory sampling and slice sampling for hyperparameters.  
- Introduces a sparse FIC variant to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) and describes rank-1 Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Demonstrates feasibility on a 1D multimodal smoothing benchmark and a 4D cart-pole system, with predictive mixtures and single-GP surrogates.  
- Conceptually clean formulation that preserves nonparametric flexibility and yields a principled posterior p(x0:T, θ | y0:T).  
- Appropriate use of modern inference machinery (PGAS) that suits the non-Markovian collapsed model and may improve mixing.  
- Clear derivations and efficient implementation details, including sparse construction and complexity analysis.  
- Predictive mixture interpretation is well-motivated and the single-GP surrogate is practical for control applications.  
- Methodological novelty is modest—mainly an application and synthesis of existing PMCMC/PGAS ideas to GP-SSMs.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: small-scale case studies without rigorous baselines, quantitative comparisons, or ablations.  
- Lacks comparisons to EM/MAP GPDM, EP/variational GPDS, and alternative PMCMC variants like PMMH or backward simulation.  
- Practical challenges are under-discussed: sensitivity to priors/kernel choice, particle number, series length, state dimensionality, and path degeneracy.  
- No mixing diagnostics (ESS/autocorrelation), trace plots, or runtime/memory scaling studies presented.  
- No systematic assessment of sparse vs dense GP trade-offs (M selection or inducing input placement) or mean function effects on identifiability.  
- Evaluation emphasizes one-step prediction; missing multi-step forecasts, predictive log-likelihoods, calibration metrics, and robustness checks.  
- Derivations and implementations appear technically sound, and slice sampling for θ|x0:T is standard and correct.  
- Paper is generally well written; collapsing derivation and CPF-AS with ancestor weights are clearly presented.  
- Needs more guidance on choosing N, inducing points, hyperpriors, and practical defaults for reproducibility.  
- Originality rated moderate: collapsing GPs and PMCMC are known, contribution is the synthesis and careful implementation with a sparse option.  
- Potentially useful to practitioners wanting fully Bayesian GP-SSMs, but broader impact depends on demonstrated scalability and robustness.  
- Suggested improvements: rigorous empirical comparisons, predictive metrics, mixing/efficiency diagnostics, sensitivity analyses, and larger/real datasets.  
- Also suggest discussing practical issues (path degeneracy, initialization, identifiability, memory, numerical stability) and inducing input selection.  
- Acceptance arguments: principled Bayesian approach, appropriate use of PGAS, and practical engineering contributions (sparse variant, factorizations).  
- Rejection arguments: limited novelty, insufficient empirical evidence, and missing discussion of key practical difficulties.  
- Recommendation: weak reject pending substantially stronger empirical evaluation and deeper discussion of practical considerations.
