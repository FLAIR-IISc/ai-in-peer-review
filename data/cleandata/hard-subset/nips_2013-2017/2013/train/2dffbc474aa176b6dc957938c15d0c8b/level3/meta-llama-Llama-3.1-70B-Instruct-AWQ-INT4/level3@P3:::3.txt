This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs) using particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) methods. The authors apply PMCMC to a state-space model with a Gaussian process prior, allowing for inference of the actual parameters beyond standard filtering and smoothing.

The paper is a straightforward application of PMCMC methodology to GP-SSMs, and the authors demonstrate the approach using two experiments: learning a nonlinear system benchmark and learning a cart and pole system. The results show that the GP-SSM can accurately capture complex dynamics and provide high-quality smoothing distributions.

However, the paper falls short in several areas. Firstly, the authors fail to properly describe and evaluate the practical challenges of getting the PMCMC scheme to work. While they mention the importance of reusing computations across time to attain efficient computational complexity, they do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational costs and the trade-offs involved.

Secondly, the paper lacks a detailed, critical evaluation of the method's strengths and weaknesses. The authors do not discuss the limitations of the approach, such as the potential for overfitting or the sensitivity to hyperparameter choices. They also do not provide a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for GP-SSMs.

Thirdly, the experimental section demonstrates the approach but does not provide a rigorous empirical evaluation. The authors do not provide a comprehensive analysis of the results, and the experiments are limited to two simple systems. A more thorough evaluation of the method's performance on more complex systems and larger datasets is needed.

Lastly, the paper would benefit from assessing practical difficulties and how well PMCMC actually performs in this setting. The authors should discuss the challenges of implementing the method in practice, such as the choice of hyperparameters, the number of particles, and the computational resources required.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the paper is not a complete piece of work, as it lacks a critical evaluation of the method's strengths and weaknesses.

The paper is clearly written, and the organization is logical. However, some sections, such as the implementation details, could be improved for better readability.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel application of PMCMC to GP-SSMs. However, the approach is not entirely new, as it builds upon existing work on PMCMC and GP-SSMs.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in the field of Bayesian inference and learning. The results have the potential to impact the development of more accurate and efficient methods for GP-SSMs.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel application of PMCMC to GP-SSMs.
