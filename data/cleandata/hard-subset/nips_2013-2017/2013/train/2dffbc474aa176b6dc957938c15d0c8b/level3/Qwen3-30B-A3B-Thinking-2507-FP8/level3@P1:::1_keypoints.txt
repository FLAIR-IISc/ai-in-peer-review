- Introduces a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing GP priors over transition dynamics and marginalizing the function f.  
- Core innovation: marginalization of f enables joint inference of states x0:T and hyperparameters θ while preserving full GP flexibility.  
- Derives the marginal prior p(x0:T | θ) (Eq. 4), which is non-Gaussian but amenable to sequential Monte Carlo.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to target the smoothing distribution p(x0:T | θ, y0:T) despite non-Markovian dependencies.  
- Provides a theoretically sound and computationally efficient sampler, building on prior PMCMC work.  
- Complexity: O(T^3) for full GP; reduced to O(M^2 T) using sparse FIC approximation.  
- Implementation contributions: practical Cholesky update/downdate methods and details that aid computational scalability.  
- Clear exposition: well-motivated derivations (Section 3.1), accessible PGAS algorithm (Algorithm 1), and elegant integration of sparse GPs (Section 4.1).  
- Experiments: nonlinear benchmark shows ability to capture multimodal smoothing despite incorrect parametric mean.  
- Experiments: cart-pole example demonstrates meaningful uncertainty quantification in learned dynamics.  
- Suggested experimental improvements: add out-of-sample forecast RMSE to Table 1 to assess generalization and compare parametric models vs. GP mean priors.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope (only two synthetic examples).  
- Weakness: abbreviation "CPF-AS" introduced before definition (Section 3.3.1).  
- Weakness: could better discuss trade-offs of using a parametric model as the GP mean (impact on smoothing vs. interpretability).  
- Significance: advances Bayesian SSM inference by reconciling nonparametric flexibility with tractable computation, enabling end-to-end dynamics learning.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, novel contribution; minor issues are revisable — recommend acceptance.
