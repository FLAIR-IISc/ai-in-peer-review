- Presents a fully Bayesian method for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using a GP prior over transition dynamics and PMCMC for inference.  
- Core contribution: tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) that marginalizes the transition function to sample from the smoothing distribution.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant to improve computational efficiency.  
- Positions work within existing GP-SSM literature and cites relevant GP and PMCMC foundational papers.  
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulation and justified marginalization of the transition function.  
- Originality: novel application and algorithmic adaptations of PGAS to GP-SSMs, including efficient implementation details.  
- Clarity: well-written, logically organized, with helpful figures (e.g., generative model).  
- Significance: enables fully Bayesian, nonparametric SSM inference that can impact time series, robotics, and control.  
- Major weakness: lacks comparisons to other PMCMC methods (e.g., PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling).  
- Lacks scalability analysis—missing diagnostics like ACF or ESS as functions of particle count (N) and time horizon (T).  
- Limited experiments: benchmarks are narrow and should include more complex, real-world datasets.  
- Implementation caveat: sparse GP fast-update practical details are under-specified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid, well-written, and promising experimental results.  
- Arguments for rejection: missing PMCMC comparisons, inadequate scalability evaluation, and limited experimental scope.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and significant work; recommended weak accept contingent on addressing comparative and scalability concerns.
