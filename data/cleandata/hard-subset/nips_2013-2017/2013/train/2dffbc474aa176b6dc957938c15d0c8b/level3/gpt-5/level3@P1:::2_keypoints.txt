- Proposes a fully Bayesian, nonparametric GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function and sampling latent states and hyperparameters with Particle MCMC (PGAS).  
- Collapsing f yields a non-Markovian prior over trajectories expressible via GP one-step predictions.  
- Designs an efficient PGAS sampler targeting p(x0:T, θ | y0:T) using forward-only CPF-AS kernels.  
- Implements careful linear-algebra engineering (Cholesky reuse, rank-1 updates/downdates) to reduce computation.  
- Provides analytic posterior over f and predictive distributions from sampled trajectories; offers a faster single sparse GP fit alternative.  
- Introduces a sparse FIC GP-SSM variant that reduces complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Experiments include a multimodal nonlinear scalar benchmark and a 4D cart-pole system showing plausible smoothing and calibrated uncertainty.  
- Methodologically grounded in PMCMC and PGAS literature; sparse construction follows standard FIC sparse GP ideas.  
- Collapsing f is technically sound and yields tractable one-step conditionals for inference.  
- PGAS kernel choice is appropriate and implementation details are valuable and well described.  
- Experimental results are qualitatively convincing (bimodality, uncertainty calibration, predictive means/variances).  
- Missing head-to-head comparisons with other PMCMC samplers (PMMH, PGBS) to substantiate claimed efficiency.  
- Lacks MCMC diagnostics (ACF, ESS, ESS/sec) and scaling analyses with particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Limited wall-clock runtime and memory profiling for the sparse variant and sensitivity analyses w.r.t. M and inducing-point selection.  
- Clarity is high: paper is well written with explicit derivations, but needs clearer statements of kernel class and priors used in experiments.  
- Should report hyperparameter posterior summaries and detail the inducing-point selection strategy for FIC.  
- Novelty lies in combining full GP collapse, a PGAS adaptation for the non-Markovian prior, and integrated sparse FIC with implementation optimizations.  
- Significance is high: a general fully Bayesian nonparametric SSM approach is broadly relevant if empirical efficiency claims hold.  
- Pros: elegant collapsed formulation, appropriate PGAS use, well-integrated sparse FIC, and illustrative experiments.  
- Cons: no PMCMC baseline comparisons, missing mixing diagnostics and scaling studies, limited analysis of sparse variant’s practical gains, and modest experimental breadth.  
- Requested revisions: add PMMH/PGBS comparisons, provide ACF/ESS-vs-N and scaling with T, include runtime/memory profiles and ablations, report ESS/sec and trace/R-hat diagnostics, and compare to EM/MAP GP-SSM baselines.  
- Overall: technically solid and novel with promising impact; acceptance recommended conditional on added comparative and diagnostic experiments.
