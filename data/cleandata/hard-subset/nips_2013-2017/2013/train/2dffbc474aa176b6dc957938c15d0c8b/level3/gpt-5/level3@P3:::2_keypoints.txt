- Applies PMCMC (PGAS) to fully Bayesian GP-SSMs by marginalizing the transition f and targeting p(θ,x0:T|y0:T), yielding a non‑Markovian latent process sampled via PGAS and alternating hyperparameter updates.  
- Introduces a sparse FIC GP-SSM variant reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Builds on Lindsten et al. (PGAS) and contrasts with prior MAP/EM and parametric surrogate GP-SSM approaches; collapsed formulation enables smoothing without instantiating f.  
- Technical exposition is sound: marginalization, Gaussian structure, CPF‑AS kernel invariance, hyperparameter sampling, complexity analysis, and sparse construction are correct and referenced.  
- Empirically demonstrates mixing and predictive dynamics on nontrivial tasks, but lacks comparisons to other PMCMC schemes and informative MCMC diagnostics, limiting claims about efficiency and scaling.  
- Reproducibility is good for experts; algorithmic and sparse implementation details are sufficient.  
- Manuscript is clear and well organized; figures informative but would benefit from standard MCMC diagnostics and scaling plots.  
- Originality: novel application of PGAS to collapsed fully Bayesian GP-SSMs and a useful FIC-based sparse extension; prior work is adequately cited though empirical PMCMC comparisons are missing.  
- Significance: important contribution for nonparametric nonlinear dynamics with uncertainty quantification and a scalable sparse option; impact would increase with stronger empirical comparisons.  
- Strengths: rigorous exposition, apt use of PGAS to handle non‑Markovianity, practical sparse FIC variant, and demonstrations on challenging scalar and 4D tasks.  
- Weakness: No comparisons to natural PMCMC baselines (PMMH, PG‑BS), hindering assessment of relative performance.  
- Weakness: Missing informative diagnostics and scaling analyses (ACF, ESS/sec, acceptance rates, CPU‑normalized ESS).  
- Weakness: No empirical exploration of expected scaling behavior with T and N or claims about PMMH vs PG variants.  
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies (PGAS vs PG‑BS vs PMMH; effect of M in FIC; sensitivity to mean function).  
- Suggested additions: compare to PMMH and PG‑BS; provide ACF/ESS/ESS‑per‑sec and acceptance/integrated autocorrelation diagnostics; study scaling vs N and T; include ablations and sensitivity analyses.  
- Questions for authors: effort/obstacles to implement PMMH and PG‑BS from CPF‑AS code; observed sensitivity to N between PGAS and PMMH; can you report ESS/sec for kernel hyperparameters and xt (early vs late times)?  
- Minor requests: cite specific PG‑BS implementations (e.g., Whiteley et al., Lindsten & Schön); add runtime breakdowns and a small study showing near‑linear scaling in T for fixed M,N if space permits.  
- Pros: clear, technically solid, original application, maintains nonparametric expressivity, practical sparse variant, promising empirical results.  
- Cons: missing PMMH/PG‑BS comparisons, absent MCMC diagnostics and scaling studies, limited practitioner guidance on tuning/trade‑offs.  
- Verdict: valuable and well‑written contribution that would be significantly strengthened by adding PMMH/PG‑BS comparisons and standard diagnostics/scaling analyses to convincingly argue for PGAS in this setting.
