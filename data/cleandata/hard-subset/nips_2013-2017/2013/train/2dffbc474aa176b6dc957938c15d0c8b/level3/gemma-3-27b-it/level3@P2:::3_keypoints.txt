- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM using PMCMC to marginalize transition dynamics, removing need for pre-defined parametric transition models.  
- Builds on prior SSM and GP-dynamics literature and introduces a tailored PMCMC sampler with sparse GP and implementation optimizations.  
- Mathematical formulation and derivations appear correct; reuse of Cholesky factorizations and sparse GPs improve computational practicality.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, with helpful generative-model diagram and explicit implementation details.  
- Demonstrated on a nonlinear benchmark and a cart-pole task, showing dynamics learning and predictive distributions.  
- Novel contribution: fully Bayesian marginalization of GP transition dynamics expands GP-SSM capabilities beyond parametric approaches.  
- Potential significance: useful for modeling complex dynamical systems where nonparametric flexibility and full Bayesian inference are desired.  
- Weakness: lacks rigorous evaluation of PMCMC performance—sensitivity to initialization, convergence diagnostics, and computational costs are not thoroughly addressed.  
- Weakness: insufficient critical analysis of method choices (e.g., effect of particle number N, hyperparameter sensitivity).  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation and comparative baselines; cart-pole lacks quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art (e.g., variational methods).  
- Weakness: missing discussion of identifiability issues inherent to nonparametric GP priors and ambiguous function explanations of data.  
- Weakness: contribution partly incremental—primarily an application of existing PMCMC methods with limited articulation of novel PMCMC-specific innovations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel Bayesian treatment, clear writing, and potential to advance nonlinear dynamical modeling.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient practical evaluation of PMCMC, limited empirical evidence of superiority, and lack of critical discussion of limitations.  
- Overall: solid contribution but needs minor revisions—add detailed PMCMC performance analysis, stronger empirical comparisons, and discussion of identifiability and limitations.
