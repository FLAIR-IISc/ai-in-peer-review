This paper presents a Bayesian method for joint state and parameter estimation in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs) using particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) with ancestor sampling. The authors address the problem of learning nonlinear nonparametric state-space models by placing a Gaussian process prior over the state transition dynamics. The proposed approach marginalizes out the transition dynamics function and infers directly the joint smoothing distribution using a tailored PMCMC sampler.

The paper is technically sound, and the presentation is clear and accessible. The authors provide a thorough introduction to GP-SSMs and the challenges associated with learning these models. The proposed method is novel, particularly in its use of online marginalization of transition dynamics via ancestor sampling. The authors also demonstrate a strong consideration of computational efficiencies, including the use of sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.

The empirical results presented in the paper are brief but sufficient, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart and pole system. However, it would be beneficial to expand on these results, including more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

One potential area for improvement is the discussion of using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean. The authors mention that the GP prior can capture complex departures from a simple parametric model, but a more detailed discussion of the implications for forecasting and out-of-sample performance would be beneficial.

Additionally, Table 1 and the preceding text are unclear whether the RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample. It would be helpful to add an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column to provide a clearer understanding of the model's performance.

A minor issue is the use of the abbreviation CPF-AS before it is defined. It would be beneficial to define this abbreviation earlier in the paper to avoid confusion.

Overall, this is a strong and novel paper that is likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models. The authors' approach to marginalizing out the transition dynamics function and using a tailored PMCMC sampler is a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to learning GP-SSMs.
* The authors demonstrate a strong consideration of computational efficiencies.
* The empirical results are sufficient to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper is well-written and clear, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The empirical results are limited, and more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods would be beneficial.
* The discussion of using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean could be expanded.
* The paper could benefit from additional clarity on the RMSE results, including the addition of an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column.
