- Presents a fully Bayesian approach for nonlinear nonparametric SSMs using GPs for transitions and PMCMC for inference.
- Marginalizes the transition dynamics, enabling direct inference of the smoothing distribution.
- Properly situates the work within GP-SSM literature and addresses limitations of parametric GP approximations.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized with logical, easy-to-follow mathematical derivations.
- Includes helpful visualizations (e.g., Figure 1) and clear explanations of implementation details.
- Provides computational optimizations for both vanilla GP and a sparse FIC GP variant.
- Experimental results demonstrate efficacy on a synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole system.
- Novelty: tailored application of PGAS (particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling) to GP-SSMs combined with analytical marginalization.
- Development of a sparse GP-SSM enhances practicality for higher-dimensional state spaces.
- Significance: enables fully Bayesian, nonparametric inference in nonlinear SSMs with broad relevance to time series, control, and robotics.
- Weakness: lacks comparative evaluation against other particle MCMC schemes (PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling).
- Weakness: limited diagnostic analysis of sampler performance — missing ACFs, ESS, and scaling plots versus number of particles (N) and time horizon (T).
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; add comparisons to PMMH/PG backward sampling and detailed diagnostics (ACF/ESS vs N, T).
