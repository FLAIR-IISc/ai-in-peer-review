- Proposes a fully Bayesian treatment of GP state-space models using PMCMC (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling) by marginalizing out the GP transition function.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM and PMCMC literature and uses sparse GPs to address computational cost.  
- Methodological choice (PMCMC/PGAS) is sound and the formulation is elegant and nonparametric.  
- Main contribution is largely a straightforward application of known methods rather than a novel algorithmic advance.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical implementation challenges (e.g., particle count selection, convergence diagnostics, mixing efficiency).  
- Presents PMCMC as a turnkey solution without addressing common difficulties in making such samplers work efficiently.  
- Experimental validation is more demonstrative than rigorous, showing the method can work but not thoroughly testing it.  
- Missing comprehensive comparisons to alternative approaches (e.g., variational or other approximate inference methods).  
- No analysis of scalability or robustness with respect to data length or state dimensionality.  
- Paper would benefit from critical assessment of strengths/weaknesses and experiments targeting performance boundaries.
