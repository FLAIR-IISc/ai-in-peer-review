- Paper proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM learning method using PMCMC by marginalizing the state transition function with a GP prior.  
- Work builds on prior GP-SSM and particle inference literature and positions itself to support methods that learn GP before filtering/smoothing.  
- Strength: theoretically sound approach with clear mathematical formulation, especially marginalization of the transition function (Section 3.1).  
- Strength: sensible use of the FIC prior for computational efficiency and clear explanation of computation reuse to achieve O(T^3) or O(M^2T) complexity.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough discussion and evaluation of practical implementation challenges for PMCMC (tuning, diagnostics, sensitivity to initialization).  
- Weakness: minimal detail on proposal distribution choices, convergence diagnostics, and ensuring adequate state-space exploration.  
- Weakness: insufficient critical self-assessment comparing the method’s strengths and weaknesses against alternatives.  
- Weakness: computational cost of PMCMC not adequately compared to other methods, especially in higher-dimensional state spaces.  
- Weakness: trade-offs between nonparametric flexibility and computational complexity are not fully explored.  
- Experimental shortcoming: evaluation limited to two benchmarks and presented mainly descriptively.  
- Experimental shortcoming: only RMSE is reported; lacks statistical significance tests and broader baseline comparisons.  
- Experimental shortcoming: no sensitivity analysis over hyperparameters or model configurations.  
- Cart-and-pole example: visually appealing but lacks quantitative evaluation beyond qualitative uncertainty descriptions.  
- Table 1 comparison to “ground truth model” appears artificial; more relevant baselines from state-of-the-art dynamical-system learning methods are missing.  
- Discussion of learning dynamics (Section 5) is brief and does not explore practical implications for control or prediction tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically sound Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs, novel PMCMC application, clear math, and demonstrations on benchmarks.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient practical/implementation discussion, lack of critical evaluation, limited empirical analysis, and reads as method description rather than full contribution.  
- Overall assessment: interesting idea but limited impact due to lack of practical guidance and rigorous evaluation; reviewer recommends weak reject.  
- Suggested improvements: add detailed practical implementation discussion and diagnostics.  
- Suggested improvements: perform more thorough empirical evaluation with statistical tests and more baselines.  
- Suggested improvements: include critical analysis of strengths/limitations and scalability.  
- Suggested improvements: expand discussion on learned dynamics’ real-world applications.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze PMCMC sensitivity to parameter choices and proposal strategies.
