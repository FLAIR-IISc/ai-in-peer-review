- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition GP and sampling states/hyperparameters with PGAS, then recovering f in closed-form GP posterior.
- Introduces a sparse FIC variant and implementation tricks reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).
- Well-situated relative to prior work; fills a gap by combining collapsing with PGAS for nonparametric dynamics learning.
- Principled and technically sound: collapsing derivations and PGAS invariance are correct.
- Clear exposition and careful algorithmic design, including complexity-aware implementations and factor reuse.
- Preserves nonparametric expressivity and yields calibrated predictive uncertainty via mixture-of-Gaussians.
- Experiments show multimodal smoothing and learning dynamics from limited, noisy data (cart-pole example).
- Missing empirical comparisons to other PMCMC baselines, notably PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (PG-BS).
- No mixing/efficiency diagnostics or scaling studies: lacks ACF, IACT/ESS (per iteration and per second), acceptance rates, and N vs T scaling plots.
- Limited ablations: sensitivity to particle number N, mean function choice, and sparse approximation (M) is not explored.
- Reproducibility details are sparse: hyperpriors, slice-sampling settings, initialization, and runtime budgets are only briefly sketched.
- Implementation claims (complexity and memory) would be strengthened by empirical validation.
- Writing is generally clear and organized, with minor suggestions on wording and consistent indexing.
- Novel application of PGAS to a collapsed GP-SSM and practical sparse-FIC adaptation appear original and useful.
- Potentially impactful for Bayesian time-series and system identification but significance would increase with efficiency/ scaling studies.
- Suggested improvements: add PMMH and PG-BS baselines, provide ACF/IACT/ESS and runtime/memory plots, ablate M and N, test mean-function sensitivity, and give full reproducibility details (ideally code).
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, novel, cleanly presented method with practical algorithmic insights and calibrated uncertainty.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing baseline comparisons and diagnostic/ scaling evidence limit empirical credibility of efficiency claims.
- Overall: technically solid and interesting paper that would be stronger with added empirical comparisons and mixing/scaling diagnostics; borderline accept pending those additions.
