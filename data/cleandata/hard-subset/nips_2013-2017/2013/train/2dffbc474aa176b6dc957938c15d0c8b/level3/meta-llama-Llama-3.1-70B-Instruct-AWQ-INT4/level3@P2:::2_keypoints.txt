- Proposes a fully Bayesian approach for inference and learning in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs) using particle MCMC.  
- Main technical contribution: a tailored particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) algorithm for efficient inference in GP-SSMs.  
- Develops a sparse GP-SSM variant to reduce computational complexity.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a thorough introduction to GP-SSMs and associated inference challenges.  
- Presents a detailed description and implementation of the PGAS algorithm.  
- Experimental results on two benchmark systems demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness.  
- Omits empirical comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes (e.g., PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling).  
- Lacks informative diagnostics (e.g., ACF or ESS plots as functions of particle count N and time horizon T) to assess scaling and sampler performance.  
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis and experiments supporting the claims.  
- Would benefit from a more detailed discussion of limitations and avenues for future research.  
- Original contribution: novel application of particle MCMC to GP-SSMs and the sparse GP-SSM variant.  
- Significant work addressing an important problem with potential broad impact.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally, provided the authors address omitted comparisons and add informative diagnostics.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; reviewer confidence: 8/10.
