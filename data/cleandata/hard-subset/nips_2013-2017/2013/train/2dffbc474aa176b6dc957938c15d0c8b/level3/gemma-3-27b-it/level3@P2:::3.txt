This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs), employing Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) to enable efficient inference by marginalizing over the transition dynamics. The core idea is to leverage the flexibility of GPs for modeling nonlinear dynamics within a state-space framework, while addressing the computational challenges through a tailored PMCMC sampler. This builds upon a rich history of work on SSMs (e.g., [10, 11]) and GP-based approaches to dynamical systems ([1, 2, 5, 6, 7, 8]). Specifically, it addresses a key limitation of previous GP-SSM methods – the need for a pre-defined model for the transition dynamics – by marginalizing over the GP function itself. 

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the mathematical formulation of the GP-SSM, the inference procedure, and implementation details, including a sparse GP variant for improved computational efficiency. The authors demonstrate the method on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart-pole system, showcasing its ability to learn dynamics and provide predictive distributions.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The proposed approach is a valuable contribution to the field by offering a fully Bayesian treatment of GP-SSMs with marginalization of the transition dynamics. This expands the capabilities of GP-SSMs beyond methods requiring pre-defined parametric models.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations appear correct, and the use of PMCMC, combined with efficient implementation strategies (sparse GPs, reuse of Cholesky factorizations), is a reasonable approach to tackle the computational challenges.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and the mathematical notation is clear. The inclusion of the generative model diagram (Figure 1b) is helpful.
* **Potential Significance:** The method has the potential to be useful for modeling complex dynamical systems in various domains where nonparametric flexibility and full Bayesian inference are desirable. The demonstrated application to a cart-pole system suggests real-world applicability.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Lack of Rigorous Evaluation of PMCMC Performance:** While the authors mention good mixing enabled by recent PMCMC developments [4], the paper lacks a detailed discussion of the practical challenges of running the PMCMC sampler. Important aspects such as sensitivity to initialization, convergence diagnostics, and computational cost for different problem settings are not thoroughly addressed.  The reviewer is left wondering how robust this method is in practice.
* **Insufficient Critical Evaluation:** The paper lacks a comprehensive evaluation of the method's strengths and weaknesses.  For example, it would be beneficial to discuss the impact of the number of particles (N) on the accuracy and computational cost. Similarly, a sensitivity analysis of the hyper-parameters would be valuable.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The experimental section, while demonstrating the method's feasibility, lacks a rigorous empirical evaluation. The RMSE comparison in the nonlinear system benchmark is useful, but a more in-depth analysis, including comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., those based on variational inference), would strengthen the findings. The cart-pole example, while illustrative, lacks quantitative evaluation.
* **Missing Discussion of Identifiability:** Given the nonparametric nature of the GP prior, the paper does not adequately address potential issues related to identifiability.  How does the method handle situations where multiple functions could explain the observed data?
* **Incremental Nature:** The work is, in some sense, a direct application of existing PMCMC methodology to a new problem setting. The authors acknowledge this, but the paper doesn’t fully articulate the specific innovations *within* the PMCMC application that are crucial for its success.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a technically sound and novel approach to GP-SSM inference.
* The method has the potential to advance the state-of-the-art in modeling nonlinear dynamical systems.
* The paper is clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a detailed evaluation of the PMCMC sampler’s practical performance is a significant concern.
* The empirical evaluation is limited and doesn’t provide strong evidence of superior performance compared to existing methods.
* The paper would benefit from a more critical discussion of the method’s limitations and potential challenges.




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid contribution to the field, but its impact is somewhat limited by the lack of a thorough evaluation of the PMCMC algorithm and a more comprehensive empirical validation.  While the technical aspects are sound, the reviewer is left wanting more evidence of the method’s robustness and scalability.  Given the potential of the approach, I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above by providing a more detailed discussion of the PMCMC sampler’s performance, adding more rigorous empirical comparisons, and addressing the potential issue of identifiability.
