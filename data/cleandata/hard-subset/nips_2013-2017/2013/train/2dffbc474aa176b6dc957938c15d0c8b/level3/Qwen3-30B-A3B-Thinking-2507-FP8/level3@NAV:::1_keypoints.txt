- Presents a fully Bayesian method for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using GP priors over transition dynamics and PMCMC with ancestor sampling (PGAS).
- Novel marginalization of the transition function preserves full GP expressivity while enabling joint state and parameter estimation.
- Method differs from prior work that used parametric transitions or finite GP approximations, allowing accurate smoothing without fixed transition models.
- Technically sound presentation of marginalization via ancestor sampling, effectively handling induced non-Markovian dependencies.
- Uses computational strategies (sparse GPs with FIC and low-rank Cholesky updates) to reduce complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2 T).
- Implementation details justify complexity reductions and practical efficiency considerations.
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world systems demonstrate effectiveness, but evaluation lacks out-of-sample forecasting metrics.
- Strength: theoretical novelty in online marginalization via ancestor sampling and clear exposition.
- Strength: thorough treatment of computational considerations and strong positioning within PMCMC literature.
- Weakness: missing explicit comparison between using parametric GP means versus full GP modeling and forecasting implications.
- Weakness: ambiguity in Table 1 about whether RMSE values are in-sample or out-of-sample.
- Weakness: abbreviation CPF-AS is used before definition; empirical analysis could expand on sparse GP performance trade-offs.
- Recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the above issues.
