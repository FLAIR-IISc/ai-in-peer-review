- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition f, yielding a non-Markovian prior over latent trajectories and sampling x0:T, θ with PGAS.
- Introduces a sparse FIC variant and efficient Cholesky rank-1 updates/downdates to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).
- Conceptual advance: collapsing f preserves nonparametric expressivity while enabling coherent Bayesian learning and smoothing.
- Technical soundness: derivations of the collapsed prior and PGAS invariance are correct and well-cited; hyperparameter updates follow standard GP conditioning.
- Practicality: careful attention to computational efficiency and implementation details makes the method usable in practice.
- Clarity: exposition is generally clear and makes the non-Markovian inference problem and algorithmic choices transparent.
- Empirical scope is limited; needs comparisons to EM/variational GP-SSM baselines (e.g., GPDM, EP-based) and parametric particle smoothers.
- Evaluation metrics are unclear: Table 1 should separate in-sample vs out-of-sample and add forecast RMSE, predictive log-likelihood, and calibration/coverage.
- Role of the mean function needs discussion and ablation (zero/linear mean) to assess extrapolation, misspecification, and identifiability issues.
- Algorithmic details missing: define CPF-AS on first use; report sensitivity to N (particles), L (MCMC samples), and M (inducing points); include runtime/memory scaling and mixing diagnostics.
- Modeling choices need brief discussion: kernel selection, priors on Q and kernel hyperparameters, and risks when both transition and measurement models are highly flexible.
- Quality: technically careful and correct; sparse GP integration is standard but well-integrated and claims are supported, though broader baselines would strengthen confidence.
- Clarity issues: some abbreviations (CPF-AS/PGAS) appear before definitions; adding an algorithm box for CPF-AS would aid reproducibility.
- Originality: high — marginalizing f and applying PGAS to the resulting non-Markovian GP-SSM is a novel contribution for the venue.
- Significance: significant potential impact for time series, control, and Bayesian inference communities by enabling fully Bayesian nonparametric SSM learning.
- Pros summarized: fully Bayesian nonparametric learning, effective PGAS use, computational thoughtfulness, and clear exposition with implementation guidance.
- Cons summarized: limited empirical comparisons and metrics, minor presentation issues, and limited discussion of sensitivity and scaling.
- Questions for authors: clarify Table 1 RMSE in-/out-of-sample and add held-out RMSE/log-likelihood and coverage.
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity to mean function and kernel (ablations: zero/linear mean; RBF vs ARD).
- Questions for authors: provide practical limits and wall-clock/memory scaling with T, state dimension, N, and M.
- Questions for authors: compare results to GPDM/EM and EP/variational GP-SSMs on benchmarks.
- Minor comments: define CPF-AS at first use, add mixing diagnostics and hyperparameter acceptance stats, and discuss extensions to missing or non-Gaussian observations.
- Recommendation: lean accept — strong, novel, technically sound paper with empirical evaluation that could be expanded.
- Reviewer confidence: high.
