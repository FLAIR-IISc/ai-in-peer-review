- Proposes a novel Bayesian inference method for GP-SSMs using particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs) that marginalizes the transition function.  
- Develops a sparse GP-SSM variant to improve computational efficiency.  
- Method is well-motivated, technically sound, and an original application of particle MCMC to GP-SSMs.  
- Writing and organization are exceptionally clear, making the complex methodology accessible.  
- Major omission: no empirical comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, notably PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.  
- Including PMMH and backward-sampling particle Gibbs comparisons would be straightforward and important for contextualizing performance.  
- Lacks informative diagnostics (e.g., ACF, ESS) to assess mixing and scaling behavior with particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Demonstrating scaling (e.g., PMMH’s particle count growth vs. particle Gibbs sublinear growth) empirically would strengthen claims.  
- Experiments are illustrative but need deeper analysis of approximation quality beyond RMSE.  
- Should report sensitivity to hyperparameters and performance across varying underlying system complexity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty (marginalizing transition), clarity, theoretical soundness, and potential broad impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing crucial comparisons, insufficient convergence/scale diagnostics, and limited empirical analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: a valuable contribution but should be accepted only after adding requested comparisons and diagnostics (ACF/ESS vs. N and T).
