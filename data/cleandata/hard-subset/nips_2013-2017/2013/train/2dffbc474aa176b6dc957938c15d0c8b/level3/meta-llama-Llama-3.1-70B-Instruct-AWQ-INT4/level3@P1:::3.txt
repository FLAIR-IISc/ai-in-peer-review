This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs) and particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) samplers. The authors aim to infer the state transition function and the state trajectory in a flexible and nonparametric manner, allowing for complex dynamical phenomena to be captured.

The main idea of the paper is to place a Gaussian process prior over the state transition dynamics, resulting in a flexible model that can adapt to the complexity of the data. The authors then use a PMCMC sampler to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution, which is the joint distribution of the state trajectory and the hyper-parameters of the model. The PMCMC sampler is used to marginalize over the state transition function, allowing for direct inference of the state trajectory.

The paper is well-motivated, and the use of GP-SSMs and PMCMC samplers is a natural choice for this problem. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the background and related work, and the technical sections are well-organized and easy to follow.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. Firstly, the paper is a straightforward application of PMCMC methodology, and the authors do not provide any significant new insights or contributions to the field. Secondly, the paper fails to properly describe and evaluate the practical challenges of getting the PMCMC scheme to work. The authors do not provide a detailed, critical evaluation of the method's strengths and weaknesses, and the experimental section is limited to a few examples that demonstrate the approach but do not provide a rigorous empirical evaluation.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors have made a good effort to provide a clear and concise presentation of their work. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed evaluation of the method's performance and limitations. The authors should also consider providing more information about the practical challenges of implementing the PMCMC scheme and how they overcame these challenges.

In terms of originality, the paper is not particularly original, as it is a straightforward application of existing methodology. However, the authors do provide some new insights into the use of GP-SSMs and PMCMC samplers for state-space models, and the paper is well-motivated and clearly written.
