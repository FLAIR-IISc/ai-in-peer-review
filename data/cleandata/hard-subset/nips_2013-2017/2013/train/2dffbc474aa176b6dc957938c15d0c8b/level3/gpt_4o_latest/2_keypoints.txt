- Introduces a novel application of particle MCMC (PGAS) to Gaussian Process State-Space Models with a fully Bayesian treatment.  
- Proposes a sparse GP-SSM methodology to reduce computational complexity while retaining model expressivity.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on nonlinear benchmarks and a cart-and-pole system, handling multimodal smoothing and uncertainty-aware predictions.  
- Tailors a PGAS sampler for GP-SSMs, advancing beyond conventional parametric approaches.  
- Provides detailed theoretical derivations for marginalizing the state transition function and associated inference algorithms.  
- Method is practically relevant, capable of handling challenging scenarios including high-dimensional state spaces.  
- Allows incorporation of prior knowledge via the GP mean function to improve performance.  
- Lacks comparisons to alternative particle MCMC schemes (e.g., PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling).  
- Missing key performance metrics such as ACF plots and ESS as functions of particle count and time steps.  
- No empirical analysis of scaling behavior with respect to number of particles (N) and time horizon (T).  
- Limited benchmarking against other GP-SSM methods or state-of-the-art Bayesian filtering/smoothing approaches.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include comparative analyses, performance metrics, and scaling experiments.
