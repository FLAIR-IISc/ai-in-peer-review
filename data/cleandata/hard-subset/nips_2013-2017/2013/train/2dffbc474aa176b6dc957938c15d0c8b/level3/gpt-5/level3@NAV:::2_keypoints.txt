- Problem and approach: Fully Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs by marginalizing the transition f and sampling joint smoothing via particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) implemented as CPF-AS; sparse FIC reduces cost from O(T^3) to O(M^2T).

- Novelty: First application of PGAS to collapsed GP-SSMs and introduction of a sparse GP-SSM variant.

- Technical quality: Methodology is sound with clear derivations and practical implementation details (rank-1 updates, Cholesky reuse); FIC choice is well motivated.

- Missing baselines: Experimental evaluation omits essential PMCMC baselines (particle marginal MH, PMMH, and particle Gibbs with backward sampling, PG-BS).

- Missing diagnostics: Lacks MCMC diagnostics such as autocorrelation functions and ESS vs particle count N and time horizon T, ideally normalized by wall-clock time.

- Scaling claims: Paper asserts favorable scaling (e.g., on required N vs T) but provides insufficient empirical evidence to support those claims.

- Clarity: Paper is clearly written and reproducible; should explicitly cite/discuss PG-BS and clarify which claims are empirical versus theoretical.

- Significance: Provides a valuable fully Bayesian alternative to EM/MAP for GP-SSMs and the sparse construction meaningfully increases applicability.

- Pros: Careful, practical integration of PGAS with GP-SSMs; compelling sparse complexity reduction; demonstrated on non-toy problems (cart-pole).

- Cons: No PMMH/PG-BS comparisons; no ACF/ESS-vs-(N,T) or runtime-per-ESS scaling plots; limited sensitivity analyses (inducing point number/placement).

- Overall: Well-written and promising paper; inclusion of PMCMC comparisons and convergence/scale diagnostics would substantially strengthen impact.
