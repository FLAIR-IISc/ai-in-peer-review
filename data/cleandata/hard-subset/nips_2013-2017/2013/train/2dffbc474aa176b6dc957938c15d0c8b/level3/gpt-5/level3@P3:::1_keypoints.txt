- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that analytically marginalizes the transition f, inducing non‑Markovian latent dependencies.  
- Performs joint state and parameter inference via Particle MCMC, specifically particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) using CPF-AS.  
- Avoids sampling/representing f during inference, reducing x–f coupling in MCMC.  
- Introduces a sparse GP (FIC) variant and exploits low‑rank/Cholesky updates for major computational savings.  
- Novelty: collapsed GP-SSM combined with PGAS for non‑Markovian latent processes is a new, useful synthesis relative to prior EM/MAP and post‑learning filtering work.  
- Technical derivations of the collapsed marginal prior and use in CPF-AS/PGAS are sound and grounded in PMCMC theory.  
- Complexity claims (vanilla O(T^3), FIC O(M^2T)) and implementation tricks (rank‑1 updates/downdates) are credible and practically important.  
- Experiments (multimodal smoothing; cart‑and‑pole) show learned dynamics and calibrated uncertainty but are brief.  
- Reproducibility: algorithmic description is sufficiently clear for experts, given cited PGAS/CPF‑AS literature.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized; collapsing exposition and CPF‑AS for non‑Markovian models is accessible.  
- Minor clarity issues: CPF‑AS is used before definition; Table 1 unclear on in‑ vs out‑of‑sample RMSE; need schematic for computational reuse.  
- Main strength: elegant collapsed Bayesian treatment with GP priors over dynamics.  
- Strength: use of PGAS with ancestor sampling to restore mixing in non‑Markovian latent models.  
- Strength: careful computational engineering (sparsity, low‑rank updates) enabling scalability.  
- Strength: clear presentation and useful connections to prior work.  
- Weakness: limited empirical breadth—missing out‑of‑sample forecasting, ablations, sensitivity analyses, and wall‑clock comparisons.  
- Suggestion: add explicit out‑of‑sample 1‑ and k‑step forecast RMSE and clarify dataset splits in Table 1.  
- Suggestion: include ablations (with/without sparse GP, ancestor sampling), sensitivity to particle count N and PGAS iterations, and timing comparisons.  
- Suggestion: expand modeling discussion on parametric transition vs GP mean function and implications for extrapolation and bias‑variance tradeoffs.  
- Suggestion: discuss scalability to higher state dimensions and memory costs; consider structured kernels and inducing‑point strategies.  
- Minor fixes: define CPF‑AS at first mention, clarify Q non‑diagonality and Λ block‑diagonalization, and state how inducing inputs are selected/updated.  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive is mixing to N and degree of nonlinearity/multimodality and what diagnostics do you recommend?  
- Question: for forecasting, do you propagate full mixture‑of‑Gaussians predictive transitions or approximate with a single GP surrogate, and what are the runtime tradeoffs?  
- Question: how does performance degrade for higher state dimensions (d ≥ 10), and any practical tips on kernels or sparsity levels?  
- Pros for acceptance: technically strong, novel, well‑executed, and practically relevant with likely follow‑on impact.  
- Cons for acceptance: empirical breadth limited and a few clarity/evaluation protocol issues that need fixing.  
- Overall: strong, novel paper recommending acceptance pending modest empirical additions and minor clarifications.
