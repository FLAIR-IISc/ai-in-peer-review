- Presents a fully Bayesian framework for GP-SSMs that marginalizes the unknown dynamics function, inducing non-Markovian temporal dependencies.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to perform inference in the resulting non-Markovian model.  
- Novel departure from prior GP-SSM work that relied on parametric approximations or point estimates, allowing full posterior inference over state trajectories.  
- Technically strong and clearly presented, with an elegant inference scheme that maintains nonparametric expressivity.  
- Identifies and addresses computational bottlenecks (O(T^3)) via efficient low-rank Cholesky updates and sparse GP approximations.  
- Empirical evaluation is brief and could be expanded beyond the nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole experiments.  
- Table 1 and preceding text unclear whether reported RMSE is in-sample reconstruction or out-of-sample prediction.  
- Recommend adding multi-step-ahead forecast RMSE on a held-out dataset to assess generalization.  
- Suggest discussing implications of using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean function for forecasting and model misspecification.  
- Minor issue: abbreviation "CPF-AS" used in Algorithm 1 before being formally defined.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, computationally robust paper; recommended for acceptance.
