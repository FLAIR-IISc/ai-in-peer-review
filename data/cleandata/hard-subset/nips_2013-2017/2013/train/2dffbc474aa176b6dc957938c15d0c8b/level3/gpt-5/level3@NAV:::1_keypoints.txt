- Presents a fully Bayesian method for joint state and hyperparameter inference in GP state-space models by marginalizing the GP transition f and sampling trajectories from the resulting non‑Markovian smoothing distribution with PGAS.
- Key idea: collapse out the GP transition to obtain a trajectory-only model, then use ancestor sampling to preserve mixing despite induced temporal dependencies.
- Introduces computational strategies (sparse GP via FIC; low‑rank Cholesky updates/downdates) yielding O(T^3) for full GPs and O(M^2 T) for sparse variants.
- Positions the work relative to prior art: goes beyond GPDM and EM/MAP approaches by providing a fully Bayesian treatment with online marginalization, and complements EP/approximate filtering/smoothing methods by directly addressing learning.
- Methodology is technically sound and carefully derived; collapsing argument, non‑Markovian SMC construction, and PGAS invariance are well used.
- Computational section is thoughtful and practically relevant.
- Empirical validation is concise and adequate but could be expanded with more experiments and ablations.
- Paper is clearly written and accessible, with minor clarity issues (CPF‑AS abbreviation appears before definition; Table 1 unclear on in‑sample vs out‑of‑sample RMSE).
- Main novelty: online marginalization of transition dynamics combined with ancestor sampling for smoothing in non‑Markovian latent models.
- Significance: likely to stimulate further work on inference in non‑Markovian SSMs and useful to practitioners needing Bayesian dynamics learning with calibrated uncertainty.
- Suggestions: contrast using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean and discuss forecasting/extrapolation implications.
- Suggestions: clarify Table 1 (in‑sample vs out‑of‑sample) and add an explicit out‑of‑sample/forecast RMSE column.
- Suggestions: report runtime and scaling with T, N, M, and provide mixing diagnostics (ESS, autocorrelation).
- Suggestions: include more baselines and ablations (mean function choice, sparse vs full GP, sensitivity to ancestor sampling).
- Arguments for acceptance: strong, novel inference scheme; solid technical development; computationally efficient implementations; clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical breadth and ablations, and minor clarity/evaluation protocol issues.
