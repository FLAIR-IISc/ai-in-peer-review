- Core idea: marginalize the GP transition function in GP-SSMs using Particle MCMC to retain nonparametric expressivity.  
- Motivation: well-motivated extension building on SMC/MCMC state estimation literature and recent PMCMC samplers.  
- Contribution: development of a sparse GP-SSM variant to improve computational efficiency for complex systems.  
- Writing and presentation: clear, logically structured, and detailed for readers familiar with GPs and SSMs.  
- Empirical demonstration: illustrative examples (nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole) effectively show potential.  
- Limitations acknowledged: authors discuss shortcomings and future research directions.  
- Major weakness: no comparative analysis with other particle MCMC schemes, notably PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.  
- Scaling concern: missing evaluation of expected scaling behavior (sublinear for proposed method vs quadratic for PMMH).  
- Diagnostic gap: lack of performance diagnostics (e.g., autocorrelation functions, Effective Sample Size) as functions of particle count and time horizon.  
- Arguments for acceptance: original application of particle MCMC to GP-SSMs, sound sparse-method development, clear writing, and illustrative results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of key baselines and insufficient diagnostic analysis to assess scaling and mixing.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding comparative results and detailed performance diagnostics.
