- Proposes a Bayesian PMCMC with ancestor sampling for joint state and parameter estimation in GP-SSMs, marginalizing the transition function.  
- Paper is technically sound with a clear, accessible presentation and a thorough introduction to GP-SSM challenges.  
- Novelty in online marginalization of transition dynamics via ancestor sampling.  
- Strong attention to computational efficiency (sparsity, low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates).  
- Empirical results show effectiveness on a nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole system but are brief.  
- Needs expanded empirical comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Discussion of using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean is limited and should address forecasting/out-of-sample implications.  
- Table 1 and preceding text do not clarify whether RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample; add an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column.  
- Abbreviation CPF-AS is used before being defined; define it earlier to avoid confusion.  
- Overall a strong, novel contribution likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and technically sound approach; good computational considerations; sufficient empirical demos; well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical comparisons; need deeper discussion on parametric vs GP mean; unclear RMSE reporting.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8.5/10.
