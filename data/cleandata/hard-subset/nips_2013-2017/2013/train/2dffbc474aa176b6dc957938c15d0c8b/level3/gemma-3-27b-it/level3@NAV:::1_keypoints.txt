- Novel Bayesian approach using GP priors over transition dynamics and PMCMC with ancestor sampling for fully Bayesian inference in nonlinear, nonparametric state-space models.
- Marginalizing the transition dynamics enables learning without committing to a specific parametric form.
- Distinguishes itself from finite GP approximations (e.g., Turner et al., 2012) by fully marginalizing the transition function.
- Provides a method for learning GP dynamics needed by prior works (Deisenroth et al., 2011, 2013).
- Key technical contributions: online marginalization via ancestor sampling and computational efficiencies via sparsity and Cholesky updates.
- Presentation is clear and makes complex topics accessible.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and should include more complex datasets and broader baseline comparisons.
- Paper should include a deeper comparison of using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean to clarify forecasting and out-of-sample behavior.
- Table 1 lacks an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column, hindering assessment of predictive performance.
- Minor stylistic issue: CPF-AS abbreviation is used before being fully defined.
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions addressing empirical and comparison gaps.
