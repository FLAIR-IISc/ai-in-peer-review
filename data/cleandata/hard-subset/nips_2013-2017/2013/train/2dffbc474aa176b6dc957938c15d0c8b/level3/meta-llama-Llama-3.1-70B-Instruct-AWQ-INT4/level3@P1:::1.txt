The paper "Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC" presents a novel approach to Bayesian inference and learning in state-space models with Gaussian process (GP) priors over the transition dynamics. The authors propose a particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithm with ancestor sampling to efficiently sample from the joint smoothing distribution of the states and hyper-parameters, while marginalizing over the transition dynamics function. This approach is technically sound and well-presented, making it accessible to readers familiar with Bayesian inference and state-space models.

One of the key contributions of the paper is the online marginalization of the transition dynamics via ancestor sampling, which enables efficient inference in non-Markovian state-space models. The authors also consider computational efficiencies, such as sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates, to reduce the computational complexity of the algorithm.

The empirical results presented in the paper are brief but sufficient to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. However, it would be beneficial to expand on these results, including more detailed comparisons with other methods and a more thorough evaluation of the performance of the algorithm in different scenarios.

One potential area for discussion is the comparison between using a parametric transition model a priori versus using it as the GP mean. The implications of this choice for forecasting and out-of-sample performance are not fully explored in the paper, and a more detailed analysis of this aspect would be valuable.

Specifically, it is unclear whether the RMSE values reported are in-sample or out-of-sample, and adding an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the algorithm's performance.
