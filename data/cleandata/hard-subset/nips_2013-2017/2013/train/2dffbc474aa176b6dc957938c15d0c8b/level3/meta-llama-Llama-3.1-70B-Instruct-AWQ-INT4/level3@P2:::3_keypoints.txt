- Proposes a fully Bayesian method for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using a Gaussian process prior over transitions and PMCMC to marginalize the transition function.
- Differs from prior GP-SSM work (Deisenroth et al., Turner et al.) by avoiding a parametric transition representation and marginalizing the function directly.
- Main contribution: a tailored PMCMC algorithm that efficiently samples the smoothing distribution while marginalizing the GP transition.
- Empirical demonstration on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart-and-pole system.
- Paper lacks a detailed, critical evaluation of the method’s strengths and weaknesses.
- Experimental section is limited and does not provide a rigorous empirical assessment of performance.
- Technically sound: theoretical analysis and presented experiments support the claims.
- Missing practical implementation details (e.g., hyperparameter choices, number of particles, tuning difficulties).
- Clearly written, logical organization, and well-defined notation.
- Certain sections (notably experiments) should be expanded to provide deeper insights.
- Originality: novel application of PMCMC to GP-SSMs but builds on existing methods and is not fundamentally new.
- Significance: potential to enable fully Bayesian inference in nonlinear nonparametric SSMs with broad application areas.
- Pros for acceptance: technically sound approach, efficient PMCMC, demonstrated on two systems.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient critical evaluation, limited experiments, lacking practical guidance for implementation.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add practical details and expand empirical evaluation.
