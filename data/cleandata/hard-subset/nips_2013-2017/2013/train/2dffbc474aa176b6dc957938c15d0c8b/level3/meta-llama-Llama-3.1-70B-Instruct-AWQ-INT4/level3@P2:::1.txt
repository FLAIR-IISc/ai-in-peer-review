This paper presents a Bayesian approach to inference and learning in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSM), which is a generalization of standard parametric state-space models. The authors propose a fully Bayesian approach to joint state and parameter estimation, where a Gaussian process prior is placed over the transition dynamics. The key contribution of this work is the development of a particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithm with ancestor sampling, which enables efficient inference over the states and hyper-parameters while marginalizing out the transition dynamics.

The authors provide a clear and accessible presentation of their method, which is technically sound. The use of PMCMC with ancestor sampling is a novel approach to online marginalization of transition dynamics, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in several experiments. The consideration of computational efficiencies, including sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates, is also a significant contribution.

The empirical results presented in the paper are brief but sufficient, and they demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in learning nonlinear state-space models. However, it would be beneficial to expand on these results, including more detailed comparisons with other methods and a more thorough evaluation of the proposed approach.

One potential area for improvement is the discussion of using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean. The authors mention that the GP-SSM can make use of a parametric model as a baseline, but it would be useful to explore the implications of this approach for forecasting and out-of-sample performance.

Additionally, the table and text describing the results in Section 6.1 are unclear regarding whether the RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample. It would be beneficial to add an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column to the table to provide a clearer understanding of the proposed method's performance.

Finally, there is a minor issue with the abbreviation CPF-AS being used before it is defined.

Overall, this is a strong and novel paper that is likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models. The authors' approach to joint state and parameter estimation using PMCMC with ancestor sampling is a significant contribution to the field, and their consideration of computational efficiencies is a notable strength.
