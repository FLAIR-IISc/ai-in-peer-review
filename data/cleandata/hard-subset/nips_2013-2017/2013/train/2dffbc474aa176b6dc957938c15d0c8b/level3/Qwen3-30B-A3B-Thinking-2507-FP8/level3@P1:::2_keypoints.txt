- Paper introduces a fully Bayesian inference framework for GP-SSMs by marginalizing the GP transition function and applying Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) for joint smoothing p(x0:T | y0:T).  
- The method preserves nonparametric GP expressivity while avoiding parametric GP approximations and their pitfalls (non-identifiability, overfitting).  
- A sparse variant using the FIC prior reduces complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) with M ≪ T.  
- Experiments on a nonlinear benchmark and a cart-pole model show accurate state smoothing and learned predictive dynamics.  
- Strength: exceptionally clear, well-structured exposition of marginalization and PGAS implementation.  
- Strength: compelling motivation for marginalization and solid theoretical grounding in GP priors and PMCMC literature.  
- Strength: novel adaptation of PGAS/CPF-AS to non-Markovian GP-SSM trajectories, filling a gap in fully Bayesian nonparametric dynamics learning.  
- Strength: practical sparse GP-SSM implementation and cart-pole results that illustrate uncertainty quantification useful for control.  
- Weakness: no comparative experiments with other PMCMC schemes (PMMH, PBGS), which are straightforward and essential for assessing scalability and efficiency.  
- Weakness: lack of MCMC diagnostics (ESS, ACF, trace plots) and wall-clock timing to support claims of “very good mixing” and computational trade-offs.  
- Weakness: sparse FIC extension is a direct application of standard sparse GP methods and its novelty is overstated.  
- Weakness: paper fails to quantify accuracy–cost trade-off for sparsity (no RMSE vs M) and the cart-pole experiment does not justify the need for sparsity.  
- Weakness: multimodal smoothing example lacks diagnostics confirming PGAS explores multiple modes adequately.  
- Recommendation: technically strong and potentially impactful but should be revised to include PMCMC comparisons and convergence diagnostics; accept conditionally pending these additions.
