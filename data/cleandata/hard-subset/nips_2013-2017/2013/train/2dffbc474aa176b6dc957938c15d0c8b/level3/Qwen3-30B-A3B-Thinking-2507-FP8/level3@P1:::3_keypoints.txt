- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP state-space model using PMCMC to marginalize the transition function and infer the smoothing distribution.  
- Builds on recent PMCMC and GP-SSM work to learn nonparametric dynamics rather than using parametric approximations.  
- Strength: theoretically sound marginalization that preserves nonparametric expressivity and reduces strong dependencies between states and dynamics.  
- Strength: uses sparse GPs to reduce computational complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2 T), which is practically significant.  
- Strength: experiments on nonlinear and cart-pole systems show the method captures complex dynamics and multimodal smoothing distributions with uncertainty estimates.  
- Weakness: application of PMCMC is a straightforward extension of existing methods, not a novel PMCMC contribution.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed, critical evaluation of practical implementation challenges (e.g., parameter sensitivity, convergence diagnostics).  
- Weakness: computational cost discussion is limited to theoretical bounds; empirical runtime and resource usage are not reported.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited—Table 1 compares RMSE on a single benchmark without statistical tests or broader analysis.  
- Weakness: scalability questions are unanswered—no analysis of dimensionality, required particle numbers, or robustness to mean-function misspecification.  
- Weakness: sparse GP implementation details are insufficient for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: contribution is mainly methodological and incremental, not advancing PMCMC or GP modeling theory significantly.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well organized with appropriate notation but omits practical guidance and limitations.  
- Suggested improvements: include convergence diagnostics, diagnostic plots, detailed comparisons with alternatives, failure-case analysis, and clearer differentiation from prior GP-SSM work.
