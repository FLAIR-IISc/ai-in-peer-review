- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing GP priors on transition dynamics and marginalizing out the function.
- Uses a tailored Particle MCMC sampler with ancestor sampling (PGAS) to infer the joint smoothing distribution while preserving nonparametric expressivity.
- Extends recent PMCMC methods to non-Markovian state-space models, addressing path degeneracy in standard SMC.
- Provides a clear derivation of the non-Gaussian prior over state trajectories and rigorous theoretical development.
- Includes computational complexity analysis and a sparse GP implementation to address practical scalability.
- Exceptionally clear writing and accessible mathematical exposition, aided by informative figures.
- Novel contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics via ancestor sampling, distinct from prior parametric or fixed-transition approaches.
- Significant impact potential for Bayesian inference in nonlinear dynamical systems (robotics, control, time series).
- Demonstrates practical utility on synthetic and cart-pole experiments.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation lacks explicit out-of-sample forecasting metrics and RMSE clarity.
- Weakness: discussion comparing using a parametric model a priori versus as the GP mean could be expanded.
- Minor issue: abbreviation "CPF-AS" is used before being defined.
- Pros: novel marginalization approach, rigorous theory, efficient implementation, demonstrated applicability, strong ties to PMCMC literature.
- Cons: limited out-of-sample evaluation, incomplete parametric-vs-GP-mean comparison, minor notation inconsistency.
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, and significant paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions for empirical/clarity issues.
