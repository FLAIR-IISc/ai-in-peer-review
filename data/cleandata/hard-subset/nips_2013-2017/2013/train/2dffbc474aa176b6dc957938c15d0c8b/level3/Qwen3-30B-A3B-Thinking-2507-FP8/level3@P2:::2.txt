

# Review for NIPS 2013

This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs), focusing on marginalizing the state transition function to enable efficient inference using Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) methods. The authors develop a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm that samples from the joint smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the transition dynamics, avoiding the need for parametric approximations of the GP. The paper also introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computational complexity.

The work relates to previous NIPS contributions on GP-based SSMs, including Deisenroth et al. (2011) who developed approximation methods for filtering and smoothing with learned GP dynamics, and Turner et al. (2009) who applied EM to learn parametric models with GP structure. The authors correctly position their contribution as providing the missing component for learning the GP model itself, which previous approaches had not addressed. The paper also builds on the theoretical foundations of PMCMC (Andrieu et al., 2010) and Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (Lindsten et al., 2012).

**Strengths:**

*Quality:* The paper is technically sound with well-motivated theoretical development. The marginalization approach is carefully derived, and the sparse GP-SSM variant using FIC is a valuable methodological contribution. The experiments demonstrate the approach's effectiveness on both synthetic and real-world benchmarks.

*Clarity:* The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical formulations are clear and well-explained, with sufficient detail to allow reproducibility. The figures effectively illustrate the key concepts and results.

*Originality:* The application of PGAS to GP-SSMs represents a novel contribution to the field. The authors successfully bridge the gap between GP-based modeling and Bayesian inference in state-space models, which previous work had not adequately addressed. The sparse GP-SSM variant further enhances the practical applicability of the approach.

*Significance:* The method enables fully Bayesian inference in nonparametric state-space models, which is a significant advancement. The ability to learn the dynamics while accounting for uncertainty in the smoothing distribution has clear implications for applications in robotics, control, and time-series analysis.

**Weaknesses:**

*Quality:* The most significant weakness is the lack of comparison to alternative PMCMC schemes, specifically Particle Marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and Particle Gibbs with Backward Sampling (Whiteley et al., 2013). As noted by the reviewer, PMMH is known to require particle count growing quadratically with time horizon T, while particle Gibbs variants typically require sublinear growth. Such comparisons are necessary to properly assess the relative merits of the proposed approach. The paper also lacks informative diagnostics (e.g., ACF, ESS) as functions of particle count N and time horizon T, making it difficult to evaluate the computational scaling of the method.

*Originality:* While the application of PGAS to GP-SSMs is novel, the paper could better distinguish itself from related work on PMCMC methods. The connection to Lindsten et al.'s PGAS algorithm is clear, but the paper misses an opportunity to explicitly compare against other PMCMC variants.

*Significance:* The significance of the work would be substantially enhanced by demonstrating the computational advantages of their approach compared to alternatives, which is currently missing.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Well-written, technically sound contribution to Bayesian inference in state-space models
- Novel application of PMCMC to GP-SSMs that enables fully Bayesian learning
- Sparse GP-SSM variant provides practical computational improvements
- Demonstrated effectiveness on both synthetic and real-world benchmarks

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- Missing critical comparison to alternative PMCMC schemes (PMMH, Particle Gibbs with Backward Sampling)
- Lack of performance diagnostics (ACF, ESS) to evaluate scaling with N and T
- Inability to properly assess computational advantages without these comparisons

**Recommendation:**
The paper presents a technically sound and well-motivated approach to Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs with clear methodological contributions. The work is well-written and addresses an important problem in the field. However, the lack of comparison to alternative PMCMC schemes and the absence of scaling diagnostics significantly limit the paper's impact. These are not minor issues but rather critical omissions that prevent a full assessment of the method's advantages.

The authors could significantly strengthen their paper by implementing these comparisons and diagnostics, which would require minimal additional computational effort. Given the importance of the problem and the quality of the core contribution, I recommend acceptance with the understanding that the paper would be substantially improved with these additions. However, without these comparisons, the paper's contribution is less clear than it could be.

In summary, this is a strong paper that would make a valuable contribution to NIPS if it includes the requested comparisons. As it stands, it's a good paper that could be excellent with minor revisions.
