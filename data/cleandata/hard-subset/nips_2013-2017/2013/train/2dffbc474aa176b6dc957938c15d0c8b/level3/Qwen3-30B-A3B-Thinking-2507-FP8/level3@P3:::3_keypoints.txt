- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function and using Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) for joint smoothing.
- Introduces a sparse GP construction using the FIC prior to reduce computational cost from O(T^3) to O(M^2T).
- Theoretically sound framework that preserves nonparametric expressivity without parametric approximations.
- Appropriate and well-motivated use of PGAS for this inference problem.
- Experimental results on nonlinear and cart-pole benchmarks show ability to capture complex dynamics and uncertainty.
- Main methodological contribution is an application of existing PMCMC methods rather than novel theory.
- Fails to describe or evaluate practical implementation challenges of PMCMC (e.g., sensitivity to particle count N, scaling with time horizon T, tuning difficulties).
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently rigorous: no statistical significance testing, no comparisons to alternative inference methods, and no analysis of mixing properties.
- Lacks reproducibility details and thorough empirical evidence to support claims like “very good mixing.”
- No detailed critical assessment of practical strengths, weaknesses, or computational trade-offs.
- Suggested additional experiments: sensitivity analysis for N, runtime comparison of vanilla vs sparse GP, robustness to different data types/noise levels, and analysis of initialization effects.
- Well-written and organized with clear mathematical formulation and helpful sparse GP implementation details.
- Originality is limited: a natural extension of prior GP-SSM work rather than a major new contribution.
- Potentially significant for flexible dynamical modeling, but impact is limited by weak empirical validation.
- Overall recommendation: reject pending substantial revision to include deeper empirical evaluation and critical assessment of practical challenges.
