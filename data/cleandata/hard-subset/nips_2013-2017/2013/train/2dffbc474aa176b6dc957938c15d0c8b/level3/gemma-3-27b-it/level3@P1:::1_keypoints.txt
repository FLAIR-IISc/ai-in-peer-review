Paper presents a fully Bayesian approach for GP-SSMs that marginalizes the state transition dynamics using a tailored PMCMC sampler with ancestor sampling.  
Method enables a truly nonparametric model for complex dynamical systems without imposing restrictive functional forms.  
Computational efficiency is addressed via sparse GPs (FIC prior) and optimized matrix operations (low-rank Cholesky updates).  
Authors position the work well relative to prior literature (Rasmussen & Williams, Turner et al., Andrieu et al.) and motivate the problem clearly.  
Technical soundness: clear derivations and a well-defined probabilistic framework for marginalizing the transition function.  
Novelty: online marginalization via ancestor sampling is a significant contribution over prior GP-SSM methods.  
Computational efficiency: practical focus makes the method more applicable to real-world problems.  
Clarity: paper is well-written with helpful figures (e.g., Figure 1b) and clear algorithm description.  
Potential significance: method can advance learning and inference for nonlinear dynamical systems and mitigate model misspecification.  
Empirical evaluation is limited; more datasets and comparisons to other state-of-the-art (including other nonparametric-transition baselines) are needed.  
Discussion of using a parametric mean function for the GP is insufficient; trade-offs and effects on forecasting/generalization should be explored.  
RMSE reporting lacks clarity—unclear whether values are in-sample or out-of-sample; add forecast/out-of-sample RMSE.  
Minor notation issue: CPF-AS is introduced before being fully defined.  
Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, technically sound and novel, computationally efficient, clearly written, and likely impactful.  
Arguments against acceptance: empirical evaluation not extensive, parametric mean-function discussion weak, and RMSE reporting ambiguous.  
Overall assessment: strong and novel paper recommended for acceptance, with suggested improvements to empirical evaluation, mean-function discussion, and RMSE clarity.
