- Proposes a fully Bayesian joint state and parameter estimation framework for GP-SSMs, placing a GP prior on transition dynamics.  
- Main technical contribution: PMCMC with ancestor sampling to efficiently infer states and hyper-parameters while marginalizing the transition dynamics.  
- Presentation is clear, accessible, and technically sound.  
- Novel use of PMCMC with ancestor sampling for online marginalization of transition dynamics.  
- Important attention to computational efficiencies (sparsity, low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates).  
- Empirical results show effectiveness but are brief and would benefit from expansion.  
- Paper needs more detailed comparisons with alternative methods and more thorough evaluations.  
- Lacks in-depth discussion of using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean and its forecasting/out-of-sample implications.  
- Section 6.1 RMSE reporting is unclear about in-sample vs out-of-sample; recommend adding a forecast/out-of-sample RMSE column.  
- Minor issue: CPF-AS abbreviation is used before being defined.  
- Overall judgment: strong, novel work likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian SSMs.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8.5/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel, technically sound, effective empirically, computational efficiency, well-written.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: limited empirical breadth, insufficient parametric-model discussion, ambiguity in RMSE reporting.
