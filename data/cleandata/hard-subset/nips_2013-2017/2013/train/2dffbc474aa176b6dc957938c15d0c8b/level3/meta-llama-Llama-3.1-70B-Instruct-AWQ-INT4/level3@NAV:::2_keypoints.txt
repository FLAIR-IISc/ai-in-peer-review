- Proposes a fully Bayesian inference and learning approach for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using GP-SSMs and PMCMC.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant that reduces computational complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2 T) with M << T.  
- Develops a particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) algorithm to sample the smoothing distribution while marginalizing the state transition function.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- The approach is an original application of particle MCMC to GP-SSMs.  
- Theoretical analysis and experimental results support the paper’s claims; method is technically sound.  
- Related work is thoroughly discussed and distinctions from prior contributions are clearly explained.  
- Lacks comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes (e.g., PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling).  
- Results lack informative diagnostics, such as performance graphs versus particle count N and time horizon T to evaluate scaling.  
- Does not provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to include comparisons to other PMCMC methods and more informative diagnostics.
