- Proposes a fully Bayesian approach for GP-SSMs by marginalizing the GP transition and applying Particle MCMC to sample latent states and hyperparameters.  
- Focuses on Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS), extending PMCMC to non-parametric, non-Markovian settings.  
- Introduces a sparse variant using the FIC prior to improve computational scaling.  
- Strength: novel and significant application of PGAS to fully retain non-parametric model expressivity over prior parametric/point-estimate methods.  
- Strength: exceptionally clear and well-written exposition guiding readers through the complex model and inference.  
- Major weakness: lacks empirical comparison to other standard PMCMC algorithms such as PMMH and Particle Gibbs with backward sampling (PGBS).  
- Major weakness: missing informative sampler diagnostics—no analysis of ESS, ACF, or other mixing measures.  
- Needs empirical scaling analysis of performance metrics (e.g., ESS/ACF) as functions of particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Recommendation: include thorough experiments comparing PGAS to PMMH/PGBS and quantitative mixing/scaling studies to fully validate claims.
