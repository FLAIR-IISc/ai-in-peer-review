- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP prior over state transitions and a tailored PMCMC algorithm to marginalize dynamics and infer the joint smoothing distribution.  
- Preserves full nonparametric expressivity of GP state-space models without fixed parametric dynamics.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM literature (Deisenroth, Turner) and addresses gap of fully Bayesian treatment.  
- Strength: theoretically sound approach that marginalizes transition dynamics correctly.  
- Strength: clear motivation and well-explained differences from previous methods.  
- Strength: experiments show ability to capture complex dynamics, including multimodal smoothing.  
- Strength: sparse GP implementation details offer practical computational insights.  
- Weakness: lacks rigorous evaluation of PMCMC implementation (no convergence diagnostics, ESS, or PMCMC variant comparisons).  
- Weakness: originality limited—mainly an application of existing PMCMC methods rather than a novel algorithmic advance.  
- Weakness: insufficient empirical comparison to state-of-the-art Bayesian state-space methods; RMSE baselines are simple parametric models.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical implementation challenges (tuning, degeneracy, sensitivity to particle count, computational cost).  
- Pro-accept: addresses an important problem with a well-motivated, theoretically sound framework and useful sparse GP implementation insights.  
- Con-accept: contribution is largely an application of known methods, experimental evaluation is weak, and practical issues are not critically assessed.  
- Recommended improvements: add PMCMC convergence analysis, compare to established Bayesian SSM methods, detail implementation pitfalls/solutions, and analyze computational requirements.  
- Final recommendation: borderline paper; reviewer recommends rejection pending substantial revisions and stronger empirical and diagnostic evidence.
