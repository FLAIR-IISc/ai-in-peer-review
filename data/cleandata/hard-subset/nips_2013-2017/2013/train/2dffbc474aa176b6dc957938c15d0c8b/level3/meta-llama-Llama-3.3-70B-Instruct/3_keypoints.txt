- Novel method for computing firing rates in tightly balanced networks that avoids linearizing approximations.  
- Technique directly identifies the non-linear relationship between firing rates, connectivity, input, and optimal signal representation.  
- Interprets spiking activity as an algorithm that optimizes a quadratic loss function of firing rates.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and balances theoretical derivations with numerical simulations.  
- Provides detailed analysis of tightly balanced network properties and demonstrates effectiveness in calculating firing rates and tuning curve shapes.  
- Offers a unified framework linking firing rate responses to underlying network mechanisms.  
- Can explain and investigate tuning curve inhomogeneity and its impact on neural computation.  
- Originality comes from a novel combination of quadratic programming and loss-optimization applied to a longstanding problem.  
- Potential broad applicability, from data analysis to studying computational neurodegeneration.  
- Lacks detailed evaluation of implementation challenges, including computational resource requirements and method limitations.  
- Needs more discussion on relationship to prior work in population coding models and spiking networks.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address implementation challenges and contextualize with previous literature.
