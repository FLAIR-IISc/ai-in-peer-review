The paper "Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC" presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models. The authors propose a Gaussian process prior over the state transition dynamics and use a tailored particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithm to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the state transition function.

The paper relates to previous work on Gaussian process-based state-space models, including the work of Deisenroth et al. (2012) and Turner et al. (2010). However, the proposed approach differs from these previous methods in that it does not rely on a parametric representation of the state transition function. Instead, the authors marginalize out the transition dynamics function and infer directly the joint smoothing distribution using PMCMC.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a PMCMC algorithm that can efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the state transition function. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart and pole system.

However, the paper lacks a detailed, critical evaluation of the method's strengths and weaknesses. The authors do not provide a rigorous empirical evaluation of the method's performance, and the experimental section is limited to demonstrating the approach rather than assessing its practical difficulties.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the authors could have provided more details on the practical challenges of getting the PMCMC scheme to work, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the number of particles.

The paper is clearly written, and the organization is logical. The authors provide a good introduction to the problem and the proposed approach, and the notation is well-defined. However, some of the sections, such as the experimental section, could be expanded to provide more details and insights.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a new application of PMCMC methodology to state-space models with Gaussian process priors. However, the approach is not fundamentally new, and the authors build on previous work in the field.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to provide a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models. The proposed approach could be useful in a variety of applications, including robotics, economics, and signal processing.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a technically sound approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models.
* The proposed PMCMC algorithm is efficient and can handle complex state-space models.
* The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach through experiments on two different systems.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper lacks a detailed, critical evaluation of the method's strengths and weaknesses.
* The experimental section is limited, and the authors do not provide a rigorous empirical evaluation of the method's performance.
* The paper could benefit from more details on the practical challenges of getting the PMCMC scheme to work.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should provide more details on the practical challenges of getting the PMCMC scheme to work and expand the experimental section to provide more insights and a rigorous empirical evaluation of the method's performance.
