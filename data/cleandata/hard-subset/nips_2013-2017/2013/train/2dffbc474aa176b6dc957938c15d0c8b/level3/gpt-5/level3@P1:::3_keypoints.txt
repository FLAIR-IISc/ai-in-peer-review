- Fully Bayesian GP-SSM: collapse f and run PMCMC (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling) to infer states x and hyperparameters θ.
- Analytically marginalizing f induces a non-Markovian prior p(x0:T | θ) handled by SMC-within-MCMC.
- Given smoothing samples, posterior over f is available in closed form; sparse FIC variant reduces complexity to O(M^2 T).
- Experiments (bimodal smoothing, 4D cart-pole) show qualitative benefits: multimodality and calibrated uncertainty.
- Builds on established PMCMC/PGAS and GP-SSM literature; main novelty is collapsing f within PMCMC rather than new methodology.
- Practical contributions include complexity analysis, reusable Cholesky updates, and explicit ancestor sampling weights for the collapsed model.
- Use of PGAS is appropriate and theoretically justified for non-Markovian latent models.
- Approach naturally captures multimodal smoothing and propagates uncertainty into learned dynamics (mixture-of-Gaussians behavior).
- Methodological novelty beyond combining existing PMCMC and sparse GP techniques is limited.
- Paper omits discussion of practical challenges: effect of state dimensionality, path degeneracy for long horizons, and stability of rank-one downdates.
- Lacks empirical rigor: no likelihood-based metrics (predictive log-likelihood/marginal likelihood) reported.
- Missing mixing diagnostics: no ACF, ESS, R̂, or similar assessments for x and θ.
- No wall-clock/runtime comparisons to alternatives (e.g., PG with backward simulation, particle EM, variational GP-SSM).
- No ablation studies (PGAS vs PG, collapsed vs non-collapsed, sparse vs dense) to isolate contributions.
- Scalability is claimed but not demonstrated; largest experiment is 4D with T=200 and no runtime/memory scaling study.
- Hyperparameter inference is under-explored: slice sampling not evaluated and coupling between θx and x not analyzed.
- Generality claim (arbitrary measurement models) is unsupported experimentally; only Gaussian-noise cases shown.
- Technical correctness is sound: collapsed trajectory prior derivation, PGAS invariance, and sparse FIC are correctly applied.
- Clarity is good overall but some implementation details need clearer treatment (numerical stability of downdates, log-space weights, memory for per-particle factorizations).
- Originality is moderate: a neat integrative combination but components are individually well-known.
- Significance is potential for the GP-SSM community, but practical impact is uncertain without stronger experiments and scaling studies.
- Suggested experiments: quantitative comparisons to GP-SSM EM/MAP, GPDS approximations, and PMCMC variants; report predictive log-likelihood, smoothing RMSE, rollout error.
- Suggested diagnostics: ESS per iteration, ACF of θ and functionals of x, acceptance rates, and wall-clock time to target error varying N, T, and state dimension.
- Suggested analyses: sensitivity to hyperpriors, mean function, kernel choice, identifiability when g known vs partially known.
- Suggested engineering: thorough scaling and memory profiling, sparse vs dense trade-offs, inducing set selection strategies.
- Suggested numerical robustness: discuss/ensure stability of rank-one updates/downdates and compute weights in log-space to mitigate degeneracy for long T.
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptually clean, fully Bayesian treatment with theoretical justification and a practical sparse alternative; qualitative demos.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited methodological novelty, insufficient empirical rigor and diagnostics, missing baselines and ablations, unclear scalability.
- Overall recommendation: lean toward rejection in current form due to lacking empirical evaluation and practical discussion, but promising if strengthened.
