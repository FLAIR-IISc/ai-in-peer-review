- Introduces a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the GP transition function, yielding a non-Markovian state sequence.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample the joint posterior over states and hyperparameters without parametric GP approximations.  
- Reduces computational complexity via sparse GPs (FIC) and efficient Cholesky updates from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Novel and significant: marginalizing dynamics and inferring directly on non-Markovian trajectories is a major conceptual advance.  
- Technically rigorous and well-motivated: solid understanding and elegant application of GP and advanced SMC/MCMC methods.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and derivations are presented precisely.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; needs experiments on higher-dimensional or real-world datasets to better demonstrate benefits and robustness.  
- Ambiguity in reported RMSE (Table 1): unclear whether results are one-step-ahead out-of-sample or in-sample smoothing; recommend clarifying and adding multi-step forecasting metrics.  
- Would benefit from deeper discussion on using a parametric GP mean (trade-offs, misspecification effects, long-term forecasting behavior).  
- Minor issue: abbreviation "CPF-AS" appears before formal definition; fix ordering/definition.  
- Pro-accept: novel principled Bayesian method, strong technical quality, practical computational considerations, and high potential impact.  
- Con-accept: limited experimental scope and ambiguous reporting of key results.  
- Overall conclusion: strong paper with minor experimental/clarity issues; recommend acceptance.
