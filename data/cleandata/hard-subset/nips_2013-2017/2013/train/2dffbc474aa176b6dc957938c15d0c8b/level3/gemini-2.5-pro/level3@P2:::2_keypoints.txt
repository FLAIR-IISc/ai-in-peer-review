- Contribution: Fully Bayesian inference for GP-SSMs by marginalizing the GP transition, yielding a non‑Markovian latent model and using PGAS to sample joint latent trajectories and hyper-parameters; posterior transitions are mixtures of GPs and a sparse FIC variant reduces complexity to O(M^2 T).

- Originality/significance: Novel and principled combination of marginalizing the GP and applying PGAS for nonparametric system identification that jointly learns dynamics and latent states while accounting for uncertainty.

- Technical quality: Derivations and methodology are technically sound and complete; the sparse FIC extension is a practical and important contribution for scalability.

- Clarity: Paper is very well written and organized, with a lucid presentation of the model, motivation for marginalization, and adaptation of PGAS.

- Empirical demonstration: Experiments show the method can capture multimodal smoothing distributions and leverage prior knowledge via the GP mean.

- Primary weakness: No comparison to other PMCMC baselines (e.g., PMMH, Particle Gibbs with backward sampling), leaving the choice of PGAS unjustified.

- Performance diagnostics lacking: Missing quantitative metrics (ESS, ACF) and analyses of sampler efficiency as functions of number of particles N and time series length T.

- Scalability concerns: No empirical investigation of how required particle count or mixing scales with T and N, which is crucial for practical guidance.

- Implementation detail praise: Section 4.2 appropriately addresses computational concerns such as efficient Cholesky updates.

- Suggested experiments: Recommend comparing PGAS to PMMH and Particle Gibbs with backward sampling, reporting ESS per unit time across varying N and T.

- Overall assessment: High-quality, novel, and significant conceptual contribution; main shortcoming is the insufficient empirical comparison and diagnostics.
