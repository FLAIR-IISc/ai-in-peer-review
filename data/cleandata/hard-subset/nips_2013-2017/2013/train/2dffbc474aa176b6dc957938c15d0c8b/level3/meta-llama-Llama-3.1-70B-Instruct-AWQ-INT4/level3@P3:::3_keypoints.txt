- Presents a fully Bayesian approach for inference and learning in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs) using particle MCMC (PMCMC).
- Applies PMCMC to GP-SSMs and demonstrates on two tasks: a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart-and-pole system.
- Results show the GP-SSM can capture complex dynamics and yield high-quality smoothing distributions.
- Method is a straightforward application of existing PMCMC methodology to GP-SSMs—novel application but builds on prior work.
- Paper is technically sound; theoretical analysis and experimental claims are supported.
- Fails to describe and evaluate practical challenges of making the PMCMC scheme work in practice.
- Does not provide detailed analysis of computational costs, efficiencies, or trade-offs despite mentioning reuse of computations.
- Lacks critical discussion of method limitations (e.g., overfitting risk, hyperparameter sensitivity).
- No comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for GP-SSMs.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and not rigorous—only two simple systems and small-scale datasets.
- Provides insufficient comprehensive analysis and interpretation of experimental results.
- Omits practical implementation guidance (choice of hyperparameters, number of particles, computational resources).
- Writing and organization are clear, but implementation details and readability of those sections could be improved.
- Significance: addresses an important Bayesian inference problem and has potential impact if validated further.
- Recommendations: add critical evaluation, larger/more complex experiments, practical implementation discussion, and deeper result analysis.
