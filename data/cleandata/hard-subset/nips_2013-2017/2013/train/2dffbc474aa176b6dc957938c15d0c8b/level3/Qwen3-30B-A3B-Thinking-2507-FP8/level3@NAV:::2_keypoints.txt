- Introduces particle MCMC for Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs, focusing on ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs (PGAS).
- Marginalizes the state transition function to enable fully Bayesian inference over states and dynamics while retaining GP nonparametric expressivity.
- Proposes a sparse GP-SSM using the FIC approximation to reduce complexity from O(T³) to O(M²T).
- Paper is well-written and situates contributions relative to prior GP-SSM work (Deisenroth, Turner, etc.).
- Contrasts with prior parametric approximations and fills a gap in fully Bayesian GP-SSM learning.
- Experiments on benchmark nonlinear systems and cart-pole show good predictive performance and capture multimodal smoothing distributions.
- Major weakness: omits comparisons to standard particle MCMC baselines (PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling).
- Lacks informative diagnostics (ACF, ESS) and analyses of scaling with particle count N and time horizon T.
- Needs comparisons because theoretical results imply different particle-scaling behavior (PMMH may require quadratic growth in T, particle Gibbs variants sublinear).
- Overall technically sound and original, but evaluation omissions weaken claims; addressing these would likely make the paper acceptable.
