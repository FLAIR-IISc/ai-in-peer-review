This paper presents a fully Bayesian method for joint state and hyperparameter inference in GP state-space models by marginalizing the transition function and sampling from the non-Markovian smoothing distribution with PMCMC, specifically particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS). The key idea is to integrate out the GP transition f to obtain a collapsed model over trajectories, then use ancestor sampling to maintain mixing despite the induced temporal dependencies. The authors further introduce computational strategies (sparse GP via FIC; low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates) that bring the complexity to O(T^3) for vanilla GPs and O(M^2T) for sparse variants. Related to prior work, this goes beyond GPDM (Wang et al.) and EM/MAP approaches (Turner et al., Ghahramani & Roweis) by providing a fully Bayesian treatment with online marginalization, and complements EP/approximate filtering/smoothing in learned GP dynamics (Deisenroth et al.) by tackling the learning problem itself.

Quality: The methodology is technically sound and carefully derived. The collapsing argument, non-Markovian SMC construction, and PGAS invariance are well used; the computational section is thoughtful and practically relevant. Empirical validation is concise but adequate; nonetheless, the experiments could be expanded (see below).

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and accessible. Minor issues: the abbreviation CPF-AS appears before being defined; Table 1 and surrounding text do not make it explicit whether RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample.

Originality: The main novelty is the online marginalization of the transition dynamics combined with ancestor sampling to perform smoothing in a non-Markovian latent model. This is a distinctive and timely contribution relative to prior NIPS papers on GP-SSMs, GPDM, and PMCMC.

Significance: The approach is likely to stimulate further work on inference in non-Markovian state-space models and will be useful to practitioners seeking Bayesian learning of dynamics with calibrated uncertainty.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Add a discussion contrasting (i) encoding a parametric transition model a priori versus (ii) using it as the GP mean, with implications for forecasting and extrapolation.
- Clarify Table 1 (in-sample vs out-of-sample) and add an explicit out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column.
- Report runtime, scaling with T, N, and M, and mixing diagnostics (ESS, autocorrelation).
- Provide more baselines and ablations (e.g., impact of mean function choice, sparse vs full GP; sensitivity to ancestor sampling).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Strong, novel inference scheme for non-Markovian GP-SSMs.
- Solid technical development with computationally efficient implementations.
- Clear presentation likely to impact the community.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited empirical breadth and ablations.
- Minor clarity issues (abbreviation, evaluation protocol).
