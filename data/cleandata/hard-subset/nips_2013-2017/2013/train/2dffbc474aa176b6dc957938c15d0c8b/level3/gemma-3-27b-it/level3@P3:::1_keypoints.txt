- Proposes a novel Bayesian method for inference and learning in GP-SSMs by marginalizing the transition function and using a tailored PMCMC sampler with ancestor sampling.  
- Extends prior GP-SSM work by learning the GP dynamics instead of assuming the transition model is known.  
- Technically sound, well-presented, and addresses inference challenges in non-Markovian state-space models.  
- Efficient computational strategies (sparsity, low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates) improve practical applicability.  
- Clear connections to related work and accurate placement within the research landscape.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited—only a nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole example are shown and results are brief.  
- Recommend expanding experiments with more datasets and baseline comparisons to strengthen evidence.  
- Suggest visualizing the learned cart-pole dynamics function to better interpret results.  
- Request a detailed discussion comparing using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean, including implications for forecasting and out-of-sample performance.  
- Clarify what Table 1 reports (in-sample vs out-of-sample RMSE) and add a column for out-of-sample/forecast RMSE.  
- Minor notational issue: introduce the abbreviation “CPF-AS” when first mentioned in Section 3.3.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel technically sound approach, efficient marginalization via ancestor sampling, careful computational considerations, clear presentation, and potential to stimulate further research.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical evaluation, insufficient discussion on parametric prior vs GP mean, unclear performance reporting in Table 1, and a minor notation issue.  
- Overall recommendation: strong and original paper; accept with minor revisions to address empirical clarity and notation.
