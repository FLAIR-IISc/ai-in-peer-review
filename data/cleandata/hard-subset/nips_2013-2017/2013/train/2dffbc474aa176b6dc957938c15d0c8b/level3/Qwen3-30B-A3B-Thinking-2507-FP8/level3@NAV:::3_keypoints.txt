- Proposes using PMCMC for fully Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs, marginalizing the transition function for nonparametric dynamics learning.
- Builds on Deisenroth et al. and Turner et al. by addressing learning the GP model rather than filtering/smoothing with fixed dynamics.
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample from the smoothing distribution while retaining nonparametric expressivity.
- Employs sparse GP approximations for computational efficiency.
- Demonstrated on two benchmarks: a nonlinear system and a cart-pole system.
- Methodology is technically sound and implementation details are well-presented.
- Contribution is incremental: applying PMCMC here is a straightforward extension rather than a significant methodological advance.
- Fails to evaluate practical PMCMC challenges such as sensitivity to particle number, convergence rates, and computational bottlenecks.
- Experimental section is descriptive and lacks ablation studies, comparisons to alternative inference methods, and analysis of limitations.
- Would benefit from a critical evaluation of PMCMC failure modes and tuning requirements.
- Overall a useful technical extension but not a demonstrable advance of the state of the art; needs more rigorous empirical validation and discussion of trade-offs for NIPS.
