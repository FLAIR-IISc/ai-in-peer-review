- Presents a fully Bayesian inference method for Gaussian process state-space models by marginalizing the transition function and using a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computational complexity.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM and PMCMC literature, filling the gap of fully Bayesian learning of GP dynamics absent in earlier work.  
- Technically sound derivations with careful marginalization and appropriate use of PMCMC theory.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation with reproducible mathematical detail and effective figures.  
- Novel application of PGAS to GP-SSMs, advancing nonparametric Bayesian state-space modeling.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on synthetic and real-world benchmarks, showing practical applicability.  
- Major weakness: no comparison to alternative PMCMC schemes (PMMH, Particle Gibbs with Backward Sampling).  
- Lacks performance diagnostics (ACF, ESS) and empirical scaling analysis as functions of particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Paper could better distinguish contributions relative to existing PMCMC methods and explicitly compare computational trade-offs.  
- Significance contingent on demonstrating computational advantages; current omissions limit impact.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding comparisons and scaling diagnostics—paper is strong but would be substantially improved with these additions.
