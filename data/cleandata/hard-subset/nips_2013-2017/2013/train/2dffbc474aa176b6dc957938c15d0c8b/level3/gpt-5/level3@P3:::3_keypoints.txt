- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition f and sampling x0:T and θ with PGAS, recovering f posterior analytically afterwards.  
- Introduces a sparse FIC variant to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Connects GP dynamical models and PMCMC literature, applying PGAS to handle the non-Markovian prior induced by marginalizing f.  

- Strength: Collapsed formulation is principled, reducing posterior coupling between x and f, and PGAS is appropriate for the non-Markovian prior.  
- Strength: Clear derivation of the marginal prior over trajectories and sensible implementation details for Cholesky reuse and sparse GPs.  
- Strength: Experiments show feasibility on multimodal nonlinear benchmarks and a 4D cart-pole, with uncertainty-aware predictions.  

- Weakness: Practical challenges of making PMCMC/PGAS work (mixing, path degeneracy, particle sensitivity, initialization, numerical stability) are not characterized.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is illustrative, not rigorous: small particle counts/iterations, no convergence diagnostics, no runtime or memory scaling.  
- Weakness: Limited comparisons — no head-to-head vs PMMH/PGBS, variational/EP, EM/MAP, or standard filters/smoothers on predictive metrics and robustness.  
- Weakness: Sparse FIC impact (choice of M, inducing placement) on accuracy, mixing, and complexity is not quantified.  

- Writing is generally clear with helpful notation; description of non-Gaussian Eq. (4) is good.  
- Missing practical details: hyperpriors, kernel and mean choices, inducing input selection, numerical safeguards, and reproducible pseudocode for implementation.  

- Originality is incremental: combines established elements (marginalizing f, PMCMC, PGAS) into a coherent application plus engineering and a sparse variant.  
- Significance: Fully Bayesian nonparametric SSM learning is relevant, but lack of thorough empirical validation limits confidence for broader adoption.  

- Suggestion: Provide PMCMC diagnostics (autocorrelation, ESS per time, sensitivity to N/T/M, slice update behavior).  
- Suggestion: Compare against baselines (EM/MAP GP-SSM, PMMH/PGBS on uncollapsed model, EP/variational GP-SSMs, EKF/UKF/particle smoothers).  
- Suggestion: Report predictive log-likelihood, calibration (PIT, coverage), RMSE, credible interval coverage, and include error bars/ablations.  
- Suggestion: Present wall-clock and memory scaling vs T, N, M, and state dimension; include stress tests on longer sequences.  
- Suggestion: Analyze mean-function misspecification, prior robustness, multimodality/label-switching, and effect of sparsity on mixing.  
- Suggestion: Release code or provide low-level implementation details (pseudocode for factor reuse, rank-1 updates/downdates, stabilization).  

- Pros: Principled fully Bayesian treatment, appropriate use of PGAS, clear links to prior work, and a sparse variant with engineering notes.  
- Cons: Limited novelty, insufficient discussion of practical pitfalls for PGAS in this setting, and an underpowered empirical evaluation.  

- Recommendation: Weak reject due to insufficient empirical assessment and lack of candid robustness analysis; strengthen experiments and diagnostics to improve the contribution.
