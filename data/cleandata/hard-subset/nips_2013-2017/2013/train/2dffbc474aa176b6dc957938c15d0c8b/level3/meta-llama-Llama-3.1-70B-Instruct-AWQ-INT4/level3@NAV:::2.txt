This paper proposes a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs) and particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithms. The authors develop a sparse GP-SSM variant to reduce computational complexity. The paper is well-written, clear, and an original application of particle MCMC.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a sparse GP-SSM variant, which reduces the computational complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2T), where M << T. The authors also propose a particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) algorithm to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the state transition function.

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work and clearly explain how their approach differs from previous contributions. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.

However, there are a few weaknesses in the paper. The authors do not provide comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, such as particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling. These comparisons would be straightforward to implement and are necessary to assess the relative performance of the proposed method. Additionally, the results lack informative diagnostics, such as graphs of performance as functions of particle count N and time horizon T to determine scaling (linear vs sublinear).

In conclusion, the paper is well-written and presents an original application of particle MCMC. However, it would be significantly improved by including comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes and providing more informative diagnostics.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper is well-written and clear.
* The proposal is an original application of particle MCMC.
* The development of a sparse GP-SSM variant is a significant contribution.
* The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper lacks comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes.
* The results lack informative diagnostics.
* The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above by including comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes and providing more informative diagnostics.
