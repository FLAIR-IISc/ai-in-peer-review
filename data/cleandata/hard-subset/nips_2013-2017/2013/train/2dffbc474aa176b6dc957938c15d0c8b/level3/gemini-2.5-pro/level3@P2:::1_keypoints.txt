- Presents a fully Bayesian framework for SSMs with GP transition priors, jointly estimating hidden states and hyperparameters.  
- Main contribution: marginalizes out the infinite-dimensional GP transition function (“collapse”), avoiding parametric approximations.  
- Collapsing introduces long-range temporal dependencies, breaking the Markovian structure of the state sequence.  
- Uses a tailored PMCMC method (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling, PGAS) to perform inference in the resulting non‑Markovian model.  
- Derivation of the marginal prior over state trajectories after integrating out the GP is correct and technically sound.  
- Application of PGAS to this non‑Markovian smoothing problem is well-motivated and appropriate.  
- Addresses computational complexity: reduces naive O(T^4) to O(T^3) via Cholesky reuse and to O(M^2 T) using sparse GP (FIC).  
- Experiments, though brief, effectively demonstrate capabilities like multimodal smoothing and learning complex nonlinear dynamics.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and accessible to readers familiar with GPs and SMC.  
- Minor clarity issue: abbreviation CPF‑AS is used before being formally defined.  
- Highly original: novel combination of marginalizing the GP dynamics and using PGAS; first truly nonparametric Bayesian learning framework for GP‑SSMs.  
- Significant and likely influential for system identification and modeling complex time series across domains.  
- Experimental weakness: unclear whether reported RMSE is in‑sample or out‑of‑sample; recommend adding one‑step‑ahead forecast RMSE on held‑out data.  
- Suggest adding discussion on practical implications of using a parametric GP mean function versus a purely parametric model, especially for long‑term forecasting.  
- Overall recommendation: technically strong, novel, and important work with minor empirical/clarity improvements needed; recommend acceptance.
