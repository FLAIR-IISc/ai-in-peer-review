## Review of “Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC”

This paper presents a novel Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear, nonparametric state-space models, leveraging Gaussian process (GP) priors over transition dynamics and employing Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) with ancestor sampling. The core contribution lies in the online marginalization of the transition dynamics via this ancestor sampling technique, enabling full nonparametric expressivity without relying on parametric approximations often seen in the literature. 

The work builds upon existing GP-SSM research (Deisenroth et al., Turner et al., Wang et al.) by tackling the challenging problem of *learning* the GP dynamics rather than assuming a pre-defined model. This is a significant step forward, as it allows the model to adaptively increase complexity with data. The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of the field, clearly positioning their work within the existing landscape and highlighting its advantages. The technical presentation is excellent – the paper is well-written, logically organized, and accessible, even for readers less familiar with the intricacies of PMCMC. The consideration of computational efficiency through sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates is commendable.

The empirical results, while sufficient to demonstrate the method’s functionality on a benchmark nonlinear system and a cart-pole example, feel somewhat brief. Expanding these results with more diverse datasets and comparisons to alternative methods would strengthen the paper. The authors correctly identify the need for a discussion comparing a parametric transition model *a priori* versus using it as the GP mean, which is a crucial point for understanding the implications for forecasting and out-of-sample performance.  Table 1 currently lacks an out-of-sample RMSE column, which, as the authors acknowledge, is essential for evaluating predictive performance.  A minor point: the abbreviation CPF-AS is used before being defined.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The online marginalization of transition dynamics via ancestor sampling is a significant contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper is rigorously developed and technically correct.
*   **Clarity:** The presentation is exceptionally clear and well-organized.
*   **Potential Significance:** This work has the potential to stimulate further research in non-Markovian state-space models and Bayesian inference for dynamical systems.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The empirical results could be more extensive and comparative.
*   **Missing Out-of-Sample Results:** The lack of out-of-sample RMSE hinders a complete assessment of predictive performance.



Overall, this is a strong and novel paper that addresses an important problem in a technically sound and accessible manner. Addressing the minor weaknesses regarding empirical evaluation and out-of-sample results would further enhance its impact and make it a highly valuable contribution to the NIPS conference. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
