This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using Gaussian process (GP) priors. The authors apply particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) methodology to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the state transition function. The proposed approach preserves the full nonparametric expressivity of the model and can make use of sparse Gaussian processes to reduce computational complexity.

The paper is well-written and provides a thorough introduction to the problem of Bayesian inference in state-space models. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach, including the marginalization of the state transition function and the use of PMCMC.

However, the paper lacks a detailed, critical evaluation of the method's strengths and weaknesses. The experimental section demonstrates the approach but does not provide a rigorous empirical evaluation. The authors do not adequately discuss the practical challenges of getting the PMCMC scheme to work, such as the choice of hyper-parameters, the number of particles, and the computational complexity.

The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and a comparison with existing methods. The authors should also provide more details on the implementation of the algorithm, including the choice of proposal distributions and the tuning of hyper-parameters.

In terms of originality, the paper applies existing PMCMC methodology to a new problem, but it does not introduce any new techniques or algorithms. The paper is well-connected to previous work in the field, and the authors provide a thorough review of related literature.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in Bayesian inference and learning, and the proposed approach has the potential to be useful in a variety of applications. However, the paper does not provide enough evidence to demonstrate the practical impact of the proposed approach.
