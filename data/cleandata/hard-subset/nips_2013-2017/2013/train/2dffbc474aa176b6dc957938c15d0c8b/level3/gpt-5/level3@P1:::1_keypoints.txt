- Proposal: Fully Bayesian learning in GP state-space models by placing a GP prior on f and analytically marginalizing it, targeting p(x0:T, θ | y0:T).  
- Inference: Use Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample non-Markovian latent trajectories after collapsing f.  
- Posterior over f: Given sampled trajectories, f’s posterior is available in closed form, yielding a predictive Gaussian mixture for f(x*).  
- Scalability: Introduce sparse GP-SSMs via the FIC prior to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Implementation: Practical tricks (rank-one Cholesky updates/downdates, reuse of factorizations) make the sampler computationally viable.  
- Empirics: Evaluated on a scalar multimodal benchmark and a 4D cart-pole; shows good mixing and calibrated predictive uncertainty but experiments are brief.  
- Relation to prior work: Contrasts with EM/MAP GP-SSM approaches by being fully Bayesian and by collapsing f instead of parameterizing it.  
- Novelty: Collapsing the GP transition and applying PGAS to the resulting non-Markovian SSM is a novel contribution within GP-SSMs.  
- Technical soundness: Collapsed prior derivation and key identity are correct; PGAS provides an invariant MCMC kernel for N ≥ 2; hyperparameter sampling is standard and appropriate.  
- Computational claims: Complexity reductions and implementation details are convincingly addressed and valuable.  
- Empirical limitations: Needs stronger baselines (GPDM/EM, MAP, non-collapsed smoothers) and more diagnostics (ESS, autocorrelations, runtimes).  
- Clarity: Well organized and accessible overall, but minor issues (acronym CPF-AS appears before definition; inconsistent y0:T vs y1:T).  
- Reporting issues: Table 1 should clarify in-sample vs out-of-sample RMSE and include predictive log-likelihoods/forecast RMSE.  
- Practical guidance: Paper should discuss choice of parametric mean vs zero-mean GP and implications for extrapolation.  
- Suggestions: Expand experimental comparisons, add sensitivity analyses (N, L, M), provide convergence diagnostics and runtime/memory profiling, and fix notation/acronym usage.  
- Pros: Principled fully Bayesian approach, careful computational engineering, sparse variant with clear complexity gains, and clear exposition.  
- Cons: Limited experimental scope, unclear reporting for some metrics, and minor clarity/notation issues.  
- Overall assessment: Strong and timely methodological contribution with substantial value, meriting acceptance subject to expanded experiments and presentation refinements.
