- Introduces a Bayesian framework for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear SSMs using a Gaussian Process prior for transition dynamics.  
- Proposes PMCMC with ancestor sampling to efficiently marginalize transition dynamics and handle the induced non‑Markovian dependencies.  
- Incorporates sparse GP methods and low-rank Cholesky updates to address computational challenges and improve efficiency.  
- Methodology is technically robust, well-presented, and supported by solid theoretical exposition.  
- Ancestor sampling for online marginalization of GP dynamics is a novel and notable contribution.  
- Empirical results (nonlinear benchmark and cart‑and‑pole) demonstrate flexibility and uncertainty‑aware predictive capability.  
- Limited discussion and evaluation of out‑of‑sample/forward‑time prediction performance; empirical evaluation could be strengthened.  
- Ambiguity in Table 1: unclear whether RMSEs are out‑of‑sample or conditioned on observed data—needs clarification and ideally an RMSE column for withheld forecasts.  
- Should expand discussion on trade‑offs between parametric transition models and GP‑based mean functions for forecasting tasks.  
- Minor clarity issue: abbreviation CPF‑AS is used before being defined.  
- Recommendation: strong contribution; recommend acceptance pending fixes to clarity and expanded out‑of‑sample evaluation.
