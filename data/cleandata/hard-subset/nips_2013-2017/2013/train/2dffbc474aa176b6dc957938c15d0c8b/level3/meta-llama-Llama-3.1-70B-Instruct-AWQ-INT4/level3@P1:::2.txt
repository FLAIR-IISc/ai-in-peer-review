This paper proposes a novel application of particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) to inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs). The authors focus on the development of a particle Gibbs sampler with ancestral sampling, which enables efficient inference in GP-SSMs. The paper is well-written, clear, and presents an original contribution to the field.

The methodological contribution of the paper is the development of a sparse GP-SSM variant, which reduces the computational complexity of the algorithm. This is a significant improvement, as GP-SSMs can be computationally expensive to evaluate. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on two nonlinear systems, showcasing the ability of the GP-SSM to capture complex dynamics.

However, there are some omissions in the paper that need to be addressed. Firstly, the authors do not provide comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, specifically particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.). These comparisons are necessary to assess the relative performance of the proposed method and would be straightforward to implement.

Furthermore, the results presented in the paper lack informative diagnostics. The authors should provide graphs of performance metrics, such as the autocorrelation function (ACF) or the effective sample size (ESS), as functions of the particle count N and the time horizon T. This would help to determine the scaling of the algorithm (linear vs sublinear) and provide a more comprehensive understanding of its performance.

In the reviewer's opinion, PMMH likely requires the particle count to grow quadratically with T (per Whiteley et al.), whereas particle Gibbs variants require sublinear growth. Therefore, including comparisons to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would significantly improve the paper.

In conclusion, the paper presents a well-written and original contribution to the field of GP-SSMs. However, to fully assess the performance of the proposed method, the authors should include comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes and provide more informative diagnostics.
