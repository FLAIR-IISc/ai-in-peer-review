- Presents a fully Bayesian, nonparametric GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function for direct joint smoothing inference.
- Combines Gaussian processes for dynamics with Particle MCMC (PGAS) for efficient sampling.
- Key contribution: learns the GP transition model during inference rather than assuming a pre-learned dynamics.
- Builds on prior GP-SSM and SMC/MCMC literature and appropriately cites related work.
- Theoretical derivations for marginalizing dynamics and the induced prior are clear and sound.
- Use of PGAS is well-motivated and appropriate for sampling the smoothing distribution.
- Computational optimizations (Cholesky reuse, FIC sparse GPs) are detailed and persuasive.
- Empirical validation on a synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole system demonstrates practical viability.
- Authors acknowledge algorithmic approximations and discuss limitations like computational cost and need for thinning.
- Limited comparative evaluation—missing benchmarks against alternative methods (e.g., variational GP-SSM approaches).
- Potential identifiability issues when both transition and observation models are flexible are not fully addressed.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, though notation is dense and SMC/MCMC familiarity is assumed.
- Implementation details are particularly valuable, but hyperparameter choices and sensitivity analysis are lacking.
- Novelty lies in marginalizing the GP transition, integrating PGAS, and applying FIC sparse GPs to reduce complexity.
- Significant potential impact for time series, robotics, and control due to flexible, fully Bayesian nonparametric modeling.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, sound theory, strong implementation, and demonstrated effectiveness.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons, identifiability concerns, computational scalability, and missing hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of acceptance pending more comprehensive evaluation and discussion of identifiability.
