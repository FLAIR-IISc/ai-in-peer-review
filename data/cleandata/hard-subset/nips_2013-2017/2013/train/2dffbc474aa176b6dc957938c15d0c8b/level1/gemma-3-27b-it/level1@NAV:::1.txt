## Review of "Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC"

This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models, leveraging Gaussian Processes (GPs) as a flexible prior over the state transition dynamics. The core contribution lies in marginalizing over the transition dynamics function and employing a tailored Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) sampler for efficient inference of the joint smoothing distribution. This avoids the need for a fixed transition model, a common limitation in existing GP-SSM methods. The authors demonstrate the approach’s ability to handle multimodal smoothing distributions and show its efficacy in both a synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole system. 

The paper is well-motivated, building on the established literature of GP-SSMs (Rasmussen & Williams, Deisenroth et al., Turner et al.). It correctly identifies a key gap: the need for a method that doesn't require pre-specifying a parametric form for the transition dynamics. The use of PMCMC, particularly the PGAS variant, is a sensible choice given the complexity of the model and the need to sample from a non-Markovian posterior. The demonstration of the method’s ability to capture multimodality and learn from limited data (cart-pole example) is encouraging. The inclusion of a sparse GP variant addresses computational concerns.

A significant strength is the technical depth and clarity of the derivations, especially the explanation of marginalizing out the state transition function and the implementation details for efficient computation (Cholesky factorization). The discussion of the computational complexity reduction via sparse GPs is valuable. The experiments showcase promising results, and the comparison against other approaches provides a reasonable benchmark. 

However, the paper would benefit from more extensive experimental validation, particularly in higher-dimensional state spaces. While the cart-pole example is illustrative, its limited dimensionality might obscure potential scalability issues. The hyperparameter sensitivity of the method isn't thoroughly explored. A more detailed ablation study demonstrating the benefits of the full Bayesian approach versus simpler alternatives (e.g., MAP estimation) would strengthen the contribution.  Additionally, the discussion on the choice of inducing points for the sparse GP could be expanded.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method is theoretically well-grounded and leverages established techniques (GPs, PMCMC) in a novel way.
*   **Originality:**  Marginalizing out the transition dynamics function is a significant contribution, addressing a key limitation in existing GP-SSM literature.
*   **Significance:**  The ability to learn nonlinear dynamics in a fully Bayesian manner has broad applicability across various scientific and engineering domains.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments, while positive, are relatively limited and don't fully demonstrate scalability or robustness.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The influence of hyperparameters on performance isn't adequately investigated.



**Overall:** I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of GP-SSMs and addresses an important open problem. Addressing the points raised regarding experimental validation and hyperparameter sensitivity would significantly strengthen the work.
