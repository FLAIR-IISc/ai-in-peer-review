- Fully Bayesian approach for nonlinear SSMs using a GP prior on transition dynamics (GP-SSM) with the dynamics function marginalized out.  
- Marginalization of f induces non-Markovian temporal dependencies across the entire state trajectory.  
- Inference performed with PMCMC, specifically Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS), to sample joint posterior of states and hyperparameters.  
- Introduces a sparse GP variant (FIC) to reduce computational cost to O(M^2 * T) from the full GP’s O(T^3).  
- Contrasts with prior parametric or MAP-based methods, claiming better uncertainty quantification and avoidance of overfitting.  
- Novelty/significance: principled, fully Bayesian joint state and system identification that can capture multimodal posteriors.  
- Technical quality: correct derivations, appropriate use of PGAS, and practical algorithmic details for complexity reduction.  
- Clarity: manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and well organized.  
- Weakness — computational complexity: remaining O(T^3) (or high) costs can be prohibitive for long time series.  
- Weakness — scalability: experiments limited to low-dimensional states (1D, 4D); scaling in state dimension nx is not analyzed.  
- Weakness — sparse GP practicalities: inducing input selection is deferred and not empirically integrated or evaluated.  
- Weakness — mixing analysis: lacks quantitative MCMC diagnostics (effective sample sizes, autocorrelations) to support mixing claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically elegant, technically sound, advances GP-SSM learning, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical scalability issues for long T and high-dimensional states, and insufficient treatment of inducing-point selection.  
- Overall: strong methodological contribution with significant clarity and correctness, tempered by practical scalability and evaluation gaps.
