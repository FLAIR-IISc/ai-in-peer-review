- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by analytically marginalizing the GP transition f, yielding a non-Markovian prior over states and using PGAS to sample x0:T and θ.
- Predictive distribution over f is obtained via standard GP regression conditioned on sampled trajectories, resulting in a mixture-of-Gaussians predictor.
- Introduces a sparse FIC variant to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) and supplies practical implementation details (rank-1 Cholesky updates, reuse of matrix products).
- Differs from prior work by collapsing GP dynamics and jointly sampling states and hyperparameters while preserving nonparametric expressivity.
- Derivation of the collapsed prior and its integration with SMC/PGAS is technically sound and consistent with GP regression identities.
- Ancestor sampling weights are correctly adapted to the non-Markovian prior; the PGAS invariance property is properly cited.
- Complexity claims and the sparse FIC construction are plausible given the described factorization reuse.
- Authors address major computational bottlenecks with detailed, practical algorithmic steps that are often missing in similar papers.
- Experiments on a nonlinear multimodal benchmark and a 4D cart-pole show capability for multimodal smoothing and calibrated one-step predictions.
- Empirical evaluation is limited in breadth (only two datasets) and lacks comparisons to strong baselines like EM/MAP GP-SSM or PMCMC without collapsing f.
- Claims of “very good mixing” are unquantified; using only 50 PGAS iterations with N=20 particles may be insufficient for difficult posteriors.
- Method is stated to handle general likelihoods, but experiments focus exclusively on Gaussian observation noise.
- Scalability with state dimension and long horizons is discussed theoretically but not validated empirically; no runtime/memory scaling plots are provided.
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and reproducible, with explicit expressions for the collapsed prior and FIC variant; notation is dense in places.
- Some practical details (CPF-AS pseudocode, slice-sampler settings, initialization, diagnostics) could be expanded for clarity.
- The combination of exact marginalization of GP transitions with PGAS for GP-SSMs is novel and constitutes a meaningful advance.
- The approach maintains full nonparametric flexibility without parametric approximation of f.
- Sparse variant and implementation guidance increase practical applicability and potential adoption.
- Main caveat: computational cost and limited empirical breadth reduce demonstrated impact; stronger benchmarks would help.
- Pros: conceptual novelty (collapsing GP + PGAS), nonparametric expressivity, careful derivations, sparse complexity reduction, handles multimodal smoothing.
- Cons: limited datasets, few quantitative baseline comparisons and ablations, no mixing diagnostics, experiments limited to Gaussian observations, sparse inducing-point sensitivity analysis.
- Suggested: add quantitative comparisons to EM/MAP GP-SSM and non-collapsed PMCMC baselines.
- Suggested: report autocorrelation/ESS for x and θ, and provide runtime/memory scaling with T, N, and state dimension.
- Suggested: include a non-Gaussian observation or missing-data experiment and ablations on particle count/iterations and FIC vs full GP.
- Suggested: discuss identifiability when both f and g are flexible and give practical guidance on mean functions and priors.
- Assessment: Quality high (minor empirical gaps); Clarity high; Originality high; Significance high potential pending broader experiments.
- Overall recommendation: Strongly positive, with request to expand empirical evaluation and diagnostics to strengthen the paper.
