- Presents a fully Bayesian nonparametric approach to learning GP-SSMs by placing a GP prior over the transition function and marginalizing it out.  
- Uses a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm to sample from the joint smoothing distribution.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM construction using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computation and improve scalability.  
- Properly situates the work relative to prior GP-SSM and parametric dynamics literature, highlighting the gap it fills.  
- Theoretical foundation for marginalizing the transition function and deriving the joint smoothing distribution is solid and carefully presented.  
- PGAS inference is well-motivated and supported by theoretical arguments.  
- Computational complexity analysis is thorough and shows how sparse approximations make the method efficient.  
- Experiments on benchmark nonlinear systems demonstrate effectiveness, especially for multimodal smoothing distributions.  
- Paper lacks sensitivity analysis regarding hyperparameters and the number of particles.  
- Some theoretical sections (marginalization and loss of Markovian structure) could use more intuitive explanations for broader accessibility.  
- Comparison with related work could be more thorough, e.g., explicit contrasts with Deisenroth et al.’s filtering methods.  
- Novelty: marginalization of dynamics and use of PGAS in this context are clear technical contributions.  
- Significance: enables fully Bayesian inference in nonparametric SSMs and handles multimodal posteriors, with practical scalability via sparse GPs.  
- Strengths: preserves nonparametric expressivity, novel marginalization, efficient PGAS sampling, scalable sparse construction, strong empirical advantages.  
- Weaknesses: limited sensitivity discussion, some inaccessible theoretical details, incomplete comparisons to prior methods, and unclear limits when inducing points are insufficient.  
- Recommendation: strong contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify comparisons and add sensitivity analysis.
