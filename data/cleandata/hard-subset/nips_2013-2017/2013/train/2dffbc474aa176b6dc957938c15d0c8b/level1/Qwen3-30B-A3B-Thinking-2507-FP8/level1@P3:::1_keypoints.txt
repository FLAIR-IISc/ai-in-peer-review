- Presents a fully Bayesian approach to GP state-space models by marginalizing the transition function and using PMCMC/PGAS to infer the joint smoothing distribution.  
- Key innovation: marginalize the GP to retain full nonparametric expressivity and handle the induced non‑Markovian state process via tailored SMC‑MCMC.  
- Strength: Marginalization is elegant and principled, avoiding parametric approximations while preserving model flexibility.  
- Strength: Thorough theoretical development with clear derivations and connections to existing CPF‑AS/PGAS work.  
- Strength: Sparse GP extension (FIC) is well justified and reduces complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2 T) with M << T.  
- Strength: Experiments convincingly show handling of multimodal smoothing and learning complex dynamics (e.g., cart‑pole).  
- Strength: Clearly differentiates from related work and addresses non‑identifiability when modeling dynamics and observation functions nonparametrically.  
- Weakness: Computational complexity analysis lacks concrete practical scaling details and more rigorous treatment of Cholesky updates.  
- Weakness: Insufficient empirical comparison to alternative nonparametric SSM methods (e.g., EP‑based GP, other sparse GP SSMs).  
- Weakness: No theoretical bounds on approximation error introduced by the particle approximation—only mixing guarantees for PGAS.  
- Weakness: Sparse GP implementation details (how intermediate matrix–vector products are reused to achieve O(M^2 T)) are underspecified.  
- Weakness: Scalability to very long time series (T > 1000) is not addressed.  
- Significance: A significant, original contribution that enables fully Bayesian learning of nonparametric SSMs and handles multimodal smoothing where parametric approaches struggle.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper recommended for acceptance; weaknesses are refinements rather than fundamental flaws.
