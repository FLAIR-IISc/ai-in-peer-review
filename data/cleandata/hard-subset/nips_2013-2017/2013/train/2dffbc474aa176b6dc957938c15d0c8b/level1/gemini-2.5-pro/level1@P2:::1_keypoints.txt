- Proposes a fully Bayesian, nonparametric GP-SSM by marginalizing the infinite-dimensional dynamics function f.  
- Marginalization breaks the Markov property, inducing long-range temporal dependencies in the state sequence.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to perform inference in the resulting non-Markovian model.  
- Reconstructs the posterior over the dynamics as a mixture of GPs after sampling states and hyperparameters.  
- Introduces a sparse GP variant based on the FIC prior to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  

Quality
- Derivation of the marginalized prior over state trajectories is correct and theoretically sound.  
- Application of PGAS to this non-Markovian latent-variable model is appropriate and sophisticated.  
- Complexity analysis and the sparse GP solution are well handled for practical viability.  
- The work is complete: model, inference algorithm, scalability solution, and experimental validation.  
- Experiments (including a bimodal posterior case) convincingly show advantages of full Bayesian sampling over point-estimate methods.  
- Evaluation compares against appropriate baselines and reports clear metrics (e.g., RMSE).  

Clarity
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow from model to inference to experiments.  
- Complex concepts (marginalization consequences, PGAS mechanics) are explained with sufficient expert-level detail.  
- Provides enough algorithmic detail for reproducibility by an expert.  

Originality
- Combining marginalization of the GP dynamics with PGAS for learning GP-SSMs is a novel and principled contribution.  
- Thoroughly positions itself relative to prior work, claiming the first fully Bayesian, nonparametric learning of GP dynamics.  

Significance
- Offers a powerful, flexible solution for system identification with broad applications (robotics, control, econometrics, neuroscience).  
- Advances the state of the art by enabling full uncertainty quantification in states and learned dynamics, important for robust decision-making.  

Weaknesses / Suggestions
- Claims of "very good mixing" lack empirical MCMC diagnostics (trace plots, autocorrelations) to support them.  
- Selection and handling of inducing points for the sparse GP are under-discussed, especially since inputs (states) are sampled.  
- O(M^2 T) can still be a bottleneck; a discussion of practical limits on T and how M scales with state dimension is missing.  

Acceptance Summary
- Arguments for acceptance: highly original, technically sound, principled Bayesian treatment, strong experiments, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing MCMC mixing diagnostics and insufficient practical detail on inducing-point selection for the sparse model.
