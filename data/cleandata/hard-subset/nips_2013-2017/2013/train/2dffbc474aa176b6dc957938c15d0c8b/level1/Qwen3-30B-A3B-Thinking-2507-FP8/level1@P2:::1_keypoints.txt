- Fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing a GP prior on transitions and marginalizing the transition function.  
- Uses tailored PMCMC samplers, specifically Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS), to infer the joint smoothing distribution.  
- Handles the non-Markovian structure introduced by marginalization by extending PMCMC methods.  
- Provides rigorous derivation of the marginal prior over state trajectories (Equations 3–4).  
- Details efficient implementation using Cholesky factorizations (Section 4.2).  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM with the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior, reducing complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2 T).  
- Experiments demonstrate capturing multimodal smoothing distributions and learning complex dynamics (synthetic and cart-pole).  
- Technically sound with clear theoretical foundations and reproducible derivations.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized, with effective figures and practical implementation details.  
- Original contribution: marginalizing GP dynamics and applying PGAS to non-Markovian latent models is novel.  
- Significant impact: enables fully Bayesian, nonparametric SSM inference and offers practical scalability via sparse extension.  
- Weakness: limited comparison with recent nonparametric/variational SSM methods beyond cited works.  
- Weakness: lacks concrete runtime/wall-clock comparisons against alternative methods on the same hardware.  
- Weakness: sparse GP section could better discuss accuracy vs. computational trade-offs.  
- Pro: addresses a fundamental limitation, is theoretically sound, practically implementable, and empirically advantageous.  
- Con: comparative and runtime analyses are insufficient; sparse trade-offs under-discussed.  
- Overall: strong, novel, well-executed contribution; recommended for acceptance.
