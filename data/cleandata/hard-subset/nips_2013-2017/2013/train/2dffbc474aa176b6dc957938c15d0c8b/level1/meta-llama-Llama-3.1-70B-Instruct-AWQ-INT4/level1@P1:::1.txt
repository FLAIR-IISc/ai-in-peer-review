This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models. The authors employ a Gaussian process (GP) prior over the state transition dynamics, allowing for a flexible model that can capture complex dynamical phenomena. To enable efficient inference, they marginalize over the transition dynamics function and instead infer directly the joint smoothing distribution using particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) samplers.

The main contribution of this paper is the development of a tailored PMCMC algorithm, referred to as particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS), which allows for efficient sampling from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the state transition function. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart and pole system.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The use of GP priors over the state transition dynamics is a natural choice, given the flexibility and expressiveness of GPs. The marginalization of the transition dynamics function is a key insight, as it allows for efficient inference over the smoothing distribution.

The PGAS algorithm is a significant contribution, as it provides a way to sample from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the state transition function. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm through experiments, showing that it can accurately capture the underlying dynamics of the system.

One potential weakness of the paper is the assumption that the observation model is known. In many real-world applications, the observation model is unknown or uncertain, and must be learned simultaneously with the state transition dynamics. The authors do not address this issue in their paper.

Another potential weakness is the computational complexity of the PGAS algorithm. While the authors demonstrate that their algorithm can be implemented efficiently, the computational complexity is still O(T^3) for the vanilla GP and O(M^2T) for the sparse GP. This may limit the applicability of the algorithm to large-scale problems.

Overall, the paper is a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian inference and learning in state-space models. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, and demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm through experiments.
