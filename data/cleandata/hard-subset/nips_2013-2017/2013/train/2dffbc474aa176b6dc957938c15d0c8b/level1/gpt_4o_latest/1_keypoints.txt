- Introduces a fully Bayesian, nonparametric GP-SSM that marginalizes state transition dynamics to preserve flexibility without parametric assumptions.
- Proposes a tailored PMCMC sampler (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling) and sparse GP priors to enable efficient inference for the induced non-Markovian model.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world (cart-pole) systems shows ability to capture multimodal smoothing distributions, outperform parametric baselines, and provide meaningful uncertainty estimates.
- Addresses an important challenge in Bayesian SSMs, offering potential impact for time series modeling, control, and reinforcement learning.

- Paper is dense and may be hard to follow for readers not versed in advanced Bayesian inference; more intuition and visual aids would help.
- Method remains computationally intensive for high-dimensional states or long time series; lacks detailed runtime and memory analysis.
- Experimental comparisons are limited and could include more recent GP-based SSMs or deep learning time-series methods.
- Assumes a known observation model and smoothness priors, which may limit applicability to highly noisy or discontinuous dynamics.

- Tackles a relevant, challenging problem with a novel, well-justified technical contribution and strong empirical results.
- Advances state of the art in learning flexible, data-adaptive Bayesian SSMs while maintaining computational tractability via sparse approximations.
- Demonstrates practical utility through experiments that highlight modeling and uncertainty benefits.

- Clarity issues, computational scalability concerns, and incomplete experimental comparisons argue for further revision before wide adoption.
- Needs clearer exposition and a more thorough analysis of computational trade-offs to increase accessibility and applicability.
- Additional baselines and larger-scale experiments would strengthen empirical claims.

- Recommendation: Accept, subject to revisions improving clarity and providing a more detailed discussion of computational trade-offs.
