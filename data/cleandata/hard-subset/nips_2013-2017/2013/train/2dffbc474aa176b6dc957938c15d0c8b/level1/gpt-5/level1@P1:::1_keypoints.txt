- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition f exactly, inducing a non-Markovian prior over trajectories.  
- Expresses the induced trajectory prior as a product of Gaussian conditionals using standard GP predictive formulas.  
- Marginalization reduces posterior coupling between f and x, enabling direct sampling from p(x0:T | y0:T, θ).  
- Uses particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) to sample trajectories under the non-Markovian prior.  
- Given samples of x and θ, the posterior over f and predictive distributions are closed-form mixtures of Gaussians.  
- Develops a sparse FIC variant to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T), with rank-1 Cholesky updates and reuse across time.  
- Situates work relative to prior GP-SSM literature, contrasting with EM/MAP and EP-based approximations and highlighting novelty in collapsing+PGAS.  
- Derivations of the collapsed prior and GP-predicted means/covariances are technically sound and correctly handle the non-Gaussian prior.  
- Use of PGAS and derivation of ancestor sampling weights for the non-Markovian case are appropriate and correct.  
- Sampling strategies for θx and θy are well motivated; θx learning reduces to GP regression with slice sampling being reasonable.  
- Computational tradeoffs are well explained, including naive O(T^3) cost and practical FIC implementation details.  
- Experiments show multimodal smoothing on a 1D benchmark and one-step predictive uncertainty on a 4D cart-pole, demonstrating core claims.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: few baselines (no strong GP-SSM comparisons), no runtime/scaling analysis, and no mixing/ESS/autocorrelation diagnostics.  
- Paper is generally clear and organized but omits reproducibility details such as pseudocode for CPF-AS and efficient ancestor sampling implementation.  
- Lacks clarification on modeling vector-valued outputs (independent GPs vs multioutput kernels) and implications for complexity.  
- Needs explicit statement of priors and sampling updates for Q and θy, and discussion of identifiability when g is known but θy unknown.  
- Recommends adding a complexity/memory table comparing vanilla and FIC implementations.  
- Originality: novel combination of exact marginalization of f with PGAS for non-Markovian smoothing in GP-SSMs.  
- Significance: offers an asymptotically exact, fully Bayesian alternative for GP-SSMs with potential impact if scaled and empirically strengthened.  
- Pros: principled Bayesian framework, effective use of PGAS, practical FIC speedups, robustness to mean misspecification, rich predictive mixtures.  
- Cons: limited experiments and baselines, assumes known observation model g, unclear multioutput GP treatment, potential computational burden for long/high-dim sequences.  
- Suggestions: compare to GPDM/EM-MAP and variational GP-SSMs, report ESS/autocorrelation and wall-clock scaling, clarify multioutput choices and priors, add longer real-world dataset.  
- Recommendation: leaning toward acceptance, contingent on strengthening empirical evaluation and reproducibility details.
