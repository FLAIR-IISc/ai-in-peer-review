- Summary: Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by analytically marginalizing the transition GP, performing joint smoothing and hyperparameter inference with PGAS on the resulting non‑Markovian trajectory prior, and recovering f via GP regression; introduces a sparse FIC variant to lower complexity.
- Relation to prior work: Novel compared to EM/MAP and EP/variational GP-DS approaches by offering a collapsed, fully Bayesian PMCMC solution building on recent PGAS advances.

Strengths:
- Conceptual novelty: Clean, fully Bayesian collapsing of the transition GP preserves nonparametric dynamics versus MAP/EM approximations.
- Technical soundness: Correct construction of the non‑Markovian prior, PGAS kernel, and specialized ancestor sampling weights with proper citations.
- Practical contributions: Useful implementation guidance (rank‑1 Cholesky updates, efficient concatenated-path computations) and an FIC sparse formulation reducing complexity to O(M^2 T).
- Empirical demonstrations: Shows multimodal smoothing, correction of misspecified means, and calibrated one‑step predictive performance on a 4D cart‑pole task.

Weaknesses / suggestions:
- Computational scaling: Vanilla method is O(T^3) per PGAS sweep; need wall‑clock timings, memory use, and sensitivity plots (RMSE/ESS vs. N, M, T) to validate sparse claims.
- Limited baselines: Missing fair comparisons to EM/MAP GPDM/GP-DS, EP-based GP-DS, and PMCMC without collapsing f; include multi‑step and long‑horizon rollouts.
- Hyperparameter sampling: θx sampling may mix poorly due to x–θ coupling; provide diagnostics (autocorrelations/trace plots) and consider elliptical slice or blocked updates.
- SMC proposal ambiguity: Clarify whether proposals use the prior or p(xt|·,yt) / lookahead proposals, since this strongly affects weight degeneracy.
- Inducing inputs: Method for selecting/optimizing inducing points is unspecified; report selection method and sensitivity to M and placement.
- Identifiability and priors: Discuss identifiability between GP hyperparameters and process noise Q and sensitivity to hyperparameter priors (e.g., log‑normal).

Quality:
- Methodologically sound with thoughtful implementation details; main caveat is computational cost and need for stronger empirical validation and diagnostics.

Clarity:
- Generally clear and organized; notation around concatenated paths and ancestor weights is dense — include schematic, CPF‑AS pseudocode for non‑Markovian case, and explicit proposal statement and complexity table.

Originality:
- High — novel fully Bayesian collapsed GP-SSM inference via PGAS relative to prior EM/variational/EP work.

Significance:
- Potentially high for nonparametric Bayesian time‑series identification; practical impact depends on achievable scalability (sparse variant and implementation tips are important).

Pros for acceptance:
- Principled, elegant Bayesian treatment of GP-SSMs with modern PMCMC.
- Strong methodological derivations for collapsed non‑Markovian PGAS.
- Practical sparse formulation and useful implementation techniques.
- Clear demonstrations on synthetic and control-system benchmarks.

Cons for acceptance:
- Insufficient empirical comparisons to strong baselines and missing runtime/scaling studies.
- High computational demands may limit applicability to long series or high dimensions.
- Ambiguities in SMC proposal choice and inducing point selection; limited hyperparameter mixing diagnostics.

Minor comments:
- Clarify whether and how inputs ut are included/scaled in the GP kernel.
- When suggesting compressing MoG predictive to a single GP, note loss of multimodality and give heuristics for when acceptable.
- Fix indexing/typographical inconsistencies and define matrices when first introduced.

Overall recommendation:
- Leaning toward acceptance, contingent on strengthening experiments (runtime/scaling, stronger baselines), hyperparameter diagnostics, and clarifications on proposal and inducing‑point selection.
