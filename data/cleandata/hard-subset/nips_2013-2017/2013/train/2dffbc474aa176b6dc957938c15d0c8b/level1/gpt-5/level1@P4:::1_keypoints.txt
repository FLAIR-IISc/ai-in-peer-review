- Proposes fully Bayesian inference for GP state-space models by marginalizing the transition function f and sampling latent states and hyperparameters with PGAS.  
- Marginalization yields a non-Markovian prior over trajectories and closed-form one-step predictive Gaussians; posterior over f is a mixture of Gaussians given state samples.  
- Supports sparse GP (FIC) to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Builds on prior GP dynamical systems, EM/MAP GPDM approaches, and PMCMC/PGAS literature; novelty is collapsing f and using PGAS for non-Markovian smoothing.  
- Derivations of collapsed one-step predictive and joint trajectory prior are mathematically consistent with GP regression plus process noise.  
- CPF-AS/PGAS construction and ancestor sampling weights are appropriate and correctly exploit cancellations in the collapsed prior.  
- Computational strategies (rank-1 Cholesky updates/downdates) reduce naive O(T^4) to O(T^3); FIC gives further reductions with caching.  
- Hyperparameters sampled via slice sampling using the collapsed GP regression view is reasonable.  
- Experiments show capability on a multimodal scalar benchmark and a 4D cart-pole, capturing multimodality and calibrated predictive uncertainty.  
- Sparse variant with 40 inducing points demonstrated practical reduction in computation.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: few datasets, mostly one-step RMSE metrics, and limited quantitative comparisons to strong GPDM/EM/EP baselines.  
- Scalability to long sequences and high-dimensional states is not extensively tested or reported.  
- Presentation is generally clear; sections on collapsed prior and PGAS sampler are precise and implementation details (Cholesky updates, FIC formulas) are useful.  
- Needs clearer treatment of multi-output GPs (assumptions, Q/kernel implications) and inducing input selection/interplay with PMCMC.  
- Reproducibility would benefit from concise pseudocode for CPF-AS including required matrix updates.  
- Originality: novel combination of exact marginalization of f, PGAS for non-Markovian smoothing, and closed-form posterior over f—distinct from prior parametric/approximate approaches.  
- Significance: advances fully Bayesian GP-SSMs and uncertainty-aware dynamics learning, with potential impact in system ID, control, and RL.  
- Pros: conceptually clean collapsing, appropriate inference machinery, preserves nonparametric expressivity, supports sparse approximations, and handles multimodal smoothing.  
- Cons: modest empirical validation, remaining scalability concerns, unspecified multi-output/inducing-input choices, and assumption of known/parametric measurement function g.  
- Suggested improvements: add quantitative comparisons to GPDM/EP/EM baselines, evaluate longer/higher-dimensional sequences and runtimes, clarify multi-output modeling and inducing-input selection, and provide CPF-AS pseudocode.  
- Overall: solid, novel, and well-presented contribution with strong theoretical/algorithmic foundations but needs broader empirical validation to strengthen practical impact.
