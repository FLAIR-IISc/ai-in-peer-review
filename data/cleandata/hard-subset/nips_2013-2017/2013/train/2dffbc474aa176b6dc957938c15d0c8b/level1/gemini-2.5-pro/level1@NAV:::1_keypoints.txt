- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing a GP prior over the state transition function for unknown nonlinear dynamics.
- Analytically marginalizes the infinite-dimensional dynamics function, which breaks the Markov property of the state sequence.
- Overcomes non-Markovian challenge using Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample the joint smoothing distribution.
- Novel departure from prior work that used parametric GP approximations or MAP estimation, enabling a true nonparametric Bayesian treatment.
- Technically sound derivations and correct application of PGAS to the marginalized non-Markovian model.
- Experimental validation on a challenging nonlinear system with a multimodal posterior demonstrates robustness.
- Acknowledges and addresses computational cost: vanilla method is O(T^3), with a principled sparse GP extension reducing cost to O(M^2 T).
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and detailed enough for expert reproduction.
- Highly original combination of marginalization and advanced PMCMC methods.
- Significant contribution likely to impact system identification, time-series analysis, robotics, and control.
- Strong case for acceptance: novel, fully Bayesian, expressive, and empirically superior.
- No substantive objections; computational scalability for very long series is a limitation but is recognized and mitigated.
