- Presents a fully Bayesian method for inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models.  
- Uses a Gaussian process prior over the state transition dynamics for flexible modeling of complex systems.  
- Marginalizes the transition function and directly infers the joint smoothing distribution to enable efficient inference.  
- Main technical contribution: a tailored particle MCMC algorithm (particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling, PGAS) for sampling the smoothing distribution while marginalizing dynamics.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart-and-pole system.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and mathematically consistent.  
- GP priors are a natural and expressive choice for transition dynamics.  
- Marginalization of the transition function is a key insight that improves inference efficiency.  
- PGAS is a significant algorithmic contribution and appears to capture underlying dynamics accurately in experiments.  
- Limitation: assumes the observation model is known, which reduces applicability to many real-world problems where it must be learned.  
- Limitation: computational complexity is high (O(T^3) for full GP, O(M^2 T) for sparse GP), potentially limiting scalability.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to Bayesian inference in state-space models with solid clarity and significance (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10).
