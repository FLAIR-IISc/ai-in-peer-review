- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function and uses a tailored PMCMC (PGAS) sampler for joint smoothing.
- Avoids needing a fixed parametric transition model, addressing a gap in existing GP-SSM methods.
- Demonstrates ability to capture multimodal smoothing distributions and learns from limited data (synthetic and cart-pole experiments).
- Uses sparse GP variant to mitigate computational costs and details efficient implementation (Cholesky factorization).
- Builds on and is well-motivated by prior GP-SSM literature, with sensible methodological choices for non-Markovian posteriors.
- Technical derivations are clear and detailed, showing how to marginalize the transition and implement the sampler efficiently.
- Experiments are promising and include reasonable comparisons to other approaches.
- Main weakness: limited experimental validation, especially lacking tests in higher-dimensional state spaces to demonstrate scalability.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is not thoroughly investigated, leaving robustness questions unanswered.
- Lacks a detailed ablation study comparing full Bayesian treatment to simpler alternatives (e.g., MAP estimation).
- Discussion on selection and placement of inducing points for the sparse GP is insufficient and could be expanded.
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, originality in marginalizing dynamics, and broad significance for learning nonlinear dynamics.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope and inadequate analysis of hyperparameter effects.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experimental validation and address hyperparameter/inducing-point discussions.
