- Presents a fully Bayesian inference and learning approach for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using Gaussian process priors over transition dynamics.
- Employs a PMCMC algorithm to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the state transition function.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations, notation, and helpful figures.
- Related to prior GP-SSM work (Deisenroth, Turner) but differs by using a nonparametric transition representation and marginalizing the function.
- Main contributions: development of a PMCMC sampler for the smoothing distribution and a sparse GP-SSM construction to reduce computational cost.
- Technically sound with theoretical support and clear methodological exposition.
- Experiments (nonlinear benchmark, cart-pole) demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.
- Quality: claims supported by analysis and experiments.
- Clarity: presentation and notation are clear.
- Originality: offers a novel nonparametric/marginalization approach within GP-SSMs.
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potentially wide applicability.
- Pros for acceptance: novel method, solid theory, effective experiments, clear writing.
- Cons for acceptance: close relation to prior GP-SSM work may limit perceived novelty.
- Cons for acceptance: computational complexity of the algorithm could be a concern.
- Cons for acceptance: would benefit from more detailed comparisons to other state-space inference methods.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
