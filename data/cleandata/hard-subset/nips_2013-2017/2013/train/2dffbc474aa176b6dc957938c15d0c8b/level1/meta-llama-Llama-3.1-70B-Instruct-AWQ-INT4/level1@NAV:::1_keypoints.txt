The paper proposes marginalizing the GP transition function and using particle MCMC to directly infer the joint smoothing distribution in Gaussian process state-space models.

A fully Bayesian GP prior over state transitions gives a flexible nonparametric model capable of capturing complex dynamical behavior.

Marginalizing the dynamics and using PMCMC preserves GP expressivity while enabling efficient inference and learning.

The method is demonstrated on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart-and-pole system, showing accurate dynamics learning and high-quality smoothing distributions.

The approach is novel and offers an efficient route to inference in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models.

Being fully Bayesian allows principled treatment and quantification of uncertainty in the learned model.

The paper is well-written, clearly explains technical details, and is well-organized and easy to follow.

Reliance on particle MCMC samplers can be computationally expensive, especially for large datasets.

The paper lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity, limiting assessment of scalability to large-scale applications.

The evaluation could benefit from more thorough comparisons with existing inference and learning methods for state-space models.

Using PMCMC to marginalize the transition dynamics is an original and significant methodological contribution.

The method has potential impact across domains like robotics, finance, and healthcare by improving modeling and control of complex dynamical systems.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of method limitations but could expand empirical comparisons.

Arguments for acceptance: methodological novelty, fully Bayesian framework, and empirical demonstrations of effectiveness.

Arguments against acceptance: computational cost concerns, missing complexity analysis, and limited comparative evaluation.
