This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models, using Gaussian process priors over the state transition dynamics. The authors employ a particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithm to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution, while marginalizing over the state transition function. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach.

The paper is closely related to previous work on Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs), such as Deisenroth et al. [5, 6] and Turner et al. [2]. However, the proposed approach differs from these previous works in that it uses a nonparametric representation of the state transition function and marginalizes over this function to obtain the smoothing distribution.

The main contributions of the paper are the development of a PMCMC algorithm for sampling from the smoothing distribution and the demonstration of the effectiveness of this approach on several examples. The authors also provide a sparse GP-SSM construction, which reduces the computational complexity of the algorithm.

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach on several examples, including a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart and pole system.
