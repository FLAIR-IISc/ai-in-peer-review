The authors propose a Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models (SSMs) using Gaussian process (GP) priors. The key contribution of this work is the development of a particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithm that enables efficient inference over the state trajectory and hyper-parameters while marginalizing out the state transition function. This approach preserves the full nonparametric expressivity of the model and can make use of sparse GPs to reduce computational complexity.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of learning nonlinear SSMs and motivate the use of GP priors. They also provide a thorough review of related work, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches and the novelty of their contribution.

The technical soundness of the paper is strong. The authors provide a detailed derivation of the GP-SSM model and the PMCMC algorithm, including the marginalization of the state transition function and the use of ancestor sampling. They also discuss the implementation details of the algorithm, including the use of sparse GPs and the efficient computation of the prior over state trajectories.

The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in learning nonlinear SSMs. The results show that the GP-SSM model can accurately capture the underlying dynamics of the system and provide a robust representation of the state transition function. The authors also demonstrate the flexibility of the model by using a parametric mean function as a baseline and showing that the GP-SSM can still accurately capture the dynamics.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide clear explanations of the technical concepts and use visualizations to illustrate the results. The notation is consistent throughout the paper, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the mathematical derivations.
