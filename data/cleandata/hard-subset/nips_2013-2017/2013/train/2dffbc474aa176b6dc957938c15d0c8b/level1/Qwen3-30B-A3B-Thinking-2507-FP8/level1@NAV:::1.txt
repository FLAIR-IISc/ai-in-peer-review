

This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs), addressing the challenge of learning nonlinear nonparametric state transition dynamics. The authors propose marginalizing out the transition dynamics function and inferring the joint smoothing distribution using Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) samplers, thereby preserving the full nonparametric expressivity of the model. This approach contrasts with previous work that typically resorts to parametric approximations of GP dynamics (e.g., Turner et al., 2009; Wang et al., 2005), while building on recent advances in PMCMC (Andrieu et al., 2010; Lindsten et al., 2012).

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with a clear theoretical foundation. The marginalization approach is elegant and well-motivated, with thorough analysis of computational complexity (O(T³) for vanilla GP and O(M²T) for sparse GP). The experiments on a nonlinear benchmark system and cart-pole dynamics effectively demonstrate the approach's ability to capture multimodal smoothing distributions and complex dynamics where parametric models fail. The sparse GP implementation using FIC (Fully Independent Conditional) prior is particularly well-executed, providing significant computational benefits.

The paper excels in originality, making a clear contribution to the field of Bayesian nonparametric modeling for dynamical systems. It directly addresses a gap in the literature by providing a fully Bayesian learning framework for GP-SSMs, which previous work (e.g., Deisenroth et al., 2011) had not adequately solved. The significance is high, as the approach enables more flexible modeling of complex dynamical systems with principled uncertainty quantification.

Minor weaknesses include somewhat limited comparison to related work (e.g., Deisenroth's GP-SSM filtering methods) and limited discussion of practical computational costs in real-world applications. The paper could also better highlight how this approach advances state-of-the-art beyond existing GP-SSM methods.

Pro: Novel marginalization approach preserves nonparametric expressivity, strong theoretical foundation, effective empirical validation, significant contribution to Bayesian inference for dynamical systems.

Con: Some related work not sufficiently contextualized, limited discussion of practical computational costs, sparse implementation details could be more accessible.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field with strong technical merits, clear presentation, and meaningful empirical results. It meets all NIPS criteria for a high-quality scientific contribution.
