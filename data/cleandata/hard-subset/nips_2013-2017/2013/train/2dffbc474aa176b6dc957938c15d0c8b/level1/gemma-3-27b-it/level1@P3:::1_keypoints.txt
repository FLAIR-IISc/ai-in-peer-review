- Presents a fully Bayesian, nonparametric GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function and enables efficient inference via a tailored PMCMC sampler.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM work but crucially learns the GP dynamics rather than approximating or fixing them.  
- Theoretical derivations (marginalization in Sec. 3.1) and the PMCMC formulation (Sec. 3.3) are correct and well-motivated, with clear links to particle filtering, SMC, and MCMC.  
- Novel use of recent PMCMC advances to handle marginalization challenges is a significant and smart contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, consistently notated, and flows logically from problem statement to experiments.  
- Experiments (synthetic benchmark and cart-pole) illustrate multimodal handling and uncertainty quantification useful for control/RL.  
- Implementation details (Section 4.2) discussing complexity and tricks like reusing Cholesky factorizations improve reproducibility and practical awareness.  
- Major concern: computational cost remains high (sparse GP O(M^2 T)), raising scalability issues for high-dimensional or large-scale problems.  
- Lacks detailed discussion on hyperparameter selection and sensitivity to prior choices, which is important in practice.  
- Missing comparisons to other Bayesian nonparametric SSM approaches (e.g., Dirichlet Process mixtures) limits assessment of relative performance and cost.  
- Choice of FIC sparse GP prior is not fully justified; discussion of alternatives (e.g., variational sparse GPs) and trade-offs is needed.  
- Experiments are illustrative but small-scale; additional complex control tasks or RL comparisons would strengthen practical claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound advance in Bayesian nonparametric SSMs with clear motivations and implementation insights.  
- Arguments for rejection: significant computational limitations, insufficient hyperparameter discussion, limited comparisons and experiment scope.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper meriting acceptance with minor revisions to address hyperparameter tuning, comparisons to alternatives, scalability, and sparse GP trade-offs.
