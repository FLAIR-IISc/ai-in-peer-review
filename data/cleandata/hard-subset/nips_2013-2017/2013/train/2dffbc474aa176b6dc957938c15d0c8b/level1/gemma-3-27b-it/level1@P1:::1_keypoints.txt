- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM approach that marginalizes the transition function and uses PMCMC for inference on the smoothing distribution.  
- Novelty: marginalizing the transition dynamics preserves full nonparametric expressivity versus parametric GP approximations.  
- Technical soundness: derivations and justification for PMCMC are clear; Algorithm 1 and implementation details are well presented.  
- Uses sparse GPs (FIC prior) and implementation optimizations to address computational challenges.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized with accessible high-level motivation; some implementation sections are dense.  
- Significance: enables learning complex nonlinear dynamics without strong parametric assumptions; promising results on cart-pole with uncertainty estimates.  
- Completeness: includes theoretical derivations, algorithmic details, and experiments; helpful discussion of implementation optimizations.  
- Main weakness: computational complexity remains a concern, especially for high-dimensional states or long time series, and scaling with T is nontrivial.  
- Experimental validation is limited: illustrative benchmarks are weak and the cart-pole lacks rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art and contextualized RMSEs.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is not addressed; no sensitivity analysis for kernel parameters or particle count.  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees on convergence or approximation error for the PMCMC sampler.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, principled Bayesian approach, clear presentation, and promising (if limited) experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational scaling concerns, limited experiments and comparisons, missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and absent theoretical guarantees.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — expand experimental validation, discuss computational limitations in more detail, and include a hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
