- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function and infers the joint smoothing distribution via PMCMC.  
- Marginalization preserves full nonparametric expressivity, avoiding parametric approximations used in prior work.  
- Builds on recent PMCMC advances to enable exact inference over latent states in GP-SSMs.  
- Strong theoretical foundation and elegant marginalization approach.  
- Provides computational complexity analysis: O(T^3) for full GP and O(M^2 T) for sparse GP.  
- Implements a sparse GP using the FIC prior, yielding substantial computational benefits.  
- Experiments (nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole) demonstrate ability to capture multimodal smoothing and complex dynamics where parametric models fail.  
- Original and significant contribution addressing a gap in Bayesian nonparametric modeling of dynamical systems.  
- Enables principled uncertainty quantification and more flexible modeling of complex dynamics.  
- Minor weakness: limited comparison to related GP-SSM work (e.g., Deisenroth et al.).  
- Minor weakness: insufficient discussion of practical computational costs in real-world applications.  
- Minor weakness: sparse implementation details could be clearer and more accessible.
