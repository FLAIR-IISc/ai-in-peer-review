- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the dynamics function f, keeping the nonparametric prior but breaking state Markovness.  
- Performs inference in the resulting non-Markovian model using Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) and a Conditional Particle Filter (CPF-AS) to sample full state trajectories.  
- Samples hyperparameters and states alternately; posterior predictive for f is obtained as a mixture of Gaussians.  
- Introduces a sparse FIC GP-SSM variant to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) for better scalability.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior work by fully marginalizing the GP and providing a principled Bayesian alternative to EM/MAP and filtering-only approaches.  

Strengths:
- Methodologically sound and original: marginalization avoids common GP-SSM approximations and the PGAS application is novel and appropriate.  
- Provides full uncertainty quantification over states and hyperparameters, demonstrated by capturing multimodal smoothing distributions.  
- Paper is clear and well organized, with a logical presentation of motivation, model, and inference.  
- Practical scalability addressed via the sparse FIC formulation for longer time series.

Weaknesses:
- Limited experimental comparison: lacks quantitative benchmarks versus variational or EM-based GP-SSM methods and quantitative metrics for tasks like cart-pole.  
- Computational cost and batch nature remain concerns; O(M^2 T) can be prohibitive for very long series or online applications.  
- Some practical implementation details are under-discussed (e.g., inducing input placement, challenges in sampling GP hyperparameters and chain mixing).

- Overall: significant, high-quality, and well-motivated contribution to Bayesian learning in nonlinear dynamical systems; recommended for acceptance.
