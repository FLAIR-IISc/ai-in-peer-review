- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function and uses Particle MCMC for efficient inference.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM work and addresses limitations of parametric approximations.  
- Core contribution is collapsing the dynamics function and leveraging recent PMCMC advances.  
- Mathematical derivations are sound and the PMCMC choice is well-justified.  
- Authors acknowledge approximations in the sampling algorithm and present clear marginalization steps.  
- Inclusion of a sparse FIC GP variant improves practicality and scalability.  
- Experiments show effectiveness but the benchmark suite is somewhat limited in scope.  
- The cart-pole experiment provides a convincing demonstration of utility.  
- Paper is dense with challenging notation for readers unfamiliar with GPs and SMC.  
- Organization is logical, but more implementation detail and pseudo-code would aid reproducibility.  
- The approach is original in collapsing transition dynamics within a fully Bayesian framework.  
- Properly situates the work relative to related methods and highlights novelty versus [2,5,8].  
- Method is significant for learning complex dynamics without strong parametric assumptions and for uncertainty quantification.  
- Ability to incorporate prior knowledge via the GP mean function is a practical advantage.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, experimental evidence, and thorough presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental realism, dense exposition, and potential reproducibility challenges.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; accept with minor revisions to add implementation details and expand experiments.
