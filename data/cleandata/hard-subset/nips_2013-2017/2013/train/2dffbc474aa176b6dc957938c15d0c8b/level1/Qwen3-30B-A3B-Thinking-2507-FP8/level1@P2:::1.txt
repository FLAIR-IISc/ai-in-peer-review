

# Review of "Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC"

This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models (SSMs) by placing a Gaussian process (GP) prior over the state transition dynamics. The authors address the key challenge of learning GP dynamics without resorting to parametric approximations by marginalizing out the transition function and inferring the joint smoothing distribution using tailored Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) samplers, specifically Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS). This contrasts with previous work that either used parametric representations of the GP dynamics (e.g., [2,8]) or treated the GP as a finite set of target points, effectively making the model parametric.

The paper builds upon and extends recent advances in PMCMC methods [3,4] to handle the non-Markovian structure introduced by marginalizing the GP dynamics. The authors provide a rigorous mathematical derivation of the marginal prior over the state trajectory (Equations 3-4) and demonstrate how to efficiently implement the inference algorithm using Cholesky factorizations (Section 4.2). The paper also introduces a sparse GP-SSM construction using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computational complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2T), where M ≪ T.

**Strengths:**

*Quality*: The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims. The authors provide a clear theoretical foundation for their marginalization approach and demonstrate effective inference through PMCMC. The experimental results (Section 6) convincingly show the model's ability to handle multimodal smoothing distributions (Figure 2) and learn complex dynamics in both synthetic and real-world systems (cart and pole example). The authors are careful to acknowledge the limitations of their approach, particularly regarding computational complexity, and address this with a sparse GP implementation.

*Clarity*: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical derivations are presented with sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the results, and the figures effectively illustrate key concepts (e.g., Figure 2 shows how the GP-SSM captures multimodal smoothing distributions that parametric models cannot). The implementation details in Section 4.2 are particularly helpful for practitioners.

*Originality*: The paper makes a significant contribution by addressing a fundamental limitation in existing GP-SSM approaches. While GP-based SSMs have been explored in prior NIPS work (e.g., [5,6,8]), this paper's approach of fully marginalizing the transition dynamics and using tailored PMCMC samplers to handle the resulting non-Markovian structure is novel. The use of PGAS for non-Markovian latent variable models (Section 3.3.1) is particularly innovative and well-justified.

*Significance*: The results are highly significant for the field of Bayesian inference in dynamical systems. The ability to perform fully Bayesian inference in nonparametric SSMs enables more flexible modeling of complex dynamics without restrictive parametric assumptions. The paper demonstrates this with compelling experiments on both synthetic and real-world systems, showing clear advantages over parametric approaches. The sparse GP extension also makes the approach practical for larger datasets.

**Weaknesses:**

The paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison with recent nonparametric SSM approaches beyond the cited works. While the authors reference Deisenroth et al.'s work on GP-SSMs [5,6], they don't explicitly compare their marginalization approach with other recent methods for learning GP dynamics in SSMs. A brief discussion of how this work differs from, say, the variational inference approaches in [7] would strengthen the contribution statement.

Additionally, while the computational complexity analysis is sound, the paper lacks concrete runtime comparisons with alternative methods. The sparse GP section (Section 4) shows promise for scalability, but it would be valuable to see how the proposed method compares in terms of wall-clock time to other approaches on the same hardware.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- Addresses a fundamental limitation in existing GP-SSM approaches by enabling fully Bayesian inference without parametric approximations
- Provides a theoretically sound and practically implementable solution using tailored PMCMC samplers
- Demonstrates clear advantages on both synthetic and real-world benchmarks
- Offers a sparse implementation to make the approach scalable to larger datasets

*Con:*
- Limited comparison with recent nonparametric SSM methods beyond the cited works
- Lacks concrete runtime comparisons with alternative approaches
- The sparse GP section could more explicitly discuss trade-offs between accuracy and computational efficiency

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of Bayesian inference in dynamical systems. The technical approach is sound, the writing is clear, and the results are significant. The authors have successfully addressed a key challenge in GP-SSMs that has limited the applicability of previous approaches. While the paper could benefit from more thorough comparisons with recent alternatives, the core contribution is novel, well-executed, and addresses a meaningful problem in the field. The sparse GP extension also makes the approach more practical for real-world applications. I recommend acceptance as a strong contribution to NIPS.
