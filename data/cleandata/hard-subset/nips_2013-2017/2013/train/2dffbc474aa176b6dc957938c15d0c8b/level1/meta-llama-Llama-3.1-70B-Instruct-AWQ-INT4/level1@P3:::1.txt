This paper presents a Bayesian approach to inference and learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs), which are nonlinear nonparametric models for time series and dynamical systems. The authors place a Gaussian process prior over the state transition dynamics, allowing for flexible modeling of complex phenomena. They then use a tailored particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithm to efficiently sample from the joint smoothing distribution, marginalizing over the state transition function.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their approach. The use of GP-SSMs is well-motivated, and the authors provide a good review of the relevant literature. The technical contributions of the paper are significant, and the authors provide a detailed description of their algorithm and its implementation.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors provide a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in GP-SSMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The use of PMCMC algorithms is well-suited to the problem, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how they adapt these algorithms to the GP-SSM setting.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including a comparison to other methods and an analysis of the computational complexity of their algorithm.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The paper assumes a significant amount of background knowledge in Gaussian processes and state-space models, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more detail on the implementation of their algorithm, particularly with respect to the choice of hyperparameters and the initialization of the Markov chain.
* The paper could benefit from more discussion of the limitations of the approach and potential avenues for future research.
