This paper presents a fully Bayesian framework for inference and learning in state-space models (SSMs) with unknown nonlinear dynamics. The authors place a Gaussian Process (GP) prior over the state transition function, resulting in a flexible, nonparametric GP-SSM. The central contribution is an elegant inference strategy that analytically marginalizes out the infinite-dimensional dynamics function. This breaks the Markov property of the state sequence, a challenge the authors overcome by employing a tailored Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) sampler, specifically Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS), to sample from the joint smoothing distribution of the latent states. This approach is a notable departure from prior work that often resorts to parametric approximations of the GP (e.g., Turner et al., AISTATS 2011) or MAP estimation (e.g., Wang et al., NIPS 2005).

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents a complete and carefully executed piece of work. The derivation of the marginalized prior and the application of PGAS to this non-Markovian model are correct and well-motivated. Claims are convincingly supported by experiments on a challenging nonlinear system with a multimodal posterior, demonstrating the method's robustness. The authors are honest about computational complexity, addressing the vanilla model's O(T³) cost by thoroughly detailing a principled sparse GP extension that reduces complexity to O(M²T).

**Clarity:** The manuscript is exceptionally well-written and organized. It guides the reader logically from the model definition and the core technical challenge to the inference algorithm and experimental validation. The notation is consistent, and the level of detail is sufficient for an expert to understand and potentially reproduce the work.

**Originality:** The work is highly original. The combination of marginalizing the GP dynamics function and then applying advanced PMCMC methods for inference is a novel and powerful idea. This allows for a truly nonparametric Bayesian treatment that was previously elusive, clearly distinguishing this contribution from existing GP-SSM literature.

**Significance:** The results are important and likely to have a substantial impact. The paper provides a principled and powerful tool for system identification in complex dynamical systems where first-principle models are unavailable. The ability to learn the dynamics and infer latent states simultaneously in a fully Bayesian way is of high interest to researchers in time-series analysis, robotics, and control. This work demonstrably advances the state of the art and is likely to be widely used and built upon.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Presents a novel and elegant solution to the fundamental problem of learning in nonparametric dynamical systems.
*   The method is technically sound, fully Bayesian, and retains the expressivity of the GP model.
*   Results on challenging benchmarks demonstrate a clear advance over standard approaches.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
*   None of significance. The computational cost, even with the sparse variant, may be a limitation for extremely long time series, but this is an inherent challenge that the authors acknowledge and address effectively.
