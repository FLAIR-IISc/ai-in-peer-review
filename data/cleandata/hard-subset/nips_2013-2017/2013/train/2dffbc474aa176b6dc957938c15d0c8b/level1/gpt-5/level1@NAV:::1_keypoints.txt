- Presents a fully Bayesian approach to nonlinear GP-SSMs by placing a GP prior on transition f and collapsing f to obtain a non‑Markovian prior over trajectories p(x0:T | θ).
- Performs joint smoothing and hyperparameter inference with Particle MCMC, specifically Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS).
- Given trajectory samples, the posterior over f is available in closed form via standard GP regression.
- Introduces a sparse FIC construction to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).
- Empirically demonstrates accurate smoothing under multimodal posteriors and competitive transition prediction on a nonlinear benchmark.
- Shows calibrated one‑step predictive uncertainty on a 4D cart‑pole system.
- Methodological strengths: collapsing f reduces state–function coupling; CPF‑AS is appropriate for non‑Markovian latents.
- Derivations (collapsed prior, FIC updates) are careful, with clear complexity analysis and practical implementation tips (rank‑one Cholesky updates).
- Empirical evaluation is modest: lacks mixing diagnostics, sensitivity analyses to particle/lag settings (N/L), robustness to kernel misspecification, and runtime comparisons to baselines.
- Measurement‑model generality is claimed but experiments only use Gaussian likelihoods.
- Clarity is generally good and actionable (sections 3–4), but notation is heavy in places and a schematic for CPF‑AS would improve readability.
- Inducing‑point selection discussion could be more prescriptive.
- Originality: novel integration of collapsed GP dynamics with PGAS, distinct from prior EM/MAP and fixed‑GP filtering/smoothing approaches.
- Significance: enables fully Bayesian learning in flexible dynamical models and could be a building block for filtering/control.
- Major concerns: scalability to long horizons and high‑dimensional states not convincingly demonstrated despite the sparse variant.
- Additional cons: limited empirical breadth and ablations; assumes known observation model so identifiability and joint learning of f and g are not addressed.
