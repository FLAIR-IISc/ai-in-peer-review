- Paper collapses the GP transition f to obtain an exact non-Markovian prior over state trajectories and uses PGAS to perform fully Bayesian inference over states and hyperparameters.
- Hyperparameters are sampled via Gibbs-within-PGAS with slice sampling for GP covariance and Q, and the posterior over f is recovered post hoc by GP conditioning on sampled trajectories.
- Introduces a sparse FIC inducing-variable prior to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) with careful Cholesky/low-rank updates across time and particles.
- Empirical illustrations include a scalar multimodal smoothing benchmark and a 4D cart-pole demonstrating predictive performance and uncertainty quantification.
- Novelty: collapsing the GP transition combined with PGAS for fully Bayesian nonparametric GP-SSMs is original and valuable.
- Strength: Conceptual clarity and technical soundness—collapsing reduces x–f coupling and PGAS with ancestor sampling is a principled choice for the induced non-Markovian model.
- Strength: Retains nonparametric expressivity; posterior over f and predictive distributions reflect smoothing uncertainty as mixtures of Gaussians.
- Strength: Useful computational derivations (collapsed prior, ancestor sampling weights) and practical FIC scalability insights.
- Strength: Practical modeling guidance on mean functions and viewing hyperparameter updates as GP regression conditioned on trajectories.
- Experimental results are promising but limited in scope and lacking comparisons to strong GP-SSM baselines (EM/MAP, variational GP-SSMs, PMMH).
- Cart-pole results are limited to one-step predictive moments; missing multi-step rollouts, predictive log-likelihoods, control metrics, and runtime/scaling measurements.
- Ambiguity in the SMC proposal (Sec. 3.2): unclear if p(xt | θ, x0:t−1) or p(xt | θ, x0:t−1, y0:t) is used and how the locally optimal proposal is computed efficiently in the non-Markovian setting.
- Scalability and memory concerns: per-particle Cholesky factors over concatenated paths imply large memory costs for big T/N, and FIC implementation details for achieving O(M^2 T) are terse.
- Identifiability concerns between GP kernel parameters and process noise Q; sensitivity to mean function misspecification and priors on Q is not analyzed.
- Inducing input placement is not thoroughly evaluated; fixing inducing sets and using a greedy method may affect inference and mixing without study of M or placement effects.
- Theoretical/diagnostic gap: no discussion of convergence/mixing when alternating θ-updates with CPF-AS in the collapsed model, and lacking diagnostics like ESS or autocorrelation.
- Clarity is generally good with helpful derivations, but Sec. 3.2, Sec. 4.2, and hyperparameter/slice sampler details need clarification and more concrete pseudo-code or complexity/memory tables.
- Overall quality: method is technically sound and well-grounded in PMCMC, but some implementation/proposal details require clarification.
- Significance: Addresses a key challenge in fully Bayesian GP-SSMs without parametric approximations and could have high impact with stronger experiments and scalability demos.
- Suggested improvements: add baselines (EM/MAP, PMMH, variational GP-SSMs), metrics (predictive log-likelihood, multi-step prediction, calibration), runtime/memory scaling, proposal ablations, and mixing diagnostics.
- Suggested improvements: analyze sensitivity to mean functions, kernels, and priors on Q; consider hierarchical priors or constraints to mitigate identifiability.
- Suggested improvements: detail inducing input selection, evaluate M and placement effects, and consider adaptive inducing strategies.
- Pros: principled fully Bayesian treatment, clever PGAS use for non-Markovian smoothing, practical scalability via FIC, and clear modeling story.
- Cons: limited empirical validation and comparisons, ambiguity in proposal mechanics, incomplete implementation/scalability details, and unaddressed sensitivity/identifiability issues.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — strong methodological contribution but needs improved empirical validation and clarifications.
