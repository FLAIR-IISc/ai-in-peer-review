- Collapses the GP transition f exactly to obtain a non-Markovian prior over state trajectories p(x0:T|θ) that factorizes via GP one-step predictive Gaussians.  
- Uses Particle MCMC (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling, PGAS) to sample states under the induced non-Markovian prior, leveraging PGAS invariance for exact smoothing.  
- Hyperparameters θ are sampled via standard GP regression given sampled trajectories; a sparse FIC variant reduces complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Implementation emphasizes reusing Cholesky factorizations and rank-one updates/downdates, and maintaining factorizations for ancestor sampling.  
- Posterior over f at test inputs is a mixture-of-Gaussians obtained by GP regression conditioned on sampled trajectories.  
- Experiments: nonlinear scalar benchmark with multimodal smoothing and a 4D cart-pole system showing uncertainty-aware predictions.  
- Related to GPDM, EM/MAP GP-SSM, and EP-based GP filtering/smoothing; novelty lies in fully Bayesian collapsing of f plus PGAS for non-Markovian sampling and explicit sparse prior implementation.  
- Strength: Conceptually clean—exact marginalization retains nonparametric expressivity and removes strong f–x posterior coupling.  
- Strength: Algorithmic design is solid—appropriate use of PGAS for non-Markovian latent structures with practical invariance arguments.  
- Strength: Integrates sparse FIC prior into trajectory prior with clear complexity reduction and implementation guidance.  
- Strength: Naturally handles multimodal smoothing and provides a rich mixture posterior over f reflecting epistemic uncertainty.  
- Strength: Can leverage prior mean functions to incorporate first-principles models.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental evaluation—no quantitative comparisons to strong baselines (EM/MAP, EP, parametric PMCMC, UKF/EKS) or runtime/scaling analyses.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns not fully addressed—per-iteration PGAS costs, effect of state dimension nx on complexity/mixing, and larger systems are unexplored.  
- Weakness: Inducing point selection and sensitivity to M/locations are unspecified and unstudied.  
- Weakness: Assumes known observation model g; identifiability and extension to unknown/GP observation models are only briefly discussed.  
- Weakness: Some derivations are terse (e.g., Eq. 3); numerical stability of repeated downdates is not discussed.  
- Quality: Technical approach is sound and consistent with prior work; PGAS for non-Markovian models is appropriate and credible.  
- Quality: Empirical support is qualitative and promising but lacks quantitative metrics and baseline comparisons needed for a strong claim.  
- Clarity: Well-written with clear setup, but notational density and compact derivations could be eased; per-iteration complexity and CPF-AS pseudocode would help.  
- Originality: Collapsing f in GP-SSMs and applying PGAS to the resulting non-Markovian prior appears novel and meaningfully advances prior EM/MAP approaches.  
- Significance: Addresses important problem (fully Bayesian GP-SSM learning/smoothing) with potential impact if scaling and baseline performance are demonstrated.  
- Questions for authors: request per-iteration complexity in N, T, nx (and M for FIC), wall-clock times, inducing location strategy and sensitivity, mixing diagnostics (ESS/autocorr), kernel/hyperprior and sampler settings, extension to unknown g and identifiability, robustness to mean-function misspecification.  
- Minor suggestions: clarify Σt includes Q, discuss PD maintenance and jitter for downdates, explain cost of p(x̃t:T|xi0:t−1) and factor reuse, add quantitative metrics (RMSE, predictive log likelihood), and include additional real-world datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled fully Bayesian treatment, novelty in collapsing plus PGAS, handles multimodal smoothing and produces uncertainty-aware dynamics models.  
- Arguments against acceptance: thin empirical evaluation, missing runtime/mixing diagnostics, unclear scalability to higher-dimensional/longer sequences, and unresolved inducing point sensitivity.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept pending stronger empirical comparisons and scaling evidence.
