

# Peer Review: "Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC"

## Summary and Context

This paper addresses a fundamental challenge in learning nonlinear state-space models (SSMs) by proposing a fully Bayesian approach for Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs). The authors correctly identify that existing methods for GP-SSMs either rely on parametric approximations of the GP (e.g., [2, 8]) or use non-Bayesian approaches (e.g., MAP estimation [8]), which fail to capture the full uncertainty in the dynamics model. The key insight is marginalizing out the state transition function f, allowing for exact Bayesian inference over both the state trajectory and the dynamics model. The authors employ Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing over f, and introduce a sparse GP-SSM construction using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computational complexity from O(T³) to O(M²T).

This work builds on recent advances in PMCMC [3, 4] and sparse GP methods [15, 16], but makes a significant contribution by applying these techniques to the GP-SSM problem in a fully Bayesian manner, which has been a notable gap in the literature. Previous work by Deisenroth et al. [5, 6] focused on inference given a learned GP dynamics model, but not on learning the GP itself in a Bayesian framework.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Soundness**: The marginalization approach (Section 3.1) is well-justified and mathematically rigorous, providing a clear path to handle the non-Markovian dependencies introduced by marginalizing f. The derivation of the marginal prior (equation 4) is particularly elegant.

2. **Computational Efficiency**: The sparse GP-SSM construction (Section 4) is a valuable practical contribution that enables application to larger problems. The authors provide detailed analysis of the computational complexity and efficient implementation strategies, which is crucial for the method's applicability.

3. **Practical Demonstration**: The experiments on the nonlinear benchmark (Section 6.1) effectively demonstrate the method's ability to handle multimodal smoothing distributions and capture dynamics when the parametric baseline is misspecified. The cart-pole experiment (Section 6.2) shows promising results on a real-world control problem, with the model appropriately capturing uncertainty in different regions of the state space.

4. **Clear Presentation**: The paper is well-organized with clear mathematical notation and good figures. The algorithm descriptions (Algorithm 1, Section 3.3.1) are particularly well-executed and would enable reproducibility.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Theoretical Guarantees**: While the paper presents a technically sound approach, it lacks theoretical analysis of the PGAS sampler's convergence properties for the non-Markovian state process. The authors claim "very good mixing" (Section 1), but provide no theoretical justification or empirical evidence of convergence rates.

2. **Scalability Concerns**: The experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale problems (T=200, 100 observations). While the sparse GP construction reduces complexity to O(M²T), it's unclear how well the method scales to longer time series (T > 1000) or higher-dimensional state spaces, which are common in real-world applications. The paper doesn't address how the number of inducing points M should scale with T.

3. **Comparison with Alternative Bayesian Methods**: The paper doesn't compare with other Bayesian approaches for learning SSMs, such as Bayesian neural networks [21] or Bayesian RNNs [22]. A comparison with these methods would strengthen the significance claim by demonstrating the unique advantages of GP-SSMs.

4. **Hyperparameter Selection**: The paper uses log-normal priors for hyperparameters without justification. It would be valuable to discuss how these priors were chosen or to include a sensitivity analysis. The authors also don't discuss how the inducing points for the sparse GP are selected in practice (beyond mentioning greedy methods [18]).

5. **Theoretical Limitations**: The paper claims "the exact marginalization of the transition dynamics" (Section 1), but the marginalization is only exact within the SMC approximation. The authors should clarify that this is an approximation to the true Bayesian posterior, not an exact solution.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong technical contribution that addresses a significant gap in the literature on GP-SSMs. The method is well-motivated, theoretically sound, and demonstrates practical utility. The main limitations are the lack of theoretical guarantees for the PGAS sampler, limited scalability analysis, and insufficient comparison with alternative Bayesian approaches.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field by enabling fully Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs while maintaining nonparametric expressivity. The sparse GP extension significantly enhances the method's practical applicability. However, the lack of scalability analysis and comparison with alternative Bayesian methods slightly limits the significance claim.

## Recommendation

**Accept** (with minor revisions). The paper is technically sound, makes a clear contribution to the literature, and is well-written. The identified weaknesses are important but do not undermine the core contribution. I recommend the authors address the following points in revision:

1. Add a brief discussion of the theoretical convergence properties of the PGAS sampler for non-Markovian state processes.
2. Include a scalability analysis showing performance on larger time series (e.g., T=1000).
3. Add a comparison with at least one alternative Bayesian approach for learning SSMs (e.g., Bayesian neural networks).
4. Provide more details on hyperparameter selection and the practical choice of inducing points.

These revisions would strengthen the paper's theoretical foundation and better establish its significance relative to existing methods.

## Final Assessment

The paper makes an important contribution to Bayesian inference in dynamical systems by providing a fully Bayesian approach for GP-SSMs that maintains nonparametric expressivity. The marginalization approach is novel and well-executed, and the sparse GP extension makes the method practical. While the experimental evaluation could be expanded, the paper is technically sound and represents a significant step forward in the field. It is a strong candidate for NIPS.
