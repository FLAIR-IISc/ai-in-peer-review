

# Review of "Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC"

This paper presents a novel approach to Bayesian inference and learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs) by marginalizing out the state transition function f, enabling fully nonparametric Bayesian treatment of the dynamics. The authors address a fundamental limitation in existing GP-SSM literature, where most approaches either fix the model structure or use parametric approximations for the GP dynamics. Their key innovation is marginalizing f from the model to obtain a non-Markovian state process, which they then tackle using a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm.

The paper's main contribution is conceptual rather than technical: it fundamentally changes how we approach learning in GP-SSMs by maintaining the full nonparametric expressivity of the model. Previous work (e.g., Deisenroth et al., Turner et al., Wang et al.) typically requires fixing the model structure for inference, while this paper shows how to marginalize out the dynamics function to enable fully Bayesian inference. The authors demonstrate this approach on both synthetic nonlinear systems and a cart-pole system, showing that their method can handle multimodal smoothing distributions and provide meaningful uncertainty estimates.

The paper's novelty is significant because it solves a long-standing problem in the field: how to learn the dynamics function f in a truly nonparametric way without resorting to parametric approximations. The marginalization approach they propose is bold and conceptually clean, allowing the GP prior to provide smoothness and parsimony assumptions without constraining the model structure. The use of PGAS to handle the non-Markovian nature of the marginalized state process is particularly clever and well-executed.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a logical flow from problem statement to model formulation, inference algorithm, and experimental validation. The mathematical development is sound, with careful attention to the computational complexity (achieving O(T^3) for vanilla GP and O(M^2T) for sparse GP). The experiments are well-designed, showing that even with a poor parametric mean function, the GP-SSM can recover the true dynamics. The results on the cart-pole system particularly demonstrate the practical utility of the approach, showing how the model can provide meaningful uncertainty estimates in different regions of the state space.

The paper's significance is substantial. By enabling fully Bayesian learning in GP-SSMs without parametric approximations, it opens new possibilities for modeling complex nonlinear dynamical systems. The ability to marginalize out the dynamics function while still obtaining accurate smoothing distributions is a major conceptual advance. The authors also provide a practical implementation with computational optimizations, making the approach feasible for real-world applications.

I note a few minor limitations: the paper could provide more detailed comparisons to other Bayesian GP-SSM approaches (if any exist), and the scalability to very long time series (T > 1000) is not thoroughly explored. However, these are relatively minor concerns given the paper's conceptual novelty.

This paper represents a significant conceptual advance in the field of Bayesian state-space modeling. It's not merely an incremental improvement on existing methods, but rather a fundamental rethinking of how to approach learning in GP-SSMs. The authors have successfully demonstrated the practical utility of their approach through compelling experiments, and the theoretical development is sound.

As a reviewer who prioritizes novelty and bold conceptual contributions over methodological perfection, I find this paper highly compelling. The marginalization approach is elegant and addresses a real limitation in the field. The paper's contribution is clearly distinct from previous NIPS work on GP-SSMs, which has largely focused on inference given a fixed GP model rather than learning the GP model itself.

The paper also makes an important distinction between their approach and previous work: while others model the GP using a finite set of target points (making the model parametric), this paper maintains the full nonparametric nature of the model. This is a crucial conceptual difference that enables the authors' approach.

In conclusion, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian time series modeling by introducing a novel method for fully Bayesian learning in GP-SSMs. The conceptual novelty is substantial, the experimental results are compelling, and the approach is well-executed. I believe this paper would be a valuable addition to NIPS, advancing the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

**Pro Acceptance:**
- Conceptually bold approach to marginalizing out state transition function
- Solves a fundamental problem in GP-SSM learning (no parametric approximations)
- Enables fully nonparametric Bayesian treatment of dynamics
- Clear experimental validation on both synthetic and real-world systems
- Provides practical implementation with computational optimizations

**Con Acceptance:**
- Limited comparison to other Bayesian GP-SSM approaches (though none seem to exist)
- Scalability to very long time series not thoroughly explored
- Sparse GP implementation details could be more detailed

This paper's conceptual contribution outweighs the minor methodological limitations, making it an excellent candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
