- Paper proposes a fully Bayesian approach for GP state-space models by marginalizing the GP dynamics and using Particle MCMC (PGAS) for inference.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computational cost.  
- Builds on prior nonparametric SSM and PMCMC work but uniquely avoids parametric approximations of the transition function.  
- Theoretical derivations (marginalized prior and PGAS algorithm) are rigorous and well-supported by literature.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, showing capability on multimodal smoothing (synthetic) and real-world control (cart and pole).  
- Demonstrates advantages over parametric baselines and provides richer uncertainty via mixture-of-Gaussians predictive distributions.  
- Authors acknowledge computational cost and offer the sparse FIC approximation as a practical remedy.  
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and uses consistent, clear mathematical notation.  
- Algorithm descriptions are sufficiently detailed for experts to implement; figures effectively illustrate concepts and results.  
- Explanation of computational complexity reduction for the sparse GP (Section 4.2) is slightly unclear and could be clarified.  
- More diagnostics on PGAS sampler performance (ESS, autocorrelation, convergence) would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Comparisons to other Bayesian nonparametric SSM methods are limited and would be valuable.  
- No sensitivity analysis provided for the choice or placement of inducing points in the sparse GP.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and significant contribution that advances GP-SSM inference while remaining practically implementable.  
- Recommendation: accept.
