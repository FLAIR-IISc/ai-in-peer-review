- Summary: Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM learned via a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm and a sparse FIC GP prior to reduce complexity from O(T⁴) to O(M²T).

- Main contribution: Fully marginalizes the GP state transition nonparametrically while using PGAS to sample the non-Markovian smoothing distribution.

- Technical novelty: Introduces an efficient sparse GP-SSM construction and implementation details enabling practical PMCMC inference.

- Theoretical soundness: Derivations rigorously address the non-Markovian process after marginalization and justify the inference approach.

- Computational efficiency (strength): FIC sparse prior and implementation reduce computational costs substantially, improving practical applicability.

- Experimental validation (strength): Demonstrates handling of multimodal smoothing, complex dynamics, and uncertainty on synthetic and cart-pole benchmarks.

- Presentation (strength): Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides precise algorithmic descriptions and reproducible experimental detail.

- Weakness — complexity: Even with FIC, O(M²T) remains relatively high compared to some alternative methods.

- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient study of sensitivity to key hyperparameters like inducing points M and particle count N.

- Weakness — lack of variational comparisons: No empirical comparison to variational inference approaches commonly used in Bayesian state-space models.

- Weakness — scalability: Experiments are limited to small T and low-dimensional states; scalability to larger, higher-dimensional systems is not demonstrated.

- Weakness — theoretical guarantees: Lacks formal convergence guarantees specific to applying PGAS in this marginalized GP-SSM setting.

- Specific comment — Section 3.3.1: Ancestor sampling probability explanation (Eq. 6) is confusing and would benefit from an intuitive clarification.

- Specific comment — Section 4.1: Unclear whether inducing inputs are fixed or updated during MCMC and how they interact with hyperparameter learning.

- Specific comment — Section 6.1: Suggest including a baseline standard GP regression treating transitions as observations for a more direct comparison.

- Specific comment — Figure 2: Recommend adding uncertainty intervals around predictive means to improve visualization of smoothing distributions.

- Specific comment — Table 1: Report standard deviations across runs to convey variability of RMSE results.

- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on technical contribution, sound methodology, and convincing experiments.

- Pro-acceptance arguments: Advances fully Bayesian nonparametric GP-SSM learning, marginalizes transition function, provides practical sparse implementation, and handles multimodal dynamics with uncertainty quantification.

- Con-acceptance arguments: Computational cost remains high, missing comparisons to variational methods, limited scalability evidence, and insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.

- Overall assessment: Technically sound, well-executed contribution that advances Bayesian learning for dynamical systems and is suitable for acceptance despite some practical and comparative limitations.
