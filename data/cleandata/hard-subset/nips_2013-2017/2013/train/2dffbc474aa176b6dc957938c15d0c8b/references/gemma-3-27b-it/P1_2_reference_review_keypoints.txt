- Presents a novel method combining Gaussian Processes with Particle MCMC to marginalize the transition function in GP-SSMs for a fully nonparametric model.  
- Tailors a PMCMC algorithm (with ancestor sampling) and uses sparse GP (FIC) for improved computational efficiency.  
- Validated on a synthetic multimodal smoothing benchmark and a cart-pole system, showing good mixing and predictive uncertainty estimation.  
- Positions work relative to prior GP-SSM literature, distinguishing from MAP/EM approaches by providing a fully Bayesian marginalization.  
- Leverages recent SMC/MCMC advances and situates itself well within relevant literature.  
- Strength: achieves true nonparametric modeling by marginalizing the transition function.  
- Strength: provides a principled Bayesian framework for coherent uncertainty quantification.  
- Strength: effective PMCMC implementation with ancestor sampling and sparse GP speedups.  
- Strength: clear mathematical derivations and implementation details that aid reproducibility.  
- Strength: experiments effectively illustrate handling of multimodal posteriors and practical predictive uncertainty.  
- Weakness: computationally demanding, with unclear scaling for high-dimensional states or long time series.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity and prior choices on performance.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons are limited; broader benchmarks against SOTA SSM methods are missing.  
- Weakness: choice of FIC prior is reasonable but lacks comparison to alternative sparse GP methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scaling discussion, expand experimental comparisons, explore hyperparameter impacts, and discuss practical limitations/future work.
