- Proposes fully Bayesian learning in GP state-space models by analytically marginalizing the GP transition f, inducing a non-Markov prior over states and sampling x0:T and θ with PGAS.
- Uses slice sampling for hyperparameters via the GP marginal likelihood conditioned on sampled state trajectories.
- Provides an O(T^3) implementation with Cholesky rank-1 updates/downdates and an O(M^2 T) sparse FIC variant to reduce computation.
- Predicts f via Monte Carlo mixtures of GP predictive distributions or by fitting a single sparse GP to posterior samples.
- Situates work relative to GPDM and EM/EP methods, differentiating by exact marginalization of f and use of PMCMC (PGAS) for non-Markov inference.
- Technical derivations for the collapsed prior and ancestor sampling weights are correct and well justified.
- CPF-AS/PGAS is appropriate for the induced non-Markov model; hyperparameter sampling approach is standard and reasonable.
- Implementation details are plausible but numerically delicate; more explicit pseudocode and stability discussion would help.
- Empirical results show strong multimodal smoothing in 1D and calibrated uncertainty in cart-pole, but lack quantitative head-to-head comparisons and runtime/ablation studies.
- Notation is heavy but mostly consistent; illustrative examples and clearer algorithmic schematics would improve clarity and reproducibility.
- The combination of collapsing f with PGAS in GP-SSMs appears novel and meaningful for Bayesian time-series learning.
- The approach mitigates bias from parametric approximations and reduces x–f posterior coupling, offering principled uncertainty quantification.
- Practical significance is tempered by computational cost and assumption of known observation model, though sparse variant and implementation guidance mitigate this.
- Strengths: fully Bayesian nonparametric treatment, elegant PGAS use, implementation insights, multimodal handling, and identifiability discussion.
- Weaknesses: limited experimental breadth (no GPDM/EM comparisons, missing RMSE/NLL, mixing diagnostics, runtimes), scalability concerns for large T, and fixed inducing input selection.
- Suggests demonstrating learning of unknown observation parameters, discussing inducing input selection strategies, and addressing numerical stability (downdates, jitter).
- Asks about sensitivity to mean-function misspecification, autocorrelation times and guidance on choosing N, performance when both f and g are GPs, and ablations on M/inducing placement.
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, novel, and significant despite needing broader experiments and more implementation detail.
