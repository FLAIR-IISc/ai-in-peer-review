- Presents a novel method combining Gaussian Processes with Particle MCMC that marginalizes the state transition function for fully nonparametric GP-SSMs.  
- Develops a tailored PMCMC algorithm to efficiently sample the smoothing distribution and perform Bayesian inference over latent states and hyperparameters.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a benchmark nonlinear system and a cart-pole control task.  
- Correctly situates the work relative to prior GP-SSM literature and differentiates from Deisenroth et al. and Turner et al.  
- Nonparametric Bayesian approach: marginalizing the transition function is a significant advance enabling greater model flexibility.  
- Efficient inference: algorithm leverages PMCMC advances and sparse GPs, showing good mixing and computational speedups.  
- Theoretical soundness: derivations for marginalization and prior formulation appear mathematically sound.  
- Empirical performance: learns complex dynamics, handles limited data and multimodality, and captures predictive uncertainty (cart-pole example).  
- Clear writing, good organization, and helpful illustrative figures.  
- Provides implementation details, complexity analysis, and optimizations that aid reproducibility.  
- Computational complexity is a concern for high-dimensional states or long time series; practical scalability needs more discussion.  
- GP performance may be sensitive to hyperparameters; deeper analysis and automatic tuning methods are needed.  
- Limited empirical comparisons (e.g., EKF, UKF); broader benchmarking would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Choice of FIC prior for sparsification is insufficiently justified compared to other sparse GP methods (e.g., variational approaches).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, nonparametric benefits, efficient PMCMC, promising results, and strong presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential computational limits, hyperparameter sensitivity, and limited comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with minor weaknesses; recommend acceptance with revisions addressing scaling, hyperparameter sensitivity, and comparisons.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
