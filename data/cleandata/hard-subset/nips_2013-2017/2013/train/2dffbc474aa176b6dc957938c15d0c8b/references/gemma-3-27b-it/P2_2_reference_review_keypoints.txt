- Paper proposes a fully Bayesian GP prior over SSM dynamics, marginalizing the GP and performing inference with tailored Particle MCMC.  
- Correctly positions work relative to prior GP-SSM literature, highlighting novelty in marginalizing f and using PMCMC.  
- Theoretical derivations for marginalizing the GP and prior over trajectories appear mathematically sound.  
- Fully Bayesian treatment with GP marginalization is a significant, novel contribution compared to parametric GP-SSMs.  
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly organized, and uses helpful figures (e.g., Figure 1b).  
- Experiments on a synthetic nonlinear system and cart-pole show the method can learn complex dynamics and quantify predictive uncertainty.  
- Authors consider practical scalability by discussing computational complexity and introducing a sparse GP (FIC) variant.  
- Computational cost remains a major concern, especially for high-dimensional states or long time series.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters is noted but not thoroughly analyzed; robustness to hyperparameter priors is unclear.  
- Experimental scope is limited to relatively simple systems; real-world, larger-scale evaluations are lacking.  
- Implementation details are insufficient for full reproducibility; code release or more specifics would help.  
- No comparison to variational inference methods, leaving trade-offs in scalability and accuracy unexamined.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel and sound Bayesian approach, and convincing empirical evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost, limited experiments, and insufficient hyperparameter analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested minor revisions: expand computational cost discussion, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, compare/discuss variational inference trade-offs, and improve reproducibility (e.g., release code).  
- Qualitative evaluation: high quality, good clarity, very good originality, and significant potential impact.
