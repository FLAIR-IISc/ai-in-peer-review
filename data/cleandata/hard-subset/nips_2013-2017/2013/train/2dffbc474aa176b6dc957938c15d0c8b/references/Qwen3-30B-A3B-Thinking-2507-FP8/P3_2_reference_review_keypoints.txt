- Proposes a fully Bayesian approach for GP-SSMs by marginalizing the transition function f and using PMCMC to sample p(x₀:T | y₀:T, θ).  
- Introduces a Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm for inference in the resulting non-Markovian latent model.  
- Presents a sparse GP-SSM using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computation.  
- Evaluated on two benchmarks: a nonlinear dynamical system and a cart-pole system.  

- Technically sound and detailed derivations of the marginal prior p(x₀:T | θ) and inference algorithms.  
- Marginalizing f is a clever solution preserving nonparametric expressivity.  
- Computational complexity analysis is thorough and the sparse GP implementation is well-motivated.  

- Lacks theoretical guarantees on PGAS convergence and particle approximation error bounds.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to two small benchmarks, lacking large-scale or real-world tests.  
- Comparisons to related recent work (e.g., Särkkä et al. 2013, Hensman et al. 2013) are superficial or missing.  
- Practical scaling behavior for larger T and M is not demonstrated despite complexity claims.  

- Notation is inconsistent for state trajectories and should be standardized.  
- The link between the GP prior and the state transition, and the role of the known observation model g, is ambiguously explained.  
- Sparse GP implementation details (Section 4.2) need more clarity to justify computational benefits.  

- Marginalizing the transition function for fully Bayesian GP-SSMs is novel and important.  
- Applying PGAS to this problem is a clever extension of PMCMC methods.  
- Sparse GP-SSM using FIC appropriately applies existing sparse GP techniques.  
- The paper does not clearly differentiate or compare sufficiently with Särkkä, Deisenroth, and Hensman’s related contributions.  

- Method is potentially significant for robotics, control, and time series due to principled uncertainty quantification.  
- High computational complexity (O(T³) vanilla, O(M²T) sparse) limits applicability to large-scale problems.  
- Lack of real-world large-scale demonstrations leaves practical significance unclear.  

- Overall: solid technical contribution with novel marginalization idea but several weaknesses to address before publication.  
- Main weaknesses: missing theoretical guarantees, limited experiments, superficial related-work comparison, and computational limits.  

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  

- Revise to include theoretical analysis of PGAS convergence and particle approximation quality.  
- Expand experiments to larger or more complex datasets and compare to recent methods (Särkkä et al., Hensman et al.).  
- Strengthen related-work discussion, particularly regarding Särkkä, Deisenroth, and Hensman.  
- Standardize notation for state trajectories across the paper.  
- Provide a more detailed analysis and practical examples of scaling with T and inducing points M.  

- Pros: novel fully Bayesian GP-SSM approach, detailed derivations, careful complexity analysis, and promising benchmark results.  
- Cons: missing theoretical guarantees, small-scale experiments, limited comparisons to recent GP-SSM work, high computational cost, and insufficient discussion of practical limitations.
