- Collapses the GP transition f to obtain an exact, non-Markovian prior over state trajectories and uses PGAS to sample exact smoothing p(x0:T | θ, y0:T).  
- Given samples of x0:T and θ, computes closed-form posterior predictive for f as a mixture of Gaussians.  
- Introduces a sparse FIC variant to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) and implements rank-1 Cholesky updates/downdates for efficiency.  
- Demonstrates method on a 1D multimodal smoothing benchmark and a 4D cart-pole, showing dynamics learning and uncertainty quantification.  
- Novelty: first combination of collapsing GP-SSM dynamics with PGAS for exact smoothing while retaining nonparametric expressivity.  
- Strength: elegant, principled Bayesian formulation that removes f–state posterior coupling and preserves nonparametric flexibility.  
- Strength: careful technical derivations of the collapsed prior, computable ancestor sampling weights, and efficient implementation details.  
- Strength: practical output — mixture-of-Gaussians predictive distributions capturing multimodality and partial-observability uncertainty.  
- Strength: empirical examples illustrate multimodal smoothing robustness and nontrivial 4D behavior.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparisons to strong GP-SSM learning baselines (EM/MAP, variational GP methods).  
- Weakness: insufficient empirical evidence on computational cost, memory, and scaling with T, N (particles), nx, and M (inducing points).  
- Weakness: inadequate details on hyperparameter sampling (slice sampling priors, mixing, diagnostics) and PGAS mixing diagnostics.  
- Weakness: FIC inducing input selection left to heuristics without evaluation of M or selection strategy effects.  
- Weakness: assumes observation model g known (up to low-dim params), limiting applicability and raising identifiability issues.  
- Quality: technically sound derivations and algorithms but empirical validation is too thin for a top-tier venue.  
- Clarity: overall clear and organized; derivations and implementation notes are informative.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: include an algorithm box for CPF-AS inside PGAS, memory-per-particle clarification, and FIC pseudo-code showing O(M^2 T) operations.  
- Significance: potentially important Bayesian nonparametric approach to SSMs with practical sparse extension, but impact depends on stronger empirical evidence.  
- Requested experiments/diagnostics: report ESS/autocorrelations for x and θ, wall-clock times and memory for varying N, T, M, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Requested comparisons: benchmark against EM/MAP GPDM/GPSSM and variational GPDS, and provide quantitative cart-pole RMSE and multi-step metrics.  
- Requested robustness checks: sensitivity to Q misspecification and priors on θx.  
- Requested FIC work: evaluate inducing input placement strategies, M selection, or adaptive/marginalized inducing schemes.  
- Extension discussion: address how to handle unknown g and identifiability considerations.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled Bayesian formulation, appropriate use of PGAS, scalable sparse alternative, and useful implementation guidance.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited experimental validation, under-specified practical choices (inducing inputs, θ sampling), and narrower applicability due to known observation model.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on stronger empirical comparisons and reporting of computational/mixing diagnostics.
