- Novel method: Uses a GP prior on transition dynamics and PMCMC inference to fully marginalize the transition function, enabling a truly nonparametric SSM.  
- Empirical demonstration: Validated on a synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole system, showing ability to learn complex dynamics and provide uncertainty estimates.  
- Relation to prior work: Clearly distinguishes from filtering/given-GP and EM/MAP GP-SSM methods by performing full Bayesian marginalization and linking to particle filtering/PMCMC literature.  
- Strength — nonparametric: Full marginalization of the transition function lets model complexity adapt to data without restrictive parametric assumptions.  
- Strength — principled Bayesian inference: PMCMC yields a rigorous posterior over all relevant variables.  
- Strength — theoretical soundness: Derivation of the marginal prior and PMCMC algorithm is clear and well-motivated.  
- Strength — empirical validation: Experiments and visualizations (smoothing distributions, uncertainties) provide convincing evidence.  
- Strength — practical considerations: Addresses computational complexity and proposes sparse GPs (FIC) and implementation details to improve scalability.  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and accessible.  
- Weakness — computational cost: Method remains computationally demanding and may not scale to high-dimensional or very long time series.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance likely depends on GP and PMCMC hyperparameters and lacks systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — limited comparisons: Lacks comparison to strong recent baselines, notably deep learning approaches (e.g., RNN-based SSMs).  
- Weakness — benchmark limitations: Synthetic benchmark is relatively simple; a more challenging real-world benchmark is needed for generalizability claims.  
- Weakness — FIC prior choice: Insufficient justification of why the FIC prior was chosen over alternatives and discussion of trade-offs.  
- Qualitative scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically sound, rigorous Bayesian framework, compelling results, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: High computational cost, limited baseline comparisons, and need for hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept, with minor revisions suggested to address hyperparameter analysis and baseline comparisons.
