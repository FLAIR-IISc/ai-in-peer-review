Summary
This paper tackles fully Bayesian learning and inference in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs). The key idea is to retain the nonparametric nature of the transition function f by marginalizing it out, which induces a non-Markovian prior over state trajectories. The authors then perform joint smoothing and hyperparameter learning using Particle MCMC, specifically particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS), which is particularly suitable for non-Markovian latent variable models. They derive the collapsed trajectory prior, show how to use SMC/PMCMC to sample x0:T and θ efficiently, and present computational strategies to achieve O(T^3) complexity for the dense GP and O(M^2 T) for a sparse variant using the FIC prior (inducing variables). After smoothing, posterior predictions for f are obtained in closed form conditioned on sampled trajectories, yielding a mixture-of-Gaussians predictive distribution. Experiments include a nonlinear benchmark with multimodal smoothing and a 4D cart-pole system, with comparisons against parametric baselines and a sparse GP-SSM.

Relation to prior work
- Compared to GPDS/GP-SSM lines (e.g., Deisenroth et al., Turner et al.), which typically either assume a learned model and focus on filtering/smoothing or employ parametric approximations/EM/MAP, this work explicitly marginalizes f and performs fully Bayesian joint inference over states and hyperparameters without reverting to a finite parametric representation.
- The use of PMCMC (PGAS) to address non-Markovian latent variables is well-motivated and builds on recent advances [3,4]. To my knowledge, combining PGAS with a collapsed GP transition to preserve nonparametric expressivity in state-space learning is novel and bold.
- Sparse GP-SSM via FIC to reduce complexity is a practical contribution, connecting to pseudo-input methods [16,17].

Strengths
- Novelty/significance: Collapsing the transition GP and using PGAS for state/hyperparameter inference in GP-SSMs is a compelling, principled advance. It preserves the full nonparametric character and avoids the common detour of parametric surrogates.
- Technical soundness: The collapsed prior over trajectories, the CPF-AS/ancestor-weight derivation, and the computational strategies (rank-one Cholesky updates; reuse of factors) are well explained and, to my reading, correct.
- Practicality: The O(T^3) dense and O(M^2 T) sparse variants, with detailed implementation guidance, make the approach more than a position paper. The mixture-of-Gaussians posterior for f enables uncertainty-aware predictions; a pragmatic single-GP approximation is suggested for control applications.
- Empirical behavior: The nonlinear benchmark illustrates the method’s ability to sample accurate multimodal smoothing trajectories even with a misspecified mean function, highlighting the benefit of marginalizing f. The cart-pole example shows calibrated uncertainty varying across the state-space.

Weaknesses / questions
- Empirical scope: Experiments are relatively small-scale (T=200; 4D states; N=20 particles; 50 PGAS iterations). There is no direct comparison against prior GP-SSM learning methods (e.g., EM/EP/variational approximations, MAP methods [2,6,8]) in terms of predictive accuracy, computational cost, and robustness. Given the novelty, I can tolerate limited scope, but a head-to-head with Turner et al. (EM) or GPDM would strengthen the case.
- Scalability and resources: While complexity is analyzed, wall-clock time, memory usage, and sensitivity to T, state dimension nx, and number of particles N are not reported. PGAS mixing vs N and T (and the sparse M) would be helpful. Non-Markovian CPF-AS can be memory-heavy; more guidance would help practitioners.
- Inducing inputs: The FIC prior is introduced, but inducing input selection is deferred (suggesting greedy methods). Given that the sparse path is central to scalability, an ablation on M and selection strategies (e.g., FITC/FIC variants, learned inducing locations) would be valuable.
- Hyperparameter sampling: Slice sampling for θx is plausible, but details on priors, tuning, acceptance/mixing diagnostics, and posterior sensitivity are sparse. Similarly, the observation model and noise assumptions are treated as generic; in practice, identifiability and confounding between Q and kernel amplitude deserve discussion.
- Clarity: The notation around K̃({xi0:t−1, x̃t:T−1}) and the practical recipe for ancestor weights could be spelled out a bit more concretely (e.g., brief pseudocode snippet or appendix). Minor nit: the linear mean m(xt)=xt in a vector-output GP needs a brief clarification.

Quality
Technically sound, with careful derivations and credible algorithmic choices. The only approximation is Monte Carlo. Some empirical evaluation aspects are light, but the core claims are supported.

Clarity
Generally clear and well organized, though dense. Implementation section is helpful; a small appendix with the CPF-AS steps and the exact reused factors would improve reproducibility.

Originality
High. Collapsing the GP transition within a PMCMC framework for GP-SSMs is a fresh and promising direction.

Significance
High potential impact. If adopted, this could reset the standard for fully Bayesian GP-SSM learning and inform downstream filtering/control methods by providing better-calibrated state and function posteriors.

Pros for acceptance
- Bold, principled method that preserves nonparametric expressivity.
- Strong conceptual contribution (collapsed GP transition + PGAS).
- Solid technical development with practical computational tricks.
- Empirically demonstrates advantages in multimodal smoothing and calibrated uncertainty.

Cons against acceptance
- Limited empirical comparisons and scalability study.
- Sparse GP inducing set selection and sensitivity not fully explored.
- Missing runtime/memory and mixing diagnostics.

Suggestions to authors
- Add a comparison to at least one prior GP-SSM learning method (EM/MAP/EP) on the nonlinear benchmark.
- Report runtime, memory, and mixing vs N, T, M; include autocorrelation plots.
- Provide a brief appendix with CPF-AS/ancestor-weight computation and the exact Cholesky updates you reuse.
- Discuss identifiability between Q and kernel amplitude; include prior choices and sensitivity.
- Consider releasing code.

Recommendation
Accept (novelty-focused). The paper proposes an original and well-founded approach that opens a significant new direction for Bayesian learning in GP-SSMs. Despite some empirical limitations, the conceptual advance and technical execution warrant acceptance. Confidence: high.
