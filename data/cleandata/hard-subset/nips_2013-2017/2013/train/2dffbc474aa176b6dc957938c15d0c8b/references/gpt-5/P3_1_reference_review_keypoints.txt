- Summary: Collapses GP transition f under a GP prior to obtain a non-Markovian p(x0:T|θ) and uses PGAS to sample states and hyperparameters, with an FIC sparse prior to reduce cost and Cholesky reuse for efficiency.  
- Relation to prior work: Novel in collapsing f and using PGAS for nonparametric GP-SSMs, differing from EM/MAP and parametric sparse approximations.  
- Technical soundness: Marginalization and predictive expressions are correct; ancestor sampling weights and PGAS kernel invariance are appropriate.  
- Algorithmic detail: Good implementation specifics (Cholesky reuse, rank-1 updates) and FIC construction; complexity discussion omits dependence on N, nx, and memory.  
- Empirical support: Scalar benchmark convincingly shows multimodality; cart-pole demonstrates 4D scalability, but lacks quantitative baselines, mixing diagnostics, and runtime scaling.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and well organized, but some notational density and missing explicit complexity terms (N, nx, memory); θx sampling details are brief.  
- Originality: The combination of collapsing the GP transition with PGAS for joint smoothing and hyperparameter learning is novel and nontrivial.  
- Significance: Provides a principled Bayesian solution for learning nonlinear dynamics with uncertainty; potentially impactful if sparse scaling holds in practice.  
- Strength: Elegant collapsing approach preserving nonparametric expressivity and exact MCMC kernels for smoothing.  
- Strength: Use of PGAS addresses path degeneracy and enables sampling of whole trajectories.  
- Strength: Clear route to hyperparameter inference and sensible θx/θy separation.  
- Strength: Thoughtful implementation and sparse FIC construction to reduce computational cost.  
- Strength: Experiments demonstrate multimodal smoothing and uncertainty-aware prediction.  
- Weakness: Complexity and memory claims lack inclusion of N and nx; actual scaling and practicable limits (e.g., T=1000) unclear.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sampling (slice sampling over θx and Q) may be challenging; no diagnostics or details on parameterization/priors.  
- Weakness: Inducing inputs for FIC are fixed heuristically (not inferred), risking bias; sensitivity to M and placement not explored.  
- Weakness: Lack of quantitative baselines and runtime metrics for cart-pole; limited reporting on RMSE/NLL and confidence intervals for scalar case.  
- Weakness: Assumes known observation model g; limited discussion on joint learning of g and f and identifiability.  
- Weakness: No theoretical or empirical discussion of mixing rates as a function of N, T, or model mismatch, nor bias from FIC.  
- Questions for authors: Request precise computational/memory complexity including N and nx, limits on T and nx, sensitivity to GP mean, parameterization/prior details for Q and θx, inducing input selection and M sensitivity, and mixing/runtime diagnostics.  
- Suggestions: Add quantitative baselines and runtimes for cart-pole, explicit complexity tables including N and nx, mixing diagnostics and sensitivity analyses for θ priors and M, and diagnostics for mean-function impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: Conceptually clean, technically sound collapsed Bayesian GP-SSM approach leveraging PGAS with practical implementation and sparse construction.  
- Cons for acceptance: Incomplete complexity/memory accounting, limited empirical comparisons and diagnostics, and heuristic treatment of inducing-point selection.
