- Presents a fully Bayesian approach for GP-SSMs by marginalizing the transition function and using Particle MCMC (PGAS) to sample p(x0:T | y0:T).  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM using the FIC prior, reducing complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2 T) with M ≪ T.  
- Method maintains the nonparametric nature of the dynamics and jointly learns dynamics and state trajectories.  
- Theoretical derivations are sound and clearly show how to compute the resulting non-Gaussian state priors.  
- PGAS implementation is well-motivated and properly handles the non-Markovian marginalized state process.  
- Sparse FIC implementation is carefully derived and justified.  
- Experiments (synthetic and cart-pole) demonstrate capturing complex dynamics, multimodal smoothing, and meaningful uncertainty estimates.  
- Novel contribution: marginalization strategy combined with PGAS enables fully Bayesian learning in GP-SSMs where prior work relied on parametric approximations.  
- Significant impact for robotics, control, and time series due to Bayesian uncertainty quantification and superior performance vs. parametric models.  
- Limited experimental comparison with other nonparametric or state-of-the-art SSM learning methods (e.g., neural-network-based approaches).  
- Lacks practical details on selection of inducing inputs for the sparse GP implementation.  
- Needs concrete runtime comparisons with alternative methods to substantiate computational advantages.  
- No sensitivity analysis for hyperparameter priors or number of particles in PGAS.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and well-executed paper meeting review criteria, with minor addressable weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: accept.
