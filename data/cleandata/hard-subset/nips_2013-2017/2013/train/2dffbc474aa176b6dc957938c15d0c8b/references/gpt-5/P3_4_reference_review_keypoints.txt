- Main idea: Marginalize the GP transition f to obtain a non-Markovian prior p(x0:T|θ) and perform joint state/hyperparameter inference with Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS).
- Implementation: Derives product of GP predictive conditionals for p(x1:T|θ) and recovers posterior over f as a mixture of GPs at test inputs.
- Complexity claims: Exact algorithm with O(T^3) via Cholesky rank-1 updates, and O(M^2 T) using a sparse FIC GP.
- Empirical results: Demonstrates multimodal smoothing and calibrated predictive uncertainty on a 1D nonlinear benchmark and a 4D cart-pole system.
- Relation to EM/MAP methods: Differs by fully marginalizing f and avoiding parametric approximations used in EM/MAP or GPDM approaches.
- Complementarity: Provides the learning component missing from model-based GP filtering/smoothing approaches (Deisenroth et al.).
- Foundations: Builds on PMCMC and PGAS to handle the non-Markovian latent structure induced by marginalizing f.
- Sparse GP use: Adopts FIC sparse GPs for scalability and practical reuse of factorization.
- Strength — conceptual: Elegant exact marginalization of transition dynamics addressing strong state–function coupling.
- Strength — methodological: Sound combination of GP regression and PGAS; CPF-AS is appropriate for non-Markovian smoothing.
- Strength — computational: Careful engineering (rank-one updates, concatenated covariance for ancestor weights) and a sparse option for scalability.
- Strength — practical utility: Produces state smoothing without fixed transition models and yields multimodal, uncertainty-aware predictions.
- Weakness — proposal ambiguity: The SMC proposal (Eq.5) is unclear; need to state whether prior proposal or auxiliary/lookahead proposals are used.
- Weakness — complexity w.r.t. N: Dependence on number of particles N is not explicit; per-iteration complexity including N, nx, and output dim is missing.
- Weakness — inducing inputs: Inducing locations are fixed and not integrated into inference; selection/optimization strategies and their costs are not discussed.
- Weakness — limited experiments: Narrow benchmarks, no quantitative baselines (EM/GPDM, variational GP-SSMs, alternative PMCMC), and missing runtime/ESS/mixing diagnostics.
- Weakness — high-dimensionality: Curse of dimensionality in GP inputs not addressed; kernel choices and structured kernels for nx > 10 are not discussed.
- Weakness — identifiability: Interactions among f, Q, and x can cause multimodality/weak identifiability; priors and robustness need discussion.
- Weakness — theoretical guidance: No analysis of PGAS mixing in the induced non-Markovian model or concrete guidance on required particle numbers.
- Quality: Technically sound but missing key implementation details and broader empirical validation.
- Clarity: Generally clear but notational overload and the proposal ambiguity hinder reproducibility; pseudocode for CPF-AS with explicit proposals would help.
- Originality: Novel combination of exact GP marginalization with PGAS and practical FIC integration.
- Significance: Addresses an important problem in Bayesian nonparametric dynamics learning and is likely influential if scalability/empirical claims are substantiated.
- Suggested improvements: Clarify proposal choice, provide explicit complexity formulas (T, N, M, nx, output dim), broaden experiments and baselines, report ESS/runtimes, and include inducing-input strategies and sensitivity analyses.
- Acceptance pros: Principled Bayesian treatment preserving nonparametric flexibility, sound use of PGAS, and useful computational techniques.
- Acceptance cons: Ambiguous algorithmic details, incomplete complexity accounting, and limited empirical/robustness evidence.
