- Proposes fully Bayesian GP-SSM learning by marginalizing the transition function instead of using parametric approximations.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample the smoothing distribution p(x₀:T | y₀:T, θ) under the marginalized GP.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computational cost.  
- Technical derivations are rigorous, with clear justification for marginalization and PGAS application.  
- Computational complexity: O(T³) for full GP and O(M²T) for the sparse GP variant.  
- Experiments are thorough: synthetic benchmark shows handling of multimodal smoothing; cart-and-pole demonstrates real-world applicability and uncertainty visualization.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge limitations, including computational cost and inducing input selection for sparse GP.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with consistent notation, clear algorithms, and effective visualizations.  
- Minor clarity gap: limited practical guidance on choosing number of particles N.  
- Original contribution: novel marginalization of the transition function preserving nonparametric GP expressivity.  
- Novel adaptation of PGAS to the non-Markovian structure induced by marginalizing the GP.  
- Sparse GP extension using FIC is useful but less novel given prior use of FIC in other GP contexts.  
- High significance due to improved flexibility and uncertainty modeling for nonlinear SSMs, with applications in control and RL.  
- Weaknesses: scalability concerns for very long time series (T > 1000) and lack of comparison with other recent Bayesian SSM methods (e.g., variational inference).  
- Recommendation: Strong accept (NIPS); substantial, well-executed contribution.  
- Final scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
