- Problem & contribution: Proposes a fully Bayesian approach for GP-SSMs by marginalizing the transition function f and using PGAS for inference, with a sparse FIC GP to reduce complexity.

- Novelty: Applies PMCMC and sparse GP techniques to enable exact Bayesian learning of the GP dynamics, filling a gap in GP-SSM literature.

- Theoretical soundness: Marginalization derivation and marginal prior (Eq. 4) are mathematically rigorous and well-justified.

- Computational efficiency: Sparse GP-FIC construction reduces complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) with detailed implementation and complexity analysis.

- Practical demonstration: Experiments show ability to handle multimodal smoothing and capture dynamics where parametric baselines are misspecified; cart-pole results show realistic uncertainty estimates.

- Clarity and reproducibility: Paper is well-organized with clear notation, figures, and algorithms enabling reproducibility (Algorithm 1).

- Lack of PGAS convergence analysis: No theoretical or empirical analysis of PGAS mixing/convergence for the non-Markovian state process despite claiming good mixing.

- Scalability concerns: Experiments are small-scale (T≈200, 100 obs); unclear performance for T>1000 or higher-dimensional states and how inducing points M should scale with T.

- Missing comparisons: No comparison to other Bayesian SSM learners (e.g., Bayesian neural networks or Bayesian RNNs) to contextualize advantages.

- Hyperparameter choices unclear: Use of log-normal priors for hyperparameters is unjustified and no sensitivity analysis is provided.

- Inducing point selection unclear: Paper does not sufficiently explain practical selection of inducing points beyond citing greedy methods.

- Approximation caveat: Claim of "exact marginalization" is misleading since marginalization is exact only within the SMC approximation and not the true posterior.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated technical contribution enabling fully Bayesian GP-SSMs with practical sparse extension, but limited by theoretical guarantees, scalability analysis, and comparative evaluation.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing PGAS convergence discussion, larger-scale experiments (e.g., T=1000), at least one comparison to alternative Bayesian methods, and more details on hyperparameter and inducing-point selection.

- Final judgment: Important and novel contribution to Bayesian dynamical systems; marginalization approach is valuable and the paper is a strong NIPS candidate, pending the suggested clarifications and additional experiments.
