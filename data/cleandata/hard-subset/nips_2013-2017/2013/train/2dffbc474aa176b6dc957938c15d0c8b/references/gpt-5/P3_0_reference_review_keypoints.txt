- Paper marginalizes the GP transition function f to obtain a non-Markovian prior over states and performs posterior inference with Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS).
- Uses GP one-step conditionals to construct p(x1:T | θ, x0), reducing posterior coupling between f and x0:T while preserving nonparametric expressivity.
- Proposes a sparse FIC variant to reduce cost from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) and gives practical linear-algebra strategies (rank-1 Cholesky updates, reuse).
- Experiments on a scalar multimodal benchmark and a 4D cart-pole show qualitative smoothing and one-step predictive performance; predictive posterior over f is a mixture-of-Gaussians across samples.
- Relation to prior work is well addressed, distinguishing from EM/MAP GP-SSMs and filtering approaches that do not fully integrate over f.
- Conceptual contribution: collapsing f is principled and reduces posterior correlation while keeping nonparametric flexibility.
- Methodological soundness: construction of the non-Markovian prior and use of PGAS/ancestor sampling are appropriate and correct.
- Implementation detail: careful attention to computational pitfalls and practical methods for scalability are strengths.
- Empirical value: scalar example highlights multimodality benefits; cart-pole example illustrates state-dependent uncertainty quantification.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: small-scale experiments, few iterations, and no diagnostics (autocorrelation, ESS, acceptance rates) to support mixing claims.
- Missing quantitative metrics for cart-pole (NLL/RMSE), no multi-step rollouts, and no comparisons to competitive baselines.
- Baseline comparisons are insufficient: should include methods that learn GP dynamics without collapsing f, variational GP-SSMs, and standard SSM baselines (EKF/UKF, GPDM, EM-based GP-SSMs).
- Scalability and robustness are underexplored: no empirical scaling plots (runtime vs T, M) or sensitivity analyses to hyperpriors, Q priors, number of particles N, or higher state dimensions.
- SMC proposal ambiguity: paper alternates between optimal and prior proposals but does not clearly state the practical choice or handling for non-Gaussian likelihoods and potential MH-within-SMC.
- Notational clarity issues: K̃ depending on x1:t in multiple roles is confusing and separation between Gaussian-looking densities and actual Gaussians is needed.
- CPF-AS (conditional PF with ancestor sampling) is only sketched; a concise algorithmic box tailored to the non-Markovian prior would improve clarity.
- Minor inconsistencies in indexing (y0:T vs y1:T) and unclear treatment/sampling of x0 within the Gibbs state.
- Originality: the combination of collapsing GP dynamics with PGAS is novel and nontrivial; incorporating FIC in this collapsed PMCMC framework is useful.
- Significance: if supported by broader experiments and comparisons, the approach could become a reference method for fully Bayesian GP-SSMs and uncertainty-aware dynamics modeling.
- Suggestion: add quantitative comparisons against GPDM/EM, variational GP-SSMs, and parametric SSMs, reporting RMSE and predictive log-likelihood including multi-step forecasting.
- Suggestion: provide mixing diagnostics and sensitivity studies for N, T, M, mean function choice, and include runtime scaling plots.
- Suggestion: explicitly state the SMC proposal used in practice for non-Gaussian likelihoods and discuss its impact on variance and degeneracy.
- Suggestion: include an appendix with explicit formulas for computing p(x̃t:T | xi0:t−1) efficiently and provide pseudo-code for CPF-AS in the non-Markovian setting.
- Suggestion: discuss identifiability concerns when both f and g are flexible, since the paper assumes g known.
- Minor issues: correct slice sampling citation (Neal 2003), unify time-index notation, ensure Table 1 and figures are present and define nx when first used.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — strong core idea and technical soundness but needs stronger experiments, diagnostics, and clarity to be a substantial NeurIPS contribution.
