- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the GP transition function, retaining nonparametric expressivity while enabling inference with PMCMC (PGAS).  
- Shows the marginalized state prior is non-Gaussian but representable as a product of Gaussians, and develops a PGAS-based inference algorithm for the resulting non-Markovian model.  
- Introduces a sparse GP implementation using FIC to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T), improving scalability.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic nonlinear systems and a cart-pole task, demonstrating handling of multimodal smoothing distributions and meaningful uncertainty capture.  
- Technically sound: marginalization and theoretical development are well-justified and correct, and PGAS is an appropriate choice for inference.  
- Novel contribution: maintains full nonparametric GP expressivity in GP-SSMs, differing from prior parametric approximations.  
- Strong experimental validation: experiments clearly show advantages over parametric approaches and capture multimodal posteriors.  
- Computational efficiency: FIC sparse GP is a practical improvement and implementation strategies are well discussed.  
- Clear presentation: well-organized writing, consistent notation, and effective figures that clarify key ideas.  
- Limited comparison to recent work, especially variational GP-SSM methods (e.g., Deisenroth et al. 2015), weakening contextual positioning.  
- Complexity claim (O(M^2 T)) needs clearer explanation and accounting of constant factors and matrix reuse.  
- Scalability limits unclear: lacks analysis or experiments for very long time series (e.g., T ≫ 200).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity not explored; unclear how results depend on chosen hyperpriors.  
- Sparse GP implementation details insufficient: selection and integration of inducing points with PGAS is not fully explained.  
- Overall quality: rigorous theoretical development and well-supported experimental claims; complete and substantial work.  
- Clarity: generally clear, but Section 4.2 and sparse GP details need more elaboration for reproducibility.  
- Originality: significant advance over prior GP-SSM work by preserving nonparametric dynamics through marginalization.  
- Significance: provides a valuable Bayesian tool for nonlinear SSMs with practical applications in systems identification and robotics.  
- Recommendation: accept — novel, technically solid, and well-evaluated contribution.  
- Pro acceptance: addresses fundamental limitation, technically justified, validated experimentally, offers computational improvements, broadly applicable.  
- Con acceptance: needs better positioning against recent methods, more sparse-GP detail, scalability discussion, and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify matrix-factorization reuse in Section 4.2, compare to variational GP-SSM methods, add experiments/discussion for longer T, compare methods using sparse GP during inference, and detail how inducing points are chosen and used with PGAS.
