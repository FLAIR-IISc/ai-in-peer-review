- Presents a novel PMCMC approach that marginalizes the GP function for full Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs, enabling simultaneous learning of dynamics and state smoothing.  
- Bridges nonparametric GP modeling and practical state estimation, providing a component useful for methods requiring learned GP dynamics.  
- Correctly situates the work relative to prior GP-SSM methods, distinguishing from MAP/EM approaches and linking to PMCMC and sparse GP literature.  
- Theoretical derivation of the marginal prior over state trajectories is clear and mathematically well-motivated.  
- Marginalizing out the GP yields true nonparametric flexibility without imposing restrictive parametric dynamics.  
- Introduces an algorithmic innovation (CPF-AS) built on PMCMC advances, with attention to computational complexity and an effective FIC prior for efficiency.  
- Empirical validation on a nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole system shows the method can learn complex dynamics and quantify uncertainty; comparisons (Table 1) are helpful.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized with clear notation and sufficient technical detail.  
- The PMCMC method is computationally demanding; scalability limits for high-dimensional states and long time series are not fully addressed.  
- Performance likely sensitive to hyperparameters (GP kernels, PMCMC tuning), but no thorough sensitivity analysis is provided.  
- Experiments are small-scale; larger or more realistic datasets and comparisons to state-of-the-art are lacking.  
- Potential for PMCMC to get stuck in local optima; convergence diagnostics (trace plots, Gelman-Rubin) are not provided.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound contribution addressing a significant Bayesian inference challenge with promising empirical results and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost, limited experimental scope, insufficient sensitivity and convergence analyses.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested minor revisions: add computational budget/wall-clock times, include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, provide convergence diagnostics, and expand discussion on scalability.
