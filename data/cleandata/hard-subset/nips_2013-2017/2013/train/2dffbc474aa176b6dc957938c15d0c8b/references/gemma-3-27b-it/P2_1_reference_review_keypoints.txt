- Proposes marginalizing out the GP state transition function in GP-SSMs and using Particle MCMC for fully Bayesian inference.  
- Leverages sparse GPs and optimized Cholesky updates to address computational bottlenecks.  
- Technical approach is rigorous and builds on established methods (GPs, PMCMC, SMC).  
- Marginalizing f is an original contribution enabling truly non-parametric dynamics learning.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with helpful figures and notation.  
- Method can learn complex dynamics and provide uncertainty estimates useful for control and prediction.  
- Empirical results on a nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole task convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Computational complexity (O(M^2 T)) remains a concern for long time series or high-dimensional states.  
- Results may be sensitive to GP hyperparameters; more discussion of priors and tuning is needed.  
- Comparisons are limited; lacks evaluation against other state-of-the-art non-parametric models (e.g., RNNs).  
- Implementation details are sparse and a public code release would aid reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical validation, clear presentation, advances the field.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, hyperparameter sensitivity, limited comparisons, no public code.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
