- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP prior over the state transition in nonlinear SSMs, marginalizing the GP function.  
- Uses Particle MCMC (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling) to sample the smoothing distribution and learn GP dynamics.  
- Provides implementation details and optimizations (O(T^3) or O(M^2T)) and validates on synthetic and cart-pole examples.  
- Positions the work relative to prior GP-SSM literature and PMCMC methods, addressing limitations of parametric approximations.  
- Builds on PMCMC (Andrieu et al., Lindsten et al.) and connects to GP filtering/smoothing work (Deisenroth et al.).  
- Principled Bayesian treatment enables uncertainty quantification and avoids point-estimate overfitting.  
- Nonparametric GP prior gives flexible modeling of complex dynamics without restrictive parametric forms.  
- Tailored PMCMC (PGAS) enables efficient smoothing inference and is a core methodological contribution.  
- Clear writing and reasonably presented mathematical derivations.  
- Empirical results show capability to capture multimodal smoothing distributions and perform on control tasks.  
- Practical focus: discusses computational complexity and implementation considerations.  
- Implementation and PGAS details are complex and may be hard to reproduce for newcomers to particle methods.  
- Scalability remains a concern for very long time series or high-dimensional states despite optimizations.  
- No detailed analysis of sensitivity to GP hyperparameters or systematic tuning strategy.  
- Comparisons to existing GP-SSM methods are limited; more direct baselines (e.g., [5,6]) are needed.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with significant PMCMC contribution and strong empirical validation.  
- Against acceptance: implementation complexity, computational cost, hyperparameter sensitivity, and limited empirical comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; benefits of fully Bayesian, nonparametric framework outweigh drawbacks.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add implementation/tuning guidance for PMCMC, expand comparisons to alternatives, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning strategies, and discuss scalability and future work.
