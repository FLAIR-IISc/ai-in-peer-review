- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function f, enabling nonparametric inference without parametric dynamics approximations.
- Proposes a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm to sample smoothing distributions in the non-Markovian marginalized GP-SSM.
- Contrasts with prior GP-SSM work that relies on fixed parametric dynamics or EM-based ML estimation.
- Theoretical derivations are rigorous and sound, with clear derivation of the marginal prior and treatment of non-Markovian structure.
- Inference algorithms are well-motivated and theoretically justified.
- Experiments on a multimodal nonlinear system and cart-pole show the GP-SSM outperforms parametric alternatives; sparse GP achieves similar performance with lower cost.
- Paper acknowledges computational limitations (naive O(T^4)) and proposes sparse approximations (O(M^2T)) to mitigate cost.
- Exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear notation, derivations, and effective figures (e.g., multimodal smoothing in Fig. 2).
- Provides sufficient implementation details for reproducibility, including sparse GP construction and complexity analysis.
- Novel contribution: marginalization of dynamics combined with PGAS for GP-SSMs, preserving nonparametric learning in a Bayesian framework.
- Sparse FIC GP extension is a valuable, if not entirely novel, practical adaptation to the GP-SSM + PGAS setting.
- Significant impact: advances Bayesian learning of nonlinear state-space models with broad applicability in robotics, control, and time series.
- Strength: maintains nonparametric GP-SSM nature, well-executed PGAS application, comprehensive theory, strong experiments, and practical sparse approximation.
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other Bayesian GP-SSM methods (e.g., variational inference), experimental comparison with parametric models could be broader, particle count vs. cost trade-offs not fully explored, and sparse GP implementation guidance could be more practical.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong technical quality, novelty, significance, and suitability for publication.
