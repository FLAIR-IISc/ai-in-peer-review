- Key idea: Marginalize the GP transition f exactly, inducing a non-Markovian prior over states and perform inference with PGAS, yielding closed-form posterior over f as a mixture of GP posteriors.  
- Complexity reduction: Introduce a sparse FIC GP prior to reduce cost from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) and use incremental Cholesky rank-1 updates for efficiency.  
- Relation to prior work: Fills gap versus parametric or MAP GP-SSM methods by retaining full nonparametric Bayesian treatment and leveraging recent PMCMC for non-Markovian models.  
- Technical soundness: Collapsing f is correct (product-of-Gaussians form) and PGAS is appropriate for the induced non-Markovian smoothing target.  
- Implementation: Ancestor-sampling weights and factorization/caching of K̃ with rank-1 updates are sensible and detailed.  
- Sparse GP: FIC prior derivations and complexity claims are consistent with sparse GP regression theory.  
- Hyperparameter sampling: Sampling θx and θy given x0:T via slice sampling is standard and reasonable.  
- Empirics: 1D nonlinear benchmark shows multimodal smoothing; cart-pole demonstrates uncertainty-aware predictions but lacks quantitative evaluation.  
- Experimental shortcomings: Limited baselines, no quantitative cart-pole results, and missing runtime/ESS/mixing diagnostics or ablation studies.  
- Complexity ambiguity: Total time/memory complexity w.r.t. number of particles N, trajectory length T, state dim nx, and inducing M is under-specified and likely depends on N.  
- Numerical stability: Repeated rank-1 downdates may be delicate; conditioning and jitter/regulation discussion is missing.  
- Ancestor-sampling cost: Computing p(x̃t:T | xi0:t−1) is expensive in non-Markovian models; need discussion of truncated lookahead or approximations for large T.  
- Inducing inputs: Treating inducing locations as fixed changes the prior and performance may be sensitive to their placement; no sensitivity analysis provided.  
- Reproducibility: Request explicit pseudo-code for CPF-AS specialized to the marginalized GP prior and matrix-update details.  
- Computational clarity: Ask for explicit dimensionalities and scaling, including dependence on N, nx, and cost of p(yt | xt) evaluations.  
- Citation/formatting: Some references are incomplete and need standardization.  
- Originality: Novel and nontrivial combination of exact GP marginalization with PGAS and tailored ancestor sampling for non-Markovian priors.  
- Practical value: Fully Bayesian nonparametric GP-SSM is useful for time series/control/RL and yields mixture-of-GP predictive posteriors for downstream use.  
- Limitations: Empirical validation and scalability need strengthening to establish state-of-the-art status.  
- Pros: Clean, technically sound marginalization; retains nonparametric expressivity; practical sparse variant; handles multimodal smoothing.  
- Cons: Thin empirical evaluation; unclear complexity/memory scaling w.r.t. N and nx; sensitivity to inducing locations unexplored; numerical downdate concerns.  
- Suggested improvements: Add quantitative metrics and baselines (GPDM/EM-MAP, PMMH), runtime and ESS diagnostics, ablations over N/M/T, complexity validation, numerical-stability discussion, and inducing-input sensitivity study.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept — solid methodology and likely influential, but empirical strengthening is needed.
