- Core contribution: a PMCMC method that marginalizes the GP transition function f for fully Bayesian learning and inference in GP-SSMs.  
- Main technical innovation: tailored PMCMC with ancestor sampling (CPF-AS) to handle the non-Markovian marginal model.  
- Theoretical soundness: clear derivation of the marginal prior (eq. 3–4) providing a rigorous basis for inference.  
- Computational strategy: uses sparse GP (FIC) and implementation optimizations (reusing Cholesky factorizations) to reduce complexity.  
- Empirical validation: strong results on a nonlinear benchmark and a cart-pole system, showing accurate dynamics learning with limited data.  
- Relation to prior work: fills the gap of learning the GP dynamics (vs. prior works that assumed learned GPs or used parametric approximations).  
- Strength — principled Bayesian approach: marginalizing f avoids biases of point estimates and better quantifies uncertainty.  
- Strength — novel PMCMC formulation: CPF-AS and ancestor sampling are well-motivated for the collapsed model.  
- Strength — computational efficiency: sparse GP techniques make the method practical for moderate problems.  
- Strength — clear presentation: paper is well-written with informative figures.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: insufficient analysis of robustness to prior/hyperparameter choices.  
- Weakness — scalability: method may be computationally demanding for high-dimensional states or very long time series.  
- Weakness — FIC choices: selection of FIC prior and M=40 inducing points lacks justification and sensitivity study.  
- Weakness — missing error bars: RMSE results lack confidence intervals, hindering statistical assessment.  
- Weakness — limited baselines: comparison omits recent deep learning time-series models (e.g., LSTMs/Transformers).  
- Pro acceptance points: novel, theoretically sound, addresses key challenge in GP-SSMs, and shows promising empirical results.  
- Con acceptance points: concerns about scalability, hyperparameter/FIC choices, and incomplete empirical comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity, error bars, scalability discussion, and comparisons to deep-learning approaches.
