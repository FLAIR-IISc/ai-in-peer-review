- Presents a fully Bayesian approach to GP-SSMs that marginalizes the transition function using PMCMC.
- Leverages Particle MCMC (PGAS) to sample smoothing distributions and enable posterior inference over GP functions and latent states.
- Allows flexible, non-parametric modeling of nonlinear dynamics with principled uncertainty quantification.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a synthetic nonlinear system and a cart-pole control problem.
- Situates work appropriately within GP-SSM literature and PMCMC advances, citing key prior papers.
- Introduces FIC sparse GPs as a computational shortcut to reduce complexity.
- Principled Bayesian treatment and marginalization are major strengths enabling true non-parametric modeling.
- Derivation of the marginal prior and clear exposition of SMC/PMCMC shows theoretical soundness.
- Tailored PGAS implementation and practical optimizations (Cholesky reuse, sparse GP) improve efficiency and mixing.
- Experimental results show the method learns complex dynamics and visualize multimodal smoothing well.
- Paper is well-written with helpful figures, equations, and practical complexity analysis.
- Computational cost remains high, especially for high-dimensional states or long time series.
- Lacks comprehensive discussion of scalability limitations and possible improvements.
- Performance likely sensitive to hyperparameters; hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analysis are missing.
- Limited empirical comparison against other RL or system identification methods weakens claims.
- Rationale for choosing the FIC prior over other sparse GP methods is not fully justified.
- Implementation details are sparse and code is not released, hindering reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically sound contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions.
- Revision recommendations: expand scalability discussion, add broader comparisons, include hyperparameter analysis, justify FIC choice, and release code.
