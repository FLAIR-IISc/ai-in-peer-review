- Proposes a fully Bayesian, nonparametric GP state-space model by marginalizing the transition function and using a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) PMCMC algorithm.  
- Leverages sparse GPs and computational tricks (e.g., reuse of Cholesky factorizations) to make inference tractable.  
- Provides clear mathematical derivation of the marginalization and shows how to exploit structure for efficient inference.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole task, capturing multimodal smoothing distributions and providing uncertainty estimates.  
- Positions work relative to prior GP-SSM literature, emphasizing novelty over methods that use MAP/EM or learned parametric GP dynamics.  
- Builds on foundational particle methods and PMCMC, adapting them specifically for nonparametric GP-SSMs.  
- Strength: fully nonparametric Bayesian treatment increases flexibility and avoids parametric limitations.  
- Strength: tailored PMCMC inference and computational optimizations are significant methodological contributions.  
- Strength: clear writing, organization, and helpful equations/figures.  
- Weakness: computational complexity remains a concern for high-dimensional states and long time series despite sparse approximations.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning strategies.  
- Weakness: comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., variational or other approximate Bayesian techniques) are limited.  
- Weakness: practical implementation details (particle degeneracy, PMCMC tuning) are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Weakness: lacks larger-scale real-world applications beyond the cart-pole example.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking authors to address scalability, hyperparameter sensitivity, broader comparisons, and practical implementation guidance.
