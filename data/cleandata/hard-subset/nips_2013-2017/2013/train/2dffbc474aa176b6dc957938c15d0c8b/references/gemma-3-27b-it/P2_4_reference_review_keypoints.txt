- Presents a novel PMCMC-based method that marginalizes out the GP transition function for fully nonparametric Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs.  
- Tailors a Particle MCMC algorithm and uses sparse GP approximations for practical implementation.  
- Clearly positions the work against prior GP-SSM methods that use parametric/finite approximations and EM-based approaches.  
- Provides a rigorous derivation of the marginalization, prior, and SMC/PMCMC algorithm.  
- Leverages recent PMCMC advances and discusses efficient implementation details.  
- Empirically validated on a synthetic nonlinear system and a cart-pole task, showing multimodal smoothing and good RMSE.  
- Demonstrates the ability to learn useful dynamics from limited data and capture uncertainty relevant to control/RL.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides enough detail for reproduction.  
- Significant potential impact by enabling genuinely nonparametric modeling of complex dynamical systems.  
- Computational cost of PMCMC remains a concern; scalability to high-dimensional states and long sequences is unclear.  
- Experiments are limited in scale and lack reported computational requirements (e.g., wall-clock times).  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters is not thoroughly investigated beyond basic priors.  
- Comparisons to other strong filtering/smoothing baselines (e.g., unscented Kalman variants) are limited.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution with compelling results; minor weaknesses do not outweigh benefits.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept, with suggested revisions to discuss computation, hyperparameter tuning, and additional baseline comparisons.
