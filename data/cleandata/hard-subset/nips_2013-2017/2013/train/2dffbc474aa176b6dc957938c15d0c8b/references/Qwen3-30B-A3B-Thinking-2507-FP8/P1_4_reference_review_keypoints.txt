- Problem: Bayesian learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models by marginalizing the transition function f to maintain full nonparametric dynamics.  
- Method: Tailored Particle MCMC—Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS)—to sample p(x0:T | y0:T) under the resulting non‑Markovian prior.  
- Computational improvement: Use of sparse GP (FIC) reduces complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2 T) with practical implementation details provided.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends GP-SSM literature by avoiding parametric approximations and adapts PMCMC to the non‑Markovian setting.  

Strengths:
- Technical soundness: Rigorous derivation of the non‑Gaussian marginal prior p(x0:T | θ) and well‑motivated, correct PGAS algorithm.  
- Computational practicality: FIC sparse GP and implementation discussion (Section 4.2) significantly improve efficiency and reproducibility.  
- Clear presentation: Well‑organized paper with illustrative figures (e.g., multimodal smoothing in Fig. 2).  
- Practical significance: Fills a gap by enabling fully Bayesian, nonparametric learning for dynamical systems.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited experiments: Only two benchmark problems with relatively superficial comparisons to alternatives.  
- Sparse GP trade-offs underexplored: No systematic analysis of RMSE vs. number of inducing points M, especially for larger T.  
- Scalability concerns: No discussion or evaluation for higher‑dimensional state spaces (e.g., >5 dimensions).  
- Inducing point selection: Sensitivity to selection and practical guidance are not addressed.  

Overall assessment and recommendation:
- Overall: Strong, novel, technically sound contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: broaden experimental comparisons, analyze sparse GP performance–complexity trade‑offs, and discuss scalability to higher dimensions.  
- Final scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
