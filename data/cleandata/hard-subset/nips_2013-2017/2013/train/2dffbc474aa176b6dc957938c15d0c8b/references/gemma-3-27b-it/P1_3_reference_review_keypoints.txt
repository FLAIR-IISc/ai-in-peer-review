- Proposes fully marginalizing the transition function f in GP-SSMs using a tailored PMCMC algorithm, a novel departure from parametric approximations.  
- Provides a fully Bayesian posterior over f, latent states, and hyperparameters.  
- Demonstrates the method on a synthetic nonlinear system and a cart-pole control problem with good empirical results.  
- Situates the work well relative to prior GP-SSM methods and highlights moving beyond MAP/parametric approaches.  
- Strength: nonparametric flexibility allows model complexity to grow with data.  
- Strength: principled Bayesian treatment yields improved uncertainty quantification.  
- Strength: tailored PMCMC appears efficient and exhibits good mixing; implementation reuses computations cleverly.  
- Strength: empirical demonstrations capture multimodality and learn complex dynamics from limited data.  
- Strength: clear presentation and largely accessible mathematical derivations.  
- Weakness: computational cost and scalability concerns, especially for higher-dimensional states and long time series; memory for inducing points (M) can be substantial.  
- Weakness: likely sensitivity to hyperparameters; need for more comprehensive tuning/sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: limited justification for selecting the FIC sparse GP prior; should compare other sparse approximations.  
- Weakness: comparisons to alternative/state-of-the-art GP-SSM learning schemes are limited.  
- Weakness: more implementation details or code release would improve reproducibility.  
- Pro: represents a significant theoretical advance with a principled, flexible approach and strong empirical benchmarks.  
- Con: computational barriers, limited baseline comparisons, and insufficient robustness analysis temper adoption.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: expand scalability discussion, include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, add stronger baseline comparisons, and consider releasing code.
