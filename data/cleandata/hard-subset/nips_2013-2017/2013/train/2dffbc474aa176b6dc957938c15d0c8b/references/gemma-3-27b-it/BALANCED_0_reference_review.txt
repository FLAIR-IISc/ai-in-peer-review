This paper presents a compelling and technically sound approach to Bayesian inference and learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs), addressing a significant challenge in time series analysis and dynamical systems. The core idea of marginalizing out the state transition function using Particle MCMC (PMCMC) is a notable contribution, allowing for a fully nonparametric model without the limitations of previous parametric approaches or finite target point methods. The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the motivation, methodology, and experimental results.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the marginalization procedure and the tailored PMCMC algorithm appears rigorous and well-justified. The use of recent advances in PMCMC sampling (specifically PGAS) to improve mixing is a strong point. The connection to existing GP regression techniques for learning the dynamics after smoothing is clearly explained.
* **Novelty:** The method’s ability to perform fully Bayesian inference without assuming a parametric form for the transition dynamics is a key novelty. This contrasts favorably with prior work like Deisenroth et al. [5, 6] and Turner et al. [2], which typically rely on learning a fixed GP model. The FIC prior implementation further extends the capability.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally very well written, with a clear and logical flow of ideas. The equations are well-presented, and the explanation of the algorithm is detailed enough to allow for reproduction. Figures 1 and 2 are helpful in illustrating the key concepts.
* **Significance:** The approach addresses a fundamental challenge in SSMs – learning the dynamics – and offers a more flexible and potentially more accurate solution. The demonstration on both a synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole system showcases the practical applicability of the method.  The ability to quantify uncertainty, particularly in the cart-pole example, is a valuable contribution.
* **Thoroughness:** The paper provides a detailed discussion of implementation considerations, including computational complexity and optimizations (sparse GP with FIC priors). The inclusion of experimental results with error analysis adds credibility to the claims. The thorough referencing of related work is also commendable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Complexity:** While the authors address computational complexity with the FIC prior, it remains a significant concern. The O(M2T) complexity (even with optimization) may limit the scalability of the method to very long time series or high-dimensional state spaces. A more detailed discussion of the practical limitations and potential bottlenecks would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper does not delve deeply into the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameter choices (both for the GP and the PMCMC sampler). A more systematic investigation of hyperparameter tuning strategies (e.g., using cross-validation) would strengthen the findings.
* **Comparison with Alternatives:** The experimental comparison is relatively limited. While the results on the benchmark system are promising, a more comprehensive comparison against state-of-the-art SSM learning methods (e.g., those based on deep learning or other Bayesian approaches) would be helpful.  Specifically, how does this method perform compared to variational inference approaches for GP-SSMs?
* **FIC Prior Selection:** The choice of 40 inducing points for the FIC prior feels somewhat arbitrary. Justification for this choice, or a discussion of the impact of varying the number of inducing points, would be valuable.
* **Algorithm 1 Clarity:** While generally well-written, Algorithm 1 could benefit from more explicit comments explaining the purpose of each step and the meaning of the variables.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a core challenge in GP-SSMs with a novel and technically sound solution.
* Offers a more flexible and potentially more accurate approach than existing methods.
* Well-written, clearly explained, and thoroughly referenced.
* Demonstrates practical applicability through experiments on both a synthetic benchmark and a realistic control problem.
* The marginalization approach is a significant theoretical contribution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Computational complexity may limit scalability.
* Limited comparison with state-of-the-art alternative methods.
* Hyperparameter tuning is not discussed in detail.
* FIC prior parameter selection lacks justification.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of GP-SSMs. The proposed method is technically sound, novel, and well-motivated. While the computational complexity and limited comparison with alternatives are valid concerns, the overall quality of the work and the potential impact of the findings justify acceptance. 

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions.

**Specific Suggestions for Revision:**

* Provide a more detailed discussion of the computational limitations and potential strategies for scaling the method to larger problems.
* Expand the experimental comparison to include more state-of-the-art SSM learning methods.
* Investigate the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameter choices and provide guidance on appropriate tuning strategies.
* Justify the choice of the number of inducing points for the FIC prior or perform a sensitivity analysis.
* Add more comments to Algorithm 1 to improve clarity.
* Briefly discuss the benefits and drawbacks of using a variational inference approach in contrast to this PMCMC approach.
