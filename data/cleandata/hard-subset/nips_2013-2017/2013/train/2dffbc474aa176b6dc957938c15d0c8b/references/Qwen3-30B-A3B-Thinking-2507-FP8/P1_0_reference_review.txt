

# Peer Review: "Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC"

## Summary

This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to learning Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs), where the state transition function is modeled nonparametrically using a Gaussian process (GP). The key innovation is the development of a tailored Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithm, specifically Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS), to efficiently perform inference while marginalizing out the state transition function. Unlike previous approaches that typically model the GP using a finite set of target points (making the model effectively parametric), this work maintains the full nonparametric expressivity of the GP-SSM.

The paper makes several important contributions: (1) a theoretical derivation of the marginal prior for the state trajectory that enables non-Markovian inference; (2) a detailed implementation of PGAS for this model; (3) a sparse GP-SSM construction using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computational complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2T) where M << T; and (4) empirical validation on both synthetic and real-world systems demonstrating the approach's ability to learn complex dynamics even when the mean function is misspecified.

This work builds upon and extends prior research in GP-based SSMs (Deisenroth et al., Turner et al., Wang et al.) by providing the crucial missing component of fully Bayesian learning of the GP dynamics, rather than assuming the dynamics are already learned as in previous inference methods.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Soundness and Novelty**: The paper presents a technically sound approach to a well-known problem in state-space modeling. The marginalization of the state transition function and the use of PGAS for inference is a significant technical contribution that differs fundamentally from previous approaches. The authors clearly articulate how their method differs from prior work, particularly noting that previous approaches "tend to model the GP using a finite set of target points, in effect making the model parametric."

2. **Theoretical Foundation**: The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the approach, with clear derivations of the marginal prior (Equations 3-4), the CPF-AS algorithm, and the sparse GP-SSM construction. The computational complexity analysis is thorough and well-motivated.

3. **Empirical Validation**: The experiments are well-designed and effectively demonstrate the approach's capabilities. The synthetic nonlinear system with multimodal smoothing (Section 6.1) clearly shows how the GP-SSM can capture the true dynamics even when the mean function is misspecified. The cart and pole system experiment (Section 6.2) demonstrates practical applicability to a real-world problem.

4. **Clarity of Presentation**: The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical derivations are presented in a logical sequence, and the figures (particularly Figure 2) effectively illustrate key concepts. The authors have made a strong effort to explain the method in a way that is accessible to readers familiar with GP models and Bayesian inference.

5. **Computational Efficiency**: The sparse GP-SSM implementation with FIC prior provides a practical solution to the computational challenges of applying GP-SSMs to longer time series, which is a significant practical contribution.

## Weaknesses

1. **Comparison with Alternative Methods**: While the paper compares the GP-SSM with parametric models, it would strengthen the contribution to include a comparison with other nonparametric approaches for learning dynamics in state-space models. For example, how does it compare with deep learning-based methods for dynamics learning (e.g., using neural networks with probabilistic outputs)? The paper focuses on GP-based approaches, but acknowledging and comparing with alternative nonparametric methods would better establish the significance of the contribution.

2. **Scalability Limitations**: The paper mentions the sparse GP-SSM reduces complexity to O(M^2T), but it would be helpful to provide more concrete examples of how the performance scales with increasing T and M. The experiments only use T=200 and M=40, so it's unclear how well the approach would scale to longer time series.

3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity**: The paper doesn't thoroughly explore the sensitivity of the approach to hyperparameter choices, particularly for the sparse GP-SSM. The experiments use fixed hyperparameters (e.g., log-normal priors for hyperparameters), but it would be valuable to understand how sensitive the results are to these choices.

4. **Limited Theoretical Guarantees**: While the paper provides empirical results, it lacks theoretical guarantees on the convergence properties of the PGAS sampler for this specific model. The paper references the PGAS paper (Lindsten et al., 2012) but doesn't specifically address how the non-Markovian nature of the state trajectory affects convergence guarantees.

## Overall Recommendation

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian state-space modeling. The technical approach is novel, well-motivated, and well-executed. The paper addresses an important problem in a way that advances the state of the art. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the approach's capabilities, particularly in handling multimodal smoothing distributions and learning complex dynamics even with a misspecified mean function.

The paper meets all the criteria for a high-quality NIPS submission: it is technically sound, well-written, original, and significant. While there are some weaknesses in the comparison with alternative methods and scalability analysis, these do not substantially undermine the paper's contribution.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. **Expand the comparison section** to include a brief comparison with other nonparametric approaches for learning dynamics (e.g., neural network-based methods) to better establish the significance of the GP-SSM approach.

2. **Add a scalability analysis** showing how the approach performs with larger time series (e.g., T=1000 or T=10000) and varying numbers of inducing points M.

3. **Include a sensitivity analysis** for key hyperparameters, particularly for the sparse GP-SSM, to demonstrate robustness of the approach.

4. **Clarify the theoretical guarantees** for the PGAS sampler in this specific context, building on the references provided.

5. **Consider adding a discussion** of how this approach could be extended to handle more complex observation models, particularly when the observation function g is also unknown.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Arguments for Acceptance**:
- Addresses a fundamental problem in state-space modeling with a technically sound solution
- Provides a fully Bayesian approach that maintains the nonparametric expressivity of the GP-SSM
- Offers a practical sparse implementation for computational efficiency
- Demonstrates clear advantages over parametric approaches in challenging scenarios
- Well-written and well-organized with strong empirical validation

**Arguments for Rejection**:
- Limited comparison with alternative nonparametric methods for learning dynamics
- Some questions about scalability to very long time series remain unanswered
- Limited discussion of theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed sampler

## Final Recommendation

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The contribution is significant, the technical approach is sound, and the experimental validation is thorough. While there are some areas for improvement, they are not substantial enough to outweigh the paper's strong contributions to the field of Bayesian state-space modeling. This work will be valuable to researchers working on nonparametric modeling of dynamical systems and provides an important tool for the broader machine learning community.
