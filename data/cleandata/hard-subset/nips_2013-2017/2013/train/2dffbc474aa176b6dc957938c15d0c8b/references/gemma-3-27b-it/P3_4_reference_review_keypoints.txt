- Introduces a method for learning nonlinear SSMs by marginalizing the GP transition function and using PMCMC for Bayesian inference.  
- Key innovation: marginalization of f enables fully non-parametric learning without parametric constraints.  
- Tailored PMCMC sampler addresses the non-Markovian smoothing problem caused by marginalizing f.  
- Provides a sparse GP (FIC) implementation to improve scalability.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole control example.  
- Positions the work clearly relative to prior GP-SSM literature and PMCMC advances.  
- Non-parametric flexibility allows model complexity to adapt to data automatically.  
- Fully Bayesian treatment gives coherent uncertainty quantification for planning and decision-making.  
- Reported good mixing of the sampler indicates effective inference performance.  
- Sparse GP approach offers a practical path toward scaling to higher-dimensional problems.  
- Experiments show ability to learn complex dynamics from limited, noisy data and capture multimodal smoothing.  
- Paper is well-written with clear derivations and useful implementation details.  
- Computational complexity (O(M^2 T)) may still be prohibitive for long time series or high-dimensional states.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning strategies.  
- Empirical comparisons are limited; missing comparisons to other state-of-the-art SSM methods.  
- Experimental scope is narrow; more diverse control tasks would strengthen practical claims.  
- No public code provided, hindering reproducibility and adoption.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant technical contributions, promising results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, insufficient hyperparameter/baseline analysis, and lack of code.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance pending addressing concerns on hyperparameters, comparisons, and code release.  
- Score: 8/10 (Strong Accept).
