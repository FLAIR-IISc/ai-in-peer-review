- Paper proposes fully Bayesian learning for nonlinear GP-SSMs by placing a GP prior on dynamics f, marginalizing f analytically, and inferring x0:T and θ with Particle MCMC (PGAS).  
- Collapsing f yields a non‑Markovian prior over x1:T expressed as a product of GP one‑step predictive Gaussians (Eq. 3–4).  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling to sample trajectories from the collapsed model and alternates with hyperparameter sampling; posterior over f recovered via GP regression on sampled transitions.  
- Introduces efficient implementation tricks (per‑particle Cholesky rank‑1 up/downdates) and a sparse FIC GP prior to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Marginalization and non‑Gaussian joint structure are technically sound and the use of PGAS/ancestor sampling for non‑Markovian models is appropriate.  
- Hyperparameter sampling by conditioning on x0:T (standard GP regression with covariance Q) and using slice sampling is justified.  
- Complexity claims and reuse of factorizations are plausible but depend critically on careful bookkeeping; ambiguity whether O(T^3) is total per sweep or per particle.  
- Ancestor sampling weight computations could become expensive unless factorizations and factorizations reuse are implemented efficiently.  
- Empirical evaluation shows multimodal smoothing and sensible uncertainty on two examples (nonlinear benchmark, cart‑pole) but is limited in scope and baselines.  
- Experiments use modest PGAS settings (50 iterations, N=20) with no mixing/ESS diagnostics, runtime/memory comparisons, or sensitivity analyses reported.  
- FIC evaluation uses 40 inducing points but lacks details on inducing input selection and sensitivity to M.  
- Paper acknowledges computational challenges and approximations, but could discuss scalability w.r.t. state dimension, horizon length, mean‑function sensitivity, and identifiability when g is unknown.  
- Writing and derivations are clear; implementation guidance (rank‑1 updates, reuse of factorizations, P matrix in FIC) is helpful.  
- Suggests adding a concise complexity/memory table, clarifying Q structure and priors over θ, including mixing diagnostics, and describing the SMC proposal used.  
- Core novelty: collapsing f in GP‑SSM combined with PGAS enables fully Bayesian nonparametric dynamics learning, filling a gap left by EM/MAP or filtering‑only GP approaches.  
- Conceptually significant and general; predictive mixture‑of‑Gaussians over f(x*) is a useful outcome for downstream tasks.  
- Practical impact limited by computational cost and assumption of known observation model g; FIC mitigates cost but scalability evidence is limited.  
- Pros: principled Bayesian formulation, exact marginalization of f, appropriate use of PGAS, solid implementation insights, demonstrates multimodal smoothing and uncertainty‑aware predictions.  
- Cons: limited empirical evaluation, unclear complexity statements regarding dependence on N and T, assumption of known g, and sparse details on hyperparameter sampling/tuning.  
- Key questions for authors: precise per‑sweep complexity and memory as functions of N, T, nx, M; sensitivity to mean‑function misspecification; ESS/mixing diagnostics and guidance for choosing N; inducing input selection strategy and sensitivity to M; experiments with unknown g.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — novel and technically sound but would be strengthened by more comprehensive experiments and clearer complexity reporting.
