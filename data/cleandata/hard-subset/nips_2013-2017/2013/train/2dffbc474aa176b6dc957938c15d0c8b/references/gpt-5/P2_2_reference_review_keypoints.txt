- Summary: Places a GP prior on the transition f, marginalizes f to obtain a non-Markovian trajectory prior, and samples x0:T and θ with particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS); f predictions reduce to GP regression yielding a mixture-of-Gaussians; introduces an FIC-based sparse variant reducing complexity to O(M^2 T).

- Novelty vs prior work: Differs from MAP/EM and variational GP-SSMs by keeping a fully nonparametric f and collapsing it exactly, and leverages PGAS (with ancestor sampling) for the induced non-Markovian prior.

- Strength: Conceptual novelty in collapsing f to remove strong x–f coupling while retaining nonparametric expressivity.

- Strength: Sound algorithmic design—correct PGAS usage, sensible hyperparameter sampling via GP marginal likelihood, and efficient Cholesky/rank-1 update strategies.

- Strength: Practical value—mixture-of-Gaussians predictive distributions capture multimodality; sparse FIC variant improves computational feasibility.

- Experimental demonstration: Shows feasibility on a multimodal synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole system, illustrating multimodal smoothing and epistemic uncertainty away from data.

- Weakness: Limited empirical evaluation—only two small datasets, no runtimes, ESS, autocorrelations, or sensitivity analyses to N, T, M, or hyperparameters.

- Weakness: Narrow metrics—mainly RMSE of f; lacks predictive log-likelihoods, long-horizon rollout accuracy, calibration metrics, and quantitative baselines for cart-pole.

- Weakness: Some technical details deferred—proposal distribution for SMC with non-Gaussian likelihoods not fully derived, and pseudocode/numerical stabilization details are missing for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Inducing input selection and its interaction with mixing/bias are not analyzed; the claim that sparse can outperform full is unsubstantiated.

- Weakness: Scalability concerns—memory/complexity scaling with state dimension nx and larger T not fully discussed, and assuming known g limits generality.

- Quality assessment: Theoretical derivations and complexity analysis are sound, but empirical claims of good mixing and efficiency lack quantitative support.

- Clarity: Generally readable and well organized, but some sections are terse or notation-dense; would benefit from pseudocode, figures, and clearer reference formatting.

- Originality: The fully collapsed GP-SSM inference combined with PGAS is novel; adapting FIC to the trajectory prior and efficient updates is a practical contribution.

- Significance: Potentially important for Bayesian nonlinear SSMs if empirically validated at larger scale and against strong baselines; current experiments limit impact.

- Requested additions: Report mixing diagnostics and runtimes, compare to EM/MAP and variational baselines, clarify proposal choices for non-Gaussian likelihoods, analyze sensitivity to mean function and inducing inputs, and provide predictive likelihoods and multi-step rollouts.

- Pros/Cons for acceptance: Pros—principled, elegant inference preserving nonparametric modeling; solid use of PMCMC and useful sparse variant. Cons—insufficient empirical validation and underexplored practical details.

- Recommendation: Weak accept.
