- Paper proposes fully Bayesian learning in GP-SSMs by marginalizing the GP transition function f, enabling nonparametric inference with tractable smoothing.  
- Adapts Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to handle the resulting non-Markovian latent process for efficient state smoothing.  
- Introduces a sparse GP implementation using the FIC prior to reduce complexity from O(T³) to O(M²T) with M ≪ T.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM work but differs by providing a fully Bayesian, nonparametric treatment rather than parametric or MAP/EM approaches.  

Strengths:
- Elegant derivation of the marginal prior (Eq.4) demonstrating tractability despite non-Markovianity.  
- Well-executed algorithmic adaptation of PGAS with clear explanation of ancestor sampling.  
- Thorough complexity analysis and practical sparse GP implementation details.  
- Experiments show ability to recover multimodal smoothing distributions and capture uncertainty (e.g., cart-pole).  
- Clear organization and presentation with consistent notation and contextualization of prior work.  

Weaknesses:
- Lacks theoretical guarantees on PGAS convergence rates or mixing-time bounds in the non-Markovian setting.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited; missing comparisons to other Bayesian nonparametric SSM methods.  
- Sparse GP inducing point selection (greedy) is used but not analyzed for its effect on performance.  
- Scalability to very long time series (T >> 1000) or high-dimensional states is not adequately addressed.  
- No sensitivity analysis of hyperparameter priors, which can heavily influence GP behavior.  

Quality and clarity:
- Theoretical derivations are sound; paper is technically strong and mostly clear.  
- Some technical derivations (Section 3.1) may be hard to follow for readers without strong GP background; an intuitive preface would help.  

Originality and significance:
- Significant contribution by enabling fully Bayesian, nonparametric dynamics learning in GP-SSMs; PGAS adaptation is a meaningful advance.  
- Sparse FIC extension improves practicality though it builds on existing sparse GP literature.  

Overall assessment and recommendation:
- Strong paper with meaningful methodological and experimental contributions; recommended for acceptance despite theoretical and experimental gaps.  

Pro-acceptance arguments:
- Novel, fully Bayesian nonparametric approach; effective PGAS adaptation; solid complexity analysis; handles multimodality; well-written.  

Con-acceptance arguments:
- Insufficient theoretical analysis of sampler convergence; limited experimental breadth; sparse GP and scalability issues not fully explored.  

Suggestions for improvement:
- Add discussion or partial analysis of PGAS convergence properties for the non-Markovian case.  
- Expand experiments to include other Bayesian nonparametric SSM baselines.  
- Provide sensitivity analysis for inducing point selection and number of inducing points.  
- Discuss strategies or limitations for scaling to very long time series (e.g., T > 10,000).  
- Add an intuitive explanation of the marginal prior before the formal derivation.
