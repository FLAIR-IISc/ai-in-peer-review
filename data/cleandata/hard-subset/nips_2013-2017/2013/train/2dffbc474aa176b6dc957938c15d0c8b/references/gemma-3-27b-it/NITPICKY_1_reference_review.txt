This paper presents a Bayesian nonparametric approach to learning Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs) using Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC). The core idea – marginalizing out the state transition function – is a valuable contribution, as it avoids the limitations of parametric approaches common in existing GP-SSM literature. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on both a synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole system, showcasing its ability to handle multimodal smoothing distributions and provide uncertainty estimates. However, despite the novelty and potential significance, the paper is riddled with numerous minor issues and a general lack of polish that, cumulatively, detract from its impact.

**Summary of Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Marginalizing out the transition function *f* is a significant departure from existing GP-SSM methods and allows for a truly nonparametric approach.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the marginal prior (Equation 3) and the use of PGAS are well-motivated and appear theoretically correct.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the method's capability to learn complex dynamics and provide uncertainty estimates. The cart-pole example is particularly compelling.
*   **Addressing a Core Problem:** The paper tackles a fundamental challenge in GP-SSM learning: how to avoid the limitations of fixed parametric models for the dynamics.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

*   **Excessive Detail & Redundancy:** The paper is overly verbose, with substantial repetition of concepts and equations. For example, the explanation of the GP formulation is repeated multiple times.
*   **Notational Inconsistencies:** Numerous minor notational inconsistencies exist throughout the paper.  For instance, *x<sub>t</sub>* sometimes represents a single state and other times a trajectory *x<sub>0:t</sub>*.  *u<sub>t</sub>* is stated as known input but then omitted in equations (2a) and (2b).
*   **Missing Justification for Design Choices:** The choice of the FIC prior isn’t fully justified compared to other sparse GP methods. The rationale for specific hyperparameter priors is also lacking.
*   **Implementation Details Are Buried:** Critical implementation details for achieving computational efficiency (e.g., the reuse of Cholesky factorizations) are described in Section 4.2 but the connection to the preceding equations is unclear. This makes replication difficult.
*   **Lack of Scalability Discussion:** While Section 4 addresses computational complexity, there’s limited discussion on the scalability of the method to high-dimensional state spaces or long time series.
*   **Limited Comparative Analysis:** The experiments compare against a limited set of baselines. The comparison lacks more modern GP-SSM methods or relevant state-of-the-art approaches in time series modeling.
*   **Formatting Issues:** Throughout the paper, there are minor formatting inconsistencies, such as inconsistent spacing and awkward equation layouts.

**Detailed Critique:**

*   **Introduction:** The introduction is too lengthy and contains phrases like “very good mixing” which are subjective and lack quantitative support.
*   **Section 2:** The statement about avoiding non-identifiability issues with a flexible *g* is not fully convincing. Flexible *g* can also lead to issues.
*   **Section 3.1:** The justification for the normality assumption in (4) is weak.  Saying it “looks very much like a multivariate Gaussian” is insufficient. More explanation is needed.
*   **Section 3.3.1:** The CPF-AS algorithm relies on a prior trajectory *x̃<sub>0:T</sub>*. The choice of this trajectory and its impact on convergence aren’t discussed.
*   **Section 4.1:** The explanation of the FIC prior feels rushed. The connection between (7) and the computational benefits isn't fully explained.
*   **Section 6.1:** The description of the synthetic system is unclear. What is the motivation for the specific dynamics? The table's column headers are poorly labelled (“Joint Smoothing sample”) and require clarification.
*   **Section 6.2:** The claim about the model reporting different uncertainty in different regions of the state space is not rigorously supported by the figure.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   Addresses a significant problem in GP-SSM learning.
*   Presents a novel and theoretically sound approach.
*   Demonstrates empirical effectiveness.

**Con:**

*   The sheer number of minor flaws and inconsistencies significantly detract from the paper's readability and credibility.
*   The lack of a thorough comparative analysis weakens the claim of significance.
*   The paper is overly verbose and lacks conciseness.
*   The implementation details are difficult to follow.

**Overall Assessment:**

I rate this paper as **weak reject**. While the core idea is novel and the potential contribution is substantial, the numerous minor flaws and lack of polish are too significant to overlook. A major revision addressing the issues raised above, including a more thorough experimental evaluation and improved clarity of presentation, would be necessary for reconsideration. The paper, as it stands, does not meet the high standards of quality expected for NIPS publication. The authors demonstrate technical competence, but their attention to detail needs substantial improvement.
