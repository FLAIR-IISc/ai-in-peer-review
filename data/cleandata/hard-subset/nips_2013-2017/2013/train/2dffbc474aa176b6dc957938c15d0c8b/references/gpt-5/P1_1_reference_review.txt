Summary
The paper tackles learning and inference in nonlinear state-space models where the state transition function is unknown and modeled nonparametrically by a Gaussian process (GP). The key idea is to marginalize the transition function f exactly, yielding a non-Markovian prior over state trajectories, and then perform posterior inference over states and hyperparameters using Particle MCMC, specifically Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS). This collapses a highly coupled variable (f) and mitigates path degeneracy via ancestor sampling. Hyperparameters are updated with slice sampling, and a sparse GP prior (FIC) is incorporated to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T). The method outputs smoothing distributions with f integrated out, and then leverages standard GP regression conditionals to obtain the posterior over f or predictive mixtures over f(x*). Experiments on a 1D nonlinear benchmark with multimodal smoothing and on a cart-pole dynamical system show good smoothing and one-step predictive performance, and illustrate the value of informative mean functions.

Relation to prior work
- GP-SSMs and GP-based dynamics: Deisenroth et al. developed filtering/smoothing assuming learned GPs; this work provides the learning component in a fully Bayesian manner rather than point estimates (e.g., EM/MAP) as in Turner et al. (EM with parametric GP-like models) and Wang et al. (GPDM with MAP over latent variables and hyperparameters).
- PMCMC: Builds on Andrieu et al. (PMCMC) and Lindsten et al. (PGAS), adapting ancestor sampling to a non-Markovian latent prior induced by marginalizing a GP transition.
- Sparse GPs: Uses the FIC construction (Quinonero-Candela & Rasmussen) to achieve scalable sampling within PMCMC.
The novel combination is the exact marginalization of f in GP-SSMs coupled with PGAS and computational schemes to make the non-Markovian sampling efficient.

Quality
- Soundness: The derivation of the collapsed trajectory prior p(x1:T|θ,x0) via GP conditionals is correct, and the use of PGAS to leave the smoothing distribution invariant is appropriate. The ancestor sampling weights w̃ ∝ w p(x̃t:T|xi0:t−1) are correctly adapted to the non-Markovian prior, with practical computation strategies via Cholesky updates or FIC.
- Algorithms: Implementation details to reduce complexity (rank-one up-/downdates; reusing factorizations; FIC) are sensible. Sampling θy and θx is standard; slice sampling for covariance hyperparameters is reasonable given the collapsed likelihood.
- Empirical support: The 1D benchmark demonstrates handling of multimodal smoothing and predictive accuracy; cart-pole shows calibrated uncertainties. However, the empirical section is relatively thin: no wall-clock comparisons, ablations, or comparisons to strong learning baselines beyond simple parametric models, and limited analysis of mixing/convergence (only N=20, 50 PGAS iterations reported).
- Claims: The central claims (fully Bayesian learning with f marginalized; efficient PGAS-based inference; scalability with FIC; ability to incorporate informative mean functions) are supported, but broader performance claims would benefit from more extensive experiments.

Clarity
- The paper is generally clear and well-organized. The presentation of the collapsed prior and its computational structure is helpful. PGAS description is concise and correct. Section 4 clearly discusses complexity and practical updates.
- Some missing details hinder reproducibility: exact kernels and priors, slice sampling schedules, convergence diagnostics, choice/placement strategy for inducing inputs, and more explicit pseudocode for the CPF-AS in the non-Markovian case, including how the predictive proposal (5) is computed in practice.

Originality
- The work is original in collapsing the GP transition within a PMCMC/PGAS framework for GP-SSMs, retaining nonparametric flexibility rather than instantiating finite function values as parameters. The adaptation of ancestor sampling weights to the GP-induced non-Markov prior and the integration of FIC into the PMCMC loop are also novel and useful.

Significance
- This is a principled advance for learning flexible, data-adaptive dynamics in SSMs with full uncertainty quantification. The approach can influence both Bayesian time series methods and practical applications where model structure is unknown but smoothness priors are reasonable.
- Significance would be strengthened by demonstrating scalability to longer sequences and higher-dimensional states, and by comparisons to contemporary variational GP-SSMs/GPDMs, EM-based approaches, and PMCMC methods that explicitly sample f-values rather than collapsing.

Strengths
- Fully Bayesian, nonparametric treatment: exact marginalization of f; credible posterior over states and hyperparameters.
- Effective use of PGAS to mitigate path degeneracy in a non-Markovian setting.
- Solid computational engineering: Cholesky rank updates; FIC-based O(M^2 T) scaling and discussion of reuse.
- Flexible incorporation of prior knowledge via mean functions.
- Predictive mixture over f(x*) that captures multimodality.

Weaknesses
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and depth; lacks comparisons to strong baselines in GP-SSM learning (e.g., GPDM/variational methods), and lacks ablations (e.g., with/without collapsing f, with/without ancestor sampling).
- No runtime or scaling studies; unclear practical N, M, T regimes where the method is competitive.
- Assumes known observation model g; the impact of mild misspecification or jointly flexible g is not explored.
- Sparse GP section leaves inducing input selection largely unspecified; sensitivity to M/locations not studied.
- Convergence diagnostics and mixing assessments (autocorrelations, ESS) are not reported.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add baselines: GPDM/EM-based methods, variational GP-SSMs, and PMCMC samplers that condition on finite f-values, to show the benefit of collapsing and ancestor sampling.
- Provide runtime and memory profiles vs. T, nx, N, and M; include wall-clock vs. accuracy trade-offs.
- Report mixing statistics and sensitivity to N, number of PGAS iterations, and proposal choices in (5).
- Detail kernel choices, priors, slice sampling settings; release code or pseudo-code that specifies the CPF-AS updates for the non-Markov prior.
- Study robustness to imperfect g and explore limited flexibility in g to address identifiability while maintaining tractability.
- For FIC, include a practical inducing input selection procedure and sensitivity analysis.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper presents a principled, technically sound, and novel approach with solid algorithmic contributions and clear potential impact. The empirical evaluation is currently modest; stronger comparisons and scaling evidence would make this a clear accept.

Pros
- Novel and principled collapsed PGAS framework for GP-SSMs.
- Careful computational treatment and sparse GP integration.
- Handles multimodal smoothing and provides calibrated predictive distributions.

Cons
- Limited experiments and comparisons; no scaling/mixing diagnostics.
- Sparse GP practicalities and inducing input selection left underspecified.
- Assumes known observation model; generality and robustness not fully demonstrated.
