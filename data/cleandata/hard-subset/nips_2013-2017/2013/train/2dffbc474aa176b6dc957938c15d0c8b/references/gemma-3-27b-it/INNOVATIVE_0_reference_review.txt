This paper presents a novel and ambitious approach to learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs) by fully marginalizing out the state transition function. This is a significant departure from existing methods that typically rely on parametric approximations after learning the GP dynamics. The authors skillfully combine Particle MCMC (PMCMC) with recent advances in efficient sampling to achieve this, enabling fully Bayesian inference over the dynamics, states, and hyperparameters. The theoretical grounding is strong, and the proposed algorithm appears well-motivated and technically sound.

The key strength of this work lies in its originality. The idea of complete marginalization elegantly sidesteps the challenges of non-identifiability inherent in simultaneously learning both dynamics and states with flexible function approximators. This allows the GP to truly act as a nonparametric prior, adapting its complexity based on the data without being constrained by a pre-defined parametric form. The experiments, while relatively simple, demonstrate the promise of the approach, particularly in a multimodal scenario where the GP-SSM correctly captures the ambiguity in the state estimates. The inclusion of a sparse GP variant to address computational challenges is also a welcome addition, showcasing practical considerations.

However, the paper is not without its weaknesses. While the theoretical justification is solid, the experimental validation feels somewhat limited. The cart-pole example is compelling, but a more extensive evaluation on diverse and challenging benchmarks would bolster the claims of broader applicability. The comparison to existing GP-SSM methods (Deisenroth et al., Turner et al.) feels cursory. A more direct comparison, potentially using a common codebase and evaluation metrics, would provide a clearer understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposed approach.  The implementation details, although present, are quite dense and could benefit from further clarification, especially regarding the efficient update strategies for the Cholesky factorization. Further, the reliance on log-normal priors might limit the generality of the results. The sensitivity to prior choice should be discussed. 

The paper does a reasonable job referencing related work, but the connections to the broader literature on non-parametric state-space modeling (beyond GPs) could be strengthened. For example, discussion of particle filters with kernelized transition operators might be relevant.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* **High Originality:** The complete marginalization of the state transition function is a novel and impactful idea.
* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:**  The method is well-grounded in Bayesian principles and leverages recent advances in PMCMC sampling.
* **Potential for Significant Impact:**  By removing the need for parametric approximations, this work opens up new possibilities for flexible and accurate modeling of complex dynamical systems.
* **Demonstrated Promise:**  The experiments, while limited, show that the method can successfully learn challenging dynamics and provide accurate state estimates.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* **Limited Experimental Validation:** The experiments are relatively simple and lack a comprehensive comparison to existing methods.
* **Dense Implementation Details:** The implementation details are challenging to follow and could benefit from further clarification.
* **Prior Sensitivity:** The impact of prior choices on the results is not fully investigated.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some limitations in experimental validation and implementation clarity, I strongly recommend accepting this paper. The novelty and theoretical elegance of the proposed approach are compelling, and the potential impact on the field is significant. The authors have tackled a challenging problem with a bold and innovative solution. While further work is needed to fully assess the practical benefits and limitations, this paper represents a substantial contribution to the literature on GP-SSMs and Bayesian non-parametric modeling. I believe the program committee will find this paper a refreshing and valuable addition to NIPS.
