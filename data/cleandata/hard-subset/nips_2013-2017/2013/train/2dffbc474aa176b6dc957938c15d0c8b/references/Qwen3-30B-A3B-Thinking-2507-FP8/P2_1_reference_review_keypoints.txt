- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the GP transition function and uses PMCMC for inference.  
- Key algorithmic innovation: use of Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample trajectories in the non-Markovian marginal model.  
- Presents a formal derivation of the marginal prior for state trajectories, highlighting the resulting non-Markovian structure.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant to reduce computational cost and improve scalability.  
- Empirical results demonstrate ability to handle multimodal smoothing distributions and complex dynamics (nonlinear benchmark, cart-and-pole).  
- Paper is technically sound with thorough theoretical derivations and reproducible methodological detail for experts.  
- Experimental design and quantitative comparisons convincingly show advantages over parametric baselines.  
- Writing, notation, figures, and algorithm descriptions are clear and well-organized.  
- Novelty: first full Bayesian marginalization of GP transition in SSMs combined with PGAS, distinct from prior parametric or MAP approaches.  
- Sparse variant adapts established inducing-point ideas to the GP-SSM context, adding practical value.  
- Significance: provides a principled framework for learning complex dynamics without parametric assumptions, useful for time-series, robotics, and control.  
- Strength: captures multimodality in smoothing distributions where parametric mean functions fail.  
- Complexity limitation: vanilla method has O(T^3) cost and sparse variant O(M^2T), with limited comparison to other GP/SSM costs.  
- Scalability concern: unclear performance for very long time series (T in thousands/millions), and sparse method’s limits not fully explored.  
- Limited empirical comparison with other Bayesian inference approaches (e.g., variational SSM methods).  
- Inducing point selection for the sparse variant is briefly treated; impact of different strategies is not explored.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., number of particles N) is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, well-executed contribution despite minor scalability and comparison gaps.  
- Suggested improvements: expand discussion/experiments on inducing point selection, report computational times, compare to variational Bayesian SSM methods, and discuss limitations for very long time series.
