- Paper marginalizes the GP transition function f exactly, yielding a non-Markovian prior over latent states and collapsing x–f dependence.
- Uses Particle MCMC (PGAS) to sample states x0:T | θ, y and then hyperparameters θ | x0:T, y; GP posterior and predictions follow from standard GP regression.
- Presents a sparse FIC variant to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) with implementation strategies (Cholesky reuse, rank-1 updates).
- Positions work between GP dynamical models and SMC/MCMC methods, building on PMCMC/PGAS and sparse GP literature.
- Conceptual novelty: collapsing f preserves nonparametric expressivity and addresses strong posterior coupling between x and f.
- Methodologically sound: correct collapsed prior derivation and appropriate use of PGAS with ancestor-sampling for non-Markov models.
- Practical contributions: detailed complexity analysis and implementation notes that improve viability.
- Flexibility: supports general (non-Gaussian) observation models and separates GP and measurement hyperparameters with suitable sampling strategies.
- Empirical results show capability on a multimodal 1D system and a 4D cart-pole, demonstrating learning and uncertainty quantification.
- Experimental scope is limited: few benchmarks and no comprehensive comparisons to strong baselines like EM/RBF, GPDM, or EP-based methods.
- Missing runtime and wall-clock comparisons; reported RMSE table not fully shown.
- Lacks diagnostics on chain mixing, ESS, sensitivity to particle count N, sequence length T, and hyperpriors.
- No empirical analysis of confounding between process noise Q and GP flexibility; sensitivity not examined.
- Important implementation/experiment details (kernel choice, priors, initialization, inducing input selection, varying M) are under-specified.
- Scalability concerns: per-iteration cost multiplied by N and PGAS iterations may be large for long sequences; limited discussion of memory/computation trade-offs and parallelization.
- Claims of “very good mixing” and efficiency are unsupported by quantitative diagnostics.
- Clarity is generally good, but notation is dense; suggests adding pseudo-code, kernel/prior spec, and appendix on rank-1 update stability.
- Originality: novel and nontrivial combination of exact GP marginalization with PGAS in SSMs; sparse variant is a meaningful extension.
- Significance: addresses an important problem for flexible dynamics learning and smoothing with uncertainty; relevant to GP and SSM communities.
- Suggested experiments/additions: mixing diagnostics, wall-clock times, comparisons to strong baselines, investigation of Q vs. GP amplitude identifiability, and sensitivity analyses for M and inducing locations.
- Acceptance rationale: principled Bayesian approach, solid algorithmic integration, and practical guidance—promising contribution.
- Rejection rationale: limited/partially reported experiments, lacking baselines and robustness/scalability evidence.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept; strengthen experiments and diagnostics to make acceptance clear.
