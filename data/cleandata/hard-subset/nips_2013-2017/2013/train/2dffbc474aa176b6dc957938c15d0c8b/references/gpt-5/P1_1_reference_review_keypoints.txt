- Proposes fully Bayesian learning for GP state-space models by exactly marginalizing the transition function f to obtain a non-Markovian prior over states.
- Performs posterior inference over states and hyperparameters using Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS), mitigating path degeneracy.
- Updates hyperparameters with slice sampling and uses a sparse FIC GP prior to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).
- Produces smoothing distributions with f integrated out and recovers posterior/predictive distributions over f via standard GP conditionals.
- Empirical tests on a 1D multimodal benchmark and a cart-pole system show good smoothing, predictive performance, and value of informative mean functions.
- Builds on prior GP-SSM and GP-dynamics work by providing a fully Bayesian learning component rather than point estimates.
- Adapts PMCMC and PGAS methods to the non-Markovian prior induced by marginalizing the GP transition.
- Integrates FIC sparse GP construction into PMCMC for scalable sampling.
- Novel combination: exact marginalization of f with PGAS and computational strategies for efficient non-Markovian sampling.
- Derivation of the collapsed trajectory prior and PGAS adaptation is sound and correctly handled for non-Markovian priors.
- Ancestor sampling weights and practical computation (Cholesky updates, FIC) are correctly adapted and implementable.
- Algorithmic optimizations (rank-one updates, reusing factorizations, FIC) are sensible and practical.
- Slice sampling for covariance hyperparameters is a reasonable choice given the collapsed likelihood.
- Experiments support key claims but are relatively thin: limited wall-clock, ablations, and strong-baseline comparisons.
- Reported PGAS iterations (N=20,50) provide limited analysis of mixing and convergence behavior.
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized; presentation of collapsed prior and computational details is helpful.
- Missing reproducibility details: exact kernels/prior settings, slice-sampling schedules, inducing input placement, and explicit CPF-AS pseudocode for the non-Markovian case.
- Original contribution is collapsing the GP transition within PGAS for GP-SSMs and adapting ancestor sampling and FIC in this context.
- Significant as a principled advance for flexible dynamics learning with uncertainty, but significance would be stronger with scalability and broader comparisons.
- Strengths: fully Bayesian nonparametric treatment, effective use of PGAS, solid computational engineering, flexible mean functions, and multimodal predictive mixtures.
- Weaknesses: limited empirical evaluation, no runtime/scaling studies, assumption of known observation model g, unspecified inducing input selection, and missing convergence diagnostics.
- Suggested improvements: add comparisons to GPDM/variational/EM baselines and samplers that condition on f, include runtime/memory scaling, report mixing statistics, and provide kernel/prior/sampling details and inducing input strategies.
- Recommendation: weak accept — technically sound and novel but requires stronger experiments and scaling evidence.
- Pros: novel collapsed PGAS framework, careful computational treatment, handles multimodal smoothing with calibrated predictive distributions.
- Cons: limited experiments/comparisons, underspecified sparse-GP practicalities, and untested robustness to observation-model misspecification.
