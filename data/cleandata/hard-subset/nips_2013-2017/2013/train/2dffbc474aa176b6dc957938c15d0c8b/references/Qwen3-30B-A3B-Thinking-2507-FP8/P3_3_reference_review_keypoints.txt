- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function f and sampling p(x0:T | y0:T) without a fixed dynamics model.  
- Develops a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to handle the non-Markovian structure from marginalization.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM using the FIC prior to reduce complexity from O(T⁴) to O(M²T).  
- Marginalizing f preserves full nonparametric expressivity compared to prior parametric GP approximations.  
- Derivation of the marginal prior p(x0:T | θ) is elegant, expressing GP predictive distribution as a product of Gaussians.  
- Computational complexity analysis is thorough, with practical implementation details (Cholesky reuse) supporting the O(T³) and O(M²T) claims.  
- Experiments show strong performance on a synthetic multimodal smoothing benchmark, demonstrating flexibility over misspecified parametric models.  
- Sparse GP-SSM performs well with only 40 inducing points in experiments.  
- Cart-pole experiment shows the method captures uncertainty across state-space regions.  
- Claim of “very good mixing” for the PMCMC sampler lacks quantitative evidence or comparison to alternative samplers.  
- Sparse GP evaluation is limited; no exploration of how varying M affects accuracy vs. cost (e.g., RMSE vs. M).  
- Insufficient comparison with other Bayesian nonparametric SSM methods (e.g., refs [5,6,15]) to establish novelty/advantages.  
- No evidence or discussion on overfitting risk and scalability to higher-dimensional state spaces beyond the 4D cart-pole.  
- Overall: technically sound and valuable, but requires additional quantitative mixing diagnostics, sparse-GP trade-off analysis, and broader comparisons; recommendation: Weak Accept (major revisions).
