- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function f under a GP prior, yielding a non-Markovian prior over state trajectories.  
- Uses Particle MCMC, specifically Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS), to jointly sample states and hyperparameters under the collapsed prior.  
- Implements computational speedups: reusing Cholesky factorizations to reduce complexity to O(T^3) and a sparse FIC GP yielding O(M^2 T) with M ≪ T.  
- Recovers posterior over f at test points as a mixture of GP predictors conditioned on sampled trajectories.  
- Experiments on a nonlinear SSM benchmark and a 4D cart-and-pole show good smoothing, calibrated uncertainty, and competitive predictive performance.  
- Novelty: exact marginalization of an infinite-dimensional transition function combined with PGAS for fully Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs.  
- Connects to prior GP dynamics/smoothing work and PMCMC/PGAS literature; frames sparse FIC as a principled prior choice.  
- Strength: elegant, principled collapse of f and appropriate use of PGAS for non-Markovian latent models.  
- Strength: practical algorithmic attention (rank-1 Cholesky updates, efficient ancestor weight computation, FIC speedups).  
- Strength: Bayesian learning yields multimodal posterior predictive mixtures capturing state uncertainty.  
- Strength: Experiments demonstrate robustness to mean-function misspecification and plausible predictions for cart-pole.  
- Strength: Modeling choice of known observation function g helps identifiability and aligns with practical sensor models.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental comparisons—missing prior GP-SSM learning methods (EM/variational) and strong nonparametric baselines.  
- Weakness: Limited diagnostics and ablations—only 50 PGAS iterations, N=20 particles; need mixing metrics, sensitivity to N, T, and M, and empirical runtime scaling.  
- Weakness: Potential identifiability between process noise Q and GP output scale; priors/regularization need discussion and posterior visualization.  
- Weakness: Unclear proposal in SMC (Eq.5)—clarify computation of the “optimal” proposal under the non-Markovian prior or correct notation.  
- Weakness: Ambiguity on multi-output modeling—whether outputs of f are independent or modeled with a multi-output GP affects complexity/performance.  
- Weakness: Inducing inputs in FIC are fixed—need selection strategy, sensitivity analysis, or discussion of learning them.  
- Weakness: Limited scope of observation model—discussion needed for extending to partially unknown parametric g.  
- Quality: Technically sound but some derivations are sketched; suggest appendix with explicit formulas, numerical stability details (jitter, downdate stability).  
- Clarity: Generally well-written; clarify Eq.(5), define nx early, state if Q is diagonal, and add CPF-AS pseudocode or illustrative example for computing weights.  
- Originality: Novel and nontrivial combination of exact marginalization of f with PGAS; FIC variant presented as principled prior.  
- Significance: Important contribution for fully Bayesian GP-SSMs with potential impact if empirical comparisons and scaling evidence are strengthened.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled treatment, adapted PGAS inference, computational efficiency, and demonstrated handling of multimodal smoothing.  
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete empirical comparisons, limited diagnostics, and some clarity gaps.  
- Questions for authors: Are output dimensions modeled independently; sensitivity to mean function; can you provide mixing diagnostics and wall-clock scaling; how are inducing inputs chosen?  
- Minor comments: Cite variational GP-SSM/GPDM work, include jitter/damping details, and consider HMC/NUTS for hyperparameter sampling.  
- Recommendation: Strong, technically sound contribution recommended for acceptance pending improvements to empirical comparisons and clarity.
