This paper presents a Bayesian nonparametric approach to learning and inference in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs) using Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC). The key idea is to marginalize out the state transition function, allowing for a fully Bayesian treatment and avoiding the limitations of parametric GP-SSM approaches. The method leverages recent advances in PMCMC samplers to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution. The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and addresses a significant problem in time series analysis and dynamical systems. 

The work builds upon a substantial body of prior research in GP-SSMs, acknowledging contributions from Deisenroth et al. [5, 6], Turner et al. [2], Ghahramani and Roweis [7], and Wang et al. [8]. The authors correctly position their work as providing a crucial component—learning the GP dynamics—needed for the inference methods proposed in [5, 6].  Compared to [2, 7, 8], which rely on parametric approximations or MAP estimation, this work offers a fully nonparametric Bayesian solution. The use of PMCMC, and specifically the Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm [4], is a significant contribution towards addressing the computational challenges of marginalizing out the transition function.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical framework is well-developed and clearly explained. The derivation of the marginal prior (Section 3.1) and the details of the SMC and PMCMC algorithms are presented with sufficient mathematical rigor. The explanation of the FIC prior and its computational advantages (Section 4) is also well-done.
* **Novelty:**  The key novelty lies in the complete marginalization of the state transition function in a GP-SSM, combined with the efficient sampling provided by the PGAS algorithm. This allows for a fully nonparametric Bayesian approach, overcoming the limitations of existing methods.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are clear and concise.  Figures 1 and 2 effectively illustrate the core concepts.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on both a synthetic nonlinear system and a cart-pole control problem. The results show the ability to accurately learn the dynamics and provide uncertainty estimates. The inclusion of a comparison to various baselines (Table 1) strengthens the empirical evaluation.
* **Implementation Details:** Providing detailed implementation considerations for computational efficiency, like the reuse of Cholesky factorizations (Section 4.2), is a significant strength. This demonstrates practical awareness and facilitates reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost:** While the authors address computational complexity, the method still appears computationally demanding, particularly for high-dimensional state spaces. The reliance on PMCMC inherently requires significant computational resources.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of GP-SSMs, and this approach specifically, is likely sensitive to the choice of hyperparameters (e.g., kernel parameters, noise variances). While priors are used, a more thorough discussion of hyperparameter tuning strategies or sensitivity analysis would be beneficial.
* **Scalability to High Dimensions:** The experiments are limited to relatively low-dimensional state spaces. The scalability of the method to significantly higher dimensions is not fully demonstrated. While the FIC prior helps, the memory requirements of the covariance matrices may still become a bottleneck.
* **Limited Discussion of Alternative PMCMC samplers**: Though PGAS is used, a more expansive discussion of alternative PMCMC samplers and justification for selecting PGAS would be helpful.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a challenging and important problem in time series analysis and dynamical systems.
* The proposed method offers a significant advance over existing approaches by providing a fully nonparametric Bayesian solution.
* The theoretical framework is sound, and the algorithms are well-described.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The computational cost of the method remains a significant concern, potentially limiting its applicability to large-scale problems.
* The paper does not fully address the sensitivity of the method to hyperparameter settings.
* Scalability to high-dimensional state spaces is not fully demonstrated.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of GP-SSMs. The technical novelty, clarity of presentation, and empirical validation are all commendable. While the computational cost and scalability limitations are valid concerns, they do not outweigh the significant advances offered by this work. The paper is suitable for acceptance to NIPS. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the concerns regarding hyperparameter sensitivity and scalability. A brief discussion of the limitations related to high-dimensional data and possible mitigation strategies would strengthen the paper.
