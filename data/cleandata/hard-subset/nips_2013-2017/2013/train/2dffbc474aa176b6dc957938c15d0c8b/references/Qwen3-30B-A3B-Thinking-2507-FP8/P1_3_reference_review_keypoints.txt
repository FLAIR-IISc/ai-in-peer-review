- Proposes a fully Bayesian method for GP-SSMs by marginalizing the state transition function and using Particle MCMC for inference.
- Introduces a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm to sample the smoothing distribution while marginalizing the GP dynamics.
- Presents a sparse GP-SSM construction using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce computational complexity.
- Demonstrates the approach on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart-pole system, showing competitive performance against parametric models.
- Technically rigorous with well-motivated derivations and thorough computational complexity analysis.
- Novel contribution: a truly nonparametric fully Bayesian learning method for GP-SSMs that contrasts with prior parametric approximations.
- PGAS adaptation for non-Markovian latent variable models is a valuable methodological contribution.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, showing capability for multimodal smoothing and practical applicability.
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized exposition, with reproducible detail and effective figures.
- Complexity analysis could include more explicit runtime comparisons to existing methods, especially on larger datasets.
- Does not thoroughly discuss or evaluate practical strategies for selecting inducing points in the sparse GP-SSM.
- Lacks broader comparisons with recent Bayesian SSM methods using other nonparametric models.
- Needs more insight into practical limitations: sensitivity to particle number, convergence behavior, and scalability to very long time series.
- Recommendation: accept—significant, technically sound, original, and clearly presented work with high relevance to robotics, control, and time series analysis.
- Final assessment rates the paper high on quality, clarity, originality, and significance; a valuable contribution to NIPS proceedings.
