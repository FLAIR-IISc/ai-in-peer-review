- Main contribution: Analytically marginalize the GP transition in GP-SSMs to obtain a non-Markovian prior over latent trajectories and perform fully Bayesian inference with Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS).

- Implementation: Presents efficient Cholesky-updated O(T^3) implementation and a sparse FIC variant reducing complexity to O(M^2 T).

- Relation to prior work: Builds on GP filtering/smoothing, GPDM/EM-style GP-SSMs, and PMCMC/PGAS, with novelty in the collapsed nonparametric treatment.

- Methodological soundness: Collapsing f is principled, reduces x–f coupling, and PGAS yields a valid invariant kernel addressing path degeneracy.

- Algorithmic detail: Sufficient implementation detail provided, including non-Markovian ancestor weights and factorization reuse for stated complexities.

- Empirical support: Positive but limited — strong 1D multimodal example and cart-pole one-step uncertainty; lacks extensive quantitative baselines and diagnostics.

- Clarity: Generally clear and organized; exposition of collapsed prior and PGAS is careful, but notation can be dense and some pseudocode/figures need improvement.

- Originality: Novel combination of exact GP marginalization, PGAS for non-Markovian latent processes, and a sparse prior option compared to prior GP-SSM work.

- Scientific significance: Provides a principled Bayesian GP-SSM approach that preserves nonparametric uncertainty and handles multimodal trajectory posteriors.

- Practical significance: Potentially useful for time-series and control but scalability concerns remain for large T or high-dimensional states.

- Pros — conceptual: Clean collapsed inference preserving nonparametric GP dynamics and robustness to mean-function misspecification.

- Pros — algorithmic: Well-motivated use of PGAS with good expected mixing and practical implementation tricks (Cholesky updates, complexity claims).

- Pros — outputs: Clear route to posterior predictive dynamics as mixtures and discussion of single-GP approximations for downstream tasks.

- Cons — empirical: Limited baselines (no EM/MAP/variational comparisons), missing ablations, mixing diagnostics (ESS/autocorr), and runtime/memory reporting.

- Cons — sparse GP practicalities: Little guidance or sensitivity analysis on choosing M and inducing input placement.

- Cons — scope: Assumes known observation model g; less applicable when g is unknown or misspecified.

- Cons — reproducibility: Dense notation and missing pseudocode/complete references may hinder nonspecialists.

- Suggestions: Add head-to-head comparisons to MAP/EM/GPDM, provide runtime/memory and mixing diagnostics, include multi-step rollout metrics, and analyze sensitivity to mean/Q and inducing choices.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid, novel methodology but needs stronger empirical validation to warrant clear accept.
