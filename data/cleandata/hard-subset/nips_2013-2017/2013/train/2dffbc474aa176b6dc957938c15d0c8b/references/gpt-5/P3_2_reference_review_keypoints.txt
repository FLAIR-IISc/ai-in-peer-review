- Paper proposes collapsing GP-SSMs by marginalizing the transition GP and performing fully Bayesian state/hyperparameter inference using Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS).  
- Introduces a sparse FIC variant to reduce computational cost from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) and gives implementation tips (Cholesky rank-1 updates/downdates).  
- Strength: Conceptually clean—marginalizing f avoids strong function-state posterior dependencies while preserving nonparametric modeling.  
- Strength: Methodologically sound use of PGAS for non-Markovian latent trajectories with correct invariance/ergodicity properties.  
- Strength: Practical algorithmic contributions addressing computational bottlenecks and reuse of factorizations.  
- Strength: Sensible modeling choices, including informative mean functions and attention to identifiability issues.  
- Strength: Experiments demonstrate capability on multimodal smoothing and a 4D cart-pole with state-dependent uncertainty.  
- Weakness: Ambiguity about the SMC proposal—unclear whether the locally optimal or prior proposal was used and what was used in CPF-AS variants.  
- Weakness: Computational complexity statements omit dependence on number of particles N, state dimension nx, and hyperparameter resampling; memory and per-particle factorization costs are unclear.  
- Weakness: Impact of resampling θ on cached FIC quantities (P, inducing caches) and resulting costs are not described.  
- Weakness: Inducing input selection is underspecified—no strategy for updating/handling inducing points as θ changes nor sensitivity analyses w.r.t. M and locations.  
- Weakness: Priors and sensitivity for GP amplitude/lengthscale vs process noise Q are under-discussed; more prior choices or sensitivity studies needed.  
- Weakness: Mixing/convergence claims are unsubstantiated—insufficient diagnostics (trace plots, ESS, autocorrelation) and likely too few iterations/particles for multimodal problems.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation incomplete—missing quantitative RMSE numbers, lacking strong baselines (GPDM, EM/MAP, variational GP-SSMs) and sparse-FIC scalability results on cart-pole.  
- Weakness: Scope limited by assuming known g; discussion missing on identifiability or extending collapsing to learn g as well.  
- Weakness: Notational density in Eq. (3)–(4); need a compact CPF-AS pseudocode specialized to GP-SSMs showing cached items and dimensions.  
- Quality: Technically solid and consistent derivations, but complexity, proposals, and numerical stability (ancestor weights) need clearer treatment.  
- Clarity: Well written overall, but would benefit from explicit algorithm boxes and fuller experimental/prior/proposal details for reproducibility.  
- Originality: Impactful combination of marginalizing GP dynamics with PGAS, but novelty relative to contemporaneous PMCMC-for-GP-SSM work must be clarified.  
- Significance: If validated with stronger experiments and clarifications, the approach meaningfully advances fully Bayesian GP-SSM learning and practical scalability.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled Bayesian treatment, appropriate PGAS use, practical contributions and a sparse alternative addressing multimodality.  
- Cons for acceptance: Unclear proposal/complexity scaling, incomplete experiments and diagnostics, underspecified inducing-point policy, and uncertain novelty positioning.  
- Overall recommendation: Lean accept conditional on strengthening empirical validation, clarifying algorithmic choices (proposals, complexity, inducing points), and positioning relative to related work.
