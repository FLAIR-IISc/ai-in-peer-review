- Main idea: Fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the GP transition function and uses tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) for inference.  
- Main idea: Introduces a sparse GP-SSM using the FIC prior to reduce complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2T) (practical reduction from O(T^3) with considerations).  

- Strength: Marginalizing the transition function preserves nonparametric expressivity and removes need for parametric approximations.  
- Strength: Well-motivated adaptation of PGAS with theoretical justification (invariance of CPF-AS) for correct sampling.  
- Strength: Sparse FIC construction meaningfully improves computational scalability for larger datasets.  
- Strength: Experiments (nonlinear benchmark, cart-pole) demonstrate ability to capture complex dynamics and multimodal smoothing.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation with careful mathematical derivations.  

- Weakness: Computational complexity claim is misleading—actual cost includes particle factor (O(N*T^3)), and trade-offs between N and cost need clarification.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental baselines; lacks comparisons with recent Bayesian/variational state-space methods.  
- Weakness: No sensitivity analysis for key parameters (number of particles N, inducing points M, mean function choice).  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of limitations in highly multimodal smoothing scenarios beyond the two-mode example.  
- Weakness: Sparse GP implementation lacks practical details on inducing point selection and concrete implementation choices.  

- Originality: Novel and significant insight to marginalize the GP transition while retaining true nonparametric learning of dynamics.  

- Significance: Advances fully Bayesian learning of nonlinear SSMs with practical sparsity, relevant for robotics, control, and time-series forecasting.  
- Significance: Provides uncertainty quantification and demonstrates multimodal smoothing capability in applied examples.  

- Recommendation (accept): Strong technical contribution, sound theory, relevant experiments, and practical sparse construction justify acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation (improvements): Clarify complexity (include particle count), expand experimental comparisons with recent methods, add sensitivity analyses, and detail inducing point selection.  

- Final recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address complexity clarification and experimental/sensitivity/details.  

- Pro: Novel marginalization preserves nonparametric expressivity.  
- Pro: Solid PGAS algorithm with theoretical foundation.  
- Pro: Practical sparse GP construction improves efficiency.  
- Pro: Clear experiments and strong theoretical contributions.  
- Con: Complexity analysis omits particle factor.  
- Con: Limited comparisons to recent Bayesian approaches.  
- Con: No sensitivity analysis for key parameters.  
- Con: Sparse GP practical implementation details are missing.
