- Paper proposes marginalizing the GP state-transition function and using PMCMC for fully Bayesian, nonparametric learning of nonlinear SSMs.
- Demonstrated on a synthetic benchmark and a cart-pole control problem, showing ability to learn complex dynamics and quantify uncertainty.
- Correctly positions itself relative to prior work, distinguishing from finite GP approximations and MAP methods, and connecting to PMCMC and particle filtering literature.
- Highlights relevance to existing GP filtering/smoothing work and the danger of overfitting in MAP approaches for high-dimensional states.
- Strength: principled nonparametric formulation by marginalizing the transition function, avoiding fixed functional forms.
- Strength: tailored PMCMC algorithm shows good mixing and leverages recent particle sampling advances for efficient inference.
- Strength: clear theoretical derivations linking the marginal prior to GP regression.
- Strength: practical experiments and visualizations effectively illustrate multimodal smoothing and dynamics capture; results competitive with existing methods.
- Strength: attention to implementation details and computational strategies (sparse GP FIC prior, Cholesky reuse) for efficiency.
- Strength: provides useful uncertainty quantification demonstrated in the cart-pole example, relevant for control/RL.
- Weakness: computational cost remains high, raising concerns about scalability to long time series or high-dimensional states.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning, priors, and sensitivity of results to hyperparameter choices.
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison—missing broader comparison to state-of-the-art GP-SSM methods (e.g., EP-based).
- Weakness: choice of FIC prior with 40 inducing points appears arbitrary; needs justification and exploration of alternatives.
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 is described concisely and would benefit from a more detailed, self-contained explanation.
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach that overcomes parametric GP-SSM limits, with convincing experiments and clear exposition.
- Against acceptance: concerns about computational cost, limited comparisons, and unclear hyperparameter treatment weaken the evaluation.
- Overall: strong contribution deserving acceptance (recommendation: Accept, rating 8.5/10).
- Suggested improvements: expand discussion on computational cost/scalability, broaden comparisons, detail hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity, clarify Algorithm 1 steps, and justify/explore sparse GP choices.
