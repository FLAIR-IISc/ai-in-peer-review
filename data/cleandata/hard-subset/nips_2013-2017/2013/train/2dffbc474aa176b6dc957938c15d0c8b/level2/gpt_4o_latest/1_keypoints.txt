- Novelty: Introduces marginalization of the state transition function for fully Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs, preserving nonparametric expressivity.  
- Technical soundness: Provides rigorous derivations and tailored PMCMC samplers (notably PGAS) to handle non-Markovian dependencies.  
- Empirical validation: Thorough experiments show ability to handle multimodal smoothing, complex dynamics, and outperform parametric baselines, including a real cart-and-pole application.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-organized presentation with clear implementation details and use of sparse GP techniques to reduce complexity.  
- Computational complexity: Remains computationally intensive, especially for high-dimensional states or long time horizons, raising scalability concerns.  
- Limited comparisons: Lacks broader empirical comparison with other state-of-the-art nonparametric or hybrid SSM approaches.  
- Observation model assumptions: Assumes a known observation model, limiting applicability when the observation model is unknown or partially known.  
- Sparse GP selection: Does not deeply explore or empirically evaluate strategies for selecting inducing inputs in the sparse GP approximation.  
- Argument for acceptance — novelty and rigor: Advances Bayesian inference in SSMs with a novel, technically solid approach.  
- Argument for acceptance — validation and utility: Demonstrated practical utility across synthetic and real datasets, suggesting broad applicability.  
- Argument for acceptance — interdisciplinary impact: Method has potential applications in engineering, economics, and reinforcement learning.  
- Argument against acceptance — scalability limits: High computational demands may prevent application to large-scale problems.  
- Argument against acceptance — incomplete benchmarking: Limited comparisons may impede full assessment of relative strengths.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability, broader comparisons, and inducing-point selection.
