- Proposes a fully Bayesian nonlinear nonparametric state-space model using a Gaussian process prior over transition dynamics and marginalizing the function for inference.  
- Infers the joint smoothing distribution directly with a tailored particle MCMC sampler to enable efficient inference.  
- Main claims: retains full nonparametric expressivity and allows efficient inference and learning of transition dynamics.  
- Experiments: evaluated on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart-and-pole system, demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Paper clarity: well-written with clear and concise explanation of the approach.  
- Strength: fully Bayesian treatment provides principled uncertainty quantification.  
- Strength: GP prior yields a flexible and expressive model for complex dynamics.  
- Strength: empirical results support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Weakness: potentially high computational complexity, especially for large datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art inference and learning methods for state-space models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, demonstrated effectiveness, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational scalability concerns and insufficient benchmarking against competitors.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
