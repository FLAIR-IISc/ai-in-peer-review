- Presents a fully Bayesian inference and learning framework for GP-SSMs using PMCMC methods.  
- Claims marginalizing the transition function yields fully Bayesian, nonparametric inference while preserving model expressivity.  
- Claims tailored PMCMC samplers (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling) efficiently infer the joint smoothing distribution.  
- Claims sparse GP implementations substantially reduce computational complexity.  
- Strong theoretical foundations with well-motivated marginalization techniques.  
- Carefully designed PMCMC samplers supporting the methodological claims.  
- Experiments on a nonlinear benchmark and cart-and-pole show advantages over parametric models in capturing complex dynamics.  
- Sparse GP variant effectively reduces computational cost in experiments.  
- Demonstrates solid literature knowledge with appropriate citations (Rasmussen, Deisenroth, Turner).  
- Methodological details are sufficiently complete for reproducibility, including vanilla and sparse GP implementations.  
- Would benefit from more extensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art Bayesian nonlinear SSM methods.  
- Experimental scope limited to two benchmarks; needs broader evaluation across problem types and sizes.  
- Requires more thorough analysis of computational efficiency across different problem sizes.  
- Discussion of limitations should be expanded to address scalability in high-dimensional state spaces.  
- Overall technically sound, clear, and practically significant; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
