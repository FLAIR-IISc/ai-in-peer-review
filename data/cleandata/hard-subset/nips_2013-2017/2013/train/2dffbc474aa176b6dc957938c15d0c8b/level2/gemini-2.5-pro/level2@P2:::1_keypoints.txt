- Places a GP prior on the transition and marginalizes out the entire dynamics f, yielding a fully Bayesian, nonparametric GP-SSM with non-Markovian state trajectories.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to perform inference in the resulting non-Markovian model, sampling joint posterior over states and hyperparameters.  
- Computes posterior predictive for f analytically as a mixture of GPs and offers a sparse GP variant reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Originality/significance: provides a truly nonparametric, fully Bayesian solution enabling proper uncertainty quantification over states and dynamics.  
- Technical quality: sound derivation of marginalized prior, appropriate application of PGAS, and thoughtful computational speed-up ideas (e.g., Cholesky reuse).  
- Clarity/organization: well-written and logically structured, with clear motivation and explanation of the Markov-property loss.  
- Experimental weakness: evaluations are on synthetic datasets only, lacking real-world benchmark validation.  
- Comparative weakness: comparisons are against simple parametric baselines; missing direct quantitative comparisons to other GP-SSM learning methods (e.g., EM-based).  
- Cart-pole results are qualitative; need quantitative metrics (multi-step prediction error or held-out log-likelihood).  
- Implementation details (Section 4.2) are brief and vague; Cholesky update descriptions are insufficient for easy reproduction.  
- Inducing-point selection for the sparse model is not addressed; experiments omit details on how inducing inputs were chosen or optimized.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled Bayesian approach; significant technical advance combining GP marginalization with tailored PGAS; flexible and scalable; clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental rigor and insufficient implementation detail, which may hinder reproducibility.  
- Overall verdict: technically strong and important contribution; weaknesses are mainly experimental and reproducibility-related but do not outweigh the paper’s novelty and impact.
