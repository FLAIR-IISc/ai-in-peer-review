- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function f, inducing a non-Markovian prior over state trajectories.  
- Uses particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) to sample the exact joint smoothing distribution p(x0:T | y0:T, θ).  
- Alternates sampling of state trajectories with GP hyperparameter updates and forms predictive f as a mixture of Gaussians over MCMC samples.  
- Implements computational tricks to reduce complexity from O(T^4) to O(T^3) and to O(M^2T) using a sparse FIC GP prior.  
- Differs from EM/MAP GPDM and prior GPDS work by maintaining a fully Bayesian, nonparametric treatment and integrating out f.  
- Builds on PMCMC foundations and leverages PGAS to handle non-Markovian dependencies; sparse construction follows FITC/FIC approximations.  
- Strength: conceptually clean—exact GP marginalization preserves nonparametric expressivity.  
- Strength: solid inference design—PGAS appropriately addresses path degeneracy with sound invariance guarantees.  
- Strength: practical implementation advice (rank-1 Cholesky updates, reuse of factorizations) and a sparse variant for reduced complexity.  
- Strength: flexible—can include informative mean functions and handle multimodal smoothing.  
- Strength: empirical demos (1D benchmark and 4D cart-pole) show sensible posterior smoothing and predictive behavior.  
- Weakness: limited empirical scope—only one synthetic 1D and one cart-pole example, no long-horizon rollouts or real-world datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks quantitative comparisons to strong baselines (EM/MAP GPDM, EP-based GPDS, SMC^2, PMMH) on RMSE, NLL, runtime.  
- Weakness: scaling/resource transparency missing—no wall-clock times, memory usage, or sensitivity analyses for N, L, M, T.  
- Weakness: assumption of known/simple observation model g; robustness when g is flexible or misspecified is unclear.  
- Weakness: inducing input selection for FIC not evaluated; sensitivity to number and placement of inducing points unstudied.  
- Weakness: predictive mixture over f evaluated only via limited RMSE; calibration metrics (NLL, PIT, CRPS) absent.  
- Quality: methodologically sound and implementation details credible, but empirical validation is only proof-of-concept.  
- Clarity: well written and organized, though notation is index-heavy; pseudo-code with cost annotations would help.  
- Originality: novel application of PGAS to GP-SSMs with f integrated out; mixture-of-Gaussians predictive is a neat byproduct.  
- Significance: principled Bayesian learning procedure with high potential impact, conditional on demonstrated scalability and comparative performance.  
- Suggestions: add quantitative baselines, report computational metrics (time, memory, sensitivity), and show autocorrelation/ESS of chains.  
- Suggestions: evaluate long-horizon rollouts, uncertainty calibration, robustness to misspecified g, and learning of observation parameters θy.  
- Suggestions: ablate mean-function choices, test inducing point selection strategies and sensitivity to M, and discuss numerical stability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, fully Bayesian GP-SSM with innovative PGAS use and practical efficiency tricks.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow experimental evaluation with limited baselines and insufficient scaling/robustness evidence.  
- Overall: strong methodological contribution that would benefit from broader empirical comparisons and scalability analysis; leaning toward acceptance if empirical concerns are addressed.
