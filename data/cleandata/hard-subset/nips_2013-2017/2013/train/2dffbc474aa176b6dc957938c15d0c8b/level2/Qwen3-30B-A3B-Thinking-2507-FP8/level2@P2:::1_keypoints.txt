- Proposes a fully Bayesian approach for nonlinear GP state-space models by marginalizing the transition function and using tailored PMCMC (PGAS) to infer p(x0:T | y0:T, u0:T).  
- Marginalization preserves nonparametric expressivity and avoids parametric approximations or overfitting common in prior GP-SSM work.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous, deriving the non-Gaussian state trajectory prior and detailing PGAS implementation for both vanilla and sparse GP-SSMs.  
- Introduces a sparse GP extension using the FIC prior, reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) and improving practical applicability.  
- Experiments on a multimodal nonlinear system and a cart-pole benchmark show accurate capture of complex dynamics and robustness to mean-function misspecification.  
- Sparse GP variant maintains performance while significantly reducing computational cost.  
- Comparison to parametric baselines and ground-truth models is appropriate; predictive mixture-of-Gaussians captures multimodality well.  
- Strength: fills a meaningful gap by enabling fully Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs while preserving nonparametric flexibility.  
- Strength: clear, well-justified explanations of marginalization and PGAS with solid mathematical derivations.  
- Strength: sparse GP extension and complexity analysis are practically valuable and well-explained.  
- Strength: convincing experimental results demonstrating advantages over existing approaches.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed computational-complexity comparison with alternative Bayesian SSM methods (e.g., variational or other MCMC approaches).  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons with recent state-of-the-art nonparametric nonlinear SSM methods are insufficient.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of scalability to very long time series (T > 1000) and sensitivity to particle count N in PGAS.  
- Weakness: claim of “very good mixing” is unsupported by concrete diagnostics (e.g., autocorrelation plots or ESS).  
- Recommendations: add complexity comparisons, more state-of-the-art experimental baselines, mixing diagnostics, discussion of practical limitations/scalability, and guidance on when to prefer this approach.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, experimentally convincing, and recommended for acceptance.
