This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs). The authors propose a novel method for marginalizing out the state transition function, allowing for efficient inference over the state trajectory and hyper-parameters. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of this approach through experiments on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart and pole system.

**Claims:**

1. The proposed method enables efficient Bayesian inference and learning in GP-SSMs, retaining the full nonparametric expressivity of the model.
2. The method can learn a model of the dynamics that can produce accurate one-step ahead predictions, even when the state transition function is unknown.

**Support for Claims:**

The paper provides a thorough theoretical framework for the proposed method, including a detailed description of the GP-SSM model, the marginalization of the state transition function, and the particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithm used for inference. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in learning accurate models of the dynamics and producing reliable predictions.

**Usefulness:**

The proposed method has the potential to be highly useful in a variety of applications, including robotics, control systems, and time series analysis. The ability to learn accurate models of complex dynamics without requiring a parametric representation of the state transition function is a significant advantage.

**Field Knowledge:**

The paper demonstrates a strong understanding of the relevant literature in GP-SSMs, Bayesian inference, and PMCMC methods. The authors provide a clear and concise overview of the existing approaches to GP-SSMs and highlight the limitations of these methods.

**Originality:**

The proposed method is a significant contribution to the field of GP-SSMs, as it provides a novel approach to marginalizing out the state transition function and enables efficient Bayesian inference and learning. The use of PMCMC methods to sample from the joint smoothing distribution is also a notable contribution.

**Completeness:**

The paper provides a thorough description of the proposed method, including the theoretical framework, algorithmic details, and experimental results. The authors also provide a clear discussion of the limitations of the method and potential avenues for future research.

**Limitations:**

The paper acknowledges the limitations of the method, including the computational complexity of the PMCMC algorithm and the potential for overfitting in certain scenarios.

**Constructive Criticism:**

One potential area for improvement is the use of more efficient sampling methods, such as those based on parallel tempering or nested sampling. Additionally, the authors could explore the application of the proposed method to more complex systems, such as those with multiple inputs and outputs.

**Rating:**

I would rate this paper as a strong accept, given its significant contributions to the field of GP-SSMs, its thorough theoretical framework, and its well-designed experiments. The paper is well-written and provides a clear and concise overview of the proposed method and its applications.
