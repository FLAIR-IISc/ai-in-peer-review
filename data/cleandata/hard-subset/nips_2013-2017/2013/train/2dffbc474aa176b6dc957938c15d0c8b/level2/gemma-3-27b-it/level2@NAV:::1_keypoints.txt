- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function and uses Particle MCMC to sample the joint smoothing distribution.  
- Marginalization preserves full nonparametric expressivity compared to parametric approximations.  
- Advances prior GP-SSM work by explicitly learning the GP transition model rather than assuming it.  
- Novel handling of dependencies from marginalization via recent PMCMC samplers.  
- Includes a sparse GP (FIC) extension to address computational complexity.  
- Strong theoretical treatment and clear algorithmic exposition.  
- Efficient implementation details provided (e.g., reuse of Cholesky factorizations).  
- Empirical validation on a synthetic nonlinear system and a cart-pole benchmark shows effectiveness, multimodal handling, and uncertainty quantification.  
- Lacks detailed comparison to other nonparametric SSM approaches beyond cited references.  
- Experiments are limited in scope and would benefit from more complex datasets and broader hyperparameter studies.  
- Insufficient discussion of FIC prior trade-offs and sensitivity to inducing point choices.  
- Code availability is not mentioned, which harms reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and address reproducibility and FIC sensitivity.
