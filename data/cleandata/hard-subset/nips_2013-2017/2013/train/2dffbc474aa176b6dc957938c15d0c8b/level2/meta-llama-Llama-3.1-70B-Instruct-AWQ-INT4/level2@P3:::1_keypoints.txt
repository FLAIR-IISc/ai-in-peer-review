- Summary: Fully Bayesian inference for nonlinear nonparametric SSMs with GP priors, marginalizing the transition function and using PMCMC (with sparse GPs) for joint smoothing.

- Strength: Novel method that marginalizes the state transition, enabling efficient joint smoothing computation.

- Strength: Preserves full nonparametric expressivity of the GP prior for flexible dynamical modeling.

- Strength: Employs PMCMC samplers and sparse GPs to reduce computational burden.

- Strength: Promising empirical results on two benchmark systems demonstrating learning of nonlinear dynamics.

- Weakness: Potentially high computational cost for large-scale systems despite complexity reductions.

- Weakness: Limited theoretical analysis—convergence properties and guarantees are not fully established.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of hyperparameter tuning, which may be crucial in practice.

- Originality: Novel combination of marginalization, PMCMC, and sparse GPs for SSM inference.

- Significance: Potentially impactful for modeling and learning nonlinear dynamical systems in many application domains.

- Quality: Well-written paper with a good balance of theoretical and empirical content.

- Clarity: Clear organization, consistent notation, and informative figures/tables.

- For acceptance: Introduces a novel, expressive approach with encouraging empirical validation.

- Against acceptance: Needs stronger theoretical guarantees, scalability evidence, and hyperparameter guidance.

- Recommendation: Accept, but request additional theoretical analysis and hyperparameter tuning discussion.
