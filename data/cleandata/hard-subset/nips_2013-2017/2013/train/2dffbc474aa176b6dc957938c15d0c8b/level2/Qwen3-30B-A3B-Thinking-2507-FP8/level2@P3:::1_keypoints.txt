- Main contribution: fully Bayesian GP-SSM inference by marginalizing the transition function f and directly inferring p(x0:T | y0:T) using a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm.  
- Preserves nonparametric expressivity of the GP prior while avoiding parametric approximations used in prior work.  
- Introduces a sparse GP variant using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to reduce complexity to O(M^2 T) with M ≪ T.  
- Strong theoretical grounding: leverages recent PMCMC advances to handle non-Markovian dependencies and provides convergence guarantees for the PGAS implementation.  
- Careful computational analysis: uses Cholesky updates and sparse approximations to achieve practical runtime and memory efficiency.  
- Experiments: nonlinear benchmark shows accurate recovery of multimodal smoothing distributions despite misspecified mean; sparse variant matches full GP predictive performance.  
- Cart-pole experiment: demonstrates meaningful uncertainty quantification across state space, important for control and RL applications.  
- Originality: novel application of marginalization + PGAS to GP-SSMs and extension of sparse GP (FIC) methods to the non-Markovian setting, distinct from EM/MAP approaches.  
- Significance: enables principled Bayesian uncertainty quantification for dynamics learning and makes nonparametric SSMs computationally viable.  
- Weakness 1: limited experimental scope (only two synthetic systems); needs a third, larger or real-world benchmark to demonstrate generalizability and scalability.  
- Weakness 2: lacks comparison to recent non-Bayesian GP-SSM methods (e.g., expectation propagation–based filtering), which would better situate the contribution.  
- Clarity: generally excellent exposition and algorithms; minor fixes suggested—explicitly state how the green mean differs from true dynamics in Figure 2 and note inducing point selection’s runtime impact.  
- Overall: strong, well-motivated technical contribution that advances Bayesian inference for nonparametric dynamical systems and fits NIPS’s focus on principled probabilistic modeling.
