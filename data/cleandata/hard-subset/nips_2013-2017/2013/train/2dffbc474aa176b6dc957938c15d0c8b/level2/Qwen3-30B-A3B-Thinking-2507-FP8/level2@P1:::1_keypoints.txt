- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function and infers the joint smoothing distribution using PMCMC, preserving nonparametric expressivity.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to handle non-Markovian dependencies introduced by marginalization; technically sound and well-motivated.  
- Employs a sparse GP construction to reduce computational complexity and make the approach feasible for larger problems.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with thorough mathematical development, notably the careful derivation of the marginal prior (Eq. 4).  
- Relates effectively to prior work (e.g., Deisenroth, Turner) and clearly articulates novel contributions versus parametric/finite-approximation approaches.  
- Provides rigorous derivations, detailed implementation for vanilla and sparse GP models, and a thorough computational complexity analysis.  
- Experiments on a synthetic nonlinear benchmark and cart-and-pole demonstrate effectiveness, especially in capturing multimodal smoothing distributions.  
- Empirical results show the GP-SSM outperforms parametric models in predictive accuracy (RMSE comparisons, Table 1).  
- Originality is significant: fully Bayesian marginalization of dynamics represents a fundamental shift from conventional SSM inference.  
- High significance: enables learning complex nonlinear dynamics without restrictive parametric assumptions, with implications for time series, dynamical systems, and control.  
- Minor suggestion: include a more detailed comparison with closely related work (e.g., Wang et al. [8]) on assumptions and computational complexity.  
- Minor suggestion: expand the sparse GP discussion to clarify how complexity reduces to O(M²T) instead of O(M²T²).  
- Minor suggestion: include a broader comparison with recent state-of-the-art nonparametric SSM inference methods to strengthen claims.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, well-demonstrated, and recommended for acceptance.
