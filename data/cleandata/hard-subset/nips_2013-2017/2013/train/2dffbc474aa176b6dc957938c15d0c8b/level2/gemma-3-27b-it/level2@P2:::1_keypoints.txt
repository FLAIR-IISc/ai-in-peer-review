- Proposes a fully Bayesian method for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using GPs for transitions and PMCMC for inference, with marginalization of the transition function.  
- Marginalizing the GP transition dynamics is novel and preserves full nonparametric expressivity.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM literature while addressing limitations of parametric GP representations.  
- Uses recent PMCMC advances to tackle computational and inference challenges.  
- Technical derivations (marginalization, SMC/PMCMC) are rigorous and well-founded.  
- The GP+PMCMC combination is a valuable and novel contribution to the field.  
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and equations are clearly defined.  
- Experiments show strong empirical performance versus parametric baselines and sparse GP variants, including a cart-pole control example.  
- Section 4 provides useful implementation details and complexity analysis, improving practicality.  
- Presentation of Equation 3 is dense and notation is overloaded; an intuitive explanation is needed.  
- Paper lacks analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (GP kernel, PMCMC settings, mean function choice).  
- Computational cost remains a concern, especially for high-dimensional states or long time series; actual runtimes are not reported.  
- Limited comparison to other recent nonparametric SSM methods weakens empirical positioning.  
- Experiments use simulated data only; real-world dataset validation is missing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key challenge in Bayesian SSMs, is technically sound, empirically effective, and practically informed.  
- Arguments against acceptance: remaining computational concerns, missing hyperparameter sensitivity and state-of-the-art comparisons, and lack of real-world validation.  
- Overall: strong, novel paper recommended for acceptance subject to addressing hyperparameter sensitivity, computational/runtime analysis, additional comparisons, and clearer exposition of Equation 3.
