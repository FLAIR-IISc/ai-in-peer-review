**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models (SSMs) using Gaussian process (GP) priors. The authors propose a method to marginalize out the state transition function, allowing for efficient inference over the joint smoothing distribution using particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) samplers. The approach preserves the full nonparametric expressivity of the model and can make use of sparse GPs to reduce computational complexity.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to inference in SSMs**: The paper proposes a new method for inference in SSMs that marginalizes out the state transition function, enabling efficient computation of the joint smoothing distribution.
2. **Retains nonparametric expressivity**: The approach preserves the full nonparametric expressivity of the GP prior, allowing for flexible modeling of complex dynamical systems.
3. **Efficient computation**: The use of PMCMC samplers and sparse GPs enables efficient computation of the joint smoothing distribution and state transition predictive distribution.
4. **Empirical results**: The paper presents promising empirical results on two benchmark systems, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach in learning nonlinear dynamics.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational complexity**: Although the paper proposes methods to reduce computational complexity, the approach may still be computationally expensive for large-scale systems.
2. **Limited theoretical analysis**: The paper provides limited theoretical analysis of the proposed method, and further research is needed to establish its convergence properties and theoretical guarantees.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on hyperparameter tuning, which may be crucial for the performance of the approach in practice.
