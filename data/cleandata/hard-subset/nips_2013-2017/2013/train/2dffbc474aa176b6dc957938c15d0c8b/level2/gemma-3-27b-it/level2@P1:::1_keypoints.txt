- Proposes a fully Bayesian nonparametric state-space model using Gaussian processes for transitions and PMCMC for inference, marginalizing the transition function (novel idea).
- Marginalization of the GP transition enables a truly nonparametric approach beyond prior parametric approximations.
- Methodology appears mathematically sound with clear derivation and justified use of the conditional particle filter with ancestor sampling (CPF-AS).
- Incorporates sparse GPs to improve computational efficiency in a practical way.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized; notation and flow are clear.
- Figure 1b is included but not referenced in the main text.
- Demonstrated significance via tasks like the cart-pole example and robustness to misspecified mean functions.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and uses somewhat simplistic benchmarks; needs more complex/realistic datasets and broader method comparisons.
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational cost scaling with state dimension and dataset size.
- Hyperparameter selection and sensitivity are under-discussed; log-normal priors need more justification and specified ranges.
- Implementation section is dense and could use pseudocode or clearer mapping from theory to code for reproducibility.
- Missing some more recent PMCMC citations and discussion of specific implementations beyond the cited [3].
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, and technically sound work — recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments, clarify hyperparameter tuning and implementation, and reference all figures.
