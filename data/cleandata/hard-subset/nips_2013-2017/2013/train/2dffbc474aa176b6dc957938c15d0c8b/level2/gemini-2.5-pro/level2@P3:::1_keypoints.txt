- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing a Gaussian Process prior over the unknown state transition function.  
- Analytically marginalizes the infinite-dimensional GP transition function, resulting in a non-Markovian prior over state trajectories.  
- Performs inference using Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample from the full joint smoothing distribution in the non-Markovian model.  
- Introduces a sparse GP variant using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) approximation to reduce complexity from cubic to linear in time series length.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior work by providing fully Bayesian learning rather than pre-learned models, MAP estimates, or parameterized inducing-point approximations.  
- Technical derivations are sound and the application of PGAS to this problem is appropriate and well-justified.  
- Method is complete, covering model specification, inference for states and hyperparameters, and prediction of dynamics.  
- Experimental results (on low-dimensional systems) support claims, notably the ability to capture multimodal smoothing distributions beyond Gaussian approximations.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; core technical contributions are explained with sufficient detail for experts.  
- The combination of marginalizing the GP dynamics and using PMCMC is an original and elegant contribution that avoids common posterior approximations.  
- Significant advance for system identification of nonlinear dynamical systems, offering principled uncertainty quantification useful for time-series, control, and robotics.  
- Strength: principled Bayesian framework avoids underestimating uncertainty and captures multimodality.  
- Strength: nonparametric flexibility retains full GP expressivity for complex dynamics.  
- Strength: novel PGAS-based inference makes the non-Markovian approach computationally feasible.  
- Strength: sparse GP extension provides a clear path toward scalability to longer time series.  
- Suggestion: add discussion on scalability with respect to state dimension nx and possible mitigation strategies.  
- Suggestion: clarify the inducing-point selection strategy for the sparse GP (fixed, optimized, or integrated out).  
- Suggestion: report practical runtimes and discuss MCMC convergence/mixing for hyperparameters as these can be bottlenecks.  
- Suggestion: include quantitative multi-step prediction metrics (e.g., long-term RMSE) for tasks like the cart-pole to assess long-term dynamics learning.
