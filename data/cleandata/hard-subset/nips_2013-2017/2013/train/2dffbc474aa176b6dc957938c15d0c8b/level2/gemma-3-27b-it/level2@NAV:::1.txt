## Review of “Bayesian Inference and Learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models with Particle MCMC”

This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to learning and inference in nonlinear, nonparametric state-space models (SSMs) using a Gaussian process (GP) prior over the state transition dynamics. The key contribution lies in marginalizing over the transition dynamics function and employing Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) to efficiently sample the joint smoothing distribution. This allows for retaining the full nonparametric expressivity of the model, a significant advantage over existing approaches that often rely on parametric approximations. 

The paper clearly builds upon prior work in GP-SSMs (Deisenroth et al., Turner et al., Wang et al.) by addressing the critical step of *learning* the GP model itself, rather than assuming a pre-defined transition dynamics. The proposed method offers a novel way to handle the dependencies introduced by marginalizing out the transition function, utilizing recent advances in PMCMC samplers. The authors effectively demonstrate the method’s ability to infer the smoothing distribution and then learn the transition function, allowing for accurate state estimation and system identification. The inclusion of a sparse GP extension (FIC prior) further addresses computational complexity concerns.

A significant strength is the thorough theoretical treatment and clear explanation of the inference algorithms. The authors detail the efficient implementation techniques, including the reuse of computations via Cholesky factorization. The experimental validation on both a synthetic nonlinear system and a cart-pole environment demonstrates the method’s effectiveness. Notably, the experiments highlight the method’s ability to handle multimodal distributions and provide uncertainty estimates, as seen in the cart-pole example. 

However, the paper lacks a detailed comparison against alternative nonparametric SSM approaches beyond the listed references. The experiments are somewhat limited in scope and could benefit from testing on more complex datasets and a wider range of hyperparameter settings. While the FIC prior is mentioned, a more detailed discussion regarding its performance trade-offs (e.g., the sensitivity to the choice of inducing points) would be valuable.  The code availability is not mentioned, which hinders reproducibility.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to fully Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs by marginalizing over the transition dynamics.
*   Solid theoretical foundation and clear algorithmic description.
*   Demonstrated effectiveness in both synthetic and benchmark control problems.
*   Addresses computational challenges with a sparse GP extension.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited comparison to other nonparametric SSM approaches.
*   Relatively limited scope of experiments.
*   Lack of discussion on FIC prior sensitivity.
*   Missing code availability.

**Overall:** Despite these minor shortcomings, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed method addresses a significant challenge in SSM learning and offers a promising approach for modeling complex dynamical systems. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the experimental scope and reproducibility concerns.
