- Paper proposes fully Bayesian learning in GP state-space models by marginalizing the GP transition, yielding a non-Markovian prior and enabling PMCMC inference.
- Core algorithm: tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) that samples exact smoothing p(x0:T | θ, y0:T) while keeping the GP collapsed.
- Provides analytic mixture-of-Gaussians predictive distribution for f(x*) and an optional sparse FIC variant to reduce complexity to O(M^2 T).
- Implementation includes reuse of Cholesky factorizations and rank-1 updates/downdates to achieve O(T^3) (full GP) or O(M^2 T) (FIC).
- Differs from prior work by collapsing f and doing fully Bayesian inference instead of EM/MAP or post-hoc approximations used in earlier GP-SSM methods.
- Conceptual advance: preserves nonparametric flexibility while performing full Bayesian learning of dynamics.
- Inference is sound: PGAS kernels leave the exact smoothing distribution invariant; ancestor sampling is justified for non-Markovian priors.
- Derivations are solid: explicit collapsed trajectory prior and PGAS weights for non-Markovian models are well developed.
- Practical contributions: clear computational strategies and a principled sparse alternative with complexity analysis.
- Expressive predictions: the mixture-of-Gaussians captures multimodality induced by ambiguous observations.
- Experiments show multimodal smoothing capability and use of informative GP mean functions to correct model mismatch.
- Empirical evaluation is limited: lacks comparisons to EM-based GP-SSM, GPDM, MAP/variational baselines, and PMMH or PG without ancestor sampling.
- Scalability analysis is insufficient: missing runtime, memory, ESS vs T, state dimension, N, and mixture size; no ablation for ancestor sampling.
- Identifiability and sensitivity issues (e.g., Q vs kernel amplitude) are under-discussed; priors/reparameterizations guidance is missing.
- Sparse GP details are thin: inducing input selection and FIC vs full GP impact on posterior accuracy are not empirically evaluated.
- Exposition can be dense: the “Gaussian-looking but not Gaussian” notation and CPF-AS mechanics need clearer intuition and compact pseudocode.
- Suggested essential improvements: add head-to-head baselines, scaling and ESS results, and discussion/experiments on identifiability and priors.
- Optional suggestions: evaluate FIC vs full GP, show non-Gaussian likelihood example, provide numerical stability notes, and release code or CPF-AS pseudocode.
- Pros: fully Bayesian collapsed dynamics, correct PGAS for non-Markovian priors, mixture predictions, complexity-aware implementation, ability to fix mean mismatch.
- Cons: limited empirical comparisons and scalability analysis, unexamined sparse GP choices, identifiability/prior sensitivity not fully addressed, exposition dense in parts.
- Recommendation: Accept (strong) based on methodological novelty and correctness, but empirical section should be strengthened.
- Reviewer confidence: high in methodological assessment, moderate regarding empirical performance due to limited comparisons.
