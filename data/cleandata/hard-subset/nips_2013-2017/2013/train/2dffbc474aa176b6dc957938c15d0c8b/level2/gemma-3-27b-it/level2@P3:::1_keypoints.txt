- Presents a fully Bayesian approach for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using GPs to model transition dynamics.
- Core contribution is a PMCMC method that marginalizes the transition function to preserve full nonparametric expressivity.
- Builds on prior GP-SSM literature and targets the often-missing component of learning the GP transition model.
- Strength: principled Bayesian treatment with elegant marginalization and use of a modern PMCMC sampler.
- Theoretical justification is clearly presented with sound mathematical derivations.
- Demonstrates feasibility via two experiments (nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole), showing robustness to an inaccurate parametric prior.
- Uses sparse GPs (FIC) to address computational complexity and provides implementation details and complexity discussion.
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art methods; RMSE results do not establish clear advantage.
- PMCMC sampler description is under-detailed—CPF-AS and Algorithm 1 need a clearer walkthrough and rationale.
- Hyperparameter learning discussion is shallow; practical issues like slice sampling step size and convergence diagnostics are omitted.
- Notation can be dense and occasionally hard to follow; needs more intermediate definitions and clearer equation organization.
- Missing discussion of limitations, notably scalability to very high-dimensional states and sensitivity to GP kernel choice.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel marginalization+PMCMC approach, theoretical soundness, demonstrated feasibility, addresses key GP-SSM challenge.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited benchmarking, insufficient algorithmic detail, weak hyperparameter treatment, limited discussion of limitations.
- Essential revisions requested: more extensive benchmarking and clearer explanation of the PMCMC algorithm; other suggestions are refinements.
