- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the GP transition f, yielding a non-Markovian prior over x0:T and using PGAS for exact smoothing.
- Given trajectory samples, the posterior over f is closed-form (GP regression), producing predictive mixtures of Gaussians.
- Introduces a sparse FIC variant reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) with inducing inputs.
- Implements efficient Cholesky rank-one updates/downdates and reuses computations for per-particle efficiency.
- Positions work relative to GPDM, GP filtering/smoothing, and EM/MAP SSM identification; key novelty is collapsing f with PGAS for non-Markovian smoothing.
- Methodology is technically sound; derivations and handling of the non-Markovian structure appear correct.
- Collapsing f appropriately reduces posterior dependencies and avoids high-dimensional sampling burdens.
- Empirically shows multimodal smoothing in a 1D benchmark, competitive transition prediction, and uncertainty-aware 4D cart-pole results.
- Experiments are limited: missing ablations (N, chain length, kernels/hyperpriors, inducing set size/placement) and sparse comparisons to strong baselines.
- Runtime, scaling with T/N/state dimension, and mixing diagnostics (ESS/autocorrelation) are not reported.
- Paper is mostly clear with precise (though heavy) notation; equations (3)–(4) and ancestor weight discussion are helpful.
- The proposal notation p(xt | θ, x0:t−1, y0:t) is ambiguous; clarify default (one-step prior predictive) vs likelihood-informed proposals.
- More explicit pseudocode and complexity accounting for CPF-AS with the collapsed prior would improve reproducibility.
- Originality is high: collapsing the GP transition and using PGAS for exact marginalization is a notable advance.
- Significance is high: fills an important gap for fully Bayesian GP-SSMs and has practical implications for system ID and RL/control.
- Strengths: principled fully Bayesian nonparametric treatment, PGAS handling of non-Markovian smoothing, coherent sparse prior, closed-form f posterior, and ability to capture multimodal posteriors and uncertainty.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: add comparisons to GPDM/EM/MAP and parametric PMCMC, report wall-clock times and scaling, perform sensitivity studies, and analyze inducing input selection strategies.
- Clarify SMC proposal choices and consider evaluating likelihood-informed proposals for variance/mixing benefits.
- Discuss practical limitations: O(T^3) complexity/memory for full GP, practical T and state-dimension limits, and assumptions about Q (e.g., diagonal/block structure).
- Provide more derivation/details for computing p(x̃t:T | xi0:t−1) in ancestor sampling, include CPF-AS pseudocode, specify priors/hyperparameters, and release code if possible.
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept — strong, novel methodological contribution but needs stronger empirical validation to be fully convincing.
