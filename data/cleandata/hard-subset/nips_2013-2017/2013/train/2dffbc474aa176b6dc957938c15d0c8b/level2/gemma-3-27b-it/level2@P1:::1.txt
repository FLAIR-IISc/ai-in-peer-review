This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models, leveraging Gaussian processes (GPs) for the state transition dynamics and employing Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) for efficient inference. The core idea of marginalizing over the transition dynamics function is a significant contribution, enabling a truly nonparametric approach, unlike previous works relying on parametric approximations. The paper is well-motivated, clearly articulating the limitations of existing methods and positioning their work as a principled solution. It builds upon a solid foundation of related work in GP-SSMs, referencing key papers like Rasmussen & Williams (2006), Deisenroth et al. (2013, 2015), and Turner et al. (2013).

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The methodology appears mathematically sound, with a clear derivation of the marginalization process and a thoughtful application of PMCMC. The explanation of the conditional particle filter with ancestor sampling (CPF-AS) is detailed and justifiable. The use of sparse GPs to improve computational efficiency is a practical and well-integrated addition.
* **Originality:**  Marginalizing out the state transition function in a GP-SSM is a novel approach, moving beyond the typical parametric approximations.  The combination of PMCMC with this marginalization strategy is a strong contribution.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is mostly clear, and the flow of ideas is logical. The inclusion of Figure 1b (though not explicitly referenced in the text, it should be) helps visualize the generative model. The explanation of the implementation details and the computational complexity analysis is valuable.
* **Significance:** The ability to perform fully Bayesian inference in a nonparametric setting addresses a crucial limitation in existing GP-SSM approaches. This has potential for broader applicability in domains requiring flexible modeling of complex dynamical systems, as demonstrated by the cart and pole example. The results demonstrating the ability to learn even with a misspecified mean function are particularly encouraging.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Experimental Evaluation:** While the experiments demonstrate the method's capability, the evaluation could be more comprehensive. The benchmark system, while illustrative, feels somewhat simplistic. More complex and realistic datasets would strengthen the claims of practical applicability.  The comparison with other methods (beyond those mentioned in the text) would also be beneficial.  More detailed analysis of the computational cost, particularly scaling with state dimension and dataset size, would be useful.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper doesn't provide much detail on hyperparameter selection. It would be helpful to discuss the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameter choices and the methods used for tuning them.  Log-normal priors are mentioned, but more details regarding their justification and range would be appreciated.
* **Implementation Details:** While the section on implementation details is good, it's dense and could be streamlined. The connection between the theoretical descriptions and the actual implementation steps isn't always immediately clear. Providing some pseudocode snippets might improve clarity.
* **Missing References:** While the related work is extensive, a more recent citation for PMCMC algorithms might be beneficial. The paper refers to [3] for PMCMC, which is a good starting point, but doesn’t mention more recent advances or specific implementations used.
* **Figure References:** As noted above, Figure 1b is introduced but never referenced in the main body of the text.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem in Bayesian inference.
* The method offers a significant advantage over existing techniques by enabling a truly nonparametric representation of the state transition dynamics.
* The experimental results, while limited, demonstrate the feasibility and potential of the proposed approach.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The experimental evaluation is not sufficiently comprehensive to fully validate the claims of practical applicability.
* The lack of detailed discussion on hyperparameter tuning and implementation details limits reproducibility.
* The paper lacks a thorough comparison with other relevant methods.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field of GP-SSMs. The originality and technical soundness of the proposed approach are commendable. While the experimental evaluation could be more extensive and the implementation details more transparent, the potential impact of this work justifies acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above, specifically focusing on expanding the experimental evaluation, providing more details on hyperparameter tuning, and clarifying the implementation aspects. The authors should also ensure all figures are referenced within the text.
