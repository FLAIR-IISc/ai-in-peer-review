Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes a fully Bayesian framework for inference and learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs). The key claims are: (1) marginalizing the transition function f to preserve nonparametric expressivity while sampling directly from the joint smoothing distribution via Particle MCMC, specifically particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS); (2) efficient implementations with O(T^3) complexity using Cholesky updates and further reduction to O(M^2T) using an FIC sparse GP prior; (3) analytically recovering the predictive distribution over f after smoothing. This approach contrasts with prior GP-SSM work that uses EM/MAP or finite parametrizations (e.g., GPDM by Wang et al., Turner et al.’s EM), and complements Deisenroth et al.’s filtering/smoothing methods by addressing the learning step. It builds on Andrieu et al.’s PMCMC and Lindsten et al.’s PGAS.

Quality: The methodology is technically sound. The collapsed prior over trajectories is derived carefully, the CPF-AS kernel and ancestor sampling weights are appropriate for the non-Markovian setting, and implementation details (rank-one updates, FIC algebra) are nontrivial and useful. Hyperparameter sampling via slice sampling is reasonable. Empirical support shows good mixing and predictive accuracy on a nonlinear benchmark and a cart-pole example, with calibrated uncertainty. However, the experimental section is limited: few datasets, no timing/ESS statistics, and modest quantitative comparisons to alternative GP-SSM learners or particle smoothers.

Clarity: Generally well-written and organized, with clear algorithmic description. Some implementation choices (e.g., selection of inducing inputs, memory costs, sensitivity to mean function) could be explained more, and explicit runtime and convergence diagnostics would aid reproducibility.

Originality: Collapsing the GP dynamics within a PGAS framework for joint smoothing and learning, plus the integration of FIC for scalability, is novel and differentiates this work from prior EM/MAP and fixed-dynamics approaches.

Significance: The ability to sample smoothing trajectories without committing to a fixed dynamics model is compelling for system identification and uncertainty-aware prediction, with relevance to control and RL.

Limitations and suggestions: Scaling to long sequences and higher-dimensional states remains challenging; provide wall-clock times, ESS, and sensitivity to N, M, and T. Include comparisons against EM/MAP GPDM, Deisenroth-style learned-GP filters/smoothers, and standard particle smoothers with parametric dynamics. Discuss identifiability with flexible g and robustness to mean-function misspecification. Evaluate uncertainty calibration (coverage/PIT) and provide code.

Pros for acceptance:
- Principled, novel, and well-engineered Bayesian treatment of GP-SSMs
- Strong theoretical grounding with practical implementation insights
- Promising results and clear applicability

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited empirical breadth and ablations
- Incomplete scalability evidence and runtime analysis
- Few direct head-to-head baselines with existing GP-SSM learners
