- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that places a GP prior on the transition function and marginalizes it out to obtain a non-Markovian state trajectory.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample from the joint posterior over state trajectories and hyperparameters, reconstructing the transition posterior as a mixture of GPs.  
- Tackles computational challenges with an O(T^3) implementation and a sparse FIC variant reducing complexity to O(M^2 * T).  
- Distinguishes itself from prior work by providing a complete sampling-based Bayesian treatment rather than variational/EM or parametric approximations.  
- Strength — Originality & Significance: Marginalizing the GP and applying PGAS is novel, principled, and handles multimodal posteriors well.  
- Strength — Technical Quality: Derivations are correct, PGAS application is appropriate, and complexity considerations and sparse extensions are well addressed.  
- Strength — Clarity: Manuscript is well-written, organized, and clearly positions itself relative to prior work.  
- Strength — Experiments: Demonstrations (bimodal nonlinear benchmark, cart-pole) effectively show posterior uncertainty capture and practical applicability.  
- Weakness — Computational Cost & Scalability: PMCMC remains expensive; paper lacks detailed runtimes, particle counts (N), and MCMC iterations (L) needed for good mixing.  
- Weakness — Comparisons: Experimental baselines omit contemporary GP-SSM methods (e.g., variational inference, EM), limiting comparative insight on accuracy and cost.  
- Weakness — Inducing Point Selection: Sparse GP-SSM does not address how to choose inducing inputs in dynamical settings; discussion or heuristics are missing.  
- Summary — For acceptance: Novel, technically sound, principled Bayesian inference for GP-SSMs with strong presentation and convincing experiments.  
- Summary — Against acceptance: Practical scalability concerns and limited experimental comparisons temper applicability claims.  
- Conclusion: High-quality, influential contribution whose strengths outweigh weaknesses, likely to inspire future work.
