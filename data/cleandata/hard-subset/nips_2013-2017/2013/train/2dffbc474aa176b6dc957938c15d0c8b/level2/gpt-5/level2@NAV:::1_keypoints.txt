- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function f and samples joint smoothing trajectories via Particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS).  
- Implements efficient inference with O(T^3) Cholesky-updates and O(M^2T) complexity using an FIC sparse GP prior.  
- Recovers the predictive distribution over f analytically after smoothing.  
- Contrasts with prior GP-SSM work that used EM/MAP or finite parametrizations and complements filtering/smoothing approaches by addressing learning.  
- Builds on established PMCMC/PGAS methodology (Andrieu et al., Lindsten et al.).  
- Methodology is technically sound with a careful collapsed prior derivation and appropriate CPF-AS/ancestor sampling for the non-Markovian setting.  
- Nontrivial, useful implementation contributions (rank-one Cholesky updates, FIC algebra) improve practical feasibility.  
- Hyperparameter sampling via slice sampling is reasonable.  
- Empirical results show good mixing, calibrated uncertainty, and competitive predictive accuracy on a nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole example.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: few datasets, no wall-clock timing or ESS diagnostics, and modest quantitative baselines.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized with clear algorithmic descriptions.  
- Some implementation choices need more explanation (inducing input selection, memory costs, sensitivity to mean function).  
- Runtime and convergence diagnostics (timings, ESS) are missing and would aid reproducibility.  
- The collapsed GP dynamics within a PGAS framework and the integration of FIC for scalability are novel contributions.  
- The approach is significant for system identification, uncertainty-aware prediction, and applications in control and RL.  
- Scalability to long sequences and higher-dimensional states remains a concern; sensitivity to N, M, and T should be reported.  
- Recommend comparisons to EM/MAP GPDM, learned-GP filters/smoothers (Deisenroth-style), and standard particle smoothers with parametric dynamics.  
- Suggest discussing identifiability with flexible observation dynamics and robustness to mean-function misspecification.  
- Recommend evaluating uncertainty calibration (coverage/PIT) and releasing code.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled, novel Bayesian treatment; strong theoretical grounding; practical implementation insights and promising results.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical breadth and ablations; insufficient scalability/runtime analysis; few direct head-to-head baselines.
