- Central contribution: fully Bayesian GP-SSM that analytically marginalizes the GP transition, yielding a non-Markovian latent trajectory and enabling inference without parametric GP approximations.  
- Inference method: employs Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample the joint smoothing distribution under the induced non-Markovian structure.  
- Originality/significance: novel and elegant combination of GP marginalization and advanced PMCMC, providing a principled nonparametric alternative to MAP/variational GP-SSM methods.  
- Technical quality: sound derivations for the marginal prior over trajectories and appropriate use of PGAS; includes a sparse GP variant to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Clarity: well-written and well-organized exposition of model, challenges, and PMCMC solution.  
- Experimental limitation 1: lacks direct quantitative comparisons to state-of-the-art GP-SSM learning methods (e.g., variational approaches) on the nonlinear benchmark.  
- Experimental limitation 2: cart-pole results should include multi-step-ahead predictions or long-term simulations to demonstrate utility beyond one-step forecasts.  
- Scalability concern: practical limits for long time series remain unclear despite sparse GP; needs discussion on sequence length constraints and inducing point selection.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, novel contribution with minor experimental breadth issues — recommend acceptance.
