The authors apply particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (particle MCMC) to inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs), focusing on the recent particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) algorithm of Lindsten et al. The paper is clearly written and presents an interesting, original application of particle MCMC. It also develops useful model-specific methodology, namely a sparse GP-SSM.

A major shortcoming is the lack of comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, in particular particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (as in Whiteley et al.). These baselines appear straightforward to implement in the proposed setting, and it would be very informative to see how they compare to the authors’ approach; adding them should not require substantial additional effort.

I would also like to see plots that characterize algorithmic performance—e.g., autocorrelation functions (ACFs) or effective sample sizes (ESS)—as functions of the number of particles N and the time horizon T. As presented, the empirical results are not very informative about scaling. Should N scale linearly with T, or can it grow sublinearly?

For these models, I believe PMMH typically requires the number of particles to increase quadratically with T (as observed by Whiteley et al.), whereas both particle Gibbs variants require N to grow sublinearly with T.

Overall, this is a well-written application of particle MCMC to GP-SSMs. The paper would be significantly stronger with empirical comparisons to PMMH and to particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
