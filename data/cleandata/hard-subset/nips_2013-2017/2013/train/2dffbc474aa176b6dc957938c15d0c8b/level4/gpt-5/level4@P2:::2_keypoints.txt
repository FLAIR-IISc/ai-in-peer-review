- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing a GP prior on the transition f, marginalizing f, and performing inference with particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS).
- Marginalizing f yields a non-Markovian latent process but allows closed-form predictive distributions as mixtures of Gaussians across MCMC samples.
- Introduces a sparse FIC-based GP-SSM variant reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).
- Builds on PMCMC and PGAS literature, adapting PGAS to the collapsed (non-Markovian) setting with implementation details.
- Demonstrates method on a multimodal smoothing benchmark and a 4D cart-pole system, showing sensible uncertainty quantification and predictive accuracy.
- Originality: elegant integration of PGAS with collapsed GP-SSMs, avoiding finite parameterizations.
- Methodological contributions: derivations for collapsed trajectory prior, efficient CPF-AS implementation with rank-one updates, and a clear sparse construction.
- Clarity: paper is well written with clear exposition of model and inference.
- Practical relevance: handles multimodal smoothing and calibrated predictive uncertainty; sparse variant increases applicability.
- Major missing baselines: no empirical comparisons to PMMH or particle Gibbs with backward sampling (PG-BS).
- Insufficient mixing/scaling diagnostics: need ACF, ESS/sec, inefficiency factors, and how mixing scales with particle count N and sequence length T.
- Suggest empirical study of how N must scale with T (e.g., PMMH vs PG variants) and sensitivity to ancestor/backward sampling and proposal choices.
- Experimental scope is narrow: add more datasets, higher-dimensional systems, ablations (vanilla GP vs FIC, effect of M, mean function choices), and runtime/memory profiles.
- Reproducibility needs: priors, initialization, MCMC iterations, burn-in, thinning, convergence diagnostics, and slice-sampling details.
- Modeling guidance lacking: advice on GP mean function selection and inducing input selection strategies.
- Specific requested additions: matched-budget comparisons to PMMH and PG-BS, ACF/ESS vs N and T, wall-clock/memory for vanilla and FIC variants, and quantitative metrics (RMSE, NLL) with error bars across seeds.
- Pros: fully Bayesian collapsed GP-SSM inference, clear derivations and efficient implementation, handles multimodal smoothing with calibrated uncertainty.
- Cons: lacks PMCMC baseline comparisons, limited diagnostics on mixing/scaling, narrow experimental ablations and practical guidance.
- Overall: technically sound and well written with high originality and potential significance, but requires stronger empirical validation and diagnostics to be fully convincing.
