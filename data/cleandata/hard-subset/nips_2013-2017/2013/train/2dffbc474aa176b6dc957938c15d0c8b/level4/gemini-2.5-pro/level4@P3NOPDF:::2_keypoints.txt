- Proposes applying particle MCMC, specifically ancestral sampling particle Gibbs (PG-AS), for inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs).  
- Paper is clearly written and presents an interesting, original application of particle MCMC to GP-SSMs.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant that improves scalability and is a useful model-specific contribution.  
- Major weakness: lacks empirical comparisons to standard particle MCMC competitors such as Particle Marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH).  
- Also missing comparisons to Particle Gibbs with backward sampling (PG-BS), a natural competitor to PG-AS.  
- Omission of PMMH and PG-BS comparisons is a significant oversight, hindering assessment of practical benefits.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently informative and does not clarify performance characteristics or scaling behavior.  
- Authors should include ACF/ESS (or similar) plots versus number of particles (N) and time series length (T) to show computational trade-offs and tuning guidance.  
- Important open question: how N must scale with T (linear, sublinear, quadratic) for reliable inference; literature suggests PMMH may scale quadratically while PG-AS/PG-BS may scale sublinearly.  
- Verifying these scaling behaviors empirically for GP-SSMs would strengthen motivation for using particle Gibbs methods over PMMH.
