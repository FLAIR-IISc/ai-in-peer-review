The authors apply particle MCMC to inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs), focusing on the recent particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling method of Lindsten et al. The paper is clearly written and presents an interesting, original application of particle MCMC. It also introduces useful model-specific methodology, notably a sparse GP-SSM formulation.

A major shortcoming is the absence of comparisons with other particle MCMC schemes, in particular particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (as in Whiteley et al.). These alternatives appear straightforward to implement in this setting, and it would be valuable to see how they perform relative to the proposed approach; adding such comparisons should not require substantial additional effort.

I would also like to see plots of algorithmic performance (e.g., ACF or ESS) as functions of N and T. As currently presented, the results are not very informative. Should N scale linearly with T, or sublinearly?

My understanding is that, for these models, PMMH typically requires a number of particles that increases quadratically with T (as observed by Whiteley et al.), whereas both particle Gibbs variants require only sublinear growth in the number of particles with T.

In summary, this is a well-written application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. The paper would be significantly improved by empirical comparisons with PMMH and with particle Gibbs using backward sampling.
