

The paper applies PMCMC sampling within a state space model (SSM) with a Gaussian process (GP) prior to estimate model parameters, extending beyond standard filtering and smoothing procedures. The application of PMCMC methodology is straightforward and well-executed. However, the paper fails to provide a thorough evaluation of the practical challenges in implementing the PMCMC scheme—a critical omission that would have constituted the paper’s main contribution. A more detailed and critical assessment of the approach’s strengths and limitations would have significantly enhanced the paper’s value, given that it is primarily an application of established PMCMC techniques rather than a novel methodological advance.  

The current experimental section focuses on demonstrating the method’s feasibility but lacks meaningful analysis of implementation hurdles, such as computational costs, convergence behavior, or sensitivity to hyperparameters. For instance, the paper does not discuss how the GP prior interacts with particle degeneracy in the PMCMC framework, nor does it quantify the trade-offs between computational efficiency and estimation accuracy. Without such evaluation, it remains unclear whether the proposed approach offers tangible advantages over simpler alternatives or standard PMCMC implementations in similar settings.  

The authors should have addressed key questions: How robust is the scheme to initialization or model misspecification? What are the practical constraints (e.g., runtime, memory) for real-world applications? How does its performance compare to baseline methods in terms of both accuracy and scalability? A rigorous empirical analysis—including ablation studies on critical design choices—would have justified the method’s utility. Instead, the paper’s evaluation appears superficial, merely confirming that the algorithm produces plausible results without establishing its practical viability.  

While it may be unreasonable to expect substantial revisions to the experimental section, the current presentation falls short of the depth required to assess the method’s real-world applicability. The paper’s core contribution would have been strengthened by shifting focus from "does it work?" to "how well does it work, and under what conditions?" For example, a comparison of runtime versus accuracy across different problem scales, or an exploration of failure cases (e.g., when the GP prior misaligns with the true dynamics), would have provided concrete insights into the approach’s practical boundaries.  

In summary, the work is technically sound but lacks the critical evaluation needed to position it as a meaningful contribution to the literature. The authors successfully demonstrate PMCMC’s applicability to parameter estimation in SSMs with GP priors, but the absence of a nuanced discussion on implementation challenges—coupled with minimal empirical scrutiny—undermines the paper’s potential impact. A revised version addressing these gaps would better serve the community by clarifying when and why this approach is preferable to existing methods. As it stands, the paper reads more like a feasibility study than a compelling methodological advancement.
