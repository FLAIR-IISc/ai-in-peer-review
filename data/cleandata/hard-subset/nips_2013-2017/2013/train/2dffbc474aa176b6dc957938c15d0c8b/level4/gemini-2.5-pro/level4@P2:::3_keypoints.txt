- Fully Bayesian GP-SSM with a GP prior on the transition function and analytical marginalization of the transition.
- Marginalizing the GP breaks the Markov property and introduces long-range temporal dependencies in the state sequence.
- Inference is performed with Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to handle the resulting non‑Markovian latent model.
- A sparse GP extension reduces computational complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) using inducing points.
- The derivation of the marginal state prior is correct and the overall method is technically sound.
- Marginalizing the dynamics preserves full nonparametric flexibility and is an elegant modeling choice.
- Inclusion of a sparse GP variant shows awareness of practical computational limitations.
- Empirical evaluation is weak and functions as a proof-of-concept rather than a rigorous assessment.
- No analysis of sampler efficiency (mixing, effective sample size, autocorrelations, acceptance rates) is provided.
- The claim that a moderate number of particles yields rapid mixing is not supported by evidence.
- The paper is well-written and the PGAS application to the model is clearly explained.
- Implementation details necessary for reproducibility (e.g., efficient Cholesky updates for ancestor weights) are lacking.
- The main novelty is the novel combination of marginalizing GP dynamics and applying PGAS to the resulting non‑Markovian model.
- The potential significance is high for fully Bayesian nonparametric dynamical system learning, but practical impact is unclear.
- The method is not compared against EM/MAP or other state-of-the-art GP-SSM learning algorithms.
- The paper provides limited insight into practical challenges of implementing and tuning the PGAS sampler at scale.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled fully Bayesian framework, novel methodological combination, and a computationally-aware sparse variant.
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient experimental evaluation, lack of competitive benchmarking, and inadequate discussion of practical challenges.
- Overall: methodologically interesting and novel but its contribution is undermined by weak experiments and missing practical assessment.
