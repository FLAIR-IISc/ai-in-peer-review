- Introduces a Bayesian method for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models, learning transition dynamics with a Gaussian process prior.
- Inference uses particle MCMC (PMCMC) and incorporates ancestor sampling; online marginalisation of transition dynamics with ancestor sampling is a notable contribution.
- Addresses computational efficiency via sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky update/downdate techniques.
- Paper is technically rigorous, well-written, clear, and accessible.
- Empirical results are concise but provide adequate support; could be expanded.
- Work prompts discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and on learning transition dynamics versus specifying parametric models a priori.
- Suggest extending the discussion to contrast using a parametric transition specified a priori with using that parametric form as the GP mean, especially for out-of-sample prediction/forecasting.
- Table 1 and its description need clarification: unclear if reported RMSEs are computed on held-out data or on data used for conditioning.
- Recommend adding an out-of-sample (forecast) RMSE column to Table 1 to better assess differences between learned and true dynamics.
- Minor presentation issue: CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before it is defined in Section 3.3.1.
- Overall assessment: strong, original paper likely to stimulate significant discussion in the field.
