- Proposes applying particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs) for inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs).

- Paper is clearly written; the application is interesting and original.

- Major strength: development of a model-specific methodology for a sparse GP-SSM.

- Significant limitation: no comparative analysis against other relevant particle MCMC schemes.

- Essential to include comparisons with Particle Marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and Particle Gibbs with backward sampling as standard benchmarks.

- Recommend reporting performance metrics (e.g., ESS, ACF) as functions of number of particles N and time-series length T.

- Need a computational-scaling analysis to show how the inference quality depends on N and T (e.g., does N need to grow linearly or sublinearly with T).

- Apply the scaling analysis to benchmark algorithms as well; hypothesize PMMH may require N to scale quadratically with T, while particle Gibbs variants may scale more favorably.

- Empirical evaluation must be expanded (benchmarks + scaling study) to rigorously validate the advantages of the proposed approach.
