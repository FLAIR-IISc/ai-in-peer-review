- Bayesian approach to state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models with a GP prior on transition dynamics.  
- Inference via PMCMC with ancestor sampling; online marginalization of transition dynamics is a novel contribution.  
- Discussion of computational efficiencies (sparsity, low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates).  
- Paper is technically sound, well-presented, and accessible.  
- Empirical results are brief but sufficient to demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness.  
- Needs more discussion comparing using a parametric transition model a priori vs using it as the GP mean function, especially for forecasting/out-of-sample prediction.  
- Table 1 unclear whether RMSE is computed in-sample or out-of-sample; recommend adding an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column.  
- Minor issue: abbreviation CPF-AS is used before being defined.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and technically sound method, unique use of GP priors with ancestor sampling, attention to computational practicality, supported by experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion on parametric vs GP-mean approaches and unclear presentation of RMSE results.  
- Overall: strong, novel paper likely to stimulate discussion; minor revisions would make it suitable for acceptance.
