- Proposes Bayesian joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models by learning transition dynamics with a Gaussian process prior and performing inference with PMCMC + ancestor sampling.  
- Uses ancestor sampling to handle non-Markovian dependencies induced by marginalizing out the GP.  
- Addresses computational efficiency via sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Presentation is clear, technically solid, and empirical results are generally convincing though brief.  
- Novel combination of GP-based transition learning with PMCMC and ancestor sampling; notable treatment of non-Markovian dependence.  
- Algorithmic components and the role of ancestor sampling are well explained.  
- Discussion of sparsity and low-rank updates is thoughtful and important for scalability.  
- Work has potential impact on inference in non-Markovian state-space models and on trade-offs between nonparametric and parametric transition modeling.  
- Unclear whether reported RMSEs (Table 1) are in-sample or out-of-sample — evaluation protocol needs clarification.  
- Stronger emphasis on out-of-sample forward forecasting would better demonstrate benefits over misspecified parametric models.  
- Needs deeper discussion comparing parametric transition models vs GP-based learning, including using parametric models as GP mean functions.  
- Recommend clarifying train/test splits, horizon lengths, and hyperparameter tuning procedures.  
- Request to report computational complexity (time/space) as functions of time steps, particles, and inducing points, plus wall-clock times.  
- Suggest an ablation isolating the effect of sparsity and low-rank updates on runtime and memory.  
- Ask for PMCMC diagnostics: effective sample sizes, autocorrelation, acceptance rates, and sensitivity to particle count and initialization.  
- Notation fixes: define CPF-AS before first use and introduce all abbreviations on first occurrence.  
- Minor: clarify whether GP hyperparameters are learned or marginalized and state priors used.  
- Minor: indicate numerical stability of Cholesky downdates and any safeguards.  
- Minor: include qualitative visualizations of learned dynamics vs ground truth if space allows.  
- Overall: strong, recommend acceptance pending clarifications on evaluation, diagnostics, and minor exposition fixes.
