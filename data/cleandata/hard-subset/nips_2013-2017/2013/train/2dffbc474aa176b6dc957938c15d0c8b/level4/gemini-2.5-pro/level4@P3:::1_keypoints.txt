- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing a GP prior on the state transition and marginalizing out the infinite-dimensional transition function.  
- Marginalizing the dynamics preserves nonparametric flexibility but induces a non-Markovian dependency structure on latent states.  
- Adapts Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) within a PMCMC framework to sample joint posterior of states and hyperparameters.  
- Enables joint state smoothing and system identification in a coherent Bayesian framework.  
- Addresses computational scalability using sparse GPs and rank-one Cholesky updates, reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Validated experimentally on a benchmark nonlinear system and a cart-pole system.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior work by providing full sample-based posterior inference rather than parametric/EM or MAP/variational approximations.  
- High technical quality: sound derivations of the marginalized prior and correct application of PGAS.  
- Practical and complete contribution: authors address implementation and computational challenges, not just theory.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation of complex techniques.  
- Minor clarity issue: CPF-AS abbreviation used before formal definition.  
- Original and creative combination of marginalization and PMCMC enabled by ancestor sampling.  
- Significant and likely influential contribution with broad applicability (control, robotics, econometrics, signal processing).  
- Suggested improvement: clarify whether RMSE in Table 1 is reported on training or held-out test data and add multi-step ahead forecasting on held-out data.  
- Suggested improvement: expand discussion on using a parametric GP mean function and how GP uncertainty interacts with it during out-of-sample extrapolation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, highly clear, and practically relevant.  
- Argument against acceptance: experimental evaluation could be strengthened with clearer held-out predictive/forecasting results.
