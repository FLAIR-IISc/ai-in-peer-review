

The authors propose applying particle MCMC to inference in Gaussian process state-space models, specifically focusing on the recent ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm by Lindsten et al. The paper is clear, presents an interesting and original application of particle MCMC, and develops useful model-specific methodology, namely sparse GP-SSM.  

A regrettable omission is the lack of comparison to other particle MCMC schemes, particularly particle marginal MH (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.). These variants could have been straightforwardly implemented, and such comparisons would provide valuable insight into the relative performance of the proposed scheme—requiring minimal additional effort from the authors.  

Furthermore, the results lack informative performance metrics. Graphs displaying algorithm performance (e.g., ACF or ESS) as a function of \(N\) and \(T\) are essential to understand scaling behavior. Without them, it remains unclear how \(N\) should scale with \(T\) (linearly, sublinearly, etc.).  

Theoretical considerations suggest PMMH would require a number of particles growing quadratically with \(T\), as observed in Whiteley et al., whereas both particle Gibbs variants require particle counts growing sublinearly with \(T\).  

This is a well-written application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. The paper could be significantly strengthened by comparing the proposed algorithm to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
