The paper applies particle MCMC for Bayesian inference in Gaussian process state-space models, with a particular emphasis on the particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) algorithm of Lindsten et al. The manuscript is clearly written and offers an interesting and original use of particle MCMC. It also contributes useful model-specific developments, notably a sparse GP-SSM construction.

A notable shortcoming is the absence of comparisons with other particle MCMC variants, especially particle marginal MH (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (as in Whiteley et al.). These baselines appear straightforward to implement, and it would be valuable to see how they perform relative to the proposed approach (and likely would not require substantial additional effort).

I would also like to see performance curves (e.g., ACF or ESS) as functions of N and T. As presented, the empirical results are not very informative regarding how to scale N with T: should N grow linearly, or sublinearly?

My expectation for these models is that PMMH would need a number of particles increasing quadratically with T, as observed by Whiteley et al., while both particle Gibbs variants should require a sublinear growth in the number of particles with T.

In summary, this is a well-written application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. The paper would be significantly strengthened by empirical comparisons against PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
