- Proposes applying particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs) to Gaussian process state-space models.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and technically sound.  
- Includes useful model-specific methodology, notably sparse GP-SSM.  
- Major missing comparison to other particle MCMC schemes, especially PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.  
- Such comparisons would be straightforward to implement and are necessary to assess relative performance.  
- Results lack informative diagnostics; requests plots of autocorrelation or effective sample size versus N and T.  
- Concern about required particle scaling with T: PMMH may need particles ∝ T^2 while particle Gibbs variants grow sublinearly.  
- Lack of comparisons significantly limits the paper’s contribution and reduces perceived originality.  
- Paper has potential significance for GP-SSMs if extended with additional comparisons and results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, original application, well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: significant omission of comparative baselines, limited and non-informative results.
