

This paper introduces a Bayesian framework for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models, leveraging a Gaussian process prior to learn transition dynamics. The inference employs particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) with ancestor sampling, enabling online marginalization of transition dynamics. The authors further address computational efficiency through sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates for covariance matrices.  

The work is technically sound, well-motivated, and presented with exceptional clarity. The online marginalization of dynamics via ancestor sampling represents a significant novelty, while the treatment of computational aspects—particularly sparsity and low-rank Cholesky operations—substantially strengthens the contribution. The empirical results, though concise, are adequate; however, the following points warrant attention.  

First, the paper would benefit from deeper discussion comparing parametric transition models (specified a priori) versus using a similar parametric form as the mean function of the GP. This distinction is critical for out-of-sample prediction scenarios, such as forecasting.  

Second, Table 1’s RMSE reporting requires clarification: it is unclear whether the metric is computed against withheld data (for forecasting) or the same data conditioned upon (denoted by *|data* in column headers). To meaningfully assess the value of learned dynamics versus ground-truth models, an out-of-sample forecast RMSE (computed on withheld future data) is essential. If Table 1 does not already include this, an additional column reporting such metrics would greatly enhance the evaluation.  

Finally, the abbreviation CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before its definition in Section 3.3.1; this should be corrected.  

This is a strong, novel contribution that will stimulate valuable discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and the trade-offs of learning dynamics versus parametric specification. The paper is well-suited for NIPS.
