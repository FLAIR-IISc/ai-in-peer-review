- Introduces a Bayesian framework for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a Gaussian process prior for transition dynamics.  
- Uses particle MCMC with ancestor sampling for online marginalization of the transition dynamics.  
- Improves computational efficiency via sparsity exploitation and low-rank updates to Cholesky factors of covariance matrices.  
- Work is technically robust, well-motivated, and clearly presented.  
- Online marginalization via ancestor sampling is a novel contribution.  
- Attention to computational aspects (sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates) significantly strengthens the paper.  
- Empirical results are concise but sufficient to support core claims.  
- Suggests broader discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs between learning transition dynamics versus specifying a parametric model beforehand; asks authors to clarify using a parametric model as the GP mean function, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.  
- Ambiguity in Table 1: unclear if reported RMSE is on holdout data or training/conditioning data (headers show "*|data"); recommends adding out-of-sample RMSE for forward prediction.  
- Minor issue: abbreviation "CPF-AS" appears in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution likely to stimulate productive dialogue in the field.
