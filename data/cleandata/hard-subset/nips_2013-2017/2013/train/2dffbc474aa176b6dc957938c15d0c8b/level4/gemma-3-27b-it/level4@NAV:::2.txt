The authors present an application of particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) to inference within Gaussian process state-space models, building upon the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm introduced by Lindsten et al. This is a clear and original contribution, with the development of sparse GP-SSM methodology being a particularly valuable addition.

However, a significant limitation is the absence of comparative analysis against other established particle MCMC methods. Specifically, performance comparisons to particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (e.g., as detailed in Whiteley et al.) would substantially strengthen the work. Implementing these alternatives appears feasible and would provide valuable insight into the relative merits of the proposed scheme – representing a relatively modest undertaking for the authors.

Furthermore, the presented results lack sufficient detail to fully assess algorithmic performance. Inclusion of figures illustrating key metrics (such as autocorrelation function or Effective Sample Size) as a function of both *N* and *T* would greatly enhance interpretability. It remains unclear how *N* should scale with *T* – linearly, sublinearly, or otherwise. Based on existing literature (e.g., Whiteley et al.), one might expect PMMH to exhibit quadratic scaling with *T*, while particle Gibbs methods demonstrate sublinear growth.

In summary, this is a well-written paper demonstrating a practical application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. The manuscript would benefit considerably from a comparative evaluation against PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
