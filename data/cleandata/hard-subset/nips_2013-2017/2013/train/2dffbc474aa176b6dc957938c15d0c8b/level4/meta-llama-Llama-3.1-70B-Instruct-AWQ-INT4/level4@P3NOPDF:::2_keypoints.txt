- Proposes an innovative application of particle MCMC to Gaussian process state-space models, focusing on ancestral sampling particle Gibbs (Lindsten et al.).  
- Paper is well-written and presents an original application with useful model-specific methodology (sparse GP-SSM).  
- Major omission: no comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes.  
- Recommend comparison to particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- Implementing PMMH and backward-sampling Gibbs variants should be straightforward and informative.  
- Request graphical performance metrics (e.g., ACF, ESS) plotted against number of particles N and time-series length T.  
- Current results are not sufficiently informative; unclear how to scale N with T (linear vs sublinear).  
- Reviewer hypothesis: PMMH may require N to grow quadratically with T, while particle Gibbs schemes may need only sublinear growth.  
- Concluding recommendation: include algorithmic comparisons and scaling analyses to clarify trade-offs and strengthen the paper.
