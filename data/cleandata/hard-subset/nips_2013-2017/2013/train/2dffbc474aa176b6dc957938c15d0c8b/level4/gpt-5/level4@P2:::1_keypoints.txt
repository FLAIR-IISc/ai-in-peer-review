- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP state-space model by analytically marginalizing the transition function and sampling states and hyperparameters via Particle MCMC (PGAS).  
- Given latent trajectory samples, the GP transition posterior is available in closed form, enabling flexible nonparametric dynamics.  
- Addresses computational challenges with sparse GP priors (FIC) and efficient Cholesky update/downdate strategies to control complexity.  
- Conceptual novelty: collapsing the transition and using PGAS for non-Markovian latent models is elegant and, to the reviewer’s knowledge, new.  
- Technical soundness: careful derivation, ancestor sampling mitigates path degeneracy and improves mixing; sparse/low-rank techniques are well motivated.  
- Clarity: generally well written and accessible, with useful connections to GP regression once latent trajectories are known.  
- Practical relevance: GP priors allow encoding prior knowledge via mean functions while modeling deviations; demonstrated on cart-pole and nonlinear benchmarks.  
- Evaluation clarity concern: ambiguity in Table 1 about whether RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample; request explicit out-of-sample multi-step forecasting results.  
- Mean function vs parametric model: ask for expanded discussion on differences in extrapolation, uncertainty calibration, and identifiability when using parametric mean vs fixed parametric transition.  
- Scalability/implementation: request guidance on practical choices (particle count N, inducing points M), runtime vs accuracy trade-offs, mixing diagnostics, and an ablation comparing vanilla GP-SSM vs FIC.  
- Notation issue: CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before definition; define acronym on first use or move definition earlier.  
- Reproducibility: ask to clarify kernel choices, hyperparameter priors, constraints on Q, and sensitivity; if slice sampling used for θx, report step-size/initialization and acceptance behavior.  
- Quality: methodology is rigorous with convincing correctness and efficiency arguments; analytic marginalization and PGAS coupling are well justified.  
- Originality/significance: novel combination of collapsed GP transitions with PGAS for non-Markovian SSMs, valuable to GP and SSM communities and improves uncertainty quantification.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant fully Bayesian inference, solid PGAS contribution to reduce path degeneracy, attention to computational efficiency, and supportive empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: experimental evaluation should emphasize out-of-sample forecasting and scalability; minor clarity issues (Table 1 labeling, CPF-AS); limited discussion on parametric mean vs fully parametric trade-offs.  
- Overall: strong and timely paper that advances GP-SSM inference; fixing evaluation clarity and adding explicit out-of-sample forecasting comparisons would strengthen the submission.
