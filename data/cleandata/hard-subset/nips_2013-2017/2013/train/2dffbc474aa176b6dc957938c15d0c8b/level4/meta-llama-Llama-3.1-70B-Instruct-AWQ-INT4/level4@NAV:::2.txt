The authors present an innovative application of particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) to Gaussian process state-space models, focusing on the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm proposed by Lindsten et al. The manuscript is well-structured and clear, offering a compelling extension of particle MCMC. Notably, the authors develop model-specific methodology, including sparse GP-SSM.

A notable omission in the paper is a comparison with other particle MCMC schemes, specifically the particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling, as described by Whiteley et al. Implementing these variants would have been relatively straightforward and would have provided valuable insights into their performance relative to the proposed algorithm.

Furthermore, the inclusion of graphical representations of algorithm performance, such as autocorrelation function (ACF) or effective sample size (ESS) as a function of N and T, would greatly enhance the informative value of the results. As presented, the results lack clarity, leaving questions about the scaling of N with respect to T. 

It is likely that the PMMH would necessitate a quadratic increase in the number of particles with T, as observed by Whiteley et al., whereas both particle Gibbs schemes would require a sublinear increase in particles with T.

Overall, the manuscript presents a well-written application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. However, the paper would benefit significantly from a comparison with the PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed algorithm's performance.
