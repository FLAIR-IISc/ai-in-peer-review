- Introduces a fully Bayesian framework for joint state estimation and system identification in nonlinear state-space models using a GP prior on transition dynamics, with inference via Particle MCMC and ancestor sampling, plus sparse GP and low-rank Cholesky update/downdate for efficiency.  
- Technically robust and clearly written.  
- On-the-fly marginalization of the transition function combined with ancestor sampling appears novel.  
- Practical implementation details (sparsity, efficient Cholesky update/downdate) strengthen the contribution.  
- Experimental section is concise but sufficiently informative.  
- Work will likely stimulate discussion on inference for non‑Markovian state-space models and trade-offs versus committing to parametric transition models.  
- Suggest expanding discussion comparing predefined parametric transition models to using comparable structures as the GP mean, especially for out‑of‑sample prediction and forecasting.  
- Table 1 and its description are unclear about whether RMSE is computed on held‑out data or conditioning data; recommend adding an out‑of‑sample RMSE column if not already present.  
- Minor editorial: CPF‑AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original paper that should prompt interesting discussion.
