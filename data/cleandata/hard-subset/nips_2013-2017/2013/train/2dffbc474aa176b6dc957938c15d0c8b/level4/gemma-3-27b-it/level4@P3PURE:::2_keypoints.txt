- Paper applies particle MCMC to Gaussian process state-space models, focusing on ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs and proposing useful model-specific methods (notably a sparse GP-SSM).  
- The application is clear, interesting, and original.  
- Major omission: no comparison to other particle MCMC schemes, especially particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- Implementing PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would be straightforward and would provide valuable comparative insight with little extra effort.  
- Results are uninformative; the paper needs graphs of performance (e.g., ACF or ESS) versus N and T to clarify scaling—does N scale linearly or sublinearly with T?  
- Expectation noted: PMMH may require particles growing quadratically with T (per Whiteley), while particle Gibbs variants should show sublinear scaling.  
- Overall: well-written but would be substantially strengthened by comparative analysis against PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
