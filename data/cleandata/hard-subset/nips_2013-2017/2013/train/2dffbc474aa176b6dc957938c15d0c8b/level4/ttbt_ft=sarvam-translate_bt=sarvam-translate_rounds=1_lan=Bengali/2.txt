The authors propose applying particle MCMC to estimate Gaussian process state-space models. Specifically, they focus on the recent genetic sample particle Gibbs algorithm of Lindsten and others. The paper is clear and provides an interesting and fundamental application of particle MCMC. Additionally, the paper develops some useful model-specific methods, such as the sparse GP-SSM.

One thing I find genuinely regrettable is the lack of comparison with other particle MCMC schemes, especially the particle edge-based MH (PMMH) scheme and backward sampling (e.g., Whittley and others). It would be interesting to know how those forms could be directly applied and compared with the proposed scheme (and it wouldn't be too much work for the authors either).

Furthermore, I would like to see graphs demonstrating the performance of the algorithms (e.g., in terms of ACF or ESS) as a function of N and T. The results as they stand are not very informative. Do I need to linearly scale N with T, or sub-linearly?

I believe that such models would require a number of particles increasing at a quadratic rate with T, as seen in Whittley and others, for PMMH. Whereas, both particle Gibbs require a number of particles increasing at a sub-linear rate with T.

GP is a well-written application of particle MCMC to state-space models. The paper could have been significantly improved if the proposed algorithm had been compared with particle Gibbs, including PMMH and backward sampling.
