The authors propose applying particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) to perform inference in Gaussian process state-space models. Specifically, they focus on the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm of Lindsten et al. The paper is well-written and presents an interesting and original application of particle MCMC. Furthermore, the authors develop useful model-specific methodology, notably a sparse GP-SSM implementation.

A significant omission, however, is the lack of comparison to other particle MCMC schemes, particularly particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (as in Whiteley et al.). Implementing these would be relatively straightforward and would provide valuable insight into how the proposed scheme performs in comparison. This comparison would not require substantial additional effort from the authors.

I would also like to see visualizations displaying algorithm performance (e.g., using autocorrelation function (ACF) or effective sample size (ESS)) as a function of both *N* and *T*. As presented, the results are not very informative. It’s unclear how *N* needs to scale with *T* – linearly, sublinearly, or otherwise? 

I anticipate that PMMH would require a number of particles increasing quadratically with *T*, consistent with observations in Whiteley et al., while both particle Gibbs variants should exhibit a sublinear scaling of particles with *T*. 

Overall, this is a well-written application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. The paper would be significantly strengthened by a comparative analysis against PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
