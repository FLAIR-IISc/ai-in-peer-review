The authors propose an innovative application of particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) to perform inference in Gaussian process state-space models. Specifically, they focus on the recent ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm of Lindsten et al. The paper is well-written, and the application of particle MCMC to this context is both interesting and original. Additionally, the authors develop useful model-specific methodology, including sparse GP-SSM.

One notable omission in the paper is a comparison with other particle MCMC schemes. In particular, the particle marginal Metropolis Hastings (PMMH) scheme and the particle Gibbs with backward sampling (as in Whiteley et al.) could have been easily implemented and compared to the proposed scheme. Such a comparison would be valuable in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed algorithm, and it would not have required a significant amount of additional work from the authors.

Furthermore, the presentation of the results could be improved. It would be helpful to include graphs displaying the performance of the algorithms, such as the autocorrelation function (ACF) or the effective sample size (ESS), as a function of the number of particles (N) and the time horizon (T). As the results stand, they are not particularly informative. For example, it is unclear how to scale the number of particles with respect to the time horizon; should it be linear, sublinear, or some other relationship?

In the context of these models, it is likely that the PMMH would require a number of particles that increases quadratically with T, as observed in Whiteley et al. In contrast, both particle Gibbs schemes may require a number of particles that grows sublinearly with T. A comparison with the PMMH and the particle Gibbs with backward sampling would provide valuable insights into the relative efficiency of these algorithms.

Overall, the paper is a well-written application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. However, it could be significantly improved with the inclusion of comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes and more informative presentations of the results.
