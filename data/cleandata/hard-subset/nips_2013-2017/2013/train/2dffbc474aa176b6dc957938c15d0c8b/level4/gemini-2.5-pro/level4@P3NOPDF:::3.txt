This paper presents an application of Particle MCMC (PMCMC) for parameter inference in state-space models (SSMs) featuring a Gaussian Process (GP) prior. The work extends the standard use of PMCMC for filtering and smoothing of latent states to the joint estimation of the model's static parameters. The proposed approach is a logical and straightforward application of the PMCMC framework to a powerful and relevant class of models.

However, the paper's primary weakness is the lack of a thorough and critical evaluation of the proposed scheme. As an application of an established methodology, the most significant contribution would have been a detailed analysis of the practical challenges, strengths, and limitations of using PMCMC in this specific context. Unfortunately, this analysis is largely absent.

A crucial aspect of any PMCMC implementation is its sensitivity to tuning parameters, such as the number of particles required for an accurate likelihood estimate and the design of efficient proposal distributions for the MCMC steps. A detailed discussion and empirical study of the difficulties encountered in getting the sampler to mix efficiently would have provided invaluable insights for practitioners. The paper misses the opportunity to explore these practical implementation details, which would have constituted its most important contribution.

Consequently, the experimental section serves more as a demonstration of the method's feasibility rather than a rigorous evaluation of its performance. A more detailed and critical assessment of the approach's strengths and weaknesses is needed. For this work to be of significant value, the empirical study should move beyond a simple demonstration. It would be substantially strengthened by assessing the practical difficulties of implementing the scheme, analyzing its computational cost, and providing a clear picture of how well it actually performs, perhaps in comparison to alternative inference methods.

In summary, while the paper tackles an interesting problem, its contribution is limited by the superficial nature of its evaluation. For an application paper, the key value lies in the practical insights it delivers. By not systematically investigating the challenges and performance of the proposed PMCMC scheme, the paper falls short of providing a truly useful contribution to the field. A more comprehensive and critical experimental analysis would be required to make this a valuable paper.
