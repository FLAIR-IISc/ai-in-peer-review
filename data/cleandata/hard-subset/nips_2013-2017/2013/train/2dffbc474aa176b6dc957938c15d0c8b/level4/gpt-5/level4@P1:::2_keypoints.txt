- Contribution: Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by analytically marginalizing the transition GP and sampling latent states and hyperparameters via particle MCMC.
- Method: Uses particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) to handle strong temporal dependencies after collapsing the GP.
- Sparse variant: Introduces an FIC-based sparse GP-SSM reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2T) with rank-one Cholesky updates for efficiency.
- Experiments: Demonstrates multimodal smoothing recovery and meaningful predictive uncertainty on a nonlinear benchmark and cart-and-pole system, even with misspecified GP mean.
- Relation to prior work: Contrasts with EM/MAP GP parametrizations and learned-GP filtering approaches; builds on PMCMC and PGAS for non-Markovian collapsed models.
- Strength — Quality: Modeling and inference are technically sound; collapsing f preserves nonparametric nature and PGAS is well justified.
- Strength — Implementation: Careful integration of sparse FIC and efficient reuse of factorizations/rank-one updates is valuable.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is clearly written, with accessible derivations and helpful complexity discussion.
- Strength — Originality: Novel combination of exact GP marginalization with PGAS for joint state/hyperparameter inference in GP-SSMs.
- Strength — Significance: Fully Bayesian, nonparametric SSM with efficient inference and expressive predictive distributions is broadly useful.
- Weakness — Missing baselines: Lacks empirical comparisons to other PMCMC variants, notably PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
- Weakness — Diagnostics: Does not report acceptance rates, mixing diagnostics, or run-time comparisons to assess PGAS preference.
- Weakness — Scaling: No systematic study of performance scaling with number of particles N and time horizon T (ACF/ESS vs N,T).
- Experimental suggestion: Provide ACF/ESS plots for key quantities and empirical guidance on how N should grow with T, plus wall-clock timings.
- Evaluation — Quality: Strong methodology but experiments need PMCMC baselines and scaling analysis.
- Evaluation — Clarity: Generally clear; adding diagnostic plots and explicit backward-sampling references would help.
- Evaluation — Originality: High; the approach is a novel and interesting application of PMCMC.
- Evaluation — Significance: Potentially high if empirical comparisons confirm mixing or efficiency gains.
- Pros for acceptance: Principled Bayesian treatment, appropriate PGAS use, useful sparse variant, demonstrated on challenging systems.
- Cons for acceptance: No PMMH/backward-sampling comparisons and no systematic mixing/ESS vs N,T study.
- Overall assessment: Technically solid and well-written; recommend acceptance conditional on adding PMCMC baselines and scaling/diagnostic plots.
