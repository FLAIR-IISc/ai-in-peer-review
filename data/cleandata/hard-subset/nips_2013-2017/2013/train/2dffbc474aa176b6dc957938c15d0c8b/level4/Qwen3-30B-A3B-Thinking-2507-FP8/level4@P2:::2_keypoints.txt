- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function f to retain nonparametric expressivity.  
- Develops a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) sampler targeting p(x0:T | θ, y0:T) to enable efficient inference.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM using the FIC prior to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) with M ≪ T.  
- Validated on nonlinear system identification and cart-pole tasks, showing accurate smoothing and predictive distributions.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear mathematical exposition and rigorous derivations.  
- Sparse implementation details are thorough, and limitations (e.g., inducing point selection) are acknowledged.  
- Marginalizing f and adapting PGAS to handle non-Markovian dependencies is an original and significant technical contribution.  
- Method is relevant to robotics, control, and neuroscience for learning complex dynamics without parametric assumptions.  
- Experimental results demonstrate multimodal smoothing and uncertainty-aware predictions consistent with theoretical claims.  
- Major weakness: lacks comparison to alternative particle MCMC methods (PMMH, PGBS), leaving efficiency claims unsubstantiated.  
- Missing convergence diagnostics (ACF, ESS) and quantification of how particle count N should scale with T.  
- Claim of "very good mixing" is unsupported without baseline benchmarks or mixing metrics.  
- Recommended revisions: add PGAS vs PMMH/PGBS comparisons, report ESS/ACF versus N and T, and discuss how PGAS avoids O(T^2) particle scaling.  
- Overall recommendation: technically strong and high-impact but needs additional empirical validation to confirm claimed scalability advantages.
