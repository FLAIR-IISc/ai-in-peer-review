- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function and uses tailored PMCMC (PGAS) for inference and learning.  
- Introduces a sparse FIC GP variant reducing computational cost to O(M^2 T) with M ≪ T.  
- Strength: Marginalization derivation and non-Gaussian state prior (Eqs. 3–4) are technically sound and rigorously presented.  
- Strength: PGAS algorithm is shown to preserve smoothing distribution invariance and enables non-Markovian inference.  
- Strength: Sparse GP extension is well-motivated and effectively addresses scalability concerns.  
- Originality: Advances prior GP-SSM work by enabling fully Bayesian, nonparametric learning of dynamics rather than assuming known dynamics.  
- Novelty: Applies recent PMCMC developments to GP-SSMs, claiming a new route to non-Markovian smoothing.  
- Significance: Method recovers robust smoothing in multimodal systems and captures state-space uncertainty (e.g., Figure 3).  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation lacks analysis of PMCMC practical challenges (convergence rates, particle sensitivity, failure modes).  
- Weakness: Theoretical complexity claims are not empirically validated or compared to baseline PMCMC implementations.  
- Weakness: Contribution insufficiently distinguished from prior PMCMC work; paper focuses on showing it works rather than explaining why it succeeds.  
- Weakness: Experiments emphasize predictive accuracy but omit sampling efficiency, mixing diagnostics, and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Pro: Novel marginalization preserving GP expressivity, sparse extension, solid theoretical derivation, and successful demonstrations.  
- Con: Insufficient empirical evaluation of PMCMC implementation, lack of benchmarking against alternative samplers, and limited analysis of practicality.  
- Recommendation: Technically sound but lacks practical viability evidence—suggest adding convergence and computational trade-off analyses; current paper judged reject for insufficient methodological novelty and experimental support.
