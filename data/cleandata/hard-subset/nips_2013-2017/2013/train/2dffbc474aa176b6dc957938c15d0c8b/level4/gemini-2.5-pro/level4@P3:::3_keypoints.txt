- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing a GP prior on the state transition and analytically marginalizing the GP, yielding a non-Markovian prior over states.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to perform inference in the resulting non-Markovian model.  
- Introduces a sparse GP (FIC) formulation to reduce computational complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Formulation is principled and elegant, avoiding parametric approximations and preserving nonparametric expressivity.  
- The choice of PMCMC/PGAS is appropriate for targeting the exact smoothing distribution without restrictive assumptions.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and technically sound; derivations and algorithm explanations are clear.  
- Practical scalability is considered via the sparse GP approximation.  
- Insufficient analysis of the PMCMC sampler: no convergence/mixing diagnostics, no study of ESS/autocorrelation versus particle count N, and no discussion of tuning challenges as dimensionality or T grow.  
- Lacks empirical runtime characterization tying theoretical complexity (O(T^3) or O(M^2 T)) to actual run times.  
- Experiments are more proof-of-concept than rigorous evaluation; missing comparisons to competing GP-SSM methods (e.g., EM or MAP) on standard benchmarks.  
- Cart-and-pole experiment is illustrative but lacks quantitative metrics (e.g., long-term prediction error) and comparisons to other learned models.  
- Overall: novel and valuable methodological contribution, but weakened by limited empirical evaluation and practical analysis of the inference machinery.
