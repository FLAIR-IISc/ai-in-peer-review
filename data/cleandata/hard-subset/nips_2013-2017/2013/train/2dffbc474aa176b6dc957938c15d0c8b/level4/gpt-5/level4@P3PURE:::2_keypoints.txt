- Application of particle MCMC (PGAS) to Gaussian process state-space models is interesting, original, and clearly written.
- Paper includes useful model-specific methodology: a sparse GP-SSM.
- Major omission: no comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, notably particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
- These baseline methods are straightforward to implement and would provide informative comparisons.
- Request for algorithmic performance diagnostics such as autocorrelation functions (ACF) or effective sample size (ESS).
- Need ACF/ESS plotted as functions of particle count N and time horizon T to assess scaling.
- Results as presented do not clarify how computational/ statistical cost scales; unclear whether N should scale linearly or sublinearly with T.
- Reviewer expectation: PMMH likely requires particles growing quadratically with T (per Whiteley et al.), while particle Gibbs variants should require sublinear growth.
- Recommendation: include empirical comparisons with PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling and provide scaling diagnostics (ACF/ESS vs N and T).
