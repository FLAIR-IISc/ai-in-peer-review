- Introduces a Bayesian framework for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a Gaussian process prior on transition dynamics.
- Performs inference with a particle MCMC algorithm enhanced by ancestor sampling.
- Exploits sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates to improve computational efficiency.
- Paper is technically robust, well-executed, and clearly presented.
- Novel contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics enabled by ancestor sampling.
- Practical value strengthened by detailed treatment of computational aspects (sparsity and low-rank updates).
- Empirical evaluation is concise but adequate to demonstrate capabilities.
- Likely to provoke community discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs between nonparametric learning and parametric specification.
- Recommendation to expand discussion comparing pre-specified parametric transition models versus using such a model as the GP mean, especially for out-of-sample prediction.
- Concern about Table 1: unclear whether reported RMSE is on held-out test data or the conditioning data; recommends adding out-of-sample forecasting results.
- Minor correction: acronym CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.
- Overall assessment: strong, innovative paper likely to generate meaningful discussion.
