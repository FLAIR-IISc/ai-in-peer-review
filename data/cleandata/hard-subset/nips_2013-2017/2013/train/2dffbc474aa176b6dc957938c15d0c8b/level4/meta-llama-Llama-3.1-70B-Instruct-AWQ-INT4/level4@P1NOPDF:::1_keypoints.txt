- Presents a Bayesian method for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models with GP priors for transition dynamics.  
- Uses PMCMC inference with ancestor sampling to perform online marginalization of the transition dynamics.  
- Addresses computational efficiencies, including exploiting sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and accessible.  
- Novel contributions: online marginalization of dynamics and use of ancestor sampling for this purpose.  
- Consideration of computational strategies (sparsity, low-rank updates) strengthens the work.  
- Empirical results are brief but generally sufficient; further experiments suggested.  
- Likely to stimulate debate on inference for non-Markovian SSMs and trade-offs of learning dynamics vs specifying parametric models.  
- Recommend discussing differences between using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean, especially for out-of-sample prediction/forecasting.  
- Results/Table 1 unclear about whether RMSE is out-of-sample or computed on conditioned data; add an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column.  
- Minor issue: abbreviation CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before being defined.
