- Proposes a fully Bayesian method for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using Gaussian processes (GPs) and Particle MCMC (PMCMC).  
- Marginalizes over the transition dynamics to enable efficient smoothing and subsequent learning of GP dynamics.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method, building on established GP and SSM literature.  
- Develops a sparse GP-SSM construction to address computational complexity.  
- Novel combination of GP flexibility with PMCMC for state estimation and system identification; advances beyond parametric approximations.  
- Method handles the non-Markovian structure induced by marginalizing the transition function.  
- Appropriately leverages recent PMCMC advances (e.g., Lindsten et al., 2012).  
- Demonstrated on a cart-pole system, illustrating practical applicability.  
- Major weakness: no empirical comparison to alternative particle MCMC schemes (PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling).  
- Authors’ claims about scaling differences (quadratic vs. sublinear) lack empirical validation.  
- Experimental analysis is limited and does not provide ACF or ESS diagnostics to assess mixing and performance.  
- Missing plots/analysis showing ESS/ACF as functions of particle count (N) and time horizon (T) to clarify scalability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, clarity, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of comparative evaluation and limited experimental performance analysis; recommend acceptance conditional on adding comparisons and ACF/ESS analyses.
