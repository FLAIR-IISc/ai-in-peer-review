- Proposes a Bayesian method to jointly estimate states and parameters in nonlinear state-space models with transition dynamics learned via a Gaussian process prior.  
- Performs inference using particle MCMC (PMCMC) and incorporates ancestor sampling for improved sampling.  
- Addresses computational efficiency with optimizations exploiting sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly presented, and strong overall.  
- Core novelty: online marginalization of transition dynamics achieved through ancestor sampling.  
- Practical computational considerations (sparsity and low-rank updates) significantly strengthen the work.  
- Empirical results are brief but sufficient to support the claims.  
- Suggest adding discussion comparing predefined parametric transition models versus using such a model as the GP mean, especially for out-of-sample prediction.  
- Table 1 and its RMSE results are unclear about whether they are in-sample or on a withheld test set; recommend reporting out-of-sample/forecast performance.  
- Minor issue: the abbreviation "CPF-AS" appears in Algorithm 1 before it is formally defined.
