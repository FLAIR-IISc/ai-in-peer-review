This paper presents a straightforward application of Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) sampling in a state-space model (SSM) with a Gaussian Process (GP) process prior, extending the methodology beyond traditional filtering and smoothing. The idea of leveraging PMCMC in this context is intriguing, and the authors demonstrate its feasibility.

However, the paper falls short in providing a comprehensive evaluation of the challenges associated with implementing the PMCMC scheme. A more detailed analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of this approach would have significantly enhanced the value of the paper. As it stands, the experimental section primarily serves as a demonstration rather than a rigorous evaluation.

A more critical assessment of the practical difficulties in implementing PMCMC in SSMs, as well as its actual performance, would have been a valuable contribution. The authors could have explored the challenges of getting the PMCMC scheme to work, such as convergence issues, particle degeneracy, or computational costs. Additionally, a more thorough comparison with existing methods would have helped to contextualize the results and provide a clearer understanding of the benefits and limitations of the proposed approach.

While it may be too much to expect a revised experimental section, the paper would have benefited from a more in-depth evaluation of the PMCMC methodology in the context of SSMs. Nevertheless, the authors' effort to apply PMCMC to SSMs is a step in the right direction, and with further development and analysis, this work could lead to more significant contributions in the future.
