- Introduces a fully Bayesian inference and learning framework for Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs) by marginalizing the GP transition function.  
- Develops a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) algorithm that samples from the smoothing distribution while preserving full nonparametric expressivity.  
- Proposes a sparse GP-SSM using the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) approximation, reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) with M ≪ T.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM and particle MCMC literature, extending PGAS specifically to GP-SSMs.  
- Theoretical development and derivations are clear, well-justified, and technically sound.  
- Experimental results show the model captures complex dynamics, including multimodal smoothing densities (e.g., bimodal sign-ambiguous system).  
- Cart-and-pole experiments demonstrate adaptive uncertainty across state-space regions and practical applicability.  
- Sparse GP-SSM implementation details are thorough and useful for practitioners.  
- Main weakness: no empirical comparison to alternative particle MCMC methods (PMMH, PGBS) to contextualize advantages.  
- Missing diagnostic analyses (ACF, ESS) and plots showing algorithm performance versus number of particles N and time horizon T.  
- Unclear whether particle count N must scale with T or can remain constant, leaving practical scaling behavior unresolved.  
- Recommendation: add comparisons between PGAS, PMMH, and PGBS and discuss computational scaling with T.  
- Recommendation: include diagnostic plots (ACF, ESS) to illustrate dependence on N and T.  
- Recommendation: clarify trade-offs between the number of inducing points M, accuracy, and computational cost.  
- Overall significance: important contribution enabling fully Bayesian nonparametric SSM inference and the ability to capture multimodal smoothing distributions.
