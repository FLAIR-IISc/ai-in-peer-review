- Proposes a Bayesian method for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models by placing a Gaussian process prior on transition dynamics and marginalising them online using particle MCMC with ancestor sampling.

- Introduces computational efficiencies through exploiting sparsity and using low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.

- Paper is technically sound with clear and accessible presentation.

- The online marginalisation of transition dynamics via ancestor sampling is a novel contribution.

- Consideration of sparsity and low-rank updates meaningfully strengthens the practical applicability.

- Empirical results are concise and generally sufficient, though some clarifications and extensions are suggested.

- The work should stimulate discussion on inference for non‑Markovian state‑space models and on learning transition dynamics versus choosing parametric models a priori.

- Recommends a more detailed comparison between using a parametric transition model a priori and using that model as the GP mean, especially regarding out‑of‑sample forecasting.

- Requests clarification on Table 1: whether reported RMSE is in‑sample or out‑of‑sample, and suggests adding an out‑of‑sample/forecast RMSE column if absent.

- Minor issue: CPF‑AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.
