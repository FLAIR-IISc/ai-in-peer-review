This paper explores the application of Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) sampling within a switching state-space model (SSM) framework, utilizing a Gaussian process (GP) prior to model the parameters directly, moving beyond traditional filtering and smoothing approaches. The application of PMCMC to this problem is conceptually sound and presented in a straightforward manner. 

However, a significant weakness lies in the limited discussion of the practical challenges associated with implementing and tuning the PMCMC scheme. A more thorough evaluation of these challenges—specifically, the difficulties encountered in achieving convergence and the sensitivity to parameter settings—would have substantially strengthened the contribution. While the paper *demonstrates* the method’s functionality, it lacks a *critical assessment* of its performance and limitations. Given that this work primarily constitutes an application of an established methodology (PMCMC), a detailed analysis of its strengths and weaknesses in this specific context is crucial for establishing value.

It may be overly ambitious to request a complete revision of the experimental section to move beyond demonstration. Nevertheless, a more focused investigation into the practical hurdles of deploying PMCMC within an SSM, along with a quantitative assessment of its efficacy under varying conditions, would have rendered this work considerably more impactful. The paper would have benefited from a more in-depth discussion of the practical difficulties encountered when applying such a scheme and a comprehensive analysis of how well it ultimately performs in overcoming those difficulties.
