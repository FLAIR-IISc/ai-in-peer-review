- Novel and original application of particle MCMC to Gaussian process state-space models, focusing on ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs (Lindsten et al.).  
- Paper is well-written and clear; model-specific methodology (e.g., sparse GP-SSM) is a notable contribution.  
- Significant omission: no comparisons with other particle MCMC schemes, particularly particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- Implementing PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would be straightforward and their inclusion would be highly informative.  
- Results lack graphical diagnostics; recommend plots of ACF or ESS as functions of particle number N and time T.  
- Key unanswered question: how must N scale with T (linear, sublinear, or quadratic)?  
- Reviewer expectation: PMMH likely requires particles growing quadratically with T (per Whiteley et al.), while particle Gibbs schemes likely require sublinear growth.  
- Overall: solid contribution but would be substantially improved by comparative experiments with PMMH and backward-sampling particle Gibbs to clarify strengths and limitations.
