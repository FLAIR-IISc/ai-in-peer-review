- Introduces a Bayesian framework for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models.  
- Key contribution: non-parametric learning of transition dynamics using a Gaussian process prior.  
- Inference via Particle MCMC enhanced with ancestor sampling to enable online marginalization of the GP.  
- Addresses practical computational challenges by exploiting sparsity and using low-rank Cholesky updates.  
- Paper is technically strong, novel, clearly written, and accessible.  
- Empirical results are brief but sufficient to demonstrate the method's potential.  
- Work will stimulate discussion on inference for (effectively) non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs in modeling choices.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion comparing fixed parametric transition models vs. using a parametric form as the GP mean, especially for forecasting/out-of-sample prediction.  
- Suggestion: clarify whether Table 1 RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample and add out-of-sample forecasting results if absent.  
- Minor presentation fix: define the abbreviation "CPF-AS" at first use.
