- Fully Bayesian framework: places a GP prior on the transition function and marginalizes it to perform inference on the joint smoothing distribution of latent states.  
- Inference method: addresses the resulting non-Markovian problem with Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS).  
- Nonparametric expressivity: avoids parametric GP approximations, preserving full GP flexibility.  
- Computational scalability: uses sparse GP approximations and rank-one Cholesky updates to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Strength — quality: technically sound, well-motivated, clear presentation, and correct theoretical claims.  
- Strength — originality: novel approach of marginalizing dynamics and tailoring PGAS, differing from prior parametric/MAP methods.  
- Strength — significance: enables fully Bayesian learning of dynamics with practical scalability and likely strong impact.  
- Weakness: needs explicit comparison between using a parametric model as the GP mean versus as the full transition, especially for forecasting.  
- Weakness: experimental clarity—unclear if reported RMSE is train or test; recommend adding out-of-sample forecasting RMSEs in Table 1.  
- Minor clarity: acronym "CPF-AS" is introduced before being defined; define it at first use.
