- Summary: Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the GP transition function, inducing non-Markovian latent dependencies, and uses PGAS for inference plus an FIC sparse variant to improve scalability; evaluated on a 1D synthetic and cart-pole system.
- Novelty/elegance: Marginalizing the infinite-dimensional GP dynamics is conceptually elegant and retains full nonparametric expressivity during inference.
- Inference method: Novel and well-motivated application of Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to handle the resulting non-Markovian posterior.
- Scalability: Introducing an FIC-based sparse GP-SSM reduces complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T), improving practical applicability to longer time series.
- Clarity: The paper is well-written with clear model presentation, derivations, and algorithmic descriptions.
- Lack of comparative evaluation: No empirical comparisons to other PMCMC algorithms (e.g., PMMH, Particle Gibbs with backward sampling) to contextualize PGAS performance.
- Insufficient sampler analysis: Claims of "very good mixing" lack quantitative MCMC diagnostics (ACF, ESS) and studies of how performance scales with particle number N and series length T.
- Limited experiments: Experimental validation is weak—mostly synthetic/illustrative, lacking quantitative metrics (test log-likelihood, long-term errors) and comparisons to competing system-identification methods or real-world datasets.
- Overall assessment: Technically strong and novel with high significance; recommend acceptance but argue the paper would be much stronger with more thorough empirical comparisons and sampler diagnostics.
