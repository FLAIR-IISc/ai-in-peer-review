- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function and using PGAS for inference, with a sparse FIC variant for scalability.  
- Strength: Elegant, principled fully Bayesian treatment that preserves nonparametric expressivity.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly explains challenges and the adapted PGAS sampler.  
- Strength: Novel application of PGAS to a non-Markovian model and a sparse GP variant reducing complexity to O(M²T).  
- Weakness: No empirical comparison to alternative PMCMC methods (e.g., PMMH, Particle Gibbs with backward sampling).  
- Weakness: Lacks rigorous quantitative MCMC diagnostics (e.g., ACF, ESS) to assess sampler mixing and efficiency.  
- Weakness: Missing analysis of scalability/statistical efficiency—how mixing and required particle number N scale with time series length T.  
- Conclusion: Solid and significant methodological contribution, but needs stronger empirical evaluation and benchmarking to be more impactful.
