The authors propose applying particle MCMC to estimate Gaussian process state-space models. Specifically, they focus on the recent genetic sample particle Gibbs algorithm by Lindsten and others. The paper provides a clear and compelling application of particle MCMC. Furthermore, the paper creates some useful model-specific methods, such as the sparse GP-SSM.

I find it truly unfortunate that particle MCMC schemes are not compared with other particle MCMC schemes, especially particle edge-based MH (PMMH) schemes and backward sampling (e.g., Whitley and others). It would be interesting to know how those forms can be directly applied and compared with the proposed scheme (and it wouldn't be too much work for the authors either).

In addition, I would like to see graphs demonstrating the performance of the algorithms (e.g., in terms of ACF or ESS) as a function of N and T. The results as they stand are not very informative. Do I need to scale N linearly with T, or sub-linearly?

I believe that such models would require a number of particles increasing at a quadratic rate with T, as seen in Whitley and others for PMMH. Whereas, both require a number of particles increasing at a sub-linear rate with T for particle Gibbs.

GP is a well-written application of particle MCMC to state-space models. If the proposed algorithm were compared with particle Gibbs, including PMMH and backward sampling, the paper could be significantly improved.
