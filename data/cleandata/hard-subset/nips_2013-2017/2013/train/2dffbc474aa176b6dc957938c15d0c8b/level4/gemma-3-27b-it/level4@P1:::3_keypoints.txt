- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function and uses PMCMC to sample the smoothing distribution.  
- Offers a flexible nonparametric model capable of capturing complex nonlinear dynamics, avoiding parametric SSM limitations.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM work but focuses on learning the GP dynamics rather than assuming a fixed GP.  
- Correctly positions the method relative to EM-based and MAP approaches and notes connections to dimensionality reduction and overfitting concerns.  
- Technically sound approach with clear, well-written exposition.  
- Provides a well-presented derivation of the GP-SSM and a thorough explanation of the PMCMC sampling procedure.  
- Includes a sparse GP extension (FIC prior) to address computational efficiency.  
- Experiments are simple but effectively demonstrate learning of nonlinear dynamics and uncertainty quantification.  
- Using a parametric model as a baseline within the GP prior cleverly demonstrates framework flexibility.  
- Lacks detailed discussion on practical PMCMC implementation, tuning, and sensitivity to initial conditions.  
- Insufficient analysis of PMCMC performance metrics (mixing rates, autocorrelation, convergence diagnostics, computational cost).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and would benefit from more comprehensive benchmarks against existing SSM methods.  
- The contribution is stronger methodologically than empirically.  
- Overall: solid and novel contribution; recommend acceptance with a request to address PMCMC implementation details and expand experimental evaluation.
