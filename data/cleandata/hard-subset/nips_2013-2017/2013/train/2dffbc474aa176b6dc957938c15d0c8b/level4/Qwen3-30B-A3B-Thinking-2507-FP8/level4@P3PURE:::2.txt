

The authors propose applying particle MCMC to inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs), with a specific focus on the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm by Lindsten et al. The paper is well-written, presents an interesting and original application of particle MCMC, and introduces useful model-specific methodology through the sparse GP-SSM framework.  

A significant limitation is the absence of comparisons to established particle MCMC schemes, particularly particle marginal MH (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.). These variants could be straightforwardly implemented, and a direct comparison would provide valuable insight into the relative performance of the proposed method. Such comparisons would require minimal additional effort from the authors but substantially strengthen the evaluation.  

Furthermore, the current results lack quantitative performance metrics. The authors should include plots displaying algorithm performance (e.g., autocorrelation function or effective sample size) as functions of the number of particles $N$ and time steps $T$. This would clarify critical practical considerations, such as whether $N$ must scale linearly or sublinearly with $T$. The paper should also explicitly address the theoretical scaling behavior: as noted in Whiteley et al., PMMH typically requires $O(T^2)$ particles for such models, while particle Gibbs variants (including the proposed method) generally require sublinear growth in $N$ with $T$.  

The work represents a solid application of particle MCMC to GP-SSMs, but would be significantly strengthened by incorporating these comparisons and performance analyses.
