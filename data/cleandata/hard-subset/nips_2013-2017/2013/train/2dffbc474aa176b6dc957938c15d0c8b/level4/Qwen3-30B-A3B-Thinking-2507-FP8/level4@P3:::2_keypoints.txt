- Proposes a fully Bayesian method for GP-SSMs that marginalizes the transition function f and samples p(x0:T | y0:T, θ) via a tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS).  
- Preserves full nonparametric expressivity of GP priors, avoiding restrictive parametric approximations.  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM using the FIC approximation, reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) with M ≪ T.  
- Validated on synthetic nonlinear systems and a cart-pole task, showing accurate smoothing and predictive performance comparable to ground truth.  
- Technical foundation and theoretical justification (including FIC details and complexity analysis) are sound and rigorous.  
- PGAS implementation correctly handles non-Markovian dependencies introduced by marginalizing f.  
- Paper is very well written, with clear problem statement, model formalization, and reproducible algorithmic details.  
- Figures effectively illustrate multimodal smoothing and uncertainty quantification.  
- Original contribution: first method for fully Bayesian joint learning of dynamics and states in GP-SSMs.  
- Novel combination of sparse GP techniques with state-space modeling expands applicability to larger problems.  
- Significant practical impact for robotics and time-series tasks where dynamics are unknown.  
- Primary weakness: no comparative experiments against alternative particle MCMC methods (PMMH, PGBS).  
- Lacks empirical performance metrics (e.g., ESS, ACF) as functions of particle count N and time horizon T to demonstrate scaling and mixing.  
- Missing runtime and accuracy comparisons (e.g., PGAS vs PMMH/PGBS) on representative datasets to substantiate scalability claims.  
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on adding comparisons to PMMH/PGBS and including inference-efficiency metrics to strengthen algorithmic claims.
