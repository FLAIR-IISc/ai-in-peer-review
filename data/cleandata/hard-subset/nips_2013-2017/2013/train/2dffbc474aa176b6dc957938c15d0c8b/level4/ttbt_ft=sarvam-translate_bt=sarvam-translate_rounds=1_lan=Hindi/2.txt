The authors propose applying particle MCMC to estimate in a Gaussian process state-space model. Specifically, they focus on Lindsten et al.'s recent ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm. The paper is clear and presents an interesting and original application of particle MCMC. The paper also develops some useful model-specific methodologies, namely sparse GP-SSM.

One thing that I find particularly disappointing is the lack of comparison with other particle MCMC schemes, especially the particle marginal MH (PMMH) scheme and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (as in Whiteley et al.). They could have been applied directly, and it would have been interesting to know how they compare to the proposed scheme (and it wouldn't be much work for the authors either).

Furthermore, I would like to see graphs demonstrating the performance of the algorithm (for example, in terms of ACF or ESS) as a function of N and T. As they stand, the results are not very informative. Do I need to scale N linearly, sublinearly, with T?

I believe that such models would require a PMMH with multiple particles increasing quadratically with T, as seen in Whiteley et al., while both particle Gibbs require particles increasing sublinearly with T.

A well-written application of particle MCMC for a GP state-space model. The paper could have been significantly improved if the proposed algorithm had been compared to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
