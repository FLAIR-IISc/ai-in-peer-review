- Introduces a Bayesian framework for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a Gaussian process prior for transition dynamics.
- Inference is performed via particle MCMC with ancestor sampling (CPF-AS).
- Addresses computational challenges by exploiting sparsity and using low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.
- Paper is technically rigorous and well written.
- Main methodological contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics enabled by effective use of ancestor sampling.
- Careful consideration of computational efficiency is a strong point.
- Empirical results are concise but generally provide sufficient validation.
- The work should provoke discussion on inference for non‑Markovian state-space models and trade-offs between learned versus parametric transitions.
- Suggest adding discussion comparing a priori parametric transition models to using the same model as the GP mean, especially for out‑of‑sample forecasting.
- Table 1 presentation is unclear—uncertainty whether RMSEs are out‑of‑sample or computed on conditioning data; recommend adding an out‑of‑sample forecast RMSE column.
- Minor notational issue: CPF-AS is introduced in Algorithm 1 before being formally defined in Section 3.3.1.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution; recommend acceptance.
