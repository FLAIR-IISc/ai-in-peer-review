- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP state-space model (GP-SSM) that marginalizes the transition function f, inducing non-Markovian latent trajectories.
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample jointly from states and hyperparameters.
- Posterior predictive dynamics are obtained as a mixture of GPs from sampled trajectories.
- Extends the approach to a sparse GP variant to improve scalability (reducing complexity from O(T³) to O(M²T)).
- Positions the method as learning the GP model itself, unlike prior work that assumed a learned model or used MAP/parametric approximations.
- Strength: addresses an important, challenging problem with an elegant and theoretically sound approach.
- Strength: marginalizing f preserves the model’s nonparametric character throughout inference.
- Strength: application of PGAS to the non-Markovian model is appropriate and well-motivated.
- Strength: sparse GP variant is a practical contribution that tackles computational bottlenecks.
- Strength: paper is generally well-written and clearly structured.
- Weakness: core inference technique is mainly an application of existing PMCMC methods, limiting novelty.
- Weakness: experimental section is more demonstrative than critical; lacks detailed analysis of sampler behavior (mixing, trace plots, ESS), sensitivity to particle number, and runtime costs.
- Weakness: limited empirical comparisons to other state-of-the-art GP-SSM learning methods.
- Weakness: misses discussion of practical implementation difficulties and guidance for successful application.
- Summary: solid, well-executed application of advanced MCMC to GP-SSMs but weakened by insufficient empirical evaluation and practical analysis.
