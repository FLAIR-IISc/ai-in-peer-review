- Presents a fully Bayesian method for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using Gaussian Process priors and PMCMC to marginalize the transition function.  
- PMCMC sampler is used to sample the joint smoothing distribution while preserving full nonparametric expressivity.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear overview of GP-based SSM literature.  
- Technical contributions are sound and the method is clearly and coherently presented.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart-and-pole system.  
- Main strength: tackles learning nonlinear nonparametric SSMs in a fully Bayesian manner.  
- Good application of existing PMCMC methodology to a new problem domain.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed, critical evaluation of practical difficulties and limitations of the PMCMC scheme (e.g., tuning, convergence).  
- Experimental section is more demonstrative than a thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Significance and broader implications of the results are not clearly discussed.  
- Originality is limited since the work builds on existing PMCMC methods, though the application to SSMs is a useful contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept, but request a more detailed critical evaluation and discussion of results and practical challenges.
