- Presents particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs) for Gaussian process state-space models and introduces a sparse GP-SSM implementation.  
- Paper is clearly written, interesting, and represents an original contribution.  
- Strength: practical application of particle MCMC to a relevant, complex model class with potential scalability gains from the sparse model.  
- Major weakness: lacks comparative analysis with other particle MCMC methods, notably PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- Recommendation: implement and compare PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling to assess relative computational cost and accuracy.  
- Results presentation insufficiently detailed to assess scalability and efficiency as problem size increases.  
- Suggestion: include plots of ACF and ESS versus number of particles (N) and time series length (T) to study computational complexity and scaling.  
- Key empirical question: determine how N must scale with T (linear vs sublinear) for the proposed method.  
- Prior-work expectation: PMMH may require particles scaling quadratically with T while particle Gibbs variants should be sublinear; empirical confirmation would strengthen the paper.  
- Conclusion: well-written work that would be substantially improved by comparative experiments and detailed performance-scaling analysis.
