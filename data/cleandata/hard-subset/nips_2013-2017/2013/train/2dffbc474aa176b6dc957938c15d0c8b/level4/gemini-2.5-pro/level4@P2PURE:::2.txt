This paper proposes an application of particle MCMC for inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs), focusing specifically on the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm. The paper is clearly written, the application is interesting and original, and the development of a model-specific sparse GP-SSM is a useful contribution.

A significant limitation of the work, however, is the lack of empirical comparisons to other relevant particle MCMC schemes, particularly the Particle Marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) algorithm and Particle Gibbs with backward sampling. These alternative methods could be implemented straightforwardly, and a comparison would provide valuable insight into the relative performance of the proposed approach.

Furthermore, the experimental results are not sufficiently informative in their current form. The evaluation would be substantially improved by providing a quantitative analysis of algorithm performance (e.g., using Autocorrelation Functions or Effective Sample Size) as a function of the number of particles (N) and the length of the time series (T). Such an analysis is crucial for understanding how N must scale with T to maintain a given level of inference quality. For instance, based on prior work, one might expect that PMMH requires N to scale quadratically with T for such models, whereas particle Gibbs variants may only require sublinear growth. Investigating this would be a valuable contribution.

In summary, this is a well-written paper on an interesting application, but its contribution would be significantly strengthened by a more thorough empirical evaluation that includes comparisons to PMMH and Particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
