- Introduces a Bayesian framework for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a Gaussian process prior for transition dynamics.
- Performs inference with particle MCMC combined with ancestor sampling.
- Addresses computational efficiency via exploiting sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.
- Paper is technically rigorous and well-written with clear presentation.
- Online marginalisation of transition dynamics through ancestor sampling is a novel contribution.
- Consideration of computational aspects (sparsity, low-rank updates) strengthens the work.
- Empirical results are concise and adequate but could be improved or expanded.
- The work will likely provoke discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs versus parametric transition models.
- Suggest expanding the discussion on using a parametric transition model as a prior versus as the GP mean function, especially for forecasting/out-of-sample prediction.
- Table 1 and its RMSE entries are unclear about whether they report withheld (out-of-sample) or conditioned data; add out-of-sample forecast results.
- Minor issue: abbreviation CPF-AS appears before its definition.
