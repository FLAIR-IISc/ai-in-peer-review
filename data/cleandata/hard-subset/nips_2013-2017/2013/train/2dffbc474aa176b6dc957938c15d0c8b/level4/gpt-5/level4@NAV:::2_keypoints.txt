- Authors apply particle MCMC (particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling) to inference in Gaussian process state-space models — clear writing and an interesting, original application.  
- Paper develops useful model-specific methodology, notably a sparse GP-SSM.  
- Major shortcoming: no comparisons to other particle MCMC methods, particularly PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- These baseline methods seem straightforward to implement and would provide informative comparisons with little extra work.  
- Results lack algorithmic-performance plots (e.g., ACF or ESS vs. N and T); unclear whether N should scale linearly or sublinearly with T.  
- The reviewer’s understanding: PMMH may require particles growing quadratically with T (per Whiteley et al.), while particle Gibbs methods need only sublinear growth.  
- Recommendation: include comparisons to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling to significantly improve the paper.
