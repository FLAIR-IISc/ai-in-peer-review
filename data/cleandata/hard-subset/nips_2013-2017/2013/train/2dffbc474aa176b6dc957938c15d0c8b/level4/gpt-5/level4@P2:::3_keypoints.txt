- Applies PGAS to fully Bayesian GP-SSMs, marginalizing the transition GP and sampling joint latent states and hyperparameters; uses sparse GP (FIC) to reduce cost to O(M^2 T).
- Contrasts with EM/MAP and filtering-only GP-SSM methods; main technical novelty is collapsing f and using PGAS for non-Markovian latent models.
- Collapsing the transition GP reduces strong x–f coupling and yields a coherent fully Bayesian treatment.
- PGAS is an appropriate sampler for the resulting non-Markovian posterior; invariance and mixing properties are correctly invoked.
- Predictive f|data represented as a mixture of GP conditionals naturally handles multimodal posteriors from ambiguous observations.
- Integrating a sparse GP prior and reusing factorization shows attention to practical computational scaling.
- Empirical evaluation of PMCMC practicality is limited; the key challenge of PGAS mixing in this setting is not thoroughly assessed.
- Missing mixing diagnostics (ACF, ESS) and sensitivity analyses to particle count N, iterations, and thinning.
- Lacks robustness studies across sequence length T, state dimensionality, prior choices, and comparisons of ancestor sampling vs alternatives.
- No wall-clock runtime or memory vs accuracy trade-offs, nor ablation studies (vanilla vs sparse GP; collapsed vs parametric).
- Comparisons are mainly demonstrations; quantitative benchmarks against EM/MAP, variational, EP, or other PMCMC variants are missing.
- Little practical guidance on initialization, hyperparameter tuning (e.g., slice sampler settings), resampling schedules, or path-degeneracy failure modes.
- Experiments are illustrative but small-scale; more diverse and higher-dimensional real-world datasets are needed to support significance claims.
- Methodology is sound and well-connected to PMCMC theory, but empirical support for efficiency and robustness is insufficient.
- Paper is generally clear with useful implementation notes but needs clearer guidance on parameter settings and diagnostic practices.
- The contribution is a neat synthesis (collapsing + PGAS) and valuable implementation, rather than a fundamentally new algorithmic advance.
- Potentially impactful for learning flexible SSMs where multimodality matters, but not demonstrated as state-of-the-art across benchmarks.
- Suggested additions: thorough mixing/sensitivity studies (ESS, ACF), ancestor-sampling metrics, and acceptance/refresh rates.
- Suggested additions: runtime/accuracy trade-offs and ablations (sparse vs dense, mean functions, collapsing vs parametric).
- Suggested additions: include competitive baselines with consistent metrics and provide tuning recommendations.
- Suggested additions: release code or detailed pseudocode for key kernels and factorization reuse to aid reproducibility.
- Pros for acceptance: principled fully Bayesian approach, well-motivated PGAS use, and useful implementation insights including sparse GP integration.
- Cons against acceptance: empirical section lacks rigorous PMCMC evaluation, limited evidence of scalability/robustness, and insufficient practical guidance.
- Overall: Solid, straightforward application of PMCMC to GP-SSMs with promising demonstrations, but needs deeper, critical empirical evaluation to substantiate practical claims.
