- Paper presents a Bayesian approach for joint state and parameter estimation using a Gaussian process prior for transition dynamics and PMCMC with ancestor sampling for inference.  
- Technical approach includes computational efficiency strategies via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- The paper is technically sound, clearly presented, and overall a strong contribution.  
- Online marginalization of transition dynamics using ancestor sampling is a novel contribution.  
- Consideration of computational issues (sparsity, low-rank updates) materially strengthens the work.  
- Empirical results are concise but generally sufficient, though some clarifications and extensions are suggested.  
- The work should stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs between learning dynamics versus specifying parametric models a priori.  
- Recommend expanding discussion comparing a priori parametric transition models versus using such models as GP mean functions, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.  
- Table 1 is unclear about whether RMSE values are in-sample or out-of-sample; adding a withheld-data forecast RMSE column is requested.  
- Minor issue: abbreviation CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before being defined.
