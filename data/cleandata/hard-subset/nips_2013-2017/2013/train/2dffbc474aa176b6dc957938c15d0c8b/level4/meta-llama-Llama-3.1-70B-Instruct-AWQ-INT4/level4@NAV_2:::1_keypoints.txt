- Bayesian approach for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a GP prior for transition dynamics.  
- Inference via PMCMC with ancestor sampling; online marginalisation of the transition dynamics is a novel contribution.  
- Considers computational efficiencies through sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Technically sound, clearly written, well-structured, with effective figures and tables.  
- Strength: enables fully Bayesian, nonparametric GP-SSM learning by marginalising the transition function.  
- Uses a tailored particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) to solve the resulting nontrivial inference problem.  
- Empirical results are promising and sufficient; sparse GP shown effective at reducing computational cost.  
- Suggestion: improve presentation by comparing learnt dynamics to ground truth (e.g., plot learnt vs true dynamics).  
- Suggestion: add discussion on differences between using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean function, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.  
- Clarify Table 1: unclear whether RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample; recommend adding out-of-sample/forecast RMSE.  
- Minor issue: CPF-AS abbreviation appears in Algorithm 1 before it is defined in Sec. 3.3.1.  
- Overall: strong, novel paper likely to stimulate discussion on inference methods for non-Markovian SSMs and trade-offs of learning transitions nonparametrically.
