- Paper applies particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs) to Gaussian process state-space models with original, model-specific developments.  
- Writing and presentation are strong; contribution is interesting and original.  
- Notable methodological development: sparse GP-SSM implementation.  
- Major omission: no empirical comparisons to established particle MCMC methods, especially PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.  
- Comparisons to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would be straightforward to implement and are essential for context.  
- Results lack algorithmic-efficiency metrics (e.g., ACF, ESS) reported as functions of particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Unclear how the required number of particles N scales with T; paper should clarify whether N scales linearly or sublinearly with T.  
- Theoretical note: PMMH likely requires O(T^2) particles while particle Gibbs variants typically need sublinear growth in N with T.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed application that would be substantially strengthened by the recommended comparisons and scaling analyses.
