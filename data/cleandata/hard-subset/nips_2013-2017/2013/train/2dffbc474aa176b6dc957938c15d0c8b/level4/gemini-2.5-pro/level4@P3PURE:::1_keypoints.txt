- Proposes a Bayesian method for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models with GP priors for transition dynamics.
- Uses particle MCMC with ancestor sampling for inference; online marginalization of transition dynamics via ancestor sampling is a key novel contribution.
- Addresses computational efficiency through sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.
- Presentation is clear, technically sound, and accessible.
- Empirical results are brief but adequate to demonstrate potential.
- Work should stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models.
- Raises important questions about nonparametric (learned) vs pre-specified parametric transition models.
- Suggests adding discussion comparing a pre-specified parametric transition model to using the same model as the GP mean, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.
- Requests clarification on Table 1 RMSE: whether it is in-sample (conditioned data) or out-of-sample; recommends adding out-of-sample forecast RMSE.
- Minor issue: abbreviation CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.
- Overall verdict: strong, novel paper likely to stimulate interesting discussion.
