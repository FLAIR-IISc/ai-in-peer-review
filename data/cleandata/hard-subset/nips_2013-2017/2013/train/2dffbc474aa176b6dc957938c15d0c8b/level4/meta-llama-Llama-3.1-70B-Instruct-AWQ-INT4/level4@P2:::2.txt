The authors propose a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models by placing a Gaussian process prior over the state transition dynamics. This approach is novel and has the potential to provide a flexible model for capturing complex dynamical phenomena. The use of particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) samplers to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution is also a significant contribution.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their methodology. The introduction of the sparse GP-SSM is also a useful contribution, as it can greatly reduce computational complexity. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in learning nonlinear systems.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, it would be beneficial to include comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, such as the particle marginal Metropolis Hastings (PMMH) scheme and the particle Gibbs with backward sampling. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed algorithm's performance and help to establish its strengths and weaknesses.

Additionally, the authors could provide more informative results by including graphs that display the performance of the algorithms as a function of the number of particles (N) and the length of the time series (T). This would help to clarify how the algorithm's performance scales with the size of the problem.

The authors' use of the particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS) algorithm is a good choice, as it is well-suited to the problem at hand. However, it would be interesting to see how the proposed algorithm compares to other particle Gibbs variants, such as the particle Gibbs with backward sampling.

In terms of the quality of the paper, it is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of their methodology and provide sufficient background information for readers who may not be familiar with the topic. The experimental results are also well-presented and provide a clear demonstration of the proposed approach's effectiveness.

Overall, the paper is a good contribution to the field, and with a few minor improvements, it has the potential to be a significant contribution.
