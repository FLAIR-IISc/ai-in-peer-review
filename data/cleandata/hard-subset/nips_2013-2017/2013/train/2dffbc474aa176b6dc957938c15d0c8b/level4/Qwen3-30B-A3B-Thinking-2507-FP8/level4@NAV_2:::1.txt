

This paper introduces a fully Bayesian framework for inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models, using Gaussian process priors over state transition dynamics and marginalizing the transition function via tailored Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) samplers with ancestor sampling. The work builds upon prior NIPS contributions such as Deisenroth et al. (2011) on GP-based filtering/smoothing and Turner et al. (2010) on parametric GP-SSM learning, while addressing a key gap in fully nonparametric dynamics learning. The authors achieve this by marginalizing the transition function, enabling efficient smoothing without assuming a fixed model structure, and leverage sparse GP implementations with low-rank Cholesky updates for computational efficiency (Section 4).

The paper excels in technical quality: the marginalization approach preserves full nonparametric expressivity, the PMCMC with ancestor sampling (CPF-AS) provides accurate smoothing despite non-Markovian dependencies, and the sparse GP implementation achieves O(M²T) complexity. The clarity is excellent, with precise algorithmic descriptions (e.g., Algorithm 1) and well-structured exposition of the non-Gaussian marginal prior (Equation 4). Originality is strong—this is the first work to fully marginalize the transition dynamics while enabling posterior inference over both states and the GP function, contrasting with prior parametric GP-SSM approaches (Turner et al., Wang et al.). Significance is demonstrated through compelling experiments: Figure 2 shows accurate smoothing even when the mean function is misspecified, and Table 1 confirms superior predictive performance over parametric baselines in the nonlinear benchmark. The cart-pole experiment (Figure 3) further validates the model’s ability to capture state-space uncertainty.

Strengths:  
- Novel marginalization technique enabling nonparametric dynamics learning  
- Rigorous computational analysis with sparse GP efficiency gains  
- Clear empirical validation of smoothing accuracy and predictive capability  
- Strong theoretical grounding in GP-SSM inference (Sections 3.1–3.3)  

Weaknesses:  
- Table 1’s RMSE calculation lacks clarity: it is ambiguous whether results use in-sample or out-of-sample data (e.g., "f(xₜ,uₜ)|data" vs. withheld test set). For meaningful comparison, an additional column reporting out-of-sample forecasting RMSE would be essential, as this is where dynamics learning should demonstrate its greatest advantage.  
- The acronym "CPF-AS" appears in Algorithm 1 without definition in Section 3.3.1 (first mention), requiring readers to backtrack to Section 3.3.1 for clarification.  

The paper makes a significant contribution to Bayesian inference for state-space models, directly addressing a critical limitation in prior work. The technical depth, computational insights, and empirical results meet NIPS standards for quality, clarity, and significance. Minor revisions to clarify the RMSE metric and acronym usage would strengthen an already strong submission. This work will stimulate valuable discussion on non-Markovian inference and nonparametric dynamics learning.
