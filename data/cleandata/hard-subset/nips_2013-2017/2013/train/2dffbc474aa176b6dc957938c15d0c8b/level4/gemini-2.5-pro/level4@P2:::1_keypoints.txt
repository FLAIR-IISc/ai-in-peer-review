- Introduces a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing a GP prior on the transition function and marginalizing it out to infer state trajectories directly.  
- Marginalization breaks the Markov property, inducing long-range temporal dependencies in the latent state sequence.  
- Employs Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample from the joint smoothing distribution under the non‑Markovian prior.  
- Incorporates sparse GP approximations to reduce computational complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Core idea of marginalizing the GP transition is novel and significant, avoiding parametric or MAP approximations.  
- Provides a principled, fully Bayesian treatment that complements existing methods for GP-SSMs.  
- Method is technically sound with a clear derivation of the marginal prior over state trajectories.  
- Use of PGAS in the non‑Markovian setting is a clever and appropriate inference choice.  
- Paper includes practical implementation details (sparse GPs, efficient Cholesky updates) that strengthen feasibility.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear motivation, model description, and stepwise development of the algorithm.  
- Suggest expanding discussion on the role of the GP mean function versus standalone parametric models and its effect on forecasting.  
- Clarify experimental reporting: state whether reported RMSEs are in-sample or out-of-sample and add multi-step-ahead prediction errors for stronger validation.  
- Minor presentation fix: define the abbreviation "CPF-AS" at first use in Algorithm 1.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel contribution likely to influence future work; main shortcoming is the need for clearer and more compelling out-of-sample experimental validation.
