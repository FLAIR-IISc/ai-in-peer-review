- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function and performing inference with tailored PMCMC samplers (PGAS).  
- Key innovation: marginalizing the transition dynamics enables nonparametric modeling while reducing computational burden.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling to handle the non-Markovian dependencies introduced by marginalization.  
- Preserves full nonparametric expressivity of the model without resorting to parametric approximations.  
- Reduces computational complexity via sparse GP approximations and efficient Cholesky factor updates.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical derivation of the marginal prior for state trajectories (Equations 3–4).  
- Technical contributions are clearly motivated and well-presented, with elegant handling of inference challenges.  
- Empirical validation on a nonlinear system and cart-pole benchmarks shows ability to capture multimodal smoothing and meaningful uncertainty.  
- Table 1 results are clear but RMSE calculation is ambiguous—unclear if reported metrics are in-sample or out-of-sample.  
- Recommends adding out-of-sample/forecasting results or clarifying whether Table 1 shows held-out performance.  
- Paper should discuss more deeply the trade-offs between using a parametric mean function versus learning the full GP transition.  
- Minor issue: abbreviation CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, strong contribution to GP-SSM learning; recommended for acceptance with minor clarifications.
