- Proposes a Bayesian method for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a Gaussian process prior for transition dynamics.
- Performs inference with particle MCMC augmented by ancestor sampling.
- Online marginalization of transition dynamics enabled by ancestor sampling is a novel contribution.
- Careful treatment of computational efficiency via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates strengthens the paper.
- Empirical results are brief but generally sufficient, though some clarifications are needed.
- The work should stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs of learning dynamics versus using parametric models.
- Recommend discussing differences between using a fixed parametric transition model and using that model as the GP mean, especially for out-of-sample prediction/forecasting.
- Table 1 and its description are unclear about whether reported RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample; add an RMSE column for withheld/forecasted data if missing.
- Minor issue: define the abbreviation CPF-AS at first use in Algorithm 1/Section 3.3.1.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clear presentation, novel, and a strong paper likely to stimulate interesting discussion.
