- Introduces a fully Bayesian approach for nonlinear state-space models using a GP prior on transitions, marginalizing the transition function with PMCMC and ancestor sampling.  
- Tackles computational challenges via sparse GP approximations and low-rank/optimized Cholesky updates.  
- Online marginalization combined with ancestor sampling is a novel and significant contribution.  
- Paper is technically rigorous and well presented.  
- Attention to computational efficiency enhances practicality.  
- Empirical validation is concise and supportive but limited in scope.  
- Work can spur discussion on inference for non-Markovian systems and trade-offs between learning dynamics versus specifying parametric models.  
- Suggest authors discuss differences between using a parametric transition model directly versus as the GP mean function, particularly for out-of-sample prediction.  
- RMSE interpretation in Table 1 is unclear (held-out vs. conditioning data); recommend providing out-of-sample/forecast RMSE, e.g., an extra column.  
- Minor formatting issue: CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before its definition in Section 3.3.1.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, computational efficiency, and potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical evaluation and need for clarification of RMSE results.  
- Overall verdict: strong, well-executed paper; recommend acceptance.
