- Introduces a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing GP priors on transition dynamics and marginalizing the transition function f.  
- Uses PMCMC with ancestor sampling (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling, PGAS) to sample from the intractable joint smoothing distribution.  
- Preserves full nonparametric expressivity of the GP model by marginalizing f instead of using parametric approximations.  
- Improves computational efficiency using sparse GP (FIC prior) and low-rank Cholesky updates, reducing complexity from O(T^4) to O(T^3) or O(M^2T).  
- Contrasts with prior GP-SSM work by enabling learning of GP dynamics rather than assuming a pre-learned GP or parametric f.  
- Builds on PMCMC and ancestor sampling theory to handle non-Markovian dependencies introduced by marginalization.  
- Technical approach is rigorous and marginalization is soundly justified.  
- PGAS sampler is carefully tailored for mixing in non-Markovian settings.  
- Computational analysis is thorough with clear complexity bounds and implementation details.  
- Empirical results show accurate multimodal smoothing and outperform parametric baselines, even with misspecified mean functions.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and accessible to a broad ML audience.  
- The marginalization + PGAS combination is novel and addresses limitations of prior GP-SSM methods.  
- Method has significant practical impact for uncertainty-aware dynamics learning in applications like robotics and control.  
- Weakness: RMSE reporting in Table 1 is ambiguous—unclear if values are in-sample or out-of-sample; recommend explicit out-of-sample evaluation.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion on trade-offs of using a parametric mean function versus purely nonparametric GP.  
- Weakness: Minor readability issue—CPF-AS abbreviation used before being defined.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong, impactful paper with minor empirical clarity issues; reviewer recommends acceptance.
