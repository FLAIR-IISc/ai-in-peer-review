- Proposes a fully Bayesian approach for nonlinear SSMs by placing GP priors on transition dynamics and marginalizing them via PMCMC (PGAS).  
- Core innovation: tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling to infer the joint smoothing distribution while preserving nonparametric GP expressivity.  
- Allows closed-form posterior inference for transition dynamics after obtaining smoothing samples and uses sparse FIC GPs to reduce complexity to O(M²T).  

- Strength: Technically significant—marginalizing GP dynamics avoids restrictive parametric approximations common in prior GP-SSM work.  
- Strength: Demonstrates GP prior smoothness can yield accurate smoothing even with misspecified mean functions.  
- Strength: Rigorous justification and clear algorithmic presentation of PGAS and a novel sparse GP implementation.  
- Strength: Experiments show ability to capture multimodal smoothing and complex dynamics (e.g., cart-pole), outperforming parametric baselines.  
- Strength: Clear exposition with well-structured derivations and implementation details.  

- Weakness: Insufficient evaluation of practical PMCMC challenges—no mixing diagnostics (autocorrelation, effective sample size) for states or hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: No ablation on particle count (N) or inducing points (M) to show accuracy vs. computation trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Sparse GP section omits analysis of inducing-point selection strategies (greedy vs. random).  
- Weakness: Experimental scope is small-scale (T=200, 100 observations); lacks large-scale/real-world validation and runtime/scaling analysis.  

- Originality: Highly original unified Bayesian treatment enabling fully nonparametric SSM learning and uncertainty-aware dynamics modeling.  
- Significance: Important contribution but significance is tempered by limited large-scale empirical validation.  

- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing evaluation gaps (add PMCMC diagnostics, sparse GP ablations, and computational scaling analysis) in the final version.
