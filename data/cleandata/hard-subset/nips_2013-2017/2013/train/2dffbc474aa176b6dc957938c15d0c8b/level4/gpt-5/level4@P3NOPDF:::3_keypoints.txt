- Summary: Applies PMCMC to GP-based state-space models to jointly infer latent states and parameters; mainly an application with illustrative experiments.
- Strength: Clear, straightforward use of PMCMC in a nontrivial GP-SSM setting of community interest.
- Strength: The problem—joint state and parameter inference in flexible nonparametric dynamical systems—is relevant.
- Strength: Exposition is sound and demonstrations show the method can be made to work.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of how particle count affects likelihood estimator variance, particle degeneracy, and MCMC mixing/acceptance.
- Weakness: No sensitivity analysis on proposal design, resampling strategy, or other tuning choices.
- Weakness: Missing diagnostics such as effective sample size, autocorrelation, and convergence measures.
- Weakness: Limited assessment of computational cost and scalability (runtime, memory, scaling with sequence length, dimension, and particle count).
- Weakness: Computational implications of the GP prior (kernel choice, hyperparameter inference, sparse/inducing methods) are not discussed.
- Weakness: No comparisons to alternative methods or baselines (EKF/UKF+EM, SMC^2, particle Gibbs, parametric MCMC/SMC, variational GP-SSMs).
- Weakness: Empirical section is a proof-of-concept rather than systematic; lacks quantification of failure modes or robustness to misspecification.
- Weakness: No ablation to separate the effects of the GP prior from the inference scheme.
- Request: Specify the GP prior details (kernel, mean), how hyperparameters are treated, identifiability, and any approximations.
- Request: Describe algorithmic choices in detail (particle filter/resampling, rejuvenation, ancestor sampling, parameter proposals, adaptation, initialization).
- Request: Provide diagnostics—acceptance rates, autocorrelations/ESS, number of particles, and evidence on pseudo-marginal likelihood variance.
- Request: Report sensitivity analyses over particle count, sequence length, noise levels, and misspecification robustness.
- Request: Improve reproducibility by clarifying tuning choices and providing code or sufficient pseudo-code.
- Suggestion: Add systematic empirical studies on synthetic data with ground truth and on real-data scalability and robustness tests.
- Suggestion: Include comparisons to reasonable baselines and alternative PMCMC variants (PMMH vs particle Gibbs/ancestor sampling).
- Suggestion: Report computational metrics and scaling: wall-clock time, ESS/sec, memory, and particle count vs variance trade-offs.
- Suggestion: If using GP approximations, evaluate accuracy–cost trade-offs (e.g., varying inducing points).
- Suggestion: Provide diagnostics and ablations (acceptance rates, trace plots, ESS, R-hat, sensitivity to initialization and proposals).
- Suggestion: Discuss failure modes and provide practical guidance (when PMCMC struggles and how to choose particle numbers/proposal scales).
- Suggestion: Better situate the work in related literature and clarify what general lessons this case study provides.
- Overall assessment: Feasible and well-presented application of PMCMC to GP-SSMs but lacks comparative analysis, diagnostics, and systematic evaluation to establish practical value.
