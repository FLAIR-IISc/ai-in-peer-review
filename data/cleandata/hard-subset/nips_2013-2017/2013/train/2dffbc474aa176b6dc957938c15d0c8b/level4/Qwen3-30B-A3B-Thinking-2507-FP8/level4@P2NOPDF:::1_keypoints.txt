- Introduces a Bayesian framework for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a GP prior on transition dynamics.
- Uses particle MCMC with ancestor sampling to marginalize transition dynamics online without losing inference tractability.
- Improves computational efficiency via sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates for covariance matrices.
- Technically robust and exceptionally well-presented work.
- Core novelty is the integration of ancestor sampling to marginalize transitions while retaining tractable inference.
- Computational pragmatism (sparsity + low-rank updates) substantially improves scalability and applicability.
- Empirical validation is concise but adequate; minor clarifications would strengthen the results.
- Suggest expanding discussion on trade-offs between GP-learned dynamics and parametric models, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.
- Table 1 is unclear about whether RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample; recommend adding an out-of-sample (*|test*) RMSE column.
- Minor editorial issue: define the acronym "CPF-AS" at first use (appears after Algorithm 1).
- Overall a strong, novel contribution with significant theoretical and practical merit; recommend publication pending minor clarifications.
