- Summary: Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the GP transition f to obtain a non-Markovian trajectory prior and uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) for joint state and hyperparameter inference.  
- Predictive: After sampling trajectories, predictive for f is a mixture-of-Gaussians via standard GP regression; introduces sparse FIC to reduce complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T).  
- Implementation: Provides practical implementation details (rank-one Cholesky updates, factor reuse) to make the sampler feasible.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on PMCMC/PGAS and known GP/GP-SSM methods (EM/MAP, EP-based GPDS), emphasizing fully Bayesian collapsed inference.  
- Quality (positive): Modeling and algorithmic development are sound; collapsing f is well motivated and PGAS is appropriate with correct theoretical claims.  
- Quality (negative): Empirical evaluation is limited — lacks mixing diagnostics, runtimes, memory profiling, and sensitivity analyses for N, M, T.  
- Comparisons: Experimental baselines focus on parametric methods and omit prior GP-SSM learning/inference approaches and alternative particle smoothers.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and well organized; derivations and implementation notes are helpful but Section 3.1 needs an intuitive remark and CPF-AS pseudo-code would improve readability.  
- Originality: Novelty is mainly in combining established components (collapsed GP, PGAS, FIC) into a practical inference procedure rather than introducing fundamentally new algorithms.  
- Significance: Relevant to time-series/control and potentially useful in practice, but impact is limited by weak empirical validation and scalability evidence.  
- Strengths: Principled Bayesian treatment, appropriate use of PGAS, practical sparse variant, and useful implementation contributions.  
- Weaknesses: Missing mixing diagnostics, runtime/memory/sensitivity studies, lacking comparisons to GP-SSM baselines, no ablation studies, and limited guidance on inducing-point/hyperprior choices or reproducibility.  
- Suggestions: Add convergence diagnostics (ACF, ESS), runtime/memory results, sensitivity studies, comparisons to EM/EP-based methods and non-collapsed samplers, ablations for collapsing/AS/FIC, scalability experiments, and guidance/code release.  
- Acceptance pro: Coherent, principled integration with practical implementation details that could benefit the community.  
- Acceptance con: Insufficient empirical validation, incremental novelty, and unclear scalability/robustness in realistic settings.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject pending substantially stronger empirical evaluation and comparisons.
