- Proposes a fully Bayesian PMCMC approach for inference and learning in GP-SSMs, inferring transition function, state trajectory, and hyperparameters.  
- Achieves this by marginalizing out the state transition function, creating a challenging inference problem solved with a tailored PMCMC sampler.  
- Provides a clear and concise introduction to GP-SSMs and their inference/learning challenges.  
- Offers a thorough literature review and clearly highlights the paper’s contributions relative to prior work.  
- Presents technical details well, including a detailed description of the marginalization and PMCMC implementation.  
- Demonstrates the method on two benchmarks (a nonlinear system and cart-and-pole), showing effective learning and accurate predictions.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and focused on demonstrating application rather than thorough assessment.  
- Paper lacks a detailed evaluation of practical challenges in getting the PMCMC scheme to work and tuning/robustness issues.  
- Missing a more comprehensive experimental comparison and deeper analysis of strengths and weaknesses in practice.  
- Overall a solid and straightforward extension of existing work, but would benefit from more detailed empirical and practical evaluation.
