- Introduces a Bayesian method for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a GP prior for transition dynamics with PMCMC and ancestor sampling.  
- Novel contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics enabled by ancestor sampling.  
- Paper is technically sound and clearly presented.  
- Discusses computational efficiencies, notably sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Empirical results are brief but sufficient to support claims; more analysis would strengthen the work.  
- Suggests expanding discussion comparing a priori parametric transition models versus using such models as the GP mean function, especially for forecasting/out-of-sample performance.  
- Table 1 is unclear about whether RMSE is evaluated in-sample or on withheld data; recommends adding an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column.  
- Minor issue: CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel paper likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models.
