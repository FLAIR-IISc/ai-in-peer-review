

The authors present a clear and original application of particle MCMC to Gaussian process state-space models, specifically leveraging the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm from Lindsten et al. Their development of sparse GP-SSM methodology is also valuable. However, the paper has a notable limitation: it omits comparisons to key particle MCMC variants, particularly particle marginal MH (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.). These comparisons would be straightforward to implement and are essential for evaluating the proposed approach's relative merits.  

The current results lack sufficient detail to assess computational efficiency. For instance, it remains unclear how to scale the number of particles $N$ with the sequence length $T$. Including performance metrics such as autocorrelation functions (ACF) or effective sample sizes (ESS) as functions of $N$ and $T$ would clarify this. Without such analysis, the results do not adequately demonstrate the algorithm's practical utility.  

Critically, the authors should address the known scaling behaviors of competing methods. As established in Whiteley et al., PMMH typically requires a number of particles growing quadratically with $T$, while particle Gibbs variants (including the proposed method) generally scale sublinearly. Explicitly contrasting these scaling properties would strengthen the paper's contribution.  

The paper would be significantly improved by adding these comparisons and performance analyses. The core application is sound and well-motivated, but the absence of benchmarking against established particle MCMC schemes weakens the evaluation. Addressing these points would provide a more comprehensive assessment of the proposed algorithm's strengths and limitations in practice.
