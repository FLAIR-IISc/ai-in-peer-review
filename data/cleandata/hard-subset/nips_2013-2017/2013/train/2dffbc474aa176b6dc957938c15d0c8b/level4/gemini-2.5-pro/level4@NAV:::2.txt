This paper presents an application of particle MCMC methods for inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs). The authors specifically employ the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm proposed by Lindsten et al. The work is clearly articulated and represents an interesting and original application of this methodology. A further contribution is the development of a model-specific adaptation, the sparse GP-SSM.

A significant weakness, however, is the absence of a comparative analysis against other relevant particle MCMC schemes. In particular, a comparison to Particle Marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and Particle Gibbs with backward sampling (as in Whiteley et al.) is warranted. These alternative algorithms could be implemented with reasonable effort, and an empirical comparison of their performance against the proposed method would be highly informative.

Furthermore, the experimental evaluation would be strengthened by a more thorough performance characterization. The inclusion of graphs illustrating metrics like the autocorrelation function (ACF) or effective sample size (ESS) as a function of the number of particles (N) and the time series length (T) is recommended. The results as presented are not very informative about the crucial scaling relationship between N and T needed to maintain a given level of performance.

It is my conjecture that for this class of models, PMMH would require the number of particles N to scale quadratically with T, as has been observed previously by Whiteley et al. In contrast, both the proposed particle Gibbs algorithm and the backward sampling variant would be expected to require only sublinear growth in N with respect to T.

In conclusion, this is a well-written contribution applying particle MCMC to GP state-space models. The paper's impact could be substantially enhanced by a rigorous empirical comparison between the proposed algorithm and established alternatives like PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
