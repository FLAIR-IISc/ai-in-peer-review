The authors present a novel application of particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods to inference within Gaussian process state-space models, building upon the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm introduced by Lindsten et al. This work is a clear, original, and potentially impactful contribution, further strengthened by the development of useful model-specific techniques, notably a sparse GP-SSM implementation.

However, a significant limitation is the absence of comparative analysis against established particle MCMC alternatives. Specifically, benchmarking against particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (e.g., as detailed in Whiteley et al.) would substantially strengthen the paper. Implementing these methods appears feasible and would provide valuable insight into the relative performance of the proposed approach – likely without requiring substantial additional effort from the authors.

Furthermore, the presented results lack sufficient detail to fully assess algorithm scalability. Inclusion of performance metrics (such as autocorrelation function or effective sample size) plotted as a function of both *N* and *T* would greatly enhance interpretability. Understanding the relationship between *N* and *T* – whether linear, sublinear, or otherwise – is crucial. Based on prior work (e.g., Whiteley et al.), it is reasonable to expect PMMH to exhibit quadratic scaling with *T*, while particle Gibbs methods should scale sublinearly.

In summary, this is a well-written and technically sound application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. The manuscript would be considerably improved by the inclusion of comparative results against PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
