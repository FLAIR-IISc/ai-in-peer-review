The paper applies PMCMC to a state-space model with a Gaussian process prior, extending inference to static parameters rather than limiting it to filtering and smoothing. This is a sensible and straightforward application of established PMCMC methodology.

However, the paper does not provide a sufficiently thorough account of the practical challenges involved in making the PMCMC scheme work in this setting. This omission is unfortunate, as such an evaluation would likely constitute the most meaningful contribution. A more detailed and critical assessment of the approach’s strengths and weaknesses—what works well, what fails, and under what conditions—would substantially increase the paper’s value, especially given that it is primarily an application of existing methods.

As presented, the experimental section functions more as a demonstration than an evaluation. It would be far more informative to analyze the practical difficulties encountered in implementation and tuning, the computational costs, the robustness of the method, and how well it actually performs in practice. Even if a full redesign of the experiments is not feasible, the paper would benefit from deeper empirical analysis and discussion that move beyond illustrative results.

In summary, this is a clear and competent application of PMCMC, but the contribution is limited by the lack of a critical, detailed evaluation of the method’s practical performance and limitations.
