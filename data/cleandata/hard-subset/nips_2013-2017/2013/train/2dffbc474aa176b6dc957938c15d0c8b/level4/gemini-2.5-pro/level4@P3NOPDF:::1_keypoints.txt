- Introduces Bayesian joint state and parameter estimation with non-parametric transition dynamics modeled by a Gaussian process (GP).
- Uses particle MCMC (PMCMC) with ancestor sampling to enable online marginalization of the GP in inference.
- Proposes practical computational efficiencies via sparsity and low-rank updates/downdates to Cholesky factors.
- Paper is technically sound, clearly presented, and the online marginalization within PMCMC is the primary novelty.
- Method provides flexible, data-driven learning of unknown dynamics and may stimulate discussion on non-Markovian inference.
- Suggestion: add a detailed discussion comparing pre-specified parametric transition models versus using them as the GP mean, especially for extrapolation/out-of-sample prediction.
- Empirical results need clarification: specify whether reported RMSE in Table 1 is in-sample or on a withheld test set, and consider adding out-of-sample forecasting results.
- Minor issue: acronym "CPF-AS" appears in Algorithm 1 header before being defined in Section 3.3.1.
- Overall conclusion: strong, novel contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
