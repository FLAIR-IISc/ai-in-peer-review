- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by analytically marginalizing the GP transition f, yielding a non-Markovian prior over trajectories.
- Performs posterior inference with Particle MCMC, specifically particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling (PGAS), alternating sampling of states x0:T and hyperparameters θ.
- Given x0:T and θ, predictive distributions over f are obtained in closed form as mixtures of Gaussians.
- Introduces computational tricks: reuse of Cholesky factorizations, rank-1 updates/downdates, and a sparse FIC variant achieving O(M^2 T) scaling.
- Contrasts with prior work that uses parametric surrogates + EM/MAP or filtering/smoothing approximations on a learned GP.
- The derivation of the collapsed prior p(x1:T | θ, x0) is correct and the PGAS construction and ancestor sampling weights are appropriate.
- Hyperparameter sampling via slice sampling conditioned on x0:T is sensible; sparse FIC construction and complexity analysis are well motivated.
- Experiments show the method recovers multimodal smoothing distributions and calibrated predictive uncertainty (including a cart-pole example).
- Empirical evaluation is brief; stronger quantitative and out-of-sample forecasting comparisons would strengthen the paper.
- Novelty lies in marginalizing f and using PGAS for fully Bayesian inference in GP-SSMs, combined with a sparse FIC prior.
- Significant because it enables scalable, nonparametric learning of transition dynamics relevant to time series, control, and RL.
- Strength: preserves nonparametric expressivity by marginalizing f rather than using parametric surrogates.
- Strength: effective use of PGAS for non-Markovian trajectories and clear discussion of invariance and mixing.
- Strength: practical computational contributions (Cholesky reuse, rank-1 updates, sparse FIC).
- Suggestion: add out-of-sample forecasting RMSEs (one-step and multi-step) and clarify if Table 1 reports held-out results.
- Suggestion: compare to EM/MAP GP-SSM methods and to particle Gibbs without ancestor sampling to quantify benefits.
- Suggestion: report diagnostics and scalability metrics (runtime, N, ESS, autocorrelation), and sparse variant details (M, inducing selection, accuracy vs M).
- Suggestion: discuss identifiability when f and g are flexible, behavior in higher-dimensional states, non-Gaussian emissions, and sensitivity to hyperpriors.
- Minor fixes: define CPF-AS at first use, clarify Table 1 headings, ensure full references, and prefer “analytic marginalization” over “online marginalization.”
- Overall recommendation: technically solid, original, and significant; recommend acceptance pending expanded empirical comparisons and minor clarifications.
