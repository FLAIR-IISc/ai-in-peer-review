- Introduces a fully Bayesian GP state-space model (GP-SSM) that learns transition dynamics with a Gaussian process prior.
- Uses particle MCMC with ancestor sampling to perform inference and marginalize over the transition function.
- Infers the joint smoothing distribution directly, preserving nonparametric expressivity and enabling sparse GP approximations for efficiency.
- Discusses computational optimizations including sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.
- Use of ancestor sampling for online marginalization is novel and computationally efficient.
- The paper is technically sound, well-presented, and easy to follow.
- Empirical results are brief but sufficient to demonstrate effectiveness.
- Suggests additional discussion comparing parametric transition models versus using them as GP mean functions, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.
- Requests clarification of Table 1: whether reported RMSE is on withheld data or conditioned data.
- Recommends adding an out-of-sample RMSE column (forecast against withheld data).
- Minor issue: abbreviation CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined.
- Overall assessment: high quality, original, and a valuable contribution likely to stimulate discussion.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, effective use of ancestor sampling and sparse GPs, strong empirical demonstration, clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear RMSE reporting, missing out-of-sample evaluation, and the minor abbreviation ordering issue.
