- Presents a fully Bayesian inference and learning method for nonlinear GP-SSMs by analytically marginalizing the GP transition function.  
- Targets the resulting non-Markovian latent-state posterior using Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS).  
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant based on the Fully Independent Conditional (FIC) prior to improve scalability (O(M²T)).  
- Main novelty: preserving nonparametric GP flexibility by marginalizing the dynamics instead of using point estimates or parametric approximations.  
- Technical quality: sound derivation of the marginal prior over state trajectories and appropriate integration with PGAS.  
- Practical extension: sparse formulation meaningfully reduces computational complexity from O(T³) to O(M²T).  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-motivated, and the algorithm is clearly presented.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical comparison to other PMCMC methods (e.g., PMMH, Particle Gibbs with backward sampling) to justify the choice of PGAS.  
- Weakness: missing performance analysis (ESS, ACF) and empirical study of sampler behavior as a function of particle count N and time-series length T.  
- Pro: conceptually elegant and significant contribution to Bayesian learning in GP-SSMs with a principled nonparametric treatment.  
- Con: incomplete experimental validation undermines assessment of practical impact and relative efficiency versus state-of-the-art samplers.
