- Proposes a Bayesian method for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a GP prior for transition dynamics and PMCMC with ancestor sampling.  
- Introduces online marginalization of transition dynamics combined with ancestor sampling as a novel contribution.  
- Discusses computational efficiencies via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and accessible.  
- Empirical results are brief but sufficient to support the contribution.  
- Likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and on learning transition dynamics versus specifying parametric models.  
- Suggests adding a comparison between using a parametric transition model a priori and using that model as the GP mean function, especially for forecasting/out-of-sample prediction.  
- Notes ambiguity in Table 1: unclear whether RMSE is computed on withheld (out-of-sample) data or on the conditioned training set; recommends adding an out-of-sample RMSE column.  
- Minor issue: CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before it is defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel paper likely to provoke interesting discussions.
