- Proposal: apply particle MCMC (ancestral sampling particle Gibbs) for inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSM).

- Novelty: clear and original application of particle MCMC to GP-SSM, distinct from prior GP/PMCMC work by using ancestral sampling particle Gibbs.

- Methodology: development of useful model-specific methods, notably a sparse GP-SSM to reduce computational complexity.

- Empirical claim: experiments demonstrate effectiveness in learning nonlinear dynamics.

- Strength: presentation is clear, paper is well-organized, and theoretically/experimentally sound.

- Major omission: no comparison to other particle MCMC schemes such as particle marginal MH (PMMH) or particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).

- Recommendation: implement and compare PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling—valuable and relatively straightforward.

- Result informativeness: current results lack analysis of scaling with number of particles (N) and time horizon (T).

- Recommendation: include plots of performance metrics (e.g., ACF, ESS) as functions of N and T to clarify algorithmic scaling and efficiency.

- Literature note: Whiteley et al. suggest PMMH may require particles scaling quadratically with T, whereas particle Gibbs variants scale sublinearly—this comparison should be discussed/validated.

- Overall assessment: promising and potentially strong contribution, but needs additional comparisons and more comprehensive evaluation to increase impact.
