- Proposes a fully Bayesian approach for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using a GP prior over transitions and PMCMC, marginalizing the transition function to infer the joint smoothing distribution.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM work and addresses limitations of finite GP approximations that constrain model flexibility.  
- Preserves the GP’s full nonparametric expressivity, helping with identifiability when both dynamics and observations are flexible.  
- Rigorous Bayesian treatment of the SSM and principled marginalization of transition dynamics are key methodological contributions.  
- Effective use of PMCMC and recent sampling advances to handle challenging inference.  
- Inclusion of a sparse GP variant improves scalability.  
- Experiments demonstrate feasibility: learning complex dynamics and capturing multimodal smoothing, though tasks are relatively simple.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical PMCMC implementation and tuning (mixing diagnostics, hyperparameter sensitivity, computational cost).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and depth and serves more as demonstration than rigorous comparison.  
- Needs a more comprehensive comparison and critical evaluation against alternative GP-SSM inference methods.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and significant theoretical/methodological contribution that, with minor revisions addressing practical and experimental concerns, is suitable for NIPS.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled Bayesian approach, effective PMCMC marginalization, preservation of nonparametric expressivity, demonstrated feasibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient practical PMCMC discussion, limited experiments, lack of comprehensive comparisons.
