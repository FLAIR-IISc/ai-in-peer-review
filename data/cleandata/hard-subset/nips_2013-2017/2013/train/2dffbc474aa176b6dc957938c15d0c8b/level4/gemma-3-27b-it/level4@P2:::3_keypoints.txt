- Presents a fully Bayesian GP-SSM that marginalizes the transition function and uses PMCMC for joint smoothing and learning.
- Extends prior GP-SSM work by providing a truly nonparametric approach and supporting sparse GPs for computational efficiency.
- Methodology is technically sound and clearly explained, building logically on existing PMCMC and GP literature.
- Authors demonstrate familiarity with related work and provide useful implementation details and complexity discussion (Sections 4.1 & 4.2).
- Experiments on a synthetic benchmark and cart-pole system show promising applicability.
- Paper lacks critical discussion of practical PMCMC implementation and tuning challenges.
- Insufficient evaluation of PMCMC performance: missing convergence diagnostics, mixing analysis, hyperparameter sensitivity, and detailed computational cost.
- Experimental section is more demonstrative than rigorously evaluative, leaving robustness and scalability unclear.
- Need clearer exploration of accuracy vs. computational cost trade-offs, especially for sparse GP variants.
- Comparisons to existing methods are present but not comprehensive enough for strong benchmarking.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, technically sound, and practically oriented contribution to Bayesian SSMs.
- Argument against acceptance: inadequate PMCMC evaluation and limited experimental scope undermine confidence in robustness and scalability.
- Overall: interesting and valuable work but requires additional PMCMC analysis and expanded experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions contingent on these fixes.
