- Proposes a particle MCMC approach for Gaussian process state-space models using ancestral sampling particle Gibbs (AS-PG).
- Paper is clearly written and presents a novel, interesting application of particle MCMC.
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM as a useful model-specific contribution.
- Major weakness: lacks empirical comparison to standard particle MCMC baselines (PMMH and PG-BS).
- Comparison to PMMH and PG-BS would be straightforward and is essential for contextual evaluation.
- Experimental evaluation lacks systematic analysis of algorithmic performance (e.g., ACF or ESS vs. number of particles N and time length T).
- Current results give limited insight into practical scaling behavior and whether N must scale linearly or sublinearly with T.
- This scaling analysis is important because PMMH is known to require particles that grow quadratically with T, while AS-PG and PG-BS are expected to require only sublinear growth.
- Overall: well-written and interesting, but would be significantly stronger with rigorous comparisons to PMMH/PG-BS and a thorough scaling analysis.
