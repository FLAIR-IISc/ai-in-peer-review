- Introduces a fully Bayesian framework for nonlinear nonparametric state-space models using GP priors and marginalizing the transition via tailored PMCMC with ancestor sampling.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM work (Deisenroth et al., Turner et al.) and fills the gap of fully nonparametric dynamics learning.  
- Marginalizing the transition function enables efficient smoothing without assuming a fixed parametric model structure.  
- Employs sparse GP implementations with low-rank Cholesky updates to achieve O(M²T) computational complexity.  
- Technical quality: marginalization preserves full nonparametric expressivity and CPF-AS provides accurate smoothing despite non-Markovian dependencies.  
- Clarity: well-written with precise algorithmic descriptions (e.g., Algorithm 1) and clear exposition of the non-Gaussian marginal prior.  
- Originality: first work to fully marginalize transition dynamics while inferring both states and the GP function posterior.  
- Significance: experiments show robust smoothing under misspecified mean functions and superior predictive performance versus parametric baselines.  
- Empirical validation: Figure 2 demonstrates smoothing accuracy under misspecification; Table 1 shows improved predictive metrics; cart-pole (Figure 3) captures state-space uncertainty.  
- Strengths summarized: novel marginalization, rigorous computational analysis, clear empirical validation, and strong theoretical grounding.  
- Weakness: Table 1’s RMSE metric is ambiguous about in-sample vs out-of-sample evaluation; an out-of-sample forecasting RMSE column is needed.  
- Weakness: the acronym "CPF-AS" is used in Algorithm 1 without immediate definition, forcing readers to backtrack for clarification.
