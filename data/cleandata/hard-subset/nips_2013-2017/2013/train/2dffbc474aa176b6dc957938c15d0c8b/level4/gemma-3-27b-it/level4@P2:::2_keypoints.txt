- Introduces a novel PMCMC-based approach for Bayesian inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs).
- Uses ancestral sampling particle Gibbs to efficiently sample the smoothing distribution while marginalizing the state transition function.
- Enables a fully nonparametric treatment of dynamics, offering an advantage over parametric SSMs.
- Proposes a sparse GP-SSM construction to improve computational efficiency.
- Well-positioned within prior work, citing relevant GP and SSM literature (e.g., Deisenroth, Turner, Wang).
- Effectively leverages Gaussian processes to model flexible nonlinear dynamics.
- Technically sound with clear, detailed explanations of algorithms and implementations for vanilla GPs and sparse FIC prior.
- Experimental results are preliminary but demonstrate the method’s ability to learn complex dynamics and provide accurate predictions.
- Lacks comprehensive comparisons to alternative particle MCMC schemes such as PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
- Unclear how the proposed method scales with increasing state dimensionality and time series length.
- Missing algorithm performance metrics (e.g., ACF, ESS) as functions of number of particles (N) and time steps (T).
- Would benefit from empirical demonstration of scaling behavior (e.g., PMMH quadratic vs. particle Gibbs sublinear in T as suggested by Whiteley et al.).
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, original sparse GP-SSM contribution, clear writing, and promising experimental demonstrations.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparative analysis and limited presentation of convergence/performance diagnostics.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with potential impact; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address comparative and performance-evaluation concerns.
