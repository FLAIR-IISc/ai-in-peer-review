The authors propose applying particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) to perform inference in Gaussian process state-space models, specifically focusing on the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm of Lindsten et al. This is a clear and interesting, original application of particle MCMC, and the paper develops some useful model-specific methodology, notably a sparse GP-SSM.

A significant omission, however, is the lack of comparison to other prominent particle MCMC schemes, particularly particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (as in Whiteley et al.). Implementing these methods would be relatively straightforward and would provide valuable insight into how the proposed scheme performs in comparison. Such a comparison would not require substantial additional effort from the authors.

Furthermore, the presented results are not particularly informative. I would like to see graphs illustrating algorithm performance (e.g., using autocorrelation function or effective sample size) as a function of both *N* and *T*. The current results leave open questions about the scaling relationship between *N* and *T* – does *N* need to scale linearly, or even sublinearly, with *T*? I anticipate that PMMH would require a number of particles increasing quadratically with *T*, as observed by Whiteley et al., while both particle Gibbs variants should exhibit sublinear scaling. 

Overall, this is a well-written application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. However, the paper would be substantially strengthened by a comparative analysis against PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
