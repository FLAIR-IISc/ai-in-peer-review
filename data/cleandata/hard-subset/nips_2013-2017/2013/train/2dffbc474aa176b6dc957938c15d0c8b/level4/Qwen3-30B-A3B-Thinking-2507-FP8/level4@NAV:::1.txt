

This paper introduces a Bayesian framework for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models, incorporating Gaussian process (GP) priors to learn transition dynamics. Inference leverages particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) with ancestor sampling, while computational efficiency is enhanced through sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates for covariance matrices.  

The work demonstrates strong technical rigor with a clear, accessible presentation. The online marginalization of transition dynamics and the novel application of ancestor sampling to achieve this represent significant contributions. The inclusion of computational considerations—specifically sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates—further strengthens the paper. Empirical results, though concise, are adequate (additional comments follow).  

Beyond its primary objectives, the paper is poised to stimulate discourse on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and the trade-offs of learning transition dynamics versus specifying parametric forms a priori.  

Given space constraints, the authors may wish to elaborate on the distinction between using a predefined parametric transition model versus employing a similar structure as the GP mean function, particularly regarding out-of-sample prediction (e.g., forecasting).  

The description of Table 1 and its associated RMSE values requires clarification. It is unclear whether the reported RMSE is computed on withheld data or the conditioning dataset (denoted by *|data in column headers). To highlight the most meaningful comparison—forecasting performance against withheld future data—the authors should consider adding a dedicated column showing out-of-sample RMSE.  

One minor point: the acronym CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 prior to its definition in Section 3.3.1. This paper is highly novel and will likely foster substantial discussion.
