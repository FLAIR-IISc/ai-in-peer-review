- Proposes a fully Bayesian approach for nonlinear state-space models by placing a Gaussian process prior over the state transition dynamics.  
- Marginalizes out the transition function and samples state trajectories and hyperparameters using a tailored PMCMC algorithm (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling, PGAS).  
- Addresses computational complexity by integrating sparse GPs and efficient low-rank Cholesky updates.  
- Originality: novel and elegant method that preserves the full nonparametric model by marginalizing the dynamics and using PGAS for the non-Markovian problem.  
- Quality: technically sound and practically minded, with solid algorithmic development.  
- Clarity: clearly written and explains the complex inference machinery well.  
- Significance: likely to stimulate further research on inference for non-Markovian models and trade-offs between nonparametric and parametric dynamics.  
- Experimental evaluation concern: RMSE reporting is ambiguous (training vs. held-out); recommend adding out-of-sample forecasting results to demonstrate generalization.  
- Model comparison suggestion: discuss conceptual/practical differences between a fixed parametric transition and using a parametric mean function for the GP, and how these choices affect out-of-sample prediction.  
- Minor presentation issue: the abbreviation CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before it is formally defined.  
- Overall verdict: strong, novel, well-motivated contribution of clear interest to the community.
