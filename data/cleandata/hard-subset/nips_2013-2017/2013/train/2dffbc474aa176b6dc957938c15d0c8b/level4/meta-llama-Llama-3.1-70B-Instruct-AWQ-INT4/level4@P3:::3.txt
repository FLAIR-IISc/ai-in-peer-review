This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models (SSMs) using Gaussian Process (GP) priors. The authors employ a Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) sampler to efficiently sample from the joint smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the state transition function. This approach allows for the preservation of the full nonparametric expressivity of the model.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear overview of the existing literature on GP-based SSMs. The technical contributions of the paper are sound, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on a nonlinear system benchmark and a cart and pole system.

The main strength of the paper is its ability to tackle the challenging problem of learning nonlinear nonparametric SSMs in a fully Bayesian manner. The use of PMCMC samplers is a nice application of existing methodology to a new problem domain. The authors also provide a clear and well-structured presentation of their approach, making it easy to follow.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed and critical evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the approach. While the experimental section demonstrates the effectiveness of the method, it would be more valuable to assess the practical difficulties in getting the PMCMC scheme to work and how well it actually works. A more thorough evaluation of the challenge to get the PMCMC scheme to work would have been a significant contribution to the paper.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the technical soundness is high, and the presentation is clear and well-structured. However, the paper could benefit from more critical evaluation and discussion of the results. The significance of the results is not entirely clear, and a more detailed discussion of the implications of the approach would be beneficial.

In terms of originality, the paper is not highly original, as it builds upon existing PMCMC methodology. However, the application of this methodology to SSMs is a nice contribution, and the authors provide a clear and well-structured presentation of their approach.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a clear presentation of the approach. However, it could benefit from a more detailed and critical evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the approach, as well as a more thorough discussion of the results and their implications.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a clear and well-structured approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric SSMs.
* The use of PMCMC samplers is a nice application of existing methodology to a new problem domain.
* The authors provide a clear overview of the existing literature on GP-based SSMs.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed and critical evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the approach.
* The experimental section is more of a demonstration than a thorough evaluation of the method.
* The paper is not highly original, as it builds upon existing PMCMC methodology.

Recommendation:

I would recommend accepting the paper, but with the suggestion that the authors provide a more detailed and critical evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the approach, as well as a more thorough discussion of the results and their implications.
