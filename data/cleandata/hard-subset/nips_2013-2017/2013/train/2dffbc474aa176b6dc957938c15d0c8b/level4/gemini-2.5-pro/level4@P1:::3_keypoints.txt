- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing a GP prior on the transition function and marginalizing out f, yielding a non‑Markovian model.
- Uses Particle MCMC (Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling) to jointly sample latent states and hyperparameters for smoothing and system identification.
- Incorporates sparse GP techniques to reduce computational complexity.

- Method is principled and elegant, keeping the model fully non‑parametric by marginalizing the dynamics.
- Targeting the full smoothing distribution p(x0:T | y0:T) is a conceptual advantage over finite‑dimensional GP approximations.
- Applying modern PMCMC/PGAS to the induced non‑Markovian inference problem is appropriate and well motivated.
- Paper is well written with clear technical exposition and a practical sparse GP formulation.

- Main weakness is lack of detailed, critical evaluation of the inference scheme and practical implementation challenges.
- Insufficient analysis of PGAS performance: mixing behavior, sensitivity to particle number N, and sampler diagnostics are not explored.
- Experimental section is mainly demonstrative (synthetic and cart‑pole) and lacks quantitative comparisons to state‑of‑the‑art alternatives (e.g., variational or other MCMC GP‑SSM methods).
- Missing a thorough discussion of the method’s limitations, failure modes, and tradeoffs (computational cost vs accuracy) under different conditions.
