- Introduces a Bayesian approach using a Gaussian process prior to learn transition dynamics in nonlinear state-space models.  
- Uses particle MCMC with ancestor sampling (PMCMC+AS) for inference, enabling online marginalization of transition dynamics.  
- Addresses computational efficiency via sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Online marginalization combined with effective ancestor sampling is a genuine novelty.  
- Strong technical presentation and careful attention to computational implementation details.  
- Empirical evaluation is concise but sufficient to demonstrate the method’s potential.  
- Work is likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs of learned vs. parametric transitions.  
- Suggestion: discuss differences between using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean function, especially for forecasting/out-of-sample prediction.  
- Clarify Table 1: specify whether RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample and add an out-of-sample RMSE column for forecasting performance.  
- Minor issue: CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution; recommendation to accept.
