The authors apply particle MCMC to inference in Gaussian process state-space models, focusing on the recent particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling algorithm of Lindsten et al. The paper is clearly written and presents an interesting and original application of particle MCMC. It also develops some useful model-specific methodology, namely a sparse GP-SSM.

A major shortcoming is the absence of comparisons to other particle MCMC methods, in particular particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (as in Whiteley et al.). These baselines seem straightforward to implement, and it would be informative to see how they compare to the proposed scheme (and it should not require much extra work).

I would also like to see plots illustrating algorithmic performance (e.g., ACF or ESS) as functions of N and T. As presented, the results are not very informative. Should N scale linearly with T, or sublinearly?

My understanding is that, for these models, PMMH would need a number of particles growing quadratically with T, as observed by Whiteley et al., whereas both particle Gibbs methods require only sublinear growth in the number of particles with T.

A well-written application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. The paper would be significantly improved by including comparisons to PMMH and to particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
