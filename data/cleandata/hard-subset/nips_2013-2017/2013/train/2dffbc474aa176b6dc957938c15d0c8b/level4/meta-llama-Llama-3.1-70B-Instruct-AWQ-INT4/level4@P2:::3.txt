This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models (SSMs) using Gaussian process (GP) priors. The authors propose a novel method that marginalizes out the transition dynamics function, allowing for efficient inference using a tailored particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) sampler. The approach retains the full nonparametric expressivity of the model, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of learning general nonlinear SSMs. The proposed method is nicely motivated, and the use of GP priors is well-justified. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of the related work, highlighting the key differences between their approach and existing methods.

The technical contributions of the paper are solid, and the authors provide a detailed description of their method, including the derivation of the marginal prior and the PMCMC sampler. The use of a sparse GP model to reduce computational complexity is also a nice touch.

The experimental section is somewhat limited, with only two examples presented. While the results are promising, it would have been nice to see a more comprehensive evaluation of the method, including a comparison with other state-of-the-art approaches. Additionally, the authors could have provided more details on the practical difficulties of implementing the PMCMC scheme and how they overcame any challenges that arose.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference and learning in nonlinear SSMs. However, a more detailed and critical evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the approach would have made the paper even more valuable.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference and learning in nonlinear SSMs.
* The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and a thorough discussion of the related work.
* The technical contributions of the paper are solid, and the authors provide a detailed description of their method.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The experimental section is somewhat limited, and a more comprehensive evaluation of the method would have been nice.
* The authors could have provided more details on the practical difficulties of implementing the PMCMC scheme and how they overcame any challenges that arose.
* A more detailed and critical evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the approach would have made the paper even more valuable.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should consider adding more details on the experimental evaluation and providing a more critical discussion of the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.
