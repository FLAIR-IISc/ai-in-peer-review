- Proposes Bayesian inference and learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models using particle MCMC — novel application.
- Contrasts with prior GP-SSM work by using a fully Bayesian approach rather than approximations.
- Relates to Deisenroth et al. (2012), Turner et al. (2010), and Ghahramani & Roweis (1996).
- Strength: clear and well-structured presentation.
- Strength: develops useful model-specific methodology, notably the sparse GP-SSM.
- Strength: demonstrated on two benchmark problems with effective learning and accurate predictions.
- Weakness: no comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes (e.g., PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling).
- Weakness: results are not sufficiently informative about sensitivity to particle count (N) and time-series length (T).
- Suggestion: provide ACF or ESS plots (and related analyses) as functions of N and T to characterize sampler performance.
- Quality: technically sound with claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Clarity: well-written, easy to follow, and consistently notated.
- Originality: presents a novel approach and an original sparse GP-SSM contribution.
- Significance: tackles an important problem with potential applications across robotics, finance, and healthcare.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing comparisons to other particle MCMC methods and providing more informative performance results.
