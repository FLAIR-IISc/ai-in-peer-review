This paper presents a Bayesian approach to state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models, learning the transition dynamics through a Gaussian process (GP) prior. The inference mechanism is based on particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC), leveraging the recently-introduced idea of ancestor sampling. The paper also discusses computational efficiencies achievable through sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates.

This is a technically sound and strong paper with a clear and accessible presentation. The online marginalization of the transition dynamics and the use of ancestor sampling to achieve this are novel contributions. The consideration of computational issues such as sparsity and low-rank updates/downdates to the Cholesky factors of covariance matrices further strengthens the work. The empirical results, while concise, are sufficient, though some suggestions for expansion are provided below.

Beyond its stated aims, this paper will likely stimulate discussion around inference methods for non-Markovian state-space models, and the potential advantages and disadvantages of learning the transition dynamics in this way compared to specifying a parametric model *a priori*.

While space is limited, the authors might consider expanding the discussion on the differences between using a parametric transition model specified *a priori* versus utilizing a similar model as the mean function within the GP framework. Specifically, examining the implications for out-of-sample prediction, such as forecasting, would be valuable.

The results in Table 1 and the accompanying description are slightly unclear. It is not immediately apparent whether the reported RMSE is calculated against a withheld dataset or the same dataset used for conditioning (*|data* in the column headings).  An RMSE metric based on out-of-sample prediction—particularly a forecast forward in time using withheld data—would be a significant addition, as this scenario is expected to reveal the greatest differences between the learned dynamics and the ground truth model. If Table 1 does not already present this, including an additional column demonstrating this would be beneficial.

One minor point: the abbreviation CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in the first paragraph of Section 3.3.1. 

Overall, this is a strong and novel paper that should stimulate interesting discussion within the community.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a mathematically rigorous and technically sound approach to a challenging problem.
*   **Novelty:** The combination of online marginalization of the transition dynamics with ancestor sampling in PMCMC is a novel contribution.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The authors address practical computational considerations, such as sparsity and low-rank approximations.
*   **Potential Impact:** The work has the potential to advance the field of Bayesian non-parametric state-space modeling.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** While the experiments are sufficient, a more extensive evaluation with different datasets and comparisons to other methods would strengthen the paper.
*   **Minor Clarity Issues:** The description of results in Table 1 could be clearer regarding the evaluation metric.
