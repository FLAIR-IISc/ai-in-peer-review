- Introduces a Bayesian framework for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using Gaussian process priors for transition dynamics.  
- Inference performed with particle MCMC augmented by ancestor sampling.  
- Addresses computational efficiency via sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly presented.  
- Novel contributions: online marginalization of transition dynamics and applying ancestor sampling to achieve this.  
- Strong treatment of computational aspects, which significantly strengthens the work.  
- Empirical results are concise but sufficient, with room for suggested improvements.  
- Likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian SSMs and GP vs parametric transition model trade-offs.  
- Recommend elaborating on using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean function, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.  
- Clarify Table 1: state whether RMSE is out-of-sample or computed on the conditioning data; add an out-of-sample RMSE column if missing.  
- Minor issue: CPF-AS is introduced in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: a strong, novel contribution that will provoke meaningful discussion.
