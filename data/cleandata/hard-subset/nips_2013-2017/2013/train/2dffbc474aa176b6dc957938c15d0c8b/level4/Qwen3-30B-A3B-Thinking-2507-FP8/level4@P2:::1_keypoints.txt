- Presents a fully Bayesian approach to nonlinear SSMs by placing a GP prior on transition dynamics, marginalizing the function, and inferring the joint smoothing distribution with a tailored PMCMC sampler and ancestor sampling.
- Key contributions: (1) novel inference preserving full nonparametric expressivity, (2) computational gains via sparse GP approximations and efficient Cholesky updates, (3) empirical validation on synthetic and real systems.
- Properly situates the work in prior GP-SSM literature and distinguishes itself by learning the GP model rather than relying on parametric transitions or only approximation methods.
- Technical approach is sound with thorough theoretical justification for marginalizing the transition and using PMCMC with ancestor sampling to handle non-Markovian effects.
- Addresses computational challenges with practical implementations reducing complexity from O(T^3) for full GP to O(M^2 T) for sparse GP and efficient matrix updates.
- Empirical results demonstrate the method captures complex dynamics and maintains uncertainty estimates (nonlinear benchmark and cart-pole).
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; mathematical derivations are accessible and the PGAS algorithm presentation is clear.
- Minor clarity issue: the abbreviation CPF-AS is introduced in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.
- Originality: marginalizing the transition function while inferring the smoothing distribution is a significant conceptual advance for GP-SSMs.
- Novel use of ancestor sampling to address non-Markovian structure builds on Lindsten et al. (2012) and solves a practical GP-SSM problem.
- Significance: advances Bayesian inference for SSMs by enabling learning of complex dynamics without restrictive parametric assumptions, with applications in control, robotics, and time-series analysis.
- Strength: conceptual novelty in preserving nonparametric expressivity while marginalizing the transition.
- Strength: rigorous and technically sound treatment of inference challenges.
- Strength: practical computational improvements (sparse GPs, Cholesky updates) making the approach feasible.
- Strength: clear presentation and successful application of PGAS to GP-SSMs.
- Strength: solid empirical validation on both synthetic and real-world systems.
- Weakness: Ambiguity in Table 1 RMSE—unclear whether values are in-sample or out-of-sample; needs clarification.
- Weakness: Lacks sufficient exploration of using a parametric mean function versus fully learned GP transitions and how that affects out-of-sample forecasting.
- Weakness (minor): CPF-AS abbreviation placement should be corrected.
- Suggested improvement: add discussion and results on forecasting (forward prediction) vs smoothing, and include an out-of-sample RMSE column in Table 1.
- Recommendation: Strong, technically sound contribution with theoretical and practical significance; should be accepted for NIPS.
