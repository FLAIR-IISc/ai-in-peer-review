- Proposes a Bayesian framework for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a Gaussian process prior for transition dynamics.  
- Uses particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) with ancestor sampling for inference.  
- Explores computational efficiencies via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-presented, accessible to a broad audience.  
- Online marginalization of transition dynamics through ancestor sampling is a novel contribution.  
- Consideration of computational issues (sparsity, low-rank updates) strengthens the work.  
- Empirical results are brief but sufficient; could be improved with additional experiments.  
- Suggests a deeper comparison between using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.  
- Table 1 and accompanying text are unclear about whether RMSE is evaluated on withheld data or training data; recommend adding out-of-sample/forecast RMSE.  
- Minor issue: CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, innovative paper likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and learned vs parametric dynamics.
