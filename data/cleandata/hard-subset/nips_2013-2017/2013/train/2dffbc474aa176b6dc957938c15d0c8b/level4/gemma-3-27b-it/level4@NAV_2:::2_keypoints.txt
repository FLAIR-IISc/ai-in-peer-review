- Proposes applying particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs) to Gaussian process state-space models.  
- Paper is clear, interesting, and presents an original application of particle MCMC.  
- Development of a sparse GP-SSM variant is a valuable, computationally useful contribution.  
- Main omission: no comparisons to alternative particle MCMC schemes (PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling).  
- Implementing PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would be straightforward and informative.  
- Missing diagnostic plots (ACF, ESS) and performance curves as functions of particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Lacks analysis of scaling behavior—how N must scale with T (linear, sublinear, quadratic) is not addressed.  
- Prior work suggests PMMH may need N growing quadratically with T, while particle Gibbs often scales sublinearly.  
- Overall: well-written and feasible demonstration but would be stronger with comparative and scaling analyses.  
- For acceptance: clear motivation, original application, and useful sparse GP-SSM development with benchmark demonstrations.  
- Against acceptance: no comparisons to established particle MCMC variants, insufficient scaling analysis, and limited performance evaluation to establish advantages.
