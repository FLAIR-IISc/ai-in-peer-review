- Introduces a fully Bayesian inference and learning method for GP-SSMs by marginalizing transition dynamics and using PGAS.  
- Proposes a novel sparse GP-SSM using the FIC prior to achieve O(M²T) computational complexity.  
- Extends prior GP and PMCMC work by enabling nonparametric learning of dynamics without parametric reduction.  
- Theoretical derivations are rigorous, with clear marginalization and smoothing distribution formulation.  
- Experiments demonstrate robust performance on benchmark nonlinear systems (e.g., cart-pole).  
- Originality: first to combine GP-SSMs with PGAS for fully Bayesian learning, distinct from prior parametric approaches.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with effective figures illustrating multimodal smoothing and uncertainty.  
- Significance: enables flexible nonparametric modeling of complex dynamics while preserving Bayesian inference.  
- Missing comparisons to alternative PMCMC variants (PMMH and backward-sampling PG / Whiteley et al.), weakening efficiency claims.  
- Lacks empirical validation of particle complexity scaling (no ACF/ESS plots vs. N and T).  
- Discussion of inducing input selection for the sparse GP-SSM is superficial and under-justified.  
- Recommendation: technically strong and original but needs minor revisions to add missing PMCMC comparisons and scaling analyses before acceptance.
