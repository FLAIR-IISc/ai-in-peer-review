- Proposes an innovative application of particle MCMC to Gaussian process state-space models, focusing on the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm (Lindsten et al.).  
- Paper is well-written, interesting, and presents an original contribution.  
- Develops useful model-specific methodology, notably a sparse GP-SSM.  
- Major omission: no comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, especially particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- Implementing PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would be straightforward and would strengthen the paper by showing relative performance.  
- Recommends graphical performance metrics (ACF, ESS) as functions of number of particles (N) and time steps (T); current results do not inform optimal scaling of N with T.  
- Opinion: PMMH likely needs particles to grow quadratically with T (per Whiteley), whereas particle Gibbs schemes likely require sublinear growth — a practically significant difference.  
- Overall: solid application but would be significantly improved by empirical comparisons to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling to clarify strengths and limitations.
