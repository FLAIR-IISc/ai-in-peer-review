- Novelty: Applies particle MCMC, specifically ancestral sampling particle Gibbs (Lindsten et al.), to Gaussian process state-space models—an interesting and original contribution.  
- Writing: Paper is well-written and clear.  
- Methodology: Develops useful model-specific techniques, including a sparse GP-SSM variant.  
- Major omission: Lacks comparisons with other particle MCMC schemes (notably PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling).  
- Ease of addition: Comparing to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would be straightforward and informative.  
- Results presentation: Current results are not very informative and need improved visualization (e.g., ACF or ESS plots).  
- Recommended plots: Show ACF/ESS as functions of particle count N and time horizon T to illustrate scaling and algorithm behavior.  
- Scaling question: Unclear how N should scale with T (linear, sublinear, etc.); paper should investigate this.  
- Expected scaling: PMMH likely needs particles increasing quadratically with T (per Whiteley et al.), while particle Gibbs schemes may require sublinear growth.  
- Overall: Solid, well-written application but would be significantly strengthened by comparative experiments and more informative result visualizations.
