- Applies particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs) to Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs); presentation is clear and application is original.  
- Contributes useful model-specific methodology, notably a sparse GP-SSM formulation.  
- Main concern: no comparisons with other particle MCMC schemes, especially particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.  
- Baseline methods (PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling) are straightforward to implement and would provide informative benchmarks.  
- Requests diagnostic plots quantifying performance (ACF, ESS, ideally ESS per second) as functions of particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Current results don’t clarify how to choose N as T grows—question whether N should scale linearly or sublinearly with T.  
- Cites prior work suggesting PMMH may require particles growing quadratically with T while particle Gibbs variants require sublinear growth; recommends empirically testing this scaling.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and promising, but would be considerably strengthened by head-to-head comparisons, scaling studies, and performance diagnostics (ACF/ESS vs. N and T).
