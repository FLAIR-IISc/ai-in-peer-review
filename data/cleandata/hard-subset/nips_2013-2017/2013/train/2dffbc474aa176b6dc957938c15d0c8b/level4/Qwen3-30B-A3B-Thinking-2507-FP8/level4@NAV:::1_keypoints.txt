- Introduces a Bayesian framework using GP priors to learn transition dynamics in nonlinear state-space models.
- Uses particle MCMC with ancestor sampling for inference.
- Improves computational efficiency via sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates for covariance matrices.
- Paper shows strong technical rigor and a clear, accessible presentation.
- Online marginalization of transition dynamics and the novel use of ancestor sampling to achieve this are significant contributions.
- Inclusion of computational considerations (sparsity and low-rank updates) substantially strengthens the work.
- Empirical results are concise but adequate; more detail could be beneficial.
- Paper will stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs between learning transitions vs specifying parametric forms.
- Suggest elaborating the distinction between using a predefined parametric transition model and using a similar structure as the GP mean function, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.
- Clarify Table 1’s RMSE: indicate whether it’s computed on withheld data or the conditioning dataset and consider adding an out-of-sample RMSE column.
- Minor issue: the acronym CPF-AS appears in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.
