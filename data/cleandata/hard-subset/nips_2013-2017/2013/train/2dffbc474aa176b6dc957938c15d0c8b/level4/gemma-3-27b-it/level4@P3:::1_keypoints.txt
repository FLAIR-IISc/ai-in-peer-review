- Presents a Bayesian state and parameter estimation method for nonlinear SSMs using a GP prior on transition dynamics.
- Uses particle MCMC with ancestor sampling as the core inference mechanism.
- Addresses computational efficiency via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates.
- Technically sound with clear and accessible presentation.
- Novel contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics combined with ancestor sampling.
- Empirical results are concise but sufficient to demonstrate effectiveness.
- Extends prior GP-SSM work by learning the GP transition model rather than assuming it.
- Differentiates from EM-based approaches by preserving nonparametric GP expressivity.
- Claims to mitigate overfitting issues in high-dimensional state spaces highlighted in prior work.
- Builds on PMCMC and ancestor sampling literature (Andrieu et al., Lindsten et al.) adapted to non-Markovian SSMs.
- Significance: likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian SSMs and the trade-offs of learning transitions vs specifying parametric models.
- Requests clearer discussion on differences between using a parametric transition model and using it as the GP mean function, especially for forecasting.
- Requests clarification of Table 1: whether RMSE is in-sample or on a withheld test set.
- Strongly recommends adding out-of-sample forecast RMSE (held-out forward prediction) to demonstrate predictive power.
- Minor stylistic issue: CPF-AS is introduced before being defined.
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on clarifying RMSE evaluation in Table 1 and including out-of-sample forecasting results.
