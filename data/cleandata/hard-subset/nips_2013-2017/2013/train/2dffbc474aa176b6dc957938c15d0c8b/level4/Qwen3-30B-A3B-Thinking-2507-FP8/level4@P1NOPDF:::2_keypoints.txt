- Clear and original application of particle MCMC to Gaussian process state-space models using ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs (Lindsten et al.) and valuable sparse GP-SSM methodology.  
- Major omission: no comparisons to key particle MCMC variants, notably particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- These baseline comparisons would be straightforward to implement and are essential to evaluate the proposed approach’s relative merits.  
- Results lack sufficient detail on computational efficiency; unclear how the number of particles N should scale with sequence length T.  
- Recommend reporting performance metrics (e.g., autocorrelation functions (ACF) or effective sample size (ESS)) as functions of N and T.  
- Paper should explicitly address known scaling behaviors: PMMH often requires particles growing ~O(T^2) while particle Gibbs variants typically scale sublinearly (Whiteley et al.).  
- Adding the missing comparisons and scaling/performance analyses would significantly strengthen the evaluation; current absence of benchmarking weakens the paper.  
- Core application is sound and well-motivated, but the above additions are needed for a comprehensive assessment of strengths and limitations.
