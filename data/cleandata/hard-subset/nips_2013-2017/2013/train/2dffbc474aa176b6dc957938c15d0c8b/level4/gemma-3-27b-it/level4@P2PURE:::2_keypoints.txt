- Applies particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs per Lindsten et al.) to inference in Gaussian process state-space models.  
- Well-written, original application; development of model-specific methods (e.g., sparse GP-SSM) is a valuable contribution.  
- Major weakness: no comparisons to other prominent particle MCMC schemes.  
- Paper should benchmark against particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (e.g., Whiteley et al.).  
- Implementing these alternative algorithms appears feasible and would offer insight into relative strengths/weaknesses with minimal additional computational burden.  
- Results lack sufficient detail; include performance plots (e.g., ACF, ESS) as functions of particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Relationship between N and T is unclear—need to clarify whether N must scale linearly with T or sublinearly; literature suggests PMMH may scale quadratically with T while particle Gibbs often scales sublinearly.  
- Overall: strong application but substantially strengthened by comparative analysis against PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
