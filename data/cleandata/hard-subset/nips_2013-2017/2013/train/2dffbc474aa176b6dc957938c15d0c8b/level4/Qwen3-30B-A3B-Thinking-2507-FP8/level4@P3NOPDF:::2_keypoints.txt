- Clear and original application of particle MCMC to GP-SSMs using Lindsten et al.'s ancestral sampling particle Gibbs.
- Development of a sparse GP-SSM methodology that addresses computational challenges and is a valuable contribution.
- Paper is well-motivated and technically sound.

- Missing critical comparisons to established particle MCMC schemes, specifically PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (PGBS).
- Without PMMH and PGBS benchmarks, it is unclear whether the proposed method offers practical efficiency or accuracy advantages.
- PMMH typically requires particle counts N scaling linearly with time horizon T, while particle Gibbs variants often require sublinear scaling—this scalability distinction should be evaluated.
- Empirical results lack quantitative diagnostics (e.g., ACF, ESS) plotted as functions of N and T to assess mixing and required particle scaling.
- Authors should clarify whether N must grow with T for stable inference or can remain constant under their method.
- Sparse GP-SSM benefits are obscured by missing comparisons; need to state if sparsity assumptions affect particle MCMC convergence versus dense models.
- Provide the exact computational cost of the sparse approximation relative to the particle MCMC steps.
- Adding PMMH/PGBS comparisons and scaling diagnostics would substantially strengthen the paper and are straightforward revisions.
