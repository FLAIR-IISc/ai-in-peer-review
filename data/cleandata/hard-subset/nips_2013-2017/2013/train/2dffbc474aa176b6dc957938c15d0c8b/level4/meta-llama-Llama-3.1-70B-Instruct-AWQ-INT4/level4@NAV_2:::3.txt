This paper presents a fully Bayesian approach to inference and learning in nonlinear nonparametric state-space models, where a Gaussian process prior is placed over the state transition dynamics. The authors propose a particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) algorithm to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution while marginalizing over the state transition function. This approach allows for nonparametric modeling of complex dynamical phenomena and can be used with sparse Gaussian processes to reduce computational complexity.

The paper is well-motivated, and the proposed approach is a natural extension of existing work on Gaussian process state-space models. The use of PMCMC to sample from the smoothing distribution is a key contribution, as it allows for efficient inference in these models. The authors also provide a clear and detailed description of the proposed algorithm, including the marginalization of the state transition function and the use of ancestor sampling.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on two nonlinear systems, including a cart and pole system used in reinforcement learning. The results show that the learned model can produce accurate one-step ahead predictions and can capture the uncertainty in different regions of the state-space.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed and critical evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the approach. For example, the authors could discuss the challenges of getting the PMCMC scheme to work, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the number of particles. Additionally, a more comprehensive evaluation of the approach, including comparisons with other methods and an analysis of the computational complexity, would be beneficial.

Overall, the paper presents a solid contribution to the field of Gaussian process state-space models, and the proposed approach has the potential to be useful in a variety of applications. However, the paper could be strengthened by a more detailed evaluation of the approach and a discussion of the challenges and limitations of the method.
