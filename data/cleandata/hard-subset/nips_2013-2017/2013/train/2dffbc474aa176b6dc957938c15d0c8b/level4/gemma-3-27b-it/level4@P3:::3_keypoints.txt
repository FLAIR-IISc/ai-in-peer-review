- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by marginalizing the transition function and using PMCMC to infer the joint smoothing distribution.  
- Builds on prior GP-SSM literature but learns GP dynamics rather than assuming a fixed model.  
- Distinguishes itself from previous marginalization approaches by using PMCMC to handle non-Markovian structure.  
- Principled Bayesian treatment enabling full uncertainty quantification and flexible modeling of complex dynamics.  
- Technically sound method with detailed inference algorithm and implementation considerations.  
- Includes a sparse GP (FIC) variant to mitigate computational complexity.  
- Experiments on a synthetic nonlinear system and cart-pole demonstrate method applicability and ability to capture complex behavior.  
- Insufficient discussion of practical challenges in implementing PMCMC (tuning proposals, convergence issues).  
- Lacks analysis of sensitivity to PMCMC parameters and computational cost of the sampling procedure.  
- Experimental section is mainly demonstrative rather than offering rigorous comparisons to alternatives.  
- Comparisons (e.g., Table 1) lack detail about competing models’ parameter settings.  
- Trade-offs between accuracy and computational efficiency for the sparse GP variant are not clearly articulated.  
- Use of a known input in benchmarks simplifies the problem; more challenging scenarios (unknown/noisy inputs) are needed.  
- Overall: solid technical contribution but weakened by limited practical evaluation and benchmarking — recommendation: weak reject, reconsider if revisions address concerns.
