- Applies ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs to inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs).  
- Paper is clearly written and presents an interesting, original application.  
- Introduces a model-specific adaptation: the sparse GP-SSM.  
- Major weakness: no comparative analysis against other particle MCMC schemes.  
- Missing explicit comparison to Particle Marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- These alternative algorithms are implementable with reasonable effort; an empirical comparison would be informative.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks thorough performance characterization (e.g., ACF or ESS plots).  
- Results do not clarify the scaling relationship between number of particles (N) and time series length (T).  
- Reviewer conjectures PMMH may require N to scale quadratically with T (per Whiteley et al.).  
- Particle Gibbs and backward sampling are expected to require only sublinear growth of N with T.  
- Conclusion: paper is a valuable contribution whose impact would increase with rigorous empirical comparisons to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
