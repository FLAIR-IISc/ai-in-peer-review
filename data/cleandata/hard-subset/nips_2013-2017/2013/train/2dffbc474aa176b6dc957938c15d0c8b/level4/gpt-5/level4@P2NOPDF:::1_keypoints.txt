- Develops a Bayesian method for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models with GP priors on transition dynamics.
- Uses PMCMC with ancestor sampling for inference; online marginalization via ancestor sampling is novel and a key strength.
- Tackles computational efficiency through sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates, adding practical value.
- Empirical results are concise but generally adequate to support claims.
- Likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs between learning dynamics vs specifying parametric models.
- Suggests expanding discussion contrasting fixed parametric transition models vs embedding parametric forms as the GP mean function.
- Requests commentary on implications for out-of-sample prediction, especially forward-time multi-step forecasting where model differences are most pronounced.
- Notes ambiguity in Table 1 regarding whether RMSE is reported on withheld test data or training/conditioning data; asks to add RMSE for forward forecasts on held-out data if missing.
- Minor editorial: define the abbreviation CPF-AS at first use (it appears in Algorithm 1 before definition).
- Overall assessment: strong, original paper with clear methodological and computational contributions; clarify evaluation protocol and forecasting/model-specification discussion.
