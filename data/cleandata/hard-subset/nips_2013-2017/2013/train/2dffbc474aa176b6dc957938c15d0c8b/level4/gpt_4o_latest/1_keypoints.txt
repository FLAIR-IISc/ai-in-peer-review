- Introduces a Bayesian framework using a GP prior to learn transition dynamics in nonlinear state-space models.  
- Inference via PMCMC with ancestor sampling; novelty is online marginalization of transition dynamics using ancestor sampling.  
- Includes computational optimizations for sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Technically robust and clearly presented with accessible exposition.  
- Empirical results are concise and adequate but could be expanded for clarity.  
- Likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian SSMs and trade-offs between learning transitions vs specifying parametric models.  
- Suggest expanding discussion on differences between using a parametric transition a priori versus as the GP mean, especially for forecasting/out-of-sample implications.  
- Table 1 is unclear about whether RMSE is out-of-sample or computed on conditioning data; recommend adding an out-of-sample/withheld-data RMSE column.  
- Minor issue: CPF-AS is introduced in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, innovative paper likely to inspire engaging discussions.
