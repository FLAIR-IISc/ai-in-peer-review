- Proposes fully Bayesian learning in GP-SSMs by marginalizing transition f with a GP prior and using PGAS to jointly infer latent states x_{0:T} and hyperparameters θ.  
- Introduces a sparse GP (FIC) variant reducing complexity from O(T^4) to O(M^2 T) with M ≪ T.  
- Strength: technically sound marginalization approach and precise PGAS implementation (Algorithm 1).  
- Strength: sparse GP extension is practical, maintains theoretical guarantees, and improves scalability.  
- Strength: paper is very clear and well-organized; equations and algorithms are detailed enough for reproducibility.  
- Strength: original contribution—applies PMCMC to learning GP dynamics end-to-end, filling a gap in prior work.  
- Strength: significant practical value—captures complex, multimodal dynamics and provides better uncertainty (mixture-of-Gaussians predictive).  
- Weakness: insufficient evaluation of PMCMC—missing convergence diagnostics (trace plots, ESS), sensitivity analyses (N, M), and comparisons to alternative samplers.  
- Weakness: lack of robustness assessment—no analysis of PGAS failure modes, tuning requirements, or how often it struggles with multimodal posteriors.  
- Weakness: experimental analysis underdeveloped—RMSE reported without error bars/statistical tests across trials.  
- Weakness: sparse GP experiments not compared to other sparsification methods or ablation over inducing-point count M.  
- Weakness: missing quantitative comparisons to parametric baselines (e.g., EKF/UKF) or other GP-SSM methods to demonstrate real-world advantage.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations—scalability to high-dimensional states, sparse-data scenarios, and sensitivity to inducing-point selection not evaluated.  
- Recommendation: technically strong and novel but significance is reduced by inadequate PMCMC evaluation; borderline weak accept—requires substantial experimental revision to be compelling.  
- Pro/Con summary: Pro—novel PMCMC application, clear presentation, useful sparse GP; Con—critical missing evaluations of PMCMC performance and superficial experiments.
