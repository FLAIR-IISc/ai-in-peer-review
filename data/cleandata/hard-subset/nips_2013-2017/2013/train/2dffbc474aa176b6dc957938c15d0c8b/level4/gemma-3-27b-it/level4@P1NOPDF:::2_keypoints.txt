- Proposes applying particle MCMC to Gaussian process state-space models, focusing on ancestral sampling particle Gibbs (Lindsten et al.).
- Paper is well-written, original, and develops useful model-specific methodology, notably a sparse GP-SSM implementation.
- Major omission: no comparison to other particle MCMC schemes, especially particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).
- Implementing PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would be relatively straightforward and provide valuable performance insight.
- Results lack informative visualizations; recommend showing autocorrelation function (ACF) or effective sample size (ESS) as functions of particle count (N) and sequence length (T).
- Unclear from current results how N must scale with T (linear, sublinear, etc.).
- Reviewer expects PMMH to require particles growing roughly quadratically with T, whereas particle Gibbs variants should exhibit sublinear scaling.
- Overall: solid contribution that would be significantly strengthened by comparative analysis against PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
