- Presents a fully Bayesian approach for GP state-space models by marginalizing the dynamics function and using PMCMC to sample the joint smoothing distribution.
- Key innovation: tailored Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to handle non-Markovian dependencies after marginalizing the dynamics.
- Preserves nonparametric expressivity of Gaussian process priors while enabling state estimation and system identification in a Bayesian framework.
- Employs sparse GP techniques to reduce computational complexity.
- Strong theoretical grounding and appropriate citations to related work (e.g., Deisenroth et al., Turner et al.).
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and real-world benchmarks (nonlinear system and cart-pole).
- Major weakness: lacks detailed evaluation of practical implementation challenges of the PMCMC scheme.
- Missing analysis of PGAS mixing and convergence properties across different problem settings.
- No thorough comparison of computational cost with alternative inference methods (e.g., variational inference, expectation propagation).
- Insufficient discussion of sensitivity to parameter choices and overall computational requirements.
- Fails to evaluate practical limitations, failure modes, and robustness in real-world scenarios.
- Sparse GP approximation impact on performance is not thoroughly assessed.
- Overall: well-written and technically sound but limited contribution due to insufficient critical/practical evaluation of the proposed approach.
