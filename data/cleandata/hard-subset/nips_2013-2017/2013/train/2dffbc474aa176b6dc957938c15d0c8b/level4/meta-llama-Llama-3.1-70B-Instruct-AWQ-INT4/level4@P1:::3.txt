The paper presents a Bayesian approach to inference and learning in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs) using Particle Markov Chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) methods. The authors propose a fully Bayesian approach, where the posterior distribution over all latent entities, including the state transition function, hidden state trajectory, and hyper-parameters, is inferred. This is achieved by marginalizing out the state transition function, resulting in a non-trivial inference problem that is solved using a tailored PMCMC sampler.

The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to GP-SSMs and the challenges associated with inference and learning in these models. The authors also provide a thorough review of existing work on GP-SSMs and highlight the contributions of their approach. The technical aspects of the paper are well-presented, and the authors provide a detailed description of their proposed method, including the marginalization of the state transition function and the use of PMCMC samplers.

The experimental section demonstrates the application of the proposed method to two benchmark problems: a nonlinear system and a cart and pole system. The results show that the method can effectively learn the state transition dynamics and provide accurate predictions. However, the experimental section is limited to demonstrating the application of the method, and a more detailed evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the approach would have been beneficial.

A critical evaluation of the paper reveals that the main contribution is the application of PMCMC methodology to GP-SSMs, which is a nice and straightforward extension of existing work. However, the paper does not provide a thorough evaluation of the challenges associated with getting the PMCMC scheme to work, which would have been a valuable contribution. Furthermore, the experimental section could have been more comprehensive, providing a more detailed assessment of the practical difficulties in implementing the proposed method and its performance.

In conclusion, the paper presents a solid contribution to the field of GP-SSMs, but could have benefited from a more detailed evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the approach. The experimental section, while demonstrating the application of the method, could have been more comprehensive, providing a more detailed assessment of the practical difficulties in implementing the proposed method and its performance.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a clear and concise introduction to GP-SSMs and the challenges associated with inference and learning in these models.
* The authors provide a thorough review of existing work on GP-SSMs and highlight the contributions of their approach.
* The technical aspects of the paper are well-presented, and the authors provide a detailed description of their proposed method.
* The experimental section demonstrates the application of the proposed method to two benchmark problems.

* The paper does not provide a thorough evaluation of the challenges associated with getting the PMCMC scheme to work.
* The experimental section is limited to demonstrating the application of the method and could have been more comprehensive.
* The paper could have benefited from a more detailed assessment of the practical difficulties in implementing the proposed method and its performance.
