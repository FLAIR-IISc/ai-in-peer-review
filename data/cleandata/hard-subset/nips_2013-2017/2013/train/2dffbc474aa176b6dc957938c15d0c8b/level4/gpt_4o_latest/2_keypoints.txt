Paper applies particle MCMC to Gaussian process state-space models, emphasizing ancestral sampling particle Gibbs.  
Manuscript is well-written and the application is original; model-specific methods (e.g., sparse GP-SSM) add value.  
Major shortcoming: no comparisons with other particle MCMC methods, specifically PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.  
These alternative methods could be implemented relatively easily and would yield valuable comparative insights.  
Paper lacks performance plots (e.g., ACF or ESS) as functions of number of particles (N) and time horizon (T); current results are not sufficiently informative.  
Unclear whether N must scale linearly, sublinearly, or otherwise with T; an empirical scaling study is needed.  
Prior findings indicate PMMH may require O(T^2) particles while particle Gibbs variants likely need sublinear growth in N with T.  
Overall positive evaluation, but the manuscript would be significantly strengthened by including the suggested method comparisons and scaling analyses.
