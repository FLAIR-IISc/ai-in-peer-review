

The authors present an application of particle MCMC for inference in Gaussian process state-space models, specifically leveraging the recent ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm by Lindsten et al. The paper is well-written and offers an interesting, original contribution to the field, with notable model-specific developments including the sparse GP-SSM methodology.  

A significant omission is the absence of comparisons to established particle MCMC schemes, particularly particle marginal MH (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.). These comparisons would have been straightforward to implement and are essential for contextualizing the proposed method’s performance.  

Furthermore, the results lack sufficient detail to assess algorithmic efficiency. Performance metrics (e.g., ACF, ESS) as functions of the number of particles $N$ and time steps $T$ should be included to clarify scaling behavior. It remains unclear whether $N$ must scale linearly or sublinearly with $T$, though theoretical insights suggest PMMH likely requires $O(T^2)$ particles (as in Whiteley et al.), while particle Gibbs variants necessitate sublinear growth in $N$ with $T$.  

This is a well-executed application of particle MCMC to GP-SSMs. The paper would be substantially strengthened by comparing the proposed approach to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
