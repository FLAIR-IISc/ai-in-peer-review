- Proposes a Bayesian framework for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a Gaussian process prior for transition dynamics.
- Employs particle Markov chain Monte Carlo inference with ancestor sampling.
- Explores computational optimizations via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates.
- Paper is technically rigorous, well-presented, and accessible.
- Novel contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics combined with ancestor sampling.
- Consideration of computational efficiency strengthens the work.
- Empirical results are concise but generally sufficient, with room for improvement.
- Suggests expanding comparison between a parametric transition model (with prior) and using that model as the GP mean, especially for forecasting.
- Requests clarification on whether Table 1’s RMSE is computed on withheld (out-of-sample) data or the conditioning data.
- Recommends adding an out-of-sample/forecasting RMSE column to Table 1 to highlight differences from the ground truth model.
- Minor issue: CPF-AS is introduced in Algorithm 1 before being formally defined in Section 3.3.1.
- Overall assessment: strong, innovative paper likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models.
