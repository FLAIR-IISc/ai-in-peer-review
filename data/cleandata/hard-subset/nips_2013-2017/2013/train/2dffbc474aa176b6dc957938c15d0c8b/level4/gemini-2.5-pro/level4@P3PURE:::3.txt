This paper applies Particle MCMC (PMCMC) sampling to a state-space model (SSM) with a Gaussian Process prior. The goal is to perform inference on the model's static parameters, extending beyond the standard tasks of filtering and smoothing latent states. The work is a straightforward application of existing PMCMC methodology.

The paper's primary weakness is the lack of a detailed discussion and evaluation of the challenges inherent in getting the PMCMC scheme to work effectively for this model. For a paper that is an application of an established methodology, the most significant contribution would have been a critical assessment of the practical difficulties, strengths, and weaknesses of the approach. A more in-depth analysis of the tuning and performance of the sampler would have made the paper considerably more valuable.

As presented, the experimental section serves more as a demonstration of the method rather than a rigorous evaluation of its performance. Given that this is an application paper, its value would be significantly enhanced by assessing the practical difficulties in implementing such a scheme and analyzing how well it actually works. A more detailed and critical evaluation of the approach's strengths and weaknesses is needed. While a complete revision of the experimental section may be too much to ask, the paper would be substantially improved by a more thorough analysis of the method's practical challenges and performance.
