- Proposes a fully Bayesian method for nonlinear nonparametric SSMs using GP priors that marginalizes the transition function for efficient inference.  
- Introduces a tailored particle MCMC sampler to perform inference while retaining full nonparametric expressivity.  
- Method is well-motivated and GP priors are appropriately justified.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction to learning general nonlinear SSMs.  
- Provides a thorough related-work discussion, clarifying differences from prior approaches.  
- Technical contributions are solid, with detailed derivation of the marginal prior and PMCMC sampler.  
- Uses a sparse GP approximation to reduce computational complexity.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to only two examples, reducing empirical validation.  
- Lacks comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods in experiments.  
- Insufficient detail on practical implementation difficulties and how they were addressed for the PMCMC scheme.  
- Would benefit from a more detailed and critical discussion of the approach's strengths and weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add more experimental evaluation and more critical/practical discussion.
