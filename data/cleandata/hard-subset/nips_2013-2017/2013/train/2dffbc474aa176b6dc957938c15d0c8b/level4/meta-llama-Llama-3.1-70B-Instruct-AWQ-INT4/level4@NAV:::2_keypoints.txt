- Novel application of particle MCMC to Gaussian process state-space models, focusing on the ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs algorithm (Lindsten et al.).  
- Manuscript is well-structured and clear, presenting a compelling extension and model-specific methodology including sparse GP-SSM.  
- Major omission: no comparison with other particle MCMC schemes, specifically PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- Implementing PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling would be relatively straightforward and yield valuable comparative insights.  
- Results lack graphical diagnostics (e.g., ACF or ESS as functions of N and T) that would improve interpretability.  
- Current presentation leaves unclear how the number of particles N should scale with sequence length T.  
- Expectation from prior work: PMMH likely needs a quadratic increase in particles with T, while particle Gibbs variants require a sublinear increase.  
- Overall recommendation: include comparisons with PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling to better assess the proposed algorithm’s performance.
