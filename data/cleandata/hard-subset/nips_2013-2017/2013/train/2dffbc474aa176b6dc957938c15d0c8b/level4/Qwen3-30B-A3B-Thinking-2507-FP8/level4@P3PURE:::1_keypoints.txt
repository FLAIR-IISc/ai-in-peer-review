- Introduces a Bayesian framework for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a Gaussian process prior for transition dynamics.  
- Inference via PMCMC with ancestor sampling enables online marginalization of transition dynamics (notable novelty).  
- Computational efficiency improved through exploiting sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates for covariance matrices.  
- The work is technically sound, well-motivated, and presented with exceptional clarity.  
- The online marginalization via ancestor sampling and the computational treatments substantially strengthen the contribution.  
- Empirical results are concise but adequate; however, evaluation details need clarification.  
- Requires deeper discussion comparing fixed parametric transition models versus using the same parametric form as the GP mean, especially for out-of-sample forecasting.  
- Table 1’s RMSE reporting is ambiguous—unclear if computed on withheld (forecast) data or conditioned data; recommend adding an out-of-sample forecast RMSE column.  
- Algorithm issue: CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1—this should be corrected.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution likely to stimulate discussion and suitable for NIPS.
