- Introduces a Bayesian framework for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models with a GP prior on transition dynamics.  
- Uses PMCMC augmented with ancestor sampling for inference, offering novel online marginalisation of transition dynamics.  
- Addresses computational improvements via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Technically sound contribution, presented clearly and accessibly.  
- Empirical results are concise and generally adequate but could be expanded.  
- Suggest elaborating differences between using a fixed parametric transition model versus using that model as the GP mean, especially for out-of-sample prediction.  
- Table 1 is unclear about whether RMSE is computed in-sample or on held-out data; request an explicit out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column.  
- Minor clarity issue: CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in the text.  
- Likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and trade-offs of learning versus specifying transition dynamics.
