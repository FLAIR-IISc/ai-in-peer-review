- Paper proposes using particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs) for inference in Gaussian process state-space models.
- Presentation is clear and the application is interesting and original.
- Authors introduce useful model-specific methodology, notably a sparse GP-SSM.
- Major omission: no comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, especially particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
- Implementing and comparing those alternative schemes would have been relatively straightforward and informative.
- Results lack performance plots (e.g., ACF or ESS) as functions of particle number N and time steps T.
- Current experiments do not clarify the relationship between N, T, and algorithm performance (e.g., required scaling of N with T).
- Reviewer’s view: PMMH likely needs N to grow quadratically with T, while particle Gibbs variants need sublinear growth.
- Overall assessment: well-written application but significantly improved by adding PMMH and backward-sampling particle Gibbs comparisons.
