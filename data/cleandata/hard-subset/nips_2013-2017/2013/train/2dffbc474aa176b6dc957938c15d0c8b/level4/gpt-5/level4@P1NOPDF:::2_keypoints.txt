- Applies particle MCMC (PGAS) to inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs); clearly written and original application.  
- Develops useful model-specific methodology, notably a sparse GP-SSM.  
- Major shortcoming: no comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, especially PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.  
- These baseline methods appear straightforward to implement in this setting and should be added with modest effort.  
- Empirical results lack algorithmic performance plots—e.g., ACFs or ESS as functions of particle number N and time horizon T.  
- Missing scaling analysis: should clarify whether N must scale linearly with T or can grow sublinearly.  
- Reviewer cites expectation/theory: PMMH typically requires N to grow quadratically with T, while particle Gibbs variants require sublinear growth.  
- Overall: solid work but would be significantly strengthened by empirical comparisons to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
