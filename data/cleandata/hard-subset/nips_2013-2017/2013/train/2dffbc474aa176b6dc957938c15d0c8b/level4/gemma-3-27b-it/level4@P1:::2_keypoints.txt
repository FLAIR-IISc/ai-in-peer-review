- Proposal: apply particle MCMC (ancestral sampling particle Gibbs) for inference in Gaussian process state-space models (GP-SSMs).
- Contribution: interesting, original application and well-written presentation.
- Methodology: introduces useful model-specific techniques, notably a sparse GP-SSM construction.
- Major limitation: no empirical comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, specifically PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
- Suggestion: implement and compare PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling to assess relative efficiency.
- Experimental gap: missing visualizations of algorithm performance (e.g., ACF or ESS) as functions of particle number N and time horizon T.
- Scaling question: unclear how N must scale with T (linear, sublinear, etc.); current results do not address computational scaling.
- Prior expectation: PMMH may require particles growing quadratically with T, while particle Gibbs variants likely need sublinear growth—this should be demonstrated empirically.
- Acceptance pros: tackles an important problem, technically sound, leverages recent advances, and offers a valuable sparse GP-SSM contribution.
- Acceptance cons: lack of comparisons to alternatives and insufficient analysis of computational scaling weaken the empirical assessment.
