- Proposes a fully Bayesian approach to nonlinear nonparametric state-space models by placing a GP prior on transition dynamics — novel and flexible for complex dynamical phenomena.
- Uses particle MCMC samplers to efficiently sample from the smoothing distribution — a significant methodological contribution.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of methodology and sufficient background for readers.
- Introduces a sparse GP-SSM variant to substantially reduce computational complexity — practically useful.
- Experimental results demonstrate the proposed approach can effectively learn nonlinear systems.
- Lacks empirical comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes (e.g., PMMH, particle Gibbs with backward sampling), limiting assessment of relative performance.
- Should include plots of algorithm performance as a function of number of particles (N) and time-series length (T) to show scaling behavior.
- Needs comparisons to other particle Gibbs variants to justify the choice of PGAS and show relative strengths/weaknesses.
- Requires a more detailed analysis of the algorithm’s computational and statistical scaling properties.
- Overall assessment: a good contribution that could be significant with minor improvements (additional comparisons and scaling analysis).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel flexible model, effective use of PMCMC, sparse GP-SSM reduces complexity, and supportive experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing PMCMC baselines, lack of N/T performance curves, and insufficient scaling analysis.
