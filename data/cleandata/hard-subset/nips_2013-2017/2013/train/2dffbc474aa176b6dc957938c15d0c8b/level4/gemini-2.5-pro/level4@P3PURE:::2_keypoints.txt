- Paper proposes applying Particle MCMC (Particle Gibbs with Ancestral Sampling, PG-AS) for inference in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs).
- Paper is clearly written and presents a novel application of this methodology.
- Authors contribute a model-specific sparse GP-SSM variant.
- Major weakness: no empirical comparisons to other standard Particle MCMC methods, especially Particle Marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and Particle Gibbs with Backward Sampling (PG-BS).
- Experimental evaluation is weak and does not analyze algorithmic scaling properties.
- Recommend reporting metrics like Autocorrelation Function (ACF) or Effective Sample Size (ESS) as functions of particle count N and time-series length T.
- Key practical question: how must N scale with T to maintain performance (linear, sublinear, etc.) remains unanswered.
- Hypothesis: PMMH may require N to scale quadratically with T, whereas Particle Gibbs variants could show more favorable sublinear scaling.
- Overall: well-written and novel, but substantially strengthened by empirical comparisons to PMMH/PG-BS and a thorough computational-scaling analysis.
