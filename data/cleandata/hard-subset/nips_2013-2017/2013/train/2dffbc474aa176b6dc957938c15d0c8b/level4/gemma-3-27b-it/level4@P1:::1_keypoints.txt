- Presents a Bayesian approach to state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a GP prior for transition dynamics.  
- Uses PMCMC with ancestor sampling to enable online marginalization of transition dynamics — a novel inference mechanism.  
- Discusses practical computational efficiencies via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Technically sound and mathematically rigorous with a clear and accessible presentation.  
- The combination of online marginalization and ancestor sampling is a novel contribution.  
- Empirical results are concise and sufficient but would benefit from broader evaluation across datasets and baselines.  
- Suggest expanding discussion comparing specifying a parametric transition model a priori versus using it as the GP mean, especially for forecasting/out-of-sample prediction.  
- Results in Table 1 are unclear about whether reported RMSE is out-of-sample; recommend adding an out-of-sample/forecast RMSE column.  
- Minor clarity issue: CPF-AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in the text.  
- Work likely to stimulate discussion and has potential impact on Bayesian nonparametric state-space modeling.
