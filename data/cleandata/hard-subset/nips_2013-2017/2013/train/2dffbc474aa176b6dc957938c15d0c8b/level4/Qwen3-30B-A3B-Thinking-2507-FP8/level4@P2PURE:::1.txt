

This paper introduces a Bayesian framework for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models, incorporating Gaussian process (GP) priors to learn transition dynamics. Inference leverages particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (PMCMC) with ancestor sampling, a recently proposed technique. The work also addresses computational efficiencies through sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates for covariance matrices.  

The paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly presented. Its novel contributions include online marginalization of transition dynamics and the application of ancestor sampling to achieve this. The treatment of computational aspects—particularly sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates—significantly strengthens the work. The empirical results, though concise, are sufficient; further suggestions are provided below.  

Beyond its primary contributions, the paper is poised to stimulate discussion on inference for non-Markovian state-space models and the trade-offs of learning transition dynamics via GP versus specifying a parametric model a priori.  

The authors might consider elaborating on the distinction between using a parametric transition model specified a priori versus employing a similar model as the GP's mean function, particularly regarding out-of-sample prediction (e.g., forecasting).  

The description of Table 1 and its preceding paragraph requires clarification: it is unclear whether the reported RMSE is computed on a withheld test set or the same data used for conditioning (indicated by "*|data" in column headers). An out-of-sample forecast RMSE against withheld data—where differences between learned dynamics and ground truth would likely be most pronounced—is the primary interest. If Table 1 does not already include this, adding an extra column for out-of-sample RMSE would be valuable.  

One minor point: the abbreviation CPF-AS is introduced in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  

This is a strong, novel contribution that will stimulate meaningful discussion.
