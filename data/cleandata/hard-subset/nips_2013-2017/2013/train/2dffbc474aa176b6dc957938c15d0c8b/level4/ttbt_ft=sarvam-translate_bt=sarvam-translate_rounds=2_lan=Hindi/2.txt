The authors propose applying Particle MCMC to estimate in a Gaussian process state-space model. Specifically, they focus on the recent ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm by Lindstein et al. The paper is clear and presents an interesting and original application of Particle MCMC. The paper also develops some useful model-specific methods, namely sparse GP-SSM.

One thing that I find particularly disappointing is the lack of comparison with other Particle MCMC schemes, especially the Particle Marginalized MH (PMMH) scheme and backward sampling with Particle Gibbs (as in Whiteley et al.). They could have been applied directly, and it would have been interesting to know how they compare to the proposed scheme (and it wouldn't have been too much work for the authors either).

Furthermore, I would like to see graphs demonstrating the performance of the algorithm (for example, in terms of ACF or ESS) as a function of N and T. As they stand, the results are not very informative. Do I need to scale N linearly, sub-linearly, with T?

I believe such models would require PMMH with several particles increasing quadratically with T, as seen in Whiteley et al., while both Particle Gibbs require particles increasing sub-linearly with T.

A well-written application of Particle MCMC for a GP state-space model. The paper could have been improved significantly if the proposed algorithm had been compared with PMMH and backward sampling with Particle Gibbs.
