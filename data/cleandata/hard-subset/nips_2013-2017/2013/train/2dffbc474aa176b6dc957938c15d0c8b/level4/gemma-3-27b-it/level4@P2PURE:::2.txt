The authors present an application of particle Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods for inference in Gaussian process state-space models, specifically utilizing the ancestral sampling particle Gibbs algorithm introduced by Lindsten et al. The paper is well-written and proposes an interesting and original use of particle MCMC. Furthermore, the development of model-specific methodology, such as the sparse GP-SSM, is a valuable contribution.

A significant weakness, however, is the absence of comparisons to other prominent particle MCMC schemes. In particular, the performance of the proposed method would benefit from being benchmarked against particle marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (e.g., as described in Whiteley et al.). Implementing these alternatives appears feasible and would provide valuable insight into their relative strengths and weaknesses compared to the proposed approach; the computational burden for the authors should be minimal. 

Moreover, the current results lack sufficient detail. The inclusion of plots illustrating algorithm performance – for example, using metrics such as autocorrelation function (ACF) or effective sample size (ESS) – as a function of both *N* and *T* would significantly enhance the analysis. The relationship between *N* and *T* is unclear; is a linear scaling of *N* with *T* necessary, or is sublinear scaling sufficient? Based on observations from Whiteley et al., PMMH is expected to require a number of particles scaling quadratically with *T*, while particle Gibbs methods generally exhibit sublinear scaling.

In summary, this is a strong application of particle MCMC to GP state-space models. However, the paper would be substantially strengthened by a comparative analysis against PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
