The paper applies PMCMC to a state-space model with a Gaussian process prior, enabling inference over model parameters rather than limiting attention to filtering and smoothing. This is a clear and straightforward application of established PMCMC methodology.

However, the work does not sufficiently describe or evaluate the practical challenges involved in making the PMCMC scheme effective in this setting. That analysis would likely constitute the most important contribution. A more detailed and critical assessment of the approach’s strengths and weaknesses—such as computational cost, mixing and convergence behavior, sensitivity to the number of particles and proposal choices, handling of GP hyperparameters, and robustness across datasets—would greatly increase the paper’s value to practitioners.

The experimental section is primarily demonstrative and lacks rigorous evaluation. While a full overhaul may be beyond scope, adding experiments that diagnose failure modes, quantify efficiency (e.g., effective sample size per second), explore sensitivity to key design choices, and compare against strong baselines would make the results far more informative. Even targeted ablations and stress tests would help clarify when the method works well, when it struggles, and why.

As it stands, the paper offers a competent application of PMCMC but does not provide enough guidance on the practical difficulties of implementing the scheme in GP-based state-space models or on its empirical performance in realistic scenarios. Strengthening the empirical analysis and offering clear recommendations for parameter settings, diagnostics, and trade-offs would significantly improve the paper’s contribution.
