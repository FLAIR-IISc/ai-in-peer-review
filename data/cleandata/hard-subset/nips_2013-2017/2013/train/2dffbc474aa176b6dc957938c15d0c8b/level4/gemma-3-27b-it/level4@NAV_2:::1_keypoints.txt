- Presents a Bayesian method for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using a Gaussian process prior for transition dynamics and PMCMC inference with ancestor sampling.  
- Addresses computational efficiency via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Technically sound with a clear and accessible presentation.  
- Novel contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics enabled by ancestor sampling.  
- Careful treatment of computational issues strengthens practical applicability.  
- Empirical results are concise but sufficient to demonstrate method efficacy; further experiments suggested.  
- Likely to stimulate discussion on inference for non‑Markovian SSMs and trade-offs between learning dynamics vs specifying a parametric model.  
- Builds on GP-based SSM literature and fills the gap of learning the GP model itself rather than fixing it.  
- Offers a compelling nonparametric alternative to parametric or MAP-based approaches (e.g., Turner et al., Wang et al.).  
- Suggested extension: compare using a parametric transition model a priori versus as the GP mean function, especially for out‑of‑sample forecasting.  
- Results/Table 1 need clarification: specify whether RMSE is in-sample or out-of-sample; recommend adding forecast RMSE on withheld data.  
- Minor editorial: CPF‑AS is used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel paper poised to generate productive community discussion.
