- Proposes a fully Bayesian GP-SSM by placing a GP prior on the transition function and marginalizing it out.  
- Marginalization yields a non-Markovian prior over state trajectories, handled via PMCMC.  
- Uses Particle Gibbs with Ancestor Sampling (PGAS) to sample states and hyperparameters jointly.  
- Introduces a sparse FIC GP-SSM reducing complexity from O(T^3) to O(M^2 T) for scalability.  
- Strength: Novel and elegant application of PMCMC to retain full nonparametric GP expressivity.  
- Strength: Well-written and clearly explains model, marginalization motivation, and PGAS usage.  
- Strength: Methodologically sound derivation and appropriate inclusion of a sparse variant.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation focuses on predictive performance but neglects justification of the chosen inference algorithm.  
- Weakness: No empirical comparison to alternative PMCMC samplers such as PMMH or Particle Gibbs with Backward Sampling.  
- Weakness: Lacks quantitative analysis of sampler efficiency (e.g., ESS, ACF) and how particle count N scales with time series length T.  
- Suggestion: Empirically compare PGAS to PMMH and backward-sampling Particle Gibbs to justify sampler choice.  
- Suggestion: Report ESS/ACF vs. N and study required N scaling with T to guide practitioners.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel combination of GP-SSMs and PMCMC, avoids parametric approximations, technically sound, and includes a practical sparse variant.  
- Rejection arguments: Weak experimental justification for PGAS choice and missing quantitative mixing/scaling analysis.
