- Introduces a Bayesian framework for joint state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models using Gaussian process priors for transition dynamics.  
- Employs particle MCMC with ancestor sampling to enable efficient online marginalization of the transition dynamics.  
- Implements computational optimizations via sparsity exploitation and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates to improve practicality.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-executed, and clearly presented.  
- Online marginalization via ancestor sampling is a novel technical contribution.  
- Integration of sparsity and low-rank Cholesky strategies substantially strengthens practical applicability.  
- Empirical evaluation is concise but generally supports the main claims.  
- Recommends a more detailed comparison between using a parametric transition model a priori versus as a GP mean function, especially for out-of-sample forecasting/extrapolation.  
- Table 1 is ambiguous about whether reported RMSEs are in-sample or out-of-sample; out-of-sample RMSE on held-out future data should be reported.  
- Minor technical inconsistency: acronym "CPF-AS" is used before being defined; define it on first use.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution likely to stimulate discussion on non-Markovian state-space inference; recommends acceptance.
