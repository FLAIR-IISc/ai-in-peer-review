

The paper applies PMCMC sampling within a state space model (SSM) equipped with a Gaussian process (GP) prior to estimate model parameters, extending beyond standard filtering and smoothing. While the methodology represents a straightforward application of PMCMC, the paper's primary weakness lies in the insufficient evaluation of the practical challenges inherent in implementing the PMCMC scheme. Specifically, the authors fail to provide a detailed analysis of the difficulties encountered in making the PMCMC approach operational, nor do they critically assess its performance characteristics. This omission is significant, as the core contribution of the work would have been substantially strengthened by a thorough investigation into the practical feasibility, computational demands, and empirical efficacy of the PMCMC implementation. A more rigorous evaluation of the method's strengths, limitations, and practical hurdles—rather than merely demonstrating its application—would have transformed the paper from a routine application into a valuable contribution to the literature. The experimental section, as currently presented, focuses on demonstration rather than evaluation, and revising it to address these critical aspects would have been essential for the paper to merit publication.
