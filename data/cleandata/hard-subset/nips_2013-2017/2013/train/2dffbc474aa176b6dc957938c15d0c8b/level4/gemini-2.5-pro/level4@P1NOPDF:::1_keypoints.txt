- Presents a Bayesian method for state and parameter estimation in nonlinear state-space models that learns transition dynamics with a GP prior and uses PMCMC with ancestor sampling.  
- Technically strong and clearly written.  
- Novel contribution: online marginalization of transition dynamics enabled by ancestor sampling.  
- Addresses practical computational efficiencies via sparsity and low-rank Cholesky updates/downdates.  
- Empirical results are concise and demonstrate potential, but could be expanded.  
- Suggest expanding discussion on trade-offs between specifying a parametric transition model a priori versus using it as the GP mean function, especially for forecasting.  
- Table 1 and its description are unclear about whether RMSE is on training or withheld test data.  
- Recommend adding out-of-sample forecasting RMSE (withheld-data predictions) to better compare learned dynamics versus ground-truth.  
- Minor issue: abbreviation "CPF-AS" used in Algorithm 1 before being defined in Section 3.3.1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel paper likely to stimulate discussion on inference in non-Markovian state-space models.
