- Paper applies particle MCMC (particle Gibbs with ancestor sampling) to Gaussian process state-space models and introduces a sparse GP-SSM formulation.
- Writing is clear and the application/originality are interesting.
- Major shortcoming: no empirical comparisons with other particle MCMC methods, notably particle marginal Metropolis–Hastings (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
- PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling appear straightforward to implement here and should be included.
- Reviewer requests algorithmic-performance plots (e.g., ACF or ESS) as functions of number of particles N and sequence length T.
- Current results are not informative about how N should scale with T (linear vs sublinear).
- Reviewer’s understanding: PMMH typically needs particles growing quadratically with T, while particle Gibbs variants require only sublinear growth.
- Conclusion: paper is solid but would be significantly improved by empirical comparisons with PMMH and particle Gibbs using backward sampling.
