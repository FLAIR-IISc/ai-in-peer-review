This paper proposes the application of Particle MCMC methods for inference in Gaussian Process State-Space Models (GP-SSMs). Specifically, the authors employ the Particle Gibbs with Ancestral Sampling (PG-AS) algorithm. The paper is clearly written and presents an interesting and novel application of this methodology. A further valuable contribution is the development of a model-specific sparse GP-SSM variant.

A significant weakness of the paper is the absence of comparisons to other relevant Particle MCMC algorithms, most notably Particle Marginal Metropolis-Hastings (PMMH) and Particle Gibbs with Backward Sampling (PG-BS). Given that these are standard methods, their inclusion would be both straightforward and highly informative for assessing the relative merits of the proposed approach.

Furthermore, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened considerably. The current results are not very informative regarding the algorithm's scaling properties. The authors should provide a more detailed analysis, for instance, by plotting performance metrics such as the Autocorrelation Function (ACF) or Effective Sample Size (ESS) as a function of the number of particles (N) and the time series length (T). This would help answer crucial practical questions, such as whether N must scale linearly or sublinearly with T to maintain a certain level of performance.

Such an analysis would likely highlight the benefits of the chosen approach. For instance, it is plausible that for this class of models, PMMH would require the number of particles N to scale quadratically with T, whereas both Particle Gibbs variants would likely exhibit more favorable sublinear scaling.

In summary, this is a well-written paper on a novel application of Particle MCMC to GP-SSMs. However, its contribution would be substantially strengthened by a proper empirical comparison against standard alternative algorithms like PMMH and PG-BS, alongside a more thorough analysis of the method's computational scaling.
