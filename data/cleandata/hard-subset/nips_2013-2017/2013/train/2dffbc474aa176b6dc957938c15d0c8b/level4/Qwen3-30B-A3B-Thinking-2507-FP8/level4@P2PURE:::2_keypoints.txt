- Proposes applying particle MCMC (ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs) to inference in Gaussian process state-space models and develops sparse GP-SSM methodology.  
- Paper is clear, interesting, and presents an original application with useful model-specific methods.  
- Major omission: no comparison to other particle MCMC schemes, especially particle marginal MH (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.  
- Comparisons to PMMH and backward-sampling particle Gibbs would be straightforward to implement and provide valuable performance insight.  
- Results lack informative performance metrics—missing plots of ACF or ESS as functions of particle count N and time horizon T.  
- Without such metrics, it is unclear how N should scale with T (e.g., linearly vs. sublinearly).  
- Theoretically, PMMH likely requires particles growing quadratically with T, whereas particle Gibbs variants require particle counts growing sublinearly.  
- Overall assessment: well-written application that would be significantly strengthened by comparisons to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
