- Proposal: apply particle MCMC to inference in Gaussian process state-space models, focusing on Lindsten et al.'s ancestral-sampling particle Gibbs.  
- Paper is clear, interesting, and an original application of particle MCMC.  
- Methodological contribution: development of a sparse GP-SSM variant.  
- Major omission: no comparisons to other particle MCMC schemes, specifically particle marginal MH (PMMH) and particle Gibbs with backward sampling (Whiteley et al.).  
- Such comparisons would be straightforward to implement and are necessary to assess relative performance.  
- Results lack informative diagnostics—need graphs of performance (e.g., ACF or ESS) as functions of particle count N and time horizon T to determine scaling (linear vs sublinear).  
- Reviewer belief: PMMH likely requires particle count growing quadratically with T (per Whiteley et al.), whereas particle Gibbs variants require sublinear growth.  
- Conclusion: well-written paper but would be significantly improved by including comparisons to PMMH and particle Gibbs with backward sampling.
