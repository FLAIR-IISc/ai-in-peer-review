- Proposes estimating density ratio r(x)=q(x)/p(x) by casting it as a Fredholm integral equation and solving with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS.  
- Presents two variants: Type I (Kp r = Kq 1) for samples from p and q, and Type II (Kt,p r ≈ q) for known q(xi) at p-sampled locations.  
- Derives closed-form representer-theorem solutions (linear systems on p-sample points) and calls the method FIRE.  
- Provides nonasymptotic high-probability L2,p error bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Decomposes error into kernel approximation (t), bias (λ), and sample/concentration terms.  
- Proposes CD-CV, an unsupervised model-selection heuristic based on moment-matching of random test functions.  
- Connects operator-theoretic view to learning-as-inverse-problem and kernel regularization literature.  
- Clarifies relations and contrasts to KMM, LSIF/KLIEP, and kernel-mean-matching approaches.  
- Notes CD-CV relates to importance-weighted CV and IPM/MMD two-sample tests, though connections are not fully explicit.  
- Strength: conceptual clarity—Fredholm framing yields a clean linear inverse formulation amenable to spectral analysis.  
- Strength: practical algorithms—closed-form representer solutions with natural out-of-sample evaluation (unlike classical KMM).  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis including manifold case, with informative error decomposition.  
- Strength: Type II addresses underexplored settings where q is known pointwise but q-sampling is hard.  
- Strength: CD-CV is simple and empirically effective in provided examples.  
- Weakness: estimator is unconstrained and can output negative or non-normalized ratios—problematic for importance weighting; positivity/normalization not enforced.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons are limited—KMM evaluation, probabilistic classification baselines, RuLSIF, IWCV, and scalability/runtime comparisons are missing.  
- Weakness: clarity and derivations need fixes—typos, inconsistent notation, unclear linear-algebra steps, and unstated normalization constants.  
- Weakness: CD-CV needs deeper study—choice of test function families, sensitivity to number F, and theoretical justification (connection to IPM/MMD) are unclear.  
- Weakness: Type II kernel bias (O(t)) and practical guidance for balancing t vs λ, especially in high dimensions, are limited.  
- Originality: nice synthesis and operator-theoretic framing; manifold-theory is a plus, but approach is close to existing kernel mean matching/density-ratio methods.  
- Significance: promising as an alternative to KMM/LSIF if positivity/normalization and broader comparisons are addressed; Type II is particularly interesting.  
- Clarity suggestions: proofreading, notation cleanup, brief algorithm boxes, and discussion of computational complexity and numerical solvers needed.  
- Key questions for authors: frequency/impact of negative/high-variance weights and remedies; connection of CD-CV to IPM/MMD; comparison of rates to Nguyen et al. (2007) and KMM analyses; results/scalability for n>10^4 (iterative/Nyström); theory for k ≠ kH.  
- Pros summary: clean Fredholm/RKHS formulation, closed-form representer solutions, nontrivial theory including manifolds, practical model-selection heuristic.  
- Cons summary: lack of positivity/normalization constraints, incomplete empirical comparisons, notational/derivational issues, sensitivity and scalability not fully explored.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject—solid ideas and theory but empirical/practical issues and clarity need strengthening before acceptance.
