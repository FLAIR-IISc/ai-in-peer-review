- Proposes estimating density ratios β(x)=q(x)/p(x) by casting as a Fredholm integral equation and solving with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS.
- Considers two settings: Type I (two-sample, general kernel, losses in L2(p)/L2(q)/RKHS) and Type II (one-sample, known q at p-samples, δ-like kernel).
- Solutions reduce to closed-form kernel-ridge-type linear systems via the representer theorem with out-of-sample extension.
- Provides nonasymptotic error bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Rd and compact manifolds, decomposing approximation, regularization, and sampling errors.
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised model-selection method validating β by testing Ep[uβ]≈Eq[u] over random test functions.
- Empirical comparisons on regression/classification under covariate shift and USPS show competitiveness with LSIF and a KDE-based TIKDE baseline.
- Connects operator-theoretic view to prior Tikhonov/kernel literature and relates closely to KMM, LSIF/KLIEP, and manifold/heat-kernel analyses.
- Strength: provides a clear unifying inverse-problem perspective that enables principled regularization and stability analysis.
- Strength: simple, closed-form kernel algorithms with immediate out-of-sample evaluation and multiple loss choices.
- Strength: substantive theoretical development with manifold rates and explicit decomposition of error sources.
- Strength: practical unsupervised model selection (CD-CV) that doesn’t require labeled targets.
- Weakness: β is not constrained to be nonnegative or normalized, risking negative weights and instability in downstream tasks.
- Weakness: Type I RKHS-loss variant closely resembles KMM; novelty and differences vs. KMM (and regularized KMM) need clearer delineation and empirical comparison.
- Weakness: Unusual theoretical rates need clearer derivation—optimize t and λ, compare to minimax rates, clarify smoothness and boundary assumptions.
- Weakness: Practicality of Type II is limited since assuming q(xi) known at p-samples is uncommon in typical covariate-shift settings.
- Weakness: Experimental comparisons are inconsistent (KMM omitted later); more end-task metrics, ablations (positivity, weight variance, sensitivity), and proper λ scaling are needed.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—heavy notation, unclear separation of kernels/operators, missing algorithm box, complexity analysis, and reference formatting.
- Suggestion: Add positivity/normalization constraints or transformations (e.g., β=exp g) and evaluate their impact.
- Suggestion: Provide full KMM comparisons with out-of-sample extension and regularization, complexity analysis, and large-scale approximations (Nyström, random features).
- Suggestion: Clarify optimal t, λ choices, compare rates to bounds, and expand experiments to report weight variance, negativity rates, and downstream performance.
- Overall quality: Technically sound and substantive theory, but some assumptions, rate interpretations, and empirical breadth need clarification.
- Originality: Moderately original—novel Fredholm/Tikhonov framing and manifold rates, but closely related to existing KMM/LSIF approaches.
- Significance: Meaningful contribution to density-ratio estimation and model selection with likely practical impact if revised.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept — interesting and useful but requires revisions on constraints, comparisons, theory clarity, and experiments.
