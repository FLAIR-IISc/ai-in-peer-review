- Proposes reformulating density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and solving it via Tikhonov-Phillips regularization in an RKHS, yielding the FIRE algorithms.  
- Connects density ratio estimation to classical inverse problems and spectral theory, offering a unified analytical framework.  
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for parameter selection without labeled data.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and convergence/error bounds for Gaussian kernels in both Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Empirically compares FIRE to baselines (KMM, LSIF, TIKDE) across multiple datasets, showing strong performance and computational efficiency.  
- Clarifies relationships to existing methods: differs from KMM (different optimization despite RKHS usage) and LSIF (nonparametric vs parametric approach).  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and logically organized with consistent mathematical notation.  
- The reformulation is an original and significant conceptual contribution to density ratio estimation.  
- CD-CV addresses a critical practical challenge in unsupervised parameter tuning for this problem.  
- Manifold-setting analysis extends applicability to complex data structures and is a notable theoretical strength.  
- Practical advantages over existing methods are not fully illustrated with concrete application examples or targeted comparative experiments.  
- Computational complexity discussion is acknowledged but remains somewhat superficial and could be expanded.  
- The paper underexplores deeper connections to manifold learning and could better justify the necessity of Type I vs Type II formulations.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper deserving acceptance; weaknesses are addressable in revision.
