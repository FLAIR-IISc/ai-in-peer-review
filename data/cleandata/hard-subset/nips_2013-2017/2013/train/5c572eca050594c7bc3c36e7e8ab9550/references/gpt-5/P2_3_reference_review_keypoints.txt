- Recasts density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral inverse problem with two formulations and solves via Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS yielding closed-form representer solutions.
- Type I: K_p r = K_q 1 uses samples from both p and q; Type II: K_{t,p} r ≈ q handles known (possibly unnormalized) q with only p-samples via δ-like kernels.
- Derives concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on R^d and compact manifolds, decomposing errors into approximation (t), regularization (λ), and sampling (n,m) terms.
- Proposes CD-CV, an unsupervised model-selection criterion based on approximate importance-sampling identities evaluated with random test functions on held-out data.
- Empirically compares FIRE to LSIF, KMM, and KDE baselines on regression/classification under covariate shift, showing promise but with limited benchmarks and uneven comparisons.
- Connects to prior work: clarifies relations to KMM, LSIF/KLIEP, and RKHS inverse-problem literature and shows KMM as the Fredholm equation in RKHS with a regularized analogue.
- Strength: conceptually unifying Fredholm framing that clarifies links to KMM and suggests multiple loss choices.
- Strength: algorithmically simple closed-form solutions with natural out-of-sample extension (unlike KMM).
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis including manifold-aware rates and careful bias/variance decomposition.
- Strength: practical unsupervised tuning via CD-CV and consideration of Type II settings relevant to MCMC and unnormalized models.
- Weakness: does not enforce non-negativity or normalization of r, risking negative or improperly scaled importance weights and downstream harm.
- Weakness: primary validation metric (JCD) is bespoke and not shown to correlate with true risk or L2,p error; synthetic experiments with known r are needed.
- Weakness: comparisons are uneven (e.g., KMM omitted in some settings), resampling schemes ad hoc, and runtimes/conditioning not reported.
- Weakness: theoretical rates are slow/opaque and guidance for choosing t, λ in practice is unclear.
- Weakness: assumptions (bounded densities, W2_2 smoothness, small-t approximations) may be strong in high dimensions; robustness when they fail is not discussed.
- Weakness: some bounds require conditions like m > λ^{1/3} n without practical selection rules ensuring they hold.
- Weakness: notation heavy/inconsistent and minor typos; relationship to KMM could be more explicitly stated when k = k_H.
- Quality: technically sound and plausible theoretical bounds, but tuning implications not operationalized and empirical evidence needs strengthening.
- Clarity: generally well-organized but needs notational cleanups and clearer explanations of computational complexity, conditioning, and constraint handling.
- Originality: moderately high due to the explicit Fredholm framing, Type II setting, manifold analysis, and CD-CV criterion.
- Significance: relevant to covariate shift, off-policy evaluation, and unnormalized models; would gain impact with stronger empirical validation and constraint handling.
- Specific suggestions: add non-negativity/normalization options and report effects; include synthetic experiments with known r; report time/memory and conditioning; discuss q known up to constant; clarify kernel choices and provide guidance.
- Pros: clean inverse-problem formulation, simple OOS-capable algorithms, theoretical bounds in Euclidean and manifold settings, and unsupervised model selection.
- Cons: lacks mechanisms for valid importance weights, incomplete empirical comparisons and bespoke metric, and slow rates with limited tuning guidance.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid conceptual and theoretical contributions and practical algorithms, but needs constraint handling and stronger experiments to be a clear accept.
