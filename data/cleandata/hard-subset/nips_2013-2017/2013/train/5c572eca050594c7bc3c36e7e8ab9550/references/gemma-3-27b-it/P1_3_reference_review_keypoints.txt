- Novel formulation: density ratio estimation is reinterpreted as solving a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Theoretical foundation: leverages operator theory, RKHS, and regularization for a principled algorithmic framework.  
- Two problem setups: presents Type I and Type II formulations to handle different sample/knowledge scenarios for q and p.  
- Algorithm: proposes FIRE algorithms based on the Fredholm formulation.  
- Model selection: introduces CD-CV (cross-density cross-validation) for unsupervised parameter tuning.  
- Theoretical results: provides convergence rates and error bounds for the Gaussian kernel and extends analysis to manifolds.  
- Clarity and positioning: clear connections to existing methods (KMM, LSIF) and distinguishes contributions; notation can be dense.  
- Flexibility: framework allows different kernels, norms, and loss functions, offering generality.  
- Empirical weakness: experimental gains over strong baselines (KMM, LSIF, TIKDE) are modest and inconsistent.  
- Experimental choices: some design choices (e.g., PCA resampling) lack clear justification.  
- Complexity concern: framework and algorithms are more complex than simpler alternatives, with unclear benefit trade-offs.  
- Limited theory scope: theoretical analysis currently only covers the Gaussian kernel, limiting generality.  
- Computational cost: paper lacks detailed comparison of computational cost, especially kernel matrix inversion overhead.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, rigorous approach with valuable theoretical insights and a practical CD-CV method.  
- Rejection arguments: weak empirical evidence, added complexity not always justified, and limited theoretical scope.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept / weak reject contingent on stronger empirical evaluation and analysis.  
- Requested revisions: run broader experiments and hyperparameter tuning, identify scenarios where FIRE outperforms, analyze computational costs vs. baselines, and evaluate additional kernels.
