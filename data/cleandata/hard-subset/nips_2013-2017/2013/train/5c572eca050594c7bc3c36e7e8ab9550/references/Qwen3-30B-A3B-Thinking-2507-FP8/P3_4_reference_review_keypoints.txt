- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solved with Tikhonov regularization in RKHS, introducing Type I/II settings and the FIRE algorithms with CD-CV for parameter selection.

- Theoretical novelty: connects density ratio estimation to Fredholm/inverse problem theory, offering a new conceptual perspective.

- Provides rigorous error bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and on manifolds.

- Practical algorithmic framework: FIRE is simple and implementable; CD-CV addresses unsupervised parameter selection.

- Clear exposition with correct mathematical development and appropriate connections to related work (e.g., KMM, LSIF).

- Experiments are limited: KMM, the most closely related method, is omitted from empirical comparisons.

- Type II has an inherent O(t) kernel approximation error that is not analyzed empirically or compared to Type I performance.

- Claimed generality is overstated since theoretical analysis is restricted to Gaussian kernels without discussion of other kernels.

- Insufficient differentiation from KMM: the paper notes connections but doesn’t clarify why its regularization/solution is practically superior.

- Computational efficiency claims are unsupported by complexity analysis or runtime comparisons.

- Overall: strong and novel theoretical contribution but incomplete empirical validation reduces practical impact.

- Recommended fixes: add KMM experiments, empirically analyze Type II O(t) error, provide computational complexity/runtime details, and better articulate advantages over KMM.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept (borderline), contingent on addressing the empirical and comparative gaps.
