- Summary: Reframes density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and introduces FIRE algorithms using RKHS regularization, with theoretical error bounds (Gaussian kernel) and a cross-density cross-validation (CD-CV) method for tuning.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions itself relative to KMM and LSIF, highlighting similarities in integral-equation foundations but differences in optimization/regularization, and cites relevant kernel/regularization literature.  
- Strength — Novel formulation: Casting DRE as an inverse problem via Fredholm equations is original and enables operator-theoretic tools.  
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: Provides concentration bounds and convergence rates (including manifold generalization) that strengthen the paper beyond empirical work.  
- Strength — Flexibility: FIRE supports different norms/losses and two formulations to handle varied data scenarios.  
- Strength — Clear algorithm derivation: Derivations yield explicit, implementable formulas.  
- Strength — Practical parameter selection: Proposes CD-CV to address unsupervised parameter tuning.  
- Strength — Comprehensive experiments: Evaluated against multiple baselines and shows competitive performance.  
- Weakness — Limited practical advantage: Experimental improvements over KMM/LSIF are often marginal and dataset-dependent.  
- Weakness — Theoretical complexity: Analysis is involved and may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with operator theory.  
- Weakness — CD-CV details ad-hoc: Choice of test functions (linear, half-space, etc.) lacks systematic justification or evaluation.  
- Weakness — Scalability concerns: Kernel matrix inversion and computational cost for large datasets are not addressed.  
- Weakness — Strong smoothness assumptions: Theoretical guarantees rely on Sobolev-type smoothness that may be restrictive in practice.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded approach with significant theoretical contributions, comprehensive experiments, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical gains not definitively shown, theoretical complexity may limit accessibility, and scalability is unaddressed.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, novel, and theoretically solid work that merits acceptance despite limited practical dominance.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: Clarify practical benefits and scenarios of advantage; simplify or give intuitive explanations for theory; expand and justify CD-CV test-function choices; discuss scalability and potential approximate-kernel remedies.
