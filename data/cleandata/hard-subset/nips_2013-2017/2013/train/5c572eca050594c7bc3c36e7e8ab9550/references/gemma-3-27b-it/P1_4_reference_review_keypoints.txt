- Novel formulation: Recasts density ratio estimation as solving a Fredholm integral equation and introduces the FIRE algorithms.  
- Two algorithm types: Type I uses samples from both densities; Type II uses samples from p and requires q(x) values at those points.  
- Theoretical analysis: Provides concentration and error bounds for the Gaussian kernel (Euclidean/manifold settings).  
- Unsupervised model selection: Proposes CD-CV for parameter tuning in unsupervised settings.  
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough comparisons against KMM, LSIF, TIKDE across datasets and settings.  
- Relation to prior work: Correctly situates the approach relative to KMM, LSIF, and kernel/regularization literature; shows KMM as a special case.  
- Generality and flexibility: Framework supports various kernels, norms, and loss functions.  
- Clear derivation: Algorithm derivations are well-explained and implementable.  
- Strengthened by operator theory: Connection to operator theory gives a strong mathematical foundation.  
- Computational complexity: Kernel matrix computations (especially in Type I) may not scale well; scalability not fully addressed.  
- Practicality of Type II: Requiring q(x) at p-sampled points may limit real-world applicability; needs clearer use-case justification.  
- Limited theoretical scope: Guarantees are restricted to the Gaussian kernel and specific settings; other kernels/distributions not analyzed.  
- Experimental detail gaps: Lacks full hyperparameter tuning details for baselines and rationale for CD-CV test function choices.  
- CD-CV sensitivity: Performance may depend on test-function choice; the paper offers little guidance on selecting them.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound approach with solid experiments and a useful unsupervised selection method.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Scalability concerns, restrictive Type II assumption, and limited scope of theoretical results.  
- Overall verdict: Significant contribution; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Revision: Add scalability discussion and approximation strategies (e.g., Nyström).  
- Revision: Provide concrete real-world examples where Type II’s q(x) assumption holds.  
- Revision: Extend theoretical analysis to other kernels/distributions or present preliminary results.  
- Revision: Give detailed baseline hyperparameter tuning and justify CD-CV test-function choices.  
- Revision: Study CD-CV robustness and provide guidance for selecting test functions.
