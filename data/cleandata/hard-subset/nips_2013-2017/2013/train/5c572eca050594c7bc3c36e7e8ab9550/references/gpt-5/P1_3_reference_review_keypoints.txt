- Paper reframes density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solved via Tikhonov regularization in RKHS, yielding kernel algorithms called FIRE.  
- Two settings: Type I uses samples from p and q to solve Kp r = Kq 1 with RKHS penalties; Type II treats pointwise-known q at p-sample locations as regression with δ-kernel.  
- Representer theorem gives closed-form linear-system solutions; finite-sample bounds and convergence rates are proved for Gaussian kernels on Rd and compact manifolds.  
- Authors propose CD-CV, an unsupervised hyperparameter selection that cross-validates by moment-matching over random test functions.  
- Relation to KMM: connects H-identity underlying KMM to Type I Fredholm formulation and offers a regularized, out-of-sample RKHS variant.  
- Relation to LSIF/KLIEP: contrasts by solving for the ratio directly via inverse-problem regularization instead of basis expansion; CD-CV resembles but is used for validation only.  
- Theoretical contribution builds on operator-theoretic regularization and RKHS learning theory, adding concentration-based finite-sample bounds including manifolds.  
- Strength: Conceptually elegant unification of density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem linking kernels and regularization.  
- Strength: Algorithmically simple closed-form solvers with flexible loss choices and natural out-of-sample evaluation.  
- Strength: Substantive nonasymptotic theory with explicit dependence on t, λ, n, m and novel manifold extensions.  
- Strength: Practical unsupervised model selection (CD-CV) that is task-agnostic and reusable.  
- Strength: Type II broadens applicability to settings where q is known pointwise (e.g., some MCMC or integrand contexts).  
- Weakness: FIRE yields unconstrained RKHS functions, so negativity and lack of normalization can harm importance sampling and weighted ERM.  
- Weakness: In Type II, if q is known up to a multiplicative constant, minimizing ||Kt,p f − q|| recovers a scaled f, not the true ratio—normalization handling is unclear.  
- Weakness: Strong assumptions (bounded p,q and smoothness of r and r/p) are atypical near low-density regions and lack explicit lower bounds on p.  
- Weakness: Unusual convergence exponents (e.g., n−1/(3.5+d/2)) need comparison to known minimax rates to assess tightness.  
- Weakness: Theory is given only when operator and RKHS use the same Gaussian kernel; paper should discuss kernel mismatch sensitivity.  
- Weakness: Experiments omit KMM at training points and some baselines (KLIEP, RuLSIF/KuLSIF), use small-to-medium datasets, and lack studies on nonnegativity/normalization effects.  
- Weakness: CD-CV needs more evidence on reliability, variance across random test-function families, and correlation with downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: Practical implementation details—complexity, conditioning, memory, and regularization tricks—are not discussed.  
- Quality: Technically solid derivations and substantial theoretical results, but assumptions may be restrictive and practical issues need addressing.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well organized but uses heavy and sometimes ambiguous notation; a notation table and clearer assumption statements are recommended.  
- Originality: Moderate to strong—Fredholm framing, Type II operator regression, and manifold theory are novel relative to KMM/LSIF.  
- Significance: Moderate; likely to influence teaching and analysis of density-ratio estimation, but broader impact depends on addressing positivity/normalization and stronger empirical comparisons.  
- Pros summary: Elegant inverse-problem formulation, closed-form kernel solvers, nontrivial theory including manifolds, and practical CD-CV model selection.  
- Cons summary: No positivity/normalization enforcement, Type II scale ambiguity, restrictive assumptions and unclear rates, and limited experimental baselines/scope.  
- Key questions for authors: How to enforce nonnegativity and its theoretical impact? How to handle q known up to scale in Type II? How do rates compare to minimax? What are computational complexity and conditioning behaviors and scalability strategies? Can additional baselines (KMM at training points, KLIEP, RuLSIF) be added?  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on clarifying positivity/normalization, adding KMM baseline at training points, and improving discussion of assumptions and rates.
