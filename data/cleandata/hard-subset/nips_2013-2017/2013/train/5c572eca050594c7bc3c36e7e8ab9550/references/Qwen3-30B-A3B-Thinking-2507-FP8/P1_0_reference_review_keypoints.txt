- Summary: Reformulates density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation and proposes FIRE (Type I/II) using RKHS regularization, with theoretical error bounds and an unsupervised CD-CV model selection method.

- Theoretical contributions: Novel framing as a Fredholm inverse problem enabling rigorous spectral analysis and comprehensive convergence rates in Euclidean and manifold settings.

- Practical algorithm design: FIRE algorithms are explicit, implementable via the representer theorem, and include a useful unsupervised hyperparameter tuning method (CD-CV).

- Experimental validation: Empirical results show FIRE outperforms baselines (KMM, LSIF, TIKDE) across tasks while being computationally efficient.

- Limited comparison with recent work: Lacks detailed comparison of theoretical guarantees with recent analyses of KMM and extensions of LSIF.

- Theoretical scope: Analysis is restricted to Gaussian kernels and does not deeply explore other kernels or loss functions.

- Implementation details: Insufficient discussion of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets or high-dimensional/manifold cases.

- Connection to related work: Needs clearer differentiation from other inverse-problem-based density estimation works (e.g., [12], [4]).

- Quality: Technically sound with well-supported proofs and comprehensive experiments.

- Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written with precise mathematical formulations and reproducible experimental setup.

- Originality: Novel application of Fredholm integral equation theory to density-ratio estimation and a notable CD-CV model selection contribution.

- Significance: Addresses an important problem (transfer learning, covariate shift, importance sampling) with theoretically grounded and practically effective methods.

- Recommendation: Accept — significant, well-executed contribution despite minor gaps in comparisons and scalability discussion.
