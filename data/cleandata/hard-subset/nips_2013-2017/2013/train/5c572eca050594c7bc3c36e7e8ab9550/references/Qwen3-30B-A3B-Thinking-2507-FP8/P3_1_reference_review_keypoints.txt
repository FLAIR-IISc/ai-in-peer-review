- Novel formulation: frames density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solved via Tikhonov regularization with RKHS penalties.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides rigorous error bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and on manifolds using spectral theory.  
- Algorithms: introduces simple, flexible FIRE algorithms (Type I and II) that generalize KMM and LSIF; Type I with RKHS norm is a regularized KMM.  
- Unsupervised model selection: proposes CD-CV, an importance-sampling-based cross-density cross-validation method for parameter tuning without labels.  
- Limited kernel scope: analysis focuses on Gaussian kernels and lacks discussion on extensions or kernel-choice effects.  
- Missing computational analysis: no detailed complexity or timing comparison (e.g., kernel matrix inversion O(n^3)) versus alternatives.  
- Type II practicality concerns: assumption that q is known at p-sampled points needs concrete application examples and interpretation of the O(t) error term.  
- Limited experiments: evaluations on only three datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) and no runtime comparisons limit empirical validation.  
- Incomplete KMM comparison: connection to KMM noted but not deeply explored—need clearer contrast between KMM’s QP over β and FIRE’s RKHS-regularized formulation.  
- Significance: provides a strong bridge between inverse problem theory and kernel methods with practical implications for transfer learning and importance sampling.  
- Overall verdict: technically strong paper; recommendation to accept.  
- Suggested final improvements: discuss other kernels, add complexity/timing analyses, give Type II use cases, expand experiments, and clarify relation to KMM.
