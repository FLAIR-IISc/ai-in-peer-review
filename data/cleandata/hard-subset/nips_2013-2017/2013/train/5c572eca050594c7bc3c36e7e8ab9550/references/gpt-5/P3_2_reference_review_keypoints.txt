- Reframes density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem for integral operators (Type I: Kp r = Kq 1; Type II: Kt,p r ≈ q) and solves with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS (FIRE closed-form solutions).  
- Uses multiple loss choices (L2,p, mixture γL2,p+(1−γ)L2,q, RKHS norm) and links the RKHS-norm variant to KMM.  
- Provides finite-sample error bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean domains and compact manifolds, with a clean bias–variance decomposition depending on t and λ.  
- Proposes CD-CV, an unsupervised model-selection method using importance-sampling identities tested against random function families.  
- Experiments compare FIRE to LSIF and a thresholded KDE baseline on regression/classification under covariate shift; algorithms are straightforward to implement.  
- Novelty is moderate/incremental: the Kp r = Kq 1 identity and RKHS/inverse-problem viewpoint closely relate to KMM and kernel mean-embedding literature.  
- Claimed novel elements include explicit Fredholm framing, Type II formulation, manifold-based bounds, and particular finite-sample rates.  
- Estimators do not enforce non-negativity or normalization of r, risking negative or poorly scaled weights that can harm downstream tasks; this is neither analyzed nor fixed.  
- Theoretical rates are unusual and lack discussion of optimality or comparison to known minimax results; some regularity/parameter conditions (Sobolev W2^2, m > λ1/3 n) need interpretation.  
- Type II practicality is asserted but not demonstrated experimentally; experiments focus on Type I/covariate-shift scenarios.  
- CD-CV lacks theoretical justification; its relation to L2,p error or MMD/IPM is not formalized and choice of test function family can cause high variance.  
- Experimental scope is limited: small/modest datasets, omission of KMM (unconvincing justification), missing comparisons to uLSIF/RuLSIF/KLIEP, no synthetic known-ratio benchmarks, and no large-n studies.  
- Scalability concerns unaddressed: computational complexity (O(n^3)) and absence of low-rank or random-feature approximations.  
- Clarity issues exist: typos, ambiguous notation (when k = kH), and unclear interplay between the δ-like smoothing kernel and the RKHS kernel.  
- Technical derivations and closed-form solvers are sound/standard; proofs are sketched and optimality of bounds remains unclear.  
- Readable but needs polishing: consolidate assumptions, clarify kernel equivalence, and discuss normalization/non-negativity.  
- Originality assessed as moderate: a clean unifier but closely related to existing kernel inverse-problem work.  
- Potential significance depends on stronger empirical validation, handling of positivity/normalization, and clearer positioning relative to prior art.  
- Requested author actions: enforce/experiment with non-negativity and normalization, compare rates to minimax bounds, formalize CD-CV consistency (link to IPM/MMD), add KMM/uLSIF baselines, propose scalable approximations, and include a Type II experiment (e.g., unnormalized q).  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — strengthen empirical comparisons, constraint handling, and positioning to be a solid contribution (reviewer confidence: medium).
