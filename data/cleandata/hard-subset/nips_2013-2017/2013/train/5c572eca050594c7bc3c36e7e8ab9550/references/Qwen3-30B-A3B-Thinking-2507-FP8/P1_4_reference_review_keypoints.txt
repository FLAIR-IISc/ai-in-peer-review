- Proposes reframing density ratio estimation q(x)/p(x) as a Fredholm integral equation solved with RKHS regularization, introducing Type I (samples from p and q) and Type II (q(x) known at p samples) formulations and the FIRE algorithms.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and sub-manifolds, and introduces an unsupervised parameter selection method (CD-CV).  
- Strength: Novel theoretical connection between density ratio estimation and Fredholm integral equations with thorough error analysis.  
- Strength: FIRE algorithms are simple, practical, and yield closed-form solutions via the Representer Theorem.  
- Strength: CD-CV offers a clever unsupervised model selection approach leveraging importance sampling.  
- Strength: Experiments across datasets and baselines (LSIF, TIKDE, KMM) show competitive performance and computational efficiency.  
- Strength: Clearly distinguishes Type I and Type II settings and their theoretical differences.  
- Weakness: Core optimization (Type I, L2,p) is similar to KMM, and the paper underplays concrete advantages over KMM and lacks direct theoretical comparison.  
- Weakness: Theoretical results rely on strong smoothness assumptions (Sobolev space membership) without discussion of practicality or empirical verification.  
- Weakness: Missing detailed computational complexity comparison, especially regarding n×n matrix inversion in Type I.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of limitations, e.g., curse of dimensionality with Gaussian kernels and practical impact of the Type II O(t) error term.  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous analysis and practical algorithmic development that meaningfully advances understanding of density ratio estimation.  
- Recommendation: Accept — paper offers valuable theoretical framing and a useful unsupervised selection method despite algorithmic similarities to existing methods.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly compare FIRE error bounds to those of KMM and LSIF to highlight advantages.  
- Suggestion: Add computational complexity analysis for FIRE, particularly for large-scale datasets.  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations of Gaussian kernels and possible extensions to other kernels.  
- Suggestion: Provide a concrete real-world example for the Type II setting (e.g., MCMC scenarios where q(x) is known but sampling is hard).
