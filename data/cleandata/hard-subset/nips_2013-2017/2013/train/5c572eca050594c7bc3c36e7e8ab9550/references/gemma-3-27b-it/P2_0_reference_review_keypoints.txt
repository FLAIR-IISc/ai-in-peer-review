- Novel framing: Recasts density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation, offering a novel theoretical perspective.
- FIRE algorithm: Proposes FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) using RKHS and regularization for density ratio estimation.
- Theoretical contributions: Provides concentration inequalities, error bounds, and convergence rates (especially for Gaussian kernels) and extends analysis to manifolds.
- Model selection: Introduces an unsupervised parameter selection method (CD-CV).
- Practical relations: Clearly connects FIRE to existing methods (KMM, LSIF), highlighting stability and out-of-sample extensions.
- Generality: Framework is flexible, supporting different norms, losses, and two formulations (Type I and Type II) for different data scenarios.
- Empirical results: Competitive performance on several datasets but improvements are not consistently dramatic.
- Empirical depth lacking: Experiments are cursory, missing ablation studies and broader baseline comparisons.
- Restrictive assumptions: Theoretical results rely heavily on Gaussian kernels and smoothness assumptions, limiting generality.
- Computational concerns: Kernel matrix inversions imply potentially high computational cost not discussed in detail.
- Type II clarity: Practical advantages and examples for Type II (when q isn't a density) are insufficiently elaborated.
- Presentation issues: Sections describing empirical algorithm versions are repetitive and could be streamlined.
- Overall assessment: Strong, mathematically rigorous contribution with moderate-to-high significance but some practical and empirical gaps.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing complexity, expanding experiments, illustrating Type II use, and reducing redundancy.
