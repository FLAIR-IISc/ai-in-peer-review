- Overall: Compelling, mathematically grounded work; recommend Accept with Minor Revisions pending clarification of practical advantages and limitations versus KMM/LSIF.  
- Core idea: Recasts density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral inverse problem solved via Tikhonov regularization in RKHS.  
- Formulations/algorithms: Proposes two formulations (Type I and II) and the FIRE algorithms, implementable via kernel expansions (representer theorem).  
- Model selection: Introduces a novel unsupervised cross-density cross-validation (CD-CV) for parameter tuning.  
- Relation to prior work: Distinct perspective from direct estimation methods, clearly connects to and differentiates from KMM and LSIF.  
- Theoretical strength: Provides convergence rates and error bounds (for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds).  
- Practical strength: Explicit, implementable algorithms with comprehensive experimental evaluation across tasks.  
- CD-CV strength: Valuable contribution for unsupervised parameter selection.  
- Empirical weakness: Performance improvements over KMM/LSIF/TIKDE are often marginal; lacks clear scenarios of significant superiority.  
- Theoretical limitation: Analysis restricted to Gaussian kernels and specific domain assumptions; broader kernel/setting coverage missing.  
- Practical guidance lacking: Insufficient advice on choosing kernel, regularization parameter, and bandwidth in practice despite CD-CV.  
- Presentation: Paper is mathematically dense and could use more intuitive explanations for broader accessibility.  
- Computational concern: Potentially expensive matrix inversions (Eqs. 8 and 10) raise scalability questions compared to KMM/LSIF.  
- Type I vs II: Unclear practical guidelines on when to use Type I versus Type II, especially with limited or noisy data.  
- Sensitivity question: Convergence rates may heavily depend on density smoothness—robustness to assumption violations is unclear.  
- CD-CV question: Importance of the choice of test function class (linear, half-space) in CD-CV and its effect on parameter selection/performance.  
- Error analysis request: Paper would benefit from a more detailed breakdown of error contributions (regularization vs approximation, etc.).
