- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and solves it via Tikhonov regularization in RKHS (the FIRE framework).
- Proposes two algorithmic settings: Type I (samples from both p and q) and Type II (q known at points sampled from p).
- Provides theoretical analysis: error bounds and convergence rates using spectral theory and concentration inequalities.
- Analysis covers Euclidean and manifold settings, with explicit results for Gaussian kernels.
- Introduces CD-CV, a novel unsupervised cross-density cross-validation model selection technique.
- Empirically outperforms baselines (KMM, LSIF, TIKDE) across regression and classification tasks.
- Contribution is highly original by connecting classical inverse problem/operator theory to density ratio estimation.
- Theoretical development is rigorous and comprehensive, clarifying effects of kernel width, regularization, and sample size.
- FIRE algorithms are simple to implement and show strong practical performance.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized, making the theory-to-algorithm connection clear.
- Theoretical scope is limited to Gaussian kernels, reducing generality to other kernel types.
- Lacks thorough experimental comparison with KMM despite noting KMM as a special case of Type I.
- Missing detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability for large-scale applications.
- Manifold analysis assumes compact smooth Riemannian submanifolds, which may not cover all practical manifold scenarios.
- Recommendation: accept — significant theoretical and practical contribution likely to interest the ML community.
