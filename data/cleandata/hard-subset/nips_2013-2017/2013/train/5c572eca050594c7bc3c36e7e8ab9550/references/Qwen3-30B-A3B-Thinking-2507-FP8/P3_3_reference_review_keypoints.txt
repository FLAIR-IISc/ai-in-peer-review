- Summary: Reframes density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation solved via Tikhonov regularization in RKHS, proposing FIRE algorithms (Type I/II), theoretical error bounds (Euclidean/manifold), and CD-CV for unsupervised model selection.

- Strength: Novel theoretical framework connecting density-ratio estimation to inverse problems and operator/spectral analysis.

- Strength: Clear algorithmic development with implementable Representer-theorem solutions for both Type I and Type II settings.

- Strength: Theoretical rigor with convergence rates/error bounds for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings.

- Strength: Practical unsupervised parameter selection method (CD-CV) grounded in importance-sampling principles.

- Weakness: Theoretical analysis relies on strong Sobolev-space assumptions (e.g., q/p, q/p^2 ∈ W^2_2(Ω)) that lack practical justification.

- Weakness: Experimental comparisons are limited (only KMM, LSIF, TIKDE) and omit recent methods like KLIEP and other advanced estimators.

- Weakness: Ambiguity in Type II setting—error term depends on kernel (O(t)) and the bandwidth/approximation trade-off is not clearly explained for practice.

- Weakness: Theoretical bounds do not provide clear guidance for choosing regularization and kernel parameters (λ, t), creating a gap between theory and CD-CV practice.

- Originality: Framing density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm equation is a fresh, valuable operator-theoretic perspective.

- Significance caveat: Despite novelty, practical significance is limited because the paper does not convincingly show substantial advantages over well-established methods.

- Overall assessment: Technically sound and novel, but would be stronger with broader empirical validation and tighter integration of theory and practice.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept — promising theoretical contribution but needs improved experiments and practical clarity.

- Author suggestion: Expand experiments to include KLIEP and recent density-ratio methods.

- Author suggestion: Discuss practical implications and ways to verify the Sobolev-type theoretical assumptions.

- Author suggestion: Clarify how the theoretical error bounds inform (or should inform) CD-CV parameter selection.

- Author suggestion: Provide a more thorough discussion of the Type II kernel bandwidth vs. approximation-error trade-off.
