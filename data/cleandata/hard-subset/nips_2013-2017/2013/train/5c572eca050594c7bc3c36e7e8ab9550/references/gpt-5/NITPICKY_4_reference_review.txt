Summary
The paper proposes to view density-ratio estimation q/p as an inverse problem via Fredholm integral equations of the first kind. Two formulations are derived:
- Type I: Kp(q/p) = Kq 1, when samples from both p and q are available.
- Type II: Kt,p(q/p) ≈ q, using a “local” kernel (e.g., Gaussian) so that convolution approximates the identity; applicable when only a sample from p and pointwise values of q(xi) are known.
Both are solved by Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding closed-form kernel algorithms (FIRE) through representer theorems. The paper also proposes an unsupervised model selection method (CD-CV) based on matching expectations of random test functions across domains, and provides concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and manifolds. Experiments compare FIRE to LSIF and a thresholded inverse KDE baseline.

Relation to prior work
The setup is closely related to KMM (Gretton et al.) and LSIF/KLIEP (Sugiyama et al.), as well as inverse-problem views of kernel learning (De Vito et al., Smola & Schölkopf). The identity Kp(q/p)=Kq1 encapsulates KMM’s moment-matching in RKHS; the authors acknowledge this, positioning FIRE as a regularized, out-of-sample alternative. Type II is akin to deconvolution with δ-family kernels. The theoretical framing via integral operators is in the spirit of existing spectral/regularization analyses.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity: reducing density-ratio estimation to an operator equation is elegant and unifies several ideas.
- Regularized RKHS formulation yields simple linear systems with out-of-sample access.
- The Type II setting covers use cases where q is known pointwise but not sampleable.
- Theoretical analysis attempts: concentration bounds and rates for Gaussian kernels in Rd and on manifolds are valuable directions.
- CD-CV is a reasonable unsupervised validation heuristic that may be practical.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and derivations:
- The closed-form coefficients for Type I (Eq. 8) and Type II seem inconsistent. For Type I, with f=KH v and loss ||Kp,p KH v − Kp,q 1||^2 + λ v^T KH v, the normal equations yield (KH Kp,p^2 KH + nλ KH) v = KH Kp,p Kp,q 1; with symmetric Kp,p this simplifies to (Kp,p KH Kp,p + nλ I) v = Kp,p Kp,q 1 when left-multiplying by KH^-1. The paper gives (Kp,p^2 KH + nλ I) instead of (Kp,p KH Kp,p + nλ I). A similar issue appears in Type II. Please re-derive and correct matrix order and factors (and ensure consistent 1/n scaling).
- Scaling factors: Kp,p includes 1/n, but nλ appears variably. The balance between empirical loss and regularizer should be checked; several equations mix scalings in inconsistent ways.
- No positivity/normalization constraints on q/p. This may produce negative weights and unstable importance sampling. KMM explicitly enforces positivity/sum constraints; FIRE should discuss or incorporate constraints or post-processing.
- CD-CV lacks theory establishing that its minimizer correlates with density-ratio estimation risk; it resembles moment matching/MMD with simple function classes, but the choice of linear/half-space functions is ad hoc.

Theory:
- Assumptions and spaces: the bounds assume q/p, q/p^2 in W^2_2 and use Gaussian convolution bias of O(t), but the statements “Moreover, ||Kt,q1 − q|| = O(t)” are informal and lack constants/norm specifications; manifold rates introduce ε, η without sharp tradeoffs.
- Rates in Corollaries 2 and 4 have suspicious exponents (e.g., n^{-1/(3.5+d/2)}; “56 d” appears in a denominator). These look like typographical errors or miscalculations; please check all exponents.
- The requirement m > λ^{1/3} n in Cor. 2 is unusual; discuss its necessity and implications.

Empirical evaluation:
- Baselines: KMM is largely omitted on the grounds of out-of-sample extension, yet it is applicable for covariate shift tasks without out-of-sample ratio values. This weakens the empirical comparisons given KMM’s prominence.
- No synthetic experiments where the true ratio is known; this is standard for ratio estimation to assess L2 error and calibration.
- No runtime/memory analysis or scaling discussion; FIRE requires solving n×n systems. No mention of low-rank approximations or preconditioning.
- Tuning ranges: λ ∈ {1e−5,…,1e−10} seem extremely small; sensitivity analyses are missing.

Clarity and presentation:
- Notational inconsistencies: q/p is written variably as qp, q p, or q/p; K, KH, and kt are overloaded. Sometimes k=kH, sometimes distinct; Section 3 silently sets them equal. δ-kernel vs δ-family language mixes “variance t” vs “bandwidth”.
- Numerous typos and grammatical errors: “TiknonovPhilips,” “a unsupervised,” “referred as,” “norms other that,” etc.
- Equation references and numbering are inconsistent; some missing parentheses/powers, e.g., in norms and exponents; a few inner products/norms omit subscripts.
- References are incomplete and inconsistent (years, venues, pages missing; placeholders like “, ,”). This is below NeurIPS standards.

Originality and significance
The operator view is insightful but not entirely new; the most novel elements are:
- Casting Type I and II uniformly with explicit RKHS-regularized solvers.
- The CD-CV validation heuristic.
Given strong existing methods (KMM, uLSIF/RuLSIF, IWCV), the incremental advance is moderate. The manifold analysis could be significant if cleaned up and verified.

Pros for acceptance
- Unifying framework linking KMM-like moment matching to inverse-problem regularization; potentially useful perspective.
- Practical, simple algorithms with out-of-sample capability.
- Ambitious theoretical analysis over Euclidean and manifold settings.

Cons for acceptance
- Potential errors in core linear system derivations; scaling inconsistencies.
- Theoretical rates appear mistyped and possibly incorrect; missing proofs in main text.
- Empirical evaluation omits a key baseline and lacks synthetic ground-truth tests.
- Many presentation/notation/grammar/reference issues; below conference quality bar.

Suggestions for improvement
- Re-derive and verify the closed-form solutions; fix matrix orderings and scalings.
- Add non-negativity (and possibly normalization) constraints or discuss their effects.
- Provide synthetic experiments with known ratios; include KMM, uLSIF/RuLSIF, IWCV baselines.
- Justify CD-CV theoretically (link to MMD or moment-matching generalization bounds) and assess sensitivity to test-function families.
- Unify notation (distinct k vs kH; consistent t definition), fix all typos, and fully standardize the bibliography.

Recommendation
Reject in current form. The idea is promising, but technical derivation issues, questionable/mistyped theoretical rates, limited/uneven experiments, and substantial presentation problems outweigh the contributions. A thoroughly revised version could be competitive.
