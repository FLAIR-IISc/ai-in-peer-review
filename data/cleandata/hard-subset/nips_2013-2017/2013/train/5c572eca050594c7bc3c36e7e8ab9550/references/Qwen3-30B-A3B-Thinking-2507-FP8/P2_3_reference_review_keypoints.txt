- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and proposes FIRE algorithms (Type I & II) using Tikhonov regularization in RKHS.  
- Key insight: density ratio estimation can be seen as "linearizing" density properties via integral operators.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in both Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Develops CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for parameter selection.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance against KMM, LSIF, and TIKDE on regression and classification tasks.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with reasonable assumptions (bounded densities, Sobolev smoothness).  
- Connects density ratio estimation to classical operator and spectral theory—novel perspective in ML.  
- FIRE offers natural out-of-sample extension and improved stability from regularization compared to KMM.  
- Notes limitation: Type II has a kernel-dependent O(t) error term and q need not be a density.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with precise mathematical formulation; minor notation inconsistency (q(x)p(x) vs q(x)/p(x)).  
- Weakness: theoretical results focus on Gaussian kernels; generalization to other kernels not discussed.  
- Weakness: experiments could include more datasets and comparisons with newer methods.  
- Weakness: computational complexity of FIRE is not analyzed in detail.  
- Overall recommendation: accept (strong contribution with theoretical and practical value).
