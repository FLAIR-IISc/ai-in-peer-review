- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solvable with operator-theoretic tools.
- Proposes FIRE algorithms using RKHS and Tikhonov regularization with explicit Representer Theorem solutions.
- Introduces two formulations (Type I and Type II) depending on sample/value availability from the target density.
- Provides a novel unsupervised parameter selection method: cross-density cross-validation (CD-CV).
- Strong theoretical analysis: concentration inequalities and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.
- Principled, flexible framework exploring different norms and loss functions within RKHS.
- Clear connections drawn to existing methods like KMM and LSIF, highlighting similarities and differences.
- Empirical results demonstrate effectiveness but are limited to a few datasets.
- Paper is dense and requires strong background in functional analysis and kernel methods, limiting accessibility.
- Theoretical results rely heavily on Gaussian kernels and smoothness assumptions; generalization to other kernels/less regular densities is not fully explored.
- Comparisons to state-of-the-art are limited; lacks evaluation against modern neural network–based density ratio estimators.
- Computational cost discussion is insufficient, particularly concerning matrix inversions and scalability to high dimensions.
- Quality rated 9/10: technically sound with strong theoretical backing.
- Clarity rated 7/10: generally clear but technically dense and could use more intuitive explanations.
- Originality rated 9/10: highly original Fredholm formulation and CD-CV method.
- Significance rated 8/10: important problem with potential to advance the field, given broader validation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically rigorous approach; practical algorithms; principled parameter tuning method.
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns, restricted theoretical scope (Gaussian/smooth densities), and limited experimental breadth/comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions — improve clarity and expand experimental comparisons with recent methods.
