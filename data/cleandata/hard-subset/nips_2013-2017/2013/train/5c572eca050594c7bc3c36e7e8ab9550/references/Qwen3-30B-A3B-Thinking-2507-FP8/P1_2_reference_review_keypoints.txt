- Reformulates density ratio estimation q(x)/p(x) as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, connecting it to inverse problem/operator theory.  
- Develops FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) algorithms for two settings: Type I (samples from p and q) and Type II (q known at p samples).  
- Applies RKHS regularization and provides theoretical error bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Proposes CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation) as an unsupervised model selection method for parameter tuning.  
- Shows FIRE unifies and extends prior methods (e.g., KMM as a special case) and offers a principled regularization framework with guarantees.  
- Strength: strong theoretical novelty by linking density ratio estimation to Fredholm/inverse problem theory.  
- Strength: solid algorithmic contribution with practically implementable, theoretically grounded FIRE methods for complementary scenarios.  
- Strength: practical CD-CV method addressing unsupervised model selection challenges.  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation showing competitive performance against baselines (KMM, LSIF, TIKDE) across tasks.  
- Weakness: paper assumes substantial background in Fredholm equations and RKHS, reducing accessibility for broader audience.  
- Weakness: Type II setting has an O(t) kernel-approximation error term that is noted but not empirically investigated.  
- Weakness: experimental focus mainly on Gaussian kernels; other kernel choices are not explored.  
- Weakness: comparisons to related work could be strengthened, notably KLIEP and deeper theoretical links to analyses like [26].  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clearly written but could be more intuitive, highly original and significant for transfer learning; recommendation: accept at NIPS.
