- Frames density ratio estimation as a Fredholm inverse problem using regularization, a novel and important contribution.  
- Well-motivated use of kernel methods with theoretical guarantees and encouraging empirical results.  
- Reformulating the task as a Fredholm integral equation enables application of regularization and operator-theoretic tools.  
- RKHS-based formulation with kernels as test functions yields practical algorithms; introduces FIRE and CD-CV.  
- Theoretical analysis covers both Euclidean and manifold settings and provides convergence rates.  
- Experimental validation supports theory and shows competitive performance against existing methods.  
- Lacks an intuitive explanation for why optimizing over {k(x,·)} ensures convergence in equality (i).  
- Motivation for mixing L2,p and L2,q in equation (9) is unclear and needs clearer connection to target applications.  
- Needs explicit discussion of when estimating q/p is preferable to directly estimating Eq for importance sampling.  
- Relationship between smoothness of p, q, and the ratio q/p requires further clarification, especially given p bounded away from zero.  
- Practical dependence on dimension d is a concern; guidance on feasible dimensionality is missing.  
- Justification and sensitivity analysis for the chosen family of test functions U in CD-CV is needed.  
- Should discuss whether using {k(x,·)} is the optimal general approach and relate to prior operator-kernel work (e.g., “Smooth Operators”).  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel formulation, practical FIRE algorithm with CD-CV, competitive experiments, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing intuition for key convergence, unclear L2 mixing rationale, lacking trade-off discussion for q/p vs Eq, and unaddressed dimensionality limits.
