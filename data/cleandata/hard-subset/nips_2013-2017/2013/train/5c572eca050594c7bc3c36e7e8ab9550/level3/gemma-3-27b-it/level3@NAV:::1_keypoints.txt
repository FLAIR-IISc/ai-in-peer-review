- Proposes a novel reformulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Leverages operator theory and Tikhonov regularization to create a flexible FIRE algorithm family.  
- Strong motivation: density ratio estimation is important for importance sampling and covariate shift.  
- Builds on prior kernel and transfer learning work; paper is well-written and relevant to NIPS.  
- Main novelty is the integral-equation viewpoint combined with regularization.  
- Theoretical claim ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t) lacks sufficient proof or direct citation.  
- Reference [24] is insufficient; a formal proof or clearer citation is needed.  
- Special case q=1 is interesting and may avoid bandwidth→0 issues; authors should expand on bandwidth’s role versus KDE.  
- Include a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.  
- Claim of superiority over naive plug-in estimator is made without clear justification.  
- Empirical and theoretical comparisons to prior methods (e.g., [12]) are unclear and need clarification.  
- Discuss minimax-optimality and quantify how close Corollaries 2 and 4 are to optimal rates.  
- Datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) lack references, reducing reproducibility.  
- Add toy experiments (e.g., 1D plots of true vs estimated p/q) to improve intuition and visualization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, well-written, potential significant impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical detail, unclear comparative performance, missing dataset references, lack of illustrative experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions.
