- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and introduces FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) using RKHS regularization.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and sub-manifolds.  
- Proposes CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation) for unsupervised model selection.  
- Paper is well-written and offers a unifying, novel perspective connecting kernel methods and inverse problems.  
- Theoretical analysis is valuable but partly relegated to supplementary material that the reviewer could not verify.  
- Key theoretical claim ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t) lacks proof or sufficient reference in the main text.  
- Lacks clear empirical and theoretical comparison to relevant prior work (notably [12] and the q=1 case).  
- Needs clearer discussion of bandwidth’s role versus KDE and whether the method attains minimax rates.  
- Methodological clarity issues: brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization is needed and the drawbacks of naive plug-in p/q estimation are not clearly explained.  
- Empirical details missing dataset references (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) and would benefit from toy plots (1D true vs estimated p/q).  
- Recommendation: promising and significant contribution but incomplete theoretical justification, comparisons, and empirical validation prevent strong endorsement for acceptance.
