- Core contribution: Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, yielding FIRE algorithms solved by Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS.

- Positioning: Connects to KMM and LSIF but distinguishes itself by treating the problem as a classical inverse problem using operator theory.

- Strength — originality/significance: Elegant, novel theoretical lens addressing an important ML problem.

- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written and derivations of FIRE are clearly presented.

- Theory gap: Key approximation ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t) needs a proof sketch or precise reference in the main text.

- Convergence rates unclear: Corollaries 2 and 4 need discussion comparing to minimax-optimal rates and to naive plug-in approaches (e.g., dividing KDEs).

- Accessibility: Add a brief, intuitive explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with inverse problems.

- Empirical intuition lacking: Include simple 1D toy experiments (plots of true vs estimated density ratio) to build intuition.

- Evaluation weakness: Empirical comparisons are incomplete — missing direct comparisons to related methods (e.g., [12]) and discussion of the q=1 special case (density estimation) and bandwidth role vs KDE.

- Reproducibility: Provide references for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) and more comprehensive experimental details.

- Overall: Promising and original work, but claims of superiority need stronger theoretical support and a more thorough, reproducible empirical evaluation.
