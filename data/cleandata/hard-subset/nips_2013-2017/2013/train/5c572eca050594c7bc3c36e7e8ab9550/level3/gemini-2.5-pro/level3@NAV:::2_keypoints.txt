- Presents a novel density-ratio estimation framing as an inverse problem via a Fredholm integral equation, with two methods (Type I and II) using Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS.  
- Connects to existing importance estimation work (KMM, LSIF) but offers a distinct operator-theoretic perspective.  
- Main strength: a principled unsupervised parameter-selection method (L2 objective enabling CD-CV), addressing a major practical gap in RKHS-based ratio estimators.  
- Practical advantage: CD-CV makes model selection reliable and directly comparable across kernels, improving usability over methods like KMM.  
- Experimental validation effectively demonstrates the approach’s effectiveness and supports the method’s significance.  
- Theoretical concern: Theorem 1 gives a slow convergence rate (factor like -t / log λ); authors should discuss whether stronger spectral or ratio assumptions yield polynomial rates.  
- Modeling concern: assuming q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) needs discussion because it may imply p/q is poorly behaved in practice.  
- Reproducibility/clarity issue: supplementary material has many typos and errors that hinder verification (e.g., Lemma 5 W2^2 should be Ws^2 and bound misstated; lines 675 and 686 incorrect; integral domain error at line 1147).  
- Recommendation: strengthen the theoretical discussion on rates and assumptions, and carefully proofread and correct the supplementary material to ensure technical claims are verifiable.
