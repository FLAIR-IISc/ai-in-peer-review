- Tackles density-ratio estimation q(x)/p(x) by reformulating it as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, connecting the problem to inverse problems theory.  
- Proposes two formulations: Type I (Kp(q/p) = Kq(1)) for when both p and q samples are available, and Type II (Kt,p(q/p) ≈ q) when q is known.  
- Solves the ill-posed equations via Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding closed-form, kernel-based FIRE algorithms.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), a novel fully unsupervised model-selection method for density-ratio estimation.  
- Relates to prior methods (KMM, LSIF) but distinguishes itself through the integral-equation/operator-theory perspective enabling new analysis.  
- Strength: original and significant idea that unifies density-ratio estimation with classical inverse-problem theory and has broad application relevance.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized derivation and easy-to-implement algorithms using standard kernel operations.  
- Strength: practical contribution of CD-CV addresses the difficult unsupervised model-selection problem effectively.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical backing (proofs in supplement), strengthening methodological soundness.  
- Weakness: needs explicit justification or citation for delta-kernel approximations (∫k_t f ≈ f) and required smoothness assumptions.  
- Weakness: convergence rates (Corollaries 2 and 4) lack context—should be compared to known minimax rates (e.g., Nguyen et al.) and discussed if suboptimal.  
- Weakness: paper could include a brief, accessible explanation of Tikhonov regularization for non-experts.  
- Weakness: should better motivate why plug-in estimators (estimate p and q then ratio, e.g., TIKDE) are inferior both theoretically and practically.  
- Weakness: discuss how kernel bandwidth t’s role here differs from its role in KDE, especially in the q=1 inverse-density special case.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation missing direct comparison with KMM, lacks simple 1D/2D toy visualizations of p, q, and q/p, and should provide dataset references for reproducibility.
