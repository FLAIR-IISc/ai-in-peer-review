- Paper reformulates density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation (inverse problem) and derives two formulations (Type I and Type II).
- Type I: E_p[k(x,·) f] = E_q[k(x,·)] for any kernel k; Type II: E_p[k_t(x,·) f] ≈ q(x) for delta-like kernels k_t.
- Ill-posed integral equations are solved with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding closed-form kernel-matrix solutions (FIRE family).
- Theoretical contributions include concentration bounds and convergence rates for FIRE using Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and sub-manifolds.
- Practical contribution: a fully unsupervised model selection method, Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), for choosing kernel and regularization parameters.
- Empirical validation shows FIRE methods perform favorably against baselines like KMM and LSIF on regression and classification tasks.
- Strength: technically strong, elegant formulation, and rigorous theory supporting claims.
- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition with explicit algorithm formulas enabling straightforward implementation.
- Strength: original perspective connecting density-ratio estimation to classical integral-equation/operator theory.
- Strength: significance due to broad applicability, computational efficiency, and practical model selection.
- Weakness: need more intuition for why Type I matching of kernel expectations ensures convergence to q/p.
- Weakness: motivation for the mixed L2,p and L2,q norm loss (Equation 9) is not fully explained; when is it preferable?
- Weakness: clarify importance-sampling motivation—if q samples are available, why estimate q/p instead of direct Monte Carlo?
- Weakness: discuss relation between RKHS smoothness assumption on f=q/p and smoothness of p and q, and note requirement p(x)>0 where q(x)>0.
- Weakness: convergence rates depend strongly on dimension d; include practical discussion of curse of dimensionality and usable dimensional ranges.
- Weakness: justify choice of test functions in CD-CV (random linear functions and half-space indicators) and sensitivity to that choice.
- Weakness: situate the work relative to other operator-theoretic ML papers (e.g., Grünewälder et al. 2012) for broader context.
- Overall recommendation: excellent paper with minor clarifications needed; strong acceptance recommendation.
