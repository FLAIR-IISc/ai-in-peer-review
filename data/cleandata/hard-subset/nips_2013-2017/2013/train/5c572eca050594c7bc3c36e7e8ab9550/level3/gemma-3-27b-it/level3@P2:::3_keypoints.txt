- Proposes a novel formulation of density ratio estimation as an inverse problem solved via Fredholm integral equations and regularization (FIRE algorithms).
- Uses RKHS framework to implement algorithms and enable theoretical analysis.
- Introduces CD-CV, a new unsupervised model selection method addressing model selection in unsupervised/semi-supervised settings.
- Provides theoretical analysis with concentration bounds and convergence rates.
- Experimental results show competitive performance on several datasets.
- Fundamental assumption of underlying densities is implicit and often unstated, which may be unrealistic in many ML contexts.
- The dominating measure ν (w.r.t. which p and q are defined) is not specified, undermining the validity of the Fredholm formulation and analysis.
- Zero–ν-measure sets and cases where densities do not exist (or p is zero) can render the framework ill-defined or ambiguous.
- Domain of x is not specified (Rd vs. general topological space), and positivity of p is not stated—modeling assumptions are unclear.
- Theoretical results rely heavily on Gaussian kernel and smoothness assumptions; generalization to other kernels or less smooth densities is unclear.
- Analysis lacks discussion of practical limitations imposed by its assumptions.
- Experimental comparisons with baselines (TIKDE, LSIF, KMM) are incomplete and lack statistical significance testing.
- Choice of test functions for CD-CV appears arbitrary and sensitivity to that choice is not explored.
- Overall recommendation: reject due to substantial issues around density assumptions and missing modeling details; revisions addressing these points could make the work significant.
