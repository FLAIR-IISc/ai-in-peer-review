- Proposes FIRE: formulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solved with RKHS regularization.  
- Main contributions: inverse-problem reformulation, flexible regularization framework, Gaussian-kernel theory (Euclidean/manifold), and CD-CV unsupervised model selection.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough for Gaussian kernels, providing concentration bounds and convergence rates for Type I and II settings.  
- Authors clearly distinguish formulations and relate work to KMM and LSIF, documenting prior connections.  
- CD-CV is a practical and useful unsupervised model selection method addressing an important challenge.  
- Experimental results on classification/regression are competitive with state-of-the-art methods (KMM, LSIF).  
- Paper is well-written and logically organized, with clear presentation of ideas.  
- Core theoretical framework omits specification of the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), a critical oversight.  
- Fails to state domain assumptions for x (R^d vs. general space) and does not require/clarify positivity of p(x), undermining validity.  
- Neglects that many ML distributions lack densities (discrete or mixed), so the density-ratio formulation may not apply in practice.  
- Theoretical results may be invalid or ambiguous due to ignored measure-theoretic issues (sets of ν-measure zero).  
- Novelty is moderate: offers a different perspective (Fredholm) but relies on standard RKHS regularization techniques.  
- Significance is limited by strong density assumptions that restrict applicability to real-world ML problems.  
- Suggested fixes: specify ν, state domain and p>0 assumptions, discuss limitations when densities do not exist, and clarify extensions to non-density cases.  
- Recommendation: reject due to fundamental theoretical shortcomings that undermine the main claims despite useful practical contributions.
