- Problem: Estimating the density ratio q/p for importance sampling and covariate shift, reformulated as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind using kernel integral operators.

- Two settings: Type I uses the identity K_p(q/p)=K_q(1) when samples from both p and q are available; Type II uses K_{t,p}(q/p) ≈ q for local kernels when q is known analytically at p-samples.

- Method: Solve the integral equations with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding the Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimators (FIRE).

- Practical algs: FIRE provides simple, closed-form solutions that are easy to implement within standard kernel-method frameworks.

- Model selection: Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), an unsupervised hyperparameter selection method leveraging the importance-sampling identity.

- Comparison: Argues L2-norm-based formulation is more amenable to principled cross-validation than RKHS-norm objectives like KMM; contrasts with KMM and LSIF.

- Theory: Provides concentration bounds and convergence rates for the estimators (analysis given for Gaussian kernels) and extends results to Euclidean and manifold settings.

- Strength — originality: Novel formulation connecting density-ratio estimation to inverse-problem/operator-inversion theory.

- Strength — unsupervised tuning: CD-CV addresses a major practical challenge (unsupervised hyperparameter selection) convincingly.

- Strength — theoretical completeness: Detailed theoretical analysis supports technical soundness.

- Weakness — convergence rates: Rates in Theorem 1 appear slow with logarithmic dependence on λ; question whether polynomial rates are possible under stronger spectral or smoothness assumptions.

- Weakness — smoothness assumption: Analysis assumes q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev), which has problematic implications for support mismatches and potential singularities; needs deeper discussion of practical consequences.

- Weakness — supplementary material: Supplement contains numerous typos/errors hindering verification; must be thoroughly revised (examples: Lemma 5 likely Ws^2 not W2^2 and missing term; lines ~675/686 have incorrect expressions though Eq.31 seems correct; line ~1147 integration domain typo).

- Recommendation: Paper is interesting and practically valuable (especially CD-CV) but acceptance should be conditional on major revision of the supplement and clearer discussion/justification of theoretical assumptions and rates.
