- Paper frames density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and gives two formulations (Type I using samples from p and q, Type II using samples from p and pointwise q evaluations) with Tikhonov-RKHS solutions (FIRE).
- Provides finite-sample concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings, and proposes an unsupervised model-selection method (CD-CV).
- Connects to prior work: KMM, LSIF/KLIEP, RKHS operator-regularization literature, heat-kernel/semigroup analyses, and likelihood-ratio minimax theory.
- Theory is well-motivated but key analytic steps (e.g., approximate-identity claims) are deferred to the supplement and need precise justification and citations.
- Theorems present rates, but the relation to minimax-optimality and dependence on dimension/smoothness/constants needs clarification.
- Type II’s approximation bias (Kt,p r ≈ q) and joint scaling of t and λ require explicit discussion and quantification.
- Empirical section is promising but comparisons need strengthening (include KMM where applicable, well-tuned LSIF, variance/error bars) and add simple 1D toy plots of true vs estimated ratios.
- Practical experiments should report time/memory complexity, ablate Type I vs Type II use-cases, and test CD-CV sensitivity to choice/number of test functions U.
- The paper should explain why plug-in KDE ratios fail (compounded bias/variance, instability in low-density regions) and include a controlled experiment comparing to TIKDE.
- Clarify whether and how nonnegativity and normalization of r are enforced (as in KMM/LSIF) and discuss impact on stability and low-density regions.
- Paper is generally well written but would benefit from a short primer on Tikhonov regularization, clearer distinction between loss/kernel k and regularizer kH, and explicit assumptions for Kt acting as an approximate identity (boundaries, rates).
- Provide references and preprocessing details for datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS), plus seeds and code for reproducibility.
- Novelty: framing ratio estimation as Fredholm equation with RKHS Tikhonov regularization is elegant and appears novel; CD-CV is a useful unsupervised selection criterion.
- Significance: framework is flexible, simple, theoretically grounded, and relevant to covariate shift and related tasks; manifold analysis is an added strength.
- Main weaknesses: some analytic steps are sketched rather than fully justified, empirical comparisons lack thorough diagnostics, and positivity/normalization constraints are not discussed.
- Recommendation: leaning accept conditional on clarifying theoretical approximations, relating rates to minimax bounds, strengthening experiments and diagnostics, and adding illustrative toy examples.
- Reviewer confidence: moderate (main paper read carefully; proofs in supplement not checked).
