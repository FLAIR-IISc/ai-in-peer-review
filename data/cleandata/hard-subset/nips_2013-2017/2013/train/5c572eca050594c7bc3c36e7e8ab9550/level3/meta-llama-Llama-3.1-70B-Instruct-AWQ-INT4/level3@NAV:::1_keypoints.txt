- Proposes a novel density ratio estimation method by reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Leverages regularization and RKHS tools enabled by the Fredholm reformulation.  
- Introduces two formulations (Type I and Type II) that differ in kernel usage and density assumptions.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel.  
- Assumptions and analytical techniques are clearly explained and well-presented.  
- Presents experiments on regression and classification tasks showing the proposed FIRE method performs well versus KMM and LSIF.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Strength: solid combination of novel formulation, theoretical rigor, and empirical validation.  
- Limitation: lacks a clear explanation of why the proposed method is superior to existing methods like KMM and LSIF.  
- Limitation: insufficient discussion of practical, real-world applications and use cases.  
- Limitation: experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets and could be expanded.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include more comparison/explanation and practical discussion.  
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.
