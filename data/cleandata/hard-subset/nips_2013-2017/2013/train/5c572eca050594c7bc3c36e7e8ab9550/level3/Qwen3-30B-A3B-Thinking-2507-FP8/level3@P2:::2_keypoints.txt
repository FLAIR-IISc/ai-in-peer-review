- Proposes FIRE: reformulates density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solved via Tikhonov regularization in RKHS, with Type I/II variants and CD-CV for unsupervised model selection.  
- Originality: Fredholm perspective is novel and enables an L2-based formulation that permits principled unsupervised CD-CV unlike KMM.  
- Theoretical rigor: Provides detailed convergence rates on Euclidean spaces and manifolds using spectral theory and links to classical inverse problems.  
- Empirical validation: Competitive results on regression/classification benchmarks; CD-CV reliably selects parameters and outperforms kernel-dependent alternatives.  
- Convergence-rate concerns: Theorem 1 contains slow factors (e.g., −t / log λ), and stronger spectral assumptions might be needed for polynomial rates.  
- Strong smoothness assumption: Requiring q/p ∈ W^2_2(Ω) is restrictive and may fail for sharp discontinuities; impact on importance weights (p/q) is not discussed.  
- Supplement contains errors: Typos and mathematical mistakes in the supplement impede verification and must be corrected.  
- Comparison to prior work: Clearly differentiates from KMM (CV suitability) and LSIF (nonparametric and theoretical guarantees), and connects to manifold/spectral literature.  
- Significance: Addresses the unsupervised model-selection gap for density-ratio estimation, important for covariate shift and importance sampling, and opens avenues for inverse-problem approaches.  
- Recommendation: Accept pending minor revisions to discuss spectral assumptions, smoothness limitations, and to fix supplementary-material errors.
