- Proposes a novel density-ratio estimation framework by casting it as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind solved via RKHS regularization.  
- Introduces two methods: Type I (importance-sampling equation as L^2 RKHS regularization) and Type II (kernel locality → 0 as regularization).  
- Derives convergence rates for both methods with Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds, providing explicit error bounds and confidence intervals (Theorems 1 and 3).  
- Presents CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation) for reliable unsupervised kernel/parameter selection, addressing kernel-dependence of RKHS objective values.  
- Establishes connections to prior work (KMM, LSIF), noting differences such as natural out-of-sample extension in the RKHS approach.  
- Strength: principled theoretical framework linking density-ratio estimation to classical inverse problems and spectral analysis.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical results covering Euclidean and manifold settings with explicit bounds.  
- Strength: practical impact of CD-CV, with experiments showing it outperforms other parameter selection methods in regression and classification.  
- Strength: clear organization, precise mathematical formulation, and thorough experimental validation including comparisons to KMM, LSIF, and TIKDE.  
- Weakness: slow convergence rate in Theorem 1 (includes a (-t / log λ) factor); authors do not investigate whether stronger spectral assumptions could yield polynomial rates.  
- Weakness: strong assumption that q/p lies in a smooth function class may be unrealistic; if q/p is smooth, p/q can behave poorly, causing potential numerical instability.  
- Weakness: supplementary material contains errors hindering verification (e.g., Lemma 5 W_s^2 vs W_2^2 and corrected bound D1 (t/-log λ)^2 + λ^{1-α} D2 ||f||_2^2; errors at lines 675, 686 contradict Eq.31; integral domain at line 1147 should be ||ξ||^2 ≥).  
- Quality/clarity/originality/significance: high technical quality, excellent clarity, strong originality (Fredholm perspective), and significant practical impact for transfer learning and unsupervised model selection.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to discuss possibility of polynomial convergence under stronger assumptions and to correct the supplementary material errors.
