- Proposes a novel reformulation of density ratio estimation (q/p) as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind solved via RKHS regularization.  
- Introduces the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) algorithm family with theoretical guarantees.  
- Key insight: for kernel k, Ep[k(x,·)f] = Eq[k(x,·)] when f = q/p, enabling empirical operator replacement and regularized inversion.  
- Establishes a principled connection between density ratio estimation and classical integral equation theory.  
- Derives convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and sub-manifolds, e.g., O(n^{-1/(3.5+d/2)}) in Euclidean case.  
- Shows improved rates when data lie on lower-dimensional sub-manifolds, mitigating curse of dimensionality.  
- Introduces CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation), an unsupervised model selection method tailored to this problem.  
- Theoretical analysis carefully examines the dependence of rates on ambient dimension d and smoothness.  
- Strong empirical results demonstrating competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Clear exposition and explicit comparison to prior work, especially KMM and LSIF.  
- Choice of kernel test functions is principled (RKHS) and analogous to bandwidth control in Gaussian kernels.  
- Method is well-motivated for importance sampling and covariate shift where q/p must be estimated at p-samples.  
- Practical limitation: method becomes ineffective in high dimensions (d > 3) unless data lie on a low-dimensional manifold.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for using the mixed L2,p/L2,q norm versus single norms.  
- Weakness: needs more discussion on smoothness assumptions and the requirement that p be bounded away from zero.  
- Weakness: lacks explicit practical guidance for handling dimensionality limits in real applications.  
- Relation to prior "Smooth Operators" work noted, but this paper differs by framing a Fredholm equation with explicit regularization and convergence proofs.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, significant contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance.
