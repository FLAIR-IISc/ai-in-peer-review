- Reformulates density-ratio estimation w = q/p as a Fredholm integral inverse problem and solves with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS.  
- Proposes two formulations: Type I (uses samples from p and q) and Type II (local kernel form when q(x) known at p-samples).  
- Both formulations admit closed-form kernel solutions via the representer theorem and allow out-of-sample prediction.  
- Introduces CD-CV, a fully unsupervised cross-validation using importance-sampling identities with random test functions.  
- Provides concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Rd and on smooth manifolds.  
- Empirically, FIRE estimators are competitive with LSIF and heuristic TIKDE; KMM is compared conceptually.  
- Strength: clear operator-theoretic unification and extension of prior density-ratio methods, including a principled Type II variant.  
- Strength: simple, closed-form algorithms with natural out-of-sample evaluations (advantage over KMM’s empirical weights).  
- Strength: practical, kernel-independent L2-based CD-CV enabling meaningful hyperparameter selection.  
- Strength: theoretical analysis separates bias and sample error and covers manifold settings.  
- Strength: experiments show robust performance and CD-CV effectiveness across test function families.  
- Weakness: convergence rates are relatively slow and contain sub-polynomial terms like (t/−log λ); stronger source/capacity assumptions might yield polynomial rates.  
- Weakness: strong smoothness assumption q/p ∈ W2^s may be unrealistic when w is unbounded; need discussion of behavior when assumptions fail and sensitivity to support mismatch.  
- Weakness: need empirical quantification of CD-CV’s sensitivity to kernel choice and to mismatches between operator kernel and RKHS kernel.  
- Weakness: no explicit positivity or normalization constraints on f, which can produce negative weights in reweighting applications; propose discussing fixes and their theoretical impact.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—solving linear systems in n requires comments on runtime, conditioning, and use of Nyström or preconditioned CG.  
- Quality: technically sound derivations but supplementary proofs contain many typos and indexing errors that hinder verification.  
- Clarity: generally well-written and pedagogically valuable; should better separate Gaussian-specific assumptions and advise when to use Type I vs Type II.  
- Originality: novel inverse-problem framing and L2,p-regularized RKHS solutions, with CD-CV for unsupervised model selection being practically important.  
- Significance: main practical contribution is reliable unsupervised hyperparameter selection for density-ratio estimation, addressing a key pain point.  
- Requested fixes: clarify coupling of t and λ and potential for polynomial rates under spectral/source assumptions; discuss realism of Sobolev assumptions and implications for unbounded ratios.  
- Supplement corrections needed (specific lemma/equation/index fixes listed) and a cleaned revised supplement for verification.  
- Experiments should add timing/conditioning, sensitivity to m vs. n, and a fair KMM comparison using an out-of-sample extension.  
- Pros: elegant formulation, unsupervised robust CV, theoretical grounding, empirical support.  
- Cons: conservative rates, reliance on strong smoothness, lack of positivity/normalization constraints, and supplement errors.  
- Overall assessment: novel and useful work recommending acceptance after clarifying rate assumptions, discussing smoothness/positivity issues, and thoroughly revising the supplement.
