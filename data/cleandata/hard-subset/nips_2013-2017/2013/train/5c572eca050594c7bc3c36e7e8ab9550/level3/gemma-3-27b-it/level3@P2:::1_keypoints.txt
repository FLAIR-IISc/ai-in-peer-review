- Proposes a novel reformulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation enabling inverse-problem techniques.  
- Develops a flexible family of algorithms (FIRE) using RKHS and regularization theory; algorithms are simple and implementable.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates (explicit for Gaussian kernels).  
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised model-selection method.  
- Paper is well-written and connects clearly to classical operator theory.  

- Lacks an explicit proof or precise citation for the δ-kernel integral approximation claim (∫k_t(x,y)f(x)dx ≈ f(y)+O(t)).  
- Insufficient discussion of the kernel bandwidth t and its relationship to standard KDE bandwidth selection.  
- Needs clarification why the special case q=1 differs from KDE and may avoid the bandwidth→0 limit.  
- Requires a concise explanation/motivation of Tikhonov regularization for non-expert readers.  
- No clear justification why plug-in density estimation is inferior to the proposed integral-equation approach.  

- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive in scope but lacks statistical rigor (e.g., significance testing) and deeper comparison details.  
- Theoretical convergence rates are presented but not discussed relative to minimax optimality or lower bounds.  
- Dataset references (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) are missing, hurting reproducibility.  
- Supplementary proofs are not included in the main text and were not verified by the reviewer.  
- Missing illustrative toy experiments (e.g., 1D plots of true vs. estimated ratios) to build intuition.  

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach with potential to advance density ratio estimation and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: empirical and comparative rigor insufficient; some theoretical and explanatory details need expansion.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing theoretical justification, empirical rigor, and clarity.
