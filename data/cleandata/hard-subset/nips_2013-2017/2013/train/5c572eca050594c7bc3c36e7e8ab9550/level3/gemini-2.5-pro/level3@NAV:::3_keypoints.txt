- Reframes density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, solved via Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding the FIRE family of algorithms.  
- Connects the density-ratio problem to classical analysis and provides a principled way to derive kernel-based estimators.  
- Positions the method relative to prior work (KMM, LSIF) but distinguishes itself by the direct inverse-problem formulation and associated regularization.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.  
- Proposes Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) as an unsupervised model-selection technique.  
- The paper’s main strength is its novel and elegant formulation that naturally leads to simple kernel algorithms.  
- The CD-CV contribution addresses the practical and difficult issue of unsupervised model selection.  
- Major concern: the analysis assumes p and q admit densities with respect to a dominating measure ν, which may not hold in ML settings (e.g., lower-dimensional supports or mutual singularity).  
- The paper does not specify the dominating measure ν, leaving key integrals and interpretations ambiguous.  
- There is no explicit assumption that p(x)>0 everywhere; zeros of p on sets of non-zero measure would make q/p ill-defined and undermine results.  
- These foundational ambiguities weaken the paper’s theoretical soundness and must be clarified for the work to be fully rigorous.
