- Proposes FIRE, a novel density-ratio estimator that reformulates density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral inverse problem using kernel operators.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates (shown for the Gaussian kernel).  
- Introduces CD-CV, a new unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model/parameter selection, reported effective for FIRE.  
- Thorough empirical comparison with existing methods (KMM, LSIF) and demonstrations on classification and regression tasks.  
- Experimental results show FIRE effectively estimates density ratios and helps downstream tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized, though some technical parts may be hard for non-experts.  
- Concerns about strong modeling assumptions, notably the assumed existence of densities (may not hold in ML settings).  
- The dominating measure ν is not specified, raising questions about the formal validity of some results.  
- Lacks clear statement of other modeling assumptions (domain of x, positivity of p), which affects interpretability and applicability.  
- Some technical details and assumptions are insufficiently explicit for full reproducibility or theoretical clarity.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, solid theory, new model-selection method, and strong empirical evidence.  
- Rejection concerns: strong/unspoken assumptions, unspecified dominating measure, incomplete modeling statements, and accessibility for non-experts.
