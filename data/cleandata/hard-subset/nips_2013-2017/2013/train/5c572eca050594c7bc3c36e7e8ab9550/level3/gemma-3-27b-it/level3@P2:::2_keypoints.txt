- Proposes novel density ratio estimation via Fredholm integral equation and Tikhonov regularization in RKHS.  
- Introduces two formulations (Type I and Type II) with theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Presents practical algorithms (FIRE) that are easily implementable.  
- Proposes Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for robust unsupervised parameter selection.  
- Well-motivated connection to importance sampling, transfer learning, and covariate shift adaptation.  
- Principled foundation using classical operator theory enabling established analytical tools.  
- Thorough comparison to related methods (KMM, LSIF) that appropriately positions advantages.  
- Strong theoretical analysis with concentration and error bounds for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.  
- Concern: Theorem 1 suggests slow convergence rate with unfavorable dependence on kernel width t and regularization λ.  
- Suggestion: Discuss whether stronger spectral or smoothness assumptions could yield polynomial rates with fixed t.  
- Concern: Assumption that q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) may be too strong for practical cases.  
- Clarity is generally good but some sections are dense with notation; organization is logical.  
- Experimental section needs more justification for dataset/resampling choices and comparison of computational cost to alternatives.  
- Originality is noteworthy despite prior kernel methods; Fredholm formulation and CD-CV are novel contributions.  
- Significance: CD-CV addresses a major practical challenge, with potential impact across transfer learning and importance sampling.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled approach, strong theory, practical CD-CV, competitive empirical performance, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: slow theoretical rates, strong smoothness assumptions, numerous errors in supplementary material hindering verification.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on correcting errors in the supplementary material before publication.
