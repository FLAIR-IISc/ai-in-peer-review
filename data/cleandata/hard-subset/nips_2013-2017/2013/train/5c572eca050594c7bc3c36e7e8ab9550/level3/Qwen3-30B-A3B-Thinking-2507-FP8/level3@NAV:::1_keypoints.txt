- Proposes FIRE: a novel density ratio estimator by reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Connects density ratio estimation to classical inverse problems and operator theory using Tikhonov regularization in RKHS.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for convergence rates and analyzes Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.  
- Introduces a new unsupervised model selection technique (CD-CV) for density ratio estimation.  
- Paper is well-written with clear connections to related work (e.g., KMM, LSIF).  
- Elegant, principled reformulation is a major strength.  
- Complete proofs are in the supplement, limiting the reviewer’s ability to fully assess technical claims.  
- Key theoretical claim ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t) lacks proper justification (proof or citation).  
- Special case q=1 (density estimation) needs deeper discussion on bandwidth selection versus KDE, since authors suggest bandwidth need not → 0.  
- Tikhonov regularization should be briefly explained for non-expert readers.  
- Paper does not clearly explain why naive plug-in estimation of p and q is inferior to the proposed method.  
- Empirical comparison to prior methods lacks discussion of minimax-optimality and whether corollaries achieve optimal rates.  
- Dataset references for CPUsmall, Kin8nm, and USPS are missing.  
- Recommend adding toy experiments (e.g., 1D plots of true vs estimated p/q) to improve empirical validation.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with strong foundations, but significant theoretical and experimental gaps require revision before acceptance.
