- Proposes a novel generative image-encoding model with non-linear feature combinations and explicit position encoding.  
- Model represents image patches as probabilistic combinations of components, each with its own mask and feature parameters.  
- Designed to capture occlusions and invariances in image patches, addressing a core visual-perception challenge.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a thorough introduction and comprehensive related work.  
- Technical sections clearly describe the model, inference algorithm, and experimental setup.  
- Thorough evaluation on both artificial and natural images, showing meaningful learned components and receptive fields.  
- Results are impressive and components resemble visual-cortex receptive fields.  
- Strengths: novelty, significance, thorough experiments, clear presentation, and discussion of implications.  
- Weakness: model and inference algorithm are complex, potentially hindering implementation and practical use.  
- Weakness: insufficient insight and motivation for the choice of cost function and importance-sampling setup.  
- Concern about potential limitations and generalizability to other domains or tasks.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—reviewer recommends acceptance for NIPS due to novelty and strong presentation.
