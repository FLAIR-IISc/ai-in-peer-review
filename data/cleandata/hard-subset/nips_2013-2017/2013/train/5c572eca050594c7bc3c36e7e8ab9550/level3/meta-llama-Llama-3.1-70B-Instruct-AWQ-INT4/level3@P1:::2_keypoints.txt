- Novel formulation of density ratio estimation as an inverse problem using the Fredholm equation of the first kind.  
- Two new methods proposed (Type I and Type II) with RKHS-norm regularization.  
- Key advantage: enables reliable unsupervised cross-validation for parameter selection.  
- Theoretical contributions: convergence results, concentration bounds, and rates for Gaussian kernels and manifold-supported distributions.  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for effective model selection.  
- Empirical validation: strong experimental performance on regression and classification tasks.  
- Relation to prior work: similar to KMM but with the distinct benefit of CV applicability over RKHS-objective methods.  
- Main contribution: principled framework for constructing algorithms and theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: methodologically novel and potentially broadly applicable (e.g., transfer learning, covariate shift).  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 convergence rate is slow due to a (-t / log λ) factor.  
- Weakness: Strong assumption that q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev).  
- Weakness: Practical issues if p/q behaves poorly in applications.  
- Weakness: Typos and errors in the supplementary material hamper verification of theoretical claims.  
- Suggestion: investigate stronger assumptions on q/p to achieve polynomial convergence rates.  
- Suggestion: discuss practical implications of the smoothness assumption on q/p.  
- Suggestion: correct and improve the supplementary material to fix typos/errors.  
- Suggestion: explore additional applications such as transfer learning and covariate shift.  
- Originality: proposed methods and framework are new and distinct from existing approaches.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential wide applicability and solid theoretical foundations.  
- Clarity and quality: paper is well-written, mathematically clear, technically sound, and experimentally convincing.
