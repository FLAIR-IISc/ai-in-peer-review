- Proposes novel density-ratio estimation by casting it as a Fredholm integral inverse problem and introduces the FIRE algorithm family.  
- Reformulates q/p estimation as an integral equation with two practical formulations: Type I (samples from p and q) and Type II (q known at p samples).  
- Builds algorithms using regularization and RKHS methods, yielding a principled theoretical framework.  
- Provides concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on R and on smooth d-dimensional submanifolds.  
- Introduces unsupervised model selection via Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV).  
- Experimental results show effectiveness and favorable comparisons to KMM and LSIF.  
- FIRE algorithms are flexible, simple, and easy to implement.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and technically strong.  
- Main weakness: slow convergence rate due to the (-t / log λ) factor.  
- Strong smoothness assumption (q/p in Sobolev-like class) may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Supplementary material has many typos/errors that impede verification of proofs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with unsupervised model selection and strong experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: slow rates, strong function-class assumptions, and flawed supplementary material.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10; overall recommended for acceptance.
