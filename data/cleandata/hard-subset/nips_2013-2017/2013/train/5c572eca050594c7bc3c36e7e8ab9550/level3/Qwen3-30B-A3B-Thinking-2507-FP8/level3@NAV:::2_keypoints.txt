- Proposes a novel density-ratio estimation framework by reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation and deriving two algorithms (Type I and Type II) with RKHS regularization.
- Establishes a principled connection between density-ratio estimation and inverse problems in operator theory.
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised parameter-selection method enabled by the L² formulation.
- Key theoretical claim: the L² formulation permits meaningful cross-validation, addressing KMM’s kernel-dependent objective problem.
- Provides detailed convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds, demonstrating theoretical rigor.
- Empirically validates CD-CV across multiple datasets and shows practical benefits.
- Shows clear advantages over KMM and LSIF in parameter selection without requiring labeled data.
- Concern: Theorem 1’s convergence rate appears slow (due to a (-t/log λ) factor); stronger spectral assumptions might yield polynomial rates with fixed t.
- Concern: Strong assumption that q/p lies in a smooth Sobolev space; paper omits discussion of cases where p/q behaves poorly in practice.
- Concern: Supplementary material contains typos and errors (e.g., Lemma 5 W_s^2 vs W_2^2 and other line-specific mistakes), which hinder verification of theoretical results.
- Overall assessment: Technically sound and significant for unsupervised learning; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
