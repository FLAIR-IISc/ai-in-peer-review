- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm inverse problem and proposes FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator).
- Positions FIRE relative to prior work (KMM, LSIF) and links to classical operator theory.
- Provides theoretical convergence rates and concentration bounds for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings.
- Offers flexibility via different norms/losses and Type I/II formulations.
- Introduces CD-CV for unsupervised model/parameter selection.
- Mathematical derivations are generally clear and well-presented.
- Implicitly assumes densities exist, a strong limitation that can invalidate the framework for measures without densities.
- Fails to specify the dominating measure ν in the integral equations, undermining the validity of results.
- Does not address handling of sets of zero measure, potentially invalidating theoretical claims.
- Omits explicit assumption about positivity of p; if p=0 in regions, q/p is undefined and analysis breaks.
- Domain of x is not clearly specified; unclear whether results hold beyond Euclidean settings.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and does not thoroughly study the impact of test-function choices in CD-CV.
- Practical concerns about computational complexity, notably kernel matrix inversion in high dimensions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, solid theoretical analysis, simple implementability, and practical CD-CV method.
- Arguments against acceptance: fundamental density assumption, unspecified dominating measure, zero-measure/positivity issues, and limited experiments.
- Overall: interesting and rigorous approach but fundamental theoretical gaps weaken the contribution.
- Recommendation: reject but encourage revision addressing density existence, specifying the dominating measure, handling zero-measure sets, and expanding experiments.
