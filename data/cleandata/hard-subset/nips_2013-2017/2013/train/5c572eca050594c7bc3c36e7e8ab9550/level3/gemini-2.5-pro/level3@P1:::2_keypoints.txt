- Reframes density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation (Type I and Type II identities).
- Proposes FIRE: Tikhonov-regularized RKHS solutions with closed-form kernel-matrix estimators.
- Type I uses E_p[(q/p) k] = E_q[k]; Type II uses local kernels where E_p[(q/p) k_t] ≈ q(x).
- Connects to existing methods: KMM is a special case; shares ties with LSIF, but differs in objective and model selection.
- Main practical contribution: CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-validation method enabled by the L2-based FIRE objective.
- FIRE framework is flexible (different norms, Type I/II) and simple to implement (linear algebra on kernel matrices).
- Theoretical analysis is included but yields very slow convergence rates (terms like (t / -log λ)).
- Suggestion: consider stronger spectral or smoothness assumptions to potentially obtain faster, polynomial rates.
- Analysis assumes q/p lies in a smooth function class, which is strong and may fail when q or p vanish in regions.
- Supplementary material contains typos and errors (e.g., Lemma 5 Sobolev space, incorrect bounds, errors in cited lines/limits), hindering verification.
- For acceptance: novel principled formulation, effective CD-CV for unsupervised model selection, and encouraging experiments.
- Against acceptance: slow theoretical rates, strong assumptions with unclear practical impact, and flawed supplementary proofs.
- Recommendation: accept if authors substantially revise and correct the supplementary material and address convergence/assumption concerns.
