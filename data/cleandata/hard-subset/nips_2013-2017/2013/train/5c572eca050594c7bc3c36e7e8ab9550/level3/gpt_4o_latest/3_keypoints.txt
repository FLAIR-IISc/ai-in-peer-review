- Proposes FIRE, a novel Fredholm integral equation–based method for density ratio estimation using RKHS regularization.
- Grounds the approach in classical operator theory and formulates density ratio learning as an inverse problem.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: concentration bounds and convergence rates (including for Gaussian kernels and manifold settings).
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for parameter selection.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness and computational efficiency on density ratio estimation and covariate-shift classification tasks versus baselines.
- Novelty: connects density ratio estimation to Fredholm integral equations, offering a fresh perspective beyond KMM and LSIF.
- Theoretical rigor: detailed error analysis for Euclidean spaces and sub-manifolds strengthens the method’s credibility.
- Flexibility: supports multiple norms, regularizers, and both Type I (density) and Type II (arbitrary function) settings.
- Experimental validation: extensive comparisons to methods like TIKDE and LSIF show superior performance in tested scenarios.
- Ambiguity in assumptions: unclear specification of the domain of x (e.g., R^d vs. general spaces) and positivity of p(x).
- Dominating measure ambiguity: the choice of dominating measure ν (Lebesgue vs. counting) is not explicitly stated, affecting theoretical clarity.
- Limited experimental comparisons: lacks comparison to KMM in settings where out-of-sample extension is unnecessary.
- Presentation complexity: paper is dense and highly technical, which may reduce accessibility for non-experts; needs clearer intuition and exposition.
- Recommendation: strong candidate for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify assumptions and improve clarity.
- Acceptance arguments: novel principled approach, strong theoretical foundations, and demonstrated practical utility.
- Rejection concerns: unclear assumptions and dominating measure, limited baseline comparisons, and overly technical presentation.
