- Presents a novel formulation of density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation, enabling classical analysis and regularization techniques.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow with logical presentation and headings.  
- Original approach that differs from existing methods and includes theoretical analysis like concentration bounds and Gaussian-kernel convergence rates.  
- Technically sound overall, with a principled framework for algorithm construction and analysis.  
- Missing theoretical detail for a key claim: the approximation ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t) lacks proof or references.  
- The special case q=1 is mentioned but not sufficiently discussed; needs clarification on how it contrasts with traditional KDE and its implications.  
- Paper should include a brief, self-contained explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks dataset details (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) and preprocessing information.  
- Would benefit from additional toy/synthetic experiments to illustrate method behavior and quality.  
- Could expand discussion on broader implications, potential applications, and limitations of the approach.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: novelty, clear presentation, and relevance to an important ML problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical detail in parts, weak treatment of q=1, and sparse empirical documentation.
