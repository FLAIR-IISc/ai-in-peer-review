- Frames density ratio estimation as an inverse problem using Fredholm integral equations.
- Proposes FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator), leveraging RKHS regularization.
- Provides theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels.
- Introduces an unsupervised model selection method, CD-CV.
- Motivation grounded in importance sampling and covariate shift applications.
- Strong connection to classical operator theory, enabling use of established analytical tools.
- Elegant, principled formulation combining kernel methods with regularization.
- Algorithms appear straightforward to implement and practically appealing.
- Theoretical guarantees are provided, increasing confidence in method performance.
- Major weakness: assumes existence of densities p and q, which is restrictive in many ML contexts.
- Omits specification of the dominating measure ν, undermining interpretation and validity.
- Analysis does not adequately address issues from sets of ν-measure zero or cases where p may be zero.
- Modeling assumptions (domain of x, p>0) are not clearly stated, causing potential ambiguity.
- Comparative analysis with related methods (KMM, LSIF) is superficial and lacks deeper trade-off discussion.
- Experimental section needs clearer dataset descriptions, setup details, error bars, and statistical tests.
- Acceptance arguments: novel formulation, principled RKHS approach, theoretical analysis, practical algorithm and CD-CV.
- Rejection arguments: strong density assumption, missing dominating measure, theoretical ambiguities, weak comparisons, and under-detailed experiments.
- Overall recommendation: reject unless authors address density existence assumption and clarify theoretical framework.
