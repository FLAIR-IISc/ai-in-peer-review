- Paper reformulates density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and proposes two solver types (Type I via importance-sampling identity; Type II via local kernel approximation), solved with Tikhonov RKHS regularization (FIRE).

- Positions work relative to KMM and LSIF, showing KMM as a special case, and emphasizes a principled unsupervised parameter selection via an L^2 Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) procedure.

- Strength: Novel formulation connecting density-ratio estimation to classical inverse problems, offering a principled and flexible framework.

- Strength: Practical unsupervised model selection (CD-CV) that enables reliable tuning of kernel and regularization parameters across settings.

- Strength: Solid theoretical analysis (convergence rates) and comprehensive experiments demonstrating FIRE and CD-CV outperform alternatives in practice.

- Weakness: Convergence rates in Theorem 1 are slow (due to a (-t / log λ) factor); authors could explore spectral assumptions or stronger smoothness to obtain polynomial rates.

- Weakness: Strong smoothness assumptions on q/p may be unrealistic or limit applicability (e.g., p/q may not be smooth if p is near zero); discuss practical implications and limitations.

- Weakness: Supplementary material contains multiple typos and incorrect expressions that obstruct verification and must be corrected for reproducibility.

- Specific supplement corrections requested: Lemma 5’s W2^2→Ws^2 and corrected final bound form; fix incorrect lines (e.g., 675, 686) and change integral domain at line 1147 to "||ξ||^2 ≥".

- Overall assessment: High-quality, significant contribution—especially CD-CV—with recommendation for acceptance pending thorough correction of the supplementary material.
