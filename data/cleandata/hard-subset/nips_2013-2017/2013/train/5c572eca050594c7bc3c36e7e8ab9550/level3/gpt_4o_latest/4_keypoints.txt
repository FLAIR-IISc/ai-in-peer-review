- Paper formulates density ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation solved via RKHS regularization, proposing the FIRE estimator and the CD-CV parameter selection method.

- Theoretical contribution: principled inverse-problem formulation with rigorous analysis, convergence rates, and error bounds (notably for Gaussian kernels).

- Algorithmic contribution: FIRE algorithms are simple, computationally efficient, easy to implement, with explicit representer-theorem solutions.

- Novel parameter selection: CD-CV provides an unsupervised method for tuning and is shown to be practically useful.

- Experimental validation: extensive comparisons to methods like LSIF and TIKDE show strong empirical performance and computational efficiency, especially under covariate shift.

- Relevance: tackles a timely problem with applications in transfer learning and statistical inference, linking classical analysis and kernel methods.

- Weakness: the combined L2,p and L2,q cost function lacks clear motivation and linkage to practical applications.

- Weakness: convergence rates depend on dimensionality, but implications for high-dimensional settings are insufficiently discussed.

- Weakness: relationship between smoothness of q/p and smoothness of q and p is mentioned but not deeply explored.

- Weakness: convergence intuition is given for Type II but a similar intuitive argument for Type I is missing.

- Weakness: choice of test function family {k(x,·)} is not well-motivated and alternatives are not explored.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach with rigorous guarantees, practical algorithms, and strong empirical results relevant to the community.

- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation for key design choices, limited discussion of high-dimensional applicability, and incomplete exploration of smoothness assumptions.

- Recommendation: overall strong paper with minor issues; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions to clarify motivations and assumptions.
