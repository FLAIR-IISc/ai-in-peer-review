- Reformulates density-ratio estimation r = q/p as a Fredholm first-kind inverse problem and solves it with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, proposing closed-form FIRE estimators.  
- Proposes two estimators: Type I fits Kp r ≈ Kq 1 in L2(p); Type II fits Kt,p r ≈ q in L2(p) using localized kernel kt with t → 0.  
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised L2(p)-based cross-validation procedure for selecting kernel and regularization parameters.  
- Positions work relative to KMM and LSIF/KLIEP but emphasizes the inverse-problem view and L2-based CV as more comparable across kernels.  
- Provides concentration bounds and convergence rates on Euclidean domains and smooth manifolds.  
- Methodology is technically sound with clear operator-theoretic grounding and representer-theorem derivations.  
- Experiments convincingly show CD-CV’s advantage for parameter selection.  
- Concern: Theorem 1 convergence appears slow due to the (-t/log λ) coupling; unclear if polynomial-in-n rates can be achieved with fixed t.  
- Suggest clarifying which terms drive the rates and consider spectral/source assumptions (e.g., Caponnetto & De Vito) or stronger smoothness on q/p to obtain polynomial rates.  
- Clarity: main paper is clear and well organized, but the supplementary material has many typos that impede verification.  
- Assumptions: request discussion of realism of assuming q/p lies in a smooth class and note the practical issue that p/q may be poorly behaved; ask for practitioner guidance/diagnostics.  
- KMM comparison: ask for empirical demonstration that RKHS-objective values are not comparable across kernels to justify L2-based CD-CV.  
- Specific supplementary corrections: Lemma 5 should use Ws^2 (not W2^2) and the bound should read D1 (t/−log λ)^2 + λ^{1−α} D2 ||f||_2^2.  
- Specific supplementary corrections: lines 675 and 686 are incorrect; Eq. (31) is correct.  
- Specific supplementary corrections: line 1147 integral domain should be “||ξ||^2 ≥”.  
- Pros: elegant inverse-problem formulation with two practical variants; strong case for L2 unsupervised CV; solid theoretical development.  
- Cons: conservative convergence dependence, strong smoothness assumptions with limited discussion, and supplement quality issues hindering reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on clarifying rates and fixing the supplementary material.
