- Proposes FIRE: density-ratio estimation via Fredholm integral equation solved by Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS with closed-form representer solutions and out-of-sample evaluation.
- Develops two settings: Type I (samples from p and q, estimate Kp r = Kq 1) and Type II (q known at p-sample locations, estimate K_{t,p} r ≈ q).
- Provides concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on R^d and on compact smooth manifolds.
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-validation method that tests the importance-sampling identity on random test functions to select kernel and regularization parameters.
- Connects to prior operator-inversion and spectral regularization literature (Tikhonov, kernel inverse-problem views) and relates to KMM and LSIF/KLIEP.
- FIRE offers explicit regularization and a natural out-of-sample RKHS estimator; an RKHS-loss variant can be seen as a regularized KMM analogue.
- Unlike LSIF/KLIEP, FIRE solves the operator equation directly without a finite basis expansion; CD-CV decouples estimation and testing functions.
- The integral-equation formulation and empirical Tikhonov objectives are technically sound with closed-form solutions.
- Several key approximations (e.g., Gaussian kernel approximate identity) lack precise statements, conditions, and citations and should be formalized as lemmas.
- Manifold and boundary effects are only briefly discussed; dependence of bias on curvature and boundary needs clearer assumptions and constants.
- Reported convergence rates (e.g., n^{-1/(3.5 + d/2)}) are nonstandard and should be related to known minimax rates and explained via bias-variance-regularization trade-offs.
- Complexity and scalability are not sufficiently addressed; time/memory costs and applicability of Nyström or randomized solvers for large n should be discussed.
- Empirical evaluation: CD-CV is clever, but comparisons omit KMM in some experiments and should include KMM where feasible.
- Recommend adding simple 1D toy examples with known p, q and ground-truth r to visualize bias/variance and sensitivity to λ and t.
- Provide dataset references and preprocessing details for CPUsmall, Kin8nm, and USPS, and report sensitivity of CD-CV to number/type of test functions.
- Compare CD-CV to importance-weighted CV (IWCV) as a baseline for model selection.
- Improve clarity by adding a primer on Tikhonov regularization in inverse problems and explicitly relating operator, source conditions, and λ’s role.
- Expand discussion of kernel width t versus regularization λ, and clarify differences from KDE, especially when q = 1.
- Explain why naive plug-in ratio estimators are inferior due to amplified denominator errors and instability in low-p regions.
- Make explicit which results depend on using the same kernel k_H and which allow different analysis/regularization kernels.
- Originality: Fredholm reformulation plus RKHS Tikhonov and CD-CV is a novel, coherent package with insightful links to KMM and manifold analysis.
- Significance: FIRE is practically valuable for covariate shift and related tasks due to simplicity, out-of-sample estimators, and unsupervised model selection.
- Pros: principled inverse-problem formulation, simple closed-form algorithms, theoretical bounds for Euclidean/manifold settings, and practical CD-CV.
- Cons: informal justifications for key approximations, unclear optimality of rates, limited experimental comparisons, missing dataset citations, and limited scalability discussion.
- Suggestions: add precise lemma/citation for Gaussian approximate identity, compare rates to minimax results, include 1D visualizations and more KMM comparisons, discuss complexity and low-rank approximations, and give CD-CV guidance and IWCV comparisons.
- Overall: Strong, well-written paper addressing an important problem; lean to accept conditional on clarifying theoretical justifications and strengthening empirical comparisons.
