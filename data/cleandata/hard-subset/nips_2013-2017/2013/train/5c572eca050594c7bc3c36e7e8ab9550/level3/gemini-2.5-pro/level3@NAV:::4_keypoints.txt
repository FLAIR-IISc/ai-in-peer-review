- Addresses density-ratio estimation f = q/p by reformulating it as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind.  
- Derives two estimators from Ep[k(x,·)f] = Eq[k(x,·)] and Ep[k(x,·)f] ≈ q(x).  
- Uses Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS to produce the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) algorithm.  
- Connects to prior methods: KMM as an unregularized variant and LSIF via adapted validation ideas.  
- Proposes an unsupervised cross-validation scheme (CD-CV) for model selection—practically useful.  
- Theoretical strength: provides convergence guarantees under smoothness assumptions.  
- Implementation strength: algorithm is straightforward to implement and experimentally validated.  
- Request for intuition: explain why test functions {k(x,·)} ensure convergence for Type I, analogous to delta-kernel for Type II.  
- Request for motivation: justify the mixed L2,p and L2,q cost in Eq. (9) and specify target applications benefiting from it.  
- Applicability question: clarify advantage of estimating f=q/p for importance sampling when q samples are available versus using empirical Eq[h] directly.  
- Assumptions & dimensionality: discuss relations between smoothness of p, q, and f, the p bounded-away-from-zero condition, and practical dimensionality limits given convergence-rate dependence on d.  
- Comparison request: contrast using {k(x,·)} test functions with operator-based perspectives such as Grünewälder et al. ("Smooth Operators").  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, timely paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; strongly recommended for acceptance with suggested clarifications.
