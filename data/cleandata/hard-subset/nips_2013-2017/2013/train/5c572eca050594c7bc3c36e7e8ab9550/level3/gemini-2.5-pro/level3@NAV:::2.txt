This paper presents a novel approach to density-ratio estimation by reformulating the problem as an inverse problem governed by a Fredholm integral equation. The authors propose two methods, Type I and Type II, which apply Tikhonov regularization in a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) to solve these equations. This work connects to a significant body of literature on importance estimation, such as Kernel Mean Matching (KMM) and LSIF, but provides a distinct framing through classical operator theory.

The primary strength and most significant contribution of this work is the development of a principled method for unsupervised parameter selection. The proposed L2-based objective function enables a reliable cross-validation procedure (CD-CV), directly addressing a major challenge for methods like KMM, whose RKHS-based objectives are kernel-dependent and thus not directly comparable for model selection. This practical advantage makes the proposed FIRE algorithm a valuable addition to the practitioner's toolkit. The experimental validation successfully demonstrates the effectiveness of this approach, highlighting the work's originality and significance.

However, the paper has some weaknesses that should be addressed. From a theoretical perspective (Quality), the convergence rate presented in Theorem 1 is slow, particularly due to the `(-t / log λ)` factor. The authors should discuss whether stronger spectral assumptions, akin to those in Caponnetto & De Vito (2007), or stronger assumptions on the ratio `q/p` itself could yield more practical polynomial convergence rates. Furthermore, the strong assumption that `q/p` lies in a smooth function class (e.g., a Sobolev space) warrants more discussion regarding its practical implications, as it may imply that the inverse ratio `p/q` is not well-behaved.

Regarding Clarity and reproducibility, the supplementary material requires significant revision. It contains numerous typos and errors that impede the verification of the theoretical claims. For instance, in Lemma 5, W2^2 should be Ws^2 and the bound is misstated; similarly, lines 675 and 686 are incorrect, and the integral domain at line 1147 needs correction.

In summary, this paper introduces an original and significant approach to density-ratio estimation, with its main contribution being the effective cross-validation scheme. While the core idea is strong, the paper would be greatly improved by strengthening the theoretical discussion and carefully proofreading the supplementary material to ensure the technical results are verifiable.
