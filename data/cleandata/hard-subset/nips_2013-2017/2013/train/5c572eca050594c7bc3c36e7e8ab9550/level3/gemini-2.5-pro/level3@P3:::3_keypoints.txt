- Summary: Reframes density ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation and proposes FIRE estimators using RKHS Tikhonov regularization, with theory and experiments supporting the approach.

- Originality: Novel and elegant formulation connecting density ratio estimation to classical integral-operator theory.

- Algorithmic contribution: Flexible, principled family of closed-form kernel-based algorithms (FIRE) that separate inverse problem from regularization choices.

- Model selection: Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), a practical unsupervised method for hyperparameter selection using importance-sampling identities.

- Theoretical support: Provides concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.

- Weakness — density assumptions: Theory assumes existence of densities p and q w.r.t. an (unspecified) dominating measure, a strong and potentially unrealistic assumption.

- Weakness — support issues: Paper omits discussion of measure-zero sets and necessary assumptions (e.g., q absolutely continuous w.r.t. p or p bounded away from zero) for q/p to be well-defined and stable.

- Weakness — lack of formalism: Problem setup lacks precise specification of the dominating measure, domain, and function space (e.g., Sobolev class) for the true ratio, reducing rigor and clarity.

- Question: Clarify precise assumptions on p and q, especially support relations and whether p(x)>0 is assumed on the domain.

- Question: How does the framework extend to estimating the Radon-Nikodym derivative dq/dp when densities w.r.t. Lebesgue may not exist?

- Question: How sensitive is CD-CV performance to the choice of test-function class U?

- Conclusion/recommendation: Novel, practical, and empirically strong work worth accepting, but authors should explicitly state and discuss all measure/density/support assumptions and limitations in the final version.
