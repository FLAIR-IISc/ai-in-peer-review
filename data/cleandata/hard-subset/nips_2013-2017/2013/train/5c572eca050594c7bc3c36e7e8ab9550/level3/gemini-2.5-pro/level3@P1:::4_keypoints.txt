- Reframes density-ratio estimation (q/p) as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind — a novel inverse-problem viewpoint.  
- Derives two formulations: Type I (uses samples from both p and q via Ep[k(·) f] = Eq[k(·)]) and Type II (uses a delta-like kernel and known q).  
- Solves the ill-posed integral equations with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, producing the FIRE family with closed-form kernel-matrix solutions.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis for Gaussian kernels, including concentration bounds and convergence rates in R^d and on smooth submanifolds.  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), a fully unsupervised model-selection method using the importance-sampling identity.  
- Empirical results show FIRE and CD-CV perform favorably compared to KMM and LSIF across multiple tasks.  
- Strength — originality: elegant, principled connection between density-ratio estimation and classical analysis.  
- Strength — theoretical soundness: rigorous convergence analysis under standard smoothness assumptions.  
- Strength — practicality/completeness: end-to-end package (algorithm, theory, model selection, experiments) with simple implementations.  
- Strength — significance: addresses covariate shift/importance weighting with a flexible method likely to influence future work.  
- Question — Type I intuition: unclear why minimizing ||K_p f − K_q 1|| guarantees f → q/p and what “rich enough” kernel formally means.  
- Question — mixed norm motivation: need clearer justification (practical or theoretical) for combining L2,p and L2,q versus using L2,p alone.  
- Question — importance-sampling context: if q samples are available (Type I), clarify when estimating q/p is still necessary or beneficial (e.g., variance reduction).  
- Question — smoothness assumptions: clarify conditions on p and q (e.g., p bounded away from zero, smoothness) that ensure q/p lies in required Sobolev class.  
- Question — dimensional dependence: discuss practical implications of strong ambient-dimension dependence and feasible dimensional ranges for the method.  
- Question — CD-CV test functions: justify choice of test function family (random linear functions, half-space indicators) and sensitivity to that choice.  
- Question — relation to prior operator-based work: situate the Fredholm approach relative to “learning with operators” literature (e.g., Grünewälder et al. “Smooth Operators”).
