- Introduces two novel density-ratio estimation methods framed as Type I and Type II Fredholm equations using regularization.  
- Type I uses importance sampling with L² regularization and RKHS terms; Type II uses a kernel with a locality parameter to ensure consistency.  
- Provides theoretical convergence results (error bounds and rates) for the proposed estimators.  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for unsupervised parameter selection in density-ratio estimation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across diverse datasets and resampling scenarios, outperforming KMM and LSIF in accuracy and efficiency.  
- Novel formulation connects density-ratio estimation to spectral and Fourier analysis, offering a principled framework for algorithm development.  
- RKHS-based regularization offers flexibility and stability in estimation.  
- Experimental results are robust and supported by theoretical analysis, adding rigor to the work.  
- Convergence rates in Theorem 1 are slow; stronger assumptions could yield polynomial rates.  
- The assumption that q/p lies in a “nice” function class (e.g., Sobolev) is restrictive and needs discussion of practical implications.  
- Supplementary materials contain numerous typos and errors, hindering verification and reproducibility.  
- Limited discussion and comparison regarding computational complexity and scalability to high-dimensional data.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on fixing supplementary errors and addressing practical implications of assumptions.
