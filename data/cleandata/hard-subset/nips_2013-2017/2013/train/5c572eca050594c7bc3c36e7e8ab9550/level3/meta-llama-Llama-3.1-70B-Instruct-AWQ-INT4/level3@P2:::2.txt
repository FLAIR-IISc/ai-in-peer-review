This paper presents a novel approach to density ratio estimation by formulating the problem as an inverse problem in terms of an integral operator corresponding to a kernel, known as the Fredholm problem of the first kind. The authors provide a principled framework for constructing algorithms and analyzing them theoretically using the techniques of regularization and Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). The resulting family of algorithms, called Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator (FIRE), is flexible, simple, and easy to implement.

The main idea of the paper is to reformulate the problem of estimating the ratio of two functions, q(x)p(x), where p is given by a sample and q(x) is either a known function or another probability density function given by a sample, as an integral equation. This formulation, combined with the techniques of regularization, leads to a simple and effective algorithm for density ratio estimation. The authors provide two types of formulations: Type I, which is useful when q is a density and samples from both p and q are available, and Type II, which is useful when the values of q are known at the data points sampled from p.

The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis of their algorithms, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel for densities defined on R and smooth d-dimensional sub-manifolds of the Euclidean space. They also propose a completely unsupervised technique, Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), for model selection and demonstrate its usefulness in experimental results.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms. The authors also provide a detailed comparison with existing methods, such as Kernel Mean Matching (KMM) and Least Squares Importance Sampling (LSIF), and highlight the advantages of their approach.
