- Proposes a novel density-ratio estimation approach by casting the problem as a Fredholm integral equation (inverse problem) using kernels, yielding the FIRE family of algorithms.  
- Develops a principled algorithmic framework within the kernel learning setting that is simple and flexible.  
- Introduces an unsupervised model-selection method, CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation).  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel.  
- Demonstrates encouraging empirical performance on several real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains technical details accessibly.  
- Offers a thorough related-work review and positions the approach relative to prior contributions.  
- Presents a unified framework applicable to tasks like importance sampling and handling covariate shift.  
- Key limitations: assumes p and q exist and are well-behaved, which may not hold in practice.  
- Lacks clear guidance on choosing the dominating measure ν, affecting result validity.  
- Needs more discussion on modeling assumptions, particularly handling cases where p is not positive everywhere.  
- Evaluation: high scores (Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance = 9/10) and a recommendation to accept for publication.
