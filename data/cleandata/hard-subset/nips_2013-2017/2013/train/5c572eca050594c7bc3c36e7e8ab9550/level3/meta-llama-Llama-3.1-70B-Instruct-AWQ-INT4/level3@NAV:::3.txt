This paper proposes a novel approach to estimating the ratio of two densities using a Fredholm integral equation. The method, called FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator), is based on reformulating the density ratio estimation problem as an inverse problem in terms of an integral operator corresponding to a kernel. The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis of the method, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel. The paper also introduces a new unsupervised technique for model selection, called CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation), which is shown to be effective in selecting the optimal parameters for the FIRE algorithm.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings. The authors also provide a thorough comparison of their method with other existing methods, including Kernel Mean Matching (KMM) and Least Squares Importance Sampling (LSIF). The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the FIRE algorithm in estimating the density ratio and its application to classification and regression tasks.

However, there are some concerns regarding the assumptions made in the paper. For example, the authors assume the existence of densities, which is a strong assumption in machine learning. Additionally, the paper does not specify the dominating measure ν, which affects the validity of the results. Furthermore, the paper does not provide a clear statement of modeling assumptions, such as the domain of x and the positivity of the density p.
