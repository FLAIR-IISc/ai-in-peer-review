- Reformulates density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation with two types: Type I (uses samples from p and q via E_p[k(·)f]=E_q[k(·)]) and Type II (local kernel approximation when q is known).

- Solves the ill-posed inverse problem via Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding closed-form, non-iterative FIRE algorithms using kernel matrix operations.

- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on R^d and on d-dimensional submanifolds.

- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-validation procedure leveraging the importance sampling identity for hyperparameter selection.

- Empirical results show strong performance versus LSIF and a kernel-density baseline, and validate the CD-CV selection method.

- Strength: principled, original formulation connecting density-ratio estimation to functional analysis/Fredholm problems.

- Strength: strong theoretical soundness with detailed proofs and rates (Theorems 1 & 3) under smoothness/boundedness assumptions.

- Strength: practical simplicity — FIRE methods are easy to implement and have closed-form solutions.

- Strength: novel and effective unsupervised model selection (CD-CV) addressing a hard practical problem.

- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation that compares methods and tests model selection.

- Question: Need more intuition for why minimizing ||K_p f - K_q 1|| forces f→q/p (role of K_p injectivity or dense range).

- Question: Request clarification on the motivation and benefits of the mixed L2,p / L2,q norm in Eq.(9) — when is mixing superior to p-weighted error alone?

- Question: Need clarification on the practical scenarios where estimating q/p is preferable to direct Monte Carlo over q-samples (variance reduction, covariate-shift learning, etc.).

- Question: Ask for discussion on the Sobolev smoothness assumption for q/p, conditions under which it holds, and the requirement that p be bounded away from zero on q's support.

- Question: Concern about strong polynomial dependence on ambient dimension d in convergence rates — discuss practical dimension limits and curse of dimensionality.

- Question: Justification requested for CD-CV’s choice of test function family (random linear functions and half-spaces), sensitivity to this choice, and possible alternatives (e.g., kernel-based families).

- Question: Suggest positioning relative to other operator-based methods (e.g., “Smooth Operators” by Grünewalder et al., ICML 2012) for broader context.

- Overall assessment: high technical quality, clear writing, original and significant contribution; recommended for acceptance.
