- Paper proposes FIRE: density-ratio estimators formed by casting ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and solving via Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS (Type I and Type II variants).
- Type I uses samples from p and q to match kernel-smoothed moments; Type II uses samples from p and pointwise q with a local kernel Kt and the approximate identity Kt,p(q/p) ≈ q.
- Provides closed-form solutions via the representer theorem, an unsupervised model-selection scheme (CD-CV), and nonasymptotic convergence bounds for Gaussian kernels on R^d and manifolds.
- Connects density-ratio estimation to classical inverse problems and spectral theory, complementing earlier work (KMM, LSIF/KLIEP, RKHS density estimation).
- Conceptual novelty: reformulating ratio estimation as a Fredholm inverse problem is elegant and principled.
- Presentation is clear, with concrete algorithms and a useful unsupervised model-selection procedure.
- Theory strength: nonasymptotic bounds and rates in Euclidean and manifold settings, which are relatively uncommon.
- Practical appeal: simple closed-form estimators, favorable computational profile, and sensible baselines in experiments.
- Missing justification for the key smoothing/approximate identity claim ∫ kt(x,y)f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t); needs proof or precise citation and norm specification.
- Comparative theory is lacking: rates are not positioned relative to minimax-optimal results (e.g., Nguyen et al.) or RKHS density-estimator rates.
- Empirical comparisons are limited, especially versus RKHS-based plug-in methods like Mukherjee & Vapnik [12], so it's unclear when FIRE outperforms plug-in or parametric bases.
- The special case q=1 (density estimation / inversion) needs clarification: bandwidth roles differ from KDE and affect bias/regularization trade-offs.
- Proofs are only in the supplement and were not checked by the reviewer; key lemmas/assumptions should be in main text.
- Missing dataset citations; more toy experiments would help illustrate estimator behavior.
- Detailed request 1: add precise statement/proof/citation for Gaussian heat-kernel smoothing identity and specify L2,p vs uniform O(t).
- Detailed request 2: clarify q=1 case and how bandwidth t controls ill-posedness versus KDE smoothing, and whether fixed t with λ→0 can ensure consistency under source conditions.
- Detailed request 3: include a brief primer on Tikhonov regularization and its bias–variance trade-off as applied here.
- Detailed request 4: explain why plug-in (estimate p and q then divide) is inferior and how FIRE avoids instability in low-density regions.
- Detailed request 5: relate Corollaries 2 and 4 to known minimax rates, state implied smoothness assumptions, and discuss potential improvements under spectral decay/source conditions.
- Detailed request 6: add empirical comparisons to SV density-estimation plug-in methods, report compute time and sensitivity to hyperparameters, and compare behavior in low-density regions to KMM/LSIF.
- Detailed request 7: add dataset references for CPUsmall, Kin8nm, and USPS.
- Detailed request 8: include simple 1D toy illustrations showing true vs estimated q/p across n, t, λ and plots of Kt,p f vs q.
- Detailed request 9: ensure reproducibility by stating key lemmas/assumptions in the main text and releasing code.
- Overall assessment: technically solid, original and significant, but needs stronger justification of approximations, clearer positioning of rates, and expanded empirical comparisons before final acceptance.
