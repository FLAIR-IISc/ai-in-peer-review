This paper presents a novel approach to estimating the ratio of two densities, q/p, by reformulating it as an inverse problem using the Fredholm equation of the first kind. The authors leverage kernel methods and regularization techniques to develop a flexible and principled framework for constructing algorithms and analyzing them theoretically. The resulting family of algorithms, FIRE, is shown to be simple, easy to implement, and effective in various settings.

Strengths:

1. The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the problem of density ratio estimation and its importance in various applications.
2. The authors present a well-motivated and novel approach to solving the problem using the Fredholm equation of the first kind.
3. The paper provides a detailed theoretical analysis of the algorithms, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel.
4. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on various datasets, including regression and classification tasks.
5. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their methods and results.

Weaknesses:

1. The paper could benefit from a more intuitive explanation of why optimizing over the set of test functions {k(x,·)} ensures convergence in equality (i), similar to the bandwidth intuition in equality (ii).
2. The choice of cost function is not fully motivated, and the authors could provide more justification for mixing L2,p and L2,q norms in equation (9).
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationships between the smoothness of p and q and the smoothness of the ratio q/p.
4. The authors could provide more insight into the dependence of their method on the dimension d and for what dimensions the method is practically useful.
5. The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with prior work, such as "Smooth Operators" (Grunewalder et al.).
