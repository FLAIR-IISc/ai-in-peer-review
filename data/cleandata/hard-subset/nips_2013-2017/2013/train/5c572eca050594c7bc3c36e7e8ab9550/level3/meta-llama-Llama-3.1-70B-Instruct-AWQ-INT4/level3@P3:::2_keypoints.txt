- Proposes two new density-ratio estimation methods (Type I and II) based on the Fredholm equation framework.  
- Provides a principled, flexible algorithmic framework and connects it to prior work.  
- Main novelty: enables effective unsupervised cross-validation for parameter selection.  
- Demonstrates advantages over existing methods (e.g., KMM) with theoretical convergence results.  
- Presents detailed theoretical analysis and convergence guarantees.  
- Experimental validation shows strong practical performance on regression and classification tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Convergence rate in Theorem 1 is slow (contains a (-t / log λ) factor); stronger assumptions might be needed for polynomial rates.  
- Strong assumption that q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) may be unrealistic and could make p/q behave poorly in practice.  
- Supplementary material contains many typos and errors, hindering verification of theoretical results.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with recommendation to accept with minor revisions.  
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.
