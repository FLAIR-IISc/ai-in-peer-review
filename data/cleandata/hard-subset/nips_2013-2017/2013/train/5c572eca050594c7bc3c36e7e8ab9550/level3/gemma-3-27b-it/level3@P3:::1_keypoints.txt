- Proposes a novel reformulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and solves it using RKHS-based regularization.
- Introduces FIRE algorithms with detailed theoretical analysis, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels.
- Well-written paper addressing an important problem with connections to importance sampling and covariate shift.
- Key theoretical step ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t) is inadequately justified; needs an explicit derivation or tighter error bound in the paper.
- Special case q=1 suggests a KDE-like density estimator that avoids bandwidth→0; the role of bandwidth t in FIRE vs KDE needs detailed comparison and bias–variance analysis.
- Needs theoretical guidance or data-driven rules for choosing t and discussion of how that relates to KDE bandwidth selection methods.
- Should include a brief, self-contained explanation of Tikhonov regularization and its connection to the RKHS norm for reader accessibility.
- Claim that direct ratio estimation (FIRE) outperforms separate estimation of p and q lacks a concrete explanation—needs discussion of variance, data efficiency, or other benefits.
- Empirical evaluation is incomplete: more comparisons to prior state-of-the-art (e.g., [12]) and broader benchmarks are required.
- Must discuss minimax-optimality: present known minimax rates in Euclidean/manifold settings and compare them to the paper’s convergence rates (Corollaries 2 and 4).
- Experiments lack dataset citations (e.g., UCI links) harming reproducibility; add explicit references for CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS.
- Visual illustrations are missing: include toy 1D plots of p, q, and estimated p/q and visualizations on complex datasets to build intuition.
- Overall recommendation: promising and conceptually strong, but acceptance should be conditional on addressing theoretical justifications, comparative evaluation, minimax discussion, and experimental details.
