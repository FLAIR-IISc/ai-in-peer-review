- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solves it via regularization in RKHS.  
- Introduces the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family for estimating q(x)/p(x) and links the problem to classical inverse operator theory.  
- Uses spectral analysis to provide theoretical guarantees and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.  
- Proves convergence rates that depend on dimension and smoothness (e.g., O(n^{-1/(3.5+d/2)}) for Euclidean domains) with improved rates on submanifolds.  
- Develops CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation) for unsupervised model selection, addressing a key practical challenge.  
- Extends and clarifies relationships to prior methods (KMM, LSIF), offering out-of-sample extension and stability via regularization.  
- Justifies use of test function set {k(x,·)} and relates this choice to the “Smooth Operators” framework.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation coupled with simple, practical algorithms and clear exposition.  
- Strength: strong experimental validation across multiple datasets, with thorough baseline comparisons (KMM, LSIF, TIKDE).  
- Weakness: needs stronger, more intuitive justification for optimizing over kernel feature space {k(x,·)} and why this ensures Ep[k(x,·)f]=Eq[k(x,·)].  
- Weakness: the mixed L2,p and L2,q cost in Eq.(9) requires motivation over using a single norm, especially for importance sampling applications.  
- Weakness: should better discuss how smoothness of p and q relates to smoothness of q/p and conditions ensuring p is bounded away from zero in practice.  
- Weakness: dimensionality dependence of rates needs deeper discussion, including practical limits as dimension increases.  
- Overall assessment: significant, original contribution to density ratio estimation and covariate shift adaptation; technically sound, well-written, and experimentally validated.
