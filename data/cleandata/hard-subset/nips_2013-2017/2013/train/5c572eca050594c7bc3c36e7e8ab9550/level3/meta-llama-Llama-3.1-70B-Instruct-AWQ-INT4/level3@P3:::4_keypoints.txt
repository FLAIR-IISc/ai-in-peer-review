- Proposes a novel density-ratio estimation method by reformulating it as an inverse problem using the Fredholm equation of the first kind and regularization (FIRE algorithms).  
- FIRE algorithms are flexible, simple, and easy to implement.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel.  
- Experimental results are encouraging and show effectiveness in multiple settings.  
- Leverages kernel methods and test functions with the representer theorem to obtain simple, explicit solutions.  
- Presents a principled framework for algorithm construction and theoretical analysis.  
- Proposes an unsupervised model-selection method (CD-CV) based on a problem-specific performance measure.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes a solid related-work discussion.  
- Concern: Mixed use of L2,p and L2,q cost functions needs clearer motivation and justification.  
- Concern: Lacks discussion of how the smoothness of p and q relates to the smoothness of the ratio q/p.  
- Concern: Insufficient guidance on dependence on dimension d and practical dimensionality limits.  
- Concern: More intuition and justification needed for the chosen family of test functions U.  
- Concern: Should address whether using {k(x,·)} is the principled/right approach for covariate-shift/q-p estimation and relate to prior work (e.g., "Smooth Operators").  
- Overall assessment: High quality, clarity, and originality; strengths outweigh weaknesses and the paper is a valuable contribution.
