- Paper proposes FIRE: a kernel-based Fredholm inverse framework to estimate density ratios w = q/p with two formulations (Type I exact operator identity, Type II approximate identity) solved by RKHS Tikhonov regularization and representer solutions.
- Proposes CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-validation that selects kernel width and regularization by matching moments across p and q using random test functions.
- Provides theoretical finite-sample concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Rd and smooth manifolds using spectral operator tools.
- Relates to prior work (KMM, LSIF/KLIEP, operator-theoretic kernel methods), clarifying connections and differences (regularization, out-of-sample extension).
- Strength: elegant, unifying Fredholm/inverse-problem perspective linking to classical regularization and kernel methods.
- Strength: practical closed-form representer algorithms with flexible loss choices and out-of-sample evaluation.
- Strength: nontrivial theory for Euclidean and manifold settings that complements existing analyses.
- Strength: simple unsupervised model selection (CD-CV) useful in covariate shift scenarios with limited labels.
- Strength: empirical comparisons to LSIF, KMM and baseline; applications to regression/classification under covariate shift.
- Weakness: measure-theoretic foundations underspecified—no dominating measure stated and equalities are not consistently qualified as p-a.e.
- Weakness: missing explicit absolute continuity/positivity assumptions (q ≪ p, lower bounds on p) needed to make ratio and rates valid.
- Weakness: domain and reference measure (Lebesgue vs. Riemannian volume) not always explicit; manifold/boundary issues underclarified.
- Weakness: Type II is presented ambiguously—needs to distinguish density-ratio estimation from general L2(p)-regression when q is arbitrary.
- Weakness: estimator does not enforce nonnegativity or normalization; negative weights and unbounded ratios are not addressed.
- Weakness: CD-CV lacks theoretical guarantees (consistency or relation to the target risk/MMD).
- Weakness: notational typos and ambiguities in theory (corollary exponents, kernel matrix powers, sampling/normalization constants) reduce clarity.
- Weakness: computational complexity and numerical stability concerns (n×n linear systems, kernel products) are not discussed; no large-n approximations suggested.
- Quality: operator-theoretic formulation and derivations are sound but finite-sample bounds rely on unspoken assumptions; empirical section is reasonable but could be expanded.
- Clarity: generally readable and organized but needs explicit modeling assumptions, consistent notation, and correction of theory typos.
- Originality: moderate—clean unification and manifold analysis add value but approach is closely related to KMM/LSIF and prior inverse-problem views.
- Significance: potentially useful for covariate shift practitioners if foundational assumptions and constraints are clarified; manifold treatment and unsupervised tuning are valuable.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled operator view, simple practical algorithms with out-of-sample extension, theoretical bounds including manifold case, and useful unsupervised tuning.
- Arguments against acceptance: imprecise measure-theoretic assumptions, lack of positivity/constraint discussion, no CD-CV theory, notation issues, and unaddressed computational scalability.
- Key suggestions: state σ-finite dominating measure and p/q as Radon–Nikodym derivatives p-a.e.; make measure explicit on Rd/manifold and clarify boundary cases.
- Key suggestions: discuss support mismatch and unbounded ratios (trimming/localized regularization), consider constrained variants enforcing nonnegativity/normalization, and empirically compare.
- Key suggestions: provide theoretical connection or justification for CD-CV (e.g., relation to MMD), fix notation/typos, detail matrix constructions and complexity, and suggest scalable approximations (Nyström, CG, preconditioning).
- Overall evaluation: promising and well-motivated but needs explicit measure-theoretic assumptions and treatment of support/positivity issues; reviewer leans borderline acceptance contingent on fixes.
