- Proposes FIRE: density-ratio estimators via Fredholm integral equation reformulated and solved with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, with Type I and Type II settings and closed-form kernel solutions plus CD-CV model selection.  
- Relates to prior work: connects to KMM (operator identity) and LSIF (L2,p minimization) and frames density ratio estimation as an inverse problem.  
- Strength: well written and organized; operator-theoretic perspective is interesting and principled for analysis and algorithm design.  
- Strength: Fredholm reformulation appears novel and unifies inverse-problem and kernel-method ideas.  
- Strength: CD-CV is a practical fully unsupervised model-selection scheme and empirical results are encouraging.  
- Weakness: Needs precise justification/proofs or citations for approximations like ∫ kt(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t) in Euclidean and manifold cases.  
- Weakness: Clarify the role of bandwidth t versus KDE—does fixed t produce asymptotic bias, and is consistency achieved only if t → 0 with λ balancing bias-variance?  
- Weakness: Add a brief primer on Tikhonov regularization (ill-posedness, source conditions, role of λ) for non-experts.  
- Weakness: Theoretical explanation is needed for why naïve plug-in q̂/p̂ is inferior (variance inflation, thresholding bias) and how spectral/Tikhonov regularization stabilizes inversion.  
- Weakness: Empirical/theoretical comparisons are incomplete—compare to [12] and recent estimators and discuss minimax-optimality versus known rates (e.g., [14]) under matched smoothness and quantify any gap.  
- Weakness: Provide dataset references (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) and include simple 1D toy experiments showing true vs estimated q/p and p,q to illustrate bias/variance behavior.  
- Pro for acceptance: Novel, principled reformulation with strong writing and useful unsupervised model selection.  
- Con for acceptance: Gaps in theoretical justification, optimality discussion, comparative evaluation, and missing explanatory context/citations.  
- Overall: Promising paper that would be a strong contribution after addressing the theoretical gaps, comparisons, and explanatory additions.
