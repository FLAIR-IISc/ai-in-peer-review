- Paper proposes estimating density ratio q/p by casting it as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solving it as an inverse problem.
- Authors introduce FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator), using kernels and Tikhonov regularization to obtain a flexible, simple algorithm family.
- Theoretical contributions include concentration bounds and convergence rates (analyzed for the Gaussian kernel).
- The paper presents CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection.
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness on regression and classification tasks.
- Strength: Novel, principled approach to density ratio estimation of interest to ML and statistics.
- Strength: Detailed theoretical analysis strengthens the methodological contribution.
- Strength: Method is practical and easy to implement.
- Weakness: Assumes existence of densities, which is strong and problematic in ML where measures close in metric may lack ν-densities.
- Weakness: Estimating a ratio of densities can be ill-posed from a machine learning perspective.
- Weakness: The dominating measure ν is not specified (e.g., Lebesgue or counting), affecting result validity.
- Weakness: Results may be ambiguous or invalid on sets of ν-measure zero.
- Weakness: Modeling assumptions (domain of x, topological space) are not clearly stated.
- Weakness: Paper does not clarify whether the base density p is assumed strictly positive everywhere.
- Recommendation: Paper is a significant contribution but should address the above concerns; rating 7/10.
