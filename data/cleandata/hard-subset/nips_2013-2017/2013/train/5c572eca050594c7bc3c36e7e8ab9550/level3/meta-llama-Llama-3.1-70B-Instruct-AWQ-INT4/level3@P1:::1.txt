This paper proposes a novel approach to density ratio estimation by reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation. The authors leverage the techniques of regularization and Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) to develop a principled framework for constructing algorithms and analyzing them theoretically. The resulting family of algorithms, known as FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator), is flexible, simple, and easy to implement.

The paper is well-written and addresses an important and interesting problem in the field of machine learning. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of density ratio estimation and its relevance to various applications, including importance sampling and covariate shift. The proposed approach is novel and distinct from existing methods, which is a significant strength of the paper.

However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved. Firstly, some theoretical details are missing, such as a proof or reference for the statement ∫ kt(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t). Additionally, the authors could provide a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with the concept.

The paper also lacks empirical and theoretical comparisons to prior methods, such as [12]. A discussion on the minimax-optimality of the proposed method and the distance of Corollaries 2 and 4 from optimal rates would be beneficial. Furthermore, the authors could provide references for the datasets used in the experiments (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).

To illustrate the performance and quality of the proposed method, the authors could include toy experiments, such as 1D plots of true vs estimated p/q. This would help to provide a clearer understanding of the method's capabilities and limitations.

Overall, the paper is well-written and proposes a novel approach to density ratio estimation. However, some theoretical and experimental details are unclear, and additional comparisons to prior methods would strengthen the paper.
