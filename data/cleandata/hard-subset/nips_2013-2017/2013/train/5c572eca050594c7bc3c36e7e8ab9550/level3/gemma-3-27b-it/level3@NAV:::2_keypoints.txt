- Novel framing: casts density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and connects it to inverse problems.  
- Methodology: proposes two algorithms (Type I and Type II) using L2 regularization in RKHS.  
- Theoretical contributions: provides convergence results and analyses, including rates for Gaussian kernels.  
- CV advantage: enables effective unsupervised cross-validation for parameter selection, claimed as a key advantage over methods like KMM.  
- Kernel-selection critique: correctly argues RKHS objective values are not comparable across kernels, motivating the CV approach.  
- Convergence concern: Theorem 1 has a slow convergence factor (-t / log λ), raising theoretical weakness.  
- Possible fixes: stronger assumptions (e.g., spectral conditions from Caponnetto & De Vito 2007 or tighter q/p bounds) could yield improved polynomial rates.  
- Smoothness assumption issue: the strong assumption that q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) needs further discussion, especially regarding implications for p/q.  
- Experiments: results are encouraging but need broader comparisons beyond KMM and LSIF.  
- Computational claims: asserted improved computational efficiency should be supported by rigorous benchmarking.  
- Supplementary material problems: contains numerous typos/errors (e.g., Lemma 5, lines 675, 686, 1147), which hinder verification of theoretical claims.  
- Required fixes: correct equations and bounds in the supplement are crucial for reproducibility and validation.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel inverse-problem framing, principled RKHS/regularization approach, effective unsupervised CV, and provided theoretical guarantees.  
- Rejection arguments: slow convergence rate, strong smoothness assumption on q/p, and many errors in the supplementary material.
