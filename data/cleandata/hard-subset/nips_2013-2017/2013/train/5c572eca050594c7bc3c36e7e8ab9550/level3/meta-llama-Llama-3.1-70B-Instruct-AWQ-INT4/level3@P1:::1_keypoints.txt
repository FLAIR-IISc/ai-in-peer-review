- Proposes a novel density ratio estimation approach by reformulating it as a Fredholm integral equation and developing FIRE algorithms using RKHS and regularization.  
- FIRE family is flexible, simple, and easy to implement.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and motivation (importance sampling, covariate shift).  
- Approach is novel and distinct from existing methods, which is a major strength.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough but missing some details, e.g., proof or reference for ∫ kt(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t).  
- Authors should add a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for non-expert readers.  
- Paper lacks empirical and theoretical comparisons to prior work (e.g., reference [12]).  
- Need discussion on minimax-optimality and how Corollaries 2 and 4 compare to optimal rates.  
- Provide references for experimental datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Include toy experiments (e.g., 1D plots of true vs estimated p/q) to illustrate performance and limitations.  
- Overall quality: technically sound with solid theoretical grounding, but some minor theoretical gaps remain.  
- Clarity: clear writing and consistent notation throughout.  
- Originality: introduces a new Fredholm-based perspective on density ratio estimation.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential impact on applications like importance sampling and covariate shift.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, solid theory, and demonstrated empirical effectiveness.  
- Rejection concerns: missing proofs/references, lack of comparisons to prior methods, need for explanatory material and toy experiments.
