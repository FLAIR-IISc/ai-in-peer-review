Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes FIRE, a family of density ratio estimators obtained by reformulating density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solving it with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS. Two settings are considered: Type I, which uses samples from p and q to match integral transforms, and Type II, which uses a δ-kernel approximation to relate Kt,p(q/p) ≈ q. The approach connects density ratio estimation to classical inverse problems and yields closed-form kernel solutions with an unsupervised model selection scheme (CD-CV). The work is related to KMM and LSIF: the authors correctly note that KMM can be viewed as an instance of the same operator identity in RKHS and that LSIF minimizes an L2,p discrepancy in a parametric family.

Strengths (clarity, originality, significance):
- Well written and well organized; the operator-theoretic perspective is interesting and provides a principled route to analysis and algorithm design.
- The Fredholm reformulation appears novel in this context and unifies several ideas from inverse problems and kernel methods.
- CD-CV is a practical, fully unsupervised selection scheme and the empirical results are encouraging.

Weaknesses and suggestions (quality, clarity, comparisons):
- Please justify and precisely state conditions for statements of the form ∫ kt(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t) (Euclidean and manifold cases). Provide proofs or cite standard results (e.g., kernel smoothing/heat-kernel approximations with smoothness and boundary conditions).
- The special case q = 1 suggests density estimation without taking bandwidth → 0. Clarify how t plays a different role than in KDE, and whether fixed t yields asymptotic bias; specify if consistency requires t → 0 and how t, λ balance bias-variance relative to KDE.
- Add a brief primer on Tikhonov regularization (ill-posedness, source conditions, role of λ) for readers outside inverse problems.
- Explain theoretically why a naïve plug-in q̂/p̂ is inferior: discuss variance inflation from division by a noisy p̂, thresholding bias, and how the Fredholm approach with spectral/Tikhonov regularization stabilizes the inversion of Kp.
- Empirical/theoretical comparison to prior methods is incomplete. Please compare to [12] and other recent estimators, and discuss minimax-optimality: how do Corollaries 2 and 4 compare to known optimal rates for likelihood ratio estimation (e.g., [14]) under matched smoothness? Quantify the gap.
- Include references for CPUsmall, Kin8nm, and USPS, and add simple 1D toy experiments (true vs. estimated q/p and p,q) to illustrate behavior and diagnose bias/variance.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel and principled reformulation; strong writing; useful unsupervised model selection; promising results.

Cons for acceptance:
- Gaps in theoretical justifications and optimality discussion; limited comparative evaluation; missing explanatory context and dataset citations.

Overall: A nice paper tackling an important problem. With the clarifications and comparisons above, it would make a strong NIPS contribution.
