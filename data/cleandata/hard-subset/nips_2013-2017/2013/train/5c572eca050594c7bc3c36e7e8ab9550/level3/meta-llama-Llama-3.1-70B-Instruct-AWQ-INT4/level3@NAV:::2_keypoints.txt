- Proposes a novel formulation of density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem using the Fredholm equation of the first kind.  
- Introduces two new methods (Type I and Type II) based on the importance-sampling equation with L^2 RKHS regularization.  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and experimental validation demonstrating practical performance.  
- Enables reliable unsupervised cross-validation for parameter selection, a notable practical advantage.  
- Gives detailed theoretical analysis for the Gaussian kernel, including concentration bounds and convergence rates.  
- Main contribution: a principled framework for constructing algorithms and analyzing them theoretically.  
- Weakness: convergence rate is slow due to a (-t / log λ) factor; unclear if stronger assumptions yield polynomial rates.  
- Weakness: relies on the assumption that q/p belongs to a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev), which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: supplementary material contains many typos and errors that hinder verification of theoretical results.  
- Weakness: paper could better discuss practical limitations and extensions to more complex scenarios.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, theoretical guarantees, experimental support, and unsupervised CV capability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: slow convergence, strong smoothness assumptions, and problematic supplementary material.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to correct supplementary typos/errors and encourage addressing identified weaknesses in future work.
