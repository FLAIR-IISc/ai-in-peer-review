- Novel reformulation: Presents a principled density ratio estimation approach via a Fredholm integral equation and RKHS/operator-theoretic framework.  
- Theoretical contributions: Derives estimators for q/p with convergence guarantees and dimension-dependent rates.  
- Empirical validation: Demonstrates strong performance on multiple datasets and covariate shift applications.  
- Builds on prior work: Extends and generalizes methods like KMM and LSIF within a more general operator framework.  
- Technical quality and clarity: Paper is technically sound, derivations are clear, and the writing is accessible.  
- CD-CV parameter selection: Introduces a promising unsupervised model-selection method that addresses a common bottleneck.  
- Request for intuition on Eq.2: Needs a more intuitive explanation of why optimizing over {k(x,·)} ensures convergence in the first integral equality.  
- Bandwidth discussion: Bandwidth selection intuition for Eq.3 is clear, but a similar insight for Eq.2 is missing.  
- Mixed-norm motivation: The choice to combine L2,p and L2,q norms in Eq.9 requires clearer application scenarios and justification.  
- Importance sampling justification: Should clarify when estimating q/p is preferable to estimating q directly.  
- Smoothness and assumptions: Request discussion on how smoothness of p and q affects the ratio and conditions like p bounded away from zero.  
- Practical dimensionality concerns: Needs guidance on computational feasibility and accuracy for different values of dimension d.  
- Test-function family U: The choice of U requires stronger justification or discussion of alternative families.  
- Relation to prior work: Suggest exploring connections to "Smooth Operators" [7] for additional context.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong contribution with theoretical rigor and experiments; minor issues on design justification do not outweigh strengths.
