- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation, connecting it to inverse problems and regularization theory.
- Proposes the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family of algorithms with theoretical convergence guarantees for Gaussian kernels.
- Introduces CD-CV, a novel unsupervised model selection technique, and evaluates it empirically.
- Novelty: the integral-equation perspective provides a significant and different analytical framework from prior work.
- Principled framework: uses Tikhonov regularization and RKHS to allow flexible norms and regularizers.
- Strong theoretical analysis: provides concentration bounds and convergence rates in Euclidean and manifold settings.
- Addresses an important problem and is generally well written and clearly presented.
- Key theoretical claim ∫k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t) requires proof or citation.
- Needs deeper discussion of the Gaussian kernel bandwidth t and how it differs from KDE bandwidth/limit issues.
- A brief, reader-friendly explanation of Tikhonov regularization would improve accessibility.
- Empirical evaluation should compare more comprehensively to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., [12]) and discuss minimax optimality relative to reported rates.
- Dataset details lack references (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS), hindering reproducibility.
- Would benefit from simple illustrative toy experiments (e.g., 1D plots of true vs estimated p/q) for intuition.
- Needs clearer explanation of why plug-in p and q estimation is inferior to the integral-equation approach.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; valuable theoretical insights; practical CD-CV method; clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: some theoretical statements lack justification; empirical comparisons are limited; missing dataset references and illustrative examples.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the noted theoretical, empirical, and clarity issues.
