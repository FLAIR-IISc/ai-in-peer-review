- Novel formulation: frames density ratio estimation as an inverse problem using Fredholm integral equations and RKHS regularization.  
- Two algorithms: Type I (uses standard importance-sampling equation) and Type II (uses localized kernels to approximate the ratio directly).  
- New model-selection method: introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for unsupervised parameter selection.  
- Theoretical analysis: provides convergence bounds for Gaussian kernels and connects to operator/spectral/Fourier analysis.  
- Empirical validation: experiments show competitive performance and demonstrate FIRE’s effectiveness versus baselines.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on and correctly positions itself relative to KMM, LSIF, and KLIEP, highlighting CD-CV’s advantage over KMM.  
- Strength: principled, flexible framework with generally clear exposition despite mathematical complexity.  
- Weakness: convergence rates in Theorem 1 are slow, dependent on both kernel width t and regularization λ.  
- Suggestion: stronger smoothness assumptions or spectral regularization theory might yield improved polynomial rates.  
- Weakness: relies on strong assumption that q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev), which may be restrictive in practice and needs more discussion.  
- Missing discussion: consequences of assuming smooth densities imply a smooth ratio and implications for p/q are not sufficiently addressed.  
- Major concern: numerous typos and errors in the supplementary material (e.g., Lemma 5 and lines 675, 686, 1147) hinder verification of theoretical claims.  
- Acceptance case: novel inverse-problem formulation, practical CD-CV, solid theory, competitive experiments, and clear connections to prior work.  
- Rejection risks: slow theoretical rates, strong smoothness assumptions, and problematic supplementary material that must be fixed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on addressing convergence-rate concerns and correcting the supplementary-material errors.
