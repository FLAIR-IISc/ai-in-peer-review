- Proposes FIRE: a novel kernel-based method that casts density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral inverse problem.  
- Reformulates ratio estimation as an inverse problem enabling regularization and RKHS techniques.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels.  
- Introduces CD-CV, a novel cross-validation–based model selection method shown to be effective.  
- Algorithm is described as flexible, simple, and easy to implement.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent mathematical notation.  
- Main contributions: Fredholm-based estimator, theoretical guarantees, and CD-CV model selection.  
- Strength: method applicability to a wide range of problems (flexibility).  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical treatment clarifies algorithm behavior.  
- Strength: CD-CV is a novel and practically effective selection method.  
- Weakness: Strong assumption of existence of densities is problematic in ML where nearby measures may lack densities.  
- Weakness: Practical implication that density-ratio estimation may be ill-posed under ML-relevant settings.  
- Weakness: Dominating measure ν is not specified (e.g., Lebesgue vs counting), undermining result validity.  
- Weakness: Modeling assumptions (domain of x, topological space) are not clearly stated.  
- Weakness: Positivity of p is not assumed or clarified (is p > 0 everywhere?).  
- Originality: Method and CD-CV are novel contributions.  
- Significance: Theoretical analysis and experiments suggest broad potential applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, solid theory, effective CD-CV, empirical evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: problematic density assumptions, unspecified dominating measure, unclear modeling/positivity assumptions.
