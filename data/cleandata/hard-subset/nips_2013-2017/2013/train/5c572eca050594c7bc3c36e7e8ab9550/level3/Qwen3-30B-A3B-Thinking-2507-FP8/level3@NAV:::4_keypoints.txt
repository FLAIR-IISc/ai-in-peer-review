- Proposes a novel framework reformulating density ratio estimation (q/p) as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solves it with kernel methods and regularization (FIRE algorithms).
- Develops FIRE algorithms for both Type I (samples from q available) and Type II (q values known at p-sampled points) settings.
- Connects density ratio estimation to classical inverse problem/operator theory and provides convergence guarantees under smoothness assumptions, with rates depending on ambient dimension.
- Provides rigorous error bounds for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and on manifolds.
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised model selection method for tuning hyperparameters.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance compared to state-of-the-art methods like KMM and LSIF.
- Lacks an intuitive explanation for why optimization over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence in equality (i), unlike the bandwidth intuition given for equality (ii).
- Motivation for mixing L2,p and L2,q norms in Equation (9) is unclear—why not use a consistent norm throughout?
- Does not sufficiently discuss when estimating q/p is preferable to directly estimating q for importance sampling and related applications.
- Needs deeper analysis of how smoothness of p and q relates to smoothness of q/p and how that affects convergence rates.
- Choice and justification of the test function family U is missing and should be clarified.
- Should better position and compare the approach to prior work (e.g., "Smooth Operators" by Grünewälder et al.) to clarify novelty.
- Practical utility for higher-dimensional problems (beyond d = 3–4) is not explicitly addressed and requires discussion.
