This paper proposes to estimate the density ratio q/p by casting it as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solving the resulting inverse problem via Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS. Two formulations are offered: Type I uses samples from P and Q to match Kp(q/p) to Kq1; Type II uses δ-like kernels so that Kt,p(q/p) ≈ q and requires only samples from P and pointwise values of q on those samples. The authors derive closed-form solutions (via representer theorem), introduce an unsupervised model-selection procedure (CD-CV), and provide concentration and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings. Experiments compare favorably against KMM and LSIF in covariate shift tasks.

Relation to prior work: The operator-theoretic view connects to inverse problems and RKHS regularization (e.g., De Vito et al.; Smola & Schölkopf), and the target task aligns with KMM and LSIF/KLIEP in density-ratio estimation and covariate shift. The Fredholm framing and manifold analysis are interesting additions.

Strengths
- Elegant inverse-problem formulation with simple, implementable closed forms.
- Theoretical rates with explicit t, λ trade-offs; extension to manifolds is valuable.
- CD-CV is practical and unsupervised, and experiments are convincing.

Weaknesses and required clarifications
- Foundational measure-theoretic assumptions are missing. The ratio q/p exists only when Q ≪ P (Radon–Nikodym), yet the paper assumes densities without specifying a dominating measure ν (Lebesgue on R^d, counting, or Riemannian volume on manifolds). Results can change with ν, and sets of ν-measure zero may invalidate identities.
- The domain and modeling assumptions are not stated crisply (is X = R^d, a general measurable space, or a submanifold equipped with its volume measure?). State (X, B, ν), p = dP/dν, q = dQ/dν, and explicitly assume Q ≪ P and p > 0 P-a.e.
- Nonnegativity and integrability of q/p are not enforced; the estimators can be negative or undefined on p = 0. Consider positivity constraints or estimating the RN derivative dQ/dP directly, and discuss support mismatch.
- Ill-posedness/injectivity: conditions under which Kp yields an identifiable solution for q/p are not given.
- CD-CV may depend strongly on the choice of test functions U; discuss robustness and potential degeneracies.
- Scalability (O(n^3)) and large-scale approximations (e.g., Nyström, CG) are not discussed.

Quality: Solid analysis given the stated setting, but the absence of explicit measure-theoretic assumptions threatens validity. Clarity: Generally well written, but notation around q/p and measures should be tightened. Originality: Moderate; novel synthesis of known tools. Significance: Potentially high if foundations are clarified.

Arguments for acceptance
- Principled operator-theoretic framework with new analysis and practical selection.
- Competitive empirical performance and simplicity.

Arguments against acceptance
- Incomplete foundational assumptions (ν, absolute continuity, positivity).
- Ambiguity around support mismatch and estimator positivity.
- Limited discussion of identifiability and scalability.

Overall: borderline reject; recommend revision addressing the above core assumptions and clarifications.
