- Proposes reformulating density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solved by Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, introducing the FIRE family of algorithms for Type I and Type II settings.  
- Provides theoretical analysis and concentration bounds for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds, plus a novel unsupervised model selection method (CD-CV).  
- Key novelty is the integral-equation viewpoint connecting density ratio estimation to operator theory and spectral analysis, distinct from prior kernel methods.  
- Empirical results show strong performance versus KMM, LSIF, and TIKDE on multiple datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Major theoretical gap: missing justification for the approximation ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t) used in the Type II formulation.  
- Proofs are deferred to supplementary material and were not independently verified by the reviewer.  
- Paper should compare its convergence rates to minimax optimal rates and discuss how close Corollaries 2 and 4 are to those rates.  
- Lacks explanation of why a naive plug-in approach (estimating p and q separately) is inferior to the proposed method.  
- Clarity is generally good and algorithms are implementable, but the paper should briefly explain Tikhonov regularization for unfamiliar readers.  
- Recommends adding simple 1D toy experiments showing true vs. estimated density ratios to improve illustration of performance.  
- Originality: the Fredholm reformulation and CD-CV model selection are genuine and significant conceptual contributions.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental problem with useful applications, though the paper should better discuss computational efficiency and provide dataset references.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical framework, comprehensive analysis, practical algorithms with strong empirical results, and useful CD-CV technique.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing theoretical justification for a key approximation, no minimax comparison, unclear plug-in comparison, missing toy experiments, and some dataset references absent.  
- Overall recommendation: strong submission with minor revisions needed (address theoretical gaps, minimax comparison, and add illustrative experiments); recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
