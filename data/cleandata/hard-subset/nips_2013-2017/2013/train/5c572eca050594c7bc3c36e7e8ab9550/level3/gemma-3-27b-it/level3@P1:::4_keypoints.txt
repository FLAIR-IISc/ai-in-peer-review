- Paper proposes a novel framework to estimate the ratio q/p by casting it as a Fredholm integral inverse problem solved with RKHS regularization.
- Method translates density ratio estimation into a tractable kernel-based optimization with two formulations (Type I and Type II) depending on sample availability.
- Theoretical contributions include concentration bounds and convergence rates (for Gaussian kernels), providing strong theoretical guarantees.
- Experiments on regression and classification show the FIRE algorithm performs competitively with KMM and LSIF.
- Strength: Novel framing linking density ratio estimation to Fredholm inverse problems.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis that supports reliability and scalability.
- Strength: Algorithms are given in explicit form, facilitating implementation.
- Strength: Comprehensive empirical evaluation across datasets and baselines.
- Strength: Proposes an unsupervised parameter selection method (CD-CV) addressing model selection challenges.
- Weakness: Lacks intuitive explanation for representing solutions via kernel functions and why optimization over that space converges, especially for Type I.
- Weakness: Insufficient motivation for combining L2,p and L2,q norms in the cost function (Eq. 9) and its practical relevance.
- Weakness: Unclear when estimating q/p is preferable to directly estimating Eq in importance sampling applications.
- Weakness: Needs discussion on interaction between smoothness of p, q, q/p and the role of regularization, and conditions (e.g., p bounded away from zero) for well-behaved solutions.
- Weakness: Theoretical dependence on ambient dimension d is stated but practical limitations and feasible dimensionality are not discussed.
- Weakness: Choice of test functions U for CD-CV (linear and half-space) lacks justification or evidence of broad suitability.
- Weakness: Connection to prior work (e.g., “Smooth Operators”) is brief; a deeper comparison and positioning is needed.
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound approach with promising empirical results and clear writing.
- Argument against acceptance: Key motivational and limitation discussions (kernel choice, cost function, dimensionality) need strengthening.
- Overall: Strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to enhance intuition, motivation, and discussion of limitations.
