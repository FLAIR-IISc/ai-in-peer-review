- Proposes reformulating density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and introduces the FIRE algorithm family.  
- FIRE is claimed to be flexible, simple, and easy to implement.  
- Builds on prior work (KMM, LSIF) but novelty is in the integral-equation formulation and principled framework.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel.  
- Introduces CD-CV, a fully unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection.  
- Missing proof or reference for the kernel identity ∫ kt(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t).  
- Role of bandwidth in the method is unclear, especially versus traditional kernel density estimation.  
- Authors claim plug-in (estimate p and q separately) is inferior but don’t clearly explain why.  
- Empirical and theoretical comparisons to prior methods (KMM, LSIF) are not clearly explained.  
- No minimax-optimality discussion or comparison of achieved rates to optimal rates.  
- Experiments omit dataset references (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) and lack toy experiments to illustrate behavior.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Originality is somewhat unclear due to building on existing work, but the problem significance is high.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with revisions to address missing details and clarifications.  
- Overall rating 8/10, confidence 8/10.
