- Paper introduces a novel Fredholm integral equation formulation for density ratio estimation, using Tikhonov regularization in RKHS and proposing the FIRE algorithms for two settings (Type I: samples from p and q; Type II: samples from p and evaluable q), plus CD-CV for model selection.

- Strength: original and significant reformulation connecting density ratio estimation to classical inverse problems.

- Strength: clear, well-written derivation and flexible, implementable kernel-based framework.

- Strength: detailed theoretical analysis with concentration bounds and convergence rates for Euclidean and manifold settings.

- Weakness: key theoretical steps (e.g., approximation ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t)) lack proof or precise citation and need justification.

- Weakness: insufficient conceptual discussion of the kernel bandwidth t, especially how its role here differs from KDE and implications for consistency.

- Weakness: paper should briefly explain Tikhonov regularization for non-expert readers to improve accessibility.

- Weakness: paper needs an explicit motivation contrasting the proposed direct ratio estimation with unstable plug-in KDE ratio methods.

- Weakness: theoretical rates (Corollaries 2 and 4) lack context and comparison to known minimax-optimal rates; discuss relation to existing results (e.g., [14]).

- Weakness: experiments are limited — recommend adding a 1D toy example (plots of p, q, true ratio, FIRE estimate) and stronger empirical comparisons to prior methods like KMM.

- Weakness: reproducibility could be improved by citing datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).

- Weakness: all proofs are in the supplement and were not checked by the reviewer; ensure clarity and correctness in main/supplement.

- Overall assessment: solid, elegant contribution recommended for acceptance, but revisions are needed to fill theoretical gaps, expand experiments, and clarify context.
