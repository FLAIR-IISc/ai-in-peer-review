- Proposes FIRE: formulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and introduces a regularized RKHS estimator.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on R^d and on manifolds.  
- Introduces a novel unsupervised model selection method (CD-CV) to address practical tuning.  
- Experimental results show competitive performance against KMM and LSIF.  
- Strength: elegant mathematical connection to classical inverse problems and a well-structured theoretical analysis for specific kernels.  
- Strength: practical contribution via the CD-CV model selection technique.  
- Major flaw: fails to specify or justify the common dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue or counting), a critical assumption for density existence.  
- Major flaw: omits specification of the domain of x (R^d vs. general topological space), leaving applicability ambiguous.  
- Major flaw: does not assume or state p>0 everywhere, which is necessary for the density ratio to be well-defined.  
- Consequence: theoretical results may be invalid or ambiguous due to ν-measure-zero issues, undermining practical relevance and general applicability.  
- Recommendation: needs a clearer, justified theoretical foundation specifying dominating measure, domain, and positivity assumptions to restore rigor and significance.
