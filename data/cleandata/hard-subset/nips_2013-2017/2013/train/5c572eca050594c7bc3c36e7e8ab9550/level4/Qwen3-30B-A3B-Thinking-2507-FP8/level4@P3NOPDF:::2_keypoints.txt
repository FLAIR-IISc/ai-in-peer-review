- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimators: a Type I formulation as a regularized Fredholm equation using an L² importance-sampling identity, and a Type II formulation using a kernel with vanishing locality parameter.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and empirical results showing practical effectiveness.  
- Major practical strength: the L² formulation enables cross-validation for parameter selection in unsupervised density-ratio estimation, unlike kernel-RKHS objectives (e.g., KMM).  
- Theorem 1 has a suboptimal convergence-rate term (-t / log λ); the authors should discuss whether spectral assumptions (as in Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) or smoothness of q/p could yield polynomial rates when λ decays with n.  
- The smoothness assumption that q/p lies in a Sobolev-type class is restrictive and requires prior knowledge; it may also cause p/q to behave poorly (e.g., singularities), harming downstream tasks like covariate-shift adaptation.  
- The paper should explicitly acknowledge and discuss limitations arising from the q/p smoothness assumption for real-world applicability.  
- The supplementary material contains typographical and mathematical errors that undermine trust in the theory and must be fixed (e.g., Lemma 5: W_2^2 → W_s^2 and corrected bound; incorrect expressions at lines 675/686; integral domain error at line 1147).  
- The manuscript should add a brief, clear remark contrasting why the L² objective permits meaningful cross-validation while RKHS-based objectives (like KMM) do not, to strengthen methodological justification.  
- Overall recommendation: the practical advance is valuable, but the paper needs deeper discussion of achievable rates under standard kernel assumptions, acknowledgment of smoothness-limitations, and correction of technical errors to solidify its theoretical contributions.
