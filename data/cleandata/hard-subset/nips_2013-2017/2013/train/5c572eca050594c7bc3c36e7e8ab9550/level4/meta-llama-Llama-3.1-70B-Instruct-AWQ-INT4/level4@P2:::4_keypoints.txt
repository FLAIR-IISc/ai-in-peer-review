- Novel formulation: estimating the density ratio q/p as an inverse problem via the Fredholm equation of the first kind.  
- Method: two estimators derived from integral operator equalities, replaced by empirical versions.  
- Implementation: choosing kernel functions as test functions enables standard kernel-method optimization.  
- Theoretical guarantees: convergence results under smoothness and boundedness assumptions on p and q.  
- Rates: convergence rates depend critically on the data dimensionality.  
- Experiments: empirical comparison of FIRE with KMM and LSIF shows practical evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed, relevant, and timely contribution deserving acceptance.  
- Missing intuition: need clearer explanation why optimizing over kernel test functions guarantees convergence to q/p.  
- Cost function choice: limited motivation for combining L2,p and L2,q — rationale should be elaborated.  
- Sampling rationale: clarify why Ep and Eq are used empirically instead of directly using Eq samples, with examples of sampling difficulty.  
- Regularization issue: unclear how the regularizer’s smoothness for q/p relates to smoothness properties of q and p.  
- Dimension discussion: manuscript should discuss for which dimensions the method is practical given dimension-dependent rates.  
- Test function family: provide stronger motivation for the choice of the family U of test functions.  
- Context and comparison: discuss generality of the approach and relationships/differences with related work (e.g., “Smooth Operators”).
