This paper introduces a method for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. The proposed approach leverages a kernel-based formulation of a Fredholm integral equation, thereby framing the estimation task as an inverse problem.

From a machine learning standpoint, the fundamental assumption that the underlying probability measures possess densities is a significant one. It is a well-established fact that for any probability measure P that has a ν-density, there exist probability measures in any of its epsilon neighborhoods that do not. This theoretical fragility makes the problem of density ratio estimation inherently challenging.

The manuscript's mathematical rigor is difficult to assess due to a few key omissions. The authors do not specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting, or another measure) being used. This ambiguity raises questions about the validity of the results, particularly concerning the handling of sets with a ν-measure of zero.

Furthermore, the paper would be strengthened by a clear and consolidated statement of its assumptions. For instance, the domain of the variable x is not defined—is it assumed to be $R^d$ or a more general topological space? Additionally, it is unclear whether the density p is assumed to be strictly positive across its entire support, a critical detail for a ratio estimation task.
