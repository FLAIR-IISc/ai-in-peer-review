- Reformulates density ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solves it via RKHS regularization.

- Introduces FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) algorithms for Type I (samples from p and q) and Type II (q known at p samples) settings.

- Extends prior methods (KMM, LSIF) by providing a principled operator-theoretic framework for algorithm design and analysis.

- Derives concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.

- Proposes CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation), a fully unsupervised model selection technique.

- Experimental results on multiple datasets show FIRE outperforms KMM and LSIF.

- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, making complex theory accessible.

- Strength: novel connection to Fredholm equations bridging classical operator theory and modern ML.

- Strength: comprehensive theoretical analysis covering both Euclidean and manifold cases.

- Strength: practical unsupervised model selection method (CD-CV).

- Weakness: Missing proofs or references for kernel approximation claims (e.g., ∫k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t)).

- Weakness: Need clearer discussion of the bandwidth parameter t versus KDE bandwidth and implications when q=1.

- Weakness: Should include a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization and its role for non-specialist readers.

- Weakness: Lacks discussion comparing to naive plug-in KDE ratio estimators and why FIRE is preferable.

- Weakness: Should relate provided upper bounds to known minimax optimal rates (e.g., [12]) and discuss any gaps.

- Weakness: Datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) need proper references for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Add a simple 1D demonstration showing true versus estimated q/p to illustrate behavior.

- Overall recommendation: Strong paper; accept with minor revisions to address the exposition and comparison points.
