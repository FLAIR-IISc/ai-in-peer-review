- Novel formulation: reframes density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation, a significant conceptual contribution.  
- Theoretical framework: connects to classical operator theory and uses Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS to derive the FIRE family of algorithms.  
- Model selection: introduces a novel unsupervised model selection method (CD-CV).  
- Theoretical guarantees: provides concentration and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and clearly explains complex ideas.  
- Missing derivation details: some mathematical claims (e.g., ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t)) lack proof or citation and need justification.  
- Accessibility: include a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.  
- Supplementary reliance: key proofs are in the supplement, which may limit reviewers’ ability to verify results.  
- Minimax comparison: lacks discussion or comparison to known minimax lower bounds (e.g., [12]) to contextualize convergence rates.  
- Benchmarking: needs direct empirical comparison against established algorithms and benchmarks.  
- Plug-in baseline: should explicitly justify superiority over naive plug-in density estimation methods.  
- Experiments: add simple toy 1D examples with known p and q and visualizations of estimated vs. true density ratios to aid interpretation.  
- Dataset citations: missing citations for CPUsmall, Kin8nm, and USPS datasets—add for reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel Fredholm formulation, principled regularization framework, CD-CV, clear presentation, and competitive empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unjustified derivations, missing minimax comparisons, lack of toy illustrations, missing dataset citations, and heavy reliance on supplementary proofs.  
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically grounded paper; recommend weak accept contingent on addressing derivation justification, minimax comparison, toy experiments, and dataset citation concerns.
