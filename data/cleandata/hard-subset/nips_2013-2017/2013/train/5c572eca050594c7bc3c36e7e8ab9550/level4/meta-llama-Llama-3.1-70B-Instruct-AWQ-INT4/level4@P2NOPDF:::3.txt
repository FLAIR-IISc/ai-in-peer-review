The authors propose a solution to estimate the ratio of two probability density functions, utilizing a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which results in an inverse problem. However, from a machine learning perspective, assuming the existence of densities is a stringent condition, as it is well-known that in any ε-neighborhood of a probability measure P with a ν-density, there exist probability measures without ν-densities. This makes the estimation of a density ratio challenging.

Upon reviewing the manuscript, I noticed that the authors do not specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or another measure) employed in their approach. Furthermore, it is unclear whether their results are rigorous, particularly with regards to sets of ν-measure zero. A clear statement of assumptions would strengthen the manuscript. For instance, it is unclear whether the random variable x belongs to Rd or a more general topological space. Additionally, the authors should specify whether the density p is positive for all x.

To improve the clarity and rigor of the manuscript, I recommend that the authors provide a detailed description of the dominating measure ν and explicitly state their assumptions regarding the probability density functions and the underlying space.
