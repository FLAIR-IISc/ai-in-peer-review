- Introduces a novel kernel-based method to estimate the Radon-Nikodym derivative f = p/q via two equivalent formulations, replacing integral operators with empirical counterparts.  
- Frames estimation as optimization over test functions {k(x,·)}, enabling standard kernel methods.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates that depend on dimensionality.  
- Requests clarification why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence for the first estimator (bandwidth dependence makes this intuitive for the second).  
- Asks for more discussion and motivation for switching cost functions from L2,p to a combination of L2,p and L2,q (eq. 9).  
- Questions the empirical approach of estimating both Ep and Eq to build the cost—why not directly use the estimated Eq; is Ep(q/p) superior?  
- Suggests explicitly stating that sampling difficulty from Eq motivates the chosen empirical strategy.  
- Recommends investigating the relationship between smoothness of p and q and the smoothness of their ratio q/p, and clarifying implications.  
- Requests elaboration on dimensional dependence of convergence rates and for which dimensions the method is most effective.  
- Asks for motivation for the chosen family of test functions U.  
- Raises the broader question of whether the chosen {k(x,·)} is an optimal generic strategy and notes conceptual similarities to prior work (Grunewalder et al., "Smooth Operators").  
- Overall assessment: strong, timely paper with rigorous analysis and supporting experiments.
