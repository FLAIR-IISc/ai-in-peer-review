- Introduces a novel Fredholm integral equation formulation for density ratio estimation, offering a distinct theoretical perspective.  
- Paper is well-written and tackles a timely, significant problem in machine learning.  
- Justification/proof needed for the claim ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t); include proof or reference for all technical assertions.  
- Clarify how bandwidth parameters differ from KDE and emphasize the advantage that the method avoids KDE’s bandwidth convergence requirement when q=1.  
- Provide a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with the technique.  
- All proofs are only in the supplementary material and have not been verified by the reviewer; ensure correctness and accessibility.  
- Contrast with a naive plug-in approach (separately estimating p and q) and explain why that is inferior.  
- Improve comparison to existing methods: relate convergence bounds (Corollaries 2 and 4) to minimax optimal rates and discuss any gaps (e.g., vs. [12]).  
- Cite dataset sources for CPUsmall, Kin8nm, and USPS.  
- Add a toy 1D example plotting true p/q versus estimated ratio to illustrate practical performance and aid reader understanding.  
- Overall: promising theoretical contribution but needs stronger technical justification, clearer comparisons to prior work, and illustrative examples for NIPS-level clarity.
