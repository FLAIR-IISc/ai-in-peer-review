The authors propose a solution to estimate the ratio of two probability density functions by leveraging a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which leads to an inverse problem. However, from a machine learning perspective, this approach relies on a strong assumption - that the densities exist - which is challenging to justify, as it is well-known that in any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P with a \nu-density, there exist probability measures without \nu-densities. Consequently, estimating the ratio of two densities is problematic in the context of machine learning.

Upon reviewing the manuscript, I noticed that the authors do not specify the dominating measure \nu (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or another measure), which raises concerns about the rigor of their results, particularly with regards to sets of \nu-measure zero. Furthermore, I found the manuscript lacking in clear statements about the underlying assumptions. For instance, it is unclear whether the variable x belongs to $R^d$ or a more general topological space, and whether the density p is positive for all x.
