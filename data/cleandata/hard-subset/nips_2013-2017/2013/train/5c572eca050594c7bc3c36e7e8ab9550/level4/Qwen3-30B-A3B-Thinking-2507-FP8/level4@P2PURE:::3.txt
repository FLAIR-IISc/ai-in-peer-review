

The authors propose a solution for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions, leveraging a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which frames the problem as an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, the assumption of densities presents significant challenges, as it is well-established that within any ε-neighborhood of a probability measure P admitting a ν-density, there exist probability measures lacking ν-densities. Consequently, density ratio estimation is inherently problematic in machine learning. To the reviewer's knowledge, the authors do not specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or another) nor clarify whether their results remain rigorous in the presence of sets of ν-measure zero. Additionally, the review lacks a precise statement of underlying assumptions: for instance, whether the domain of x is $\mathbb{R}^d$ or a general topological space, and whether the density p is positive everywhere.
