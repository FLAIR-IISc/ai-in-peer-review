- Paper tackles estimating q(x)/p(x) via Fredholm integral equations, yielding the FIRE family of kernel-regularized estimators with theory and experiments.
- Reformulates density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem and applies RKHS regularization.
- Introduces CD-CV for unsupervised model selection.
- Strong theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates (Gaussian kernel) on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.
- Algorithmic flexibility: easy-to-implement FIRE variants using different norms and regularizers.
- Empirical results competitive with existing methods (e.g., KMM, LSIF).
- Novel formulation connecting operator theory and kernel methods.
- Useful practical contribution with unsupervised model-selection technique.
- Need clearer intuition for convergence in setting (i) regarding optimization over kernel functions {k(x,·)}.
- Rationale for combining L2,p and L2,q norms (eq. 9) should be better linked to concrete applications.
- Motivational gap: clarify advantages of estimating q/p versus directly estimating Ep and Eq, especially when sampling from q is hard.
- Clarify how smoothness of q and p relates to smoothness of their ratio and implications for estimation.
- Discuss dependence of convergence rates and practical performance on data dimensionality d.
- Provide stronger justification for the chosen test function family U beyond convenience.
- Include deeper comparison to related work (e.g., Smooth Operators) highlighting pros/cons.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, empirically strong, and CD-CV is valuable.
- Arguments against acceptance: some motivational/intuition gaps and limited discussion of limitations.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; recommended for acceptance.
