- Proposes estimating the density ratio q/p by reformulating it with a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, casting it as an inverse problem.  
- Points out the density assumption is delicate: near any measure with a ν-density there exist measures without one, so estimating a density ratio can be problematic in ML settings.  
- Criticizes lack of specification of the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue vs counting), making rigor unclear, especially regarding sets of ν-measure zero.  
- Notes missing explicit assumptions about the domain and densities (e.g., whether x ∈ Rd or a general space, and whether p(x) > 0 everywhere).
