- Proposes a kernel-based method that frames density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation treated as an inverse problem.  
- Central concern: the foundational assumption that underlying probability measures possess densities is strong and fragile, since arbitrarily close measures may lack densities.  
- This fragility makes the framed density-ratio estimation problem fundamentally challenging and potentially ill-posed.  
- The paper does not specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), creating ambiguity about ν-zero sets and theoretical claims.  
- Several technical assumptions are unclear, preventing assessment of the rigor of the results.  
- Missing clear statement of the domain X (e.g., R^d vs. general topological space) and whether the density p is assumed strictly positive.  
- Recommendation: consolidate and explicitly state all assumptions to strengthen theoretical guarantees and clarity.
