- Paper addresses density-ratio estimation by formulating it as an inverse problem using a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation.
- The foundational assumption that densities exist is strong and unrealistic in many ML settings.
- For any measure with a density, arbitrarily close measures need not be absolutely continuous, making density-ratio estimation fundamentally challenging.
- The manuscript fails to specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue vs. counting), undermining the results' validity.
- Omitting ν complicates treatment of sets of ν-measure zero and related technical arguments.
- Key assumptions are not clearly stated, such as whether the domain of x is R^d or a more general topological space.
- It is not specified whether the density p is assumed strictly positive.
- Overall rigor is questioned due to these unspecified measures and missing explicit assumptions.
