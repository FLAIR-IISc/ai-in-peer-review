- Proposes a novel framework (FIRE) that reformulates density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation and solves it with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS.  
- Introduces two settings: Type I (importance-sampling identity) and Type II (local-kernel approximation) to derive the integral equations.  
- Develops Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), an unsupervised model-selection method enabled by the L2 objective.  
- Emphasizes that using an L2 objective (vs. RKHS-norm objectives like KMM) allows meaningful cross-validation across kernels.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including convergence rates for Gaussian kernels, but the presented rates appear slow for fixed kernel width.  
- Suggests discussing potential for faster polynomial convergence under stronger assumptions (e.g., spectral decay or extra smoothness) following Caponnetto & De Vito (2007).  
- Notes the strong functional-class assumptions (e.g., Sobolev-type smoothness) on q/p and cautions about practical violations and asymmetry (smooth q/p doesn't imply smooth p/q).  
- Reports empirical validation across datasets showing effectiveness of the proposed methods.  
- Points out multiple typos and errors in the supplementary material (e.g., Lemma 5 notation, incorrect expressions at specific lines, integration-domain typo) that need careful revision.
