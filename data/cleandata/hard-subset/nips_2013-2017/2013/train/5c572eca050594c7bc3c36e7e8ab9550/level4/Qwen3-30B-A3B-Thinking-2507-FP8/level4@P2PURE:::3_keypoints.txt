- Paper proposes estimating density ratios via a kernel-based Fredholm integral formulation, treating it as an inverse problem.
- Reviewer argues the density assumption is problematic: in any ε-neighborhood of a measure with a ν-density there exist measures without ν-densities, making density-ratio estimation ill-posed in ML.
- Authors fail to specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting, or other), which is essential for density definitions and results.
- No discussion of behavior or rigor on sets of ν-measure zero and whether results hold in their presence.
- Missing precise statement of underlying assumptions: the domain of x (ℝ^d vs. a general topological space) is unspecified.
- Unclear whether the paper assumes the density p is strictly positive everywhere or allows zeros, which affects identifiability and estimation.
