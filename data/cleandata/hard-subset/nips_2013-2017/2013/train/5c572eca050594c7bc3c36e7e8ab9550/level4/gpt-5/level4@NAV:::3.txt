Review- The authors present a method for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. The approach employs a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which turns the problem into an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, assuming the existence of densities is a stringent requirement, since it is well known that in any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P that has a ν-density, there exist probability measures that have no ν-densities. Therefore, estimating a ratio of two densities is problematic from an ML standpoint. As far as I can tell, the authors do not specify which dominating measure ν is used (Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or something else), and it is unclear to me whether their results are rigorous in light of sets of ν-measure zero. I also miss a clear, explicit statement of the assumptions. For example, does x lie in R^d or in a general topological space? Is the density p positive for all x?
