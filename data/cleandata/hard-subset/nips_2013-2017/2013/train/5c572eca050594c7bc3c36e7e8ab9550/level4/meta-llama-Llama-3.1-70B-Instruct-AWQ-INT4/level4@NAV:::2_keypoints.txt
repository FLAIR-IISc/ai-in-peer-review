- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimators: a Type I method (importance sampling + RKHS regularization → Type I Fredholm equation) and a Type II method (locality-kernel with shrinking locality → Type II Fredholm equation).  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and empirical evaluation demonstrating practical performance.  
- Uses an L^2 formulation that enables cross-validation for parameter selection in density-ratio estimation.  
- Claims advantage over Kernel Mean Matching (KMM) because KMM’s RKHS objective depends on the kernel and is not comparable across kernels, preventing CV-based kernel selection.  
- Notes Theorem 1’s convergence rate is slow due to the (-t / log λ) factor for fixed t.  
- Recommends exploring stronger assumptions (e.g., operator spectral assumptions like Caponnetto & De Vito 2007 or regularity of q/p) to obtain polynomial sample-rate convergence.  
- Points out that assuming q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and may be unrealistic; also warns this can make p/q behave poorly—suggests discussing this limitation.  
- Flags numerous typos/minor errors in the supplementary material that impede verification (e.g., Lemma 5 notation and bounds, incorrect expressions at lines cited, wrong integral domain) and urges revision.  
- Concludes the main strength is the novel approach’s ability to apply cross-validation effectively for unsupervised density-ratio estimation.
