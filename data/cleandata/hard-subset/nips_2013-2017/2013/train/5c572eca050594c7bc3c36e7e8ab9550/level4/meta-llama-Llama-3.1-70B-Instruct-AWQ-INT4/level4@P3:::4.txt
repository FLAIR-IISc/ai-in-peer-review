The paper "Inverse Density as an Inverse Problem: the Fredholm Equation Approach" presents a novel approach to estimating the ratio of two densities, p and q, by reformulating the problem as an inverse problem using the Fredholm equation of the first kind. The authors develop a principled framework for constructing algorithms and analyzing them theoretically, resulting in a family of algorithms called FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator).

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise summary of their contributions, including a new formulation of the density ratio estimation problem, a flexible and principled framework for solving it, and a detailed theoretical analysis of the resulting algorithms. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in various settings, including classification and regression tasks.

The authors' use of the Fredholm equation of the first kind to reformulate the density ratio estimation problem is a significant contribution, as it allows for the application of classical techniques from inverse problem theory, such as regularization and Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). The resulting algorithms are simple, flexible, and easy to implement, making them a valuable addition to the existing literature on density ratio estimation.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified framework for density ratio estimation, which can be applied to various settings, including covariate shift and importance sampling. The authors also provide a detailed theoretical analysis of their algorithms, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel, which demonstrates the robustness and efficiency of their approach.

The experimental results presented in the paper are also impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in various settings, including classification and regression tasks. The comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as Kernel Mean Matching (KMM) and Least Squares Importance Sampling (LSIF), shows that the proposed approach can achieve better performance in certain scenarios.
