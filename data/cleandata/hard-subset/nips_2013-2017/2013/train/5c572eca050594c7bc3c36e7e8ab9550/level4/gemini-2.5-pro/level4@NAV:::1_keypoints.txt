- Main contribution: Reformulates density-ratio estimation p/q as a Fredholm integral equation — novel approach compared to existing methods.

- Strong: Paper is well-written and clear.

- Strong: Density-ratio estimation is an important and relevant problem for the community.

- Missing derivations: Several claims (e.g., ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t)) lack justification or citations and need proofs or references.

- KDE connection: In the special case q=1, the method implies bandwidth need not go to zero for consistency — this distinction from standard KDE should be discussed in detail.

- Regularization clarity: Include a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization to improve accessibility for non-expert readers.

- Proofs availability: All proofs are deferred to the supplement and were not checked in this review.

- Baseline comparison: Paper should explain why direct estimation is preferable to a naive plug-in approach (separately estimating p and q then dividing).

- Theoretical guarantees: Convergence rates (Corollaries 2 and 4) are given as upper bounds but their tightness and relation to minimax optimal rates (e.g., [12]) are not discussed.

- Experimental details: Provide citations for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).

- Illustrative experiments: Missing simple 1D toy example (plots comparing true vs estimated density ratio) to build intuition.

- Summary critique: Well-presented work but lacks sufficient comparison to state-of-the-art theoretical guarantees and simple illustrative experiments to demonstrate estimator quality.
