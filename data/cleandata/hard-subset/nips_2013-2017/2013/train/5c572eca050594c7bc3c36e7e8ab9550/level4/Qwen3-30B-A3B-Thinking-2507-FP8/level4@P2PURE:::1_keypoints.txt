- Introduces a novel Fredholm-integral reformulation for density ratio estimation p(x)/q(x), a significant theoretical contribution.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and tackles an important problem.

- Key technical claims need justification (e.g., the claim ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t) lacks proof or reference).
- Clarify how the method’s bandwidth parameters differ from KDE and explain the claimed consistency advantage when q(x)=1.
- Provide a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.
- All proofs are in the supplementary material and have not been verified in the review; bring key proofs or verification into main text or ensure correctness.
- Include a comparison to the naive plug-in approach (estimating p and q separately) to justify superiority.
- Discuss how the provided convergence upper bounds (Corollaries 2 and 4) compare to known minimax optimal rates (e.g., [12]).
- Add references for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).
- Provide a simple 1D toy example plotting true vs. estimated p(x)/q(x) to illustrate estimation quality.
- Overall: well-motivated theoretically but lacks comparative performance analysis and illustrative examples to assess practical utility.
