- Introduces reframing density ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind (an inverse problem).  
- Proposes two formulations: Type I (uses samples from p and q) and Type II (uses local kernels when q is known).  
- Solves the integral equations with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family.  
- Provides closed-form, simple-to-implement algorithms with competitive empirical performance versus LSIF and KDE baselines.  
- Develops a fully unsupervised model-selection method, Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), enabling hyperparameter selection across kernels.  
- Supplies detailed theoretical analysis, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Strength: novel, principled connection between density-ratio estimation and classical functional analysis offering flexible regularization choices.  
- Strength: unsupervised cross-validation via L2 objective overcomes KMM’s kernel-dependent model-selection issue.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical rigor supporting algorithmic claims.  
- Weakness: theoretical rates could be strengthened—need discussion/analysis of achieving polynomial rates for fixed kernel width under spectral-decay assumptions.  
- Weakness: reliance on smoothness assumptions for q/p (e.g., Sobolev) may be strong and limits applicability; implications when roles of p and q swap should be discussed.  
- Weakness: supplementary material contains typos, notation inconsistencies, and errors that hinder verification and require careful revision.  
- Overall: a strong, novel contribution with practical and theoretical merit, but needs deeper rate analysis and cleaner supplementary proofs.
