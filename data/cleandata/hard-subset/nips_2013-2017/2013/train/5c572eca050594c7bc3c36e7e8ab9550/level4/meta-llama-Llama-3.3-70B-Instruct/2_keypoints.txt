- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimation methods: a Type I Fredholm formulation with RKHS regularization and a Type II kernel-locality approach; provides theoretical convergence and experiments.  
- L^2 formulation enables cross-validation for parameter selection, a major advantage over existing RKHS-based methods (e.g., KMM) that are unsuitable for kernel selection via CV.  
- Theorem 1 yields a slow convergence rate due to a (-t / log λ) factor; suggest exploring stronger assumptions (e.g., on operator spectrum or q/p) to obtain polynomial rates (c.f. Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).  
- Notes that assuming q/p lies in a specific function class (e.g., Sobolev) is a strong/limiting assumption and can imply poor behavior for the reciprocal p/q; this limitation should be discussed.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typos and errors that impede verification of theoretical results (e.g., issues in Lemma 5, lines 675/686, integral domain); revision is recommended.  
- Overall assessment: the new approach is noteworthy, with CV applicability as a particularly strong contribution.
