- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimators: Type I (importance-sampling-derived L2 regularization in RKHS, a Fredholm Type I equation) and Type II (local-kernel L2 regularization with locality → 0, a Fredholm Type II equation).  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and empirical evaluations demonstrating performance.  
- Key practical advantage: L2 formulation naturally enables hyperparameter tuning via cross-validation, addressing a major unsupervised-learning challenge unlike KMM.  
- Suggestion: explicitly explain why conventional RKHS objectives (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for cross-validation because objective values depend on the chosen kernel and are not comparable.  
- Theorem 1’s rate includes a slow (−t / log λ) factor; authors should discuss whether polynomial sample-rate bounds can be achieved under stronger, reasonable assumptions (e.g., spectral assumptions like Caponnetto & De Vito).  
- Recommend a broader discussion of modeling assumptions: requiring q/p to lie in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and may imply p/q is ill-behaved, limiting applicability when ratio direction is not known.  
- Supplementary material needs substantial correction and proofreading: numerous typos and mistakes hinder verification of theoretical claims.  
- Specific supplementary errors to fix: Lemma 5’s W2^2 should be Ws^2; likely correction to the bound (D1 (t/−log λ)^2 + λ^(1−α) D2 ||f||_2^2); incorrect expressions at lines 675 and 686 (Eq. 31 is fine); integration domain at line 1147 should be ||ξ||^2 ≥ T.  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution with practical CV-enabled model selection, but theoretical rates and assumptions discussion should be strengthened and supplementary proofs corrected.
