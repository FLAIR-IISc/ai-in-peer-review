- Proposes a novel kernel-based method using a Fredholm integral equation to estimate the ratio of two probability densities by casting it as an inverse problem.  
- Notes the inherent difficulty: any epsilon neighborhood of a measure with a ν-density can contain measures without ν-densities, making density-ratio estimation problematic.  
- Method relies on the strong assumption that densities exist; this assumption may be unrealistic in many ML settings.  
- Calls for explicit statement of assumptions: choice of dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue or counting), domain of x (e.g., R^d or general topological space), and properties of p (e.g., positivity).  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel in multiple settings.  
- Introduces FIRE, a simple and efficient Fredholm inverse regularized estimator algorithm for density-ratio estimation.  
- Experimental results show the proposed method compares favorably to existing alternatives.  
- Strength: novel formulation of density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical treatment supporting the method.  
- Strength: simple, efficient, and well-presented algorithm and exposition.  
- Weakness: insufficient clarity on key assumptions (dominating measure and density properties).  
- Weakness: limited discussion of the broader implications and limitations of the method in machine learning.  
- Originality: unique use of a Fredholm integral equation to frame density-ratio estimation.  
- Significance: potential to improve accuracy in applications like importance sampling and covariate shift, with practical applicability.  
- Overall: significant and well-written contribution with areas for improvement on assumptions and contextual discussion.  
- Pro-accept: novelty, simplicity, theoretical rigor, and strong experimental validation.  
- Con-accept: unclear assumptions, underdeveloped discussion of ML implications, and limited treatment of limitations/future work.
