- Method derives estimators for f = p/q from two equalities relating kernel expectations: (i) E_p[k(x,·) f] = E_q[k(x,·)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,·) f] ≈ q(x).  
- Integral operators are replaced by empirical approximations using samples.  
- The kernel family {k(x,·)} is used as test functions to minimize discrepancy, leveraging standard kernel-method optimization.  
- Convergence guarantees are provided under smoothness and boundedness of p and q, with rates critically depending on ambient dimension.  
- Clarification requested: provide a brief argument that optimizing over {k(x,·)} suffices for convergence to the ratio in equality (i).  
- Clarification requested: justify the cost function choice—equation (9) mixes L2(p) and L2(q) norms though optimization is presented in L2(p); link to application objectives is needed.  
- Clarification requested: explain why the method uses empirical estimates of E_p and E_q instead of directly using empirical E_q, and elaborate the intended application where sampling from q is difficult.  
- Clarification requested: discuss the relationship between smoothness assumptions on p and q versus smoothness of the ratio q/p, including nontrivial converse scenarios.  
- Clarification requested: discuss practical dimensionality limits—what range of ambient dimensions is the method effective for in practice.  
- Clarification requested: motivate the specific choice of test function family U.  
- Methodology perspective: reflect on whether extracting densities via test functions is the right generic cost design for covariate shift, and compare to related approaches (e.g., [1]) where expectation operators and test functions are used.  
- Overall assessment: paper is timely, makes a strong contribution, and includes thorough convergence analysis and empirical validation.
