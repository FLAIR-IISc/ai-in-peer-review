- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation, yielding the FIRE family of algorithms.
- Presents two formulations: Type I (samples from both distributions) and Type II (q known at p samples).
- Applies Tikhonov regularization in RKHS to derive theoretically grounded algorithms that allow natural cross-validation.
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised L²-based cross-density cross-validation method for model selection in density ratio estimation.
- Clearly contrasts advantages over KMM and LSIF, highlighting CD-CV’s suitability for cross-validation where KMM’s objective fails.
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis with concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and manifolds.
- Notes potential for stronger theoretical rates under additional spectral assumptions (e.g., Caponnetto & De Vito) to improve Theorem 1’s slow log-based rate.
- Flags a significant practical limitation: the strong Sobolev-space assumption on q/p, which may be unrealistic and can imply poor behavior of p/q.
- Empirical results across multiple datasets and tasks show consistent performance and computational gains, with CD-CV effective for parameter tuning.
- Identifies issues in the supplementary material (typos and proof errors) that must be corrected to restore confidence in the theoretical claims.
- Overall: strong theoretical framing and practical utility make the work a strong NIPS candidate, pending revision of assumptions and supplementary fixes.
