- Proposes estimating a density ratio via a kernel-based Fredholm integral formulation, viewing the problem as an inverse problem.  
- Core concern: method assumes existence of probability densities, which may not hold for empirical or complex data-generating measures.  
- Practical ill-posedness: measures arbitrarily close to a density-bearing P may lack densities, making direct density-ratio estimation unstable.  
- Theoretical framework lacks a clear, consolidated statement of assumptions, reducing rigor and assessability.  
- Dominating measure ν is not specified (e.g., Lebesgue vs. counting), creating ambiguity about interpretation and null sets.  
- Domain of input x is unspecified (R^d vs. general topological spaces), so applicability and scope are unclear.  
- Support assumptions for p and q are not stated—unclear if densities must be strictly positive and how zeros in p or q are handled.  
- Summary: idea is interesting but currently disconnected from ML practicalities and needs explicit assumptions to establish validity and relevance.
