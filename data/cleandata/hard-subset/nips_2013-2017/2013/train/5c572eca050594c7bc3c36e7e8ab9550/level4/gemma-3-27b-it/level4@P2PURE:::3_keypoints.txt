- Paper tackles density-ratio estimation via kernel methods by framing it as a Fredholm integral equation / inverse problem.  
- Estimating density ratios is fundamentally challenging because arbitrarily close probability measures may lack densities w.r.t. the same dominating measure.  
- The manuscript fails to specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), which is essential for density and ratio definitions.  
- Results may be non-rigorous or invalid on sets of ν-measure zero; this issue is not addressed.  
- Key assumptions are unclear or unstated, undermining theoretical claims.  
- It is unspecified whether the domain of x is R^d or a more general topological space, affecting applicability.  
- The paper does not state whether the density p is assumed strictly positive everywhere, a crucial condition for ratio existence and stability.
