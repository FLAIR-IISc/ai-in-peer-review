- Proposes kernel-based estimator for density ratio via a Fredholm integral equation, framing the task as an inverse problem.  
- Assumes existence of densities, which is a very strong/fragile requirement in ML since nearby measures may lack densities.  
- Density ratio estimation is fundamentally challenging under this framework due to those non-density perturbations.  
- Fails to specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue or counting), creating potential ambiguities from ν-zero sets.  
- Omits clear statement of core assumptions: domain of x (ℝᵈ vs. general topological space) is unspecified.  
- Does not establish positivity of p(x) (p(x)>0 everywhere), leaving key conditions unverified.  
- These omissions prevent rigorous assessment of the paper’s theoretical guarantees and practical applicability.
