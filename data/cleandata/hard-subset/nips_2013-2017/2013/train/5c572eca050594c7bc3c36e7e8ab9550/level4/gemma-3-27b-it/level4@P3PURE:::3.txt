The authors propose a method for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions, leveraging a kernel-based approach and formulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation, resulting in an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, the assumption of underlying densities is a strong one. It is well-established that within any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P possessing a density with respect to a measure ν, there exist probability measures lacking such a density. Consequently, estimating the ratio of two densities is inherently problematic. As far as I can ascertain, the authors do not specify the dominating measure ν employed (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or another), and it remains unclear whether their results are mathematically rigorous given the potential influence of sets of ν-measure zero. Furthermore, a clear statement of assumptions is absent. For instance, is the domain of x restricted to $R^d$, or is it a more general topological space? Is the density p strictly positive for all x?
