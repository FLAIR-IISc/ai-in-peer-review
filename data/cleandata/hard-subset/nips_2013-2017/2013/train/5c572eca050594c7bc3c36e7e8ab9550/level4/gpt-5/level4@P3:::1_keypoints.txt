- Summary: Recasts density ratio estimation as a Fredholm inverse problem and proposes RKHS-Tikhonov estimators (FIRE) with Type I/II formulations, spectral analysis, and an unsupervised model-selection (CD-CV), evaluated against LSIF, KMM, and KDE baselines.

- Relation to prior work: Connects KMM to a Fredholm equation and contrasts with LSIF/KLIEP; situates analysis within operator-theoretic inverse problems and kernel regularization.

- Strength: Principled inverse-problem RKHS formulation yielding simple closed-form algorithms.

- Strength: Nontrivial theoretical guarantees (concentration bounds and convergence rates) for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.

- Strength: Practical unsupervised model selection via CD-CV that leverages samples from both p and q.

- Strength: Well-organized presentation and clear elucidation of the connection to KMM.

- Weakness 1: Need precise statement and assumptions for the approximate-identity claim (∫kt f = f + O(t)), with lemma, proof sketch, and references.

- Weakness 2: Clarify FIRE’s reduction to density estimation (q ≡ 1), role of bandwidth t vs. regularization, and consistency requirements (t → 0?).

- Weakness 3: Include a short primer on Tikhonov regularization to explain ill-posedness, stability, and bias–variance tradeoff in this context.

- Weakness 4: Main text should state key assumptions (smoothness, boundedness, manifold regularity, boundaries) alongside theorems and explain terms in the rates.

- Weakness 5: Provide analysis/experiment showing why KDE plug-in r̂ = q̂/p̂ is inferior and how direct regularized estimation improves stability (toy 1D example suggested).

- Weakness 6: Relate upper-bound rates to known minimax lower bounds to position FIRE’s optimality.

- Weakness 7: Strengthen empirical comparisons (more baselines, KMM in-sample or regularized variants) and report computational complexity/time.

- Weakness 8: Discuss positivity and normalization constraints (FIRE appears unconstrained); comment on clipping or constrained variants.

- Weakness 9: Cite and describe datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) with sizes and dimensionalities in the main text.

- Weakness 10: Add simple low-dimensional toy demonstrations plotting true r and estimates from FIRE, LSIF, and KDE to assess bias/variance.

- Quality: Technically sound with appropriate regularization and operator-theoretic grounding; missing explicit main-text assumptions and positivity discussion.

- Clarity: Generally well written but needs Tikhonov primer, sharper approximate-identity lemma, explicit theorem assumptions, and guidance on roles of t and λ.

- Originality: Novel and insightful recasting of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm inverse problem, unifying related methods under RKHS-Tikhonov.

- Significance: Broadly applicable, theoretically grounded, and practically implementable with useful unsupervised model selection.

- Arguments for acceptance: Principled formulation with closed-form algorithms, solid theoretical analysis, practical CD-CV selection, and clear extensions of kernel methods.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited empirical comparisons and toy demos, unclear rate optimality, missing details on constraints and assumptions, and some unsubstantiated statements.

- Overall: Strong and interesting submission that would be considerably improved by addressing items (i)-(ii), (5)-(7), and (10).
