The authors propose a solution to the problem of estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. Their approach is based on using a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which yields an inverse problem. 

From a machine learning perspective, the assumption of densities is a challenging one. It is well-known that in any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P, which has a \nu-density, there are probability measures that have no \nu-densities. Therefore, estimating the ratio of two densities is problematic from a machine learning viewpoint.

Upon reviewing the manuscript, I noticed that the authors do not specify which dominating measure \nu is used. Is it the Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or something else? This lack of clarity raises concerns about the rigor of their results, particularly with regards to sets of \nu-measure zero.

Furthermore, I found the manuscript to be lacking in clear statements regarding the assumptions made. For instance, is the variable x assumed to belong to $R^d$ or a more general topological space? Additionally, is the density p assumed to be positive for all x? Clarifying these assumptions would significantly strengthen the manuscript.

In light of these concerns, I believe the authors should provide a more detailed explanation of their methodology, including the choice of dominating measure and the assumptions underlying their approach. This would help to address the potential issues with sets of \nu-measure zero and provide a clearer understanding of the applicability and limitations of their method.
