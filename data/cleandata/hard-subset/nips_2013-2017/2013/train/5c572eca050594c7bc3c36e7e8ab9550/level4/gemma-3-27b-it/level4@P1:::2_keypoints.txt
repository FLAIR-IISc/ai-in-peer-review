- Proposes estimating density/function ratios by formulating a Fredholm equation of the first kind and introducing FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) using RKHS regularization.  
- Provides theoretical analysis (concentration bounds, convergence rates for Gaussian kernel) and empirical results, including covariate-shift classification.  
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised model-selection method leveraging the L2 formulation to enable cross-validation — a notable practical contribution.  
- FIRE is flexible and relates to existing methods (KMM, LSIF), offering various norms and regularization options.  
- Main theoretical concern: Theorem 1 shows relatively slow convergence rates with adverse dependence on kernel width t; stronger smoothness assumptions might yield polynomial rates (see Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).  
- Paper should better discuss limitations: density-ratio estimation often requires strong regularity assumptions (e.g., Sobolev), which may be unrealistic and cause poor performance when p/q is ill-behaved.  
- Supplementary material has numerous typos and errors that impede verification (specific mistakes cited in Lemma 5 and several equation/line references); these must be corrected.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve convergence discussion, acknowledge assumptions/limitations, and fix supplementary-material errors.
