- Method: kernel-based formulation leading to a Fredholm integral equation, casting density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem.  
- Core assumption that both measures admit densities w.r.t. a common dominating measure ν is very strong and may fail in ML settings.  
- Small perturbations of a distribution can produce measures without ν-densities, making density-ratio estimation inherently fragile.  
- The paper does not specify the dominating measure ν (Lebesgue, counting, etc.), raising concerns about rigor on ν-null sets.  
- Missing explicit assumptions about the sample space and densities (e.g., domain of x, whether p>0 everywhere); these must be clarified to strengthen theoretical claims.
