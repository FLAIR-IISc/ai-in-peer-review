- Proposes a kernel-based method that frames density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral inverse problem.  
- Assumes underlying densities exist, which is a strong and problematic assumption in ML since nearby measures may lack densities.  
- Consequently, estimating the ratio of two densities can be inherently ill-posed in practical ML settings.  
- The paper fails to specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), a crucial omission.  
- Not specifying ν and how ν-measure-zero sets are handled undermines the rigor of the theoretical results.  
- Lacks clear statement of other underlying assumptions, e.g., whether the domain of x is R^d or a more general topological space.  
- Unclear if the density p is assumed positive everywhere (no zeros), which is important for validity of the approach.
