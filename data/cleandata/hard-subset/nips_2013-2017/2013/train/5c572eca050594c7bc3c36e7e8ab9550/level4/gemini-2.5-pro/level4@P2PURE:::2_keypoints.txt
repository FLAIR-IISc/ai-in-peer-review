- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimators: a Type I Fredholm formulation (L2 deviation + RKHS regularizer) and a Type II with a locality kernel that must shrink to zero.  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and experimental validation for the proposed methods.  
- Major advantage: L2 formulation enables principled cross-validation for parameter selection in an otherwise unsupervised problem.  
- Recommend explicitly noting that RKHS-norm objectives (e.g., KMM) are ill-suited for CV because their objective values depend on the kernel, preventing meaningful cross-kernel comparisons.  
- Theorem 1’s convergence rate is logarithmic (contains a (-t/log λ) factor); ask whether polynomial rates can be obtained under stronger spectral/regularity assumptions (e.g., following Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).  
- Point out the strong modeling assumption that q/p lies in a well-behaved function class (e.g., Sobolev), and request discussion of its implications for tail behavior and ill-behaved p/q.  
- The supplementary material needs significant revision: many typos/minor errors impede verification (e.g., Lemma 5: W2^2→Ws^2 and corrected bound; incorrect expressions on lines 675/686 though Eq.31 is correct; integration domain in Lemma 5 proof should be ||ξ||^2 ≥ T).  
- Overall assessment: novel and valuable contribution, with the key strength being a framework that allows effective cross-validation for density-ratio estimation.
