- Strength: Well-written, engaging paper presenting a novel Fredholm integral reformulation for density ratio estimation with potential impact.
- Strength: Notes density estimation as a special case when q=1 and claims that bandwidth need not converge to zero, a potential advantage over KDE.
- (i) Needs rigorous proof or citation for claims like ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t) and similar mathematical justifications.
- (ii) Requires expanded discussion comparing the proposed method to standard KDE, clarifying distinct roles and interpretations of bandwidth parameters.
- (iii) Recommend a brief, accessible introduction of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with the technique.
- (iv) Reliance on supplementary material for all proofs is concerning and increases reviewer burden to verify theoretical claims.
- (v) Missing discussion on why naive plug-in (estimate p and q separately then ratio) is suboptimal compared to the integral equation approach.
- (vi) Corollaries 2 and 4 give convergence upper bounds but the paper lacks comparison to known minimax optimality results (e.g., relation to [12]).
- (vii) Experimental datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) are not properly referenced; citations needed for reproducibility.
- (viii) Lacks simple toy problem demonstrations (e.g., 1D visualizations of true vs estimated p/q) that would aid intuition and interpretability.
- Overall: Promising and novel contribution, but requires stronger mathematical justification, clearer comparisons to prior work, dataset citations, and illustrative experiments to fully establish impact.
