- Authors derive two kernel-based estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q using identities (i) E_p[k(x,·) f] = E_q[k(x,·)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,·) f] ≈ q(x), replacing integral operators with empirical counterparts.  
- Convergence guarantees are provided under smoothness and boundedness of densities, with rates critically depending on ambient dimension (matching density-estimation expectations).  
- Paper is well-executed, timely, and includes both theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Convergence-rate comment: need a concise argument showing optimization over {k(x,·)} suffices for convergence in setting (i).  
- Cost-function motivation: discrepancy between using L2_p in optimization and Eq. (9)’s combination of L2_p and L2_q—provide justification tied to specific application objectives.  
- Application context: clarify why one should estimate via E_p and E_q instead of directly using E_q in importance-sampling scenarios, and emphasize the practical case where sampling from q is difficult.  
- Smoothness assumptions: regularizer enforces smoothness on q/p, but Theorem 1 assumes smoothness on p and q—clarify the relationship and implications given non-uniqueness of density pairs yielding the same ratio.  
- Dimension dependence: discuss the practical dimensionality regime where the method remains effective and usable.  
- Test-function family U: provide stronger motivation for choosing {k(x,·)} as the test functions and discuss whether this choice is optimal.  
- Methodological positioning: relate and compare to prior operator-based work (e.g., Smooth Operators) and clarify whether the kernel-test approach is the best or merely a natural alternative.
