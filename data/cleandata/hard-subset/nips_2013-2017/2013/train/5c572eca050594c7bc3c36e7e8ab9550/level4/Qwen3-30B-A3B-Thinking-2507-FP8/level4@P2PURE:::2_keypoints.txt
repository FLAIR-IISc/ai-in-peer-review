- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimators: Type I (importance-sampling equation as an L²-regularized RKHS problem) and Type II (kernel with vanishing locality parameter, also a regularization problem).  
- Establishes theoretical convergence guarantees and validates methods empirically.  
- Key advantage: L² formulation permits cross-validation for parameter selection in unsupervised density-ratio estimation.  
- Paper should explicitly note RKHS-based objectives (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for cross-validation because objective values depend on the kernel and are not comparable across kernels.  
- Theorem 1 yields a slow factor of −t/ log λ for fixed t; authors should discuss whether additional assumptions (e.g., on spectrum of K_{p,t} or stronger conditions on q/p) can produce polynomial sample-rate convergence as in Caponnetto & De Vito (2007).  
- Assuming q/p lies in a smooth class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and requires prior knowledge of ratio/tails; under this assumption p/q may behave poorly—this limitation should be discussed.  
- Type II method’s consistency requires the kernel locality parameter to vanish; this dependency should be emphasized.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typos and errors that impede verification (e.g., Lemma 5: W_s^2 not W_2^2, corrected bound D1(t/−log λ)^2 + λ^{1−α}D2‖f‖_2^2; wrong expressions at lines 675/686; integral domain at line 1147), and needs substantial revision.  
- Because convergence results are a core contribution, the authors must correct these supplementary issues before publication.  
- Overall: a valuable contribution, with the principal strength being effective application of cross-validation for hyperparameter selection.
