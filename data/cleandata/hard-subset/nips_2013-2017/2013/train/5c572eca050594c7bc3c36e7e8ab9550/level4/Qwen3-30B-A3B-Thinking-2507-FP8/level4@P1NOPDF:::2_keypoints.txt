- Introduces two novel density-ratio estimators: Type I (importance-sampling reformulated as L² + RKHS regularization) and Type II (local-kernel regularization with vanishing locality), with theory and experiments.

- Major strength: L² formulation allows principled cross-validation for parameter selection, a practical advantage over methods like KMM.

- Recommend explicitly noting that RKHS-derived objectives (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for cross-validation because objective values depend on kernel choice and are not comparable.

- Theorem 1’s convergence rate (with fixed t) is pessimistic due to a −t / log λ factor; literature suggests polynomial rates may be achievable under spectral assumptions.

- Ask whether assuming spectral properties of K_{p,t} or stronger regularity of q/p could yield polynomial sample-rate bounds for fixed t; discussion would strengthen theory.

- The smoothness assumption on q/p is strong, often unrealistic, and can imply poor behavior of p/q; the paper should acknowledge this limitation and its impact on applicability.

- Supplementary material contains typographical and technical errors that undermine trust in the theoretical results (e.g., Lemma 5: W_2^2 → W_s^2 and corrected bound; errors at lines 675/686; missing integral domain bound at line 1147).

- Because convergence analysis is central, the authors must fix the supplementary errors before publication; current supplement hinders verification.

- Overall: valuable contribution enabling CV in unsupervised density estimation, but needs revisions on convergence-rate discussion, smoothness-assumption limitations, and supplemental corrections to increase impact.
