- Proposes a novel method (FIRE) that estimates density ratio q/p by reformulating it as a Fredholm integral equation (inverse problem) using a kernel integral operator.  
- Applies regularization with an RKHS norm penalty to solve the inverse problem, yielding a flexible, simple, and easy-to-implement family of algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the method, related work, and implementation details.  
- Provides a detailed theoretical analysis, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel.  
- Experimental results are encouraging and demonstrate effectiveness across settings, including covariate-shift classification.  
- Introduces an unsupervised cross-validation technique (CD-CV) for tuning algorithm parameters.  
- Main contributions: inverse-problem formulation for density-ratio estimation, RKHS-regularized solutions, and rigorous analysis for Gaussian kernels.  
- Strength: principled framework that leverages classical regularization and RKHS theory, applicable to importance sampling and covariate shift.  
- Limitation: relies on smoothness and boundedness assumptions for densities that may not hold in practice.  
- Limitation: convergence rates depend on ambient dimension, potentially limiting performance in high-dimensional settings.  
- Suggestion: paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential extensions.
