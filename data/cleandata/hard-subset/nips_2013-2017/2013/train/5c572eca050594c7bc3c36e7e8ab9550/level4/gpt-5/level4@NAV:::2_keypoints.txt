- Proposes two new density-ratio estimators: a Type I method (importance-sampling identity as L^2+RKHS-regularized problem) and a Type II method (local kernel with vanishing locality parameter, also regularized); provides convergence guarantees and empirical results.

- Key advantage: the L^2 formulation enables cross-validation for algorithmic parameter selection in unsupervised density-ratio estimation, filling a common gap.

- Notes that RKHS objective methods (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for kernel selection via CV because objective values depend on the kernel and are not comparable.

- Theorem 1 has a (-t/log λ) factor causing slow convergence for fixed t; asks whether stronger/spectral assumptions (e.g., Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) or extra assumptions on q/p could yield polynomial rates and requests discussion of such results.

- Warns that assuming q/p lies in a regular class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and may implicitly require knowledge of tails; the reverse ratio p/q can behave poorly, so this limitation should be discussed.

- Calls for substantial revisions to the supplementary material due to typos and errors that hinder verification (specific corrections suggested for Lemma 5, lines 675/686, Eq. (31), and an integral domain at line 1147).

- Overall assessment: the approach is novel and useful, particularly because CV can be effectively used for parameter selection.
