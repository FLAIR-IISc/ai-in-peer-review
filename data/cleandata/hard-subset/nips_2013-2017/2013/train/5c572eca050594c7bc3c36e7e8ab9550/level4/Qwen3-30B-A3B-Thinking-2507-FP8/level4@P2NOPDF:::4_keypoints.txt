- Proposes a kernel-based method to estimate the Radon-Nikodym derivative f = p/q by enforcing two operator equalities and replacing integrals with empirical operators over kernel test functions.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates that deteriorate with ambient dimension.  
- Request for intuitive explanation why optimizing over the kernel family for equality (i) ensures convergence to f, beyond bandwidth arguments.  
- Ask for clear motivation of the cost function mixing L2,p and L2,q norms (eq. 9), linked to concrete target tasks (e.g., importance sampling, covariate shift).  
- Question the practical importance-sampling motivation: if computing q/p needs both E_p and E_q, explain why one cannot just use estimated E_q and clarify when sampling from q is difficult.  
- Ask for discussion of how smoothness assumptions on p and q relate to smoothness of f, and whether smooth f implies existence of smooth p, q generating it.  
- Request practical guidance on dimensional limits: discuss for which dimensions the method is feasible in real-world settings (e.g., small d thresholds).  
- Ask for a principled justification for choosing the kernel family {k(x,·)} as test functions beyond computational convenience, and whether it is optimal under some criteria.  
- Suggest positioning the method within broader density-ratio estimation literature (e.g., connections to Grunewalder et al.) and whether the approach is a natural/optimal alternative.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, rigorous, and experimentally validated; addressing the above clarifications would strengthen clarity and impact—recommended for acceptance.
