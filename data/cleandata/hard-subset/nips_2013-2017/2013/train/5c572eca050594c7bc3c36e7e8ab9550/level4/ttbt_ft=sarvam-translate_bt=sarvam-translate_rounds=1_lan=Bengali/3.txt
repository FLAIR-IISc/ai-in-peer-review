The authors have proposed a solution to the problem of how to estimate the ratio of two probability density functions. This method is based on the use of a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation. This results in an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, estimating the density is very difficult because it is well known that for any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P with a density \nu, there are some probability measures with no density \nu. Therefore, estimating the ratio of two densities is a problem from a machine learning perspective. As far as I understand, the authors have not described which dominant measure \nu has been used (Lebesgue measure, enumeration measure, or something else), and it is not clear to me whether their results are rigorous because the set of \nu-measures is zero. Also, I have not found a clear statement about what has been assumed. For example, is x in $R^d$, or in some general topological space? Is the density p positive for all x?
