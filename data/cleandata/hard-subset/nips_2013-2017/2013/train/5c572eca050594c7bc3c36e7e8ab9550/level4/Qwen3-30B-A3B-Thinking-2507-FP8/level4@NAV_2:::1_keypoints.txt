- Introduces FIRE: reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and solves it via RKHS regularization, connecting to operator theory and spectral analysis.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and on sub-manifolds, and proposes unsupervised model selection CD-CV.  
- Builds on and extends KMM and LSIF, offering a more flexible framework with explicit theoretical analysis and a classical analysis perspective.  

- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation bridging classical operator theory with modern ML.  
- Strength: Comprehensive theoretical analysis with error bounds and convergence rates for Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Strength: CD-CV is a fully unsupervised model selection method addressing a practical challenge.  
- Strength: Detailed experimental evaluation comparing FIRE to state-of-the-art across multiple datasets and tasks.  

- Weakness: The approximation ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t) is stated without proof or citation; provide reference or proof.  
- Weakness: Needs clearer discussion of bandwidth t vs KDE bandwidth and explanation of why t need not →0 for consistency.  
- Weakness: Brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization should be included for unfamiliar readers.  
- Weakness: Compare convergence rates in Corollaries 2 and 4 with minimax optimal rates in [14] to contextualize performance.  
- Weakness: Include a simple 1D toy example with known p and q to visualize true vs estimated density ratio.  
- Weakness: Provide standard references for benchmark datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Weakness: Add discussion on why naive plug-in (estimating p and q separately) would perform worse than the proposed method.
