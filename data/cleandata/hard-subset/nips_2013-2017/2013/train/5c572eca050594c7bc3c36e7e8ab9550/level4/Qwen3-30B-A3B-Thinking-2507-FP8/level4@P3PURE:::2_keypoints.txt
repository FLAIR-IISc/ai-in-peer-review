- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimators: a Type I Fredholm formulation using an L2 penalty plus RKHS regularization, and a Type II Fredholm formulation using a vanishing-locality kernel.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both methods and empirical validation via experiments.  
- Major strength: the L2 formulation enables practical cross-validation for hyperparameter selection, addressing a gap in unsupervised density-ratio estimation.  
- Suggest explicitly state that RKHS-based objectives (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for cross-validation because their values depend on kernel choice and are not comparable across kernels.  
- Theorem 1 includes a -t / log λ factor leading to suboptimal rates for fixed t; discuss whether spectral assumptions on the integral operator or stronger conditions on q/p could yield polynomial sample-size rates.  
- The smoothness assumption on q/p (e.g., Sobolev class) is restrictive, often implies poor behavior of p/q, and limits practical applicability; this limitation should be discussed.  
- Supplementary material requires substantial revision: numerous typos and errors hinder verification of theoretical results and must be corrected.

- Overall takeaway: novel, CV-friendly density-ratio methods with promising theory and experiments, but theoretical exposition and supplementary proofs need clarification and correction.
