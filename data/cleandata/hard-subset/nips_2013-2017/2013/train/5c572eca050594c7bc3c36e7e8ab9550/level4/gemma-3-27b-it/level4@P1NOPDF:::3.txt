The authors propose a solution to the problem of estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. Their approach leverages a kernel method and formulates the problem as a Fredholm integral equation, resulting in an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, the assumption of underlying densities is a strong one. It is well-known that, within any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P possessing a density with respect to a measure ν, there exist probability measures that *do not* have such a density. Consequently, estimating the ratio of two densities is inherently problematic in a machine learning context. 

As far as I can tell, the authors do not specify the dominating measure ν used in their analysis (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or another). This omission, coupled with the potential for issues arising from sets of ν-measure zero, makes the rigor of their results unclear to me. Furthermore, I find a clear statement of the underlying assumptions lacking. For example, is the domain of x assumed to be $R^d$, or a more general topological space? Is the density p assumed to be positive everywhere?
