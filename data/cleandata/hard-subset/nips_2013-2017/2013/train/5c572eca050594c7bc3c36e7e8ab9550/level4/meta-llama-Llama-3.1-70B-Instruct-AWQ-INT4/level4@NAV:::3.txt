Here's a paraphrased version of the review:

The authors present a solution for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions by leveraging a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which ultimately formulates an inverse problem. However, from a machine learning perspective, this approach is challenging due to the assumption of densities, as it is well established that in any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P with a \nu-density, there exist probability measures lacking \nu-densities, rendering density ratio estimation problematic. 

Upon reviewing the manuscript, I noticed that the authors do not specify the dominating measure \nu (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or otherwise) used in their analysis, which raises concerns regarding the rigor of their results, particularly with respect to sets of \nu-measure zero. Furthermore, I found the manuscript lacking in explicit statements regarding the underlying assumptions. For instance, it is unclear whether the variable x belongs to $R^d$ or a more general topological space, and whether the density p is positive for all x.
