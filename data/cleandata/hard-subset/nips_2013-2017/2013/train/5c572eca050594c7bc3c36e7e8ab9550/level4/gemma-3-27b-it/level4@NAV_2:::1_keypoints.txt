- Paper proposes estimating density ratio p/q by reformulating as a Fredholm integral equation and introduces FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator).
- Leverages operator-theoretic regularization (Tikhonov) to derive a family of algorithms.
- Provides theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates.
- Experimental results are promising and show competitive performance.
- Well-written, motivated, and offers a novel connection to classical integral equation theory.
- Needs a brief justification or citation for the kernel approximation ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t).
- Should include a concise explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers less familiar with functional analysis.
- Must clarify the role of kernel parameter t in FIRE versus KDE bandwidth and how FIRE avoids bandwidth convergence issues.
- Should contextualize the provided convergence rates against known minimax bounds (e.g., [12]) and discuss any gaps.
- Experimental evaluation should include simple illustrative toy examples (e.g., 1D Gaussian mixtures) with visualizations of estimated vs true ratios.
- Datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) need fuller referencing and description.
- Add a brief discussion explaining why naive plug-in density estimation is suboptimal compared to FIRE.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, principled regularization framework, solid theoretical foundations, strong empirical results, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: some theoretical claims lack justification/references, missing optimality context, absence of toy demonstrations, and limited comparison to plug-in approaches.
