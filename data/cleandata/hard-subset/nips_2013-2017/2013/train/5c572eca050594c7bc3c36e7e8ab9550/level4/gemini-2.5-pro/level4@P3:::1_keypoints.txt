- Reframes density-ratio estimation q(x)/p(x) as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind (Type I and Type II formulations).  
- Introduces FIRE: Tikhonov-regularized RKHS algorithms with closed-form kernel-matrix solutions for the inverse problem.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for Euclidean and manifold data using Gaussian kernels.  
- Proposes Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), an unsupervised hyperparameter selection method.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness in unsupervised and supervised (regression/classification under covariate shift) settings.  
- Situates work relative to prior methods (KMM, LSIF) with clear connections and distinctions.  
- Strength: novel, elegant formulation linking density-ratio estimation to operator theory and inverse problems.  
- Strength: principled, flexible algorithmic framework (Tikhonov in RKHS) yielding simple, implementable estimators.  
- Strength: thorough, rigorous theoretical treatment supporting the proposed estimators.  
- Strength: practical solution for unsupervised model selection (CD-CV).  
- Strength: paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible.  
- Weakness: some technical claims lack justification or references (e.g., kernel approximation ∫k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y)+O(t)); add proofs/references and high-level motivation for Tikhonov.  
- Weakness: theoretical convergence rates need comparison to known minimax optimal rates to assess tightness.  
- Weakness: clarify the role of kernel bandwidth t and contrast it with KDE bandwidth behavior for consistency.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation should include a KDE plug-in baseline and a simple 1D toy example with plots comparing true and estimated q/p.  
- Weakness: add dataset references (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) to improve reproducibility.
