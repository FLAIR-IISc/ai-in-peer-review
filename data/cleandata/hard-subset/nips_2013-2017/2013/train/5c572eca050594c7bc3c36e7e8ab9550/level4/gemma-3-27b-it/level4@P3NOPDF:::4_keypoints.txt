- Strong, well-executed method for estimating the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q with two estimators, kernel test functions, convergence guarantees, and experiments.  
- Two estimators derived: (i) E_p[k(x,.) f] = E_q[k(x,.)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,.) f] ≈ q(x); choice of kernel test functions enables standard kernel method optimization.  
- Convergence rates are provided and scale with dimensionality as expected for density estimation.  
- Request for an intuitive explanation of why estimator (i) enjoys similar convergence properties to estimator (ii), despite bandwidth dependence in (ii).  
- Ask for further motivation of the cost function using L2,p and L2,q norms (equation (9)) and linkage to practical applications.  
- Question the advantage of estimating both E_p and E_q (versus directly using estimated E_q) for importance sampling and clarify whether sampling difficulty from q is the primary motivation.  
- Suggest discussing the relationship between smoothness assumptions on p and q and the smoothness of the ratio q/p (and vice versa).  
- Request analysis of how dimensionality d affects convergence and practical performance—guidance on applicable problem dimensions.  
- Ask for deeper justification of the chosen test-function family U and which properties make it advantageous.  
- Raise the broader question of whether the expectation-operator + test-function strategy (using {k(x,.)}) is optimal, and note similarities to prior work (e.g., "Smooth Operators").  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; addressing these clarifications would improve clarity and impact.
