- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimation methods: an L2-norm RKHS regularization formulation (Type I Fredholm equation) and a kernel-based local approach with shrinking locality (Type II Fredholm equation).  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and empirical experiments demonstrating performance.  
- Main strength: L2-norm formulation enables standard cross-validation for hyperparameter (regularization and kernel) selection, a practical advantage over existing methods like KMM.  
- CV advantage is significant because unsupervised hyperparameter tuning is hard and the proposed objective gives a principled CV target.  
- Improvement: Explicitly motivate why conventional RKHS objectives (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for kernel selection via CV due to scale-dependence across kernels.  
- Improvement: Discuss convergence rates—Theorem 1’s rate (with a (-t/log λ) factor) seems slow; analyze whether polynomial rates in sample size can be obtained under stronger assumptions (e.g., spectral assumptions on the integral operator or extra smoothness of q/p).  
- Improvement: Discuss inherent assumptions on the density ratio (q/p in an RKHS or Sobolev space), their strength, implications for tail behavior, and limitations when reversing the ratio (p/q may be ill-behaved).  
- Improvement: Revise supplementary material thoroughly—numerous typos and errors (e.g., Lemma 5 W2^2 vs Ws^2 and missing terms, incorrect expressions at lines 675/686, integral domain typo at line 1147) impede verification of theoretical claims.  
- Conclusion: Promising framework with cross-validation advantage as the main contribution, but needs stronger discussion of rates and assumptions plus corrected, clear proofs in the supplement.
