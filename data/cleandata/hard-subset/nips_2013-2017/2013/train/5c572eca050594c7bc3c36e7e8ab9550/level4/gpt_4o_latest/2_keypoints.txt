- Introduces two new density-ratio estimators: a Type I method reformulating the importance-sampling equality as an L^2+RKHS regularization (Fredholm Type I) and a Type II kernel-based method with locality parameter → 0 (Fredholm Type II).  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and empirical experiments validating practical utility.  
- Key advantage over existing methods (e.g., KMM): L^2-based formulation permits using cross-validation for kernel/parameter selection in this unsupervised problem.  
- Notes that RKHS-objective methods like KMM are unsuitable for CV because objective values depend on the kernel and are thus incomparable across kernels.  
- Theorem 1’s rate contains a (-t / log λ) factor causing slow convergence for fixed t; suggests exploring stronger assumptions to obtain polynomial rates.  
- Recommends investigating spectral assumptions (as in Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) or extra regularity on q/p to derive faster, polynomial rates with fixed t.  
- Highlights the restrictive nature of assuming q/p lies in a well-behaved class (e.g., Sobolev): requires prior knowledge and can imply poor behavior for the inverse ratio p/q; paper should discuss this limitation.  
- Supplementary material has numerous typos/errors that impede verification of theory; specific corrections noted (e.g., Lemma 5 W2^2 → Ws^2, corrected bound D1 (t/-log λ)^2 + λ^{1-α} D2 ||f||_2^2, wrong expressions on lines 675/686, domain correction in Lemma 5 proof).  
- Overall assessment: novel method with the important contribution that CV can be effectively used for parameter selection in density-ratio estimation.
