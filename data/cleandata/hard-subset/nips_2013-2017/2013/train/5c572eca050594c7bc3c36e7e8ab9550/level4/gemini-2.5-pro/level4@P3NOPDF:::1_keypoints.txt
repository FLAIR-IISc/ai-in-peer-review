- Paper proposes a novel, theoretically principled method for density ratio estimation via a Fredholm integral formulation solved with Tikhonov regularization.  
- The manuscript is well-written, clearly motivated, and enjoyable to read.  
- Addresses an important and widely applicable problem in density ratio estimation.  
- Casting the problem as an integral equation is elegant and conceptually appealing.  
- Several key derivations are missing or insufficiently justified (e.g., the integral kernel approximation ∫k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t)).  
- The paper should include a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization to improve accessibility for non-specialists.  
- Needs a discussion comparing the method to a naive plug-in baseline (separately estimate p and q then take the ratio).  
- Expand and clarify the connection to density estimation/KDE, especially the different role of bandwidth and why bandwidth need not go to zero here.  
- Compare the provided convergence rates to known minimax lower bounds to contextualize theoretical optimality.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks a simple illustrative toy example showing true vs. estimated ratio and should cite dataset sources (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) for reproducibility.  
- Proofs are in the supplement but were not fully verified by the reviewer; overall, empirical and theoretical comparisons are needed to strengthen the paper.
