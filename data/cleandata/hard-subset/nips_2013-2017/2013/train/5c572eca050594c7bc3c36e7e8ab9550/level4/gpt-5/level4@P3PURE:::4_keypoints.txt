- Proposes two kernel-based estimators of the Radon–Nikodym derivative using identities linking E_p and E_q and optimizes over test functions {k(x,·)} with empirical operators.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates that depend critically on ambient dimension.  
- Well-executed, timely contribution with both theoretical analysis and supporting experiments.  
- Request for high-level intuition for why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees consistency for estimator (i), beyond the formal proof.  
- Question about choice of objective/norms: why optimize in L2,p but combine L2,p and L2,q in eq. (9), and how these choices map to target tasks (when to weight errors under p vs q).  
- Motivation for estimating the ratio in importance-sampling-like settings is unclear: if E_p and E_q are estimated empirically, when is estimating q/p preferable, and is sampling difficulty from q the main target scenario?  
- Regularization vs assumptions: ask for a principled relationship between smoothness of p and q and smoothness of the ratio (e.g., conditions like p bounded away from zero), and discussion of whether a smooth ratio implies existence of smooth p and q.  
- Request discussion of dimensional dependence in the rates and for which d and sample-size regimes the methods are practically useful.  
- Question about the choice U = {k(x,·)}: seek principled motivation beyond computational convenience, including approximation or identifiability considerations.  
- Broader perspective: ask whether {k(x,·)} is a canonical/test-function choice for covariate-shift-type problems and note conceptual similarity to prior work (e.g., “Smooth Operators”) that also leverages transformations to access target quantities.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper addressing an important problem with clear methodological contributions, convergence study, and empirical validation.
