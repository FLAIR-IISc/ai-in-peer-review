- Paper proposes a kernel-based method using a Fredholm integral to estimate the ratio of two probability density functions, framing it as an inverse problem.  
- Fundamental concern: assuming existence of densities is problematic — measures with densities have arbitrarily close measures that lack densities, undermining density-ratio estimation.  
- The dominating measure nu is never specified (Lebesgue, counting, etc.), creating rigor issues related to sets of nu-measure zero.  
- Key assumptions are omitted: domain of x (e.g., R^d vs. general topological space) and positivity of p are not stated, leaving the framework ambiguous.
