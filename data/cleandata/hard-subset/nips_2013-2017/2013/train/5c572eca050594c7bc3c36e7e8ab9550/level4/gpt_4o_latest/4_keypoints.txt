- Proposes two equalities to derive estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q using kernel test functions and empirical integral operators.  
- Chooses the test-function set {k(x,·)} to minimize discrepancy, enabling use of standard kernel optimization methods.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates that deteriorate with ambient dimensionality.  
- Reviewer considers the work well-executed and a valuable contribution.  
- Requests an intuitive explanation for why optimization over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence for equality (i), as is clearer for (ii).  
- Questions the motivation for the combined L2,p and L2,q cost in Eq. (9) versus optimizing purely over L2,p as stated earlier.  
- Asks why the method estimates expectations via p and q in importance-sampling settings rather than directly using Eq, and whether hard-to-sample q is the primary application.  
- Requests discussion on the relationship between smoothness of q and p and smoothness of their ratio q/p (and implications for regularization).  
- Requests clarification on practical dimensionality limits: for which dimensions is the method expected to be effective.  
- Asks for a compelling motivation for the chosen test-function family U.  
- Asks whether the {k(x,·)} approach is the “right” general framework for covariate-shift/radonic-derivative estimation and notes similarity to Grünewälder et al. (“Smooth Operators”).  
- Concludes that the paper is timely, robust, provides theoretical guarantees, and includes experimental validation.
