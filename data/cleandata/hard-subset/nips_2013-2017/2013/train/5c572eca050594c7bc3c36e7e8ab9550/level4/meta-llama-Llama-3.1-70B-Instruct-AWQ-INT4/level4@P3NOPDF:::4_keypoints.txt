- Proposes a novel method to estimate the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q using two equalities, empirical operators, and test functions {k(x,.)} to leverage kernel methods.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates that depend critically on ambient dimension.  
- Paper is well-executed with a thorough convergence study and supporting experiments.  
- Request for a short intuitive argument why the first equality’s convergence rate also depends on dimension, similar to the second equality.  
- Cost function choice is insufficiently motivated: authors optimize over L2,p but later use a mix of L2,p and L2,q (eq. 9); need clear rationale and links to applications.  
- Importance-sampling application is unclear: why estimate Ep and Eq empirically when Eq could be used directly, and why sampling from Eq is assumed difficult—need more context.  
- Regularizer enforces smoothness of the ratio q/p, but theorems assume smoothness of p and q; discuss how smoothness of p,q relates to smoothness of the ratio (and vice versa).  
- Discuss the practical implications of dimension-dependent rates and indicate for which dimensions the method is useful.  
- Motivation for the chosen family of test functions U is weak; justify why this family is appropriate or optimal.  
- Relate the approach to prior work (e.g., “Smooth Operators”) and discuss similarities, differences, and whether this is the “right” approach.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor revisions requested; likely to have significant impact after addressing comments.
