The paper presents a method for estimating the ratio q/p of two probability density functions. The technique reformulates the problem using a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, thereby casting it as an inverse problem.

From a machine learning standpoint, the density assumption is delicate: it is well known that within any epsilon-neighborhood of a probability measure P that admits a ν-density, there exist probability measures that do not have a ν-density. Hence, estimating a ratio of two densities can be problematic in ML contexts. Moreover, I could not find a clear specification of the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting, or otherwise), which makes it unclear whether the results are fully rigorous, especially regarding sets of ν-measure zero. I also miss an explicit statement of the assumptions: for instance, does x lie in Rd or in a more general topological space? Is p(x) strictly positive for all x?
