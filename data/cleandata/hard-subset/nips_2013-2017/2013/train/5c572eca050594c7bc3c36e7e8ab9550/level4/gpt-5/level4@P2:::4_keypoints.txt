- Reformulates density-ratio estimation f = q/p as a Fredholm integral equation and solves it with RKHS Tikhonov regularization.  
- Introduces two formulations: Type I (Kp f = Kq 1 estimated from p and q samples) and Type II (Kt,p f ≈ q for local kernels when q is known/observable).  
- Uses empirical averages and the representer theorem to yield closed-form FIRE family estimators with out-of-sample prediction.  
- Provides concentration bounds and nonasymptotic convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Rd and on smooth submanifolds.  
- Proposes CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-density moment-matching model-selection scheme using test functions.  
- Experiments show FIRE is competitive or superior to KMM, LSIF, and thresholded inverse KDE under covariate shift and importance weighting.  
- Conceptual strength: principled inverse-problem view linking operator theory and kernel learning.  
- Algorithmic strength: simple closed-form RKHS solutions and natural out-of-sample estimation (unlike KMM).  
- Theoretical strength: explicit finite-sample bounds, bandwidth/regularization dependence, and intrinsic-dimension (manifold) rates.  
- Practical strength: CD-CV offers a reasonable unsupervised model-selection aligned with the moment-matching objective.  
- Empirical strength: stable and competitive performance across tasks tested.  
- Identifiability concern: need clearer intuition/conditions for Type I injectivity of Kp and links to universality/characteristic kernels and spectrum.  
- Loss-choice question: more guidance needed on when to use L2,p, L2,q, or mixtures and how to choose γ relative to downstream tasks.  
- Importance-sampling motivation: clarify when learning a reusable f is preferable to directly estimating Eq[u] (many u, covariate-shift classifiers, Type II with known q, AIS/MCMC).  
- Regularization/smoothness gap: discuss conditions under which smooth p,q imply smooth f and whether constraints (nonnegativity, normalization) should be incorporated like in KMM.  
- Dimensional dependence: rates worsen with ambient dimension; practical guidance on n vs d and bandwidth scaling would help practitioners.  
- CD-CV test-function choice: motivate use of linear/half-space functions, match U to downstream function classes, and analyze sensitivity/robustness.  
- Positioning vs prior work: expand relation to KMM and “Smooth Operators” and clarify novelty (inverse-problem framing, regularized solution, explicit density-ratio rates).  
- Quality: technically sound derivations and bounds, but add intuition on operator injectivity and kernel requirements.  
- Clarity: well written; suggested additions include Type I identifiability intuition, loss-selection guidance, constraint discussion, and when to use Type I vs Type II.  
- Originality: novel synthesis recasting density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm inverse problem with manifold-aware rates and unsupervised CD-CV.  
- Significance: addresses core transfer-learning/importance-weighting problems with principled methods, theory, and practical model selection.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled formulation, solid theory (including manifolds), useful unsupervised selection, strong motivation, and competitive empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited intuition on Type I identifiability, sparse guidance on loss/U choices, no constraint handling for valid ratios, and limited practical advice on dimensionality regimes.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, timely contribution recommended for acceptance after addressing the noted clarifications.
