- Proposes reformulating density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solving it via RKHS regularization.  
- Introduces the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family of algorithms.  
- Provides theoretical analysis for FIRE with Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and compact sub-manifolds.  
- Proposes CD-CV, a fully unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection.  
- Establishes a principled connection between density ratio estimation and classical inverse problems, linking statistical learning and operator theory.  
- Develops a flexible, theoretically grounded, and computationally efficient kernel-based algorithm family.  
- Derives concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in both Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Empirical results show strong performance and improved computational efficiency versus KMM, LSIF, and TIKDE.  
- CD-CV addresses a major practical gap by providing unsupervised model selection for density ratio estimation.  
- Major weakness: the paper does not specify the dominating measure ν used in the density ratio formulation, undermining mathematical rigor.  
- Unclear assumptions about domain and density properties (e.g., whether data lie in R^d, general topological space, or whether p(x) > 0 everywhere).  
- Insufficiently distinguishes the theoretical and practical advantages of the proposed regularization approach over related methods like KMM.  
- Notation ambiguity—especially between the integral operator kernel k and the RKHS kernel k_H—needs clarification.  
- Experimental comparisons should include more recent baselines beyond KMM, LSIF, and TIKDE to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Overall: theoretically strong and practically promising, but requires clarifications on measure-theoretic and domain/density assumptions to validate the theoretical results.
