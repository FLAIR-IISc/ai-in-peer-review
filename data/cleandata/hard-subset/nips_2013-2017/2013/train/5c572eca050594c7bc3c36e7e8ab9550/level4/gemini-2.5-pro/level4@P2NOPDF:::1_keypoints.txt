- Proposes a novel density-ratio estimator by reformulating estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind.  
- Paper is well-written and addresses an important problem for the ML community.  
- Lacks theoretical comparison to minimax or other optimal rates (e.g., those in [12]) to assess statistical optimality.  
- Needs discussion comparing the proposed direct approach to naive plug-in estimators and why solving the integral equation is preferable.  
- Empirical evaluation is incomplete: no direct comparison to state-of-the-art density-ratio estimation algorithms.  
- Recommends adding a simple low-dimensional toy example (e.g., 1D Gaussians) to provide intuition and visual assessment.  
- Several technical claims lack proofs or citations (e.g., the identity ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t)).  
- Clarify the role of the kernel bandwidth t, especially the claim that consistency may not require t → 0, contrasting with KDE.  
- Provide an intuitive explanation of Tikhonov regularization and its necessity for the ill-posed Fredholm equation.  
- Minor: cite sources for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Minor: all proofs are deferred to supplementary material and were not checked as part of the review.  
- Conclusion: promising and interesting approach but needs stronger theoretical/empirical comparisons and a toy demonstration to be more convincing.
