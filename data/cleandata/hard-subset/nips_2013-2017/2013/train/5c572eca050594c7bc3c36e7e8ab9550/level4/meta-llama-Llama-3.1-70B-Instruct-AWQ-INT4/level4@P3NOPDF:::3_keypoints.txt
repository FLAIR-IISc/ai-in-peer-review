- Paper proposes kernel + Fredholm integral equation approach to estimate ratio of two probability density functions (inverse problem).
- Fundamental concern: assuming existence of densities is problematic—near any measure with a density there exist measures without densities, making density-ratio estimation fragile from an ML viewpoint.
- Manuscript fails to specify the dominating measure ν (Lebesgue, counting, or other), which is essential for rigor and interpretation.
- Lack of clarity about handling sets of ν-measure zero undermines theoretical claims.
- Assumptions are not clearly stated: domain of x (R^d vs. general topological space) and whether p(x) is assumed strictly positive everywhere are unspecified.
- Recommendation: authors must explicitly state the dominating measure and all model/space/density assumptions and discuss implications for applicability and limitations.
