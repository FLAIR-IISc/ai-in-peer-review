- Introduces two novel density-ratio estimation methods framed as Fredholm-equation-based regularization: Type I (importance-sampling, L2 deviation + RKHS regularizer) and Type II (kernel with locality parameter → 0).  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and experimental validation for the proposed methods.  
- Key strength: the L2 formulation enables effective cross-validation for parameter selection, addressing a gap in unsupervised density-ratio tuning and outperforming methods like KMM whose RKHS-norm objectives hinder CV.  
- Concern: Theorem 1 gives a slow convergence rate scaling like (-t / log λ) for fixed t, whereas kernel-method literature often achieves polynomial rates in sample size.  
- Recommendation: investigate stronger assumptions (e.g., operator-spectrum conditions à la Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) or stronger assumptions on q/p to potentially obtain polynomial convergence rates and discuss these possibilities.  
- Important limitation not sufficiently acknowledged: assuming q/p lies in a well-behaved function class (e.g., a Sobolev space) is a strong, often unrealistic prior and can imply poor behavior for the inverse ratio p/q.  
- Supplementary material needs substantial revision—numerous typos and mathematical errors hinder verification (e.g., Lemma 5: W2^2 should be Ws^2 and bound corrections; incorrect expressions at lines 675/686; mis-specified integral domain in Lemma 5).  
- Overall: promising approach with the practical CV advantage; addressing convergence-rate issues and thoroughly correcting the supplement would significantly strengthen the paper.
