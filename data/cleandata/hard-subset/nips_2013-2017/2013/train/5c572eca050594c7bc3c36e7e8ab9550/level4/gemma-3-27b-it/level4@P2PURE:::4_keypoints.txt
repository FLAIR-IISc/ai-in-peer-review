- Proposes two estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative using equalities (i) E_p[k(x,·)f]=E_q[k(x,·)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,·)f]≈q(x), leveraging {k(x,·)} as test functions and kernel methods for optimization.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates that depend on data dimensionality.  
- Request: give an intuitive explanation for why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence for equality (i).  
- Request: justify the cost choice—optimization on p uses L2,p while Eq. (9) mixes L2,p and L2,q—and relate this to target tasks/applications.  
- Question: for importance sampling, explain why the method estimates E_p[q/p] instead of directly using an E_q estimate, and clarify whether sampling difficulty from q is the primary motivating scenario.  
- Suggestion: explore the relationship between smoothness of p and q and smoothness of the ratio q/p, clarifying implications and identifiability issues.  
- Request: discuss the dimensional dependence of convergence rates and specify the problem dimensionality range where the method remains practical.  
- Request: motivate the specific choice of the test function family U.  
- Broader question: argue whether selecting {k(x,·)} is an optimal strategy for defining the cost function in covariate-shift–type problems, noting conceptual similarities to "Smooth Operators" [1].  
- Overall verdict: timely, strong paper with solid theory and experiments; a few clarifications would strengthen the presentation.
