- Novel contribution: reformulates density ratio estimation q(x)/p(x) as a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Paper is well-written, engaging, and addresses an important problem with practical applications (importance sampling, transfer learning).  
- Strong theoretical foundation from classical operator theory with convergence rate bounds.  
- Lacks sufficient detail and citations for key claims (e.g., ∫k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t)).  
- Needs clearer discussion of bandwidth roles and how this method differs from standard KDE.  
- Tikhonov regularization is central but requires a brief explanation for readers unfamiliar with it.  
- Proofs are in the supplement; the main text should highlight key proof insights or summaries.  
- Should include a comparison to a naive plug-in approach (separately estimating p and q) to justify the integral-equation approach.  
- Convergence rates are presented but not compared to known minimax bounds or prior results (e.g., [12]).  
- Experiments should include citations for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Recommend adding toy problem experiments (e.g., 1D plots comparing true vs. estimated q/p) for visual assessment.  
- Overall assessment: solid and novel contribution but missing comparisons, toy demonstrations, and some explanatory details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, strong theory, clear presentation, important applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing minimax comparisons, no toy demonstrations, insufficient explanations of motivations and reliance on supplementary proofs.
