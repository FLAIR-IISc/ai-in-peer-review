- Proposes reformulating density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solved with RKHS regularization, yielding the FIRE algorithm family that generalizes KMM and LSIF.
- Provides theoretical guarantees for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and smooth d-dimensional submanifolds, including concentration bounds and convergence rates.
- Introduces CD-CV, a fully unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for parameter selection.
- Establishes strong theoretical links to classical inverse problems in analysis, framing density ratio estimation in a new mathematical context.
- Extends prior work by delivering convergence rates for distributions supported on manifolds.
- Experimental results show promising performance versus KMM, LSIF, and TIKDE across multiple datasets.
- Technical approach is sound and well-justified, and the Fredholm perspective is an original contribution.
- High practical significance for covariate shift adaptation, importance sampling, and transfer learning.
- Paper omits specification of the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue vs. counting), weakening mathematical rigor.
- Domain assumptions are unclear—paper does not state whether x lies in R^d or a more general topological space.
- Fails to explicitly assume or address positivity of p, which is necessary for q/p to be well-defined.
- Does not clearly handle or discuss sets of measure zero, complicating assessment of generality.
- Insufficiently differentiates from previous NIPS work on related theoretical analyses (e.g., KMM in [26]).
- Presentation would benefit from more precise and explicit statement of foundational assumptions in the introduction and theory sections.
