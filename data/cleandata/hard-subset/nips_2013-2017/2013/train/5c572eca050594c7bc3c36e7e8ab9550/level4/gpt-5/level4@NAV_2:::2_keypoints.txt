- Introduces two complementary methods for estimating q/p: Type I (importance-sampling identity → RKHS-regularized inverse problem) and Type II (localized kernel approximating identity → regularized inverse formulation), with convergence analysis and empirical results.  
- Key advantage over prior work (e.g., KMM): the L2-based formulation naturally supports cross-validation for tuning parameters in an unsupervised setting.  
- Suggest explicitly noting that RKHS-norm objectives (as in KMM) are ill-suited for kernel selection via CV because the objective depends on the kernel and is not comparable across kernels.  
- Theorem 1’s (−t / log λ) factor implies slow rates for fixed t; ask whether stronger assumptions (e.g., spectral conditions like Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) or assumptions on q/p can yield polynomial sample-rate results and recommend discussing such cases.  
- Point out that assuming q/p lies in a regular function class (e.g., Sobolev) is a strong requirement that effectively presumes knowledge of ratio or tail behavior, and that p/q may behave poorly under this assumption; recommend discussion of this limitation.  
- Supplementary material needs careful revision: numerous typos and errors (examples in Lemma 5, incorrect expressions and integration domain) hinder verification of theoretical claims.  
- References: recommend citing/connecting to Caponnetto & De Vito (2007) for optimal rates in related regularized least-squares settings.  
- Overall: a promising new approach whose main strength is enabling effective CV-based parameter selection for density ratio estimation.
