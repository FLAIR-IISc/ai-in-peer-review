- Paper introduces two kernel-based estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative (p/q) using test functions {k(x,·)}, replacing integral operators with empirical versions, and provides convergence guarantees that suffer from strong dependence on data dimensionality.

- Convergence rates: request for an intuitive explanation why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence for estimator (i) as it does for estimator (ii).

- Choice of cost function: ask for clearer motivation for optimizing over L2(p) initially but using a combination of L2(p) and L2(q) in (9), and how these choices relate to downstream tasks (e.g., importance sampling, covariate shift).

- Motivation for importance sampling: question the advantage of estimating q/p when samples from q could directly estimate E_q; request discussion of scenarios where indirect estimation is preferable and whether this is the primary application.

- Smoothness assumptions and regularization: ask to clarify relationship between smoothness regularization on the ratio and theoretical smoothness assumptions on p and q, since smooth ratio does not imply smooth component densities.

- Dependence on dimensionality: request discussion of practical implications of the convergence rates’ dependence on dimension and the dimensional range where the method is useful.

- Choice of test functions: ask for more detailed motivation for selecting the specific family U of test functions.

- Relation to other approaches: suggest positioning the method within the broader literature on expectation-matching and operator-based estimation, and comparing similarities/differences with related works (e.g., [1]).

- Overall assessment: positive — timely, well-executed contribution with theoretical guarantees and experiments, but would be strengthened by addressing the above clarifications.
