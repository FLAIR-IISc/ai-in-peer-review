- Proposes a novel density-ratio estimator by reformulating q/p estimation as a Fredholm equation of the first kind and solving it with regularization in RKHS; algorithms named FIRE, noted as flexible and easy to implement.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates, with explicit results for the Gaussian kernel.  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for model selection, useful in unsupervised and semi-supervised settings.  
- Empirical results on multiple datasets show FIRE often outperforms state-of-the-art methods in tasks like importance sampling, covariate shift correction, and classification.  
- Discusses relationship between smoothness of p and q and smoothness of the ratio q/p, and argues optimizing over test functions {k(x,·)} suffices for convergence.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a principled RKHS + Fredholm framework.  
- Strength: method is flexible, simple, and broadly applicable.  
- Suggestion: provide more motivation for the chosen cost function and link it to specific applications.  
- Suggestion: discuss how the convergence rates depend on the ambient dimension.  
- Suggestion: give more insight into the choice of the family of test functions U.  
- Minor concern: some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Overall: timely, strong contribution with minor clarifications needed; arguments for acceptance outweigh the concerns.
