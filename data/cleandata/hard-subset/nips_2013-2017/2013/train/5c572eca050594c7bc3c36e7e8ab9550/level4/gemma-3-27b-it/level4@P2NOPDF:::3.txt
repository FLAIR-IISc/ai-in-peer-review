The authors address the challenging problem of estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. Their proposed solution leverages kernel methods and formulates the problem as a Fredholm integral equation, resulting in an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, estimating probability densities is inherently difficult. It is a well-established fact that within any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure *P* possessing a density with respect to a measure *ν*, there exist probability measures lacking such a density. Consequently, accurately estimating the ratio of two densities presents a fundamental challenge. 

A key omission in the paper is a clear specification of the dominating measure *ν*. It is unclear whether Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or another measure is employed. This lack of clarity raises concerns about the rigor of the results, particularly regarding the potential influence of sets of *ν*-measure zero. A precise statement of the underlying assumptions is also missing. For instance, the space to which *x* belongs – whether it's *R<sup>d</sup>* or a more general topological space – is not defined. Furthermore, it is not stated whether the density *p* is assumed to be positive everywhere. 

These ambiguities significantly hinder a complete assessment of the work’s validity and potential impact. Addressing these points would substantially strengthen the theoretical foundations and improve the clarity of the contribution.
