- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimation methods cast as regularization problems: one from the importance-sampling equation (Type I Fredholm) and one using a local kernel (Type II Fredholm).  
- Method 1 combines an L^2 deviation term with RKHS regularization to solve a Type I Fredholm equation.  
- Method 2 uses a kernel with a locality parameter that must tend to zero for consistency, yielding a Type II Fredholm equation.  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and empirical experiments demonstrating practical performance.  
- Methods share similarities with existing approaches (e.g., KMM) but differ in formulation and implementation.  
- Key advantage: L^2 formulation enables use of cross-validation for parameter selection in an unsupervised setting.  
- RKHS-derived objectives (like KMM) are unsuitable for kernel selection via CV because objective values are kernel-dependent and incomparable.  
- Theorem 1 includes a (-t / log λ) factor, giving slow convergence for fixed t and suggesting room to seek polynomial rates.  
- Suggests exploring stronger assumptions (e.g., on the spectrum of K_{p,t} or on q/p) to potentially achieve polynomial convergence rates, citing Caponnetto & De Vito (2007).  
- Notes that assuming q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) is common but strong and may be unrealistic, and can make p/q behave poorly.  
- Supplementary material needs substantial revision due to typos and errors that impede verification of theoretical results.  
- Lists specific supplement errors to fix: in Lemma 5 replace W2^2 with Ws^2 and correct the bound to D1 (t/-log λ)^2 + λ^{1-α} D2 ||f||_2^2; fix incorrect expressions at lines 675 and 686 (Eq.31 is correct); change the integral domain at line 1147 to ||ξ||^2 ≥.  
- Overall assessment: novel approach with the notable strength of enabling effective CV-based parameter selection, but theoretical-rate discussion and supplement corrections need attention.
