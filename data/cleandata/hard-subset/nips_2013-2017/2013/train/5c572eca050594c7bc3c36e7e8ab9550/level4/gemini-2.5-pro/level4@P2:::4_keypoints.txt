- Addresses density-ratio estimation by reframing it as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind.  
- Derives two formulations: Type I (uses samples from both p and q) and Type II (uses q values on p-sampled points with a localizing kernel).  
- Solves the ill-posed inverse problem via Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding the FIRE family of estimators.  
- Provides closed-form, implementable algorithms compatible with standard kernel-method libraries.  
- Gives rigorous theoretical analysis with explicit error bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.  
- Proposes Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), a fully unsupervised method for model selection.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance against baselines like LSIF and kernel-density-based methods.  
- Novel and principled connection between density-ratio estimation and classical integral-operator theory.  
- Technical soundness: methods are well-executed and practically usable.  
- Comprehensive theoretical treatment adds significant rigor to the approach.  
- CD-CV is a valuable practical contribution for unsupervised parameter tuning.  
- Request for clearer intuition in Type I: why optimizing over test functions {k(x,·)} suffices to recover q/p.  
- Ask for motivation and guidance on choosing loss functions (L2,p vs combinations with L2,q) for different applications.  
- Need clarification of application scenarios where sampling from both p and q is realistic or advantageous (importance-sampling context).  
- Suggest exploring the relationship between smoothness assumptions on p and q and induced smoothness of q/p, and discussing dimensionality limits in practice.  
- Request justification for experimental choices in CD-CV, specifically the selected families of test functions U.  
- Recommend situating the work relative to related operator-based approaches (e.g., Grünewälder et al., "Smooth operators") to place contributions in broader context.  
- Overall conclusion: a timely, strong paper whose clarity and impact would improve by addressing the above minor points.
