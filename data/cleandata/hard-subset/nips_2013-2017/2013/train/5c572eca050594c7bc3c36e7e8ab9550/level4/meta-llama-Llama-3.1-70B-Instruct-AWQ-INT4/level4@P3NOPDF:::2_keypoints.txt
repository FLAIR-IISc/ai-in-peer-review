- Paper introduces two novel density-ratio estimators: a Type I Fredholm approach using an importance-sampling equation with RKHS regularization, and a Type II Fredholm approach using a kernel whose locality parameter must go to zero.  
- Both methods are formulated as L^2 regularization problems and come with theoretical convergence results and empirical evaluations.  
- Key advantage: the L^2 formulation permits use of cross-validation for kernel/parameter selection, addressing a general gap in unsupervised parameter tuning.  
- Contrast with KMM: RKHS/KMM objectives are not suitable for kernel selection via CV because objective values depend on the kernel and are not comparable.  
- Theorem 1 shows a slow convergence rate with a (-t / log λ) factor for fixed t, unlike typical polynomial rates in kernel methods.  
- Reviewer asks whether stronger assumptions (e.g., on the spectrum of K_{p,t}, as in Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) or on q/p could yield polynomial rates for the estimator.  
- Important limitation: assuming q/p lies in a specific function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong, requires prior knowledge of tails, and can make the reverse ratio p/q behave poorly; this should be acknowledged and discussed.  
- Supplementary material needs substantial revision—contains many typos and errors that hinder verification (e.g., Lemma 5: Ws^2 not W2^2 and corrected bound D1 (t/-log λ)^2 + λ^{1-α} D2 ||f||_2^2; incorrect expressions at lines 675/686 though Eq.(31) is correct; integral domain at line 1147 should be ||ξ||^2 ≥).  
- Overall assessment: a novel density-ratio estimation approach with the significant practical benefit of enabling cross-validation for parameter selection.
