- Proposes a novel reformulation of density ratio estimation p/q as a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Paper is clear, readable, and well-presented.  
- Important problem: density ratio estimation is a valuable research area and the work is commendable.  
- Missing justification for key claims, e.g., the statement ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t) needs proof or reference.  
- Many other statements lack sufficient derivation or references and should be substantiated.  
- Method applies to density estimation (q=1) and interestingly may not require bandwidth → 0; this deserves deeper discussion.  
- Clarify the distinct roles of bandwidth parameters here versus standard KDE.  
- Provide a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.  
- Ensure and state that proofs in the supplementary material have been checked for correctness.  
- Explain why naive plug-in (estimate p and q separately) is inferior to the proposed approach.  
- Compare more explicitly to existing methods (e.g., reference [12]) and clarify how close the provided upper bounds are to minimax optimal rates.  
- Cite the datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) and provide references.  
- Include toy problem demonstrations (e.g., 1D plots of true p/q vs estimated p/q) to illustrate estimation quality.  
- Overall positive assessment but weakened by lack of comparative clarity and absence of toy demonstrations.
