- Paper proposes two new density-ratio estimators: an L2-regularized formulation from the importance-sampling equation (Type I Fredholm) and a locality-kernel-based formulation requiring shrinking locality (Type II Fredholm).  
- Strong advantage: L2 formulation allows practical cross-validation (CV) for parameter selection, addressing a gap in unsupervised model selection.  
- Important remark: RKHS objective values (e.g., KMM) are not comparable across kernels, making them unsuitable for CV-based kernel selection.  
- Paper gives a clear introduction linking to importance sampling and covariate shift, and presents a principled framework for algorithm construction and analysis.  
- Authors provide detailed theoretical results including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels.  
- Concern: Theorem 1 yields slow convergence (factor (-t / log λ)) for fixed t; reviewer asks whether stronger assumptions (e.g., spectral assumptions like Caponnetto & De Vito 2007 or smoothness of q/p) can yield polynomial rates.  
- Important limitation to discuss: assuming q/p lies in a nice function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and often implies p/q is ill-behaved; the paper should address practical implications of such assumptions.  
- Supplementary material needs substantial revision: many typos/mistakes (e.g., Lemma 5 W2^2→Ws^2, corrected bound form, incorrect expressions at specific lines, integral domain typo) that hinder verification of theoretical claims.  
- Experimental results are encouraging and show effectiveness (including under covariate shift), but more comparisons with state-of-the-art methods are needed.  
- Paper is overall well-written with clear explanations, but contains minor textual errors and typos that should be fixed.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with a valuable CV-enabled L2 approach, but needs additional discussion of limitations, possible stronger-assumption rates, more empirical baselines, and corrected supplementary proofs.
