- Proposes kernel-based method using a Fredholm integral equation to estimate the ratio of two probability density functions (an inverse problem).
- Method crucially assumes both densities exist, a strong and problematic assumption in ML since measures arbitrarily close to a density-bearing measure may lack densities.
- As a result, estimating density ratios is conceptually fragile in the machine learning setting.
- Manuscript does not specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), undermining rigor and raising issues about ν-null sets.
- Key assumptions are not clearly stated: the domain of x (R^d vs. a general topological space) and whether p is positive everywhere are unspecified.
