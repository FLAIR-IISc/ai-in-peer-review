- Proposes a kernel-based method for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions via a Fredholm integral equation, resulting in an inverse problem.

- The assumption that the distributions admit densities is very strong and fragile; small perturbations can produce measures without densities, undermining robustness.

- Because any ε-neighborhood of a measure with a density can contain measures without densities, estimating density ratios is problematic for ML applications.

- The paper does not specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue or counting), leaving key definitions and proofs ambiguous.

- Treatment of ν-null sets is unclear, which is crucial for defining densities, handling ratios, and ensuring the integral equation is well defined.

- Missing explicit statement of the sample space: unclear whether x ∈ R^d or a more general measurable/topological space.

- No clear specification of which dominating measure ν is used in the analysis.

- Unclear whether densities are assumed strictly positive or how zero-density regions are handled when forming ratios.

- Lacks measurability and regularity assumptions on the kernel and on the densities.

- No assumptions given on support overlap to ensure the ratio is well defined where needed.

- Recommendation to add a formal problem setup specifying (X, B, ν), absolute continuity conditions, positivity/support-overlap assumptions, domain of x, and structural/kernel assumptions to strengthen rigor and applicability.
