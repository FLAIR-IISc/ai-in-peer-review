This paper addresses the problem of estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. The proposed approach formulates this as an inverse problem by employing a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation.

However, the work's foundational premise—the existence of densities—is a strong assumption from a machine learning perspective. It is well-known that for any probability measure P possessing a \nu-density, there exist other probability measures within any epsilon-neighborhood of P that are not absolutely continuous with respect to \nu and thus lack a density. This makes the general problem of density ratio estimation fundamentally challenging.

The manuscript's rigor is further called into question by a lack of clarity on several key points. The authors do not specify the dominating measure \nu (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure) with respect to which the densities are defined. This omission makes it difficult to assess the validity of the results, particularly concerning the treatment of sets of \nu-measure zero. Additionally, the paper would benefit significantly from a clear statement of its assumptions. For instance, it is not specified whether the domain of x is $\mathbb{R}^d$ or a more general topological space, nor is it clear if the density p is assumed to be strictly positive.
