The paper addresses the challenging problem of estimating the ratio of two probability density functions, proposing a method leveraging kernel techniques and framing the problem as a Fredholm integral equation – inherently an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, the core assumption of underlying densities is highly restrictive; it’s a known issue that any epsilon neighborhood of a measure with a density can contain measures lacking one, making density ratio estimation fundamentally difficult. The manuscript currently lacks clarity regarding the choice of dominating measure, ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting, or otherwise), and the potential impact of sets of ν-measure zero on the rigor of the presented results is unclear. Furthermore, key assumptions underpinning the work are not explicitly stated. Specifically, the domain of x – whether $R^d$ or a more general topological space – and the positivity of the density p(x) are left unspecified.
