- Presents a novel framework that casts density ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind using kernel integral operators.  
- Introduces two formulations: Type I (uses samples from p and q) and Type II (uses q values at p-sampled points), both leading to RKHS Tikhonov regularization.  
- Proposes the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family of simple, closed-form kernel-based algorithms.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis with concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and submanifolds.  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), a fully unsupervised hyperparameter selection method leveraging the importance sampling identity.  
- Empirical results show FIRE and CD-CV compare favorably to existing methods like LSIF and kernel density baselines.  
- Strength: elegant, well-motivated connection between density ratio estimation and classical Fredholm inverse problems.  
- Strength: major practical contribution in unsupervised hyperparameter selection (CD-CV), addressing a key challenge in unsupervised learning.  
- Strength: flexible framework supporting multiple loss choices and regularizers, with simple kernel-matrix solutions.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical treatment that supports the methodology.  
- Weakness: derived convergence rates appear slow for fixed kernel width t; paper should discuss possibility of faster polynomial rates under stronger assumptions.  
- Weakness: strong smoothness assumptions on q/p (e.g., Sobolev class) may be unrealistic and have practical implications; discussion needed.  
- Weakness: supplementary material contains typos and errors that impede verification of theoretical claims and must be carefully revised.  
- Weakness: lacks explicit explanation why RKHS-norm objectives (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for kernel selection via cross-validation; clarify this to motivate L2-based CV.  
- Recommendation: overall high-quality and likely influential work; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing supplement clarity and discussing rates/assumptions.
