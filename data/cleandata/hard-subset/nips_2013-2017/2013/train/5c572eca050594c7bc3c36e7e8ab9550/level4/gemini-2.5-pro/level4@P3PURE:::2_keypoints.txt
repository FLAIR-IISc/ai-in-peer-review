- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimators: a Type I L2/RKHS regularization (importance-sampling identity) and a Type II method using a shrinking-locality kernel; provides convergence results and experiments.  
- Major advantage: L2 formulation naturally enables cross-validation for model selection, unlike objectives (e.g., KMM) that are kernel-dependent and not directly comparable across kernels.  
- Suggestion: explicitly explain why the L2 objective supports CV while RKHS-norm-based objectives do not.  
- Concern: Theorem 1’s rate is slow due to the (-t / log λ) factor; ask whether polynomial convergence rates can be obtained under stronger spectral/smoothness assumptions (e.g., along the lines of Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).  
- Recommend adding a discussion of faster rates under clearly stated additional assumptions (e.g., spectrum decay or extra smoothness of q/p).  
- Broader limitation: method assumes q/p lies in a well-behaved function class (e.g., Sobolev), which depends on tail behavior; note asymmetry since p/q may not be well-behaved and this limits symmetric use.  
- Supplementary material has many typos and errors that impede verification of proofs (e.g., Lemma 5 notation and bound, incorrect expressions at lines 675/686 though Eq.31 is correct, integral domain at line 1147); requires significant revision.  
- Overall: promising and novel approach with the key strength of enabling principled CV for parameter selection.
