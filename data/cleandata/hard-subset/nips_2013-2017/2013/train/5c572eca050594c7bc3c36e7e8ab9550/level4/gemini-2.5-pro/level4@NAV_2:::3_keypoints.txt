- Problem addressed: estimating the density ratio q/p for tasks like importance sampling and covariate shift correction.
- Novel formulation: reframes density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind.
- Method: solves the inverse problem using Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding the FIRE algorithm family.
- Settings: develops algorithms for Type I (samples from p and q) and Type II (samples from p and known q values at sample points).
- Relation to prior work: connects to KMM and LSIF, claiming improved stability and a more principled optimization via regularization.
- Theory: provides rigorous concentration bounds and convergence rates, including results for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.
- Model selection: proposes an unsupervised Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) procedure for hyperparameter selection.
- Strength — originality: the Fredholm formulation offers a novel and potentially influential perspective.
- Strength — theoretical soundness: technically strong with principled framework and detailed analysis.
- Strength — practical contribution: CD-CV addresses hyperparameter selection without labeled data, aiding practical use.
- Strength — clarity/experiments: algorithms are simple to implement and experiments compare against relevant baselines.
- Weakness — assumptions clarity: introductory setup should more clearly state density and domain assumptions (e.g., absolute continuity w.r.t. Lebesgue measure).
- Weakness — support mismatch: paper lacks explicit discussion of issues when supp(p) does not cover supp(q) and ratio blow-up.
- Weakness — robustness to non-AC measures: method relies on strong density assumptions and needs discussion of behavior when those fail.
- Overall: high-quality, novel, and well-supported work that would benefit from clearer foundational assumptions and a discussion of support-related practical/theoretical issues.
