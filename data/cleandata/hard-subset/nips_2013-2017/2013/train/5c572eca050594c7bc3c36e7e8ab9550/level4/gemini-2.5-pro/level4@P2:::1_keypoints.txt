- Reformulates density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation (inverse problem) and derives a principled RKHS-based estimator family (FIRE) via Tikhonov regularization.  
- Proposes two problem settings (Type I and Type II) and develops explicit, implementable algorithms within the kernel methods framework.  
- Framework is flexible, supporting different norms (L2,p, mixed L2,p/L2,q, RKHS) and regularization choices.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Introduces an unsupervised hyperparameter selection method (Cross-Density Cross-Validation, CD-CV) for model tuning.  
- Strength: elegant, original connection to classical operator theory that grounds the method theoretically.  
- Strength: simple, explicit algorithms that are easy to implement and adapt.  
- Strength: substantial theoretical support increases credibility of the approach.  
- Strength: practical contribution of an unsupervised validation criterion addresses a common real-world need.
