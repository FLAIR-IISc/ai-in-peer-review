The authors propose a solution to the problem of estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. Their approach is based on using a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which leads to an inverse problem. However, from a machine learning perspective, the assumption of densities is a challenging one. It is well-known that in any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P with a nu-density, there exist probability measures without nu-densities. Therefore, estimating the ratio of two densities is problematic from a machine learning standpoint.

Upon reviewing the manuscript, I noticed that the authors do not specify the dominating measure nu used in their analysis (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or something else). This omission raises concerns about the rigor of their results, particularly with regards to sets of nu-measure zero. Furthermore, I found the manuscript lacking in clear statements about the underlying assumptions. For instance, it is unclear whether the variable x belongs to Rd or a more general topological space. Additionally, the authors do not specify whether the density p is positive for all x.
