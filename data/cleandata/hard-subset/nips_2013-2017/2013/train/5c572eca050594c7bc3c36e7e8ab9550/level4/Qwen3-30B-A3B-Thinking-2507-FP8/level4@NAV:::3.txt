

The authors propose a solution for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions, leveraging a kernel-based approach and a Fredholm integral equation, which frames the problem as an inverse task. From a machine learning perspective, the assumption of densities presents significant challenges, as it is well-established that within any ε-neighborhood of a probability measure \(P\) possessing a \(\nu\)-density, there exist probability measures lacking a \(\nu\)-density. Consequently, estimating the ratio of two densities is inherently problematic in machine learning. The authors do not specify the dominating measure \(\nu\) (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or another), and it remains unclear whether their results are rigorous due to potential issues involving sets of \(\nu\)-measure zero. Furthermore, the review lacks a clear articulation of foundational assumptions, such as whether the domain \(x\) resides in \(\mathbb{R}^d\) or a more general topological space, and whether the density \(p\) is strictly positive for all \(x\).
