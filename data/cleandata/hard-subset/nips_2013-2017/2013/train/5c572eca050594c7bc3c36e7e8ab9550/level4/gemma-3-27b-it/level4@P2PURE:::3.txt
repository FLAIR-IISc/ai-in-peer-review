The authors address the problem of estimating the ratio of two probability density functions, proposing a solution based on kernel methods and a Fredholm integral equation, which frames the problem as an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, estimating such a ratio is fundamentally challenging. It is well-established that in any epsilon-neighborhood of a probability measure P possessing a density with respect to a measure ν, there exist probability measures that *lack* such a density. Consequently, the estimation of a density ratio is inherently problematic. The paper does not specify the dominating measure ν employed (e.g., Lebesgue, counting, or another), and the rigor of the results is questionable due to potential issues arising from sets of ν-measure zero. Crucially, the assumptions underlying the method require clarification. For instance, it is unclear whether the space of x is $R^d$ or a more general topological space, and whether the density p is assumed to be strictly positive everywhere.
