- Paper frames density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral inverse problem and introduces the FIRE family of algorithms using regularization.  
- Uses RKHS-based methods to implement FIRE, yielding flexible and implementable algorithms.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates (including Gaussian kernels).  
- Extends applicability to densities on R and smooth sub-manifolds.  
- Proposes CD-CV, a novel unsupervised model-selection method for this setting.  
- Builds on and cites relevant prior work in density-ratio estimation, covariate shift, and kernel methods.  
- Novel reformulation connects to classical operator theory and facilitates theoretical insights.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates practical performance.  
- Paper lacks clarity on the dominating measure ν used to define densities, raising rigor concerns.  
- Underlying assumptions (domain of x, positivity of p, etc.) are not precisely stated.  
- Differences and practical advantages of FIRE over related methods like Kernel Mean Matching are not fully elucidated.  
- Experimental section would benefit from focused ablation studies on FIRE design choices.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and original; recommend acceptance if authors clarify measure/assumptions and deepen comparisons.
