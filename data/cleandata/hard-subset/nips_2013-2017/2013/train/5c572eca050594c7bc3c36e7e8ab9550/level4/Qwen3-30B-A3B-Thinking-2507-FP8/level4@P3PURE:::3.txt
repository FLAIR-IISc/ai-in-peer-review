

The authors propose a solution for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions, based on a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation formulation, which reduces the problem to an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, the assumption of well-defined densities is problematic, as it is well-known that within any ε-neighborhood of a probability measure \(P\) possessing a \(\nu\)-density, there exist probability measures lacking a \(\nu\)-density. Consequently, the estimation of a density ratio presents significant challenges in machine learning. The authors fail to specify the dominating measure \(\nu\) (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or another choice), and it remains unclear whether their results are rigorous due to potential issues involving sets of \(\nu\)-measure zero. Furthermore, the paper lacks a clear statement of foundational assumptions: it is unspecified whether the domain of \(x\) is \(\mathbb{R}^d\) or a general topological space, and it is not stated whether the density \(p\) is strictly positive everywhere.
