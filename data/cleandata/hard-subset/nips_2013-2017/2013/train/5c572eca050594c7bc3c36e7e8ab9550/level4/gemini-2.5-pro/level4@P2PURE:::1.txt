This paper introduces a novel method for estimating the density ratio p/q. While several existing methods address this problem, the key contribution is the reformulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation.

**Strengths:**
The paper is well-written and clear. The problem of density ratio estimation is both important and of significant interest.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**
(i) Certain claims in the paper lack sufficient justification. For example, the statement `\int k_t(x,y)f(x)dx=f(y)+ O(t)` should be accompanied by a proof (either in the main text or supplementary material) or a citation. This applies to all similar technical statements, even if their proofs are straightforward.

(ii) In the special case where q=1, the proposed method can be used for density estimation. Notably, this approach does not require the kernel bandwidth to converge to zero for consistency, unlike standard kernel density estimation (KDE), where this would lead to an inconsistent estimator. The paper would be strengthened by a more detailed discussion on this point, clarifying for the reader the distinct role of the bandwidth parameters in this framework compared to their role in KDE.

(iii) As Tikhonov regularization may not be familiar to the entire NIPS audience, a brief explanation of this technique would improve the paper's accessibility.

(iv) All proofs are located in the supplementary material and have not been checked for this review.

(v) The paper should include a discussion on why the proposed direct estimation method is preferable to a naive plug-in approach, which would involve estimating the densities p and q separately and then computing their ratio.

(vi) The paper's performance relative to other methods is not clearly established. For instance, reference [12] also derives minimax bounds for this problem. While Corollaries 2 and 4 provide upper bounds on the convergence rate, it is unclear how these bounds relate to the optimal minimax rates.

(vii) Please provide citations for the datasets used in the experiments (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).

(viii) The paper would benefit significantly from a demonstration on a simple toy problem with known p and q. For example, for one-dimensional densities, a plot showing the true p/q ratio versus the estimated ratio would provide readers with a clear and intuitive assessment of the estimation quality.

In summary, this is a nice paper on density ratio estimation. However, it is not clear how the proposed method performs compared to other algorithms (e.g., [12]), and the paper would be improved by a demonstration of the approach on a simple toy problem, for instance, using one-dimensional p and q densities.
