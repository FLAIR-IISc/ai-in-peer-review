- Proposes estimating density ratios via a Fredholm integral equation — novel idea but lacks theoretical rigor and clarity on assumptions.
- Assumes underlying probability measures have densities, a strong and restrictive premise in ML contexts.
- Fails to acknowledge that arbitrarily close measures may not be absolutely continuous, making density-ratio estimation sensitive and potentially ill-posed.
- Dominating measure ν is never specified (e.g., Lebesgue, counting, or other), creating ambiguity in problem formulation and interpretation.
- Input domain for x is undefined (R^d, subset, or general topological space), undermining validity of kernel and integral assumptions.
- No assumptions or discussion on supports of p and q, nor treatment of cases where q(x)=0 or approaches zero.
- Paper does not address sets of ν-measure zero and related theoretical complications.
- Overall conclusion: interesting approach but insufficiently rigorous; authors must state assumptions and tackle foundational theoretical challenges.
