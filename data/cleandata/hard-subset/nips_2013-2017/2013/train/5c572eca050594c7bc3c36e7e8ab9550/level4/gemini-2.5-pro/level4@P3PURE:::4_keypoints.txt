1. Proposes two kernel-based estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative p/q using operator equalities and RKHS test functions, with convergence guarantees; rates depend on data dimensionality.

2. Overall assessment: well-executed, valuable contribution with solid convergence analysis and experiments.

3. Convergence-rate intuition: need clearer intuition for why minimizing over {k(x,·)} suffices for convergence in case (i), analogous to the bandwidth-based intuition in case (ii).

4. Choice of cost function: equation (9) mixes L2,p and L2,q norms but lacks motivation; explain why this specific combination is used and its relevance to applications.

5. Application scenarios: clarify practical settings—if samples from p are available for training the ratio, why not directly estimate E_p[g]? Expand on cases where p is hard to sample at test time but training samples from both distributions are available.

6. Smoothness assumptions: clarify relation between smoothness of p and q versus smoothness of the ratio p/q (e.g., when smooth p and q with q bounded away from zero imply a smooth ratio, and converses).

7. Dimensional dependence: discuss practical dimensionality limits of the method and for what ranges of d the approach is useful.

8. Motivation for test functions: give more detailed justification for choosing the unit ball in an RKHS as the family U of test functions.

9. Relation to prior operator-based work: position the method relative to similar operator-manipulation approaches (e.g., "Smooth Operators") and discuss whether RKHS test functions are the most natural choice.
