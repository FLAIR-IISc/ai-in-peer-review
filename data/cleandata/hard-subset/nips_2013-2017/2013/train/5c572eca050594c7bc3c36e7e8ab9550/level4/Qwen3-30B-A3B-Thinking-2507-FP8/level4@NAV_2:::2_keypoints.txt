- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and introduces FIRE (Tikhonov regularization in RKHS) for principled estimation.  
- Establishes a novel theoretical connection to integral equations and proposes CD-CV, an unsupervised model selection method.  
- Provides convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings using spectral regularization analysis.  
- Empirically competitive with KMM, LSIF, and TIKDE on regression and classification under covariate shift.  

Strengths:  
- Originality: Fredholm reformulation offers a fresh theoretical lens distinct from previous direct optimization approaches.  
- Novel practical contribution: CD-CV addresses unsupervised parameter selection, filling a critical gap.  
- Theoretical quality: Theorems 1–4 give rigorous concentration bounds across Euclidean, manifold, and boundary domains.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-structured exposition and clear algorithmic descriptions.  

Weaknesses:  
- Convergence rate: Theorem 1 shows suboptimal dependence on t and λ; stronger spectral assumptions could yield polynomial rates.  
- Restrictive assumption: Requiring q/p ∈ W^2_2(Ω) limits applicability to irregular or multimodal ratios and lacks discussion on p/q.  
- Supplementary errors: Mistakes in proofs (e.g., Lemma 5 and incorrect bounds) undermine confidence and need correction.  

Pro/Con for acceptance:  
- Pro: Novel theoretical framework, practical CD-CV, strong empirical results, and clear relevance to covariate shift.  
- Con: Suboptimal rates, restrictive Sobolev assumption, and corrigible but important supplementary material errors.  

Overall:  
- High-quality, valuable contribution with practical utility; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to fix theoretical assumptions and supplementary mistakes.
