- Novel formulation: reframes density ratio estimation as solving a Fredholm integral equation, connecting to mathematical physics and spectral theory.  
- Algorithmic contribution: develops a family of algorithms (FIRE) using regularization and spectral tools.  
- Theoretical guarantees: provides concentration bounds and convergence rates (notably for Gaussian kernels).  
- Practical method: introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for unsupervised parameter selection.  
- Motivation and positioning: well-motivated links to importance sampling, covariate shift, and transfer learning.  
- Related work: correctly identifies and positions against methods like KMM and LSIF.  
- Unclear practical impact: the benefits of the Fredholm-equation perspective for practice are not fully clarified.  
- Missing measure discussion: insufficient treatment of the underlying dominating measure ν and existence/uniqueness of densities.  
- Unstated assumptions: domain of x (Rd vs. general space) and density positivity are not clearly specified.  
- Experimental detail lacking: choices for CD-CV test functions and resampling strategies are not justified.  
- Comparative evaluation weak: needs a more comprehensive comparison, especially KMM with fair out-of-sample extension.  
- Over-referencing: many cited works are listed without substantive analysis of their contributions.  
- Argument for acceptance: introduces a novel, mathematically grounded approach with solid theoretical analysis and a practical parameter-selection method.  
- Argument against acceptance: theoretical foundations weakened by unclear measure/assumptions and empirical evaluation is insufficient to judge practical effectiveness.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept—technically interesting but requires clearer assumptions and stronger experimental validation.
