- Novelty: Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation; paper is well-written and addresses an important problem.  
- Missing justification: The claim ∫k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t) (and similar statements) needs a proof or citation.  
- Density-estimation extension: Method naturally extends to density estimation when q=1; authors should emphasize this and how the bandwidth’s role differs from KDE (avoiding KDE’s consistency issues).  
- Tikhonov regularization: Add a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization (its purpose and common uses) for accessibility.  
- Unverified proofs: Proofs are in the supplement but their correctness was not checked by the reviewer—ensure clarity/correctness.  
- Plug-in baseline: Discuss limitations of naive plug-in (estimate p and q separately then take ratio) and why direct ratio estimation is preferable.  
- Comparison to prior work: Provide a more thorough comparison to existing methods and clarify how the derived convergence rates relate to known minimax rates (e.g., [12]).  
- Dataset references: Give citations/details for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) to aid reproducibility.  
- Illustrative examples: Include simple, known-distribution examples (e.g., 1D plots of true p/q vs estimated) to visually demonstrate accuracy.  
- Overall recommendation: Promising contribution but needs clearer theoretical justification and stronger empirical/theoretical comparisons to improve impact.
