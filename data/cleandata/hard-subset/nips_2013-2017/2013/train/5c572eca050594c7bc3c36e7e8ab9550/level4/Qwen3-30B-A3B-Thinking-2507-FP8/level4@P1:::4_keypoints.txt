- Proposes FIRE: a novel Fredholm integral equation formulation for density ratio estimation (q/p).  
- Uses RKHS regularization to derive theoretically grounded algorithms for Type I (samples from p and q) and Type II (q known at p samples) settings.  
- Provides convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and sub-manifolds.  
- Introduces CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation) for unsupervised model selection, avoiding explicit density estimation.  
- Empirical results show FIRE outperforms baselines (KMM, LSIF, TIKDE) in regression and classification under covariate shift.  
- Strength: Elegant, unifying Fredholm perspective that clarifies relations to existing methods like KMM.  
- Strength: Rigorous convergence analysis extending to manifolds, with clear dependence on dimensionality and smoothness.  
- Strength: CD-CV is a compelling, simple unsupervised parameter selection strategy validated experimentally.  
- Strength: Demonstrated computational efficiency and accuracy across diverse datasets (USPS, CPUsmall, Kin8nm).  
- Weakness: Type I cost function (weighted L2,p/L2,q) lacks explicit practical motivation linking it to specific tasks.  
- Weakness: Conceptual tension between assuming q/p smooth (Sobolev) and enforcing smoothness via RKHS; needs clarification on when q/p inherits smoothness from q and p.  
- Weakness: Convergence rates depend unfavorably on dimensionality; paper should discuss practical applicability for high-dimensional data.  
- Weakness: Test function choice for CD-CV (random linear/half-space) is pragmatic but under-justified; needs explanation for why it works well.  
- Overall: Timely, well-executed contribution with strong theory, practical innovation, and thorough experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
