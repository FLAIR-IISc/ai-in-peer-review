- Proposes a kernel + Fredholm integral equation formulation that reduces density-ratio estimation to an inverse problem.
- Assumes well-defined densities, which is problematic because arbitrarily close probability measures may lack a dominating-density.
- Neglects to specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), making definitions and results ambiguous.
- Potential rigor issues from sets of ν-measure zero are not addressed, so theoretical claims may be invalid.
- Fails to state key foundational assumptions: domain of x (ℝ^d vs. general topological space) is unspecified.
- Does not clarify whether the target density p is strictly positive everywhere, leaving identifiability and inversion conditions unclear.
