- Introduces a novel density-ratio estimation method by reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Strengths: clear, engaging writing and addresses an important problem.  
- Missing justification/proof or reference for the kernel approximation claim ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t).  
- Needs expanded discussion on bandwidth roles vs. KDE, especially since the method avoids bandwidth→0 for consistency when q=1.  
- Provide a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.  
- All proofs are in the supplementary material and were not independently verified by the reviewer.  
- Add discussion on why naive plug-in estimation (estimating p and q separately) is suboptimal compared to the proposed approach.  
- Lacks clear comparison to existing methods (e.g., [12]); unclear how the presented upper bounds relate to minimax optimal rates.  
- Provide references for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Include toy 1D experiments (plots of true vs. estimated density ratio) to visually demonstrate method behavior and estimation quality.  
- Overall: well-written paper but unclear comparative performance and missing illustrative examples.
