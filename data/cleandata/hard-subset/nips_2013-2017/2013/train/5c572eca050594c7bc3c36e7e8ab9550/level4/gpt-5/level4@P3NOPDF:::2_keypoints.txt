- Proposes two RKHS-based density-ratio estimators (Type I and Type II) as Tikhonov-regularized solutions to Fredholm equations with convergence guarantees and empirical evaluation.  
- Key advantage: L2(p) loss enables principled cross-validation for model selection, unlike kernel-norm objectives (e.g., KMM).  
- Strength: Clear connection between importance sampling and operator inversion, yielding two practical algorithms.  
- Strength: L2 loss under p makes CV natural and comparable across kernels and regularization parameters.  
- Strength: Theoretical consistency results with explicit (though conservative) convergence rates.  
- Strength: Empirical results are encouraging and presentation clarifies relation to KMM.  
- Main theoretical concern: Convergence rate includes a t/(-log λ) term yielding slow rates for fixed t; polynomial rates may be achievable under standard source and capacity assumptions.  
- Request: Add corollary/discussion showing polynomial rates under reasonable source conditions on r and spectral decay assumptions on Kp,t (e.g., following Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).  
- Assumptions on r are strong: need explicit discussion of smoothness assumptions (Sobolev/RKHS), tail behavior, and consequences when p/q is ill-behaved.  
- Practical guidance requested: how to schedule the Type II locality parameter with n and sensitivity to that schedule.  
- Practical guidance requested: recommended CV objective and how to implement data splitting with only samples from p and q.  
- Practical guidance requested: computational complexity and numerical stability considerations for operator inversions.  
- Relation to prior work: clarify similarities/differences with LSIF/KLIEP, KMM, and kernel ridge regression regarding loss, regularization, and CV comparability.  
- Presentation issue: Supplement contains numerous typos and mathematical errors (specific lemma/equation corrections and integral domain fixes); supplement needs careful revision.  
- Suggestion: State explicitly whether the L2 loss is w.r.t. p or q, and discuss when to prefer Type I vs Type II (data regime, kernel choice, anticipated smoothness).  
- Suggestion: Add brief discussion on robustness to kernel mis-specification and extreme-density regions, and whether tail trimming/regularization is advisable.  
- Overall assessment: Compelling, practically useful contribution thanks to CV-enabled L2 objective; strengthen theory (polynomial rates) and fix supplement errors to improve acceptance.
