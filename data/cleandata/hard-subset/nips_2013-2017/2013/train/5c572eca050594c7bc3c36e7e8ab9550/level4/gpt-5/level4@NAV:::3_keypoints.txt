- Paper proposes estimating density ratios via a kernel-based Fredholm integral formulation, converting it into an inverse problem.  
- Requiring existence of densities is a strong/fragile assumption in ML, since arbitrarily close probability measures may lack a dominating ν-density.  
- Estimating a ratio of two densities is therefore problematic from a machine-learning standpoint.  
- The authors do not specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), creating ambiguity.  
- Unclear whether results handle ν-measure-zero sets rigorously.  
- Missing explicit assumptions: domain of x (R^d or general topological space) and whether p is strictly positive for all x.
