- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimation methods: (Type I) importance-sampling-based L^2 + RKHS regularization (Type I Fredholm) and (Type II) local-kernel regularization with locality parameter → 0 (Type II Fredholm), supported by convergence theory and experiments.  
- Major strength: the L^2 formulation enables effective cross-validation for parameter selection, addressing a gap in unsupervised parameter tuning and differentiating the method from KMM.  
- KMM’s RKHS-derived objective is kernel-dependent and thus poorly suited for cross-kernel CV-based kernel selection.  
- Theorem 1’s convergence rate, containing the (-t/log λ) factor, appears slow for fixed t; stronger spectral or regularity assumptions (à la Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) might yield polynomial rates.  
- The common assumption that q/p lies in a well-behaved function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and deserves explicit discussion, since it implies p/q may behave poorly and requires prior knowledge of tails/regularity.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typos and errors that hinder verification; specific corrections include Lemma 5’s W2^2 → Ws^2, the correct bound D1 (t/-log λ)^2 + λ^{1-α} D2 ||f||_2^2, incorrect expressions on lines 675 and 686 (Eq. 31 is correct), and the integral domain on line 1147 should be ||ξ||^2 ≥ — recommend thorough revision.  
- Overall takeaway: a novel, CV-friendly approach to density-ratio estimation with solid theoretical and empirical support.
