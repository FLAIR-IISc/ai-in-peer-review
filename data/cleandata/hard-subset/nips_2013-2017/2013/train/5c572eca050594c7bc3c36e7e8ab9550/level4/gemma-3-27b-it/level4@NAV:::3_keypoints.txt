- Method frames density-ratio estimation via a kernel-based approach that yields a Fredholm integral equation and an inverse problem.  
- Fundamental identifiability concern: nearby probability measures may lack densities, undermining the density-ratio formulation.  
- Primary omission: the dominating measure ν is not specified (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), creating ambiguity.  
- Sets of zero ν-measure and related issues are not addressed, calling the rigor of results into question.  
- Key assumptions are unstated—e.g., the domain of x (ℝ^d or general topological space) and whether p is strictly positive everywhere.
