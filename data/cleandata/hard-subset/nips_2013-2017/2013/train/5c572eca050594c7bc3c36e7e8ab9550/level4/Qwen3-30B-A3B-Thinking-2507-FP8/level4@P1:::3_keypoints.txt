- Proposes a novel formulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solved via regularization (FIRE) using RKHS with theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Well-motivated by applications in covariate shift adaptation, transfer learning, and importance sampling.
- Establishes a connection between density ratio estimation and classical inverse problems, offering a fresh perspective.
- Develops a flexible family of algorithms applicable to both Type I (samples from both distributions) and Type II (q known at sample points) settings.
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis for the Gaussian kernel on Euclidean spaces and sub-manifolds, including concentration bounds and convergence rates.
- Introduces CD-CV, a fully unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection.
- Demonstrates strong technical quality with rigorous derivations and clear distinction of Type I vs Type II bounds.
- Empirical results show FIRE outperforms KMM and LSIF on regression and classification under covariate shift.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and effectively relates to prior work.
- Significant practical impact due to principled, theoretically grounded, and computationally efficient framework.
- Concerns: paper should explicitly state the dominating measure, domain assumptions, and positivity requirement for p.
- Clarification: the draft concern about assuming densities is a misunderstanding—standard common-measure density assumptions are appropriate and addressed by the authors.
- Overall assessment: strong paper meeting quality, clarity, originality, and significance criteria; recommend acceptance.
