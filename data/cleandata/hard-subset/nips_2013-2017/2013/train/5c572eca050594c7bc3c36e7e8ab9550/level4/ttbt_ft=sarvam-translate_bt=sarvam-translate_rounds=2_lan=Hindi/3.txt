The authors propose a solution to the problem of how to estimate the ratio of two probability density functions. This approach is based on using a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which leads to a counterintuitive problem. From a machine learning perspective, the concept of density is very difficult because it is well known that the probability measure P with density \nu has probability measures \nu-distinct in any epsilon neighborhood. Therefore, estimating the ratio of two densities is problematic from an ML perspective. As far as I can see, the authors do not specify which effective measure \nu was used (Lebusque measure, count measure, something else), and it is not clear to me whether their results are rigorous due to the zero set of \nu-measures. I also don't recall what assumptions were made. For example, is x related to $R^d$ or to some general topological space? Is the density p positive for all x?
