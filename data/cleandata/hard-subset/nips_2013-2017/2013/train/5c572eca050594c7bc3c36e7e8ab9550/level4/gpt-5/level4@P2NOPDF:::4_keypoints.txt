- Proposes two estimators of the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q using (i) E_p[k(x,·) f(·)] = E_q[k(x,·)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,·) f(·)] ≈ q(x) via KDE limits.  
- Replace expectations with empirical averages and minimize discrepancy over test functions {k(x,·)}, enabling standard kernel optimization machinery.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness of p and q, with rates that degrade with ambient dimension.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed, timely contribution with solid theory and convincing experiments.  
- Request intuition for why optimizing over {k(x,·)} suffices to recover the ratio—does this rely on the kernel being characteristic/universal so k(x,·)-moments are dense in L2,p?  
- Ask for motivation of the objective: paper frames optimization in L2,p but mixes L2,p and L2,q later—why this combination and how each loss maps to downstream tasks (e.g., covariate shift, off-policy evaluation)?  
- Clarify importance-sampling implications: when to estimate p/q vs q/p, why not use directly available expectations (E_q) when possible, and state clearly the sampling regimes (difficulty sampling under E_q) where each estimator is advantageous.  
- Reconcile regularization with assumptions: you regularize for smoothness but assume p and q are smooth—clarify when p,q smooth ⇒ p/q smooth (e.g., q bounded away from zero) and the reverse.  
- Discuss dimensional dependence concretely: for what d and sample sizes is the method practical, and provide guidance (theoretical/empirical) for bandwidth and regularization choices as d grows.  
- Motivate the test-function family U = {k(x,·)} beyond computation (e.g., identification via characteristic kernels, RKHS density in L2, control of IPMs) and note scenarios where other test classes might be preferable.  
- Situate method in broader paradigms: compare to alternatives like Stein operators, other integral transforms, and “Smooth Operators,” and explain why the kernel test-function route is preferable or one of several viable options.  
- Suggested improvements: add intuition for estimator (i), clearer motivation connecting objectives to applications, and a discussion of dimensional scaling and test-function design to strengthen the paper.
