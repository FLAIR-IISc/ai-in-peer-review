- Summary: Proposes two RKHS-based kernel estimators for the density ratio f = p/q, with tractable empirical implementations and convergence guarantees under smoothness/boundedness assumptions; experiments support claims.

- Strength: Elegant RKHS formulation yielding practical density-ratio estimators.

- Strength: Solid finite-sample theoretical analysis showing dependence on smoothness and ambient dimension.

- Strength: Methodologically unifies moment-matching and regression-style approaches via kernels.

- Strength: Empirical results substantiate the method.

- Main question 1 (intuition/identifiability): Provide an intuitive explanation for estimator (i) showing how rich RKHS test functions (e.g., characteristic kernels) identify f in L2(p).

- Main question 2 (norm/objective choice): Motivate why optimization mixes L2(p) and L2(q), and give guidance on which objective to use for downstream tasks (importance-weighted ERM, off-policy evaluation) and whether bounds imply task-specific guarantees.

- Main question 3 (applications/value): Clarify intended direction (estimating p/q vs q/p), when estimating f is preferable to directly estimating expectations, and highlight reusable-use cases (multiple functionals, covariate shift, domain adaptation, off-policy evaluation).

- Main question 4 (regularization vs density assumptions): Explain how smoothness of p and q implies smoothness of f, list minimal sufficient conditions, and describe failure modes (spiky f when q is small) and how regularization mitigates them.

- Main question 5 (dimensional dependence): Discuss practical dimensional regimes where the method is effective and how additional structure (manifolds, additive models, product kernels) can reduce the curse of dimensionality; provide sensitivity analysis versus d and bandwidth.

- Main question 6 (test functions and kernel choice): Justify U = {k(x,·)} via identifiability (characteristic kernels), RKHS approximation properties, and stability; give practical kernel/bandwidth selection guidance (median heuristic, CV).

- Main question 7 (relation to prior work): Sharpen contrasts with kernel mean matching, KLIEP, uLSIF, and “Smooth Operators,” specifying novel guarantees, identifiability conditions, or improved rates relative to prior art.

- Main question 8 (practicalities): Clarify sample-splitting/cross-fitting recommendations to avoid overfitting, how regularization parameters are chosen, and computational complexity scaling with sample size and bandwidth.
