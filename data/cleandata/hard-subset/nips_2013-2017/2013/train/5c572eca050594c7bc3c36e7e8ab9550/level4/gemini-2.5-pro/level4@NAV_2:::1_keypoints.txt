- Reframes density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and derives Type I/II formulations solved via Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS (FIRE algorithms).  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for unsupervised parameter selection and provides theoretical convergence rates plus empirical comparisons to KMM and LSIF.  
- Strength — Originality: connecting density-ratio estimation to classical inverse/operator theory is novel and significant.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is well-written and the derivation from integral equations to kernel algorithms is easy to follow.  
- Strength — Theoretical quality: provides concentration bounds and convergence rates for Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Strength — Practical utility: CD-CV addresses parameter selection and experiments show strong performance versus baselines.  
- Weakness — Technical detail gaps: some claims lack proofs or precise citations (e.g., kernel approximation ∫k_t f = f + O(t)); Tikhonov regularization merits a brief explanation; dataset references are missing.  
- Weakness — Needs contextual comparison to standard approaches (e.g., plug-in KDE ratio) and discussion of bandwidth role, especially when q is uniform/Type II.  
- Weakness — Theoretical rates are not compared to minimax optimal rates; broader theoretical context is missing.  
- Weakness — Empirical evaluation would benefit from a simple illustrative toy example (e.g., 1D plot of true vs estimated ratio) to build intuition.  
- For acceptance: novel, principled, theoretically sound framework with practical model selection and promising empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: evaluation and contextualization gaps, missing illustrative experiments, and minor technical/experimental clarifications required.  
- Overall: high-quality and significant contribution; presentation and evaluation issues appear addressable in revision.
