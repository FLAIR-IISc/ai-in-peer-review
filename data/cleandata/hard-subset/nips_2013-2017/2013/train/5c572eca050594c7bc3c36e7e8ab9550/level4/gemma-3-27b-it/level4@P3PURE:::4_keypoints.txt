- Paper derives two estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q from two equivalent relations and replaces integral operators with empirical counterparts.  
- Choosing {k(x,·)} as test functions enables using standard kernel methods for optimization.  
- Convergence guarantees are provided under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates that deteriorate with ambient dimension (curse of dimensionality).  
- Request for a concise, intuitive explanation of why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence for relationship (i) as it does for (ii).  
- Ask for more motivation for using the L2,p norm in the optimization and the mixed L2,p / L2,q objective in equation (9), and linking cost choice to applications.  
- Questioning the importance-sampling use case: why estimate both E_p and E_q to get q/p instead of directly using an E_q estimate, and whether E_p(q/p) could improve over E_q.  
- Note potential mismatch between regularizing the ratio’s smoothness and assuming smoothness of p and q; requests clarification of how smoothness of p and q relates to smoothness of q/p.  
- Request discussion of practical dependence on dimension d—specifically for which d the method remains useful.  
- Ask for stronger justification for the chosen family of test functions U.  
- Broader question whether the {k(x,·)} approach is a generally optimal strategy compared to other test-function expectation methods (noting similarity to prior work [1]).  
- Overall judgment: the paper is timely, strong, provides convergence analysis and experiments, but several clarifications and motivations are needed.
