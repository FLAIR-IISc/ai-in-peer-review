1. Proposes a novel density-ratio estimation method by reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation.  
2. Strength: paper is well-written and addresses an important problem.  
3. Some technical claims (e.g., ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t)) lack proofs or citations and need justification.  
4. When q=1 the method yields density estimation without requiring kernel bandwidth → 0; paper should clarify the different role of bandwidths versus KDE.  
5. Include a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.  
6. All proofs are in the supplementary material and were not checked in this review.  
7. Discuss why direct density-ratio estimation is preferable to a naive plug-in approach (estimate p and q separately).  
8. Unclear how the provided convergence bounds relate to existing minimax results (e.g., [12]); comparative performance vs other methods is not established.  
9. Provide citations for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
10. Add a simple toy experiment with known p and q (e.g., 1D) showing true versus estimated p/q to demonstrate estimation quality.  
11. Overall: promising paper but needs clearer comparative evaluation and illustrative toy demonstrations.
