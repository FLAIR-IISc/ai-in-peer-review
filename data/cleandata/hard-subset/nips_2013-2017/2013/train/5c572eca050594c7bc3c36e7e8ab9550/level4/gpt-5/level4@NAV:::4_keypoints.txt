- Paper derives two estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q from identities (i) E_p[k(x,·) f] = E_q[k(x,·)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,·) f] ≈ q(x), uses empirical integral operators and kernel test functions, and gives convergence guarantees under smoothness/boundedness; rates depend on ambient dimension.  
- Ask for an intuitive explanation why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence for identity (i), similar to the bandwidth intuition for (ii).  
- Request motivation for mixing L2,p and L2,q in the objective (eq.9) and guidance on how objective choices should be driven by downstream tasks/applications.  
- Question application motivation: if both E_p and E_q are empirically estimated, why not use empirical E_q directly (e.g., for importance sampling); is the method intended mainly for cases where sampling from q is difficult?  
- Regularization concern: authors regularize smoothness of q/p while the theorem assumes smoothness of p and q—ask for clarification of the relationship between smoothness of p,q and smoothness of their ratio.  
- Ask for discussion of dimension dependence: for which ranges of ambient dimension d is the method practically useful?  
- Request a principled rationale for the choice of test-function family U.  
- Broader question: is using {k(x,·)} the “right” generic strategy for covariate-shift/ratio estimation via test functions, and how does this approach relate to prior work such as Grunewalder et al. (“Smooth Operators”)?  
- Overall verdict: timely and strong paper with solid convergence analysis and empirical results.
