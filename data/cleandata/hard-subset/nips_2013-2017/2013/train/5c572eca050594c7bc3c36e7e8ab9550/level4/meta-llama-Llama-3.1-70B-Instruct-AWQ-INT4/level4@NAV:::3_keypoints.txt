1. Proposes estimating a density ratio using a kernel and Fredholm integral, framing the task as an inverse problem.

2. Method relies on existence of densities, which is problematic since measures arbitrarily close to a given measure may lack a dominating density, making density-ratio estimation ill-posed.

3. The manuscript does not specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue or counting), weakening the theoretical foundations.

4. Failure to address sets of ν-measure zero raises concerns about the rigor and applicability of results.

5. Assumptions about the domain of x (R^d versus a general topological space) are not stated.

6. The paper does not clarify whether the density p is positive everywhere, which is critical for defining the ratio.
