- Introduces two novel density-ratio estimators: an L^2-regularized RKHS formulation (Type I Fredholm) and a locality-kernel-based formulation requiring the locality parameter → 0 (Type II Fredholm).  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and experimental validation supporting the methods.  
- Major advantage: L^2 formulation enables principled cross-validation for parameter selection, addressing a gap in unsupervised tuning for density-ratio estimation.  
- Notes that RKHS-objective-based methods (e.g., KMM) are ill-suited for CV-driven kernel selection because objective values depend on the kernel, complicating comparisons.  
- The convergence rate in Theorem 1 contains a (-t / log λ) factor that seems slow for fixed t; kernel literature often reports polynomial rates after fixing t and choosing λ.  
- Suggests exploring stronger, reasonable assumptions (e.g., on the spectrum of K_{p,t} or on q/p) to potentially obtain polynomial convergence rates, citing Caponnetto & De Vito (2007) as precedent.  
- Recommends adding discussion of rates under strengthened assumptions and fixed t to strengthen the theoretical contribution.  
- Advises explicitly acknowledging the strong standard assumption that q/p lies in a well-behaved function class (e.g., Sobolev), since it implies prior knowledge about tails and makes p/q potentially ill-behaved.  
- Calls for a discussion of the inherent limitations of density-ratio estimation stemming from such assumptions.  
- Flags substantial issues in the supplementary material: numerous typos and errors that impede verification of theory and require thorough revision.
