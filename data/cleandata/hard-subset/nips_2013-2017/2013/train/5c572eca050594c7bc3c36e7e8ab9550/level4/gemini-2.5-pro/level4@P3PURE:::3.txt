The authors propose a method for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions by formulating the problem as an inverse problem based on a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation.

A central concern is the paper's foundational assumption that the underlying probability measures possess densities. From a machine learning perspective, this is a strong assumption, as it is well-known that in any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P that has a density with respect to a measure \nu, there exist probability measures that do not have a \nu-density. This theoretical fragility makes the direct estimation of a density ratio a potentially problematic endeavor.

Furthermore, the paper's technical exposition lacks rigor in several key areas. The authors do not specify the dominating measure \nu (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure) with respect to which the densities are defined. This omission makes it unclear how the results handle sets of \nu-measure zero, casting doubt on their rigor.

Finally, the paper would benefit from a clear statement of its underlying assumptions. For instance, it is not specified whether the domain of x is $\mathbb{R}^d$ or a more general topological space. Additionally, it is unclear if the density p is assumed to be strictly positive for all x. Clarifying these foundational assumptions is essential for evaluating the scope and validity of the proposed method.
