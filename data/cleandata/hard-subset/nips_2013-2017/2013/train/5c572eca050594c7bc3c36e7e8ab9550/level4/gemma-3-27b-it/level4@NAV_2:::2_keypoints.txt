- Introduces a novel formulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral inverse problem.  
- Proposes two FIRE algorithms using RKHS regularization for the reformulated problem.  
- Develops a fully unsupervised model selection method, Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV).  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel.  
- Demonstrates encouraging empirical performance competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Connects the approach to prior work (KMM, LSIF) and classical operator theory.  
- Highlights the L2 formulation advantage: enables cross-validation for kernel selection in unsupervised settings.  
- Notes RKHS-based objectives are not directly comparable across kernels, limiting naive CV for kernel choice.  
- Critiques Theorem 1’s relatively slow convergence rates (dependence on -t/log λ) and suggests exploring extra assumptions for polynomial rates.  
- Recommends leveraging existing kernel-method results (e.g., Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) to potentially improve rates via operator spectrum or smoothness assumptions.  
- Flags strong assumptions required on q/p (smoothness, boundedness) and the negative impact these can have on p/q; requests more discussion and mitigation strategies.  
- Reports supplementary material contains numerous typos and errors that hinder verification (e.g., Lemma 5: W2^2 → Ws^2; incorrect bounds at lines 675 and 686; unclear integral domain at line 1147).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, practical unsupervised model selection (CD-CV), solid theoretical guarantees, competitive experiments, and clear literature connections.  
- Arguments against acceptance: slow convergence rates in Theorem 1, substantial errors in supplementary material, and limited discussion of strong density-ratio assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on correcting the supplementary material and investigating avenues to achieve faster convergence rates.
