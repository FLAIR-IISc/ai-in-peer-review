- Main contribution: Reformulates density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation and derives Tikhonov-regularized RKHS estimators (FIRE) with closed-form kernel solutions.  
- Setting distinction: Defines Type I (samples from p and q) and Type II (samples from p plus evaluations of q) estimation scenarios.  
- Theoretical strength: Provides rigorous concentration bounds and convergence rates for FIRE using Gaussian kernels on R^d and on manifolds.  
- Practical strength: FIRE yields simple, implementable closed-form solutions via kernel matrices.  
- Model selection: Proposes an unsupervised Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for tuning kernel width and regularization.  
- Relation to prior work: Connects to and positions FIRE relative to KMM and LSIF, claiming improved stability and natural out-of-sample extension.  
- Writing and organization: Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and logically structured.  
- Weakness — implicit assumptions: Several foundational assumptions (e.g., dominating measure) are not stated explicitly and need clarification.  
- Weakness — support and near-zero p: Paper lacks discussion of support conditions for q relative to p and implications/behavior when p(x) is near zero.  
- Weakness — density existence assumption: Assumes P and Q have densities; this strong measure-theoretic assumption should be acknowledged and its limitations noted.  
- Question — CD-CV sensitivity: Need discussion of how CD-CV performance depends on the choice and number of test functions U.  
- Question — theoretical rates context: Suggests comparing provided convergence rates to known minimax optimal rates for density-ratio estimation.  
- Question — computational cost: Requests explicit comparison of FIRE’s computational complexity versus LSIF and KMM, as empirical efficiency claims are not quantified.
