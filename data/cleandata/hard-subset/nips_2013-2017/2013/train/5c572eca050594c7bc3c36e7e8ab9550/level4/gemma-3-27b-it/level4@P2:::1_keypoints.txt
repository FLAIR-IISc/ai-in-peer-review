- Reformulates density ratio estimation q(x)/p(x) as a Fredholm integral equation, introducing the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and methodological presentation.  
- Connects to classical integral equation theory to provide a principled algorithmic and analytic framework.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates (for Gaussian kernels).  
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised parameter-selection method, which is practically valuable.  
- Missing justification/citation for the kernel approximation ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t); needs proof or reference.  
- Paper should include a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.  
- Clarify and expand on the claim that FIRE avoids KDE bandwidths converging to zero; distinguish the role of FIRE’s 't' from KDE bandwidth.  
- Relationship of Corollaries 2 and 4 to known minimax optimality results (e.g., [24]) is unclear; compare to theoretical lower bounds.  
- Experimental section should include simple 1D toy examples with known p and q for visual and intuitive illustration.  
- Broaden empirical comparisons to more alternatives (e.g., [12]) to better contextualize performance.  
- Provide references for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Add a short justification explaining why naive plug-in density estimation is inferior to the proposed method.  
- Strengths supporting acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, algorithmic simplicity/practicality, and unsupervised model selection.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: missing details/citations, limited comparative analysis, and lack of simple illustrative examples.  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper; accept with minor revisions addressing the points above.
