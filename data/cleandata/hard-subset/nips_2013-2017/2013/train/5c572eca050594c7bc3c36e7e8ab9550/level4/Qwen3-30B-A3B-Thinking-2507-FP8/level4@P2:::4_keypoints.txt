- Reformulates density ratio estimation (q/p) as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind.
- Proposes FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator), a family of RKHS-regularized algorithms.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and d-dimensional submanifolds.
- Introduces CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation) for unsupervised model selection.
- Connects density ratio estimation to classical operator theory, enabling spectral analysis techniques.
- Establishes clear theoretical relationships to KMM and LSIF while highlighting key optimization and property differences.
- Paper is technically sound with comprehensive, reproducible theoretical analysis.
- Experiments show FIRE outperforms KMM and LSIF in regression and classification, supporting theoretical claims.
- Originality is strong: reframes density ratio estimation as an inverse problem tied to classical analysis.
- Significance is substantial for importance sampling, covariate shift adaptation, and transfer learning; practical utility demonstrated.
- Strength: novel theoretical framework linking to Fredholm equations.
- Strength: detailed convergence rates across multiple settings.
- Strength: practical algorithmic performance and the new CD-CV selection method.
- Weakness: motivation for choosing specific cost functions (L2,p, L2,q, combinations) could be clearer in practical terms.
- Weakness: relationship between smoothness assumptions on p, q and their ratio needs more discussion.
- Weakness: practical implications of dimensionality dependence in convergence rates should be expanded.
- Weakness: some applications could be more explicitly tied to the theoretical results.
- Suggestion: provide a more explicit discussion of trade-offs between Type I and Type II settings; note Type II’s usefulness when q is known but hard to sample (e.g., MCMC).
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, clear, original, and significant.
