The paper proposes a novel method for density ratio estimation, p/q. While several approaches to this problem already exist, the key contribution of this work is a reformulation of the density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation. 

The paper possesses several strengths. It is well-written and engaging, and the density ratio estimation problem is both important and interesting.

However, there are several areas that could be improved. 

(i) The paper lacks detail in certain areas. For instance, the statement \int k_t(x,y)f(x)dx=f(y)+ O(t) requires justification. Providing either a proof (within the paper or supplementary material) or a relevant reference would strengthen this claim, even if the proof appears straightforward.

(ii) As a special case where q=1, the method effectively performs density estimation. It is noteworthy that this approach does not require the bandwidth to converge to zero, a limitation of standard kernel density estimation which is inconsistent in such cases. A more in-depth discussion of this distinction would be beneficial, allowing readers to clearly understand the differing roles of the bandwidth parameters in this method versus those used in KDE.

(iii) I recommend including a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization, as it may not be familiar to a significant portion of the NIPS audience.

(iv) The proofs are relegated to the supplementary material. While acceptable, I have not had the opportunity to verify their correctness.

(v) It would be valuable to include a few sentences explaining why a naive plug-in approach – separately estimating densities p and q – is inferior to the proposed method.

(vi) The paper’s performance relative to other existing methods is unclear. For example, [12] also derives minimax bounds. While Corollaries 2 and 4 present upper bounds on the convergence rate, it remains unclear how these bounds compare to the optimal minimax rate.

(vii) Please provide references for the datasets used in the experiments (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).

(viii) I would appreciate seeing a demonstration of the proposed method’s performance on simple toy problems with fixed and known p and q. For example, a plot comparing the true p/q and the estimated p/q for 1-dimensional distributions could help readers assess the quality of the estimation. 

Overall, this is a promising paper on density ratio estimation. However, clarifying its performance compared to existing algorithms (e.g., [12]) and demonstrating its effectiveness on illustrative toy problems (e.g., with simple 1D p and q densities) would significantly strengthen the work.
