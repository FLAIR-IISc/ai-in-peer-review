- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimation methods framed as regularized Fredholm equations (Type I: importance sampling + L^2 deviation; Type II: limiting kernel parameter).  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and experimental validation of method performance.  
- Key advantage: L^2 formulation enables practical cross-validation for parameter selection, addressing a major gap in unsupervised tuning.  
- Contrasts with KMM/RKHS objectives, which are kernel-dependent and less suited to cross-validated kernel selection.  
- Theorem 1’s convergence rate (with factor (-t / log λ)) seems relatively slow for fixed t; many kernel analyses obtain polynomial rates under different assumptions.  
- Suggests exploring stronger spectral/operator assumptions (e.g., as in Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) to potentially achieve polynomial convergence rates and examining assumptions on q/p.  
- Urges explicit acknowledgment that assuming q/p lies in a well-behaved function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and requires prior knowledge about density tails.  
- Warns that assuming smoothness of q/p can imply ill-behaved behavior for p/q and this trade-off should be discussed.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typos and errors that hinder verification of proofs (specific issues: Lemma 5: W2^2→Ws^2 and corrected bound form; incorrect expressions at lines 675/686; integral domain clarification at line 1147; Eq.(31) appears correct).  
- Overall assessment: promising approach with significant benefit for cross-validation, but theoretical-rate improvements and careful revision of supplementary proofs are needed.
