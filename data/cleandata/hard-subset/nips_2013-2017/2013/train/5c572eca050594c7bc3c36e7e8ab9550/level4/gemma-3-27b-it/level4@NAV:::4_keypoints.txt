- Presents two estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q using equalities between expectations and replaces integral operators with empirical counterparts using test functions k(x,.).
- Selection of test functions {k(x,.)} enables use of standard kernel methods for optimization.
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates depending on the space dimensionality.
- Clarify why the convergence-rate argument (and bandwidth dependence) that explains estimator (ii) also applies intuitively to estimator (i).
- Motivate the transition from an L2,p norm to the combined L2,p and L2,q cost in equation (9), and relate this choice to performance in key applications.
- For the importance-sampling application, justify why both E_p and E_q are estimated rather than relying on a direct estimate of E_q or importance-weighting.
- Discuss the relationship between smoothness assumptions on p and q and the smoothness enforced on their ratio q/p by the regularizer.
- Provide explicit discussion of how convergence rates scale with dimension d and specify the dimensional regimes where the method is effective.
- Give further justification for the chosen test-function family U and its suitability for the problem.
- Consider whether the test-function approach {k(x,.)} is optimal for covariate-shift settings and compare more directly to related work such as “Smooth Operators.”
- Overall assessment: timely, strong paper with thorough convergence analysis and experiments, but needs clarifications on the points above.
