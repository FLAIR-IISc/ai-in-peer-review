This paper introduces a novel method for estimating the density ratio p/q by reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation. While several methods for this problem already exist, this reformulation constitutes the primary contribution of the work.

**Strong Points:**
The paper is well-written and presents its ideas with clarity. The problem of density ratio estimation is both important and of significant interest to the community.

**Weak Points, Comments, and Questions:**
(i) The manuscript omits certain derivations. For instance, statements like \int k_t(x,y)f(x)dx=f(y)+ O(t) require justification. Such claims, even if straightforward, should be accompanied by either a proof (in the main text or supplement) or a proper citation.

(ii) In the special case where q=1, the proposed method can be applied to density estimation. An interesting aspect of this application is that the bandwidth parameter is not required to converge to zero for consistency, unlike standard kernel density estimation (KDE). The paper would be strengthened by a more detailed discussion of this point, clarifying for the reader how the role of the bandwidth parameter in this framework differs from its role in traditional KDE.

(iii) I recommend including a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization. This would improve the paper's accessibility, as this concept may not be familiar to the entire target audience.

(iv) All proofs have been deferred to the supplementary material and were not checked during this review.

(v) The paper would benefit from a discussion explaining why the proposed direct estimation approach is advantageous compared to a naive plug-in baseline, which would involve estimating densities p and q separately before computing their ratio.

(vi) The performance of the proposed method relative to existing approaches is not sufficiently clear. The convergence rates presented in Corollaries 2 and 4 provide upper bounds, but their tightness is not discussed in the context of established minimax optimal rates, such as those derived in [12].

(vii) Please provide citations for the datasets used in the experiments (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).

(viii) A demonstration on a simple, illustrative "toy" problem is missing. For example, using 1-dimensional densities for p and q, a plot comparing the true density ratio with the estimated ratio would provide readers with a more intuitive assessment of the estimator's quality.

In summary, this is a well-presented paper on an important problem. However, its primary weaknesses are the insufficient comparison against the performance and theoretical guarantees of state-of-the-art methods like [12], and the absence of simple, illustrative experiments to build intuition for the proposed approach.
