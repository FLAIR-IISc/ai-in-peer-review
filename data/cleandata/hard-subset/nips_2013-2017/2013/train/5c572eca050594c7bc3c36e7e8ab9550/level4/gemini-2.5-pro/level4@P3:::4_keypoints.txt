- Addresses density-ratio estimation q(x)/p(x) by reformulating it as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind.  
- Derives two settings: Type I (samples from p and q using E_p[k(·) f]=E_q[k(·)]) and Type II (known q, samples from p using local kernel approximation).  
- Solves the ill-posed inverse problems with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding the FIRE family of kernel-based estimators with closed-form solutions.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for both Euclidean and manifold data.  
- Proposes Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), an unsupervised method for hyperparameter/model selection.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance against KMM and LSIF on regression and classification under covariate shift.  
- Elegant, novel formulation linking modern ML problem to classical operator theory.  
- Flexible, practical algorithms that are simple to implement via representer theorem and kernel matrix operations.  
- Thorough theoretical coverage including manifold settings, adding technical depth.  
- CD-CV is a valuable practical contribution for unsupervised model selection.  
- Experiments are well-designed and show strong empirical validation.  
- Needs clearer intuition for convergence in the Type I setting and why matching kernel expectations ensures f=q/p.  
- Should clarify motivation for choosing among loss functions (L2,p vs. combinations) and link choices to applications.  
- Discuss relationship between smoothness assumptions on p, q and smoothness of the ratio q/p (and conditions like p bounded away from zero).  
- Address practical implications of dimensionality on convergence rates and state where method is expected to perform well.  
- Provide more justification and sensitivity analysis for CD-CV test function families (random linear functions, half-space indicators).  
- Overall: technically strong, well-written, and practically relevant paper; minor clarifications recommended; strong accept recommendation.
