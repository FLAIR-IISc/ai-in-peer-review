- Reformulates density-ratio estimation r(x)=q(x)/p(x) as a Fredholm integral equation and solves it as an inverse problem with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS (FIRE).  
- Considers two settings: Type I (samples from p and q) and Type II (q known/evaluable at p-samples).  
- Derives closed-form kernel solutions via representer theorems and provides natural out-of-sample evaluation.  
- Proposes an unsupervised model-selection scheme (CD-CV) based on cross-density validation.  
- Gives concentration and convergence-rate results, including for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and smooth manifolds.  
- Positions the work relative to KMM and LSIF/KLIEP, linking density-ratio estimation to operator-theoretic inverse problems and RKHS regularization.  
- Clear, well-written manuscript presenting an elegant inverse-problem perspective that unifies algorithm and analysis.  
- Produces simple closed-form kernel algorithms with theoretical guarantees and practical unsupervised model selection.  
- Missing precise technical details/citations for kernel approximation statements (e.g., ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y)+O(t)); needs conditions/references or proofs.  
- Unclear treatment of special case q≡1 (density estimation); role and necessity of kernel bandwidth vs. regularization needs clarification and discussion versus KDE.  
- Suggests adding a brief primer on Tikhonov–Phillips regularization to improve accessibility for non-specialist readers.  
- Key proofs are only in the supplement; recommended to move main lemmas or proof sketches into the paper.  
- Lacks discussion/comparison to plug-in estimators (e.g., KDE-based q̂/p̂), including variance amplification and bandwidth sensitivity; empirical comparisons requested.  
- Convergence-rate bounds (Corollaries 2 and 4) need context versus minimax lower bounds and discussion of dependence on dimension and smoothness.  
- Missing dataset citations/statistics for reproducibility (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Recommends adding 1D or low-dimensional toy demonstrations plotting true r(x) vs. FIRE estimates to aid intuition and sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall: promising and thoughtful contribution with novel formulation and useful algorithms, but would be strengthened by tighter technical justification, comparisons, and illustrative examples.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, principled inverse-problem formulation, clean RKHS algorithms, theoretical bounds, and practical unsupervised model selection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: some technical claims lack precise conditions/citations, insufficient discussion of rate optimality, weaker empirical baselines and toy illustrations, and heavy reliance on supplementary proofs.
