- Paper introduces two novel density-ratio estimators: Type I (importance-sampling equation as L^2 regularization with RKHS; Fredholm Type I) and Type II (kernel with shrinking locality parameter; Fredholm Type II).  
- Type I uses RKHS regularization and an L^2 loss derived from the importance-sampling equation.  
- Type II requires a kernel locality parameter that must approach zero for consistency and is formulated as a regularization problem.  
- Theoretical convergence results are provided, and experiments demonstrate practical performance.  
- Major advantage: L^2 formulation allows use of cross-validation (CV) for kernel/parameter selection in an unsupervised setting.  
- Methods are similar to existing work (e.g., KMM) but differ because RKHS objective values in KMM are not comparable across kernels, preventing CV-based kernel selection.  
- Theorem 1 gives a slow convergence rate involving a (-t / log λ) factor; reviewer suggests exploring stronger assumptions to obtain polynomial rates.  
- Recommend investigating spectral assumptions (e.g., Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) or stronger smoothness on q/p to achieve polynomial convergence with fixed t.  
- Note limitation: assuming q/p lies in a nice function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and may be unrealistic; under such assumptions p/q can behave poorly.  
- Supplementary material has numerous typos and errors that hinder verification of theoretical results; specific issues noted (e.g., Lemma 5 notation/bound errors, incorrect expressions at lines 675/686, integral domain at line 1147) and should be revised.
