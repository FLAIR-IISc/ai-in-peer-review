- Paper proposes two novel density-ratio estimators: a Type I (L² regularized Fredholm equation) and a Type II (requires vanishing kernel locality) with theoretical guarantees and experiments.  
- Major strength: enables effective cross-validation (CV) for parameter selection via L² formulation, unlike RKHS-based methods (e.g., KMM).  
- Suggestion: add a brief remark explaining why L² objectives allow meaningful CV comparisons across kernels while RKHS objectives do not.  
- Theorem 1 convergence rate contains a suboptimal factor (-t/ log λ) yielding slow rates under fixed t.  
- Request: discuss whether stronger assumptions (spectral conditions on K_{p,t} or additional smoothness of q/p) can yield polynomial-in-sample-size convergence rates.  
- Concern: reliance on assumption q/p ∈ a smooth class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and asymmetric, often implying poor behavior of p/q and limiting applicability.  
- Request: clarify the practical implications and asymmetry of the q/p smoothness assumption.  
- Supplementary material has multiple typographical and technical errors that hinder verification and reproducibility.  
- Specific corrections requested: Lemma 5 should use W_s^2 not W_2^2 and the bound should be D1 (t / -log λ)^2 + λ^{1-α} D2 ||f||_2^2.  
- Specific corrections requested: expressions at lines 675 and 686 are incorrect (Equation (31) is correct).  
- Specific corrections requested: in Lemma 5 proof the integral domain at line 1147 must be “||ξ||^2 ≥ …”.  
- Overall verdict: contribution (enabling CV via L² regularization) is compelling and experiments are adequate, but major revision required to address rate discussion, assumption limitations, and fix supplementary errors.
