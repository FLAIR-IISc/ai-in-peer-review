- Proposes two density-ratio estimators: Type-I (importance-sampling identity, L^2 residual with RKHS regularization) and Type-II (local kernel with shrinking locality parameter, regularized formulation).  
- Provides convergence results and empirical demonstrations.  
- Key advantage: L^2 formulation permits principled cross-validation for tuning parameters in unsupervised density-ratio estimation.  
- Notes that RKHS-mismatch objectives (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for CV-based kernel selection because objective values are not comparable across kernels.  
- Theorem 1 yields slow rates for fixed t due to the (-t / log λ) factor; questions whether polynomial sample-rate bounds are attainable under additional assumptions.  
- Suggests discussing conditions (e.g., spectral/source regularity like Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) under which polynomial rates with fixed t could be derived.  
- Raises a broader limitation: assuming q/p lies in a regular function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong, implies knowledge about tails, and the inverse ratio p/q may behave poorly.  
- Supplementary material needs significant revision for typos and errors that impede verification (specific errors in Lemma 5, lines 675/686, integral domain at line 1147, etc.).  
- Overall: promising method with the notable strength that cross-validation can be used effectively for parameter selection.
