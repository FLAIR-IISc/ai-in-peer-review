- Proposes two equalities to estimate the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q: (i) Ep[k(x, ·) f] = Eq[k(x, ·)] and (ii) Ep[k(x, ·) f] ≈ q(x), and replaces integral operators with empirical counterparts.  
- Uses the family {k(x, ·)} as test functions so standard kernel methods can optimize the discrepancy between both sides.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions on p and q.  
- Rates of convergence depend critically on the ambient dimension, as expected for density estimation.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed, valuable contribution with solid analysis and informative experiments.  
- Request for intuition: explain why convergence reasoning that applies to (ii) (bandwidth dependence) also carries over to (i).  
- Clarify objective choice: (page 2) optimization is over L2,p but equation (9) mixes L2,p and L2,q — motivate this cost and tie choices to downstream tasks.  
- Application/motivation question: for importance sampling (needing q/p), why estimate Ep and Eq to build the loss instead of directly using Eq; clarify when Ep(q/p) helps and whether difficulty sampling from Eq is the primary use-case.  
- Regularization concern: authors enforce smoothness of ratio q/p but theorems assume smooth p and q — discuss principled relationships between smoothness of p,q and their ratio and implications.  
- Ask for explicit discussion of dependence on dimension d: for what ranges of d is the method practical?  
- Question the test-function family U: provide motivation for selecting {k(x, ·)} and whether it is strongly justified.  
- Broader question: is {k(x, ·)} the “right” generic probe for expectation-operator approaches (covariate shift, q/p estimation)? Note similarity to prior work (Smooth Operators) and position this method relative to that literature.
