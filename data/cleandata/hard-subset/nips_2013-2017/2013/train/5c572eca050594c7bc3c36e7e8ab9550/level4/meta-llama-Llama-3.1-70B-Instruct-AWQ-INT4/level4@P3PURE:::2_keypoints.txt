- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimation methods: a Type I Fredholm formulation (importance-sampling equation + RKHS regularization) and a Type II Fredholm formulation (locality-kernel with vanishing bandwidth), with theoretical convergence and experiments.  
- Main advantage: L^2 formulation enables use of cross-validation (CV) for parameter selection in unsupervised density-ratio estimation.  
- Notes that RKHS objective methods (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for kernel selection via CV because objective values depend on the kernel and are not comparable.  
- Theorem 1 gives a slow convergence rate due to a (-t / log λ) factor; asks whether stronger assumptions could yield polynomial rates.  
- Suggests exploring polynomial convergence rates under additional assumptions (e.g., spectral assumptions like Caponnetto & De Vito 2007 or smoothness of q/p).  
- Warns that assuming q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and may be unrealistic; also notes p/q can behave poorly under such assumptions.  
- Requests a discussion of this limitation of density-ratio estimation in the paper.  
- Calls for substantial revision of the supplementary material due to typos and errors that hinder verification of theoretical results (gives specific examples in Lemma 5 and other lines).  
- Concludes the paper’s strong contribution is enabling effective CV for parameter choice in density-ratio estimation.
