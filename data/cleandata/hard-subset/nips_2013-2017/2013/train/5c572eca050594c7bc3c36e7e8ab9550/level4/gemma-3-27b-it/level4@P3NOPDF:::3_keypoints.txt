- Paper proposes a novel kernel-based approach to density-ratio estimation via a Fredholm integral equation (inverse problem).
- Fundamental issue: density estimation is ill-posed since measures arbitrarily close to one with a density may lack a density; authors do not address this.
- The dominating measure ν is never specified (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), yet this choice critically affects validity and guarantees.
- The manuscript fails to analyze sensitivity to sets of ν-measure zero, which can undermine theoretical claims.
- Core assumptions are unspecified: the domain of x (R^d or general topological space) is not stated.
- Unclear whether the target density p is assumed strictly positive or allowed to have zero-density regions.
- The Fredholm formulation feels disconnected from practice without a clear measure-space setup and kernel specifications.
- Lack of detail on kernel properties (positive definiteness, smoothness) and their impact on solutions.
- Overall conclusion: idea is interesting but the missing clarifications on assumptions, ν, and zero-measure issues raise serious theoretical and practical concerns, preventing endorsement.
