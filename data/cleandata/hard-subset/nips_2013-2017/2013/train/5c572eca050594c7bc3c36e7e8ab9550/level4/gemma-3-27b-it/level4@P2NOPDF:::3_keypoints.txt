- Paper tackles density-ratio estimation via kernel methods by casting it as a Fredholm integral equation (an inverse problem).
- Estimating probability densities (and thus their ratio) is fundamentally hard: near any measure with a density there exist measures without densities.
- The dominating measure ν is never specified (e.g., Lebesgue, counting, or other), which is a major omission.
- Lack of a specified ν raises rigor concerns, particularly about the role of ν-measure-zero sets.
- Key assumptions are missing: the domain of x (R^d or a more general topological space) is not defined.
- It is not stated whether the density p is assumed to be strictly positive everywhere.
- These ambiguities prevent a full assessment of validity and impact; clarifying them would strengthen theoretical foundations and clarity.
