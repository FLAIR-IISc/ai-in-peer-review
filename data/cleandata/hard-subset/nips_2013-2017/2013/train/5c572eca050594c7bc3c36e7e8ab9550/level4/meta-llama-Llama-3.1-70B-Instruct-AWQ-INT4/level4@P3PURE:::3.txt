The authors propose a solution to the problem of estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. The approach is based on using a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which yields an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, the assumption of densities is a challenging one, as it is well-known that in any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P with a \nu-density, there exist probability measures without \nu-densities. Consequently, estimating the ratio of two densities is problematic from a machine learning viewpoint.

Upon reviewing the manuscript, I noticed that the authors do not specify the dominating measure \nu (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or another measure). This omission raises concerns about the rigor of their results, particularly with regards to sets of \nu-measure zero. Furthermore, I found it unclear which assumptions are made about the underlying space and the densities involved. For instance, it is not explicitly stated whether the variable x belongs to $R^d$ or a more general topological space. Additionally, the authors do not clarify whether the density p is positive for all x. Providing clear statements about these assumptions would be essential to ensure the validity and applicability of the proposed method.
