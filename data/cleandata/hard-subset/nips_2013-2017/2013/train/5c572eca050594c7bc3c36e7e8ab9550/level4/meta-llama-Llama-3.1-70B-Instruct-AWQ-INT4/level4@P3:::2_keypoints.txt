- Introduces a novel density-ratio estimation approach based on the Fredholm equation of the first kind.
- Proposes two methods (Type I and Type II) that cast density-ratio estimation as regularization problems.
- Type I uses the importance-sampling equation; Type II uses a kernel with a locality parameter that →0 for consistency.
- Both methods are formulated in an L2 regularization framework.
- L2 formulation enables cross-validation for parameter selection, an advantage over RKHS-based methods like KMM.
- Provides theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates (including for the Gaussian kernel).
- Theorem 1’s convergence rate is slow with fixed t; stronger assumptions (e.g., on operator spectrum or q/p) might yield polynomial rates.
- Assumes q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev); this common assumption has limitations and may require knowledge of tails, while p/q can behave poorly.
- Supplementary materials contain typos and minor mistakes that hinder verification of theoretical results and need revision.
- Strengths: novel Fredholm-based approach, L2/CV advantage, and solid theoretical analysis.
- Weaknesses: slow convergence rate under current assumptions, limiting assumptions on q/p, and imperfect supplementary materials.
- Scores: originality 8/10, quality 8/10, clarity 7/10, significance 8/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations, explore stronger assumptions for improved rates, and fix the supplementary materials.
