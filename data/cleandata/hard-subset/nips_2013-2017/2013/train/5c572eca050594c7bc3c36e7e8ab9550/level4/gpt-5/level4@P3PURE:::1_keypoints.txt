- Paper proposes a new density-ratio estimation method by reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Strong: paper is clearly written and engaging.  
- Strong: density-ratio estimation is an important and timely problem.  
- Missing technical detail: need justification/proof or citation for claims like ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t).  
- Clarify bandwidth role: discuss why, in the q=1 case, this method can estimate densities without bandwidth→0 while KDE is inconsistent in that regime.  
- Explain Tikhonov regularization briefly for readers unfamiliar with it.  
- Proofs are relegated to supplementary material and have not been verified by the reviewer.  
- Add discussion of why naive plug-in (estimate p and q separately, then ratio) is inferior to the proposed method.  
- Comparative performance unclear: relate Corollaries 2 and 4 to known minimax bounds (e.g., [12]) and discuss optimality.  
- Provide references for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Include a simple toy demonstration (e.g., 1D p and q with plots of true p/q vs estimate) to visually assess estimation quality.  
- Overall: interesting and promising formulation, but needs stronger technical justifications, comparisons to existing methods, and illustrative experiments to be convincing.
