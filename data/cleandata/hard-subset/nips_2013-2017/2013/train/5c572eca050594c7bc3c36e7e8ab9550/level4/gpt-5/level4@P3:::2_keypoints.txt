- Proposes estimating density ratios by casting the problem as a Fredholm integral equation and solving with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS.
- Introduces two settings: Type I (use identity Ep[r k] = Eq[k] to get regularized L2(p) estimates and variants) and Type II (use δ-kernel approximation K_t,p r ≈ q for cases where q is observed at p-samples).
- Derives explicit closed-form FIRE estimators via the representer theorem with natural out-of-sample extension.
- Provides theoretical analysis: finite-sample concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on R^d and smooth manifolds.
- Proposes Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for unsupervised selection of kernel and regularization parameters using importance-sampling identities.
- Empirically compares FIRE to LSIF, KMM, and a thresholded inverse KDE baseline, including covariate shift classification experiments.
- Strength: technically sound formulation leveraging operator theory and RKHS regularization with stable, explicit algorithms.
- Strength: original and unifying Fredholm inverse-problem perspective and practical unsupervised model selection (CD-CV).
- Strength: significant applicability to covariate shift, domain adaptation, and importance sampling; manifold analysis is a plus.
- Strength: main ideas are clearly conveyed and algorithmic forms are explicit; discussion contrasts with KMM/LSIF.
- Weakness: convergence rates depend intricately on t and λ; paper lacks explicit source/spectral assumptions to obtain polynomial rates for fixed t.
- Suggestion: state source/spectral conditions on K_{p,t} and smoothness of r to derive polynomial rates and discuss minimax status versus prior work.
- Weakness: strong boundedness and Sobolev/smoothness assumptions on r may be restrictive and implicitly enforce tail conditions.
- Suggestion: discuss practical diagnostics for estimability (overlap/tails), behavior under covariate-shift violations, and safeguards like clipping or localized regularization.
- Weakness: Type II introduces O(t)-bias; needs guidance on joint selection of t and λ and sensitivity analysis in experiments.
- Weakness: supplementary material contains typos and errors that hinder verification; proofs and statements need thorough correction and alignment with the main text.
- Pro: conceptually clean inverse-problem framing, closed-form stable algorithms, practical CD-CV, and solid theory for Gaussian kernels/manifolds.
- Con: need stronger rate results under standard spectral assumptions, limited practical guidance for strong smoothness assumptions, and incomplete supplementary material.
- Recommendation: generally favorable toward acceptance conditional on fixing the supplement, clarifying rate assumptions, and adding discussions/experiments on kernel/t sensitivity and KMM comparisons.
- Minor requests: report computational complexity and memory costs for FIRE variants, unify notation early, and show robustness to clipping or other variance-control measures.
