- Paper proposes a new density-ratio estimation method reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation.
- Novelty claimed despite existing approaches; main contribution is the Fredholm formulation.
- Strength: paper is well written and engaging.
- Strength: density-ratio estimation is an important and interesting problem.
- Missing technical justification: provide proof or reference for ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t).
- Clarify role of bandwidth: unlike KDE, fixed bandwidth here may not require shrinkage—expand discussion and contrast with KDE inconsistency.
- Add a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.
- All proofs are in the supplementary material and have not been verified by the reviewer.
- Explain why naïve plug-in (estimate p and q separately, then ratio) performs worse than the proposed method.
- Comparative performance vs. existing methods is unclear; position results relative to known minimax/optimal rates (e.g., relation to [12]).
- Corollaries give upper bounds; clarify how close these are to optimal minimax rates and cite related work.
- Provide references for datasets used (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).
- Include simple toy experiments with known p and q (e.g., 1-D plots of true vs estimated p/q) to demonstrate estimation quality.
- Overall: promising paper but needs clearer comparisons to other algorithms and a toy example to strengthen presentation.
