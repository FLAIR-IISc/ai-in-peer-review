- Proposes reframing density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem using kernels and a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Provides a principled framework (FIRE) leveraging operator theory and regularization to construct algorithms.  
- Strong theoretical analysis with concentration bounds and convergence rates, especially for Gaussian kernels.  
- Analysis covers densities on R^d and smooth d-dimensional submanifolds.  
- Introduces an unsupervised model selection method (CD-CV).  
- Experimental results show promising performance compared to KMM and LSIF.  
- Assumption that both distributions have densities may be restrictive; probability measures may not admit densities w.r.t. a given dominating measure.  
- Paper does not explicitly state the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue or counting), raising rigor concerns about measure-zero sets.  
- Underlying domain assumptions are unclear (Is x ∈ R^d or a general topological space? Is p strictly positive?).  
- Initial motivation linking the approach to importance sampling and covariate shift could be more clearly articulated.  
- Related-work discussion is comprehensive but could better focus on specific connections to the proposed method.  
- Theoretical results rely heavily on specific kernel choices (notably Gaussian), limiting generality.  
- Needs a more detailed discussion of method limitations and directions for future work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, practical algorithm with good experiments, and useful CD-CV model selection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive density assumptions, unspecified dominating measure, kernel-specific theory, and weaker motivation linkage to importance sampling/covariate shift.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution worth acceptance with minor revisions to clarify assumptions and limitations.
