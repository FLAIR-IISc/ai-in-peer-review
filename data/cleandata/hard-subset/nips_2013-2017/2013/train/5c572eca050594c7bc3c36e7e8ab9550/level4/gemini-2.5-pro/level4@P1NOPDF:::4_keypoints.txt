- Proposes two kernel-based estimators for the density ratio f = p/q derived from two moment-matching equalities and implemented by replacing expectations with empirical averages.  
- Uses an RKHS family of test functions {k(x,·)} to enable efficient optimization via standard kernel methods.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions on p and q, with rates that deteriorate with input dimensionality.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed, relevant contribution with theoretical analysis and empirical validation.  
- Requests intuitive explanation for why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence for estimator (i), analogous to the kernel-bandwidth intuition for estimator (ii).  
- Asks for motivation of the chosen cost function: optimization is described in L2(p) but Equation (9) mixes L2(p) and L2(q); wants connection to downstream tasks and why these norms are appropriate.  
- Questions the importance-sampling application: if samples from p are available to train the estimator, why not estimate E_p[h] directly rather than estimate f and reweight q-samples; asks for clarification when direct sampling is impractical.  
- Requests discussion of the relation between the method’s smoothness regularizer on the ratio and the theoretical smoothness assumptions on p and q, including whether ratio smoothness implies existence of smooth p,q pairs.  
- Wants practical guidance on the dimensionality dependence: for what ranges of d is the method expected to be useful in practice.  
- Asks for deeper motivation for choosing the RKHS test function class U beyond computational convenience—why it is suitable compared to other function classes.  
- Suggests situating the approach within broader literature (e.g., Grünewald et al.’s “smooth operators”) and commenting on whether {k(x,·)} is a natural or primarily convenient test-function choice.
