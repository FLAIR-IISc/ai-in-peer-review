- Main contribution: novel reformulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Strong point: paper is well-written and addresses an important problem.  
- (i) Several technical statements lack formal justification; provide proofs or citations (e.g., ∫k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t)).  
- (ii) For q=1 the method yields density estimation without requiring bandwidth→0; clarify how bandwidth’s role differs from KDE.  
- (iii) Recommend adding a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for broader accessibility.  
- (iv) All proofs are in the supplementary material; reviewer did not verify their correctness.  
- (v) Discuss why direct estimation is preferable to a plug-in approach and the plug-in method’s drawbacks.  
- (vi) Comparison to existing methods is insufficient; unclear how provided convergence rates compare to minimax/other state-of-the-art (e.g., [12]).  
- (vii) Provide citations for experimental datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- (viii) Include a simple toy 1D experiment with known p and q (plot true vs estimated ratio) to illustrate performance.  
- Summary: solid paper but needs clearer comparisons to state-of-the-art and a simple illustrative example.
