- Proposes two estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q using equalities Ep[k(x,·)f] = Eq[k(x,·)] and Ep[k(x,·)f] ≈ q(x), optimized over kernel test functions.  
- Replaces integral operators with empirical versions and minimizes the discrepancy over a set of test functions using standard kernel methods.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates depending on ambient dimension as expected for density estimation.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed, timely contribution with convergence analysis and experiments.  
- Request for an intuitive argument why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence to the ratio, especially for equality (i).  
- Ask to motivate the choice of cost function (combining L2,p and L2,q) and connect it to important applications.  
- Ask to clarify motivation for estimating q/p when both Ep and Eq are empirically estimated, and discuss relevance to applications like importance sampling.  
- Suggest exploring how smoothness of p and q relates to smoothness of the ratio and how dimensionality affects convergence rates.  
- Recommend justifying the choice and generality of the test-function family U and comparing to alternative methods (e.g., [1]).  
- Recommend addressing whether the {k(x,·)} approach is the most principled/suitable method for RN-derivative estimation under covariate shift.
