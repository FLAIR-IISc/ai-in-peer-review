- Proposes a kernel-based method that frames density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation / inverse problem.  
- Critiques the strong assumption that underlying distributions have densities, noting nearby measures may lack densities and thus ratio estimation is problematic.  
- Authors fail to specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), which is essential for density definitions.  
- Raises doubt about mathematical rigor due to potential effects of sets of ν-measure zero.  
- Notes absence of clear assumptions about the domain of x (R^d vs. a general topological space).  
- Questions whether the density p is assumed strictly positive everywhere, which is not stated.
