- Introduces two novel kernel-based density-ratio estimators: Type I (importance-sampling reframed as L^2 regularization / Fredholm I) and Type II (local kernel with vanishing locality parameter / Fredholm II), with theoretical guarantees and experiments.  
- Major contribution: L^2 formulation allows use of cross-validation for hyperparameter selection in unsupervised density-ratio estimation.  
- Authors should explicitly note that conventional RKHS objectives (e.g., KMM) depend on the kernel, rendering objective values incomparable across kernels and unsuitable for CV.  
- Theorem 1 shows slow convergence due to the (-t / log λ) factor for fixed t; reviewer questions whether polynomial sample-rate convergence can be achieved under additional assumptions.  
- Suggest adopting Caponnetto & De Vito (2007)-style spectral assumptions on the K_{p,t} operator or stronger regularity assumptions on q/p to obtain polynomial (optimal) rates.  
- Paper should discuss the strong modeling assumption that q/p lies in a well-behaved function class (e.g., Sobolev) and note the practical limitation that p/q may be ill-behaved.  
- Experimental results demonstrate practical performance, but their significance depends on the correctness of the convergence proofs.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typographical and mathematical errors (e.g., Lemma 5 notation/bounds, incorrect expressions on lines 675/686, integration-domain error at line 1147) and must be thoroughly revised for verifiability.  
- Overall: method is a valuable advance because it enables CV in density-ratio estimation, but theoretical exposition, supplemental corrections, and discussion of stronger-assumption rates are needed.
