- Introduces two novel density-ratio estimation methods formulated as regularization problems from Fredholm equations of the first and second kind.  
- First method: uses importance sampling, an L² norm for deviation, and RKHS regularization.  
- Second method: employs a kernel with a locality parameter tending to zero for consistency, also cast as a regularization problem.  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and empirical demonstrations.  
- Key strength: L² formulation enables cross-validation for parameter selection, filling a gap in unsupervised density-ratio estimation.  
- Correctly notes that RKHS objectives like KMM are less CV-friendly because their values depend on the kernel, complicating kernel comparisons.  
- Concern: Theorem 1 convergence rate has a slow dependence on t due to the (-t/log λ) factor.  
- Suggestion: Under stronger spectral or regularity assumptions (e.g., following Caponnetto & De Vito 2007), polynomial sample-rate bounds might be achievable and should be investigated.  
- Practical limitation: Assuming q/p lies in a well-behaved function class (e.g., Sobolev) is a strong prior and problematic, since p/q may behave poorly; this limitation should be discussed.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typos/errors that impede verification; specific corrections in Lemma 5, lines 675/686, and proof line 1147 are indicated.  
- Recommendation: Thoroughly revise the supplementary material to ensure the accuracy and clarity of the core convergence results.  
- Overall: A valuable approach (notably for enabling CV); addressing convergence-rate issues, assumption realism, and fixing the supplement would substantially strengthen the paper.
