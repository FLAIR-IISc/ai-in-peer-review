- Presents a motivated kernel-based approach to estimate the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q via kernel integral equations and empirical operator replacements.  
- Derives two estimators from equalities involving expectations of kernel functions and leverages RKHS for optimization.  
- Provides rigorous convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates depending on ambient dimension.  
- Empirical validation demonstrates practical utility and computational feasibility.  
- Strength: theoretical analysis is rigorous and aligns with established density estimation principles.  
- Strength: using test-function minimization is a principled alternative to ad-hoc methods.  
- Improvement 1: Provide a concise intuition/justification for convergence of estimator (i) linking the equality to the target ratio.  
- Improvement 2: Clarify rationale for using L^2_p norm (and the mix in Eq. (9)) and relate norm choice to downstream tasks like variance reduction.  
- Improvement 3: Explicitly motivate importance sampling application by stating when direct sampling from q is infeasible, justifying the indirect ratio estimation.  
- Improvement 4: Discuss the relationship between smoothness assumptions on p, q and smoothness of f, and whether assumptions are necessary or sufficient.  
- Improvement 5: State practical dimensionality limits (e.g., for which d the method is effective) given the dimension-dependent rates.  
- Improvement 6: Justify choice of test-function family {k(x,·)} and why RKHS functions are appropriate or near-optimal.

- Overall: Strong, timely contribution with clear writing; accept with minor revisions to address the above points.
