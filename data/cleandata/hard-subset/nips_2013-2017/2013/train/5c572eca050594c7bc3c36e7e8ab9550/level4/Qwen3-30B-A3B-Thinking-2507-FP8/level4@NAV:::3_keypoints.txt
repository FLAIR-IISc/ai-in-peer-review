- Method: kernel-based estimator that casts density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem via a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Fundamental concern: assuming existence of densities is fragile—any ε-neighborhood of a measure with a ν-density can contain measures without a ν-density, so density-ratio estimation is inherently problematic.  
- Ambiguity: the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting) is not specified.  
- Rigor issue: potential problems involving sets of ν-measure zero make it unclear whether the results are mathematically sound.  
- Missing domain specification: it is not stated whether x lies in R^d or a more general topological space.  
- Positivity assumption unclear: the paper does not state whether the density p is strictly positive for all x.  
- Overall: foundational assumptions are not clearly articulated, undermining clarity and validity of the approach.
