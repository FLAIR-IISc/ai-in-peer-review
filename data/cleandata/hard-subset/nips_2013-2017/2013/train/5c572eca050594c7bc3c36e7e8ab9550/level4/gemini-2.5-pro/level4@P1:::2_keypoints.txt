- Proposes two density-ratio estimators: Type I (importance-sampling identity + Tikhonov regularization with RKHS penalty) and Type II (delta-kernel with locality parameter → 0, in same regularization framework).  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and empirical evidence of practical effectiveness.  
- Main strength: L2 objective enables principled cross-validation for model selection, addressed via the proposed Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) scheme.  
- CD-CV offers a practical solution to an otherwise difficult unsupervised parameter tuning problem.  
- Needs clearer comparison to KMM: RKHS-norm objectives depend on the kernel and are not directly comparable across kernels, complicating CV-based model selection.  
- Concern about convergence rates: Theorem 1’s rate is slow for fixed kernel width t (due to the (-t/log λ) factor); discussion of possible polynomial rates under stronger assumptions is missing.  
- Suggestion to consider stronger assumptions (spectral decay of K_{p,t}, smoothness of q/p) to potentially obtain faster, optimal rates (e.g., as in Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).  
- Assumptions that q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) are strong and imply specific tail behavior; paper should discuss practical implications and asymmetry (q/p well-behaved does not imply p/q is).  
- Supplementary material contains typographical and substantive errors (e.g., Lemma 5 notation/bounds, incorrect expressions on specific lines, integration domain issue) and must be thoroughly revised for verification.  
- Overall: promising and well-motivated work with valuable practical contributions, but theoretical rates, assumption discussion, and supplemental correctness need improvement.
