- Proposes two novel density-ratio estimation methods: Type I (importance-sampling reformulated as L^2+RKHS regularization) and Type II (kernel with vanishing locality parameter, also regularized).  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and experimental validation of practical performance.  
- Shares similarities with existing methods like KMM but differs by using an L^2 formulation.  
- Key strength: L^2 objective enables cross-validation for principled hyperparameter selection in unsupervised density-ratio estimation.  
- Recommend adding a remark that RKHS-based objectives (e.g., KMM) are unsuitable for CV because their values depend on the kernel and are not directly comparable.  
- Critique of Theorem 1: convergence rate with factor (−t / log λ) is slow for fixed t; under stronger assumptions (e.g., spectral conditions on K_{p,t} or smoother q/p) polynomial rates may be achievable and should be discussed.  
- Notes that assuming q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and problematic (it implies poor behavior of p/q); the paper should acknowledge this limitation.  
- Supplementary material needs careful revision: multiple typos and errors (e.g., Lemma 5: W_2^2 → W_s^2 and corrected bound; incorrect expressions at lines 675/686; integral domain issue in Lemma 5 proof) that impede verification of theoretical claims.  
- Overall core contribution: enabling effective parameter selection via cross-validation for density-ratio estimation.
