This paper presents a novel approach to density ratio estimation, a problem for which several methods already exist. The key innovation lies in reformulating the estimation as a Fredholm integral equation.

The paper is well-written and engaging; I found it a pleasant read. Density ratio estimation is a valuable and interesting research area. However, several points could be addressed to strengthen the contribution.

First, the paper occasionally lacks sufficient detail. For instance, the claim that \int k_t(x,y)f(x)dx=f(y)+ O(t) requires justification. A proof – either within the paper or the supplementary material – or a relevant citation would be beneficial for all such statements, even those seemingly straightforward.

Second, the method naturally extends to density estimation when q=1. This is a potentially significant advantage, as the approach avoids the consistency issues associated with standard kernel density estimation (KDE) where bandwidth must converge to zero. Expanding the discussion to highlight this distinction and clearly articulate the differing roles of the bandwidth parameter in this work compared to KDE would be valuable for readers.

Third, given the potential unfamiliarity within the NIPS community, a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization would enhance accessibility. A few sentences outlining its purpose and common uses would be helpful.

Fourth, while the proofs are relegated to the supplementary material, their correctness remains unverified by me. 

Fifth, a discussion regarding the limitations of a naive plug-in density estimation approach (separate estimation of p and q) compared to the proposed method would strengthen the motivation. Why is directly estimating densities and then taking the ratio less effective?

Sixth, a more thorough comparison to existing methods is needed. The paper provides convergence rate bounds (Corollaries 2 and 4), but it’s unclear how these bounds relate to known minimax rates, such as those derived in [12]. Understanding the gap between achieved and optimal rates is crucial for assessing the method's efficiency.

Seventh, please provide references for the datasets utilized in the experiments: CPUsmall, Kin8nm, and USPS. This will aid reproducibility and allow readers to understand the characteristics of the data.

Eighth, the inclusion of illustrative examples using simple, known probability distributions (p and q) would greatly aid understanding. For example, in one dimension, a plot comparing the true density ratio p/q with the estimated ratio would provide a concrete demonstration of the method’s performance and allow for visual assessment of its accuracy.

In summary, this is a promising paper addressing an important problem. However, clarifying its performance relative to competing techniques (e.g., [12]) and demonstrating its efficacy on basic test cases would substantially improve its impact.
