- Proposes two density-ratio estimators: a Type I RKHS L^2 least-squares (Fredholm) method from importance-sampling identity, and a Type II localized-kernel regularized approach requiring kernel bandwidth → 0 for consistency.  
- Provides convergence results and empirical evaluations demonstrating the methods.  
- Key advantage: the L^2 formulation naturally enables cross-validation for selecting algorithmic parameters in unsupervised density-ratio estimation.  
- Notes relation to existing methods like KMM but argues RKHS objective values (e.g., KMM) are not comparable across kernels, making them ill-suited for CV—this should be made explicit.  
- Critiques Theorem 1: current rate is slowed by the (-t / log λ) factor for fixed t; asks whether polynomial sample-rate bounds can be obtained under additional/spectral assumptions (e.g., Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).  
- Recommends discussing stronger assumptions (on operator or q/p) that would yield polynomial convergence rates for fixed t.  
- Points out the strong and potentially unrealistic assumption that q/p lies in a regular function class (e.g., Sobolev), and that this implies prior knowledge and may cause p/q to behave poorly; asks for a discussion of this limitation.  
- Requests substantial proofreading and correction of the supplementary material (numerous typos and incorrect expressions that hinder verification), with specific cited errors in Lemma 5, lines 675/686, and line 1147.  
- Overall summary: novel approach with the important practical strength that CV can be effectively used for parameter selection in density-ratio estimation.
