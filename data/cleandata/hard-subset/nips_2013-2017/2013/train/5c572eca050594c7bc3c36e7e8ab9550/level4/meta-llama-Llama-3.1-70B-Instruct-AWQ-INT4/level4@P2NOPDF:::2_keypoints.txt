- Introduces two novel density-ratio estimation methods: Type I (importance-sampling equation as L^2+RKHS regularization) and Type II (kernel with locality parameter → 0, also as regularization).

- Provides theoretical convergence results and experimental validation of practical performance.

- L^2 formulation allows effective use of cross-validation (CV) for parameter selection, addressing a gap in unsupervised methods.

- Methods are similar to existing approaches (e.g., KMM) but have the advantage that CV is applicable, unlike RKHS objective values which are kernel-dependent and not comparable.

- Theorem 1 yields slow convergence for fixed t due to the (-t / log λ) factor, unlike typical polynomial rates in kernel methods.

- Recommends exploring stronger assumptions (e.g., spectrum conditions à la Caponnetto & De Vito 2007 or smoothness of q/p) to potentially obtain polynomial rates with fixed t.

- Notes that assuming q/p lies in a nice function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and problematic, since it requires knowledge of tails and may imply p/q behaves badly; this limitation should be discussed.

- The Type II method requires the kernel locality parameter to shrink to zero for consistency, a notable practical constraint.

- Supplementary material contains numerous typos and errors (e.g., Lemma 5 notation and bounds, incorrect expressions at specific lines, integral domain mistake) that hinder verification of theoretical claims.

- Because theoretical convergence is a primary contribution, the authors should thoroughly revise and correct the supplementary material.

- Overall assessment: a novel and useful approach with a strong advantage in enabling CV for parameter tuning, but theoretical-rate clarity and supplementary corrections are needed.
