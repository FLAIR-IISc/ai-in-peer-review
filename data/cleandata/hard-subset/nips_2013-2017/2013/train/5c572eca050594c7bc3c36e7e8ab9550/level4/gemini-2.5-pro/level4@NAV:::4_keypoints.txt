- Proposes two kernel-based estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q derived from equalities (i) E_p[k(x,·)f] = E_q[k(x,·)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,·)f] ≈ q(x).  
- Estimation via empirical integral operators and minimizing discrepancy over a family of test functions {k(x,·)} enables standard kernel optimization techniques.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under standard smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates that depend critically on ambient dimensionality.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed, valuable, and timely contribution with supporting experiments.  
- Request: add an intuitive explanation why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence in setting (i), not just (ii) where bandwidth intuition helps.  
- Request: clarify motivation for the chosen cost function—why frame optimization in L2,p but use a combined L2,p and L2,q objective in Eq. (9), and link to downstream application needs.  
- Question: for importance sampling (estimating q/p), explain the advantage of this indirect method given it requires empirical E_q; clarify scenarios (e.g., sampling from q difficult) where the method is preferable.  
- Theoretical note: reconcile regularizer-imposed smoothness on the ratio f with the convergence theorems’ smoothness assumptions on p and q; discuss when smooth p and q imply a smooth ratio.  
- Practical note: discuss implications of the dimensionality dependence—state the dimensional regimes where the method is expected to be useful.  
- Request: give more detailed motivation for the specific choice of the test function family U.  
- Broader context: position the test-function/expectation-operator approach relative to other high-level density-ratio strategies (e.g., transformation-based methods) and argue whether the kernel test-function approach has unique advantages.
