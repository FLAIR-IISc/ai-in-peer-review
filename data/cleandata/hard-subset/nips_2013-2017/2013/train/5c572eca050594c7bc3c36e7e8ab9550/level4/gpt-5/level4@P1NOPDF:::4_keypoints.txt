- Proposes two kernel-based estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q using identities (i) E_p[k(x,·) f] = E_q[k(x,·)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,·) f] ≈ q(x), replacing integral operators with empirical ones.  
- Uses the family of kernel test functions {k(x,·)} to frame optimization and enable standard RKHS methods.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions; rates depend critically on ambient dimension.  
- Reviewer judges the work well-executed, timely, and supported by solid analysis and experiments.  
- Request for added intuition: explain why optimizing over {k(x,·)} suffices for convergence in case (i), not just formal proofs.  
- Question about objectives: clarify the mixed use of L2,p and L2,q in (9) and motivate choice of cost relative to downstream tasks.  
- Application/motivation concern: explain why estimating p/q is preferable when E_p and E_q are already estimated, and clarify scenarios (e.g., hard-to-sample q) motivating reweighting.  
- Regularization/smoothness question: clarify the principled relation between smoothness assumptions on p, q and smoothness/regularization of the ratio f, and which implications are required.  
- Dimensional dependence: request more concrete discussion of how rates scale with dimension and for what d (relative to sample size and smoothness) the method is practical.  
- Choice of test functions: ask for principled motivation for U = {k(x,·)} beyond computational convenience and when alternative families would be better.  
- Broader perspective: suggest discussing connections to related work (e.g., Smooth Operators) and whether the RKHS/test-function strategy is the “right” general approach.
