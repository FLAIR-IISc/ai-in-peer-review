- Paper derives two estimators for the Radon-Nikodym derivative f = p/q using kernel test functions and empirical integral operators.  
- Using {k(x,·)} as test functions lets the authors leverage standard kernel-method optimisation.  
- Convergence guarantees are provided under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates that worsen with ambient dimension.  
- Reviewer finds the work well-executed and timely with solid experiments.  
- Request for an intuitive explanation why optimisation over {k(x,·)} yields convergence for equality (i) as it does for (ii).  
- Ask for motivation of the cost function mixing L2,p and L2,q (eq. (9)) and links to target applications that justify this choice.  
- Question why one should estimate Ep and Eq separately instead of using the empirical Eq directly for importance-sampling-style applications; clarify sampling difficulty motivation.  
- Ask about the relationship between smoothness of p and q and smoothness of the ratio q/p, and implications for the regulariser.  
- Request discussion of practical dependence on dimension d—i.e., for what dimensions the method is useful.  
- Ask for justification or motivation for the chosen family of test functions U.  
- Pose a broader question whether using {k(x,·)} is the “right” generic approach for constructing cost functions under covariate shift, noting similarity to prior work (Smooth Operators).
