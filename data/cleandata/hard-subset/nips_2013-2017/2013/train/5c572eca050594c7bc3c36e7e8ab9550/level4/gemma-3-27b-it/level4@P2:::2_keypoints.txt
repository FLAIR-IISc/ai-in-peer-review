- Introduces a novel formulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral inverse problem.  
- Proposes two FIRE algorithms in an RKHS with regularization: Type I (direct ratio estimation) and Type II (localized kernel, avoids explicit target density).  
- Provides theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels.  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), a practical unsupervised model selection method.  
- Core strength: principled RKHS-based formulation enabling effective cross-validation for unsupervised tuning.  
- Insight: RKHS-norm objectives (e.g., KMM) are less suitable for cross-validation due to kernel dependence.  
- Main critique: convergence rates in Theorem 1 appear slow (involving a (-t/log λ) factor).  
- Suggestion: exploring stronger assumptions (smoothness of q/p or kernel spectral properties) might yield polynomial sample-rate improvements (cf. Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).  
- Important unaddressed assumption: requiring q/p to lie in a well-behaved function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and may be unrealistic; p/q can behave poorly.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typos and errors that hinder theoretical verification and need careful revision.  
- Specific reported errors: Lemma 5 notation and bound mistakes, wrong expressions at lines 675/686, incorrect inequality domain at line 1147 (and other typos).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, principled RKHS approach, effective CD-CV method, theoretical results, and competitive empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially slow convergence rates, lack of discussion of strong smoothness assumptions, and flawed supplementary material.  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on fixing supplementary material errors and, if possible, investigating stronger assumptions to improve convergence rates.
