- Introduces two density-ratio estimators: an RKHS-regularized least-squares (Fredholm equation of the first kind) and a localized-kernel variant (Fredholm equation of the second kind).  
- Provides convergence guarantees and empirical evaluations.  
- L2 formulation gives a natural, comparable risk across kernels, enabling practical cross-validation for model and kernel selection.  
- Should explicitly state that RKHS-norm-based objectives (e.g., KMM) are not suitable for kernel selection via CV because their values depend on the kernel and are not comparable.  
- Theorem 1 yields a slow factor t/(-log λ) for fixed t; request discussion of additional assumptions (e.g., spectral conditions like Caponnetto & De Vito or source/smoothness conditions on q/p) that could yield polynomial rates.  
- Assumptions on q/p need more emphasis: requiring q/p in a regular class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong, imposes tail/structure knowledge, and the reverse ratio p/q may be ill-behaved—discuss when these assumptions are plausible.  
- Supplementary material has many typos and errors (specific corrections cited); it needs careful revision for accuracy and clarity because the convergence theory is central.  
- Overall: promising, with the key strength being L2-risk-driven CV; recommending discussion of sharper rates under stronger assumptions and a thoroughly corrected supplement.
