

The authors propose a solution for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions using a kernel-based approach that formulates a Fredholm integral equation, thereby posing an inverse problem. From a machine learning perspective, the assumption of density existence represents a very strong requirement. It is well-known that any ε-neighborhood of a probability measure P possessing a ν-density contains probability measures lacking ν-densities. Consequently, density ratio estimation presents significant challenges under this ML framework. Crucially, the authors fail to specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue, counting, or another measure), which undermines the rigor of their results due to potential ambiguities arising from sets of ν-measure zero. Furthermore, the review lacks a clear statement of fundamental assumptions. For instance, it remains unspecified whether the domain of the random variable x is ℝᵈ or a more general topological space, and the positivity of the density p(x) for all x is not established. These omissions prevent a rigorous assessment of the theoretical guarantees and practical applicability of the proposed method.
