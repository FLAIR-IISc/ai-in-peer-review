- The assumption that both distributions admit densities w.r.t. a fixed dominating measure is strong and problematic because nearby probability measures may not be absolutely continuous.  
- The paper should discuss how the method behaves under small perturbations and provide robustness guarantees when densities are absent or only approximately defined.  
- The dominating measure ν is not specified (e.g., Lebesgue, counting, etc.), leaving density statements and invariance to ν unclear.  
- Handling of ν-measure-zero sets and any arguments that rely on them is not clarified, undermining rigor.  
- There is no consolidated statement of basic assumptions: domain of x (R^d, manifold, general space), positivity/bounds of p and q, and required regularity/smoothness/support conditions.  
- The required properties of the kernel (which kernels are essential versus optional, e.g., Gaussian or δ-family behavior) are not clearly specified.
