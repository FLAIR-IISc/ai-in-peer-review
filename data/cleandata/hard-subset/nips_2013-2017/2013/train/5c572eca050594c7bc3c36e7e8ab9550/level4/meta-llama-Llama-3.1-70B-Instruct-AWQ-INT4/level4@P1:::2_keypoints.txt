- Proposes two density-ratio estimators: Type I (importance-sampling equation as L^2 regularization in RKHS) and Type II (kernel with locality parameter t→0, also regularized).
- L^2 formulation allows application of cross-validation for parameter selection, addressing a gap in unsupervised parameter tuning.
- RKHS objective used in KMM is inappropriate for kernel selection via CV because objective values depend on the kernel and are not comparable.
- Theorem 1 gives convergence but with a slow rate for fixed t due to a (-t / log λ) factor.
- A polynomial convergence rate might be obtainable under stronger spectral/operator assumptions (e.g., Caponnetto & De Vito) or stronger assumptions on q/p.
- Assuming q/p lies in a smooth class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong, requires knowledge of tails, and can make the reciprocal p/q behave poorly.
- Supplementary material contains many typos and errors that impede verification (e.g., Lemma 5: W2^2→Ws^2; bound should be D1 (t/-log λ)^2 + λ^{1-α} D2 ||f||_2^2; lines 675/686 incorrect; integral domain at line 1147 must be ||ξ||^2 ≥; Eq.(31) is correct).
- Experiments demonstrate practical performance of the proposed methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel Fredholm+regularization approach, practical CV advantage over KMM, and supporting theoretical/experimental results.
- Arguments against acceptance: slow theoretical rate, strong/possibly unrealistic smoothness assumptions on q/p, and flawed supplementary material hindering proof verification.
