- Proposes estimating a density ratio via a kernel-based Fredholm integral equation, casting the task as an inverse problem.

- Assumes both distributions admit densities w.r.t. a common dominating measure, which is a very strong and potentially fragile assumption affecting robustness and applicability.

- The manuscript fails to specify the dominating measure ν, leaving definitions ambiguous and obscuring treatment of ν-null sets and ν-almost-everywhere statements.

- Lacks a clear statement of the domain of x (e.g., R^d vs. a general measurable/topological space).

- Does not state whether densities are strictly positive everywhere or only on their support, nor whether any uniform positivity is assumed.

- Omits regularity conditions on the densities and the kernel (continuity, boundedness, integrability, etc.).

- Does not address whether the two distributions share the same support or how boundary/support-mismatch cases are handled.

- As an inverse problem, the paper does not specify solvability and uniqueness conditions for the Fredholm equation or how ill-posedness is mitigated (e.g., regularization) with accompanying guarantees.

- Needs explicit statements of assumptions and their roles in the proofs to assess rigor and applicability.
