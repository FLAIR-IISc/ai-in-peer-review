- Proposes FIRE: a novel density ratio estimation approach that formulates the problem as a Fredholm integral equation (inverse problem) using kernels.  
- Uses regularization and RKHS techniques to build a principled, flexible, and easy-to-implement algorithmic framework.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel on Rd and smooth d-dimensional submanifolds.  
- Introduces CD-CV, a novel unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection in density ratio estimation.  
- Empirical results compare FIRE to KMM and LSIF, showing competitive or superior performance on several datasets.  
- Strength: novel inverse-problem formulation leveraging classical analysis techniques.  
- Strength: principled framework enabling algorithm construction and theoretical study.  
- Strength: new unsupervised model selection method (CD-CV).  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical results including concentration and convergence guarantees.  
- Weakness: assumes existence of densities, which may not hold in practical settings and needs discussion or relaxation.  
- Weakness: unclear statement of key assumptions (dominating measure and density properties) that should be clarified.  
- Weakness: some theoretical results (e.g., convergence rates) are hard to interpret without more context or explanation.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets and could be expanded for stronger empirical support.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarity on assumptions, interpretability of theory, and expanded experiments.
