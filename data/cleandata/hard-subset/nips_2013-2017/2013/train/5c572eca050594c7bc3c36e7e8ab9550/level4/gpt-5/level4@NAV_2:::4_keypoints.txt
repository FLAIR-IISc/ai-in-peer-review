- Proposes two RKHS-based formulations to estimate density ratio f = q/p using identities Ep[k(x,·) f] = Eq[k(x,·)] and Ep[k(x,·) f] ≈ q(x) for localized kernels.  
- Replaces integral operators by empirical analogues and treats {k(x,·)} as test functions, enabling optimization via representer theorems and Tikhonov regularization.  
- Introduces two estimators (Type I and Type II, FIRE) with explicit closed-form solutions.  
- Provides spectral/operator-theoretic analysis with nonasymptotic concentration bounds and convergence rates under smoothness and boundedness assumptions.  
- Derives rates for both Euclidean domains and compact manifolds, showing intrinsic-dimension dependence.  
- Proposes an unsupervised model selection scheme (CD-CV) based on cross-density validation.  
- Empirical results show favorable performance compared to KMM, LSIF, and an inverse KDE baseline; discusses links to covariate shift and importance sampling.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed contribution with clear algorithms, careful analysis, and supportive experiments.  
- Suggestion: add intuition why the same test-function span suffices for the exact identity (i), beyond operator-theoretic arguments.  
- Suggestion: motivate the loss choices (L2,p vs γL2,p + (1−γ)L2,q) with guidance on when each is preferable for target tasks like covariate-shift risk vs stability.  
- Question: clarify when estimating via Ep(q/p ·) offers practical advantages over directly using available Eq in downstream tasks, and whether difficulty sampling from q is the main motivation.  
- Request: discuss the relationship between regularizing f and smoothness assumptions on p and q (when does smooth p,q imply smooth f, and vice versa).  
- Suggestion: provide rule-of-thumb guidance on the dimensional ranges (or intrinsic manifold dimensions) where FIRE is practically effective given the d-dependence in rates.  
- Suggestion: further justify the CD-CV validation function families (random linear and half-space indicators) and how they target downstream functionals or IPMs.  
- Broader positioning: compare and contrast the test-function choice {k(x,·)} with related approaches (e.g., “Smooth Operators”), clarifying similarities/differences in objectives and guarantees.
