- Proposes estimating the ratio of two densities via a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, treating it as an inverse problem.
- Fundamental critique: assuming both distributions have densities is problematic—arbitrarily close probability measures may lack a dominating density, making ratio estimation ill-posed in ML settings.
- The dominating measure ν is not specified (e.g., Lebesgue, counting), leaving the mathematical setup ambiguous.
- Unclear handling of sets of ν-measure zero and whether the results are rigorous on those sets.
- Lacks clear underlying assumptions: domain of x (R^d or a general topological space?) and whether p is strictly positive are not stated.
