- Proposes a novel kernel- and Fredholm-integral-based formulation to estimate the ratio of two probability densities, framing it as an inverse problem.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a logical progression from problem setup to algorithm and analysis.  
- Strong literature grounding and clear connections to kernel methods, regularization, and spectral analysis.  
- Theoretical analysis (Section 3) is thorough, using concentration inequalities and spectral theory to derive bounds and convergence rates.  
- Experimental evaluation (Section 4) is comprehensive, with clear dataset/resampling descriptions and informative comparisons to KMM and LSIF.  
- Concern: the assumption that densities p and q are well-defined and strictly positive everywhere may be unrealistic and needs discussion.  
- Concern: choice and role of the dominating measure ν require clearer justification due to issues with sets of ν-measure zero.  
- Suggestion: provide more detail on the computational complexity of the FIRE algorithm.  
- Suggestion: discuss potential extensions to more general settings (e.g., non-Euclidean spaces, nonparametric densities).  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to ML and statistical inference, deserving acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; Rating 8/10.
