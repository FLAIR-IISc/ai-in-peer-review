- Proposed method: estimate the ratio of two probability density functions using a kernel-based Fredholm integral equation, yielding an inverse problem formulation.  
- The assumption that both distributions admit densities is problematic in ML since arbitrarily close probability measures may lack a density, making ratio estimation ill-posed.  
- The manuscript omits specification of the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue or counting), undermining rigor, especially regarding sets of ν-measure zero.  
- The authors do not state assumptions on the domain/topology (e.g., x ∈ R^d vs. a general space), leaving the setting ambiguous.  
- It is unclear whether the density p is assumed strictly positive everywhere; this and other regularity assumptions should be explicitly stated to ensure validity.
