- Addresses density-ratio estimation by reformulating it as a Fredholm integral inverse problem using RKHS and regularization.
- Proposes the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family of algorithms for ratio estimation.
- Provides theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels across settings.
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised model selection method for parameter tuning.
- Situates work well within existing literature on density ratio estimation and transfer learning.
- Novel perspective: connects density-ratio estimation to operator theory and classical analysis.
- Theoretical contributions (bounds and rates) are a significant technical advance.
- CD-CV is a useful practical contribution for unsupervised parameter selection.
- Fails to discuss foundational issues about existence of densities and choice of dominating measure (e.g., Lebesgue).
- Key assumptions (domain, positivity and properties of p, topological space) are not explicitly stated.
- Experimental section is dense and could better link different experiments (parameter selection, regression, classification) and practical implications.
- Comparisons to methods like TIKDE and LSIF are present but lack deeper practical discussion.
- Paper would benefit from moving detailed derivations to supplementary material to streamline presentation.
- Overall: strong, theoretically grounded submission but needs clearer assumptions and improved experimental clarity — reviewer recommends weak accept contingent on fixes.
