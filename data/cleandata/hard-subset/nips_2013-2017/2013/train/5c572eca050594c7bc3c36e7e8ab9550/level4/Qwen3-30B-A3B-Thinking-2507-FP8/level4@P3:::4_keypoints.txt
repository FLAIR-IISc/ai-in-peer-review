- Proposes framing density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and regularizing it in RKHS, yielding the FIRE family of algorithms.
- Connects density ratio estimation to classical inverse problems and operator/spectral theory, offering a new analytical perspective.
- Provides a flexible framework supporting multiple loss functions/norms (L2,p, L2,q, combinations) while keeping computation simple.
- Derives convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in both Euclidean and manifold settings.
- Introduces CD-CV, a fully unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection.
- Delivers rigorous theoretical analysis with concentration bounds and clear error bounds for Type I and Type II settings.
- Empirical results on real datasets show strong performance and computational efficiency compared to KMM, LSIF, and TIKDE.
- Paper is clear, well-structured, and provides sufficient mathematical detail for reproduction.
- Originality is high: approach differs from KMM and LSIF by providing out-of-sample extension and explicit regularization via inverse problem formulation.
- Significant for applications in covariate shift and importance sampling, with practical theoretical guarantees.
- Suggestion: better motivate the specific choice of test functions {k(x,·)} and why they are optimal.
- Suggestion: clarify practical implications of the dimension dependence (d) in the convergence rates and indicate effective dimensional regimes.
- Suggestion: expand discussion on sampling from q and scenarios where q is known but sampling is computationally prohibitive.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution, recommend acceptance (pros: principled, theoretically grounded, efficient; cons: needs stronger motivation for test functions and clearer discussion of dimension effects).
