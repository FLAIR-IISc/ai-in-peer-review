- Proposes a novel reformulation of density ratio estimation (p/q) as a Fredholm integral equation.  
- Paper is well-written and tackles an important, interesting problem.  
- The approximation ∫ k_t(x,y) f(x) dx = f(y) + O(t) lacks justification and needs a proof or citation.  
- When q=1 the method reduces to density estimation and does not require bandwidth → 0; the distinction from KDE and differing roles of bandwidth should be discussed.  
- Brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization is recommended for readers unfamiliar with it.  
- All proofs are in the supplementary material and have not been independently verified by the reviewer.  
- Include an explanation of why a naive plug-in approach (estimating p and q separately) is expected to perform worse.  
- The paper’s theoretical performance relative to existing methods (e.g., [12]) and optimal minimax rates is unclear and should be clarified.  
- Provide references for the experimental datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).  
- Add toy experiments with known 1D distributions and plots comparing true vs. estimated p/q to illustrate behavior.  
- Overall: strong contribution but needs clearer comparisons to prior work and illustrative toy demonstrations.
