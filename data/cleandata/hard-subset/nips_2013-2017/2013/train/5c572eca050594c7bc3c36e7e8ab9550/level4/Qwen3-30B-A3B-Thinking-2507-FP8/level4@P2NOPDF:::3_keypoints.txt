1. Proposed kernel/Fredholm integral approach reframes density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem but its theoretical foundation lacks clarity and rigor.  
2. The dominating measure ν is unspecified, which is essential for defining densities and the ratio.  
3. Existence of a ν-density for one measure does not imply existence for nearby measures (total variation), a standard measure-theoretic issue the paper ignores.  
4. Without an explicit choice of ν the problem can be ill-posed and mathematical validity is unclear.  
5. The paper fails to address ν-null sets (e.g., points where q=0), which can make p/q undefined and break uniqueness or representativeness of solutions.  
6. Key assumptions are missing: the domain X (R^d or general space), strict positivity of p and q, and required regularity conditions (continuity, boundedness).  
7. Concrete counterexamples (e.g., q vanishing on an open set in R^d, or discrete X requiring counting measure) are not discussed, undermining applicability.  
8. No justification is given for the choice of ν in ML settings, so theoretical results cannot be generalized to standard continuous/discrete cases.  
9. Recommended fixes: explicitly define and justify ν, specify X and properties of p,q, treat ν-null sets, and state whether results hold ν-almost-everywhere.  
10. Overall, theoretical claims lack grounding; a more precise measure-theoretic foundation is required before evaluating practical utility.
