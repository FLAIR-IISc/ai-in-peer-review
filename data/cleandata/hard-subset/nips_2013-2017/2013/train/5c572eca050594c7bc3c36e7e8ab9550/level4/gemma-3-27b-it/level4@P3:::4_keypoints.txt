- Presents a novel density ratio estimation formulation as an inverse problem using Fredholm integral equations and RKHS, yielding the FIRE algorithm family.  
- Establishes theoretical guarantees (concentration bounds and convergence rates) for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Introduces the CD-CV unsupervised model selection method to address practical model tuning.  
- Connects the approach to classical operator theory and existing kernel methods (KMM, LSIF), providing a principled analysis framework.  
- Algorithms are simple to implement, relying on standard kernel techniques.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and shows competitive performance across datasets and tasks.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Needs more intuitive explanation for why optimizing over the kernel family {k(x,·)} ensures convergence to the density ratio (clarify Eq.1 vs Eq.2).  
- The motivation for the combined L2,p and L2,q cost in Eq.(9) should be strengthened, with clearer ties to specific applications like importance sampling.  
- The relationship between smoothness assumptions on p and q and the smoothness of their ratio q/p is underexplored.  
- Convergence rate dependence on data dimensionality (d) requires a more detailed discussion and practical guidance on dimensional regimes where the method performs well.  
- Justification for the test function family U used in CD-CV (random linear and half-space functions) is insufficient.  
- Broader context comparison to alternative approaches for covariate shift and generic cost functions (e.g., Smooth Operators) is limited.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, strong empirical results, fills a literature gap, and code is available.  
- Arguments against acceptance: some theoretical and motivation aspects need more elaboration, and more analysis of dimensionality and test function choices would improve impact.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with solid theory and experiments; recommended for acceptance despite areas needing clarification.
