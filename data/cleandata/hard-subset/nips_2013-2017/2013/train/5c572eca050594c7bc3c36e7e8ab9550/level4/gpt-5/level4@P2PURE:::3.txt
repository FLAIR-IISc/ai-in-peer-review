The authors propose a method for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. The approach uses a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation, which turns the task into an inverse problem.

From a machine learning perspective, assuming the existence of densities is a very strong requirement: it is well known that within any ε-neighborhood of a probability measure P that has a ν-density, there exist probability measures that have no ν-densities. Consequently, estimating a ratio of two densities is problematic from the ML viewpoint.

Moreover, the paper does not specify the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue measure, counting measure, or something else), and without this it is unclear whether the results are rigorous, especially regarding sets of ν-measure zero. I also miss a clear statement of the assumptions. For instance, does x lie in R^d or in a more general topological space? Is the density p positive for all x?
