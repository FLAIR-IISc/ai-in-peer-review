This paper introduces a novel approach to density ratio estimation, specifically addressing the problem of estimating p/q. While several methods already exist for this task, the key contribution of this work lies in reformulating the problem as a Fredholm integral equation.

The paper is clearly written and presents an important and engaging problem. 

However, several points would benefit from further elaboration. First, justifications – either through proof within the manuscript or a citation – are needed for statements like \int k_t(x,y)f(x)dx=f(y)+ O(t). Even seemingly straightforward assertions require support. 

Second, the paper notes the method’s applicability to density estimation as a special case (q=1), highlighting a valuable advantage: it does not necessitate bandwidth convergence to zero, a limitation of standard kernel density estimation. Expanding the discussion to clearly delineate the role of bandwidth parameters in this method versus KDE would significantly strengthen the paper.

Third, given the potential audience, a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization would be beneficial. 

Fourth, while the inclusion of proofs in the supplementary material is appreciated, their correctness remains unverified.

Fifth, a discussion of why a naive plug-in density estimation approach is inferior to the proposed method would provide valuable context. 

Sixth, the paper lacks a comparative performance analysis. Specifically, it’s unclear how this method stacks up against existing approaches, such as that presented in [12] which also provides minimax bounds. The upper bounds presented in Corollaries 2 and 4 should be discussed in relation to established minimax rates.

Seventh, please provide citations for the datasets used in the experiments (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).

Finally, the inclusion of a demonstration on synthetic data, perhaps through visualizations of estimated and true p/q for simple 1-dimensional cases, would help readers better understand the method’s behavior and estimation quality. 

Overall, this is a promising paper on density ratio estimation. However, clarifying its performance relative to existing algorithms (e.g., [12]) and demonstrating its efficacy on toy problems would significantly enhance its impact.
