- Introduces FIRE: reformulates density ratio estimation as an inverse problem using Fredholm integral equations.  
- Proposes two algorithms (FIRE) applying Tikhonov regularization within an RKHS.  
- Enables parameter selection via cross-validation, addressing model selection in unsupervised settings.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and promising empirical results.  
- Builds on KMM and LSIF but distinguishes itself by the inverse-problem framing and direct RKHS optimization enabling better out-of-sample behavior and stability.  
- Theoretical analysis includes concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels.  
- Theorem 1’s convergence rate (involving (-t/log λ)) appears slow compared to typical polynomial rates.  
- Recommends discussing additional smoothness assumptions (e.g., Caponnetto & De Vito) that could yield faster, polynomial rates with fixed kernel width.  
- Lacks discussion of strong regularity assumptions on q/p (e.g., Sobolev-type conditions) and implications for p/q and tail behavior.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typos and errors that hinder verification of theoretical results (e.g., mistakes in Lemma 5, lines 675/686, and integral domain at line 1147).  
- Strongly recommends careful revision and correction of the supplementary material.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, principled regularization and parameter selection, effective cross-validation, theoretical guarantees, and competitive empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: slow stated convergence rate, missing discussion of required assumptions, and errors in supplementary materials.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address convergence-rate discussion, assumption clarification, and supplementary corrections.
