- Reformulates density ratio estimation as an inverse problem via the Fredholm equation of the first kind.  
- Introduces FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator), a family of algorithms built from that formulation.  
- Leverages regularization and RKHS to create a principled, flexible solution framework.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates (Gaussian kernel).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on classification and regression tasks.  
- Compares favorably to state-of-the-art methods like KMM and LSIF in some scenarios.  
- Produces simple, flexible, and easy-to-implement algorithms that generalize to covariate shift and importance sampling.  
- Paper is well-written with clear notation, concise contributions, and good exposition.  
- Significant novelty by applying classical inverse-problem techniques to density ratio estimation.  
- Potentially impactful across machine learning, statistics, and signal processing applications.  
- Lacks sufficient motivation for the chosen cost function in specific application contexts.  
- Needs deeper exploration of how smoothness of p and q relates to smoothness of the density ratio.  
- Comparisons with other methods are limited, especially for particular application settings.
