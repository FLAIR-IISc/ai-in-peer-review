- Proposes estimating the ratio of two probability density functions via an inverse problem using a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation.

- Relies on the strong foundational assumption that the underlying probability measures possess densities, which is problematic in ML contexts.

- Theoretical fragility: any epsilon neighborhood of a measure with a ν-density can contain measures without a ν-density, undermining direct density-ratio estimation.

- Technical exposition lacks rigor by not specifying the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue or counting measure).

- Omission of ν creates ambiguity in how sets of ν-measure zero are handled, casting doubt on the results’ validity.

- Fails to clearly state key assumptions (e.g., domain of x: R^d vs a general topological space; whether p is strictly positive everywhere).
