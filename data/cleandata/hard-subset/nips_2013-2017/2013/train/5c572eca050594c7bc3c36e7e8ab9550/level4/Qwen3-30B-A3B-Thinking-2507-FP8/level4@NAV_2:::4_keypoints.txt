- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, casting it as a well-studied inverse problem.  
- Derives two estimators for f = q/p: (i) Ep[k(x,·)f] = Eq[k(x,·)] and (ii) Ep[k(x,·)f] ≈ q(x), using empirical integral operator approximations.  
- Uses test functions {k(x,·)} to enable kernel/RKHS-based optimization and principled estimation.  
- Provides comprehensive convergence guarantees for both settings under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates depending on ambient dimension.  
- Introduces CD-CV for unsupervised model selection and validates methods experimentally, showing strong performance versus existing approaches.  
- Strength: elegant inverse-problem reformulation linking operator theory and modern kernel methods.  
- Strength: detailed theoretical analysis with convergence rates and solid experimental support.  
- Needs clearer intuitive explanation why optimizing over {k(x,·)} ensures convergence in setting (i), especially regarding bandwidth effects.  
- Needs stronger motivation for switching from L2,p to the combined L2,p + (1-γ)L2,q loss in equation (9) and connection to applications.  
- Should clarify why estimating q/p via this framework is preferable to directly estimating expectations under q for tasks like importance sampling, given empirical estimation of both Ep and Eq.  
- Requires more discussion on how smoothness of p and q relates to smoothness of their ratio and conditions guaranteeing ratio smoothness.  
- Should expand on practical implications of dimension-dependent convergence rates and indicate regimes (dimensions) where the method is most effective.  
- Needs better justification for the chosen family of test functions U in CD-CV.  
- Recommend positioning and comparison with related work (e.g., “Smooth Operators”) to clarify similarities and advantages of the current formulation.
