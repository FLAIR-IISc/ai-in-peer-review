- Proposes two density-ratio estimators: Type I (importance-sampling identity regularized in RKHS, a Fredholm Type I formulation) and Type II (local-kernel approach requiring locality → 0, a Fredholm Type II formulation).  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and experimental validation of practical performance.  
- L2 formulation allows use of cross-validation for model selection, a key advantage over existing methods like KMM.  
- Recommend explicitly noting that RKHS-norm–based objectives (e.g., KMM) are kernel-dependent and thus ill-suited for CV because scores from different kernels are incomparable.  
- Theorem 1 includes a slow factor (-t/log λ); ask whether polynomial convergence rates (in sample size) can be obtained under stronger, reasonable assumptions (e.g., spectral/smoothness conditions as in Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).  
- Suggest discussing the possibility of achieving polynomial rates for the estimator under stronger smoothness or spectral assumptions on q/p.  
- Raise the general limitation that assuming q/p lies in a well-behaved function class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong and implies prior knowledge (e.g., tail behavior); note this can make p/q ill-behaved and limits applicability.  
- Supplementary material has numerous typos and errors (specific incorrect lemmas/equations cited) that hinder verification; must be thoroughly revised because theoretical results are central.
