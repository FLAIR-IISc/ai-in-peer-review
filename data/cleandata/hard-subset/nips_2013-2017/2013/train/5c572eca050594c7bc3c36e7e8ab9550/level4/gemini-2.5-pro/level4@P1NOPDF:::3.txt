The authors propose a method for estimating the ratio of two probability density functions. The approach is based on a kernel method that formulates the problem as a Fredholm integral equation, which in turn is treated as an inverse problem.

A central concern with the proposed framework is its foundational assumption that the underlying probability measures possess densities. From a machine learning perspective, this is a strong assumption. It is well-known that in any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P that has a density with respect to a measure ν, there are other probability measures arbitrarily close to P that do not possess such a density. This inherent fragility makes the task of estimating a density ratio, as framed in this paper, fundamentally challenging.

Furthermore, the paper lacks clarity on several key technical assumptions, which makes it difficult to assess the rigor of the results. Specifically, the authors do not state the dominating measure ν being used (e.g., the Lebesgue measure, the counting measure, or another choice). This ambiguity raises questions about the theoretical guarantees, particularly concerning the handling of sets of ν-measure zero.

The paper would also be significantly strengthened by a clear and consolidated statement of its assumptions. For instance, it is unclear what space x belongs to (e.g., $R^d$ or a more general topological space), or whether the density p is assumed to be positive for all x.
