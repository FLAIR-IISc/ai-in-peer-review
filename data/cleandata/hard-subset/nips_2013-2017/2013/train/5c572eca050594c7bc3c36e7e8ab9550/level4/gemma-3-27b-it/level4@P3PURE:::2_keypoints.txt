- Introduces two novel density-ratio estimation methods: Type I (importance-sampling reformulated as L2 + RKHS regularization / Type I Fredholm) and Type II (locality-kernel with vanishing parameter / Type II Fredholm).  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and empirical demonstrations of practical performance.  
- L2 formulation allows cross-validation for parameter selection, addressing a gap in unsupervised tuning and offering an advantage over RKHS-norm objectives like KMM for kernel selection.  
- Theorem 1’s rate contains a (-t / log λ) factor implying slow convergence for fixed t; authors should consider stronger assumptions (e.g., spectral conditions on K_{p,t} à la Caponnetto & De Vito) to possibly obtain polynomial rates.  
- The common assumption that q/p lies in a smooth class (e.g., Sobolev) is strong, requires prior knowledge of tails, and can make the inverse ratio p/q behave poorly; this limitation should be discussed.  
- Supplementary material has numerous typos and errors that impede verification of proofs (examples: Lemma 5 notation and bound errors, incorrect expressions at lines cited, integral domain restriction); a thorough revision is needed.  
- Overall verdict: compelling new approach with the notable practical strength of enabling CV for parameter selection.
