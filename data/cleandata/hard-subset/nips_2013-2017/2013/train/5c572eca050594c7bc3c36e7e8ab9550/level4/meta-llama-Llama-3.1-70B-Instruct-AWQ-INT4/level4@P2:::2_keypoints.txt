- Proposes two new density-ratio estimation methods: Type I (importance-sampling equation as L2 + RKHS regularization) and Type II (local-kernel approach with locality parameter →0, also as regularization).
- Provides theoretical convergence results and experimental demonstrations comparing with existing methods.
- Key advantage: L2 formulation enables effective use of cross-validation for parameter selection in this unsupervised problem.
- Notes that RKHS objectives like KMM are inappropriate for CV because objective values depend on the kernel and are not comparable.
- Thorough analysis connecting the methods to classical operator theory and spectral analysis.
- Method is similar to existing approaches (e.g., KMM) but offers the practical benefit of CV-based tuning.
- Concern: Theorem 1 gives a slow convergence rate with fixed t; polynomial rates may be attainable under stronger spectral assumptions (e.g., as in Caponnetto & De Vito 2007).
- Suggests the authors discuss stronger assumptions that could yield polynomial convergence rates for fixed t.
- Concern: Assuming q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) is very strong and requires knowledge of ratio/tails; p/q may behave poorly—this limitation should be discussed.
- Supplementary material contains many typos and mistakes that hinder verification of theoretical results (e.g., Lemma 5 W2^2→Ws^2 and corrected bounds; incorrect expressions at lines 675/686; integral domain at line 1147; Eq.(31) is correct) and should be revised.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
