- Problem: Estimation of the ratio p/q motivated by importance sampling and covariate shift.  
- Method: Reformulates ratio estimation as solving a Fredholm integral equation using regularization and RKHS tools.  
- Algorithm: Introduces FIRE, a family of simple, flexible algorithms derived from the integral equation formulation.  
- Theory: Provides detailed theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels.  
- Model selection: Proposes a novel unsupervised model selection technique (CD-CV) and validates it empirically.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation connecting density-ratio estimation to classical inverse-problem analysis.  
- Strength: Detailed convergence analysis is a significant contribution.  
- Strength: Empirical results show strong performance compared to existing methods.  
- Clarification needed: Explain why convergence is guaranteed in setting (i) where minimization is over kernel functions.  
- Clarification needed: Motivate the combined L2,p and L2,q norm in equation (9) by linking to specific application scenarios.  
- Clarification needed: Expand discussion of primary use cases where sampling from q is difficult and their relevance.  
- Technical question: Discuss whether smoothness of q/p follows from (or implies) smoothness of p and q and consequences.  
- Practical concern: Discuss how dimension d affects applicability given convergence rates’ dependence on d.  
- Clarification needed: Provide deeper justification for the choice of test functions U and why the selected family is appropriate.  
- Related work: Acknowledge connection to prior expectation-operator/test-function approaches (e.g., Grünewälder et al. 2012) and contrast advantages.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel formulation, strong theory, simple algorithms, clear implementation, and new unsupervised model selection.  
- Rejection concerns: Motivation for some choices (combined norm, test functions), dimensionality impact, and clearer articulation of scenarios with clear advantages.
