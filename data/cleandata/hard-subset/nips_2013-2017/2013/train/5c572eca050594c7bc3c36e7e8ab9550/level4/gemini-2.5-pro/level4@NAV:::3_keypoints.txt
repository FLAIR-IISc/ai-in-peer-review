- Introduces a kernel-based Fredholm integral equation method that frames density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem.  
- Relies on the strong assumption that the underlying probability measures admit densities, which is theoretically fragile.  
- Notes that measures arbitrarily close to a ν-density measure may lack densities, making density-ratio estimation inherently challenging.  
- Omits specification of the dominating measure ν (e.g., Lebesgue or counting), creating ambiguity about validity and handling of ν-zero sets.  
- Lacks a consolidated statement of assumptions: domain of x (R^d vs. general topological space) and whether p is strictly positive on its support are not specified.
