- Proposes novel reformulation of density ratio estimation p/q as a Fredholm integral equation.
- Paper is well-written and addresses an important, interesting problem.
- Needs justification or reference for the kernel approximation statement ∫ k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t).
- When q=1 the method reduces to density estimation; discuss how its bandwidth behavior differs from KDE and why bandwidth need not go to zero.
- Include a brief explanation of Tikhonov regularization for readers unfamiliar with it.
- Proofs are in the supplementary material and have not been independently verified by the reviewer.
- Explain why a naive plug-in approach (estimate p and q separately) is inferior to the proposed method.
- Clarify how the presented convergence bounds (Corollaries 2 and 4) compare to known minimax rates and related work such as [12].
- Provide references for the experimental datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).
- Add simple toy experiments (e.g., 1D p and q) showing plots comparing true p/q to estimated p/q to illustrate estimator quality.
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but needs clearer comparisons to existing algorithms and more illustrative experiments.
