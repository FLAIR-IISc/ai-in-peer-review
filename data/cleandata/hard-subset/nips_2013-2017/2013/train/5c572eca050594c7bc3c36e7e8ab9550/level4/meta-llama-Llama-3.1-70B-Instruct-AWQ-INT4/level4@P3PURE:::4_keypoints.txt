- Paper derives two estimators for the density ratio f = p/q from (i) E_p[k(x,·) f] = E_q[k(x,·)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,·) f] ≈ q(x), using empirical integral operators and kernel test functions.  
- Choosing {k(x,·)} as test functions enables optimization with standard kernel methods.  
- The authors provide convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions; rates depend critically on the ambient dimension.  
- Reviewer considers the work well-executed and timely.  
- Request for a short intuitive argument why optimizing over {k(x,·)} yields convergence in case (i), analogous to the bandwidth intuition for (ii).  
- Ask for motivation of the cost function choice: optimization over L2,p initially but (9) mixes L2,p and L2,q, and a link to target applications would help justify the choice.  
- Question the application setup (e.g., importance sampling): since Ep and Eq are empirically estimated anyway, why not use Eq directly — is sampling difficulty from Eq the main motivating application?  
- Ask about the relation between smoothness assumptions on p and q and the smoothness of the ratio q/p, and whether bounds or conditions linking them can be given.  
- Request discussion of the dependence on dimension d: for which dimensions is the method practically useful?  
- Ask for motivation for the chosen family of test functions U.  
- Pose a broader question whether {k(x,·)} is the “right” generic approach for covariate-shift/ratio estimation and note similarity to prior work (e.g., Smooth Operators).
