- Reformulates density ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, linking it to inverse problem theory.  
- Uses a kernel-defined integral operator to relate the unknown ratio to estimable quantities.  
- Type I setting: samples from both p and q; solve K_p(f) = K_q(1) for f = q/p.  
- Type II setting: samples from p and pointwise evaluations of q; solve approximate equation K_{t,p}(f) ≈ q.  
- Applies Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family with closed-form solutions.  
- Provides theoretical analysis for the Gaussian kernel: concentration bounds and convergence rates on Euclidean spaces and sub-manifolds.  
- Proposes Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) as an unsupervised method for model/parameter selection.  
- Experiments show competitive performance compared to KMM and LSIF on several datasets.  
- Strength: novel, principled formulation connecting density-ratio estimation to classical analysis.  
- Strength: algorithmically simple and flexible, with representer-theorem-based explicit solutions.  
- Strength: solid theoretical treatment supporting the methods (proofs assumed correct).  
- Strength: practical contribution via CD-CV and clear, well-written exposition.  
- Weakness: key technical assumptions (e.g., ∫k_t(x,y)f(x)dx = f(y) + O(t)) lack proof or citation and need justification.  
- Weakness: experiments omit a naive plug-in baseline (e.g., KDE-based ratio) for comparison.  
- Weakness: lacks simple low-dimensional synthetic experiments (e.g., 1D Gaussian) to visualize ground-truth recovery.  
- Weakness: needs deeper discussion of relation to KDE, especially the role of kernel bandwidth t and consistency differences.  
- Weakness: theoretical convergence rates are not compared to known minimax optimal rates to contextualize performance.  
- Weakness: accessibility and reproducibility could improve with a brief primer on Tikhonov regularization and dataset citations (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS).
