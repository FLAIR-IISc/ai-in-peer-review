- Proposes estimating the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q using two equalities involving expectations of kernel test functions and approximating integral operators empirically.  
- Uses a chosen family of test functions {k(x,·)} so standard kernel methods can optimise the discrepancy between the two sides.  
- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates deteriorating with data dimension.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed, timely, and strong contribution with thorough convergence analysis and experiments.  
- Request: provide intuitive explanations for the convergence rates and why optimisation over {k(x,·)} suffices for convergence in both settings.  
- Request: clarify motivation for the chosen cost (combination of L2,p and L2,q) and relate it to practical applications.  
- Question: justify the importance-sampling formulation—why estimate Ep and Eq to obtain q/p instead of using Eq directly when available?  
- Request: explore how smoothness of p and q translates to smoothness of the ratio q/p and implications for the regulariser used.  
- Request: discuss dimension dependence more explicitly and specify the dimensional regimes where the method is expected to be useful.
