The authors propose a solution to the problem of how to estimate the ratio of two probability density functions. This approach is based on using a kernel and a Fredholm integral equation. This leads to a counterintuitive problem. From a machine learning perspective, the concept of density is very difficult because it is well known that in any epsilon neighborhood of a probability measure P with density \nu, there are probability measures with density \nu-different. Therefore, estimating the ratio of two densities is problematic from an ML perspective. As far as I can see, the authors do not specify which effective measure \nu was used (Lebusque measure, counting measure, something else), and it is not clear to me whether their results are rigorous due to the zero set of \nu-measures. I also don't recall a clear statement of what assumptions were made. For example, is x related to $R^d$ or to some general topological space? Is the density p positive for all x?
