- Proposes two estimators for the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q using equalities (i) E_p[k(x,·) f] = E_q[k(x,·)] and (ii) E_p[k(x,·) f] ≈ q(x), replacing integral operators with empirical counterparts and optimizing over kernel test functions.

- Key advantage: treating {k(x,·)} as test functions allows optimization with standard kernel methods.

- Provides convergence guarantees under smoothness and boundedness assumptions; convergence rates critically depend on the ambient dimension.

- Requests an intuitive explanation for why optimizing over {k(x,·)} guarantees convergence in setting (i), analogous to the bandwidth-dependent intuition for (ii).

- Asks for motivation for optimizing over L2,p when equation (9) mixes L2,p and L2,q, and for linking this cost choice to target applications.

- Questions the importance-sampling example: since both E_p and E_q are estimated, why not use E_q directly—clarify whether difficulty sampling from q is the main application.

- Notes tension between regularizing the smoothness of the ratio q/p and assuming smoothness of p and q in the theorem; asks for discussion of how smoothness of p and q relates to smoothness of q/p.

- Requests explicit discussion of how convergence rates depend on dimension d and for which values of d the method is practical.

- Asks for justification for the specific choice of test-function family U and whether {k(x,·)} is in any sense optimal, noting similarity to prior work (Smooth Operators).

- Overall verdict: timely, strong, rigorously analyzed paper with valuable contributions and experimental validation.
