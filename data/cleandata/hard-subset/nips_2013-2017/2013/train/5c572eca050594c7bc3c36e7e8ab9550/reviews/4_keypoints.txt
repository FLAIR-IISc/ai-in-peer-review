- Paper derives two estimators of the Radon–Nikodym derivative f = p/q from equalities Ep[k(x,·) f] = Eq[k(x,·)] and Ep[k(x,·) f] ≈ q(x), replacing integral operators with empirical versions.
- Using {k(x,·)} as test functions allows leveraging kernel methods for optimisation.
- Convergence guarantees are provided under smoothness and boundedness assumptions, with rates depending critically on the ambient dimension.
- Overall assessment: well-executed, timely, strong paper with theoretical and experimental results.
- Request for an intuitive argument why optimisation over {k(x,·)} ensures convergence in equality (i), similar to (ii) where bandwidth intuition helps.
- Question about the choice of cost function: optimisation initially over L2,p but (9) mixes L2,p and L2,q — ask for motivation and links to target applications.
- Application/motivation concern for importance sampling: since Ep and Eq are estimated to form the empirical cost, why not use the estimated Eq directly — clarify when estimating q/p helps versus using Eq.
- Regulariser and smoothness: ask for discussion of relationships between smoothness of p and q and smoothness of the ratio q/p (and conditions like p bounded away from zero).
- Request discussion of dependence on dimension d: for what dimensions is the method practically useful?
- Ask for motivation and justification for the chosen family of test functions U.
- Principled question: is using {k(x,·)} the “right” general approach for covariate-shift / q/p estimation, and how does this relate to prior work like “Smooth Operators” (Grunewalder et al.)?
