- Proposes two new density-ratio estimation methods: Type I (importance-sampling equation as L^2 regularization with RKHS) and Type II (kernel with locality → 0, also as regularization / Fredholm Type II).  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and experimental validation of practical performance.  
- Key advantage: L^2 formulation enables reliable cross-validation (CV) for parameter selection in an unsupervised setting.  
- Notes similarity to existing methods (e.g., KMM) but emphasizes CV applicability as a strong benefit over RKHS-objective methods.  
- Argues RKHS objective values (as in KMM) are not appropriate for kernel selection via CV because they are kernel-dependent and not comparable.  
- Critiques Theorem 1: convergence rate is slow due to the (-t / log λ) factor; asks if stronger assumptions can yield polynomial rates with fixed t.  
- Suggests spectral assumptions (e.g., Caponnetto & De Vito 2007) or stronger assumptions on q/p might give polynomial convergence rates.  
- Urges discussion of the strong assumption that q/p lies in a smooth function class (e.g., Sobolev) and the practical limitation that p/q may then behave poorly.  
- Requests improvement of the supplementary material due to many typos and errors that hinder verification of theoretical results.

- Overall assessment: novel approach with main contribution being effective CV-based parameter selection for density-ratio estimation.
