- Proposes framing density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and solving it with RKHS regularization, producing the FIRE algorithms.
- Well-motivated application to importance sampling and covariate shift with a solid mathematical foundation.
- Correctly positions work relative to KMM and LSIF, highlighting differences and advantages.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates, especially for Gaussian kernels and manifold-supported distributions.
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised model selection method for parameter tuning.
- Empirical results show competitive performance against existing methods.
- Mathematical exposition is dense in places and could use more intuitive explanations.
- Discussion of FIRE variants (Type I vs. Type II) is unfocused and could be clearer and more concise.
- Experimental section is fragmented and would benefit from a more cohesive narrative and deeper ablation studies of FIRE variants.
- Connection to prior integral equation and regularization literature is acknowledged but not deeply explored; relating convergence rates to known results would help.
- Theoretical analysis relies heavily on the Gaussian kernel; more general kernel results or exploration of other kernels is needed.
- Notation is sometimes heavy and could be simplified for readability.
- Paper lacks discussion of computational complexity and runtime behavior of the proposed methods.
- Overall assessment: solid, novel, and theoretically strong work worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, experiments, and computational discussion.
