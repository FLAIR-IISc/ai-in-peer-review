This paper introduces a novel approach to estimating the ratio of two densities, q/p, where p is a density function and q is another density or an arbitrary function. The authors reformulate the problem as an inverse problem in terms of an integral operator corresponding to a kernel, known as the Fredholm problem of the first kind. This formulation, combined with regularization techniques, leads to a principled framework for constructing algorithms and analyzing them theoretically. The resulting family of algorithms, called FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator), is flexible, simple, and easy to implement.

The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis, including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel for densities defined on R and smooth d-dimensional sub-manifolds of the Euclidean space. They also introduce a simple unsupervised method for model selection, called CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation), which is based on a performance measure unique to their setting.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the experimental results are encouraging. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of related work, which helps to situate their contribution within the broader context of density ratio estimation and transfer learning.

Weaknesses:
