- Proposes a novel formulation of density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem via the Fredholm integral equation of the first kind.  
- Uses kernel-based integral operators and regularization to build a principled estimation framework.  
- Introduces the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) algorithm with Type I and Type II settings.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on R and smooth d-dimensional submanifolds.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness of FIRE in experiments, including covariate-shift classification.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with consistent notation.  
- Gives a comprehensive review of related work, including KMM and LSIF.  
- Strength: novel, principled approach to a fundamental statistical problem.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical guarantees supporting the method.  
- Strength: FIRE is simple, flexible, and empirically promising.  
- Weakness: relies on smoothness/regularity assumptions for p and q that may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear discussion of FIRE’s computational complexity and scalability.  
- Weakness: does not provide comprehensive empirical comparison with state-of-the-art methods (e.g., KMM, LSIF).  
- Significance: potential impact across machine learning, statistics, and signal processing.  
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing computational complexity details and providing broader empirical comparisons.
