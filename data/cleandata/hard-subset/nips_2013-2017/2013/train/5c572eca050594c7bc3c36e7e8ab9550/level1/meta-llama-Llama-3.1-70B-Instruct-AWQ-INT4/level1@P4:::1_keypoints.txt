- Introduces a novel Fredholm integral equation reformulation for estimating the density ratio q/p and derives a family of algorithms called FIRE using regularization.  
- FIRE algorithms are presented as flexible, simple, and easy to implement.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel on R and on smooth d-dimensional submanifolds.  
- Proposes CD-CV, a simple unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection tailored to their setting.  
- Experimental results are encouraging and demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness on multiple datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and includes a comprehensive related-work review.  
- Strength: roots the method in a well-established mathematical framework (Fredholm equations).  
- Strength: thorough theoretical guarantees for the proposed estimator.  
- Weakness: assumes p and q are smooth and well-behaved, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough empirical comparison to state-of-the-art density-ratio methods.  
- Weakness: CD-CV may be sensitive to outliers or noisy data (potential lack of robustness).  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—add stronger comparisons, discuss limitations, and consider more robust model-selection options.
