- Proposes viewing density-ratio estimation r(x)=q(x)/p(x) as a Fredholm integral equation with two formulations (Type I: Kp r = Kq 1; Type II: Kt,p r ≈ q) solved by Tikhonov regularization in RKHS yielding FIRE estimators.  
- Introduces closed-form kernel-expansion solutions via the representer theorem, enabling natural out-of-sample evaluation.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: finite-sample concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in R^d and for measures on smooth manifolds, with transparent bias/regularization/stochastic error decomposition.  
- Proposes CD-CV, a fully unsupervised hyperparameter selection method based on matching empirical expectations (an IPM/moment-matching criterion).  
- Connects the approach to prior work: KMM, KLIEP/LSIF, RKHS inverse-problem regularization, and manifold/kernel-operator methods.  
- Strength: conceptual unification of ratio estimation as an inverse problem, clarifying KMM and motivating regularization.  
- Strength: algorithmic simplicity and explicit out-of-sample extensibility compared to KMM.  
- Strength: nontrivial theoretical contributions including manifold extensions and clear error decomposition.  
- Strength: practical, easy-to-implement unsupervised model selection (CD-CV).  
- Strength: appropriate applications demonstrated for covariate shift tasks (weighted SVM/regression).  
- Weakness: strong/unclear assumptions (requires q≪p, q/p and q/p^2 ∈ W^2_2, only upper bound on p)—near-zero p regions and support mismatch not sufficiently addressed.  
- Weakness: method does not enforce non-negativity or normalization of r; negative estimates can hurt importance sampling and downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: derived convergence rates are relatively slow and optimality compared to minimax or other density-ratio bounds is unclear.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter-scaling conditions in corollaries are non-intuitive (odd exponents); practical tuning guidance for t and λ is lacking.  
- Weakness: CD-CV could be framed/compared to MMD/IPM choices (e.g., characteristic kernels) for stronger validation signals.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons omit important baselines (KMM, KLIEP/uLSIF/RuLSIF, IWCV) and rely on a weak TIKDE baseline; sensitivity to clipping/support mismatch not reported.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—dense linear solves are O(n^3)/O(n^2) memory; no discussion of Nyström, random features, or iterative solvers.  
- Quality: generally sound and insightful derivations but some assumptions stronger than needed and require clearer justification.  
- Clarity: well-written but dense notation; would benefit from pseudocode, complexity summaries, and clearer guidance on when Type II applies and on absolute continuity needs.  
- Originality: the Fredholm-equation formulation and manifold extensions are original and elegant.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for covariate shift and importance weighting if empirical comparisons, positivity handling, and scalability are strengthened.  
- For acceptance: conceptual unification, out-of-sample algorithm, nontrivial theory, and unsupervised hyperparameter selection argue in favor.  
- Against acceptance: missing key baselines and positivity constraints, opaque/strong assumptions and slow rates, limited scalability discussion, and incomplete experiments argue against.  
- Suggested improvements: enforce or analyze non-negativity/normalization (clipping, constrained/log parameterizations); add KMM/KLIEP/uLSIF/RuLSIF/IWCV baselines and MMD-based CD-CV; give practical tuning guidance for t vs. λ and discuss rate optimality; clarify support/absolute-continuity assumptions; address scalable solvers and runtime.
