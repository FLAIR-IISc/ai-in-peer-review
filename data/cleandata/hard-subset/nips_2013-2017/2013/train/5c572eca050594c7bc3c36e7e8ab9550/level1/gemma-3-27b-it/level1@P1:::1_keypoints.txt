- Novel formulation: reframes density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and introduces FIRE algorithms.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides concentration bounds and convergence rates (Gaussian kernels on Euclidean and manifold domains).  
- Practical model selection: introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised method for hyperparameter selection.  
- Good contextualization: connects to importance sampling and covariate shift and differentiates from KMM via explicit regularization.  
- Clarity and completeness: well-written, consistent notation, thorough related work and algorithmic exposition.  
- Flexibility: framework supports different norms and loss functions for varied applications.  
- Computational concern: matrix inversions in Equations 8 and 10 may be expensive; scalability not thoroughly addressed.  
- Kernel limitation: analysis focuses on Gaussian kernels; sensitivity to other kernels and bandwidth selection is not well explored.  
- Experimental scope: evaluations are mostly on small datasets; scalability and performance on large, complex data are not demonstrated.  
- CD-CV sensitivity: more detail needed on choice and properties of test functions U and method robustness.  
- Type II limitation: strong assumption of knowing q at sample points needs clearer emphasis and discussion.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with competitive empirical performance and a practical model-selection tool.  
- Cons for acceptance: potential scalability issues, limited kernel/parameter exploration, and small-scale experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor revisions suggested for acceptance.  
- Suggested revisions: address computational complexity/approximation strategies, evaluate other kernels, run larger-scale experiments, analyze CD-CV sensitivity, and explicitly discuss Type II limitations.
