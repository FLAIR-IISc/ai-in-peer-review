- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and solves it via regularization in RKHS (FIRE).
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV) for unsupervised model selection.
- Provides theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels.
- Empirically compares FIRE to KMM and LSIF, showing competitive or superior accuracy and efficiency.
- Connects to classical operator theory and spectral analysis, distinguishing it from prior methods.
- Novel and theoretically rigorous reformulation bridging operator theory and ML.
- FIRE is flexible, supports various norms/regularizers, and is simple to implement.
- CD-CV addresses a key unsupervised/semi-supervised model selection challenge.
- Experimental results validate FIRE across multiple datasets and settings.
- Contributions are clearly stated (theory, algorithms, applications).
- Presentation is dense and may be inaccessible to readers unfamiliar with RKHS/operator theory.
- Limited experimental comparison with KMM, especially in scenarios where KMM may excel.
- Scalability for large-scale datasets is not thoroughly discussed or empirically evaluated.
- Focus on Gaussian kernels and smooth manifolds may limit applicability to non-smooth or high-dimensional cases.
- Paper makes a significant theoretical and practical contribution to density ratio estimation.
- FIRE framework is principled, flexible, and supported by theoretical guarantees.
- CD-CV fills an important gap in unsupervised model selection.
- Concerns: dense presentation, unclear scalability/applicability to large/non-smooth settings, and incomplete experimental comparisons.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestion to improve clarity and address scalability in revisions.
