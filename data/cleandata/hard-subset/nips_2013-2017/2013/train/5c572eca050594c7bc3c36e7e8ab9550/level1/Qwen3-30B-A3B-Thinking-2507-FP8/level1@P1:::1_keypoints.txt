- Reformulates density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solves it via RKHS regularization.  
- Introduces the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family of algorithms.  
- Proposes CD-CV, a novel unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rate analysis for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and on d-dimensional manifolds (Theorems 1,3; Corollary 2).  
- Positions the approach relative to prior work, showing KMM can be seen as a special case of their Type I formulation.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor with precise error bounds and manifold extensions.  
- Strength: CD-CV addresses unsupervised model selection, a practical challenge.  
- Strength: paper is clear, well-structured, and connects the Fredholm formulation to existing methods.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed explanation why the Fredholm approach improves practical performance beyond theory.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Weakness: theoretical sections are technical and could use more intuitive explanations for broader accessibility.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to more recent density-ratio methods (e.g., [26] and neural-network based approaches).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel conceptual link to inverse problems, practical algorithms (FIRE) and CD-CV, rigorous analysis, and competitive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear practical/computational benefits over simpler methods and limited empirical scalability comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation from reviewer: Accept.
