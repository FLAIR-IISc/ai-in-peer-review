- Reformulates density ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, enabling a principled inverse-problem view.
- Uses Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS to derive the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family of algorithms.
- Connects and generalizes prior methods (e.g., KMM, LSIF) within the Fredholm/RKHS framework and emphasizes stability from explicit regularization.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with concentration bounds and convergence rates for Euclidean spaces and manifolds.
- Proposes a practical unsupervised model-selection method (CD-CV) to choose hyperparameters.
- Clearly written and well-structured presentation, with helpful Type I vs Type II setting distinction.
- Lacks more intuition in the main text about error bounds and the interaction between regularization λ, kernel width t, and sample sizes.
- Experimental evaluation is solid but would be strengthened by synthetic high-dimensional experiments with known ground-truth ratios.
- Algorithmic solutions reduce to variants of kernel ridge regression, so novelty primarily lies in the formulation and analysis rather than radically new algorithms.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, and significant work likely to be influential, with minor addressable weaknesses.
