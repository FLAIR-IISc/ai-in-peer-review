- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, linking inverse problem theory and ML.
- Proposes FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) using Tikhonov regularization with RKHS norms for a unified estimation framework.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.
- Introduces CD-CV, a novel unsupervised model selection method that effectively addresses model selection in this setting.
- Positions work within NIPS literature, showing KMM fits as a special case in their Type I framework but with different optimization.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance vs. KMM, LSIF, and TIKDE on classification and regression tasks.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.
- Originality is high: new Fredholm-equation perspective opens avenues for theoretical analysis.
- Significance is high due to broad applicability (covariate shift, importance sampling, transfer learning) and practical advantages.
- Minor weakness: computational complexity of FIRE could be highlighted more compared to simpler methods, though experiments include efficiency comparisons.
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution with principled theory and strong empirical results; recommended for acceptance.
