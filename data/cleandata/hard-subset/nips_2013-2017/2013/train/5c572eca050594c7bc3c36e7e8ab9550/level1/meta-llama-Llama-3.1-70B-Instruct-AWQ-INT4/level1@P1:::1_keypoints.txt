- Addresses estimation of q(x)/p(x) by reformulating as a Fredholm equation of the first kind and solving via regularization in RKHS.  
- Proposes the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family: simple, flexible, and easy-to-implement algorithms.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel on R and smooth d-dimensional submanifolds.  
- Introduces CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation), a completely unsupervised model-selection method and demonstrates its practical utility.  
- Strength: novel inverse-problem formulation of density-ratio estimation.  
- Strength: principled RKHS + regularization framework enabling algorithm construction and analysis.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical results for Gaussian kernels and manifold settings.  
- Strength: useful unsupervised model-selection procedure (CD-CV).  
- Weakness: assumes kernel k(x,y) is symmetric positive definite, which may not hold in all applications.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive empirical comparison to existing methods like KMM and LSIF.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets and needs expansion.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel, principled approach to a fundamental problem with rigorous theory and useful CD-CV contribution.  
- Con-acceptance: restrictive kernel assumptions, limited experiments, and missing thorough comparisons to prior methods.  
- Overall: significant and original contribution but would benefit from more extensive experiments and comparative evaluation.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10; Clarity 9/10 (well written but some derivations could be more explicit); Originality 9/10; Significance 8.5/10.
