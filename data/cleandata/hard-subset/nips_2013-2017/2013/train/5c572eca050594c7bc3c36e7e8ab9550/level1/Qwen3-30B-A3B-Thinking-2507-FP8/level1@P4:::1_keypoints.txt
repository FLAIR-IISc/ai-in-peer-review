- Introduces FIRE: a novel formulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation solved via inverse-problem regularization.  
- Uses RKHS regularization to construct a principled estimator framework for the inverse problem.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.  
- Covers both Type I and Type II settings with bounds that balance approximation, estimation, and regularization errors.  
- Proposes CD-CV, a novel unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection.  
- Clear, well-structured exposition with logical flow from formulation to theory to experiments.  
- Effectively situates work relative to prior methods (KMM, LSIF), highlighting differences like out-of-sample extension and regularization.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation on multiple datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) comparing FIRE to KMM, LSIF, TIKDE.  
- Introduces and uses the JCD performance measure for parameter selection, demonstrating CD-CV utility.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over baselines, especially in classification tasks.  
- Novelty is significant: applying Fredholm/inverse-problem perspective to density ratio estimation is original and impactful.  
- Work is broadly significant for applications (transfer learning, importance sampling, covariate shift).  
- Paper is technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments; suitable for NIPS.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of theoretical assumptions/limitations (e.g., Sobolev space conditions).  
- Weaknesses: lacks detailed computational efficiency comparison with alternatives.  
- Recommendation: accept.
