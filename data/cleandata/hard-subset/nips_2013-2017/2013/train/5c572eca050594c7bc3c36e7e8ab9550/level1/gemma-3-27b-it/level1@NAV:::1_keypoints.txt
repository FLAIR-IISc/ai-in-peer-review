- Tackles density ratio estimation by framing it as an inverse problem using Fredholm integral equations and Tikhonov regularization.  
- Proposes a family of algorithms (FIRE) leveraging operator theory and RKHS tools.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and concentration bounds for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Introduces CD-CV, a novel unsupervised model selection technique, and evaluates it empirically.  
- Positions the work relative to prior methods like KMM and LSIF, highlighting differences and connections.  
- Emphasizes the benefit of a principled operator-theoretic framework enabling spectral and Fourier analysis.  
- Key strength: solid theoretical foundation with rigorous convergence rate analysis.  
- Key strength: flexible framework accommodating different norms and loss functions.  
- Key strength: practical unsupervised model selection (CD-CV) and competitive empirical performance.  
- Limitation: theoretical analysis restricted to the Gaussian kernel, limiting generality.  
- Limitation: experimental section could be more comprehensive, especially deeper comparisons to KMM with careful tuning.  
- Limitation: lacks discussion of computational complexity and clearer experimental visualization/statistical testing.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and original; reviewer recommends acceptance but suggests extending theory to other kernels and strengthening experiments.
