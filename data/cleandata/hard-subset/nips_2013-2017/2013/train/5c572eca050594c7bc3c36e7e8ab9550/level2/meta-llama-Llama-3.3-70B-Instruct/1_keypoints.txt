- Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm equation of the first kind (an inverse problem).
- Solves the inverse problem using RKHS-norm regularization, yielding the FIRE algorithm (simple, flexible, easy to implement).
- Establishes a connection between density ratio estimation and classical analysis, enabling a principled algorithmic framework.
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel.
- Introduces CD-CV, a fully unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection.
- Experimental results show FIRE performs well on regression and classification and compares favorably to KMM and LSIF.
- Thorough discussion of related work and connections to other kernel methods.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated inverse-problem formulation.
- Strength: principled and flexible RKHS-based regularization framework.
- Strength: theoretical guarantees and a novel unsupervised model-selection technique.
- Weakness: assumes smooth, bounded densities which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis restricted to the Gaussian kernel; extensions to other kernels are needed.
- Weakness: experiments could be more comprehensive (more datasets and comparisons).
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the listed weaknesses.
