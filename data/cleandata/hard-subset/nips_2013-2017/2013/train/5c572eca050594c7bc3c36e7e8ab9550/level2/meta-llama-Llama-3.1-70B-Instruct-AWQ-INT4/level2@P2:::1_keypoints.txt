- Problem: Estimates the density ratio q(x)/p(x) when p is given by samples and q is a known function or given by samples.
- Method: Reformulates density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solves it with regularization in an RKHS.
- Algorithmic outcome: Produces a family of simple, flexible kernel-based algorithms for density ratio estimation.
- Main contribution 1: Casting density ratio estimation as a classical inverse problem.
- Main contribution 2: A principled, flexible framework for algorithm construction and theoretical analysis.
- Main contribution 3: Detailed theoretical analysis for the Gaussian kernel case.
- Main contribution 4: A fully unsupervised technique for cross-validating algorithm parameters.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations.
- Theory: Thorough theoretical analysis leveraging operator/spectral and Fourier tools.
- Experiments: Encouraging empirical results demonstrating effectiveness.
- Related work: Compares to KMM and LSIF and situates work within kernel-method literature.
- Strength: Novel, principled approach to density ratio estimation.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical treatment clarifies performance.
- Strength: Empirical results support the method’s effectiveness.
- Weakness: Assumes p and q are smooth, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: Requires careful selection of regularization parameter and kernel width.
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art density ratio methods.
- Originality: Novel use of Fredholm equation + RKHS provides a new, principled perspective.
- Significance: Addresses an important problem (importance sampling, covariate shift) with potential wide impact.
- Overall: Recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
- Rating/confidence: 8/10 rating, 8/10 reviewer confidence.
