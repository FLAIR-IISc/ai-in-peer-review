- Reformulates density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem using the Fredholm equation and RKHS regularization (FIRE algorithms).  
- Provides a novel and interesting formulation of the density ratio problem.  
- Delivers detailed theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates (Gaussian kernel).  
- Proposes simple, flexible, and easy-to-implement FIRE algorithms applicable in unsupervised and semi-supervised settings.  
- Presents encouraging experimental results, including covariate-shift classification applications.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets and settings.  
- Comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods are not comprehensive.  
- Theoretical guarantees rely on data assumptions (e.g., smoothness) whose realism is unclear for general settings.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions (add more experiments and comparisons).  
- Pros for acceptance: novel formulation, solid theory and experiments, and practical algorithms.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited evaluation, incomplete comparisons, and potentially restrictive assumptions.
