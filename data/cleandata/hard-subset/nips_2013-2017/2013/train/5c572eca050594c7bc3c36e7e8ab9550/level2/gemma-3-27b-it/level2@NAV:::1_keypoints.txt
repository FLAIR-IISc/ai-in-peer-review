- Novel formulation: frames density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation (inverse problem) enabling a principled mathematical treatment.
- Algorithmic contribution: proposes the FIRE family of algorithms within an RKHS regularization framework.
- Theoretical contributions: provides concentration bounds and convergence rates, including results for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.
- Model selection: introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised model selection technique.
- Connections: clearly relates and differentiates the approach from existing methods like KMM and LSIF.
- Experimental performance: shows competitive empirical results.
- Practical gap: limited discussion on how theoretical bounds translate to real-world performance, notably selection of regularization parameter λ.
- Experimental weakness: lacks detailed ablation studies to isolate the contribution of individual components (FIRE variants, CD-CV).
- Accessibility concern: dense mathematical notation and presentation may hinder readers unfamiliar with functional analysis; needs more intuitive explanations.
- Recommendation: overall strong theoretical novelty and results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand practical/experimental analysis.
