- Reformulates density-ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and applies Tikhonov RKHS regularization to produce the FIRE family with closed-form kernel-matrix solutions.  
- Provides concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian-kernel estimators on Euclidean spaces and submanifolds.  
- Proposes an unsupervised model-selection method (Cross-Density Cross-Validation, CD-CV) using the importance-sampling identity and test functions.  
- Connects the density-ratio problem to classical inverse-problem and regularization theory, unifying and relating to methods like KMM and LSIF.  
- Strength: original, principled formulation that leverages operator theory and regularization machinery.  
- Strength: flexible and simple algorithms handling both Type I and Type II settings with non-iterative closed-form solutions.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorems and Corollaries) giving rates and sample-size behavior.  
- Strength: practical unsupervised hyperparameter selection (CD-CV) that is well-motivated and empirically effective.  
- Weakness: lacks a direct empirical comparison with KMM and a discussion of computational trade-offs versus KMM’s constrained QP.  
- Weakness: sensitivity of CD-CV to the choice of test-function family U is not fully explored; no guidelines given.  
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions require q/p (and its square) to lie in a Sobolev space; practical implications and potential violations are not discussed.  
- Weakness: Type II formulation introduces an O(t) approximation error from the integral operator, creating a kernel-width vs. error trade-off that needs more practical discussion.  
- Overall: technically sound, original, and practically useful work with significant theoretical and empirical contributions; recommended for acceptance.
