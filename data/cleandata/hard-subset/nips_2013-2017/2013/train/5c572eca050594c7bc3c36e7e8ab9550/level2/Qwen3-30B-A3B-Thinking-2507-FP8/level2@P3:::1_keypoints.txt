- Proposes a novel density ratio estimation approach by formulating it as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind and solving via RKHS regularization (FIRE).  
- Establishes a principled connection between density ratio estimation and classical inverse problems, enabling systematic algorithm construction and analysis.  
- Provides detailed error bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and on manifolds.  
- Introduces CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation) for unsupervised model selection.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong and technically sound, covering both Type I (samples from both distributions) and Type II (known q values at sample points) settings.  
- Assumptions and concentration bounds are appropriate and support the theoretical results.  
- Experimental validation shows competitive performance versus LSIF, KMM, and TIKDE on multiple datasets (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) with careful test-function-based model selection.  
- Clarity is excellent: precise mathematical formulation, well-organized presentation, reproducible algorithm descriptions, and consistent notation.  
- Originality is significant: the Fredholm formulation offers a fresh, unifying perspective and shows how KMM fits within this framework.  
- Significance is high: method has broad applications (transfer learning, covariate shift, importance sampling), good computational efficiency, and opens new theoretical directions.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more concrete practical guidance for choosing parameters (t, λ) for different problem sizes/distributions and include computational complexity comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: well-motivated, rigorous, and practically useful work that is a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
