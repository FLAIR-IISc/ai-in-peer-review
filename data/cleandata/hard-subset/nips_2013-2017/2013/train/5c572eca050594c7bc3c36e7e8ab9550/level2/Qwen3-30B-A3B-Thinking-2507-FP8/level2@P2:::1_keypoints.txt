- Proposes a novel density ratio estimation framework by casting it as a Fredholm integral equation solved via RKHS regularization, introducing the FIRE family (Type I and II) and CD-CV for model selection.

- Principled connection between density ratio estimation and classical inverse problem theory.

- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorems 1–4) with error bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and sub-manifolds.

- Introduces CD-CV (Cross-Density Cross-Validation) to address unsupervised parameter selection.

- Experimental results show competitive performance versus KMM and LSIF and highlight computational efficiency.

- Insufficient comparison with related work, especially lacking explicit contrasts with KMM in implementation, theoretical guarantees, and practical advantages.

- Convergence rates (Corollaries 2 and 4) appear similar to prior minimax results; paper should clarify how its theoretical advances go beyond existing work.

- The Type II claim that q need not be a density is interesting but lacks concrete practical application examples.

- Theoretical assumptions that p and q are bounded may be restrictive for real-world cases with unbounded support or heavy tails.

- Paper is generally well-written and organized; Fredholm connection and algorithms are clearly presented.

- Experimental section is thorough but should include more recent baselines beyond LSIF and KMM.

- Recommended improvements: (1) add detailed comparison with KMM/related methods, (2) provide practical examples for Type II, (3) clarify theoretical contributions relative to minimax rates, (4) address or relax boundedness assumptions.

- Overall assessment: strong, meaningful contribution with rigorous theory and useful practical tools; with suggested revisions it would be well-positioned for NIPS.
