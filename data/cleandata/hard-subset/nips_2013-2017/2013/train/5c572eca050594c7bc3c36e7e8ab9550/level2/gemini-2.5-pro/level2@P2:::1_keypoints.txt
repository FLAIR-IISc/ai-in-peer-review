- Reformulates density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind with two settings: Type I (samples from both p and q) and Type II (q known analytically).  
- Solves the ill-posed inverse problems with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family.  
- FIRE algorithms have closed-form solutions expressed via standard kernel matrix operations, making them simple and non-iterative.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels in Euclidean spaces and on manifolds.  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), an unsupervised model selection procedure for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Effectively situates the work relative to prior methods (e.g., KMM, LSIF), drawing clear connections and distinctions.  
- High technical quality: clear, correct derivations and strong formal guarantees supported by experiments.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized; motivations, Type I/II distinction, and algorithmic formulas are clear and reproducible.  
- The Fredholm inverse formulation is highly original and provides a principled foundation beyond a notational change.  
- Significant practical impact: a flexible, theoretically-grounded alternative with computationally appealing closed-form solutions.  
- Experiments are thoughtfully designed, evaluating downstream tasks and the effectiveness of CD-CV.  
- Weakness: computational complexity O(n^3) due to n×n matrix inversion; should discuss scalable approximations (Nystrom, random features).  
- Weakness: omission of experimental comparison to KMM despite claimed advantages; a direct out-of-sample comparison is recommended.  
- Weakness: choice of CD-CV test functions (random linear, half-space) seems arbitrary; provide theoretical intuition or practical guidance for selecting U.
