- Proposes FIRE: framing density-ratio estimation r(x)=q(x)/p(x) as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind with two formulations (Type I and Type II).  
- Solves the inverse problem with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding closed-form representer-theorem solutions.  
- Framework supports multiple loss norms (L2,p, L2,q, mixtures) and an RKHS loss.  
- Provides theoretical finite-sample concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on R^d and compact manifolds, with explicit dependence on t, λ, n, m.  
- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised model-selection method validating density-ratio estimates by matching expectations of random test functions.  
- Empirically evaluated on regression and classification under covariate shift (CPUsmall, Kin8nm, USPS) against LSIF, KMM (where applicable), and a kernel-density baseline (TIKDE).  
- Connects and contrasts to prior work: covariate shift literature (Shimodaira, Sugiyama), LSIF/KLIEP, KMM, and RKHS inverse-problem/spectral regularization literature.  
- Type I RKHS-loss variant interpreted as a regularized form of KMM, clarifying operator identity in H.  
- Mathematical setup and operator-theoretic derivation are sound and clearly presented.  
- Optimization is well-posed with explicit solutions and natural out-of-sample evaluation.  
- Theoretical results are strong and cover both Euclidean and manifold settings.  
- Empirical results generally supportive but comparisons are limited in scope and scale.  
- Originality: novel and insightful framing of density-ratio estimation as an inverse problem with manifold-aware rates.  
- Strengths: unified operator-theoretic view, closed-form RKHS solutions, theoretical guarantees, flexible losses, CD-CV, and competitive empirical performance.  
- Key weakness: does not enforce nonnegativity or normalization of r, which can harm downstream weighting.  
- Scalability concern: kernel-matrix inversions imply O(n^3) time and O(n^2) memory; no discussion of scalable approximations.  
- CD-CV lacks theoretical guarantees and sensitivity analysis to choice/number of test functions U.  
- Experimental limitations: modest datasets, missing KLIEP and some KMM comparisons, and no runtime/memory metrics.  
- Practical guidance lacking on choosing kernel width t and regularization λ and behavior in low-density or unbounded ratio settings.  
- Essential camera-ready suggestions: enforce positivity/normalization (or report post-processing), expand comparisons to KMM/KLIEP and report runtime/memory, and provide practical guidance on t and λ.  
- Nice-to-have: sensitivity study of CD-CV, sketch scalable variants (Nyström, random features, iterative solvers), and clarify smoothness/boundedness assumptions and minimax rates.  
- Reasons to accept: strong conceptual contribution, solid theory including manifolds, practical algorithms, and promising empirical results.  
- Reasons to reject: limited experiments/comparisons, unaddressed scalability, and lack of theoretical backing for CD-CV.  
- Overall: thoughtful, technically solid paper that would benefit from modest empirical and practical additions to be a strong NIPS contribution.
