- Novel Fredholm equation/operator-theoretic formulation for density ratio estimation offers a fresh, principled perspective.  
- Theoretical guarantees (concentration bounds and convergence rates) are derived, including extensions to manifolds.  
- Introduction of CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method for model selection.  
- Clear writing, well-organized presentation, and easy-to-follow mathematical notation and algorithms.  
- Strong connections and comparisons to existing methods (KMM, LSIF) with extensive referencing.  
- Convergence rates rely on strong assumptions and may have limited practical impact, especially in high dimensions.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope; more datasets, higher-dimensional and complex distributions, and baseline tuning details are needed.  
- Computational complexity (matrix inversions and scalability) is not explicitly addressed and could be a bottleneck.  
- Some sections are repetitive and could be streamlined; a few derivations include unnecessary detail.  
- Assumption that qp lies in a Sobolev space is strong; robustness to assumption violations is not discussed.  
- For acceptance: novel theoretically sound approach, CD-CV is a valuable unsupervised contribution, and the paper is well-written.  
- Against acceptance: uncertain practical impact of theoretical results, limited empirical validation, and missing computational analysis.  
- Overall: solid, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address practical implications, computational/runtime/memory considerations, and expanded empirical evaluation.
