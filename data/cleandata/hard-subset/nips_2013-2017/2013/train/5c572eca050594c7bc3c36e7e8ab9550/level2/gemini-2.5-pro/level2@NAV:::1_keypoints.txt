- Reformulates density-ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind, casting it as a classical inverse problem.  
- Applies Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS to this formulation, deriving a flexible family of closed-form algorithms called FIRE.  
- Positions FIRE relative to prior methods (KMM, LSIF), framing it as a well-grounded, regularized alternative.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and manifolds.  
- Introduces Cross-Density Cross-Validation (CD-CV), an unsupervised, practical model-selection method.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and logically structured.  
- Experiments show strong performance but could be more comprehensive; direct empirical comparison to KMM is omitted from main results.  
- Omits discussion of limitations, notably computational complexity (inverting n×n kernel matrix scales cubically, harming scalability).  
- Overall: significant, elegant contribution with strong theory and practical tools; minor weaknesses do not undermine acceptance.
