- Reformulates density ratio estimation q/p as a Fredholm integral equation of the first kind using an integral operator with a kernel.

- Develops two settings: Type I (samples from both p and q) using Kp(q/p)=Kq(1), and Type II (q known, samples from p) using local kernel approximation Kt,p(q/p)≈q.

- Addresses ill-posedness with Tikhonov regularization in an RKHS, yielding the FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) family.

- Derives closed-form solutions as linear combinations of kernel functions centered at data points for easy implementation.

- Provides theoretical results: concentration bounds and convergence rates for Gaussian kernels on Euclidean spaces and smooth sub-manifolds.

- Introduces CD-CV, an unsupervised cross-density cross-validation method using the importance sampling identity for hyperparameter tuning.

- Empirical results show FIRE and CD-CV perform well compared to LSIF and a kernel density-based baseline.

- High technical quality: clear derivations, complete contribution spanning formulation, theory, algorithms, model selection, and experiments.

- Well-written and organized; recommends clarifying notation for multiple kernel matrices (e.g., with a summary table) and contextualizing convergence rates versus known minimax rates.

- Highly original: novel application of Fredholm integral framework and regularization to density ratio estimation, with natural out-of-sample extension and a new CD-CV procedure.

- Significant potential impact for covariate shift, transfer learning, and importance sampling due to theoretical grounding and practical simplicity.

- Experimental comparison could be strengthened by a more direct comparison to KMM, e.g., by fitting a regressor to KMM weights for out-of-sample evaluation.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled formulation, simple implementable closed-form algorithms, strong theoretical guarantees, and a practical unsupervised model-selection method.

- Arguments against acceptance: reviewer finds no major flaws; only minor clarity and experimental-comparison improvements suggested.
