- Frames density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and solves it via regularization (FIRE algorithm).
- Introduces a novel unsupervised model selection method (CD-CV).
- Provides theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates.
- Demonstrates empirical performance on multiple datasets, comparing to KMM and LSIF.
- Well-motivated connection to importance sampling and covariate shift applications.
- Reformulation via Fredholm equations is novel and enables operator-theoretic tools.
- RKHS regularization and derivation of FIRE algorithms are sound and appropriate.
- Theoretical guarantees are a significant strength, though technically complex.
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and shows competitive results.
- CD-CV addresses the challenging problem of unsupervised model selection.
- Notation is sometimes inconsistent (e.g., Kp vs Kt) and could be standardized.
- Theoretical presentation is dense and could be made more accessible to broader audiences.
- Lacks practical discussion of convergence rates (how many samples needed for target accuracy).
- Experimental details on hyperparameter tuning are insufficient (beyond t and λ ranges).
- No in-depth sensitivity analysis of performance to hyperparameters.
- Justification for chosen CD-CV test functions (linear and half-space) is weak and unexplained.
- Claims about KMM lacking out-of-sample extension are overstated and should be clarified.
- Computational complexity, especially matrix inversion costs for large datasets, is not discussed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, strong theory, thorough experiments, and CD-CV.
- Arguments against acceptance: dense theory, limited hyperparameter analysis, weak CD-CV justification, minor related-work inaccuracies, and missing computational-cost discussion.
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution and recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions to improve clarity and experimental/computational details.
