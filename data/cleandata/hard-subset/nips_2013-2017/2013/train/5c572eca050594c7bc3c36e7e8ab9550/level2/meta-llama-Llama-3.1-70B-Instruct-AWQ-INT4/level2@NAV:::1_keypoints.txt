- Proposes estimating density ratio q/p as an inverse problem via the Fredholm equation of the first kind.  
- Introduces FIRE (Fredholm Inverse Regularized Estimator) algorithms built with RKHS regularization.  
- Claims FIRE is flexible, simple, and easy to implement.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates for the Gaussian kernel.  
- Presents a principled framework for constructing algorithms using regularization techniques and RKHS.  
- Experimental results show effectiveness but are limited to a small number of datasets.  
- Assumes a Gaussian kernel; extensions to other kernels are left for future work.  
- Demonstrates strong field knowledge of kernel methods, regularization, and inverse problems.  
- Novel contribution: the Fredholm-equation formulation and FIRE algorithms are new.  
- Completeness: clear problem formulation, algorithmic framework, and detailed theoretical treatment.  
- Limitation: empirical evaluation is narrow and may not generalize across datasets.  
- Suggestion: include more datasets and more detailed comparisons with existing density-ratio methods.  
- Pro acceptance: novel, principled approach with simple, practical algorithms and solid theory.  
- Con acceptance: limited experiments and insufficient empirical comparison to baselines.  
- Overall: well-written and significant contribution, but needs broader empirical validation.
