- Proposes a novel formulation of density ratio estimation as a Fredholm integral equation and solves it via operator-theoretic regularization.  
- Introduces the FIRE algorithms derived from this inverse-problem perspective.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including concentration bounds and convergence rates (notably for Gaussian kernels).  
- Uses Tikhonov regularization; the mathematical derivation and justification are sound.  
- Positions the work correctly within covariate shift and importance sampling literature, with accurate references to KMM, LSIF, etc.  
- The connection to KMM is insightful, clarifying differences in optimization and regularization.  
- Proposes an unsupervised model selection method (CD-CV), a useful practical contribution.  
- Empirical results show effectiveness but lack comparisons to all relevant baselines and broader dataset coverage.  
- Theoretical guarantees rely on restrictive assumptions (smoothness, bounded densities).  
- Limited discussion of computational complexity and the cost of kernel matrix inversion in high dimensions.  
- Notation is dense and operator-theory background may hinder accessibility for non-experts.  
- Initial sections would benefit from more intuitive explanations of the Fredholm formulation.  
- Section 2 (algorithms) is generally clear but could be more concise; connections between formulations (Type I/II, norms) need clarity.  
- Experimental setup and resampling descriptions need more detail for reproducibility.  
- Originality is strong: applies Fredholm equations and regularization to the problem rather than re-packaging existing methods.  
- Potential significance is substantial if scalability and empirical performance on complex datasets are demonstrated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel inverse-problem formulation, strong theory, practical algorithms, CD-CV, comprehensive related work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive theoretical assumptions, limited complexity discussion, incomplete empirical comparisons, dense notation, weaker link from theory to practical performance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational complexity, clarity/intuitions, and expanded experimental evaluation.
