- Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm for learning graphical-model structure from non-stationary time series by tracking weighted sufficient statistics and using a pooled p-value from Mahalanobis distances to detect shifts.  
- On change detection, LoSST up-weights recent data and probabilistically retriggers a standard structure learner to infer the new model.  
- Evaluated against a batch baseline on synthetic and real financial data; experiments show LoSST adapts to abrupt changes and outperforms a stationary batch learner.  
- Strength: addresses the practical and important problem of learning from dynamic, non-stationary data streams.  
- Originality is questionable—prior work (e.g., Siracusa & Fisher 2009) already addresses time-varying graphical structures via a Bayesian approach.  
- Related work is misleadingly framed; many score-based methods use locally decomposable scores and can be adapted to online settings.  
- Clarity is poor: the paper fails to define whether the core task is change-point detection or structure inference, and whether it introduces a new model or just an algorithmic wrapper.  
- Presentation problems include confusing punctuation, an unclear definition of the pooled p-value ρ_{r+1}, and low-quality figures with tiny fonts and inadequate legends.  
- Contribution appears modest and heuristic-heavy; the p-value construction is hard to follow and justify.  
- The claim that better tracking offsets computational costs is unsubstantiated and likely domain-dependent.  
- Overall recommendation: reads as a heuristic solution that lacks sufficient novelty and clarity; reviewer does not recommend acceptance.
