- Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical model structure in locally stationary environments that outperforms batch methods when structure changes occur.  
- Fills an important gap versus standard structure learning approaches that assume i.i.d. data from a single, stationary model.  
- Technically sound: tracks sufficient statistics online using Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values, with dynamic relearning and solid convergence vs. diligence theory.  
- The claim that LoSST would perform similarly with “any other standard” structure learning algorithm is too strong and unsubstantiated.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, thorough algorithm description, and effective illustrative figures.  
- Real-world case study on US price index volatility demonstrates practical relevance and interpretable economic insights.  
- Original: provides a general, real-time solution for time-varying graphical model learning distinct from changepoint or time-indexed methods.  
- Significant for real-world, non-stationary data streams because it processes each datapoint once with minimal overhead and maintains performance in stable periods.  
- Strength: well-motivated, theoretically grounded approach with promising experimental gains in non-stationary settings.  
- Strength: clear exposition and a compelling real-world application.  
- Weakness: Scope is effectively limited to SEMs but this limitation is not clearly stated up front.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited to the PC algorithm and lacks comparisons to other standard methods (e.g., score-based approaches).  
- Weakness: Generalization claims to other algorithms lack empirical or theoretical support.  
- Pro-acceptance: addresses an under-studied, important problem with strong theory, clear writing, and compelling experiments.  
- Against-acceptance: SEM-specific scope unclear, limited baselines, and unsupported generality claim.  
- Recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to explicitly state SEM scope and justify or expand experimental baselines.
