- Proposes LoSST, an online hypothesis-testing method to learn changing graphical models from time-series data.  
- Claim of novelty disputed because Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handled non-iid data and varying generating structures with a Bayesian approach.  
- Paper is well-organized and explains the algorithm and components clearly.  
- Related-work discussion is misleading; many score-based Bayesian network methods do not assume a single generating model and use factorized/local scores.  
- Paper clarity suffers from confusing paragraph/equation punctuation and unclear definition of ρ_{r+1}.  
- Figures (1–4) have small fonts and lack color or clear legend descriptions, reducing interpretability.  
- Results appear correct, but the claim that improved tracking justifies extra computational cost is unclear and domain-dependent.  
- Originality is limited: approach is somewhat original but lacks clear novel ideas and the p-value construction is hard to follow.  
- Significance is modest; work reads as heuristic development and existing ML methods might address the problem.  
- For acceptance: novel online hypothesis-testing approach, clear algorithm exposition, simulation and real-world results included.  
- Against acceptance: disputed novelty, misleading related work, clarity and figure problems, and modest significance.  
- Recommend clarifying novelty and addressing related work more accurately.  
- Recommend improving clarity by fixing punctuation, definitions, and figure design.  
- Recommend providing stronger evidence of significance and impact.  
- Recommend revising to better align with conference focus on novel and significant contributions.
