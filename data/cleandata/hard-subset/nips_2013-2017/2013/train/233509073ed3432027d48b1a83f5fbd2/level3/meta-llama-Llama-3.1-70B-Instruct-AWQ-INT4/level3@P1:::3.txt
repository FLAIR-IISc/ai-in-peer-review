**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure" proposes a novel, heuristic structure learning algorithm called LoSST (Locally Stationary Structure Tracker) that tracks changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner. The algorithm assumes that the generating process is locally stationary, meaning that the data are i.i.d. at each time point, but the underlying structure can change over time. LoSST uses a combination of online learning and changepoint detection techniques to track the sufficient statistics of the graphical model and relearn the structure as necessary.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds on previous work in graphical model structure learning, online learning, and changepoint detection. The authors acknowledge the limitations of standard graphical model structure learning algorithms, which assume that the data come from a single generating structure. They also discuss the limitations of online learning algorithms, which can detect changes in the learning environment but are not capable of general, graphical model structure learning.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The algorithm is carefully designed to balance the trade-off between convergence and diligence, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's behavior in different parameter regimes. The experimental results are promising, showing that LoSST can successfully learn graphical model structure in an online manner and outperform a standard batch-mode structure learning algorithm in certain scenarios.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, and the algorithm is described in detail. The experimental results are presented in a clear and organized manner, making it easy to understand the performance of the algorithm.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel algorithm that addresses an important and under-studied problem in graphical model structure learning. The combination of online learning and changepoint detection techniques is original and effective in tracking time-varying graphical structures.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for applications where batch-mode structure learning is infeasible or inappropriate, such as in real-time data analysis or dynamic systems. The algorithm has the potential to perform well in a range of challenging contexts, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in a real-world case study.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

**Pro:**

* The paper addresses an important and under-studied problem in graphical model structure learning.
* The algorithm is novel and effective in tracking time-varying graphical structures.
* The experimental results are promising, showing that LoSST can outperform a standard batch-mode structure learning algorithm in certain scenarios.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

**Con:**

* The claim that "other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" is too strong and requires further justification.
* The experimental results are limited to a PC-method comparison and may not generalize to other algorithms.
* The extension of the approach to Bayesian networks may be nontrivial due to difficulties maintaining sufficient statistics.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

* Provide further justification or clarification for the claim that "other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly."
* Consider extending the experimental results to include comparisons with other algorithms.
* Clearly state the SEM-specific scope of the paper and note that extensions to Bayesian networks may not be straightforward.
