- Proposes LoSST, an online hypothesis-testing algorithm to track changing graphical models from time-series data.
- Novelty is disputed because Siracusa and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid data and varying generating structures.
- Empirical results appear correct and demonstrate effectiveness on simulated and real datasets.
- Related-work discussion is misleading; many score-based Bayesian network methods do not assume a single generating model.
- Oversight of prior literature suggests an incomplete or shallow literature review.
- The precise learning task is unclear: change-point detection versus inferring the current generating structure.
- Ambiguity whether the contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.
- Presentation suffers from confusing punctuation, unclear definition of ρ_{r+1}, and general readability problems.
- Figures (1–4) have small fonts and lack color/legend descriptions, reducing interpretability.
- Claim that improved tracking justifies extra computational cost is vague and seems domain-dependent, needing evidence.
- Originality is limited; the p-value construction is hard to follow and its novelty is not evident.
- Significance is modest; the work reads as heuristic and existing ML methods might already address the problem.
- Overall technically sound but weakened by unclear contributions, presentation issues, and limited novelty.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 6/10, Clarity 5/10, Originality 4/10, Significance 5/10.
- Pros: novel online approach and validated results; Cons: disputed novelty, misleading related work, lack of clear novel ideas, and presentation issues.
