**Review of "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure"**

**Summary**

This paper presents a novel algorithm, LoSST, for tracking changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner. The authors address the problem of learning graphical model structure in situations where the underlying generating process is only locally stationary, meaning that the data are not globally stationary. The proposed algorithm uses a combination of techniques, including online learning, changepoint detection, and structure learning, to track changes in the graphical model structure.

**Strengths**

* The paper tackles an important and under-explored problem in the field of graphical models.
* The proposed algorithm is novel, practical, and computationally efficient.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of the algorithm using synthetic data and a real-world dataset.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the algorithm and its components.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper lacks a theoretical analysis or guarantees for the algorithm's performance.
* The algorithm relies heavily on heuristics, which may not be well-justified in certain situations.
* The use of Hotelling's T2 and Liptak's method for pooling p-values may not be theoretically justified in this context.
* The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the algorithm's components and their relationships.

**Originality**

* The paper presents a novel algorithm for tracking changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner.
* The authors combine techniques from online learning, changepoint detection, and structure learning in a creative way.

**Significance**

* The paper addresses an important problem in the field of graphical models, which has significant implications for real-world applications.
* The proposed algorithm has the potential to be widely applicable in situations where the underlying generating process is only locally stationary.

**Suggestions for improvement**

* Provide a theoretical analysis or guarantees for the algorithm's performance.
* Justify the use of heuristics and provide more detailed explanations of the algorithm's components and their relationships.
* Consider using alternative methods for pooling p-values or detecting changepoints.
* Provide more detailed explanations of the algorithm's behavior in different scenarios.

**Rating**

* Quality: 7/10
* Clarity: 8/10
* Originality: 9/10
* Significance: 9/10

**Recommendation**

* Accept with minor revisions.
* The authors should address the concerns mentioned above and provide more detailed explanations of the algorithm's components and their relationships.
* The paper has the potential to be a significant contribution to the field of graphical models, and with some revisions, it can be even stronger.
