- Problem: Detecting and adapting to changes in dependency structure for SEMs over a data stream is important and under-explored.  
- Method: LoSST tracks weighted sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size) where new points are weighted by Mahalanobis “surprisingness.”  
- Testing/aggregation: Individual MD p-values use Hotelling’s T2, pooled via Liptak’s method into a significance statistic ρ, with a noisy-OR accumulator to trigger batch PC relearning.  
- Experiments: Empirical comparisons to vanilla PC on synthetic parameter and structure shifts across graph sizes/degrees, plus a US price-index real-data case (529 monthly points, six variables).  
- Positioning: Positioned against online changepoint detection and time-varying graphical models, emphasizing real-time, general SEM structure tracking.  
- Empirical quality: Experiments are adequate, include parameter sensitivity, and the real-data example is informative.  
- Heuristic design: Algorithm relies heavily on heuristics and applies statistical tools in ways that depart from their standard guarantees.  
- Statistical concerns: Hotelling’s T2 with weighted/effective sample size and Liptak pooling (assuming independence) likely violate assumptions under serial dependence and adaptive weighting—need explicit discussion and calibration checks.  
- Convergence/discussion: “Convergence vs. diligence” discussion is interesting but lacks rigorous conditions; proofs in footnotes reduce readability.  
- Clarity/presentation: Writing and literature review are solid, but figures are small/under-labeled, captions terse, and some experimental details are insufficient.  
- Originality: Novel, pragmatic integration of MD, Hotelling’s T2, Liptak pooling, and PC into a streaming structure-tracker for SEMs.  
- Significance: Practical and potentially impactful, likely to spur follow-up work on online structure learning.  
- Suggested improvements: Add theoretical discussion or clearly flag heuristics, include additional baselines (sliding-window PC, CPD+relearn, kernel CD), address edge-addition underperformance via decoupling updates or two-tier triggers, and improve figures/experimental detail.  
- Pros: Addresses an important problem, offers a novel and efficient design, includes sensible experiments, and is well written.  
- Cons: Lacks theoretical guarantees, risks miscalibrated statistical testing and independence assumptions, and has presentation/experimental-description issues.
