- Introduces LoSST, a heuristic algorithm for online structure learning of graphical models under non-stationary data streams.
- Tracks weighted sufficient statistics (mean and covariance) online, giving more weight to recent data when change is detected.
- Detects change using Mahalanobis distances pooled via Liptak’s method to be robust to outliers and sensitive to persistent shifts.
- Triggers probabilistic relearning of graph structure by feeding updated statistics into a batch learner (PC algorithm).
- Correctly identifies a gap in the literature for online structure learning as distinct from batch learners and online parameter trackers.

Strengths:
- Addresses an important and under-studied problem enabling structure learning in dynamic environments.
- Elegant, principled approach combining weighted sufficient statistics with pooled p-value change detection and theoretical analysis of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off.
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation with modular algorithmic components and update equations.
- Empirical validation shows parity with batch PC on stable data and superior performance under changing structure; convincing real-world demo on US price indices.

Weaknesses:
- Overstates generalizability; claims about other structure learners working similarly (e.g., score-based methods) are unsupported.
- Limited scope: focused on continuous SEMs; extending to discrete/Bayesian networks has substantial computational and memory challenges that are under-acknowledged.
- Experimental comparison is narrow, lacking baselines like sliding-window PC or segmentation via formal changepoint methods.

Evaluation:
- Quality: technically sound for SEMs/PC with clear derivations and useful theoretical insights, but weakened by unsupported generality claims.
- Clarity: excellently written and easy to follow.
- Originality: highly original combination of techniques for online structure learning.
- Significance: high potential impact for dynamic domains and a strong foundation for further work.

Summary:
- Strong, novel, and well-motivated paper with correctable issues around overstated generality and limited experimental baselines.
