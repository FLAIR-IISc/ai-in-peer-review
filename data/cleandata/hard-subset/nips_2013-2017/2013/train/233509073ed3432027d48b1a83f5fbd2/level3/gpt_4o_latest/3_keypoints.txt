- Tackles important, underexplored problem: structure learning for SEMs in time-dependent, locally i.i.d. data rather than globally stationary settings.  
- Proposes LoSST, a novel heuristic for online structure learning that updates sufficient statistics and adapts to changing data-generating structures.  
- Fills a methodological gap and could inspire further work on dynamic graphical models.  
- Manuscript is well-written, organized, and makes technical content accessible.  
- Clear explanation of LoSST’s theory and practical implementation using the PC algorithm.  
- Experiments show LoSST effectively detects and adapts to structural changes in synthetic and real-world data.  
- Comparison with batch-mode learning highlights LoSST’s advantages in dynamic environments while remaining comparable in stable settings.  
- Claim that other standard structure-learning algorithms would perform similarly is overgeneralized and lacks evidence.  
- Limitation about extending the approach to Bayesian networks (sufficient-statistics challenges) is only mentioned late and should be disclosed earlier.  
- Framing should explicitly acknowledge challenges and potential extensions to other graphical models (Bayesian networks, MRFs).  
- Experimental validation is limited by relying solely on the PC method and a single real-world dataset; broader algorithmic and dataset evaluation is needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to temper claims, disclose limitations earlier, and expand experimental validation.
