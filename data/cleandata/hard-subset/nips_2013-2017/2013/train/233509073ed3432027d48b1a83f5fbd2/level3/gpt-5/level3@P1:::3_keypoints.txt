- Introduces LoSST: an online heuristic that tracks time-varying graphical structure in linear-Gaussian SEMs via weighted sufficient statistics and per-datum weights ar.
- Weights rise when recent data are unlikely under the current model using Mahalanobis distances pooled across time with Liptak’s method.
- When pooled p-value drops, LoSST increases forgetting and probabilistically triggers PC-based graph relearning.
- Maintains online weighted means, covariances, and an “effective” sample size with O(p^2) state and single-pass updates.
- Connects to score/constraint-based structure learning, TD/EWMA forgetting, online change-point detection, and batch time-indexed structure learning.
- Simulations show parity with batch PC under stationarity and markedly better performance after parameter or graph changes.
- Includes a real-world case study on US price index volatility.
- Provides a discussion of convergence vs. diligence with conditions for each, though some proofs are sketchy.
- Algorithmic components are simple, computationally lightweight, and clearly described.
- Use of Liptak pooled p-values for sustained nonstationarity detection is well motivated.
- Main empirical concern: CI test calibration under weighted covariances and an “effective” N is not justified.
- Recommends empirically validating CI calibration (Type I/II error, coverage) or using CI tests robust to weighting.
- Sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters T, γ, ν are limited and tuning reported is unsystematic.
- Missing stronger baselines: sliding-window PC, EWMA-PC with fixed forgetting, BOCPD+batch relearn, and periodic relearn baselines.
- Computational cost per step and wall-clock/runtime comparisons are not reported.
- Clarity is good, but the paper should state up front that the implemented method targets continuous linear-Gaussian SEMs with PC.
- Extensions to discrete Bayesian networks are nontrivial due to sufficient-statistics and CI-test differences.
- The “effective sample size” update needs more intuition and justification; specify which CI tests and significance levels are used with weighted covariances.
- Numerical stability concerns: discuss covariance regularization/shrinkage and inversion safeguards when effective N is small.
- Originality: novel combination of pooled Mahalanobis p-values, online sufficient-statistics tracking, and probabilistic relearning for online constraint-based learning.
- Significance: addresses an important problem for streaming nonstationary structure learning but empirical scope is narrow (PC-only).
- Strengths summarized: important problem, modular/simple method, clear tradeoff analysis, promising simulations, and readable presentation.
- Weaknesses/suggestions summarized: tone down generality claims, explicitly state SEM scope, justify CI calibration, add stronger baselines, perform systematic sensitivity sweeps, test robustness to non-Gaussianity/outliers, and report runtime.
- Acceptance arguments: novel, practical, simple, likely adoptable, and demonstrates improvements post-change.
- Rejection arguments: limited empirical evaluation (PC-only), missing calibration checks, and partly sketched theoretical claims.
- Overall stance: recommend weaker acceptance contingent on clearer scope, added baselines, calibration checks, and more systematic sensitivity/runtime analyses.
