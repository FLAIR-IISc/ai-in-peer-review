- Introduces LoSST: an online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical model structure under local stationarity by updating sufficient statistics and triggering probabilistic relearning.  
- Addresses gap where existing methods assume global stationarity, targeting real-world dynamic environments.  

Strengths:
- Novel, well-motivated contribution to an under-studied, practically relevant problem.  
- Elegant, computationally efficient core idea using Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values.  
- Simple, low-overhead implementation that avoids prohibitive costs in stable regimes.  
- Clear, well-organized writing with explicit update rules and trade-off analysis.  
- Thorough experiments showing parity with batch methods under global stationarity and superiority under local stationarity, plus a real-world US price index case study.  
- Honest discussion of limitations and future extensions.

Weaknesses:
- Overclaims general applicability; experiments only use PC algorithm, so claim that any structure learner would perform similarly is unsupported.  
- LoSST is effectively SEM-specific; extending to discrete Bayesian networks requires nontrivial changes (e.g., CPT sufficient statistics).  
- Lacks broader benchmarking against other online/incremental structure-learning methods.  
- Real-world validation limited to a single economic dataset, reducing demonstrated generalizability.

Originality and significance:
- Highly original focus on online structure learning for locally stationary data, distinct from batch or change-point methods.  
- Significant practical impact due to minimal overhead in stable environments and applicability to finance, healthcare, and monitoring.

Quality and clarity:
- Strong theoretical analysis of convergence vs. diligence and well-powered experiments (500 runs/condition).  
- Excellently clear presentation enabling reproducibility.  
- Minor gaps: limited justification of parameter choices and missing runtime complexity analysis for large graphs.

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revision: explicitly state LoSST’s SEM-specific scope in abstract/introduction and clarify that comparisons are limited to PC due to methodological constraints.
