- Proposes LoSST, a novel heuristic for online structure learning of SEMs in locally stationary environments.  
- Core idea: track sufficient statistics online and dynamically relearn the graphical model to handle time-dependent data.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains the algorithm and theory step-by-step.  
- Simulations show LoSST outperforms batch learning in non-stationary settings, handling both parameter and structure changes.  
- Theoretical discussion addresses the trade-off between convergence and diligence in changing environments.  
- Demonstrates a real-world application to US price index volatility, illustrating practical utility.  
- Situates work within graphical model and online learning literature and highlights limitations of prior time-series methods that require full datasets.  
- Strength: tackles an important, understudied problem with a practical online algorithm.  
- Strength: effective experimental results that convincingly show tracking of changing structures.  
- Weakness: overstates generalizability—claim that other standard structure learners would behave similarly is unsupported beyond PC.  
- Weakness: scope is SEM-specific; extending to other graphical model classes (e.g., discrete Bayesian networks) may be non-trivial.  
- Weakness: limited experimental evaluation—comparison only to PC and limited varieties of structural change.  
- Recommendation for acceptance: solid contribution, clear exposition, promising empirical and theoretical results, real-world demo.  
- Reasons against acceptance: need to qualify generalizability, explicitly state SEM-specific scope, and broaden experimental comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor issues addressable via further experiments and clearer scope statements; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
