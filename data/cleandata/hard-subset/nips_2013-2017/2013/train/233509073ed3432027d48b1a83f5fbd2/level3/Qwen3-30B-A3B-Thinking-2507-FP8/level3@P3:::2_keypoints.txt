- Addresses online detection and adaptation to changing dependency structure in streaming data, relaxing global stationarity assumptions.  
- Proposes LoSST: an online tracker maintaining weighted sufficient statistics and triggering batch PC learning on detected shifts.  
- Uses Mahalanobis distance (Hotelling’s T²), pools p-values with Liptak’s method into statistic ρ, and noisy-OR to probabilistically decide relearning.  
- Novel and significant: first practical approach for real-time structure tracking in SEMs without exponential memory.  
- Advantages over prior work: handles local stationarity and avoids large data buffers required by Bayesian changepoint methods.  
- Experiments (synthetic): shows LoSST matches batch PC in stable regimes and outperforms during structural shifts across variable sizes/degrees.  
- Real-data validation: detects the “Great Moderation” in US price-index data and avoids false alarms from short volatility spikes.  
- Sensitivity analysis: evaluates T and γ parameters and shows robustness to infrequent probabilistic relearning.  
- Major weakness: no theoretical guarantees; Hotelling’s T² and Liptak pooling violate independence/i.i.d. assumptions and lack justification.  
- Missing analysis on how assumption violations affect convergence/detection rates and validity of pooled ρ as a p-value.  
- Heuristic-heavy design with unexplained choices (e.g., T=0.05, γ=3, Eq. (4) weighting) and insufficient ablation studies.  
- Fundamental methodological flaw: parameter-only shifts sometimes trigger structural relearning; authors should decouple parameter and structure updates.  
- Presentation issues: poorly formatted figures and misplaced footnotes/proofs reduce clarity; proofs should be moved to supplementary material.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept conditional on major revisions addressing theoretical gaps, heuristic justification, presentation, and explicit statistical limitations.  
- Required revisions: state statistical limitations explicitly; provide ablation studies and decoupling for parameter/structure updates; improve figures and move proofs; add discussion of theoretical limitations and future convergence analysis.
