- Proposes LoSST, a novel algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical structure in locally stationary environments.  
- Tracks sufficient statistics online (means, covariances, sample size) and uses Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values to detect changes.  
- Dynamically triggers structure learning via the PC algorithm when changes are detected.  
- Well-motivated and clearly written, addressing an under-studied problem of online structure learning for time-dependent data.  
- Provides a clear theoretical foundation for handling locally stationary data.  
- Experimental results: LoSST matches batch learning in stable settings and outperforms in time-varying scenarios.  
- Thoughtful analysis of the convergence–diligence tradeoff.  
- Simulations show change detection within ~250 datapoints; real-world application to US price index volatility shows practical relevance.  
- Overstated claim that “other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly” without justification.  
- Experimental evaluation limited to PC comparisons, weakening generalizability.  
- Scope should be explicitly limited to SEMs; extensions to Bayesian networks may be nontrivial due to difficulty maintaining sufficient statistics.  
- Overall: technically sound, well-written, and impactful, but should temper generalization claims and broaden experimental validation.
