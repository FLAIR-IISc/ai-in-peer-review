- Proposes LoSST: an online heuristic for tracking time-varying DAG structure in linear-Gaussian SEMs using weighted sufficient statistics and pooled p-values to adapt forgetting and relearning.

- Core mechanism: per-datum weights updated by pooled p-values of recent Mahalanobis distances; low pooled p-values increase weight on recent data and raise probability of relearning structure via PC.

- Authors analyze a convergence–diligence trade-off controlled by parameters T and γ and provide simulations with structural and parameter changes plus a CPI case study showing known regime shifts.

- Relation-to-prior-work claims are overstated; prior works (e.g., Siracusa & Fisher, time-varying GGMs/DBNs, windowed/forgetting-factor methods) already address similar problems.

- Strength: tackles a practically important locally-stationary setting with changing structure and parameters.

- Strength: simple, lightweight updates (means, covariances, effective sample size) and an intuitive pooled p-value change detector.

- Strength: clear articulation of convergence–diligence trade-off as a useful conceptual contribution.

- Strength: simulations show competitive stationary performance and faster adaptation post-change; CPI case study supports pooled p-value robustness.

- Strength: probabilistic relearning schedule reduces computational cost with limited accuracy loss.

- Weakness: limited novelty — LoSST assembles known components (EWMA, Mahalanobis distances, Liptak pooling, PC relearning).

- Weakness: misleading related-work framing that oversimplifies score-based learners’ adaptability via windows/discounting or CPD.

- Weakness: underspecified learning objective — unclear whether goal is CPD, current-graph estimation, or joint tracking.

- Weakness: clarity issues around notation/indexing (ρr, pr, Sr), circularity footnote, and punctuation near equations.

- Weakness: methodological gaps — no covariance regularization for small Sr/large p, no calibration of false-alarm trade-offs, limited sensitivity analysis, and reliance on Gaussianity without robustness checks.

- Weakness: missing runtime results and weak baselines (only batch PC); should compare to sliding-window PC, CPD+relearn, forgetting-factor score-based search, time-varying graphical lasso.

- Quality: components are correctly assembled but theory is informal and practical issues (regularization, CI-test calibration, multiple testing) are unaddressed.

- Quality: simulations are sensible but comparative evaluation, hyperparameter tuning, ablations, and sensitivity studies are limited.

- Clarity: overall readable but requires precise definitions, clearer indexing, and improved figures (fonts, legends, color palettes).

- Originality: moderate — online pooled p-value modulation is a reasonable twist but built from well-known parts and overlaps prior work.

- Significance: modest — method is practical but heuristic and does not clearly outperform strong adaptive baselines.

- Suggested improvements: clarify task and metrics (detection vs structure accuracy), precisely define ρr and pooling choice, add covariance regularization, conduct sensitivity analyses and principled tuning, expand baselines and runtime reporting, and fix presentation issues.

- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important online setting with a simple practical method, promising empirical behavior, and an insightful convergence–diligence framing.

- Arguments against acceptance: novelty overstated, incomplete related work, heuristic method with limited theory/statistical calibration, weak baselines, and presentation problems.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject — interesting idea but needs clearer positioning, stronger baselines, sharper definitions, and better empirical/statistical grounding.
