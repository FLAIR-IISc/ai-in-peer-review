- Tackles important problem: online structure learning for non-stationary graphical models, which is under-explored and practically relevant.  
- Proposes LoSST: an online heuristic that tracks SEM structure and parameters using weighted sufficient statistics.  
- Uses Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values (Liptak's method) to detect model misfit and trigger batch relearning with PC algorithm.  
- Maintains computational and memory efficiency by processing each data point once.  
- Empirical results: performs comparably to batch PC in stationary settings and outperforms it when structure changes; includes illustrative real-world case study.  
- Well-written motivation and related work positioning that clarify the contribution.  
- Major weakness: heavily heuristic with no theoretical guarantees on convergence or change-detection rates.  
- Statistical validity concerns: Hotelling’s T-squared may be inappropriate using a weighted “effective sample size.”  
- Statistical validity concerns: Liptak’s p-value pooling assumes independence, violated by temporally correlated, model-dependent tests.  
- Many algorithmic components are ad-hoc (effective sample size update, weight update, noisy-OR trigger) without justification or ablation.  
- Presentation issues: small/poorly labeled figures and imprecise experimental descriptions (e.g., fixed relearning schedule).  
- Explanation for poorer performance on edge removals after parameter changes is unconvincing and needs deeper analysis or structural/parameter decoupling.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical approach; clever synthesis of tools; efficient online applicability; promising empirical evidence.  
- Arguments for rejection: statistical soundness concerns, heuristic/ad-hoc design, lack of theoretical analysis, and presentation shortcomings.  
- Overall: interesting and practical first attempt, but significant methodological and theoretical weaknesses must be addressed before stronger endorsement.
