

This paper addresses an important yet under-explored problem in graphical model learning: detecting changes in dependency structure over data streams for Structural Equation Models (SEMs). The authors propose LoSST, a practical algorithm that tracks locally stationary sufficient statistics (weighted means, covariance, effective sample size) and triggers batch PC structure learning when significant changes are detected. The approach creatively combines Mahalanobis distance-based "surprisingness" with Liptak's p-value pooling and a noisy-OR relearning criterion.

The paper makes a valuable contribution by filling a significant gap in online structure learning literature. Unlike previous methods that assume global stationarity or require batch processing, LoSST operates in real-time on streaming data while maintaining comparable performance to batch methods during stable periods. The authors demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness through comprehensive synthetic experiments and a compelling real-world application to US price-index data, where it successfully detects known economic shifts while ignoring short-term fluctuations.

Strengths include the algorithm's novelty, computational efficiency, and thorough evaluation framework. The literature review is well-executed, and the writing is clear despite the technical complexity. The paper properly situates its work within the broader context of online learning and changepoint detection.

However, several critical weaknesses undermine the scientific contribution. The work lacks theoretical analysis or guarantees regarding convergence, detection, or statistical validity, particularly concerning the application of Hotelling's T² to weighted effective sample sizes. The heuristic-heavy design (e.g., reweighting scheme, effective sample size calculation, relearn criteria) lacks sufficient justification, and the paper's statistical methods appear misapplied without acknowledging these limitations. The presentation suffers from poor figure quality, excessive footnotes for proofs, and unclear explanations of certain results (e.g., LoSST's poorer performance on edge additions during parameter-only changes).

While the paper presents an interesting first attempt at online structure tracking, it requires significant theoretical development, clearer justification of heuristic choices, and improved presentation to be considered a strong contribution to the field. The authors should explicitly acknowledge where methods are used outside their theoretical guarantees and move detailed proofs to supplementary material.
