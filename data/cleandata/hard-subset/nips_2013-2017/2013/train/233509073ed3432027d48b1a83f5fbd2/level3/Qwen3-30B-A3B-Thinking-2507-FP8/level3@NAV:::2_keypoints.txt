Addresses detecting changes in dependency structure for SEMs in data streams, an important under-explored problem.

Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm that tracks locally stationary sufficient statistics and triggers batch PC learning on detected changes.

Combines Mahalanobis-distance “surprisingness” with Liptak p-value pooling and a noisy-OR relearning criterion.

Fills a gap vs prior methods by enabling real-time structure learning without assuming global stationarity or constant batch processing.

Demonstrates comparable performance to batch methods during stable periods and effective change detection in synthetic experiments.

Shows a compelling real-world application on US price-index data, detecting known economic shifts while ignoring short-term noise.

Strengths: novel approach, computational efficiency, thorough evaluation, good literature review, and generally clear writing.

Major weakness: lacks theoretical analysis or guarantees (convergence, detection, statistical validity), especially for applying Hotelling’s T² to weighted effective sample sizes.

Design is heuristic-heavy (reweighting, effective sample size, relearn criteria) with insufficient justification and potential misapplication of statistical methods.

Presentation issues: poor figure quality, excessive footnotes for proofs, and unclear explanations for some empirical results (e.g., poor edge-addition performance).

Recommendations: develop theoretical foundations, explicitly acknowledge where methods exceed theoretical guarantees, justify heuristic choices, and move detailed proofs to supplementary material.
