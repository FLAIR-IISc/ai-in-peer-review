- Addresses learning graphical model structure under local (non-stationary) conditions rather than i.i.d. assumptions.  
- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic online algorithm to track changing graphical structure and parameters.  
- Novel use of sufficient-statistic tracking (means, covariances, sample size) with differential weighting for dynamic data.  
- Integrates sufficient-statistic tracking with the PC algorithm for practical online structure learning.  
- Well-motivated, clearly explained, and described with enough detail for reproducibility.  
- Thoughtful analysis of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off in dynamic settings.  
- Simulation results show LoSST matches batch methods in stable regimes and outperforms them when structure changes.  
- Real-world application (U.S. price index volatility) provides a compelling, if brief, demonstration.  
- Builds on relevant literature in graphical models, online learning, and changepoint detection.  
- Unsupported/overstated claim that other standard structure learning algorithms would perform similarly to PC.  
- Heavy focus on recursive SEMs; the SEM-specific scope should be stated explicitly.  
- Extension to Bayesian/discrete models is nontrivial—tracking sufficient statistics for discrete variables is challenging.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: mainly compared to PC and lacks scenarios like gradual drift or frequent structural changes.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify SEM scope and justify or temper the generalizability claim.
