- Paper applies classification-based modified policy iteration (CBMPI) to Tetris to test a conjecture about policies vs. value functions.  
- Conjecture: good policies are easier to represent and learn than their value functions, so policy-space ADP should outperform value-space ADP.  
- Experimental comparison includes CBMPI, cross-entropy (CE) method, and λ-policy iteration (λ-PI).  
- CBMPI outperforms other ADP algorithms (including λ-PI) and matches CE performance with far fewer samples.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with detailed algorithm descriptions and implementation details.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and includes analysis and comparisons of best learned policies.  
- Results are reproducible due to detailed implementation descriptions.  
- Technical soundness: claims are well-supported and the work is carefully evaluated.  
- Originality: novel application of CBMPI to Tetris and new insights into ADP performance in this domain.  
- Significance: addresses an important ADP problem and could impact algorithm development and applications.  
- Main weakness: focus is narrow—primarily evaluates CBMPI in Tetris without broader analysis of algorithm strengths/weaknesses.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion on generalization and potential difficulties of applying the approach to other domains.  
- Recommendation: accept—paper is a solid, technically sound contribution with strong experiments and clear presentation.
