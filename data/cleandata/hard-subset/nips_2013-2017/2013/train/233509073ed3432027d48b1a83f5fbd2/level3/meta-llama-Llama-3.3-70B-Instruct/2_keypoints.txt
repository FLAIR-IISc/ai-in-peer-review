- Applies classification-based modified policy iteration (CBMPI) to Tetris, achieving state-of-the-art results on small and large boards.  
- Empirical evidence supports the conjecture that Tetris policies are easier to represent and learn than corresponding value functions.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making methodology and arguments easy to follow.  
- Tackles an under-explored problem in approximate dynamic programming (ADP) and reinforcement learning (RL).  
- Proposes a novel and practical algorithmic approach by searching in policy space rather than value-function space.  
- Provides adequate experiments and a solid review of prior work demonstrating CBMPI’s effectiveness.  
- Main weakness: lack of theoretical guarantees for the CBMPI approach.  
- Reliance on heuristics and use of some methods outside their original contexts raise robustness concerns.  
- Presentation of figures and experimental details could be improved for clarity and readability.  
- Authors should clarify algorithmic decisions such as feature choice and hyperparameter selection.  
- Supplementary materials (proofs and detailed settings) would improve reproducibility and readability.  
- Recommend providing more insight into trade-offs between components (value-function approximation vs. policy search).  
- Future work: derive theoretical guarantees for CBMPI and evaluate it in other domains.  
- Future work: decouple parameter-update and structure-update steps (e.g., “sparking params” vs structure updates) to study their effects.
