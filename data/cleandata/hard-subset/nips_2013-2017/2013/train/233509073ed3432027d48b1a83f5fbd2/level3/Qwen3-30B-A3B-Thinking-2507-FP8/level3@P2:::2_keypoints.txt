- Paper introduces LoSST, a heuristic algorithm for real-time tracking of time-varying graphical model structure using weighted sufficient statistics and Mahalanobis-distance-based changepoint detection.  
- Addresses gap: most structure-learning methods assume global stationarity, LoSST targets locally stationary streaming data with structural changes.  
- Novelty: combines Mahalanobis distance, pooled p-values via Liptak's method, and probabilistic relearning for online graphical-structure tracking.  
- Significance: relevant to real-world streaming applications (e.g., finance, sensors) where batch processing is infeasible; demonstrated on US price index volatility.  
- Empirical quality: comprehensive experiments on synthetic data (various sizes and changes) and a real-world case; outperforms PC baseline in non-stationary settings.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized; algorithm and math are clearly presented, though some figures and details need improvement.  
- Key weakness — theory: no formal theoretical analysis or guarantees; convergence vs. diligence trade-offs and heuristic elements lack formal justification.  
- Key weakness — heuristics: several ad-hoc design choices (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning criteria) are insufficiently justified and may affect performance.  
- Statistical assumptions: use of Hotelling's T² with effective sample sizes and Liptak pooling assumes conditions (e.g., test independence) that are not discussed.  
- Error behavior: explanation for worse edge-removal performance under parameter-only changes is unsatisfactory; suggests decoupling parameter and structure updates.  
- Presentation issues: figures (1–3) are small, poorly labeled, and captions lack detail; some procedural descriptions (e.g., relearn schedule) are unclear.  
- Related work: should better differentiate from prior time-indexed graph structure methods that operate in batch mode (Talih & Hengartner 2005; Siracusa & Fisher III 2009).  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept with significant revisions required.  
- Required revisions: add discussion of theoretical limitations/assumptions, better justify heuristic choices, improve figures and captions, and clarify relation to prior time-indexed graph work.
