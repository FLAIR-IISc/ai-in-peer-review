- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic online algorithm that tracks sufficient statistics and relearns graphical models when change is detected.
- Addresses an important problem: adapting structure learning to non-stationary time-series data.
- Evaluated on simulated data and a real-world US price index dataset, showing some practical applicability.
- Results appear correct but the heuristic algorithm lacks theoretical guarantees, raising concerns about robustness and generalizability.
- Novelty is limited; prior work (notably Siracusa & Fisher III, AISTATS 2009) addressing time-varying graphical models is downplayed.
- The claim that most score-based BN algorithms assume a single generating model is misleading.
- P-value construction and statistical methods used are pragmatic but not novel and are difficult to follow in the paper.
- Clarity problems: inconsistent punctuation, unclear definition of ρ_{r+1}, and choppy flow that obscures key concepts.
- The interaction and roles of parameters (γ, T, ν) and components (sufficient-stat tracking, Mahalanobis change detection, p-values, graph relearning) are poorly explained.
- Real-world application lacks detailed comparative analysis and quantitative demonstration of improvement over established time-series methods.
- Significance is modest: LoSST is a useful heuristic but not a clear advance over existing approaches and may be domain-dependent in its benefits.
- Recommendation: reject—authors should better engage related work (especially Siracusa & Fisher III), improve clarity, provide stronger theoretical or empirical justification, and more convincingly demonstrate advantages.
