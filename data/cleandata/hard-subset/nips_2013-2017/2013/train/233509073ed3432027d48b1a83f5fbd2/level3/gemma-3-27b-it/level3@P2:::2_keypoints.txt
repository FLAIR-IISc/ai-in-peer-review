- Tackles online detection of changes in dependency structure for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) with a practical algorithm, LoSST.  
- LoSST combines batch PC structure learning with streaming-triggered re-learning using tracked statistics and Mahalanobis-based “surprisingness.”

- Literature review is thorough and positions LoSST well among graphical model, change-point, and time-series work.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with accessible algorithm description and acknowledged limitations.

- Novel and significant problem; addresses non-stationary dynamics where batch methods fail.  
- Creative algorithm design: Mahalanobis distance for change detection, Liptak p-value pooling, and noisy-OR trigger are well-considered.  
- Experiments cover parameter and structure shifts, varying graph sizes/degrees, and include a real-world US price index dataset.  
- Writing clarity aids understanding of rationale and implementation.

- Major absence of theoretical analysis: no guarantees on convergence, detection delay, or false positive rates.  
- Heavy reliance on heuristics (reweighting, effective sample size, re-learning criteria) with ad-hoc design choices and limited justification.  
- Statistical validity concerns: application of Hotelling’s T2 and Liptak pooling may violate theoretical assumptions (e.g., independence).  
- Poor presentation of results: small/unclear figures, weak captions/legends, and unclear experiment descriptions.  
- Weak explanation for poor edge-removal performance under parameter-only changes; separating parameter and structure updates might help.  
- Excessive footnotes for proofs disrupt flow; detailed proofs belong in supplementary material.

- For acceptance: fills a significant gap, proposes a novel practical method, shows promising empirical results on synthetic and real data, and is well-written.  
- Against acceptance: lacks theoretical grounding, relies on heuristics without rigorous justification, risks misuse of statistical methods, and needs clearer presentation.

- Overall: interesting and promising first attempt but not ready for acceptance; recommend rejection with major revisions focused on theoretical analysis, heuristic justification, statistical assumption clarity, and presentation improvements.
