- Introduces LoSST: an online heuristic for tracking time-varying linear-Gaussian SEM structure under local stationarity using weighted sufficient statistics and adaptive per-datum weights.
- Weights ar are updated via pooled p-values over Mahalanobis distances using Hotelling’s T^2 and Lipták pooling to trigger more frequent relearning when evidence of change is high.
- Structure relearning uses PC on current weighted statistics with fixed or probabilistic relearning schedules.
- Paper sketches conditions showing a trade-off between diligence (fast response to change) and convergence (stability under stationarity).
- Synthetic experiments show LoSST matches batch PC under stationarity and outperforms it after structural changes; CPI case study shows qualitative changepoint detection.
- Differentiates from online CPD, sliding windows, and batch time-indexed models by offering a single-pass, low-memory method integrating change detection and relearning.
- Method is situated within constraint-based SEM learners; argues for frequentist, decomposable online updates for scalability.
- Sufficient-statistic updates are standard and appropriate; the pooled p-value update is well-motivated but heuristic and approximately calibrated due to serial dependence.
- Use of Hotelling’s T^2 and Lipták pooling is reasonable for Gaussian SEMs, but ρ should not be interpreted as an exact p-value under adaptivity.
- Convergence/diligence discussion is clear with sketched proofs, but calibration caveats remain.
- Empirics are convincing for the PC setting, including nuanced results for parameter-only changes; CPI application lacks ground truth and quantitative validation.
- Evaluation is limited to PC; claims that other structure learners would behave similarly are not experimentally supported.
- Paper is complete for SEM+PC under Gaussian assumptions but could expand hyperparameter sensitivity, calibration, and baseline comparisons.
- Writing is clear; algorithmic components (stat updates, pooling, relearning schedule) are well described.
- Recommend stating SEM-specific linear-Gaussian scope up front and noting nontrivial challenges for discrete BNs and conditional sufficient statistics.
- Suggest rephrasing or supporting the claim that other graphical learners would perform similarly, and explicitly state experiments focus on PC.
- Request for pseudocode with complexity annotations and details on efficient inverse-covariance updates (e.g., Sherman–Morrison) to avoid O(p^3) per step.
- Novelty lies in integrating pooled p-value driven adaptive forgetting, online sufficient-statistic tracking, and probabilistic triggering of constraint-based relearning.
- Significance: addresses an important under-studied problem; single-pass nature and gains after changepoints are practically relevant.
- Recommend softening generalization claims and adding stronger baselines and additional model families for broader impact.
- Specific suggestions: clarify dependence among sequential p-values, consider bootstrap/permutation calibration, and derive the effective sample size update in an appendix.
- Ask for sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters T, γ, ν, and metrics like time-to-recovery, false-alarm rate, and steady-state error.
- Baselines to add: sliding-window PC, fixed-forgetting-weighted PC, CPD+PC, and time-varying GGM methods where feasible.
- Reporting improvements: include SHD/F1 for edges and orientations and time-to-stabilization after change; add predictive log-likelihoods for real-data case study.
- Scalability concerns: detail per-step time/memory complexity, use of rank-one updates, numerical stability (regularization/shrinkage).
- Consider middle-ground extensions like per-variable or per-clique weights to capture localized changes.
- Pros: important problem, simple/practical approach, clear exposition, strong synthetic results, single-pass low-memory framework.
- Cons: evaluation limited to PC, pooled p-value calibration under adaptivity not fully addressed, limited baselines, qualitative real-data validation.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, conditional on clarifying SEM focus, tempering generalization beyond PC, and strengthening evaluation.
