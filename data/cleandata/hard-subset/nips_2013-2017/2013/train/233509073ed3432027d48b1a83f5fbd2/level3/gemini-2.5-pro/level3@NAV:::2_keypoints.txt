- Problem: Detecting and adapting to changes in dependency structures of SEMs from a continuous data stream.  
- Method: Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm tracking weighted sufficient statistics (means, covariances).  
- Surprise measure: Uses Mahalanobis distance of incoming points to compute "surprisingness" and update weights.  
- Change detection: Pools p-values using Liptak’s method to decide when to trigger batch relearning.  
- Relearning step: Triggers full structure relearning with the PC algorithm when change is detected.  
- Novelty: Creative synthesis of online parameter tracking, changepoint detection, and constraint-based structure learning.  
- Strength: Original and significant; addresses a practical, under-explored problem.  
- Strength: Computationally efficient and presented as an elegant first solution.  
- Empirics: Experiments show LoSST outperforms vanilla batch PC on synthetic shifts and produces plausible results on a real US price-index dataset.  
- Writing: Well-written with a good literature overview.  
- Major weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis and formal guarantees on detection and convergence.  
- Major weakness: Questionable statistical use—Hotelling’s T2 on an ad-hoc “effective” sample size and Liptak pooling assume conditions likely violated.  
- Major weakness: Many ad-hoc design choices (reweighting function, noisy-OR trigger) are insufficiently justified.  
- Minor issues: Poor presentation (proofs in footnotes), small/poorly labeled figures hindering reproducibility, and unconvincing explanation for specific failure modes.  
- Overall: Promising proof-of-concept but undermined by unjustified heuristics and questionable statistical applications, not yet a complete contribution.
