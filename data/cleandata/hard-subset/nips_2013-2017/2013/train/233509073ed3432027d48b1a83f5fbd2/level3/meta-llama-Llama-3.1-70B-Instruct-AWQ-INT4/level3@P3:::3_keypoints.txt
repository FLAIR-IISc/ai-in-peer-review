- Proposes LoSST, a novel heuristic for online tracking of time-varying graphical structure assuming only locally i.i.d. data.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Provides detailed algorithmic description: tracking sufficient statistics, computing weights, and learning structure.  
- Discusses trade-offs between convergence and diligence and gives a proof sketch for convergence/diligence conditions.  
- Experiments: LoSST matches batch-mode when structure is globally stationary and outperforms when only locally stationary.  
- Demonstrates effective real-time tracking of changes in both structure and parameters.  
- Claim that “other standard algorithms would perform similarly” is too strong and lacks evidence.  
- Experimental comparison is limited to the PC algorithm; additional baselines are needed.  
- Extending to Bayesian networks may be nontrivial due to maintaining sufficient statistics; paper should state its SEM-specific scope.  
- Needs more analysis of computational cost and memory requirements versus batch methods.  
- Paper is original and significant for real-world streaming applications (finance, healthcare, social networks).  
- Overall positive recommendation with minor revisions suggested.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, innovative LoSST algorithm, promising experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overstated claims about other algorithms, limited experimental comparisons, lacking computational/memory analysis.
