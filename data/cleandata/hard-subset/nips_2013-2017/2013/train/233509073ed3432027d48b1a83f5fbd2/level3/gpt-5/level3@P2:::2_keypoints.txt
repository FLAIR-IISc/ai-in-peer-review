- Addresses online tracking of time-varying graphical structure in locally stationary recursive linear SEMs over changing DAGs.
- Proposes LoSST: an online heuristic maintaining adaptive weighted sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size).
- Measures surprise per datum using Mahalanobis distance → Hotelling’s T² p-values, pooled over time via Liptak’s method into statistic ρ.
- Uses ρ to adapt per-datapoint weights (growth γ, threshold T) and a probabilistic noisy-OR schedule to trigger PC-based structure relearning.
- Provides analysis and discussion of a “convergence vs. diligence” trade-off controlled by T and γ.
- Novel combination of streaming weighted statistics, pooled surprise, and probabilistic relearning; single-pass and memory-efficient.
- Empirically matches batch PC under stationarity and outperforms PC after structural or parameter changes across simulations and a macroeconomic case study.
- Algorithmic components and incremental update equations are clearly described; real-data case study is appropriate.
- Methodology is heavily heuristic with limited formal theory or guarantees on error rates, detection delay, or false alarms.
- Effective sample size update is ad hoc and inconsistent with standard ESS definitions (e.g., Kish’s ESS or (∑w)²/∑w²).
- Use of Hotelling’s T² with weighted covariances and nonstandard “effective N” raises calibration concerns.
- Liptak pooling assumes independence or known dependence; serial dependence among p-values and reuse of estimated covariances likely invalidates assumptions.
- These statistical choices risk miscalibrated ρ and brittle trigger behavior; convergence/diligence discussion is insightful but informal.
- Baselines are limited to vanilla batch PC; missing practical baselines like sliding-window PC, EWMA-PC, CPD+relearn hybrids, and prior offline methods for context.
- No ablation studies for γ, T, ν or design choices (e.g., Liptak vs EWMA), and insufficient diagnostic metrics (false positives, detection delay).
- Clarity issues: important derivations in footnotes, small/under-labeled figures, missing experimental hyperparameter reporting, and no full algorithm pseudocode.
- Suggested improvements: calibrate Hotelling’s T² or replace it, derive or validate ESS, handle p-value dependence (block bootstrap/downweighting), add baselines and ablations, and provide reproducible code/pseudocode.
- Additional recommendations: consider decoupling parameter vs structure updates, apply covariance regularization for high-dimensional settings, and report runtime/memory.
- Pros for acceptance: tackles important problem, practical single-pass algorithm, novel idea integration, and promising empirical results.
- Cons for acceptance: heavy reliance on heuristics, potential miscalibration of statistics, sparse baselines and ablations, and presentation/clarity shortcomings.
- Overall recommendation: interesting and promising but needs stronger calibration/theory, additional baselines/ablations, and improved presentation; current stance leans weak reject/weak accept depending on revisions.
