- Introduces LoSST, a novel heuristic for learning graphical structure from locally i.i.d. time-varying data by tracking sufficient statistics online and relearning graphs dynamically.  
- Tackles an important, understudied problem of non-stationary structure learning that standard algorithms assume away.  
- Builds on the PC algorithm and is well-motivated and clearly written, with easy-to-follow methodology.  
- Uses pragmatic techniques (weighted statistics and probabilistic graph relearning) that are conceptually simple and effective.  
- Demonstrates promising simulation results and comparable performance to batch methods under stationarity.  
- Discusses trade-offs between convergence and diligence and analyzes algorithm behavior across parameters.  
- Provides a brief but compelling real-world application to US price index data.  
- Claim that other standard structure learning algorithms would perform similarly to PC is overstated and needs justification.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to comparison with PC; inclusion of at least one score-based method is recommended.  
- Approach is SEM-specific; this limitation should be emphasized in the introduction due to challenges extending to Bayesian networks or other graphical model types.  
- Choice of parameters (e.g., γ, T) appears arbitrary; a systematic parameter-sensitivity study is needed.
