- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic for online graphical-structure learning that dynamically weights data points by fit to the current model.  
- Evaluated on simulations and US price-index data; shows adaptability to changing relationships.  
- Results appear sound but novelty, clarity, and presentation weaknesses reduce contribution.  
- Related work is selective/misleading and omits relevant prior work (e.g., Siracusa & Fisher III 2009).  
- Incorrectly claims many score-based BN methods require a single generating model; several support incremental updates.  
- Central contribution is unclear: new model, algorithmic innovation, or application of existing techniques.  
- Algorithm mixes TD(0), the PC constraint-based method, and a Mahalanobis-distance/p-value weighting; pooling definition (ρ_{r+1}) is unclear.  
- Technical soundness seems reasonable; performs comparably to batch in stable settings and better in non-stationary ones.  
- Figures suffer from poor visual design (small fonts, unclear colors, missing legends), hindering interpretation.  
- Convergence vs. diligence trade-off is interesting but underdeveloped; cost-vs-tracking claims are unsubstantiated and domain-dependent.  
- Writing has minor but distracting issues (inconsistent punctuation, occasional ambiguity).  
- Strengths: technically plausible algorithm, relevant problem, promising blend of time-series and graphical-model ideas.  
- Weaknesses: limited novelty, unclear contribution, presentation flaws, and heuristic approach with modest theoretical grounding.  
- Acceptance arguments: relevant problem and promising empirical performance; potential utility for online applications.  
- Rejection arguments: overstated novelty, incomplete related work, poor clarity/presentation, limited significance.  
- Overall recommendation: reject; authors should fix related-work omissions, clarify contributions (and ρ_{r+1}), and substantially improve presentation.
