- Paper proposes LoSST: an online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical model structures under local stationarity, using Mahalanobis-weighted data and pooled p-values.  
- Experiments are sound: thorough simulations show LoSST matches batch performance in stable periods and outperforms PC during structural shifts.  
- Related-work claim is incorrect: many score-based BN methods use decomposable scores and sufficient statistics, enabling online updates.  
- The authors omit Siracusa & Fisher (AISTATS 2009), which addresses time-varying graphs and undermines the novelty claim.  
- Core algorithmic components (weight updates, Eq. 4) are heuristic with weak justification; construction of ρ_{r+1} is ambiguous.  
- Clarity problems: ambiguous definition of ρ_{r+1}, poorly punctuated/missing equations, and general readability issues.  
- Figures 1–4 are low quality (unreadable fonts, missing legends, insufficient axis labels), hindering quantitative assessment.  
- The paper conflates changepoint detection ("when" structure changes) with structure estimation ("what" the structure is), without resolving which task LoSST performs.  
- Originality is limited: weighted statistic adaptation and pooled p-values reuse existing changepoint/online ideas rather than introduce a fundamentally new approach.  
- Probabilistic relearning schedule is a practical refinement but not a substantial novel contribution.  
- Significance is modest: gains are context-specific (only during shifts) and computational cost of relearning is not empirically justified.  
- Pros: strong empirical validation on synthetic data, real-world application shown, implementable algorithm that performs in target scenarios.  
- Cons: overstated novelty, misleading related work, unclear task definition, presentation flaws, limited theoretical contribution.  
- Recommendation: Reject; authors should address Siracusa & Fisher, clarify task scope, improve presentation/figures, and strengthen theoretical grounding before resubmission.
