- Introduces LoSST, a heuristic algorithm for online structure learning in locally stationary, dynamically changing graphical models.  
- LoSST tracks sufficient statistics online and adapts to changes in underlying graphical model structure.  
- Demonstrates performance on simulations and a real-world U.S. price index volatility dataset.  
- Incorrectly claims novelty: prior work (Siracusa III and Fisher, AISTATS 2009) addresses non-iid and varying generating structures.  
- Conflates model assumptions (e.g., exchangeability) with algorithmic assumptions (e.g., factorized scoring), risking reader confusion about scope.  
- Unclear learning task: ambiguous whether focus is change-point detection, structure inference, or both.  
- Ambiguous separation between the proposed model and the algorithm implementing it.  
- Presentation issues: hard-to-follow text at the end of page 3, small figure fonts, and poor color descriptions.  
- Discussion of computational cost versus tracking accuracy is domain-specific and not generally generalizable.  
- Use of pooled p-values for weighting is potentially novel but under-explained and unclear.  
- Algorithm is heuristic and relies on existing structure learners (e.g., PC algorithm), limiting theoretical innovation.  
- Originality appears incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Limited theoretical grounding reduces the work’s potential to advance the state of the art.  
- Applicability to rapidly changing structures or mixed datasets is untested and uncertain.  
- Results are promising but developmental; the method may inspire future work but needs strengthening.  
- Pros: tackles a relevant problem, shows promising empirical results, and offers a practical real-time algorithm.  
- Cons: misleading novelty claims, unclear task and contributions, limited theory, heuristic approach, and accessibility/presentation problems.  
- Recommendation: not ready for acceptance in current form; revisions addressing related work, clarity, theoretical support, and presentation are needed.
