- Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm to track time-varying graphical model structures in locally stationary settings.  
- Authors claim novelty for handling non-iid, changing structures, but the related-work discussion is misleading.  
- Siracusa and Fisher III (AISTATS 2009) already studied time-varying graphical structures with Bayesian methods (though not real-time).  
- Paper incorrectly asserts that “none are capable of general, graphical model structure learning” despite existing online/Bayesian CPD methods.  
- Core algorithm components: Mahalanobis-distance weighting, Liptak’s p‑value pooling, and probabilistic relearning governed by ρ_{r+1}.  
- Simulation results (Figures 1–3) appear correct.  
- Fails to clearly state whether the contribution is a new task (change-point detection vs. current-structure inference) or just a new algorithm.  
- Major clarity issues: ρ_{r+1} is poorly defined (Eq.5), punctuation/presentation is confusing, and figures have unreadable fonts and weak legends.  
- Section 6 claim that “improved tracking can justify extra computational cost” is domain-dependent and unsupported.  
- Originality is limited: p‑value construction is hard to follow and the approach resembles existing score-based Bayesian network methods for non-stationarity (e.g., Chickering 2002).  
- Quality is moderate: technically sound algorithm and correct simulations, but key related work is overlooked.  
- Significance is modest: could spur Bayesian follow-ups but reads as a heuristic adaptation rather than a substantial advance.  
- Recommended fixes: clarify problem statement, compare properly to Siracusa & Fisher (2009), and resolve the change-point vs. structure-inference ambiguity.  
- Real-world application shows practical utility, but limited novelty and presentation flaws make the contribution incremental.
