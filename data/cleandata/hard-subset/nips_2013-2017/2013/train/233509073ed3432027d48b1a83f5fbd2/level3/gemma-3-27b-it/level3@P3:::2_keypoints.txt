- Tackles change detection in dependency structure for streaming SEMs — an important, under-explored problem.  
- Proposes LoSST: combines batch PC structure learning triggered by streaming statistics, weighted means/covariances, Mahalanobis reweighting, and a noisy-OR relearning gate.  
- Well-written with a solid literature review and clear organization.  
- Computationally and memory efficient, suitable for online learning scenarios.  
- Experiments on synthetic and a US price-index dataset show effectiveness detecting parameter and structural shifts.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis — no convergence or detection guarantees provided.  
- Heavy reliance on heuristic/ad hoc design choices (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning criteria) that need stronger justification.  
- Potential misuse of statistical methods: applying Hotelling’s T^2 to weighted/effective samples and using Liptak’s pooling under possible dependence require explicit discussion.  
- Presentation issues: proofs in footnotes (should move to supplement), figures too small/poorly labeled, and experiment descriptions need more detail.  
- Edge removal anomaly: explanation for poorer performance on edge additions during parameter-only changes is unconvincing; decoupling parameter and structure updates should be investigated.  
- Convergence vs. diligence discussion is insightful but should be more concise and integrated with algorithm-parameter discussion.  
- Implementation is technically sound and claims are empirically supported, but formal guarantees would substantially improve confidence.  
- Original and significant contribution with potential broad applicability if heuristics are justified and theoretical grounding added.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing clarity and statistical validity, and add discussion of potential theoretical guarantees as future work.
