- Tackles detecting changes in dependency structure for streaming SEMs, an important under-explored problem.  
- Proposes LoSST, a practical algorithm that triggers batch PC structure learning based on tracked streaming statistics.  
- Core idea combines weighted means, correlation matrices, effective sample size, and Mahalanobis “surprisingness” to adapt re-learning frequency.  
- Addresses a gap where most structure learning assumes stationarity.  
- Algorithm appears computationally efficient and well-motivated.  
- Experimental evaluation is adequate, includes parameter sensitivity and real-world US price index application.  
- Writing and literature review are clear and thorough.  
- Real-world application compelling: detects known economic shifts and shows robustness to noise.  
- Major weakness: lacks theoretical analysis or formal guarantees (convergence, detection rates, statistical validity).  
- Heavy reliance on heuristics (reweighting, effective sample size, re-learning criterion) with limited justification.  
- Use of Hotelling’s T² and Liptak’s pooling on weighted/effective sample sizes raises concerns about violating statistical assumptions.  
- Presentation issues: figures too small/unclear, proofs buried in footnotes rather than supplement.  
- Explanation for poorer performance on edge additions during parameter-only changes is inadequate; decoupling parameter and structure updates suggested.  
- Acceptance arguments: important problem, novel algorithm, adequate experiments, clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: missing theoretical grounding, heuristic-heavy design, potential misuse of statistics, and presentation flaws.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting practical first attempt but requires significant theoretical and presentation refinements; recommend rejection with encouragement to resubmit after addressing concerns.
