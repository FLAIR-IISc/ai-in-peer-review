- Introduces LoSST, an algorithm for learning graphical-model structure from locally stationary (time-varying) data by tracking sufficient statistics online and relearning on detected change.  
- Addresses an important, under-studied problem: most structure-learning methods assume global stationarity (i.i.d.).  
- Method leverages Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values for change detection while maintaining online sufficient statistics.  
- Conceptually simple, clearly presented, and builds on online learning and changepoint detection literature.  
- Experimental results: LoSST matches batch learning when structure is stable and outperforms it when structure changes.  
- Real-world validation on US price index volatility demonstrates practical utility.  
- Paper is well-written with accessible technical exposition, motivated derivations, and effective figures.  
- Methodologically sound with appropriate theoretical foundations (convergence/diligence properties).  
- Novelty: applying adaptive-weight sufficient-statistic tracking to graphical-structure learning is new.  
- Significance: enables real-time tracking of structural changes in domains like finance and social networks.  
- Weakness: claim that other standard structure-learning algorithms would perform similarly is too strong given experiments only compare to PC.  
- Weakness: scope limitation to SEMs should be stated up front; extensions to Bayesian networks are nontrivial and under-emphasized.  
- Weakness: experimental validation is limited (only PC algorithm); broader comparisons would strengthen generality claims.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify SEM-specific scope, temper generality claims, and expand experimental comparisons.
