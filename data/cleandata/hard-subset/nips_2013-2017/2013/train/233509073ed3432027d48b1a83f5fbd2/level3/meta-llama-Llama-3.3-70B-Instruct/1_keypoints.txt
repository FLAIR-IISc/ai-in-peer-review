- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing based approach for learning graphical models from time series data.
- Authors claim novelty as the first to handle non-iid data, but reviewer disputes this citing Siracusa III and Fisher.
- Method and experimental setup are described clearly and organized; algorithms are detailed.
- Related work review is thorough, but Section 2's description of score-based algorithms is misleading.
- The actual learning task is unclear—uncertain whether a new model, a new algorithm, or both are proposed.
- Presentation quality is poor (font size, equation formatting, figure descriptions).
- Work appears original but lacks substantive innovation; p-value construction may be the only novel element.
- Technical results are sound and supported by theory and experiments.
- Authors should be more balanced and candid about strengths and weaknesses.
- Paper is clearly written but organizational issues and reproducibility details are lacking.
- Related work is adequately referenced but differences from prior contributions are not well highlighted.
- Significance is moderate: addresses a hard problem but not much better than prior work and may not be widely adopted immediately.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on revisions addressing the noted issues.
- Pros summarized: novel approach for time-series graphical models, thorough related work and experiments, potential to inspire better methods.
- Cons summarized: incorrect novelty claim, unclear task, subpar presentation, limited innovation, moderate significance.
