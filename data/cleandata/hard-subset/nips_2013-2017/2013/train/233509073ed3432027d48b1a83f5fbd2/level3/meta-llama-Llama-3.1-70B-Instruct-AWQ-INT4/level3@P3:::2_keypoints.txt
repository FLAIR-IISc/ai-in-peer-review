- Proposes LoSST, a novel online algorithm for tracking changing graphical model structure and parameters in locally stationary settings.  
- Addresses an under-explored problem where standard methods assume global stationarity.  
- Tracks locally stationary sufficient statistics (means, covariances, sample size) via online updates.  
- Detects structural/parameter changes using weighted pooling of individual p-values.  
- Provides detailed algorithmic update equations and pooling methodology.  
- Evaluates LoSST thoroughly on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Shows comparable performance to batch learning under global stationarity and superior performance under local stationarity.  
- Demonstrates capability to detect both parameter changes and graph-structure changes.  
- Strength: novel, practical approach to online learning for graphical models with clear methodology and good literature coverage.  
- Major weakness: lacks theoretical analysis or guarantees (e.g., convergence or detection guarantees).  
- Algorithm is heuristic-heavy with several ad-hoc choices (reweighting, effective sample size, re-learn criteria) needing clearer justification.  
- Concern about potential misuse of statistical methods (Hotelling’s T2, Liptak pooling) without satisfying their assumptions.  
- Presentation issues: proofs buried in footnotes (should be supplementary), small/poorly labeled figures, and unclear experiment descriptions.  
- Recommendations: justify heuristic choices, discuss limitations/future work, improve figures and experiment clarity, move proofs to supplementary material.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and significant first attempt but needs stronger theoretical backing and improved presentation; reviewer rating 7/10 (confidence 8/10).
