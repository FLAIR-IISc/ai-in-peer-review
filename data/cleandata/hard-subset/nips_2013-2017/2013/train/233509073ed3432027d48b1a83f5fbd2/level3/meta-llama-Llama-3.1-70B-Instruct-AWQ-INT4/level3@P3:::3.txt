This paper proposes a novel approach to tracking time-varying graphical structure in data streams, which is a crucial problem in many real-world applications. The authors introduce the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST) algorithm, a heuristic method that learns graphical model structure in an online fashion, assuming only that the data are locally i.i.d.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear overview of the problem, related work, and the proposed solution. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the challenges and limitations of existing methods, including Bayesian and frequentist online changepoint detection algorithms, and provide a compelling argument for the need for a new approach.

The LoSST algorithm is described in detail, including its components, such as the tracking of sufficient statistics, the computation of weights, and the learning of graphical model structure. The authors also discuss the trade-offs between convergence and diligence in the algorithm and provide a proof sketch for the conditions under which LoSST is convergent or diligent.

The experimental results are promising, showing that LoSST performs comparably to batch-mode learning when the generating graphical structure is globally stationary and significantly better when it is only locally stationary. The authors also demonstrate the effectiveness of LoSST in tracking changes in graphical model structure and parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner.

However, the claim that "other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" is too strong and should be rephrased or justified with additional evidence. The experiments focus on the PC algorithm, and it is unclear whether other algorithms would perform similarly.

Additionally, extending the approach to Bayesian networks may be nontrivial due to difficulties maintaining sufficient statistics. The paper should state its SEM-specific scope upfront and note that extensions may not be straightforward.

The experimental results are limited to a comparison with the PC algorithm, and it would be beneficial to include comparisons with other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms. Furthermore, the authors could provide more insights into the computational cost and memory requirements of LoSST, especially in comparison to batch-mode learning.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel approach to tracking time-varying graphical structure, which is a significant contribution to the field. The use of locally stationary structure learning and the introduction of the LoSST algorithm are new and innovative.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to address a crucial problem in many real-world applications, such as finance, healthcare, and social networks, where data streams are common and graphical model structure learning is essential. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of LoSST in tracking changes in graphical model structure and parameters, which is a significant advancement in the field.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed approach is innovative and significant. However, some minor revisions are necessary to address the concerns mentioned above.
