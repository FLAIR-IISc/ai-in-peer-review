- Introduces LoSST, a heuristic algorithm for learning SEM structure from locally stationary, time-varying data streams.  
- Core contribution: systematic tracking of sufficient statistics (means, covariances) via a dynamic weighting scheme.  
- Weighting uses Mahalanobis distance of incoming points to detect distributional shifts and adaptively re-weight history.  
- When a significant change is detected, the method triggers a standard structure learning algorithm (experiments use PC).  
- Positions the work between traditional global i.i.d. structure learning and online changepoint detection for graphical model discovery.  
- Strengths: original, clearly presented, addresses an important under-studied problem with simple, effective techniques.  
- Empirical claim: LoSST outperforms a batch baseline in dynamic environments and performs comparably in stable ones.  
- Limitation: Claim that other standard structure learning algorithms would perform similarly is unsubstantiated—experiments only use PC and should soften or justify this.  
- Limitation: Scope restricted to SEMs where sufficient statistics are compact; extending to models like Bayesian networks or discrete variables is non-trivial and should be stated explicitly.  
- Limitation: Experimental evaluation compares only to a single batch-mode algorithm and needs broader baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality and significant; minor revisions to moderate claims and clarify scope would strengthen the paper.
