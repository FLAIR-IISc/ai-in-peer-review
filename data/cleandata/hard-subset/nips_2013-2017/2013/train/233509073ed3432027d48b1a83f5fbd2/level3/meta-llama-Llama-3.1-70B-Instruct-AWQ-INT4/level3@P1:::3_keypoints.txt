- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic online algorithm to track time-varying graphical model structure in real time.  
- Assumes local stationarity: data are i.i.d. at each time but structure/parameters can change over time.  
- Combines online learning with changepoint detection to track sufficient statistics and relearn structure as needed.  
- Builds on prior work in structure learning, online learning, and changepoint detection, addressing limitations of static batch methods.  
- Technically sound: theoretical analysis and parameter-regime behavior are carefully presented.  
- Experimental results are promising: LoSST can learn structure online and can outperform a standard batch PC-method in some scenarios.  
- Paper is clear and well-written; algorithm and experiments are described in detail.  
- Original contribution: novel combination of online learning and changepoint detection for dynamic graphical structure tracking.  
- Significant for applications requiring real-time or dynamic-structure analysis; demonstrated in a real-world case study.  
- Overclaim: statement that “other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly” is too strong and needs justification.  
- Experimental limitation: comparisons are limited to the PC-method and may not generalize across other algorithms.  
- Extension concern: extending the approach to Bayesian networks may be nontrivial due to maintaining sufficient statistics.  
- Suggested improvements: justify the strong comparative claim, include broader algorithmic comparisons, and specify the SEM-specific scope and limits for Bayesian-network extensions.
