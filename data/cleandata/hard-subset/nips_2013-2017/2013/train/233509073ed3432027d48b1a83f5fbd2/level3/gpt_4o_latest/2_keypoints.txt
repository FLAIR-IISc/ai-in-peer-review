- Addresses underexplored problem of tracking changing dependency structures in data streams.  
- Proposes LoSST: a novel algorithm combining PC algorithm batch learning with statistical tracking (Mahalanobis distance + Liptak’s weighted p-values).  
- Operates online with single-pass processing, making it efficient for real-time applications.  
- Evaluated on synthetic (ground truth) and real-world US price index data, showing detection of meaningful trends and robustness to transient noise.  
- Innovative use of pooled p-values to reduce sensitivity to outliers and short-lived changes.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review and situates LoSST within graphical model and changepoint literature.  
- Writing is generally clear and discusses convergence vs. diligence trade-offs thoughtfully.  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees or convergence proofs, weakening scientific rigor.  
- Heavy reliance on heuristics (weighting scheme, probabilistic relearning) without systematic justification or analysis.  
- Uses Hotelling’s T² and Liptak’s p-values beyond their original guarantees without adequate acknowledgment.  
- Experimental descriptions are sometimes cryptic and figures are small/poorly labeled, reducing clarity.  
- Inadequate explanation for LoSST’s weaker performance on edge addition with parametric changes; decoupling parameter and structure updates needs more exploration.  
- Presentation issues: proofs in footnotes should be moved to supplementary materials for readability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — improve figure clarity, expand experimental descriptions, and explicitly acknowledge heuristic limitations while encouraging future theoretical work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical, efficient algorithm with demonstrated utility that opens new research directions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of theoretical guarantees, overreliance on heuristics, and clarity issues in experiments and figures.
