- Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm for learning graphical model structure under local stationarity by tracking weighted sufficient statistics (mean, covariance, effective sample size) for SEMs.
- New data are weighted adaptively using Mahalanobis distance; sequences of deviations increase recent weights to “forget” past structure, and a structure learner is periodically rerun on weighted statistics.
- Positions the work as filling the gap between static structure learning and changepoint detection for real-time structure learning.

Strengths:
- Significant and original: addresses the practical problem of learning causal/dependency structures from non-stationary data with a novel online framework.
- Clear presentation: paper is well-written, organized, and explains online updates and the convergence vs. diligence trade-off clearly.
- Strong technical quality: simple, intuitive method; Mahalanobis-based reweighting is principled and experiments show LoSST tracks changes and outperforms a stationary batch baseline.

Weaknesses / suggestions:
- Overstated generality: claim that “any” standard structure learner would behave similarly is unsupported, as experiments only use the constraint-based PC algorithm.
- Scope ambiguity: approach and sufficient-statistics theory are specific to linear SEMs over continuous variables; extensions to discrete models are non-trivial and should be acknowledged.
- Narrow experiments: comparisons are limited to a batch-mode PC baseline; should include adaptive baselines like sliding-window PC or fixed exponential-forgetting online learners.

Update after author response:
- Reviewer remains unconvinced that generality and experimental-comparison concerns are fully addressed, though the core contribution is still considered solid.
