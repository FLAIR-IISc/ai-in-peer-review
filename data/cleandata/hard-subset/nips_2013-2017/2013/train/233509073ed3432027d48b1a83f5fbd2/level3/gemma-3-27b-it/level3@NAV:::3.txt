## Review of “Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure”

This paper presents LoSST, a novel heuristic algorithm for learning graphical model structure from locally i.i.d. time-varying data. The core idea of tracking sufficient statistics online and dynamically relearning the graph structure addresses an important and understudied problem – a significant limitation of existing structure learning algorithms that assume stationarity. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, clearly articulating the motivation and methodology. The approach, building upon the PC algorithm, demonstrates promising results in simulated environments where the underlying graphical structure changes over time. 

The strength of this work lies in its pragmatic approach to a challenging problem. The proposed techniques for handling non-stationarity – weighted statistics and probabilistic graph relearning – are conceptually simple yet effective, and are demonstrated to perform comparably to batch methods when the data *are* stationary. The authors thoughtfully discuss the trade-offs between convergence and diligence, providing valuable insights into the algorithm’s behavior under different parameter settings. The application to US price index data, while brief, provides a compelling example of the algorithm’s potential in a real-world setting.

However, the claim that other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly to PC under the same conditions feels overstated. While plausible, this requires justification – a brief discussion of why the core principles of LoSST would translate to other algorithms (e.g., those based on scores) would strengthen this assertion. Furthermore, the experimental evaluation is currently limited to a comparison with the PC algorithm. Expanding this to include at least one score-based method would provide a more comprehensive assessment of the approach’s generality. 

The paper correctly identifies the SEM-specific nature of the approach. However, this limitation should be made more prominent upfront in the introduction, as extending LoSST to Bayesian networks (or other graphical model types) will likely encounter significant challenges with maintaining sufficient statistics.  Finally, while the simulation results are encouraging, the choice of parameters (e.g., γ, T) appears somewhat arbitrary.  A more systematic exploration of parameter sensitivity would be beneficial.

**Overall:** This is a strong contribution that tackles a relevant problem with a novel and well-motivated approach. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses an important and under-explored problem in graphical model learning.
*   Presents a practical and effective algorithm with clear theoretical underpinnings.
*   Demonstrates promising results in simulation and a compelling real-world application.
*   Well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The claim regarding the generalizability to other algorithms requires further support.
*   Limited experimental evaluation (only PC algorithm comparison).
*   SEM-specific limitations need more upfront emphasis.
