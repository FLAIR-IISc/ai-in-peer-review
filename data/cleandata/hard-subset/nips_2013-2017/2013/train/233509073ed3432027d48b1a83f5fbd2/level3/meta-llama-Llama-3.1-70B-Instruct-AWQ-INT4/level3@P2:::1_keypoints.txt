- Proposes LoSST, an online hypothesis-testing algorithm to track changing graphical model structure/parameters in real time.  
- Assumes locally i.i.d. data and learns graphical model structure online via locally stationary sufficient statistics.  
- Tracks means, covariances, and sample size with weighted mean/covariance updates where weights reflect fit to incoming data.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data and a US price index volatility dataset, showing ability to detect structural changes.  
- Empirical results: LoSST matches batch learning when structure is globally stationary and outperforms it when only locally stationary.  
- Novelty is disputed: Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already address non-iid data and varying generating structures.  
- Related-work discussion is misleading; many score-based BN methods use factorized/local scores and don’t require a single global model.  
- The paper is unclear about its precise task (change-point detection vs. inferring current generative structure) and whether it introduces a new model, algorithm, or both.  
- Presentation issues: confusing punctuation, unclear notation (e.g., definition of ρ_{r+1}), and general readability problems.  
- Figures are hard to read due to small fonts and lack of color/legends, reducing interpretability of results.  
- Theoretical/statistical elements (p-value construction) are hard to follow and not well explained.  
- Claim that improved tracking justifies extra computational cost is vague and domain-dependent.  
- Originality and significance are modest—approach reads as heuristic and may mainly motivate further Bayesian work rather than constitute a major advance.  
- Recommendation: revise substantially—clarify task and novelty, acknowledge related prior work, improve presentation/figures, and provide clearer p-value and methodological details.  
- Overall rating: 6/10; interesting idea but needs significant revision before acceptance.
