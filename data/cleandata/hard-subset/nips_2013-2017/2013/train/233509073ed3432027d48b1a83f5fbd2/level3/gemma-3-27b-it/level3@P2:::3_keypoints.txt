- Introduces LoSST, a heuristic algorithm for tracking graphical model structure in locally i.i.d. (locally stationary) real-time settings.  
- Key idea: adapt structure learning to local rather than global stationarity, important for many real-world non-stationary processes.  
- Empirical claim: LoSST matches batch-mode learning when structure is stable and outperforms it when structure changes.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explaining the problem, solution, and related work.  
- Situates work effectively within graphical model learning, online learning, and changepoint detection literature.  
- Builds on established techniques (PC algorithm, temporal-difference learning) and explains sufficient-statistic updates clearly.  
- Includes a useful discussion on the convergence vs. diligence trade-off in online learning.  
- Strength: novel and important contribution to learning from non-stationary data.  
- Strength: technically sound algorithm with clear justification and implementation details.  
- Strength: convincing simulations and a real-world US price index application demonstrating practical utility.  
- Weakness: Overstated generalizability — claims about applicability to other structure-learning algorithms (beyond PC/SEMs) need justification or rephrasing.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparisons are limited, primarily against PC; broader algorithmic comparisons (constraint- and score-based methods) are missing.  
- Weakness: Parameter sensitivity (γ, T, ν) is inadequately explored; a thorough sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important, underexplored problem with a sound, well-validated solution and clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: needs stronger justification of generalizability, broader empirical comparisons, and deeper parameter analysis.  
- Overall: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to clarify scope (SEM focus), temper generalizability claims, and expand experimental/sensitivity analyses.
