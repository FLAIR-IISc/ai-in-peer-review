- Addresses an important, under-explored problem: online tracking of time-varying graphical model structure in streaming data.
- Proposes LoSST, which combines online sufficient-statistic tracking with a probabilistic relearning mechanism triggered by data "surprisingness."
- Core innovation: efficient tracking of weighted sufficient statistics and use of Mahalanobis distances to detect structural shifts.
- Adapts Hotelling's T² to weighted samples and uses Liptak's method to pool p-values, forming a significance metric ρ.
- Uses a noisy-OR based probabilistic relearning gate to balance convergence vs. diligence in model updates.
- Clever combination of established techniques into a novel, practical framework for online structure learning.
- Experimental results strong: matches batch PC in stable regimes and outperforms it during structural changes across synthetic settings.
- Real-world application (US price index volatility) compelling—identifies Great Moderation and is robust to short-term fluctuations.
- Writing, organization, and literature review are clear and place the work appropriately in context.
- Lacks theoretical guarantees or formal analysis of convergence and detection performance under data-generating assumptions.
- Statistical methods are applied outside their standard assumptions (weighted Hotelling's T², dependent p-value pooling) without sufficient justification.
- Presentation issues: small/poorly labeled figures and insufficient experimental detail (e.g., fixed graph relearning schedule).
- Heuristic-heavy design choices need more documentation and justification before strong acceptance; better suited to an applied venue or future revision with theory and improved presentation.
