- Proposes LoSST, a novel online algorithm for detecting changes in SEM structure and parameters over data streams.
- Addresses need for online structure learning under non-stationary data, motivated by limitations of existing algorithms.
- LoSST combines tracking weighted means, correlation matrices, effective sample sizes, and Mahalanobis-distance weighting by "surprisingness."
- Demonstrates effectiveness via simulations and a real-world US price index volatility application.
- Algorithm is practical and computationally efficient.
- Experiments are adequate, including parameter sensitivity analysis and a real dataset.
- Literature review is strong and writing is clear.
- Major weakness: no theoretical analysis, guarantees, or proofs of convergence/detection under assumptions.
- Design is heuristic-heavy with many ad-hoc choices (reweighting, effective sample size, relearn criteria) needing clearer justification.
- Potential misuse of statistical methods (e.g., Hotelling's T2, Liptak pooling) when their assumptions may not hold; authors should explicitly state such limitations.
- Presentation issues: excessive footnotes for proofs (should be moved to supplementary material).
- Figures are too small, poorly labeled, and captions/legends lack detail.
- Experiment descriptions need clarity; explanation for worse performance on edge additions under parameter-only changes is unsatisfactory.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions for presentation and documentation; encourage more detailed theoretical development in future work.
- Arguments for acceptance: important under-explored problem, novel and efficient algorithm, adequate experiments, good literature review.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of theory, heuristic design, potential statistical misuse, and presentation shortcomings.
