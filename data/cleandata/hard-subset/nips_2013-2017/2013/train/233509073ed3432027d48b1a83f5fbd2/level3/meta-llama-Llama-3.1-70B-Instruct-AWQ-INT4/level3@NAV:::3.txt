This paper proposes a novel method, LoSST, for tracking time-varying graphical structure in locally stationary data. The algorithm is designed to handle situations where the underlying generating structure changes over time, and it does so by tracking the sufficient statistics in an online fashion and dynamically relearning the graphical model structure as needed.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and it addresses an important problem in the field of graphical models. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, and they provide a thorough review of related work in the area. The proposed algorithm is described in detail, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how it works.

The experimental results are promising, and they demonstrate the effectiveness of the LoSST algorithm in tracking time-varying graphical structure. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of the limitations of the algorithm and potential avenues for future research.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the claim that "other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" is not fully justified. The authors only compare their algorithm to the PC algorithm, and they do not provide a thorough comparison to other algorithms. Additionally, the authors could provide more detail on the computational complexity of the algorithm and how it scales to large datasets.

Overall, the paper is well-written and makes a significant contribution to the field of graphical models. The proposed algorithm is novel and effective, and it has the potential to be widely used in a variety of applications.
