- Introduces LoSST: an online heuristic for learning graphical-model structure under local stationarity using weighted sufficient-statistics, Mahalanobis-distance-based fit, pooled p-values for change detection, and probabilistic relearning.

- Addresses an important practical gap between batch structure learning (assumes stationarity) and online parameter/changepoint methods (which typically don’t relearn structure).

- Strength — significance & originality: novel and principled—though heuristic—combination of online stats, robust change detection, and probabilistic relearning for non-stationary structure learning.

- Strength — clarity: paper is very well written; algorithm and online update derivations are clear; the convergence vs. diligence distinction is insightful.

- Strength — quality: method is technically sound; experiments show parity with PC in stationary settings and strong gains when structure changes; real-world US price index case study is compelling.

- Weakness — scope/generality: experiments focus on SEMs over continuous variables but the paper frames the problem as general to graphical models; extending to discrete/mixed models poses significant challenges and should be explicitly limited in scope.

- Weakness — unsupported claim: statement that “any other standard” structure learner would behave like PC is too strong and unsubstantiated, especially versus score-based methods.

- Weakness — limited baselines: comparison only to batch PC is weak for non-stationary settings; a sliding-window PC or other heuristics should be included to isolate LoSST’s specific advantages.

- Overall: high-quality, valuable contribution likely to interest the community; weaknesses are minor framing and experimental-coverage issues that can be fixed by softening claims and expanding baselines.
