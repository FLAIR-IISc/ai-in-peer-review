- Proposes LoSST, a novel online hypothesis-testing algorithm to learn changing graphical models from time-series data.  
- Designed to track structural or parameter changes in real time for non-iid, varying generating processes.  
- Algorithm details include weighted mean, covariance, and sample size updates; convergence and diligence are discussed.  
- Simulation results: LoSST matches batch learning when structure is globally stationary and outperforms when only locally stationary.  
- Related work discussion is thorough but the novelty claim is disputed because Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) address non-iid and varying structures.  
- Presentation and readability issues: confusing paragraph and end-of-equation punctuation, unclear definition of ρ_{r+1}, and other clarity problems.  
- Figures suffer from small fonts and lack color/legend explanations.  
- Originality is limited; the paper lacks clearly novel ideas and the p-value construction is hard to follow.  
- Significance is modest; work may spur Bayesian follow-ups but reads as heuristic relative to existing ML methods.  
- Reviewer recommends clarifying the precise learning task and whether a new model, algorithm, or both are proposed.  
- Reviewer asks for a more detailed and precise related-work comparison.  
- Reviewer requests fixes to presentation, notation, and figure readability.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and a useful scientific contribution but requires revisions for clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 6/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel online approach, handles non-iid/varying structures, strong simulation performance in locally stationary settings.  
- Arguments for rejection: prior related work challenges novelty, presentation and notation issues, limited originality and hard-to-follow p-value method.
