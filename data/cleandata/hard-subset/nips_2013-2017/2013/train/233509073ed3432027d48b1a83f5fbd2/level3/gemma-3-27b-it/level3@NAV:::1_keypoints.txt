- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic for online structure learning in non-stationary graphical models that tracks sufficient statistics and relearns structure using a p-value-based weighting scheme.  
- Evaluated on simulated structural-change data and a real-world US price indices dataset; results appear correct.  
- Primary concern: novelty overstated—related work (e.g., Siracusa & Fisher 2009) is not adequately acknowledged.  
- Mischaracterizes existing score-based BN methods as assuming a single generating model; factorized scores can allow local adaptation.  
- Objective is unclear: is the goal change-point detection, inferring the current structure, or both?  
- Unclear whether the contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or a novel combination of existing techniques.  
- Presentation and clarity issues, including ambiguous definition of ρ_{r+1}, punctuation problems, and hard-to-follow exposition.  
- Figures 1–4 suffer from small fonts and lack informative captions or legends.  
- Justification for computational cost is weak and seems domain-dependent rather than generally convincing.  
- Experimental advantage over simpler adaptive methods is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- P-value construction appears of limited originality and is difficult to follow in rationale.  
- Pros: addresses an important problem, offers a potentially useful heuristic, and simulations show functionality.  
- Cons: limited novelty, unclear contribution, readability and figure problems, weak computational justification, and modest overall significance.  
- Overall recommendation: reject; suggest addressing related work gaps, clarifying contributions, improving readability/figures, and providing stronger justification for benefits.
