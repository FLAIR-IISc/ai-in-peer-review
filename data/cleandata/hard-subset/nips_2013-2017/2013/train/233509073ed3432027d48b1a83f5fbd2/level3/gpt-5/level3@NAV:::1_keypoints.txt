- Proposal: LoSST is an online heuristic that tracks time-varying graph structure via weighted sufficient statistics and pooled p-values of Mahalanobis distances, with PC used at relearning steps.
- Empirical claim: Simulations show parity with batch PC in stationary phases and faster adaptation after changes; a CPI volatility case study suggests responsiveness to regime shifts.
- Overstated novelty: Claims of general novelty are too strong given prior work (e.g., Siracusa & Fisher, Talih & Hengartner) and existing score-based/sliding-window or switching-DBN approaches.
- Missing related methods: Change-point/BOCPD approaches and decomposable score-based BN methods are directly relevant and not adequately discussed.
- Evaluation underpowered: Only compared to batch PC; key baselines are missing (sliding-window PC/score search, BOCPD+relearning, Siracusa & Fisher).
- Sensitivity and ablation gaps: No systematic sensitivity analysis for T, γ, ν or ablation studies of the p-value pooling mechanism.
- Runtime claims speculative: Assertion that tracking benefits offset extra compute lacks runtime/accuracy tradeoff curves or domain-dependent analysis.
- Task underspecified: It’s unclear whether the goal is change-point detection, timely recovery of current structure, or both, and whether LoSST is a new model or an algorithmic wrapper on PC.
- Presentation/readability problems: Ambiguous notation/indexing (ρr+1), unclear role of Sr in T2 parameters, punctuation/equation issues, and small/under-labeled figures.
- P-value construction: The pooled p-value approach is heuristic and hard to follow, weakening the methodological clarity.
- Originality assessment: Combination of pooled p-values with adaptive relearning has some novelty but is primarily heuristic and not clearly superior to existing methods.
- Significance assessment: Modest — idea could inspire more principled or Bayesian variants, but as presented it resembles heuristics already implementable with existing toolkits.
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, implementable online heuristic with promising simulation behavior and a clear exposition of convergence–diligence trade-offs.
- Cons for acceptance: Overstated novelty, incomplete related work and baselines, missing sensitivity/ablation studies, clarity/notation issues, and limited evidence of superior performance or domain impact.
- Suggested fixes: Precisely define the task; add recommended baselines and runtime–accuracy curves; provide sensitivity analyses and CIs; clarify ρr+1/Sr and notation; enlarge/annotate figures; and temper novelty claims with citations.
