- Reviewer disputes the authors' claim of novelty, noting Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid data and varying generating structures.  
- Paper proposes an online hypothesis-testing approach to learn changing graphical models from time-series data and evaluates on simulated and real datasets.  
- Results appear correct, but the related-work discussion is misleading: many score-based BN algorithms do not assume a single generating model, only factorized scoring/local terms.  
- The paper is unclear about the precise learning task (detecting change points vs inferring the current generating structure) and whether it proposes a new model, algorithm, or both.  
- Presentation issues: confusing paragraph/end-of-equation punctuation, unclear definition of ρ_{r+1}, and other readability problems.  
- Figures 1–4 suffer from small font sizes and lack of color/legend descriptions.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking can justify extra computational cost is unclear and seems domain-dependent.  
- Originality is limited: while the approach is somewhat original, it lacks clear novel ideas and the p-value construction is hard to follow.  
- Significance is modest: the work may spur further (Bayesian) development but reads as heuristic development to a problem existing ML methods could address.
