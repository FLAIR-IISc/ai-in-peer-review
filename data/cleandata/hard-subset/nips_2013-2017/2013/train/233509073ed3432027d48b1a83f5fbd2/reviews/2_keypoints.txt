- Tackles under-explored problem: detecting changes in dependency structure over a data stream for SEMs.  
- Proposes LoSST: a practical, efficient algorithm that uses batch PC structure learning triggered by tracked streaming statistics.  
- Tracks weighted means, correlation matrix, effective sample size, and weights new points by "surprisingness" via Mahalanobis distance.  
- Computes MD p-values using Hotelling's T2, pools them with Liptak's weighted p-value method to form a significance statistic ρ, uses a noisy-OR with previous step to decide re-learning.  
- Evaluated against vanilla PC on synthetic shifts in parameters and structure across varying variables and degrees.  
- Tested on a real US price-index dataset (529 monthly points, six variables) showing detection of known economic changes and robustness to short-lived fluctuations.  
- Pros: Addresses an important, under-explored problem with room for further work.  
- Pros: Algorithm is novel, practical, and computationally/memory efficient, combining several methods creatively.  
- Pros: Experiments are adequate and include parameter sensitivity and a real-world dataset.  
- Pros: Good literature review and overall clear writing.  
- Major con: Lacks theoretical analysis or guarantees (convergence/detection under assumptions); should at least be discussed as future work.  
- Major con: Heuristic-heavy design; many ad-hoc decisions (reweighting, effective sample size, re-learn criteria) need clearer documentation.  
- Major con: Potential misuse of statistical methods—Hotelling's T2 applied to effective/weighted sample sizes and Liptak pooling assumes conditions (e.g., independent tests) that may not hold.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly state when methods are used outside their theoretical guarantees to avoid misleading readers.  
- Other issues: Excessive use of footnotes for proofs—move detailed proofs to supplementary material.  
- Other issues: Figures are too small, poorly labeled, and captions/legends lack details; experiment descriptions (e.g., p.7) are unclear.  
- Other issues: Explanation for LoSST performing worse on edge additions under parameter-only changes is unsatisfactory; consider decoupling parameter updates from structure updates.  
- Overall: Interesting and practical first attempt but needs clearer justification of heuristic choices, better presentation, and theoretical development.
