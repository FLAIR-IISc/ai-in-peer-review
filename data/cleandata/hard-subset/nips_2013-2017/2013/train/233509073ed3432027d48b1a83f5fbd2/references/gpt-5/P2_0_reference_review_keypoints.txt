- Proposes LoSST: an online single-pass algorithm for tracking time-varying DAG structure in linear-Gaussian SEMs using weighted sufficient statistics and pooled p-value change detection.  
- Adapts weights (effective sample size) and increases probability of PC-based structure relearning when pooled p-values indicate distributional change.  
- Theoretical framing distinguishes “convergence” vs “diligence” regimes controlled by T and γ, with convergence under fixed weights and diligence when weights grow; only proof sketches provided.  
- Online updates for weighted mean/covariance are standard; the effective sample size update and p-value pooling/weight mapping are heuristic and require justification.  
- Use of Hotelling’s T^2 with weighted covariance and an “effective” sample size is an approximation that needs calibration or theoretical backing.  
- Lipták p-value pooling and Eq. 4 mapping are plausible but heuristic and not unique choices.  
- Theory lacks finite-sample guarantees, bounds on false alarms/detection delay, and consistency guarantees under slow drift.  
- Experiments on synthetic SEMs show LoSST matches batch PC in stationary segments and adapts faster after abrupt changes; probabilistic relearning reduces computation without hurting error.  
- Missing empirical comparisons to key baselines: sliding-window PC, exponential-forgetting PC, CPD-triggered relearning (e.g., BOCPD/ADWIN), and time-varying GGM methods.  
- No ablations on hyperparameters (T, γ, ν), p-value pooling choice, or robustness to outliers vs sustained shifts.  
- No runtime/memory profiling, scalability experiments, or evaluation in high-dimensional (p >> n) regimes.  
- Real-data CPI volatility case study is illustrative but lacks ground truth, quantitative evaluation, and baseline comparisons.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation in T^2 p-value expression and unclear appropriateness under weighted estimates.  
- Numerical stability concerns when inverting covariance with small effective sample sizes; regularization/shrinkage not discussed.  
- Role and tuning guidance for T, γ, ν are under-motivated; sensitivity plots and fuller proofs should be added to supplement.  
- Originality: novel combination of pooled p-values driving adaptive forgetting for online DAG learning, but conceptually a natural composition of known components and related literatures are insufficiently engaged.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and could be a practical baseline for streaming SEMs, but current evidence is limited by missing baselines, lack of guarantees, and modest scale.  
- Pros: clear formulation, practical single-pass algorithm, intuitive detection-to-forgetting mechanism, and convincing synthetic adaptation results.  
- Cons: heuristic statistical machinery lacking calibration, missing strong baselines and sensitivity analyses, potential numerical and scalability issues, and limited real-data validation.  
- Reviewer suggestions: provide ablations for hyperparameters and pooling, justify or calibrate T^2 under weighting, compare to sliding-window/exponential-forgetting/CPD-triggered baselines and time-varying GGMs, add covariance regularization, and report runtime/memory tradeoffs.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; promising approach but needs stronger baselines, sensitivity analyses, runtime results, and clearer theoretical/statistical justification.
