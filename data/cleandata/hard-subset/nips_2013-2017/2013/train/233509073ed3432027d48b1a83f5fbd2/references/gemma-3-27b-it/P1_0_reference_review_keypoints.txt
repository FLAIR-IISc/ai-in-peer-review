- Tackles online learning of time-varying graphical model structure, a largely unexplored and important problem.  
- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic online algorithm that tracks sufficient statistics and relearns the graph dynamically.  
- Balances convergence (stability) and diligence (adaptation) via tunable parameters (γ and T).  
- Updates (mean, covariance, sample size) enable computationally efficient online operation.  
- Empirical results (simulations and US price index volatility) show LoSST can track structural and parameter changes and outperform batch PC in dynamic settings.  
- Related work coverage is adequate and distinguishes LoSST from stationary, batch, and data-intensive changepoint methods.  
- Novel combination of established techniques (PC algorithm, Mahalanobis distance, change detection) applied to online structure learning.  
- Potentially significant for many non-stationary real-world domains (finance, healthcare, climate, social networks).  
- Algorithm relies on several heuristic choices (Mahalanobis distance, p-value pooling, weight update, relearning probability) lacking rigorous justification.  
- Performance likely sensitive to parameter choices (α, γ, T, ν); missing systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Simulations are limited in scope (simple graphs, few change types); need testing on high-dimensional and more complex change scenarios.  
- No formal theoretical guarantees provided (convergence rates, error bounds, detection delay).  
- Mahalanobis-distance reliance risks false detections due to outliers and noise.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance as a solid, practical contribution but asks for future work addressing sensitivity, broader simulations, and limitations; confidence 7/10.
