

# Review of "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure"

## Summary
The paper proposes the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST) algorithm for online learning of graphical model structures in time-varying environments. It addresses a significant gap in the literature by extending standard graphical model structure learning to handle non-stationary environments, where the underlying structure may change over time. The authors demonstrate that LoSST can detect structural changes and adapt to new structures while maintaining performance comparable to batch-mode learning in stable environments. The paper provides theoretical analysis of convergence properties, simulation results, and a real-world application to US price index volatility.

## Strengths
The paper identifies a genuine gap in the literature and proposes a well-motivated solution. The theoretical analysis of convergence vs. diligence is insightful and provides a principled way to balance these competing desiderata. The simulation results are comprehensive and clearly demonstrate the advantages of LoSST over batch-mode learning in non-stationary settings. The real-world application to US price index volatility is relevant and well-executed, providing concrete evidence of the algorithm's practical utility.

## Weaknesses

### Technical Errors and Inconsistencies
The paper contains several significant technical errors that undermine the credibility of the method:

1. **Equation (4) is mathematically incorrect**: The expression for the weight update is written as:
   ar+1 = ar ·max { 1, γT − γρr+1 + ρr+1/T }
   This formulation is confusing and mathematically problematic. The term γT - γρr+1 + ρr+1/T is not well-defined. The intended expression appears to be:
   ar+1 = ar · max{1, γ(T - ρr+1)/(T - ρr+1 + ρr+1)} = ar · max{1, γ(T - ρr+1)/T}
   The current formulation lacks clarity and could lead to implementation errors.

2. **Incorrect TD(0) analogy**: The claim that "If ar = αbr, then the learning is the same as if one uses TD(0) learning for each covariance with a learning rate of α" is inaccurate. The TD(0) update is Et+1(X) = (1-α)Et(X) + αXt, while the paper's formulation would produce Cr+1 = (Cr + αXr+1Xr+1^T)/(1+α), which is not equivalent.

3. **Hotelling's T² distribution error**: The statement "The Mahalanobis distance of a V-dimensional datapoint from a covariance matrix estimated from a sample of size N is distributed as Hotelling's T² with parameters p = V and m = N - 1" is incorrect. Hotelling's T² is distributed as T² = (N-1)V/(N-V)·F(V, N-V), not with parameters p=V and m=N-1.

### Formatting and Presentation Issues
The paper has numerous formatting inconsistencies that reduce readability:

1. **Inconsistent notation**: The paper uses both "br" and "b_r" interchangeably throughout, which is confusing. For example, in equation (1), it's "br" but in equation (2), it's "br+1" without consistent subscript notation.

2. **Equation formatting errors**: Equation (2) is written as "Cr+1Xi,Xj" which should be "Cr+1_{i,j}" or "C_{r+1}(X_i,X_j)" for clarity. Similarly, the Mahalanobis distance equation (Dr+1 = (Xr+1 − µr)(Cr)−1(Xr+1 − µr)^T) should be written as a scalar, not a vector.

3. **Reference formatting**: Many references lack proper formatting. For example, "Bayesian online changepoint detection, R.P. Adams, D.J.C. MacKay, Technical report,," is missing author names, venue, and publication year. This is unprofessional for a NIPS submission.

### Theoretical and Methodological Concerns
The paper makes several claims that lack sufficient justification:

1. **Incomplete derivation**: The paper states "after some algebra" for the covariance update (equation 2) without showing the derivation. For a technical paper, this omission is unacceptable.

2. **Unjustified claim about PC algorithm**: The authors state "we conjecture that any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly" to PC, but provide no evidence or theoretical justification for this claim.

3. **Inadequate treatment of edge case**: The paper does not address what happens when ar+1 = 0 (which could occur when ρr+1 = 0), making equation (3) undefined in those cases.

4. **Proof sketch incompleteness**: The proof sketch for convergence (footnote 4) is overly vague and lacks sufficient mathematical detail to be convincing.

## Overall Assessment
While the paper addresses an important problem and makes a valuable contribution to the field of graphical model structure learning, the numerous technical errors, inconsistent formatting, and incomplete derivations significantly undermine the paper's credibility. The authors have presented a promising approach, but the implementation details and theoretical foundations are not sufficiently rigorous for NIPS.

The paper's strengths in problem identification, simulation results, and real-world application are overshadowed by the methodological inconsistencies. For a venue like NIPS, which emphasizes technical rigor, these issues are particularly concerning.

## Recommendation
Reject. The paper contains multiple significant technical errors and presentation flaws that prevent it from meeting the high standards of NIPS. The authors should address these issues before the paper can be considered for publication. The core idea is promising, but the current version lacks the technical precision required for a NIPS submission.
