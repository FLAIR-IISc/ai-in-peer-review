- Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm that tracks time-varying graphical model structure via weighted sufficient statistics and probabilistic relearning.  
- Uses Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values to weight observations and decide when to relearn structure.  
- Derives rigorous weighted update equations for means, covariances, and effective sample size.  
- Provides theoretical analysis characterizing convergence vs. diligence based on parameters γ and T (proof sketch).  
- Demonstrates comprehensive simulations comparing LoSST to a batch PC baseline across structure and parameter change scenarios.  
- Applies LoSST to US price index volatility and detects the Great Moderation, showing practical utility.  
- Claims novelty as the first general-purpose online method for time-varying graphical structure learning.  
- Creative mechanism for distinguishing outliers from structural changes using pooled p-values.  
- Shows no performance penalty in stable environments, matching batch methods when structure is static.  
- Limited theoretical depth: convergence vs. diligence analysis is brief and would benefit from fuller proofs.  
- Applicability is restricted to recursive SEMs with continuous variables; extensions to discrete or other models are vague.  
- Comparison to alternative online structure-learning approaches is limited and could be expanded.  
- Unclear handling of very rapid structure changes under the “locally stationary” assumption.  
- Minor clarity issues: transition from Mahalanobis distances to pooled p-values and link to probabilistic relearning need clearer exposition.  
- Discussion of potential application domains and concrete examples is somewhat vague.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — solid, novel contribution with strong experiments and practical relevance despite some theoretical and scope limitations.
