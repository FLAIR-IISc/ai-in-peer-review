- Summary: Proposes LoSST, a single-pass online algorithm that maintains weighted sufficient statistics and adaptively forgets old data using pooled p-values of Mahalanobis distances to trigger probabilistic DAG structure relearning (PC).

- Relation to prior work: Positions itself against batch PC, TDL, CPD and some time-varying graph work but omits key literature on time-varying GGMs/DBNs and sliding-window/exponential-forgetting baselines.

- Soundness: Weighted mean/covariance updates and effective sample size heuristic are reasonable, but pooled p-values and calibration assumptions (effective N, independence) are questionable and proofs are sketchy without structural-consistency guarantees.

- Experiments: Simulations show LoSST tracks abrupt changes and saves compute via probabilistic relearning, but baselines are weak and missing important comparisons; limited sensitivity, robustness, high-dimensional, and computational analyses.

- Reproducibility: Core equations given but many implementation details (CI tests with weighted stats, regularization, parameter selection, code) are missing.

- Clarity: Writing and algorithm description are generally clear, but there are notation slips, incomplete citations, and missing complexity/numerical-stability discussion.

- Originality: Novel practical combination of online sufficient-statistics tracking with pooled p-value–driven forgetting and probabilistic relearning for DAGs, but contribution is more engineering than theoretical.

- Significance: Important practical problem and a promising lightweight template, but empirical support and calibration/compute discussion are insufficient for broad adoption.

- Pros: Addresses an under-explored online DAG problem; simple and implementable design; sensible simulations and honest trade-off discussion; useful convergence vs. diligence framing.

- Cons: Lacks strong online baselines; heuristic/statistical components lack calibration; theoretical claims incomplete; limited experimental breadth; clarity and computational detail gaps.

- Suggestions: Add strong baselines (windowed PC, EWMA, BOCPD+PC), systematic sensitivity and robustness analyses, p-value calibration checks, alternative detectors, and detail computational/regularization updates and runtimes.

- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising but needs stronger empirical comparisons, calibration/robustness analyses, and broader related-work and computational detail.
