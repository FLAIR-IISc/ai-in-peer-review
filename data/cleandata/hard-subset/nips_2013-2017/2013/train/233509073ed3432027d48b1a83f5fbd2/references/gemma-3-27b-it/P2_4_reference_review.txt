## Review of "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging problem of learning graphical model structure in non-stationary environments, specifically when data arrives sequentially (online learning). Existing structure learning algorithms assume a fixed underlying graph, making them unsuitable for dynamic systems. The authors propose LoSST (Locally Stationary Structure Tracker), a heuristic algorithm that tracks sufficient statistics online and dynamically relearns the graphical model structure when changes are detected based on Mahalanobis distance and a pooled p-value.  The algorithm aims to be performant during both stable and changing periods, and avoids the costs of batch-mode learning when the structure is stable.  The paper includes simulation results demonstrating LoSST’s ability to track changes in both graph structure and parameters, as well as a real-world application to US price index data.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The authors correctly position their work within the context of both Bayesian/score-based and constraint-based graphical model structure learning. They clearly articulate the limitations of existing online learning techniques (TDL, changepoint detection) for this specific task.  The related work section effectively distinguishes LoSST from prior attempts at time-indexed graph learning (Talih & Hengartner 2005, Siracusa & Fisher III 2009), highlighting its online capability and frequentist approach.  The references are generally comprehensive and relevant.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Learning in non-stationary environments is critical for real-world applications. This paper fills a significant gap in the literature by proposing a method for online graphical model structure learning.
* **Practical Algorithm:** LoSST is conceptually sound and seems practically implementable. The use of sufficient statistics tracking and dynamic weighting of data points provides a reasonable approach to handle changing structure.
* **Theoretical Considerations:** The discussion of convergence vs. diligence is insightful and demonstrates a thoughtful consideration of the algorithm’s properties. Establishing provable diligence (under certain parameters) is a notable strength.
* **Empirical Validation:** The simulation results provide compelling evidence that LoSST can effectively track changes in both graph structure and parameters. The comparison to batch-mode learning (PC algorithm) clearly demonstrates the benefits of online learning in dynamic scenarios. The application to US price index data provides a real-world demonstration of its usefulness.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to understand the proposed algorithm and its theoretical underpinnings.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Heuristic Nature:** The algorithm relies on several heuristic choices (e.g., Mahalanobis distance threshold, parameter ν for relearning, weighting scheme based on pooled p-value).  The sensitivity of performance to these choices is not thoroughly investigated. While the authors acknowledge this, more detailed ablation studies or a sensitivity analysis would be beneficial.
* **Parameter Tuning:** The parameter settings (T = 0.05, γ = 3, ν = 0.005) appear somewhat arbitrary, chosen through "unsystematic investigation." A more principled approach to parameter selection would strengthen the results.
* **Computational Cost:** Although the authors mention computational expense, a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of LoSST (particularly the graphical model relearning step) would be valuable. While tracking sufficient statistics is efficient, repeated calls to the PC algorithm could be a bottleneck for large-scale problems.
* **Limited Scope of Simulation:** The simulation scenarios are somewhat limited in their diversity. Expanding the simulations to include more complex graph structures, different types of changes (e.g., gradual changes instead of abrupt ones), and higher-dimensional data would enhance the robustness of the evaluation.
* **Real-World Application Depth:** The application to US price index data is suggestive but lacks depth. A more rigorous evaluation, potentially including a comparison to alternative time-series modeling techniques, would be desirable.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and valuable contribution to the field of graphical model learning. The paper addresses an important problem, proposes a practical and theoretically motivated algorithm, and provides convincing empirical evidence of its effectiveness. While the heuristic nature of the algorithm and the limited scope of the simulations represent weaknesses, they do not fundamentally detract from the paper’s overall quality.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant open problem in graphical model learning.
* Proposes a novel and practical algorithm (LoSST).
* Provides strong empirical evidence of LoSST’s performance.
* Demonstrates insightful theoretical considerations regarding convergence and diligence.
* Well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Reliance on heuristic choices without thorough sensitivity analysis.
* Limited exploration of parameter space.
* Computational cost of relearning step is not fully addressed.
* Simulations could be more diverse and rigorous.




**Rating:**  **Strong Accept (7/10)**. 

I believe this paper is a significant contribution and deserves to be accepted to NIPS.  I recommend the authors address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly concerning parameter tuning and sensitivity analysis, in a future version of the work.
