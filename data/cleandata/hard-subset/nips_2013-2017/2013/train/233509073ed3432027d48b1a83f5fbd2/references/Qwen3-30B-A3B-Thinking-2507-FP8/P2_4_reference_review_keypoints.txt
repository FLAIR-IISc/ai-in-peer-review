- Introduces LoSST for online learning of time-varying graphical structure, tracking means/covariances/sample size with weighted updates and probabilistic relearning using Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values.

- Novelty: fills a gap between static structure learning and changepoint detection; pooled p-values weighting and probabilistic relearning are methodological contributions.

- Practical significance: designed for streaming settings, processes data in one pass, adapts quickly to structural changes, and demonstrated on US price index volatility.

- Empirical performance: matches batch methods during stable periods and outperforms after structural changes; validated across multiple graph sizes, degrees, and change types.

- Theoretical grounding: provides thoughtful discussion (convergence vs. diligence) but lacks formal proofs and rigorous performance guarantees.

- Parameter selection: key parameters (e.g., T=0.05, γ=3) chosen by unsystematic search; needs principled selection or sensitivity analysis.

- Scope limitation: focused on recursive SEMs (Gaussian DAGs); extensions to discrete or more complex graphical models and scalability are not demonstrated.

- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear mathematical exposition and informative figures.

- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and practically useful contribution; recommended for acceptance despite theoretical and scope limitations.

- Pro-accept reasons: addresses important gap, novel algorithm with clear motivation, comprehensive experiments, real-world utility, and clear presentation.

- Con-accept reasons: lacks formal theoretical justification, parameter choices need better justification, and limited discussion/demonstration of extensions.
