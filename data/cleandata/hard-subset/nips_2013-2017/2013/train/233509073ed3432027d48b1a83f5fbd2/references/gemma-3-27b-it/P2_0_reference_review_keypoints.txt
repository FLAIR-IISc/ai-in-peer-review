- Tackles the important problem of learning graphical model structure in non-stationary, online settings.
- Proposes LoSST, a practical heuristic that dynamically updates sufficient statistics and relearns structure based on fit to incoming data.
- Algorithm leverages temporal difference learning and change point detection concepts.
- Demonstrates via simulations that LoSST tracks changes in graph structure and parameters effectively.
- Shows LoSST performs comparably to batch PC during stable periods and better when changes occur.
- Includes a real-world case study on US price index volatility.
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical exposition and algorithm description.
- Provides a thoughtful discussion of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and parameter effects.
- Strength: addresses a significant gap in existing graphical model literature for streaming data.
- Strength: comprehensive simulation evaluation and comparison to a standard batch method.
- Strength: theoretical grounding in established online/adaptive learning concepts.
- Weakness: LoSST is heuristic and some design choices (e.g., weight update formula, Mahalanobis distance) appear ad-hoc.
- Weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of algorithm properties and guarantees.
- Weakness: performance sensitive to parameters (T, γ) with limited guidance on selection.
- Weakness: computational cost of frequent relearning with PC can be high, especially in high dimensions.
- Weakness: limited real-world evaluation (single case study), reducing confidence in broad applicability.
- Quality assessment: technically sound and complete but weakened by heuristic elements and limited theory.
- Originality/significance: novel and potentially impactful approach for dynamic graphical model learning.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions emphasizing more justification for design choices, systematic sensitivity analysis, discussion/optimization of computational cost, and broader empirical validation.
