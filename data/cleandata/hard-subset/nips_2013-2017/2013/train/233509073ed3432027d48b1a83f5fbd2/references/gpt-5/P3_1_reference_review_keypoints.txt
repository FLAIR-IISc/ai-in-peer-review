- Proposal: LoSST tracks time-varying linear-Gaussian DAGs using exponentially weighted sufficient statistics with adaptive weights triggered by pooled Mahalanobis p-values and probabilistic PC relearning.
- Positioning: Compared to batch PC, BOCPD, and time-indexed graph methods, but misses key prior work (dynamic BNs, time-varying graphical models, CPD for dependency structures).
- Strength: Tackles an important single-pass online nonstationary structure-tracking problem.
- Strength: Simple, modular design separating statistic tracking from structure learning.
- Strength: Memory-efficient online updates (only O(p^2) covariance stored) without replay.
- Strength: Offers useful conceptual analysis of convergence vs diligence regimes.
- Strength: Synthetic experiments show advantages over batch PC after abrupt changes and reduced computation via probabilistic relearning.
- Strength: Real-world CPI case study plausibly illustrates detection of sustained volatility shifts.
- Major concern: Pooled p-values lack statistical validity—p-values are dependent/weighted and Hotelling T^2 with the proposed Sr is unjustified under nonstationarity.
- Major concern: No analysis or calibration of false-alarm rate or detection delay under model misspecification.
- Major concern: The proposed ESS update is ad hoc and inconsistent with standard weighted ESS formulas, affecting test calibration and DoF.
- Major concern: CI testing is unstable for small Sr and lacks regularization/shrinkage to ensure PD covariances or stable partial correlations.
- Baseline gap: Comparisons only to batch PC; missing sliding-window PC, CPD+reset, and time-varying GLasso or other natural baselines.
- Hyperparameter issue: Sensitivity to T, γ, ν is unexplored and no practical tuning guidance is provided.
- Numerical/scalability issue: No runtime or scalability measurements; inverting Cr online may be unstable for p ≥ Sr or near-singular covariance.
- Real-data limitation: CPI study is qualitative without external validation, predictive checks, or domain-expert sanity checks.
- Related-work gap: Under-covers time-varying precision/graph estimation and change-detection literature, weakening novelty claims.
- Quality: Method is coherent and implementable but rests on heuristic statistical constructs with limited theoretical guarantees and suggestive experiments.
- Clarity: Generally well-written, though key derivations (Eq. 2/3), and definitions around T^2 and Sr need more detail; minor typos.
- Originality: Novel practical combination of ideas, but situated in a well-studied area and lacking thorough comparison to related methods.
- Significance: Potentially useful in practice if strengthened, but current empirical/theoretical grounding is insufficient for strong claims.
- Suggested fixes: Calibrate or replace the change score (EWMA/CUSUM/BOCPD), justify ESS, and adapt CI tests to weighted data with shrinkage.
- Suggested fixes: Add strong baselines (sliding-window, CPD+reset, TV-GLasso), sensitivity analyses, ablations (pooling/no pooling), and runtime/scalability results.
- Suggested fixes: Extend simulations to gradual drift, non-Gaussian noise/outliers, and strengthen real-data validation with predictive checks and stability analyses.
- Overall recommendation: Weak reject — promising idea but needs stronger statistical justification, comparative evaluation, and robustness analyses.
