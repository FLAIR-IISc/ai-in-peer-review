

# Review of "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure"

## Summary
This paper addresses an important gap in graphical model learning: the inability of current methods to learn structure from data with time-varying generating processes. The authors propose LoSST (Locally Stationary Structure Tracker), an online algorithm that tracks sufficient statistics for graphical model structure learning while dynamically responding to structural changes. The paper builds on related work in online learning (TD(0)), changepoint detection, and graphical model structure learning, distinguishing itself by enabling real-time structure learning without requiring batch data processing. The authors provide theoretical development of their approach, simulation results demonstrating performance advantages over batch methods during structural changes, and a real-world application to US price index volatility.

## Strengths

The paper makes a strong contribution to the field by addressing a genuinely important problem that has been overlooked in previous work. The motivation is compelling, and the authors clearly articulate the limitations of existing methods in handling time-varying structures. The technical development of LoSST is sound and well-justified, particularly the use of Mahalanobis distances and weighted pooling of p-values to detect structural changes. The simulation results are thorough and demonstrate that LoSST performs comparably to batch methods during stable periods and significantly better after structural changes. The real-world application to US price index data provides a meaningful validation of the approach. The paper also thoughtfully discusses the trade-off between convergence and diligence, providing valuable theoretical insights about the limitations of learning algorithms in dynamic environments.

## Weaknesses

While the paper is well-written and technically sound, it has several limitations. First, the theoretical guarantees are limited to specific parameter regimes (convergent vs. diligent), and the paper doesn't provide formal guarantees for the non-convergent, non-diligent regime (0 < T < 1) that the authors use in their simulations. Second, the probabilistic relearning mechanism appears somewhat ad-hoc; the paper doesn't provide strong justification for the specific parameters used (T = 0.05, γ = 3, ν = 0.005), and the paper would benefit from a more systematic exploration of these parameters. Third, the real-world application is limited to a single dataset with only 6 variables, which doesn't fully demonstrate the algorithm's scalability to larger problems. Finally, the paper doesn't compare LoSST to alternative online approaches for structure learning (beyond the related work section), which would strengthen the case for the proposed method.

## Overall Assessment

The paper addresses an important problem in graphical model learning with a technically sound approach. The algorithm is well-motivated, the theoretical development is solid, and the empirical results are convincing. The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field, particularly for applications where online structure learning is essential.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. The paper would benefit from a more systematic exploration of the parameter space (T, γ, ν) to better understand their impact on performance and to provide more principled guidance for practitioners.

2. The authors should consider comparing LoSST to alternative online approaches for structure learning (e.g., online versions of PC or other constraint-based methods) to more clearly demonstrate the advantages of their approach.

3. The theoretical analysis could be strengthened by providing more formal guarantees for the parameter regime (0 < T < 1) that the authors use in their simulations.

4. The real-world application could be expanded to include larger datasets or more diverse applications to better demonstrate scalability and practical utility.

5. The paper should more clearly define terms like "diligence" and "convergence" early in the paper for readers unfamiliar with these concepts.

## Recommendation

This paper presents a novel and valuable approach to online graphical model structure learning. While the theoretical guarantees could be strengthened and some aspects of the approach could be better justified, the paper makes a solid contribution to the field. The strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses, and with the suggested improvements, it would be a strong candidate for publication at NeurIPS.

**Pro Acceptance**: Strong problem motivation, technically sound algorithm, thorough empirical validation, clear theoretical insights about convergence vs. diligence trade-offs.

**Con Against Acceptance**: Limited theoretical guarantees for the main parameter regime used, somewhat ad-hoc probabilistic relearning mechanism, limited real-world validation on larger datasets.

The paper meets the quality, clarity, originality, and significance criteria for NeurIPS, with minor weaknesses that could be addressed in a revised version. I recommend acceptance.
