- Tackles online learning of time-varying graphical structures via LoSST, which tracks sufficient statistics and relearns graphs using Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values for change detection.  
- Positions work well relative to prior batch and online methods, highlighting advantages of a frequentist online approach and discussing convergence vs. diligence trade-offs.  
- Addresses an important and underexplored problem with clear real-time applicability.  
- Provides online capability to process sequential data and adapt to changing structures.  
- Presents a clear algorithm with well-defined update equations and intuitive use of Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values.  
- Offers theoretical discussion on the convergence/diligence trade-off and parameter influences (γ, T).  
- Empirically validated: simulations show LoSST tracks changing graphs and matches batch methods when stable; includes a real-world US price index application.  
- Discusses practical deployment issues like parameter tuning and a probabilistic relearning schedule.  
- Heuristic nature: key algorithmic choices (e.g., max in weight update Eq.4) lack rigorous justification.  
- Sensitive to parameters (γ, T, ν) with only unsystematic investigation; needs thorough sensitivity analysis across dimensions and change rates.  
- Lacks explicit computational complexity analysis, particularly for the graph relearning step, raising scalability concerns.  
- Limited simulation scope: should test pure parameter shifts, very rapid structural changes, and a wider range of graph sizes/complexities.  
- Real-world application is superficial and needs deeper economic interpretation of detected structural changes and implications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant problem, novel practical solution, solid empirical/theoretical evidence, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heuristic design without full justification, parameter sensitivity, limited simulations, and shallow real-world analysis.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, promising contribution with addressable weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; suggest justifying algorithmic choices, performing parameter sensitivity and complexity analyses, expanding simulation scenarios, and deepening the real-world analysis.
