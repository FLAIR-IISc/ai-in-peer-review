- Tackles online learning of time-varying graphical model structure and proposes LoSST, an online tracker with change detection and periodic relearning.  
- Novelty: combines online sufficient-statistic tracking with change detection and adapts Temporal Difference Learning ideas for covariance updates.  
- Method: uses a Mahalanobis distance–based change detector, weighted sample sizes, and probabilistic relearning with the PC algorithm for structure learning.  
- Empirical results: simulations show LoSST tracks structural and parameter changes and matches batch performance during stable periods; applied to US price index volatility.  
- Positioning: situates work well relative to batch and online methods and TDL; would benefit from discussion of dynamic Bayesian networks (DBNs).  
- Theoretical contribution: analyzes the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and specifies conditions for each behavior.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and explains the algorithm in enough detail for implementation.  
- Heuristic dependence: heavy reliance on heuristics and ad-hoc parameter choices (γ, T, ν) with insufficient systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Computational cost: relearning and use of the PC algorithm may be a runtime bottleneck; lacks detailed runtime comparisons.  
- Change detection limits: Mahalanobis-based detector is sensitive to outliers and pooling may not robustly separate spurious signals from true structural changes.  
- Scalability concerns: unclear how LoSST performs in high-dimensional settings due to the PC algorithm’s complexity.  
- Pooling choice: choice of Liptak method for pooling p-values is not well-explained or justified.  
- Pro acceptance points: addresses an important problem, presents a novel and practical algorithm, provides empirical validation and theoretical analysis, and is clearly written.  
- Con acceptance points: relies on heuristics, may be computationally costly, may be sensitive to outliers, and has unclear scalability.  
- Suggested revisions: perform systematic sensitivity analysis, provide runtime/computational comparisons, investigate outlier-robust change detection, discuss scalability strategies, and justify the Liptak pooling choice.
