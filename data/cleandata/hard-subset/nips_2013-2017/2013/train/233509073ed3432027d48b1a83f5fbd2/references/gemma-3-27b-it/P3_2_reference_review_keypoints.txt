- Paper proposes LoSST, an online heuristic algorithm that tracks time-varying graphical model structure by weighting recent data and updating sufficient statistics.  
- LoSST uses the PC algorithm for structure learning and adapts weights based on model fit to detect structural changes.  
- Positions LoSST relative to prior work: differs from Bayesian/constraint-based, changepoint detection, and batch time-varying methods; discusses limitations of Temporal Difference Learning.  
- Citations and related-work coverage are comprehensive and appropriate.  

Strengths:
- Addresses an under-explored problem: true online structure learning for graphical models.  
- Practical design leveraging established tools (PC algorithm, weighted sufficient statistics).  
- Explicit discussion of convergence vs. diligence and parameter effects, with proof sketches.  
- Convincing simulations showing tracking of structure/parameter changes and competitive batch-period performance.  
- Real-world application to US price index volatility and clear, well-organized writing.  

Weaknesses:
- Algorithm is heuristic-heavy (e.g., Mahalanobis distance, specific weighting, noisy-OR) with limited justification or exploration of alternatives.  
- Computational cost concerns: repeated PC runs and scalability to high-dimensional data not fully analyzed.  
- Simulations focus on relatively simple graphs and change scenarios, lacking more complex/frequent-change settings.  
- Sensitivity to tuning parameters (T, γ, ν) is insufficiently explored, including in the real-world example.  
- Limited discussion of using alternative structure-learning algorithms better suited for high dimensions.  

Pro acceptance arguments:
- Tackles a significant, novel problem and offers a practical, well-motivated solution with good empirical support and clear exposition.  

Con acceptance arguments:
- Reliance on heuristics, unclear computational scaling, limited simulation scope, and underexplored parameter sensitivity.  

Overall assessment:
- Valuable contribution likely worth acceptance, with weaknesses addressable via minor revisions.  

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions.  

Required revisions suggested by reviewer:
- Justify heuristic choices and explore alternatives.  
- Provide a detailed discussion/analysis of LoSST’s computational complexity and mitigation strategies.  
- Expand simulations to cover more complex graphs and change patterns.  
- Analyze parameter tuning and its impact on detection and tracking.  
- Discuss potential use and impact of alternative structure-learning algorithms within LoSST.
