- Tackles learning graphical model structure in non-stationary, online settings where batch i.i.d. assumptions fail.  
- Proposes LoSST: an online heuristic that tracks sufficient statistics and dynamically relearns structure when fit degrades.  
- Core idea: differentially weight observations to emphasize recent data that better explain current behavior.  
- Aims to match batch performance when structure is stable and outperform when structure changes.  
- Demonstrates promising simulation results and a real-world application to US price index volatility.  
- Novelty: combines online sufficient-statistics tracking with change-triggered graph relearning, addressing a gap in online structure learning.  
- Provides theoretical framework with update equations and proofs/sketches for convergence and diligence trade-offs.  
- Empirical validation shows LoSST can track changing structures and compete with standard batch methods.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and clearly explains the algorithm and theory.  
- Heuristic design choices (Mahalanobis distance, pooled p-value, weight updates) lack strong justification and need sensitivity analysis.  
- Computational cost of repeated graph relearning (using PC algorithm) is potentially high and under-analyzed, especially as variable count grows.  
- Limited real-world evaluation; US price index case is promising but interpretations are speculative and more datasets are needed.  
- Performance depends on the PC algorithm; alternative structure learners might change outcomes and aren’t explored.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 4/5, Clarity 5/5, Originality 4/5, Significance 4/5.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel/theoretically grounded method, promising experiments, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on heuristics, potential computational bottlenecks, limited real-world validation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add detailed sensitivity analysis, analyze/discuss computational complexity and optimizations, and evaluate on additional real-world datasets.
