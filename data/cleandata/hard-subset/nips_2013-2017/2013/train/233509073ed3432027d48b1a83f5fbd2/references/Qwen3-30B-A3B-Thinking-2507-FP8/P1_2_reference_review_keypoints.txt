- Introduces LoSST: an online algorithm tracking time-varying graphical model structure via online sufficient statistics and adaptive weight updates.
- Uses Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values to detect structural changes and adjust learning parameters online.
- Addresses a literature gap: most existing methods assume static i.i.d. data and cannot learn structure in real time.
- Theoretical contribution: formal discussion and proof sketch of a fundamental convergence vs. diligence tradeoff for online structure tracking.
- Empirical results: comprehensive simulations showing LoSST matches batch methods in stable periods and outperforms after structure changes.
- Real-world validation: application to US price index volatility data demonstrating practical utility.
- Strength: strong motivation and clear problem framing grounded in real-world needs for online structure learning.
- Strength: technically sound algorithm with clear mathematical formulation for tracking sufficient statistics.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation across multiple scenarios and graph sizes, with PC algorithm as a baseline.
- Strength: insightful and well-justified analysis of the convergence vs. diligence tradeoff.
- Strength: honest discussion of limitations and suggested future work.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis is limited—proofs are sketchy and no formal error bounds for structure learning are provided.
- Weakness: parameter sensitivity not thoroughly analyzed; selected parameter values lack strong justification.
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to other possible online or adapted constraint-based methods.
- Weakness: insufficient implementation details on how the PC component is updated online and on computational complexity.
- Weakness: real-world results lack concrete interpretation of what structural changes (e.g., "Great Moderation") mean in graph terms.
- Quality assessment: paper is technically solid, well-supported by theory and experiments, and avoids overclaiming.
- Clarity assessment: well-written and reproducible for experts; pooled p-value calculation could use more intuitive explanation.
- Originality: novel problem framing and method—online time-varying graphical structure learning using pooled p-values is new.
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem with clear applicability in finance, neuroscience, and social networks.
- Recommendation: accept—strengths outweigh weaknesses and the paper is a meaningful contribution.
- Improvement 1: provide a detailed parameter sensitivity analysis for T, γ, ν and guidance for practitioners.
- Improvement 2: give concrete interpretation of detected structural changes in the US price index application.
- Improvement 3: compare LoSST to online/adapted versions of PC or other constraint-based methods.
- Improvement 4: expand theoretical discussion with more formal statements and illustrative examples of the tradeoff.
- Improvement 5: clarify computational complexity and the cost of probabilistic relearning steps.
