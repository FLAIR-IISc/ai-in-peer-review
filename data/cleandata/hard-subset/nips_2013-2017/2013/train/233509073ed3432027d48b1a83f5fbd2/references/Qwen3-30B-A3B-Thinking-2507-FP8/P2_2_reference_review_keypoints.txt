- Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm for learning time-varying graphical model structure.  
- Tracks locally stationary sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size) online.  
- Uses Mahalanobis-distance weighting and pooled p-values to weight data and trigger relearning.  
- Provides theoretical analysis, including a proven trade-off between convergence and diligence.  
- Derives weighted update equations and characterizes algorithm behavior under different parameters.  
- Extensive simulations show LoSST matches batch methods in stable periods and outperforms them after structural changes.  
- Real-world application to US price index volatility demonstrates detection of meaningful economic shifts (Great Moderation).  
- Acknowledges limitations: performance degrades with very rapid changes or when data is a random mixture from multiple structures.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized with clear algorithm description and informative figures.  
- Minor clarity issues: insufficient justification for choosing Liptak’s method for pooling p-values.  
- Minor clarity issues: more justification needed for specific parameter choices (T=0.05, γ=3).  
- Novel combination of Mahalanobis weighting, pooled p-value tracking, and probabilistic relearning—first true online solution with minimal memory.  
- No significant overlap with recent NIPS proceedings; contribution appears original.  
- Addresses a significant practical problem with wide applicability (finance, social networks, biology).  
- Demonstrates no extra cost in stable periods while providing substantial benefit after changes.  
- Could strengthen impact by explicitly detailing computational complexity and scalability.  
- Real-world application would benefit from deeper analysis of the insights gained.  
- Probabilistic relearning mechanism is practical but lacks theoretical justification.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically strong, original, well-validated, and significant for the community.
