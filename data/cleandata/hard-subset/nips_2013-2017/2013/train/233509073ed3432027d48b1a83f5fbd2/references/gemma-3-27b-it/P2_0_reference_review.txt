## Review of "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning graphical model structure in a non-stationary environment, specifically when data arrives sequentially (online learning).  Existing methods assume i.i.d. data or require batch processing, making them unsuitable for dynamic real-world scenarios. The authors propose the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST) algorithm, a heuristic approach that dynamically adjusts sufficient statistics and relearns the graphical model structure based on the fit between the current model and incoming data.  The algorithm leverages concepts from temporal difference learning and change point detection.  The authors demonstrate through simulations that LoSST can effectively track changes in both graph structure and parameters, performing comparably to batch methods during stable periods and outperforming them when changes occur. A real-world application to US price index volatility is also presented.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Learning in non-stationary environments is crucial for many real-world applications, and this work tackles a significant gap in existing graphical model literature.
* **Practical Algorithm:** LoSST is designed for online learning, making it directly applicable to streaming data scenarios where batch processing is impractical.
* **Theoretical Grounding:**  The algorithm is grounded in established concepts like temporal difference learning and change point detection, providing a sensible framework for dynamic adaptation.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The simulations are well-designed, exploring changes in both graph structure and parameters. They also include comparisons against a standard batch-mode algorithm (PC) and investigate the trade-off between convergence and diligence. The real-world application provides a compelling case study.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the algorithm’s mechanics.  The mathematical formulations are presented clearly and logically.
* **Discussion of Tradeoffs:** The insightful discussion of convergence vs. diligence and the exploration of different parameter settings demonstrate a good understanding of the algorithm's limitations and potential.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Heuristic Nature:** LoSST is explicitly described as a heuristic, and the justification for certain design choices (e.g., the specific formula for updating weights, the choice of the Mahalanobis distance) feels somewhat ad-hoc. More rigorous theoretical analysis of the algorithm's properties would strengthen the paper.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:**  The performance appears to be sensitive to parameters like *T* and *γ*, and the optimal settings likely depend on the specific application.  More guidance on parameter selection would be helpful. While the authors explore some settings, a more systematic investigation would be beneficial.
* **Computational Cost:** While the algorithm is designed for online learning, the relearning step using the PC algorithm can be computationally expensive, especially for high-dimensional graphs. This is acknowledged but could be discussed in more detail, along with potential alternative (and potentially faster) structure learning algorithms.
* **Limited Real-World Evaluation:** While the application to US price indices is interesting, it’s a single case study.  Expanding the empirical evaluation to other real-world datasets would strengthen the claims of practical applicability.



**Addressing Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, although the heuristic nature of the algorithm and some design choices weaken the theoretical justification. The experimental results are well-presented and support the claims.  It’s a complete piece of work in the sense that it presents a functional algorithm and evaluation, but a deeper theoretical analysis would enhance the quality.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and generally easy to understand. The mathematical notation is clear and the explanations are logical.
* **Originality:** The problem itself is well-motivated and the approach of combining online statistics tracking with dynamic relearning of graphical model structure is novel. The authors correctly position their work relative to existing literature.
* **Significance:** The potential impact of this work is significant. Enabling online learning of graphical models in dynamic environments has broad applicability across various fields, including finance, healthcare, and environmental monitoring. The demonstrated improvements over batch methods in changing environments highlight the practical value of the approach.

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant and unsolved problem in graphical model learning.
* Proposes a practical and well-motivated algorithm.
* Provides compelling simulation results demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach.
* Clear and well-written presentation.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The heuristic nature of the algorithm and lack of strong theoretical guarantees.
* Potential sensitivity to parameter settings.
* Limited real-world evaluation.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed LoSST algorithm offers a practical solution to the challenging problem of learning time-varying graphical structures. While the heuristic nature of the algorithm and the limited real-world evaluation are weaknesses, the strengths of the paper – the importance of the problem, the practical algorithm, the compelling simulations, and the clear writing – outweigh these concerns. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept** with minor revisions. 

I recommend the authors consider the following revisions:

*   Elaborate on the justification for specific design choices in the algorithm (e.g., the weight update formula).
*   Provide more guidance on parameter selection and potentially conduct a more systematic sensitivity analysis.
*   Discuss the computational cost of the relearning step and explore potential optimizations.
*   Consider expanding the empirical evaluation to include additional real-world datasets.
