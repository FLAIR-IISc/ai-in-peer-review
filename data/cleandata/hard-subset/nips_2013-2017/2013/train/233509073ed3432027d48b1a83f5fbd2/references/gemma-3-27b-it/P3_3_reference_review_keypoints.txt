- Tackles the important problem of learning graphical model structure in non-stationary, online settings with limited data.
- Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm that tracks sufficient statistics and re-learns structure when change is detected.
- Uses differential weighting of datapoints (Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values) to emphasize recent data after changes.
- Positions LoSST as a frequentist, real-time alternative to Bayesian and batch time-indexed methods, with connections to Temporal Difference Learning.
- Novel combination of online statistic tracking and dynamic graph re-learning addressing a gap in existing methods.
- Authors provide a theoretical framework analyzing the convergence vs. diligence trade-off via γ and T parameters.
- Empirical results show LoSST tracking structural and parameter changes in simulations and outperforming batch PC in dynamic settings.
- Real-world case study on US price index data demonstrates practical applicability.
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly organized, and explains the algorithm in detail.
- Method relies on heuristics (choice of PC algorithm and weighting functions), raising sensitivity concerns.
- Requires careful tuning of multiple parameters (α, γ, T, ν); current choices (e.g., T=0.05, γ=3) lack strong justification.
- Theoretical analysis is insightful but stops short of strong performance guarantees across scenarios.
- Computational cost of repeated graph re-learning (PC algorithm) may be high for high-dimensional data and is under-discussed.
- Real-world evaluation is limited to a single dataset; broader empirical validation is needed.
- Pros: addresses a critical problem, introduces a promising algorithm, offers theoretical insight, and shows strong empirical performance.
- Cons: heuristic dependence, parameter sensitivity, limited theoretical guarantees, and potential computational bottlenecks.
- Key author questions: provide sensitivity analysis for α, γ, T, ν; consider alternative structure learners; detail computational complexity; extendability to discrete variables; and deeper interpretation of US price index results.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, specifically expanding sensitivity analysis, discussing computational complexity, and addressing alternative structure learning options.
