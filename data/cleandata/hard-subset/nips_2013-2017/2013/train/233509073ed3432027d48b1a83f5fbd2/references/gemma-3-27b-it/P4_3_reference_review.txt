## Review of “Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure”

This paper introduces the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST), a heuristic algorithm for online learning of graphical model structure in non-stationary environments. The core idea is to track sufficient statistics online and dynamically relearn the graphical model structure when changes are detected via a Mahalanobis distance-based change point detection mechanism. The authors position their work within the context of limitations of existing structure learning algorithms, which assume i.i.d. data, and existing online learning methods, which either lack the capacity for general graphical model learning or require storing substantial amounts of data.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly articulating the problem of learning in non-stationary graphical models and highlighting the limitations of current approaches. The proposed LoSST algorithm is logically presented, with a detailed explanation of the update equations for weighted means, covariances, sample sizes, and change detection weights. The inclusion of a discussion on the trade-off between convergence and diligence is a valuable addition, demonstrating a thoughtful consideration of the algorithm’s behavior. The simulation results are comprehensive, covering scenarios with changes in both structure and parameters, and the real-world application to US price index volatility provides a practical demonstration of the algorithm. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:**  The approach of combining online sufficient statistic tracking with dynamic graphical model relearning is a novel contribution. While individual components (online statistics, change point detection, structure learning) are known, their integration for this specific problem is new.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanations of the algorithm’s steps are detailed and intuitive.
* **Technical Soundness:** The algorithm appears technically sound, with well-defined update equations and a logical change detection mechanism. The convergence/diligence analysis adds to the theoretical grounding.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The simulation results are thorough, examining different scenarios and comparing LoSST to a batch-mode baseline. The real-world application further validates the algorithm’s potential.
* **Discussion of Tradeoffs:** The explicit acknowledgement and discussion of the convergence vs. diligence tradeoff adds significant depth to the analysis.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Heuristic Nature:** The algorithm relies on several heuristic choices, such as the Mahalanobis distance threshold (T), the weight scaling factor (γ), and the relearning probability (ν). While these are explored in the simulations, a more formal analysis of their impact on performance would be beneficial.
* **Computational Cost:** Although the paper claims LoSST can function as a single-pass learner for large datasets, the computational cost of the PC algorithm (used for structure learning) is not thoroughly addressed, particularly as the number of variables increases.  The cost of relearning the graph frequently could become a bottleneck.
* **Sensitivity to Outliers:** The Mahalanobis distance is sensitive to outliers. The paper acknowledges this but doesn’t deeply explore strategies for robustifying the change detection process beyond the pooled p-value approach.
* **Limited Comparison to Alternative Online Methods:**  The paper focuses on comparing to a batch-mode algorithm. A more comprehensive comparison to other online change point detection and adaptive graphical model learning methods (if they exist) would strengthen the evaluation.
* **Lack of Theoretical Guarantees:** Beyond the convergence/diligence discussion, the paper lacks formal theoretical guarantees on the performance of LoSST, such as bounds on the error rate or convergence speed.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant and practically relevant problem – learning in non-stationary graphical models.
* The proposed LoSST algorithm is novel, technically sound, and well-explained.
* The evaluation is comprehensive, including simulations and a real-world application.
* The paper demonstrates a thoughtful understanding of the tradeoffs involved in the algorithm’s design.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The algorithm relies on several heuristic choices, and a more formal analysis of their impact would be valuable.
* The computational cost of the structure learning step could be a limitation for large datasets.
* The paper lacks strong theoretical guarantees on performance.




**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of graphical model learning. While the algorithm is heuristic and lacks formal guarantees, its novelty, clarity, technical soundness, and comprehensive evaluation make it a strong candidate for acceptance. The authors have clearly articulated the problem, proposed a reasonable solution, and demonstrated its potential through simulations and a real-world application. I recommend **acceptance**, with a minor recommendation for the authors to acknowledge the potential computational bottlenecks and explore strategies for mitigating them in the camera-ready version.
