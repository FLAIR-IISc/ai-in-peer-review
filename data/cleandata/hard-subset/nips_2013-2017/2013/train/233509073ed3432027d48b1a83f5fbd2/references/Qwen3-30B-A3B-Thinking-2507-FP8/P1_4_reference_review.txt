

# Peer Review: "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure"

## Summary

This paper introduces the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST) algorithm for online learning of graphical model structures when the underlying structure may change over time. The authors identify a critical gap in the literature: standard graphical model structure learning algorithms assume stationary data (i.i.d.), while real-world data often involves time-varying structures. LoSST tracks sufficient statistics (means, covariances, sample size) using weighted updates that adapt to changes in the underlying structure. It uses Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values to detect structural changes and relearns the graphical model when necessary. The paper provides theoretical analysis showing how LoSST can be tuned to balance between convergence (stabilizing on stable structures) and diligence (responding quickly to changes). The authors validate the algorithm through comprehensive simulations and a real-world application to US price index volatility data.

The work distinguishes itself from previous approaches:
- Standard batch-mode structure learning (Bayesian/score-based, constraint-based) assumes stationary data
- Online learning methods like TD(0) track single variables but not graphical structures
- Changepoint detection algorithms store substantial data and assume abrupt changes
- Previous time-indexed graph structure work requires full datasets

## Strengths

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper provides clear mathematical formulations for the algorithm, including the weighted sufficient statistics update rules. The theoretical analysis of convergence vs. diligence is rigorous and well-motivated, with a proof sketch for the convergence properties. The algorithm's ability to maintain performance identical to batch learning during stable periods while adapting to changes is particularly compelling.

2. **Experimental Validation:** The simulations are well-designed, comparing LoSST to the PC algorithm in both structure change and parameter change scenarios. The results clearly demonstrate LoSST's advantage after structural changes (e.g., stabilizing within 250 datapoints after a changepoint). The real-world application to US price index volatility effectively demonstrates the algorithm's practical utility, showing detection of the Great Moderation period (1980-2007).

3. **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-organized with clear explanations of the algorithm's components. The figures effectively illustrate the algorithm's performance, and the writing is accessible to experts in graphical models. The derivation of the covariance update equation, while mathematically dense, is presented with sufficient detail for reproducibility.

4. **Significance:** The problem addressed is highly relevant across multiple domains (finance, social networks, biology, etc.). The paper demonstrates that LoSST incurs no additional cost in stable environments while providing significant advantages when structures change. This makes it applicable to many real-world scenarios where batch learning is infeasible.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Scope:** The paper focuses exclusively on recursive SEMs (linear models with normally distributed errors) and directed acyclic graphs. While the authors mention extensions to other graphical models, the theoretical and empirical results are limited to this specific case. A discussion of how the approach might generalize to discrete Bayesian networks or undirected models would strengthen the paper.

2. **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance of LoSST appears to depend on carefully chosen parameters (T and γ). The paper uses T=0.05 and γ=3 based on "unsystematic investigation" - a more systematic parameter analysis would provide better guidance for practitioners.

3. **Computational Complexity:** The paper mentions graph learning is computationally expensive but doesn't thoroughly analyze how this impacts the online nature of the algorithm. A computational complexity analysis or empirical timing results would help assess the algorithm's practical feasibility.

4. **Comparison with Recent Work:** While the related work section is comprehensive, it could better contrast with more recent online structure learning methods. For example, there has been recent work on online structure learning for Bayesian networks (e.g., "Online Bayesian Network Structure Learning" by Wang et al., 2021) that might be relevant.

## Arguments for Acceptance

- Addresses a significant gap in graphical model learning (online learning of time-varying structures)
- Provides a theoretically grounded algorithm with strong experimental validation
- Demonstrates practical utility in a real-world economic application
- Well-written and clearly organized, with sufficient detail for reproducibility

## Arguments Against Acceptance

- Limited scope to specific model types (linear SEMs, directed graphs)
- Parameter sensitivity could be better addressed
- Could include more thorough comparison with recent online structure learning work

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a clear contribution to the field of graphical models. LoSST fills a critical gap by enabling online learning of time-varying graphical structures with no additional cost in stable environments. The theoretical analysis and experimental results are convincing, and the real-world application demonstrates practical relevance.

While the scope is limited to specific model types and parameter sensitivity could be better addressed, these limitations do not undermine the paper's main contribution. The problem addressed is important, the solution is novel, and the results are compelling.

The paper meets all four NIPS criteria: it is technically sound with well-supported claims, clearly written and organized, original in addressing an important gap in the literature, and significant in its potential impact across multiple domains.

**Recommendation: Accept**
