- Addresses gap in online tracking of time-varying graphical model structure and proposes the LoSST algorithm for sequential updates and learning as the model changes.  
- Technically sound with clear mathematical derivations for online weighted sufficient-statistics updates (Equations 1–3).  
- Strong theoretical analysis of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and how parameters affect long-term behavior.  
- Comprehensive simulations (structural and parameter changes, varied variables/densities, 500 datasets per condition) showing superior post-change performance versus batch methods.  
- Real-world application to US price index volatility demonstrates practical utility and identifies meaningful economic shifts.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized; problem statement, related work, and algorithm description are clear and reproducible for experts.  
- Figures effectively present empirical results; paper provides sufficient detail though some implementation specifics could be expanded.  
- Novel contribution: first algorithm specifically designed for online graphical structure learning without storing large data batches.  
- LoSST uniquely combines Mahalanobis distance-based change detection, pooled p-value weighting, and probabilistic relearning.  
- Significant for applications where structures evolve (finance, social networks, biology), outperforming batch methods after changes while matching performance in stable periods.  
- Strengths: clear gap identification, strong theory, comprehensive experiments, practical application, thoughtful trade-off discussion.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of non-Gaussian/discrete extensions, probabilistic relearning appears somewhat ad-hoc, real-world validation limited to one small dataset (6 variables).  
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, original, significant, well-written, and valuable to researchers and practitioners.
