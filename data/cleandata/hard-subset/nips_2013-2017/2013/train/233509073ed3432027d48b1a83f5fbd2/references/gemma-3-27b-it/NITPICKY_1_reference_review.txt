The paper proposes a heuristic algorithm, LoSST, for tracking time-varying graphical model structure in an online fashion. The core idea is to maintain sufficient statistics (means, covariances, sample size) in a weighted manner, adjusting weights based on the Mahalanobis distance of incoming data points to the current model. This allows the algorithm to detect and respond to structural changes without requiring full datasets or batch processing. The authors demonstrate the algorithm's performance through simulations and a real-world application to US price index data.

While the problem addressed is genuinely important – online structure learning in non-stationary environments – and the proposed approach is reasonably intuitive, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor flaws, ranging from imprecise language and overly verbose explanations to inconsistencies in notation and a lack of rigorous justification for several design choices. The sheer *number* of these issues, while individually small, collectively detract significantly from the paper’s credibility and make it difficult to assess the true value of the contribution.

Specifically, the presentation of the algorithm is unnecessarily convoluted. Equations (1)-(3) are presented without sufficient motivation or explanation of why these particular update rules were chosen. While the authors attempt to connect them to existing methods like TD(0), the connection feels superficial. The justification for the weight update rule in Equation (4), relying on a threshold T and scaling factor γ, feels ad-hoc and lacks a clear theoretical basis. The discussion of convergence vs. diligence (Section 3.1) is interesting but also suffers from a lack of precision; the conditions for convergence and diligence are stated but not fully justified, and the link between these properties and the parameter choices (γ, T) is not thoroughly explored.

The simulation section is similarly problematic. The statement that LoSST was “basically indistinguishable” from PC before the changepoint requires quantitative support – simply stating it is insufficient. Figures 1 and 2 lack clear labeling of axes and consistent scaling, making it difficult to directly compare the performance of LoSST and PC. The parameter choices for the simulations (e.g., the fixed graph relearning schedule) are not adequately justified. The final simulation in Section 4, applying LoSST to US price index data, is intriguing but lacks detail. The authors claim LoSST detected shifts corresponding to the Great Moderation and the 2000 recession, but this is presented as an observation without rigorous statistical validation. The choice of using month-to-month differences is stated without justification.

Numerous minor inconsistencies and stylistic issues further detract from the paper's quality. The notation is sometimes ambiguous (e.g., the use of ‘r’ as both a time index and a variable in the equation for ρr+1). The writing is often verbose and repetitive, with unnecessary jargon and overly cautious phrasing. Several citations are missing key details (e.g., missing year for reference [1]). The phrase "provably diligent" appears repeatedly without a formal proof being included in the main body, only a "proof sketch."

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses an important and challenging problem – online structure learning in dynamic environments.
*   Presents a potentially useful heuristic algorithm for tackling this problem.
*   Demonstrates encouraging empirical results through simulations and a real-world case study.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The algorithm's design choices lack rigorous justification and theoretical grounding.
*   The presentation is convoluted, verbose, and riddled with minor inconsistencies.
*   The simulation results are not presented with sufficient clarity and rigor.
*   The real-world application lacks statistical validation.
*   The sheer number of minor flaws undermines the paper’s credibility.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the novelty of the problem and the potential of the approach, the numerous flaws in this paper outweigh its strengths. The lack of theoretical rigor, the imprecise presentation, and the insufficient validation of the results make it difficult to assess the true value of LoSST. The algorithm *may* be useful in practice, but this paper does not provide enough evidence to support that claim. I recommend **rejection**. A substantial revision addressing the issues outlined above, with a focus on clarity, rigor, and validation, would be necessary for reconsideration.
