- Paper introduces LoSST: an online algorithm that tracks sufficient statistics and uses Mahalanobis distances plus pooled p-values to detect and relearn time-varying graphical structure.
- Addresses a literature gap: no prior methods learn graphical model structure online when the structure changes during data collection.
- Provides theoretical analysis of convergence vs. diligence for the proposed tracker.
- Demonstrates that LoSST matches batch methods on stable structure and outperforms them when structure changes in simulations.
- Validates practical utility on a real-world US price index volatility dataset, detecting meaningful economic shifts.
- Strength: clearly identifies and motivates a significant gap in online structure learning literature.
- Strength: technically sound algorithm with rigorous mathematical foundations.
- Strength: thorough experimental validation across multiple scenarios and metrics.
- Strength: balanced, parameterized trade-off between convergence and responsiveness (diligence).
- Strength: practical implementation with minimal memory requirements and concrete parameter choices.
- Weakness: limited analysis of boundary conditions for rapidly changing generating structures.
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity and scalability comparison to batch methods.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to other online structure-learning approaches in the literature.
- Weakness: parameter sensitivity (T, γ) not systematically investigated.
- Weakness: real-world application could better contextualize detected change points with known economic events.
- Quality assessment: technically sound, complete work with strong theoretical and empirical support.
- Clarity assessment: well-organized and clearly written, though pooled p-value approach may challenge non-statistical readers.
- Originality assessment: novel contribution to online graphical structure learning; pooled p-value detection is particularly novel.
- Significance assessment: broadly applicable to domains with streaming data and time-varying structure, offering practical advantages over batch methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: fills an important gap, rigorous theory, comprehensive experiments, real-data demonstration, meaningful contribution.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of fast-change limitations, computational analysis, parameter robustness, and comparisons to other online methods.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance at NIPS.
- Specific suggestion: add analysis/empirical evidence on rate-of-change limits LoSST can handle.
- Specific suggestion: include detailed time/memory complexity and scalability comparisons with batch methods.
- Specific suggestion: compare LoSST to other online structure-learning approaches where available.
- Specific suggestion: perform systematic parameter sensitivity experiments for T and γ.
- Specific suggestion: better link detected change points to known economic events in the real-data study.
- Specific suggestion: discuss extensions to discrete variables and other graphical model types.
