The paper addresses the problem of learning graphical model structure in an online fashion, a challenging task given the prevalence of non-stationary environments and the need for real-time adaptation. The core idea, the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST) algorithm, dynamically tracks sufficient statistics and relearns the graphical model when changes are detected based on Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values. This is a significant problem with limited prior work addressing true online learning of graphical model structure.

The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the motivation, related work, algorithm details, and simulation results. The authors do a good job of positioning their work with respect to existing literature on graphical model learning, online learning, and change point detection, correctly identifying the limitations of existing approaches for their specific problem. The algorithm itself is presented with sufficient detail to allow for implementation, and the convergence/diligence discussion adds nuance and theoretical grounding. The simulation results are comprehensive, demonstrating performance in scenarios with both structure and parameter changes, and comparing favorably to a batch-mode baseline. The application to US price index data provides a compelling example of the algorithm's potential in a real-world setting.

One strength is the thoroughness of the related work section. The authors demonstrate an understanding of the landscape and clearly articulate the gaps their work fills. Another strength is the explicit discussion of the trade-off between convergence and diligence, and the parameter tuning to achieve a balance between the two. The inclusion of simulations with both parameter and structure changes provides a more robust evaluation than many papers in this area. The application to real-world data is a valuable addition, even if it's exploratory.

However, there are some weaknesses. While the algorithm’s update equations are clearly presented, the rationale behind some design choices (e.g., the specific form of the weight update equation (4)) isn’t fully explained. A more detailed justification or sensitivity analysis would be beneficial. The computational complexity of the PC algorithm used for structure learning isn’t discussed, and its scalability to high-dimensional data could be a concern, potentially limiting the applicability of LoSST. While the application to price index data is encouraging, it’s limited to a single dataset and lacks a more rigorous evaluation against alternative methods. The discussion on Bayesian methods could be expanded; acknowledging limitations beyond computational cost (e.g., prior specification) would strengthen the argument for the chosen frequentist approach. The notation is generally good, but could be slightly improved for readability (e.g., consistent use of subscripts for time steps).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The problem of online graphical model structure learning is under-explored, and LoSST provides a novel heuristic approach.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The algorithm is well-defined, and the simulation results demonstrate its effectiveness in various scenarios.
*   **Significance:** The ability to adapt to changing structures has practical relevance in many real-world applications.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the core ideas and technical details.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   **Heuristic Nature:** The algorithm is based on heuristics, and a more rigorous theoretical analysis would be desirable.
*   **Scalability:** The reliance on the PC algorithm could limit the scalability of LoSST to high-dimensional data.
*   **Limited Real-world Evaluation:** The application to US price index data is exploratory and lacks a comprehensive evaluation.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of graphical model learning. Despite being a heuristic approach, LoSST addresses a significant problem and demonstrates promising results. The clarity of the presentation and thoroughness of the evaluation outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend acceptance, with minor revisions to address the points raised above (specifically, justifying design choices and discussing scalability concerns). The paper is of high quality and suitable for presentation at NIPS.
