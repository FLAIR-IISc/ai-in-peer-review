- Summary: Introduces LoSST, an online heuristic that updates sufficient statistics and relearns graphical structure to track time-varying graphical models, balancing convergence and diligence.  
- Related work: Good literature review that correctly positions LoSST versus static structure learners, online methods, and batch time-indexed graph approaches.  
- Strength: Addresses an important real-world limitation of static-structure assumptions in graphical models.  
- Strength: Operates online, processing streaming data without full historical storage.  
- Strength: Explicit treatment of the convergence–diligence trade-off controlled by parameters γ and T, with theoretical discussion.  
- Strength: Strong experimental validation via simulations and a US price-index real-world example showing tracking and competitive batch performance during stable periods.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation and accessible mathematical formulation.  
- Strength: Practical discussion of computational complexity and relearning-frequency trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Heuristic nature with limited theoretical guarantees and dependence on the chosen structure learner (PC).  
- Weakness: Sensitive to parameters γ and T, with only unsystematic tuning reported and unclear practical guidance.  
- Weakness: Use of a fixed Mahalanobis-distance threshold T feels arbitrary and may be sensitive to outliers.  
- Weakness: Limited scope of real-world validation; needs more diverse datasets (higher dimensionality, frequent changes).  
- Weakness: Repeated use of the PC algorithm can be computationally prohibitive in high dimensions.  
- For acceptance: Tackles an important problem, offers a practical online solution, provides compelling experiments, and contributes theoretically on the trade-off.  
- Against acceptance: Lacks strong theoretical guarantees, has challenging parameter tuning, and may be sensitive to outliers via Mahalanobis distance.  
- Overall assessment: Strong contribution recommended for acceptance despite heuristic limitations.  
- Minor suggestions: Provide deeper complexity analysis, investigate adaptive thresholding, perform systematic parameter sensitivity studies, and evaluate on additional real-world datasets.
