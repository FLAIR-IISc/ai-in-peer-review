- Paper proposes LoSST: an online DAG-structure learner using adaptively weighted sufficient statistics and pooled Mahalanobis p-values to trigger intermittent PC relearning; studies a convergence–diligence tradeoff and shows synthetic + CPI experiments.

- Strength: Addresses a timely, underexplored problem of online DAG/causal structure tracking.

- Strength: Memory- and pass-efficient sufficient-statistic updates (O(p^2) state, single pass), attractive for streaming.

- Strength: Convergence vs. diligence framing is insightful and highlights distinct qualitative regimes.

- Strength: Synthetic experiments are reasonably thorough across graph sizes/degrees and cover both parameter and structure changes.

- Strength: Method can reduce spurious edges after structural changes relative to a naive batch-PC baseline.

- Major weakness: Statistical calibration of the change signal is questionable — Hotelling’s T^2 and pooled p-values are not justified with adaptive, dependent weights and S estimated from past data.

- Major weakness: The proposed recursion for effective sample size Sr is ad hoc and does not match standard ESS formulas; it affects CI thresholds and degrees of freedom.

- Major weakness: Using PC with weighted covariances and Sr lacks justification — null distribution of partial correlations and type I error control are unclear.

- Major weakness: The diligence schedule (T>1) forces ar growth even under stationarity, shrinking Sr and potentially degrading steady-state accuracy.

- Major weakness: Numerical stability and regularization (ensuring invertible/PD Cr after abrupt changes or small Sr) are not discussed.

- Major weakness: Experimental baselines are weak/unfair — missing sliding-window PC, fixed EWMA-PC, CPD+relearn, time-varying GGM baselines and ablation studies for key components/parameters.

- Major weakness: Sensitivity analyses for T, γ, ν are limited and unsystematic; no ablations showing contribution of pooled p-values vs. fixed forgetting.

- Major weakness: Real-data CPI case study is anecdotal — no validation against standard volatility-change tests, no learned-graph visualizations or domain comparisons.

- Clarity issues: Typos/notation errors; paper needs pseudo-code listing maintained state, explicit description of how PC uses weighted stats, complexity analysis, and parameter/stability guidance.

- Conceptual clarity: Convergence/diligence section mixes proof sketches with informal claims; needs clearer separation of rigorous results vs conjectures and alignment with standard change-detection metrics.

- Originality: Novel synthesis (pooled Mahalanobis p-values + intermittent PC relearning) for DAGs, but many components echo existing ideas and related literature is incompletely cited.

- Significance: Approach could be impactful if given solid statistical grounding and stronger benchmarks; in current form heuristics and lack of calibration limit practical adoption.

- Questions for authors summarized: justify T^2 with adaptive weights and calibrate ρr under stationarity; specify how PC tests are adapted and how α is controlled; compare to sliding-window/EWMA/CPD+relearn baselines; describe regularization for Cr and safeguards when Sr is small; provide sensitivity/ablation studies; show learned graphs and econometric baselines for CPI.

- Recommendation: Lean reject — promising direction but needs stronger theoretical/empirical calibration, better baselines, fuller experiments, and clearer algorithmic details.
