1. Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical model structure using weighted sufficient statistics.  
2. Detects structural changes via Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values and relearns graphs when needed.  
3. Theoretical analysis characterizes a trade-off between convergence (stability) and diligence (adaptivity).  
4. Validated with simulations (compared to PC) showing fast recovery after changepoints and with a real-world US price index volatility application.  
5. Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulation and convergence proof sketch demonstrating parity with batch learning during stable periods.  
6. Experimental validation: well-designed simulations and a convincing real-world example (Great Moderation detection).  
7. Clarity: well-organized, accessible exposition with reproducible derivations and effective figures.  
8. Significance: addresses an important practical gap applicable across multiple domains with no extra cost in stable settings.  
9. Limited scope: analysis and experiments restricted to linear recursive SEMs with Gaussian errors and directed acyclic graphs.  
10. Parameter sensitivity: performance depends on tuning (T and γ) with only informal parameter selection reported.  
11. Computational complexity: lacks detailed analysis or empirical timing to assess online feasibility given expensive graph learning.  
12. Comparison gap: could better contrast with recent online structure-learning work (e.g., recent online Bayesian network methods).  
13. Arguments for acceptance: fills a critical gap, is theoretically grounded, empirically strong, and practically relevant.  
14. Arguments against acceptance: model scope limits, parameter sensitivity concerns, and incomplete comparisons to recent related work.  
15. Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with convincing results; recommendation: accept.
