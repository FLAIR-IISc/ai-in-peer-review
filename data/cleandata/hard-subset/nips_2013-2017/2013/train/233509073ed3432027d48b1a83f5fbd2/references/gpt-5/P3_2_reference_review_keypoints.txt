- Summary: Proposes LoSST, an online heuristic that tracks time-varying DAG structure by maintaining weighted sufficient statistics and using pooled Mahalanobis-distance p-values (Liptak) to adapt per-datum weights and trigger probabilistic PC relearning.

- Summary: Authors analyze convergence vs diligence trade-offs, simulate structural/parametric changes vs batch PC, and give a brief US price-index volatility case study.

- Relation to prior work: Positions between online CPD and batch structure learning; claims single-pass online tracking without long history storage.

- Relation to prior work: Misses deeper comparison to time-varying graphical model and dynamic network literature (e.g., Kolar/Song/Xing, EWMA/CUSUM, kernel methods).

- Strength: Clear problem formulation for locally stationary SEMs with single-pass constraints.

- Strength: Simple, implementable adaptive updates for means/covariances and intuitive misfit pooling.

- Strength: Thoughtful qualitative treatment of convergence vs diligence and parameter regimes.

- Strength: Simulations show matching pre-change performance and fast adaptation post-change; probabilistic retraining can reduce compute.

- Strength: Case study demonstrates qualitative sensitivity to macro phenomena.

- Weakness: Statistical calibration of pooled p-values is flawed—Hotelling p-values from weighted data are not Uniform(0,1) and temporal dependence violates Liptak’s assumptions, risking false alarms or muted detection.

- Weakness: Heuristic effective-sample-size Sr update is unjustified; Kish’s n_eff = b_r^2 / τ_r should be used and affects test dof.

- Weakness: Numerical stability concerns—Cr can be ill-conditioned with small n_eff; paper omits regularization and efficient inverse-updates.

- Weakness: PC conditional-independence tests assume i.i.d. samples; unclear how weighted covariances and ambiguous sample sizes affect thresholds and Type I error under frequent relearning.

- Weakness: Theoretical guarantees are informal; no formal bounds on detection delay, false alarm rate, or structural risk under local stationarity.

- Clarity: Needs to state precisely how PC tests use Sr/n_eff, significance levels, and partial-correlation computation under weights.

- Clarity: Lacks algorithm pseudocode summarizing state variables, per-step costs, and initialization details.

- Clarity: Should clearly separate the dual roles of ρr (weight modulation vs relearning trigger) and clean up typographical interruptions.

- Originality: The combination appears novel for online DAG tracking but requires deeper comparison to related dynamic network and CPD methods.

- Significance: Method is practically promising, but heuristic calibration, weak baselines, and n_eff issues limit generality of claims.

- Experimental: Baselines are limited/unfavorable to PC; recommend sliding-window/EWMA PC, CPD+restart, BOCPD, and time-varying GGM/SEM baselines.

- Experimental: Missing variability/error bars, detection-delay and false-alarm metrics; report per-step time, memory, and average relearning cost.

- Experimental: Real-data results should show evolving graphs over time and more interpretability, not only misfit signals.

- Suggestions: Replace Sr with principled n_eff, recalibrate tests, or use prequential/GLR/EWMA log-likelihoods; consider bootstrap calibration for ρr.

- Suggestions: Add regularization and stable inverse-covariance updates (e.g., ridge, Sherman–Morrison–Woodbury).

- Suggestions: Evaluate against stronger baselines, include ablations over T, γ, ν, and provide calibration guidance.

- Suggestions: Discuss extensions to non-Gaussian/discrete models and robustness to outliers (robust covariance/Mahalanobis).

- Pros for acceptance: Tackles an important online problem with a simple, modular, implementable method and promising empirical behavior.

- Cons for acceptance: Key statistical components are heuristic/un-calibrated, baselines are limited, and theoretical/evaluation support is insufficient.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject — promising idea and engineering but requires stronger statistical calibration, baselines, and formal/evaluative support before acceptance.
