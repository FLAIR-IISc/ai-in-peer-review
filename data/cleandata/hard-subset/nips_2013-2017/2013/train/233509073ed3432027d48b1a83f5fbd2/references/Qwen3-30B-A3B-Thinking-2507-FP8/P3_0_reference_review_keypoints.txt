- Summary: Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm that tracks weighted sufficient statistics and uses pooled Mahalanobis-distance p-values to detect and probabilistically relearn changing graphical structure.

- Strength (Technical Soundness): Algorithm is well-derived with solid theoretical analysis of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and clear update equations.

- Strength (Evaluation): Thorough simulations and a real-world U.S. price-index volatility example convincingly show LoSST outperforms batch methods in changing environments.

- Strength (Limitations Acknowledged): Authors acknowledge limitations (rapid changes, discrete variables) and discuss directions for extension.

- Originality (Problem Framing): Identifies a novel and important gap—online structure learning for time-varying graphical models.

- Originality (Change Detection): Innovative adaptation of pooled p-values (Liptak's method) on Mahalanobis distances for structural change detection.

- Originality (Related Work): Provides a clear comparison distinguishing LoSST from prior Bayesian online, TDL, and CPD methods.

- Significance (Practical Relevance): Addresses important real-world needs where structures change over time (finance, biology, social systems).

- Significance (Performance Gains): Demonstrates faster adaptation to structural changes with minimal overhead in stable regimes.

- Weakness (Comparisons): Lacks comparison with existing online structure-learning approaches (e.g., online Bayesian network or DBN methods).

- Weakness (Theoretical Guarantees): No formal performance guarantees (e.g., detection time or convergence rate after change).

- Weakness (Distributional Assumption): Relies on multivariate Gaussian assumption for Mahalanobis distances, limiting applicability to discrete or non-Gaussian data.

- Weakness (Clarity - Math): Some derivations (pooled p-values, weighted updates) are dense and need more intuition for non-experts.

- Weakness (Clarity - Relearning): Probabilistic relearning mechanism and choice of ν (used value ν=0.005) are vague and lack practical guidance.

- Weakness (Novelty of Core): Core idea (weighted updates with Mahalanobis distances) is somewhat incremental relative to other online weighting schemes.

- Weakness (Scalability): Computational cost (Mahalanobis O(d^3)) and scalability to large graphs (100+ variables) are not sufficiently addressed.

- Weakness (Parameter Sensitivity): Dependence on parameters T and γ is understated; current choices are based on unsystematic tuning.

- Suggestion (Theory): Add formal theorems characterizing expected detection time or sample complexity for structural changes.

- Suggestion (Parameters): Provide principled guidance or heuristics for selecting T and γ based on change rates or noise levels.

- Suggestion (Comparisons): Empirically compare LoSST to other online structure-learning and dynamic time-series methods (online BN, DBN).

- Suggestion (Real Data): Deepen real-world analysis by interpreting specific structural changes (e.g., relationships among price indexes during events).

- Suggestion (Complexity): State and discuss computational complexity and scalability explicitly; consider approximations for large d.

- Suggestion (Discrete Data): Outline concrete approaches to extend to discrete variables (e.g., chi-square or other distance statistics).

- Overall Assessment: Strong, well-written paper making a valuable contribution to online graphical-structure learning.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Pro-Accept: Fills an important gap, theoretically grounded, empirically strong, minimal overhead in stable settings.

- Con-Accept: Needs broader online-method comparisons, stronger theoretical performance guarantees, clearer parameter rationale, and scalability/discrete-data treatment.
