- Tackles online graphical model structure learning for non-stationary sequential data and proposes LoSST.  
- LoSST updates weighted sufficient statistics using Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values and periodically relearns structure with the PC algorithm.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off.  
- Empirical validation: simulations show LoSST outperforms batch PC after changepoints; includes a US price index real-world example.  
- Correctly situates work relative to prior stationary Bayesian/constraint-based methods and online changepoint detection; contrasts with Talih & Hengartner (2005) and Siracusa & Fisher III (2009); draws analogy to temporal difference learning.  
- Strength: addresses an important, underexplored problem in non-stationary environments.  
- Strength: novel combination of dynamic weighting of sufficient statistics and periodic structure relearning.  
- Strength: theoretical framework clarifies algorithm behavior under different parameter settings.  
- Strength: compelling simulation results and initial real-world support.  
- Strength: generally clear writing and accessible notation.  
- Weakness: algorithm is heuristic with several design choices lacking rigorous justification.  
- Weakness: performance is sensitive to parameters (γ, T, ν) and needs tuning guidance.  
- Weakness: periodic PC re-learning can be computationally expensive in high dimensions and is under-discussed.  
- Weakness: limited real-world evaluation beyond a single dataset.  
- Weakness: reliance on Mahalanobis distance for change detection is vulnerable to outliers; robustness not fully explored.  
- Quality: technically sound but heuristic nature prevents it from being outstanding.  
- Clarity: exposition is generally very clear and logical.  
- Originality: novel integration of dynamic weighting, sufficient-statistics tracking, and probabilistic relearning.  
- Significance: potentially impactful if made robust and scalable.  
- For acceptance: addresses a hard problem, offers a novel algorithm, provides empirical evidence, and includes theoretical analysis.  
- Against acceptance: heavy reliance on heuristics, parameter sensitivity, potential computational costs, and limited real-world validation.  
- Overall assessment: strong accept with minor revisions to expand parameter sensitivity discussion, address computational cost, and consider more robust outlier handling.
