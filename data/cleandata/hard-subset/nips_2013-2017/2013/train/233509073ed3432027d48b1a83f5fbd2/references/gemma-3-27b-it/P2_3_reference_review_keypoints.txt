- Tackles learning graphical model structure in non-stationary environments and proposes LoSST, an online heuristic that updates sufficient statistics and relearns structure as needed.
- LoSST aims to match batch-method performance during stable periods while adapting quickly to structural shifts; evaluated via simulations and a US price index case study.
- Addresses an important and under-explored practical problem with clear real-world relevance.
- Proposes a sensible, practical heuristic centered on tracking sufficient statistics online.
- Offers a valuable theoretical discussion of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and the impact of tuning parameters (γ, T).
- Empirical results are solid: LoSST performs comparably to batch methods in stable regimes and better after structural changes, with useful comparison to the PC algorithm.
- Includes a preliminary real-world application demonstrating potential applicability.
- Paper is generally well-written and the algorithm is clearly presented.
- Algorithm is heuristic and lacks strong theoretical guarantees beyond qualitative trade-off discussion.
- Performance likely sensitive to parameters (ν, γ, T, Mahalanobis threshold); more systematic sensitivity analysis is needed.
- Simulation study scope is limited—needs broader range of graph types, change frequencies, noise levels, and more complex simultaneous changes.
- Real-world application is exploratory; deeper analysis and comparison to alternatives are required to establish practical benefits.
- Use of Mahalanobis distance assumes multivariate Gaussianity; robustness to deviations from this assumption is not fully addressed.
- Overall quality judged 7/10, clarity 8/10, originality 7/10, significance 7/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, practical and intuitive algorithm, solid simulations, thoughtful trade-off discussion.
- Arguments against acceptance: heuristic nature with weak guarantees, parameter sensitivity and limited simulations, preliminary real-world validation.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: conduct thorough parameter sensitivity analysis, expand simulation scenarios, discuss Mahalanobis limitations and alternatives, and explore incorporating prior knowledge or constraints.
