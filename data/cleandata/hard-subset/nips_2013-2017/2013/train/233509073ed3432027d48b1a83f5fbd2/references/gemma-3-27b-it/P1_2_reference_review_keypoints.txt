- Problem/Summary: Proposes LoSST, an online heuristic that tracks time-varying graphical model structure using adaptive weighting of data and the PC algorithm, validated in simulation and on US price index volatility data.
- Positioning: Correctly situates work relative to Bayesian, constraint-based, CPD, time-indexed graph, and TDL literature, highlighting differences.
- Novelty: Introduces a practical online structure-learning heuristic combining Mahalanobis-distance-based weights and pooled p-values.
- Theoretical support: Provides a concise theoretical description of sufficient statistic updates and discusses convergence vs. diligence trade-off.
- Empirical evidence: Strong simulations show tracking of structural and parameter changes and competitive batch performance during stable periods.
- Real-world demo: Compelling application to US price index volatility demonstrating practical applicability.
- Writing: Paper is generally well-written and organized, making core ideas accessible.
- Practicality: Operates in a single pass without storing large datasets, unlike many CPD methods.
- Heuristic limitation: Algorithm is heuristic; lacks rigorous convergence proofs and formal performance bounds.
- Parameter sensitivity: Performance likely depends on hyperparameters (γ, T, ν) with no systematic sensitivity analysis provided.
- Computational cost: No detailed analysis of computational complexity or scalability; repeated PC relearning may be expensive.
- Scope limitation: Reliance on the PC algorithm restricts the classes of graphical models learnable; alternatives not explored.
- Robustness concern: Mahalanobis distance may be sensitive to outliers; alternative metrics or outlier handling not investigated.
- Reproducibility/quality: Technically sound with sufficient algorithmic detail for reproduction.
- Clarity gap: Some derivations (e.g., covariance update) need clearer exposition.
- Originality statement: The specific combination of adaptive weighting, pooled p-values, and online structure tracking is novel.
- Significance: Addresses an important limitation of existing methods and has broad potential applicability.
- Acceptance rationale: Novel practical method, strong empirical and real-world results, and clear trade-off articulation argue for acceptance.
- Rejection concerns: Heuristic nature, lack of rigorous theory, and potential parameter/scalability issues argue against acceptance.
- Recommended revisions: Expand parameter-sensitivity discussion/guidelines, analyze computational cost/scalability, and acknowledge PC-algorithm limitations with possible alternatives.
