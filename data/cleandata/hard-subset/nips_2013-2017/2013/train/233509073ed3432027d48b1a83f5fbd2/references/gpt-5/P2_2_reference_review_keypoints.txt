- Summary: Proposes LoSST, a single-pass online heuristic maintaining weighted sufficient statistics and pooled p-values to adapt weights and trigger probabilistic relearning of linear-Gaussian DAG structure.

- Novel contribution: Combines adaptive weighted statistics, Liptak-pooled Mahalanobis p-values, and probabilistic PC relearning for directed models; frames a convergence vs. diligence trade-off.

- Relation to prior work: Positioned between online CPD, exponential forgetting, and batch structure learning but omits key related lines (dynamic Bayesian networks, sliding-window/decayed structure learning, time-varying GGMs).

- Technical soundness — positives: Online mean/covariance updates and effective sample-size heuristic are sensible; Mahalanobis distance appropriate for Gaussian data.

- Technical soundness — concerns: P-value pooling assumes independence despite sequential correlation; lack of analysis/calibration of dependence effects on false alarms; convergence/diligence claims are sketched but not proved.

- Testing/statistics concern: No discussion of CI test validity under weighted/decayed samples, degrees-of-freedom adjustments, multiple testing, or regularization with small effective sample size.

- Empirical support — positives: Synthetic experiments show LoSST adapts post-change where batch PC fails; probabilistic relearning appears to control recomputation.

- Empirical support — shortcomings: Limited baselines (no sliding-window/decay PC, CPD+relearn, or time-varying GGM/DBN baselines), no ablations/sensitivity to hyperparameters or outliers, no runtime/memory profiling.

- Real-data evaluation: Illustrative qualitative case on U.S. price indices but lacks ground truth or predictive validation.

- Clarity — positives: Generally clear exposition and useful convergence vs. diligence narrative.

- Clarity — gaps: Missing pseudocode, underspecified use of weighted Σ in CI tests, hyperparameter selection guidance, and numerical/regularization details.

- Originality assessment: Moderate-to-high in combining components for directed online structure learning, but closely related to existing adaptive-weight and time-varying graph literature.

- Significance assessment: Important and promising problem; approach is memory- and compute-efficient but current evidence is insufficient to claim superiority over standard streaming alternatives.

- Pros: Practical single-pass updates, simple adaptive weighting/relearning mechanism, insightful trade-off framing, convincing synthetic behavior.

- Cons: Uncalibrated p-value pooling under dependence, incomplete theoretical guarantees, limited baselines, unexplored hyperparameter sensitivity and stability, and missing related-work coverage.

- Suggested improvements: Compare to sliding-window/decay PC and CPD+relearn baselines; perform ablations over T, γ, ν; calibrate pooled p-values via simulations; add pseudocode, CI testing details, df/regularization discussion, and runtime metrics.

- Overall assessment: Moderate quality and clarity, moderate-to-high originality, moderate significance; promising but needs stronger empirical/theoretical support.

- Verdict: Leaning borderline/weak accept if substantial revisions (baselines, calibration, clarity) are added; otherwise weak reject.
