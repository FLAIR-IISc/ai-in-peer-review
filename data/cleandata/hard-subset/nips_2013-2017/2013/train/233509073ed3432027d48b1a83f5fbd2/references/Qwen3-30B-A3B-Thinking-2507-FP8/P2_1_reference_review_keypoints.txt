- Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical model structure by updating sufficient statistics and dynamically relearning structure using Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of a convergence vs. diligence trade-off, characterizing when LoSST stabilizes vs. detects changes.  
- Presents comprehensive simulations testing structural and parameter changes and a real-world application to US price-index volatility demonstrating practical utility.  
- Builds on established methods (PC algorithm, SEMs, TD(0)) but combines them in a novel way for time-varying structure learning.  
- Technically sound and well-supported; theoretical results and simulation design are strong and insightful.  
- Real-world experiment convincingly detects meaningful economic shifts (e.g., the Great Moderation).  
- Acknowledges limitations: performs poorly with rapidly changing structures or random-order mixtures of structures.  
- Parameter choices (T=0.05, γ=3) are empirical and somewhat ad-hoc, lacking systematic justification.  
- Computational complexity analysis is incomplete and not thoroughly addressed.  
- Does not evaluate or extend the method to discrete variables, limiting applicability to Bayesian networks.  
- Well-written and organized with clear algorithm description, though some technical derivations may challenge non-experts.  
- Would benefit from a more detailed discussion of parameter selection and a summary table of parameter effects.  
- Novel contribution: first to specifically address online learning of time-varying graphical structures with this combination of techniques.  
- Demonstrated significance: no extra cost in stable environments and substantially better performance than batch methods after structural changes.  
- Overall recommendation: accept (final rating 7 — Strong Accept) despite minor issues with parameters, discreteness, and complexity analysis.
