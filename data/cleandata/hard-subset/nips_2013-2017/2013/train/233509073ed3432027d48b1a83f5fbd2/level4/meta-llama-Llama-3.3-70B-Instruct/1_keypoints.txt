- Claim of novelty is disputed: Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid data and limited variations in generating graphical structure, so claiming to be first is misleading.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based method for online learning of graphical models from time series where the generating model may change frequently, evaluated on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear correct, but Section 2 mischaracterizes related work: score-based Bayesian network algorithms do not require a single generating model and rely on factorized local scoring; exchangeability is a model assumption, not an algorithmic one.  
- Major clarity issues about the actual learning task: unclear whether goal is change-point detection, inferring the latest generating structure, or something else, and whether a new model, a new algorithm, or both are proposed.  
- Specific presentation problems: paragraph and equation for ρ_{r+1} on page 3 are confusing, displayed-equation punctuation is inconsistent, figure fonts are too small, and figure color meanings are not described.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking could offset extra computation is vague and depends on domain-specific trade-offs, making the argument unclear.  
- Originality is limited: the work seems original but lacks a clearly novel idea; any novelty in p-value construction is not well explained.  
- Significance is modest: could inspire better (e.g., Bayesian) methods but reads as heuristic applied to an ambiguously defined problem that existing techniques might already handle.
