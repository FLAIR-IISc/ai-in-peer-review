- Addresses tracking changes in dependency structure in data streams and focuses on efficient SEM structure learning.  
- Proposes LoSST: a streaming algorithm that leverages batch PC structure learning and tracked summary statistics to trigger relearning.  
- Key tracked statistics: weighted means, correlation matrix, weighted sample size, Mahalanobis distances per point.  
- Data points are weighted by “surprise” and per-point MD p-values are pooled with Liptak’s weighted pooling to compute an overall significance ρ.  
- A noisy-OR with prior steps and ρ decides when to trigger new structure learning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets with parameter or structure changes, varying variables and degree.  
- Applied to a real US price-index dataset (529 months, six variables) and detected known economic trends while ignoring transients.  
- Strength: tackles an interesting, underexplored problem with strong practical relevance.  
- Strength: introduces a novel, practical, and computationally efficient algorithm combining multiple methods.  
- Strength: experiments are adequate, include parameter studies and a meaningful real-world dataset.  
- Strength: literature review is thorough.  
- Strength: paper is generally well written.  
- Major concern: no theoretical guarantees are provided; the paper should state achievable guarantees under simplifying assumptions or list this as a clear future direction.  
- Major concern: heavy reliance on heuristics needs clearer documentation; some methodological choices may violate assumptions of the used statistical tools.  
- Specific methodological worry: using Hotelling’s T² to estimate MD p-values is questionable because it assumes unweighted complete samples.  
- Specific methodological worry: using Liptak’s weighted p-value pooling may be inappropriate since it assumes independent meta-analytic tests, not the streaming/contextual tests used here.  
- Presentation issues: proofs in footnotes are unconventional; supplementary material should be used instead.  
- Presentation issues: figures are too small, poorly labeled, and missing legends; some experiment descriptions are unclear.  
- Model behavior question: explanation for LoSST’s poor performance on edge additions under parameter-only change is unsatisfactory.  
- Suggestion: decoupling parameter updates from structure updates would be desirable.  
- Overall: promising research direction with a practical heuristic implementation as a first step.
