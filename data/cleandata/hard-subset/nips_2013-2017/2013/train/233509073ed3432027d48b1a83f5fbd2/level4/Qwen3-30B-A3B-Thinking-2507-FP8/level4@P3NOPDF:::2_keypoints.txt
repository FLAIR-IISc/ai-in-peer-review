- Addresses an important, underexplored problem: detecting shifts in dependency structures in streaming time-series and proposes LoSST for SEM models.  
- LoSST is algorithmically novel and resource-conscious, using weighted statistics, adaptive reweighting, and Liptak’s pooling to avoid heavy computation.  
- Experiments are empirically rigorous, including parameter sensitivity studies and a real-world US price index case study.  
- Literature review is thorough and the paper is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Major omission: no theoretical guarantees—statistical assumptions (e.g., Hotelling’s T² i.i.d. multivariate normal, Liptak’s independence/fixed-sample assumptions) are violated by the weighted, streaming setting.  
- Over-reliance on heuristics without sufficient justification (e.g., using effective sample size S^r as N in Hotelling’s T²; arbitrary noisy-OR thresholding and relearning criteria).  
- Methodological limitations and heuristic choices need to be explicitly stated or theoretically justified.  
- Presentation issues: move proof sketches from footnotes to supplementary material.  
- Figures need resizing, axis labels, and clearer captions.  
- Experimental issue: insufficient explanation for poor performance on edge additions; should decouple parameter vs. structure updates (e.g., separate thresholds).  
- Real-world case study is promising but requires stronger links between detected changes and known economic events.  
- Overall assessment: promising, practical approach but insufficient theoretical grounding and unjustified heuristics; recommend reject with major revisions.  
- Conditional path forward: with clear theoretical analysis (even under restrictive assumptions) and rigorous justification of heuristics, the paper could be reconsidered.
