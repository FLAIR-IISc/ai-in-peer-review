- Reviewer disputes the authors’ claim of novelty, noting Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid data and changing generating structure within a class of graphical models.
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based method for online learning of graphical models from time series with frequently changing structure, evaluated on simulated and real data.
- Results appear correct, but the related-work discussion mischaracterizes score-based Bayesian network methods as assuming a single generating model.
- Clarify whether the core task is change-point detection, inferring the most recent structure, or something else, and whether the contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.
- Presentation issues: paragraph at end of page 3 is hard to follow, the equation for ρ_{r+1} is ambiguous, and authors omit periods after displayed equations.
- Figures are hard to read: fonts are too small (should be doubled) and color meanings in Figures 1–2 are not described.
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking can justify increased computational cost is questionable and depends on domain-specific trade-offs.
- Originality: work seems original but lacks a substantial novel core idea; the p-value construction might be novel but is not fully assessable.
- Significance: could spur development of improved (likely Bayesian) methods, but is more development/heuristic work than fundamental research and addresses a problem arguably already solvable with existing techniques.
