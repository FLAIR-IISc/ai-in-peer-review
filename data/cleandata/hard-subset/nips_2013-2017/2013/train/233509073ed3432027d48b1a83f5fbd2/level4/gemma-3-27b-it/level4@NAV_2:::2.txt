The paper tackles an important and relatively unexplored problem: tracking changes in the underlying dependency structure between variables in a streaming dataset. The authors focus on an efficient (computational/memory-wise) solution for recursive Structural Equation Models (SEMs). They develop a novel algorithm, LoSST, which leverages a standard batch learning algorithm (PC algorithm [Spirtes et al., 2000]) for structure learning and tracks statistics from the data stream to determine when to initiate new structure learning. These tracked statistics include weighted means, the covariance matrix (eq. 1,2), and a weighted sample size. A weighting scheme is applied to each new data point based on its “surprisingness” (Eq. 3,4). The Mahalanobis distance (MD) is computed for each point, and the resulting p-values (approximated using Hotelling's T2 distribution) are combined using Liptak's weighted pooling of p-values to create an overall significance statistic, ρ. This statistic then informs a noisy-OR gate that decides whether to trigger new structure learning. The algorithm is evaluated on synthetic data with induced changes in parameters or structure, varying the number of variables and maximum degree. Finally, it's applied to real-world US price index data (1967-2011, 6 variables) demonstrating detection of known economic shifts.

**Strengths:**

1.  The paper addresses a compelling and underexplored problem. As the authors note, significant potential exists for developing practical solutions and establishing theoretical foundations in this area.
2.  LoSST is a novel and practical algorithm. It creatively integrates several existing methods.
3.  The experimental setup appears reasonable, attempting to isolate the effects of different parameters and including a relevant real-world dataset.
4.  The authors provide a thorough review of related work.
5.  The paper is generally well-written and clearly presented.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  A significant limitation is the lack of theoretical guarantees. While the authors acknowledge the trade-off between convergence and diligence (p. 4), some form of guarantees under simplifying assumptions would substantially strengthen the work. Further discussion of theoretical avenues would be valuable.
2.  LoSST relies heavily on heuristics. While this enables a practical solution, the rationale behind key design decisions warrants greater clarity. Specifically, the weighting scheme, effective sample size calculation, and re-learning trigger should be more explicitly justified. The authors apply methods outside their established guarantees, and this should be clearly stated to avoid misinterpretation (e.g., using Hotelling’s T2 distribution with weighted samples and Liptak’s method for pooled p-values from potentially non-independent tests).
3. The explanation for LoSST’s performance on edge removal when only parameters change is not fully convincing.

**Other Comments:**

*   The use of footnotes for proofs is unconventional and should be moved to a supplementary materials document, alongside other missing details.
*   Figures are too small and lack sufficient detail (labels, captions, legends, spacing). The experimental descriptions are also unclear in places (e.g., the first paragraph on p. 7 is cryptic).
*   Decoupling parameter and structure updates may be a desirable extension, given the observed behavior.

**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

*   The problem is important and timely, with potential for significant impact.
*   LoSST provides a promising practical approach to online structure learning.
*   The experimental results are encouraging, demonstrating LoSST’s ability to track changing dependencies.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the challenges and potential of the approach.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

*   The lack of theoretical guarantees limits the confidence in the method’s robustness and generalizability.
*   The heavy reliance on heuristics raises concerns about the sensitivity of the algorithm to parameter tuning and specific dataset characteristics.
*   The presentation of results could be significantly improved with larger, more informative figures and clearer experimental descriptions.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is an interesting and potentially valuable contribution. While the lack of theoretical grounding and the reliance on heuristics are weaknesses, the practical novelty and promising experimental results justify acceptance, *conditional on* the authors addressing the concerns regarding figure quality, experimental clarity, and a more thorough discussion of the theoretical limitations and future research directions.
