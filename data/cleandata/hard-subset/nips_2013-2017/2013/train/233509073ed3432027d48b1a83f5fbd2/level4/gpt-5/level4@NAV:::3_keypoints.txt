- Proposes a principled method for structure learning in SEMs when data are only locally i.i.d. (time-varying underlying models).
- Paper is clearly written and addresses the important, under-studied problem of learning from time-dependent data.
- Techniques for adapting learning to time-dependent data are simple and effective and demonstrated with experiments using the PC method.
- Experiments focus on PC; the claim that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly” is too strong.
- Suggest rephrasing that claim or providing justification/experiments showing other methods behave similarly.
- Point out scope ambiguity: applying the same ideas to Bayesian network structure learning may be nontrivial because of difficulties maintaining sufficient statistics.
- Recommend framing the paper’s goal more directly around SEMs and noting that extensions to other models may require more than minor tweaks.
- Overall assessment: well-written, easy-to-implement formulation with promising results, though experiments are limited to PC; reviewer has read authors’ feedback.
