This manuscript presents a systematic approach to structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) that can accommodate data sets with local independence and identically distributed (i.i.d.) properties, where the data may be generated from distinct underlying models over time. The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and addresses a significant problem that has been largely overlooked by many commonly used algorithms for structure learning, namely learning from time-dependent data. The proposed techniques to extend learning to time-dependent data are simple yet effective, as demonstrated by the experiments using the PC method.

To clarify the paper's contributions, I suggest two improvements. Firstly, in the experiments, the statement "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly, ..." is overly broad. The differences among methods for structure learning can be substantial. It would be better to rephrase this statement to emphasize that the experiments specifically focus on the PC method for time-independent and time-dependent data, and then speculate that similar results might be obtained with other methods; or to retain the original assertion and provide a detailed explanation for why this would be the case.

Secondly, the conclusions discuss the potential challenges of applying these ideas to learning the structure of Bayesian networks, primarily due to difficulties in maintaining sufficient statistics. Although this issue is not central to the proposal, it highlights that extending the learning procedure to Bayesian networks and other models may require more than minor modifications. I believe the paper would benefit from disclosing this limitation from the outset, presenting the paper in more direct terms about its goal of SEM models, while still acknowledging the possibility of extending the approach to other models (albeit with non-trivial challenges). In short, the paper could be more precise about its scope.

Overall, this is a well-written paper about learning the graph structure for SEMs under the assumption that data are only locally i.i.d. The formulation is simple to understand and implement, and the experimental results are promising, even if focused solely on a comparison using the PC method.
