- Addresses structure learning for SEMs under realistic local i.i.d. (time-varying) data; problem is well-motivated.  
- Proposes adapting the PC algorithm to handle temporal shifts; method is simple, implementable, and effective.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data convincingly show the adapted method outperforms naive time-independent approaches.  
- Paper is well-written and logically structured, accessible to readers familiar with graphical models.  
- Concern: Claim that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" is overstated given experiments only on PC; needs clarification or evidence.  
- Suggestion: Either restrict claims to PC or provide justification/evidence that the adaptation generalizes to other algorithms.  
- Scope clarification: Emphasize early that the contribution targets SEMs and that extending to Bayesian networks is non-trivial and discussed as future work.  
- Experimental note: Briefly acknowledge PC’s constraint-based strengths to justify its selection as the primary test case.
