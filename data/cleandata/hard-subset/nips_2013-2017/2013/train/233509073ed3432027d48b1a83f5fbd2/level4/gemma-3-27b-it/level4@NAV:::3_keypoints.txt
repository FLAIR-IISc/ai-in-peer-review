Paper proposes a systematic method for SEM structure learning under locally i.i.d. (time-dependent) data, addressing shifts in data-generating processes.  
Clear, accessible writing and tackles an important, often-overlooked challenge of learning from time-dependent data.  
Proposed extensions are straightforward and effective, with experiments (using the PC algorithm) supporting their utility.  
The claim that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly” is too strong and should be rephrased or justified.  
Recommend clarifying experiments focus on PC (standard vs. adapted for time-dependence) and treating generalization to other methods as speculative unless supported.  
Observation about difficulties extending to Bayesian network learning (maintenance of sufficient statistics) is insightful and warrants clearer scope delineation.  
Suggest framing the contribution primarily as for SEM structure learning while acknowledging potential—but nontrivial—extensions to other model classes.  
Overall positive verdict: well-written, valuable, easy to implement, with encouraging experimental results; reviewer has read authors’ response to prior feedback.
