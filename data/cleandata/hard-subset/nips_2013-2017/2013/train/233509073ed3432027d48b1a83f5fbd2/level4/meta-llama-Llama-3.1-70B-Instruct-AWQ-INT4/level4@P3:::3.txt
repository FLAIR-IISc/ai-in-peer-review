This paper presents a novel approach to learning the structure of graphical models from time-varying data, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that assume a stationary generating process. The authors propose the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST) algorithm, a heuristic method that tracks changes in graphical model structure or parameters in real-time. The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly explained, making it easy to follow.

The LoSST algorithm is designed to work with data that is only locally i.i.d., meaning that the generating process can change over time. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through simulations, showing that LoSST performs comparably to batch-mode learning when the generating graphical structure is globally stationary and significantly better when it is only locally stationary.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of traditional structure learning algorithms. They also provide a thorough discussion of related work, highlighting the differences between their approach and existing methods.

The experimental results are promising, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the performance of LoSST in various scenarios. However, I do have some suggestions to improve the paper.

Firstly, in the experiments, the authors claim that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly." I believe this assertion is too strong, and it would be better to rephrase it to make it clearer that the experiments are specific to the PC method. Alternatively, the authors could provide a more detailed explanation of why they believe other methods would perform similarly.

Secondly, in the conclusions, the authors mention that applying the same ideas to learning the structure of Bayesian networks might be more challenging due to difficulties in keeping the sufficient statistics. I think it would be beneficial to disclose this limitation earlier in the paper, perhaps in the introduction or related work section. This would help set the scope of the paper and manage the reader's expectations.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that presents a novel approach to learning the structure of graphical models from time-varying data. The ideas are clearly explained, and the experimental results are promising. With some minor revisions to address the points mentioned above, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
