Paper Summary:
This paper addresses the underexplored problem of tracking changes in the underlying dependency structure between variables in a stream of data. The authors focus on developing an efficient solution for simpler Structural Equation Models (SEMs). They introduce the LoSST algorithm, which leverages a standard batch learning algorithm (PC by Spirtes et al, 2000) for structure learning and monitors various statistics from the data stream to determine when to initiate new structure learning. These statistics include distance from the weighted mean of each variable, correlation matrix, and weighted sample size. The authors employ a weighting scheme for new data points based on their "surprisingness" and utilize the Mahalanobis distance and Hotelling's T2 distribution to compute an overall "significance" statistic. This statistic is then used in a noisy OR configuration to decide whether to start new structure learning. The authors evaluate their algorithm using synthetic datasets with introduced changes in parameters or structure and real-life data from the US price index.

Pros:

1. The paper tackles an interesting and underexplored problem, highlighting the need for further development of practical solutions and theoretical guarantees.
2. The LoSST algorithm is novel and efficient, making creative use of various methods to address this problem.
3. The experiments are adequate, investigating the effects of various parameters and including an interesting real-life dataset.
4. The authors provide a comprehensive review of previous work on this problem.
5. The paper is well-written.

Cons:

1. A significant limitation of the paper is the lack of theoretical guarantees or convergence analysis, which should be explored in future work.
2. The LoSST algorithm relies heavily on heuristics, and the decisions involved in the method should be more clearly justified. The authors' creative use of existing methods, such as Hotelling's T2 distribution and Liptak's weighted p-values, may void the guarantees of these methods.

Other comments:

- The authors' use of footnotes to outline proofs may not be the most effective approach; consider using supplementary materials instead.
- The figures are too small and lack necessary details, such as labels and captions.
- The explanation for LoSST's poor performance on edge addition when only parameters are changed is unsatisfactory.
- Decoupling parameter updates and structure updates may be a desirable direction for future research.
- The paper presents an interesting direction for future research, with a practical, albeit heuristic, implementation as a first step.
