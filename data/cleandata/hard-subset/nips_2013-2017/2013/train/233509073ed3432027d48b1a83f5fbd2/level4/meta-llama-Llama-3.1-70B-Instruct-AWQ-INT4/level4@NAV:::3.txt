This manuscript introduces a systematic approach to adapting structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) to accommodate data sets that exhibit local independence and identically distributed (i.i.d.) properties, where the underlying data generation model may change over time. The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and addresses a significant problem often overlooked by popular structure learning algorithms: learning from time-dependent data. The proposed techniques for extending structure learning to time-dependent data are straightforward yet effective, as demonstrated by experiments employing the PC method.

To further enhance the paper's contributions, two suggestions are offered. Firstly, the experimental results assert that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly." This claim may be too broad, as differences between structure learning methods can be substantial. It is recommended to rephrase this statement to emphasize that the experiments focus on the PC method for both time-independent and time-dependent data adaptations, noting that similar performance is speculated for other methods. Alternatively, the assertion could be maintained, accompanied by a detailed explanation of why this might be the case.

Secondly, the conclusions discuss the potential challenges of applying these ideas to learning the structure of Bayesian networks due to difficulties in maintaining sufficient statistics. Although this issue is not central to the proposal, it highlights that extending the learning procedure to Bayesian networks and other models may require more than minor adjustments. It is suggested that the paper could benefit from explicitly stating this limitation from the outset, presenting the work in more direct terms regarding its focus on SEM models while acknowledging the possibility of extending to other models, albeit with non-trivial challenges. In summary, the paper would benefit from a clearer definition of its scope. This is a well-written paper about learning graph structures for SEMs under the assumption of locally i.i.d. data. The formulation is simple to understand and implement, and experimental results appear promising, even if focused solely on comparing the PC method. The authors' feedback has been acknowledged.
