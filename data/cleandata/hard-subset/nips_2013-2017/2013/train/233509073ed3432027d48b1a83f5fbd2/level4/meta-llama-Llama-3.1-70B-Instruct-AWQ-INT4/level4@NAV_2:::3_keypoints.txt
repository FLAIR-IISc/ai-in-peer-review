1. Presents LoSST, a novel heuristic algorithm for online structure learning of SEMs under locally i.i.d. (locally stationary) data.  
2. Targets an important, under-addressed problem: learning graphical structure from time-dependent data.  
3. Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
4. Provides a concise related-work overview comparing standard structure-learning and online methods.  
5. Describes LoSST in detail, including sufficient-statistic updates and change-detection mechanism.  
6. Discusses algorithmic properties (convergence and diligence) and how parameters control them.  
7. Thorough empirical evaluation on synthetic data and U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics real-world data.  
8. Results: LoSST matches batch-mode learning when globally stationary and outperforms it when only locally stationary.  
9. Strengths: clear writing, thorough evaluation, and frank discussion of limitations and extensions.  
10. Weakness: Claim that other standard structure-learning algorithms would behave like PC is asserted but not well-supported.  
11. Weakness: Discussion of extending LoSST to other graphical models (e.g., Bayesian networks) is brief and lacks depth.  
12. Suggestion: Paper could use more detailed discussion of limitations and potential extensions.  
13. Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the above weaknesses.
