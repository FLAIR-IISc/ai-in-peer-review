This manuscript offers a systematic approach to enable structure learning in Structural Equation Models (SEMs) with datasets that are only locally independent and identically distributed – acknowledging potential shifts in the underlying data generating models over time. The paper is clearly written and readily accessible, tackling a significant challenge: learning from time-dependent data, an aspect often overlooked by established SEM structure learning algorithms. The proposed techniques for extending learning to these time-dependent scenarios are both straightforward and demonstrably effective, as evidenced by the experimental results using the PC algorithm.

I have two suggestions to refine the presentation of the paper’s contributions. First, the statement in the experiments section that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly…” appears overly assertive. The performance of different structure learning methods can vary considerably. I recommend either rephrasing this to clarify that the experiments specifically examine the PC method, both in its standard time-independent form and with the proposed adaptation for time-dependent data – highlighting the relevance of this comparison – and then stating the similarity to other methods as a speculation; or retaining the assertion, but providing a detailed justification for why this might be the case. 

Second, the discussion in the conclusions regarding the challenges of applying these ideas to Bayesian network structure learning, specifically concerning the maintenance of sufficient statistics, is insightful. While not central to the paper's primary contribution, it underscores that extending this learning procedure to other model classes may necessitate further considerations. Therefore, I believe the paper would benefit from a clearer delineation of its scope, perhaps framing it more directly as a contribution to SEM structure learning, while acknowledging the potential – though not necessarily straightforward – extension to other models. 

Overall, this is a well-written and valuable paper addressing graph structure learning for SEMs under the realistic assumption of local i.i.d. data. The formulation is easily understood and implemented, and the experimental results are encouraging, even with their current focus on comparisons utilizing the PC method. I have reviewed the authors’ response to previous feedback.
