- Problem: tracking changes in dependency structure in data streams for simpler SEMs; proposes LoSST algorithm.  
- Method: LoSST uses batch PC structure learning and tracks weighted mean distances, correlation matrix, and effective sample size.  
- Weighting: new points are weighted by "surprisingness" via Mahalanobis distance; MD p-values estimated with Hotelling’s T2.  
- Aggregation: p-values combined with Liptak’s weighted pooling and used with a noisy-OR to decide when to relearn structure.  
- Evaluation: experiments on synthetic datasets (parameter and structure changes) and a short analysis on US price index data.  
- Pro: tackles an interesting, under-explored problem with a novel, practical approach.  
- Pro: experiments are adequate, explore parameter effects, and include real-life data.  
- Pro: paper gives a good literature review and is well written.  
- Con: lacks theoretical guarantees or convergence analysis; needs discussion of assumptions and future work.  
- Con: heavy reliance on heuristics; key decisions (reweighting, effective sample size, relearn criteria) are under-specified.  
- Con: applies methods outside original contexts (Hotelling’s T2, Liptak pooling) without clearly stating loss of original guarantees.  
- Comment: proofs in footnotes are unconventional—better placed in supplementary material.  
- Comment: figures are too small, poorly labeled, and captions/experiment descriptions are unclear.  
- Comment: explanation for LoSST’s poor performance on edge addition under parameter-only changes is unsatisfactory.  
- Suggestion: future work should consider decoupling parameter updates from structure updates.
