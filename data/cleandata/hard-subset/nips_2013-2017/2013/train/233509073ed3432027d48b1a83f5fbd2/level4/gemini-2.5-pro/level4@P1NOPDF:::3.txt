This manuscript presents a systematic framework for structure learning in Structural Equation Models (SEMs) from datasets that are only locally i.i.d. This setting addresses the important and often-overlooked problem of learning from time-dependent data, where the underlying generative model may change over time. The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The proposed techniques for adapting to non-stationary data appear simple yet effective, as demonstrated by the experiments using the PC algorithm.

I have two suggestions that could further strengthen the paper and clarify its contributions.

First, the claim in the experiments that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly..." seems too strong, as performance can vary considerably among different algorithms. The authors should either temper this claim—making it clear that the experiments successfully demonstrate their adaptation for the PC algorithm, while speculating that similar improvements might be seen for other methods—or they should substantiate the assertion by providing a more detailed justification for why other algorithms are expected to behave similarly.

Second, the conclusion discusses that applying these ideas to learn the structure of Bayesian networks may not be straightforward due to challenges in maintaining sufficient statistics. While this issue is not central to the paper's core proposal for SEMs, it highlights that extending this framework to other model classes may require non-trivial modifications. The paper would benefit from acknowledging this limitation earlier. Framing the contributions more directly in the context of SEMs from the outset, while still noting that extensions to other models are conceivable but not necessarily direct, would give the paper a sharper and more precise scope.

In summary, this is a well-written paper on learning the graphical structure for SEMs under the assumption of locally i.i.d. data. The formulation is straightforward to understand and implement, and the experimental results are promising, even though they are focused on a comparison using the PC algorithm.
