Reaction to author feedback:
I do not agree with point (3). Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) do not assume iid data and allow the generating structure to vary within a restricted family of graphical models. While the proposed method may be more flexible in some respects, it is misleading to claim it is the first to address this problem.

Summary:
The paper presents a hypothesis-testing approach for online learning of graphical models from time series, targeting settings where the “data-generating” structure may change relatively frequently. The method is evaluated on both simulated and real data.

Quality:
The results appear correct. However, the discussion of related work (Section 2) is somewhat misleading. Typical score-based algorithms for learning Bayesian networks do not assume the data come from a single generating model; rather, they rely on a decomposable (locally factorized) scoring function. While commonly studied models assume exchangeability (iid) for convenience, this is a modeling assumption, not an algorithmic requirement.

Clarity:
The paper is not sufficiently clear about the precise learning objective. Is the goal change-point detection under specified assumptions, inference of the structure generating the most recent observation window, or something else? It is also unclear whether the contribution is primarily a new model (i.e., assumptions and objectives), a new algorithm (i.e., computational procedures), or both.

The paragraph at the end of page 3 is difficult to follow. For example, does the equation for ρ_{r+1} provide its definition, or was it defined earlier? Please also end sentences with periods even when they conclude with displayed equations.

Figures 1–4 use very small fonts; doubling the font size would improve readability. Adding legends that explain the colors in Figures 1 and 2 would also help. In Section 6, the claim that improved tracking compensates for additional computational cost needs justification; that trade-off is inherently domain-specific and depends on how one values tracking accuracy relative to computation.

Originality:
The work seems original but does not clearly articulate a substantial novel idea. There may be an innovative aspect in the construction of the p-values, but the current presentation makes it hard to assess.

Significance:
If published, the main value may be in motivating more principled (likely Bayesian) approaches to the problem. As presented, the method comes across as a heuristic for a somewhat ill-defined learning objective—one that existing machine learning techniques might already address. Clarifying the task, situating the work more accurately within prior literature, and highlighting the precise innovation would strengthen the paper’s potential impact.
