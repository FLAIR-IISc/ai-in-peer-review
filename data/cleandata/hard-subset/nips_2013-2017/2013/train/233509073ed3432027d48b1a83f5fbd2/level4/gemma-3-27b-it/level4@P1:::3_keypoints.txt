- Proposes a systematic method to adapt SEM structure learning to locally i.i.d. (time-varying) data.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and addresses the important neglected problem of learning from time-dependent data.  
- Techniques are simple and effective, demonstrated using the PC algorithm.  
- Experiments focus on the PC method; the claim that other standard structure learning algorithms would behave similarly is too strong.  
- Suggest rephrasing or justifying the generalizability claim and/or discuss likely behavior of other methods.  
- Extending the approach to Bayesian networks is potentially challenging due to difficulties maintaining sufficient statistics.  
- Recommend stating the SEM-focused scope up front and acknowledging nontrivial modifications needed for other model types.  
- LoSST algorithm is intuitive and appears effective at tracking changes in graphical structure.  
- Simulation results are convincing in both stationary and non-stationary settings.  
- Provides insights into the trade-off between convergence and diligence in the algorithm’s behavior.  
- Demonstrates real-world applicability with a U.S. price index volatility case study.  
- Criticisms: generalizability claim may be overstated; scope needs clearer limitations; convergence vs. diligence requires more rigorous theoretical analysis.
