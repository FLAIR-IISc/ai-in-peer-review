- Reviewer disputes the authors' novelty claim, noting Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) also handles non-iid data and changing generating structure, so claiming first-to-address is misleading.
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based online method for learning graphical models from time series with frequently changing generating models, evaluated on simulated and real data.
- Results seem correct, but Section 2's related-work discussion is misleading: score-based Bayesian network algorithms do not require a single generating model, they rely on local score factorization.
- Major clarity issues: the precise learning task is unclear (change-point detection vs inferring the latest structure vs other), and it's unclear whether the paper introduces a new model, a new algorithm, or both.
- Specific presentation problems: confusing paragraph at end of page 3, ambiguous definition of ρ_{r+1}, odd punctuation after displayed equations, and small font sizes in Figures 1–4.
- Figures lack clear color legends for different curves/lines in Figures 1 and 2.
- Claim in Section 6 that improved tracking offsets extra computation is unclear and depends on domain-specific trade-offs between accuracy and cost.
- Originality is limited: some possible novelty in p-value construction, but overall the idea lacks substantial innovation and is hard to fully assess.
- Significance is moderate: could inspire better (likely Bayesian) methods but is primarily a heuristic step toward development rather than a foundational contribution, addressing a vaguely defined problem existing techniques might already solve.
