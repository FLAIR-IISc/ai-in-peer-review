This manuscript proposes a principled approach to structure learning for SEMs when data are only locally i.i.d.—that is, generated by evolving models over time. The paper is clearly written, addresses an important and underexplored problem (structure learning from time-dependent data), and demonstrates a simple yet effective extension using the PC algorithm. The experimental results are encouraging.

Two suggestions could clarify and sharpen the contributions:
1) The paper states that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly.” This claim appears too strong, as structure-learning methods can differ substantially. I recommend rephrasing to emphasize that the experiments evaluate the PC algorithm in the time-independent setting and its adaptation for time-dependent data, and to frame similar behavior by other methods as a conjecture. Alternatively, if the claim is retained, please provide a detailed justification for why comparable performance should be expected across methods in this context.
2) The conclusion notes that extending the same ideas to Bayesian networks may be nontrivial due to challenges in maintaining sufficient statistics. Although this issue is tangential to the current proposal, it suggests that generalizing beyond SEMs may require more than minor adjustments. It would be helpful to acknowledge this limitation earlier, positioning the paper explicitly as focusing on SEMs while noting that extensions to other models may be possible but are not necessarily straightforward. In short, sharpening the scope would strengthen the contribution.

Overall, this is a well-written paper on SEM structure learning under local i.i.d. assumptions. The formulation is easy to understand and implement, and the results are promising despite being limited to comparisons based on the PC algorithm. I have read the authors’ feedback.
