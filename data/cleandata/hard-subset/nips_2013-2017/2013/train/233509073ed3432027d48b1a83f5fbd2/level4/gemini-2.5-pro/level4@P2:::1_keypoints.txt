- Introduces LoSST, an online heuristic that tracks mean/covariance with adaptive weighting using Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values to detect changes and rerun PC for structure updates.  
- Shows LoSST matches batch learners in stationary settings and outperforms them when structure/parameters change; demonstrates on US price index data.

Strengths:
- Tackles the important problem of structure learning in non-stationary, streaming settings.  
- Single-pass online method is practical for large/streaming data and uses an intuitive re-weighting based on fit.  
- Simulations convincingly show adaptation to abrupt and gradual changes with little cost in stable environments.  
- Real-world application (US price index volatility) illustrates practical utility and identifies known macroeconomic change.

Weaknesses / Suggestions:
- Contribution framing is unclear: unclear whether the paper proposes a new time-varying model or an algorithmic heuristic for an existing task (changepoint detection vs. recent-graph inference vs. full-history tracking).  
- Positioning relative to prior work needs refinement; the distinction between algorithmic assumptions and statistical model assumptions is misstated, and related work on time-varying graphs (e.g., Siracusa & Fisher 2009) should be better acknowledged.  
- Originality is mainly an effective engineering combination of existing techniques rather than a new theoretical advance; the paper should acknowledge this.  
- Presentation issues: the update equations (e.g., ρ_{r+1}, η, τ) are hard to follow, figures have small fonts and unlabeled lines, and some claims (e.g., trade-offs in Section 6) are speculative.  
- Minor formatting fixes needed (e.g., punctuation after displayed equations).
