- Problem: tracking changes in variable dependency structure from streaming data for SEMs; authors propose LoSST which uses PC for structure learning and tracks weighted statistics to trigger relearning.

- Method: LoSST weights points by “surprise” using Mahalanobis distance, estimates p-values via Hotelling’s T², combines them with Liptak pooling to form a significance ρ, and uses a noisy-OR with the previous ρ to trigger structure learning.

- Experiments: Synthetic tests with parameter or structural changes and a real-world US price index analysis (529 monthly points, 6 variables) showing detection of known changes while ignoring short-term fluctuations.

- Contribution: Addresses an important, under-explored problem and offers a practical, efficient algorithmic solution.

- Novelty/practicality: LoSST is a creative combination of methods and appears computationally and memory efficient.

- Empirical evaluation: Experiments are adequate, explore parameter effects, and include an informative real-world case study.

- Writing and background: Paper is generally well-written and provides a thorough literature review.

- Major weakness: No theoretical analysis or performance guarantees are provided; even simplified guarantees should be discussed or listed as future work.

- Heuristic reliance: LoSST depends heavily on heuristics (re-weighting scheme, effective sample size, trigger criteria) and these design choices need clearer acknowledgment and justification.

- Statistical assumptions: Some applied methods may violate their theoretical assumptions—e.g., using Hotelling’s T² with non-integer re-weighted effective sample sizes and using Liptak pooling despite likely dependence among tests—these caveats should be explicitly stated.

- Presentation issues: Proof outlines belong in supplementary material rather than footnotes.

- Figures and formatting: Figures are too small, have excessive whitespace, missing axis labels, captions, and clear legends.

- Experimental clarity: Description of experiments (notably the first paragraph on p.7) is unclear and needs clarification.

- Interpretation and design suggestions: Explanation for LoSST’s worse performance on edge addition after parameter change is unconvincing; decoupling triggers for parameter vs. structure updates seems desirable.

- Overall: Interesting direction and a practical first implementation, but more theoretical grounding, justification of heuristics, clearer presentation, and improved experiments are needed.
