- Problem: Detecting changes in dependency structure in data streams; authors propose LoSST for efficient structure-change detection in simple SEMs.
- Method: LoSST combines batch PC with maintained streaming statistics (weighted mean, correlation matrix, effective sample size) and a reweighting scheme based on Mahalanobis distance.
- Significance test: MD p-values estimated via Hotelling’s T2, combined with Lipták’s weighted pooling to produce a statistic ρ used (with a noisy-OR) to trigger relearning.
- Experiments: Evaluated on synthetic datasets (parameter vs. structural changes, varying variables/degree) and a real U.S. price-index dataset (529 months, 6 variables).
- Positive strengths: tackles an important, under-explored problem; algorithm is novel, practical, and efficient; experiments and real-data evaluation are adequate; related work is thorough; writing is clear.
- Major weakness: No theoretical guarantees or formal analysis under plausible assumptions; needs discussion or simplified-theory directions.
- Heuristic reliance: Key design choices (reweighting, effective sample size, relearn triggers) are heuristic and should be transparently documented and justified.
- Misapplied assumptions: Using Hotelling’s T2 for weighted samples and Lipták pooling for potentially dependent tests likely violates those methods’ assumptions and should be explicitly acknowledged.
- Presentation issues: Footnote proofs and technical details overflow format — move to supplement; figures are too small, sparsely labeled, and have unclear captions/legends.
- Clarity gaps: Some experimental descriptions are cryptic (e.g., top of p.7); explanation for worse performance on edge additions under parameter change is unconvincing.
- Recommendation: Consider decoupling parameter-update triggers from structure-update triggers to improve detection behavior.
- Overall: A promising, practical first implementation; would be strengthened by clearer documentation of heuristics, explicit acknowledgment of assumption violations, better figures/experiments, and discussion or provision of theoretical guarantees.
