- Presents a systematic method to enable structure learning for SEMs when data are only locally i.i.d., addressing time-dependent data.  
- Tackles an important and often neglected problem since many structure-learning algorithms assume i.i.d. data.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and the proposed extensions are simple and implementable.  
- Techniques appear effective, demonstrated empirically using the PC algorithm.  
- Claim that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly” is too strong—either rephrase to limit scope or provide justification.  
- Suggest explicitly acknowledging earlier that extending the approach to Bayesian networks is nontrivial due to maintaining sufficient statistics, and frame the work’s scope as focused on SEMs.  
- Experimental results are promising but currently limited to comparisons involving only the PC method.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with clear formulation and useful experiments; scope and generalization to other models should be clarified.
