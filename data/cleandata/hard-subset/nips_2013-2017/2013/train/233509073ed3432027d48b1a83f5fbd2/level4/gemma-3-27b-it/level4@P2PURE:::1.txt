I disagree with point (3). The work of Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) does not require data to be independently and identically distributed, and it accommodates variations in the underlying “generating structure” – albeit within a limited set of graphical models. While the present method offers increased flexibility, it is inaccurate to claim it is the first to address this problem.

*

The paper introduces a hypothesis-testing based approach for learning graphical models online from time series data. The method is specifically designed for scenarios where the underlying “data generating” graphical model changes relatively frequently, and its performance is evaluated using both simulated and real datasets.

Quality
The results appear to be correct. However, the discussion of related work (Section 2) contains a misleading characterization: score-based algorithms for Bayesian network learning do not necessarily assume a single generating model, but rather assume that the scoring function can be factored into local terms. While commonly studied models often assume exchangeability (or iid data), this is a property of the model itself, not the algorithm.

Clarity
The presentation is generally good, but could be improved. The core learning task remains somewhat unclear: is the goal to infer change points (under clearly defined assumptions about their existence and nature), to infer the structure generating the most recent data point, or something else? Furthermore, it is unclear whether the work introduces a novel model (i.e., new assumptions and learning goals) or a novel algorithm (i.e., new computational methods to achieve established learning goals), or both.

The paragraph at the end of page 3 is difficult to follow; for example, it is unclear whether the equation for \rho_{r+1} defines the variable or refers to a previously established definition. I also note the authors’ omission of periods when ending sentences containing displayed equations. An equally important concern is the small font size in Figures 1-4, which should be doubled for readability. Adding descriptions for the different colors in Figures 1 and 2 would also be beneficial. In Section 6, the claim that improved tracking can compensate for increased computational cost is questionable, as this would depend on a domain-specific trade-off between tracking accuracy and computational resources.

Originality
The work appears original, although it lacks substantial innovation or a novel core idea. The construction of the p-values may contain a novel element, but I am unable to fully assess this. 

Significance
If published, this work could potentially stimulate the development of improved, likely Bayesian, methods for this problem. However, this should be viewed as development work rather than fundamental research. The paper presents a heuristic approach to a learning problem that is arguably already solvable using existing machine learning techniques.
