- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing based online method to learn graphical models from time series with frequently changing data-generating graphs.  
- Method’s performance is demonstrated on both simulated and real datasets.  
- Results appear correct.  
- Related-work discussion is misleading: score-based Bayesian network algorithms don’t require a single generating model but usually rely on locally factorizing scores; exchangeability is a model assumption, not an algorithmic one.  
- Unclear learning task: is the goal change-point detection, estimating the current graph, or something else?  
- Unclear whether the contribution is a new model (assumptions/goals), a new algorithm (computation), or both.  
- Specific exposition problems: a difficult-to-follow paragraph (end of p.3), ambiguous definition/use of rho_{r+1}, and punctuation issues with displayed equations.  
- Presentation issues in figures: small font sizes in Figures 1–4 and missing color legends for Figures 1–2.  
- Claim in Section 6 that improved tracking can offset extra computational cost is unconvincing and seems domain-dependent.  
- Work appears original but lacks substantive novel ideas or innovation.  
- Construction of the p-values is hard to follow and may contain the paper’s only non-obvious element.  
- Significance is limited: could lead to practical Bayesian developments but reads as development/heuristic rather than fundamental research, and existing ML methods might already address the problem.
