- Reviewer remains unconvinced by rebuttal point (3); prior work (Siracusa III & Fisher AISTATS 2009) already handles non-iid, varying data-generating structures.  
- Results appear technically sound but the related-work discussion is misleading.  
- Typical score-based Bayesian network learning algorithms do not assume a single generating model; they use factorized local scoring—exchangeability is a model assumption, not an algorithmic one.  
- Core learning task is ambiguous: unclear whether goal is change-point detection, recovering the recent data-point structure, or something else.  
- It's unclear whether the paper proposes a new formal model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- Specific exposition issues: paragraph at end of page 3 is hard to follow; definition of rho_{r+1} is unclear.  
- Formatting and figure problems: missing periods after displayed equations; Figures 1–4 need much larger fonts and Figures 1–2 need color legends.  
- Claim in Section 6 that improved tracking offsets computational cost is not justified and depends on domain-specific trade-offs.  
- Originality is limited: some novelty may exist (p-value construction), but overall lacks substantive innovation.  
- Significance is limited: approach is heuristic, addresses an ill-defined problem, and seems more like development work than novel research.
