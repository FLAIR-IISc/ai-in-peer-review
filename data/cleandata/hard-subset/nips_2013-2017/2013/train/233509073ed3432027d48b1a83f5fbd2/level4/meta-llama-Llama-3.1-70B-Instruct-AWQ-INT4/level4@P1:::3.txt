This manuscript presents a systematic approach to extend structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) to accommodate data sets that are only locally independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.), where the data may be generated from distinct underlying models over time. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, addressing an important problem that has been largely neglected by many commonly used algorithms for structure learning.

The authors propose the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST) algorithm, a heuristic structure learning algorithm that tracks changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner. The algorithm assumes only that the generating process is locally stationary, meaning that data are generated i.i.d. from the true underlying generative model at each time step, but it is not necessarily the case that the model remains the same over time.

The LoSST algorithm is capable of exhibiting different long-run properties, depending on its parameters. The algorithm can be convergent, diligent, or strike a balance between these two desirable properties. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's behavior under different parameter settings and demonstrate its performance through simulations.

The simulation results show that LoSST performs comparably to batch-mode learning when the generating graphical structure is globally stationary and significantly better when it is only locally stationary. The algorithm is also able to detect changes in the underlying structure and adapt to the new structure.

The authors also apply the LoSST algorithm to real-world data, specifically to seasonally adjusted price index data from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics. The results demonstrate the algorithm's ability to detect changes in the volatility of the relationships between the price indexes.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the LoSST algorithm and its properties. The experimental results are promising, and the application to real-world data demonstrates the algorithm's potential in practice.

However, there are a few suggestions that could clarify the paper's contributions. Firstly, in the experiments, the authors state that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly, ..." This assertion may be too strong, and it would be better to rephrase it to make it clearer that the experiments are about the PC method for time-independent and with an adaptation for time-dependent data. Alternatively, the authors could explain in detail why they believe other methods would perform similarly.

Secondly, in the conclusions, the authors discuss the potential difficulties of extending the LoSST algorithm to learn the structure of Bayesian networks. While this is not a central issue to the proposal in this paper, it highlights the challenges of applying the same learning procedure to other models. The authors could benefit from disclosing this fact from the beginning and presenting the paper in more direct terms about its goal in SEM models, while still pointing out that the extension to other models might be possible but not necessarily straightforward.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that presents a systematic approach to learning the graph structure for SEMs under the assumption that data are only locally i.i.d. The formulation is simple to understand and implement, and the experimental results look promising.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel approach to extend structure learning for SEMs to accommodate locally i.i.d. data.
* The LoSST algorithm is well-explained, and its properties are thoroughly analyzed.
* The simulation results demonstrate the algorithm's performance and its ability to detect changes in the underlying structure.
* The application to real-world data shows the algorithm's potential in practice.

Weaknesses:

* The assertion that other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly could be too strong and may require further clarification.
* The paper could benefit from disclosing the potential difficulties of extending the LoSST algorithm to learn the structure of Bayesian networks from the beginning.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and systematic approach to learning the graph structure for SEMs under the assumption that data are only locally i.i.d.
* The experimental results demonstrate the algorithm's performance and its potential in practice.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the LoSST algorithm and its properties.

Arguments for rejection:

* The paper may benefit from further clarification on the assertion that other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly.
* The potential difficulties of extending the LoSST algorithm to learn the structure of Bayesian networks could be more explicitly discussed from the beginning.
