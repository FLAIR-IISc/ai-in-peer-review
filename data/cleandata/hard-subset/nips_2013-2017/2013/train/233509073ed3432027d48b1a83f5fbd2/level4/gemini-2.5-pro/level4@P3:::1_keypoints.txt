- Problem: Learns graphical model structure from non-stationary data streams, addressing the unrealistic i.i.d. assumption of most structure learners.

- Method overview: LoSST is an online heuristic that tracks weighted sufficient statistics (mean, covariance, effective sample size) and updates weights by Mahalanobis-distance-based fit.

- Adaptation mechanism: Uses pooled p-values over recent data to increase weight on new points when fit is poor, and triggers probabilistic structure relearning after suspected changes.

- Comparison baseline: Contrasted with batch learners (fail under non-stationarity) and online changepoint methods (require storing much history).

- Empirical evaluation: Synthetic experiments show LoSST matches batch PC in stationary regimes and outperforms when graphs or parameters change; applied to U.S. price index data for real-world illustration.

- Strength — significance: Tackles a practically important problem of online structure learning under distribution shift.

- Strength — novelty/intuitiveness: Novel synthesis of online sufficient-stat updates, Mahalanobis-based change detection, and p-value pooling with an intuitive reweighting idea.

- Strength — experiments: Thorough simulations and a probabilistic-relearning experiment demonstrating intended behavior and advantages.

- Strength — conceptual framing: Clear discussion of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and how parameters (γ, T) affect it.

- Weakness — task formulation: Learning objective is imprecise (changepoint detection vs. recent-graph estimation vs. whole-series accuracy), making the contribution unclear.

- Weakness — positioning: Overstates novelty relative to prior work on time-varying graphs; needs a more nuanced comparison highlighting LoSST’s specific advantages.

- Weakness — heuristic choices: Key components (weight update form, noisy-OR relearning) lack theoretical justification and sensitivity analysis; hyperparameters chosen by “unsystematic investigation.”

- Weakness — presentation issues: Pooled p-value derivation is hard to follow, figures suffer from tiny fonts and missing legends, and some trade-off claims are vague.

- Overall judgment: Strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance contingent on clarifying the task, situating related work better, providing sensitivity/theoretical discussion, and improving presentation.
