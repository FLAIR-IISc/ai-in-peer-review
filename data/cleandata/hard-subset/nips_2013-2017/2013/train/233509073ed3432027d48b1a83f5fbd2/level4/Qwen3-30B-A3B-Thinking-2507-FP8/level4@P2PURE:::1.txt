

Reaction to author feedback:  
I disagree with point (3): The work of Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) does not assume data are iid and allows the "generating structure" to vary (even within a limited set of graphical models). While the proposed method offers greater flexibility, it is misleading to claim it is the first to address this problem.  

The paper presents a hypothesis-testing approach for learning graphical models in an online setting from time series data, specifically targeting scenarios where the data-generating graphical model changes relatively frequently. Performance is demonstrated using both simulated and real data.  

Quality:  
The results appear correct. However, the related work description (Section 2) is misleading: typical score-based algorithms for Bayesian network learning do not assume data come from a single generating model; rather, they assume the scoring function factorizes into local terms. While most studied models assume exchangeability (i.e., iid data), this pertains to the model, not the algorithm.  

Clarity:  
The paper’s presentation is generally acceptable but requires improvement. Crucially, it remains unclear what the core learning task entails: inferring change points (under well-defined assumptions), inferring the generating structure of the final observed data point, or another objective? Additionally, it is ambiguous whether the work proposes a novel model (formulating assumptions and learning goals) or a novel algorithm (computational methods to achieve learning goals), or both. The paragraph at the end of page 3 is difficult to follow; for instance, the equation for $\rho_{r+1}$ appears to define it, but the definition may have been stated earlier. The authors also omit periods after sentences ending with displayed equations. Furthermore, Figures 1–4 have insufficient font sizes (doubling would be beneficial), and Figures 1–2 lack color legends. In Section 6, the claim that improved tracking compensates for higher computational cost seems domain-specific, as the trade-off depends on application-dependent valuations of tracking accuracy versus computational cost.  

Originality:  
The work appears original but lacks substantial innovation or novel ideas. The construction of p-values may contain novelty, though I was unable to fully follow it.  

Significance:  
If published, the work’s potential significance lies in enabling rapid development of superior Bayesian methods for this problem. However, this should be viewed as methodological development rather than research. The approach is heuristic and addresses a learning problem that existing machine learning frameworks should already resolve.
