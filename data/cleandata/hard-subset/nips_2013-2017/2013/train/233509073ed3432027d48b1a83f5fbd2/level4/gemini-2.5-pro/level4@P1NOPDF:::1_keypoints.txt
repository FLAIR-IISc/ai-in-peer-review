- Disagrees with authors: Siracusa & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-i.i.d. data and varying generating structures, so claiming to be first is misleading.  
- Paper proposes an online hypothesis-testing method to learn graphical models from time-series with frequently changing structure, evaluated on simulated and real data.  
- Technical results appear correct.  
- Related work section is misleading: score-based BN algorithms do not require a single generating model; factorization of the scoring function, not i.i.d., is the critical assumption.  
- Core learning objective is unclear: is the goal change-point detection, learning the most recent structure, or something else?  
- Unclear whether the main contribution is a new probabilistic model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- Paragraph at end of p.3 is hard to follow; equation for ρ_{r+1} is ambiguous and displayed-equation punctuation is inconsistent.  
- Figures need improvement: font sizes are too small and Figures 1–2 lack legends or color explanations.  
- Section 6’s claim that better tracking offsets extra computational cost is misleading—this trade-off is application-dependent and cannot be asserted universally.  
- Originality: method seems novel but lacks a clear conceptual innovation; p-value construction may be the only new piece but is insufficiently explained.  
- Significance: contribution is incremental and heuristic, problem formulation is vague, and the task may be solvable with existing ML frameworks; could inspire more principled Bayesian approaches.
