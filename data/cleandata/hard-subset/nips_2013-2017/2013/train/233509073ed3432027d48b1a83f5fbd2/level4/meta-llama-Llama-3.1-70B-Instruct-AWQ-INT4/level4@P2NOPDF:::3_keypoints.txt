- Paper proposes a systematic method for structure learning in SEMs that handles locally i.i.d. data generated by distinct underlying models over time.  
- Manuscript is well-written, easy to follow, and addresses the important, under-studied problem of learning from time-dependent data.  
- Proposed extensions to handle time-dependent data are simple and empirically effective, demonstrated using the PC algorithm.  
- Experimental claim that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" is overly broad and should be rephrased or justified.  
- Authors should either limit claims to experiments with PC and speculate about generality, or provide reasons/evidence why other methods would behave similarly.  
- Discussion about extending the approach to Bayesian networks (e.g., maintaining sufficient statistics) indicates such extensions may not be straightforward and should be disclosed earlier.  
- Recommend clarifying the paper’s scope up front: focus on SEMs with a note that extensions to other models (like Bayesian networks) may require more than minor modifications.  
- Overall: formulation is simple, implementable, and experimental results are promising despite being primarily PC-focused.
