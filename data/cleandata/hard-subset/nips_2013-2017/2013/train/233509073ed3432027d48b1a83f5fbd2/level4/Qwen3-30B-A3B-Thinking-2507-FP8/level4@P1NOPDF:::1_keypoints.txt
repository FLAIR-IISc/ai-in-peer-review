- Reviewer disagrees with the novelty claim: Siracusa & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handles non-iid data and varying generating structures within constrained graphical models.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing framework for online learning of graphical models from time series where the generating model changes frequently, validated on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear technically sound.  
- Related work discussion is misleading: score-based Bayesian network algorithms do not inherently require iid data—the iid assumption is a modeling choice, not an algorithmic one.  
- Core learning objective is ambiguous: is the goal change-point detection, recovering the most recent generating structure, or something else?  
- Unclear whether the main contribution is a novel model, a novel algorithm, or both.  
- Exposition problems: poorly explained paragraph at end of page 3, undefined notation (e.g., ρ_{r+1}), and missing punctuation after displayed equations.  
- Figures need formatting fixes: larger fonts (about double) and color legends for Figures 1–2 to improve legibility.  
- The claim that "improved tracking compensates for computational cost" is unsupported and depends on domain-specific trade-offs.  
- Originality is limited: contribution seems incremental; potential novelty in the p-value construction is not clearly explained.  
- Significance is limited: method is heuristic and practical rather than a fundamental theoretical advance; existing ML frameworks might address the problem with clearer formulation.
