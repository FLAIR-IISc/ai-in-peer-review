- Reviewer disagrees with the authors’ claim of being first: Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) also handles non-iid data and varying generating structures.
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based online method to learn graphical models from time-series with frequently changing generating graphs, evaluated on simulated and real data.
- Results appear correct but the related-work discussion is misleading about score-based BN algorithms and their assumptions (algorithm vs model/exchangeability).
- Major clarity issue: the precise learning task is unclear (change-point detection vs inferring current structure vs other goals).
- Unclear whether the contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.
- Specific exposition problems: the paragraph at the end of page 3 (and the definition of ρ_{r+1}) is hard to follow.
- Presentation flaws: omitted periods after displayed equations, figures’ font sizes too small, and missing legends for colors in Figures 1–2.
- Confusing claim in Section 6 that improved tracking could offset additional computational cost—this tradeoff is domain-dependent and needs clarification.
- Originality is limited: work seems original but lacks a genuinely novel idea; construction of p-values might contain novelty but is not clearly explained.
- Significance is limited: contribution may mainly prompt development of better (likely Bayesian) methods; overall the approach feels heuristic and the problem formulation appears already addressable by existing ML methods.
