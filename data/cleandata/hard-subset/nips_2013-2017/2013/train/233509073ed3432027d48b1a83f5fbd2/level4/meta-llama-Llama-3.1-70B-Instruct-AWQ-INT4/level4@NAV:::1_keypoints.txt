- Reviewer disputes authors' claim of novelty: Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid data and limited variations in generating graphical structure.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing online method to learn graphical models from time series with frequently changing generating models; evaluated on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear correct, but Section 2’s related-work discussion is misleading about score-based Bayesian network algorithms and their assumptions.  
- Clarifies that score-based methods typically assume factorable scoring functions, not a single global generating model; exchangeability/iid is a model assumption, not an algorithmic one.  
- Presentation is generally clear but key confusions remain about the actual learning task (change points vs. current-structure inference vs. something else).  
- It is unclear whether the contribution is a new formal model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- Specific clarity issues: the paragraph/equation for ρ_{r+1} is hard to follow; displayed equations need clearer punctuation and explanation.  
- Presentation/figure issues: small font in Figures 1–4 and missing legends/descriptions for colors in Figures 1–2.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking justifies extra computation is confusing and depends on domain-specific trade-offs.  
- Originality is limited: method seems original but lacks a strong novel idea or real innovation; p-value construction might be the only potentially novel aspect.  
- Significance is limited: could lead to development of better (possibly Bayesian) methods but is more developmental than research; approach feels heuristic and tackles an unclearly defined problem that existing methods might handle.
