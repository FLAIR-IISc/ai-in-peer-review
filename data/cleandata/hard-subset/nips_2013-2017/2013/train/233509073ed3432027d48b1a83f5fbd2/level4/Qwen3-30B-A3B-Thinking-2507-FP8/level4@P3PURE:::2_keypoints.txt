- Addresses tracking changes in dependency structure in streaming data; proposes LoSST for efficient SEM structure learning.  
- LoSST leverages batch PC algorithm and tracks weighted means, correlation matrix, and effective sample size to decide when to relearn structure.  
- Weights are assigned by Mahalanobis-distance-based p-values (Hotelling’s T²) and combined with Liptak pooling into a significance statistic ρ, used with a noisy-OR prior to trigger relearning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic scenarios (parameter/structure shifts, varying variables/degrees) and on real U.S. price-index data, showing detection of known trends and robustness to transient noise.  
- Pro: tackles an important, underexplored problem with a novel, practical, and computationally/memory-efficient algorithm.  
- Pro: experiments are systematic and include an insightful real-world dataset; literature review is thorough and writing is clear.  
- Con: lacks any theoretical guarantees; should at least provide or outline guarantees under simplified assumptions as future work.  
- Con: relies on several heuristic design choices (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning triggers) that need clearer justification.  
- Con: applies statistical tools outside their validated contexts (Hotelling’s T² for weighted/partial samples, Liptak pooling assumptions) without explicit caveats.  
- Other: proofs in footnotes should be moved to supplementary material; figures are too small, poorly labeled, and captions/experimental descriptions need clarification.  
- Other: unsatisfactory explanation for poor performance on edge addition when only parameters change; recommend decoupling parameter updates from structure updates.  
- Overall: promising, practical heuristic-first implementation that opens directions for future theoretical and methodological work.
