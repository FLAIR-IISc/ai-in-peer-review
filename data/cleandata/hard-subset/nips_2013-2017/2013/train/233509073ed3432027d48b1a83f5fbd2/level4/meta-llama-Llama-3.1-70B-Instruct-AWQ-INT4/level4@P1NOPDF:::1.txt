Reaction to the author feedback:

I must respectfully disagree with point (3) regarding the work of Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009). Their approach does not assume the data are independent and identically distributed (iid), and it allows the "generating structure" to vary, albeit within a limited set of graphical models. While the proposed method offers greater flexibility in some respects, I find it misleading to claim that it is the first to address this problem.

The paper presents a hypothesis-testing-based approach to learning graphical models in an online fashion from time series data. The proposed method is specifically designed for scenarios where the "data generating" graphical model may change relatively frequently. The performance of the method is demonstrated using both simulated and real data.

Quality:

The results appear to be correct. However, I find the description of related work in Section 2 to be somewhat misleading. Typical score-based algorithms for learning Bayesian networks do not assume that the data come from a single generating model; instead, they assume the scoring function can be factorized into local terms. While it is true that many commonly studied models assume exchangeability (or iid data), this assumption pertains to the model, not the algorithm.

Clarity:

The presentation of the paper is generally clear, but there is room for improvement. A significant issue is that the paper does not clearly define the actual learning task: is it inferring change points (under well-formulated assumptions about the existence and nature of change points), inferring the generating structure of the most recent data point seen, or something else? Additionally, it is unclear whether the work proposes a new model (i.e., a new set of assumptions and learning goals) or a new algorithm (i.e., computational means to achieve the learning goals) or both.

The paragraph at the end of page 3 is difficult to follow, particularly the equation for \rho_{r+1}; it is unclear whether this equation defines \rho_{r+1} or if its definition has been provided earlier. I suggest using periods (.) to end sentences that conclude with a displayed equation. Furthermore, the font size in Figures 1-4 is too small and could be increased. Adding descriptions for the different colors used in Figures 1 and 2 would also improve clarity. In Section 6, I found the idea that improved tracking could compensate for the additional computational cost to be confusing; doesn't this depend on a domain-specific valuation of tracking accuracy against computational cost?

Originality:

The work appears to be original, but it seems to lack a novel idea or significant innovation. Perhaps there is something innovative in the construction of the p-values, but I must admit that I was unable to fully follow this aspect.

Significance:

The potential significance of this work, if published, lies in its potential to lead to the development of superior, presumably Bayesian, methods for this problem. However, this should be viewed as a development rather than a research contribution. The approach seems heuristic, and the learning problem is not clearly defined; existing machine learning machinery should already be able to address this problem.
