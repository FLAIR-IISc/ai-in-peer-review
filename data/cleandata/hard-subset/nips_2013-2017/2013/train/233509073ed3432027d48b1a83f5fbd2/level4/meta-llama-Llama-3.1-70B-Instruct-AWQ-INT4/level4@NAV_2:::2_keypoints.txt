- Tackles an interesting, under-explored problem: tracking changes in dependency structure in streaming data for Structural Equation Models (SEMs).
- Focuses on a computationally and memory-efficient solution for simpler SEMs.
- Proposes LoSST (Locally Stationary Structure Tracker), a new algorithm combining batch PC structure learning with streaming statistics to trigger re-learning.
- LoSST tracks weighted means, the correlation matrix, and an effective weighted sample size from the stream.
- New-point weights are based on "surprisingness" via Mahalanobis distance; p-values are estimated using Hotelling’s T2 and pooled with Liptak’s method.
- Uses a noisy-OR combination with previous step significance to decide when to initiate new structure learning.
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets with parameter or structural changes across varied variable counts and max-degree configurations.
- Applied to a real US price-index dataset (529 monthly points, six variables), detecting known economic changes while ignoring short-lived shifts.
- Provides a solid review of related work in graphical models, online learning, and changepoint detection, motivating the approach.
- Strength: novel, practical algorithm addressing an important problem with adequate experiments and clear writing.
- Major weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees or convergence/detection analysis under simplifying assumptions.
- Method is heuristic-heavy; many design choices and workarounds are not clearly justified or theoretically grounded.
- Specific statistical concerns: using Hotelling’s T2 with reweighted/partial counts and Liptak pooling outside their original assumptions may be inappropriate.
- Authors should explicitly state when methods are used beyond their formal guarantees and clarify decision rationale.
- Presentation issues: proofs in footnotes should be moved to supplements; figures are too small, have extra spacing, and lack labels/legends and detailed captions.
- Experimental description is unclear, and the explanation for LoSST’s poorer performance on edge additions under parameter-only changes is unsatisfactory.
- Suggestion: consider decoupling parameter updates and structure updates, and discuss theory or future work to provide guarantees.
- Overall: promising, well-written practical contribution with presentation and theoretical/justification gaps that need addressing.
