- Paper proposes hypothesis-testing approach for online graphical model learning from time series that may change frequently; evaluated on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear technically sound overall.  
- Related work is potentially mischaracterized; many score-based BN methods can handle nonstationarity and do not strictly require a single fixed generative model.  
- Claim of being the “first” to address changing structures is inaccurate—Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) handles non-i.i.d. data and structural variation.  
- Reviewer disagrees with authors’ response to prior feedback about novelty.  
- Main learning objective is ambiguous: unclear if task is change-point detection, recent-structure estimation, or something else.  
- Unclear whether contribution is a new model formulation, a novel algorithm, or both.  
- Specific clarity/format issues: paragraph at end of page 3 is hard to follow; definition of ρ_{r+1} lacks context; displayed equations lack sentence punctuation.  
- Figures 1–4 have font sizes too small and lack color/key descriptions for clarity.  
- Claim that better tracking justifies higher computation is overly broad and unjustified without domain-specific valuation.  
- Originality: implementation is somewhat original but lacks a clearly novel core idea; possible novelty in p-value construction is not well explained.  
- Significance: impact seems incremental and engineering-focused; approach appears heuristic and not a fundamental advance over existing ML techniques.
