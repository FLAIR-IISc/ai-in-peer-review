- Introduces a systematic method to adapt SEM structure learning for locally i.i.d., time-varying data.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and tackles the often-overlooked problem of learning from time-dependent data.  
- Proposed extensions to structure learning are straightforward and empirically effective, demonstrated using the PC method.  
- Experimental validation focuses on PC; the claim that other standard methods would behave similarly is too broad and should be softened or justified.  
- Recommend rephrasing or providing rationale if asserting comparable performance across different structure learning algorithms.  
- Notes potential challenges in extending the approach to Bayesian networks due to maintaining sufficient statistics.  
- Suggest stating the limitation and scope (focus on SEMs) explicitly up front, acknowledging non-trivial work needed to extend to other models.  
- Overall: simple, implementable formulation with promising results, albeit experiments are limited to the PC method; authors’ feedback acknowledged.
