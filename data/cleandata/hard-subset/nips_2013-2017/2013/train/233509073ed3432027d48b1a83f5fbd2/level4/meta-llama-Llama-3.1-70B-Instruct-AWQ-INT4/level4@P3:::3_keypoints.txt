- Proposes LoSST, a novel heuristic for tracking graphical model structure from time-varying (locally i.i.d.) data, departing from stationary assumptions.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains ideas and methodology.  
- LoSST works under local i.i.d. conditions and tracks changes in structure or parameters in real time.  
- Simulations show LoSST matches batch-mode learning when structure is globally stationary and outperforms it when structure is only locally stationary.  
- Authors thoroughly discuss related work and clearly motivate limitations of traditional structure learning.  
- Experimental results are promising with detailed analysis across scenarios.  
- Claim that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly” is too strong and should be rephrased or justified (experiments focused on PC method).  
- Limitation about extending the approach to Bayesian networks (difficulty maintaining sufficient statistics) should be disclosed earlier (e.g., introduction/related work).  
- Paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of LoSST.  
- Quality: technically sound with clear exposition and solid experimental presentation.  
- Clarity: paper is easy to follow and well-presented.  
- Originality: presents a significant and novel departure from traditional structure-learning approaches.  
- Significance: has potential to make an important contribution to graphical models and structure learning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of approach, clear advantages over traditional methods, and promising experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overbroad claims about other algorithms, late disclosure of limitations for Bayesian networks, and missing computational-complexity analysis.
