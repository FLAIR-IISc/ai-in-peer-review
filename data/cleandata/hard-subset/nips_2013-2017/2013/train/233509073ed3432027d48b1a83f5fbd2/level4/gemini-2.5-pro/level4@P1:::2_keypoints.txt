- Paper proposes LoSST, an efficient online method to track changing SEM structures by dynamically triggering batch PC re-learning using online-updated statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size).  
- New-data weighting based on Mahalanobis-distance p-values and Hotelling’s T-squared estimate; p-values are combined via Liptak pooling to produce a significance ρ used in a noisy-OR trigger.  
- Experiments compare LoSST to batch PC on synthetic shifts in parameters or structure across varied settings and an application to US price-index data showing detection of macro shifts.  
- Strength: Addresses an important, under-explored problem with potential practical impact for SEMs and concept-drift settings.  
- Strength: Introduces a novel, practical, and computationally efficient algorithm combining existing methods in a creative way.  
- Strength: Experiments are adequate, exploring parameter effects and including a real-world dataset demonstrating potential.  
- Strength: Good literature review situating the contribution within related work.  
- Strength: Paper is generally well written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis or performance guarantees, even under simplifying assumptions.  
- Weakness: LoSST is largely heuristic; key design choices (re-weighting, effective sample size, trigger) need clearer justification and acknowledgment as heuristics.  
- Weakness: Uses statistical tools outside their original assumptions—Hotelling’s T-squared on weighted/non-integer effective sample sizes and Liptak pooling on dependent sequential p-values—potentially invalidating guarantees.  
- Recommendation: Authors should explicitly state limitations when applying statistical tools beyond their guaranteed contexts.  
- Other: Proof sketches in footnotes are unconventional; move proofs/omitted details to a supplementary appendix.  
- Other: Figures are too small, poorly formatted, with missing labels and unclear legends—need improved presentation.  
- Other: Experimental setup descriptions (e.g., fixed relearning schedule) are unclear and should be clarified.  
- Other: Explanation for LoSST’s worse edge-removal performance after parameter changes is unconvincing; consider decoupling triggers for parameter vs. structure updates.  
- Conclusion: Paper is a promising, practical first step but should more thoroughly discuss heuristic limitations and non-standard use of statistical components.
