- Paper proposes LoSST, a heuristic online algorithm for learning time-varying graphical model structure under local stationarity without batch processing.  
- LoSST tracks graphical structure in real-time using weighted sufficient statistics, Mahalanobis distances, and pooled p-values.  
- Simulations show LoSST matches batch performance during stability and outperforms during structural shifts; real-world application detects the "Great Moderation."  
- Strength — Quality: Technical approach is sound; updates and statistical tests are well-motivated and simulations (500 datasets) are rigorous.  
- Strength — Theoretical: Provides a useful convergence/diligence trade-off analysis (Section 3.1).  
- Strength — Clarity: Core algorithm and real-world application are clearly described; simulation figures are well organized.  
- Strength — Originality: Novel integration of pooled p-values for tracking structure online, filling a gap in time-varying graphical model methods.  
- Strength — Significance: Enables real-time structure learning in domains (e.g., finance) where batch methods are infeasible.  
- Weakness — Related work: Mischaracterizes Siracusa & Fisher (2009); must clarify differences between their Bayesian batch approach and LoSST’s online frequentist approach.  
- Weakness — Clarity: Learning objective is ambiguous (tracking current vs. all intermediate structures); definition of ρ_{r+1} appears after its use and is confusing.  
- Weakness — Originality/Significance: Core method is heuristic (e.g., Equation 4) rather than a principled new statistical framework; novelty is overstated without stronger contrast to existing methods.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — valuable contribution but needs corrections to related work and clearer task framing.  
- Pro: Novel online structure-tracking framework, rigorous simulations, real-world validation, clear convergence analysis.  
- Con: Misattribution in related work, ambiguous task definition, heuristic approach lacks deeper theoretical novelty.  
- Revision suggestions: Correct Siracusa & Fisher comparison; explicitly state that LoSST tracks the current generating structure; define ρ_{r+1} before Equation 4; add figure legends and increase font sizes in Figures 1–4.
