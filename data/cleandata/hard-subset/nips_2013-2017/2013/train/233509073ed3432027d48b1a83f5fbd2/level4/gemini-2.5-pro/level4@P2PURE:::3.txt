This manuscript presents a systematic approach for learning the structure of Structural Equation Models (SEMs) from data that is only locally i.i.d., addressing scenarios where the underlying data-generating model may change over time. The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and addresses the important problem of learning from time-dependent data—a challenge often overlooked by standard structure learning algorithms. The proposed techniques for extending learning to this setting are simple yet appear effective, as demonstrated by the experiments conducted with the PC algorithm.

I have two suggestions that could further clarify the paper's contributions.

First, the experimental section asserts that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly." This claim seems overly strong, as performance differences between structure learning methods can be considerable. The authors should either rephrase this statement to clarify that the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of their adaptation specifically for the PC algorithm (a relevant contribution in itself) while speculating that similar results might hold for other methods, or they should provide a detailed justification for why this generalization is expected to be true.

Second, the conclusion notes that applying these ideas to learn the structure of Bayesian networks is non-trivial due to challenges with maintaining sufficient statistics. While this issue is not central to the paper's main proposal, it highlights that extending this learning procedure to other models would require more than minor adjustments. Therefore, the paper would benefit from stating this limitation earlier. The authors could frame their contribution more directly as a method for SEMs, while noting that extensions to other model classes are possible but not necessarily straightforward. In essence, clarifying the method's scope from the outset would strengthen the paper.

Overall, this is a well-written paper on learning graphical structure for SEMs under the assumption of locally i.i.d. data. The proposed formulation is simple to understand and implement, and the experimental results are promising, even though they focus on a comparison using only the PC algorithm.
