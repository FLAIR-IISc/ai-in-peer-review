- Presents a systematic approach for structure learning of SEMs when data are only locally i.i.d. (time-dependent/coming from distinct models).
- Tackles an important, neglected problem: learning from time-dependent data rather than assuming global i.i.d.
- Techniques to extend learning to time-dependent data are simple, effective, and demonstrated experimentally with the PC method.
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the formulation is simple to understand and implement.
- Experimental results are promising but primarily focus on comparisons using the PC algorithm.
- Suggestion 1: The claim that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" is overly broad—rephrase to focus on PC or provide justification.
- Suggestion 2: Clarify scope regarding extensions to Bayesian networks (maintaining sufficient statistics may require more than minor modifications); disclose this limitation up front and state the paper’s focus on SEMs.
