- Reviewer disagrees with authors’ novelty claim: Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid, varying graphical structures.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing approach for online learning of changing graphical models; evaluated on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear correct, but Section 2’s related-work discussion is misleading about score-based algorithms’ assumptions.  
- Score-based BN algorithms do not inherently require a single generating model; exchangeability is a modeling assumption, not an algorithmic requirement.  
- The paper is unclear about the precise learning objective (change-point detection vs. recent-window structure inference vs. other).  
- It’s ambiguous whether the main contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- A paragraph (end of p.3) and the ρ_{r+1} equation are hard to follow and need clearer definitions and punctuation.  
- Figures 1–4 use very small fonts and lack legends for color meanings; readability should be improved.  
- The claim that improved tracking compensates for extra computation lacks justification and depends on domain-specific trade-offs.  
- Originality is uncertain: p-value construction might be novel, but the paper does not make the innovation clear.  
- Significance is limited as the method reads like a heuristic for an ill-defined objective; existing ML techniques may already address similar problems.  
- Recommendation: clarify the task, correctly situate the work in prior literature, and explicitly highlight the precise innovation to strengthen impact.
