- Problem: Tracks time-varying dependency structures in streaming data via online structure learning for linear SEMs (DAGs).
- Contribution: Proposes LoSST—maintains weighted sufficient statistics and uses batch PC relearning driven by pooled p-value fit statistic.
- Mechanism: Computes Mahalanobis distance → Hotelling’s T2 p-value → Lipták pooled statistic ρ to drive adaptive reweighting and probabilistic relearning triggers.
- Empirics: Evaluated on synthetic stationary vs. changing regimes (structural/parametric) across sizes/degrees and a U.S. price index case study.
- Main empirical claim: LoSST matches batch PC in stationary regimes and adapts substantially better to changes, avoiding overreaction to transients in real data.
- Relation to prior work: Fills gap for online directed-graph tracking; contrasts with batch, undirected, or changepoint-specific prior methods.
- Strength: Addresses timely, important problem with a practical, lightweight, and novel algorithmic synthesis that integrates off-the-shelf learners.
- Strength: Sensible empirical evaluation and appropriate literature positioning; clear discussion of convergence vs. diligence trade-offs.
- Weakness: Limited formal theoretical guarantees; needs clearer separation of proved results vs. conjectures and concrete theoretical directions.
- Weakness: Statistical heuristics stretch classical assumptions—effective sample size for Hotelling’s T2 under reweighting departs from i.i.d. theory.
- Weakness: Lipták pooling assumes independent tests; pooling temporally correlated, adaptively weighted p-values likely violates this.
- Weakness: Paper should explicitly acknowledge these departures and discuss their implications or calibrate via simulation.
- Reproducibility issues: Figures are too small/poorly labeled, captions terse, and some experimental descriptions (parameters T, γ, ν, schedules, metrics) are cryptic.
- Diagnostics needed: Brief explanation for worse edge-removal after parameter changes; add more analysis/plots to explain error patterns.
- Design choice concern: Structure and parameter updates are coupled via one trigger—consider or discuss decoupling/having separate thresholds.
- Presentation: Move proof sketches from footnotes to appendix/supplement and provide pseudocode to aid reproducibility.
- Arguments for acceptance: Practical, efficient method addressing an important gap with clear empirical benefits and extensibility.
- Arguments against acceptance: Weak theoretical underpinning, statistical components applied outside standard assumptions, and presentation/clarity issues impede evaluation.
- Quality assessment: Solid empirical work and thoughtful design but theory is light and some statistical assumptions are stretched.
- Clarity assessment: Generally well written but figures and experimental details require substantial revision.
- Originality: Novel combination of online sufficient-statistics tracking with pooled p-values to trigger DAG relearning.
- Significance: High practical potential for streaming, nonstationary settings and likely to spur follow-up research.
- Suggested fixes: State assumptions/limitations of T2 and Lipták pooling, run sensitivity analyses or calibrations, expand parameter-change analysis, discuss decoupled triggers, improve figure quality, and add appendix with proofs and pseudocode.
