- Disagrees with authors: Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handles non-iid and varying data-generating structure, so claiming novelty is misleading.  
- The proposed method is more flexible but not the first to address this problem.  
- Results appear correct.  
- Section 2 misstates related work: standard score-based Bayesian network learning algorithms do not assume a single generating model; they use factorized scoring.  
- Exchangeability/iid assumption pertains to the model, not to the algorithm.  
- The core learning task is ambiguous (inferring change points, final-data structure, or something else?).  
- It is unclear whether the contribution is a new model (assumptions/goals), a new algorithm (computation), or both.  
- Page 3 paragraph and the equation for ρ_{r+1} are confusing; displayed equations should end with periods.  
- Figures 1–4 need much larger fonts; Figures 1–2 also need color-key descriptions.  
- Section 6’s claim that better tracking offsets computational cost is domain-specific and unsupported.  
- Originality is limited; the paper lacks substantive conceptual innovation, though the p-value construction might contain an unclear insight.  
- Significance is limited: the approach is heuristic, targets an ambiguously defined problem, and is more applied development than advancing machine-learning frameworks.
