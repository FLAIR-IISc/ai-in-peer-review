- Introduces LoSST: an online, single-pass algorithm for learning graphical model structure under local stationarity in non-stationary data streams.
- Tracks sufficient statistics (weighted mean and covariance) with dynamic weighting to adapt to changes.
- Uses Mahalanobis distance to upweight poorly fitting new points and a pooled p-value to guard against outliers.
- Triggers structure relearning probabilistically, with probability rising when change is detected.
- Provides theoretical analysis of the diligence (quick change detection) versus convergence (stability) trade-off and how parameters control it.
- Empirical results: matches batch PC algorithm in stationary settings and outperforms it when graph structure or parameters change.
- Real-world case study on U.S. price index data shows practical utility in identifying meaningful shifts.
- Strength: addresses an important practical limitation (non-stationarity) with a novel, principled, single-pass solution.
- Strength: paper is clear, well-organized, and presents detailed algorithmic updates and experiments.
- Strength: technically sound design—Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-value balance sensitivity and robustness.
- Strength: complete work including algorithm, theory, synthetic experiments, and a real-world application.
- Weakness: strong claim that other standard structure learning algorithms would perform similarly is unsubstantiated.
- Suggestion: soften the generalization claim and frame benefits as demonstrated with PC, hypothesized for other methods.
- Weakness: scope ambiguity—method is tailored to linear SEMs using covariance; extension to discrete or other models is non-trivial.
- Suggestion: explicitly frame the contribution as an online method for SEMs while noting possible but nontrivial extensions.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with minor clarifications needed; recommend acceptance.
