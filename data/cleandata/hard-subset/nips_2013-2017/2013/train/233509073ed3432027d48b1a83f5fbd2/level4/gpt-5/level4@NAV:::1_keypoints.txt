- Reviewer disputes claim of novelty, citing Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) as prior non-iid work allowing changing generating structure.
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing method for online learning of time-varying graphical models, evaluated on simulated and real data.
- Results appear correct but related-work discussion is misleading about score-based Bayesian network algorithms and iid assumptions.
- Primary learning objective is unclear: change-point detection, recent-structure inference, or something else is not precisely specified.
- Unclear whether the contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.
- Several exposition issues: paragraph at end of page 3 is hard to follow; definition of rho_{r+1} is ambiguous; punctuation around displayed equations is inconsistent.
- Figures need improvements: font sizes in Figures 1–4 should be larger and Figures 1–2 need legends for colors.
- Questionable claim in Section 6 that improved tracking can offset extra computational cost—depends on domain-specific trade-offs.
- Originality is limited: approach seems novel but lacks a clearly new idea; p-value construction might be the main innovation.
- Significance is modest: likely to spur engineering/Bayesian developments rather than fundamental research, and the problem formulation seems imprecise given existing methods.
