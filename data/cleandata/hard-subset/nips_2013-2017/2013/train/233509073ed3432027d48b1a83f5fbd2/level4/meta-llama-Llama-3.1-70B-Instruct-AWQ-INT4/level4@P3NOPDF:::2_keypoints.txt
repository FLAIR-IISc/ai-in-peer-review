- Addresses tracking changes in dependency structure in data streams for simple SEMs.  
- Proposes LoSST: uses PC batch learning plus tracked streaming statistics to decide when to relearn structure.  
- Tracks weighted mean distance, correlation matrix, and effective sample size with a surprise-based reweighting scheme.  
- Computes Mahalanobis distances, estimates p-values via Hotelling’s T2 (with effective sample size), pools p-values with Liptak’s method to get significance ρ.  
- Uses a noisy-OR combination with previous step to trigger new structure learning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets with parameter/structure changes across varying graph sizes and degrees.  
- Applied to a real US price-index dataset (529 monthly points, six variables) and detected known economic trends while ignoring short-lived changes.  
- Strength: tackles an interesting, under-explored problem and provides a novel, practical, and efficient algorithm.  
- Strength: experiments are adequate, include relevant parameter sweeps, and the paper gives a good literature review and clear writing.  
- Major weakness: lacks theory or formal guarantees; needs discussion of potential guarantees under simplifying assumptions as future work.  
- Major weakness: heavy reliance on heuristics; choices (reweighting, effective sample size, relearn triggers) must be explicitly stated and justified.  
- Concern: some methods are used beyond their theoretical guarantees (e.g., Hotelling’s T2 with effective sample size) and this limitation should be highlighted.  
- Presentation issues: excessive footnote proofs (move to supplement), figures too small/misaligned/poorly labeled, and unclear experiment descriptions.  
- Technical gap: unsatisfactory explanation for LoSST’s poor performance on edge additions when only parameters change.  
- Suggestion/future work: consider decoupling parameter-update triggers from structure-update triggers; overall, a promising heuristic first attempt for further development.
