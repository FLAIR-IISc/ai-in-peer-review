This manuscript introduces a principled approach to enable structure learning in Structural Equation Models (SEMs) with data that is only locally independent and identically distributed – acknowledging potential shifts in the underlying data generating process over time. The paper is clearly written and logically structured, tackling a significant challenge in structure learning: adapting to time-dependent data, a limitation of many existing algorithms. The proposed techniques for extending learning to these non-stationary datasets are both straightforward and demonstrably effective, as evidenced by the experimental results using the PC algorithm.

To further strengthen the paper’s contribution, I offer two suggestions. First, the statement that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly…” appears overly broad. Performance can vary considerably between different structure learning methods. I recommend either clarifying that the experiments specifically focus on the PC algorithm, both in its standard (time-independent) form and with the proposed adaptation for time-dependent data – highlighting the relevance of this work – or, if maintaining the generalization, providing a detailed justification for why similar performance is expected across different methods. 

Second, the discussion in the conclusions regarding the challenges of applying these ideas to Bayesian network structure learning – specifically concerning the maintenance of sufficient statistics – is insightful. While not central to the paper’s primary focus, it underscores the potential complexities of uniformly applying this learning procedure across different model types. Therefore, I suggest explicitly acknowledging this limitation earlier in the paper, perhaps by framing the work more directly in terms of its goals for SEMs while still acknowledging the possibility (but not guaranteed simplicity) of extension to other models. This would sharpen the paper’s scope and focus.

Overall, this is a well-written and valuable contribution to the field of structure learning for SEMs under the realistic assumption of local i.i.d. data. The formulation is accessible and readily implementable, and the experimental results are encouraging, even within the context of a comparison limited to the PC algorithm. I have reviewed the authors’ response to previous feedback.
