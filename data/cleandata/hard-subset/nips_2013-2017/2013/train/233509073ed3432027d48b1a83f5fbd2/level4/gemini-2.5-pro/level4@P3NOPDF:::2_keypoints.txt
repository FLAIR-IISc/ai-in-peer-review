- Problem: Tracks changes in dependency structure in streaming data for SEMs and proposes an efficient solution.  
- Method: Proposes LoSST, which uses PC for batch structure learning and a trigger mechanism to decide when to relearn.  
- Core mechanism: Tracks weighted means/correlations, weights points by Mahalanobis surprise, computes p-values via Hotelling’s T², pools with Liptak’s method, and uses a noisy-OR trigger.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on synthetic scenarios (parameter drift and abrupt structural changes) and a real CPI dataset, showing detection of major shifts and robustness to short-term noise.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant, under-explored problem in adapting graphical models to non-stationary streams.  
- Strength: Novel, practical algorithm that avoids constant re-computation by combining existing statistical tools.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation is reasonably thorough across conditions and includes a real-world case study.  
- Strength: Solid literature review situating the contribution.  
- Weakness: No theoretical guarantees or analysis of algorithm behavior; paper should discuss this and future directions.  
- Weakness: Heuristic approach and potential misapplication of statistical tools—Hotelling’s T² may not be valid for re-weighted/non-i.i.d. streaming data.  
- Weakness: Liptak’s p-value combination assumes independence, which likely fails for sequential streaming p-values.  
- Suggested fix: Authors should explicitly state when methods are used outside their guaranteed domains to avoid overstating statistical rigor.  
- Presentation issues: Figures too small, missing labels/legends/captions, and some experimental descriptions are unclear, hurting reproducibility.  
- Formatting/flow: Unconventional use of footnotes for proofs; move proofs and implementation details to supplementary material.  
- Analysis gap: Explanation for LoSST’s worse performance on edge-addition under parameter changes is unconvincing and needs deeper analysis.  
- Future direction: Decouple triggers for parameter updates vs. full structural updates to avoid unnecessary costly structure searches.  
- Conclusion: Creative and practical first step with heuristic limitations and lack of formal grounding, but opens promising future research directions.
