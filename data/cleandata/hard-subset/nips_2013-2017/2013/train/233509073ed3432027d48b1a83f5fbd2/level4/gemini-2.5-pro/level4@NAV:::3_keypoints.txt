- Proposes a method to adapt structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) to locally i.i.d. datasets where the data-generating process can shift over time.  
- Tackles the important, often-overlooked problem of learning from time-dependent data.  
- Paper is clearly written, accessible, and easy to understand.  
- Proposed techniques are elegant and appear effective based on experiments using the PC algorithm.  
- Claim that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" is too strong; either moderate the statement, justify the generalization, or limit conclusions to PC.  
- Extending the approach to Bayesian networks may be non-trivial (issues with maintaining sufficient statistics); this limitation should be stated earlier and the scope framed around SEMs.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, simple to implement, and promising experimental results, though experiments are focused on the PC method.  
- Reviewer has read and considered the authors' feedback.
