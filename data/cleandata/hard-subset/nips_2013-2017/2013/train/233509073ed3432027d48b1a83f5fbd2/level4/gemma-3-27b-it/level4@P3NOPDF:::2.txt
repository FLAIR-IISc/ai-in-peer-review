## Review of LoSST: Lightweight Online Structure Tracking

This paper addresses the compelling and relatively unexplored problem of tracking changes in the dependency structure of variables within a data stream. The authors focus on developing an efficient algorithm, LoSST, for Structural Equation Modeling (SEM), specifically leveraging the PC algorithm for structure learning. LoSST intelligently monitors incoming data, using statistics like distance from weighted means, correlation matrices, and weighted sample size, to determine when to re-initiate structure learning, thereby avoiding computationally expensive full re-estimation with every data point. The algorithm employs a novel weighting scheme based on the “surprisingness” of new data points, quantified using Mahalanobis distance and Hotelling’s T² distribution combined with Liptak’s weighted p-value pooling to generate a significance statistic, ρ, that triggers structural updates. The authors demonstrate LoSST’s performance on synthetic datasets featuring both parameter and structural changes, as well as on a real-world US price index dataset.

**Strengths:**

1. **Important Problem:** The paper tackles a significant and under-explored area in statistical learning – adapting graphical models to dynamic environments. The authors rightly highlight the need for practical solutions and further theoretical development.
2. **Novel and Practical Algorithm:** LoSST presents a novel and computationally efficient approach to online structure tracking. The algorithm’s creativity lies in its clever integration of several existing methods, including weighted statistics and hypothesis testing.
3. **Adequate Experiments:** The experimental evaluation appears well-designed, systematically exploring the impact of various parameters and employing a relevant real-world dataset. The inclusion of the US price index data offers a tangible demonstration of the algorithm's capabilities.
4. **Strong Related Work:** The paper provides a comprehensive review of prior work in this domain, establishing a clear context for the proposed approach.
5. **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Theoretical Guarantees:** A significant limitation is the absence of theoretical analysis or guarantees regarding the algorithm’s performance. While acknowledging the trade-off between convergence and diligence, the authors should at least explicitly mention the development of theoretical bounds as a direction for future work. This is particularly crucial given the inherent challenges of online structure learning.
2. **Heuristic Nature and Justification:**  LoSST relies heavily on heuristics, which, while enabling a practical solution, require clearer justification. The key decisions relating to reweighting schemes, effective sample size determination, and the triggers for re-learning are not fully elucidated. Moreover, the authors frequently leverage statistical methods outside their established theoretical contexts. For instance, applying Hotelling’s T² distribution to weighted samples raises concerns, as it is typically derived for complete, unweighted data. Similarly, Liptak’s weighted p-value pooling is designed for independent tests, an assumption potentially violated in this context. The authors should explicitly acknowledge these deviations from standard assumptions and discuss their potential impact on the validity of the results. 
3. **Presentation and Clarity:** Several aspects of the presentation could be improved. The extensive use of footnotes for proofs is cumbersome and detracts from readability; supplementary materials would be more appropriate. Figures are excessively small, lack sufficient labeling, and have inadequate captions. The description of the experiments, particularly the initial paragraph on page 7, is unclear and requires further elaboration. The explanation for LoSST’s decreased performance during parameter changes involving edge additions is unconvincing.

**Other Comments:**

* Decoupling parameter updates from structural updates could prove beneficial. The current implementation seems to conflate these two processes, which might hinder performance when only parameter shifts occur.
* The authors should consider expanding the analysis of the real-world dataset. A more detailed comparison to established economic indicators or alternative time-series analysis techniques would strengthen the evaluation. 



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting and promising approach to the challenging problem of online structure tracking. While the reliance on heuristics and the lack of theoretical guarantees are notable weaknesses, the algorithm’s practical efficiency and initial empirical results suggest a worthwhile contribution. The paper's value lies in its exploration of a nascent research area and its provision of a tangible starting point for future investigation. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above, particularly concerning the justification of heuristics, clarification of experimental details, and improvement of presentation.
