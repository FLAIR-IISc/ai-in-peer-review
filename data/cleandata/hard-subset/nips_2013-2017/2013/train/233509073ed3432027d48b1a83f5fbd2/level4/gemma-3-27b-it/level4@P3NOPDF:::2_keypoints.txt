- Paper tackles online tracking of dependency-structure changes in data streams and introduces LoSST—an efficient SEM tracker using the PC algorithm and weighted monitoring to avoid full re-estimation.
- Strength: Addresses an important, under-explored problem in adapting graphical models to dynamic environments.
- Strength: Proposes a novel, practical algorithm combining weighted statistics, Mahalanobis “surprisingness,” Hotelling’s T², and Liptak p-value pooling for trigger-based updates.
- Strength: Experiments are adequate, covering synthetic parameter/structural changes and a real-world US price index dataset.
- Strength: Strong and comprehensive related work review.
- Strength: Paper is generally clearly written and easy to follow.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical guarantees or analysis; needs explicit discussion of convergence/error bounds as future work.
- Weakness: Heuristic choices (reweighting, effective sample size, triggers) are insufficiently justified; applying Hotelling’s T² to weighted samples and Liptak pooling under dependence may violate assumptions and should be acknowledged.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—proofs in footnotes (should be supplemental), figures too small/poorly labeled, unclear experimental descriptions, and an unconvincing explanation for LoSST’s poorer performance when edges are added.
- Other comment: Recommend decoupling parameter updates from structural updates to improve handling of pure parameter shifts.
- Other comment: Expand analysis of the real dataset with comparisons to established economic indicators or other time-series methods.
- Overall: Promising and practical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to justify heuristics, clarify experiments, and improve presentation.
