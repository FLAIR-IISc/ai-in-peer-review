This manuscript presents a systematic approach to enable structure learning for recursive Structural Equation Models (SEMs) in scenarios where data are only locally i.i.d., meaning the data may be generated from distinct underlying models over time. The paper is well-written and accessible, and addresses an important problem – learning from time-dependent data – which has received limited attention from many standard structure learning algorithms. The techniques proposed to extend learning to time-dependent data are conceptually simple yet effective, as demonstrated by the experimental results using the PC algorithm.

There are two suggestions that could clarify the paper’s contributions. First, in the experiments, the statement that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly" seems too strong. Differences among structure learning methods can be substantial. It would be beneficial to rephrase this assertion, clarifying that the experiments focus on the PC method’s performance in both time-independent and time-dependent contexts – a relevant and valuable comparison – or to substantiate the claim with a detailed explanation of why similar performance would be expected from other methods. Second, the discussion in the conclusions regarding the challenges of applying these ideas to Bayesian network structure learning – particularly the difficulties in maintaining sufficient statistics – is insightful. While this extension isn’t central to the paper’s primary focus, highlighting this complexity earlier would sharpen the paper’s scope. Presenting the work more directly as a contribution to SEMs, while acknowledging the potential (but not necessarily straightforward) extension to other models, would be advantageous.

In short, this is a well-written paper addressing graph structure learning for SEMs under the assumption of local i.i.d. data. The formulation is straightforward to understand and implement, and the experimental results are promising, though currently focused on comparisons using the PC method. I acknowledge having read the feedback from the authors.
