- Reviewer disputes claim of novelty: Siracusa & Fisher (2009) already handle non-iid generating structures, so calling this the first to address the problem is misleading.  
- Paper proposes an online hypothesis-testing framework for learning time-varying graphical models and evaluates it on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear correct, but Section 2 mischaracterizes score-based Bayesian network learning—exchangeability/iid is a model assumption, not an algorithm requirement.  
- The learning task is poorly defined: unclear if goal is change-point detection, final-structure recovery, or something else.  
- Unclear whether contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both, obscuring the work’s novelty.  
- Technical presentation issues: the definition/derivation of ρ_{r+1} is confusing and displayed equations lack proper punctuation.  
- Figure problems: fonts too small and Figures 1–2 lack color legends, impairing readability.  
- Claim that "improved tracking compensates for computational cost" is unjustified; trade-offs are domain-dependent and not demonstrated.  
- Originality is limited: p-value construction may have technical merit, but the overall approach resembles existing hypothesis-testing frameworks.  
- Significance is limited: the method is heuristic, the task poorly specified, and standard tools (online Bayesian methods, changepoint detection) could address the problem with stronger modeling.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound but insufficient theoretical/methodological innovation for NIPS; leans toward engineering contribution rather than fundamental research.  
- Major concerns summarized: mischaracterized prior work, unclear problem statement, ambiguity of contribution type, poorly defined technical details and figures, overstated novelty, and limited scientific significance.
