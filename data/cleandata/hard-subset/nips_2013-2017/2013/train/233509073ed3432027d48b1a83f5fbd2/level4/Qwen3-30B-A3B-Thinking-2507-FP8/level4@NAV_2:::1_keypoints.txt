- Disagrees with the authors’ claim of novelty: Siracusa and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-i.i.d. data and varying generating structures, so claiming to be first is misleading.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based online method for learning graphical models from time series with frequently changing generating structures.  
- Experimental results are technically sound and well-supported by synthetic and real-world evaluations.  
- Related work section contains inaccuracies: score-based Bayesian network algorithms do not inherently assume a single generating model; their scores factorize locally and i.i.d. assumptions belong to model specification, not the algorithm.  
- Core task is ambiguous: unclear whether goal is change-point detection, inferring the current generating structure, or something else.  
- Unclear whether the contribution is a new model formulation, a novel algorithm, or both.  
- Specific exposition issues: paragraph at end of page 3 needs clarification about whether the equation for ρ_{r+1} is a definition or uses a prior definition.  
- Formatting and presentation problems: missing periods after displayed equations, small figure fonts (Figures 1–4) that should be larger, and missing color legends for Figures 1 and 2.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking can offset higher computational costs is insufficiently justified and appears domain-specific.  
- Originality: method is novel in online structure learning context but lacks deep conceptual innovation.  
- Technical novelty may exist in the pooled p-value construction, but overall approach is a straightforward application of existing statistical techniques.  
- Significance: work could spur development of more sophisticated (e.g., Bayesian) online structure-learning methods but is more developmental/heuristic and of limited conceptual impact because existing ML frameworks could potentially address the problem.
