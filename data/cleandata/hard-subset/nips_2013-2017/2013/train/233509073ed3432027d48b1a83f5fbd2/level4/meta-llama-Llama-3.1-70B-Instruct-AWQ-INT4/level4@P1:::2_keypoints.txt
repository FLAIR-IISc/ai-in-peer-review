- Tackles tracking changes in dependency structure over data streams; focuses on efficient solutions for simple SEMs.  
- Proposes LoSST: uses PC for structure learning and tracks per-variable weighted means, correlation matrix, and effective sample size to decide when to relearn.  
- Weighs new points by "surprise" using Mahalanobis distances, estimates p-values via Hotelling's T2, and combines them with Liptak's weighted pooling into a significance statistic ρ.  
- Uses a noisy-OR combination with previous step to trigger structure relearning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets with parameter/structure changes across varying variable counts and degrees.  
- Tested on a real US price-index dataset (529 monthly points, six variables) and detected known economic changes while ignoring short-lived fluctuations.  
- Novelty: LoSST is a creative, practical, and computationally/memory-efficient algorithm combining several methods.  
- Experiments: adequate range, parameter sensitivity explored, and inclusion of an interesting real-world dataset.  
- Writing: overall clear and well-organized with a good literature review.  
- Major weakness: paper lacks theoretical guarantees (convergence/detection guarantees) and should at least mention this as future work.  
- Heavy reliance on heuristics; key decisions about reweighting, effective sample size, and relearning criteria are not clearly isolated or justified.  
- Statistical-method concerns: using Hotelling's T2 with reweighted/partial counts and Liptak pooling may violate the original assumptions of those methods; authors should clearly state such caveats.  
- Presentation issues: excessive use of footnotes for proofs—move proofs to supplementary material.  
- Figures are too small, have poor spacing/labels/legends, and captions lack detail.  
- Experimental description is unclear in places (e.g., paragraph on p.7 is cryptic).  
- Explanation for LoSST performing worse on edge additions under parameter-only changes is insufficient.  
- Suggestion: decouple triggers for parameter updates from structure updates.  
- Overall quality: technically sound and well-presented, but weakened by lack of theory and heavy heuristics.  
- Clarity: generally well-written, but hampered by poor figures and unclear experimental descriptions.  
- Originality: method is novel and creative relative to prior work.  
- Significance: important and relevant; shows practical detection of structural changes not found by batch PC.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel practical solution, efficient algorithm with decent evaluation, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing theoretical guarantees, reliance on heuristics, and poor figure presentation.
