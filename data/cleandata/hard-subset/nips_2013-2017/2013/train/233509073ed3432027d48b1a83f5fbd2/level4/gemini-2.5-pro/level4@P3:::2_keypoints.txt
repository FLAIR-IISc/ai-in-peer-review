- Summary: Proposes LoSST, an online method that tracks weighted sufficient statistics, uses Mahalanobis "surprise" and Liptak-pooled p-values to weight data and trigger PC-based structure relearning for non-stationary graphical models.

- Strength — Significance & Originality: Tackles under-explored non-stationary structure learning and combines Mahalanobis distance, Hotelling's T², and Liptak pooling into a novel online framework.

- Strength — Practicality & Efficiency: Updates sufficient statistics online and only triggers expensive structure learning when needed, making it suitable for real-time and large-scale streaming data.

- Strength — Experimental Evaluation: Simulations show comparable performance to batch learners in stationary settings and superior performance under structural/parametric changes; includes a brief real-world case study.

- Strength — Clarity & Organization: Well-written, well-motivated, and includes a useful discussion of the "convergence vs diligence" trade-off.

- Weakness — Heuristic Methods & Theoretical Guarantees: The use of Hotelling's T² with re-weighted (non-i.i.d.) data breaks the distributional assumptions, making the p-value validity unclear.

- Weakness — P-value Pooling Assumptions: Liptak's method assumes independent tests, but sequential Mahalanobis p-values are dependent, so pooling guarantees do not hold.

- Weakness — Need for Explicit Caveats: Authors should explicitly acknowledge these heuristic uses and lack of statistical guarantees and frame them as future-theory directions.

- Weakness — Presentation Issues: Figures are too small with wasted space, missing axis labels/captions, and dense experiment descriptions that need clarification.

- Weakness — Specific Empirical Explanation Lacking: The explanation for LoSST's worse edge-removal behavior after parameter-only changes is not convincing and needs deeper analysis.

- Detailed comment — Reposition Proofs: Unconventional footnote proof sketches and implementation details should be moved to supplementary material to aid readability and reproducibility.

- Detailed comment — Decouple Updates Suggestion: Consider decoupling continuous parameter-statistic updates from less frequent structural relearning to reduce expense and improve responsiveness.

- Overall: Interesting and practical contribution with promising results, but requires clearer presentation and explicit treatment of the heuristic/statistical limitations.
