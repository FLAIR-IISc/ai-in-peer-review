- Addresses detecting changes in dependency structure in streaming data and proposes an efficient method for simple SEMs.
- Introduces LoSST, a streaming algorithm that triggers batch structure learning (PC) using maintained streaming statistics.
- Tracked statistics include weighted means, correlation matrix, weighted sample size, and Mahalanobis distances for new samples.
- New samples are weighted by “surprise”; p-values from MDs (via Hotelling’s T2) are combined with Liptak’s method to produce a significance rho.
- A noisy-OR combination with previous rho values decides when to trigger structure relearning.
- Experiments compare LoSST to batch PC on synthetic parameter/structure-change scenarios and a real US price-index dataset.
- Pros: tackles an underexplored, interesting problem with a novel, practical, and efficient algorithm.
- Pros: experiments are adequate (parameter exploration + real dataset) and paper gives a solid prior-work review and clear writing.
- Main con: lacks theory or formal guarantees under stated assumptions; should note this and propose theory as future work.
- Main con: LoSST is heavily heuristic and decision points (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning criteria) need clearer justification.
- Concern: statistical tools (Hotelling’s T2, Liptak pooling) are applied outside their standard assumptions without explicit caveats about validity.
- Other issues: proofs in footnotes should be moved to supplementary material; figures are small/unclear and captions/legends insufficient.
- Other issues: experimental description is unclear; explanation for LoSST’s underperformance on edge additions is unsatisfactory; consider decoupling parameter and structure update triggers.
