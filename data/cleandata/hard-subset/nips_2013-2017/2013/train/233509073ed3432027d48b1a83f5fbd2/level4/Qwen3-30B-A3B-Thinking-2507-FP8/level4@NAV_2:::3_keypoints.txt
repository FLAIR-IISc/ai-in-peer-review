- Introduces LoSST, a heuristic for online structure learning under local stationarity where structure changes but remains stable in short intervals.  
- Fills a gap versus standard methods (PC) that assume global stationarity and prior online approaches that lack generality or require batch processing.  
- Builds on Talih & Hengartner (NIPS 2005) and recent online learning advances, extending them to track structural and parametric changes in real time without retrospective data.  
- Novel design uses weighted sufficient statistics and pooled p-values to dynamically adjust learning weights, bridging batch and online learning.  
- Technically sound derivation of online sufficient statistics (Eqs. 1–3) with a clear convergence versus diligence trade-off analysis.  
- Simulations show LoSST outperforms batch PC during structural changes and incurs minimal cost in stable regimes (e.g., ~250 datapoints to re-stabilize).  
- Exposition is clear with well-structured pseudocode and intuitive change signals (Mahalanobis distance).  
- Real-world validation on US price index volatility demonstrates practical applicability.  
- Weakness: Overgeneralized claim that any standard structure learning algorithm would perform like PC; score-based methods may behave differently and need clarification.  
- Weakness: Scope ambiguity — core contribution is specific to SEMs, and Bayesian network extensions should be framed as future work up front.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept; significant, well-executed contribution with minor scope clarifications suggested.
