This manuscript presents a systematic approach to enabling structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) with datasets that are only locally independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.). This addresses a crucial problem – learning from time-dependent data – which has been overlooked by many commonly used structure learning algorithms, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow. The techniques proposed to extend learning to time-dependent data are simple yet effective, as demonstrated by experiments using the PC algorithm.

I have two suggestions that could clarify the paper’s contributions. First, the experiments state that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly…”. I believe this assertion is too strong. Differences among structure learning methods can be substantial. It would be better to rephrase this to clarify that the experiments focus on the PC algorithm for time-independent data and its adaptation for time-dependent data – which is a relevant and valuable contribution – and then speculate that other methods might behave similarly. Alternatively, if the assertion is maintained, a detailed explanation of *why* this would be the case is needed.

Second, the conclusions discuss the challenges of applying the same ideas to learning the structure of Bayesian networks, specifically the difficulty in maintaining sufficient statistics. While this issue isn’t central to the paper’s proposal, it highlights that extending the learning procedure to Bayesian networks and other models might require significant modifications. Therefore, I think the paper would benefit from acknowledging this limitation earlier, perhaps by framing the work more directly in terms of its goal for SEMs, while still noting the potential (though not necessarily straightforward) for extension to other models. In short, the paper could be more focused in its scope. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper concerning graph structure learning for SEMs under the assumption of local i.i.d. data. The formulation is simple to understand and implement, and the experimental results are promising, even though they are currently limited to a comparison using the PC algorithm. I have read the feedback from the authors and acknowledge it.
