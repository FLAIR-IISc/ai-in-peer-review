- Related work is mischaracterized: score-based algorithms fundamentally assume factorization of the score, not necessarily a single generating model.  
- The precise learning task is ambiguous—unclear if goal is changepoint detection, learning the most recent segment’s structure, or something else; a formal problem statement is needed.  
- It is unclear whether the contribution is a new statistical model for time-varying structures or a new computational algorithm for an existing model; this distinction should be clarified.  
- The paragraph introducing ρ_{r+1} (end of page 3) is hard to parse and unclear whether it is a definition or a derived result.  
- Minor presentation issues: add periods after displayed equations, increase font size in Figures 1–4, and add legends explaining colored lines in Figures 1–2.  
- The statement that improved tracking would offset extra computational/memory cost should be framed as an application-dependent trade-off rather than a general claim.  
- Originality: the specific combination of weighted sufficient statistics, p-value pooling, and probabilistic relearning appears novel, with the main innovation in heuristic engineering.  
- The pooled p-value construction might be a technical novelty, but its description is currently insufficient to fully assess.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem in non-stationary environments and provides a pragmatic, empirically effective solution.  
- Limitation: as a heuristic, the method lacks formal guarantees; it would be valuable as a benchmark and to motivate more formal (e.g., Bayesian) approaches.
