

This manuscript presents a systematic approach to enabling structure learning for structural equation models (SEMs) with datasets that are only locally i.i.d., meaning the underlying generating models may change over time. The paper is well-written, clear, and addresses an important problem—learning from time-dependent data—that has been overlooked by many standard structure learning algorithms. The proposed techniques for extending learning to time-dependent data are simple yet effective, as demonstrated by experiments using the PC method.

Two suggestions would strengthen the paper's clarity and contribution:  
First, the claim that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" is overly strong. The performance differences among structure learning methods can be substantial. To clarify, the paper should either rephrase this to specify that the experiments focus on the PC method (both for time-independent and time-dependent data, with the adaptation being particularly relevant), and note that the similarity is speculative for other methods; or retain the assertion but provide a detailed justification for why it would hold generally.  

Second, the conclusion notes that extending the approach to Bayesian networks may not be straightforward due to challenges in maintaining sufficient statistics. While this is not central to the paper's focus on SEMs, acknowledging this limitation upfront would sharpen the paper's scope. The manuscript would benefit from explicitly framing its contribution as being specifically for SEMs, while noting that extensions to other models (like Bayesian networks) may require additional work beyond the current framework. This would prevent overgeneralization and clarify the paper's precise contribution.  

Overall, this is a well-written paper presenting a simple, implementable formulation for SEM structure learning under locally i.i.d. data. The experimental results are promising, though focused primarily on the PC method. The paper would be significantly improved by addressing the scope clarification and the overgeneralization in the performance claim. I acknowledge having read the authors' feedback.
