- Extends structure learning for SEMs to locally i.i.d. (locally stationary) data where the underlying model can change over time.  
- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic algorithm that tracks graphical model structure and parameter changes in real time.  
- Assumes only local stationarity: data are i.i.d. at each time step but the generative model may vary over time.  
- Shows LoSST can be convergent, diligent, or balance both depending on parameter settings, with thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Simulations: LoSST matches batch learning under global stationarity and outperforms it when structure is only locally stationary.  
- Demonstrates LoSST’s ability to detect and adapt to structural changes in simulations.  
- Real-data application on seasonally adjusted U.S. price index data shows LoSST detects changes in volatility of relationships.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a concise explanation of the algorithm and its properties.  
- Recommendation to soften or justify the claim that “other standard algorithms would perform similarly,” and clarify experiments focus (PC method and its adaptation).  
- Recommendation to disclose early that extending LoSST to Bayesian networks is nontrivial and frame the paper primarily as an SEM contribution.  
- Strengths: novel approach, clear exposition, thorough analysis, promising experiments, and practical real-data demonstration.  
- Weaknesses: potentially overstrong comparative claim about other algorithms and insufficient upfront discussion of limitations/extensions (Bayesian networks).  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, systematic method with solid experimental support and clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: needs clearer justification/comparison to other methods and more explicit discussion of extension challenges.
