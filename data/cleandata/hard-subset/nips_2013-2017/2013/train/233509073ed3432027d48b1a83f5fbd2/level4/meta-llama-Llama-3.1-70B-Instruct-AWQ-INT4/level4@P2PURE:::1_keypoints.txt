- Disagrees with authors' novelty claim: Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid generating structures within a limited class of graphical models, so claiming to be first is misleading.  
- Proposes a hypothesis-testing-based online method for learning graphical models from time series where the generating model may change frequently; evaluated on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear correct, but Section 2's related-work discussion is misleading about score-based Bayesian-network algorithms.  
- Notes that score-based methods typically assume a factorized scoring function, not a single generating model—distinguish model vs algorithm assumptions about exchangeability/iid.  
- Presentation is mostly clear but the actual learning task is ambiguous: is it change-point detection, recent-structure inference, a new model, a new algorithm, or both?  
- The definition and presentation of rho_{r+1} (end of p.3) are confusing; recommend clearer definitions and punctuation for equations.  
- Figures need larger fonts and color legends; Section 6’s claim that better tracking can offset extra computation is vague and domain-dependent.  
- Originality is limited: work lacks major novel ideas, though the p-value construction might be interesting but is hard to follow.  
- Significance is modest: could motivate future Bayesian methods but currently reads as a heuristic for an unclearly defined problem that existing ML tools might already handle.
