- Disagreement with authors: Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) did not assume iid data and allowed the generating structure to vary across a small set of graphical models, so claiming novelty is misleading.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing approach for online learning of graphical models from time series where the generating graph may change frequently.  
- Experimental results appear correct, but Section 2’s related-work discussion is misleading about existing score-based Bayesian network algorithms.  
- Clarification: score-based learners typically assume factorized local scores, not a single generating model; iid/exchangeability is an assumption about the model, not the algorithm.  
- The actual learning task is unclear (detect change points, infer most recent structure, or something else) and the paper does not clearly state whether it introduces a new model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- Specific clarity issues: the paragraph and equation for ρ_{r+1} on page 3 are hard to follow and may be poorly defined; use punctuation after displayed equations for clarity.  
- Presentation problems: figures 1–4 have too-small font sizes and lack color legends, making them hard to interpret.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking could offset extra computational cost is confusing and too dependent on domain-specific trade-offs.  
- Originality: the work seems original but lacks strong novel ideas; the p-value construction may be interesting but is not clearly explained.  
- Significance: could inspire development of better (Bayesian) methods, but currently reads as heuristic, incremental, and addresses an ambiguously defined problem that existing methods might already handle.
