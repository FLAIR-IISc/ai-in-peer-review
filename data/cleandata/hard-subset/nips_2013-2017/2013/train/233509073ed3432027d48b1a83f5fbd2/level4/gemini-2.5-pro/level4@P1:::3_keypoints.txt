- Introduces LoSST: an algorithm for learning SEM structure from locally i.i.d., non-stationary data by tracking sufficient statistics with dynamic weighting.  
- Tackles the important, often-neglected problem of structure learning in changing environments.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Proposed tracking techniques are simple and empirically effective.  
- Experiments compare an online-adapted PC algorithm against its standard batch counterpart, showing promising results.  
- The claim that other standard structure-learning methods would behave similarly is too strong and should be rephrased or justified.  
- Experimental validation should emphasize results for the PC algorithm specifically or provide stronger evidence for generalization to other methods.  
- Extension to other model classes (e.g., discrete Bayesian networks) is non-trivial due to difficulties in tracking required sufficient statistics.  
- Recommend stating upfront that the method is tailored to SEMs and that extensions to other models may be challenging.  
- Overall: a valuable, straightforward-to-implement contribution with promising experimental support.
