- Problem importance: Tackles the interesting, under-explored task of tracking changing dependency structures in data streams (SEMs).  
- Method: Introduces LoSST, a novel, practical, and efficient algorithm leveraging batch PC learning plus lightweight stream statistics.  
- Statistics tracked: LoSST monitors weighted means, correlation matrix, and weighted sample size with a surprise-based weighting scheme.  
- Change detection: Uses Mahalanobis distances, p-value estimation, Liptak’s weighted pooling, and a noisy-OR with previous step to trigger structure relearning.  
- Experiments: Synthetic tests vary variables and degrees; real-world analysis on US price-index data shows plausible detections and ignores short-lived changes.  
- Writing and background: Paper is well-written and provides a good review of prior work.  
- Main weakness: Lacks theoretical guarantees or analysis; convergence/diligence trade-offs are unaddressed and should be discussed or left as future work.  
- Heuristic reliance: LoSST depends heavily on heuristics (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning triggers) that need clearer justification and documentation.  
- Misuse of statistical tools: Using Hotelling’s T2 with reweighted samples and Liptak’s pooling outside their standard assumptions likely voids guarantees and should be explicitly noted.  
- Presentation issues: Figures are too small/misaligned, captions and legends lack detail, and some experimental descriptions are unclear.  
- Explanatory gaps: The explanation for LoSST’s poorer performance on edge addition under parameter changes is unsatisfactory.  
- Design suggestion: Authors should consider decoupling parameter-update triggers from structure-update triggers for clarity and potentially better performance.
