- Paper: Tackles streaming tracking of evolving SEM structures with LoSST, using weighted means/correlations, Mahalanobis distances, Liptak pooling, and noisy-OR triggers; evaluated on synthetic data and U.S. price index time series.

- Novel problem focus: addresses an important, underdeveloped area with high potential.

- Practical novelty: creatively integrates weighted statistics and p-value pooling into an efficient streaming algorithm.

- Adequate experiments: systematic synthetic tests and a real-world economic case study.

- Comprehensive literature review: prior streaming structure-learning work is well-summarized and contextualized.

- Clear writing and accessible organization.

- Lacks theoretical guarantees: no convergence or detection guarantees; should include simplified assurances or explicitly mark as future work.

- Heuristic dependencies without caveats: uses Hotelling’s T² and Liptak pooling outside their standard assumptions (reweighted/streaming data, dependent statistics) and needs explicit caveats.

- Footnotes for proofs are inappropriate; move technical derivations to supplementary material.

- Figures and presentation need fixing: poor scaling, missing labels/legends, excessive spacing, and unclear experimental descriptions.

- Edge-addition performance unexplained: worse performance when only parameters change; suggest decoupling parameter updates from structure updates.

- Real-world analysis promising but needs clearer linkage between detected changes and known economic events.

- Overall: valuable and pragmatic contribution but limited by theoretical grounding and presentation issues.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
