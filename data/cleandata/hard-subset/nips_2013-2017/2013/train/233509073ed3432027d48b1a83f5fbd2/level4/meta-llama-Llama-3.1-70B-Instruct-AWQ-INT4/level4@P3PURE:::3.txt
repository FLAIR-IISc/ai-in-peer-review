This manuscript presents a systematic approach to enabling structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) to accommodate data sets that are only locally independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.), where the data may be generated from distinct underlying models over time. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, addressing an important problem that has been neglected by many commonly used algorithms for structure learning, namely learning from time-dependent data. The techniques proposed to extend learning to time-dependent data are simple yet effective, as demonstrated by the experiments with the PC method.

There are two suggestions that could clarify the paper's contributions. Firstly, in the experiments, the statement "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly, ..." seems overly broad. The differences among methods for structure learning can be considerable. To improve clarity, it would be better to rephrase this statement to emphasize that the experiments specifically focus on the PC method for time-independent and time-dependent data, and then speculate that similar results might be obtained with other methods; or to retain the assertion and provide a detailed explanation for why this would be the case.

Secondly, the conclusion discusses the potential challenges of applying the proposed ideas to learning the structure of Bayesian networks, particularly with regards to maintaining sufficient statistics. Although this issue is not central to the proposal in this paper, it highlights that extending the same learning procedure to Bayesian networks and other models may require more than minor modifications. Therefore, I believe the paper would benefit from disclosing this fact from the outset, presenting the work in more direct terms about its goal of SEM models, while still acknowledging the possibility of extending the approach to other models, albeit with potential complications. In short, the paper could be more precise about its scope.

Overall, this is a well-written paper about learning the graph structure for SEMs under the assumption that data are only locally i.i.d. The formulation is simple to understand and implement, and the experimental results look promising, even if focused primarily on a comparison using the PC method.
