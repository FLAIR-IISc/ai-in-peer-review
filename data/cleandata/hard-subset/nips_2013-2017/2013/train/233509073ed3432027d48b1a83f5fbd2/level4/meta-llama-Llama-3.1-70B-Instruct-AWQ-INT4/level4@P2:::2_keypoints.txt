- Problem/paper: Tracks changing dependency structure in data streams for simpler SEMs; proposes LoSST combining batch PC learning with stream statistics to trigger relearning.  
- LoSST mechanics: Tracks weighted mean distance, correlation matrix, and weighted sample size; weights based on Mahalanobis "surprisingness", p-values pooled with Liptak, and a noisy-OR with previous step to decide relearn.  
- Relation to prior work: Good literature review; points out standard structure-learning assumes fixed structure and discusses limits of online methods (e.g., TDL) and changepoint detection.  
- Strength: Tackles an interesting, under-explored problem with a novel and practical approach.  
- Strength: Creative integration of multiple methods, showing strong methodological understanding.  
- Strength: Experiments are adequate, explore parameter effects, and include a real-world dataset.  
- Strength: Comprehensive review of related work.  
- Strength: Well-written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: No theoretical guarantees or analyses under simplifying conditions to clarify algorithm behavior.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on heuristics; key decisions (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning triggers) need clearer justification.  
- Weakness: Uses methods beyond original guarantees (Hotelling's T2 for Mahalanobis p-values, Liptak pooling) without sufficient justification.  
- Other: Excessive footnote use for proofs; better moved to supplementary material.  
- Other: Figures are too small, have unnecessary spacing, and lack clear labels/captions/legends.  
- Other: Experimental descriptions and result explanations need to be clearer and more detailed.  
- Other: Explanation for worse performance on edge additions when only parameters change is unsatisfactory.  
- Other/future: Decoupling parameter updates from structure updates is a desirable research direction.  
- For acceptance: Novel practical solution, good empirical performance, and clear writing support acceptance.  
- Against acceptance: Lack of theory, heuristic-heavy design, and questionable use of methods outside their guarantees argue against acceptance.  
- Overall: Promising starting point that would be stronger with added theoretical justification, clearer methodological justification, and improved clarity of experiments/figures.
