- Addresses the important and underexplored problem of tracking changing dependency structures in streaming data.  
- Proposes LoSST, a novel, practical algorithm combining PC algorithm for structure learning with streaming statistics tracking.  
- Tracks weighted means, covariance, and effective sample size from the stream to decide when to relearn structure.  
- Applies a surprise-based weighting scheme per data point and computes Mahalanobis distance-based p-values.  
- Pools p-values using Liptak’s weighted method and approximates p-values via Hotelling’s T2 to form an overall significance ρ.  
- Uses a noisy-OR decision gate based on ρ to trigger new structure learning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data with induced parameter and structure changes across varying graph sizes and degrees.  
- Demonstrated on real-world US price index data (1967–2011) and detected known economic regime shifts.  
- Strength: tackles a timely, impactful problem with potential for theoretical and practical advances.  
- Strength: LoSST creatively integrates existing methods into a workable online structure-learning approach.  
- Strength: Experimental setup is reasonable and includes a relevant real dataset.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical guarantees; convergence/robustness analysis under simplifying assumptions is missing.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on heuristics with insufficient justification for weighting, sample-size calc, and trigger decisions.  
- Weakness: Uses statistical approximations (Hotelling’s T2, pooled p-values) outside established assumptions without clear caveats.  
- Weakness: Explanation for edge-removal behavior when only parameters change is unconvincing.  
- Other: Move proofs/technical details from footnotes to supplementary materials and clarify missing details.  
- Other: Figures are too small and experimental descriptions (e.g., p.7) need clarification and better labeling.  
- Suggestion: Consider decoupling parameter updates from structure updates as a valuable extension.  
- Pro-accept: Practical novelty, promising empirical results, clear writing, and an important problem justify acceptance.  
- Con-accept: Lack of theory, heuristic sensitivity, and poor presentation of figures/experiments argue for rejection or major revision.  
- Overall: Interesting, potentially valuable contribution warranting conditional acceptance if theoretical discussion, figure quality, and experimental clarity are improved.
