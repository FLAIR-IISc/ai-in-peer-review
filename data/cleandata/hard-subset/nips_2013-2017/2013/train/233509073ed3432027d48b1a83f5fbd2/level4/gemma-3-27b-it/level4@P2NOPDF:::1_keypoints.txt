- Results appear technically sound, but the authors mischaracterize prior work and overclaim novelty.  
- It is inaccurate to state this is the first method for changing graphical structures; Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) allow non-i.i.d. structure changes and should be acknowledged.  
- The core learning task is ambiguous: is the aim change-point detection, inferring the most recent model, or something else?  
- It is unclear whether the paper proposes a new probabilistic model (with assumptions) or primarily a new algorithmic procedure.  
- The paragraph at the end of page 3 and the definition/equation for ρ_{r+1} are hard to parse and need clearer exposition (definition vs. derived relation).  
- Stylistic/formatting issues: missing periods after displayed equations, small font sizes in Figures 1–4, and absent color labels in Figures 1–2 hinder readability.  
- The claim in Section 6 that improved tracking can justify greater computational cost needs concrete discussion of domain-specific trade-offs.  
- The combination of techniques is moderately original but not a major conceptual advance; the contribution feels incremental.  
- The p-value construction may have novel elements but is difficult to follow and requires clearer explanation.  
- The work could spur development of Bayesian/dynamic graphical-model methods, but currently reads as a pragmatic/heuristic solution that existing ML techniques might address.
