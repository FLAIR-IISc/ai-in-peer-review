- Addresses the important and understudied problem of tracking evolving variable dependencies in data streams.  
- Proposes LoSST, a computationally and memory-efficient algorithm for SEM that triggers batch re-learning (PC algorithm) based on stream monitoring.  
- Monitoring statistics include distances from weighted means, the correlation matrix, and a weighted effective sample size.  
- Introduces a weighting scheme using Mahalanobis distance and Hotelling’s T2 p-values, combined via Liptak’s pooling to produce a significance statistic ρ.  
- Uses the ρ statistic with prior assessments to decide when to initiate structural re-learning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets (parameter and structural changes, varied variables and degrees) and a real US price index dataset (529 monthly observations, six variables).  
- Empirically detects known economic shifts while filtering transient fluctuations.  
- Strength: tackles a significant problem and highlights the need for practical and theoretical advances.  
- Strength: presents a novel, pragmatic integration of diverse statistical techniques for dynamic structure learning.  
- Strength: reasonable experimental setup and a relevant real-world application.  
- Strength: provides a comprehensive related-work overview and clear writing.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis or formal guarantees; potential trade-offs (convergence vs. diligence) are not formally explored.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on heuristic choices (weighting, effective sample size, re-learning triggers) with insufficient justification and potential misuse of statistical methods (weighted Hotelling’s T2, independence assumptions for Liptak).  
- Other: proofs in footnotes are unconventional—move to supplementary material; figures are too small and unclear and need better labels/captions.  
- Other: experimental descriptions need clarification; inadequate explanation for reduced performance on edge additions when only parameters change.  
- Other: recommend decoupling parameter updates from structural updates to potentially improve performance.  
- Conclusion: LoSST is a practical, largely heuristic first step toward dynamic dependency tracking with promising empirical results but needs stronger theoretical grounding and clearer methodological justification.
