- Proposes LoSST, a real-time algorithm for tracking rapidly time-varying graphical structures under local stationarity.
- Authors claim novelty on non-iid data handling, but prior work (Siracusa III & Fisher, AISTATS 2009) also addresses varying generating structures.
- Results appear correct, though the related-work description contains inaccuracies about score-based Bayesian network assumptions.
- Notes that exchangeability/iid is a model property, not a requirement of typical score-based structure-learning algorithms.
- Presentation is mostly clear but the learning task (change points vs. current-structure inference) and whether a new model or algorithm is proposed are ambiguous.
- Some paragraphs are hard to follow and Figures 1–4 have small fonts and unlabeled color schemes.
- The approach seems original but may lack a clear novel innovation; p-value construction might be the only potentially unique element.
- Views the contribution as more developmental (heuristic) than a clear research advance that existing ML methods couldn’t address.
- For acceptance: introduces a novel algorithm, targets frequent model changes, and provides thorough empirical evaluation on simulated and real data.
- Against acceptance: misleading related-work discussion, unclear task/goals, limited novelty, and marginal significance.
- Recommend clarifying related work, especially score-based Bayesian network literature and non-iid precedents.
- Recommend explicitly defining the learning objective and whether the paper presents a new model, algorithm, or both.
- Suggest adding technical details on p-value construction to support claims of novelty.
- Advise improving readability: increase figure font sizes, add color legends/descriptions, and clean up unclear paragraphs.
