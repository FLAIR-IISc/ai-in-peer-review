Response to the Author's Response:

I disagree with point (3): The work of Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) does not assume that data is an ID, and it allows for "generating structures" (even if it's a small set of graphical models). Although the current proposed method is, in some sense, more flexible, I would be wrong to claim that the proposed method is the first method to solve the problem.

*   The paper presents a heuristic-based method for learning graphical models from time series data in an online manner. The proposed method is specifically designed for situations where "data generating" graphical models may change relatively frequently. The performance of the method using both simulated and real data has been demonstrated.

**Quality**

The results seem correct. However, I find the description of related work (in section 2) somewhat misleading: general score-based algorithms for learning Bayesian networks do not assume that data comes from a single generating model – rather, they assume that the scoring function factors into local terms. It is true that the models typically studied assume interchangeability (or, ID data), but this is related to the model, not the algorithm.

**Clarity**

While the paper's presentation is mostly correct, there is room for improvement. Most importantly, the paper is somewhat unclear about what the actual learning task in question is: is it about estimating the points of change (under some well-defined assumptions about the existence and nature of the points of change) or estimating the generating structure of the last data point seen, or something else? Also, it is difficult to say whether the work proposes a (new) model (i.e., the formula for the assumptions and learning goal) or a (new) algorithm (i.e., a computational way to achieve the learning goal), or both.

The paragraph at the end of page 3 is difficult to follow; for example, does the equation given for \rho_{r+1} define it, or is the definition given earlier? I wonder why the authors do not use a period (.) when ending a sentence with the equation shown. Almost equally important is the font size in Figure 1-4: can it be doubled? Also, I don't mind if there are different color descriptions in Figure 1 and 2. In section 6, I was confused by the idea that improved tracking could offset the additional computational cost – does it not depend on some domain-specific assessment of tracking accuracy versus computational cost?

**Originality**

The work seems original. Yet it seems to lack any real innovation or new ideas. Perhaps there is something in the construction of the p-values (which I, unfortunately, cannot fully follow).

**Significance**

The potential significance of the current work (if published) is that it will likely lead to the development of improved, possibly Bayesian, methods for rapid problem-solving. It must be seen as development as opposed to research. A heuristic-informed approach to the learning problem that existing machine learning tools should already be solving is somewhat vaguely defined.
