- Introduces LoSST: an online heuristic for learning linear SEM/DAG structure under local stationarity using adaptive, time-varying weights on sufficient statistics.  
- Tracks weighted means, covariances, and an effective sample size via single-pass updates driven by pooled p-values from Mahalanobis distances.  
- Triggers probabilistic structure relearning using PC, with relearning frequency tied to pooled p-values; analyzes convergence vs. responsiveness via γ, T, and ν.  
- Positions LoSST between batch stationary structure learning, online change-point detection, and batch time-varying graphical models, filling a gap for online time-varying SEM structure learning.  
- Strength: Addresses an important, understudied setting (local stationarity) relevant for streaming/nonstationary data.  
- Strength: Method is simple, practical, easy to implement, and uses single-pass updates suitable for streaming/large-scale data.  
- Strength: Clear exposition and helpful derivations for online updates of weighted sufficient statistics.  
- Strength: Theoretical discussion of the convergence–diligence trade-off provides useful intuition about parameter regimes.  
- Strength: Experiments show LoSST matches batch PC under stationarity, outperforms PC after changes, and probabilistic relearning reduces computation without losing accuracy; real-data CPI case study is informative.  
- Weakness: Claim that “any other standard graphical model learners would perform similarly” is too strong without empirical or theoretical support; should be softened or tested.  
- Weakness/Scope: Paper should state earlier that it targets linear Gaussian SEMs/DAGs and that extensions (e.g., discrete Bayesian networks) are nontrivial due to sufficient-statistic tracking.  
- Additional: Provide guidance on selecting hyperparameters (T, γ, ν) via sensitivity analyses, heuristics, or rules of thumb for practitioners.  
- Additional: Discuss reliability of pooled p-values (Liptak’s method) given dependence in sequential statistics and when pooling assumptions hold.  
- Additional: Include runtime/memory discussion or empirical results on computational cost and the impact of probabilistic relearning.  
- Additional: Add brief qualitative interpretation of structural differences in the CPI real-data before/after detected shifts to increase case-study value.  
- Qualitative eval — Quality: Generally sound with supportive simulations and real-data example, but generality claims need tempering.  
- Qualitative eval — Clarity: Well-written and organized; explicitly stating scope earlier would improve clarity.  
- Qualitative eval — Originality & Significance: Novel combination of adaptive sufficient-statistic tracking, pooled p-values, and probabilistic relearning; likely useful to practitioners and researchers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles a relevant problem, offers a simple implementable approach, shows empirical benefits under nonstationarity, and provides theoretical insight.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Evaluation limited to PC (no evidence of broader applicability), late clarity on scope beyond SEMs, and limited guidance on hyperparameters and computational trade-offs.
