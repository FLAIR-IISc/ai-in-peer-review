- Tackles detecting changes in dependency structure in data streams for Structural Equation Models (SEMs).
- Proposes LoSST, an efficient streaming algorithm that uses batch PC structure learning triggered by monitored statistics.
- Monitors weighted mean distance, correlation matrix, and weighted sample size to decide when to relearn structure.
- Weights new points by "surprisingness" using Mahalanobis distance; computes p-values via Hotelling’s T² and pools them with Liptak’s method to form a significance ρ.
- Uses a noisy-OR with previous state and ρ to trigger structure relearning.
- Evaluated against batch PC on synthetic setups with parameter and structural changes, and on a 6-variable US price index real dataset.
- Experiments show LoSST finds long-term trends and filters transient fluctuations in real data.
- Addresses an important, underexplored problem with potential for further theoretical and practical work.
- LoSST is novel and designed for practical computational and memory efficiency, combining diverse statistical tools.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and includes a compelling real-world case study.
- Provides a thorough related-work survey and is clearly written.
- Lacks theoretical analysis or performance guarantees; authors should pursue guarantees under simplified assumptions.
- Algorithm is largely heuristic; key design choices (re-weighting, effective sample size, triggers) need clearer justification.
- Statistical tools are applied in settings that may violate assumptions (Hotelling’s T² for re-weighted/effective N; Liptak’s pooling for dependent sequential tests).
- Authors should explicitly acknowledge when methods are used outside their theoretical frameworks.
- Proofs in footnotes are misplaced; detailed derivations belong in supplementary material.
- Figures are too small, have excessive whitespace, missing axis labels, and weak captions/legends.
- Experimental setup description is unclear in parts and needs clarification.
- Explanation for LoSST’s poorer performance on edge additions during parameter-only changes is unconvincing.
- Suggestion: decouple triggers for parameter updates from triggers for full structure relearning.
