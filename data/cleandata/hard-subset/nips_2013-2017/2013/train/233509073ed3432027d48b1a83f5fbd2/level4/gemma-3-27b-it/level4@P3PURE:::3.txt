This manuscript presents a systematic approach to enable structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) with datasets that are only locally independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.). This addresses an important and often neglected problem: learning from time-dependent data, which many commonly used structure learning algorithms do not adequately handle. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the proposed techniques for extending learning to time-dependent data appear simple yet effective, as demonstrated by experiments using the PC algorithm.

I have two suggestions that could clarify the paper’s contributions. First, the statement in the experiments section that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly…” seems too strong. Differences among structure learning methods can be considerable. It would be beneficial to rephrase this, clarifying that the experiments focus on the PC method for time-independent data and its adaptation for time-dependent data – a relevant contribution in itself – and then speculate on whether similar results might hold for other methods. Alternatively, the assertion could be retained, but only with a detailed explanation of *why* this would be the case. 

Second, the discussion in the conclusions regarding the challenges of applying these ideas to Bayesian network structure learning, specifically the difficulties in maintaining sufficient statistics, is insightful. While not central to the paper’s main proposal, it highlights that extending the same learning procedure to Bayesian networks and other models may require substantial modifications. Therefore, the paper would benefit from explicitly acknowledging this limitation earlier, perhaps by framing the work more directly as focused on SEM models, while still acknowledging the *potential* for extension to other models (though not necessarily straightforwardly). In short, the paper could benefit from a sharper focus on its scope. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper on learning graph structure for SEMs under the assumption of local i.i.d. data. The formulation is simple to understand and implement, and the experimental results are promising, even if currently limited to a comparison using the PC method. I acknowledge having read the feedback from the authors.
