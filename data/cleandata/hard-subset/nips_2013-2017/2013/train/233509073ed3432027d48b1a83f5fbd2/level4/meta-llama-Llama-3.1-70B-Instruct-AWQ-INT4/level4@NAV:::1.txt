Reaction to the author feedback: 
I disagree with point (3) regarding the work of Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009), as it does not assume iid data and allows for variations in the "generating structure" within a limited set of graphical models. Although the proposed method offers increased flexibility, it is inaccurate to claim that it is the first to address this problem.

The paper introduces a hypothesis-testing-based approach for learning graphical models from time series data in an online setting. The method is designed for scenarios where the "data generating" graphical model may undergo frequent changes. The performance of the method is evaluated using both simulated and real data.

Quality 
The results appear to be correct. However, I find the description of related work in Section 2 to be somewhat misleading. Typical score-based algorithms for learning Bayesian networks do not assume a single generating model for the data; instead, they assume the scoring function can be factorized into local terms. While it is true that commonly studied models assume exchangeability (or iid data), this assumption pertains to the model rather than the algorithm.

Clarity 
The presentation of the paper is generally clear, but there is room for improvement. A major point of confusion is the actual learning task: is it to infer change points under specific assumptions or to infer the generating structure of the most recent data point, or something else? Additionally, it is unclear whether the work proposes a new model (formulations of assumptions and learning goals) or a new algorithm (computational means to achieve learning goals) or both.

The paragraph at the end of page 3 is difficult to follow, particularly the equation for \rho_{r+1}; it is unclear whether this equation defines it or if the definition has been provided earlier. I suggest using periods "." to end sentences with displayed equations. Furthermore, the font size in Figures 1-4 could be increased, and adding descriptions for the different colors in Figures 1 and 2 would be helpful. In Section 6, I found the idea that improved tracking could compensate for the additional computational cost confusing, as it seems to depend on domain-specific valuation of tracking accuracy against computational cost.

Originality 
The work appears to be original, but it lacks a novel idea or real innovation. Perhaps there is something innovative in the construction of the p-values, which I was unable to fully comprehend.

Significance 
The potential significance of this work, if published, lies in its potential to lead to the development of superior, presumably Bayesian, methods for this problem. However, this should be viewed as development rather than research. The approach seems heuristic and is applied to a somewhat unclearly defined learning problem that existing machine learning machinery should already be able to solve.
