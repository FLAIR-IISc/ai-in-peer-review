This manuscript introduces a compelling and systematic approach to structural equation modeling (SEM) structure learning in scenarios where data are only locally independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.). This addresses an important and often overlooked problem: learning from time-dependent data, a challenge for many existing SEM structure learning algorithms. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow, and the proposed techniques, demonstrated through extensions to the PC algorithm, appear both simple and effective.

However, I have two suggestions that would strengthen the paper’s contribution. First, the claim that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly” seems overly broad. While the experiments focus on the PC algorithm and its adaptation for time-dependent data – a valuable contribution in itself – differences between structure learning methods can be substantial. I recommend either rephrasing this statement to explicitly acknowledge the focus on the PC method and speculate on the likely behavior of other algorithms, or providing a detailed justification for the claim's generality. 

Second, the paper briefly mentions in the conclusion the challenges of extending this approach to Bayesian network structure learning due to difficulties maintaining sufficient statistics. While this isn’t a core component of the current work, it highlights a crucial point: adapting a single learning procedure across different graphical models might require significant modifications.  Presenting this limitation earlier, perhaps by focusing the paper’s scope more directly on SEM models while acknowledging potential, but non-trivial, extensions to other models, would sharpen the paper’s focus and clarify its contribution.

In summary, this is a well-written and promising paper addressing a significant problem in SEM structure learning under the realistic assumption of locally i.i.d. data. The formulation is both accessible and readily implementable, and the experimental results, though currently limited to comparisons using the PC method, are encouraging. I have reviewed the authors’ response and appreciate their clarifications.
