- Tackles online tracking of changes in dependency structure for data streams and proposes LoSST for linear SEMs.
- LoSST maintains weighted sufficient statistics and an effective sample size via online updates.
- New observations are scored by Mahalanobis distance → Hotelling T^2 p-values, pooled over time with Liptak to form a global fit ρ.
- A probabilistic relearning trigger (noisy-OR combining previous probability and 1−ρ) decides when to relearn structure with PC.
- Positions LoSST against batch/constraint-based learners, online tracking methods, changepoint detection, and prior time-varying graph work, emphasizing its single-pass, real-time advantage.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic data with injected changepoints and a U.S. price index case study shows promising detection of macro shifts and robustness to spikes.
- Strength: timely and well-motivated problem with practical, lightweight algorithmic design.
- Strength: novel integration of weighted statistics, Mahalanobis distances, pooled p-values, and probabilistic relearning.
- Strength: solid empirical exploration across regimes and a compelling real-data example.
- Weakness: limited formal theory and guarantees for online behavior; stronger results or explicit future-work emphasis needed.
- Weakness: several heuristic design choices and questionable use of statistical tools (Hotelling T^2 with reweighted effective N; Liptak pooling assumes independent tests).
- Weakness: presentation issues—small figures, sparse captions/legends, and unclear experimental details.
- Weakness: insufficient explanation for LoSST’s behavior under parameter-only changes, especially edge additions.
- Weakness: coupling of parameter adaptation and structure relearning may cause over/underreaction; decoupling desirable.
- Suggestions: formalize T^2 validity with effective sample sizes or provide calibration/corrections; analyze dependence among pooled p-values.
- Suggestions: add diagnostics (calibration of ρ, sensitivity analyses, ablations) and evaluate variants that decouple parameter vs. structural drift.
- Suggestions: improve figures, move proofs to supplement, and provide clearer dataset/parameter/relearning descriptions and pseudocode.
- Overall: a promising, practical first step toward online structure learning, but currently relies heavily on heuristics and needs stronger theory, validation, and clearer presentation.
