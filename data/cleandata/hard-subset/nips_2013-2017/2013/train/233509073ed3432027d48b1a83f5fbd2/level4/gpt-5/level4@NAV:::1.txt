Review- Reaction to the author feedback:
I cannot agree with point (3): The work by Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) does not assume iid data and permits the generating structure to change (albeit within a restricted class of graphical models). While the present method may be more flexible in some respects, it is misleading to claim it is the first to address this problem.
*
The paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based method for learning graphical models online from time-series data, tailored to settings where the data-generating graph may change fairly frequently. The approach is evaluated on both simulated and real datasets.

Quality
The results appear correct. However, the related work discussion (Section 2) is somewhat misleading: typical score-based Bayesian network learning algorithms do not assume data arise from a single fixed generating model; rather, they require a scoring function that decomposes into local terms. It is true that commonly studied models assume exchangeability (iid data), but that pertains to the modeling assumptions, not the algorithm itself.

Clarity
The presentation is mostly adequate, but several points need clarification. Most importantly, the learning task is not precisely specified: is the objective to detect change points (under explicit assumptions about their existence and form), to infer the generating structure at the most recent observation, or something else? It is also unclear whether the paper introduces a new model (assumptions and learning objectives), a new algorithm (computational procedures), or both.
The paragraph at the end of page 3 is hard to follow; for example, does the equation for rho_{r+1} define it, or was it defined earlier? I also wonder why sentences ending with displayed equations do not conclude with periods. Nearly as important, the font size in Figures 1–4 should be roughly doubled, and legends describing the colors in Figures 1 and 2 would help. In Section 6, I am puzzled by the claim that improved tracking can compensate for additional computational cost—doesn’t that depend on a highly domain-specific trade-off between tracking accuracy and computational expense?

Originality
The work appears original, yet I do not see a truly novel idea. Perhaps there is innovation in the construction of the p-values (which I unfortunately could not fully follow).

Significance
If published, the likely impact would be to spur the development of superior—presumably Bayesian—methods for this problem. That seems more like engineering development than research. Overall, this is a heuristic-seeming approach to a somewhat imprecisely defined learning problem that existing machine-learning techniques should arguably already address.
