- Presents a systematic, well-written approach to enable SEM structure learning with locally i.i.d. (time-dependent) data.  
- Addresses an important, neglected problem: learning from time-dependent data for structure-learning algorithms.  
- Proposed techniques are simple and effective; experiments with the PC method demonstrate promising results.  
- Claim that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly” is too strong and should be rephrased, justified, or labeled as speculation.  
- Experiments focus on PC and its time-dependent adaptation; clarify this focus rather than overgeneralizing to other methods.  
- Extending the approach to Bayesian networks is nontrivial due to difficulties maintaining sufficient statistics and may require more than minor technical adjustments.  
- Paper should disclose this limitation up front and frame contributions explicitly as focused on SEMs, while noting that extensions to other models are possible but not straightforward.
