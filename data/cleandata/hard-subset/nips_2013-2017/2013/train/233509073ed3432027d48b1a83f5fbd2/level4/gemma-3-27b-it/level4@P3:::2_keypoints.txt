- Paper addresses online tracking of time-varying dependency structure using recursive SEMs and proposes LoSST, which combines the PC algorithm with online statistic tracking to trigger re-learning.
- LoSST adaptively weights data by “surprisingness” (Mahalanobis distance + pooled p-value) to adjust effective sample size and decide when to relearn structure.
- Experiments include synthetic datasets varying variables, degree, and change type, plus a brief real-world demonstration on US price index data.
- Strength: Tackles an important, underexplored problem in dynamic graphical model learning with practical relevance.
- Strength: LoSST is computationally feasible by leveraging an established batch algorithm and efficient online updates.
- Strength: Comprehensive simulation experiments and a real-data example support empirical evaluation.
- Strength: Related work section is thorough and well-situated within existing online learning and changepoint literature.
- Weakness: No theoretical guarantees or analysis of convergence rates or conditions for successful change detection.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on heuristics (weighting scheme and re-learning decision) with limited justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: Use of Hotelling’s T2 for p-values and Liptak’s pooling is questionable given weighted, non-i.i.d. data and needs justification.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—small figures, missing labels/captions, and unclear experimental setup details hinder interpretation.
- Weakness: Explanation for poorer performance on edge removal when only parameters change is unconvincing.
- Weakness: Current implementation intermixes parameter and structure updates; decoupling them (more frequent parameter updates) could improve performance.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, practical approach with promising empirical results and clear exposition.
- Arguments for rejection: Lack of theoretical rigor, heuristic design choices without sufficient justification, and presentation/experimental clarity issues.
- Overall: Borderline contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Required revisions: add theoretical discussion or preliminary guarantees, justify heuristics and analyze sensitivity, improve figures and experiment descriptions, and acknowledge statistical-method limitations when used outside standard conditions.
