- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic online method that tracks sufficient statistics and dynamically relearns graphical model structure under non-stationarity.  
- Evaluated via simulations and a real-world US price index volatility analysis.

- Addresses the important problem of learning graphical models in non-stationary environments.  
- Offers a practical, implementable approach by integrating established techniques.  
- Simulation results show comparable performance to batch learning in stable settings and superior tracking when structure changes.  
- Real-world application suggests potential for economic time-series analysis.

- Mischaracterizes prior work (e.g., Siracusa & Fisher III 2009) and incorrectly claims to be the first to address time-varying graph learning.  
- Lacks a precise problem statement (change-point detection vs. current-structure estimation is unclear).  
- Heuristic method has limited theoretical foundation; parameter choices are empirical and convergence/trade-offs are unexplored.  
- Presentation problems: unclear equation definitions, poorly labeled figures, and some hard-to-follow text.  
- Novelty is incremental, primarily integrating existing methods rather than introducing fundamentally new techniques.

- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a relevant challenge, provides a practical solution, convincing simulations, and a plausible real-world example.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inaccurate related-work claims, weak theoretical underpinning, unclear goals, presentation issues, and limited novelty.

- Overall: a reasonable but incremental contribution with promising results, undermined by related-work inaccuracies and lack of theoretical depth.  
- Recommendation: weak accept with major revisions.

- Requested revision 1: Correct and accurately describe related work, including Siracusa & Fisher III (2009).  
- Requested revision 2: Precisely define the learning task and algorithm goals.  
- Requested revision 3: Strengthen theoretical justification and analyze convergence/diligence trade-offs and parameter selection.  
- Requested revision 4: Improve presentation (equation definitions, figure labeling, prose clarity).  
- Requested revision 5: Expand and deepen the real-world application analysis.
