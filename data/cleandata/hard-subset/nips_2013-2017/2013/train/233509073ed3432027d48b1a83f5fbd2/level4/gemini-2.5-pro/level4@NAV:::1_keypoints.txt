- Dissent: Claim that this is the first method to handle non‑iid changing graphical models is misleading — Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already allows changing generating structure within constrained graphical models.  
- Paper summary: Proposes a hypothesis‑testing approach for sequential learning of graphical models from time series where the generating structure can change.  
- Evaluation: Method validated on synthetic and real‑world datasets; results appear sound.  
- Prior work mischaracterized: Section 2 potentially misleading — score‑based Bayesian network learners do not require a single generating model, only a decomposable scoring function.  
- Method clarity: The precise learning objective is ambiguous (change‑point detection vs. inferring the latest structure vs. something else).  
- Contribution unclear: Uncertain whether the main contribution is a new probabilistic model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- Presentation issues: Paragraph at end of page 3 is hard to parse; equation for ρ_{r+1} is unclear whether it is a definition; missing periods after displayed equations.  
- Figure problems: Fonts in Figures 1–4 are too small; Figures 1 and 2 lack legends explaining color‑coding.  
- Practical claim questioned: Argument that better tracking justifies higher computational cost is application‑dependent and not generally convincing.  
- Originality uncertain: Work seems original but central novelty is unclear; construction of p‑values might be novel but insufficiently explained.  
- Significance modest: Likely to stimulate more Bayesian methods but reads more like engineering/heuristic development than fundamental research.  
- Problem formulation critique: Learning problem is not rigorously defined and comparable solutions may already exist in current ML frameworks.
