- Addresses under-explored problem of tracking changing dependency structures in streaming/time-series data (SEMs).
- Proposes LoSST: a practical, efficient algorithm using PC for structure learning plus tracked statistics to trigger relearning.
- Tracks weighted means, correlation matrix, and effective sample size with a surprise-based weighting scheme for new points.
- Computes Mahalanobis distances, estimates p-values, and pools them with Liptak’s method to form a significance statistic ρ.
- Uses a noisy-OR combination with previous step to decide when to initiate new structure learning.
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets with parameter/structure changes and a real US price-index dataset showing plausible detected trends.
- Strong literature review and generally well-written presentation.
- Major limitation: lacks theoretical guarantees (convergence/detection under assumptions); should be noted as future work.
- Heavy reliance on heuristics (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning criteria) without clearly stating decision rationale.
- Potential misuse of statistical methods: Hotelling’s T2 applied to reweighted/effective sample sizes and Liptak pooling despite dependence—assumptions likely violated.
- Authors should explicitly state when methods are used outside their theoretical guarantees.
- Presentation issues: figures too small/missing labels, unclear captions/legends, and cryptic experimental descriptions.
- Footnote-embedded proofs are inappropriate; move detailed proofs/derivations to supplementary material.
- Explanation for LoSST’s poorer performance on edge additions (parameter-only changes) is insufficient; suggest decoupling parameter updates from structure updates.
