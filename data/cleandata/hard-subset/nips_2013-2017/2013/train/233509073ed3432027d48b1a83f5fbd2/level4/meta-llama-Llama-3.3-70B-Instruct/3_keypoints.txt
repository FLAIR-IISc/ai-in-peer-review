- Proposes a systematic approach for structure learning of SEMs that handles locally i.i.d. and time-varying data.  
- Paper is well-structured, clear, and addresses the important problem of learning from time-dependent data often ignored by standard methods.  
- Techniques to extend learning to time-dependent data are straightforward and effective, validated experimentally using the PC algorithm.  
- Experimental validation is currently limited to comparison using the PC method.  
- Claim that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly” is too broad and should be rephrased or supported with justification.  
- Extending the approach to Bayesian networks may be challenging due to difficulties maintaining sufficient statistics, implying nontrivial modifications for other model classes.  
- Recommend clearly stating scope and limitations up front: focus on SEMs and acknowledge potential but complex extensions to other models.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, implementable formulation with promising results, but scope and experimental breadth should be clarified.
