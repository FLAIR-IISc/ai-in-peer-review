- Proposal: LoSST, an online structure-learning heuristic for SEMs assuming local stationarity where data are i.i.d. from a current generative model that may change over time.  
- Method: Tracks weighted sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size) in a single pass and uses Mahalanobis distances with Liptak’s pooled p-values to adapt weights and trigger probabilistic “relearning.”  
- Implementation: Uses the PC algorithm to recover graph structure at selected times.  
- Empirical results: Matches batch PC under stationarity and outperforms it when structure or parameters change; real-data U.S. price-index case shows plausible change detection.  
- Contribution: Addresses a gap where most structure learning assumes global stationarity and prior online methods don’t support general graphical learning or require storage/abrupt-change assumptions.  
- Conceptual insight: Clearly articulates the convergence–diligence trade-off.  
- Suggestion 1: Claim that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly” is too strong without empirical support; rephrase or provide justification/small-scale comparison.  
- Suggestion 2: Scope is unclear regarding Bayesian networks and other models; state upfront that the primary target is linear Gaussian SEMs and treat extensions as nontrivial future work.  
- Overall assessment: Clear, well-organized paper with simple, single-pass updates that are empirically effective in tested settings.  
- Pros summarized: tackles an important problem, lightweight updates, strong empirical evidence, and useful conceptual discussion.  
- Cons summarized: Unsubstantiated generality claims across learners and unclear scope beyond SEMs.
