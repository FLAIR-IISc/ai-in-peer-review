- Addresses tracking changes in dependency structure over data streams and proposes an efficient solution for simple SEMs.  
- Introduces LoSST: a streaming algorithm that invokes batch PC structure learning based on tracked statistics.  
- Tracks weighted mean distance, correlation matrix, and effective sample size; weights new points by "surprisingness."  
- Uses Mahalanobis distances, estimates p-values (Hotelling’s T2), and combines them via Liptak’s weighted pooling to form a significance statistic ρ.  
- Uses a noisy-OR combination with the previous step’s signal to decide when to relearn structure.  
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets with parameter/structure changes and on a 6-variable US price-index real dataset.  
- Pros: tackles an under-explored, interesting problem with a novel and practical algorithm.  
- Pros: experiments explore various parameters and include a meaningful real-world dataset.  
- Pros: provides a solid literature review and is generally well-written.  
- Con: lacks theoretical guarantees or convergence analysis and should discuss possible guarantees or future directions.  
- Con: heavy reliance on heuristics; key components (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning) need clearer justification and explanation.  
- Con: uses Hotelling’s T2 and Liptak pooling outside their original assumptions, risking invalid guarantees—this should be explicitly noted.  
- Other: proofs in footnotes are unconventional; missing or supplementary details should be moved to appendices.  
- Other: figures are too small, lack labels/legends, and have poor layout; experiment descriptions are sometimes unclear.  
- Other: explanation for LoSST’s poor performance on edge-addition parameter changes is unsatisfactory; decoupling parameter and structure updates is suggested.  
- Overall: an interesting practical direction and starting point, but needs clearer justification, improved presentation, and theoretical or empirical validation of heuristics.
