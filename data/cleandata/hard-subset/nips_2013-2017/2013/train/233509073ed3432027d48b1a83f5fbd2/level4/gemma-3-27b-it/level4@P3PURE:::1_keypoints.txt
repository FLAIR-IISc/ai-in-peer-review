- Reviewer disputes authors’ claim of novelty; cites Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) as prior work that handles non-iid data and varying graphical models.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing based online method to learn graphical models from time series that may change frequently, evaluated on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear correct, but the related-work discussion (Section 2) is misleading about score-based algorithms’ assumptions.  
- Score-based learning algorithms do not inherently assume a single generating model; exchangeability/iid concerns the model specification, not the algorithm.  
- Major clarity issue: the precise learning task is not well defined (change-point detection vs inferring current structure vs something else).  
- Unclear whether the contribution is a new formal model, a new algorithmic method, or both.  
- Specific exposition problems: paragraph at end of page 3 is hard to follow (e.g., definition of ρ_{r+1}); authors omit periods after displayed equations.  
- Presentation/figures need improvement: fonts in Figures 1–4 are too small and colors in Figures 1–2 are not described.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking can justify increased computational cost is confusing and overly domain-dependent.  
- Originality: work seems original but offers limited innovation; potential novelty may lie in the p-value construction, which is not clearly explained.  
- Significance: likely a developmental/heuristic contribution that could spur Bayesian methods, but addresses a loosely defined problem that existing ML techniques might already handle.
