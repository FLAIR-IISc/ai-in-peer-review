- Introduces an online hypothesis-testing method for learning graphical models from time series in non-stationary environments, evaluated on synthetic and real data.  
- Strength: Tackles the important and challenging problem of learning dynamic graphical models from streaming data.  
- Strength: The hypothesis-testing framework appears novel.  
- Strength: Empirical results are internally consistent and demonstrate the method’s intended behavior.  
- Weakness: Misleading positioning—claiming to be the “first” is inaccurate; prior work (e.g., Siracusa III & Fisher 2009) handles non-iid data and changing structure.  
- Weakness: Imprecise characterization of score-based learning methods; exchangeability is a model property, not a fundamental limitation of the paradigm.  
- Weakness: Unclear problem formulation—ambiguous whether goal is change-point detection, recent-structure inference, or both, and whether the contribution is a new model or an algorithm.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical/probabilistic grounding and reads as heuristic/engineering rather than a fundamental ML advance; p-value construction is unclear.  
- Weakness: Limited significance—problem might be better addressed by principled frameworks (e.g., Bayesian nonparametrics or state-space models); contribution feels incremental.  
- Presentation issue: Several unclear derivations (e.g., role/definition of ρ_{r+1}) and consistent formatting errors (missing periods after displayed equations).  
- Figure issues: Fonts in Figures 1–4 are too small; Figures 1–2 need legends explaining color coding.  
- Trade-off claim: Asserting that improved tracking justifies higher computational cost is subjective and needs a defined loss function or application-specific context.
