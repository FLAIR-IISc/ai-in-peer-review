- Proposes a hypothesis-testing based online method to learn graphical models that change frequently; validated on simulated and real data.  
- Results seem correct, but the related-work discussion is misleading: score-based BN methods don't require a single generating model, only a factorizing scoring function.  
- The paper is unclear about the actual learning task (change-point detection vs. inferring the current generating structure vs. something else).  
- It's ambiguous whether the contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- Paragraph at the end of page 3 and the definition/use of ρ_{r+1} are hard to follow and possibly inconsistent.  
- Formatting issues: authors omit periods after displayed equations and use very small font in Figures 1–4.  
- Figures 1–2 lack color legends/descriptions for different colors.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking can offset extra computational cost is questionable without domain-specific valuation.  
- Originality: the setting is novel, but the method appears to lack significant innovation; the p-value construction is not clearly explained.  
- Significance: work could foster development of Bayesian methods but reads more as applied development than foundational research.  
- Recommendation: acceptable with revision — clarify the learning task and contributions, correct related-work framing, improve presentation, and strengthen novelty.
