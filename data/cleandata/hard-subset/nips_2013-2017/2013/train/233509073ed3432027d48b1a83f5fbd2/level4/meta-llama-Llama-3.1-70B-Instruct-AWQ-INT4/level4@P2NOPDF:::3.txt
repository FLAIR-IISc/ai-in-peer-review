This manuscript presents a systematic approach to structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) that can accommodate data sets with local independence and identical distribution (i.i.d.), where the data may be generated from distinct underlying models over time. The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and addresses the important problem of learning from time-dependent data, which has been overlooked by many commonly used algorithms for structure learning. The proposed techniques for extending learning to time-dependent data are simple yet effective, as demonstrated by experiments using the PC method.

The paper's contributions could be clarified with two suggestions. Firstly, in the experiments, the statement "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" seems overly broad. The differences among methods for structure learning can be significant. It would be more accurate to rephrase this statement to emphasize that the experiments focus on the PC method for time-independent and time-dependent data, and then speculate that similar results might be obtained with other methods. Alternatively, the authors could retain the original statement and provide a detailed explanation for why they believe other methods would perform similarly.

Secondly, in the conclusions, the authors discuss the potential challenges of applying these ideas to learning the structure of Bayesian networks, particularly in maintaining sufficient statistics. Although this issue is not central to the proposal, it highlights the possibility that extending the learning procedure to Bayesian networks and other models may require more than minor modifications. To strengthen the paper, the authors could disclose this aspect from the beginning, presenting the paper in more direct terms about its goal of applying the approach to SEM models while noting that extensions to other models may be possible but not necessarily straightforward. In short, the paper could benefit from a clearer definition of its scope.

Overall, this is a well-written paper about learning the graph structure for SEMs under the assumption that data are only locally i.i.d. The formulation is simple to understand and implement, and the experimental results look promising, even if focused primarily on a comparison using the PC method.
