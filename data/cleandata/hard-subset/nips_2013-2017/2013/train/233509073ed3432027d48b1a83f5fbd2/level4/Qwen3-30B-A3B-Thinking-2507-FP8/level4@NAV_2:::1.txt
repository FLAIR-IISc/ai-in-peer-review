

Reaction to the author feedback:  
I cannot agree with point (3): The work of Siracusa and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) does not assume the data are i.i.d., and it allows the "generating structure" to vary (even if only within a limited set of graphical models). While the proposed method offers greater flexibility, claiming it is the first to address this problem would be misleading.

The paper introduces a hypothesis-testing-based approach for learning graphical models in an online manner from time series data. The method is specifically designed for scenarios where the data-generating graphical model may undergo relatively frequent changes, with performance demonstrated through both synthetic and real-world data.

Quality  
The results appear technically sound and well-supported by the experimental evidence. However, the related work discussion in Section 2 contains inaccuracies: standard score-based algorithms for Bayesian network learning do not inherently assume data come from a single generating model; rather, they rely on scoring functions that factorize into local terms. While typical model assumptions involve exchangeability (or i.i.d. data), this pertains to the model specification, not the algorithm itself.

Clarity  
The paper's presentation is generally clear but could benefit from several improvements. The core learning task remains ambiguous: is the objective to infer change points (under well-defined assumptions about their nature), to infer the generating structure of the most recent data point, or to accomplish something else entirely? It is also unclear whether the contribution is primarily a new model formulation (defining assumptions and learning goals) or a novel algorithm (computational methods to achieve these goals), or both. The paragraph at the end of page 3 requires clarification, particularly regarding whether the equation for ρ_{r+1} provides its definition or builds upon a previously established definition. The absence of periods following displayed equations is inconsistent with standard academic writing conventions. Additionally, the font size in Figures 1-4 appears too small and could be doubled for better readability. Including color legends for Figures 1 and 2 would enhance interpretability. In Section 6, the claim that improved tracking might compensate for additional computational costs seems overly domain-specific without explicit justification.

Originality  
The work appears novel in its approach to online graphical model structure learning. However, the method lacks significant conceptual innovation. While the construction of pooled p-values may contain subtle technical novelty, the overall approach seems to represent a rather straightforward application of existing statistical techniques rather than a fundamentally new idea.

Significance  
The potential significance of this work lies in its potential to catalyze the development of more sophisticated, presumably Bayesian, methods for online structure learning. However, this contribution appears more developmental than research-oriented. The paper presents a heuristic approach to a learning problem that existing machine learning frameworks should theoretically be capable of addressing, making the significance of the work somewhat limited.
