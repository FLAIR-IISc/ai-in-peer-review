- Reviewer disputes authors’ claim of novelty, citing Siracusa and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) which allowed non-iid, time-varying generating structures.
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based method to learn graphical models online for frequently changing time-series graphs, evaluated on simulated and real data.
- Results seem sound, but the related-work discussion mischaracterizes score-based Bayesian network learners as requiring a single fixed generating model.
- Clarify whether the learning objective is changepoint detection, recent-structure estimation, or another target, and whether the contribution is a new model, algorithm, or both.
- Mathematical exposition needs fixes (e.g., clarity on definition of rho_{r+1}, punctuation after displayed equations).
- Presentation issues: small figure fonts, missing legends for Figures 1–2, and hard-to-parse paragraph at end of page 3.
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking can justify extra computation lacks a clear, domain-specific evaluation criterion or trade-off analysis.
- Originality is limited: method seems novel in parts (e.g., pooled p-values) but no clearly fundamental new idea and some parts are hard to follow.
- Significance appears incremental: likely to spur further (probably Bayesian) work but not a substantive advance; approach feels heuristic and problem formulation is not crisply defined.
