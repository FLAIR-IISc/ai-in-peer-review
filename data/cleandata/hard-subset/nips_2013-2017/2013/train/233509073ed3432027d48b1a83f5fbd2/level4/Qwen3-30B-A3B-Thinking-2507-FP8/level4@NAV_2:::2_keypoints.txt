- Paper tackles real-time tracking of time-varying graphical structures under local stationarity and proposes LoSST, which tracks weighted sufficient statistics and uses Mahalanobis distances, pooled p-values, a noisy-OR relearning rule, and the PC algorithm.  
- Evaluated on synthetic controlled structural/parameter shifts and a real-world U.S. price-index volatility case study (1967–2011), showing LoSST detects macroeconomic shifts and ignores short-term volatility spikes.  
- Novelty: addresses an underexplored online structure-learning problem distinct from batch learning or abrupt changepoint methods.  
- Practical significance: applicable to dynamic domains (e.g., finance, evolving causal systems).  
- Experimental quality: thorough systematic testing across variable counts, graph densities, and change scenarios; LoSST matches batch PC under stationarity and outperforms during structural shifts.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: generally well-written with sufficient technical detail and useful figures.  
- Major weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees (no formal analysis of convergence vs. diligence trade-offs).  
- Major weakness: key heuristics (Hotelling’s T² with effective sample size, Liptak pooling) are used outside standard theoretical guarantees and need explicit caveats.  
- Presentation issues: figures need improvements (small fonts, missing labels, inconsistent legends).  
- Methodological explanation gap: insufficient explanation for LoSST’s higher edge removal error during parameter-only changes; decoupling parameter updates from structural relearning is suggested.  
- Future work priority: emphasize developing theoretical guarantees and methods for rapidly changing structures rather than treating them as incidental directions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, practical algorithm, and strong empirical validation on synthetic and real data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: underdeveloped theoretical foundations, heuristic choices lacking justification, and presentation issues that impede reproducibility.  
- Overall: valuable contribution that, with minor revisions addressing theory and methodological clarity, would be a strong fit for NIPS.
