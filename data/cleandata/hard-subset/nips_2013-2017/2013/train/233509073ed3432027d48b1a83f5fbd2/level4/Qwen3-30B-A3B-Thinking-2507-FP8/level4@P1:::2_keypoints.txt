- Problem: Tracks changes in dependency structure in streaming data (time-varying graphical models).
- Proposal: Introduces LoSST, a heuristic online algorithm for efficient structure learning using tracked sufficient statistics.
- Method: Uses Mahalanobis-distance-based weighting of surprising points and Liptak pooling of p-values to compute a significance statistic ρ for probabilistic relearning.
- Evaluation: Thorough synthetic simulations varying graph/parameter changes, variables, and degrees; real-world test on U.S. price index data demonstrating meaningful shift detection.
- Strength: Addresses an important, underexplored problem with clear practical relevance against global-stationarity assumptions.
- Strength: LoSST is novel and practical, balancing real-time learning with batch-equivalent performance in stable regimes.
- Strength: Experimental design is systematic and comprehensive; real-world application is compelling.
- Strength: Literature review is comprehensive and situates LoSST relative to online learning, changepoint detection, and time-varying models.
- Strength: Paper is generally well written with clear algorithmic explanations.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical guarantees or formal analysis even under simplifying assumptions; needs discussion of achievable guarantees.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on heuristics without clear justification for key choices (e.g., Hotelling’s T² on weighted samples; Liptak pooling for sequential dependent tests).
- Weakness: Inadequate explanation for higher edge removal errors when only parameters change; mechanism for LoSST’s conservatism vs. PC unclear.
- Comment: Footnoted proofs are unconventional for NIPS and belong in supplementary material.
- Comment: Figures are poorly formatted (small, spaced, missing labels/legends), impairing interpretability.
- Comment: Description of the third simulation (Section 4.3) is confusing and needs clarification.
- Suggestion: Decouple parameter updates from structure updates to address potential causes of edge removal errors.
- Overall: Interesting, well-executed empirical contribution with weak theoretical grounding; recommend minor revisions to strengthen theory and clarify implementation details.
