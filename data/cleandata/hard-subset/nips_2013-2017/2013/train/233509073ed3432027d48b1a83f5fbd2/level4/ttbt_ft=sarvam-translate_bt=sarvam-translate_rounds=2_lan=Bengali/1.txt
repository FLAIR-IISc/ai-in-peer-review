*   The paper presents a heuristic-based method for learning graphical models from time series data in an online manner. The proposed method is specifically designed for situations where "data generating" graphical models may change relatively frequently. The performance of the method using both simulated and real data has been demonstrated.

**Quality**

The results seem to be correct. However, I find the description of related work (in section 2) somewhat misleading: general score-based algorithms for learning Bayesian networks do not assume that this information comes from a single generating model - rather they assume that the scoring function factors in local conditions. It is true that models generally assume interchangeability (or, ID data) in the study, but this is related to the model, not to the algorithm.

**Clarity**

Although the paper's presentation is mostly correct, there is room for improvement. Most importantly, the paper is somewhat unclear about what the actual learning task in question is: is it about estimating the points of change (under some well-defined assumptions about the existence and nature of the points of change) or estimating the generating structure of the last data point seen, or something else? Also, it is difficult to say whether the work proposes a (new) model (i.e., a formula for the assumptions and learning goals) or a (new) algorithm (i.e., a computational way to achieve the learning goals), or both.

The paragraph at the end of page 3 is difficult to follow; for example, does the equation given for \rho_{r+1} define it, or is the definition given earlier? I wonder why the authors do not use a period (.) when ending a sentence with the equation shown. Almost equally important is the font size in figures 1-4: can it be doubled? Also, I don't mind if there are different color descriptions in figures 1 and 2. In section 6, I was confused by the idea that improved tracking could offset additional computational costs - does it depend on some domain-specific assessment of tracking accuracy or on computational costs?

**Originality**

The work seems original. Yet it seems to lack any real innovation or new ideas. Perhaps there is something in the construction of the p-values (which I, unfortunately, cannot fully follow).

**Importance**

The potential significance of the current work (if published) is that it will likely lead to the development of improved, perhaps Bayesian, methods for solving problems quickly. It should be seen as development rather than research. A heuristic-informed method for learning problems that existing machine learning tools should already be able to solve is somewhat vaguely defined.
