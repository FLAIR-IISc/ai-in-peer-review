This manuscript introduces a systematic approach to structural learning in Structural Equation Models (SEMs) when the data are only locally independent and identically distributed – that is, when the data generating process may change over time. The paper is well-written and clearly presented, and tackles an important problem: learning from time-dependent data, a challenge often overlooked by existing structure learning algorithms. The proposed techniques for extending learning to these time-dependent datasets are both simple and effective, as demonstrated by the experiments using the PC algorithm.

I have two suggestions that could further clarify the paper’s contributions. First, the statement in the experiments section that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly…” appears too strong. Differences between structure learning methods can be substantial. It would be more precise to rephrase this to emphasize that the experiments focus on the PC algorithm, both in its standard time-independent form and with the adaptation for time-dependent data – which is a valuable contribution in itself – and then speculate that similar results might hold for other methods. Alternatively, if the assertion is maintained, a detailed justification for its validity should be provided. 

Second, the conclusion notes that applying the same ideas to Bayesian network structure learning may be challenging due to difficulties in maintaining sufficient statistics. While this issue isn’t central to the paper’s primary contribution, it highlights that extending this learning procedure to Bayesian networks and other models might require significant modifications. Therefore, I believe the paper would benefit from explicitly acknowledging this limitation earlier, perhaps by framing the work more directly as focused on SEMs while still acknowledging the potential – though not necessarily straightforward – for generalization to other models. In summary, this is a well-written paper addressing graph structure learning for SEMs under the assumption of local i.i.d. data. The formulation is easy to understand and implement, and the experimental results are promising, even with the focus on comparisons using the PC algorithm.
