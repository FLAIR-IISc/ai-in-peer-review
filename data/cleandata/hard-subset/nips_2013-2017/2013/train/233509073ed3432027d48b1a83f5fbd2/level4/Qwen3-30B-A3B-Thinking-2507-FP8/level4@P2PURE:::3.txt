

This manuscript presents a systematic approach to enabling structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) under the assumption of locally i.i.d. data, where the underlying generating model may change over time. The paper is well-written, well-structured, and addresses an important problem—learning from time-dependent data—that has been overlooked by many standard structure learning algorithms. The proposed techniques for extending learning to time-dependent data are simple yet effective, as demonstrated by experiments using the PC method.  

Two suggestions would strengthen the paper’s clarity and contribution:  
1. The claim that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" is overly strong. The differences between structure learning methods can be substantial. The paper should either rephrase to clarify that the experiments specifically compare the PC method (for time-independent vs. time-dependent data, which is particularly relevant), and note that the similarity for other methods is speculative; or retain the assertion and provide a detailed justification for why it would hold generally.  
2. The conclusion notes that extending the approach to Bayesian networks may not be straightforward due to challenges in preserving sufficient statistics. While this is not central to the paper’s focus, acknowledging this limitation upfront would sharpen the paper’s scope. The authors should explicitly frame the work as targeting SEMs, while noting that extensions to other models (like Bayesian networks) may require additional effort beyond simple adaptations.  

This is a well-written paper presenting a promising approach for SEM structure learning under locally i.i.d. data. The formulation is straightforward to implement, and the experimental results are encouraging, though focused primarily on the PC method. I confirm having reviewed the authors' feedback.
