- Addresses the important gap of tracking changing graphical model structure in streaming, real-time, locally stationary settings.
- Introduces LoSST, a novel heuristic for online tracking of time-varying graphical model structure.
- Builds on the PC algorithm and tracks sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size) for online updates.
- Dynamically decides when to relearn structure using Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values (Liptak).
- Thorough literature review positioning the work relative to online learning, changepoint detection, and time-varying structure methods.
- Strong experimental validation with comprehensive simulations under two scenarios: graph-structure changes and parameter-only changes.
- LoSST outperforms batch-mode learning after structure changes while matching batch performance during stable periods.
- Real-world application to US price index volatility convincingly detects known economic shifts (e.g., the Great Moderation) and ignores short-term noise.
- Experimental design tests multiple variable counts and graph complexities with clear presentation and appropriate error metrics.
- Originality is high: novel combination of Mahalanobis tests, Liptak pooling, and probabilistic relearning for online structure tracking.
- Authors thoughtfully discuss the convergence–diligence tradeoff as a fundamental limitation of online structure learning.
- Significant practical impact for domains needing real-time dependency tracking (finance, sensors, biology) with minimal overhead vs. batch methods.
- Strength: well-motivated problem addressing a significant literature gap.
- Strength: practical, efficient algorithm justified by comprehensive experiments.
- Strength: compelling real-world demonstration on economic data.
- Strength: thorough related-work review and clear contribution positioning.
- Strength: generally clear, well-organized writing with appropriate technical detail.
- Weakness: lacks formal theoretical guarantees; needs discussion of possible guarantees under simplifying assumptions.
- Weakness: several heuristic choices (effective sample size in Hotelling’s T², Liptak pooling) are insufficiently justified and require discussion of impacts.
- Weakness: some experimental explanations are unclear (e.g., why LoSST underperforms batch on edge removal during parameter changes).
- Weakness: figure presentation is poor—small, unlabeled, and with inadequate captions limiting interpretability.
- Suggested improvements: add brief theoretical analysis, justify heuristic choices, clarify experimental details, and improve figure clarity.
- Promising future work: decoupling parameter updates from structure updates to address current limitations.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical and methodological clarifications.
