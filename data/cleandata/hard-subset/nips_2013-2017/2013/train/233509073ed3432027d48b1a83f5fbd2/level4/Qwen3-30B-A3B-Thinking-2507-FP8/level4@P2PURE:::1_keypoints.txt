- Disagreement with novelty claim: Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handles non-iid data and varying generating structures.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing method for online learning of graphical models from time series with frequently changing structure; evaluated on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear correct, but Section 2 mischaracterizes related work: score-based BN algorithms assume score factorization/locality, not a single generating model.  
- Core learning task is unclear: is it change-point detection, inferring the final data-point structure, or something else?  
- Unclear whether contribution is a new model (assumptions/goals), a new algorithm (computational method), or both.  
- Paragraph at end of page 3 is hard to follow; definition of ρ_{r+1} is confusing or redundant.  
- Formatting/editing issues: missing periods after displayed equations.  
- Figure problems: Figures 1–4 use too-small fonts (should be doubled); Figures 1–2 lack color legends.  
- Section 6 claim that better tracking justifies higher computation is domain-specific and unsubstantiated.  
- Originality is limited: work seems novel but lacks substantial new ideas; p-value construction might be novel but is not well explained.  
- Significance is modest: primarily a methodological development that is heuristic and tackles a problem existing ML frameworks should already address.
