- Presents a novel, systematic approach (LoSST) for structure learning in SEMs under non-stationary, locally i.i.d. data.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the under-addressed problem of adapting structure learning to time-dependent data.  
- Proposed techniques are conceptually simple and shown to be effective in experiments adapting the PC algorithm.  
- Situates contribution within literature, contrasting with Bayesian/score-based and constraint-based methods that assume stationarity.  
- Discussion of Temporal Difference Learning and online changepoint detection provides useful context and limits of existing approaches.  
- LoSST algorithm is described with sufficient detail for implementation and understanding.  
- Experimental claim that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly” is overly strong and should be softened or justified.  
- Recommend clarifying experiments focus specifically on PC method and either speculate carefully about other algorithms or provide comparative rationale.  
- Conclusion’s discussion on extending to Bayesian network learning correctly highlights practical challenges (tracking sufficient statistics for discrete variables).  
- Suggest acknowledging extension difficulties earlier and tightening scope to SEMs while noting possible but nontrivial extensions.  
- Empirical validation via simulations demonstrates LoSST’s advantage over standard batch-mode algorithms in dynamic settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, empirical validation, and real-world significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited scope and generalizability due to experiments focused only on the PC algorithm.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to scope clarification and tempering of generalization claims.
