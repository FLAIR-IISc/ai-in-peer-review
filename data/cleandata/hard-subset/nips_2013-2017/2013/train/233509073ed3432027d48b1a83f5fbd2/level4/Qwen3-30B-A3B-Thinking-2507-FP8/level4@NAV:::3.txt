

This manuscript introduces a systematic approach for structure learning in SEMs when data are only locally i.i.d., meaning the underlying generating model may change over time. The paper is well-written, addresses an important gap in learning from time-dependent data (neglected by many standard algorithms), and presents simple yet effective techniques for extending structure learning to such data, as demonstrated through experiments with the PC method.  

Two suggestions would strengthen the contribution clarity. First, the claim that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly" appears overly broad. It is recommended to clarify that the experiments specifically compare the PC method for time-independent versus adapted time-dependent data (which is the core contribution), and either rephrase the assertion to reflect this scope or provide justification for the generalization to other methods.  

Second, while the paper's focus is on SEMs, the conclusion notes potential challenges in extending the approach to Bayesian networks due to sufficient statistics constraints. To sharpen the paper's scope, it would be beneficial to explicitly state from the outset that the work targets SEMs specifically, noting that extensions to other models (like Bayesian networks) may require additional considerations beyond the presented framework. This would prevent overgeneralization while acknowledging potential future directions.  

Overall, this is a well-written paper with a clear formulation, promising experimental results (though focused on the PC method), and a simple, implementable approach for SEM structure learning under locally i.i.d. data. I have read the authors' responses to prior feedback.
