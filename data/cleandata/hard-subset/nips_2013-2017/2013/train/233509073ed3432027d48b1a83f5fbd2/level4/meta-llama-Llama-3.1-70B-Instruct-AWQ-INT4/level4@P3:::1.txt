The paper "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure" proposes a novel algorithm, LoSST, for learning graphical models in an online fashion from time series data, where the underlying generating structure may change frequently. The authors demonstrate the performance of LoSST using both simulated and real data.

The paper relates to previous work on graphical models, including Bayesian/score-based methods and constraint-based structure learning algorithms. However, the authors' claim that their method is the first to address the problem of learning time-varying graphical models is not entirely accurate. For example, the work of Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) does not assume the data are iid and allows the generating structure to vary, albeit in a limited set of graphical models.

The quality of the paper is good, and the results seem correct. However, the description of related work in Section 2 is somewhat misleading. Typical score-based algorithms for learning Bayesian networks do not assume that the data come from a single generating model, but rather that the scoring function factorizes into local terms. The assumption of exchangeability (or iid data) concerns the model, not the algorithm.

The clarity of the paper is mostly fine, but there are some areas for improvement. The paper is unclear on what the actual learning task is: inferring change points, inferring the generating structure of the last data point seen, or something else. It is also hard to tell whether the work proposes a new model or a new algorithm, or both. Some paragraphs, such as the one at the end of page 3, are difficult to follow, and the font size in Figures 1-4 could be increased. Additionally, descriptions for the different colors in Figures 1 and 2 would be helpful.

The originality of the work is good, but it seems to lack any real innovation or novel idea. The construction of the p-values is potentially novel, but it is not entirely clear.

The significance of the paper is that it could lead to the development of superior methods for learning time-varying graphical models. However, the approach seems heuristic, and the learning problem is not clearly defined. The paper could benefit from a more rigorous formulation of the learning task and a clearer explanation of the proposed algorithm.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning graphical models in an online fashion.
* The authors demonstrate the performance of LoSST using both simulated and real data.
* The paper relates to previous work on graphical models and highlights the limitations of existing methods.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper's claim to be the first to address the problem of learning time-varying graphical models is not entirely accurate.
* The description of related work in Section 2 is somewhat misleading.
* The paper is unclear on what the actual learning task is and whether it proposes a new model or a new algorithm.
* The approach seems heuristic, and the learning problem is not clearly defined.

Recommendation: The paper could benefit from revisions to address the above concerns. Specifically, the authors should clarify the learning task, provide a more accurate description of related work, and explain the proposed algorithm in more detail. Additionally, the authors could consider providing more rigorous evaluations of the performance of LoSST and comparing it to other existing methods.
