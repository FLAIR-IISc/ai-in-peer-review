- Tackles tracking changes in dependency structure in data streams for SEMs and proposes LoSST to trigger structure relearning efficiently.
- LoSST monitors weighted mean distances, the correlation matrix, and an effective sample size to decide when to relearn structure.
- New data are weighted by "surprisingness" via Mahalanobis distance; p-values estimated with Hotelling's T² and combined with Liptak’s method into a significance ρ.
- A noisy OR gate using ρ and previous state decides whether to initiate a new round of PC-based structure learning.
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets with parameter and structural changes and on a six-variable US price index time series; shows detection of known economic shifts and robustness to short-term fluctuations.
- Strength: addresses an interesting, under-explored problem with practical importance.
- Strength: proposes a novel, efficient algorithm that creatively integrates existing methods.
- Strength: experimental setup is adequate and includes a relevant real-world dataset.
- Strength: provides a comprehensive related-work review and is generally well-written.
- Major weakness: lacks theoretical analysis or guarantees under any assumptions; should at least outline future directions for theory.
- Major weakness: heavy reliance on heuristics with insufficient justification for key choices (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning criteria).
- Questionable method use: applying Hotelling’s T² to reweighted/effective sample sizes and using Liptak pooling (which assumes independent tests) outside their guaranteed contexts.
- Presentation issues: excessive use of footnotes for proofs—move to supplementary material—and figures are too small, poorly labeled, and captions/legends incomplete.
- Experimental clarity issues: some descriptions (e.g., top paragraph p.7) are unclear and the explanation for LoSST’s poorer performance on edge addition when only parameters change is unconvincing.
- Recommendation: consider decoupling parameter updates from structure updates; overall a promising but heuristic first implementation that warrants further theoretical and empirical refinement.
