Reaction to the author feedback:
I disagree with point (3): Siracusa and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) do not assume iid data and explicitly allow the “generating structure” to vary (albeit over a restricted family of graphs). Although the present approach may be more flexible in some respects, it is misleading to portray it as the first to tackle this problem.

The paper introduces a hypothesis-testing-based method to learn graphical models online from time series, targeting settings where the underlying “data-generating” graph may change relatively often. The approach is evaluated on both simulated and real datasets.

Quality
The results appear sound. However, the discussion of related work (Section 2) is somewhat inaccurate: typical score-based Bayesian network learners do not assume data arise from a single fixed generating model; rather, they rely on scores that decompose into local components. While commonly used models assume exchangeability (or iid sampling), that is an assumption about the model class, not an inherent limitation of the learning algorithm.

Clarity
The exposition is generally acceptable, but several aspects need clarification. Most notably, the paper does not clearly specify the precise learning objective: is the goal changepoint detection under explicit assumptions about their existence and form, estimation of the generating structure at the most recent timepoint, or something else? It is also unclear whether the contribution is a new problem formulation/model (assumptions and learning targets), a new algorithmic procedure to meet those targets, or both.
The paragraph at the end of page 3 is hard to parse; for example, is the displayed formula for rho_{r+1} the definition, or was rho_{r+1} defined earlier? Also, sentences ending with displayed equations should still be punctuated. The font in Figures 1–4 is quite small; doubling it would help. A legend for the colors in Figures 1 and 2 would also improve readability. In Section 6, the suggestion that improved tracking could outweigh extra computational cost seems to require a domain-specific trade-off; please clarify the evaluation criterion for this comparison.

Originality
The paper appears original, but I do not see a clear, fundamentally new idea. There may be novelty in the construction of the pooled p-values, but I could not fully follow that part.

Significance
If published, the likely impact would be to catalyze development of stronger, probably Bayesian, methods for this problem. That trajectory feels more like incremental development than substantive research advance. Overall, the approach is heuristic and the problem formulation is not crisply defined, in an area where existing machine learning techniques arguably should already be applicable.
