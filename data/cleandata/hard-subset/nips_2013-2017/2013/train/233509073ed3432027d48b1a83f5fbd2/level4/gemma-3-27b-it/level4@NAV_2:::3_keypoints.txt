- Presents a systematic, well-written approach for structure learning of recursive SEMs when data are only locally i.i.d.  
- Addresses an important and underexplored problem: learning from time-dependent (nonstationary) data.  
- Proposed techniques to handle time dependence are conceptually simple and empirically effective (evaluated with the PC algorithm).  
- Experimental results are promising but currently focused on the PC method.  
- The claim that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithm would perform similarly" is too strong and should be rephrased or supported.  
- If asserting broader applicability, either substantiate why similar performance is expected from other methods or restrict claims to PC/SEMs.  
- The discussion about difficulties extending to Bayesian network structure learning (e.g., preserving sufficient statistics) is insightful and should be highlighted earlier.  
- Recommend framing the paper primarily as a contribution to SEMs while acknowledging possible but nontrivial extensions to other model classes.
