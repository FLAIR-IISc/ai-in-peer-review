- Disagreement with authors’ claim of novelty: Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid data and varying generating structure.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based online method to learn graphical models from time series with frequently changing generating models.  
- Empirical validation provided on simulated and real datasets.  
- Results appear correct but Section 2’s related-work discussion is misleading.  
- Score-based BN algorithms do not require a single generating model; they require a scoring function that factorizes locally.  
- The usual exchangeability/iid assumption pertains to the model, not the learning algorithm.  
- Presentation is mostly clear but the actual learning task is ambiguous (change-point detection vs. last-point structure vs. other).  
- Unclear whether the contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- Equation for ρ_{r+1} (end of page 3) and the paragraph around it are difficult to follow; formatting of displayed equations should be fixed.  
- Figures 1–4 have fonts that are too small and lack color legends for clarity.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking justifies extra computation is confusing and domain-dependent.  
- Work seems original but lacks strong novel ideas; construction of p-values may be unclear.  
- Potential significance is limited: could inspire Bayesian developments but currently appears heuristic and addresses a poorly defined problem that existing methods may already cover.
