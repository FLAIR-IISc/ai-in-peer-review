- Reviewer disagrees with authors’ claim of novelty: Siracusa and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-i.i.d. data and varying graphical structures.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing method for online learning of graphical models under frequently changing data-generating structures.  
- Empirical evaluation includes both simulated and real-world datasets.  
- Technical results seem correct, but the related-work discussion (Section 2) is misleading.  
- Score-based Bayesian-network learning does not inherently require a single generating model; exchangeability is a model property, not a learning-algorithm prerequisite.  
- The learning task is ill-defined: unclear whether goal is change-point detection, recent-structure inference, or something else.  
- It's ambiguous whether the main contribution is a new formal model, a new algorithmic method, or both.  
- Presentation issues reduce clarity: an unclear paragraph at end of page 3 and ambiguous definition/use of ρ_{r+1}.  
- Formatting and readability problems: missing periods after displayed equations, fonts in Figures 1–4 too small, and Figures 1–2 lack legends for colors.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking performance universally justifies extra computational cost is unjustified and domain-dependent.  
- Originality is limited: approach seems novel in details but lacks major conceptual innovation.  
- Possible novelty in the construction of p-values, but assessment hindered by presentation.  
- Significance is modest: contribution feels more engineering/heuristic than fundamental and may mainly prompt development of more principled Bayesian methods.
