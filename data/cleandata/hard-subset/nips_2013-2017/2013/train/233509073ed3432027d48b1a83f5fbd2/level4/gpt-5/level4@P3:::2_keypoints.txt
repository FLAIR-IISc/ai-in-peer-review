- Proposes LoSST: an online heuristic that maintains weighted sufficient statistics and triggers occasional DAG relearning via PC using pooled p-values from Mahalanobis surprises.  
- Novelty: combines adaptive weighted statistics, pooled p-values (Lipták), and a probabilistic noisy-OR relearning trigger for single-pass tracking.  
- Addresses an important gap: real-time, memory-light structure learning for time-varying graphical models.  
- Practical and efficient: incremental updates (means, covariances, effective sample size) enable single-pass operation with low memory.  
- Empirics: simulations cover structure and parameter changes across graph sizes/degrees and include a macroeconomic case study with plausible detections.  
- Clear positioning: relates to batch structure learning, online change-point detection, and time-varying graph estimation; focuses on linear SEMs for tractability.  
- Statistical validity concern: Hotelling’s T^2 p-values use an “effective” sample size and adaptive weights, violating i.i.d. assumptions and challenging pooling independence assumptions.  
- Calibration needed: request null simulations and empirical assessment of false-alarm rates, sensitivity, and specificity across T, γ, ν.  
- Numerical stability issue: inverting weighted covariance Cr can be ill-conditioned for small effective sample sizes or high dimension; clarify use of shrinkage/regularization and effects on detection.  
- PC adaptation unclear: specify how constraint-based CI tests are adapted to weighted sufficient statistics and whether Type I error control is preserved.  
- Ablations & sensitivity: ask for systematic sensitivity analysis of T, γ, ν and ablation of pooled-p-value mechanism versus alternatives (e.g., EWMA).  
- Presentation problems: figures are small and under-labeled; move proof sketches to supplement and expand experimental descriptions for reproducibility.  
- Explanation gap: need clearer analysis of transient behavior when only parameters change (why PC adds spurious edges while LoSST shrinks Sr).  
- Related work expansion: compare more fully to time-varying Gaussian graphical models and dynamic Bayesian networks learned with windowing, kernel smoothing, or fused penalties.  
- Limits and resources: request runtime and memory comparisons vs. batch baselines and tests under harder regimes (higher dimensionality, faster switching, slow drift).  
- Reproducibility: require implementation details (initialization, regularization, exact parameters) and ideally code release.  
- Pro-accept reasons: timely problem, simple and efficient algorithm, strong empirical adaptation to change while competitive under stationarity, likely to spur follow-up work.  
- Con-accept reasons: heavy reliance on heuristics lacking theoretical guarantees, limited calibration and stability analysis, and unclear decoupling of parameter vs. structure updates.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on clarifying heuristic nature of tests, providing empirical calibration, improving figures and experimental details, discussing numerical stability and PC adaptation, and expanding sensitivity analyses.
