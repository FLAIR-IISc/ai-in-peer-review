- Tackles the underexplored problem of tracking changing dependency structure in data streams and focuses on simpler SEMs.
- Proposes LoSST: a novel, efficient algorithm that uses PC for structure learning, monitors statistics (distance from weighted mean, correlation matrix, weighted sample size), weights new data by "surprisingness", uses Mahalanobis distance and Hotelling's T2, combines signals with a noisy-OR to trigger re-learning, and is evaluated on synthetic and US price-index data.

Pros:
- Addresses an interesting, underexplored problem with practical relevance.
- Introduces a novel, efficient algorithm that creatively combines existing methods.
- Experiments are adequate, analyze parameter effects, and include a real-world dataset.
- Provides a comprehensive review of prior related work.
- Paper is well-written.

Cons:
- Lacks theoretical guarantees or convergence analysis; needs formal study in future work.
- Heavily heuristic: key design decisions are insufficiently justified and may invalidate guarantees of used statistical methods.

Other comments:
- Footnote proofs are inappropriate—use supplementary material instead.
- Figures are too small and lack necessary labels/captions.
- Explanation for LoSST's poor performance on edge addition under parameter changes is unsatisfactory.
- Decoupling parameter updates from structure updates could be a valuable future direction.
- Overall, the paper presents a promising practical first step toward a deeper research direction.
