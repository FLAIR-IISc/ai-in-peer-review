Reaction to the author feedback:
I cannot agree with point (3). The work of Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) does not assume iid data and allows the “generating structure” to vary (albeit within a limited set of graphical models). While the proposed method here may be more flexible in some respects, it is misleading to claim it is the first to address this problem.

Summary:
The paper presents a hypothesis-testing-based approach to learning graphical models online from time-series data, targeting scenarios in which the data-generating graphical model may change relatively frequently. Performance is demonstrated on both simulated and real datasets.

Quality:
The results appear correct. However, the discussion of related work (Section 2) is somewhat misleading. Typical score-based algorithms for learning Bayesian networks do not assume that all data come from a single generating model; rather, they rely on a scoring function that factorizes into local terms. It is true that commonly studied models assume exchangeability (or iid data), but that is a modeling assumption, not an algorithmic one.

Clarity:
The presentation is mostly fine, but there is room for improvement.
- The learning task is not precisely specified. Is the goal to detect change points (under clearly stated assumptions about their existence and nature), to infer the generating structure for the most recent time point, to maintain a distribution over current structures, or something else? Relatedly, it is unclear whether the paper primarily proposes a new model (assumptions/objectives), a new algorithm (computational approach), or both.
- The paragraph at the end of page 3 is difficult to follow. For example, does the displayed equation for ρ_{r+1} introduce its definition, or was it defined earlier?
- Please end sentences that conclude with displayed equations with a period.
- The font size in Figures 1–4 is too small; doubling it would improve readability. Adding a legend or explicit descriptions of the different colors in Figures 1 and 2 would also help.
- In Section 6, the claim that improved tracking could compensate for additional computational cost is confusing. Whether this is true depends on a domain-specific valuation of tracking accuracy relative to computational cost.

Originality:
The work appears original, but it seems to lack a clear, novel core idea. There may be novelty in the construction of the p-values, though the exposition made it difficult for me to fully follow that aspect.

Significance:
The potential significance, if published, may be to motivate the rapid development of superior—presumably Bayesian—approaches to this problem. That would be better characterized as development rather than research. Overall, the current approach reads as heuristic and targets a learning problem whose formulation is somewhat unclear and that existing machine learning methods arguably should already address.
