- Addresses tracking changes in dependency structure in data streams and proposes LoSST, a selective re-run PC-based SEM structure tracker.  
- LoSST monitors weighted means, correlation matrix, and effective sample size, using Mahalanobis-distance p-values combined via Liptak to compute a trigger ρ and a noisy-OR decision to re-run structure learning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic parameter/structure shifts across variable counts and degrees, compared to batch PC, and applied to a US price-index dataset showing detection of long-term economic shifts.  
- Strength: tackles an important, under-explored problem with clear novelty and practical importance.  
- Strength: proposes a novel, computationally and memory-efficient algorithm that creatively combines statistical methods.  
- Strength: experiments are adequate and systematic, and inclusion of a real-world dataset demonstrates applicability.  
- Strength: provides a thorough literature review and the paper is generally clear and readable.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees or formal analysis; should at least discuss directions for provable results under simplifying assumptions.  
- Weakness: relies on heuristic design and applies statistical tools outside their usual assumptions (e.g., Hotelling’s T² with re-weighted effective sample size; Liptak’s method on dependent sequential p-values) without explicit caveats.  
- Weakness: presentation issues—figures are small/poorly labeled, experimental descriptions can be unclear, and proofs in footnotes hurt readability (better moved to appendix).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of poorer performance on edge-addition after parameter changes and conflates parameter drift with structural change triggers; consider decoupling triggers or a two-tiered approach.  
- Conclusion: a promising, practical first attempt that would be stronger with clearer acknowledgement of heuristics, improved experimental presentation, and added theoretical grounding.
