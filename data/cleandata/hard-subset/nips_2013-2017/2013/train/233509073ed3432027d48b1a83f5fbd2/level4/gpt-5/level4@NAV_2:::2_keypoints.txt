- Paper tackles tracking dependency-structure shifts in streaming linear SEMs and introduces LoSST, an online structure-learning method that maintains weighted means/covariances and triggers PC re-learning using Mahalanobis-based p-values, Liptak pooling, and a noisy-OR update; evaluated on synthetic and U.S. price-index data.
- Pro: Addresses a timely, underexplored problem with room for more methods and theory.
- Pro: LoSST is novel, computationally and memory efficient, and integrates multiple techniques for practicality.
- Pro: Experiments are sensible, explore parameter effects, and include a relevant real-world case study.
- Pro: Related work discussion is thorough and the paper is generally well written.
- Con: Lacks theoretical guarantees under explicit assumptions; should at least flag this as future work.
- Con: Method is heuristic/ad hoc; key design choices (reweighting, effective sample size, relearning triggers) need clearer justification.
- Con: Use of Hotelling’s T^2 with weighted/effective sample sizes and Liptak pooling may violate standard validity conditions and should be explicitly caveated.
- Other: Move lengthy footnote proofs and missing details to a supplement rather than main text.
- Other: Figures are too small, have excessive whitespace, missing axes/labels, and underspecified captions/legends.
- Other: Experimental description has unclear parts (e.g., top paragraph on p.7) that need clarification.
- Other: Explanation for LoSST performing worse on edge additions when only parameters change is unconvincing; recommend decoupling parameter-update and structure-update triggers.
- Overall: Compelling direction and practical first step, but relies on heuristics and needs clearer justification and theoretical framing.
