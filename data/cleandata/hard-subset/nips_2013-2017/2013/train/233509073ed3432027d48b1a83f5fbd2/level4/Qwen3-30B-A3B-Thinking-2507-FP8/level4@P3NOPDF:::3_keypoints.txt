- Addresses important gap: structure learning for SEMs under locally i.i.d. (time-varying) data.  
- Well-motivated: standard algorithms ignore temporal dependencies in many real datasets.  
- Method is conceptually simple and implementable: uses sliding windows with standard conditional independence tests.  
- Theoretical soundness: framework is well-formulated for extending structure learning to locally i.i.d. data.  
- Empirically validated: adaptation of the PC algorithm shows clear improvements over naive stationary methods, especially for gradual model shifts.  
- Manuscript quality: clearly written, logically structured, and makes a valuable contribution to non-stationary graphical model learning.  
- Main contribution: practical framework for SEM adaptation that avoids complex temporal modeling and integrates into existing pipelines.  
- Overgeneralization concern: claim that "any other standard" algorithms would behave similarly is unsupported and should be tempered.  
- Recommendation: rephrase to state results are demonstrated for PC and plausible (but unverified) for other constraint-based methods; leave extension to other algorithms as future work.  
- Scope clarification needed: emphasize early that the work focuses on SEMs and leverages linear-Gaussian structure for sufficient statistics.  
- BN extension limitation: direct application to Bayesian networks is non-trivial due to lack of efficient sufficient statistics in non-linear/discrete settings—this should be highlighted earlier.  
- Overall verdict: technical content strong, authors addressed prior concerns, minor clarifications recommended, and the paper is recommended for acceptance.
