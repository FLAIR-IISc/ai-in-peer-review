- Problem: Learns graphical model structure from non-stationary data where generative process can change over time.  
- Contribution: Proposes LoSST, an online heuristic that tracks weighted sufficient statistics to adaptively learn structure.  
- Detection mechanism: Weights datapoints by Mahalanobis “surprise,” computes p-values via Hotelling’s T^2, and pools them with Liptak’s method to form statistic ρ.  
- Update rule: Probabilistically triggers full structure relearning using a batch PC algorithm when ρ indicates poor fit.  
- Theoretical discussion: Analyzes the trade-off between convergence to a stable structure and diligence in detecting changes, but lacks formal guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough synthetic experiments show LoSST matches batch PC in stable periods and outperforms it after change points.  
- Real-world case study: Application to U.S. price index data demonstrates detection of known macroeconomic shifts.  
- Strength — significance: Addresses an important, under-explored, practically relevant problem in online structure learning.  
- Strength — methodology: Computationally efficient, end-to-end design using weighted statistics and a principled heuristic for updates.  
- Strength — clarity: Generally well-written with good related-work context.  
- Weakness — theoretical guarantees: No rigorous theoretical guarantees; stronger analysis under simplified assumptions is needed.  
- Weakness — heuristic assumptions: Use of Hotelling’s T^2 with weighted “effective” sample size and Liptak’s pooled p-values may violate their assumptions and needs justification or clearer caveats.  
- Weakness — presentation: Proof sketches in footnotes, small figures, and limited experimental detail hinder clarity and reproducibility.  
- Weakness — result analysis: Insufficient explanation for worse edge-removal errors after parameter-only changes; needs deeper analysis of effective sample size and constraint tests.  
- Weakness — update coupling: Always relearning full structure may be inefficient; decoupling parametric from structural updates is a promising future direction.
