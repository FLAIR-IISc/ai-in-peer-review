- Problem: Detecting changes in dependency structure over data streams, focusing on efficient solutions for SEMs.  
- Method: Proposes LoSST, a streaming algorithm that uses batch PC for structure learning and streaming statistics to trigger relearning.  
- Tracked statistics: Maintains deviations from weighted means, the correlation matrix, and a weighted sample size.  
- Reweighting: New points reweighted by Mahalanobis distance; MD p-values estimated via Hotelling’s T2 using an effective sample size.  
- Aggregation: MD p-values combined with Lipták’s weighted pooling to form an overall significance score ρ.  
- Triggering: Uses a noisy-OR with the previous signal to decide when to relearn structure.  
- Experiments: Evaluation on synthetic datasets with parameter/structure changes and on a real US price-index dataset; shows detection of known economic shifts.  
- Pro: Tackles a timely, underexplored problem with room for practical and theoretical advances.  
- Pro: Algorithm is novel, practical, efficient, and integrates multiple ideas creatively.  
- Pro: Experimental evaluation is adequate, exploring parameters and including a real-world dataset.  
- Pro: Related work is thorough and paper is clearly written.  
- Con: Lacks theoretical guarantees or formal analysis; needs stated guarantees or explicit future work.  
- Con: Heavy reliance on heuristics; key design choices (reweighting, effective sample size, triggers) need clearer justification.  
- Con: Statistical tools may be used beyond assumptions—Hotelling’s T2 with reweighting and Lipták’s independence assumption may be invalid here; must be explicitly acknowledged.  
- Other: Move proof sketches from footnotes to supplement; improve figure size/labels/captions and clarify experimental descriptions.  
- Other: Explanation for LoSST’s poorer edge-addition performance is unconvincing; consider decoupling parameter updates from structure updates.
