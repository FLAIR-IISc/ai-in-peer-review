- Addresses detecting changes in dependency structure in data streams — an interesting, underexplored problem.  
- Proposes LoSST, an efficient method for simpler SEMs that leverages the PC algorithm for structure learning.  
- Tracks statistics from the stream: distance from weighted mean, correlation matrix, and weighted sample size with a "surprisingness" weighting scheme.  
- Computes Mahalanobis distances and p-values via Hotelling’s T2, then pools p-values using Liptak’s weighted method to form an overall significance statistic.  
- Uses a noisy-OR decision rule on the pooled significance to trigger new structure learning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data with parameter/structure changes and a brief real-world US price index case study.  
- Empirical results: detects known changes and trends while ignoring short-lived/noisy changes.  
- Strengths: tackles a compelling problem, presents a novel and practical algorithm, adequate experiments, good related-work review, and clear writing.  
- Major weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis; heavily heuristic.  
- Methodological concern: application of Hotelling’s T2 and Liptak pooling outside their standard theoretical contexts; needs explicit caveats.  
- Suggestions: add proofs/details in supplementary material, improve figure quality and descriptions, and clarify edge-addition performance explanation.  
- Future direction: decouple parameter updates from structure updates and develop theoretical foundations.  
- Overall verdict: promising heuristic approach that merits further exploration and formal analysis.
