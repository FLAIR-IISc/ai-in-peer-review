- Proposes LoSST, an online heuristic that tracks SEM sufficient statistics (means, covariance) with dynamic weighting based on Mahalanobis distance to learn graphical structure under locally i.i.d. non-stationarity.

- Addresses a significant practical gap between stationary structure learning and online/changepoint methods by enabling adaptive structure learning.

- Elegant, original update scheme using a pooled p-value of Mahalanobis distances and a thoughtful theoretical discussion of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off.

- Manuscript is very clear, well-organized, and explains algorithm and parameters in sufficient detail for understanding and replication.

- Strong empirical validation: LoSST matches batch PC in stationary settings and substantially outperforms it under structural and parametric changes.

- Overstated claim that other standard structure-learning algorithms would behave like PC; this should be softened or better justified since sensitivity to estimation errors can vary.

- Extension to other model classes (e.g., discrete Bayesian networks) is non-trivial; the paper should explicitly frame its primary focus on SEMs earlier to manage generalizability expectations.

- Overall: high-quality, novel, and empirically supported work with minor framing/claim clarifications needed.
