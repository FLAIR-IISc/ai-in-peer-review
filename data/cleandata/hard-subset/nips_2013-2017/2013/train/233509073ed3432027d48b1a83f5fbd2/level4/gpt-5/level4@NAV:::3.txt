This manuscript proposes a principled approach to enabling structure learning for SEMs with data that are only locally i.i.d., meaning the data may be generated by different underlying models over time. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow, and it addresses an important problem—learning from time-dependent data—which many widely used structure-learning algorithms have overlooked. The techniques for extending learning to time-dependent data are simple yet effective, as demonstrated by experiments with the PC method.

There are two suggestions that could clarify the paper’s contributions. First, in the experiments, it is stated that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly, ...” I find this claim too strong. Differences among structure-learning methods can be substantial. It would be better either to rephrase, making clear that the experiments concern the PC method for time-independent data and its adaptation for time-dependent data (and then speculate that others might behave similarly), or to retain the claim and provide detailed justification for why it should hold.

Second, the conclusions discuss that applying the same ideas to Bayesian network structure learning may not be straightforward due to difficulties in maintaining sufficient statistics. Even if this issue is not central to the proposal here, it indicates that using the same learning procedure for Bayesian networks and other models may require more than minor tweaks. Thus, the paper would benefit from disclosing this upfront—framing the goal more directly around SEMs while noting that extensions to other models may be possible (but not necessarily straightforward). In short, the paper could be sharper about its scope.

Overall, this is a well-written paper on learning the graph structure for SEMs under the assumption that data are only locally i.i.d. The formulation is easy to understand and implement, and the experimental results are promising, even though they focus on a comparison using the PC method. I acknowledge having read the authors’ feedback.
