- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical structure, addressing unrealistic global-stationarity assumptions.  
- Technically sound with simulations: matches batch learning under global stationarity and outperforms when only locally stationary.  
- Correctly tracks changes in both structure and parameters of recursive SEMs.  
- Related work is mischaracterized: score-based methods do not inherently assume a single generating model; i.i.d. assumptions pertain to modeling choices, not the algorithms.  
- Claim that Siracusa and Fisher (2009) assumes i.i.d. data is inaccurate; their method allows varying structure but operates in batch mode.  
- Theoretical analysis of the convergence vs. diligence tradeoff is solid, though proof sketches lack completeness.  
- Learning task is ambiguously stated (change points vs most recent structure vs other goals).  
- Blurs distinction between proposing a new model (assumptions/goals) and proposing a new algorithm (computational method).  
- Equation for ρ_{r+1} is confusing without context and the paper omits periods after displayed equations.  
- Figures suffer from small font sizes and unlabeled color coding, hindering interpretation.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking compensates for computational costs is speculative without domain-specific valuation.  
- Novel elements include using Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values for weighting in online structure learning.  
- Overall technical novelty is limited: LoSST is largely a heuristic extension rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Claims of being the first to address time-varying structure are misleading given prior batch-mode work.  
- Significance is practical (enables online learning when batch is infeasible) but overstated regarding leading to superior Bayesian methods.  
- Real-world application to US price index volatility is interesting but narrow in scope.  
- Strengths: addresses an important problem, strong simulations, clear convergence/diligence analysis, practical application, efficient sufficient-statistics tracking.  
- Weaknesses: misrepresents prior work, ambiguous objectives, limited novelty, incomplete proofs, poorly presented figures, and overstated significance.  
- Overall recommendation: reject for NIPS; better fit for a more application-focused venue.
