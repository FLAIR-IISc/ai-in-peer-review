- Addresses an important gap: online tracking of time-varying graphical model structure rather than assuming global stationarity.  
- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic incremental algorithm that detects and adapts to changes in structure and parameters.  
- Properly situates contribution against batch methods (PC) and existing online/CPD approaches, arguing local stationarity is realistic.  
- Strong motivation from real-world streaming settings where batch processing is infeasible.  
- Algorithmic novelty: combines Mahalanobis novelty detection, Liptak p-value pooling, and a probabilistic relearning mechanism.  
- Comprehensive synthetic experiments show LoSST matches batch performance in stable regimes and outperforms during structural changes.  
- Real-world application (US price index volatility) convincingly detects meaningful economic shifts while ignoring short-term noise.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and gives useful implementation details (weighted sufficient statistics, relearning decision rules).  
- Major weakness: no theoretical guarantees or formal analysis of convergence/behavior under assumptions.  
- Methodological concern: use of Hotelling’s T² with weighted data and Liptak pooling in unfamiliar contexts needs explicit acknowledgment and discussion of validity.  
- Insufficient explanation for LoSST’s higher edge-removal errors when only parameters change; suggests decoupling parameter updates from structure updates.  
- Experimental reproducibility issues: insufficient dataset-generation detail and poorly presented figures (small, unlabeled).  
- Overall: promising and practically relevant work but requires revisions on theory, statistical assumptions, experimental detail, and presentation for strong acceptance.
