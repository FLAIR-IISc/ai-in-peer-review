This paper introduces a coherent approach to enable structure learning for SEMs when data are only locally i.i.d., i.e., the generating process may switch models over time. The manuscript is clear and easy to follow, and it addresses an important problem—learning from time-dependent data—that many widely used structure-learning algorithms overlook. The mechanisms used to accommodate temporal variation are straightforward yet, based on the evaluation with PC, appear effective.

I have two suggestions to clarify the contribution. First, the experiments state that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly...” This claim seems too strong. Differences across structure-learning methods can be substantial. Either temper the statement—making clear that the experiments evaluate the PC method in the time-independent setting and with an adaptation for time-dependent data (and then hypothesize that other methods might behave similarly)—or retain the claim but provide a detailed rationale for why it should hold.

Second, the conclusion notes that applying the same ideas to Bayesian networks may not be straightforward due to challenges in maintaining sufficient statistics. Although this issue is not central to the proposed method, it suggests that extending the procedure to Bayes nets and other models could require more than minor adjustments. The paper would benefit from signaling this earlier, presenting the scope more explicitly as targeting SEMs while noting that extensions may be possible but are not necessarily immediate. In short, sharpening the stated scope from the outset would strengthen the paper.

Overall, this is a well-written paper on learning SEM graph structure under the assumption of locally i.i.d. data. The formulation is easy to understand and implement, and the empirical results are promising, even though they focus on a comparison using the PC method. I acknowledge having read the authors’ feedback.
