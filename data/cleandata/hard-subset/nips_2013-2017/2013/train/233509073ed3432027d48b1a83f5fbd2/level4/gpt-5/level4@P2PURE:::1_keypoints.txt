- Reviewer disputes authors' claim of novelty: Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid data with changing generating structures, so claiming to be first is misleading.
- Paper proposes an online hypothesis-testing method to learn graphical models from time-series where the generating model may change frequently; evaluated on simulated and real data.
- Results appear correct, but related-work discussion is misleading: score-based BN algorithms assume decomposable scores, not necessarily iid data—exchangeability is a model assumption, not an algorithmic one.
- Presentation is adequate but unclear about the precise learning task (change-point detection vs. recent-structure inference vs. something else).
- Unclear whether the contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.
- Specific clarity issues: paragraph at end of page 3 and the definition of ρ_{r+1} are hard to follow; punctuation after displayed equations is inconsistent.
- Figures need readability improvements: increase font size in Figures 1–4 and add legends for colors in Figures 1 and 2.
- The claim that improved tracking can justify added computational cost is confusing and overly general—it depends on domain-specific trade-offs.
- Originality is questionable: some novelty may exist (e.g., p-value construction), but no clearly novel idea is evident and the p-value part is hard to follow.
- Significance is limited: work seems heuristic and addresses an ill-defined problem; likely impact is to prompt further methodological development rather than deliver a definitive solution.
