- Introduces LoSST, a novel online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical structures under local stationarity.  
- Addresses an important gap: most existing methods assume fixed structure and operate in batch mode.  
- Algorithm can detect structural changes and adapt in real time while matching batch performance during stable periods.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough literature review distinguishing related work.  
- Claims to provide the first general graphical model structure learning algorithm in an online setting.  
- Technical development is sound: describes online tracking of sufficient statistics and a parameterized balance between convergence and diligence.  
- Experiments are comprehensive: three simulation scenarios compare LoSST to the PC batch algorithm.  
- Results show LoSST equals batch methods during stability and outperforms them after structural changes.  
- Real-world case study on US price index volatility demonstrates practical utility and detects the Great Moderation.  
- Elegant connection between Mahalanobis distances, pooled p-values, and the data-point weighting scheme.  
- Weakness: unsubstantiated claim that other standard structure learning algorithms would perform similarly to PC.  
- Weakness: scope unclear—method is focused on SEMs (continuous, linear) and extensions to other graphical models (e.g., Bayesian networks) are nontrivial and should be emphasized earlier.  
- Convergence vs. diligence discussion is insightful but mathematically dense for some readers.  
- Limited economic interpretation of detected real-world structural changes; needs more context.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to temper generalization claims and clarify scope.
