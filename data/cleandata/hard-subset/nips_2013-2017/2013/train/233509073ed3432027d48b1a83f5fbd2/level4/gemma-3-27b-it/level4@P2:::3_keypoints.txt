- Proposes LoSST, a novel method for SEM structure learning on non-stationary (locally i.i.d.) time-dependent data.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.
- Tackles an important, under-explored problem: structure learning from time-dependent/online data.
- LoSST extends existing methods in a simple, effective way and is demonstrated using the PC algorithm.
- Core idea: dynamically weight data points by consistency with the current model to adapt to changing generative processes.
- Algorithm is particularly useful in online settings where batch processing is impractical.
- Claim that “other standard structure learning algorithms would perform similarly” is overly strong and needs nuance or justification.
- Experiments should be framed as comparisons of PC in time-independent vs. time-dependent settings, or provide reasons why other methods would behave similarly.
- Recommend clarifying scope by explicitly targeting SEMs and acknowledging non-trivial challenges in extending to Bayesian networks (e.g., tracking sufficient statistics for discrete variables).
- Overall assessment: LoSST is conceptually straightforward, appears effective in simulations, and is easy to implement.
- Pros: addresses important problem, clear algorithm, convincing experiments, simple implementation, well-written.
- Cons: insufficient justification for generalizability to other algorithms; scope and extension limitations need clearer articulation.
