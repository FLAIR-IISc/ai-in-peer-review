- Reviewer disputes claim of novelty: Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid and varying generating structures, so claiming “first” is misleading.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based approach for online learning of graphical models from time series where the generating model may change frequently.  
- Method evaluated on both simulated and real data; reviewer finds the results appear correct.  
- Related-work discussion is misleading: score-based Bayesian network algorithms do not require a single generating model and assume factorizing scores, not necessarily iid data.  
- Main clarity issue: the learning task is ambiguously defined—change-point detection, recent-structure inference, or something else is not clearly specified.  
- Unclear whether the contribution is a new model (assumptions/goals), a new algorithm (computational method), or both.  
- Specific presentation problems: paragraph at end of page 3 hard to follow; equation for ρ_{r+1} ambiguous; omission of periods after displayed equations.  
- Figures need fixes: font sizes (Figures 1–4) are too small and should be doubled; color legends/descriptions are missing for Figures 1 and 2.  
- Section 6 claim confusing: suggestion that improved tracking could justify increased computational cost lacks domain-specific evaluation and is not generally justified.  
- Originality: work is somewhat original but lacks substantial innovation or a truly novel idea.  
- Construction of p-values may be a novel contribution, but the reviewer could not follow the details.  
- Significance: could stimulate development of better (likely Bayesian) methods, but feels like development/heuristic work on an unclearly defined problem that existing ML techniques could likely handle.
