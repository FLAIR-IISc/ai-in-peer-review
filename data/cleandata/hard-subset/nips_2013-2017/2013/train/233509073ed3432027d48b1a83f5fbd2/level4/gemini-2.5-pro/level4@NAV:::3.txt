This manuscript introduces a methodical approach to adapt structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) to datasets that are only locally independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.). This addresses the scenario where the data-generating process may shift over time. The paper is clearly written, accessible, and tackles the important problem of learning from time-dependent data, an issue often overlooked by many conventional structure learning algorithms. The techniques proposed to extend learning to such data are elegant yet appear effective, based on the experimental results with the PC algorithm.

Two suggestions could further clarify the paper's contributions. Firstly, the experiments section claims that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly...". This assertion may be too strong, as the performance differences between structure learning methods can be considerable. It would be better to moderate this statement, perhaps by focusing on the demonstrated value of the adaptation for the PC method—a very relevant finding—and then speculating that similar results might be achievable with other methods. Alternatively, to retain the original assertion, a more detailed justification for this expected generalization would be required.

Secondly, the conclusion discusses that applying these same concepts to learn Bayesian network structures might not be a simple task, citing difficulties with maintaining sufficient statistics. While this problem is not central to the paper's proposal, it implies that extending this learning procedure to other models may require non-trivial modifications. Consequently, the paper would benefit from disclosing this limitation earlier on. We suggest framing the paper's scope more directly around SEMs from the beginning, while still noting that extensions to other models are possible but not necessarily straightforward. In short, the paper could be sharper in defining its scope.

To conclude, this is a well-written paper on learning the graphical structure for SEMs under the assumption of locally i.i.d. data. The formulation is simple to understand and implement, and the experimental results look promising, even if they are focused on a comparison using the PC method.
