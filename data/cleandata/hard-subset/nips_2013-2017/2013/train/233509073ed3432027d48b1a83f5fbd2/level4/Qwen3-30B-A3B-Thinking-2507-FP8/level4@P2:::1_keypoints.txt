- Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical model structure in locally stationary environments.  
- Focuses on estimating locally stationary sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size) with weighted updates.  
- Uses Mahalanobis-distance-based weights and pooled p-values (Liptak) to update statistics online.  
- Demonstrates comparable performance to batch methods in stable periods and substantially better tracking when structure changes, validated in simulations and U.S. price index data.  
- Technically sound with well-designed experiments that evaluate different conditions appropriately.  
- Provides solid theoretical justification for the weighted statistics and the convergence/diligence trade-off.  
- Related work section misrepresents score-based structure learning by claiming single-model assumptions; needs clarification about exchangeability vs. local factorization.  
- Real-world application (price index volatility) is well-motivated and shows practical utility.  
- Core learning task is ambiguous (change-point detection vs. current-structure inference vs. both), which affects evaluation and interpretation.  
- Specific technical exposition problems: unclear definition of ρr+1, need to state whether it’s novel or prior work.  
- Presentation issues: figures need larger fonts and color descriptions; some prose (page 3 paragraph) is hard to follow.  
- Claim of being the “first” to address time-varying graphical structure is misleading—Siracusa & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) considered time-indexed graphs and should be acknowledged.  
- Construction of pooled p-value via Liptak’s method is not novel, and the algorithm’s heuristic weight-update lacks substantial theoretical innovation.  
- Paper overstates broader theoretical impact (e.g., leading to superior Bayesian methods); main significance is practical utility for real-time, non-stationary settings.  
- Authors’ rebuttal regarding Siracusa & Fisher is insufficient; relationship to that prior work must be clarified.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—useful practical contribution—but require revisions to related work, precise task definition, and improved clarity/presentation.
