- Paper introduces a hypothesis-testing framework for online learning of graphical models with frequently changing structures, evaluated on simulations and real datasets.  
- Reported results appear accurate.  
- Related-work section is misleading: score-based BN algorithms assume score factorization into local terms, not necessarily a single generative model.  
- Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) does not require iid data and can handle varying generating structures, so this is not the first work addressing the problem.  
- Core task is ambiguous: unclear if goal is change-point detection, inferring the most recent structure, or something else.  
- Unclear whether contribution is a new model formulation (assumptions/goals), a new algorithm, or both.  
- Paragraph at end of page 3 is hard to parse; definition/use of ρ_{r+1} is unclear and equation punctuation is inconsistent.  
- Figures 1–4 suffer from small font and lack color descriptions, reducing readability.  
- Claim in Section 6 that improved tracking can justify higher computation is domain-dependent and not generally established.  
- Originality is incremental; possible novelty in p-value construction but details are hard to follow.  
- Significance is limited: could spur more advanced (likely Bayesian) methods but represents a developmental rather than fundamental contribution.  
- Approach reads as a heuristic that established ML techniques might already address and lacks a clearly defined objective.
