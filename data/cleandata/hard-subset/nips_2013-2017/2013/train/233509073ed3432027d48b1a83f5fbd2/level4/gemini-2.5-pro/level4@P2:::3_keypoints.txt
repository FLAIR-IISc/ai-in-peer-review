- Introduces LoSST, an online heuristic that tracks weighted sufficient statistics to learn graphical model structure from locally stationary data.  
- Addresses the practical problem that most structure learning assumes global stationarity and adapts via Mahalanobis-distance-based weighting and pooled p-values.  
- Demonstrates comparable performance to PC in stationary settings and superior performance when structure or parameters change.  
- Strength: tackles an important, realistic problem with a novel, coherent approach relevant to many applications.  
- Strength: exceptionally clear, well-motivated presentation with insightful discussion (convergence vs. diligence) and a logical algorithm description.  
- Strength: technically sound method—weighted sufficient statistics balance stability and adaptability—and experiments convincingly show tracking ability with little cost in stable environments.  
- Weakness: overstates generality by claiming other structure learning algorithms would behave similarly; this is unsubstantiated and should be rephrased or removed.  
- Weakness: limited scope—method is developed for continuous-variable SEMs where covariance suffices; extending to discrete/Bayesian networks is non-trivial and should be made explicit earlier.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, solid contribution; accept after clarifying scope and tempering broad applicability claims.
