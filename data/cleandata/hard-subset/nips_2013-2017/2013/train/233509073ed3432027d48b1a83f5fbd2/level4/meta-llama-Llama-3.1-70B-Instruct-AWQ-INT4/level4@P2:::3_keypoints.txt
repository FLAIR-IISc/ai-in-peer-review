- Proposes a systematic method (LoSST) for structure learning in SEMs under locally i.i.d. (time-dependent) data.
- Paper is well written and easy to follow.
- Addresses the important, often-neglected problem of learning from time-dependent data.
- Techniques to handle time-dependence are simple and effective, validated with PC-method experiments.
- Presents a novel, real-time approach to track changes in graphical model structure/parameters and situates it relative to prior work.
- Discusses why existing online learning and changepoint-detection methods are insufficient for this task.
- LoSST algorithm is clearly described, including how it works and its convergence vs. diligence trade-offs.
- Experiments show LoSST matches batch learning under global stationarity and outperforms it under local stationarity.
- Suggestion: the claim that other standard structure-learning algorithms would perform similarly is too strong—rephrase or provide justification.
- Suggestion: clarify scope by emphasizing focus on SEMs and note that extending to Bayesian networks is nontrivial due to sufficient-statistics challenges.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10; overall recommendation—accept with revisions.
