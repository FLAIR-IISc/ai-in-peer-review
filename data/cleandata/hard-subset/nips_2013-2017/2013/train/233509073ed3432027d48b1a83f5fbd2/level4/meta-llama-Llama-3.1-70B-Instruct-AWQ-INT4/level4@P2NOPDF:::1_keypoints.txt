- Disagrees with authors’ novelty claim: Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) also handles non-iid data and varying graphical structures.
- Proposes a hypothesis-testing-based online method for learning frequently changing graphical models, evaluated on simulated and real data.
- Results seem correct, but Section 2 mischaracterizes related work and is misleading about assumptions.
- Score-based Bayesian network methods typically assume factorized scoring, not a single generating model; exchangeability is a model assumption, not an algorithmic one.
- Major clarity issue: unclear learning objective—detect change points, infer last-structure, or something else—and whether a new model or algorithm is proposed.
- Specific presentation problems: the paragraph and the equation for ρ_{r+1} on page 3 are hard to follow and definition is unclear.
- Formatting issues: missing periods after displayed equations, small font in Figures 1–4, and no color legend for Figures 1–2.
- Construction of p-values might be novel but is not well explained; overall originality is limited.
- Significance is modest: could lead to better (possibly Bayesian) methods, but current approach is heuristic and addresses a loosely defined problem.
- Claim that improved tracking justifies extra computation is unclear and depends on domain-specific trade-offs.
