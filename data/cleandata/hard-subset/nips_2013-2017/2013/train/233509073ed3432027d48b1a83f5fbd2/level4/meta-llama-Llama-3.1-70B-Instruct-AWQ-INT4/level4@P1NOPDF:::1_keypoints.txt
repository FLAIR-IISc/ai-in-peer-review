- Disagreement with authors’ claim of novelty: prior work (Siracusa III & Fisher, AISTATS 2009) already handles non-iid and changing generating structure within a limited set of graphical models.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based online method to learn graphical models from time series when the generating structure may change frequently.  
- Empirical results (simulated and real data) appear to be correct.  
- Related work section is misleading: score-based Bayesian network algorithms do not necessarily assume a single generating model; they assume factorable local scoring, and iid assumptions pertain to models, not algorithms.  
- The actual learning task is not clearly defined: change-point detection vs. inferring the current generating structure vs. something else.  
- It is unclear whether the contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- Specific exposition issues: the paragraph around the equation for ρ_{r+1} is hard to follow and its definition/status is unclear.  
- Presentation problems: small font in Figures 1–4 and missing color legends for Figures 1–2.  
- Section 6’s claim that improved tracking can justify extra computational cost is confusing and depends on domain-specific trade-offs.  
- Originality is limited: the approach seems heuristic and lacks a clear novel idea; the p-value construction might be innovative but is not well explained.  
- Significance is modest: method could motivate future (Bayesian) developments but currently reads as a heuristic application of existing machine-learning tools to an ill-defined problem.
