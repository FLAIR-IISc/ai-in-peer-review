- Reviewer disagrees with the novelty claim: Siracusa III & Fisher (AISTATS 2009) already handle non-iid data and varying generating structures.  
- Paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based online method to learn graphical models from time series with frequently changing generating graphs, evaluated on simulated and real data.  
- Results appear correct, but Section 2’s related-work discussion is misleading.  
- Score-based Bayesian network algorithms do not inherently assume a single generating model; exchangeability is a modeling assumption, not an algorithmic constraint.  
- Learning task is not precisely specified (change-point detection, current-structure inference, maintaining a distribution over structures, or something else).  
- It is unclear whether the paper’s main contribution is a new model, a new algorithm, or both.  
- Exposition problems: the paragraph at the end of page 3 is hard to follow and the definition/introduction of ρ_{r+1} is unclear.  
- Formatting and clarity issues: sentences ending with displayed equations need periods; Figures 1–4 have too-small font and lack legends or color descriptions.  
- Section 6’s claim that better tracking could justify extra computation is confusing and domain-dependent.  
- Originality is plausible but the paper lacks a clear novel core idea; the p-value construction may be novel but is poorly explained.  
- Significance is limited: approach reads as heuristic, problem formulation is unclear, and existing ML methods might already address the task; potential value is motivating further Bayesian development.
