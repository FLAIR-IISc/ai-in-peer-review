This manuscript proposes a systematic approach to structure learning for SEMs when the data are only locally i.i.d.—that is, when the underlying generating model may change over time. The paper is well written and easy to follow. It tackles an important problem—learning from time-dependent data—that many widely used structure-learning algorithms have largely neglected. The techniques for extending learning to time-dependent data are simple yet, based on the experiments with the PC algorithm, effective.

I have two suggestions to clarify the contributions:
1) In the experiments, the paper states that “any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly.” This claim seems too strong; performance differences among structure-learning methods can be substantial. It would be better either to (a) rephrase to make clear that the experiments evaluate the PC method in the time-independent setting and its adaptation for time-dependent data—then present it as a conjecture that other methods might behave similarly—or (b) retain the assertion but provide a detailed justification for why it should hold.
2) The conclusions note that applying the same ideas to learning the structure of Bayesian networks may not be straightforward because of difficulties in maintaining sufficient statistics. Although this issue is not central to the present proposal, it indicates that using the same learning procedure for Bayesian networks and other models may require more than a few simple tricks. The paper would benefit from stating this upfront, framing the contribution explicitly around SEMs while noting that extensions to other models may be possible but are not necessarily straightforward. In short, the scope could be stated more sharply.

Overall, this is a well-written paper on learning the graph structure for SEMs under the assumption that data are only locally i.i.d. The formulation is simple to understand and implement, and the experimental results are promising, even though they focus on comparisons using only the PC method. I acknowledge having read the authors’ feedback.
