- Tackles an important, underexplored problem of tracking dependency-structure changes in data streams with practical relevance.  
- Introduces LoSST, a novel pragmatic algorithm combining the PC algorithm for structure learning with streamed statistics to trigger re-learning.  
- Uses a “surprisingness” weighting scheme based on Mahalanobis distance, Hotelling’s T^2 p-values, and Liptak’s weighted p-value pooling to decide when to re-learn.  
- Experimental evaluation includes synthetic datasets varying variables and degrees and a real-world US price-index case study showing detection of known economic shifts.  
- Provides a comprehensive related-work review and is generally well written.  
- Major limitation: lacks theoretical guarantees; should offer limited guarantees under simplifying assumptions and mark this as future work.  
- Heavy reliance on heuristics with insufficient justification for re-weighting, effective sample size choices, and re-learning triggers.  
- Potential misuse of statistical tools: applying Hotelling’s T^2 to re-weighted/incomplete samples and Liptak’s pooling where test independence may not hold, calling p-value validity into question.  
- Explanation for LoSST’s poor performance on edge additions during parameter-only changes is unconvincing.  
- Suggestion: decouple parameter updates from structural updates to potentially improve performance.  
- Presentation issues: excessive footnotes for proofs (use supplementary material) and poorly formatted figures with unclear captions and experiment descriptions.
