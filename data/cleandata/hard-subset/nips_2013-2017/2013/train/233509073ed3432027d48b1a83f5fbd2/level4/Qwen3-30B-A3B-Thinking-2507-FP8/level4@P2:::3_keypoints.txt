- Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical structures under a locally stationary assumption.  
- Fills a literature gap: enables online structure learning where most methods assume global stationarity or require batch processing.  
- Differentiates from prior work (e.g., TD(0), changepoint methods, previous time-varying graph approaches) by operating online on structure.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical foundations, including convergence and diligence properties under different parameter settings.  
- Comprehensive simulations show LoSST matches batch-mode when stationary and outperforms when only locally stationary.  
- Real-world case study on US price index volatility demonstrates practical significance and applicability.  
- Notes limitations: degrades with very rapid structural changes or random-order mixtures of multiple structures.  
- Clarity is excellent: problem statement, algorithm, and experiments are well-organized; equations and figures are effective.  
- Originality is substantial: real-time tracking of structural changes rather than batch or single-edge-change approaches.  
- High significance for domains with streaming, evolving relationships (e.g., economics, finance).  
- Reproducibility: algorithm presented with sufficient detail; experiments are thorough and well-designed.  
- First suggested improvement: temper or justify the claim that other standard structure-learning algorithms would perform similarly to PC.  
- Second suggested improvement: explicitly state in the introduction that the paper focuses on SEMs and that extensions to other graphical models require additional work.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, technically sound contribution; recommend acceptance with minor clarifying revisions.
