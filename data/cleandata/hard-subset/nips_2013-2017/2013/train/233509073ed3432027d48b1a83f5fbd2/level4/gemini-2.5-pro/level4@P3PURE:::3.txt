This manuscript presents a systematic approach to structure learning for Structural Equation Models (SEMs) using datasets that are only locally i.i.d., meaning the data-generating model may change over time. The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and addresses the important and often-neglected problem of learning from time-dependent data. The techniques proposed to extend structure learning to this setting are simple yet effective, as demonstrated by the experiments with the PC algorithm.

I have two suggestions that could further clarify the paper's contributions.

First, the experimental section asserts that "any other standard graphical model structure learning algorithms would perform similarly...". This claim may be too strong, as performance differences between structure learning algorithms can be considerable. The authors could either rephrase this statement to more precisely frame the experiments as a comparison between the standard PC algorithm and its proposed time-dependent adaptation—while speculating that similar improvements might be seen with other methods—or they could retain the assertion but provide a detailed justification for why this generalization is expected to hold.

Second, the conclusion discusses the difficulty of applying these ideas to learn the structure of Bayesian networks due to challenges in maintaining sufficient statistics. While this issue is not central to the paper's proposal for SEMs, it highlights that extending the method to other model classes is not trivial. Therefore, the paper would benefit from being more explicit about its scope from the outset. Framing the contribution more directly as a method for SEMs, while noting that extensions to other models may be possible but are not necessarily straightforward, would sharpen the focus of the manuscript.

In summary, this is a well-written paper on learning the graphical structure of SEMs under the assumption of locally i.i.d. data. The proposed formulation is clear and straightforward to implement, and the experimental results are promising, even though they focus on a comparison using only the PC method.

I acknowledge to have read the feedback from the authors.
