- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic for online learning of graphical model structure in non-stationary settings.  
- Demonstrated via simulations and US price index data; results appear technically sound.  
- Related-work discussion mischaracterizes score-based Bayesian network methods (they assume factorized scores, not a single generating model).  
- Claiming to be first to handle non-i.i.d. structure learning is incorrect; Siracusa and Fisher III (AISTATS 2009) addressed this.  
- Core learning task is unclear: change-point detection, recent-structure inference, or something else?  
- Unclear distinction between proposing a new model (assumptions/goals) and proposing a new algorithm (computational method).  
- Paragraph at end of page 3 (ρ_{r+1} and its prior) is hard to follow and needs clarification.  
- Minor style issues: inconsistent punctuation for displayed equations.  
- Figures 1–4 need larger fonts and color legends for readability.  
- Section 6’s discussion on trading tracking accuracy vs. computational cost is context-dependent and under-justified.  
- Approach shows some originality in tracking time-varying structures, but core ideas are not a substantial innovation.  
- Possible novelty in the construction of p-values for change detection, but this requires more clarification.  
- Potential significance: could stimulate more advanced/Bayesian methods for online graphical model learning.  
- Method reads as engineering/heuristic rather than fundamental research; problem formulation is somewhat ill-defined.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, shows promising empirical performance, and could spur further research.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overstated novelty, poor positioning within literature, heuristic nature with no theoretical guarantees, and clarity issues.  
- Overall recommendation: reject due to clarity problems, overstated originality, and lack of theoretical grounding; improvements in problem definition and literature placement could help.
