Reaction to the author feedback: 
I disagree with point (3) of the author feedback. The work of Siracusa III and Fisher (AISTATS 2009) does not assume iid data and allows the generating structure to vary within a small set of graphical models. Although the proposed method is more flexible, it is misleading to claim that it is the first to address this problem.

The paper proposes a hypothesis-testing-based approach for online learning of graphical models from time series data. This method is designed for scenarios where the data-generating graphical model changes frequently. The authors demonstrate the method's performance using both simulated and real data.

Quality 
The results appear to be correct. However, the description of related work in Section 2 is somewhat misleading. Typical score-based algorithms for learning Bayesian networks do not assume a single generating model for the data; instead, they assume the scoring function can be factorized into local terms. While many models assume exchangeability or iid data, this assumption pertains to the model, not the algorithm.

Clarity 
The paper's presentation is mostly clear, but there is room for improvement. The primary issue is that the actual learning task is unclear: is it inferring change points under specific assumptions, inferring the generating structure of the last observed data point, or something else? Additionally, it is difficult to determine whether the work proposes a new model (formulations of assumptions and learning goals) or a new algorithm (computational means to achieve learning goals), or both.

The paragraph at the end of page 3 is challenging to follow, particularly the equation for \rho_{r+1}. It is unclear whether this equation defines \rho_{r+1} or if the definition was provided earlier. The absence of periods at the end of sentences with displayed equations is also noticeable. Furthermore, the font size in Figures 1-4 could be increased for better readability. Adding descriptions for the different colors in Figures 1 and 2 would also be beneficial. In Section 6, the idea that improved tracking could compensate for the additional computational cost is confusing, as this seems to depend on domain-specific valuations of tracking accuracy versus computational cost.

Originality 
The work appears to be original, but it lacks real innovation or novel ideas. The construction of p-values might be a unique aspect, but it is not entirely clear.

Significance 
The potential significance of this work lies in its potential to lead to the development of superior, presumably Bayesian, methods for this problem. However, this should be viewed as a development rather than research. The approach appears heuristic and addresses a somewhat unclearly defined learning problem that existing machine learning machinery should already be able to solve.
