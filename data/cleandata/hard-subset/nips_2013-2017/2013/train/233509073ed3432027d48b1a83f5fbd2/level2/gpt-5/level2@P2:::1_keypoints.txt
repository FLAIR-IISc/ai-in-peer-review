- Proposes LoSST: an online heuristic for tracking time-varying DAGs using weighted sufficient statistics and an effective sample size.  
- Adaptivity driven by pooled p-value over Mahalanobis distances (Hotelling’s T^2); low p-value increases per-sample weight and triggers PC relearning.  
- Provides update equations for weighted means/covariances, an effective sample size recursion, and a noisy-OR relearning schedule.  
- Simulations show LoSST matches batch PC in stationary phases and improves when data mix structures; CPI case study shows qualitative volatility detection.  
- Related work section omits substantial prior literature on time-varying graphical models (sliding-window/kernel-smoothed GGMs, fused graphical lasso, dynamic BNs/DBNs).  
- Approach is closest to adaptive/forgetting-factor updating plus periodic relearning; explicit comparisons to these methods are missing.  
- Positive: clear description of online sufficient-statistics updates and an intuitive, lightweight pooled p-value mechanism.  
- Positive: relearning schedule is simple and aligned with change-detection signals.  
- Concern: Statistical validity of Hotelling’s T^2 under time-varying weights and heuristic Sr is unclear; false positive/negative rates are not quantified.  
- Concern: Pooled p-value pooling (Liptak) assumes independent tests; temporal dependence of Mahalanobis distances is not addressed.  
- Concern: Limited baselines — only batch PC used; missing sliding-window PC, fixed forgetting PC, CPD+segmented PC, time-varying GGM and DBN methods.  
- Concern: Hyperparameters (T, γ, ν) chosen unsystematically; no sensitivity analysis or practitioner guidance provided.  
- Concern: Numerical stability issues (inverting Cr, CI tests with small Sr) not addressed; no covariance/precision regularization discussed.  
- Concern: Theory is sketchy; needs formal statements, assumptions (bounded drift, minimal change size), and proofs for convergence/diligence claims.  
- Concern: Experimental details missing (PC parameters, runtime, relearning frequency, orientation identifiability limits).  
- Concern: Real-data CPI study is descriptive without external validation or predictive/out-of-sample metrics.  
- Clarity: Paper readable but has typos, unclear footnotes, incomplete references, and some undefined variables on first use.  
- Originality: Integration of pooled-p-value-driven forgetting with online statistics is novel as a package, but components are known; distinctiveness unclear without comparisons.  
- Significance: Potentially useful lightweight online structure learner if validated, but current evidence limited by baselines and sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggestions: Add relevant baselines (sliding-window, fixed forgetting, CPD+restart, time-varying GGM), sensitivity and calibration studies, and regularization experiments.  
- Suggestions: Provide PC settings, runtime/relearning frequency trade-offs, expand related work, strengthen theory or label as conjectures, and improve real-data validation.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear problem formulation, low-overhead practical algorithm, sensible integration of change detection and relearning, promising simulation gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: heuristic underpinnings insufficiently validated, missing strong baselines, hyperparameter instability, sketch-level theory, qualitative real-data validation.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos (“TDL”→“TD learning,” remove “graph2”), complete references, clarify Hotelling’s T^2 parameterization and Sr usage, and ensure notation consistency.
