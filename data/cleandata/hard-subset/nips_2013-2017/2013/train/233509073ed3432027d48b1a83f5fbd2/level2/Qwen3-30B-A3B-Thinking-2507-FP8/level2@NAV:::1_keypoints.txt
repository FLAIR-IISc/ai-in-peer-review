- Proposes LoSST, a novel heuristic for real-time tracking of time-varying graphical model structure, effective under local stationarity and comparable to batch methods under global stationarity.  
- Fills a gap in literature by enabling online structure learning where most methods assume global stationarity.  
- Novel approach: tracks sufficient statistics with dynamically weighted data points to enable online updates.  
- Correctly identifies limitations of existing online methods: Bayesian updating cannot track structural changes and changepoint detection demands large memory.  
- Experimental evaluation includes three simulation settings (structure+parameter changes, parameter-only changes, probabilistic relearning) demonstrating LoSST’s effectiveness.  
- Real-world application (US price index volatility) shows LoSST can detect meaningful economic shifts (e.g., the Great Moderation).  
- Authors acknowledge limitations in handling rapid structural changes and random-order mixtures.  
- Algorithm is technically sound with solid theoretical grounding, notably the convergence vs. diligence trade-off.  
- Derivation of online sufficient-statistics updates is elegant and well-explained.  
- Paper is very clear with precise math and helpful figures.  
- Lacks thorough comparison to the broader literature on dynamic/time-varying graphical models.  
- Missing detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability to larger graphs.  
- Insufficient analysis of parameter sensitivity and guidance for selecting optimal parameters across applications.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and impactful work for streaming applications; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.
