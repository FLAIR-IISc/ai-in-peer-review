- Presents LoSST, a novel online algorithm for learning graphical model structure from non-stationary data streams.  
- Core idea: track weighted sufficient statistics (mean, covariance, effective sample size) and weight new points by Mahalanobis distance to the current model.  
- Poor fit to the model increases weight on recent data and raises the probability of triggering full structure relearning.  
- Novel real-time approach that combines structure learning, online learning, and changepoint detection, differing from batch time-varying graph methods.  
- Significance and originality: addresses a practical, under-explored problem with an elegant, novel solution.  
- Quality: technically sound with thorough simulations showing LoSST outperforms batch PC in changing environments and is competitive in stable ones; includes a real-world economic data case study.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a sophisticated discussion of the "convergence vs. diligence" trade-off.  
- Clarity: well-written, logically explained algorithm components, and effectively presented experiments.  
- Minor weakness: relies on several hyperparameters (T, γ, ν) chosen via "unsystematic investigation" without sensitivity analysis or principled setting guidance.  
- Minor weakness: probabilistic relearning mechanism is heuristic; choice of noisy-OR update rule lacks comparative motivation.  
- Recommendation: strengths far outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
