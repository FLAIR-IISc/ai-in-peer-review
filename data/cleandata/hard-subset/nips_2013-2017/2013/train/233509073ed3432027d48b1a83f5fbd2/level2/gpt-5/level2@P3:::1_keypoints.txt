- Proposal: LoSST, an online algorithm to track time-varying DAG/SEM structure using locally stationary weighted sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size).  
- Weighting mechanism: adapt weights via Mahalanobis distances pooled over time with Liptak’s method; low pooled p-value increases recent-data weight and shrinks effective sample size.  
- Relearning: structure relearning is probabilistically triggered and performed with the constraint-based PC algorithm.  
- Theoretical analysis: characterizes trade-off between convergence and diligence and identifies parameter regimes for each behavior.  
- Empirical results: synthetic experiments show LoSST matches batch PC under stationarity and outperforms after structural changes; CPI case study suggests macro regime-shift detection.  
- Relation to prior work: situates against batch DAG learning, online change detection, and time-varying undirected/edge-Bayesian methods; novelty is online directed-graph structure learning.  
- Technical soundness: weighted update equations are reasonable and memory-efficient; effective sample size recursion is heuristic but motivated.  
- Statistical concern: use of Hotelling’s T^2 with weighted covariances and Sr is an approximation that needs justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Inference/tests issue: need to confirm Type I error control when using weighted sufficient statistics and Sr, and describe regularization when Cr is ill-conditioned.  
- Baselines missing: main comparison is batch PC; lacking streaming baselines like sliding-window or EWMA-PC, BOCPD+restart, and time-varying GGM methods.  
- Empirical strengths: thorough synthetic studies across sizes/degrees, clear edge metrics, and demonstration of computational savings with probabilistic relearning.  
- Empirical weaknesses: CPI real-data validation is qualitative and would benefit from quantitative metrics (predictive likelihood, out-of-sample fit).  
- Clarity: generally clear and well organized but lacks algorithmic pseudocode, explicit PC test choices, and a parameter guide; contains minor typos.  
- Originality: novel combination of pooled p-value monitoring, adaptive weighting, and probabilistic relearning for online DAG/SEM; convergence/diligence framing is distinctive.  
- Significance: addresses an important real-time nonstationary problem with a simple, memory-efficient method compatible with standard learners.  
- Pros: clear problem formulation, lightweight O(V^2) algorithm, insightful convergence/diligence analysis, solid synthetic evidence and interpretable case study.  
- Cons: omitted streaming baselines, incomplete statistical calibration of T^2, limited parameter-selection guidance and runtime profiling, qualitative real-data validation.  
- Essential improvements: add at least one streaming baseline, empirically justify T^2 with weighted covariances/Sr, and provide algorithmic details (PC tests, regularization, V≥Sr handling).  
- Highly desirable additions: sensitivity analysis for T, γ, ν; runtime vs. relearning frequency trade-offs; quantitative validation for CPI results.  
- Optional suggestions: compare DAG skeleton recovery to time-varying GGM methods on synthetic data and release pseudocode/code for reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: timely, practical contribution with promising results; stronger baselines and modest statistical calibration would make it a strong conference submission.
