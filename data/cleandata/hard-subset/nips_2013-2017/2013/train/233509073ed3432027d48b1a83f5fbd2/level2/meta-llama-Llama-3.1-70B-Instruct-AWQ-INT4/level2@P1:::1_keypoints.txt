- Introduces LoSST, a novel online structure learning algorithm for graphical models that tracks time-varying structure/parameters in real time.
- Demonstrates effectiveness via simulations and a real-world US price index volatility application.
- Performs comparably to batch learning when the generating structure is globally stationary and significantly better when it is only locally stationary.
- Algorithm and sufficient-statistics tracking are clearly and thoroughly explained.
- Related work is comprehensive and motivates the need for an online approach.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes helpful figures/tables.
- Makes a significant and original contribution to online structure learning for nonstationary settings.
- Potentially widely applicable to real-world problems with time-varying structure.
- Assumes local stationarity of the generating process, which may not hold in all real-world scenarios.
- Does not provide a detailed computational complexity analysis, raising concerns for large-scale use.
- Lacks empirical comparison with other online structure learning algorithms to contextualize performance.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address limitations.
- Requested revisions: justify or relax the local-stationarity assumption, include complexity analysis, and add comparisons to other online methods.
- Overall rating: 8/10.
