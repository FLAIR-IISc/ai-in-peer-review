## Review of "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure"

This paper addresses the important problem of learning graphical models in non-stationary environments, a significant limitation of traditional structure learning algorithms. The authors present LoSST, a novel heuristic algorithm designed to track changes in graphical model structure and parameters in an online manner. The core idea of dynamically weighting data points based on their fit to the current model is promising and well-motivated.

The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing methods, positioning LoSST as a valuable contribution by enabling real-time learning in locally stationary environments. The algorithm itself is described with sufficient detail, and the authors thoughtfully discuss the trade-offs between convergence and diligence, offering a parameterization to balance these properties. The simulation results are comprehensive, demonstrating LoSST’s performance is comparable to batch learning in stable environments and superior when the underlying structure changes. The application to US price index data provides a compelling, albeit preliminary, demonstration of real-world applicability. 

However, the paper has some weaknesses. While the simulation results are convincing, the parameter tuning (e.g., ν, γ, T) appears somewhat ad-hoc. A more systematic exploration of the parameter space and its impact on performance would strengthen the findings. The discussion of the theoretical properties (convergence vs. diligence) is insightful, but could benefit from more concrete examples illustrating how parameter choices influence these behaviors. Additionally, the comparison to existing online changepoint detection algorithms feels somewhat cursory; a more detailed benchmark against state-of-the-art methods would be valuable. The choice of the PC algorithm as the baseline is reasonable, but acknowledging potential sensitivity to this choice would be prudent.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant gap in the literature regarding online structure learning.
*   Presents a novel and well-motivated algorithm (LoSST).
*   Demonstrates strong performance in simulations, including comparisons to batch learning.
*   Provides a compelling real-world application.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Parameter tuning appears somewhat arbitrary.
*   Theoretical discussion could be more concrete.
*   Limited benchmarking against alternative online changepoint detection methods.

**Overall:**

Despite some minor weaknesses, this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed LoSST algorithm is a promising approach to online structure learning, and the authors have provided a solid empirical evaluation. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more thorough discussion of parameter sensitivity and a more detailed comparison to relevant online changepoint detection algorithms. The potential impact of this work is significant, and further development of LoSST could lead to important advances in various applications involving dynamic graphical models.
