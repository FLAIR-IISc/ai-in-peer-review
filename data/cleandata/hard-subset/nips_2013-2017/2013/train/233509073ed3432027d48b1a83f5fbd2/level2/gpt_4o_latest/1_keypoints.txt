- Presents LoSST: a novel heuristic algorithm for online structure learning under locally stationary conditions that tracks sufficient statistics, detects structure changes, and relearns as needed.
- Addresses a significant gap by focusing on locally (not globally) stationary environments and real-time applicability.
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulations for tracking statistics, change detection, and weight updates.
- Uses Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values for effective change detection.
- Comprehensive evaluation with extensive simulations showing advantages over batch-mode learning in dynamic settings.
- Introduces a probabilistic relearning mechanism that balances computational efficiency and accuracy.
- Real-world case study on U.S. price index volatility demonstrates practical utility and detects macroeconomic shifts (e.g., Great Moderation).
- Well-structured paper with clear organization, and discussion of limitations and future directions.
- Limited comparison with alternative online methods (e.g., Bayesian online changepoint detection and frequentist approaches).
- Some algorithm components (weighting scheme, probabilistic relearning) are heuristic and lack deeper principled justification or alternatives.
- Scalability concerns: computational cost for high-dimensional graphs is acknowledged but not deeply analyzed.
- Limited real-world validation: only a single case study; more diverse applications needed to strengthen practical claims.
- Recommendation: accept, but encourage future work addressing benchmarking, principled design choices, and scalability.
