- Addresses the gap that existing structure learning assumes global stationarity while real data is often locally stationary.  
- Well-motivated; clearly explains practical importance of online structure learning for non-stationary data.  
- Main contribution: LoSST, a heuristic algorithm that tracks weighted sufficient statistics online and triggers relearning probabilistically.  
- Uses Mahalanobis distances and pooled p-values to detect structural change and decide when to relearn.  
- Provides clear update equations for weighted means, covariances, and effective sample size.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis characterizing the trade-off between convergence (stability) and diligence (responsiveness).  
- Simulation experiments comprehensive: matches batch performance in stable regimes and outperforms batch when structure changes.  
- Real-world validation on US price index volatility demonstrates practical utility.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and distinguishes LoSST from online feature-tracking and changepoint-detection methods.  
- Strength: timely, important problem with a practical, theoretically grounded algorithm and minimal overhead in stable settings.  
- Strength: empirical validation covers both structural and parameter changes and includes real data.  
- Weakness: probabilistic relearning parameter ν needs more detailed guidance for optimal setting.  
- Weakness: scope limited to recursive SEMs; extension to other graphical models (e.g., discrete Bayesian networks) is needed.  
- Weakness: connection and explicit comparison to recent NIPS structure-learning work could be stronger.  
- Weakness: Mahalanobis thresholding strategy (threshold T) is somewhat ad hoc and would benefit from a more principled selection method.  
- Recommended improvements: add practical guidance or empirical rules for choosing ν and T.  
- Recommended improvements: discuss extensibility to other graphical model types and discrete variables.  
- Recommended improvements: provide a clearer, explicit comparison to recent related NIPS papers.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound, meaningful advance for non-stationary structure learning, and recommended for acceptance.
