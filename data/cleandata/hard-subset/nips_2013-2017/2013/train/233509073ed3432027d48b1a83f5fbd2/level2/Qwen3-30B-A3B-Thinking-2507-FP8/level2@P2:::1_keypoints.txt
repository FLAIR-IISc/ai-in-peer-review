- Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical model structure addressing locally stationary processes.  
- Tracks sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size) online and uses Mahalanobis distances with pooled p-values to detect structural changes and reweight data.  
- Clearly motivates the problem and argues existing structure-learning approaches assuming global stationarity are insufficient.  
- Solid theoretical foundation with derived update equations and analysis of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off.  
- Simulations show LoSST matches batch methods in stable periods and outperforms them when structure changes.  
- Real-world application to US price index volatility identifies the Great Moderation, demonstrating practical relevance.  
- Related work section inadequately explains why existing online methods or changepoint/Bayesian approaches cannot be adapted; Bayesian argument needs stronger justification.  
- Fails to clearly define “locally stationary” for graphical models beyond per-time-point i.i.d. assumption.  
- Simulation parameter choices (T=0.05, γ=3) appear arbitrary; requires systematic theoretical or empirical exploration.  
- Assumes multivariate Gaussian data for Mahalanobis calculations with no discussion of non-Gaussian robustness or extensions.  
- Real-world analysis is superficial—lacks detailed inspection of learned graphs, interpretability, or comparisons to domain knowledge and other methods.  
- Omits computational complexity and scalability analysis; needs discussion of runtime and scaling with variables and retraining frequency.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound but overclaims novelty; the “first” claim needs stronger comparative support.  
- Overall judgment: promising and important work but requires substantial revisions (theoretical justification, experiments, comparisons, and complexity analysis); reviewer recommends rejection pending resubmission after fixes.
