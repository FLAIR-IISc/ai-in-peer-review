- Motivating problem: Real-world systems are often non-stationary, and LoSST aims to adaptively track changing graphical model structure in real time.  
- Clarity and organization: Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with an effective introduction and positioning.  
- Related work: Reasonably thorough coverage of score-based, constraint-based methods, online changepoint detection, and time-indexed graphs.  
- Algorithm description: LoSST’s update equations and design are detailed and logically presented.  
- Empirical performance: Simulations show LoSST matches batch learning when stable and outperforms when locally stationary.  
- Real-data application: US price index volatility example is a compelling, if preliminary, demonstration of applicability.  
- Key strength: Careful treatment of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off with a useful convergence analysis.  
- Algorithm design strengths: Adaptive weighting and probabilistic relearning are sensible and well-justified heuristics.  
- Major weakness — heuristics: Algorithm depends on several heuristics (Mahalanobis threshold T, max weight γ, relearning frequency ν) without rigorous justification or tuning guidance.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Unclear sensitivity to parameter choices; needs systematic exploration and practical selection rules.  
- Complexity/scalability: Lacks detailed runtime complexity analysis; repeated PC algorithm calls raise scalability concerns for larger variable sets.  
- Comparative baselines: Limited comparisons to other online/dynamic structure-learning methods (e.g., dynamic Bayesian networks).  
- Real-data details lacking: US price index application needs more preprocessing details and stronger statistical support for claims like detecting the “Great Moderation.”  
- Notation issues: Some inconsistent or unclear notation (e.g., subscript ‘r’) requiring clarification.  
- Recommendation: Overall a significant and novel contribution; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing parameter sensitivity, complexity analysis, and broader comparisons.
