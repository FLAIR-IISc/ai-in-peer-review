**Review of "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure"**

**Summary**

This paper presents a novel algorithm, LoSST, for tracking changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of LoSST through simulations and a real-world application to US price index volatility.

**Claims**

The main claims of this paper are:

1. LoSST can track changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner.
2. LoSST performs comparably to batch-mode learning when the generating graphical structure is globally stationary.
3. LoSST significantly outperforms batch-mode learning when the generating graphical structure is only locally stationary.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide strong support for their claims through simulations and a real-world application. The simulations demonstrate that LoSST can effectively track changes in graphical model structure or parameters, even when the generating structure is only locally stationary. The real-world application to US price index volatility shows that LoSST can detect changes in the relationships between price indexes over time.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in this paper are practically useful, as they can be applied to a wide range of real-world problems where graphical models are used. The ability to track changes in graphical model structure or parameters in real-time can be particularly useful in applications such as finance, healthcare, and climate modeling.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper reflects common knowledge in the field of graphical models and structure learning. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and demonstrate a clear understanding of the strengths and limitations of existing approaches.

**Novelty**

The LoSST algorithm presents a significant innovation over existing approaches to graphical model structure learning. The use of a heuristic algorithm that can track changes in graphical model structure or parameters in real-time is a novel contribution to the field.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including a clear description of the LoSST algorithm and its implementation. However, some aspects of the algorithm, such as the choice of parameters, could be further clarified.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach, including the assumption of local stationarity and the potential for poor performance if the generating structure changes very rapidly.

**Constructive Criticism**

One potential area for improvement is the use of more normative procedures for certain aspects of the algorithm, such as the calculation of weights. Additionally, the authors could consider extending the LoSST algorithm to other types of graphical models, such as Bayesian networks.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The LoSST algorithm presents a significant innovation over existing approaches to graphical model structure learning.
* The paper provides strong support for its claims through simulations and a real-world application.
* The ideas presented in this paper are practically useful and can be applied to a wide range of real-world problems.

Con:

* The assumption of local stationarity may not hold in all cases, and the algorithm may perform poorly if the generating structure changes very rapidly.
* Some aspects of the algorithm, such as the choice of parameters, could be further clarified.

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of graphical models and structure learning, and I recommend its acceptance.
