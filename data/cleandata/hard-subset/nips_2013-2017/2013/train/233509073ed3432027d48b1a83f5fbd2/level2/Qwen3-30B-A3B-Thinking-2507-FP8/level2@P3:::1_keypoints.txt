- Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm for learning graphical model structure under local stationarity (slow/incremental changes).  
- Identifies a literature gap: most methods assume global stationarity, failing in slowly changing real-world processes.  
- Tracks weighted sufficient statistics (means, covariances, sample size) online for real-time updates.  
- Detects structural changes using Mahalanobis distances combined with pooled p-values to trigger relearning.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and how parameter choices balance them.  
- Extends TD(0)-style updates to graphical model structure learning with efficient recursive updates.  
- Introduces a probabilistic relearning schedule controlled by pooled p-values and a ν parameter.  
- Empirical results show LoSST matches batch performance in stationary settings and outperforms batch methods in non-stationary ones.  
- Real-world case study on US price index volatility detects known shifts (Great Moderation) and avoids false positives from transients.  
- Technical exposition is precise with clear update equations and thorough mathematical detail.  
- Experimental design is robust, testing multiple change scenarios and comparing to appropriate baselines (PC algorithm).  
- Original contribution: brings online learning concepts to graphical structure learning, differentiating from prior change-point and batch methods.  
- Significant practical impact for domains with non-stationary data (finance, biology, social sciences).  
- Minor suggested improvements: expanded sensitivity analysis for T and γ, clearer justification and guidance for ν, and discussion of extensions to other graphical models (e.g., discrete/Bayesian networks).  
- Practical limitation: current focus on recursive SEMs; extending to other graphical model types and demonstrating scalability to larger variable sets would strengthen the work.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, novel, and significant — merits acceptance.
