- Introduces LoSST, a novel online heuristic that maintains sufficient statistics and dynamically relearns graphical model structure in real time.
- Core idea: detect misfit and trigger probabilistic relearning to track changing structures while operating online.
- Simulations show LoSST matches batch-mode learning when structure is stable and outperforms it under local stationarity.
- Demonstrated application to US price index volatility as a practical use case.
- Problem is well-motivated: addresses non-stationary environments where i.i.d. assumptions fail.
- Algorithm presentation is clear and detailed, with an insightful discussion of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off.
- Related work: appropriate connections to TDL and changepoint detection literature.
- Change detection uses Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values; weighting scheme and probabilistic relearning are well-explained.
- Strengths: technically sound, novel, clear, convincing simulations, practical application, and a useful theoretical discussion.
- Weakness: parameter choices (T and γ) appear arbitrary and need a thorough sensitivity analysis and practical tuning guidelines.
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison beyond the PC algorithm and no clear comparison of computational costs, especially under frequent changes.
- Weakness: poorer performance on edge removal errors when only parameters change; mitigation strategies (e.g., improved weighting or alternative learners) are unexplored.
- Weakness: claim that other standard structure learners would behave like PC is unsupported and needs empirical or theoretical backing.
- Weakness: US price index application is brief and would benefit from deeper analysis of learned models and economic implications.
- Recommendation: significant contribution; accept with minor revisions to address parameter sensitivity, computational cost comparison, edge-removal mitigation, and expanded application analysis.
