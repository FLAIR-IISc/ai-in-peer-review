- Presents LoSST, a novel online algorithm for tracking changes in graphical model structure/parameters under a local i.i.d. assumption.  
- Addresses limitation of standard structure-learning methods that assume a fixed generating process.  
- Introduction and related-work review are clear and thoroughly differentiate this approach from prior methods.  
- LoSST algorithm and update equations for weighted mean, covariance, and sample size are well-described and easy to follow.  
- Uses Mahalanobis distance to detect changes in generating structure; motivation for this choice is convincing.  
- Simulations show LoSST matches batch learning when structure is globally stationary and outperforms it when only locally stationary.  
- Real-world application to US price index volatility demonstrates LoSST’s ability to detect changing relationships in practice.  
- Strength: tackles a significant limitation of existing graphical model learning and is practically well-motivated.  
- Strength: algorithm presentation is clear and implementation details are provided.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion of limitations, e.g., rapid structure changes or random-order mixtures from multiple structures.  
- Weakness: insufficient information on computational cost and comparison to batch methods.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of relationship to other online methods (e.g., Bayesian online changepoint detection).  
- Originality: introduces a novel online structure-learning algorithm and a new use of Mahalanobis distance for change detection.  
- Significance: method has broad applicability (finance, economics, social sciences) and meaningful empirical results.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, well-explained algorithm with strong simulation and real-data results showing practical value.  
- Rejection arguments: needs deeper analysis of limitations, runtime costs, and connections to related online approaches.  
- Recommendations: expand limitations analysis (rapid/mixed-structure scenarios), provide computational-cost comparisons, relate to other online algorithms, and discuss broader applications.
