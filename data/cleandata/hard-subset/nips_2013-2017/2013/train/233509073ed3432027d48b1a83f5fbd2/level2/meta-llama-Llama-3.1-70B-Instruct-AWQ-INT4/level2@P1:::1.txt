This paper introduces the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST) algorithm, a novel online structure learning algorithm for graphical models that can track changes in the underlying graphical model structure or parameters in real-time. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of LoSST through simulations and a real-world application to US price index volatility data.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem of tracking time-varying graphical structures and motivate the need for an online structure learning algorithm. The related work section is comprehensive and provides a clear understanding of the existing approaches and their limitations. The LoSST algorithm is well-explained, and the authors provide a detailed description of the sufficient statistics tracking and the structure learning process.

The simulation results demonstrate the effectiveness of LoSST in tracking changes in the underlying graphical model structure or parameters. The authors show that LoSST performs comparably to batch-mode learning when the generating graphical structure is globally stationary and significantly better when it is only locally stationary. The real-world application to US price index volatility data further demonstrates the potential of LoSST in tracking changes in the underlying structure.

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of graphical models and online structure learning. The LoSST algorithm is a novel approach that addresses the limitations of existing methods, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of its effectiveness.

Strengths:

* The paper introduces a novel online structure learning algorithm for graphical models that can track changes in the underlying graphical model structure or parameters in real-time.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of the effectiveness of LoSST through simulations and a real-world application.
* The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the generating process is locally stationary, which may not always be the case in real-world applications.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of LoSST, which could be a limitation in large-scale applications.
* The paper does not provide a comparison with other online structure learning algorithms, which would be useful in understanding the relative performance of LoSST.
