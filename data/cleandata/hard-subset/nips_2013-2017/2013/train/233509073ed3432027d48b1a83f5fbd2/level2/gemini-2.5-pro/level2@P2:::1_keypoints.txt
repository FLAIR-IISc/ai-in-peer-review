- Introduces LoSST: an online algorithm for learning time-varying graphical model structure under a local stationarity assumption using weighted tracking of sufficient statistics, Mahalanobis-based change detection, and periodic PC re-learning.

- Well-positioned relative to prior work: distinguishes from batch structure learning, online parameter estimation, and changepoint methods, filling an underexplored gap.

- Significance & originality: tackles an important non-stationary structure-learning problem with a novel, intuitive combination of online tracking and batch structure learning; Mahalanobis-weighting is a clever core mechanism.

- Technical quality: update rules for weighted mean/covariance are correct; Liptak’s method for pooling Mahalanobis p-values is principled; discussion of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and parameters (γ, T) adds theoretical insight.

- Experimental evaluation: thorough — matches batch PC in stationary settings, substantially outperforms batch PC under changes, differentiates structure vs. parameter changes, and shows a compelling real-world US price-index case study.

- Heuristic components & parameter sensitivity: relies on several heuristics (linear weight update, noisy-OR relearning) and unsystematically chosen hyperparameters (T=0.05, γ=3); needs systematic sensitivity analysis and practitioner guidance.

- Baseline choice: comparing only to batch PC is weak in non-stationary settings; should include stronger baselines such as sliding-window PC or other simple online alternatives.

- Scope of applicability: claims of easy adaptability to undirected graphs or discrete BNs are undersubstantiated; extensions to discrete models would be non-trivial and require more detail or toned-down claims.

- Overall assessment: well-written, significant, and practical contribution with strong empirical support; minor methodological and evaluation gaps notwithstanding, recommend acceptance.
