- Summary: Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm that tracks changes in graphical model structure/parameters under a local-stationarity assumption by updating sufficient statistics and using constraint-based structure learning while trading off accuracy and relearning cost.

- Strength — Novel approach: Introduces a new real-time method for tracking graphical model changes.

- Strength — Efficient computation: Updates relevant sufficient statistics online using only previous values and a few extra variables.

- Strength — Robust performance: Matches batch-mode learning when globally stationary and outperforms it under local stationarity.

- Strength — Real-world application: Demonstrated on U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics data.

- Weakness — Assumes local stationarity: Relies on a local-stationarity assumption that may not hold in many real settings.

- Weakness — Heuristic approach: Uses heuristics to track sufficient statistics that may be suboptimal.

- Weakness — Computational cost: Could remain computationally expensive, especially for large datasets.

- Weakness — Limited evaluation: Empirical validation is limited to a few simulations and one real-world dataset.

- Originality: Novel combination of online learning/changepoint ideas with graphical model structure learning.

- Significance: Potentially valuable for real-time data analysis where batch structure learning is infeasible.

- Pro acceptance: Novel method, promising empirical performance, and potential applicability to challenging real-time contexts.

- Con acceptance: Dependence on local stationarity, heuristic methods, and limited empirical validation are concerns.

- Revision — More evaluation: Add more simulations and diverse real-world applications.

- Revision — Alternative tracking: Investigate non-heuristic or alternative methods for tracking locally stationary statistics.

- Revision — Better changepoint methods: Consider more robust changepoint-detection techniques.

- Revision — Computational analysis: Provide detailed computational cost and scalability analysis.
