This paper introduces LoSST, a novel heuristic algorithm for tracking changes in graphical model structure in a dynamic, real-time manner. The work addresses a significant gap in existing structure learning algorithms, which largely assume stationary environments. The problem tackled is important, particularly in real-world scenarios where underlying generative processes are often non-stationary. 

The paper is well-motivated and clearly articulates the limitations of existing approaches. The related work section provides a concise overview of relevant techniques – Bayesian/score-based methods, constraint-based methods, TDL, and changepoint detection – and convincingly argues why these are insufficient for the proposed task. The description of the LoSST algorithm is detailed and logically structured. The authors thoughtfully address the trade-offs between convergence and diligence, providing a nuanced discussion of the algorithm’s behavior under different parameter settings. The simulation results are comprehensive, demonstrating LoSST's performance in various scenarios, including both stable and changing graphical structures. The inclusion of a real-world application to U.S. price index volatility further strengthens the paper.

A key strength is the algorithm's ability to perform comparably to batch-mode learning when the generating structure is stable, avoiding a performance penalty in typical situations. The detailed explanation of the update equations for sufficient statistics is particularly valuable, allowing for a clear understanding of the algorithm’s computational efficiency. The discussion of the Mahalanobis distance and its relation to change detection is also well-presented. 

However, there are areas where the paper could be improved. While the mathematical formulation of the algorithm is detailed, the intuition behind some design choices could be made more explicit. For example, the specific choice of the update equation for the sample size (Equation 3) and its connection to the effective sample size could benefit from further elaboration.  Additionally, the parameter sensitivity analysis, while present, could be more systematic. Investigating the impact of different learning rates and thresholds (T and γ) on performance across a wider range of scenarios would be beneficial.

The application section, while demonstrating the algorithm’s applicability to real data, is somewhat limited in scope. It would be valuable to compare LoSST’s performance on this dataset to other online anomaly detection or change point detection methods specifically designed for time series data. The discussion of the detected shifts in volatility is also rather brief. Elaborating on the economic significance of these shifts and validating them against known events would strengthen the application.

Finally, the notation used throughout the paper is generally clear, but can be dense. Providing a table summarizing the key symbols and their definitions would enhance readability.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant and under-explored problem in graphical model learning.
*   Presents a novel and well-motivated algorithm (LoSST) with a clear theoretical foundation.
*   Demonstrates strong performance through comprehensive simulations.
*   Provides a compelling real-world application.
*   Offers a nuanced discussion of the trade-offs between convergence and diligence.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Some design choices lack sufficient intuitive explanation.
*   Parameter sensitivity analysis could be more systematic.
*   The application section could be expanded and compared to relevant baselines.
*   Dense notation might hinder readability for some readers.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of graphical model learning. The proposed LoSST algorithm is a significant step towards addressing the challenges of learning in non-stationary environments. While there are some areas for improvement, the strengths of the work outweigh the weaknesses.  This reviewer recommends acceptance, with minor revisions to address the points raised above. Specifically, clarifying the intuition behind the sample size update equation, expanding the parameter sensitivity analysis, and providing a more thorough analysis of the real-world application would substantially enhance the paper’s impact.
