- Introduces LoSST, a novel heuristic for real-time tracking of graphical model structure in non-stationary environments.  
- Addresses an important, under-explored problem relevant to real-world non-stationary data.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly articulates limitations of existing stationary-focused methods.  
- Related work section concisely covers Bayesian/score-based, constraint-based, TDL, and changepoint detection methods and motivates the gap.  
- LoSST algorithm is described in detail and presented in a logical, reproducible manner.  
- Authors discuss the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and behavior under different parameter settings.  
- Simulation results are comprehensive, showing performance across stable and changing graphical structures.  
- Real-world application to U.S. price index volatility strengthens the paper’s practical relevance.  
- LoSST matches batch-mode learning performance when the generating structure is stable, avoiding penalties in typical settings.  
- Clear derivation of update equations for sufficient statistics highlights computational efficiency.  
- Discussion of Mahalanobis distance and its role in change detection is well-presented.  
- Some design choices lack intuitive explanation—e.g., the rationale and interpretation of the sample size update (Equation 3).  
- Parameter sensitivity analysis exists but should be more systematic across learning rates and thresholds (T and γ).  
- Application section is limited: needs comparisons to online anomaly/change-point methods and deeper economic validation of detected shifts.  
- Notation can be dense; a symbol table would improve readability.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to clarify Eq.3 intuition, expand parameter sensitivity experiments, and deepen the real-data analysis.
