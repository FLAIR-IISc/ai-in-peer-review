- Presents LoSST, a novel single-pass online algorithm for learning graphical model structure from non-stationary continuous data streams.  
- Tracks weighted sufficient statistics (mean, covariance, effective sample size) and updates them online.  
- Uses Mahalanobis distance to measure how well a new data point fits the current model and assign dynamic weights.  
- Pools p-values of Mahalanobis distances over time with Liptak’s method to distinguish outliers from structural change.  
- Increases weights for new data and probabilistically schedules relearning when pooled p-values indicate sustained poor fit.  
- Relearns graph structure probabilistically, with higher relearning chance after suspected change.  
- Technical presentation and update equations are clear and well-motivated; Mahalanobis choice is appropriate.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on synthetic scenarios (structure change vs parameter-only change) and a real-world US price-index case study.  
- Demonstrates comparable performance to batch PC in stationary settings and superior performance under structural change; detects known economic regime change.  
- Strong and original contribution: first single-pass online structure-learning algorithm combining weighted stats, pooled p-values, and probabilistic relearning.  
- Formal discussion of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off with parameter regimes (γ, T) adds theoretical depth.  
- Several algorithmic components are heuristic (weight update form, noisy-OR relearning schedule) without formal derivation.  
- Key parameters chosen via “unsystematic investigation” with no sensitivity analysis, raising robustness/generalizability concerns.  
- Baseline comparison (batch PC on all data) is weak for non-stationary settings; sliding-window or other online baselines are missing.  
- Potential scalability issues due to O(V^3) matrix inversion per time step are noted but not thoroughly explored.  
- Well-written and clear paper; strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommendation: accept.
