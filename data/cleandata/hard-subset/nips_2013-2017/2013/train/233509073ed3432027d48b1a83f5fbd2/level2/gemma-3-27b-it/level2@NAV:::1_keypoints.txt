- Introduces LoSST, a novel heuristic for online structure learning in locally stationary graphical models.  
- Core idea: track sufficient statistics and dynamically relearn graph structure as needed.  
- Motivation clearly explains shortcomings of global-stationarity assumptions in real-world dynamic systems.  
- Algorithm mechanics are well explained, including online updates of means, covariances, and sample sizes.  
- Proposes a novel weighting scheme using Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values to detect structural changes.  
- Convergence vs. diligence discussion provides useful insight into parameter-driven behavior.  
- Simulations show LoSST matches batch learning in stable settings and outperforms in dynamic scenarios, with detailed error breakdowns.  
- Real-world application to US price index data offers promising validation.  
- Clear writing and comprehensive related work situate LoSST well within existing literature.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations such as sensitivity to rapid changes and potential computational costs for complex extensions.  
- Heuristic nature and empirically chosen parameters (T and γ) are weaknesses that need stronger justification or adaptive tuning.  
- Lacks formal theoretical analysis of convergence/diligence trade-offs.  
- Reliance on the PC algorithm may present scalability bottlenecks; alternative structure-learning methods should be explored.  
- Overall judgment: strong, novel contribution warranting acceptance with revisions on parameter justification, theoretical analysis, and scalability.
