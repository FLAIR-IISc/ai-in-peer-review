- Presents LoSST, a novel online algorithm for graphical model structure learning that tracks changing generating processes.  
- Main claim: LoSST matches batch learning under global stationarity and outperforms when only local stationarity holds, supported by simulations and real data.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear overview of related work and limitations of existing approaches.  
- LoSST algorithm is described in detail with thorough analysis of properties, including convergence and diligence.  
- Simulation results show LoSST effectively tracks structural changes.  
- Real-world application demonstrates LoSST’s practical potential.  
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, thoroughness, clear motivation, and solid empirical validation.  
- Weakness: algorithm complexity may hinder adoption and understanding.  
- Weakness: lacks clear comparisons to other online learning algorithms, especially regarding convergence and long-run behavior.  
- Weakness: mentions an extension where the learned model influences weights but provides no concrete description.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of LoSST’s limitations and practical challenges in real-world deployment.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add comparisons to other online methods, clarify limitations, and detail the proposed weight-influence extension.
