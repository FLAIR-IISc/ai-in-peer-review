This paper presents a novel algorithm, LoSST, for tracking changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner. The authors address a significant limitation of standard graphical model structure learning algorithms, which assume that the underlying generating process does not change over time. In contrast, LoSST assumes only that the data are locally i.i.d. and learns graphical model structure in an online fashion.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, discussing the limitations of current algorithms and the need for online learning in real-world applications. They also provide a thorough review of related work, highlighting the differences between their approach and previous methods.

The LoSST algorithm is well-described, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the update equations for the sufficient statistics, including the weighted mean, covariance, and sample size. The use of the Mahalanobis distance to detect changes in the underlying generating structure is also well-motivated.

The simulation results demonstrate the effectiveness of LoSST in tracking changes in graphical model structure and parameters. The authors show that LoSST performs comparably to batch-mode learning when the generating graphical structure is globally stationary and significantly better when it is only locally stationary.

The application to US price index volatility data is also interesting, and the authors demonstrate the ability of LoSST to detect changes in the volatility of the relationships between price indexes.

Strengths:

* The paper addresses a significant limitation of standard graphical model structure learning algorithms.
* The LoSST algorithm is well-described and easy to follow.
* The simulation results demonstrate the effectiveness of LoSST in tracking changes in graphical model structure and parameters.
* The application to real-world data is interesting and demonstrates the potential of LoSST in practical applications.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of LoSST, such as its performance in situations where the generating structure changes rapidly or the datapoints are a random-order mixture from multiple structures.
* The authors could provide more information on the computational cost of LoSST, particularly in comparison to batch-mode learning algorithms.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between LoSST and other online learning algorithms, such as Bayesian online changepoint detection.

Originality:

* The use of the Mahalanobis distance to detect changes in the underlying generating structure is a new approach.
