- Presents LoSST, a novel real-time algorithm for tracking changes in graphical model structure or parameters.
- Claims LoSST can track dynamic changes in graphical models in real time.
- Claims LoSST matches batch-mode learning when the generating structure is globally stationary.
- Claims LoSST outperforms batch methods when the generating structure is only locally stationary.
- Provides simulations and a US price index volatility case study to support the claims.
- Demonstrates practical usefulness for applications like finance, healthcare, and climate modeling.
- Shows solid field knowledge with thorough related-work review and understanding of existing methods.
- Novel contribution: a heuristic algorithm enabling real-time tracking of graphical structure changes.
- Paper is largely reproducible with clear algorithm description, though some parameter choices need clarification.
- Limitation: assumes local stationarity and may fail if the underlying structure changes very rapidly.
- Suggests using more normative procedures for aspects like weight calculation and extending to other graphical model types.
- Pros: significant innovation, strong empirical support, and broad practical applicability.
- Cons: reliance on local stationarity and unclear parameter selection in parts of the algorithm.
- Overall recommendation: paper makes a significant contribution and is recommended for acceptance.
