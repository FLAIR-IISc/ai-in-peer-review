This paper introduces LoSST, a heuristic online algorithm for tracking time-varying graphical structure in linear-Gaussian SEMs. The core idea is to maintain weighted sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size) with adaptive weights driven by pooled p-values of Mahalanobis distances, thereby increasing “forgetting” after apparent changes. Structure is relearned using PC on a probabilistic schedule driven by the same pooled evidence. The paper also analyzes a convergence–diligence trade-off via parameters T and γ, and evaluates performance on synthetic SEMs with changepoints and on US CPI volatility data. Claims are that LoSST matches batch learning under global stationarity and outperforms it when structure or parameters shift; simulations largely support these claims.

Quality: Methodologically, the online sufficient-statistics updates are sound, and the pooled-p-value mechanism is a plausible, lightweight proxy for change evidence. The convergence/diligence discussion is thoughtful, though many results are provided as sketches. Empirical support is solid for linear-Gaussian SEMs with PC, but baselines are limited: there is no comparison to sliding-window/exp-weighted PC, Bayesian online changepoint detection with restart, dynamic Bayesian networks, or time-varying GGMs (e.g., TV-GLASSO). The real-data case study is qualitative without external validation. Sensitivity analyses for T, γ, ν are acknowledged but unsystematic; statistical significance of improvements is not reported. Notational issues exist (e.g., Hotelling T2 parameters appear inconsistent: p should equal dimensionality V, not N).

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written and organized; providing pseudo-code, complexity analysis (time/memory per update and per relearn), and a consolidated list of hyperparameters would improve reproducibility. Some footnote/notation glitches should be corrected.

Originality: The combination of pooled p-values to adapt forgetting with online structure relearning is novel within constraint-based structure learning. The explicit framing of convergence vs diligence is interesting. However, related work on dynamic Bayesian networks and time-varying sparse GGMs should be discussed and used as baselines.

Significance: For practitioners needing online structure tracking under local stationarity, LoSST is potentially useful, with minimal cost under stationarity. Scope is limited to continuous, linear-Gaussian settings and PC-based learning.

Constructive suggestions:
- Add baselines: sliding/exp-window PC, BOCPD+restart, DBNs, and TV-GLASSO.
- Provide ablations over T, γ, ν; report detection delay/false alarm trade-offs.
- Use robust distances to mitigate outliers; discuss high-dimensional regularization.
- Include pseudo-code and release code/data for reproducibility.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Clear problem formulation; practical online method with lightweight updates.
- Novel adaptive weighting via pooled p-values; useful convergence–diligence perspective.
- Empirical gains in nonstationary settings; no loss under stationarity.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Missing strong baselines; limited scope to linear-Gaussian SEMs.
- Incomplete sensitivity/robustness analysis and statistical testing.
- Minor technical/notation issues and limited real-world validation.
