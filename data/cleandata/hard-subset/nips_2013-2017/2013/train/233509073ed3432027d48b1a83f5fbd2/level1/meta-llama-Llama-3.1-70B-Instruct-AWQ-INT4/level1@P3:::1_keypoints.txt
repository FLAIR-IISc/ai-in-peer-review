- Proposes LoSST, a novel heuristic algorithm for tracking graphical model structure/parameters in real time under changing generating processes.  
- Targets environments with time-varying structure where standard batch/online methods fall short.  
- Clearly positions LoSST relative to prior work, highlighting differences from Bayesian and frequentist online changepoint methods.  
- Tracks sufficient statistics (means, covariances, sample size) online and uses a constraint-based learner (e.g., PC) for structure updates.  
- Simulation results: matches batch learning when global stationarity holds and outperforms when only local stationarity holds.  
- Real-world application on seasonally adjusted price indices shows LoSST detects volatility changes (e.g., Great Moderation).  
- Strength: addresses an important problem of learning under nonstationarity in graphical models.  
- Strength: algorithm is novel and potentially broadly applicable (finance, healthcare, social networks).  
- Strength: paper is well-written, organized, and includes illustrative figures/tables.  
- Weakness: assumes local stationarity, which may not hold in all real-world settings.  
- Weakness: LoSST is heuristic; performance may not be optimal across scenarios.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough analysis of computational complexity.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed empirical comparisons with other online learning algorithms.  
- Originality: introduces a new approach for dynamic structure tracking with clear novelty.  
- Significance: could make a notable contribution to structure learning under time-varying processes.  
- Quality/Clarity: clear exposition and convincing simulations plus a real-data demonstration.  
- Acceptance case: addresses a significant problem, presents a novel method, and shows promising results.  
- Rejection concerns: reliance on local stationarity assumption, heuristic nature, and missing complexity/comparative analyses.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add computational complexity analysis and comparisons to other online methods.
