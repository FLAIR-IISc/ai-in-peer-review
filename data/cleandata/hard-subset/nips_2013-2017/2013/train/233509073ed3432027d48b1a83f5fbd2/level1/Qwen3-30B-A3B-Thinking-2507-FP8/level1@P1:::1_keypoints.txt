- Addresses gap in graphical model learning by handling locally stationary, time-varying structure in real time.  
- Proposes LoSST, an online algorithm that tracks graphical structure with minimal computational overhead.  
- Clearly situates LoSST relative to batch structure learning, online feature tracking, and changepoint detection.  
- Novel combination of Mahalanobis distance–based change detection and online sufficient-statistic tracking.  
- Theoretical foundations for tracking weighted sufficient statistics are sound and well-derived.  
- Provides a strong convergence vs. diligence analysis showing parameter effects on behavior.  
- Comprehensive simulations (500 runs across graph sizes/degrees) give robust empirical support.  
- Real-world application to US price index volatility demonstrates practical relevance and meaningful detections.  
- Well-written, organized, and reproducible presentation with clear figures and explanations.  
- Lacks systematic theoretical guidance for selecting threshold T and weight scaling γ.  
- Omits concrete computational complexity analysis and benchmarks against batch learning.  
- Method is currently limited to recursive linear SEMs with Gaussian errors (does not cover discrete or more complex models).  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, practically significant contribution that merits acceptance despite noted limitations.
