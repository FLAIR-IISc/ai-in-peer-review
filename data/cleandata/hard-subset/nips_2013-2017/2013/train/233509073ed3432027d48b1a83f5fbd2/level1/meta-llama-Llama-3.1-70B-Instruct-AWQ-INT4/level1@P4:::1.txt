This paper proposes a novel algorithm, Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST), for tracking changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner. The authors address a significant gap in the current literature by developing an online learning algorithm that can learn graphical model structure in an online fashion, assuming only that the data are locally i.i.d.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, related work, and the proposed algorithm. The LoSST algorithm is described in detail, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of the sufficient statistics tracking, the update equations, and the learning process.

The paper's strengths include:

* The LoSST algorithm is a significant contribution to the field, as it addresses a long-standing problem in graphical model structure learning.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's properties, including convergence and diligence.
* The paper includes a comprehensive set of simulations that demonstrate the algorithm's performance in various scenarios.
* The application to real-world data (US price index volatility) is a nice touch, as it shows the algorithm's potential in practical settings.

The paper's weaknesses include:

* The algorithm's complexity and the number of parameters that need to be tuned may make it challenging to apply in practice.
* The authors acknowledge that the algorithm may not perform well if the generating structure changes very rapidly or if the datapoints are a random-order mixture from multiple structures.
* Some of the heuristic aspects of the algorithm, such as the use of Liptak's method for weighted pooling of p-values, may not be optimal and could be improved.
