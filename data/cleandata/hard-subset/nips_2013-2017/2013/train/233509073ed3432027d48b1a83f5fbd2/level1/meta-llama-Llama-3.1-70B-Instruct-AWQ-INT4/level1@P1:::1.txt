**Summary**

This paper presents the Locally Stationary Structure Tracker (LoSST) algorithm, a novel, heuristic structure learning algorithm for graphical models that can track changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner. The authors argue that standard graphical model structure learning algorithms assume a globally stationary generating process, which is not realistic in many real-world environments. The LoSST algorithm is designed to learn graphical model structure in an online fashion, assuming only that the data are locally i.i.d. The algorithm uses a combination of constraint-based structure learning and online learning methods to track changes in the generating structure.

**Relation to previous work**

The authors provide a thorough review of related work in graphical model structure learning, including Bayesian/score-based methods and constraint-based structure learning algorithms. They also discuss online learning methods, such as the classic TDL algorithm, and online changepoint detection algorithms. The authors argue that these methods are insufficient for learning graphical model structure in an online manner, as they either assume a globally stationary generating process or require storing substantial portions of the input data.

**Quality**

The paper presents a well-motivated and well-designed algorithm for online graphical model structure learning. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the algorithm, including its components and parameters. The use of simulation studies to evaluate the performance of the algorithm is also a strength of the paper. However, the authors could have provided more theoretical analysis of the algorithm's properties, such as its convergence and stability.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the algorithm, and the technical details are well-explained. The use of equations and figures to illustrate the algorithm's components and performance is also helpful. However, some of the paragraphs are quite long and could be broken up for easier reading.

**Originality**

The LoSST algorithm is a novel contribution to the field of graphical model structure learning. The authors' use of online learning methods and constraint-based structure learning algorithms to track changes in the generating structure is a new and innovative approach. The algorithm's ability to learn graphical model structure in an online fashion, without requiring a globally stationary generating process, is a significant improvement over existing methods.

**Significance**

The LoSST algorithm has the potential to be widely applicable in many real-world domains, such as finance, healthcare, and social network analysis. The algorithm's ability to track changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner makes it particularly useful for applications where the underlying structure is changing over time. The authors' use of simulation studies and real-world data to evaluate the algorithm's performance also demonstrates its potential for practical applications.
