- Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm for graphical model structure learning in locally stationary environments.  
- Tracks sufficient statistics (means, covariances, sample size) online and uses Mahalanobis distances with pooled p-values to detect structural changes and weight data.  
- Addresses a critical gap: existing methods assume global stationarity or require batch processing.  
- Demonstrates strong technical quality with thorough simulations; matches batch learning when stationary and outperforms when structure changes.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off with clear conditions.  
- Real-world application (US price index volatility) shows practical utility and correctly detects the "Great Moderation" without reacting to short spikes.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, accessible algorithm description, helpful pseudocode, and informative figures.  
- Novel contribution: first real-time online solution for general graphical model structure learning in locally stationary settings, distinct from prior batch methods.  
- Significant for real-world streaming applications (finance, economics, neuroscience) due to single-pass, low-overhead processing.  
- Strength: clear identification of a literature gap and a novel algorithmic approach.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundations and comprehensive simulation validation.  
- Strength: practical demonstration and efficient online processing.  
- Weakness: parameter choices (e.g., T = 0.05, γ = 3) appear ad hoc with limited justification.  
- Weakness: probabilistic relearning mechanism lacks strong theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of performance under extremely rapid structural changes.  
- Weakness: limited connection to the broader online learning literature beyond change-point detection.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel and theoretically sound algorithm, strong empirical results, and compelling real-world application.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need for more rigorous parameter justification, stronger theory for relearning, and deeper exploration of rapid-change limits.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better justify parameter choices and discuss limitations for rapidly changing structures.
