- Presents LoSST, an online algorithm for learning graphical model structure from locally stationary data streams.  
- Core mechanism: track sufficient statistics (mean, covariance, effective sample size) with dynamic weighting of incoming data.  
- Weights are based on Mahalanobis distance; low pooled p-value over recent data increases weight and triggers relearning.  
- Bridges gap between batch structure learning (i.i.d. assumption) and online changepoint detection (no general structure learning).  
- Positioned against prior work (Talih & Hengartner 2005; Siracusa & Fisher 2009) and supports real-time operation on directed acyclic graphs.  
- Strength: original and significant approach addressing non-stationary environments in practice.  
- Strength: elegant combination of established statistical tools with a clear theoretical discussion of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation—comparable to batch PC in stationary settings and superior after structural/parametric shifts; validated on U.S. price index data.  
- Weakness: relies on several heuristic components and hyperparameters (e.g., weight update, relearning probability ν) tuned via unsystematic investigation.  
- Weakness: lacks a systematic sensitivity analysis to demonstrate robustness to hyperparameter choices.  
- Weakness: computational complexity not fully addressed—Mahalanobis distance requires covariance inversion each timestep, limiting scalability to higher dimensions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses significant limitation, novel well-motivated algorithm, strong theoretical framing, thorough empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heuristic parameter choices without full sensitivity analysis and potential scalability concerns for high-dimensional data.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; strongly recommend acceptance.
