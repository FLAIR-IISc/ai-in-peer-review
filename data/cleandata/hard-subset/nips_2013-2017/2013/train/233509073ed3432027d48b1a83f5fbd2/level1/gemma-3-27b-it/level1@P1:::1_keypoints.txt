- Introduces LoSST, a novel heuristic for online tracking of graphical model structure using online sufficient statistics and dynamic relearning.  
- Addresses an important gap: most structure learning methods assume stationarity; LoSST handles local nonstationarity in real time.  
- Problem statement and related work (TDL, changepoint detection, time-indexed graph learning) are clearly articulated.  
- Algorithm is presented with solid mathematical detail; update equations and convergence/diligence trade-offs are discussed.  
- Simulation results show LoSST matches batch methods under stationarity and outperforms them under local stationarity.  
- Real-world application to US price index data strengthens empirical claims.  
- Novelty & importance: tackles an underexplored, practically relevant problem in online graphical model learning.  
- Technical soundness: derivations and algorithmic details are well-explained.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: thorough simulations and parameter sweeps provide convincing evidence.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with consistent notation.  
- Trade-off awareness: insightful discussion of convergence versus diligence and algorithm limitations.  
- Heuristic nature: reliance on Mahalanobis distance and chosen weighting scheme lacks deeper justification.  
- Parameter sensitivity: performance depends on parameters (e.g., T, γ) without systematic sensitivity analysis or practitioner guidance.  
- Computational complexity: potential bottleneck from using the PC algorithm; scalability to high-dimensional data is not fully addressed.  
- Related work scope: misses some recent advances in online Bayesian structure learning and concept-drift detection (e.g., dynamic BN/Kalman filter approaches).  
- Presentation: Figures 1 and 2 are cluttered with many lines, reducing clarity of trends.  
- Argument for acceptance: meaningful, novel contribution with sound methodology and strong empirical results.  
- Argument for acceptance: demonstrates practical applicability with a real dataset and clear understanding of trade-offs.  
- Argument against acceptance: heuristic design and lack of theoretical guarantees may concern reviewers.  
- Argument against acceptance: parameter sensitivity and scalability issues could limit real-world use.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper meriting acceptance, with acknowledged limitations that do not undermine its contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to justify heuristics, analyze parameter sensitivity, discuss complexity, expand related work, and improve figure clarity.
