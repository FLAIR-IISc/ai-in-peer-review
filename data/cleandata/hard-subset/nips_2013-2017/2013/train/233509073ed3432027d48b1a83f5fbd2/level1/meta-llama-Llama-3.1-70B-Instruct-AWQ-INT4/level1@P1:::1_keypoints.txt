- LoSST is a novel online algorithm that tracks changes in graphical model structure/parameters assuming only local i.i.d. data, combining constraint-based structure learning with online methods.  
- Authors position LoSST against standard methods that assume global stationarity and claim those are unsuitable for real-time, changing environments.  
- Related work is thoroughly reviewed, covering Bayesian/score-based, constraint-based, online learning, and changepoint detection; prior methods either assume stationarity or require storing large past data.  
- The algorithm is well-motivated and clearly described, with detailed components and parameters and strong simulation-based evaluation.  
- The paper lacks sufficient theoretical analysis of algorithmic properties such as convergence and stability.  
- The manuscript is well-written and technically clear with useful equations and figures, but some paragraphs are overly long and impede readability.  
- LoSST is an original contribution by combining online learning with constraint-based structure discovery to operate without global stationarity.  
- The method is significant and broadly applicable to real-world, time-varying domains (finance, healthcare, social networks) and is validated on simulations and real data.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, practical relevance, clear exposition, and empirical evaluation on simulated and real datasets.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient theoretical guarantees, presentation issues (long paragraphs), and limited discussion of limitations and extensions.  
- Suggested improvements: include theoretical convergence/stability analysis, break up long paragraphs, discuss limitations and extensions, and detail implementation and computational complexity.
