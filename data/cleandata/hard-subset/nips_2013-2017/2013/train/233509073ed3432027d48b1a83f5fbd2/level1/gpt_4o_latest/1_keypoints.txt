- Introduces LoSST, an online algorithm for structure learning in locally stationary graphical models that tracks changing structure and parameters in real time.
- Novelty: fills a gap by enabling online structure learning under local stationarity using locally stationary sufficient statistics and Mahalanobis-distance change detection.
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulation for tracking statistics, weighting data, and probabilistic relearning, with analysis of convergence vs. diligence trade-offs.
- Empirical validation: simulations show comparable performance to batch methods in global stationarity and superior performance in locally stationary scenarios; real-world U.S. price index application demonstrates practical utility.
- Clarity: well-written, well-structured paper with a thorough related-work discussion and clear differentiation from existing methods.
- Weakness — limited real-world evaluation: only one real-world case study; broader domain tests needed to establish generalizability and robustness.
- Weakness — computational complexity: potential high cost of frequent graph relearning in high dimensions; scalability analysis is insufficient.
- Weakness — heuristic choices: weighting mechanism and probabilistic relearning schedule rely on heuristics without deeper theoretical justification or alternative comparisons.
- Weakness — rapid changes: limited investigation into performance under very rapid structural changes; behavior in such settings remains unclear.
- For acceptance: makes a significant, novel contribution with theoretical grounding and promising empirical results that open new research directions.
- Against acceptance: heuristic reliance and limited exploration of scalability and rapidly changing regimes, plus narrow real-world evaluation, may weaken impact.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing weaknesses by expanding real-world evaluations and providing more detailed computational/scalability analysis.
