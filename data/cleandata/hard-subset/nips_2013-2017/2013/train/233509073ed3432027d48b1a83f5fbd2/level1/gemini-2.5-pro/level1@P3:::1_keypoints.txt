- Problem: Learning graphical model structure from non-stationary data streams is important and under-addressed.
- Contribution: Proposes LoSST, an online single-pass algorithm that tracks structure and parameters via weighted sufficient statistics.
- Core mechanism: Uses Mahalanobis distance per point, Liptak’s pooled p-values to detect changes, and increases weighting of new data when change is detected.
- Relearning trigger: Probabilistic full structure relearning (PC algorithm) with probability increasing on detected change.
- Theory: Discusses tunable tradeoff between being “convergent” (stable) and “diligent” (responsive).
- Strength — Significance/Originality: Novel, well-motivated combination of online statistics, robust change detection, and adaptive relearning.
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, logically organized, and clearly motivated with relation to prior work.
- Strength — Evaluation: Strong empirical tests (simulations with structure/parameter changes), validation of probabilistic relearning, and a real-world US price index case study.
- Weakness — Baselines: Lacks stronger non-stationary baselines (e.g., sliding window, fixed exponential forgetting).
- Weakness — Computational complexity: Mahalanobis distance requires covariance inversion per point (O(V^3)), experiments limited to V ≤ 20; scalability not addressed.
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Key parameters (T, gamma, nu) chosen unsystematically; needs sensitivity analysis and practitioner guidance.
- Weakness — Justification of effective sample size: Update rule is heuristic and needs more rigorous statistical justification given its role in change detection.
- Overall assessment: High-quality, original work with convincing results; recommended for acceptance and likely to inspire follow-up research.
