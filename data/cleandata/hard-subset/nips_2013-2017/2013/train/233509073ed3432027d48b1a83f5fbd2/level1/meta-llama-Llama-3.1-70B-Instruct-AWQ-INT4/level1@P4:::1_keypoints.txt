- Proposes LoSST, a novel online algorithm for tracking changes in graphical model structure/parameters under a local i.i.d. assumption.
- Fills a significant gap by enabling real-time online structure learning for graphical models.
- Paper is well-written with clear problem setup, related work, and algorithm description.
- LoSST is described in detail, including sufficient-statistics tracking, update equations, and learning process.
- Strong theoretical analysis provided, including convergence and diligence properties.
- Comprehensive simulations demonstrate performance across varied scenarios.
- Real-world application (US price index volatility) shows practical potential.
- Main weakness: algorithm complexity and many tunable parameters may hinder practical use.
- Performance may degrade when the generating structure changes very rapidly or data are random-order mixtures of multiple structures.
- Some heuristic components (e.g., Liptak's method for weighted p-value pooling) may be suboptimal and improvable.
- Originality: method is a novel contribution distinct from prior online learning and changepoint detection work.
- Significance: demonstrates ability to track dynamic graphical model changes in real time with promising empirical results.
- Quality and clarity: well-organized, clearly explained, and supported by simulations and applications.
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address complexity and tuning issues in future work.
