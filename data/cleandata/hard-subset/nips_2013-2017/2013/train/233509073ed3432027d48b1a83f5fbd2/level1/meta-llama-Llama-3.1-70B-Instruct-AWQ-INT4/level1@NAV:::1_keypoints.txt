- LoSST: a novel algorithm for online tracking of graphical model structure in locally stationary environments.
- Tracks sufficient statistics online and uses a constraint-based structure learner to avoid batch processing.
- Computation is designed to be efficient for real-time operation.
- Shows robust simulation performance, outperforming batch-mode learners under changing structures.
- Demonstrated on real-world data by detecting changes in volatility among US price indexes.
- Applicability is limited to locally stationary settings and may not generalize to all real-world scenarios.
- Relies on heuristic methods that may not be optimal in every case.
- Computational complexity may still be high for large datasets.
- Theoretical guarantees are limited, with unclear behavior under rapidly changing structures.
- Original contribution distinct from prior work in online graphical-structure learning.
- Significant practical impact where batch learning is infeasible, with minimal cost in static settings.
- Pro: novel approach, efficient computation, robust performance, and real-world relevance.
- Con: limited scope, heuristic design, possible computational burden, and incomplete theoretical analysis.
- Recommendation: Accept, but request that authors address limitations and provide stronger theoretical guarantees.
