This paper introduces LoSST, a heuristic algorithm for tracking changes in graphical model structure in a dynamic, real-time manner. The core idea is to track sufficient statistics online and dynamically relearn the graphical model when necessary, balancing convergence and diligence. The authors demonstrate through simulation that LoSST performs competitively with batch-mode learning when the structure is stable, and significantly better when it’s locally stationary. A real-world application to US price index volatility is also presented.

The paper is well-structured and addresses a relevant problem. The motivation is clearly articulated – most existing structure learning algorithms assume stationarity, which is often violated in real-world scenarios. The authors correctly identify the limitations of existing online learning methods in handling general graphical model structure learning. The introduction provides sufficient background and sets the stage for the proposed approach. The related work section is comprehensive, covering both Bayesian/score-based and constraint-based structure learning, as well as online changepoint detection and time-varying graphical models. The authors do a good job of highlighting the shortcomings of each approach in the context of their problem.

The LoSST algorithm itself is described in detail, with clear explanations of the updates to the sufficient statistics (mean, covariance, sample size) and the weighting scheme based on the Mahalanobis distance and pooled p-values. The discussion of the trade-off between convergence and diligence is particularly insightful, and the mathematical formulation of the algorithm is commendable. The simulation results are well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of LoSST, especially in scenarios with changing structures. The application to US price index volatility provides further evidence of the algorithm's potential in real-world applications. The authors are careful to mention limitations and potential future directions.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The problem of online graphical model structure learning is largely unexplored, and LoSST provides a valuable contribution to the field.
* **Technical Soundness:** The algorithm is mathematically well-defined and the derivations of the update equations are clear.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The simulation results are thorough and demonstrate the algorithm's performance in various scenarios. The real-world application adds further support.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand, despite the technical complexity of the topic. The explanations are detailed and the notation is consistent.
* **Trade-off Discussion:** The authors proactively discuss and analyze the fundamental trade-off between convergence and diligence.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Heuristic Nature:** LoSST is described as a heuristic algorithm, and the authors acknowledge this. While the simulations are promising, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the algorithm's properties would be beneficial. It’s not entirely clear *why* the chosen parameters (T, γ) work well, beyond the empirical observations.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance of LoSST likely depends on the choice of parameters (e.g., learning rate, threshold T, maximum weight γ). The paper provides some guidance on setting these parameters, but a more systematic sensitivity analysis would be helpful.
* **Computational Complexity:** The paper does not explicitly discuss the computational complexity of the algorithm, especially the graph relearning step. This could be a concern for large-scale datasets. The PC algorithm itself is known to be computationally expensive.
* **Limited Scope of Application:** The application to US price index volatility is interesting, but it's limited to a single dataset. Applying LoSST to other real-world problems would strengthen the evidence of its practical utility.
* **Missing comparison to other online structure learning algorithms:** Although the authors mention the limitations of existing algorithms, a direct comparison to those that *do* attempt online learning (even if not for graphical models) would be helpful.


**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant and largely unexplored problem in graphical model learning.
* The proposed algorithm is technically sound and well-motivated.
* The simulation results are convincing and demonstrate the effectiveness of LoSST.
* The real-world application provides further evidence of the algorithm's potential.
* The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The heuristic nature of the algorithm and the lack of rigorous theoretical analysis.
* The potential parameter sensitivity and computational complexity.
* The limited scope of the real-world application.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field and should be accepted. The problem is important, the proposed algorithm is novel and well-designed, and the experimental results are compelling. While the heuristic nature of the algorithm and the potential parameter sensitivity are weaknesses, they are outweighed by the strengths of the paper. Addressing the computational complexity more explicitly in a revised version would be beneficial. I rate this paper as a **Strong Accept**.
