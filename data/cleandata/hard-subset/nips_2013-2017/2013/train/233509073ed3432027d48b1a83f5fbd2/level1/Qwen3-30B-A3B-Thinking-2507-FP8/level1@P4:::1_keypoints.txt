- Introduces LoSST: an online algorithm for tracking graphical model structure in locally stationary (slowly changing) environments.
- Identifies a gap: most prior work assumes global stationarity or relies on batch methods or abrupt changepoint detection unsuitable for gradual change.
- Tracks sufficient statistics online (means, covariances, effective sample size) with explicit update equations.
- Detects structural change using Mahalanobis distances, pooled p-values via Liptak’s method, and adaptive weighting to ignore isolated outliers.
- Provides a formal analysis of the convergence vs. diligence trade-off and shows LoSST can be tuned to balance them.
- Simulation experiments test both stationary and locally stationary cases with appropriate baselines (PC algorithm) and clear metrics (edge addition/removal/orientation errors).
- Real-world demonstration on US price index volatility data shows practical utility and robustness to outliers.
- Novelty lies in combining online tracking, pooled p-values, and adaptive weights to follow gradual structural evolution rather than just changepoints.
- Technically sound: rigorous mathematical motivation, well-justified algorithm, and accessible theoretical presentation.
- Clear and well-organized exposition with precise algorithmic details and well-labeled figures.
- Limited real-world validation: evaluation relies mainly on synthetic data with only one real dataset.
- Parameter sensitivity concerns: key parameters T and γ were chosen via an “unsystematic investigation” and need systematic tuning guidance.
- Lacks explicit empirical comparison to other potential online structure-learning methods.
- Computational complexity not analyzed in detail—graph relearning costs could be problematic in practice.
- Overall assessment: significant, well-executed contribution addressing an important problem; reviewer recommends acceptance.
