## Review of "Tracking Time-varying Graphical Structure"

This paper introduces LoSST, a novel heuristic algorithm for online structure learning in graphical models, addressing the critical gap in existing literature which largely assumes static generative processes. The core idea is to track sufficient statistics online and dynamically relearn the graphical model structure as changes are detected, offering a compelling approach for real-world applications where data is locally, rather than globally, stationary. The authors clearly articulate the motivation, positioning their work against both traditional batch-mode learning and existing online learning methods that lack graphical model structure learning capabilities.

The strengths of the paper lie in its clear identification of a significant problem – adapting graphical models to dynamic environments – and its practical, heuristic solution. The algorithm’s design, leveraging weighted sufficient statistics and a probabilistic relearning schedule, appears well-motivated and allows for a balance between convergence and diligence, a point the authors thoughtfully discuss. The simulation results are convincing, demonstrating LoSST’s comparable performance to batch methods in stable environments and its superiority in dynamic ones. The application to US price index data provides a compelling, albeit preliminary, demonstration of real-world applicability. 

However, the paper also has weaknesses. While the authors acknowledge the heuristic nature of their approach, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of its convergence properties, even under specific assumptions, would strengthen the contribution. The parameter tuning (γ, T, ν) appears somewhat ad-hoc, and a more systematic exploration of their impact on performance is needed. The comparison to existing changepoint detection methods feels brief; a more detailed discussion of why these methods are insufficient for *structure* learning would be beneficial.  The presentation of the simulation results could be improved with statistical significance testing. Finally, the discussion of the application to US price index volatility is descriptive rather than analytical; a quantitative evaluation of LoSST’s performance against a baseline in this domain would be valuable.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant and under-explored problem in graphical model learning.
*   Presents a practical and well-motivated heuristic algorithm.
*   Demonstrates promising performance through simulations and a real-world case study.
*   Clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of convergence properties.
*   Parameter tuning is somewhat ad-hoc.
*   Limited comparative analysis with existing changepoint detection methods.
*   Real-world application lacks quantitative evaluation.



**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of graphical model learning. While further theoretical and experimental work would strengthen the paper, the novelty of the problem addressed, the practicality of the proposed solution, and the promising results justify acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
