- Proposes LoSST, an online heuristic for learning graphical-model structure under local (time-varying) stationarity.  
- Maintains weighted sufficient statistics (mean, covariance, effective sample size) and updates them online.  
- Detects distributional/structural changes using Mahalanobis distances and a pooled p-value (Liptak's method).  
- Adapts by increasing weight on new data (forgetting old data) and probabilistically triggering structure relearning with PC.  
- Differentiates LoSST from batch learners, online parameter estimators, CPD methods, and prior batch time-varying graph methods.  
- Technically sound: correct online update equations and well-motivated change-detection heuristic.  
- Empirically strong: matches batch-PC in stable settings and significantly outperforms it when structure/parameters change.  
- Includes a useful experiment showing probabilistic relearning can reduce computation without major performance loss.  
- Insightful discussion of the convergence (stability) vs. diligence (responsiveness) trade-off.  
- Main weakness: reliance on several heuristic parameters (T, γ, ν) chosen via unsystematic investigation.  
- Clarity: exceptionally clear, well-organized exposition and reproducible-level detail.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of weighted statistics, Mahalanobis detection, and online relearning for non-stationary structure learning.  
- Significance: high practical relevance for dynamic domains (finance, climate, sensor networks) and general adaptability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an under-explored problem, proposes a well-motivated algorithm, provides strong empirical evidence, is clearly written, and offers conceptual depth.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heuristic tuning required, limited scalability analysis (only up to 20 variables), and extensibility claims not empirically demonstrated.  
- Conclusion: strengths outweigh weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
