- Paper introduces LoSST, a heuristic algorithm for online tracking and relearning of graphical model structure using online sufficient-statistic updates.
- Core idea: balance convergence vs. diligence by weighting sufficient statistics and relearning structure when Mahalanobis-based tests signal change.
- Demonstrated in simulation: LoSST matches batch learning when structure is stable and outperforms when structure is locally stationary.
- Real-world application to US price index volatility shows practical potential, though limited to a single dataset.
- Motivation and problem framing are clear: most structure-learning methods assume stationarity, which is often violated in practice.
- Related work coverage is comprehensive, contrasting Bayesian/score-based, constraint-based, changepoint detection, and time-varying graphical models.
- Algorithm description and update equations are detailed and mathematically well-defined.
- Trade-off between convergence and diligence is thoughtfully discussed and central to the method.
- Evaluation is thorough in simulation and includes a useful real-data illustration.
- Strength: novel contribution to an underexplored problem of online graphical model structure learning.
- Strength: clear writing, consistent notation, and insightful discussion of limitations and future work.
- Weakness: LoSST is heuristic with limited theoretical analysis explaining parameter choices (T, γ) and convergence properties.
- Weakness: potential parameter sensitivity; systematic sensitivity analysis is lacking.
- Weakness: computational complexity, especially graph relearning and use of PC, is not explicitly analyzed and may limit scalability.
- Weakness: limited empirical scope—only one real-world dataset tested and missing direct comparisons to other online structure-learning methods.
- Recommendation: reviewer favors acceptance (Strong Accept) given novelty, soundness, and convincing experiments, but suggests addressing complexity and theoretical/empirical sensitivity in revision.
