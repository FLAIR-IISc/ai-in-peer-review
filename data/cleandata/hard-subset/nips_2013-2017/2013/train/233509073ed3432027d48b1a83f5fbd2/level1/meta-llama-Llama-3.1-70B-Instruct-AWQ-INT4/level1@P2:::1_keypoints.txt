- Proposes LoSST, a novel heuristic algorithm for real-time tracking of graphical model structure and parameters.  
- Core idea: track locally stationary sufficient statistics and use them to learn the graphical model structure; update equations and learning method provided.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm’s properties, including convergence and diligence.  
- Identifies a convergence vs. responsiveness trade-off: can converge to true structure in the limit but becomes less responsive to changes.  
- Parameter γ controls diligence: increasing γ makes the algorithm more diligent but less convergent/responsive.  
- Simulation results show LoSST is comparable to batch-mode learning when structure is stable and significantly better when structure changes.  
- Demonstrates the importance of pooled p-values for detecting changes in underlying structure.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, making the methods and results easy to follow.  
- Strength: detailed algorithm description, theoretical results, and well-presented simulations.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear, detailed comparison to other online structure-learning algorithms.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of the algorithm’s limitations and the types of structures for which it is unsuitable.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address comparison to related methods and clearer limitation discussion.
