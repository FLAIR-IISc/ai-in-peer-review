- Proposes LoSST, a heuristic online structure-learning algorithm that tracks sufficient statistics and triggers relearning using Mahalanobis-distance change detection.  
- Addresses the important gap of non-stationary generative processes, contrasting LoSST with Bayesian/score-based, constraint-based, online changepoint, and time-indexed graph methods.  
- Well-motivated, practically focused, and clearly presented with careful discussion of the convergence vs. diligence parameter trade-off.  
- Provides detailed mathematical formulations for updating sufficient statistics and weight computation, adding technical rigor.  
- Simulations show LoSST can track changes in graph structure and parameters and compete with batch learning when structure is stable.  
- Real-world application to US price index volatility demonstrates practical potential.  
- Simulations are limited in scope and should include more complex dynamics (e.g., gradual changes, multiple interacting changing variables).  
- Reliance on the PC algorithm for structure learning may be a bottleneck and limit performance.  
- Sensitivity of results to the probabilistic relearning schedule is insufficiently explored and requires more comprehensive analysis.  
- Using Mahalanobis distance as the sole change indicator may be vulnerable to outliers and high dimensionality; alternative detectors should be considered.  
- Related-work discussion is thorough but does not fully engage with recent advances in online nonparametric graphical model learning.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting expanded sensitivity analysis and broader simulation studies.
