- Proposes LoSST, an online heuristic that tracks time-varying directed graph structure by dynamically reweighting sufficient statistics under a local-stationarity assumption.  
- Uses Mahalanobis distance → p-values → Liptak pooled p-value to detect systematic model deviation and adjust data weights (forgetting old data).  
- Employs a probabilistic relearning mechanism to avoid expensive structure learning every time step, increasing relearn probability when changes are detected.  
- Theoretical analysis frames a diligence vs. convergence trade-off, explaining how parameters control adaptation speed versus stability.  
- Online update equations for weighted means/covariances are correctly derived and standard; the p-value pooling idea is a novel synthesis for structure learning.  
- Experiments are thorough: LoSST matches batch PC in stationary settings and outperforms it under structural/parameter changes.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-motivated, and figures effectively support claims.  
- The approach is original in combining online sufficient-statistic weighting, Mahalanobis-based change scoring, and probabilistic relearning for online structure learning.  
- High potential impact across domains (finance, climate, biology) since it relaxes the common stationarity assumption with little cost in stationary cases.  
- Key practical weakness: sensitivity to parameters (T, gamma, nu) chosen by unsystematic search; needs systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Experimental limitation: evaluation uses only the PC constraint-based learner on continuous linear SEMs; lack of score-based and discrete-model experiments narrows applicability evidence.  
- Scope limitation: current focus on linear Gaussian SEMs; extending to discrete/Bayesian networks poses nontrivial challenges (tracking many conditional counts).  
- Proofs for diligence/convergence are brief sketches; full derivations or appendix proofs would strengthen technical completeness.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — significant, well-executed contribution with minor weaknesses that suggest useful future work.
