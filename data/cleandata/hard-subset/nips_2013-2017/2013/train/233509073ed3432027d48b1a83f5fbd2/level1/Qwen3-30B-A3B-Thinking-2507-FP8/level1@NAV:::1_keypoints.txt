- Introduces LoSST, a heuristic algorithm for online learning of time-varying graphical model structure under local stationarity.  
- Addresses gap where standard structure learning assumes global stationarity and existing online methods cannot handle general graphical structure learning.  
- Tracks weighted sufficient statistics (means, covariances, effective sample size) using Mahalanobis distance-based weighting.  
- Dynamically triggers graph relearning based on pooled p-values.  
- Shows via simulations that LoSST matches batch-mode performance under global stationarity.  
- Demonstrates substantial outperforming of batch methods when structures change locally or parametrically.  
- Validates real-world applicability with a US price index volatility case study.  
- Provides sophisticated theoretical analysis of the convergence–diligence trade-off (Section 3.1).  
- Comprehensive simulations across multiple graph sizes and change scenarios; algorithm has “no cost” in stable environments.  
- Writing and technical exposition are clear and rigorous (Sections 3–4).  
- Lacks discussion or experiments for non-Gaussian extensions (e.g., discrete/Bayesian networks).  
- Probabilistic relearning schedule is heuristic; choice of ν=0.005 is not empirically justified.  
- Scalability to large graphs (e.g., >100 variables) is untested.  
- Comparison baseline limited to PC; inclusion of score-based methods (e.g., BDeu) would strengthen claims.  
- Overall recommendation: significant, high-quality contribution with minor weaknesses; accept.
