This paper proposes a novel, heuristic structure learning algorithm called LoSST (Locally Stationary Structure Tracker) that tracks changes in graphical model structure or parameters in a dynamic, real-time manner. The authors address the problem of learning graphical model structure in environments where the underlying generating process can change over time, which is a common scenario in real-world applications.

The authors provide a clear and concise overview of the related work, highlighting the limitations of existing structure learning algorithms and online learning methods. They also explain how LoSST differs from previous approaches, such as Bayesian online changepoint detection and frequentist online changepoint detection, which require storing substantial portions of the input data.

The LoSST algorithm is described in detail, including the tracking of sufficient statistics, such as means, covariances, and sample size, in an online fashion. The authors also explain how the algorithm learns the graphical model structure using a constraint-based structure learning algorithm, such as the PC algorithm.

The paper presents simulation results that demonstrate the performance of LoSST in tracking changes in graphical model structure and parameters. The results show that LoSST performs comparably to batch-mode learning when the generating graphical structure is globally stationary and significantly better when it is only locally stationary.

The authors also apply LoSST to real-world data, specifically seasonally adjusted price index data from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics. The results demonstrate the algorithm's ability to detect changes in the volatility of the relationships between price indexes, including the Great Moderation.
