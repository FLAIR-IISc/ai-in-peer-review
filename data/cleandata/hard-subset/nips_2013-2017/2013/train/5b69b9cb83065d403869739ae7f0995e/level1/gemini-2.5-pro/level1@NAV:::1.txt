This paper presents an algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction based on Approximate Message Passing (AMP), derived from belief propagation on a graphical model. The authors extend a prior AMP framework [9] to handle not only posterior mean estimation but also Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) estimation, unifying both problems through a single inverse temperature parameter. The work's primary contribution is a novel and effective application of this framework to K-means clustering. This is achieved by reformulating clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction task where one factor matrix is constrained to contain one-hot encoded vectors representing cluster assignments. This approach connects to the broad literature on AMP in signal processing [6, 7] and recent work applying it to matrix factorization and blind calibration problems [13, 14].

**Strengths:**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The derivation is a logical extension of existing AMP literature. The claims of superior performance are well-supported by thorough numerical experiments on both synthetic and real-world (ORL faces) data, which consistently show the proposed AMP-based clustering algorithms outperforming strong baselines like Lloyd's K-means and K-means++. Proposition 1 provides a valuable theoretical insight, linking the fixed points of the proposed algorithm for the MAP problem to those of the Iterated Conditional Modes (ICM) algorithm.
*   **Originality:** The main novelty lies in the successful application of the AMP framework to K-means clustering. The resulting update rule for cluster assignment (Eq. 18b) is particularly insightful, as it modifies the standard distance minimization with principled correction terms that account for the influence of a data point on its current cluster center and the relative size of clusters. This provides a fresh and principled perspective on a classic problem.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The problem is clearly formulated, the proposed algorithm is presented logically, and the connection between the general matrix reconstruction problem and the specific clustering application is seamless.
*   **Significance:** The results are highly significant. The paper presents a new algorithm that demonstrably improves upon K-means++, a widely used and effective clustering method. Given the ubiquity of clustering, an algorithm that offers better performance with a comparable computational cost is a valuable contribution that researchers and practitioners are likely to build upon.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The theoretical contribution, while solid, is an incremental extension of an existing line of research on AMP algorithms. The state evolution analysis, a powerful tool for characterizing the performance of AMP algorithms, is mentioned but not included, which would have further strengthened the paper's theoretical foundation.

**Arguments Pro/Con Acceptance:**
*   **Pro:** The paper introduces a novel and high-performing algorithm for the fundamental problem of clustering, supported by strong empirical evidence and clear theoretical motivation.
*   **Con:** The core AMP methodology is an extension of prior work, not a completely new theoretical framework.

This is a strong paper with clear practical and theoretical contributions. The novel application to clustering is compelling and the results are impressive. I strongly recommend acceptance.
