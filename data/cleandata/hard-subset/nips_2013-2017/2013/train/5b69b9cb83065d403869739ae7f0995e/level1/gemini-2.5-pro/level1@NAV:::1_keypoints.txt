- Paper introduces an AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending AMP to handle both posterior mean and MAP estimation via an inverse temperature parameter.
- Main contribution is applying this unified AMP framework to K-means by reformulating clustering as low-rank factorization with one-hot cluster assignment constraints.
- The derived cluster-assignment update (Eq. 18b) adds principled correction terms accounting for a point's influence on its cluster center and cluster sizes.
- Theoretical link: Proposition 1 connects the MAP fixed points of the proposed algorithm to Iterated Conditional Modes (ICM).
- Empirical evidence: thorough experiments on synthetic and ORL face data show the AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++.
- Quality: derivation is technically sound and a logical extension of existing AMP literature.
- Originality: novelty lies in the successful and insightful application of AMP to K-means rather than in new AMP theory.
- Clarity: the paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly formulating the problem and algorithm.
- Significance: offers a practically valuable clustering algorithm that improves on a widely used baseline at comparable computational cost.
- Weakness: theoretical contribution is incremental relative to prior AMP work and lacks a state evolution analysis that would strengthen theoretical claims.
- Acceptance arguments — Pro: novel, high-performing algorithm with strong empirical and theoretical motivation.
- Acceptance arguments — Con: core AMP methodology is an extension of prior work, not a wholly new theoretical framework.
- Recommendation: strong recommendation for acceptance.
