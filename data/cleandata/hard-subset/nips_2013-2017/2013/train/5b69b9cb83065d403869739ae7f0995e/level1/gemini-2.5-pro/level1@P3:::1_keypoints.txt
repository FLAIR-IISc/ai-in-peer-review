- Proposes a general AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles posterior marginals and full MAP estimation.
- Introduces an inverse temperature parameter β to unify posterior mean and MAP estimation within the AMP framework.
- Resulting algorithm (Algorithm 2) is linear-time in the number of matrix entries and includes Onsager correction terms absent in VB/ICM.
- Applies the AMP framework to K-means by modeling cluster assignments as one-hot factors, yielding a new iterative clustering algorithm (Algorithm 3).
- The modified assignment update penalizes reassigning a point to its own cluster and favors smaller clusters, helping escape poor local minima.
- Strong empirical results on synthetic and real datasets: AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and VB-based clustering in loss and accuracy.
- Originality/significance: novel and principled AMP-derived improvement to Lloyd’s algorithm with clear intuition for the correction term.
- Technical quality: solid derivation from belief propagation and useful theoretical result (Proposition 1) linking MAP-AMP fixed points to ICM fixed points.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, problem setup and connection between low-rank reconstruction and clustering are clearly explained.
- Omission of state evolution analysis: lacks large-system theoretical characterization that would provide formal performance guarantees.
- Convergence concerns: assumes algorithm convergence, reports oscillations in experiments, and lacks thorough discussion of damping/stability techniques.
- Handling of noise variance τ is ad hoc (iterative heuristic); sensitivity analysis or principled estimation is missing.
- Full derivation is in the supplementary material, limiting immediate vetting of technical details from the main paper.
- Overall evaluation: significant, well-motivated contribution with empirical support; recommendation to accept.
