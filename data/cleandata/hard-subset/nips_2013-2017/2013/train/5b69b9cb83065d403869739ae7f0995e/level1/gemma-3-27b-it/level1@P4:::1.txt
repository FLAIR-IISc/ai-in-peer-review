This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework, extending previous work on AMP for matrix reconstruction and applying it to the clustering problem. The core idea is to leverage the flexibility of the Bayesian approach to incorporate structural properties of the matrix factors, while utilizing AMP for efficient inference. The authors claim their algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

The paper is well-structured and the introduction clearly motivates the problem and the proposed solution. The connection to existing work is reasonably well established, citing relevant papers on low-rank matrix reconstruction, Bayesian inference, AMP, and clustering. The problem setting is meticulously defined, and the derivation of the AMP algorithm is presented in a logical manner, building upon previous research (specifically [9]). The application to clustering is presented as a natural extension of the reconstruction problem. The numerical experiments are comprehensive, including both artificial and real-world datasets, and demonstrate promising results. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivation of the AMP algorithm appears to be solid, building upon established theory. The extension to handle MAP estimation is a valuable contribution. The connection to variational Bayes methods is discussed, providing context.
*   **Novelty:** The extension of AMP to the general-rank case, enabling MAP estimation, represents a novel advance over [9]. The application of the AMP algorithm to clustering, specifically addressing the constraint of exclusive cluster assignment, is also a significant contribution.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The notation is consistent, and the mathematical expressions are clearly presented.
*   **Significance:**  The potential for more efficient and accurate clustering, particularly in high-dimensional settings, is a significant contribution. The theoretical discussion and connection to state evolution (though not fully detailed due to space constraints) suggest a deeper understanding of the algorithm’s performance.  The algorithm’s performance compared to K-means and K-means++ is well-demonstrated experimentally.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Convergence Guarantee:** The paper acknowledges that convergence of the AMP algorithm isn't guaranteed. A more detailed discussion of conditions under which convergence can be expected, or potential remedies for non-convergence, would be beneficial. Proposition 1 provides *some* assurance in certain scenarios, but a broader discussion of stability would be appreciated.
*   **Parameter Selection:**  The discussion of parameter selection (e.g., τ) is limited. While an empirical method is proposed for estimating τ, a more thorough analysis of the algorithm’s sensitivity to parameter choices and guidance for selecting appropriate values would improve practical usability.
*   **Complexity of Implementation:** The algorithm, while efficient in theory, may present implementation challenges due to the need to compute the functions *f* and *G*, particularly when closed-form expressions are not available.  A discussion of practical implementation considerations and computational cost would be helpful.
*   **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** While the comparison to K-means and K-means++ is valuable, a comparison to more recent and sophisticated clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering, deep clustering methods) would strengthen the assessment of the proposed method’s competitiveness.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, outperforming K-means and K-means++ in several scenarios.
*   The theoretical grounding of the algorithm is solid, building upon established research in AMP and Bayesian inference.
*   The application to clustering with the exclusive assignment constraint is a significant contribution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of a comprehensive convergence analysis and parameter selection guidelines could limit the algorithm’s practical applicability.
*   The comparison to a wider range of state-of-the-art clustering algorithms would provide a more robust assessment of the method’s performance.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The technical soundness, novelty, and experimental results are compelling. The weaknesses are relatively minor and do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the issues of convergence analysis, parameter selection, and comparison to more state-of-the-art algorithms. Specifically, the authors should expand on the conditions under which the algorithm is guaranteed to converge and provide more practical guidance on parameter selection.  A brief discussion of the computational complexity of implementing the algorithm would also be beneficial.
