- Presents a novel AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction within a flexible Bayesian framework allowing priors on factors U and V.  
- Unifies marginalization (posterior means) and MAP estimation via an inverse temperature parameter β.  
- Derives a general AMP that handles both posterior mean and MAP, extending prior AMP work limited to means.  
- Reformulates K-means clustering as low-rank reconstruction with one-hot assignment factor and derives AMP-KM enforcing single-cluster membership.  
- Proposes AMP-MA ("maximum accuracy") variant alongside AMP-KM.  
- Empirical comparisons on synthetic and ORL face data show AMP methods outperform Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and VBMF in K-means loss and accuracy.  
- Algorithm derivations are technically sound and consistent with AMP literature; detailed derivations are in the supplement.  
- Proposition 1: any MAP-AMP fixed point is also an ICM (e.g., Lloyd's) fixed point, offering theoretical insight into solution landscapes.  
- Notes potential for oscillations in AMP and proposes a simple practical stopping criterion.  
- Lacks state evolution analysis in the main paper, which would strengthen theoretical justification.  
- Paper is very well written, organized, and clear; algorithm presentation and clustering derivation are especially lucid.  
- Provides intuition for bias-correction terms that differentiate AMP from Lloyd’s K-means.  
- Original contribution: generalization of AMP to MAP and marginalization and first specialized message-passing algorithm enforcing one-hot clustering constraints.  
- Significance: offers a powerful tool for Bayesian low-rank models and a practical, high-performing alternative to standard clustering methods.  
- Potential for broader impact by incorporating other priors (e.g., sparsity) to create new constrained or semi-supervised clustering methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel general AMP, strong empirical performance, theoretical insight, clear presentation, and practical significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of state evolution in main text and limited demonstration of framework flexibility beyond clustering.
