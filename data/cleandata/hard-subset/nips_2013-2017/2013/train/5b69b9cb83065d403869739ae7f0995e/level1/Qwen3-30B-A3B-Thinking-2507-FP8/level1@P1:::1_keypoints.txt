- Summary: Proposes a Bayesian AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, unifies marginalization and MAP via parameter β, and applies it to K-means clustering with strong empirical gains.

- Strength — Technical soundness: Rigorous extension of AMP to general-rank matrices with Proposition 1 linking AMP and ICM fixed points and flexible Bayesian modeling of factor structure.

- Strength — Novelty: Extends AMP beyond rank-one, unifies marginal/MAP treatment with β, and provides a novel clustering formulation enforcing one-cluster-per-datum.

- Strength — Experiments: Comprehensive synthetic (500 instances) and real-data (ORL faces) experiments showing consistent accuracy and faster convergence vs. Lloyd’s, variational Bayes, and K-means++.

- Strength — Clarity: Well-organized, consistent notation, and clearly explained algorithms (notably Algorithm 3 comparing to Lloyd’s).

- Weakness — State evolution: Does not provide state evolution analysis, omitting a key theoretical AMP property and performance predictor.

- Weakness — Computational complexity: Claims O(mN) per iteration but lacks detailed practical comparison to alternatives (e.g., variational Bayes).

- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: No discussion on sensitivity or selection of β, though it crucially affects MAP vs. marginalization behavior.

- Weakness — Comparison to prior work: Insufficient detailed comparison to related AMP work (e.g., Matsushita & Tanaka 2012, Krzakala et al. 2013).

- Argument for acceptance: Strong theoretical contribution extending AMP to low-rank problems with practical clustering application and robust empirical improvements.

- Argument for acceptance: Unified β-based treatment is elegant and practically useful for switching between marginalization and MAP.

- Argument for acceptance: Novel clustering formulation that enforces discrete assignment constraints and empirically outperforms baselines.

- Argument for acceptance: Thorough numerical validation demonstrating consistent superiority and competitive convergence speed.

- Argument for acceptance: Addresses an important ML problem with clear practical implications.

- Argument against acceptance: Missing state evolution weakens theoretical completeness.

- Argument against acceptance: Insufficient analysis of real computational costs and comparisons for practical scalability.

- Argument against acceptance: Lacks guidance on parameter (β) selection and sensitivity.

- Overall assessment: Significant, well-validated contribution extending AMP to low-rank reconstruction and clustering, with theoretical gaps but overall merits outweigh weaknesses.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Additional comments: Authors should provide a high-level discussion of state evolution for their method and include a more detailed computational complexity comparison with variational Bayes.
