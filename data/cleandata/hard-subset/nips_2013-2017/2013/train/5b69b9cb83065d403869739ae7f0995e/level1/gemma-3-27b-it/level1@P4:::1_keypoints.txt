- Proposes a Bayesian approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering with exclusive assignments.  
- Leverages Bayesian flexibility to incorporate structural priors on matrix factors while using AMP for efficient inference.  
- Claims superior clustering performance versus Lloyd’s K-means (and K-means++) especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Paper is well-structured with a clear introduction and a carefully defined problem setting.  
- Derivation of the AMP algorithm is logical and builds on prior work (notably [9]).  
- Numerical experiments are comprehensive (synthetic and real data) and show promising results.  
- Technical soundness: mathematical derivation appears solid and extension to MAP estimation is valuable.  
- Novelty: extends AMP to general-rank MAP estimation and adapts it for exclusive cluster assignments.  
- Clarity: writing, notation, and mathematical presentation are consistent and readable.  
- Significance: potential for more efficient and accurate clustering; links to state evolution indicate theoretical understanding.  
- Weakness — Convergence: no comprehensive convergence guarantee; Proposition 1 is limited and broader stability analysis is needed.  
- Weakness — Parameter selection: limited discussion and sensitivity analysis for parameters (e.g., τ); only an empirical estimator is provided.  
- Weakness — Implementation complexity: practical computation of functions f and G may be challenging when closed forms are unavailable; complexity discussion is missing.  
- Weakness — Limited baselines: comparisons are restricted to K-means variants; missing comparisons to spectral clustering, deep clustering, and other modern methods.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, technically sound, experimentally effective, and significant application to clustering.  
- Rejection arguments: practical applicability hindered by incomplete convergence analysis, limited parameter guidance, and insufficient comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand convergence discussion, provide parameter-selection guidance, and include computational complexity and stronger baseline comparisons.
