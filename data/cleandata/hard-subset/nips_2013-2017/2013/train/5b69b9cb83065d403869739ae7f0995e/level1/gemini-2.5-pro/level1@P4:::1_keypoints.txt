- Proposes a novel AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that unifies marginal posterior and MAP estimation via an inverse temperature parameter β.
- Derives an AMP specialization for K-means (AMP-KM) by modeling cluster assignments as one-hot factor rows.
- AMP-KM update rules include Onsager/corrective terms that account for a point's influence on its cluster center and bias assignments toward smaller clusters.
- Theoretical result (Proposition 1) shows any AMP MAP fixed point is also an ICM fixed point, positioning AMP relative to ICM.
- Algorithm derivation follows established belief-propagation/AMP methodology and correctly identifies differences from VBMF and ICM.
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic and real data shows AMP-KM/AMP-MA outperform Lloyd's K-means, K-means++, and VBMF on loss and accuracy.
- Results are reported with appropriate metrics and statistical summaries over multiple runs.
- Computational cost is competitive (O(mN) per iteration), making the method practically appealing.
- The general Bayesian AMP framework is flexible and applicable to other tasks (e.g., matrix completion with sparsity priors).
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and provides insightful interpretation of new update terms.
- Original contributions include AMP extension to MAP via β-parameterization and the novel AMP-based clustering algorithm derived from first principles.
- Convergence of the algorithm is not guaranteed and is assumed; the paper uses an ad-hoc stopping criterion to handle oscillations.
- Omission of detailed state-evolution or convergence analysis limits theoretical guarantees.
- The noise-parameter (τ) estimation in K-means is heuristic and sensitivity to this choice is not thoroughly studied.
