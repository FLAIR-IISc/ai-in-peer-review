- Summary: Proposes a β‑tempered AMP for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization that unifies marginal (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference, with separable denoisers, Onsager corrections, and a proposition linking AMP-MAP fixed points to blockwise MAP optima; specialized to one-hot V giving an AMP variant of K‑means.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on AMP and bilinear AMP literature (Donoho, Rangan, Rangan & Fletcher, Krzakala et al., etc.), extending to general rank and MAP/marginal unification and connecting to VB/ICM.
- Strength — Conceptual clarity: Clean unification of marginal and MAP via β-tempering with implementable updates for broad priors.
- Strength — Algorithmic novelty: General-rank bilinear AMP with MAP capability and a principled AMP‑Kmeans specialization with Onsager corrections for assignments.
- Strength — Theoretical insight: Proposition 1 links AMP fixed points to blockwise optima and relates AMP fixed points to ICM; Eq. (16) clarifies Onsager roles.
- Strength — Empirical improvements: AMP‑KM lowers K‑means loss relative to Lloyd’s/Kmeans++; Bayesian AMP shows higher accuracy than VBMF‑MA; ORL faces show improvements in most trials.
- Strength — Practical efficiency: O(mN) per iteration with separable denoisers and fast empirical convergence.
- Weakness — Limited empirical scope: Experiments focus mainly on clustering; other structured priors (sparse/nonnegative) and tasks (denoising, completion) are not validated.
- Weakness — Insufficient comparisons: Missing head‑to‑head baselines like GMM/EM, spherical K‑means, spectral clustering, and related bilinear AMP (BiG‑AMP).
- Weakness — Incomplete theory/convergence: State evolution omitted, convergence assumed, oscillations observed and handled ad hoc without damping or diagnostics.
- Weakness — Sensitivity/robustness unclear: No systematic study of prior mismatch, τ estimation, rank misspecification, or noise model violations; empirical failures (collapse) occurred.
- Weakness — Practical denoiser tractability: For general priors f and G may need r̂‑dimensional integration/optimization; implementation details left underspecified.
- Quality: Technically plausible and well‑motivated, but strength of claims limited by missing state evolution, convergence analysis, and broader experiments.
- Clarity: Well‑organized and precise with consistent notation; supplement needed for derivations; would benefit from more intuition on Onsager terms and guidance for f/G and τ.
- Originality: Moderately strong — meaningful extension to MAP and novel exact one‑hot clustering within AMP, but novelty could be sharpened with comparisons to contemporaneous work.
- Significance: Promising and potentially broadly applicable, but impact is moderate until broader benchmarks, theory, and comparisons are provided.
- Suggestions: Add state evolution/ MSE validation and damping discussion; compare to BiG‑AMP, GMM/EM, spectral and spherical K‑means; test robustness to prior/rank/noise mismatch; demonstrate other structured priors; clarify computational tricks for f/G.
- Arguments for acceptance: Principled β‑AMP unification, novel AMP‑Kmeans with empirical gains, useful theoretical links to ICM, and an efficient framework with practical promise.
- Arguments against acceptance: Missing state evolution and convergence guarantees, narrow empirical validation and baselines, lack of comparison to related AMP methods, and under‑specified implementation details.
- Overall recommendation: Lean accept.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
