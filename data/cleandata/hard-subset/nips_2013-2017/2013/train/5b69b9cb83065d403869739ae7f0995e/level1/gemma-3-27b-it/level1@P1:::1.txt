This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework, extending previous work on AMP for matrix reconstruction. It also applies this algorithm to the K-means clustering problem, reformulating it as a specific case of low-rank reconstruction. The key contribution is extending an existing AMP algorithm to allow for MAP estimation, and demonstrating superior performance compared to Lloyd’s K-means, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

The paper is well-motivated. Low-rank matrix reconstruction is a fundamental problem with broad applications, and incorporating prior knowledge (like sparsity) is often crucial. The Bayesian approach offers flexibility, but requires efficient approximate inference methods. AMP has shown promise in related areas like compressed sensing, motivating its application here. The connection to K-means clustering is also a natural and interesting direction. The paper places the work in context with references to relevant literature, including variational Bayes methods, IterFac, and recent advances in AMP for dictionary learning and blind calibration. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Extension of AMP:** Extending the existing AMP algorithm to handle MAP estimation is a valuable contribution. This allows for a broader range of applications beyond just posterior mean estimation.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The numerical experiments demonstrate that the proposed algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, especially when the data are high-dimensional. The experiments on both artificial and real (ORL faces) datasets add to the credibility of the findings.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a theoretical basis for the algorithm, drawing connections to belief propagation and the Bethe approximation. The discussion of Proposition 1 regarding convergence and optimality is insightful.
* **Clear Formulation:** The problem setup and the algorithm itself are clearly presented, making the paper relatively easy to follow. The use of equations to define the algorithms is helpful.
* **Connection to Clustering:** The reformulation of K-means as a low-rank reconstruction problem provides a fresh perspective and leads to a potentially more efficient clustering algorithm.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited State Evolution Discussion:** While the authors mention the possibility of using state evolution to predict performance, they do not present any such analysis. Including even a brief discussion of state evolution would strengthen the theoretical foundation and provide insights into the algorithm’s behavior.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance of the algorithm likely depends on the choice of the noise variance parameter τ. The paper does not discuss how to choose this parameter optimally, and the estimation method described is brief. A more thorough investigation of parameter sensitivity would be beneficial.
* **Comparison to other recent AMP methods:** While the paper acknowledges related work (Krzakala et al., Parker et al.), it doesn’t provide a detailed comparative analysis.  How does this AMP approach compare to more recent advances in AMP for matrix factorization or dictionary learning in terms of performance and computational cost?
* **Clarity on Convergence:** The condition for stopping the AMP algorithm in the clustering experiments (V t = V t−1 or V t = V t−2) is somewhat ad-hoc.  A more principled stopping criterion, perhaps based on a change in the objective function, would be preferable.
* **The role of β:** The parameter β is introduced as a way to unify the marginalization and MAP problems. However, its impact on performance is not clearly demonstrated. Exploring the effect of different β values could provide valuable insights.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.
* The paper builds upon a strong theoretical foundation and provides a rigorous analysis.
* The work addresses an important problem in machine learning and signal processing.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a detailed state evolution analysis weakens the theoretical justification.
* The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of parameter sensitivity and comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.
* The convergence criterion in the clustering experiments is somewhat arbitrary.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a solid contribution to the field and warrants acceptance. The proposed algorithm is innovative, well-motivated, and supported by strong empirical evidence. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not significantly detract from the overall quality of the work.  Addressing the suggested improvements in a revised version would further strengthen the paper. I rate it as **Accept**.
