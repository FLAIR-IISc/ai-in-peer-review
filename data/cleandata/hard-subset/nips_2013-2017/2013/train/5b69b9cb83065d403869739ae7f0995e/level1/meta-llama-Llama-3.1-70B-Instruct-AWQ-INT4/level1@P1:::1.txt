The paper "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing" presents a novel approach to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering using approximate message passing (AMP) algorithms. The authors formulate the problem in a Bayesian framework, allowing for the incorporation of structural properties of matrices, such as sparsity and non-negativity. They propose an efficient AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, which can be used for both marginalization and MAP problems.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a good balance between theoretical derivations and numerical experiments. The authors provide a thorough review of previous work on low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering, highlighting the limitations of existing methods and the advantages of their proposed approach.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, which is shown to be efficient and effective in numerical experiments. The algorithm is derived using a one-parameter extension of the posterior distribution, which allows for the treatment of both marginalization and MAP problems in a unified manner. The authors also provide a proof of the optimality of the output of the AMP algorithm in a certain sense.

The paper also presents an application of the proposed algorithm to clustering, which is formulated as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem with an additional structural property. The authors show that the AMP algorithm outperforms Lloyd's K-means algorithm in numerical experiments, both in terms of K-means loss and accuracy.
