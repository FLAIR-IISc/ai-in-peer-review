- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means clustering.  
- Extends prior AMP work to handle both marginalization (posterior mean) and MAP estimation, enabling general estimation objectives.  
- Reformulates K-means as low-rank reconstruction with assignment constraint (each row of V is a standard basis vector), directly enforcing single-cluster assignment.  
- Algorithm 3 empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and variational Bayes matrix factorization on synthetic and ORL face datasets.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees about the algorithm’s fixed points (Proposition 1).  
- Technical soundness: clear derivation and rigorous justification for the AMP extension to MAP.  
- Argues AMP’s Bethe approximation better captures dependencies than variational Bayes’ mean-field approximation.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, with consistent notation and thoroughly described experiments.  
- Novelty: first to extend AMP for matrix reconstruction to both marginalization and MAP and to handle clustering assignment constraints properly.  
- Significance: offers a computationally efficient alternative with improved reconstruction error and clustering accuracy, especially in high dimensions.  
- Weakness: convergence of the AMP algorithm is not guaranteed in general; analysis assumes convergence.  
- Weakness: comparison to related AMP-based methods ([13,14]) is superficial and lacks detailed technical/performance contrast.  
- Weakness: limited analysis explaining why AMP outperforms variational Bayes in practice beyond the Bethe argument.  
- Weakness: computational complexity analysis is limited—number of iterations to converge is not characterized.  
- Overall verdict: technically sound, convincing results, and novel—recommend acceptance.
