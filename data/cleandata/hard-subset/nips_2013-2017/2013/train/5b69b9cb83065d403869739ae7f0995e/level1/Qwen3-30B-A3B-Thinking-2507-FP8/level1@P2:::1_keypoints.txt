- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework, allowing structural priors (e.g., sparsity) in addition to low-rankness.  
- Extends AMP to handle both posterior mean (marginalization) and MAP estimation via a tunable parameter β.  
- Applies the method to K-means by reformulating clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction with the constraint that each datum belongs to exactly one cluster.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior AMP/related work by different treatment of the rank parameter and relaxing component-wise independence assumptions.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous derivation and a convergence result (Proposition 1) linking AMP fixed points to ICM fixed points.  
- Experimental evaluation: thorough testing (500 synthetic instances, 50 real-data trials) showing consistent improvements over Lloyd’s K-means, VBMF-MA, and K-means++.  
- Reports both K-means loss and accuracy, and justifies computational cost of O(mN) per iteration.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with detailed pseudocode (Algorithms 2 and 3) enabling replication.  
- Novelty: first to extend AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction with a unified marginalization/MAP framework and flexible structural priors.  
- Significance: demonstrates practical benefits on high-dimensional data (including ORL face dataset) and promises scalability for large-scale matrix problems.  
- Strengths: novel algorithmic extension, solid theory, comprehensive experiments, practical clustering improvements, and clear writing.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient motivation for why AMP outperforms variational Bayes beyond Bethe vs. mean-field intuition.  
- Weaknesses: unclear differentiation from other low-rank clustering approaches (e.g., Xu et al., 2003) regarding the “one-cluster-per-datum” constraint.  
- Weaknesses: would benefit from more diverse real-world datasets to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, significant, and empirically superior—recommend acceptance.
