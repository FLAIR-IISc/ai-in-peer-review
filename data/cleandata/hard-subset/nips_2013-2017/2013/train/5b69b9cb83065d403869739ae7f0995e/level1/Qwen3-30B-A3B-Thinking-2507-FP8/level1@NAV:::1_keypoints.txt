- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending AMP beyond compressed sensing.  
- Extends previous AMP-based low-rank methods to handle MAP estimation and incorporate flexible structural priors (e.g., sparsity).  
- Unifies marginalization and MAP estimation in a single AMP framework.  
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a low-rank reconstruction problem with a hard one-cluster-per-datum constraint, claimed as a novel treatment.  
- Provides a clear theoretical derivation of the AMP algorithm and proves optimality properties (Proposition 1).  
- Algorithm is computationally efficient, with O(mN) complexity per iteration.  
- Well-motivated comparison to variational Bayes approaches.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and ORL face data show consistent improvements in clustering accuracy and convergence speed over Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and VB.  
- Numerical evaluation is thorough and well-presented.  
- Significant contribution by extending AMP to a broader class of low-rank problems with theoretical grounding and practical value for clustering.  
- Lacks thorough discussion of very recent AMP-based matrix reconstruction work [13, 14], especially regarding differing asymptotic assumptions.  
- Could better clarify differences from other low-rank-based clustering approaches.  
- Some theoretical analysis (state evolution) is omitted due to space constraints.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution with technical soundness and strong empirical validation — recommend acceptance.
