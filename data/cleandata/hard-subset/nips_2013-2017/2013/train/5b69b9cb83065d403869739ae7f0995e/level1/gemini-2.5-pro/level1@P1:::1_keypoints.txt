- Introduces an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction allowing flexible priors on factor matrices.  
- Derives AMP from belief propagation with a temperature parameter β that unifies posterior marginals (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).  
- Applies the framework to K-means by constraining one factor to one-hot rows, yielding the AMP-KM algorithm with Onsager “memory” corrections.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real clustering tasks show AMP-based methods outperform Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and a variational Bayes baseline in loss and accuracy.  
- Positions AMP (Bethe approximation) as a more accurate alternative to mean-field variational Bayes for matrix factorization problems.  
- Strength: original and significant extension of AMP to general-rank factorization and a novel, well-motivated clustering algorithm.  
- Strength: technically sound derivation, elegant β unification, and useful theoretical insight via Proposition 1 about avoiding poor local minima.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with intuitive comparison to Lloyd’s updates.  
- Strength: convincing empirical validation with consistent improvements over strong baselines.  
- Weakness: full AMP derivations are relegated to the supplement, limiting verification in the main text.  
- Weakness: convergence is assumed but not guaranteed; algorithm can oscillate and required heuristic stopping in experiments.  
- Weakness: noise variance τ is estimated heuristically online in clustering, with no discussion of effects on behavior or convergence.  
- Weakness: experiments focus only on clustering, leaving the claimed generality (e.g., matrix completion) insufficiently demonstrated.  
- Pro-accept: novel, powerful algorithm with strong empirical gains and solid theoretical motivation.  
- Con-accept: theoretical gaps (convergence, τ handling) and limited scope of experimental validation.  
- Conclusion: overall strong paper with minor weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
