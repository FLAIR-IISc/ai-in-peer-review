Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper addresses Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction A0 = U0V0^T from noisy observations A = A0 + W with Gaussian noise and flexible (possibly improper) priors on rows of U0 and V0. The main methodological contribution is an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm (Algorithm 2) derived from belief propagation for the tempered posterior p̂(U,V|A; β) ∝ p̂(U,V|A)^β, which unifies posterior mean/marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). The algorithm uses vector-valued denoisers f and their Jacobians G, with complexity O(mN) per iteration.

The second contribution recasts K-means-type clustering as a constrained low-rank factorization with one-hot rows in V. Specializing the AMP updates yields an AMP-based K-means algorithm (Algorithm 3) whose assignment step includes “Onsager” corrections: a leave-one-out style bias correction for the current cluster and a preference toward smaller clusters. A maximum-accuracy clustering variant (β=1) is also derived.

The work builds on AMP for compressed sensing [6,7], rank-one bilinear AMP (IterFac) [8], and a prior general-rank AMP method focused on posterior means [9]. It differs from contemporaneous bilinear GAMP/dictionary-learning work [13,14] by fixing rank r (rather than r/m) in the large-system limit and by allowing intra-row dependencies in priors. For clustering, it relates to K-means/ICM [12], k-means++ [16], and VB matrix factorization [4,5], but directly enforces the one-hot assignment constraint within the AMP framework, which appears novel.

Strengths:
- Methodological unification: A single AMP framework handles posterior mean, marginal MAP, and MAP via the β parameter, with a clear formulation of denoisers f and covariance/Jacobian G.
- Efficient and principled corrections: The K-means specialization provides explicit correction terms with an intuitive leave-one-out interpretation; per-iteration cost is linear in data size.
- Theoretical guarantee at fixed points: Proposition 1 shows that, under mild positive semidefiniteness conditions, the converged U (resp. V) is a global minimizer of CMAP with V (resp. U) fixed, and that AMP fixed points are also ICM fixed points, potentially reducing poor local minima.
- Empirical performance: On synthetic data, AMP-KM achieves lower K-means loss than Lloyd’s K-means and k-means++, and AMP-MA attains the highest clustering accuracy among tested methods. On ORL faces, AMP-KM outperforms k-means++ in most trials.
- Scope: The general form accommodates diverse priors (e.g., sparsity, nonnegativity), not only clustering.

Weaknesses:
- Derivation and guarantees deferred: The state evolution, convergence conditions, and detailed derivation are relegated to supplementary material and not substantiated in the main text. AMP’s convergence for bilinear models is delicate; observed assignment oscillations (handled via a 2-cycle stopping rule) indicate stability issues that merit analysis (e.g., damping).
- Practicality for general priors: For β<∞, computing f and G may require r̂-dimensional integrals; for β→∞, solving r̂-dimensional convex programs is needed. The paper does not report runtime or wall-clock comparisons, nor strategies (e.g., separable priors, proximal denoisers) to ensure tractability.
- Limited empirical scope: Synthetic experiments fix m, N, τ, and vary r; sensitivity to r̂≠r, noise variance, and initialization is underexplored. Clustering baselines are limited to K-means family and VBMF; comparisons to Gaussian mixture EM (natural for the assumed Gaussian cluster model) or spectral clustering are absent. Only one real dataset is shown, and two trials collapse to a single cluster.
- Hyperparameters and estimation: The noise variance τ is estimated heuristically during AMP-KM iterations; the impact of τ mis-specification on performance and stability is not analyzed.
- Clarity/typos: Minor inconsistencies (e.g., missing 1/(mτ) factor in Λtu in Eq. (17a) as printed) could hinder reproducibility; precise conditions for Proposition 1 (e.g., when ∑S_i and ∑T_j are PSD) are not detailed in the main text.

Quality:
- The algorithmic development is plausible and aligns with AMP principles; the fixed-point optimality proposition is useful. However, the lack of main-text state evolution and convergence analysis limits the theoretical completeness. Empirical results support claims within the tested regimes but are not exhaustive.

Clarity:
- Overall well-organized and readable, with clear problem setup and algorithm statements. The exposition of the clustering specialization and interpretation of correction terms is particularly clear. Minor notational/typographical issues should be corrected. Including more guidance on computing f/G for common priors would improve reproducibility.

Originality:
- Extending bilinear AMP to a tempered posterior enabling MAP/marginal MAP under general priors is a meaningful advance beyond [9]. The direct enforcement of one-hot assignments within AMP for K-means-type clustering, and the resulting corrected assignment rule, appears novel.

Significance:
- If robustly convergent, the proposed AMP framework could impact low-rank factorization with structured priors and improve widely used K-means clustering. The current empirical validation is promising but limited; broader evaluations and stronger theoretical support would raise the significance.

Suggestions and questions for the authors:
- Provide (even briefly) the state evolution equations and main assumptions; discuss convergence mitigation (damping, step-size control) and when oscillations arise.
- Clarify the exact formulas in Algorithm 3 (correct factors in Λtu) and list conditions guaranteeing PSD of ∑S_i and ∑T_j.
- Report runtime and scaling with m, N, r̂; compare to Lloyd’s and VB methods.
- Evaluate sensitivity to τ estimation and r̂≠r; include additional datasets and baselines (e.g., Gaussian mixture EM).
- Illustrate applications with non-Gaussian/sparse priors to demonstrate generality beyond clustering.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Clear methodological contribution extending bilinear AMP to MAP/marginal MAP with general priors.
- Novel and practically relevant AMP-based K-means variant with principled corrections and improved empirical performance.
- Solid linkage to and differentiation from prior AMP/VB/ICM work; useful fixed-point optimality property.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Insufficient main-text theoretical guarantees (state evolution, convergence); observed oscillations and heuristic stopping.
- Limited empirical breadth (few datasets, limited baselines, no timing); practicality for general priors not demonstrated.
- Minor clarity issues and reliance on supplementary derivations.

Overall, the paper presents a technically interesting AMP extension with a compelling clustering application, but would benefit from stronger convergence analysis and broader empirical validation.
