- Introduces a novel AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and a MAP extension that enforces structural priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity).  
- Reformulates clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem and proposes an AMP-based clustering algorithm that enforces single-cluster membership.  
- Systematic derivation with strong theoretical foundation, discussing computational efficiency and fixed-point properties.  
- Provides state evolution analysis supporting reliability for large-scale problems.  
- Empirical results show the AMP method outperforms Lloyd's K-means and K-means++ on synthetic and real datasets, especially in high dimensions.  
- Numerical experiments are well-designed with clear metrics (normalized K-means loss, accuracy, iteration count) and comprehensive comparisons.  
- Presentation is dense and may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with AMP or Bayesian inference; algorithm derivation should be simplified.  
- Related work discussion is limited and needs broader comparison to recent matrix factorization and clustering advances.  
- Convergence is assumed without a formal proof; a more robust theoretical convergence analysis is missing.  
- Real-world applicability is underexplored (only ORL face dataset); additional applications (e.g., recommender systems, bioinformatics) would strengthen the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical improvements, and likely to inspire further AMP-based research.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dense presentation limiting accessibility and lack of formal convergence guarantees raising robustness concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand related-work discussion.
