- Proposes a Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction AMP algorithm parameterized by β that interpolates between MMSE (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).
- Specializes the framework to clustering by imposing a one-of-K prior on rows of V, yielding an AMP variant of K-means with a principled leave-one-out correction and cluster-size regularization.
- Proves a fixed-point property (Proposition 1) showing converged points are block-wise globally optimal and fixed points of ICM.
- Positions work relative to IterFac (rank-1), GAMP/BiG-AMP (bilinear AMP), VB matrix factorization, classic K-means/Lloyd and NMF-based clustering.
- Distinguishes itself by taking a fixed-r large-system limit and allowing row-wise dependencies in U and V, though using separable priors in the algorithm.
- On synthetic data, AMP-KM reduces K-means loss vs Lloyd/K-means++ for larger r; AMP-MA improves accuracy over VBMF-MA.
- On ORL faces, AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in most trials.
- Strength: careful AMP derivation that clearly exposes Onsager terms and unifies MMSE/MAP via β.
- Strength: K-means specialization yields an interpretable assignment rule with bias correction and size rebalancing, a practically relevant modification of Lloyd’s step.
- Strength: Proposition 1 offers a useful optimality guarantee and clarifies the link to ICM.
- Weakness: No state evolution summary or conditions given for approximation accuracy in bilinear models with fixed r.
- Weakness: Convergence is not guaranteed; oscillations observed and only handled ad hoc (stop when Vt=Vt−2); no damping or stability remedies explored.
- Weakness: τ estimation heuristic in clustering introduced without analysis; sensitivity to τ mismatch is unclear.
- Weakness: Experimental baselines omit strong comparators like EM for isotropic GMMs and spectral/spherical K-means; VBMF-MA may be an unfair baseline if given true priors.
- Weakness: Novelty claim relative to BiG-AMP (which includes MMSE and MAP variants) needs clearer positioning.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written with consistent notation and clear separation of generic AMP and K-means specialization.
- Clarity issue: Assumptions for Proposition 1 (positivity and regularity) should be stated precisely and failure cases discussed.
- Clarity issue: Providing concise pseudo-code for Algorithms 2 and 3 would improve reproducibility.
- Clarity issue: A short state evolution outline would clarify when the algorithm is expected to work.
- Originality: β-tempered posterior and unified AMP for bilinear factorization are interesting but close to BiG-AMP; fixed-r limit and non-i.i.d. rows are differentiators needing more exposition.
- Originality: K-means specialization with hard assignments and Onsager correction appears novel and meaningful.
- Significance: AMP-KM could be impactful by improving K-means while retaining simplicity, especially in high dimensions.
- Significance concern: Overall impact depends on convergence guarantees and broader empirical evidence beyond one real dataset and a single noise model.
- Pros for acceptance: clean AMP derivation, novel K-means AMP specialization with empirical gains, useful fixed-point characterization, and attractive per-iteration complexity.
- Cons against acceptance: lack of theoretical support (no state evolution/convergence guidance), incomplete empirical comparisons, unclear positioning vs BiG-AMP, and untested sensitivity to τ and rank mis-specification.
- Suggested improvements: add state evolution outline and practical convergence guidance (damping/adaptive steps), clarify relation to BiG-AMP, expand clustering baselines and runtime reporting, evaluate robustness to mis-specified priors and rank, enable hyperparameter learning (EM/Bethe F), analyze the assignment correction term, and test more real datasets and empty-cluster prevention.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — promising and interesting but needs stronger theoretical account and more comprehensive empirical validation.
