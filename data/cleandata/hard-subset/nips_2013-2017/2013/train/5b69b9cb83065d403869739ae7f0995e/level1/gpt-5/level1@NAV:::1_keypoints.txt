- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that unifies posterior-mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) via a temperature parameter β.
- Extends prior AMP/bilinear work by enabling MAP estimation, allowing non-separable priors on r-dimensional factor rows, and giving an Onsager-corrected VB/ICM variant.
- Reformulates K-means clustering as constrained low-rank reconstruction with one-hot priors on V and derives an AMP-based clustering algorithm with principled correction terms.
- Empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ on synthetic high-dimensional data and the ORL faces dataset.
- The maximum-accuracy AMP variant exceeds VBMF in accuracy while keeping O(mN) per-iteration complexity.
- Derivation is sound within AMP heuristics; β-extension and MAP limit (with Hessian-based G∞) are well motivated.
- Proposition 1 gives a useful optimality guarantee at fixed points.
- State evolution and convergence conditions are only sketched in the main text and deferred to the supplement.
- Empirical validation is limited in scope (single noise level, specific dimensions) and lacks robustness studies.
- Comparisons omit stronger clustering baselines (EM for GMMs, spectral/normalized cuts, biG-AMP variants).
- Algorithmic presentation is clear with transparent links to VB/ICM.
- Implementation details (computing f and G for non-Gaussian/non-separable priors, numerical stability, ad hoc τ-estimate) need expansion.
- Minor notational choices (e.g., scaling noise by m) should be justified earlier.
- The β-continuation to MAP and explicit one-hot constrained AMP clustering with Onsager corrections appear novel relative to related work.
- If supported by broader experiments and theory, the approach could significantly impact scalable structured matrix factorization and high-dimensional clustering.
- Pros: neat β-bridge between Bayesian averaging and MAP, novel AMP K-means formulation, low per-iteration cost, practical bias/size-regularization insights, partial fixed-point optimality.
- Cons: missing main-paper state-evolution/convergence proofs, limited empirical scope and baselines, sensitivity to initialization and oscillations not fully addressed, heuristic rank selection and τ-estimation.
- Suggestions: add state-evolution and convergence discussion, compare to biG-AMP/EM-GMM/spectral methods, analyze sensitivity to τ/prior/initialization, provide runtimes and ablations, and release code for reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: promising and in scope for NeurIPS but would benefit from stronger empirical and theoretical support.
