- Proposes a novel Bayesian approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction that incorporates prior structural knowledge.  
- Applies the AMP algorithm to clustering by formulating clustering as low-rank matrix reconstruction with an additional structural constraint.  
- Paper is well-written and the algorithm derivation and implementation are thoroughly explained.  
- Provides detailed analysis of algorithmic properties: computational cost, convergence behavior (assumed), and optimality claims.  
- Numerical experiments on synthetic and real data show strong performance, outperforming Lloyd’s K-means and variational Bayes matrix factorization in reported cases.  
- Strength: algorithmic versatility in handling structural priors for low-rank recovery and clustering.  
- Strength: comprehensive analysis of computational cost and claimed optimality.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate effectiveness across multiple datasets.  
- Weakness: convergence of the algorithm is not guaranteed; analysis assumes convergence.  
- Weakness: the role and selection/estimation of parameter τ is unclear and treated with a temporary solution.  
- Weakness: empirical comparisons are limited to a few baselines and datasets; broader benchmarking is needed.  
- Overall assessment: significant and well-presented contribution with promising results, but practical reliability and broader comparisons require further work.  
- Pro-accept: novel, effective method with solid analysis and strong experimental evidence.  
- Con-accept: unresolved convergence guarantees, unclear τ selection, and limited comparative evaluation.
