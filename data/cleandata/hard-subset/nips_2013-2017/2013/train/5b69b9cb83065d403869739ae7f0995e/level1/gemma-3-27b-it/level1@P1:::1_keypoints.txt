- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and extends it to MAP estimation.  
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a specific low-rank reconstruction problem and applies the AMP algorithm to clustering.  
- Key contribution: extending AMP to allow MAP estimation, broadening applicability beyond posterior means.  
- Empirical results show the proposed method outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Experiments include both synthetic data and real ORL faces dataset, supporting empirical claims.  
- Paper is well-motivated: low-rank reconstruction is fundamental and Bayesian priors (e.g., sparsity) are useful.  
- AMP is a natural, efficient approximate inference choice given prior successes in related domains.  
- Good literature context: relates to variational Bayes, IterFac, AMP for dictionary learning and blind calibration.  
- Strength: novel technical extension of AMP to MAP estimation.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance and credible experimental setup.  
- Strength: theoretical connections to belief propagation and Bethe approximation; Proposition 1 discussion on convergence/optimality.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and algorithm presentation with helpful equations.  
- Strength: fresh perspective on clustering via low-rank reconstruction leading to a potentially more efficient algorithm.  
- Weakness: no state-evolution analysis presented to predict or explain algorithm performance.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and investigation of sensitivity to noise-variance parameter τ and its estimation.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparative analysis with more recent AMP methods for matrix factorization/dictionary learning.  
- Weakness: ad-hoc convergence/stopping criterion in clustering experiments; lacks a principled alternative.  
- Weakness: the role and impact of β (interpolating marginalization and MAP) is not empirically explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound algorithm with strong empirical evidence and theoretical grounding addressing an important problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing state-evolution theory, limited parameter-sensitivity study, and arbitrary convergence criteria.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution recommended for acceptance, with suggested improvements to theory, parameter analysis, and comparisons.
