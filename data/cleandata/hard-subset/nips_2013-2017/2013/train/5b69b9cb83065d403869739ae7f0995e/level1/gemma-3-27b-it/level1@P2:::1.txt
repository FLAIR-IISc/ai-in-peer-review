This paper presents an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering. The core idea is to leverage the Bayesian framework with flexible prior distributions and utilize AMP for efficient inference, extending previous work on AMP for matrix reconstruction. The application to clustering is framed as a specific instance of low-rank reconstruction with constraints on the factor matrix V. The paper details the algorithm derivation, theoretical considerations, and numerical experiments demonstrating performance gains over Lloyd’s K-means and variational Bayes methods.

The paper is well-structured, starting with a clear introduction of the problem and motivation, followed by detailed mathematical derivations and experimental results. The authors correctly position their work within the existing literature on low-rank matrix reconstruction, Bayesian methods, and AMP algorithms, citing relevant papers such as [6, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12]. The connections to previous work, particularly highlighting the extension of [9] and the relationship to variational Bayes methods, are valuable. The algorithm derivation appears sound, and the proposed AMP algorithm is a reasonable extension of existing techniques. The application to clustering, formulating it as a constrained low-rank problem, is a clever and potentially impactful approach.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations, while complex, seem logically consistent and build upon established AMP theory. The use of a one-parameter extension for the posterior is a useful generalization.
*   **Originality:** The extension of AMP to handle MAP estimation for low-rank reconstruction (beyond posterior means) and the application to clustering with explicit cluster assignment constraints represent novel contributions.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The notation is mostly clear, although some familiarity with AMP and Bayesian inference is helpful. The algorithm presentation is clear, with equations presented in a readable format.
*   **Significance:** The proposed algorithm demonstrates performance improvements over existing methods (K-means and variational Bayes) in the experiments, suggesting practical relevance. The convergence properties discussed, linking to the ICM algorithm, are insightful. The idea of directly enforcing cluster constraints within the low-rank framework is a potentially powerful approach for clustering problems.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Convergence Guarantee:** While Proposition 1 provides some assurance of optimality, a full convergence analysis of the AMP algorithm is lacking. The discussion around convergence feels somewhat hand-wavy.
*   **Computational Complexity:** The paper states linear computational cost, but the constant factor associated with calculating f and G, particularly for non-trivial priors, is not thoroughly analyzed. This could be a limiting factor for very large-scale problems.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The parameter τ (noise variance) is mentioned as being estimated but details of this estimation procedure are lacking. The sensitivity of the algorithm to this parameter and other hyperparameters (e.g., prior distributions) is not thoroughly investigated.
*   **Limited Real-Data Evaluation:** The real-data experiment, while positive, is somewhat limited in scope. Evaluation on a wider range of datasets, especially those with varying dimensionality and noise levels, would strengthen the conclusions. The comparison to K-means++ relies on the same initial value, which may not be a fair comparison.
*   **State Evolution:** The paper mentions state evolution analysis but does not present it due to space limitations. Inclusion of at least a brief discussion of the state evolution results would provide further insight into the algorithm's performance guarantees.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of AMP algorithms to a challenging problem.
*   Demonstrated performance improvements over established methods.
*   Well-written and technically sound presentation.
*   Potential impact on both low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of rigorous convergence analysis.
*   Limited discussion of computational cost and parameter sensitivity.
*   Insufficient evaluation on diverse real-world datasets.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper with original contributions and promising results. While the lack of a comprehensive convergence analysis and limited discussion of practical considerations are weaknesses, the technical soundness, novelty, and potential impact of the work outweigh these concerns. I believe the paper is suitable for acceptance, with a recommendation that the authors address the concerns regarding convergence, parameter sensitivity, and expand the real-data evaluation in a future version.

**Rating:** Accept (with minor revisions)
