This paper addresses Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with additional structural priors on the factors and derives an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm that unifies posterior-mean inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞) via a temperature parameter. The algorithm extends prior AMP work on low-rank/bilinear models (e.g., AMP/GAMP for compressed sensing and bilinear estimation) by enabling MAP estimation, allowing non-separable priors within r-dimensional factor rows, and providing an Onsager-corrected variant of variational-Bayes/ICM updates. A second contribution reformulates K-means clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem with one-hot priors on V and develops an AMP-based clustering algorithm that adjusts assignments with principled correction terms. Empirically, the method outperforms Lloyd’s K-means (and K-means++) on synthetic high-dimensional data and the ORL faces dataset, and the maximum-accuracy AMP variant exceeds VBMF on accuracy, with O(mN) per-iteration cost.

Quality: The derivation is sound at the level of AMP heuristics; the β-extension and the MAP limit (with Hessian-based G∞) are clearly motivated. Proposition 1 provides a useful optimality guarantee at fixed points. However, the paper only sketches state evolution and convergence conditions, deferring proofs to the supplement; empirical validation is limited in scope (single noise level, specific dimensions) and comparisons omit strong baselines for clustering (e.g., EM for Gaussian mixtures, spectral/normalized cut methods, or biG-AMP variants).

Clarity: The algorithmic presentation is clear, with transparent links to VB/ICM. Some implementation details (computing f and G for non-Gaussian/non-separable priors, numerical stability, and the ad hoc τ-estimate) should be expanded. Minor notational choices (scaling by m in the noise) could be justified earlier.

Originality: The β-continuation to MAP for general-rank bilinear AMP and the explicit one-hot constrained clustering via AMP with Onsager corrections appear novel relative to [9], GAMP, and related bilinear AMP. The connection to K-means with principled bias corrections is particularly interesting.

Significance: If substantiated with broader experiments and theory, the approach could impact scalable matrix factorization with structure and practical clustering in high dimensions.

Pros for acceptance:
- Conceptually neat β-bridge between Bayesian averaging and MAP in bilinear AMP
- Novel AMP formulation for K-means with improved empirical performance
- Low per-iteration complexity and practical insights (bias correction, size regularization)
- Partial optimality guarantee at fixed points

Cons for acceptance:
- Missing state-evolution analysis and convergence guarantees in the main paper
- Limited empirical scope and baselines; no robustness/prior-mismatch studies
- Sensitivity to initialization and occasional oscillations not fully addressed
- Rank selection and τ-estimation handled heuristically

Suggestions: Include state evolution and convergence discussion; compare to biG-AMP and EM-GMM/spectral methods; analyze sensitivity to τ, priors, and initialization; provide runtime and ablations; release code for reproducibility. Overall, in scope for NeurIPS and promising but would benefit from stronger empirical and theoretical support.
