Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured priors on factors U and V, and derives an AMP algorithm (Algorithm 2) parameterized by β that interpolates between sum-product/MMSE (β=1) and max-sum/MAP (β→∞). A second contribution specializes the framework to clustering by imposing a one-of-K prior on rows of V, yielding an AMP variant of K-means (Algorithm 3) with a principled “leave-one-out” correction in the assignment step and a cluster-size regularization effect. A fixed-point property (Proposition 1) shows that any converged point is block-wise globally optimal and is a fixed point of ICM.

Relative to prior work: IterFac [8] handles rank-1 via AMP; [9] treats general rank but reportedly only posterior means. GAMP [7] includes both sum-product and max-sum in linear mixing; bilinear GAMP (BiG-AMP) [14] is contemporaneous and highly related. The paper also connects to VB matrix factorization [4,5] and classic K-means/Lloyd [12] and K-means++ [16]. The clustering-as-factorization viewpoint is aligned with NMF-based clustering [10,11], but here the hard one-of-K constraint is handled directly within an AMP derivation with Onsager corrections. The paper positions itself by fixing rank r in the large-system limit (instead of r/m fixed), and by allowing row-wise dependencies in U and V (though the algorithm still uses separable priors).

On synthetic data, the AMP-K-means (AMP-KM) improves K-means loss vs Lloyd/K-means++ for larger r, and the AMP maximum-accuracy (AMP-MA) improves accuracy over VBMF-MA. On ORL faces, AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in most trials.

Quality
Strengths:
- The AMP derivation is careful and the algorithmic form clearly exposes Onsager terms (the last terms in (9a, 9c)), which explains improved behavior over VB/ICM. The β-parameter unification of MMSE and MAP is elegant.
- The specialization to K-means yields an interpretable assignment rule (18b) with intuitive bias correction and size rebalancing; this is a novel and practically relevant modification to Lloyd’s step.
- Proposition 1 provides a useful optimality guarantee at fixed points, and clarifies the relation to ICM.

Weaknesses and concerns:
- The derivation and state evolution analysis are deferred; no conditions are given under which the approximations are accurate for bilinear models with fixed r. Given known subtleties in bilinear AMP (BiG-AMP) regarding convergence and required damping, the lack of even a brief state evolution summary and convergence discussion is a gap.
- Convergence is not guaranteed; oscillations are observed and handled ad hoc by stopping when Vt=Vt−2. No damping, step-size control, or line search is explored; stability remedies common in AMP are not discussed.
- The τ estimation heuristic in clustering is introduced without analysis; sensitivity to τ estimation and to mismatch is unclear.
- Experimental comparisons for clustering omit strong baselines such as EM for isotropic Gaussian mixtures (soft K-means) and spectral/spherical K-means. VBMF-MA is not a standard clustering baseline, and using the true priors there may advantage AMP-MA in fairness.
- The claim of novelty for MAP in general-rank bilinear AMP needs a crisper positioning relative to BiG-AMP [14], which includes both MMSE and MAP variants for bilinear models.

Clarity
- Generally well written with consistent notation and clear separation between the generic AMP and its K-means specialization. The roles of f, G and their max-sum limits are explained. However:
  - The assumptions needed for Proposition 1 (positivity of ∑Si and ∑Tj and the regularity for the implicit-function argument) should be stated more precisely, and examples where they may fail should be discussed.
  - A short pseudo-code block for Algorithm 2 and 3 would improve reproducibility.
  - A concise outline of the state evolution (even if full proofs are deferred) would greatly strengthen clarity on when the algorithm is expected to work.

Originality
- The β-tempered posterior and unified AMP handling of MMSE/MAP in a bilinear factorization are interesting, but proximate to BiG-AMP [14]. The paper’s specific large-system limit (fixed r) and allowance for non-i.i.d. row components are differentiators, but how these materially affect the algorithm or analysis is not fully fleshed out.
- The K-means specialization with explicit hard assignments and Onsager-based correction appears novel and practically meaningful.

Significance
- If robust, the K-means variant could be impactful: it keeps K-means’ simplicity while adding principled corrections that improve performance, especially in high dimensions.
- The general AMP framework for structured low-rank factorization is valuable, but its significance hinges on convergence guarantees and broader empirical evidence beyond one real dataset and a single noise model.
- Lack of comparisons to key clustering baselines and limited datasets temper claims.

Pros for acceptance
- Conceptually clean AMP derivation that unifies MMSE and MAP with interpretable Onsager terms.
- Novel and effective K-means AMP specialization with clear empirical gains on synthetic and ORL.
- Useful fixed-point characterization vis-à-vis ICM.
- Computationally attractive per-iteration complexity.

Cons against acceptance
- Insufficient theoretical support: no state evolution summary, no convergence guidance, and ad hoc stopping for oscillations.
- Incomplete empirical comparison set for clustering; reliance on true priors in AMP-MA comparisons.
- Positioning vs contemporaneous BiG-AMP (including max-sum) is underdeveloped.
- Sensitivity to τ and to rank mis-specification not evaluated; no hyperparameter learning or model selection (e.g., via Bethe free energy/EM).

Suggestions for improvement
- Add a brief state evolution outline and practical convergence guidance (damping, adaptive step sizes, or mean-removal safeguards) akin to practices in GAMP/BiG-AMP.
- Clarify the relationship to BiG-AMP (when do the algorithms coincide? how does the fixed-r limit change predictions?), and to max-sum bilinear GAMP.
- Expand clustering baselines (EM for GMM with tied isotropic covariance; spectral/spherical K-means), and report runtime as well as iterations.
- Evaluate robustness to mis-specified priors and rank, and explore hyperparameter learning (τ, prior parameters) via EM on the Bethe free energy.
- Analyze the assignment correction term (18b) as an approximate leave-one-out adjustment; quantify its effect vs Lloyd’s step and K-means++ seeding.
- Consider additional real datasets and report variance across initializations; discuss empty-cluster prevention (e.g., with Dirichlet-like priors on cluster weights).

Recommendation: Weak accept. The AMP formulation and the K-means specialization are interesting and show practical promise, but the paper would benefit from a stronger theoretical account and more comprehensive empirical comparisons.
