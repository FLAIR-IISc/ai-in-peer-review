- Presents a novel AMP-based approach for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.  
- Formulates the problem in a Bayesian framework enabling incorporation of structural priors (sparsity, non-negativity).  
- Proposes an efficient AMP algorithm usable for both marginalization and MAP estimation.  
- Derives the algorithm via a one-parameter extension of the posterior to treat marginalization and MAP in a unified way.  
- Provides a proof of optimality of the AMP output in a certain sense.  
- Applies the method to clustering by casting it as a structured low-rank reconstruction problem.  
- Demonstrates empirically that the AMP algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means on K-means loss and accuracy.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and balances theoretical derivations with numerical experiments.  
- Offers a thorough literature review highlighting limitations of existing methods.  
- Strength: novel algorithm and relevant application with strong empirical performance.  
- Weakness: assumes Gaussian noise, which may be unrealistic in some settings.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity.  
- Weakness: does not address robustness to outliers or non-Gaussian noise.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
