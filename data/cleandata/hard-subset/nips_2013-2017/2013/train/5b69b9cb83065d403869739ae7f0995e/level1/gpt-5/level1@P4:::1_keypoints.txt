- Paper proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction from Gaussian-noisy observations, handling flexible (possibly improper) row priors.  
- Main methodological novelty: tempering parameter β in AMP unifies posterior mean, marginal MAP, and MAP estimation within one framework.  
- Algorithm uses vector denoisers f and their Jacobians G, with per-iteration complexity O(mN).  
- Second contribution: casting K-means as constrained low-rank factorization with one-hot rows and deriving an AMP-based K-means (AMP-KM) with Onsager-like corrections.  
- AMP-KM introduces leave-one-out bias corrections and a preference toward smaller clusters; a β=1 maximum-accuracy variant (AMP-MA) is derived.  
- Builds on prior AMP and bilinear AMP work but differs by fixing rank in the large-system limit and allowing intra-row prior dependencies.  
- Strength: unified AMP framework handling multiple inference regimes via β and clear denoiser/Jacobian formulation.  
- Strength: explicit, principled correction terms for clustering with linear per-iteration cost.  
- Strength: Proposition 1 gives fixed-point optimality for U or V when the other is fixed and links AMP fixed points to ICM fixed points.  
- Empirical strength: lower K-means loss than Lloyd’s and k-means++ on synthetic data, and higher clustering accuracy for AMP-MA; improved performance on ORL faces in most trials.  
- Scope strength: framework accommodates diverse priors (e.g., sparsity, nonnegativity), not limited to clustering.  
- Weakness: state evolution, convergence conditions, and detailed derivations are relegated to the supplement and not presented in the main text.  
- Weakness: observed assignment oscillations and lack of convergence analysis; only a heuristic 2-cycle stopping rule is used.  
- Weakness: computing f and G may require high-dimensional integrals or convex solves, with no runtime, wall-clock, or tractability strategies reported.  
- Weakness: limited empirical exploration—fixed m,N,τ, varying r only; sensitivity to r̂≠r, noise, initialization not analyzed; few baselines and only one real dataset.  
- Weakness: τ (noise variance) estimated heuristically during AMP-KM; impact of mis-specification on performance and stability is unexamined.  
- Weakness: minor clarity/typo issues (e.g., missing factor in Eq. (17a)) and incomplete statement of PSD conditions for Proposition 1 in the main text.  
- Suggestions: include state evolution and convergence assumptions, discuss damping/step-size, and analyze oscillation causes and remedies.  
- Suggestions: correct and clarify Algorithm 3 formulas, state PSD conditions explicitly, and provide runtime scaling with comparisons to Lloyd’s and VB methods.  
- Suggestions: evaluate sensitivity to τ and r̂≠r, add more datasets and baselines (e.g., Gaussian mixture EM, spectral clustering), and demonstrate non-Gaussian/sparse prior applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: meaningful extension of bilinear AMP to tempered posterior and MAP/marginal MAP with general priors; novel AMP-KM with principled corrections and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient main-text theoretical guarantees, limited empirical breadth and timing/tractability evidence, and reliance on supplementary derivations.
