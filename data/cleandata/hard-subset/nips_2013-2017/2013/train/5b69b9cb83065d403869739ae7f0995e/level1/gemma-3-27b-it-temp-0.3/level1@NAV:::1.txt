## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous work by enabling MAP estimation and applying it to a clustering problem. The authors formulate clustering as a low-rank reconstruction problem with a specific structural constraint, and demonstrate through numerical experiments that their AMP-based clustering algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, particularly in high-dimensional data.

The paper builds upon a strong foundation of prior work in low-rank matrix reconstruction (e.g., [1, 2, 3, 4]) and AMP algorithms for compressed sensing [6] and matrix factorization [8, 9]. The motivation for using AMP is well-articulated – its efficiency and ability to approximate Bayesian inference with various priors. The extension to MAP estimation is a valuable contribution, and the application to clustering, while not entirely novel [10, 11], is presented with a unique constraint handling approach. The connection to K-means [12] provides a clear benchmark for comparison.

**Strengths:** The paper is technically sound, presenting a clear derivation of the AMP algorithm and a well-defined problem formulation. The numerical experiments are comprehensive, comparing the proposed method against established algorithms (K-means, K-means++, VBMF) on both synthetic and real datasets. The results consistently demonstrate the superior performance of the proposed AMP-based clustering, especially in terms of accuracy. The inclusion of theoretical justification through Proposition 1 is a strong point, suggesting a potential for global optimality under certain conditions.

**Weaknesses:** While the paper provides a good overview of related work, the discussion of recent advancements in AMP for dictionary learning and blind calibration [13, 14] feels somewhat cursory. A more detailed comparison of the proposed algorithm with these methods, particularly regarding the differing scaling limits, would strengthen the contribution. The explanation of the parameter τ, introduced for scaling, could be more intuitive. The notation is dense and could benefit from more explanation, especially for readers unfamiliar with AMP. The convergence guarantee is stated as an assumption, and a discussion of conditions under which convergence might fail would be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of AMP to MAP estimation for low-rank matrix reconstruction.
*   Effective application of the algorithm to clustering, outperforming K-means in experiments.
*   Strong theoretical grounding and justification.
*   Clear presentation and comprehensive experimental evaluation.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited detailed comparison with very recent related work in AMP.
*   Dense notation and potential lack of clarity for some readers.
*   Convergence guarantee is stated as an assumption.



**Overall:** This is a solid contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The proposed algorithm is well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically validated. While some areas could benefit from further clarification and comparison, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
