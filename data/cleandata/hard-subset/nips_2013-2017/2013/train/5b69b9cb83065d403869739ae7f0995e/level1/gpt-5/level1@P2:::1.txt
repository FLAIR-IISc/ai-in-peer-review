Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with additional structure on the factors (e.g., sparsity, nonnegativity, and in particular one-hot cluster assignments). It derives an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for general rank r that treats both marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞) through a one-parameter family of posterior distributions p̂(U,V|A;β). The algorithm yields posterior means (or MAP points) via separable denoising functions f and their Jacobians G, and includes Onsager-type correction terms. A proposition links fixed points of the AMP-MAP algorithm to blockwise global optima of the MAP objective.

A notable contribution is the specialization to clustering by choosing a one-hot discrete prior on V, yielding an AMP variant of K-means with explicit corrections to the assignment step. Empirically, on synthetic data and the ORL faces dataset, the proposed AMP variants outperform Lloyd’s K-means (and K-means++) in loss and, for the Bayesian variant, in accuracy.

This sits within a line of work on AMP for linear inverse problems [Donoho et al. 2009; Rangan 2011], bilinear AMP for rank-1 [Rangan & Fletcher 2012], and more general bilinear formulations [Matsushita & Tanaka 2012; Krzakala et al. 2013; Parker et al. 2013]. The paper extends beyond [9] by treating both marginalization and MAP, connects to variational Bayes/ICM (showing that removing Onsager terms recovers those updates), and applies AMP to hard-assignment clustering within a low-rank factorization formulation.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity: unifies marginal and MAP inference via a β-temperature posterior, making the algorithmic transition explicit and yielding implementable updates for broad priors.
- Algorithmic novelty: general-rank bilinear AMP with MAP capability, and a clean specialization to K-means with principled Onsager corrections that can be interpreted as bias and size corrections in assignments.
- Theoretical insight: Proposition 1 characterizes fixed-point optimality against blockwise minimizations and neatly relates AMP fixed points to ICM fixed points; intuitive reformulation (16) explains the role of Onsager terms.
- Empirical improvements: on synthetic clustering tasks, the AMP-KM attains lower K-means loss than Lloyd’s and K-means++; AMP-MA attains higher accuracy than VBMF-MA; on ORL, AMP-KM improves loss and accuracy in most trials.
- Practical efficiency: O(mN) per iteration, with per-row/column separable denoisers; fast convergence in experiments.

Weaknesses
- Limited empirical scope: experiments focus almost exclusively on clustering; the general low-rank reconstruction claim (with flexible priors) is not validated on tasks like sparse/nonnegative factorization, matrix denoising, or completion.
- Comparisons: lacks head-to-head comparisons with strong clustering baselines such as GMM/EM, spherical K-means, spectral clustering, or recent bilinear AMP algorithms (BiG-AMP) that are contemporaneous and very relevant.
- Theory incomplete: state evolution is claimed but omitted; convergence is assumed and oscillations observed (handled by ad hoc stopping). No damping or convergence diagnostics are discussed.
- Sensitivity and robustness: success depends on priors p̂u, p̂v and on τ; robustness to prior mismatch, rank misspecification, and noise model violations is not systematically evaluated. In ORL, two trials collapsed to a single cluster.
- Practical denoiser tractability: for general priors, f and G may require r̂-dimensional integration/optimization; the paper leaves many implementation details and choices (e.g., handling nonconvex priors) to the reader.

Quality
Technically sound at a high level: the AMP derivation is plausible and consistent with prior AMP work; the use of β-tempering to link marginalization and MAP is well-motivated; Proposition 1 is useful (proof in supplement). However, the absence of state evolution, convergence analysis, and more diverse experiments limits the strength of claims. Empirical methodology is mostly fair, though using true priors benefits AMP-MA; at least VBMF-MA is given the same advantage. The clustering specialization is carefully derived and interpretable.

Clarity
The paper is generally well-organized and clear. The algorithm is precisely specified, notation is consistent, and the connections to VB/ICM are explicit. Some readers will need the supplement for derivations and proofs. It would help to provide more intuition for the Onsager corrections in the clustering updates and guidance for choosing/implementing f and G for common priors. The role and estimation of τ in clustering is explained but could be emphasized earlier.

Originality
Moderately strong. Extending general-rank bilinear AMP to support MAP estimation via β and connecting fixed points to ICM is a meaningful contribution. The clustering specialization that enforces exactly-one assignment within an AMP framework is novel and yields a principled K-means variant. Given contemporaneous work on bilinear/generalized AMP, the paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison and discussion to sharpen its novelty.

Significance
Promising but not yet definitive. If broadly applicable, the framework could impact a variety of structured matrix factorization problems with efficient AMP updates. The clustering results show practical improvements over a very widely used baseline. However, without broader benchmarks, theory (state evolution), and comparisons to closely related AMP approaches and stronger clustering baselines, the demonstrated impact is moderate.

Suggestions and questions
- Provide at least a brief empirical state evolution validation (e.g., MSE trajectories) and discuss damping to mitigate oscillations.
- Compare to BiG-AMP on overlapping tasks, and to GMM/EM, spherical K-means, and spectral clustering on the same datasets.
- Evaluate robustness to prior mismatch and τ estimation; study sensitivity to rank misspecification.
- Demonstrate another structured prior (e.g., sparsity or nonnegativity) to validate generality.
- Clarify computational costs for common priors and any numerical tricks for f/G (e.g., proximal forms for MAP).

Arguments for acceptance
- Clear, principled AMP derivation with a useful β-unification of marginal and MAP.
- Novel and interpretable AMP-based K-means variant with consistent empirical gains.
- Useful theoretical link to ICM and insight into fixed points/penalization.
- Efficient and potentially broadly applicable framework.

Arguments against acceptance
- Missing state evolution and convergence analysis; observed oscillations.
- Narrow empirical validation centered on clustering; limited datasets and baselines.
- No comparison to closely related bilinear AMP methods or stronger clustering algorithms.
- Practical implementation details for general priors remain under-specified.

Overall recommendation
Lean accept. The paper advances bilinear AMP in a practically meaningful way, and the clustering application is novel and promising. Strengthening theory and comparisons would make it a clear accept; as is, it is a solid contribution likely to interest the NIPS community.
