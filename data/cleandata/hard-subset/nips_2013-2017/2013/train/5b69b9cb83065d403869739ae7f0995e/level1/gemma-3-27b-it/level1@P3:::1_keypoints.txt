- Novelty: Extends AMP to handle general-rank matrices and explicitly enforces exclusive cluster assignments within a Bayesian low-rank framework — a novel contribution.  
- Theoretical motivation: AMP is well-motivated via belief propagation and relates to prior work; authors mention potential state-evolution analysis but do not present it.  
- Performance: Numerical experiments show competitive or superior performance to Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Positioning in literature: Paper cites and situates itself well within low-rank matrix completion, sparse recovery, and AMP literature.  
- Clarity (sections): Problem setting and algorithm derivations are reasonably clear after the initial setup.  
- Algorithm detail: Algorithms 2 and 3 are described in sufficient detail to enable implementation.  
- Lack of theoretical guarantees: No presented convergence or performance guarantees (e.g., state evolution results), leaving uncertainty about when the algorithm will succeed.  
- Computational complexity: Claim of low cost is not fully supported — iterative updates and numerical integrations/minimizations in `f` and `G` need a detailed complexity analysis.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Insufficient discussion of sensitivity to parameters (noise variance τ, priors p̂u/p̂v) and how they are chosen in practice.  
- Limited comparisons: Baselines are limited (mainly K-means/variational Bayes); lacking comparisons to recent alternating-minimization or state-of-the-art matrix factorization methods.  
- Experimental details missing: Key experimental settings are omitted (initialization strategy, specific parameter choices) making reproducibility difficult.  
- Organization and notation: Overall paper feels dense with heavy notation and could be reorganized for clarity and smoother transitions.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel approach, promising numerical results, and solid algorithmic detail support acceptance potential.  
- Rejection arguments: Major concerns are lack of theoretical guarantees, insufficient complexity/parameter analysis, limited empirical comparisons, and clarity issues.  
- Overall assessment: Reviewer rates the paper as weak accept — promising work that needs stronger theoretical analysis and more thorough experimental evaluation.
