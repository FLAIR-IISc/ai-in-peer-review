- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that can encode structural priors (sparsity, non-negativity, etc.).  
- Extends AMP to handle both marginalization and MAP estimation for matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means by reformulating clustering as low-rank reconstruction.  
- Advances prior AMP work by enabling MAP/other inference (beyond posterior mean) and by fixing rank r rather than the r/m ratio in the asymptotic limit.  
- Strength: elegant, theoretically grounded extension of AMP allowing diverse structural priors; derivation is technically sound.  
- Strength: insightful connection drawn between the AMP algorithm and variational Bayes (Section 4.2).  
- Strength: thorough experiments show AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in accuracy and convergence speed on synthetic and ORL Faces data.  
- Strength: clear explanation of how the proposed clustering method handles cluster-assignment constraints differently from standard approaches.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification for why AMP should outperform methods like Lloyd’s K-means; omitted state evolution analysis is a major gap.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison with recent low-rank reconstruction methods ([13,14]) in the clustering context.  
- Weakness: assumption that rank r is known limits practical applicability where true rank is often unknown.  
- Concern: claim of highest accuracy is potentially misleading because comparisons include slower VBMF-MA and do not explain superiority over ICM-based methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, valuable contribution with strong empirical validation and clear presentation; recommended for acceptance.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel AMP extension, rigorous experiments, demonstrated practical clustering advantages, well-written.  
- Contra-acceptance arguments: insufficient theoretical explanation for empirical gains, incomplete method comparisons, reliance on known rank.
