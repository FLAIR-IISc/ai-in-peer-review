- Paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured priors on factor rows and derives an AMP algorithm handling general vector-valued priors.  
- Introduces a temperature parameter β to interpolate between Bayesian posterior mean (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞).  
- Algorithm supports non-independent components within rows and has O(mN) per-iteration cost.  
- Specialization to one-hot priors yields AMP-KM, an AMP-based K-means with Onsager (leave-one-out) corrections to assignments.  
- β=1 with discrete prior yields an AMP “maximum accuracy” clustering approximating marginal MAP.  
- Builds on prior AMP work (compressed sensing, GAMP, IterFac) but extends to bilinear factorization with MAP and discrete priors.  
- Proposition 1 shows fixed points (under a PSD condition) are coordinate-wise global minima of CMAP and ICM fixed points, supporting AMP-KM’s avoidance of poor minima.  
- AMP derivation follows standard approximations, but state evolution is not presented and convergence is assumed rather than proved.  
- The β-tempered formulation is elegant and unifies posterior mean and MAP limits.  
- Specialization to one-hot priors is clean and produces an intuitive leave-one-out correction in the assignment step.  
- Empirically, AMP-KM attains lower K-means loss than Lloyd’s and K-means++; AMP-MA achieves highest clustering accuracy and faster convergence than VBMF on synthetic data.  
- On ORL faces, AMP-KM improves loss and accuracy in most runs but occasionally collapses to a single cluster.  
- Experiments are informative but limited: one real dataset, few baselines, no runtime plots, and limited scope.  
- Paper is well organized and technically clear, but defers AMP derivation and state evolution entirely to the supplement.  
- Practical algorithmic details (damping, stopping, oscillation handling, empty clusters) are under-discussed despite noted oscillations.  
- τ estimation during AMP-KM is heuristic; guidance on stability and sensitivity is lacking.  
- Novelty: unified AMP for bilinear factorization with general priors and explicit one-hot treatment producing K-means with Onsager corrections appears original.  
- Significance: method is flexible and computationally attractive, but limited empirical validation and missing theoretical guarantees reduce impact.  
- Pros: principled AMP derivation, novel AMP-KM with leave-one-out correction, empirical gains vs standard K-means, low complexity and support for arbitrary priors.  
- Cons: no state evolution or convergence analysis, limited comparisons (no EM for GMMs, spectral clustering, NMF variants), occasional mode collapse, sensitivity and robustness not thoroughly studied.  
- Suggested improvements: include condensed Onsager derivation/state evolution, discuss damping/annealing, compare to EM/spectral/NMF baselines with runtimes, analyze τ estimation and robustness to rank/prior misspecification, ablate Onsager corrections, and handle missing data.  
- Overall: solid and promising contribution with clear novelty; would benefit from stronger theoretical characterization and broader empirical evaluation before being a top-tier conference paper.
