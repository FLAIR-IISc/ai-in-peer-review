- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that enables MAP estimation and applies it to clustering.  
- Leverages AMP’s efficiency and flexibility to incorporate priors on matrix factors.  
- Positions the work within existing literature on low-rank matrix completion, Bayesian matrix factorization, AMP advances, dictionary learning, and generalized observation models.  
- Theoretical grounding is solid and includes a proposition on optimality of the algorithm’s fixed points.  
- Extending AMP to MAP estimation is a novel and valuable contribution.  
- Framing clustering as constrained low-rank reconstruction is sensible and potentially impactful.  
- Numerical results show performance gains over Lloyd’s K-means, especially in high-dimensional settings, with comparisons to related algorithms.  
- Presentation is dense and could use more intuitive explanations.  
- Experimental section lacks detail on parameter tuning and specific datasets beyond ORL.  
- Claim of faster convergence versus variational Bayes is insufficiently justified.  
- Discussion of the state evolution analysis is mentioned but incomplete.  
- Justification for parameter τ appears ad-hoc and its effect on performance needs further study.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty (AMP→MAP), effective clustering results, strong theory, good literature connections.  
- Rejection arguments: opaque presentation, limited experimental detail, incomplete state evolution discussion, narrow empirical comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept, contingent on minor revisions to improve clarity and experimental detail.
