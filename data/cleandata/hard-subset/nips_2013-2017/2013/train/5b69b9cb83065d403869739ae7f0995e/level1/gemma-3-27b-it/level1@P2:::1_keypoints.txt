- Presents an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction applied to clustering using a Bayesian framework with flexible priors, extending prior AMP work.
- Frames clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem with constraints on the factor matrix V.
- Provides algorithm derivation, theoretical considerations, and numerical experiments showing gains over Lloyd’s K-means and variational Bayes.

Strengths:
- Technical soundness: derivations appear logically consistent and build on established AMP theory; one-parameter posterior extension is useful.
- Originality: novel extension of AMP to MAP estimation for low-rank reconstruction and explicit cluster-assignment constraints for clustering.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized with mostly clear notation; algorithm equations are readable.
- Significance: empirical performance improvements over K-means and variational Bayes; linking convergence properties to ICM is insightful.
- Practical idea: directly enforcing cluster constraints within the low-rank framework is potentially impactful.

Weaknesses:
- Convergence guarantee: full convergence analysis of the AMP algorithm is lacking and the discussion is somewhat hand-wavy despite Proposition 1.
- Computational complexity: claimed linear cost ignores potentially large constant factors for computing f and G with non-trivial priors.
- Parameter sensitivity: estimation procedure and sensitivity analysis for τ (noise variance) and other hyperparameters are insufficiently detailed.
- Limited real-data evaluation: real-data experiments are narrow; comparisons (e.g., K-means++) may be unfair due to shared initialization.
- State evolution: state evolution is mentioned but not presented; absence of even brief results weakens theoretical support.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel and technically solid extension of AMP to a challenging problem.
- Demonstrated empirical improvements over established methods.
- Clear presentation and solid positioning within related work.
- Potential to impact both low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering research.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Insufficient rigorous convergence analysis.
- Incomplete discussion of computational cost and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Evaluation on real-world datasets is too limited.

Overall assessment:
- Solid, original paper with promising results; main weaknesses are addressable and do not outweigh the contributions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address convergence analysis, parameter sensitivity, and expanded real-data evaluation.

Rating: Accept (with minor revisions).
