This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization and specializes it to clustering. The main idea is to use the AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, which is derived from the belief propagation algorithm. The algorithm is then applied to a clustering problem by reformulating it as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem with an additional structural property. The authors also provide numerical experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

The paper is well-structured, and the main point is easy to grasp. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem setting, the proposed algorithm, and the numerical experiments. However, there are some concerns regarding the quality, originality, and significance of the paper.

One major concern is the quality of the mathematical derivations. The derivations are very sketchy and omit many non-trivial steps, even in the appendix. For example, in Appendix line 139, it is unclear why the second term of (11) is O(1/√m), and β appears to be omitted in Section 2. The asymptotic analysis, including the derivation of (19), seems to assume that m and n grow at the same order, but the arguments are terse and lack rigor. Additionally, in line 167, the claim that m τ_w^2 equals E[A_{il}^2] is disputed, as A is observed data and m τ_w^2 would only be the likelihood variance. The approximation needs justification.

Another concern is the originality of the algorithm. The reviewer suggests that the algorithm may essentially be Expectation-Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization, pointing to Stern et al. (WWW 2009) and standard EP updates in the appendix. This raises questions about the novelty of the proposed algorithm.

Furthermore, the significance of the paper is limited by the focus on comparison with k-means++. Given the clustering literature, it is insufficient to only compare with one algorithm. Additionally, the sketchy proofs and possible overlap with prior EP-based work reduce confidence in the practical and technical contribution of the paper.

Regarding the originality of the clustering algorithm, the reviewer suggests a potential connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the EP "clutter" problem (Minka). However, this is framed as a suggestion rather than an attack.

To address the concerns, the authors should provide more rigorous mathematical derivations, clarify the relationship between their algorithm and EP, and broaden the comparison with other clustering algorithms.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel application of the AMP algorithm to clustering.
* The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
* The paper is well-structured and easy to follow.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The mathematical derivations are sketchy and lack rigor.
* The originality of the algorithm is questionable due to potential overlap with EP.
* The significance of the paper is limited by the focus on comparison with k-means++.

Recommendation: The paper requires significant revisions to address the concerns regarding quality, originality, and significance. The authors should provide more rigorous mathematical derivations, clarify the relationship between their algorithm and EP, and broaden the comparison with other clustering algorithms.
