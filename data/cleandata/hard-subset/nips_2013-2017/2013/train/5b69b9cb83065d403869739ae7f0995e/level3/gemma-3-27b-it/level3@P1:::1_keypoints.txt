- Introduces an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and extends it to MAP estimation for K-means clustering.
- Leverages AMP to address computational challenges, offering efficiency and scalability for large matrices with structured factors.
- Builds on existing AMP literature (e.g., Donoho et al. 2009; Rangan & Fletcher 2012).
- Connects low-rank reconstruction to K-means clustering, emphasizing exclusive cluster assignment within the reconstruction framework.
- Primary strength: novel algorithmic contribution with thorough empirical validation.
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance vs variational Bayes, Lloyd’s K-means, and K-means++ on synthetic and ORL face datasets.
- Shows improvements in both reconstruction error and clustering accuracy, with comparatively fast convergence.
- Provides clear algorithmic descriptions (Algorithms 2 & 3) enabling straightforward implementation and reproducibility.
- Offers analysis linking the proposed algorithm to variational Bayes and discusses potential convergence properties.
- Major weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical convergence guarantees for the AMP algorithm.
- Discussion of K-means theory is limited and could better connect to provable approximation guarantees from theoretical computer science.
- Handling of the noise parameter τ appears ad-hoc and needs stronger justification or theoretical underpinning.
- Assumption of independent components within rows of U0 and V0 may limit applicability to some domains.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, clarity, and significant practical potential.
- Arguments against acceptance: theoretical weakness, limited theoretical context on K-means, and parameter sensitivity concerns.
- Overall: promising and practically valuable contribution deserving consideration, but should address theoretical limitations in future work.
