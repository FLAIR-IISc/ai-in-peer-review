- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction derived from BP in the large-dimension limit.  
- Unified framework uses inverse temperature β to interpolate between posterior marginals (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞).  
- Demonstrates application by reformulating K-means clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem with discrete constraints.  
- Empirically, AMP-KM yields lower K-means loss than Lloyd’s and K-means++; AMP-MA outperforms VBMF in clustering accuracy and converges faster.  
- Theoretical insight: Proposition 1 shows any MAP fixed point of AMP is also a fixed point of ICM, suggesting reduced susceptibility to poor local minima.  
- Major clarity issues: dense, hard-to-follow mathematical exposition that impedes understanding and reproducibility.  
- Notation is cluttered (excessive hats/tildes/subscripts); simplifying indices (e.g., time t) would improve readability.  
- Key algorithmic equations (especially 9a–d) lack sufficient intuition and explanation of Onsager correction terms and functions f and G.  
- Exposition misses explicit connections (e.g., Eq. 5 as negative log-posterior of Eq. 2, Eq. 8 as posterior^β) that would aid comprehension.  
- Full derivation relegated to supplementary material; a high-level derivation summary in the main paper is needed.  
- Evaluation is narrow: method is presented as general but evaluated only on clustering, limiting assessment on standard matrix-reconstruction tasks.  
- No empirical or theoretical analysis of how asymptotic approximations perform when m, N are finite or deviations from assumptions occur.  
- Novelty concern: method is largely a straightforward application of existing AMP ideas to matrix factorization, limiting perceived originality.  
- Related work is under-discussed: missing citations and discussion on matrix factorization–clustering links and Expectation Propagation (EP); BP-origin should be emphasized earlier.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical clustering results, unified MAP/marginal inference framework, and useful theoretical connection to ICM.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity and notation, limited and non-standard evaluation, and incomplete literature positioning.  
- Recommendation: not ready for publication without major revisions to improve clarity, broaden experimental benchmarks, and strengthen related-work discussion.
