This paper presents a novel algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction from noisy observations, focusing on matrices with additional factor assumptions. The authors leverage the Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the traditional Bayesian method, which is computationally expensive. The proposed algorithm is applied to K-means clustering, reformulating it as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem with an additional structural property.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow. The authors provide a solid background on low-rank matrix reconstruction and the Bayesian approach, as well as a detailed explanation of the AMP algorithm and its properties. The connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering is well-established, and the authors demonstrate how their proposed algorithm can be applied to this problem.

The experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing the proposed method to k-means and k-means++. The results show that the proposed approach is faster and more efficient than the competing methods, with improved clustering quality. The authors also evaluate the performance using both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering quality, which is commendable.

One of the main strengths of the paper is the novelty of the AMP-based contribution. The authors extend the AMP algorithm to the general-rank case, allowing for other estimations such as the maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimation. The proposed algorithm is also efficient, with a low computational cost and a form similar to that of an algorithm based on the variational Bayesian matrix factorization.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. The main weakness is the lack of strong theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm. The authors acknowledge this limitation and suggest that citing more theoretical computer science work that provides provable guarantees for K-means would be beneficial.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques. The application of the AMP algorithm to low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering is new and interesting. The authors also provide a detailed comparison of their work with existing methods, highlighting the differences and similarities.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to advance the state of the art in low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering. The proposed algorithm is faster and more efficient than existing methods, and its application to real-world problems could lead to improved performance and insights.

Overall, this is a solid paper with a promising contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering. While it has some limitations, the authors provide a thorough evaluation and discussion of their work, making it a valuable addition to the literature.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel application of the AMP algorithm to low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering
* Efficient algorithm with low computational cost
* Thorough experimental evaluation and comparison with existing methods
* Potential to advance the state of the art in low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Lack of strong theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm
* Limited discussion of the relationship between the proposed algorithm and existing methods in the field of theoretical computer science

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* Provide more theoretical analysis and convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm
* Discuss the relationship between the proposed algorithm and existing methods in the field of theoretical computer science
* Consider applying the proposed algorithm to more real-world problems and datasets to demonstrate its potential and limitations.
