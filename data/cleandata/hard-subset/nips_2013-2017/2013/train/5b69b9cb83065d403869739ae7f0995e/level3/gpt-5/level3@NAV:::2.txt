This paper proposes an AMP/BP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering by framing K-means as a constrained factorization task. The core idea is to approximate loopy belief propagation under an asymptotic regime (m,N → ∞ with fixed r), yielding an algorithm that generalizes prior AMP for low-rank models to handle both posterior means (β=1) and MAP limits (β→∞). The authors further derive a K-means-specific instantiation and report empirical advantages over Lloyd’s K-means and a VBMF baseline.

Strengths
- Potentially useful contribution: a practical, efficient AMP variant that empirically improves K-means assignments and loss, with plausible mechanisms (Onsager-like corrections) for avoiding poor local minima.
- Mathematical framework is mostly followable; the β-homotopy unifies marginalization and MAP.
- Experiments on synthetic and ORL show consistent gains over Lloyd/K-means++ and suggest speed advantages over VBMF in comparable accuracy regimes.
- Clear relation to variational methods; the side-by-side interpretation of Algorithm 2 versus VBMF/ICM is helpful.

Weaknesses and suggestions
Quality
- Validity of asymptotic approximations (large m,N, fixed r; noise scaling by m) is neither tested nor analyzed in the main text. Please add finite-size diagnostics/state-evolution checks, ablations with/without Onsager terms, and sensitivity to τ estimation.
- Evaluation centers on a non-standard “clustering-as-factorization” proxy; competitiveness on standard matrix reconstruction/CF benchmarks (e.g., Netflix/ML-100K/1M, MovieLens with missing data) remains unclear. Either include such results or justify their omission.
- MAP derivations and key steps are relegated to the supplement; summarize essential derivations and assumptions in the main paper.

Clarity
- Notation is heavy (hats/tildes/subscripts/superscripts, unnecessary time indices); state conventions, make β a clearly global parameter, and simplify where possible.
- Several equations are hard to parse (e.g., 9a–9e: unexplained factors and asymmetries; clarify the role of m in noise variance and effects on row/column symmetry). Define fβ and Gβ immediately at first use with intuition.
- Exposition misses helpful equivalences (e.g., eq.5 is −log of eq.2; eq.8 is eq.2^β; near-symmetry of Algorithm 2). Add a diagram akin to Supplement Fig. 1 to the main text.

Originality and scholarship
- Novelty appears incremental: a fairly direct BP/AMP treatment where prior work used VMP/VBMF. Please discuss and cite EP and continuous BP/EP connections (e.g., Minka 2001) and related AMP/VAMP literature; state the BP nature earlier and more explicitly.
- Clarify and cite the exact VBMF-MA implementation/reference followed.
- Acknowledge prior clustering-via-factorization work; avoid implying novelty where the connection is well-known.

Significance
- Promising, but broader impact depends on validation on standard reconstruction tasks and clearer justification of the asymptotics.

Pros for acceptance
- Practical algorithm with empirical benefits and clear potential impact.
- Unifies MAP and posterior-mean cases; computationally attractive.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited novelty vs. existing BP/AMP/VMP lines.
- Insufficient validation of approximations and non-standard evaluation.
- Clarity and scholarship need substantial revision.

Recommendation: borderline reject; publishable after significant clarity, citation, and evaluation improvements.
