- Proposes a novel AMP algorithm, derived from belief propagation, for low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured priors (sparsity, non-negativity).
- Claims AMP is computationally efficient and handles a wide variety of prior distributions.
- Connects low-rank matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering, casting clustering as a special case of matrix reconstruction.
- Introduces an AMP-based clustering algorithm that, in experiments, outperforms Lloyd’s K-means.
- Provides solid experimental evaluation on synthetic and real datasets, comparing to k-means and k-means++.
- Evaluates both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering quality in experiments.
- Main weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical convergence guarantees for the AMP algorithm.
- Authors offer plausible properties of AMP but no formal proof of convergence is provided.
- Related work coverage is reasonable but misses more theoretical CS work on provable K-means guarantees (e.g., k-means++).
- Suggestion: improve paper by adding citations to provable K-means literature and by providing a rigorous convergence analysis.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-written contribution with promising empirical results but limited by theoretical gaps.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
