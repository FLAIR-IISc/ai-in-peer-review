- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering by framing clustering as low-rank reconstruction with structural priors.  
- Experiments show improved clustering accuracy and faster convergence than k-means++ on synthetic and real data.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written; algorithmic presentation and matrix–clustering connection are easy to follow.  
- Major mathematical shortcomings: derivations are sketchy with many omitted non-trivial steps, even in the appendix.  
- Q1: Unjustified claim that the second term in Eq.(11) is O(1/√m); β appears omitted, which is critical for asymptotic analysis.  
- Q2: Asymptotic analysis assumes m and n grow at the same order but lacks rigorous justification; derivation of Eq.(19) is particularly unsubstantiated.  
- Q3: Claim mτ_w^2 = E[A_{il}^2] is questionable because A is observed data while mτ_w^2 would represent likelihood variance; approximation needs justification.  
- Q4: No justification for exchanging the limit and minimization of KL divergence to claim finite-β message passing converges to the MAP solution.  
- Q5: Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and unclear, specifically the derivation of Eq.(17).  
- Originality concerns: algorithm appears essentially equivalent to Expectation-Propagation (EP) for matrix factorization; prior EP work (e.g., Stern et al. 2009, Minka) is not adequately acknowledged or differentiated.  
- Significance concerns: evaluation compares only to k-means++, omitting important baselines (spectral, hierarchical, other matrix factorization methods), limiting practical significance.  
- Overall assessment: promising empirical results but weakened by theoretical gaps and possible lack of novelty.  
- Recommendation: Reject unless substantially revised—needs rigorous theoretical foundations, clearer differentiation from EP literature, and broader experimental comparisons.
