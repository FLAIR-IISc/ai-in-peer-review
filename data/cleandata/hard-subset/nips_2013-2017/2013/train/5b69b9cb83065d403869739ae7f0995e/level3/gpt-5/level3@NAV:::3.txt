This paper proposes an AMP-style algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization, introduces a β-tempered posterior to interpolate between marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), and specializes the MAP limit to obtain a clustering method that outperforms k-means++ on synthetic data and ORL faces. The formulation relates to prior AMP work for bilinear models (e.g., IterFac, BiG-AMP) and to variational Bayes/ICM for matrix factorization; the specialization to clustering connects to the K-means objective and suggests bias-correction and size-regularization effects in the assignment step.

Quality: The empirical section is promising, but the theoretical development is too sketchy for NIPS standards. Key derivation steps are omitted even in the appendix, and the asymptotic regime and scaling assumptions are not made precise. Specific technical questions:
- Q1 (Appx line 139): Please justify why the second term in (11) is O(1/√m) and ensure β is consistently carried from (8) into q̂(·) and the Onsager terms in (9a),(9c). The current text seems to omit β in Sec. 2, yet 1/β appears later.
- Q2: The asymptotics (including (19)) appear to assume m,n→∞ with m/n→c and fixed r. Please state assumptions (randomness, independence, moments) and provide a state-evolution-style argument or a precise limit theorem; the current argument is terse and not rigorous.
- Q3 (line 167): The identity m τw^2 = E[Ail^2] is not correct in general since A contains signal plus noise. If this is a plug-in approximation, please justify it and/or use an unbiased estimator for τw from residuals with a degrees-of-freedom correction.
- Q4: Clarify how finite-β message passing converges to MAP as β→∞. Exchanging limit and minimization of KL is delicate; a Γ-convergence or epi-convergence argument, or conditions under which f→f∞ and G→G∞ uniformly, would help.
- Q5: The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief; please derive (17) explicitly (completing the square and showing how Onsager terms yield the proximal-like correction) and state conditions for positive semidefiniteness.

Clarity: The paper is well organized and easy to follow at a high level; adding the missing derivations would substantially improve reproducibility.

Originality: There may be substantial overlap with Expectation Propagation applied to matrix factorization (e.g., Stern et al., WWW’09; see also standard EP site updates), with AMP differing by the Onsager correction. Please clarify the relationship and novelty. For clustering, consider discussing links to EP treatments of the “clutter” problem (Minka) and how your assignment update compares.

Significance: Limiting comparisons to k-means++ is insufficient given the literature. Please include spectral clustering, GMM-EM, DP-means, k-medoids, and modern AMP/EP baselines (e.g., BiG-AMP) and report runtime and sensitivity to β, τ-estimation, and initialization.

Pros:
- Clear problem setup; practical algorithm; strong empirical gains over k-means++.
- Insightful bias and cluster-size corrections in the assignment rule.

Cons:
- Insufficient theoretical rigor and missing derivation steps.
- Questionable variance approximations; unclear β→∞ limit justification.
- Limited empirical baselines; originality relative to EP not clearly established.

Overall: Promising but currently below the bar; I would be positive if the theory and positioning vs EP/AMP are strengthened and the evaluation broadened.
