- Proposes an AMP algorithm for matrix factorization and applies it to clustering by reformulating clustering as low-rank matrix reconstruction.  
- Well-motivated algorithm with a clear Bayesian interpretation.  
- Experimental results show better clustering accuracy and lower K-means loss than k-means++.  
- Paper is well-structured and easy to follow.  
- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, even in the appendix.  
- Asymptotic analysis is terse, assumes m and n grow at the same rate, and lacks rigor.  
- No clear explanation of how the finite-β message passing algorithm converges to the MAP solution.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and lacks detail.  
- Possible lack of novelty—algorithm may be equivalent to Expectation-Propagation applied to matrix factorization.  
- Comparison limited to k-means++; insufficient engagement with broader clustering literature.  
- Sketchy proofs and potential overlap with EP reduce confidence in technical and practical contribution.  
- Suggested fixes: provide detailed derivations, clarify asymptotics, give a rigorous proof of Proposition 1, and explain relation to prior EP-based work.  
- Suggested fixes: expand experimental comparisons to include a wider set of clustering baselines.  
- Arguments for acceptance: new matrix factorization/clustering algorithm with promising experiments, principled Bayesian framing, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak/sketchy derivations, insufficient asymptotic rigor, unclear MAP convergence, brief proofs, possible non-novelty, and narrow empirical comparisons.
