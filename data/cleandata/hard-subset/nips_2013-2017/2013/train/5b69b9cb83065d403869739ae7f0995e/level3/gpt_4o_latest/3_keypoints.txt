- Introduces an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix factorization and extends it to clustering by reformulating clustering as low-rank reconstruction.
- Frames matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian context using structural priors (e.g., sparsity) and addresses both marginalization and MAP problems.
- Experimental results show the AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s k-means and k-means++ in K-means loss and accuracy, especially in high dimensions.
- Paper is clear and well-structured, with an effective articulation of the connection between low-rank factorization and clustering.
- Novel application: using AMP with explicit constraints on cluster assignments is a new contribution.
- Provides comprehensive numerical experiments on synthetic and real datasets validating the approach.
- AMP demonstrates fast convergence and linear computational complexity in matrix size, making it scalable.
- Mathematical derivations are incomplete in places (e.g., justification for O(1/√m) term and growth assumptions for m and n).
- Proofs and convergence analysis are insufficiently detailed, notably Proposition 1 and convergence to MAP for finite β.
- Experimental comparisons are limited to simple baselines (k-means, k-means++), lacking comparisons to stronger methods like spectral clustering or GMMs.
- Originality concerns: the algorithm appears closely related to Expectation-Propagation (EP) and its novelty over EP-based methods is unclear.
- The connection to the EP “clutter” problem and related prior work is not explored, missing potential theoretical insight.
- Recommendation: weak reject pending fixes — address theoretical gaps, broaden experimental comparisons, and clarify novelty.
- Pros for acceptance: novel AMP application to clustering, strong empirical performance, and clear presentation.
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete mathematical derivations/proofs, limited advanced comparisons, and ambiguity about originality.
