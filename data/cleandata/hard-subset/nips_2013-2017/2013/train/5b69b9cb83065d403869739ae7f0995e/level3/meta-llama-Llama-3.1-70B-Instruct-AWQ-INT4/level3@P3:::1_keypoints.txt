- Proposes a novel AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with additional factor structure.
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a structured low-rank matrix reconstruction problem and applies the algorithm to it.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with accessible presentation.
- Provides solid background on low-rank reconstruction and a detailed explanation of AMP and its properties.
- Establishes the connection between low-rank reconstruction and K-means convincingly.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against k-means and k-means++.
- Results show the proposed method is faster and more efficient with improved clustering quality.
- Evaluation uses both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering-quality metrics.
- Novel contribution: extends AMP to the general-rank case and enables MAP-style estimation.
- Algorithm is computationally efficient and resembles variational Bayesian matrix factorization in form.
- Main weakness: lacks strong theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Paper would benefit from citing more theoretical computer-science work that gives provable guarantees for K-means.
- Originality: interesting and novel combination of established techniques applied in a new way.
- Significance: potential to advance state of the art in low-rank reconstruction and clustering with practical impact.
- Suggestions: add theoretical analysis/convergence guarantees, relate to theoretical-CS methods, and test on more real-world datasets.
