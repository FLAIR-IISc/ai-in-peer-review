- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering by framing clustering as low-rank reconstruction.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements over Lloyd’s K-means in high-dimensional settings.  
- Leverages AMP and prior compressed sensing/Iterative Factorization work; core idea is interesting.  
- Major derivations are relegated to the Supplementary Material; key steps should be summarized in the main text.  
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on the clustering-as-factorization task and omits standard matrix-reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix), limiting comparisons.  
- Novelty appears limited; the approach seems a fairly straightforward extension of BP/Variational Message Passing and is not well positioned within that literature.  
- Significant citation and scholarship issues: prior matrix-factorization–for–clustering work is not properly acknowledged.  
- Missing discussion of Expectation Propagation (EP) and its connection to BP for continuous distributions; BP nature should be highlighted earlier (ideally in the abstract).  
- Mathematical presentation is dense with unexplained notation and confusing equations (notably 9a, 9b, 9d, 9e); readability suffers.  
- The role of the 'm' factor in the noise variance is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Authors miss opportunities to simplify and show connections between equations that would aid understanding.  
- English and phrasing require polishing throughout the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: potentially useful algorithm, demonstrated empirical benefits, and a sound mathematical foundation (albeit obscured).  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, narrow evaluation on a non-standard task, poor citation/contextualization, and unclear mathematical exposition with derivations pushed to the supplement.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form with encouragement to resubmit after substantial revisions addressing clarity, validation, and scholarship.
