- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization with a temperature parameter β unifying marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), including Onsager-like corrections.
- Specializes the method to clustering by encoding one-hot assignments, yielding a modified K-means with bias-correction and cluster-size terms.
- Connects to prior AMP/GAMP and biG-AMP work and to variational Bayes matrix factorization; claimed novelty is handling general priors and the β→∞ MAP limit plus one-hot constraint handling.
- Strength: clear motivation and readable exposition; main ideas are easy to grasp.
- Strength: elegant algorithmic form showing VB/ICM updates arise by dropping Onsager terms.
- Strength: clustering specialization is computationally light and yields intuitive corrections; experiments show improvements over K-means++ on synthetic and ORL data with fast convergence.
- Concern Q1: Appendix skips justification that a term is O(1/√m); missing randomness/concentration assumptions and explicit β dependence.
- Concern Q2: Asymptotic limits assume m,N grow proportionally with fixed r but this is not stated; need explicit LLN/CLT/SE assumptions and valid distributional conditions on U0,V0,W.
- Concern Q3: Replacing E[A_il^2] by m τw^2 ignores signal contribution; requires justification or a consistent τ estimator with concentration bounds.
- Concern Q4: Claim that β→∞ yields MAP fixed points is delicate; need conditions for exchanging β→∞ with KL/BETHE minimization and convergence guarantees.
- Concern Q5 / Prop.1: Proof is too brief—must derive (17)/(18b) step-by-step from Algorithm 2 for one-hot priors and show how Onsager terms produce bias/size corrections.
- Additional rigor: state evolution is mentioned but no equations or proof sketch; provide SE system or pointer to rigorous bilinear AMP results.
- Scaling: clarify the mτ noise variance scaling earlier and its role since τ is later estimated in clustering.
- Originality: algorithm resembles EP/biG-AMP; authors should clarify differences from EP-based matrix factorization and prior biG-AMP work.
- Significance: empirical baselines are too narrow—compare to EM for GMMs, DP-means, spectral clustering, normalized cuts, and EP-based clustering where applicable.
- Clarity/reproducibility: provide a self-contained derivation of Algorithm 3 from Algorithm 2, precise asymptotic assumptions, SE description, pseudocode with stopping criteria and τ estimator, and code/seeds.
- Pros summary: clear idea, principled link between AMP and K-means, competitive performance, fast convergence, and applicability to non-Gaussian priors.
- Cons summary: incomplete derivations, unclear novelty relative to EP/biG-AMP, limited clustering baselines, and weak justification of MAP/variance approximations.
- Actionable questions for authors: supply β-dependent scaling and O(1/√m) justification; state formal asymptotic assumptions and expand (19); justify m τw^2 approximation or provide τ estimator; give conditions for β→∞ interchange and MAP convergence; expand Proposition 1 proof and derivations for one-hot priors.
- Recommendation: borderline reject in current form due to rigor and originality concerns and limited evaluation; could be strong with tighter theory, clearer differentiation from EP/biG-AMP, and broader experiments.
