- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering by reformulating clustering as matrix reconstruction.  
- Derives an AMP-based algorithm extending prior AMP work, with novelty claimed in enforcing one-cluster-per-point constraint.  
- Claims empirical advantages over Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF, with faster convergence and better accuracy on synthetic and ORL face data.  
- Strength: mathematically sound derivations (mostly in supplementary material).  
- Strength: clear presentation of algorithmic computational properties.  
- Strength: promising empirical results on the presented datasets.  
- Weakness: evaluation is non-standard and omits conventional matrix-reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix), limiting assessment of general utility.  
- Weakness: novelty is questionable—appears to be a straightforward BP application similar to prior Variational Message Passing work without sufficient differentiation.  
- Weakness: asymptotic approximations (m,N→∞) are neither validated nor clearly justified in the main text; key derivation steps are relegated to the supplement.  
- Poor scholarship: fails to cite seminal matrix factorization works (e.g., [10,11]) and does not properly reference EP or clarify the BP nature in the abstract.  
- Presentation issues: confusing equations and excessive notation (hats, tildes, subscripts), inconsistent use of β, and unclear role of the m factor in noise variance.  
- Clarity issues: key functions (fβ, Gβ) are not defined upon first use and the mathematical presentation is hard to parse.  
- Recommendation: paper is potentially publishable after substantial revision—validate approximations, move key derivations to main text, clarify notation, properly cite related work, and evaluate on standard datasets.
