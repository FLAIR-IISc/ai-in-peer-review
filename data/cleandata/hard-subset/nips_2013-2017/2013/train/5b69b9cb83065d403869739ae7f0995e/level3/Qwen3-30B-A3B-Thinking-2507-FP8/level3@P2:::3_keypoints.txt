- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering by adding structural constraints, extending rank-one AMP to general rank and enabling both marginalization and MAP estimation.  
- Claims the proposed AMP clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ on synthetic and ORL face datasets in accuracy and convergence speed.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presented, with a unified AMP framework that handles marginalization and MAP problems.  
- Experimental results are comprehensive versus K-means variants and show improved accuracy and convergence.  
- Theoretical derivations are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, undermining confidence in the analysis.  
- Asymptotic analysis assumptions (m and N growth) lack rigorous justification.  
- Specific mathematical issues: unexplained O(1/√m) term in (11) and omitted β, questionable claim mτ_w^2 = E[A_{il}^2], and unclear derivation of (17) and Proposition 1.  
- The claimed connection between finite-β AMP and MAP solutions is unjustified because exchanging limits and KL minimization may be invalid.  
- Contribution appears to be an application/specialization of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization rather than a novel algorithm.  
- Paper insufficiently cites and differentiates from prior EP-based matrix factorization work (e.g., Stern et al. 2009, Minka’s clutter problem).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to K-means comparisons and omits relevant state-of-the-art clustering methods (spectral, hierarchical, recent approaches).  
- Theoretical weaknesses and potential overlap with EP methods reduce the paper’s significance.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear presentation, promising experimental improvements, novel AMP application to clustering constraints, favorable convergence vs variational Bayes.  
- Cons against acceptance: weak theoretical foundation, insufficient distinction from EP, limited experimental baselines, omitted critical mathematical steps.  
- Recommendation: reject with suggestion for major revision to strengthen theoretical justification, explicitly compare and position relative to EP literature, and broaden experimental comparisons.
