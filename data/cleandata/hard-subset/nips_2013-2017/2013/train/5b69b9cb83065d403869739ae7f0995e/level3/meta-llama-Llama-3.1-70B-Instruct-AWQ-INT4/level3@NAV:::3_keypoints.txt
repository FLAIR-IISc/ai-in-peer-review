- Paper proposes an AMP algorithm for matrix factorization, specialized to clustering, and is well-structured and easy to follow.  
- Strength: efficiently solves low-rank matrix reconstruction with low computational cost (time complexity O(mN)).  
- Strength: can approximate marginal posterior distributions and MAP estimates.  
- Strength: algorithm resembles variational Bayesian matrix factorization but includes extra terms capturing U–V dependence.  
- Major concern: mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps (e.g., derivation of eq. (11) and unjustified O(1/√m) term).  
- Major concern: asymptotic analysis is terse and lacks rigor, relying on the assumption that m and n grow at the same rate (e.g., derivation of eq. (19)).  
- Concern: discrepancies in notation and terminology (β omission in Section 2; unclear definition of q̂(u; b, Λ, p̂)).  
- Originality concern: algorithm may essentially be Expectation-Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization (cf. Stern et al. 2009).  
- Concern: connection to EP “clutter” problem (Minka) and related EP interpretations is not fully explored.  
- Significance limited by lack of comparisons to other clustering algorithms and datasets.  
- Evaluation relies on a single metric (K‑means loss), limiting assessment of practical advantages.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness but are not comprehensive.  
- Overall conclusion: interesting idea but requires significant revisions to address quality, originality, and significance concerns.
