- Presents a novel AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that supports posterior mean, MAP, and marginal inference.
- Formulates K-means clustering as a special case of low-rank matrix reconstruction with indicator constraints and uses it as the main evaluation.
- Strong empirical performance: AMP-KM and AMP-MA outperform Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and VBMF on synthetic and real data, with fast convergence for AMP-MA.
- Flexible Bayesian framework: allows incorporation of structural priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity), suggesting wide applicability.
- Theoretical insight: Proposition 1 shows AMP fixed points are also ICM fixed points, implying potential robustness to poor local minima.
- Major evaluation weakness: lacks experiments on standard low-rank/matrix-reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix, MovieLens), limiting assessment of general utility.
- Presentation clarity issues: notation is dense and cluttered (many superscripts/subscripts/hats/tildes), making equations hard to parse.
- Key algorithmic derivation is deferred to the supplement, leaving insufficient intuition for Algorithm 2 in the main text.
- Important functions/parameters (f, G, β) are introduced without clear explanation of their roles.
- Modeling choice of noise-variance factor m is not well-motivated in the main text and needs brief justification.
- Scholarship/positioning: the clustering-as-factorization idea is not novel and should be acknowledged; main novelty is the AMP solver.
- Needs clearer relation to other message-passing/approximate inference methods (e.g., Expectation Propagation) and stronger contextualization of the BP derivation.
- Argument for acceptance: significant empirical gains on clustering, flexible Bayesian approach, and a useful theoretical result explaining performance.
- Argument for rejection: inadequate evaluation on core matrix-reconstruction benchmarks, substantial clarity and reproducibility issues, and insufficient literature positioning.
- Overall verdict: promising algorithm with valuable contributions, but requires major revisions to evaluation, clarity, and literature positioning before publication.
