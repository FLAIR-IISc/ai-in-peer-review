- Proposes a new Bayesian AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction that incorporates structural properties (e.g., sparsity).  
- Extends AMP to the general-rank case, enabling posterior mean and MAP estimation.  
- Applies the AMP to clustering (clustering-as-factorization) and shows it outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in experiments.  
- Evaluation is limited to a non-standard clustering-as-factorization task and lacks tests on standard matrix-reconstruction datasets (e.g., Netflix) or justification for omission.  
- The method appears to be a fairly straightforward application of belief propagation to a problem previously treated with Variational Message Passing, limiting perceived novelty.  
- Asymptotic approximations (N→∞, m→∞) are presented but not validated or clearly justified in the main text.  
- Paper has citation gaps and scholarship issues—fails to cite prior matrix-factorization-for-clustering work and omits discussion of Expectation Propagation and related BP work for continuous distributions.  
- Mathematical presentation is generally followable but contains confusing equations and unexplained multiplicative factors.  
- Notation is cluttered (excessive hats/tildes/sub/superscripts and unnecessary time indices); conventions should be stated and simplified.  
- Misses obvious simplifications and connections (e.g., symmetry in Algorithm 2) and fails to clearly define functions like fβ and Gβ when first introduced.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and promising but needs clearer exposition, validation of approximations, and improved scholarship before acceptance.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: effective clustering performance and novel general-rank extension.  
- Arguments against acceptance: non-standard evaluation, unjustified asymptotics, cluttered notation, and insufficient clarity/scholarship.
