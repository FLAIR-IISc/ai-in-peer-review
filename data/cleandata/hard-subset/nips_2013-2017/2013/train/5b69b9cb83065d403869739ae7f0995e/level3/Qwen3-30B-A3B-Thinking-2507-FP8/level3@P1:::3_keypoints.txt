- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural priors, extending AMP from compressed sensing to matrix factorization.  
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem and derives a novel clustering method via AMP.  
- Main technical claim: unified AMP framework handling general-rank matrices and both marginalization and MAP estimation.  
- Empirical claim: proposed algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means/K-means++ on synthetic and ORL face data, with lower K-means loss, higher accuracy, and comparable or faster convergence.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and well-structured, accessible presentation.  
- Strength: strong experimental results demonstrating empirical improvement over K-means++.  
- Strength: insightful connection articulated between matrix reconstruction and clustering.  
- Major concern: mathematical derivations are sketchy, with omitted non-trivial steps even in the supplement.  
- Major concern: asymptotic analysis assumptions (e.g., growth rates of m and N, derivation of eq.19) are unjustified.  
- Major concern: questionable identity mτ_w^2 = E[A_il^2] conflates likelihood variance and data statistics without justification.  
- Major concern: convergence of finite-β AMP to the MAP solution is not rigorously established; proofs (e.g., Proposition 1, eq.17) are too brief/unclear.  
- Originality concern: algorithm appears essentially equivalent to Expectation-Propagation (EP) for matrix factorization (e.g., Stern et al. 2009), with insufficient differentiation from prior EP work.  
- Missing connections: does not address related work such as Minka’s "clutter" problem or broader matrix-factorization clustering literature (NMF, convex MF).  
- Significance limited by insufficient comparisons to relevant prior methods and lack of clarity on improvements over established approaches.  
- Overall: interesting empirical application but technical shortcomings and weak theoretical justification substantially weaken the contribution.  
- Acceptance arguments: clear presentation, strong empirical performance, novel framing of clustering via matrix reconstruction.  
- Rejection arguments: sketchy mathematics, likely equivalence to existing EP methods, inadequate literature comparisons, and insufficient theoretical rigor—paper does not meet conference quality standards without major revision.
