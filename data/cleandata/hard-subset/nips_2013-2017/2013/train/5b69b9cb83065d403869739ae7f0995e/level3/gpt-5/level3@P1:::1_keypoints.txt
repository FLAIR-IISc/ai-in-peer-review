- Proposal: AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors to encode sparsity, non-negativity, or discrete constraints.  
- β-family: Introduces a β-parameterized posterior unifying marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), with an AMP iteration producing posterior means, marginal MAPs, and MAP estimates.  
- Clustering specialization: Enforces one-hot rows in V to yield a K-means-type formulation; AMP-KM adds Onsager-like corrections that downweight current assignments and encourage balanced clusters.  
- Empirical results: Synthetic and ORL face experiments show improved K-means loss and often higher clustering accuracy vs Lloyd’s K-means and k-means++; faster convergence than variational Bayes in some cases, with occasional collapse to a single cluster.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends general-rank AMP to MAP and marginal-MAP for arbitrary factor priors, distinguishes itself from NMF/convex-factorization and recent bilinear AMP works by fixed-rank scaling, and situates the K-means connection within known PCA/K-means links.  
- Technical soundness: AMP derivation is standard and symmetric for U/V with denoisers and Jacobians; β-trick is neat and Proposition 1 gives MAP fixed-point optimality, but convergence guarantees are limited and state evolution is omitted.  
- Reproducibility: Algorithm is clearly specified and clustering case transparent, but details need clarification (choice of priors in non-clustering experiments; damping/stepsize; stopping criteria; oscillation handling; τ estimation sensitivity).  
- Clarity: Well written; mapping from Bayesian model to AMP is clear and computational complexity and VB/ICM connections are well discussed.  
- Suggested theoretical additions: Include a concise state-evolution summary and explicit conditions (Gaussian noise, m,N→∞ with fixed r, properties of priors) predicting performance.  
- Suggested clarification: Specify allowed within-row dependencies for U and V, and practical convergence aids (damping, step-size, β-annealing) and safeguards for non-PSD updates or oscillations.  
- τ sensitivity: Discuss impact and estimation of τ in clustering and provide sensitivity analysis.  
- Originality: Novel extension of general-rank AMP to MAP/marginal-MAP with arbitrary factor priors and an original K-means instantiation with principled Onsager corrections.  
- Significance: Potentially competitive alternative to K-means and VB for high-dimensional clustering and structured factorization with O(mN) per-iteration cost and links to state-evolution performance prediction.  
- Weaknesses: Missing state-evolution/theoretical characterization, limited set of clustering baselines (no spectral/SVD+K-means or EM/GMM), incomplete robustness analyses, and underexplained practical details.  
- Practical concerns: For multivariate non-Gaussian priors, f and G require r-dimensional integrals—discuss approximations or closed-form cases and computational overhead.  
- Recommended experiments: Add spectral/SVD+K-means and EM GMM baselines, compare to BiG-AMP where applicable, analyze initialization/damping/τ robustness, and explain ORL failure cases.  
- Pros for acceptance: Clear principled AMP extension, effective Onsager-corrected K-means variant, strong empirical gains, and good exposition.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited theoretical guarantees, missing baselines and robustness studies, and some ad-hoc or underexplained implementation details.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—methodologically strong with promising empirical results, but would be substantially strengthened by state-evolution results, stronger baselines, and robustness analyses.
