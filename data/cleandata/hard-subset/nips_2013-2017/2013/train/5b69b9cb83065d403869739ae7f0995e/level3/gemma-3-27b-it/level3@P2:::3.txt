This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework, and demonstrates its application to clustering, achieving performance improvements over Lloyd’s K-means algorithm on certain datasets. The approach extends prior AMP work on matrix reconstruction and aims for flexibility in incorporating prior knowledge about the matrix factors.

The core idea is sound and well-motivated. Low-rank matrix completion and clustering are important problems with broad applicability, and AMP offers a potentially efficient and scalable solution. The formulation leveraging Bayesian principles and the extension to handle MAP estimation are positive contributions. The experimental results demonstrating superiority over K-means++ for high-dimensional data are encouraging. The paper is clearly structured and the key concepts are readily understandable.

However, despite the promising premise, significant concerns regarding technical rigor and originality limit my assessment of this work. The mathematical derivations throughout the paper, and particularly in the Supplementary Material, are often presented sketchily, omitting crucial steps and justifications. This lack of detail makes it difficult to fully evaluate the correctness and generality of the proposed algorithm. 

Specifically, several questions arise from the details. First, in Appendix line 139, the claim that the second term in equation (11) is O(1/√m) appears unsubstantiated, and the omission of β in Section 2 feels inconsistent with the overall parameterization. Second, the asymptotic analysis used to derive key equations, such as (19), relies on the assumption that *m* and *N* grow at the same rate. This assumption, while common, is stated tersely, and the rigor of the resulting derivations is questionable. Third, in line 167, the statement equating *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* with E[A<sub>il</sub><sup>2</sup>] requires further justification. *A* represents the observed data, while *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* corresponds to the likelihood variance – the connection needs careful explanation. Fourth, the argument for convergence to the MAP solution in the limit as β approaches infinity is problematic; simply exchanging the limit and minimization of the KL divergence is not generally valid and requires a more robust justification. Fifth, the proof of Proposition 1 is overly concise. The derivation of equation (17) is unclear and lacks sufficient detail to assess its validity. 

Further, the originality of the proposed algorithm warrants scrutiny. The authors position their work as an extension of existing AMP algorithms, but a strong argument can be made that the presented method is effectively Expectation-Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization. The updates resemble standard EP updates, as seen in works such as Stern et al. (WWW 2009), and the lack of explicit comparison to EP-based methods is a significant omission. Moreover, the proposed clustering algorithm bears a resemblance to the “clutter” problem encountered in EP, as discussed by Minka, although this is presented as a potential connection for further exploration rather than a direct critique.

Finally, the significance of the results is somewhat diminished by the limited scope of the experimental evaluation. Focusing solely on comparison with K-means++ neglects a vast body of existing clustering literature. Coupled with the concerns regarding technical rigor and potential overlap with prior EP-based work, this limited evaluation casts doubt on the overall practical and theoretical contribution of the paper.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Presents a novel application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
*   Demonstrates improved performance over K-means++ on specific datasets.
*   Clear presentation and well-structured paper.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of mathematical rigor in derivations and proofs.
*   Potential overlap with existing Expectation-Propagation (EP) methods.
*   Limited experimental evaluation, lacking comparison to broader clustering literature.
*   Questionable assumptions in the asymptotic analysis.



**Overall Assessment:**

I rate this paper as **weak reject**. While the ideas presented are interesting and the experimental results are promising, the lack of technical detail, potential overlap with existing work, and limited evaluation prevent me from recommending acceptance. Substantial revisions addressing the raised concerns, particularly regarding mathematical rigor and a more thorough comparison with related work, would be necessary for reconsideration.
