- Proposes an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, derived as a BP approximation under large-dimension asymptotics.  
- Focuses evaluation on clustering, framing clustering as a special case of low-rank matrix factorization.  
- Empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and a Variational Bayes Matrix Factorization baseline on the presented tasks.  
- Builds on the established AMP framework, providing a theoretically grounded approach adapted from compressed sensing.  
- Mathematical derivation appears generally sound though dense and relegated largely to supplementary material.  
- Primary evaluation is limited and non-standard, preventing assessment on conventional low-rank benchmarks (e.g., collaborative filtering); needs standard datasets or a strong justification.  
- Central asymptotic approximations are not validated or discussed sufficiently in the main text.  
- Novelty is limited—approach seems a direct application of BP/AMP and lacks discussion of related methods like Variational Message Passing and Expectation Propagation.  
- Scholarship is incomplete, with missing citations and insufficient connection to prior work on matrix factorization and clustering.  
- Clarity suffers from cluttered and inconsistent notation, confusing equations, and prose that requires significant polishing; key derivation steps should be summarized in the main paper.  
- Conclusion: promising algorithm but paper is not ready for publication; major revision required to address evaluation, novelty, scholarship, and clarity issues.
