This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, specializing it to clustering and comparing it experimentally to k-means++. The paper is well-structured and the main idea is easy to grasp.

The strengths of the paper lie in its ability to efficiently solve the low-rank matrix reconstruction problem and its application to clustering. The proposed AMP algorithm has a low computational cost, with a time complexity of O(mN), and can be used to approximate the marginal posterior distribution and the maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimate. The algorithm also has a form similar to that of an algorithm based on the variational Bayesian matrix factorization, but with additional terms that allow it to capture the dependence between the variables U and V.

However, there are several concerns regarding the quality of the paper. The mathematical derivations are very sketchy and omit many non-trivial steps, even in the appendix. For example, the derivation of equation (11) is not clear, and the second term is stated to be O(1/√m) without justification. Additionally, the asymptotic analysis, including the derivation of equation (19), assumes that m and n grow at the same order, but the arguments are terse and lack rigor.

Furthermore, there are some discrepancies in the notation and terminology used in the paper. For instance, β appears to be omitted in Section 2, and the definition of the function q̂(u; b,Λ, p̂) is not clear.

The originality of the paper is also a concern. The algorithm may essentially be Expectation-Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization, as pointed out by Stern et al. (WWW 2009) and standard EP updates in the appendix. Additionally, the connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the EP "clutter" problem (Minka) is not fully explored.

The significance of the paper is also limited by the lack of comparison with other clustering algorithms and the reliance on a single metric, the K-means loss function. The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithm and a clearer discussion of its advantages and limitations.

In conclusion, while the paper proposes an interesting algorithm for matrix factorization and clustering, it requires significant revisions to address the concerns regarding quality, originality, and significance.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm for matrix factorization and clustering.
* The algorithm has a low computational cost and can be used to approximate the marginal posterior distribution and the MAP estimate.
* The paper provides some experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
