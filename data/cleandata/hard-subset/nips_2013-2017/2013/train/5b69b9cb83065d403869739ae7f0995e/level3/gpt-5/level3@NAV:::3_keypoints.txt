- Proposes an AMP-style algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization and a β-tempered posterior interpolating between marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞); MAP limit yields a clustering method that beats k-means++ on synthetic data and ORL faces.
- Connects formulation to prior AMP bilinear models (IterFac, BiG-AMP) and to variational Bayes/ICM; clustering specialization links to K-means and implies bias-correction and size-regularization in assignments.
- Empirical results are promising but the theoretical development is too sketchy for NIPS standards, with key derivations omitted and asymptotic/scaling assumptions not made precise.
- Q1: Must justify why the second term in (11) is O(1/√m) and consistently carry β from (8) into q̂(·) and the Onsager terms—β seems omitted earlier yet 1/β appears later.
- Q2: Asymptotic claims (including (19)) appear to assume m,n→∞ with m/n→c and fixed r; the paper should state randomness/independence/moment assumptions and provide a state-evolution-style argument or precise limit theorem.
- Q3: The identity m τw^2 = E[Ail^2] is generally incorrect when A contains signal+noise; justify this plug-in approximation or use an unbiased τw estimator from residuals with DOF correction.
- Q4: Clarify how finite-β message passing converges to MAP as β→∞; exchanging limit and KL minimization requires Γ-/epi-convergence arguments or uniform convergence conditions.
- Q5: Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief—derive (17) explicitly (complete the square), show how Onsager terms produce the proximal-like correction, and state PSD conditions.
- Clarity: Paper is well organized and high-level exposition is clear; including missing derivations would substantially improve reproducibility.
- Originality: Potential substantial overlap with Expectation Propagation for matrix factorization; authors should clarify novelty and relationship to EP (and EP treatments of “clutter” for clustering).
- Clustering discussion: Compare assignment update to EP treatments and clarify differences from EP-style updates.
- Significance: Comparisons limited to k-means++; should include spectral clustering, GMM-EM, DP-means, k-medoids, and AMP/EP baselines (e.g., BiG-AMP), and report runtime and sensitivity to β, τ-estimation, and initialization.
- Pros: Clear problem setup, practical algorithm, and strong empirical gains over k-means++; insightful bias and cluster-size correction in assignment rule.
- Cons: Insufficient theoretical rigor and missing derivations; questionable variance approximations and unclear β→∞ justification; limited empirical baselines and unclear novelty vs EP.
- Overall: Promising work but currently below the bar—would be acceptable if theory, positioning vs EP/AMP, and empirical evaluation are substantially strengthened.
