- Studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured priors and proposes an AMP-style algorithm approximating loopy belief propagation over U and V.  
- Introduces a one-parameter family of power posteriors p(U,V|A)^β to unify marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞).  
- Algorithm includes Onsager-type correction terms; removing them yields a VB/ICM-style alternating scheme, clarifying connections to prior work.  
- Specializes framework to clustering by enforcing one-hot rows in V, producing an AMP variant of K-means with principled bias-corrected assignment steps.  
- Empirical results: improves K-means loss and accuracy over Lloyd’s and K-means++ on synthetic data and ORL Faces; AMP “maximum accuracy” outperforms a VBMF baseline on synthetic data.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on well-established AMP/GAMP and variational MF; novelty is (i) extending bilinear AMP to MAP via β→∞ and (ii) handling exactly-one cluster constraints within AMP.  
- Technical soundness: derivation plausible and consistent with AMP; Proposition 1 gives fixed-point optimality but core derivations and state evolution are relegated to the supplement.  
- Asymptotic assumptions (m,N→∞, fixed r, i.i.d. Gaussian noise) are not validated empirically or theoretically in the main text.  
- Convergence concerns: oscillations in clustering observed but not analyzed; no discussion of damping or other stabilization strategies.  
- Empirics limited in scope: careful clustering experiments but lacking evaluations on standard low-rank reconstruction/denoising benchmarks and broader Bayesian MF baselines.  
- Reproducibility: algorithmic details and stopping criteria provided, but key derivations and choices (e.g., τ estimation) are insufficiently explained in the main text.  
- Clarity issues: heavy, hard-to-parse notation; undefined symbols; inconsistent treatment of β; roles of functions f and G and other identities need clearer exposition.  
- Originality: incremental extension of bilinear AMP to MAP and novel AMP-based enforcement of one-hot constraints, but significant prior art exists and EP/continuous BP are not discussed.  
- Significance: method could be practically relevant (O(mN) per iteration, better local minima) if asymptotics and broader performance were validated; current impact limited.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled AMP formulation, unification of marginal and MAP inference, novel bias-corrected K-means variant, strong clustering results and iteration efficiency.  
- Cons for acceptance: narrow evaluation, deferred theoretical validation, scholarship gaps on related methods/baselines, and substantial clarity/notation problems.  
- Suggested improvements: bring concise derivation and state-evolution intuition into main text; validate asymptotics by scaling experiments; evaluate on standard MF/denoising tasks and baselines (BPMF, VBMF, alternating minimization); add stronger clustering baselines; clarify experimental priors/implementations; simplify notation and treat β consistently; address oscillations (damping/tie-breaking).  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject — promising work but needs stronger validation, clearer exposition, and improved positioning against prior literature.
