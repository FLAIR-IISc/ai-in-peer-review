- Proposes a BP/AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction extended to general rank and MAP estimation.  
- Core idea builds on prior compressed sensing and matrix factorization work but is not well positioned within that literature.  
- Extension to MAP from prior AMP is incremental and its impact is limited.  
- Application to clustering is interesting but framed as novel without properly citing existing matrix-factorization clustering methods.  
- Fails to discuss relationship to Expectation Propagation (EP), an important related technique.  
- Technical approach is plausible but key derivations are relegated to the Supplementary Material, hurting understanding.  
- Equations (9a, 9b, 9d, 9e) are hard to parse and contain unexplained factors.  
- Notational clutter (hats, tildes, subscripts/superscripts) and inconsistent conventions reduce readability.  
- Misses obvious simplifications and connections between equations that would aid comprehension.  
- Definitions of functions (fβ, Gβ) are unclear at first use and need explicit clarification.  
- The role of the 'm' factor in noise variance and its effect on row-column symmetry is unexplained.  
- Asymptotic approximations (N→∞, m→∞) lack justification and empirical validation.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a clustering task rather than standard matrix-reconstruction benchmarks, reducing generalizability assessment.  
- Baseline implementations (e.g., VBMF-MA) lack clear citation or description, making comparisons ambiguous.  
- Supplementary Material contains unclear derivation steps and could be better organized and explained; overall English and presentation need polishing.  
- Empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ in experiments, showing promise.  
- Reviewer recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after major revisions addressing clarity, validation, and scholarship.
