- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural priors (e.g., sparsity).  
- Extends AMP to handle both marginalization (posterior mean) and MAP estimation problems.  
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction and applies AMP to clustering.  
- Correctly situates work relative to prior AMP matrix reconstruction literature and contrasts with variational Bayes via Bethe approximation.  
- Novelty lies in using AMP for Bayesian inference with structural priors and addressing MAP estimation not just posterior means.  
- Novel contribution in enforcing the “one cluster per data point” constraint within a low-rank framework.  
- Strength: technically sound mathematical formulation and algorithmic development.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments on synthetic data (500 instances) and real face clustering (50 trials).  
- Strength: evaluates multiple metrics (K-means loss, accuracy, convergence speed) and compares to Lloyd’s, K-means++, variational Bayes.  
- Strength: well-written, clearly organized, with clear problem statements and algorithm descriptions.  
- Strength: supplementary material appears sufficient for reproducibility.  
- Strength/Originality: provides a fresh AMP-based perspective on clustering and low-rank reconstruction.  
- Significance: computationally efficient alternative with empirical advantages in convergence and solution quality, especially in high dimensions.  
- Weakness: lacks rigorous convergence guarantees for the proposed AMP algorithm.  
- Weakness: could better contextualize and differentiate from prior low-rank clustering/factorization work (e.g., PCA-related and Ding et al.).  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis and limits compared to existing methods are underdeveloped.  
- Pro-accept: novel approach, strong empirical evaluation, well-written, and insightful clustering application.  
- Con-accept: limited theoretical convergence guarantees and weaker contextualization with prior clustering literature.  
- Overall: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen theoretical discussion and contextualization.
