This paper proposes a new belief propagation (BP)-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using approximations assuming large m and N. The algorithm is evaluated by framing clustering as matrix reconstruction and compared to Lloyd's K-means and Variational Bayes Matrix Factorization (VBMF). The paper is interesting and potentially useful, and the mathematics are generally followable.

However, there are several concerns that need to be addressed. First, the evaluation on a non-standard task (clustering-as-factorization) makes it hard to judge performance on conventional matrix-reconstruction datasets (e.g., Netflix). The authors should use standard datasets or justify not doing so. Second, the algorithm appears to be a fairly straightforward application of BP to a problem previously addressed with Variational Message Passing, so novelty is limited. Third, the asymptotic approximations (N→∞, m→∞) are interesting but their validity is not tested or clearly justified in the main text.

Additionally, the paper has several issues with citation and scholarship. The paper implies novelty of matrix factorization for clustering without citing prior work or stating the connection is well-known. Expectation Propagation (EP) and the relation to applying BP to continuous distributions are not cited or discussed. The BP nature of the algorithm should be stated earlier (e.g., in the abstract).

The paper also has several issues with clarity and presentation. The derivation is relegated to supplementary material, which is a drawback. Key derivation steps should appear or be summarized in the main paper. The mathematical presentation is hard to parse, with specific confusing equations (9a, 9b, 9d, 9e) and unexplained factors. Notational clutter (excessive hats, tildes, subscripts/superscripts, unnecessary time indices) makes the algorithm harder to read. The authors should simplify notation and state conventions.

Furthermore, the paper misses obvious simplifications and connections (e.g., eq.5 = negative log of eq.2; eq.8 = eq.2^β; symmetry in Algorithm 2) that would aid readability. Definitions and meanings of functions (e.g., fβ, Gβ) are not clearly explained near first use and should be clarified. The use of β subscripts and other dependencies is inconsistent; β is a global parameter and notation should reflect that or be simplified. The role and significance of the m factor in the additive noise variance and its effect on row-column symmetry are unclear and should be explained.

Finally, the paper has several minor issues with notation and style. The use of tilde for column vectors is inconsistent with capitalization of N versus m. A clear citation for the VBMF-MA implementation used as a baseline is needed. The supplementary material has clarity issues (e.g., suggestion to show product of exponentials, unclear step from eq.14 to 15, and unclear relation of approximations to prior literature). The English and phrasing need polishing throughout, and the title and many sentences could be improved for clarity.

Overall, the algorithm is promising and publishable, but the paper currently lacks sufficient clarity, validation of approximations, and scholarship to be accepted as-is.

**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

1. The algorithm is promising and has shown good performance in experiments.
2. The paper is well-organized and easy to follow.
3. The authors provide a clear explanation of the algorithm and its properties.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

1. The evaluation on a non-standard task makes it hard to judge performance on conventional matrix-reconstruction datasets.
2. The algorithm appears to be a fairly straightforward application of BP to a problem previously addressed with Variational Message Passing, so novelty is limited.
3. The asymptotic approximations are interesting but their validity is not tested or clearly justified in the main text.
4. The paper has several issues with citation and scholarship.
5. The paper has several issues with clarity and presentation.

**Recommendations for Improvement:**

1. Evaluate the algorithm on standard matrix-reconstruction datasets (e.g., Netflix).
2. Clearly justify the asymptotic approximations and provide evidence for their validity.
3. Improve citation and scholarship by citing prior work and stating the connection to well-known methods.
4. Simplify notation and state conventions to improve readability.
5. Clarify definitions and meanings of functions (e.g., fβ, Gβ) near first use.
6. Improve the presentation of the algorithm and its properties.
7. Provide a clear citation for the VBMF-MA implementation used as a baseline.
8. Polish the English and phrasing throughout the paper.
