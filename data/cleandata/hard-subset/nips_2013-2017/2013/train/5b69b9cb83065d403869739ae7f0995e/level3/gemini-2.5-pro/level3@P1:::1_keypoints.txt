- Novel AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using Bayesian priors on factors U and V.  
- Introduces a temperature parameter β to unify posterior mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) estimation.  
- Derives an AMP-based clustering algorithm by casting K-means as low-rank factorization with one-hot V rows.  
- AMP updates include Onsager reaction terms, distinguishing the method from VB and ICM.  
- Main technical contribution: extension of AMP to perform approximate Bayesian and MAP inference for matrix factorization.  
- Empirical results: consistently outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and a VB approach on synthetic data and ORL faces in both K-means loss and clustering accuracy.  
- Experiments are thorough, comparing optimization objective and task-specific metrics.  
- Paper is clearly written and effectively relates the method to prior work.  
- Strength: originality and significant empirical gains on difficult, high-dimensional clustering tasks.  
- Weakness: no theoretical convergence guarantees; observed oscillations and reliance on heuristic stopping.  
- Weakness: insufficient positioning relative to existing theoretical K-means literature and provable approximation algorithms.  
- Weakness: the noise-variance parameter τ is heuristic; its impact and sensitivity are not deeply analyzed.  
- Overall: strong empirical and algorithmic contribution; recommended for acceptance despite theoretical gaps.
