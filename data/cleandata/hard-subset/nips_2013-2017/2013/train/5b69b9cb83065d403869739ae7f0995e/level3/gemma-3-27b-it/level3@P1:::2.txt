This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending prior work on AMP for this problem and applying it to clustering by reformulating clustering as a low-rank reconstruction task. The authors aim to provide a flexible method incorporating structural properties beyond low-rankness and demonstrate improved performance compared to Lloyd’s K-means algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

The work builds upon the established connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and Bayesian inference, leveraging AMP as an efficient approximation technique. It extends existing AMP algorithms [9] to handle MAP estimation, a crucial step for practical applications. The application to clustering is presented as a novel approach directly addressing the constraint of unique cluster assignment within the low-rank framework [10, 11]. The algorithm’s performance is evaluated through numerical experiments on both artificial and real (face recognition) datasets.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Extension of AMP:** The extension of the existing AMP algorithm to handle MAP estimation is a valuable contribution, broadening the applicability of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction.
*   **Promising Empirical Results:** The numerical experiments demonstrate that the proposed algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means algorithm, particularly on high-dimensional data. The clustering results on the ORL face database are encouraging.
*   **Unified Framework:** The formulation that encompasses both matrix reconstruction and clustering within a single framework is conceptually appealing and offers potential for future extensions.
*   **Potential for Scalability:** The AMP algorithm’s computational efficiency, with a complexity of O(mN) per iteration, suggests good scalability to larger datasets.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty:** While extending AMP is valuable, the core idea of applying message passing techniques to matrix factorization is not entirely novel. The connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering is well-established, and the paper lacks sufficient acknowledgement of prior work in this area. Specifically, related work on Expectation Propagation (EP) and its application to continuous distributions, which shares similarities with the BP nature of this algorithm, is not discussed.
*   **Lack of Standard Benchmarking:** The evaluation is largely confined to comparisons with Lloyd's K-means. The absence of comparisons with more established matrix reconstruction algorithms on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix prize data) limits the assessment of the algorithm’s true competitiveness. The reliance on clustering-as-factorization as the primary evaluation task makes it difficult to directly gauge performance on conventional matrix reconstruction problems.
*   **Asymptotic Approximations:** The algorithm relies on asymptotic approximations (m, N → ∞). The validity of these approximations and their impact on performance in practical scenarios are not thoroughly investigated or justified.
*   **Clarity and Presentation:** The mathematical presentation is dense and often difficult to follow. Equations (9a, 9b, 9d) and the introduction of numerous notational elements (hats, tildes, subscripts, superscripts) contribute to the lack of clarity. The exposition lacks obvious simplifications and connections between equations, hindering understanding. The definitions of functions like fβ and Gβ are not immediately clear.
*   **Insufficient Detail in Supplementary Material:** While the full derivation is relegated to the supplementary material, crucial steps are not detailed sufficiently, making it difficult to verify the correctness of the derivation.
*    **Inconsistent Notation:** Inconsistent capitalization of 'N' versus 'm' and the use of β as both a global parameter and subscripted create ambiguity. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel extension of AMP for low-rank matrix reconstruction.
*   Empirical results show promise and outperform a standard algorithm (K-means) in specific settings.
*   The unified framework offers potential for future research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited novelty given existing work in related areas.
*   Lack of standard benchmarking and evaluation on conventional matrix reconstruction datasets.
*   Insufficient clarity in presentation and mathematical notation.
*   Unvalidated asymptotic approximations.
*   Inadequate acknowledgment of prior art.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents an interesting and potentially useful algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. However, the current manuscript requires significant improvements in clarity, justification of approximations, and acknowledgment of prior work before it can be considered for acceptance. A major revision addressing these issues, including incorporating comparisons with standard matrix reconstruction benchmarks and streamlining the mathematical presentation, is strongly recommended. The derivation of core equations should be summarized within the main body of the paper, not exclusively in the supplementary material.
