This paper proposes an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured factor priors, and shows how clustering (K-means–type) can be cast as a special case via one-hot constraints on the right factor. The method introduces a temperature parameter β to unify marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), extending prior AMP work from rank-one IterFac and posterior-mean–only bilinear AMP to handle general rank and MAP estimation. The application to clustering leverages the classic connection between K-means and low-rank factorization (e.g., PCA/K-means relations, with known 2-approximation via PCA under certain formulations), but the use of an AMP engine and the resulting assignment update with Onsager corrections appear novel. Experiments compare against Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++, evaluating both Frobenius residuals and clustering accuracy, and show consistent gains, including on ORL faces.

Quality: The empirical evaluation is careful and uses appropriate metrics and competitive baselines, with evidence of faster convergence and better solutions. However, the theoretical side is light: there is no state evolution characterization, no finite-sample convergence guarantee, and only a fixed-point optimality proposition under PSD conditions. Observed oscillations and the heuristic τ estimation underscore the need for convergence analysis and stability safeguards.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written with a clear derivation sketch and relation to variational approaches. Some implementation choices (β selection, τ estimation, initialization, stopping) could be explained more systematically, and ablations to isolate the impact of the AMP/Onsager terms would help.

Originality: Adapting AMP to structured low-rank factors and to MAP estimation, and instantiating it for K-means, is novel relative to prior AMP literature. The K-means–low rank connection itself is well known.

Significance: If stabilized and better understood theoretically, the approach could impact scalable clustering and structured matrix factorization.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel AMP formulation for general-rank matrix reconstruction and MAP.
- Strong empirical results vs K-means/K-means++, with both reconstruction and clustering metrics.
- Computationally attractive O(mN) iterations and practical assignment improvements.

Cons against acceptance:
- Lacks convergence/state-evolution guarantees; limited theory beyond a fixed-point condition.
- Sensitivity to initialization/oscillation not fully addressed.
- Limited baselines (e.g., spectral clustering/PCA, NMF-based clustering, EM for GMMs).

Suggestions: Add state evolution (even asymptotic) and damping/stability analysis; include ablations comparing to VB/ICM without Onsager terms; evaluate robustness to rank mis-specification and non-Gaussian noise; and cite theoretical K-means guarantees (e.g., Kanungo et al. 2002 local search, Arthur & Vassilvitskii 2007 k-means++, Peng & Wei 2007 SDP, Har-Peled & Mazumdar 2004 PTAS, and work on spectral/PCA approximations such as Ding & He 2004; Boutsidis et al. 2009). Overall: a solid AMP-based contribution with promising experiments but weaker theory.
