- Proposes a novel AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction from noisy observations using a Bayesian formulation that supports structural priors.  
- Extends AMP successes in compressed sensing to general-rank matrix factorization.  
- Applies the framework to K-means clustering by framing clustering as low-rank reconstruction with a discrete constraint on one factor.  
- Derivation of a K-means-like algorithm from AMP is original and provides intuitive bias-correcting and cluster-size-aware updates.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing against strong baselines (k-means, k-means++) on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Demonstrates superior performance on both Frobenius-norm reconstruction and external clustering accuracy metrics.  
- Main weakness: lacks theoretical convergence guarantees; convergence is assumed and Proposition 1 does not ensure reachability of fixed points.  
- Secondary weakness: insufficient engagement with theoretical CS literature on clustering and provable K-means approximations for contextual comparison.  
- Overall: solid, novel contribution with strong empirical results but limited impact due to weak theoretical foundations; appropriate for the conference audience.
