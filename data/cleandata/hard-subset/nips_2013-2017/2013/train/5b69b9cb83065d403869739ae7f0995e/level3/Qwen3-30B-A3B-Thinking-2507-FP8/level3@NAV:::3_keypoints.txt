- Paper proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction applied to clustering, unifying marginalization and MAP estimation, and compares to k-means++.
- Main experimental claim: AMP yields better clustering accuracy and lower K-means loss than k-means++ on synthetic and ORL face datasets.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the connection between matrix factorization and clustering.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough for k-means++ comparisons and shows consistent empirical improvements.
- Mathematical derivations are very sketchy with multiple non-trivial steps omitted, undermining technical credibility.
- Missing justification why the second term in eq.(11) is O(1/√m) (Q1).
- Asymptotic analysis assumes m and n grow at the same order without rigorous justification (Q2).
- Claim that mτ_w² equals E[A_il²] is questionable because A is observed data (Q3).
- Convergence to the MAP solution for finite β is not properly justified (Q4).
- Proposition 1’s proof is too brief and omits key derivation steps (Q5).
- Originality is questionable: approach appears to be an EP application to matrix factorization with connections to prior work (e.g., Stern et al. 2009).
- Significance is limited by comparing only to k-means++ (omitting spectral/hierarchical methods) and by lack of rigorous theory.
- Overall recommendation: reject due to insufficient mathematical rigor and limited originality.
