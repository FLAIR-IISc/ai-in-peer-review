

# Review for "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with additional structural priors (e.g., sparsity) on the factor matrices. The authors extend previous AMP work to handle both marginalization and MAP estimation problems, and then apply the algorithm to K-means clustering by reformulating it as a constrained low-rank matrix reconstruction problem. The key novelty lies in using AMP to efficiently perform Bayesian inference for low-rank matrices with structural priors, which enables a new approach to clustering.

The paper builds on the growing body of work applying AMP to inference problems. It correctly situates itself relative to prior AMP work for matrix reconstruction (Matsushita & Tanaka, 2012) and distinguishes its approach from variational Bayes methods by leveraging the Bethe approximation. The connection between low-rank factorizations and clustering (e.g., through PCA) is well-known, but the paper makes a novel contribution by directly addressing the constraint that each data point must belong to exactly one cluster within the low-rank framework.

**Strengths:**

*Quality:* The paper presents a technically sound approach with rigorous mathematical formulation. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including both synthetic data (with multiple parameter settings) and real-world face clustering. The authors evaluate multiple metrics (K-means loss, accuracy, convergence speed) across 500 synthetic instances and 50 trials on real data, providing strong empirical support for their claims. The comparison to established baselines (Lloyd's K-means, K-means++, variational Bayes) is thorough and well-justified.

*Clarity:* The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical formulation is clear, with well-defined problem statements and algorithm descriptions. The connection between the matrix reconstruction problem and clustering is elegantly explained. The supplementary material (referenced but not included in the main paper) appears to provide sufficient detail for reproducibility.

*Originality:* The AMP-based approach to low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural priors is novel. The paper makes a clear distinction from related work, particularly in handling the MAP estimation problem (where previous AMP work was limited to posterior mean estimation). The application to clustering is both novel and well-motivated, providing a fresh perspective on an established problem.

*Significance:* The proposed algorithm offers a computationally efficient alternative to existing methods for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The empirical results demonstrate clear advantages in both convergence speed and solution quality over standard approaches, particularly in high-dimensional settings. The approach could potentially be extended to other problems involving low-rank structures.

**Weaknesses:**

*Quality:* While the empirical results are strong, the theoretical foundation is somewhat limited. The paper lacks rigorous convergence guarantees for the proposed AMP algorithm, which is a notable omission given the importance of convergence in iterative methods. The authors acknowledge this in the key points but do not provide sufficient theoretical analysis to support their claims.

*Originality:* The connection between low-rank factorizations and clustering is well-established (e.g., PCA as a 2-approximation for K-means), and the paper could have better articulated how their approach differs from previous work on this connection (e.g., the work of Ding et al. on convex and semi-nonnegative matrix factorizations).

*Significance:* The significance could be strengthened by more clearly articulating how this work advances the state of the art. While the empirical results are promising, the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the theoretical limits of their approach compared to existing methods.

**Pro and Con Arguments for Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Novel AMP-based approach to low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural priors
- Strong empirical evaluation showing clear advantages over baselines
- Well-written and technically sound paper
- Interesting application to clustering that provides new insights

*Con:*
- Limited theoretical convergence guarantees
- Connection to prior clustering work could be better contextualized
- Theoretical analysis could be strengthened to better support the empirical claims

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of matrix reconstruction and clustering. The AMP-based approach is novel, well-motivated, and demonstrated to be effective through comprehensive experiments. While the theoretical foundation could be strengthened, the empirical results are compelling and the algorithm shows clear practical advantages. The paper addresses an important problem with a technically sound approach that would be of interest to the NIPS community.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen the theoretical discussion and better contextualize the connection to prior clustering work. The authors should consider adding a brief discussion of convergence properties (even if limited) and more explicitly differentiate their approach from previous low-rank clustering methods. The paper is well-suited for NIPS and would be a valuable contribution to the proceedings.
