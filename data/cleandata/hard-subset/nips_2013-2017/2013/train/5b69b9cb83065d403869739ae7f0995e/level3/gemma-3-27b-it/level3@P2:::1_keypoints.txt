- Introduces an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending AMP beyond sparse vectors and rank-one matrices.  
- Uses a Bayesian framework to incorporate structural priors on matrix factors (e.g., sparsity).  
- Demonstrates an application to K-means by reformulating clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem.  
- Major algorithmic contribution: derivation of an AMP scheme capable of MAP estimation, not just posterior means.  
- Introduces a β-parameterized posterior to balance exploration and exploitation during inference.  
- Algorithm description is reasonably clear but some derivation details could use further elaboration.  
- Connects appropriately to compressed sensing, AMP literature, Bayesian matrix factorization, and variational Bayes.  
- Provides a proposition on optimality of algorithm fixed points, a useful theoretical contribution needing clearer assumption statements.  
- Strong experimental evaluation on synthetic and ORL face data, comparing against Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes.  
- Empirically outperforms baseline methods in speed and clustering/reconstruction accuracy, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Lacks strong theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed AMP algorithm; relies on empirical convergence behavior.  
- Assumption of positive semi-definiteness in Proposition 1 requires more justification and emphasis.  
- Clarity issues: dense notation, non-intuitive explanations of functions f and G, and abrupt motivation/description of parameter τ.  
- Originality: novel extension of AMP to low-rank MAP estimation and direct handling of single-cluster assignment constraint.  
- Significance: broad applicability across signal processing, ML, and vision; notable improvement over traditional K-means in high dimensions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — address convergence concerns and clarify assumptions/notation to strengthen the paper.
