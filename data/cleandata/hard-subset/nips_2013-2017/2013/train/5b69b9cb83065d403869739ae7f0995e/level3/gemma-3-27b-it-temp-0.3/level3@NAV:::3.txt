## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction within a Bayesian framework, extending prior work on AMP for sparse recovery. A key contribution is the application of this algorithm to clustering, framing it as a specific instance of low-rank reconstruction. The authors demonstrate, through numerical experiments, that their clustering algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the core ideas. The motivation for using AMP, leveraging its efficiency and flexibility in incorporating prior knowledge, is well articulated. The connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering is also intuitively explained. However, the technical details require significant strengthening. The mathematical derivations are consistently sketchy, omitting crucial steps even in the supplementary material. 

Specifically, my concerns are as follows: 
**Q1:** In Appendix line 139, the claim that the second term of (11) is O(1/√m) lacks justification. Furthermore, the variable β, central to the algorithm’s generalization, appears to be omitted in Section 2’s initial formulation.
**Q2:** The asymptotic analysis, including the derivation of (19), relies on the assumption that *m* and *n* grow at the same order. This assumption feels restrictive and the arguments supporting it are terse and lack rigor.
**Q3:** In line 167, equating *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* to E[A<sub>il</sub><sup>2</sup>] is problematic. *A* represents observed data, while *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* represents the likelihood variance. This approximation requires substantial justification.
**Q4:** The convergence of the message passing algorithm for finite β to the MAP solution is not clearly established. Exchanging the limit and minimization of the KL divergence is a non-trivial step that needs careful consideration.
**Q5:** The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief. The derivation of equation (17) is not sufficiently detailed to allow for independent verification.

Furthermore, the originality of the work is questionable. The proposed algorithm bears a strong resemblance to Expectation Propagation (EP), a well-established technique. The authors should address the connection to EP, particularly referencing Stern et al. (WWW 2009) and providing a detailed comparison of the update rules. The reviewer also notes a potential link to the EP “clutter” problem (Minka), which could be explored.

The significance of the work is currently limited by the narrow comparison to K-means++. A more comprehensive evaluation against other state-of-the-art clustering algorithms is necessary. The sketchy proofs and potential overlap with existing EP-based work further diminish confidence in the technical contribution.

**Arguments for acceptance:** Clear presentation of ideas, promising empirical results.
**Arguments against acceptance:** Lack of mathematical rigor, potential overlap with existing work, insufficient evaluation, questionable originality.

**Overall:** While the paper presents an interesting idea, the lack of technical depth and potential redundancy with existing work prevent me from recommending acceptance in its current form. A major revision addressing the outlined concerns is required.
