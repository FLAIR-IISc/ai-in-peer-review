- Extends AMP to handle MAP estimation for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction.  
- Applies low-rank reconstruction formulation to clustering, enforcing unique cluster assignments.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, especially in high-dimensional data (ORL face database).  
- Presents a unified framework covering both matrix reconstruction and clustering, enabling future extensions.  
- Algorithm has promising computational scalability (O(mN) per iteration).  
- Core idea has limited novelty given prior message-passing and matrix factorization work.  
- Paper insufficiently acknowledges related methods (e.g., Expectation Propagation) that are similar in spirit.  
- Evaluation lacks standard benchmarking against established matrix reconstruction algorithms and datasets (e.g., Netflix).  
- Reliance on clustering-as-factorization makes it hard to judge performance on conventional matrix reconstruction tasks.  
- Method depends on asymptotic approximations (m, N → ∞) without adequate validation for practical finite-size settings.  
- Mathematical exposition is dense and hard to follow; notation (hats, tildes, subscripts, superscripts) is cluttered.  
- Definitions of key functions (e.g., fβ, Gβ) and equations are not clearly presented.  
- Supplementary material omits crucial derivation steps, preventing verification of correctness.  
- Notation is inconsistent (e.g., capitalization of N vs m; β used ambiguously), creating ambiguity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel AMP extension, promising empirical performance, and a conceptually appealing unified framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, poor benchmarking, unclear presentation, unvalidated approximations, and inadequate prior-art discussion.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially useful but requires major revision—improve clarity, justify approximations, acknowledge prior work, include standard benchmarks, and move key derivations into the main text.
