- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured factor priors and shows clustering as a special case via one-hot constraints.  
- Introduces temperature parameter β to unify marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), extending AMP from rank-one/posterior-mean to general rank and MAP.  
- Uses AMP to derive an Onsager-corrected assignment update for clustering, claimed as a novel component.  
- Connects to classic K-means/low-rank (PCA) relations; the low-rank connection itself is not new.  
- Empirical comparisons to Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ use Frobenius residuals and clustering accuracy and show consistent gains, including on ORL faces.  
- Experimental evaluation is careful with appropriate metrics and baselines, showing faster convergence and improved solutions.  
- Theory is weak: no state evolution, no finite-sample convergence guarantees, only a fixed-point optimality under PSD conditions.  
- Observed oscillations and heuristic τ estimation indicate stability/convergence issues needing analysis.  
- Paper is generally well written with clear derivations and links to variational methods.  
- Some implementation choices (β selection, τ estimation, initialization, stopping) require more systematic explanation and ablation.  
- Adapting AMP to structured low-rank factors and MAP estimation is novel relative to prior AMP work.  
- Potentially significant for scalable clustering/structured factorization if stabilized and better understood theoretically.  
- Pros: novel general-rank/MAP AMP formulation, strong empirical results, computationally attractive O(mN) iterations.  
- Cons: lacks convergence/state-evolution theory, sensitivity to initialization/oscillation, limited baselines (missing spectral/PCA/NMF/EM comparisons).  
- Suggested additions: provide state-evolution or asymptotic analysis, damping/stability techniques, ablations without Onsager terms, robustness tests to rank/noise mis-specification, and citations to K-means theoretical work.  
- Overall: solid AMP-based contribution with promising experiments but needs stronger theoretical/stability support.
