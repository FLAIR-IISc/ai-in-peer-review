## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to the clustering problem, demonstrating performance gains over k-means++. The core idea of formulating clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem is well-motivated and clearly presented. The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear connection made between Bayesian inference, AMP, and the specific application to clustering.

However, significant concerns exist regarding the technical rigor and novelty of the work. The mathematical derivations, even in the appendix, are exceptionally sketchy, omitting crucial steps. Specifically, my questions from the initial reading remain unanswered. Q1: The justification for the O(1/√m) term in Appendix line 139 is missing, and the omission of β in Section 2 is unclear. Q2: The asymptotic analysis, particularly the derivation of equation (19), relies on assumptions about m and n growing at the same rate, which are stated tersely and lack thorough justification. Q3: The claim in line 167 equating mτ_w^2 to E[A_{il}^2] seems unsupported given that A is observed data and the former represents likelihood variance. 

Furthermore, Q4 regarding the convergence of the AMP algorithm to the MAP solution remains unanswered. The argument relying on exchanging the limit and minimization of the KL divergence is not always valid and requires more careful justification. Q5: The proof of Proposition 1 is insufficient, with the derivation of equation (17) being particularly opaque.

A major concern centers on originality. The proposed algorithm bears a strong resemblance to Expectation-Propagation (EP), particularly as highlighted in the suggestion that it is effectively EP applied to matrix factorization, with relevant prior work being Stern et al. (WWW 2009). The connection to the EP “clutter” problem (Minka) is a valid and insightful observation. The paper doesn't adequately differentiate itself from these existing approaches.

Finally, while the comparison to k-means++ demonstrates some improvements, relying solely on this benchmark is insufficient. A more comprehensive evaluation against other state-of-the-art clustering algorithms, as well as a more thorough analysis of the algorithm’s properties, is needed. The lack of detailed theoretical analysis and the potential overlap with prior EP work diminish confidence in the practical and technical contributions. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Clear presentation of the algorithm and its application to clustering.
* Demonstrable improvement over k-means++ in some experiments.
* Intuitive connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*  Insufficient mathematical rigor and missing derivation steps.
*  Potential overlap with existing Expectation-Propagation (EP) work.
*  Limited scope of experimental evaluation.
*  Lack of a compelling theoretical justification for the algorithm's performance.



**Overall Assessment:** Reject. The paper presents an interesting idea but falls short of NIPS standards due to the lack of theoretical depth and insufficient novelty. A major revision addressing the identified weaknesses is needed before reconsideration.
