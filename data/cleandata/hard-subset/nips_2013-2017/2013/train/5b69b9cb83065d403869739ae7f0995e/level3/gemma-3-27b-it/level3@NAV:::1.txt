## Review of “Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing”

This paper presents an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous work to allow for MAP estimation and applying it to the problem of K-means clustering. The core idea of leveraging AMP, a computationally efficient alternative to Bayesian methods, for matrix reconstruction is novel and potentially impactful. The connection made between low-rank reconstruction and K-means is well-known (e.g., PCA providing a 2-approximation), but the authors' attempt to directly address the hard-clustering constraint within the reconstruction framework is interesting.

The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the problem setting, prior work, and the proposed algorithm. The experimental evaluation is a significant strength, demonstrating empirically that the proposed method outperforms both standard K-means and K-means++ on both artificial and real datasets, in terms of both reconstruction error (Frobenius norm) and clustering quality. The inclusion of both metrics is commendable, providing a comprehensive assessment. The ablation study with the ORL face database is particularly convincing. 

However, the most significant weakness lies in the lack of strong theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed AMP algorithm. While the authors allude to the potential for analysis via state evolution, this is not presented, leaving the theoretical foundations somewhat lacking.  The proposition regarding optimality at fixed points is a valuable contribution, but doesn't constitute a full convergence proof. To strengthen the work, the authors should cite more theoretical computer science literature that provides provable guarantees for K-means and related clustering algorithms; this would better contextualize the empirical improvements.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction with a focus on MAP estimation.
*   Strong empirical results demonstrating improved performance compared to established K-means algorithms.
*   Clear exposition of the algorithm and experimental setup.
*   Addresses both reconstruction error and clustering accuracy.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of rigorous theoretical convergence analysis for the proposed AMP algorithm.
*   Limited discussion of the assumptions required for the theoretical results to hold.



**Overall:** This is a solid contribution with promising experimental results, but the lack of robust theoretical guarantees weakens the paper. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing the theoretical concerns, potentially through further analysis or a more thorough discussion of the limitations in the current manuscript.
