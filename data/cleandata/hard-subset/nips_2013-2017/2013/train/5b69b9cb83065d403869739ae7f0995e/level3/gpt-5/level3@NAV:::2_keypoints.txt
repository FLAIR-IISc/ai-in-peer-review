- Proposes an AMP/BP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and a K-means instantiation via constrained factorization.
- Claims an AMP generalization that handles posterior means (β=1) and MAP limits (β→∞) using a β-homotopy.
- Empirically reports improvements over Lloyd’s K-means and a VBMF baseline on synthetic and ORL data, with potential speed benefits over VBMF.
- Strength: practical, efficient algorithm with plausible Onsager-like corrections that may avoid poor local minima.
- Strength: mathematical framework is mostly followable and unifies marginalization and MAP via β-homotopy.
- Strength: experiments show consistent gains and the Algorithm 2 vs VBMF/ICM comparison is informative.
- Weakness: validity of asymptotic approximations (large m,N, fixed r; noise scaling) is not tested—needs finite-size diagnostics, state-evolution checks, and ablations on Onsager terms and τ sensitivity.
- Weakness: evaluation uses a non-standard clustering-as-factorization proxy; lacks benchmark results on standard matrix reconstruction/CF datasets (e.g., MovieLens, Netflix).
- Weakness: MAP derivations and key steps are deferred to the supplement; essential derivations and assumptions should be summarized in the main text.
- Clarity issue: heavy, confusing notation (hats/tildes/time indices); clarify conventions and make β a clearly global parameter.
- Clarity issue: several equations (e.g., 9a–9e) are hard to parse; clarify m’s role in noise variance and row/column symmetry and define fβ and Gβ at first use with intuition.
- Clarity suggestion: explicit equivalences (eq.5 vs eq.2, eq.8 vs eq.2^β) and a main-text diagram (like Supplement Fig.1) would aid understanding.
- Originality concern: novelty seems incremental over existing BP/AMP/VMP work; should explicitly state BP nature earlier and discuss EP/continuous BP and AMP/VAMP literature (e.g., Minka 2001).
- Scholarship: clarify and cite the specific VBMF-MA implementation used and acknowledge prior clustering-via-factorization work to avoid overstating novelty.
- Significance: promising but broader impact depends on validation on standard reconstruction tasks and justification of asymptotics.
- Pros for acceptance: practical algorithm with empirical benefits, unifies MAP and posterior-mean cases, computationally attractive.
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty relative to existing work, insufficient validation of approximations, non-standard evaluation, and substantial clarity/citation issues.
- Recommendation: borderline reject; publishable after significant revisions to clarity, citations, and evaluation.
