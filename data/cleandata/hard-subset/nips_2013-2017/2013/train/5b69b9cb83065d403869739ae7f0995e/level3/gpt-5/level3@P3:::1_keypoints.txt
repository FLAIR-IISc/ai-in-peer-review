- Studies low-rank matrix reconstruction with factor-structured priors and develops an AMP algorithm unifying Bayesian marginal (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference.  
- Introduces a β-parameterized posterior, a general-rank AMP with Onsager corrections, and proximal-like f, G updates to compute posterior means, marginal MAPs, or MAP solutions.  
- Reformulates K-means as a low-rank factorization with one-hot V and derives AMP-KM (bias-corrected assignment) and AMP-MA for maximum-accuracy clustering.  
- Situates work relative to AMP for sparse linear models, generalized AMP, and prior bilinear AMP (IterFac, BiG-AMP), noting the K-means AMP specialization appears novel.  

Strengths:
- Technical: Clear, general AMP derivation that unifies marginal and MAP inference and reduces to VB-like updates when Onsager terms are removed.  
- Novelty: AMP applied directly to the K-means objective with principled assignment corrections is new and interesting.  
- Practicality: Algorithm is O(mN) per iteration; f and G adapt to priors (often closed-form for discrete priors or convex-proximal for MAP).  
- Empirical: Solid comparisons to K-means/K-means++; reports both Frobenius residuals and clustering accuracy; AMP-MA and AMP-KM show strong performance.  
- Interpretability: Clean connection between AMP-MAP and K-means and convincing intuition for the assignment bias-corrections.  

Weaknesses:
- Theory: Convergence guarantees are weak; state evolution is mentioned but not developed and no general convergence/global optimality proof is provided.  
- Baselines: Experimental comparisons omit spectral clustering, GMM/EM, NMF-based clustering, SDP/local-search methods, and minibatch K-means; wall-clock times are not reported.  
- Robustness: AMP derivation relies on dense i.i.d. Gaussian models and large-system limits; off-model behavior (non-Gaussian noise, heavy tails, rank misspecification, correlated factors) is not systematically evaluated.  
- Practical guidance: Limited discussion of damping, step-sizes, β-scheduling/annealing, and sensitivity to initialization and choice of priors.  

Quality:
- Derivation appears technically sound with correct Onsager terms, but main claims depend on empirical validation; theoretical guarantees and state-evolution analysis are deferred.  

Clarity:
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized; algorithm equations and β-unification are explained clearly.  
- Suggested improvements: add pseudocode with damping options, move key derivation/state-evolution summary into main text, and discuss computational cost of f/G for common priors.  

Originality:
- The general-rank AMP handling both posterior means and MAP for flexible factor priors and its direct K-means specialization are novel despite classical factorization–clustering links.  

Significance:
- Approach could impact scalable clustering and Bayesian low-rank modeling if stabilized and adopted, but lack of convergence theory tempers immediate impact.  

Specific suggestions:
- Theory: provide at least an outline of state evolution for β=1, conditions making Onsager corrections PSD, and discussion of damping/stepsize and convergence diagnostics; report failure modes and mitigations.  
- Baselines: add spectral clustering, GMM/EM, NMF-clustering, local-search/k-means++ with many restarts and minibatch variants; include wall-clock time and memory.  
- Robustness: test sensitivity to r̂ ≠ r, non-Gaussian/heavy-tailed noise, correlated factors, varying N/m, mis-specified priors, and ablate assignment correction terms.  
- References: cite theoretical K-means literature (k-means++, PTAS/local-search, smoothed analysis, stability results) and clarify distinctions from BiG-AMP and earlier low-rank AMP.  

Questions for the authors:
- Does empirical state-evolution track hold for β=1 and can you report MSE trajectories vs. predictions?  
- What damping or annealing schedule do you recommend to avoid oscillations in AMP-KM?  
- How sensitive are results to the τ estimation heuristic and to initialization of V?  
- Can f/G be computed in closed form for common continuous priors (Gaussian, Laplacian, spike-and-slab) and what are practical runtimes?  

Pros:
- Novel AMP framework for low-rank reconstruction with flexible priors and a clean β-unification of marginal and MAP inference.  
- Effective and elegant AMP specialization to K-means with principled assignment corrections.  
- Strong empirical results on synthetic and real data.  

Cons:
- No strong theoretical convergence guarantees and state-evolution analysis omitted.  
- Limited empirical baselines, no runtime comparisons, and robustness not thoroughly evaluated.  

Overall assessment:
- A solid, promising AMP-based contribution that advances low-rank Bayesian inference and provides a novel AMP algorithm for K-means-style clustering; recommend acceptance contingent on strengthening theoretical discussion (or empirical state-evolution validation), expanding experiments, and adding relevant references.
