- Proposes a novel approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction from noisy observations.  
- Connects low-rank matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering as an application domain.  
- AMP derivation from belief propagation yields low computational cost and a form similar to variational Bayesian matrix factorization.  
- Empirical evaluation compares against k-means and k-means++, showing the proposed method is faster and more efficient.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, reporting Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering quality.  
- Presentation is clear and well organized, making the algorithm easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough discussion of algorithm properties, including computational cost and some convergence analysis.  
- Main weakness: lacks strong theoretical convergence guarantees despite some theoretical propositions.  
- Original contribution is significant, though applying low-rank reconstruction to clustering is not entirely new and builds on existing work.  
- Paper is practically significant, demonstrating efficiency on real data and offering a useful theoretical/pragmatic approach.  
- Recommendation: accept, but encourage stronger theoretical convergence results and more rigorous optimality analysis.  
- Suggestion: cite more theoretical computer science work providing provable guarantees for K-means and expand convergence details.
