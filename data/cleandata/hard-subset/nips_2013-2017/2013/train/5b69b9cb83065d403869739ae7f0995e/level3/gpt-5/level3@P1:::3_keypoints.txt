- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a β-parameter family interpolating posterior-mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference.  
- Specializes MAP case to clustering by enforcing a one-hot prior on V, yielding an AMP variant of K-means with Onsager corrections (AMP-KM).  
- Empirically, AMP-KM reduces K-means loss vs Lloyd/K-means++ on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and ORL; AMP-MA (β=1) outperforms a VB matrix factorization baseline.  
- Builds on prior AMP work for compressed sensing and bilinear models and connects to matrix factorization clustering and K-means literature.  
- Claimed novelties: general-rank AMP bridging marginalization and MAP (beyond prior posterior-mean work) and direct one-hot enforcement in V within AMP.  
- Strength: clear exposition and well-motivated link between low-rank reconstruction and clustering.  
- Strength: practical algorithmic contributions with O(mN) per iteration, simple steps, and explicit K-means specialization (Algorithm 3).  
- Strength: β-trick offers a unifying view of posterior mean vs MAP and Onsager corrections are extended to clustering.  
- Strength: empirical gains and fast convergence in experiments; AMP-MA often matches AMP-KM in speed when accuracies are similar.  
- Weakness: derivations are sketchy and several non-trivial approximations lack detailed justification or proofs.  
- Q1: Need justification for claiming a term is O(1/√m) and clear scaling assumptions for A, U, V, noise, and the role of β in scaling arguments.  
- Q2: Asymptotic/state-evolution arguments require explicit assumptions (growth rates, randomness/independence, bounded moments) and a more rigorous derivation or roadmap referencing appropriate theorems.  
- Q3: The relation m τw^2 = E[Ail^2] is incorrect/confusing; the noise and observed-data moments need distinction and the τ estimator requires bias/variance analysis.  
- Q4: The β→∞ MAP justification is nontrivial—interchanging limits with approximate Bethe/AMP fixed points and KL/Betha minimization needs justification or conditions for validity.  
- Q5: Proposition 1 proof is too brief; derive the penalized update explicitly, show how the Onsager term produces the negative quadratic penalty, and discuss PSD conditions for the summed matrices.  
- Convergence/stability: observed oscillations and ad-hoc stopping need discussion of damping, step-size, divergence diagnostics, and whether theory predicts convergence domains.  
- Sensitivity: need practical guidance and experiments on robustness to mis-specified rank r̂ and prior mismatch.  
- Originality concern: update structure resembles Expectation Propagation for matrix factorization; authors should position relative to EP, bilinear GAMP, IterFac, and prior AMP work and clarify what's genuinely new.  
- Empirical evaluation limited: comparisons only to Lloyd/K-means++ and VBMF are insufficient—should add GMM-EM, spectral clustering, NMF variants, modern K-means methods, and report wall-clock time and per-iteration costs.  
- Experiments should include sensitivity analyses (initialization, r̂, τ estimation) and ablations (with/without Onsager) to attribute improvements to AMP corrections.  
- Clarity suggestions: unify notation and scaling, consistently carry β through formulas, move key derivation steps into main text, and provide a proof sketch for Proposition 1.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical, simple AMP algorithm with promising gains; unifying β-framework; direct one-hot constraint treatment within AMP.  
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete/tenuous derivations, unclear novelty vs EP/biG-AMP, and limited empirical breadth—paper currently falls short on rigor and evaluation.  
- Overall verdict: promising ideas and potentially useful algorithms, but borderline reject until theoretical justifications (Q1–Q5), novelty clarification, and broader empirical validation are addressed.
