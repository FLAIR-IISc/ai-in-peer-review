- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and extends AMP to matrix factorization.  
- Core contribution: Algorithm 2 unifies marginalization and MAP estimation via a temperature-like parameter β.  
- Applies the AMP framework to K-means by reformulating clustering as constrained low-rank matrix reconstruction.  
- Empirical claim: consistent improvements over Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF in clustering accuracy and K-means loss, especially in high dimensions.  
- The algorithm has a clear derivation from belief propagation and a justified per-iteration complexity of O(mN).  
- Connection to the ICM framework (Proposition 1) provides useful theoretical grounding.  
- The clustering formulation directly handles discrete cluster-assignment constraints, which is practically appealing.  
- Highlights Bayesian flexibility to incorporate structural priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity).  
- Novelty concerns: clustering-as-reconstruction is not new and key prior works (Samaria & Harter 1994; Xu et al. 2003) are omitted.  
- Fails to clearly distinguish its AMP extension from Matsushita & Tanaka (2012) and omits citations to EP/BP literature (e.g., Yedidia et al. 2005).  
- Notational clutter and inconsistencies (β subscripts, hats/tildes, time indices) reduce clarity.  
- Key equations lack intuitive explanation (e.g., why noise variance scales with m, derivation of f and G); symmetry and β limits are not motivated.  
- Critical derivations are relegated to unclear supplementary material rather than the main text.  
- Asymptotic approximations (m,N → ∞) central to AMP are not empirically validated, with no small-scale ablation.  
- Evaluation is limited to clustering tasks and omits standard low-rank reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix, MovieLens), limiting generality claims.  
- Baseline comparison unclear: paper does not justify whether VBMF-MA uses the same prior as AMP-MA, weakening significance of reported gains.  
- Overstated significance: claims of being “first” for clustering via low-rank reconstruction ignore prior work (e.g., Ding et al., 2010) and lack reconstruction-error analysis.  
- Pro/con summary: pro — efficient, theoretically grounded, empirical clustering gains, Bayesian flexibility; con — overstated novelty, poor citation, inadequate evaluation, unclear notation, unvalidated asymptotics.  
- Recommendation: technically sound but not NIPS-ready; authors should add missing citations, run standard low-rank experiments, simplify and clarify notation (including role of m), and move key derivations into the main text.
