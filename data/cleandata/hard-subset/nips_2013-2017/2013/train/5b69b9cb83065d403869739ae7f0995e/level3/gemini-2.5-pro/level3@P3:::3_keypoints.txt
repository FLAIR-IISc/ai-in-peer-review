- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization, specialized to clustering with one-hot indicator factors.  
- Framework is flexible, allowing various priors to encode structure like sparsity.  
- Derivation is via approximations to belief propagation on the factor graph.  
- Empirical comparison to k-means++ shows better K-means loss and clustering accuracy on synthetic and real data.  

- Paper is well-structured and clearly written; main idea is easy to follow.  
- Experiments are promising and suggest improved ability to escape poor local minima versus Lloyd’s algorithm and k-means++.  
- Connection between AMP updates and a penalty discouraging staying in the current configuration gives useful intuition.  

- Major lack of mathematical rigor: derivations are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps in both paper and appendix.  
- Q1: Crucial approximation (second term of Eq. (11) is O(1/√m) in Appx. line 139) is asserted without justification; inverse-temperature β omitted in Section 2 derivation.  
- Q2: Asymptotic analysis assumes m,n grow at same order but this is unstated and key results (e.g., Eq. (18b)) lack rigorous derivation.  
- Q3: Replacing m τ_w^2 with E[A_{il}^2] is unjustified and confuses observed data with noise-model expectation.  
- Q4: Claim that finite-β algorithm converges to MAP as β→∞ is unproven; exchanging limit and KL minimization requires formal argument.  
- Q5: Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief; derivation of Eq. (16) from AMP updates is not shown.  

- Strong suspicion that the algorithm is not novel and essentially re-derives Expectation-Propagation (EP); message updates resemble EP moment-matching.  
- Authors fail to cite or discuss related EP work (e.g., Stern et al. 2009) and connections to clutter/outlier EP formulations (Minka 2001).  

- Significance is limited by sketchy theory and narrow experiments; comparison only to k-means++ is insufficient.  
- Needs broader empirical baselines (e.g., spectral clustering, GMM/EM) to establish practical value.  

- Pros: clear writing, promising empirical performance vs a strong k-means baseline, and an interesting AMP application to clustering.  
- Cons: technical unsoundness from sketchy derivations, questionable originality vis-à-vis EP, and too-limited experimental evaluation.  

- Conclusion: work is interesting but requires rigorous theoretical proofs and proper contextualization with EP literature and broader experiments to be a solid contribution.
