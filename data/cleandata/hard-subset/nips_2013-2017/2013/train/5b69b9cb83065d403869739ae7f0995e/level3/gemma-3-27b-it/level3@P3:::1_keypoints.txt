- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering via a constrained low-rank formulation.  
- Extends prior AMP work to MAP estimation and structural constraints, representing a novel contribution.  
- Empirically outperforms Lloyd’s k-means and k-means++ in speed and sometimes in clustering quality.  
- Evaluates performance using both Frobenius norm residuals and clustering accuracy for a well-rounded assessment.  
- Builds on established Bayesian and AMP literature, situating the work within relevant prior research.  
- Framing clustering as constrained low-rank reconstruction is valuable, though not entirely novel given previous connections.  
- Provides a partial theoretical result (Proposition 1) linking AMP fixed points to global cost minima under certain conditions.  
- Algorithm is clearly presented and the paper is generally well-written.  
- Lacks strong theoretical convergence guarantees beyond the partial proposition.  
- Comparisons are limited to k-means variants and should include more recent clustering and Bayesian matrix factorization methods.  
- Performance on real data depends on a heuristic estimate of noise variance τ, reducing robustness.  
- Should cite additional theoretical work on provable guarantees for k-means.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient approach with compelling empirical speed gains and a well-motivated link between low-rank reconstruction and clustering.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical convergence guarantees and limited benchmarking against state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall assessment: solid, practically useful contribution likely worth acceptance if theoretical limitations are addressed in future work.
