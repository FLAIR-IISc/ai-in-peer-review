- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending prior work to allow MAP estimation.  
- Applies the AMP framework to clustering by reformulating clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem.  
- Empirical results show the proposed clustering method outperforms Lloyd’s K‑means (K‑means++) in high-dimensional settings.  
- Paper is well-structured and the core idea of using AMP for reconstruction and clustering is clearly presented.  
- Major lack of mathematical rigor: derivations are sketchy and key steps are omitted, including in the supplement.  
- Missing justification for the O(1/√m) term in Appendix (line 139) and the omission of β in Section 2.  
- Asymptotic analysis (including derivation of equation (19)) assumes m and n grow at the same rate without discussing other growth regimes.  
- Assertion that m τ_w^2 equals E[A_il^2] (line 167) is unjustified; equating observed-data moments with likelihood variance needs detailed explanation.  
- Unclear how message passing converges to the MAP solution for finite β; the limit/minimization exchange for the KL divergence is not justified.  
- Connection between iterative AMP updates and the global MAP optimum is not established.  
- Derivation of Proposition 1 and equation (17) is too brief and needs a more rigorous proof.  
- Significant originality concern: algorithm closely resembles Expectation Propagation (EP) and related prior work (e.g., Stern et al. 2009).  
- Potential relation to EP “clutter” problem (Minka) suggests additional limitations or failure modes that are not addressed.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to comparison with K‑means++; lacks broader comparisons to state‑of‑the‑art clustering methods.  
- Recommendation: reject due to insufficient theoretical rigor, potential overlap with EP, and limited experimental scope; requires substantial revision.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear presentation, promising empirical gains over K‑means++, and good paper structure.
