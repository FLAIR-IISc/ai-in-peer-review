- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction extended to general rank.  
- Frames clustering as a low-rank reconstruction problem with exclusive cluster-assignment constraints.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements over Lloyd’s K-means in numerical experiments.  
- Builds on prior Bayesian matrix factorization work (Monte Carlo, variational Bayes) and AMP literature.  
- Clear and well-structured presentation making the main idea accessible.  
- Claims MAP estimation capability for AMP in this setting.  
- Derivations are sketchy and omit crucial mathematical steps, undermining reproducibility.  
- Specific unexplained issues: unjustified O(1/√m) term, omission of β in Section 2, and terse asymptotic assumptions.  
- Questionable identification of mτ_w^2 with E[A_{il}^2] without proper justification.  
- Unclear justification for convergence of finite-β algorithm to the MAP solution; limit/minimization exchange not proven.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 and derivation of eq. (17) lack sufficient detail for verification.  
- Method appears strongly similar to Expectation-Propagation (EP) for matrix factorization and needs explicit comparison.  
- Suggestion that the algorithm may encounter EP-like “clutter” issues (per Minka) is noted but not explored.  
- Significance limited by narrow empirical comparison (mainly against Lloyd’s K-means / k-means++).  
- Overlap with existing EP techniques and sketchy proofs reduce confidence in originality and theoretical contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel AMP application to clustering, promising empirical gains, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of mathematical rigor, potential EP overlap, limited comparisons, dubious asymptotic claims, and unjustified approximations.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting idea but significant mathematical and comparative shortcomings; recommend rejection unless revisions address rigor, assumptions, and related-work positioning.
