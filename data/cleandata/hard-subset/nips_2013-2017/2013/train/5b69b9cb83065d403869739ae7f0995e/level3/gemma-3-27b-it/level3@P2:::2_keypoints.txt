- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and recasts clustering as a constrained reconstruction task.
- Leverages Bayesian framework to approximate posterior distributions efficiently using AMP.
- Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, showing competitive performance versus Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.
- Builds on substantial prior work in low-rank matrix completion, Bayesian inference, and AMP.
- Correctly notes Bayesian approach benefits for incorporating prior knowledge about matrix structure.
- Using AMP is reasonable due to its efficiency and theoretical guarantees in compressed sensing.
- Acknowledges related variational Bayes and AMP-based methods but discussion of prior work could be deeper.
- Application of low-rank reconstruction to clustering is not novel, though authors address exclusive cluster assignment constraints.
- Technically sound: algorithm is a logical extension of existing AMP techniques with a clear procedural formulation.
- Empirical performance: outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in some settings, especially higher-dimensional data.
- Novelty is incremental: extends AMP to general rank and clustering constraints but not revolutionary.
- Potential significance: algorithm could be valuable for scalable low-rank reconstruction and clustering if validated broadly.
- Evaluation scope limited: lacks comparisons on standard matrix completion benchmarks (e.g., Netflix, image inpainting).
- Novelty limited by missing connections to expectation propagation and other Bayesian methods.
- Reliance on large-m and large-N asymptotic approximations is insufficiently justified for practical settings.
- Clarity and presentation are poor: cluttered notation, hard-to-parse equations, delayed explanations, and unclear noise variance scaling.
- Scholarship issues: insufficient citations and misleading novelty claims; VBMF baseline needs clearer citation/implementation details.
- Key derivations placed in supplementary material, making core methodology harder to follow; should be summarized in the main text.
- Acceptance arguments: improved performance over K-means, sound technical derivation, and concrete demonstration via clustering.
- Rejection arguments: limited evaluation, weak novelty and contextualization, presentation issues, and unjustified asymptotic assumptions.
- Overall assessment: promising algorithm but current manuscript hampered by clarity, limited evaluation, and insufficient literature contextualization; recommend rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
- Recommended revisions: evaluate on standard datasets, clarify math and notation, expand related-work discussion (including EP), justify asymptotics, and improve readability.
