- Introduces a novel AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending AMP to MAP estimation for general-rank problems and structured factors (e.g., sparsity).
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction task, enabling AMP-based clustering.
- Per-iteration computational cost is linear in matrix size, making the algorithm scalable to large datasets.
- Empirical results show the method outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes on normalized K-means loss, clustering accuracy, and convergence speed, especially in high dimensions and on datasets like ORL faces.
- Provides practical guidance on initialization strategies and parameter estimation, improving usability.
- Lacks strong theoretical convergence guarantees; convergence is assumed but not rigorously justified.
- Could strengthen theoretical context by citing and discussing more provably accurate K-means literature.
- Observes occasional oscillations in cluster assignments that may need extra heuristics to resolve.
- Performance shows sensitivity to initialization, with occasional failures such as empty clusters in real-data experiments.
- Recommendation: accept—experimental and practical strengths outweigh theoretical gaps, but authors should address theoretical guarantees and related work in revision.
