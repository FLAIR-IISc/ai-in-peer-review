- Proposes a new BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, evaluated via clustering-as-factorization.  
- Paper is interesting and mathematically followable; algorithm appears promising in experiments.  
- Evaluation uses a non-standard task (clustering-as-factorization), making it hard to judge performance on standard matrix-reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix).  
- Novelty is limited: algorithm seems a straightforward BP application to a problem previously addressed with Variational Message Passing.  
- Asymptotic approximations (N→∞, m→∞) are neither clearly justified nor empirically validated in the main text.  
- Important prior work and connections are missing: matrix factorization for clustering, Expectation Propagation, and BP for continuous distributions are not cited or discussed.  
- The BP nature of the algorithm should be stated earlier (e.g., abstract).  
- Key derivations are relegated to supplementary material; main paper should include or summarize essential steps.  
- Mathematical presentation is hard to parse; specific confusing equations (9a, 9b, 9d, 9e) and unexplained factors need clarification.  
- Notational clutter (excess hats, tildes, subscripts/superscripts, unnecessary time indices) hampers readability; notation should be simplified and conventions stated.  
- Obvious simplifications and connections are missed (e.g., eq.5 = −log eq.2; eq.8 = eq.2^β; symmetry in Algorithm 2) and should be highlighted.  
- Definitions and roles of functions (fβ, Gβ) and parameters (β, m) are unclear or inconsistently notated; clarify near first use and simplify β-dependence.  
- The significance of the m factor in additive noise variance and its effect on row/column symmetry is not explained.  
- Minor notation/style issues: inconsistent use of tildes, capitalization (N vs m), and unclear variable conventions.  
- Baseline implementation (VBMF-MA) lacks a clear citation.  
- Supplementary material has clarity gaps (e.g., product of exponentials, step from eq.14 to 15, relation of approximations to prior literature).  
- English, phrasing, and the title require polishing for clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: algorithm promising, experiments show good performance, and paper organization is generally clear.  
- Arguments against acceptance: non-standard evaluation, limited novelty, unvalidated asymptotic approximations, poor citation/scholarship, and presentation issues.  
- Recommended improvements: evaluate on standard datasets, justify/validate asymptotic approximations, improve citations, simplify notation, clarify function definitions and β usage, explain m’s role, cite VBMF-MA, and polish language and presentation.
