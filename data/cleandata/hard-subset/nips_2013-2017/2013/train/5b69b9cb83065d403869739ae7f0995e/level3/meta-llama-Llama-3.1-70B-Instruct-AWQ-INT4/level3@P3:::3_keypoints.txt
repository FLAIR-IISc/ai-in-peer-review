- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian matrix factorization and applies it to clustering via a low-rank reconstruction with added structure.  
- Paper is well-structured, explains problem and algorithm clearly, and includes numerical experiments.  
- Mathematical derivations are sketchy with many omitted non-trivial steps (even in the appendix).  
- Specific gaps: why the second term of Eq.(11) is O(1/√m) and apparent omission of β in Section 2.  
- Asymptotic analysis (e.g., derivation of Eq.(19)) assumes m and n grow at the same rate and arguments are terse and nonrigorous.  
- The claim m τ_w^2 = E[A_{il}^2] is disputed—A is observed data so this equality needs justification.  
- Unclear how message passing at finite β converges to the MAP solution; exchanging limit and KL minimization is not justified.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and the derivation of Eq.(17) is not explained.  
- The algorithm may essentially be a form of Expectation-Propagation (EP); connections to Stern et al. (WWW 2009) and standard EP updates are noted.  
- Originality is questionable since the method may be a variant of existing algorithms.  
- Significance is limited because experiments only compare to k-means++; broader comparisons to other clustering methods are needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel BP-derived algorithm and supporting numerical experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: sketchy math, weak asymptotic justification, unproven approximations and convergence, brief proofs, unclear novelty, and limited empirical comparison.
