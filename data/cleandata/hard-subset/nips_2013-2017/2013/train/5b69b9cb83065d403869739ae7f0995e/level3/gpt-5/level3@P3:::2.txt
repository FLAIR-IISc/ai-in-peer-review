Summary
This paper derives an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction starting from belief propagation (BP) with asymptotic approximations in the dense limit (m,N → ∞ and r fixed). A temperature-like parameter β unifies marginal inference (β = 1) and MAP (β → ∞), and the authors instantiate the framework to “clustering as factorization” by constraining V to one-hot rows, yielding an AMP variant of K-means with Onsager-like corrections. On synthetic problems and the ORL faces dataset, the method improves K-means loss and accuracy compared to Lloyd’s algorithm, K-means++, and a Variational Bayes Matrix Factorization (VBMF) baseline.

Relation to prior work: The paper builds on AMP for linear models [Donoho et al.; Rangan], and bilinear/low-rank AMP (rank-1 IterFac; low-rank AMP; BiG-AMP). Variational message passing (VMP/VBMF) and ICM are the standard deterministic baselines, and numerous works connect matrix factorization and clustering (e.g., NMF for clustering, convex/semi-NMF, spectral methods, Gaussian mixture/EM). Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to BP/TAP/Bethe free energy are also relevant to the chosen approximations.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The algorithm is plausible and matches known AMP structures, including Onsager corrections and state-evolution claims. However, almost all derivations are deferred to the supplement, and the main text does not clearly justify the large-system approximations or test their validity.
- Empirical support: The clustering instantiation shows encouraging gains over K-means/K-means++ and a VBMF-based marginal method. But the evaluation focuses on a non-standard re-framing (clustering-as-factorization) rather than conventional low-rank reconstruction/completion benchmarks, making it difficult to assess competitiveness on standard tasks (e.g., Netflix/MovieLens, matrix completion with missing entries).
- Carefulness: Some design choices (e.g., noise variance scaling by m, β usage, τ estimation) are insufficiently motivated and not ablated.

Clarity
- Exposition is mathematically dense and hard to parse. Equations (9a–9d) are particularly confusing (e.g., unclear origins of 1/(βmτ) versus 1/(mτ) factors; purpose of the subtraction terms; the role of Sti and Ttj; and when G is required). Please include a concise derivation or at least a roadmap that names the Bethe/free-energy approximation, central-limit arguments, and the Onsager corrections explicitly.
- Notation is cluttered (many hats/tilde/superscripts/subscripts, unnecessary iteration superscripts on global quantities, inconsistent m/N capitalization, and tilde to denote column vectors). Establish conventions early and simplify where possible.
- Functions fβ and Gβ (and their MAP limits f∞, G∞) should be defined and interpreted immediately on first use; make explicit that β is global and avoid carrying β as an index everywhere.
- Missed expository simplifications: point out that eq. 5 is the negative log of eq. 2; eq. 8 is simply p(U,V|A)β; symmetries between U and V in Algorithm 2 and Algorithm 3; interpretation of Λ and G as precision and scaled covariance.
- Supplement clarity: show the product-of-exponentials step explicitly; clarify the transition from eq. 14 to 15; connect the approximations to TAP/Bethe and prior AMP derivations.

Originality
- The BP/Bethe-to-AMP application to low-rank bilinear models is known (IterFac, low-rank AMP, BiG-AMP). The β unification of marginal/MAP is standard in annealed/tempered posteriors. The novelty seems to be (i) a MAP-capable extension for general-rank and (ii) a clean specialization to hard-assignment clustering with principled corrections. Overall, the methodological novelty is incremental, but the clustering instantiation is interesting.

Significance
- If the AMP variant consistently improves over K-means/K-means++ at small additional cost, it could be practically useful. The broader low-rank reconstruction impact requires validation on standard datasets and comparisons to PMF/BPMF, VBMF, and BiG-AMP.

Pros
- Clear problem framing; potentially useful β-unified inference/MAP view.
- Derivation connects to BP/AMP and suggests O(mN) iterations.
- Empirical gains over K-means and VBMF on the tasks shown.
- Proposition 1 is a nice optimality property at convergence.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to prior bilinear AMP and VMP.
- Derivation largely in the supplement; key steps and assumptions are opaque in the main text.
- Asymptotic validity (m,N → ∞, fixed r) not empirically tested; no state evolution.
- Evaluation focuses on a non-standard task; lacks standard matrix reconstruction/completion benchmarks and stronger baselines.
- Scholarship gaps and missing citations (EP; prior clustering-via-factorization/NMF; precise VBMF baseline details).

Detailed suggestions
- Validation of approximations: empirically probe the large-system assumptions by varying (m,N,r), measuring mismatch between AMP fixed points and ground truth (synthetic) and tracking whether “state evolution” predictions hold. Provide at least a summary SE derivation.
- Benchmarks: add standard low-rank reconstruction/completion experiments (e.g., MovieLens/Netflix, Yahoo, synthetic matrix completion), compare with PMF/BPMF [Salakhutdinov & Mnih], VBMF variants, and BiG-AMP [Parker et al.].
- Baselines for clustering: include Gaussian Mixture/EM and spectral clustering/NMF (Xu et al.; Ding et al.), not only K-means.
- Scholarship: cite and discuss EP (Minka; Opper & Winther), expectation-consistent approximations, and the relationship to continuous BP. Be explicit that the algorithm is BP/Bethe-derived in the abstract and introduction.
- Clarify the m factor in noise variance and its effect on row/column symmetry. Explain why Λ includes 1/(βmτ) terms and how the subtraction terms arise (Onsager).
- Notation and readability: reduce hats/tilde; remove unnecessary time indices; define f, G, q̂ precisely at first use with a short intuitive paragraph; ensure consistent use of β as a global parameter.
- Algorithmic details: explain τ estimation in AMP-KM and test sensitivity; report when Σi Si and Σj Tj are positive semidefinite in practice (assumption of Proposition 1).
- VBMF baseline: provide an exact citation and implementation details for “VBMF-MA” (which reference and which priors/hyperparameters?).
- Add diagrams: a factor graph, message flow, and an illustration of Algorithm 2’s symmetry (Fig. 1 in the supplement is helpful; more would aid comprehension).
- Language/style: polish English and tighten the title.

Questions for the authors
- Can you provide a concise main-text derivation explaining the Onsager terms in (9a, 9c) and the origin of the 1/β scaling?
- How sensitive is AMP-KM to τ estimation and initialization compared to K-means++?
- Do your approximations remain accurate for moderate m,N? Any evidence via SE curves?
- Which VBMF formulation did you implement for VBMF-MA? Please cite and specify priors/hyperparameters.

Recommendation
Borderline reject. The algorithm is promising and likely useful, but the paper in its current form lacks sufficient clarity, validation of the asymptotic approximations, and scholarship. With stronger derivational exposition, standard benchmarks, additional baselines, and improved related work, I would be inclined to support acceptance.
