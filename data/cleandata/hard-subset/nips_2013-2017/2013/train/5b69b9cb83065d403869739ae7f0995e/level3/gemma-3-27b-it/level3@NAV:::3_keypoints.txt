- Well-motivated formulation of clustering as constrained low-rank reconstruction and clear presentation of the core idea.  
- Paper is well-structured and makes a clear connection between Bayesian inference, AMP, and clustering.  
- Proposed AMP algorithm shows empirical improvement over k-means++ in some experiments.  
- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit crucial steps, even in the appendix.  
- Missing justification for the O(1/√m) term and unclear omission of β (Q1).  
- Asymptotic analysis assumptions (m and n growing at same rate) are tersely stated and lack justification, undermining equation (19) (Q2).  
- Claim equating mτ_w^2 to E[A_{il}^2] is unsupported given A is observed data while τ_w relates to likelihood variance (Q3).  
- Convergence of AMP to the MAP solution is not convincingly proved; exchanging limit and minimization of KL is not generally valid (Q4).  
- Proof of Proposition 1 is insufficient; derivation of equation (17) is opaque (Q5).  
- Strong resemblance to Expectation-Propagation (EP) and prior matrix-factorization EP work (e.g., Stern et al. WWW 2009); insufficient differentiation from prior EP approaches.  
- Insightful connection to EP “clutter” problem (Minka), suggesting overlap with existing literature.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only compared to k-means++); broader benchmarking against state-of-the-art clustering methods is needed.  
- Lack of detailed theoretical analysis and potential overlap with prior work reduce confidence in novelty and technical contribution.  
- Overall assessment: reject; interesting idea but fails NIPS standards without major revision to address theoretical rigor, novelty, and experiments.
