- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured factor priors, extending AMP to matrix factorization and MAP estimation, and applies it to K-means via a constrained low-rank reformulation.  
- Key novelty: AMP-based approach for both marginalization and MAP estimation, and directly enforcing exact cluster assignment constraints within the low-rank framework.  
- Empirical claim: AMP-KM outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ on synthetic and ORL Faces datasets.  
- Experiments are thorough and well-designed, evaluating both K-means loss and actual clustering accuracy (using the Hungarian algorithm).  
- AMP-MA shows superior accuracy and faster convergence compared to variational Bayes (VBMF-MA).  
- Algorithm is computationally efficient (O(mN) per iteration) and recovers ICM as a special case when β→∞.  
- Main weakness: lacks strong theoretical guarantees for convergence or global optimality; Proposition 1 only links AMP fixed points to ICM/local optima.  
- Omits state evolution analysis standard in AMP literature, leaving theoretical performance and convergence predictions unaddressed.  
- Empirical convergence speed is reported but without theoretical justification, so practical efficiency claims remain partly empirical.  
- Related-work discussion could better differentiate this AMP extension from prior matrix-reconstruction AMP works ([13,14]) and contextualize clustering links ([10,11]).  
- Pros for acceptance: novel MAP extension of AMP, first enforcement of exact cluster constraints in low-rank framework, strong empirical results, scalable method.  
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete theoretical analysis, clustering connection not entirely novel, needs clearer positioning against related work.  
- Overall assessment: solid, practically impactful technical contribution with manageable theoretical gaps; recommended for acceptance.
