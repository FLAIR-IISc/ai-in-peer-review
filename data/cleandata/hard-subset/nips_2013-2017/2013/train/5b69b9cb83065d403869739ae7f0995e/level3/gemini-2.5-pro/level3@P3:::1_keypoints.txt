- Introduces an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on factors.  
- Specializes the framework to K-means by constraining rows of V to one-hot vectors, producing a novel AMP-KM update rule.  
- AMP-derived K-means update includes a non-trivial "memory" term that corrects for bias from previous assignments.  
- Relates to prior variational and AMP-based work (e.g., dictionary learning) and leverages the known link between low-rank factorization and clustering.  
- Strong experimental evaluation on synthetic data and ORL faces, comparing against Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++.  
- Measures performance using both normalized K-means loss and external clustering accuracy.  
- Empirical results: AMP-KM yields lower K-means loss and AMP-MA achieves higher clustering accuracy; algorithms converge in few iterations.  
- Major weakness: No theoretical convergence guarantees; Algorithm 2’s convergence is assumed but not proven and state-evolution analysis is omitted.  
- Proposition 1 characterizes fixed points (they coincide with ICM fixed points) but does not ensure the algorithm reaches them.  
- Paper could better position itself relative to theoretical CS work on K-means (spectral methods, provable approximation algorithms, K-means++ guarantees).  
- Suggest expanding intuition and explanation for the corrective terms in the K-means update rule.  
- Overall: Novel, fast, and empirically effective contribution with practical benefits, but weakened by lack of theoretical analysis.
