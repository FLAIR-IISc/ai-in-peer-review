- Proposes a novel AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction derived from belief propagation.  
- Formulates the problem in a Bayesian framework to incorporate structural priors.  
- Algorithm claimed to be computationally inexpensive and capable of handling non-convex problems.  
- Applies the AMP algorithm to clustering and reports better performance than K-means.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via numerical experiments on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Provides detailed comparisons with existing methods like variational Bayes matrix factorization and Lloyd’s K-means.  
- Presentation of main ideas is generally clear and concise.  
- Evaluation is thorough across multiple experiments.  
- Mathematical derivations (especially in supplementary material) are confusing and use unclear notation.  
- Paper lacks sufficient background/context on AMP and its applications.  
- Needs more discussion of limitations, potential extensions, and connections to prior work.  
- Evaluation misses standard benchmark datasets (e.g., Netflix) and broader competitiveness tests.  
- Choice of evaluation metrics (normalized K-means loss) is insufficiently justified.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially useful work; recommend accept with minor revisions (rating 7/10).
