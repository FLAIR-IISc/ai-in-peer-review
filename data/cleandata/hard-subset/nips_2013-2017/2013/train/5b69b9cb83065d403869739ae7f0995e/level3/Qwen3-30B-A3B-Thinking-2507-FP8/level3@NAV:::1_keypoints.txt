- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured factor constraints, extending AMP beyond vector problems.  
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction task and applies the AMP-based approach to clustering.  
- Empirically outperforms K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes on synthetic and real-world clustering benchmarks.  
- Strong experimental design with comprehensive metrics (Frobenius norm residuals and clustering accuracy) bolstering empirical claims.  
- Algorithm is computationally efficient (O(mN) per iteration) and shows faster convergence than existing methods.  
- Original application of AMP to constrained matrix factorization, though link between low-rank factorization and K-means is known.  
- Situates work well within Bayesian matrix factorization and AMP literature.  
- Main weakness: lacks strong theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed AMP algorithm.  
- Proposition 1 shows AMP fixed points coincide with ICM fixed points but does not prove convergence or global optimality.  
- Reviewer recommends citing theoretical CS work on provable K-means guarantees to better contextualize contribution.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with compelling experimental results; recommend acceptance but encourage strengthening theoretical foundations in future work.
