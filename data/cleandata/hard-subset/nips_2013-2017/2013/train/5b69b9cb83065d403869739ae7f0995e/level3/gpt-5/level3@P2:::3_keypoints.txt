- Paper derives an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a β-tempered posterior to unify marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), and specializes to a one-hot prior yielding an AMP-corrected K-means.  
- Work connects to extensive AMP/bilinear literature (IterFac, low-rank AMP, bilinear GAMP) and to EP/variational matrix factorization; relation to EP/GAMP needs clarification.  

- Major derivations are terse and omit nontrivial steps; several scaling and normalization justifications are missing (Q1).  
- The asymptotic limit and distributional assumptions for AMP/state-evolution (e.g., N/m→δ, r fixed) are not stated nor are concentration arguments provided (Q2).  
- The noise variance estimation and use of E[A_il^2] to infer mτ is unjustified without accounting for signal; consistency via residuals/state evolution should be explained (Q3).  
- The β→∞ MAP limit is not rigorously justified; exchanging limit and minimization needs Γ/epi-convergence or a direct update-level argument (Q4).  
- Proposition 1’s proof is too brief; derivation of (17), identification of Onsager terms, and PSD assumptions for aggregate matrices must be detailed (Q5).  

- Convergence/stability details are missing: report use of damping, convergence diagnostics, and address observed assignment oscillations.  
- Experiments: need fair comparisons by matching seeding (random vs K-means++) for all methods, report multiple seeds with error bars, and test sensitivity to rank mis-specification.  

- Clarity improvements: define matrix dimensions, collect f/G and β→∞ limits, keep β explicit, and provide pseudocode with stopping criteria and damping.  
- Originality: β-tempering is a neat unifyer and one-hot enforcement is interesting, but similarities to EP/Bethe and bilinear GAMP reduce novelty unless distinctions are made.  
- Significance: bias-corrected assignments and fast per-iteration cost are promising, but narrow empirical baselines and sketchy theory limit impact.  

- Pros: clear formulation, unified β approach, intuitive clustering specialization, low per-iteration complexity, empirical gains on synthetic and ORL.  
- Cons: terse/omitted derivations, unclear relation to EP/GAMP, limited experimental baseline coverage, no convergence guarantees or damping details.  

- Recommendation: borderline reject; encourage revision with stronger derivations (Q1–Q5), clarified EP/GAMP positioning, and broader experiments.  
- Constructive suggestions: expand supplement with full proofs and assumptions, clarify EP connection and Onsager effects (ablation), broaden clustering baselines and sensitivity analyses, and document damping and a state-evolution sketch.
