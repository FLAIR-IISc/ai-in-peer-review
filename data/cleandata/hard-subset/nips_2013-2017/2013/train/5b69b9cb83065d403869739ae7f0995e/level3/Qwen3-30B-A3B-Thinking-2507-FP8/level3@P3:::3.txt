

# Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, which is then specialized to clustering problems. The authors frame clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem with additional structural constraints, and derive an AMP-based algorithm for this purpose. The approach is compared experimentally to k-means++ and shows promising results on both synthetic and real data.

The paper builds on previous work in AMP algorithms for compressed sensing [6, 7, 8, 9] and matrix factorization [3-5]. It extends the AMP framework to handle general low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural priors, and applies it to clustering as a special case of this framework. The connection to existing work is generally well-motivated, though the originality claims could be strengthened by more precise differentiation from Expectation-Propagation (EP) approaches.

## Strengths
The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with the main ideas easily graspable. The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the proposed AMP algorithm's superiority over k-means++ in terms of both clustering accuracy and convergence speed, particularly for high-dimensional data. The algorithmic presentation is clear, and the connection between matrix reconstruction and clustering is well-explained.

## Weaknesses

### Mathematical Rigor
The paper suffers from significant mathematical shortcomings that undermine confidence in the theoretical contributions. The derivations are extremely sketchy, omitting numerous non-trivial steps even in the appendix. Specifically:

Q1: The reviewer questions why the second term in equation (11) is O(1/√m) (Appendix line 139), noting that β appears to be omitted in Section 2. This omission is critical to the asymptotic analysis.

Q2: The asymptotic analysis assumes m and n grow at the same order, but the arguments are terse and lack rigorous justification. The derivation of equation (19) appears particularly unsubstantiated.

Q3: The claim that mτ_w^2 = E[A_{il}^2] (line 167) is questionable. A is observed data, while mτ_w^2 would only represent the likelihood variance. The approximation requires proper justification.

Q4: The paper claims that the message passing algorithm for finite β converges to the MAP solution, but fails to justify why exchanging the limit and minimization of KL divergence is valid.

Q5: The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief, and it's unclear how equation (17) was derived.

### Originality Concerns
The proposed algorithm appears to be essentially an Expectation-Propagation (EP) application to matrix factorization. The EP updates in the appendix closely resemble standard EP updates, and the paper should acknowledge Stern et al. (WWW 2009) and the broader EP literature. The reviewer suggests the algorithm may be equivalent to EP applied to the "clutter" problem as described by Minka, though this is presented as a suggestion rather than a criticism.

### Significance Concerns
Focusing solely on comparison with k-means++ is insufficient for a clustering paper. The clustering literature contains many more relevant baselines (e.g., spectral clustering, hierarchical clustering, other matrix factorization approaches). The sketchy proofs and potential overlap with prior EP work reduce confidence in both the technical and practical contributions.

## Overall Assessment

**Quality:** The paper has significant theoretical weaknesses that undermine its technical contribution. The mathematical derivations are too sketchy to be considered rigorous, and the asymptotic analysis lacks proper justification. While the experimental results are promising, they cannot compensate for the theoretical shortcomings.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The main ideas and algorithmic presentation are accessible, though the mathematical details are insufficiently developed.

**Originality:** The core algorithm appears to be a straightforward application of EP to matrix factorization, with insufficient differentiation from prior work. The connection to EP (Stern et al., Minka) should be explicitly addressed.

**Significance:** The significance is limited by the narrow comparison (only to k-means++) and the questionable novelty. While the experimental results are positive, the theoretical foundation is too weak to support the claims of being a significant contribution to the field.

## Recommendation

This paper has promising experimental results but suffers from significant theoretical shortcomings. The mathematical derivations are too sketchy to be considered rigorous, and the algorithm appears to be a standard EP application rather than a novel contribution. The paper would need substantial revision to address these concerns, including a more rigorous theoretical foundation, better differentiation from prior EP work, and a more comprehensive experimental comparison with relevant clustering methods.

**Recommendation:** Reject. While the experimental results are interesting, the theoretical weaknesses and potential lack of novelty make this paper insufficient for publication at NIPS. The authors should substantially revise the paper to provide rigorous mathematical justification and better differentiate their approach from existing EP-based methods before resubmission.
