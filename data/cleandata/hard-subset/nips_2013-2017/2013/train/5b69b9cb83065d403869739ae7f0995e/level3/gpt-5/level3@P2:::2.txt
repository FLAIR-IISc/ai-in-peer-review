Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with the ability to encode additional structure (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity) via priors on the factors U and V. It derives an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm from belief propagation (BP) on a dense bilinear factor graph, including Onsager-type corrections, and introduces a “temperature” parameter β so a single algorithm interpolates between marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). A fixed-point optimality proposition shows that, under mild conditions, the AMP fixed points are also fixed points (and coordinate-wise minima) of the corresponding ICM/VB objective, suggesting favorable fixed-point geometry. As an application, the authors recast K-means clustering as a structured low-rank factorization with one-hot V and derive an AMP variant that directly enforces the “exactly one cluster per data point” constraint. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and the ORL faces dataset show improvements over Lloyd’s K-means (and K-means++ seeding), and over a variational Bayes matrix factorization (VBMF) baseline on a maximum-accuracy criterion.

The work builds on AMP/GAMP for linear inverse problems (Donoho, Maleki, Montanari ’09; Rangan ’11) and the rank-one bilinear AMP (IterFac; Rangan & Fletcher ’12). It is close in spirit to later bilinear GAMP and dictionary learning/blind calibration via AMP (Parker et al. ’13; Krzakala et al. ’13). The variational Bayes line (Lim & Teh ’07; Nakajima & Sugiyama ’11) is a standard baseline. The connection between clustering and (constrained) matrix factorization is well-known (e.g., NMF-based clustering (Xu et al. ’03; Ding et al. ’10)); the paper’s novelty is to enforce one-hot assignments within an AMP framework. The use of a tempered posterior p(U,V|A)β relates to Bethe free-energy minimization and to expectation propagation (EP), which is not discussed.

Strengths
- A general AMP framework for bilinear low-rank reconstruction with arbitrary separable priors, covering both posterior means and MAP via β.
- Clear relation to VB/ICM: removing Onsager terms recovers VB updates; fixed-point result provides useful intuition.
- Practical and timely application to clustering with a principled correction to the K-means assignment step that accounts for current assignments and cluster sizes.
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over Lloyd’s K-means and competitive accuracy/iterations versus VBMF on synthetic data; gains also on ORL faces.
- Per-iteration cost O(mN) and algorithmic symmetry in U and V are attractive.

Weaknesses
- Novelty is incremental: the algorithm is a fairly direct AMP/BP extension of existing bilinear AMP ideas; the β→∞ “MAP-AMP” is natural but not deeply analyzed.
- Validation is narrow. The evaluation focuses on “clustering-as-factorization,” not on standard low-rank reconstruction/completion benchmarks (e.g., MovieLens/Netflix, image inpainting/denoising, missing data), making it hard to assess competitiveness on canonical tasks.
- The asymptotic justification (m,N→∞ with fixed r) and state evolution claims are not substantiated in the main text; no empirical scaling studies or SE predictions are shown.
- Exposition and notation are difficult. Key derivations are relegated to the supplement; equations around (9a–9d) have unexplained factors/signs; β-dependence and the roles of f and G are not introduced where first used; excessive hats/tildes/time indices obscure the main ideas.
- Scholarship gaps: EP and its relation to BP on continuous variables are not cited; the matrix-factorization view of clustering is long-standing and should be more carefully situated. The VBMF-MA baseline needs a precise citation/implementation details.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The updates resemble standard AMP with Onsager corrections, and the K-means specialization is plausible. Proposition 1 is a nice property. However, convergence behavior is not deeply studied; oscillations are observed; no damping or step control is discussed. The scaling choice for the Gaussian noise variance (factor m) is motivated but not clearly explained in terms of preserving row/column symmetry and SE limits.
- Empirics: Positive but limited in scope. No noise/mismatch sensitivity, no ablations (e.g., removing Onsager terms to recover VB), and no runtime/memory comparisons.

Clarity
- The paper is hard to parse. Suggestions:
  - State upfront that the algorithm is derived via BP/Bethe and relate to EP/GAMP.
  - Simplify notation; avoid unnecessary hats/tildes; collect time indices; define f(b,Λ; p̂) and G at first use and clarify that G is β-scaled covariance.
  - Explain that (5) is the negative log of (2) and that (8) is a tempered posterior pβ.
  - Justify the m factor in the noise variance and discuss implications for symmetry.
  - Summarize key derivation steps in the main text; relegate only technical details to the supplement; add a diagram of the factor graph.

Originality
- Moderate. The application of AMP to general bilinear low-rank factorization with a β path to MAP is incremental over prior AMP/GAMP work; the clustering instantiation with exact one-hot constraints is a nice twist but conceptually close to existing factorization-based clustering.

Significance
- Potentially useful for practitioners who need fast structured factorization and for clustering at high dimension. Broader impact on low-rank reconstruction would require validation on standard tasks and more thorough analysis (SE, convergence, robustness).

Arguments for acceptance
- Practical algorithm with clear performance gains over K-means/K-means++ on synthetic and ORL data.
- Unifies marginal/MAP estimation in a single AMP framework; provides insights into the relation with VB/ICM.
- Low per-iteration complexity; proposition on fixed points is useful.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to existing AMP/GAMP literature; missing discussion of closely related work (EP, bilinear GAMP).
- Evaluation does not cover standard low-rank reconstruction/completion benchmarks; little analysis of asymptotics or convergence.
- Exposition and notation need substantial polishing; key derivations are not in the main text.

Requested clarifications/improvements
- Add comparisons on standard matrix reconstruction/completion datasets (e.g., MovieLens/Netflix, image inpainting with missing entries), including posterior mean metrics when applicable.
- Provide a brief state evolution derivation or at least empirical SE validation; include scaling experiments in m,N.
- Discuss convergence (damping, step-size), sensitivity to prior mismatch, and computational cost of f/G for non-Gaussian/discrete priors.
- Cite and contrast with EP (e.g., Minka 2001) and bilinear GAMP; better situate clustering within NMF/spectral clustering literature.
- Clarify equations (9a–9d), β notation, and the role of the m factor; simplify notation as suggested.
- Specify the exact VBMF variant used and implementation details.

Recommendation: Borderline reject. The algorithm is promising and the clustering application is interesting, but the current version lacks sufficient clarity, validation of the asymptotic approximations, and positioning relative to closely related work. Strengthening the empirical evaluation and exposition would make this a solid contribution.

Reviewer confidence: Medium.
