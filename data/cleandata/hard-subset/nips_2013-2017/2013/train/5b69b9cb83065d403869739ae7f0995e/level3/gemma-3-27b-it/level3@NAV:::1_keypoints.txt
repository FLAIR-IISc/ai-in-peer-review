- Novel AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction enabling MAP estimation, offering a computationally efficient alternative to Bayesian methods.  
- Interesting attempt to directly incorporate hard-clustering constraints into the reconstruction framework, noting the known PCA–K-means connection.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the problem setting, prior work, and proposed algorithm.  
- Strong empirical evaluation showing the method outperforms K-means and K-means++ on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Uses both Frobenius reconstruction error and clustering-quality metrics; ablation study on the ORL face database is particularly convincing.  
- Major weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed AMP algorithm; state-evolution analysis is not provided.  
- Proposition on optimality at fixed points is valuable but does not constitute a full convergence proof.  
- Authors should cite more theoretical computer science literature that provides provable guarantees for K-means and related algorithms.  
- Limited discussion of the assumptions required for the theoretical results to hold.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution with promising experiments; accept conditional on addressing theoretical gaps or more thoroughly discussing limitations.
