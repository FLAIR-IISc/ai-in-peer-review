This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, which is then specialized to clustering. The algorithm is compared experimentally to k-means++. The main idea is to use AMP to efficiently solve the Bayesian inference problem for matrix reconstruction, which can be applied to clustering by reformulating the clustering problem as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem.

The paper has several strengths. First, the algorithm is well-motivated and has a clear connection to the Bayesian framework. Second, the experimental results show that the proposed algorithm outperforms k-means++ in terms of clustering accuracy and K-means loss. Third, the paper is well-structured and easy to follow.

However, there are also several weaknesses. First, the mathematical derivations are very sketchy and omit many non-trivial steps, even in the appendix. This makes it difficult to fully understand the algorithm and its properties. Second, the asymptotic analysis assumes that m and n grow at the same order, but the arguments are terse and lack rigor. Third, the paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the message passing algorithm for finite β converges to the MAP solution. Fourth, the proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and lacks details.

Regarding originality, the reviewer suggests that the algorithm may essentially be Expectation-Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization, which has been explored in prior work. This raises concerns about the novelty of the proposed algorithm.

In terms of significance, the paper's focus on comparison with k-means++ is insufficient given the vast clustering literature. The sketchy proofs and possible overlap with prior EP-based work reduce confidence in the practical and technical contribution of the paper.

To address these concerns, the authors could provide more detailed mathematical derivations, clarify the asymptotic analysis, and provide a more rigorous proof of Proposition 1. Additionally, they could discuss the relationship between their algorithm and prior EP-based work, and provide a more comprehensive comparison with other clustering algorithms.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a new algorithm for matrix factorization and clustering, which has shown promising experimental results.
* The algorithm has a clear connection to the Bayesian framework, which provides a principled approach to matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The mathematical derivations are too sketchy and lack rigor, making it difficult to fully understand the algorithm and its properties.
* The asymptotic analysis is terse and lacks rigor, and the assumption that m and n grow at the same order may not be justified.
* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the message passing algorithm for finite β converges to the MAP solution.
* The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and lacks details.
* The algorithm may not be novel, and the relationship with prior EP-based work needs to be clarified.
* The comparison with other clustering algorithms is insufficient, and the paper's focus on k-means++ is too narrow.
