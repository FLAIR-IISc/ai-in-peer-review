- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix factorization and derives a clustering method by framing clustering as matrix reconstruction, claiming improvements over k-means++ on synthetic and real data.  
- Positions the work within AMP/ compressed-sensing literature and as an alternative to variational Bayes for inference.  
- Main strength: clear exposition — problem is well-motivated, paper is well-structured, and core idea is easy to follow.  
- Experimental results are promising but limited in scope.  
- Major weakness: mathematical rigor is lacking; many non-trivial derivation steps are omitted.  
- Missing justification for the O(1/√m) scaling in equation (11).  
- Asymptotic analysis assuming m and n grow at the same rate is terse and unconvincing.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and does not explain how equation (17) is obtained.  
- Claim about approximating the noise variance (line 167) needs clarification.  
- Claim that the algorithm finds the MAP solution as β → ∞ is unsupported; exchange of limit and minimization is not justified.  
- Originality concerns: the algorithm appears to be a special case of Expectation-Propagation (EP), a connection not discussed.  
- Update equations strongly resemble standard EP updates; prior work (e.g., Stern et al. 2009, Minka) is relevant and not cited/discussed.  
- Significance is weakened by technical gaps and narrow experimental comparisons (only against k-means++), ignoring much of the clustering literature.  
- Recommendation: major revision required to provide rigorous proofs, clarify technical claims, and properly position the method relative to EP and prior work.
