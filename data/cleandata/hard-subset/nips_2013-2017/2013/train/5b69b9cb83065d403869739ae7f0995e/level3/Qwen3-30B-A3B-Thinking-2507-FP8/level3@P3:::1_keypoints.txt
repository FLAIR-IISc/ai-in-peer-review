- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction that incorporates structural priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity) and applies it to K-means clustering.  
- Uses a Bayesian formulation to allow flexible priors on factor matrices beyond low-rankness.  
- Derives an AMP algorithm that is computationally efficient (linear complexity) and faster than variational Bayes.  
- Reformulates clustering by incorporating the constraint that each data point belongs to exactly one cluster, connecting low-rank reconstruction to K-means.  
- Two main contributions: (1) AMP for general-rank matrix reconstruction handling marginalization and MAP; (2) novel application to K-means with explicit cluster-assignment constraints.  
- Builds on prior AMP work (compressed sensing, dictionary learning, matrix reconstruction) but focuses on fixed-rank asymptotics and clustering.  
- Strengths: solid theoretical grounding, linear-time complexity, comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real data, evaluates both Frobenius residuals and clustering accuracy.  
- Empirical results: AMP-based method consistently outperforms K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes in speed and quality, especially in high dimensions.  
- Weakness: lacks strong convergence guarantees for the AMP algorithm; only shows fixed-point relation to ICM without convergence bounds or iteration complexity.  
- Weakness: insufficient citation/discussion of theoretical CS work on K-means provable guarantees (e.g., K-means++, complexity results).  
- Weakness: novelty may be perceived as limited because PCA–K-means connections are well-known; AMP–clustering motivation could be clearer.  
- Originality: AMP approach to matrix reconstruction is novel; incorporating cluster-assignment constraint within low-rank framework is a meaningful extension.  
- Significance: high practical relevance due to improved clustering performance and computational efficiency.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen theoretical convergence discussion and related-theory citations.
