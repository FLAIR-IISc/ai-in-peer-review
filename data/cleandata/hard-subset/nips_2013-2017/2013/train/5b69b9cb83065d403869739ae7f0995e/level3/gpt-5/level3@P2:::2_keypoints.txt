- Summary: Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization with separable priors on U,V and a temperature β interpolating between posterior means (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on AMP/GAMP and bilinear AMP literature; novelty is enforcing one-hot clustering constraints within AMP and introducing β-path; EP and some clustering/matrix-factorization literature not fully cited.

- Strength: General AMP framework for bilinear low-rank reconstruction supporting arbitrary separable priors and both marginal/MAP estimation via β.  
- Strength: Clear relation to VB/ICM—removing Onsager terms recovers VB updates and a fixed-point proposition links AMP and VB/ICM optima.  
- Strength: Practical clustering application that enforces exact one-hot assignments with principled corrections to K-means assignment.  
- Strength: Empirical improvements over Lloyd’s K-means/K-means++ and competitive gains vs VBMF on synthetic and ORL faces.  
- Strength: Per-iteration complexity O(mN) and symmetric treatment of U and V.

- Weakness: Incremental novelty—direct extension of bilinear AMP ideas and MAP-AMP not deeply analyzed.  
- Weakness: Narrow validation focused on clustering-as-factorization; lacks standard low-rank reconstruction/completion benchmarks.  
- Weakness: Asymptotic/state-evolution claims not substantiated—no SE derivation, empirical scaling, or SE predictions included.  
- Weakness: Exposition and notation are confusing; key derivations in supplement and several equations/notations unexplained.  
- Weakness: Scholarship gaps—EP and other related work not cited; clustering-as-factorization literature not fully situated; VBMF baseline details missing.

- Quality (technical): Updates resemble standard AMP and Proposition 1 is useful, but convergence behavior isn't studied and oscillations/damping are not addressed.  
- Quality (empirics): Positive but limited—missing noise/mismatch sensitivity, ablations (e.g., without Onsager), and runtime/memory comparisons.

- Clarity: Paper is hard to parse; needs upfront BP/Bethe/EP framing, notation simplification, clearer definitions of f and G and β-dependence, and explanation of scaling choices (m factor).  
- Clarity: Suggests moving fewer derivations to main text, adding factor-graph diagram, and reducing hats/tildes/time indices.

- Originality: Moderate—useful clustering instantiation (one-hot constraints) but conceptually close to prior AMP/GAMP and factorization-based clustering.

- Significance: Potentially useful for fast structured factorization and clustering, but broader impact requires more benchmarks and theoretical/robustness analysis.

- Arguments for acceptance: Practical algorithm with performance gains over K-means, unifies marginal/MAP in one AMP framework, low per-iteration cost, and a useful fixed-point proposition.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited novelty vs existing AMP work, missing discussion of EP/bilinear GAMP, insufficient evaluation on standard benchmarks, and poor exposition.

- Requested improvement: Add comparisons on standard matrix completion/reconstruction datasets (e.g., MovieLens, image inpainting) and include posterior-mean metrics where relevant.  
- Requested improvement: Provide a state-evolution derivation or empirical SE validation and scaling experiments in m,N.  
- Requested improvement: Discuss convergence strategies (damping/step-size), prior-mismatch sensitivity, and computational cost for non-Gaussian/discrete priors.  
- Requested improvement: Cite and contrast with EP (Minka 2001) and bilinear GAMP; better situate clustering within NMF/spectral clustering literature.  
- Requested improvement: Clarify equations (9a–9d), β notation, role of m factor, simplify notation, and move key derivation steps into main text.  
- Requested improvement: Specify the exact VBMF variant used and provide implementation details for baselines.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject pending stronger empirical validation, clearer exposition, and better positioning relative to related work.

- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
