## Review of “Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing”

This paper presents an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous work to handle more general cases and applying it to the K-means clustering problem. The core idea is to leverage the Bayesian framework with AMP to efficiently reconstruct matrices with structural properties, and demonstrate its effectiveness in clustering high-dimensional data. The approach is novel in extending existing AMP algorithms for matrix reconstruction to allow for MAP estimation, and in directly formulating K-means as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem. 

The paper is well-motivated, connecting to established areas like low-rank matrix completion and K-means clustering. The authors appropriately position their work within the existing literature, referencing relevant papers on Bayesian matrix factorization, variational methods, and AMP algorithms. The experimental evaluation is a significant strength, comparing the proposed method against standard K-means and K-means++ algorithms on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The results consistently show improved performance in terms of both reconstruction error (Frobenius norm) and clustering accuracy, demonstrating a practical benefit. The inclusion of both metrics is commendable, providing a comprehensive assessment.

However, the most significant weakness lies in the lack of strong theoretical convergence guarantees. While the authors acknowledge this, it limits the paper’s impact. The connection to existing theoretical work on K-means is superficial; citing more recent and rigorous results from theoretical computer science regarding K-means convergence rates and approximation guarantees would strengthen the analysis. For example, work on the initialization sensitivity of K-means and the landscape of the loss function could provide valuable context. Furthermore, the discussion of the state evolution, while mentioned, is relegated to the supplementary material, diminishing its visibility. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering.
*   Strong empirical results demonstrating improved performance over existing methods.
*   Clear presentation and connection to relevant prior work.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of rigorous theoretical guarantees regarding convergence.
*   Limited discussion of state evolution analysis.



**Overall:** This is a solid contribution with promising experimental results. While the absence of strong theoretical backing is a concern, the practical benefits demonstrated in the experiments make it a worthwhile addition to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the theoretical shortcomings by including more relevant citations and expanding the discussion of the state evolution analysis.
