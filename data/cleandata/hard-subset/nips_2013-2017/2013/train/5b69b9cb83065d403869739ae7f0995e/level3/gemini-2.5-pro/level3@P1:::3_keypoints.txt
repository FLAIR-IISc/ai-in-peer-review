- Introduces an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction allowing flexible priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity).  
- Extends AMP to handle general MAP estimation (not just posterior means) via a one-parameter (β) extension of the posterior.  
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a constrained low-rank matrix factorization and derives a specialized AMP clustering algorithm.  
- The AMP-based clustering method modifies standard assignment steps with bias-correction terms that have intuitive interpretation (Eq. 18b).  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data show the AMP clustering consistently outperforms Lloyd's K-means and k-means++ in loss and accuracy.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and motivates the low-rank–clustering connection effectively.  
- The β-parameter unification of MAP and marginalization is an elegant theoretical device.  
- Mathematical derivations are too brief and omit non-trivial steps, making correctness hard to verify.  
- Appendix approximations (e.g., claim that a term is O(1/√m)) lack clear justification (Q1).  
- Asymptotic analysis assumes m and N grow similarly, but the terse presentation leaves rigor questionable (Q2).  
- Key approximations replace data-dependent quantities with noise statistics (e.g., replacing A-based moments with τ), which is unjustified because A contains signal (Q3).  
- Claim that finite-β algorithm converges to MAP as β→∞ requires a formal proof for exchanging limits and minimization (Q4).  
- Proof of Proposition 1 and derivation of Eq. (16) are too brief and need detailed steps (Q5).  
- Novelty is unclear: the algorithm resembles Expectation Propagation (EP) and may be an EP re-derivation; relationship to prior EP work is not discussed.  
- Bias-correction terms resemble EP self-contribution corrections; the paper lacks contextual discussion linking to EP literature.  
- Significance is limited by narrow experimental comparisons (only k-means and k-means++); stronger baselines like spectral clustering or GMM/EM are missing.  
- Sketchy proofs and potential overlap with EP reduce confidence in the paper’s technical contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel AMP application to clustering, promising empirical gains, clear exposition.  
- Arguments for rejection: substantive technical-soundness issues, unclear originality relative to EP, and limited experimental benchmarking.
