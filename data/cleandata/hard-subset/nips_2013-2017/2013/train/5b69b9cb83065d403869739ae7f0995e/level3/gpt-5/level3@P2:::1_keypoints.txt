- Paper proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural constraints (e.g., sparsity, nonnegativity).  
- Introduces β-tempering to unify marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞).  
- Derives update equations for factor means and scaled covariances including AMP-style Onsager corrections.  
- Proposition 1 links MAP-AMP fixed points to global minima of partially minimized objectives, connecting AMP to ICM-like updates.  
- Applies the method to K-means by encoding one-hot constraints on V, producing an AMP variant of K-means.  
- K-means updates include two interpretable corrections: leave-one-out de-biasing and a term favoring currently smaller clusters.  
- Method supports a marginal MAP clustering variant when using discrete priors for V.  
- Builds on compressed sensing and generalized AMP literature but extends to MAP, general rank, and non-row-independent factors.  
- Novel explicit AMP treatment of assignment constraints and derived correction terms appears original.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and ORL face data shows favorable loss and accuracy versus Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and VB.  
- Convergence appears fast and per-iteration complexity is linear in observed entries; extra AMP terms cost only O(m+N).  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees: no state evolution, convergence conditions, or global optimality beyond fixed-point property.  
- Relies on a heuristic noise-variance estimate τ during clustering without robustness analysis to τ mis-specification.  
- Experiments mainly use r̂ = r; robustness to rank mismatch and prior mis-specification is not evaluated.  
- Real-data evaluation is limited (only ORL); no wall-clock runtime or memory comparisons provided.  
- Some derivation details are deferred to the supplement; guidance on computing f and G for common priors and numerical stability is limited.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-positioned relative to belief propagation and AMP, with insightful link to ICM.  
- Clarity is generally good but would benefit from concrete f/G examples, pseudo-code for clustering, and details on damping/τ updates.  
- Originality is moderate–high due to MAP extension, β-tempering, and one-hot constraint handling in AMP.  
- Potential significance is high for large-scale constrained low-rank inference, though broader benchmarks and stronger theory would increase impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel MAP-AMP formulation, interpretable correction terms, strong empirical gains, and low per-iteration cost.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical analysis, limited robustness/scalability evaluation, sparse real-data experiments, and incomplete comparisons to related bilinear GAMP work.  
- Suggested improvements: add state evolution or empirical validation, test robustness to rank/prior/τ mismatch, report runtime/memory, compare stronger baselines, expand related work, and provide code and f/G implementation details.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and practically interesting; lean toward acceptance if theoretical discussion and empirical evaluation are strengthened.
