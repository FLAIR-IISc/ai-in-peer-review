- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, unified by a β parameter to handle both marginalization and MAP.
- Primary evaluation reformulates K-means as a low-rank reconstruction instance and compares AMP to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF on synthetic and ORL face data, showing improved loss and clustering accuracy.
- Strength: addresses an important problem and shows promising empirical performance on the chosen clustering task.
- Major problems in clarity, evaluation, and scholarship prevent acceptance in current form.
- Derivations are dense but generally followable; the resulting algorithm is interesting.
- Central claims rely on large-system (m,N→∞) approximations that are neither theoretically justified nor empirically validated.
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on clustering and omits standard low-rank reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., collaborative filtering), limiting assessment of general applicability.
- Notation is cluttered with excessive hats, tildes, subscripts, and superscripts, harming readability and reproducibility.
- Paper lacks intuitive exposition (e.g., simple relationships between key equations and symmetry of U/V updates are not highlighted).
- Key functions (f and G) are defined mathematically but lack intuitive explanation at first use.
- Main derivations are relegated to the supplement; the paper should summarize key steps and approximations in the main text.
- Some terms and scalings (e.g., factor m in noise variance) are unexplained and their effects on row-column symmetry are unclear.
- Originality is limited: approach is a straightforward BP/AMP application and close relations to existing methods are not discussed.
- Scholarship is weak: prior work on matrix factorization for clustering and related inference methods (e.g., EP, variational message passing) are not adequately cited.
- Potential significance: a fast, flexible AMP for Bayesian matrix factorization could be valuable and seems capable of escaping local optima that trap Lloyd’s algorithm.
- Recommendation: evaluate on standard matrix completion/reconstruction benchmarks or justify clustering as a sufficient testbed.
- Recommendation: validate large-system approximations with theoretical conditions or empirical scaling studies across m and N.
- Recommendation: simplify notation, explain scaling choices, provide intuitive explanations for key functions, and bring core derivation steps into the main paper.
- Recommendation: strengthen literature review and explicitly discuss connections to EP and variational methods; cite the specific VBMF implementation used.
- Recommendation: improve writing and proofreading, consider a clearer title, and add a factor-graph diagram to motivate the BP derivation.
