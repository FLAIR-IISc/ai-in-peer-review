This paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction under additive Gaussian noise with the ability to encode additional structure on the factors (e.g., sparsity, nonnegativity) via priors. The main technical contribution is an AMP-style algorithm derived as an approximation to loopy belief propagation for the joint posterior over U and V. A one-parameter family of “power posteriors” p(U,V|A)β is introduced to unify marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). The algorithm includes Onsager-type correction terms; when these are dropped it reduces to a variational Bayes/ICM-style alternating scheme, making the connection to prior work explicit. The paper then specializes the framework to clustering by encoding the exactly-one-assignment constraint on rows of V, yielding an AMP variant of K-means with principled bias corrections to the assignment step. Empirically, the proposed methods improve K-means loss and accuracy over Lloyd’s algorithm and K-means++ on synthetic data and on the ORL Faces dataset; the AMP “maximum accuracy” variant also outperforms a VBMF baseline on synthetic data.

Relation to prior work: AMP/GAMP for linear inverse problems and bilinear models is well-established [6–9,14], as are variational approaches to matrix factorization [4,5] and the view of K-means as alternating minimization [12]. Applying matrix factorization to clustering is also known [10,11]. The novelty here is (i) extending prior bilinear AMP to handle MAP estimation via the β→∞ limit, and (ii) explicitly handling the one-hot cluster constraint within the AMP framework, yielding a modified assignment step with bias corrections.

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The algorithmic development is plausible and consistent with AMP constructions. The connection to VB/ICM by removing Onsager terms is correct and useful, and Proposition 1 gives a reassuring optimality property at fixed points. However, the core derivation and the state evolution analysis are deferred to the supplement, and the asymptotic assumptions (m,N→∞ with fixed r; Gaussian i.i.d. noise) are not validated empirically or theoretically in the main text. Convergence issues (oscillations in clustering) are noted but not analyzed; damping or other stabilization strategies are not discussed.
- Empirics: The clustering experiments are carefully set up and show clear gains over Lloyd/K-means++. Yet the evaluation is largely confined to the clustering-as-factorization setting, making it difficult to judge impact on standard low-rank reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., noisy matrix denoising with known priors; Bayesian MF baselines on public datasets).
- Reproducibility: Algorithm details and stopping criteria are given, but key derivations and choices (e.g., τ estimation) are not sufficiently explained in the main text.

Clarity:
- The mathematical presentation is often hard to parse. Equations (9a–9d) introduce many symbols without immediate intuition; the roles of f and G, their β dependence, and conditions for differentiability should be explained at first use. The global β parameter is sometimes carried as a subscript/superscript, sometimes not. There is notational clutter (hats, tildes, time indices) that could be consolidated; symmetry between U and V could be emphasized to halve exposition. Simple identities and connections that aid comprehension (e.g., eq.5 is −log of eq.2; eq.8 is a power posterior) should be stated explicitly. The reason for the m factor in the noise variance and its effect on symmetry should be clarified. English and phrasing need polishing throughout.

Originality:
- Extending bilinear AMP beyond posterior means to encompass MAP via β is incremental but useful. The clustering specialization that enforces exactly-one assignments within AMP appears novel and yields a concrete algorithmic twist (bias-corrected assignments). However, the broader idea—applying BP/AMP to bilinear MF with structured priors—has considerable prior art [8,9,13,14], and the paper does not sufficiently position itself against Expectation Propagation and related approximate BP methods on continuous variables.

Significance:
- If the derivation and asymptotic predictions were substantiated and if performance were demonstrated on standard MF tasks, the contribution would be practically relevant: an O(mN)-per-iteration, flexible Bayesian factorization method with better local minima than VB/ICM. The clustering results already show promise. As written, the impact is limited by evaluation scope and missing validation of the AMP approximations in the presented regimes.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, principled AMP formulation connecting to and improving on VB/ICM via Onsager terms.
- Unification of marginal and MAP inference through the β parameter.
- Novel and effective AMP-based K-means variant with intuitive bias corrections.
- Strong synthetic and ORL clustering results; competitive iteration counts.

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited evaluation: no standard low-rank reconstruction/denoising or matrix factorization benchmarks; unclear competitiveness beyond clustering.
- Key derivations and state evolution deferred; asymptotic validity not demonstrated empirically.
- Scholarship gaps: clustering via MF is known; EP/continuous BP not discussed; unclear which VBMF baseline was implemented.
- Clarity issues: heavy notation, confusing equations, undefined symbols at first use; English requires polishing.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Bring a concise derivation and a high-level explanation of the Onsager corrections and state evolution into the main paper; validate asymptotics by scaling m,N and comparing with SE predictions.
- Evaluate on standard MF tasks (e.g., Gaussian denoising with known priors; Bayesian MF on public datasets), and compare against BPMF [3], VBMF [4,5], and strong alternating-minimization baselines; report wall-clock times.
- In clustering, add baselines beyond K-means: EM for GMMs with tied covariance, spectral clustering, and NMF-based clustering; study sensitivity to initialization and rank mis-specification.
- Clarify priors p̂u, p̂v used in experiments and specify the VBMF-MA implementation; discuss EP and related approximate BP literature.
- Simplify notation; define f and G at first use; consistently treat β as a global parameter; explain the m factor in the noise variance; highlight symmetry.
- Address oscillations via damping or tie-breaking; analyze when Proposition 1’s PSD conditions hold in practice.

Recommendation: Borderline reject. The algorithm is promising and the clustering results are encouraging, but the paper requires stronger validation on standard MF tasks, clearer derivation and exposition, and improved scholarship to meet NIPS standards.
