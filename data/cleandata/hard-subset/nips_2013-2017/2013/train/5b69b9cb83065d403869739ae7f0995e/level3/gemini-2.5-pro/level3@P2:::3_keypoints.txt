- Proposes an AMP-based Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction algorithm, extended to MAP, and applies it to K-means clustering with one-hot constraints, claiming better performance than Lloyd and K-means++.

- Strength: paper is clear and well-structured, with a logical progression to the clustering application.

- Strength: novel application of AMP to K-means produces an intuitive update (Eq.18b) that corrects a point's bias on its cluster center and favors smaller clusters.

- Strength: empirical results (synthetic and ORL faces) show AMP-KM and AMP-MA find better local minima and higher clustering accuracy than standard K-means variants.

- Weakness: mathematical derivations are overly condensed and several key steps and assumptions lack sufficient justification.

- Weakness: claim that the second term in Eq.(11) is O(1/√m) needs a detailed justification.

- Weakness: the parameter β in Eq.(8) is introduced without being part of the initial problem formulation and is not clearly motivated.

- Weakness: asymptotic analysis assumes m and N grow at the same order; this strong assumption is argued too briefly and not rigorously.

- Weakness: the approximation mτ ≈ m⁻¹N⁻¹||A - U(V)ᵀ||_F² conflates likelihood variance with total residual (signal+noise) and needs clearer justification.

- Weakness: connection between finite-β algorithm and MAP (β→∞) is not formally established; limit and optimization interchange is not shown.

- Weakness: Proposition 1 and derivation of Eq.(16) are presented too briefly and require a more detailed proof.

- Originality concern: algorithm closely resembles Expectation Propagation for matrix factorization (moment-matching updates); authors should discuss relations to prior EP work (e.g., Stern et al. 2009) and leave-one-out bias-corrections (Minka).

- Significance concern: experimental comparison is narrow (only K-means and K-means++); needs comparisons to spectral clustering, GMM/EM, and more datasets to assess practical impact.

- Acceptance arguments: clear writing, novel AMP-to-clustering idea, and empirical gains over K-means variants.

- Rejection arguments: sketchy technical derivations, potential unaddressed overlap with EP, and insufficient experimental breadth.
