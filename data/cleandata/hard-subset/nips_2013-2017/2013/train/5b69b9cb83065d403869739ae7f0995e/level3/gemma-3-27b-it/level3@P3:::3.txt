This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework, extending prior work by [9] to allow for MAP estimation. A key contribution is applying this algorithm to clustering, reformulating the problem as a constrained low-rank reconstruction. The authors demonstrate, through experiments, that their clustering algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional data. The paper is well-structured and the core idea – leveraging AMP for both reconstruction and clustering – is clearly presented.

However, despite the clarity of the overall approach, the paper suffers from a significant lack of mathematical rigor. Derivations are frequently “sketchy,” omitting crucial steps even in the supplementary material. This makes it difficult to assess the validity of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical properties. The reliance on intuition, while helpful for understanding the high-level concepts, is insufficient for a NIPS publication.

Several specific points raised by the original reviewer remain valid concerns. Firstly, the justification for the O(1/√m) term in Appendix line 139 is lacking, and the omission of β in Section 2 is indeed perplexing. Secondly, the asymptotic analysis – including the derivation of (19) – relies on the assumption that *m* and *n* grow at the same rate. The terse nature of the arguments and the lack of careful consideration for different growth rates weaken the analysis. The reviewer rightfully questions the rigor of these claims.

Thirdly, the assertion in line 167 that *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* equals E[*A<sub>il</sub><sup>2</sup>*] requires substantial justification. *A* represents observed data, while *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* is presented as the likelihood variance. Simply equating the two requires a much more detailed explanation of the underlying assumptions and approximations. 

The reviewer’s fourth point, questioning how the message passing algorithm converges to the MAP solution for finite β, is also valid. The argument about exchanging the limit and minimization of the KL divergence is not necessarily justified and needs a more thorough examination. The connection between the iterative AMP steps and the global MAP optimum is not clearly established.

Regarding Proposition 1, the derivation of equation (17) is indeed too brief. The reviewer's request for a more detailed explanation is reasonable, and a more rigorous proof would strengthen the theoretical foundation of the work.

A significant originality concern is that the proposed algorithm appears to be closely related to Expectation-Propagation (EP). The reviewer correctly points to the work of Stern et al. (WWW 2009) and the standard EP updates presented in the appendix. While the authors attempt to differentiate their work, the core update rules bear a strong resemblance to EP applied to matrix factorization. The potential connection to the EP “clutter” problem (Minka) is a thoughtful observation, highlighting a possible limitation or challenge of the approach.

Finally, the significance of the work is limited by the sole focus on comparison with K-means++. While demonstrating improvement over K-means++ is valuable, it’s insufficient given the extensive clustering literature. The sketchy proofs and the potential overlap with prior EP-based work further diminish confidence in the practical and technical contribution. A more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art clustering algorithms, and a deeper exploration of the theoretical underpinnings, are needed to establish greater significance.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Clear presentation of the core idea: applying AMP to matrix factorization and clustering.
*   Empirical results showing improvements over K-means++ in certain scenarios.
*   Well-structured paper.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of mathematical rigor and incomplete derivations.
*   Potential overlap with existing EP-based work.
*   Limited scope of experimental evaluation.
*   Questionable assumptions in the asymptotic analysis.
*   Insufficient justification for key approximations.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **rejection**. While the paper presents an interesting idea and shows some promising empirical results, the lack of mathematical rigor and the potential overlap with existing work outweigh the strengths. The paper requires substantial revision, including a more detailed and rigorous derivation of the algorithm, a thorough comparison with related EP methods, and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation. Addressing the reviewer’s specific concerns is crucial for establishing the technical soundness and originality of this work.
