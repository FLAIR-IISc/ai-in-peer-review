- Proposes a novel AMP-based algorithm for general-rank low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework supporting structural priors and MAP/marginal inference.  
- Extends previous rank‑one/posterior-mean AMP work to the general-rank case and derives Onsager correction terms.  
- Recasts K-means as a low-rank factorization with one-hot cluster assignment factors, yielding a new AMP-KM clustering algorithm.  
- AMP-KM’s update rule explicitly accounts for a data point’s influence on its cluster center and cluster sizes via principled correction terms.  
- Empirical evaluation shows AMP-KM and AMP-MA consistently outperform Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ on synthetic and real datasets in loss and clustering accuracy.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly relates the algorithm to variational Bayes and highlights the distinguishing Onsager terms.  
- Main weakness: no theoretical convergence guarantees; convergence of the proposed iterative algorithm is assumed rather than proven.  
- Proposition 1 identifies fixed points (matching ICM fixed points) but does not ensure algorithmic convergence in practice.  
- Discussion of clustering-related theoretical literature is limited; connections to provable methods (PCA/SVD, local search, coresets) should be strengthened.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled and novel approach, strong empirical performance, flexibility to incorporate priors, and interdisciplinary relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical foundations (convergence) and incomplete placement within clustering theory.  
- Overall: a strong, novel contribution with excellent empirical results; primary shortcoming is the lack of rigorous convergence analysis.
