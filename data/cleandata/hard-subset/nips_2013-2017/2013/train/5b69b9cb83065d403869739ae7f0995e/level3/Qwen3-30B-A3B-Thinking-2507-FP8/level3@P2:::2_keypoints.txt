- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering.  
- Reformulates clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem with explicit cluster-assignment constraints.  
- Reports empirical improvements over Lloyd’s K-means and variational Bayes in accuracy and convergence on synthetic and ORL face data.  
- Extends prior AMP work to handle general-rank matrices and various structural priors.  
- Argues theoretical advantages of AMP over variational Bayes due to better handling of variable dependence.  
- Notational complexity (many hats/tildes/subscripts) and putting derivations in the supplement harm readability.  
- Novelty is questionable: appears a straightforward extension of prior AMP work and fails to adequately cite prior matrix-factorization-for-clustering literature.  
- Asymptotic approximations (m,N→∞) are not validated empirically and the role of factor m in noise variance is unclear.  
- Lacks comparison to standard low-rank reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix), limiting evaluation of core task performance.  
- Scholarship issues: fails to acknowledge established clustering–factorization connections and omits relevant BP/EP literature; BP nature should be stated earlier.  
- Pro acceptance points: theoretically sound, empirically promising for clustering, and provides a unifying AMP framework.  
- Con acceptance points: poor clarity/notation, overstated novelty, limited/ nonstandard evaluation, and key derivations hidden.  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but requires substantial revisions (clarify notation, contextualize literature, validate asymptotics, broaden experiments); recommend rejection now with encouragement to resubmit.
