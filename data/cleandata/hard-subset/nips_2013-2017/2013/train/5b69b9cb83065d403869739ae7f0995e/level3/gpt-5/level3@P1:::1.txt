This paper proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction from noisy observations, with the ability to encode additional factor structure (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity, or discrete constraints) via flexible priors. The authors introduce a β-parameterized family of posteriors that unifies marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), and derive an AMP iteration that yields approximate posterior means, marginal MAPs, and MAP estimates. A key application is clustering: by constraining V’s rows to be one-hot, the problem reduces to a K-means-type formulation. The resulting “AMP-KM” algorithm can be seen as K-means augmented with Onsager-like corrections that downweight current assignments and encourage balanced clusters. Experiments on synthetic data and the ORL faces dataset show improved K-means loss and, in several settings, higher clustering accuracy relative to Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ (and faster convergence than variational Bayes for maximum-accuracy clustering).

Relation to prior work: AMP has been highly successful in compressed sensing and generalized linear estimation [Donoho et al. 2009; Rangan 2011]. For rank-one matrix estimation, IterFac implements AMP-style updates [Rangan & Fletcher 2012], and for general rank, a related AMP algorithm for posterior means was proposed in [Matsushita & Tanaka 2012]. This paper extends the general-rank AMP framework to treat MAP (β→∞) and marginal MAP for arbitrary factor priors, which is a substantive step beyond [9]. It also connects directly to clustering, distinguishing itself from NMF- and convex-factorization approaches [1,2,10,11] by enforcing one-hot assignments within the AMP framework. The paper is cognizant of recent bilinear AMP advances [13,14] and clarifies differences in scaling assumptions (fixed rank rather than rank fraction). The K-means connection is well-known (e.g., K-means and PCA connections), and k-means++ is appropriately included as a baseline.

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The AMP derivation follows standard dense-graph approximations and yields symmetric updates for U and V with clear roles for “denoisers” f and their Jacobians G. The β-trick provides a neat unification of inference and optimization, and Proposition 1 establishes a useful optimality property of fixed points for the MAP variant. However, theoretical convergence guarantees are limited. State evolution is mentioned but omitted; conditions for convergence and accuracy (scaling, randomness, prior mismatch) are not formalized here.
- Empirics: The synthetic study is well controlled (large m,N; multiple r; known priors) and evaluates both loss and accuracy. The real-data study (ORL) uses k-means++ initialization and shows consistent improvements in K-means loss and often accuracy, with a small number of failure cases (collapse to one cluster). Reporting iteration counts is helpful.
- Reproducibility: The algorithm is clearly specified; the clustering specialization is especially transparent. Some details would benefit from clarification (choice of priors in non-clustering experiments; damping/stepsize; stopping criteria and oscillation handling; τ estimation sensitivity).

Clarity:
- The paper is generally well written and organized. The mapping from the Bayesian model to the AMP updates is clean, and the clustering specialization is easy to follow. The discussion of computational complexity and the connection to variational Bayes/ICM is helpful.
- Suggestions:
  - Add a concise state-evolution summary and explicit conditions (e.g., Gaussian noise, scaling m,N→∞ with fixed r, properties of p̂u,p̂v) under which it predicts performance.
  - Clarify what dependencies within rows of U and V are allowed and which are required for derivation (factorization across rows vs. within-row covariance).
  - Describe practical convergence aids (damping, step-size, annealing in β) and safeguards for non-PSD S,T or oscillations.
  - Discuss the impact and estimation of τ in clustering; analyze sensitivity.

Originality:
- Extending general-rank AMP beyond posterior means to MAP and marginal-MAP with arbitrary factor priors is novel and useful. The K-means specialization that introduces principled Onsager corrections to Lloyd’s assignment step is an original and appealing twist. While the low-rank/cluster connection is known, the algorithmic instantiation here is new relative to typical EM/GMM, spectral, and NMF-based approaches.

Significance:
- If broadly validated, the method offers a competitive alternative to K-means and variational Bayes for high-dimensional clustering and structured matrix factorization with O(mN) per-iteration complexity and good empirical speed. The explicit link to AMP opens the door to principled performance prediction via state evolution and to leveraging the extensive AMP toolbox.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Theory: Convergence and correctness are not theoretically characterized beyond Proposition 1. Including state evolution (even in supplement) with discussion of prior mismatch and finite-size effects would strengthen the paper. For clustering, relate the AMP-KM objective and dynamics to known guarantees for K-means and its variants (e.g., k-means++ approximation bounds, smoothed analysis of Lloyd’s algorithm, spectral/SVD-based initializations, and coreset-based approximation algorithms) and add citations to this literature.
- Baselines: For clustering, consider adding spectral/SVD+K-means and EM for GMMs (with diagonal covariance) to disentangle the effects of soft/hard assignments and initialization. For matrix reconstruction with structure, compare against BiG-AMP-style methods when applicable.
- Robustness: Report sensitivity to initialization, damping, and τ estimation; analyze the two ORL failures and whether simple safeguards (e.g., minimum cluster sizes) resolve them.
- Practicality: For continuous non-Gaussian priors, f and G require r-dimensional integrals; discuss approximations or closed-form cases (Gaussian/Laplace/nonnegativity) and their computational overhead.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, principled AMP extension enabling MAP and marginal-MAP for general factor priors.
- Elegant and practically effective K-means variant with Onsager corrections.
- Strong empirical improvements on synthetic and real data with careful metrics and runtime.
- Well-written with thoughtful connections to variational Bayes and ICM.

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited theoretical guarantees; no state-evolution analysis presented.
- Missing some standard clustering baselines and robustness analyses.
- A few practical details (τ estimation, damping) are ad-hoc or underexplained.

Overall recommendation: Weak accept. The methodological contribution and promising empirical results outweigh the theoretical gaps. Adding state-evolution results (even abbreviated), stronger clustering baselines, and a brief robustness study would elevate the paper further.
