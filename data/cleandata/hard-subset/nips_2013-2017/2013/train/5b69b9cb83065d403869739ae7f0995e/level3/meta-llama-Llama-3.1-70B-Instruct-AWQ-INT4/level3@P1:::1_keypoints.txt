- Proposes an AMP algorithm derived from belief propagation for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
- Uses a Bayesian framework to incorporate additional structural properties beyond low-rankedness.
- Reformulates clustering as a structured low-rank matrix reconstruction problem and applies AMP to it.
- Empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and k-means++ in speed and efficiency on evaluated tasks.
- Provides solid experimental evaluation and detailed comparisons, including variational Bayes matrix factorization.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations, consistent notation, and good figures/tables.
- AMP algorithm is novel and the application to clustering is a meaningful contribution.
- The connection between low-rank factorizations and K-means is well-known; more background/context would help.
- Main weakness: theoretical convergence guarantees for AMP are weak/incomplete compared to alternatives.
