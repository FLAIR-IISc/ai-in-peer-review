- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering, claiming improvements over Lloyd’s K-means on some datasets.  
- Extends prior AMP work to incorporate flexible priors and MAP estimation.  
- Core idea is sound, tackles an important problem, and AMP offers potential efficiency and scalability.  
- Bayesian formulation and MAP-extension are positive contributions; experiments showing superiority over K-means++ in high dimensions are encouraging.  
- Paper is clearly structured and concepts are understandable.  
- Major concern: mathematical derivations (main paper and Supplementary Material) are sketchy and omit crucial steps, hindering correctness assessment.  
- Appendix: claim that a term is O(1/√m) is unsubstantiated and omission of β in Section 2 is inconsistent.  
- Asymptotic analysis assumes m and N grow at the same rate but states this tersely and lacks rigorous justification for resulting equations.  
- Claim that m τ_w^2 equals E[A_il^2] needs clearer justification because A is observed data while m τ_w^2 denotes likelihood variance.  
- Argument that the method converges to MAP as β→∞ is questionable; exchanging limit and KL minimization is not generally valid and needs stronger justification.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 and derivation of equation (17) are overly concise and insufficiently detailed.  
- Originality concern: method may effectively be Expectation-Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization; updates resemble standard EP and lack explicit comparison to EP-based methods.  
- Algorithm resembles EP “clutter” problems (Minka); this connection is noted but not addressed as overlap.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only compared to K-means++), ignoring a broad body of clustering methods and weakening the demonstrated significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel AMP application, improved performance vs K-means++ on some datasets, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient mathematical rigor, potential overlap with EP, limited experiments, and questionable asymptotic assumptions.  
- Overall verdict: weak reject pending substantial revisions addressing mathematical rigor and more thorough comparisons to related work.
