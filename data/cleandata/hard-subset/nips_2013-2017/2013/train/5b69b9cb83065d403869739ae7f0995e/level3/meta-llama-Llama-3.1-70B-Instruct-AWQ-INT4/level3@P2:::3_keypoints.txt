- Proposes using approximate message passing (AMP) for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization and applies it to clustering via a structural reformulation.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains problem, algorithm, and experiments.  
- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, including Appendix gaps (e.g., Appendix line 139 and omitted β).  
- Asymptotic analysis (derivation of Eq. (19)) is terse and assumes m and n grow similarly without rigorous justification.  
- Specific approximation claim disputed: equating m τ_w^2 with E[A_{il}^2] is unjustified because A is observed data.  
- Possible overlap with Expectation-Propagation (EP); algorithm may essentially be EP for matrix factorization (references: Stern et al. WWW 2009 and standard EP updates).  
- Suggested connection between the clustering method and EP “clutter” problem (Minka), raising questions about novelty.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited—comparison only to k-means++ is insufficient given clustering literature.  
- Numerical experiments show effectiveness, supporting practical potential.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application of AMP to clustering, decent experimental results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak/unfinished proofs, questionable originality due to EP overlap, limited significance from narrow comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Major revisions required—provide rigorous derivations, clarify relationship to EP, and broaden empirical comparisons.
