- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction using flexible priors and a temperature parameter β that unifies marginal posterior (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).
- Shows K‑means clustering fits the framework via a one‑hot constraint on factor V, yielding an AMP-based clustering algorithm that enforces single‑cluster assignments.
- Empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K‑means and K‑means++ on reconstruction loss and clustering accuracy on synthetic data and the ORL face dataset.
- Novel extension of general‑rank AMP to handle both MAP and marginal MAP via the β-parameterized posterior, unlike prior AMP work focused on posterior means.
- Algorithm retains O(mN) per-iteration complexity and includes Onsager-like corrections absent in variational Bayes, with a fixed‑point optimality property relative to ICM.
- AMP‑KM directly encodes one‑hot assignments and improves the K‑means objective; AMP‑MA yields superior clustering accuracy and competitive convergence.
- Experimental evaluation measures both Frobenius reconstruction loss and clustering accuracy, uses reasonable protocols and multiple baselines.
- Main theoretical weakness: convergence of AMP iterations is assumed but not proven, and state‑evolution analysis is deferred.
- Manuscript should cite more theoretical computer science results on provable/approximation guarantees for K‑means to better contextualize empirical gains.
- Derivation is careful and consistent with AMP literature; fixed‑point result is useful, though some technical details are relegated to the supplement.
- Paper is generally clear with precise notation and a neat β‑parameterization; pointing readers to specific supplemental lemmas/theorems would improve clarity.
- Originality: the β‑trick for MAP and the strict one‑hot AMP instantiation for K‑means are novel and contrast with variational Bayes factorizations.
- Significance: scalable, structured low‑rank factorization with demonstrated empirical gains is practically relevant; stronger theoretical support would increase impact.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on strengthening discussion of theoretical guarantees (even partial) and adding citations to relevant K‑means theory.
