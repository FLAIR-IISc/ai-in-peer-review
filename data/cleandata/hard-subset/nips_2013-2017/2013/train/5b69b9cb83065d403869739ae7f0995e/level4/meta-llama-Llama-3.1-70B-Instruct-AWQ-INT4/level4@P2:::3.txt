This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization and its application to clustering. The authors show that the matrix factorization problem can be seen as a generalization of the clustering problem, and they specialize the AMP algorithm for matrix factorization to clustering. The paper also presents experiments comparing the proposed algorithm with the k-means++ algorithm.

Quality: The mathematical derivations in this paper are very sketchy and omit many non-trivial steps, even in the appendix. Several concerns regarding the mathematical rigor and clarity of the derivations are raised. For instance, the approximation of the second term in equation (11) as O(1/√m) is unclear, and the omission of β in Section 2 is confusing. Additionally, the asymptotic analysis in this section is terse and lacks mathematical rigor. The expectation of A{il}^2 is approximated as mτw^2, but the rational for such approximation is not verified. Furthermore, it is unclear how the message passing algorithm derived for finite β converges to the MAP problem. The proof of Proposition 1 is also too brief and lacks clarity.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured, and it is not difficult to grasp the main point of the paper. However, the mathematical derivations and proofs could be improved for better clarity and rigor.

Originality: The proposed algorithm appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, which has been done before in the literature (e.g., http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). While the authors may have made some novel contributions, the connection to existing work on EP is not clearly established. The clustering algorithm proposed by the authors may also have an interesting connection to the clutter problem of the original EP paper, but this is not explored in the paper.

Significance: The authors mainly focus on the application of the algorithm to clustering, but comparing only to k-means++ may not be sufficient to prove the practical usefulness of the algorithm. The paper lacks a thorough evaluation of the algorithm's technical correctness, and the proofs, even in the appendix, are very sketchy. Overall, the significance of the paper is difficult to assess due to the lack of clarity and rigor in the mathematical derivations and proofs.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel application of the AMP algorithm to clustering.
* The experiments show promising results compared to k-means++.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The mathematical derivations and proofs lack clarity and rigor.
* The connection to existing work on EP is not clearly established.
* The paper lacks a thorough evaluation of the algorithm's technical correctness.
* The significance of the paper is difficult to assess due to the lack of clarity and rigor in the mathematical derivations and proofs.
