- Presents an AMP-based algorithm for matrix reconstruction from noisy observations, targeting low-rank matrices with additional factor assumptions and accelerating Bayesian methods.
- Connects matrix reconstruction to k-means clustering, offering an interesting application for the proposed algorithms.
- Applying AMP to matrix reconstruction appears novel and conceptually interesting.
- Notes that low-rank factorizations’ link to k-means is known (e.g., PCA gives a factor-two approximation), but this connection enables a compelling experimental setup.
- Empirical comparison against k-means and k-means++ shows the proposed approach is faster and more efficient.
- Evaluation measures both Frobenius-norm residual and resulting clusterings, which is commendable.
- Main weakness: scarcity of theoretical guarantees, particularly regarding convergence.
- Recommends citing additional Theoretical Computer Science work that provides provable algorithms for the k-means objective.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with promising experiments but somewhat weak theoretical backing.
