This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for probabilistic matrix factorization. The authors first frame matrix factorization as a generalization of the clustering problem. They then derive a message passing algorithm for the general model and specialize it for clustering. Finally, they present experiments comparing their proposed clustering algorithm with the k-means++ algorithm.

While the paper is well-structured and presents an interesting perspective, it suffers from several significant weaknesses related to its technical correctness, originality, and the significance of its contribution.

**Major Concerns**

1.  **Insufficient Mathematical Rigor and Justification:** My primary concern lies with the mathematical derivations, which are often sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, even in the appendix. This makes it very difficult to verify the technical correctness of the central claims and algorithm. Specific issues include:
    *   The asymptotic analysis in Section 3 is terse. The derivation of (19), for instance, seems to implicitly assume that *m* and *n* grow at the same rate. The rigor of this analysis is questionable without a more detailed treatment of the assumptions.
    *   The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief to be followed. Specifically, the derivation of (17) is not explained.
    *   The justification for certain approximations is weak. For example, in line 167, the expectation of A_{il}^2 is approximated by mτ_w^2. Since A is observed data and mτ_w^2 is the variance of the likelihood, this is not an obvious equality, and the rationale for this approximation needs to be properly justified.
    *   The theoretical claim regarding the convergence of the algorithm to the MAP solution as β → ∞ is not substantiated. Message passing algorithms typically minimize KL divergence. It does not necessarily hold that the limit of the minimum KL divergence is equal to the minimum of the limit of the KL divergence (i.e., lim(β→∞) min(KL) ≠ min(lim(β→∞) KL)). This point requires a formal proof.

2.  **Questionable Originality:** The proposed algorithm appears to be a direct application of Expectation Propagation (EP) to the matrix factorization problem. The estimation of the first and second moments of the marginal posterior is the standard procedure in EP. This approach has been explored previously in "Matchbox: Large Scale Bayesian Recommendations" by Stern et al. (WWW 2009), where EP was applied to matrix factorization. The update procedures detailed in Section 1 of the Appendix, in particular, seem to be standard EP updates. The authors should explicitly clarify the relationship of their work to EP and clearly differentiate their contribution from this prior work. If the algorithm is indeed a new application of EP, the novelty is significantly diminished.

3.  **Insufficient Experimental Evaluation:** The paper's empirical contribution is not convincingly demonstrated. The authors focus primarily on the application to clustering but compare their algorithm only to k-means++. Given the vast and mature literature on clustering, this comparison is insufficient to establish the practical utility or superiority of the proposed method. To demonstrate its significance, the algorithm should be benchmarked against a wider range of modern and state-of-the-art clustering techniques on multiple datasets.

**Detailed Questions for the Authors**

To help clarify the technical aspects, I would ask the authors to elaborate on the following points:

1.  In the Appendix (line 139), could you provide a detailed derivation showing why the second term of Eq. (11) is O(1/√m)?
2.  The parameter β seems to be introduced in the appendix but is missing from the model description in Section 2. Could you clarify its role in the main model?
3.  How is Eq. (17) derived in the proof of Proposition 1? Please provide the intermediate steps.
4.  Could you provide a formal proof for the claim that the message passing algorithm converges to the MAP solution in the limit of β → ∞?
5.  What are the key differences between the proposed algorithm and the EP-based matrix factorization method of Stern et al. (2009)?

**Minor Comments**

On a positive note, the paper is well-structured, and the high-level ideas are communicated clearly. The connection drawn between matrix factorization and clustering is insightful. Additionally, there may be an interesting connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the "clutter problem" in Minka's original EP paper if the likelihood were a uniform mixture of Gaussians. A discussion of this could further contextualize the work.

**Conclusion**

In summary, while the paper presents an interesting idea, its contribution is undermined by serious concerns regarding its technical correctness, originality, and the significance of its experimental results. The lack of detailed mathematical proofs makes the algorithm unverifiable, and its potential overlap with existing work on EP for matrix factorization calls its novelty into question. Without substantial revisions to address these fundamental issues, I cannot recommend this paper for acceptance.
