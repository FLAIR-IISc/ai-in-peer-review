The paper presents an algorithm for matrix reconstruction from noisy observations. It targets low-rank matrices under additional assumptions on the factors and uses an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the classical, computationally expensive Bayesian methodology. The authors also connect matrix reconstruction to k-means clustering, highlighting an interesting application domain for the proposed algorithms.

To the best of my knowledge, applying AMP to matrix reconstruction is novel and interesting. While the connections between low-rank matrix factorizations and k-means are well known (e.g., PCA provides a factor-two approximation algorithm for k-means), this link enables a compelling experimental evaluation. The authors compare their method against k-means and k-means++, and, according to their empirical results, the proposed approach appears faster and more efficient than both. It is also commendable that the evaluation considers both the Frobenius-norm residual and the resulting clusterings.

The main weakness is the scarcity of theoretical guarantees, particularly regarding convergence. This limitation is somewhat expected, given that many algorithms for k-means also have relatively weak theoretical properties. Still, the paper would benefit from citing additional work in Theoretical Computer Science that provides provably accurate algorithms for the k-means objective.

Overall, this is a solid paper presenting AMP algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction and k-means, with a promising experimental evaluation but somewhat weak theoretical results.
