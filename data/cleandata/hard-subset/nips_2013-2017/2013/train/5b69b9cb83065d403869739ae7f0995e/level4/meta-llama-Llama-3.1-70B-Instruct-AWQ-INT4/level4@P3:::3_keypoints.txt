- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering, allowing incorporation of structure like sparsity.  
- Derives an efficient AMP algorithm and reformulates clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem.  
- Paper is well-structured and the main idea is easy to grasp.  
- Mathematical derivations are sketchy, omitting non-trivial steps (e.g., second term of Eq. (11) claimed O(1/√m) without justification; β omitted in Section 2).  
- Some analyses (e.g., derivation of Eq. (19)) appear to assume m and n grow at the same rate, which may not hold generally.  
- Originality is questionable: approach appears similar to prior EP applications to matrix factorization and needs differentiation from existing work.  
- The paper could explore connections to the clutter problem in the original EP literature (e.g., uniform Gaussian mixture likelihood).  
- Significance is limited by evaluation: only compared to k-means++, lacking broader clustering baselines from the literature.  
- Proofs in the appendix are very sketchy, hindering assessment of technical correctness.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel AMP application to clustering, empirical demonstration of effectiveness, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: sketchy math and proofs, unclear novelty relative to EP work, limited experimental comparisons.  
- Suggestions: provide detailed mathematical derivations and complete proofs (especially in the appendix), clarify differences from prior EP/matrix-factorization work, and perform more comprehensive comparisons with other clustering algorithms.
