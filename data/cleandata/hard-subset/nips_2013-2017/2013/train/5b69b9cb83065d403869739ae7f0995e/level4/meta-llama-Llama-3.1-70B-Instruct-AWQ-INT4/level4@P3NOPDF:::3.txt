This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, demonstrating its application to clustering problems. The authors derive the message passing algorithm for matrix factorization and specialize it to clustering. Experimental results are presented to compare the proposed algorithm with the k-means++ algorithm.

**Quality**

The mathematical derivations in this paper are somewhat sketchy, omitting several non-trivial steps, even in the appendix. Several concerns regarding the mathematical rigor and clarity of the derivations are raised below:

1. In line 139 of the Appendix, the second term of (11) is stated to be O(1/\sqrt(m)). However, the reasoning behind this statement is unclear, particularly with regards to the omission of β in Section 2.
2. Some of the analysis, including the derivation of (19), appears to assume that m and n grow at the same order. The asymptotic analysis in this section is brief and lacks mathematical rigor, raising concerns about its validity.
3. In line 167, the expectation of A{il}^2 is approximated as m \tauw^2. However, A is observed data, and m\tau_w^2 represents only the variance of the likelihood. The rationale behind this approximation requires further clarification.
4. The convergence of the message passing algorithm derived for finite β to the MAP problem is unclear. While the message passing algorithm minimizes the KL divergence, it is not guaranteed that \lim\beta \min KL = \min \lim\beta KL.
5. The proof of Proposition 1 is too concise and lacks clarity. The derivation of (17) requires further explanation.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-structured, and its main point is easily understandable. However, the mathematical derivations and analysis could benefit from more detailed explanations to improve clarity.

**Originality**

The algorithm proposed in this paper appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, primarily focusing on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior. This approach has been previously explored in the literature, such as in the paper by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). The standard EP update procedures in Section 1 of the Appendix seem to support this observation. It would be beneficial to clarify the novelty of the proposed algorithm in relation to existing work.

Additionally, an interesting connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf) can be explored. Specifically, if the likelihood of the clutter problem is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions, a connection between the two approaches may be established.

**Significance**

While the authors focus on the application of their algorithm to clustering, the comparison with only k-means++ may not be sufficient to demonstrate its practical usefulness. The sketchy proofs, even in the appendix, make it challenging to evaluate the technical correctness of the paper. Furthermore, the potential application of expectation-propagation to matrix factorization, as noted earlier, raises concerns about the originality of the proposed approach.
