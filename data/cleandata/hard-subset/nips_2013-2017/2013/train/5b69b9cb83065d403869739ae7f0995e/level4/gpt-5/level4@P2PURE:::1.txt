The paper proposes an algorithm for matrix reconstruction from noisy observations. It focuses on low-rank matrices with additional assumptions on the factors and employs an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the classical Bayesian method, which is computationally expensive. The work also links matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering, offering an interesting application domain for the proposed algorithms.

To my knowledge, applying AMP to matrix reconstruction is novel and compelling. While the connections between low-rank factorizations and K-means are well known (e.g., PCA yields a factor-2 approximation for K-means), this perspective enables a clear experimental evaluation against k-means and k-means++. Empirically, the proposed method appears faster and more efficient than standard k-means and k-means++ according to the reported data. The experiments compare both the Frobenius-norm residual and the resulting clusterings, which is a valuable aspect of the evaluation.

The primary weakness is the scarcity of theoretical guarantees, particularly regarding convergence. This is not unexpected—many K-means algorithms have only limited theoretical properties—but the paper would benefit from citing additional results in theoretical computer science that provide provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting AMP-based methods for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, with promising empirical performance but relatively weak theoretical results.
