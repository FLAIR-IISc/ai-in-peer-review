- Proposes a Bayesian AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction that supports both posterior mean and MAP estimation via an inverse-temperature parameter β.  
- Derives a general-rank AMP algorithm that unifies marginalization and MAP problems.  
- Applies the AMP framework to K-means by enforcing one-hot cluster assignments, yielding a novel iterative clustering algorithm.  
- Shows the new K-means update (Eq. 18b) offers corrections to Lloyd’s algorithm accounting for self-influence and cluster-size penalties.  
- Positions the approach between Monte Carlo methods and Variational Bayes, arguing AMP captures dependencies better than mean-field VB.  
- Notes novelty relative to prior low-rank–clustering links (e.g., Ding et al. 2010) by directly handling one-hot constraints in AMP.  
- Strong experimental support on synthetic data and ORL face dataset, comparing to Lloyd’s, K-means++, and VB matrix factorization.  
- Empirical results show superior K-means loss reduction, higher clustering accuracy, and fast convergence for AMP methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains the connection between matrix reconstruction and clustering effectively.  
- Main theoretical weakness: no formal convergence proof for the AMP algorithm; Proposition 1 does not ensure convergence.  
- Empirically observed oscillations require a modified stopping criterion, indicating potential instability in practice.  
- Related-work discussion is brief; needs more detail on differences with other recent AMP algorithms and rank-vs-dimension scaling assumptions.  
- Should acknowledge broader theoretical CS literature on provable K-means approximations to clarify the method is a heuristic without worst-case guarantees.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with convincing empirical performance; recommendation for acceptance despite theoretical limitations.
