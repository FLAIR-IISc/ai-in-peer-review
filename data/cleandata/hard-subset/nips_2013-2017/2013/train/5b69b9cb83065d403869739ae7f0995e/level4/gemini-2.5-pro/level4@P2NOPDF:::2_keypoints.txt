- Proposes a new low-rank matrix reconstruction algorithm derived from Belief Propagation with large-matrix approximations.  
- Evaluates the method by framing clustering as matrix reconstruction and compares to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.  

- Strength: Novel application of BP with a sound mathematical framework and promising empirical gains over K-means.  
- Strength: The paper is interesting and potentially useful; the BP approach is a valuable contribution.  

- Major concern: Evaluation is non-standard (clustering framed as reconstruction), making it hard to compare to established matrix-reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix).  
- Recommendation: Include experiments on standard datasets or justify their omission to assess competitiveness.  

- Novelty is incremental: mainly a direct BP application to an existing model; the large-matrix approximations are the most interesting part but are only in the supplement and untested.  
- Recommendation: Improve explanation and empirical validation of the asymptotic approximations.  

- Scholarship issues: Fails to cite prior work connecting matrix factorization to clustering and omits discussion of Expectation Propagation; the BP connection should be stated earlier (e.g., in the abstract).  
- Recommendation: Properly contextualize and cite related literature and clarify relation to EP.  

- Clarity problems: Full derivation is relegated to the supplement; include a high-level derivation outline in the main text.  
- Notation is overly cumbersome (excess hats/tildes/superscripts); many symbols and terms could be simplified or factored to improve readability.  

- Exposition needs improvement: explicitly relate equations (e.g., eq.5 = -log eq.2), explain functions like f_β and G_β in prose, and highlight algorithmic symmetries to shorten descriptions.  
- Inconsistent use of subscripts (β) and unnecessary time superscripts in message updates should be simplified.  

- Unanswered modeling choices: The role of the factor m in noise variance and whether it breaks row-column symmetry is not discussed.  
- Suggestion: Address modeling decisions and their implications for algorithm behavior.  

- Detailed comments: Clarify nonstandard notation (tilde for column vectors), capitalization choices (N vs m), and provide a citation for the VBMF-MA implementation used.  
- Supplement issues: Steps using big-O notation and the novelty of the message approximation need clearer explanation; include more illustrative diagrams like supplemental Figure 1 in the main paper.  

- Minor language fixes: Several small grammatical corrections suggested (e.g., "Since properties" → "Since the properties", "stuck to" → "stuck in").  

- Conclusion: The approach is interesting and potentially important but the paper requires substantial revisions in evaluation, citations, and presentation before publication.
