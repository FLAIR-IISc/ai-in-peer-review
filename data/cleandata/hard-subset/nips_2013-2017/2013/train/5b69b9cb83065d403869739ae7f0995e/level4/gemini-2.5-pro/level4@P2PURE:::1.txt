This paper proposes an algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations, subject to specific assumptions on the matrix factors. The authors employ an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the computationally expensive classical Bayesian method. The paper further connects this matrix reconstruction problem to K-means clustering, providing an interesting application domain for the proposed algorithm.

To my knowledge, the application of Approximate Message Passing to matrix reconstruction is a novel and interesting contribution. While the connection between low-rank matrix factorization and K-means is well-established (e.g., PCA providing a 2-approximation for K-means), the authors leverage this link effectively to conduct a strong experimental evaluation against K-means and K-means++. The empirical results are noteworthy, suggesting the proposed algorithm is both faster and more efficient than these standard baselines. A further strength of the evaluation is its thoroughness, as the authors compare performance based on both the Frobenius norm residual and the quality of the resulting clusters.

The paper's main weakness is the lack of theoretical guarantees for the proposed algorithm's convergence, although this is a common limitation for many K-means-related methods. To strengthen the paper, the authors could better situate their work by citing more of the Theoretical Computer Science literature on provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective.

In summary, this is a solid paper presenting a novel AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means. It features a promising experimental evaluation, though the theoretical results are somewhat weak.
