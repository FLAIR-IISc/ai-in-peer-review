- Introduces an AMP-based algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy data with assumptions on constituent factors.
- Draws a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering and uses K-means as a primary application.
- Use of Approximate Message Passing for matrix reconstruction is novel and interesting.
- Leverages the known relation between low-rank factorization and K-means to design the experimental evaluation.
- Benchmarked against k-means and k-means++, with empirical results indicating superior speed and efficiency.
- Evaluation is comprehensive, assessing both Frobenius norm residual and final cluster quality.
- Primary limitation: absence of strong theoretical convergence guarantees for the algorithm.
- Recommendation: cite additional Theoretical Computer Science literature on provably accurate K-means algorithms.
- Overall: solid paper with promising experimental results but somewhat underdeveloped theoretical analysis.
