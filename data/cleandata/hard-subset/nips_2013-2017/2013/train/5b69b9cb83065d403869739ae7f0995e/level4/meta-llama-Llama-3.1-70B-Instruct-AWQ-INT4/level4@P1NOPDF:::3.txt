This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, demonstrating its generality by specializing the algorithm to the clustering problem. The authors also conduct experiments to compare their proposed algorithm with the k-means++ algorithm.

Quality: While the paper presents an interesting approach, the mathematical derivations are often sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, even in the appendix. Several specific concerns arise:

1) In line 139 of the Appendix, the reasoning behind the second term of (11) being O(1/\sqrt(m)) is unclear, particularly with regards to the omission of \beta in Section 2.
2) Some analyses, including the derivation of (19), appear to assume that m and n grow at the same order. However, the asymptotic analysis in this section is somewhat terse, and I am concerned about its mathematical rigor.
3) In line 167, the approximation of m \tau_w^2 as the expectation of A_{il}^2 is not clearly justified. Since A is observed data, m\tau_w^2 represents only the variance of the likelihood; the rationale for this approximation should be verified.
4) It is unclear how the message passing algorithm derived for finite \beta converges to the MAP problem. Although the message passing algorithm minimizes the KL divergence, the equality \lim\beta \min KL = \min \lim\beta KL does not necessarily hold.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is overly brief, and the derivation of (17) is not provided.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured, making it relatively easy to grasp the main point. However, the lack of detailed mathematical derivations may hinder understanding for some readers.

Originality: The authors primarily focus on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior, which raises the question of whether the entire algorithm is simply an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. EP has been previously used in matrix factorization, as seen in the paper by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Furthermore, Section 1 of the Appendix appears to follow standard EP update procedures. I would appreciate clarification on this point. Additionally, an interesting connection may exist between the proposed clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood of the clutter problem is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. I would like to emphasize that this is a suggestion, rather than a criticism of the paper's originality.

Significance: While the authors mainly focus on the application to clustering, the vast amount of literature on clustering suggests that comparing their algorithm only to k-means++ may not be sufficient to prove its practical usefulness. Furthermore, the sketchy proofs, even in the appendix, make it challenging to evaluate the technical correctness of the paper. As mentioned earlier, I suspect that the algorithm may be an application of expectation-propagation to matrix factorization, which has already been explored by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
