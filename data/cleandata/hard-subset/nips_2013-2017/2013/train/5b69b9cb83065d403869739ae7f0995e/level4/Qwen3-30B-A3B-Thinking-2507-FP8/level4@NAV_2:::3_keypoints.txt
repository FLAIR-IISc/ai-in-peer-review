- Proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering by reformulating clustering as a special case of matrix factorization.  
- Derives a specialized message passing algorithm and compares it empirically to k-means++ with extensive numerical experiments.  
- Mathematical derivations are sparse and omit non-trivial steps, undermining rigor and reproducibility.  
- Appendix Line 139: justification that the second term in (11) is O(1/√m) is incomplete; β is not properly accounted for in Section 2.  
- Asymptotic analysis assuming proportional growth of m and N lacks mathematical rigor given the terse presentation.  
- Line 167: claim that mτ_w^2 represents E[A_ij^2] is unjustified—mτ_w^2 is the likelihood variance, not the observed-data expectation.  
- Convergence for finite β to the MAP solution is not rigorously established; the link between KL minimization and β → ∞ needs careful treatment.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed, with Equation (17) derivation unclear.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly communicates main contributions despite mathematical gaps.  
- Originality is questionable: the algorithm appears to mainly estimate posterior moments akin to Expectation-Propagation (EP), and prior work (Stern et al., 2009) already applied EP to matrix factorization.  
- Possible connection to EP’s clutter problem (Minka, 2001) should be discussed, especially under a uniform mixture-of-Gaussians likelihood.  
- Significance is limited: experiments compare only to k-means++, insufficient against broader clustering literature, and the suspected equivalence to EP further weakens the contribution.
