1) Paper proposes an AMP algorithm for matrix factorization and adapts it to clustering, validated only against k-means++.

2) Mathematical justifications are weak with many omitted derivation steps, even in the appendix.

3) Justification for the O(1/√m) term (App. eq. 11, line 139) is unclear and seems to ignore the parameter β from Section 2.

4) Asymptotic analysis (including derivation of eq. 19) is terse and appears to assume an unstated relationship between growth rates of m and n.

5) The claim E[A_il^2] = m τ_w^2 (line 167) is not adequately explained; equating observed data moment to likelihood variance lacks justification.

6) Convergence behavior as β → ∞ and the claimed interchange of limits/minimization (lim_β min KL = min lim_β KL) are not justified.

7) Proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed; the derivation leading to eq. 17 is unclear.

8) Originality is questionable: core algorithm (estimating marginal posterior moments) closely resembles Expectation Propagation (EP) for matrix factorization (Stern et al.).

9) Appendix update procedures closely match standard EP implementations, reinforcing the similarity to prior work.

10) Potentially interesting link to Minka’s EP “clutter” problem is noted but presented as further investigation rather than novelty.

11) Significance is not established: limited experimental comparison (only k-means++), lack of rigorous proofs, and suspicion the work re-applies existing EP techniques limit perceived impact.
