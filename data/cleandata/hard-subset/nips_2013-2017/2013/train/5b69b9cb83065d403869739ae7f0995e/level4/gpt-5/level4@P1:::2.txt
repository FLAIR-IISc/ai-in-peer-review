Review of “Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing”

Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that accommodates structural priors on the factors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity). A one-parameter family p̂(U,V|A; β) interpolates between posterior inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), yielding a unified algorithm. The method is then specialized to clustering by casting K-means as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem with one-hot assignments (V’s rows in {e1,…,er̂}). The resulting AMP variant differs from Lloyd’s K-means by adding principled debiasing and cluster-size effects in the assignment step. Experiments on synthetic data and the ORL faces dataset show improvements over Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and a variational Bayes matrix factorization (VBMF) baseline.

The work is positioned relative to AMP in compressed sensing (Donoho et al., Rangan) and prior bilinear/low-rank AMP (IterFac, Matsushita–Tanaka), and to VBMF (Lim–Teh; Raiko–Ilin–Karhunen). The paper also mentions related AMP for dictionary learning and bilinear GAMP. The K-means connection is consistent with long-standing links between clustering and matrix factorization/nonnegative MF (e.g., Xu–Liu–Gong; Ding–Li–Jordan), though the manuscript underplays this prior art.

Strengths
- A unified AMP framework that treats posterior averaging and MAP via β, with explicit updates f and G and a clear specialization to K-means-style clustering.
- Computationally attractive (O(mN) per iteration) and practically effective, with strong empirical gains over Lloyd’s K-means/K-means++ in both loss and accuracy, and over VBMF baselines in maximum-accuracy clustering.
- Useful algorithmic insights for clustering: assignment updates include de-biasing for self-influence and an implicit preference for smaller current clusters, both emerging from the AMP corrections.
- Proposition 1 gives a meaningful optimality property at fixed points and clarifies the connection to ICM/coordinate descent, suggesting fewer poor fixed points than ICM.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Evaluation scope: The main empirical validation is via the clustering reformulation. This makes it hard to infer performance on standard matrix reconstruction tasks where VBMF and related methods are commonly evaluated (e.g., Netflix data in Lim–Teh; Raiko et al.). If Netflix or comparable data were not feasible, the paper should state why and provide an alternative standard benchmark to demonstrate competitiveness beyond clustering.
- Derivation and approximations: The two large-system approximations (m→∞ and N→∞) that justify the AMP updates are only in the supplement and are not directly stress-tested. A brief outline in the main paper and a targeted empirical sanity check of the approximations’ consequences would strengthen the technical case.
- Positioning and citations: The abstract and Section 2.2 read as though casting clustering as low-rank reconstruction is novel. This connection is known; please either state that it is well-known or cite representative work (e.g., Xu–Liu–Gong; Ding–Li–Jordan). The paper should also cite expectation propagation (EP) and clarify how the proposed AMP relates to EP for continuous distributions. Finally, since the core algorithm is BP-inspired, say so in the abstract rather than deferring to Section 4.1.
- Clarity and notation: The algorithmic presentation is hard to parse. Constants like 1/(mτw) could be factored out to improve readability, and the last two terms in (9b) and (9e) can be combined. There are many hats, tildes, subscripts, and superscripts (e.g., τw vs τ; Section 4 uses only hatted symbols); consider judicious simplification (e.g., drop hats within Section 4 with a statement at the outset; avoid per-iteration superscripts when assignment arrows suffice). Equations (5) and (8) could be motivated explicitly (Eq. 5 is the negative log of Eq. 2; Eq. 8 is Eq. 2 raised to the power β). The near row–column symmetry of Algorithm 2 could be highlighted and leveraged to shorten the exposition. Define the meanings of f and G at their introduction (e.g., “f is the mean and G is the β-scaled covariance under q̂”), rather than relying on later remarks. The β-dependence is inconsistently propagated: f and G depend on β only through q̂; treating β as a global parameter and removing β subscripts elsewhere would simplify the notation. Before (15)–(16), state explicitly that taking β→∞ yields the MAP forms.
- Model scaling questions: The additive noise has variance mτ per entry. What is the conceptual role of the factor m? Does it arise solely from the large-system scaling used in the derivation, and is it the only source of row–column asymmetry? A brief discussion would be helpful.
- Experimental details: Please cite the primary reference guiding the VBMF-MA implementation. Two VBMF citations are given; clarify which one you implemented (or both) and any key settings. For the ORL experiments, note the sensitivity to initialization (empty clusters) and how τ is estimated at each iteration, as proposed in Section 4.3.
- Supplementary material: In Eq. 5, writing the z-dependent terms as a product of exponentials would clarify which is the pdf. The step from Eq. 14 to 15 (big-O in m) was hard to follow; more detail would help. The paragraph on “approximate representation of these messages in terms of a few parameters” should more explicitly connect to prior work and clarify what is novel in your approximation.

Quality, originality, significance, clarity (NIPS criteria)
- Quality: The algorithm is plausible and well-motivated; Proposition 1 is useful. However, the derivation relies on approximations whose practical impact is not assessed, and standard benchmarks are missing.
- Originality: Extending AMP to general-rank matrix factorization with a β-parameterized path to MAP, and enforcing one-hot assignments directly within AMP for clustering, are interesting. The clustering formulation itself is not novel and needs clearer positioning.
- Significance: Results suggest practical impact for clustering, but broader significance for matrix reconstruction is under-supported empirically.
- Clarity: The paper is generally readable, but the algorithmic derivation and notation need streamlining. Consider minor language edits (e.g., articles and phrasing), and add explanatory figures if space permits.

Minor questions
- Is using a tilde to denote column vectors standard in your community?
- Why is N uppercase and m lowercase?
- Several minor English issues could be polished (e.g., “Since the properties…”, “according to the problem”, “motivates the use of”, “Previous work”, “stuck in bad local minima”). The title might read more naturally as “Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering using an approximate message passing algorithm.”

Recommendation
The algorithm is promising and the clustering results are strong, but the paper needs clearer positioning, improved exposition, and broader empirical validation. I recommend rejection in its current form, with encouragement to revise along the lines above.
