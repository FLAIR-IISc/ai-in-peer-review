- Proposes an algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations with additional assumptions on matrix factors.  
- Uses an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) framework to speed up Bayesian inference.  
- Establishes a connection between the matrix reconstruction problem and K-means clustering as an application domain.  
- Novelty: applying AMP to matrix reconstruction appears to be a new and interesting contribution.  
- Leverages known links between low-rank factorization and K-means to design a strong experimental evaluation.  
- Empirical results show the method is faster and more efficient than standard k-means and k-means++.  
- Evaluation is thorough, comparing Frobenius norm residuals and the quality of resulting cluster assignments.  
- Primary weakness: limited theoretical guarantees for the algorithm’s convergence.  
- Suggestion: strengthen the paper by citing more theoretical computer science literature on provably accurate K-means algorithms.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper with promising experimental support but somewhat limited theoretical results.
