- Proposes a new AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering.  
- Algorithm is derived from a Bayesian model and the mathematics are generally followable.  
- Experiments are clearly presented and show the algorithm's potential.  
- Evaluation uses a non-standard task (clustering as matrix factorization), limiting generalizability to conventional matrix reconstruction problems.  
- Authors should evaluate on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) to compare with predecessors.  
- The algorithm appears to be a straightforward application of AMP and overlaps with prior Variational Message Passing approaches.  
- Asymptotic approximations (N→∞, m→∞) are introduced but not empirically validated or clearly justified.  
- Serious citation issues: prior work connecting matrix factorization and clustering is not properly cited or explained.  
- Novelty of the algorithm’s derivation is not clearly articulated relative to existing literature.  
- Exposition is weak: notation is cumbersome (excess hats/tildes/subscripts/superscripts) and should be simplified.  
- Definitions are not given near function appearances; parameter β should be treated and explained globally.  
- Language and writing quality have distracting issues and need revision.  
- Overall recommendation: paper is interesting but requires major revisions (experiments, citations, exposition, empirical validation) before acceptance.
