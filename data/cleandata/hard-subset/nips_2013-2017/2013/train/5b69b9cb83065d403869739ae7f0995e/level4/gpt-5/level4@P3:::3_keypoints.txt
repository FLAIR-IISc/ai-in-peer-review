- Paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with general priors, derives an AMP algorithm with a temperature β to interpolate posterior mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), and applies it to clustering via a one-hot V constraint.
- Builds on prior AMP/bilinear AMP work (IterFac, rank-r AMP, biG-AMP, dictionary-learning AMP) and claims novelty in enabling MAP via β and treating one-hot constraints directly.

- Strength: Clear formulation accommodating structural priors and a unified β-parameterized view of marginalization vs MAP.
- Strength: Practical AMP algorithm with O(mN) per-iteration cost and Onsager corrections, distinguishing it from VB/ICM.
- Strength: Interesting clustering application embedding one-hot assignments into bilinear AMP and producing interpretable corrections to K-means.
- Strength: Favorable synthetic empirical results (improved K-means loss/accuracy) and fast convergence in practice.

- Weakness: Several technical steps are terse or deferred to supplement; need explicit scaling and consistent handling of β (e.g., justify second term of (11) being O(1/√m)).
- Weakness: Missing precise large-system limit statement and assumptions on aspect ratio (m,N→∞ with fixed ratio) and implications for extreme aspect ratios.
- Weakness: Unjustified substitution E[Ail^2] ≈ mτw^2 for observed A; needs connection between state-evolution motivation and empirical τ estimation.
- Weakness: Unclear whether taking β→∞ commutes with fixed-point characterization; need conditions or heuristics (e.g., Laplace) and empirical evidence linking limits to MAP.
- Weakness: Proposition 1 proof sketch is too brief; require detailed derivation of (17) and justification of positivity assumptions and limiting arguments for MAP.
- Weakness: Lack of discussion on AMP convergence safeguards (damping, denoiser smoothing), reported oscillations in AMP-KM, and chosen stopping rules.
- Weakness: Some notation and definitions are incomplete (domains of f,G; dimensions of Λu,Λv); absence of state-evolution summary and performance regimes.

- Originality concern: Method is in AMP tradition but closely related to EP; need explicit comparison to EP (what matches/differs, role of Onsager terms) and relation to prior EP matrix factorization work.
- Originality concern: Need to sharpen novelty vs prior bilinear AMP variants and precisely state which cannot handle MAP and why the β extension is nontrivial.

- Significance: Clustering application is compelling with promising synthetic gains, but real-data evaluation is limited (only ORL) and comparisons are narrow.
- Significance: Recommend broader baselines (GMM/EM, spectral clustering, NMF), sensitivity analyses to prior/rank mis-specification, runtime/scalability plots, and more datasets.

- Pros for acceptance: Unifies marginalization and MAP via β, principled one-hot constraint handling, and empirically competitive with O(mN) iterations.
- Cons for acceptance: Sketchy technical derivations (scaling, β→∞, Proposition 1), limited real-data experiments, and unclear relation to EP and concurrent bilinear AMP work.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject in current form but promising; major revisions needed to expand derivations, clarify AMP vs EP, justify β→∞ MAP limit, discuss convergence strategies, and broaden empirical evaluation.
