- Paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization and connects it to clustering, with experiments comparing to k-means++.
- Mathematical derivations are incomplete and omit crucial steps, even in the appendix.
- The claim that the second term of (11) is O(1/√m) (line 139, Appendix) is unclear, and the omission of β in Section 2 is not justified.
- The asymptotic analysis (e.g., derivation of (19)) appears to assume m and n grow at the same rate but lacks detailed, rigorous justification.
- The statement that m τ_w^2 equals E[A_il^2] needs clarification because A is observed; m τ_w^2 more correctly represents the likelihood variance and the approximation must be justified.
- Convergence of the message-passing algorithm for finite β to the MAP solution is unclear; exchanging limit and minimization of KL is not justified.
- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and needs more explanation, especially the derivation of (17).
- Manuscript clarity is good: well-structured and the main idea is easy to grasp.
- Originality is questionable: the method appears to be essentially Expectation-Propagation applied to matrix factorization, overlapping prior work (e.g., Stern et al.).
- Appendix EP update procedures follow standard practice; authors should clarify novelty and consider connections to the EP “clutter” problem by using a uniform Gaussian mixture likelihood.
- Significance concerns: experimental comparison only to k-means++ is insufficient, and sketchy proofs make it hard to assess technical correctness.
