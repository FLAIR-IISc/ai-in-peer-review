- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a β-parameterized posterior interpolating between Bayesian inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).  
- Derives AMP updates for that family and claims extension of AMP to handle MAP and marginal-MAP estimators.  
- Specializes framework to clustering by encoding one-hot assignments on V, yielding an AMP variant of K-means with Onsager-like corrections for cluster sizes and self-bias.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and ORL faces show improved K-means objective, higher assignment accuracy for max-accuracy clustering, and fast convergence.  
- Connects to existing AMP for compressed sensing/bilinear models, variational Bayes matrix factorization, and low-rank/NMF clustering approaches.  
- Highlights direct enforcement of single-assignment constraints, differing from standard NMF relaxations.  

Strengths:
- Unifies posterior-mean and MAP objectives via the β-parameterized posterior with a coherent AMP algorithm applicable to discrete priors.  
- Clustering specialization provides principled corrections to Lloyd’s steps accounting for bias and cluster sizes.  
- Empirical results show promising gains over K-means/K-means++ with reasonable iteration counts.  

Major technical correctness concerns:
- Appendix derivation too terse: need explicit scaling showing the second term of Eq. (11) is O(1/√m), including how β enters.  
- Asymptotic assumptions unclear: must state and justify m,n growth rates (e.g., m/n→const) used in derivations like Eq. (19).  
- Replacement of empirical A_{iℓ}^2 by m τ_w^2 is unjustified: clarify that A is signal+noise and state conditions for this approximation.  
- β→∞ limit to obtain MAP is nontrivial: provide conditions/arguments ensuring β-continuation yields MAP fixed points (or specify only stationary-point correspondence).  
- Proposition 1 proof sketch insufficient: expand derivation of Eq. (17), the surrogate quadratic penalty, and conditions for Σ_i S_i and Σ_j T_j to be PSD in the β→∞ limit.  

Additional technical points requested:
- Provide a concise state-evolution statement, applicability conditions (random A, independence of U,V), or more empirical validation if omitted.  
- Discuss practical per-iteration complexity: O(mN) plus r̂-dimensional integrals/optimizations, and scalability with r̂.  
- Discuss stability methods (damping/line-search) needed in practice.  
- Justify and analyze the τ̂_t estimator for clustering, including scaling assumptions and robustness to mis-specification.  

Clarity and presentation issues:
- Ensure β is introduced and used consistently where it affects scaling.  
- Make m,N→∞ assumptions explicit early and used consistently throughout.  
- Expand appendix derivations to include omitted nontrivial steps and define all symbols at first use (e.g., τ_w).  
- State conditions under which priors may be improper and ensure posterior propriety.  

Originality and relation to prior work:
- Algorithm appears closely related to Expectation Propagation (EP) for matrix factorization; clarify precise differences from EP updates.  
- Need clearer differentiation from recent bilinear AMP methods (IterFac, bilinear GAMP) and prior EP-based recommender-system work.  
- For clustering, relate and contrast to Minka’s EP clutter problem and other mixture/assignment EP treatments.  

Significance and empirical scope:
- Clustering application is interesting but empirical evaluation is narrow and should include stronger baselines (EM for GMMs, spectral clustering, spherical K-means, NMF-based methods).  
- Recommend reporting sensitivity to initialization, mis-specified r̂/prior choices, and providing runtime comparisons (not just iteration counts).  
- Investigate and report on failure modes (e.g., collapsing to a single cluster) and remedies like damping or alternative τ estimation.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Conceptually clean unification of posterior mean, marginal-MAP, and MAP in an AMP framework.  
- Direct enforcement of single-assignment constraints with principled Onsager corrections.  
- Promising empirical improvements over K-means/K-means++ with few iterations.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Key derivations and asymptotic assumptions are sketchy and need expansion/justification.  
- Novelty relative to EP and existing bilinear AMP methods is not clearly delineated.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks breadth, robustness analyses, and runtime reporting.
