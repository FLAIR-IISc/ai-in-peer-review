- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering by reformulating clustering as a low-rank problem with exclusivity constraints.

- Extends prior AMP work (compressed sensing and rank-one cases) to general-rank matrix reconstruction, with the key novelty of enforcing that each data point belongs to exactly one cluster.

- The approach is theoretically motivated and appears well-grounded, with a clear derivation and accessible algorithm presentation.

- Asymptotic analysis (Section 2.1) assumes m and N grow at the same rate but lacks justification or discussion of practical implications.

- Appendix claim that the second term of (11) is O(1/√m) omits the β parameter, an important theoretical oversight.

- The connection between finite-β AMP and the MAP problem is not rigorously justified; the paper fails to show why limβ→∞ min KL = min limβ→∞ KL.

- Proof of Proposition 1 is under-detailed; the derivation of equation (17) is unexplained, preventing verification of convergence claims.

- Paper is overall well-structured and clearly written; algorithm presentation and experiments are accessible and logically presented.

- Main originality is extending AMP to general-rank clustering with exclusivity, but the novelty relative to EP-based matrix factorization is insufficiently clarified.

- The algorithm appears to implement EP-like updates; authors should acknowledge prior EP work (e.g., Stern et al. 2009) and discuss connections to the EP “clutter problem” (Minka 2001).

- Empirical results are promising, showing strong performance (especially in high dimensions) and outperforming K-means++ in tested scenarios.

- Experimental validation is limited: comparisons only to K-means++, no scalability analysis, and no comparison to other clustering or factorization methods.

- Theoretical results on convergence and performance are limited; stronger, more rigorous theoretical justification is needed to assess broader impact.

- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, theoretically motivated algorithm, insightful matrix–clustering connection, and clear writing.

- Arguments against acceptance: sketchy mathematical derivations, insufficient distinction from EP methods, limited experimental comparisons, and incomplete theoretical justification—recommend revision before acceptance.
