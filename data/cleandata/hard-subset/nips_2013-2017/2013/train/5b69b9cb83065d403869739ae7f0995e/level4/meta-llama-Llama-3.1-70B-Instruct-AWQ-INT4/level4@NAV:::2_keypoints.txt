- Paper proposes a novel BP-based Bayesian algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using large-N approximations (details in supplementary material).  
- Evaluation frames clustering as matrix factorization and compares to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF, which is a non-standard benchmark.  
- Using a non-standard task makes it hard to generalize results to conventional matrix reconstruction; authors should evaluate on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix).  
- Choice of evaluation raises concerns about whether BP’s advantages over variational methods are convincingly demonstrated under standard criteria.  
- Algorithm is essentially an application of BP to a problem previously addressed with Variational Message Passing, so novelty is limited.  
- The main technical novelty is the N,m → ∞ approximations, but their validity is not empirically tested and explanations are confined to the supplement.  
- Significant citation and scholarship issues: the link between matrix factorization and clustering is well-known and not adequately acknowledged.  
- The paper should cite and discuss Expectation Propagation and clarify its relation to the presented BP approach.  
- Pros: the algorithm is new and potentially useful, and the mathematics is mostly followable.  
- Experiments provide a fair K-means comparison and show benefits of the new algorithm in this setup.  
- Clarity: introduction and related work are clear, but derivation relegated to the supplement is a drawback and the mathematics is the weakest part.  
- Notation is cumbersome (excessive hats/tildes/sub/superscripts), inconsistent capitalization, nonstandard vector notation, and some undefined functions/equations.  
- Supplementary derivations contain unclear steps (e.g., transition from eq.14 to eq.15) and need clearer exposition.  
- Language is generally good but has grammatical/article usage issues.  
- Recommendations: evaluate on standard datasets, fix citation gaps, improve notation and exposition, define functions clearly, correct language, and add diagrams to aid understanding.  
- Overall: interesting first attempt to apply BP to matrix factorization but requires substantial revision on clarity and scholarship before acceptance.
