- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction under noisy observations that speeds up Bayesian methods.  
- Incorporates additional structural properties of the latent factors to increase modeling flexibility.  
- Establishes a novel connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering.  
- Applies AMP to K-means, enabling a comprehensive experimental evaluation in a new application domain.  
- Empirical comparisons to k-means and k-means++ show faster convergence and improved Frobenius-norm residual and clustering accuracy.  
- Main weakness: limited theoretical guarantees on the algorithm’s convergence.  
- Recommends citing relevant Theoretical Computer Science work that provides provable guarantees for K-means objectives.  
- Paper is technically sound with a clear, well-organized presentation of algorithm and experiments.  
- Originality is high due to the novel use of AMP and the new perspective linking matrix reconstruction and clustering.  
- Significance is notable because of the efficient practical performance, but theoretical limitations diminish overall impact.
