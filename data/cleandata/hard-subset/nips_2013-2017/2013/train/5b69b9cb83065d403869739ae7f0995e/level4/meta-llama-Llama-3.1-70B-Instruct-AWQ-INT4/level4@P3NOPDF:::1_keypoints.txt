- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for noisy low-rank matrix reconstruction with additional factor assumptions.
- Connects matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering, offering an application domain for the algorithm.
- Applying AMP to matrix reconstruction appears novel and interesting.
- Notes that the low-rank factorization—K-means relationship is known (e.g., PCA gives a factor-two approximation).
- Provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation leveraging the matrix–K-means connection.
- Empirically outperforms K-means and K-means++ in speed and efficiency according to reported results.
- Evaluates both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering accuracy, adding depth to experiments.
- Primary weakness: limited theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm.
- Suggests citing TCS literature on provably accurate K-means algorithms to strengthen theoretical context.
- Overall: solid empirical contribution demonstrating promise for AMP in low-rank reconstruction and K-means despite weaker theory.
