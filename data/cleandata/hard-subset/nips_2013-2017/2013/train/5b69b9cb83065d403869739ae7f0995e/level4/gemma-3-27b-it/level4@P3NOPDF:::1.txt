This paper presents an algorithm for matrix reconstruction under noisy observations, focusing on low-rank matrices with specific assumptions on their factors. The authors leverage an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the computationally expensive classical Bayesian methods typically used in this context. A particularly compelling aspect of the work is the connection drawn between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, opening up an interesting application domain for the proposed algorithms.

To the best of my knowledge, the application of AMP to matrix reconstruction represents a novel and worthwhile contribution. While the link between low-rank matrix factorizations and K-means is established – for instance, PCA provides a factor-two approximation for K-means – the authors skillfully utilize this connection to provide a strong experimental evaluation. This evaluation thoughtfully compares their algorithm against standard K-means and K-means++ implementations. The empirical results are promising, demonstrating that the proposed approach appears faster and more efficient than both classical K-means and K-means++. Importantly, the authors evaluate performance using both the Frobenius norm residual *and* the quality of the resulting clusterings, providing a comprehensive assessment. 

The primary weakness of the paper lies in the limited theoretical guarantees regarding the algorithm’s convergence. This is a common challenge in the field, as even many established K-means algorithms possess only weak theoretical properties. However, the authors could strengthen the paper by citing more relevant work from Theoretical Computer Science that focuses on provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function. Expanding the discussion to acknowledge the existing theoretical landscape would contextualize the current work and highlight the challenges inherent in obtaining strong guarantees for AMP-based methods. 

Overall, this is a solid paper presenting a promising AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and its application to K-means. The experimental evaluation is well-designed and convincing, despite the somewhat limited theoretical results. I believe this work represents a valuable contribution to the field and would recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the point regarding theoretical context as suggested above.
