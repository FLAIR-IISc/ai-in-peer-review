This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, demonstrating a connection to the clustering problem. The algorithm, derived for matrix factorization, is then specialized for clustering, and experimental results are presented comparing its performance to k-means++.

**Quality:** The mathematical derivations throughout the paper are often sketchy, omitting several non-trivial steps even within the appendix. 

1) In line 139 of the Appendix, the justification for the second term of equation (11) being O(1/√m) is unclear. The parameter β appears to be omitted from the discussion in Section 2, which impacts this analysis.
2) The analysis, including the derivation of equation (19), appears to assume that *m* and *n* grow at the same rate. The asymptotic analysis in this section is terse overall, and I have concerns regarding its mathematical rigor.
3) In line 167, the statement that mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup> represents the expectation of A<sub>il</sub><sup>2</sup> is questionable. Since A is observed data, mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup> is only the variance of the likelihood. The rationale for this approximation requires further verification.
4) It is unclear how the message passing algorithm, when derived for finite β, converges to the MAP problem. While message passing algorithms minimize KL divergence, the interchange of limit and minimization – that is, lim<sub>β→∞</sub> min<sub>q</sub> KL(q||p) = min<sub>β→∞</sub> lim<sub>q</sub> KL(q||p) – does not generally hold and should be justified.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed. Specifically, the derivation of equation (17) is not clearly explained.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured, and the core ideas are readily understandable.

**Originality:** The authors primarily focus on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior distribution. Consequently, it appears the proposed algorithm is essentially an application of Expectation Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. EP has been previously applied to matrix factorization, as demonstrated in Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Section 1 of the Appendix appears to describe standard EP update procedures; please clarify if this assessment is incorrect. 

Furthermore, there seems to be an interesting connection between the clustering algorithm proposed in this work and the clutter problem originally addressed in the EP paper by Minka (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), if the likelihood in the clutter problem is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. To clarify, I am offering this as a potential connection and not as a direct criticism of the paper’s originality.

**Significance:** The authors emphasize the application of their algorithm to clustering. However, given the extensive literature in clustering, comparison to k-means++ alone may be insufficient to establish the practical significance of this approach. The proofs, even those provided in the appendix, are very sketchy, making it difficult to evaluate the technical correctness of the proposed method. Additionally, I suspect that this work represents an application of expectation-propagation to matrix factorization, a technique already explored by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
