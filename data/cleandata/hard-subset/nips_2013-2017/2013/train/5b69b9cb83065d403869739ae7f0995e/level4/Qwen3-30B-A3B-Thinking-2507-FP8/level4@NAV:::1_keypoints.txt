- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations using additional structural assumptions on matrix factors.  
- Uses AMP to accelerate the classical Bayesian reconstruction approach, addressing computational bottlenecks.  
- Establishes a connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, presenting a novel application of the method.  
- Notes novelty and compelling contribution of applying AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction.  
- Recognizes the known relationship between low-rank factorizations and K-means but highlights that this enables direct experimental comparison with k-means and k-means++.  
- Empirical results show superior speed and efficiency versus k-means and k-means++, evaluated by Frobenius norm residual and clustering quality.  
- Primary limitation is the lack of strong theoretical convergence guarantees; recommends citing provable K-means algorithms from theoretical CS to strengthen the paper.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with promising experimental evaluation and modest theoretical foundations.
