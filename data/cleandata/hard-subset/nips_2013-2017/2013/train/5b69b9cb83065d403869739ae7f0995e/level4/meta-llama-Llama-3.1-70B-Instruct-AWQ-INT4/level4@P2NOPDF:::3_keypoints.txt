- Proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization and specializes it to clustering.  
- Experiments compare the proposed algorithm only to k-means++, limiting empirical evaluation.  
- Mathematical derivations are often incomplete, with crucial steps omitted even in the appendix.  
- The justification that the second term of Eq. (11) is O(1/√m) is unclear, and β is omitted in Section 2.  
- Asymptotic analyses (e.g., derivation of Eq. (19)) appear to assume m and n grow at the same rate but are terse and lack rigor.  
- The approximation E[A_il^2] ≈ m τ_w^2 (line 167) is unjustified since A is observed data and m τ_w^2 only reflects likelihood variance.  
- It is unclear how the finite-β message passing algorithm converges to the MAP problem; the β→∞ limit may not commute with KL minimization.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 is overly brief, and the derivation of Eq. (17) is not clearly explained.  
- Paper is well-structured and readable, but missing mathematical detail hinders understanding.  
- The approach appears to be an application of Expectation Propagation (EP) for matrix factorization, similar to Stern et al.  
- The clustering algorithm may relate to EP’s clutter problem (with a uniform Gaussian mixture likelihood), suggesting limited novelty.  
- Significance is limited by sparse experimental comparisons, lack of detailed proofs, and overlap with existing EP/matrix factorization literature.
