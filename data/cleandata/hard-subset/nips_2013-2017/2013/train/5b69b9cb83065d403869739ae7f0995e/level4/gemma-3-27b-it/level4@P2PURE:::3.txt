This paper introduces an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, demonstrating its applicability to clustering by specializing the derived algorithm. The authors then present experimental results comparing their approach to k-means++.

Quality: The mathematical derivations lack sufficient detail, omitting several non-trivial steps even in the appendix. 
1) In Appendix, line 139, the justification for the second term of equation (11) being O(1/\sqrt{m}) is unclear. The role of \beta appears to be undefined in Section 2. 
2) The analysis, including the derivation of equation (19), appears to rely on the assumption that *m* and *n* grow at similar rates. The asymptotic analysis in this section is generally terse, raising concerns regarding its mathematical rigor.
3) In line 167, the rationale for approximating the expectation of A<sub>il</sub><sup>2</sup> with *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* is not adequately explained. Since A is observed data, and *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* represents only the variance of the likelihood, this approximation requires further justification.
4) The convergence behavior of the message passing algorithm as \beta approaches infinity, and its relation to the maximum a posteriori (MAP) problem, is unclear. While message passing minimizes the KL divergence, the interchange of limit and minimization (\lim\beta \min KL = \min \lim\beta KL) is not guaranteed to hold and needs to be addressed.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed. Specifically, the derivation of equation (17) is not readily apparent.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured and the core ideas are generally easy to follow. 

Originality: The algorithm appears to primarily focus on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior. Consequently, it seems to be a direct application of Expectation Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization – a technique already explored in prior work, such as Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Section 1 of the appendix describes procedures consistent with standard EP updates; the authors should clarify if this interpretation is incorrect. 
Furthermore, there appears to be a connection between the authors’ clustering algorithm and the clutter problem originally addressed in the EP framework (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), if the likelihood of the clutter problem were to be replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. I offer this as a potential connection for consideration, and not as a direct criticism of the paper’s originality.

Significance: The authors primarily highlight the application to clustering; however, given the extensive existing literature in this field, comparison to only k-means++ may not be sufficient to establish the practical significance of their algorithm. The limited detail in the proofs, even in the appendix, hinders a thorough evaluation of the technical correctness of the proposed method. Moreover, the work appears to be an application of expectation-propagation to matrix factorization, a line of inquiry already pursued by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
