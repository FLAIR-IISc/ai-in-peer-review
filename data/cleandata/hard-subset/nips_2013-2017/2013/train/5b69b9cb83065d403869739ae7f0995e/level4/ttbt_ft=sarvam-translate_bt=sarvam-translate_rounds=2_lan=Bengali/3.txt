This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization. After showing that the matrix factorization problem can be viewed as a generalization of the clustering problem, the message passing algorithm obtained for matrix factorization is specifically designed for clustering. Finally, the proposed algorithm was tested to be compared with the k-means++ algorithm.

**Quality:** The mathematical derivations in this paper are very vague, and many important steps in the appendix have been omitted.

1) In line 139 of the appendix, why is the second term in (11) O(1/\sqrt(m))? It seems that \beta has been omitted in section 2.
2) In some analyses with the derivation of (19), it is assumed that m and n increase in the same order. Overall, the asymptotic analysis in this section is somewhat brief, and I am concerned about its mathematical rigor.
3) Why is m \tauw^2 expected to be A{il}^2 in line 167? A is the observed data, and m\tau_w^2 is only the variance of the probability; a reasonable reason for such an assumption should be verified.
4) How will the message passing algorithm obtained for finite \beta converge in the MAP problem? The message passing algorithm reduces KL divergence, but \lim\beta \min KL = \min \lim\beta KL may not always be true.
5) The proof of proposition 1 is too brief for me. How is (17) derived?

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured, and its main statement is not difficult to understand.

**Originality:** Since the authors are primarily interested in estimating the first and second moments of the marginal background, is the entire algorithm not an application of expectation propagation (EP) for matrix factorization? EP has already been used in matrix factorization in the following paper: http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf. Section 1 of the appendix seems to be the standard EP update method; please correct me if I am wrong.

Also, it seems that there should be an interesting connection between the authors' proposed clustering algorithm and the clustering problem in the main EP paper: http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf, if the probability of the clustering problem is transformed into a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. Just to clarify, in this paragraph, I am only suggesting a connection and not attacking the originality of the paper.

**Importance:** The authors primarily focused on its application to clustering, but considering the vast amount of literature on clustering, it may not be enough to prove the practical applicability of the algorithm by comparing it only with k-means++. The proof in this paper, even in the appendix, is very vague, and therefore it is difficult to assess its technical correctness. Also, I suspect that this is an application of expectation propagation for the matrix factorization problem, which has already been done by Stern et al. http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf
