- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy measurements with structural priors on factors.  
- Recasts K-means clustering as a special case of the low-rank reconstruction framework, yielding an AMP-based clustering method.  
- Applying AMP to this matrix reconstruction problem is novel and compelling.  
- The low-rank–K-means connection is known, but this perspective allows clear empirical comparisons to K-means and K-means++.  
- Empirically, the proposed method is faster and more effective than classical K-means and K-means++ in the reported experiments.  
- Experiments appropriately evaluate both Frobenius reconstruction error and clustering accuracy.  
- Main weakness is limited theoretical guarantees, particularly about convergence.  
- Paper would be stronger by citing additional theoretical CS results that provide provable guarantees for the K-means objective.  
- Overall: solid empirical contribution with promising results but comparatively weaker theoretical support.
