This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization. It demonstrates that matrix factorization can be viewed as a generalization of the clustering problem, and then specializes the derived message passing algorithm for application to clustering. The authors conclude with experiments comparing the proposed algorithm to k-means++.

**Quality:** The mathematical derivations in this paper are often very sketchy, omitting many non-trivial steps even in the appendix. 

1) In line 139 of the Appendix, the justification for the second term of equation (11) being O(1/√m) is unclear. It appears that β is omitted in Section 2, which impacts this derivation.
2) Several analyses, including the derivation of equation (19), seem to assume that *m* and *n* grow at the same rate. The asymptotic analysis in this section is generally terse, and I have concerns regarding its mathematical rigor.
3) In line 167, the reasoning behind equating E[A<sub>il</sub><sup>2</sup>] to *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* is not convincing. A is observed data, while *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* represents only the variance of the likelihood. The authors should justify this approximation.
4) It's unclear how the message passing algorithm, when derived for finite β, converges to the MAP problem. While message passing algorithms minimize KL divergence, the interchange of limit and minimum – lim<sub>β</sub> min KL = min lim<sub>β</sub> KL – doesn’t necessarily hold and requires justification.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief. How is equation (17) derived?

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and the main idea is relatively easy to follow.

**Originality:** The authors focus primarily on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior. Is the entire algorithm simply an application of Expectation Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization? EP has already been applied to matrix factorization in previous work (e.g., http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Section 1 of the Appendix appears to describe standard EP update procedures; please correct me if I am mistaken. 

Furthermore, there appears to be an interesting connection between the authors’ proposed clustering algorithm and the clutter problem originally addressed in EP (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), if the likelihood of the clutter problem is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. To clarify, I am suggesting a potential connection and not directly questioning the originality of the work.

**Significance:** The authors concentrate on the application of their algorithm to clustering. However, given the extensive existing literature on clustering, comparison solely to k-means++ may not be sufficient to demonstrate the practical advantages of the proposed algorithm. The proofs, even those provided in the appendix, are very sketchy, making it difficult to assess the technical correctness of the work. Additionally, I suspect that the work is essentially an application of expectation propagation to the matrix factorization problem, a connection that has already been explored by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
