- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations using additional structure on latent factors.  
- Applies AMP to accelerate a Bayesian reconstruction approach, which is novel in this setting.  
- Connects low-rank reconstruction to k-means clustering, giving a practical application domain.  
- Notes that links between low-rank factorizations and k-means are known but leveraging them enables a clear empirical study.  
- Empirical comparison against k-means and k-means++ shows favorable speed and efficiency for the proposed methods.  
- Evaluation uses both Frobenius-norm reconstruction error and clustering quality, strengthening the experimental evidence.  
- Main weakness is the lack of strong theoretical guarantees (convergence and optimality) for the proposed AMP approach.  
- Recommends broader discussion and citations to theoretical CS work on provable k-means guarantees.  
- Suggests that even partial theoretical analysis (convergence conditions or error bounds) would substantially improve the paper.  
- Overall assessment: solid and compelling empirical contribution with an interesting AMP-based method, but comparatively weak theoretical development.
