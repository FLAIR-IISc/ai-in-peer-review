- Evaluation uses a non-standard clustering-as-matrix-factorization task, making it hard to judge performance on conventional matrix reconstruction and lacking comparability to standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) or justification for their absence.  
- The algorithm is essentially a straightforward application of belief propagation to a problem previously handled with variational message passing, so novelty is limited.  
- Two approximations (limits N→∞ and m→∞) are interesting but their validity is not tested and explanations are relegated to the supplementary material and are unclear.  
- The paper misrepresents novelty: the matrix-factorization→clustering connection is well-known and prior work is not properly cited or acknowledged.  
- Expectation Propagation (EP) is not cited and the relationship between EP and the proposed BP-based algorithm is not clarified.  
- The fact that the algorithm is an application of BP is not stated prominently (only appears in Section 4.1) and should be highlighted in the abstract.  
- Pros: the proposed algorithm is engaging, potentially useful, and the experiments (within the chosen criterion) convincingly show benefits over baselines.  
- Key derivation is placed in supplementary material; a brief derivation outline should be included in the main paper.  
- Mathematical presentation is difficult to follow: notation is cumbersome (excess hats/tildes/subscripts/superscripts) and could be simplified for readability.  
- Specific equation/notation issues: factoring common terms in equations 9a–9e, combining terms in 9b/9e, explaining eq.5 and eq.8 relations, and clarifying functions in eq.10 and the scaled covariance Gβ.  
- The parameter β is inconsistently propagated through notation and should be handled more clearly (or removed from redundant subscripts).  
- The role and significance of the multiplicative m factor in the additive noise variance is unclear and appears to break row–column symmetry; this should be addressed.  
- Some figures/plots are hard to interpret in black and white; include more diagrams (the supplementary Fig.1 is helpful) in the main paper.  
- Notational and stylistic questions: clarify use of tilde for column vectors, capitalization choice for N vs m, and provide a clear citation for VBMF-MA implementation.  
- Supplementary material needs clearer exposition: represent z-related terms as exponentials in eq.5, clarify big-O step from eq.14→15, and state the novelty of the derivation.  
- Language/grammar issues throughout (many small phrasing fixes suggested); propose retitling to mention "approximate message passing algorithm."  
- Conclusion: interesting and potentially important first BP application to matrix factorization, but requires substantial improvements in clarity, notation, citations, and validation before acceptance.
