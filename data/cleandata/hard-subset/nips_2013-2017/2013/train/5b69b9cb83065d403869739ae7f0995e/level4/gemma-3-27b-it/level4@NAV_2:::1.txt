The authors address the problem of reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations, formulating it within a Bayesian framework to incorporate structural properties beyond rank, such as sparsity. They propose an efficient approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm derived from belief propagation for Bayesian inference in this context. A key contribution is extending existing AMP algorithms to handle MAP estimation, and they successfully apply the method to K-means clustering by reformulating it as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem. Numerical experiments demonstrate performance gains over Lloyd’s K-means algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional data.

This work presents a novel and interesting application of the Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to low-rank matrix reconstruction. While the connection between low-rank matrix factorization and K-means clustering is established in prior work (e.g., [10, 11] and PCA-based approximations), the authors’ specific formulation and AMP-based algorithm offer a compelling alternative. The experimental evaluation is well-designed, comparing the proposed methods with established algorithms like K-means [12] and K-means++ [16] on both synthetic and real-world (ORL faces dataset [20]) data. Reporting both the Frobenius norm residual and clustering accuracy provides a comprehensive assessment of performance.

A potential limitation of the paper lies in the limited theoretical guarantees regarding the convergence of the proposed AMP algorithm. This is a common challenge with iterative algorithms for non-convex optimization, including K-means. However, strengthening the theoretical analysis, or at least discussing the conditions under which convergence is likely, would enhance the contribution. For instance, citing related work providing convergence guarantees for similar iterative schemes [15] or provably accurate algorithms for K-means (even if not directly applicable) could strengthen the discussion. 

Overall, this is a solid paper presenting a promising AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering. The empirical results are encouraging, and the method demonstrates practical benefits in terms of efficiency and accuracy.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural priors.
*   Extension of existing AMP algorithms to MAP estimation.
*   Successful application to K-means clustering with demonstrated performance improvements.
*   Comprehensive experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real-world data.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited theoretical analysis of algorithm convergence.
*   Potential for improved discussion of related theoretical work addressing convergence of similar algorithms.
