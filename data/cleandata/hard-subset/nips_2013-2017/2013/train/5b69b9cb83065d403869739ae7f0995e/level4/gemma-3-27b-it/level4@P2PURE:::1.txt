This paper introduces an algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations, leveraging Approximate Message Passing (AMP) to accelerate the traditionally computationally demanding Bayesian approach. The authors further establish a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, offering a compelling application area for their method. 

To my knowledge, the application of AMP to matrix reconstruction is a novel and valuable contribution. While the relationship between low-rank matrix factorization and K-means is established—for example, PCA provides a two-factor approximation for K-means—this work facilitates a robust experimental evaluation, enabling comparisons with standard K-means and K-means++ algorithms. Notably, the empirical results suggest that the proposed approach is both faster and more efficient than these classical K-means methods. The authors’ evaluation is strengthened by their consideration of both Frobenius norm residuals and clustering accuracy.

The primary limitation of this paper lies in the limited theoretical guarantees regarding the algorithm’s convergence. This is not uncommon, as many K-means algorithms also lack strong theoretical foundations. However, the authors could benefit from citing more work from Theoretical Computer Science that develops provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective. Overall, this is a solid contribution presenting AMP algorithms for both low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, with promising experimental validation and comparatively weaker theoretical results.
