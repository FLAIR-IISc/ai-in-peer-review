- Introduces an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, specialized to clustering and compared to k-means++.
- Mathematical derivations lack sufficient detail; several non-trivial steps omitted even in the appendix.
- Appendix (line 139): justification for second term of Eq.(11) being O(1/√m) is unclear; β is undefined in Section 2.
- Asymptotic analysis (including derivation of Eq.(19)) appears to assume m and n grow at similar rates and is presented tersely, raising rigor concerns.
- Line 167: unjustified approximation of E[A_il^2] with m τ_w^2, since A is observed and τ_w^2 denotes likelihood variance.
- Convergence as β→∞ and connection to the MAP problem are unclear; interchange of limit and minimization is not justified.
- Proof of Proposition 1 is under-detailed; derivation of Eq.(17) is not apparent.
- Paper is well-structured and core ideas are generally easy to follow.
- Method seems to focus on estimating first and second posterior moments and may be a direct application of Expectation Propagation (EP).
- Possible connection to the EP “clutter” problem (with a uniform Gaussian mixture likelihood) should be clarified.
- Experimental comparison only to k-means++ is limited and may not demonstrate practical significance given existing clustering literature.
- Overlap with prior EP-based matrix factorization work (e.g., Stern et al.) calls the paper’s originality into question.
