This work introduces an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization. The authors begin by framing the matrix factorization problem as a generalization of clustering, then derive a message passing algorithm for the former and subsequently specialize it to the latter. The paper concludes with experiments comparing the proposed clustering algorithm against the k-means++ baseline.

**Quality**
The mathematical derivations throughout the paper and its appendix are presented in a condensed manner, frequently omitting significant intermediate steps.

1.  In the Appendix (line 139), the justification for the second term of equation (11) being O(1/√m) is not provided. Furthermore, the parameter β appears to be absent from the problem formulation in Section 2.
2.  Several analyses, such as the derivation of equation (19), seem to presuppose that m and n grow at a similar rate. The asymptotic analysis in this section is generally terse, which raises concerns about its mathematical rigor.
3.  The rationale for approximating the expectation of A{il}^2 with mτw^2 (line 167) is unclear. Given that A is observed data and mτw^2 represents the variance of the likelihood, this approximation requires explicit justification.
4.  It is not clear how the message passing algorithm, derived for a finite β, converges to the Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) solution. Message passing algorithms typically minimize KL divergence, but the interchange of the limit and the minimization operator (i.e., limβ min KL = min limβ KL) is not guaranteed to hold and needs to be addressed.
5.  The proof of Proposition 1 is overly brief. The derivation of equation (17), in particular, lacks the necessary detail for verification.

**Clarity**
The paper is well-organized, and its central thesis is presented in a way that is straightforward to understand.

**Originality**
A key question regarding the paper's novelty is whether the proposed algorithm constitutes a new contribution or is an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. The methodology, especially the update procedures detailed in Section 1 of the Appendix, appears to align with standard EP. If this is the case, the work's originality is diminished by prior research that has already applied EP to matrix factorization (e.g., Stern et al., http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). I would appreciate clarification on this point.

Additionally, there may be an interesting connection between the authors' clustering algorithm and the clutter problem described in the foundational EP paper by Minka (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood in the clutter problem is modeled as a uniform mixture of Gaussians. This is offered as a potential point of discussion rather than a criticism of the work's originality.

**Significance**
The paper's impact is not fully demonstrated. The primary application is focused on clustering, yet the empirical evaluation is limited to a comparison with k-means++, which may not be sufficient to establish its practical utility in such a well-researched field. Furthermore, the technical correctness of the work is difficult to evaluate due to the sketchy nature of the proofs, even within the appendix. This, combined with the concern that the method may be a direct application of EP to matrix factorization as previously explored by Stern et al., tempers the overall significance of the contribution.
