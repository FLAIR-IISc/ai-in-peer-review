- Introduces a Bayesian AMP framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction by factoring A0 = U0V0^T and placing priors on U0 and V0.  
- Uses AMP to perform approximate Bayesian inference, unifying marginal posterior estimation and MAP via a temperature-like parameter β.  
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem and derives a specialized AMP algorithm for clustering.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and ORL face dataset show the AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ in loss and accuracy.  
- Strength: Novel extension of AMP from compressed sensing to general low-rank matrix reconstruction.  
- Strength: Flexible Bayesian formulation allows incorporation of structural priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity) via p̂u and p̂v.  
- Strength: Elegant unified treatment of marginalization and MAP using the β parameter.  
- Strength: Clustering update rules intuitively correct for self-influence and appear effective at avoiding poor local minima.  
- Weakness: Mathematical derivations are overly condensed, omitting crucial details needed to verify correctness.  
- Weakness: Asymptotic analysis lacks explicit assumptions and justification (scaling of m and N, O(1/√m) claims).  
- Weakness: Approximation and estimator for noise variance τ in the clustering derivation are not justified or analyzed for impact on performance/convergence.  
- Weakness: Claim that finite-β solutions converge to MAP as β→∞ is not formally justified; interchange of limits is nontrivial.  
- Weakness: Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and omits the derivation of a key reformulation (Eq. 16).  
- Weakness: Connection to Expectation Propagation (EP) is not discussed despite strong resemblance to EP updates; novelty may be overstated.  
- Weakness: Possible links to Minka’s EP "clutter" problem are unexamined and could provide useful context.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is narrow—comparison only to Lloyd’s and K-means++; missing baselines like GMM/EM or spectral clustering.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, flexible AMP-based approach with promising clustering results and clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: substantial concerns about technical rigor, unaddressed relation to EP, and limited experimental scope; reviewer would reconsider if authors address proofs, EP connections, and broaden experiments.
