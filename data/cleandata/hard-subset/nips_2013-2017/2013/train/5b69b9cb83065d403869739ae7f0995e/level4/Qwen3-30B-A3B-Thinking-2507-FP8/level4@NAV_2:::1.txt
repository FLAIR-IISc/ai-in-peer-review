

The authors present a novel approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with additional structural constraints on the factor matrices. The paper formulates the reconstruction problem within a Bayesian framework, allowing for flexible incorporation of structural properties like sparsity, and derives an efficient AMP-based inference method that addresses both marginalization and MAP estimation problems. The authors further demonstrate a novel application of their approach to K-means clustering by reformulating the clustering problem as a constrained low-rank matrix reconstruction task, which is distinct from previous work that typically treats clustering as a separate problem. Their experimental evaluation compares the proposed AMP-based clustering algorithm against Lloyd's K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes methods using both artificial and real datasets, evaluating performance through multiple metrics including normalized K-means loss, clustering accuracy, and convergence speed.

The paper makes a significant contribution by extending AMP algorithms to general-rank matrix reconstruction, which has not been previously addressed in the literature. The connection between low-rank matrix factorization and clustering is well-established (e.g., PCA provides a factor-two approximation for K-means), but the authors' AMP-based approach to this relationship represents a meaningful advancement. The experimental results are particularly compelling, showing that the AMP algorithm for K-means clustering achieves lower K-means loss than competing methods while demonstrating faster convergence than variational Bayes approaches, even when achieving comparable accuracy. The comprehensive evaluation that includes both Frobenius norm residuals and actual cluster assignments is a notable strength.

The primary limitation concerns the theoretical analysis of convergence properties. While the paper provides a fixed-point analysis for the MAP problem (Proposition 1), it lacks broader theoretical guarantees for convergence in general cases, which is expected given the non-convex nature of the problem. The authors could strengthen their theoretical foundation by citing more recent work in theoretical computer science that provides provably accurate algorithms for K-means clustering, particularly those addressing the specific challenges of high-dimensional data. Despite this limitation, the paper presents a well-motivated approach with solid experimental validation, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Strengths: Novel AMP-based approach for low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural constraints; clear connection between matrix reconstruction and clustering; comprehensive experimental evaluation with multiple performance metrics; promising empirical results showing improved performance over existing methods.

Weaknesses: Limited theoretical convergence guarantees beyond the fixed-point analysis; could strengthen theoretical foundation with additional citations to theoretical computer science literature on provably accurate K-means algorithms.
