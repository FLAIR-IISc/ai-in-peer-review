- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction from noisy observations, leveraging structural assumptions on matrix factors.  
- Establishes a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering and applies the method to clustering tasks.  
- Novel application of AMP to matrix reconstruction, representing an interesting methodological contribution.  
- Strong empirical performance: often faster and more efficient than Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation using both Frobenius norm residual and clustering accuracy.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation of the algorithm and experiments.  
- Limited theoretical guarantees on convergence; lacks rigorous analysis of when the algorithm converges.  
- Potential to get stuck in local minima, a common issue for K-means-like algorithms.  
- Suggestion to cite provable K-means algorithms or discuss conditions for convergence to strengthen the paper.  
- Overall assessment: solid, promising work with strong experiments but weakened by limited theoretical analysis.
