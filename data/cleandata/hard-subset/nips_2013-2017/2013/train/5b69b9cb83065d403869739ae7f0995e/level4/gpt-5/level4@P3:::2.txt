Summary
The paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on the factors, and introduces a temperature-like parameter β that unifies posterior marginalization (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). Compared to prior AMP work on low-rank/bilinear models, the authors argue their algorithm extends to MAP estimation and accommodates discrete/structured priors. As a second contribution, they recast K-means clustering as a constrained low-rank factorization with one-hot rows in V, yielding an AMP-based algorithm for K-means and a “maximum-accuracy” clustering method based on approximate marginal MAP. Experiments on synthetic data and the ORL Faces dataset show improved K-means loss and, in many cases, accuracy over Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++, and gains over a variational Bayes matrix factorization (VBMF) baseline.

Relation to prior work
AMP and generalized AMP (GAMP) have been widely studied for linear and bilinear models (e.g., Rangan 2011; IterFac for rank-1; Matsushita & Tanaka 2012; Parker et al. 2013; Krzakala et al. 2013). The paper’s positioning would benefit from a clearer comparison to bilinear GAMP and IterFac, emphasizing what is genuinely new (e.g., the β-parameterized bridge to MAP and the ability to handle discrete priors for clustering). The reduction of clustering to matrix factorization is well known in the NMF/K-means literature (e.g., Xu et al. 2003; Ding et al. 2005/2010); the paper should acknowledge this earlier and more prominently. Finally, the algorithm’s connection to belief propagation and the Bethe free energy is relevant, and the relationship to expectation propagation (EP; Minka 2001) should be discussed.

Strengths
- A principled AMP formulation that unifies marginalization and MAP via β, with explicit update equations and a useful optimality proposition at fixed points.
- An elegant re-interpretation of K-means within the AMP framework, leading to intuitive corrections in the assignment step (accounting for center bias and cluster-size effects).
- Empirical gains over Lloyd’s K-means/K-means++ on synthetic and ORL data; AMP-based marginal methods converge quickly and achieve high accuracy in the synthetic setting.
- Computation per iteration scales as O(mN), and the symmetry of the updates makes the method broadly implementable with many priors.

Weaknesses and concerns
Quality
- The derivation and key approximations are relegated to the supplementary material, and their validity is not empirically stress-tested (e.g., by ablation or synthetic checks of the large-system assumptions). Given the reliance on m,N→∞ arguments, some validation is needed.
- The experimental evaluation focuses on clustering. There are no results on standard matrix factorization/completion benchmarks (e.g., Netflix/MovieLens), which makes it difficult to gauge competitiveness on canonical tasks where VBMF and PMF are typically evaluated. If such datasets are unsuitable for the proposed model, the paper should explain why.
- Convergence properties and the role of β are not analyzed (no state evolution or even a brief summary). Practical guidance on β, rank mis-specification, and τ estimation is limited.

Clarity
- Notation is heavy (excessive hats/tilde/superscripts). Several equations could be simplified for readability:
  - Factor out common constants (e.g., 1/(mτ)) in (9a–9d) and combine repeated terms.
  - Use consistent symbols (e.g., τ vs τw); avoid unnecessary hats where no ambiguity arises; consider using assignment arrows and dropping pervasive t superscripts.
  - Explain early that (5) is the negative log of (2), and that (8) is p̂(U,V|A) raised to β.
  - State explicitly near (10–11) that f is the mean and G is the β-scaled covariance of q̂; then later reference this rather than re-deriving.
  - Point out the near row–column symmetry of Algorithm 2 to reduce duplication.
- The significance of the m factor in the noise variance should be motivated (scaling for state evolution? symmetry breaking?) and its practical consequences clarified.

Originality
- The β-parameterized AMP that covers both marginalization and MAP and the concrete treatment of one-hot constraints for clustering appear novel and useful. However, the paper should more clearly differentiate from bilinear GAMP and IterFac and explain what aspects (e.g., MAP with general/discrete priors, specific Onsager terms) are new.

Significance
- The clustering application is promising and may be of broad interest. That said, without results on standard matrix factorization datasets, the impact on low-rank reconstruction per se is unclear. Strengthening the empirical section would substantially increase the paper’s significance.

Specific questions for the authors
- What is the role of the m factor in the noise variance beyond scaling for the large-system limit? Does it materially affect symmetry or performance?
- How sensitive is performance to τ estimation and to rank mis-specification (r̂ ≠ r)?
- Can you clarify the novelty relative to bilinear GAMP (Parker et al., 2013), especially regarding MAP estimation and the extra terms in (9a, 9c)?
- Which VBMF implementation was used for VBMF-MA? Please cite the precise variant and hyperparameter settings.
- Can you provide any empirical checks of the approximations (e.g., comparing with exact BP on tiny problems or showing that the Onsager corrections materially improve over variational updates)?

Suggestions for improvement
- Add at least one standard low-rank reconstruction experiment (or justify its omission), and report posterior mean and MAP quality where applicable.
- Include a short derivation outline in the main text and a brief state-evolution summary, with references.
- Improve notation and presentation as outlined above; unify τ; reduce hats; factor constants; emphasize symmetry.
- Cite earlier clustering-as-factorization work more prominently (e.g., Xu et al., Ding et al.), and discuss the link to EP and Bethe approximations.
- Clarify the relation to prior AMP/bilinear methods and highlight the genuinely new components.
- Add a brief discussion of convergence behavior (oscillations, stopping criteria) and guidance on β.
- Minor language edits throughout (articles, phrasing) and more informative figure captions; consider adding schematic diagrams similar to the helpful figure in the supplement.

Arguments for acceptance
- Conceptually clean AMP formulation that unifies marginalization and MAP with flexible priors.
- A practical and effective clustering algorithm with clear empirical benefits and low per-iteration cost.
- Potentially useful beyond clustering, given the generality of the priors and updates.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited empirical validation on standard matrix factorization tasks; unclear competitiveness versus established baselines in that regime.
- Heavy reliance on supplementary derivations with limited testing of approximations; missing state-evolution discussion.
- Presentation/notation issues and incomplete scholarship on prior work (clustering-as-factorization, EP, bilinear GAMP).

Overall
This is an interesting and potentially useful contribution, especially for clustering with structured priors, but it needs stronger positioning relative to existing AMP/bilinear methods, clearer exposition, and a broader experimental evaluation to meet the bar for NIPS. I would encourage a revision addressing the points above.
