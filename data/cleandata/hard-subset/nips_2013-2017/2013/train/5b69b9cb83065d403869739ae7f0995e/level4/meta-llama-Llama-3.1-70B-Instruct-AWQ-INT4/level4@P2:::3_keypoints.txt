- Proposes an AMP algorithm for matrix factorization and its specialization to clustering, with experiments vs k-means++.

- Mathematical derivations are sketchy, omitting non-trivial steps and lacking rigor throughout (even in appendix).

- Specific unclear approximations: second term in Eq.(11) as O(1/√m), omission/role of β, and terse asymptotic analysis.

- Expectation approximations (e.g., E[A_{il}^2] ≈ mτw^2) are unjustified and not verified.

- Unclear how the finite-β message passing algorithm converges to the MAP problem; Prop.1 proof is too brief and unclear.

- Paper is well-structured and the main idea is graspable, but mathematical clarity and proof rigor need improvement.

- Appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation to matrix factorization; connection to prior EP work is not clearly established.

- Potential link between the clustering method and the “clutter” problem from EP literature is not explored.

- Evaluation is limited (only compared to k-means++), so practical usefulness and technical correctness are insufficiently demonstrated.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel application of AMP to clustering and promising experimental results vs k-means++.

- Arguments against acceptance: lack of mathematical rigor, weak connection to prior EP work, insufficient evaluation, and unclear overall significance.
