- Introduces a BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using asymptotic approximations (detailed in the Supplementary Material).  
- Evaluates the method by casting clustering as matrix reconstruction and compares against Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.  
- Evaluation choice (clustering formulation) is non-standard and hinders comparison to prior work; a standard dataset (e.g., Netflix) should be included or justified.  
- The algorithm is a relatively straightforward BP application to a problem previously treated by Variational Message Passing, limiting perceived novelty.  
- The asymptotic approximations for m,N → ∞ are only in the supplement and lack direct validation or clear explanation in the main text.  
- Critical citation omissions: the matrix factorization–clustering link is not acknowledged as prior work, EP is not cited, and the BP framing should be stated in the abstract.  
- Strength: the algorithm is interesting and potentially useful; mathematics is generally followable and K-means experiments show clear benefit.  
- Major presentation flaw: core algorithm derivation is relegated to the Supplementary Material rather than the main paper.  
- Some experimental figures lack clarity when printed in black-and-white.  
- Mathematical exposition problems: unexplained factors (e.g., 1/m τ_w), redundant and inconsistent notation (excess hats, t-superscripts, τ_w vs τ), and unclear equation relationships.  
- Definitions and functions (e.g., Eq. 10, G_β) are ambiguous, and β subscripting is unjustified and inconsistently applied.  
- Conceptual gaps: unexplained role of the m factor in noise variance and potential symmetry breaking; odd notation choices (tilde for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization of N vs m).  
- Section 5 needs a proper citation for VBMF-MA implementation; existing variational MF citations are ambiguous.  
- Language and minor grammatical errors are frequent and distracting; several specific phrasing corrections are suggested.  
- Supplementary Material’s Figure 1 is helpful but more diagrams would improve clarity.  
- Conclusion: promising first BP application to matrix factorization, but the paper requires substantial revisions on clarity, notation, citations, and experimental justification before acceptance.
