- Presents a novel AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction derived from Belief Propagation, extending AMP to general-rank and MAP estimation.  
- Applies the algorithm to clustering by reformulating clustering as a structured low-rank matrix factorization.  
- Strong empirical results: AMP-based clustering outperforms K-means, K-means++, and VBMF on synthetic and real datasets (lower K-means loss, higher accuracy).  
- Mathematical derivation appears plausible and grounded in statistical physics/information theory, though not independently replicated.  
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on clustering, lacking standard low-rank benchmarks (e.g., Netflix), making broader competitiveness unclear.  
- Positioning risks being seen as incremental; framing clustering-as-factorization as novel is misleading because the connection is known.  
- Important related work is missing or mis-positioned: Expectation Propagation and BP foundations should be discussed and highlighted earlier.  
- Main-text derivation is deferred to supplements, leaving insufficient intuition and a dense, hard-to-parse Algorithm 2 presentation.  
- Notation is cumbersome (excessive hats/tildes/superscripts); simple typographic simplifications would improve readability.  
- Mathematical steps and roles of functions (e.g., f and G) are not explained where introduced, reducing clarity.  
- Key technical aspects are left unexplained—e.g., the role and significance of the factor m in the noise variance and symmetry breaking.  
- Frequent minor English/style errors and awkward phrasing detract from the paper’s presentation.  
- Overall: interesting and empirically strong work but requires major revisions on evaluation scope, positioning/citations, and clarity before acceptance.
