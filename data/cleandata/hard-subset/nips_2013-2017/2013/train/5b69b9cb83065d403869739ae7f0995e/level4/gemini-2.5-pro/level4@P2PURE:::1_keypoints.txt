- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm to speed up Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction under specific factor assumptions.  
- Connects low-rank matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering, presenting K-means as an application domain.  
- Application of AMP to matrix reconstruction appears novel and interesting.  
- Leverages the known link between low-rank factorization and K-means (e.g., PCA 2-approximation).  
- Strong experimental evaluation comparing against K-means and K-means++, showing the proposed method is faster and more efficient.  
- Evaluation measures both Frobenius norm residual and resulting cluster quality, adding thoroughness.  
- Main weakness: lacks theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm.  
- Suggestion: better situate work by citing theoretical CS literature on provable K-means algorithms.  
- Overall: solid paper with a novel algorithm and promising experiments but relatively weak theoretical results.
