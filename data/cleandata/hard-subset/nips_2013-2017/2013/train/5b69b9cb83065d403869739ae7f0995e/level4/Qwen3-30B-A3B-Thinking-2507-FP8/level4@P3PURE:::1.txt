

The authors present a novel algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction under noisy observations, leveraging Approximate Message Passing (AMP) to accelerate the computationally intensive Bayesian approach. The work incorporates specific structural assumptions on the matrix factors and establishes a meaningful connection to K-means clustering, providing a compelling application domain for the proposed method.  

To the best of my knowledge, the application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction is novel and well-motivated. While the link between low-rank factorizations and K-means (e.g., via PCA as a factor-2 approximation for K-means) is well-established, the authors effectively utilize this connection to enable a thorough experimental evaluation. Their comparison against k-means and k-means++ demonstrates significant speed and efficiency gains in empirical results. The evaluation is strengthened by reporting both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering performance, offering a comprehensive assessment beyond standard metrics.  

The primary limitation is the lack of strong theoretical guarantees regarding convergence for the proposed AMP-based algorithm. This is a common challenge in K-means literature, but the authors should address it by citing relevant theoretical computer science work on provably accurate K-means algorithms. A solid contribution overall, with promising experimental results, though theoretical guarantees remain limited.
