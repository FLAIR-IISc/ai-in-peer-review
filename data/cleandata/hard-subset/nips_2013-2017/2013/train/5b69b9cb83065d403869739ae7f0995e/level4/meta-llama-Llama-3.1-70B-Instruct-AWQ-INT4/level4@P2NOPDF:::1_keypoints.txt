- Proposes an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for noisy low-rank matrix reconstruction with specific factor assumptions.  
- Establishes a novel connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, providing an application domain.  
- Applying AMP to matrix reconstruction appears novel and intriguing.  
- Notes the known relation between low-rank factorizations and K-means (e.g., PCA approximation), framing the approach.  
- Provides comprehensive experimental comparisons to K-means and K-means++, showing faster and more efficient performance.  
- Empirical evaluation measures include Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering quality.  
- Primary limitation: scarce theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm.  
- Suggests citing theoretical CS work that gives provable algorithms for the K-means objective to strengthen the paper.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution to AMP for low-rank reconstruction and K-means with promising experiments but limited theory.
