- Proposes a new BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction; math is readable and the method is potentially useful and improves on existing methods in experiments.  
- Novel evaluation framing: formulates clustering as matrix reconstruction and compares against Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.  
- Major concern: evaluation on a non-standard problem (clustering-as-matrix-factorization) makes it hard to predict performance on conventional reconstruction tasks; authors should use or justify not using standard datasets (e.g., Netflix).  
- The algorithm may be a relatively straightforward application of BP to a problem previously treated with Variational Message Passing, so the novelty/impact is limited.  
- Important large-N, large-m approximations are presented only in the supplement, never directly tested; their validity needs clearer explanation and empirical verification.  
- Serious citation/claim issue: paper implies matrix-factorization-for-clustering is novel without citing prior work or stating it is well-known.  
- Key derivation relegated to supplementary material; the main paper should at least outline the derivation for clarity.  
- Mathematical presentation has weaknesses and hard-to-parse expressions (e.g., unexplained 1/(m τ_w) factors in equations 9a/9b/9d/9e).  
- Notation is cluttered with excessive hats, tildes, subscripts/superscripts and unnecessary time indices; simplify or explain notation choices.  
- Specific supplementary issues: eq.5 would be clearer as a product of exponentials; step from eq.14 to eq.15 (big-O in m) is unclear.  
- Language and exposition need polishing (article use, title phrasing) to avoid distracting readers.  
- Recommendation summary: improve clarity and exposition, fix citation omissions, justify/validate approximations and evaluation choices, and consider standard benchmark datasets.
