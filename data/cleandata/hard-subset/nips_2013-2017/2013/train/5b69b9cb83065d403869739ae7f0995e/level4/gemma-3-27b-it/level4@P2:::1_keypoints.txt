- Introduces an AMP algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations using known structural priors on factors.
- Formulates the problem in a Bayesian framework to leverage structural properties for reconstruction.
- Extends existing AMP methods to handle general rank and enables MAP estimation, which prior work [9] lacked.
- Novel application: reformulates K-means clustering as a low-rank reconstruction problem enforcing exclusive cluster assignment.
- Empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, particularly in high-dimensional settings.
- Technically sound with clear connections to AMP literature [6,7,8].
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, using Frobenius reconstruction error and clustering accuracy, and comparing to K-means and K-means++ [16].
- Experiments include both synthetic data and real-world data (ORL face database [20]), strengthening empirical validation.
- Lacks detailed theoretical convergence guarantees; this omission weakens the theoretical contribution.
- Algorithm derivation is dense and would benefit from clearer exposition of key steps and assumptions.
- Insufficient comparison with variational Bayes approaches [4,5]; needs deeper discussion of AMP’s advantages (e.g., capturing dependencies).
- Recommend citing theoretical CS work on provable approximation algorithms for K-means to provide additional context.
