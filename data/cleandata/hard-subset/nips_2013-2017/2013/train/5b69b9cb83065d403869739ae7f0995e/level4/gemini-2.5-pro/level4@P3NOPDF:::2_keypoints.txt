- Introduces a new BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with two large-system approximations and evaluates it by framing clustering as matrix reconstruction.  
- Evaluation is unconventional and insufficiently justified, preventing comparison to standard benchmarks (e.g., Netflix) and leaving competitiveness unclear.  
- Paper fails to properly cite and position prior work: the clustering–matrix factorization connection is presented as novel, EP is not discussed, and BP use is not highlighted early.  
- Technical novelty is limited—the method is largely a straightforward BP application—and the large-N/m approximations are described only in the supplement and not validated.  
- The algorithm itself is a valuable contribution as one of the first BP applications to this class of matrix factorization problems.  
- Experimental results are promising within the chosen framework, outperforming K-means and VBMF on the clustering task.  
- The paper’s introduction and motivation are well-written and the core mathematical framework is broadly followable.  
- Key derivations should be moved into or outlined in the main text rather than relegated to supplementary material.  
- The mathematical exposition is hard to parse; connections between equations (e.g., Eq5, Eq2, Eq8) must be made explicit.  
- Notation is overly cumbersome—remove unnecessary hats/tildes/subscripts, factor common terms, and simplify time superscripts (use “←” for updates).  
- Functions and quantities (e.g., f_β, G_β) need prose explanations earlier, not late in the text.  
- Clarify modeling choices such as the role of the factor m in the noise variance and its effect on row–column symmetry.  
- Provide a specific citation for the VBMF-MA implementation used in comparisons.  
- Explain the step from Eq14 to Eq15 (O(m) terms) more clearly and clarify the novelty of the message approximation relative to prior work.  
- Proofread English for missing articles and minor language issues.  
- Conclusion: idea and results are promising but the paper requires substantial revision on evaluation, citation/positioning, and technical clarity before acceptance.
