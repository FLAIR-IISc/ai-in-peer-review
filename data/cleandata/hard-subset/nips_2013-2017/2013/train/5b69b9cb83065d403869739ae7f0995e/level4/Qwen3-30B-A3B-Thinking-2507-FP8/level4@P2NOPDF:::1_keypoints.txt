- Introduces an AMP algorithm for noisy low-rank matrix reconstruction using structural assumptions to speed Bayesian methods.  
- Establishes a link between matrix reconstruction and k-means clustering, leveraging known PCA–k-means relations.  
- Enables a compelling empirical comparison against standard clustering baselines.  
- Shows significant computational advantages over k-means and k-means++ (faster convergence, competitive clustering quality).  
- Evaluates both Frobenius norm residual and actual cluster assignments for comprehensive performance assessment.  
- AMP framework is a novel contribution with an experimental design that highlights practical utility.  
- Speed and efficiency gains suggest applicability to large-scale, computationally constrained settings.  
- Theoretical foundation is underdeveloped; paper lacks discussion of existing convergence or provable guarantees for AMP in this setting.  
- Authors should cite recent provable K-means/approximation results from TCS to better situate the work.  
- Experiments are strong but need additional scalability tests on larger datasets and comparisons with modern baselines (spectral, deep-learning methods).  
- Overall promising and acceptable with minor revisions focused on strengthening theoretical context and convergence discussion.
