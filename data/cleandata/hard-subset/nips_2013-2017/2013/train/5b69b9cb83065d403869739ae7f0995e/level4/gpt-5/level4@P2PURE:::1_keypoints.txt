- Proposes an AMP algorithm for matrix reconstruction from noisy observations, targeting low-rank matrices with assumptions on the factors.
- Employs AMP to speed up the classical Bayesian method, which is computationally expensive.
- Connects matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering, providing an application domain for the method.
- Applying AMP to matrix reconstruction appears novel and compelling.
- Notes that links between low-rank factorization and K-means are known, but this view enables direct experimental comparisons to k-means and k-means++.
- Empirically, the proposed method is faster and more efficient than standard k-means and k-means++ according to reported results.
- Experiments evaluate both Frobenius-norm residuals and resulting clusterings, strengthening the evaluation.
- Primary weakness is the lack of theoretical guarantees, particularly about convergence.
- Recommends citing additional theoretical computer science results that give provable algorithms for the K-means objective.
- Overall assessment: solid empirical contribution with promising performance but relatively weak theoretical analysis.
