- Novel application of AMP to K-means clustering, producing Algorithm 3 with intuitive bias-correction terms for cluster center estimation.
- Unified framework that handles marginal posterior estimation and MAP estimation via an inverse temperature parameter β.
- Promising empirical results: AMP-based clustering outperforms K-means and K-means++ on the presented synthetic and real-world tasks, especially in high dimensions.
- Proposition 1: any AMP MAP fixed point is also an ICM fixed point, giving useful theoretical insight.

- Main derivations are under-specified: many non-trivial steps are omitted in the main text and supplementary material.
- Asymptotic analysis is terse and lacks a formal statement/justification of the assumed scaling relationship between m and N.
- The claim that finite-β solutions converge to the MAP as β→∞ is not rigorously justified; interchange of limit and minimization needs validation.
- Proof of Proposition 1 is too condensed; the key reformulation (Eq. 16) and its derivation are missing.
- Several technical justifications are absent (e.g., a term claimed O(1/√m) and an approximation for E[A_{ij}^2] in the supplement).

- The paper does not discuss the connection to Expectation Propagation (EP), raising questions about novelty.
- The algorithm’s moment-based updates resemble EP; the authors should clarify whether their method is an instance of EP.
- Prior EP work on matrix factorization (e.g., Stern et al., Matchbox 2009) is not compared or discussed.
- It would be useful to relate the clustering approach to Minka’s "clutter problem" from EP literature.

- Experimental evaluation is narrow: comparisons are limited to K-means and K-means++, omitting many modern state-of-the-art clustering methods.
- Overall: interesting and promising approach but not ready for publication without major revisions to clarify derivations, establish novelty versus EP, and broaden experimental validation.
