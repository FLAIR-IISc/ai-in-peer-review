- Paper proposes a new BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with large-N and large-m approximations.
- Algorithm evaluated by formulating clustering as matrix reconstruction and compared to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.
- Evaluating on clustering (non-standard task) limits ability to judge performance on conventional matrix reconstruction problems.
- Authors should test on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) or justify why such datasets cannot be used.
- The method is essentially a straightforward BP/variational-message-passing application and not highly groundbreaking.
- The asymptotic approximations (N→∞, m→∞) are interesting but their validity is not empirically tested or clearly explained.
- Significant omissions and unclear citation of prior work: matrix factorization for clustering appears well-known and was not acknowledged.
- Expectation Propagation (EP) literature should be cited and the relation to the proposed algorithm discussed.
- The paper delays stating that the core algorithm is Belief Propagation until Section 4.1; this should be in the abstract.
- Experiments show benefits for the proposed algorithm, suggesting practical promise despite evaluation concerns.
- Main derivation relegated to Supplementary Material; important derivation should be at least outlined in the main paper.
- Mathematical presentation is overly cumbersome and hard to parse—excess hats, tildes, subscripts/superscripts, and inconsistent notation.
- Recommend notational simplifications (e.g., drop hats, simplify τw to τ, replace "=" with "←" for message updates).
- Explanations linking equations and steps (e.g., eq.5 vs eq.2, eq.8 as eq.2^β) are missing and would improve clarity.
- Algorithm 2 has row-column symmetry that should be pointed out to shorten and clarify presentation.
- Functions in eq.10 and Gβ should be defined in words (e.g., fβ is the mean, Gβ is a scaled covariance).
- β-dependence and subscripting are inconsistent and should be clarified or removed where unnecessary.
- The role and interpretation of the factor m in additive noise variance (and its effect on row-column symmetry) is not explained.
- Several minor notation/style questions: tilde for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization of N vs m.
- Supplementary Material contains unclear steps (e.g., eq.14→15 big-O argument) and language issues that need correction.
- Missing or ambiguous citations for VBMF-MA implementation and the relation of the approximation technique to prior message-approximation literature.
- More diagrams would help; Figure 1 in the Supplementary Material was useful and more visuals should be included.
- English is generally understandable but has awkward article use and other wording issues; title could be more descriptive.
- Conclusion: paper is interesting and relevant but requires substantial revision, better scholarship, clearer derivations, and stronger empirical validation before acceptance.
