- Paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, shows matrix factorization generalizes clustering, and specializes the algorithm for clustering with experiments vs k-means++.

- Mathematical derivations are overly sketchy, omitting non-trivial steps even in the appendix.

- Appendix (line 139): unclear why the second term of Eq. (11) is O(1/√m); parameter β appears omitted in Section 2.

- Section 3 and derivation of Eq. (19): analysis seems to assume m and n grow at the same rate; asymptotic arguments are terse and potentially unjustified.

- Line 167: question why mτ_w^2 equals E[A_il^2]; A is observed data, and mτ_w^2 is just the likelihood variance—this approximation needs verification.

- Convergence to MAP as β→∞ is not justified: message passing minimizes KL divergence and interchange of limit and minimization is nontrivial.

- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief; derivation of Eq. (17) is not shown.

- Paper is well-structured and main contribution is clearly conveyed.

- Novelty is unclear: algorithm appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization.

- Prior work (Stern et al., 2009) already applied EP to matrix factorization; Appendix’s EP updates suggest overlap—authors should clarify.

- Possible connection to Minka’s clutter problem (2001) when using a uniform Gaussian mixture likelihood; authors should acknowledge and discuss this relation.

- Significance limited by narrow clustering focus, comparison only to k-means++, and sketchy proofs preventing assessment of technical correctness.
