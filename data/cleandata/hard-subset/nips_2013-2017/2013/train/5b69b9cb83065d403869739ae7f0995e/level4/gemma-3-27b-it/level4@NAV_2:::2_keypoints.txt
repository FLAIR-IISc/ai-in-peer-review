- New BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction is proposed, with derivation in the Supplementary Material using large-dimension approximations.
- Evaluation casts clustering as matrix reconstruction and compares against Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.
- Experimental choice (clustering formulation) is non-standard, making it hard to extrapolate to conventional matrix reconstruction tasks.
- Authors should evaluate on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) or justify why they cannot.
- The method is a relatively straightforward BP application to a problem previously treated with Variational Message Passing, so novelty is limited.
- Large-matrix-limit approximations are interesting but not directly validated and need clearer explanation.
- Paper misleads by implying matrix factorization for clustering is new; prior work must be cited or acknowledged.
- Expectation Propagation (EP) should be cited and the relationship to the proposed algorithm explained.
- The fact the algorithm is BP-based should be stated in the abstract rather than buried in Section 4.1.
- Paper is interesting and potentially useful; mathematics appears sound and experiments indicate benefits over baselines.
- Derivation relegated to Supplementary Material reduces accessibility; a main-paper outline would help.
- Mathematical presentation is hard to follow: cumbersome notation, overuse of hats/tildes/subscripts/superscripts, and unclear algebraic structure.
- Specific notation fixes suggested (factor out common terms, remove unnecessary hats and ‘t’ superscripts, simplify τ notation).
- Exposition needs explicit links between key equations (e.g., eq.5 = −log(eq.2), eq.8 = eq.2^β) and notes on algorithmic symmetry to shorten presentation.
- Definitions of functions (e.g., f_β, G_β) should include intuitive descriptions near their formal definitions.
- Inconsistent use of β subscripts should be cleaned up and limits (β → ∞) should be stated explicitly before related equations.
- Important conceptual question: significance of the m factor in the noise variance and its effect on row–column symmetry needs addressing.
- Minor clarifications requested: tilde convention for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization (N vs m), and a citation for VBMF-MA implementation.
- Supplementary Material has presentation issues (use product notation for pdfs, unclear big-O steps, typos) and would benefit from more diagrams.
- Overall verdict: algorithm and mathematics merit publication but the paper requires substantial revision for clarity, validation of approximations, and proper citation of prior work.
