- Proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization and specializes it to clustering, comparing to k-means++.

- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, even in the appendix.

- In Appendix line 139, the O(1/√m) claim for the second term of (11) is unclear and the omission of β is unexplained.

- Some analyses (e.g., derivation of (19)) appear to assume m and n grow at the same order; the asymptotic analysis is terse and possibly non-rigorous.

- In line 167, approximating m·τ_w^2 by E[A_il^2] is not justified since A is observed and m·τ_w^2 denotes likelihood variance.

- The convergence of the finite-β message passing algorithm to the MAP solution is unclear; swapping limit and minimization of KL is not guaranteed.

- Proof of Proposition 1 is overly brief and the derivation of equation (17) is missing.

- Paper is well-structured but the lack of detailed mathematical derivations impedes reader understanding.

- Originality is questionable: the method may be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) for matrix factorization (cf. Stern et al.), and Appendix Section 1 follows standard EP updates.

- Possible conceptual link to the EP "clutter" problem if the clutter likelihood is replaced by a uniform Gaussian mixture, noted as a suggestion.

- Significance concerns: comparing only to k-means++ is insufficient given clustering literature, and sketchy proofs hinder evaluation of technical correctness.
