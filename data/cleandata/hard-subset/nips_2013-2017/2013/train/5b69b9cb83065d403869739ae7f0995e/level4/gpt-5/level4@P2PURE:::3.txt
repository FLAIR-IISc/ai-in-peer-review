This paper introduces an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization. The authors frame matrix factorization as a generalization of clustering, specialize the derived AMP algorithm to clustering, and compare it empirically with k-means++.

Quality: The mathematical derivations are quite sketchy and omit many nontrivial steps, even in the appendix.
1) In the appendix (line 139), why is the second term of equation (11) O(1/sqrt(m))? Also, β appears to be omitted in Section 2.
2) Parts of the analysis, including the derivation of (19), seem to assume that m and n grow at the same rate. Overall, the asymptotic arguments are terse, and I am concerned about their mathematical rigor.
3) In line 167, why is m τ_w^2 taken as the expectation of A_{il}^2? A is observed data, and m τ_w^2 is merely the variance of the likelihood; the rationale for this approximation should be justified.
4) For finite β, how does the message passing algorithm converge to the MAP solution? Message passing minimizes KL divergence, but lim_β min KL = min lim_β KL does not necessarily hold.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is too terse. How is equation (17) derived?

Clarity: The paper is well structured, and the main ideas are straightforward to follow.

Originality: Because the method targets the first and second moments of the marginal posterior, isn’t it essentially applying Expectation Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization? EP has already been used for matrix factorization (see: http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Section 1 of the appendix appears to reproduce standard EP update procedures; please correct me if I am mistaken.
There also seems to be a potentially interesting connection between the authors’ clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), if the clutter likelihood is replaced by a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. To be clear, I am proposing a possible link here, not challenging the paper’s originality.

Significance: The empirical evaluation focuses on clustering, but given the breadth of the clustering literature, comparing only against k-means++ may not suffice to demonstrate practical impact. The proofs, including those in the appendix, are quite sketchy, making it difficult to assess technical correctness. I also suspect that the approach is an application of EP to matrix factorization, which was already studied by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
