The authors propose a novel algorithm for reconstructing matrices from noisy observations, focusing on low-rank matrices with specific factor assumptions. This approach leverages Approximate Message Passing to enhance the efficiency of the traditional, computationally intensive Bayesian method. Furthermore, the authors establish a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, providing an interesting application domain for their proposed algorithm.

To my knowledge, the application of Approximate Message Passing to matrix reconstruction is an innovative and intriguing concept. Although the relationship between low-rank matrix factorizations and K-means is well-established (notably, PCA offers a factor two approximation algorithm for K-means), this connection enables the authors to conduct a thorough experimental evaluation of their algorithm, comparing it to k-means and k-means++. Notably, their approach appears to be faster and more efficient than traditional k-means and k-means++ based on their empirical results. A notable feature of their experimental evaluation is the comparison of both the Frobenius norm residual and the actual clusterings.

The primary limitation of this paper lies in the scarcity of theoretical guarantees regarding the convergence of the proposed algorithm. This is not unexpected, as many K-means algorithms also exhibit weak theoretical properties. The authors may want to consider citing additional papers from Theoretical Computer Science that present provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function. Overall, this is a solid paper that presents Approximate Message Passing algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction and k-means, featuring a promising experimental evaluation, albeit with somewhat limited theoretical results.
