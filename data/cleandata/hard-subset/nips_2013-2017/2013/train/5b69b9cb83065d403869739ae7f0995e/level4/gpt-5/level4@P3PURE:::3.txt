This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization. After showing that matrix factorization can be viewed as a generalization of clustering, the authors specialize their message passing algorithm to the clustering setting. Finally, they compare the proposed approach against k-means++ empirically.

Quality: The mathematical derivations are quite sketchy and omit many nontrivial steps, even in the Appendix. Specific concerns:
1) In line 139 of the Appendix, why is the second term of (11) O(1/sqrt(m))? In addition, beta seems to be omitted in Section 2.
2) Parts of the analysis, including the derivation of (19), appear to assume that m and n grow at the same rate. Overall, the asymptotic analysis in this section is terse, and I am concerned about its mathematical rigor.
3) In line 167, why is m tau_w^2 taken to be the expectation of A_il^2? A is observed data, and m tau_w^2 is only the variance of the likelihood; the rationale for this approximation should be justified.
4) How does the message passing algorithm derived for finite beta converge to the MAP problem? The message passing algorithm minimizes the KL divergence, but lim_beta min KL = min lim_beta KL does not necessarily hold.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief. How is (17) derived?

Clarity: The paper is well structured, and the main ideas are easy to follow.

Originality: Because the algorithm focuses on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior, it appears closely related to Expectation Propagation (EP). EP has already been applied to matrix factorization in Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Section 1 of the Appendix seems to present standard EP update procedures; if this interpretation is incorrect, please clarify the distinctions. In addition, there seems to be a potentially interesting connection between the authors’ clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood in the clutter model is replaced by a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. To be clear, this paragraph is intended to suggest a connection rather than challenge the paper’s originality.

Significance: The empirical evaluation focuses on clustering, but given the extensive literature in this area, comparing only to k-means++ is likely insufficient to demonstrate practical utility. The proofs, even in the Appendix, are quite sketchy, making it difficult to assess technical correctness. Moreover, I suspect the method is essentially an application of EP to matrix factorization, which was already explored by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
