

This paper introduces an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, demonstrating that matrix factorization generalizes the clustering problem and specializing the derived algorithm to clustering. Experiments compare the proposed method against k-means++.  
Quality: The mathematical derivations are excessively sketchy, omitting non-trivial steps even in the appendix.  
1) In Appendix line 139, why is the second term of (11) O(1/√m)? β is omitted in Section 2.  
2) Analysis, including derivation of (19), assumes m and n grow proportionally; the asymptotic treatment is terse and raises concerns about mathematical rigor.  
3) In line 167, why is mτ_w² the expectation of A_il²? A is observed data, while mτ_w² is only the likelihood variance; the approximation rationale requires verification.  
4) How does the finite-β message passing algorithm converge to the MAP problem? Message passing minimizes KL divergence, but lim_{β→∞} min KL ≠ min lim_{β→∞} KL necessarily.  
5) Proposition 1’s proof is too brief; how is (17) derived?  
Clarity: The paper is well-structured and clearly conveys the main contribution.  
Originality: Since the authors focus on estimating first and second moments of the marginal posterior, is the entire algorithm merely an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization? EP was previously applied to matrix factorization in Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf), and Section 1 of the appendix appears to be standard EP updates (please correct if mistaken). Additionally, a potential connection exists between the authors’ clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the clutter likelihood is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussians. (This paragraph suggests a connection, not an originality critique.)  
Significance: The focus on clustering applications requires broader comparison beyond k-means++ given the extensive clustering literature. The sketchy proofs (even in the appendix) hinder technical evaluation, and the approach appears to be an EP application to matrix factorization, as demonstrated by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
