- Paper proposes a BP-based message-passing algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction using large-system (m, N → ∞) approximations and evaluates it by casting clustering as matrix reconstruction versus Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.  
- Strength: tackles an important problem and introduces a BP method with clear empirical gains on the proposed clustering-as-reconstruction benchmark.  
- Strength: writing and related-work overview are accessible; experiments are understandable and figures mostly readable; supplementary schematic is helpful.  
- Main concern: evaluation is non-standard (clustering-as-reconstruction) and lacks established benchmarks (e.g., Netflix), making true competitiveness unclear.  
- Main concern: novelty appears incremental over prior message-passing methods; the key large-system approximations are not validated and are relegated to the supplement.  
- Scholarship issue: missing or unclear citations—need to cite the well-known clustering↔factorization link and Expectation Propagation, and state BP explicitly in the abstract.  
- Math/clarity issue: derivations are confined to the supplement; notation is overloaded and could be simplified (factor constants, reduce hats/superscripts, rename τ_w, present β limits).  
- Presentation suggestion: provide high-level derivation and intuition in main text, combine symmetric updates, and define f_β and G_β with plain-language interpretation at introduction.  
- Conceptual question: clarify the origin and role of the factor m in the additive noise variance and its effect on row–column asymmetry.  
- Additional methodological detail: state which VBMF implementation/reference was used and any deviations from prior works.  
- Supplementary material fixes: clarify pdf notation, show O(m) derivation steps, fix typos, and explain how the approximation relates to prior “approximate message” techniques.  
- Minor language/style suggestions: propose a clearer title and multiple small wording/grammar fixes throughout the manuscript.  
- Overall recommendation: Reject for now; encourage resubmission after adding standard benchmarks or justification, validating/stress-testing large-system approximations, improving citations and abstract, and streamlining notation and exposition.
