- Paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural priors and derives an AMP algorithm for efficient approximate inference.  
- Extends AMP to handle both posterior means and MAP estimation via a temperature parameter β, unifying marginalization and MAP.  
- Casts K-means as constrained low-rank factorization with one-hot assignments and derives an AMP-based clustering algorithm enforcing the “exactly one cluster per point” constraint.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and ORL faces report reconstruction (Frobenius) error and clustering accuracy, showing improvements over Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ and competitive performance vs VBMF.  
- Positions work relative to prior AMP and low-rank factorization literature, noting novelty in enabling MAP estimation and explicit one-hot constraints for clustering.  
- Strength: clean, flexible AMP formulation that incorporates structural priors and unifies marginalization/MAP.  
- Strength: low per-iteration complexity O(mN) and updates that relate to and improve upon variational-Bayes/ICM-style methods.  
- Strength: principled AMP derivation for K-means including correction terms addressing assignment bias and cluster size effects.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation reporting both objective loss and clustering accuracy with consistent gains over common baselines.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical guarantees—no state evolution, convergence conditions, or recovery guarantees provided.  
- Weakness: practical implementation issues (τ estimation, oscillations, occasional collapse to a single cluster) need deeper discussion and stabilization heuristics.  
- Weakness: comparisons limited to K-means variants and VBMF; broader baselines (spectral clustering, provable K-means algorithms) and additional citations are missing.  
- Suggestion: add state-evolution analysis or discussion on accuracy/stability of AMP fixed points and positivity of Onsager-like terms.  
- Suggestion: cite and compare to provable K-means and spectral methods, discussing performance where guarantees exist.  
- Suggestion: include ablation studies for correction terms and report robustness to initialization and τ estimation.  
- Overall: solid, timely contribution extending AMP to low-rank MAP estimation and clustering; empirically promising but weakened by lack of theory.
