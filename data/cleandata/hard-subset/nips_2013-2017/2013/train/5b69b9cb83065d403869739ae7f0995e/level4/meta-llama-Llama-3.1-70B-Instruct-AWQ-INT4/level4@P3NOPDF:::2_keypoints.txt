- Proposes a BP-based message-passing algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with large-N, large-m approximations.  
- Algorithm derivation is in the Supplementary Material and uses two simplifying asymptotic approximations.  
- Evaluated via clustering framed as matrix factorization, which is a non-standard test and makes results hard to interpret for typical reconstruction tasks.  
- Lacks evaluation on common benchmarks (e.g., Netflix) or explanation why they were not used.  
- The method appears to be a relatively straightforward application of BP/Variational Message Passing rather than a major theoretical advance.  
- The asymptotic approximations are interesting but their validity is not directly tested or clearly justified.  
- Serious citation omissions: the connection between matrix factorization and clustering is presented as novel but is well-known and should be acknowledged/cited.  
- Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to the proposed algorithm are not cited or discussed.  
- Positive: presents a new practical algorithm with generally followable mathematics and experiments that show some benefit over competitors.  
- Clarity issues: key derivation is relegated to supplementary material, making the algorithm hard to understand from the main paper.  
- Notation is cumbersome (excessive hats/tildes/subscripts/superscripts) and should be simplified.  
- The role and significance of the m factor in the additive noise variance is unclear and needs explanation.  
- Minor presentation issues: nonstandard use of tilde for column vectors and inconsistent capitalization of N and m.  
- Missing citation for VBMF-MA implementation details and other relevant prior work.  
- Supplementary Material fixes needed: clarify eq.5 to show the pdf term, and better justify the big-O step from eq.14 to eq.15.  
- Novelty of the derivation should be explicitly clarified in the main text (what is new vs. known).  
- English/style issues throughout (several suggested wording corrections), plus some language problems in the Supplementary Material.  
- Overall recommendation: the paper has promise but requires substantial revisions to clarity, scholarship, notation, and experimental validation.
