- Proposes a new BP-based message-passing algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, evaluated via clustering-as-matrix-factorization.
- Novel evaluation comparing against Lloyd’s K-means and Variational Bayes Matrix Factorization (VBMF).
- Major concern: evaluation on a non-standard task (clustering encoding) makes it hard to judge performance on conventional reconstruction problems.
- Reviewer suggests evaluating on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) or explaining why such datasets cannot be used.
- Algorithm appears to be a relatively straightforward BP application to a problem previously tackled with Variational Message Passing, so not highly groundbreaking.
- Key approximations assume large m,N (m×N → ∞) but their validity is asserted in the supplement and never directly tested.
- Serious citation and scholarship issues: presents clustering-as-factorization as if novel without citing prior work or stating it’s well-known.
- Fails to cite or discuss Expectation Propagation (EP) and the relation between EP and the proposed algorithm.
- BP connection is not made explicit until Section 4.1 and should be mentioned in the abstract.
- Mathematical derivation relegated to Supplementary Material; the main paper should outline the derivation for clarity.
- Notation is cluttered and inconsistent (excessive hats/tildes/subscripts, inconsistent β propagation, unnecessary time superscripts).
- Specific algebraic simplifications and clearer explanations (e.g., relations between equations 2,5,8; limits β→∞) are missing and would aid readability.
- Important implementation/derivation details (role of the m factor, row-column asymmetry) are unexplained and raise conceptual questions.
- Section 5: unclear which VBMF reference guides the competing algorithm implementation; add a citation for VBMF-MA.
- Supplementary Material contains unclear steps (e.g., Big-O step from eq.14→15, relation to previously published approximations) and needs clearer exposition.
- Numerous English phrasing issues and wording fixes are suggested to improve readability and professionalism.
- Reviewer liked Figure 1 in the supplement and recommends adding more diagrams to aid understanding.
- Overall: mathematics are interesting and the algorithm is promising, but the paper needs substantial improvement in evaluation, clarity, citation of prior work, and exposition before acceptance.
