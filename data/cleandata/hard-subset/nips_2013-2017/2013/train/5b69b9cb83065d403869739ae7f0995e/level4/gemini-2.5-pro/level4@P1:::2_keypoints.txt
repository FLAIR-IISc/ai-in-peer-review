- Proposes a novel AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction derived from a BP Bayesian model.  
- Main contribution is a flexible Bayesian framework that can incorporate structural priors (e.g., sparsity) on factor matrices.  
- Novel application: reformulates K-means clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction task and applies the algorithm to it.  
- Empirical results show the AMP-based method outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and a VBMF baseline on clustering loss and accuracy.  
- Paper is generally well-written and derivations in the supplement appear detailed enough for reproducibility.  

- Fails to properly situate the work relative to prior literature; the clustering/low-rank connection is well-known and not acknowledged.  
- Does not discuss relationship to Expectation Propagation (EP) despite deriving from BP on a continuous model.  
- Belief Propagation basis of the algorithm is introduced too late (Section 4.1) and should be stated in the abstract/introduction.  

- Experimental evaluation is narrow (focused on clustering) and omits standard matrix reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix), leaving general utility unclear.  
- The main derivation is relegated to the supplement; the paper needs a high-level overview of key approximations (large-system limit) and empirical validation of those approximations.  
- Algorithm presentation is cluttered and hard to parse due to cumbersome notation, repeated factors, and awkward iteration notation.  
- Exposition should clarify relationships between key equations and highlight near row-column symmetry; functions f and G need intuitive explanations near their definitions.  

- Notation inconsistencies (e.g., noise variance τ vs. τ_w, overuse of hats) hinder readability and should be streamlined.  
- Algorithm 2 could be simplified by factoring out common terms (e.g., 1/mτ) and combining redundant terms in Λ_u^t and Λ_v^t.  
- The role and practical significance of the scaling factor m in the noise variance is unexplained and breaks row-column symmetry; this must be justified.  
- Supplement derivation steps (e.g., Eq. 14→15 with big-O) are hard to follow and need more detailed explanation and novelty clarification.  
- Clarify which VBMF implementation was used for the VBMF-MA baseline and cite the guiding paper explicitly.  

- Conclusion: promising and well-motivated AMP approach with strong clustering results, but requires substantial revisions on positioning, broader evaluation, and clearer presentation before publication.
