This paper introduces an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, and demonstrates its specialization to the clustering problem after establishing a connection between the two. The authors then present experimental results comparing the proposed algorithm to k-means++.

**Quality:** The mathematical derivations throughout the paper lack sufficient detail, even within the appendix. Several steps appear non-trivial and are omitted, hindering a full understanding and verification of the results. 

Specifically:

1.  In the appendix, line 139, the justification for the second term in equation (11) being O(1/√m) is unclear. The role of β, introduced in Section 2, is not evident in this derivation.
2.  The asymptotic analysis, particularly the derivation of equation (19), appears to implicitly assume that *m* and *n* grow at comparable rates. This section is generally terse, and I have concerns regarding the overall mathematical rigor.
3.  Line 167 states that *mτw²* is the expectation of A<sub>il</sub>². However, *A* represents observed data, and *mτ<sub>w</sub>²* is solely the variance of the likelihood. The reasoning behind this approximation requires further justification.
4.  It remains unclear how the message passing algorithm, as derived for finite β, converges to the Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) solution. While message passing minimizes the KL divergence, the interchange of limit and minimization—that is, whether  lim<sub>β</sub> min KL = min lim<sub>β</sub> KL—is not justified and may not hold.
5.  The proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed. The derivation of equation (17) is not adequately explained.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and the central ideas are readily accessible.

**Originality:** The core of the algorithm appears to center on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior distribution. This leads me to question whether the approach is essentially an application of Expectation Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. EP has been previously employed in this context, as demonstrated in Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Section 1 of the appendix outlines procedures consistent with standard EP updates; I would appreciate clarification on this point.

Furthermore, an interesting connection may exist between the authors’ proposed clustering algorithm and the ‘clutter’ problem originally addressed in EP (Minka, http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood function of the clutter problem were replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. I offer this as a potential connection for consideration and do not intend to directly challenge the paper’s originality.

**Significance:** While the authors focus on the application of this algorithm to clustering, given the extensive existing literature in that field, a comparison solely to k-means++ may be insufficient to establish practical utility. The lack of detailed proofs, even in the appendix, makes it challenging to assess the technical soundness of the work. Moreover, the potential connection to existing EP-based matrix factorization methods (Stern et al.) raises concerns about the novelty of the proposed approach.
