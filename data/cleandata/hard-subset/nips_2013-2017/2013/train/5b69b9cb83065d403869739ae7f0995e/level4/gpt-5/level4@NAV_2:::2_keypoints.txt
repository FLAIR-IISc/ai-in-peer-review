- Introduces a BP-style message-passing algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction within a Bayesian framework.  
- Derivation is deferred to the Supplement and relies on two large-system approximations (N→∞ and m→∞).  
- Empirical evaluation recasts clustering as low-rank reconstruction, comparing to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.  
- Evaluation focus on clustering makes it hard to judge performance on standard matrix-reconstruction benchmarks.  
- Missing standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) or an explanation for their omission leaves competitiveness uncertain.  
- Methodologically, the contribution feels like a relatively direct BP application to a problem previously treated with variational message passing.  
- The validity and applicability of the N→∞ and m→∞ approximations are not tested or clearly explained in the main text.  
- Related-work framing is misleading: the clustering↔matrix-factorization connection is presented as novel but is already known and needs proper citation.  
- Expectation Propagation (EP) is not cited; the relation between EP and the proposed algorithm should be clarified.  
- The fact that the algorithm arises from belief propagation is only mentioned late (Section 4.1) and should appear in the abstract.  
- Paper is engaging and mathematically readable; the derivations appear tractable to check.  
- Framing the benchmark via the K-means objective is reasonable, and experiments show an advantage for the proposed method.  
- Relegating the core derivation to the Supplement is a weakness; a concise derivation outline should be in the main paper.  
- Algorithm presentation is hard to parse; some expressions (e.g., repeated 1/(m τ_w) factors) should be factored for readability.  
- Notation is heavier than necessary (many hats/tildes/superscripts); suggest simplifying symbols and indicating dropped hats early.  
- Iteration superscripts (t) could be removed for clarity by using assignment arrows or other notation.  
- Key equations/relations should be explicitly stated (e.g., eq. (5) is negative log of (2); eq. (8) is (2)^β).  
- Functions f_β and G_β should be interpreted at definition (mean and β-scaled covariance) and β treated as a global parameter to avoid proliferation.  
- The role of the factor m in additive noise variance and its contribution to row–column asymmetry needs clearer explanation.  
- Minor notational/style queries: tilde for column vectors, inconsistency of uppercase N vs lowercase m, and unclear VBMF implementation reference.  
- Supplement needs clearer algebraic steps (e.g., transition in eqs. (14)→(15)), clearer explanation of prior approximations, and minor wording fixes.  
- Add illustrative diagrams (like Supplement Figure 1) to the main paper to improve clarity.  
- Language and phrasing need polishing (many small edits suggested; title could be improved to reflect the AMP angle).  
- Overall: promising and publishable with revisions, but currently requires substantial improvements in clarity, scholarship, and validation before conference acceptance.
