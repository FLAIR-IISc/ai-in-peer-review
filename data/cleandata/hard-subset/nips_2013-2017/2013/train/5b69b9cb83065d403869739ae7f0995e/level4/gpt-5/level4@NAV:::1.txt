The authors introduce an algorithm for matrix reconstruction under noisy measurements. The setup focuses on low-rank matrices with additional assumptions on the factors, and employs an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) strategy to accelerate the standard, computationally heavy Bayesian approach. They also draw a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, which provides a compelling application area for their methods.

To the best of my knowledge, applying AMP to matrix reconstruction in this manner is novel and interesting. While the links between low-rank factorizations and K-means are fairly well-established (e.g., PCA yields a factor-2 approximation for K-means), this perspective enables a clear and useful empirical assessment of their algorithms against k-means and k-means++. Notably, their method appears faster and more efficient than standard k-means and k-means++ based on their reported experiments. The evaluation considers both Frobenius norm residuals and the resulting cluster assignments, which is a valuable aspect of the experimental design.

The primary limitation is the scarcity of theoretical guarantees, particularly regarding convergence. This is somewhat anticipated, as many K-means algorithms also have limited theoretical assurances. The authors could at least reference additional works in Theoretical Computer Science that give provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting AMP-based methods for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, with promising empirical results and somewhat limited theoretical support.
