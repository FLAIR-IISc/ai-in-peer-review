The paper proposes an algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy measurements. The setting assumes low rank with additional structural priors on the factors, and uses an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) framework to make Bayesian inference computationally tractable. The authors also recast K-means clustering as a special case of their low-rank reconstruction setup, yielding an AMP-based clustering method.

To my knowledge, applying AMP to this matrix reconstruction problem is both new and compelling. While the connection between low-rank factorization and K-means is well established (e.g., PCA gives a factor-2 approximation for K-means), this perspective enables a clear empirical evaluation against K-means and K-means++. Empirically, the proposed approach appears faster and more effective than classical K-means and K-means++ in the reported settings. The experimental section commendably compares both reconstruction error in Frobenius norm and clustering accuracy.

The principal weakness is the limited theoretical guarantees, especially regarding convergence. This is not unusual given the landscape of K-means algorithms, but the paper would be stronger by referencing additional theoretical computer science results that provide provable guarantees for the K-means objective. Overall, this is a solid contribution presenting AMP-based methods for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, with promising empirical results and comparatively weaker theory.
