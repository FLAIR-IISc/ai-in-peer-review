- Proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization and applies it to clustering, deriving a message-passing clustering method.  
- Mathematical derivations are concise but omit non-trivial steps, even in the appendix, raising rigor concerns.  
- Concern 1: In Appendix line 139, the claim that the second term of (11) is O(1/√m) is unclear because the role of β in that approximation is not explained.  
- Concern 2: Some analyses (e.g., derivation of (19)) seem to assume m and n grow at the same rate, but the asymptotic arguments are terse and potentially non-rigorous.  
- Concern 3: In line 167, approximating E[A_il^2] as mτ_w^2 is questionable since A is observed and mτ_w^2 is merely the likelihood variance—this needs justification.  
- Concern 4: The convergence argument that the message-passing algorithm for finite β yields the MAP solution is unclear; it’s not shown that lim_{β→∞} min KL = min lim_{β→∞} KL.  
- Concern 5: Proof of Proposition 1 is brief and unclear, with the derivation of equation (17) not explicitly shown.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured and main idea is easy to grasp, but mathematical details should be expanded for rigor.  
- Originality: The method appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation to matrix factorization and relates to prior EP work (including the clutter problem), reducing novelty.  
- Significance: Evaluating only against k-means++ is insufficient given clustering literature breadth; terse proofs make assessing technical correctness difficult.
