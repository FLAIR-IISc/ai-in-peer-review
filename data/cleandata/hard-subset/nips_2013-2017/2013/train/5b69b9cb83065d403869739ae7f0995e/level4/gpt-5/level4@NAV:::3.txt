This manuscript introduces an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization. The authors first argue that matrix factorization generalizes clustering, then specialize the derived message passing algorithm to clustering, and finally compare it empirically against k-means++.

Quality: The mathematical development is quite terse and skips many nontrivial steps, even in the appendix.
1) In Appendix line 139, why is the second term of (11) O(1/√m)? Also, β appears to be dropped in Section 2.
2) Parts of the analysis, including the derivation of (19), seem to assume m and n grow at the same rate. Overall, the asymptotic arguments are brief, and I am concerned about their rigor.
3) At line 167, why is m τ_w^2 taken as E[A_{il}^2]? A is observed data, and m τ_w^2 is only the variance of the likelihood; the justification for this approximation should be provided.
4) How does the message passing algorithm derived for finite β converge to the MAP solution? The algorithm minimizes the KL divergence, but lim_β min KL ≠ min lim_β KL does not necessarily hold.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is too succinct. How is equation (17) obtained?

Clarity: The paper is well organized, and the main message is straightforward to follow.

Originality: Since the method primarily estimates first and second moments of the marginal posterior, isn’t the algorithm essentially an application of Expectation Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization? EP has already been used for matrix factorization in the following work: http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf. Section 1 of the Appendix appears to be standard EP update procedures; please correct me if this interpretation is inaccurate.
Additionally, there seems to be a potentially interesting connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper: http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf, especially if the likelihood is switched to a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. To be clear, I am suggesting a connection here, not challenging the paper’s originality.

Significance: The empirical focus is on clustering, but given the extensive clustering literature, comparison solely to k-means++ may be insufficient to demonstrate practical impact. The proofs are quite sketchy, making it hard to assess technical correctness. I also suspect the method is an application of EP to matrix factorization, which was previously explored by Stern et al.: http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf.
