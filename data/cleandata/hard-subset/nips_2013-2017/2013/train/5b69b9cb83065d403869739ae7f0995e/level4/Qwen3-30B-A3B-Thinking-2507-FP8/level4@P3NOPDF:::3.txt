

This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, framing it as a generalization of clustering. The derived algorithm is then specialized to clustering, with experimental comparisons against k-means++. While the high-level approach is interesting, significant concerns regarding mathematical rigor, originality, and significance limit its suitability for publication.  

**Quality:** The mathematical foundations are underdeveloped, with critical gaps in derivations and assumptions. Key issues include:  
1. *Appendix A, Line 139:* The claim that the second term in (11) is $O(1/\sqrt{m})$ lacks justification. The parameter $\beta$ (defined in Section 2) appears omitted in this analysis, undermining the asymptotic argument.  
2. *Section 3.2:* The derivation of (19) and subsequent asymptotic analysis assume $m$ and $n$ grow at the same rate. This is not stated, and the analysis is too terse to verify rigor.  
3. *Appendix A, Line 167:* The assertion that $m \tau_w^2 = \mathbb{E}[A_{ij}^2]$ is unsupported. $A_{ij}$ is observed data, while $m\tau_w^2$ is the variance of the likelihood; the approximation’s validity requires explicit justification.  
4. *Convergence to MAP:* The paper claims the algorithm converges to the MAP solution as $\beta \to \infty$, but the message passing framework minimizes KL divergence. The equality $\lim_{\beta \to \infty} \min_{\text{KL}} = \min_{\text{KL}} \lim_{\beta \to \infty}$ is not established, and this critical step is omitted.  
5. *Proposition 1:* The derivation of (17) is insufficiently detailed. A clear path from prior equations to (17) is needed for verification.  

**Originality:** The core algorithm appears to be a direct application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, rather than a novel contribution. Section 1 of the appendix mirrors standard EP updates, as demonstrated in Stern et al. (2009), who applied EP to matrix factorization (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). The proposed clustering algorithm also bears a conceptual resemblance to the "clutter problem" in Minka’s EP work (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood is reinterpreted as a uniform mixture of Gaussians. While this connection is suggestive rather than a claim of novelty, it highlights that the method builds on established EP techniques, not a new framework. The paper should explicitly acknowledge this relationship and clarify the distinction from prior work.  

**Significance:** The experimental validation is inadequate for claiming practical utility. Comparing only to k-means++ is insufficient in the crowded clustering literature; baselines should include established methods (e.g., spectral clustering, DBSCAN, or other EP-based approaches) to demonstrate clear advantages. More critically, the technical sketchiness of the proofs (even in the appendix) prevents meaningful assessment of the algorithm’s correctness or scalability. Without rigorous analysis or broader empirical validation, the significance of the proposed method remains unproven.  

**Summary:** While the paper identifies a plausible connection between matrix factorization and clustering via message passing, the lack of mathematical rigor, potential overlap with existing EP-based methods, and insufficient experimental validation undermine its contribution. The work would benefit from: (1) complete derivations with clear justifications for all asymptotic claims; (2) explicit comparison to EP-based matrix factorization (Stern et al.) and clustering baselines; and (3) a clearer articulation of how this approach differs from or improves upon established techniques. As written, the paper fails to demonstrate either technical novelty or practical significance. I recommend rejection.
