- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles both marginal posterior estimation and MAP estimation.  
- Derives a unified AMP framework supporting structural priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity) on factor matrices.  
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem and provides an AMP-based K-means algorithm enforcing exact cluster assignments.  
- Key novelty: extends AMP to the general-rank case for MAP estimation, filling a gap in prior AMP work.  
- Provides Algorithm 2 for marginalization and MAP with rigorous derivation and connections to belief propagation and variational Bayes.  
- Addresses computational bottlenecks (linear cost O(mN) per iteration) and gives a convergence analysis (Proposition 1) showing AMP fixed points coincide with ICM fixed points under mild conditions.  
- Empirical evaluation: extensive synthetic (500 trials) and real-data (50 ORL face runs) experiments measuring loss, accuracy (Hungarian matching), and convergence speed.  
- Results: AMP-KM outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ (especially for r ≥ 5); AMP-MA yields higher accuracy and faster convergence than VBMF-MA.  
- Paper is well-organized and clear; problem setting and algorithm descriptions are precise with intuitive explanations of key terms.  
- Originality: novel MAP extension for AMP and a significant constraint-handling approach for clustering compared to prior low-rank methods.  
- Significance: practical improvement over K-means sensitivity to initialization, with broader applicability due to flexible structural priors.  
- Main weakness: limited theoretical guarantees beyond Proposition 1—no convergence to global optima or landscape characterization for non-convex cases.  
- Additional weaknesses: no comparison to other AMP-based matrix reconstruction methods and insufficient intuitive justification for the bias-correction term in Equation 18b.  
- Suggestion: include higher-level state-evolution discussion and cite relevant K-means theoretical advances to strengthen theoretical credibility.  
- Recommendation: accept—strong empirical and practical contribution with suggested minor theoretical clarifications for the final version.
