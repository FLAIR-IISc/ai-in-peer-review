The authors present an algorithm for matrix reconstruction from noisy observations. Their setting focuses on low-rank matrices with specific assumptions on the underlying factors, and they leverage an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the typically computationally expensive classical Bayesian methods. They also establish a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, representing an interesting application domain for their proposed algorithms.

To the best of my knowledge, the application of AMP to matrix reconstruction is both novel and interesting. While the relationship between low-rank matrix factorizations and K-means is well-established – for example, PCA provides a factor-two approximation algorithm for K-means – this connection allows the authors to present a compelling experimental evaluation, comparing their algorithm to both K-means and K-means++. Notably, their empirical results suggest their approach is faster and more efficient than these classical K-means methods. The authors’ evaluation is further strengthened by their comparison of both the Frobenius norm residual *and* the resulting clusterings. 

The primary weakness of the paper lies in the limited theoretical guarantees regarding convergence of the proposed algorithm. This is not entirely unexpected, as many existing K-means algorithms also possess only weak theoretical properties. However, the authors could benefit from citing more work in Theoretical Computer Science that presents provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function. 

Overall, this is a solid paper presenting AMP algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means. The experimental evaluation is promising, though the theoretical results remain somewhat weak.
