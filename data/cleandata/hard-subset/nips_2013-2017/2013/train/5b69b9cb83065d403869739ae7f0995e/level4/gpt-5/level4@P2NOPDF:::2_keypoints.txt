- Proposes a BP-derived algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using large-row/column approximations and evaluates it via clustering-as-factorization.
- Evaluation is non-standard (clustering proxy), making it hard to assess performance on conventional matrix reconstruction benchmarks like Netflix.
- Paper should justify choice of datasets or include standard benchmarks to demonstrate competitiveness with prior VBMF work.
- Novelty is incremental: algorithm is a direct BP counterpart to variational message-passing, with main novelty in asymptotic approximations that are only in the supplement and lack experimental validation.
- The large-N and large-m approximations need clearer exposition and empirical validation.
- Scholarship and positioning are weak: the clustering–matrix factorization link and EP literature are not properly cited or discussed.
- The paper should state up front (ideally in the abstract) that the method is an instance of BP and clarify its relation to EP.
- Strength: the problem is interesting and the algorithm could be practically useful.
- Strength: the narrative is generally readable and the equations appear reproducible.
- Strength: K-means benchmark, while non-standard, can be informative and highlights advantages in experiments.
- Derivation relegated to supplement weakens the paper; include an outline of key derivation steps in the main text.
- Some plots are hard to read in black-and-white, though experiments are otherwise clearly described.
- Equations suffer readability issues (repeated factors, combinable terms) and should be algebraically simplified for clarity.
- Notation overload (hats, tildes, subscripts, superscripts, inconsistent tau/N/m conventions) obscures the main ideas and should be simplified.
- Explanatory links are missing: explicitly state relationships between key equations (e.g., Eq. 5 vs Eq. 2, Eq. 8 as power of Eq. 2) and exploit symmetry in Algorithm 2 to shorten presentation.
- Definitions and beta-dependence need clarification: define f_beta/G_beta near introduction and consider removing redundant beta subscripts treating beta as global.
- Conceptual questions remain: clarify the role of factor m in noise variance, vector notation conventions, and inconsistent use of uppercase/lowercase for dimensions.
- Related work/implementation: add a citation for the VBMF-MA implementation choice and cite EP while explaining its relation to the proposed approximation.
- Supplement needs clearer steps (e.g., Eq. 14→15 big-O argument), more diagrams, and minor language fixes; some derivation details should be moved to main text.
- Language and title: improve English in several phrases and consider retitling to emphasize the approximate message-passing algorithm.
- Conclusion: promising algorithm but requires stronger positioning, clearer derivation/notation, validation of approximations, and better-aligned evaluation before publication.
