- Paper proposes an AMP approach for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors and a beta parameter unifying marginal inference (beta=1) and MAP (beta→∞), including a one-hot constraint specialization for clustering and experiments on synthetic data and ORL Faces.

- Builds on prior AMP/bilinear AMP and VBMF work but needs clearer positioning and citations for the factorization–clustering connection and for expectation propagation (EP).

- Strength: clear AMP formulation that covers posterior means and MAP via beta and yields a principled K-means-like variant with Onsager corrections.

- Strength: competitive clustering performance (especially in high dimensions and on ORL), fast convergence, flexible priors, and O(mN) per-iteration complexity with near symmetry between U and V updates.

- Weakness: empirical evaluation focuses on clustering and lacks tests on standard matrix reconstruction/completion benchmarks (e.g., Netflix), limiting claims of broader competitiveness.

- Weakness: paper overstates novelty of the factorization–clustering link and omits important prior citations and a discussion of EP as a related approximation framework.

- Weakness: large-system approximations (m,N→∞) and state-evolution are not empirically probed; key derivation steps are relegated to the supplement and need main-text sanity checks.

- Weakness: notation and algorithm presentation are difficult to parse (excessive hats/tildes/superscripts, repeated factors), and the near-symmetry of U/V updates is not emphasized.

- Quality: technically sound and well motivated, with natural MAP specialization and practical relevance, but limited validation leaves open questions about general applicability and robustness.

- Clarity: introduction and experiments are clear, but derivations should be summarized in the main text and notation simplified; specific recommendations include factoring common constants, clarifying meanings of f and G, and treating beta globally.

- Originality: incremental but useful contributions—unified beta path to MAP and direct one-hot handling in AMP; clustering within AMP is novel but the underlying idea is known.

- Significance: suggests practical gains for clustering, but impact would increase with demonstrations on standard reconstruction/completion datasets.

- Specific suggestions: cite clustering-via-factorization (e.g., Xu et al. 2003; Ding et al. 2010) and EP, mention BP in the abstract, clarify VBMF baseline used, simplify notation, add state-evolution/sanity checks, clarify supplement steps, and perform language edits; consider retitling.

- Pros for acceptance: interesting AMP formulation, practical clustering gains, efficient algorithm, clear motivation.

- Cons for acceptance: evaluation not aligned with standard benchmarks, incomplete citations/positioning, insufficient derivation/approximation validation, and heavy notation.

- Recommendation: reject in current form; encourage resubmission after stronger empirical validation on standard tasks, clearer positioning/citations, and improved exposition.
