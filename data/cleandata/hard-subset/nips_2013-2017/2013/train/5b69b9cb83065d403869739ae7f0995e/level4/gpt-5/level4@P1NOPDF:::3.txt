This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization. The authors first show that matrix factorization can be viewed as a generalization of clustering, then specialize their AMP derivation to clustering, and finally compare the proposed algorithm to k-means++ in experiments.

Quality: The mathematical derivations are very sketchy and omit many nontrivial steps, even in the appendix.
1) Appendix, line 139: Why is the second term of (11) O(1/√m)? Also, β appears to be omitted in Section 2; please clarify where β enters the scaling.
2) Parts of the analysis, including the derivation of (19), seem to assume that m and n grow at the same rate. Overall, the asymptotic analysis in this section is terse, and I am concerned about its rigor. Please state the precise asymptotic regime (e.g., m,n→∞ with n/m→α) and justify which terms are negligible under that regime.
3) Appendix, line 167: Why is m τ_w^2 taken as E[A_{il}^2]? A is observed data, whereas m τ_w^2 is the variance parameter of the likelihood. The rationale behind this approximation should be explicitly stated and justified (e.g., law of large numbers under a specific generative model), including any independence assumptions.
4) How does the message passing algorithm derived for finite β converge to the MAP solution? AMP often minimizes a KL divergence under a variational approximation, but in general lim_{β→∞} min KL ≠ min lim_{β→∞} KL. Please clarify the limiting procedure, objective continuity, and any exchange-of-limits conditions that justify convergence to MAP.
5) Proposition 1: The proof is too brief. How is (17) derived? Please provide the missing steps or a clear reference, and specify the assumptions required for the approximation.

Clarity: The paper is well organized, and the main ideas are easy to follow.

Originality: Since the algorithm targets the first and second moments of the marginal posterior, it appears closely related to Expectation Propagation (EP). EP has previously been applied to matrix factorization (e.g., Stern et al., “Matchbox,” WWW 2009: http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Section 1 of the appendix appears to reproduce standard EP update procedures; please correct me if I am mistaken and highlight the precise differences (e.g., factorization structure, damping, or Onsager terms beyond EP).
Additionally, there seems to be a potentially interesting connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the “clutter” problem in the original EP paper (Minka, UAI 2001: http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood in the clutter model is replaced by a uniform mixture of Gaussians. To be clear, I am only suggesting a conceptual link and not questioning the paper’s originality; if such a connection exists, it would be helpful to articulate it.

Significance: The experimental evaluation focuses on clustering, but given the extensive literature on clustering, comparison only to k-means++ is not sufficient to establish practical value. Additional baselines (e.g., Gaussian mixture models with EM/variational inference, spectral clustering, or recent Bayesian clustering methods) would strengthen the case. Moreover, because many proofs are sketched and key derivations are omitted, it is difficult to assess technical correctness. Finally, I suspect the method is essentially an application of EP to matrix factorization, which was already explored by Stern et al. (WWW 2009). If the proposed AMP differs fundamentally from EP (e.g., via state evolution, Onsager corrections, or different factor/graph structure), those distinctions should be made explicit both theoretically and empirically.
