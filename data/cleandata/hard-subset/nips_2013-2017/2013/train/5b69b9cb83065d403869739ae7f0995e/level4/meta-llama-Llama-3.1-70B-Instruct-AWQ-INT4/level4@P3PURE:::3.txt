This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization by first showing that the matrix factorization problem can be viewed as a generalization of the clustering problem. The message passing algorithm is then specialized to clustering. The authors also conduct experiments to compare the proposed algorithm with the k-means++ algorithm.

Quality: The mathematical derivations in this paper are often sketchy and omit several non-trivial steps, even in the appendix. Specifically, I have several concerns regarding the mathematical rigor of the paper.

1. In line 139 of the Appendix, the second term of equation (11) is stated to be O(1/\sqrt(m)), but the reasoning behind this is unclear, particularly with regards to the omission of \beta in Section 2.

2. Some of the analysis, including the derivation of equation (19), appears to assume that m and n grow at the same order. However, the asymptotic analysis in this section is somewhat terse, and I am concerned about its mathematical rigor.

3. In line 167, the expectation of A{il}^2 is approximated as m \tau_w^2. However, A is observed data, and m\tau_w^2 is only the variance of the likelihood. The rationale behind this approximation should be verified.

4. I am also concerned about how the message passing algorithm derived for finite \beta converges to the MAP problem. The message passing algorithm minimizes the KL divergence, but it is not clear that \lim\beta \min KL = \min \lim\beta KL holds.

5. The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and lacks clarity. Specifically, the derivation of equation (17) is not provided.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured, and the main point of the paper is easy to grasp.

Originality: The algorithm proposed in this paper appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, as it primarily focuses on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior. This approach has been previously explored in the context of matrix factorization (see http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Furthermore, the updated procedures in Section 1 of the Appendix seem to be standard EP procedures. I would appreciate clarification on this point.

Additionally, I suggest that there may be an interesting connection between the clustering algorithm proposed by the authors and the clutter problem of the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), if the likelihood of the clutter problem is switched to a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. I would like to emphasize that this is a suggestion rather than a criticism of the paper's originality.

Significance: The authors primarily focus on the application of their algorithm to clustering, but given the vast amount of literature on clustering, comparing only to k-means++ may not be sufficient to demonstrate the practical usefulness of the algorithm. Furthermore, the proofs in this paper, even in the appendix, are often sketchy, making it difficult to evaluate the technical correctness of the algorithm. I also suspect that the algorithm is an application of expectation-propagation to matrix factorization, which has been previously explored by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
