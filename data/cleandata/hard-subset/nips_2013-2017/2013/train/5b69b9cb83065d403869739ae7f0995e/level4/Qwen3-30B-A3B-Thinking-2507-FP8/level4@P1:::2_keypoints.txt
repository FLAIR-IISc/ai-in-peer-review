- Paper introduces an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural priors and applies it to clustering.

Strengths:
- Novel extension of AMP to general low-rank matrix factorization with structural priors.
- Empirical results show AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means (and compared to VBMF) especially in high dimensions.
- Mathematical derivation is well-structured and links the Bayesian formulation to AMP and variational Bayes.
- Generally well-written with appropriate notation once understood.

Weaknesses:
- The clustering ↔ matrix factorization connection is not novel and is insufficiently acknowledged.
- Key algorithm derivation is relegated to the supplement; main text lacks an outline of the derivation.
- Evaluation uses a non-standard clustering-as-matrix-factorization task and omits standard matrix-reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix).
- Notation is overly complicated (excessive hats/tildes/superscripts) and reduces readability.
- The role of the factor m in the noise variance and resulting row/column asymmetry is not clearly explained.

Recommendation / impact:
- Valid technical contribution but needs clearer presentation, explicit prior-work acknowledgment, and standard evaluations to strengthen its case.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel AMP application to low-rank reconstruction with structural priors; strong clustering results; solid mathematical presentation.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Prior work reduces novelty; derivation buried in supplement; non-standard experiments limit perceived significance; notation hinders clarity.

Final recommendation:
- Weak acceptance pending substantial improvements to presentation, justification, and evaluation.
