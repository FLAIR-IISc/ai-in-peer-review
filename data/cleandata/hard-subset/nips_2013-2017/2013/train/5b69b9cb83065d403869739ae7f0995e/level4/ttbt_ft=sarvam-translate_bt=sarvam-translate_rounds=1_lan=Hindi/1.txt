The authors present an algorithm for reconstructing matrices under noisy observations. The algorithm looks at low-rank matrices with additional assumptions on specific setting factors and uses an approximate message passing approach to accelerate the classical, computationally expensive, Bayesian approach. The authors also connect matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering, which is an interesting application domain for the proposed algorithm.

To my knowledge, the approximate message passing approach for matrix reconstruction is new and interesting. The relationship between low-rank matrix factorization and K-means is quite well known (for example, PCA provides a factor two approximation algorithm for K-means). However, this article allows the authors to provide a good practical evaluation of their algorithm and compare it to K-means and K-means++. Interestingly, their approach seems faster and more efficient than classical K-means and K-means ++ according to their empirical data. The authors compare both Frobenius norm residuals, as well as actual clustering, which is a nice feature of their empirical evaluations.

The main weakness of the paper is that the proposed algorithm comes with some theoretical guarantees in terms of convergence. This is expected, as many other algorithms for K-means also have only weak properties. The authors would like to cite more papers in theoretical computer science that provide provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function. A solid paper presenting an approximate message passing algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means. Promising empirical evaluations, somewhat weak theoretical results.
