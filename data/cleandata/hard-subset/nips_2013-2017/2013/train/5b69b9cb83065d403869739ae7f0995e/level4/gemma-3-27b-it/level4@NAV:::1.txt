This paper introduces an algorithm for reconstructing matrices from noisy data, focusing on low-rank matrices with specific factor properties. The authors leverage an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the typically computationally demanding Bayesian methods. A noteworthy aspect of this work is the connection established between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, opening up a relevant application area for their algorithm.

The application of AMP to matrix reconstruction appears to be a novel and promising contribution. While the relationship between low-rank matrix factorization and K-means is established (with PCA, for instance, offering a 2-approximation for K-means), this work facilitates a compelling experimental validation, enabling comparisons with standard K-means and K-means++ algorithms. Empirical results suggest the proposed approach achieves faster and more efficient performance. The evaluation is thorough, considering both Frobenius norm residuals and clustering quality.

A limitation of the paper is the relative lack of formal convergence guarantees for the algorithm. This is a common challenge in the field, as many K-means algorithms also lack strong theoretical foundations. However, referencing relevant work from Theoretical Computer Science that *does* offer provable accuracy for the K-means objective function would be beneficial. Overall, this is a strong paper presenting an AMP-based solution for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, supported by encouraging experimental findings, despite the limited theoretical analysis.
