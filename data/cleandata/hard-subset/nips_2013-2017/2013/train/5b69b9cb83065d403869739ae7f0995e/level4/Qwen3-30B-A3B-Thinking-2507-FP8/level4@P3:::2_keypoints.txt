- Proposal: An AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending AMP from sparse vectors to matrix factorization.  
- Application: Reformulates clustering as a constrained matrix reconstruction problem and applies the AMP algorithm to clustering.  
- Claim: AMP-based clustering handles marginalization and MAP estimation and outperforms Lloyd’s K-means on synthetic and real data.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on recent AMP advances and extends IterFac to general rank; claims novelty in enforcing “one datum per cluster” constraint in the reconstruction.  
- Strength — Quality: The algorithm is theoretically derived and evaluated with comprehensive experiments against multiple baselines, showing consistent improvements.  
- Strength — Originality: Applies AMP to matrix reconstruction/clustering, a new application area for AMP methods.  
- Strength — Significance: Offers a principled, efficient (linear-cost) approach with theoretical guarantees, promising for high-dimensional settings.  
- Strength — Clarity: Introduction and problem formulation are well-structured and experiments are clearly presented.  
- Weakness — Theory justification: Large-dimension approximations underlying AMP are not sufficiently justified; more theoretical or empirical validation needed.  
- Weakness — Prior work acknowledgment: Overstates novelty of clustering–matrix-factorization connection and needs stronger citation/discussion of prior work.  
- Weakness — Notation and presentation: Excessively complex notation (hats/tildes/subscripts) and key derivations left to supplementary material hinder readability.  
- Weakness — Algorithm detail: The use of an m factor in the noise variance (mτ) and resulting row–column asymmetry is unexplained and should be clarified.  
- Weakness — Applicability: Lacks evaluation on standard matrix-reconstruction applications (e.g., recommender systems) to demonstrate broader utility.  
- Minor issues: Small grammatical mistakes; overall paper is strong but requires revisions.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept pending major revisions addressing theoretical justification, prior-work framing, notation clarity, and additional evaluations.
