- Presents an AMP-based algorithm for matrix reconstruction from noisy observations.  
- Targets low-rank matrices with specific assumptions on underlying factors.  
- Uses Approximate Message Passing to speed up classical Bayesian methods.  
- Establishes a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering.  
- Application of AMP to matrix reconstruction is novel and interesting.  
- Notes the well-known relation between low-rank factorizations and K-means (e.g., PCA factor-two approximation).  
- Leverages this connection for a compelling experimental comparison to K-means and K-means++.  
- Empirical results indicate the proposed approach is faster and more efficient than classical K-means methods.  
- Evaluation compares both Frobenius norm residuals and resulting clusterings, strengthening empirical claims.  
- Primary weakness: limited theoretical guarantees on algorithm convergence.  
- Suggests citing more Theoretical Computer Science work that provides provable algorithms for the K-means objective.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper with promising experiments but somewhat weak theoretical results.
