- Proposes a novel AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction that incorporates additional structural priors.  
- Derivation is from a Bayesian framework, yielding a flexible approach to matrix reconstruction.  
- Formulates K-means clustering as a special case of low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies the algorithm to clustering.  
- Provides a clear, concise summary of prior work and highlights connections between low-rank reconstruction and K-means.  
- Experimental results show the proposed method outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in K-means loss and accuracy.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation with comparisons to Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++, showing strong performance, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Main strength is bridging low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, offering a novel application domain for AMP.  
- The Bayesian motivation and AMP derivation are well-presented and well-motivated.  
- Lacks detailed theoretical guarantees on convergence and performance of the algorithm.  
- Insufficient analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Limited comparisons to other algorithms in terms of theoretical guarantees and computational complexity.  
- Overall a solid empirical contribution that would be strengthened by more theory and complexity analysis.
