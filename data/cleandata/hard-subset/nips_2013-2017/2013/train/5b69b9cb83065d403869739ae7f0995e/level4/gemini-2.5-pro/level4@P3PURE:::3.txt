This paper introduces an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization. The authors frame matrix factorization as a generalization of the clustering problem and, based on this connection, specialize their algorithm for clustering applications. The proposed clustering algorithm is then experimentally compared against k-means++.

**Quality**

The mathematical derivations throughout the paper and its appendix are often sketchy, omitting many non-trivial steps. This lack of detail raises concerns about the paper's mathematical rigor. Specific issues include:

1.  In the Appendix (line 139), the justification for the second term of equation (11) being O(1/√m) is unclear. Additionally, the parameter β appears to be omitted from the discussion in Section 2.
2.  The asymptotic analysis, including the derivation of equation (19), appears to implicitly assume that m and n grow at the same rate. The analysis in this section is terse, and its mathematical rigor is questionable without more explicit assumptions and detailed proofs.
3.  On line 167, the paper approximates the expectation of A_{il}^2 with mτ_w^2. This is questionable, as A is observed data, while mτ_w^2 is the variance of the likelihood. The rationale for this approximation needs to be clarified and justified.
4.  The paper claims convergence to the MAP solution in the limit of β, but the argument is incomplete. The message passing algorithm minimizes KL divergence. It is not necessarily true that the limit of the minimum KL divergence is equal to the minimum of the limit of the KL divergence (i.e., lim_{β→∞} min KL = min lim_{β→∞} KL). This interchange of the limit and minimization operators requires justification.
5.  The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief to be followed. Specifically, the derivation of equation (17) is not explained.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-structured, and its central claims are presented in a way that is easy to follow.

**Originality**

The originality of the proposed algorithm is a significant concern. The method focuses on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior, which strongly suggests it is an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP). The use of EP for matrix factorization is not novel and was previously explored by Stern et al. ("Matchbox: Large Scale Bayesian Recommendations," WWW 2009). Furthermore, the update procedures detailed in Section 1 of the Appendix appear to be standard EP updates. The authors should clarify how their method differs from this prior work.

On a related note, there appears to be an interesting connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the "clutter problem" described in Minka's original EP paper, particularly if the likelihood in the clutter problem is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. This is noted as a potential connection for the authors to consider, not as a criticism of the work's originality.

**Significance**

The significance of this work is limited by several factors. First, while the paper focuses on the application to clustering, the experimental comparison is limited to only k-means++. Given the vast literature on clustering, this is insufficient to demonstrate the practical utility of the proposed algorithm. Second, as noted under 'Quality', the technical correctness is difficult to evaluate due to the sketchy proofs provided throughout the paper and appendix. Finally, the potential lack of originality is a major concern; the method appears to be a direct application of Expectation Propagation to matrix factorization, a problem previously addressed by Stern et al. (2009).
