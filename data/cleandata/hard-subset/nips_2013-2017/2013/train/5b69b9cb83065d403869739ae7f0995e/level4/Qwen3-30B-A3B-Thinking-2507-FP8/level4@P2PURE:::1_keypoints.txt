- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations with extra assumptions on matrix factors.
- Uses Approximate Message Passing to accelerate computationally intensive Bayesian reconstruction.
- Establishes a connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, offering a novel application domain.
- AMP-based approach appears novel and well-motivated.
- Empirical comparison against k-means and k-means++ shows superior speed and efficiency.
- Evaluation is comprehensive, assessing both Frobenius norm residuals and clustering quality.
- Primary limitation: absence of strong theoretical convergence guarantees; should cite provable K-means literature.
- Overall assessment: promising paper with strong empirical results but limited theoretical foundations.
