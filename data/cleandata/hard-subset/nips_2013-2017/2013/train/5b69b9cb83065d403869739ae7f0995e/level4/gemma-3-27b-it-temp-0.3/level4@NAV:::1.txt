This paper introduces an algorithm for reconstructing matrices from noisy data, focusing on low-rank matrices with specific factor constraints, and leverages an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to improve computational efficiency over traditional Bayesian methods. A noteworthy aspect of the work is the connection established between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, opening up a relevant application area for the proposed techniques.

The application of AMP to matrix reconstruction appears to be a novel and promising contribution. While the relationship between low-rank factorization and K-means is established (with PCA, for instance, offering a 2-approximation for K-means), the authors effectively utilize this connection to provide a compelling experimental evaluation. Their results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm achieves faster and more efficient performance compared to standard K-means and K-means++ implementations. The experimental design is well-considered, evaluating performance based on both Frobenius norm residual and clustering accuracy.

A primary limitation of the work is the lack of strong theoretical convergence guarantees. This is a common challenge in this field, as many K-means algorithms also lack robust theoretical foundations. The authors could strengthen the paper by referencing relevant work in Theoretical Computer Science that offers provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective. Overall, this is a solid contribution presenting AMP algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, supported by promising empirical results, though limited by weaker theoretical analysis.
