- Paper proposes a BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with large-m, large-N approximations and evaluates it via clustering-as-matrix-reconstruction.
- Evaluation uses a non-standard clustering task (K-means) instead of common benchmarks (e.g., Netflix), making competitiveness on standard matrix reconstruction unclear.
- Algorithm appears to be a relatively straightforward BP application compared to prior variational message-passing work; novelty hinges on the large-size approximations.
- The validity of the N → ∞ and m → ∞ approximations is only in the Supplementary Material and is not empirically or clearly justified in the main paper.
- Serious citation and positioning issues: casting clustering as matrix factorization is presented ambiguously and prior work is not properly cited.
- Expectation Propagation (EP) is not cited; the relation between EP and the proposed BP-style algorithm should be explained and BP should be stated earlier (e.g., abstract).
- Strength: the algorithm is interesting, potentially useful, and the mathematics is generally followable.
- Experiments show an advantage over Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF, but the new evaluation metric introduces uncertainty about real-world competitiveness.
- Core derivation is relegated to the Supplementary Material; an outline should be included in the main paper.
- Mathematical presentation is hard to follow: cumbersome notation, excessive hats/tildes/superscripts, and some expressions could be algebraically simplified for readability.
- Specific notation issues: inconsistent use of τ_w vs τ, unnecessary time superscripts, unclear β subscripts on functions/messages, and unclear vector notation (tilde for column vectors).
- Exposition could be improved by explicitly noting simple relationships (e.g., Eq.5 = −log Eq.2) and pointing out symmetries to shorten algorithm descriptions.
- Functions in Eq.10 and G_β should be explained in words (means, scaled covariances) when defined.
- The role of the factor m in the additive noise variance is unclear and may break row–column symmetry; the paper should explain its significance.
- Minor issues: inconsistent capitalization (N vs m), missing citation for VBMF-MA implementation, and stylistic language fixes throughout the text.
- Supplementary Material problems: unclear presentation of Eq.5, an opaque big-O step (Eq.14→15), insufficient connection to prior approximation techniques, and a desire for more explanatory diagrams.
- Language and phrasing need polishing (numerous suggested edits provided), and the title could be clearer about using an approximate message-passing algorithm.
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage revision addressing derivation clarity, positioning/citations, empirical benchmarking, notation cleanup, and supplementary clarifications.
