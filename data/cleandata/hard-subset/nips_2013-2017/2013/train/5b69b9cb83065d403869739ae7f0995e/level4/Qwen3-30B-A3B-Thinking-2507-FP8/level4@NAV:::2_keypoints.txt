- Proposes a BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and frames clustering as matrix reconstruction.  
- Evaluation compares the method to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF but omits the standard Netflix dataset without justification.  
- Framing clustering as matrix factorization is non-standard and complicates generalization to conventional reconstruction tasks.  
- The algorithm is essentially a straightforward BP application to a problem previously addressed by Variational Message Passing, so novelty is limited.  
- Uses asymptotic approximations (N→∞, m→∞) whose validity is untested and poorly explained in the Supplementary Material.  
- Misrepresents prior work: fails to acknowledge the established matrix-factorization↔clustering connection and omits EP citation; BP foundation should be stated in the abstract.  
- Positive: presents a potentially useful, accessible algorithm with promising mathematics.  
- K-means comparison shows clear benefits for the new method despite the unconventional evaluation criterion.  
- Significant clarity problems: key derivations are missing from the main paper and pushed to the Supplementary Material.  
- Notation is cumbersome and inconsistent (excessive hats/tildes/subscripts, unclear β usage, redundant superscripts), impeding comprehension.  
- Missing explanations for several equations and symbols (e.g., 1/(m τ_w) factors, meaning of G_β, role of β→∞, and how m affects noise variance/asymmetry).  
- Algorithm 2’s row–column symmetry is unnoted and Section 4 is overly verbose and notationally cluttered.  
- Miscellaneous issues: English needs polishing, Section 5 lacks a primary citation for VBMF-MA implementation, and the Supplementary Material contains typos and notation gaps.  
- Figures: Figure 1 is helpful; additional diagrams would improve clarity.  
- Overall assessment: promising mathematical approach and a useful niche contribution, but clarity and scholarly shortcomings require revision before acceptance.
