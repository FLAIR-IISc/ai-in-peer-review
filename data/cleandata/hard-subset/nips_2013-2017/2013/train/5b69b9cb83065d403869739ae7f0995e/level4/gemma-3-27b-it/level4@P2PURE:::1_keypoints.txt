- Introduces an AMP-based algorithm to reconstruct low-rank matrices from noisy observations, accelerating Bayesian approaches.  
- Establishes a connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, providing an application area.  
- Applying AMP to matrix reconstruction is novel and valuable.  
- Builds on known links between low-rank factorization and K-means (e.g., PCA), but enables robust experimental comparisons.  
- Empirical results show the proposed method is faster and more efficient than standard K-means and K-means++.  
- Evaluation uses both Frobenius norm residuals and clustering accuracy, strengthening the experimental validation.  
- Main limitation: limited theoretical guarantees on algorithm convergence.  
- Suggestion: cite more Theoretical Computer Science work on provably accurate K-means algorithms.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with promising experiments but comparatively weaker theoretical results.
