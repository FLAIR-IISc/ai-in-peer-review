- Introduces an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization and frames matrix factorization as a generalization of clustering.  
- Tailors the message passing algorithm specifically to clustering and compares it experimentally to k-means++.  
- Mathematical derivations are incomplete and omit several non-trivial steps, including in the appendix.  
- Missing parameter β in Section 2 and unclear justification for the claim that the second term of equation (11) is O(1/√m).  
- Analysis appears to assume m and n grow at the same rate (e.g., derivation of equation (19)), but asymptotic arguments are terse and not fully justified.  
- The claim that m τw^2 equals the expectation of A_il^2 is questionable because A is observed data and m τw^2 represents only likelihood variance.  
- The convergence argument from finite-β message passing to the MAP problem is not justified; swapping limit and min for KL is nontrivial and unproven.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 is overly concise; derivation of equation (17) is not shown.  
- Paper is well-organized and main contributions are clearly presented, aiding readability.  
- The algorithm seems to primarily estimate first and second posterior moments and appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP).  
- Prior work (Stern et al., 2009) applied EP to matrix factorization and should be discussed; connections to Minka’s clutter problem (2001) may be relevant.  
- Experimental comparison is limited to k-means++; broader comparisons to state-of-the-art clustering methods are needed to establish practical significance.
