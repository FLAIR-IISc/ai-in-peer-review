The authors propose an algorithm for matrix reconstruction under noisy observations. The setting considers low-rank matrices with additional assumptions on the latent factors, and employs an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate a classical, computationally expensive Bayesian method. The paper also draws a connection between matrix reconstruction and k-means clustering, offering an interesting application domain for the proposed algorithms.

To the best of my knowledge, applying AMP to matrix reconstruction in this way is novel and interesting. While the connections between low-rank factorizations and k-means are well known (e.g., PCA yields a factor-two approximation algorithm for k-means), this linkage enables a clear and relevant experimental evaluation. The authors compare their approach to k-means and k-means++, and, based on their empirical results, the proposed method appears faster and more efficient than these classical baselines. It is also a strength that the evaluation reports both the Frobenius-norm residual and the quality of the resulting clusterings.

The main weakness is the lack of strong theoretical guarantees, particularly regarding convergence. This is somewhat expected, as many algorithms for k-means also offer only limited guarantees, but it remains a shortcoming. The paper would be strengthened by citing additional work in Theoretical Computer Science that provides provably accurate algorithms for the k-means objective.

Overall, this is a solid paper presenting AMP-based algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction with a compelling link to k-means. The experimental evaluation is promising, though the theoretical results are relatively weak.
