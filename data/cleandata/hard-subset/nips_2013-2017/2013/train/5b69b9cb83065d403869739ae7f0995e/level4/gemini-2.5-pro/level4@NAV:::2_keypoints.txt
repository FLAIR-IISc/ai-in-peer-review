- Paper proposes a BP-derived algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using large-dimension approximations and compares it to K-means and VBMF.
- Evaluation casts clustering as matrix factorization, which is non-standard and limits extrapolation to conventional benchmarks (e.g., Netflix).
- Lack of standard dataset benchmarks (Netflix) or justification raises doubts about true competitiveness versus existing methods.
- Algorithm is a relatively direct application of BP/Variational Message Passing, so contribution feels incremental rather than groundbreaking.
- The large-dimension approximations are the most interesting part but are underdeveloped and not experimentally validated.
- Treatment of prior work is problematic: framing clustering-as-matrix-factorization as novel is misleading and lacks citation of established literature.
- Paper should cite and discuss Expectation Propagation and clearly state the BP basis of the algorithm earlier (e.g., in the abstract).
- Paper is engaging and mathematically tractable; derivations appear reproducible and the K-means comparison shows some empirical advantage.
- Core technical sections lack clarity; full derivation is in supplement and a high-level main-paper outline is needed.
- Notation is cumbersome and inconsistent (excessive hats/tildes/subscripts, unnecessary iteration superscripts); simpler notation and assignment arrows recommended.
- Mathematical exposition needs clearer connections between equations and explanations of introduced functions (e.g., G_β as scaled covariance).
- Inconsistencies around β notation and message labeling should be fixed; treat β as a global parameter with clear limiting statements.
- Important theoretical/practical roles of parameters (e.g., m in additive noise variance) and row-column symmetry require clarification.
- Many minor editorial and grammatical issues throughout the text that distract from readability.
- Specific questions: clarify vector notation (tilde), capitalization of matrix dimensions (N vs m), and which VBMF reference guided VBMF-MA implementation.
- Supplementary-material suggestions: rewrite certain PDF expressions, clarify O(m) term steps, and better explain novelty relative to prior message-parameter approximations.
- More factor-graph diagrams and a clearer Algorithm 2 presentation (highlight row-column symmetry) would improve comprehension.
- Overall recommendation: promising and publishable after substantial revision to address clarity, notation, prior work citations, experimental validation, and approximation verification.
