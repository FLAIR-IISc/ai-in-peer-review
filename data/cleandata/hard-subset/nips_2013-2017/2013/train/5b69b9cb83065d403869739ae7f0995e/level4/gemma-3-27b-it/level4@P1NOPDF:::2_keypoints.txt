- Introduces a new BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction applied to clustering via matrix factorization.
- Uses simplifying approximations assuming large matrix dimensions (m, N → ∞) detailed in the Supplementary Material.
- Evaluates the algorithm against Lloyd’s K-means (by casting clustering as matrix reconstruction) and against VBMF.
- Experimental evaluation on a non-standard task (clustering-as-factorization) makes it hard to extrapolate performance to standard benchmarks.
- Paper should compare to standard datasets used by prior VBMF work (e.g., Netflix) or explain why that isn’t feasible.
- The method is a relatively direct application of BP/Variational Message Passing, so not fundamentally groundbreaking.
- The asymptotic approximations (m, N → ∞) are interesting but neither clearly explained nor empirically validated.
- Serious citation omissions: prior work connecting matrix factorization to clustering is not acknowledged.
- Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to the algorithm are not cited or explained.
- The paper delays stating that the core algorithm is BP until Section 4.1; this should appear in the abstract.
- Paper is interesting and potentially useful; mathematics seems reproducible and experiments show benefits.
- Derivation relegated to Supplementary Material; an outline should be included in the main paper.
- Notation and mathematical presentation are cumbersome and hard to parse (excessive hats, tildes, subscripts/superscripts).
- Specific unclear equation formatting and terms (e.g., factors 1/(m τ_w) in eqs. 9a–9e) should be simplified or explained.
- Inconsistent use of β subscripts and message notation; suggest eliminating unnecessary subscripts and simplifying temporal notation.
- Key functions (fβ, Gβ) and their meanings should be explained immediately where defined.
- Important algorithmic questions unanswered: role of the m factor in noise variance and its effect on row-column symmetry.
- Several unclear or unjustified steps in the Supplementary Material (e.g., big-O step from eq.14→15) need clarification.
- The relation of the approximation technique to prior message-approximation literature is not made explicit.
- Requests for implementation details/citations for competing methods (e.g., VBMF-MA) to clarify comparisons.
- Numerous English and stylistic issues and suggested title revision; recommend copy-editing and more diagrams (Figure 1 helpful).
- Conclusion: algorithm merits publication eventually but requires clearer exposition, better scholarship, more convincing experiments, and revisions before acceptance.
