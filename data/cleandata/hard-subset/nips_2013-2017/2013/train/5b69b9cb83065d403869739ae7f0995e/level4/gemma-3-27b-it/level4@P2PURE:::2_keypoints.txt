- Paper proposes a new BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using large-N and large-m approximations.
- Algorithm evaluated by casting clustering as matrix reconstruction and compared to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.
- Evaluation is non-standard, making it hard to extrapolate performance to conventional matrix-reconstruction tasks.
- Authors should evaluate on standard benchmarks (e.g., Netflix) or explain why such datasets are impractical.
- The contribution is novel but incremental — a fairly direct application of BP/EP rather than a major breakthrough.
- The large-N and large-m approximations are interesting but their validity is not empirically tested or clarified.
- Important prior work is missing or unclear: matrix-factorization-for-clustering literature and Expectation Propagation (EP) should be cited and related to the method.
- The paper fails to state early that the core algorithm is an application of Belief Propagation; this should appear in the abstract.
- Derivation is relegated to supplementary material; an outline should be included in the main paper.
- Mathematical presentation is hard to follow: excessive hats/tildes/subscripts/superscripts and cumbersome notation reduce readability.
- Specific equation and notation fixes suggested (factor out common terms, combine terms, drop unnecessary hats/t’s, simplify τw to τ).
- Inconsistencies and unclear propagation of β subscripts; need to state limit behavior (β → ∞) explicitly.
- Unanswered technical questions remain (role of m factor, source of row-column asymmetry, vector notation conventions, capitalization of N vs m).
- Experimental details unclear: cite the primary reference for VBMF-MA and clarify which VBMF papers are used for comparison.
- Supplementary material has unclear steps (e.g., transition from eq.14→15, big-O of m terms) and insufficiently relates approximations to prior work.
- Exposition improvements needed: clarify meanings of equations (e.g., eq.5 as −log eq.2), define fβ and Gβ more clearly, and show row/column symmetry in algorithms.
- Presentation enhancements recommended: include more diagrams (Figure 1 helpful), move key derivation outline into main text, and improve plot readability for black-and-white printing.
- English and writing issues distract; numerous phrasing and wording corrections are suggested and the title could be improved.
- Overall judgment: interesting and potentially useful algorithm with accessible math and promising experiments, but the paper needs clearer exposition, better scholarship, additional experiments, and validation of approximations before acceptance.
