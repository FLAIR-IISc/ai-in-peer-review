- Proposes a new belief-propagation (BP) derived algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with two large‑dimension approximations (N→∞ and m→∞).  
- Evaluation is unconventional: clustering is cast as matrix reconstruction and compared mainly to Lloyd’s K‑means and VBMF.  
- Hard to judge competitiveness on standard benchmarks; should evaluate on Netflix or similar datasets or justify their exclusion.  
- The method reads as a direct BP application to problems previously handled by Variational Message Passing, so novelty is limited.  
- The large‑N and large‑m approximations are a key contribution but are described only in the supplement and not empirically validated.  
- Positioning relative to prior work is weak: matrix‑factorization for clustering is presented as if novel—cite or acknowledge prior art.  
- Expectation Propagation (EP) should be cited and the relationship between EP and the proposed BP method clarified; BP framing should appear earlier (e.g., in the abstract).  
- Experiments show an advantage for the proposed method, but the non‑standard metric raises skepticism about generality.  
- Main derivation is relegated to supplemental material; an outline should appear in the main text.  
- Mathematical presentation is hard to parse: overloaded hats/tildes/subscripts, confusing symbols (τ_w vs τ), and awkward iteration notation.  
- Specific equation/readability issues: factors of 1/(m τ_w) could be factored, some terms combined, and functions in eq.10 should be defined where introduced.  
- Inconsistent or unclear use of β subscripts on messages, f, and G; recommend treating β as a global parameter or removing unnecessary subscripts.  
- The role of factor m in the additive noise variance and its relation to row/column asymmetry needs explicit clarification.  
- Missing implementation citation: specify which VBMF reference guided the VBMF‑MA implementation.  
- Supplementary material needs more detail (e.g., steps from eq.14→15, justification of O(m) terms) and clearer links to prior approximation techniques.  
- Notation/format questions to resolve: meaning of tilde for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization of N vs m, and clearer diagramming (more figures helpful).  
- English/style fixes suggested throughout and a more descriptive title recommended (e.g., include “approximate message passing”).  
- Recommendation: promising algorithm and results, but substantial revision required for clarity, validation, and stronger positioning before acceptance.
