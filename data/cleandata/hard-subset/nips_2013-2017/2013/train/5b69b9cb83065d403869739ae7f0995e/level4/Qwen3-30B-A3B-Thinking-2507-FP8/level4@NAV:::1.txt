

The authors propose an algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations, leveraging additional structural assumptions on the matrix factors and employing an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) framework to accelerate the computationally intensive classical Bayesian approach. The work further establishes a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, presenting a novel application domain for the proposed methodology.  

To the best of my knowledge, the application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction represents a novel and compelling contribution. While the relationship between low-rank factorizations and K-means (e.g., PCA as a factor-two approximation for K-means) is well-established, this linkage enables the authors to conduct a thorough experimental evaluation against k-means and k-means++. Their empirical results indicate that the proposed approach achieves superior speed and efficiency compared to these baselines. Notably, the evaluation assesses both the Frobenius norm residual and the quality of the resulting clusterings, providing a comprehensive empirical assessment.  

The primary limitation lies in the absence of strong theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm. This is somewhat expected given the common theoretical challenges in K-means optimization, but the authors should consider citing additional theoretical computer science literature on provably accurate K-means algorithms to strengthen this aspect. Overall, this is a solid contribution presenting an AMP-based method for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, with a promising experimental evaluation and modest theoretical foundations.
