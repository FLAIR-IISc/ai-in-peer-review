- Paper introduces a new Belief Propagation (BP) message-passing algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction.  
- Algorithm uses large-dimensional simplifying approximations (N, m → ∞) detailed in the Supplementary Material.  
- Evaluation frames clustering as a matrix reconstruction task and compares against Lloyd's K-means and VBMF.  
- Evaluating on clustering (non-standard task) makes it hard to generalize results to conventional matrix reconstruction benchmarks.  
- Lack of standard dataset comparisons (e.g., Netflix) weakens claims of competitiveness versus prior work.  
- The algorithm is a relatively straightforward BP application to a problem previously tackled by Variational Message Passing, so novelty is limited.  
- Validity of the large-dimensional approximations is not directly tested or clearly justified in the main text.  
- Citation and scholarship issues: the matrix-factorization→clustering connection is presented as novel but is well-known and should be cited.  
- Expectation Propagation (EP) and the relation to EP/BP are not cited or discussed; the method should be labeled as BP earlier (e.g., in the abstract).  
- Paper is interesting and potentially useful; experiments indicate a benefit for the proposed algorithm.  
- Mathematics is generally comprehensible but hard to verify; derivations relegated to Supplementary Material hinder readability.  
- Notation is cluttered (excess hats, tildes, superscripts, inconsistent symbols like τ_w) and could be simplified for clarity.  
- Key equations and functions (e.g., eq.5→eq.2 relation, definitions around eq.10, role of β and G_β) need clearer explanation and placement.  
- Algorithm description is repetitive due to row-column symmetry and could be shortened by explicitly noting symmetry.  
- Technical questions remain unanswered: significance of the m factor in noise variance and whether it causes algorithm asymmetry.  
- Supplementary Material has clarity issues (e.g., ambiguous big-O step, notation suggestions, minor typos) and would benefit from more diagrams.  
- Experimental section and plots are clear overall but some figures print poorly in black-and-white.  
- Overall recommendation: algorithm merits publication but requires substantial revision to improve clarity, validate approximations, and correct citation/scholarship before acceptance.
