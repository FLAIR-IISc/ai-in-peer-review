- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural constraints, applied to clustering via reformulation.  
- Uses a Bayesian framework to incorporate structural priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity) on factor matrices.  
- AMP provides a computationally efficient alternative to variational Bayes and other expensive optimization methods.  
- Reformulates K-means as a low-rank reconstruction problem and claims to directly enforce the one-hot cluster-assignment constraint.  
- Two main contributions: (1) unified AMP for both marginalization and MAP estimation, and (2) application to clustering with exact assignment handling.  
- Experiments (synthetic and ORL face data) show AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ in accuracy and reconstruction error and converges faster.  
- Technical derivation is clear, building on prior AMP work, and includes theoretical foundations and convergence analysis.  
- Paper is well-organized and mathematically detailed, though some supplementary material lacks intuitive explanations.  
- Originality is limited by the established link between low-rank factorization and clustering, but the AMP-based implementation and exact-assignment handling are novel.  
- Significance demonstrated by practical advantages, faster convergence, and ability to incorporate structural priors for high-dimensional data.  
- Strengths: clear technical contribution, comprehensive experiments, superior accuracy and speed, and appropriate comparisons to prior work.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical convergence guarantees, not wholly novel connection to clustering, insufficient discussion of failure cases, and some derivation details are hard to follow.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions; recommend acceptance.
