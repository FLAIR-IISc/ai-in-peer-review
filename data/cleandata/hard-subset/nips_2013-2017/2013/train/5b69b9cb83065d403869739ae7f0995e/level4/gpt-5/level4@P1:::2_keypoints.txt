- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a β parameter interpolating posterior averaging (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), specialized to clustering via one-hot assignments.
- Casts K-means as constrained low-rank reconstruction and derives an AMP variant that adds debiasing and cluster-size effects to assignment updates.
- Empirical results on synthetic data and ORL faces show improvements over Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and a VBMF baseline in loss and accuracy.
- Positions work relative to prior AMP and VBMF literature but underplays known links between clustering and matrix factorization.
- Strength: unified β-parameterized AMP framework with explicit updates and clear K-means specialization.
- Strength: computationally efficient (O(mN) per iteration) with strong empirical clustering gains.
- Strength: algorithmic insights for clustering (self-debiasing and implicit small-cluster preference) derived from AMP corrections.
- Strength: Proposition 1 provides a useful optimality property and connection to ICM/coordinate descent.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation focuses on clustering, leaving performance on standard matrix-reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix) unclear.
- Weakness: derivation relies on large-system approximations (m,N→∞) presented in supplement and not empirically validated or summarized in main text.
- Weakness: positioning and citations are incomplete—must acknowledge prior work on clustering-as-factorization and cite EP and related BP-inspired algorithms earlier.
- Weakness: notation and presentation are cluttered and hard to parse; many redundant hats/tildes/subscripts and unclear definitions for f and G and β-dependence.
- Weakness: model scaling and role of the factor m in noise variance need clarification as potential source of row–column asymmetry.
- Weakness: experimental details lacking—clarify which VBMF variant was implemented, initialization sensitivity, and how τ is estimated in ORL experiments.
- Weakness: supplement needs clearer exposition for key steps (e.g., Eq.5, step from Eq.14→15, message-approximation novelty and links to prior work).
- Assessment: algorithm is plausible and original in extending AMP with β-path and one-hot assignment enforcement, but broader empirical support is needed.
- Assessment: significance currently stronger for clustering than for general matrix reconstruction due to limited benchmarks.
- Assessment: clarity needs improvement—streamline notation, give motivations for key equations, and consider explanatory figures.
- Minor questions: is tilde notation for column vectors standard; why N uppercase and m lowercase; several English phrasing issues to fix.
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage revision addressing positioning, exposition, and broader empirical validation.
