The authors present an algorithm for matrix reconstruction under noisy observations, focusing on low-rank matrices with additional assumptions on the factors. They employ an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to speed up the classical, computationally expensive, Bayesian method. Furthermore, the authors establish a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, providing an interesting application domain for their proposed algorithm.

To the best of my knowledge, the application of AMP to matrix reconstruction is novel and interesting. While the relationship between low-rank matrix factorizations and K-means is fairly well-established (e.g., PCA provides a factor two approximation algorithm for K-means), the authors utilize this connection to provide a comprehensive experimental evaluation of their algorithm. They compare their approach to K-means and K-means++, demonstrating that it is faster and more efficient according to their empirical results. Notably, the authors assess both the Frobenius norm residual and the actual clusterings, which adds depth to their experimental evaluation.

The primary weakness of the paper lies in the limited theoretical guarantees provided for the proposed algorithm's convergence. This is not unexpected, as many other K-means algorithms also exhibit weak properties. To strengthen their work, the authors may want to cite relevant papers in Theoretical Computer Science that offer provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the theoretical landscape surrounding their algorithm.

In conclusion, this paper presents a solid contribution to the development of AMP algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means. The experimental evaluation is promising, and while the theoretical results are somewhat weak, the authors demonstrate the potential of their approach through empirical evidence.
