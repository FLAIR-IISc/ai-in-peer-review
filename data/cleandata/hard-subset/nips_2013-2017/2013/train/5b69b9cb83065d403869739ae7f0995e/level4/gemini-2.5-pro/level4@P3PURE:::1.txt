This paper introduces an algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations, with additional assumptions on the matrix factors. The authors employ an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) framework to accelerate the computationally expensive Bayesian inference process. Furthermore, they establish a connection between this matrix reconstruction problem and K-means clustering, providing a compelling application domain for their proposed method.

To my knowledge, the application of Approximate Message Passing to matrix reconstruction is a novel and interesting contribution. While the connections between low-rank matrix factorization and K-means are generally known (e.g., the use of PCA to obtain a factor-two approximation for K-means), the authors leverage this link to conduct a strong experimental evaluation. Their method is compared against standard k-means and k-means++, and the empirical results suggest it is both faster and more efficient. A notable strength of the evaluation is its thoroughness, as the authors compare not only the Frobenius norm residual but also the quality of the resulting cluster assignments.

The primary weakness of the paper lies in the limited theoretical guarantees for the proposed algorithm's convergence. While it is true that many algorithms for K-means also lack strong theoretical properties, the paper would be strengthened by citing more of the literature from theoretical computer science that provides provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function.

In summary, this is a solid paper presenting an Approximate Message Passing approach for low-rank matrix reconstruction and k-means. The work is supported by a promising experimental evaluation, though its theoretical results are somewhat limited.
