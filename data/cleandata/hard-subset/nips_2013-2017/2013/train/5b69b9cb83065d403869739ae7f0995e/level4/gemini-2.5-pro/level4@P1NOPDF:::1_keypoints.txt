- Proposes an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations.  
- Works in a setting with additional assumptions on the matrix factors to enable the approach.  
- Draws a connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering as a motivating application.  
- Application of AMP to this matrix reconstruction problem appears novel and is a significant contribution.  
- Empirical evaluation compares against k-means and k-means++, showing the proposed method is faster and more efficient.  
- Evaluation uses multiple metrics, including Frobenius norm residual and clustering quality.  
- Primary weakness: lack of theoretical guarantees, especially convergence results.  
- Suggestion: cite and discuss relevant Theoretical Computer Science work that provides provable guarantees for K-means.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper with novel algorithm and promising experiments, but limited by missing theoretical results.
