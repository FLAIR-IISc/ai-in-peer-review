- Extends AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction, offering a unified framework for marginalization and MAP estimation.
- Demonstrates superior performance to Lloyd’s K-means in certain high-dimensional clustering experiments.
- Algorithm is grounded in a Bayesian framework and leverages theoretical insights from approximate message passing.
- Potentially computationally efficient and scalable due to AMP-based design.

- Evaluation is limited to a clustering formulation, making general performance on standard low-rank reconstruction benchmarks unclear.
- Lacks comparisons to established low-rank recovery methods (e.g., singular value thresholding, alternating minimization), weakening novelty claims.
- Core derivations rely on large-dimension approximations (m, N → ∞) whose validity and practical implications are not sufficiently examined.
- Key theoretical derivations are relegated to the supplement, hindering assessment of correctness and applicability.
- Initial framing downplays prior work connecting clustering and matrix factorization and insufficiently discusses connections to Expectation Propagation (EP).
- Mathematical notation and exposition are overly complex, reducing readability and obscuring core ideas.

- Paper has promising technical contributions and competitive empirical results, but presentation and evaluation gaps reduce impact.
- Recommendation: reject in current form, with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing major concerns.
- Required revisions: evaluate on standard low-rank matrix completion benchmarks, compare to a broader set of baselines, analyze and justify limiting approximations, explicitly contextualize prior work (including EP), and simplify/clarify notation and exposition.
