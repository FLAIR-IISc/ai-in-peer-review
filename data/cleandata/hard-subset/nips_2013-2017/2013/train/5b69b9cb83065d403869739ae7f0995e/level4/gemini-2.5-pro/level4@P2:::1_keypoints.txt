- Proposes a novel AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles a broad class of priors and both posterior mean and MAP estimation.  
- Formulates the problem in a Bayesian setting, allowing structural priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity) on matrix factors.  
- Extends AMP to the general-rank case and highlights differences from VB/ICM via additional “memory” terms; Proposition 1 characterizes fixed points.  
- Shows K-means clustering as a specific instance of the low-rank reconstruction problem and derives a corresponding AMP-based clustering algorithm.  
- AMP-KM includes novel correction terms (e.g., accounting for cluster size and self-influence) that distinguish it from Lloyd’s algorithm.  
- Empirically, AMP-based algorithms (AMP-KM, AMP-MA) outperform Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ on synthetic and ORL face data in loss and accuracy.  
- Reports fast convergence for AMP-MA as a practical advantage.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized; derivations are clear in final form with supplementary details.  
- Main weakness: lacks theoretical convergence guarantees and shows oscillatory behavior in practice, requiring ad-hoc stopping criteria.  
- Could better situate the K-means contribution within clustering literature, especially work on provable approximation algorithms.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel, well-motivated algorithm with strong empirical results and practical significance.  
- Con-acceptance: limited theoretical analysis due to absence of convergence proofs.  
- Overall: solid and valuable contribution whose empirical strengths outweigh the theoretical limitations.
