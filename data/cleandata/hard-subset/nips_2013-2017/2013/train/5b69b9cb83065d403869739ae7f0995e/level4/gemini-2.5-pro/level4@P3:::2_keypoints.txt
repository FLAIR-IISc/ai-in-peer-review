- Proposes a novel AMP-based Bayesian algorithm for general low-rank matrix reconstruction that unifies posterior marginalization and MAP via an inverse temperature β.  
- Applies this framework to K-means by casting clustering as low-rank matrix reconstruction with discrete constraints, yielding AMP-KM and AMP-MA algorithms.  
- Empirical results show AMP methods outperform Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and a VBMF baseline on synthetic data and the ORL face dataset in both K-means loss and clustering accuracy.  
- Novelty: extends AMP from rank-one to general-rank settings and provides a unified marginal/MAP inference approach.  
- Theoretical contribution: Proposition 1 shows AMP MAP fixed points are also ICM fixed points, suggesting AMP dynamics may avoid poor local minima.  
- Major clarity issues: core derivation is relegated to the supplement and the main paper lacks a high-level derivation sketch and intuition for key equations.  
- Algorithm presentation is dense and hard to parse, with cumbersome notation (many hats/tildes/superscripts) and opportunities for simple typographic simplifications.  
- Missing exposition: delayed explanations for key quantities (e.g., interpretation of f and G) and missing explicit links between equations reduce accessibility and reproducibility.  
- Scholarly/contextual oversights: presents clustering-as-matrix-factorization as novel despite prior work and fails to credit that connection adequately.  
- BP/EP relation neglected: the Belief Propagation foundation is only mentioned late and the relation to Expectation Propagation is not discussed or cited.  
- Experimental evaluation is narrow: restricting experiments to clustering makes it hard to judge general utility for low-rank reconstruction; no collaborative-filtering benchmarks (e.g., Netflix) are included.  
- For acceptance: novel, non-trivial AMP extension; strong empirical clustering performance; interesting theoretical links to classic methods.  
- Against acceptance: significant clarity and presentation flaws, citation oversights, and a limited experimental scope.  
- Suggestions: simplify notation, include a derivation sketch in the main text, expand intuition for algorithmic components (role of m, correction terms), clarify difficult supplement steps, state BP connection early, discuss EP, and specify which VBMF variant was used.  
- Minor edits: improve article usage and minor grammar for readability.
