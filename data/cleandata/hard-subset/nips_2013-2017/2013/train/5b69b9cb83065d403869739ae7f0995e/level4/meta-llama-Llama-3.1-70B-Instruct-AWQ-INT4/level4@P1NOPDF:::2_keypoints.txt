- Proposes a novel BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using large-N, large-m approximations and evaluates it by casting clustering as matrix factorization.

- Evaluation on clustering (non-standard benchmark) limits generalizability; authors should test on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) or justify why they cannot.

- Using a new evaluation criterion for BP vs VBMF raises doubts about real competitiveness; comparison to prior work is insufficient.

- Algorithm is a relatively straightforward application of BP/variational ideas rather than a major breakthrough; the large-N/m approximations are the most interesting part.

- Validity of the large-N, large-m approximations is not tested and their derivation is only in the Supplementary Material; explanation should be clearer and included in main text.

- Serious citation and scholarship issues: the paper implies novelty of clustering-as-matrix-factorization despite it being well-known; prior work should be cited or its commonality stated.

- Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to the proposed method are not cited or discussed and should be included.

- Paper is interesting and potentially useful; mathematics is generally followable and experiments indicate benefit over competitors.

- Key derivation placed in Supplementary Material; an outline should be in the main paper to aid verification and clarity.

- Mathematical exposition is weak: excessive notation (hats, tildes, subscripts, superscripts) and awkward expressions make the algorithm hard to follow.

- Specific equation-level suggestions: factor out common terms (e.g., 1/m τ_w), combine like terms in equations 9b/9e, and explain relationships between equations (e.g., eq.5 vs eq.2, eq.8 vs eq.2^β).

- Notational inconsistencies and confusions: usage of β subscripts, tilde for column vectors, and inconsistent matrix dimension symbols (N vs m) should be simplified and standardized.

- Missing citation needed for VBMF-MA implementation details in Section 5.

- Supplementary-material clarifications requested: rewrite eq.5 as product of exponentials, clarify step from eq.14 to 15, and state the novelty of the derivation in the main text.

- English and grammar issues throughout with many suggested textual corrections; language polishing is needed.

- Figure 1 in the Supplementary Material is helpful; adding more diagrams would improve clarity.

- Overall: paper contains interesting math and a useful algorithm worthy of publication but requires significant improvements in evaluation, citations, clarity, and scholarship before acceptance.
