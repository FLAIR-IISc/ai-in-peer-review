- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that incorporates structural priors (sparsity, non-negativity) beyond low-rankness.  
- Extends AMP from sparse vector and rank-one matrix settings to general matrix factorization with an efficient O(mN) per-iteration complexity.  
- Introduces a unified algorithmic framework parameterized by β that interpolates between marginalization and MAP estimation, generalizing prior AMP approaches.  
- Applies the framework to K-means by reformulating clustering as a constrained low-rank matrix reconstruction, addressing the assignment-of-exactly-one-cluster requirement.  
- Correctly situates the work relative to recent AMP and matrix factorization literature and highlights differences from prior clustering-factorization connections.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound, connects to variational Bayes, and includes analysis of fixed points and relationships to ICM (Proposition 1).  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive (synthetic and ORL Face Database) and shows consistent improvements in clustering accuracy and convergence speed, especially for r ≥ 5.  
- Clarity and presentation are generally good with logical derivations, consistent notation, and sufficient supplementary technical details; initialization description could be clearer.  
- Originality is strong as the first systematic AMP treatment of general low-rank reconstruction with flexible structural priors and a novel handling of cluster assignments.  
- Significance is substantial: provides an efficient, practical method with advantages in high-dimensional clustering where K-means struggles.  
- Main weakness: limited theoretical analysis of global convergence and iteration bounds for the general AMP algorithm beyond the MAP case.  
- Minor issues: K-means assignment constraint is handled implicitly via a discrete prior rather than explicit enforcement in Equation 6, and there are small formatting/notation inconsistencies.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, well-executed experiments and theory, recommended for acceptance.
