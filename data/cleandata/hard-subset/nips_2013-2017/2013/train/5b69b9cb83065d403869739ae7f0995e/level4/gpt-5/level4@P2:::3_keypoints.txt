- Paper: proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a unified β parameter for posterior mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), and casts K-means–type clustering as a discrete-prior special case with AMP-based clustering showing improvements over Lloyd/K-means++.

- Main quality concern: derivations are terse and omit key steps; appendix lacks justifications making correctness hard to verify.

- Q1: Need justification that the second term of (11) is O(1/√m) and clear statement of where/how β enters the derivations.

- Q2: Must state the precise asymptotic regime and assumptions (m and N growth rates, rank scaling r vs m, independence/moment conditions) for the state evolution and approximations.

- Q3: Require rationale for approximating E[A_il^2] by m τ_w^2 (or m τ w^2) and conditions under which empirical second moments can be replaced by expectations.

- Q4: Need rigorous justification that AMP iterates converge to MAP as β→∞ (conditions for interchanging limit and minimization; e.g., Γ-convergence, stability analysis).

- Q5: Proposition 1 proof is too brief—provide full derivation of equation (17), the surrogate objective, role of Onsager terms, PSD assumptions, and connection to block coordinate minimization.

- Clarity issues: include missing algebraic steps, explicit assumptions (scalings, independence, moments), map belief-propagation/Bethe objective to AMP updates, clarify notation (τ vs τ_w, m vs N, indexing), and fix equation numbering.

- Originality concern: method resembles Expectation Propagation (EP); authors should position work relative to EP and prior EP-based matrix factorization (e.g., Matchbox), and explain differences (factor graph, Onsager corrections, state evolution, convergence).

- Conceptual link: clarify similarities/differences between the clustering formulation and EP “clutter”/mixture models (Minka’s work).

- Significance/experiments: comparisons limited to K-means/K-means++; add baselines (GMM+EM, spectral clustering, NMF variants, variational/Bayesian MF), and report runtime, memory, initialization sensitivity, and robustness to misspecification.

- Pros: unified AMP framework linking MAP/mean via β; novel clustering application with promising empirical gains; O(mN) per-iteration cost and symmetric U/V updates.

- Cons: sketchy derivations and unstated assumptions; unclear novelty vs EP; insufficient experimental baselines and unclear practical advantage.

- Suggestions: expand appendix with full derivations and scalings, provide a careful β→∞ treatment, give detailed proof of Proposition 1 (derive (17)), clarify relation to EP and claimed AMP benefits, and broaden empirical evaluation (additional baselines, ablations, runtime).
