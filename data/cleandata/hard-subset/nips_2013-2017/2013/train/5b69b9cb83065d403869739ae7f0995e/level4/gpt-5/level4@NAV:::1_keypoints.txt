- Introduces an algorithm for matrix reconstruction from noisy measurements targeting low-rank matrices with additional factor assumptions.  
- Uses an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) strategy to speed up the computationally heavy Bayesian approach.  
- Connects matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering, proposing a compelling application domain.  
- Applying AMP to matrix reconstruction in this way appears novel and interesting.  
- Acknowledges established links between low-rank factorizations and K-means but argues the perspective enables clear empirical comparison.  
- Empirical results show the proposed method is faster and more efficient than standard k-means and k-means++.  
- Evaluation measures include Frobenius norm residuals and resulting cluster assignments, which strengthens the experimental design.  
- Primary limitation is scarce theoretical guarantees, notably regarding convergence.  
- Suggests adding references to TCS literature that provide provable algorithms for the K-means objective.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper with promising empirical performance but limited theoretical support.
