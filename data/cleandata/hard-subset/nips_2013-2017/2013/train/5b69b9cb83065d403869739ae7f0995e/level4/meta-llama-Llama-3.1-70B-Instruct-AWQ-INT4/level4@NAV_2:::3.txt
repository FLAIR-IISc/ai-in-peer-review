This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering problems. The authors formulate the matrix reconstruction problem in a Bayesian framework, allowing for the incorporation of structural properties such as sparsity. They derive an efficient AMP algorithm for Bayesian inference and demonstrate its effectiveness in clustering tasks.

The paper is well-structured, and the main idea is easy to grasp. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem setting, the proposed algorithm, and its application to clustering. However, some of the mathematical derivations and analysis are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps.

One of the main concerns is the mathematical rigor of the asymptotic analysis in Section 2. The authors assume that the dimensions m and N grow in the same order, but the implications of this assumption are not thoroughly discussed. Additionally, some of the approximations, such as the expectation of A{il}^2 being m \tauw^2, require further justification.

The connection between the proposed algorithm and Expectation-Propagation (EP) is also worth exploring. The authors' approach seems to be an application of EP to matrix factorization, which has been done before in the literature. The authors should clarify the novelty of their approach and its relation to existing work.

The experimental evaluation is limited to a comparison with the k-means++ algorithm, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the practical usefulness of the proposed algorithm. A more comprehensive evaluation, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art clustering algorithms, would be beneficial.

In terms of quality, the paper has some minor issues, such as the omission of the parameter β in Section 2. The proof of Proposition 1 is also too brief and requires further elaboration.

Overall, the paper proposes an interesting approach to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. However, it requires more rigorous mathematical analysis, a clearer connection to existing work, and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation to demonstrate its significance.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* The experimental results show promise, with the proposed algorithm outperforming k-means++ in some cases.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The mathematical derivations and analysis are sketchy and require further elaboration.
* The connection to existing work, such as Expectation-Propagation, is not clearly established.
* The experimental evaluation is limited and requires more comprehensive comparisons with other algorithms.
* The paper has some minor issues, such as the omission of the parameter β in Section 2.
