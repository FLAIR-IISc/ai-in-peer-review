- Novel AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural constraints on factor matrices.  
- Bayesian formulation allows flexible incorporation of structural priors (e.g., sparsity) and handles both marginalization and MAP estimation.  
- Efficient AMP-based inference method derived for the reconstruction problem.  
- Extension of AMP to general-rank matrix reconstruction, a claimed novel contribution.  
- Recasts K-means clustering as a constrained low-rank matrix reconstruction task, differing from prior clustering approaches.  
- Empirical comparisons to Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes on synthetic and real datasets using normalized K-means loss, clustering accuracy, and convergence speed.  
- Results show AMP yields lower K-means loss and faster convergence than variational Bayes, with comparable accuracy.  
- Comprehensive evaluation (including Frobenius norm residuals and cluster assignments) is a notable strength.  
- Primary weakness: limited theoretical convergence guarantees beyond a fixed-point analysis for the MAP problem (Proposition 1).  
- Recommendation: strengthen theoretical foundation by citing recent theoretical-CS work on provably accurate K-means algorithms, especially for high-dimensional settings.
