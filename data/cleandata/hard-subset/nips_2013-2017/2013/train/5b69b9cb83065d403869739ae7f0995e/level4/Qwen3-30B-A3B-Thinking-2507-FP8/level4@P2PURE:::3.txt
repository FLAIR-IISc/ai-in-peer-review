

This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization. It demonstrates that the matrix factorization problem generalizes the clustering problem, then specializes the derived message passing algorithm to clustering. Experiments compare the proposed algorithm against k-means++.  
Quality: The mathematical derivations are excessively sketchy, omitting non-trivial steps even in the appendix.  
1) In line 139 of Appendix, why is the second term of (11) O(1/√m)? β appears to be omitted in Section 2.  
2) Some analysis, including the derivation of (19), assumes m and n grow at the same rate. The asymptotic analysis in this section is terse, raising concerns about mathematical rigor.  
3) In line 167, why is mτ_w² the expectation of A_{il}²? A represents observed data, while mτ_w² is only the variance of the likelihood; the rationale for this approximation requires verification.  
4) How does the message passing algorithm for finite β converge to the MAP problem? Message passing minimizes KL divergence, but lim_{β→∞} min KL ≠ min lim_{β→∞} KL in general.  
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed. How is (17) derived?  
Clarity: The paper is well-structured and clearly conveys its main contribution.  
Originality: Since the authors focus on estimating first and second moments of the marginal posterior, the entire algorithm appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. EP was previously applied to matrix factorization in Stern et al. (2009, http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Section 1 of the appendix describes standard EP update procedures; please clarify if this is incorrect.  
Additionally, an interesting connection may exist between the authors' clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (Minka, 2001, http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the clutter likelihood is replaced by a uniform mixture of Gaussians. This observation is intended as a suggestion, not a criticism of originality.  
Significance: The authors primarily focus on clustering applications, but comparing only to k-means++ is insufficient to demonstrate practical utility given the extensive clustering literature. Furthermore, the sketchy proofs (even in the appendix) make technical correctness difficult to assess. I suspect the work is an application of EP to matrix factorization, as established by Stern et al. (2009).
