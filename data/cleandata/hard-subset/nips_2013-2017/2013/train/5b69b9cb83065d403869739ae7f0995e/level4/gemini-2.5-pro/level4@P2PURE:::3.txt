This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization. The authors frame matrix factorization as a generalization of the clustering problem and then specialize their algorithm for this task. Experiments are conducted comparing the proposed algorithm with k-means++.

**Quality:** The mathematical derivations throughout the paper are sketchy and omit many non-trivial steps, even in the appendix, making it difficult to verify the paper's correctness. Specific concerns include:

1) In line 139 of the appendix, the justification for why the second term of Equation (11) is O(1/√m) is unclear. Additionally, the parameter β appears to be omitted from the discussion in Section 2.
2) Some analyses, such as the derivation of Equation (19), seem to assume that m and n grow at the same rate. Overall, the asymptotic analysis in this section is terse, raising concerns about its mathematical rigor.
3) In line 167, the paper approximates the expectation of A_{il}^2 with mτ_w^2. However, A is observed data, while mτ_w^2 is the variance of the likelihood. The rationale for this approximation requires justification.
4) The paper does not explain how the message passing algorithm, derived for a finite β, converges to the MAP solution as β → ∞. Message passing minimizes KL divergence, but the interchange of the limit and minimization operators (i.e., lim_β min KL = min lim_β KL) does not hold in general and needs to be justified in this context.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed. Specifically, the derivation of Equation (17) is not provided.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured, and its main points are presented clearly.

**Originality:** The originality of the proposed algorithm is questionable. Given that the authors focus on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior, the algorithm appears to be a direct application of Expectation Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. This has been previously explored by Stern et al. (2009) in the following paper: http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf. Furthermore, the update procedures described in Section 1 of the appendix appear to be standard EP updates.

On a related note, there may be an interesting connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the "clutter problem" in the original EP paper by Minka (2001) (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), if the likelihood in the clutter problem were replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussians. This is offered as a potential connection for the authors to consider, not as a criticism of the work's originality.

**Significance:** The paper's primary application is clustering. However, given the extensive literature in this field, the experimental comparison against only k-means++ is insufficient to demonstrate the practical significance of the proposed algorithm. The paper's contributions are diminished by two major concerns. First, the technical correctness is difficult to assess because the proofs are overly brief and lack rigor. Second, the work's novelty is in doubt, as the proposed method appears to be a re-derivation of Expectation Propagation for matrix factorization, a problem previously addressed by Stern et al. (2009).
