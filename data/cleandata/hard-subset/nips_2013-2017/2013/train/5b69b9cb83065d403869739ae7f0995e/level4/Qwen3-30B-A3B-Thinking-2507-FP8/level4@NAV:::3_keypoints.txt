- Paper: Introduces an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, argues it generalizes clustering, and compares it to k-means++.  
- Quality: Mathematical derivations are too sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, even in the appendix.  
- (1) Appendix line 139: unclear why the second term of (11) is O(1/√m); β is omitted in Section 2 and needs justification.  
- (2) Asymptotics: analysis (including derivation of (19)) assumes m and n grow proportionally but the asymptotic treatment is terse and raises rigor concerns.  
- (3) Line 167: question why E[A_il^2] is taken as m τ_w^2 when A is observed data and m τ_w^2 is only the likelihood variance—approximation rationale required.  
- (4) MAP limit: unclear how the finite-β message passing converges to the MAP problem since lim_{β→∞} min KL may not equal min lim_{β→∞} KL.  
- (5) Proposition 1: proof is too brief; derivation of equation (17) is not shown.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured and clearly communicates the main contribution.  
- Originality: The algorithm may be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization (cf. Stern et al.), and connections to EP’s clutter problem are plausible.  
- Significance: Need broader empirical comparisons beyond k-means++; sketchy proofs hinder technical evaluation and the method appears closely related to prior EP work.
