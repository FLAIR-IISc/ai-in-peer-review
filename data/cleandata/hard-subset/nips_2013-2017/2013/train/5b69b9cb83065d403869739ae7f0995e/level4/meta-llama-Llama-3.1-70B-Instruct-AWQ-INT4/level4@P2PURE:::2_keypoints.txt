- Proposes a novel BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with large-N, large-m approximations.  
- Evaluated on a non-standard task (clustering formulated as matrix factorization), making generalization to typical reconstruction problems unclear.  
- Did not use common benchmarks (e.g., Netflix); authors should justify dataset choice.  
- Introduces a new evaluation criterion that raises questions about competitiveness and comparability to prior work.  
- Algorithm is a relatively straightforward application of BP to a problem previously tackled by Variational Message Passing—novel but not groundbreaking.  
- The large-N, large-m approximations are the most interesting part but are only in the supplement and are not empirically validated.  
- Serious citation and scholarship issues: presents matrix-factorization-for-clustering as if novel, which is misleading.  
- Fails to cite or discuss Expectation Propagation (EP) and how EP relates to the proposed algorithm.  
- Experiments show benefit and the math is generally followable, so the algorithm may be useful.  
- Key derivation is relegated to Supplementary Material, hindering readability and assessment.  
- Notation is cumbersome and sometimes unnecessary (excess hats, tildes, subscripts/superscripts).  
- Several equations and functions are not sufficiently explained (e.g., relations among Eqs. 2, 5, 8, 10).  
- The role and significance of the m factor in noise variance and approximations is unclear and should be discussed.  
- Missing citation for VBMF-MA implementation and unclear steps in supplementary derivations (e.g., Eq. 14→15).  
- English is generally adequate but contains some awkward article usage; overall paper needs clearer exposition and better scholarship before acceptance.
