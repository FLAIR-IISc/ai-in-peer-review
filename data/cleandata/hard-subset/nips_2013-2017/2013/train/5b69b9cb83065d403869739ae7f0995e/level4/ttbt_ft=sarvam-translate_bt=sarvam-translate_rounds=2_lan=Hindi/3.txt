This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization. After showing that the matrix factorization problem can be viewed as a generalization of the clustering problem, the derived message passing algorithm for matrix factorization is specialized for clustering. Finally, the proposed algorithm is compared to the k-means++ algorithm.

Quality: The mathematical derivations in this paper are very vague, and many non-trivial steps have been omitted even in the appendix.
1) In line 139 of the appendix, why is the second term of (11) O(1/\sqrt(m))? \beta seems to be omitted in section 2.
2) Some analysis, including the derivation of (19), seems to assume that m and n increase in the same order. Finally, the asymptotic analysis in this section is a bit concise, and I am concerned about its mathematical rigor.
3) Why is m \tauw^2 expected to be greater than A{il}^2 in line 167? A is the observed data, and m\tau_w^2 is only the variance of the probability; the argument for such an approximation should be verified.
4) How will the derived message passing algorithm converge in a MAP problem for a finite \beta? The message passing algorithm reduces the KL deviation, but \lim\beta \min KL = \min \lim\beta KL is not necessarily the case.
5) The proof of proposition 1 is too concise for me. How is (17) derived?

Clarity: The paper is well-structured, and it is not difficult to understand what the main point of the paper is.

Novelty: Since the authors are mostly concerned about estimating the first and second moments of finite पश्च, is the entire algorithm not an application of expectation propagation (EP) for matrix factorization? EP was already used in matrix factorization in the following paper: http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf Section 1 of the appendix seems to be the standard EP update procedure; please correct me if I am wrong.

Furthermore, it seems that there should be an interesting connection between the clustering algorithm proposed by the authors and the disorder problem of the original EP paper: http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf , if the probability of the disorder problem is transformed into a mixture similar to a Gaussian distribution. Just to clarify, in this paragraph I am only suggesting a connection and not attacking the originality of the paper.

Importance: The authors mainly focus on its application for clustering, but considering the vast amount of literature on clustering, comparing it only to k-means++ may not be enough to prove the practical utility of the algorithm. The proof in this paper, even in the appendix, is very vague, and thus it is difficult to evaluate its technical correctness. Moreover, I doubt that this is an application of expectation propagation for the matrix factorization problem, which had already been done by Stern et al. http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf
