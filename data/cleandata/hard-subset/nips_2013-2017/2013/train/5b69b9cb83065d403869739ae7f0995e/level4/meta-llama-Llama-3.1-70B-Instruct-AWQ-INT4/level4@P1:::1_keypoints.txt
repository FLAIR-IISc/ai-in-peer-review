- Presents an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction under noisy observations.  
- Assumes additional structure on matrix factors and uses AMP to speed up computationally expensive Bayesian methods.  
- Connects low-rank matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering as an application domain.  
- Notes that AMP for matrix reconstruction appears novel and interesting.  
- Reminds that connections between low-rank factorizations and K-means are already known (e.g., PCA gives a factor-two approximation).  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and compares to relevant baselines including k-means and k-means++.  
- Reports the proposed approach is faster and more efficient than classical k-means and k-means++ in experiments.  
- Evaluation compares both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering outputs.  
- Main weakness: limited theoretical guarantees on algorithm convergence.  
- Suggests adding citations to theoretical CS work that gives provable guarantees for K-means.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clearly presented, and original, but reduced significance due to weak theoretical results.
