- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering, showing clustering as a special-case with structural constraints.  
- Derives AMP procedures for both marginalization and MAP estimation and reports better performance than K-means++ in experiments.  
- Technical contributions are interesting but mathematical foundations are underdeveloped with serious gaps in theoretical analysis.  
- Appendix claim about the second term of (11) being O(1/√m) omits the β parameter, creating scaling confusion.  
- Several asymptotic derivations (e.g., equation 19) assume m and N grow at the same rate without justification or discussion of finite-size implications.  
- The justification that mτw² equals E[Aᵢⱼ²] is stated without proper derivation and is insufficient.  
- The connection between AMP at finite β and the MAP problem is unclear; exchanging limβ→∞ and min KL is not generally valid and needs stronger justification.  
- Proposition 1’s proof is too brief and omits the derivation of equation (17), which is crucial for understanding optimality.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the link between low-rank reconstruction and clustering; contributions and experiments are easy to follow.  
- The approach appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation to matrix factorization rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Prior work (Stern et al., 2009) on EP for matrix factorization is not sufficiently acknowledged; potential link to Minka (2001) clutter problem is unexamined.  
- Significance is limited by narrow experimental comparison (primarily vs. K-means++); lacks benchmarks against spectral, hierarchical, or modern deep clustering methods.  
- Pros: interesting conceptual connection, potential computational benefits of AMP, and promising empirical results on some datasets.  
- Cons: weak theoretical foundation, questionable originality due to EP connection, and insufficient experimental breadth for a top-tier conference.
