This paper introduces an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, and then specializes it to the clustering problem by framing matrix factorization as a generalization of clustering. The algorithm’s performance is evaluated through comparison with k-means++.

**Quality:** The mathematical derivations presented lack sufficient detail, with several non-trivial steps omitted even in the appendix. 

1) In the appendix, line 139, the justification for the O(1/√m) scaling of the second term in equation (11) is unclear. The role of β, as defined in Section 2, is not apparent in this derivation.
2) The analysis, including the derivation of equation (19), appears to rely on the assumption that *m* and *n* grow at comparable rates. The asymptotic analysis in this section is generally terse, raising concerns regarding its mathematical rigor.
3) Line 167 presents an equality where the expectation of A<sub>il</sub><sup>2</sup> is equated to *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>*. Given that A is observed data and *mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup>* represents the variance of the likelihood, the basis for this approximation requires further justification.
4) The convergence behavior of the message passing algorithm as β approaches infinity, and its relation to the maximum a posteriori (MAP) problem, is not fully explained. While message passing minimizes KL divergence, the interchange of limit and minimization operations (lim<sub>β</sub> min KL = min lim<sub>β</sub> KL) is not generally valid and requires justification.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed. The derivation of equation (17) is not clearly presented.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and the core contributions are readily understandable.

**Originality:** The proposed algorithm appears to primarily focus on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior distribution. This leads to the question of whether the work constitutes a novel application of Expectation Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, a technique already explored in previous work (e.g., Stern et al., http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). The procedures outlined in Section 1 of the appendix resemble standard EP update rules; clarification on this point would be appreciated. Furthermore, a potentially interesting connection exists between the authors’ clustering algorithm and the clutter problem originally addressed in the EP literature (Minka, http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood function of the clutter problem is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. (Note: this is offered as a potential connection for consideration, not a critique of originality).

**Significance:** While the authors emphasize the application to clustering, the extensive existing literature in this field suggests that comparison to k-means++ alone may be insufficient to demonstrate the practical advantages of the proposed algorithm. The lack of detailed proofs, even in the appendix, hinders a thorough evaluation of the technical correctness of the work. Moreover, the possibility that this work represents an application of EP to matrix factorization, a technique previously demonstrated by Stern et al., raises concerns about the overall impact of the contribution.
