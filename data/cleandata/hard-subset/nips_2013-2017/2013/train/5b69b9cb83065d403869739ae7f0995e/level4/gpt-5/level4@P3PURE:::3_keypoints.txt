- Proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, specialized to clustering, and empirically compared to k-means++.

- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit many nontrivial steps, including in the Appendix.

- Specific concern 1: In Appendix line 139, the claim that the second term of (11) is O(1/√m) is unexplained, and beta appears omitted in Section 2.

- Specific concern 2: Parts of the analysis (e.g., derivation of (19)) seem to assume m and n grow at the same rate; the asymptotic analysis is terse and potentially not rigorous.

- Specific concern 3: In line 167, replacing A_il^2 by m τ_w^2 is unjustified because A is observed data and m τ_w^2 is only the likelihood variance.

- Specific concern 4: Unclear how the finite-β message passing algorithm converges to the MAP problem; interchanging limit and minimization of KL is not justified.

- Specific concern 5: Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and the derivation of (17) is not shown.

- Clarity: paper is well structured and the main ideas are easy to follow.

- Originality: The method appears closely related to Expectation Propagation (EP); prior work (Stern et al.) applied EP to matrix factorization and Section 1 of the Appendix looks like standard EP updates.

- Potential connection: The clustering algorithm may relate to the EP “clutter” problem (with a uniform Gaussian-mixture likelihood), which should be discussed.

- Significance: Empirical evaluation is limited (only clustering, only compared to k-means++), so practical utility is not convincingly demonstrated.

- Overall concern: Sketchy proofs and possible redundancy with existing EP-based matrix factorization work undermine the paper’s technical correctness and novelty.
