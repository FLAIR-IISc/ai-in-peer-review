- Proposes a BP-based approximate message passing algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and evaluates it via clustering framed as matrix factorization.  
- Experimental evaluation is problematic: using clustering as proxy makes results hard to generalize to standard matrix reconstruction tasks.  
- Important prior benchmarks (e.g., Netflix used by VBMF papers) were not used; replicating them would strengthen claims.  
- Introduces a novel evaluation metric that is interesting but creates ambiguity about true performance relative to established methods.  
- The algorithm appears to be a straightforward BP adaptation of methods previously addressed with Variational Message Passing, limiting perceived novelty.  
- The asymptotic approximations (N, m → ∞) are only in the supplement and lack clear explanation or validation.  
- Paper misrepresents novelty by implying matrix factorization for clustering is new; key prior work/citations are missing and must be acknowledged.  
- Fails to cite Expectation Propagation and to explain the relationship between EP and the proposed BP method.  
- Core BP application is buried (first mentioned in Sec. 4.1) and should be highlighted in the abstract.  
- Mathematical notation is cluttered and confusing (unnecessary symbols, excessive hats/tildes, inconsistent capitalization), hindering comprehension.  
- Several equations and derivations lack clear justification or simplification (e.g., roles of factors in Eqs. 9a–9e, why Eq. 5 is negative log of Eq. 2, why Eq. 8 is a power of Eq. 2).  
- Algorithm description is redundant in places (row-column symmetry could be noted to reduce repetition).  
- Missing explanations for modeling choices (role of m in noise variance, use of ~z for column vectors, capitalization inconsistency for N vs m).  
- Section 5 omits a citation for VBMF-MA’s main reference.  
- Minor language errors and a title that should explicitly name the proposed method.  
- Strengths: algorithm appears potentially useful and mathematically coherent, clustering-as-reconstruction is a reasonable benchmark, experiments show clear advantages, introduction and early sections are clear, and Suppl. Fig. 1 is helpful.  
- Conclusion: current submission is insufficient for publication due to major gaps in clarity, scholarship, and justification; fixing notation, validating approximations, and properly contextualizing contributions are required.
