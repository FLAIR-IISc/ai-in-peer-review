- Evaluation is weak: framing clustering as a non-standard matrix reconstruction task makes it hard to judge performance on conventional matrix reconstruction problems.
- Missing baseline/dataset: not evaluating on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) or explaining why prevents fair comparison to prior VBMF work.
- Incremental contribution: algorithm is an extension of existing BP/variational message-passing approaches rather than a major breakthrough.
- Large-dimension approximations are a key idea but remain untested and need clearer justification and validation.
- Presentation of related work is misleading: the paper implies novelty for applying matrix factorization to clustering without proper citations.
- Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to the proposed method are not discussed but should be.
- Belief Propagation foundation is not emphasized early enough and should appear in the abstract.
- Despite issues, the algorithm is interesting and potentially useful, with largely accessible mathematics.
- Experimental results on K-means show benefits for the proposed method, suggesting the benchmark has value.
- Placing the derivation in the Supplementary Material reduces accessibility; key derivation steps should be in the main paper.
- Mathematical presentation is hard to follow due to excessive and inconsistent notation (hats/tildes/subscripts/superscripts).
- Specific notation problems: inconsistent use of τ vs. τ_w, missing β subscripts, and unclear role of the m factor in noise variance.
- Functions introduced in Section 4.1 (e.g., f_β) are not immediately explained; algorithmic symmetries and relationships between equations should be made explicit.
- Minor clarity fixes needed: clarify tildes for column vectors, consistent capitalization (N vs. m), and add citation for “VBMF-MA.”
- Supplementary Material needs clearer steps (e.g., product of exponentials in Eq.5, big‑O step from Eq.14→15) and a better statement of novelty.
- Miscellaneous editorial issues: article usage and figure placement could be improved; consider adding more diagrams.
- Overall assessment: promising research but requires substantial revisions in evaluation, clarity, and related-work framing.
- Recommendation: Reject, with strong encouragement to resubmit after addressing the noted concerns.
