- Presents an approximate message passing algorithm for probabilistic matrix factorization, framing MF as a generalization of clustering and deriving a clustering-specialized message passing method evaluated against k-means++.

- Primary concern: lack of mathematical rigor—derivations are too brief and omit crucial non-trivial steps.

- Asymptotic analysis appears to rely on unstated assumptions (e.g., m and n grow at same rate) that must be explicitly stated and justified.

- Several approximations lack sufficient rationale (e.g., approximating E[A_il^2] by mτ_w^2) and need explanation.

- Claim that algorithm converges to MAP as β → ∞ is not justified because limits involving KL minimization are nontrivial and require proof.

- Multiple proofs and derivations are overly condensed or unclear (e.g., Proposition 1, Equation (17), appendix Equation (11) term order) and need step-by-step detail.

- Parameter β is missing from the problem formulation in Section 2 but used in the appendix; this inconsistency should be clarified.

- Paper is well-structured and clearly written despite mathematical detail issues.

- Originality is questionable—method seems like a direct application of Expectation Propagation and related prior work (e.g., Stern et al.); authors should clarify novel contributions.

- Suggests a possible connection to Minka’s EP "clutter problem" that the authors may wish to discuss.

- Significance is limited by sketchy proofs and weak empirical evaluation—comparing only to k-means++ is insufficient; broader experimental baselines are needed.

- Overall: interesting problem but undermined by lack of rigor, unclear novelty, and inadequate experiments.
