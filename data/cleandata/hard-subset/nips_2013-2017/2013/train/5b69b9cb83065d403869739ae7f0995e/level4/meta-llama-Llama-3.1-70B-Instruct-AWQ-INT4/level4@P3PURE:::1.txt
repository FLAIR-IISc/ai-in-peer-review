The authors present an algorithm for matrix reconstruction under noisy observations, focusing on low-rank matrices with additional assumptions on the factors. They utilize an Approximate Message Passing approach to speed up the classical, computationally expensive, Bayesian approach. Notably, the authors also establish a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, providing an interesting application domain for the proposed algorithm.

To the best of my knowledge, the application of Approximate Message Passing to matrix reconstruction is novel and interesting. Although the connections between low-rank matrix factorizations and K-means are fairly well-established (e.g., PCA provides a factor two approximation algorithm for K-means), this allows the authors to provide a comprehensive experimental evaluation of their algorithm, comparing it to k-means and k-means++. The empirical results suggest that their approach is faster and more efficient than classical k-means and k-means++. A notable aspect of their experimental evaluation is the comparison of both the Frobenius norm residual and the actual clusterings.

The main weakness of the paper lies in the limited theoretical guarantees provided for the proposed algorithm's convergence. While this is not unexpected, given that many K-means algorithms also have limited theoretical properties, the authors would benefit from citing relevant papers in Theoretical Computer Science that present provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function. This would strengthen the theoretical foundation of their work.

Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a promising Approximate Message Passing algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and k-means. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and promising, although the theoretical results could be improved with additional supporting literature.
