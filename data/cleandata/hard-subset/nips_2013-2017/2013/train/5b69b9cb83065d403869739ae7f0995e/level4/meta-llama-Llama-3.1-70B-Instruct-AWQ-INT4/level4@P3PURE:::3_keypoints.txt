- Paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, specialized to clustering, with experiments comparing to k-means++.

- Mathematical derivations are often sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, reducing rigor.

- Line 139/eq.(11): second term claimed O(1/√m) without clear justification, and omission of β is unexplained.

- Asymptotic analysis (e.g., derivation of eq.(19)) appears to assume m and n grow at the same rate, but the argument is terse and unclear.

- Line 167: approximating E[A_il^2] by m τ_w^2 is questionable since A is observed data and m τ_w^2 is only the likelihood variance.

- Convergence from finite-β message passing to the MAP problem is unclear; interchange of limit and minimization in KL is not justified.

- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and lacks the derivation of eq.(17).

- Paper is well-structured and the main idea is easy to follow.

- Method appears to be essentially Expectation-Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization, similar to Stern et al. (www09.pdf); appendix updates look like standard EP.

- Possible interesting connection to the EP "clutter" problem (Minka) is suggested but not explored.

- Experimental comparison only to k-means++ is insufficient given extensive clustering literature; practical usefulness not convincingly demonstrated.
