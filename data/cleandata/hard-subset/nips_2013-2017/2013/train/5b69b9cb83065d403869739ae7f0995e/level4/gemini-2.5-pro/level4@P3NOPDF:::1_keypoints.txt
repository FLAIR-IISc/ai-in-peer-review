- Proposes a novel AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction under specific structural assumptions, linking probabilistic modeling with scalable inference.  
- Draws a connection between low-rank reconstruction and K-means clustering, using K-means as a practical validation domain.  
- Empirical results: AMP is faster and often yields higher-quality solutions than K-means and K-means++, evaluated by Frobenius reconstruction error and cluster quality.  
- Main weakness: limited theoretical analysis—no formal convergence or solution-quality guarantees are provided.  
- Suggestion: better situate the work within theoretical CS literature on provable K-means algorithms and discuss trade-offs with heuristic methods.  
- Conclusion: solid, promising empirical contribution; strengthening theoretical grounding would make it more impactful.
