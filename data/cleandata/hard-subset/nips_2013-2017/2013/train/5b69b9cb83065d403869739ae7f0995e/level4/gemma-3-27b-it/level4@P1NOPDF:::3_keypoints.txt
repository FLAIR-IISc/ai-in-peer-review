- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit many non-trivial steps, even in the appendix.  
- Appendix line 139: the claim that the second term of eq.(11) is O(1/√m) is unclear; β may have been omitted affecting the derivation.  
- Several analyses (e.g., derivation of eq.(19)) seem to assume m and n grow at the same rate; the asymptotic analysis is terse and potentially non-rigorous.  
- Appendix line 167: equating E[A_il^2] to m τ_w^2 is unconvincing—A is observed data while m τ_w^2 is just the likelihood variance and needs justification.  
- It’s unclear how the message passing algorithm for finite β converges to the MAP solution; interchange of limit and minimum for KL is not justified.  
- Proposition 1’s proof is too brief; derivation of eq.(17) is not explained.  
- Clarity: paper is well-structured and the main idea is relatively easy to follow.  
- Originality concern: the algorithm may be essentially Expectation Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization, overlapping prior work (e.g., Stern et al.).  
- Appendix Sec.1 appears to describe standard EP updates; the authors should clarify distinctions from EP.  
- Noted potential connection to the EP “clutter problem” when using a uniform mixture of Gaussians as the likelihood—interesting but needs elaboration.  
- Significance: empirical comparison is limited (only to k-means++), which is insufficient given extensive clustering literature.  
- Overall: sketchy proofs and possible overlap with prior EP work undermine confidence in technical correctness and novelty.
