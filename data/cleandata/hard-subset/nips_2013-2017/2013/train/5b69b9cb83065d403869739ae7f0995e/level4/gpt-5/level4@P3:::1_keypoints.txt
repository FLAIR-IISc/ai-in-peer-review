- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles posterior inference and MAP estimation via a temperature parameter β.  
- Extends prior bilinear AMP formulations (including IterFac) to general rank and enables MAP/marginal-MAP estimators for broad priors, including discrete constraints.  
- Reformulates K-means as a low-rank reconstruction with a discrete assignment prior, yielding an AMP-based K-means (and β=1 “maximum-accuracy” variant) with O(mN) per-iteration cost and Onsager-like corrections.  
- Proves a fixed-point proposition showing block-wise optimality (global minima under PSD conditions) at convergence.  
- Situates work well within AMP literature and acknowledges contemporaneous bilinear AMP and dictionary learning papers.  
- Technical derivation follows standard AMP/BP-to-AMP reduction; discrete-prior specialization gives interpretable debiasing and size-regularization terms.  
- Fixed-point result is valuable but weaker than full global convergence guarantees.  
- Synthetic and ORL face experiments show consistent improvements by AMP in K-means loss and clustering accuracy, particularly at higher ranks.  
- Experiments omit wall-clock runtimes, observe oscillations without systematic mitigation, and only briefly study sensitivity to initialization, β, and τ-estimation.  
- No state evolution analysis or convergence proof for the proposed general-rank scheme in the main text (deferred to supplement).  
- PSD conditions in the proposition are plausible but not validated for finite-sample behavior.  
- Robustness to model mismatch (non-Gaussian noise, misspecified rank) and sensitivity to prior choices are not systematically explored.  
- Comparisons miss natural clustering baselines such as Gaussian mixture EM, spectral clustering, and PCA+K-means.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized; algorithmic presentation is precise and the β-trick cleanly unifies inference and optimization.  
- Recommends adding guidance for computing f and G for common priors, elaborating τ estimation and damping strategies, and reporting runtime alongside iterations.  
- Novelty: general-rank AMP that supports MAP/marginal-MAP with structured/discrete priors is original; AMP-KM enforcing one-hot assignments appears new relative to MF clustering.  
- Significance: approach could be a practical tool for large-scale structured matrix factorization and a promising alternative to Lloyd’s K-means in high dimensions, but limited theory and empirical breadth reduce immediate impact.  
- Pros: unified AMP framework across inference objectives, novel AMP-KM with interpretable corrections, O(mN) complexity, and useful fixed-point optimality.  
- Cons: lacks state evolution/convergence guarantees, limited baselines and sensitivity analyses, no runtime reporting, and missing links to provable K-means theory.  
- Suggested improvements: include state evolution or empirical calibration, add EM/spectral/PCA+K-means baselines, report wall-clock time and ablations (τ, initialization, r̂), and expand related theoretical clustering references.  
- Overall: solid, timely, and original contribution with promising empirical results; recommend acceptance conditional on stronger convergence discussion, broader baselines, and clearer practical guidance.
