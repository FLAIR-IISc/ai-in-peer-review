- Novel algorithm applying Approximate Message Passing (AMP) for noisy low-rank matrix reconstruction to accelerate Bayesian inference.  
- Method leverages structural assumptions on matrix factors and connects to K-means clustering as an application domain.  
- Application of AMP to this problem is novel and well-motivated.  
- Authors effectively use the PCA–K-means connection to design experiments.  
- Empirical comparison against k-means and k-means++ shows significant speed and efficiency gains.  
- Evaluation reports both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering performance for a comprehensive assessment.  
- Primary limitation: lack of strong theoretical convergence guarantees for the AMP-based algorithm.  
- Reviewer recommends citing theoretical computer-science work on provably accurate K-means algorithms to address the gap.
