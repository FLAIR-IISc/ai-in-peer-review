- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix factorization and applies it to clustering, showing competitive performance versus k-means++.

- Quality: mathematical derivations are terse and omit non-trivial steps, raising concerns about approximation justifications and asymptotic rigor.

- Appendix claim that the second term is O(1/√m) lacks sufficient explanation and β is applied inconsistently in Section 2.

- The asymptotic analysis (especially derivation of (19)) seems to assume m and N grow at comparable rates and is too brief to be convincing.

- Approximating E[A_il^2] by m τ_w^2 is unclear and appears to use likelihood variance rather than empirical expectation from A.

- Convergence of AMP as β → ∞ to the MAP solution is not fully justified; interchange of limits and KL minimization is questionable.

- Proof of Proposition 1 and derivation of equation (17) are overly concise and need further elaboration.

- Clarity: paper is well-structured, generally accessible, and logically presented.

- Originality: approach estimates first and second posterior moments and closely resembles Expectation Propagation (EP); novelty relative to EP (e.g., Stern et al.) is not clearly delineated.

- Potential connection to EP treatments of the clutter/uniform Gaussian mixture problems is not explored, weakening originality claims.

- Significance: promising clustering results but evaluation is limited to k-means++, so practical impact is not fully established.

- Limited proof detail and possible overlap with prior EP-based matrix factorization work reduce perceived technical and conceptual contribution.

- Arguments for acceptance: structured AMP approach to low-rank reconstruction, competitive clustering performance, and a novel perspective on clustering.

- Arguments against acceptance: insufficiently detailed derivations, questionable originality versus EP, narrow experimental comparison, and incomplete convergence guarantees.
