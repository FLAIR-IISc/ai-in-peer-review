- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors and a temperature parameter β that interpolates posterior marginals (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).  
- Extends AMP to handle MAP estimation and discrete/structured priors, and formulates K-means as constrained low-rank factorization with one-hot rows, yielding an AMP-based clustering and marginal-MAP clustering method.  
- Experiments (synthetic and ORL Faces) show improved K-means loss and often higher accuracy than Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++; gains over a VBMF baseline reported.  
- Needs clearer comparison and positioning relative to prior AMP/GAMP and IterFac/bilinear methods to clarify what is genuinely new.  
- Should acknowledge earlier clustering-as-factorization literature (e.g., Xu et al., Ding et al.) more prominently.  
- The connection to belief propagation/Bethe free energy and the relation to expectation propagation (EP) are relevant and should be discussed.  
- Strength: principled β-parameterized AMP formulation unifying marginalization and MAP with explicit update equations and a fixed-point optimality proposition.  
- Strength: elegant reinterpretation of K-means within AMP that corrects assignments for center bias and cluster-size effects.  
- Strength: fast empirical convergence in synthetic settings, low per-iteration cost O(mN), and symmetric updates supporting many priors.  
- Weakness (quality): key derivations and approximations are relegated to the supplement without empirical validation of large-system assumptions or ablations.  
- Weakness: experiments focus narrowly on clustering; no evaluation on standard matrix factorization/completion benchmarks (e.g., Netflix/MovieLens).  
- Weakness: lack of analysis of convergence, state evolution, or the practical role and selection of β, τ estimation, and rank mis-specification.  
- Clarity issues: heavy and inconsistent notation (excessive hats/tilde/superscripts), repeated terms that could be factored, and explanations that should be moved earlier or simplified.  
- Methodological issue: the significance and practical consequences of the m factor in the noise variance need clearer motivation.  
- Originality: β-parameterized bridge to MAP and handling one-hot constraints appear novel, but the paper must better distinguish these contributions from bilinear GAMP and IterFac (e.g., specific Onsager terms).  
- Significance: promising clustering application with potential broader utility, but unclear impact on low-rank reconstruction without wider empirical validation.  
- Key questions for authors: role of the m factor; sensitivity to τ estimation and rank misspecification; clear novelty vs Parker et al. (2013); exact VBMF variant and hyperparameters used; empirical checks of approximations/Onsager corrections.  
- Suggested improvements: add standard low-rank experiments or justify omission; include a main-text derivation outline and state-evolution summary; simplify and unify notation; cite clustering-as-factorization literature and discuss EP/Bethe links.  
- Suggested improvements (cont.): clarify relation to prior AMP/bilinear work, discuss convergence/β guidance, and improve language and figure captions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptually clean AMP unifying marginalization and MAP, practical and effective clustering algorithm with low cost and flexible priors.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation on canonical tasks, heavy reliance on supplementary derivations without testing approximations, and presentation/incomplete-scholarly issues.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially useful contribution (especially for clustering) but requires stronger positioning, clearer exposition, and broader experiments to meet the conference bar.
