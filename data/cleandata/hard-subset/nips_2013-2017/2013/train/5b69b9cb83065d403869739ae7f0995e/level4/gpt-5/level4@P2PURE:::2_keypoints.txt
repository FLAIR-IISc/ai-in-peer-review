- Proposes a new BP-derived algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with two large-dimension approximations (N → ∞, m → ∞).  
- Evaluation is unconventional: clustering is cast as matrix reconstruction and compared to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.  
- Using clustering as the testbed makes it hard to judge competitiveness on standard matrix reconstruction tasks.  
- The paper omits experiments on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix); authors should justify or include such evaluations.  
- The algorithm is a fairly direct application of BP/variational message passing and is not especially groundbreaking.  
- The asymptotic approximations are the most interesting contribution but are described only in the Supplementary Material and are neither clearly explained nor validated.  
- Serious citation omissions: the connection between matrix factorization and clustering is presented as novel but is actually well known and needs explicit citations.  
- Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to the proposed method are not cited or discussed.  
- The paper fails to state early (e.g., in the abstract) that the algorithm is an instance of Belief Propagation.  
- Pros: the paper is engaging, math development is generally followable, and experiments show clear benefits for the proposed method.  
- The core derivation being relegated to the Supplementary Material is a drawback; a concise derivation should appear in the main text.  
- The algorithm description and mathematics are hard to follow and contain confusing factors and notation choices.  
- Overuse of hats, tildes, subscripts/superscripts, and unnecessary temporal indices makes the presentation cumbersome.  
- Exposition could be tightened: clarify relations between equations, exploit symmetry to shorten algorithms, and define functions (e.g., f_β, G_β) where they appear.  
- Inconsistent β-subscript usage is confusing; β should be treated as a global parameter or consistently propagated.  
- Modeling choices need clarification, especially the role of the m factor in noise variance and any induced row–column asymmetry.  
- Minor notation and style questions: use of tilde for column vectors, uppercase N vs lowercase m, and missing citation for VBMF-MA implementation.  
- Supplementary Material needs clearer probability notation, justification for big-O steps, and clarification of how the message approximations relate to prior work.  
- Language and stylistic issues throughout; title could be improved (suggested longer descriptive title).  
- Specific phrasing and grammar edits are recommended across multiple pages.  
- Figure 1 in the Supplementary Material is helpful; adding more diagrams would improve clarity.  
- Overall: the mathematics and algorithm are interesting and publishable, but substantial revisions are required for clarity, completeness, and proper scholarship before acceptance.
