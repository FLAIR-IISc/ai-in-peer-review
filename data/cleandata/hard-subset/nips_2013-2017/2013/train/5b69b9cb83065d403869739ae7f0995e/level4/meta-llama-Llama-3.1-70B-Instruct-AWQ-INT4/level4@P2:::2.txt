Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering"

This paper proposes a new algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction based on approximate message passing (AMP) and applies it to clustering problems. The algorithm is derived from a Bayesian model of the reconstruction problem and is evaluated against Lloyd's K-means algorithm and Variational Bayes Matrix Factorization (VBMF).

The paper contributes a new algorithm that can potentially be useful for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The mathematics of the paper is possible to follow, and the experiments section is clear. However, there are some concerns regarding the clarity and scholarship of the paper.

One of the main concerns is that the algorithm is evaluated on a non-standard problem (clustering encoded as matrix factorization), making it difficult to interpolate the experimental results to predict how the algorithm would perform on more conventional matrix reconstruction problems. The authors should consider using a standard dataset, such as the Netflix dataset, to evaluate their algorithm against predecessors.

Another concern is that the algorithm itself appears to be a straightforward application of AMP to a problem that has been previously addressed with Variational Message Passing. While the approximations introduced in the limit $N\to\infty$ and $m\to\infty$ are interesting, their validity is not directly tested, and they could be explained more clearly.

There are also some serious problems regarding the citation of prior work. The connection between matrix factorization and clustering is well-known, but the paper does not make this clear to the reader. The authors should cite prior work and explain the novelty of their algorithm's derivation.

The exposition of the paper could be improved. The notation is cumbersome, and the authors could simplify it by dropping unnecessary hats, tildes, subscripts, and superscripts. The meaning of functions should be explained near their definition, and the parameter $\beta$ could be treated as a global variable.

The paper also has some language issues, which can be distracting for some readers. The authors should consider revising the paper to improve clarity and scholarship.

Overall, the paper needs more work before it can be accepted. While the algorithm is interesting and potentially useful, the paper's lack of clarity and scholarship puts an unacceptable burden on readers.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a new algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* The algorithm is derived from a Bayesian model of the reconstruction problem.
* The experiments section is clear, and the results show the potential of the algorithm.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The algorithm is evaluated on a non-standard problem, making it difficult to interpolate the experimental results.
* The algorithm appears to be a straightforward application of AMP to a problem that has been previously addressed with Variational Message Passing.
* The paper has serious problems regarding the citation of prior work.
* The exposition of the paper could be improved, and the notation is cumbersome.
* The paper has some language issues.

Recommendation:

* The authors should revise the paper to address the concerns mentioned above.
* The authors should consider using a standard dataset to evaluate their algorithm against predecessors.
* The authors should cite prior work and explain the novelty of their algorithm's derivation.
* The authors should simplify the notation and improve the exposition of the paper.
* The authors should revise the paper to improve clarity and scholarship.
