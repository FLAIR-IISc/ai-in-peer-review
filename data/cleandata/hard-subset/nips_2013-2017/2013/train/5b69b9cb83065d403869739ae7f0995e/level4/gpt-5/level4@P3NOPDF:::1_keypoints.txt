- Proposes an AMP algorithm for structured low-rank matrix reconstruction that aims to combine Bayesian modeling flexibility with substantial speedups and is evaluated also as a clustering method via the low-rank/k-means connection.  
- Applying AMP to this specific matrix reconstruction setting is novel and interesting; using the low-rank ↔ k-means link for benchmarking is reasonable.  
- Experiments are a strength: report both Frobenius residuals and clustering outcomes, showing compelling speed and accuracy gains over k-means and k-means++ with fair comparisons.  
- Main weakness: lack of theoretical guarantees on convergence and performance; AMP methods often admit state-evolution analyses, so even partial theory or discussion would strengthen the paper.  
- Recommend adding a discussion of convergence behavior (damping, step sizes, fixed points, failure modes) and what assumptions would enable state-evolution or partial guarantees.  
- Suggest situating the work within TCS literature on provable k-means algorithms (e.g., k-means++ seeding, separation analyses, spectral/PCA guarantees, SDP approaches).  
- Clarify modeling assumptions and identifiability: explicitly state factor structure (sparsity, nonnegativity, priors), equivalence classes of solutions, and when assumptions are critical.  
- Clarify the noise model in derivation and experiments, and discuss robustness to model misspecification.  
- Provide algorithmic details: per-iteration time/memory complexity, stopping criteria, typical iteration counts, and comparisons to classical Bayesian and baseline clustering methods.  
- Discuss scalability with matrix size, rank, and cluster count, and note opportunities for parallelization or streaming updates.  
- Make the mapping from low-rank factors to hard cluster assignments explicit (rounding/post-processing), its cost, and whether the method optimizes a surrogate k-means objective.  
- Recommend reporting multiple clustering metrics (ARI/NMI/purity) and demonstrating consistency across metrics.  
- Expand experiments: sensitivity analyses (noise, rank misspecification, cluster count, init strategies), more baselines (PCA+k-means, spectral, NMF, mini-batch k-means, GMM EM), and ablations of key components.  
- Improve reproducibility: detail hyperparameter selection/tuning, provide code, random seeds, and hardware to substantiate claimed speedups.  
- Overall assessment: solid and promising paper with encouraging empirical results; stronger theoretical grounding, clearer assumptions, and broader experiments would make it a much stronger contribution.
