This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, which is then applied to the clustering problem. The authors demonstrate the connection between matrix factorization and clustering, and derive a message passing algorithm for clustering based on their matrix factorization approach.

Quality: The mathematical derivations in this paper are concise but omit some non-trivial steps, even in the appendix. Several concerns arise from the analysis:

1. In line 139 of the Appendix, the second term of (11) is stated to be O(1/√m), but the role of β in this approximation is unclear.
2. Some analyses, including the derivation of (19), appear to assume that m and n grow at the same rate. However, the asymptotic analysis in this section is somewhat terse, raising concerns about its mathematical rigor.
3. In line 167, the expectation of A{il}^2 is approximated as mτw^2. Since A is observed data, and mτw^2 is only the variance of the likelihood, the rationale for this approximation should be verified.
4. The convergence of the message passing algorithm for finite β to the MAP problem is not clearly explained. While the message passing algorithm minimizes the KL divergence, it is not guaranteed that limβ→∞ min KL = min limβ→∞ KL.
5. The proof of Proposition 1 is brief and lacks clarity. The derivation of (17) is not explicitly shown.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured, and its main point is easy to grasp. However, some mathematical derivations and analyses could be more detailed and rigorous.

Originality: The algorithm proposed in this paper appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, which has been explored before (e.g., http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). The connection between the clustering algorithm proposed by the authors and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf) is also worth noting.

Significance: While the authors focus on the application of their algorithm to clustering, comparing it only to k-means++ may not be sufficient to demonstrate its practical usefulness, given the vast literature on clustering. The proofs in this paper, even in the appendix, are concise and make it challenging to evaluate the technical correctness of the algorithm.
