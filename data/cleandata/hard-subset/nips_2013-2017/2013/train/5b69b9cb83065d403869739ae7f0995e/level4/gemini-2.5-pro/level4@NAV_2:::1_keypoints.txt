- Problem & approach: Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction using priors to encode factor structure (e.g., sparsity).
- Main algorithmic contribution: An AMP-based inference algorithm that unifies posterior marginalization and MAP estimation via a β parameter.
- Clustering application: Reformulates K-means as low-rank reconstruction with assignment-constrained factor and derives a novel AMP-based clustering algorithm.
- Relation to prior work: Connects to existing matrix-factorization–clustering literature [10,11] and extends previous AMP methods [8,9].
- Novelty/impact: General-rank AMP that includes Onsager corrections and links to ICM/variational methods, potentially avoiding poor local minima.
- Interpretability of clustering updates: Derived update (18b) corrects self-bias and favors smaller clusters, offering an interpretable alternative to Lloyd’s algorithm.
- Empirical performance: Thorough experiments show AMP-KM and AMP-MA outperform Lloyd’s, K-means++, and variational Bayes on synthetic and ORL face data.
- Convergence speed: AMP methods demonstrate fast practical convergence in experiments.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, formally defined, and clearly presents the algorithm and clustering connection.
- Theoretical limitation: Limited theoretical analysis—no convergence proof and state evolution analysis omitted.
- Related-work depth: Discussion of related AMP works is brief and needs deeper comparison of technical assumptions (fixed r vs. r/m).
- Clustering context: Should acknowledge broader provable K-means algorithms from theoretical CS to better frame contributions.
- Acceptance pros: Novel, well-motivated AMP algorithm with a clever clustering application and strong empirical validation.
- Acceptance cons: Lack of formal convergence guarantees is a notable theoretical shortcoming.
