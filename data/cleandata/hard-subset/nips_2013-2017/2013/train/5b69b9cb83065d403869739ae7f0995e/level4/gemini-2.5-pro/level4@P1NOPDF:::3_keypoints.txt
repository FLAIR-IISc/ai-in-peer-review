- Mathematical derivations are overly brief and omit non-trivial steps, making results hard to verify.  
- Appendix: claim that the second term of Eq.(11) is O(1/√m) is unjustified; parameter β is missing from Section 2 discussion.  
- Asymptotic analysis (e.g., derivation of Eq.(19)) seems to assume m and n grow at the same rate and is too terse to be convincing.  
- Approximation E[A_{il}^2] ≈ m τ_w^2 is unclear and unjustified because A is observed data while m τ_w^2 is a likelihood variance.  
- It is unclear how the finite-β message passing algorithm converges to the MAP solution; the authors do not justify exchanging limit and minimization of KL.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 and derivation of Eq.(17) are too brief to follow.  
- Paper is well-structured and central claims are easy to follow.  
- Novelty is questionable: the method appears to be a direct application of Expectation Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization.  
- Prior work (Stern et al., 2009) already addresses similar problems and should be clearly differentiated from.  
- Appendix update rules look like standard EP updates and need clearer distinction from existing literature.  
- Suggestion: explore connection to Minka’s EP “clutter” problem by using a uniform mixture of Gaussians as the likelihood.  
- Significance is limited: experiments focus only on clustering and comparison solely to k-means++ is insufficient.  
- Overall technical contribution is hard to assess due to sketchy proofs, and the method may lack novelty given prior EP-based solutions.
