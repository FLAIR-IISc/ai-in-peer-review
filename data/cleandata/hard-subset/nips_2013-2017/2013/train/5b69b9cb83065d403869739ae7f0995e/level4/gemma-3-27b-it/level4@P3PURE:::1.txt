The authors present an algorithm for matrix reconstruction under noisy observations, focusing on low-rank matrices with specific assumptions on their factors. They leverage an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the computationally expensive classical Bayesian method. A compelling aspect of this work is the connection established between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, providing a relevant application domain for the proposed algorithms.

To the best of my knowledge, the application of AMP to matrix reconstruction is novel and represents an interesting contribution. While the relationship between low-rank matrix factorizations and K-means is established (e.g., PCA provides a factor-two approximation for K-means), this connection allows the authors to present a strong experimental evaluation, comparing their method to standard K-means and K-means++ algorithms. Notably, their empirical results suggest their approach is faster and more efficient than these classical K-means techniques. The authors wisely evaluate performance using both the Frobenius norm residual and the quality of the resulting clusterings. 

The primary weakness of the paper lies in the limited theoretical guarantees regarding algorithm convergence. However, this is not uncommon, as many K-means algorithms also lack strong theoretical properties. The authors could strengthen the paper by citing relevant work in Theoretical Computer Science that offers provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting AMP algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, with promising experimental evaluation and somewhat limited theoretical results.
