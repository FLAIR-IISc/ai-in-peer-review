- Proposes an algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction from noisy observations with specific factor assumptions.
- Uses Approximate Message Passing (AMP) to speed up classical Bayesian reconstruction.
- Establishes a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering as an application.
- Novelty: applying AMP to matrix reconstruction appears to be a new and interesting contribution.
- Notes that the relationship between low-rank factorizations and K-means is known (e.g., PCA approximations).
- Leverages that connection to perform thorough experiments comparing to K-means and K-means++.
- Empirical results show the proposed method is faster and more efficient than K-means and K-means++.
- Evaluation includes both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering performance, which is valuable.
- Main limitation: scarce theoretical guarantees for algorithmic convergence.
- Suggests strengthening the paper by citing theoretical CS work on provably accurate K-means algorithms.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution to AMP for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means with promising experiments but limited theory.
