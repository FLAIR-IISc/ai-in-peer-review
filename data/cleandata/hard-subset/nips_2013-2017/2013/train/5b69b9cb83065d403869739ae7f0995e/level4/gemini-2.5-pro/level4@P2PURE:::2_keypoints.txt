- Proposes a BP-derived algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using two large-dimension approximations (details in supplement).  
- Evaluation is unconventional: clustering cast as matrix factorization and compared to Lloyd's K-means, obscuring performance on standard matrix-reconstruction tasks.  
- Missing standard benchmarks (e.g., Netflix) makes it hard to compare against prior VBMF and other methods.  
- Core contribution is incremental: straightforward BP application with the main novelty in asymptotic approximations.  
- The large-matrix approximations are relegated to the supplement, not empirically validated, and need clearer exposition.  
- Serious citation gaps: claims about matrix factorization for clustering are misleading without prior-work citations.  
- Fails to cite or discuss Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to the proposed algorithm.  
- The paper delays stating that the method is an application of Belief Propagation until Section 4.1; this should appear in the abstract.  
- Positives: algorithm is new and potentially useful; manuscript is engaging and derivations appear sound and reproducible in principle.  
- The novel evaluation (K-means framing) is somewhat fair for demonstration but insufficient to establish competitiveness.  
- Major clarity issues: full algorithm derivation is in the supplement—an outline belongs in the main text.  
- Notation is overly cumbersome (excessive hats/tildes/superscripts, redundant t superscripts, awkward symbols like τ_w).  
- Exposition lacks explicit connections (e.g., how equations 5, 8 relate to equation 2) and misses noting row-column symmetry in Algorithm 2.  
- Function meanings (e.g., f_β, G_β) and interpretations (G_β as scaled covariance) are explained too late and inconsistently use β subscripts.  
- Conceptual gaps: the role and significance of the factor m in additive noise variance and symmetry breaking are not explained.  
- Miscellaneous issues: unclear use of tilde for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization of N vs m, missing citation for the VBMF-MA implementation used.  
- Supplement problems: derivation steps using big-O between eq.14–15 are hard to follow; novelty of the supplement’s approximation technique needs clarification with prior work.  
- Presentation suggestions: include more diagrams in the main paper (Figure 1 of supplement is helpful) and fix numerous minor grammatical errors.  
- Recommendation: manuscript not ready for acceptance—requires significant revisions in experimental evaluation, prior-work positioning, and mathematical clarity.
