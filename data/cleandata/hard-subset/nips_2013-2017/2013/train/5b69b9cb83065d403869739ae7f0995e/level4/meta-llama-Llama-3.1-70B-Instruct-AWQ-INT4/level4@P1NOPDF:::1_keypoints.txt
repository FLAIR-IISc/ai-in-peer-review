- Proposes an algorithm for noisy matrix reconstruction targeting low-rank matrices with additional factor assumptions.
- Uses Approximate Message Passing (AMP) to speed up the classical Bayesian reconstruction approach.
- Establishes a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, offering an application domain.
- Applying AMP to matrix reconstruction appears novel and interesting.
- Notes that low-rank factorizations’ links to K-means are known (e.g., PCA as a factor-2 approximation).
- Provides comprehensive experiments comparing the method to k-means and k-means++.
- Empirical results indicate the proposed method is faster and more efficient than k-means and k-means++.
- Experimental strengths include evaluating both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering quality.
- Main weakness: limited theoretical guarantees, particularly about convergence.
- Suggests citing theoretical CS work on provable K-means algorithms to better contextualize limitations.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with promising experiments but relatively weak theoretical analysis that should be strengthened.
