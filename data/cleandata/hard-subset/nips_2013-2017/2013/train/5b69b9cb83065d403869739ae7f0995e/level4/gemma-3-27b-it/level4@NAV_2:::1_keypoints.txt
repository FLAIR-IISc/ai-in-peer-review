- Paper targets Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural priors (e.g., sparsity) from noisy observations.
- Proposes an efficient AMP algorithm derived from belief propagation for Bayesian inference in this setting.
- Key contribution: extends existing AMP methods to handle MAP estimation.
- Reformulates K-means clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem and applies the AMP approach.
- Experiments show performance gains over Lloyd’s K-means, notably in high-dimensional settings.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing against K-means and K-means++ on synthetic and ORL face data, reporting both Frobenius residual and clustering accuracy.
- Novel and interesting application of AMP to low-rank reconstruction with structural priors, offering a compelling alternative to PCA-based methods.
- Main limitation: limited theoretical guarantees or analysis on convergence of the proposed AMP algorithm.
- Suggestion: strengthen theoretical discussion or cite related convergence results for similar iterative schemes to improve the paper.
- Overall assessment: solid empirical contribution with practical efficiency and accuracy benefits, but weakened by lack of convergence theory.
