- Mathematical derivations are overly condensed with many intermediate steps omitted, reducing verifiability.
- Appendix: no justification that the second term of eq.(11) is O(1/√m) and parameter β is missing from Section 2 formulation.
- Asymptotic analyses (e.g., derivation of eq.(19)) assume m and n grow similarly and are presented too tersely to be rigorous.
- Unclear justification for approximating E[A_{il}^2] with mτ_w^2, since A is observed data while mτ_w^2 is a likelihood variance.
- Unclear how the finite-β message passing algorithm converges to the MAP solution; interchange of limit and minimization (limβ min KL = min limβ KL) is not justified.
- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief; derivation of eq.(17) lacks necessary detail for verification.
- Paper is well-organized and the central thesis is presented clearly.
- Originality is questionable: the method appears similar to standard Expectation-Propagation (EP) and may not be novel relative to prior work (e.g., Stern et al.).
- Potential conceptual link to Minka’s EP “clutter” problem is noted and could be discussed, but isn’t a criticism per se.
- Significance is limited by evaluation: experiments only compare to k-means++, which is insufficient to establish practical utility.
- Overall impact is tempered by sketchy proofs and the possibility that the method is a direct application of existing EP approaches.
