

The authors propose an algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices under noisy observations, incorporating additional assumptions on the matrix factors. Their approach leverages Approximate Message Passing (AMP) to accelerate the computationally intensive Bayesian reconstruction procedure. The work further establishes a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, presenting a novel application domain for their method.  

To the best of my knowledge, the AMP-based approach for matrix reconstruction is novel and well-motivated. While the link between low-rank matrix factorizations and K-means is established (e.g., PCA yields a factor-2 approximation for K-means), this connection enables a compelling experimental evaluation. The authors compare their method against k-means and k-means++, demonstrating superior speed and efficiency through empirical results. Their evaluation comprehensively assesses both Frobenius norm residuals and clustering quality, a notable strength.  

The primary limitation is the absence of strong theoretical guarantees regarding convergence. Though common in K-means algorithms, the authors should strengthen this by citing relevant theoretical computer science literature on provably accurate K-means solvers. Overall, this is a promising paper presenting AMP for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, with strong empirical results but limited theoretical foundations.
