- Presents a novel BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with large-N, large-m approximations, but derivation is mainly in the Supplementary Material.  
- Evaluation frames clustering as matrix reconstruction, making it hard to judge performance on standard matrix-reconstruction tasks.  
- Lacks use of common benchmarks (e.g., Netflix) from VBMF literature; authors should justify dataset choice or include standard comparisons.  
- Compares to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF, but the non-standard evaluation raises questions about the BP variant’s true competitiveness.  
- The algorithm is a relatively direct application of BP/Variational Message Passing; the main novelty is in the large-dimension approximations, which need validation.  
- Validity and explanation of the large-N, large-m approximations are insufficiently presented in the main paper and require stronger testing/exposition.  
- Paper misleadingly implies novelty of using matrix factorization for clustering; prior work should be cited and the connection acknowledged.  
- Fails to discuss relationship to Expectation Propagation (EP); the BP nature of the algorithm should be stated earlier (ideally in the abstract).  
- Pros: algorithm is interesting and potentially valuable, math is mostly comprehensible, experiments indicate benefits, and replication seems feasible.  
- Major derivation relegated to Supplementary Material—an outline or key steps should be included in the main text.  
- Notation is overly cumbersome (hats, tildes, many subscripts/superscripts); simplify notation and remove unnecessary symbols.  
- Specific equation readability issues (e.g., 9a, 9b, 9d, 9e) — factor common terms and combine terms to improve clarity.  
- Clarify functional relationships (e.g., meaning of functions in eq.10, role of Gβ as scaled covariance) and state limiting behavior (β → ∞) explicitly.  
- Address conceptual questions: significance of the m factor in noise variance, its effect on approximations and row-column symmetry.  
- Minor fixes: notation conventions (tilde for column vectors?), capitalization of N vs m, add citation for “VBMF-MA.”  
- Supplementary Material needs clearer exposition (product-of-exponentials form, big-O steps, novelty of approximations) and language polishing.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after substantial revisions addressing clarity, scholarship, and validation.
