

Quality: The paper's mathematical derivations lack sufficient detail, omitting non-trivial steps even in the appendix. Key concerns include:  
1) In Appendix Line 139, the claim that the second term in (11) is $O(1/\sqrt{m})$ requires justification, particularly regarding the omission of $\beta$ in Section 2.  
2) The asymptotic analysis (e.g., derivation of (19)) appears to assume $m$ and $n$ grow at the same rate. The terseness of this analysis raises questions about mathematical rigor.  
3) Appendix Line 167 states $m\tau_w^2$ approximates $\mathbb{E}[A_{il}^2]$, but $A$ is observed data while $m\tau_w^2$ represents likelihood variance. The justification for this approximation is missing.  
4) The convergence of the message-passing algorithm to the MAP problem as $\beta \to \infty$ is unproven. The claim that minimizing KL divergence implies $\lim_{\beta \to \infty} \min \text{KL} = \min \lim_{\beta \to \infty} \text{KL}$ is not rigorously established.  
5) Proposition 1’s proof is insufficiently detailed; the derivation of (17) is unclear.  

Clarity: The paper is well-structured and effectively communicates its core contributions.  

Originality: The approach—estimating first and second moments of the marginal posterior via message passing—appears to be a direct application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. This is explicitly covered in Stern et al. (2009), where Section 1 of the appendix details standard EP updates. The authors’ clustering algorithm also suggests a connection to the clutter problem in Minka’s EP work (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood is replaced with a uniform Gaussian mixture. While this connection is offered as a potential observation (not a critique of originality), the paper’s core method aligns with established EP techniques.  

Significance: The focus on clustering applications is limited by comparisons restricted to k-means++. Given the extensive literature on clustering, this comparison does not sufficiently demonstrate the algorithm’s practical utility. Furthermore, the sketchy proofs throughout the paper—including in the appendix—prevent a rigorous assessment of technical correctness. The work’s primary contribution appears to be an application of EP to matrix factorization, a technique already established by Stern et al. (2009). Without novel algorithmic or theoretical advances, the significance of the proposed method is questionable.
