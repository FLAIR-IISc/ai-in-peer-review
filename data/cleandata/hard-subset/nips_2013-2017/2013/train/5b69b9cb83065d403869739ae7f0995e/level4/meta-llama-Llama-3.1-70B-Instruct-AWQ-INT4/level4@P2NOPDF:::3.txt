This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, demonstrating its application to clustering by specializing the derived algorithm to this specific problem. The authors also conduct experiments to compare their proposed algorithm with the k-means++ algorithm.

Quality: The mathematical derivations in this paper are often incomplete and omit crucial steps, even in the appendix. Several concerns regarding the mathematical rigor of the paper are outlined below.

Firstly, the explanation for the second term of equation (11) being O(1/√m) in line 139 of the appendix is unclear, particularly with regards to the omission of β in Section 2. 

Secondly, some analyses, including the derivation of equation (19), appear to assume that m and n grow at the same rate. However, the asymptotic analysis in this section is somewhat concise, and its mathematical rigor is a concern.

Thirdly, the expectation of A{il}^2 is approximated as mτw^2 in line 167, but the rational for this approximation is not adequately justified. A is observed data, and mτ_w^2 represents only the variance of the likelihood.

Fourthly, it is unclear how the message passing algorithm derived for finite β converges to the MAP problem. While the message passing algorithm minimizes the KL divergence, the limit of β does not necessarily commute with the minimization of KL.

Lastly, the proof of Proposition 1 is overly brief, and the derivation of equation (17) is not clearly explained.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured, and its main point is easily grasped. However, the lack of detailed explanations for certain mathematical derivations may hinder understanding.

Originality: The algorithm proposed in this paper appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, primarily focusing on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior. A similar approach was previously used in matrix factorization by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Furthermore, the clustering algorithm proposed in this paper may have an interesting connection to the clutter problem in the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood of the clutter problem is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions.

Significance: While the authors mainly focus on the application of their algorithm to clustering, the comparison with only k-means++ may not be sufficient to demonstrate the practical usefulness of the algorithm. The lack of detailed proofs in the paper, even in the appendix, makes it challenging to evaluate the technical correctness of the algorithm. Moreover, given the existing literature on EP and matrix factorization, the significance of this work may be limited.
