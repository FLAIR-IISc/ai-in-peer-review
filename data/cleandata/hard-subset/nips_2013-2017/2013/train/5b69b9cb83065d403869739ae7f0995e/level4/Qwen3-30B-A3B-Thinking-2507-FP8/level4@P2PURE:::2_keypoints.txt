- Proposes a BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with asymptotic approximations for large m and N.  
- Evaluates via clustering reformulated as matrix reconstruction, a non-standard benchmark that limits generalizability.  
- Omits standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) used in prior VBMF work and provides no explicit justification for this choice.  
- Using clustering as the novel evaluation criterion raises doubts about the algorithm’s competitiveness vs. variational methods.  
- Algorithm is largely a straightforward application of BP/variational message passing and is not highly novel.  
- Main claimed novelty—the asymptotic approximations as m,N → ∞—is only in the Supplementary Material, not empirically validated, and lacks clear explanation.  
- Misrepresents the novelty of matrix factorization for clustering; prior work exists and should be cited or clarified.  
- Fails to cite Expectation Propagation (EP) and does not explain the relationship between EP and the proposed method; BP framing should appear in the abstract.  
- Experiments (especially K-means comparison) show benefits and are reasonably fair despite evaluation concerns.  
- Mathematical derivations appear reproducible but should include a high-level derivation in the main paper rather than only in the Supplementary Material.  
- Notation is overly complex and sometimes ambiguous (excess hats/tildes/subscripts, unexplained factors like 1/(m τ_w), redundant β subscripts).  
- Key equations and algorithmic symmetries lack explanatory notes and clarity (e.g., why Eq.5 is the negative log, Algorithm 2 row-column symmetry).  
- Conceptual gaps: unclear impact of the m factor in noise variance, non-standard use of tilde for vectors, inconsistent capitalization, and unclear VBMF-MA citation.  
- Supplementary Material has opaque steps (e.g., Eq.14→15 and O(m) arguments) and unclear novelty of the message approximation relative to prior work.  
- Presentation issues: some plots hard to read (black-and-white), more diagrams would help, and many minor English/article-usage errors throughout.  
- Suggested title improvement: include “approximate message passing” to better reflect the method.  
- Overall verdict: interesting and potentially useful algorithm but insufficient clarity, rigor, and empirical validation; requires significant revision (reviewer recommends rejection).
