- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extended to general rank and applied to K-means clustering.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in high-dimensional settings.  
- Mathematical derivations are technically complex but lack sufficient detail and rigor, especially in the appendix.  
- Justification for approximations (e.g., O(1/√m) term) is insufficiently explained.  
- The role and interpretation of the β parameter introduced in Section 2 need clearer elaboration.  
- Asymptotic analysis depends on scaling assumptions for m and N that are not fully discussed or justified.  
- The link between observed data variance and the noise parameter is weakly justified and needs strengthening.  
- The convergence argument as β → ∞ (for MAP estimation) is unconvincing; minimizing KL divergence alone doesn't ensure limit equivalence.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 is abbreviated and the derivation of equation (17) is unclear.  
- Paper is generally well-written and logically structured; core ideas and motivation are accessible.  
- Dense mathematical notation and missing intermediate steps in the appendix hinder comprehension; more descriptive variable names would help.  
- The work builds on existing AMP and Bayesian matrix factorization but closely resembles Expectation Propagation (EP).  
- Authors should explicitly address and differentiate their method from EP-based approaches (e.g., Stern et al.).  
- The suggested connection to the clutter problem within the EP framework is interesting and underexplored.  
- Comparing only to K-means++ is insufficient; evaluations should include stronger baselines like spectral clustering and Gaussian mixture models.  
- Concerns about sketchy proofs raise questions about robustness, scalability, and theoretical guarantees.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel AMP extension for MAP, interesting reformulation of clustering, promising empirical performance in high dimensions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient mathematical detail, unclear connection to EP, limited empirical comparisons, and weak theoretical guarantees.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form; revise with detailed derivations, clearer connections to related work, and stronger theoretical/empirical validation.
