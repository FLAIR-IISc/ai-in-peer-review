- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extends AMP to MAP estimation, and applies it to clustering, reporting better results than Lloyd’s K-means.

- Technical derivations lack rigor and omit crucial steps, requiring significant clarification and justification.

- Appendix claim about the second term scaling as O(1/√m) is unsubstantiated and appears to omit the earlier-introduced β parameter.

- Asymptotic analysis depends on m and N growing at the same rate but does not state or discuss this assumption and its implications.

- The approximation E[A_il^2] ≈ m τ_w^2 is unclear and mixes an empirical expectation with a model variance without adequate justification.

- Convergence argument as β → ∞ to approximate MAP is not convincingly demonstrated; limit interchange for KL divergence is questionable.

- Proof of Proposition 1 and the derivation of equation (17) are too condensed and need fuller, detailed exposition.

- Paper is well-structured and motivation/organization are clear.

- Novelty is questionable: the method strongly resembles Expectation Propagation (EP) for matrix factorization and clustering; the authors should explicitly address and distinguish from prior EP work.

- Significance is limited by comparison only to K-means++; lack of rigorous proofs and possible overlap with EP hinder assessment of practical impact.

- Pros for acceptance: empirical improvement over K-means++, Bayesian flexibility, and a potentially useful MAP extension of AMP.

- Cons for acceptance: insufficient mathematical rigor, probable overlap with EP (limited novelty), narrow evaluation, and terse derivations.

- Overall recommendation: lean towards reject pending substantial revisions to strengthen mathematical justification, clarify relation to EP, and expand empirical evaluation.
