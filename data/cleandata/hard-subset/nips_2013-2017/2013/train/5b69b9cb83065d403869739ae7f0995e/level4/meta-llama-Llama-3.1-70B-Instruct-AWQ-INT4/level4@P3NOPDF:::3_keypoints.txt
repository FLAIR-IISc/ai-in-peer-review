- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit several non-trivial steps, even in the appendix.  
- The justification for the O(1/√m) claim in Appendix line 139 is unclear, especially given omission of β.  
- Asymptotic analysis (e.g., derivation of (19)) seems to assume m and n scale similarly and lacks rigor.  
- Approximating E[A_{il}^2] as m τ_w^2 (line 167) is questionable because A is observed data and m τ_w^2 is only the likelihood variance.  
- Convergence from finite-β message passing to the MAP solution is unclear; swapping limit and minimization of KL is not justified.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 and derivation of (17) are too concise and need more explanation.  
- Paper is well-structured and main idea is easy to follow, but mathematical clarity needs improvement.  
- The algorithm appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, similar to prior work (e.g., Stern et al.).  
- Authors should clarify novelty relative to existing EP-based matrix factorization approaches.  
- A connection to the EP “clutter” problem could be explored by using a uniform mixture-of-Gaussians likelihood.  
- Experimental comparison to only k-means++ is insufficient to demonstrate practical usefulness.  
- Sketchy proofs and potential overlap with EP work raise concerns about technical correctness and originality.
