- Novel AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction formulated in a Bayesian framework allowing structural priors like sparsity.  
- Clear and well-presented mathematical derivation of the AMP algorithm.  
- AMP shown to outperform Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF on clustering tasks and effective on real-world data.  
- Experimental results are convincing and comparisons with existing methods are thorough.  
- Paper is technically sound; theoretical analysis and experiments support the claims.  
- Needs a deeper discussion of the relationship between AMP and existing methods such as VBMF.  
- More insight required into the significance of the m factor in the additive noise variance and its practical role.  
- Notation is overly complex; reduce use of hats, tildes, subscripts, and superscripts.  
- Minor English errors and awkward phrasings should be corrected for readability.  
- Paper is well-organized; introduction and summary of prior work are clear.  
- Algorithm derivation and experiments are clear, though some plots could be improved.  
- Original and interesting application of AMP to low-rank reconstruction and clustering.  
- Addresses an important problem with results that could impact matrix factorization and clustering.  
- Recommendation: acceptable with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
