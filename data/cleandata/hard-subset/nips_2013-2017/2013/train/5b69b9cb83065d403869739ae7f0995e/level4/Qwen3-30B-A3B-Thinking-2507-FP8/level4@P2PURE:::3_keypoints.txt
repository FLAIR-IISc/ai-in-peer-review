- Paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, specialized to clustering, and compares it to k-means++.

- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, including in the appendix.

- Issue 1: In Appendix line 139, it is unclear why the second term of (11) is O(1/√m); β seems omitted in Section 2.

- Issue 2: Several analyses (e.g., derivation of (19)) assume m and n grow at the same rate; asymptotic arguments are terse and raise rigor concerns.

- Issue 3: In line 167, the claim that m τ_w^2 equals E[A_{il}^2] is questionable since A is observed data and m τ_w^2 is a likelihood variance approximation.

- Issue 4: Unclear how message passing for finite β converges to the MAP problem; interchanging lim_{β→∞} and KL minimization may be invalid.

- Issue 5: Proof of Proposition 1 lacks detail; derivation of equation (17) is not shown.

- Clarity: The paper is well-structured and clearly conveys its main contribution.

- Originality: The algorithm appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization; Stern et al. (2009) previously applied EP in this context.

- Suggestion: There may be a connection between the authors’ clustering approach and the EP “clutter” problem (Minka, 2001), e.g., using a uniform mixture of Gaussians.

- Significance: Comparing only to k-means++ is insufficient given clustering literature; sketchy proofs and apparent overlap with prior EP work reduce perceived novelty and technical reliability.
