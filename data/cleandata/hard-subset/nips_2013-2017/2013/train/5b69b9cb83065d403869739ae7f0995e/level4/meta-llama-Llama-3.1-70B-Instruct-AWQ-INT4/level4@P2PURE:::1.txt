The authors propose an algorithm for matrix reconstruction from noisy observations, focusing on low-rank matrices with specific assumptions on the factors. They employ an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the computationally expensive classical Bayesian method. Additionally, the authors establish a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, providing an interesting application domain for their algorithm.

To the best of my knowledge, applying the AMP approach to matrix reconstruction is a novel and intriguing contribution. While the relationship between low-rank matrix factorizations and K-means is fairly well-established (e.g., PCA offers a factor two approximation algorithm for K-means), this connection enables the authors to conduct a thorough experimental evaluation of their algorithm and compare it to K-means and K-means++. Notably, their approach appears to be faster and more efficient than classical K-means and K-means++ based on their empirical results. The authors' evaluation of both the Frobenius norm residual and the actual clusterings is a valuable aspect of their experimental analysis.

The primary limitation of the paper lies in the scarcity of theoretical guarantees for the proposed algorithm's convergence. This is not entirely unexpected, as many K-means algorithms also exhibit weak properties. However, the authors could strengthen their paper by citing more theoretical computer science literature that provides provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function. Overall, the paper presents a solid contribution to Approximate Message Passing algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means, with promising experimental results, albeit somewhat limited by the theoretical guarantees.
