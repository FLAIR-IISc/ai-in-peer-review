- Proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization and connects it to clustering, with empirical comparison to k-means++.

- Mathematical derivations lack rigor; several steps are omitted or glossed over, including in the appendix.

- Appendix line 139: unclear why the second term of (11) is O(1/√m); role of β not clearly explained.

- Asymptotic analysis (e.g., derivation of (19)) seems to assume m and n grow at the same rate and is too terse.

- Appendix line 167: unclear justification for treating m τ_w^2 as the expectation of A_{il}^2; this may only be the likelihood variance and needs verification.

- Unclear how the finite-β message passing algorithm converges to the MAP problem; limit interchange of β and KL minimization is not justified.

- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief; derivation of (17) needs more detailed explanation.

- Paper is well-structured and the main idea is easy to grasp.

- Originality is questionable: approach appears similar to Expectation-Propagation applied to matrix factorization (e.g., Stern et al.), and Appendix updates look like standard EP procedures.

- Suggests a possible connection to EP’s clutter problem (with a uniform Gaussian mixture likelihood), but this is a suggestion rather than a claim of novelty.

- Significance limited by evaluation: comparison only to k-means++ may be insufficient to show practical usefulness.

- Overall technical correctness is hard to assess due to lack of rigorous proofs and insufficient distinction from prior EP-based work.
