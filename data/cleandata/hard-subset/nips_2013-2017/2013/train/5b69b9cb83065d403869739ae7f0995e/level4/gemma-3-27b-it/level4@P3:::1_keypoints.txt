- Introduces an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction extended to MAP estimation, applied to K-means clustering.  
- Leverages flexible priors to encode structural properties beyond low-rankness.  
- Novel use of AMP for MAP estimation, extending prior AMP work.  
- Offers a computationally efficient alternative to MCMC and variational Bayes.  
- Empirical results show better reconstruction error and clustering accuracy than Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++, especially in high dimensions.  
- Clarifies differences from recent AMP works (rank scaling and factor independence assumptions).  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of convergence and optimality for the non-convex problem.  
- Proposition 1 is presented but feels isolated; conditions guaranteeing global optimality are not fully explored.  
- The scaling parameter τ is chosen empirically and its effect on performance is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Some mathematical notation is dense and certain equations (e.g., 9a–9d) need more intuitive explanation.  
- Strengths summarized: novelty, efficiency, strong empirical performance, and practical relevance to clustering.  
- Weaknesses summarized: limited theoretical guarantees, parameter sensitivity concerns, and readability issues.  
- Recommendation: Acceptable paper with encouragement to address theoretical limitations and parameter sensitivity in future work.
