- Proposes a BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with two large-system approximations (N→∞, m→∞) derived in the Supplementary Material.  
- Evaluates method via clustering-as-matrix-factorization and compares to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF, a non-standard evaluation that limits extrapolation to conventional tasks.  
- Paper should evaluate on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) or explicitly justify why such datasets cannot be used.  
- Algorithm is essentially a direct application of BP to a problem previously treated with variational message passing—novel but not groundbreaking.  
- The validity and empirical testing of the large-N and large-m approximations are insufficient and unclearly explained.  
- Prior work connecting clustering and matrix factorization is not cited adequately; the paper gives the false impression of novelty.  
- Expectation Propagation (EP) is missing from citations and the relationship between EP and the proposed algorithm should be clarified.  
- The paper delays labeling the method as BP until Section 4.1 and should state this earlier (e.g., in the abstract).  
- Paper is engaging and the mathematics are generally followable, suggesting the algorithm is potentially useful.  
- K-means experiments favor the proposed method, but the choice of evaluation criterion raises concerns about competitiveness and generality.  
- Major derivation relegated to supplement; an outline of the derivation should appear in the main text.  
- Algorithmic description is hard to parse and the notation is cumbersome (overuse of hats, tildes, subscripts, superscripts, and time indices).  
- Specific equation readability improvements recommended (factor out repeated terms, combine terms in eqs. 9b/9e).  
- Functions in eq.10 and G_β should be defined and their meanings (mean, scaled covariance) stated when first introduced.  
- β-dependence handling is inconsistent; suggest treating β as a global parameter and removing unnecessary β subscripts.  
- Discuss the role of factor m in the additive noise variance, its effect on row–column asymmetry, and implications for the approximations.  
- Notational inconsistencies/questions: tilde usage for column vectors, uppercase N vs lowercase m should be clarified.  
- Add a citation for the VBMF-MA implementation referenced in Section 5.  
- Supplementary material needs clarification (e.g., eq.5 formatting, step from eq.14→15) and better connection to prior approximation methods.  
- Language and style require polishing, with many specific wording fixes suggested and a more descriptive title recommended.  
- Include more illustrative diagrams (similar to Supplementary Figure 1) to improve clarity.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and publishable in principle, but requires clearer mathematics, stronger literature positioning, and more standard empirical evaluation before acceptance.
