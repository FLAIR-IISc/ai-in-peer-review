

This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and demonstrates its application to clustering problems. The authors establish a connection between matrix factorization and clustering by reformulating the clustering problem as a special case of low-rank matrix reconstruction, then derive a specialized message passing algorithm for clustering. Extensive numerical experiments are conducted to compare the proposed method with k-means++.

Quality: The mathematical derivations in the paper are notably sparse, omitting several non-trivial steps even in the appendix. 
1) In Appendix Line 139, the justification for the second term in (11) being O(1/\sqrt(m)) appears incomplete, as \beta is not properly accounted for in Section 2. 
2) The asymptotic analysis in Section 2 assumes m and N grow proportionally, but the mathematical rigor of these asymptotic arguments is questionable given the terseness of the presentation. 
3) In Line 167, the claim that m\tau_w^2 represents the expectation of A_{ij}^2 requires justification, as m\tau_w^2 is merely the variance of the likelihood, not the expectation of the observed data. 
4) The convergence of the message passing algorithm for finite \beta to the MAP problem is not rigorously established; the relationship between minimizing KL divergence and taking the limit \beta \to \infty requires careful mathematical treatment. 
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed; the derivation of Equation (17) is particularly unclear.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and effectively communicates its main contributions, making it straightforward to understand the core ideas.

Originality: The authors focus on estimating first and second moments of the marginal posterior, which suggests the proposed algorithm is essentially an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. EP has already been applied to matrix factorization in Stern et al. (2009), where Section 1 of their appendix details standard EP update procedures. The authors should clarify whether their approach is truly novel or simply an application of EP. Additionally, there appears to be an interesting connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (Minka, 2001), particularly if the clutter likelihood is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions.

Significance: While the application to clustering is promising, the experimental comparison is limited to k-means++ alone, which is insufficient to establish the practical utility of the algorithm given the extensive literature on clustering. The technical correctness of the proposed method is difficult to assess due to the sketchy proofs throughout the paper and its appendix. The suspicion that the approach is merely an application of EP to matrix factorization (as demonstrated by Stern et al., 2009) further undermines the significance of the contribution.
