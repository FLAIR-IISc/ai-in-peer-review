- Presents a new algorithm applying Belief Propagation to a Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction problem.  
- Uses two simplifying approximations assuming large numbers of rows and columns; derivations relegated to the supplementary material.  
- Evaluated by formulating clustering as matrix factorization and compared to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF, showing empirical benefit.  
- Experimental setup is non-standard (clustering-as-factorization), limiting generalization to typical matrix-reconstruction tasks.  
- Authors did not use common benchmarks (e.g., Netflix); they should justify that omission.  
- The method is largely a straightforward BP application rather than a groundbreaking contribution.  
- Main novelty—approximations in the N→∞, m→∞ limit—is not directly validated in experiments.  
- Serious citation and scholarship issues: the matrix-factorization–clustering connection is not properly attributed.  
- Paper should cite Expectation Propagation and discuss its relation to the proposed algorithm.  
- Mathematical derivations mostly in the supplement, making the main paper hard to assess.  
- Notation is cumbersome and confusing (excessive hats/tildes/sub/superscripts); simplify for readability.  
- Specific presentation issues: unexplained m factor in noise variance, nonstandard tilde for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization of N vs m.  
- Missing citation for the primary reference used to implement VBMF-MA.  
- Supplementary material needs clearer explanations and diagrams to support the algorithm’s derivation and novelty.  
- English is generally acceptable but has article-usage errors that can distract readers.  
- Conclusion: algorithm is interesting and publishable in principle, but paper requires substantial improvements in clarity and scholarship before acceptance.
