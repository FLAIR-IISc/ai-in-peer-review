This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, drawing an interesting connection between matrix factorization and the clustering problem. The authors then specialize the message passing algorithm to clustering and evaluate its performance by comparing it with the k-means++ algorithm.

Quality: While the paper presents some promising ideas, the mathematical derivations are not rigorous enough. Several steps in the derivations are omitted or glossed over, even in the appendix. Specifically, I have the following concerns:

1. In line 139 of the Appendix, it is unclear why the second term of (11) is O(1/\sqrt(m)). The role of \beta is not clearly explained in Section 2, which may be related to this issue.
2. Some of the analysis, including the derivation of (19), appears to assume that m and n grow at the same rate. However, the asymptotic analysis in this section is somewhat terse, and I am concerned about its mathematical rigor.
3. In line 167, it is not clear why m \tau_w^2 is considered the expectation of A{il}^2. Since A is observed data, m\tau_w^2 only represents the variance of the likelihood. The rationale behind this approximation should be verified.
4. It is unclear how the message passing algorithm derived for finite \beta converges to the MAP problem. While the message passing algorithm minimizes the KL divergence, it is not guaranteed that \lim\beta \min KL = \min \lim\beta KL.
5. The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and does not provide sufficient details. It would be helpful to explain how (17) is derived.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured, and the main idea is easy to grasp.

Originality: The algorithm appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, primarily focusing on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior. This approach has been explored before in the context of matrix factorization (e.g., http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). The updated procedures in Section 1 of the Appendix seem to be standard EP procedures. I would appreciate it if the authors could clarify or correct this point. Additionally, there may be an interesting connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), if the likelihood of the clutter problem is modified to a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. I would like to emphasize that this is a suggestion rather than a criticism of the paper's originality.

Significance: While the authors primarily focus on the application of their algorithm to clustering, the comparison with only k-means++ may not be sufficient to demonstrate the practical usefulness of the algorithm. Furthermore, the lack of rigorous proofs in the paper makes it challenging to evaluate its technical correctness. As mentioned earlier, the algorithm appears to be an application of expectation-propagation to matrix factorization, which has been explored before by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
