- Proposes a novel BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with large-m, large-N approximations.
- Algorithm evaluated by casting clustering as matrix reconstruction and compared to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.
- Experimental setup uses a non-standard problem and bespoke evaluation criterion, making extrapolation to standard reconstruction tasks difficult.
- Authors did not use standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) used in prior VBMF work, and should justify this choice or include those comparisons.
- The new criterion for evaluating the BP variant raises doubts about competitiveness; explanation for not using standard benchmarks is needed.
- The method is a relatively straightforward application of BP/variational message passing, so novelty is incremental rather than groundbreaking.
- The asymptotic approximations (m,N → ∞) are the most interesting contribution but are only in the supplementary material and are not empirically validated.
- Serious citation omissions: matrix factorization-to-clustering link is well-known and should be acknowledged.
- Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to the proposed algorithm should be cited and discussed.
- The main algorithm should be stated explicitly as an application of Belief Propagation in the abstract.
- Positive: the algorithm is potentially useful and the mathematical derivation is followable.
- Experiments (K-means formulation) show benefits for the new algorithm, though the evaluation criteria are questionable.
- Paper clarity is poor: key derivations are tucked in the supplement and the mathematics/notation are cumbersome and confusing.
- Specific notation problems: nonstandard use of tilde for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization (N vs m), and excess hats/tildes/subscripts/superscripts.
- Missing citation: Section 5 needs a reference for the implementation of the competing VBMF-MA algorithm.
- Supplementary material has language issues, lacks diagrams, and should better explain/validate approximations.
- Recommendation: algorithm merits publication after substantial revision to improve clarity, scholarship, empirical validation, and citation completeness.
