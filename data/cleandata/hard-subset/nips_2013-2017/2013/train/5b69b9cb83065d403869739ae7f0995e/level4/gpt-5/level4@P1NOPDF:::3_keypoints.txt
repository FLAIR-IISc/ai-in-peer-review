- Contribution: Proposes an AMP algorithm for matrix factorization, specializes it to clustering, and compares to k-means++ in experiments.

- Quality (general): Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit many nontrivial steps, including in the appendix.

- Issue 1: Appendix line 139 — the claim that the second term of (11) is O(1/√m) is unexplained, and β is omitted from Section 2’s scaling without clarification.

- Issue 2: Asymptotics — the analysis seems to assume m and n grow at the same rate; the paper must state the precise asymptotic regime (e.g., n/m→α) and justify which terms are negligible.

- Issue 3: Appendix line 167 — treating m τ_w^2 as E[A_{il}^2] is unjustified; the paper should state the generative model, independence assumptions, and LLN reasoning behind this approximation.

- Issue 4: MAP limit — it’s unclear how finite-β message passing converges to the MAP solution; the authors must justify exchange-of-limits, objective continuity, and conditions for lim_{β→∞} convergence.

- Issue 5: Proposition 1 — the proof is too brief; derivation of (17) needs the missing steps or a clear reference and explicit assumptions.

- Clarity: Paper is well organized and main ideas are easy to follow.

- Originality: The algorithm targets first/second posterior moments and appears closely related to Expectation Propagation; the paper should clarify precise differences from EP and cite prior work (e.g., Stern et al. WWW 2009).

- Potential link: There may be a conceptual connection to the EP “clutter” problem (Minka 2001) that should be articulated if relevant.

- Significance (experiments): Experimental comparison is limited to k-means++; stronger baselines (GMM with EM/VI, spectral clustering, Bayesian clustering methods) are needed to establish practical value.

- Overall concern: Many proofs are sketched, making technical correctness hard to assess; the authors must explicitly distinguish AMP from EP (state evolution, Onsager terms, factorization/damping) both theoretically and empirically.
