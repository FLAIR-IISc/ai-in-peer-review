- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction from noisy observations, leveraging assumptions on latent factors to speed up a Bayesian method.
- Novel application of AMP to this matrix reconstruction setting; considered interesting and original.
- Establishes a connection between low-rank factorization and k-means, providing a relevant application domain.
- Empirically compares to k-means and k-means++ and appears faster and more efficient than these baselines.
- Evaluation reports both Frobenius-norm residuals and clustering quality, which is a strength.
- Experimental results are promising and the empirical evaluation is clear and relevant.
- Main weakness: lacks strong theoretical guarantees, particularly on convergence.
- Suggests citing additional Theoretical Computer Science work that gives provable algorithms for the k-means objective.
- Overall assessment: solid empirical paper with compelling linkage to k-means but relatively weak theoretical results.
