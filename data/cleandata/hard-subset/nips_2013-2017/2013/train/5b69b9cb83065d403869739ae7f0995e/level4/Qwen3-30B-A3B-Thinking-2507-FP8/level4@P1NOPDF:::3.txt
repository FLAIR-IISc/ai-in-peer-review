

This paper proposes an approximate message-passing algorithm for matrix factorization, framed as a generalization of clustering. The authors derive the message-passing scheme for matrix factorization and specialize it to clustering, then compare it empirically to k-means++.  

**Quality:** The mathematical derivations are insufficiently detailed, omitting non-trivial steps even in the appendix.  
1) In Appendix line 139, why does the second term in Equation (11) scale as $O(1/\sqrt{m})$? $\beta$ appears to be omitted in Section 2.  
2) The analysis of Equation (19) and related asymptotic results seem to assume $m$ and $n$ grow at the same rate. The asymptotic arguments are terse, raising concerns about mathematical rigor.  
3) In Appendix line 167, why is $m\tau_w^2$ the expectation of $A_{il}^2$? $A$ is observed data, while $m\tau_w^2$ is the variance of the likelihood; the rationale for this approximation requires justification.  
4) How does the finite-$\beta$ message-passing algorithm converge to the MAP solution? Message passing minimizes KL divergence, but $\lim_{\beta \to \infty} \min \text{KL} \neq \min \lim_{\beta \to \infty} \text{KL}$ in general.  
5) Proposition 1’s proof is too brief; specifically, the derivation of Equation (17) is unclear.  

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly communicates its core contributions.  

**Originality:** The authors focus on estimating first and second moments of the marginal posterior, suggesting their algorithm is essentially an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. EP has already been applied to matrix factorization in Stern et al. (2009), where Section 1 of the appendix details standard EP updates. If this is indeed the case, the novelty is limited. Furthermore, the proposed clustering algorithm appears related to the clutter problem in Minka’s EP paper (2001), particularly if the clutter likelihood is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussians. This observation does not undermine the paper’s novelty but merits clarification.  

**Significance:** While the authors emphasize clustering applications, comparing solely to k-means++ is insufficient to establish practical utility given the extensive literature on clustering. Crucially, the sketchy proofs—both in the main text and appendix—make it difficult to assess technical correctness. The algorithm’s apparent reliance on EP (already established by Stern et al., 2009) further weakens its significance.
