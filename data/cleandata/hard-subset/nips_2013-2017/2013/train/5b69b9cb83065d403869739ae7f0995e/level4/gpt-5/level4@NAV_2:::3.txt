This paper presents an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization in a Bayesian setting. The authors note that clustering is a special case of low-rank factorization under a discrete assignment constraint and then tailor their AMP method to this setting, yielding a clustering algorithm. Finally, they compare the proposed approach to the k-means++ algorithm experimentally.

Quality: The mathematical development is terse and skips several nontrivial steps, even in the appendix.
1) In Appendix line 139, why is the second term of (11) O(1/√m)? Also, β appears to be missing in Section 2.
2) Parts of the analysis, including the derivation of (19), seem to rely on m and n scaling at the same rate. Overall, the asymptotics are presented rather briefly, and I am concerned about the rigor.
3) In line 167, why is m τw^2 taken as E[Ail^2]? A is observed data, whereas m τw^2 is the variance parameter of the likelihood; the justification for this approximation should be provided.
4) How does the finite-β message passing algorithm converge to the MAP solution? The algorithm is framed as minimizing a KL divergence, but interchanging limβ and argmin (i.e., limβ min KL vs. min limβ KL) is not obviously valid.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is too condensed. How is equation (17) obtained?

Clarity: The paper is well organized, and the main contributions are easy to follow.

Originality: Because the method centers on estimating marginal means and covariances, the overall approach appears closely related to expectation propagation (EP) for matrix factorization. EP has been applied to matrix factorization previously (e.g., Stern et al., http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Section 1 of the appendix reads like standard EP update rules; please clarify if this is not the case. There also seems to be a potential link between the proposed clustering method and the clutter model in Minka’s EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf) if the likelihood is replaced by a uniform mixture of Gaussians. To be clear, I am pointing out a possible connection rather than questioning novelty outright.

Significance: The empirical evaluation focuses on clustering, but given the breadth of clustering literature, comparison solely to k-means++ may be insufficient to demonstrate practical impact. Moreover, because several proofs (including in the appendix) are quite sketchy, it is difficult to fully assess technical correctness. I also suspect the method may essentially instantiate EP for matrix factorization, as in Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf), which affects the perceived contribution.
