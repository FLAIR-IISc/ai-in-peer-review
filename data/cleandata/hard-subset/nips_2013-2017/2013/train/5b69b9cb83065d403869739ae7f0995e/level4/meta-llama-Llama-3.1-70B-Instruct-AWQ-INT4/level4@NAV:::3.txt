This paper presents an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, drawing a connection to the clustering problem. By specializing the derived algorithm to clustering, the authors demonstrate its applicability. Experimental comparisons with the k-means++ algorithm are also provided.

Quality: The mathematical derivations in this manuscript are somewhat incomplete, omitting crucial steps even in the appendix.
1) The reasoning behind the second term of (11) being O(1/\sqrt(m)) in line 139 of the Appendix is unclear, particularly with regards to the omission of \beta in Section 2.
2) Some analyses, including the derivation of (19), appear to assume that m and n grow at the same rate. However, the asymptotic analysis in this section lacks detail, raising concerns about its mathematical rigor.
3) The statement in line 167 that m \tau_w^2 is the expectation of A{il}^2 requires clarification. Since A is observed data, m\tau_w^2 represents only the variance of the likelihood, and the rationale behind this approximation should be verified.
4) The convergence of the message passing algorithm for finite \beta to the MAP problem is unclear. Although the algorithm minimizes the KL divergence, it does not necessarily follow that \lim\beta \min KL = \min \lim\beta KL.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is too concise and warrants further explanation, particularly with regards to the derivation of (17).

Clarity: The manuscript is well-structured, and its main point is easily grasped.

Originality: Given the primary focus on estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior, it appears that the proposed algorithm is essentially an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. This approach has been previously explored in the context of matrix factorization (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). The EP update procedures in Section 1 of the Appendix seem to follow standard practices; please clarify if this is incorrect. Furthermore, a potential connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf) could be explored by substituting the likelihood of the clutter problem with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions.

Significance: While the authors primarily focus on the application to clustering, the comparison to only k-means++ may be insufficient to demonstrate the practical utility of the algorithm. The proofs, even in the appendix, are somewhat sketchy, making it challenging to evaluate the technical correctness. Additionally, the algorithm appears to be an application of expectation-propagation to the matrix factorization problem, which has been previously explored by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf).
