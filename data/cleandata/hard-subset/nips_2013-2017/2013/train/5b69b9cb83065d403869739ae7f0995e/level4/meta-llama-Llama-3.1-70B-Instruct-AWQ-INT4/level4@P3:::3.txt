This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering problems. The authors formulate the matrix reconstruction problem in a Bayesian framework, allowing for the incorporation of structural properties such as sparsity. They derive an efficient AMP algorithm for Bayesian inference and demonstrate its application to clustering by reformulating the clustering problem as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem.

The paper is well-structured, and the main idea is easy to grasp. However, the mathematical derivations are sometimes sketchy, omitting non-trivial steps even in the appendix. For instance, the second term of equation (11) is stated to be O(1/√m) without clear explanation, and the parameter β seems to be omitted in Section 2. Additionally, some analyses, including the derivation of equation (19), appear to assume that m and n grow in the same order, which may not always be the case.

The paper's originality is also a concern. The algorithm seems to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization, which has been done before (e.g., [http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf](http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf)). The authors should clarify how their work differs from existing EP applications in matrix factorization. Furthermore, an interesting connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in the original EP paper could be explored, particularly if the likelihood of the clutter problem is switched to a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions.

The significance of the paper is also questionable, as the authors mainly focus on its application to clustering, but only compare it to k-means++. Given the vast literature on clustering, a more comprehensive comparison with other algorithms would be necessary to demonstrate the practical usefulness of the proposed algorithm. Moreover, the proofs in the paper, even in the appendix, are very sketchy, making it challenging to evaluate the technical correctness of the work.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithm through numerical experiments.
* The paper is well-structured, and the main idea is easy to grasp.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The mathematical derivations are sometimes sketchy, omitting non-trivial steps.
* The originality of the paper is questionable, as it appears to be an application of EP to matrix factorization.
* The significance of the paper is limited, as it only compares the proposed algorithm to k-means++.

**Suggestions for improvement:**

* Provide more detailed mathematical derivations, particularly in the appendix.
* Clarify how the proposed algorithm differs from existing EP applications in matrix factorization.
* Conduct a more comprehensive comparison with other clustering algorithms to demonstrate the practical usefulness of the proposed algorithm.
* Provide more detailed proofs, particularly in the appendix, to ensure the technical correctness of the work.
