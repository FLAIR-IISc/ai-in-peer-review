This paper introduces an algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations, leveraging an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate computation compared to traditional Bayesian methods. The work focuses on scenarios with specific assumptions regarding the matrix factors and notably establishes a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, offering a compelling application area for the proposed techniques.

The application of AMP to matrix reconstruction appears to be a novel and promising contribution. While the relationship between low-rank matrix factorization and K-means is established – for instance, PCA provides a 2-approximation for K-means – this connection motivates a thorough empirical evaluation. The authors effectively demonstrate the performance of their algorithm through comparisons to standard K-means and K-means++ implementations. Notably, their experiments suggest the proposed approach achieves improved speed and efficiency. The evaluation is further strengthened by considering both Frobenius norm residual *and* the quality of resulting clusterings.

A primary limitation of the paper is the relative lack of rigorous theoretical guarantees regarding convergence. This is not uncommon in the field – many K-means algorithms also lack strong theoretical foundations – but addressing this would strengthen the work. The authors could benefit from citing more research from the Theoretical Computer Science literature that presents provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective. 

Overall, this is a solid paper presenting an AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with a valuable application to K-means. The experimental results are promising, although the theoretical analysis could be further developed.
