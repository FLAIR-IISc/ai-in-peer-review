- Proposes a BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with two large-matrix approximations.  
- Evaluated on clustering (framed as matrix reconstruction) instead of standard benchmarks, making general performance unclear.  
- Lacks comparisons on conventional datasets (e.g., Netflix) used by prior VBMF work, hindering direct competitiveness claims.  
- Misleading framing: treating matrix-factorization-for-clustering as novel without citing prior work.  
- Fails to state up front that the method is essentially Belief Propagation; this appears only in Section 4.1.  
- Does not cite or discuss Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to the proposed algorithm.  
- Core novelty (large-matrix approximations) relegated to the supplement, not validated or compared to prior approximations.  
- Mathematical presentation is cluttered and confusing (excessive hats/tildes/subscripts/superscripts); notation should be simplified.  
- Exposition omits key explanations linking equations and functions, making derivations hard to follow.  
- Algorithm symmetry and simplifications are not highlighted; many definitions (e.g., f_beta, G_beta) lack immediate intuitive descriptions.  
- Important modeling details are unexplained (e.g., role of factor m and its effect on row-column symmetry).  
- Supplementary derivations (dropping O(1/m) terms) are unclear and need clearer justification.  
- Unclear novelty relative to previous approximate message-passing techniques; relationship to prior methods must be clarified.  
- Minor fixes requested: add specific citation for VBMF-MA implementation and clarify links to prior VBMF papers.  
- Overall: interesting idea but insufficient scholarship, evaluation, and presentation; requires major revision before acceptance.
