- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for noisy low-rank matrix reconstruction using structural assumptions on factor matrices.  
- Connects matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering, providing a meaningful application context.  
- AMP methodology for this problem appears novel and well-motivated.  
- Empirical benchmarks against K-means and K-means++ show superior speed and efficiency.  
- Evaluation measures both Frobenius norm residual and clustering quality, giving comprehensive empirical support.  
- Main limitation: limited theoretical guarantees on convergence.  
- Recommends citing and discussing provable K-means solvers (e.g., Kumar et al., Ostrovsky et al.) to strengthen theoretical context.  
- Overall: valuable and promising computational contribution, but theoretical shortcomings limit full impact and require further development.
