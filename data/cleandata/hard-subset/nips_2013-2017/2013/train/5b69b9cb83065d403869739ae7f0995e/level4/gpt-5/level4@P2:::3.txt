Summary
The paper introduces an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on the factors, and extends it to handle both posterior mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) estimation within a unified framework. The authors further show that a K-means–type clustering problem can be cast as a special case of low-rank reconstruction with a discrete prior on assignments, yielding an AMP-based clustering algorithm. Empirically, the proposed methods are compared to Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++, with improved performance reported on synthetic data and the ORL faces dataset.

Quality
The technical development is promising, but the derivations are often terse and omit key steps even in the appendix, making it difficult to verify correctness. Specific questions:

1) Appendix, line 139: Why is the second term of (11) O(1/√m)? Please justify the scaling carefully. Also, β appears to be omitted in Section 2; clarify where β is introduced and how it propagates through the derivations.

2) Several steps (e.g., the derivation leading to (19)) seem to assume m and N grow at the same rate. Please state the precise asymptotic regime and conditions (e.g., fixed rank r vs r/m, independence/moment assumptions) under which the state evolution and approximations are valid.

3) Appendix, line 167: Why is E[Ail^2] approximated by m τ w^2? A is observed data, and m τ w^2 corresponds to the variance term from the likelihood. The rationale and conditions for replacing empirical second moments with this expectation need to be made explicit (e.g., law of large numbers under the AMP state evolution, or a bias/variance decomposition showing the signal term vanishes under the scaling).

4) Convergence to MAP as β→∞: The message-passing fixed points are typically characterized as stationary points of a Bethe free energy/KL objective for finite β. Interchanging limit and minimization is nontrivial; limβ min KL = min limβ KL need not hold. Please provide conditions under which the β→∞ limit of your AMP iterates yields stationary points of the MAP objective, or a rigorous argument (e.g., via Γ-convergence or stability analysis of f and G as β→∞).

5) Proposition 1: The proof is too brief. In particular, how is equation (17) obtained? A more detailed derivation of the surrogate objective and the role of the Onsager terms would help, including the assumptions ensuring positive semidefiniteness of the summed covariance terms and the precise connection to block coordinate minimization.

Clarity
The paper is generally well structured and the main ideas are easy to follow. However, the appendix should include the missing algebraic steps, explicit statements of assumptions (scalings, independence, moments), and a clear mapping between the belief-propagation/Bethestyle objective and the AMP updates. Clarify notation (e.g., τ vs τw, m vs N, index conventions) and ensure equation numbering matches the main text.

Originality
The algorithm appears closely related to Expectation Propagation (EP). Since the method focuses on matching first and second moments of marginal posteriors, much of Section 1 of the appendix reads like standard EP updates. EP has been applied to matrix factorization before (e.g., Stern et al., “Matchbox,” WWW 2009: http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Please position your contribution relative to EP explicitly: in what sense does your AMP derivation differ from EP (factor graph structure, Onsager corrections, state evolution predictions, convergence properties)? Also, there seems to be a conceptual link between your clustering formulation and the “clutter” model from the original EP work (Minka’s EP paper: http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf) if the likelihood is a uniform mixture of Gaussians; clarifying similarities/differences would strengthen the originality claim.

Significance
The clustering application is appealing, and the empirical results against K-means/K-means++ are encouraging, especially in high dimensions. However, given the extensive clustering literature, comparisons limited to K-means/K-means++ are not sufficient to establish practical impact. Please consider additional baselines, such as:

- Gaussian mixture models with EM (and with diagonal/full covariances),
- Spectral clustering,
- NMF-based clustering variants (including sparse/nonnegative constraints),
- Variational/Bayesian MF methods used for clustering.

Reporting runtime, memory usage, sensitivity to initialization, and robustness to model misspecification would also help demonstrate practical relevance.

Pros for acceptance
- Unified AMP framework for Bayesian low-rank reconstruction with a clear MAP/mean connection via β.
- Novel and potentially effective application to clustering, with promising empirical improvements over K-means/K-means++.
- Efficient O(mN) per-iteration cost and symmetry between U and V updates.

Cons for acceptance
- Derivations and proofs are sketchy; several steps and assumptions are unstated or insufficiently justified.
- Unclear originality relative to EP-based matrix factorization; connections not discussed.
- Limited experimental baselines for clustering; practical advantages not conclusively established.

Suggestions to improve the paper
- Expand the appendix with full derivations (scalings, limits, and the role of Onsager terms), a precise asymptotic regime, and a careful treatment of the β→∞ limit.
- Provide a detailed proof of Proposition 1, including the derivation of (17).
- Clarify the relationship to EP (and prior EP-based MF/clustering work) and articulate the specific benefits of the AMP formulation (e.g., state evolution accuracy, convergence speed).
- Broaden the empirical evaluation with additional clustering baselines, ablations, and runtime analyses.
