- Paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, specialized to clustering, with experiments comparing to k-means++.

- Mathematical derivations are under-detailed, with crucial steps omitted even in the appendix.

- Unclear justification that the second term of Eq.(11) is O(1/√m); treatment of β in Section 2 is not explained.

- Derivations such as Eq.(19) appear to assume m and n grow at the same rate without stating this; asymptotic analysis is incomplete.

- Claim that m τ_w^2 represents E[A_{il}^2] is questionable because A is observed data and m τ_w^2 corresponds to likelihood variance; rationale is needed.

- Convergence of the message passing algorithm to the MAP problem for finite β is not established; minimizing KL then taking β→∞ may not commute.

- Proof of Proposition 1 is too concise; derivation of Eq.(17) requires more detailed explanation.

- Manuscript organization and presentation are good, making the main contribution easy to follow.

- Originality is questionable: the method resembles Expectation-Propagation (EP) for matrix factorization and related prior work (e.g., Stern et al.).

- Appendix updates follow standard EP procedures and may not be novel; a connection to the EP clutter problem is plausible and should be discussed.

- Significance is limited by experiments that only compare to k-means++; broader benchmarking against clustering literature is needed.

- Sketchy proofs and similarities to existing EP work undermine confidence in the manuscript’s technical contribution and overall importance.
