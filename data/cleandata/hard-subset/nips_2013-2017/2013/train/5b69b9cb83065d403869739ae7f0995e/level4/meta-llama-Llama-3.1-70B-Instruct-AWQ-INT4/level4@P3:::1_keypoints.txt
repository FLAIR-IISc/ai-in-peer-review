- Proposes an efficient AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction from noisy observations, applied to K-means clustering.  
- Novel use of AMP for matrix reconstruction and a new perspective on the connection between low-rank factorizations and K-means.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating superior Frobenius norm residuals and clustering accuracy versus K-means and K-means++.  
- Clear, detailed derivation and description of the algorithm and its properties; paper is well-written and organized.  
- Experiments cover both synthetic and real-world datasets and are convincing.  
- Theoretical guarantees are limited, with few convergence results provided.  
- Suggests adding citations to Theoretical Computer Science work on provably accurate K-means algorithms.  
- Overall contribution is significant and valuable to the field despite theoretical gaps.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, thorough experiments, clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient theoretical analysis and missing relevant citations.
