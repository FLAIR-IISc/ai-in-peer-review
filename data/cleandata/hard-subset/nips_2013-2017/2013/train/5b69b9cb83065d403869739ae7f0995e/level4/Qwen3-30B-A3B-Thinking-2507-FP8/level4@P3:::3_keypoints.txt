- Proposes an AMP algorithm extending AMP to Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, handling both marginalization and MAP estimation.
- Applies the AMP framework to clustering by reformulating K-means as a constrained low-rank matrix reconstruction problem that enforces one-cluster-per-point.
- Builds on recent AMP developments for matrix reconstruction and extends them to a wider range of estimation objectives.
- Main contributions: (1) generalized AMP for low-rank reconstruction (marginalization + MAP), (2) AMP-based clustering that respects cluster-assignment constraints.
- Empirical results show the AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ on synthetic and real datasets (ORL Face Database).
- The approach is theoretically motivated, algorithmically sound, and computationally efficient (O(mN) per iteration).
- Mathematical derivations lack sufficient rigor: asymptotic analysis assumes m and N grow at the same rate without justification.
- Appendix omits or inadequately explains non-trivial steps (e.g., O(1/√m) term, derivation of Eq.19, justification of mτw² as E[Aᵢⱼ²]).
- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief, making Equation (17) difficult to verify.
- Novelty concerns: core algorithm resembles Expectation Propagation (EP) updates and the paper under-discusses the EP connection and related clutter problem.
- Significance is promising but limited by narrow experimental comparisons; should include more state-of-the-art clustering baselines (spectral, hierarchical, Bayesian methods).
- Strengths: clear problem-to-algorithm exposition, flexible AMP framework, strong empirical performance, and low computational cost.
- Weaknesses: insufficient mathematical rigor, underdeveloped discussion of relation to EP, and limited comparative experiments.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing mathematical gaps and strengthening discussion/comparisons.
