- Novel application of AMP to general low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending prior rank-one/posterior-mean work and appearing mathematically sound and useful.  
- Strong empirical performance: AMP-KM and AMP-MA outperform Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and VBMF on synthetic and real clustering tasks (K-means loss and accuracy).  
- Elegant unified framework via β that interpolates between posterior-mean marginalization and MAP inference.  
- Algorithm presentation and derivation are unclear; Algorithm 2 is hard to parse and key approximations are relegated to the supplement.  
- Notation is overly complex (many superscripts/subscripts/hats); symmetric U/V updates could be highlighted and expressions simplified.  
- Key components lack conceptual explanation (e.g., f and G should be introduced as scaled mean and covariance when first presented).  
- Poor positioning/scholarship: presents clustering-as-matrix-factorization as novel despite being well-established and missing relevant citations.  
- Fails to discuss relation to Expectation Propagation (EP) despite deriving an algorithm from Belief Propagation on continuous variables.  
- BP origin of the algorithm is disclosed too late (Section 4.1); this should appear in the abstract/introduction.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (only clustering); lacks evaluation on standard matrix-reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., Netflix/collaborative filtering) to gauge broader impact.  
- Notation suggestion: replace iterative superscripts with assignment arrows and consider dropping/clarifying hats for readability.  
- Question the role and justification of the m scaling in the noise variance (mτ)—need theoretical or practical motivation.  
- Clarify which VBMF variant was used for the VBMF-MA baseline (specify citation/implementation).  
- Supplementary derivation (Eq.14→15) is hard to follow; clarify order-of-magnitude arguments and state which derivation steps are novel vs. adapted from AMP literature.  
- Minor grammatical and phrasing edits recommended to improve polish.  
- Overall: promising and empirically strong work, but requires substantial revisions for clarity, literature positioning, and broader experimental validation before acceptance.
