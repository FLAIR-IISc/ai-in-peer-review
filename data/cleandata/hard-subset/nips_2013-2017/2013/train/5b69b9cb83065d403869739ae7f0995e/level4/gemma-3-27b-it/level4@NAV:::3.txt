This paper introduces an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, and then specializes it to the clustering problem after establishing a connection between the two. The authors validate the algorithm with comparisons to k-means++.

However, the mathematical justifications presented require significant strengthening. Derivations are often incomplete, omitting crucial steps even within the appendix. Specifically:

1) The justification for the O(1/√m) term in line 139 of the Appendix (equation 11) is unclear, and appears to lack consideration of the parameter β, defined in Section 2.
2) The asymptotic analysis, including the derivation of equation (19), appears to implicitly assume a specific relationship between the growth rates of *m* and *n*. The analysis is generally terse and raises concerns regarding its overall mathematical rigor.
3) The claim in line 167 that E[A<sub>il</sub><sup>2</sup>] = mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup> is not adequately explained. Given that A<sub>il</sub> represents observed data, and mτ<sub>w</sub><sup>2</sup> is merely the variance of the likelihood, a stronger rationale for this approximation is needed.
4) The convergence behavior of the AMP algorithm as β approaches infinity, and its relationship to the Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) problem, is unclear. The interchange of limits and minimization – assuming lim<sub>β</sub> min KL = min lim<sub>β</sub> KL – is not justified.
5) The proof of Proposition 1 lacks sufficient detail. The derivation leading to equation (17) is not clear.

Despite being well-structured and generally understandable, the paper’s originality is questionable. The core of the algorithm – estimating the first and second moments of the marginal posterior – closely resembles Expectation Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization, a technique previously demonstrated in the work of Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). The appendix’s update procedures (Section 1) appear consistent with standard EP implementations. Furthermore, a potentially interesting connection exists between the proposed clustering algorithm and the original clutter problem addressed in Minka’s EP paper (http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf), particularly if the likelihood of the clutter problem is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. (Note: this is offered as a potential avenue for further investigation, not a critique of originality). 

Finally, the significance of the proposed algorithm is not fully established. While applied to clustering, the comparison to only k-means++ is insufficient given the extensive existing clustering literature. The lack of rigorous proofs, even in the appendix, hinders a thorough evaluation of the technical correctness and overall impact of this work. It is also worth noting the suspicion that this work may represent a re-application of existing EP techniques to matrix factorization, as presented by Stern et al.
