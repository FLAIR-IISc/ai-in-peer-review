- Introduces a novel AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction under noisy observations using structured factor assumptions to speed up Bayesian inference.  
- Establishes a theoretical and empirical link between low-rank matrix factorization and K-means clustering, building on prior approximation-algorithm results.  
- Frames the AMP method as a scalable alternative to classical k-means/k-means++, demonstrating significant computational advantages.  
- Strong experimental design: evaluates both reconstruction accuracy (Frobenius norm) and clustering quality (assignment metrics) against state-of-the-art baselines.  
- AMP approach addresses a gap in efficient Bayesian inference for structured matrices and is a meaningful technical contribution.  
- K-means application is well-motivated and empirically shows faster convergence and better scalability than k-means/k-means++.  
- Theoretical foundation is weak: the paper lacks strong convergence guarantees and does not adequately contextualize this limitation.  
- Reviewer advises citing recent theoretical computer science work on provable K-means algorithms (e.g., Kumar et al., Awasthi et al.) to clarify trade-offs.  
- Paper clarity is generally good but factor assumptions need more precise specification (e.g., orthogonality, sparsity constraints).  
- Experimental section should include computational-complexity discussion and absolute runtime comparisons to better contextualize reported speedups.  
- Overall assessment: promising empirical contribution with practical value; theoretical gap notable but not fatal for NIPS submission.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept; actionable suggestion—add theoretical context/citations and clarify factor assumptions.
