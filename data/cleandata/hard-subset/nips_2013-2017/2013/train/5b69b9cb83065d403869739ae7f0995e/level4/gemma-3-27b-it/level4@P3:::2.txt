## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering"

This paper presents a new algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction based on an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm derived from belief propagation (BP) within a Bayesian framework. The algorithm extends previous work by handling general rank estimation and maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimation, and demonstrates a novel application to clustering by reformulating it as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem. The authors show their proposed algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means algorithm in certain settings.

**Summary of Strengths:**

*   **Novel Application of AMP:** The paper extends AMP to the low-rank matrix reconstruction problem and provides a unified framework for marginalization and MAP estimation.
*   **Competitive Performance:** The algorithm demonstrates superior performance compared to Lloyd's K-means algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional settings, as shown in the experiments.
*   **Theoretical Foundation:** The algorithm is grounded in a Bayesian framework and leverages theoretical insights from approximate message passing.
*   **Potential for Efficiency:** AMP algorithms are known for their computational efficiency and scalability, making this approach potentially valuable for large-scale problems.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

*   **Evaluation on a Non-Standard Problem:** Evaluating the algorithm primarily on a clustering task (formulated as matrix factorization) makes it difficult to assess its general performance on standard low-rank matrix reconstruction benchmarks. The connection to the broader field of matrix completion and recovery isn't fully established.
*   **Limited Comparison to Existing Methods:** While the paper compares to Variational Bayes Matrix Factorization (VBMF), it lacks comparison to other established low-rank matrix completion algorithms, such as those employing singular value thresholding or alternating minimization techniques. The claim of novelty could be stronger with more thorough comparisons.
*   **Lack of Clarity Regarding Approximations:** The core theoretical foundation relies on approximations valid in the limit of large matrix dimensions (m, N → ∞). The validity and implications of these approximations are not thoroughly investigated or discussed, and the derivations are primarily relegated to the supplementary material.
*   **Insufficient Contextualization of Prior Work:** The paper initially presents clustering as a novel application of matrix factorization, overlooking prior work on this connection. While clarified later, this initial framing creates a misleading impression. The relationship to Expectation Propagation (EP) is not sufficiently explained.
*   **Notational Complexity and Clarity Issues:** The mathematical notation is overly complex and potentially obscures the core ideas. Excessive use of subscripts, superscripts, and symbols hinders readability. The exposition could be significantly improved with more intuitive explanations and a streamlined presentation.

**Detailed Comments:**

The paper's core contribution—extending AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction—is interesting and potentially valuable. However, the paper's impact is diminished by several issues. The evaluation strategy, focusing on clustering, raises concerns about the algorithm’s applicability to more conventional matrix reconstruction problems. A comparison with established methods like those based on singular value thresholding would significantly strengthen the paper.

The theoretical underpinnings, particularly the limiting assumptions and approximations, require more detailed discussion and justification. The supplementary material contains crucial derivations but burying them makes it harder for the reader to assess the algorithm’s validity.

The framing of the work regarding the relationship between matrix factorization and clustering is misleading at first. Acknowledging prior art more explicitly upfront is crucial. Furthermore, the connection to Expectation Propagation (EP), a related technique, deserves more detailed discussion.

The clarity of the presentation is a significant concern. The mathematical notation is overly dense and convoluted, making it difficult to follow the derivations and understand the algorithm’s workings. Simplifying the notation, providing more intuitive explanations, and clarifying the relationships between different equations would substantially improve readability. For example, clearly stating that equation 5 is the negative logarithm of equation 2 would improve understanding.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The algorithm represents a novel extension of AMP to a challenging problem.
*   The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on the chosen task.
*   The paper’s theoretical foundation, though requiring further clarification, is promising.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The evaluation is limited to a specific application, hindering assessment of generalizability.
*   The lack of comparison to a broader range of existing methods weakens the claim of novelty.
*   The clarity of the presentation, particularly the mathematical notation, is a significant drawback.
*   The initial framing of the work regarding prior art is misleading.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a potentially valuable contribution, but significant revisions are needed to address the weaknesses in clarity, contextualization, and evaluation. While the technical content is sound, the presentation hinders its accessibility and impact. I recommend **rejection** in its current form, with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing the concerns outlined above. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Evaluate the algorithm on standard low-rank matrix completion benchmarks.
2.  Provide a more thorough comparison to existing methods.
3.  Elaborate on the implications and validity of the limiting assumptions.
4.  Acknowledge and properly contextualize prior work.
5.  Significantly improve the clarity and readability of the presentation, simplifying the notation and providing more intuitive explanations.
