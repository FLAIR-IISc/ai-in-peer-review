The paper proposes an algorithm for reconstructing low-rank matrices from noisy observations. It assumes additional structure on the latent factors and uses an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) scheme to accelerate the otherwise costly Bayesian approach. The work also connects this reconstruction framework to k-means clustering, providing a practical application domain for the proposed methods.

To my knowledge, applying AMP to matrix reconstruction in this setting is novel and technically interesting. While the connections between low-rank factorizations and k-means are well known (e.g., PCA offers a 2-approximation for k-means), leveraging this link enables a clear and relevant empirical study. The experiments compare the proposed methods against k-means and k-means++, and the reported results indicate favorable speed and efficiency. The evaluation includes both Frobenius-norm reconstruction error and clustering quality, which strengthens the empirical case.

The main weakness is the lack of strong theoretical guarantees, particularly regarding convergence and optimality. Although this is common in the k-means literature, the paper would benefit from a broader discussion and citations to theoretical computer science work that provides provable guarantees for the k-means objective. Even partial analysis—such as conditions under which AMP converges or bounds on reconstruction error—would substantially improve the contribution.

Overall, this is a solid paper: it introduces an interesting AMP-based approach for low-rank matrix reconstruction and its application to k-means, with promising empirical results. The theoretical development is comparatively weak, but the practical performance and clarity of the evaluation make the work compelling.
