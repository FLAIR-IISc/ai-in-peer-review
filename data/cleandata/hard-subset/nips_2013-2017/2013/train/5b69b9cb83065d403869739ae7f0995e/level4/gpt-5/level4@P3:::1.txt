Summary and relation to prior work
This paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured priors on the factors and proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm that handles both posterior inference and MAP estimation via a temperature parameter β. The method generalizes prior AMP formulations for bilinear models: it extends the rank-one IterFac algorithm and earlier general-rank AMP that focused on posterior means by enabling MAP and marginal-MAP estimators for broad prior families, including discrete constraints. A second contribution is a reformulation of K-means clustering as a low-rank reconstruction problem with a discrete prior on assignments, yielding an AMP-based algorithm for K-means (and a β=1 “maximum-accuracy” variant) with O(mN) cost per iteration and Onsager-like correction terms. A fixed-point proposition shows block-wise optimality at convergence.

The work is well positioned within the AMP literature (Donoho et al. 2009; Rangan 2011; Bayati & Montanari 2011), relates to IterFac (Rangan & Fletcher 2012), and acknowledges contemporaneous bilinear AMP and dictionary learning works (Parker et al. 2013; Krzakala et al. 2013). The clustering connection builds on known links between MF and K-means (and compares to Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++), while variational Bayes MF provides a deterministic baseline (Lim & Teh 2007; Raiko et al. 2007).

Quality
- Technical soundness: The derivation follows the standard AMP/BP-to-AMP reduction with a temperature parameter to interpolate between marginalization (β=1) and MAP (β→∞). The specialization to discrete priors for clustering is elegant and leads to interpretable correction terms akin to leave-one-out debiasing and size regularization. The fixed-point proposition (block-wise global minima given PSD conditions) is valuable, though weaker than global convergence guarantees.
- Empirical support: Synthetic experiments (m=800, N=1600) compare K-means loss and clustering accuracy across K-means, K-means++, VBMF, and two AMP variants, showing consistent improvements by AMP, especially in higher ranks. On ORL faces, AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in most runs when seeded identically, with modest iteration counts. However, wall-clock runtimes are not reported, oscillations are noted but not systematically addressed, and sensitivity to initialization, β, and τ-estimation is only briefly discussed.
- Limitations: There is no state evolution analysis or convergence proof for the proposed general-rank scheme; the paper defers state evolution to the supplement. The PSD conditions in the proposition are plausible but unverified in finite samples. Robustness to model mismatch (non-Gaussian noise, misspecified r̂) and sensitivity to priors are not systematically explored. Comparisons omit standard Gaussian mixture EM, spectral clustering, or PCA+K-means baselines, which are natural for isotropic Gaussian clusters.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized. The algorithmic presentation is precise, and the β-trick unifies inference and optimization cleanly. A few suggestions:
- Provide more guidance on computing f and G for common priors (e.g., sparse, nonnegative), including closed forms or efficient numerical routines.
- Elaborate on τ estimation and its stability; clarify whether damping or step-size control is used to mitigate oscillations.
- Report runtime in addition to iteration counts, since per-iteration costs differ between methods.
- Clean up several reference formatting issues and ensure comprehensive related work coverage in clustering.

Originality
The extension of general-rank AMP to encompass MAP and marginal-MAP with structured (including discrete) priors is novel and useful. The specialized AMP for K-means that directly enforces one-hot assignments within the AMP framework, along with interpretable correction terms, appears new compared to prior MF-based clustering approaches. The paper situates itself appropriately with respect to IterFac, variational MF, and contemporaneous bilinear AMP papers; further clarifying differences in assumptions (e.g., fixed-r vs. proportional limits, dependence within rows) would strengthen the contribution.

Significance
If validated more broadly, the proposed AMP formulations could become practical tools for large-scale Bayesian matrix factorization with structure, and the AMP-KM variant is a promising alternative to Lloyd’s K-means in high-dimensional regimes. The lack of theoretical convergence guarantees and limited empirical breadth temper the immediate impact, but the approach has clear potential and is aligned with NIPS interests in scalable inference.

Pros
- Conceptually clean AMP framework that unifies posterior mean, marginal-MAP, and MAP for bilinear models via β.
- Novel and practical AMP instantiation for K-means with interpretable debiasing/size-correction terms.
- O(mN) per-iteration complexity and favorable empirical results on synthetic and real data.
- Useful fixed-point optimality proposition.

Cons
- No state evolution analysis or convergence guarantees for the presented general-rank algorithms; oscillations observed.
- Limited comparisons and sensitivity studies (initialization, β, τ, misspecified rank, noise models).
- No runtime reporting; iteration counts may not reflect actual efficiency across methods.
- Clustering baselines do not include EM for GMMs or spectral methods; theory side omits connections to provable K-means algorithms.

Suggestions for improvement
- Include a brief state evolution derivation (even in supplement) or empirical calibration showing prediction accuracy; discuss damping and convergence diagnostics.
- Add baselines such as Gaussian mixture EM (spherical/isotropic), PCA+K-means, and spectral clustering; explore robustness to r̂ ≠ r and non-Gaussian noise.
- Report wall-clock time and memory usage; provide ablations on τ estimation and initialization.
- Expand related theoretical work in clustering and K-means (e.g., Ostrovsky et al. 2006; Awasthi and co-authors; PTAS/approximation and seeding analyses) to contextualize the optimization landscape and initialization sensitivity.

Overall assessment
A solid and timely paper that extends AMP to structured low-rank reconstruction and delivers a compelling AMP-based K-means algorithm with promising empirical performance. While the theoretical guarantees are limited and the experimental scope could be broadened, the contribution is original and relevant to the NIPS community. I lean toward acceptance, contingent on improved discussion of convergence, expanded baselines, and clearer practical guidance.
