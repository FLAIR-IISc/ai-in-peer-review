This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction within a Bayesian framework. The authors extend existing AMP approaches to handle general rank cases and demonstrate its application to K-means clustering, formulating the latter as a special case of the reconstruction problem. Numerical experiments demonstrate that the proposed algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in high-dimensional settings.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically complex algorithm, and while the overall approach is sound, the mathematical derivations lack sufficient detail and rigor, particularly in the supplementary material. Several points require clarification. First, the justification for approximations made in the appendix (e.g., line 139 regarding the O(1/√m) term) is not fully explained, and the role of the β parameter introduced in Section 2 needs further elaboration. Second, the asymptotic analysis relies on assumptions regarding the scaling of m and N; a more detailed discussion of these assumptions and their impact on the results would be beneficial. Third, the connection between the observed data variance and the noise parameter (line 167) requires stronger justification. Fourth, the convergence argument for the algorithm as β approaches infinity—essential for MAP estimation—is not fully convincing; simply minimizing the KL divergence does not guarantee the equivalence of limits. Finally, the proof of Proposition 1 feels abbreviated, and the derivation of equation (17) is unclear. Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the technical contribution.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured, making the core ideas accessible. The motivation for using an AMP approach and its connection to clustering are clearly articulated. However, the density of mathematical notation and the lack of detailed explanations in the appendix hinder full comprehension. More descriptive variable names and additional intermediate steps in the derivations would improve clarity.

**Originality:** The paper builds upon existing work in AMP and Bayesian matrix factorization. While the extension of the AMP algorithm to handle MAP estimation is a contribution, the core technique bears a strong resemblance to Expectation Propagation (EP). The authors should explicitly address this connection and differentiate their work from existing EP-based approaches for matrix factorization, such as the work by Stern et al. (http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf). Furthermore, the authors mention a potential link to the clutter problem within the EP framework, which is an interesting observation that could be explored further. A thorough discussion of related work, including the nuances of applying EP versus AMP, would enhance the paper’s originality assessment.

**Significance:** The application of the proposed algorithm to clustering is a practical and potentially valuable direction. However, comparing performance solely to K-means++ may not be sufficient to demonstrate the algorithm’s broader significance. Given the extensive literature on clustering algorithms, a comparison to more sophisticated techniques (e.g., spectral clustering, Gaussian mixture models) would provide a more comprehensive evaluation. Furthermore, the reliance on somewhat sketchy proofs raises concerns about the robustness and scalability of the proposed method. A clearer validation of the algorithm’s theoretical foundations would be crucial for establishing its long-term impact.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel extension of the AMP algorithm to low-rank matrix reconstruction with MAP estimation.
*   The reformulation of clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem offers a potentially interesting perspective.
*   Empirical results demonstrate promising performance, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The mathematical derivations lack sufficient detail and rigor.
*   The connection to existing Expectation Propagation methods needs further clarification.
*   The significance of the work could be strengthened by a more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art clustering algorithms.
*   The lack of robust theoretical guarantees hinders a full evaluation of the algorithm's properties.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. However, the lack of mathematical rigor and the potential overlap with existing EP-based approaches raise significant concerns. I recommend **rejection** in its current form. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by providing more detailed derivations, clarifying the connection to existing literature, and strengthening the theoretical justification for their approach. A revised version addressing these issues would be a strong candidate for acceptance.
