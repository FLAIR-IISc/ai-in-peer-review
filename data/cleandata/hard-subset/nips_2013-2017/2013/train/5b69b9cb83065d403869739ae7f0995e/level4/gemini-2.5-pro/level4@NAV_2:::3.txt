This paper introduces an algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction based on Approximate Message Passing (AMP). The problem is formulated within a Bayesian framework, which allows for the flexible incorporation of structural priors on the factor matrices, such as sparsity. The authors derive the AMP algorithm from belief propagation and show how it can be used to solve both marginalization problems (e.g., computing posterior means) and MAP estimation problems. As a primary application, the paper recasts K-means clustering as a specific instance of low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural constraints. The resulting AMP-based clustering algorithm is then evaluated experimentally, demonstrating superior performance over Lloyd‚Äôs K-means algorithm on synthetic and real-world data.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Application:** The application of the AMP framework to K-means-type clustering is novel and interesting. The reformulation that directly handles the constraint of assigning each datum to exactly one cluster within the low-rank reconstruction framework is a notable contribution.
*   **Framework Flexibility:** The proposed Bayesian AMP framework is general and flexible. By allowing for different choices of prior distributions `pŐāu` and `pŐāv`, it can be adapted to a wide variety of problems where low-rank matrices possess additional structural properties.
*   **Promising Empirical Results:** The numerical experiments, though limited in scope, show that the proposed algorithm (AMP-KM) outperforms standard K-means and K-means++ in terms of both K-means loss and clustering accuracy on the tested datasets. The improved performance in high-dimensional settings is particularly noteworthy.
*   **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The motivation, problem setting, and proposed algorithm are presented in a logical sequence that is easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Technical Rigor and Completeness:** The mathematical derivations throughout the paper and appendix are overly condensed, omitting many non-trivial steps that are crucial for verifying the technical correctness of the claims. This lack of detail makes the paper difficult to fully evaluate.

*   **Originality and Relation to Prior Work:** The connection between the proposed algorithm and Expectation Propagation (EP) is not discussed. The update equations appear very similar to standard EP updates applied to this model. Given that EP has previously been used for matrix factorization (e.g., Stern et al., "Matchbox: large scale online Bayesian recommendations," WWW 2009), the authors should explicitly clarify the novelty of their AMP-based approach and differentiate it from this existing work. The core contribution may be an application of a known technique rather than a new algorithmic development.

*   **Significance of Experimental Evaluation:** While the results are positive, the experimental evaluation is narrow. The primary application is clustering, yet the comparison is limited to K-means and K-means++. Given the vast literature on clustering, demonstrating superiority over only this baseline is insufficient to establish the practical significance of the proposed method. A comparison against a broader set of modern clustering algorithms is needed to make a convincing case.
