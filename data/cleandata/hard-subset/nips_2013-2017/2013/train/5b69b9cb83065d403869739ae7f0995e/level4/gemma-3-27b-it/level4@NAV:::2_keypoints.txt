- Evaluation is on a non-standard task (clustering as matrix factorization), making it hard to judge performance on traditional matrix reconstruction.
- Paper should compare on standard datasets (e.g., Netflix) or clearly justify why such datasets are infeasible.
- The algorithm is largely a direct application of BP/variational message-passing and not a major paradigm shift.
- The key approximations in the N→∞, m→∞ limits are interesting but currently untested and need validation.
- Prior work and the known connection between matrix factorization and clustering are not presented or cited clearly.
- Expectation Propagation (EP) and its relation to the presented method are not cited or discussed and should be included.
- The paper delays stating the core BP nature of the algorithm until Section 4.1; this should appear in the abstract.
- Experimental framing against Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF is novel but may not convincingly show broad competitiveness without standard benchmarks.
- Mathematics and derivations are relegated to the Supplementary Material; an outline should appear in the main text.
- The mathematical presentation is hard to parse, with confusing notation (excessive hats, tildes, subscripts, superscripts).
- Specific equations (e.g., 9a–9e, 5, 8, 10) would benefit from algebraic simplification and clearer explanation of probabilistic meaning.
- Notation suggestions: remove unnecessary hats, drop β subscripts where redundant, and clarify variable naming (e.g., τw vs τ).
- Algorithm 2 shows near row-column symmetry; the paper should point this out and simplify presentation accordingly.
- Important algorithmic details/questions are unaddressed, e.g., the role of the m factor in additive noise variance and its effect on symmetry.
- Minor stylistic/clarity issues: tilde usage for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization (N vs m), and awkward English in places.
- Supplementary Material has unclear steps (e.g., equation 14→15 big-O argument) and needs more explanation and context.
- More diagrams (like Figure 1) would aid understanding of the derivation and algorithm.
- Overall: the method is interesting and promising and shows experimental advantage, but the paper requires substantial improvements in clarity, validation, and scholarly rigor before acceptance.
