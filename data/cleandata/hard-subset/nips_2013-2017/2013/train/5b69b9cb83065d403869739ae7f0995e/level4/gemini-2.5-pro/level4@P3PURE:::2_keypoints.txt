- Introduces a novel BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, one of the first BP applications to this problem.  
- Reframes clustering as a matrix reconstruction evaluation and shows the proposed method outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in that setting.  
- Paper is generally well written and the mathematical development appears sound and in principle replicable.  
- Main evaluation is on a non-standard clustering task, lacking comparisons on conventional benchmarks (e.g., collaborative filtering/Netflix) used in VBMF papers, making performance hard to situate.  
- Novelty is incremental: mainly a direct application of BP vs. prior variational message-passing methods; the large-matrix approximations are the most interesting part but are not directly validated.  
- Prior work and positioning are insufficiently cited: the matrix factorization–clustering connection is presented as novel, Expectation Propagation and other relevant literature are not properly discussed, and BP’s central role is not stated early.  
- Technical derivation is relegated to the supplement; the main paper needs a high-level derivation outline to assess correctness.  
- Notation is cumbersome and redundant (excess hats/tildes/subscripts, unnecessary time superscripts, factorizable common terms) and should be simplified.  
- Exposition lacks intuition and explanations for key steps (e.g., relation between eqns, meanings of f_β and G_β, symmetry of Algorithm 2).  
- Inconsistent use of β subscripts on messages/functions; suggest treating β as global or being consistent.  
- Unanswered modeling choices: role of factor m in noise variance, use of tilde for column vectors, and inconsistent capitalization of N vs m need clarification.  
- Supplementary derivations (big-O steps) are hard to follow and should be clarified in the supplement or main text.  
- Missing implementation citation: need a specific reference for the VBMF-MA implementation used in experiments.  
- Recommend moving helpful supplementary figures into the main paper and adding more diagrams to aid understanding.  
- Multiple minor grammatical errors and language phrasing issues throughout the paper that should be corrected.  
- Overall conclusion: interesting and potentially valuable work but requires significant revisions (benchmark comparisons, proper citations/positioning, clearer technical exposition) before acceptance.
