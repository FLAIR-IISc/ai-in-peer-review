- Introduces a BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction with two large-dimension approximations described in the supplement.  
- Evaluates the method by casting clustering as matrix reconstruction and comparing to Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF.  
- Main experimental concern: evaluation on a non-standard clustering-as-factorization task makes results hard to extrapolate to standard benchmarks (e.g., Netflix), and no justification for omitting such datasets is given.  
- Algorithmic novelty is modest: a relatively straightforward application of BP/EP-like message passing to a problem previously addressed by variational methods.  
- The large-dimension approximations are the most interesting part but their validity is neither tested nor clearly explained in the main text.  
- Serious citation and scholarship issues: the clustering↔matrix-factorization connection is presented as novel despite prior work, and Expectation Propagation (EP) is not cited or related to the method.  
- Belief Propagation is not mentioned early enough (should appear in abstract) and the relationship to prior message-passing work is unclear.  
- Positives: engaging paper, understandable mathematics, and experiments that show clear benefits of the new algorithm on the chosen task.  
- Major clarity issue: full derivation relegated to supplementary material—key steps should appear in the main paper.  
- Notation and mathematical presentation are overly complex and confusing (excessive hats/tildes/superscripts, unnecessary t superscripts, awkward factors like 1/mτ).  
- Exposition could be improved by explicitly connecting equations (e.g., eq.5 = -log eq.2, eq.8 = eq.2^β), highlighting row-column symmetry, and defining functions in eq.10 near their usage.  
- Supplementary-material problems: unclear asymptotic steps (e.g., eq.14→15 big-O of m), insufficient explanation of approximation technique and its relation to prior work.  
- Open technical questions: why additive noise variance includes factor m (breaks symmetry), confusing use of tilde for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization of N vs. m.  
- Missing or unclear references: cite the primary implementation reference for the competing VBMF-MA algorithm and resolve which variational papers are intended.  
- Minor language issues throughout main text and supplement; Figure 1 in supplement is useful and more diagrams would help.  
- Overall judgment: mathematically interesting and worth publishing as an initial BP application to matrix factorization, but requires significant revisions on clarity, citations, experiments, and notation before acceptance.
