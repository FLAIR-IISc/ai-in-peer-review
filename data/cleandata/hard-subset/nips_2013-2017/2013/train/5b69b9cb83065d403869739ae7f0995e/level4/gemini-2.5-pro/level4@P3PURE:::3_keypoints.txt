- Proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, framed as a generalization of clustering and compared experimentally to k-means++.

- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, raising concerns about rigor.

- Appendix: unclear justification that the second term of Eq.(11) is O(1/√m); β seems omitted from Section 2 discussion.

- Asymptotic analysis (including derivation of Eq.(19)) is terse and appears to implicitly assume m and n grow at the same rate without stating assumptions or detailed proofs.

- Questionable approximation on line 167 where E[A_{il}^2] is replaced by mτ_w^2 despite A being observed data; this rationale needs clarification.

- Incomplete argument for convergence to the MAP solution as β→∞; interchange of limit and minimization for KL divergence is not justified.

- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and the derivation of Eq.(17) is unexplained.

- Paper is well-structured and central claims are presented clearly.

- Originality is dubious: method appears to estimate first/second posterior moments like Expectation-Propagation (EP) and resembles prior work (Stern et al., 2009).

- Appendix update steps look like standard EP updates; authors should clarify differences from existing EP-based matrix factorization.

- Noted potential connection to Minka's EP "clutter problem" (replace clutter likelihood with uniform Gaussian mixture) as an interesting observation.

- Significance is limited by narrow experimental comparison (only k-means++), sketchy proofs that hinder correctness assessment, and possible lack of novelty relative to prior EP work.
