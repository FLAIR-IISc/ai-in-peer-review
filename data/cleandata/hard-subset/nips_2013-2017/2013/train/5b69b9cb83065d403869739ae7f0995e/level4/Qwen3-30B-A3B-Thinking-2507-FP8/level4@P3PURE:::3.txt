

This paper proposes an approximate message passing algorithm for matrix factorization, demonstrating that the matrix factorization problem generalizes the clustering problem. The derived algorithm is then specialized for clustering, and experiments compare its performance with k-means++.  

**Quality:** The mathematical derivations are excessively sketchy, omitting non-trivial steps even in the appendix.  
1) In Appendix line 139, why is the second term of (11) $O(1/\sqrt{m})$? The parameter $\beta$ appears to be omitted in Section 2.  
2) Analysis in Section 3 (including derivation of (19)) seems to assume $m$ and $n$ grow at the same rate. The asymptotic analysis is terse, raising concerns about mathematical rigor.  
3) In line 167, why is $m\tau_w^2$ the expectation of $A_{il}^2$? $A$ represents the observed data, and $m\tau_w^2$ is only the variance of the likelihood; the justification for this approximation requires verification.  
4) How does the message passing algorithm for finite $\beta$ converge to the MAP problem? Message passing minimizes KL divergence, but $\lim_{\beta \to \infty} \min \text{KL} \neq \min \lim_{\beta \to \infty} \text{KL}$ in general.  
5) The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief. How is (17) derived?  

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly conveys its main contribution.  

**Originality:** Since the authors focus primarily on estimating first and second moments of the marginal posterior, the entire algorithm appears to be an application of Expectation-Propagation (EP) to matrix factorization. EP was previously applied to matrix factorization in Stern et al. (2009): http://research.microsoft.com/pubs/79460/www09.pdf. Section 1 of the appendix describes standard EP update procedures; please clarify if this is accurate. Additionally, a potential connection exists between the proposed clustering algorithm and the clutter problem in Minka (2001): http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/minka/papers/ep/minka-ep-uai.pdf, if the clutter problem's likelihood is replaced with a uniform mixture of Gaussian distributions. This note is offered to clarify potential connections, not to challenge originality.  

**Significance:** The focus on clustering applications is limited; comparing solely to k-means++ is insufficient to establish practical utility given the extensive clustering literature. The proofs, even in the appendix, are too sketchy to evaluate technical correctness. The algorithm may simply be an application of EP to matrix factorization, as demonstrated by Stern et al. (2009).
