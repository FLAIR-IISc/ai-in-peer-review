- Presents an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for noisy low-rank matrix reconstruction with structural assumptions on factors.  
- Novel application of AMP to matrix reconstruction, which is interesting and to the reviewer’s knowledge new.  
- Establishes a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, offering an application domain.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation comparing the method to k-means and k-means++, showing faster and more efficient performance.  
- Evaluation reports both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering quality, strengthening empirical claims.  
- Main weakness: limited theoretical guarantees on algorithm convergence.  
- Suggests adding citations to Theoretical Computer Science work that gives provable guarantees for K-means to bolster theoretical grounding.  
- Overall assessment: solid and promising work with strong experiments but could improve theoretical support.
