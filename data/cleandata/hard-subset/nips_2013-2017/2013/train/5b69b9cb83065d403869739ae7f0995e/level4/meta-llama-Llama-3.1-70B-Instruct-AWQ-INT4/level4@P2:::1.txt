The authors present an algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction under noisy observations, leveraging an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to speed up the computationally expensive Bayesian method. The algorithm incorporates additional structural properties of the factors, enhancing its flexibility. Furthermore, the authors establish a connection between matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering, providing a novel application domain for their proposed algorithms.

The AMP approach for matrix reconstruction is novel and interesting, and its application to K-means clustering allows for a comprehensive experimental evaluation. The authors' comparison of their algorithms to k-means and k-means++ demonstrates the efficiency and effectiveness of their approach, with faster convergence and better performance in terms of the Frobenius norm residual and clustering accuracy.

The paper's main weakness lies in the limited theoretical guarantees provided for the algorithm's convergence. While this is not uncommon in K-means algorithms, the authors could strengthen their contribution by citing relevant papers in Theoretical Computer Science that offer provably accurate algorithms for the K-means objective function.

Overall, the paper presents a solid contribution to the field, with a promising experimental evaluation and a novel application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction and K-means clustering. However, the authors should consider addressing the theoretical limitations to further enhance the paper's impact.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
