- Evaluation uses clustering framed as matrix reconstruction, which is non-standard and limits extrapolation to typical matrix reconstruction tasks.
- Paper should evaluate on common benchmarks (e.g., Netflix) or explicitly justify why they are infeasible.
- The algorithm is essentially a direct application of Belief Propagation to a known problem, so the novelty is incremental rather than groundbreaking.
- Interesting approximations are introduced in the limits N→∞ and m→∞, but their validity is not tested or clearly explained.
- Key derivation moved to Supplementary Material; the main paper should outline the derivation for clarity.
- Important prior work and the known link between matrix factorization and clustering are not adequately cited or acknowledged.
- Expectation Propagation (EP) literature and its relation to this algorithm are not cited or discussed; BP should be highlighted earlier (e.g., abstract).
- Experimental results suggest benefits over Lloyd’s K-means and VBMF, but the unconventional evaluation criterion raises questions about competitiveness.
- Mathematical presentation and notation are overly cumbersome (excessive hats/tildes/subscripts, confusing τw), harming readability.
- Several equations are unnecessarily complex or could be simplified/combined for clarity (e.g., repeated 1/mτw factors).
- Section 4’s notation and message labeling are inconsistent; a clear statement about dropping hats/superscripts would help.
- Definitions of functions (e.g., fβ, Gβ) and roles of β and m should be introduced when first used and explained more intuitively.
- Figures and plots are sometimes hard to interpret in grayscale; more descriptive diagrams (like Figure 1 SM) would help.
- Supplementary Material has unclear steps (e.g., eqn transitions, big-O handling) and lacks context for referenced techniques.
- English and title could be improved; a more descriptive title (e.g., including "approximate message passing") is recommended.
- Overall: algorithm is promising and merits publication as a first BP application to matrix factorization but requires substantial revisions for clarity, citations, and rigorous evaluation.
