- Introduces an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization and a specialization to clustering, with experiments comparing to k-means++.
- Mathematical derivations throughout (including appendix) lack sufficient detail, omitting non-trivial steps and hindering verification.
- Appendix, line 139: unjustified claim that the second term in Eq.(11) is O(1/√m); the role of β is unclear.
- Asymptotic analysis (derivation of Eq.(19)) is terse and appears to assume m and n grow at comparable rates without justification.
- Line 167: claiming m τ_w^2 equals E[A_il^2] is unjustified because A is observed data; the approximation needs justification.
- Unclear how the finite-β message passing converges to the MAP solution; the interchange of limit and minimization of KL is not justified.
- Proof of Proposition 1 is insufficiently detailed; derivation of Eq.(17) is not adequately explained.
- Paper is well-structured and central ideas are readily accessible.
- Approach seems to mainly estimate first and second posterior moments, raising the question of whether it is essentially Expectation Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization.
- Authors should clarify relation to prior EP work (e.g., Stern et al.) and whether their updates are standard EP updates.
- Potential connection to the EP “clutter” problem (with uniform Gaussian mixtures) is noted as an interesting avenue.
- Comparison only to k-means++ is likely insufficient given clustering literature; lack of detailed proofs and overlap with EP methods raises concerns about novelty and practical significance.
