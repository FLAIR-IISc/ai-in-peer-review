- Mathematical derivations are sketchy and omit non-trivial steps, making technical verification difficult.  
- Asymptotic analysis (Section 3) is terse; derivation of Eq. (19) seems to assume m and n grow at the same rate without stating assumptions.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 is too brief and the derivation of Eq. (17) is unexplained.  
- Justification for key approximations is weak (e.g., approximating E[A_{il}^2] by mτ_w^2 lacks rationale).  
- The claim that the algorithm converges to the MAP solution as β → ∞ is unsubstantiated and requires a formal proof about limits and KL divergence.  
- Questionable originality: the algorithm appears to be a direct application of Expectation Propagation (EP), overlapping with prior work like Stern et al. (2009).  
- Authors should explicitly clarify the relationship to EP and differentiate their contribution from previous EP-based matrix factorization.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: only compared to k-means++; needs benchmarking against a wider range of modern clustering methods and datasets.  
- Request: provide detailed derivation showing the second term of Eq. (11) is O(1/√m).  
- Request: clarify the role of parameter β, which is introduced in the appendix but missing from Section 2.  
- Request: show intermediate steps for deriving Eq. (17) in Proposition 1.  
- Request: provide a formal proof that the message passing algorithm converges to the MAP as β → ∞.  
- Request: explain key differences between the proposed method and Stern et al. (2009) EP-based matrix factorization.  
- Positive: paper is well-structured and communicates high-level ideas clearly; the matrix factorization–clustering connection is insightful.  
- Suggestion: discuss potential connection to Minka’s EP "clutter problem" to better contextualize the work.  
- Conclusion: contribution is undermined by concerns about technical correctness, novelty, and experiments; cannot recommend acceptance without substantial revisions.
