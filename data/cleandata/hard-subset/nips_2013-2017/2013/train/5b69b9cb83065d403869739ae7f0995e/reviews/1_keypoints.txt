- Presents an algorithm for matrix reconstruction from noisy observations focused on low-rank matrices with additional factor assumptions.  
- Uses an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) approach to accelerate the traditional computationally expensive Bayesian method.  
- Connects low-rank matrix reconstruction to K-means clustering as an application domain.  
- AMP for matrix reconstruction appears novel and interesting.  
- Notes that connections between low-rank factorizations and K-means are well-known (e.g., PCA gives a 2-approximation).  
- Provides a solid experimental evaluation comparing the proposed method to k-means and k-means++.  
- Empirical results suggest the proposed approach is faster and more efficient than k-means and k-means++.  
- Evaluations include both Frobenius norm residuals and actual clustering quality, which is commendable.  
- Main weakness: the algorithm lacks strong theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Suggestion: cite more theoretical computer science work that gives provable guarantees for K-means.  
- Overall assessment: solid AMP-based contribution with promising experiments but somewhat weak theoretical results.
