- Paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for matrix factorization, specializes it to clustering, and compares it experimentally to k-means++.

- Quality concern: mathematical derivations are very sketchy and omit many non-trivial steps, even in the appendix.

- Q1: In Appendix line 139, the reviewer questions why the second term of (11) is O(1/√m) and notes β appears omitted in Section 2.

- Q2: The asymptotic analysis (including derivation of (19)) seems to assume m and n grow at the same order; the reviewer finds these arguments terse and questions their rigor.

- Q3: In line 167, the reviewer disputes the claim that m τ_w^2 equals E[A_{il}^2], noting A is observed data and m τ_w^2 would only be the likelihood variance — the approximation needs justification.

- Q4: The reviewer asks how the message passing algorithm for finite β converges to the MAP solution, pointing out that exchanging limit and minimization of KL is not necessarily valid.

- Q5: The proof of Proposition 1 is too brief; specifically, the reviewer asks how equation (17) was derived.

- Clarity: the paper is well-structured and the main point is easy to grasp.

- Originality concern: the reviewer suggests the algorithm may essentially be Expectation-Propagation (EP) applied to matrix factorization, pointing to Stern et al. (WWW 2009) and standard EP updates in the appendix.

- Additional originality note: reviewer suggests a potential connection between the proposed clustering algorithm and the EP “clutter” problem (Minka), but frames it as a suggestion rather than an attack.

- Significance concern: focusing only on comparison with k-means++ is insufficient given clustering literature; sketchy proofs and possible overlap with prior EP-based work reduce confidence in practical and technical contribution.
