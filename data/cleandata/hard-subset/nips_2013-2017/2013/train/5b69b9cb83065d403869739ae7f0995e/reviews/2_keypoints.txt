- Proposes a new BP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction using approximations assuming large m and N; full derivation is in the Supplementary Material.  
- Evaluated by framing clustering as matrix reconstruction and compared to Lloyd's K-means and Variational Bayes Matrix Factorization (VBMF).  
- Concern: evaluation on a non-standard task (clustering-as-factorization) makes it hard to judge performance on conventional matrix-reconstruction datasets (e.g., Netflix); authors should use standard datasets or justify not doing so.  
- Algorithm appears to be a fairly straightforward application of BP to a problem previously addressed with Variational Message Passing, so novelty is limited.  
- The asymptotic approximations (N→∞, m→∞) are interesting but their validity is not tested or clearly justified in the main text.  
- Poor citation/scholarship: the paper implies novelty of matrix factorization for clustering without citing prior work or stating the connection is well-known.  
- EP (Expectation Propagation) and the relation to applying BP to continuous distributions are not cited or discussed; the BP nature of the algorithm should be stated earlier (e.g., in the abstract).  
- Paper is interesting and potentially useful; mathematics are generally followable and algorithm shows empirical benefits in experiments.  
- Using K-means as a comparison is reasonable but the novel evaluation criterion still raises questions about competitiveness against established benchmarks.  
- Derivation relegated to supplementary material is a drawback; key derivation steps should appear or be summarized in the main paper.  
- Mathematical presentation is hard to parse, with specific confusing equations (9a,9b,9d,9e) and unexplained factors.  
- Notational clutter (excessive hats, tildes, subscripts/superscripts, unnecessary time indices) makes the algorithm harder to read; recommend simplifying notation and stating conventions.  
- Exposition misses obvious simplifications and connections (e.g., eq.5 = negative log of eq.2; eq.8 = eq.2^β; symmetry in Algorithm 2) that would aid readability.  
- Definitions and meanings of functions (e.g., fβ, Gβ) are not clearly explained near first use and should be clarified.  
- Inconsistent use of β subscripts and other dependencies; β is a global parameter and notation should reflect that or be simplified.  
- Unclear role and significance of the m factor in the additive noise variance and its effect on row-column symmetry; authors should explain.  
- Minor notation/style questions: use of tilde for column vectors, inconsistent capitalization of N versus m.  
- Need a clear citation for the VBMF-MA implementation used as a baseline—unclear which variational reference was followed.  
- Supplementary material has clarity issues (e.g., suggestion to show product of exponentials, unclear step from eq.14 to 15, and unclear relation of approximations to prior literature).  
- English and phrasing need polishing throughout; title and many sentences could be improved for clarity.  
- Visuals: Figure 1 in the supplement is helpful; adding more diagrams would improve understanding.  
- Overall: the algorithm is promising and publishable, but the paper currently lacks sufficient clarity, validation of approximations, and scholarship to be accepted as-is.
