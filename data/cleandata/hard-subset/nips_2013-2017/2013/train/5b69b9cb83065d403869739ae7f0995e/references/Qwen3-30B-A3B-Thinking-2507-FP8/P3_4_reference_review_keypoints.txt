- Summary: Proposes an AMP algorithm extending AMP to Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction including MAP estimation and applies it to K-means-style clustering with hard assignment constraints.

- Strength: Theoretical grounding — derives a β-parameterized posterior AMP that unifies marginalization and MAP estimation.

- Strength: Computational efficiency — algorithm runs in O(mN) per iteration with modest extra cost over variational Bayes.

- Strength: Practical application — presents an AMP-based clustering algorithm (Algorithm 3) that is well-motivated and empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in experiments.

- Strength: Theoretical insight — Proposition 1 shows any AMP fixed point is also an ICM fixed point, suggesting potential to avoid local minima.

- Weakness: Incremental novelty — unclear how substantial the MAP extension is beyond prior AMP work (e.g., [9]) and needs clearer articulation of novelty.

- Weakness: Theoretical limitations — lacks detailed state-evolution/convergence analysis and insufficient justification for the O(1) iteration claim.

- Weakness: Overstated clustering claim — the “first” to handle one-cluster-per-datum constraint is questionable given prior NMF and convex NMF clustering work.

- Weakness: Experimental limitations — comparisons are limited (mainly Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++); should include stronger, more recent clustering baselines.

- Overall assessment: Solid, technically sound incremental contribution to AMP for matrix factorization with promising empirical results but needs clearer novelty positioning and deeper theoretical analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Suggestion: Clarify relationship to [9] and explicitly state what is novel in the MAP extension.

- Suggestion: Re-evaluate and revise the “first” clustering claim, citing relevant NMF/convex NMF work.

- Suggestion: Provide more state-evolution or convergence detail to support the iteration complexity claim.

- Suggestion: Expand experiments to compare against more recent/state-of-the-art clustering methods (e.g., deep clustering).
