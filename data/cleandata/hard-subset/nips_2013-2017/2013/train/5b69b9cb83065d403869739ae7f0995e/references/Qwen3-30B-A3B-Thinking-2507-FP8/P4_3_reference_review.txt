

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with applications to clustering. The authors extend existing AMP frameworks to handle both marginalization and MAP problems in a unified manner, addressing the limitations of previous approaches that could only treat posterior mean estimation. The key innovation lies in developing an AMP-based algorithm that can handle various structural constraints on matrix factors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity) while providing theoretical guarantees for the MAP problem. The authors then apply their algorithm to K-means clustering, demonstrating that their approach directly handles the constraint that each datum should be assigned to exactly one cluster—a limitation of prior low-rank reconstruction-based clustering methods.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality. The authors provide a clear mathematical formulation of the low-rank matrix reconstruction problem, derive their AMP algorithm with careful justification, and prove a key proposition (Proposition 1) showing that fixed points of their algorithm correspond to local minima of the objective function. The theoretical analysis is sound and properly contextualized within the existing AMP literature (e.g., [6,7,9]). The numerical experiments are comprehensive, including both synthetic data (with 500 problem instances) and real-world data (ORL Database of Faces), and they compare against relevant baselines (Lloyd's K-means, K-means++, variational Bayes matrix factorization). The results consistently show that the proposed AMP algorithms outperform existing methods in terms of accuracy, convergence speed, and clustering quality.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The mathematical notation is consistent throughout, and the algorithmic descriptions are precise and complete. The authors make a strong effort to connect their work to prior literature, explicitly comparing their approach with related AMP-based methods [9,13,14] and clarifying key differences (e.g., fixing rank r rather than r/m in the asymptotic limit, not assuming statistical independence among factor components). The supplementary material is referenced appropriately for additional technical details.

Regarding novelty, the paper makes a meaningful contribution by extending AMP to handle both marginalization and MAP problems in low-rank matrix reconstruction within a unified framework. The application to clustering is particularly novel in how it directly incorporates the cluster assignment constraint, which previous low-rank matrix reconstruction approaches for clustering failed to address properly. The authors carefully distinguish their work from related NIPS papers (e.g., [9,13,14]) in the introduction and discussion.

The significance of this work is evident from the experimental results, which demonstrate clear improvements over existing methods. The AMP-based clustering algorithm achieves higher accuracy and faster convergence than Lloyd's K-means and variational Bayes approaches, particularly for high-dimensional data. The theoretical properties of the algorithm (e.g., low computational cost O(mN) per iteration, guaranteed local optimality) further enhance its practical utility. The work addresses a fundamental problem in machine learning with potential applications in matrix completion, dimensionality reduction, and unsupervised learning.

Strengths:
1. Theoretical contribution: Unified AMP framework for both marginalization and MAP problems
2. Practical contribution: Novel AMP-based clustering algorithm with demonstrable improvements
3. Rigorous analysis: Proof of local optimality (Proposition 1) and comprehensive experiments
4. Clear writing and organization
5. Appropriate comparison with relevant prior work

Weaknesses:
1. The convergence of the AMP algorithm is assumed but not rigorously established (the paper notes this is not guaranteed in general)
2. The experimental section could provide more insight into why the AMP algorithm performs better, particularly regarding the bias correction term in equation (18b)
3. The parameter τ estimation method (using m^{-2}N^{-1}||A - U^t(V^t)^T||_F^2) is presented without sufficient justification for its validity in the clustering context

The paper is clearly within the scope of NIPS, addressing a fundamental problem in machine learning with theoretical and practical contributions. The work builds upon but significantly extends previous AMP-based methods for low-rank matrix problems, demonstrating novelty through both algorithmic development and application to clustering.

The authors have made a solid contribution to the field. The technical content is sound, the experimental evaluation is thorough, and the paper is well-written. The proposed AMP algorithm for clustering represents a meaningful improvement over existing methods, with theoretical guarantees and practical benefits in terms of accuracy and convergence speed. The paper would be a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings, particularly for researchers working on matrix factorization, clustering, and message-passing algorithms.

Recommendation: Accept
