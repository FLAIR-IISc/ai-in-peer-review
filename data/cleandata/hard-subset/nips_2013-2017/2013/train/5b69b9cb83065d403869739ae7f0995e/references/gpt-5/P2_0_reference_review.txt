Summary and relation to prior work
The paper derives an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on factors U and V. A one-parameter “temperature” β interpolates between standard Bayesian marginalization (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞), enabling a unified treatment. The algorithm resembles variational Bayesian matrix factorization (VBMF) but adds AMP “Onsager” corrections with negligible extra cost. A fixed-point property (Proposition 1) shows that, at convergence and under PSD conditions, the solution is a blockwise global minimum of the MAP objective.

The second contribution applies this framework to clustering by encoding assignments via one-hot rows of V. For MAP with a one-hot prior on V and uniform prior on U, the authors derive an AMP variant of K-means that differs from Lloyd’s algorithm in the assignment step by adding two terms: a leave-one-out bias correction and a cluster-size balancing term. They also propose an AMP variant for maximum-accuracy clustering (β=1) with discrete V prior. Empirically, on synthetic data (m=800, N=1600) and ORL faces (N=400, r̂=40), their AMP-KM achieves lower K-means loss than Lloyd/K-means++ and their AMP-MA attains higher accuracy than VBMF-MA.

Relative to prior AMP work, they extend [9] beyond posterior means to MAP via β→∞, and they highlight differences from recent bilinear/generalized AMP [13,14] (e.g., fixed rank r in the large-system limit and allowing dependencies within rows of U,V). The clustering angle connects to NMF-based clustering [10,11] and to Lloyd’s algorithm [12], but here the one-hot constraint is handled directly within an AMP derivation.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The AMP updates are plausible and consistent with dense-graph AMP derivations. The β-trick is a standard statistical physics device that justifies both posterior means and MAP within one algorithm. The link to VBMF via removal of Onsager terms is insightful. Proposition 1 (blockwise optimality at fixed points) is a useful property, though contingent on PSD conditions and on convergence.
- However, key derivations (algorithm details, proof of Proposition 1, and especially state evolution) are deferred; the main text only asserts that state evolution exists and is accurate. Without the supplement, it is difficult to fully verify correctness or the precise conditions (random model, scaling, damping) under which the algorithm is guaranteed to work.
- Empirical support: Synthetic experiments are carefully controlled and show consistent improvements in loss (AMP-KM) and accuracy (AMP-MA) across r. Real data results on ORL show modest but consistent gains vs. K-means++. Still, the empirical section is limited: only one real dataset, no runtime comparisons, and few baselines for clustering beyond K-means-family and VBMF-MA. For matrix factorization proper (beyond clustering), there are no experiments illustrating the claimed generality to different priors or noise models.

Clarity
- The paper is generally clear and well-organized, with precise notation and algorithmic equations. The interpretation of the AMP-KM assignment correction (bias correction and cluster-size effect) is helpful.
- Some aspects could be clarified: conditions for convergence and when PSD of ∑S_i and ∑T_j holds; practical stabilization (damping, step sizes); sensitivity to τ estimation; how to compute G∞ when f∞ is nondifferentiable; and how the algorithm behaves with rank mismatch (r̂≠r), which is mentioned but not explored.
- The link to known bilinear AMP algorithms (BiG-AMP) could be elaborated with a more direct comparison of assumptions and update forms.

Originality
- Extending bilinear AMP to encompass MAP estimation via β→∞ and providing a principled way to compute both posterior means and marginal MAPs is a meaningful conceptual and technical step beyond [9].
- The AMP formulation of K-means with explicit one-hot constraints, yielding assignment corrections that resemble a leave-one-out/size-normalization, appears novel and interesting. While K-means and GMM-EM are classical, this particular AMP-based correction and its derivation from a Bayesian bilinear model is new to my knowledge.
- That said, the paper’s originality claims would be strengthened by a more thorough comparison to BiG-AMP and to EM for spherical GMMs (a natural baseline for the clustering setting), as well as to spectral clustering for high-dimensional data.

Significance
- If the derivations and state evolution hold under standard conditions, this work meaningfully broadens the toolbox of AMP for matrix factorization with general priors and for MAP inference. That’s valuable for both theory and practice.
- The clustering application is practically relevant, and the observed improvements over K-means/K-means++ (especially in high dimensions) could be impactful. The bias-correction insight may inspire improved assignment rules in other iterative clustering schemes.
- However, the current empirical scope is narrow: no tests on diverse real datasets, no ablations of Onsager terms, no comparisons to stronger clustering baselines (e.g., GMM-EM, spectral methods), and no results beyond the clustering specialization to demonstrate the general low-rank reconstruction utility. Occasional oscillations and collapse to a single cluster in some runs raise stability concerns that deserve further analysis.

Pros for acceptance
- Conceptually solid extension of AMP to a β-parameterized posterior enabling both marginalization and MAP.
- Clear algorithmic connection and improvement over VBMF/ICM via Onsager terms; useful fixed-point optimality property.
- Novel and interpretable AMP variant of K-means with consistent empirical gains.
- Potentially broad applicability to low-rank problems with structured priors.

Cons for acceptance
- Critical theoretical elements (derivation details, state evolution, convergence conditions) are not presented in the main text; strength depends on the supplement.
- Limited empirical evaluation: few baselines, one real dataset, no runtime, no robustness analyses (rank/τ mismatch, damping).
- Stability issues (oscillations, cluster collapse) are acknowledged but not systematically addressed.
- Claims of novelty relative to BiG-AMP and other bilinear AMP variants could be substantiated more thoroughly.

Questions/suggestions for authors
- Provide in the supplement: full derivation, precise assumptions for state evolution, and convergence conditions; discuss damping strategies and their impact.
- Compare to BiG-AMP and to EM for spherical GMMs and spectral clustering on the same datasets; include runtime and iteration counts.
- Study sensitivity to r̂ and τ, and robustness to prior mismatch (especially for AMP-MA).
- Report ablations that remove the Onsager terms to quantify their effect on K-means loss and accuracy.
- Expand experiments to other real datasets and to non-clustering factorization tasks with different priors to demonstrate generality.

Overall evaluation
This paper presents a promising and reasonably novel extension of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction that unifies posterior mean and MAP estimation and yields a practically useful AMP variant of K-means. The ideas are well-motivated and the empirical results are encouraging, but the current submission lacks breadth in experiments and defers essential theory to the supplement. I view this as a borderline submission; with stronger empirical validation and clearer theoretical exposition (even in the supplement), it would be a solid contribution. As is, I lean toward a weak reject, primarily due to limited validation and incomplete presentation of theory.
