Summary
The paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction when structural properties on factors U and V can be encoded via priors. It develops an AMP-style algorithm for a one-parameter “tempered” posterior p(U,V|A;β) that interpolates between MMSE/marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞). Compared to prior AMP work for low-rank problems (rank-1 IterFac; general-rank AMP that estimates posterior means), the authors claim their algorithm also handles MAP and marginal MAP. As a second contribution, they instantiate the MAP-style AMP to K-means clustering by encoding the one-hot constraint on V, deriving a practical algorithm (Algorithm 3) that modifies Lloyd’s assignment step with Onsager-like corrections and an online noise estimate. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and ORL faces suggest better K-means loss/accuracy than Lloyd/K-means++ and VBMF-based marginal accuracy, with fast convergence.

Relation to prior work
This builds on AMP/GAMP for linear models (Donoho et al., Rangan) and its bilinear/low-rank variants (IterFac; Matsushita & Tanaka; BiG-AMP/Parker et al.; Krzakala et al. for dictionary learning). The clustering mapping is a standard K-means-as-factorization perspective. The main claimed difference is enabling β→∞ (MAP) updates within AMP and directly enforcing one-hot V in the K-means instantiation.

Strengths
- A unified AMP derivation that explicitly treats a β-temperature and shows how MAP denoisers arise as β→∞ is conceptually clean and aligns with known AMP practice (MMSE vs MAP denoisers).
- The K-means instantiation leads to an intuitive “leave-one-out”/size-correction assignment rule that plausibly improves over Lloyd by debiasing the current center estimate. The link between these corrections and Onsager terms is insightful.
- Computationally attractive outer iterations (O(mN)) with closed-form updates for the K-means case; the exposition of functions f and G clarifies how general priors could be plugged in.
- Proposition 1 gives a mild optimality guarantee at fixed points (blockwise global optimality), helping interpret the algorithm relative to ICM.
- Synthetic experiments are relatively large (500 trials) and report both K-means loss and accuracy; ORL results show practical gains in many runs.

Weaknesses and concerns
Methodological/theoretical
- The state evolution (SE) analysis, which is central to AMP’s credibility and guides both performance prediction and algorithm design, is omitted “due to space.” Without SE (or at least extensive empirical calibration of SE predictions), the theoretical claims are incomplete. Convergence is explicitly not guaranteed; oscillations are observed and handled ad hoc (Vt=Vt−2 stopping).
- The novelty of “AMP for MAP” is limited: replacing MMSE denoisers by MAP/prox denoisers is standard in the AMP/GAMP literature, including bilinear settings (e.g., BiG-AMP, which already couples denoisers with EM and MAP-type updates). The paper should clearly articulate what is new beyond known β-temperature or proximal-MM algorithms within AMP.
- Proposition 1 relies on positive semidefiniteness of aggregated covariance terms; practical conditions ensuring this at convergence are not discussed, and the claim that it holds in “almost all cases” is not substantiated.
- Some equations appear inconsistent (e.g., scaling in (17a): Λu seems to miss a 1/(mτ) factor compared to (9a)), suggesting potential derivation or typographical errors. Precise scaling matters in AMP.
- Practical use with non-Gaussian priors: for general p̂u, f∞ requires solving r̂-dimensional convex problems per row; complexity and solver choices are not discussed.

Experimental
- Baselines are weak/incomplete for clustering. Comparisons are limited to Lloyd/K-means++, VBMF-based marginal accuracy, and the proposed methods. Absent are standard, stronger baselines: EM for spherical/full-covariance GMMs, spherical K-means, spectral clustering, mixture of von Mises-Fisher (for high-dim unit-norm), subspace clustering, and modern NMF-based clustering. Without these, significance is unclear.
- Synthetic setup is favorable to the method: r̂=r (no rank misspecification), true priors used for maximum-accuracy methods, and Gaussian noise exactly matches the assumed model. Realistic robustness (rank mismatch, prior mismatch, different SNRs, non-Gaussian noise) is not evaluated.
- The τ estimation is heuristic (residual-based) and its impact is not studied. The method sometimes collapses to a single cluster on ORL (2/50 trials), indicating instability. No sensitivity analyses (initialization, τ, r̂) or ablations (e.g., removing the Onsager-size terms) are provided.
- ORL results on raw pixels without dimensionality reduction (e.g., PCA/whitening) are not a strong testbed; many face clustering baselines would perform differently under standard preprocessing.
- No wall-clock times or memory usage are reported. Reporting only iteration counts is insufficient, particularly since AMP and Lloyd have very different per-iteration costs and inner solves may be needed for general priors.

Clarity
- The paper is notation-heavy and some definitions are deferred; more implementation details (e.g., explicit priors used in each experiment, how f/G are computed, how β→∞ is realized numerically) would improve reproducibility. The Supplementary presumably contains derivations; critical aspects (SE, convergence) should not rely on supp alone.

Pros for acceptance
- Interesting and plausible AMP-based correction to Lloyd’s rule with measurable empirical gains.
- A unifying perspective connecting K-means to AMP with one-hot priors; potentially useful to the community.
- Clean formulation of β-tempered AMP bridging MMSE and MAP.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to existing bilinear AMP (e.g., BiG-AMP) and known MAP/denoiser variants.
- Missing SE analysis and convergence guarantees; observed oscillations and failures on real data.
- Incomplete and arguably unfair empirical evaluation with weak baselines and favorable assumptions.
- Some technical/scaling inconsistencies; insufficient implementation detail.

Questions for authors
1. How does your algorithm compare empirically and conceptually to BiG-AMP (Parker et al.) in both MMSE and MAP modes? Can you clarify what is new beyond adopting MAP denoisers?
2. Can you provide SE equations and at least empirical SE validation on synthetic data?
3. Please clarify the scaling in (17a) and any other typos; provide a self-contained derivation of Algorithm 3.
4. How sensitive are results to τ estimation, rank misspecification (r̂≠r), and initialization? Can you ablate the correction terms in (18b)?
5. Could you include stronger baselines (EM-GMM, spherical K-means, spectral clustering) and standard preprocessing (e.g., PCA) on ORL?

Recommendation
Reject. The ideas are promising, but the paper lacks rigorous theoretical support (SE/convergence), the novelty versus established bilinear AMP is limited, and the empirical evaluation is not sufficiently thorough against strong baselines or under realistic mismatches. I encourage the authors to strengthen theory, fix derivational issues, and broaden experiments; the K-means AMP variant could be a valuable contribution with more rigorous validation.

Confidence: High.
