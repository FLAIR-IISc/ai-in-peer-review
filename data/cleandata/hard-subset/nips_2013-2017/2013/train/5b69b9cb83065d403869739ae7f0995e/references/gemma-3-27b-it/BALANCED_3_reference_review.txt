This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means clustering. The core idea is to extend existing AMP algorithms to handle general rank estimation and MAP estimation, and to leverage the connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The paper builds upon previous work in AMP, Bayesian matrix factorization, and K-means, and aims to provide a more efficient and accurate algorithm, particularly for high-dimensional data.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Extension of AMP:** Extending the existing AMP framework to handle MAP estimation in low-rank matrix reconstruction is a valuable contribution. This addresses a limitation of previous AMP approaches, which were often limited to posterior mean estimation.
* **Connection to Clustering:** The application of the algorithm to K-means clustering is insightful and provides a practical use case.  The formulation directly enforces the constraint of exclusive cluster assignment, which is often handled indirectly in other low-rank based clustering methods.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides some theoretical grounding for the algorithm, referencing the state evolution and providing a proposition regarding optimality of the fixed point.  The comparison to variational Bayes and ICM is also helpful in contextualizing the approach.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments on both artificial and real data (ORL faces) demonstrate the performance of the proposed algorithm, showing improvements over standard K-means and other related methods.
* **Clear Algorithm Presentation:** Algorithms 2 and 3 are well-defined and relatively easy to follow, given the complexity of the underlying theory.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited State Evolution Discussion:** While mentioning state evolution, the paper does not present the equations or provide a detailed discussion of how it can be used to predict the performance of the algorithm. This is a significant omission, as state evolution is a key advantage of AMP-based methods.
* **Convergence Proof Missing:** The paper states the convergence of Algorithm 2 is assumed, but doesn’t provide a proof or detailed discussion of conditions under which convergence is guaranteed. This is a crucial aspect for any iterative algorithm.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The role of the noise parameter τ is not fully explored. While it’s acknowledged that it appears due to scaling in the derivation, its practical impact and sensitivity on performance are not thoroughly investigated.
* **Computational Complexity Discussion:** While the paper claims low computational cost (O(mN)), a more detailed analysis comparing it with other methods like variational Bayes or stochastic gradient descent would be beneficial. The cost of calculating f and G, especially for non-Gaussian priors, needs further elaboration.
* **Clarity of Notation:** Some notation is a bit dense and could benefit from more explanation, particularly related to the functions f and G, and the scaling in the derivations.
* **Comparison to Recent Work:** The paper references work up to 2013.  There has been substantial progress in AMP and related areas since then. A more thorough review of recent literature (e.g., work on structured AMP, non-convex optimization with AMP) is needed.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of the AMP algorithm to a challenging problem.
* The connection to K-means clustering provides a practical application and demonstrates the algorithm's effectiveness.
* The empirical results are promising and show improvements over existing methods.
* The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the relevant literature.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The lack of a convergence proof and detailed discussion of state evolution weaken the theoretical contribution.
* The limited analysis of parameter sensitivity and computational complexity raises concerns about the algorithm's practical usability.
* The paper doesn’t adequately address the wider landscape of recent research in AMP and related areas.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and its application to clustering. The proposed AMP algorithm is a valuable extension of existing methods, and the empirical results are encouraging. However, the lack of a convergence proof, detailed state evolution analysis, and a more thorough comparison to recent work are significant weaknesses. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. The authors should focus on:

1. **Providing a convergence proof or a thorough discussion of convergence conditions.**
2. **Presenting the state evolution equations and analyzing the algorithm’s performance using this framework.**
3. **Conducting a more comprehensive analysis of the algorithm's sensitivity to parameters and its computational complexity.**
4. **Updating the related work section to include recent advances in AMP and related fields.**
5. **Clarifying the notation and providing more detailed explanations of key concepts.**

Addressing these weaknesses would significantly strengthen the paper and make it a more compelling contribution to the NIPS community.
