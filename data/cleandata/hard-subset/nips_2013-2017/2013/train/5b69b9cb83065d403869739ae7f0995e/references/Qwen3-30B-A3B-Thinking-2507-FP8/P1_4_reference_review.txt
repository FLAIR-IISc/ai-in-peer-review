

# Peer Review: "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

## Summary

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that can handle both marginalization problems (posterior means) and MAP estimation. The authors extend previous AMP work for low-rank matrix reconstruction to incorporate flexible prior distributions and provide a unified framework that can be specialized for different estimation tasks. The key contribution is applying this framework to K-means clustering, where they derive a novel algorithm that directly handles the constraint that each data point must be assigned to exactly one cluster. The paper demonstrates through both synthetic and real data experiments that their AMP-based clustering algorithms outperform existing methods like Lloyd's K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes approaches in terms of accuracy and convergence speed, particularly for high-dimensional data.

The paper relates well to previous NIPS work, building on the AMP framework developed for compressed sensing [6,7] and extending the recent work on AMP for low-rank matrix reconstruction [9]. It distinguishes itself from related work [13,14] by fixing the rank r rather than the ratio r/m in the asymptotic limit and by not assuming statistical independence among components of U0 and V0.

## Strengths

**Theoretical Soundness and Novelty:** The paper makes a strong theoretical contribution by extending AMP to handle both marginalization and MAP estimation in low-rank matrix reconstruction. The derivation of Algorithm 2 is well-motivated and grounded in the belief propagation framework. The authors provide a convergence analysis (Proposition 1) that establishes a connection between their AMP algorithm and the ICM algorithm, showing that AMP fixed points are also fixed points of ICM. This is a significant theoretical contribution that helps explain why AMP might avoid getting stuck in poor local minima.

**Practical Impact:** The application to clustering is particularly compelling. The authors correctly identify that existing approaches to clustering via low-rank matrix reconstruction (e.g., [10,11]) don't properly handle the constraint that each data point must be assigned to exactly one cluster. Their AMP-KM algorithm directly addresses this constraint, and the experiments clearly demonstrate superior performance over standard K-means methods.

**Experimental Rigor:** The numerical experiments are thorough and well-designed. The synthetic data experiments with varying cluster numbers (r) and the real data experiments on the ORL face database provide convincing evidence of the algorithm's superiority. The authors carefully evaluate multiple performance metrics (normalized K-means loss, accuracy, convergence speed) and compare against multiple baselines.

**Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized. The mathematical formulations are precise, the algorithms are clearly presented, and the connections between the matrix reconstruction problem and clustering are thoroughly explained. The supplementary material is referenced appropriately for more detailed derivations.

## Weaknesses

**Convergence Analysis:** While the paper provides Proposition 1 about the optimality of fixed points, it does not establish conditions under which the AMP algorithm actually converges. The authors state they "assume convergence" without discussing the practical conditions or limitations. For a paper about an iterative algorithm, this is a significant gap that should be addressed.

**Comparison to Recent AMP Work:** The paper acknowledges related AMP work [13,14] but doesn't provide a detailed comparison of their algorithm with these more recent approaches. The differences in asymptotic assumptions (fixing rank r vs. ratio r/m) are noted, but a more thorough discussion would strengthen the paper's position in the literature.

**Parameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't analyze how sensitive the algorithm is to the choice of β (the parameter in the extended posterior distribution). In practice, this could be crucial for performance, but the paper doesn't explore this aspect.

**Computational Complexity Analysis:** While the paper states the computational complexity per iteration is O(mN), it doesn't provide a detailed analysis of the total number of iterations required for convergence. This is particularly important for practical applications, as the overall complexity depends on both the per-iteration cost and the number of iterations.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The theoretical framework is sound, the experimental results are compelling, and the application to clustering addresses a clear gap in the literature. The paper is well-written and well-organized, making it accessible to the NIPS audience.

The main weaknesses relate to the convergence analysis and the limited comparison with the most recent AMP-based matrix reconstruction methods. However, these do not detract from the paper's overall contribution, which is substantial and well-supported by both theory and experiments.

## Recommendation

**Accept** with minor revisions.

The paper has strong theoretical grounding, novel contributions, and compelling experimental results. The weaknesses identified are important but not critical to the paper's main contribution. The authors should address the convergence analysis and provide a more detailed comparison with recent AMP work in the revised version.

## Arguments for Acceptance

- Novel AMP framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles both marginalization and MAP estimation
- First algorithm that directly handles the "exactly one cluster" constraint in the low-rank matrix reconstruction framework for clustering
- Clear theoretical connection to ICM algorithm (Proposition 1)
- Strong experimental results showing superior performance over multiple baselines
- Well-written and well-organized paper with clear presentation of algorithms and results

## Arguments Against Acceptance

- Limited convergence analysis (assumes convergence without establishing conditions)
- Limited comparison with most recent AMP work [13,14]
- Limited discussion of parameter sensitivity (particularly β)
- Incomplete computational complexity analysis (missing number of iterations)

## Final Comments

This is an excellent paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and its applications. The AMP framework they propose is both theoretically sound and practically useful, with clear advantages over existing methods for clustering high-dimensional data. The paper is well-written and well-executed, and the experimental results are convincing. The minor weaknesses identified are important to address in revision but do not undermine the paper's overall quality and contribution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the convergence analysis and comparison with recent AMP work.
