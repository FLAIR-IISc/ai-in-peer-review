## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

**Summary:**

This paper presents an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending existing AMP approaches to handle MAP estimation beyond posterior mean estimation.  A key contribution is applying this framework to K-means type clustering, formulating it as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem and presenting a novel clustering algorithm. The authors highlight the algorithm's flexibility in incorporating prior knowledge, its computational efficiency (linear in matrix size), and present numerical results showing improvements over Lloyd's K-means, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon the growing body of work leveraging AMP for large-scale inference, citing seminal papers on compressed sensing (Donoho et al.) and generalized AMP (Rangan). It connects to existing Bayesian matrix factorization techniques (Salakhutdinov & Mnih, Raiko et al.) and variational Bayes methods, positioning AMP as a potentially more effective alternative due to its ability to capture dependencies between factors.  The application to clustering references established methods like Lloyd's K-means (Lloyd) and non-negative matrix factorization based clustering (Xu et al.). The authors correctly situate their work within the broader context of applying low-rank methods to clustering (Ding et al.).

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper is grounded in a solid theoretical framework based on Bayesian inference and AMP, aiming for asymptotic guarantees (though state evolution analysis is relegated to supplementary material).
* **Generality & Flexibility:** The algorithm’s flexibility in incorporating prior distributions on the factors U and V is a significant strength. This allows adaptation to various problem settings beyond standard low-rank reconstruction.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The claimed linear computational complexity (O(mN)) is a major advantage, especially for large-scale datasets.
* **Novel Clustering Algorithm:** The formulation of K-means as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem and the resulting AMP-based algorithm represent a novel contribution.
* **Empirical Results:** The numerical experiments demonstrate promising results, particularly in scenarios where Lloyd’s K-means struggles (high dimensionality). The clear presentation of the performance metrics (K-means loss, accuracy, iteration count) is helpful.
* **Careful Comparison:** The authors carefully compare their method to existing algorithms (Lloyd’s, K-means++, VBMF-MA, AMP-MA) and acknowledge the limitations of each.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:**  While the paper motivates the algorithm through theoretical reasoning, the lack of detailed state evolution analysis in the main text weakens the theoretical claims.  The proof of Proposition 1, while important, feels somewhat isolated.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance likely depends on the choice of the noise parameter τ. While estimated in the experiments, a more thorough investigation of its impact and sensitivity would be valuable.
* **Clarity of Notation:**  The notation can be dense and challenging to follow, particularly in Section 4.1.  More intuitive variable names and clearer explanations of the functions *f* and *G* would improve readability.
* **Implementation Details:** The paper lacks sufficient details regarding implementation choices that could influence performance, such as the specific stopping criteria for the AMP iterations.
* **Scope of Experiments:** The experiments, while well-presented, primarily focus on synthetic data. Further evaluation on a wider range of real-world datasets would strengthen the conclusions.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically motivated algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* The algorithm offers potential computational advantages over existing methods, especially for large-scale problems.
* The empirical results are promising and demonstrate the algorithm’s effectiveness in specific scenarios.
* The work addresses a relevant and challenging problem in machine learning.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The theoretical analysis is incomplete, lacking a detailed examination of state evolution.
* The paper’s clarity could be improved through more careful notation and explanations.
* The experimental validation is somewhat limited, primarily relying on synthetic data.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a **strong submission** to NIPS. It presents a technically sound and potentially impactful contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. While the theoretical analysis could be more complete and the presentation improved, the novel algorithm, its computational efficiency, and the promising empirical results warrant acceptance.  The authors effectively address the reviewing criteria: the paper is of good **quality**, reasonably **clear** (with some room for improvement), demonstrates **originality** in applying AMP to the K-means problem, and has a good level of **significance**, particularly for large-scale applications.



**Recommendation:** **Accept** with minor revisions.  I recommend the authors:

1.  Consider adding a brief discussion of state evolution analysis to the main text, even if the full details are in the supplementary material.
2.  Improve the clarity of the notation and explanations, particularly in Section 4.1.
3.  Expand the experimental validation to include a wider range of real-world datasets.
4.  Provide more details regarding implementation choices and parameter tuning.
