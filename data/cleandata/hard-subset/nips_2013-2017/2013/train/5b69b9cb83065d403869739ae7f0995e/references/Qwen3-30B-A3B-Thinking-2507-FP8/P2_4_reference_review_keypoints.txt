- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that unifies marginalization and MAP estimation via a single parameter β.  
- Applies this unified AMP framework to K-means clustering, enforcing the constraint that each datum is assigned to exactly one cluster.  
- Derives Algorithm 2 from established AMP principles and provides a clear algorithmic description (including Algorithm 3 for clustering).  
- Presents Proposition 1 showing fixed points of the AMP MAP algorithm correspond to fixed points of ICM, giving an optimality property.  
- Numerical experiments on synthetic data (500 instances across ranks) and ORL faces show superior normalized K-means loss, accuracy, and faster convergence versus Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, variational Bayes, and competing AMP methods.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with consistent notation and helpful figures; supplementary material likely contains derivation details.  
- Original contributions: extends AMP to both marginalization and MAP (beyond prior posterior-mean-only work) and introduces a novel clustering approach handling exclusive assignments in the low-rank framework.  
- Distinguishes itself from related work by fixing rank r (not r/m) and not assuming independence among factor matrix components.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem with empirically validated improvements and potential impact for high-dimensional clustering and matrix factorization tasks.  
- Limitation: convergence is not guaranteed and the algorithm requires initialization.  
- Theoretical analysis is limited—no full convergence proof or error bounds provided.  
- Experiments could use more real-world datasets to demonstrate generalizability beyond ORL faces.  
- Some implementation/details (e.g., choosing τ) and the reason for faster convergence relative to variational Bayes are not fully explained.  
- Overall recommendation: accept at NeurIPS—technically sound, original, empirically strong, with weaknesses outweighed by contributions.
