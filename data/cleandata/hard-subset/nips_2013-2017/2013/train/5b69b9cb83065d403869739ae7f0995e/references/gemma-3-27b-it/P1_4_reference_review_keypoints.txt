- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction extended to MAP estimation and applied to clustering.  
- Frames K-means–type clustering as a special case of low-rank reconstruction with constraints.  
- Claims improved performance over Lloyd’s K-means, especially in high-dimensional settings, and provides a proposition guaranteeing optimality at a fixed point.  
- Correctly positions the work relative to Bayesian, AMP, IterFac, and variational methods, citing relevant prior work.  
- Differentiates from prior AMP/matrix-factorization work by extending AMP to MAP estimation and a specific problem formulation.  
- Uses a flexible Bayesian framework allowing various priors on factors U and V.  
- Provides a theoretical proposition offering optimality justification for the algorithm’s fixed point.  
- Presents clustering as an elegant instance of the low-rank reconstruction framework, suggesting broad applicability.  
- Shows promising empirical results versus Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++, including experiments on the ORL face dataset.  
- Paper is generally well-written and the algorithm is clearly presented step-by-step.  
- Omits a detailed state evolution derivation, limiting predictive understanding of performance across regimes.  
- Lacks a general convergence guarantee beyond fixed-point optimality conditions.  
- Insufficient discussion and analysis of sensitivity to the noise-variance parameter τ and robustness to its estimation errors.  
- Claims linear O(mN) cost but doesn’t quantify constant factors or computational expense of f and G for non-Gaussian priors.  
- Limited experimental baselines (missing spectral clustering and recent matrix factorization methods) weaken empirical claims.  
- Scalability not convincingly demonstrated—no large-scale experiments (e.g., millions of points) despite scalability claims.  
- Quality assessed 8/10: technically sound but hampered by convergence and scalability gaps.  
- Clarity assessed 8/10: generally clear but theoretical sections could be simplified.  
- Originality assessed 7/10: novel MAP extension and clustering formulation, but builds on established AMP matrix-factorization ideas.  
- Significance assessed 7/10: potentially impactful for high-dimensional clustering, contingent on demonstrated efficiency and robustness.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded algorithm with promising empirical results and flexible framework.  
- Against acceptance: missing convergence proof, absent state evolution analysis, limited parameter sensitivity and computational detail, and incomplete experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking for added state evolution discussion, parameter sensitivity analysis, and computation-time/scalability evidence.
