Summary
The paper develops an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction under additive Gaussian noise, with factor-wise structural priors. The key idea is to introduce a one-parameter family of posteriors p̂(U,V|A; β) ∝ p̂(U,V|A)^β, unifying sum-product (β=1) and max-sum/MAP (β→∞) inference. The resulting AMP updates couple Gaussian “denoisers” f, G derived from the priors with Onsager corrections. The authors (i) extend prior AMP formulations for bilinear models to support MAP estimation and general (not necessarily componentwise separable) row-priors, and (ii) instantiate the framework for clustering by enforcing a one-hot constraint on rows of V, yielding an AMP variant of K-means with assignment rules corrected by Onsager terms. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and ORL faces suggest improvements over Lloyd’s K-means and a VBMF baseline, particularly in high dimension.

Relation to prior work
- AMP/GAMP: The framework is closely related to AMP (Donoho et al.) and GAMP (Rangan), with bilinear extensions (IterFac; BiG-AMP). The proposed β-trick to bridge MMSE and MAP is conceptually akin to the distinction between sum-product and max-sum (MAP) GAMP; please clarify novelty relative to max-sum BiG-AMP, which targets MAP in bilinear models.
- Variational Bayes/IICM: Removing Onsager terms recovers a VBMF/ICM-like scheme. The paper rightly emphasizes the role of Onsager corrections and the resulting fixed-point properties.
- Clustering: Casting K-means as a constrained low-rank factorization is classical. The novelty here is an AMP derivation that yields leave-one-out and cluster-size correction terms in the assignment step.

Strengths
- Methodological: Clean AMP derivation that supports both β=1 and β→∞ updates with general row-wise priors p̂u, p̂v (not necessarily factorized across latent dimensions), which is useful in practice.
- Clustering instantiation: The assignment rule in Algorithm 3 makes intuitive sense (bias correction for current assignment; size regularization) and is derived rather than heuristic.
- Theoretical insight: Proposition 1 gives a reassuring optimality property at fixed points (coordinate-wise global minima), and the connection to Bethe approximations is clear.
- Empirics: On synthetic high-dimensional mixtures, AMP-KM achieves lower K-means loss than Lloyd/K-means++, and AMP-MA attains higher accuracy than VBMF-MA. ORL results also show improvements in most runs.

Weaknesses and points needing clarification
- Novelty vs. existing AMP: The claim that prior AMP for general-rank “can only treat posterior means” is too strong in light of max-sum GAMP and BiG-AMP variants that target MAP. Please position the β-parameterization and your MAP updates against max-sum BiG-AMP (and EM-BiG-AMP). What is strictly new?
- Missing or deferred theory: State evolution is advertised but omitted. Given the methodological pitch, even a summary SE (assumptions; recursion; predicted MSE/accuracy) and a small empirical validation would substantially strengthen the paper.
- Convergence and stability: AMP is known to require damping/stepsize control in non-ideal conditions. You acknowledge oscillatory behavior and introduce ad-hoc stopping; but there is no discussion of damping, adaptive steps, or conditions guaranteeing positivity of ∑S_i, ∑T_j. Practical guidance is needed.
- Computational cost: While the dominant cost is A V and A^T U (O(mNr̂)), the denoiser f and Jacobian G require r̂-dimensional integrals (β<∞) or optimizations (β→∞) per row/column. This can be nontrivial for moderate r̂. Please detail the per-iteration complexity constants, discuss choices of priors enabling fast closed forms, and report wall-clock times.
- τ estimation and scale sensitivity: In clustering, τ is not part of the classical K-means objective, yet it scales the distance term relative to size corrections. The proposed plug-in τ̂ = ||A−U V^T||_F^2/(m^2 N) is ad hoc and may explain degenerate one-cluster solutions in two ORL runs. Analyze sensitivity to τ, or estimate τ jointly (e.g., EM-like update) to avoid pathological behavior.
- Empirical comparisons: 
  - Synthetic: Using true priors for AMP-MA while not doing the same for baselines may bias results. Please compare against EM for Gaussian Mixtures (equal spherical covariance), which directly matches the data model and targets accuracy. Also report multi-restart Lloyd with K-means++ seeding to control for initialization.
  - ORL: Provide accuracy and loss distributions, not only best/average. Include runtime and iteration counts. Consider competitor methods common for faces (PCA+K-means, spectral clustering, NMF variants).
- Rank mismatch and robustness: All experiments fix r̂=r. How does performance degrade under over/underestimation of rank, non-Gaussian noise, or non-uniform cluster sizes?

Quality
- The derivation appears technically sound at a high level, but key details (SE, convergence conditions, exact assumptions for Proposition 1, handling of improper priors) are deferred. The empirical section is promising but limited in baselines and ablation (e.g., remove Onsager terms to quantify their effect).

Clarity
- Generally well-organized and readable. Helpful to: (i) collect all assumptions for the AMP derivation (randomness/scaling, fixed r vs. r/m→const); (ii) summarize computational complexity; (iii) clarify the role and estimation of τ in clustering; (iv) provide pseudo-code with damping options. Some minor typographical/notation issues can be cleaned up.

Originality
- Moderate. The β-unification and the specific K-means AMP instantiation are interesting. However, the MAP capability per se seems close to max-sum (Bi)GAMP; novelty should be sharpened.

Significance
- Potentially useful: a practical AMP alternative to Lloyd’s K-means with principled corrections and good performance in high-d settings is valuable. Without SE analysis, stronger robustness studies, and broader baselines, the impact is limited.

Questions/suggestions for authors
- Clarify novelty vs. max-sum BiG-AMP and whether your algorithm can be seen as a special case or offers provable advantages (e.g., fixed-r SE).
- Provide damping/stepsize guidance and report its effect.
- Add comparisons to GMM-EM and multi-restart K-means++, plus runtime.
- Include an ablation showing the effect of the Onsager terms (remove the last terms in (9a),(9c)/(17b) and compare).
- Study rank mismatch and τ estimation robustness.
- Consider adding a brief SE summary and empirical validation.

Pros
- Clear AMP formulation unifying MMSE and MAP with general row-priors.
- Novel and intuitive AMP-based K-means assignment rule.
- Empirical gains on synthetic and ORL; low per-iteration cost.

Cons
- Unclear novelty relative to existing (Bi)GAMP MAP.
- Missing SE analysis and convergence guidance; ad-hoc stopping.
- Limited baselines and sensitivity analyses; τ handling is fragile.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The ideas are promising and potentially impactful, especially the clustering variant, but the paper needs stronger positioning vs. existing MAP (Bi)GAMP, more robust experiments (including GMM-EM and multi-restart K-means), and at least a concise SE/convergence discussion. Confidence: high.
