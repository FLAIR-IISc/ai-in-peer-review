- Derives an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a temperature β that interpolates between posterior means (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).  
- Adds Onsager corrections to a VBMF-like algorithm with negligible extra cost and proves a blockwise optimality fixed-point property under PSD conditions (Proposition 1).  
- Applies the framework to clustering by encoding one-hot V, deriving AMP-KM (MAP) that modifies Lloyd’s assignments with leave-one-out bias correction and cluster-size balancing terms.  
- Proposes an AMP variant for maximum-accuracy clustering (β=1) with discrete V prior and shows empirical gains on synthetic data and ORL faces versus K-means++ and VBMF-MA.  
- Technical derivations and state evolution are plausible and consistent with dense-graph AMP; β-trick is a standard unifying device.  
- Key theoretical details (algorithm derivations, proof of Proposition 1, and especially state evolution and precise assumptions) are deferred to the supplement, limiting verification.  
- Empirically, synthetic results are convincing and ORL shows modest consistent gains, but experiments are limited to one real dataset, few baselines, no runtimes, and no non-clustering factorization tests.  
- Paper is generally clear with precise notation; interpretation of AMP-KM corrections is helpful.  
- Clarifications needed on convergence conditions, when PSD conditions hold, practical stabilization (damping), τ estimation sensitivity, nondifferentiable f∞ handling, and behavior under rank mismatch.  
- Novelty: meaningful extension of AMP to MAP via β→∞ and a novel AMP-based K-means handling one-hot constraints directly.  
- Novelty claims would be strengthened by more explicit comparisons to BiG-AMP, EM for spherical GMMs, and spectral clustering.  
- Significance: broadens AMP toolbox for low-rank problems and offers a practically relevant clustering variant with potentially impactful bias-correction insights.  
- Limitations: narrow empirical scope, lack of ablations on Onsager terms, and observed stability issues (oscillations, cluster collapse) needing systematic analysis.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid conceptual extension to β-parameterized AMP, clear link/improvement over VBMF via Onsager terms, fixed-point optimality, and novel interpretable AMP-KM with empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: essential theory deferred, limited empirical validation and baselines, and unresolved stability and novelty-comparison concerns.  
- Key author suggestions: include full derivations and state-evolution assumptions in supplement, discuss damping strategies, compare to BiG-AMP/EM/spectral methods with runtimes, study sensitivity to r̂ and τ, and provide Onsager ablations and more real datasets.  
- Overall: promising and novel but borderline due to incomplete theoretical presentation and limited experimental validation; reviewer leans weak reject pending stronger theory and broader experiments.
