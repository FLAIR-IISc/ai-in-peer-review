This paper presents an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and, interestingly, applies it to K-means clustering. The core idea – extending existing AMP formulations to handle MAP estimation and leveraging this for clustering – is quite compelling. The formulation connecting low-rank reconstruction and K-means is well-established, but the proposed AMP approach promises efficiency gains, particularly in high-dimensional settings. The authors correctly position their work within the broader landscape of AMP algorithms and highlight key differences from existing methods, namely the fixed rank assumption and the independence assumptions on the matrix factors.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The application of AMP to MAP estimation in the low-rank setting, and subsequently to K-means, is a genuinely novel contribution. While AMP is established, extending it in this way, and demonstrating its efficacy for clustering is a significant step.
*   **Theoretical Grounding:** The paper establishes a clear connection to existing theory around AMP and belief propagation, hinting at potential performance guarantees (though not fully explored in this submission). The proposition regarding fixed points and global optimality is a strong theoretical result.
*   **Potential for Efficiency:** The authors emphasize the linear computational cost, which is highly attractive for large-scale problems. This is a crucial advantage over variational Bayes methods that can suffer from computational bottlenecks.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments, while not extensive, demonstrate promising results. The performance improvements over Lloyd's K-means in high dimensions are notable, and the comparison with other algorithms provides a baseline.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the connection to existing AMP theory is mentioned, a more detailed analysis of convergence properties, or state evolution, would significantly strengthen the paper. The claim of optimality (Proposition 1) relies on assumptions about the positive semidefiniteness of certain matrices; a more rigorous justification or discussion of the limitations would be beneficial.
*   **Experimental Scope:** The experiments are somewhat limited. The results are presented for relatively simple settings. Exploring a wider range of datasets, noise levels, and rank values would build confidence in the algorithm's robustness.  The choice of τ in the real-data experiment seems somewhat ad-hoc.
*   **Clarity in Algorithm Presentation:**  While Algorithm 2 is presented, some of the notations (especially regarding functions *f* and *G*) could be made more explicit and easier to follow. The connection between the general AMP algorithm and the specialized K-means version (Algorithm 3) could be highlighted more clearly.
*   **Missing Details:** The explanation of how the parameter β is utilized for switching between marginalization and MAP estimation could be more detailed.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a novel and potentially impactful algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
*   The connection to AMP theory and the theoretical result regarding optimality are strong points.
*   The empirical results, while limited, are encouraging.
*   The potential for efficiency gains is significant.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical analysis is incomplete.
*   The experimental validation is not exhaustive.
*   Some aspects of the algorithm presentation could be clearer.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution, despite some minor flaws. The novelty of the approach, combined with the potential for efficiency gains, outweighs the shortcomings. The theoretical framework is sound, and the empirical results are promising. I'm willing to overlook the incomplete validation and limited theoretical analysis, given the boldness of the approach and the potential impact. While the paper could benefit from more detailed analysis and more extensive experiments, it presents a compelling new direction for research in low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.

**Recommendation:** Accept. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, primarily focusing on clarifying the algorithm presentation, expanding the experimental validation, and providing a more thorough discussion of the theoretical guarantees. Addressing the comments regarding the justification for the assumptions in Proposition 1 would also be beneficial.
