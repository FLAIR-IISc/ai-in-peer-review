Summary
The paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible, per-row priors on U and V. The model is bilinear, A = U0V0⊤ + W, with additive Gaussian noise scaled as N(0, mτ), and factorized priors p̂U(U)=∏i p̂u(ui), p̂V(V)=∏j p̂v(vj) that allow arbitrary dependence across the r̂ components within each row. A one-parameter “temperature” β unifies posterior marginalization (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). The resulting AMP algorithm yields iterative updates resembling variational Bayes/ICM when the Onsager-like corrections are removed; with them, the authors argue improved performance and fewer poor fixed points. A proposition shows that at any fixed point (under PSD conditions on certain sums of covariance terms), U∞ is a global minimizer of CMAP(·,V∞) and V∞ is a global minimizer of CMAP(U∞,·).

A key application is K-means clustering: by taking a one-hot prior on rows of V and a flat prior on U, the authors derive an AMP variant (AMP-KM) that provably enforces the “exactly-one-cluster” constraint. The AMP assignment step includes correction terms (interpreted as debiasing w.r.t. current assignments and balancing cluster sizes), deviating from Lloyd’s updates. For “maximum accuracy clustering,” they also instantiate an AMP (β=1) that approximates marginal MAP assignments.

Experiments on synthetic data (m=800, N=1600) show that AMP-KM achieves lower K-means loss than Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++, and AMP-MA attains higher assignment accuracy than VB matrix factorization and the K-means baselines. On ORL faces (40-way clustering), AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in 47–48/50 trials but occasionally collapses to one cluster; AMP uses a heuristic residual-based estimator for τ.

Relation to prior work: The paper builds on AMP in compressed sensing and generalized AMP (GAMP), extending iterfac for rank-one and prior bilinear AMP work (e.g., Matsushita & Tanaka; BiG-AMP by Parker et al.) by handling general-rank and explicit MAP (β→∞), and by directly addressing the one-hot constraint in clustering. It also relates to VB matrix factorization and ICM, clarifying their connection to AMP via the presence/absence of Onsager terms.

Strengths
- Methodological: A clear, general AMP derivation for bilinear factorization with per-row priors; the β-trick neatly unifies marginalization and MAP. The specialization to discrete one-hot V yields an elegant, efficient algorithm for K-means that directly enforces exclusivity.
- Insight: Interpretable correction terms in the K-means specialization that debias cluster centers/assignments and encourage balanced clusters; connection to VB/ICM is well explained.
- Practicality: Per-iteration cost O(mN) with small overhead for AMP corrections; closed-form updates in the K-means case.
- Theoretical nugget: Proposition on blockwise optimality at AMP fixed points (under PSD assumptions) and implications about fixed points vs. ICM.
- Empirical: On synthetic and ORL, AMP variants are competitive and often better than common baselines; AMP-MA converges quickly.

Weaknesses
Quality
- The derivation and state evolution are deferred to the supplement; there is no theoretical validation in the main text. Convergence is not guaranteed; oscillations and occasional cluster collapse are observed, with ad-hoc stopping.
- No robustness analysis: sensitivity to τ estimation, damping, initialization, r̂ mismatch, prior misspecification, non-Gaussian noise, or finite-sample effects is missing.
- Empirical baselines are limited: for clustering, no comparisons to EM for GMMs (spherical/diagonal), spectral clustering (normalized cuts), spherical K-means, or modern scalable K-means variants; for bilinear inference, no comparison to BiG-AMP/bilinear GAMP (closely related).
- The use of “true priors” in AMP-MA is unrealistic; performance under prior mismatch is not shown.
- No ablations to quantify the contribution of the AMP corrections vs. Lloyd/ICM components.

Clarity
- Many crucial details (derivation, state evolution, proof of Prop. 1, assumptions ensuring PSD of covariance sums, computation of f/G for common priors) are missing from the main text.
- The claim “does not assume statistical independence among the components of each row” is true within rows, but the paper still imposes independence across rows; this should be clarified.
- τ estimation for K-means is heuristic; its rationale and impact are not analyzed.
- References contain formatting issues/incomplete entries.

Originality
- The β-parameterization for unifying marginal/MAP is known in related contexts, but its application here, plus the explicit treatment of the one-hot constraint within an AMP framework, appears novel. However, the connection/differences to BiG-AMP (which also treats bilinear models) are not thoroughly analyzed.

Significance
- Potentially significant for scalable Bayesian low-rank factorization and clustering, especially the direct one-hot enforcement and interpretable corrections. However, the lack of stronger theory (state evolution, convergence) and broader, rigorous empirical comparisons limits immediate impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide state evolution and convergence analysis (with damping/stepsize rules), at least for Gaussian priors and the K-means specialization; specify conditions under which the PSD assumptions in Prop. 1 hold.
- Add thorough comparisons to BiG-AMP/bilinear GAMP, EM for GMMs (including spherical), spectral clustering, and spherical K-means; include runtime and iteration counts.
- Perform ablations: remove AMP corrections to quantify their effect; test sensitivity to τ estimation and propose a principled estimator or cross-validation scheme.
- Study robustness: prior mismatch, r̂ ≠ r, non-Gaussian noise, initialization strategies (including K-means++), damping, and oscillation mitigation.
- Clarify the independence assumptions (within-row vs. across-row), and provide practical recipes for f/G for common priors (Gaussian, Laplace, Dirichlet-categorical for clustering).
- Clean up references and expand related work discussion.

Pros for acceptance
- Conceptually clean AMP formulation that unifies marginal/MAP and yields a novel, efficient K-means variant with compelling intuition.
- Promising empirical gains with low computational overhead.
- Useful theoretical proposition about blockwise optimality at fixed points.

Cons for acceptance
- Insufficient theory in the main text (no state evolution or convergence guarantees).
- Limited and partly unrealistic experiments; important baselines and ablations are missing.
- Observed instabilities (oscillation, cluster collapse) without principled remedies.

Recommendation: Weak reject
The ideas are appealing and potentially impactful, but the paper needs stronger theoretical support and broader, more rigorous empirical validation (including comparisons to closely related bilinear AMP and modern clustering baselines), plus clarity improvements. I encourage resubmission after addressing these points.
