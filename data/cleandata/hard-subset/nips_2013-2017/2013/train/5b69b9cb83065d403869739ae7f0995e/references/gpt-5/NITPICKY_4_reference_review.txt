Summary
The paper proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on the factors U and V. The authors introduce a “temperature” parameter β to unify marginalization (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), yielding a general algorithm (Alg. 2) that produces posterior means/approximate marginal posteriors for β=1 and approximate MAP solutions for β→∞. As a concrete application, they formulate K-means clustering as a low-rank reconstruction problem with a hard one-hot prior on V and derive a specialized AMP algorithm (Alg. 3) that modifies Lloyd’s assignment step via Onsager-like corrections that depend on current assignments and cluster sizes. They present experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and the ORL faces dataset, showing lower K-means objective and (in many runs) higher accuracy vs. Lloyd/K-means++ and improved accuracy vs. a VBMF baseline for maximum-accuracy clustering.

Relation to prior work: The paper situates itself among AMP/GAMP for sparse linear models, IterFac (rank-one), and bilinear AMP variants (e.g., Parker et al., Krzakala et al.). The stated novelty is (i) extension to handle MAP via β→∞ within a unified AMP framework for general rank r (fixed as m,N→∞), and (ii) a clustering algorithm that enforces the exact one-hot constraint within the AMP framework, with explicit Onsager corrections in the assignment step.

Strengths
- Conceptual unification: The β-parameterization is a clean way to cover posterior mean, marginal MAP, and MAP within one algorithmic template; the link between β→∞ and an ICM-like limit is clearly articulated.
- Clear K-means instantiation: The derivation of the K-means AMP with explicit, interpretable correction terms (+2m/nl for current cluster, −m/nl for cluster size) is elegant and provides intuition about bias correction and class-balance.
- Low per-iteration complexity and symmetry: The updates are O(mN) with simple matrix multiplications, symmetric in U and V, and reduce to standard procedures when Onsager terms are dropped (connecting to variational Bayes/ICM).
- Empirical results: On synthetic data, AMP-KM reduces the K-means objective vs. Lloyd/K-means++ and AMP-MA improves accuracy vs. VBMF-MA. On ORL, AMP-KM usually improves loss and accuracy vs. K-means++ with the same initialization.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and derivation
- Potential scaling typo in Alg. 3: In (17a), Λu^t is written as Λu^t = mτ (V^t)⊤V^t. From (9a), with T=0 in the K-means case, Λu^t should be (1/(mτ))(V^t)⊤V^t. This matters because S^t = (Λu^t)−1 and propagates to (17b). The later re-expression (18b) matches the 1/(mτ) scaling in the distance term, suggesting a sign/scaling mistake in (17a). Please clarify/correct.
- Convergence and correctness: Convergence is assumed. No damping, step-size, or practical convergence guidance is provided, although oscillations are reported, and AMP is known to require damping in non-ideal conditions. Proposition 1 (in the supplement) hinges on Σi S∞i and Σj T∞j being PSD; for β→∞, G∞=(Λ−H)−1 requires Λ−H ≻0, which is not guaranteed for nonconvex priors. The paper asserts “almost always” PSD but does not provide conditions. State evolution is not presented, reducing theoretical support.
- τ estimation: τ is estimated on the fly as m−2N−1∥A−UV⊤∥2F. Given W_ij~N(0,mτ), E[∥W∥2_F]/(m^2N)=τ, so the estimator is sensible asymptotically. However, its use inside the assignment step can create feedback instabilities, especially early or near degenerate solutions; this may explain oscillations and collapsed clusters. No robustness analysis is given.

Scope and empirical evaluation
- Limited baselines: For clustering, comparison is only to Lloyd/K-means++ and a VBMF-MA proxy. Since the data model is Gaussian mixtures with isotropic covariance, EM for GMMs (or spherical k-means), spectral clustering, or SDP relaxations would be informative. For matrix factorization, comparisons to bilinear GAMP/VAMP/OAMP variants, or recent AMP for matrix factorization papers, are missing.
- Narrow data regimes: Synthetic experiments fix m=800, N=1600, τ=0.1, and balanced clusters, varying r only. No study of rank mismatch (r̂≠r), mis-specified priors, imbalanced clusters, anisotropic/noisy features, or robustness to SNR variations. Real-data evaluation shows two trials collapsing to a single cluster—this is a serious failure mode that needs mitigation (e.g., damping, regularization, or safeguards against empty/small clusters).
- Runtime: No wall-clock performance is reported; only iteration counts. As f/G may be costly for general priors (r̂-dimensional integrals/minimizations), practical scalability with r̂ should be discussed.

Clarity and presentation
- Missing derivations: Key derivations, state-evolution, and the proof of Prop. 1 are deferred to the supplement. The main text would benefit from at least a high-level derivation outline of the Onsager terms for the K-means case.
- Notational inconsistencies and minor errors:
  - As noted, likely scaling error in (17a).
  - S^t is used without index in Alg. 3, whereas earlier S^t_i was per-row; clarify that S^t is shared across rows here due to the chosen priors.
  - Several small typographical issues (e.g., “low-rankedness,” stray commas), inconsistent use of ⊤, and some equations with cramped notation.
  - References are inconsistently formatted; some entries lack complete bibliographic details.
- Novelty claims: The claim that this is the first algorithm to directly handle the one-hot constraint in a low-rank reconstruction framework should be nuanced. Bilinear GAMP can incorporate discrete priors; clarifying the precise difference (e.g., fixed-r asymptotics, specific Onsager structure, MAP limit) would strengthen the positioning.

Questions for the authors
1. Please confirm/correct the scaling in Alg. 3 (17a) and propagate the fix to S^t and (17b).
2. What damping/line-search or step-size heuristics improve stability in practice? Can you report sensitivity to damping and τ-estimation schemes?
3. Under what conditions on p̂u, p̂v (e.g., convex −log p̂) do Σi S^t_i and Σj T^t_j remain PSD in the β→∞ limit?
4. How does the method perform with r̂≠r, imbalanced clusters, anisotropic covariances, or mis-specified priors? Please add experiments.
5. Can you compare to EM for GMMs (isotropic/spherical) and to bilinear GAMP/VAMP? Please report wall-clock runtimes.

Evaluation
- Quality: Mixed. The core idea is sound and the K-means specialization is insightful, but theoretical support (SE, convergence) is thin, and there appears to be a scaling typo in a central algorithm. Empirical scope is limited, with observed degeneracy on real data.
- Clarity: Generally readable, but with important derivations deferred, some notation slips, and reference formatting issues.
- Originality: Moderate. The β-trick and bilinear AMP are known; the hard one-hot K-means AMP with explicit bias/size corrections may be new and useful, but the relation to bilinear GAMP needs sharpening.
- Significance: Potentially useful for clustering and low-rank inference, but current experimental and theoretical support are not yet sufficient for NeurIPS.

Pros
- Clean unification of posterior mean/MAP within AMP.
- Intuitive K-means assignment corrections; improved K-means objective empirically.
- Low per-iteration complexity; flexible priors (including correlated components).

Cons
- Possible core typo; missing SE/convergence analysis; fragile behavior (oscillations, collapse).
- Limited baselines and stress tests; no runtime analysis.
- Presentation issues and overstatements of novelty.

Recommendation: Reject in current form. With corrected algorithms, stronger theory (or at least robust stabilization guidelines), broader and fairer empirical comparisons, and improved clarity, this work could be impactful. Confidence: high.
