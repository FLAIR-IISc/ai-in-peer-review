Summary and contributions
This paper derives an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a temperature parameter β that unifies posterior-marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). Compared to prior AMP for low-rank factorization that targets posterior means only (e.g., Matsushita & Tanaka ’12), the present derivation yields a flexible estimator family via f, G operators defined by arbitrary (possibly improper) priors on the factors. A proposition establishes that, at convergence and under mild PSD conditions, each block update attains a global minimum of CMAP holding the other block fixed.

The paper’s second contribution is to specialize the AMP to K-means-type clustering by choosing a one-hot prior on V and an uninformative prior on U. The resulting algorithm reduces to a simple update for U and a modified assignment step for V that includes Onsager-like corrections: a leave-one-out bias correction for points currently assigned to a cluster and a cluster-size balancing term. This yields a principled, AMP-based alternative to Lloyd’s algorithm that explicitly enforces one-hot constraints within the low-rank reconstruction framework. On synthetic data (m=800, N=1600), the AMP K-means variant attains lower K-means loss than Lloyd/K-means++ at moderate-to-large r, and the AMP “maximum-accuracy” variant outperforms a VBMF baseline. On ORL faces, AMP K-means beats K-means++ in most trials on both loss and accuracy.

Relation to prior work
- AMP/GAMP have been used for sparse linear inverse problems and bilinear models; rank-one IterFac and general-rank AMP exist, but largely for posterior means or under restrictive priors. The β-tempered formulation and the explicit MAP-oriented f∞/G∞ operators appear to be a novel and useful extension.
- Prior clustering via low-rank factorization (e.g., NMF-based methods) does not directly impose one-hot constraints within an AMP/BP derivation. The derived assignment rule with size/bias corrections is, to my knowledge, new and conceptually compelling.
- The work is related to bilinear GAMP and dictionary learning AMP, but differs in asymptotics (fixed r as m,N→∞) and in allowing dependent components within rows.

Strengths
- Novelty: The β-parameterized AMP that unifies marginal inference and MAP for general priors is elegant and broadly applicable. The specialization to hard-assignment clustering yields a concrete, simple algorithm with an interpretable Onsager correction that improves on Lloyd’s—a neat insight that could influence clustering practice.
- Conceptual clarity: The mapping from the AMP fixed-point equations to an approximate factorized posterior and to marginal/MAP estimators is clear. The reformulation (Eq. 16) and the coordinate-wise optimality proposition give some theoretical reassurance at convergence.
- Practical impact: Per-iteration O(mN) complexity with O(1) iterations in the SE regime makes the approach potentially very scalable. The assignment correction is easy to implement and could be dropped into existing K-means code.
- Empirical results: On synthetic data the method consistently improves K-means loss and accuracy, with fast convergence; on ORL faces it beats K-means++ in most seeds.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Missing theory in main text: The state evolution (SE) that would justify accuracy and O(1) iterations is deferred. Given the centrality of SE to AMP credibility, a condensed SE description and at least a sketch of its validity assumptions would strengthen the paper. Similarly, the proof of Proposition 1 is relegated to the supplement.
- Convergence/oscillations: The clustering AMP sometimes oscillates; the paper uses ad-hoc stopping (Vt=Vt−1 or Vt=Vt−2). Please discuss damping, adaptive step sizes, or “swept” updates and provide a brief empirical study of their effect on oscillations and performance.
- Fairness and breadth of baselines: For clustering, comparison is limited to Lloyd/K-means++ and VBMF-MA. Since the method optimizes K-means loss, K-means++ is appropriate, but additional baselines would help: EM for GMM (with tied/diagonal covariance), spherical K-means, and spectral clustering. For the “maximum-accuracy” setting, consider reporting results when priors are misspecified, not only the true priors.
- Sensitivity and ablations: The revised assignment (Eq. 18b) adds two terms; an ablation showing the effect of each (self-bias correction and size balancing) would be informative. Please also analyze sensitivity to initialization, to τ estimation, and to r̂ mismatch, including quantitative runtime rather than only iteration counts.
- Notational/typo issues: Some equations appear to have scaling inconsistencies (e.g., Λtu in (17a) vs earlier definitions with 1/(mτ)). Also S notation switches between St and Sti; please unify. References are often incomplete; please fix.
- Scope: The Gaussian noise model and the τ parameter are borrowed from a generative setting; in K-means practice the noise model is not specified. The heuristic online τ estimate is sensible, but a short justification or robustness test would help.

Quality: The core derivation appears sound and consistent with AMP literature; however, the lack of SE and convergence treatment in the main text weakens rigor. Experiments are promising but somewhat narrow; runtime and broader baselines would strengthen claims.

Clarity: Generally well-written and organized; the algorithms are presented clearly. Some notation/scaling issues and incomplete references should be fixed. A brief, high-level derivation sketch for Algorithm 3 would help readers who won’t consult the supplement.

Originality: High. The β-tempered extension of AMP for matrix factorization and the hard-assignment clustering AMP with principled corrections are both novel and interesting.

Significance: Potentially high. If stabilized and adopted, the corrected assignment step could become a drop-in improvement for K-means, and the general β-AMP framework could impact a range of structured matrix factorization problems.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong conceptual novelty with a clean, general framework (β-AMP) and a concrete, impactful application (K-means with Onsager corrections).
- Empirical gains over widely used baselines with minimal overhead.
- Clear path for broader applicability to other priors/constraints.

Cons against acceptance
- Missing SE analysis and limited theoretical guarantees in main text.
- Some convergence fragility and ad-hoc stopping.
- Limited experimental breadth and minor fairness concerns.

Recommendation
Accept (leaning). The paper advances AMP for low-rank factorization in a meaningful way and introduces a novel, principled improvement to a foundational algorithm (K-means). Despite some missing theoretical and empirical breadth, the ideas are bold and likely to spur follow-up work. My enthusiasm stems from the novelty and the elegant link between AMP and clustering; I encourage the authors to strengthen the theory section and broaden experiments in the final version.

Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
