- Paper develops an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction using a β-parameterized posterior unifying MMSE (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference.
- AMP updates couple Gaussian denoisers with Onsager corrections and extend bilinear AMP to MAP and general row-wise priors.
- Instantiates framework for clustering by enforcing one-hot rows in V, producing an AMP variant of K‑means with Onsager-corrected assignment rules.
- Empirical results on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and ORL faces show improvements over Lloyd’s K‑means and a VBMF baseline in many cases.
- Framework is closely related to AMP/GAMP and bilinear extensions (IterFac, BiG-AMP); novelty vis-à-vis max-sum BiG-AMP and EM-BiG-AMP needs clarification.
- Removing Onsager terms recovers VBMF/ICM-like schemes; the paper emphasizes the importance of Onsager corrections and fixed-point properties.
- Casting K‑means as constrained low-rank factorization is classical; novelty lies in deriving leave-one-out and cluster-size correction terms via AMP.
- Methodological strength: clean derivation supporting both β regimes and non-factorized row priors, useful in practice.
- Clustering strength: derived assignment rule provides intuitive bias and size regularization corrections.
- Theoretical insight: Proposition 1 gives coordinate-wise global optimality at fixed points and links to Bethe approximations.
- Empirical strength: lower K‑means loss on synthetic high-d mixtures and higher accuracy for AMP-MA vs VBMF-MA; ORL shows improvements in most runs.
- Claim that prior AMP “can only treat posterior means” is too strong; must position β-parameterization relative to max-sum BiG-AMP and EM-BiG-AMP.
- State evolution (SE) is advertised but omitted; a summary of assumptions, recursions, and empirical SE validation is needed.
- Convergence and stability issues: AMP oscillations acknowledged but no discussion of damping, adaptive steps, or guarantees for positivity of key sums; practical guidance missing.
- Computational cost underreported: denoisers require r̂-dimensional integrals/optimizations per row/column; need per-iteration constant details, priors for closed forms, and wall-clock times.
- τ estimation and scale sensitivity: τ is not in classical K‑means, the plug-in estimator is ad hoc and may cause degenerate clustering; joint estimation or sensitivity analysis is required.
- Empirical comparison gaps: using true priors for AMP-MA may bias results; compare to GMM-EM, multi-restart K‑means++, and report runtime/iterations and distributions of accuracy/loss.
- Rank mismatch and robustness untested: experiments fix r̂=r; need evaluation under over/underestimated rank, non-Gaussian noise, and non-uniform cluster sizes.
- Key technical details deferred: SE, convergence conditions, assumptions for Proposition 1, and handling of improper priors need to be included.
- Clarity suggestions: collect AMP derivation assumptions, summarize complexity, clarify τ’s role/estimation, add pseudo-code with damping options, and fix minor typos.
- Originality: moderate—β-unification and K‑means AMP instantiation are interesting but MAP capability is close to existing (Bi)GAMP MAP methods.
- Significance: potentially useful practical alternative to Lloyd’s K‑means for high-d settings, but impact limited without SE, robustness studies, and broader baselines.
- Suggested experiments: compare to GMM-EM and multi-restart K‑means++, ablation removing Onsager terms, study damping effects, rank mismatch, and τ robustness.
- Pros: unified AMP formulation for MMSE/MAP with general priors; novel AMP-based K‑means assignment; promising empirical gains and low per-iteration cost.
- Cons: unclear novelty vs existing MAP (Bi)GAMP, missing SE and convergence guidance, ad-hoc stopping, limited baselines, and fragile τ handling.
- Recommendation: Weak reject—promising ideas but needs stronger positioning, SE/convergence discussion, and more robust experiments.
