

# Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

## Summary
This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous AMP-based approaches to handle both marginalization and MAP estimation problems in a unified framework. The authors then apply this algorithm to K-means clustering, presenting a novel method that directly addresses the constraint that each datum should be assigned to exactly one cluster. The work builds on the AMP framework developed for compressed sensing (Donoho et al., 2009) and extends it to matrix reconstruction (Matsushita & Tanaka, 2012), while also relating to variational Bayes methods for matrix factorization (Salakhutdinov & Mnih, 2008).

## Strengths
1. **Technical contribution:** The paper provides a significant extension to existing AMP algorithms by handling both marginalization and MAP estimation, whereas previous work (e.g., Matsushita & Tanaka, 2012) could only treat posterior mean estimation. The unified framework with the β parameter is elegant and well-motivated.

2. **Novel application to clustering:** The application to clustering is particularly valuable as it directly addresses a key constraint (each datum to exactly one cluster) that previous low-rank matrix reconstruction approaches for clustering (e.g., Xu et al., 2003) did not properly handle. The AMP-based clustering algorithm is theoretically well-grounded and empirically validated.

3. **Comprehensive experiments:** The numerical experiments on both artificial and real datasets (ORL face database) are thorough and convincing. The results demonstrate clear advantages over standard methods in terms of clustering quality (accuracy and normalized K-means loss) and convergence speed, particularly for high-dimensional data.

4. **Theoretical insight:** Proposition 1 provides an important theoretical guarantee about the optimality of fixed points, showing that AMP fixed points are also fixed points of the ICM algorithm. This insight helps explain why AMP might avoid bad local minima.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited theoretical analysis:** While the paper assumes convergence of the algorithm, it lacks a rigorous convergence analysis, which is particularly important for a method based on message passing. The authors should provide conditions under which convergence is guaranteed.

2. **Incomplete comparison with state-of-the-art:** The comparison is limited to Lloyd's K-means and K-means++, but doesn't include more recent clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering, DBSCAN, or other modern approaches) that might provide better baselines for high-dimensional data.

3. **Parameter sensitivity analysis missing:** The paper doesn't thoroughly investigate how sensitive the algorithm is to hyperparameters (e.g., τ, β), which is crucial for practical application. The authors mention using a temporary estimate for τ, but don't analyze how this choice affects performance.

4. **Insufficient theoretical justification for AMP advantage:** While the paper claims AMP can better reflect dependencies between variables than variational Bayes, it lacks a rigorous theoretical justification for why this should lead to improved performance in clustering applications.

5. **Limited discussion of computational complexity:** The paper states the computational complexity is O(mN), but doesn't provide a detailed comparison with other methods' complexity, particularly for large-scale problems where the O(mN) complexity might become a bottleneck.

## Overall Assessment
This is a well-written paper with a strong technical contribution that makes a meaningful extension to the AMP framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction. The application to clustering is novel and well-justified, with compelling experimental results demonstrating clear advantages over standard approaches. The paper addresses an important gap in the literature by properly handling the constraint that each datum must be assigned to exactly one cluster.

The main weaknesses are the lack of rigorous convergence analysis and insufficient comparison with more recent clustering methods. However, these are not fatal flaws in the paper's contribution. The theoretical insights provided (particularly Proposition 1) and the empirical results are strong enough to support acceptance.

## Recommendation
**Accept** - This paper makes a significant contribution to the field by extending AMP to handle both marginalization and MAP estimation for low-rank matrix reconstruction, and then applying this to clustering in a novel way. The experimental results are convincing, and the theoretical foundation is solid. The weaknesses identified are important but can be addressed in the camera-ready version.

## Suggestions for Improvement
1. Include a rigorous convergence analysis for the proposed AMP algorithm.
2. Add a comparison with more recent clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering, deep clustering methods) to provide a more complete picture of the state-of-the-art.
3. Conduct a thorough analysis of parameter sensitivity, particularly for τ and β.
4. Expand the theoretical discussion to provide a more rigorous justification for why AMP should outperform variational Bayes in this specific application.
5. Provide a more detailed comparison of computational complexity with other methods, especially for large-scale problems.

The paper is well-suited for NIPS as it combines theoretical insights with practical applications, addresses a significant problem in machine learning, and makes a clear contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
