This paper proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means clustering. The core idea is extending existing AMP formulations to handle MAP estimation and leveraging this for both reconstruction and clustering, framing the latter as a special case of the former. While the topic is relevant to NIPS, and the extension of AMP to MAP estimation is a potentially valuable contribution, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor flaws and lacks sufficient clarity, hindering a positive assessment.

The introduction, while attempting to situate the work, is somewhat verbose and lacks concise motivation. The references cited ([1, 2]) are standard examples and don't justify the length devoted to setting the stage. The statement about differing from recent work ([13, 14]) regarding the scaling of the rank is presented without sufficient detail to understand its implications. 

Section 2.1 defines the problem setting, which is standard. However, the justification for the ‘m’ factor in the noise variance feels ad-hoc and isn't thoroughly explained beyond allowing for proper scaling in the limit. The leap from assuming a specific conditional distribution p(A|U0,V0) to discussing arbitrary prior distributions p̂U and p̂V feels abrupt. The description of marginalization and MAP problems is mathematically correct but could benefit from more intuitive explanation.

Section 2.2 formulating clustering as a low-rank reconstruction problem is reasonable. However, stating that the proposed algorithm is the *first* to directly address the single cluster assignment constraint feels overstated; previous work, while perhaps not framed identically, certainly incorporates this constraint implicitly.

Section 3 adequately reviews related work, but lacks critical comparison. Merely stating differences without explaining their impact is insufficient. The discussion of variational Bayes is superficial.

Section 4.1, the core of the paper, is where the majority of the problems lie. The introduction of the one-parameter extension (β) is reasonable, but the subsequent derivation of the AMP algorithm is presented with insufficient explanation. Many equations appear without clear justification or intuitive connection to the underlying principles of AMP. The functions f and G are defined without sufficient explanation of their properties or how they are practically computed for different priors p̂u and p̂v. The claim that the algorithm is "almost symmetric" is misleading, as the update equations still differ. The discussion of Proposition 1 feels tacked on and lacks connection to the practical aspects of the algorithm; the assumption that ∑m i=1 S ∞ i and ∑N j=1 T ∞ j are positive semidefinite is stated without sufficient justification.

Section 4.2 discusses properties, but again suffers from hand-waving arguments. The claim of low computational cost (O(mN)) is only valid under specific assumptions and requires more detailed analysis. The comparison to variational Bayes feels self-serving and lacks empirical support.

Section 4.3 lacks clarity. The justification for the specific parameterization of the prior distributions for clustering is weak, and the rationale for estimating τ within the algorithm isn’t well explained.

Section 5 presents numerical experiments. The experimental setup is reasonable, but the presentation of results is insufficient. Figures 1 and 2 lack proper labeling and detailed captions. The interpretation of the results is also lacking depth. Why does the AMP algorithm outperform K-means++? What specific aspects of the AMP algorithm contribute to this improvement? These questions remain unanswered. The claim of “significant” outperformance is not adequately substantiated. The statement about the AMP algorithm yielding empty clusters highlights a potential instability issue that should be addressed.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* **Lack of Clarity:** The paper is riddled with unexplained equations, ad-hoc justifications, and a generally unclear presentation.
* **Insufficient Detail:** The derivation of the AMP algorithm and the explanation of its properties are too superficial.
* **Overstated Claims:** The claims of novelty and superiority are often overstated without sufficient evidence.
* **Weak Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental results are presented inadequately, and the analysis lacks depth.
* **Numerous Minor Flaws:** Throughout the paper, there are a multitude of minor flaws that, cumulatively, detract from its overall quality.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* **Potentially Valuable Contribution:** The extension of AMP to MAP estimation is a potentially useful theoretical result.
* **Relevant Topic:** Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering are important areas of research.
* **Interesting Application:** Applying AMP to K-means clustering is an interesting idea.



Overall, while the paper addresses a relevant problem and proposes a potentially valuable contribution, its numerous flaws, lack of clarity, and weak experimental evaluation outweigh its strengths. I recommend **rejection**. Significant revisions addressing the points outlined above would be required for reconsideration.
