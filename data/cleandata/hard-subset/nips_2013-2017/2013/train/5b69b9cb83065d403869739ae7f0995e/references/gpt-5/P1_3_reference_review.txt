Summary and relation to prior work
The paper develops an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on the factors. The central idea is to introduce a one-parameter family of posteriors p̂(U,V|A; β) ∝ p̂(U,V|A)^β that unifies marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). The resulting AMP iterations produce factor-wise Gaussian approximations with “Onsager” corrections, and can handle general (possibly improper) priors p̂u, p̂v that need not be componentwise independent within a row. The authors show how to specialize the algorithm to K-means clustering by choosing a discrete prior p̂v supported on one-hot vectors and uniform (improper) p̂u. This yields an AMP variant of K-means with correction terms interpretable as a leave-one-out bias correction and a size regularizer. They prove a fixed-point optimality property (Proposition 1): at convergence, each block is a global minimizer of the corresponding partially minimized objective under a mild PSD condition.

The work relates to: AMP/GAMP in compressed sensing (Donoho-Maleki-Montanari; Rangan), rank-one IterFac (Rangan & Fletcher), a prior AMP for low-rank matrices that handled only posterior means, BiG-AMP and bilinear GAMP variants, and VB matrix factorization. The clustering application connects to Lloyd’s algorithm as ICM and to K-means++ seeding. The authors emphasize their large-system limit differs by fixing rank r (not r/m) and by allowing intra-row dependence in priors.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The AMP construction follows standard Bethe/free-energy approximations; the β-unification and specializations are consistent with prior AMP literature. The fixed-point optimality proposition is sensible and useful; proof details are deferred to the supplement. The K-means specialization is derived transparently and yields an intuitive correction term.
- Support for claims: Synthetic experiments (high-dim, r varied) and a real dataset (ORL faces) show improvements over Lloyd’s and K-means++ in K-means loss and accuracy, and over VBMF for maximum-accuracy clustering. However, evaluation breadth is limited (one real dataset; balanced clusters only; Gaussian/spherical noise only). No state evolution or rigorous convergence characterization is provided, and oscillations are observed and handled ad hoc by a termination rule. For maximum-accuracy clustering, experiments use the true priors, which favors Bayes-optimal approaches.
- Completeness: The main derivations are said to be in the supplement. Practical details for computing f and G, damping, and numerical stabilization are only lightly discussed. Time/complexity comparisons are not reported empirically.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized. The algorithm is presented compactly with helpful intuition (e.g., the bias correction in K-means). Notational load is high but standard for AMP papers. Helpful clarifications: when f/G have closed forms vs requiring integration/optimization; how τ is estimated in practice; and when the PSD condition in Prop. 1 is guaranteed. A brief “recipe” section for implementing f/G for common priors would aid reproducibility.

Originality
- Methodological: Extending low-rank AMP beyond posterior means to handle MAP via β→∞, general intra-row priors, and a clean unification of marginal/MAP estimators is novel and useful. The explicit K-means specialization that exactly enforces one-hot assignments with AMP-style Onsager corrections appears new and interesting.
- Relation to prior AMP work: The paper distinguishes itself from earlier bi-linear AMP variants and IterFac; pointers are appropriate, though a deeper comparison to BiG-AMP (capabilities, assumptions, and empirical behavior) would strengthen the positioning.

Significance
The work has two potentially impactful contributions:
- A flexible AMP framework for Bayesian low-rank factorization accommodating general priors and MAP/marginal tasks, which could be reused beyond clustering.
- A practically appealing K-means variant that consistently improves K-means loss on high-dimensional data with negligible extra cost per iteration.

That said, the empirical evidence is limited, and theoretical guarantees (state evolution, precise conditions for convergence) are absent here. With stronger evaluation and some theory, this line could influence both AMP-based matrix estimation and practical clustering.

Strengths
- Clear generalization of low-rank AMP to a β-parametrized family covering posterior means and MAP.
- Ability to handle non-factorized intra-row priors.
- Intuitive and novel K-means AMP algorithm with interpretable corrections.
- Useful fixed-point optimality guarantee (blockwise global minima).
- Competitive empirical results on synthetic and ORL data; fast convergence.

Weaknesses
- No state evolution analysis or convergence guarantees; observed oscillations addressed heuristically.
- Narrow empirical evaluation: one real dataset, balanced/spherical clusters, no imbalanced or misspecified-r studies, no robustness to prior/noise mismatch.
- Comparisons miss some strong baselines: EM for spherical GMMs, spectral clustering, modern K-means accelerations, and BiG-AMP variants on the same tasks.
- Practical computation of f/G for general priors is underspecified; no discussion of damping/stepsizes.
- The “small-cluster preference” term may bias solutions with imbalanced clusters; no ablations to isolate impact of Onsager terms.

Questions/suggestions for authors
- Provide at least a sketch and empirical validation of state evolution for simple priors; discuss conditions ensuring PSD of ∑S_i and ∑T_j and convergence/damping strategies.
- Add ablations: remove Onsager terms to quantify gains over VB/ICM; study sensitivity to τ estimation; assess imbalanced clusters and misspecified rank r̂.
- Compare to EM for isotropic GMMs, spectral clustering, and BiG-AMP on the same datasets; report wall-clock time and iterations.
- Demonstrate other priors (e.g., sparse/nonnegative) to highlight generality, and discuss how f/G are implemented (closed-form, proximal maps, or numerical routines).
- Explore a β-annealing schedule for MAP and report its effect on convergence/quality.
- Consider additional real datasets (e.g., text/image clustering) and non-Gaussian noise.

Broader impact
If stabilized and broadened, the proposed AMP framework could be a practical tool for structured low-rank estimation and clustering with principled corrections and low per-iteration cost, complementing VB/EM approaches.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper presents a novel and promising extension of AMP with an elegant K-means instantiation and encouraging results, but would benefit from stronger empirical breadth, comparisons, and at least partial theoretical validation. Confidence: medium-high.
