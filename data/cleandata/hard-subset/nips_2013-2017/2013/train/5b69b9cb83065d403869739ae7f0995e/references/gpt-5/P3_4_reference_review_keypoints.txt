- Paper develops an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a β parameter bridging MMSE (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).
- Algorithm includes Onsager-type corrections and reduces to VB/ICM when corrections removed; fixed-point proposition gives coordinatewise global optimality under a PSD assumption.
- Instantiates framework for clustering using one-hot priors on V to produce an AMP variant of K-means with bias-corrected assignment steps.
- Empirically, AMP-KM improves K-means loss on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and AMP-MA outperforms VBMF on accuracy; gains also shown on ORL faces with fast convergence.
- Work builds on prior AMP/BiG-AMP lines (IterFac, BiG-AMP) and claims novelties: unified β-parametrization, fixed-r derivation with intra-row dependencies, and direct hard one-hot treatment.
- Strength: conceptual clarity—β unifies MMSE and MAP and yields interpretable f/G updates (means/covariances vs proximal maps/Hessians).
- Strength: algorithmic insight—assignment rule derives leave-one-out bias correction and cluster-size regularization from Onsager terms rather than heuristics.
- Strength: practical efficiency—per-iteration complexity O(mN) with small Onsager overhead and closed-form updates for discrete priors.
- Strength: theory—fixed-point coordinatewise optimality clarifies relation to ICM/VB and potential to avoid poor local minima.
- Strength: empirics—demonstrated reductions in K-means loss and improved accuracy in synthetic and one real dataset.
- Weakness: limited theoretical support—state evolution omitted and PSD conditions for proposition not established; practical oscillations handled ad hoc.
- Weakness: unclear relation to BiG-AMP/max-sum GAMP—MAP limit appears similar and needs precise positioning and citation/contrast.
- Weakness: narrow experimental scope—only isotropic balanced Gaussians and one real dataset; missing regimes like mis-specified r, unbalanced clusters, non-spherical clusters, noise models, and missing data.
- Weakness: missing baselines—no comparisons to EM for GMMs, spherical K-means, spectral clustering, or scalable modern clustering methods.
- Weakness: sensitivity—τ estimation from residuals and assignment correction scaling (m/n_l) may cause instability or empty clusters; sensitivity not analyzed.
- Weakness: clarity gaps—several derivations deferred, conditions for identities like G∞=(Λ−H)^{-1) not stated, and PSD claims lack justification.
- Quality assessment: technically consistent with AMP principles but weakened by missing state evolution, convergence guarantees, and limited ablations.
- Clarity assessment: generally well organized, but needs a comparison table vs VBMF/ICM/BiG-AMP, a derivation sketch of Onsager terms for clustering, and discussion of damping/stepsize.
- Originality: moderate—β trick and MAP limit are known, but one-hot AMP K-means and non-factorized row components are interesting contributions.
- Significance: potentially important for large-scale clustering if robustness is demonstrated; broader impact depends on wider empirical validation and clearer novelty claims.
- Key constructive suggestions: add state evolution for a representative prior, compare to GMM-EM/spectral/spherical K-means with runtime, analyze τ sensitivity and damping, study mis-specified r and unbalanced clusters, clarify relation to max-sum BiG-AMP, and ablate correction terms.
- Pros for acceptance: principled AMP derivation, novel K-means instantiation, empirical gains, low overhead, and useful fixed-point proposition.
- Cons for acceptance: weak theoretical backing, narrow experiments, missing baselines, and ambiguous novelty relative to existing max-sum BiG-AMP.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept pending clarification of relation to max-sum BiG-AMP, addition of a GMM-EM baseline, and a brief state-evolution validation; otherwise borderline reject.
