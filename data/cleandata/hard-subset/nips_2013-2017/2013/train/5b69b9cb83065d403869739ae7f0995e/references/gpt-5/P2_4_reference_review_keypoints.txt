- Develops an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a β-parameterized posterior unifying marginalization (β=1) and MAP (β→∞); Alg.2 resembles VB with Onsager corrections and Alg.3 applies to K-means with one-hot constraints and bias correction.

- Claims novelty in explicitly handling MAP via a max-sum limit and in enforcing one-hot constraints inside AMP for a new K-means assignment rule.

- Derivation follows standard AMP approximations; Prop.1 gives coordinate-wise global optimality of fixed points under PSD conditions.

- Convergence is assumed; state evolution (SE) is claimed possible but omitted and not empirically validated.

- τ estimation heuristic in K-means instantiation is ad hoc and sensitivity to τ is not studied.

- Some convergence issues observed empirically (oscillations, occasional collapse to one cluster in 2/50 trials).

- Synthetic experiments show clear gains in K-means loss and clustering accuracy (AMP variants vs Lloyd/K-means++); ORL faces results show consistent improvements.

- Baselines are limited; missing comparisons to spectral clustering, spherical K-means, EM for equal-covariance GMMs, and BiG-AMP (sum-product and max-sum); runtime and memory not reported.

- Framework claims generality for rich priors, but experiments only use one-hot or Gaussian priors; no tests with sparse/nonnegative priors or missing data.

- Paper is well organized and readable; AMP updates and K-means specialization are clearly derived and interpreted.

- Need to state precise assumptions under which G∞ = (Λ − H)^{-1} holds and when Λ − H is invertible.

- Must discuss computational/memory complexity beyond O(mN) because storing S_i and T_j costs O((m+N) r̂^2), which can be significant.

- Clarify the unusual noise scaling Var(Wij)=mτ, explain how τ is estimated, and discuss its impact on assignments.

- Summarize supplement’s derivation and conditions for the Onsager terms to improve clarity.

- Originality: K-means one-hot AMP appears novel and insightful; MAP-AMP novelty relative to max-sum GAMP/BiG-AMP is plausible but not crisply positioned.

- Significance: potentially impactful if robust (better K-means loss and interpretable corrections), but current empirical scope and missing SE/convergence guidance limit theoretical and practical impact.

- Suggest comparing against sum-product and max-sum BiG-AMP and clearly articulating differences in assumptions, denoisers, Onsager terms, and SE regime.

- Recommend adding damping/stepsize strategies and reporting their effects on convergence and collapse failures.

- Recommend adding clustering baselines (spectral, spherical K-means, EM GMM) and reporting wall-clock/runtime.

- Recommend demonstrating at least one nontrivial prior (e.g., sparse or nonnegative factors) to substantiate generality.

- Suggest including a brief SE derivation or empirical SE validation; otherwise temper SE-related claims.

- Recommend analyzing sensitivity to τ estimation and initialization and providing practical guidelines.

- Pros: clear principled derivation unifying marginalization and MAP; novel bias-corrected K-means assignment; low per-iteration complexity and coordinate-wise optimality; good exposition linking to VB/ICM.

- Cons: missing comparisons to related bilinear AMP, no SE/convergence analysis, limited empirical scope (one real dataset, few baselines, no runtime), use of true priors optimistic, sensitivity issues and occasional catastrophic collapse.

- Recommendation: Borderline accept — promising but needs clearer positioning vs BiG-AMP/max-sum GAMP, expanded empirical comparisons (including runtime), and SE/convergence analysis or robustification to be stronger.
