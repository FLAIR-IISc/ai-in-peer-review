

# Review: Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing

## Summary
This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous work to handle both marginalization (posterior mean) and MAP estimation. The authors derive a unified algorithm that can incorporate various structural properties of the low-rank factors through different prior distributions. The second contribution applies this algorithm to K-means clustering, providing a novel method that directly handles the constraint that each datum must be assigned to exactly one cluster. The paper demonstrates the algorithm's efficiency (O(mN) per iteration) and superior performance compared to existing methods on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

## Strengths

**Quality (High):** The paper is technically sound and well-supported. The authors provide a clear theoretical foundation for their AMP-based approach, including Proposition 1 that establishes the convergence properties of the algorithm for the MAP problem. The numerical experiments are comprehensive and well-designed, evaluating performance across multiple metrics (K-means loss, accuracy, convergence speed) on both synthetic data (with 500 problem instances) and the ORL Faces dataset. The comparison against multiple baselines (Lloyd's K-means, K-means++, VBMF-MA, AMP-MA) is fair and thorough.

**Clarity (High):** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The problem setting is clearly defined, and the algorithmic derivation follows a logical progression. The authors provide detailed explanations of the key concepts (e.g., the β-extension of the posterior distribution) and make the technical content accessible to readers familiar with Bayesian inference and AMP. The algorithmic descriptions (Algorithm 2, 3) are precise and complete, with sufficient detail for reproducibility.

**Originality (High):** The paper makes two significant contributions. First, it extends previous AMP algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction to handle both marginalization and MAP estimation, which is a notable advancement over [9] that could only treat posterior mean estimation. Second, it applies this to clustering in a novel way that directly addresses the constraint of exact cluster assignment, differentiating it from previous approaches like [10, 11] that did not handle this constraint within the low-rank reconstruction framework. The authors clearly articulate how their work differs from related work, particularly in the handling of the rank parameter and assumptions about component independence.

**Significance (High):** The problem of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering is fundamental to many applications in data analysis. The proposed method offers both computational efficiency (O(mN) per iteration) and superior performance compared to existing approaches. The experimental results demonstrate clear advantages in both synthetic and real-world settings, with the AMP-based clustering algorithm outperforming Lloyd's K-means in 48/50 trials on the ORL Faces dataset. The paper advances the state of the art by providing a more efficient and effective solution to a well-studied problem.

## Weaknesses

**Limited discussion of limitations:** While the paper acknowledges the approximation nature of AMP, it doesn't thoroughly discuss the specific limitations of their approach. For instance, it doesn't explore how sensitive the algorithm is to the choice of prior distributions or the initialization of cluster assignments. The authors note that the AMP algorithm for MAP can sometimes have increasing CMAP values during iterations but don't provide guidance on how to handle this.

**Insufficient comparison with related clustering work:** The claim that their method "directly deals with the constraint that each datum should be assigned to exactly one cluster" is not sufficiently differentiated from previous work. While [10, 11] are cited, the paper doesn't clearly explain how their approach differs from these in handling the cluster assignment constraint. A more detailed comparison would strengthen the novelty claim.

**Limited exploration of hyperparameters:** The paper uses a heuristic for estimating τ in the clustering algorithm but doesn't systematically explore how sensitive the algorithm is to this parameter or other hyperparameters. A more thorough analysis of parameter sensitivity would strengthen the practical applicability of the method.

## Recommendation

This is a strong paper that makes significant contributions to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The technical content is sound, the experiments are comprehensive, and the writing is clear and well-organized. The proposed AMP algorithm advances the state of the art by providing a more efficient and effective solution to a fundamental problem.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- Novel extension of AMP to handle both marginalization and MAP estimation for low-rank matrix reconstruction
- Clear theoretical justification with Proposition 1
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation showing superior performance over multiple baselines
- Well-written and clearly organized paper
- Practical significance for clustering applications

**Arguments against acceptance (minor):**
- Some limitations could be better discussed (sensitivity to initialization, prior choice)
- The novelty claim regarding cluster assignment constraint could be more clearly differentiated from previous work

The paper meets all NIPS review criteria at a high level. The quality is excellent, the clarity is outstanding, the originality is significant, and the significance is clear. The minor weaknesses identified do not detract from the overall contribution of the paper, which represents a meaningful advance in the field.

**Recommendation: Accept**
