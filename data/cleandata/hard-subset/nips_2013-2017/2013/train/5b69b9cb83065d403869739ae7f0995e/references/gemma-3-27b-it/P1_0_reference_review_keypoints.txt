- Proposes an AMP algorithm extended to MAP estimation for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction.  
- Frames K-means clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem and applies the AMP method to clustering.  
- Positions the work within prior AMP and low-rank/clustering literature and cites relevant recent advances.  
- Novel extension: MAP-capable AMP broadens applicability beyond posterior means.  
- Unified theoretical perspective: clustering seen as a special low-rank reconstruction with exclusive cluster-assignment constraints.  
- Claims computational efficiency: linear complexity and potentially fast convergence for AMP.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on synthetic and face-recognition data showing promising performance versus Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++.  
- Theoretical grounding: Proposition 1 claims optimality of the AMP fixed point.  
- Presentation is generally clear and the algorithm is well laid out.  
- Limited theoretical depth: convergence properties and limitations are not thoroughly analyzed; state evolution is relegated to supplements.  
- Experimental scope is narrow: parameter sensitivity (e.g., τ) and broader dataset/algorithm comparisons are missing.  
- Algorithm shows sensitivity to initialization; relies on K-means++ and needs more discussion of this dependence.  
- Missing direct experimental comparison to variational Bayes (VB) methods.  
- Notation can be confusing due to multiple indices (e.g., u0,i, v0,j).  
- For acceptance: the paper is novel, technically sound, and empirically promising.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient theoretical analysis, limited experiments, and initialization sensitivity leave open robustness questions.  
- Overall: solid contribution but needs deeper theory and broader experiments—reviewer rates as Weak Accept.  
- Recommended improvements: strengthen convergence/state-evolution analysis in main text, expand experiments and baselines (spectral clustering, DBSCAN, VB), systematically study initialization sensitivity, and clarify notation.
