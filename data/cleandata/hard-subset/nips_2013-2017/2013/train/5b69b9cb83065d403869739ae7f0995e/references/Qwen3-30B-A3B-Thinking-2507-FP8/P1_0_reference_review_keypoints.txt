- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles both marginalization (posterior mean) and MAP estimation, and applies it to K-means clustering enforcing one-cluster-per-datum, outperforming standard baselines.

- Theoretical soundness: clear derivation from the posterior, Proposition 1 linking AMP fixed points to local minima, and a well-explained connection to variational Bayes.

- Novel clustering application: AMP formulation properly enforces the one-cluster-per-datum constraint and yields superior empirical performance.

- Comprehensive experimental evaluation on synthetic data and the ORL face dataset, comparing against five algorithms using appropriate metrics (K-means loss, accuracy, convergence speed).

- Clear presentation with helpful notation and pseudocode, and an accessible explanation of the link between matrix reconstruction and clustering.

- Limited convergence analysis: Proposition 1 is useful but the paper lacks a full discussion of convergence conditions and asymptotic behavior.

- Practical implementation details are sparse: the τ parameter estimation is only briefly justified and its impact on performance is unclear.

- Incomplete comparison with recent AMP literature (e.g., dictionary learning, generalized observation models); relationship to those works needs clarification.

- Computational complexity claim of O(mN) is asserted but not supported with runtime measurements or empirical evidence versus variational Bayes.

- Recommendation: accept — novel extension of AMP, strong theoretical foundations, thorough experiments, and practical value.

- Pro-accept points: novelty in handling marginalization+MAP, first to properly enforce one-cluster-per-datum in this framework, solid theory, comprehensive validation, and superior results.

- Con-accept points: limited convergence analysis, insufficient justification for τ estimation, lacking detailed comparison to recent AMP work, and missing runtime validation.
