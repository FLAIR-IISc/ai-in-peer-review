

# Review: Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing

## Summary

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, with a novel application to K-means clustering. The authors extend existing AMP algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction to handle general-rank matrices and both marginalization and MAP estimation problems within a unified framework using a parameter β. The key innovation is applying this AMP framework to clustering, where they directly handle the constraint that each datum must be assigned to exactly one cluster—a problem that previous low-rank reconstruction approaches for clustering have not adequately addressed. The paper provides theoretical justification for their algorithm and demonstrates through extensive experiments that their AMP-based clustering outperforms standard methods like Lloyd's K-means and K-means++ in both artificial and real-world datasets.

## Quality

The paper is technically sound and well-supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results. The derivation of the AMP algorithm follows a logical progression from the Bayesian formulation of the problem, and the authors provide a clear proof (Proposition 1) showing that fixed points of their AMP algorithm correspond to fixed points of the ICM algorithm. The numerical experiments are comprehensive, with 500 problem instances for artificial data and 50 trials on real data, providing strong statistical evidence for their claims. The paper appropriately acknowledges limitations (e.g., convergence not guaranteed in general) and provides a thorough comparison with relevant baselines including Lloyd's K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes matrix factorization.

## Clarity

The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written. The problem setup is precisely defined, the derivation of the AMP algorithm is presented in a step-by-step manner, and the connection to clustering is made transparent. The notation is consistent throughout, and the experimental results are presented with appropriate figures and statistical measures. The comparison with related work (Section 3) is particularly strong, clearly articulating how this work differs from previous AMP algorithms [9, 13, 14] and variational Bayes approaches [4, 5]. The writing style is accessible to readers familiar with matrix factorization and AMP methods.

## Originality

The paper makes several significant original contributions. First, it extends AMP to handle general-rank matrix reconstruction with flexibility for both marginalization and MAP estimation, unlike previous work [9] that only handled posterior mean estimation. Second, the unified framework using parameter β to interpolate between marginalization and MAP problems is a novel conceptual contribution. Most importantly, the application to clustering with direct handling of the "exactly one cluster" constraint is a clear advance over previous approaches [10, 11], which typically used indirect methods to enforce this constraint. The paper carefully situates these contributions within the literature, acknowledging the related work while clearly differentiating their approach.

## Significance

The significance of this work is substantial. Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering are fundamental problems with wide applications in machine learning, and the proposed AMP algorithm provides a computationally efficient and theoretically grounded approach that outperforms existing methods. The paper demonstrates this through both artificial and real-world experiments (ORL Database of Faces), showing improvements in both accuracy and convergence speed. The efficiency (O(mN) per iteration) and theoretical guarantees (via state evolution) make this algorithm particularly attractive for large-scale applications. The authors' demonstration that AMP can directly handle the cluster assignment constraint represents a significant step forward in the application of low-rank matrix reconstruction to clustering.

## Weaknesses

While the paper is strong overall, there are some areas for improvement. First, the paper mentions state evolution analysis but omits it due to space constraints. Given that state evolution is a key theoretical advantage of AMP algorithms, including even a brief summary would strengthen the paper. Second, the sensitivity analysis of the parameter τ (which appears in the algorithm but is not clearly explained) is limited. Third, while the paper demonstrates performance on K-means clustering, it doesn't explore how the algorithm would perform on other clustering paradigms (e.g., density-based clustering), which would further demonstrate the versatility of the approach.

## Recommendation

This is a high-quality paper that makes significant contributions to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The theoretical framework is sound, the experimental results are compelling, and the application to clustering represents a meaningful advancement over previous work. While the omitted state evolution analysis and limited sensitivity analysis are minor weaknesses, they do not detract from the overall quality of the work. The paper clearly advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way and addresses an important problem in machine learning with practical significance.

I recommend acceptance of this paper. It represents a solid contribution to the field with clear theoretical insights, strong experimental validation, and meaningful applications. The work is well-positioned to be of interest to the NIPS community, particularly given the growing interest in efficient and theoretically grounded algorithms for large-scale machine learning problems.

## Summary of Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Novel extension of AMP to general-rank matrix reconstruction with unified framework for marginalization and MAP
- First method to directly handle "exactly one cluster" constraint in low-rank clustering
- Comprehensive experimental validation on artificial and real data
- Strong theoretical grounding with clear comparison to existing methods
- Efficient algorithm with O(mN) computational cost per iteration

**Weaknesses:**
- Omission of state evolution analysis (though understandable due to space)
- Limited sensitivity analysis for parameter τ
- Focus primarily on K-means rather than exploring other clustering paradigms

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field and meets all the criteria for acceptance at NIPS.
