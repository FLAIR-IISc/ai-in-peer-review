- Summary: Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a unified β parameter for posterior-mean and MAP, and applies it to K-means clustering with experiments showing improved performance over Lloyd’s K-means and variational Bayes.

- Strength 1 (Theoretical Foundation): Introduces β to unify marginalization and MAP, and shows AMP fixed points are ICM fixed points (Proposition 1), offering insight into AMP’s avoidance of poor local minima.

- Strength 2 (Algorithmic Innovation): Presents a novel AMP clustering algorithm that directly enforces the one-cluster-per-point constraint, differing from prior low-rank clustering approaches.

- Strength 3 (Computational Efficiency): Algorithm runs in O(mN) per iteration (linear in matrix size), substantially cheaper than typical variational Bayes.

- Strength 4 (Empirical Validation): Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate consistent improvements in clustering loss and accuracy, especially in high dimensions.

- Weakness 1 (Insufficient Differentiation): Novelty relative to prior work [9, 14] is not fully justified or explained; the key distinction (fixing r vs r/m) needs clearer exposition and comparison.

- Weakness 2 (Theoretical Gaps): State evolution and rigorous convergence/performance guarantees are omitted; analysis relies on an “assumption of convergence” without full justification.

- Weakness 3 (Practical Implementation Details): Introduces parameter τ for clustering without thorough sensitivity analysis or guidance for optimal selection.

- Weakness 4 (Comparison with SOTA): Lacks comparisons to more recent clustering methods (e.g., convex optimization or deep learning approaches) that may be relevant baselines.

- Overall Assessment: Valuable, well-written contribution extending AMP to low-rank reconstruction with a compelling clustering application, though theoretical and comparative gaps remain.

- Recommendation: Accept — novel, computationally efficient, empirically validated method with meaningful advances for high-dimensional clustering.
