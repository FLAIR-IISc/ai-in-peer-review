This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means clustering. The core idea is to extend existing AMP algorithms to handle the general rank case and to utilize a flexible Bayesian framework for incorporating prior knowledge about the factors. The paper aims to address the computational cost associated with traditional Bayesian methods and to outperform standard clustering algorithms like Lloyd's K-means, particularly in high-dimensional data. The work builds upon previous research in AMP for compressed sensing, low-rank matrix factorization, and clustering, referencing relevant papers such as [6, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12].

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the AMP algorithm appears well-motivated and builds logically on existing AMP literature. The formulation using a one-parameter extension of the posterior distribution is a clever way to unify the marginalization and MAP problems. The connection to variational Bayes methods is also clearly articulated. Proposition 1 provides a theoretical guarantee for the optimality of the algorithm under certain conditions.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The problem setting and proposed approach are clearly described. Mathematical notation is generally consistent. The algorithmic descriptions are concise and easy to follow.
*   **Novelty:** Extending AMP algorithms to handle general rank low-rank matrix reconstruction and MAP estimation is a non-trivial contribution. The application of AMP to K-means clustering, explicitly enforcing the single cluster assignment constraint, also represents a novel approach. The analysis relating the algorithm to variational Bayes and ICM algorithms is insightful.
*   **Significance:** The proposed algorithm has the potential to improve the efficiency and accuracy of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering, particularly in high-dimensional settings. The numerical experiments demonstrate the algorithm’s effectiveness compared to existing methods, including K-means and variational Bayes approaches. The reported performance gains on both artificial and real datasets suggest practical relevance.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While Proposition 1 provides a theoretical guarantee, a more comprehensive theoretical analysis of the algorithm's convergence properties and performance bounds would strengthen the paper. The state evolution analysis, mentioned but not included due to space constraints, would be valuable.
*   **Computational Complexity Discussion:** The paper mentions linear computational cost (O(mN)). While correct, a deeper discussion of the practical implications of this complexity, especially concerning the cost of calculating f and G, would be helpful. Specifically, the computational cost of the numerical integration in f(·, ·; p̂) could be significant for certain priors.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance of AMP algorithms can be sensitive to parameter settings. The paper lacks a thorough discussion of how to choose the noise variance parameter τ and the impact of different prior distributions on the algorithm's performance.
*   **Comparison with Recent Work:** While the paper references some relevant work, it could benefit from a more detailed comparison with more recent advances in AMP and related areas, such as the work on bilinear generalized approximate message passing [13].
*   **Experiment Design:** The experimental setup lacks details on hyperparameter tuning for each algorithm. Reporting the standard deviation for all measures would improve clarity.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
*   The algorithm leverages the strengths of AMP and Bayesian inference, offering a promising approach for solving challenging problems in high dimensions.
*   The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm compared to existing methods.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the core ideas and contributions.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The theoretical analysis is limited and could be strengthened with a more comprehensive treatment of convergence and performance bounds.
*   The paper lacks a thorough discussion of parameter sensitivity and hyperparameter tuning.
*   The comparison with recent related work could be more extensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper with valuable contributions to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The proposed AMP algorithm is novel, technically sound, and demonstrates promising performance in numerical experiments. While the theoretical analysis and discussion of parameter sensitivity could be improved, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, primarily addressing the points raised regarding theoretical analysis, parameter tuning, and comparison with recent work.
