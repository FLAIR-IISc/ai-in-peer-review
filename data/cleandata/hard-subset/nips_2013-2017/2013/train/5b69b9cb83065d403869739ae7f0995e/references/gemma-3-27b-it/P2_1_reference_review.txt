## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

**Summary:**

This paper presents an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous work to enable MAP estimation beyond just posterior mean estimation. A key contribution is applying this algorithm to K-means clustering, framing it as a specific instance of the reconstruction problem with constraints enforcing exclusive cluster assignment. The authors demonstrate through numerical experiments that their AMP-based clustering algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, particularly in high-dimensional data.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds on the growing body of work leveraging AMP for various inference problems, referencing key contributions in compressed sensing and matrix factorization (e.g., Donoho et al., Rangan et al.). It differentiates itself from existing AMP approaches for low-rank reconstruction (Matsushita & Tanaka) by enabling MAP estimation. The application to clustering connects to prior work utilizing low-rank techniques for clustering (Xu et al., Ding et al.) but distinguishes itself by explicitly handling the exclusive assignment constraint within the reconstruction framework.  The comparison to variational Bayes methods (Lim & Teh, Raiko et al.) positions the AMP algorithm as potentially more accurate due to its better representation of dependencies between factors.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical grounding:** The paper connects the proposed algorithm to the broader framework of approximate message passing and Bayesian inference, providing a theoretical basis for its performance. The mention of state evolution (though not detailed due to space constraints) suggests a path for rigorous analysis.
* **Generality:** The algorithm’s flexibility in incorporating various prior distributions is a significant strength, allowing it to adapt to different problem settings and leverage specific domain knowledge.
* **Novelty:** Extending the existing AMP algorithm to MAP estimation is a valuable contribution, widening its applicability. The application of AMP to clustering with the explicit exclusive assignment constraint is a novel approach.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness, outperforming Lloyd's K-means in high-dimensional scenarios. The experiments cover both artificial and real datasets.
* **Clear Derivation:** The algorithm is described in a step-by-step manner and provides enough detail for implementation, even without full derivation in the main text (supplementary material is mentioned).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the paper mentions state evolution, a deeper theoretical analysis of convergence and performance bounds would strengthen the contribution.  The proposition guaranteeing optimality is conditional on positive semi-definiteness, which isn't always guaranteed and needs further discussion.
* **Implementation Details:** The paper lacks explicit details about parameter tuning (e.g., the noise variance parameter τ) and computational complexity. A discussion on practical considerations for large-scale datasets would be helpful.
* **Comparison to State-of-the-Art Clustering Algorithms:** While the comparison to Lloyd's K-means is valuable, a comparison to more sophisticated clustering algorithms (e.g., DBSCAN, spectral clustering) would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the algorithm's performance.
* **Clarity of Notation:** The paper uses a lot of notation, and while it’s mostly consistent, it could benefit from a more comprehensive index or explanation of each variable in the initial section.




**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (8/10):**  The paper presents a technically sound algorithm with a solid theoretical foundation. The empirical results support the claims, but a more rigorous analysis would significantly enhance the quality.
* **Clarity (7/10):** The paper is generally well-written, but the density of notation and occasional lack of detail in explanation hinder clarity. More illustrative examples and step-by-step explanations would improve readability.
* **Originality (8/10):** The extension to MAP estimation and the application to constrained clustering are novel and represent significant contributions.
* **Significance (8/10):** The algorithm offers a potentially more efficient and accurate approach to both low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering, particularly in high-dimensional settings.  The framework’s flexibility and potential for further development suggest a lasting impact.

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Novel application of AMP to constrained clustering.
* Demonstrated performance improvement over Lloyd’s K-means.
* Strong theoretical grounding in AMP and Bayesian inference.
* Algorithm’s flexibility and generality.

**Against:**

* Limited theoretical analysis, particularly regarding convergence.
* Lack of detailed implementation and computational complexity analysis.
* Comparison to a relatively limited set of clustering algorithms.

**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **accepting** this paper.  While there are areas for improvement, the contributions are significant and the work is well-executed. The novelty of the approach, combined with the promising empirical results and solid theoretical foundation, makes this a valuable addition to the NIPS program.  Addressing the weaknesses outlined above in a future revision would further strengthen the work. I believe the potential impact on both low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering justifies its inclusion.
