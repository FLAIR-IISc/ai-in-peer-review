Summary
The paper develops an AMP-style algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization with flexible (possibly improper) priors on the rows of U and V, and introduces a “temperature” parameter β that unifies sum-product (β=1) and max-sum/MAP (β→∞) variants. The resulting updates include Onsager-type corrections and reduce to variational Bayes/ICM if these corrections are removed. As a main application, the authors instantiate the MAP limit to derive an AMP algorithm for K-means with explicit hard one-hot constraints, yielding a leave-one-out-like correction in the assignment step. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and the ORL face dataset show improved K-means objective and accuracy relative to Lloyd’s/K-means++, and faster/more accurate clustering than a VBMF-based marginalization baseline under matched priors.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds on AMP/GAMP ideas from compressed sensing and bilinear inference (e.g., Donoho et al. 2009; Rangan 2011; Bayati & Montanari 2011; IterFac), and is close to bilinear GAMP/AMP for matrix factorization (e.g., Matsushita & Tanaka 2012; Parker et al. 2013). The novelty claimed is (i) a general-rank AMP that supports β→∞ (MAP) in addition to β=1, and (ii) an AMP formulation of K-means that enforces one-hot constraints directly and yields bias corrections in the assignment step. The connection to variational Bayes (VB)/ICM via removal of Onsager terms is well positioned.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The high-level derivation is deferred to the supplement. The main algorithm matches standard AMP structure and the β-parameterization is natural. Proposition 1 gives a useful coordinate-wise optimality at fixed points under PSD conditions.
- However, several important technical details are missing or unclear:
  - Convergence/stability: No damping, step-size, or adaptive β/τ strategies are discussed, although AMP often benefits from them; the reported oscillations and occasional collapse to a single cluster in ORL highlight this.
  - Conditions for the PSD assumption in Proposition 1 are not rigorously stated in the β→∞ case; G∞(b,Λ; p̂) = (Λ − H)^{-1} requires Λ − H ≻ 0, which is not guaranteed.
  - The state evolution is not presented; claims of O(1) iterations are not supported for this bilinear setting.
  - Handling of empty clusters and non-invertibility: In Algorithm 3, Λu = (1/(mτ)) V^T V (up to an apparent missing 1/(mτ) factor in the text), requires invertibility; if some ntl=0, Λu is singular and S^t undefined. No regularization or re-seeding is described.
  - The improper prior case and conditions ensuring a proper posterior are mentioned but not specified.
  - Identifiability/scaling issues for general priors are not discussed; the algorithm may need prior-induced regularization.

Clarity
- The exposition of the general algorithm is mostly clear; the mapping to VB/ICM is helpful.
- Notational/formatting issues:
  - In Algorithm 3, Λtu seemingly misses the 1/(mτ) factor (cf. Eq. 9). Please check all scalings.
  - The τ plug-in estimator m^{-2}N^{-1}||A−U V^T||_F^2 is consistent with Var(W_ij)=mτ, but this nonstandard scaling could be emphasized earlier.
  - Several minor typos/inconsistencies: “informaion” (ref 10), repeated commas in references, spacing around transposes (V ⊤0 etc.), “low-rankedness” wording, and inconsistent capitalization/reference styles.
  - The definition of q̂ and the role of β are clear, but examples of f,G for common priors (Laplace, nonnegativity) should be included in the main text.
- Reproducibility: No code; stopping criteria for β=1 methods (rel tol 1e-15) are unusually stringent and likely unnecessary; key hyperparameters (damping, τ updates) are unspecified.

Originality
- Extending a bilinear AMP to explicitly cover β→∞ (MAP) is a useful contribution; however, max-sum GAMP is known in the linear case and, for bilinear, similar ideas exist (BiG-AMP). The paper should clarify the relation to max-sum versions of (bi)linear GAMP/AMP and whether prior works already allowed MAP-like objectives.
- The K-means AMP with one-hot constraints and explicit assignment bias correction is interesting and, to my knowledge, not previously derived in this exact form. That said, relations to hard-EM for spherical GMMs, leave-one-out correction, and NMF/orthogonality-based links to K-means (e.g., Ding et al. 2005/2010) should be discussed more thoroughly.

Significance
- If robust and stable, the approach could matter for large-scale clustering and structured matrix factorization. The assignment correction is intuitively compelling and shows gains over K-means/K-means++.
- However, the experimental scope is limited: only one synthetic setting (fixed m,N, SNR, equal cluster sizes) and one real dataset (raw-pixel ORL) without dimensionality reduction. No runtime comparisons, no sensitivity to r̂≠r, no tests with common priors (sparsity, nonnegativity) despite motivating them, and no comparison to bilinear GAMP/BiG-AMP implementations on similar tasks. The occasional collapse to one cluster raises robustness concerns.

Pros
- Clear AMP formulation unifying β=1 and β→∞ with general priors.
- Insightful connection to VB/ICM via removing Onsager terms.
- Novel K-means AMP update with principled assignment bias correction.
- Competitive to strong baselines on reported datasets; improvement on K-means objective/accuracy; few iterations.

Cons
- Incomplete theory: no state evolution, weak convergence discussion, unclear conditions for Proposition 1 in MAP limit.
- Algorithmic fragility: oscillations, cluster collapse; no damping/regularization described; empty-cluster handling missing.
- Limited experiments and comparisons; no evaluation with sparse/nonnegative priors; no sensitivity analyses; no wall-clock runtime.
- Several notational/formatting errors; a likely scaling typo in Algorithm 3; references inconsistent.
- Claim of “first” direct one-hot factorization should be better justified relative to bilinear GAMP and hard-EM/GMM literature.

Questions for the authors
1. Please clarify the exact scalings in Algorithm 3, especially Λtu. Is there a missing 1/(mτ)?
2. How do you prevent singular Λtu when clusters are empty or tiny? Any regularization or re-seeding?
3. Did you use damping or annealing (in β or step size)? Can you report its effect on stability and the ORL collapses?
4. Can you provide at least a sketch/state-evolution approximation and the assumptions under which it is valid for fixed r, m,N→∞?
5. How does your MAP variant relate to max-sum (bi)linear GAMP? What is genuinely new?
6. Please add experiments with sparse and/or nonnegative priors, and sensitivity to mis-specified r̂, τ, and priors.
7. Runtime comparisons and ablations of initialization (random vs K-means++) would strengthen the empirical claims.

Recommendation
Reject (as is). The paper contains promising ideas and some interesting empirical improvements for K-means, but the theoretical development is incomplete, the algorithmic stability and edge-case handling are under-specified, and the empirical evaluation is too narrow. With fixes to the derivation/stability, clearer relation to prior bilinear GAMP/MAP methods, corrected/cleaned presentation, and expanded experiments (including structured priors and broader baselines), this could become a strong submission.
