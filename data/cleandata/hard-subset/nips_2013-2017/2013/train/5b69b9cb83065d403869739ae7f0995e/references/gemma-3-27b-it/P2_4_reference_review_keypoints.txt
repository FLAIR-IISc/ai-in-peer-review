- Summary: Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction extending AMP to MAP estimation and applies it to K-means clustering with empirical and theoretical support.
- Relationship to prior work: Builds on AMP literature, differs from prior low-rank AMP by enabling MAP, and connects to low-rank clustering while enforcing hard single-cluster assignments.
- Theoretical foundation: Provides a clear AMP derivation, links to belief propagation/Bethe approximation, and offers Proposition 1 showing optimality under certain conditions.
- Generality & flexibility: Bayesian formulation with flexible priors allows modeling diverse structural properties of factors U and V.
- Novel clustering algorithm: Introduces a novel AMP-based K-means that directly enforces single-cluster constraints and outperforms Lloyd’s algorithm in some settings.
- Clear presentation: Algorithm and notation are presented clearly and systematically.
- Empirical validation: Strong numerical experiments on synthetic and real data with comparisons to K-means and K-means++ showing improved performance in high dimensions.
- Limited state evolution discussion: State evolution is mentioned but not developed, leaving deeper theoretical analysis incomplete.
- Parameter sensitivity: The role and optimal tuning of τ (noise variance) are not thoroughly investigated beyond basic estimation.
- Computational complexity: Claims O(mN) cost but glosses over the practical cost of r̂-dimensional integrations for finite β; more detailed analysis is needed.
- Assumptions for Proposition 1: Conditions (e.g., positive semi-definiteness) required for the optimality guarantee are not extensively discussed and may be restrictive.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable theoretical and empirical contributions, though some practical and theoretical details need expansion.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel MAP extension of AMP, significant clustering performance gains, solid theoretical grounding, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient discussion of practical parameter choices and computational costs, and potentially restrictive optimality conditions.
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing parameter selection, computational complexity, Proposition 1 limitations, and expansion of state evolution analysis.
- Qualitative evaluation: Quality – Very Good; Clarity – Good; Originality – Good; Significance – Good.
