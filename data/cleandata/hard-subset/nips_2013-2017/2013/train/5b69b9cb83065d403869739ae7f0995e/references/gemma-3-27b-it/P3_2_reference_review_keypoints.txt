Overall assessment: Well-motivated, technically sound extension of AMP to Bayesian low-rank reconstruction and K-means; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

Main idea: Extends AMP to general-rank reconstruction and MAP estimation, and shows K-means can be cast as low-rank reconstruction enabling an AMP-based clustering algorithm.

Positioning: Properly situates within Bayesian low-rank and AMP literature but underdeveloped comparison to recent AMP-based methods (dictionary learning, blind calibration, etc.).

Strength — Technical soundness: Rigorous derivation of AMP extension with MAP capability, details in supplementary material.

Strength — Theoretical motivation: Strong linkage to AMP and Bayesian inference with potential for state-evolution analysis.

Strength — Novel application: Framing K-means as low-rank reconstruction and proposing an AMP-based clustering method is novel and useful in high dimensions.

Strength — Empirical results: Promising performance over Lloyd’s K-means on synthetic data and reasonable results on ORL faces dataset.

Strength — Problem formulation: Clear Bayesian setup and well-defined problem statement.

Weakness — Clarity & density: Paper is densely written with many equations and lacks intuitive explanations.

Weakness — Incremental novelty: Contributions feel like adaptations of existing AMP ideas rather than a transformative advance.

Weakness — Limited empirical evaluation: Experiments are narrow; need broader, larger, and more diverse real-world datasets.

Weakness — Missing state-evolution results: Mentions state evolution but provides no empirical or theoretical results from it.

Weakness — Parameter sensitivity unaddressed: No analysis of sensitivity to algorithmic parameters (e.g., τ).

Arguments for acceptance: Technically solid extension of AMP, novel and promising clustering application, strong theoretical foundation and efficiency benefits.

Arguments against acceptance: Incremental novelty, dense presentation harming clarity, and insufficient empirical breadth.

Suggestions — Improve clarity: Rewrite key sections to emphasize intuition and reduce equation density.

Suggestions — Expand related work: Compare more deeply to recent AMP-based methods and clarify differences.

Suggestions — Enhance experiments: Evaluate on larger, more diverse datasets and compare to modern clustering baselines beyond Lloyd’s/K-means++.

Suggestions — Analyze parameters: Include sensitivity analysis for key algorithm parameters.

Suggestions — Include state evolution: Provide at least a brief discussion or results relating to state evolution if possible.

Confidence: 7/10.
