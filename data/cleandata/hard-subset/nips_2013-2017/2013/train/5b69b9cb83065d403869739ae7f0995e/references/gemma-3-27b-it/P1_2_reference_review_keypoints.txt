- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction extended to general rank and MAP estimation.  
- Frames K-means clustering as a low-rank reconstruction problem and derives a novel AMP-based clustering algorithm.  
- Empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in high-dimensional settings on synthetic and ORL faces datasets.  
- Extends AMP beyond posterior means to include MAP estimation, broadening applicability.  
- Positions work relative to prior AMP low-rank and dictionary-learning literature, highlighting differences in scaling and independence assumptions.  
- Strength: general AMP framework that handles marginalization and MAP estimation.  
- Strength: novel clustering algorithm offering promising high-dimensional performance.  
- Strength: some theoretical grounding (Proposition 1) and mention of state evolution for performance prediction.  
- Strength: empirical validation showing improved loss and accuracy over Lloyd’s K-means.  
- Strength: clear connection and positioning within existing literature.  
- Weakness: limited presentation of state evolution limits theoretical understanding and scaling analysis.  
- Weakness: computational complexity claim O(mN) is oversimplified; cost of computing f and G (e.g., numerical integration) is not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: insufficient study of parameter sensitivity (e.g., noise variance τ) and tuning.  
- Weakness: convergence is stated but lacks a formal proof or specified conditions for guarantee.  
- Weakness: notation can be dense and some parameters (notably β) are not intuitively explained.  
- Detailed comment: β parameter introduction is abrupt and needs clearer intuition about trade-offs.  
- Detailed comment: Algorithm 2 needs concrete examples of f and G for common priors (Gaussian, Laplacian, etc.).  
- Detailed comment: Proposition 1 depends on positive semidefiniteness of summed matrices; conditions and failure cases should be discussed.  
- Detailed comment: using estimated τ in Algorithm 3 is justified heuristically; the effect of estimation error should be analyzed.  
- Detailed comment: experiments should cover more datasets, parameter settings, and comparisons to other state-of-the-art clustering methods.  
- For acceptance: novel AMP extension with broad applicability and promising clustering results in high dimensions.  
- Against acceptance: incomplete state evolution and complexity analysis, limited empirical breadth, and lack of formal convergence proof.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions requesting added state evolution discussion, deeper complexity analysis, parameter sensitivity studies, and convergence condition discussion.
