- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction extending AMP to MAP estimation.  
- Formulates K-means-style clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem and derives a novel AMP-based clustering algorithm.  
- Highlights flexibility to incorporate priors on factors U and V, enabling adaptation to varied problem settings.  
- Claims linear computational complexity O(mN), making it suitable for large-scale datasets.  
- Empirical results show improvements over Lloyd’s K-means, especially in high-dimensional scenarios, with clear performance metrics.  
- Situates work within AMP literature (Donoho, Rangan) and connects to Bayesian matrix factorization and NMF-based clustering methods.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid and grounded in Bayesian AMP, with asymptotic guarantees referenced in supplementary material.  
- State evolution analysis and detailed theoretical treatment are relegated to supplementary material, weakening main-text theoretical claims.  
- Performance likely sensitive to the noise parameter τ; estimation used in experiments but more sensitivity analysis needed.  
- Notation is dense and sometimes unclear (notably Section 4.1); functions f and G need clearer explanation and more intuitive variable names.  
- Missing implementation details that affect performance (e.g., AMP stopping criteria and parameter tuning).  
- Experiments focus mainly on synthetic data; broader evaluation on diverse real-world datasets is needed.  
- Authors provide careful comparisons to Lloyd’s, K-means++, VBMF-MA, and AMP-MA and acknowledge limitations of baselines.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically motivated algorithm with computational benefits and promising empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete theoretical analysis, clarity issues, and limited experimental validation.  
- Overall assessment: strong submission warranting acceptance, but requires minor revisions to improve theory presentation, clarity, experiments, and implementation details.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; suggested fixes include adding brief state-evolution discussion, improving notation/explanations (Sec. 4.1), expanding real-data experiments, and detailing implementation/parameter choices.
