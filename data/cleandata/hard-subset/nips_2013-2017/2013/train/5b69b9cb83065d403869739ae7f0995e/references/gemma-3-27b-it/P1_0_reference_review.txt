## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

**Summary:**

This paper presents an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous work to allow for MAP estimation beyond posterior mean estimation. Furthermore, it demonstrates a novel application of this algorithm to K-means clustering, framing it as a special case of low-rank reconstruction with specific constraints. The authors argue that their AMP-based approach offers computational efficiency, flexibility in incorporating priors, and potential performance advantages over existing methods like variational Bayes and Lloyd’s K-means, particularly in high-dimensional settings. They support their claims with numerical experiments on both artificial and real (face recognition) datasets.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper correctly positions its contribution within the broader context of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. It acknowledges the prevalence of Bayesian approaches, the computational challenges they pose, and the rise of AMP as a promising solution (citing Donoho et al., Rangan, and Matsushita et al.). The connection to Iterative Factorization (IterFac) and previous AMP work for low-rank reconstruction is clearly established. The authors also rightly highlight existing applications of low-rank techniques to clustering (Xu et al., Ding et al.) and contrast their approach with those, emphasizing the explicit constraint of exclusive cluster assignment. The reference to recent advances in AMP for dictionary learning and blind calibration (Krzakala et al., Parker et al.) demonstrates awareness of related developments.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Extension of AMP:** Extending the existing AMP algorithm to handle MAP estimation is a valuable contribution, broadening its applicability.
* **Unified Framework:**  Framing clustering as a specific case of low-rank reconstruction provides a theoretically elegant and potentially powerful perspective. The formulation explicitly enforces the single-cluster assignment constraint.
* **Potential for Efficiency:** The authors convincingly argue for the computational advantages of AMP, highlighting its linear complexity and potential for fast convergence (although a thorough state evolution analysis is relegated to supplementary material).
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments provide evidence supporting the claims of improved performance, particularly in the high-dimensional setting (artificial data) and on a real-world dataset. The comparison against Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ is relevant and informative.
* **Theoretical Justification (Proposition 1):** The proposition establishing the optimality of the AMP fixed point provides theoretical grounding for the algorithm's performance.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a logical flow of ideas.  The inclusion of the algorithm as a set of equations is a helpful convention.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Depth:** While the paper introduces the AMP algorithm and provides a Proposition on optimality, the theoretical analysis feels somewhat superficial.  A more detailed exploration of the algorithm's convergence properties and limitations would be beneficial.  The state evolution discussion, while mentioned, is not presented in the main body.
* **Experimental Scope:** The numerical experiments, while encouraging, are limited in scope. The parameter settings (e.g., τ) are somewhat arbitrarily chosen, and the sensitivity of the algorithm to these parameters is not thoroughly investigated. More extensive comparisons against other state-of-the-art clustering algorithms beyond K-means and K-means++ would strengthen the claims.
* **Dependence on Initialization:** Algorithm 3 (AMP for K-means) explicitly states the need for careful initialization using the K-means++ method, implying the algorithm is sensitive to starting conditions. This weakness should be more prominently discussed.
* **Missing Comparison to Variational Bayes (VB):** While the paper mentions VB as a contrasting approach, there's a limited direct experimental comparison.  VB provides an alternative approach to approximate Bayesian inference, and a more detailed benchmark against it would be insightful.
* **Notation:** The use of multiple indices (e.g.,  u0,i, v0,j) can occasionally be confusing.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it in a creative way to the clustering problem.
* The numerical results are promising and suggest the potential for improved performance in certain scenarios.
* The framing of clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem offers a theoretically appealing perspective.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The theoretical analysis lacks depth and leaves open questions about the algorithm’s convergence and robustness.
* The experimental validation is limited and could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation.
* The sensitivity to initialization needs to be addressed more thoroughly.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field.  The extension of AMP to MAP estimation and its application to clustering are both novel and interesting. The paper demonstrates a good understanding of related work and provides initial evidence supporting the claims of improved performance. However, the lack of in-depth theoretical analysis and the limited experimental scope prevent me from giving it a strong recommendation.

**Rating:**  **Weak Accept**

**Recommendations for the Authors:**

* **Strengthen the theoretical analysis:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the algorithm's convergence properties and limitations.  Consider including a more comprehensive presentation of the state evolution analysis in the main text.
* **Expand the experimental validation:** Conduct more extensive experiments with a wider range of parameter settings and datasets. Include comparisons to other state-of-the-art clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering, DBSCAN).
* **Investigate Initialization Sensitivity:**  Perform a more systematic study of the algorithm's sensitivity to initialization and explore potential strategies to mitigate this issue.
* **Include VB comparison:**  Add a direct experimental comparison with a well-implemented variational Bayes algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* **Improve Notation:**  Review the notation for clarity and consistency.
