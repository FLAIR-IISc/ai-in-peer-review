- Presents an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that extends prior work to support MAP estimation as well as posterior mean estimation.
- Shows that the AMP framework naturally encompasses K-means clustering by explicitly enforcing the “exactly one cluster” constraint.
- Claims a novel clustering algorithm that empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in high-dimensional settings (tested on synthetic and ORL faces datasets).
- Situates the work appropriately within Bayesian low-rank recovery, variational Bayes, compressed sensing, and existing AMP literature.
- Emphasizes the benefit of AMP’s message-passing framework versus variational Bayes for this problem class.
- Strength: unified, flexible framework allowing both marginalization and MAP estimation.
- Strength: solid theoretical derivation grounded in AMP, with references to state evolution (though not fully presented).
- Strength: novel connection of MAP estimation to optimal clustering accuracy and an AMP-based clustering method.
- Strength: empirical results show improved clustering accuracy and use both synthetic and real datasets.
- Strength: paper is generally well-written with clear equations and consistent notation.
- Weakness: lacks a presented state evolution analysis, limiting assessment of theoretical guarantees and scalability.
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed computational complexity breakdown, especially regarding f and G computations and r̂-dimensional integrations.
- Weakness: limited discussion of parameter sensitivity (e.g., noise variance τ) and practical guidance for parameter selection.
- Weakness: comparisons are mainly to Lloyd’s K-means/K-means++; missing comparisons to stronger clustering baselines (e.g., spectral clustering, DBSCAN).
- Weakness: Proposition 1 requires positive semidefiniteness of certain sums (∑i S∞i and ∑j T∞j) but this condition isn’t guaranteed or justified.
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically motivated extension of AMP with potentially impactful clustering application and promising experimental gains.
- Against acceptance: missing detailed state evolution, richer complexity and sensitivity analyses, and broader empirical comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with weaknesses that are addressable; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: add state evolution overview, provide detailed complexity breakdown, analyze parameter sensitivity, compare to more clustering methods, and justify/prove the PSD conditions in Proposition 1.
