- Presents an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction extended to general rank and MAP estimation.  
- Novel application of AMP to K-means clustering with a hard single-cluster assignment constraint.  
- Grounded in a Bayesian framework, connecting to belief propagation, variational Bayes, and Bethe approximation.  
- Claims computational efficiency with cost linear in matrix dimensions, leveraging AMP scalability.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and ORL face data show improved accuracy over Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++.  
- Problem formulation and Bayesian assumptions are clearly stated.  

- Lacks detailed theoretical analysis (state evolution and “asymptotically exact” claims not fully supported).  
- Noise parameter τ treatment is ad-hoc and its sensitivity/robustness is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Algorithm 3 (AMP for K-means) is described abruptly and lacks motivation and linkage to the K-means objective.  
- Comparisons are limited to K-means variants; missing benchmarks against more advanced clustering methods (e.g., spectral clustering, deep clustering).  
- Convergence analysis is limited; oscillation issues and broader convergence guarantees are not addressed.  

- Paper is technically sound but feels incomplete without deeper theory; reads like a strong starting point.  
- Generally well-written, but Algorithm 3 and τ estimation need clearer exposition.  
- The approach is original and potentially significant for scalable accurate clustering in high dimensions.  

- Pros: novel AMP application, strong empirical gains over K-means, solid (if incomplete) theoretical grounding, and scalability.  
- Cons: insufficient theoretical guarantees, limited benchmarking, parameter sensitivity, and unclear algorithmic exposition.  

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on adding theoretical sketches, clearer Algorithm 3 explanation, improved τ discussion, and broader experimental comparisons.
