- Paper proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction that unifies marginal posterior inference and MAP via a β parameter p̂(U,V|A;β) ∝ p̂(U,V|A)^β.  
- Algorithm handles general row-wise priors (not necessarily component-wise independent) with O(mN) per-iteration cost and reduces to previous bilinear AMP for posterior means.  
- Key technical novelty: inclusion of Onsager-like correction terms distinguishing AMP from VB/ICM; removing them recovers VB/ICM with Gaussian priors.  
- The authors prove a blockwise optimality property at fixed points for the MAP case (Proposition 1).  
- K-means is cast as a bilinear model with one-hot V and flat U, yielding a specialized AMP-KM (Algorithm 3) that augments Lloyd’s update with a leave-one-out bias correction and a small-cluster encouragement term.  
- An AMP variant for maximum-accuracy clustering (β=1) with discrete priors on V is also presented.  
- Synthetic experiments (m=800, N=1600) show AMP-KM achieves lowest K-means loss and AMP-MA highest accuracy, with fast convergence.  
- Real-data test (ORL Faces) shows AMP-KM often outperforms K-means++ in loss and accuracy when both are K-means++ initialized.  
- Technical soundness: derivation follows established dense bilinear AMP methods and the β interpolation is sensible, but the paper omits state evolution (SE) and convergence/damping guidance.  
- Practical robustness concern: reported oscillations in Algorithm 3 with no recommended damping/annealing strategies.  
- Algorithm correctness/clarity: specialization to K-means is elegant, but there is a likely typo in Algorithm 3’s Λ scaling that should be corrected and verified.  
- Empirics limitations: no wall-clock runtime comparisons, maximum-accuracy results use true priors (optimistic), and robustness to prior misspecification or r̂ ≠ r is not evaluated.  
- Baselines are limited—missing comparisons to EM-GMM (tied spherical), spectral clustering, NMF-based clustering, and BiG-AMP configured with discrete priors.  
- Presentation issues: minor notation/typos, reference formatting glitches, and the τ estimation via residuals is ad hoc and needs justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Reproducibility suggestion: provide more examples of p̂u/p̂v and closed-form f,G for common priors (nonnegativity, sparsity, one-hot).  
- Originality: novel extension of bilinear AMP to MAP via β→∞ and a new AMP-based interpretation of K-means with Onsager corrections.  
- Relation to prior work: relation to BiG-AMP is mentioned but not deeply compared—clarify what fixed-r limit and non-factorized row priors enable beyond [14].  
- Significance: potentially impactful for AMP theory/practice and clustering, but immediate uptake limited by lack of SE, convergence guidelines, and broader comparisons.  
- Pros: principled β-unified AMP derivation including MAP; MAP fixed-point property; novel K-means AMP with LOO correction; empirical gains with low overhead; O(mN) complexity.  
- Cons: no SE/convergence analysis or damping guidance; Algorithm 3 typo and clarity issues; limited baselines; untested robustness to prior misspecification and r̂ ≠ r; no wall-clock timings.  
- Suggested improvements: include SE (even in supplement) and damping tips; fix Algorithm 3 scaling and derive mean-of-assigned-points link; add EM-GMM/spectral/NMF/BiG-AMP baselines; test prior misspecification and r̂ ≠ r; report runtimes and memory; justify/update τ via EM and release code/pseudocode.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept—conceptually clean and promising, but needs stronger theory (SE/convergence) and broader empirical comparisons.
