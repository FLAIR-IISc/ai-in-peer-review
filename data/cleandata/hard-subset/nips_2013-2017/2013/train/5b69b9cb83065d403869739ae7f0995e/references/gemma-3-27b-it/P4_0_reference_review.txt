This paper introduces an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means clustering. The authors extend a previous AMP algorithm to handle MAP estimation and demonstrate its application to clustering, framing it as a special case of low-rank reconstruction with constraints on cluster assignment. The core idea builds upon recent work utilizing AMP for related problems, emphasizing a different scaling limit than some prior approaches and a formulation avoiding independence assumptions between the factors of the low-rank matrix. Numerical experiments demonstrate performance gains over Lloyd’s K-means algorithm in high-dimensional data and showcase competitive accuracy in clustering tasks.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the AMP algorithm appears logically sound, building on established AMP principles. The extension to MAP estimation is a valuable contribution. The proposition regarding optimality under certain conditions strengthens the theoretical justification.
*   **Novelty:** Applying AMP directly to K-means with the specified constraints is a novel approach, differentiating it from previous low-rank matrix factorization-based clustering methods. The focus on a different scaling limit and the relaxation of independence assumptions also represent incremental contributions.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The problem setting and motivation are clearly described. The algorithmic presentation using equations is concise and effective.
*   **Significance:** The potential to improve clustering performance, particularly in high-dimensional settings, is significant. The connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering provides a unified framework. The analysis of convergence and fixed points adds to the understanding of the algorithm’s behavior.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Depth:** While the authors mention state evolution and its potential for performance prediction, they do not present it in the paper, limiting the theoretical insights. A deeper analysis of convergence properties would be beneficial.
*   **Experimental Scope:** The numerical experiments, while illustrative, are somewhat limited. A more extensive evaluation across diverse datasets and parameter settings would strengthen the empirical validation.  The comparison with other state-of-the-art clustering algorithms (beyond K-means and K-means++) is lacking.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The effect of the noise parameter 'τ' is not explored in detail. Its estimation procedure could be further elaborated. The paper briefly mentions that initialization matters for the K-means clustering, but there is no clear discussion of the effect of initialization choices for the proposed AMP algorithm.
*   **Comparison to Related Work:** While related work is cited, the comparison with other AMP-based algorithms (e.g., [13, 14]) feels superficial. A more detailed analysis of the differences and trade-offs would be helpful. The advantages of not assuming independence in U0 and V0 aren't fully demonstrated in practice.
*   **Algorithm 3 Complexity:** The discussion of the algorithm for K-means clustering in Section 4.3 highlights the implementation complexity due to the parameter τ, which is absent in the original K-means formulation. This point could be more clearly articulated.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound application of AMP to K-means clustering, offering a promising alternative to traditional methods.
*   The theoretical foundations are reasonably well-established, and the proposition regarding optimality provides some assurance of the algorithm's performance.
*   The numerical experiments demonstrate tangible improvements over Lloyd's K-means, especially in high dimensions.
*   The connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering provides a valuable theoretical perspective.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of a comprehensive theoretical analysis (e.g., state evolution) and limited experimental scope weaken the overall impact of the work.
*   The comparison with related work could be more thorough and insightful.
*   The sensitivity to parameters and initialization could be better addressed.
*   The practical benefits over other advanced clustering methods are not fully demonstrated.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. While not a breakthrough, it offers a novel approach with demonstrated performance gains and a solid theoretical foundation. The weaknesses are addressable in future work, and the paper is of sufficient quality to be accepted, potentially with minor revisions to expand the experimental evaluation and provide a more detailed comparison with related algorithms.
