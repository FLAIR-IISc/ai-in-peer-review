Summary
The paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with general (possibly non-Gaussian and non-factorized within each vector) priors over the r̂-dimensional rows of U and V. A temperature parameter β interpolates between approximate marginal (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference on p̂(U,V|A), yielding a unified algorithm (Alg. 2). A byproduct is an AMP-style algorithm for K-means-type clustering (Alg. 3) that enforces one-hot assignments exactly; the authors derive a correction term in the assignment step that accounts for the bias induced by including the current point in its centroid. A proposition asserts that fixed points of the MAP-AMP are coordinatewise globally optimal for CMAP under a PSD condition on certain covariance aggregates. Experiments on synthetic data (m=800,N=1600) and the ORL faces dataset suggest that the AMP-based K-means variant improves the K-means objective and, in synthetic settings, that the β=1 variant improves accuracy relative to VBMF and standard K-means.

Relation to prior work
The paper is positioned against VBMF and prior AMP work in compressed sensing and bilinear models (IterFac; AMP for general low-rank; bilinear generalized AMP). The main algorithm appears closely related to bilinear GAMP/AMP with Onsager corrections; the “temperature” continuation is conceptually akin to max-sum (MAP) vs sum-product (MMSE) variants. Application to clustering via one-hot priors relates to hard EM/spherical GMM and K-means, and to NMF-based clustering.

Strengths
- A clean, general AMP derivation that handles non-Gaussian r̂-dimensional priors via functions f and G; the β-parameter provides a unifying view of MAP vs marginal inference.
- Clear connection to VBMF/ICM: removing Onsager terms recovers known variational/coordinate-descent updates; the Onsager terms are O(m+N) to compute.
- The K-means AMP variant (Alg. 3) is intuitive and includes a principled “leave-one-out” correction of assignment bias and a balancing term that discourages tiny clusters.
- Proposition 1 offers a useful optimality guarantee at fixed points (coordinatewise global optimality) under stated PSD conditions.
- Empirical results show improved K-means loss vs Lloyd/K-means++ on synthetic data and often on ORL; β=1 variant attains higher accuracy in synthetic experiments.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness/clarity
- Likely scaling error in Alg. 3 (Eq. 17a): Λt_u is written as Λt_u = mτ (Vt)⊤Vt. This contradicts Alg. 2, where Λt_u = (1/(mτ))(Vt)⊤Vt (up to Onsager terms). With the current 17a, Ut = B^t_u(Λ^t_u)^{-1} yields Ut ∝ AV(V⊤V)^{-1}/(m^2τ^2), not the usual centroid update AV(V⊤V)^{-1}. The subsequent derivation (Eq. 18a–b) implicitly assumes Λ^t_u = (1/(mτ))(V⊤V). Please fix and check all subsequent formulas (including the coefficients in 17b–18b).
- Several notational inconsistencies: e.g., p̂U(U0) vs p̂U(U) in Sec. 2.1; indices in Eq. (3); mixing of u0,i (rows) and ũ0,l (columns) with limited clarification; inconsistent use of aj vs a j.
- The discrete prior case (one-hot V) invokes f∞ that is piecewise constant and G∞=0 “almost everywhere”; the algorithm relies on derivatives that are undefined on boundaries. This should be acknowledged and handled (e.g., tie-breaking, subgradients, or measure-zero arguments).

Theory
- State evolution is claimed possible but omitted. Given that SE is one of the main selling points of AMP and that bilinear AMP can be fragile, at least a brief summary of the SE recursions and conditions would strengthen the paper substantially.
- Convergence and damping are not discussed. The authors report oscillations and an ad hoc stopping rule (Vt=Vt−1 or Vt=Vt−2), but do not consider standard AMP remedies (damping, step-size control, adaptive Onsager). Without this, reproducibility and robustness are questionable.
- Proposition 1 hinges on ∑i S∞i and ∑j T∞j being PSD; the paper asserts this holds in “almost all cases” without conditions. A precise statement of sufficient conditions (e.g., convex −log p̂ and Λ≽0 implying G∞=(Λ−H)−1≽0) would help.

Experimental evaluation
- The title promises “reconstruction and clustering,” but experiments focus exclusively on clustering with a one-hot prior for V and uniform prior for U. There is no evaluation on generic low-rank reconstruction with structured priors (e.g., sparse or nonnegative), no mismatch studies (r̂≠r), no missing-data settings, and no real-valued priors where f,G require integration/optimization.
- Comparisons are limited. For clustering, EM for spherical GMM (hard/soft), mini-batch K-means, and spectral or globalized variants (k-means||) are absent. For bilinear inference, no results vs BiG-AMP/bilinear GAMP variants, which are the closest baselines.
- Reporting emphasizes iteration counts but not wall-clock time, which matters since f and G may require r̂-dimensional integrations or optimizations per row/column; the constant factor can be large.
- In synthetic maximum-accuracy experiments, the algorithm is given the true priors, which biases results in its favor; real-data priors and hyperparameter selection are not discussed.
- On ORL, pre-processing (normalization, dimensionality reduction) is not described; two runs collapsed to one cluster without analysis. Statistical significance (error bars) and sensitivity to initialization are not quantified.

Claims and related work
- The novelty claim “first to directly enforce one-hot constraints in low-rank matrix reconstruction” is too strong given that hard EM for GMM/K-means already enforces this constraint; within AMP for bilinear models, bilinear GAMP and related max-sum variants are highly relevant baselines. Please clarify the precise novelty relative to these.

Minor issues and formatting
- Several references are poorly formatted (stray commas, inconsistent venues and dates, missing page ranges). Please fix to NeurIPS style.
- Typographical issues: inconsistent spaces around parentheses and operators; Eq. numbering sometimes unclear; define all symbols (e.g., O denotes zero matrix?).
- The proposed τ estimator m−2N−1||A−U(V)⊤||^2 lacks derivation and dimensional consistency; justify and report its practical impact.

Pros
- Unifying β-framework bridging marginal and MAP AMP.
- Intuitive K-means AMP with bias correction; improved K-means objective in experiments.
- Low per-iteration arithmetic complexity and clear link to VBMF/ICM.
- Proposition offering coordinatewise optimality at fixed points.

Cons
- Possible equation error (critical), several notational/formatting problems.
- Missing SE/convergence analysis and stability measures; ad hoc stopping.
- Narrow experiments; title not matched by scope; limited baselines; lack of runtime reporting.
- Overstated novelty relative to bilinear AMP and hard EM/GMM.

Recommendation: Reject (borderline). The ideas are promising, but the paper needs (i) corrected and carefully checked derivations, (ii) at least a brief SE summary or convergence discussion, (iii) broader and fairer experiments (reconstruction tasks and stronger baselines), and (iv) improved clarity/formatting. I encourage resubmission after addressing these issues.

Confidence: High.
