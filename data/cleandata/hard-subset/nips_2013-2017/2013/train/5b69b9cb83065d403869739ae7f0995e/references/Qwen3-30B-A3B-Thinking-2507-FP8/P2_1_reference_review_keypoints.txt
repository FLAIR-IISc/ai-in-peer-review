- Proposes an AMP algorithm extending AMP from vector estimation to Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering as a special case.  
- Key contributions: an AMP method for both posterior mean (marginalization) and MAP estimation, and a novel AMP-based clustering algorithm.  
- Strong theoretical foundation grounded in established AMP theory, with a valuable proof (Proposition 1) linking AMP MAP fixed points to ICM fixed points.  
- Comprehensive numerical experiments (synthetic and ORL Faces) showing AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and variational Bayes on loss, accuracy, and convergence speed.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, noting the AMP K-means required more iterations than K-means++ in real data despite claimed O(1) complexity.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized writing, consistent notation, and reproducible algorithm presentations (Algorithm 3 for K-means is explicit).  
- Original extension of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction; novel handling of the one-cluster-per-point constraint within the AMP framework.  
- Unified framework that handles both marginalization and MAP estimation via a single parameter β.  
- Significant practical impact: better clustering performance on high-dimensional data and potential to inspire future AMP-clustering work.  
- Strengths: solid theory, extensive experiments, direct cluster-assignment handling, clear presentation, and good related-work contextualization.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical justification for why AMP outperforms ICM for clustering, apparent contradiction between claimed O(1) iteration complexity and experiments, and unclear scalability to much larger datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — high-quality, significant, original work that merits publication despite noted theoretical and scalability clarifications.
