## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

**Summary:**

This paper presents an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending existing work to handle general rank and MAP estimation.  A key contribution lies in applying this framework to K-means clustering, offering a novel algorithm that directly enforces the single-cluster assignment constraint. The authors claim improved performance, particularly in high-dimensional settings, compared to Lloyd's K-means.  The work builds upon the growing field of AMP algorithms, motivated by their efficiency and theoretical guarantees, and seeks to leverage them for problems beyond compressed sensing.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Application of AMP:**  The application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction *and* clustering is a noteworthy contribution.  While AMP has been applied to related areas (dictionary learning, blind calibration - as the authors acknowledge), extending it to handle MAP estimation in the general rank case and then specifically applying it to K-means with a hard assignment constraint represents a novel direction.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper is grounded in a Bayesian framework and draws connections to belief propagation and variational Bayes, providing a solid theoretical basis for the proposed algorithm.  The discussion of the Bethe approximation and potential for state evolution analysis (though not fully detailed due to space constraints) is promising.
* **Potential for Efficiency:** AMP algorithms are known for their speed and scalability, which is a significant advantage. The authors claim a computational cost linear in the matrix dimensions, a benefit over iterative methods like variational Bayes.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments on both artificial and real datasets (ORL faces) demonstrate promising results, showing improvements over standard K-means, especially in terms of accuracy. The experiments are reasonably comprehensive, covering a range of parameters and a comparison to relevant baselines (K-means++, VBMF-MA).
* **Clear Problem Formulation:** The paper clearly defines the problem setting, including the Bayesian assumptions and the connection between low-rank reconstruction and clustering.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the paper mentions state evolution, a detailed analysis is absent.  Providing even a sketch of the performance guarantees attainable through state evolution would significantly strengthen the paper. This is especially important given the claim of "asymptotically exact" behavior.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The role and impact of the noise parameter 'τ' is not thoroughly discussed.  The method for estimating it from the data (described in Section 5) feels somewhat ad-hoc and could benefit from more justification. How robust is the algorithm to inaccuracies in τ?
* **Clarity of Algorithm 3:** The description of Algorithm 3 (AMP for K-means) is a bit abrupt and lacks a detailed explanation of the motivation behind the specific modifications compared to the general AMP algorithm.  The connection to the K-means objective function could be made more explicit.
* **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art Clustering:** The comparison is primarily to Lloyd's K-means and K-means++.  A comparison to more recent and sophisticated clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering, deep embedded clustering) would provide a stronger benchmark for assessing the proposed method's significance.
* **Convergence Analysis:** While Proposition 1 provides a guarantee under certain conditions, a broader analysis of convergence properties would be valuable. The authors acknowledge potential oscillations, and a discussion of how these are handled or mitigated would be helpful.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and claims are generally supported by the experiments. However, the lack of detailed theoretical analysis weakens this aspect. It feels slightly incomplete, more like a strong starting point for further research than a fully polished contribution.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. However, Algorithm 3 and the discussion around parameter τ could benefit from further clarification.
* **Originality:** The application of AMP to this specific problem is novel and represents a meaningful contribution.  The authors clearly position their work within the existing literature.
* **Significance:** The potential for efficient and accurate low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering is significant. The demonstrated improvements over K-means, especially in high-dimensional settings, suggest this work could be valuable to researchers and practitioners.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Novel application of AMP to low-rank reconstruction and K-means clustering.
* Strong empirical results demonstrating improvements over K-means.
* Solid theoretical foundation (though incomplete).
* Potential for scalability and efficiency.

**Con:**

* Lack of detailed theoretical analysis (state evolution, convergence guarantees).
* Limited comparison to state-of-the-art clustering algorithms.
* Parameter sensitivity and ad-hoc parameter estimation.
* Algorithm 3 lacks sufficient explanation.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field.  The novel application of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering, combined with promising empirical results, justifies acceptance. However, the paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by providing a more detailed theoretical analysis (even a sketch of the state evolution) and expanding the comparison to more state-of-the-art clustering algorithms.  The authors should also clarify the explanation of Algorithm 3 and the parameter estimation process.  Addressing these points would elevate the paper from a promising contribution to a truly impactful one.
