Summary
The paper develops an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on the factor rows. A key device is a “temperature” parameter β that interpolates between posterior inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞), yielding a unified derivation and algorithm. The resulting vector-AMP-like iterations include Onsager corrections and reduce to a variational Bayes/ICM-style algorithm if those terms are dropped. The authors then instantiate the framework for clustering by encoding K-means as a special case of low-rank factorization with discrete one-hot factors. The derived AMP-KM algorithm modifies Lloyd’s assignment step with a bias-correction and a cluster-size-dependent term. They also propose an AMP-based “maximum accuracy” clustering (β=1), which approximates marginal MAP assignments. Experiments on synthetic data (m=800, N=1600) and the ORL faces dataset show improved K-means loss (AMP-KM vs Lloyd/K-means++) and improved labeling accuracy (AMP-MA vs VBMF and K-means variants). A proposition establishes that any fixed point of the AMP-MAP algorithm is a blockwise global minimizer of the MAP objective.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on AMP for compressed sensing and bilinear problems (IterFac; generalized/bilinear AMP such as GAMP/BiG-AMP). Compared with these, the paper claims (i) an explicit β parameter enabling MAP in the general-rank case; (ii) factor-row priors that need not be coordinate-wise independent; and (iii) a specific application to K-means with a principled correction to Lloyd’s updates. The authors reference closely related work (Rangan, Parker-Schniter-Cevher, Krzakala-Mézard-Zdeborová), and connect AMP fixed points to Bethe free energy, contrasting to variational Bayes factorizations.

Strengths
- Clear algorithmic advance: a practical AMP scheme for general-rank matrix factorization with flexible (potentially improper) row-wise priors, and an explicit MAP limit via β→∞. The reduction to VB/ICM by removing Onsager terms is insightful.
- Clustering application is neat: the derivation yields an assignment rule with an intuitive leave-one-out-like bias correction and a cluster-size term; empirically, this outperforms Lloyd/K-means++ on both synthetic and ORL.
- Computationally attractive: O(mN) per iteration with simple sufficient statistics; symmetric updates and clear separation of prior-specific denoisers f,G.
- Proposition 1 provides a useful optimality guarantee for fixed points (blockwise global optimality), clarifying the relationship to ICM fixed points.

Weaknesses
- Novelty relative to BiG-AMP/GAMP and EC/EP is somewhat incremental. Introducing β (“temperature”) and taking a MAP limit in AMP-like frameworks is a known idea; the paper would benefit from a sharper positioning versus BiG-AMP (which also treats bilinear models, MMSE/MAP variants, EM, and non-separable priors via vector denoisers).
- Theory is underdeveloped. State evolution is claimed possible but omitted; convergence is assumed; conditions for the positive semidefiniteness in Proposition 1 are hand-waved as “almost all cases.” No damping/stability discussion or diagnostics beyond a heuristic 2-cycle stopping rule.
- Empirical evaluation is narrow. Clustering comparisons are limited to K-means and VBMF; missing baselines include GMM-EM, spherical K-means, spectral clustering, and, critically, BiG-AMP on the same tasks. Only one real dataset is used; preprocessing on ORL (e.g., centering/PCA/whitening) is not specified. Occasional mode collapse (all points in one cluster) is reported.
- Practical details are sparse: how f/G are computed for the “true priors” in AMP-MA, sensitivity to τ estimation, damping, initialization, wall-clock/runtime vs K-means/VBMF, and robustness to mis-specified r̂ are not reported.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The AMP updates are plausible and consistent with prior bilinear AMP derivations. The blockwise optimality proposition is helpful, but assumptions and proofs are deferred. The omission of state evolution and convergence safeguards reduces confidence in reliability across regimes.
- Empirics support the main claims (better K-means loss and accuracy), but breadth and ablations are limited. The oscillation and collapse issues suggest more engineering (damping, debiasing, or safeguards) is needed.

Clarity
- Generally well-written and organized. The algorithm is clearly stated, with good intuition for the clustering corrections. However, reproducibility is hindered by incomplete details: exact priors used in AMP-MA, computation of f/G (closed-form vs numerical), τ estimation protocol, and preprocessing. The extremely tight stopping tolerances (1e-15) seem unrealistic; practical guidance would help.

Originality
- Moderate. The unified β framework and MAP limit for general-rank AMP is a useful packaging, and the explicit K-means instantiation with a principled correction is novel in this framing. However, relative to BiG-AMP and prior bilinear AMP, the methodological advance is incremental.

Significance
- Potentially useful. Practitioners of low-rank matrix factorization and clustering could benefit from a simple, faster alternative to VB/ICM with better local-minima behavior. The K-means improvement is practically relevant, especially in high dimensions. Broader impact would be stronger with more convincing evidence across datasets and comparisons to state-of-the-art bilinear AMP and clustering methods.

Questions and suggestions
- Please compare directly to BiG-AMP (and its MAP/EM variants) on the same synthetic and real datasets, and clarify what your algorithm does that BiG-AMP cannot (beyond fixed-r asymptotics).
- Provide either a concise state-evolution characterization or at least empirical calibration curves (MSE/accuracy vs iterations) and convergence safeguards (damping, step-size, spectral normalization).
- Detail the priors used for AMP-MA, how f and G were computed (analytic vs numerical integration), and sensitivity to prior mis-specification.
- Report runtime and iteration counts vs K-means/K-means++/VBMF, and include damping parameters/initialization strategies. Address the observed collapse modes and whether size-penalty terms or damping mitigate them.
- Expand clustering experiments to include GMM-EM, spherical K-means, spectral methods, and additional datasets; standardize preprocessing (center/PCA).
- Discuss the connection of your K-means correction to leave-one-out or cross-validation bias corrections and to size-regularized K-means; cite related work if applicable.

Pros for acceptance
- Practical, simple AMP algorithm with clear improvements over standard K-means and VB on tested tasks.
- Conceptually clean unification of MMSE/MAP via β and a useful fixed-point optimality property.
- Potentially broad applicability with flexible row-wise priors.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited theoretical development (no state evolution, weak convergence guarantees).
- Incremental novelty relative to existing bilinear AMP literature.
- Narrow empirical validation and missing strong baselines; occasional pathological behavior.

Recommendation: Weak accept. The paper presents a useful and potentially impactful algorithm with promising results and a clear derivation, but would benefit from stronger positioning vs BiG-AMP, more rigorous analysis, and broader experiments.
