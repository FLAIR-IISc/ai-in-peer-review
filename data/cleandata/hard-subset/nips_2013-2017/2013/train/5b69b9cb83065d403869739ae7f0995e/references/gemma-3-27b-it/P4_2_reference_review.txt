This paper introduces an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means clustering. The core idea is to extend existing AMP algorithms for low-rank reconstruction to handle MAP estimation, and to leverage the connection between low-rank reconstruction and K-means clustering. The authors claim their algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, particularly in high-dimensional settings, and provides theoretical justifications through state evolution (though the details of state evolution are omitted due to space constraints).

The paper is well-structured and builds upon existing work in AMP, Bayesian matrix factorization, and K-means clustering. It correctly identifies the computational challenges of Bayesian approaches and positions AMP as an efficient alternative. The extension of the existing AMP algorithm to MAP estimation is a logical step and potentially valuable. The application to K-means is also a reasonable direction, given the inherent link between the problems. The presentation of Algorithm 2 and 3, while dense, is relatively clear and follows a standard notation.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the AMP algorithm appears sound, building on existing literature and addressing the limitations of previous work (e.g., [9]). The connection to the variational Bayes framework is also clearly stated. Proposition 1 provides theoretical support for the algorithm’s optimality.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The problem setting and the motivation for using AMP are clearly explained. The notation is consistent.
*   **Novelty:** Extending AMP algorithms to handle MAP estimation for low-rank matrix reconstruction is a novel contribution. The application of this extended AMP algorithm to clustering, framing K-means as a low-rank reconstruction problem with specific constraints, also represents a novel approach.
*   **Significance:** The potential for an efficient and theoretically grounded algorithm for both low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering is significant. The reported improvements over Lloyd’s K-means, especially in high dimensions, are promising.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Lack of Detailed State Evolution Analysis:** The authors mention the importance of state evolution for analyzing the algorithm’s performance but omit the details due to space constraints. This makes it difficult to assess the theoretical guarantees and limitations of the proposed algorithm.
*   **Limited Comparison to Other State-of-the-Art Methods:** While comparisons are made to Lloyd’s K-means and the variational Bayes approach, a more comprehensive comparison to other recent and competitive methods for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering would strengthen the paper. Specifically, more recent deep learning based approaches should be considered.
*   **Dependence on Parameter τ:** The introduction and subsequent estimation of the parameter τ, while explained, feels somewhat ad-hoc and potentially sensitive. A deeper discussion of its impact and robustness would be beneficial.
*   **Experimental Setup:** The experimental setup lacks detail regarding hyperparameter tuning and the justification for chosen parameters.  The convergence criteria for different algorithms also aren’t fully aligned, making a fair comparison difficult. The impact of initialization, especially for the real dataset, is significant and requires more careful consideration.
*   **Theoretical Connection to Clustering:** The assertion that the proposed algorithm addresses the constraint that each datum should be assigned to exactly one cluster isn't entirely clear. Standard K-means algorithms already enforce this constraint, and it's unclear how the AMP formulation *directly* handles this beyond the constraints imposed on V.

**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
*   The algorithm builds upon established techniques (AMP, Bayesian methods) and addresses limitations in existing approaches.
*   The experimental results demonstrate promising performance improvements over Lloyd’s K-means.
*   The theoretical framework, despite the omission of state evolution details, provides a foundation for understanding the algorithm’s behavior.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

*   The lack of a detailed state evolution analysis hinders a complete understanding of the algorithm’s theoretical properties.
*   The limited comparison to other state-of-the-art methods weakens the claim of superiority.
*   The experimental setup could be more rigorous and transparent.
*   The connection between the algorithm and the unique cluster assignment constraint isn’t fully explained.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The proposed AMP algorithm is novel, technically sound, and shows promising results. While some weaknesses exist, particularly regarding the lack of detailed state evolution analysis and limited comparison to other methods, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should consider adding a discussion of the sensitivity of the algorithm to parameter settings and potentially including a comparison to more recent deep learning based approaches. A more detailed explanation of the connection between the formulation and the single cluster assignment constraint would also be beneficial.
