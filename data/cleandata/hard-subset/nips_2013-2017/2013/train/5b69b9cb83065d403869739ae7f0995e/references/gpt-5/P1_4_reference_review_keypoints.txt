- Paper develops a bilinear AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that incorporates flexible row-factorized priors on U and V.  
- Introduces a β-parameterized posterior p̂(U,V|A;β) that unifies posterior mean (β=1), marginal MAP, and MAP (β→∞) estimators.  
- Derives Algorithm 2 (general bilinear AMP) producing approximate posterior means and MAP/marginal MAP via local denoisers f and G.  
- Proves (Proposition 1) that a converged AMP fixed point is a global minimizer in each block when the other is fixed and coincides with ICM fixed points under mild PSD conditions.  
- Specializes AMP to K-means (one-hot V constraints) yielding Algorithm 3 with Onsager-like corrections: leave-one-out bias correction and cluster-size balancing.  
- Empirically, AMP-KM attains lower K-means loss than Lloyd/K-means++ on synthetic data and AMP-MA achieves highest clustering accuracy with fast convergence.  
- On ORL faces (r̂=40), AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in most trials on both loss and accuracy.  
- Builds on AMP literature (Donoho/Rangan; IterFac; Matsushita & Tanaka) and contrasts with BiG-AMP by focusing on fixed-r asymptotics and allowing intra-row prior dependence.  
- K-means connection relates to VB/ICM work but enforces exact one-hot constraints and yields novel Bethe/Onsager-like assignment corrections.  
- Strength: principled Bethe/AMP derivation with clearly identified Onsager terms and negligible extra cost.  
- Strength: β-trick provides a unified algorithmic framework for multiple estimators.  
- Strength: Proposition 1 offers a useful fixed-point optimality property.  
- Strength: K-means specialization is elegant, interpretable, and empirically effective.  
- Weakness: Omission of state evolution and theoretical performance/convergence guarantees beyond Proposition 1.  
- Weakness: Practical convergence issues (oscillations) noted but damping/step-size/stopping strategies not systematically addressed.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical evaluation—single real dataset, no BiG-AMP comparisons, no runtime results, and no robustness studies (rank/prior/noise mis-specification).  
- Weakness: Lack of experiments beyond clustering to validate general structured-prior claims (e.g., sparse/nonnegative priors).  
- Weakness: Cluster collapse to a single cluster occurs sometimes; mitigation strategies (initialization, priors, damping, annealing) not explored.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written and organized; algorithm updates and Onsager roles are clearly explained.  
- Clarity: Important derivations and proofs are deferred to supplement; short derivation sketch and examples of f/G for common priors would aid reproducibility.  
- Clarity: The connection of certain terms to Onsager corrections should be stated explicitly for readers familiar with AMP.  
- Originality: Nontrivial extension of general-rank bilinear AMP to marginal MAP/MAP via β-parameterization.  
- Originality: Novel K-means specialization enforcing one-hot assignments and producing leave-one-out/size-correction terms distinct from Lloyd/ICM.  
- Significance: Potentially useful alternative to VB/ICM for structured low-rank problems if made robust and scalable; K-means improvements are practically relevant.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled AMP extension, novel effective K-means specialization, and low per-iteration complexity.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing state evolution/convergence analysis, limited empirical scope, practical stability issues, and sparse validation of general-prior claims.  
- Key suggestions: provide state evolution or discuss its validity in fixed-r regime; empirically compare to BiG-AMP and report runtimes.  
- Key suggestions: give guidelines for damping/annealing and handling empty/small clusters; consider priors on cluster proportions.  
- Key suggestions: add experiments for mis-specified r/prior, other priors (nonnegative/sparse), and more real datasets to show generality.  
- Key suggestions: clarify f/G for common priors, include pseudo-code, and discuss computational costs (e.g., r×r inverses).  
- Key suggestions: discuss τ estimation, scale-invariance, and sensitivity to τ updates.  
- Key suggestions: relate assignment correction to leave-one-out risk or information criteria if possible.
