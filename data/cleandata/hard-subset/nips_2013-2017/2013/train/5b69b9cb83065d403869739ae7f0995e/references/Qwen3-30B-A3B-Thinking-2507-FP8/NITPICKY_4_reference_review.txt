

# Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

## Summary
This paper proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering. The authors extend previous AMP work for rank-one matrices to general-rank cases and handle both marginalization and MAP problems. They claim their algorithm is the first to directly incorporate the cluster assignment constraint in low-rank matrix reconstruction for clustering. The numerical experiments show improved performance over Lloyd's K-means and variational Bayes methods.

## Strengths
The paper presents a theoretically grounded extension of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction with clear connections to existing work. The algorithmic formulation is well-motivated, and the numerical experiments demonstrate consistent improvements in clustering performance. The authors provide a thoughtful comparison with related work, particularly the variational Bayes approach.

## Weaknesses

1. **Notational inconsistencies**: The paper uses inconsistent notation for parameters and variables throughout. For example:
   - The noise model specifies Wi,j ~ N(0, mτ) in Section 2.1, but later uses mτ as a variance parameter without justification. This is confusing because m is the number of rows (a dimension), making mτ an unusual scaling factor.
   - In Equation (1), the Gaussian distribution is written as N(0, mτ), but the standard notation should be N(0, mτ) for variance or N(0, √(mτ)) for standard deviation. The paper should clarify this.
   - Algorithm 2 uses inconsistent notation for matrices (e.g., "Λ t u" vs "Λtu" in the text).

2. **Technical inaccuracies**: 
   - The posterior distribution in Equation (8) has a factor of 1/(2mτ) in the exponent, but this is inconsistent with the noise model. The paper should either adjust the noise model or the posterior to be consistent.
   - In Algorithm 3, the authors write "τ appears because m^{-2} ∑_{i=1}^m A^2_{ij} S^t_i was estimated by τm^{-1} ∑_{i=1}^m S^t_i," but this derivation is not provided and appears to be hand-wavy. This weakens the theoretical justification.

3. **Methodological gaps**:
   - The paper claims their clustering algorithm is "the first that directly deals with the constraint that each datum should be assigned to exactly one cluster in the framework of low-rank matrix reconstruction." However, they don't clearly explain how their approach differs from [10, 11] which also address clustering via low-rank matrix reconstruction.
   - The numerical experiments on real data report that "the AMP algorithm yielded just one cluster with all data assigned to it in two trials." This indicates a potential failure mode that isn't discussed, raising concerns about algorithm stability.

4. **Missing details**:
   - The paper references Proposition 1 being in the supplementary material, but the main paper should at least state the key implications of the proposition. This is a significant oversight for a theoretical contribution.
   - They claim "the AMP algorithm for maximum accuracy clustering achieves the highest accuracy," but don't provide statistical significance tests for the results, only averages and standard deviations.

5. **Formatting issues**:
   - The references have inconsistent formatting (e.g., "vol. 37, no. 1, pp. 23–35, May 1997." vs "vol. 5, pp. 1457–1469, Dec. 2004.").
   - The paper uses "r ≪ m, N" in the introduction but later specifies "r ≪ m, N" in the problem setting, which is confusing and should be clarified.

## Recommendation
While the paper presents a theoretically interesting extension of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction with potential practical applications, the numerous minor but substantial technical and notational inconsistencies undermine the paper's credibility. The inconsistent use of mτ as a parameter, the unexplained scaling in the noise model, and the lack of clarity on how the algorithm differs from prior work on clustering via low-rank matrix reconstruction are significant concerns. The paper's claim that their algorithm is the first to handle the cluster assignment constraint is not sufficiently justified.

The paper would benefit from a complete notational cleanup, a clearer explanation of the noise model scaling, a more detailed comparison with [10, 11], and a discussion of the failure modes observed in the real data experiments. Without these corrections, the paper's technical contribution is difficult to evaluate.

Given the paper's focus on minor technical details and the presence of multiple inconsistencies that affect the paper's technical soundness, I recommend rejection. While the approach is potentially interesting, the current presentation contains too many unresolved technical issues to be considered a strong contribution to NIPS.
