- Overall: Valuable extension of AMP to Bayesian low-rank reconstruction and clustering; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem/Summary: Extends AMP for low-rank matrix reconstruction to MAP estimation and connects reconstruction to K-means clustering via a novel AMP-based clustering algorithm.  
- Positioning: Well-situated in literature; distinguishes itself by focusing on fixed rank r and non-independence of matrix factors; references comprehensive.  
- Strength — Generality: Bayesian AMP framework offers flexibility to encode priors (sparsity, non-negativity, etc.).  
- Strength — Theory: Mathematically sound extension with state-evolution discussion and an optimality proposition.  
- Strength — Efficiency: AMP is computationally efficient (linear in matrix size), suited to large-scale problems.  
- Strength — Novel clustering: AMP-based clustering relaxes exclusive assignment constraints and is a novel application.  
- Strength — Empirics: Empirical results outperform Lloyd’s K-means in high dimensions and show strong performance on the ORL face database.  
- Strength — Presentation: Paper is clear, well-organized, and concise.  
- Weakness — State evolution omission: Full state-evolution analysis is missing and should be sketched.  
- Weakness — Convergence: No formal convergence proof or clear discussion of convergence conditions for the algorithm.  
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Treatment and sensitivity of the noise parameter τ and its estimation are under-discussed.  
- Weakness — Limited baselines: Comparisons are limited to K-means/K-means++; missing comparisons to spectral clustering and advanced matrix-factorization methods.  
- Weakness — Computational cost: Computing functions f and G can be costly for complex priors; mitigation strategies are not detailed.  
- For acceptance: Technically sound, efficient, empirically promising, and presents a novel clustering link.  
- Against acceptance: Lack of detailed state-evolution theory, convergence guarantees, and comprehensive comparisons.  
- Recommendations: Add a state-evolution sketch; discuss convergence conditions; analyze τ sensitivity; expand baseline comparisons; propose ways to reduce f and G computational cost.  
- Conclusion: Strong contribution that would be improved by addressing the listed minor theoretical, empirical, and practical gaps.
