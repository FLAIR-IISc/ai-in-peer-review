- Summary: Proposes an AMP-based unified framework for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction supporting both marginal posterior means and MAP estimation, and applies it to K-means clustering with a direct one-cluster-per-data constraint, showing empirical gains over Lloyd's K-means and variational Bayes.

- Quality: Technically rigorous with a sound derivation, convergence analysis linking AMP fixed points to ICM, and comprehensive experiments (500 synthetic, 50 real trials); acknowledges convergence limitations and required assumptions.

- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized, consistent notation, clear pseudocode (Algorithms 2–3), and effective figures that aid reproducibility and understanding.

- Originality: Two main contributions — (1) extending AMP to a unified marginalization+MAP low-rank framework, and (2) a novel clustering application that directly enforces one-cluster-per-data within that framework.

- Significance: Practically valuable due to O(mN) per-iteration complexity and demonstrated improvements in accuracy and convergence speed, including real-world ORL face data.

- Strength 1: Clear theoretical foundation with rigorous AMP derivation.

- Strength 2: Unified treatment of marginalization and MAP problems.

- Strength 3: Novel clustering approach that enforces assignment constraints directly.

- Strength 4: Comprehensive experimental evaluation on synthetic and real datasets.

- Strength 5: Theoretical analysis showing connections to ICM and potential avoidance of poor local minima.

- Strength 6: Well-written presentation with clear pseudocode enabling reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient explanation for why the performance gap between AMP and Lloyd’s K-means increases with higher r (number of clusters).

- Weakness 2: Claim of being first to handle one-cluster-per-data constraint could use a more thorough comparison with prior work [10,11].

- Weakness 3: Lacks clear explanation for why AMP converges faster than variational Bayes for maximum accuracy clustering.

- Weakness 4: No statistical significance testing reported for experimental differences (e.g., t-tests).

- Weakness 5: Superficial explanation for the appearance of parameter τ despite it not being part of the K-means objective.

- Argument for Acceptance 1: Technically sound extension of AMP to handle both marginalization and MAP in low-rank reconstruction.

- Argument for Acceptance 2: Novel and well-motivated clustering application.

- Argument for Acceptance 3: Strong theoretical analysis and extensive empirical validation.

- Argument for Acceptance 4: Low computational complexity with demonstrated empirical advantages.

- Argument for Acceptance 5: Clear writing and organization that support reproducibility.

- Argument Against Acceptance 1: Needs deeper explanation for performance scaling with cluster count r.

- Argument Against Acceptance 2: Requires more thorough comparisons to related clustering works [10,11].

- Argument Against Acceptance 3: Experimental results lack rigorous statistical significance analysis.

- Argument Against Acceptance 4: Incomplete explanation of the role of parameter τ.

- Overall Recommendation: Strong paper recommended for acceptance with confidence; weaknesses are relatively minor and addressable in a camera-ready version.
