Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization under additive Gaussian noise, with the goal of incorporating rich structure on factors via priors. It derives an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for general-rank matrices that unifies marginalization (sum-product) and MAP (max-sum) inference using a temperature parameter β, extending prior AMP approaches for bilinear models that primarily targeted posterior means. A second contribution specializes the framework to clustering by encoding one-hot assignments via a discrete prior on rows of V, yielding an AMP variant of K-means with principled “Onsager” corrections in the assignment step. The authors provide a fixed-point optimality property for the MAP version and demonstrate empirical benefits over Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and the ORL faces dataset; for maximum accuracy clustering (β=1), their AMP variant outperforms a variational Bayes matrix factorization (VBMF) baseline.

This work is related to IterFac for rank-one [Rangan & Fletcher], AMP/GAMP for linear inverse problems [Donoho et al.; Rangan], and bilinear/matrix AMP variants (dictionary learning, blind calibration, “BiG-AMP” by Parker et al., and Krzakala et al.). Compared to prior bilinear AMP, the authors emphasize: (i) a β-tempered derivation to handle both marginalization and MAP; (ii) a derivation that fixes rank r as m,N→∞; (iii) allowance for within-row dependencies in priors; and (iv) a direct discrete prior for K-means assignments.

Strengths
- Technical contribution: A clear AMP derivation for the bilinear problem that interpolates between sum-product (β=1) and max-sum (β→∞), with explicit update equations and a fixed-point property (Proposition 1) that links AMP fixed points to block-wise optima of the MAP objective. The form reveals the Onsager corrections relative to VB/ICM.
- Practical algorithm for clustering: The specialization to K-means with one-hot V yields a simple assignment rule with data-dependent corrections that intuitively debias cluster center estimates and favor underpopulated clusters. This is a novel, principled alternative to Lloyd’s algorithm within the same loss.
- Empirical results: On synthetic high-dimensional Gaussian mixtures, AMP-KM achieves lower K-means loss than Lloyd/K-means++ and AMP-MA achieves higher assignment accuracy than VBMF. On ORL, AMP-KM improves both K-means loss and accuracy in most trials.
- Computational considerations: Per-iteration complexity is O(mN). The algorithmic connection to VB/ICM makes the improvement interpretable; extra terms are O(m+N).

Weaknesses
Quality
- Theory is incomplete: State evolution (SE)—central to AMP’s predictivity and correctness—is not provided. The β→∞ limit and the use of general (possibly improper) priors raise technical questions about convergence, stability, and correctness without SE or damping analysis. Proposition 1 is useful but limited (applies at fixed points under PSD conditions, does not address existence/uniqueness, convergence, or global optimality).
- Practical robustness: The clustering algorithm exhibits oscillations and occasional collapse to a single cluster; mitigation via ad hoc stopping is unsatisfactory. No discussion of standard AMP stabilizers (damping, adaptive step sizes, mean-removal, spectral initialization).
- Inference cost: For β<∞, f and G can require r̂-dimensional integration; computational burden and numerical strategies are not detailed for nontrivial priors.
- Evaluation breadth: Comparisons for clustering omit strong baselines such as EM for Gaussian mixtures (with spherical or diagonal covariances), spectral clustering, or modern initialization heuristics beyond K-means++. For matrix factorization, no benchmarks vs. BiG-AMP, or ablations showing the impact of Onsager terms relative to VB/ICM. Runtime (wall-clock) comparisons are not reported. Sensitivity to rank mis-specification, noise level τ, class imbalance, and non-Gaussian noise is not explored.

Clarity
- Overall readable, but some derivation details are deferred; convergence conditions and the role of the scaling mτ could be better motivated. The notation is dense; guidance on computing f/G for common priors (e.g., sparsity, nonnegativity) would improve reproducibility. Reference formatting needs attention.

Originality
- The β-tempered unification and direct discrete-prior AMP formulation for K-means assignments are interesting. However, the relation to existing max-sum/sum-product bilinear AMP (e.g., BiG-AMP’s MAP variant) should be clarified more rigorously. The claim of allowing within-row dependence is intriguing, but how this departs from standard factorized priors in bilinear AMP needs sharper exposition.

Significance
- A practical, better-performing alternative to K-means/Lloyd in high dimensions is valuable. The general AMP framework for structured matrix factorization could impact broader applications. However, the missing SE analysis, limited comparisons, and noted instabilities reduce immediate impact.

Pros for acceptance
- Principled AMP derivation bridging marginal and MAP inference in bilinear models.
- Novel and effective K-means specialization with interpretable corrections and wins over strong K-means++ on real data.
- Low per-iteration cost; clear algorithmic form; proposition linking fixed points to block-wise optima.

Cons for acceptance
- No state evolution or convergence analysis; observed oscillations/collapses in clustering.
- Limited breadth of empirical comparisons (missing EM/spectral, BiG-AMP), no runtime reporting, no sensitivity studies.
- Practical computation of f/G for nontrivial priors under β<∞ is under-specified.
- Claims of difference from related bilinear AMP work need clearer positioning.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide SE (even empirically validated) for both β=1 and β→∞, or at least damping/stability analysis and recommended settings.
- Compare against EM for GMMs, spectral clustering, and BiG-AMP (sum-product and max-sum), including ablations removing Onsager terms to quantify their contribution.
- Report wall-clock time and scalability; study robustness to initialization, τ estimation, rank mis-specification, class imbalance, and non-Gaussian noise/missing data.
- Include practical guidance (and code) for computing f/G under common priors; discuss when integrals/minimizations are tractable.
- Clarify the independence assumptions and how the proposed derivation accommodates within-row dependencies.

Recommendation: Weak reject
The paper has appealing ideas and promising empirical results, especially for K-means, but lacks theoretical backing (SE/convergence), broader comparisons, and robustness analysis expected for NeurIPS. With stronger theory and a more comprehensive empirical evaluation (including comparisons to bilinear AMP baselines and classical clustering methods), it could become a solid contribution. Confidence: medium.
