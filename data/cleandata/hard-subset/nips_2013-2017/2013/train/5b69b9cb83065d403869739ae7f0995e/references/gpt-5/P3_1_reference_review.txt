Summary and relation to prior work
This paper develops an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction under a general bilinear model A = U0V0^T + W with Gaussian noise, where the factor priors p̂u and p̂v can encode diverse structural constraints (e.g., sparsity, nonnegativity, discreteness). A one-parameter “temperature” β is introduced: β=1 recovers MMSE-style marginal posterior approximations, and β→∞ targets MAP, yielding a single algorithmic framework. The method produces iterative updates with Onsager-like correction terms; the associated “denoisers” f and Jacobians G are the posterior mean/covariance (β=1) or MAP/Hessian inverse (β→∞) under the local surrogate q̂. As an application, the authors instantiate the framework to K-means by taking a discrete prior on the rows of V (one-hot cluster assignments) and a flat prior on U, leading to an AMP variant of K-means (AMP-KM) with an assignment update that includes explicit corrections for self-influence and cluster-size effects. They also propose an AMP-based “maximum accuracy” clustering (AMP-MA) using β=1 and categorical priors.

The work builds on AMP for sparse linear models [Donoho et al. 2009; Rangan 2011], the IterFac rank-1 AMP [Rangan and Fletcher 2012], and an earlier general-rank AMP for posterior means [Matsushita and Tanaka 2012], and is related to bilinear GAMP (BiG-AMP) [Parker et al. 2013] and recent AMP for dictionary learning [Krzakala et al. 2013]. The main claimed advances are: (i) extending bilinear AMP to support MAP via a β→∞ limit, yielding f∞ and G∞; (ii) an explicit K-means specialization that directly enforces the one-hot constraint and introduces principled Onsager corrections in the assignment step.

Quality
- Methodological correctness: The AMP derivation is plausible and consistent with Bethe/Onsager reasoning for dense random models. The denoiser-based formulation and the recognition that the extra terms vs. VB/ICM are Onsager corrections are sound. Proposition 1 provides a useful blockwise optimality statement at fixed points, helping interpret the algorithm as a proximalized ICM; however, the required assumptions (positive semidefiniteness of sums of site-wise Jacobians) and the relevance to discrete priors could be discussed more rigorously.
- Theory: The paper states that state evolution (SE) exists but omits it for space. For NeurIPS, at least a succinct statement of SE equations, their assumptions (randomness, large-system limit with fixed r̂), and a sanity check via simulation would strengthen the contribution. Convergence is not guaranteed; practical stabilization (damping/annealing) is only briefly mentioned. For the K-means specialization, conditions under which Λt_v remains positive semidefinite (or how violations are handled) are not analyzed.
- Experiments: The synthetic study (m=800, N=1600) shows that AMP-KM improves K-means loss over Lloyd and k-means++ for larger r, and that AMP-MA achieves higher accuracy than VBMF-MA. However, there are fairness concerns: Lloyd/k-means++ use different initializations than AMP variants in the synthetic study (random vs careful seeding), and the VB baseline is not clearly instantiated with the same informative priors as AMP-MA. The experiments vary r but not noise level τ, rank mismatch r̂≠r, cluster imbalance, or covariance structure (all clusters are spherical and equally likely). The ORL experiment is promising but lacks details (PCA/normalization, τ learning dynamics) and comparisons to standard alternatives (spherical GMM with EM, spectral clustering, deterministic annealing k-means). Runtime comparisons report iterations, not wall-clock or per-iteration cost, which matters because f/G can be expensive for general priors.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized. The AMP updates are presented compactly; it would help to:
- Simplify Λ expressions to highlight the Onsager term ((1/β − 1) factor).
- Provide concrete examples of f/G for common priors (sparse Laplace, nonnegativity, one-hot).
- Describe damping/annealing and stopping criteria recommended to address oscillations (noting Vt=Vt−2 termination suggests limit cycles).
- Clarify the τ update (the residual-based estimator) and whether a principled EM-style update is possible.
- For ORL, specify preprocessing (centering, scaling, dimensionality reduction).

Originality
The β-tempering to unify MMSE and MAP in an AMP framework is interesting and practically useful. The explicit K-means specialization that preserves the one-hot constraint and yields assignment corrections via Onsager terms is a nice contribution and distinguishes the approach from VBMF and Lloyd/ICM. However, differentiation from BiG-AMP should be sharpened: BiG-AMP also treats bilinear models with general priors and can be run with MAP denoisers. The claimed distinctions (fixing rank rather than rank ratio in asymptotics; allowing dependence among components within a row via non-factorized p̂u/p̂v) are worth highlighting more explicitly, ideally with pointers to where prior work falls short (e.g., specific independence assumptions or SE regimes).

Significance
If validated more broadly, the approach could impact both Bayesian matrix factorization (flexible priors, MAP/MMSE from one engine) and clustering (a principled AMP alternative to Lloyd). The K-means instantiation is attractive in high dimensions, and the reported gains are encouraging. To maximize impact, the paper should (i) provide at least minimal SE evidence, (ii) include stronger baselines for clustering, and (iii) demonstrate robustness to model misspecification (r̂, τ, non-spherical clusters).

Specific suggestions and questions
- Please include a brief SE summary and a numerical SE-vs-empirical check on synthetic data.
- For clustering, compare to EM for spherical GMMs, spectral clustering, and deterministic annealing k-means (Rose, 1998), which is closely related to your β parameter.
- Use consistent initialization across methods (e.g., k-means++ seeding for all) or report performance vs. number of restarts.
- Report wall-clock times; comment on cost of f/G for various priors and how it scales with r̂.
- Provide an annealing schedule in β and damping for stability; your observed V oscillations suggest these would help.
- Analyze robustness to r̂≠r and unbalanced clusters; what happens when clusters have different variances?
- Clarify Proposition 1’s conditions for discrete priors; when does ∑Tj become zero and how does that affect the proximal interpretation?
- Discuss relation to BiG-AMP more deeply and clarify what your framework enables that BiG-AMP does not.
- Minor: ensure posterior propriety when using improper priors; fix small notation glitches and explicitly show the simplification in Λ updates.

Pros
- Conceptually clean AMP framework that unifies MMSE and MAP via β.
- Principled K-means specialization with clear algorithmic differences from Lloyd/ICM.
- Empirical gains on synthetic and a real dataset; simple O(mN) per-iteration scaling.
- Useful fixed-point optimality interpretation (Proposition 1).

Cons
- Missing state evolution and limited convergence analysis; some claims deferred to supplement.
- Comparisons and ablations are limited; initialization fairness and baselines could be improved.
- Practical details (τ learning, damping, β schedules, preprocessing) are thin.
- Novelty vs. BiG-AMP not fully clarified.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The paper is promising and likely to be impactful with strengthened theory and experimental validation. Addressing the above points—especially SE evidence and more comprehensive, fair comparisons—would make it a strong submission.

Confidence: 0.8/1.0
