Summary
This paper develops an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction in the bilinear setting A ≈ UV⊤ with flexible priors on the rows of U and V. A key idea is introducing a temperature/annealing parameter β so that the same algorithm addresses both marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). The algorithm generalizes prior AMP work for bilinear models (e.g., IterFac for rank-1; prior AMP for posterior means only) by handling a broad class of priors p̂u, p̂v and enabling MAP via a limiting f∞ operator. The authors also derive a specialized AMP instance for K-means-type clustering by enforcing one-hot constraints on rows of V, yielding a novel hard-clustering algorithm with Onsager corrections to the assignment step. They provide a convergence fixed-point optimality property (Proposition 1) and empirical evaluations on synthetic Gaussian-mixture data and the ORL faces dataset, showing improved K-means loss and accuracy over Lloyd’s K-means/K-means++ and improved accuracy and speed for the “maximum accuracy” (marginal MAP) variant relative to a VB matrix factorization baseline.

Relation to prior work
- AMP/GAMP/BiG-AMP: The paper builds on AMP/GAMP ideas (Donoho et al. 2009; Rangan 2011) and IterFac (Rangan & Fletcher 2012) toward bilinear inference. It is close to contemporaneous bilinear AMP (BiG-AMP; Parker et al. 2013) and AMP for dictionary learning/blind calibration (Krzakala et al. 2013), but differs by fixing rank r (not r/m) in the asymptotics and by allowing dependencies within each row vector (only row-wise independence). It also makes an explicit β-bridging between marginal and MAP inference and provides a K-means instantiation that directly enforces one-hot constraints.
- Variational Bayes/ICM: The derived updates reduce to VB/ICM if Onsager terms are dropped; this connection is clearly described.
- Clustering: The low-rank formulation of K-means is classical; the novelty here is the AMP derivation that modifies assignments with principled corrections.

Strengths
- Conceptual: Clear unification of marginal and MAP inference via β, and a principled AMP derivation that retains dependence between U and V (Bethe approximation) compared to factorized VB.
- Algorithmic: General-purpose f/G denoisers enable a wide range of priors; for MAP, f∞ can be computed via a local quadratic plus prior term, and G∞ via Hessians.
- K-means specialization: The resulting assignment rule includes Onsager corrections that account for center bias and cluster size, providing a plausible mechanism for escaping poor local minima.
- Theory: Proposition 1 gives a useful fixed-point optimality property (coordinate-wise global minima under PSD conditions).
- Empirics: On synthetic data, AMP-KM attains better K-means loss than Lloyd/K-means++ and AMP-MA attains higher accuracy than VBMF-MA with fast convergence. On ORL faces, AMP-KM often improves both loss and accuracy over K-means++.

Weaknesses
- Theoretical completeness: State evolution (SE), which is central to AMP’s predictive power and convergence characterization, is deferred entirely. The β→∞ limit and use of G∞ via the implicit function theorem are stated without detailed conditions. Convergence is not guaranteed; oscillations occur and are handled by ad hoc stopping.
- Stability/robustness: No discussion of damping or parameter tuning, which are often necessary in bilinear AMP. The practical estimation of τ is heuristic; sensitivity analyses (e.g., to τ, rank mis-specification, non-Gaussian noise) are absent.
- Experimental scope and baselines: Clustering comparisons omit strong and natural baselines such as EM/VB for mixture of Gaussians (equal/unknown covariance), spectral clustering, spherical K-means, or NMF-based clustering. Only a single real dataset is used; no timing or memory comparisons; no ablations of the Onsager terms or β.
- Reproducibility details: Precise priors p̂ for AMP-MA, initialization schemes beyond random/K++ and stopping/damping are not fully specified; no code is provided.
- Claims of novelty: While the one-hot AMP instantiation appears new, the paper should more carefully position itself relative to BiG-AMP (bilinear generalized AMP) for both posterior means and MAP, and clarify what is genuinely unavailable in those frameworks (e.g., exact one-hot hard constraints vs. softmax priors).

Quality
Technically sound overall with a correct AMP structure and reasonable specialization to K-means; fixed-point property is useful. However, the lack of SE analysis and convergence/stability guidelines limits the rigor. Empirical evidence is promising but not comprehensive.

Clarity
Generally clear and well-organized. The β-augmented posterior and the roles of f/G are explained well. Some derivations are deferred; adding a short pseudo-code block and a subsection on practicalities (initialization, damping, τ estimation) would improve readability and reproducibility.

Originality
Moderate to good. Extends general-rank AMP to handle MAP estimation with broad priors and directly enforces one-hot constraints for clustering in an AMP framework. The β-bridging is neat, though related annealing ideas exist.

Significance
Potentially significant for scalable Bayesian matrix factorization and hard clustering. If stabilized and broadened, practitioners could benefit. Current empirical scope and theoretical guarantees fall short of fully demonstrating impact.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear and principled AMP derivation with β-bridging; general-purpose framework
- Novel and effective AMP-based K-means variant with interpretable corrections
- Fixed-point optimality property
- Promising empirical gains on synthetic and a real dataset

Cons for acceptance
- No state evolution or convergence/stability analysis; observed oscillations
- Limited baselines and datasets; no timing or robustness studies
- Practical guidance (damping, τ selection, rank mis-specification) is minimal
- Relation to BiG-AMP and other bilinear AMP works needs deeper comparison

Questions for authors
- Can you provide damping or step-size strategies that stabilize AMP-KM and AMP-MA? Did you try adaptive damping?
- How sensitive are results to τ estimation and to rank mis-specification r̂ ≠ r?
- How does your method compare to EM/VB for spherical/full-covariance GMMs and to spectral clustering on the same datasets?
- Can you include brief SE results or empirical SE calibration (even in supplement)?
- For the one-hot prior, could a softmax/Dirichlet prior perform similarly without hard constraints?

Recommendation
Borderline. I lean weak reject in the current form due to missing SE/convergence analysis and limited empirical validation against strong baselines, but I find the ideas interesting and promising. With strengthened theory (SE or convergence guidance) and broader experiments (including standard clustering baselines, robustness, and runtime), this work would merit acceptance.
