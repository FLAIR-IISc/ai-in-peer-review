Summary and relation to prior work
The paper develops an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with separable row-wise priors p̂U(U)=∏i p̂u(ui) and p̂V(V)=∏j p̂v(vj). A “temperature” parameter β bridges posterior-mean inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞) within a single derivation. The resulting algorithm has Onsager-type corrections and reduces to variational-Bayes/ICM updates if those corrections are removed. A fixed-point optimality proposition shows that, under a mild PSD assumption, each converged iterate is coordinatewise globally optimal. The authors then instantiate the framework for clustering by taking a one-hot prior on rows of V, obtaining an AMP variant of K-means whose assignment step includes principled bias-correction terms. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and ORL faces indicate improvements over Lloyd’s K-means and variational Bayes matrix factorization (VBMF) in loss or accuracy, with fast convergence.

The work is close to prior AMP/BiG-AMP lines: IterFac for rank-one; AMP for general rank; and bilinear GAMP (BiG-AMP) for generalized observation models. The claimed novelties are: (i) a unified β-parametrization enabling both MMSE and MAP (the latter not treated in [9]); (ii) a derivation that fixes rank r (not r/m) and allows intra-row dependencies within ui and vj; and (iii) a direct treatment of hard one-hot assignment constraints for K-means within AMP.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity: the β-parameter unifies MMSE and MAP and yields clean f/G updates, with an intuitive interpretation (means/covariances for β=1; proximal maps/Hessians for β→∞).
- Algorithmic insight: the AMP K-means assignment rule includes leave-one-out bias correction and cluster-size regularization terms derived from Onsager corrections, not ad hoc heuristics.
- Practicality: per-iteration complexity O(mN) with small overhead for Onsager terms; closed-form updates for discrete priors; convex subproblems for many priors in the MAP limit.
- Theory: the coordinatewise optimality proposition at fixed points is useful and clarifies the relationship to ICM/VB (AMP has no more fixed points and may avoid poor local minima).
- Empirics: On synthetic high-dimensional mixtures, AMP-KM reduces K-means loss relative to Lloyd and K-means++; AMP-MA achieves higher accuracy than VBMF; improvements also shown on ORL with K-means++ seeding.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Limited theoretical support: State evolution is claimed feasible but omitted; the convergence behavior and conditions ensuring PSD of ∑i S^t_i, ∑j T^t_j (assumed in Proposition 1) are not established. Practical oscillations are reported and handled by ad hoc stopping.
- Relation to BiG-AMP/max-sum GAMP: The β→∞ limit appears to correspond to max-sum (MAP) GAMP. The paper should precisely position its MAP variant relative to max-sum BiG-AMP, clarify what is new (e.g., block-vector priors over R^r rather than scalar-separable entries, fixed-r scaling), and cite/maximally contrast algorithmic forms and guarantees.
- Experimental scope: Synthetic data are isotropic Gaussians with balanced clusters and known r; only one real dataset (ORL) is used. Important regimes (mis-specified r̂, unbalanced cluster sizes, non-spherical clusters/covariances, non-Gaussian noise, missing data) are not evaluated. No runtime comparisons are provided, and the tolerance 1e-15 is unusually strict.
- Baselines: Missing comparisons to EM for Gaussian mixtures (with shared spherical covariance), spherical K-means, spectral clustering, or recent scalable clustering methods. Since the clustering model corresponds to a GMM with equal covariances, EM is a natural baseline for both loss and accuracy.
- Sensitivity and hyperparameters: The K-means variant requires estimating τ from residuals; sensitivity to this choice is not analyzed. The assignment correction scales like m/n_l and may dominate distances for small clusters, potentially inducing empty/unbalanced clusters (the real-data section notes collapse without careful seeding).
- Clarity gaps: Several derivational steps are deferred; the identity G∞=(Λ−H)^{-1} needs conditions (convex −log p̂ and Λ−H ≻ 0). The “almost all cases” claim for PSD of aggregated G/G∞ needs justification.

Quality
Technically sound at a high level; the derivation and algorithmic forms are consistent with AMP principles. However, the absence of state evolution and rigorous convergence conditions, and limited ablations/sensitivity analyses, weaken the empirical and theoretical support. Proposition 1 is useful but rests on assumptions that need checking in practice (especially for the discrete prior).

Clarity
Generally well organized. The algorithmic forms and interpretations are clearly stated, and the K-means specialization is elucidated. The paper would benefit from:
- A clearer comparison table vs VBMF/ICM and (max-sum) BiG-AMP (assumptions, priors, β handling).
- A short derivation sketch of the Onsager terms for the clustering case to make (18b) less of a “black box.”
- A discussion on damping/stepsize and practical convergence safeguards.

Originality
Moderate. The β-trick and MAP limit are standard in the GAMP literature, but applying AMP with hard one-hot constraints to derive a corrected K-means assignment appears novel and interesting. Allowing non-factorized components within each row is also a differentiator, though its practical impact is not demonstrated.

Significance
Potentially significant for large-scale clustering and matrix factorization with structured priors, especially if the assignment correction systematically improves over Lloyd in high dimensions. Broader impact hinges on demonstrating robustness beyond the narrow experimental settings and clarifying novelty over existing BiG-AMP variants.

Constructive suggestions and questions
- Provide a supplement with state evolution equations for a representative prior (e.g., Gaussian) and an empirical validation plot.
- Compare to EM for GMM with shared spherical covariance and to spectral/spherical K-means; include runtime.
- Analyze sensitivity to τ estimation and add damping; report how often oscillations occur and how damping affects convergence and quality.
- Study mis-specified r̂ and unbalanced clusters; report empty-cluster handling.
- Clarify the relation to max-sum BiG-AMP and whether your algorithm reduces to it under specific separability assumptions.
- Ablate the correction terms in (18b) to quantify their effect.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear, principled AMP derivation with a novel and useful K-means instantiation.
- Empirical gains on high-dimensional synthetic and a real dataset; low computational overhead.
- The fixed-point optimality proposition and connection to VB/ICM are insightful.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited theoretical backing (no state evolution, convergence guarantees) and practical oscillations.
- Narrow experimental evaluation and missing standard baselines.
- Ambiguity in novelty relative to max-sum BiG-AMP; some claims need tighter positioning.

Overall recommendation
Borderline accept (weak accept if the authors clarify the relation to max-sum BiG-AMP, add at least a GMM-EM baseline and a brief state-evolution validation; otherwise borderline reject). The AMP-based K-means variant is the most compelling contribution and merits wider visibility if robustness is further substantiated.
