- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means clustering.  
- Extends existing AMP to handle general-rank matrices and supports both marginalization and MAP estimation via a unified β parameter.  
- Novelly applies AMP to clustering while directly enforcing the "exactly one cluster" assignment constraint.  
- Provides theoretical justification, including a proof that AMP fixed points correspond to ICM fixed points (Proposition 1).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, outperforming Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ on artificial and real datasets.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and statistically robust (500 artificial instances, 50 real-data trials).  
- Derivation and algorithm presentation are clear, well-organized, and use consistent notation.  
- Comparison with prior AMP and variational Bayes work is thorough and clearly differentiates contributions.  
- Conceptual novelty: unified β framework interpolating marginalization and MAP and first direct handling of exact cluster constraint in low-rank clustering.  
- Significant practical benefits: O(mN) per-iteration efficiency and theoretical grounding (state evolution mentioned).  
- Weakness: omits state evolution analysis from the paper due to space, weakening the theoretical exposition.  
- Weakness: limited sensitivity analysis for algorithm parameter τ and unclear explanation of τ’s role.  
- Weakness: evaluation focuses on K-means only and does not explore other clustering paradigms (e.g., density-based methods).  
- Recommendation: strong accept — paper is a substantial, well-supported contribution suitable for NIPS.
