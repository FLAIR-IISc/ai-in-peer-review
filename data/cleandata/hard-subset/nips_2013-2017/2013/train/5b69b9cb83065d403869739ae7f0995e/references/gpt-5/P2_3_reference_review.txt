Summary
The paper proposes an AMP-based framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on the factors and shows how it yields (i) a unified algorithm that covers both marginalization (MMSE/marginal MAP) and MAP estimation via a “temperature” parameter β, and (ii) a new K-means-type clustering algorithm that directly enforces one-hot cluster assignments within the low-rank factorization view. The algorithm resembles variational Bayes (VB) factorization but adds AMP “Onsager” correction terms that account for dependence between U and V. A simple optimality proposition is provided for fixed points in the MAP limit. Experiments on synthetic data and the ORL faces dataset show that the AMP variant reduces K-means loss vs. Lloyd/K-means++ and increases accuracy for a Bayesian “maximum accuracy” variant, with relatively few iterations.

Relation to prior work
The paper is well-situated within AMP/GAMP literature, referencing work on vector AMP, IterFac for rank-1, and a prior general-rank AMP [9] that estimates posterior means. It also cites BiG-AMP-like work [14] (bilinear generalized AMP) and dictionary learning AMP [13]. The connection to VB matrix factorization [4,5] is explicit: removing the extra AMP terms reproduces VB/ICM updates, clarifying the algorithmic novelty and the role of Onsager corrections. On the clustering side, the reduction of K-means to a constrained low-rank factorization is known, but imposing the one-hot constraint within an AMP derivation seems new. That said, the comparison to BiG-AMP is not sufficiently developed; BiG-AMP can often be instantiated for MAP or MMSE via appropriate denoisers, and the paper should clarify empirically and theoretically how the proposed algorithm differs beyond the scaling assumption (fixed r vs. r/m constant) and prior independence across components.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The algorithmic development appears consistent with standard AMP derivations (with β-temperature to interpolate between MMSE and MAP). The key updates and the interpretation of the extra terms are plausible, and the proposition gives limited but meaningful guarantees at fixed points (coordinate-wise global minimizers under PSD conditions). However, the state evolution (SE) analysis—central to AMP’s credibility—is deferred, leaving theoretical claims about performance and iteration complexity unsubstantiated. The clustering specialization is carefully derived; the correction terms in the assignment step are well-motivated (debiasing current assignments and size-regularization).
- Empirics: Synthetic experiments are reasonably sized and repeated over many instances, showing consistent improvements in K-means loss for AMP-KM and accuracy for AMP-MA. On ORL, the AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in most trials, though it occasionally collapses to a single cluster and exhibits oscillations (handled with a heuristic stopping rule). Comparisons are limited: there is no BiG-AMP baseline, no EM for GMMs (a natural comparator to K-means), and no robustness tests to prior mismatch (AMP-MA uses true priors).
- Reproducibility: Many derivation details are in the supplement; algorithmic details are mostly clear, but τ estimation is heuristic, and code availability is not mentioned.

Clarity
The paper is dense but generally well-organized. The AMP updates are clearly specified; the mapping between the general algorithm and the clustering specialization is explicit. The role of β, f, and G is well explained, including MAP limit behavior and the identity G∞ = (Λ − H)−1. The discussion contrasting VB/ICM vs. AMP via the extra O(m+N) correction terms is helpful. Points to improve:
- More explicit, side-by-side comparison with BiG-AMP and with the general-rank AMP [9] (what precisely could not be done there, and why β>1 enables MAP here).
- Brief derivation sketch in main text (one or two key approximations/assumptions) to guide readers who may not consult the supplement.
- Clarify the assumption “no independence among components of each row” and how it is incorporated in f/G (e.g., multivariate priors across the r̂ dimensions).

Originality
Moderately strong. Using β to interpolate between MMSE and MAP within AMP is not new per se, but applying it to a bilinear low-rank setting with general multivariate priors and showing an explicit reduction to a practical K-means algorithm with interpretable corrections is novel and useful. The claim of being the first to handle the exact one-hot assignment constraint within a low-rank AMP framework appears credible; still, the relationship to BiG-AMP (which in principle can handle discrete priors) should be clarified to cement novelty.

Significance
Promising, but the current evidence is mixed:
- The AMP-KM algorithm shows consistent gains over standard K-means/K-means++ on synthetic and ORL, with small iteration counts. This could matter to practitioners if the gains persist more broadly and the occasional failures (oscillations, collapse) are mitigated.
- The general low-rank AMP with MAP capability could be impactful if backed by SE analysis and broader benchmarks (e.g., recommendation, denoising with non-Gaussian priors).
- Adoption would benefit from stronger baselines (BiG-AMP, EM-GMM), ablations (demonstrating the effect of Onsager terms), robustness to prior mismatch, and larger-scale timing studies.

Pros
- Clear, principled AMP derivation that unifies MMSE and MAP via β.
- Insightful link to K-means with explicit, interpretable correction terms.
- Solid synthetic evidence and meaningful real-data improvement on ORL.
- Low per-iteration complexity and few iterations in practice.

Cons
- No SE analysis; limited theoretical guarantees.
- Insufficient comparison to the closely related BiG-AMP framework and EM-GMM for clustering.
- Occasional nonconvergence/collapse on real data; heuristic stopping.
- AMP-MA results rely on true priors; robustness not assessed.

Questions for authors
- How does your algorithm differ from BiG-AMP instantiated for MAP/MMSE with multivariate priors? Can you include a direct empirical comparison?
- Can you provide SE equations (even briefly) and any empirical validation?
- How sensitive is AMP-MA to prior misspecification?
- Can the oscillations/collapse in AMP-KM be addressed (e.g., damping, adaptive τ, or explicit regularization on cluster sizes)?
- Runtime comparisons vs. K-means++ and EM-GMM?

Recommendation
Borderline accept. The paper offers a neat, interpretable AMP-based improvement to K-means and a general framework that plausibly extends AMP to MAP estimation for bilinear factorization with multivariate priors. To meet NeurIPS standards convincingly, the camera-ready should strengthen the comparison to BiG-AMP/EM-GMM, include at least a sketch of SE, and address stability/robustness issues.
