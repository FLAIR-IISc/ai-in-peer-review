- Introduces an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending AMP to MAP estimation for general rank and applying it to K-means clustering with hard single-cluster constraints.  
- Empirical results show the AMP-based clustering (AMP-KM) outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Properly situates the work among prior AMP and low-rank methods, highlighting its novelty in MAP extension and constrained clustering formulation.  
- References are comprehensive and relevant to the topic.  
- Novel application of AMP to MAP low-rank reconstruction and directly enforced hard clustering constraint.  
- Provides theoretical justification via belief propagation/Bethe approximation; Proposition 1 offers optimality under certain conditions.  
- Algorithm has linear computational complexity, making it suitable for large-scale problems.  
- Strong empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrating consistent performance gains.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented with clear equations and algorithmic detail.  
- Does not present state evolution analysis or predictions despite discussing its potential benefits.  
- Practical implementation depends on tuning parameters (e.g., τ) without thorough discussion of selection or sensitivity.  
- Lacks a formal convergence proof for the general AMP algorithm.  
- Comparisons to other clustering methods are limited; more comparisons (e.g., spectral clustering, DBSCAN) are needed.  
- Provides little guidance on how to choose priors for different applications.  
- Missing implementation details, which hinders reproducibility.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, empirically strong, well-presented, and relevant to the community.  
- Against acceptance concerns: absence of state evolution analysis, lack of convergence proof, limited benchmarking, and insufficient implementation details.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add state evolution discussion/results; elaborate parameter selection strategies; provide implementation details; expand comparisons to other clustering algorithms; discuss prior selection strategies.
