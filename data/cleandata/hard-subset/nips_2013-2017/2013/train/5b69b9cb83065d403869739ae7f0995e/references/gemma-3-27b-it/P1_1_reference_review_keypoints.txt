- Summary: Proposes an AMP algorithm extending prior work to MAP low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means, claiming better high-dimensional clustering than Lloyd’s K-means.
- Relation to prior work: Well positioned within low-rank reconstruction, Bayesian methods, and AMP literature, noting different scaling/assumptions from recent AMP variants and links to low-rank clustering methods.
- Strength — MAP extension: Extends AMP to MAP estimation, addressing a limitation of previous AMP work.
- Strength — Clustering application: Formulates K-means as low-rank reconstruction and derives a novel clustering algorithm with strong high-dimensional performance.
- Strength — Theory: Provides theoretical foundations including a proposition on fixed-point optimality and connections to ICM.
- Strength — Experiments: Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real data showing advantages over Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, VBMF-MA, and AMP-MA in certain regimes.
- Strength — Presentation: Generally clear, well-organized problem formulation and algorithm description.
- Weakness — State evolution omitted: Does not present state evolution analysis, a key theoretical tool for AMP, citing space constraints.
- Weakness — Convergence: Convergence is not guaranteed and stopping criteria may be suboptimal; more rigorous analysis needed.
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Lacks sensitivity analysis on choice of priors (p̂u, p̂v) and their impact on performance.
- Weakness — Limited comparison: Comparison to VBMF-MA is limited; deeper trade-off analysis versus variational methods is needed.
- Weakness — Reproducibility: Implementation details and code are not provided, hindering reproducibility and practical assessment.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with solid foundations and experiments, but weakened by missing state evolution and convergence concerns.
- Clarity assessment: Well written overall, though derivations may be hard to follow without message-passing background.
- Originality assessment: Original in combining AMP MAP extension with a low-rank formulation of K-means.
- Significance assessment: Significant potential for high-dimensional clustering and opens new research directions.
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded algorithm with strong experimental support and contribution to AMP literature.
- Against acceptance: Missing state evolution, convergence guarantees, and code-limit reproducibility.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: Provide more convergence discussion/alternative stopping criteria, add a (even qualitative) state evolution discussion, supply implementation details and code, and expand comparison to VBMF-MA.
