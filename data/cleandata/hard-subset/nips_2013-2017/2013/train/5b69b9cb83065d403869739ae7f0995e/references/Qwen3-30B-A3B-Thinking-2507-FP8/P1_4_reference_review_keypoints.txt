- Summary: Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction handling both posterior means and MAP, applied to clustering with a novel AMP-KM that enforces the “exactly one cluster” constraint and shows strong empirical performance.

- Theoretical soundness and novelty: Extends AMP to marginalization and MAP estimation for low-rank reconstruction with a principled derivation and connection to belief propagation.

- Convergence theory contribution: Provides Proposition 1 linking AMP fixed points to ICM fixed points, helping explain AMP’s avoidance of poor local minima.

- Practical impact (clustering): AMP-KM directly enforces one-cluster-per-point, outperforming Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes in accuracy and convergence speed for high-dimensional data.

- Experimental rigor: Thorough synthetic and real-data (ORL faces) experiments, multiple metrics (loss, accuracy, convergence) and comparisons against several baselines.

- Clarity and organization: Well-written, precise mathematical formulation, clear algorithms, and useful supplementary derivations.

- Convergence analysis weakness: Does not establish conditions guaranteeing AMP convergence and simply assumes convergence without practical discussion.

- Comparison to recent AMP work: Insufficient detailed comparison with the most recent AMP-based matrix reconstruction methods [13,14], beyond noting differing asymptotic assumptions.

- Parameter sensitivity: Lacks analysis of sensitivity to β (extended posterior parameter), which may crucially affect practical performance.

- Computational complexity analysis: Gives per-iteration O(mN) cost but omits discussion of typical/required iteration counts and overall runtime complexity.

- Overall assessment: Strong, substantial contribution with solid theory and convincing experiments; writing and organization are excellent.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address convergence guarantees and deeper comparison with recent AMP literature.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel unified AMP framework for marginalization and MAP; first method enforcing exact cluster assignment in this framework; theoretical connection to ICM; strong empirical results; clear exposition.

- Arguments against acceptance: Incomplete convergence guarantees; limited comparison to recent AMP work; missing parameter sensitivity study (β); incomplete total complexity analysis (iterations).
