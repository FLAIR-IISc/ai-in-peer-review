- Paper develops an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction under A = U0V0^T + W, introducing a temperature β that unifies MMSE (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) estimators with Onsager-like corrections.  
- Denoisers f and Jacobians G correspond to posterior mean/covariance (β=1) or MAP/Hessian inverse (β→∞) under a local surrogate; K-means specialization enforces one-hot constraints and yields assignment updates with self-influence and cluster-size corrections.  
- Builds on prior AMP work (Donoho/Rangan, IterFac, Matsushita/Tanaka) and is related to BiG-AMP and AMP for dictionary learning; claimed advances are MAP extension via β→∞ and an explicit one-hot K-means AMP.  
- Derivation is plausible and consistent with Bethe/Onsager reasoning; Proposition 1 gives useful blockwise optimality but relies on assumptions (PSD sums of site-wise Jacobians) that need more rigorous discussion, especially for discrete priors.  
- Paper omits state evolution (SE) equations and their assumptions; a brief SE statement and empirical SE-vs-empirical check are needed for NeurIPS.  
- Convergence is not guaranteed; stabilization techniques (damping/annealing) are only briefly mentioned and oscillatory behavior (limit cycles) is observed without clear remedies.  
- Experimental results show AMP-KM improves K-means loss for larger r and AMP-MA improves accuracy versus VBMF-MA, but fairness concerns exist (different initializations, unclear VB baseline priors).  
- Experiments lack variation in noise level, rank mismatch, cluster imbalance, and non-spherical covariances; ORL experiment lacks preprocessing and τ-learning details and omits comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Runtime reporting uses iteration counts rather than wall-clock or per-iteration cost, which is important because f/G computations can be expensive for general priors.  
- Clarity could be improved by simplifying Λ expressions to highlight Onsager terms, giving concrete f/G examples for common priors, and describing recommended damping/annealing and stopping criteria.  
- The τ update (residual-based estimator) should be clarified and the possibility of principled EM-style updates discussed.  
- The β-tempering idea is an interesting unifying contribution and K-means specialization is a useful, differentiating application.  
- Distinction from BiG-AMP is insufficiently clarified; authors should explicitly state where BiG-AMP falls short (e.g., asymptotic regimes, independence assumptions, intra-row dependencies).  
- To increase impact, the paper should provide SE evidence, stronger clustering baselines (spherical GMM EM, spectral clustering, deterministic annealing), and robustness checks to model misspecification.  
- Specific recommended additions: include SE summary and numerical check, compare to additional clustering methods, use consistent initialization or report restarts, report wall-clock times and f/G scaling, give annealing/damping schedules, analyze r̂≠r and unbalanced clusters, and clarify Proposition 1 conditions for discrete priors.  
- Pros: clean AMP framework unifying MMSE/MAP, principled K-means specialization with Onsager corrections, empirical gains, and useful fixed-point optimality interpretation.  
- Cons: missing state evolution and limited convergence analysis, incomplete and potentially unfair experimental comparisons, thin practical details (τ learning, damping, β schedules, preprocessing), and novelty vs. BiG-AMP not fully clarified.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; promising work that requires stronger theory (SE), fairer and broader experiments, and clearer distinctions from prior bilinear AMP methods.
