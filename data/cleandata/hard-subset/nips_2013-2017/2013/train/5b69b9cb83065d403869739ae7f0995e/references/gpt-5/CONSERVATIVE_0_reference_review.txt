Summary
The paper proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on factors U and V. A one-parameter family p̂(U,V|A;β) is introduced to interpolate between MMSE-style marginalization (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞), yielding a unified algorithm (Algorithm 2) that updates U and V with Onsager-like corrections. A fixed-point property (Proposition 1) asserts that, under PSD conditions, the limit point is a global minimum in each block when the other block is fixed. The second contribution applies the framework to clustering by using a discrete one-hot prior for V. The resulting method (Algorithm 3) reduces to K-means-like updates with additional terms that depend on current assignments and cluster sizes. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and the ORL face dataset show improved K-means loss and accuracy over Lloyd’s K-means/K-means++ and a VB matrix factorization baseline; the AMP maximum-accuracy variant is also included on synthetic data.

Relation to prior work
The approach builds on AMP/GAMP [Donoho et al. 2009; Rangan 2011] and prior AMP for bilinear/low-rank settings, including IterFac for rank-1 and general-rank AMP [Matsushita & Tanaka 2012], as well as bilinear GAMP (BiG-AMP) [Parker et al. 2013] and dictionary learning/calibration AMP [Krzakala et al. 2013]. The β parameterization essentially corresponds to temperature scaling; in modern AMP/GAMP literature, using general denoisers (including MAP) is standard. The clustering formulation coincides with K-means/Gaussian mixtures (equal spherical covariance); the presented assignment correction resembles leave-one-out or bias-correction heuristics seen in EM/GMM and Hartigan-style K-means variants. The paper compares to VBMF and K-means, but omits comparisons to EM for GMMs, spectral clustering, or deterministic annealing K-means.

Strengths
- Unifying view: a clean AMP formulation that moves from MMSE to MAP via β, with explicit update equations.
- The fixed-point optimality proposition (under PSD conditions) is useful for interpreting convergence points relative to block coordinate descent/ICM.
- The clustering specialization yields an intuitive correction to K-means assignments accounting for center bias and cluster sizes.
- Per-iteration complexity is O(mN), and the algorithms are easy to implement in the discrete-prior case.
- Some empirical improvements over K-means/K-means++ and VBMF are reported on synthetic and ORL data.

Weaknesses
Quality
- The derivation and state evolution are deferred and not presented; no theoretical performance characterization is provided despite claims that it is possible.
- Convergence is not guaranteed; oscillations are observed and handled with a heuristic stopping rule; damping or safeguards are not analyzed.
- The τ estimation in clustering is ad hoc and its impact is not quantified.
- Experiments are limited in scope and scale (N=1600 synthetic; ORL with N=400). No wall-clock runtimes, memory, or scalability beyond O(mN) are reported.
- Baselines are incomplete: missing EM for GMM (with equal spherical covariance), spectral clustering, soft K-means/deterministic annealing, or Hartigan K-means. These are standard for this problem and could change conclusions.
- Synthetic experiments use “true priors” for maximum-accuracy clustering, which favors the proposed Bayesian methods and is unrealistic.
- In real data, the initialization protocol is inconsistent across methods (K-means++ seeding used for AMP only in real-data experiments; random init otherwise), and two AMP runs collapse to one cluster. Robustness to initialization is not carefully studied.

Clarity
- The paper is generally readable, but key derivations and the supplementary proofs/SE are omitted here. Reproducibility is hindered by lack of implementation details for f/G under common priors, damping, and τ estimation.
- Several references are incomplete and some claims (e.g., “first to directly enforce one-hot within low-rank MF”) are overstated: K-means already does this; BiG-AMP with MAP denoisers is closely related.

Originality
- The main algorithmic idea—AMP with a β parameter and MAP denoisers—is incremental relative to existing GAMP/BiG-AMP frameworks that already accommodate MAP/MMSE denoisers. The specific K-means specialization with assignment-size corrections is interesting but close to known bias-correction/leave-one-out ideas.

Significance
- Potentially useful if the AMP variant consistently improves K-means and Bayesian clustering. However, without stronger theory and broader, fair comparisons to established baselines, the practical impact is uncertain.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear AMP formulation bridging MMSE/MAP with a simple algorithm.
- Intuitive correction to K-means assignments; promising empirical results on two datasets.
- Fixed-point property provides some optimality interpretation.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to BiG-AMP/GAMP with MAP denoisers; “first” claims for clustering are not convincing.
- Missing state evolution/theoretical guarantees; convergence issues acknowledged but not addressed.
- Experimental validation is narrow, with incomplete and sometimes biased baselines; small-scale datasets; no runtime analysis.
- Reproducibility gaps (omitted derivations, details, and code).

Questions/suggestions
- Provide the full derivation, state evolution, and conditions under which the PSD assumptions in Proposition 1 hold, especially for discrete priors.
- Compare against EM for GMM with equal spherical covariance, spectral clustering, soft K-means/deterministic annealing, and Hartigan K-means on both synthetic and real data. Include PCA+K-means and larger datasets (e.g., MNIST/CIFAR features, RCV1).
- Report wall-clock time and memory, and study sensitivity to initialization, rank mis-specification, τ estimation, and prior mismatch.
- Clarify connections and differences to BiG-AMP and prior AMP methods that already allow MAP denoisers.
- Provide damping/regularization to prevent oscillations and collapse; analyze empty-cluster handling.

Recommendation
Reject. While the paper presents a neat AMP instantiation and an appealing K-means variant, the novelty over established bilinear AMP/GAMP is limited, and the theoretical and empirical validation falls short of NeurIPS standards. With stronger theory, broader and fairer comparisons, and larger-scale experiments, this work could become competitive. Confidence: high.
