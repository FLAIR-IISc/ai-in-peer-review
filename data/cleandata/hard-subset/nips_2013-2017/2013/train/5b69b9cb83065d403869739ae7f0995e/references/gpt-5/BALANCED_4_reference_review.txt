Summary and relation to prior work
The paper considers Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction A0 = U0 V0^T under structural priors on the factors. It derives an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm that introduces a temperature parameter β to unify posterior-marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞). Compared to prior AMP work on matrix factorization (e.g., IterFac for rank-one; prior general-rank AMP that targeted posterior means), the authors claim their derivation handles MAP estimation directly and allows more general, possibly non-factorized priors over the rows of U and V. A fixed-point optimality proposition relates AMP fixed points to blockwise global minima of the MAP objective.

A second contribution is to cast K-means as a constrained low-rank factorization with V being one-hot rows, yielding an AMP-based hard clustering algorithm. The resulting updates differ from Lloyd’s K-means by Onsager-like correction terms that debias cluster-center updates and favor smaller clusters, and a “maximum accuracy” variant for β=1. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and ORL faces show the proposed AMP-KM improves K-means loss and accuracy over Lloyd/K-means++ (and AMP-MA improves accuracy), with few iterations.

The work builds on AMP/GAMP for linear and bilinear models [Donoho-Maleki-Montanari; Rangan; Bayati-Montanari], connects to VB matrix factorization [Lim & Teh; Raiko et al.], and relates to bilinear GAMP and dictionary learning AMP [Krzakala et al.; Parker et al.]. The K-means connection follows standard equivalences (hard-EM for spherical GMM, low-rank formulations), but the explicit AMP with one-hot constraints and derived correction terms seems novel.

Strengths
- Conceptual unification via β that recovers both marginalization and MAP, and a clean algorithmic form with clear dependence on priors through proximal/denoising functions f and G.
- Generality in allowing non-factorized priors across the r̂ components of each row (beyond Gaussian priors commonly used in VBMF).
- Insightful K-means specialization: the assignment rule includes principled debiasing and cluster-size terms derived from AMP, not ad hoc.
- Empirically promising: on high-dimensional synthetic data AMP-KM attains the lowest K-means loss; AMP-MA attains the best accuracy; on ORL faces AMP-KM beats K-means++ in most trials with similar iteration counts.
- Proposition 1 gives a useful optimality characterization of fixed points relative to blockwise MAP minimization (under mild PSD conditions).

Weaknesses
- Theory is incomplete. The state evolution (SE) is not presented; convergence and correctness guarantees are not provided. The derivation and validity conditions (e.g., randomness assumptions, scaling with fixed r vs. aspect ratio) are deferred to the supplement.
- Practical stability is unclear. Oscillations and collapse-to-one-cluster failures are observed; remedies (damping, annealing β, safeguards against empty clusters) are not developed or analyzed.
- Noise variance τ is estimated heuristically from residuals within iterations; sensitivity and its effect on Onsager terms and SE are not studied.
- Experimental scope is narrow relative to claims. Although the method is positioned as general low-rank reconstruction with flexible priors, results focus almost exclusively on clustering with uniform priors on U (or true Gaussian priors in MA). No demonstrations with sparsity/non-negativity priors, missing data, or mismatched rank r̂ ≠ r.
- Baselines are limited. For clustering, comparisons omit EM for spherical GMMs, spherical K-means, spectral methods, or modern bilinear AMP/BiG-AMP variants on the same task. An ablation that removes AMP correction terms (reducing to VB/ICM) would strengthen claims about the AMP advantage.
- Some novelty claims could be better positioned. Hard-assignment constraints have been treated in hard-EM for GMMs and in binary/orthogonal NMF formulations; what is distinct here is the AMP-based correction and SE-predictability (not shown).
- Clarity/Presentation: Notation is heavy; key algorithmic steps could be aided by concise pseudocode and implementation notes (damping, stopping, τ update). Several references are incomplete.

Qualitative evaluation
- Quality: Promising algorithmic contribution and a rigorous proposition, but missing SE and convergence analysis limit the theoretical depth. Empirical evidence is positive but not comprehensive.
- Clarity: Generally well written but dense; some derivations and practical choices (e.g., τ estimation, initialization, handling empty clusters) need clearer exposition.
- Originality: The β-parameterized AMP for MAP in general-rank bilinear models and the explicit AMP-K-means specialization with derived correction terms appear novel.
- Significance: Moderate. If stabilized and theoretically characterized, the approach could influence scalable clustering and structured matrix factorization. As presented, impact is constrained by theory gaps and limited experimental breadth.

Questions for the authors
1. Can you provide state evolution for the β→∞ limit and discuss conditions (randomness of A0, W, priors) under which SE tracks the algorithm? How does adaptive τ estimation affect SE validity?
2. What damping/annealing strategies mitigate oscillations and cluster collapse? Did you try β-annealing from 1 to ∞?
3. How sensitive is performance to mis-specified τ and to rank mismatch r̂ ≠ r? Any automatic rank selection (e.g., ARD or BIC) within this framework?
4. Can you quantify the benefit of the AMP correction by comparing to the same updates without the Onsager terms on the same datasets?
5. How does the method perform with non-Gaussian priors (sparse/nonnegative U) or with missing entries?

Suggestions for improvement
- Include at least a summarized SE and convergence discussion (with proofs or pointers) and practical stabilization (damping, step-size, β-annealing).
- Expand experiments: (i) ablations on Onsager terms; (ii) robustness to τ and r̂; (iii) comparisons to spherical GMM EM and spectral clustering; (iv) examples with other priors (sparse/NMF) to substantiate generality; (v) missing data.
- Provide pseudocode and implementation guidelines, including τ estimation schedule and handling of empty clusters.
- Clarify novelty vs. hard-EM/NMF literature; tighten and complete references.

Recommendation: Weak reject
Rationale: The idea is appealing and results are promising, but the absence of state evolution and convergence analysis, limited empirical breadth beyond clustering, and stability issues make the current submission below the NeurIPS bar. With added theory and broader, controlled experiments (including ablations), this could become a strong submission.
