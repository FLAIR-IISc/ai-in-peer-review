- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that extends AMP to MAP estimation and applies it to K-means clustering with exclusive assignment constraints.  
- Empirically outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in high-dimensional synthetic and real datasets.  
- Builds on existing AMP and low-rank clustering literature; novelty lies in MAP extension and explicit handling of exclusive cluster assignments.  
- Theoretical grounding provided via the AMP framework and mention of state evolution, but detailed analysis is limited.  
- Algorithm is flexible, able to incorporate various prior distributions to adapt to different settings.  
- Provides a clear, step-by-step algorithm description with supplementary derivations for implementation.  
- Limited theoretical analysis: convergence and performance bounds are not fully developed; optimality result depends on a PSD condition that may not hold.  
- Lacks explicit implementation details such as parameter tuning (e.g., noise variance τ) and computational complexity analysis for large-scale datasets.  
- Experimental comparisons are narrow—primarily against Lloyd’s K-means—and omit more advanced clustering methods like DBSCAN or spectral clustering.  
- Notation is dense and could benefit from a clearer index or initial variable explanations.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel constrained-clustering application of AMP, demonstrated empirical gains, strong theoretical basis, and algorithmic generality.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical convergence analysis, missing practical/complexity details, and limited benchmarking.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggested improvements in theoretical analysis, implementation details, and broader experimental comparisons.
