Summary
The paper proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with arbitrary (row-factorized) priors on the factors, introducing a “temperature” parameter β to interpolate between posterior-marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). As a second contribution, it specializes the MAP-AMP to K-means-type clustering by encoding hard one-hot assignments via a discrete prior on V, yielding an algorithm with a modified assignment rule that includes leave-one-out-style bias corrections and cluster-size regularization. Numerical experiments on synthetic data and ORL faces compare the proposed methods against Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and a VBMF baseline.

Strengths
- Unifies MMSE-like and MAP-like bilinear inference via a β-parameterized posterior, leading to a single AMP algorithm that can target both regimes.
- The factor priors allow intra-row dependencies (non-separable within r), which is less restrictive than many AMP treatments.
- The K-means specialization yields an interpretable assignment rule with bias correction for current membership and an implicit balancing of cluster sizes.
- Empirically, the proposed clustering variants outperform K-means/K-means++ in several settings and converge in few iterations.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and completeness
- Several equations contain scaling inconsistencies that likely indicate typos or derivation errors. For example:
  - (17a) appears to set Λu^t = mτ (V^t)⊤V^t, whereas (9a) suggests Λu^t should scale as (1/(mτ))(V^t)⊤V^t (up to Onsager corrections). With B_u^t = (1/(mτ)) A V^t, U^t = B_u^t (Λ_u^t)^{-1} would then be off by factors of (mτ).
  - (17b) gives Λ_v^t = (1/(mτ))(U^t)⊤U^t − (1/τ) S^t. From (9c), in the β→∞ limit and with T=0 we would expect Λ_v^t ≈ (1/(mτ))(U^t)⊤U^t − S^t (no 1/τ factor). These mismatches propagate into the assignment rule (18b).
  - Please carefully re-derive and correct the scalings of Λu, Λv, S, and T; otherwise empirical performance/interpretation may be misleading.
- Proposition 1 as stated is dubious without additional convexity assumptions. Claiming that U∞ is a global minimizer of CMAP(U, V∞) and vice versa requires convexity in U (resp. V), e.g., −log p̂u convex and Λu PSD. The current statement assumes only Σ S_i and Σ T_j PSD and “almost all cases,” which is insufficient to ensure global optimality. This needs correction (either restrict assumptions or weaken the claim to stationarity/local minimality).
- Convergence is assumed and oscillations are handled ad hoc (stopping if V^t = V^{t−1} or V^{t−2}). No damping or stabilization is discussed, and no state-evolution is provided (even qualitatively). Given known fragility of bilinear AMP, this is a significant gap.

Novelty and positioning
- The extension from posterior mean to MAP via β→∞ is interesting, but related work is not adequately discussed. In particular, Bilinear GAMP (Parker et al., 2013/2014) and subsequent derivatives consider general priors/likelihoods and can be run in MAP-like modes; the differences to your β-scheme should be crisply delineated. Similarly, recent AMP variants for bilinear models (e.g., VAMP/PE-VAMP/BAdVAMP) should be cited and contrasted.
- The claim of being the “first to directly enforce one-hot assignment in low-rank matrix reconstruction” is overstated. Hard assignment formulations exist (e.g., discrete NMF/binary MF, orthogonal NMF, projective clustering; also MoG-EM with hardening). Provide accurate citations and qualify the novelty.

Empirical evaluation
- Synthetic experiments use the true priors for maximum accuracy clustering, which is favorable to AMP-MA. Please also report mismatched prior scenarios and sensitivity to τ estimation.
- Baselines are limited: no comparison to EM for Gaussian mixtures (spherical/full), spectral clustering, mini-batch K-means, Hartigan-Wong, or modern k-means initializations beyond k-means++. For bilinear inference, comparisons to BiG-AMP or variational methods beyond one VBMF variant are needed.
- Only iteration counts are reported; no wall-clock runtimes or memory usage. Error bars or confidence intervals are not shown. Dataset diversity is limited (one face dataset with specific preprocessing).
- The AMP-KM occasionally collapses to a single cluster; discuss when/why (e.g., τ misestimation, small n_l penalties) and how to mitigate (damping, temperature annealing, safeguards against empty/collapsed clusters).

Clarity and presentation
- The manuscript has numerous typographical and formatting issues: repeated commas in references, inconsistent notation (e.g., U0 vs U), stray spaces and missing parentheses, inconsistent use of hats, and occasional grammar issues (“low-rankedness”). Several equations could be clarified for dimensions and scaling. Figures are referenced but not shown here; ensure they include error bars and axis labels.
- Key derivations and proofs are deferred to supplementary material (not provided). At least a sketch of state evolution and critical steps of the derivation should appear in the main text.

Quality
- The core idea is plausible, but correctness concerns (scaling, Proposition 1) and lack of convergence analysis undermine confidence. The empirical section suggests promise but is not comprehensive.

Originality
- Moderate. The β-trick and AMP for bilinear models are known; supporting MAP via a temperature parameter is incremental. The K-means specialization is interesting but close in spirit to leave-one-out corrected assignments and prior AMP treatments of discrete variables.

Significance
- Potentially useful if the derivation is corrected and convergence/robustness addressed. As is, the impact is limited by technical gaps and narrow evaluation.

Pros
- Unifying formulation (β) for MMSE/MAP.
- Allows non-separable row priors.
- Interpretable K-means specialization with bias corrections.
- Promising empirical trends.

Cons
- Equation/scaling inconsistencies; questionable proposition.
- Missing convergence and state-evolution analysis.
- Limited baselines and evaluation; reliance on true priors.
- Presentation/formatting issues; overstated novelty.

Questions for authors
1) Please correct and verify the scalings in (17a,b) and the resulting assignment cost (18b). Provide a derivation snippet in the main text.
2) Under what conditions is Proposition 1 valid? If convexity of −log p̂u/v is required, please state it explicitly.
3) How do you stabilize AMP (damping, adaptive stepsizes)? Can you quantify oscillation frequency and the effect of damping?
4) Compare against BiG-AMP and EM for Gaussian mixtures. Report runtimes and error bars.
5) How sensitive is AMP-KM to τ estimation? Does annealing β help?

Recommendation
Reject (below threshold). The paper has interesting ideas and some promising results, but the accumulation of technical inconsistencies, missing analyses, limited baselines, and presentation issues makes it premature for NeurIPS. I encourage a thorough revision addressing the points above; with corrected derivations and stronger evaluation, this could become a solid contribution.
