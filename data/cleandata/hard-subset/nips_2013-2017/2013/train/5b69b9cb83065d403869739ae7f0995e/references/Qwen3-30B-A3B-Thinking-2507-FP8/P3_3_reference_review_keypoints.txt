- Summary: Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that unifies marginalization and MAP via a β parameter and applies it to K-means-like clustering with promising empirical gains.

- Strength: Unified β-based framework elegantly handles both posterior mean and MAP estimation.

- Strength: Proposition 1 theoretically links AMP fixed points to ICM fixed points, providing a meaningful connection to established methods.

- Strength: Empirical evaluation is comprehensive—synthetic and ORL face data—and compares against several baselines (Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, VBMF-MA, AMP-MA).

- Strength: Extends prior AMP work by enabling MAP estimation (not just posterior mean) and directly incorporating cluster-assignment constraints into the low-rank framework.

- Weakness: Major theoretical omission—no state evolution analysis is provided, weakening expected NIPS-level theoretical guarantees on performance and convergence.

- Weakness: Novelty over clustering methods is overstated; AMP-KM resembles Lloyd’s K-means with different assignment rules, so the distinction needs clearer justification.

- Weakness: Computational-complexity discussion is insufficient—claimed "low cost" matches Lloyd’s O(mN) per iteration, with no clear practical speed or resource advantages shown.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to recent related AMP matrix-reconstruction work [13,14]; differences (fixed rank vs ratio, independence assumptions) are mentioned but not thoroughly analyzed.

- Weakness: Modest empirical gains (e.g., ORL accuracy 0.635 vs 0.690) may not justify added algorithmic complexity for practitioners.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing theoretical, novelty, comparison, and complexity explanation issues.

- Requested revisions: add a concise state-evolution analysis or discussion, clarify relation to Lloyd’s and other K-means variants, provide a detailed comparison with recent AMP work [13,14], and better explain practical computational advantages.
