- Proposes an AMP-based framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction that interpolates between MMSE/marginal MAP and MAP estimation via a temperature β and yields a K-means–type clustering algorithm enforcing one-hot assignments.  
- Adds AMP “Onsager” correction terms to VB-like updates to account for U–V dependence; provides a fixed-point optimality proposition in the MAP limit.  
- Experimental results: synthetic and ORL faces show AMP-KM reduces K-means loss and AMP-MA increases accuracy with few iterations.  
- Well-situated in AMP/GAMP literature and explicitly connects to VB/ICM; shows removing AMP terms reproduces VB updates.  
- Novelty: applying β-interpolation and enforcing exact one-hot constraints within a low-rank AMP derivation appears new and useful.  
- Main theoretical gap: no state evolution (SE) analysis provided, leaving iteration-complexity and performance claims unsubstantiated.  
- Empirics limited: lacks comparisons to BiG-AMP, EM for GMMs, and robustness tests to prior mismatch (AMP-MA uses true priors).  
- Stability concerns: AMP-KM sometimes collapses to one cluster and exhibits oscillations, handled only by a heuristic stopping rule.  
- Reproducibility issues: many derivation details in supplement, τ estimation is heuristic, and code availability is not mentioned.  
- Clarity: paper is dense but organized; AMP updates and role of β, f, G are clearly explained, with helpful contrast to VB/ICM.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: include side-by-side comparison with BiG-AMP and general-rank AMP [9], and a brief derivation sketch in the main text.  
- Originality judged moderately strong but tempered by need to clarify relation to BiG-AMP (which can handle discrete priors).  
- Significance promising if SE analysis, broader benchmarks (recommendation systems, non-Gaussian denoising), and robustness studies are added.  
- Pros: principled AMP derivation unifying MMSE/MAP, interpretable K-means corrections, solid synthetic evidence, low per-iteration cost.  
- Cons: missing SE, insufficient BiG-AMP/EM-GMM baselines, occasional nonconvergence, and reliance on true priors for AMP-MA.  
- Key questions for authors: how does this differ from BiG-AMP for MAP/MMSE with multivariate priors; can you provide SE equations/validation; sensitivity to prior misspecification; remedies for oscillations/collapse; runtime comparisons vs K-means++ and EM-GMM.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept—publishable if camera-ready strengthens BiG-AMP/EM-GMM comparisons, includes SE sketch, and addresses stability/robustness.
