Summary and relation to prior work
This paper develops an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with general priors on the factors and introduces a one-parameter “temperature” β that unifies marginalization (β=1) and MAP (β→∞). The algorithm computes both posterior means and (marginal) MAP estimates via denoising functions f and G defined from the tilted local posterior q̂(u; b, Λ, p̂), with Onsager-like corrections that make it distinct from variational Bayes/ICM. A proposition establishes that at fixed points (under mild PSD conditions), the algorithm is coordinate-wise globally optimal in U (for fixed V) and vice versa.

A second contribution applies this framework to K-means by enforcing the one-of-K constraint on V explicitly, yielding an AMP variant of K-means with assignment updates containing principled bias-correction/regularization terms that depend on current cluster sizes. Empirically, the method outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ in high dimensions on synthetic data and improves both K-means loss and accuracy on the ORL faces dataset in most trials.

Relative to IterFac (rank-1 AMP) and earlier AMP for low-rank reconstruction [Matsushita & Tanaka 2012], this work generalizes to arbitrary priors and to MAP via β. Relative to generalized/bilinear AMP (GAMP/BiG-AMP; Rangan 2011; Parker et al. 2013) and AMP for dictionary learning [Krzakala et al. 2013], the authors highlight different asymptotics (fixed rank r rather than fixed r/m) and do not assume component-wise independence within each row. The clustering formulation connects to prior “MF for clustering/NMF” works but, to my knowledge, the explicit one-of-K enforcement within an AMP derivation and the resulting corrected assignment rule are novel.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: A clean β-parameterization that unifies MMSE and MAP in bilinear AMP with general priors, and an AMP derivation that outputs both posterior means and marginal MAP. The fixed-point optimality proposition (coordinate-wise global optimality) is useful and clarifies the relation to ICM.
- Methodological boldness: Casting hard K-means (with exact one-of-K constraints) within the AMP framework and deriving an Onsager-corrected assignment rule is elegant and, to my knowledge, new. The correction terms have a clear leave-one-out/bias-correction interpretation and are likely to matter in high dimension.
- Practical promise: For K-means, the algorithm preserves O(mN) cost/iteration, adds only cheap corrections, and shows consistent gains over Lloyd/K-means++ on both synthetic high-d data and ORL faces, with few iterations to converge.
- Flexibility: The framework allows arbitrary p̂u and p̂v (including improper priors) and supports both marginal inference and MAP. The discussion on when f∞ yields convex subproblems (Λ PSD and −log p̂ convex) is helpful.

Weaknesses and points needing clarification
- Positioning vs BiG-AMP/GAMP: The paper should more carefully distinguish its contributions from BiG-AMP (bilinear generalized AMP) and discuss whether BiG-AMP already supports MAP via β→∞, and how the present derivation and fixed-r asymptotics change either the algorithm or its predicted performance. The claim about not assuming independence within rows is intriguing but needs to be made precise: where in the derivation is this used, and what dependence structures are permitted?
- Missing state evolution: State evolution is a key strength of AMP. The paper claims it can be provided but omits it. Even a succinct SE for a representative case (Gaussian priors or the K-means limit) or empirical SE validation would substantially strengthen the work.
- Convergence and stability: The algorithm can oscillate (as observed for assignments) and may increase CMAP during iterations. Practical stabilizers (damping, step-size control, adaptive variance estimation) should be documented. The ad hoc τ-estimation for K-means is sensible but should be analyzed or justified; sensitivity plots would help.
- Experimental scope: For clustering, the baselines are limited. Including spherical GMM-EM, spectral clustering, and spherical K-means would better situate performance. Reporting wall-clock time and memory, and robustness across r̂≠r, noise levels τ, and different initializations would improve credibility. The two ORL failures collapsing to one cluster suggest the need for safeguards against degeneracy (e.g., empty-cluster handling, damping).
- Theoretical details: Proposition 1’s assumptions (PSD sums of S_i, T_j; existence/regularity of f∞ and G∞) and the identity G∞=(Λ−H)−1 deserve more explicit conditions. Also, the derivation of Algorithm 3 from Algorithm 2 (limits, approximations used) should be made self-contained or included in the supplement.
- Clarity: While generally well written, several steps are dense. For reproducibility, please specify the exact priors used in AMP-MA experiments, all stopping criteria and damping (if any), and the exact τ update. Minor typos exist.

Questions for the authors
1) How does your algorithm relate, in equations, to BiG-AMP when β=1? Can BiG-AMP recover your MAP limit by β→∞ or by taking small-variance priors? If not, what concretely differs?
2) Can you provide an empirical state-evolution curve for a simple Gaussian prior setting to confirm predicted MSE and the O(1) iteration claim?
3) How sensitive is AMP-KM to τ estimation and to r̂ mismatch? Do the correction terms in (18b) remain beneficial for unbalanced clusters?
4) Did you try damping for the assignment updates to avoid the observed 2-cycle oscillations?
5) Can you release code?

Pros for acceptance
- Novel, principled AMP view of K-means with exact one-of-K constraints and insightful correction terms with demonstrated empirical gains.
- Unified β-AMP treatment of marginalization and MAP for low-rank factorization with general priors; useful fixed-point optimality property.
- Simple, scalable updates that can have impact beyond clustering (e.g., structured matrix factorization).

Cons for acceptance
- Limited positioning vs established bilinear AMP (BiG-AMP) and missing state-evolution analysis.
- Experimental comparisons and stability analyses could be broader; occasional degeneracy in real-data runs.

Recommendation
Borderline accept. The K-means application and the β-AMP unification are sufficiently novel and promising to merit presentation, even with incomplete theory and restrained experiments. Strengthening the positioning vs BiG-AMP, adding at least an empirical SE validation, and expanding baselines would significantly improve the paper.
