- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction supporting both marginalization and MAP, and applies it to clustering with a novel AMP-based K-means that reportedly outperforms Lloyd’s and variational Bayes.

- Technical soundness: rigorous extension of AMP, well-motivated derivation (Algorithm 2) and Proposition 1 giving convergence guarantees; connection to variational Bayes is explained.

- Practical significance: AMP-based clustering shows improved accuracy and faster convergence vs standard K-means and variational Bayes, especially in high-dimensional settings.

- Clarity and organization: paper is well-written, structured, and makes the link between low-rank reconstruction and clustering explicit.

- Insufficient differentiation from related work: fails to clearly distinguish or justify novelty relative to recent works [9] and [14]; claim of being “first” for MAP estimation appears inaccurate.

- Rank assumption: algorithm assumes the true rank r is known and lacks discussion or methods for rank selection or estimation.

- Weak theoretical analysis for MAP: analysis of the β→∞ MAP case is minimal and provides no clear rationale for why AMP would avoid local minima better than ICM.

- Experimental limitations: synthetic experiments cover a limited parameter range (r < 9); ORL experiment fixes cluster count to subjects (r̂=40); initialization and robustness comparisons (e.g., K-means++ variants) are limited.

- Critical assessment: contribution is valuable but novelty is overstated; authors should clarify differences from prior AMP work, particularly regarding rank treatment.

- Suggested improvements: include more comparisons with recent AMP-based factorization methods, test diverse clustering scenarios, and provide insight/analysis for AMP’s faster convergence vs variational Bayes.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Pros summarized: solid theoretical foundation, practical algorithm with demonstrated high-dimensional benefits, clear derivation of AMP-based clustering, and reasonable experimental validation.

- Cons summarized: inadequate differentiation from recent AMP literature, overstated MAP novelty, no rank-selection discussion, and experiments could be more comprehensive.
