## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

**Summary:**

This paper presents an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous work to handle MAP estimation and applying it to a K-means type clustering problem. The authors argue that their approach offers flexibility in incorporating prior knowledge about the factors U and V, computational efficiency due to AMP, and improved performance for high-dimensional clustering compared to Lloyd’s K-means.  They provide theoretical justification and empirical validation of their method.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The extension of the existing AMP algorithm to encompass MAP estimation is a valuable contribution.  The unification of marginalization and MAP estimation via a single parameterized algorithm (using β) is elegant.
* **Algorithmic Novelty:** Applying AMP directly to K-means clustering, with the specific constraint of exclusive cluster assignment, is a novel approach. The connection to existing algorithms like ICM and Lloyd’s K-means is well-established.
* **Potential for Efficiency:** AMP algorithms are known for their efficiency and scalability, which is crucial for large-scale matrix reconstruction and clustering problems. The authors rightly point this out as a key advantage.
* **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments on both artificial and real (faces) datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional settings, and demonstrate competitive or superior performance to baseline methods. The ablation study on the ORL dataset is particularly compelling.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the key ideas accessible. The use of equations to represent the algorithms is a useful convention.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited State Evolution Discussion:** While the authors mention state evolution analysis, they do not present any results or insights from it.  State evolution is a powerful tool for predicting performance and understanding algorithm behavior, and its absence weakens the theoretical justification.
* **Convergence Guarantee:** The authors acknowledge that convergence isn’t guaranteed and state an assumption about positive semidefiniteness of matrices for Proposition 1 to hold. A more robust convergence analysis, or at least a discussion of conditions under which convergence is likely, would be beneficial.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:**  The sensitivity of the algorithm to parameters (e.g., noise variance τ) is not thoroughly investigated. A more detailed analysis of parameter tuning would be valuable.
* **Comparison to Other Recent Methods:** The paper briefly mentions related work but doesn’t offer a detailed comparison to more recent advancements in AMP-based matrix factorization or spectral clustering techniques.  For example, a comparison to more sophisticated variational inference methods would be insightful.
* **Assumptions on Prior Distributions:**  While flexibility in priors is touted, the experiments mainly use relatively simple priors. Exploring more complex and informative priors, and their impact on performance, would further strengthen the work.



**Addressing Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical derivations appear reasonable, and the experimental results provide support for the claims. The limitations (convergence, parameter sensitivity) are acknowledged. It feels like a complete piece of work for a conference paper, although further investigation into convergence and parameter tuning would enhance it.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clearly written, although some sections (particularly the mathematical derivations) could benefit from further explanation.  The notation is consistent, and the organization is logical.
* **Originality:**  The combination of AMP with K-means clustering, enforcing exclusive assignment, and extending the algorithm for MAP estimation, represents a novel contribution.  While AMP is not new, its application to this specific problem with these extensions is original.
* **Significance:**  The results are significant, particularly the demonstrated improvement over standard K-means in high-dimensional settings.  The potential for efficient large-scale clustering is valuable.  The work addresses a difficult problem (clustering with strong constraints) in a potentially better way than previous approaches.


**Arguments For & Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Novel and technically sound approach to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* Clear theoretical justification and empirical validation.
* Potential for significant performance improvements in high-dimensional settings.
* Well-written and clearly presented.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Limited discussion of state evolution analysis.
* Convergence is not guaranteed and could be a practical limitation.
* Insufficient analysis of parameter sensitivity.
* Could benefit from more comprehensive comparison to recent related work.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept** for NIPS.  The work presents a novel and technically sound algorithm with promising results. While there are areas for improvement (particularly regarding convergence analysis and parameter tuning), the contributions are significant enough to warrant acceptance. The paper addresses a relevant problem and offers a potentially valuable solution.  I encourage the authors to address the weaknesses outlined above in a future iteration of the work.
