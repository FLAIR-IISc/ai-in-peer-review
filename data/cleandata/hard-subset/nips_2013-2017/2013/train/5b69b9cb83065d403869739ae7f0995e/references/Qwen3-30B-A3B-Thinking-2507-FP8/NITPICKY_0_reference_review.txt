

# Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous work to handle both marginalization and MAP estimation. The authors further apply their algorithm to K-means clustering, claiming it is the first to directly handle the constraint that each datum should be assigned to exactly one cluster within the low-rank matrix reconstruction framework.

## Summary of main ideas
The paper introduces a unified AMP framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction that can handle both marginalization and MAP estimation. The authors extend the AMP algorithm from [9] to handle MAP estimation, which was previously limited to posterior mean estimation. They then apply this to clustering, deriving a novel AMP-based K-means algorithm that outperforms standard approaches in numerical experiments.

## Strengths
The paper makes a theoretically sound contribution by extending AMP to handle MAP estimation in low-rank matrix reconstruction. The numerical experiments demonstrate clear performance improvements over existing methods, particularly for high-dimensional data. The connection between clustering and low-rank matrix reconstruction is well-explained, and the proposed AMP-based clustering algorithm shows promising results on both artificial and real datasets.

## Weaknesses

1. **Notational inconsistencies**: The paper suffers from severe notational inconsistencies throughout. For example:
   - Inconsistent use of superscripts (t vs t) in equations like "U t" vs "U^t"
   - Inconsistent use of "r" and "r̂" for rank (e.g., "r ≪ m, N" vs "r̂ is an assumed number of clusters")
   - Inconsistent spacing and formatting in mathematical expressions (e.g., "∥A− U0V ⊤0 ∥2F" vs "||A - UV^T||_F^2")
   - Confusing notation like "mτ" used both as a single symbol and as "m*τ" in different places

2. **Minor technical errors**:
   - "low-rankedness" should be "low-rankness" (introduced in the first paragraph)
   - Equation (1) has inconsistent formatting of the Frobenius norm
   - The definition of "AMP" as an acronym is missing at first use
   - In Algorithm 2, "Btu = 1/mτ AV t - 1/mτ U t-1" should use consistent superscript notation

3. **Overclaiming of novelty**:
   - The claim that their approach is "the first that directly deals with the constraint that each datum should be assigned to exactly one cluster" appears questionable. Reference [11] (Convex and semi-nonnegative matrix factorizations) specifically addresses this constraint. The paper should more clearly differentiate their approach from [11] and explain why their method is fundamentally different.

4. **Lack of clarity in key definitions**:
   - The relationship between "r" (true rank) and "r̂" (assumed rank) is not clearly explained when they differ
   - The paper claims "the first contribution" is extending the algorithm to handle MAP estimation, but [9] states "we propose an AMP algorithm for the general-rank case, which, however, can only treat estimation of posterior means." The authors should clarify how their extension differs from [9]'s approach.

5. **Formatting and presentation issues**:
   - The paper uses inconsistent spacing in mathematical expressions (e.g., "1 2 u⊤Λu" should be "1/2 u^⊤Λu")
   - Inconsistent use of boldface for vectors/matrices (e.g., "u0,i" vs "u0,i")
   - The paper claims to be the first to handle the constraint in clustering, but fails to properly cite and differentiate from prior work that already addressed this

## Overall assessment

While the paper presents a technically sound extension of AMP to handle MAP estimation in low-rank matrix reconstruction, and the numerical results are promising, the numerous notational inconsistencies and minor technical errors significantly undermine the paper's quality. The notational issues are pervasive throughout the paper, making it difficult to follow the mathematical derivations. These are not merely cosmetic issues but fundamental problems that affect the readability and reproducibility of the work.

The claim of novelty regarding the clustering constraint is questionable given reference [11], and the paper fails to adequately distinguish their approach from prior work in this specific aspect. The authors should have been more careful in their literature review and more precise in their claims about novelty.

Given the stringent standards of NeurIPS, these minor but pervasive issues are substantial enough to warrant rejection. The paper has potential, but the current version is not sufficiently polished or carefully written to meet the publication standards expected at NeurIPS.

## Recommendation
Reject
