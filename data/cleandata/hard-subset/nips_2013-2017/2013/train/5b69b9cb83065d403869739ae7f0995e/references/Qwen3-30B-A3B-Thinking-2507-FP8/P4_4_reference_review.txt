

# Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

## Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that unifies marginalization and MAP estimation. The authors extend previous AMP-based approaches for low-rank matrix reconstruction [9] to handle both posterior mean estimation (marginalization problem) and MAP estimation in a single framework. The key innovation is a one-parameter extension of the posterior distribution that allows the algorithm to address both problems by varying the parameter β. The paper demonstrates that the proposed AMP algorithm can be applied to K-means clustering as a special case of low-rank matrix reconstruction, where the constraint that each datum must be assigned to exactly one cluster is directly incorporated into the framework. The work relates to previous NIPS papers on AMP algorithms [6, 7, 8, 9] and variational Bayes matrix factorization [4, 5], while distinguishing itself by fixing rank r rather than r/m in the asymptotic limit and not assuming statistical independence among factor components.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Soundness**: The paper presents a well-justified extension of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction. The theoretical foundation is sound, with Proposition 1 establishing that AMP fixed points are also fixed points of the ICM algorithm, providing a meaningful connection to existing methods. The authors carefully analyze the relationship between AMP and variational Bayes approaches, noting that AMP can better capture dependencies between factors.

2. **Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The problem formulation, algorithm derivation, and properties are presented with clear mathematical notation. The comparison between AMP and variational Bayes methods is particularly well-explained, with helpful insights into why AMP might outperform variational Bayes in practice.

3. **Novelty**: The unified treatment of marginalization and MAP problems via the parameter β represents a significant extension of existing AMP work. The application to clustering with the direct incorporation of the "exactly one cluster" constraint is presented as novel, with the authors correctly noting that previous low-rank matrix reconstruction approaches to clustering [10, 11] did not handle this constraint directly.

4. **Significance**: The numerical experiments on both artificial and real data demonstrate clear advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches. The AMP algorithm for K-means clustering achieves lower K-means loss and higher accuracy than Lloyd's algorithm and K-means++, with comparable or faster convergence. The real-world application to face clustering demonstrates practical utility.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Theoretical Analysis**: While the paper references state evolution analysis for AMP algorithms [6, 8, 18], it does not provide state evolution equations for the proposed algorithm, which would strengthen the theoretical contribution. The absence of this analysis makes it difficult to fully assess the theoretical guarantees of the method.

2. **Convergence Guarantees**: The paper states that convergence of the algorithm is assumed, but does not provide conditions under which convergence is guaranteed. This is a notable gap in the theoretical analysis, particularly for a method that is presented as being robust to local minima.

3. **Implementation Details**: The paper mentions using m⁻²N⁻¹‖A - Uᵗ(Vᵗ)⊤‖²F as a temporary estimate of τ, but doesn't provide sufficient detail on how this estimate affects performance. More comprehensive discussion of parameter sensitivity would strengthen the practical utility of the method.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Pro-acceptance arguments**:
- The paper makes a clear and significant extension to AMP algorithms for low-rank matrix reconstruction.
- The unified framework for marginalization and MAP problems is novel and well-justified.
- The numerical experiments demonstrate clear performance improvements over state-of-the-art methods.
- The connection to clustering is well-motivated and the algorithm shows practical benefits.

**Con-acceptance arguments**:
- The theoretical analysis lacks state evolution equations for the proposed algorithm.
- Convergence guarantees for the algorithm are not provided.
- The paper does not adequately address the computational cost of calculating f(·, ·; p̂) for general priors.

## Overall Assessment

The paper presents a technically sound and well-executed extension of AMP to low-rank matrix reconstruction that addresses both marginalization and MAP problems. The theoretical foundation is solid, with appropriate connections to previous work, and the experimental results demonstrate clear advantages over existing methods. The main weaknesses are the lack of state evolution analysis and convergence guarantees, which are important for a NIPS paper on algorithmic development. However, these gaps do not undermine the core contributions of the paper, which are novel, well-motivated, and well-supported by experiments.

The paper is clearly written, makes a significant contribution to the field, and has potential for practical impact in clustering and low-rank matrix reconstruction problems. The authors have addressed related work thoroughly and made a clear case for the novelty of their approach. While the theoretical analysis could be strengthened, the practical performance improvements and clear exposition make this a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
