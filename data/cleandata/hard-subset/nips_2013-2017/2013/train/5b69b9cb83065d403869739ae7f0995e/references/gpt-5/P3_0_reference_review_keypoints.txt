- Develops a β-tempered AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, unifying MMSE (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).

- Iterations use generic denoisers f and their Jacobians/covariances G; removing Onsager-like corrections yields VB/ICM updates.

- Proposition: at fixed points, each block (U or V) is globally optimal given the other under mild PSD conditions, but this does not guarantee descent or convergence.

- Specializes framework to clustering with a one-hot prior on V and flat prior on U, producing an AMP variant of K-means with two Onsager corrections (leave-one-out debiasing and small-cluster preference).

- Proposes a “maximum accuracy” clustering (β=1) approximating marginal MAP assignments.

- Empirically on synthetic data, AMP-KMeans reduces K-means loss and increases accuracy over Lloyd/K-means++; β=1 AMP attains the highest accuracy among compared methods.

- On ORL faces, AMP-KMeans often outperforms K-means++ but collapsed to a single cluster in 2/50 runs.

- Derivation aligns with bilinear/generalized AMP literature; β-tempering and MAP transition are standard.

- Claimed differences from prior work: fixed-r large-system scaling and allowing within-row dependencies in priors.

- Full derivations and any state evolution (SE) analysis are deferred or omitted; no convergence guarantees and observed oscillations (authors stop when Vt repeats).

- Practical τ estimation via the residual is heuristic and its stability/consistency is not analyzed.

- Complexity is attractive at O(mN) per iteration, but r̂-dimensional denoiser integrals/minimizations can be expensive unless closed-form denoisers exist.

- Experiments are narrow: single noise level, balanced clusters, fixed m/N; lacks sensitivity analyses to τ, imbalance, misspecified rank, or higher rank.

- Real-data evaluation uses weak baselines (K-means on raw pixels) without PCA/whitening, damping/stepsize details, timing, or robustness analyses; stronger clustering baselines are missing.

- Originality is moderate: β-tempering in bilinear AMP and the one-hot prior instantiation are interesting, but MAP denoisers and denoiser-centric views exist in prior work.

- Potential significance: if robust, AMP-KMeans is appealing (same per-iteration cost as K-means with principled corrections), but the lack of SE and limited experiments reduce immediate impact.

- Paper is generally clear and well-organized with good intuition for Onsager corrections, though some notation and omitted derivations/SE need tightening.

- Pros: unified MMSE/MAP AMP formulation, intuitive efficient AMP-based K-means with principled corrections, empirical improvements on benchmarks, supports within-row dependent priors.

- Cons: limited novelty relative to bilinear/GAMP literature, no SE or convergence theory, oscillations observed, narrow experiments, sparse practical guidance (damping, initialization, τ estimation), and no runtime comparisons.

- Reviewer questions: clarify novelty versus BiG-AMP and MAP-BiG-AMP, describe damping/adaptive steps used and sensitivity to τ/initialization, provide SE predictions and empirical comparisons, assess robustness to imbalance/misspecified r̂/τ, and supply runtime comparisons to K-means/EM.

- Minor comments: cite spherical K-means and EM for equal-variance GMMs, consider PCA/whitening for ORL preprocessing, and provide code to encourage adoption.

- Recommendation: borderline reject—promising and neat AMP perspective on K-means but needs stronger theoretical support, broader empirical validation, and clearer novelty.
