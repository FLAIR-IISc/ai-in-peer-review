Summary
The paper proposes an AMP-style algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with separable priors on rows of U and V. A temperature parameter β interpolates between marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), yielding a unified algorithm (Alg. 2). A key instantiation enforces one-hot constraints on V to derive an AMP variant of K-means (Alg. 3), which modifies the assignment step by a bias/size correction reminiscent of Onsager/leave-one-out terms. A block-optimality proposition states that fixed points are coordinate-wise optima under PSD conditions. Experiments on synthetic data and ORL faces show improved K-means loss and/or accuracy versus Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and a VBMF baseline.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds on AMP/GAMP for linear estimation and bilinear problems (e.g., IterFac; Matsushita & Tanaka ’12; Rangan ’11; Bayati & Montanari ’11). The β-trick is closely related to tempered/annealed posteriors and MAP limits used widely in variational and BP literature. For bilinear inference, BiG-AMP (Parker, Schniter & Cevher ’13) appears especially relevant; a careful empirical/theoretical comparison is missing. The connection between AMP’s Onsager term and leave-one-out corrections in clustering is interesting and aligns with known improvements to iterative algorithms on dense graphs.

Strengths
- Unifying view: Using β to bridge posterior mean, marginal MAP, and MAP within AMP is elegant and practically useful.
- Derivation leads to a simple K-means variant with clear intuition (bias correction for current assignment; small-cluster encouragement).
- Computationally attractive O(mN) per iteration, and assignment reduces to argmin over r̂ labels.
- Proposition 1 provides a (limited) optimality guarantee at fixed points.
- Synthetic experiments are large-scale and suggest consistent improvements; ORL results show better loss/accuracy in most runs.

Weaknesses
Quality
- The derivation and state evolution are deferred; there is no theoretical characterization (or even a sketch) of state evolution, convergence conditions, or finite-sample behavior. Given that AMP can diverge without damping/denoiser conditions, this is a major omission.
- Proposition 1 is weak: the PSD conditions are assumed, not verified; no guidance is given for when they hold, nor how often they fail. The proposition ensures only blockwise optimality, which ICM also has; it does not address global optimality or quality of local minima.
- Practical stability: oscillations are observed; the remedy is an ad-hoc stopping rule. No discussion of damping, step-size control, or convergence diagnostics is given.
- Experimental setup for “maximum accuracy” uses true priors, which unfairly advantages Bayesian methods and is rarely available in practice. Sensitivity to prior mis-specification is not studied.
- Only one real dataset, with modest gains and two degenerate runs (all points in one cluster). No runtime/memory comparisons or wall-clock results are reported. Termination thresholds (1e-15 relative changes) are atypically stringent and may not reflect practical runtime.
- Despite claiming generality for arbitrary priors, experiments focus almost exclusively on clustering. There is no demonstration on other structured priors (e.g., sparse/nonnegative), denoising, or matrix completion.

Clarity
- Notation is heavy; f/G are central but their concrete forms are not given for common priors. For the K-means case, give explicit closed-form expressions and computational details (e.g., how Λ is regularized when near-singular).
- The role and estimation of τ in Alg. 3 is under-explained; the ad-hoc residual estimator may be biased early in iterations.
- Some references are incomplete; several claims (e.g., “first to directly enforce one-hot constraint in low-rank reconstruction”) require stronger positioning versus BiG-AMP and EM for GMMs.

Originality
- Applying AMP to K-means with explicit one-hot constraints is a neat twist, but the broader idea of AMP for bilinear factorization is well-established, and temperature-based unification is standard. The novelty seems incremental unless clearly distinguished from BiG-AMP and related bilinear AMP variants (dictionary learning, blind calibration).
- The “size correction” effect in assignments is interesting, but similar leave-one-out heuristics exist; the paper should better articulate what is genuinely new.

Significance
- If reliably more accurate/faster K-means were demonstrated across diverse datasets and initializations, impact could be meaningful. As is, evidence is limited, and gains on ORL are modest with occasional catastrophic failures. For general Bayesian matrix factorization claims, no supporting experiments are provided.

Questions for the authors
1. How does the proposed algorithm compare empirically and conceptually to BiG-AMP for bilinear inference (including MAP variants)? Can BiG-AMP with discrete priors on V recover your Alg. 3?
2. Do you use damping or adaptive step-size? Without it, how often do oscillations/divergence occur on larger datasets?
3. How sensitive is AMP-MA to prior mis-specification? Please show accuracy vs. mismatch in prior/τ.
4. What happens under rank mismatch r̂≠r? Please report performance when r̂ is over/under-specified.
5. Provide wall-clock times and memory vs. K-means++, mini-batch k-means, and EM for GMMs.

Suggestions for improvement
- Include state evolution (even in supplement) and conditions ensuring convergence; provide empirical SE calibration.
- Add damping/linesearch and ablate their effects on stability.
- Compare to stronger baselines: EM for Gaussian mixtures (same generative model), mini-batch/spherical k-means, spectral clustering, and BiG-AMP.
- Evaluate on multiple real datasets (text TF-IDF, vision features, other image collections), with varied dimensionality and cluster separations.
- Study prior/τ mis-specification and rank mismatch; report robustness.
- Provide runtime/memory and code for reproducibility.
- Clarify the novelty relative to existing bilinear AMP methods; tighten references and claims.

Pros
- Clean AMP formulation with unified β parameter.
- Intuitive K-means variant with principled corrections.
- Promising synthetic results; generally better than Lloyd/K-means++.
- Low per-iteration complexity.

Cons
- Insufficient theoretical guarantees (no SE, limited proposition).
- Limited and favorable experiments; modest real-data gains; instability observed.
- Missing comparisons to closely related bilinear AMP and standard clustering baselines.
- General claims (arbitrary priors) not substantiated experimentally.

Recommendation
Leaning to reject. The idea is plausible and potentially useful, but the current submission lacks the theoretical analysis and breadth/rigor of empirical validation needed to justify acceptance, especially given prior art in bilinear AMP and clustering. Strengthening theory, comparisons, robustness studies, and real-data evaluation would substantially improve the case.
