This paper presents an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering. The core idea of extending the existing AMP framework to handle MAP estimation, alongside the application to clustering with explicit constraint enforcement, is a genuinely novel contribution. The work builds on recent advances in AMP but cleverly adapts it to a different problem setting – one that has strong ties to both matrix factorization and clustering – and tackles the MAP estimation challenge within that framework. The extension to clustering, framing it directly as a constrained low-rank problem, is also a particularly insightful connection.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The extension of AMP to MAP estimation in the low-rank setting, and the formulation of K-means clustering *directly* within a constrained low-rank reconstruction framework, are both strong points. This isn't merely incremental improvement; it’s a different angle on established problems.
*   **Theoretical grounding:** The connection to belief propagation and the potential for state evolution analysis (though not fully presented here due to space) suggests a solid theoretical underpinning. Proposition 1, guaranteeing optimality under certain conditions, is a positive sign.
*   **Potential for efficiency:** The claim of linear computational cost (O(mN)) is compelling, especially for large-scale problems where traditional methods struggle.
*   **Empirical results:** The numerical experiments, while relatively limited in scope, show promising results. The AMP-based clustering algorithm outperforming Lloyd's K-means in the presented scenarios is encouraging. The improvement on the ORL dataset is notable.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited validation:** The experimental section feels somewhat cursory. The number of datasets tested is small, and the analysis could be more thorough. A more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art clustering algorithms (beyond Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++) would strengthen the findings.
*   **Assumptions and prior specification:** The choice of priors, while flexible, isn’t deeply justified. The paper acknowledges this but doesn’t fully explore the sensitivity of the algorithm to different prior choices. This is critical for Bayesian methods.
*   **State evolution absent:** The mention of state evolution analysis without actually presenting it is a missed opportunity. This would significantly bolster the theoretical claims and provide a predictive tool for performance.
*   **Convergence guarantees:** The authors acknowledge the lack of guaranteed convergence. While Proposition 1 provides *some* assurance, a more rigorous analysis of convergence properties would be beneficial.
*   **Clarity in algorithm description:** While the algorithm is presented mathematically, some steps could benefit from more intuitive explanations. Specifically, the role of the 'tau' parameter in the clustering algorithm could be clarified.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The work presents a genuinely novel approach to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
*   The theoretical foundation is sound, and the connection to AMP and belief propagation is well-established.
*   The empirical results, though limited, are promising and suggest that the algorithm has the potential to outperform existing methods.
*   The paper introduces a new perspective on clustering as a constrained low-rank problem.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The experimental validation is not comprehensive enough.
*   The lack of state evolution analysis weakens the theoretical claims.
*   The assumptions about priors are not fully justified.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper is a strong submission with a significant amount of originality. While the experimental validation could be more thorough and the theoretical analysis more complete, the novelty of the approach and the potential for impact outweigh these weaknesses. The framing of clustering as a constrained low-rank problem is particularly insightful. I lean towards acceptance, believing that this work opens up interesting new avenues for research in both low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The potential benefits of a more efficient and theoretically grounded clustering algorithm justify overlooking the current limitations. I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions focusing on expanding the experimental evaluation and potentially including a brief discussion of state evolution.



**Score:** 7.5/10 (Strong Accept)
