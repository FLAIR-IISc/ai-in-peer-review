## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

**Summary:**

This paper presents an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending existing AMP approaches to handle MAP estimation beyond posterior mean estimation.  Furthermore, it demonstrates the application of this algorithm to K-means type clustering, formulating the problem as a special case of low-rank reconstruction with specific constraints. The authors claim the proposed algorithm outperforms Lloyd's K-means, especially in high-dimensional settings, and provide theoretical justification via a proposition guaranteeing optimality under certain conditions. The work builds upon recent advancements in AMP for compressed sensing and matrix factorization, offering a unified framework for both reconstruction and clustering.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the landscape of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. It acknowledges previous Bayesian approaches (e.g., Monte Carlo, variational Bayes) and highlights the computational challenges they face. The connection to compressed sensing and AMP algorithms by Donoho et al. is well established.  The authors clearly differentiate their work from IterFac and Rangan et al. by extending AMP to MAP estimation and by their specific formulation of the problem.  The relation to variational Bayes methods and Lloyd's K-means is explicitly discussed, framing the AMP algorithm as a generalization with potential advantages.  The citations to related work in dictionary learning (Krzakala et al.) and generalized observation models (Parker et al.) provide helpful context. 

**Strengths:**

* **Generality and Flexibility:** The Bayesian framework and the use of AMP provide a flexible approach that can incorporate various prior structures on the factors U and V, making it adaptable to different applications.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The proposition guaranteeing optimality of the fixed point is a valuable contribution, providing a theoretical basis for the algorithm's performance.
* **Unified Framework:**  Presenting clustering as a specific instance of low-rank reconstruction is an elegant approach and suggests broader applicability of the method.
* **Empirical Results:** The numerical experiments, particularly the comparison with Lloyd's K-means and K-means++, demonstrate the potential for improved performance, especially in higher dimensions.  The results on the ORL face database are promising.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and the algorithm is presented in a clear, step-by-step manner.

**Weaknesses:**

* **State Evolution Absent:**  The authors mention the possibility of analyzing performance using state evolution but omit the detailed derivation. This significantly limits the ability to predict performance and understand the algorithm’s behavior in different regimes. Including a simplified version or at least a more in-depth discussion would be beneficial.
* **Convergence Guarantee Limited:** While Proposition 1 provides conditions for optimality at a fixed point, a general convergence guarantee is not provided. This is a common issue with AMP-based algorithms, but acknowledging this limitation more explicitly would be helpful.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The role and tuning of the parameter τ (noise variance) isn't thoroughly discussed. The paper states it’s estimated, but details on the robustness of the performance to inaccuracies in this estimate would be helpful.  How does performance vary with different estimations of τ?
* **Computational Cost Details:** While claiming linear computational cost O(mN), the constant factor is not explicitly addressed.  For high-dimensional problems, the cost of computing *f* and *G* (especially for non-Gaussian priors) could be significant.  A more detailed computational analysis would be useful.
* **Limited Baselines:** The comparisons in the numerical experiments are limited. While comparing with Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ is good, including other state-of-the-art clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering) or more recent matrix factorization techniques would strengthen the claims of superiority.
* **Lack of Scalability demonstration**: The experiments are performed with m = 800 and N = 1600. While the authors claim scalability, there is no demonstration on a very large scale (e.g. millions of data points), which is the target regime where AMP is expected to shine.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper presents a technically sound algorithm with a solid theoretical foundation. The proposition regarding optimality is a strong point. However, the lack of a convergence guarantee and detailed scalability analysis detracts from the overall quality.
* **Clarity (8/10):** The paper is generally clearly written, and the algorithm is well-explained. Some sections, particularly those discussing the theoretical aspects, could benefit from further simplification.
* **Originality (7/10):** Extending AMP to MAP estimation and applying it to clustering is a novel contribution, but the core idea of leveraging AMP for matrix factorization is not entirely new. The originality lies in the specific formulation and the resulting algorithm.
* **Significance (7/10):** The potential to improve clustering performance, particularly in high-dimensional settings, is significant. However, the impact will depend on the practical efficiency and robustness of the algorithm, which is not fully demonstrated.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Presents a novel and theoretically grounded algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* Shows promising empirical results, especially in comparison to Lloyd's K-means.
* Offers a flexible framework that can accommodate various prior structures.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Lacks a complete convergence guarantee.
* Omits a detailed state evolution analysis.
* Limited discussion of parameter sensitivity and computational cost.
* The experiments could be more comprehensive.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above – particularly including a simplified state evolution analysis and discussing parameter sensitivity in more detail – would significantly strengthen the paper. The authors should also provide some indication on the computation time needed, to confirm scalability claims. The empirical results are promising, and the theoretical justification provides a solid foundation for further research.
