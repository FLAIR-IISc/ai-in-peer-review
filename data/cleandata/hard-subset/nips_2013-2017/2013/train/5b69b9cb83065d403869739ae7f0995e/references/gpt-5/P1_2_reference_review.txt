Summary
The paper develops an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization A0 = U0V0^T with flexible priors on the rows of U and V. The authors introduce a one-parameter family of posteriors p̂(U,V|A; β) ∝ p̂(U,V|A)^β that unifies posterior-mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference. They derive an AMP scheme (Algorithm 2) that uses per-row denoisers f and their Jacobians/covariances G built from the assumed priors p̂u, p̂v, allowing dependencies among components within each r-dimensional factor row. They show that removing certain Onsager terms reduces the method to VB/ICM-style updates. For β→∞ they obtain an AMP algorithm for MAP, together with a fixed-point optimality property (Proposition 1) under PSD conditions on certain matrices.

As a second contribution, they instantiate this framework for clustering by modeling V as one-hot assignments and U as cluster centers. This yields an AMP variant of K-means (Algorithm 3) with an explicit “leave-one-out/size” correction in the assignment step. They also propose an AMP method for “maximum accuracy” clustering (β=1) under a discrete prior on V. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian-mixture data show improved K-means loss and accuracy compared to Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++, and improved accuracy and speed compared to a VB matrix factorization baseline. On the ORL faces dataset, the AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in most trials.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds on AMP for compressed sensing and its extensions to generalized and bilinear settings (e.g., GAMP, IterFac, bilinear GAMP). Compared to Matsushita & Tanaka (2012), which handled general rank but focused on posterior means, the present work explicitly treats MAP via β→∞, gives a unifying β-parametrization, and allows non-separable within-row priors. It is related to recent AMP developments for dictionary learning/blind calibration and bilinear generalized AMP but differs in the asymptotic regime (fixed rank r as m,N→∞) and in the prior structure. Casting K-means as low-rank MAP factorization is known, but deriving a principled AMP algorithm that enforces exact one-of-K assignments and adds AMP/Onsager corrections appears novel.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The AMP updates are plausible and consistent with known bilinear AMP derivations; the use of per-row denoisers with general r-dimensional priors is a nice generalization. The β-parameterization cleanly unifies inference objectives. Proposition 1 provides a useful fixed-point optimality statement, though key conditions and the proof are deferred to the supplement; clarifying when the PSD assumptions hold (especially in the β→∞, discrete-prior case) would strengthen the paper.
- Practicality: The per-iteration cost is O(mN), and per-row computations scale with r. For continuous priors with β=1, f and G may require r-dimensional numerical integration; for MAP, convexity of −log p̂ is important. These tradeoffs are acknowledged.
- Empirics: Synthetic results are encouraging and show consistent improvements, and the ORL experiment is a nice sanity check. However, the empirical section is relatively narrow: only one real dataset, little sensitivity analysis (e.g., mis-specified r̂, different noise levels, non-Gaussian noise), and no runtime comparisons.

Clarity
The presentation is generally clear and well organized. The mapping from the general AMP to the clustering case is explained cleanly, and the interpretation of the correction terms is insightful. That said:
- Important derivations and all proofs are in the supplement; a brief outline of the derivation and a summary of the state-evolution predictions in the main text would help.
- Convergence behavior (oscillations, stopping rules) and damping/annealing heuristics deserve more discussion.
- Some references are malformed; please fix citation formatting.

Originality
- Extending bilinear AMP to handle MAP via β→∞ and to general non-separable within-row priors is a meaningful contribution.
- The AMP-based K-means with an explicit Onsager correction and exact one-hot constraints seems novel within the low-rank factorization/AMP framework.
- Please more crisply position your contributions relative to bilinear GAMP (Parker et al.) and other contemporaneous bilinear AMP works: what can your algorithm do (e.g., MAP, non-separable priors) that BiG-AMP cannot, and vice versa?

Significance
If robust, the proposed AMP factorization could be a useful tool for low-rank estimation with structured priors. The clustering instantiation is practically interesting and could influence K-means variants in high dimensions. The theoretical state-evolution analysis (claimed but omitted) would further increase impact. At present, the experimental scope limits a firm conclusion about broad practical significance.

Pros
- Clear, principled AMP derivation accommodating MAP and general within-row priors.
- Novel and effective AMP variant of K-means with interpretable corrections.
- Solid synthetic improvements; promising real-data results.
- Useful fixed-point optimality property for MAP updates.

Cons
- Convergence not guaranteed; observed oscillations; no damping/annealing study.
- Limited empirical scope (few datasets, little sensitivity analysis, limited baselines beyond K-means/VBMF).
- Key theoretical details and proofs are deferred; state evolution only mentioned.
- τ estimation is heuristic; robustness and sensitivity are not analyzed.
- Claim about “first to enforce exact one-of-K” in this framework is plausible but should be more carefully contrasted with NMF-based clustering with simplex/one-hot constraints.

Suggestions and questions for authors
- Provide a concise derivation sketch in the main paper and a summary of state-evolution predictions (e.g., accuracy vs SNR, iteration counts).
- Add damping/under-relaxation or adaptive β (annealing from β=1 to ∞) to stabilize and potentially improve MAP performance; report on oscillation mitigation.
- Evaluate sensitivity to r̂ ≠ r, different τ (and non-Gaussian noise), and to mismatched priors for AMP-MA to assess robustness.
- Compare with bilinear GAMP/BiG-AMP (MAP if possible), spectral clustering, and EM for spherical GMMs (K-means limit), and report wall-clock times.
- Clarify when ∑S_i and ∑T_j are PSD in practice for discrete priors; does G vanish a.e. suffice?
- Provide strategies for empty clusters and tie-breaking; discuss the potential bias toward equal-sized clusters induced by the −m/n_l term.
- Fix citation formatting and add standard references for the K-means⇔spherical-GMM connection.

Recommendation: Weak accept
The paper presents a technically interesting and reasonably novel AMP formulation that unifies posterior-mean and MAP low-rank matrix factorization with structured priors, and a compelling application to K-means-style clustering with improved performance. To strengthen the contribution, I encourage the authors to expand the empirical evaluation and clarify theoretical aspects in the supplement.
