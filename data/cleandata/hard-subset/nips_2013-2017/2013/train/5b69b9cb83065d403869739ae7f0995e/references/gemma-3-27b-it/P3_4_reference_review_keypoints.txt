- Presents an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending prior AMP work to support MAP estimation as well as posterior-mean estimation.  
- Demonstrates a connection between low-rank reconstruction and K-means clustering, proposing an AMP-based clustering algorithm that outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in high-dimensional settings.  
- Correctly situates the work among Bayesian matrix factorization, AMP literature, and related AMP-based low-rank methods (e.g., IterFac, Rangan et al.); acknowledges dictionary learning/blind calibration work.  
- Would benefit from a more detailed discussion of limitations of existing AMP-based low-rank methods to better motivate the contribution.  
- Strength: flexible handling of different priors via parameterized p̂u and p̂v, enabling adaptation to varied problem settings.  
- Strength: valuable extension of AMP to MAP estimation, broadening AMP’s applicability for low-rank reconstruction.  
- Strength: insightful clustering application with explicit single-cluster assignment constraint and strong high-dimensional performance.  
- Strength: references to state evolution and a proposition on optimality provide theoretical grounding.  
- Strength: empirical results on synthetic and real data (notably ORL faces) convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail and analysis of state evolution, limiting clarity on theoretical guarantees and performance characterization.  
- Weakness: algorithm implementation appears complex; functions f and G may be computationally demanding depending on priors.  
- Weakness: performance sensitivity to the noise parameter τ, with no thorough guidance or adaptive estimation strategy provided.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited to Lloyd’s K-means/K-means++; missing comparisons to more recent, state-of-the-art clustering methods (e.g., spectral approaches leveraging low-rank structure).  
- Weakness: notation is occasionally dense and could be simplified for readability (e.g., consistent iteration subscripts).  
- Overall: significant, well-motivated contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; empirical and theoretical bases are promising.  
- Arguments for acceptance: introduces a novel, general AMP-based MAP-capable algorithm; strong clustering connection and empirical gains; technically sound and well-referenced.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete state evolution analysis; potential practical issues from implementation complexity and parameter sensitivity; inadequate external algorithm comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: expand state evolution discussion (possibly in supplement); provide implementation details and optimizations for f and G; investigate adaptive τ estimation strategies; include comparisons to recent clustering methods; refine notation for clarity.
