- Proposes a β-parameterized AMP for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that interpolates between posterior-marginal (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference.
- Extends prior bilinear/low-rank AMP by supporting general priors via functions f and G and explicitly handling the MAP limit.
- Instantiates the framework for clustering, showing K-means as a special case (one-hot V) and deriving an AMP-K-means with Onsager-like corrections to assignments and cluster sizes.
- Proposes a mild optimality result (Proposition 1): any fixed point is block-wise globally optimal given the other block under PSD conditions.
- Synthetic experiments (Gaussian mixtures) show AMP-KM lowers K-means loss vs Lloyd/K-means++ and AMP-MA (with true priors) improves assignment accuracy vs VBMF.
- Real-data (ORL faces) results are encouraging with typical improvements, but occasional collapse to a single cluster is observed.
- Relates to VBMF and prior bilinear AMP (IterFac, Rangan, Matsushita & Tanaka) but claims novelty in MAP specialization and one-hot discrete prior handling.
- Technical derivations are deferred to the supplement; AMP updates appear reasonable but lack state evolution analysis or convergence guarantees.
- Convergence is assumed; oscillations are observed and handled heuristically (damping), with no stability or local-minima quality analysis.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: only one real dataset, missing wall-clock time, ablations for Onsager terms, robustness to mis-specified priors/rank, and broader baselines (EM-GMM, spectral clustering, BiG-AMP).
- Practical complexity is standard O(mN) per iteration with extra O(m+N) terms; however f and G computations may require r̂-dimensional integrals unless closed forms are provided.
- The τ estimation heuristic for K-means is ad hoc and its impact is not analyzed.
- Clarity is generally good and the K-means specialization is well-motivated, but many crucial details (derivations, PSD conditions) are in the supplement.
- Some notation and scaling choices lack early intuition; references have formatting issues and a few are incomplete.
- Reproducibility would benefit from explicit f/G for common priors, pseudocode with damping/stepsize guidance, and worked computational details for G∞ via Hessians.
- Originality rated moderate-to-high: the β-AMP unification and one-hot clustering AMP appear novel relative to prior works, though stronger comparisons to BiG-AMP/GAMP are needed.
- Significance is potential but limited by lack of theoretical backing (state evolution), narrow empirical scope, and no demonstrations beyond clustering (e.g., nonnegativity or sparsity).
- Pros: principled AMP derivation, practical K-means variant with intuitive corrections, empirical improvements over strong baselines, and low per-iteration complexity.
- Cons: no state evolution/convergence guarantees, deferred derivations, narrow experiments, sparse practical guidance for general priors, and occasional catastrophic failures.
- Suggested improvements: add empirical state evolution validation, more convergence diagnostics and damping strategies, ablations for Onsager terms, runtime comparisons, and comparisons to EM-GMM, spectral clustering, and BiG-AMP.
- Further suggestions: instantiate additional priors (nonnegative/sparse) with worked f/G, analyze sensitivity to τ, rank mismatch, and initialization, and broaden empirical evaluations across datasets.
- Recommendation: weak reject—promising and elegant ideas but insufficient theoretical support and empirical breadth to accept in current form.
