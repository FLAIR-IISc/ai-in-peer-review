Summary
The paper develops an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction under flexible priors on the factors U and V. The key technical idea is a one-parameter “temperature” extension p̂(U,V|A;β) ∝ p̂(U,V|A)^β that unifies posterior mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) estimation in a single AMP framework. This yields update equations involving local denoisers f and their Jacobians/covariances G, and an Onsager-type correction. The authors then instantiate this framework for clustering by encoding the one-hot constraint on V directly via a discrete prior. This gives a new AMP-based K-means algorithm whose assignment step includes principled bias-correction and cluster-size balancing terms. A proposition characterizes fixed points of the MAP-AMP iteration as block-wise global minimizers under mild PSD conditions. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and the ORL faces dataset show improvements in K-means objective and accuracy versus Lloyd’s K-means and k-means++, and competitive speed; AMP for maximum-accuracy clustering also outperforms a variational-Bayes baseline.

Relation to prior work
AMP has been successfully applied to sparse linear inverse problems and generalized linear models, and there are bilinear/low-rank AMP variants (IterFac for rank-1; earlier general-rank AMP that targets posterior means; BiG-AMP/bilinear GAMP; AMP for dictionary learning/blind calibration). This work differs by (i) the β-temperature device enabling a single algorithm to cover marginalization and MAP (and the corresponding change of denoiser from posterior mean to proximal/MAP), (ii) a direct treatment of one-hot V in a low-rank factorization framework for clustering with explicit, derived Onsager corrections in the assignment step, and (iii) derivation in a limit with fixed r as m,N→∞, rather than fixed rank ratios.

Strengths
- Novelty and conceptual unification: The β-parameterized posterior is a simple but powerful idea that cleanly bridges AMP for posterior means with AMP for MAP via a change in the denoiser. The resulting algorithm appears to subsume VB/ICM updates plus principled Onsager terms.
- Direct one-hot modeling for clustering: Encoding the K-means constraints through the prior and deriving an AMP assignment rule that corrects for center bias and adapts to cluster sizes is elegant and, to my knowledge, new in the AMP literature.
- Interpretability: The assignment correction terms in the K-means variant admit clear interpretations (bias correction proportional to 1/cluster size; encouragement of smaller clusters), which is rare for message-passing derivations.
- Efficiency: Per-iteration cost O(mN) with only O(m+N) overhead for Onsager terms; the algorithm converges in a handful of iterations in experiments.
- Fixed-point characterization: Proposition 1 is reassuring about the quality of converged points; also clarifies the relationship to ICM/VB and why AMP may avoid bad local minima.
- Empirical gains: On high-dimensional synthetic mixtures, AMP-KM improves K-means loss over Lloyd and k-means++, and AMP-MA improves accuracy over VBMF; on ORL, AMP-KM wins in most trials.

Weaknesses and questions
- Missing state evolution and convergence analysis: The paper claims a SE exists and could be proved “like” prior work, but does not provide it. Given the non-i.i.d. structure in bilinear problems and the fixed-r asymptotics, even a sketch or empirical SE validation would strengthen the contribution.
- Stability/oscillations: The K-means AMP variant sometimes oscillates (authors stop when Vt=Vt−2). Damping strategies, conditions guaranteeing monotonicity, or practical stability guidance are needed. What happens when Λv becomes indefinite in (17b)? Any safeguards?
- Limited experimental scope: Comparisons are largely to Lloyd/k-means++ and a VBMF baseline. Missing are EM for Gaussian mixtures (with tied/known covariance), Hartigan-Wong K-means, mini-batch K-means, spherical K-means, spectral clustering, and contemporary robust K-means variants. More diverse real datasets and higher-dimensional benchmarks would help.
- Sensitivity and robustness: No study of mis-specified priors, unknown/overestimated r̂, or SNR/noise-variance estimation errors (τ is estimated on the fly). How does performance degrade under mismatch? Does the cluster-size correction bias results when clusters are truly imbalanced?
- Practical details: For β<∞ and non-Gaussian priors, computing f and G may require r̂-dimensional integration; guidance on tractable priors and numerical implementation is light. For β→∞, the denoiser is a proximal-MAP; examples beyond the discrete V and uniform U would be helpful.
- Clarity/organization: The core AMP derivation is relegated to the supplement; the main text could better contrast with BiG-AMP/bilinear GAMP and clarify the precise assumptions (distributional conditions on A0, W, and initialization) under which the approximation is expected to hold.

Suggestions for improvement
- Include at least an empirical state evolution showing predictive accuracy for MSE/overlap, and outline the key steps/assumptions in the SE derivation for the fixed-r limit.
- Add damping/stepsize heuristics and diagnostics to mitigate oscillations, and discuss the PSD conditions in Proposition 1 and how they are enforced/checked in practice.
- Expand experiments: add GMM-EM, Hartigan-Wong, spectral/spherical K-means baselines; evaluate sensitivity to r̂ and τ; report wall-clock times; test on additional vision/text datasets with varying cluster imbalance.
- Provide implementation details for f/G under common priors and release code.
- Clarify the novelty relative to bilinear GAMP and dictionary-learning AMP, especially regarding MAP and discrete priors.

Evaluation
- Quality: Solid algorithmic development with a plausible fixed-point property and convincing, if limited, experiments. Lack of SE/rigor in the main text is a drawback, but the methods appear technically sound.
- Clarity: Generally clear with helpful intuition; however, the main derivation is offloaded to the supplement, and some practical aspects could be better documented.
- Originality: High. The β-unification of AMP for posterior mean and MAP, and the direct one-hot AMP formulation for K-means with principled assignment corrections, are novel and interesting directions.
- Significance: Promising. If adopted, the approach can influence both low-rank Bayesian inference and scalable clustering. The improvements over standard K-means in high dimensions are practically relevant.

Recommendation
Lean accept. The paper introduces a fresh and potentially impactful direction—using AMP beyond posterior means, up to MAP, and applying it to clustering with exact one-hot constraints. Despite missing theory details and a somewhat narrow experimental scope, the conceptual advance and empirical promise merit inclusion. I encourage the authors to strengthen theory and broaden empirical validation in the final version.
