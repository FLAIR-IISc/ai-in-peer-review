- Proposes an AMP algorithm extending Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction to MAP estimation and applies it to K-means clustering by enforcing one-hot cluster assignments.
- Theoretical derivation of Algorithm 2 is well-justified via belief propagation and Bethe approximation, but convergence is assumed without proof.
- Numerical experiments (synthetic and real) are comprehensive, show lower K-means loss, higher accuracy, and faster convergence than Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and VBMF-MA.
- Uses strong statistical evidence (500 synthetic instances, 50 real-data trials) to support empirical claims.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; connection between low-rank reconstruction and clustering is well explained.
- Notation could confuse readers due to dual use of U/V for true and estimated matrices.
- Novel contribution: extends AMP to MAP estimation beyond prior work [9] and handles fixed rank asymptotics differently than [13,14].
- The clustering application is novel by directly incorporating the one-cluster-per-point constraint within the low-rank framework.
- Significance: offers an efficient, effective approach with potential implications for other low-rank and Bayesian inference problems.
- Theoretical gaps: lacks conditions or analysis for AMP convergence and does not provide state evolution analysis.
- Limited comparison to recent related work ([13,14]) weakens the novelty claim.
- Practical limitations: does not address performance with noisy or incomplete data or fully justify why AMP outperforms standard K-means for the specific problem.
- Missing sensitivity analysis for key parameter τ, which is important for practical implementation.
- Strengths highlighted: MAP extension, solid clustering connection, thorough experiments, and low O(mN) per-iteration complexity with fast convergence.
- Recommendation: accept—meaningful extension to AMP, well-executed empirical validation, with theoretical gaps to be addressed in future work.
