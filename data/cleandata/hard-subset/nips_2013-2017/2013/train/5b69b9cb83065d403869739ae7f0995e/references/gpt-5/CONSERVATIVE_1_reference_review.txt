Summary
The paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured factors and proposes an AMP-based algorithm that introduces a temperature parameter β to unify posterior-mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference. Compared to prior AMP work on bilinear models (e.g., IterFac for rank-1 and Matsushita & Tanaka 2012 for general rank), the authors claim their algorithm handles MAP estimation in addition to posterior means. They then instantiate the framework for clustering by constraining V to be one-hot. This yields an AMP variant of K-means with an assignment rule that includes an “Onsager-like” correction (a leave-one-out style adjustment), and a maximum-accuracy clustering variant under a discrete prior. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and ORL faces suggest improvements over Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ in K-means loss and accuracy, and faster convergence than a VBMF baseline in the “maximum accuracy” setup.

Strengths
- A clear AMP formulation for bilinear factorization with a β parameter that interpolates between posterior mean and MAP. The connection to variational Bayes and the identification of the extra AMP terms are well explained.
- The K-means instantiation leads to a simple, computationally efficient assignment update with an intuitive bias correction for cluster centers influenced by their current members.
- Complexity per iteration is linear in mn with modest overhead beyond standard K-means.
- Proposition 1 gives a coordinate-wise optimality guarantee at fixed points under mild PSD conditions, and clarifies the relationship to ICM/Lloyd.
- The empirical gains on synthetic data (large m, N) and a classical face dataset are suggestive.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Novelty relative to prior AMP for bilinear models is not fully established. BiG-AMP (Parker et al., 2013) already treats generalized bilinear models and has been used with MAP-like denoisers/prox operators. The paper cites [14] but does not compare experimentally or clarify whether the proposed β→∞ mode and the resulting updates differ materially from using MAP denoisers within BiG-AMP/GAMP. A direct theoretical and empirical comparison to BiG-AMP (and to more recent matrix-factorization AMP variants) is essential.
- Theory is incomplete. The state evolution—a key AMP contribution—is deferred. Convergence is assumed; oscillations are observed and handled heuristically (Vt=Vt−1 or Vt=Vt−2). There is no damping/stepsize scheme or convergence characterization. Proposition 1 is useful but does not address convergence or global optimality of the joint problem, which remains highly nonconvex.
- The clustering contribution feels incremental. The “first that directly deals with the constraint that each datum should be assigned to exactly one cluster” is overstated; the equivalence between K-means and matrix factorization with one-hot V is classical (Ding & He, 2004; Ding et al., 2010) and there is a substantial body of work on discrete/orthogonal/indicator-matrix factorizations and hard-EM for mixtures. The main novelty is the specific AMP-derived leave-one-out correction term; this resembles known self-influence corrections and would benefit from a sharper connection to leave-one-out K-means, Hartigan-Wong variants, and spherical/high-dimensional K-means analyses.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and in places potentially biased:
  - Synthetic: only a single (m,N,τ) and Gaussian, balanced clusters; β=1 experiments use the true priors and true r, which is unrealistic and favors Bayesian methods. No tests of mis-specified priors, imbalanced clusters, varying SNR, or the effect of r̂≠r. No ablation to show the effect of the AMP correction terms versus the “VB/ICM” updates they augment.
  - Baselines: For clustering, comparisons are only to Lloyd and K-means++ (and VBMF in the MA variant). Absent are standard alternatives: EM for GMMs with shared/diagonal covariances, spherical K-means, spectral clustering, NMF/orthogonal NMF clustering, or SDP/convex relaxations. For matrix reconstruction, there is no comparison to BiG-AMP.
  - ORL: Occasionally collapses to a single cluster; this points to stability issues. Only one dataset is used. No runtime measurements; iteration counts alone are not sufficient.
- The role of τ in K-means is ad hoc: τ is estimated via residuals at each iteration. This choice is not derived from a principled EM update and may interact with assignments in unpredictable ways. Sensitivity analyses and a principled noise/scale handling would strengthen the claim.
- Clarity/reproducibility: Many derivations are deferred; the definition and computation of f and G (and f∞, G∞) for non-Gaussian priors is nontrivial and likely a bottleneck. The termination tolerance 1e-15 is unrealistically tight; implementation details, damping, and code availability are not provided.

Quality
The algorithmic development is plausible, but theoretical guarantees (state evolution, convergence) and empirical validation against strong baselines are insufficient. Claims about speed and scalability are not backed by wall-clock data or larger-scale experiments.

Clarity
Generally well written with helpful intuition. However, several key pieces (derivations, state evolution, proofs) are deferred; the novelty relative to BiG-AMP and prior bilinear AMP work is not crisply articulated. The clustering novelty claim should be toned down and better situated in the literature.

Originality
Moderate. The β-trick and the MAP denoiser viewpoint are standard in AMP/GAMP circles; the specific K-means assignment correction is interesting but incremental.

Significance
Potentially useful if the AMP updates consistently improve K-means in high dimensions. As presented, the impact is limited by missing theory and narrow experimental comparisons.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide state evolution (even if heuristic) and a convergence-stabilized implementation (damping/stepsizes/annealing), along with empirical convergence diagnostics.
- Compare directly to BiG-AMP (with MAP/denoiser variants) for both reconstruction and clustering tasks; include EM for spherical/diagonal-covariance GMMs and spectral/spherical K-means.
- Add ablations removing the AMP correction terms to quantify their contribution, and sensitivity to initialization and τ estimation.
- Evaluate robustness: mis-specified priors, imbalanced clusters, varying τ, r̂≠r, larger m,N, more real datasets.
- Clarify the relationship to indicator-matrix factorizations and prior leave-one-out/self-influence corrections in K-means.

Recommendation
Lean reject. The idea is promising, but the novelty relative to established AMP frameworks is unclear, theory is incomplete, and the experimental validation against strong, established baselines is not yet convincing. I encourage the authors to strengthen the comparisons, add state evolution/convergence analysis, and broaden the empirical study. Confidence: high.
