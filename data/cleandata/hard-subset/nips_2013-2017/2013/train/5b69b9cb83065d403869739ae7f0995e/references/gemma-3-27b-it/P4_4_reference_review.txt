This paper introduces an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and extends it to K-means type clustering. The authors build upon existing AMP work for sparse recovery and matrix factorization, aiming for a flexible algorithm capable of incorporating various prior structures on the factors. The main contributions are (1) extending a previous AMP algorithm to handle MAP estimation and (2) applying the derived AMP algorithm to clustering, formulating it as a low-rank reconstruction problem with specific constraints.

The paper is well-structured, clearly defining the problem setting and building the proposed algorithm incrementally. The connection to existing work in AMP, variational Bayes, and K-means is adequately discussed. The mathematical derivations appear sound, although a full verification requires a deep dive into the supplementary material. The notation is generally clear, though the numerous variables (Btu, Btv, Λtu, Λtv etc.) can be slightly overwhelming. The presentation of Algorithm 2 is comprehensive, and the explanations accompanying it are helpful. The numerical experiments demonstrate promising results, particularly showing improved performance over standard K-means for high-dimensional data.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The algorithm is derived using a principled AMP framework, building upon established theoretical foundations. The connection to existing Bayesian approaches and the extension to MAP estimation are well-motivated.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The problem setup, algorithm description, and experimental setup are explained clearly.
*   **Novelty:** Extending the AMP algorithm to handle MAP estimation in the low-rank matrix reconstruction context and applying it to clustering with explicit constraints represents a novel contribution.
*   **Significance:** The proposed algorithm potentially offers advantages in terms of computational cost and accuracy compared to existing methods, particularly in high-dimensional settings. The extension to clustering is a valuable application of the developed framework.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper includes experiments on both artificial and real datasets, comparing the proposed algorithm against established methods like Lloyd's K-means and K-means++.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the authors mention state evolution analysis, it is not included in the paper due to space constraints. This limits the theoretical understanding of the algorithm's performance guarantees.
*   **Convergence Discussion:** The convergence of the AMP algorithm is assumed rather than proven. While Proposition 1 provides some guarantees under specific conditions, a more robust convergence analysis would be beneficial.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The sensitivity of the algorithm’s performance to the noise variance parameter (τ) is not thoroughly investigated.
*   **Comparison to Recent AMP-based methods:** While related work is discussed, a more detailed comparison with very recent advances in AMP algorithms for matrix reconstruction (e.g., those cited as [13] and [14]) would be valuable, especially concerning the specific differences highlighted by the authors.
*   **Implementation Details:** More details on the implementation, such as how the functions *f* and *G* are approximated for different priors, would enhance reproducibility.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and novel algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
*   The algorithm is motivated by a strong theoretical framework (AMP) and demonstrates promising performance in numerical experiments.
*   The extension to clustering is a valuable contribution with potential practical applications.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed methodology.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of a detailed state evolution analysis limits the theoretical understanding of the algorithm.
*   The convergence analysis is incomplete, and the parameter sensitivity is not fully investigated.
*   The comparison to the most recent related work could be more thorough.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper with a novel and technically sound contribution. While some theoretical aspects could be strengthened, the promising experimental results and the clear presentation make it a worthwhile addition to the NIPS conference. The proposed algorithm has the potential to be a valuable tool for researchers and practitioners working on low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering problems.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
