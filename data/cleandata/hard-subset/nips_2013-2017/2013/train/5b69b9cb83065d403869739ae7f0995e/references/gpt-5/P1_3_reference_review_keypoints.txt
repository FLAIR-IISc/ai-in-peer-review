- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction using a β‑parametrized posterior to unify marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).
- AMP iterations produce factor-wise Gaussian approximations with Onsager corrections and handle general (possibly improper) intra-row priors.
- Specializes to K-means by using one-hot discrete priors for V and uniform prior for U, yielding an AMP K-means with leave-one-out bias correction and a size regularizer.
- Proves a fixed-point optimality property (Prop. 1): converged blocks are global minimizers of partially minimized objectives under a mild PSD condition.
- Situates work relative to AMP/GAMP, IterFac, BiG-AMP, bilinear GAMP, and VB matrix factorization; clarifies differences in large-system limits and intra-row dependence.
- AMP construction and β‑unification are technically sound and consistent with prior AMP literature; K-means specialization derivation is transparent.
- Empirical results (synthetic, ORL faces) show improvements over Lloyd’s, K-means++, and VBMF for accuracy and K-means loss.
- Empirical evaluation is narrow: one real dataset, balanced clusters only, Gaussian/spherical noise, and experiments that use true priors for maximum-accuracy clustering.
- No state evolution or rigorous convergence analysis provided; oscillations observed and handled heuristically via termination rules.
- Main derivations are deferred to the supplement; practical computation details for f and G, damping, and numerical stability are lightly discussed.
- No empirical time/complexity comparisons reported.
- Presentation is generally clear and organized, though notation is heavy; useful clarifications are provided about closed-form vs numerical f/G and τ estimation.
- Recommends a brief “recipe” for implementing f/G for common priors to aid reproducibility.
- Methodologically novel: extends low-rank AMP to handle MAP via β→∞ and non-factorized intra-row priors, plus a new AMP-style exact one-hot K-means variant.
- Would benefit from deeper comparison to BiG-AMP and other prior bilinear AMP work to clarify capabilities and assumptions.
- Potentially significant: flexible AMP framework reusable beyond clustering and a practically appealing K-means variant with low per-iteration cost.
- Lacks theoretical guarantees (state evolution, convergence conditions) and broader empirical validation, limiting immediate impact.
- Strengths: β‑parametrization, intra-row prior handling, intuitive K-means AMP with interpretable corrections, fixed-point optimality, and competitive empirical speed/accuracy.
- Weaknesses: no state evolution/convergence theory, narrow empirical scope, missing strong baselines (EM, spectral clustering, BiG-AMP), underspecified f/G computation and damping, possible bias toward small clusters untested.
- Suggestions: provide state-evolution sketch/validation, PSD condition discussion, ablations removing Onsager terms, sensitivity to τ and imbalanced/misspecified settings, comparisons to EM/spectral/BiG-AMP, time/iteration costs, and more priors/datasets.
- Recommend exploring β‑annealing schedules and reporting effects on convergence and quality.
- Broader impact: if stabilized and broadened, framework could be a practical tool for structured low-rank estimation and clustering, complementing VB/EM methods.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel and promising but needs stronger empirical breadth, comparisons, and partial theoretical validation.
