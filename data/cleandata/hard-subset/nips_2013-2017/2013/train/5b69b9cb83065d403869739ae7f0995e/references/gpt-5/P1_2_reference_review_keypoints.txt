- Introduces a one-parameter posterior family p̂(U,V|A; β) unifying posterior-mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference.
- Derives an AMP algorithm using per-row denoisers f and their Jacobians/covariances G for r-dimensional row priors.
- Shows removing certain Onsager terms recovers VB/ICM-style updates.
- Provides an AMP MAP algorithm for β→∞ with a fixed-point optimality property (Proposition 1) under PSD conditions.
- Instantiates the framework for clustering: models V as one-hot assignments and U as cluster centers to produce an AMP variant of K-means (AMP-KM) with a leave-one-out/size correction.
- Proposes an AMP method for “maximum accuracy” clustering (β=1) under a discrete prior on V.
- Synthetic Gaussian-mixture experiments show improved K-means loss and accuracy over Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++; AMP outperforms a VB baseline in accuracy and speed.
- ORL faces experiment: AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in most trials, but real-data evaluation is limited.
- Builds on existing bilinear/generalized AMP literature but extends to MAP via β→∞ and non-separable within-row priors; differs from Matsushita & Tanaka (2012) and bilinear GAMP in regime and prior structure.
- Technical derivations are plausible and consistent with bilinear AMP; β-parameterization is a clean unification.
- Key theoretical conditions and proofs (e.g., PSD assumptions for Proposition 1) are deferred to the supplement and need clarification.
- Per-iteration cost is O(mN); per-row computations scale with r, and β=1 may require r-dimensional integration—practical tradeoffs acknowledged.
- Empirical evaluation is encouraging but narrow: only one real dataset, little sensitivity analysis (r̂ mismatch, noise levels, non-Gaussian noise), and no runtime comparisons vs broader baselines.
- Presentation is generally clear, but important derivations and state-evolution predictions should be summarized in the main text rather than only in the supplement.
- Convergence behavior (oscillations), stopping rules, and damping/annealing heuristics are insufficiently discussed.
- Some citations are malformed and need fixing.
- Original contributions: MAP extension of bilinear AMP, handling of non-separable within-row priors, and an AMP-based K-means with explicit Onsager correction and exact one-hot enforcement.
- Significance is potentially high if robust, but omitted state-evolution analysis and limited experiments reduce confidence in broad practical impact.
- Pros: principled AMP derivation supporting MAP and structured priors; novel effective AMP-KM; solid synthetic gains; useful MAP fixed-point property.
- Cons: convergence not guaranteed and oscillatory; limited empirical scope and baselines; key theory deferred; heuristic τ estimation without robustness analysis; claim about first one-of-K enforcement should be more carefully contrasted with prior NMF work.
- Suggestions: add a concise derivation sketch and state-evolution summary in the main text; study damping/annealing (adaptive β) to mitigate oscillations; evaluate sensitivity to r̂, τ, and noise model mismatch; compare with BiG-AMP, spectral clustering, EM for GMMs, and report wall-clock times.
- Questions to clarify: when are ∑S_i and ∑T_j PSD for discrete priors; does G vanish a.e. suffice; strategies for empty clusters and tie-breaking; effect of −m/n_l term on cluster-size bias.
- Recommendation: Weak accept—interesting and novel work that needs expanded empirical evaluation and clearer theoretical exposition.
