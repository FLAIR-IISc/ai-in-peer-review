- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured factors, extending AMP to both marginalization and MAP estimation.
- Frames K-means clustering as a special case with binary factor constraints and develops an AMP-based clustering algorithm.
- Main contributions: (1) unified AMP for marginalization and MAP via a β-parameter, and (2) AMP clustering that enforces one-cluster-per-data-point constraints.
- Distinguishes from prior work by fixing rank r (not r/m) in asymptotics and not assuming independent factor components.
- Theoretical result (Proposition 1) gives convergence guarantees, showing AMP fixed points coincide with ICM fixed points to avoid bad local minima.
- Experiments (synthetic and ORL faces) are thorough; AMP outperforms Lloyd’s and K-means++ on clustering accuracy and K-means loss.
- AMP shows faster convergence than variational Bayes in experiments.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with consistent notation and implementable algorithm descriptions; supplementary material covers theoretical details.
- Novel extension of AMP to MAP estimation and novel direct handling of clustering constraints are original contributions.
- Significance: offers a computationally efficient alternative to variational Bayes and is applicable to other structured low-rank factorization problems.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with recent related work, particularly Parker et al. (2013) bilinear GAMP.
- Weakness: assumes known rank r without discussion or experiments on rank estimation and its impact.
- Weakness: insufficient quantification of computational complexity dependence on rank r and cost of computing key functions.
- Weakness: mentions state evolution but does not develop it to support iteration-complexity claims.
- Overall recommendation: accept — strong, theoretically sound, and empirically effective contribution with minor issues to address.
