- Proposes an AMP algorithm extended to MAP estimation for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means–style clustering.  
- Unifies marginalization and MAP estimation through a single parameterized algorithm (β).  
- Novel application of AMP enforcing exclusive cluster assignments; connects to ICM and Lloyd’s K-means.  
- Highlights AMP’s efficiency and scalability for large-scale matrix reconstruction and clustering.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and face (ORL) datasets shows strong performance, especially in high dimensions; includes an informative ablation study.  
- The MAP extension of AMP is a notable theoretical contribution and elegant unification.  
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and uses clear equations.  
- Does not present state evolution results or insights despite mentioning them, weakening theoretical analysis.  
- Lacks robust convergence guarantees; relies on a positive-semidefiniteness assumption for Proposition 1.  
- Insufficient investigation of parameter sensitivity (e.g., noise variance τ) and tuning strategies.  
- Limited comparison to recent AMP-based matrix factorization, spectral clustering, and advanced variational methods.  
- Experiments primarily use simple priors; impact of more complex/informative priors is unexplored.  
- Overall technically sound with supportive experiments but would benefit from deeper convergence, state-evolution, and parameter analyses.  
- Original and significant: improves over standard K-means in high-dimensional settings and provides a potentially efficient large-scale clustering approach.  
- Reviewer recommendation: strong accept for NIPS, with encouragement to address the noted weaknesses in future work.
