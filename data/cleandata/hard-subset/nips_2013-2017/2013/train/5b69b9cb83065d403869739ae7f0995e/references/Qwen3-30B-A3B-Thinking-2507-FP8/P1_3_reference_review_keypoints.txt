Summary: Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles both marginalization and MAP, applies it to K-means with an exact single-cluster constraint, provides convergence analysis (Prop.1), and shows strong empirical results.

Technical quality: Solid derivation and theoretical foundation; convergence result links AMP fixed points to ICM and per-iteration cost is O(mN).

Experimental validation: Thorough synthetic and ORL face experiments showing AMP outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and VBMF-MA across multiple metrics and trials.

Novelty and significance: Extends AMP to handle both marginalization and MAP in low-rank reconstruction and is the first to directly enforce exact one-cluster assignments within this framework.

Lack of intuition for performance gains: Paper demonstrates empirical superiority but lacks theoretical or intuitive explanation for why AMP yields better clustering.

Limited comparisons: Evaluations omit more recent or advanced clustering methods, limiting the benchmarking against state-of-the-art.

Parameter sensitivity: The τ parameter is not well-motivated for K-means and sensitivity to its estimation is not explored.

Clarity of Algorithm 3: A complex correction term in Algorithm 3 is insufficiently explained, leaving its intuition unclear to readers unfamiliar with AMP.

Overall assessment: Strong, significant contribution with sound theory and comprehensive experiments despite some explanatory and comparative gaps.

Recommendation: Accept for NIPS; final score 8 (Strong Accept).
