- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization with a temperature β interpolating posterior inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞).  
- AMP iterations include Onsager corrections and reduce to VB/ICM if Onsager terms are dropped.  
- Frames K-means as one-hot low-rank factorization and derives AMP-KM with a leave-one-out-like bias correction and cluster-size term.  
- Proposes AMP-MA (β=1) to approximate marginal MAP cluster assignments.  
- Experiments (synthetic m=800,N=1600 and ORL faces) show AMP-KM improves K-means loss and AMP-MA improves labeling accuracy versus VBMF and K-means variants.  
- Proposition: any fixed point of AMP-MAP is a blockwise global minimizer of the MAP objective.  
- Claims novelty over prior bilinear AMP (BiG-AMP/GAMP) via explicit β, non-coordinate-wise row priors, and the K-means correction.  
- Strength: clear algorithmic advance for general-rank factorization with flexible row-wise priors and explicit MAP limit.  
- Strength: clustering instantiation yields intuitive correction and empirically outperforms Lloyd/K-means++.  
- Strength: computationally attractive O(mN) per iteration with simple sufficient statistics and separable denoisers.  
- Strength: Proposition 1 provides a useful fixed-point optimality guarantee.  
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental relative to BiG-AMP/GAMP/EC/EP—β-for-MAP is known in related frameworks.  
- Weakness: Theory underdeveloped—no state evolution, no convergence proofs, and stability/damping discussions are missing.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is narrow—missing key baselines (BiG-AMP, GMM-EM, spectral methods), only one real dataset, and preprocessing details unspecified; mode collapse reported.  
- Weakness: Practical implementation details sparse (computation of f/G, τ estimation, damping, initialization, runtime, robustness to mis-specified rank).  
- Quality: Updates are plausible and consistent with bilinear AMP literature, but omitted theoretical safeguards reduce confidence.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized, but reproducibility hindered by missing implementation specifics and unrealistic stopping tolerances.  
- Originality: Moderate—useful unification and K-means framing, but incremental relative to prior bilinear AMP work.  
- Significance: Potentially useful to practitioners, but broader validation and stronger comparisons are needed to demonstrate impact.  
- Suggestions: compare directly to BiG-AMP, provide state-evolution or empirical convergence curves, detail priors/f and G computations, report runtimes and damping/initialization, expand baselines/datasets, and discuss ties to leave-one-out/size-regularized K-means.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical/simple AMP with demonstrated K-means/VB improvements, unified MMSE/MAP via β, and flexible priors.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited theoretical development, incremental novelty, and narrow/partial empirical validation with occasional pathological behavior.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
