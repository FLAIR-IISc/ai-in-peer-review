- Proposes an AMP-style algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix factorization using a β‑parametrized posterior that unifies MMSE (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference with Onsager corrections.  
- Introduces vector-valued denoisers and Jacobians acting on r̂-dimensional rows, allowing non-separable (intra-row) priors.  
- Proves that any fixed point is block-wise globally optimal under mild PSD conditions (each block optimal given the other).  
- Specializes framework to K-means (AMP-KM) using an improper uniform prior for U and one-hot prior for V, deriving assignment updates with a leave-one-out bias and cluster-size regularizer.  
- Empirical results: AMP-KM lowers K-means objective vs Lloyd/K-means++; AMP-MA can outperform VBMF-MA on synthetic data and often improves ORL face clustering accuracy.  
- Builds on AMP/GAMP and IterFac literature; extends prior general-rank AMP to MAP and vector denoisers.  
- Closely related to BiG-AMP; claimed distinctions are fixed-r asymptotics and vector denoisers enabling non-separable priors.  
- Lacks a direct empirical or theoretical comparison to BiG-AMP to clearly establish novelty.  
- Methodological strength: coherent β-bridging AMP derivation and explicit support for intra-row correlated priors.  
- Practical strength: O(mN) per-iteration cost and closed-form K-means specialization with interpretable corrections.  
- Theoretical strength: useful fixed-point optimality proposition and interpretation of Onsager terms as proximal penalties.  
- Empirical strength: demonstrated improvements in synthetic experiments and many ORL runs; fast convergence reported.  
- Main novelty concern: overlap with BiG-AMP’s sum-product/max-sum variants not fully resolved.  
- Missing state evolution and convergence analysis; damping/stepsize discussion and rigorous convergence conditions are absent.  
- Fragility observed (oscillations) with ad-hoc stopping; no systematic damping strategy provided.  
- τ estimation in clustering is ad-hoc (residual plug-in) and its effect on stability/performance is not analyzed.  
- Use of improper priors (p̂u=1) raises questions about posterior propriety and identifiability without stated conditions.  
- Experimental baselines are limited; missing comparisons to BiG-AMP, EM for spherical GMM, Hartigan-Wong K-means, and leave-one-out K-means.  
- Experimental scope lacks tests for rank mis-specification (r̂≠r), growing-r regimes, structured priors, and sensitivity to initialization.  
- Computational cost for general (β<∞) priors requiring r̂-dimensional integration is not quantified or exemplified beyond discrete priors.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, but several technical details need clarification (Λ dimensions/PSD, τ schedule, denoiser costs, mapping to VB/ICM).  
- Originality: moderate — vector denoisers and K-means instantiation are novel, but core ideas overlap with existing bilinear AMP work.  
- Significance: potentially useful for fast MF with non-separable priors and clustering, if stabilized and benchmarked more broadly.  
- Suggested improvements: provide state evolution or empirical SE validation, add damping/convergence analysis, compare to BiG-AMP, include stronger baselines and ablations, analyze rank mis-specification and τ sensitivity, and clarify computational/prior issues.  
- Pros summary: principled AMP framework bridging MMSE/MAP, efficient K-means specialization, and promising empirical gains.  
- Cons summary: limited novelty vs BiG-AMP, no state evolution, fragility and ad-hoc τ handling, limited baselines, and modest real-data gains.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept conditional on stronger BiG-AMP comparisons and convergence/SE evidence; otherwise borderline.
