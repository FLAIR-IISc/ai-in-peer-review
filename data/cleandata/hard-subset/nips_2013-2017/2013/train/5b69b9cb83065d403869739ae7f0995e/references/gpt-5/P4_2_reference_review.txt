Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction when the matrix A0 = U0V0^T is observed with additive Gaussian noise and when structural properties of the factors can be encoded via priors. The authors derive an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm that treats a one-parameter family of posteriors proportional to p̂(U,V|A)^β, unifying marginal inference (β=1) and MAP estimation (β→∞). The algorithm iterates linear “denoising” steps with Onsager-type corrections and factor-wise nonlinear updates defined via the mean and (scaled) covariance of tilted factor posteriors q̂(u; b,Λ,p̂). For β→∞, these become proximal/MAP denoisers, enabling structured MAP estimation beyond posterior means.

A second contribution is an AMP-based clustering method by casting K-means as a special case of low-rank factorization with one-hot constraints on V. The resulting algorithm differs from Lloyd’s K-means in the assignment step by including correction terms that (i) debias a datapoint’s influence on its current cluster center (a leave-one-out-like correction) and (ii) regularize toward balanced cluster sizes. The paper further describes an AMP algorithm for “maximum accuracy clustering” (marginal MAP assignments under a discrete prior on one-hot vectors). A proposition states that any fixed point of the MAP-AMP algorithm is blockwise globally optimal in each variable given the other, under PSD conditions.

The work relates to AMP and GAMP for linear inverse problems [6,7], rank-1 IterFac [8], AMP for general-rank matrix factorization [9], dictionary learning and bilinear models [13,14], and variational methods for matrix factorization [4,5,15]. Compared to [9], which focuses on posterior means, the proposed β-parametrization covers MAP; compared to variational Bayes and ICM, the AMP updates include Onsager corrections that capture dependencies neglected by mean-field. The paper also differs from [13,14] by fixing rank r as m,N→∞ and allowing within-row dependencies in priors.

Strengths
- Methodological: A clear AMP derivation that unifies MMSE and MAP via a temperature parameter β, yielding a practical algorithm for a wide range of priors, including discrete ones for clustering.
- Novel clustering angle: A principled AMP-based K-means variant with explicit one-hot constraints and transparent corrections that can reduce bias and encourage balanced clusters.
- Theoretical property: Proposition showing blockwise global optimality at fixed points under mild PSD conditions; AMP fixed points are also ICM fixed points.
- Computational: Per-iteration cost O(mN) plus O((m+N) cost of f,G), with simple r̂×r̂ operations; practical online estimation of τ in clustering.
- Empirical: On synthetic data, AMP-KM achieves lower K-means loss than K-means and K-means++ and AMP-MA achieves higher accuracy than VBMF-MA with faster convergence. On ORL faces, AMP-KM improves K-means loss and accuracy in most trials.

Weaknesses
- Limited theory in the main text: State evolution and convergence guarantees are deferred; no empirical study of robustness to deviations from AMP assumptions (e.g., finite-size effects, damping).
- Experimental scope: Comparisons restricted mainly to K-means, K-means++, and VBMF; missing comparisons to EM for Gaussian mixtures, spectral clustering, or recent bilinear AMP (e.g., BiG-AMP) when adapted to clustering.
- Sensitivity and hyperparameters: Limited analysis of τ estimation, β selection beyond β=1/∞, mis-specified priors, or mis-specified rank r̂≠r.
- Stability: Reported oscillations in assignments and two ORL runs collapsing to a single cluster; no discussion of damping, line search, or safeguards.
- Clarity of computational costs: While per-iteration cost is O(mN), the cost of evaluating f and G for general priors (numerical integration or optimization in r̂ dimensions) may be significant for larger r̂; this is not quantified.
- Scope: Gaussian noise model only; extension to other likelihoods is not demonstrated, though related work [14] suggests feasibility.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The algorithm aligns with standard AMP derivations, with appropriate Onsager terms and denoisers defined by tilted posteriors. The β→∞ specialization to MAP denoisers and the identity G∞=(Λ−H)^{-1} are consistent with implicit differentiation. The fixed-point optimality proposition is plausible. However, without state evolution or convergence analysis in the main text, guarantees are limited to fixed-point properties if convergence occurs. Practical issues (oscillation, collapse) indicate that additional stabilization may be needed.
- Empirical support: Results support the claims on both synthetic and one real dataset. However, broader baselines and runtime measurements would strengthen the case.

Clarity
- The paper is clearly organized, with precise notation and explicit update equations. The connection to variational Bayes/ICM is well explained. Some choices (online τ estimation; stopping criteria; handling oscillations) are ad hoc and could be clarified. The main derivation and proof are deferred to the supplement; including at least a brief derivation sketch and key assumptions would help.

Originality
- Extending AMP for general-rank matrix factorization to handle MAP via a β-parametrization, and directly handling discrete one-hot priors for clustering within AMP, represent novel and useful contributions relative to [9,13,14]. The K-means assignment correction terms derived from AMP are particularly insightful.

Significance
- The approach is promising for large-scale, structured matrix factorization and clustering. If robustly implemented, the AMP-based K-means variant could be broadly useful, especially in high dimensions. The general β framework could impact other bilinear problems with non-Gaussian/discrete priors. The empirical evidence is encouraging but somewhat limited; demonstrating robustness, broader applicability, and comparisons to stronger baselines would increase impact.

Arguments for acceptance
- Conceptually clean integration of MAP/MMSE within AMP for bilinear models.
- Novel, principled improvement to K-means with demonstrated gains.
- Low per-iteration complexity and practical implementability.
- Fixed-point optimality guarantees and clear relation to mean-field/ICM.

Arguments against acceptance
- Missing state evolution and convergence analysis in the main text; limited theoretical guarantees.
- Experimental scope limited; absent comparisons to strong clustering baselines and bilinear AMP variants.
- Some instability in practice; hyperparameter sensitivity and rank mis-specification not analyzed.
- Computational costs of denoisers for general priors not benchmarked.

Suggestions for improvement
- Include (at least summarized) state evolution and empirical validation of SE predictions; discuss damping and convergence safeguards.
- Add comparisons to EM for GMMs, spectral clustering, and a BiG-AMP variant adapted to clustering.
- Analyze sensitivity to τ estimation, β, rank mismatch, and prior mis-specification; report runtimes.
- Provide an ablation showing the effect of the Onsager correction terms vs. ICM to highlight their contribution.
- Discuss extensions beyond Gaussian noise and connections to generalized bilinear AMP.
