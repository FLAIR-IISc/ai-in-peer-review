- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles general rank and MAP estimation.  
- Applies the AMP framework to clustering by framing K-means as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem.  
- Claims empirical improvements over Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Provides a theoretical claim (Proposition 1) about optimality under certain conditions and links to belief propagation.  
- Builds on existing AMP literature (Donoho, Rangan, Matsushita & Tanaka, Rangan & Fletcher) and contrasts with variational Bayes and NMF approaches.  
- Strength: Extending AMP to MAP estimation increases the algorithm’s utility beyond posterior mean estimation.  
- Strength: Attempts theoretical grounding and connection to possible state evolution analysis.  
- Strength: Novel and insightful framing of K-means clustering within the low-rank reconstruction setting.  
- Strength: Strong numerical experiments demonstrating performance gains in targeted regimes.  
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with a step-by-step algorithm description.  
- Weakness: Does not include full state evolution analysis, limiting theoretical guarantees.  
- Weakness: Convergence treatment is superficial and uses ad-hoc stopping criteria in experiments.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity and tuning of key parameter τ are insufficiently explored and analyzed.  
- Weakness: Claims about O(mN) per-iteration complexity lack comparative discussion versus alternatives.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical comparisons to more modern or diverse clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral methods, DBSCAN).  
- Qualitative: Technically sound with reasonable theoretical justification and significant empirical support.  
- Qualitative: Mostly clear presentation, though some theoretical parts (e.g., Proposition 1) need more elaboration.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—expand convergence discussion, provide τ sensitivity analysis, compare computational complexity to other methods, and comment on limitations.
