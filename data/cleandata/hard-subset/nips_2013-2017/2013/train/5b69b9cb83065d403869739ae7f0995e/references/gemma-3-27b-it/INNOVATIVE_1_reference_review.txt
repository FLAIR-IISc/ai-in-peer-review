This paper introduces an Approximate Message Passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and extends its application to K-means clustering. The core idea – leveraging AMP’s efficiency and theoretical guarantees for a flexible Bayesian framework – is quite appealing and represents a potentially significant contribution. The authors correctly identify a gap in existing AMP literature, specifically extending prior work to handle MAP estimation and demonstrating application to clustering with hard assignment constraints. The connection made between low-rank reconstruction and clustering is well-established, but the AMP-based approach presented here offers a fresh perspective.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The extension of AMP to handle MAP estimation in the low-rank setting is a genuine contribution. Applying AMP directly to clustering with hard assignments, rather than relying on soft assignments as in variational Bayes approaches, is a strong point.
* **Theoretical grounding:** The paper is rooted in a solid theoretical foundation, building upon existing work in AMP and Bayesian inference. The attempt to connect the proposed algorithm to existing belief propagation and variational Bayes techniques is commendable.
* **Potential for efficiency:** AMP algorithms are known for their computational efficiency, particularly in high-dimensional settings. This is a key advantage over traditional methods like variational Bayes or MCMC.
* **Empirical results:** The numerical experiments demonstrate promising performance, particularly for high-dimensional data where the proposed AMP-KM algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++.
* **Clear problem formulation:** The problem statement is clear and concise, and the mathematical notation is generally well-defined.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited theoretical analysis:** While the paper references state evolution analysis, it does not present it. A more detailed discussion of the algorithm's theoretical properties (convergence guarantees, performance bounds) would strengthen the contribution. The claim of optimality based on Proposition 1 feels a bit hand-wavy without further justification of the positive semidefiniteness condition in practical scenarios.
* **Experimental scope:** The experimental evaluation, while encouraging, is somewhat limited. More comprehensive comparisons with state-of-the-art clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering, DBSCAN) and a wider range of datasets would be beneficial.  The justification for choosing τ = 0.1 seems arbitrary and could benefit from a more principled approach or sensitivity analysis.
* **Implementation details:** The paper lacks details regarding implementation. Are there specific libraries used? Are there any crucial implementation tricks for AMP’s convergence?
* **Clarity on assumptions:** The assumption about the true conditional distribution p(A|U0,V0) being known is a strong one and needs further discussion. In many real-world scenarios, this distribution is unknown or misspecified. How robust is the algorithm to deviations from this assumption?
* **Comparison to related work:** While the authors acknowledge related work, the comparison is superficial. A deeper dive into the differences between the proposed algorithm and existing methods for dictionary learning, blind calibration, and matrix reconstruction with generalized observation models would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper introduces a novel and potentially impactful approach to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
* The application of AMP to the clustering problem with hard assignments is a significant contribution.
* The empirical results are promising and suggest that the proposed algorithm can outperform existing methods in certain settings.
* The theoretical framework is sound and provides a foundation for future research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of detailed theoretical analysis and more extensive experimental evaluation weakens the overall contribution.
* The assumptions made about the conditional distribution and the limited discussion of robustness are concerning.
* The comparison to related work could be more thorough.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a promising paper with a novel idea and encouraging preliminary results. While not entirely polished, the boldness of the approach and potential impact outweigh the current weaknesses. I lean towards acceptance, acknowledging that further refinement and validation would be beneficial. The potential for this work to stimulate further research in AMP-based Bayesian methods is significant. I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more detailed discussion of theoretical properties and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation. The authors should also address the concerns regarding the assumptions made and provide a more thorough comparison to related work.



**Rating:** Accept (with minor revisions)
