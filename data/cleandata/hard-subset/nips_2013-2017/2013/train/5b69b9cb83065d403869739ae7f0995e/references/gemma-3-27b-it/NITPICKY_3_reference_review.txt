This paper presents an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending prior work to handle MAP estimation and applying it to K-means clustering. While the idea of leveraging AMP for matrix reconstruction is promising and builds upon a strong foundation of recent work (Donoho et al., Rangan et al., Krzakala et al.), the presentation suffers from numerous issues, ranging from minor notational inconsistencies to a lack of rigorous justification for certain claims and a concerning level of detail omitted from the experimental setup.

**Summary of Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Foundation:** The extension of AMP to MAP estimation is a valid contribution, addressing a limitation of prior AMP-based approaches for low-rank matrix reconstruction.
*   **Application to Clustering:** The formulation of K-means as a low-rank reconstruction problem within this framework is a natural and potentially beneficial connection.
*   **Potential for Efficiency:** AMP algorithms are known for their efficiency, and the proposed method *could* offer speed advantages over traditional methods, particularly for large-scale problems.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

*   **Notational Inconsistencies:** Several notational choices are jarring and make the paper harder to follow. For example, the use of both *U<sub>0</sub>* and *U* to represent different entities is confusing. Similarly, *r* sometimes denotes the true rank, while *r̂* denotes the assumed rank without consistent differentiation.
*   **Lack of Rigor:** The claim of optimality in Proposition 1 relies on the positive semidefiniteness of ∑<sub>i</sub> *S<sub>∞i</sub>* and ∑<sub>j</sub> *T<sub>∞j</sub>*, but the justification for this being “almost always” true feels hand-wavy. A more detailed analysis or demonstration would be crucial.
*   **Insufficient Experimental Detail:** The numerical experiments lack critical details. What specific parameter settings were used (e.g., learning rates, stopping criteria)? What were the computational times for each algorithm? How sensitive were the results to initialization? The statement about using the same initial value for VBMF-MA and AMP-MA needs explanation - it’s unlikely to be a fair comparison if the algorithms have drastically different convergence properties. The experiment on the ORL dataset is also severely lacking in details.
*   **Missing Comparisons:** While comparisons to Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ are provided, the lack of comparison to other modern clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering) is a significant oversight.
*   **Unclear Justification for Parameter Choice:** The introduction of the *m* factor in the noise variance (τ) feels ad-hoc without a clear justification beyond “allowing a proper scaling”. This is not adequately explained.
*   **Repetitive and Wordy:** The introduction is overly verbose, repeating the benefits of the Bayesian approach and AMP multiple times.
*    **State Evolution Omission:** The mention of state evolution analysis, a powerful tool for understanding AMP algorithms, but its subsequent dismissal due to space limitations is a significant weakness. Without it, it's hard to assess the theoretical guarantees of the proposed algorithm.

**Specific Comments:**

*   Equation (1): The constant term is missing in the exponent.
*   Section 2.2: The connection between the K-means loss function and the low-rank reconstruction formulation is not clearly explained. The constraints on *V<sub>0</sub>* are not fully leveraged in the description of the MAP problem.
*   Algorithm 2: The functions *f* and *G* are defined, but their specific forms for common prior distributions (e.g., Gaussian) are not provided, hindering practical implementation.
*   Section 4.3: The justification for using *m<sup>-2</sup>N<sup>-1</sup>∥A − U<sub>t</sub>(V<sub>t</sub>)<sup>⊤</sup>∥<sup>2</sup><sub>F</sub>* as an estimate of τ is weak and requires further support.
*   Figures 1 & 2: Axis labels and error bars are missing or unclear. Legend is missing in Figure 2.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The extension of AMP to MAP estimation is a novel contribution.
*   The application to clustering demonstrates the versatility of the proposed framework.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The numerous notational inconsistencies and lack of rigor undermine the technical soundness of the paper.
*   The insufficient experimental detail makes it difficult to assess the practical performance of the algorithm.
*   The lack of comparison to relevant baselines weakens the claim of superiority.
*   The overall presentation is lacking in clarity and precision.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potentially interesting ideas presented, the paper suffers from too many flaws to warrant acceptance in its current form. The lack of rigor, insufficient experimental details, and notational inconsistencies raise serious concerns about the technical soundness and reproducibility of the work. While the theoretical extension of AMP is valuable, the paper feels incomplete and requires substantial revision. I recommend **rejection**. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, provide a more thorough experimental evaluation, and improve the clarity and precision of the presentation. A more complete derivation of the state evolution would also greatly strengthen the paper.
