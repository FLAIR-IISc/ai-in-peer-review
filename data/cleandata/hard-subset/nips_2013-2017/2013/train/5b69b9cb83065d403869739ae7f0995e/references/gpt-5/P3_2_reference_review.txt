Summary
The paper proposes an AMP-style algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with flexible priors on the factors. It introduces a β-parametrized posterior p̂(U,V|A;β) that unifies sum-product (β=1; MMSE/marginal inference) and max-sum (β→∞; MAP) variants, leading to an iterative scheme with Onsager corrections. The denoisers f and Jacobians G act on r̂-dimensional vectors, enabling non-separable priors within each row (i.e., intra-factor correlations). A proposition shows that any fixed point is block-wise globally optimal (for each block given the other) under mild PSD conditions. As a second contribution, the authors instantiate the framework to K-means by using a uniform improper prior for U and a discrete one-hot prior for V, yielding an “AMP-KM” assignment rule that corrects the usual K-means distortion by (i) a leave-one-out bias term and (ii) a cluster-size regularizer. Experiments on synthetic data and ORL faces suggest improved K-means objective and, for β=1 with correct priors, improved accuracy over VBMF.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on AMP/GAMP (Donoho-Maleki-Montanari; Rangan) and IterFac for rank-one. Matsushita & Tanaka previously derived a general-rank AMP for posterior means; this paper extends to MAP and vector-valued denoisers. It is closely related to Bilinear GAMP (BiG-AMP), which also treats bilinear estimation with both sum-product and max-sum variants. The authors mention BiG-AMP but argue differences in asymptotic regime (fixed r) and the ability to use non-separable (within-row) priors; this is indeed a meaningful technical distinction from scalar-separable denoisers common in GAMP. The connection drawn between K-means, VBMF/ICM, and the proposed AMP via Onsager corrections is insightful; the derived assignment rule resembles leave-one-out K-means updates and naturally encourages balanced clusters.

Strengths
- Methodological: A coherent AMP derivation for matrix factorization with β parametrization that bridges MMSE and MAP; explicit vector denoisers allow intra-row dependencies in priors.
- Practical: O(mN) per-iteration cost with simple linear algebra; closed-form specialization to K-means with intuitive correction terms.
- Theory: The fixed-point optimality proposition gives a useful guarantee (block-wise global minimality) and helps interpret Onsager terms as proximal penalties that reduce bad fixed points relative to ICM.
- Empirics: On synthetic data, AMP-KM lowers K-means loss versus Lloyd/K-means++; AMP-MA improves accuracy vs VBMF-MA and converges fast. On ORL, AMP-KM improves objective and accuracy in most runs.

Weaknesses
- Novelty vs BiG-AMP: The distinction with BiG-AMP (sum-product and max-sum forms already exist there) is not fully articulated. The unique aspects here seem to be (i) fixed-r asymptotic derivation and (ii) vector-valued denoisers (non-separable priors). A direct empirical/theoretical comparison to BiG-AMP is missing.
- Theory gaps: State evolution is not provided (even in summary), and convergence conditions/damping are not discussed. The ad-hoc stopping for oscillations in AMP-KM suggests fragility; a damping scheme or proof of convergence region would help.
- Modeling/estimation: τ is unknown in clustering and is estimated via a residual plug-in; the impact on stability/performance is not analyzed. Improper priors (p̂u=1) raise questions about posterior propriety and identifiability; conditions ensuring propriety should be stated.
- Experimental scope: Baselines are limited. For clustering, EM for spherical GMM and Hartigan-Wong K-means, as well as leave-one-out K-means, are natural comparators. No experiments on rank mis-specification (r̂≠r), structured priors, or growing-r regimes. Real-data gains are modest and two runs collapsed to a single cluster.
- Practicality of general priors: For β<∞, f and G may require r̂-dimensional numerical integration; the paper does not quantify the computational burden or provide examples beyond the discrete prior.

Quality
The algorithmic development is technically plausible and consistent with AMP/GAMP literature. The fixed-point proposition is useful, though limited. However, the absence of state evolution and convergence discussion reduces confidence in robustness. The empirical evaluation supports the claims in the specific settings tested but is not comprehensive.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-organized. The AMP updates are presented cleanly. Some details need clarification: dimensions and PSD properties of Λu,Λv; conditions for propriety with improper priors; the τ estimation schedule; denoiser implementation costs; and a more explicit mapping showing that dropping Onsager terms recovers VB/ICM.

Originality
Moderate. The β-bridging and vector denoisers for matrix factorization within AMP are valuable. The K-means instantiation that yields principled corrections is novel and insightful. However, the core idea overlaps with BiG-AMP’s sum-product/max-sum variants; the paper should sharpen and empirically validate its distinct contributions.

Significance
Potentially useful to practitioners needing fast MF with non-separable priors and to clustering with hard assignment constraints. If stabilized (damping, τ handling) and benchmarked more broadly, the AMP-KM variant could be impactful for high-dimensional clustering.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a concise state evolution or at least empirical SE validation, and discuss damping/stepsize for convergence.
- Compare directly against BiG-AMP (sum-product and max-sum) on the same MF tasks and clustering instantiation (with hard one-hot priors).
- Add baselines: Hartigan-Wong K-means, EM for spherical GMM, leave-one-out K-means; perform ablations removing Onsager terms to quantify their benefit.
- Analyze rank mis-specification and growing r; study sensitivity to initialization and τ estimation; report rate of oscillations and effectiveness of damping.
- Clarify computational costs for general priors; provide examples beyond the discrete prior.
- For ORL, include standard preprocessing (centering/PCA) and report variance across seeds; discuss collapsed-cluster failure cases.

Pros
- Principled AMP framework with vector denoisers; unifies MMSE and MAP.
- Clear, efficient specialization to K-means with interpretable corrections.
- Empirical improvements on synthetic data; solid presentation.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to BiG-AMP; no state evolution.
- Fragility (oscillations), ad-hoc τ estimation; limited baselines.
- Modest real-data gains; missing analysis of rank mis-specification.

Overall recommendation: weak accept if the authors strengthen the relation to and comparisons with BiG-AMP and add convergence/SE evidence; otherwise, borderline. Confidence: high in AMP background, medium in novelty assessment given rapidly evolving bilinear AMP literature.
