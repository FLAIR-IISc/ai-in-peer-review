- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles both marginalization and MAP estimation.  
- Formulates clustering as a special case of low-rank reconstruction and develops an AMP-based K-means that enforces the one-cluster-per-datum constraint.  
- Novelty: extends AMP to low-rank settings in a unified framework, fixes rank r (not r/m) in the limit, and relaxes independence assumptions among rows.  
- Theoretical result (Proposition 1): AMP fixed points coincide with ICM fixed points, implying AMP cannot be trapped in worse local minima than ICM.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, using synthetic data and the ORL Face Database with multiple baselines and metrics.  
- Empirical results show AMP-based clustering outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ in accuracy and convergence speed, especially in high dimensions.  
- Algorithm descriptions are precise and reproducible, with clear problem formulation and pseudocode-like presentation.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and complete rather than a position paper.  
- Limitation: requires estimation of τ in practice, which is acknowledged but remains a practical concern.  
- Clarity issue: the bias correction term in equation (18b) lacks intuitive explanation and should be better motivated.  
- Comparison with recent AMP-based methods ([13,14]) is present but could be more detailed to highlight differences.  
- Could provide more intuition on why AMP empirically outperforms variational Bayes beyond theoretical dependence claims.  
- Strengths summarized: novel AMP extension, solid theory, comprehensive experiments, insightful clustering connection, strong presentation.  
- Weaknesses summarized: unclear bias-term intuition, need for deeper comparison with related works, and more intuition on AMP vs VB.  
- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to address explanatory and comparative gaps.
