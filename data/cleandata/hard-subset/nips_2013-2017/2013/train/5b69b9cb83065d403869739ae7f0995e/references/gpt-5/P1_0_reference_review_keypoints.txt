- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian bilinear low-rank matrix reconstruction A ≈ UV⊤ with flexible row-wise priors and an annealing parameter β linking marginal (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) inference.
- Generalizes prior bilinear AMP work by handling broad priors p̂u, p̂v, fixed rank r asymptotics, and providing a MAP f∞ operator via a β→∞ limit.
- Derives a K-means specialization enforcing one-hot constraints on V rows, yielding a hard-clustering algorithm with Onsager corrections to assignments.
- Presents Proposition 1 giving a fixed-point optimality property (coordinate-wise global minima under PSD conditions).
- Empirical results on synthetic GMM data show AMP-KM improves K-means loss over Lloyd/K-means++; AMP-MA improves accuracy and speed over a VB MF baseline.
- ORL faces experiments show AMP-KM often improves both K-means loss and clustering accuracy compared to K-means++.
- Situates work relative to AMP/GAMP/BiG-AMP and IterFac, noting differences (fixed r, row dependencies, explicit β bridging, one-hot enforcement).
- Notes updates reduce to VB/ICM when Onsager terms are dropped, clarifying the connection to variational methods.
- Conceptual strength: clean unification of marginal and MAP inference via β and principled AMP derivation preserving U–V dependence.
- Algorithmic strength: general-purpose f/G denoisers and computable MAP operators (f∞) with Hessian-based G∞.
- K-means strength: Onsager-corrected assignments account for center bias and cluster size, helping escape poor local minima.
- Theoretical strength: useful fixed-point optimality guarantee for coordinate-wise minima.
- Empirical strength: promising gains on synthetic and one real dataset, with faster convergence for AMP-MA vs VBMF-MA.
- Major theoretical weakness: omission of state evolution analysis and incomplete conditions for the β→∞ limit and G∞ derivation.
- Convergence concerns: no guaranteed convergence, observed oscillations, and ad hoc stopping criteria.
- Stability/robustness gap: no discussion of damping/adaptive strategies, heuristic τ estimation, and no sensitivity analyses (τ, rank misspecification, non-Gaussian noise).
- Experimental weaknesses: limited baselines (missing EM/VB for GMMs, spectral/spherical K-means, NMF), only one real dataset, no timing/memory comparisons, no ablations of Onsager or β.
- Reproducibility issues: priors, initialization, stopping/damping details under-specified and no code released.
- Novelty concern: needs clearer positioning relative to BiG-AMP and other bilinear AMP works to justify unique contributions (e.g., exact one-hot constraints).
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well-organized; β and f/G roles are explained, but some derivations are deferred and practicalities are missing.
- Originality: moderate—extends general-rank AMP for MAP and one-hot clustering, with annealing idea related to prior work.
- Significance: potentially impactful for scalable Bayesian MF and hard clustering if stabilized and broadened, but current theory and experiments are insufficient.
- Pros for acceptance: principled β-bridged AMP framework, novel AMP-based K-means with interpretable corrections, fixed-point property, and promising empirical gains.
- Cons for acceptance: no SE/convergence analysis, limited baselines/datasets, minimal practical guidance, and incomplete relation to prior bilinear AMP work.
- Key questions for authors: provide damping/step-size strategies, sensitivity to τ and rank misspecification, comparisons to EM/VB and spectral clustering, include SE results or empirical calibration, and discuss softmax/Dirichlet alternatives to one-hot priors.
- Recommendation: borderline (lean weak reject) pending stronger SE/convergence theory and broader, more robust experiments and comparisons.
