

# Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles both marginalization and MAP problems in a unified framework. The authors extend previous AMP work for compressed sensing [6,7] and the rank-one case [8] to the general-rank matrix reconstruction problem. The key innovation is a one-parameter extension of the posterior distribution that allows the same algorithm to handle both marginalization and MAP problems by varying the parameter β. The paper then applies this framework to K-means clustering, demonstrating that the proposed AMP-based clustering algorithm outperforms traditional methods like Lloyd's K-means and K-means++.

The paper relates to previous work at NIPS, particularly the AMP algorithms for compressed sensing [6,7] and the IterFac algorithm for the rank-one case [8]. It also connects to variational Bayes methods for matrix factorization [4,5] and previous work on low-rank matrix reconstruction for clustering [10,11]. The authors clearly distinguish their approach from related work [9,13,14] by noting that they fix the rank r rather than the ratio r/m in the asymptotic limit and do not assume statistical independence among components of the factor matrices.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical soundness:** The paper provides a theoretically grounded derivation of the AMP algorithm, including a proof of convergence properties (Proposition 1) showing that fixed points of the AMP algorithm are also fixed points of the ICM algorithm. The numerical experiments on both artificial and real datasets (ORL Database of Faces) demonstrate clear empirical advantages of the proposed method over competing approaches.

2. **Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with detailed mathematical formulations of the algorithm and its components. The connection between low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering is well-explained, and the algorithm is presented with sufficient mathematical detail for reproducibility.

3. **Originality:** The unified AMP framework for handling both marginalization and MAP problems is novel, extending previous work [9] that could only handle posterior mean estimation. The application to clustering is also novel in that it directly addresses the constraint that each datum should be assigned to exactly one cluster, which previous low-rank matrix reconstruction approaches [10,11] did not properly handle.

4. **Significance:** The results demonstrate that the proposed AMP-based clustering algorithm achieves better accuracy and faster convergence than existing methods, particularly for high-dimensional data. The paper shows clear practical significance for clustering applications.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited comparison with recent clustering methods:** While the paper compares with Lloyd's K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes matrix factorization, it does not compare with more recent state-of-the-art clustering methods, such as spectral clustering or deep learning-based approaches, which would better demonstrate the significance of the proposed method.

2. **Computational complexity details:** The paper mentions the computational cost is O(mN) per iteration but doesn't provide a detailed comparison with the computational complexity of other methods, making it difficult to assess the practical efficiency of the proposed algorithm.

3. **Lack of theoretical analysis of convergence rates:** While Proposition 1 establishes optimality properties of fixed points, the paper does not provide theoretical analysis of convergence rates or conditions for convergence, which would strengthen the theoretical contribution.

4. **Parameter sensitivity:** The paper does not thoroughly discuss sensitivity to parameter choices, particularly the noise variance parameter τ, which appears in the algorithm but is not well-justified for real-world applications.

**Pro/Con Acceptance Arguments:**

Pro:
- Novel unified AMP framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles both marginalization and MAP problems
- Clear theoretical derivation with convergence properties (Proposition 1)
- Strong empirical results on both synthetic and real datasets showing better accuracy and faster convergence than competing methods
- Well-written and clearly organized paper

Con:
- Limited comparison with recent clustering methods beyond the traditional ones
- Insufficient detail on computational complexity comparisons
- No thorough analysis of parameter sensitivity for real-world applications
- Missing detailed theoretical analysis of convergence rates

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The proposed AMP algorithm is theoretically sound, well-motivated, and empirically validated. While there are some limitations in the comparison with recent methods and the lack of detailed computational complexity analysis, these do not detract from the core technical contributions. The paper clearly advances the state of the art in clustering algorithms and provides a useful theoretical framework that could be applied to other related problems.

The paper meets all NIPS criteria: it is technically sound with well-supported claims, clearly written, original in its unified AMP framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction, and significant in its application to clustering with demonstrated empirical advantages over existing methods. I recommend acceptance.
