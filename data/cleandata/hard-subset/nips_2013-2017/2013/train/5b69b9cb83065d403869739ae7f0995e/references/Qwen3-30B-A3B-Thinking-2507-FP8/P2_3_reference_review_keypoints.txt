- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that supports both posterior mean (marginalization) and MAP estimation.
- Derives a unified algorithm that can incorporate various prior distributions to encode structure in low-rank factors.
- Applies the AMP framework to K-means clustering, directly enforcing the one-cluster-per-datum assignment constraint.
- Algorithm is computationally efficient: O(mN) per iteration.
- Provides a clear theoretical foundation, including Proposition 1 establishing MAP convergence properties.
- Comprehensive numerical experiments (500 synthetic instances + ORL Faces) showing superior performance to multiple baselines.
- High clarity and reproducibility: well-written with precise algorithmic descriptions (Algorithms 2,3).
- Original contribution: extends prior AMP work to MAP estimation and differently handles rank/component independence.
- Significant practical impact: outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in 48/50 ORL Faces trials and shows overall superior metrics.
- Weakness: limited discussion of algorithmic limitations, including sensitivity to priors and initialization.
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed comparison with related clustering methods ([10,11]) regarding assignment constraint handling.
- Weakness: limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity, notably the heuristic τ estimation.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong, significant contribution with minor addressable weaknesses.
