Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction for A0 = U0 V0^T from noisy observations A = A0 + W, allowing flexible structural priors on the factors via user-specified row-wise priors p̂u and p̂v. The authors derive an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm that operates on a “tempered” posterior p̂(U,V|A;β) ∝ p̂(U,V|A)^β, unifying sum-product inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞). Unlike earlier AMP formulations for bilinear models that primarily target posterior means in the general-rank case, they explicitly develop max-sum (MAP) updates, including expressions for the Jacobian G∞ via the Hessian of the log prior. They note their derivation keeps r fixed as m,N→∞ and allows dependence among components within each r-dimensional row (i.e., non-separable within-row priors), while maintaining a product structure across rows.

As a second contribution, they formulate K-means clustering as a special case of the MAP problem with p̂u uniform and p̂v supported on one-hot vectors, yielding an AMP variant of K-means (with Onsager corrections). The resulting assignment step adjusts for (i) bias from using the current point in its own cluster-center estimate and (ii) cluster-size imbalance. They also provide a maximum-accuracy clustering variant (β=1), which approximates marginal MAP assignment under user-specified priors.

The work relates to: IterFac for rank-one AMP [8]; a prior AMP for general-rank posterior means [9]; GAMP for linear models [7]; bilinear AMP variants [13,14]. Compared to variational Bayes matrix factorization (VBMF) [4,5], the authors argue that AMP’s Bethe approximation better captures U–V dependencies and yields O(mN) per-iteration cost with state-evolution predictability (analysis deferred). They also connect their K-means AMP to Lloyd’s algorithm [12] and K-means++ seeding [16].

Quality (technical soundness):
- Derivation and algorithm: The AMP updates are stated clearly with Onsager-like corrections and vector-valued denoisers f, G that allow within-row coupling. The β-tempering elegantly unifies sum-product and max-sum forms, with plausible limits as β→∞. For MAP, the identity G∞ = (Λ − H)^{-1} (when applicable) provides a computable Jacobian.
- Convergence and guarantees: A full state evolution or rigorous convergence analysis is not provided. Proposition 1 establishes that any fixed point that satisfies PSD conditions on the accumulated Jacobians is coordinatewise globally optimal, giving a meaningful stationarity property. However, the PSD conditions are argued to hold “in almost all cases” without proof, and oscillations are observed in practice for K-means.
- Computational considerations: Per-iteration complexity is O(mN), but f(b,Λ;p̂) for β<∞ may require r̂-dimensional integration; the paper focuses on small fixed r̂ and highlights discrete/analytic cases (e.g., clustering) where this is tractable. For MAP, convexity conditions on −log p̂ and Λ determine tractability; this is reasonable but not explored empirically beyond the clustering case.
- Experimental evaluation: Synthetic experiments (m=800, N=1600) compare AMP-KM and AMP-MA against Lloyd, K-means++, and VBMF-MA. Results indicate lower K-means loss for AMP-KM and higher assignment accuracy and faster convergence for AMP-MA (using true priors for both AMP-MA and VBMF-MA). On ORL faces (r̂=40), AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in most trials but occasionally collapses to a single cluster. Runtime metrics are not reported; figures are referenced but numerical values are limited.

Clarity:
The manuscript is generally clear and well-organized. The algorithmic equations are explicit. Some implementation details are deferred to the supplement (derivation, state evolution). A few practical aspects could be clarified: handling empty clusters in AMP-KM, the behavior and tuning of τ estimation, damping or safeguards for oscillations, and preprocessing in the real-data experiment.

Originality:
- The β-tempered AMP that explicitly covers the MAP limit for general-rank bilinear factorization with within-row coupled priors appears novel relative to [9], and goes beyond VBMF by incorporating Onsager corrections. While GAMP has known max-sum variants for linear models, extending these ideas to the general bilinear case with non-separable row priors and fixed-r asymptotics is a meaningful contribution.
- The AMP-based K-means algorithm that enforces one-hot assignments directly, with explicit bias/size corrections in the assignment step, is a distinctive and practically relevant instantiation.

Significance:
- For matrix factorization, the work broadens the applicability of AMP to MAP estimation with structured priors and could inform future designs of denoisers for small, fixed ranks.
- For clustering, the proposed algorithm demonstrates improved K-means objective and accuracy on high-dimensional data and a real dataset, suggesting practical value. However, broader validation (non-Gaussian noise, mis-specified r̂ and priors, runtime comparisons, robustness) would strengthen the case.

Strengths:
- Clear AMP formulation that unifies MMSE and MAP via β.
- Allows non-separable within-row priors; explicit Jacobian expressions.
- Insightful K-means specialization with interpretable corrections.
- Empirical gains over Lloyd/K-means++ and VBMF-MA (with true priors).
- Proposition 1 provides a fixed-point optimality property.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Missing state evolution and convergence analysis; observed oscillations suggest the need for damping or adaptive steps. Suggest adding damping, line search, or adaptive β schedules and reporting their effects.
- Limited baselines for bilinear AMP: comparison to bilinear GAMP variants [14] (and max-sum formulations, where applicable) would better position novelty and performance.
- Practicality outside clustering: examples with other priors (e.g., sparse nonnegative factors) would illustrate generality; report computational cost and runtime vs VBMF/ICM.
- Sensitivity analyses: mis-specified r̂; non-Gaussian noise; varying τ; empty-cluster handling; and robustness to initialization. Clarify how empty clusters are treated in AMP-KM updates.
- Real-data details: describe preprocessing (e.g., normalization), and add standard clustering metrics (NMI/ARI). Include numerical summaries for figures and runtime.

Pros for acceptance:
- Methodologically solid extension of AMP to MAP for bilinear models with structured priors.
- Novel and effective AMP-based K-means variant with consistent improvements in experiments.
- Clear presentation of algorithms and connections to VB/ICM and K-means.

Cons for acceptance:
- Lack of state evolution/convergence theory and limited discussion of stabilization.
- Incomplete empirical comparison to existing bilinear AMP methods and limited breadth beyond clustering.
- Occasional pathological behavior (cluster collapse) not fully addressed; missing runtime analysis.

Overall, the work provides a technically interesting AMP formulation and a practically promising clustering algorithm. Strengthening theoretical guarantees and empirical breadth would improve its impact.
