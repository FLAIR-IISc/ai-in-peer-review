Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction under general separable (across rows) priors on the factors U ∈ Rm×r and V ∈ RN×r, allowing non-separable structure within each r-dimensional row. The observation model is A = U0V0⊤ + W with Gaussian noise scaled as Var(Wij) = mτ, leading to a posterior proportional to exp(−(2mτ)−1||A−UV⊤||2F) p̂U(U)p̂V(V). The authors introduce a “temperature” parameter β to define a family p̂(U,V|A;β) ∝ p̂(U,V|A)β that unifies marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞). They derive an AMP algorithm (Algorithm 2) that iteratively updates estimates of U and V using Onsager-type corrections expressed via matrices of sensitivities G(·) and their sums ∑iSi, ∑jTj. For β=1, the fixed points provide approximate posterior marginals and means; for β→∞, replacing f with the max-sum operator f∞ gives a MAP solver. They state a proposition that any fixed point satisfies coordinate-wise global optimality (U is a global minimizer for fixed V and vice versa) under positive semidefiniteness conditions on the summed sensitivities.

As a second contribution, they specialize the framework to clustering by encoding one-hot cluster assignments in V (rows in {e1,…,er̂}) and use an improper uniform prior on U. This yields an AMP-based K-means variant (Algorithm 3) whose assignment update contains bias-correction and size-regularization terms compared to Lloyd’s algorithm. They also present a “maximum-accuracy” clustering variant (β=1 with a discrete prior over {e1,…,er̂}) that approximates marginal MAP assignments. Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and the ORL face dataset compare AMP-KM against Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes matrix factorization (VBMF), showing lower K-means loss for AMP-KM and higher assignment accuracy for the AMP marginal version in high-dimensional settings.

This work builds on AMP for sparse linear models [Donoho et al. 2009; Bayati & Montanari 2011], iterative bilinear AMP in the rank-1 setting [Rangan & Fletcher 2012], and general-rank bilinear/generalized AMP [e.g., Parker, Schniter & Cevher 2013]. Relative to these, the paper (i) offers a β-parameterized derivation to cover both sum-product (MMSE/marginals) and max-sum (MAP) inference; (ii) emphasizes fixed-rank asymptotics rather than fixed-ratio r/m; and (iii) allows vector-valued priors per row without requiring component-wise independence. The clustering instantiation connects to K-means and NMF-based clustering [Hoyer 2004; Ding et al. 2010] but directly enforces one-hot assignments within the AMP framework.

Strengths
- Technical contribution: A clear AMP formulation for low-rank bilinear inference that unifies β=1 (marginal) and β→∞ (MAP) cases via common update structure, including the definition of f and G for general vector priors. The connection to variational Bayes/ICM is explicit: removing Onsager terms recovers VB/ICM-type iterates.
- Insightful K-means specialization: The derivation of assignment updates with explicit bias correction for current-cluster attraction and small-cluster regularization is well motivated and practically useful.
- Empirical evidence: On synthetic high-dimensional Gaussian mixtures, AMP-KM achieves lower K-means loss than Lloyd and K-means++, and the AMP-marginal variant attains higher accuracy than VBMF with faster convergence. On ORL faces, AMP-KM generally improves both loss and accuracy over K-means++ given the same seeding.
- Proposition 1 provides a useful guarantee about coordinate-wise optimality of fixed points, clarifying the landscape relative to ICM.

Weaknesses and suggestions
Quality (soundness and evaluation)
- Derivation details and state evolution: The AMP derivation and state evolution claims are deferred to the supplementary and not summarized. Since convergence and accuracy of AMP critically depend on modeling assumptions (large-system limits, randomness, and Onsager terms), an abbreviated outline and clear conditions for validity would strengthen the main paper.
- Possible error in Algorithm 3 scaling: In (17a), Λtu is written as Λtu = mτ (Vt)⊤Vt, which appears inconsistent with (9a). To recover the least-squares update U = AV(V⊤V)−1, it should be Λtu = (1/(mτ))(Vt)⊤Vt (then U t = Btu(Λtu)−1 yields AV(V⊤V)−1). Please check and correct.
- Stability and convergence: The AMP-KM occasionally collapses to a single cluster (reported in 2 trials). The paper would benefit from a brief discussion of damping, step-size control, or safeguards for empty/degenerate clusters, and a sensitivity analysis to τ estimation and initialization.
- Baselines and scope of experiments: For clustering, comparisons are limited to K-means, K-means++, and VBMF. Including Gaussian mixture EM with shared/diagonal covariance, spectral clustering, or recent scalable K-means variants would contextualize gains. For the general matrix reconstruction setting (beyond clustering), there are no experiments demonstrating benefits with other priors (e.g., sparsity, non-negativity), despite this being a stated motivation.
- Runtime reporting: While per-iteration complexity is O(mN), actual wall-clock comparisons (including cost of f/G evaluations and matrix inversions in r̂) are not provided.

Clarity
- Generally well organized, with explicit algorithms and motivations. A few notational inconsistencies/typos occur (e.g., the Λtu scaling noted above; minor reference formatting). Clarify that priors are separable across rows but can be arbitrary within rows, and discuss conditions under which G∞ exists and is PSD.
- The role of τ in clustering: Although its introduction is justified by the AMP derivation, a short practical guide to estimating τ and the impact of its misestimation on assignments would help practitioners.

Originality
- The β-parametrization unifying marginal and MAP in a single AMP framework and the vector-prior treatment are useful. However, related bilinear AMP work (e.g., BiG-AMP) includes both sum-product and max-sum variants; explicitly contrasting capabilities (e.g., dependence within row vectors, fixed-rank asymptotics) would clarify novelty. The clustering instantiation that directly enforces one-hot assignments within AMP appears novel compared to NMF-based clustering.

Significance
- If robust and broadly applicable, the approach could impact large-scale clustering and bilinear inference with structured priors. The K-means improvements in high dimensions are promising. Demonstrations beyond clustering (e.g., sparse/nonnegative factors) and comparisons to established bilinear AMP variants would increase significance.

Pros for acceptance
- Solid, general AMP formulation covering both marginal and MAP inference with vector priors.
- Clear and practically meaningful specialization to K-means with improved empirical performance.
- Insightful link to VB/ICM and a fixed-point optimality proposition.
- Low per-iteration cost and simple updates.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited theoretical exposition in the main paper; no state evolution results presented.
- Potential error in Algorithm 3 scaling; occasional instability (cluster collapse) reported.
- Experimental scope narrow (few baselines; no reconstruction tasks beyond clustering).
- Limited discussion of conditions for convergence and robustness to model mismatch.

Suggestions for improvement
- Correct and clarify Algorithm 3 scaling; add damping/stability guidance.
- Include at least one non-clustering reconstruction experiment exploiting non-Gaussian priors (e.g., sparse or non-negative factors) to showcase generality.
- Compare with a max-sum bilinear AMP baseline (e.g., BiG-AMP) where applicable and discuss differences (fixed-rank limit, within-row dependence).
- Provide brief state evolution equations and assumptions, or at minimum summarize key predictions and empirical alignment.
- Report runtimes and iteration counts across methods.

Overall, the paper presents a technically sound and potentially impactful AMP framework with a novel and effective application to K-means-type clustering. Strengthening theory presentation and broadening experiments would materially improve the submission.
