Summary and relation to prior work
The paper develops an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction under general (possibly improper) priors on the factor rows u_i and v_j. A one-parameter family of posteriors p̂(U,V|A; β) is introduced to unify marginalization (β=1) and MAP (β→∞). The resulting algorithm (Alg. 2) resembles variational Bayes (VB) matrix factorization but includes Onsager-like correction terms; when those are dropped, it reduces to a VB/ICM-style algorithm. A fixed-point optimality result (Prop. 1) shows coordinate-wise global optimality of the limit (under PSD conditions). As a concrete application, the paper instantiates the algorithm to the K-means setting by enforcing one-hot constraints on V, producing a modified assignment rule (Alg. 3; Eq. 18b) that includes bias correction for current assignments and a small-cluster regularizer. The authors also discuss a “maximum accuracy” clustering variant (β=1). Experiments on synthetic Gaussian mixtures and the ORL faces dataset compare AMP variants to Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, and VBMF.

This work builds on AMP for compressed sensing [Donoho et al., Rangan], IterFac for rank-1 [Rangan & Fletcher], and low-rank AMP [Matsushita & Tanaka], and is related to the bilinear/generalized AMP literature (e.g., BiG-AMP [Parker et al.], blind calibration/dictionary learning [Krzakala et al.]). The contribution relative to prior AMP for matrix factorization is to (i) explicitly handle MAP estimation via a “max-sum”-type limit (β→∞), and (ii) apply the framework to K-means with exact one-hot constraints, deriving a new assignment rule.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The derivation (details in supplement) follows standard AMP approximations. The correspondence to VB when Onsager terms are removed is clearly explained. Prop. 1 is a useful (if limited) guarantee for fixed points. However, the paper assumes convergence; state evolution (SE) is claimed possible but omitted, and no empirical SE validation is provided. The τ estimation heuristic in the K-means instantiation is ad hoc; sensitivity is not studied. Convergence issues are acknowledged (oscillations, collapse to one cluster in 2/50 trials).
- Empirical support: Synthetic experiments are well controlled and show clear gains in K-means loss (AMP-KM vs Lloyd/K-means++) and in clustering accuracy (AMP-MA vs VBMF). The real-data experiment (ORL) shows consistent improvements over K-means++ with reasonable iteration counts. That said, baselines are limited: for clustering, spectral clustering, spherical K-means, and EM for equal-covariance GMMs are natural comparators; for AMP/MAP matrix factorization, comparisons to BiG-AMP (sum-product and max-sum) would better contextualize performance. Runtime and memory usage are not reported.
- Generality vs practice: Although the framework accommodates rich priors (non-Gaussian, non-iid within each row), all empirical results use either one-hot v or Gaussian priors with known parameters; there is no demonstration with sparse/nonnegative priors or missing data.

Clarity
The paper is generally well organized and readable. The AMP updates are stated clearly, and the K-means specialization is nicely derived and interpreted (the correction term 2m/ntl I(vtj=el) and the small-cluster term −m/ntl in Eq. 18b). Some points that would improve clarity:
- Precisely state assumptions under which G∞ = (Λ − H)−1 holds and when Λ − H is invertible.
- Discuss computational/memory complexity beyond O(mN): storing S_i and T_j as r̂×r̂ matrices costs O((m+N)r̂^2), which may be material when r̂ is moderate.
- Explain the unusual noise scaling Var(Wij)=mτ and its practical implications; clarify how τ is estimated and its impact on assignments.
- Summarize (even briefly) the supplement’s derivation and conditions for the Onsager terms.

Originality
The idea of using AMP for low-rank bilinear models is not new, and variants like BiG-AMP treat generalized likelihoods and priors. The paper’s claims of novelty rest on: (i) explicit handling of MAP (β→∞) within the bilinear AMP framework, and (ii) a direct K-means instantiation enforcing one-hot constraints inside AMP. The latter appears novel and insightful. For (i), there is related “max-sum” GAMP literature; the paper should better position its MAP-AMP relative to max-sum GAMP/BiG-AMP and clarify what is new technically (e.g., handling non-iid row priors, fixed-rank SE limit, specific Onsager forms). As written, the originality over existing bilinear AMP is plausible but not crisply established.

Significance
If robust, the K-means variant could be impactful: it achieves lower K-means loss than Lloyd/K-means++ on high-dimensional data with similar iteration counts, and the assignment correction has an appealing interpretation. The general AMP-MAP framework could broaden applicability of AMP beyond posterior means, useful for structured priors. However, the current empirical scope is narrow, and missing comparisons to closely related AMP baselines limit the strength of the claim. The absence of SE analysis or convergence guidance also tempers the theoretical significance.

Specific suggestions
- Compare against (and cite clearly) sum-product and max-sum BiG-AMP; articulate differences (assumptions, denoisers, Onsager terms, SE regime).
- Provide damping/stepsize strategies and report their effect on convergence and the “collapse” failures.
- Add clustering baselines (spectral/spherical K-means, EM for equal-covariance GMM) and report wall-clock/runtime.
- Demonstrate at least one nontrivial prior (e.g., sparse or nonnegative factors) to substantiate generality.
- Either include a brief SE derivation or an empirical SE validation; otherwise, temper claims.
- Analyze sensitivity to τ estimation and initialization; discuss practical guidelines.

Pros
- Clear, principled derivation of an AMP algorithm unifying marginalization and MAP (β-parameterization).
- Insightful and novel K-means instantiation with bias-corrected assignments; consistent empirical gains over Lloyd/K-means++.
- Low per-iteration complexity and simple updates; coordinate-wise optimality at fixed points (Prop. 1).
- Good exposition and connection to VB/ICM; helpful interpretation of Onsager corrections.

Cons
- Missing comparisons to closely related bilinear AMP (sum-product/max-sum BiG-AMP); novelty not crisply positioned.
- No state evolution or convergence analysis; practical convergence issues observed.
- Limited empirical scope (one real dataset; few baselines; no runtime); use of true priors in synthetic “maximum accuracy” is optimistic.
- Sensitivity to τ and initialization not analyzed; occasional catastrophic collapse.

Recommendation
Borderline accept. The K-means AMP variant is an interesting and potentially useful contribution with empirical benefits, and the general β-AMP framing for MAP is promising. However, to merit strong acceptance, the paper should better position itself relative to BiG-AMP/max-sum GAMP, expand empirical comparisons (including runtime), and provide at least partial SE/convergence analysis or robustification. If these issues can be addressed in the final version, the contribution would be solid for NeurIPS.
