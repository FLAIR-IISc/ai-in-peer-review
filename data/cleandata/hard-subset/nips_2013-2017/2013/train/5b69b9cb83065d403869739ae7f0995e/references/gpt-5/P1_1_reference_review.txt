Summary
The paper studies low-rank matrix reconstruction when additional structure on the factors is known a priori, and proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm that unifies marginal posterior inference and MAP estimation via a one-parameter family p̂(U,V|A; β) ∝ p̂(U,V|A)β. The algorithm handles general row-wise priors p̂u, p̂v over Rr̂ (not necessarily component-wise independent), has O(mN) per-iteration cost, and reduces to previous AMP for bilinear models [9] when computing posterior means. A key technical point is the inclusion of Onsager-like correction terms that distinguish it from variational Bayes (VB) and ICM; when those terms are removed, the updates become equivalent to VB/ICM with Gaussian priors [4]. The authors also prove a blockwise optimality property at fixed points (Proposition 1).

As a second contribution, the paper formulates K-means clustering as a special case of the bilinear model with a one-hot prior on rows of V and a flat prior on U, and derives a specialized AMP algorithm (Algorithm 3) for K-means. The resulting assignment rule augments Lloyd’s step with two corrections: a leave-one-out bias correction and a term encouraging assignments to small current clusters. They also present an AMP variant for maximum-accuracy clustering (β=1) with discrete priors on V.

Experiments on synthetic data (m=800, N=1600) compare Lloyd’s K-means, K-means++, VB matrix factorization, and the proposed AMP variants; AMP-KM achieves the smallest K-means loss and AMP-MA the highest accuracy, with fast convergence. On the ORL Faces dataset, AMP-KM outperforms K-means++ in most trials in loss and accuracy when both are initialized via K-means++.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The AMP derivation follows the established path for dense bilinear models and introduces a β parameter to interpolate between marginalization (β=1) and MAP (β→∞). The fixed-point characterization (Prop. 1) is a useful guarantee for the MAP case. However, the paper explicitly omits the state evolution (SE) analysis despite claiming it can be given; without SE or convergence/damping guidance, practical robustness is unclear. Oscillations in Algorithm 3 are reported; no damping or annealing strategy is provided.
- Correctness/clarity of algorithms: The specialization to K-means is elegant; the leave-one-out correction explains the improved behavior. There appears to be a likely typo in Algorithm 3: to recover cluster means ũl from U=B (Λ)−1, Λtu should be (1/(mτ)) V⊤V (so that U equals sample means); as written Λtu=mτ V⊤V would be inconsistent with Eq. (18a). Please verify and correct.
- Empirics: Synthetic experiments are clean and show sizable gains in loss/accuracy and iteration counts; however, runtime comparisons (wall-clock) are missing. The maximum-accuracy results use the true priors, which is optimistic; robustness to prior misspecification is not studied. Baselines for clustering are limited to K-means variants and a VB factorization; comparisons to EM-GMM (with tied spherical covariance), spectral clustering, or NMF-based clustering (and to BiG-AMP [14] with suitable priors) would strengthen claims. The ORL experiment is small-scale but suggests practical promise.

Clarity
The paper is generally well organized and readable. The β-trick neatly unifies inference modes. The role of f and G is clear, and computational complexity is discussed. Some presentation issues:
- Minor notation/typo issues (Algorithm 3 Λ scaling; several reference formatting glitches).
- The estimation of τ via residuals (Sec. 4.3) is ad hoc; briefly justify or derive it (e.g., as an EM update), and report sensitivity.
- More examples of p̂u/p̂v and the corresponding closed-form f, G (e.g., nonnegativity, sparsity, one-hot) would improve reproducibility.

Originality
- Extending bilinear AMP to directly accommodate MAP via β→∞ and to produce marginal MAP approximations is novel and useful, especially given that [9] focused on posterior means.
- Casting K-means with one-hot constraints into this AMP framework with explicit Onsager corrections appears to be new; the resulting assignment rule is distinct from Lloyd/ICM.
- Relation to closely related bilinear AMP methods (e.g., BiG-AMP [14]) is mentioned but not deeply compared; clarify what your fixed-r limit and non-factorized row priors enable that [14] cannot, and whether [14] could handle the discrete one-hot prior similarly.

Significance
The work is potentially impactful for both AMP theory/practice and clustering:
- For matrix factorization, a practical AMP that supports MAP and general priors fills a gap between VB/ICM and posterior-mean AMP.
- For clustering, the AMP-KM variant has a simple form, nearly the same per-iteration cost as Lloyd’s algorithm, and shows improved solutions in high dimensions.
However, the absence of SE-based performance predictions, limited comparisons, and lack of convergence guidelines somewhat limit immediate uptake.

Pros
- Clear, principled AMP derivation unified via β; includes MAP.
- Useful fixed-point optimality property for MAP.
- Novel and appealing AMP interpretation of K-means with leave-one-out correction.
- Empirical gains on synthetic and a real dataset with low overhead.
- O(mN) per-iteration complexity; only r̂×r̂ inversions.

Cons
- No state evolution or convergence analysis; oscillations observed; no damping/annealing guidance.
- Likely typo in Algorithm 3; some clarity issues.
- Limited baselines (no EM-GMM/spectral/NMF; no comparison to BiG-AMP).
- Robustness to prior misspecification not evaluated; r̂ ≠ r not explored.
- No wall-clock runtime comparisons.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide SE equations (even in supplement) and practical damping/stepsize recommendations; report their effect on oscillations.
- Fix Algorithm 3 scaling and include a brief derivation linking it to the mean-of-assigned-points update.
- Add baselines: EM for GMM with tied spherical covariances (equivalent to K-means in the limit), spectral clustering, NMF-based clustering, and BiG-AMP configured with discrete priors.
- Evaluate sensitivity to misspecified priors for AMP-MA, and to r̂ ≠ r.
- Report wall-clock times and memory use.
- Justify/update τ via an EM step; report sensitivity.
- Release code or pseudocode for f, G for common priors.

Recommendation
Borderline accept. The paper presents a conceptually clean and practically promising extension of bilinear AMP to MAP and a novel AMP-based K-means algorithm with compelling empirical improvements. Strengthening theory (SE/convergence) and broadening comparisons would solidify its contribution.
