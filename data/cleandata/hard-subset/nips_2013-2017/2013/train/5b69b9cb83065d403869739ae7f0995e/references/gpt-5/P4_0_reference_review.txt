Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with additional structure on the factors and proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm that (i) handles general rank r with separable priors across rows but allowing arbitrary dependencies within each r-dimensional row, and (ii) unifies posterior mean (marginalization) and MAP estimation via a “temperature” parameter β, recovering marginal inference at β=1 and MAP as β→∞. A notable byproduct is a K-means-type clustering algorithm derived as a special case when the right factor rows are one-hot vectors, yielding an AMP-corrected assignment rule that differs from Lloyd’s K-means by including Onsager-like corrections for current assignments and cluster sizes.

The approach builds on prior AMP/GAMP developments for linear inverse problems and bilinear/factorization models (e.g., Rangan 2011; IterFac for rank-1; earlier AMP for general rank that computes posterior means). It also relates to variational Bayesian matrix factorization (VBMF), and to Lloyd’s K-means (interpretable as ICM on the K-means objective). Compared to VBMF, the proposed AMP uses Bethe-style approximations that account for dependencies between U and V, and includes Onsager corrections. The paper positions itself relative to recent bilinear AMP variants (dictionary learning, bilinear GAMP), emphasizing fixed-r (as m,N→∞) and non-factorized within-row priors. The clustering application directly enforces the one-hot constraint within the AMP framework, differing from NMF/spectral relaxations.

Quality
- Technical correctness: The derivation is consistent with AMP principles. The algorithm (Alg. 2) includes appropriate Onsager terms via sums over S and T, and uses scalar/vector denoisers f and their Jacobians G defined via tilted distributions q̂. The β-trick is standard (power posterior) and cleanly unifies MMSE/MAP. For MAP, the max-sum form f∞ and the implicit Jacobian via the Hessian are appropriate. The K-means specialization (Alg. 3) yields a simple update for U (least squares) and a modified assignment rule for V with clear interpretations (bias correction and size correction).
- Theoretical support: The paper states (but defers to supplementary) state evolution and a fixed-point optimality proposition (blockwise global optimality of U at fixed V and vice versa under PSD conditions). These are valuable but not shown in the main text. Convergence is not guaranteed and damping or safeguards are not discussed.
- Empirics: Synthetic experiments (m=800, N=1600) compare AMP-KM to Lloyd and k-means++, and maximum-accuracy variants (AMP-MA vs VBMF-MA) under matched priors. AMP-KM attains lower K-means loss; AMP-MA attains higher accuracy and converges quickly. Real-data test (ORL faces) shows consistent improvements in K-means loss and accuracy over k-means++, with two degenerate runs producing a single cluster. Baselines are limited and ablations are minimal.

Clarity
The problem setup, notation, and algorithms are clearly presented. The role of β, the definitions of f/G and their MAP limits, and the specialization to K-means are well-explained, including an intuitive interpretation of the modified assignment rule. Some implementation details (choice of τ and its residual-based estimate; handling oscillations, empty clusters; damping) are only briefly noted. Proof details and state evolution are omitted from the main paper.

Originality
- Extending general-rank AMP for matrix factorization to accommodate both marginal posterior and MAP estimation via a single formalism, with non-factorized within-row priors, appears novel relative to earlier IterFac (rank-1) and prior AMP works focused on MMSE-only or factorized priors. It would be helpful to explicitly relate to max-sum variants of GAMP/bilinear GAMP where MAP analogs exist, but the presented formulation covers a broader class of within-row priors.
- The application to clustering that enforces the one-hot constraint within the AMP derivation and yields a concrete corrected assignment rule is a distinctive contribution over Lloyd/ICM and NMF-based approaches.

Significance
The approach is potentially impactful: a flexible AMP framework for low-rank matrix reconstruction (with arbitrary within-row priors) that can handle both MMSE and MAP and yields practical gains for K-means-like clustering. However, the empirical scope is limited (one synthetic setting across r, one real dataset, few baselines). The theoretical claims (state evolution, convergence behaviors) are referenced but not developed in the main paper. Broader adoption would benefit from more extensive comparisons and robustness analysis.

Strengths
- Clear AMP derivation that unifies marginalization and MAP via β and supports non-factorized within-row priors.
- Computationally efficient per-iteration cost O(mN), with simple denoisers for discrete/convex priors.
- Insightful K-means specialization with interpretable corrections; consistent improvements over Lloyd/k-means++ in the presented experiments.
- Fixed-point proposition establishing blockwise optimality provides some optimality guarantee at convergence.

Weaknesses
- Missing main-text state evolution and convergence analysis; practical guidance (damping, step-size) is limited.
- Limited empirical evaluation: few baselines (no GMM/EM, spherical/spectral k-means, mini-batch k-means), no runtime comparisons, no ablations of Onsager terms, and no robustness to prior mismatch.
- For clustering, τ estimation is ad hoc; degeneracy (single cluster) occurs in some runs with limited discussion of mitigation.
- Relation to max-sum/bilinear GAMP literature could be strengthened to clarify novelty relative to MAP-capable variants.

Suggestions for improvement
- Include (or summarize) state evolution equations and provide empirical validation of predictions (MSE, overlaps), at least in the supplement.
- Add damping/stepsize guidelines and convergence diagnostics; analyze sensitivity to τ estimation in clustering.
- Expand baselines: EM for Gaussian mixtures (equal/diagonal covariances), spherical/spectral k-means, NMF-based clustering; and compare to bilinear GAMP implementations (MMSE and MAP).
- Provide runtime/iteration complexity and wall-clock comparisons; add ablations removing Onsager terms to quantify their impact.
- Discuss handling of empty/degenerate clusters and initialization strategies beyond k-means++.

Arguments for acceptance
- Methodologically solid AMP extension covering both MMSE and MAP with broader priors.
- Novel and practically useful K-means variant with improved performance in high dimensions.
- Clear formulation and potential to influence subsequent work on AMP for matrix factorization and clustering.

Arguments against acceptance
- Insufficient theoretical development presented in the main text; convergence/state-evolution not substantiated.
- Empirical evaluation is narrow with limited baselines and robustness analysis.
- Some practical issues (τ estimation, degeneracies) are unresolved.

Overall, the paper presents a promising and technically sound direction with clear ideas and initial evidence of benefits, but would benefit from stronger theoretical exposition and broader empirical validation.
