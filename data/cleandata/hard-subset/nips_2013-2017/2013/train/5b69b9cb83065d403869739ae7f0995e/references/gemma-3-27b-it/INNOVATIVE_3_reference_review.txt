This paper presents an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending prior work to handle MAP estimation and applying it to a novel clustering framework. The core idea – leveraging AMP’s efficiency and theoretical guarantees for a problem traditionally tackled with computationally intensive Bayesian methods – is highly promising and represents a significant departure from standard approaches. The application to clustering, framing it directly as a constrained low-rank problem and addressing the single assignment constraint, is a particularly interesting contribution.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of AMP with Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction *and* its application to clustering with explicit constraint handling is genuinely novel. While AMP has seen increased usage, its application in this specific context, particularly addressing MAP estimation and the clustering constraint, is a significant contribution. The extension beyond posterior mean estimation (as in [9]) is crucial.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The grounding in approximate message passing and the connection to belief propagation provide a strong theoretical basis. The mention of state evolution (though not detailed due to space constraints) suggests a path toward rigorous performance analysis.
* **Potential Efficiency:** AMP algorithms are known for their computational efficiency. The claim of linear complexity (O(mN)) is compelling, especially for large-scale problems.
* **Promising Experimental Results:** The numerical experiments demonstrate performance advantages over Lloyd’s K-means, particularly in higher-dimensional spaces. The results on the ORL face database further suggest practical applicability. The comparison with K-means++ is particularly relevant.
* **Clear Formulation:** The problem setting and mathematical formulation are well-defined and relatively easy to follow, given the complexity of the underlying concepts.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Detail:** The paper alludes to the state evolution analysis but doesn’t present it. A more thorough theoretical treatment, even in a supplementary material, would significantly strengthen the work. The proposition regarding optimality, while interesting, feels somewhat isolated without a more complete theoretical justification.
* **Experimental Scope:** The experiments, while demonstrating improvements, are limited in scope. More diverse datasets and a wider range of parameter settings would bolster the claims of robustness and generalizability. A more detailed ablation study examining the effect of different prior distributions (p̂U, p̂V) would also be valuable.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The algorithm introduces the parameter τ, and its estimation method described in section 4.3 feels somewhat ad-hoc. A deeper investigation into the sensitivity of the algorithm to this parameter, and its optimal selection strategies, is warranted.
* **Comparison to State-of-the-Art Clustering:** While comparisons to K-means and K-means++ are present, a comparison to more advanced clustering methods (e.g., spectral clustering, DBSCAN) would further contextualize the performance of the proposed algorithm.
* **Clarity of Connection to Existing AMP Work:** While the paper attempts to differentiate itself from related work ([13, 14]), a more detailed comparison, perhaps in a table, outlining the key differences in assumptions and algorithmic approaches would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper tackles a challenging problem with a novel and theoretically grounded approach.
* The proposed algorithm offers a promising alternative to existing methods, particularly in scenarios where efficiency is paramount.
* The clustering application is innovative and addresses a practical limitation of traditional low-rank based clustering methods.
* The experimental results provide initial evidence of superior performance.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The lack of detailed theoretical analysis weakens the overall impact of the paper.
* The limited scope of the experiments raises questions about the generalizability of the results.
* The parameter estimation strategy could be improved.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some weaknesses, this paper presents a compelling and potentially significant contribution to the field. The novelty of the approach, the strong theoretical foundation, and the promising experimental results outweigh the limitations. The algorithm's potential for efficiency makes it particularly attractive. I believe this paper is well-suited for NIPS, even with the minor theoretical and experimental gaps. I would recommend **acceptance** with a request for the authors to expand the theoretical analysis (perhaps in the supplementary material) and conduct more comprehensive experiments in a revised version. The work demonstrates a bold approach and has the potential to influence future research in low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
