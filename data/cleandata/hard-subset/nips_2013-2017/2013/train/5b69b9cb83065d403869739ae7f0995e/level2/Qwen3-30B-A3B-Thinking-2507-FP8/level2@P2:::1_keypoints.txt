- Proposes an AMP algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework that can incorporate structural properties (e.g., sparsity).  
- Presents a unified AMP-based method handling both marginalization and MAP estimation for general-rank matrix reconstruction.  
- Provides a theoretical link (Proposition 1) between AMP fixed points and the ICM algorithm for MAP estimation.  
- Applies the AMP framework to K-means clustering by reformulating clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem.  
- Shows empirical improvement: AMP-based clustering (Algorithm 3) outperforms Lloyd’s K-means in numerical experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation includes both synthetic data (varying cluster counts) and the ORL Face Database, using multiple metrics (normalized K-means loss, accuracy, convergence speed).  
- Paper is well-written with clear math, precise algorithm descriptions, and an appropriate computational-complexity analysis (O(mN) per iteration).  
- Effectively contrasts with prior AMP work by fixing rank r rather than the ratio r/m in asymptotics.  
- Insightful discussion of relationships between AMP and variational Bayes matrix factorization.  
- Insufficient discussion of how this clustering approach differs from prior low-rank clustering work (e.g., handling the one-cluster-per-datum constraint).  
- Theoretical justification is limited: convergence properties of the proposed AMP algorithm are not thoroughly analyzed or empirically validated.  
- Experimental results report means and SDs over 500 trials but lack formal statistical significance testing.  
- Limited exploration of practical limitations and applicability to more challenging real-world scenarios.  
- Could better position and contrast the method against other Bayesian matrix factorization and clustering approaches in recent literature.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and promising empirical performance; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the above weaknesses.
