- Novel application of the AMP framework to K-means clustering, producing an algorithm (Alg. 3) distinct from Lloyd’s with bias-correcting assignment terms.  
- Technical development appears sound; Proposition 1 links AMP MAP fixed points to ICM and suggests reduced susceptibility to poor local minima.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured, with a precise problem setup and clear presentation of the algorithm and its relation to Lloyd’s.  
- Strong empirical validation: AMP-KM/AMP-MA outperform K-means++, Lloyd’s, and VB baselines on synthetic data and the ORL face dataset.  
- Full AMP derivation is relegated to the supplement; the main text would benefit from a high-level derivation sketch.  
- Omission of state evolution analysis is a missed opportunity; including predictions and comparison to empirical results would strengthen theory.  
- Related work coverage is brief; a deeper comparison to concurrent AMP work (e.g., dictionary learning, bilinear problems) is needed, especially regarding fixing r vs. r/m.  
- The adaptive noise-parameter (τ) estimation used in K-means lacks justification, convergence analysis, and sensitivity discussion.  
- Real-data experiments had 2/50 failures; the paper should discuss possible failure modes (divergence or trivial solutions) and robustness to initialization.  
- Pro-accept: novel, effective, and empirically strong AMP-based clustering with solid contributions.  
- Con-accept: needs deeper theoretical analysis (state evolution), more self-contained derivation, and better justification of practical algorithmic choices.
