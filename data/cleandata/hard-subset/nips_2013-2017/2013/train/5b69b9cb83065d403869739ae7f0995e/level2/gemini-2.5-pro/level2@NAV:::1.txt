This paper presents a novel algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction based on Approximate Message Passing (AMP). It connects the belief propagation literature to the well-studied problem of matrix factorization, extending previous AMP-based work to handle general rank and MAP estimation, not just posterior means. The paper’s main contribution is the application of this framework to K-means clustering. By formulating clustering as a low-rank matrix reconstruction problem with a one-hot constraint on one of the factor matrices, the authors derive a new, efficient clustering algorithm (AMP-KM). This work relates to previous research in both AMP for signal processing and matrix factorization for clustering, but offers a novel, principled derivation of a clustering algorithm that directly handles the discrete assignment constraints within the AMP framework.

**Strengths:**
*   **Originality & Significance:** The derivation of a K-means-like algorithm from the AMP framework is highly original and significant. The resulting update rules (Eq. 18b) provide an intuitive bias-correction mechanism that helps the algorithm escape poor local minima that plague traditional methods like Lloyd's algorithm. This provides a fresh perspective on a classic problem.
*   **Quality:** The claims are well-supported by strong empirical evidence. The experiments on both synthetic and real-world data (ORL faces) clearly demonstrate that the proposed AMP-based clustering algorithms outperform established baselines like K-means++ and Variational Bayes Matrix Factorization in terms of both K-means loss and clustering accuracy. The theoretical analysis, showing that AMP fixed points are also ICM fixed points, provides a solid justification for the algorithm's potential to find better solutions.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and logically structured. The connection between the general matrix reconstruction problem and the specific application to clustering is explained clearly.

**Weaknesses:**
*   **Theoretical Completeness:** The paper assumes convergence of the proposed algorithm, which is not guaranteed for AMP in general. While state evolution analysis is mentioned as a tool for predicting performance, its omission due to space constraints leaves the theoretical grounding partially incomplete.
*   **Heuristic Parameter Selection:** The noise parameter τ, which is absent in the original K-means problem, is introduced via the AMP derivation and handled with a heuristic estimator during iterations. A more principled discussion or analysis of this parameter's role and selection would strengthen the method.

In summary, this is a strong paper with a novel core idea and compelling experimental validation. The weaknesses are minor and understandable given conference page limits.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   Presents a novel and insightful connection between AMP and K-means clustering.
*   The proposed algorithm demonstrates superior performance over strong baselines on multiple datasets.
*   Opens up new avenues for developing clustering algorithms from first principles.

**Arguments for rejection:**
*   Lacks a formal convergence analysis.
