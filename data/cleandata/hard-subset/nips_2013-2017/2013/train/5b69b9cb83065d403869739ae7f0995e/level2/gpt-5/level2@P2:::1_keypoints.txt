- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with a temperature parameter β unifying posterior mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) estimation.  
- Derivation yields a Bethe-approximate inference that preserves within-row U/V dependencies, unlike mean-field VB.  
- Specializes to clustering by casting K-means as rank-r factorization with one-hot V, producing an AMP-based K-means with an Onsager-like correction in assignments.  
- Proposition: AMP fixed points with PSD aggregate covariances give block-wise global minima, linking AMP fixed points to ICM/K-means fixed points.  
- Empirical results: improved K-means loss and accuracy over Lloyd’s/K-means++ on synthetic and ORL; AMP-MA outperforms a VB baseline in accuracy and speed on synthetic data.  
- Builds on AMP/GAMP literature (Donoho, Rangan, Bayati & Montanari) and prior bilinear AMP works, clarifying differences (fixed r, non-factorized row priors).  
- Strength: clean, general AMP formulation accommodating non-factorized priors and β→∞ MAP specialization with useful G∞ identity.  
- Strength: clear AMP-Kmeans derivation with intuitive leave-one-out/cluster-size correction; links to VB/ICM by removing Onsager terms.  
- Strength: theoretical optimization interpretation (Proposition 1) strengthens understanding of AMP fixed points.  
- Strength: empirical improvements and linear per-iteration complexity with negligible Onsager overhead.  
- Weakness: Convergence assumed; practical stability issues (oscillations) and only heuristic stopping rule; no damping/stepsize study.  
- Weakness: No state evolution analysis or empirical SE vs. measurement comparisons, losing a key AMP advantage.  
- Weakness: Narrow experiments—single synthetic regime, no tests for rank or prior mis-specification, limited real-data baselines, occasional collapse to single cluster.  
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons incomplete for maximum-accuracy clustering (missing EM GMM, spectral clustering, NMF variants).  
- Weakness: Practical computation of f and G for general priors requires r̂-dimensional integrals; limited guidance beyond simple priors.  
- Weakness: Claim of novelty for enforcing one-hot assignments overstated; matrix-factorization view of K-means is classical—novelty lies in AMP solver.  
- Quality: Technical development is sound and consistent with AMP literature but reduced confidence due to absent SE and limited convergence discussion.  
- Clarity: Generally well organized with clear notation and algorithm; some derivation details deferred and practical computation questions left open.  
- Originality: Meaningful extension of general-rank bilinear AMP to MAP (β→∞) and non-factorized within-row priors; novel AMP-based K-means correction.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful as a practical K-means replacement and flexible low-rank framework, but empirical scope limits demonstrated significance.  
- Suggested improvements: add state evolution (analytic or empirical), damping/adaptive stepsize and convergence diagnostics, and sensitivity experiments (m,N,τ, r̂, priors, noise).  
- Suggested improvements: expand baselines (EM GMM, spectral, NMF), more datasets, report collapse frequency and remedies (balanced priors, annealing), and runtime/ablation studies for Onsager terms.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify numerical strategies for f/G with generic priors, provide code/pseudocode, and discuss τ estimation or EM-style τ updates.  
- Acceptance pros: solid AMP contribution, useful β unification, non-factorized priors, and promising AMP-KM performance.  
- Acceptance cons: missing SE and convergence analysis, limited experimental robustness and baselines, and occasional pathological behavior not fully addressed.
