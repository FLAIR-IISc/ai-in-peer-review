## Review of "Low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering via approximate message passing"

This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending previous work to handle general rank and MAP estimation. A key contribution is applying this algorithm to K-means clustering, reformulating it as a low-rank reconstruction problem with cluster assignment constraints. The authors demonstrate through numerical experiments that their algorithm outperforms Lloyd’s K-means, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing the need for flexible reconstruction methods incorporating matrix structure beyond low-rankness. The use of AMP, building on its success in compressed sensing, is a reasonable approach. The extension to MAP estimation and the application to clustering are novel and potentially valuable. The authors correctly position their work within the existing literature, referencing relevant papers on Bayesian matrix factorization, variational Bayes, and AMP algorithms. 

A strength of the paper lies in the theoretical justification of the algorithm, connecting it to belief propagation and providing a proposition guaranteeing optimality under certain conditions. The numerical experiments are comprehensive, including both artificial and real-world datasets (ORL faces). The results consistently show improvements over standard K-means, and the ablation study highlights the benefits of the proposed approach. The discussion of computational cost and convergence is also helpful.

However, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in certain sections. The derivation of the AMP algorithm, while referenced to supplementary material, is difficult to follow without delving into those details. The explanation of the parameter τ and its estimation could be more transparent. While the authors acknowledge limitations, a more thorough discussion of the algorithm’s sensitivity to parameter settings and potential failure modes would be beneficial. The comparison to other clustering algorithms beyond K-means is also missing; benchmarking against spectral clustering or DBSCAN, for example, would strengthen the claims of significance.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel application of AMP to clustering with explicit cluster constraints.
*   Strong empirical results demonstrating improved performance over K-means.
*   Theoretical justification connecting the algorithm to belief propagation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of clarity in the algorithm derivation.
*   Limited comparison to other clustering algorithms.
*   Insufficient discussion of limitations and sensitivity analysis.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a promising approach to low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. While the presentation could be improved, the novelty of the approach, strong empirical results, and theoretical grounding suggest a worthwhile contribution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the clarity of the derivation, expanding the comparative analysis, and providing a more thorough discussion of limitations.
