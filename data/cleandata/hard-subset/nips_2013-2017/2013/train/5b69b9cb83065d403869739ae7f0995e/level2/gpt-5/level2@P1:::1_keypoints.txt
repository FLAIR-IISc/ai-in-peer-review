- Paper proposes Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction via an AMP algorithm with a β parameter unifying marginal inference (β=1) and MAP (β→∞), and derives a clustering specialization enforcing one-of-K assignments.  
- Derives AMP updates that output approximate marginals, posterior means, or MAP solutions depending on β, and a specialized AMP-KM with debiasing/cluster-size corrections in assignments.  
- Builds on prior AMP/bilinear works; key novelties are (i) extending low-rank AMP to MAP via β→∞, (ii) allowing non-independent intra-row priors, and (iii) hard-assignment clustering with a principled leave-one-out-like correction.  
- Technical strength: clear extension linking to Bethe free energy; Onsager-term removal recovers ICM/VBMF.  
- Practical strength: AMP-KM performs standard centroid updates but corrects assignments for bias and cluster size, offering a simple drop-in alternative to Lloyd’s.  
- Empirical strength: AMP-KM achieves lower K-means objective than Lloyd’s/K-means++ on synthetic data and often on ORL; AMP-MA shows higher clustering accuracy than VBMF-MA.  
- Computational strength: per-iteration cost O(mN) with only r̂×r̂ matrix inversions.  
- Weakness: Experiments are narrowly focused on clustering; paper lacks general low-rank reconstruction benchmarks (e.g., NMSE vs SNR), robustness tests, and comparisons to MCMC/VB on standard factorization datasets.  
- Weakness: Clustering baselines are limited—missing GMM-EM (spherical), spectral clustering, PCA+k-means, GMM, and modern K-means variants/multi-restart strategies.  
- Weakness: Convergence and stability issues of bilinear AMP are not fully addressed—damping, state evolution derivations, and convergence diagnostics are omitted and oscillations are handled ad hoc.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity analyses absent for τ estimation, model-order mismatch (r̂≠r), and initialization; ORL required K-means++ seeding to avoid collapse.  
- Weakness: Novelty relative to contemporaneous bilinear AMP work (BiG-AMP, Krzakala/Parker) is not deeply compared empirically or theoretically.  
- Quality: Derivation is consistent with AMP principles and Proposition 1 gives block-wise optimality, but lack of state evolution and convergence details weakens robustness claims.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written; mapping to clustering is clear. Suggested improvements include moving derivations/state evolution to supplement, clarifying f/G computation costs, and documenting damping strategies.  
- Originality: Moderate—β-based MAP extension and hard-assignment AMP-KM are useful contributions within an active research area.  
- Significance: Clustering contribution is practically appealing; broader impact for general low-rank reconstruction depends on further validation with diverse priors and datasets.  
- Questions requested from authors: provide state evolution and MSE vs SE validation; specify damping/step-size strategy; analyze τ sensitivity; evaluate performance under r̂≠r; compare to GMM-EM and spectral clustering; include experiments with structured priors (sparsity/nonnegativity).  
- Minor comments: confirm scaling in equation (17a), report wall-clock time not just iterations, and relate the assignment correction term to leave-one-out or bias-correction literature.  
- Overall recommendation: Solid and useful AMP formulation with a practical clustering specialization; lean toward acceptance if state evolution, broader experiments, stronger baselines, and convergence details are added.
