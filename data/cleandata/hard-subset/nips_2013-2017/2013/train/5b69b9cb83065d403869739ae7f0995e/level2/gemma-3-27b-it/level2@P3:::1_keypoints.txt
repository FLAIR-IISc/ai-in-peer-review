- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending prior AMP work and applying it to clustering.
- Bayesian formulation and derivation within belief propagation are clearly presented.
- Extends AMP to handle MAP estimation via a β parameter, unifying marginalization and MAP approaches.
- Connects the method to variational Bayes and ICM, and includes a theoretical proposition about optimality.
- Demonstrates empirical improvement over Lloyd’s K-means on high-dimensional data in experiments with synthetic and real datasets.
- Motivation is strong: addresses low-rank reconstruction with structural constraints relevant to signal processing and ML.
- Lack of detailed justification for why AMP should outperform variational Bayes in this context.
- Claim that AMP “reflects dependence of random variables” is vague and needs concrete elaboration.
- Functions f and G are explained tersely; more intuition and simple examples are needed.
- State evolution is mentioned but not discussed; including an overview would strengthen theoretical claims.
- Convergence analysis is weak—paper states convergence is not guaranteed without discussing likely conditions.
- Explanation and justification for the τ parameter and its estimation are insufficient.
- Experimental section lacks detailed parameter settings and sensitivity analysis for algorithms.
- Comparison baselines are limited (mainly K-means and VBMF); broader comparisons to state-of-the-art are needed.
- Justification for dataset choices is limited and should be expanded.
- The phrase “Algorithm 3 is rewritten as” is misleading; it should be clarified as a derivation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel AMP extension with MAP, successful clustering application, and clear Bayesian connections.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical analysis (state evolution/convergence), weak explanation for AMP's advantages, and sparse experimental detail.
- Overall assessment: promising and worthwhile contribution but weakened by theoretical and experimental omissions.
- Recommendation: Conditional acceptance pending revisions to expand theory, clarify functions and τ, add experimental detail and baselines, and rephrase Algorithm 3 statement.
