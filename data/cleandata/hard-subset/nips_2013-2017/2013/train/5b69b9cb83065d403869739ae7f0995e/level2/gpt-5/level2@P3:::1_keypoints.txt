- Proposes Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with structured priors and an AMP algorithm that unifies posterior marginalization and MAP via an inverse-temperature β.
- β=1 approximates posterior marginals/posterior means; β→∞ yields a MAP algorithm with tractable factor-wise updates.
- Provides a fixed-point optimality proposition: at convergence, each block update is a global minimum given the other block fixed.
- Reformulates K-means as low-rank factorization with a discrete one-hot prior on V and derives AMP-KM with Onsager-like corrections for assignments.
- Synthetic and ORL experiments show improvements over Lloyd’s K-means/K-means++ and over a variational Bayes MF baseline in maximum-accuracy clustering.
- Situates work within AMP literature (AMP for CS, GAMP, IterFac, earlier general-rank AMP) and notes contemporaneous bilinear AMP/dictionary learning methods.
- Claims advantage over variational Bayes MF by capturing U–V dependencies via the Bethe approximation and adding Onsager corrections at negligible cost.
- Derivation from belief propagation and β-parameterization is coherent and technically sound; Proposition 1 is meaningful.
- State evolution analysis is deferred; convergence is not guaranteed and oscillations are observed for AMP-KM.
- Empirical evaluation is fair on synthetic data but limited in scope; ORL results show improvements yet indicate sensitivity (two runs collapsed).
- Use of true priors in synthetic experiments may bias results toward Bayesian methods; robustness to prior mismatch is unexplored.
- Reproducibility: key update equations and stopping criteria provided, but proofs/state evolution in supplement and practical guidance (damping/initialization/τ) is brief.
- Presentation is generally clear with consistent notation and helpful special-case derivations; K-means interpretation and correction-term explanations are good.
- Some implementation details (damping, β-scheduling, computational cost of f/G for general priors) need expansion.
- Novel contributions: unified β-AMP handling both marginalization and MAP; AMP-based K-means enforcing exact one-hot assignments with principled Onsager corrections.
- Potential significance: could influence scalable Bayesian matrix factorization and offer practical K-means improvements in high dimensions, but empirical scope is limited.
- Strengths: clear general AMP derivation, principled Onsager corrections, fixed-point optimality linking to ICM, and competitive empirical performance with low per-iteration cost.
- Weaknesses: convergence/oscillation issues not characterized or systematically handled; narrow empirical evaluation; omission of strong baselines (GMM-EM, BiG-AMP); heuristic τ estimation without sensitivity analysis.
- Essential suggested improvements: compare to BiG-AMP and GMM-EM; provide damping/stepsize/initialization guidance and convergence diagnostics; perform robustness studies (prior mismatch, rank misspecification, τ sensitivity).
- Nice-to-have suggestions: summarize state evolution and its empirical fidelity; test additional datasets and observation models (e.g., missing data); clarify computational cost for general priors and provide implementation tips or code.
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptually clean AMP extension unifying marginalization and MAP, novel practical AMP-K-means with empirical gains, and a clear relation to variational Bayes/ICM with a fixed-point result.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical breadth and missing key baselines, unresolved convergence/oscillation concerns, and reliance on true priors that may overstate practical performance.
