- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to clustering by reformulating clustering as constrained low-rank reconstruction.  
- Extends existing AMP techniques to matrix factorization and handles both marginalization and MAP estimation within a unified framework.  
- Formulates the problem in a Bayesian framework allowing incorporation of structural priors like sparsity and non-negativity.  
- Provides a clear theoretical foundation including a convergence proof for Proposition 1.  
- Derivation covers the general-rank case and extends prior AMP work to jointly handle marginalization and MAP problems.  
- Numerical experiments on synthetic and real data show consistent performance improvements over baselines, particularly in high dimensions.  
- Benchmarks include comparisons to Lloyd’s K-means and variational Bayes matrix factorization, which are reasonable baselines.  
- Presentation is generally clear with well-motivated problem formulation and detailed algorithm descriptions, though reliance on supplementary material may hinder readability.  
- Practical implementation details need more discussion, notably the selection of the clustering parameter τ.  
- Originality is moderate: the AMP-based unified handling is novel, but connections between clustering and low-rank reconstruction are known.  
- Paper should better distinguish itself from related methods by providing more detailed comparisons of algorithmic structure and theoretical guarantees.  
- Empirical significance is strong: better clustering accuracy, faster convergence, and improved robustness to initialization compared to traditional methods.  
- Suggested improvements: include statistical significance tests, discuss limitations (when low-rank or structural assumptions fail), and provide Big-O computational complexity.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with solid theoretical and empirical results, somewhat incremental novelty, and suitable for publication with suggested clarifications.
