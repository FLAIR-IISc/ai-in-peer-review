- Proposes a new AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction with A = UV' + Gaussian noise.  
- Framework allows flexible structural priors on factor matrices U and V.  
- Derives a general AMP algorithm that unifies marginal posterior estimation and MAP estimation via an inverse-temperature β.  
- Extends prior AMP matrix-factorization work and defines functions f and G precisely for inference.  
- Applies framework to K-means by constraining V to one-hot rows, deriving a novel AMP-KM clustering algorithm.  
- AMP-KM is a principled modification of Lloyd’s algorithm, adding correction terms that mitigate self-bias and favor smaller clusters.  
- Provides Proposition 1 showing algorithm fixed points are also ICM fixed points, suggesting improved avoidance of poor local minima.  
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic data and ORL faces shows AMP-KM/AMP-MA outperform K-means, K-means++, and VBMF-MA in loss and accuracy.  
- Algorithm is computationally efficient (O(mN) per iteration), making it practical for large-scale problems.  
- Paper is technically solid, well-written, and presents clear intuition for algorithmic improvements.  
- Work is highly original: general-purpose AMP for varied priors and the novel derivation of a clustering algorithm from first principles.  
- Significant contribution with broad applicability to matrix factorization and clustering practitioners.  
- Main theoretical limitation: formal convergence/state-evolution analysis is omitted from the main paper.  
- Practical weakness: handling and updating of noise parameter τ in K-means is heuristic and sensitivity is not thoroughly discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh minor weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
