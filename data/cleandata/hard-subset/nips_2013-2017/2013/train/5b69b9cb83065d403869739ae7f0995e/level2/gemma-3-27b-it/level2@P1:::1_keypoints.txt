- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction and applies it to K-means clustering, claiming improvements over Lloyd’s algorithm in high dimensions.  
- Leverages Bayesian formulation to incorporate priors (sparsity, non-negativity) and extends AMP to MAP estimation for general rank.  
- Novelty: extension of AMP to general rank and single-assignment clustering constraint is a tangible advance.  
- Theoretical grounding: connects to belief propagation and message-passing theory, providing justification for the algorithm.  
- Performance: numerical experiments (synthetic and real data) show superior performance to Lloyd’s K-means in high-dimensional settings.  
- Clarity in parts: mathematical formulation and motivation for AMP over variational Bayes are generally well-presented.  
- Main clarity issue: lacks intuitive explanation of why AMP outperforms other methods and underdeveloped discussion of state evolution.  
- Implementation details are insufficient, e.g., how functions f and G are approximated for non-Gaussian priors, hindering reproducibility.  
- Parameter sensitivity: performance depends on parameters like τ, but practical tuning guidance is limited.  
- Baseline comparison is incomplete; needs evaluation against other clustering methods (spectral clustering, DBSCAN, etc.).  
- Related work: insufficient discussion of recent AMP-based matrix reconstruction advances and unclear statements about fixing r vs r/m.  
- Presentation issue: repetitive notation and repeated equations reduce readability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: algorithm is novel, theoretically sound, and shows promising empirical results, with a creative clustering application.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing implementation details, limited explanation of advantages over alternatives, incomplete comparisons, and presentation clarity concerns.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requiring detailed implementation notes (f and G approximations), explanation of theoretical advantages (state evolution), broader algorithm comparisons, practical parameter-tuning guidance, and streamlined presentation.
