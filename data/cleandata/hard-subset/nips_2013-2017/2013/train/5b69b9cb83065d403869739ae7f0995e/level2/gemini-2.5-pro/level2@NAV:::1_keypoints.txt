- Novel AMP-based algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction, extended to general rank and MAP estimation.  
- Main contribution: cast K-means as low-rank reconstruction with a one-hot constraint, yielding the AMP-KM algorithm.  
- Principled derivation that directly handles discrete assignment constraints within the AMP framework.  
- Update rules (Eq. 18b) include an intuitive bias-correction that helps escape poor local minima of Lloyd’s algorithm.  
- Strong empirical evidence: outperforms K-means++ and Variational Bayes MF on synthetic and ORL face data (lower K-means loss, higher accuracy).  
- Theoretical support: AMP fixed points are also ICM fixed points, justifying potential for better solutions.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the link between matrix reconstruction and clustering.  
- Weakness: assumes convergence of AMP without a formal convergence guarantee.  
- Weakness: omits state evolution analysis (mentioned but left out), leaving theoretical grounding partially incomplete.  
- Weakness: noise parameter τ is introduced and estimated heuristically; lacks principled analysis or selection guidance.  
- Argument for acceptance: presents a novel, insightful connection between AMP and K-means.  
- Argument for acceptance: demonstrates superior performance over strong baselines on multiple datasets.  
- Argument for acceptance: opens new avenues for deriving clustering algorithms from first principles.  
- Argument for rejection: lacks formal convergence analysis.
