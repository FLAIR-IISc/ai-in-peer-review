This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework, extending previous work on AMP. The authors also demonstrate an application of this algorithm to the K-means clustering problem, claiming improvements over the standard Lloyd's algorithm, especially in high-dimensional settings.

The core idea of leveraging AMP for low-rank matrix reconstruction is well-motivated, building on its success in compressed sensing and sparse recovery. The paper clearly articulates the Bayesian formulation and the flexibility it offers in incorporating prior knowledge about matrix factors (sparsity, non-negativity, etc.). The extension of existing AMP algorithms to handle MAP estimation is a valuable contribution. Furthermore, framing K-means clustering as a low-rank reconstruction problem with specific constraints is a reasonable approach.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Extending AMP to the general rank case and enabling MAP estimation represents a tangible advance over [9]. The application to clustering, specifically addressing the single assignment constraint, is also a novel contribution.
*   **Theoretical grounding:** The paper draws connections to belief propagation and provides a theoretical justification for the algorithm's efficacy, referencing relevant works in message passing theory.
*   **Performance:** The numerical experiments demonstrate the superior performance of the proposed algorithm compared to Lloyd’s K-means, particularly in high dimensions and with appropriate parameter settings. The experiments with both artificial and real datasets enhance confidence in the results.
*   **Clarity (in parts):** The mathematical formulation is generally clear, and the algorithm is presented in a structured manner. The motivation for choosing AMP over variational Bayes is also adequately explained.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity (overall):** While the mathematical formulation is generally clear, the paper suffers from a lack of intuitive explanation of *why* the proposed AMP algorithm outperforms existing methods, especially the variational Bayes approach. The connection to the state evolution mentioned in the introduction is not elaborated.
*   **Implementation Details:** The paper lacks specific details regarding the implementation of the algorithms, such as how the functions *f* and *G* are approximated in practice, especially for non-Gaussian priors. This makes reproducibility challenging.
*   **Parameter sensitivity:** The performance appears sensitive to parameters like *τ*, and the practical guidelines for selecting these parameters are limited. More discussion on parameter tuning is needed.
*   **Comparison to baselines:** While comparisons to Lloyd’s K-means are presented, a more comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art clustering algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering, DBSCAN) would strengthen the claims of superiority.
*   **Limited Discussion of Related Work:** While related work is cited, the connection to more recent advancements in AMP-based algorithms for matrix reconstruction [13, 14] could be further explored, specifically addressing how the proposed approach differs in a more detailed manner than provided. The statement about fixing 'r' versus 'r/m' needs more explanation.
* **Repetitive notation:** Several equations are repeated with minor differences. This makes the paper harder to follow.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically grounded algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering.
*   The numerical results demonstrate clear improvements over existing methods.
*   The application to K-means clustering is a creative and valuable contribution.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The paper lacks sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.
*   The explanation of the algorithm's advantages over existing methods is limited.
*   The comparison to other relevant algorithms is incomplete.
*   The clarity of the presentation could be improved.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field. The proposed algorithm is novel, theoretically sound, and demonstrates promising performance. However, the lack of implementation details and the limited discussion of the algorithm's advantages and limitations prevent me from giving it a strong recommendation for acceptance in its current form. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**, contingent on the authors addressing the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Provide more details regarding the implementation of the algorithm, including how *f* and *G* are approximated for different priors.
2.  Elaborate on the theoretical advantages of the proposed AMP algorithm over variational Bayes methods, potentially discussing the state evolution.
3.  Include a more comprehensive comparison to other relevant algorithms, including state-of-the-art clustering methods.
4.  Provide more practical guidelines for parameter tuning.
5.  Streamline the presentation and reduce redundancy in the equations.
