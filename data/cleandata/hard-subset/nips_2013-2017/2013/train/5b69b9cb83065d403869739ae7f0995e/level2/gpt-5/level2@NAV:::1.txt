Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that flexibly incorporates structural priors on factors and unifies marginalization and MAP estimation via a temperature parameter β, recovering MAP as β→∞. The authors further instantiate the framework for clustering by enforcing one-hot assignments, yielding an AMP variant for K-means and a “maximum accuracy” variant via approximate marginal MAP. This builds on AMP/GAMP for linear estimation (Donoho et al., Rangan), IterFac for rank-one (Rangan & Fletcher), and prior low-rank AMP (Matsushita & Tanaka), but claims novelty in handling MAP objectives, fixed-rank asymptotics, relaxing independence assumptions within rows, and explicitly enforcing one-hot constraints in a low-rank AMP formulation. The approach is contrasted with variational Bayes matrix factorization and ICM/Lloyd’s K-means.

Quality: The algorithm is clearly stated with Onsager-like corrections and a useful optimality Proposition ensuring block-wise global minima at fixed points. However, convergence is assumed; state evolution and proofs are deferred and absent from the main paper. The τ estimation heuristic and handling of oscillations (ad-hoc stopping) suggest stability issues; damping/line-search are not discussed. Experiments (synthetic Gaussian mixtures; ORL faces) show improved K-means loss and accuracy over Lloyd/K-means++ and VBMF, but use true priors for maximum-accuracy, and lack statistical significance tests and stronger baselines (e.g., EM for GMM, spherical K-means, spectral clustering).

Clarity: The exposition is dense yet mostly precise. It would benefit from concise pseudocode, explicit guidance for f/G under common priors (discrete, sparse, nonnegative), memory/runtime reporting, and a clearer description of β scheduling and τ estimation.

Originality: The β-tempered AMP that yields MAP, and the AMP formulation of K-means with an explicit one-hot constraint and bias-corrected assignment step, appear novel relative to prior bilinear AMP and VBMF.

Significance: If stabilized, the method could impact scalable matrix factorization and clustering by improving over K-means-style objectives with minimal overhead. Broader impact depends on robustness to model mismatch and stronger empirical validation.

Constructive suggestions:
- Provide state evolution and convergence analysis (even abbreviated), and include damping or adaptive step-size recipes.
- Automate τ and rank selection (EM/BIC) and test rank mismatch.
- Compare against EM for GMMs, spectral clustering, spherical K-means, and NMF-based clustering; report wall-clock time and memory.
- Evaluate robustness to prior mismatch, non-Gaussian noise, and missing data; release code.

Pros for acceptance:
- Conceptually neat AMP–MAP unification and practical clustering instantiation.
- Clear algorithm with O(mN) per iteration and Onsager corrections.
- Strong empirical improvements on synthetic and ORL (mostly).

Cons against acceptance:
- Missing theoretical guarantees and stability guidance in main text.
- Limited baselines and reliance on true priors; occasional degenerate solutions.
- Incomplete analysis of hyperparameters, initialization sensitivity, and rank mismatch.
