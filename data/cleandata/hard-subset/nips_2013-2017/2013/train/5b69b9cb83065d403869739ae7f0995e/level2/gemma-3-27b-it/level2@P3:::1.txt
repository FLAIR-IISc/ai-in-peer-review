This paper proposes an approximate message passing (AMP) algorithm for low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework, extending prior work on AMP for this problem and applying it to clustering. The work aims to provide a flexible reconstruction method capable of incorporating structural properties of the underlying factors and demonstrates improved performance over Lloyd’s K-means algorithm for high-dimensional data.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a common problem in signal processing and machine learning – reconstructing low-rank matrices with additional structural constraints. The formulation within a Bayesian framework is clearly presented, and the derivation of the AMP algorithm is positioned within the context of belief propagation. The extension to handle MAP estimation is a positive contribution. The application to clustering, framing it as a constrained low-rank reconstruction problem, is a reasonable and potentially beneficial approach. The numerical experiments provide evidence supporting the algorithm’s efficacy, particularly in demonstrating superior performance compared to K-means in certain scenarios.

A strength of the work is the attempt to unify marginalization and MAP estimation within a single algorithmic framework via the β parameter. The connection to existing techniques like variational Bayes and ICM is also well-articulated. The inclusion of a theoretical proposition regarding optimality is a positive aspect. The experimental setup, using both artificial and real datasets, adds weight to the empirical findings.

However, the paper has several weaknesses. While the motivation for using AMP is clear, a more detailed explanation of *why* AMP is expected to outperform variational Bayes in this specific context would be beneficial. The claim that AMP can "reflect dependence of random variables" is vague; elaborating on how this manifests in the algorithm and its performance would strengthen the argument. The explanation of the functions *f* and *G* is rather terse; providing more intuition about their role, and potentially illustrating them with simple examples, would aid understanding.

The discussion of the state evolution is notably absent, despite being mentioned. Including a brief overview of the state evolution results would significantly enhance the theoretical understanding of the algorithm’s performance guarantees. The convergence analysis is limited to stating convergence is not guaranteed; some discussion of conditions under which convergence might be expected would be valuable.  The explanation regarding the parameter τ, and its estimation, feels somewhat tacked on and could be more thoroughly justified.

The experimental section is relatively light on detail. More details about the parameter settings used for each algorithm, and a discussion of the sensitivity of the results to those parameters, are necessary. A more comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art low-rank matrix reconstruction algorithms beyond K-means and VBMF would also be helpful. The justification for the choice of datasets is limited. Furthermore, the statement that “Algorithm 3 is rewritten as” feels somewhat misleading. It’s a derivation, not a simple rewrite.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of AMP algorithm to low-rank matrix reconstruction with MAP estimation.
*   Successful application of the algorithm to clustering, outperforming K-means in some scenarios.
*   Clear Bayesian formulation and connection to existing techniques.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of detailed theoretical analysis, particularly regarding state evolution and convergence.
*   Limited discussion of why AMP outperforms other methods.
*   Insufficient detail in the experimental setup and results.
*   Some explanations are vague or lack sufficient intuition.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of low-rank matrix reconstruction and clustering. The proposed AMP algorithm shows promise, and the empirical results are encouraging. However, the lack of thorough theoretical analysis and the limited detail in the experimental section detract from the overall quality of the work. 

**Recommendation:**

Conditional Acceptance. The paper should be accepted contingent upon addressing the following revisions:

1.  **Expand the discussion of the theoretical advantages of AMP over variational Bayes.** Provide a more concrete explanation of how AMP captures dependencies in the data.
2.  **Include a brief overview of the state evolution results.** Even a qualitative description would be beneficial.
3.  **Provide more detail in the experimental section.** Include parameter settings, sensitivity analysis, and a comparison to additional baselines.
4.  **Clarify the explanation of functions *f* and *G*, and provide more intuition.**
5.  **Rephrase the statement regarding Algorithm 3, clarifying it as a derivation.**
6. **Provide a more thorough justification for the parameter τ estimation method.**



These revisions would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its contribution to the field.
