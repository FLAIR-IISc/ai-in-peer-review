- Proposes an AMP-based algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that incorporates structural priors and a temperature β to unify marginalization and MAP (MAP recovered as β→∞).  
- Instantiates the framework for clustering by enforcing one-hot assignments, yielding an AMP variant for K-means and a “maximum accuracy” approximate marginal MAP variant.  
- Builds on prior AMP/GAMP, IterFac, and low-rank AMP work but claims novelty in handling MAP objectives, fixed-rank asymptotics, relaxing within-row independence, and explicit one-hot constraints.  
- Algorithm is clearly stated with Onsager-like corrections and an optimality proposition guaranteeing block-wise global minima at fixed points.  
- Convergence is assumed rather than proven; state evolution and proofs are deferred and absent from the main text.  
- τ estimation is heuristic and oscillation handling is ad-hoc (stopping); no discussion of damping or adaptive step-size for stability.  
- Experiments (synthetic Gaussian mixtures, ORL faces) show improved K-means loss and accuracy over Lloyd/K-means++ and VBMF.  
- Maximum-accuracy experiments use true priors, reducing realism of comparisons.  
- Lacks statistical significance testing and omits stronger baselines such as EM for GMMs, spherical K-means, spectral clustering, and NMF-based clustering.  
- Exposition is dense but mostly precise; would benefit from concise pseudocode and clearer practical guidance.  
- Needs explicit guidance for choosing f/G under common priors (discrete, sparse, nonnegative), and clearer descriptions of β scheduling and τ estimation.  
- Missing memory/runtime (wall-clock) and complexity reporting for practical evaluation.  
- The β-tempered AMP and the bias-corrected AMP formulation of K-means with one-hot constraints appear novel.  
- If stabilized and theoretically justified, the method could impact scalable matrix factorization and clustering with minimal overhead.  
- Broader impact and applicability hinge on robustness to model mismatch and stronger empirical validation.  
- Suggested additions: state evolution and convergence analysis (even brief), damping/adaptive step-size recipes, and automated τ/rank selection (EM/BIC).  
- Suggested empirical work: compare vs EM, spectral clustering, spherical K-means, NMF-based clustering; test rank mismatch and prior mismatch; evaluate non-Gaussian noise and missing data.  
- Recommend reporting wall-clock time and memory, stress-testing initialization sensitivity, and releasing code.  
- Pros: conceptually neat AMP–MAP unification, clear O(mN) per-iteration algorithm with Onsager corrections, and promising empirical gains.  
- Cons: missing theoretical guarantees and stability guidance, limited baselines and reliance on true priors, occasional degenerate solutions, and incomplete hyperparameter/initialization analysis.
