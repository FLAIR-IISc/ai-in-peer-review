- Novel application of AMP to K-means: reformulates clustering as low-rank matrix reconstruction with a discrete prior, yielding a principled modification of Lloyd’s algorithm (Algorithm 3 / Eq.18b).  
- Strong empirical performance: AMP-based algorithms (AMP-KM, AMP-MA) outperform K-means, K-means++, and VBMF on synthetic and ORL face data in loss and accuracy with comparable iterations.  
- Unified, flexible framework: General AMP (Algorithm 2) handles arbitrary separable priors and unifies posterior mean (β=1) and MAP (β→∞) estimation.  
- Clear relation to existing methods: Positions AMP relative to VB and ICM; Proposition 1 shows MAP fixed points of AMP are fixed points of ICM, suggesting AMP can avoid some poor local minima.  
- Missing theoretical analysis: State evolution and sharp performance predictions are referenced but not presented, weakening theoretical contribution.  
- Unproven convergence: Algorithm convergence is assumed, oscillations observed required heuristic stopping rules, so no formal convergence guarantee is provided.  
- Derivation clarity issues: Core AMP derivation is entirely in supplementary material; a brief sketch of key approximation steps is missing from the main text.  
- Heuristic parameter estimation: Noise variance τ (not present in original K-means) is estimated ad hoc via residuals; its role and sensitivity are not thoroughly justified.  
- Overall assessment: Well-written paper with a solid, novel contribution—AMP for K-means—and strong empirical support despite omitted theoretical details.  
- Argument for acceptance: Proposes a principled, effective K-means algorithm with strong empirical gains and insightful connection between AMP and Lloyd’s method.  
- Argument for acceptance: General matrix-reconstruction framework is flexible and broadly applicable.  
- Argument for rejection: Lacks the key theoretical AMP analysis (state evolution) in the paper.  
- Argument for rejection: No formal convergence proof; relies on heuristics to handle oscillations.
