- Proposes an AMP extension for MAP low-rank matrix reconstruction in a Bayesian framework and applies it to K-means clustering.  
- Novelty: enables MAP estimation with AMP and reframes K-means as a constrained low-rank reconstruction.  
- Theoretical foundation: grounded in Bayesian AMP literature and references state-evolution results.  
- Empirical validation: outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and is compared to VBMF-MA and K-means++, especially in high dimensions.  
- Presentation: paper is well-structured and generally clearly written; some equations could use more explanation.  
- Weakness: definitions of functions f and G (eqs.10–11) are abstract; needs concrete examples for common priors (e.g., Gaussian).  
- Weakness: introduction of scaling factor m in noise variance τ is not fully justified or explained.  
- Weakness: convergence of Algorithm 2 is asserted but not rigorously proven; discussion of convergence conditions is lacking.  
- Weakness: lacks parameter sensitivity analysis, particularly robustness to mis-estimated noise variance τ.  
- Weakness: compares mainly to K-means baselines; should include more modern clustering methods (e.g., spectral clustering, DBSCAN).  
- Weakness: limited theoretical explanation for why the AMP-based approach yields better clustering; deeper analysis needed.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded algorithm with compelling empirical improvements.  
- Argument for rejection: reproducibility and adoption hindered by unclear function definitions, scaling justification, and convergence analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify f/G, justify noise-scaling, analyze parameter sensitivity, and expand clustering comparisons.
