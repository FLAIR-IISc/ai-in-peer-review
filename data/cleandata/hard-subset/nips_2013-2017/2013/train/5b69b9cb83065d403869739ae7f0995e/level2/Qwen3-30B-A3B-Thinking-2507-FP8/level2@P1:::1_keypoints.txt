- Proposal: Introduces an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extending AMP to matrix factorization with structural priors and unifying marginalization and MAP via parameter β.  
- Key innovation: Adapts AMP to the matrix setting while preserving flexibility for diverse structural priors and reformulates K-means as constrained low-rank reconstruction (AMP-KM).  
- Technical quality & novelty: Clear theoretical contribution bridging a gap in prior work, using Bethe approximation and providing Proposition 1 convergence guarantees linking AMP and ICM fixed points.  
- Clarity & presentation: Well-structured paper with precise algorithm descriptions, clear motivation for bias correction, logical clustering connection, and thorough numerical experiments.  
- Significance: AMP-KM substantially outperforms Lloyd’s K-means and K-means++ on synthetic and real datasets, yielding lower K-means loss and higher accuracy with comparable iterations.  
- Empirical rigor: Strong validation via large-scale synthetic experiments (500 instances, r=2–15), real-data trials (ORL faces, 50 trials), and insightful convergence-speed comparisons.  
- Weakness — convergence analysis: Lacks practical discussion of convergence behavior (typical iteration counts across r and noise levels); include average-iteration tables.  
- Weakness — clustering assumptions: Evaluation assumes Gaussian clusters with uniform priors; should test non-Gaussian or overlapping clusters to assess robustness.  
- Weakness — computational details: States O(mN) cost but omits how r̂-dimensional integrals in f(·,·; p̂) scale with r̂ and potential numerical integration costs.  
- Weakness — comparison depth: Comparisons to related work ([13,14]) are brief; should contrast rank-scaling assumptions (fixed r vs. r/m) to better contextualize novelty.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, technically sound, and practically useful contribution with minor, easily addressable weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
