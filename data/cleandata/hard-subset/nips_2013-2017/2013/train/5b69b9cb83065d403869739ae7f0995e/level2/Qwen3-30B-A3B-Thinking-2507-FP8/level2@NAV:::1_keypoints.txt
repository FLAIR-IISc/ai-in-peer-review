- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction that handles structural properties (sparsity, non-negativity) of factor matrices.
- Introduces a unified framework using parameter β to handle both marginalization and MAP problems.
- Applies the framework to K-means by reformulating clustering as a constrained low-rank matrix reconstruction problem.
- Claims the AMP algorithm is a flexible, efficient approach for Bayesian low-rank reconstruction.
- Claims the AMP-based clustering outperforms existing methods on artificial and real-world data.
- Provides strong theoretical analysis, including a proposition proving optimality of AMP fixed points.
- Experiments are well-designed, comparing against Lloyd's K-means, K-means++, and variational Bayes on artificial and ORL face datasets.
- Results show consistently lower K-means loss, higher accuracy, and faster convergence than competitors (notably faster than variational Bayes).
- Original contribution: extends AMP to structural constraints and enforces one-cluster-per-data-point in clustering, which prior work did not address.
- Strengths: elegant β unification, low computational cost O(mN) per iteration, theoretical guarantees, and convincing real-world demonstrations.
- Weaknesses: scalability to extremely large matrices (m, N > 10^6) not discussed; clustering parameter τ chosen heuristically with no sensitivity analysis; limited explanation for why AMP outperforms variational Bayes.
- Paper is well-written, organized, reproducible, and considered a significant contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
