- Proposes an AMP algorithm for Bayesian low-rank matrix reconstruction, extended to clustering via a structural constraint.  
- Novel formulation of clustering as a constrained low-rank reconstruction, yielding a new clustering algorithm.  
- Well-motivated: leverages Bayesian flexibility and AMP computational efficiency; logical extension of prior work.  
- Mathematical derivation is sound and includes Proposition 1 guaranteeing optimality under certain conditions.  
- Strong theoretical development and clear presentation of the algorithm.  
- Synthetic experiments show improved performance over Lloyd’s K-means and some baselines.  
- Empirical validation is limited: lacks comparisons with recent state-of-the-art clustering methods.  
- No sensitivity analysis to the noise parameter τ.  
- ORL face dataset results show improvement but lack detailed quantitative metrics beyond K-means loss and accuracy.  
- Limited discussion of computational complexity and scalability to very high-dimensional datasets.  
- Only a brief comparative analysis with related AMP work (dictionary learning/matrix reconstruction); needs deeper discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty in applying AMP to structured low-rank reconstruction, sound theory, and demonstrated empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical breadth, missing scalability/complexity analysis, and cursory related-work comparison.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on expanded experiments and more detailed discussion of scalability and related work.
