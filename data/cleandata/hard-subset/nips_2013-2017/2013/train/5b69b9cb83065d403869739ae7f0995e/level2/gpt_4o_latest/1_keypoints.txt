- Contributions: Novel AMP algorithm extending AMP to MAP estimation and a clustering formulation that embeds structural constraints into low-rank matrix reconstruction.
- Technical novelty: Extends AMP beyond posterior mean estimation and integrates structural constraints for clustering.
- Performance: Outperforms Lloyd's K-means and K-means++ on K-means loss and accuracy, especially in high dimensions; strong results on ORL faces.
- Efficiency: Linear computational complexity in input size and faster convergence than variational Bayes.
- Theoretical foundations: Derivation from belief propagation, links to the Bethe approximation, and discussion of state evolution and fixed points.
- Experiments: Comprehensive tests on synthetic and real data with multiple metrics (normalized loss, accuracy, iterations).
- Clarity issue: Dense mathematical exposition may limit accessibility for readers not familiar with AMP or Bayesian inference.
- Comparative analysis gap: Limited comparisons to other state-of-the-art high-dimensional clustering methods.
- Convergence concerns: Convergence of the AMP algorithm is assumed but not guaranteed; more analysis or empirical study needed.
- Real-world robustness: Only ORL faces used; additional diverse real-world datasets would better demonstrate generalizability.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand comparative and convergence analyses.
- Pros summary: Novel MAP-AMP, strong empirical performance, scalability, and rigorous theory.
- Cons summary: Dense exposition, limited baselines, incomplete convergence guarantees, and narrow real-world evaluation.
