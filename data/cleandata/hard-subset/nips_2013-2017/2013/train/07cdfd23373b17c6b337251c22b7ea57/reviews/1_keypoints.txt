- Paper proposes PTM to reduce MMTM’s O(K^3) parameters to O(K) for faster inference and presents stochastic variational inference plus scalability tricks.  
- Unclear practical advantage: need to show how learning MMTM with large K helps, since mixed-membership models may tolerate small K.  
- Missing experiments: should compare PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K to demonstrate benefit of large-K PTM.  
- Model selection unclear: paper does not explain how K was chosen for real datasets, risking biased comparisons with competitors.  
- Simplifying assumptions under-analyzed: authors should discuss scenarios where PTM fails (e.g., true B having O(K^3) distinct values).  
- Synthetic experiments incomplete: true B choices not described and should include cases both favorable and unfavorable to PTM.  
- Approximation tricks insufficiently studied: impact of threshold δ on accuracy vs. scalability is not analyzed.  
- Questioning claims about downsampling: why PTM avoids accuracy loss that harms a-MMSB, and why ignoring non-edges is justified here but not in MMSB.  
- Local-update approximation concerns: impact of O(K) approximation and different sampling sizes (2K, 4K, 100K) on quality, especially as K grows, is unexplored.  
- Experimental fairness doubted: reviewer not convinced competitors were evaluated fairly given missing analyses and selections.  
- Writing quality is good: paper is clear and well-organized.  
- Originality moderate: modeling is incremental, but constraining parameters for scalability is an interesting direction.  
- Significance conditional: method could impact practice if simplifying assumptions hold, since scalability is rare for latent-space network models.  
- Title critique: title is too broad relative to the paper’s specific contribution (scalable PTM).  
- Probabilistic validity questioned: bag-of-triples assumption may not correspond to any real network, so PTM/M M T M might lack a proper generative likelihood and place mass on “non-networks.”  
- Overall concern: side-effects of modeling and inference decisions are poorly investigated.
