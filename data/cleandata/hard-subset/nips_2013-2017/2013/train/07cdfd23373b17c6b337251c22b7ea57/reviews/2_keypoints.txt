- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle-count statistics as a bag-of-words analog for graphs.  
- Main modeling advance: parameterize triangle configurations to identify K roles, reducing parameters and improving scalability.  
- Applies stochastic variational inference, deriving global and local update rules for the model.  
- Novel contribution is a straightforward combination of aMMSB with MMTM, though it requires tedious bookkeeping and could be better presented.  
- Provides an intuitive and intriguing approximation for the local update rule despite most of the algorithm being standard SVI.  
- Lacks discussion of how the method would extend to directed networks.  
- Questionable claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs”; ERGMs use more varied statistics and the analogy is unclear.  
- Unclear whether the inferred latent space is interpretable and what it reveals about graph similarities/differences.  
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.  
- Unclear why subsampling triangles per node (using delta) yields an adequate approximation.  
- If the goal is recovering a “set of non-zero roles,” the model might need a more binary notion of role membership.  
- Robustness of latent-space recovery to the chosen threshold (0.125) is not evaluated and seems arbitrary.  
- Experiments do not specify which latent-space model generates power-law networks; many models can do so.  
- Confusion over complexity: PTM is claimed to have O(K) parameters but PTM CGS is reported as K^3 complexity.  
- Concern about method performance with fewer observed edges; experiments only hold out 10% of edges.  
- Counterintuitive result: the method outperforms MMSB on networks synthetically generated by MMSB (Figure 1).  
- Overall: a mixed-membership blockmodel on triangle counts that enables a more scalable O(NK) algorithm, but with several methodological and presentation concerns.
