- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and stochastic natural gradient inference to enable scalable latent-space inference for large networks.  
- Reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing and uses a bag-of-triangles representation.  
- Achieves O(NK) computational complexity and demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups on synthetic and real networks (including a 1M-node dataset).  
- Correctly situates work relative to MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM, and prior triangular representations and variational methods.  
- Explicitly compares to recent scalable a-MMSB and shows empirical advantages.  
- Main strength: clear and substantial scalability improvements unlocking analysis of million-node networks.  
- Strength: parsimonious parameterization that maintains accuracy while drastically reducing model size.  
- Strength: efficient stochastic variational inference with mini-batching and adaptive step sizes.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation covering recovery accuracy, held-out link prediction, and real-world scalability tests.  
- Paper is well-written with standard, well-defined notation and clear presentation.  
- Weakness: justification for the chosen structured mean-field approximation is insufficiently detailed.  
- Weakness: δ-subsampling parameter (δ=50) appears arbitrary and lacks sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: method shows sensitivity to initialization; seeding with ground truth is an unrealistic assumption.  
- Weakness: PTM is not a full generative model of networks, and implications for applications need clearer discussion.  
- Pro arguments: significant scalability gains, novel parsimonious modeling, solid empirical validation, and clear writing.  
- Con arguments: limited rationale for structured mean-field, arbitrary δ choice, initialization reliance, and under-emphasized generative limitation.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with substantial contributions to scalability; recommends acceptance (Strong Accept, 8/10).  
- Suggested improvements: expand structured mean-field discussion, perform δ sensitivity analysis, explore robust initializations, detail generative-model implications, and report computational resources for reproducibility.
