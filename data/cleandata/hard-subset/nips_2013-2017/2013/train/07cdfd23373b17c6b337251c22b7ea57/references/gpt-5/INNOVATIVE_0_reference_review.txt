Summary
The paper tackles scalable probabilistic latent space inference for large networks by combining: (i) a succinct bag-of-triangles representation that captures higher-order structure; (ii) a parsimonious probabilistic model—the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM)—that reduces the triangle-generating parameterization from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) via parameter sharing over role-triplet equivalence classes; and (iii) a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with structured mean-field and a novel “mixture-of-deltas” local approximation that reduces per-triangle updates from O(K^3) to O(K). The method claims near-linear scaling and demonstrates inference of 100 roles on a 1M-node YouTube network in ~4 hours on a single 8-thread machine, with competitive or improved accuracy on synthetic latent-role recovery and link prediction versus MMSB and MMTM baselines.

Relation to prior work
- MMSB/a-MMSB: Edge-based latent-space models; a-MMSB reduces parameters (O(K^2)→O(K)) and uses SVI, but still depends on an O(N^2) adjacency representation. The current work adopts parameter sharing akin to a-MMSB but for triadic motifs.
- MMTM: Introduced the bag-of-triangles representation with a K^3 tensor and blocked Gibbs; good fidelity but scales poorly in K. This paper’s PTM trades expressivity for O(K) parameters and enables SVI.
- ERGM/motifs: Aligns with the established importance of triads for capturing transitivity/closure.
- SVI/natural gradients/structured variational: Methodologically standard, but applied in a novel, motif-centric setting with a specialized local approximation.

Strengths
- Novelty and conceptual boldness: The O(K) parameterization of a triadic latent role model is a substantial simplification that unlocks scalability in K without abandoning higher-order structure. The mixture-of-deltas structured variational family is a clever and pragmatic way to keep local updates O(K).
- Scalability: The combination of motif subsampling (δ), stochastic natural gradients, and discarding local variational parameters between iterations is thoughtfully engineered. The reported 1M-node, 100-role run on a single machine is compelling.
- Empirical results: Consistent improvements or parity in latent role recovery and link prediction on synthetic/real-world data, with large speedups versus MMSB variational and MMTM/PTM Gibbs. Rapid convergence (2–5 passes) is attractive.
- Clarity of modeling choices: The equivalence-class construction for role-triplets is well motivated and explained; structured mean-field over per-triangle variables is appropriate.

Weaknesses and questions
- Expressivity trade-off: Collapsing all off-diagonal role triplets to a single B0 (and all “two-equal” patterns to Bxx shared across minority roles) may be too restrictive for networks where specific cross-role triads matter. An ablation comparing MMTM (small K) vs PTM on the same data would better quantify the cost of this parsimony.
- Complexity claims and consistency: Different parts of the paper alternately claim O(NK) versus O(Nδ^2K) runtime. Since the number of triangles after δ-subsampling is O(Nδ^2), the end-to-end cost per pass seems O(Nδ^2K) plus O(NK) for the global update. Please standardize these claims and be explicit about the dependence on δ and network sparsity.
- Approximation analysis: While any choice of A yields a valid lower bound, the statistical impact of the mixture-of-deltas restriction and random re-sampling of A is not quantified. Some empirical sensitivity analysis (e.g., varying |A|, structured vs random A) would increase confidence.
- δ-subsampling sensitivity: The approach hinges on δ. Results suggest setting δ above average degree, but more systematic sensitivity plots (accuracy vs δ) on heavy-tailed networks would help. Also, a brief discussion on bias induced by node-centric sampling of open vs closed triangles would be useful.
- Link prediction evaluation details: The metric (AUC/precision@k), computational cost of marginalizing over k, and baselines beyond MMSB (e.g., strong heuristic baselines that exploit triads) should be clarified. Given the model is not a generative model for edges, this positive result is notable; more detail would strengthen it.
- Related work coverage: It would help to situate the method with other scalable higher-order or motif-based representations (even if less probabilistic), and clarify differences with a-MMSB beyond the analogy on parameter sharing.

Quality
Technically sound overall; the variational objective and natural-gradient updates are standard, and the structured/local approximations are reasonable. Theoretical guarantees are limited to convergence of SVI to stationary points; there is no formal bound on approximation error due to parameter sharing or the mixture-of-deltas restriction—acceptable given the strong empirical evidence, but worth acknowledging more explicitly.

Clarity
Generally clear and well organized. The exposition of equivalence classes and parameter sharing is particularly good. Minor issues: unify complexity statements; include the omitted local-update equations (φxxx and φxxy) and derivations in the appendix; specify link prediction metrics; and provide more detail on δ selection and sensitivity.

Originality
High. While built on known ingredients (triadic motifs, SVI, parameter sharing), the combination and, in particular, the O(K) triad parameterization with an efficient structured variational scheme is a meaningful step forward.

Significance
High practical impact if broadly adopted: enabling higher-order latent role models to scale to million-node graphs with large K on commodity hardware is valuable. The approach could inspire analogous parsimony in other motif-based models.

Pros
- Elegant O(K) parameterization for triadic latent roles.
- Scalable SVI with structured local updates and practical engineering choices.
- Strong empirical scalability and competitive accuracy; fast convergence.
- Clear path to wider adoption in large-scale network analysis.

Cons
- Potential loss of expressivity from aggressive parameter sharing.
- Some inconsistencies/ambiguities in complexity and evaluation details.
- Limited analysis of approximation and subsampling effects.

Recommendation
Accept. The paper introduces a significant and novel direction—parsimonious, motif-based probabilistic latent space inference that truly scales. While some analyses could be deepened, the conceptual contribution and demonstrated scalability outweigh the minor shortcomings.
