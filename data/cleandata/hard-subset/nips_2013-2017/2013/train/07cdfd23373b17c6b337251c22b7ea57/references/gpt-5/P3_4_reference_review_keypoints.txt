- Paper proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using bag-of-triangles, parameter tying (Bxxx, Bxx, B0) and an SVI algorithm with structured mean-field and natural gradients to scale triangular-role inference to million-node graphs.
- Builds on prior work: triangular motifs/MMTM, parameter tying (a-MMSB), and SVI/natural gradients; structured mean-field over role triples is well-motivated.
- Strength: Parameter tying reduces O(K^3) → O(K) and mixture-of-deltas yields O(K) per-triangle updates enabling large-scale inference with mini-batching and natural gradients.
- Strength: Modeling choices and equivalence classes of triangle types are clearly explained; authors are explicit that this is for latent inference not a generative network model.
- Strength: Empirical claims show substantial speedups and scalability (e.g., 1M nodes, 100 roles in ~4 hours) with comparable or better accuracy than MMSB/MMTM Gibbs.
- Strength: Structured variational posterior captures higher-order dependencies ignored by naive mean-field.
- Weakness: Aggressive parameter tying (single B0 and Bxx) reduces expressiveness and cannot capture heterogeneous specific inter-role triad interactions.
- Weakness: Convergence/theoretical guarantees unclear because three approximations interact: δ-subsampling changes data distribution, mixture-of-deltas restricts variational family and yields a moving bound, and local-update completeness/stability not fully detailed in main text.
- Weakness: Reported step-size schedule appears incorrect (κ reported as −0.5) which would make learning rates increase over time and violates standard SVI conditions.
- Weakness: Complexity/memory accounting ambiguous—unclear whether γ is updated sparsely or all N nodes each iteration and memory/precision details for 1M-node run are missing.
- Weakness: Experimental gaps—limited baselines (no scalable SBMs/BigCLAM/spectral baselines), unspecified link-prediction metric and potential leakage from triangle extraction, initialization sensitivity not fully evaluated, and missing quantitative/ablation details (variance, effect of δ, A, K, batch size).
- Weakness: Applicability limited for tasks requiring fine-grained role-role interactions since model collapses many distinctions and is not edge-generative.
- Question: Please correct/clarify the step-size κ and provide the exact schedule used in experiments.
- Question: Provide a formal convergence statement or monotonicity guarantee for SVI with the mixture-of-deltas approximation (e.g., expected ELBO or surrogate objective).
- Question: Explain how you prevent leakage in link prediction when triangles are extracted after edge removal.
- Question: Report wall-clock time, memory footprint (γ storage, B parameters), and numeric precision used for the 1M-node YouTube experiment.
- Question: Provide ablations on the size/composition of A and the impact of δ-subsampling on accuracy and speed.
- Pro for acceptance: Addresses key scalability bottleneck with principled modeling parsimony and SVI, showing strong practical impact and clear ideas.
- Con for acceptance: Loss of expressiveness, insufficient theoretical justification for approximations, missing experimental details/baselines, and potential implementation errors.
- Recommendation: Weak reject—promising and potentially impactful but requires corrected optimization details, stronger theoretical justification, and more comprehensive empirical evaluation.
