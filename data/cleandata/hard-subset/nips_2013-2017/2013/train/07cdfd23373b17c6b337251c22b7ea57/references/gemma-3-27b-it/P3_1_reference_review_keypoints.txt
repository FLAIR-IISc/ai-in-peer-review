- Paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent space inference method for million-node networks using a bag-of-triangles representation and the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM).  
- PTM reduces parameter space relative to MMTM and uses stochastic natural gradient ascent with O(NK) complexity.  
- Demonstrated scalability: inference on a 1M-node YouTube network with 100 roles in ~4 hours.  
- Builds on MMSB, a-MMSB, and MMTM, logically adopting the triangular representation and improving parameter scalability.  
- Uses established stochastic variational inference and natural gradients as theoretical foundation.  
- Main strength: clear, significant scalability advancement for probabilistic network inference.  
- Strength: parsimonious parameter-sharing in PTM effectively addresses MMTM’s scalability limits.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world networks, with ablations and convergence analysis.  
- Strength: well-written, well-organized presentation with helpful tables and figures.  
- Weakness: PTM’s parameter-sharing imposes potentially restrictive assumptions about role relationships, unclear when violated.  
- Weakness: subsampling parameter δ choice lacks systematic sensitivity analysis and its impact on representation quality is underexplored.  
- Weakness: justification for the chosen structured mean-field approximation is brief; trade-offs not fully discussed.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent scalable latent-space or network models beyond MMSB/a-MMSB/MMTM.  
- For acceptance: tackles a crucial scalability bottleneck, offers a significant advance, and backs claims with strong empirical evidence.  
- Against acceptance: applicability concerns from PTM assumptions, limited δ sensitivity analysis, and insufficient mean-field justification.  
- Overall: strong contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions to discuss PTM assumptions and subsampling effects.  
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
