Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses scalable probabilistic latent space inference for large networks by combining (i) a succinct bag-of-triangles representation [8], (ii) a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) with O(K) triangle-generating parameters, and (iii) a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with structured mean-field updates and mini-batching. Unlike MMSB [1] and a-MMSB [5], which operate on edge-level representations and scale poorly in N and/or K, PTM shares parameters across classes of role triplets to avoid the O(K3) tensor in MMTM [8], enabling O(K) local computation per motif and O(NK) global updates (treating the δ triangle subsampling level as a constant). Experiments report accurate latent role recovery on synthetic MMSB and power-law networks, improved or competitive link prediction, and fast convergence on million-node networks, substantially faster than prior work [5, 8]. The paper builds on established ideas: triadic motifs [8], mixed-membership latent roles [1], and SVI with natural gradients [10, 18], but contributes a new parameter-sharing design and a practical O(K) local update via a mixture-of-deltas approximation.

Strengths
- Scalability: The combination of triangle-based data reduction and O(K) parameterization yields per-iteration costs that scale linearly in N and K (given fixed δ), enabling million-node, 100-role runs on a single multi-core machine.
- Modeling parsimony: Grouping role-triplet configurations by the number of distinct roles and sharing parameters (B0, Bxx, Bxxx) is a clean, well-motivated extension of the “assortative” simplification in a-MMSB [5], reducing O(K3) to O(K).
- Inference design: The structured mean-field over per-triangle role triplets addresses the high-rank nature of q(x,y,z), and the mini-batched natural-gradient SVI is appropriate for large datasets.
- Empirical results: Clear speedups over [5, 8] and strong latent role recovery on synthetic data; link prediction gains on larger social networks are consistent with the utility of triadic features.

Weaknesses and points needing clarification
- Technical correctness and typos:
  - Equation (6) appears incorrect: for φxyzijk with distinct x,y,z, the term Eq[log θi,x + log θj,x + log θk,x] should depend on θi,x, θj,y, θk,z, not all on x. This needs correction.
  - Step size schedule: ρt = τ0(τ1 + t)−κ with κ = −0.5 (as stated in Section 5.2) implies an increasing step size, violating the stated convergence conditions (∑ ρt = ∞, ∑ ρt^2 < ∞). Likely κ = 0.5 was intended; please correct and ensure consistency throughout.
- Convergence guarantees vs approximations: The claim that stochastic natural gradients “are guaranteed to converge to a stationary point of L(η,γ)” relies on unbiased gradients of a fixed objective. The O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” restriction (random A re-sampled each local update) changes the variational family across iterations. It is unclear whether the resulting gradient remains an unbiased estimator of the gradient of a single, fixed objective. A theoretical justification (or a statement limiting guarantees to the restricted objective) would strengthen the work.
- Complexity statements: The paper alternates between O(NK) and O(Nδ2K) claims. Since |I| = O(Nδ2) after subsampling, per-pass cost is O(Nδ2K); the O(NK) statement holds only when δ is treated as a small constant. Clarify the dependence on δ, and discuss how runtime scales when δ must be set above average degree (as recommended).
- Expressiveness trade-off: Sharing a single B0 across all all-distinct role triplets and a single Bxx per majority role may limit the model’s ability to capture heterogeneous inter-role triadic patterns. An ablation comparing PTM to a less aggressive sharing (e.g., a small number of B0 clusters) would help characterize the bias-variance trade-off.
- Fairness/rigor of comparisons:
  - Threading: PTM SVI uses 4 threads; MMTM/PTM Gibbs and MMSB are single-threaded in your runs. You partially mitigate this by reporting time per data pass, but absolute time comparisons should control for thread count.
  - Baseline tuning: Ensure MMSB and a-MMSB baselines use competitive hyperparameters and optimizers. For link prediction, clarify whether triangle-based ranking sums over all k exactly or via sampling, and report computational cost of evaluation.
- Reproducibility and details: The paper references Table 1 and the Appendix for key conditionals, gradients, and the synthetic data generator; ensuring these are precise and complete is important. Provide the exact forms of the natural gradients and the local updates for the x= y ≠ z and x=y=z cases in the main text or a clearly accessible appendix.

Assessment by review criteria
- Quality: The modeling and inference ideas are well-motivated and largely sound; however, the noted errors (Eq. 6, step size sign) and the lack of theoretical treatment of the random restricted local updates weaken the rigor. Empirical evaluation is extensive and supports the claims, though some comparisons could be more controlled.
- Clarity: The exposition is generally clear. The partitioning of role-triplet configurations and equivalence classes is well explained. Correcting typos, unifying complexity statements, and surfacing key update equations would improve readability.
- Originality: The triangle representation is from [8], and SVI/natural gradients from [10]. The main novelty lies in the O(K) parameter-sharing scheme for triads and the O(K) local variational approximation. This is an incremental but meaningful contribution.
- Significance: If the reported scalability and accuracy hold, the method is practically valuable for large-scale network analysis, enabling mixed-membership latent role inference at previously infeasible scales. This is of interest to the NeurIPS community.

Pros for acceptance
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups with competitive accuracy on large real networks.
- Introduces a simple, effective parameter-sharing design that materially reduces complexity.
- Provides a structured SVI approach tailored to triadic data with practical implementation details (mini-batching, parallelization, memory savings).

Cons against acceptance
- Technical correctness issues (equation and step-size typos) and missing theoretical justification for the randomized restricted local updates.
- Potential expressiveness limitations from aggressive parameter sharing are not fully analyzed.
- Some fairness and methodological details in experiments need clarification.

Suggestions for improvement
- Correct Eq. (6) and the step-size specification; report the actual κ used.
- Provide a brief lemma/theorem (or clear discussion) on the convergence behavior when using the randomized restricted local variational family; at minimum, state that convergence is to a stationary point of the restricted objective.
- Add an ablation contrasting exact O(K3) local updates (for small K) versus the O(K) approximation to quantify approximation impact.
- Clarify complexity with δ, and discuss practical setting of δ vs. accuracy/speed trade-offs.
- Ensure baselines are appropriately threaded/tuned, and detail the link prediction scoring computation and cost.
