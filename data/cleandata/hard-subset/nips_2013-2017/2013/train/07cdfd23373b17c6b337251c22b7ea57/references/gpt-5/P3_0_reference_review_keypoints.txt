- Paper proposes scalable probabilistic latent-role inference by modeling graphs as bags of triangles, introducing a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) with O(K) triangle parameters and an SVI algorithm reducing per-triangle updates from O(K^3) to O(K).

- Builds on MMSB/a-MMSB and MMTM by replacing edge-based likelihoods with triadic likelihoods and symmetry-based parameter tying to avoid cubic parameter blowup.

- Strength: Clear scalability strategy combining triangle-based reduction, O(K) parameterization, and stochastic natural gradients.

- Strength: Elegant symmetry-driven parameter sharing (all-same, two-same, all-distinct) that drastically reduces parameter count while keeping key role distinctions.

- Strength: Well-motivated inference engineering (structured mean-field and mixture-of-deltas) balancing speed and accuracy.

- Strength: Strong empirical scale demonstrated (up to 1M nodes, K=100) with fast convergence on a single multi-core machine.

- Strength: Practical utility shown via competitive latent role recovery and link prediction, with good qualitative justification for motif-based modeling.

- Weakness: Missing the most relevant probabilistic baseline—no head-to-head a-MMSB with SVI comparison on same hardware/settings.

- Weakness: Aggressive tying (single Bxx and B0) may reduce expressivity when minority-role identity matters; no ablation or alternative (e.g., low-rank tensor) discussed.

- Weakness: Claim that gradients are unbiased for the true objective is incorrect after restricting to mixture-of-deltas; the algorithm optimizes a different lower bound and the text conflates them.

- Weakness: δ-subsampling of triangles biases the objective if inference is defined on the subsampled bag without importance weighting; no bias analysis or correction provided.

- Weakness: Equation/notation errors (e.g., mistaken log θ terms in Eq.6) and a likely sign error in step-size schedule (κ sign).

- Weakness: PTM is not a full generative graph model (triangles can be inconsistent; many edges lack triangles); paper does not state handling or quantify fraction of such edges.

- Weakness: Evaluation protocol may be unfair (seeding strategy advantages); missing random restarts, sensitivity analyses, and ablations for mixture size A, structured vs naive mean-field, and δ.

- Weakness: Complexity claims need clarity—global updates are O(NK) but per-pass cost depends on m triangles (m = O(N δ^2)), so total cost is O(mK).

- Clarity: Generally well written with clear intuition, but missing appendix details, some typos/equation issues, and insufficient algorithmic complexity breakdown impede reproducibility.

- Originality: The combination of triangle representation, O(K) symmetry-based tying, and structured SVI is novel and non-trivial relative to prior work.

- Significance: If validated against stronger baselines and methodological clarifications, PTM offers an impactful scalable probabilistic route to motif-based latent roles at million-node scales.

- Suggestions: Include a-MMSB+SVI comparisons; fix Eq.(6) and step-size; provide exact natural gradient derivations; clarify which bound is optimized with mixture-of-deltas and ablate its size A.

- Suggestions: Analyze/quantify δ-subsampling bias or apply importance weighting; report handling and coverage of dyads not in triangles; consider augmenting likelihood with edge terms.

- Suggestions: Add ablations for structured vs naive mean-field, effect of seeding/random restarts, sensitivity to K, and the role of δ and mixture size.

- Pros for acceptance: Simple, scalable idea; solid SVI engineering; impressive empirical scale and reasonable accuracy.

- Cons for acceptance: Missing key baseline and ablations, technical inaccuracies/typos, ambiguous convergence claims, and potential expressivity loss from parameter tying.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept contingent on addressing baseline comparisons and clarifying the methodological and technical issues noted.
