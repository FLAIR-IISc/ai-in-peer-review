- Tackles scaling probabilistic latent space models to networks with millions of nodes via a new "Parsimonious Triangular Model" (PTM).
- PTM combines a bag-of-triangles representation and parameter-sharing to reduce triangle parameters from O(K³) to O(K).
- Stochastic variational inference for PTM converges in 2–5 passes, enabling O(NK) time and analysis of up to 1M nodes on a single machine.
- Empirical results show superior scalability to MMSB, MMTM, and PTM Gibbs on synthetic and real networks, with runtime improvements from days to minutes.
- Triangle-based modeling yields better link-prediction performance than edge-based MMSB on large social networks, supporting the modeling choice.
- Technical exposition is clear, well-grounded, and reproducible, with a detailed appendix and honest discussion of limitations.
- Main concern: triangular representation may lose information, but the paper does not quantify this loss or its impact on accuracy.
- Parameter-sharing may be too restrictive for networks with asymmetric role interactions; the paper lacks analysis of such failure cases.
- Experimental scope is limited to synthetic and a few social datasets; broader tests (biological, citation, transportation) are missing.
- No comparison to popular non-probabilistic embedding methods (e.g., node2vec, DeepWalk), which are standard baselines for large-scale networks.
- Paper lacks a nuanced discussion of trade-offs between scalability and accuracy and where PTM may underperform more flexible models.
- Overall assessment: strong and novel contribution that achieves O(NK) complexity, is technically sound, and merits acceptance.
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested revisions to quantify triangle information loss, analyze restrictive parameter-sharing scenarios, add non-probabilistic comparisons, and broaden experimental diversity.
