- Paper proposes a scalable latent-role inference method using a bag-of-triangles representation, a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) tying triangle parameters to O(K), and a structured SVI with an O(K) per-triangle mixture-of-deltas local update.

- Builds on Ho et al.’s triangular representation and a-MMSB/MMSB ideas for parameter tying, and on SVI with natural gradients from Hoffman et al.

- Strength: O(K) global parameterization and O(K) per-triangle local update enable strong scalability and memory efficiency, with parallelization and discarding local params between iterations.

- Strength: Structured variational family (joint triad-role tensor) and the mixture-of-deltas approximation balance accuracy and speed effectively.

- Strength: Parameter-tying via role-multiplicity equivalence classes is intuitively principled and matches a-MMSB parsimony.

- Strength: Empirical results show faster convergence, competitive/better link prediction than MMSB on large networks, and wall-clock improvements on large datasets.

- Weakness: The mixture-of-deltas and δ-subsampling introduce biased gradients; no theoretical analysis of the bias, stationary points, or tradeoff with subset size is provided.

- Weakness: Reported step-size schedule appears inconsistent (uses κ = −0.5), which violates Robbins–Monro conditions and undermines convergence claims.

- Weakness: PTM’s parameter tying collapses many distinct triplet interactions, limiting expressivity (e.g., no role-specific triadic closure or nuanced heterophily), with no ablation quantifying this tradeoff.

- Weakness: Initialization favors convergence using 2% seeded nodes; comparisons lack fair unsupervised initializations and variability reporting across seeds.

- Weakness: Computational comparisons are unfairly matched (different threading); runtimes reported per pass but not matched end-to-end wall-clock with equal cores.

- Weakness: Baselines are limited (mainly MMSB); missing comparisons to triangle-aware heuristics, tensor factorizations, modern embeddings, and scalable SBMs/Poisson blockmodels.

- Weakness: Missing ablations on size of sampled subset A, δ-subsampling, and comparisons to exact O(K^3) local updates on small graphs.

- Modeling concern: Bag-of-triangles is not a generative graph model, inducing selection bias that can shift posterior mass and affect link prediction; no correction or analysis provided.

- Complexity clarity: Paper alternates between O(NK) and O(Nδ^2K); per-iteration cost as a function of mini-batch size and passes to converge is unclear and needs clarification.

- Quality: Variational construction and engineering are sound, but theoretical analysis for the approximate local update and step-size issue is incomplete.

- Clarity: Paper is overall clear and well organized, but key derivations are deferred to the appendix and some statements (step-size, complexity) need correction.

- Originality: Novel combination of parameter tying for triangular models and a tailored structured SVI with a mixture-of-deltas approximation; reasonably novel relative to prior work.

- Significance: If scalability and accuracy hold under stricter, fairer evaluation and corrected SVI details, the method materially advances higher-order probabilistic network modeling at scale.

- Suggestions: Fix and clarify learning-rate schedule; provide convergence diagnostics and sensitivity analyses.

- Suggestions: Empirically or theoretically bound the bias from mixture-of-deltas and size of A; include ablations and exact-update comparisons on small graphs.

- Suggestions: Quantify expressivity gap vs. MMTM via controlled synthetics and broaden fair baselines with matched threading and initializations.

- Suggestions: Analyze δ-subsampling bias (importance weighting) and clearly report per-iteration complexity and matched wall-clock runtimes.

- Pros: Elegant parameter tying (O(K)), practical structured SVI with O(K) local updates, strong scalability and memory footprint, and competitive large-scale performance.

- Cons: Gaps in convergence theory and step-size error, unquantified expressivity loss vs. MMTM, fairness and ablation shortcomings in evaluation, and unaddressed triangle-selection bias.

- Overall: Promising and practically important work, but acceptance should require correcting the step-size/convergence issue and adding more empirical validation and ablations.
