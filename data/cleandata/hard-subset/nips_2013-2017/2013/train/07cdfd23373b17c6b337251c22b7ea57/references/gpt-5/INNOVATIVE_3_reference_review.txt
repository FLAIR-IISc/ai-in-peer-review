Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) for probabilistic latent space inference on large networks, coupled with a scalable structured stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm. The key idea is to represent a network as a bag of triangular motifs (open/closed triangles), as in Ho et al. [8], and to dramatically reduce the parameterization compared to the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM) by sharing parameters across role-triplet configurations according to the number of distinct roles in the triplet. Concretely, the O(K^3) tensor in MMTM is replaced by O(K) parameters {B0, Bxx, Bxxx}, analogous in spirit to the O(K) parameterization of inter-role links in a-MMSB [5] vs. O(K^2) in MMSB [1]. On the inference side, the authors develop a structured mean-field SVI with natural gradients [10], coupled with an O(K) local update approximation that restricts the posterior over (si,jk, sj,ik, sk,ij) to a mixture of “diagonals” and a randomly resampled O(K) subset of off-diagonals.

Empirically, the paper reports:
- Synthetic experiments for latent role recovery under MMSB-like block structure and a power-law latent model, showing high NMI with only 1–2 data passes for SVI, and much faster time per pass than MMSB variational and MMTM/PTM Gibbs.
- Heldout link prediction where the triangle-based PTM outperforms MMSB on larger social networks.
- Large-scale runs on real networks up to 1.1M nodes and 100 roles, converging in hours on a single 8-thread machine, with 2–5 data passes.

Strengths:
- Novel and bold modeling contribution: the parameter-tying scheme that reduces O(K^3) triangle parameters to O(K) is elegant and seems the decisive enabler for NK-scale inference on triangle-based latent models. This is a meaningful conceptual advance beyond [8].
- Scalable inference design: structured SVI with natural gradients and mini-batching over triangles is well-motivated; the O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” local variational family is a clever approximation that retains higher-order dependencies without O(K^3) cost.
- Clear path to parallelization and low memory footprint by discarding per-triangle local variational parameters across iterations.
- Strong empirical scalability: results on million-node networks and K=100 with short wall-clock times on modest hardware are compelling.
- Practical relevance: triangle-based modeling aligns with triadic closure and motif literature; the δ-subsampling makes the approach viable on high-degree graphs.
- Writing is generally clear, with careful decomposition of the ELBO and implementation choices.

Weaknesses and questions:
- Expressivity trade-off: the PTM shares parameters across all configurations with two equal roles and across all-distinct roles. This is a strong inductive bias. While it yields scalability, it could underfit networks where the identity of the minority role (or triplet combination) matters. An ablation on the cost of this tying (e.g., comparing to a modestly richer parameterization between O(K) and O(K^3)) would strengthen the case.
- Local variational approximation: the mixture-of-deltas restriction and random choice of A yield an O(K) update, but the theory is a bit underspecified. The ELBO remains a valid bound for any fixed local family; however, the unbiasedness statement ES[∇LS]=∇L holds for the chosen (restricted) objective, not the original L. Please clarify:
  - Are you optimizing an ELBO L’ defined by the restricted local family? If so, state that the unbiased gradient pertains to L’.
  - Do you re-sample A every time to reduce bias/variance, and does this preserve monotonicity in expectation? Any empirical sensitivity to the size and composition of A?
- Initialization and fairness: synthetic experiments seed 2% of nodes with ground-truth roles; baselines likely benefit less from such partial supervision depending on their sensitivity. Please report results with unsupervised initializations (e.g., your suggested 3-triangle heuristic) and include a-MMSB [5] as a baseline quantitatively (you mention runtime comparisons but not accuracy).
- Link prediction details: the ranking for PTM marginalizes over a third node k; how is this computed efficiently in practice? Are the metrics AUC/precision@k? Please specify metrics, variance over runs, and provide results on low-clustering networks to test the triangle-only representation.
- Scope and limitations: as acknowledged, the model is not a generative model of full networks. It would help to discuss when triangle-only modeling might be problematic (e.g., very sparse/low transitivity graphs), and show sensitivity to δ.
- Reproducibility: more details on triangle extraction cost, memory usage vs. N, K, δ; release of code would be valuable.

Minor comments and clarity:
- Provide explicit update formulas for φxxx and φxxy in the main text or a clearer pointer to the appendix.
- Table/figure references (Tables 1–3, Figure 1–2) are crucial; ensure they include dataset stats and evaluation metrics.
- Typos/formatting: a few minor spacing issues; consistent notation for Dirichlet parameters and indices would improve readability.

Significance and originality:
I view this as a substantial, original step toward scalable probabilistic latent role inference leveraging motifs. The O(K) parameterization for triangle models and the O(K) local SVI approximation are the core innovations that can influence follow-up work on higher-order network models. The empirical scaling is impressive and of practical interest.

Recommendation:
Accept. The paper introduces a genuinely new modeling and inference combination that enables a previously out-of-reach regime (million nodes, hundreds of roles) with principled probabilistic semantics. While some analyses could be deepened (effects of parameter tying; theoretical framing of the local approximation; broader baselines), the conceptual advance and empirical impact justify acceptance.
