- Paper proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via equivalence-class parameter sharing.  
- Inference uses structured mean-field SVI with natural gradients and a “mixture-of-deltas” variational restriction to achieve O(K) local updates and claimed O(NK) per-iteration cost.  
- Empirical results show competitive or better latent role recovery and link prediction, with large speedups (e.g., 100 roles on 1M-node YouTube in ~4 hours on 8 threads).  
- PTM builds on MMSB/a-MMSB and MMTM, extending assortative parameter-sharing ideas to triadic motifs and combining them with SVI/natural gradients.  
- Technical soundness: PTM likelihood and SVI derivation are clear and standard for conjugate models; structured MF and mixture-of-deltas make updates scalable.  
- Caveat: Mini-batch unbiased gradients hold for the restricted variational bound, not the original ELBO; this distinction should be stated and quantified.  
- Potential bug: Step-size schedule in Alg.1 likely has a sign error—experiments use κ = −0.5 which would make step-sizes grow; likely intended κ ∈ (0.5,1] or κ = 0.5.  
- Expressivity concern: aggressive parameter tying (single B0 for all three-distinct-role triplets, shared Bxx for minority-majority patterns) may underfit heterogeneous interactions.  
- Experiments: synthetic NMI and link prediction are appropriate, and timing is impressive, but some experimental details are missing or unclear.  
- Missing link-prediction details: metric (AUC? precision@k?), negative sampling strategy, and scoring complexity (marginalization over k) should be specified.  
- Initialization: methods are seeded with 2% ground-truth roles—this aids fairness but is unrealistic; report unsupervised runs and sensitivity to initialization.  
- Baselines: a-MMSB SVI results should be included across tasks; also compare to scalable non-probabilistic link predictors (LINE/DeepWalk/Node2Vec).  
- δ-subsampling: the δ choice and its accuracy-vs-speed tradeoff require sensitivity analysis.  
- Theoretical gaps: no formal bounds on approximation error from the restricted variational family or variance due to randomized support A.  
- Clarity: paper is generally readable, but fix step-size notation, the duplicated tuple typo in A construction, and complexity statements that omit dependence on number of sampled triangles m.  
- Suggestion: include at least one worked φ-update example beyond distinct-roles, and ablations comparing full structured MF vs. mixture-of-deltas and varying A size/strategy.  
- Suggestion: report sensitivity to τ0, τ1, κ and provide ablations on intermediate parameterizations to show when PTM may underfit.  
- Originality: main novelties are O(K) parameter sharing for triangle motifs, structured MF SVI for triangles, and the randomized mixture-of-deltas O(K) local update—combination appears novel and practical.  
- Significance: method enables scalable motif-based mixed-membership inference on million-node graphs with many roles and could be widely useful when triangles are informative.  
- Pros: elegant parameter sharing, scalable O(K) local and O(NK) global updates, structured MF avoids naive MF limits, strong empirical scalability and competitive accuracy.  
- Cons: risk of underfitting from aggressive tying, informal theoretical treatment of restricted variational family, incomplete baseline coverage, missing experimental details, and the step-size typo.  
- Recommendation: overall a strong submission with practical impact; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending clarifications and additional experiments.
