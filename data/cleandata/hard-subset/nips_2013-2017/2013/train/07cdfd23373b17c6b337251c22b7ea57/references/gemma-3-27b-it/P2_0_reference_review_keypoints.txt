- Paper proposes a scalable latent-space inference method using a bag-of-triangles representation, a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), and stochastic natural gradient ascent.
- PTM’s core technical innovation is parameter sharing that reduces triangle-parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Method demonstrates strong empirical scalability and accuracy on synthetic and real networks, with large speedups over MMSB and MMTM.
- Experiments are thorough, evaluating recovery accuracy and link prediction across diverse datasets.
- Writing and organization are clear overall, with logical presentation of motivation and components.
- The algorithm remains complex (triangle extraction, subsampling, variational inference) and may be hard for practitioners to deploy.
- Significant parameter tuning is required (α, λ, ρt, δ), and the paper offers limited guidance on selection.
- The justification for the chosen structured mean-field approximation is somewhat hand-wavy and needs stronger motivation.
- Choice of a 10% mini-batch size appears arbitrary; the impact of mini-batch size on performance/convergence is underexplored.
- Comparisons are limited to MMSB/MMTM; the paper should compare or discuss relations to modern scalable embeddings (node2vec, DeepWalk, GNNs).
- Some sections, especially the variational inference details, are dense and could benefit from more visual aids or clearer exposition.
- The parameter-sharing idea and use of stochastic natural gradients are original contributions with high potential impact.
- Overall assessment: strong accept — technically significant, empirically compelling, but should address clarity, mean-field justification, parameter tuning, and broader comparisons.
- Recommended minor revisions: improve explanation of structured mean-field, provide guidance on hyperparameters/mini-batch selection, and add discussion/comparison to graph embedding methods.
