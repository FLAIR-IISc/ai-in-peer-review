- Summary: Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (bag-of-triangles) plus stochastic natural gradient to scale probabilistic latent space inference to millions of nodes with O(NK) complexity and strong empirical results.

- Strength — Problem importance: Directly addresses the critical scalability bottleneck in network latent-space inference.

- Strength — Principled design: Method guided by succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference principles.

- Strength — Novelty: PTM’s parameter-sharing reduces complexity from O(K^3) to O(K), enabling much larger latent spaces.

- Strength — Empirical validation: Substantial speedups over a-MMSB and MMTM Gibbs and competitive accuracy, including results on a 1M-node YouTube network.

- Strength — Technical depth: Detailed model and algorithmic exposition, including stochastic natural gradient and computational approximations.

- Strength — Writing: Generally clear and well-organized presentation of complex ideas.

- Weakness — Practical constants and tuning: O(NK) complexity hides constant factors and mini-batch effects; δ-subsampling requires careful tuning and guidance.

- Weakness — Approximation justification: Structured mean-field approximation is insufficiently justified; more explanation of its benefits over naive mean-field is needed.

- Weakness — Limited baselines: Comparisons limited to a-MMSB and MMTM Gibbs; should include other recent scalable network models if available.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: No thorough sensitivity analysis for α, λ, and step-size parameters; robustness claims need strengthening.

- Weakness — Clarity in inference: Section 4 is dense and could benefit from illustrative examples or diagrams.

- Quality: Theoretical complexity analysis and experiments are convincing and technically sound.

- Clarity (criterion): Generally well-written but certain sections are dense and need more illustrative material.

- Originality: Combination of bag-of-triangles, PTM parameter sharing, and stochastic natural gradient is novel and well-referenced.

- Significance: Work meaningfully advances ability to apply probabilistic latent-space models to much larger networks.

- Pro (accept): Solves a critical scalability problem with a novel, well-motivated approach and strong empirical evidence.

- Con (reject concerns): O(NK) may still be large for very big K; structured mean-field needs stronger theoretical grounding; limited comparisons.

- Overall: Strong paper with minor issues; recommend acceptance with revisions to clarify the mean-field choice and add a brief hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept (minor revisions).
