- Summary: Proposes PTM — a triangle-based latent-role model with O(K) parameters via role-triplet equivalence classes and a structured SVI algorithm for scalable inference on very large graphs.  
- Key technical contributions: bag-of-triangles representation, parsimonious O(K) parameterization (B0, Bxx, Bxxx), structured mean-field with mixture-of-deltas local variational family, and natural-gradient SVI.  
- Scalability claim: Global updates are O(NK); demonstrated scaling to ~1M nodes and K=100 in hours on 8 threads.  
- Strength: Sound modeling idea — parameter sharing reduces K^3 blowup while preserving key triadic distinctions.  
- Strength: Algorithmic design — O(K) per-triangle updates, retained triplet dependencies, memory-efficient local-variable discard.  
- Strength: Empirics — synthetic role recovery, fast convergence (1–2 passes), and reported runtime/accuracy gains versus MMTM and MMSB.  
- Theoretical concern: Convergence guarantees unclear because of the restricted variational family, approximate local maximization, and re-sampling, meaning the algorithm may optimize a different bound than stated.  
- Expressiveness concern: O(K) sharing collapses heterogeneous all-distinct and two-same triplets, potentially missing role-specific triadic patterns.  
- Comparison concern: No direct a-MMSB implementation under same hardware/stopping criteria; link prediction comparisons limited to MMSB and omit other scalable baselines.  
- Evaluation concern: Synthetic experiments seed 2% of nodes (may bias results); effects of δ-subsampling on the objective and need for reweighting are not fully clarified.  
- Minor issues: Step-size typo (κ sign incorrect in text), small notational slips, and dependence on appendix/table for key update details.  
- Clarity: Overall readable and well-organized, but needs to state the exact objective optimized under the mixture-of-deltas approximation and fix typos.  
- Originality: Novel combination of triangle-based modeling, O(K) parameter sharing, and structured SVI relative to prior work.  
- Significance: Practically important for scaling latent-role inference to million-node graphs with many roles on modest hardware.  
- Suggestions: Provide ablation vs exact O(K^3) updates, include or report attempts to run a-MMSB baseline, correct and report step-size parameters and sensitivity, consider modestly richer sharing schemes, and clarify δ-subsampling treatment.  
- Overall recommendation: Lean accept.
