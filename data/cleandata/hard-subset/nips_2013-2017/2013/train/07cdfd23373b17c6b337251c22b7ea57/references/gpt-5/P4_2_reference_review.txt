Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses scalable probabilistic latent space inference for large networks. It adopts the bag-of-triangles representation [8], where each network is summarized as a multiset of connected 3-node motifs (open/closed triangles), which typically scales as O(ND^2) rather than O(N^2) edges. Building on this representation, the authors propose the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), a mixed-membership latent role model in which per-node role mixtures θi generate per-triangle role assignments (si,jk, sj,ik, sk,ij). Unlike the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM) [8] that uses an O(K^3) tensor of triangle parameters, PTM shares parameters across groups of role configurations, reducing the triangle-generating parameters to O(K): Bxxx for all-three-same roles, Bxx for two-equal-one-different configurations (with role x as the majority), and a global B0 for all-distinct roles. This idea parallels the parameter sharing of a-MMSB [5], which compresses inter-role connectivity from O(K^2) to O(1) for distinct-role edges.

For inference, the paper develops a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm [10] with structured mean-field over the per-triangle latent role triples, optimizing a variational lower bound decomposed into global and local terms. Exact local updates are O(K^3); to scale, the authors introduce a “mixture-of-deltas” variational family that retains mass on the K diagonal (a,a,a) and O(K) randomly sampled off-diagonal (a,b,c) configurations, yielding O(K) per-triangle local updates. Global parameters are updated by stochastic natural gradients [2,20], and per-iteration complexity scales as O(NK) due to the O(K) global parameterization.

Experiments on synthetic data (including MMSB-generated and power-law networks) evaluate latent role recovery via NMI after thresholding, with K growing with N. Comparisons include MMSB batch variational [1], MMTM collapsed Gibbs [8], and a PTM collapsed Gibbs variant. On real networks up to 1.1M nodes, the method reports fast convergence of the variational bound and shows held-out link prediction performance superior to MMSB on larger social graphs. The authors also report large speedups relative to timings reported in prior work (e.g., a-MMSB [5] and MMTM [8]).

Quality
- Modeling: The parameter-sharing scheme is well-motivated and reduces variance in estimating triangle probabilities. The model explicitly targets triangle data rather than full graphs, consistent with the stated goal of latent space inference (not network generation).
- Inference: The SVI objective and mini-batch estimator are standard and correct for the chosen variational family. The structured mean-field over role triples is appropriate; the mixture-of-deltas restriction makes the local update O(K) and still yields a valid ELBO, though it optimizes a surrogate bound different from the full structured family. The paper’s statement of unbiased gradients should be qualified: unbiasedness holds for the mini-batch estimator of the chosen bound; with the restricted local family and δ-subsampling of triangles, convergence is to a stationary point of that surrogate objective.
- Complexity and implementation: The claimed per-iteration costs (local O(K) per sampled triangle; global O(NK)) are consistent with the design. Memory savings via discarding local factors are sensible.
- Empirics: The synthetic studies demonstrate high NMI across sizes; however, all methods are seeded with 2% ground-truth nodes, which can materially affect outcomes. Baseline choices omit a-MMSB [5] in direct comparisons. Link prediction compares only to MMSB, and the proposed edge ranking (via triangle probabilities marginalizing the third node) is reasonable but non-standard; additional baselines (common neighbors, Katz, node2vec) would strengthen claims. Reported speedups against prior work rely partly on times reported in their papers rather than head-to-head runs.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-organized. The partition of triangle configurations and induced equivalence classes is well explained, though it relies on a table in the appendix—duplicating a concise version in the main text would help. The derivations of local and natural gradient updates are appropriately deferred. Clarifying the scope of the convergence claims under the mixture-of-deltas approximation and δ-subsampling would improve precision. Experimental setup details (seed sensitivity, A size for off-diagonals, δ choices relative to degree) could be expanded.

Originality
- Novel: O(K) parameterization for triangle-based mixed-membership modeling; structured SVI with an O(K)-cost local approximation over role triples; scaling latent role inference to millions of nodes in a triangle-based framework.
- Relation to prior work: Extends [8] by compressing the triangle parameter tensor; conceptually related to parameter sharing in a-MMSB [5]; leverages SVI/natural gradients [10,2,20]. The combination and instantiation in the triangle setting appear new.

Significance
The work addresses an important scalability bottleneck and demonstrates substantial speed and scale improvements with competitive or better accuracy on synthetic tasks and link prediction. If broadly adopted, the approach could enable latent role analyses at scales previously impractical. The impact would be stronger with broader baselines and ablations.

Strengths
- O(K) parameterization for triangle models; principled grouping of role configurations.
- Efficient SVI with structured local factors and O(K) local updates.
- Strong scalability: million-node, hundred-role inference in hours on a single machine.
- Clear empirical improvements in runtime and competitive accuracy.

Weaknesses
- Limited baseline coverage: no direct comparison to a-MMSB [5] or modern scalable embeddings/overlapping community methods.
- Seeding with ground-truth roles may inflate recovery performance; robustness to random initialization not shown.
- Convergence guarantees are stated for unbiased gradients; with the restricted local family and δ-subsampling, guarantees apply to a surrogate bound—this should be clarified.
- Link prediction evaluation includes only MMSB; additional baselines and metrics would strengthen claims.
- Sensitivity analyses (δ, size of A in the mixture-of-deltas) are not reported.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add direct comparisons to a-MMSB [5] and at least one scalable non-probabilistic baseline (e.g., BigCLAM, node2vec) for link prediction.
- Report robustness to initialization (unseeded runs, multiple random starts) and variance across runs.
- Provide ablations on δ and the number/composition of off-diagonal triples A; quantify the accuracy/speed trade-off.
- Clarify the convergence statement: specify that SVI converges to a stationary point of the ELBO under the chosen variational family, and discuss implications of δ-subsampling.
- Include additional link prediction metrics and details (AUC/APS, edge sampling protocol).

Arguments for acceptance
- Addresses a central scalability challenge with a principled, effective design.
- Novel O(K) triangle parameterization with a well-motivated structured SVI algorithm.
- Strong empirical scaling and practical utility demonstrated on large real networks.

Arguments against acceptance
- Baseline coverage and evaluation breadth are limited; some comparisons are indirect.
- Dependence on seeding and lack of robustness analyses.
- Theoretical claims on convergence could be stated more precisely for the approximate local family.

Overall, the paper presents a technically sound and practically meaningful advance in scalable latent role inference with clear novelty relative to triangle-based and MMSB-based prior work.
