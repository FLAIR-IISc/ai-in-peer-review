- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) for scalable latent space inference using a bag-of-triangles representation and parameter sharing.  
- Reduces parameterization from O(K³) to O(K) by sharing parameters across triangle role configurations.  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity enabling scalability to 1M+ node networks.  
- Demonstrates competitive accuracy on synthetic and real networks while achieving large runtime improvements.  
- Technical derivations (variational updates, natural gradient ascent) are detailed and theoretically sound.  
- Uses a structured mean-field approximation, improving performance over naive mean-field.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, comparing against multiple baselines with clear metrics for accuracy (NMI) and runtime.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides implementable algorithm descriptions and examples.  
- Transparent about limitations: triangular representation doesn't capture all network information and the model cannot generate valid networks solely from triangles.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of convergence properties and error bounds for the stochastic variational algorithm.  
- Does not systematically analyze the impact of the δ-subsampling parameter on the speed–accuracy trade-off.  
- Unclear whether triangular motifs are equally effective across diverse domains (e.g., biological or citation networks).  
- Missing comparison or discussion relative to more recent scalable network models (e.g., scalable GNN work).  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, novel, and practical advance with minor addressable weaknesses.
