- Addresses scalable probabilistic latent space inference for million-node networks by proposing three principles: succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, scalable inference.  
- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that groups triangle role configurations into three equivalence classes to share parameters.  
- Parameter sharing reduces triangle-generating parameters from O(K³) to O(K).  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) time complexity enabling inference on networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of roles.  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability (e.g., 1M-node YouTube network in ~4 hours) and competitive accuracy on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Thorough complexity analysis and clear theoretical justification for the parameter-sharing scheme.  
- Strong experimental evaluation: latent space recovery, link prediction, scalability, and fair comparisons to MMSB, MMTM Gibbs, PTM Gibbs.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and provides clear algorithm pseudocode and helpful examples/tables.  
- Core innovation (O(K) parameterization of triangle model) is novel and advances prior bag-of-triangles work.  
- Limited theoretical guarantees for the stochastic variational inference (convergence/approximation bounds not fully addressed).  
- Model does not generate valid networks (operates on triangle representation only), raising questions for downstream tasks.  
- Parameter-sharing explanation and the O(K) approximation details are somewhat complex and could use more intuition and sensitivity discussion.  
- Lacks comparison to popular non-probabilistic large-scale methods (e.g., node2vec, DeepWalk) and clearer domain/application impact examples.  
- Relies on 2% seeded initialization for fair comparison; alternative initialization strategies and memory-usage specifics are not fully evaluated.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant scalability advance, well-presented, with minor limitations—strong recommendation to accept.
