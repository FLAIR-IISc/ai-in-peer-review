Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles scalable probabilistic latent space inference on very large networks by combining: (i) a succinct bag-of-triangles representation (triadic motifs) that has been shown to capture key higher-order structure (Ho et al. NIPS’12); (ii) a new parsimonious triangular model (PTM) that reduces the number of triangle-generating parameters from O(K3) in MMTM to O(K) via parameter tying based on role-equivalence classes; and (iii) a structured stochastic variational inference algorithm with natural gradients and an O(K) local update via a mixture-of-deltas approximation, enabling near O(NK) complexity per data pass under δ-subsampling. Empirically, the method reportedly recovers latent roles on synthetic data with growing K, performs competitively or better on held-out link prediction, and scales to 1M-node networks with 100 roles in hours on a single multi-core machine—substantially faster than a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.) and MMTM.

This work builds directly on: MMSB (Airoldi et al.), a-MMSB’s parameter tying and stochastic VI, the triangular representation of networks (Ho et al.), and SVI with natural gradients (Hoffman et al.). The novelty lies in bringing the triangle representation together with a principled O(K) parameterization for triads and a structured VI scheme that keeps the key dependencies among triad role assignments but at O(K) cost.

Strengths
- Novel and bold modeling idea: the O(K) parameterization for triadic probabilities via equivalence classes is elegant, intuitive, and dramatically improves scalability without abandoning higher-order structure.
- Algorithmic innovation: a structured mean-field with a mixture-of-deltas local family to approximate the full K3 tensor posterior at O(K) cost is a clever compromise that appears to work well in practice.
- Strong empirical scalability: million-node/100-role inference in hours on a single machine, with 2–5 passes to converge, is impressive and highly relevant.
- Solid empirical validation: synthetic latent-space recovery across regimes where K grows with N, and real-world link prediction and perplexity comparisons, support practical utility.
- Clear articulation of trade-offs: the paper is upfront that PTM is not a generative model of full graphs and that the focus is latent space inference.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Expressivity vs. parsimony: tying all “two-equal-one-different” configurations to Bxx and all “all-different” to a single B0 may be too restrictive for heterogeneous roles; the paper would benefit from analyzing when this induces systematic bias or role collapse.
- Local variational approximation: the mixture-of-deltas subset A is resampled each update. While the authors state any A yields a valid lower bound, the bound itself changes over time; a short lemma or citation clarifying that the resulting stochastic natural gradient remains unbiased and convergent would strengthen the claims.
- Link prediction evaluation: scoring edges by marginalizing over k in triangles is reasonable given the model, but comparability to edge-based MMSB is nontrivial. Please clarify whether both methods see identical training data (e.g., with the same held-out edges removed prior to triangle extraction) and whether the ranking accounts for δ-subsampling.
- Initialization: synthetic experiments rely on seeding 2% of nodes with ground-truth roles for all methods; this favors convergence but is unrealistic. While an unsupervised heuristic is suggested, results without seeding (or with more neutral initializations) would help.
- Theoretical guarantees: the paper relies on δ-subsampling preserving the open/closed triangle distribution; a brief summary of approximation guarantees (or bounds on induced bias in parameter estimates) would be helpful.
- Reproducibility details: some crucial update equations are deferred to the appendix (e.g., φxxx and φxxy updates, natural gradients). Public code or more implementation details (digamma caching, batching policies, negative sampling alternatives) would aid adoption.

Quality
Overall technically sound with a coherent model, a carefully designed SVI procedure, and plausible complexity analysis. The main algorithmic step—O(K) local updates via mixture-of-deltas—needs a bit more theoretical justification regarding unbiasedness of the stochastic natural gradient and stability of the lower bound when the variational family is stochastically changed. Empirical evidence is strong but some comparisons rely on seeded initialization, and details of baseline tuning and fairness could be expanded.

Clarity
Generally clear and well-organized. The explanation of role-equivalence classes and induced motif equivalence is helpful. Minor improvements: explicitly state how Eq[log θi,x] are computed (digamma terms), summarize the omitted local updates, and describe the link prediction scoring precisely. A table summarizing complexities and memory footprints would help.

Originality
High. The parameter-tying scheme for triadic probabilities down to O(K) and the O(K) structured variational local update are, to my knowledge, new in the context of triangle-motif latent space models, and represent a bold departure from O(K3) tensors. The integration with SVI is principled, not just an engineering tweak.

Significance
High. If the reported scalability and accuracy hold broadly, this enables probabilistic latent space inference with hundreds of roles on million-node graphs on commodity hardware—very impactful for practical network analysis. The approach may inspire analogous parsimony schemes for other motifs and higher-order models.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Can you provide a short proof or citation that using a randomly changing subset A still yields an unbiased natural gradient and converges to a stationary point of the true variational objective?
- How sensitive are results to α and λ? Any guidance or automatic tuning?
- Can PTM be extended to a small number of Bxyy parameters (e.g., a low-rank or clustering of roles) to regain some expressivity while keeping near-linear complexity?
- How do results change with unsupervised initialization? Please include at least one such curve.
- For high-degree nodes, does δ-subsampling bias role estimates for hubs? Any adaptive δ strategy?
- Please clarify the fairness of link prediction comparisons and whether MMSB was also trained on data with identical edge removals before any triangle extraction.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong, novel idea that substantially reduces parameter count while preserving key higher-order structure.
- Clever and scalable inference scheme; compelling speedups to million-node scale.
- Broad potential impact and practical relevance.

Cons for acceptance
- Expressivity may be limited by aggressive parameter tying; more analysis warranted.
- Some theoretical details of the stochastic approximation and fairness of evaluations could be bolstered.
- Dependence on seeded initialization in some experiments.

Overall recommendation
Accept. The paper introduces a significant and original direction for scalable probabilistic network modeling, with convincing empirical results and a thoughtful algorithmic design. While some theoretical clarifications and additional ablations would strengthen it, the novelty and potential impact outweigh these limitations.

Confidence
High on assessment of novelty and significance; medium on nuanced theoretical guarantees due to some details being in the appendix.
