- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) for latent space inference that reduces triangle parameters from O(K³) to O(K).  
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity enabling scalable inference on million-node networks.  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability: 1M-node YouTube network inferred in ~4 hours on a single 8-thread machine.  
- Key contributions: parsimonious triangle parameterization, structured mean-field variational approximation, and stochastic natural gradient ascent.  
- Theoretical complexity analysis is provided and contrasted favorably with MMSB and MMTM.  
- Experiments are comprehensive: synthetic recovery, held-out link prediction, and real networks from 58K to 1.1M nodes.  
- Structured mean-field is shown to outperform naive mean-field in experiments.  
- Convergence is fast (2–5 data passes) compared to batch methods (10+ passes).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and provides clear notation, figures, and pseudocode for reproducibility.  
- Sufficient implementation and parameter details (α=λ=0.1, δ=50, minibatch size=10%) are provided for replication.  
- Originality: novel combination of bag-of-triangles, parsimonious parameterization, and stochastic inference despite related prior work.  
- Significance: enables practical latent-space analysis on previously intractable large-scale networks with large speedups and competitive accuracy.  
- Weakness: triangular representation may lose information for networks with few triangles and this is not fully analyzed.  
- Weakness: runtime comparisons lack detailed hardware specifications for full transparency.  
- Weakness: scalability to 10M+ nodes and distributed implementations are not explored.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of why structured mean-field outperforms naive mean-field.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — high scores on quality (9/10), clarity (9/10), originality (8/10), and significance (9/10).
