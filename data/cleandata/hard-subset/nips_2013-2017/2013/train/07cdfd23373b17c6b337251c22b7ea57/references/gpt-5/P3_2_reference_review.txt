Summary
The paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space model for large networks built on a bag-of-triangles representation. The Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) shares parameters across classes of role-triplet configurations so that the number of triangle-generating parameters scales as O(K) rather than O(K3) as in MMTM, while retaining mixed-membership node roles. To fit the model, the authors develop a stochastic variational inference (SVI) scheme with structured mean-field over triangle role triplets, coupled with a stochastic natural gradient update for global parameters. For efficiency, they introduce a “mixture-of-deltas” approximation that restricts the support of the per-triangle variational distribution to O(K) combinations. Experiments on synthetic data (both MMSB-like and power-law networks) and several real networks suggest improved scalability (to 1M nodes and K=100 on a single multi-core machine) with competitive or better performance in latent-space recovery and link prediction compared to MMSB variational inference and MMTM Gibbs sampling.

Relation to prior work
The work is closely related to: (i) MMSB and its scalable variants (e.g., a-MMSB) that reduce parameterization (K2 to K) and use SVI, (ii) triangular/motif-based network representations and the MMTM model that motivated using triangles for scalability and expressivity, and (iii) ERGM literature that emphasizes higher-order structures (triangles, k-stars). The PTM’s parameter-sharing idea mirrors a-MMSB’s use of a single inter-role parameter, but adapts it to the triangle domain. The structured variational approach is in line with recent SVI approaches that avoid fully factorized posteriors when strong local dependencies exist. The paper would benefit from deeper comparison to degree-corrected SBMs, latent distance models (Hoff et al.), and strong non-probabilistic baselines (e.g., BigCLAM, Node2Vec) where appropriate for link prediction.

Strengths
- Scalability via modeling and inference: The O(K) parameterization of triangle-generating probabilities, combined with mini-batch SVI and a structured variational family, is a principled way to push performance to million-node graphs with large K.
- Sensible use of motifs: The bag-of-triangles representation is well motivated by triadic closure and ERGM statistics, and can be substantially smaller than edges in sparse networks.
- Structured mean-field: Modeling (si,jk, sj,ik, sk,ij) jointly is important; the paper correctly notes that naive mean-field is a poor rank-1 approximation for the rank-rich role-tensor.
- Practical engineering: Discarding local variational parameters between iterations, δ-subsampling, and easy parallelization are sound engineering choices for large data.
- Empirical results: Clear runtime advantages (often orders of magnitude) while maintaining or improving latent-role recovery and link prediction relative to probabilistic baselines.

Weaknesses and points needing clarification
- Objective and unbiasedness: The SVI objective L(η,γ) is defined using the exact max over local parameters. The proposed “mixture-of-deltas” restriction modifies the variational family, yielding a different ELBO. That is fine, but the gradient remains unbiased only for the modified objective. The text should clarify that the unbiasedness claim pertains to the restricted family and say whether A’s random re-sampling changes the effective objective.
- Inconsistency in complexity claims: The paper alternates between O(NK) and O(Nδ2K) statements. The local step dominates at O(|S|K) per iteration and O(Nδ2K) per pass. Please make the per-iteration and per-pass complexities explicit and consistent throughout, and separate global O(NK) from total costs.
- Step-size schedule: The experiments list κ = −0.5 for ρt = τ0(τ1 + t)−κ. This would make step sizes grow with t. Likely a typo; please confirm κ ∈ (0.5,1] as in standard SVI theory and report actual values used.
- Update equation typo: In Eq. (6) for distinct x,y,z, the term Eq[log θi,x + log θj,x + log θk,x] appears; it should be Eq[log θi,x] + Eq[log θj,y] + Eq[log θk,z].
- Expressivity trade-off: PTM cannot differentiate all (x,x,y) cases by y nor (x,y,z) triples by role identity; it collapses many interaction patterns into Bxx and B0. A brief discussion or ablation of the accuracy versus parameter-sharing trade-off (e.g., performance degradation when role interactions are heterogeneous) would strengthen the paper.
- Generative mismatch and evaluation: The model is not a generative model of networks; triangles are generated independently. For link prediction, it would be helpful to (i) ensure that held-out edges are removed before triangle extraction to avoid leakage, and (ii) clarify computational cost of scoring by marginalizing over all k. Also, compare against strong heuristic baselines (Common Neighbors, Adamic–Adar) and modern embedding methods to contextualize gains.
- Sensitivity analyses: The paper fixes δ=50 and hyperparameters (α,λ). Please report sensitivity to δ (subsampling bias/variance), to α/λ, to the size of A in the mixture-of-deltas, and to initialization in fully unsupervised settings.
- Missing details: The forms of the natural gradients for η and γ (in the Appendix) are not summarized; including at least their high-level structure (digamma expectations, sufficient statistics) would improve reproducibility. Clarify memory footprint for 1M-node runs, minibatch sizes, number of passes, and stopping criteria.

Quality
Technically sound overall, with a well-motivated variational formulation and credible engineering choices. Some theoretical statements (step-size schedule, unbiasedness with restricted support) need clarification/correction. Typos in equations should be fixed.

Clarity
Generally clear and well organized. However, the complexity claims are inconsistent; Eq. (6) contains a likely typo; and the step-size exponent appears wrong. Including more detail on global update forms and evaluation protocol would improve clarity and reproducibility.

Originality
The combination of triangle-based representation with O(K) parameter sharing and structured SVI is novel and useful. The parameter-sharing idea draws from a-MMSB but is adapted thoughtfully to motifs.

Significance
High practical significance for scalable mixed-membership inference on large networks with many roles. If clarified and strengthened as suggested, the approach is likely to be adopted and extended.

Questions for authors
1) Please confirm the correct step-size schedule and κ. 2) Does re-sampling A at each local update optimize a single expected ELBO or a changing objective? 3) How sensitive are results to δ and the size of A? 4) For link prediction, how do you compute scores efficiently over all k, and do you compare to heuristic/embedding baselines? 5) Can you report performance when role interactions are deliberately heterogeneous across (x,x,y)?

Pros
- Strong scalability with principled variational design
- Competitive or better accuracy vs probabilistic baselines
- Clear practical impact; runs on million-node graphs
- Sound use of structured mean-field and natural gradients

Cons
- Some theoretical/notation issues and clarity problems
- Limited analysis of expressivity trade-offs
- Missing comparisons to strong non-probabilistic baselines
- Sensitivity analyses are sparse

Recommendation: Weak accept (borderline). The idea and results are strong and timely; fixing the noted issues would make this a solid contribution. Confidence: High.
