- Addresses scalable latent-space inference for very large networks (>100k nodes).  
- Proposes three principles for large-scale inference: succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable/parallel inference.  
- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing.  
- Develops a stochastic natural-gradient variational inference algorithm with O(NK) time complexity.  
- Demonstrates practical scalability: infers 100-role latent space on a 1M-node YouTube network in ~4 hours on one 8-thread machine.  
- Achieves orders-of-magnitude runtime speedup over prior methods while maintaining or improving accuracy.  
- Extends and improves on MMSB and MMTM limitations (complexity and parameter explosion).  
- Theoretical derivations and algorithmic design are sound and well-justified.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive (synthetic + real networks) and shows consistent performance gains.  
- Runtime complexity analysis is thorough and clearly contrasts with prior methods (MMSB, MMTM).  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, particularly initialization and triangular representation trade-offs.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, with consistent notation and reproducible experimental details.  
- Novel contributions: clever parameter sharing and a structured mean-field approximation for improved scalability and performance.  
- Significant practical impact by enabling probabilistic latent inference at million-node scale.  
- Strengths: clear problem framing, novel parameter sharing, efficient O(NK) inference, strong empirical validation, good writing.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of when triangular representation fails vs edge-based models.  
- Weaknesses: initialization strategy may be sensitive and relies on prior knowledge; heuristic needs more detail.  
- Weaknesses: lacks broader embedding-quality comparisons beyond NMI and downstream-task evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical convergence analysis specific to their stochastic variational algorithm.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Specific author suggestions: discuss triangle-representation failure modes; detail and test initialization heuristics and sensitivity; compare embeddings on downstream tasks; strengthen convergence theory; clarify how subsampling parameter δ relates to network properties.
