- Summary: Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation and parameter sharing to reduce complexity from O(K^3) to O(K), enabling scalable latent-space inference on million-node networks.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on MMSB/a-MMSB and MMTM, addressing MMTM’s cubic complexity while leveraging stochastic variational inference and natural gradients in a novel context.  
- Strength — Scalability: Dramatically reduces parameter complexity enabling inference on networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of roles.  
- Strength — Principled design: Systematically addresses succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Strong experiments on synthetic and real datasets, including a 1M-node YouTube network, showing superior runtime and competitive accuracy.  
- Strength — Novelty of techniques: Innovative combination of bag-of-triangles, parameter sharing, and stochastic natural gradient ascent.  
- Strength — Clarity: Clear writing, consistent notation, and informative figures.  
- Weakness — Structured mean-field: Insufficient explanation and intuition behind the chosen structured mean-field approximation versus naive mean-field.  
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Limited sensitivity analysis for the δ-subsampling parameter and robustness to its choices.  
- Weakness — Generative limitations: PTM is not a full generative model; implications and limitations are under-discussed.  
- Weakness — Comparative analysis depth: Lacks deeper analysis explaining in which network structures PTM outperforms alternatives.  
- Weakness — Reproducibility: No publicly available implementation provided.  
- Quality (qualitative): Technically sound with convincing theoretical and experimental support.  
- Clarity (qualitative): Generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Originality (qualitative): Highly original combination of methods and parameter-sharing strategy.  
- Significance (qualitative): Potentially impactful by enabling probabilistic latent models for much larger real-world networks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses crucial scalability problem, offers a novel effective solution, strong empirical results, and is technically sound.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs more detail on structured mean-field, thorough δ sensitivity analysis, and public code release.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with significant scalability contributions and minor addressable weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify structured mean-field, analyze δ sensitivity, and preferably release code.
