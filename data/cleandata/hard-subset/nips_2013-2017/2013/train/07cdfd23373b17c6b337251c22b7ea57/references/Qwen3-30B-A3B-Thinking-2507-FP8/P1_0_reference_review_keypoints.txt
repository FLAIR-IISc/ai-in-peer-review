- Paper proposes PTM (parsimonious triangular model) + stochastic variational inference to scale latent space inference for large networks.  
- PTM reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing across role configurations.  
- Uses a bag-of-triangles representation to lower data complexity to O(Nδ^2) and achieve overall O(NK) complexity.  
- Empirical results show convergence on 1M-node networks in ~4 hours with competitive or better accuracy than prior methods.  
- Builds on Ho et al. (bag-of-triangles) and improves on a-MMSB by removing adjacency matrix dependency.  
- Technical quality is strong with clear theoretical justification and thorough complexity analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic and real networks, multiple metrics (NMI, link prediction), and convergence behavior.  
- The parameter sharing strategy is original and well-motivated, distinct from prior inter-role simplifications.  
- Demonstrated practical significance: enables analysis at scales far beyond prior work (e.g., 1M vs. 100K nodes).  
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and accessible to the target audience.  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees for convergence of the stochastic variational algorithm and recovery quality under PTM.  
- Insufficient sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (α, λ, δ, step-size).  
- Comparisons could better include more recent scalable methods (e.g., graph neural network approaches).  
- Real-world applicability questions: behavior on different network types (sparse vs. dense, directed vs. undirected) and δ-subsampling effects.  
- Initialization relies on seeding 2% of nodes or 3-triangle counts; more validation and guidance for no-ground-truth scenarios are needed.  
- Recommendation: accept — strengths (scalability, novelty, evaluation) outweigh weaknesses, with suggested improvements on theory, sensitivity, comparisons, network variants, and initialization.
