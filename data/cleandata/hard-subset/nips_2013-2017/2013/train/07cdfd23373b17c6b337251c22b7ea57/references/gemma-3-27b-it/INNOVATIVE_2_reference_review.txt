This paper tackles a critical problem in network analysis: scaling probabilistic latent space inference to truly large graphs. The core idea – combining a bag-of-triangles representation with a parsimonious statistical model and stochastic variational inference – is compelling and represents a significant departure from existing approaches. The authors correctly identify the limitations of prior work (MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM) and convincingly argue why their approach addresses these limitations. The O(NK) complexity claim is particularly noteworthy and, if true, opens doors to analyzing networks previously intractable for probabilistic methods.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of techniques – parsimonious triangular model and stochastic natural gradient ascent – is genuinely novel. The parameter sharing in the PTM is a clever way to address the scalability issues of previous triangular models.
*   **Scalability:** The claimed O(NK) complexity is a major strength. The experimental results, demonstrating analysis of a 1M-node network in 4 hours, strongly suggest that this claim holds in practice. This is a significant advance.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a clear rationale for each design choice, grounding it in the principles of succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference.
*   **Strong Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The comparison to existing approaches, particularly highlighting the runtime improvements, is convincing.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The authors do a good job of explaining complex concepts in a clear and accessible manner.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Structured Mean-Field Assumption:** While the authors acknowledge the limitations of a naive mean-field approach, the justification for their chosen “structured” approximation feels somewhat hand-wavy. More detailed explanation or justification for the specific structure chosen would be beneficial. The impact of this structure on the accuracy of the approximation isn't thoroughly investigated.
*   **δ-subsampling:** While the authors cite previous work supporting δ-subsampling, the impact of different δ values on performance (beyond the single value tested) isn't explored. A sensitivity analysis would strengthen the results.
*   **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The comparison to MMSB and MMTM is strong, but the paper could benefit from a comparison to more recent scalable network embedding techniques (e.g., node2vec, GraphSAGE, though these are not probabilistic in the same sense). This would better position the work within the broader landscape of network analysis methods.
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:** Details regarding the hyperparameter tuning process (e.g., how α, λ, τ0, τ1, and κ were selected) are somewhat limited.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a genuinely novel and potentially highly impactful approach to scalable latent space inference.
*   The O(NK) complexity claim is backed up by strong experimental evidence.
*   The authors clearly identify and address the limitations of previous work.
*   The paper is well-written and thoroughly evaluated.
*   Even with minor flaws, the potential benefits of this work outweigh the drawbacks.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The justification for the structured mean-field approximation could be stronger.
*   The limited exploration of the δ-subsampling parameter is a minor weakness.
*   Lack of comparison to more recent, non-probabilistic network embedding techniques.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of network analysis. The novelty of the approach, combined with its demonstrated scalability and strong empirical results, makes it a compelling submission. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I believe this paper is a valuable addition to NIPS and **recommend acceptance**. I am willing to overlook the incomplete validations (e.g. limited hyperparameter exploration) given the significant leap in scalability and the innovative nature of the proposed approach. This is precisely the kind of bold, novel work that NIPS should champion.
