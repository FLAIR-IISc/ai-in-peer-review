- Proposes a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle-generating parameters from O(K3) to O(K) by grouping role-triplets into classes (Bxxx, Bxx, B0).  
- Introduces structured mean-field variational inference with stochastic natural-gradient updates over minibatches of triangles.  
- Proposes a “mixture-of-deltas” O(K) per-triangle local update that restricts mass to K diagonal triplets and O(K) random off-diagonals for scalable parallel inference.  
- Demonstrates competitive or better latent-role recovery and link prediction versus MMSB/a-MMSB/MMTM with orders-of-magnitude faster runtimes on large networks.  
- Positions work relative to MMSB, ERGMs, and MMTM, with the main novelty being O(K) parameter sharing and triangle-based motifs.  
- Scalability is a major strength: O(K) parameterization, O(K) local updates, minibatched SVI, and practical memory decisions enable million-node scaling.  
- The parameter-tying by distinct-role cardinality is a simple, effective modeling device that preserves key motif semantics like triadic closure.  
- The structured mean-field and stochastic natural-gradient inference design are appropriate and effective.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic recovery with growing K, strong link prediction on large social graphs, and very fast convergence in wall-clock time.  
- Complexity claims need clarification: ambiguous statements mixing O(NK) and O(|I|K) where |I| = O(Nδ2); request to separate local vs. global per-pass costs.  
- Step-size schedule likely has a typo (κ sign); current reported κ would violate Robbins–Monro conditions—please correct and report actual κ.  
- The mixture-of-deltas resampling changes the variational family across iterations, complicating SVI convergence guarantees; request formalization of the objective (e.g., expected ELBO) and discussion of bias.  
- Request ablation comparing exact O(K3) local updates vs. O(K) approximation for small K to quantify approximation error.  
- Parameter tying limits expressiveness (e.g., cannot model role-specific heterophily/assortativity); ask for discussion, failure cases, and sensitivity analysis.  
- Triangle extraction and δ-subsampling pipeline complexity is understated; request detailed algorithm, runtime proportion vs. inference, and sensitivity to δ.  
- Link prediction protocol is unclear: clarify whether marginalization over third node is exact or sampled, computational cost, and fairness vs. MMSB baselines.  
- Reproducibility concerns: many details in Appendix—request code release or more algorithmic pseudocode and clearer update equations in main text.  
- Overall technically sound, but step-size issue and changing variational family must be clarified; suggest evaluating fully unsupervised initializations as well.  
- Writing and organization are good, but fix κ sign, unify complexity statements, correct duplicated sampling typo, and add a compact table or running example for equivalence classes.  
- The combination of O(K) parameterization and O(K) local SVI is novel and represents an interesting middle ground between MMTM and a-MMSB.  
- High practical significance due to massive speedups at million-node scale while maintaining competitive accuracy.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong scalability, elegant parameter tying, sound SVI framework, and good empirical performance.  
- Cons for acceptance: theoretical/clarity issues (step-size, changing variational family), limited discussion of expressiveness limits, and missing ablations on δ and local approximation.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept leaning to accept, contingent on clarifying step-size, unifying complexity claims, and adding brief ablations/discussion on O(K) approximation and expressiveness trade-offs.
