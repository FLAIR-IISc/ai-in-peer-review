Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space model for large networks based on triangular motifs. It builds on triangular “bag-of-motifs” representations (Ho et al. NIPS’11) to reduce data size relative to edge-based methods, and introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which shares triangle-generation parameters across role configurations to reduce O(K^3) parameters (as in MMTM) to O(K). For inference, the authors develop a structured mean-field stochastic variational inference (SVI) method with natural gradients and a “mixture-of-deltas” local variational approximation to reduce local update cost from O(K^3) to O(K). Experiments report fast convergence and competitive accuracy vs. MMSB (Airoldi et al.) and MMTM (Ho et al.), including large-scale runs (e.g., 1M nodes, 100 roles).

This work is related to MMSB and a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.) in using mixed-membership latent roles; to MMTM in using triangles; to ERGM literature which values higher-order motifs; and to SVI/natural gradients (Hoffman et al., Amari) for scalable inference.

Strengths
- Addresses scalability along both N and K via a well-motivated parameter sharing scheme (O(K) instead of O(K^3)), plus SVI with structured mean-field.
- Clear articulation of why triangular motifs can be competitive and more succinct than edges, with δ-subsampling to control computational cost.
- Practical algorithmic engineering: mini-batches, discarding local variational parameters, parallelization over triangles.
- Empirically promising: claims of orders-of-magnitude speedups on large real networks, and good latent role recovery on synthetic data.

Major concerns (technical correctness and clarity)
1) Inconsistent and possibly incorrect complexity claims:
- Early in the paper you claim an O(NK) time algorithm; later (Sec. 5.2) you state the runtime of your method is O(N δ^2 K). Given |I| ≈ O(N δ^2) triangles and O(K) work per triangle, the per-pass complexity is O(N δ^2 K), not O(NK). The paper should consistently state and justify the true complexity and when the O(NK) claim applies (only global updates).
2) Erroneous local update expression:
- Equation (6) for distinct x, y, z uses Eq[log θi,x + log θj,x + log θk,x]; this appears wrong. For φxyz you should have Eq[log θi,x + log θj,y + log θk,z]. As written, the update overuses x and would severely bias inference. This is a serious technical error unless it is merely a typo; it must be corrected and verified in the Appendix.
3) Questionable unbiasedness claims:
- Stochastic gradients are unbiased only if local variational parameters are exactly optimized for the current global parameters and the bound matches the model’s ELBO. You both (a) optimize only over a restricted “mixture-of-deltas” family, and (b) sample a random set A of off-diagonals each time. Then ES[∇LS] = ∇L is generally false for the original ELBO; at best you optimize a different, random lower bound. Please clarify the target objective and whether the reported gradients are unbiased for it.
4) Stepsize schedule inconsistency:
- You define ρt = τ0(τ1 + t)^(−κ) and state convergence requires Σ ρt = ∞, Σ ρt^2 < ∞, typically satisfied with κ ∈ (0.5, 1]. Yet in experiments you set κ = −0.5, which would make steps grow with t and violate the conditions. This looks like a sign error; please fix and report the actual values used.
5) Missing crucial derivations and details:
- Table 1 and the Appendix are referenced for conditional probabilities and local updates (including the 1/2 and 1/3 factors under equivalence classes). They are necessary to reproduce the method and to verify correctness, but are not provided. The paper should include these derivations.
6) Ambiguity in log-expectations with equivalence factors:
- Notation like log(B0,1/3) is unclear. If you mean log(B0,1) − log 3, please write it explicitly and confirm the expectation is computed correctly under Dirichlet posteriors.
7) Data subsampling and triangle extraction:
- δ-subsampling is said to be O(1) per sample; uniform sampling over incident open/closed triangles generally requires nontrivial preprocessing. Also, node-centric sampling can yield duplicates across high-degree nodes; the paper does not explain deduplication when forming I = {(i, j, k): i<j<k}. Please detail the procedure and its complexity.
8) Link prediction protocol and fairness:
- It is unclear whether triangles are re-extracted after removing 10% of edges; otherwise leakage may occur. Ranking by marginalizing over k is computationally heavy; no complexity or approximations are given. Also, MMSB is disadvantaged on very large graphs due to O(N^2K^2) cost; were stronger scalable edge-based baselines (e.g., a-MMSB with careful negative sampling) included?
9) Initialization bias:
- Seeding 2% of nodes with true roles is unrealistic and can strongly affect convergence. Although given to all methods, it may not reflect typical usage; please report performance from realistic, unsupervised initializations.

Additional clarity and presentation issues
- The model is not a generative model of networks. Please discuss implications for link prediction and whether your scoring function is consistent with the generative assumptions.
- Identifiability and label-swapping among Bxx and Bxxx across roles are not discussed.
- Memory and runtime for the link prediction marginalization over k are not reported.
- The claim “converges in 2–5 passes” needs a precise definition of a “pass” when using mini-batches and resampling A.

Formatting and references
- Many references are incomplete or malformed (missing years, venues, titles truncated). Please fix to NeurIPS standards.
- Inconsistent notation spacing (Dirichlet (α) vs Dirichlet(α)), Discrete vs Categorical.
- Several footnotes are embedded mid-paragraph; consider moving to an Appendix or clarifying inline.

Pros
- Well-motivated parameter sharing that reduces K^3 to K parameters.
- Structured mean-field over triangle roles; practical O(K) local approximation.
- Strong empirical speedups and scale.

Cons
- Apparent technical errors (Eq. (6); stepsize κ).
- Inconsistent and overstated complexity claims.
- Unclear unbiasedness and objective under the restricted local family.
- Missing derivations (Table 1, Appendix), incomplete experimental details.
- Evaluation protocols and baselines need strengthening.

Recommendation
Reject. The core idea is promising and potentially impactful, but the paper in its current form has several technical inaccuracies, inconsistencies, and missing details that undermine confidence in correctness and reproducibility. If the authors fix the errors (especially Eq. (6) and stepsize), reconcile complexity claims, provide full derivations, clarify the SVI objective under the restricted family, and strengthen the evaluation, this work could be competitive.
