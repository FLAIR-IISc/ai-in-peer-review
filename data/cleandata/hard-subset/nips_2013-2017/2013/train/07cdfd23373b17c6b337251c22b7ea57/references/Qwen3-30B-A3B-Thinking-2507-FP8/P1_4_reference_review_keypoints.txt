- Proposes a scalable approach (PTM + stochastic variational inference) that reduces triangle-generating parameters from O(K³) to O(K), enabling O(NK) inference.  
- Combines triangular motif representation, parsimonious parameterization, and SVI to scale latent space inference to million-node networks.  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability: comparable or better accuracy than baselines and ability to analyze 1M-node networks in hours.  
- Provides solid theoretical justification for the parameter reduction and a clear derivation of the core contribution.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across synthetic and real networks, with thorough baseline comparisons (MMSB, MMTM Gibbs, PTM Gibbs).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and accessible, with detailed algorithm description sufficient for replication.  
- Distinguishes clearly from prior work (a-MMSB and MMTM) and makes a non-trivial advancement over those methods.  
- Significant practical impact: orders-of-magnitude speedup while maintaining latent recovery and link-prediction performance.  
- Strengths include clear problem framing, strong theoretical foundation, and extensive empirical validation.  
- Limitation: triangular representation does not capture all network information, potentially missing structure in some networks.  
- Limitation: mixture-of-deltas approximation for local updates lacks theoretical justification and needs more discussion.  
- Limitation: initialization that seeds 2% of nodes with true roles may be unrealistic in practice; robustness to this is not explored.  
- Limitation: paper does not evaluate performance when network structure violates triangular representation assumptions.  
- Recommendation: accept — high-quality, novel, well-executed work with significant impact potential.  
- Suggestion: briefly discuss extensions to dynamic networks or incorporating additional network features beyond triangles.
