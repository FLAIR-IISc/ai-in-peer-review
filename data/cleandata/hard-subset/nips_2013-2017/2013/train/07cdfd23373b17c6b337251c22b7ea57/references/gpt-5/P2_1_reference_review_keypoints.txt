- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that models networks using a bag-of-triangles and ties triangle parameters to reduce global parameters from O(K3) to O(K).
- Introduces a mixture-of-deltas structured variational family and SVI with natural gradients to enable O(K) local updates and scalable inference.
- Empirically converges in a few passes and scales to 1M nodes and 100 roles on one machine, with good synthetic latent recovery and improved link prediction vs MMSB/MMTM.

- Parameter-tying scheme and structured mean-field grouping are reasonable and clearly specified.
- The unbiased gradient claim is misleading: it holds only if local variational parameters are exactly optimized, but the mixture-of-deltas optimizes a different (modified) bound and should be clarified.
- Reported step-size schedule contains a sign/typo (κ = −0.5 would increase steps) and must be corrected and matched to code.
- Complexity claims are inconsistent (O(NK) vs O(Nδ2K)); per-pass cost is O(Nδ2K) plus O(NK) for globals under δ-subsampling and sensitivity to δ should be detailed.
- The model is intentionally triangle-only (not full network generative), limiting likelihood-based tasks; this trade-off should be explicitly discussed.

- Missing head-to-head stochastic variational a-MMSB comparisons on the same hardware/datasets weakens empirical claims.
- Latent-space recovery used 2% seeded ground-truth roles for initialization, which may bias results — include random initializations and variance across runs.
- Link prediction metric is unclear (AUC vs accuracy) and comparisons may be unfair since PTM ranks triangles while MMSB ranks edges.

- Paper is generally well written and the equivalence-class parameter-tying argument is intuitive.
- Need to fix step-size typo, state theoretically valid ranges, and report actual schedule used.
- Must explicitly state that SVI with mixture-of-deltas optimizes a modified bound and explain stability when resampling the A set each iteration.
- Provide exact per-iteration complexity in terms of N, K, δ, mini-batch size, and threads, and distinguish “per pass” from “wall-clock”.
- Report triangle extraction time and memory footprint for large graphs and detail behavior on high-degree networks.
- Move key local-update equations (e.g., φxxx, φxxy) from the appendix into the main text or summarize them.

- Builds on prior work (bag-of-triangles, parameter tying, SVI) but novelty lies in combining triangle modeling with O(K) parameterization and O(K) local-update approximation preserving triangle dependencies.
- Contribution is incremental but well engineered and addresses an important K-scaling bottleneck.

- High practical significance: scales to millions of nodes and hundreds of roles on a single multicore machine with competitive or better accuracy.
- Parameter reduction likely improves generalization in high-K regimes and the approach is extensible (e.g., low-rank tensor parameterizations).

- Pros: principled parameter tying reducing O(K3) to O(K); structured SVI with efficient O(K) local updates; natural gradients; strong scalability (1M nodes, 100 roles); good synthetic recovery and improved link prediction; practical engineering (mini-batching, parallelization).
- Cons: theoretical clarity gaps (step-size, unbiasedness claim); expressivity trade-offs from collapsing distinct-role interactions into B0; evaluation limitations (seeded init, missing a-MMSB SVI comparison, unclear metrics and extraction costs).

- Requests: correct and clarify step-size schedule and convergence guarantees under the actual settings.
- Requests: ablation on size/composition of A set in mixture-of-deltas and its accuracy/speed trade-offs.
- Requests: add results from random initializations and report variance across runs.
- Requests: provide head-to-head a-MMSB SVI comparisons on same datasets/hardware.
- Requests: report triangle extraction time and memory per dataset and discuss δ sensitivity and high-degree graph behavior.
- Suggestion: compare to low-rank tensor parameterizations (e.g., CP) as a middle ground between O(K3) and O(K).

- Overall: addresses an important scalability problem with a well-motivated model and efficient inference; leans toward accept contingent on clarifications and stronger empirical comparisons.
