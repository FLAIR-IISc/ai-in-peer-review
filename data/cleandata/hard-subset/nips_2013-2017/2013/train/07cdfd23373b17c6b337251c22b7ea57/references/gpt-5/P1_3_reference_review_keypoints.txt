- Paper proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM): triangle-based latent-role model with parameter tying to reduce parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) and scalable SVI inference.
- Uses structured mean-field posterior over per-triangle role triplets and a “mixture-of-deltas” approximation to achieve O(K) per-triangle local updates.
- Builds on prior work: adopts mixed-membership ideas (MMSB/a-MMSB), improves over MMTM by parameter tying and scalable SVI, and leverages SVI/natural gradient methodology.
- Major strength: strong scalability — O(K) parameterization and O(K) local updates enable handling 1M nodes with 100 roles on a single multi-core machine.
- Strength: triangle-based modeling captures higher-order structure and is empirically faithful while remaining compact.
- Strength: inference design (structured variational family and mixture-of-deltas) avoids rank-1 MF limits and is simple and effective.
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation showing fast convergence (2–5 passes) and competitive or better performance than MMSB/MMTM on recovery and link prediction.
- Strength: practical considerations (δ-subsampling, memory, parallelization, step-size scheduling) are discussed clearly.
- Expressiveness concern: parameter tying (single B0 and Bxx) may overly restrict triangle semantics; accuracy gap vs. MMTM on small K should be quantified.
- Local update concern: mixture-of-deltas uses a random subset A that changes per iteration, so the variational bound is randomized and not guaranteed to monotonically improve; needs theoretical justification and ablation on A’s size/selection.
- Step-size/Convergence bug: reported κ = −0.5 would increase step sizes and violate standard stochastic approximation conditions; authors should correct and report actual κ used.
- Global update complexity unclear: need clarification whether γ is updated only for minibatch nodes (with scaling) and explicit per-iteration vs per-pass complexity.
- Evaluation scope limited: comparisons largely to MMSB; should include MMTM (on small graphs), degree-corrected SBMs, and other scalable community/embedding methods plus δ-subsampling sensitivity analysis.
- Reproducibility issues: some critical update equations and referenced tables are deferred or missing; include all formulas and exact natural-gradient sufficient statistics for reproducibility.
- Quality: technically sound with careful SVI integration, but main gaps are step-size typo and limited theory for randomized local variational family.
- Clarity: generally clear and well organized, but needs fixes to step-size exposition, complete local-update formulas, and global update clarification.
- Originality: moderate–strong — novelty in O(K) parameter tying for triangles and O(K) local updates via restricted variational support.
- Significance: high practical impact for large-scale latent-space inference; offers orders-of-magnitude speedups with competitive accuracy.
- Pros summary: elegant O(K) parameterization, scalable parallel SVI, fast convergence, and convincing large-scale experiments.
- Cons summary: limited theoretical justification for randomized local restriction, potential expressiveness loss vs MMTM, step-size/implementation details deferred, and narrow comparisons/limited δ-subsampling analysis.
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on addressing clarifications (notably step-size correction and stronger justification/ablation for the local approximation).
