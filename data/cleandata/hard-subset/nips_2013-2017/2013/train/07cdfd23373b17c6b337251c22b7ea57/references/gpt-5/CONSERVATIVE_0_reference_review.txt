Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), a latent-space mixed-membership model defined over a bag-of-triangles representation of a network. To address scalability, the authors (i) replace the O(K^3) triangle-generating tensor in MMTM with an O(K) parameterization via parameter sharing across equivalence classes of role triplets; (ii) develop a structured stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with natural gradients; and (iii) approximate the per-triangle local updates with a “mixture-of-deltas” variational family to reduce O(K^3) to O(K) per triangle. They combine this with δ-subsampling of triangles. Empirically, they report faster convergence than MMSB, MMTM Gibbs, and show results up to 1M nodes, K=100 on YouTube.

This work builds directly on MMTM (Ho et al. NIPS), MMSB (Airoldi et al.), and a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.). The parameter-sharing idea mirrors a-MMSB’s reduction of O(K^2) to O(K) for inter-block links. The use of SVI and natural gradients follows Hoffman et al. The triangular representation and δ-subsampling also follow Ho et al.

Strengths
- Clear motivation for triangle-based representations and a well-articulated scalability goal.
- The O(K) parameter sharing is simple, intuitive, and practically impactful; it plausibly reduces overfitting relative to O(K^3) tensors.
- Structured SVI with natural gradients is appropriate; mini-batching over motifs enables parallelism and memory savings.
- Empirical results suggest substantial runtime improvements and show feasibility on million-node graphs.
- The paper is generally well written and explains the equivalence classes and updates (though Table/Appendix details are critical).

Weaknesses and concerns
- Modeling expressiveness: PTM is not a generative model of networks and models only connected triples, discarding empty/single-edge triples and not enforcing edge consistency across overlapping triangles. The paper acknowledges this but does not quantify when the loss of information materially affects tasks. It also assumes shared parameters Bxx across all minority roles, which may induce underfitting; no theoretical approximation guarantees or identifiability analysis are provided.
- Approximate local updates: The “mixture-of-deltas” restriction and random selection of off-diagonal configurations A are pragmatic, but the claim that “any choice of A yields a valid lower bound” is insufficient. Because A is re-sampled per update, the optimized local bound changes across iterations; unbiasedness of the stochastic natural gradient and monotonicity of the ELBO are not established. No ablation compares exact structured local updates vs. the mixture-of-deltas approximation to quantify accuracy loss.
- Complexity claims: The abstract and several sections assert O(NK) time, but elsewhere the algorithm is O(N δ^2 K), which can be large for modest δ. The runtime reported includes 4–8 threads, while baselines are single-threaded; comparisons to a-MMSB (the most relevant scalable baseline) are not run under the same environment, relying instead on numbers from the literature. Overall, scalability claims would be stronger with apples-to-apples wall-clock comparisons and standardized threading.
- Baselines and fairness: Key comparisons are missing or weak. a-MMSB is not evaluated directly. MMTM/PTM Gibbs are single-threaded; MMSB batch is known to be O(N^2 K^2) and uncompetitive at this scale. Initialization on synthetic data uses 2 ground-truth seeds per role for all methods—a strong and unrealistic prior that can dominate outcomes. No experiments evaluate unsupervised initialization quality or sensitivity.
- Triangle extraction cost and δ-subsampling: Triangle enumeration/sampling on large graphs is nontrivial; the claim that extraction is <1% of inference time needs empirical evidence. Sensitivity to δ, and how δ relative to degree distribution affects accuracy and bias (especially around hubs), is not analyzed.
- Evaluation breadth: Link prediction is evaluated, but methodology for summing over all third nodes k is not detailed, and computational cost is unclear. No results are shown on graphs with low clustering where triangle statistics may be less informative. Lack of variance/error bars and statistical significance tests.

Quality
Technically competent overall, but several crucial claims (objective lower bound with randomized A, unbiased natural gradients under the approximate local family, O(NK) vs O(N δ^2 K), triangle extraction overhead) are asserted rather than established. Empirical validation lacks key baselines and ablations, and relies on favorable initialization.

Clarity
Generally clear, but important pieces are deferred (e.g., Table 1, Appendix with update details). The complexity discussion needs to be unified and precise about dependencies on δ and the number of triangles. More detail is needed on triangle extraction and the exact link prediction scoring.

Originality
Moderate. The parameter-sharing scheme is an adaptation of ideas from a-MMSB to the triangular setting; the use of SVI/natural gradients is standard. The “mixture-of-deltas” local approximation is a practical contribution but not deeply analyzed.

Significance
Potentially useful for practitioners needing scalable probabilistic latent role inference with triangle information. However, without stronger theoretical support, fair comparisons, and ablations, it is difficult to assess robustness and generality beyond social networks with high clustering.

Questions for the authors
1) Provide a proof or detailed argument that the mixture-of-deltas local family with randomly re-sampled A still yields an overall valid ELBO and unbiased natural gradients for SVI.
2) Clarify the true asymptotic complexity: is the total cost O(m K) with m ~ O(N δ^2), and under what δ scaling does this reduce to O(NK)?
3) Report triangle extraction times and memory, and sensitivity to δ on accuracy/speed.
4) Include controlled, multithreaded comparisons to a-MMSB and a recent MMTM-style SVI (if available), plus ablations of exact vs. approximate local updates.
5) Evaluate unsupervised initializations and sensitivity; remove seeded ground-truth.
6) Test on low-clustering graphs and provide negative results if applicable. Report multiple runs with variance.

Pros
- Addresses an important scalability bottleneck with a plausible parameter reduction.
- Practical SVI design with parallelism and low memory.
- Promising speed and some accuracy gains on social graphs.

Cons
- Insufficient theoretical justification for key approximation; unclear convergence properties.
- Missing strong baselines and fair runtime comparisons.
- Overstated/ambiguous complexity claims; triangle extraction overhead not substantiated.
- Heavy reliance on seeded initialization; limited analysis of failure modes.

Recommendation: Reject (borderline). The idea is interesting and potentially impactful, but the paper needs stronger theoretical backing for the approximate inference, more rigorous and fair experimental comparisons (including a-MMSB), ablations, and clearer complexity/runtime accounting to meet NIPS standards.

Confidence: High.
