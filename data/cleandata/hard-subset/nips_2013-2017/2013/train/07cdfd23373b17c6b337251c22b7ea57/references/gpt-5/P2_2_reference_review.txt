Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent space model for large networks by building on the bag-of-triangles representation (Ho et al., NIPS’12). The key modeling contribution is the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which shares triangle-generating parameters across equivalence classes of role triplets (all-same, two-same-one-different, all-different), reducing parameters from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K). The inferential contribution is a structured mean-field stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with natural gradients and mini-batched triangles. A further practical approximation uses a “mixture-of-deltas” variational family to reduce per-triangle local updates from O(K^3) to O(K). Empirically, the method converges in a few passes and scales to 1M nodes, outperforming MMSB and MMTM in speed while maintaining or improving accuracy on synthetic latent role recovery and link prediction on several real networks. The work connects to MMSB (Airoldi et al.), a-MMSB (Gopalan et al., NIPS), SVI (Hoffman et al., JMLR), and prior triangle-based modeling (Ho et al., NIPS’12).

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The PTM’s parameter sharing is clearly defined via role-triplet equivalence classes and yields a tractable O(K) global parameterization. The structured variational family for triangle-specific role assignments addresses the inadequacy of naive mean-field (rank-1) for triadic dependencies. The SVI procedure, with mini-batching and natural gradients, is standard and appropriate. The mixture-of-deltas restriction for local updates is a sensible and effective approximation.
- Claims vs. support: The scalability and convergence claims are supported by complexity analysis and experiments showing 2–5 passes to convergence, and a 1M-node, K=100 run in ~4 hours on 8 threads. Accuracy claims are supported on synthetic data generated from MMSB and a power-law latent-space generator, and by link-prediction results on real networks. However:
  - The “unbiased gradient” statement applies to the objective with exact local maximization; when the local family is restricted (mixture-of-deltas), the algorithm optimizes a different lower bound. This is fine in principle, but the text should be explicit that the unbiasedness refers to the restricted objective, not the original L(η,γ).
  - Evaluation relies on δ-subsampling; while prior work argues it approximately preserves triangle distributions, a sensitivity analysis to δ would strengthen the empirical case.
  - Initialization seeds 2% of nodes with true roles for all methods; while controlled, this gives the model helpful side information that may not be available in practice. An ablation with unsupervised initializations (the heuristic the authors suggest) would be informative.
  - Baseline coverage is limited to MMSB and MMTM/PTM Gibbs; additional comparisons to other scalable overlapping-community or role models (e.g., nonparametric latent feature models; or at least the a-MMSB implementation on the same hardware) would improve the empirical foundation.
- Rigor and honesty: The paper clearly states that PTM is not a generative model of full networks and motivates the bag-of-triangles choice; it also explains why naive mean-field fails.

Clarity:
- The paper is overall well-written and organized. The generative process, equivalence classes, and parameter sharing are clearly explained with helpful intuitions. The SVI algorithm is described at the right level, with derivations deferred to the appendix.
- Suggestions:
  - Be explicit about the objective being optimized under the mixture-of-deltas restriction and clarify theoretical guarantees (convergence to a stationary point of the restricted bound).
  - Include the missing Table 1 probabilities in the main text or a concise summary; it is central to understanding PTM.
  - Provide more details on the link-prediction scoring: computational cost of marginalizing over k, any approximations used, and how triangle-only modeling interfaces with edge prediction.

Originality:
- The main novelty lies in (i) introducing a parsimonious, symmetry-based parameter sharing scheme for triangle-role configurations, reducing O(K^3) to O(K), and (ii) combining structured SVI with an O(K) local-update approximation tailored to triangles. While parameter sharing ideas exist (e.g., a-MMSB’s inter/intra-role tying), the specific application to triangular motifs and the mixture-of-deltas local family are new and practically important. The work is an incremental but meaningful advance over Ho et al. (NIPS’12) and complements scalable MMSB variants.

Significance:
- The paper addresses a central bottleneck in probabilistic network modeling: scaling to millions of nodes and many roles. Demonstrating a 1M-node, K=100 run in hours on a single machine, with competitive or better accuracy than common baselines, is impactful. The approach will be useful to practitioners needing interpretable mixed-membership embeddings and to researchers exploring higher-order network representations. The ideas (parameter tying by symmetry, structured SVI with mini-batched motifs) are likely to be built upon.

Pros:
- Clear, principled reduction of parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via symmetry-based tying.
- Well-motivated structured SVI with natural gradients and an effective O(K) local approximation.
- Strong scalability with convincing wall-clock and pass-to-convergence results, including million-node data.
- Competitive or improved accuracy on latent role recovery and link prediction.
- Practical engineering choices (mini-batching triangles, discarding local vars between iterations) that reduce memory and enable parallelization.

Cons:
- Restricted expressivity: tying all “two-same-one-different” cases to a single Bxx may miss heterophilous patterns that depend on the specific minority role; a discussion or empirical check on datasets with strong heterophily would help.
- Theoretical subtlety: the unbiased gradient claim should be aligned with the restricted variational family; current wording can mislead.
- Limited baselines and lack of head-to-head runs against a-MMSB under identical settings; results for competing methods on large graphs are partly borrowed from prior papers.
- Dependence on δ-subsampling and seeding initialization; more ablations on sensitivity and unsupervised starts would strengthen the case.

Recommendation and justification:
Overall, this is a solid and timely contribution that advances scalable probabilistic latent space inference by exploiting motif-level structure and principled parameter tying. The paper meets the NeurIPS criteria well: it is technically sound with reasonable clarity, introduces nontrivial ideas beyond prior NIPS work on triangles and MMSB, and demonstrates significant practical impact. I recommend acceptance, contingent on clarifying the SVI objective under the local approximation, expanding baseline comparisons where feasible, and adding sensitivity analyses for δ and initialization.
