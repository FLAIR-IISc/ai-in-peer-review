- Paper proposes scalable latent space inference for very large networks using a "bag-of-triangles" representation plus a parsimonious parameterization (PTM).  
- PTM reduces triangle-generating parameters from O(K³) (MMTM) to O(K) via parameter sharing by grouping configurations.  
- Authors develop a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity.  
- Empirical claim: method handles 1M-node networks in ~4 hours on a single machine, far faster than prior methods.  
- Builds on MMTM, MMSB/a-MMSB, and stochastic variational inference but makes a novel scalability contribution.  
- Technical development is sound, with a clear generative model and logically motivated parameter sharing.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic and real networks, multiple baselines, and appropriate metrics (NMI, link prediction).  
- Reproducibility is supported with implementation details, hyperparameters, and clear figures.  
- Main theoretical gap: no formal analysis of approximation error from the "mixture-of-deltas" local update (empirically effective but not theoretically bounded).  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and accessible.  
- Originality: novel O(K) parameterization is a significant technical innovation addressing a key bottleneck.  
- Significance: enables latent-space inference on million-node networks, with strong practical impact for social and recommendation systems.  
- Strengths: clear problem identification, novel PTM, orders-of-magnitude speedup, practical implementation, balanced theory and experiments.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical analysis of approximation error; comparison with MMSB may be unfair (edge- vs triangle-based); subsampling δ trade-offs not fully explored; no 10M+ node results.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, well-executed contribution with important practical implications despite minor theoretical gaps.
