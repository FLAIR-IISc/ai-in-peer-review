Summary
This paper tackles scalable probabilistic latent space inference on large networks by combining (i) a succinct bag-of-triangles representation, (ii) a parsimonious generative model (PTM) that ties triangle parameters to reduce dimensionality from O(K^3) to O(K), and (iii) a stochastic variational inference procedure with structured mean-field, natural gradients, and an O(K) per-triangle local update via a “mixture-of-deltas” approximation. The paper argues that triangle-based representations preserve informative higher-order structure while being much sparser than O(N^2) edge representations. Empirically, the method converges in a few passes, scales to 1M nodes and 100 roles on a single machine in hours, and yields competitive or improved latent role recovery and link prediction against MMSB and MMTM baselines.

Relation to prior work
- Builds directly on the mixed-membership triangular model (MMTM) and the triangle/motif-based representation (Ho et al., NIPS’12), replacing the O(K^3) triangle tensor with parameter tying by role-equivalence classes (B0, Bxx, Bxxx), akin to the a-MMSB simplification (Gopalan et al., NIPS’12) that reduces O(K^2) link parameters to O(K).
- Uses stochastic variational inference with natural gradients (Hoffman et al., JMLR) and structured mean-field for triplets; proposes a practical O(K) local update by restricting support to diagonals and a randomly refreshed set of off-diagonals.

Quality
Strengths
- Sound modeling idea: the parameter tying is well-motivated by symmetry/equivalence in triangle configurations; it substantially reduces variance and complexity.
- The structured mean-field form for (si,jk, sj,ik, sk,ij) is appropriate; the naive mean-field failure mode is correctly identified.
- The SVI machinery is standard and appropriate; mini-batching triangles gives an unbiased gradient estimator for the fixed variational family.
- Implementation details are pragmatic: re-picking the off-diagonal set A per triangle/update to avoid bias, discarding local variational tensors to save memory, and parallelizing over triangles.
- Experiments convincingly demonstrate scalability and fast convergence (2–5 passes), and provide both synthetic latent-role recovery and real-data link prediction, including million-node runs.

Weaknesses/technical concerns
- Convergence guarantees are stated for SVI with exact local optima in a fixed variational family. The additional “mixture-of-deltas” restriction with a randomly changing support A makes the global objective time-varying; no theoretical discussion is given on whether the resulting gradient estimates remain unbiased for any fixed objective or whether convergence still follows. Empirically it works, but some discussion or a lemma bounding the approximation gap or showing ascent for each fixed A would strengthen the paper.
- There is an apparent step-size typo: ρt = τ0(τ1 + t)−κ with κ ∈ (0.5,1] is standard; the experiments state κ = −0.5, which would make step-sizes grow with t. Please clarify; likely κ = 0.5.
- Complexity claims are inconsistent. The intro and Section 3/4 emphasize O(NK), but the per-pass cost scales with the number of (subsampled) triangles, i.e., O(|I| K) ≈ O(N δ^2 K). Later the experiments acknowledge O(N δ^2 K). Please state this clearly up front to avoid confusion.
- The expressivity trade-off from parameter tying is not analyzed. In heterogeneous graphs where probabilities differ across specific (x,y,z) triples with two equal roles, PTM could underfit. A brief discussion, or an ablation comparing PTM to a low-rank or hierarchical variant, would help.
- The link prediction evaluation compares mainly to MMSB batch VI. Given the claims of orders-of-magnitude scalability, a comparison to a-MMSB (or other scalable baselines) on at least one medium-scale network would be informative. For network embedding baselines (e.g., later methods), time of writing may preclude, but a-MMSB is directly relevant.
- Details on how edge probabilities are computed efficiently from triangle probabilities (marginalizing over k and roles) are light; a complexity analysis or caching strategy would help reproducibility.

Clarity
- Generally well-written and organized; the role-equivalence partition and induced equivalence classes of triangle types are clearly explained with helpful intuition.
- Algorithm 1 and the decomposition of the ELBO into global/local terms are clear; missing details are deferred to the appendix.
- Minor issues:
  - Fix the κ sign as noted.
  - Be consistent about the overall complexity (O(N δ^2 K) versus O(NK)).
  - Provide the exact updates for φxxx and φxxy in the main text or a clearer pointer.

Originality
- Moderate but meaningful. The key novelty is the O(K) parameter tying applied to triangle-based latent role models plus a structured SVI with an O(K) local approximation. The tying idea is analogous to a-MMSB, but its application in the triangular/motif setting and the specific structured variational design appear new. The mixture-of-deltas trick is a pragmatic contribution.

Significance
- High practical significance: the method scales latent role inference to million-node graphs with hundreds of roles on a single machine, with compelling speedups over prior probabilistic models while maintaining accuracy.
- The triangle-centric view aligns with known importance of triadic closure; demonstrating that it can be leveraged in a scalable probabilistic framework is valuable.
- Potential to influence subsequent work on higher-order network modeling and efficient variational approximations.

Pros
- Strong scalability with clear engineering choices; parallelizable; memory-efficient.
- Competitive or better accuracy on synthetic latent-role recovery; solid link prediction on real networks.
- Clear, intuitive model that ties parameters by symmetry.

Cons
- Theoretical gap regarding convergence with the randomized restricted local variational family.
- Limited comparisons to the most relevant scalable probabilistic baseline (a-MMSB).
- Some clarity issues (step-size, complexity) and missing derivations in the main text.

Questions for authors
- Please clarify the κ value used in practice and ensure it meets SVI conditions.
- Can you provide any guarantee (or empirical study) on the effect of the random A selection (size, composition) on the ELBO and convergence?
- How sensitive are results to δ, α, λ, and mini-batch size? Any guidance for practitioners?
- How do you compute link probabilities efficiently at scale when marginalizing over k?
- How does PTM perform on low-clustering networks where triangles are scarce?

Recommendation
I lean toward acceptance. The paper addresses an important problem, introduces a principled and effective simplification of a prior triangular model, and delivers substantial empirical advances in scalability with reasonable accuracy. Tightening the convergence discussion for the local approximation, fixing the step-size issue, and clarifying complexity would further strengthen an already strong submission.

Confidence: high.
