- Paper proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) combining bag-of-triangles, parameter sharing, and stochastic natural gradient ascent for scalable latent-space inference.
- PTM reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) via novel parameter sharing.
- Uses succinct network representation (bag-of-triangles) and parsimonious statistical modeling to enable scalability.
- Inference uses stochastic variational inference with natural gradients for scalability.
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups and competitive or improved accuracy, including inference on a 1M-node YouTube network with 100 roles in ~4 hours.
- Builds on and compares favorably to prior work (MMSB/a-MMSB and MMTM), addressing their scalability and parameter explosion issues.
- Correctly situates method within literature on stochastic variational inference, natural gradients, and network motifs/triadic closure.
- Strength: directly tackles a critical scalability bottleneck in network analysis with a technically sound approach.
- Strength: the parameter-sharing idea is clever and substantially reduces model complexity.
- Strength: strong empirical validation and detailed ablation comparisons against MMTM and MMSB.
- Strength: clear writing, logical organization, and sound theoretical justification for the inference approach.
- Weakness: explanation of stochastic natural gradient ascent is not sufficiently accessible or intuitive for all readers.
- Weakness: sensitivity to chosen mini-batch size (10%) is not analyzed or justified.
- Weakness: impact of δ-subsampling on accuracy and guidelines for choosing δ are underexplored.
- Weakness: O(K) local update approximation’s trade-off between accuracy and speed is not rigorously quantified.
- Weakness: experiments lack diversity across a broader range of real-world network types and characteristics.
- For acceptance: novel, effective, and practically relevant scalability improvements with strong empirical support.
- Against acceptance: needs clearer exposition of natural gradients, more analysis of mini-batch/δ choices, and broader evaluations.
- Overall assessment: strong paper that merits acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity and sensitivity analyses.
- Rating & recommendation: Strong Accept; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (add natural gradient explanation, mini-batch/δ sensitivity analysis, broader network evaluations).
