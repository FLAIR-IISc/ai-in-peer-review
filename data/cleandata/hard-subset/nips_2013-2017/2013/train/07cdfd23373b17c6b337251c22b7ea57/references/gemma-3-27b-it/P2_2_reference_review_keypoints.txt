- Tackles scalable latent space inference for very large networks (millions of nodes).
- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation.
- Introduces parameter sharing to reduce dimensionality and enable scalability to many roles K.
- Uses stochastic natural gradient ascent for variational inference with O(NK) complexity.
- Demonstrates competitive accuracy and orders-of-magnitude speedups on synthetic and real datasets.
- Clearly situates work relative to MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM and prior triangular representation [8].
- Leverages and appropriately cites stochastic variational inference and natural gradient literature.
- Scalability is the core contribution, enabling inference on networks previously intractable.
- Novel parameter-sharing strategy effectively balances expressiveness and compactness.
- Thorough experimental evaluation with strong baselines and realistic datasets.
- Paper is well-written and detailed enough for replication by knowledgeable readers.
- Practical impact is high due to demonstrated speedups on real-world networks.
- Potential bias from the mini-batch (O(K)) approximation is acknowledged but not deeply analyzed.
- Reliance on δ-subsampling lacks systematic sensitivity analysis across δ values.
- Justification for the structured mean-field approximation is somewhat hand-wavy and could be stronger.
- Limited theoretical analysis of convergence for stochastic natural gradient under the structured mean-field setup.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance with revisions.
- Arguments for acceptance: scalability, accuracy, novel parameter sharing, clear presentation, practical importance.
- Arguments against acceptance: possible mini-batch bias, δ-subsampling sensitivity, and need for stronger theory.
- Reviewer rating: Strong Accept (8/10) with recommendation for minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
