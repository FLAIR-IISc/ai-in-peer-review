- Summary: Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles and stochastic natural gradient to reduce parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), enabling inference on million-node networks with hundreds of roles.

- Strength — tackles crucial problem: Directly addresses scalability in probabilistic latent space inference for very large networks.

- Strength — novel technical contribution: PTM’s parameter sharing is an effective dimensionality reduction without obvious loss of expressiveness.

- Strength — novel algorithm: Introduces a fast stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm tailored for the model.

- Strength — strong empirical validation: Demonstrates faster convergence and comparable/improved accuracy versus a-MMSB and MMTM, including 4-hour inference on a 1M-node YouTube network.

- Strength — clear exposition: Technical details and variational update steps are well explained and understandable.

- Strength — solid context: Effectively situates the work relative to prior network modeling methods and their limitations.

- Weakness — algorithm complexity: The proposed algorithm is intricate, involving multiple steps and approximations that increase implementation complexity.

- Weakness — approximation discussion limited: Paper lacks thorough analysis of the impact of δ-subsampling and mixture-of-deltas approximations on accuracy and robustness.

- Weakness — initialization dependence: Performance appears sensitive to careful initialization, suggesting potential fragility.

- Weakness — limited baseline comparisons: Comparisons omit some recent scalable methods (e.g., graph-embedding–based approaches) that would strengthen empirical evaluation.

- Weakness — theoretical guarantees lacking: Paper provides intuition but not rigorous convergence or approximation-quality guarantees.

- Qualitative — quality: Method is technically sound and experimentally well-supported.

- Qualitative — clarity: Generally well-written, though algorithmic complexity requires careful reading.

- Qualitative — originality: Combination of PTM and stochastic natural gradient is novel and significant.

- Qualitative — significance: Work could substantially expand applicability of probabilistic latent models to large-scale networks.

- For acceptance — solves critical problem: Provides a practical solution to a major scalability bottleneck in network analysis.

- For acceptance — technically sound and novel: Introduces new model/algorithmic ideas with strong empirical support.

- For acceptance — strong empirical gains: Demonstrates substantial scalability and competitive accuracy versus state-of-the-art.

- Against acceptance — accessibility concerns: Complex algorithm may limit adoption and reproducibility.

- Against acceptance — insufficient approximation analysis: Needs deeper investigation of approximation effects and sensitivity.

- Against acceptance — missing theory: Lacks stronger theoretical analysis of convergence and approximation quality.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable contributions; strengths outweigh weaknesses.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Revision suggestions: Add sensitivity analysis for δ-subsampling, explore more robust initialization strategies, discuss method limitations in specific networks, and include brief theoretical discussion.
