- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM): scalable latent-space model using a bag-of-triangles with O(K) triangle-generating parameters and mixed-membership node roles.
- Inference uses stochastic variational inference with structured mean-field over triangle role triplets and stochastic natural-gradient updates.
- Introduces a “mixture-of-deltas” approximation restricting per-triangle variational support to O(K) combinations for efficiency.
- Empirical claims: scales to ~1M nodes and K=100 on a single multi-core machine with competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction vs MMSB VI and MMTM Gibbs.
- Related to MMSB/a-MMSB (parameter sharing), motif-based models/MMTM, and ERGMs; parameter-sharing idea is adapted from a-MMSB to triangle motifs.
- Lacks deeper comparisons to degree-corrected SBMs, latent distance models, and strong non-probabilistic baselines (BigCLAM, Node2Vec).
- Strength: O(K) parameterization plus mini-batch SVI and structured variational family yields strong scalability.
- Strength: Bag-of-triangles motif representation is well-motivated (triadic closure, ERGM statistics) and compact for sparse graphs.
- Strength: Structured mean-field captures local dependencies better than naive mean-field rank-1 approximations.
- Strength: Practical engineering (dropping local params between iterations, δ-subsampling, parallelization) supports large-scale runs.
- Strength: Empirical runtime advantages often orders of magnitude while maintaining accuracy against probabilistic baselines.
- Weakness: Unclear/unqualified unbiasedness claim — mixture-of-deltas changes the ELBO, so gradients are unbiased only for the restricted family; need clarification on A resampling’s effect on the objective.
- Weakness: Inconsistent complexity claims (O(NK) vs O(Nδ2K)); need explicit, consistent per-iteration and per-pass cost statements and separation of local vs global costs.
- Weakness: Step-size schedule typo — κ listed as −0.5 would increase step sizes; should confirm κ ∈ (0.5,1] and report actual values used.
- Weakness: Typo in Eq. (6) — expectation term combines incorrect role indices and should sum per-node expectations with correct indices.
- Weakness: Expressivity trade-off — PTM collapses distinctions in patterns like (x,x,y) and (x,y,z), potentially harming accuracy when role interactions are heterogeneous; needs discussion or ablation.
- Weakness: Generative mismatch — triangles are generated independently; for link prediction must ensure held-out edges are removed before triangle extraction and clarify marginalization cost over all k; compare to heuristics (Common Neighbors, Adamic–Adar) and embedding baselines.
- Weakness: Sparse sensitivity analyses — need experiments varying δ, α, λ, size of A, and initialization to assess bias/variance and robustness.
- Weakness: Missing implementation details — natural-gradient forms summarized, memory footprint for 1M-node runs, minibatch sizes, number of passes, and stopping criteria.
- Quality: Technically sound overall with credible engineering, but some theoretical statements and typos require correction.
- Clarity: Generally clear and organized, but complexity inconsistencies, Eq.(6) typo, and step-size exponent error hinder reproducibility.
- Originality: Novel and useful combination of triangle-based O(K) parameter sharing with structured SVI; thoughtfully adapts ideas from a-MMSB.
- Significance: High practical significance for scalable mixed-membership inference; likely adoptable if issues are addressed.
- Key questions for authors: confirm step-size κ; does re-sampling A optimize a single expected ELBO or change the objective; sensitivity to δ and size of A; how scoring over all k is computed for link prediction and are heuristic/embedding baselines compared; performance under deliberately heterogeneous role interactions.
- Pros summary: Strong scalability, competitive accuracy vs probabilistic baselines, practical impact, good use of structured mean-field and natural gradients.
- Cons summary: Theoretical/notation issues, limited expressivity analysis, missing strong non-probabilistic baselines, and sparse sensitivity analyses.
- Recommendation: Weak accept (borderline); addressing noted clarifications and analyses would make it a solid contribution.
