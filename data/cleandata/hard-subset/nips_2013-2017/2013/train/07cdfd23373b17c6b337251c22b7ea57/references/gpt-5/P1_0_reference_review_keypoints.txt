- Proposes scalable latent-role inference by combining bag-of-triangles, a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) reducing O(K^3)→O(K), and structured SVI with mixture-of-deltas local approximation.

- PTM parameter-sharing groups role-triplets into Bxxx, Bxx, and B0, collapsing many configurations to few parameters.

- Structured mean-field posterior over triangle role triplets avoids rank-1 failures of naive mean-field.

- Randomized O(K) local updates approximate the full O(K^3) updates by using K diagonals plus sampled off-diagonals, yielding O(N δ^2 K) overall complexity.

- Empirical results show fast convergence (2–5 passes), competitive-or-better link prediction vs MMSB on large social networks, and scalability to 1M nodes, K=100 in ~4 hours on 8 threads.

- Builds on prior work (MMTM, a-MMSB, SVI/natural gradients) and connects to ERGM/triadic-closure literature.

- PTM and structured SVI derivation are technically sound; mixture-of-deltas is a principled variational reduction preserving a valid ELBO.

- Complexity claims are consistent: global updates O(NK) per iteration and end-to-end per pass O(mK) with m≈Nδ^2 triangles after subsampling.

- Needs correction: Equation (6) contains a typo for the all-distinct case (log θ indices incorrect).

- Clarify whether re-sampling A across iterations preserves unbiased stochastic natural gradients and how ELBO tracking works with changing variational family.

- The claim that triangle extraction and δ-subsampling are O(1) per sample is terse—add algorithmic detail and complexity in terms of degree distribution.

- Experiments are thorough on scalability but baseline comparisons lack fairness and breadth.

- Fairness issues: multi-threaded implementation vs single-threaded competitors—report wall-clock per core or normalize across cores.

- Compare to stochastic or triangle-augmented versions of MMSB and other scalable embedding/link-prediction methods to ensure fair baselines.

- Model expressiveness concern: sharing Bxx across all minority roles may underfit; recommend ablation with multiple Bxx groups to explore accuracy–complexity trade-off.

- Initialization reliance on seeding 2% with ground-truth biases results—include purely unsupervised initialization and sensitivity analysis.

- Provide experiments varying size/composition of A in mixture-of-deltas to quantify accuracy–speed trade-off and report whether ELBO is monotone on average despite re-sampling.

- Add ablations on δ, K, step-size, memory usage, and report how many passes are needed per dataset and how m scales with δ in practice.

- Acknowledge and discuss limitation that model is not generative of full graphs and whether a post-hoc edge model could be layered for other tasks.

- Minor fixes: correct Eq. (6), ensure Table 1 is consistent, clarify O(1) per-triangle-sample claim, and state code/data release plans for reproducibility.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical combination yielding significant scalability and strong empirical results; relevant to NIPS topics.

- Arguments against acceptance: experimental fairness concerns, limited ablations on key approximations, and potential loss of expressiveness from aggressive sharing.

- Recommendation: weak accept to accept, contingent on addressing experimental fairness, clarification points, and additional ablations.
