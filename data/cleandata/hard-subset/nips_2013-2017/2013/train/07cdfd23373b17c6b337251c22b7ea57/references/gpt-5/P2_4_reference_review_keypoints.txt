- Proposes scalable latent-role inference using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parameter-tying PTM that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), and SVI with structured mean-field and a mixture-of-deltas local approximation.  
- Argues triangles preserve higher-order network structure while being much sparser than O(N^2) edge representations.  
- Empirically converges in a few passes and scales to ~1M nodes and 100 roles on one machine in hours, with competitive or improved latent-role recovery and link prediction versus MMSB/MMTM.  
- Builds on MMTM and motif-based representations, applying a-MMSB-style role-equivalence tying to triangles to cut complexity.  
- Uses standard SVI with natural gradients and structured mean-field over triplets; proposes an O(K) per-triangle update by restricting support to diagonals plus a random off-diagonal set A.  
- Strength: parameter tying is well-motivated by symmetry, reducing variance and complexity substantially.  
- Strength: structured mean-field for triplets is appropriate and avoids naive mean-field failure modes.  
- Strength: pragmatic implementation choices (re-picking A, discarding local tensors, parallelization) and convincing large-scale experiments.  
- Weakness: theoretical gap — randomized mixture-of-deltas makes the global objective time-varying and no proof/lemma shows unbiasedness or convergence under the changing support.  
- Weakness: apparent step-size typo (κ sign) — experiments state κ = −0.5 which would violate SVI conditions; likely should be κ = 0.5.  
- Weakness: inconsistency in complexity claims — paper sometimes states O(NK) but per-pass cost is O(|I|K) ≈ O(N δ^2 K); needs clarity up front.  
- Weakness: potential underfitting from parameter tying in heterogeneous graphs; expressivity trade-offs not analyzed or ablated.  
- Weakness: limited baseline comparisons — should compare to scalable probabilistic baselines like a-MMSB on at least one medium-scale network.  
- Weakness: insufficient details on efficient computation of edge probabilities from triangle marginals and related complexity/caching strategies.  
- Clarity: generally well-written; role-equivalence and ELBO decomposition are clear, but some updates and derivations are missing from main text.  
- Originality: moderate — main novelty is O(K) tying for triangles plus the structured SVI and mixture-of-deltas trick, which is pragmatic but analogous to prior a-MMSB ideas.  
- Significance: high practical impact — scales probabilistic latent-role inference to million-node graphs with hundreds of roles and leverages triadic structure.  
- Pros summary: strong scalability, memory efficiency, parallelizability, competitive accuracy, and intuitive symmetry-based modeling.  
- Cons summary: theoretical convergence gap for the randomized local approximation, missing comparisons to key scalable baselines, and some clarity/derivation issues.  
- Questions for authors: clarify κ used and SVI validity; bounds or empirical effects of random A (size/composition) on ELBO and convergence; sensitivity to δ, α, λ, mini-batch size; efficient link-probability computation; performance on low-clustering networks.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance pending tightening of convergence discussion for the local approximation, fixing the step-size issue, and clarifying complexity and missing derivations.
