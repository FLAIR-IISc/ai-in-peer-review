- Tackles scalable probabilistic latent space inference for large networks via a new Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM).
- PTM builds on a bag-of-triangles representation and reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing.
- Uses stochastic natural gradient ascent for variational inference with empirical complexity O(NK).
- Demonstrates strong empirical scalability and performance, including a 1M-node YouTube analysis completed in 4 hours.
- Positions work relative to prior models (MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM) and claims to address representation, modeling, and inference limitations jointly.
- Citations are comprehensive, covering foundational network modeling and inference literature.
- Primary strength: substantial scalability improvement enabling previously intractable probabilistic latent inference.
- Novelty: the parameter-sharing PTM is a genuinely new approach to the triangle-based representation problem.
- Theoretical soundness: clear derivation and justified use of natural gradients for optimization.
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments on synthetic and real networks with thorough comparisons to existing algorithms.
- Presentation is clear and well-organized despite technical complexity.
- Insightful rejection of naive mean-field and justification for the chosen structured mean-field approximation.
- Weakness: triangular representation can lose network information and needs deeper discussion of trade-offs and failure cases.
- Weakness: sensitivity to subsampling parameter δ (fixed at 50) lacks systematic analysis.
- Weakness: choice of 10% mini-batch size not thoroughly explored for effects on convergence and accuracy.
- Weakness: potential dependence on initialization; more robust strategies or robustness checks needed.
- Weakness: missing comparison or discussion relative to modern deep learning network embedding methods (e.g., GNNs).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a major bottleneck, presents a novel and sound method, and shows compelling empirical gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: triangular-information loss, parameter sensitivity, and lack of deep-learning comparisons leave gaps.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with minor issues; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
