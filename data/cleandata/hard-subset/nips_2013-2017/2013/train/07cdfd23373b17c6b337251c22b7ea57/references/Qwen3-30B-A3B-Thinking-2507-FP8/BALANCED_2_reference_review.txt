

# Review of "A Scalable Approach to Probabilistic Latent Space Inference of Large-Scale Networks"

## Summary
This paper proposes a scalable approach for probabilistic latent space inference on large networks by combining a bag-of-triangles representation with a parsimonious parameterization. The authors argue that existing methods (MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM) fail to simultaneously achieve scalability and accuracy, and introduce the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces the parameter space from O(K^3) to O(K) through clever parameter sharing. They develop a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity, demonstrating convergence on 1M-node networks in hours compared to days for prior methods.

## Strengths
1. **Technical Contribution**: The paper makes a significant technical contribution by addressing a genuine scalability bottleneck in network analysis. The parameter sharing scheme that reduces the triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) is elegant and effective.

2. **Thorough Experimental Validation**: The authors provide comprehensive experiments on both synthetic and real-world networks, demonstrating that their approach achieves competitive or better accuracy than prior work while being orders of magnitude faster. The comparison with MMSB, MMTM, and PTM Gibbs on networks up to 10,000 nodes convincingly shows the scalability advantage.

3. **Practical Impact**: The paper demonstrates convergence on real-world networks (Brightkite, Stanford) in minutes rather than days or hours, which is a major practical contribution to the field. The implementation details (δ-subsampling, parallelization) are well-described and practically valuable.

4. **Clear Presentation**: The paper is well-written with clear explanations of the model, inference algorithm, and experimental setup. The figures effectively illustrate the key findings.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited Theoretical Justification**: While the paper claims the triangular representation is sufficient, it lacks a thorough theoretical justification for why triangles (and not other network motifs) capture sufficient structure for latent space inference. The authors should better address what network properties are lost by using only triangles, and whether there are theoretical guarantees for their approach.

2. **Inadequate Baseline Comparison**: The experimental comparison primarily focuses on MMSB and MMTM, but there are other recent scalable network analysis approaches (e.g., [25] on deep network embeddings, [26] on graph neural networks) that could provide more comprehensive context for the contribution. Including a comparison with at least one modern deep learning-based approach would strengthen the significance claim.

3. **Missing Discussion of Limitations**: The paper doesn't adequately address potential limitations of their approach. For instance:
   - How does the method perform on networks with very high clustering coefficients or specific structural patterns not captured by triangles?
   - What is the impact of the δ-subsampling threshold on model quality, and how should it be chosen in practice?
   - How does the method scale with extremely large K (e.g., K > 100)?

4. **Weak Connection to Network Motif Literature**: While the paper references network motifs, it doesn't sufficiently connect to the extensive literature on motif-based network analysis (e.g., the work by Milo et al. [15] or the systematic studies of motif distributions). A more thorough discussion of how their approach relates to and extends this literature would strengthen the paper's position.

## Recommendations for Improvement
1. **Expand Theoretical Discussion**: Include a section discussing the theoretical advantages of using triangles as a network representation for latent space inference, perhaps by analyzing what structural properties triangles capture compared to edges.

2. **Broaden Baseline Comparisons**: Add a comparison with at least one state-of-the-art deep learning-based network analysis method to better position the contribution within the broader landscape of network analysis.

3. **Address Limitations**: Include a discussion of the method's limitations, particularly regarding network structure types where triangles might be insufficient, and how to choose the δ-subsampling parameter.

4. **Strengthen Connection to Network Motif Literature**: Expand the discussion of network motifs in the related work section to better position the paper within this well-established literature.

## Overall Assessment
This is a strong technical contribution that addresses a genuine scalability challenge in network analysis with a well-designed solution. The paper demonstrates clear practical advantages over existing methods, with results that are both theoretically sound and practically significant. While there are some weaknesses in the theoretical justification and comparative analysis, these are relatively minor compared to the paper's core contribution.

**Decision**: Accept

**Justification**: The paper makes a significant contribution to scalable network analysis with clear technical advances, thorough experimental validation, and practical impact. The weaknesses identified are addressable through minor revisions, but the core contribution is strong and represents a meaningful advance in the field. The work aligns well with NIPS' focus on novel, impactful contributions to machine learning and statistical methods.
