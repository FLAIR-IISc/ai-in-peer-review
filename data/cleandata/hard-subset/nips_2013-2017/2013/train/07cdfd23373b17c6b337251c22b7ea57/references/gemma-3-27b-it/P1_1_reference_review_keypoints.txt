- Addresses scaling probabilistic latent space inference to very large networks (millions of nodes).  
- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation and stochastic natural gradient ascent.  
- PTM’s parameter sharing reduces complexity from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K), enabling larger latent spaces (K) and network sizes (N).  
- Inference achieves O(NK) complexity and empirically handles networks of 1M nodes and 100 roles in ~4 hours.  
- Builds on MMSB, a-MMSB, and MMTM, improving MMTM’s scalability and adapting stochastic variational inference to PTM.  
- Core strength: demonstrated scalability with significant speedups and competitive accuracy on synthetic and real data.  
- Strength: novel parameterization (PTM) effectively reduces model complexity without large accuracy loss.  
- Strength: clear theoretical motivation emphasizing succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation showing faster convergence than prior work and good accuracy.  
- Strength: well-written, well-organized presentation and appropriate use of stochastic natural gradient ascent.  
- Weakness: δ-subsampling’s impact and sensitivity not thoroughly investigated; requires more ablation.  
- Weakness: justification for the chosen structured mean-field approximation is somewhat hand-wavy and needs stronger rationale/comparison.  
- Weakness: PTM is not a full generative model; implications for latent-space interpretation are under-discussed.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited—should broaden evaluation to include more recent/alternative network models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a major scalability bottleneck with a novel, effective method and strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: possible bias from δ-subsampling, insufficient mean-field justification, and limited comparative baselines.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with minor weaknesses that do not significantly detract from the work’s value.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add δ ablation, strengthen mean-field justification, discuss generative limitations, and broaden comparisons.
