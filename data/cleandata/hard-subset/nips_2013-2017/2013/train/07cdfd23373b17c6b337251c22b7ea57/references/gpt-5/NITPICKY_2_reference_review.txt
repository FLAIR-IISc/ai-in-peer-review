Summary
The paper proposes a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) for latent role inference on large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation. PTM replaces the O(K^3) triangle-parameter tensor of MMTM with O(K) shared parameters (Bxxx, Bxx, B0) determined by the number of distinct roles in a role triple. Inference is done via a structured variational method with stochastic natural gradients, using a per-triangle variational posterior over role triplets. To avoid O(K^3) local updates, the authors propose a “mixture-of-deltas” approximation that restricts mass to O(K) diagonal and randomly chosen off-diagonal triplets. They claim O(NK) scalability (and O(Nδ^2K) elsewhere) and demonstrate competitive latent role recovery and link prediction on synthetic and real networks, including million-node networks.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds on the triangular representation of networks (Ho et al., NIPS 2012) and borrows the parameter-sharing idea of a-MMSB (Gopalan et al., NIPS 2013) to reduce parameter dimension. It adopts stochastic variational inference (Hoffman et al., JMLR 2013) and natural gradients (Amari). The positioning relative to MMSB/MMTM is clear conceptually, though the experimental comparison omits a-MMSB in core tasks.

Strengths
- Timely problem: scaling probabilistic latent-role inference to N≥10^6 and K in the hundreds.
- Model parsimony: O(K) triangle parameters is elegant and potentially robust.
- Structured variational family over (si,jk, sj,ik, sk,ij) is more faithful than naive mean-field.
- Practical engineering: mini-batch over triangles, memory savings by discarding per-triangle variational parameters, coarse-grained parallelization.
- Empirical indications of fast convergence in data passes and strong runtimes.

Weaknesses (technical correctness, clarity, and evaluation)
- Incorrect local update (Eq. 6): For distinct x,y,z, the term uses Eq[log θi,x + log θj,x + log θk,x]. This should be Eq[log θi,x + log θj,y + log θk,z]. As written, the update is wrong and would bias inference.
- Step-size schedule: The algorithm defines ρt = τ0(τ1 + t)^−κ and uses κ = −0.5 in experiments, which implies increasing step sizes. Standard SVI requires κ ∈ (0.5, 1]. This is a serious mistake; convergence guarantees cited (Robbins–Monro, Hoffman et al.) do not apply with κ<0 or negative exponent usage.
- Unbiased gradient claim vs. restricted local family: LS(η,γ) is unbiased for the objective that maximizes local φ over the full K^3 simplex. The mixture-of-deltas restriction changes the variational family; ES[∇LS] is not unbiased for the original L unless the restriction is accounted for. Moreover, randomly re-picking A each iteration changes the objective over time; no analysis of stationary points or lower bound monotonicity is provided.
- Complexity inconsistencies: The paper alternately claims O(NK) and O(Nδ^2K) complexity. Since m≈O(Nδ^2) triangles are the data, two data passes are O(mK). The “Global Update O(NK)” statement ignores the dominant triangle-local work; the abstract and Section 1 claim O(NK), but Section 5 uses O(Nδ^2K). This should be reconciled.
- δ-subsampling and unbiasedness: The method subsamples triangles for high-degree nodes but does not use sampling weights (e.g., Horvitz–Thompson) in the objective/gradients. Claims that distributions are “approximately preserved” are not quantified; bias introduced by δ-subsampling is neither analyzed nor corrected.
- Natural gradient details deferred to Appendix (not provided here): Fisher information forms and exact natural gradients for Dirichlet factors are nontrivial; without the appendix the method is not reproducible.
- Model expressivity and identifiability: Sharing Bxx across all minority roles and B0 for all-distinct triples drastically reduces expressivity; no analysis of what classes of triangle distributions can be captured, nor identifiability conditions. Empirical evidence is not sufficient to support that this loss does not harm important regimes.
- Link prediction protocol unclear and potentially infeasible: Ranking i−j by marginalizing over all k and roles is O(NK^3) per pair in the exact model; with the mixture-of-deltas approximation it may still be large. No details on computational shortcuts are given. The training objective is triangle likelihood, not edge likelihood; the calibration between them is not discussed.
- Experimental fairness: 
  - Comparisons exclude a-MMSB (the most relevant scalable baseline) on key tasks; only MMSB batch variational is used in link prediction and synthetic recovery.
  - Initialization gives 2 seed nodes per role (2% labeled); this favors methods and may not reflect realistic unsupervised settings. Thresholding θ̂ at 0.125 is arbitrary and discards magnitude information.
  - Runtimes are compared across different threading (4 vs 8 threads) and possibly different hardware; “orders of magnitude” claims are not backed by controlled benchmarks. Reporting “time per data pass” for some methods but wall-clock for others is inconsistent.
- Reproducibility gaps: Figures/Tables and the Appendix are referenced but not present; hyperparameters are partly given but the most critical details (minibatch size vs. m, convergence criteria, stabilization tricks) are missing. No code availability.
- Presentation/formatting:
  - Many references lack years/venues/pages; several are incomplete or inconsistent.
  - Table 1–3 and Figure 1–2 are missing; footnotes appear inline and disrupt readability; duplication in tuple types (“(a,a,b), (a,b,a) or (a,a,b)”) suggests editing errors.
  - Notational slips (e.g., 6= spacing, Θ(·) in prose), and ambiguous Dirichlet(λ) definition across simplices of different dimensions.

Questions for the authors
- Please correct Eq. (6) and provide the full local updates for all cases (including φxxx and φxxy) in the main text.
- Clarify the step-size schedule; confirm κ ∈ (0.5,1] and adjust reported experiments accordingly.
- Is the stochastic gradient unbiased for the objective you claim, given the restricted local variational family and re-sampling of A? If not, what convergence guarantee applies?
- Provide a precise complexity analysis per iteration in terms of N, K, δ, minibatch size |S|, and number of passes, and reconcile the O(NK) vs O(Nδ^2K) claims.
- How is δ-subsampling accounted for in gradient estimates? If unweighted, quantify the bias and its impact.
- Detail the link prediction computation and its complexity; are approximations used?
- Add comparisons to a-MMSB (and, if possible, scalable triangle-based baselines), with matched threading/hardware.

Pros
- Addresses an important scalability bottleneck with a principled modeling idea.
- Attractive structured variational formulation and practical implementation instincts.
- Promising empirical speed and some accuracy gains.

Cons
- Several serious methodological errors (local update, step-size) and unjustified unbiasedness claims.
- Complexity and convergence statements are inconsistent or under-specified.
- Experimental comparisons are incomplete and sometimes unfair.
- Missing appendices/figures/tables and numerous presentation issues.

Recommendation: Reject. The core idea is promising, but the current version contains critical technical errors, incomplete justifications, and clarity/reproducibility problems that undermine confidence in the claims. I encourage a revised submission addressing the above points.
