- Summary: Introduces PTM, a bag-of-triangles latent-space model that reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) and uses stochastic variational inference achieving O(NK) scalability, demonstrated on million-node networks.

- Strength: Dramatic scalability improvements—runs inference on a 1M-node YouTube network with 100 roles in ~4 hours.

- Strength: Novel parameterization—strategic sharing of triangle parameters (B0, Bxx, Bxxx) yields an elegant O(K) representation.

- Strength: Effective inference algorithm—structured mean-field, O(K) local updates, and stochastic natural gradients enable practical scalability.

- Strength: Comprehensive experiments—synthetic and real-world evaluations with multiple baselines (MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM, PTM Gibbs).

- Strength: Clear presentation—well-organized paper with sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical guarantees—no analysis of consistency, convergence of the stochastic variational algorithm, or statistical efficiency relative to MMTM.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to recent methods—omits comparisons to scalable deep learning graph methods (e.g., GCNs, GraphSAGE).

- Weakness: Missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis—no study of δ (subsample threshold), minibatch size, or choice of A in local updates.

- Weakness: Limited interpretability analysis—little qualitative insight into the inferred roles (e.g., what the 100 YouTube roles represent).

- Weakness: Assumption risk—insufficient analysis of when triangle-based representations fail or underperform for certain network structures.

- Weakness: Sparse discussion of limitations—insufficient coverage of missing higher-order structures beyond triangles.

- Technical concern: Mixture-of-deltas approximation needs stronger justification and analysis of how the choice of A affects approximation quality.

- Technical concern: Initialization sensitivity—seeding 2% nodes with true roles is unrealistic; need practical initialization strategies without ground truth.

- Technical concern: Lack of comparison to non-probabilistic scalable methods like node2vec or DeepWalk.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed contribution that substantially advances scalable latent-space inference; recommended accept with minor revisions.

- Recommended revision: Add brief theoretical discussion on PTM statistical properties (consistency, convergence rates).

- Recommended revision: Include at least one comparison to a recent deep learning–based scalable network method (e.g., GraphSAGE or GAT).

- Recommended revision: Provide sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (δ, minibatch size, choice of A).

- Recommended revision: Add qualitative analysis of inferred latent roles on a real-world dataset.

- Recommended revision: Expand discussion of limitations, especially regarding triangular representation assumptions and missed higher-order structures.

- Pros: Addresses a critical scalability bottleneck, offers a clear parameterization strategy, demonstrates impressive empirical scalability, provides comprehensive experiments, and is well-written.

- Cons: Missing theoretical guarantees, limited deep-learning comparisons, inadequate discussion of triangular representation limitations, and minimal qualitative role analysis.
