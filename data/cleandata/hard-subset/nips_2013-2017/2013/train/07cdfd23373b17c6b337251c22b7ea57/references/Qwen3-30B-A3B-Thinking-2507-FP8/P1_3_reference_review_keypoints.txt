- Addresses scalable latent space inference for million-node networks via a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameters from O(K³) to O(K).
- Introduces a parameter-sharing strategy grouping role configurations by distinctness to overcome MMTM’s O(K³) bottleneck.
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity enabling inference on 1M-node networks in ~4 hours on a single machine.
- Empirical results show strong scalability and competitive or improved accuracy on latent recovery and link prediction, with dramatic runtime gains (e.g., 12 minutes vs. 8 days on Brightkite).
- Comparisons to relevant baselines (MMSB, MMTM Gibbs, PTM Gibbs) and convergence analyses are thorough and well-presented.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with precise mathematical formalization and effective figures.
- Limited discussion and quantification of information loss from using triangle-based representations versus edge-based models.
- The "mixture-of-deltas" local update approximation is ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification or analysis of how choices affect results.
- Initialization is sensitive and current seeding (2% nodes with true roles) is unrealistic; alternative initialization based on triangle counts is not thoroughly evaluated.
- Lacks a theoretical convergence analysis or guarantees for the proposed stochastic variational inference algorithm.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and significant contribution that advances scalable network analysis and meets NIPS criteria.
- Recommendation: accept — technical and empirical strengths outweigh the noted limitations.
- Pro arguments: solves a critical scalability problem, provides orders-of-magnitude speedup, novel parameter sharing, and well-executed experiments.
- Con arguments: limited analysis of triangle trade-offs, approximation theoretical gaps, and questionable initialization practicality.
