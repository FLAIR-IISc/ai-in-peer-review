- Tackles scalable probabilistic latent space inference for large networks via a new model and algorithm.
- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation to reduce complexity.
- PTM shares parameters across latent roles, cutting parameter space from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Introduces a stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm with O(NK) complexity.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world data, including 1M-node YouTube inference in 4 hours.

- Clearly positions work relative to MMSB/a-MMSB (adjacency scalability limits) and MMTM (parameter tensor blowup).
- Leverages the triangular representation and connects to stochastic variational inference and natural gradient literature.
- Directly addresses scalability bottlenecks in probabilistic network modeling.

- Major scalability improvement: O(NK) algorithm with empirical speedups by orders of magnitude.
- Parsimonious modeling: effective parameter-sharing scheme that greatly reduces model size.
- Strong empirical validation: convincing recovery on synthetic data and practical scalability on large real networks.
- Theoretically grounded: stochastic variational inference with natural gradients is well-motivated.
- Clear writing and organization, with consistent mathematical notation.
- Explicitly addresses limitations of prior methods.

- Justification for the chosen structured mean-field (“mixture-of-deltas”) approximation is insufficiently explained.
- Performance is sensitive to δ-subsampling; more sensitivity analysis and robustness to power-law degrees needed.
- Lacks comparison to non-probabilistic baselines (e.g., Louvain, node2vec) for broader context.
- Initialization sensitivity is acknowledged but the impact of different seeding strategies is not fully explored.
- Limited discussion of PTM’s generative assumptions and their applicability to varied network types.

- For acceptance: addresses a critical scalability bottleneck, provides a significant algorithmic and modeling advance, and shows strong empirical results with solid exposition.
- Against acceptance: needs deeper justification for the structured mean-field choice, more δ-sensitivity analysis, and comparisons to non-probabilistic methods.

- Overall: strong contribution that substantially advances scalable probabilistic latent space inference; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance (8.5/10).

- Recommendations: better justify the structured mean-field approximation.
- Conduct a comprehensive δ-subsampling sensitivity analysis.
- Add comparisons to relevant non-probabilistic methods.
- Discuss PTM’s modeling assumptions and limitations.
- Study the impact of different initialization strategies.
