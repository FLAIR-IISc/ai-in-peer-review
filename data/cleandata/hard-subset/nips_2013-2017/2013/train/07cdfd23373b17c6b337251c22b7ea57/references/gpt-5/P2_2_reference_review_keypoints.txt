- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that ties triangle-generating parameters across role-triplet equivalence classes, reducing parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Introduces structured mean-field SVI with natural gradients and mini-batched triangles for scalable inference.
- Proposes a “mixture-of-deltas” variational family to reduce per-triangle local updates from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Empirically converges in 2–5 passes, scales to 1M nodes (K=100) in ~4 hours on 8 threads, and is faster than MMSB/MMTM while matching or improving accuracy.
- Connects to prior work: MMSB, a-MMSB, SVI literature, and triangle-based modeling (Ho et al., NIPS’12).
- Technical soundness: parameter sharing and structured variational family are well-defined and address triadic dependencies; SVI with mini-batching and natural gradients is appropriate.
- The mixture-of-deltas approximation is sensible and effective but changes the optimized bound.
- Claim about “unbiased gradient” is misleading unless explicitly tied to the restricted variational objective; should be clarified.
- Evaluation relies on δ-subsampling; a sensitivity analysis to δ is needed to strengthen empirical claims.
- Initialization seeds 2% of nodes with true roles for all methods; an ablation with unsupervised initializations is necessary.
- Baseline coverage is limited—lacks head-to-head runs against a-MMSB and other scalable overlapping-community/role models under identical settings.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized; generative process, equivalence classes, and parameter sharing are clearly explained.
- Suggested clarifications: state the exact objective optimized under mixture-of-deltas and theoretical convergence guarantees for the restricted bound.
- Missing or important details to add: include Table 1 probabilities in main text and explain link-prediction scoring and computational costs.
- Originality: novelty lies in symmetry-based parameter tying for triangle motifs and the mixture-of-deltas local family; incremental but meaningful over prior triangle work.
- Significance: addresses scalability bottleneck in probabilistic network models and is likely useful to practitioners and researchers.
- Pros: principled O(K) parameter reduction, structured SVI, strong scalability, competitive accuracy, and practical engineering choices (mini-batching, discarding local vars).
- Cons: reduced expressivity (tying may miss heterophilous patterns), theoretical subtlety on gradient claims, limited baselines, and dependence on δ-subsampling and seeded initialization.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying the SVI objective under the local approximation, expanding baseline comparisons where feasible, and adding sensitivity analyses for δ and initialization.
