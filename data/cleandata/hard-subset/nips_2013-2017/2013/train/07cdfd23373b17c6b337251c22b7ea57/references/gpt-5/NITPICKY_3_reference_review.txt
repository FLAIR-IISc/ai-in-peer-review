Summary
The paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space model for large networks based on a bag-of-triangles representation. The Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reduces the number of triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) (as in MMTM) to O(K) by sharing parameters across classes of role configurations: all-same (Bxxx), two-equal (Bxx), and all-distinct (B0). Inference uses stochastic variational inference (SVI) with structured mean-field for per-triangle role triplets and natural gradients for global parameters. To avoid O(K^3) local updates, the authors further introduce a “mixture-of-deltas” restriction that selects O(K) off-diagonal role combinations per triangle, yielding O(K) local updates. Triangle subsampling (δ) is used to maintain near-linear scaling in N. Experiments on synthetic and real networks report strong scalability (1M nodes, 100 roles, 4 hours on 8 threads) and competitive or improved accuracy compared to MMSB and MMTM.

Relation to prior work: The approach builds on triangle-based representations (Ho et al., 2011) and the idea of parameter sharing across inter-role interactions (a-MMSB). The use of SVI with natural gradients follows Hoffman et al. (2013).

Strengths
- Addresses an important scalability bottleneck in mixed-membership network models, especially scaling in both N and K.
- Clear modeling idea: grouping role-triplet configurations to obtain O(K) parameters is elegant and practically impactful.
- Structured mean-field for the triangle latent role triplet is reasonable; the O(K) mixture-of-deltas approximation is clever and reduces local complexity substantially.
- Solid empirical focus on scalability; the 1M-node, 100-role demonstration is impressive.
- Triangle-centric modeling aligns with known importance of triadic closure and motifs; the δ-subsampling strategy is sensible and leverages prior work.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and claims
- Equation (6) appears incorrect for the “distinct x,y,z” case: it uses log θi,x + log θj,x + log θk,x. It should involve x, y, z separately (log θi,x + log θj,y + log θk,z). This is not a minor typo: it affects the local update and any implementation derived from it.
- SVI unbiasedness and convergence claims are muddled. The text asserts that ES[∇LS]=∇L when local variational parameters are optimized. However, the “mixture-of-deltas” restriction optimizes over a reduced family, so the gradient is unbiased for a different objective (a restricted ELBO), not the original L. The manuscript should clearly state that convergence is to a stationary point of the restricted ELBO and adjust theoretical claims accordingly.
- Step-size schedule inconsistency: the paper states ρt=τ0(τ1+t)^−κ with constraints ensuring ∑ ρt=∞ and ∑ ρt^2<∞, which typically requires κ∈(0.5,1]. But the experiments specify κ=−0.5, which would make the exponent positive, causing step sizes to increase—clearly inconsistent. This needs correction.
- Complexity inconsistencies: Early claims suggest overall O(NK) time; elsewhere the method is O(N δ^2 K) due to triangles (with δ constant). The paper should consistently differentiate global update cost O(NK) from total training cost (proportional to number of triangle updates times O(K)) and state assumptions on δ.

Modeling choices and expressiveness
- The parameter sharing (Bxx and B0 pooled across minority roles and distinct triples) may limit expressiveness in heterogeneous networks where specific role interactions matter. An ablation comparing PTM to a partially shared model (e.g., separating majority/minority role identities or a small number of role-group buckets) would clarify trade-offs.
- The approach is not a generative model of networks (acknowledged). Yet link prediction is evaluated using triangle-based probabilities marginalizing over a third node. The computational method and approximation used for this marginalization are not described; exact marginalization over all k is O(N) per candidate edge and impractical without approximations. Please specify the estimator, its complexity, and how it is made scalable.

Experimental design
- Baselines: Comparisons omit a-MMSB (the more scalable variant) and other scalable community/role inference methods. Quoting runtimes from [5] is not a substitute for controlled experiments on the same hardware. Including a-MMSB with identical hardware and preprocessing would strengthen the empirical case.
- Initialization uses 2 seeded ground-truth nodes per role on synthetic data. While the same seeds are given to all methods, this is a strong and unrealistic prior; algorithms’ sensitivity to seeds should be studied (e.g., random inits, the heuristic init proposed in the footnote, and more moderate seeding levels).
- Evaluation metric: Thresholding θ at 0.125 because “≤4 roles per node” is ad hoc and may affect methods differently (especially with Dirichlet smoothing). Please report sensitivity to the threshold and include metrics that account for soft assignments (e.g., AUPRC/ROC on role membership, or measures invariant to scaling).
- Triangle subsampling (δ): The claim that extraction and subsampling cost <1% of inference time and can be done in O(1) per sample needs more detail. Triangle enumeration is typically nontrivial; please describe the algorithm, data structures, and wall-clock for preprocessing.

Clarity and presentation
- Several notational issues and typos: e.g., Eq. (6) error; ambiguous “Eq[log(B0,1/3)]”; inconsistent complexity claims; “κ=−0.5” issue; various LaTeX artifacts; mismatched footnotes; some reference entries are incomplete or truncated.
- Important update equations are deferred to the Appendix; ensure these are complete and correct. Provide explicit digamma-based expectations for Dirichlet posteriors.

Quality, originality, significance, clarity
- Quality: Mixed. The core idea is sound and experiments are promising, but there are nontrivial technical inconsistencies (local update equation, SVI convergence claims, step size) and missing baseline comparisons.
- Originality: The parameter-sharing over triangle role configurations is a neat extension of the a-MMSB-style parsimony to triadic motifs. The O(K) mixture-of-deltas local update is a practical contribution.
- Significance: Potentially high for scalable probabilistic network role discovery, given the 1M-node result. However, the limited set of baselines and methodological issues weaken the current evidence.
- Clarity: Generally readable, but marred by notational errors, some contradictory statements, and incomplete references.

Requests for rebuttal
- Correct Eq. (6), the step-size schedule, and clarify the objective being optimized under the mixture-of-deltas restriction; adjust convergence claims accordingly.
- Provide details on link prediction scoring (marginalization over k, approximations, complexity).
- Add controlled comparisons to a-MMSB on shared hardware and at least one more scalable baseline.
- Report sensitivity to initialization and the θ-threshold for NMI.
- Clarify preprocessing cost and method for triangle extraction/subsampling.

Minor comments
- Use consistent big-O vs Θ; avoid unsubstantiated “O(1) per sample” claims without algorithmic detail.
- Clarify whether Bxx and Bxxx are role-specific for all x and how symmetry is enforced.
- Complete and correct the reference list.

Recommendation
Weak reject. The paper contains a promising and practically valuable idea with strong empirical scalability, but several technical inconsistencies and missing experimental comparisons prevent me from confidently recommending acceptance. With corrections and stronger baselines, I could be persuaded. Confidence: high.
