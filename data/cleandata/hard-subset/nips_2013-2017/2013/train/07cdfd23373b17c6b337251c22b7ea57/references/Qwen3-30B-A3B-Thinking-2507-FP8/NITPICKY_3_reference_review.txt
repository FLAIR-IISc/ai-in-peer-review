

# Review of "A Scalable Approach to Probabilistic Latent Space Inference of Large-Scale Networks"

This paper proposes a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) for scalable latent space inference on large networks, using a bag-of-triangles representation instead of adjacency matrices. The authors claim to achieve O(NK) time complexity by reducing the parameter space from O(K³) to O(K) through parameter sharing, and by using stochastic variational inference with a "mixture-of-deltas" approximation for local updates. The method is evaluated on synthetic and real networks, claiming to outperform existing methods in scalability and accuracy.

**Strengths:**
The paper addresses a genuinely important problem in network analysis: scaling latent space inference to large networks. The use of triangular motifs instead of adjacency matrices is a reasonable approach to reduce computational complexity. The parameter sharing strategy (reducing from O(K³) to O(K)) is conceptually sound and potentially valuable. The experimental results on synthetic data demonstrate that the proposed method achieves high accuracy across various network sizes, and the real-world experiments show promising scalability improvements over existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Critical complexity claim discrepancy:** The paper claims "time complexity O(NK)" (Section 1, 5), but the actual complexity is O(Nδ²K), where δ is the subsampling parameter (set to 50 in experiments). The paper fails to clarify that δ is a constant (typically set to 50), making the complexity effectively O(NK) only for this specific implementation. This is a significant omission that misrepresents the algorithm's complexity.

2. **Lack of evidence for key approximation:** The paper claims "this approximation works as well as the full parameterization" (Section 4) but provides no evidence for this claim. The "mixture-of-deltas" approximation is central to the method's efficiency, yet there is no empirical comparison showing that the approximation quality is comparable to the full parameterization.

3. **Inadequate experimental details:** The runtime comparisons (e.g., "converged on Brightkite in 12 minutes" vs. "8 days" for a-MMSB) lack critical hardware details. Without knowing whether these comparisons were made on identical hardware, the claims of "orders of magnitude faster" are unverifiable and potentially misleading.

4. **Missing statistical significance:** The paper claims "our method exhibits high accuracy (NMI close to 1)" and "MMSB Variational exhibits degraded performance," but provides no statistical significance tests. Without these, it's impossible to determine if the observed differences are meaningful.

5. **Formatting inconsistencies:** The paper contains numerous inconsistent mathematical notations (e.g., O(N2K2) vs. O(N²K²)), spacing issues around mathematical symbols (e.g., "N ≥ 100, 000"), and inconsistent use of subscripts (e.g., "K" vs. "k" for number of roles). These small but pervasive errors undermine the paper's professionalism.

6. **Incomplete reference formatting:** Several references (e.g., [1], [5], [8]) are missing essential bibliographic information such as volume numbers, page ranges, and publication years, which is unacceptable for a NIPS submission.

7. **Insufficient discussion of related work:** The paper briefly mentions a-MMSB [5] but fails to adequately explain how PTM differs from this prior work. The key distinction (parameter sharing vs. other approaches) is not clearly articulated.

8. **Unjustified hyperparameter choices:** The paper states "hyperparameters α = λ = 0.1" without justification. These values could significantly impact results, and their selection should be explained or evaluated.

9. **Lack of convergence guarantees:** The paper claims "converges after 2 passes on each triangular motif" but provides no theoretical justification for this claim or explanation for why this is better than other algorithms.

**Conclusion:**
While the paper addresses an important problem and contains some promising ideas, the numerous technical and presentation flaws significantly undermine its credibility. The complexity claim discrepancy is particularly problematic as it misrepresents the algorithm's theoretical properties. The lack of evidence for key claims, inadequate experimental details, and formatting inconsistencies collectively make it difficult to assess the validity of the results. As a perfectionist reviewer, I cannot recommend acceptance due to these substantial issues, despite the paper's potential significance. The authors should thoroughly revise the paper to address these concerns before reconsideration.

**Recommendation: Reject**
