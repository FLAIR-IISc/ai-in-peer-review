- Problem: addresses scalability of probabilistic latent space models for very large networks.
- Method: combines a bag-of-triangles representation with a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) to enable O(NK) inference.
- Complexity improvement: reduces inference complexity dramatically compared to MMSB (O(N²K²)) and MMTM (O(Nδ²K³)).
- Key technical contribution: parameter sharing via role-equivalence classes reduces parameter space from O(K³) to O(K).
- Inference algorithm: develops a stochastic variational inference procedure that scales to million-node networks.
- Empirical evaluation: synthetic experiments (N=500–10,000, K=N/100) show high accuracy while scaling much better than baselines.
- Real-world results: converges in minutes on networks of 58K–1.1M nodes versus days for prior methods, with competitive accuracy.
- Trade-offs: triangle-based representation sacrifices some adjacency information, though loss is minimal for evaluated tasks.
- Heuristics: uses a practical initialization heuristic and a "mixture-of-deltas" local-update approximation that works empirically.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clearly organized, with consistent notation, pseudocode, and reproducible implementation details.
- Originality: main novelty is the PTM parameter-sharing insight building on prior triangle representations, not a mere combination of existing methods.
- Significance: enables latent-space analysis for million-node networks and hundreds of roles on a single machine, with clear practical impact.
- Strengths: clear problem identification, novel complexity reduction, comprehensive experiments, practical and scalable implementation.
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical analysis of PTM’s statistical properties, ad hoc mixture-of-deltas approximation, and reliance on initialization.
- Acceptance arguments: addresses a critical bottleneck, offers novel and practical contributions, and shows strong empirical gains.
- Rejection concerns: lack of deeper theoretical justification and the practical dependency on initialization and approximations.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept.
