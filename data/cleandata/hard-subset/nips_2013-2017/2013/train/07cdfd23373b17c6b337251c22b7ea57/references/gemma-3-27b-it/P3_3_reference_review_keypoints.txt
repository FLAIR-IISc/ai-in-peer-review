- Tackles scalable latent space inference for large networks via a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation and stochastic natural gradient ascent.
- Key technical innovation: parameter sharing in PTM reduces complexity from O(K^3) to O(K), enabling inference on million-node networks with 100 roles in ~4 hours.
- Empirical claim: competitive accuracy and substantial speedups over state-of-the-art methods (a-MMSB, MMTM) on synthetic and real networks.
- Positions work relative to MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM and connects to ERGM and triadic-closure literature; uses stochastic natural gradients in the variational inference context.
- Strength — Scalability: demonstrates inference at previously infeasible scale with major runtime improvements.
- Strength — Novel parameter sharing: parsimonious modeling effectively reduces dimensionality without losing accuracy.
- Strength — Strong empirical validation: comprehensive experiments and compelling comparisons to key baselines.
- Strength — Clear presentation: well-written, logically organized, explains motivation and trade-offs clearly.
- Strength — Theoretical justification: supports approach via variational inference and stochastic natural gradients.
- Weakness — Triangle representation trade-offs: insufficient analysis of information loss effects across network types (degree distributions, clustering).
- Weakness — Mini-batch size sensitivity: choice of 10% feels arbitrary and lacks thorough convergence/performance sensitivity study.
- Weakness — Structured mean-field approximation: needs clearer explanation of how it captures higher-order dependencies and theoretical guarantees.
- Weakness — Parameter initialization: seeding strategy appears ad-hoc and could harm reproducibility/robustness.
- Weakness — Limited comparison to recent methods: lacks benchmarks against newer scalable graph-embedding/latent-space approaches (e.g., GNNs).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a major scalability challenge, offers substantial runtime/scale improvements, is novel, empirically strong, and well-presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete exploration of triangle trade-offs, limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, inadequate detail on structured mean-field, and narrower related-work comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address triangle trade-offs, structured mean-field explanation, and broader comparisons.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
