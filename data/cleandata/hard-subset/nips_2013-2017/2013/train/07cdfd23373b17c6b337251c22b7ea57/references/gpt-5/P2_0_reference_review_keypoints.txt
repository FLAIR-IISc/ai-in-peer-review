- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that shares parameters across triangle role configurations to reduce triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Develops a structured mean-field SVI using mini-batched triangles, natural gradients, O(NK) global updates, and an O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” local variational family.  
- Empirical results show good latent-space recovery, competitive/better link prediction on real networks, and a 1M-node, 100-role run in ~4 hours on one 8-core machine.  
- Modeling idea of parameter sharing and structured per-triangle variational factors is sensible and technically well motivated.  
- The SVI formulation and natural-gradient updates follow established practice and appear sound; the mixture-of-deltas approximation is computationally attractive.  
- Synthetic experiments are thorough across N and K and support accuracy and speed claims; scalability results are compelling.  
- Step-size schedule likely has a sign error: κ is reported as −0.5 despite convergence requiring κ∈(0.5,1], needs correction/clarification.  
- Algorithm is not a full generative model for whole graphs, so degree distribution and many single-edge/empty-triple stats are not modeled.  
- The mixture-of-deltas local approximation is heuristic and lacks ablation versus exact O(K^3) updates or analysis of bias introduced.  
- Missing important baselines: no a-MMSB comparison under comparable settings, and absent nonprobabilistic/embedding baselines (e.g., BigCLAM, node2vec) for link prediction.  
- Some evaluation choices (seeding 2% nodes, arbitrary NMI threshold 0.125) could bias results and need sensitivity analysis.  
- Triangle extraction/δ-subsampling costs, memory usage, and sensitivity to δ are not quantified; practical extraction costs should be reported.  
- Paper is generally well written with clear design principles, but some technical details are deferred to the appendix, harming reproducibility.  
- The parameter-sharing trick and structured SVI are novel and a meaningful extension of triangle-motif modeling to large-scale networks.  
- If validated broadly, PTM enabling O(NK) inference for million-node graphs with hundreds of roles would be significant and useful.  
- Pros: principled reduction in parameter dimensionality, scalable structured SVI, practical O(K) local updates, strong scalability demonstration.  
- Cons: step-size bug, limited baseline coverage, heuristic local approximation without ablations, potentially biased evaluation choices, and not modeling whole-graph statistics.  
- Suggested improvements: fix/clarify step-size schedule; add ablations (mixture-of-deltas vs exact, effect of |A|, δ-subsampling); compare to a-MMSB and strong nonprobabilistic baselines; report triangle extraction time and memory; release code.  
- Overall recommendation: strong and timely paper; lean toward acceptance contingent on fixing the step-size issue and expanding empirical comparisons and ablations.
