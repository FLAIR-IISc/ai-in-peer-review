- Tackles scalable latent-space inference for million-node networks; proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles and stochastic natural gradient with claimed O(NK) complexity.  
- Builds on MMSB/MMTM and bag-of-triangles work, addressing a-MMSB’s O(N^2) adjacency dependency and MMTM’s O(K^3) parameter cost.  
- Guided by three principles: succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference.  
- Theoretical contribution: O(NK) complexity guarantee.  
- Empirical strength: substantial speedups and competitive accuracy on synthetic and real datasets versus a-MMSB and MMTM.  
- Clear exposition and well-motivated parameter-sharing design.  
- Thoughtful algorithmic design leveraging stochastic natural gradient and structured mean-field approximations for scalability.  
- Method complexity: many components (bag-of-triangles, parameter sharing, approximations) may hinder adoption.  
- δ-subsampling parameter: guidance is limited; “larger than average degree” is insufficiently analyzed.  
- O(K) approximation in local updates: potential accuracy impact not fully explored; more ablation needed.  
- Limited comparisons to other emerging scalable network modeling techniques.  
- Initialization sensitivity: seeding 2% of nodes appears arbitrary and under-justified.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, theoretically sound, strong empirical gains, clear writing, high impact potential.  
- Against acceptance: complexity, insufficient study of δ and local-update approximation, and limited baselines.  
- Overall: reviewer’s verdict = strong accept (8/10), praising scalability and contribution despite noted gaps.
