Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent space model for large networks using a triangle-based representation. Building on the “bag-of-triangles” idea (Ho et al., NIPS’12), the authors introduce the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which reduces the number of triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) in MMTM to O(K) by sharing parameters across equivalence classes of role configurations: B0 for all-distinct roles, Bxx when two roles match (shared across the minority role), and Bxxx when all three match. They pair PTM with a structured variational inference scheme and a stochastic natural gradient optimizer that subsamples triangles, yielding a per-pass cost of O(N δ^2 K) (with δ-subsampling to handle high-degree nodes) and global updates of O(NK). They also propose a “mixture-of-deltas” variational approximation to reduce local updates from O(K^3) to O(K).

Experiments on synthetic data (from MMSB and a power-law latent model) and several real networks show competitive to improved latent role recovery (by NMI), better or comparable link prediction on larger social networks, and dramatic speedups over MMSB batch variational and MMTM/PTM Gibbs. They report convergence on a 1M-node YouTube graph with K=100 in 4 hours on 8 threads.

Relative to prior work: MMSB (Airoldi et al.) offers edge-based latent membership with O(N^2K^2) inference; a-MMSB (Gopalan et al., NIPS’12) reduces parameter count and uses SVI but still operates on edges and downsampled zeros; MMTM (Ho et al., NIPS’12) uses triangles but is O(K^3) in parameters and Gibbs updates. The paper advances the triangular approach with a more parsimonious parameterization and scalable SVI with structured mean-field, bringing the method to million-node scale.

Strengths
- Clear scalability contribution: O(K) parameterization for triangle motifs and O(NK) global updates; O(N δ^2 K) per data pass with δ-subsampling. Demonstrated orders-of-magnitude speedups.
- Thoughtful variational design: structured mean-field over (si,jk, sj,ik, sk,ij) avoids rank-1 artifacts; stochastic natural gradients and minibatching are appropriate and well-motivated.
- Practical approximation: the mixture-of-deltas local variational family offers an effective O(K) local update; randomized selection of off-diagonal configurations reduces bias in practice.
- Strong empirical section: both synthetic (including adversarial to edge-only models) and real networks; convergence curves, wall-clock times, and comparisons to MMSB and MMTM/PTM Gibbs. The 1M-node/K=100 result is compelling.
- Good discussion of what PTM is and is not (not a full generative network model; focused on latent space inference).

Weaknesses and concerns
- Expressivity trade-off: sharing Bxx across all pairs with a given majority role ignores heterogeneity among different minority roles. This could limit the model when particular role-pairs have distinct closure/transitivity patterns. An ablation quantifying accuracy loss vs. parameter savings would help.
- Approximate local update theory: while restricting q yields a valid lower bound, repeatedly re-randomizing the support A changes the objective across iterations. A brief theoretical justification or convergence discussion (e.g., optimizing the expectation over A, or citing randomized variational bounds) would strengthen claims. Empirical sensitivity to the size/composition of A is also missing.
- δ-subsampling and triangle extraction: the claim of O(1) per sample and <1% preprocessing time deserves more detail. Triangle counting is nontrivial at scale; please specify the enumeration/subsampling algorithm, complexity, and its effect on bias/variance. Include an ablation over δ showing speed/accuracy trade-offs, especially on heavy-tailed graphs.
- Step size specification: the paper states ρt = τ0(τ1 + t)^−κ with κ = −0.5 in experiments. This appears inconsistent with Robbins–Monro conditions (κ should be in (0.5, 1]). Please correct or clarify.
- Evaluation protocol details: for link prediction, ensure triangles are recomputed after edge removal to avoid leakage; clarify how triples containing held-out edges are handled (some may drop out). For role recovery, seeding 2% of nodes with ground-truth is helpful but optimistic; include results from practical initializations (the heuristic you describe) with multiple restarts.
- Limited baselines: while comparisons to MMSB and MMTM/PTM Gibbs are relevant, further baselines that scale and/or capture overlapping structure (e.g., BigCLAM or other scalable overlapping community detectors) would contextualize practical performance, at least on link prediction and role recovery even if models differ.
- Clarity issues: Table 1 (conditional probabilities) is referenced but not shown; some update equations are deferred to the appendix. It would help to include the key cases inline, and to be explicit about the Dirichlet parameter dimensions for Bxxx, Bxx, and B0. Some references are incomplete.

Quality
- The modeling and algorithmic choices are generally sound. The structured mean-field and natural gradient SVI are appropriate. The mixture-of-deltas approximation is sensible but would benefit from additional analysis (or at least sensitivity studies).
- Claims about convergence guarantees hinge on standard SVI conditions; the step-size typo undermines the presentation and should be corrected.
- The model’s non-generativity for full graphs is acknowledged; this limits use-cases but is acceptable given the stated goal.

Clarity
- Overall well-written and organized. Adding missing table/equations, fixing the step-size issue, and expanding on triangle extraction and δ-subsampling would improve readability and reproducibility.

Originality
- The O(K) parameterization for triangle motifs via equivalence-class sharing, combined with structured SVI and an O(K) local update approximation, is a novel and useful contribution beyond MMTM and a-MMSB.

Significance
- Strong practical impact: scaling latent space inference to million-node graphs with many roles on modest hardware. Results are likely to be used and extended.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel, principled modeling that materially improves scalability.
- Solid empirical evidence of speed and competitive accuracy at unprecedented scales for triangle-based latent models.
- Appropriate use of modern variational methods.

Arguments against acceptance
- Incomplete theoretical treatment of the randomized local approximation and δ-subsampling effects.
- Potential expressivity loss from parameter sharing not quantitatively characterized.
- Some clarity/reproducibility issues (step-size typo, missing details/tables).

Suggestions for improvement
- Fix the step-size exponent and cite precise SVI convergence conditions used.
- Provide sensitivity analyses: size of A in local updates; δ choices; K scaling; hyperparameters α, λ.
- Quantify expressivity trade-offs: compare PTM vs MMTM on smaller K where MMTM is feasible.
- Detail triangle extraction/subsampling methodology and its cost.
- Expand baselines where feasible; add non-probabilistic scalable methods for context.
- Release code and scripts for reproducibility.

Recommendation: Accept (borderline-strong). The scalability advances and practical impact outweigh the noted gaps, which are addressable.
