Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes a probabilistic latent space model for very large networks that operates on a succinct “bag-of-triangles” representation rather than the full adjacency matrix. The key modeling contribution is the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which reduces the number of triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) in the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM) to O(K) by sharing parameters across role configurations grouped by the number of distinct roles in a triangle (all same, two same/one different, all different). On the inference side, the authors develop a scalable stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with a structured mean-field posterior over triangle role triplets, and further introduce an O(K) local update via a “mixture-of-deltas” variational family that concentrates mass on diagonal and a randomly refreshed O(K)-size subset of off-diagonal role combinations. They use stochastic natural gradients and mini-batching over triangles, and exploit δ-subsampling of triangles to keep the data size manageable.

Empirically, they show: (i) accurate latent role recovery on synthetic graphs generated from MMSB and a power-law latent model, with K scaling linearly in N; (ii) improved or competitive link prediction vs. MMSB; and (iii) strong scalability, including 100 roles on a 1M-node YouTube network in ~4 hours on 8 threads, with convergence in 2–5 passes.

The work builds on MMSB (Airoldi et al.), a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.), and MMTM (Ho et al.), and aligns with ERGM/motif-based motivations that triangles capture salient social structure. The main novelty is the O(K) parameterization for triangle models and the practical SVI with structured local posteriors.

Strengths
- Novel and bold modeling idea: grouping the O(K^3) triangle role configurations by symmetry/indistinguishability to achieve an O(K) parameterization, analogous in spirit to a-MMSB but extended to triangle motifs. This is a substantial conceptual simplification that opens up previously intractable K.
- Scalable inference design: structured mean-field for role triplets avoids the rank-1 pathology of naive mean-field; the mixture-of-deltas approximation is clever and keeps local updates O(K) while maintaining a valid variational lower bound.
- Strong empirical scaling: convergence in a few passes, million-node network with 100 roles in hours on a single machine, memory-efficient by discarding local variational parameters.
- Practical impact: Triangles often capture essential social structure; enabling triangle-based mixed-membership latent spaces at scale is valuable for community detection, personalization, and link prediction.

Weaknesses and questions
- Complexity accounting and claims: Early in the paper you state “time complexity O(NK).” Later, overall runtime is effectively O(|I|·K) with |I|=O(Nδ^2) after δ-subsampling, plus an O(NK) global step. Please clarify the end-to-end complexity per pass and make the dependence on δ explicit in all claims (and discuss how δ should scale with N and degree distributions).
- Expressiveness vs. parsimony: The PTM shares parameters across many triangle configurations. While this enables scaling, it reduces expressivity relative to MMTM’s O(K^3) tensor. Provide more analysis or ablations showing when PTM’s sharing hurts (or does not) on networks with heterogeneous cross-role triadic interactions, and whether accuracy degrades as role diversity increases.
- Approximate local updates: The mixture-of-deltas with a randomly refreshed set A changes the variational family across iterations. While any fixed family yields a lower bound, the objective effectively changes as A changes. Please clarify: (i) is the resulting procedure optimizing a single, well-defined bound in expectation? (ii) do you have empirical or theoretical guarantees that this does not introduce bias or instability?
- Evaluation protocol:
  - The role recovery uses a hard threshold at 0.125 and seeds 2% of nodes with ground-truth roles for all methods. This is helpful but potentially favorable to methods that can quickly reinforce seeds. Please include sensitivity to the threshold and to unseeded initialization (you sketch an alternative heuristic; showing results would strengthen the case).
  - Baselines: For synthetic data, only MMSB batch variational and MMTM/PTM Gibbs are included. Given that a-MMSB explicitly targets scalability in K, including it (even if edge-based) on the same hardware would make the comparisons more compelling. For link prediction, triangle-based PTM is compared to edge-based MMSB; broader comparisons (e.g., scalable latent embeddings or overlapping community methods) would better contextualize performance.
  - Runtime comparisons to prior works use numbers reported in those papers; differences in implementation and hardware complicate such comparisons. Where possible, include your reimplementations or common hardware runs, even on a smaller network.
- Not a generative network model: As acknowledged, PTM models only connected triples (2–3 edges) and does not enforce edge consistency across overlapping triangles. This is fine for latent inference, but the limitation should be highlighted more prominently in the abstract/intro and discussed in terms of downstream tasks.
- Clarity: Some important details (e.g., Table 1 of conditional probabilities, exact forms of natural gradients, link prediction scoring formula with marginalization over k) are deferred to the appendix. A concise summary in the main text would improve readability and reproducibility.

Quality
Overall technically sound, with sensible variational derivations and careful engineering choices. The main theoretical gap is a lack of analysis of the approximation error from the mixture-of-deltas local family and the implications of resampling A. Empirical evidence is strong but could be broadened in baselines and ablations.

Clarity
Generally well-written and organized. Please fix the complexity claim inconsistency, surface key formulas from the appendix, and better separate the per-pass costs (local vs. global).

Originality
High. The O(K) parameterization for triangle-based mixed-membership models and the specific structured SVI with O(K) local updates are, to my knowledge, new and impactful.

Significance
High. Making motif-based mixed-membership latent spaces practical at million-node scale is valuable, and the approach is likely to be adopted and extended.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear and substantial new idea (O(K) PTM) with significant practical impact
- Well-designed scalable variational inference with thoughtful approximations
- Strong empirical scaling and competitive accuracy
- Advances triangle-based latent modeling beyond prior work

Cons against acceptance
- Some overstatements/ambiguities in complexity claims
- Limited baseline coverage (esp. a-MMSB and other scalable alternatives)
- Limited analysis of approximation effects and expressiveness trade-offs
- Seeding and thresholding choices in evaluation could bias results

Recommendation
Accept. The paper introduces a significant new concept and direction in large-scale latent space inference by reconciling triangle-based modeling with linear-in-K parameterization and scalable SVI. Despite some evaluation and theoretical gaps, the novelty and demonstrated scalability outweigh the imperfections. I encourage the authors to clarify complexity, add ablations/baselines where feasible, and better articulate the approximation trade-offs.
