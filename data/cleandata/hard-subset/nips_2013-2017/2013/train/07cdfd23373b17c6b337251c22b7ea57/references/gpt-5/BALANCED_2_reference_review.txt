Summary and relation to prior work
This paper tackles scalable probabilistic latent space inference for large networks by moving from an edge-centric to a triangle-centric representation. Building on the “bag-of-triangles” idea (Ho et al., NIPS 2011), the authors propose the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which shares parameters across role-triplet configurations to reduce triangle-generation parameters from O(K^3) (in MMTM) to O(K). They pair PTM with a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm that uses a structured mean-field approximation over per-triangle role triplets, and an O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” approximation for local updates. Stochastic natural gradients with mini-batches of triangles yield an algorithm that empirically converges in 2–5 passes. The approach is contrasted with MMSB (Airoldi et al., JMLR 2008), a-MMSB (Gopalan et al., NIPS 2012), and MMTM (Ho et al., NIPS 2011). Experiments show fast convergence and competitive or better performance on synthetic latent role recovery, held-out link prediction, and large real networks (up to 1M nodes, 100 roles) with strong wall-clock gains over prior work. The paper also references ERGM-style higher-order statistics and SVI (Hoffman et al., JMLR 2013) and natural gradients (Amari, 1998).

Strengths
- Clear modeling advance: the O(K) parameterization (B0, Bxx, Bxxx) is a principled reduction via equivalence classes of role-triplets that preserves the key triadic distinctions (all-same, two-same, all-different), making large-K inference feasible.
- Scalable inference: structured mean-field over triangle role triplets avoids rank-1 artifacts of naive mean-field; the mixture-of-deltas yields O(K) local updates and keeps the bound valid; SVI with natural gradients is well-motivated and practical.
- Strong empirical results: consistent fast convergence (2–5 passes), large speedups vs. a-MMSB and MMTM, and the 1M-node/100-role result in hours on a single multicore machine is compelling.
- Sensible use of δ-subsampling and triangle mini-batching; memory efficiency by discarding local variational parameters after accumulating sufficient statistics.

Weaknesses
- Expressivity vs. parsimony tradeoff: sharing a single parameter across all inter-role triplets (B0) and using Bxx to summarize all “two-same” cases may sacrifice important inter-role heterogeneity captured by MMTM’s O(K^3) tensor. The paper does not characterize regimes where this loss matters (e.g., networks where triadic patterns depend strongly on specific role identities).
- Comparisons: baselines are limited and sometimes unfair in runtime. MMSB batch variational and MMTM/PTM Gibbs are single-threaded while the proposed method uses 4–8 threads; more scalable or modern baselines (e.g., a-MMSB with careful engineering, heuristic link predictors like common neighbors/Adamic–Adar, or other scalable overlapping-community methods) would strengthen the case, especially on link prediction.
- Evaluation choices: seeding 2% of nodes with true roles could bias latent recovery; while shared across methods, it reduces realism. For link prediction, since PTM is not a generative model over edges, the proposed ranking proxy (triad completion probability marginalizing over k) is reasonable but ad hoc; comparisons to strong triangle-based heuristics would help.
- Clarity/internals: there is some inconsistency in complexity statements (O(NK) vs. O(Nδ^2K) per pass; the latter seems the cost for a full pass over triangles). More detail on the size/sampling of the off-diagonal set A and its sensitivity would improve reproducibility. The paper claims unbiased gradients by uniform triangle sampling, but a brief justification given overlapping motifs would help readers.
- Model selection and sensitivity: no discussion on choosing K, nor sensitivity to α, λ, δ, and SVI step sizes. An ablation showing the effect of δ and the mixture-of-deltas size on accuracy/speed would be valuable.

Quality
Technically solid overall: the variational objective is standard; the structured local updates are well-motivated; stochastic natural gradients are appropriate. The mixture-of-deltas family is a legitimate restriction preserving the lower bound. Claims about unbiasedness of the stochastic gradient for the variational bound are plausible, though a short derivation would strengthen the presentation. The expressivity loss due to parameter sharing is the main modeling caveat; the paper would benefit from diagnostics quantifying this tradeoff.

Clarity
Generally clear and well-organized. The role-triplet equivalence classes and induced probabilities are explained nicely. Some notational issues (complexity statements) and missing details (exact construction/size of A, sensitivity) detract slightly. Figures/tables (referred to) appear to support claims; making runtime comparisons fairer (threads, implementation notes) should be clarified.

Originality
The O(K) parameterization for triadic models and the specific structured SVI with a mixture-of-deltas local family are novel and useful. Building on triangles per se is not new (Ho et al., NIPS 2011), but the combination of parsimonious modeling plus scalable inference is a meaningful contribution.

Significance
High practical significance: enabling 100+ roles on million-node networks on a single machine is impactful. If accuracy is competitive while orders faster, practitioners will use this. The approach encourages further work on higher-order motif-based inference at scale.

Arguments for acceptance
- Elegant, principled reduction in parameterization enabling large-K triadic modeling.
- Scalable, well-engineered SVI with convincing wall-clock wins and convergence behavior.
- Strong results on large real networks; clear path to practical adoption.

Arguments against acceptance
- Potential loss of expressivity; limited analysis of when PTM may underfit compared to MMTM.
- Baseline comparisons and runtime fairness could be improved; link prediction baselines are thin.
- Some clarity gaps (complexity, sensitivity, construction of A) and limited ablations.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a theoretical or empirical characterization of when PTM’s parameter sharing harms performance; e.g., synthetic setups where inter-role triadic probabilities vary and compare PTM vs. MMTM.
- Add baselines: common neighbors/Adamic–Adar, BigCLAM/other scalable overlapping-community methods, and a well-optimized a-MMSB; report both single-thread and multi-thread wall-times.
- Clarify complexity: per mini-batch, per pass, and dependence on δ; report sensitivity to δ and mini-batch size.
- Detail the mixture-of-deltas: exact sizes for diagonal/off-diagonal sets, re-sampling strategy, and sensitivity experiments.
- Discuss model selection for K and hyperparameter robustness (α, λ); consider simple online model selection heuristics based on the variational bound.
- For link prediction, ensure held-out edges are removed before triangle extraction and compare to triangle-based heuristics.

Recommendation: weak accept
The paper presents a timely and practically important advance—parsimonious triadic modeling with scalable SVI—supported by strong empirical evidence. Addressing the fairness of comparisons and providing more analysis of the expressivity tradeoff would further strengthen the work.
