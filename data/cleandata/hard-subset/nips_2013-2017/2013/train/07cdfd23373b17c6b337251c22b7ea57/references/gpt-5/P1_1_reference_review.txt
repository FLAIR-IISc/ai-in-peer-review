Summary
The paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space model for large networks using a triangular (motif-based) representation. It builds on prior work showing that bags of triangular motifs can succinctly capture informative higher-order structure while being much smaller than the O(N2) edge representation. The key contributions are: (1) a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces the number of triangle-generating parameters from O(K3) in MMTM to O(K) by grouping role-triplet configurations by the number of distinct roles and sharing parameters within groups (Bxxx, Bxx, B0); (2) a structured mean-field variational inference approach combined with stochastic natural gradient updates over minibatches of triangles; and (3) an O(K) per-triangle local update via a “mixture-of-deltas” approximation that restricts mass to K diagonal triplets and O(K) randomly chosen off-diagonal triplets, enabling parallel inference that scales to million-node networks and 100+ roles. Experiments on synthetic and real networks show competitive or better latent role recovery and link prediction vs. MMSB/a-MMSB/MMTM, with orders-of-magnitude lower runtime (e.g., 12 minutes vs. 8 days on Brightkite; 4 minutes vs. 18.5 hours on Stanford).

Relation to prior work
The work is positioned relative to MMSB/a-MMSB (Airoldi et al.; Gopalan et al., NIPS), ERGMs (Hunter et al.), and the mixed-membership triangular model MMTM (Ho et al., NIPS), and leverages stochastic variational inference and natural gradients (Hoffman et al.; Amari). The central novelty over MMTM is the O(K) parameter sharing across role-triplet classes, and over a-MMSB is using triangles rather than edges, which better capture triadic closure and motifs at scale.

Strengths
- Scalability: The O(K) parameterization, O(K) local updates, and minibatched SVI enable impressive wall-clock improvements with multi-threading. The decision to discard local variational factors between iterations is practical for memory-limited settings.
- Modeling idea: Grouping role-triplets by cardinality of distinct roles (all same, two same, all different) is a simple, clear device that yields the O(K) parameter reduction while retaining key motif semantics (e.g., triadic closure via Bxxx and Bxx vs. B0).
- Inference design: The structured mean-field (grouping per-triangle role assignments) is appropriate; the naive mean-field critique is valid. The stochastic natural gradient formulation is standard and effective.
- Empirical results: Comprehensive synthetic latent-space recovery with growing K; strong link prediction on larger social networks; and very fast convergence (2–5 passes) on million-node networks.

Weaknesses and questions
- Complexity claims: The paper states “yielding a fast inference procedure that has time complexity O(NK)” but later the overall cost is O(|I|K) with |I| = O(Nδ2) triangles after subsampling. Please clarify the asymptotic per-pass cost and separate local O(|I|K) and global O(NK) updates consistently.
- Step-size schedule: The Robbins–Monro conditions are stated, but the experimental setting gives κ = −0.5, which would make ρt grow with t and violates convergence conditions. This looks like a typo; please correct and report the actual κ used (typically 0.5 < κ ≤ 1).
- Variational family and guarantees: The “mixture-of-deltas” local approximation restricts support to a randomly chosen set A that is re-sampled at each local update. While any fixed restriction defines a valid ELBO, changing the variational family across iterations complicates the usual SVI convergence guarantees. Please formalize the objective actually being optimized (e.g., an expected ELBO over A) and discuss convergence and potential bias. Also provide ablations comparing exact O(K3) local updates vs. O(K) approximation for small K to quantify approximation error.
- Expressiveness trade-offs: PTM shares parameters across all pairs in the “two-same-one-different” case and uses a single B0 for all-distinct roles. This precludes modeling role-specific heterophily/assortativity and any triad-type that depends on particular role combinations. Please discuss scenarios where this hurts (e.g., strong disassortative structure with specific role pairs) and provide a failure-case or sensitivity analysis.
- Triangle extraction and δ-subsampling: Stating O(1) time per sample is optimistic. Please detail the triangle enumeration/subsampling pipeline, its complexity, and wall-clock proportion vs. inference. Provide sensitivity to δ and the effect on accuracy and runtime.
- Link prediction protocol: The scoring for PTM marginalizes over the third node k. Is this done exactly or by sampling? What is the computational cost? To ensure fairness, please clarify whether PTM has access to any triad involving the held-out edge after edge removal and whether MMSB uses the same node set without triangles.
- Reproducibility: Many crucial details are in the Appendix (update equations, natural gradients). Code/public release or more algorithmic pseudocode would help reproducibility.

Quality
Technically sound overall. The PTM parameter tying is clearly specified, and the variational setup is standard. However, the step-size issue and the changing variational family for local updates should be clarified. Some evaluation choices (e.g., latent recovery seeding with 2% ground truth) are reasonable but should be complemented with more realistic, fully unsupervised initializations (the suggested heuristic could be evaluated). Statistical efficiency vs. expressiveness trade-offs merit deeper discussion and empirical stress tests.

Clarity
Generally well-written and organized, with a clear generative story and algorithm outline. A few issues: fix the κ sign; unify complexity statements; the sampling of A lists “(a,a,b), (a,b,a) or (a,a,b)” twice—likely meant (a,a,b), (a,b,a), (b,a,a). Consider presenting the Eijk coding and equivalence classes in a compact table in the main text and providing a small running example. Explicitly separate per-pass complexity into local vs. global to avoid confusion.

Originality
The parsimonious O(K) parameterization for triadic mixed-membership models and the O(K) local SVI via mixture-of-deltas are, to my knowledge, novel combinations relative to prior NIPS work. The approach is a principled and interesting middle ground between MMTM and a-MMSB.

Significance
High practical significance: orders-of-magnitude speedups at million-node scale with competitive accuracy. The approach should be of interest to researchers and practitioners doing large-scale network representation learning and community detection, and it may stimulate further work on scalable higher-order motif modeling.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong scalability with compelling empirical speedups
- Simple, elegant parameter-tying that preserves key motif information
- Sound SVI framework with structured mean-field
- Good accuracy on synthetic and real networks, including link prediction

Cons for acceptance
- Some theoretical and clarity issues (step-size, changing variational family) need correction
- Limited discussion of expressiveness limits and failure modes
- Missing ablations on δ, local O(K) approximation, and initialization strategies

Recommendation
Borderline accept leaning to accept, contingent on clarifying the step-size schedule, unifying complexity claims, and adding brief ablations or discussion on the O(K) local approximation and expressiveness trade-offs. The scalability and practical impact are strong. Confidence: medium.
