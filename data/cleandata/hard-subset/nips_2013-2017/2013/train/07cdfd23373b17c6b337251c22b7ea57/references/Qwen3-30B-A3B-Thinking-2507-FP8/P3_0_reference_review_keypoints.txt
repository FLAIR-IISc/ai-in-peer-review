- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) and uses stochastic variational inference with O(NK) complexity for scalable latent space inference.  
- Combines triangular network representation, parsimonious parameterization, and stochastic inference to handle networks with 1M nodes and 100 roles in ~4 hours on one machine.  

Strengths:
- Technical approach is well-motivated and theoretically sound in reducing parameter complexity via parameter sharing.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across synthetic and real networks and shows clear scalability gains versus baselines.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations of the triangular representation and discuss accuracy–scalability trade-offs.  
- Provides implementation details (δ-subsampling, initialization, hyperparameters) that aid reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- Triangular representation likely incurs information loss; the paper lacks rigorous quantitative comparison to edge-based methods.  
- The parameter sharing scheme (Bxxx, Bxx, B0) appears arbitrary and lacks principled justification or exploration of alternatives.  
- Real-world experiments are limited to a few networks and may not demonstrate generalizability across diverse network types.  
- The stochastic variational inference lacks formal theoretical convergence guarantees.

Clarity:
- Paper is well-organized with clear exposition of core concepts and effective figures/tables.  
- Explanation of the parameter sharing equivalence classes (Table 1) is ambiguous and needs clarification.  
- δ-subsampling is insufficiently explained intuitively and implementation specifics (e.g., generation of random set A) are missing.

Originality:
- Novel and impactful reduction of parameter space from O(K^3) to O(K) via strategic sharing.  
- Novel integration of triangular representation with stochastic variational inference for scalable latent models.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental over MMTM and similar to sharing in a-MMSB; lacks comparison to recent deep learning approaches.

Significance:
- Directly addresses a major scalability bottleneck in latent network inference and enables previously intractable analyses.  
- Demonstrated practical impact (large-scale runs far faster than prior methods) though accuracy improvements are limited or unproven.  
- Scope is focused on community detection/link prediction; broader applications and comparisons to GNNs are lacking.

Overall assessment:
- Well-executed, technically strong paper with significant scalability contribution, but needs stronger accuracy validation, parameterization justification, and broader evaluations.

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions.

Specific suggestions:
- Add quantitative accuracy comparisons between triangular and edge-based methods (e.g., NMI, AUC).  
- Provide principled motivation or experiments exploring alternative parameter-sharing patterns.  
- Broaden real-world datasets to include varied network types (social, biological, citation).  
- Discuss/compare to recent deep learning (GNN) approaches for scalable network modeling.  
- Clarify and better justify δ-subsampling and provide missing implementation details (e.g., how random set A is generated).
