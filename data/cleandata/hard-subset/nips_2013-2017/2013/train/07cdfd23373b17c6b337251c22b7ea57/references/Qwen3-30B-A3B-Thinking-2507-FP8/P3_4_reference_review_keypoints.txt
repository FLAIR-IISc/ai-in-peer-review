- Paper proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM): reduces parameters from O(K³) to O(K) via parameter sharing, uses a bag-of-triangles and stochastic variational inference with O(NK) complexity for million-node networks.  
- Major strength: substantial scalability improvement enabling inference on 1M+ node networks in hours (vs days) with compelling Brightkite and Stanford results.  
- Major strength: novel three-way grouping of role configurations (all same, two same, all distinct) that cuts O(K³) → O(K) parameters.  
- Major strength: careful, practical implementation (δ-subsampling, parallelization, memory efficiency) making the method usable at scale.  
- Major strength: comprehensive evaluation on synthetic and real networks using NMI, link prediction, and held-out likelihood.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical justification for why triangle-based representation preserves sufficient information for latent inference; potential information loss not rigorously analyzed.  
- Weakness: incomplete comparisons — misses recent scalable graph representation methods (GraphSAGE, DeepWalk, node2vec) to position the work in the broader landscape.  
- Weakness: limited diversity of real-world experiments (only Brightkite and Stanford), reducing evidence of general applicability across domains.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported to support claims of "competitive or improved accuracy."  
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of trade-offs from ignoring empty/single-edge triples and how that affects tasks like link prediction on sparse networks.  
- Critical assessment: elegant solution to the O(K³) bottleneck with convincing scalability results, but would be strengthened by theoretical justification for triangles, broader comparisons, more datasets, and significance testing.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, technically sound, practically impactful; suggested revisions: add theoretical motivation for triangles, compare to recent embedding methods, expand real-world experiments, and include statistical tests.
