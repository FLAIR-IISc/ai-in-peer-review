- Problem & contribution: Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) to scale motif-based latent-space inference for large networks.  
- Modeling idea: Reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) by tying triad interactions into Bxxx, Bxx, and B0 based on distinct-role counts.  
- Inference method: Uses structured mean-field variational inference with stochastic natural-gradient ascent.  
- Computational trick: Proposes an O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” approximation to avoid O(K^3) per-triangle costs.  
- Subsampling: Employs triangle mini-batch subsampling (δ) to handle high-degree nodes and obtain unbiased gradient estimates.  
- Complexity claim: Global updates scale roughly O(NK); local updates scale O(|triangles|×K) with truncation, enabling large-scale inference.  
- Empirical results: Recovers mixed memberships accurately on synthetic data and runs substantially faster than baselines.  
- Real-data scalability: Demonstrates runtime and convergence on networks up to ~1.1M nodes and K≈100 in hours on a multi-core machine.  
- Baseline performance: Shows favorable link-prediction vs MMSB and major runtime gains over MMTM/PTM Gibbs samplers.  
- Strength — theory/practice: Solid variational formulation and correct use of mini-batch SVI with natural gradients.  
- Strength — approximation: Mixture-of-deltas truncation is practical, well-motivated, and clearly specified.  
- Strength — implementation: Convincing complexity analysis and practical engineering (discarding locals, parallelism).  
- Strength — experiments: Extensive synthetic and large real-network experiments with competitive accuracy and strong runtime improvements.  
- Concern — randomized truncation: Resampling A across updates changes bounds and monotonicity; need clarification on unbiasedness, variance bounds, or references for randomized truncations.  
- Concern — step-size typo: κ listed as −0.5 contradicts Robbins–Monro; authors should correct and report the actual κ used.  
- Concern — baseline fairness: Runtimes mix threading; MMSB is batch rather than stochastic a-MMSB; δ-subsampling not applied to baselines—need comparable a-MMSB and equal hardware/threading.  
- Concern — scoring bias: Triangle-based scoring marginalizes over third nodes and may favor PTM versus pairwise MMSB; suggest augmenting MMSB or comparing to other scalable methods (BigCLAM, spectral).  
- Concern — expressiveness tradeoff: Parameter tying (B0, Bxx) may underfit inter-role triples; request synthetic ablations or partially-untied variants to characterize underfitting.  
- Concern — evaluation details: Report sensitivity to δ and mini-batch size, number of off-diagonal tuples in A, ablation of full vs truncated local updates, and memory/runtime breakdown including triangle extraction.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally readable with a helpful algorithm, but several materials (Table 1, Appendix, simulator details) are missing and some notation needs defining at first use.  
- Complexity clarity: Reconcile statements about total complexity—local step is O(|S|K) per iteration with truncation while global is O(NK).  
- Link-prediction protocol: Clarify how held-out edges are excluded from triangle extraction to avoid leakage.  
- Originality: O(K) parameter-tying and the structured SVI with randomized truncation are novel and address a core scaling bottleneck.  
- Significance: Enables 100-role inference on million-node graphs on a single machine—practically impactful if theoretical and baseline concerns are addressed.  
- Pros summary: Strong scalability, clear algorithmic innovations, solid empirical demonstrations, and practical implementation details.  
- Cons summary: Unfair baselines, unclear theoretical justification for randomized truncation and step-size, and potential underfitting from aggressive tying.  
- Recommendation: Accept (borderline-strong) pending clarifications and stronger baselines.  
- Reviewer confidence: High on technical and empirical assessment; medium on completeness due to missing appendix/tables.
