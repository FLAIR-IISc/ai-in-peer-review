- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation and parameter-sharing to reduce triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) time complexity enabling inference on networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of roles.
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speed improvements while maintaining or improving accuracy on synthetic and real networks.
- Clearly identifies scalability limitations of MMSB, a-MMSB, and MMTM and articulates three principles for scalable inference.
- Provides a theoretically sound justification for the O(K) parameter-sharing via careful enumeration of role configurations.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing to relevant baselines (MMSB, MMTM, PTM Gibbs) across varied synthetic settings.
- Reports quantitative (NMI, link prediction) and qualitative (runtime, convergence) advantages over baselines.
- Shows PTM scales to N=1M and K=100 in ~4 hours while baselines fail or are much slower.
- Acknowledges limitations: bag-of-triangles loses some network information and O(K) parameterization may be restrictive for some structures.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized with consistent notation and a reproducible Algorithm 1.
- Uses figures and tables effectively to illustrate triangle equivalence classes and convergence behavior.
- Novel contribution by combining bag-of-triangles, parameter-sharing, and a tailored stochastic VI; O(K) parameterization is the central innovation.
- Positions the work well in the literature with appropriate citations and contrasts to prior methods.
- Demonstrates substantial practical significance by making latent-space modeling feasible for large real-world networks.
- Reports 8–100x speedups on real datasets (Brightkite, Stanford), reducing runtimes from days to hours.
- Strengths: clear scalability focus, novel O(K) parameterization, solid inference algorithm, thorough experiments, real-world scalability.
- Weaknesses: potential information loss from motif-based representation, possible limits of O(K) expressiveness, limited comparisons to newer scalable methods.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with high practical impact; recommend acceptance.
