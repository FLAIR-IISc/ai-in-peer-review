- Summary: Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) with bag-of-triangles and stochastic natural gradient ascent achieving O(NK) inference; demonstrates competitive accuracy and 4-hour inference on a 1M-node YouTube network.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on MMSB/a-MMSB, triangular representations/MMTM, and stochastic variational inference, positioning PTM to fill gaps in representation, parsimony, and scalability.  
- Strength — Scalability: O(NK) complexity enables probabilistic latent inference on networks previously intractable.  
- Strength — Parsimonious modeling: Parameter sharing across role groups reduces complexity without sacrificing performance.  
- Strength — Theoretical foundation: Clear variational framework and justified use of stochastic natural gradient ascent.  
- Strength — Empirical evaluation: Tests on synthetic and real datasets with NMI and link-prediction metrics, comparing to strong baselines.  
- Strength — Clarity and practicality: Well-written exposition and practical choices (δ-subsampling, parallelization, memory optimizations).  
- Weakness — Structured mean-field: Chosen structured mean-field approximation is somewhat heuristic and needs deeper justification and empirical study.  
- Weakness — Mini-batch sensitivity: Lacks analysis of sensitivity to mini-batch size and trade-offs for convergence and accuracy.  
- Weakness — Baselines: Missing comparisons to more recent scalable network methods (e.g., graph neural network–based approaches).  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter tuning: Insufficient detail on tuning procedures and sensitivity to hyperparameters.  
- Weakness — Dataset diversity: Real-world evaluation could include more diverse datasets beyond the YouTube example.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles an important scalability problem with a novel, theoretically grounded method and compelling empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs deeper analysis of the structured mean-field, broader baseline comparisons, and hyperparameter sensitivity studies.  
- Overall assessment: Strong contribution with significant impact potential; minor weaknesses do not undermine the work.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to justify structured mean-field, analyze mini-batch sensitivity, expand baseline comparisons, and detail hyperparameter tuning.
