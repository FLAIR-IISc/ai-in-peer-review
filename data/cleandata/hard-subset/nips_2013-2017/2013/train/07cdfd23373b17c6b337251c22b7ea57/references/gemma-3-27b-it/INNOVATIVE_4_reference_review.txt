This paper presents a compelling approach to scaling probabilistic latent space inference for large networks, addressing a critical bottleneck in the field. The core idea – leveraging a bag-of-triangles representation combined with a parsimonious statistical model and stochastic variational inference – is genuinely novel and, if successful, represents a significant step forward. The O(NK) complexity is a huge improvement over existing methods, and the reported results on a 1M-node YouTube network are impressive. The authors convincingly argue for the limitations of prior approaches and clearly articulate the principles guiding their design choices.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of bag-of-triangles, parsimonious modeling via parameter sharing, and stochastic natural gradient ascent is a genuinely new contribution. The parameter sharing strategy within the PTM is a clever and effective way to address the scalability issues of prior triangular models.
* **Scalability:** The claimed O(NK) complexity is a major advantage. Demonstrating inference on a 1M-node network in 4 hours is strong evidence of this scalability.
* **Clear Motivation & Problem Statement:** The paper clearly identifies the scalability challenges in latent space inference and persuasively argues for the importance of the proposed approach.
* **Strong Empirical Results:**  The synthetic experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in recovering latent roles, and the comparison to baselines is thorough. The real-world results on the YouTube network solidify the practical impact.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations and algorithmic descriptions appear sound, and the use of stochastic natural gradients is a sensible choice.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Structured Mean-Field Approximation:** The justification for the structured mean-field approximation, while mentioning the shortcomings of a naive mean-field approach, could be more detailed. A deeper dive into *why* this specific structure is beneficial would be helpful.
* **δ-subsampling:** The impact of the δ-subsampling on accuracy isn’t thoroughly explored. While the authors state it approximately preserves the distribution, a more detailed analysis or sensitivity analysis to δ would strengthen the argument.
* **Synthetic Data Generation:** The description of the synthetic data generation process, particularly the “power-law” network model, could benefit from more detail. Knowing the specific parameters used and how they affect the difficulty of the inference task would be valuable.
* **Comparison to a-MMSB on Large Graphs:** While the paper convincingly argues against a-MMSB’s scaling, a direct comparison on the 1M YouTube network – even if a-MMSB fails to complete – would provide a more compelling illustration of the performance gap.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance dependency on hyperparameter settings like α, λ, and the step size parameters (τ0, τ1, κ) isn’t thoroughly investigated. A sensitivity analysis would be valuable.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* This paper introduces a genuinely novel approach to a long-standing problem in network analysis.
* The reported scalability results are impressive and open up new possibilities for analyzing very large networks.
* The technical contribution is substantial, combining several key ideas in a creative and effective way.
* The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the motivation, methods, and results.
* Despite some minor weaknesses, the overall impact of this work is significant.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The justification for the chosen structured mean-field approximation could be more robust.
* The impact of δ-subsampling on accuracy needs further investigation.
*  The hyperparameter sensitivity of the algorithm isn’t fully explored.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant potential impact. The novelty of the approach, the impressive scalability results, and the clear presentation make it a valuable contribution to the field. While there are minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I believe the potential benefits of this approach far outweigh the limitations.  I would lean towards **Accept**, with encouragement to the authors to address the minor weaknesses in a camera-ready version. This work represents a bold step towards enabling large-scale probabilistic latent space inference, and I am excited to see how it will be used by the community.
