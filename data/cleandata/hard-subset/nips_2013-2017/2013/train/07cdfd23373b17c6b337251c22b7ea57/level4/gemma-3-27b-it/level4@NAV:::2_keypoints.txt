- Introduces a novel “bag-of-triangles” technique that models triangle count statistics to learn social network structure.  
- Central contribution: defines K roles via triangle configurations (rather than all pairwise community interactions).  
- Targeted parameterization improves scalability compared to full pairwise models.  
- Leverages stochastic variational inference and provides both global and local update rules.  
- Method is a reasonable combination of aMMSB and MMTM, but the presentation lacks clarity.  
- Local update approximation is intuitive and shows promise despite relying on standard SVI techniques.  
- Suggestion: discuss extending the approach to directed networks in future work.  
- Claim that PTM is a latent-space counterpart to ERGMs is overstated and needs tempering.  
- Interpretability of the learned latent space is unclear and requires further exploration.  
- The difference between Delta1 and Delta2 is not clearly explained.  
- The rationale for approximating triangle counts via subsampling (delta) needs stronger justification.  
- Consider using an explicit binary representation for identifying “non-zero roles.”  
- Sensitivity to the arbitrary threshold value (0.125) should be investigated.  
- Experiments should specify which latent-space model generated the power-law networks.  
- Reported O(K^3) PTM CGS complexity appears inconsistent with the earlier O(K) parameter claim.  
- Concern about performance on sparser graphs and biased triangle distributions from edge removal (only a 10% holdout).  
- Unexpected result: PTM outperforming MMSB on data generated by MMSB requires further investigation.  
- Overall: presents a scalable mixed-membership blockmodeling approach using triangle counts with claimed O(NK) complexity.
