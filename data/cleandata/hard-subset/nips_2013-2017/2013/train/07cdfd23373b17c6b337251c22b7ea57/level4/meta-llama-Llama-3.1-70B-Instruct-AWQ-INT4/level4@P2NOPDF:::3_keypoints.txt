- Presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model and a novel parameter-sharing subclass enabling efficient inference.  
- Uses stochastic variational inference to infer posteriors over very large networks.  
- Paper is well-written, well-executed, and overall acceptable.  
- Clarify the term "parsimonious" on page 1, as it may be unfamiliar in probabilistic modeling contexts.  
- The claim that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy (citing Gopalan et al.) may be misleading and should be re-evaluated.  
- The analogy to LDA on page 4 is not fully convincing since the triangle model can generate non-network-like objects.  
- Cite Hoffman et al. in addition to Amari and Sato for handling local variables in the stochastic variational inference setting.  
- Provide more detail on how power-law networks are generated in the latent space (page 6) for clarity and reproducibility.  
- Compare the method to Gopalan et al.'s algorithm for speeding MMSB, not just to MMSB batch, for a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Minor edits: replace "mixture-membership" with "mixed-membership" (page 2).  
- Suggest removing the final sentence on page 8, as the preceding sentence gives a stronger conclusion.  
- In the bibliography, cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."
