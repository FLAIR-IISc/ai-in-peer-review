This paper presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. The authors introduce a powerful subclass of this model that utilizes a novel parameter-sharing scheme and apply stochastic variational inference to perform posterior inference on very large networks. The paper is well-written, well-executed, and presents a valuable contribution. I recommend it for acceptance.

However, I have several points that the authors should address to improve the paper:

**Major Points**

*   **p1, "Parsimonious modeling":** This term is central to the paper's motivation, but it is not a standard expression in the context of probabilistic models. I suggest the authors add a sentence to unpack this term and clarify its specific meaning for the reader.

*   **p1, Characterization of Gopalan et al.:** The sentence in the introduction regarding Gopalan et al. is misleading. My understanding is that their method of down-sampling zero-valued entries does not compromise accuracy. This statement should be revised to more accurately reflect the contribution of the cited work.

*   **p4, Analogy to LDA:** While I am sympathetic to the pragmatic utility of the triangle model, the defense of it by analogy to LDA is not appropriate. The generative process of LDA always produces an object that is a valid (if nonsensical) document. In contrast, the generative process for the proposed model can produce objects that are not valid networks. Although this does not diminish the model's practical value, the justification via LDA is flawed and should be reconsidered.

*   **p5, Citation for Stochastic VI:** The paper should cite Hoffman et al. (2013) alongside Amari and Sato. Sato's algorithm does not apply in this scenario, which involves "local variables" (using Hoffman et al.'s terminology). The necessary theory for applying stochastic variational inference in this setting is developed in Hoffman et al.

*   **p6, Experimental Details:** The description of how power-law networks were generated in the latent space is vague. Please provide more details on this procedure to ensure the experiments are clear and reproducible.

*   **p6, Experimental Comparison:** The authors cite Gopalan et al. as a method for speeding up MMSB, which makes it a critical baseline for comparison. The experimental evaluation should include a comparison to their algorithm in addition to (or instead of) the batch MMSB baseline.

**Minor Comments**

*   **p2:** "mixture-membership" should be "mixed-membership".
*   **p8:** The final sentence of the paper is weaker than the penultimate one. I suggest removing the last sentence for a stronger conclusion.
*   **Bibliography:** Please cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference".
