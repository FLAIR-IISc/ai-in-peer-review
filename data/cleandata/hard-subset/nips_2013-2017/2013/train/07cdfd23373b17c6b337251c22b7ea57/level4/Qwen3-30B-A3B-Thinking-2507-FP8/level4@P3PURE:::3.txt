

The authors develop a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. They introduce a powerful subclass of the model that cleverly shares parameters, enabling the use of stochastic variational inference to infer the posterior for very large networks. The paper is well written and well executed. I recommend acceptance.

**Major Comments:**
p1: The term "parsimonious" is used without definition. This is not a standard expression in probabilistic modeling contexts, and its meaning is critical to the paper's contribution. Please unpack this term in a sentence.

p1: The claim that "down-sampling the zeros" compromises accuracy (as stated in the introduction) contradicts Gopalan et al.'s findings. Their work demonstrates that down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy, making your statement misleading.

p4: While I acknowledge the triangle model's pragmatic utility, the defense via appeal to LDA is inappropriate. LDA's generative process produces a valid document (albeit nonsensical), whereas your model can generate non-network objects. This distinction matters for model validity, even if the model itself is practical.

p5: Cite Hoffman et al. (with Amari and Sato) for the relevant theory. In your scenario with "local variables" (Hoffman's terminology), Sato's algorithm does not apply; the necessary theory is established in Hoffman.

p6: The method for generating power-law networks in the latent space is unclear. The paper lacks sufficient detail on this point.

p6: Since you cite Gopalan et al. as a method for speeding up MMSB, please compare your approach directly to their algorithm (either in addition to or instead of comparing to batch MMSB).

**Minor Comments:**
p2: "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership"
p8: Remove the final sentence; the second-to-last sentence provides a stronger concluding statement.
Bibliography: Cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."
