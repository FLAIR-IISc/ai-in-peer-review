- Paper proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that uses per-node triangle counts (bag-of-words analogy) and models K roles based on triangle configurations for scalable latent-structure learning in networks.

- Strength: Novel parsimonious mixed-membership model that replaces O(K²) between-community parameters with K triangle-based roles, improving scalability for large graphs.

- Strength: Efficient inference via stochastic variational inference and a novel local-update approximation that yields an O(NK) algorithm.

- Strength: Clear and effective framing using the triangle-counts-as-bag-of-words analogy.

- Weakness: Presentation is dense with bookkeeping; the derivation could be streamlined for better reader comprehension.

- Weakness: Distinction between triangle sets Δ¹ and Δ² is insufficiently explained and needs clarification.

- Weakness: Claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is overstated and requires rigorous justification or tempering.

- Weakness: Lack of theoretical or empirical justification for triangle subsampling (δ) used in scalable inference.

- Experimental gap: No qualitative analysis of interpretability—need case studies showing what inferred roles mean in real networks.

- Experimental gap: Sensitivity to the 0.125 threshold for determining "non-zero roles" is untested and may be arbitrary.

- Experimental gap: Synthetic power-law experiments omit the specific generative model used, hurting reproducibility and context.

- Experimental gap: Performance under sparser observed edges is not evaluated—triangle-based preprocessing may be sensitive to missing edges.

- Experimental concern: PTM outperforming MMSB on data generated from MMSB is counterintuitive and needs explanation (data artifact, metric, or deeper model behavior).

- Technical question: Apparent complexity discrepancy—paper claims O(K) parameters but PTM CGS is reported as O(K³); clarify why CGS doesn't benefit from parsimonious parameterization.

- Technical suggestion: Consider adding an explicit sparsity-inducing prior to recover sets of non-zero roles per node more directly.

- Technical suggestion: Discuss or outline extensions to directed networks, since many real-world graphs are directed.

- Conclusion: PTM is an interesting, scalable approach but requires clearer presentation, stronger justification of claims/approximations, and more thorough experimental validation (interpretability, robustness, sparsity).
