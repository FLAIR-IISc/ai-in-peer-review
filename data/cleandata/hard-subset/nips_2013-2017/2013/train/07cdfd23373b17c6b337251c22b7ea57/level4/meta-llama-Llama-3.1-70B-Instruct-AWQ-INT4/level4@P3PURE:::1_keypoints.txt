- Paper proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing MMTM's O(K^3) parameters to O(K) and develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with additional scalability approximations.  
- Empirical value of scalability in K is unclear; paper does not show how learning MMTM with large K helps in practice.  
- Missing experiments comparing PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K to demonstrate PTM's advantage when K is large.  
- Selection of K for real datasets and model-selection methodology are not described and could bias results.  
- Authors do not analyze when PTM's simplifying constraints will fail; PTM should perform poorly if true B has O(K^3) distinct values.  
- Synthetic-data construction (true B) is not clearly described; no experiments with B matrices that disfavor PTM.  
- The paper introduces useful approximation tricks for scalability but fails to describe or analyze their empirical impact in detail.  
- Unanswered question: how does threshold δ affect accuracy vs. scalability, and what δ ranges are appropriate on real and synthetic data?  
- Unanswered question: why does downsampling harm a-MMSB but supposedly not harm PTM, and why can PTM ignore certain triples/non-edges when MMSB cannot?  
- Unanswered question: impact of O(K) approximation to local updates is unclear—what happens if you sample 2K, 4K, or many triples as K grows?  
- Reviewer doubts experimental fairness to competitors due to the above omissions and potential model-selection bias.  
- Paper is very clearly written and well-organized.  
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental but the parameter-constraining approach to scalability is an interesting and original direction.  
- Potential significance is high if the modeling/inference assumptions hold, since few latent-space network models scale to large data.  
- Title is too broad; it overgeneralizes beyond this specific scalable latent-space model.  
- Concern that PTM/M MTM may not be true generative probabilistic models for networks (bag-of-triples issue), raising questions about the meaning of probability and likelihood in PTM.  
- Overall: proposed modeling and inference ideas are promising, but experimental validation, analysis of assumptions/approximations, and discussion of limitations are inadequate.
