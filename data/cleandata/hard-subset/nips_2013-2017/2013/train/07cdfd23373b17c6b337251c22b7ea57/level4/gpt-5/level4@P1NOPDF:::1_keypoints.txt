- PTM reduces MMTM’s parameterization from O(K^3) to O(K), enabling faster inference.  
- Practical value of scalability in K is unclear; experiments varying K (PTM vs MMTM) are missing and model-selection procedures are not described.  
- The impact of PTM’s simplifying assumptions is underexplored; PTM should fail when the true B matrix has O(K^3) distinct values, but synthetic B choices are unclear.  
- The paper lacks tests on adversarial or diverse B matrices to delineate when PTM succeeds or fails.  
- Approximation tricks are under-analyzed: the effect of threshold δ on accuracy vs scalability is not studied.  
- Unclear why PTM can ignore non-edges or downsample safely while other methods (a-MMSB) cannot; theoretical/empirical justification is missing.  
- The O(K) local-update sampling approximation’s impact and sensitivity to sampling size (e.g., 2K, 4K, 100K) are not evaluated.  
- Experimental setup may be unfair to competing methods and does not adequately discuss weaknesses of the model or inference.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental but timely: constraining parameters for scalability is interesting.  
- If assumptions hold, the approach could have substantial impact by scaling latent-space network models.  
- Title is too broad given the paper targets a specific scalable algorithm for one model.  
- Doubts about PTM being a proper generative/probabilistic model: bag-of-triples may not correspond to actual networks and likelihood interpretation is questionable.  
- Overall: proposes scalable modeling and inference tricks, but side-effects, limitations, and fair empirical validation are insufficiently investigated.
