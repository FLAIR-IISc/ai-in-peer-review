The authors develop a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the “triangle model” of networks. They develop a powerful subclass of the model that shares parameters in a clever way, and they use stochastic variational inference to infer the posterior with very large networks. The paper is well written and well executed. I believe it is acceptable.

p1: What is “parsimonious” modeling? This term isn’t common in the context of probabilistic models. I suggest unpacking this term in a sentence, as it is important to the paper.

p1: From my reading of Gopalan et al., down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy. Your sentence in the introduction is misleading.

p4: I sympathize with the triangle model being acceptable from a pragmatic perspective, but I don’t think appealing to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) is appropriate. If we run the generative process of LDA, we obtain something that "is" a document, albeit a simple one, even if it's not the original. Running your model, however, can produce objects that are not networks. Again, this doesn’t bother me—it’s a good and practical model—but the defense by means of LDA is not appropriate.

p5: You should cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato. In your scenario, where there are “local variables” (in Hoffman’s language), Sato’s algorithm does not apply. The necessary theory is in Hoffman et al. [10].

p6: How did you generate power-law networks in the latent space? The paper is vague around this point.

p6: Earlier, you cite Gopalan et al. as a way of speeding up MMSB. You should compare to their algorithm, in addition to (or instead of) MMSB batch.

Small comments:

p2: change "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership."

p8: I suggest removing the last sentence. The second-to-last sentence is a stronger way to end the paper.

In the bibliography, cite the *Journal of Machine Learning Research* version of “Stochastic Variational Inference.”

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant advance in scalability for latent space inference in large networks.
*   The proposed parsimonious triangular model and stochastic variational inference algorithm achieve substantial speedups compared to state-of-the-art methods.
*   The experimental results demonstrate competitive or improved accuracy on both synthetic and real-world networks.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details of the proposed approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The explanation of “parsimonious” modeling could be clearer.
*   The comparison to Gopalan et al. is incomplete and potentially misleading.
*   The analogy to LDA feels misplaced and weakens the argument for the model's validity.
*   The description of power-law network generation is too brief.



References:
