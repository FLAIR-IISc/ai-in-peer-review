- Proposes PTM, a parsimonious triangular model that reduces parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) for faster latent-space inference.  
- Represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs, lowering computational cost compared to edge-based models.  
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm and additional approximation tricks (threshold δ, O(K) local update) to scale PTM.  
- Claims ability to analyze networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of roles on a single machine in hours.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; experiments show effectiveness in accuracy and scalability.  
- Experimental setup may be unfair: unclear how number of roles K was chosen for PTM and competing MMSB, risking model-selection bias.  
- Lacks experiments comparing PTM vs MMTM as a function of K to show how performance scales with role count.  
- Authors downplay limitations of simplifying assumptions; PTM should perform poorly when the full B matrix has O(K^3) distinct values.  
- Missing experiments that vary B to show scenarios that favor or contradict PTM, leaving strengths/weaknesses underexplored.  
- Inadequate analysis of approximation tricks: impact of threshold δ and the O(K) local-update approximation on accuracy/scalability is not quantified.  
- Title is too broad—paper offers a scalable algorithm for a specific latent-space model (PTM), not all latent-space models.  
- Probabilistic claim is unclear: concern that PTM may place most posterior mass on modeling non-networks and needs more discussion.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7, Clarity 9, Originality 8, Significance 8.
