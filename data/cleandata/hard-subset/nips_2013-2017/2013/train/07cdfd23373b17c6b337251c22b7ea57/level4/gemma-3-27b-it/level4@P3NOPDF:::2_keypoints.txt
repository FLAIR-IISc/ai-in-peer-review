- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle-count statistics as a bag-of-words analogue for networks.  
- Builds on attribute mixed-membership stochastic blockmodels (aMMSB) to identify K latent roles while reducing parameter space for scalability.  
- Uses stochastic variational inference (SVI) with derived global and local update rules to fit the model.  
- Core contribution is combining aMMSB principles with a mixed-membership triangle model (MMTM), but the integration presentation is cumbersome and needs clarification.  
- Introduces an intuitive approximation for the local update step that is compelling despite reliance on standard SVI techniques.  
- Restriction to undirected networks is a limitation; extensions to directed networks should be discussed.  
- Claim that the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is problematic and overly broad.  
- Interpretability of inferred latent roles is unclear; need to explain what recovered roles reveal about graph similarities/differences.  
- Distinction between Δ1 and Δ2 is inadequately explained, hindering comprehension.  
- Rationale for subsampling triangles per node (using delta) requires stronger justification; a binary role representation might be preferable.  
- Sensitivity of results to the chosen threshold (0.125) appears arbitrary and should be investigated.  
- Experiments lack specification of which latent space model generated the power-law networks used.  
- Complexity claim inconsistency: PTM-CGS reported as O(K^3) conflicts with earlier emphasis on O(K) parameterization and needs clarification.  
- Potential issues with sparse networks: withholding only 10% of edges may bias adjacent-triangle distributions and limit robustness assessment.  
- Counterintuitive finding that PTM outperforms MMSB on MMSB-generated synthetic data (Figure 1) requires further investigation.  
- Overall: method is a valuable, scalable O(NK) contribution using SVI on triangle statistics, but needs better interpretation, justification of approximations, and robustness checks.
