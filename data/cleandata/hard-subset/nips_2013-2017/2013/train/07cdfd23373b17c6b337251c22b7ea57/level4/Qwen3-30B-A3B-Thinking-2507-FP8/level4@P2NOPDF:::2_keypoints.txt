- Proposal: PTM models triangle-count statistics to learn latent social-network roles, reducing parameters vs. full between-community parameterization.  
- Scalability: Uses SVI with novel global/local updates to achieve O(NK) implementation.  
- Strength: Intuitive local-update approximation for SVI is a notable contribution.  
- Presentation: Paper needs much clearer exposition and better intuition for technical derivations.  
- Directed networks: Treatment of directed networks is underdeveloped and requires extension.  

Major concerns:
- ERGM comparison: Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is misleading and should be rephrased.  
- Latent-space interpretability: The paper does not show whether inferred latent positions are meaningful or provide insights about graph similarity.  
- Delta notation: The distinction between Δ₁ and Δ₂ is unclear and inadequately explained.  
- Subsampling justification: No theoretical or empirical support for subsampling triangles per node (using Δ) as an adequate approximation.  
- Role definition: If targeting “non-zero roles,” the model should include a binary role indicator rather than only continuous parameters.  
- Threshold sensitivity: Robustness to the arbitrary 0.125 threshold is untested and may affect results.  
- Power-law model: The synthetic power-law network model used is unspecified (e.g., LFR, Kronecker), making experiments unclear.  
- Complexity contradiction: Paper claims O(K) parameters but also states PTM-CGS has K³ complexity—this inconsistency must be resolved.  
- Edge sparsity: Performance under low edge density is unexamined; holding out only 10% of edges limits generalizability to sparse networks.  
- Synthetic-data paradox: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated data is surprising and unexplained, requiring deeper analysis.  

Additional/minor issues:
- Misrepresentation: The phrasing equating PTM and ERGMs misrepresents both frameworks.  
- Ambiguous “roles”: The term “roles” is used ambiguously; a binary-role formulation might improve interpretability.  
- Technical clarity: Derivation of local updates and SVI intuition is insufficient for readers unfamiliar with SVI.  

Requested clarifications/actions:
- Revisit and rephrase ERGM comparison.  
- Provide empirical/theoretical evidence for the triangle subsampling approximation.  
- Clarify and test sensitivity of the 0.125 threshold.  
- Discuss and extend the model to directed networks.  
- Resolve and explain the K³ vs. O(K) complexity discrepancy.
