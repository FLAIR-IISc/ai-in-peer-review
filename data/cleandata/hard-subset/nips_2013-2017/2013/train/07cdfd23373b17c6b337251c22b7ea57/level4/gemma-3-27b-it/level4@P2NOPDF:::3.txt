This paper presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the “triangle model” of networks. The authors introduce a parameter-sharing scheme that effectively defines a powerful subclass of the model, and then leverage stochastic variational inference to estimate the posterior distribution in very large networks. Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound; I recommend it for acceptance.

However, I have several suggestions for improvement.

On page 1, the authors use the term “parsimonious” modeling. While potentially accurate, this is not a standard phrase within the context of probabilistic modeling and may not be readily understood by all readers. I recommend briefly defining or unpacking this term in a single sentence to ensure clarity, given its importance to the paper's core contribution.

Also on page 1, the authors state that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy, citing Gopalan et al. My reading of Gopalan et al. suggests the opposite – that down-sampling zeros does *not* negatively impact accuracy. This statement appears misleading and should be revised.

On page 4, the authors defend the triangle model by drawing a comparison to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). While I appreciate the pragmatic utility of the triangle model, I find the analogy to LDA unconvincing.  Generating samples from LDA will always produce a valid (though potentially nonsensical) document. In contrast, the generative process of the triangle model can yield outputs that are not valid networks. This distinction is important. While I do not see this as a fatal flaw – the triangle model remains a good and practical approach – the justification using LDA feels inappropriate.

On page 5, when discussing related work on variational inference, the authors should cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato. Specifically, in scenarios involving “local variables” (as defined by Hoffman et al.), Sato’s algorithm is not directly applicable. The relevant theoretical foundation for the proposed approach is found in the work of Hoffman et al.

On page 6, the method for generating power law networks in the latent space is not clearly explained. The paper would benefit from a more detailed description of this process.

Also on page 6, the authors cite Gopalan et al. as a technique to accelerate MMSB. A direct comparison to the Gopalan et al. algorithm, in addition to or instead of MMSB batch, is necessary to fully evaluate the performance improvements.

Finally, a few minor points:

*   Page 2: Change "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership."
*   Page 8: I recommend removing the final sentence. The penultimate sentence provides a stronger and more impactful conclusion.
*   In the bibliography, please cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."
