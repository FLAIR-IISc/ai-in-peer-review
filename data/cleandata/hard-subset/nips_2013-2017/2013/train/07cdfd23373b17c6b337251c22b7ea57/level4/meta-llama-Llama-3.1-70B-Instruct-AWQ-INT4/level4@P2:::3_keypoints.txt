- Scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model using a parameter-sharing subclass and stochastic variational inference for very large networks.  
- Represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs with a parsimonious statistical model and efficient SVI.  
- Claims ability to analyze networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine in hours.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains the problem and proposed method.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real networks shows recovery of latent roles, link prediction, and superior scalability/accuracy vs MMSB and MMTM.  
- Could clarify the notion of "parsimonious" modeling more explicitly.  
- Comparison to Gopalan et al. (a-MMSB) is insufficiently detailed—needs clearer distinction.  
- More detail required on how power-law networks were generated in the latent space.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, scalable approach with strong empirical results and practical significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient clarity on parsimonious modeling and incomplete comparison to a-MMSB.  
- Recommendations: clarify parsimonious modeling, expand discussion/comparison with a-MMSB, and provide detailed latent-network generation procedure.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Overall verdict: Acceptable for publication.
