- Proposes learning latent structure from social networks using triangle-count statistics (bag-of-words analog).
- Main modeling advance: inspired by aMMSB, uses triangle configurations to define K roles, reducing parameters and improving scalability.
- Employs stochastic variational inference with derived global and local update rules.
- Provides an intuitive approximation for the local update rule, though most learning follows standard SVI.
- Core contribution is essentially a straightforward combination of aMMSB and MMTM; presentation is bookkeeping-heavy and could be clearer.
- Lacks discussion of how the method would extend to directed networks.
- The claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is questionable and overstated.
- Unclear whether inferred latent spaces are interpretable or what insights they yield about graph similarities/differences.
- The distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is not clearly explained.
- Insufficient justification that subsampling triangles per node (using delta) provides an adequate approximation.
- If the goal is identifying non-zero roles, a more binary role formulation within the model might be preferable.
- Sensitivity of latent space recovery to the 0.125 threshold is unclear and appears arbitrary.
- Experiments do not specify which latent-space model was used for power-law network comparisons.
- Claim that PTM CGS has K³ complexity conflicts with earlier statements that the model has O(K) parameters.
- Concern about performance when fewer edges are observed; only 10% edges held out in experiments may hide degradation.
- It is counterintuitive that PTM outperforms MMSB on data generated by MMSB (Figure 1).
- Overall: SVI applied to a restricted mixed-membership blockmodel based on triangle counts yields a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
