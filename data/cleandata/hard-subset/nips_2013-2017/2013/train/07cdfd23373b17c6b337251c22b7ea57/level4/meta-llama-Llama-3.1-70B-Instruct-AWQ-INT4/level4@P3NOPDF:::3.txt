This paper presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. The authors develop a powerful subclass of this model that shares parameters in a clever way, leveraging stochastic variational inference to efficiently infer the posterior distribution in very large networks. The paper is well-written and well-executed, making it an acceptable contribution.

On page 1, the authors mention "parsimonious" modeling, a term that may not be widely recognized in the context of probabilistic models. To improve clarity, it would be beneficial to provide a brief explanation of this term, as it plays a crucial role in the paper.

Also on page 1, the statement regarding down-sampling zeros compromising accuracy appears to be misleading. According to Gopalan et al., down-sampling zeros does not seem to compromise accuracy. This discrepancy should be addressed to ensure the accuracy of the introduction.

The comparison to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) on page 4 is not entirely convincing. While the triangle model may be a pragmatic choice, it does not produce valid network objects when run through its generative process. In contrast, running LDA's generative process yields a document, albeit a potentially nonsensical one. Although this does not detract from the model's practical value, the defense by analogy to LDA is not entirely appropriate.

On page 5, the authors should cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato, as the scenario presented involves "local variables." In this context, Sato's algorithm does not apply, and the relevant theory is actually presented in Hoffman's work.

The paper lacks clarity on page 6 regarding the generation of power-law networks in the latent space. This point should be elaborated upon to provide a clearer understanding of the methodology.

Additionally, the authors should compare their algorithm to Gopalan et al.'s, which is cited as a means of speeding up the Mixed Membership Stochastic Blockmodel (MMSB). This comparison would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithm's performance.

Minor corrections include changing "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership" on page 2. On page 8, the last sentence could be removed, as the second-to-last sentence provides a stronger conclusion. Furthermore, the bibliography should cite the Journal of Machine Learning Research (JMLR) version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."
