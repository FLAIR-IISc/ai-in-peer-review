- Positive overall: paper presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model with a clever parameter-sharing subclass and stochastic variational inference; clearly written and recommended for acceptance.

- p1: Clarify what is meant by “parsimonious” modeling, since the term is not standard in probabilistic modeling.

- p1: Revise or qualify the claim that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy and cite Gopalan et al. appropriately, since their work suggests it does not.

- p4: The analogy to LDA is unconvincing because LDA still generates valid “documents,” whereas the triangle model can produce objects that are not valid networks—LDA is not a suitable defense.

- p5: Cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato, because Sato’s algorithm does not apply in the presence of local variables and Hoffman et al. provide the necessary theory.

- p6: Provide details on how power-law networks were generated in the latent space, including the generative mechanism and parameters used.

- p6: Compare against Gopalan et al.’s accelerated MMSB algorithm (in addition to or instead of batch MMSB).

- Small comment (p8): Consider removing the last sentence; the penultimate sentence is a stronger conclusion.
