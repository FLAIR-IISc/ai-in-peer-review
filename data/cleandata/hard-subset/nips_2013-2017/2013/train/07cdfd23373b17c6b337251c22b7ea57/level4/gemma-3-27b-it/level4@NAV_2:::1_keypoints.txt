- Proposal: PTM constrains MMTM’s O(K^3) parameter space to O(K) for faster inference with a stochastic variational algorithm and scalability approximations.
- Main empirical gap: lack of experiments showing how PTM and MMTM performance vary as a function of K to justify scalability benefits.
- Model selection unclear: the procedure and chosen K values for PTM, MMTM, and MMSB on real data are under-described and may bias comparisons.
- Limitation concern: authors do not analyze when PTM fails—e.g., when the true B matrix requires O(K^3) distinct values, PTM should perform poorly.
- Synthetic experiments insufficiently diverse: need B matrices that both favor and challenge PTM to characterize success/failure conditions.
- Approximation analysis lacking: unclear impact of threshold δ on accuracy and scalability and how small δ can be made without harming performance.
- Downsampling critique: authors claim a-MMSB loses accuracy from downsampling, but do not explain why PTM would avoid similar issues or why non-edges can’t be ignored in MMSB.
- Local-update approximation concerns: no study of how sampling 2K, 4K, or many more triples affects approximation quality, especially as K grows.
- Experimental fairness: reviewer not convinced competitors were treated fairly due to missing comparisons and methodological details.
- Clarity: paper is very clearly written and well-organized.
- Originality: modeling contribution is incremental but the parameter-constraining scalability direction is interesting and somewhat original.
- Significance: PTM could be impactful if its simplifying assumptions and inference approximations prove practical at scale.
- Title critique: title is too broad — the method is a scalable algorithm for a specific model (PTM), not a general scalable latent-space approach.
- Probabilistic validity concern: questioning whether PTM/MMTM are truly generative/probabilistic since a bag-of-triples may not correspond to a valid network and may assign probability mass to non-networks.
- Overall: authors propose useful scalability choices, but the side effects and limitations of those modeling and inference decisions are poorly investigated.
