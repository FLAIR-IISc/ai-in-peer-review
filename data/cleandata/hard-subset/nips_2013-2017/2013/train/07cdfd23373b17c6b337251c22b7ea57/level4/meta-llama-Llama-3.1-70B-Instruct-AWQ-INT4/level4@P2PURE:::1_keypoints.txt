- Paper proposes PTM to reduce MMTM’s O(K^3) parameters to O(K), with stochastic variational inference and extra approximations for scalability; experiments show competitive accuracy.  
- Reviewer questions practical scalability gains in K and calls for experiments comparing PTM with large K versus MMTM with small K.  
- Real-data experiments lack clear description of how K was chosen for PTM and MMSB, raising concerns about biased model selection.  
- Authors do not analyze failure cases from their simplifying assumptions; PTM should perform poorly if true B has O(K^3) distinct values.  
- Synthetic-data setup is underspecified: the true B used was not clearly described and should include cases that both favor and disfavor PTM.  
- Several introduced approximation techniques are interesting but their impact is poorly described and analyzed.  
- Unanswered question 1: effect of threshold δ on accuracy and scalability; how low can δ be set without harming results?  
- Unanswered question 2: why PTM is immune to accuracy loss from downsampling (a-MMSB issue); why non-edges can be ignored in PTM but not MMSB.  
- Unanswered question 3: impact of the O(K) approximation to local updates; how approximation quality changes when sampling different numbers of triples and as K increases.  
- Overall the reviewer is unconvinced experiments were fair to competitors and that model weaknesses were adequately discussed.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Originality: approach is somewhat incremental but interesting; using parameter constraints for scalability is a novel direction.  
- Significance: could be impactful if the model and inference assumptions hold, since few latent-space models scale to large data.  
- Title is too broad and overclaims applicability beyond PTM.  
- Skepticism about whether PTM is truly probabilistic: bag-of-triples may not correspond to real networks, potentially invalidating likelihood-based interpretation and concentrating mass on "non-networks."
