- Introduces a model that learns latent structure from triangle count statistics treated like a bag-of-words.
- Parameters interactions via K roles derived from triangle configurations (parsimonious compared to modeling all between-community interactions).
- Leads to a more scalable model and algorithmic design.
- Applies stochastic variational inference and derives global and local updates for the proposed model.
- Novel synthesis of assorted MMSB (aMMSB) and Mixed-Membership Topic Model (MMTM), though presentation of the combination could be clearer.
- Local update uses an intuitive approximation that is intriguing but needs more justification.
- Suggestion to discuss extension of the method to directed networks, given their prevalence.
- Claim that PTM is a “latent-space counterpart to ERGMs” is questionable and ill-defined.
- Paper does not discuss whether the inferred latent space is interpretable or what it reveals about graph similarities/differences.
- The distinction between the Δ¹ and Δ² statistics is not clearly explained.
- Subsampling triangles per node using δ is not adequately justified as a sufficient approximation.
- If the goal is recovering a set of non-zero roles, the model might require a more explicit binary or sparse role assignment mechanism.
- Robustness of latent space recovery to the 0.125 threshold is unclear and needs sensitivity analysis.
- Experiments on power-law networks do not specify which latent space model generated the data.
- Complexity claims conflict: model asserted to have O(K) parameters while PTM CGS algorithm is stated as O(K³); needs clarification.
- Model robustness on sparser networks is untested—only 10% edge holdout used and larger removals would bias triangle counts.
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforming MMSB on data generated from MMSB (Figure 1) requires further explanation.
- Overall: uses SVI to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel from triangle counts with modeling choices and approximations that yield a scalable O(NK) algorithm.
