The authors present a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. A key contribution is the development of a parameter-sharing subclass within the model, enabling the application of stochastic variational inference to very large networks and efficient posterior inference. The paper is well-written and technically sound, and I recommend it for acceptance.

However, I have a few suggestions for improvement. 

On page 1, the use of the term "parsimonious" modeling could be clarified. While I may be mistaken, it’s not a standard expression in the field of probabilistic modeling, and briefly defining it would enhance clarity given its importance to the paper. Also on page 1, the discussion of down-sampling zeros appears to contradict findings in Gopalan et al., which suggest that accuracy is maintained with this approach. The current phrasing is therefore potentially misleading.

On page 4, the analogy to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) feels inappropriate. Unlike the triangle model, running the generative process of LDA always produces a valid document (even if uninformative), while the triangle model can generate structures that are not networks. While I acknowledge the model's practical utility, this justification based on LDA is not convincing.

On page 5, the citation of Hoffman et al. should be included alongside Amari and Sato. Specifically, in scenarios with "local variables" (as described by Hoffman), Sato’s algorithm is not applicable, and the relevant theoretical foundation is found in Hoffman's work.

On page 6, the method for generating power law networks in the latent space is unclear and requires further elaboration. Furthermore, given the earlier citation of Gopalan et al. as a means of accelerating MMSB, a comparison to their algorithm – either in addition to or instead of MMSB batch – would be valuable.

Finally, some minor comments: on page 2, “mixture-membership” should be corrected to “mixed-membership.” On page 8, I suggest removing the final sentence, as the preceding sentence provides a stronger concluding statement. Additionally, please cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference" in the bibliography.
