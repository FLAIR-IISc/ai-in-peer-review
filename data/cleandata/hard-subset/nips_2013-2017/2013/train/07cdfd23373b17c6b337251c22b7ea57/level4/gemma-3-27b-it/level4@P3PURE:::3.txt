The authors develop a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the “triangle model” of networks. They introduce a powerful subclass of the model that leverages parameter sharing in a clever way, and employ stochastic variational inference to infer the posterior in very large networks. The paper is well written and well executed, and I find it acceptable.

**p1:** The term “parsimonious” modeling is used without definition. While I may be mistaken, this is not a common expression in the context of probabilistic models. I suggest unpacking this term in a single sentence, as it is central to the paper’s contribution.

**p1:** My reading of Gopalan et al. suggests that down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy. The corresponding statement in the introduction is therefore misleading.

**p4:** I understand the pragmatic justification for the triangle model, but the comparison to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) is not convincing. Running the generative process of LDA will produce a valid, albeit potentially trivial, document. However, running your model can produce outputs that are not valid networks. While this does not negate the model's utility—it is a good and practical model—the defense using LDA is inappropriate.

**p5:** The authors should cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato. In the scenario described, where “local variables” exist (in Hoffman’s terminology), Sato’s algorithm is not applicable. The relevant theoretical background can be found in Hoffman et al.

**p6:** The method for generating power law networks in the latent space is unclear. The paper is vague on this point and would benefit from further explanation.

**p6:** The authors previously cite Gopalan et al. as a method for accelerating MMSB. A comparison to their algorithm, in addition to or instead of MMSB batch, would strengthen the evaluation.

**Small comments:**

**p2:** Change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership”.

**p8:** I suggest removing the final sentence. The second-to-last sentence provides a stronger and more concise conclusion.

**Bibliography:** Please cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference”.
