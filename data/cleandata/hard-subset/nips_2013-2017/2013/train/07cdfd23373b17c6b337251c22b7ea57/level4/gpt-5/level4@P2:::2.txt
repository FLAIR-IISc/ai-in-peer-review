Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper proposes a scalable latent-space model for large networks by representing a graph as a bag of triangular motifs and performing stochastic variational inference (SVI) over a parsimonious parameterization. Building on the triangular representation of Ho et al. (MMTM) and the parameter-sharing idea in a-MMSB, the authors introduce the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which shares parameters across groups of triangle role configurations, reducing the number of triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) to O(K). They then develop a structured mean-field SVI algorithm with an efficient O(K) local-update approximation that subsamples a small set of off-diagonal role triples per triangle, and a stochastic natural-gradient update for global parameters. On synthetic and real datasets (including million-node networks), the method runs orders of magnitude faster than MMSB and MMTM baselines, with competitive or improved performance for latent role recovery and link prediction.

Strengths:
- Clear scalability contribution: O(NK) global updates and O(K) approximate local updates yield large speedups, enabling analysis of million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single multicore machine.
- Sensible modeling trade-off: parameter sharing across triangle role configurations dramatically reduces dimensionality while preserving key higher-order structure absent in edge-only models.
- Structured variational approximation: grouping the three per-triangle role variables improves over naive mean-field; the “mixture-of-deltas” local approximation is intuitive, cheap, and empirically effective.
- Solid empirical evaluation: strong wall-clock results, convergence in a few passes, and competitive accuracy on role recovery and link prediction across synthetic and large real networks.
- Practical implementation details: mini-batching over triangles, parallel local updates, and discarding local variational parameters to save memory.

Weaknesses and questions:
- Positioning relative to ERGMs: describing PTM as a “latent-space counterpart to ERGMs” is misleading. ERGMs model a broad set of network statistics beyond triangles, and “latent-space counterpart” could be claimed for many latent models. Please clarify the intended comparison and scope.
- Interpretability: Are the learned roles semantically interpretable? What do we learn about similarities/differences across datasets (e.g., role profiles, motif usage, or mixing patterns)? Some qualitative analysis would strengthen the narrative.
- Notation and clarity: The distinction between Δ^1 and Δ^2 (Dirichlet on 2 vs. 3 outcomes) is not clearly introduced. A brief, explicit definition early on would help, along with a compact summary of B0, Bxx, Bxxx semantics and induced equivalence classes.
- Triangle subsampling (δ): The paper relies on prior work to justify δ-subsampling but does not recap why it preserves key statistics. Please summarize the approximation, its assumptions, and expected bias/variance, and provide sensitivity analyses to δ on real data.
- Local update approximation: The mixture-of-deltas approximation is appealing, but its properties are not discussed. How sensitive are results to the size and construction of set A? An ablation (varying number/type of off-diagonals and whether A is re-sampled) would be useful.
- Collapsed Gibbs complexity: The PTM collapsed blocked Gibbs sampler is reported as O(K^3) per triangle despite PTM having O(K) global parameters. Please reconcile this (number of latent role combinations vs. number of parameters) and clarify when PTM-Gibbs is practical.
- Synthetic evaluation choices: If the goal is to recover the set of nonzero roles, the 0.125 threshold seems ad hoc. Please report sensitivity to this threshold and consider a model-based binary role indicator or posterior decision rule.
- Power-law simulator: Many latent-space models can yield power-law degree distributions. Which specific model was used, and with what parameters? Provide details to facilitate reproducibility.
- Missing data robustness: Only 10% edges are held out. How does performance degrade with sparser observations, especially since triangle counts are affected by edge removal? Consider evaluating at higher holdout rates or with node-pair subsampling schemes that preserve triangle distributions.
- Beating MMSB on MMSB-generated data: It is surprising that PTM outperforms MMSB on networks generated by MMSB. Please discuss why parameter sharing and triangle statistics can confer an advantage in finite-sample estimation under model misspecification, and show additional runs to rule out initialization effects.
- Directed networks: Given the prevalence of directed graphs, a discussion of how PTM extends to directed triangles (more motif types and parameter grouping) would broaden impact.

Quality:
Technically sound overall, with a careful SVI derivation and sensible algorithmic choices. The local approximation is well motivated, but a deeper analysis of its bias/variance and sensitivity would strengthen the paper. Some notational and explanatory gaps hinder readability.

Clarity:
Generally understandable, but sections on parameterization (Dirichlet simplices, equivalence classes), δ-subsampling, and the local approximation would benefit from more precise exposition and a concise figure/table summarizing cases.

Originality:
The main novelty is an effective synthesis: triangle-based representation plus a-MMSB-style parameter sharing, combined with structured SVI and an O(K) local approximation. This is incremental conceptually but impactful in enabling scale.

Significance:
High practical significance: the method scales to settings previously out of reach and delivers competitive accuracy. Likely to be useful to practitioners working with large social and information networks.

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong scalability and engineering; orders-of-magnitude speedups.
- Competitive or better accuracy on multiple tasks and datasets.
- Clear algorithmic ideas (parameter sharing, structured SVI, O(K) local update) that others can reuse.

Cons for acceptance:
- Some claims/positioning (ERGMs) are overstated or unclear.
- Limited analysis of approximation sensitivity (A selection, δ, thresholding).
- Interpretability and directed extensions are not addressed.

Suggestions to improve the manuscript:
- Clarify Δ^1/Δ^2, B0/Bxx/Bxxx, and the equivalence classes with a concise figure/table.
- Provide a brief recap and sensitivity analysis for δ-subsampling.
- Add an ablation on A selection and size; report sensitivity to the 0.125 threshold.
- Reconcile PTM-Gibbs complexity vs. parameter count.
- Expand discussion on directed motifs and role interpretability with qualitative examples.
