- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces MMTM parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) and introduces a scalable stochastic variational inference algorithm.

- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMTM and MMSB and scales to million-node networks with hundreds of roles, achieving competitive accuracy.

- Significant practical contribution: enabling latent inference on very large networks on a single machine in hours.

- Novel engineering: aggressive parameter parsimony combined with a tailored stochastic inference algorithm and O(K) local-update approximations.

- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and explains motivation, model, and inference logically.

- Missing practical motivation for extremely large K and no experiments showing PTM with large K outperforms competitors constrained to small K.

- Real-world experiments lack description of how K was chosen, risking biased comparisons.

- Insufficient analysis of when the O(K) compression assumption fails; synthetic experiments from full-rank O(K^3) tensors are needed to probe failure modes.

- Impact of key approximations is under-studied: sensitivity to triangle subsampling threshold δ and justification for ignoring triplets with fewer than two edges are not analyzed.

- Quality of the O(K) "mixture-of-deltas" local-update approximation is not evaluated as sampling increases (e.g., 2K, 4K), leaving questions about degradation with larger K.

- Fundamental concern: the bag-of-triangles generative process may produce motif sets that do not correspond to any valid network, undermining probabilistic validity and interpretability of posterior/likelihood.

- Conclusion: strong, practical, and novel engineering advance but an incomplete scientific evaluation that needs deeper motivation for large K, robustness analyses, and discussion of generative-model validity.

- Arguments for acceptance: dramatic speedup enabling new-scale analysis, novel combination of parsimony and scalable inference, and clear exposition.

- Arguments for rejection: shallow experimental evaluation on large K, lack of sensitivity analyses for approximations, and unresolved fundamental validity questions about the generative model.
