- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) representing networks as a bag of triangular motifs to enable scalable latent-space inference.
- Reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) by sharing parameters across latent role configurations, similar to a-MMSB.
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm with a novel O(K) "mixture-of-deltas" approximation for local updates, yielding overall O(NK) time complexity.
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over state-of-the-art methods (a-MMSB, MMTM) on million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine.
- Effectively synthesizes ideas from triangle-based representation, parameter sharing, and stochastic natural gradients in a novel combination.
- The mixture-of-deltas approximation is a practical algorithmic innovation that reduces per-triangle complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) without noticeable performance loss.
- Empirical validation is thorough: synthetic latent space recovery, real-network link prediction, and convergence analysis on massive datasets show competitive or superior accuracy.
- Claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" needs clarification and a more precise comparison to ERGM scope and statistics.
- The distinction between simplexes ∆¹ and ∆² is unclear; specify category counts for corresponding Dirichlet distributions to improve clarity.
- Justification for δ-subsampling of triangles relies on prior work; include a brief intuition to make the paper more self-contained.
- Lack of discussion on interpretability of inferred latent roles; qualitative examples from real networks would add value.
- Consider encouraging sparsity in mixed-membership vectors (θi) and report sensitivity of latent recovery (NMI) to the chosen 0.125 threshold.
- Synthetic data generation (specific power-law model) is not detailed or cited, hindering reproducibility.
- Clarify why the PTM Gibbs sampler reportedly has O(Nδ²K³) complexity despite PTM’s O(K) parameterization—explain why Gibbs sampling doesn’t benefit from parsimony.
- Evaluate robustness to edge sparsity: how does triangle-based performance degrade when more than 10% of edges are held out and the bag-of-triangles changes?
- Explain surprising result where PTM outperforms MMSB on MMSB-generated data—discuss potential estimation issues with MMSB’s O(K²) parameterization.
- Note scope limitation: model is for undirected networks; discuss potential challenges or extensions to directed graphs.
