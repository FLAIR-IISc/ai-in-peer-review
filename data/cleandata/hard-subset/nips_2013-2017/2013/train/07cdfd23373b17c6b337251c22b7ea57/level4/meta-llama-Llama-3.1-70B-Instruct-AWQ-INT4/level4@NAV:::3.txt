The authors propose a scalable approximate inference algorithm tailored to the "triangle model" of networks. They introduce a novel subclass of this model, characterized by a parameter-sharing mechanism, and leverage stochastic variational inference to efficiently infer the posterior distribution in the context of large-scale networks. The paper is well-structured and effectively executed, making it an acceptable contribution.

p1: To enhance clarity, consider providing a brief explanation of the term "parsimonious" modeling, as it may not be a widely recognized concept in the realm of probabilistic models.

p1: Upon reviewing Gopalan et al., it appears that down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy. The statement in the introduction may be misleading and warrants revision.

p4: While the triangle model may be pragmatically justifiable, the comparison to LDA is not entirely accurate. Unlike LDA, which generates documents through its generative process, the proposed model can produce non-network objects. Although this does not detract from the model's practical value, the analogy to LDA is not suitable.

p5: It is recommended to cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato, as their work provides the necessary theoretical foundation for the scenario involving "local variables," which is not addressed by Sato's algorithm.

p6: The method used to generate power-law networks in the latent space is not clearly described and requires further elaboration.

p6: In addition to comparing with MMSB batch, consider including a comparison with Gopalan et al.'s algorithm, which is cited as a means to accelerate MMSB.
