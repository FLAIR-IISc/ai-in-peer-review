Summary and recommendation
The authors develop a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the “triangle model” of networks. They introduce a powerful subclass that shares parameters in a clever way and use stochastic variational inference (SVI) to infer the posterior on very large networks. The paper is well written and well executed. I recommend acceptance.

Major comments
- p1: Please clarify what you mean by “parsimonious” modeling. This is not a run-of-the-mill expression in the context of probabilistic models (I may be wrong), and because it is important to the paper, unpacking it in a sentence would help.
- p1: From my reading of Gopalan et al., down-sampling the zeros does not compromise accuracy. The relevant sentence in the introduction is therefore misleading.
- p4: I am sympathetic to the triangle model as a pragmatic choice, but the defense by analogy to LDA is not appropriate. If we run LDA’s generative process, we obtain something that “is” a document (perhaps a silly one), even if it is not the original. Running your model, however, can produce objects that are not networks. This does not bother me—your model is good and practical—but appealing to LDA does not justify this aspect.
- p5: Please cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato. In your scenario, where there are “local variables” (in Hoffman’s language), Sato’s algorithm does not apply. The needed theory is in Hoffman.
- p6: How did you generate power-law networks in the latent space? The paper is vague on this point; please provide details.
- p6: Earlier you cite Gopalan et al. as a way to speed up MMSB. You should compare to their algorithm in addition to (or instead of) MMSB in batch form.

Small comments
- p2: mixture-membership → mixed-membership.
- p8: I suggest removing the last sentence; the second-to-last sentence is a stronger way to end the paper.
- Bibliography: Please cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference.”
