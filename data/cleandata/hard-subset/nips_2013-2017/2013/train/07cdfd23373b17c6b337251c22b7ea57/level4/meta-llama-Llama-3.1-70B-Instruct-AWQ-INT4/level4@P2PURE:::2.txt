This paper proposes a technique for learning latent structure in social network data by fitting a model to triangle count statistics obtained from a preprocessing step, analogous to a bag-of-words representation for text documents. The main modeling advance is motivated by the Affiliation Mixed Membership Stochastic Blockmodel (aMMSB), where instead of parameterizing all between-community interactions, the authors consider the configurations of the triangles to identify a set of K roles to parameterize. This approach reduces the number of parameters in the model, resulting in a more scalable implementation with a time complexity of O(NK). The authors apply recent advances in stochastic variational inference, deriving both global and local updates for the proposed model.

The novel modeling contribution is a combination of the aMMSB with the Mixed Membership Triangular Model (MMTM), although the presentation of this material could be improved, as it requires somewhat tedious bookkeeping. While most of the learning algorithm is a direct application of stochastic variational inference, the authors provide an intuitive and intriguing approximation for the local update rule.

Given the prevalence of directed networks, a detailed discussion of how these methods might be extended to accommodate such networks would be welcome.

Several concerns remain:
- The statement "our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to Exponential Random Graph Models (ERGMs), that represents the processes of generating triangular motifs" is unclear. Specifically, ERGMs can include a wide range of statistics beyond just triangles, and it is unclear how the PTM constitutes a "latent space counterpart" unless all latent space network models are considered as such.
- The interpretability of the inferred latent space is uncertain. If it is interpretable, what insights do we gain into the similarities and differences among these graphs?
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.
- The paper does not provide a clear justification for subsampling triangles for each node using delta as an adequate approximation.
- If the goal is to recover the "set of non-zero roles," perhaps a more binary notion of "role" should be incorporated into the model.
- The robustness of the latent space recovery results to the threshold of 0.125, which appears somewhat arbitrary, is unclear.
- The paper does not specify which latent space model was used to generate power-law networks in the experiments.
- The claim that PTM with collapsed Gibbs sampling (CGS) has K^3 complexity is confusing, as the authors argue that there are O(K) parameters rather than O(K^3).
- A concern is how this method will perform when fewer edges are observed. In the experiments, only 10% of the edges are held out, likely because the distribution of adjacent triangles will be biased if too many edges are removed prior to the preprocessing step.
- It is counterintuitive that the method outperforms MMSB on a synthetic network generated via MMSB (Figure 1, left 4 panels).
This work utilizes stochastic variational inference to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel (with a restricted set of parameters) on the triangle counts in a graph, allowing for a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
