- Paper introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) and proposes scalable SVI with approximations for large networks.
- Authors claim PTM yields stronger statistical power on synthetic data and competitive or better accuracy plus large speedups on real networks versus MMSB/a-MMSB.
- Relation to prior work: builds on MMTM, mixed-membership models (MMSB), triangle-based representations, and scalable Bayesian inference (SVI, structured mean-field).
- Main quality concern: scalability in K is not convincingly demonstrated—mixed-membership models often work with small K, so benefits of large K need clearer evidence.
- Experimental design critique: missing systematic experiments varying K on real networks and unclear/model-selection choices for K that might bias comparisons.
- Misspecification risk: parameter sharing may fail when true triangle tensor B is not compressible to O(K) structure; paper lacks analysis of such failure modes.
- Synthetic experiments insufficiently described—no clarity on how true B was generated or experiments spanning B regimes favorable and unfavorable to PTM.
- Approximations are under-analyzed: need empirical sensitivity studies for δ-subsampling on accuracy/runtime and diminishing returns as δ changes.
- Need explanation why omitting non-edges/single-edge triples hurts MMSB but is acceptable for PTM; information-loss tradeoffs unclear.
- O(K) local-update approximation needs sensitivity analysis (e.g., sampling 2K, 4K, 100K triples) and assessment of degradation as K grows.
- Overall concern that experimental setup may be unfair to competitors and lacks transparency about PTM’s weaknesses and failure cases.
- Clarity: paper is well written, well organized, and exposition of model and algorithm is crisp.
- Originality: incremental relative to MMTM but parameter-sharing and structured SVI for scalability are interesting and timely.
- Significance: contributions could be impactful if assumptions hold, given rare scalable probabilistic latent-space methods—but robustness across domains is unclear without deeper analyses.
- Pros: clear model, well-motivated parameter sharing, scalable SVI with strong runtime results, competitive accuracy, and readable presentation.
- Cons: lacks K-varying experiments, insufficient analysis of parameter-sharing appropriateness, limited evaluation of δ-subsampling and local-update approximations, and unclear comparisons regarding ignored non-edges.
- Additional critique: title is too broad relative to paper’s focus on PTM.
- Conceptual concern: modeling a bag-of-triangles may not define a proper likelihood over graphs (generated bags may not correspond to graphs), so the notion of “probability” and potential side effects of abandoning a graph likelihood need discussion.
