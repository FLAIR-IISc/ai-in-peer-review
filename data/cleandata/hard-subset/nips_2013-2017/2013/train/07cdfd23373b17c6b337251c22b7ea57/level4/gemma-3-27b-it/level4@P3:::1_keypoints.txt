- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces MMTM’s O(K³) parameters to O(K) via parameter sharing for scalability.
- Introduces stochastic variational inference with approximations (δ-subsampling, O(K) local update) to speed inference.
- Experiments on synthetic and real networks show competitive accuracy and large speedups over baselines.
- Main motivation—enabling large K for large networks—is sound but not convincingly demonstrated as beneficial.
- Paper fails to systematically vary K for PTM vs MMTM to show when larger K improves results.
- Model selection procedure for K on real datasets is unclear, raising potential bias concerns.
- Lacks thorough discussion of limitations and failure modes when the true model requires full O(K³) structure.
- Synthetic data generation details (particularly the true B matrix structure) are insufficiently described.
- Needs experiments that test sensitivity to parameter-sharing assumptions (data favoring/disfavoring sharing).
- Requires rigorous analysis of approximation techniques’ impacts (e.g., how δ affects accuracy/scalability).
- Justification for ignoring single-edge triplets is weak given known downsampling issues in related methods.
- O(K) local update approximation needs evaluation across different sample sizes as K grows (e.g., 2K, 4K).
- Overall technical contribution is solid but experimental validation is currently insufficient to prove broad applicability.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically accessible.
- The parameter-sharing approach applied to triangular models is novel and an interesting scalability contribution.
- PTM could be significant for large-scale latent-space modeling if its simplifying assumptions hold in practice.
- Significance is conditional on demonstrating practical benefits of larger K and a clearer understanding of limitations.
- Title is too broad; should be more specific to PTM and its contribution.
- The “probabilistic” claim is questionable due to lack of a proper likelihood and reliance on a bag-of-triples representation.
- Authors should acknowledge the probabilistic framing limitation and discuss its implications.
- Pro: addresses an important scalability challenge, offers a novel, technically sound method, shows substantial speedups, and is clearly presented.
- Con: experimental evaluation and analysis of approximations are inadequate, probabilistic framing is questionable, and baseline comparisons may be unfair.
- Recommendation: Weak reject pending stronger empirical validation (varying K, sensitivity analyses), clearer model selection, and a deeper discussion of limitations.
