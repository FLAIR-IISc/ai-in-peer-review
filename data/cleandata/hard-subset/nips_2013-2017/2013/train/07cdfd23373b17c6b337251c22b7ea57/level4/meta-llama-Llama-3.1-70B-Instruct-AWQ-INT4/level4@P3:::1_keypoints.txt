- Proposes PTM + stochastic variational inference to scale probabilistic latent-space inference for large networks.  
- PTM reduces parameter count from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) by sharing parameters among latent roles.  
- Uses a bag-of-triangular-motifs representation and several approximation tricks for further scalability.  
- Builds on prior work (MMTM, MMSB) and positions PTM as a specialization of MMTM.  
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical analysis and experimental results supporting claims.  
- Reviewer questions whether reducing variables to O(K) trades off statistical power or harms accuracy.  
- Reviewer is concerned about practical advantages for large K and when PTM assumptions break down.  
- Choice of true B in synthetic experiments is unclear; reviewer wants experiments both favorable and unfavorable to PTM.  
- Experimental setup may be unfair to competitors; comparison and weaknesses are poorly described.  
- Missing analysis on impact of threshold δ, O(K) approximation to local updates, and approximation quality as K grows.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized with consistent notation.  
- Modeling contribution is incremental, but the scalability-by-parameter-constraint idea is interesting and original.  
- Significance depends on whether simplifying assumptions hold in practice; could substantially improve scalability if they do.  
- Title is too broad and may overstate scope; reviewer questions whether PTM is truly probabilistic.  
- Reviewer urges investigation of side-effects of modeling assumptions and warns against abandoning likelihood principles.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel scalable approach, clear presentation, and potential practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: doubts about scalability benefits, fairness of experiments, and insufficient analysis of assumptions and approximations.
