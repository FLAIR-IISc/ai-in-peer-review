- Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method using a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and stochastic variational inference.  
- Addresses three scalability challenges: succinct network representation, parsimonious statistical modeling, and efficient inference.  
- Leverages bag-of-triangles to reduce representation from O(N²) to O(Nδ²).  
- Introduces parameter sharing in PTM to reduce triangle-generating parameters from O(K³) (MMTM) to O(K).  
- Achieves O(NK) time complexity for inference, enabling million-node, hundred-role networks on a single machine.  
- Demonstrates practical scalability: 1M-node YouTube network with 100 roles converges in ~4 hours on 8 threads versus days/weeks for prior methods.  
- Strong experimental results on synthetic and real networks showing competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction accuracy.  
- Orders-of-magnitude runtime reductions compared to state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound, with justified parameter sharing and a structured mean-field approximation that outperforms naive mean-field.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with detailed algorithm description and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Original contribution in combining bag-of-triangles, parameter sharing, and SVI to address all three scalability challenges simultaneously.  
- Significant impact: enables latent-space analysis for networks with millions of nodes without sacrificing accuracy.  
- Minor issue: term "parsimonious" needs clearer explanation for unfamiliar readers.  
- Misleading phrasing about a-MMSB down-sampling; should acknowledge Gopalan et al.'s claim of maintained accuracy.  
- Inappropriate comparison to LDA for defending model limitations; should instead acknowledge generation limitations and focus on inference utility.  
- Citation error: cite Hoffman et al. for stochastic variational inference rather than Amari and Sato.  
- Experimental detail lacking: vague description of how power-law latent-space networks were generated.  
- Suggest stronger experimental comparison with Gopalan et al.'s a-MMSB as a primary baseline.  
- Minor typos (e.g., "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership") and a redundant concluding sentence to remove.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution; recommend acceptance.
