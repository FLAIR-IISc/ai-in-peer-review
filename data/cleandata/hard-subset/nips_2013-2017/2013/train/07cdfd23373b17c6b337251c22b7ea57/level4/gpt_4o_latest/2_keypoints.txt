- Introduces a method to uncover latent structure using triangle-count statistics from a bag-of-words-like preprocessing.  
- Models triangle configurations as K roles inspired by aMMSB, reducing parameter count and enabling scalability.  
- Leverages stochastic variational inference with derived global and local update rules.  
- Main novelty is a straightforward integration of aMMSB with MMTM, though the derivation requires tedious bookkeeping and the presentation lacks clarity.  
- Proposes an intuitive and intriguing approximation for the local update rule.  
- Needs discussion on extending the method to directed networks.  
- Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is puzzling: ERGMs cover far more statistics than triangles, and the “latent space counterpart” characterization is not justified.  
- Unclear whether inferred latent spaces are interpretable and what insights they provide about graph similarities/differences.  
- The distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is not clearly explained.  
- Paper does not justify why subsampling triangles per node (using Δ) is an adequate approximation.  
- If the goal is recovering a “set of non-zero roles,” the model might need a more binary notion of role presence.  
- Sensitivity of latent-space recovery to the 0.125 threshold is not assessed and appears arbitrary.  
- Which latent-space model was used to generate power-law networks in experiments is not specified.  
- Contradiction: PTM CGS reported as having complexity O(K³) despite earlier claims of O(K) parameters.  
- Concern about robustness when fewer edges are observed; only 10% edge holdout may avoid biases introduced by preprocessing.  
- Results seem counterintuitive: PTM outperforming MMSB on data generated by MMSB (Figure 1, left panels).  
- Modeling choices and approximations yield a more scalable O(NK) training algorithm.
