- Presents the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle-generation parameters from O(K³) to O(K) via parameter sharing to scale latent-role models.
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with further approximations (node-centric triangle subsampling and O(K) local-update approximation) for scalability.
- Empirical results show PTM is orders of magnitude faster than MMTM and MMSB and can handle million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine.
- PTM achieves competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link-prediction accuracy in the reported experiments.
- Core idea (parameter constraint for scalability) is well-motivated, technically sound, and a novel evolution of MMTM.
- Experiments do not convincingly demonstrate the practical benefit of scaling K (no comparison of performance vs K between PTM and MMTM).
- The procedure for selecting K in real-world experiments is not specified, raising fairness concerns in baseline comparisons.
- Strong parameter-sharing assumptions are insufficiently analyzed; PTM may fail when true networks require complex O(K³) triplet interactions.
- Synthetic experiments should stress-test PTM by generating data from full MMTM tensors to reveal limitation regimes.
- Key algorithmic approximations lack ablation/quantitative analysis: triangle-subsampling threshold (δ), ignoring empty/single-edge triples, and the O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” local-update approximation.
- No study of the speed–accuracy trade-off for δ or how the local-update approximation quality scales with K.
- Foundational concern: the bag-of-triangles generative process does not guarantee realizable graphs, so PTM is not a proper probabilistic generative model for networks.
- Theoretical implications of allowing posterior mass on non-realizable graph configurations are not adequately discussed.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
- If its assumptions hold in practice, PTM could have high impact by enabling large-scale latent-role inference with competitive accuracy.
- Acceptance arguments: tackles critical scalability problem, novel parameter-sharing idea, solid stochastic inference, and strong empirical speed gains.
- Rejection arguments: insufficient experimental justification for scaling in K, weak analysis of limitations and approximations, and the model’s departure from standard generative-modeling principles.
- Minor: the title is possibly too broad—should be more specific to triangular-motif–based latent models.
