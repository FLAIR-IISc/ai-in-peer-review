- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameters from O(K³) to O(K) and enables faster, scalable inference with a stochastic variational algorithm.  
- Novel idea: achieve scalability by constraining the model parameter space rather than only improving inference.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the model and inference.  
- Major concern: insufficient motivation for needing very large K in mixed-membership models and lack of experiment comparing PTM (large K) to MMTM (smaller K).  
- Missing description of how K was selected for real-world experiments, raising potential model-selection bias.  
- Insufficient analysis of when PTM’s strong O(K) parameter constraint fails—no tests on synthetic data generated from interaction matrices that violate PTM assumptions.  
- Lack of clarity about the true B matrix used in synthetic experiments; should test robustness across diverse ground-truth Bs.  
- Poorly analyzed algorithmic approximations: sensitivity of thresholding (δ) to accuracy/efficiency trade-offs is not studied.  
- Downsampling claim (harms a-MMSB but acceptable for PTM) is inadequately justified and needs stronger argumentation.  
- Unclear how the local O(K) sampling approximation quality depends on the number of sampled triples and on K, and whether it degrades with larger K.  
- Originality: an incremental specialization of MMTM, but the principled constraint-for-scalability angle is a valuable contribution.  
- Title is overly broad given the method targets a specific class of triangular models, not all latent space models.  
- Probabilistic foundation concern: modeling a “bag-of-triples” may not correspond to valid graphs, potentially placing posterior mass on non-existent networks—this needs discussion.  
- Conclusion: promising and potentially impactful approach, but experimental evaluation and analyses are insufficient to support the paper’s claims.
