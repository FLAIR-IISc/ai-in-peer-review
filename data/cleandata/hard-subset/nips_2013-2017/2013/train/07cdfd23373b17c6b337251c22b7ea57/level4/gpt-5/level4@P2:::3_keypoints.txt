- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that represents networks as a bag-of-triangles and shares parameters to reduce triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Introduces a structured stochastic variational inference algorithm using minibatches of triangles and natural gradients to achieve near O(NK) inference.
- Empirically demonstrates processing million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine within hours and shows large speedups over edge-based methods and triangle Gibbs samplers.
- Claims competitive or improved accuracy on latent role recovery and link prediction compared to baselines.
- Modeling and inference contributions are technically sound; structured mean-field and natural-gradient SVI are appropriate and well motivated.
- Writing is generally clear and organized, but a few terms and claims need clarification and some citations require correction.
- The O(K) parameter-sharing scheme is an original and notable innovation in this modeling family.
- The ability to infer hundreds of roles on million-node networks on a single machine is a significant practical contribution.
- Clear motivation for triangle-based representation and its scalability benefits.
- Elegant O(K) parameterization preserves key structural distinctions while enabling fast global updates.
- Structured variational treatment of local role triples with an effective O(K) approximation for local updates.
- Thorough empirical evidence supports claimed speedups and performance; implementation details (minibatching, natural gradients, parallelization) are practical and reproducible.
- Define “parsimonious” modeling explicitly, as it’s central but nonstandard in this context.
- Soften or clarify the claim that down-sampling zeros in a-MMSB compromises accuracy; Gopalan et al. argue their subsampling retains accuracy.
- The LDA analogy is unpersuasive since PTM can generate triangle sets that don’t correspond to valid networks; reframe the argument.
- Citation fixes: add Hoffman et al. (JMLR SVI) alongside Amari and Sato; be precise about which theory applies given local triangle-specific variables.
- Detail the power-law network generator (procedure, parameters, refs) to enable reproducibility.
- Include a direct comparison to a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.) or at least a smaller-scale comparison to strengthen empirical claims.
- Discuss when the triangle bag suffices for downstream tasks (e.g., regimes of degree/triadic closure) and consider empirical ablations.
- Justify the 0.125 threshold used in latent role recovery metric and report sensitivity if possible.
- Summarize trade-offs of the O(K) local update approximation (bias vs. variance) and clarify how often A is resampled; add a complexity table comparing PTM, MMTM, and MMSB.
- Put key hyperparameters (α, λ, step-size schedule) and thread counts near main results, not only in the appendix, for reproducibility.
- Minor editorial fixes: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership” and consider removing the final sentence on p8.
- Bibliography: cite the JMLR 2013 version of “Stochastic Variational Inference” (Hoffman et al.).
- Pros: principled scalability via O(K) parameterization and structured SVI; extensive empirical results including million-node runs; clear practical impact for large-scale network analysis.
- Cons: missing direct comparison to a-MMSB; some citation/theoretical attributions need correction; a few overstated claims and insufficient simulation details.
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing citation corrections, clarifying claims, specifying the power-law generator, and adding a comparison to a-MMSB if feasible.
