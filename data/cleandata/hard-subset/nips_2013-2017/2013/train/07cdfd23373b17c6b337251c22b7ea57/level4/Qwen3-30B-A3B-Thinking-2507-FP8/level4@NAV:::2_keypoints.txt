- Introduces a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that models triangle-count statistics as a bag-of-words analogue for graphs.  
- Key innovation: adapts aMMSB to identify K roles via triangle configurations, reducing parameters and enabling scalable implementation.  
- Employs stochastic variational inference (SVI) with derived global and local updates for the model.  
- Provides an intuitive and compelling approximation for the local update rule, though much of the learning follows standard SVI.  
- Presentation could be improved, especially clarity around the main modeling contribution (combining aMMSB with MMTM).  
- Needs a detailed discussion of extensions to directed networks given their prevalence.  
- Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is unclear and potentially misleading since ERGMs use diverse statistics beyond triangles.  
- Questionable interpretability: are the inferred latent spaces meaningful and what insights do they give about graph similarities/differences?  
- The distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is not clearly explained.  
- Unclear why subsampling triangles per node (using delta) yields a valid approximation.  
- If the goal is recovering “non-zero roles,” the model might need an explicit binary role component.  
- Robustness concern: results may be sensitive to the arbitrary threshold of 0.125.  
- Experimental detail missing: which latent space model generated the power-law networks used?  
- Complexity inconsistency: PTM (CGS) shows K³ complexity despite claiming O(K) parameters.  
- Evaluation may not generalize to sparse graphs since only 10% of edges are held out to avoid biasing triangle distributions.  
- Empirical oddity: PTM outperforming MMSB on data generated by MMSB is counterintuitive and unexplained.  
- Overall contribution: uses SVI to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel over triangle counts, yielding a scalable O(NK) algorithm.
