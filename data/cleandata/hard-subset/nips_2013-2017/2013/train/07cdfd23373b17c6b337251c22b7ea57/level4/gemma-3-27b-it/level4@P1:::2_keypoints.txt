- Paper proposes learning latent structure from triangle count statistics (preprocessing like bag-of-words) for social networks.  
- Model builds on aMMSB by parameterizing K latent roles via triangle configurations, reducing parameters and improving scalability.  
- Authors apply stochastic variational inference and derive global and local update rules.  
- Novelty: sensible combination of aMMSB with MMTM, but presentation needs clarification.  
- Authors introduce an intuitive approximation for the local update rule that appears promising.  
- Missing discussion on extending the method to directed networks.  
- Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is imprecise and needs qualification.  
- Need to clarify whether and how inferred latent spaces are interpretable and what insights they provide.  
- The distinction between the Δ1 and Δ2 distributions is unclear and should be explained.  
- Justification for approximating triangle statistics via node-centric subsampling (δ) is insufficiently elaborated.  
- Suggestion to consider a more explicit binary notion of “role” given the goal of recovering non-zero roles.  
- Sensitivity of latent space recovery to the chosen threshold (0.125) is not analyzed and seems arbitrary.  
- Synthetic experiments should specify which latent space model generated the power-law networks.  
- Clarify the apparent contradiction: PTM Gibbs has K^3 complexity while model is claimed to have O(K) parameters.  
- Potential limitation on sparse graphs: experiments only hold out 10% edges, larger removal may bias triangle distribution.  
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforming MMSB on data generated by MMSB needs further explanation.  
- Summary point: approach yields an efficient O(NK) algorithm using restricted parameterization and SVI.
