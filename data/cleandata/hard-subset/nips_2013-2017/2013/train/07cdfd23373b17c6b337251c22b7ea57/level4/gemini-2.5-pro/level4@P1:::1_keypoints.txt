- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces MMTM’s O(K³) parameter space to O(K) via role parameter sharing.
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm with an O(K) approximation for local updates and δ-subsampling to enable large-scale inference.
- Demonstrates on synthetic data that parameter reduction can improve statistical power.
- Shows competitive or superior accuracy on real networks while being orders of magnitude faster, enabling analysis of million-node graphs with hundreds of roles.
- Novelty lies in a principled, scalability-focused constraint on parameters rather than a new model class.
- Key critique: no clear demonstration that large K provides practical benefits over small-K mixed-membership models; lacks comparisons of PTM (large K) vs MMTM (small K) and details on how K was chosen.
- Trade-off from parameter constraints is insufficiently analyzed; paper does not explore failure modes when the true process requires O(K³) interactions.
- Impact of algorithmic approximations is not quantified: effects of δ-subsampling, ignoring triples with <2 edges, and the O(K) local-update sampling size are unstudied.
- Conceptual concern: PTM’s triangle-bag generative process can produce “non-networks,” raising questions about whether the likelihood meaningfully models real networks.
- Writing and organization are clear and accessible.
- Suggested improvements: add experiments varying K and comparing to small-K baselines, analyze limitations of the parsimonious parameterization, perform ablations on approximations, discuss generative/likelihood implications, and consider a more specific title.
- Overall: technically strong and highly scalable, but evaluation and discussion of modeling and approximation trade-offs are not yet convincing.
