- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle-count “bag-of-words” preprocessing instead of raw edges.
- Main modeling advance: parameterize K roles via triangle configurations, reducing parameters compared to a full aMMSB.
- Novelty: combination of aMMSB with Mixed Membership Matrix Triad Model (MMTM), though exposition suffers from tedious bookkeeping.
- Uses stochastic variational inference with derived global and local updates and an intuitive approximation for the local update.
- Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is questionable given ERGMs’ broader statistics.
- Unclear whether the inferred latent space is interpretable or what insights it yields about graph similarities/differences.
- The distinction between Δ^1 and Δ^2 is not explained, nor is the justification for subsampling triangles (δ) as an adequate approximation.
- If goal is recovering “non-zero roles,” model may need a more binary notion of role membership.
- Robustness of latent-space recovery to the arbitrary threshold 0.125 is not demonstrated.
- Experimental setup lacks clarity about which latent space model was used (important since many models can produce power-law networks).
- Complexity claim: unclear why PTM with collapsed Gibbs sampling would be O(K^3) when authors argue there are O(K) parameters.
- Concern about performance when fewer edges are observed—experiments only hold out 10% edges and preprocessing may bias adjacent-triangle distribution if many edges removed.
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforms MMSB on synthetic data generated via MMSB, which needs explanation.
- Positive point: modeling choices yield a more scalable O(NK) algorithm; extension to directed networks should be discussed.
