- Introduces PTM, which reduces MMTM’s O(K^3) parameterization to O(K), with a stochastic variational inference algorithm and further scalability approximations; reports competitive accuracy on real networks and improved power on synthetic data.  
- The practical benefit of scalability in K is not clearly demonstrated; experiments do not show performance as a function of K for PTM vs MMTM.  
- The procedure for selecting K on real data is not described, raising concerns that model selection could have biased comparisons.  
- The paper underplays consequences of its simplifying constraints — it is unclear when PTM will fail, especially if the true B matrix has O(K^3) distinct values.  
- Synthetic experiments do not specify the true B or explore settings both favorable and unfavorable to PTM, limiting conclusions about robustness.  
- The threshold δ heuristic is under-analyzed: effects of different δ values on speed and accuracy are not reported.  
- Treatment of subsampling and ignored events is unclear: why PTM supposedly avoids downsampling issues that affect a-MMSB, and why ignoring non-edges is acceptable for PTM but not MMSB, are not justified.  
- The O(K) local update approximation lacks analysis of trade-offs with the number of sampled triples (e.g., 2K, 4K, 100K) and behavior as K grows.  
- Overall experimental fairness is questionable and potential failure modes from modeling and inference approximations are insufficiently examined.  
- Paper clarity is good; model and inference presentation is generally clear and well organized.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental but timely; emphasizing parsimony for scalability is an interesting and original direction.  
- If assumptions hold, the approach could be significant for scalable latent-space network modeling.  
- The title is overly broad given the paper’s focused contribution.  
- Concern that PTM/MMTM “bag-of-triples” formulation may not correspond to valid networks with high probability, challenging probabilistic generative claims and interpretation of likelihood/posterior.  
- Recommended fixes: report performance vs K, provide transparent model-selection details, ablate δ and sampling budgets, and run stress tests on adversarial B to clarify when PTM succeeds or fails.
