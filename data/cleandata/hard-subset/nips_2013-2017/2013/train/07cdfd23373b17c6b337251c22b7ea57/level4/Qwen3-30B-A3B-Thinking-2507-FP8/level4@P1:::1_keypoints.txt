- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing mixed-membership triangular model parameters from O(K³) to O(K) for faster inference with stochastic variational inference and scalability approximations.  
- Claims competitive latent-space recovery and link prediction accuracy while achieving orders-of-magnitude speedups on large networks.  
- Technically sound contribution addressing scalability of latent-space inference for large networks.  
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete: missing critical comparisons showing PTM with large K outperforms MMTM with small K.  
- Paper fails to state how K was chosen in real-world experiments, risking bias in results.  
- Authors are vague about limitations of simplifying constraints; no clear discussion of scenarios where PTM will fail (e.g., true B with O(K³) distinct values).  
- Synthetic experiments do not explore diverse B matrices to test when PTM assumptions break down.  
- Key approximations are insufficiently analyzed: impact of the δ subsampling threshold on accuracy is not studied.  
- The claim that a-MMSB downsampling harms accuracy lacks explanation for why PTM avoids the same issue, given both ignore single-edge triangles.  
- The O(K) local-update approximation is not properly evaluated: no analysis of performance when sampling different numbers of triples (2K, 4K, 100K) or behavior as K grows.  
- Overall, reviewer is unconvinced experiments fairly compare PTM to competitors and that weaknesses are adequately analyzed.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized with clear technical exposition.  
- Modeling approach is a modest extension but novel in focusing on scalability via parameter constraints rather than generalization.  
- Potentially significant practical impact if assumptions hold (enables analysis on millions of nodes/hundreds of roles), but impact is tempered by limited evaluation of limitations.  
- Title is too broad—paper presents a scalable algorithm for a specific model, not a general scalable latent-space inference approach.  
- Fundamental concern: PTM’s "probabilistic" claim is questionable because bags-of-triangles rarely correspond to real networks, undermining the model’s likelihood and probabilistic foundation.
