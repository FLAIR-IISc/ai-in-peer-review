- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that fits triangle-count statistics (bag-of-triangles) to learn K roles, reducing parameters and improving scalability.  
- Combines ideas from aMMSB and MMTM; presentation is somewhat direct and could be clearer.  
- Uses stochastic variational inference with derived global and local updates; offers an intuitive approximation for the local update.  
- Lacks discussion of extension to directed networks despite prevalence of directed graphs.  
- Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is puzzling and underspecified (ERGMs are broader than triangle statistics).  
- Unclear whether the inferred latent spaces/roles are interpretable and what they reveal about analyzed graphs.  
- The distinction between Δ^1 and Δ^2 is not explained.  
- No clear justification for why subsampling triangles per node (using δ) is an adequate approximation.  
- If goal is recovering non-zero roles, model should explicitly encourage sparsity or binary role activation.  
- Sensitivity of latent-space recovery to the arbitrary 0.125 threshold is not assessed.  
- Experiments do not specify which latent-space model was used to generate power-law networks.  
- Questionable complexity claim: why does PTM CGS have K^3 complexity if model uses O(K) parameters?  
- Robustness to edge sparsity is unclear—experiments only hold out 10% of edges; how does performance degrade with more missing edges?  
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated synthetic data needs explanation (priors, inference mismatch, or metric alignment).  
- Overall: method yields O(NK) algorithm and improved scalability, but paper needs clearer ties to ERGMs, role interpretability, Δ^1 vs Δ^2, triangle subsampling justification, complexity clarification, and robustness analyses.
