- Proposes PTM reducing MMTM’s O(K^3) parameters to O(K) for faster inference with stochastic variational inference and scalability tricks.
- Unclear practical value of scalability in K; need to show how learning MMTM with large K helps in practice.
- Missing experiments comparing PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K to justify large-K scalability.
- No clear description of how K was selected on real datasets; model-selection may have biased results.
- Insufficient analysis of when PTM’s simplifying constraints fail—PTM should perform poorly if true B has O(K^3) distinct values.
- Synthetic experiments lack clear specification of the true B and do not test B’s that disfavor PTM.
- Approximation tricks (threshold delta, downsampling, O(K) local updates) are introduced but not well analyzed.
- Need empirical study of the threshold delta: its impact on accuracy and how low it can be set without harming performance.
- Question why PTM is claimed immune to downsampling issues that hurt a-MMSB and why ignoring non-edges is acceptable for PTM but not MMSB.
- Need analysis of the O(K) local update approximation: how quality changes when sampling 2K, 4K, or many triples and as K grows.
- Reviewer not convinced experiments were fair to competitors and that weaknesses of model and algorithm are sufficiently discussed.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental but interesting in pursuing scalability via parameter constraints.
- Potentially significant if assumptions hold, but impact depends on real-world validity of simplifying assumptions.
- Title is too broad; paper addresses a scalable algorithm for one specific latent-space model (PTM).
- Skepticism about PTM being a true probabilistic/generative model: bag-of-triples assumption may place posterior mass on non-networks and undermines likelihood principles.
