- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter space from O(K^3) to O(K) versus MMTM.  
- Presents a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity enabling analysis of million-node networks with hundreds of roles on one machine.  
- Builds on triangular motif representation (Ho et al. 2012) and mixed-membership frameworks (Airoldi et al. 2008; Gopalan et al. 2013).  
- Technically sound: PTM design uses parameter sharing to cut complexity and inference is efficiently implemented.  
- Experiments report orders-of-magnitude speedups over prior work while maintaining competitive accuracy.  
- Insufficient comparison: authors do not show PTM with large K versus MMTM with small K to demonstrate scalability trade-offs.  
- Limited synthetic experiments: need more diverse B parameter matrices to reveal when PTM succeeds or fails.  
- Subsampling parameter δ is under-analyzed; paper should study its effect on accuracy versus scalability and how low δ can go.  
- Unsubstantiated claim: PTM’s avoidance of a-MMSB accuracy issues is not well explained given both methods ignore some network structures.  
- Clarity is excellent: well-written, organized, reproducible, and clear relation to prior work.  
- Originality: novel application of drastic parameter reduction to triangular-motif latent models within this context.  
- Significance: high practical impact if PTM assumptions hold, but limited by insufficient investigation of failure modes.  
- Strengths summarized: novel parameter reduction, demonstrated scalability to million-node networks, competitive accuracy, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses summarized: incomplete MMTM comparison, inadequate analysis of PTM assumptions and δ, and unsupported claims versus a-MMSB; recommends revision but accepts.
