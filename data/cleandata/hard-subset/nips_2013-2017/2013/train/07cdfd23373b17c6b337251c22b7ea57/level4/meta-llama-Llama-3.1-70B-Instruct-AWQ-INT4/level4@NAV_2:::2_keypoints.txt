- Proposes a novel probabilistic latent-space inference method for large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious statistical model, and stochastic variational inference.  
- Demonstrates scalability: analyzes networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine in hours.  
- Core idea: partition latent role triplet configuration space into groups and share parameters within groups to reduce dimensionality.  
- Results in a compact, lower-dimensional model enabling more efficient inference.  
- Introduces a fast stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm for variational inference with O(NK) time complexity.  
- Improves on MMSB and MMTM by reducing parameter count and achieving greater scalability.  
- Strength: novel modeling that combines advantages of MMSB and MMTM.  
- Strength: derives both global and local stochastic variational updates.  
- Strength: empirically demonstrates scalability and accuracy on large real-world networks.  
- Concern: presentation could better explain the novel modeling contribution.  
- Concern: lacks detailed discussion of extending the method to directed networks.  
- Concern: interpretability of inferred latent space is not fully addressed.  
- Concern: robustness to the threshold used for role recovery is not fully explored.  
- Quality: technically sound with theoretical and experimental support.  
- Clarity: generally well-organized but some sections need clearer explanations.  
- Originality: a novel combination of familiar techniques that differs significantly from prior work.  
- Significance: addresses a hard problem and advances the state of the art in network latent-space modeling.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/significant contribution, technically sound, demonstrated scalability and accuracy.  
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation issues, missing directed-network extension, unclear interpretability and threshold robustness.
