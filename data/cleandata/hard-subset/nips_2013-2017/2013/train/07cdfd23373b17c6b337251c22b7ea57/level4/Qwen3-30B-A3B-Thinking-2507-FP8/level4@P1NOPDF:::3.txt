

The authors present a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks, introducing a parameter-sharing subclass that enables efficient inference on very large networks via stochastic variational inference. The paper is well written and executed, and I recommend acceptance.

Major comments:  
p1: Define "parsimonious" modeling. This term is not standard in probabilistic modeling contexts and is critical to the paper's contribution; a brief clarification would strengthen the exposition.  
p1: The claim that downsampling zeros compromises accuracy contradicts Gopalan et al.'s findings. The current phrasing misrepresents their results and should be revised.  
p4: The LDA analogy is inappropriate. While LDA generates interpretable documents (even if nonsensical), the triangle model can produce non-network objects. This is a pragmatic model, but the LDA defense is misleading.  
p5: Cite Hoffman et al. (with Amari and Sato) for the relevant theory. Sato's algorithm does not apply in scenarios with "local variables" (Hoffman's terminology), so the necessary theoretical foundation is in Hoffman.  
p6: Clarify how power-law networks were generated in the latent space. The current description is insufficiently detailed.  
p6: Since you cite Gopalan et al. for accelerating MMSB, benchmark against their method (not just batch MMSB) for a more comprehensive comparison.  

Minor comments:  
p2: "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership" (standard term).  
p8: Remove the final sentence; the second-to-last sentence provides a stronger conclusion.  
Bibliography: Cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."
