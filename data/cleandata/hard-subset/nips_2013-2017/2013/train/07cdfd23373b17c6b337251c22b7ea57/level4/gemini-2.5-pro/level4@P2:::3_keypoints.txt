- Presents a scalable latent-space inference approach using (1) "bag of triangular motifs," (2) a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), and (3) an O(K) stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm.  
- Demonstrates ability to infer hundreds of roles on million-node networks in hours on a single machine, claiming orders-of-magnitude speedups over prior methods.  

Strengths:
- Major strength is impressive scalability addressing a key bottleneck in network analysis.  
- Novel technical contributions: parameter-sharing PTM and O(K) local variational update approximation.  
- Paper is generally well-written, well-structured, and motivates design choices clearly.

Weaknesses / Suggestions:
- Experimental baselines are insufficient: lacks direct comparison to a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.), a relevant scalable competitor.  
- Experimental details are vague about how synthetic power-law networks were generated, hindering reproducibility.  
- Introduction may mischaracterize a-MMSB’s down-sampling as inherently degrading accuracy; should clarify that sub-sampling can be unbiased.  
- Justification for using a non-generative model is weak and the LDA analogy is not fully appropriate; provide a clearer rationale.  
- Missing citation to Hoffman et al. (JMLR 2013) for stochastic variational inference / stochastic natural gradient ascent.

Minor comments:
- Clarify the meaning of "parsimonious modeling" at first use.  
- Typo: "mixture-membership" should be "mixed-membership".  
- Update bibliography to cite the JMLR version of Hoffman et al.  
- Final sentence of conclusion is redundant and could be tightened.

Summary / Recommendation:
- Novel, technically strong, and highly scalable framework with substantial empirical speedups; primary weakness is experimental evaluation gaps.  
- Recommend acceptance contingent on addressing experimental comparisons (add a-MMSB), clarifying synthetic data generation, correcting citations, and tightening presentation.
