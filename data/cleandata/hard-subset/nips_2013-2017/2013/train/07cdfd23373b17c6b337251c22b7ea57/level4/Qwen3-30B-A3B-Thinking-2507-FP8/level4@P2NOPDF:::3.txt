

The paper presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. The authors introduce a parameter-sharing subclass that enhances model expressiveness while maintaining computational tractability, and leverage stochastic variational inference to handle very large networks. The work is well-motivated, technically sound, and clearly written, with strong empirical results. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

Specific comments to improve clarity and rigor:

1.  **Section 1 (Page 1):** The term "parsimonious" is used without definition. In probabilistic modeling contexts, this is not a standard term; please clarify its meaning (e.g., "minimizing the number of parameters" or "using a compact representation") in a single sentence to avoid confusion.

2.  **Section 1 (Page 1):** The claim that "down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy" contradicts Gopalan et al. (2013), who demonstrated that down-sampling preserves accuracy in similar models. The current phrasing is misleading; revise to accurately reflect their findings.

3.  **Section 1 (Page 1):** The analogy to LDA is problematic. While LDA generates interpretable documents (even if "silly"), the triangle model can generate non-network objects (e.g., invalid adjacency matrices). This is a pragmatic strength, but the LDA comparison is inappropriate as a defense. Remove or reframe this justification.

4.  **Section 1 (Page 5):** Cite Hoffman et al. (2010) *along with* Amari and Sato (2001) for the relevant theoretical foundation. The context involves "local variables" (Hoffman's terminology), where Sato's algorithm does not apply; Hoffman's work provides the necessary theory.

5.  **Section 2 (Page 6):** The method for generating power-law networks in the latent space is insufficiently detailed. Specify the exact procedure (e.g., degree distribution sampling method, parameters) to ensure reproducibility.

6.  **Section 2 (Page 6):** When referencing Gopalan et al. as a speedup for MMSB, explicitly compare against *their* algorithm (not just MMSB batch). The comparison to MMSB batch alone is inadequate; include a direct performance comparison with Gopalan's method.

7.  **Minor Corrections:**
    *   Page 2: "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership".
    *   Page 8: Remove the final sentence; the second-to-last sentence provides a stronger concluding statement.
    *   Bibliography: Replace the conference version of "Stochastic Variational Inference" with the JMLR (2013) citation.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to scalable network modeling. The core algorithm is well-executed, and the empirical results convincingly demonstrate scalability and effectiveness. Addressing the points above will significantly strengthen the paper's clarity and technical precision. I consider the work acceptable for publication with these revisions.
