- Proposes discovering latent structure in networks using triangle-count statistics, analogous to bag-of-words in text.
- Key innovation: based on aMMSB, models triangle configurations to identify K roles, reducing parameters and improving scalability.
- Uses stochastic variational inference to derive global and local updates for the model.
- Combines aMMSB with MMTM in a straightforward way, but requires meticulous bookkeeping.
- Presentation is unclear in places and could be improved for readability.
- Introduces an intuitive approximation for the local update rule that is noteworthy.
- Needs discussion on extending the approach to directed networks, which are common.
- Comparison to ERGMs is misleading because ERGMs can include many statistics beyond triangles.
- The claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart” to ERGMs is unclear and needs justification.
- Interpretability of the inferred latent space is not well explained.
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is unclear and should be clarified.
- The rationale for subsampling triangles requires justification.
- The model’s binary notion of “role” is questionable and merits discussion.
- Choice of a .125 threshold for latent space recovery is arbitrary and its impact should be analyzed.
- Experiments depend on a specific latent space model to generate power-law networks, which may bias results.
- Claimed K^3 complexity of PTM CGS vs. arguments for O(K) parameters needs clarification.
- Evaluation uses only 10% edge withholding; performance with fewer observed edges is a concern.
- Unexpected finding: MMSB outperforms on a synthetic network generated by MMSB—this needs explanation.
- Overall: employs SVI to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel constrained to triangle counts, claiming improved scalability to O(NK) through modeling choices and approximations.
