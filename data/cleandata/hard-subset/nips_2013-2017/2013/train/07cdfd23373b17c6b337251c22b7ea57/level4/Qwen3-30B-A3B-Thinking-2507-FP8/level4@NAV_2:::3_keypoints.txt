- Contribution: Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method using a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and stochastic variational inference representing networks as bags of triangular motifs for compact parameterization and O(NK) inference.
- Scalability claim: Method scales to networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of roles on a single machine within hours, outperforming MMSB and MMTM in speed and scalability.
- Model efficiency: PTM’s parameter-sharing reduces parameter complexity from O(K³) to O(K) while maintaining accuracy.
- Inference performance: Stochastic variational inference converges rapidly (typically within 2–5 passes) and is implemented efficiently.
- Empirical validation: Experiments on synthetic and real networks show orders-of-magnitude speedups and competitive or improved accuracy for latent recovery and link prediction.
- Strength: Addresses succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference for large-scale network analysis.
- Strength: Parameter-sharing strategy is well-motivated and effectively lowers model complexity without degrading performance.
- Strength: SVI approach is justified, efficient, and demonstrates clear convergence behavior in experiments.
- Strength: Experimental section is thorough with compelling speed and accuracy comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Weakness: The term “parsimonious” is used without sufficient explanation and should be defined in the introduction within the probabilistic modeling context.
- Weakness: Claim that down-sampling zeros in a-MMSB is insufficient for N ≥ 100,000 is potentially misleading given Gopalan et al.’s results showing down-sampling can preserve accuracy.
- Weakness: Defending the model by comparison to LDA is problematic; the pragmatic choice that triangles may not generate valid networks should be acknowledged without inappropriate LDA analogy.
- Weakness: Should cite Hoffman et al.’s stochastic variational inference (JMLR) instead of Amari and Sato, since the latter does not apply to the local variable setting used.
- Weakness: Description of the latent-space power-law network generation in experiments is too vague and needs more detail.
- Weakness: Missing comparison to Gopalan et al.’s a-MMSB algorithm; should include this baseline rather than only original MMSB batch method.
- Minor: Correct “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership” (page 2).
- Minor: Remove the final sentence of the paper—second-to-last sentence is a stronger conclusion (page 8).
- Minor: Bibliography should cite the JMLR version of Hoffman et al.’s “Stochastic Variational Inference.”
- Overall recommendation: Strong technical contribution with significant practical impact; well-written and validated—recommend acceptance.
