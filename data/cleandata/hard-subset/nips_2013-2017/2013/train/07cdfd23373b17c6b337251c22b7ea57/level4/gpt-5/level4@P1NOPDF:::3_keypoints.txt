- Presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model, introduces a clever parameter-sharing subclass, uses stochastic variational inference for very large networks; well written and acceptable.

- Clarify what you mean by “parsimonious” modeling—this term is not standard and should be unpacked in one sentence.

- The Introduction’s claim about down-sampling zeros is misleading: Gopalan et al. show down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy.

- The appeal to LDA is inappropriate: unlike LDA’s generative outputs, running your model can produce objects that are not networks, so the LDA analogy is not well founded.

- Cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato: with local variables, Sato’s algorithm does not apply and the necessary theory is in Hoffman et al.

- Explain how you generated power-law networks in the latent space—the method is vague on p6.

- Compare to Gopalan et al.’s algorithm for speeding up MMSB (stochastic/fast MMSB), not just to batch MMSB.

- Minor edit: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership” (p2).

- Suggest removing the last sentence of the paper; the second-to-last sentence ends it more strongly (p8).

- Bibliography: cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference.”
