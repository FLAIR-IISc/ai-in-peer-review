- Presents a scalable method for probabilistic latent-space inference using a "bag of triangles," the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), and fast stochastic variational inference.  
- PTM reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K), enabling scalability to many latent roles.  
- Derives a stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm with time complexity O(NK).  
- Demonstrates analysis of networks with >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups and competitive or improved accuracy on latent recovery and link prediction.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, technically sound, well-written; recommended for acceptance.  
- Strength: original combination of succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference enabling practical large-network analysis.  
- Strength: rigorous stochastic variational inference derivation, effective O(K) local updates, and extensive empirical evaluation.  
- Strength: clear writing and good positioning within related work.  
- Weakness: Experimental baselines omit comparison to a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.), a more relevant scalable competitor.  
- Weakness: Define "parsimonious modeling" explicitly in the introduction for clarity.  
- Weakness: Clarify/temper the LDA analogy—PTM's generative process can produce non-graphs and the comparison may be misleading.  
- Weakness: Cite Hoffman et al. (JMLR, 2013) for stochastic variational inference alongside Amari and Sato.  
- Weakness: Provide full details of the synthetic power-law network generative process for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Rephrase discussion of a-MMSB to avoid implying down-sampling is a flaw; acknowledge its role in scalability.  
- Minor: Fix "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership"; consider trimming the final sentence on p.8; update SVI bibliography to Hoffman et al. (2013).
