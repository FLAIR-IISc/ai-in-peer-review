This paper proposes a method for learning latent structure in social network data by fitting a model to triangle-count statistics computed in a preprocessing step, analogous to a bag-of-words representation for text. The core modeling idea, motivated by aMMSB, is to parameterize configurations of triangles to identify a set of K roles rather than parameterizing all between-community interactions. This reduces the number of parameters and yields a more scalable implementation. The authors apply recent advances in stochastic variational inference, deriving both global and local updates for the proposed model.

The primary modeling contribution appears to be a fairly direct combination of aMMSB with MMTM, albeit with some tedious bookkeeping; the presentation of this material could be clearer. While much of the learning algorithm follows standard SVI, the paper offers an intuitive and intriguing approximation for the local update.

Given the prevalence of directed networks, a detailed discussion of how these methods might extend to directed graphs would be welcome.

Ongoing concerns:
- The claim that “our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs, that represents the processes of generating triangular motifs” is puzzling. (1) ERGMs encompass a much broader set of statistics than triangles alone; and (2) it is unclear in what precise sense PTM is a “latent space counterpart,” unless any latent-space network model would qualify as such.
- Are the inferred latent spaces interpretable? If so, what do we learn about similarities and differences across the analyzed graphs?
- The difference between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clear.
- The paper does not make it clear why subsampling triangles for each node (using delta) provides an adequate approximation.
- If the goal is to recover the “set of non-zero roles,” it seems the model should explicitly encourage sparsity or a more binary notion of role activation.
- How sensitive are the latent space recovery results to the 0.125 threshold (which seems somewhat arbitrary)?
- Many latent space models can generate power-law networks. Which specific model did you use in the experiments?
- Why does PTM CGS have K^3 complexity? Earlier you argued there are O(K) parameters rather than O(K^3).
- Performance when fewer edges are observed is a concern. The experiments hold out only 10% of edges, presumably because removing too many edges before preprocessing would bias the distribution of adjacent triangles. How does performance degrade as the held-out fraction increases?
- It is counterintuitive that the method outperforms MMSB on synthetic data generated by MMSB (Figure 1, left four panels). Please explain the conditions under which this occurs (e.g., mismatch in priors, inference approximations that favor triangle statistics, or evaluation metrics that align better with PTM).

Overall, this work uses stochastic variational inference to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel with a restricted parameterization on triangle counts. The modeling choices and approximations yield an O(NK) algorithm and improved scalability. Clarifying the relationship to ERGMs, the interpretability of the learned roles/latent spaces, the Delta^1 vs. Delta^2 distinction, the justification for triangle subsampling, the K^3 vs. O(K) complexity, and the robustness to edge sparsity and threshold choices would strengthen the paper.
