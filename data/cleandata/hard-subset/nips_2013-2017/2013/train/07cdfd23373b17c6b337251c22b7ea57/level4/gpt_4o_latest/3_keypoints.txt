- Paper proposes a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model, a parameter-sharing subclass, and uses stochastic variational inference for very large networks; overall well-written and acceptable.
- Clarify what is meant by "parsimonious" modeling, as the term is not standard in probabilistic models.
- Intro sentence about down-sampling zeros (citing Gopalan et al.) is misleading—down-sampling zeros may not compromise accuracy as implied.
- The analogy/comparison to LDA is inappropriate: LDA always generates a (possibly nonsensical) document, whereas this model can generate outputs that are not networks.
- Cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato: Sato’s algorithm is not applicable in settings with local variables, and Hoffman's work provides the needed theoretical foundation.
- The procedure for generating power-law networks in the latent space is unclear and needs more detail.
- Compare the proposed approach to Gopalan et al.'s accelerated MMSB algorithm rather than (or in addition to) only comparing to batch MMSB.
- Minor wording: replace "mixture-membership" with "mixed-membership."
- Suggest removing the final sentence on p8 because the penultimate sentence is a stronger conclusion.
- In the bibliography, cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."
