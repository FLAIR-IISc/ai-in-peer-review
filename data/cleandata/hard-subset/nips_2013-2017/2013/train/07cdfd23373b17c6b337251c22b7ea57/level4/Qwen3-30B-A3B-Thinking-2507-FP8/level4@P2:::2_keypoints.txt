- Proposes PTM: a parsimonious triangular latent-role model using triangle motifs and parameter sharing to reduce parameter count from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Develops an SVI algorithm with an O(K) local update approximation achieving overall O(NK) complexity, enabling inference on >1M-node networks on one machine.
- Empirically matches or outperforms MMTM Gibbs and MMSB variational in accuracy while being orders of magnitude faster, converging in ~2–5 passes.
- Thorough experiments on synthetic and real networks (including a 1M-node YouTube graph), with ablation studies and careful computational complexity analysis.
- Technical implementation and justification for the local update approximation are sound and experimentally validated.
- Originality is moderate: combines prior triangle-motif and parameter-sharing ideas but yields a novel, practical scalability result.
- Significance is high: directly addresses scalability bottlenecks and enables new large-scale network applications.
- Key strengths: compelling scalability solution, rigorous experiments, clear computational advantages, and well-justified theoretical/practical contributions.
- Concern: The claim that “PTM is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is misleading and needs clarification of the relationship.
- Concern: Insufficient discussion of interpretability—how inferred latent roles map to meaningful network structures or communities.
- Concern: The NMI threshold of 0.125 appears arbitrary; authors should justify it or show sensitivity analysis.
- Concern: Clarify the discrepancy between PTM CGS having K^3 complexity and the claim of O(K) parameters—distinguish sampling complexity from parameter count.
- Concern: Triangle subsampling (δ-subsampling) lacks theoretical justification; need explanation why it preserves necessary network statistics.
- Suggested improvement: More explicit explanation of the parameter-sharing scheme and the role of equivalence classes (Table 1).
- Suggested improvement: More explicit discussion of how latent roles correspond to interpretable network features and rationale for thresholding.
- Suggested improvement: Brief discussion of extending the method to directed networks.
- Overall recommendation: Strong technical contribution, well-written and significant; recommend acceptance with minor clarifications to address the above concerns.
