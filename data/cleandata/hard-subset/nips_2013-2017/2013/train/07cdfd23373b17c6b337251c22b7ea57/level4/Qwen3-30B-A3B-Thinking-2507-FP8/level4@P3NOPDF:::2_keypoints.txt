- Introduces PTM: a triangle-based latent structure model using triangle counts as features, adapting aMMSB/MMTM ideas.  
- Core innovation: parameterizes interactions by triangle configurations instead of all community pairs, reducing parameters.  
- Employs stochastic variational inference with novel global and local update rules presented as intuitive approximations.  
- Strength: much lower parameterization (O(K) vs O(K²)) and efficient SVI (O(NK)), enabling scalability to large networks.  
- Strength: the local update approximation is well-motivated and insightful.  
- Misleading theoretical claim: calling PTM “a latent space counterpart to ERGMs” overstates equivalence and mischaracterizes ERGMs’ scope.  
- Technical exposition unclear: distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is not explained.  
- Subsampling justification weak: insufficient explanation why triangle subsampling via Δ approximates global triangle counts and its statistical validity.  
- Complexity mismatch: PTM CGS has K³ complexity in practice, contradicting the claimed O(K) parameter advantage and needing reconciliation.  
- Missing interpretability analysis: latent embeddings/roles are not assessed—what roles mean, how they vary across graphs, and interpretability tests are absent.  
- Arbitrary thresholding: the 0.125 threshold for role inference lacks justification and robustness analysis.  
- Counterintuitive experimental result: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated synthetic data is unexplained and requires stronger justification.  
- Experimental limitations: only 10% edge holdout evaluated, ignoring performance under sparser observations (e.g., 5%) and biases from removing edges affecting triangle statistics.  
- Dataset/model clarity lacking: unclear which latent space model generated the synthetic power-law networks and why PTM would outperform MMSB on them.  
- Directed networks treatment is superficial; no concrete extension to oriented triangles is developed.  
- Role formulation confusion: claim of “recovering non-zero roles” implies binary roles, but the model appears continuous and needs a clearer binary formulation if intended.  
- Overall verdict: idea has merit for scalability, but technical ambiguities, insufficient experimental justification, and unclear exposition undermine novelty and impact; major revisions required.
