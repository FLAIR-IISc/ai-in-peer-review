- Overall assessment: solid, well-written contribution introducing a scalable approximate inference method for the triangle model; recommend acceptance.
- Clarify the meaning of "parsimonious modeling" in the paper's context, as it is not standard for probabilistic models.
- Correct or clarify the characterization of Gopalan et al.; down-sampling of non-links in that work yields an unbiased estimator and does not necessarily compromise accuracy.
- Reconsider the LDA analogy used to justify generating potentially invalid networks, since LDA always produces valid documents whereas the triangle model can produce invalid (e.g., asymmetric) networks.
- Update the theoretical citation for the natural gradient/SVI updates: Sato is not directly applicable with local latent variables; cite Hoffman et al.'s stochastic variational inference for proper justification.
- Provide detailed, reproducible methodology for how power-law networks were generated in the latent space.
- Include a comparison against Gopalan et al.'s accelerated MMSB inference as a stronger, more relevant scalable-inference baseline than batch MMSB.
- Fix wording: change "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership."
- Consider removing the final sentence of the conclusion, as the penultimate sentence is a stronger ending.
- Update the "Stochastic Variational Inference" citation to the JMLR version in the bibliography.
