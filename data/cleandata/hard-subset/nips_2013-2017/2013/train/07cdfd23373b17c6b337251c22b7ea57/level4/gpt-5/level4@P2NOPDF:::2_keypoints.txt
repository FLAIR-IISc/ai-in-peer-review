- Introduces a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that models triangle-motif count vectors (bag-of-words style) to learn latent structure in social networks.  
- Key modeling idea: parameterize roles for triangle configurations (inspired by aMMSB) to reduce parameters and improve scalability.  
- Develops a tailored stochastic variational inference (SVI) scheme with global and local updates for the model.  
- The contribution is largely a straightforward combination of aMMSB and MMTM, and the exposition of those connections needs clarification.  
- Proposes an intuitive, practically useful approximation for the local update despite most learning following standard SVI.  
- Missing discussion on how the approach would extend to directed networks.  
- Questioning the claim that PTM is a “latent-space counterpart to ERGMs,” since ERGMs cover many statistics beyond triangles and the precise relationship is unclear.  
- Raises whether the inferred latent spaces are interpretable and what substantive insights they provide across graphs.  
- The distinction, definitions, and roles of Delta^1 and Delta^2 are not clearly explained or justified.  
- Unclear why subsampling triangles per node (controlled by delta) is an adequate approximation; requests bias–variance analysis, effect on ELBO, and diagnostics/guarantees.  
- Suggests using explicit sparsity or binary role indicators if the goal is to recover a set of non-zero roles.  
- Questions sensitivity to the arbitrary threshold 0.125 and requests sensitivity analysis or theoretical justification.  
- Requests specification of which latent-space model/parameterization was used to generate power-law degree distributions in experiments.  
- Asks to reconcile PTM CGS’s stated O(K^3) complexity with earlier claims of only O(K) parameters and the SVI updates’ complexity.  
- Wants evaluation when fewer edges are observed or strategies to jointly handle missing edges and triangle formation, since 10% edge holdout may be insufficient.  
- Finds it counterintuitive that PTM outperforms MMSB on MMSB-generated data and asks for explanation of conditions where triangle summarization beats a matched model.  
- Overall: promising O(NK) algorithm applying SVI to mixed-membership over triangle counts, but needs clearer relation to prior work, stronger justification of approximations, and more robustness analyses.
