- Proposes PTM to reduce MMTM’s O(K^3) parameter space to O(K) with a stochastic variational inference algorithm and additional scalability tricks.  
- Practical benefit of scalability in K is not demonstrated; unclear what is gained by learning MMTM with large K versus using small K.  
- Missing experiments: need performance curves for PTM and MMTM as a function of K to show PTM’s advantage.  
- Model-selection details (how K was chosen) for real-data experiments are absent and could bias comparisons.  
- Impact of PTM’s simplifying assumptions is under-discussed; authors should state when PTM will fail (e.g., when B has genuinely O(K^3) distinct values).  
- Synthetic experiments are under-specified—the true B matrices are not well described and should include both favorable and adversarial cases.  
- Several scalability tricks are interesting but lack empirical analysis: effect of threshold δ on accuracy/runtime is not reported.  
- Questionable claim about downsampling: need explanation why PTM/MMTM can ignore certain triplets/non-edges without degrading accuracy while MMSB cannot.  
- O(K) local-update approximation lacks empirical evaluation—should test sampling 2K, 4K, 100K triples and behavior as K grows.  
- Overall experimental setup may be unfair to competitors and does not sufficiently probe algorithm/model weaknesses.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental but the parameter-constraining approach for scalability is an original, worthwhile direction.  
- Potential significance is high if assumptions hold, but practical impact depends on those assumptions.  
- Title is too broad; method applies to a specific scalable model variant.  
- Raises a fundamental concern about probabilistic validity: PTM/MMTM may not be true generative models for networks (posterior mass on “non-networks”), so claiming probabilistic modeling requires clarification.
