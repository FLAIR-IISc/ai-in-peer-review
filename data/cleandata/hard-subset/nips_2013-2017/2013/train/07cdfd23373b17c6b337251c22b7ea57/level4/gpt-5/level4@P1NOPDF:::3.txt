The authors present a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the “triangle model” of networks. They introduce a useful subclass that shares parameters in a clever way, and they use stochastic variational inference to estimate the posterior on very large networks. The paper is well written and well executed. I think it is acceptable.

p1: What do you mean by “parsimonious” modeling? This is not, to my knowledge, a run-of-the-mill term in probabilistic modeling (though I may be mistaken). Because the notion is important to your paper, please unpack it in a sentence.

p1: From my reading of Gopalan et al., down-sampling the zeros does not compromise accuracy. The corresponding sentence in your introduction is therefore misleading.

p4: I am sympathetic to the triangle model from a pragmatic perspective, but I do not think the appeal to LDA is appropriate. If we run the generative process of LDA, we obtain something that is a document (albeit perhaps a silly one), even if it is not the original. Running your model, however, can produce objects that are not networks. This does not bother me—your model is good and practical—but the defense via analogy to LDA is not well founded.

p5: You should cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato. In your setting, where there are “local variables” (in Hoffman’s terminology), Sato’s algorithm does not apply; the necessary theory is in Hoffman et al.

p6: How did you generate power-law networks in the latent space? The paper is vague on this point.

p6: Earlier you cite Gopalan et al. as a method for speeding up MMSB. You should compare to their algorithm, in addition to (or instead of) MMSB in batch form.

Small comments:
- p2: “mixture-membership” → “mixed-membership.”
- p8: I suggest removing the last sentence. The second-to-last sentence ends the paper more strongly.
- In the bibliography, please cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference.”
