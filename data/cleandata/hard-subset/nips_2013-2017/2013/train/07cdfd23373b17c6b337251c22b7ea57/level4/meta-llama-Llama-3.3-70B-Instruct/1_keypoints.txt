- Paper proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) and develops stochastic variational inference plus scalability approximations.
- Practical scalability advantages with respect to K are unclear; the paper lacks experiments showing when large K benefits MMTM and how PTM compares as K varies.
- Choice of K in real-data experiments is not clearly described, risking biased comparisons between PTM and baselines (e.g., MMSB).
- The authors underemphasize limitations of the simplifying constraints; if the true B matrix has O(K^3) distinct values, PTM may perform poorly.
- Synthetic-data setup omits details on the true B used and fails to explore B settings where PTM would fail, not just succeed.
- Several approximation techniques are interesting and potentially effective but are insufficiently described and analyzed.
- Key unanswered questions about approximations: effect of threshold δ on results, why PTM avoids a-MMSB downsampling issues, and how the O(K) approximation affects local updates and approximations with more sampled triples.
- Experimental setup may be unfair to competing methods, and overall discussion of algorithmic and modeling weaknesses is inadequate.
- Paper is well written and organized (clarity is good).
- Modeling approach is somewhat incremental but the idea of achieving scalability via parameter constraints is an interesting direction (originality).
- Potential significance is high if PTM’s assumptions hold in practice, since many latent-space network models struggle to scale.
- Title may be too broad given existence of other scalable latent-space approaches and the work’s focus on a specific model.
- Doubts about whether PTM is truly probabilistic: the generative model is not properly defined and the claim likening assumptions to bag-of-words is unconvincing.
- Concern that the model places most posterior mass on non-networks, which could violate likelihood principles and have unintended consequences.
