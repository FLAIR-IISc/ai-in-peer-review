- Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method using a bag-of-triangles representation, the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), and stochastic variational inference.  
- Moves beyond edge-based representations (e.g., MMSB, a-MMSB) to triangle-based counts to enable scalability to million-node networks and hundreds of roles.  
- PTM reduces parameter complexity by sharing parameters across latent roles, cutting complexity from O(K^3) toward O(K).  
- Uses a stochastic variational inference algorithm with mini-batching and a structured mean-field approximation for further scalability.  
- Empirical results show significant speedups over state-of-the-art methods with competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction.  
- Builds on prior work (MMSB, MMTM) by combining triangle representation, parameter sharing, and stochastic inference into a cohesive framework.  
- Acknowledges that individual components (triangle counts, parameter sharing, SVI) are not novel alone, but their integration is a valuable contribution.  
- Strength: convincingly demonstrates scalability to networks with over a million vertices.  
- Strength: the triangle-based + parsimonious + SVI combination is an effective, sensible approach to scalability challenges.  
- Strength: provides clear theoretical justification, especially for the PTM parameter-sharing scheme.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation on synthetic and real-world networks for accuracy and runtime.  
- Strength: algorithm description and approximate local updates are clearly presented and implementable.  
- Weakness: unclear and potentially misleading claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs”; needs clarification.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion/examples of interpretability of inferred latent roles in application contexts.  
- Weakness: delta-subsampling procedure justification is thin; impact of subsampling on accuracy should be quantified.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to the 0.125 threshold for latent recovery is not explored; parameter sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Weakness: insufficient details on the power-law synthetic network generator for reproducibility.  
- Weakness/inconsistency: stated complexity of PTM CGS conflicts with the O(K) parameter claim and needs correction.  
- Argues for acceptance based on significant scalability gains, sound theory, clear algorithms, and strong empirical results.  
- Argues against acceptance due to unclear motivation/explanations, limited interpretability discussion, and minor methodological concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, interpretability, and address methodological details.
