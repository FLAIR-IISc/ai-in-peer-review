- Central contribution: introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a "bag of triangular motifs," reducing parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing.
- Inference method: develops a scalable stochastic variational inference algorithm with a structured mean-field approximation and O(K) local updates.
- Scalability: demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups and the ability to infer ~100 roles on million-node networks on a single multi-core machine.
- Model design: PTM is a well-motivated simplification of MMTM that appears more robust and reduces overfitting on large synthetic datasets.
- Technical quality: SVI development and design choices (structured mean-field, O(K) approximations) are sound and contribute to accuracy and efficiency.
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written and organized with clear motivation and sufficient methodological detail.
- Experimental baseline weakness: main comparison is to older batch MMSB; should include direct comparisons to a-MMSB on large datasets to substantiate speed/accuracy claims.
- Terminology clarity: the term "parsimonious statistical modeling" should be explicitly defined (e.g., parameter complexity linear in K).
- Reproducibility: synthetic power-law network generation is described vaguely; provide details on how latent vectors produce the network structure.
- a-MMSB discussion: criticism of a-MMSB for relying on O(N^2) adjacency should be phrased more carefully given down-sampling of zeros makes it scalable for sparse networks.
- Generative-model claim: analogy to LDA is inappropriate—PTM may produce triangle sets not realizable as graphs; state this limitation directly and justify pragmatically.
- Related work/citation: cite Hoffman et al. (JMLR, 2013) on Stochastic Variational Inference as foundational and more applicable than Sato (2001).
- Minor issues: fix typo "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership"; reconsider the LDA appeal; remove redundant final sentence; update Hoffman et al. reference to JMLR version.
- Overall assessment: well-executed, significant contribution to scalable network inference; recommend acceptance, but strengthen experiments and clarify points above.
