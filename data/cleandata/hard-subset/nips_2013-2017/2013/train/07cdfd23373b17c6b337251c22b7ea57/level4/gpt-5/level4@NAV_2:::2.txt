This paper introduces a method for inferring latent structure in network data. The model is trained on triangle-based statistics produced in preprocessing, in a manner analogous to using a bag-of-words representation for text. The central modeling idea, inspired by a-MMSB, avoids fully parameterizing all cross-role interactions by exploiting triangle configuration structure to define K role-specific parameters, which substantially reduces the number of parameters and enables a more scalable implementation. The authors then apply stochastic variational inference, deriving both global and local updates tailored to the proposed model.

The modeling novelty largely amounts to a direct combination of ideas from a-MMSB and MMTM, albeit with nontrivial bookkeeping; the exposition of these components could be clearer.

While much of the learning procedure follows standard stochastic variational inference, the paper proposes a simple, appealing approximation for the local updates that is intuitive and potentially impactful.

Given how common directed networks are, a fuller discussion of how the approach would extend to directed settings would be valuable.

Ongoing concerns:
- “our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs, that represents the processes of generating triangular motifs.” This characterization is puzzling: (1) ERGMs can include a much broader set of sufficient statistics than triangles, and (2) it is not obvious in what sense PTM is a “latent space counterpart,” beyond the fact that it is a latent variable model over motifs.
- Are the learned latent spaces interpretable? If so, what insights emerge about similarities and differences across the analyzed graphs?
- The distinction between Δ^1 and Δ^2 is not clearly conveyed.
- The paper does not clearly justify why per-node triangle subsampling via δ suffices as an accurate approximation in practice.
- If the objective is to recover the “set of non-zero roles,” a more explicitly binary formulation of role presence/absence in the model might be appropriate.
- How sensitive are the recovery results to the 0.125 threshold (which appears somewhat arbitrary)?
- Many latent space models can yield power-law degree distributions; which specific one is used in the experiments?
- Why does PTM collapsed Gibbs sampling have K^3 complexity if the model parameters are O(K)? Please reconcile this with the earlier parsimony argument.
- Performance when fewer edges are observed is a concern. The experiments hold out only 10% of edges; removing more edges may distort the distribution of adjacent triangles used in preprocessing.
- It is surprising that the method outperforms MMSB on data generated by MMSB (Figure 1, left four panels).

In sum, this work applies stochastic variational inference to estimate a mixed-membership blockmodel with a restricted parameterization on triangle-count representations of graphs. The modeling choices and approximations lead to an O(NK) algorithm with improved scalability.
