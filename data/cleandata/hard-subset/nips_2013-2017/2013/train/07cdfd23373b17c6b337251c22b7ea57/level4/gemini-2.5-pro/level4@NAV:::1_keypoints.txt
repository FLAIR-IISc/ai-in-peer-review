- Introduces PTM that reduces MMTM’s parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) for faster inference.
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm and additional approximations to improve PTM scalability.
- Synthetic experiments suggest parameter reduction can improve statistical power.
- Real-world results show PTM’s accuracy is competitive with existing approaches.
- Practical utility of scalability with respect to K is not convincingly established.
- Paper fails to justify why learning MMTM with a large K is beneficial for mixed-membership models.
- Experiments should report PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K but do not.
- No clear description of how K was chosen for PTM or MMSB in real-data experiments, raising selection-bias concerns.
- Authors underplay limitations caused by PTM’s simplifying assumptions and do not specify failure conditions.
- If the true B matrix has O(K^3) distinct values, PTM is expected to perform poorly; this scenario is not tested.
- Synthetic experiments lack diverse B matrices that both favor and challenge PTM’s assumptions.
- Paper inadequately describes and analyzes the impact of the proposed scalability “tricks.”
- Unclear effect of threshold δ on the scalability–accuracy trade-off; experiments do not report this sensitivity.
- Unclear why PTM’s ignoring of many triplets/non-edges would not harm accuracy similarly to a-MMSB downsampling.
- Impact of the O(K) approximation to local updates is not analyzed; no study of sampling 2K, 4K, or more triples or behavior as K grows.
- Reviewer is unconvinced the experimental setup was fair to competitors and that weaknesses were sufficiently discussed.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental, though constraining parameters for scalability is an interesting, original direction.
- Work could be significant for large networks if the model and inference assumptions hold in practice.
- Title is overly broad; method targets a specific model (PTM), not general latent-space models.
- Raises fundamental concern that PTM/MMTM may not be true probabilistic generative models for networks because bag-of-triples rarely correspond to valid graphs.
- Side-effects of modeling assumptions and inference approximations are poorly investigated.
