- Introduces PTM that reduces MMTM’s parameter tensor from O(K^3) to O(K) to achieve scalability.
- Derives a scalable stochastic variational inference algorithm with further approximations for efficiency.
- Synthetic results suggest parameter reduction can improve statistical power.
- Real-world experiments show competitive accuracy at much lower computational cost.
- Practical benefit of modeling very large K is not clearly demonstrated; need experiments showing performance vs K and K-selection method.
- Paper needs a more thorough discussion of when the simplifying O(K) assumption fails (e.g., when true interactions require O(K^3) parameters).
- Synthetic experiments should explore a wider range of generative B tensors, including cases unfavorable to PTM.
- Impact of algorithmic approximations is insufficiently analyzed; subsampling threshold δ requires sensitivity analysis.
- Need clearer justification why ignoring triples with <2 edges won’t harm accuracy similarly to a-MMSB’s downsampling of non-edges.
- Require analysis of the O(K) local-update approximation and how performance changes if sampling 2K, 4K, etc., as K grows.
- Paper is very well-written and clearly organized; modeling and inference are explained logically.
- The idea is an incremental extension of MMTM but novel in systematically constraining parameter space for scalability.
- Work has potential high impact if assumptions hold, enabling analysis of million-node networks with hundreds of roles on one machine.
- Title may overstate generality; the method applies to a specific class of triangular-motif latent-space models.
- Raises conceptual concern that PTM’s bag-of-triangles generative view can produce non-network configurations, complicating likelihood interpretation.
