

This paper introduces a method for learning latent structure in social network data by modeling triangle count statistics, analogous to a bag-of-words representation for text documents. The primary modeling innovation, inspired by aMMSB, reduces parameterization by identifying a set of K roles through triangle configurations rather than directly modeling all between-community interactions. This yields fewer parameters and enables a more scalable implementation, leveraging recent advances in stochastic variational inference to derive both global and local update rules.  

The core modeling contribution combines aMMSB with MMTM in a straightforward manner, though the presentation requires improvement due to somewhat tedious bookkeeping. While most of the learning algorithm applies standard stochastic variational inference, the authors provide an intuitive and intriguing approximation for the local update rule. Given the prevalence of directed networks, a detailed discussion of potential extensions would be valuable.  

Ongoing concerns:  
- The claim that "our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs, representing the processes of generating triangular motifs" is problematic: 1) ERGMs accommodate far more varied statistics beyond triangles, and 2) it is unclear how PTM qualifies as a "latent space counterpart" unless all latent space network models are similarly "counterparts."  
- Are the inferred latent space embeddings interpretable? If so, what insights do they provide about similarities and differences among the graphs?  
- The distinction between $\Delta^1$ and $\Delta^2$ remains unclear.  
- It is not justified why subsampling triangles per node (using $\delta$) provides an adequate approximation.  
- If the goal is recovering the "set of non-zero roles," the model should incorporate a stronger binary notion of role.  
- How robust are the latent space recovery results to the arbitrary threshold of 0.125?  
- Which latent space model was used for experiments, given that multiple can generate power-law networks?  
- Why does PTM CGS exhibit $K^3$ complexity when the model is argued to have $O(K)$ parameters?  
- Performance under sparse edge conditions is concerning: experiments only hold out 10% of edges, likely due to bias in adjacent triangle distributions if more edges are removed during preprocessing.  
- It is counterintuitive that PTM outperforms MMSB on a synthetic network generated via MMSB (Figure 1, left four panels).  

This work applies stochastic variational inference to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel (with restricted parameters) on triangle counts in graphs, enabling a more scalable $O(NK)$ algorithm.
