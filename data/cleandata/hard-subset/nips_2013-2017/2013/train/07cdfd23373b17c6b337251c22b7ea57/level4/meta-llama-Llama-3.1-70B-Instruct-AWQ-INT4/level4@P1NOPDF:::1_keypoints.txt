- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing MMTM's O(K^3) parameters to O(K) for faster inference.  
- Develops stochastic variational inference and additional approximation tricks for scalability.  
- Synthetic experiments show reducing variables can increase statistical power.  
- Real-data results claim PTM is competitive in accuracy with existing methods.  
- Doubts whether scalability in K yields practical benefits given mixed-membership models may work with small K.  
- Lacks experiments comparing PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K to justify large-K advantages.  
- Unclear how K was chosen for PTM and competitors in real-data experiments, risking biased results.  
- Paper understates failure modes; PTM should perform poorly if true B has O(K^3) distinct values.  
- Synthetic data experiments do not describe or vary true B to test scenarios that favor or oppose PTM.  
- Introduces useful scalability tricks but provides poor analysis of their impact and limitations.  
- Missing analysis on the effect of threshold δ (how choice affects accuracy and scalability).  
- Unclear why PTM avoids downsampling accuracy issues that harm a-MMSB and whether non-edges can be ignored in MMSB.  
- No study of the O(K) local-update approximation’s impact when sampling different numbers of triples as K grows.  
- Overall experimental setup may be unfair to competitors and insufficiently investigates model weaknesses.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Modeling is somewhat incremental but the scalability-by-constraints direction is interesting and original.  
- Potentially significant if assumptions hold, since few latent-space models scale to large data.  
- Title is too broad; PTM is a scalable algorithm for a specific model, not all latent-space models.  
- Raises concern that PTM/MMTM may not be proper generative/probabilistic models (bag-of-triples may not correspond to real networks).  
- Side effects of modeling assumptions and inference tricks are poorly investigated.
