This paper proposes a technique for learning latent structure in social network data by fitting a model to triangle-count statistics obtained in a preprocessing step, analogous to a bag-of-words representation for text documents. The main modeling idea, motivated by aMMSB, is to avoid parameterizing all between-community interactions and instead use configurations of triangles to identify a set of K roles to parameterize. This reduces the number of parameters and yields a more scalable implementation. The authors apply recent advances in stochastic variational inference, deriving global and local updates for the proposed model.

The novel modeling contribution is essentially a straightforward combination of aMMSB with MMTM, albeit with some tedious bookkeeping; the presentation of this material could be improved. Although most of the learning algorithm follows directly from stochastic variational inference, the paper introduces an intuitive and intriguing approximation for the local update rule. Given the prevalence of directed networks, a more detailed discussion of how these methods might extend to directed settings would be welcome.

Ongoing concerns and requests for clarification:
- The claim that “our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs, that represents the processes of generating triangular motifs” is puzzling: (1) ERGMs can include a much broader set of sufficient statistics than triangles alone, and (2) it is unclear in what sense PTM is a “latent space counterpart” beyond the generic fact that many network models can be cast in latent space terms.
- Are the inferred latent spaces interpretable? If so, what do we learn about similarities and differences among the graphs considered?
- The difference between Δ^1 and Δ^2 is not clear.
- The paper does not make it clear why subsampling triangles for each node (using δ) provides an adequate approximation.
- If the goal is to recover the “set of non-zero roles,” the model may benefit from a more explicitly binary notion of “role.”
- How robust are the latent-space recovery results to the threshold of 0.125, which seems somewhat arbitrary?
- Multiple latent space models can produce power-law networks; which one was used in the experiments?
- Why does PTM CGS have K^3 complexity? This appears at odds with the claim that there are O(K) parameters rather than O(K^3).
- Performance when fewer edges are observed is a concern. In the experiments, only 10% of edges are held out, presumably because the distribution of adjacent triangles could be biased if too many edges are removed prior to preprocessing. Please comment on this limitation and its practical implications.
- It is counterintuitive that the method outperforms MMSB on a synthetic network generated via MMSB (Figure 1, left four panels). Further explanation would be helpful.

In summary, this work uses stochastic variational inference to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel—with a restricted set of parameters—on triangle counts in a graph. The modeling choices and approximations enable a more scalable O(NK) algorithm, but several claims, complexity justifications, and empirical design choices require clarification.
