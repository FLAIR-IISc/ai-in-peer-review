- Proposes a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model, with a clever parameter-sharing subclass.  
- Introduces a succinct representation of networks as a bag of triangular motifs.  
- Presents a parsimonious statistical model (term not clearly defined in paper).  
- Uses efficient stochastic variational inference to scale to >1M vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine in hours.  
- Demonstrates improved scalability and accuracy versus existing methods (MMSB, MMTM) in experiments.  
- Thorough evaluation on synthetic and real networks showing recovery of latent roles and strong link prediction.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and well-executed.  
- Lacks a clear definition/explanation of “parsimonious” modeling.  
- Comparison to Gopalan et al.’s faster MMSB work is incomplete and needs a direct comparison.  
- Minor edits suggested: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership” and remove the unnecessary last sentence.
