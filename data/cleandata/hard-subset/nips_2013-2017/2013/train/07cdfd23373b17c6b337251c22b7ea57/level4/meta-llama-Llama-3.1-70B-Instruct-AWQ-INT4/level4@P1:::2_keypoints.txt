- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle-count statistics (bag-of-words style preprocessing) for social networks.  
- Main modeling advance: parameterizes triangle configurations (inspired by aMMSB) to identify K roles, reducing parameters versus modeling all pairwise community interactions.  
- Resulting model is more scalable and leads to an O(NK) algorithm.  
- Uses stochastic variational inference with derived global and local updates.  
- Novelty is mainly a straightforward combination of aMMSB and MMTM, with tedious bookkeeping and presentation that could be clearer.  
- Provides an intuitive approximation for the local update rule that is central to computational efficiency and scalability.  
- Lacks discussion on extending the method to directed networks, which would improve applicability.  
- Claim relating PTM to ERGMs is unclear; relationship and definition of "latent space counterpart" should be clarified.  
- Interpretability of the inferred latent space is under-explored; more insight into graph similarities/differences is needed.  
- Distinction and significance of Delta^1 vs Delta^2 are not well explained.  
- Subsampling triangles per node using delta is not fully justified; need clearer explanation of approximation adequacy.  
- If goal is recovering non-zero roles, consider incorporating a more binary notion of "role" in the model.  
- Sensitivity/robustness of latent space recovery to the 0.125 threshold is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- The latent-space model used to generate power-law networks in experiments is not specified.  
- Complexity claim confusion: PTM collapsed Gibbs sampler said to be K^3, which seems inconsistent with O(K) parameter claim—needs clarification.  
- Concern about performance on sparser networks: experiments only hold out 10% of edges, which may not reflect sparse settings.  
- Counterintuitive result: method outperforming MMSB on data generated from MMSB (Figure 1 left) needs a detailed explanation.  
- Overall: scalable mixed-membership blockmodel on triangle counts with useful approximations, but addressing above concerns would strengthen the paper.
