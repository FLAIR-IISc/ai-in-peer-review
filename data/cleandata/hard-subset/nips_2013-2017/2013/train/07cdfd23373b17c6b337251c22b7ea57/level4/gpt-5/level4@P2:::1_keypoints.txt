- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that ties O(K^3) triangle parameters into O(K) groups (all-same, two-same-one-different, all-different).  
- Proposes scalable SVI with structured mean-field, an O(K) local approximation sampling off-diagonals, and δ-based triangle subsampling to target O(NK) per global update.  
- Shows PTM recovers latent roles on synthetic data and gives competitive or better link prediction on million-node networks while being much faster than probabilistic baselines.  
- Extends MMTM by parameter tying analogous to a-MMSB’s O(K) reduction and builds on SVI and triadic motif literature.  
- Strength: clear, compact modeling idea for grouping triplet patterns.  
- Strength: scalable inference design (structured mean-field, subsampling, O(K) approximation) and straightforward parallelization.  
- Strength: strong empirical scaling and fast runtimes with competitive accuracy on large graphs.  
- Strength: technically well organized with sound high-level SVI derivations.  
- Concern: no experiments varying K to show whether larger K yields tangible accuracy/recovery improvements versus baselines.  
- Concern: unclear model selection protocol and fairness of comparisons (how K, hyperparameters, early stopping, initialization, hardware/threading were chosen or matched).  
- Concern: potential misspecification when true triangle tensor B is complex; synthetic tests should vary B complexity to expose failure modes.  
- Concern: missing sensitivity analysis for δ-subsampling—need accuracy vs. runtime across δ (e.g., 20,50,100,∞).  
- Concern: inadequate comparison to a-MMSB downsampling—why subsampling zeros is acceptable in PTM but harmful in MMSB needs principled discussion and matched experiments.  
- Concern: O(K) local approximation trade-offs not studied—vary number of sampled off-diagonals (K,2K,4K,10K) and report impacts on speed, variational bound, and tasks, especially as K grows.  
- Concern: generative semantics unclear—PTM models bags of triangles not full graphs; formalize likelihood over triangle bags and relate to pseudo-/composite likelihood viewpoints.  
- Clarity request: explicitly state how K was selected per dataset, provide ablation (with/without δ, O(K) approximation, naive vs structured mean-field), and report matched hardware/clock comparisons.  
- Originality: incremental but meaningful—parameter sharing across triplets is a natural extension with practical inference contributions.  
- Significance: valuable for scalable latent-role inference on million-node networks if parameter-sharing assumptions hold.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple idea with large scalability gains, strong empirical speedups, competitive accuracy, and practical algorithmic details.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited exploration of benefits from larger K, insufficient failure-mode and sensitivity analyses, and ambiguity about generative semantics.  
- Additional suggestions: consider narrowing the title, clarify initialization and multiple-seed results, and open-source code and scripts for reproducibility.
