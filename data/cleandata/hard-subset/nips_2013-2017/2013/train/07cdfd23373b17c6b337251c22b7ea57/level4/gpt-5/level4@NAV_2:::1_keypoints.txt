- Introduces PTM, reducing MMTM’s O(K^3) parameters to O(K) and enabling faster inference with competitive accuracy and much lower runtime.  
- Practical benefit of scaling in K is not fully demonstrated; need evidence that larger K yields downstream gains unattainable with smaller K.  
- Experiments should report performance of PTM and MMTM/MMSB as a function of K; current selection of K on real datasets is unclear and may bias results.  
- Simplifying parameter-sharing assumptions risk underfitting when the true triangle tensor B is dense; paper doesn’t specify the ground-truth B in simulations or test failure cases.  
- Lacks a systematic study that delineates the regimes where PTM succeeds versus fails compared to full models.  
- Approximation 1 (δ-thresholding): empirical trade-off between δ, accuracy, and runtime is not analyzed on synthetic and real data.  
- Approximation 2 (downsampling/non-edges): unclear why PTM would be immune to information loss that harms MMSB when single-edge triples are excluded.  
- Approximation 3 (O(K) local-update): need experiments varying sampled triples (e.g., 2K, 4K, 100K) to show how approximation quality scales with K.  
- Overall experimental fairness to competing methods is questioned and potential failure modes are insufficiently discussed.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental, but parameter sharing for scalability is an interesting and timely direction.  
- If PTM’s assumptions broadly hold, the approach could significantly impact scalable probabilistic latent-space modeling for large networks.  
- Title may be too broad given the paper presents a scalable solution for a specific model rather than a general class of latent-space models.  
- Raises a fundamental probabilistic concern: bag-of-triples analogy may not define a proper generative model for networks, since sampled triple bags often do not correspond to any actual network—unclear what probability measure PTM defines.  
- Requests authors’ perspective on the generative validity and the side effects of modeling choices and inference heuristics.
