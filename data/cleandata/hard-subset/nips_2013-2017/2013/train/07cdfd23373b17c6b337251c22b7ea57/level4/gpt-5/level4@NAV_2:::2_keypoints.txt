- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that infers latent structure from triangle-based statistics computed in preprocessing (analogous to bag-of-words).
- Central modeling idea (inspired by a-MMSB) avoids full cross-role parameterization by using triangle configuration structure to define K role-specific parameters, reducing parameter count and improving scalability.
- Applies stochastic variational inference and derives tailored global and local updates for the proposed model.
- Modeling novelty is mainly a direct combination of ideas from a-MMSB and MMTM, with nontrivial bookkeeping; exposition could be clearer.
- Proposes a simple, intuitive approximation for the local updates that is potentially impactful.
- Lacks discussion of how the approach would extend to directed networks, which are common.
- The claim that “PTM is a latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is puzzling and insufficiently justified (ERGMs are broader; unclear what “counterpart” means).
- Unclear whether the learned latent spaces are interpretable and what insights they provide across analyzed graphs.
- The distinction between Δ^1 and Δ^2 is not clearly explained.
- No clear justification that per-node triangle subsampling via δ is an accurate practical approximation.
- If the goal is recovering the “set of non-zero roles,” a more explicit binary role presence/absence formulation may be more appropriate.
- Sensitivity of recovery results to the chosen 0.125 threshold is unclear and seems arbitrary.
- Experiments claim power-law degree behavior but do not specify which latent space model produces it.
- Apparent contradiction: PTM is touted as parsimonious (O(K) parameters) but collapsed Gibbs sampling reportedly has K^3 complexity—needs reconciliation.
- Concern about robustness when fewer edges are observed; experiments only hold out 10% of edges and more removal may distort triangle distributions used in preprocessing.
- Surprising empirical result: PTM outperforms MMSB on data generated by MMSB (Figure 1, left panels), which needs explanation.
- Overall: applies SVI to a mixed-membership blockmodel restricted to triangle-count representations, producing an O(NK) algorithm with improved scalability.
