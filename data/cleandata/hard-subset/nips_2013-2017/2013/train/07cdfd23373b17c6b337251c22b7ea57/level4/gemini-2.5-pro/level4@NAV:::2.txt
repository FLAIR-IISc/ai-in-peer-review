This work introduces a method for inferring latent structures within social network data. The proposed model is trained on triangle count statistics, which are derived from a preprocessing stage, similar to how a bag-of-words representation is used for text. Drawing inspiration from aMMSB, the primary modeling contribution is to parameterize a set of K roles based on triangle configurations, rather than modeling all possible interactions between communities. This approach reduces the number of model parameters, leading to a more scalable implementation. The authors employ recent developments in stochastic variational inference, for which they derive both global and local update rules.

While the novel modeling aspect is a relatively direct combination of aMMSB and MMTM, it appears to have necessitated considerable bookkeeping. The clarity of this section could be enhanced.

Although the learning algorithm is largely a standard application of stochastic variational inference, the authors introduce a compelling and intuitive approximation for the local update.

Considering the ubiquity of directed networks, a discussion on potential extensions of this method to handle such data would be a valuable addition.

Ongoing concerns:
-   The claim that "our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs, that represents the processes of generating triangular motifs" is questionable. First, ERGMs can incorporate a much wider range of statistics beyond triangles. Second, it is unclear how PTM is a "latent space counterpart" in a way that other latent space network models are not.
-   The interpretability of the inferred latent space is not discussed. What insights do these spaces provide about the similarities and differences between the analyzed graphs?
-   The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained to the reader.
-   The paper lacks justification for why subsampling triangles for each node using delta yields a sufficient approximation.
-   If the objective is to recover a "set of non-zero roles," the model might benefit from incorporating a more binary concept of role assignment.
-   The robustness of the latent space recovery to the threshold value of 0.125, which appears arbitrary, is not explored.
-   The paper should specify which of the various latent space models capable of producing power-law networks was used for the experiments.
-   There is a seeming contradiction in the complexity analysis: why does PTM CGS have K^3 complexity when the number of parameters was argued to be O(K), not O(K^3)?
-   A significant concern is the model's performance in sparser settings with fewer observed edges. The experiments only hold out 10% of edges, likely because removing more would heavily bias the distribution of adjacent triangles calculated during preprocessing.
-   The result where the proposed method outperforms MMSB on a synthetic network generated from the MMSB model itself (Figure 1, left 4 panels) is surprising and warrants further explanation.

In summary, this paper utilizes stochastic variational inference to fit a mixed-membership blockmodel with a constrained parameter set to a graph's triangle counts. The modeling decisions and algorithmic approximations result in a scalable O(NK) inference procedure.
