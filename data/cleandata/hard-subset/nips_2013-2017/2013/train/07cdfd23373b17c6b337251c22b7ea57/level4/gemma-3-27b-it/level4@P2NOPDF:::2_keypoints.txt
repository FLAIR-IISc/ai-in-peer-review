- Introduces modeling triangle counts as a bag-of-words to learn K latent roles, reducing parameters and improving scalability.  
- Core contribution: integrates aMMSB principles with a mixed-membership triangle model (MMTM), though presentation needs clearer exposition.  
- Applies stochastic variational inference with derived global and local updates; the local-update approximation is intuitive and promising.  
- The claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is imprecise; ERGMs capture broader statistics and the analogy is too general.  
- Unclear interpretability: paper lacks discussion on how discovered roles provide meaningful insights into graph similarities/differences.  
- Technical ambiguity: distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is not clearly explained.  
- Subsampling triangles (delta) is used without sufficient justification for adequacy of the approximation.  
- Suggestion to explore an explicit binary representation for recovering non-zero roles.  
- Sensitivity to the threshold value 0.125 is not assessed and appears arbitrary.  
- Experimental detail missing: which latent space model was used to generate the power-law networks?  
- Complexity inconsistency: claim of O(K) parameter count conflicts with stated K³ complexity for PTM-CGS—needs clarification.  
- Potential weakness on sparse networks: experiments with only 10% withheld edges may not reflect performance with fewer observed edges.  
- Counterintuitive result where PTM outperforms MMSB on MMSB-generated data (Figure 1 left panels) requires further investigation.  
- Overall: promising scalable triangle-based model, but needs improvements in clarity, approximation justification, interpretability, and robustness.
