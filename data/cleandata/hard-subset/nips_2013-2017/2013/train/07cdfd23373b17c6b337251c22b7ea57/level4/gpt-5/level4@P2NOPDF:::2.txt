This paper introduces a method for learning latent structure in social networks by modeling triangle-motif count vectors produced in a preprocessing step, analogous to a bag-of-words representation for text. The key modeling idea, inspired by aMMSB, is to parameterize roles associated with triangle configurations rather than all pairwise community interactions, thereby reducing the number of parameters and improving scalability. The authors develop a stochastic variational inference (SVI) scheme with global and local updates tailored to this model.

The modeling contribution largely amounts to a straightforward combination of aMMSB with MMTM, albeit with nontrivial bookkeeping; the exposition of these connections could be clearer. While most of the learning algorithm follows standard SVI, the paper proposes an intuitive and interesting approximation for the local update that seems practically useful. A discussion of how the approach would extend to directed networks would strengthen the work.

Ongoing concerns and questions:
- The claim that “our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent-space counterpart to ERGMs, that represents the processes of generating triangular motifs” is puzzling. (1) ERGMs can include a broad array of network statistics beyond triangles; and (2) it is unclear in what precise sense PTM is a “latent-space counterpart” to ERGMs, unless all latent-space network models qualify as such.
- Are the inferred latent spaces interpretable? If so, what substantive insights do they provide about similarities and differences across the analyzed graphs?
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained. Please define each precisely, justify why both are needed, and provide intuition for their roles in the model and inference.
- The paper does not make it clear why subsampling triangles for each node (controlled by delta) yields an adequate approximation. A discussion of the bias–variance trade-off, its effect on the ELBO/objective, and any guarantees or empirical diagnostics would be helpful.
- If the goal is to recover the “set of non-zero roles,” the current formulation may benefit from a more explicitly sparse or binary notion of role membership (e.g., sparsity-inducing priors, hard-thresholded or Bernoulli role indicators).
- How sensitive are the latent-space recovery results to the threshold of 0.125, which appears arbitrary? Please include a sensitivity analysis or justify this choice theoretically.
- Many latent-space models can produce power-law degree distributions. Which specific model and parameterization did you use in the experiments?
- Why does PTM CGS have O(K^3) complexity? Earlier arguments suggest the model has O(K) parameters rather than O(K^3); please reconcile the parameter count with the computational complexity of the sampler and the SVI updates.
- How does the method perform when fewer edges are observed? The experiments hold out only 10% of edges, likely because removing too many edges before preprocessing would distort triangle counts. Please evaluate more aggressive sparsification or propose a strategy that jointly handles missing edges and triangle formation.
- It is counterintuitive that the method outperforms MMSB on data generated by MMSB (Figure 1, left four panels). Please explain the conditions under which summarizing the graph by triangle counts can surpass a model matched to the data-generating process (e.g., due to regularization, aggregation benefits, or inference biases in MMSB).

Overall, this work applies SVI to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel over triangle-count features, with a restricted parameterization that improves scalability. The modeling choices and approximations yield an O(NK) algorithm in practice. The approach is promising, but the paper would benefit from clearer exposition of the model’s relationship to prior work, a more thorough justification of key approximations, and additional robustness analyses.
