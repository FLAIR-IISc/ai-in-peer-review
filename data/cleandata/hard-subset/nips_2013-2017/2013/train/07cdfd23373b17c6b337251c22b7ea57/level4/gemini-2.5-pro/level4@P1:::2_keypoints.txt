- Presents a scalable method (Parsimonious Triangular Model, PTM) for inferring latent structure in large networks using a "bag of triangular motifs," a parsimonious model, and stochastic variational inference.
- PTM reduces triangle-generating parameterization from O(K³) to O(K) by sharing parameters across latent-role configurations, inspired by a-MMSB.
- Authors derive a stochastic variational inference algorithm with a structured mean-field approximation and an efficient local-update approximation, yielding O(NK) runtime.
- Method scales to million-node networks with hundreds of roles and runs in hours on a single machine.
- Empirically competitive or superior on latent-space recovery and link prediction across synthetic and real datasets, with fast convergence (typically 2–5 passes).
- Key algorithmic contribution: local-update approximation that cuts complexity from O(K³) to O(K), addressing a critical bottleneck.
- Claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is tenuous and should be toned down or better justified since ERGMs capture many more graph statistics.
- The critical delta-subsampling of triangles preprocessing needs clearer justification for why it approximates the true triangle distribution adequately.
- The distinction between simplexes ∆¹ and ∆² (Section 3) is unclear and should be explained more explicitly.
- Clarify why the PTM Gibbs sampler baseline has O(K³) complexity despite PTM’s O(K) parameterization.
- Provide more detail on the latent-space model used to generate power-law networks for reproducibility.
- Link prediction experiments hold out only 10% of edges; evaluating sparser settings or larger edge hold-outs is needed since triangle-based methods may struggle with sparse data.
- It is surprising PTM outperforms MMSB on data generated by MMSB; discuss whether PTM’s regularization explains this counterintuitive result.
- The thresholding (0.125) used to convert mixed-membership vectors to discrete role sets is arbitrary; report sensitivity analysis and reconcile continuous outputs with discrete evaluation.
- Add qualitative analysis of inferred latent spaces on real networks to demonstrate role interpretability and practical insights.
- Discuss extensions to directed graphs, since the current triangle-based method is designed for undirected networks.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated contribution with major scalability and empirical advantages, but clarity, evaluation robustness, and scope extensions would improve the paper.
