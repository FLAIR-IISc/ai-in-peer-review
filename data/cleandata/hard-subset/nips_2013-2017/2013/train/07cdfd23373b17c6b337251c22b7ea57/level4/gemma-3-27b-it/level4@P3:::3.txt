This paper presents a scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference for large-scale networks. The authors address a critical challenge in network analysis—the computational cost of existing methods when applied to networks with millions of nodes—by proposing a novel combination of a succinct network representation (using triangular motifs), a parsimonious statistical model, and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. The core contribution lies in the “Parsimonious Triangular Model” (PTM), which reduces the parameter space through strategic parameter sharing, enabling scalable inference. The experimental results demonstrate significant speedups compared to state-of-the-art methods, with competitive or improved accuracy on both synthetic and real-world networks.

The paper is generally well-written and clearly articulates the motivation, technical details, and experimental findings. The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of prior work in the field, particularly concerning mixed-membership stochastic blockmodels (MMSB) and exponential random graph models (ERGM). The focus on scalability is timely and addresses a significant limitation of existing approaches. The experimental evaluation is thorough, including comparisons to relevant baselines on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Strengths:**

*   **Scalability:** The primary strength of this work is its scalability. The authors successfully demonstrate the ability to analyze networks with over a million vertices in a reasonable timeframe, a feat beyond the reach of many existing methods.
*   **Novelty:** The PTM, with its parameter sharing strategy, represents a novel and effective approach to reducing the computational complexity of latent space inference.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed stochastic variational inference algorithm appears well-designed and theoretically justified, leveraging recent advances in natural gradient methods.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach, demonstrating both speedups and competitive accuracy.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the key concepts and technical details.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Definition of “Parsimonious”:** The term “parsimonious” is used prominently but is not clearly defined in the context of probabilistic modeling. Expanding on this terminology in the introduction would improve readability.
*   **Misleading Statement Regarding Gopalan et al.:** The claim that down-sampling zeros in Gopalan et al. does not compromise accuracy is potentially misleading. A more nuanced description acknowledging potential tradeoffs would be preferable.
*   **Justification for Model Validity:** The authors’ analogy to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) to justify the model’s validity is weak. While the pragmatic value of the model is clear, the comparison to LDA is not convincing, as the generative process of the proposed model doesn’t guarantee the creation of valid network structures.
*   **Citation Omission:** The citation of Hoffman et al. (JMLR version) alongside Amari and Sato is necessary, as the theoretical framework for the stochastic variational inference relies heavily on their work, particularly regarding the treatment of “local variables”.
*   **Lack of Detail in Power-Law Network Generation:** The description of how power-law networks were generated in the latent space is vague. Providing more details about this process would enhance reproducibility.
*   **Baseline Comparison:** Comparing against Gopalan et al.’s a-MMSB algorithm, in addition to or instead of the standard MMSB batch algorithm, would provide a more direct and relevant comparison.

**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant challenge in network analysis (scalability) with a novel and effective solution.
*   The PTM is a significant contribution that reduces computational complexity without sacrificing accuracy.
*   The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed approach.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

*   The minor weaknesses regarding clarity (definition of “parsimonious”, justification for model validity) and citation omissions detract slightly from the overall quality of the paper.
*   The statement regarding Gopalan et al. could be misinterpreted.
*   Lack of detail in network generation.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend accepting this paper. While there are a few minor weaknesses, the strengths of this work – particularly its scalability, novelty, and empirical validation – significantly outweigh them. The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of network analysis and has the potential to enable the analysis of much larger and more complex networks than previously possible. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper.
