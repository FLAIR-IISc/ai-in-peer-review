- Introduces a method to learn latent structure from social networks by fitting models to triangle-count statistics (bag-of-words analogue), motivated by aMMSB.  
- Uses triangle configurations to define K roles, reducing parameter count and improving scalability.  
- Employs stochastic variational inference and derives global and local update rules; the local-update approximation is intuitive and compelling.  
- Main modeling contribution is a straightforward combination of aMMSB with MMTM, but the presentation is detailed and needs clarification.  
- Lacks discussion of extensions to directed networks, which are common in practice.  
- The claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is questionable: ERGMs use broader statistics and the label is overly broad.  
- Interpretability of the inferred latent space is unclear; the paper doesn’t explain what insights the representations give about graph similarities/differences.  
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.  
- The rationale for approximating by subsampling triangles per node (using delta) is inadequately justified.  
- If the aim is to identify active roles, the model might benefit from a more binary role representation.  
- The robustness of latent-space recovery to the chosen threshold (0.125) is not investigated and seems arbitrary.  
- Need to clarify which latent-space model was used in experiments, since multiple models can generate power-law networks.  
- The reported K^3 complexity of PTM CGS conflicts with the authors’ O(K) parameter claim and is puzzling.  
- Concern about performance on sparse networks; experiments only withhold 10% of edges, likely to avoid biased triangle distributions from more aggressive removal.  
- It is counterintuitive that the method outperforms MMSB on data synthetically generated by MMSB (Figure 1 left panels).  
- Overall summary: leverages SVI to learn a parsimonious mixed-membership blockmodel based on triangle counts, yielding a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
