- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) to reduce parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) and a stochastic variational inference algorithm with scalability tricks.  
- Practical scalability advantages in K are questionable and not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Experiments should compare MMTM vs PTM as a function of K to show whether PTM with large K outperforms MMTM with small K; such experiments are missing.  
- Model selection and choice of K for PTM and MMSB on real data are not clearly described and may bias results.  
- Authors under-discuss the limits of their simplifying assumptions and fail to characterize when PTM will fail (e.g., when B truly has O(K^3) distinct values).  
- Synthetic experiments lack clear description of the true B used and do not test cases that disfavor PTM.  
- Scalability tricks and approximations are interesting but poorly analyzed; their empirical impact is unclear.  
- The effect of the threshold δ and how different δ values affect results on real/synthetic data is not evaluated.  
- Claim that a-MMSB fails due to downsampling needs justification; it is unclear why PTM would not suffer similarly.  
- The impact of O(K) approximations to local updates (e.g., sampling 2K, 4K, or 100K triples) is not discussed or tested.  
- Reviewer is unconvinced the experimental setup was fair to competitors.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized.  
- Modeling is somewhat incremental but the idea of achieving scalability by constraining parameters is interesting and original.  
- Potential significance is high if assumptions hold, since few latent-space network models scale to large data.  
- Title is too broad; paper presents a scalable algorithm for a specific model (PTM), not all latent-space models.  
- Doubts raised about whether PTM is truly probabilistic and about the bag-of-triples approximation breaking likelihood-based interpretation.
