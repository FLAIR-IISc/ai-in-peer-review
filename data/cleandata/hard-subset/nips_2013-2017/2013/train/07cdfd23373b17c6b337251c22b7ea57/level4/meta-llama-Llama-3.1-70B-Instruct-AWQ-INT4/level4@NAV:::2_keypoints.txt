- Introduces a novel triangle-count-based model for latent structure in networks, analogous to bag-of-words for documents.  
- Parameterizes K roles via triangle configurations (inspired by aMMSB) to reduce parameters and improve scalability.  
- Applies stochastic variational inference with derived global and local updates; local update uses an intuitive approximation.  
- Presentation and clarity could be improved, especially around the combined aMMSB/MMTM contribution.  
- Should discuss extension to directed networks given their prevalence.  
- Claim that PTM is a latent-space counterpart to ERGMs is unclear and potentially misleading.  
- Paper does not address interpretability of the inferred latent space or insights about graph similarities/differences.  
- Distinction between Δ^1 and Δ^2 is unclear, and the triangle subsampling scheme (δ) lacks justification.  
- Role recovery objective could be clearer with a binary notion of role presence/absence.  
- Robustness of latent-space recovery to the 0.125 threshold is untested, and the latent-space model used in experiments is unspecified.  
- Stated PTM CGS complexity O(K^3) contradicts earlier claim of O(K) parameters.  
- Experiments only hold out 10% of edges; performance with sparser observations is a concern.  
- Superior performance over MMSB on MMSB-generated synthetic data is counterintuitive and needs explanation.  
- Overall, uses SVI to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel restricted to triangle counts, enabling a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
