- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing MMTM parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) with stochastic variational inference and additional scalability approximations.  
- Synthetic experiments suggest parameter reduction can yield stronger statistical power; real-data results show competitive accuracy.  
- Practical advantage of scaling in K is not clearly demonstrated; unclear how learning large-K mixed-membership models helps in practice.  
- Missing experiments comparing PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K to show PTM’s benefit over a small-K MMTM.  
- Model selection details (how K was chosen for PTM and MMSB) are not provided, risking biased comparisons.  
- Paper lacks discussion of failure modes from simplifying assumptions; PTM may perform poorly if true B has O(K^3) distinct values.  
- Synthetic experiments insufficiently diverse—need B matrices that both favor and violate PTM assumptions.  
- Several introduced scalability techniques are inadequately analyzed; their empirical impacts are left unanswered.  
- Unanswered: effect of threshold δ on the scalability–accuracy trade-off.  
- Unanswered: why PTM is claimed immune to downsampling accuracy loss and why ignoring non-edges is acceptable for PTM but not MMSB.  
- Unanswered: impact of the O(K) approximation for local updates and how sampling more triples (2K, 4K, 100K) or increasing K affects approximation quality.  
- Reviewer not convinced experimental setup was fair to competitors and criticizes lack of discussion on algorithmic weaknesses.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Originality is moderate: constraining parameters for scalability is an interesting and original direction despite incremental modeling.  
- Potential significance is high if assumptions hold, as the work could enable scalable latent-space models for large networks.  
- Title may be overly broad relative to the specific model and algorithm contribution.  
- Fundamental concern: bag-of-triples assumption may break probabilistic validity—most generated triple bags may not correspond to valid graphs, questioning the model’s likelihood and meaning of probability.
