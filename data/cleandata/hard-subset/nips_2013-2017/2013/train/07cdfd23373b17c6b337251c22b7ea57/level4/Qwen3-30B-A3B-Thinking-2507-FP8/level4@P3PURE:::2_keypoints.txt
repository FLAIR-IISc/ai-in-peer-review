- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle-count statistics (a bag-of-words style preprocessing) for social networks.  
- Main modeling advance: builds on aMMSB by parameterizing only K roles via triangle configurations instead of all inter-community interactions.  
- Role-based parameterization reduces model parameters and enables more scalable implementation.  
- Applies stochastic variational inference and derives both global and local update rules for the proposed model.  
- Novelty: combines aMMSB with MMTM, though presentation and derivations are somewhat tedious and could be clearer.  
- Provides an intuitive approximation for the local update rule that is interesting and nonstandard.  
- Lacks discussion of extensions to directed graphs, which are common in practice.  
- Questionable claim that PTM is a latent-space counterpart to ERGMs: ERGMs use many statistics beyond triangles and PTM’s relation to latent-space models is unclear.  
- Unclear whether inferred latent spaces are interpretable or what insights they provide about graph similarities/differences.  
- The distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is not clearly explained.  
- Insufficient justification for subsampling triangles per node (using Δ) as an adequate approximation.  
- If goal is recovering "non-zero roles," a more explicit binary role formulation might be beneficial.  
- Robustness of latent space recovery to the 0.125 threshold is untested and appears arbitrary.  
- Experiments do not specify which latent-space model was used to generate power-law networks.  
- PTM CGS has K³ complexity, conflicting with the authors’ claim of O(K) parameters—this discrepancy needs clarification.  
- Performance under sparse-edge conditions is not studied; holding out only 10% of edges is a limitation when fewer edges are observed.  
- It is counterintuitive that PTM outperforms MMSB on networks generated by MMSB (Figure 1), raising questions about evaluation.  
- Overall: uses SVI to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel on triangle counts, yielding a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
