- PTM reduces MMTM’s parameter complexity from O(K³) to O(K), enabling faster inference.  
- The practical benefit of scaling in K is unclear; need to justify why learning MMTM with large K is useful.  
- Experiments should show performance of PTM and MMTM as a function of K to demonstrate PTM’s value.  
- The choice and model selection procedure for K in real-data experiments are not well-explained and may introduce bias.  
- Constraining parameters achieves scalability but the paper insufficiently analyzes scenarios where PTM will fail (e.g., true B has O(K³) distinct values).  
- Synthetic experiments lack diversity in true B matrices; authors should test cases favorable and unfavorable to PTM.  
- Several scalability approximations are interesting but their effects are inadequately analyzed (threshold δ, downsampling, O(K) approximation).  
- Specific missing analyses: how δ affects accuracy/scalability, why PTM avoids downsampling accuracy loss that affects a-MMSB, and impact of sampling more triples (2K, 4K, etc.).  
- The experimental setup may not provide a fair comparison to competitors; more thorough baselines and ablations are needed.  
- Writing and organization are clear and well-presented.  
- The modeling contribution is somewhat incremental, but parameter-constraint-based scalability is an interesting, original direction.  
- The paper’s significance is conditional: if assumptions hold, PTM could enable scalable latent-space modeling for large networks.  
- The title is too broad; paper presents a scalable algorithm for a specific model (PTM), not a general solution for latent-space model scalability.  
- Doubts about PTM being a true probabilistic/generative model: bag-of-triples assumption may not correspond to realistic network likelihoods, raising interpretability concerns.
