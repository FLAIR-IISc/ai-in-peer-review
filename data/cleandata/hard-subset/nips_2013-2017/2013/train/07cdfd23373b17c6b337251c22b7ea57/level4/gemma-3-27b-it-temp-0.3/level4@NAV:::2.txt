This paper introduces a novel technique for uncovering latent structure within social network data by modeling triangle count statistics – effectively a “bag-of-triangles” approach. The core innovation builds upon aMMSB by focusing on triangle configurations to define *K* network roles, thereby reducing the parameter space and improving scalability. The authors leverage recent advances in stochastic variational inference, developing both global and local update rules for their model.

The primary contribution lies in the integration of aMMSB with the MMTM, although the presentation of this combination could benefit from further clarity. While the learning algorithm largely applies standard stochastic variational inference techniques, the authors present a compelling approximation for the local update step. 

Considering the prevalence of directed networks, a discussion of potential extensions to handle such structures would strengthen the work. 

Several points warrant further consideration:

- The claim that the proposed Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” feels imprecise, as ERGMs can incorporate a broader range of statistics than solely triangles, and the notion of a “latent space counterpart” seems broadly applicable to many latent network models.
- The interpretability of the inferred latent space is unclear. Exploring what these latent dimensions reveal about the similarities and differences between the analyzed graphs would be valuable.
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 requires clarification.
- The justification for approximating triangle counts via node-level subsampling (using delta) is not fully explained.
- Given the goal of identifying a “set of non-zero roles,” a more explicit binary representation of roles within the model might be beneficial.
- The sensitivity of the latent space recovery to the chosen threshold of 0.125 (which appears somewhat arbitrary) should be investigated.
- The specific latent space model used to generate the power-law networks in the experiments should be specified.
- The reported K^3 complexity of PTM CGS appears inconsistent with the argument that the model possesses O(K) parameters.
- The method’s performance with sparse graphs is a concern, potentially exacerbated by the limited 10% edge holdout in the experiments, which may introduce bias in the triangle statistics.
- The observation that the method outperforms MMSB on a synthetic network generated *by* MMSB (Figure 1, left 4 panels) is unexpected and warrants further investigation.

In summary, this work presents a scalable approach to mixed-membership blockmodeling using stochastic variational inference on triangle counts, achieving an O(NK) complexity through strategic modeling choices and approximations.
