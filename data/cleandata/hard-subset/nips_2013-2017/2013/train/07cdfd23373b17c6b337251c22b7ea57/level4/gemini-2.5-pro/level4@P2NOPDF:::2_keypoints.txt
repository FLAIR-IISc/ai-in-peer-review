- Introduces PTM: models triangle counts as a "bag-of-triangles" to learn latent social roles.  
- Parsimonious parameterization reduces parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), improving scalability.  
- Uses stochastic variational inference (SVI) with an efficient, clever approximation for local updates.  
- Novel integration of ideas from aMMSB and MMTM, though components are familiar.  
- The distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is unclear and needs a precise definition.  
- No thorough theoretical or empirical justification for subsampling triangles per node as a sufficient approximation.  
- The claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is insufficiently justified and needs clarification.  
- Potential mismatch between goal of recovering sparse/non-zero roles and the model’s continuous role memberships; consider sparsity-inducing priors.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis of inferred roles to assess interpretability and insights from real networks.  
- Apparent contradiction: paper claims O(K) parameters but states collapsed Gibbs sampler has O(K³) complexity—needs clarification.  
- The generative model used for power-law network experiments is unspecified and should be detailed.  
- The result of PTM outperforming MMSB on data generated by MMSB is counterintuitive and requires explanation.  
- Experiments use a 90/10 train/test split; need analysis of robustness to much sparser data (larger edge removal).  
- Sensitivity to the threshold of 0.125 for latent-space recovery is not explored and should be analyzed.  
- Paper focuses only on undirected networks; discussion of extending PTM to directed graphs would broaden impact.  
- Overall: promising, scalable O(NK) approach, but needs clearer methodology, stronger experimental validation, and justification of modeling claims.
