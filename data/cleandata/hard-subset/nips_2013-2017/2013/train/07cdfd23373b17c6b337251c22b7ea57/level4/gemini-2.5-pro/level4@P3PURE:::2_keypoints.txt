- Proposes a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that learns latent roles from triangle counts, combining ideas from aMMSB and mixed-membership topic models.
- Uses stochastic variational inference with derived global and local update rules; includes an intuitive approximation for the local update.
- Claims PTM is a latent-space counterpart to ERGMs, but this comparison is unclear and likely overstated.
- Presentation could be improved to better explain the novel modeling choices and approximations.
- Unclear whether inferred latent spaces are interpretable and what they reveal about graph similarities/differences.
- The distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is not clearly explained and needs clarification.
- Need justification for subsampling triangles per node (δ) and why it provides an adequate approximation.
- Suggests considering a binary or sparse representation for roles if the goal is to recover “non-zero roles.”
- The 0.125 threshold for latent space recovery seems arbitrary; robustness to threshold variation must be evaluated.
- The paper should specify which latent-space model was used to generate power-law networks for experiments.
- Contradiction about complexity: model claimed to have O(K) parameters, yet PTM CGS is said to have K³ complexity; source of K³ must be clarified.
- Concern about robustness to sparse data: experiments hold out only 10% of edges and performance under higher edge missingness is unclear.
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforming MMSB on networks generated by MMSB needs explanation.
- Would benefit from discussion on extending the method to directed graphs.
- Claims modeling choices and approximations yield a scalable O(NK) algorithm, which is a key selling point.
