This paper develops a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. The authors introduce an expressive subclass of the model that shares parameters effectively and employ stochastic variational inference to handle very large networks. The work is well-written and well-executed. Overall, this is a solid contribution and I recommend acceptance.

**Major Comments:**

*   **p. 1 (Clarification of "parsimonious modeling"):** The term "parsimonious modeling" is central to the paper's motivation. As this is not a standard term in the context of probabilistic models, the authors should clarify its specific meaning in this context, perhaps by expanding on it in the introduction.
*   **p. 1 (Characterization of Gopalan et al.):** The introduction suggests that the down-sampling of non-links (zeros) in Gopalan et al. compromises accuracy. My understanding of that work is that it provides an unbiased estimator, thus preserving accuracy. The authors should clarify or correct this characterization.
*   **p. 4 (Analogy to LDA):** The paper defends the triangle model's potential to generate invalid networks by drawing an analogy to LDA. I find this analogy unconvincing. The generative process for LDA always produces a valid (if perhaps nonsensical) document. In contrast, the generative process for the proposed model can produce objects that are not valid networks (e.g., violating symmetry in an undirected graph). While the model's practical utility is not in question, this specific justification via an appeal to LDA should be reconsidered.
*   **p. 5 (Citation for SVI theory):** The citation to Amari and Sato for the natural gradient update is not fully appropriate. Sato's algorithm applies to models without local latent variables. Given the presence of "local variables" (in the terminology of Hoffman et al.), the relevant theoretical justification for the updates used here is found in Hoffman et al.'s work on stochastic variational inference, which should be cited.
*   **p. 6 (Experimental Details):** The experiments involve generating power-law networks in the latent space. The paper should provide more specific details on how this was accomplished, as the current description is too vague for reproducibility.
*   **p. 6 (Experimental Comparison):** The paper mentions Gopalan et al. as a method for accelerating MMSB inference. Given this, a comparison against their algorithm would be a more compelling baseline than the batch MMSB algorithm, as it represents a more direct state-of-the-art competitor for scalable inference on network data. The authors should consider including this comparison.

**Minor Comments:**

*   **p. 2:** "mixture-membership" should be "mixed-membership".
*   **p. 8:** The final sentence of the conclusion could be removed. The penultimate sentence provides a stronger and more impactful ending to the paper.
*   **Bibliography:** The citation for "Stochastic Variational Inference" should be updated to the JMLR version.
