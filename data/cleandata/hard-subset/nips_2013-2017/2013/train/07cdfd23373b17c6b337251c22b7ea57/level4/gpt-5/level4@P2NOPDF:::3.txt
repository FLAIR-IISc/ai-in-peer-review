This paper presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model of networks. The authors introduce a well-designed subclass with clever parameter sharing and use stochastic variational inference to handle very large networks. The paper is clearly written and carefully executed. I recommend acceptance.

Comments by page:
- p1: Please clarify what you mean by “parsimonious” modeling. This term is not standard in probabilistic modeling, and because it is central to your argument, a brief explanation would help readers.
- p1: My reading of Gopalan et al. suggests that down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy. The statement in your introduction implies otherwise and is therefore misleading. Please revise or qualify this claim and cite appropriately.
- p4: While I’m sympathetic to the triangle model as a pragmatic choice, the analogy to LDA is not convincing. Generating from LDA yields something that is still a “document,” however simplistic; generating from your model can produce objects that are not valid networks. This does not undermine the practical value of your approach, but LDA is not an appropriate defense.
- p5: Please cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato. In the presence of local variables (in Hoffman’s terminology), Sato’s algorithm does not apply; the necessary theory is provided by Hoffman et al.
- p6: How did you generate power-law networks in the latent space? The paper is vague on this point. Please detail the generative mechanism and parameters used.
- p6: Since you cite Gopalan et al. as a way to accelerate MMSB, you should compare against their algorithm, in addition to (or instead of) MMSB in batch form.

Small comments:
- p2: “mixture-membership” → “mixed-membership.”
- p8: Consider removing the last sentence; the second-to-last sentence is a stronger conclusion.
- Bibliography: Please cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference.”

Overall, this is a strong and practical contribution that scales a useful model to large networks. With the clarifications and comparisons above, the paper will be even stronger. I recommend acceptance.
