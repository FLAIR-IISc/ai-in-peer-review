- Paper proposes the parsimonious triangular model (PTM) reducing MMTM parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) with a stochastic variational inference algorithm and scalability approximations.  
- Synthetic results suggest variable reduction can increase statistical power; real-world experiments claim competitive accuracy versus existing methods.  
- Practical advantage of PTM is unclear because the paper focuses on scalability in K without showing PTM with large K outperforming MMTM with small K.  
- Missing evaluation of performance as a function of K on real networks; paper does not report results across K values.  
- K selection for real-data experiments is not explained, creating risk of model selection bias between PTM and competitors (e.g., MMSB).  
- Paper fails to discuss model failure modes: if B contains O(K^3) distinct values, PTM should fail, but this is not analyzed.  
- Synthetic experiments do not specify the true B matrices nor test diverse B instances (favorable and unfavorable to PTM).  
- Key approximation details are inadequately analyzed: the tradeoff between threshold δ, scalability, and accuracy is unexplored.  
- The claim that a-MMSB fails due to downsampling inaccuracies is not addressed for PTM, which also ignores triplets with one edge.  
- Unclear impact of O(K) approximation on local updates—how sampling sizes (2K, 4K, 100K triples) affect approximation quality as K increases is not studied.  
- Overall experimental setup appears unfair to competitors and the model’s weaknesses are poorly characterized.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized (clarity).  
- Novelty is incremental, but using parameter constraints to achieve scalability is a worthwhile and distinct direction.  
- If PTM’s assumptions hold, it could significantly advance scalable latent-space network modeling (significance).  
- Title is overly broad; PTM is a scalable algorithm for a specific latent-space model, not a general scalable approach.  
- Probabilistic foundation is questionable: generating “bags-of-triples” rarely matches actual networks, so PTM’s posterior may concentrate on non-networks and the generative interpretation lacks rigor; side effects are not investigated.
