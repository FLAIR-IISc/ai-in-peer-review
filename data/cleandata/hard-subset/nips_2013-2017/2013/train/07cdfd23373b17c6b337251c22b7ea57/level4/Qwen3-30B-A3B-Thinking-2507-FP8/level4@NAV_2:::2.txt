

This paper introduces a method for inferring latent structures in large social networks by modeling triangular motif counts, akin to a bag-of-words approach for text. The core modeling innovation builds upon the aMMSB framework by recognizing that triangle configurations allow for a reduced parameterization of between-role interactions, resulting in O(K) parameters instead of O(K²) or O(K³). This parsimonious parameterization enables a more scalable inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity. The authors apply stochastic variational inference techniques to their model, developing both global update rules and an efficient approximation for the local update step that requires only O(K) work per triangle.

The modeling contribution represents a straightforward integration of the aMMSB and MMTM approaches, though the presentation could be strengthened to better highlight the technical nuances. While most of the inference algorithm directly applies established stochastic variational inference methods, the proposed approximation for the local update rule provides an intuitive and computationally efficient solution.

Key concerns include:
- The claim that "PTM is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs" requires clarification, as ERGMs incorporate diverse network statistics beyond triangles, and the relationship to latent space models is not uniquely defined.
- The interpretability of the inferred latent space remains unclear—what meaningful insights about network structure do the recovered roles provide?
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not sufficiently explained.
- The justification for triangle subsampling (using parameter δ) lacks sufficient theoretical grounding.
- The threshold of 0.125 for role assignment appears arbitrary; its robustness to different threshold values should be examined.
- For the power-law network experiments, it is unclear which specific latent space model was used as the generative process.
- The PTM collapsed Gibbs sampler has O(Nδ²K³) complexity despite having O(K) parameters, which requires further explanation.
- The evaluation only holds out 10% of edges, raising questions about performance when fewer edges are observed.
- The results showing PTM outperforming MMSB on synthetic networks generated by MMSB (Figure 1, left 4 panels) seem counterintuitive and warrant further investigation.

Overall, the work successfully applies stochastic variational inference to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel with restricted parameters on triangle counts, achieving O(NK) scalability while maintaining competitive accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction.
