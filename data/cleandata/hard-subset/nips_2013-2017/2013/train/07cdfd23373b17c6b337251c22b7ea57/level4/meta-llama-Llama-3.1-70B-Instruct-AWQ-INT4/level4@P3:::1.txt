This paper proposes a scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks, based on a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) and a stochastic variational inference algorithm. The PTM constrains the O(K^3) parameter space of the mixed-membership triangular model (MMTM) to O(K) for faster inference. The authors develop several approximation tricks to make the algorithm further scalable.

Summary of the main ideas and relation to previous work:
The paper builds upon previous work on latent space models, particularly the MMTM and the mixed-membership stochastic blockmodel (MMSB). The authors argue that existing approaches are limited in their ability to scale to large networks, and propose a new approach that combines a succinct representation of networks as a bag of triangular motifs, a parsimonious statistical model, and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. The PTM is a specialization of the MMTM, which reduces the number of parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) by sharing parameters among certain groups of latent roles.

Quality:
The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their approach. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the reviewer raises several concerns about the practical advantage of achieving good scalability in terms of K, the number of possible roles. The reviewer questions whether the reduction of the number of variables may lead to stronger statistical power, and whether the proposed method is competitive with existing methods in terms of accuracy.

The reviewer also points out that the authors are reticent about the impact of simplifying assumptions they make, and that it would be beneficial to know when the PTM would fail. The choice of true B in synthetic data experiments is not clearly described, and the reviewer suggests that the authors should have experimented with various B's that are in favor of PTM and some that are against the model.

The reviewer is also not convinced that the experimental setup was fair to competitors, and that the description of potential weaknesses of the algorithm/model is poor. The reviewer raises several questions about the impact of choosing the threshold δ, the impact of O(K) approximation to local update, and the quality of approximation when K gets larger.

Clarity:
The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The authors provide a detailed and easy-to-follow presentation of their approach, and the notation is consistent throughout the paper.

Originality:
The paper is incremental in terms of modeling, but achieving scalability via constraining parameters is an interesting and original direction to pursue. The reviewer notes that recently, a lot of work has been focused on generalizing existing models, and the authors' approach is a refreshing change.

Significance:
Provided the simplifying assumptions in the model and the inference algorithm do hold in practice, the paper would have a good impact on the practice of latent-space models on networks. The reviewer notes that not many of these models actually scale to large data, and the authors' approach is a significant contribution in this regard.

Additional Comments:
The reviewer suggests that the title is too broad, and that the authors propose a scalable algorithm to a latent space model, PTM. The reviewer is also not sure that PTM is really a probabilistic method, and argues that the authors' argument that it is similar in spirit to the bag-of-words assumption is not convincing. The reviewer suggests that the authors should investigate the side-effects of their modeling assumptions and inference tricks, and that it might be dangerous to abandon basic principles of likelihood in "probabilistic" methods.

Arguments for and against acceptance:

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks.
* The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their approach, and the notation is consistent throughout the paper.
* The paper has the potential to make a significant impact on the practice of latent-space models on networks.
