- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle count statistics (bag-of-triangles analogy).
- Uses triangle configurations to define K roles instead of parameterizing all between-community interactions, reducing parameters and improving scalability.
- Derives stochastic variational inference updates (global and local) for the proposed model.
- Introduces a mixture-of-deltas variational approximation for local updates, enabling much faster inference — a key contribution.
- Modeling is essentially a combination of a-MMSB and MMTM; derivations are tedious and need clearer exposition and intuition.
- Empirical results show competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link prediction and orders-of-magnitude speedups on large networks.
- Presentation could be more concise; key derivation steps lack sufficient intuition and explanation.
- Paper lacks discussion on extending the method to directed networks, which are common in practice.
- Interpretability of the inferred latent space and cross-graph insights are unclear and underexplained.
- Insufficient justification for modeling choices such as subsampling triangles (delta) and the threshold for non-zero roles.
- Unclear why the PTM Gibbs sampler is claimed to have K^3 complexity despite arguments of O(K) parameters.
- Missing robustness analyses: sensitivity to the non-zero-role threshold and the impact of observing fewer edges.
- Overall a solid, potentially impactful contribution if clarity, justification, and robustness concerns are addressed.
- Strengths: novel technique, scalable SVI algorithm, strong empirical performance; Weaknesses: clarity, intuition, extensions, and modeling justifications.
