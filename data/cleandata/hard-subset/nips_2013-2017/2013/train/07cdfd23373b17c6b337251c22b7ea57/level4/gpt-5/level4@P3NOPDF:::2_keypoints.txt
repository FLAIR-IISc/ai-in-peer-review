- Proposes a parsimonious latent-role model using triangle-count statistics to identify K roles, analogous to bag-of-words for documents.
- Combines ideas from assortative MMSB and motif-based models (MMTM) to parameterize interactions via triangular motifs, reducing parameters and improving scalability.
- Develops a stochastic variational inference (SVI) procedure with global and local updates and an intuitive, practical local update approximation.
- Empirically claims linear scaling in N and K and that triangle aggregation yields a scalable alternative to edge-level modeling.

- Clear motivation for using triangle statistics to reduce parameterization versus full blockmodels.
- Role-based parameterization is parsimonious and can capture higher-order structure with fewer parameters.
- The SVI adaptation is well done and the local update approximation is appealing and useful in practice.
- Learning on aggregated triangular motifs offers potential scalability benefits.

- Novelty is unclear: model appears to be a straightforward combination of aMMSB and existing motif models, requiring clearer articulation of new contributions.
- The claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is misleading and needs refinement or justification.
- Interpretability is under-addressed: need examples of inferred roles, node-role distributions, and role–role interaction summaries.
- No discussion of extending the model to directed graphs and the challenges of directed motifs.
- Delta^1 vs. Delta^2 distinction is unclear; definitions and impacts on updates, bias/variance, convergence, and runtime are needed.
- Subsampling triangles per node (delta) lacks theoretical justification—need to show unbiasedness/consistency and variance dependence on delta and sparsity.
- Model lacks consideration of sparsity-inducing priors or binary latent variables to recover discrete nonzero roles.
- Choice of 0.125 threshold for nonzero roles is arbitrary; sensitivity analysis across thresholds is required.
- Experiments don’t clarify the generative model for power-law networks or parameter choices and how they align with PTM assumptions.
- Complexity mismatch: K^3 computational claims conflict with O(K) parameter count—explain where cubic scaling arises and how SVI avoids it.
- Concern about performance under high edge missingness; need experiments with greater holdout rates and discussion of preprocessing bias.
- Synthetic result anomaly: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated data needs explanation and diagnostics ruling out hyperparameter/optimization artifacts.

- Exposition of triangle configurations and mapping to K roles should include a concise generative story and a small worked example.
- Expand intuition, approximation error analysis, and regimes where the local update approximation helps or harms.
- Provide clearer notation for triangle statistics and a symbol-definition table (including Delta^1 and Delta^2).
- Include per-iteration runtime and memory complexity and wall-clock comparisons on large graphs to substantiate O(NK) claims.

- Better position the work relative to MMSB variants, motif-based latent models, and ERGMs, clarifying contributions in parsimony, inference, and scalability.

- Suggested experiments: ablations over delta, K, and nonzero-role thresholds with effects on accuracy, calibration, and runtime.
- Explore sparsity-inducing priors (IBP/Beta-Bernoulli, spike-and-slab) to sharpen role recovery and threshold robustness.
- Add directed-network experiments or a concrete extension plan with complexity estimates.
- Revisit and temper ERGM comparisons; clarify conceptual connections precisely.
- Add evaluation metrics beyond link prediction (e.g., triangle closure prediction, motif count reconstruction).

- Overall assessment: a sensible, potentially impactful approach with moderate technical novelty; parsimony and the local-update approximation are appealing but the work needs clearer exposition, theoretical justification of subsampling, sensitivity analyses, and reconciled complexity claims to be fully convincing.
