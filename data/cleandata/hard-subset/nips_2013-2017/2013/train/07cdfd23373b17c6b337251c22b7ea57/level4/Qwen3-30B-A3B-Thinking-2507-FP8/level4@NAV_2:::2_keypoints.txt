- Introduces a triangle-motif-based latent-structure model (bag-of-words analogy) for large social networks.
- Key modeling innovation: reduced between-role parameterization via triangle configurations, cutting parameters to O(K) from O(K²)/O(K³).
- Parsimonious parameterization enables a scalable inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity.
- Uses stochastic variational inference with derived global updates and an efficient O(K) per-triangle local update approximation.
- Modeling is a straightforward combination of aMMSB and MMTM; presentation should better highlight technical nuances.
- The proposed local-update approximation is intuitive and computationally efficient, though largely applying established SVI methods.
- Claim that “PTM is essentially a latent-space counterpart to ERGMs” is unclear and needs clarification given ERGMs’ broader statistics.
- Interpretability of the recovered latent roles is not demonstrated—unclear what meaningful network insights are obtained.
- The difference between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is insufficiently explained.
- Triangle subsampling via parameter δ lacks theoretical justification.
- The 0.125 threshold for role assignment appears arbitrary; robustness to other thresholds is not assessed.
- The generative latent-space model used for the power-law network experiments is not specified.
- PTM’s collapsed Gibbs sampler reportedly has O(N δ² K³) complexity despite O(K) parameters—needs further explanation.
- Evaluation holds out only 10% of edges, leaving questions about performance with sparser observations.
- PTM outperforming MMSB on networks generated by MMSB (Figure 1) is counterintuitive and requires investigation.
- Overall: successfully applies SVI to a mixed-membership blockmodel with restricted triangle-based parameters, achieving O(NK) scalability and competitive accuracy.
