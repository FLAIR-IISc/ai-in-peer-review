- PTM reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) and uses stochastic variational inference plus approximations for scalability.
- Experiments report competitive accuracy with existing methods and substantial computational efficiency gains.
- Justification for prioritizing scalability in K is unconvincing; unclear that larger K yields new analytical value over modest K.
- No direct experimental comparison of PTM with large K versus MMTM with smaller K; model-selection procedure for K is not clearly described and may bias results.
- Authors do not analyze when PTM’s simplifying assumptions degrade performance; synthetic experiments should include cases where full O(K^3) structure matters.
- Impact of approximation techniques is underexplored: effect of triangle-subsampling threshold δ on accuracy vs. scalability is not investigated.
- Need clearer explanation why PTM’s downsampling avoids the accuracy compromises attributed to a-MMSB, since PTM also ignores certain triplets.
- O(K) approximation to local updates lacks analysis: impact of different sample sizes (e.g., 2K, 4K, 100K) and behavior as K increases is unknown.
- Experimental setup lacks sufficient controls for fair comparison with competitors.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized.
- The modeling contribution is incremental but original and promising by constraining parameter space for scalability.
- If assumptions hold, PTM could significantly impact scalable latent-space modeling for large networks.
- The title may be too broad; paper presents a scalable algorithm for a specific model rather than a general solution for all latent-space models.
- Claiming PTM is “probabilistic” is questionable because PTM/MMTM can generate triangle configurations that do not correspond to valid networks, undermining a proper likelihood interpretation.
- Authors focus on scalable inference but underexplore consequences of abandoning a proper likelihood and the interpretation of posterior probabilities.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses scalability, competitive empirical performance, clear presentation, and novel parameter reduction.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak justification for focus on K, insufficient experimental controls, limited discussion of weaknesses, questionable probabilistic framing, and inadequate analysis of approximations.
- Overall assessment: leaning toward weak reject pending stronger justification for design choices, controlled experiments, and deeper analysis of limitations.
