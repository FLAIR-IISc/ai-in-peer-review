- Proposes learning latent social network structure by fitting a model to triangle-count statistics (bag-of-triangles preprocessing).
- Models triangle configurations to identify K roles, avoiding full parameterization of between-community interactions and reducing parameters.
- Claims improved scalability and derives stochastic variational inference updates (global and local).
- Core modeling is a straightforward combination of aMMSB and MMTM with tedious bookkeeping; presentation could be clearer.
- Local update approximation is intuitive and interesting despite much of the algorithm following standard SVI.
- Lacks discussion of extending the approach to directed networks, which are common in practice.
- The claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is unclear and arguably misleading (ERGMs use many statistics; unclear what “counterpart” means).
- Unclear whether the inferred latent space is interpretable and what it reveals about graph similarities/differences.
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.
- No justification for why subsampling triangles per node (using delta) yields an adequate approximation.
- If the goal is identifying non-zero roles, the model may benefit from a more explicit binary role formulation.
- Sensitivity of latent space recovery to the chosen threshold (0.125) is not examined and seems arbitrary.
- Experiments claim power-law generation but do not specify which latent space model was used.
- Complexity question: why does PTM CGS have O(K^3) complexity when the model was argued to have O(K) parameters?
- Performance with fewer observed edges is a concern; experiments only hold out 10% of edges, likely due to preprocessing bias when removing many edges.
- It is counterintuitive that PTM outperforms MMSB on data generated by MMSB (Figure 1 left panels).
- Overall summary: uses SVI to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel on triangle counts with restricted parameters, yielding a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
