- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), a novel latent-space model that represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs for scalable inference.  
- PTM reduces triangle-generating parameter dimensionality from O(K^3) to O(K), enabling much more efficient modeling than prior triangular models.  
- Introduces a fast stochastic natural gradient variational inference algorithm with time complexity O(NK).  
- Demonstrates scalability: can analyze networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine in hours.  
- Combines ideas from aMMSB and MMTM to achieve a more parsimonious, scalable implementation.  
- Presents an intuitive approximation for the local update rule that is central to the SVI algorithm.  
- Experiments show PTM effectively recovers latent roles and performs link prediction on synthetic and real networks.  
- Presentation needs improvement, especially clearer explanation of PTM and its relationship to ERGMs.  
- Requires more discussion on interpretability of inferred latent spaces and insights from graph similarities/differences.  
- Unclear distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 and insufficient justification for subsampling triangles per node as an adequate approximation.  
- Experimental concerns: robustness of latent recovery to the 0.125 threshold is not analyzed.  
- Missing evaluation of method performance when fewer edges are observed (sparser data scenario).
