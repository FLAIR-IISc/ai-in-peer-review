- Proposes learning latent structure from social networks using triangle count statistics as a bag-of-words–style preprocessing.
- Main modeling advance: a parsimonious variant inspired by aMMSB that parameterizes roles via triangle configurations, reducing parameter count.
- Combines aMMSB with the Mixed Membership Matrix Triad Model (MMTM); integration requires tedious bookkeeping and presentation could be clearer.
- Applies stochastic variational inference, deriving global and local updates for the proposed model.
- Provides an intuitive approximation for the local update rule despite most updates being standard SVI.
- Lacks discussion on extending the method to directed networks, which are common.
- Questionable claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart” to ERGMs, since ERGMs use many statistics beyond triangles.
- Unclear whether the inferred latent space is interpretable or what insights it yields about graph similarities/differences.
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.
- Unclear justification for why subsampling triangles per node (using delta) yields an adequate approximation.
- If goal is recovering “non-zero roles,” model might need a more binary notion of role membership.
- Robustness to the arbitrary threshold of 0.125 for latent space recovery is not evaluated.
- Experiments do not clarify which latent space model (among many that produce power-law networks) was used to generate data.
- Complexity claim: PTM stated as O(K^3) for collapsed Gibbs sampler conflicts with earlier claim of only O(K) parameters.
- Concern about method performance with fewer observed edges; experiments only hold out 10% of edges, potentially biasing triangle distribution.
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforms aMMSB on synthetic data generated by aMMSB (Figure 1, left panels).
- Overall claim: modeling and approximations enable a more scalable O(NK) algorithm for mixed-membership learning on triangle counts.
