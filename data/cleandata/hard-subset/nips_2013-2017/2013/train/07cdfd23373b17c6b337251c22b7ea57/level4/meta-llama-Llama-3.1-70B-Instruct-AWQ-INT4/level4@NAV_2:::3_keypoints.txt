- Presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for a "triangle model" of networks using a clever parameter-sharing subclass.  
- Uses stochastic variational inference to handle very large networks efficiently.  
- Succinct representation of networks as a bag of triangular motifs and a parsimonious statistical model.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough discussion of strengths/weaknesses and a useful pro/con list for the program committee.  
- Scales to millions of nodes and hundreds of latent roles, demonstrated on several large real networks.  
- Empirical results show competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link-prediction accuracy.  
- Good literature review with appropriate citations and relation to prior NIPS work.  
- Minor issue: the term "parsimonious" is used without a clear explanation and should be unpacked.  
- Minor issue: introductory claim about down-sampling zeros is misleading relative to Gopalan et al.’s findings.  
- Minor issue: analogy to LDA is not fully appropriate because the triangle model can generate non-network objects.  
- Citation/algorithmic issue: should cite Hoffman (and Amari/Sato context) since Sato’s algorithm doesn’t apply with local variables.  
- Methodological detail missing: need more information on how power-law networks were generated in latent space.  
- Experimental comparison incomplete: should compare to Gopalan et al.’s algorithm (rather than or in addition to MMSB batch).  
- Overall judgment: well-executed and acceptable paper.
