- Proposes inferring latent structure from triangle count statistics extracted in preprocessing (analogous to bag-of-words).
- Parameterizes K roles by triangle configurations (inspired by aMMSB), reducing parameter count and improving scalability.
- Derives global and local stochastic variational inference updates and introduces a compelling approximation for the local update.
- Modeling is a fairly direct combination of aMMSB and MMTM and involves substantial bookkeeping; clarity of that section could be improved.
- Lacks discussion of extensions to ubiquitous directed networks.
- Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is questionable because ERGMs include many more statistics and the claimed correspondence is not established.
- Does not discuss interpretability of the inferred latent space or what insights it gives about graph similarities/differences.
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.
- No justification given that subsampling triangles per node using delta yields a sufficient approximation.
- If the goal is recovering a “set of non-zero roles,” the model may need a more binary notion of role assignment.
- Robustness of latent space recovery to the arbitrary threshold value 0.125 is not explored.
- Paper fails to specify which latent-space model able to produce power-law networks was used in experiments.
- Complexity analysis appears contradictory: PTM CGS reported as K^3 despite an argued parameter count of O(K).
- Major concern about performance in sparse graphs; experiments only hold out 10% of edges, likely because greater removal biases triangle preprocessing.
- Surprising empirical result: PTM outperforming MMSB on data generated by MMSB (Figure 1) is unexpected and needs further explanation.
- Summary point: SVI fitting of a mixed-membership blockmodel on triangle counts yields a scalable O(NK) inference procedure.
