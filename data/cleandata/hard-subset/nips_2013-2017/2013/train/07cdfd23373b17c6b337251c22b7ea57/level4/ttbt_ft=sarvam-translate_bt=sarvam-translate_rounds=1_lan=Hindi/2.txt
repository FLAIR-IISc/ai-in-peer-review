This paper proposes a technique for learning the latent structure in social network data. This model is suitable for triangle computation statistics obtained from a preprocessing step, similar to bag-of-words representation for text documents. The main modeling advancement is inspired by aMMSB: instead of parameterizing all the "community" interactions, they consider the configuration of triangles to identify a set of K roles to parameterize. This results in fewer parameters in the model and, in turn, a more scalable implementation. They apply recent advances in Stochastic Variational Inference – obtaining a global update and local updates for the proposed model.

The new modeling contribution is a direct combination of aMMSB with MMTM, although some extent of tedious bookkeeping was required. The presentation of this material could be improved.

Although most learning algorithms are a direct application of Stochastic Variational Inference, the authors have provided a intuitive and interesting approximation for the local update rule.

Given the breadth of directed networks, a detailed discussion on how these methods can be extended would be welcome.

Ongoing Concerns:
- "Our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space equivalent of ERGM, representing processes for generating triangular motifs". This seems like a strange statement: 1) ERGM can include much more diverse statistics than just triangles, and 2) I don't see how PTM is a "latent space equivalent", unless all latent space network models are "latent space equivalents".
- Are the inferred latent spaces interpretable? If so, what do we learn about the similarities and differences between these graphs?
The difference between delta^1 and delta^2 is not clear (at least to this reader).
- It is not clarified (in this paper) why the subsampling of triangles (using delta) for each node provides a sufficient approximation.
- If the goal is to recover a "set of non-zero roles", then perhaps the model should have a more binary notion of "role".
- How robust are the latent space retrieval results for the .125 limit (which seems somewhat arbitrary)?
- Different types of latent space models can produce power-law networks. Which one did you use for the experiments?
- Why is PTM CGS of complexity K^3? Didn't you argue that there are O(K) parameters instead of O(K^3)?
- One concern is how it will perform when fewer edges are observed. In the experiments, they only kept 10% of the edges, possibly because the distribution of adjacent triangles would be quite biased if too many edges were removed before the preprocessing step.
- It is not intuitive that the method beats MMSB on a synthetic network generated through MMSB (Figure 1, left 4 panel).

This work uses Stochastic Variational Inference to learn a mixed-membership block model (with a restricted set of parameters) on triangle computations in a graph. Their modeling choices and approximations allow for a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
