- Paper introduces a scalable latent-space inference approach using a bag-of-triangles representation, a new Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), and an O(NK) stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm.  
- Core contribution: PTM reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) via parameter sharing across role-triplet configurations.  
- Core contribution: SVI algorithm with structured mean-field and O(K) local updates enables inference on million-node networks in hours.  
- Experimental results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMTM and a-MMSB baselines with comparable or better latent recovery and link prediction.  
- Strength: Demonstrates significant scalability and practical utility for large probabilistic network modeling.  
- Strength: Technical novelty in the parsimonious parameter-sharing scheme that preserves expressiveness while reducing complexity.  
- Strength: Clear writing, good organization, and well-described experiments supporting claims.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparisons omit direct quantitative evaluation against a-MMSB on synthetic latent-recovery tasks, weakening competitiveness claims.  
- Weakness: Generative model can produce triangle sets that do not correspond to valid networks; the LDA analogy is unconvincing and a more rigorous justification (e.g., composite/pseudo-likelihood framing) is needed.  
- Weakness: Some technical clarity issues—define "parsimonious statistical modeling," detail latent-space generation for power-law networks, and rephrase the description of a-MMSB’s zero down-sampling for precision.  
- Weakness: Missing citation of Hoffman et al. (2013) "Stochastic Variational Inference" as foundational work for SVI with local and global variables.  
- Minor: Typo "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership"; tighten concluding sentence on p8; cite JMLR version of Hoffman et al. (2013) in bibliography.  
- Overall: Well-executed, novel, and empirically strong paper with minor experimental and framing weaknesses; recommendation: accept.
