- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle-count preprocessing, analogous to bag-of-words for graphs.  
- Main modeling idea: parsimonious parameterization using triangle configurations to identify K roles inspired by aMMSB, yielding a more scalable model.  
- Applies stochastic variational inference with derived global and local updates; introduces an intuitive approximation for the local update.  
- Core contribution is a non-trivial combination of aMMSB and MMTM, but presentation and bookkeeping are tedious and could be clearer.  
- Suggests extending the method to directed networks; discussion of such extension is missing.  
- Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is overstated—ERGMs capture many statistics beyond triangles and distinction from other latent models is unclear.  
- Unclear whether the inferred latent space is interpretable or what insights it gives about graph similarities/differences.  
- The distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is not clearly explained and should be clarified.  
- No clear justification or empirical validation that subsampling triangles per node (using δ) provides an adequate approximation.  
- If recovering the “set of non-zero roles” is a primary goal, the model should consider explicit binary role-selection mechanisms rather than post-hoc thresholding.  
- The chosen recovery threshold (0.125) appears arbitrary; robustness to threshold choice is not evaluated.  
- The exact generative model used for the reported power-law network experiments is not specified.  
- Contradiction about complexity: paper claims O(K) parameters but CGS baseline is described as O(K³); explanation for cubic complexity is unclear.  
- Concern about performance on sparse data: 90/10 split may bias triangle counts, and robustness when holding out more edges is not analyzed.  
- Surprising result that PTM outperforms MMSB on data generated from MMSB; no explanation provided.  
- Overall summary: a mixed-membership blockmodel with restricted parameterization and scalable O(NK) algorithm learned from triangle counts, with several methodological and empirical clarifications needed.
