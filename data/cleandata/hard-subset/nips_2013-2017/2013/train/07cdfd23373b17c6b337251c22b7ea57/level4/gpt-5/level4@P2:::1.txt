Summary
This paper introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), a latent-space model for networks built on the “bag of triangles” representation. PTM reduces the number of triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) in the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM) to O(K) by grouping role-triplet configurations and sharing parameters within groups. The authors develop a scalable stochastic variational inference (SVI) procedure with structured mean-field updates and an O(K) local approximation that uses a sampled set of off-diagonal role configurations. Combined with δ-based triangle subsampling, the method targets O(NK) time per global update. Experiments on synthetic data suggest PTM can recover latent roles accurately and, on real networks up to a million nodes, achieve link prediction performance competitive with or better than state-of-the-art probabilistic methods (e.g., MMSB/a-MMSB and MMTM) while being orders of magnitude faster.

Relation to prior work: PTM extends the triangular modeling approach of MMTM by tying parameters across role-triplet patterns, analogous in spirit to a-MMSB’s reduction of O(K^2) to O(K) edge parameters. It also builds on SVI techniques, with an emphasis on structured variational forms to avoid rank-1 limitations of naive mean-field. The motivation from ERGM/triadic statistics is well aligned with prior literature on the informativeness of 3-node motifs.

Quality
Strengths
- Clear modeling idea: tie the O(K^3) triangle parameters into O(K) groups (all-same, two-same-one-different, all-different), yielding a compact model.
- Scalable inference: structured mean-field with SVI, mini-batch triangle subsampling, and a practical O(K) local approximation; straightforward parallelization.
- Strong empirical scaling: convergence in few data passes and very fast runtimes on large graphs; competitive link prediction on large social networks.
- The paper is technically well organized and the derivations for SVI are sound at a high level.

Concerns and requests for clarification
- Value of large K: The primary motivation is scalability in K, yet the experiments do not show how performance varies with K for both PTM and baselines. A key test would compare PTM at large K against MMTM (and MMSB/a-MMSB) at smaller K to reveal whether scaling K yields tangible accuracy gains. The current results leave unclear whether “more roles” translates into better predictive or recovery performance in practice.
- Model selection and fairness: It is not clear how K was chosen on real datasets for PTM and MMSB/a-MMSB, nor whether hyperparameters and early stopping were tuned comparably. Please describe the model selection protocol and ensure comparable initialization, mini-batch schedules, and threading/hardware settings in runtime comparisons.
- Failure modes of parameter sharing: PTM assumes that many O(K^3) entries in the triangle parameter tensor can be tied. When the true generative structure requires heterogeneous inter-role interactions (i.e., many distinct entries in B), PTM may be misspecified. Synthetic experiments should vary “true B” complexity—some favoring PTM’s grouped structure, others requiring rich triplet interactions—and report where PTM degrades relative to MMTM. The current synthetic setup does not make the true B explicit.
- δ-subsampling sensitivity: The paper advocates δ-subsampling of triangles, but does not analyze its impact. Please report accuracy vs. runtime as a function of δ on both synthetic and real networks (e.g., δ ∈ {20, 50, 100, ∞}) and quantify how much δ can be reduced before accuracy suffers materially.
- Comparison to a-MMSB downsampling: The paper suggests a-MMSB’s downsampling of non-edges harms accuracy, while triangle subsampling in PTM does not. Why is omitting many zero entries acceptable in PTM yet problematic in MMSB? A principled discussion (and an empirical comparison under matched sparsification budgets) would help.
- O(K) local approximation: The mixture-of-deltas approximation selects O(K) off-diagonals per triangle. Please study the trade-off by varying the number of sampled off-diagonals (e.g., K, 2K, 4K, 10K) and report effects on speed, the variational bound, and downstream tasks. Also, discuss whether approximation quality degrades as K grows.
- Generative semantics: PTM (like MMTM) models bags of triangles rather than full graphs; overlapping triangles need not correspond to a coherent adjacency matrix. While the paper states that the goal is latent space inference rather than network simulation, it would be useful to formalize the probabilistic object (likelihood over triangle bags) and clarify implications for tasks that depend on full-graph likelihoods. A short discussion relating this to pseudo-/composite likelihood viewpoints would strengthen the positioning.

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and well organized. The intuition for parameter tying and the structured variational approach is easy to follow. To further improve clarity:
- State explicitly how K is selected on each real dataset.
- Provide a concise ablation: with/without δ-subsampling, with/without the O(K) local approximation, and naive vs. structured mean-field.
- Report hardware, threading, and wall-clock comparisons for all methods under matched conditions.

Originality
The modeling contribution is incremental but meaningful: parameter sharing across role-triplet configurations is a natural extension of ideas from a-MMSB to triangular models. The structured mean-field with an O(K) local approximation is a practical inference contribution.

Significance
If the parameter-sharing assumptions hold in practice, the ability to fit latent role models on million-node networks within hours is valuable. The contribution is strongest as a pragmatic advance in scalable latent-space inference, provided the paper addresses the open questions on model selection, fairness, and sensitivity analyses.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear, simple idea leading to substantial scalability gains.
- Strong empirical speedups with competitive accuracy on large real networks.
- Well-presented algorithmic framework with practical implementation details.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited exploration of when larger K actually helps; unclear model selection protocol.
- Insufficient analysis of failure modes when the true interaction tensor is complex.
- Missing sensitivity studies for δ-subsampling and the O(K) local approximation.
- Ambiguity about generative semantics and implications for likelihood-based evaluations.

Additional comments
- The title may be overly broad; consider narrowing to emphasize the triangular-motif latent-space setting and the specific scalable SVI method for PTM.
- Please clarify initialization across methods; seeding strategies can substantially affect local optima. Providing results with multiple random seeds would increase confidence.
- Open-sourcing code and scripts used for the sensitivity and ablation studies would facilitate adoption.
