- Presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model, introducing a parameter-sharing subclass and using stochastic variational inference for very large networks.  
- Paper is well-motivated, technically sound, clearly written, with strong empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Define "parsimonious" explicitly in Section 1 (e.g., what is meant by minimizing parameters or compact representation).  
- Revise the claim that "down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy" to align with Gopalan et al. (2013), who showed down-sampling can preserve accuracy.  
- Remove or reframe the LDA analogy; it’s an inappropriate defense because the triangle model can generate non-network objects.  
- Cite Hoffman et al. (2010) alongside Amari and Sato (2001) for the theoretical foundation involving local variables where Sato’s algorithm does not apply.  
- Provide detailed procedure for generating power-law networks in latent space (degree sampling method, parameters) to ensure reproducibility.  
- When comparing speedups, explicitly compare against Gopalan et al.’s MMSB speedup algorithm, not just MMSB batch.  
- Minor corrections: change "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership" (p.2); remove the final sentence on p.8; replace conference SVI citation with the JMLR (2013) version in the bibliography.
