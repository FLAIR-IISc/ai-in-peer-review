- Summary: Proposes PTM, an O(K) parameterized triangle-based latent-role model with structured SVI using triangle mini-batches and natural gradients for scalable inference on huge graphs.

- Strength: Introduces a parsimonious O(K) triangle-parameter sharing scheme that preserves expressiveness while enabling O(NK) global updates.

- Strength: Provides a scalable, well-engineered structured SVI algorithm (triangle mini-batches, mixture-of-deltas) reducing local update cost from O(K3) to O(K).

- Strength: Strong empirical results showing orders-of-magnitude speedups and competitive or improved role recovery and link prediction on synthetic and very large real networks.

- Strength: Clear exposition with intuitive motivation from triadic network structure and ERGM literature.

- Weakness: “Parsimonious” is central but undefined; needs a brief formal explanation linking parameter sharing to reduced complexity and statistical efficiency.

- Weakness: The discussion of a-MMSB may mischaracterize its accuracy vs. speed tradeoff; clarify that downsampling zeros in a-MMSB was reported not to hurt accuracy and separate computational and accuracy claims.

- Weakness: LDA analogy is inappropriate for defending non-generativity at the graph level and should be removed or reframed because PTM can generate triangle collections that don't form valid graphs.

- Weakness: Missing citation to Hoffman et al. (JMLR SVI) — Sato’s conditions are insufficient with per-triangle local variables; Hoffman's theory should be cited for structured SVI with local variables.

- Weakness: Power-law network generator is underspecified; describe the generative process or point precisely to Appendix details, including how degree heterogeneity arises from roles.

- Weakness: Must compare directly to a-MMSB (the scalable MMSB variant) rather than only batch MMSB, especially for large-scale comparisons.

- Weakness: Empirical fairness concern: MCMC baselines are single-threaded — clarify whether multi-threaded implementations would change runtime conclusions.

- Weakness: Link prediction metric needs more details (exact marginalization over roles, handling of subsampling) to ensure reproducibility.

- Quality: Model and inference are technically sound with careful derivations and justified approximations; O(K) parameterization and SVI setup are well motivated.

- Clarity: Generally readable; specific fixes requested — define “parsimonious,” reframe LDA analogy, expand power-law generator description, and minor wording fixes.

- Originality: Novel combination of O(K) triangle parameter-sharing and SVI for triangle-based latent roles; mixture-of-deltas local approximation is a practical contribution.

- Significance: High impact potential for large-scale network analysis, enabling inference with millions of nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.

- Pros for acceptance: Major scalability advance with empirical evidence, reduced parameter count without performance loss, and a fast-converging practical SVI algorithm.

- Cons for acceptance: Missing a-MMSB comparison, tightened theoretical framing/citations, vague power-law generator details, and inappropriate LDA analogy.

- Small comments: Fix “mixture-membership” → “mixed-membership,” consider removing the final sentence on p8, and cite Hoffman et al. JMLR SVI paper in the bibliography.

- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing clarifications, adding missing citations, and including comparison to a-MMSB.
