- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle-count statistics (bag-of-triangles) instead of raw edges.
- Key modeling idea: avoid parameterizing all between-community interactions by using triangle configurations to identify K roles, reducing parameters and improving scalability.
- Applies stochastic variational inference and derives global and local updates for the proposed model.
- Novelty is primarily a straightforward combination of aMMSB with MMTM, with substantial bookkeeping; presentation could be improved.
- Introduces an intuitive approximation for the local update rule that is intriguing.
- Lacks discussion of how the method extends to directed networks, despite their prevalence.
- The claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is puzzling and underjustified.
- Unclear whether the inferred latent spaces are interpretable and what they reveal about graph similarities/differences.
- The distinction between Δ^1 and Δ^2 is not clearly explained.
- It’s unclear why subsampling triangles per node (δ) yields an adequate approximation.
- If recovering a “set of non-zero roles” is the goal, a more explicit binary role formulation may be beneficial.
- Robustness of latent-space recovery to the arbitrary threshold of 0.125 is questionable.
- Which latent-space model that can produce power-law networks was used in experiments is not specified.
- The claimed PTM CGS complexity of K^3 conflicts with the O(K) parameter claim and needs justification.
- Experiments use only 10% held-out edges, raising concerns about performance when fewer edges are observed and bias in triangle preprocessing.
- It is counterintuitive that PTM outperforms MMSB on data generated by MMSB; this result needs further explanation.
- Overall: method yields a scalable O(NK) algorithm by restricting parameters and using SVI, but several claims, complexity arguments, and empirical design choices require clarification.
