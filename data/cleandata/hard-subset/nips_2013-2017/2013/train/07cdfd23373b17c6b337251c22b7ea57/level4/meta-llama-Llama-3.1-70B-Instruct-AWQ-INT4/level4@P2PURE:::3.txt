The authors present a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks, developing a powerful subclass of the model that shares parameters in a clever way. They utilize stochastic variational inference to efficiently infer the posterior distribution in very large networks. The paper is well-written and well-executed, making it acceptable for publication.

On page 1, the term "parsimonious" modeling may benefit from a brief explanation, as it may not be a commonly used expression in the context of probabilistic models. Adding a sentence to clarify this term would enhance the paper's accessibility.

Also on page 1, the statement regarding down-sampling zeros compromising accuracy appears to be misleading. According to Gopalan et al., down-sampling zeros does not seem to affect accuracy.

On page 4, while the triangle model may be pragmatic, the comparison to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) is not entirely fair. Unlike LDA, which generates documents through its generative process, the triangle model can produce objects that do not resemble networks. Although this does not detract from the model's practicality, the LDA analogy is not an apt defense.

On page 5, the authors should cite Hoffman et al. in conjunction with Amari and Sato, as Sato's algorithm does not apply in the scenario with "local variables" (as described by Hoffman). Hoffman's work provides the necessary theoretical foundation.

On page 6, the method used to generate power-law networks in the latent space is unclear and warrants further explanation. Additionally, since the authors cite Gopalan et al. as a means to speed up Mixed Membership Stochastic Blockmodels (MMSB), they should compare their algorithm to Gopalan et al.'s approach, either in addition to or instead of the MMSB batch algorithm.

Minor corrections include changing "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership" on page 2. On page 8, consider removing the last sentence, as the second-to-last sentence provides a stronger conclusion. In the bibliography, the authors should cite the Journal of Machine Learning Research (JMLR) version of "Stochastic Variational Inference".
