- Paper presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for a triangle-based network model and is recommended for acceptance.
- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing.
- Uses stochastic variational / stochastic natural gradient ascent to enable inference on very large networks (≥1M nodes) on a single machine.
- Experimental results show substantial speedups over existing probabilistic methods (MMSB, MMTM) with competitive or improved accuracy.
- Demonstrates convergence analysis and a large-scale experiment on a 1M-node YouTube network, highlighting practical scalability.
- Writing and technical execution are strong; overall approach is well-motivated within the three principles: succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, scalable inference.
- Request to clarify the term “parsimonious” modeling with a brief definition for readers unfamiliar with the term.
- Point out a misleading claim about Gopalan et al.: down-sampling zeros does not necessarily compromise accuracy—revise that sentence.
- Argues the LDA analogy to justify the generative process is weak, since PTM can produce objects that are not valid networks whereas LDA outputs valid documents.
- Recommend citing Hoffman et al. (JMLR SVI) alongside Amari and Sato because Hoffman et al. cover the theory for models with local variables.
- Ask for details on how power-law networks were generated in the latent space; the current description is vague.
- Suggest comparing against Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB algorithm (or in addition to MMSB batch) in experiments.
- Small editorial suggestions: replace “mixture-membership” with “mixed-membership,” remove the last sentence on p8, and cite the JMLR version of SVI in the bibliography.
- Arguments for acceptance: scalability to >1M nodes, large efficiency gains, competitive accuracy, technical soundness, and novel parameter-sharing strategy.
- Arguments against acceptance / minor revisions: need clarifications on “parsimonious,” interpretation of Gopalan et al., LDA analogy, power-law generation details, and an experimental comparison with Gopalan et al.
