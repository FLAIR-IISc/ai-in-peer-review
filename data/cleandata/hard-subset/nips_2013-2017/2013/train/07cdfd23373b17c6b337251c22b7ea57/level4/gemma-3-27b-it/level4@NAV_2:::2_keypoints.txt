- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation, parameter sharing, and stochastic variational inference for scalable latent space inference.  
- PTM reduces parameter complexity to O(K) and the inference algorithm has O(NK) complexity, enabling analysis of networks with >1M nodes and hundreds of roles.  
- Combines strengths of a-MMSB and MMTM to achieve both scalability and accuracy by shrinking parameter space and enabling efficient inference.  
- Empirical results show significant speedups over state-of-the-art methods on large networks while maintaining competitive or improved accuracy.  
- Technical contributions include the PTM parameterization, a stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm, and approximations for computational efficiency.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world networks provide strong evidence of effectiveness and scalability.  
- Clear problem formulation and technical soundness are strengths of the paper.  
- Detailed algorithmic exposition and discussion of computational approximations are commendable.  
- Need clearer connection between PTM and Exponential Random Graph Models (ERGMs).  
- Require deeper discussion of interpretability and concrete meaning of recovered latent roles.  
- Choice and impact of the δ-subsampling parameter need more justification and analysis.  
- Impact of the 0.125 threshold on latent space recovery accuracy should be investigated.  
- The specific power-law model used in synthetic experiments should be explicitly stated.  
- Address apparent discrepancy in reported complexity of the PTM Gibbs sampler.  
- Explain why PTM performs strongly compared to MMSB on data generated by MMSB.  
- Paper does not explore extension of the method to directed networks and should discuss potential adaptations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: improved scalability, technical soundness, strong empirical validation, and novelty of combining bag-of-triangles, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need for clarifications (ERGMs, interpretability), stronger justification for parameter choices, and lack of directed-network extension.
