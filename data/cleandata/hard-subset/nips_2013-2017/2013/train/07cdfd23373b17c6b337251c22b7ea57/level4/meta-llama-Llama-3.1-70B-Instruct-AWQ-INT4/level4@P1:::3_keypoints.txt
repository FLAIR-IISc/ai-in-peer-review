- Authors introduce a powerful model subclass with clever parameter sharing.
- Uses stochastic variational inference to scale posterior inference to very large networks.
- Paper is well written, well executed, and overall acceptable.

- Cite Hoffman et al. (in addition to Amari and Sato) for SVI theory involving local variables.
- Explain in detail how power-law networks were generated in the latent space.
- Compare the algorithm to Gopalan et al.'s scalable a-MMSB algorithm (not just MMSB batch).

- Minor edit: remove the paper's last sentence to strengthen the conclusion.
- Bibliography should cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference".

- Clarity: clearly written and reproducible for expert readers.
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques with clear distinctions from prior work.
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem and improves over prior research with unique contributions.
