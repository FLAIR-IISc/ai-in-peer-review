- Proposes PTM + stochastic variational inference representing networks as bags of triangles, with parameter sharing reducing complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) and SVI giving O(NK) runtime.  
- Enables analysis of networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of roles on a single machine in hours, outperforming existing methods.  
- Strong technical contribution addressing a real scalability challenge in network analysis.  
- Parameter-sharing scheme is elegant and reduces model dimensionality without sacrificing accuracy.  
- Experiments show orders-of-magnitude speedups while maintaining or improving accuracy on latent recovery and link prediction.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Clarify the meaning of “parsimonious” in the introduction.  
- Fix misleading claim about downsampling zeros; acknowledge Gopalan et al. (2013) showing downsampling does not compromise accuracy.  
- Remove or revise the defense-by-comparison to LDA since PTM can generate triangle configurations that may not correspond to valid networks.  
- Cite Hoffman et al. (2013) for the stochastic variational inference framework, as Sato’s algorithm is not appropriate for the described local variables.  
- Specify how power-law networks were generated in Section 5.1 for clarity.  
- Compare directly to Gopalan et al.’s a-MMSB algorithm instead of only the original MMSB batch method.  
- Minor wording/bibliography fixes: “mixture-membership” → “mixed-membership”; consider removing the final sentence on p.8; cite the JMLR version of Hoffman et al. (2013).  
- Overall recommendation: with these minor revisions, the paper is an excellent fit for NIPS.
