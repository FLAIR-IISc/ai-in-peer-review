- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle count statistics (bag-of-words analogue) to model social networks.
- Main modeling advance: restrict parameterization to triangle configurations inspired by aMMSB to identify K roles, reducing parameters and improving scalability.
- Employs stochastic variational inference with derived global and local updates for the proposed model.
- Novelty is mainly a straightforward combination of aMMSB and MMTM, requiring tedious bookkeeping and clearer presentation.
- Provides an intuitive and useful approximation for the local update rule—one of the notable contributions.
- Strength: achieves scalable inference (claimed O(NK) algorithm) suitable for large-scale networks.
- Claim that PTM is a latent-space counterpart to ERGMs is unclear and potentially misleading.
- Unclear whether the inferred latent space is interpretable or what insights it yields about graph similarities/differences.
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.
- Unclear why subsampling triangles per node (using delta) is an adequate approximation.
- Paper lacks discussion on extending the method to directed networks.
- If goal is recovering a “set of non-zero roles,” model might need a more binary notion of roles.
- Experimental choices (e.g., threshold = 0.125) seem arbitrary and robustness to this choice is not assessed.
- The latent space model used in experiments is not specified, leaving ambiguity about results (e.g., power-law behavior).
- Complexity claim that CGS is O(K^3) contradicts earlier argument of O(K) parameters and needs clarification.
- Concern about performance when many edges are missing—experiments only hold out 10% of edges and preprocessing may bias triangle distribution.
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforming aMMSB on synthetic data generated by aMMSB requires further explanation or investigation.
- Overall: method is scalable and promising but several modeling, experimental, and clarity issues must be addressed to strengthen the work.
