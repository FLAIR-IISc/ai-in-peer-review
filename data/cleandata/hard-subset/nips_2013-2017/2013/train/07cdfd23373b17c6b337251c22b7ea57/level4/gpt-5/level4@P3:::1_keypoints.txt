- Summary: Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via role-tying, with structured SVI and an O(K) local approximation, achieving large-scale, fast inference and competitive accuracy.

Strengths
- Clear modeling idea: principled parameter sharing across triangle role-configurations reducing complexity analogously to a-MMSB.
- Scalable inference: structured mean-field, natural-gradient SVI, motif minibatching, and an O(K) local-update approximation with sensible implementation choices.
- Strong empirical scaling: converges in few passes and runs on million-node graphs with ~100 roles on a single machine.
- Solid performance: competitive or better link prediction and latent role recovery on synthetic and real data.
- Well-written and organized, with an easy-to-follow algorithmic pipeline.

Weaknesses / Questions (Quality)
- Benefit of large K not convincingly demonstrated; paper lacks accuracy vs K comparisons showing that larger K improves results.
- Model selection for K is unclear; dataset K choices and selection protocol are not specified, raising fairness concerns.
- Impact of heavy parameter tying underexplored; lack of stress tests where true interactions violate tying assumptions.
- Approximation analysis limited:
  - No sensitivity study for δ-triangle subsampling (runtime vs accuracy tradeoffs).
  - No controlled comparison to a-MMSB/MMSB downsampling that equalizes discarded information.
  - No sensitivity analysis for the O(K) mixture-of-deltas local update (number/type of off-diagonals sampled, scaling with K).

Clarity
- Generally clear but needs details on how K was set/tuned, construction and resampling of candidate set A, and practical guidance on choosing δ relative to degree distributions.

Originality
- Conceptually incremental but nontrivial: adapts parameter-tying to triangle motifs and combines with structured SVI and O(K) approximations for practical scalability.

Significance
- Potentially valuable for large-K latent role inference if tying assumptions hold, but requires characterization of when assumptions are appropriate.

Pros for acceptance
- Elegant O(K^3)→O(K) reduction with clear intuition and implementation.
- Demonstrated orders-of-magnitude speedups and ability to handle large graphs and many roles.
- Competitive empirical performance and clear exposition enabling reproducibility.

Cons for acceptance
- Insufficient analysis of when parameter-sharing fails and how performance degrades.
- No systematic evaluation of performance as a function of K or clear model-selection protocol.
- Missing sensitivity studies for δ and O(K) local approximation, and potential unfairness in information-discard comparisons.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add accuracy vs K curves for PTM and baselines and report K-selection procedure (e.g., held-out likelihood or validation AUC).
- On synthetic data, vary the true triangle-generating tensor to include both tying-favorable and tying-adverse cases.
- Provide δ-sensitivity analyses (runtime vs accuracy) on synthetic and representative real networks with guidance for choosing δ.
- Evaluate O(K) local approximation by varying numbers/structure of sampled off-diagonals (e.g., 2K, 4K, 10K, 100K) and test larger K.
- Clarify probabilistic interpretation of PTM as a motif-based (composite/pseudo) likelihood and discuss implications for tasks like link prediction.
- Ensure comparisons control for the amount and type of information subsampled/ignored across methods.

Overall assessment
- A well-written, practically impactful paper that makes scalable latent-role inference more practical, but needs additional analyses on K selection, sensitivity to δ and local approximations, and stress tests against its tying assumptions.
