This paper presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. The authors introduce a powerful subclass of this model that utilizes an effective parameter sharing scheme, enabling them to apply stochastic variational inference to analyze very large networks. The paper is well-written and the work is well-executed. I find it to be a solid contribution and recommend its acceptance.

However, I have several suggestions for improvement that the authors should address.

**Major Comments**

1.  **On "Parsimonious" Modeling (p. 1):** The authors introduce the concept of "parsimonious modeling," which is central to their motivation. As this is not a standard term in the literature on probabilistic models, I suggest the authors add a sentence or two to clarify its specific meaning in this context.

2.  **On the Characterization of Prior Work (p. 1):** The characterization of the work by Gopalan et al. could be misleading. The text implies that down-sampling zero-valued entries necessarily compromises accuracy. My understanding of that work is that accuracy is preserved. The authors should revise this statement to more accurately represent the prior art.

3.  **On the Analogy to LDA (p. 4):** The authors defend the generative properties of the triangle model by drawing an analogy to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA). This comparison is not entirely appropriate. While the generative process for LDA always produces a valid document (i.e., a collection of words), the generative process for the proposed model can produce objects that do not correspond to valid networks. Although this does not diminish the model's practical utility, the defense via an analogy to LDA is unconvincing.

4.  **On Missing Citations (p. 5):** The discussion of natural gradients should also cite Hoffman et al. (2013) alongside Amari and Sato. The theory developed by Sato does not directly apply to models with local variables (in the parlance of Hoffman et al.); the necessary theoretical justification for applying stochastic variational inference in this setting is provided in the Hoffman et al. paper.

5.  **On Experimental Details (p. 6):** The paper is vague on the procedure used to generate power-law networks in the latent space for the experiments. Could the authors please provide more detail on this process?

6.  **On Experimental Comparisons (p. 6):** Given that the paper cites Gopalan et al. as a method for speeding up MMSB, the experimental evaluation would be significantly strengthened by a direct comparison to their algorithm. This would be a more relevant baseline than, or a valuable addition to, the current comparison with batch MMSB.

**Minor Comments**

*   **p. 2:** "mixture-membership" should be "mixed-membership".
*   **p. 8:** The final sentence of the conclusion is weak. The paper would have a stronger ending if it concluded with the second-to-last sentence.
*   **Bibliography:** Please update the citation for "Stochastic Variational Inference" to the JMLR version.
