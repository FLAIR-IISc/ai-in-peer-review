- Paper proposes a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" and introduces a novel parameter-sharing subclass using stochastic variational inference for large networks.  
- Overall well-structured and an acceptable contribution.

- Clarify the term "parsimonious" modeling with a brief explanation for readers unfamiliar with the concept.  
- Revise the introduction’s claim about down-sampling zeros: Gopalan et al. suggest down-sampling zeros does not hurt accuracy, so the current statement may be misleading.

- Reconsider the analogy to LDA: the triangle model can generate non-network objects, making the LDA comparison inaccurate.  
- Cite Hoffman et al. in addition to Amari and Sato to cover theory for scenarios involving local variables not addressed by Sato’s algorithm.

- Provide a clearer description of how power-law networks are generated in the latent space.  
- Add a comparison with Gopalan et al.’s algorithm (an MMSB acceleration) in addition to the MMSB batch baseline.

- Minor: replace "mixture-membership" with "mixed-membership" for consistency.  
- Minor: remove the final sentence on p8, as the previous sentence suffices.  
- Bibliography: cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."
