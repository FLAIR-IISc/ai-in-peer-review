- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing MMTM parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) for faster inference.
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm and extra approximations to improve PTM scalability.
- Synthetic experiments claim parameter reduction can increase statistical power.
- Real-world experiments show PTM is competitive with existing methods on accuracy.
- Reviewer questions practical benefit of scaling in K and requests experiments comparing PTM (large K) vs MMTM (small K).
- Paper does not clearly state how K was chosen for real-data experiments; potential model-selection bias.
- Lack of experiments varying true B matrices — need tests where B both matches and violates PTM assumptions to reveal failure modes.
- Insufficient analysis of the impact of approximation/tricks introduced for scalability.
- Missing ablation on threshold δ: how different δ values trade off scalability and accuracy.
- Unclear why PTM avoids downsampling accuracy issues alleged for a-MMSB and why ignoring non-edges is acceptable here.
- Need evaluation of the O(K) local-update approximation: performance when sampling 2K, 4K, 100K triples and behavior as K grows.
- Reviewer doubts experimental setup fairness to competitors and overall underreporting of PTM weaknesses.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental but the parameter-constraining scalability direction is interesting/original.
- If PTM assumptions hold, the approach could be significant for scaling latent-space models to large networks.
- Title is overly broad given the paper targets a specific scalable model (PTM).
- Raises conceptual concern that PTM/MMTM may not be true generative/probabilistic models (bag-of-triples issue) and questions what PTM probabilities represent.
- Side-effects of modeling assumptions and inference tricks are inadequately investigated.
