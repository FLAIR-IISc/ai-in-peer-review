- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) modeling networks as bags of triangle motifs with parameter tying reducing triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Introduces a structured mean-field SVI algorithm with natural gradients and triangle mini-batching, using a “mixture-of-deltas” variational family for O(NK) global and O(K) local updates.
- Empirically competitive or improved latent role recovery and link prediction on synthetic and large real networks, with runtimes orders of magnitude faster than MMSB VI and MMTM Gibbs.
- Scales to >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single multi-core machine.
- Clear motivation for moving from edges to triangle motifs and good discussion of triadic closure and motif statistics.
- Elegant parameter sharing that preserves useful structure while dramatically reducing parameter size.
- Well-designed structured SVI with practical memory-saving by discarding local variational parameters between iterations.
- Strong empirical results demonstrating both accuracy and dramatic speedups; exposition and algorithmic choices are well justified.
- Missing experimental comparison to the closest scalable MMSB baseline (a-MMSB), weakening claims about relative scalability and accuracy.
- The LDA analogy defending non-generative modeling of graphs is inappropriate and should be removed or reframed.
- Key approximation (mixture-of-deltas) needs more analysis: ablations against full K^3 tensor, sensitivity to construction/resampling of candidate set A, and effects on bound/gradients.
- Synthetic power-law network generation in latent space is underspecified and needs reproducible details (priors, link functions, degree control).
- Intro statement about a-MMSB zero downsampling affecting accuracy is misleading relative to Gopalan et al.; revise or qualify.
- Some theoretical statements (unbiasedness and bound properties under mixture-of-deltas) require clearer exposition or pointers to proofs.
- Writing is generally clear, but a few terminology and citation issues (e.g., cite Hoffman et al. JMLR 2013 for SVI) should be fixed.
- Suggest concretely define “parsimonious” (e.g., O(K^3)→O(K) parameter tying) and correct “mixture-membership”→“mixed-membership.”
- Request more algorithmic details on constructing/resampling the candidate set A and diagnostics showing the approximation’s adequacy.
- Recommendation to remove or weaken the paper’s final concluding sentence.
- Pros: novel, practical scaling of triangle-based latent role models; significant empirical speedups; clean, implementable algorithm with likely practical impact.
- Cons: lacks experimental comparison to a-MMSB; some key approximations not fully analyzed/ablated; minor clarity and citation issues.
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance pending addressing comparison to a-MMSB and clarifications/analyses noted above.
