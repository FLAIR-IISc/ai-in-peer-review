- Introduces PTM: reduces MMTM parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), derives SVI with extra approximations, reports competitive accuracy.  
- Strength: O(K) parameter constraint is appealing and timely for large-scale network modeling.  
- Strength: The SVI framework and practical implementation tricks that improve scalability are interesting and potentially impactful.  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Concern: Empirical value of large-K scalability is not convincingly demonstrated; need performance vs K under fixed compute comparing PTM to MMTM/MMSB.  
- Concern: Model selection and fairness are unclear—K selection, hyperparameter search, and whether baselines used comparable approximations or computation budgets are unspecified.  
- Concern: Impact of PTM’s strong structural constraint on B is uncharacterized; need experiments varying B from structured to fully dense to identify failure modes.  
- Concern: Approximations are insufficiently analyzed—threshold δ lacks guidance and sensitivity analysis (accuracy vs δ; runtime vs δ).  
- Concern: The claim about downsampling vs ignoring events is not justified; need principled rationale or comparison explaining why ignoring non-edges is acceptable for PTM but harms MMSB.  
- Concern: O(K) local-update sampling effects are unclear; ablations varying sample size (e.g., 2K, 4K, 100K) and K regimes are needed to assess robustness.  
- Concern: Probabilistic semantics ambiguous—unclear if PTM/MMTM define a proper likelihood over graphs or a pseudo-likelihood over triple multisets; clarify target data-generating object and implications.  
- Detailed question: How were K and other hyperparameters selected for PTM and baselines (search ranges, selection criteria, matched compute)?  
- Detailed question: Provide performance vs K curves for PTM, MMTM, MMSB under matched compute (or best K achievable for baselines within same time/memory).  
- Detailed question: Report sensitivity to δ and practical guidance on choosing δ to trade speed vs accuracy.  
- Detailed question: Show how performance changes when sampling multiples of K triples for local updates and identify regimes of instability or bias.  
- Detailed question: On synthetic data, vary B from PTM-friendly to dense and report how PTM degrades relative to MMTM.  
- Detailed question: Formalize the probabilistic model (likelihood and prior) over networks vs triples and discuss any mismatch between inference objective and graph evaluation.  
- Originality: The O(K^3)→O(K) modeling move is incremental but worthwhile; scalability focus is useful.  
- Clarity: Manuscript is clear, but critical experimental and hyperparameter details are missing.  
- Significance: PTM could expand applicability of mixed-membership models if assumptions hold, but needs stronger empirical demonstrations and principled analysis.  
- Minor suggestions: Consider a more specific title; include runtime/memory vs accuracy plots for all methods; discuss adaptive/hybrid relaxations of PTM constraints.  
- Overall: Promising and well-written work that requires clearer experimental protocols, stronger ablations, and candid characterization of failure modes and probabilistic semantics.
