- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing MMTM parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) with a stochastic variational inference algorithm and extra approximations for scalability.  
- Synthetic experiments claim reduced-parameter PTM yields stronger statistical power.  
- Real-world experiments show PTM achieves competitive accuracy versus existing methods.  
- Practical advantage of scaling in K is unclear—paper doesn’t show why reducing K-related complexity matters if MMTM can be learned with large K.  
- Missing crucial comparison: PTM with large K vs MMTM with small K to demonstrate PTM’s real benefit.  
- Unclear how K was chosen for real-data experiments and competitors (possible model-selection bias).  
- Insufficient analysis of PTM’s failure modes—no tests on diverse true B matrices, especially ones with O(K^3) distinct values where PTM should fail.  
- Paper introduces approximation tricks but provides little evaluation of their effects on accuracy or robustness.  
- No analysis of threshold δ’s impact: how δ affects performance and the minimum safe δ.  
- Unclear justification for claiming a-MMSB fails due to downsampling—both PTM and MMTM ignore many triplets, so why can’t MMSB similarly ignore non-edges?  
- No evaluation of the O(K) local-update approximation: how performance degrades when sampling different numbers of triples (2K, 4K, 100K) as K grows.  
- Experimental setup may be unfairly evaluated against competitors and lacks comprehensive baseline comparisons.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-structured.  
- Modeling is somewhat incremental but original in focusing on parameter-space constraints for scalability.  
- Significance is conditional: PTM could be impactful if its simplifying assumptions hold in practice.  
- Title is overly broad—PTM targets a specific scalable latent-space model, not scalable latent-space methods generally.  
- Questionable probabilistic status: bag-of-triples analogy to LDA is flawed because PTM/MMTM generated triple-bags rarely correspond to valid networks, undermining likelihood-based interpretation.  
- Side effects of assumptions and approximations (conceptual and empirical) are not sufficiently investigated.
