- Introduces PTM to reduce MMTM’s O(K^3) parameters to O(K) for faster inference with stochastic variational methods and scalability tricks.  
- Unclear practical benefit of scaling in K: need evidence that large-K MMTM/PTM helps in real tasks.  
- Missing experiments evaluating performance of MMTM and PTM as a function of K to demonstrate PTM’s advantage.  
- Unclear how K was selected in real-data experiments; model-selection procedure may bias comparisons.  
- Simplifying assumptions are downplayed; authors must identify when PTM fails (e.g., when B truly has O(K^3) distinct entries).  
- Synthetic experiments lack a clear specification of the true B and should include both favorable and unfavorable B regimes.  
- Scaling tricks are under-analyzed: the effect of threshold δ on accuracy and scalability is not evaluated.  
- Unclear why PTM tolerates downsampling/non-edge ignoring while a-MMSB allegedly fails; need explanation.  
- Impact of the O(K) approximation to local updates is not studied; need experiments varying sampled triples (2K, 4K, 100K) and K.  
- Experimental setup may be unfair to competitors and lacks discussion of algorithmic/model weaknesses.  
- Paper is very clearly written and well organized.  
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental but constraining parameters for scalability is an interesting/original direction.  
- Potential significance is conditional: PTM could matter if its assumptions hold in practice.  
- Title is too broad; claims PTM may not be genuinely probabilistic or a valid generative model for networks, raising concerns about abandoning likelihood principles.
