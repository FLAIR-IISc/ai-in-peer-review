

The authors develop a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. They introduce a powerful subclass that shares parameters through a clever mechanism and employ stochastic variational inference to infer the posterior for very large networks. The paper is well written and well executed; I recommend acceptance.  

p1: Define "parsimonious" modeling, as this is not a standard term in probabilistic modeling contexts (though I may be mistaken). The term is central to the paper and warrants clarification.  
p1: Gopalan et al. demonstrate that zero-downsampling does not compromise accuracy; your introduction's claim is misleading.  
p4: While pragmatically sound, the triangle model's justification via LDA is inappropriate. LDA generates interpretable documents even when nonsensical, whereas the triangle model may produce non-network objects. This is acceptable for a practical model, but the LDA analogy is unsuitable.  
p5: Cite Hoffman et al. (with Amari and Sato) and clarify that Sato’s algorithm does not apply here (as "local variables" exist), per Hoffman’s theory.  
p6: Explain how power-law networks were generated in the latent space; the paper is vague on this point.  
p6: When citing Gopalan et al. as a speedup for MMSB, compare directly to their algorithm (not just MMSB batch).  

Small comments:  
p2: "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership"  
p8: Remove the final sentence; the second-to-last sentence provides a stronger conclusion.  
Bibliography: Replace "Stochastic Variational Inference" citation with the JMLR version.
