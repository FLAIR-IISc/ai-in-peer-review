- Positive assessment: presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model, a parameter-sharing subclass, and SVI for very large networks; well-written and acceptable.  
- Clarify terminology: briefly explain "parsimonious" modeling on page 1 for readers unfamiliar with the term.  
- Incorrect citation claim: the paper’s statement that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy (citing Gopalan et al.) is potentially misleading because Gopalan et al. do not support that claim.  
- Questionable analogy: comparing the triangle model’s defense to LDA is unfair, since the triangle model can generate non-network objects and the LDA analogy is not fully appropriate.  
- Missing citation/theory: on page 5, cite Hoffman et al. in addition to Amari and Sato, since Sato’s algorithm doesn’t cover models with local variables and Hoffman provides the needed theory.  
- Insufficient method detail: on page 6, clarify how power-law networks are generated in the latent space.  
- Comparative evaluation: on page 6, compare the proposed algorithm to Gopalan et al.’s MMSB speedup algorithm rather than (or in addition to) only MMSB batch.  
- Minor wording fix: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership” on page 2.  
- Minor edit suggestion: remove the last sentence on page 8 because the second-to-last sentence gives a stronger conclusion.  
- Bibliography correction: cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference.”
