

The authors develop a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. They introduce a powerful subclass of the model that shares parameters in a clever manner and employ stochastic variational inference to infer the posterior for very large networks. The paper is well written and well executed. I recommend acceptance.

p1: What is meant by "parsimonious" modeling? This term is not standard in the context of probabilistic models and may confuse readers. Please unpack this term in a sentence, as it is important to the paper's contribution.  
p1: Gopalan et al. demonstrate that downsampling zeros does not compromise accuracy. Your statement in the introduction is misleading.  
p4: While I acknowledge the triangle model's pragmatic utility, appealing to LDA as a defense is inappropriate. Running LDA's generative process yields a valid document (albeit nonsensical), whereas your model can generate non-network objects. This is not a concern for me—it is a practical model—but the LDA comparison is invalid.  
p5: Cite Hoffman et al. (with Amari and Sato) instead of Sato alone. In your scenario with "local variables" (Hoffman's terminology), Sato's algorithm does not apply; the relevant theory is in Hoffman.  
p6: The paper is vague about generating power-law networks in the latent space. Clarify this procedure.  
p6: Since you cite Gopalan et al. as a method to accelerate MMSB, compare to their algorithm (in addition to or instead of batch MMSB).  

Minor comments:  
p2: "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership"  
p8: Remove the final sentence; the second-to-last sentence provides a stronger conclusion.  
Bibliography: Cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."
