- Paper addresses scalable latent-space inference in very large networks where existing probabilistic methods fail beyond ~100k nodes.
- Builds on MMSB/a-MMSB and MMTM but overcomes their adjacency-matrix dependence and MMTM's O(K³) parameter bottleneck.
- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter complexity from O(K³) to O(K) by sharing parameters across three role-configuration groups.
- Partitioning of role space: (1) all three roles identical (Bxxx), (2) two roles identical (Bxx), (3) all roles distinct (B0).
- Enables stochastic variational inference with O(NK) complexity, allowing analysis of networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.
- Empirical results show competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction accuracy with orders-of-magnitude speedups.
- Demonstrated ability to process a 1M-node YouTube network in ~4 hours on a single 8-thread machine.
- Strength: Significant scalability advance from O(N²K²)/O(Nδ²K³) to O(NK), directly addressing a major bottleneck.
- Strength: Well-motivated, technically sound parameter-sharing strategy that retains model expressiveness.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real networks with convincing comparisons to state-of-the-art.
- Strength: Clear exposition of technical innovations, including structured mean-field approximation for higher-order dependencies.
- Weakness: Claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is confusing and poorly supported given ERGMs' broader scope.
- Weakness: Use of an NMI threshold of 0.125 appears arbitrary and lacks justification or robustness analysis.
- Weakness: Delta^1 vs Delta^2 parameter distinction is not clearly explained, causing parameterization confusion.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of performance on sparse networks or with larger edge holdouts beyond the 10% used.
- Weakness: Counterintuitive claim that PTM outperforms MMSB on MMSB-generated synthetic data needs more explanation.
- Presentation: Paper is generally well-written but would benefit from clearer, more intuitive explanations of parameter sharing and triangle equivalence classes.
- Recommendation: Strong technical contribution with practical impact and compelling empirical validation, tempered by clarity issues and unanswered questions about sparsity and some claims.
