- Paper introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) and a stochastic variational inference algorithm with further scalability approximations.  
- Experiments claim PTM can yield greater statistical power on synthetic data and competitive predictive accuracy on real-world datasets.  
- Central motivation—scalability in the number of roles K—is not sufficiently justified; no empirical demonstration that learning with large K is practically beneficial.  
- Missing crucial experiment: no comparison of PTM vs MMTM as a function of K to show PTM’s advantage when MMTM is forced to use small K.  
- Model-selection procedure for K in PTM and competitors (e.g., MMSB) on real datasets is not described, risking biased experimental comparisons.  
- Paper lacks discussion of limitations from PTM’s simplifying assumptions and scenarios where PTM would fail (e.g., when true interaction tensor B has O(K^3) distinct values).  
- Synthetic experiments omit details about the true parameter tensor B and do not test diverse B structures, including ones that violate PTM assumptions.  
- Approximations used for scalability are not analyzed thoroughly: the sensitivity and accuracy/scalability trade-off of the threshold δ is unexplored.  
- PTM’s strategy of ignoring triplets with only one edge is a form of subsampling, yet the paper does not convincingly justify why this is acceptable while downsampling is argued to harm MMSB.  
- Impact of the O(K) approximation for local variational updates is unexamined; no evaluation of how performance changes if more triples are sampled (e.g., 2K or 4K) or as K increases.  
- Overall experimental evaluation is unconvincing due to potential unfairness to competing methods and insufficient analysis of model and algorithm weaknesses.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- The modeling contribution is incremental but the idea of enforcing parameter parsimony for scalability is interesting and original in focus.  
- Potential significance is high if PTM assumptions and approximations hold in practice, as few latent-space network models scale to large datasets.  
- Title is overly broad; the work presents a scalable algorithm for a specific model (PTM) rather than a general scalable modeling approach.  
- Fundamental concern: PTM/MMLM’s “bag-of-triples” approach may not define a valid generative model for graphs, since most bags-of-triples are not realizable as networks, calling into question the probabilistic interpretation of the model.
