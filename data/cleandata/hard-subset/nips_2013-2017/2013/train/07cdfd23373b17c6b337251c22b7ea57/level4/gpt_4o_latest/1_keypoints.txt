- Proposes PTM to reduce MMTM parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) and develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with extra approximations for scalability.  
- Synthetic experiments claim that reducing variables can increase statistical power; real-world experiments show PTM is competitive in accuracy.  
- Practical benefit of scalability in K is unclear; reviewer questions how larger K helps learning in mixed-membership models.  
- Lacks experiments showing performance of PTM and MMTM as a function of K; missing evidence that PTM with large K outperforms MMTM with small K.  
- Unclear how K was chosen for PTM and comparator MMSB in real-data experiments, raising concerns about experimental bias.  
- Paper does not analyze when PTM’s simplifying constraints fail; if true B has O(K^3) distinct values, PTM may degrade.  
- Synthetic experiments omit crucial details about the true B and do not test B configurations that challenge PTM.  
- Approximation techniques are insufficiently analyzed: the impact of the threshold δ on accuracy vs scalability is not evaluated.  
- Unclear why PTM is claimed immune to downsampling accuracy loss (a-MMSB issue); need explanation why ignoring non-edges is acceptable in PTM but not MMSB.  
- No analysis of the O(K) local-update approximation: how quality changes with sampling size (2K, 4K, 100K) or as K grows remains unknown.  
- Overall experimental setup does not convincingly ensure fairness to competing methods and understates algorithmic/model weaknesses.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized; clarity is a strength.  
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental but the scalability-by-constraint idea is a novel and promising direction.  
- PTM could be significant if its simplifying assumptions hold in practice, addressing scalability challenges for latent-space network models.  
- Title is overly broad relative to the specific scalable PTM contribution.  
- Probabilistic validity is questioned: bag-of-triples assumption may not correspond to real networks, so PTM/M M T M may not be truly generative and the meaning of probability is unclear.  
- Conclusion: proposed assumptions and inference techniques enable scalability, but their side effects and limitations are insufficiently explored.
