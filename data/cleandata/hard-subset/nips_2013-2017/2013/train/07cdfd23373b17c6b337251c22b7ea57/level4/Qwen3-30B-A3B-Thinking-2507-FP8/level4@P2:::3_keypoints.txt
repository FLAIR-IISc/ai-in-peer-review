- Presents a scalable latent space inference method (Parsimonious Triangular Model, PTM) that reduces parameters from O(K³) to O(K) via parameter sharing.  
- Enables efficient stochastic variational inference with O(NK) complexity, handling networks with >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine in hours.  
- Builds on triangular motif representation (Ho et al. 2013) to provide a succinct network representation.  
- Simultaneously addresses three core challenges: succinct representation, parsimonious statistical modeling, and scalable inference.  
- Demonstrates significant practical advancement over MMSB and MMTM by achieving scalability without sacrificing accuracy.  
- Technical quality: provides a thorough theoretical foundation and rigorous derivation of the stochastic variational inference algorithm.  
- Experimental validation: strong empirical results on synthetic and real-world networks, showing competitive or improved accuracy and orders-of-magnitude speedups.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written with clear explanations and well-structured arguments.  
- Originality: introduces a novel parameter-sharing strategy that changes the model’s scalability properties.  
- Minor weakness: the term “parsimonious modeling” is introduced without a brief definition for readers unfamiliar with the term.  
- Minor weakness: the claim about down-sampling zeros not compromising accuracy needs clarification relative to Gopalan et al. (2013).  
- Minor weakness: comparison to LDA for defending generative capabilities is misleading given differing goals (latent inference vs. generation).  
- Minor weakness: synthetic experiment details (how power-law networks were generated) are vague and need a brief technical description for reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: accept at NIPS, with minor revisions to clarify the down-sampling point and define “parsimonious modeling.”
