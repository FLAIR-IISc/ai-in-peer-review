This paper introduces a novel technique for uncovering latent structure in social network data by leveraging triangle count statistics, analogous to bag-of-words representations in text documents. The proposed model, motivated by the aMMSB, parameterizes a set of K roles based on triangle configurations, resulting in a more scalable implementation with fewer parameters. The authors apply recent advances in stochastic variational inference, deriving global and local updates for the model.

The primary modeling contribution is a combination of aMMSB and MMTM, although the presentation could be improved. While most of the learning algorithm is a direct application of stochastic variational inference, the authors provide an intuitive approximation for the local update rule.

A detailed discussion on extending these methods to directed networks would be beneficial, given their prevalence. Several concerns remain:

- The authors' claim that the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is a latent space counterpart to ERGMs is unclear, as ERGMs can include varied statistics beyond triangles, and the PTM's classification as a "latent space counterpart" is ambiguous.
- The interpretability of the inferred latent space and the insights gained about graph similarities and differences are not addressed.
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is unclear, and the subsampling of triangles for each node using delta lacks a clear justification.
- The model's goal of recovering non-zero roles could be improved with a binary notion of "role."
- The robustness of latent space recovery results to the threshold of 0.125 is uncertain, and the choice of latent space model used in experiments is not specified.
- The PTM CGS complexity is stated as O(K^3), contradicting the earlier claim of O(K) parameters.
- The performance of the method with fewer observed edges is a concern, as the experiments only hold out 10% of the edges.
- The method's superiority over MMSB on a synthetic MMSB-generated network is counterintuitive.

This work utilizes stochastic variational inference to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel with a restricted set of parameters on triangle counts in a graph, allowing for a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
