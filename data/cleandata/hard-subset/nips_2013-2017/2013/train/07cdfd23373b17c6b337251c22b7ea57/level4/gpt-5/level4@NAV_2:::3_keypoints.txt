- Contribution and verdict: Proposes a scalable approximate-inference framework for triangle-based network models with parameter sharing and SVI; paper is well-written and recommended for acceptance.

- Clarify terminology: Define what is meant by “parsimonious” modeling with an operational explanation.

- Correct misleading claim: Revise the intro sentence implying down-sampling zeros degrades accuracy — Gopalan et al. suggest it does not.

- Reconsider analogy to LDA: The LDA comparison is inappropriate since samples from your model can yield invalid networks, unlike documents from LDA.

- SVI theory citation: Cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato — Sato’s algorithm doesn’t cover models with local variables; Hoffman et al. provide the relevant theory.

- Experimental detail: Fully specify the procedure used to generate power-law networks in latent space.

- Baseline comparison: Add a comparison to Gopalan et al.’s scalable MMSB method rather than (or in addition to) only the MMSB batch baseline.

- Minor edits: Change “mixture-membership” → “mixed-membership”; consider removing the paper’s final sentence; cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference” in the bibliography.

- Strengths recap: Principled subclass, effective parameter sharing, scalable SVI algorithm, and compelling empirical results on very large graphs.
