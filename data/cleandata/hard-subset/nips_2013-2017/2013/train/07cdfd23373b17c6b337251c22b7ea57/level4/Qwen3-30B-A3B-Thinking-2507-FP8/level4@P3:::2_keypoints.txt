- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation to enable scalable latent space inference in large networks.  
- PTM reduces parameter complexity from O(K³) (MMTM) to O(K) via strategic parameter sharing across latent role configurations.  
- Enables a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity, scaling to networks with >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.  
- Builds on prior work (a-MMSB and MMTM), addressing their scalability and representation limitations.  

Strengths:
- Clear technical contribution addressing a major scalability bottleneck in network analysis.  
- O(K) parameterization is well-motivated and substantially lowers computational cost versus prior models.  
- Strong empirical performance: near-perfect latent role recovery on synthetic data (NMI > 0.95) across sizes and role counts.  
- Orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over baselines (e.g., converges in 12 minutes on Brightkite vs. days for prior methods).  
- Thorough comparisons across metrics (latent recovery, link prediction, likelihood bounds) and rapid SVI convergence (2–5 passes).  
- Technically sound model design with comprehensive empirical validation and practical implementation.  

Weaknesses:
- Distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 parameter spaces is unclear, hindering understanding of parameter sharing.  
- Justification for triangle subsampling (δ-subsampling) lacks theoretical guarantees or empirical validation.  
- Role-recovery binarization threshold (0.125) appears arbitrary; no sensitivity analysis provided.  
- Claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is misleading and should be rephrased.  
- No discussion of extending the model to directed networks, limiting applicability to common social-network settings.  
- Counterintuitive result where PTM outperforms MMSB on MMSB-generated data needs further explanation.  

Originality and significance:
- Original combination of triangle motifs with parsimonious parameterization, building on existing models but contributing a novel scalability strategy.  
- High significance: enables practical latent-space inference on networks orders of magnitude larger than previously feasible, validated on real-world data.  

Clarity:
- Exposition needs improvement: clarify parameter-sharing, triangle equivalence classes, and provide intuitive examples/visualizations.  
- Make the derivation of the local update approximation more accessible and rephrase the ERGM comparison.  

Recommendation:
- Accept — valuable and well-executed contribution with presentation issues that can be resolved in revision.  

Pro-acceptance arguments:
- Orders-of-magnitude faster inference on very large networks (1M+ nodes).  
- Competitive or improved accuracy on latent recovery and link prediction.  
- Rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Practical implementation enabling real-world analysis.  
- Clear theoretical justification for parameter reduction.  

Con-acceptance arguments:
- Key model details (Delta^1 vs Delta^2) require clarification.  
- Triangle subsampling justification is insufficient.  
- Arbitrary threshold choice for role recovery lacks validation.  
- Missing discussion on directed-network extensions.  
- Counterintuitive synthetic results need deeper analysis.
