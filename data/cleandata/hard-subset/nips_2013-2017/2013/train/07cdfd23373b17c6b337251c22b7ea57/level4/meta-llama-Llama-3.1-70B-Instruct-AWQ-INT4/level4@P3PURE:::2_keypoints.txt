- Paper proposes learning latent social-network structure by fitting a model to triangle-count “bag-of-words” preprocessing of graphs.  
- Main modeling idea: use aMMSB-style triangle configurations to identify K roles instead of parameterizing all between-community interactions, yielding fewer parameters and better scalability.  
- Authors apply stochastic variational inference, deriving global and local updates for the proposed model.  
- The core model is essentially a combination of aMMSB and the Mixed Membership Matrix Triangulation Model (MMTM), with somewhat tedious bookkeeping.  
- Presentation and clarity of the modeling derivation could be improved.  
- Most of the learning algorithm is a standard application of SVI, but the paper offers an intuitive approximate local update that is interesting.  
- The paper should discuss how the method would extend to directed networks.  
- Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart” to ERGMs is questionable and not well-justified.  
- It is unclear whether the inferred latent space is interpretable and what insights it provides about graph similarities/differences.  
- The difference between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.  
- Justification for subsampling triangles per node (using delta) as an adequate approximation is lacking; a more binary notion of “role” may be needed if recovering non-zero roles is the goal.  
- Robustness to the arbitrary threshold of 0.125 is not evaluated.  
- The specific latent space model used in experiments is not specified, despite many models producing power-law networks.  
- The claim that PTM with collapsed Gibbs sampling has K^3 complexity conflicts with earlier claims of O(K) parameters.  
- Concern about performance when many edges are missing; experiments only hold out 10% of edges, which may bias triangle distributions.  
- It is counterintuitive that the method outperforms MMSB on data generated by MMSB; method achieves a scalable O(NK) algorithm by learning mixed-membership on triangle counts.
