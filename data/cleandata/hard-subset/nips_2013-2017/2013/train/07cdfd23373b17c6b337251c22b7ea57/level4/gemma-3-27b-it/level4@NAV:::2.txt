This paper introduces a novel technique for learning underlying structure within social network data by modeling triangle count statistics – effectively a “bag-of-triangles” approach. The central contribution builds upon aMMSB by focusing on triangle configurations to define *K* roles, rather than modeling all pairwise interactions between “communities.” This targeted parameterization improves scalability. The authors leverage recent advancements in stochastic variational inference, developing both global and local update rules for their model.

The approach represents a sensible combination of aMMSB and the MMTM, although the presentation of the method would benefit from greater clarity. While the majority of the inference procedure relies on standard stochastic variational inference techniques, the proposed local update approximation is both intuitive and promising.

Future work could benefit from a discussion of extending the methods to directed networks. 

Several points warrant further consideration:

- The claim that the “Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs” feels overstated, as ERGMs can incorporate a broader range of statistics than just triangles, and the notion of a “latent space counterpart” seems broadly applicable to many latent network models.
- The interpretability of the inferred latent space remains unclear. Further exploration of what these latent representations reveal about the similarities and differences between networks is needed.
- The distinction between Delta<sup>1</sup> and Delta<sup>2</sup> is not readily apparent.
- The justification for approximating triangle counts via subsampling (using delta) requires further explanation.
- Given the goal of identifying a “set of non-zero roles,” a more explicit binary representation of roles within the model might be considered.
- The sensitivity of latent space recovery to the threshold value of 0.125 – which appears somewhat arbitrary – should be investigated.
- The paper would benefit from specifying which latent space model was used to generate the power-law networks in the experimental evaluation.
- The reported O(K<sup>3</sup>) complexity of PTM CGS seems inconsistent with the earlier argument of O(K) parameters.
- The performance of the method with sparser graphs (i.e., with fewer observed edges) is a concern, potentially exacerbated by the biased triangle distribution resulting from extensive edge removal during preprocessing – evidenced by the modest 10% holdout in the experiments.
- The finding that the method outperforms MMSB on a synthetic network generated *using* MMSB (Figure 1, left four panels) is unexpected and deserves further investigation.

In summary, this work presents a scalable approach to mixed-membership blockmodeling using stochastic variational inference on triangle counts, achieving an O(NK) computational complexity through carefully considered modeling choices and approximations.
