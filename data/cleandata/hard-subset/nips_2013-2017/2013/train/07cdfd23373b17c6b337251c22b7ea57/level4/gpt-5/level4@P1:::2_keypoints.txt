- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that models triangle counts instead of edges and represents networks as a bag-of-triangles.  
- PTM reduces triangle-generation parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) by sharing parameters across equivalence classes of role configurations.  
- Develops an SVI algorithm with structured mean-field updates and an O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” approximation for local updates, yielding overall O(NK) complexity.  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability to million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine.  
- Shows competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link-prediction performance versus MMSB, a-MMSB, and MMTM.  
- Situates work at intersection of MMSB/a-MMSB, MMTM triangle motifs, and SVI; main idea adapts a-MMSB-style parameter sharing to triangle-based generative models.  
- Strength: clear motivation for using triangles to reduce data size while retaining informative higher-order structure.  
- Strength: parsimonious parameterization plausibly improves scalability and statistical efficiency versus full O(K^3) tensors.  
- Strength: structured mean-field family captures dependencies within triangles and the O(K) local-update approximation is intuitive and practical.  
- Strength: fast convergence and large wall-clock improvements over prior methods.  
- Weakness: Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is confusing and needs clearer phrasing and justification.  
- Weakness: Interpretability of inferred roles/latent positions is unclear and should be discussed.  
- Weakness: Notation and distinction between 1-simplex and 2-simplex parameterizations (B0, Bxx, Bxxx) are not clearly explained.  
- Weakness: δ-subsampling of triangles is used without sufficient justification of bias/variance trade-offs or conditions when it is adequate.  
- Weakness: Role-recovery evaluation lacks a sparse/binary prior; current approach depends on a post hoc threshold.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to the chosen threshold (0.125) is not analyzed and appears arbitrary.  
- Weakness: Experimental details missing about the power-law simulator used for synthetic data.  
- Weakness: Why PTM collapsed Gibbs sampler has O(K^3) complexity despite O(K) parameters is not reconciled.  
- Weakness: Robustness to missing or noisy edges (beyond 10% holdout) is not evaluated.  
- Weakness: Surprising result that PTM outperforms MMSB on MMSB-generated data needs additional explanation.  
- Weakness: No discussion or treatment of directed networks and extension to directed motifs.  
- Weakness: Presentation could be streamlined; bookkeeping obscures main insights.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical scalability, principled parameter sharing reducing complexity, effective local-update approximation, and leveraging informative triangle motifs.  
- Cons for acceptance: clarity issues, limited sensitivity/robustness analysis, surprising unexplained results, and no directed-network treatment.  
- Overall: Natural combination of existing ideas with thoughtful engineering and approximations producing substantial scalability gains and competitive accuracy, but needs clarifications and additional analyses.
