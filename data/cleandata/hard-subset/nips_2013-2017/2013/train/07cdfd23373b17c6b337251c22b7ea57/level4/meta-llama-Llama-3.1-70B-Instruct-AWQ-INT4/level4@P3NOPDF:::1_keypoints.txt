- Proposes PTM to reduce MMTM’s O(K^3) parameters to O(K) and develops scalable stochastic variational inference with additional approximations.  
- Experiments show PTM can increase statistical power on synthetic data and is competitive on real data.  
- Key practical advantage of scalability in K is questionable and not empirically demonstrated.  
- Missing experiments comparing PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K to show whether large-K PTM outperforms small-K MMTM.  
- Unclear how K was chosen for real-data experiments and model-selection procedures may bias comparisons with MMSB.  
- Authors do not analyze when PTM’s simplifying constraints will fail; PTM should perform poorly if true B has O(K^3) distinct values.  
- Synthetic data setup (choice of true B) is not clearly described and should include B matrices that both favor and disfavor PTM.  
- The scalability tricks and approximations are poorly described and their empirical impact (e.g., threshold δ) is not analyzed.  
- Unclear how choice of threshold δ affects real/synthetic performance and how far δ can be reduced without harming accuracy.  
- Claim that a-MMSB fails due to downsampling is unconvincing; authors do not explain why PTM would avoid the same issues.  
- Both MMTM and PTM ignore triplets with one edge, so it’s unclear why MMSB cannot ignore non-edges while PTM can.  
- Impact of the O(K) approximation to local updates is unclear and should be evaluated as K increases.  
- Reviewer is not convinced the experimental setup was fair to competitors and criticizes lack of discussion on algorithmic/model weaknesses.  
- Paper is well-written and organized.  
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental but the constrained-parameter approach to scalability is interesting and original.  
- Potentially significant if assumptions hold in practice, since many latent-space network models do not scale.  
- Title is too broad; paper targets a specific scalable algorithm for PTM rather than all scalable latent-space methods.  
- Concern that PTM may not be a truly probabilistic/generative model for networks (bag-of-triples issue) and may place posterior mass on “non-networks,” raising interpretability concerns.
