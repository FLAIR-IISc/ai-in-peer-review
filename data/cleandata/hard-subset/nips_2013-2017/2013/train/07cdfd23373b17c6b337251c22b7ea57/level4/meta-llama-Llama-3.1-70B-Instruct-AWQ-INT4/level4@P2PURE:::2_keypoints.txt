- Proposes learning latent structure from triangle-count “bag-of-words” preprocessing on social networks.
- Models triangle configurations to identify K roles, inspired by the Affiliation MMSB, reducing between-community parameters.
- Claims a more scalable implementation with O(NK) time complexity by restricting parameters to triangle roles.
- Applies stochastic variational inference, deriving global and local updates and an intuitive approximation for local updates.
- Novel contribution is combining aMMSB with the Mixed Membership Triangular Model (MMTM), though presentation requires tedious bookkeeping.
- Paper largely applies known SVI techniques; only the local update approximation is notably novel and intriguing.
- Lacks discussion of extensions to directed networks despite prevalence of directed data.
- The claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart” to ERGMs is unclear and potentially misleading given ERGMs’ broader statistics.
- Unclear interpretability of the inferred latent space and what insights it provides across graphs.
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.
- No clear justification for subsampling triangles per node using delta as an adequate approximation.
- If goal is identifying non-zero roles, suggests a binary notion of “role” might be more appropriate than current formulation.
- Unclear robustness of results to the arbitrary-looking threshold of 0.125.
- Experiments do not specify which latent space model generated the power-law networks.
- The claim that PTM with collapsed Gibbs sampling has K^3 complexity conflicts with the argument of O(K) parameters.
- Concern about performance when fewer edges are observed—holding out only 10% may hide bias introduced by preprocessing.
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforming MMSB on data generated by MMSB (Figure 1) raises questions about experimental validity.
