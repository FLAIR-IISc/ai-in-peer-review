This paper introduces a method for discovering latent structure in social network data. The model is trained on triangle-count statistics produced in a preprocessing stage, akin to a bag-of-words representation for text. The central modeling idea, inspired by aMMSB, is to consider triangle configurations to define a set of K roles to parameterize rather than specifying all between-“community” interactions. This reduces the number of parameters and thus enables a more scalable implementation. The authors exploit recent developments in stochastic variational inference, deriving both global and local updates for the proposed model.

The modeling novelty amounts to a fairly direct combination of aMMSB with MMTM, though it requires some tedious bookkeeping. The presentation of this material could be strengthened.

Although much of the learning algorithm is a straightforward application of stochastic variational inference, the authors propose an intuitive and interesting approximation for the local update step.

Given the ubiquity of directed networks, a more detailed discussion of potential extensions to that setting would be valuable.

Ongoing concerns:
- “our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs, that represents the processes of generating triangular motifs.” This reads oddly: (1) ERGMs can include far more statistics than just triangles, and (2) it is unclear in what sense PTM is a “latent space counterpart,” unless that label would apply to all latent space network models.
- Are the inferred latent spaces interpretable? If so, what do they reveal about similarities and differences across these graphs?
The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clear (at least to this reader).
- It is not established (in this paper) why subsampling triangles for each node (using delta) yields an adequate approximation.
- If the goal is to recover the “set of non-zero roles,” the model might benefit from a more explicitly binary notion of “role.”
- How sensitive are the latent-space recovery results to the .125 threshold (which seems somewhat arbitrary)?
- Many latent space models can produce power-law networks. Which specific one was used in the experiments?
- Why does PTM CGS have K^3 complexity? Didn’t you claim there are O(K) parameters rather than O(K^3)?
- A concern is how the method performs when fewer edges are observed. The experiments hold out only 10% of edges, presumably because the distribution of adjacent triangles would be quite biased if too many edges were removed prior to the preprocessing step.
- It is counterintuitive that the method outperforms MMSB on a synthetic network generated by MMSB (Figure 1, left four panels).

This work employs stochastic variational inference to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel (with a restricted parameter set) from triangle counts in a graph. The modeling choices and approximations yield a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
