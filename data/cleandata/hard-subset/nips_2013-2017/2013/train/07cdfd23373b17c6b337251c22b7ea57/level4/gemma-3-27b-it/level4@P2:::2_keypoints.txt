- Introduces a scalable latent-space inference approach using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious statistical model, and stochastic variational inference.
- Core idea reduces parameter count by sharing parameters across groups of latent roles, achieving O(NK) complexity versus much higher complexities of prior methods.
- Builds on prior work (a-MMSB, MMTM) to overcome scalability bottlenecks rather than proposing a wholly novel theoretical framework.
- Demonstrates competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link prediction on synthetic and real networks, including a 1M-node YouTube network.
- Main contribution is a practical, efficient algorithm enabling large-scale latent-space analysis on a single machine.
- The local update approximation within the stochastic variational inference framework is an insightful and impactful technical contribution.
- Presentation needs streamlining; some model motivations and connections are unclear.
- The claimed connection between the PTM and ERGMs is imprecise and requires clarification.
- The interpretability of the inferred latent spaces is underexplored and should be expanded.
- Experimental results are strong but lack deeper analysis of what structural or semantic features the model learns beyond accuracy metrics.
- Require justification for triangle subsampling (δ) and analysis of its impact on accuracy.
- The 0.125 threshold for role assignment appears arbitrary and its sensitivity should be evaluated.
- Need detailed description of the power-law network generator used in synthetic experiments.
- Possible discrepancy: PTM Gibbs sampler complexity stated as K^3 conflicts with claim of O(K) parameters—needs clarification.
- Address potential performance limitations on sparse graphs and justify the choice of a 10% edge holdout.
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated networks requires investigation and explanation.
- Arguments for acceptance: demonstrated scalability to >1M nodes, competitive/improved accuracy, novel parsimonious triangular model + SVI combination, and practical single-machine implementation.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, insufficient justification for design choices, limited interpretability analysis, and unexplained counterintuitive experimental findings.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with important contributions; accept with minor revisions to address the above clarifications and analyses.
