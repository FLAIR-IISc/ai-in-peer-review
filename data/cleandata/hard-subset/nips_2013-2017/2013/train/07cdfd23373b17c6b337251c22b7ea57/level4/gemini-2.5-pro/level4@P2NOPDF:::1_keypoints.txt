- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter complexity from O(K³) to O(K) and proposes a stochastic variational inference algorithm with additional approximations for scalability.
- Synthetic results suggest parameter reduction can improve statistical power; real-world experiments show PTM is competitive in predictive accuracy.
- Major concern: insufficient motivation and empirical validation for learning very large K — no experiment showing a large-K PTM outperforming a small-K MMTM constrained by computation.
- Model selection for K is not described for PTM or competitors (e.g., MMSB), risking biased comparisons.
- Lacks analysis of PTM’s limitations and trade-offs; no experiments testing failure cases where the true interaction matrix B has O(K³) complexity.
- Synthetic experiments do not report or vary the structure/rank of the true B matrix, missing tests where PTM’s O(K) assumption is unfavorable.
- Algorithmic approximations are introduced without sensitivity analyses, leaving unclear their impact on accuracy and runtime.
- Specific missing ablations: sensitivity to the triplet subsampling threshold δ, justification for the chosen form of data reduction vs. other downsampling, and the quality trade-off in the O(K) local update approximation as K grows.
- Conceptual concern: framing MMTM/PTM as full probabilistic generative models is questionable because a “bag-of-triples” typically does not correspond to a valid graph, so the model’s likelihood may place mass on non-graph objects.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making motivations and methods easy to follow.
- The core idea of parameter-constraining for scalability is original and timely, though incremental relative to MMTM.
- Conclusion: interesting and potentially impactful work but currently not ready for publication without more rigorous experimental validation and critical discussion of limitations and approximations.
- Additional note: the title is overly broad — the paper addresses a specific scalable model (PTM), not a general framework for scalable probabilistic latent space models.
