- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces MMTM parameter space from O(K^3) to O(K) for faster inference.  
- PTM models a bag-of-triangular-motifs generative process with vertices having mixture distributions over K latent roles.  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm for PTM with O(NK) time complexity.  
- PTM is an interesting specialization of MMTM, but practical advantage of improved scalability in K is unclear.  
- Synthetic results suggest stronger statistical power, but the true parameter matrix B used is not clearly described.  
- Authors should test PTM against varied choices of B, including cases with O(K^3) distinct values that disfavor PTM.  
- Several approximation tricks introduced (mixture-of-deltas variational distribution, stochastic natural gradients) lack thorough impact analysis.  
- The effect of the threshold δ on real and synthetic experiments is not explored; trade-offs between lowering δ, scalability, and accuracy are unclear.  
- Paper is very clearly written and well-organized.  
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental, but constraining parameters for scalability is an original and interesting direction.  
- PTM could be significant if its simplifying assumptions hold, since few latent-space models scale to large networks.  
- Title is too broad; paper addresses a scalable algorithm for a specific model (PTM), not general latent-space models.  
- PTM is not a full generative model of networks: it omits empty/single-edge motifs and does not enforce consistency for overlapping triangles—implications unclear.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel PTM, appropriate SVI for large networks, empirical effectiveness on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Cons for acceptance: unclear practical benefits of K scalability, insufficient analysis of approximation effects, and non-generative aspects may be problematic.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—clarify practical advantages of K scalability, analyze approximation impacts (including δ), and discuss consequences of not being a full generative network model.
