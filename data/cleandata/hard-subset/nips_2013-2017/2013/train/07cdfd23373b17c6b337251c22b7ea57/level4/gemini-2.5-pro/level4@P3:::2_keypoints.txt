- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) for scalable probabilistic latent-space inference in large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation.  
- Builds on three principles: succinct network representation, parsimonious statistical model, and scalable inference algorithm.  
- Adopts the MMTM bag-of-triangles representation, which is often much smaller than the full adjacency matrix.  
- PTM reduces triangle-generating parameters from O(K³) to O(K) by grouping latent-role configurations and sharing parameters.  
- Derives a stochastic variational inference algorithm with a structured mean-field approximation that captures dependencies within a triangle.  
- Introduces a novel O(K) "mixture-of-deltas" approximation for the local variational update, avoiding an O(K³) step.  
- Overall algorithm runs in O(NK) per pass and scales to networks with over a million nodes.  
- Demonstrates exceptional scalability and speedups (e.g., million-node network with 100 roles in hours; Brightkite: 12 minutes vs. 8 days).  
- Parsimonious parameterization acts as effective regularization, improving recovery and sometimes outperforming MMSB even on MMSB-generated data.  
- The O(K) local update approximation is a non-trivial, principled algorithmic contribution enabling tractability.  
- Empirical validation is thorough: synthetic and real data, latent-space recovery and link prediction tasks, and comparisons to relevant baselines.  
- Convergence plots show stochastic inference is more efficient than batch implementations.  
- Clarity issues: distinction between simplexes ∆¹ and ∆² is not clearly explained.  
- Confusing complexity claim: PTM is parameter-efficient but the Collapsed Gibbs Sampler still has O(K³) complexity—needs clarification.  
- δ-subsampling of triangles is cited to prior work but should include a brief intuition for self-containment.  
- Evaluation concern: thresholding mixed-membership vectors at 0.125 for latent-recovery is arbitrary and needs sensitivity analysis or justification.  
- Missing qualitative analysis: lack of interpretable examples of inferred roles or communities on real networks.  
- Reproducibility gap: the model used to generate power-law synthetic networks is not specified.  
- Overstated claim: calling PTM a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is too strong and should be softened or better justified.  
- Scope limits: experiments hold out only 10% of edges—performance under higher sparsity is unexplored and should be discussed.  
- Applicability limits: paper focuses on undirected networks and should discuss challenges and extensions to directed graphs.  
- Summary judgement: strong, practically important contribution with impressive scalability and accuracy; minor clarity and experimental-detail issues do not undermine the core contribution—recommend accept.
