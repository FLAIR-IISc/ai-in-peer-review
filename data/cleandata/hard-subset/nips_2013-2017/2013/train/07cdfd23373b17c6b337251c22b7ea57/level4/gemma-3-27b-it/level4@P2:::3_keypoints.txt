- Introduces a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) + stochastic variational inference enabling inference on networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of latent roles; recommendation: accept.
- Represents networks as a “bag of triangular motifs” to capture higher-order structural statistics succinctly.
- Parameter sharing in PTM reduces model complexity from O(K^3) to O(K), greatly improving scalability.
- Stochastic variational inference yields time complexity O(NK), contributing to practical scalability.
- Experimental results show significant speedups over state-of-the-art methods with competitive or improved accuracy.
- Strong empirical comparison against a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.) and MMTM Gibbs sampler (Ho et al.) demonstrating impressive speedups.
- Strengths: clever parameter sharing, well-justified use of SVI, and thorough evaluation on synthetic and real datasets.
- Clarify the term “parsimonious” modeling—define it explicitly in the probabilistic model context.
- Correct or soften the claim that Gopalan et al.’s down-sampling compromises accuracy, since Gopalan et al. show it does not.
- The LDA analogy is weak: unlike LDA, PTM can generate outputs that are not valid networks and this limitation should be acknowledged.
- Theoretical citation issue: SVI discussion should cite Hoffman et al. (JMLR) for local variables; Sato’s algorithm is not directly applicable here.
- Insufficient detail on how power-law networks are generated in the latent space; expand for reproducibility.
- Comparison set should directly include Gopalan et al.’s algorithm (in addition to or instead of MMSB batch) for fairness.
- Minor edits: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership” (p.2) and consider removing the final sentence on p.8 as the prior sentence is stronger.
