The authors present a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the “triangle model” of networks. A key contribution is the development of a parameter-sharing subclass of the model, enabling efficient stochastic variational inference for large networks. The paper is well-written and technically sound, and I recommend it for acceptance.

However, I have a few suggestions for improvement. On page 1, the term “parsimonious” modeling should be clarified, as it may not be standard terminology within the field of probabilistic models. A brief definition would be helpful given its importance to the paper. Also on page 1, the discussion of down-sampling zeros appears to contradict findings in Gopalan et al., which suggest accuracy is maintained with this approach. The current phrasing is therefore potentially misleading.

On page 4, the analogy to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) as a defense of the triangle model is not entirely convincing. While LDA’s generative process produces a valid, albeit potentially trivial, “document,” the triangle model can generate outputs that do not correspond to valid networks. While this is not a critical flaw – the model remains practical and useful – the comparison to LDA is not a strong justification.

Regarding page 5, when discussing related work, the authors should cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato. Specifically, in scenarios with “local variables” (as defined by Hoffman), Sato’s algorithm is not applicable, and the relevant theoretical foundation is found in Hoffman’s work.

On page 6, the method for generating power-law networks in the latent space is unclear and requires further elaboration. Additionally, given the citation of Gopalan et al. in the context of accelerating MMSB, a direct comparison to their algorithm would be valuable, either in addition to or instead of comparing to MMSB batch.

Finally, a few minor points: on page 2, “mixture-membership” should be corrected to “mixed-membership.” On page 8, I recommend removing the final sentence, as the preceding sentence provides a more impactful conclusion. Furthermore, please ensure the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference” is included in the bibliography.
