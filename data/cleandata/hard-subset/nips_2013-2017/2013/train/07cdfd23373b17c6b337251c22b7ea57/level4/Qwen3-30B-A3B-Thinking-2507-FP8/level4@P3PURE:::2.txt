

This paper proposes a technique for learning latent structure in social network data by fitting a model to triangle count statistics derived from a preprocessing step, analogous to a bag-of-words representation for text documents. The main modeling advance builds on aMMSB: rather than parameterizing all inter-"community" interactions, the authors leverage triangle configurations to identify a set of K roles for parameterization. This reduces model parameters and enables a more scalable implementation. The authors apply recent advances in stochastic variational inference, deriving both global and local update rules for the proposed model.

The novel modeling contribution combines aMMSB with the MMTM, though the presentation could be improved and the derivation involves somewhat tedious bookkeeping. While most of the learning algorithm directly applies stochastic variational inference, the authors provide an intuitive and intriguing approximation for the local update rule.

Given the prevalence of directed networks, a detailed discussion of potential extensions to directed graphs would be valuable.  

Ongoing concerns:  
- The statement that "our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs, representing the processes of generating triangular motifs" is questionable. Specifically: (1) ERGMs accommodate far more varied statistics beyond triangles, and (2) the PTM’s relationship to latent space models is unclear unless all latent space network models are considered "latent space counterparts."  
- Are the inferred latent spaces interpretable? If so, what insights do they provide about similarities and differences among the graphs?  
- The distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is not clear.  
- The justification for subsampling triangles for each node (using Δ) as an adequate approximation is insufficiently explained.  
- If the goal is to recover the "set of non-zero roles," the model might benefit from a more explicit binary role formulation.  
- How robust are the latent space recovery results to the threshold of 0.125 (which appears arbitrary)?  
- The paper does not specify which latent space model was used for experiments (as multiple can generate power-law networks).  
- PTM CGS exhibits K³ complexity, yet the authors claim O(K) parameters; this discrepancy requires clarification.  
- Performance under sparse edge conditions is unaddressed. Experiments hold out only 10% of edges, likely to avoid biasing adjacent triangle distributions—a limitation when fewer edges are observed.  
- It is counterintuitive that PTM outperforms MMSB on a synthetic network generated via MMSB (Figure 1, left 4 panels).  

This work employs stochastic variational inference to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel (with a restricted parameter set) on graph triangle counts, yielding a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
