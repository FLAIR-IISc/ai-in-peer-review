- Paper proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM): represents graphs as bags of triangles, shares triangle-role parameters to reduce O(K^3) → O(K), and develops structured SVI with O(K) local-update approximation and natural-gradient global updates.
- Empirical claim: PTM runs orders of magnitude faster than MMSB/MMTM and scales to million-node networks with competitive or improved role recovery and link prediction.

Strengths:
- Clear scalability contribution: O(NK) global updates and O(K) local updates enable large speedups on multicore machines.
- Sensible modeling trade-off: parameter sharing drastically reduces dimensionality while keeping higher-order triangular structure.
- Structured variational approximation: grouping per-triangle role variables and the mixture-of-deltas local approximation is intuitive, cheap, and effective.
- Strong empirical evaluation: fast convergence, good wall-clock performance, and competitive accuracy on synthetic and large real networks.
- Practical engineering: mini-batching over triangles, parallel local updates, and memory-saving by discarding local variational parameters.

Weaknesses / Questions:
- Positioning vs ERGMs: calling PTM a “latent-space counterpart to ERGMs” is misleading; clarify the intended comparison and scope.
- Interpretability: unclear if learned roles are semantically interpretable; paper lacks qualitative analysis of role profiles and motif usage.
- Notation/clarity: Δ^1 vs Δ^2 and semantics of B0/Bxx/Bxxx/equivalence classes are not clearly defined early on.
- Triangle subsampling (δ): relies on prior work without summarizing why δ-subsampling preserves statistics; needs recap, assumptions, bias/variance discussion, and sensitivity analyses.
- Local update approximation: properties and sensitivity to the size/construction of set A (and whether A is re-sampled) are not analyzed; ablation needed.
- Collapsed Gibbs complexity: PTM-Gibbs is reported O(K^3) per triangle despite O(K) parameters — reconcile combinatorial latent-space vs parameter count and practicality.
- Synthetic evaluation choices: thresholding (e.g., 0.125) for nonzero roles is ad hoc; report sensitivity or use model-based decision rules.
- Power-law simulator: simulator details and parameters are missing; specify model and settings for reproducibility.
- Missing-data robustness: only 10% held-out edges reported; need evaluation under sparser observations and schemes that preserve triangle distributions.
- PTM beating MMSB on MMSB-generated data: surprising result requires explanation (finite-sample effects, parameter sharing) and additional runs to rule out initialization artifacts.
- Directed networks: extension to directed motifs is not discussed; addressing this would broaden impact.

Quality:
- Technically sound with careful SVI derivation and sensible algorithmic choices, but deeper bias/variance analysis of the local approximation would strengthen the work.

Clarity:
- Generally understandable, but parameterization, δ-subsampling, and local approximation sections need clearer exposition and a concise figure/table summarizing cases.

Originality:
- Novelty is a practical synthesis (triangle representation + a-MMSB-style sharing + structured SVI + O(K) local update); conceptually incremental but impactful for scale.

Significance:
- High practical significance: enables analysis at scales previously infeasible and is useful for practitioners handling large social/information networks.

Pros for acceptance:
- Major scalability and engineering contributions with large speedups.
- Competitive or superior accuracy on multiple tasks and datasets.
- Clear, reusable algorithmic ideas (parameter sharing, structured SVI, O(K) local updates).

Cons for acceptance:
- Overstated/unclear positioning (ERGMs) and gaps in sensitivity analyses (A selection, δ, thresholding).
- Limited treatment of interpretability and directed-graph extensions.

Suggested manuscript improvements:
- Clarify Δ^1/Δ^2, B0/Bxx/Bxxx, and equivalence classes with a concise figure/table.
- Provide a brief recap of δ-subsampling, its assumptions, expected bias/variance, and sensitivity analyses.
- Add ablations varying A selection/size and threshold sensitivity (e.g., 0.125).
- Reconcile PTM-Gibbs complexity vs parameter count and clarify practical regimes for Gibbs.
- Expand discussion and examples on directed motifs and role interpretability with qualitative illustrations.
