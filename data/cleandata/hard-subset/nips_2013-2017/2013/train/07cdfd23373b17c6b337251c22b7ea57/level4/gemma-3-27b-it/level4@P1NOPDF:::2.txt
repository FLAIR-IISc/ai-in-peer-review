This paper proposes a technique for learning latent structure in social network data. The model is fit to triangle count statistics obtained through a preprocessing step, analogous to a bag-of-words representation for text. The main modeling advance is motivated by aMMSB: rather than parameterizing all between-"community" interactions, the authors consider triangle configurations to identify a set of *K* roles to parameterize. This results in fewer parameters and a more scalable implementation. They apply recent advances in stochastic variational inference, deriving both global and local update rules for their proposed model.

The core modeling contribution is a straightforward combination of aMMSB and MMTM, although the presentation requires some tedious bookkeeping and could be improved for clarity. While most of the learning algorithm directly applies stochastic variational inference, the authors provide an intuitive and intriguing approximation for the local update rule. 

Given the prevalence of directed networks, a discussion of how these methods might be extended to handle them would be valuable. 

I have several ongoing concerns:

- The authors state that “our Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) is essentially a latent space counterpart to ERGMs, that represents the processes of generating triangular motifs.” This feels like a curious claim: firstly, ERGMs can incorporate a wider range of statistics than just triangles, and secondly, the characterization of PTM as a “latent space counterpart” seems broadly applicable to all latent space network models. 

- Are the inferred latent spaces interpretable? If so, what insights do they provide regarding the similarities and differences among the graphs being analyzed?

- The distinction between Δ¹ and Δ² is unclear.

- The paper does not sufficiently justify why subsampling the triangles for each node (using delta) provides an adequate approximation.

- If the goal is to recover the “set of non-zero roles,” then incorporating a more binary notion of “role” within the model might be beneficial.

- How sensitive are the latent space recovery results to the threshold of 0.125, which appears somewhat arbitrary?

- A variety of latent space models can generate power-law networks. Which model was used for comparison in the experiments?

- The authors claim PTM CGS has K³ complexity. However, didn't they argue that the model has O(K) parameters, rather than O(K³)? 

- I am concerned about performance when fewer edges are observed. In the experiments, only 10% of edges are held out, presumably because removing more edges prior to preprocessing would significantly bias the distribution of adjacent triangles.

- It is counterintuitive that the method outperforms MMSB on a synthetic network generated *using* MMSB (Figure 1, left four panels).

In summary, this work uses stochastic variational inference to learn a mixed-membership blockmodel (with a restricted parameter set) based on triangle counts in a graph. The modeling choices and approximations lead to a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
