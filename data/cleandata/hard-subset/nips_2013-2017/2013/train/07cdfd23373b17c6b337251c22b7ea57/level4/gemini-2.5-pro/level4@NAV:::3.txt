This paper presents a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the network "triangle model." The authors propose a potent subclass of this model that utilizes an intelligent parameter-sharing scheme and employs stochastic variational inference to estimate the posterior for very large networks. The work is well-written and the execution is solid. I recommend the paper for acceptance.

p1: The term "parsimonious modeling" is central to the paper's motivation, but it is not a standard term within the probabilistic modeling community. The authors should clarify its meaning, perhaps by dedicating a sentence to its definition.

p1: The introduction's characterization of Gopalan et al. seems inaccurate. My understanding of that work is that down-sampling zeros does not negatively impact accuracy, which contradicts the claim made here. This sentence should be revised for clarity.

p4: While the pragmatic value of the triangle model is clear, the justification by analogy to Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) is not convincing. The generative process for LDA always yields an object that is structurally a document, even if nonsensical. In contrast, the generative process for the proposed model can result in structures that are not valid networks. Although this does not detract from the model's practical utility, using LDA as a defense is inappropriate.

p5: The citation to Amari and Sato should be augmented with a citation to Hoffman et al. The scenario described in this paper, which involves "local variables" (to use Hoffman's terminology), requires the theoretical framework presented in Hoffman et al., as Sato's algorithm is not applicable in this context.

p6: The procedure for generating power law networks in the latent space is not sufficiently detailed. The paper should provide a clearer explanation of this experimental step.

p6: Given that Gopalan et al. is cited as a method for accelerating the Mixed-Membership Stochastic Blockmodel (MMSB), the experimental evaluation should include a comparison against their algorithm. This would be a valuable addition to, or replacement for, the current comparison with batch MMSB.

Small comments
p2: The term "mixture-membership" should be "mixed-membership".
p8: Consider removing the final sentence of the paper. The penultimate sentence provides a more impactful conclusion.
In the bibliography, the reference for "Stochastic Variational Inference" should be updated to cite the JMLR publication.
