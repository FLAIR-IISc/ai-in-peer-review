- Paper proposes PTM that reduces MMTM parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) with a stochastic variational inference algorithm and additional scalability approximations.  
- Synthetic results claim reduced variables can increase statistical power; real-data results show competitive accuracy with existing methods.  
- Questionable practical advantage of scalability in K: missing experiments showing PTM with large K outperforms MMTM with small K and unclear K selection/model selection procedure.  
- Simplifying assumptions insufficiently analyzed—no clear discussion of when PTM will fail (e.g., when true B has O(K^3) distinct values).  
- Synthetic experiments lack description of the true B and do not test cases unfavorable to PTM.  
- Approximation tricks are poorly analyzed: impact of threshold δ, how δ affects accuracy vs. scalability, and sensitivity to δ are not studied.  
- Unclear effects of other approximations: rationale for why PTM avoids downsampling pitfalls, ignoring non-edges, O(K) local update approximation, and how sampling different numbers of triples affects quality.  
- Experimental setup may be unfair to competitors; results could be biased by model-selection choices.  
- Clarity: paper is very clearly written and well-organized.  
- Originality: modeling is somewhat incremental, but constraining parameters to achieve scalability is an interesting, original direction.  
- Significance: could have good impact if assumptions hold, since few latent-space network models scale to large data.  
- Title is too broad relative to the specific contribution.  
- Doubts about PTM being a true probabilistic generative model for networks; bag-of-triples assumption may not correspond to valid networks and undermines likelihood interpretation.  
- Side-effects of modeling and inference choices are poorly investigated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel PTM specialization, effective SVI and approximations, competitive accuracy.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially unfair experiments, unclear synthetic setup and assumptions, insufficient analysis of approximations, broad title, and questionable probabilistic validity.
