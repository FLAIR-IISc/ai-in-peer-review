- Proposes modeling triangle-count statistics as a bag-of-words-like representation for social network latent structure.  
- Builds on aMMSB by using triangle configurations to identify K roles, reducing parameters and improving scalability.  
- Applies stochastic variational inference and derives both global and local update rules for the proposed model.  
- Core contribution is a straightforward combination of aMMSB and MMTM, but presentation requires tedious bookkeeping and clearer exposition.  
- Provides an intuitive and intriguing approximation for the local update rule despite most updates being standard SVI.  
- Lacks discussion of extensions to directed networks, which are prevalent in practice.  
- The claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is questionable because ERGMs cover broader statistics and the label is overly broad.  
- Unclear whether the inferred latent spaces are interpretable and what insights they yield about graph similarities/differences.  
- The distinction between Δ^1 and Δ^2 is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Justification for approximating triangle counts by per-node subsampling (using δ) is inadequate.  
- Given the goal of recovering a “set of non-zero roles,” a more binary notion of role within the model might be preferable.  
- Sensitivity of latent-space recovery to the 0.125 threshold is unclear and appears arbitrary.  
- The latent space model used to generate the power-law networks in experiments is not specified.  
- The reported O(K^3) complexity for PTM CGS seems inconsistent with the claim of O(K) parameters.  
- Potential performance degradation on sparser graphs is a concern; experiments only withhold 10% edges, possibly to avoid bias.  
- Counterintuitively, PTM outperforms MMSB on synthetic networks generated by MMSB, which raises questions about the evaluation.  
- Overall, presents an SVI-based mixed-membership blockmodel restricted to triangle counts that enables a more scalable O(NK) algorithm.
