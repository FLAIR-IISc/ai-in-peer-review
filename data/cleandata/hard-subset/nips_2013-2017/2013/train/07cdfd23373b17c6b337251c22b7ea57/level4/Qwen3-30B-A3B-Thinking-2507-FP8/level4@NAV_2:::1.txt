

This paper introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which reduces the parameter space complexity of the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM) from O(K³) to O(K), enabling more scalable inference for large networks. The authors develop a stochastic variational inference algorithm with additional approximation techniques to further enhance scalability, demonstrating competitive accuracy in latent space recovery and link prediction on both synthetic and real-world networks.

Quality: The PTM represents an interesting specialization of MMTM, but the practical advantage of achieving scalability in terms of K (number of latent roles) remains questionable. To properly evaluate this method, it would be critical to demonstrate that PTM with large K outperforms MMTM with small K, which is not shown in the experiments. The paper lacks clear description of how K was chosen in real data experiments for both PTM and MMSB, potentially introducing bias in the comparison. Additionally, the authors do not adequately address when the model might fail: if the true parameter matrix B (following their notation) has O(K³) distinct values, PTM would likely perform poorly. The synthetic experiments do not explore various B matrices that would test the model's limitations, instead focusing only on cases favorable to PTM. The paper also provides insufficient analysis of the approximation techniques: the impact of the δ threshold on accuracy versus scalability is not explored, the reason why PTM avoids the downsampling issues affecting a-MMSB is unclear (especially since both MMTM and PTM ignore single-edge triples), and the effect of varying the number of sampled triples in the O(K) approximation (e.g., 2K, 4K, 100K) is not investigated. Overall, the experimental setup appears unfair to competitors, and the description of potential weaknesses is inadequate.

Clarity: The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized, with clear explanations of technical concepts.

Originality: While the modeling approach represents some incremental progress, the focus on achieving scalability through parameter space constraints is an interesting and original direction, especially considering the recent trend toward generalizing existing models rather than optimizing for scalability.

Significance: If the simplifying assumptions hold in practice, this work could significantly impact the application of latent space models to large-scale networks, as few existing methods scale to millions of nodes with hundreds of roles.

Additional Comments: The title is overly broad; the contribution is specifically a scalable inference algorithm for the PTM, not a general approach to latent space inference. Furthermore, the paper's claim of being "probabilistic" is questionable. While the authors compare PTM to bag-of-words models in Section 3, the key distinction is that LDA generates a bag-of-words that corresponds to an actual document (with a proper likelihood function), whereas PTM generates a bag-of-triangles that almost never corresponds to a valid network. This means the majority of the posterior probability mass is placed on modeling non-networks, which potentially violates fundamental principles of probabilistic modeling. The authors should clarify what "probability" means in PTM and address this concern more thoroughly.
