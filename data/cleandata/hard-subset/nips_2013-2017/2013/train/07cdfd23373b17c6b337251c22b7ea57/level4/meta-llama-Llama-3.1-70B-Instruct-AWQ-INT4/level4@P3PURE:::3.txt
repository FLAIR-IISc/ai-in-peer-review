The authors develop a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the "triangle model" of networks. They propose a powerful subclass of the model that shares parameters in a clever way, utilizing stochastic variational inference to infer the posterior in very large networks. The paper is well-written and well-executed, making it acceptable.

On page 1, the authors mention "parsimonious" modeling, a term that may not be commonly used in the context of probabilistic models. To improve clarity, it would be helpful to provide a brief explanation of this term in a sentence, as it plays a crucial role in the paper.

Also on page 1, the authors state that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy, citing Gopalan et al. However, upon reviewing Gopalan et al., it appears that down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy, making the sentence in the introduction potentially misleading.

On page 4, the authors defend the triangle model by comparing it to LDA. However, this comparison may not be entirely fair. While running the generative process of LDA can produce a document, albeit a silly one, running the triangle model can produce objects that are not networks. Although this does not detract from the model's practicality, the defense by means of LDA is not entirely appropriate.

On page 5, the authors should cite Hoffman et al. in addition to Amari and Sato. In the scenario presented, where there are "local variables" (in Hoffman's language), Sato's algorithm does not apply, and the needed theory can be found in Hoffman.

On page 6, the authors mention generating power law networks in the latent space but do not provide sufficient details on the process. It would be beneficial to clarify this point in the paper.

Additionally, on page 6, the authors cite Gopalan et al. as a means of speeding up MMSB. However, they should also compare their algorithm to Gopalan et al.'s algorithm, either in addition to or instead of MMSB batch.

Minor comments include correcting "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership" on page 2. On page 8, it is suggested to remove the last sentence, as the second-to-last sentence provides a stronger conclusion to the paper.

Lastly, the bibliography should cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference".
