- Proposes PTM to reduce MMTM’s O(K^3) parameters to O(K) for faster inference with stochastic variational inference and scalability tricks.  
- Practical advantage of scaling with large K is unclear; need to show how learning MMTM with large K helps.  
- Experiments lack comparison of PTM vs MMTM performance as a function of K to justify large-K benefits.  
- Rationale for chosen K values in real-data experiments is not explained, risking biased model selection.  
- Simplifying assumptions’ implications are underexplored; PTM may fail if true B has many distinct O(K^3) values.  
- Synthetic experiments insufficiently varied: authors should test a wider range of true B matrices that both favor and challenge PTM.  
- Several approximation/optimization tricks are interesting but their impacts are weakly analyzed.  
- Unclear how threshold δ affects accuracy/scalability and how low δ can go without degrading performance.  
- Insufficient explanation why PTM avoids accuracy loss from downsampling (a-MMSB) and why non-edges can’t be ignored in MMSB.  
- Unclear effect of O(K) approximation on local updates and how sampling more triples (2K, 4K, 100K) would change results.  
- Reviewer not convinced experiments fairly compare competitors; discussion of limitations is lacking.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Modeling contribution is somewhat incremental, but constraining parameters for scalability is an interesting direction.  
- Potentially significant if assumptions hold, as it could enable scalable latent-space models for large networks.  
- Title is overly broad; method scales a specific model (PTM), not latent-space models in general.  
- Raises conceptual concern whether PTM is truly probabilistic: bag-of-triples may not correspond to a valid network likelihood, so probability interpretation is unclear.
