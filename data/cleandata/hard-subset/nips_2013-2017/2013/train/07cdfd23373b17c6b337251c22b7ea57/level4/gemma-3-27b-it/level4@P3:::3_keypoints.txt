- Presents a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference method for large networks using triangular motifs, a parsimonious model, and stochastic variational inference.
- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter space via strategic parameter sharing for scalable inference.
- Proposes an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm leveraging natural gradient methods for large-scale networks.
- Demonstrates significant speedups over state-of-the-art methods while maintaining competitive or improved accuracy on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Clearly written, with well-articulated motivation, technical details, and experimental findings.
- Strong understanding and contextualization with prior work (MMSB, ERGM).
- Scalability is the primary strength, enabling analysis of networks with over a million vertices.
- Novelty in PTM’s parameter-sharing strategy effectively reduces computational complexity.
- Technical soundness: SVI algorithm appears well-designed and theoretically justified.
- Empirical validation is thorough, with relevant baselines and diverse datasets.
- The term “parsimonious” is used without a clear definition in the probabilistic modeling context.
- Claim that down-sampling zeros in Gopalan et al. does not compromise accuracy is potentially misleading and needs a nuanced discussion.
- The analogy to LDA to justify model validity is weak; the generative process does not guarantee valid network structures.
- Omission: should cite Hoffman et al. (JMLR) for the SVI theoretical framework, especially regarding local variables.
- Description of power-law network generation in latent space is vague and needs more detail for reproducibility.
- Baseline comparison should include Gopalan et al.’s a-MMSB algorithm for a more direct and relevant comparison.
- Pro-acceptance: addresses a significant scalability challenge with a novel, effective solution and compelling experiments.
- Con-acceptance: minor clarity/citation issues, a potentially misleading statement about prior work, and insufficient detail on network generation.
- Overall recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to address clarity, citations, and reproducibility.
- Minor edits: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership” (p2), remove last sentence on p8, and cite JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference".
