- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm for scalable latent-space inference in large networks.
- Represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs to enable more efficient, scalable inference algorithms.
- Demonstrates competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link prediction on synthetic and real networks.
- Achieves much faster convergence than recent work, reportedly in 2–5 data passes.
- Thorough empirical evaluation across synthetic and real-world datasets supports effectiveness and scalability claims.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the method and its advantages.
- Assumes triangle-motif representation, which may not suit all network types or structural patterns.
- Lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art latent-space/network models.
- Offers limited discussion of the method’s limitations and possible extensions.
- Novel combination of a parsimonious statistical model with scalable stochastic variational inference.
- Introducing triangular motifs as a core representation is a new and interesting idea.
- Addresses the key challenge of scalability for latent-space inference in large networks.
- Potentially applicable to community detection, link prediction, and exploratory network analysis.
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, strong empirical evaluation, and clear presentation.
- Rejection concerns: restrictive motif assumption, insufficient SOTA comparisons, and limited discussion of limits/extensions.
