- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and a stochastic variational inference algorithm for scalable probabilistic latent-space inference on large networks.  
- Claims three principles enable success: succinct informative representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable/parallel inference.  
- Builds on triangular network representations and mixed-membership models (MMSB, MMTM) but claims improved scalability and accuracy.  
- Technically sound: PTM is well-defined and the inference algorithm is valid and efficient.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world networks demonstrating scalability and accuracy.  
- Can handle networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of latent roles, useful for community detection, link prediction, and exploratory analysis.  
- Writing and presentation are clear and well-organized overall.  
- Notation issues: multiple symbols for the same quantity and some equations could be simplified or reorganized.  
- Missing methodological details: initialization and hyperparameter choices need more explanation.  
- Comparative analysis could be deeper: more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art latent-space methods is needed.  
- Original but incremental: introduces a novel scalable model/algorithm while building on prior triangular and mixed-membership work; needs clearer differentiation from past methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, scalable, technically sound, and well-evaluated.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor gaps in initialization/hyperparameter details, notation clarity, and discussion of differences from prior work.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10.
