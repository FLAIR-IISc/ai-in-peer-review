- Proposal: Scalable latent-space inference using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious triangular model (PTM), and stochastic variational inference.  
- Problem addressed: Prior methods scale poorly (O(N²) for edges or O(K³) for roles), making million-node networks infeasible.  
- Key technical innovation: PTM reduces parameter space from O(K³) to O(K) via parameter sharing.  
- Complexity result: Enables an inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity.  
- Quality: Technically sound with thorough theoretical analysis and comprehensive synthetic and real-data experiments.  
- Empirical performance: Achieves competitive or better accuracy than baselines while being orders of magnitude faster.  
- Limitation noted: Insufficient exploration of when triangular motifs fail—e.g., networks with weak triadic closure.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-organized and detailed; reproducible model, algorithm, and experiments with effective comparisons to prior work.  
- Originality: Novel reduction from O(K³) to O(K) is well-motivated despite prior use of triangular motifs.  
- Significance: Enables analysis of million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine in hours.  
- Experimental impact: Demonstrates 100–1000× speedups on Brightkite and Stanford networks with competitive link prediction.  
- Weaknesses and gaps: Limited discussion of motif applicability and no comparison to non-probabilistic embedding methods (DeepWalk, Node2Vec).  
- Overall verdict: Significant contribution with solid validation; recommendation for acceptance.
