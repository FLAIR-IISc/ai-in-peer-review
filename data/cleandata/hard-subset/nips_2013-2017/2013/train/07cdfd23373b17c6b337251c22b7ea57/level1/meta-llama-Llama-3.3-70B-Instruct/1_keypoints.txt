- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs.
- Introduces a fast stochastic variational inference algorithm for approximate inference under PTM.
- PTM uses a more compact, lower-dimensional parameterization than existing latent-space models.
- Paper is well-written with clear technical exposition and a thorough related-work review.
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world networks show competitive or improved latent-space recovery.
- Experiments show strong link-prediction performance.
- Method is orders of magnitude faster than recent scalable latent-space network models.
- Strength: novel and efficient statistical model for latent-space network analysis.
- Strength: development of a fast, scalable inference algorithm.
- Strength: comprehensive empirical demonstration and comparisons with existing methods.
- Weakness: modeling assumption that networks can be represented as a bag of triangles may not hold universally.
- Weakness: use of a simple subsampling procedure could cause information loss.
- Weakness: lacks thorough analysis of the algorithm's convergence properties.
- Overall: significant contribution with potential applications in community detection, link prediction, and visualization.
- Pro-accept: novelty, accuracy, speed, and thorough evaluation argue for acceptance.
- Con-accept: motif assumption, subsampling risks, and convergence concerns argue against acceptance.
