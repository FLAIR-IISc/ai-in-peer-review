- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that ties triangle-generating parameters across role triplets, reducing parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Introduces structured stochastic variational inference (SVI) with a mixture-of-deltas O(K)-time local update to scale inference.  
- Builds on triangle-based mixed-membership work (MMTM) and complements SVI and ERGM literature; main novelty is parameter tying and scalable structured SVI.  
- Parameter sharing (B0, Bxx, Bxxx) is well-defined and reduces variance/cost but limits expressivity for minority-role patterns and heterophily.  
- Structured mean-field posterior and SVI derivation are appropriate; exact updates are intractable, mixture-of-deltas gives a valid lower bound for the restricted family.  
- Resampling the off-diagonal set A each iteration means the algorithm optimizes a changing bound; unbiasedness of natural gradients holds only for a fixed family, theoretical implications need discussion.  
- Complexity: global updates O(NK), local O(K) per sampled triangle, overall per-pass cost O(Nδ^2K); paper sometimes ambiguously states O(NK).  
- Empirically scales to >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single multi-core machine with orders-of-magnitude speedups and competitive accuracy.  
- Synthetic experiments show robust accuracy (NMI) and large speed gains; initialization used 2% seeding which is optimistic.  
- Comparisons to MMSB and MMTM are generally fair, but single-threaded baselines vs multi-threaded PTM favor the proposed method.  
- Comparison to a-MMSB relies on reported times rather than controlled reruns on same hardware/datasets.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized; equivalence classes and B-parameters are precisely described.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent complexity statements regarding triangle count and δ, and unclear implications of resampling A on convergence guarantees.  
- Originality: novel parameter tying for triangle-role configurations and the specific structured SVI approximation appear novel and meaningful.  
- Significance: important practical contribution by enabling probabilistic latent-space inference at million-node scale with hundreds of roles.  
- Strengths: large scalability gains, clean O(K) parameterization, structured SVI with natural gradients, memory-efficient implementation, strong real-data results.  
- Weaknesses: expressivity trade-offs for specific role interactions, potential unfairness in baseline comparisons, sensitivity to initialization, and limited hyperparameter robustness studies.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate datasets with heterophily, run a-MMSB comparators on equal hardware, report no-seed results and variance over inits, study hyperparameter sensitivity, and discuss theoretical effects of resampling A.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled scalability solution, novel parameter tying and structured SVI, strong empirical performance at scale.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited expressivity analysis, some uncontrolled comparisons, and minor theoretical/complexity inconsistencies needing clarification.
