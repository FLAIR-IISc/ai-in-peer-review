**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

The paper proposes a scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks. The authors introduce a parsimonious statistical model, called the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. The PTM is based on a triangular representation of networks, which has been shown to be effective in extracting informative mixed-membership roles from networks. The authors argue that their approach is the first to simultaneously achieve scalability and sufficient accuracy by incorporating three principles: succinct but informative representation of networks, parsimonious statistical modeling, and scalable and parallel inference algorithms.

The paper is related to previous work on latent space models for network analysis, such as the Mixed-Membership Stochastic Blockmodel (MMSB) and the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM). However, the authors claim that their approach is more scalable and accurate than previous methods, which are limited by their high computational complexity and inability to handle large numbers of latent roles.
