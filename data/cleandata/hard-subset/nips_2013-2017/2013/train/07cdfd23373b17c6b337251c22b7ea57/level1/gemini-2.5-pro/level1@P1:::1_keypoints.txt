- Problem: existing latent-space models (MMSB, MMTM) do not scale well with number of vertices N and roles K.  
- Solution principles: succinct network representation, parsimonious statistical model, and scalable inference.  
- Representation: uses "bag-of-triangles" (Ho et al. 2012) as a compact alternative to adjacency matrices.  
- Model: introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing.  
- Analogy: parameter-sharing in PTM is conceptually similar to a-MMSB’s O(K) reduction from O(K^2).  
- Inference: develops an SVI algorithm that processes mini-batches of triangles and uses natural gradients for faster convergence.  
- Key algorithmic novelty: a “mixture-of-deltas” O(K) approximation for local variational updates that avoids O(K^3) complexity.  
- Complexity: overall effective complexity is O(NK), enabling analysis of million-node networks with hundreds of roles in hours on one machine.  
- Empirics: comprehensive experiments against strong baselines (MMSB, MMTM Gibbs) on synthetic and large real datasets.  
- Experimental rigor: careful design including controls for initialization, demonstrating strong latent recovery, link prediction, and heldout likelihood.  
- Quality: technically high-quality, well-motivated model, and sound SVI derivation; polished implementation and results.  
- Significance: substantial advance in scaling probabilistic network models, with orders-of-magnitude speedups and practical impact.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written with clear framing, detailed descriptions, and informative figures/tables.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of prior ideas and key algorithmic novelty in the mixture-of-deltas approximation.  
- Weakness 1: the mixture-of-deltas local update is heuristic and lacks theoretical analysis or sensitivity studies on set A size.  
- Weakness 2: PTM is not a full generative model—it ignores singleton edges, empty dyads, and consistency across overlapping triangles.  
- Weakness 3: method depends on triangle motifs being informative; may underperform on networks where higher-order/non-local structures dominate.  
- Pro accept: tackles scaling, demonstrates SOTA performance, offers a practical recipe (parsimonious model + SVI), and has high impact potential.  
- Con accept: core ideas build on prior work (engineering/synthesis focus) and the O(K) approximation is heuristic without theoretical guarantees.  
- Recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
