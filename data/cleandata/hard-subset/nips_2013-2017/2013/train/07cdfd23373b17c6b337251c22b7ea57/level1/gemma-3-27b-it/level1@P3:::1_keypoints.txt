- Presents a scalable approach for probabilistic latent space inference on large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious model, and efficient stochastic variational inference.  
- Core contribution is the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter complexity dramatically compared to prior work (e.g., from O(K^3) to O(K)).  
- Introduces a fast stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm with a structured mean-field approximation for efficient inference.  
- Demonstrates significant speedups and competitive accuracy on synthetic and real networks, including processing a 1M-node YouTube network on a single machine.  
- Well-motivated paper that clearly identifies and addresses scalability bottlenecks in representation, modeling, and inference.  
- Using triangular motifs as a succinct representation is a strong, well-justified design choice with acknowledged trade-offs.  
- The PTM’s reduction in model complexity is a critical improvement enabling large-scale applicability.  
- The stochastic variational inference setup (structured mean-field + natural gradients) is well-explained and likely key to observed speedups.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against relevant baselines (MMSB, MMTM) and showing both speed and accuracy gains.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge limitations and explain design choices (e.g., subsampling parameter δ).  
- Needs stronger theoretical justification for why the structured mean-field outperforms naive mean-field and which dependencies it captures.  
- Should discuss and compare to other scalable inference techniques (e.g., ADVI, SGLD) in terms of theory, cost, and implementation.  
- Lacks a sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (α, λ, τ0, τ1, κ, δ) to assess robustness and failure regimes.  
- Link prediction methodology needs more detail on how marginalization over k is computed and its computational cost.  
- Would benefit from a more detailed computational complexity breakdown for motif extraction, local updates, and global updates.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant scalability and novelty merits acceptance with minor revisions to address the structured mean-field justification and additional methodological details.
