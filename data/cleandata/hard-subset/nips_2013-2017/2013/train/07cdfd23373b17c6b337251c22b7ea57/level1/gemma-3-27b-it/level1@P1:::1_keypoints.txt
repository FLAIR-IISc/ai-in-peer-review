- Addresses scalable probabilistic latent-space inference for very large networks (millions of nodes, hundreds of roles).  
- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation and parameter sharing.  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with natural gradients for scalable learning.  
- Demonstrates large runtime speedups over state-of-the-art while keeping competitive accuracy.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written; introduction and motivating principles are strong.  
- Technical formulation of PTM and inference is rigorous; design choices (parameter sharing, structured mean-field) are justified.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic validation and real-world large-network scalability tests.  
- Comparisons to baselines (MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM) are fair and informative.  
- Primary strength: scalability to networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of roles.  
- Novelty: combination of bag-of-triangles, parsimonious modeling, and stochastic VI is innovative.  
- Technical soundness: principled use of stochastic variational inference and natural gradients.  
- Evaluation breadth: includes both synthetic and multiple real datasets.  
- Clarity: accessible exposition even for non-experts.  
- Weakness: δ-subsampling parameter choice and its impact on accuracy lack a systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: structured mean-field approximation justification is mostly empirical; theoretical explanation is limited.  
- Weakness: link prediction evaluation is limited in metrics and comparison breadth (more metrics and recent baselines needed).  
- Weakness: sensitivity to initialization acknowledged but alternative strategies and effects are underexplored.  
- Weakness: missing discussion of related work in graph neural networks (GNNs) that learn latent node representations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a major scalability challenge, is novel and effective, and shows compelling experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need more rigorous justification of δ-subsampling, deeper analysis of structured mean-field, and stronger link-prediction evaluation.  
- Overall: strong contribution, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address δ-subsampling, structured mean-field explanation, link-prediction experiments, and a brief GNN-related discussion.
