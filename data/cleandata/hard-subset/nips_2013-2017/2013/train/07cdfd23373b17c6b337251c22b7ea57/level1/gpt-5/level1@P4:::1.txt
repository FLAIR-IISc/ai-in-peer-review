Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space model and inference method for large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation. Building on prior work that advocated triangle-based representations for mixed-membership role discovery (MMTM [8]), the authors introduce a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) that ties triangle-generating parameters across large subsets of role triplets, reducing parameter count from O(K^3) to O(K). They then derive a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with a structured mean-field family for per-triangle latent roles, and further an O(K)-time local update via a “mixture-of-deltas” approximation that samples a small set of off-diagonal role combinations. The method scales to networks with >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single multi-core machine and achieves competitive accuracy for latent role recovery and link prediction versus MMSB [1] and MMTM [8], while being orders of magnitude faster.

Compared to edge-based latent factor/block models (MMSB [1], a-MMSB [5]) that typically require O(N^2) data and O(K^2) parameters for role interactions, this work follows [8] in exploiting higher-order motifs (triangles) to reduce data size and improve structural signal. It complements SVI for Bayesian networks/mixture models [10, 20] by tailoring structured mean-field and natural gradients to the PTM, and relates to ERGM motivations for higher-order statistics [11, 16]. The work’s main novelty is the specific parameter tying across role triplets in triangles and the scalable structured SVI scheme.

Quality (technical soundness)
- Modeling: The PTM’s parameter sharing by equivalence classes of role triplets is well-defined and yields O(K) triangle-generating parameters: B0 (all roles distinct), {Bxx} (two same), and {Bxxx} (all same). This reduces variance and cost but also restricts expressivity (cannot distinguish among different minority-role combinations or specific heterophily patterns).
- Inference: The structured mean-field posterior over (si,jk, sj,ik, sk,ij) is appropriate (naive mean-field would be too restrictive). The SVI derivation follows standard practice: maximize a variational lower bound with exact local updates (intractable O(K^3)), and then approximate local updates with an O(K) mixture-of-deltas family. The latter produces a valid lower bound for the restricted family; however, the claimed unbiasedness of the stochastic natural gradient is rigorous only for the fixed variational family. Because A (the set of off-diagonals) is re-sampled each iteration, the algorithm effectively optimizes a sequence of changing bounds; convergence is empirically strong, but a short discussion of theoretical implications would improve rigor.
- Complexity: Global updates are O(NK). Local updates are O(K) per sampled triangle. With δ-subsampling yielding O(Nδ^2) triangles, total cost per pass is O(Nδ^2K). The paper occasionally states O(NK) complexity more broadly; clarifying per-pass vs. per-iteration costs and the role of δ would improve precision.
- Experiments: Synthetic studies use NMI for recovering non-zero roles and show clear gains in speed and robust accuracy. The initialization protocol (seeding 2% nodes with true roles for all methods) is controlled but optimistic; results without seeding would be informative. Comparisons to MMSB (batch VI) and MMTM/PTM Gibbs are fair in methodology, though single-threaded baselines versus a multi-threaded implementation favor the proposed approach. Real-data scaling results are compelling; however, the comparison to a-MMSB [5] is via reported times rather than reruns on the same hardware/datasets.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-organized. The description of equivalence classes and the resulting B-parameters is precise. Algorithm 1 succinctly summarizes SVI. Some derivations are deferred to the appendix (natural gradients, special-case φ updates), which is acceptable. Two clarity issues to address:
- Make complexity statements consistent and explicit about dependence on the number of triangles and δ.
- Clarify the implications of re-sampling A in the mixture-of-deltas local approximation on the bound and convergence guarantees.

Originality
The parameter tying for triangle-role configurations and the specific structured SVI with an O(K) local approximation appear novel relative to [8] and stochastic MMSB variants [5]. The combination of triangle-based representation, parsimonious parameterization, and scalable structured SVI is a meaningful contribution.

Significance
Scaling probabilistic latent-space inference to million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine is significant. The approach offers a practical alternative to edge-based models when triangle statistics are informative, and the method’s speed can broaden applicability in large-scale social network analysis. The expressivity trade-off should be acknowledged, but empirical results suggest strong utility.

Strengths
- Substantial scalability gains (orders of magnitude faster) with competitive or improved accuracy.
- Clean O(K) parameterization for triangle generation; structured SVI with natural gradients.
- Strong empirical evidence on large real networks; clear implementation details (minibatches, step sizes, parallelism).
- Memory efficiency by discarding local variational parameters between iterations.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Expressivity limits: Tying parameters across distinct minority roles may miss role-specific triangle patterns. Suggest evaluating on datasets with known heterophily/assortativity structure across role pairs.
- Fairness and breadth of baselines: Include a-MMSB [5] runs under comparable hardware and multi-threading. Consider non-probabilistic baselines prevalent later (if within scope), or justify exclusion.
- Initialization sensitivity: Provide results without seeding and report variance across random inits.
- Hyperparameter robustness: Study sensitivity to α, λ, δ, minibatch size, and step-size schedule.
- Convergence theory: Briefly discuss the effect of re-sampling A on the unbiasedness of gradients and convergence guarantees; if optimizing a changing bound, state this explicitly.
- Link prediction evaluation: Clarify computational cost of marginalizing over the third node, and consider additional metrics (e.g., precision-recall) and calibration.

Arguments for acceptance
- Addresses an important scalability bottleneck with a principled probabilistic approach.
- Novel and effective parameter tying plus structured SVI lead to practical O(Nδ^2K) inference.
- Strong empirical performance on both synthetic and million-scale real networks.

Arguments against acceptance
- Reduced expressivity may limit modeling of nuanced role interactions; analysis of this trade-off is limited.
- Some comparisons are not fully controlled (threading, absence of a-MMSB runs).
- Minor inconsistencies in complexity claims and limited discussion of theoretical convergence under the local approximation.

Overall, the paper makes a solid, well-executed contribution to scalable probabilistic network modeling, with clear potential impact.
