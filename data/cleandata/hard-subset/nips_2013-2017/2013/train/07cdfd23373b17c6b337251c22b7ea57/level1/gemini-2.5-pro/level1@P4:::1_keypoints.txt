- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) to scale triangle-based latent role models by reducing parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing.  
- Builds on "bag-of-triangles" representation from MMTM to avoid using the full adjacency matrix.  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with a structured mean-field approximation modeling dependencies within each triangle.  
- Introduces an O(K) approximation for local variational updates that samples role configurations to avoid O(K^3) cost.  
- Demonstrates ability to analyze networks with over a million nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine within hours.  
- Empirical evaluation is extensive and convincing: synthetic latent recovery, real-world link prediction, and large-scale scalability tests.  
- Significant speedups of several orders of magnitude over recent state-of-the-art methods (MMTM, a-MMSB).  
- Technical derivations (including use of natural gradients) and overall presentation are high quality and self-contained.  
- Authors are transparent about limitations of the triangle-based representation (not a fully generative network model).  
- Main novelty is the effective synthesis and engineering of existing ideas rather than a wholly new modeling paradigm.  
- The O(K) local-update approximation is a key novel technical contribution.  
- Important clarity issue: paper claims O(NK) complexity but true per-pass cost is O(Nδ^2 K), depending on sampled triangles and δ.  
- The O(NK) claim can be misleading for graphs where the number of triangles (or δ) is large and should be clarified.  
- Overall significance is very high because the method materially expands feasible scales for probabilistic network inference.
