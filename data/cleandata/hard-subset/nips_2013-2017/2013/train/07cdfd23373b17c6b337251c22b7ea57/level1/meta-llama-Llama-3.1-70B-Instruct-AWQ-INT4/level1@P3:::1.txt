**Summary**

The paper proposes a scalable approach for probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks. The authors introduce a parsimonious statistical model, called the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. The PTM represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs, reducing the dimensionality of the parameter space from O(K^3) to O(K), where K is the number of latent roles. The stochastic variational inference algorithm has a time complexity of O(NK), making it scalable to large networks.

**Strengths**

1. **Scalability**: The proposed approach can handle large-scale networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of latent roles, which is a significant improvement over existing methods.
2. **Efficient inference algorithm**: The stochastic variational inference algorithm is efficient and has a low time complexity, making it suitable for large-scale networks.
3. **Competitive accuracy**: The approach achieves competitive or improved accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction compared to existing methods.
4. **Theoretical soundness**: The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed approach, including a detailed derivation of the PTM and the stochastic variational inference algorithm.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The evaluation of the proposed approach is limited to a few synthetic and real-world networks. More extensive evaluation on diverse datasets is needed to fully assess the approach's performance.
2. **Simplifying assumptions**: The PTM makes simplifying assumptions about the network structure, such as ignoring empty and single-edge motifs. While these assumptions may be reasonable for some networks, they may not hold for others.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The paper does not provide a detailed discussion on hyperparameter tuning, which is crucial for the performance of the stochastic variational inference algorithm.
4. **Comparison to state-of-the-art methods**: While the paper compares the proposed approach to some existing methods, a more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods is needed to establish the approach's superiority.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks, which combines a parsimonious statistical model with an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. The approach is original and contributes to the existing literature on network modeling and inference.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for network analysis and modeling, particularly for large-scale networks. The proposed approach can be applied to various domains, such as social network analysis, recommendation systems, and link prediction.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and clear in its presentation. The authors provide a detailed derivation of the PTM and the stochastic variational inference algorithm, making it easy to follow for readers familiar with the subject matter.

**Quality**

The paper is of high quality, with a solid theoretical foundation and a well-designed evaluation. The authors provide a detailed discussion of the proposed approach, its strengths, and its limitations.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper for publication in NIPS, subject to minor revisions. The authors should address the limitations mentioned above, particularly the need for more extensive evaluation and a more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
