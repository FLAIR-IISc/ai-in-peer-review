- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), enabling scalable latent-space inference for very large networks.  
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm using a structured mean-field approximation and a mixture-of-deltas variational distribution for efficient inference.  
- Well-motivated paper with a clear overview of challenges and a thorough review of existing approaches and their limitations.  
- Experimental results show high accuracy recovering latent roles on synthetic data and strong real-world performance, outperforming MMSB Variational and MMTM Gibbs.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation, clear figures, and organized presentation of technical details.  
- Strength: Novel, scalable approach addressing large-scale latent-space inference with significant parameter reduction.  
- Strength: Well-designed, efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm.  
- Weakness: Assumes networks can be represented as a bag of triangular motifs, which may not always hold.  
- Weakness: Lacks thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method’s properties.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is focused on synthetic networks; needs more real-world experiments.  
- Originality: PTM and the SVI algorithm are novel contributions.  
- Significance: Addresses a major challenge with broad potential applications (social networks, recommender systems, community detection).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add theoretical analysis and more real-world experiments.  
- Overall rating: 9/10.
