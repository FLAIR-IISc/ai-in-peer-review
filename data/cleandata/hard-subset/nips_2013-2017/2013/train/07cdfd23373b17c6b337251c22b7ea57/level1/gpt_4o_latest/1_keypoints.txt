- Presents Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using a bag-of-triangles representation for scalable latent space inference in large networks.  
- Reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing and a triangular motif encoding.  
- Uses stochastic variational inference that scales linearly in vertices N and roles K, enabling million-node, hundred-role analyses on a single machine.  
- Empirically achieves competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link prediction versus state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strength: Exceptional scalability with time complexity O(NK), handling much larger networks than prior models.  
- Strength: Efficient inference converging in 2–5 data passes, faster than batch or Gibbs approaches.  
- Strength: Demonstrates competitive or superior accuracy on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength: Novel and effective parameter-sharing scheme that substantially reduces model complexity.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation showing scalability, accuracy, and robustness.  
- Weakness: Triangular representation discards single-edge and empty triples, potentially losing important network information.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to a fixed initialization scheme, which may not generalize without prior role knowledge.  
- Weakness: Limited baselines—lacks comparison to other scalable models such as graph neural networks.  
- Weakness: Not a fully generative model; does not enforce consistency across overlapping triangles.  
- Argument for acceptance: Tackles a critical scalability challenge with a well-designed model and efficient inference, yielding strong empirical results.  
- Argument against acceptance: Reliance on triangular motifs may limit generalizability to networks where edge-based or higher-order features matter, and comparisons to deep-learning approaches are missing.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant contribution to scalable network modeling with strong theory and practical utility, with limitations suggested for future work.
