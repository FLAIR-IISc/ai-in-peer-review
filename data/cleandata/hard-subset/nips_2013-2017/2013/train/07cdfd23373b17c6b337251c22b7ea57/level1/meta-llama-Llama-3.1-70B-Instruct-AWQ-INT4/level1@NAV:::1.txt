This paper proposes a scalable approach for making inference about latent spaces of large networks. The authors present a parsimonious statistical model, called the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. The PTM represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs, which allows for more efficient inference algorithms scalable to larger networks.

The authors demonstrate that their approach achieves competitive or improved accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction on synthetic and real networks, while being orders of magnitude faster than recent work on scalable latent space network modeling. The experiments show that the proposed method converges in a fraction of the time reported by recent work, achieving convergence in 2-5 data passes.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and efficient approach for latent space inference in large networks, which is a key challenge in network analysis.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method on both synthetic and real-world networks, demonstrating its effectiveness and scalability.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed approach and its advantages over existing methods.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a specific representation of networks as a bag of triangular motifs, which may not be applicable to all types of networks.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, which would be useful to assess the performance of their approach.
* The paper could benefit from more discussion on the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed approach.

Originality:

* The paper proposes a novel approach for latent space inference in large networks, which combines a parsimonious statistical model with an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm.
* The use of triangular motifs as a representation of networks is a new and interesting idea, which allows for more efficient inference algorithms.

Significance:

* The paper addresses a key challenge in network analysis, which is the scalability of latent space inference methods to large networks.
* The proposed approach has the potential to be applied to a wide range of network analysis tasks, such as community detection, link prediction, and exploratory analysis.
* The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach on real-world networks, which is an important step towards its practical application.
