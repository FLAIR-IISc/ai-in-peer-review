- Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method combining triangular motif representation, a parsimonious O(K) parameterization, and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm.
- Builds on Ho et al. (triangular representation) and a-MMSB but significantly improves scalability to N > 1M and K > 100.
- Empirically demonstrates analyzing a 1M-node YouTube network with 100 roles in ~4 hours on one machine, while competitors are much slower or infeasible.
- Strength — Quality: Technically sound with clear theory for parameter reduction and thorough experimental validation on synthetic and real networks.
- Strength — Clarity: Exceptionally well-written, organized, and reproducible with clear figures and sufficient appendix details.
- Strength — Originality: Novel O(K) parameter reduction from O(K^3) and a structured mean-field with O(K) local updates enabling large-K scaling.
- Strength — Significance: Addresses a key scalability bottleneck, enabling probabilistic latent-space models to be applied to massive real-world networks.
- Weakness — Quality: Theoretical justification for the specific parameter-sharing scheme (B0, Bxx, Bxxx) is insufficiently developed.
- Weakness — Clarity: The "mixture-of-deltas" approximation (Section 4.2) is described unclearly, risking reproducibility of the sampling mechanism.
- Weakness — Originality/context: Needs a clearer contrast with edge-based models and more explicit comparison to similarities with a-MMSB.
- Weakness — Significance/application: Real-world impact of the accuracy improvements on downstream tasks is not well contextualized or demonstrated.
- For acceptance: Significant, practical advance with sound methods and comprehensive experiments that enable new large-scale applications.
- Against acceptance: Narrow focus on latent-space inference may limit generality to other network-analysis tasks, though the problem is important.
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept with confidence; minor weaknesses are addressable in a camera-ready version.
