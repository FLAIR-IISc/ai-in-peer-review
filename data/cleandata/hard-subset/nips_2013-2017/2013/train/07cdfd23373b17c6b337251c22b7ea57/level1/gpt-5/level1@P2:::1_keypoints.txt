- Authors combine a bag-of-triangles representation, a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) with parameter sharing, and structured SVI with an O(K) mixture-of-deltas local variational family for scalable latent-role inference.
- PTM reduces triangle-generation parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via role-configuration partitioning (B0, Bxx, Bxxx), regaining tractability.
- The structured SVI uses per-triangle joint variational distributions and natural gradients, with unbiased stochastic gradients under triangle subsampling.
- The mixture-of-deltas local family yields O(K) local updates and preserves a valid variational lower bound, enabling inference on >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.
- Key novelties are the parameter-sharing scheme for triangles and the structured SVI that achieves O(K) local updates.
- Technical soundness is good: model specification and SVI derivation are clear; structured mean-field addresses high-rank posterior issues.
- Missing theoretical analysis: convergence behavior when the local variational support A is randomly resampled across iterations needs more rigor.
- Empirical ablations are lacking to quantify the gap between exact K^3 local updates and the O(K) approximation on the bound and performance.
- Synthetic experiments are well designed (MMSB-like and power-law generators) and scale K with N; seeding with 2% ground-truth roles equalizes initialization but is optimistic.
- Runtime and convergence comparisons per pass are fair and demonstrate substantial speedups; real-network wall-clock results strengthen claims.
- Link prediction and latent recovery are competitive or better than MMSB/MMTM on clustered social graphs, but more baselines are needed.
- Limitations: not a full generative model of networks; bag-of-triangles discards dyads outside triangles and treats overlapping triangles independently.
- PTM’s shared parameters (B0, Bxx) trade expressiveness for scalability and may miss fine-grained inter-role heterogeneity.
- Applicability may be limited for low-transitivity or non-triadic graphs; robustness outside social networks is untested.
- Clarity is good overall, but complexity claims should explicitly distinguish O(NK) vs. O(Nδ^2K) per pass, and convergence theory for changing local support should be addressed.
- Originality is moderately high: builds on prior triangular and mixed-membership work but introduces novel parameter sharing and scalable structured SVI.
- Significance is high in practice: enables hundred-role inference on million-node networks efficiently, especially for highly clustered graphs.
- Pros: elegant parameter sharing, simple parallelizable structured SVI, strong scalability, and competitive accuracy with clear exposition of assumptions.
- Cons: lack of theoretical guarantees for mixture-of-deltas with random A, reliance on seeded initializations, limited baselines, untested on low-clustering graphs, and constrained expressiveness.
- Suggested improvements: ablations comparing exact vs. approximate local updates, experiments on low-transitivity networks, random/data-driven initializations, comparison to nonprobabilistic baselines, and clearer complexity/memory breakdowns.
- Overall assessment: solid, timely contribution with practical impact and some theoretical/empirical gaps; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
