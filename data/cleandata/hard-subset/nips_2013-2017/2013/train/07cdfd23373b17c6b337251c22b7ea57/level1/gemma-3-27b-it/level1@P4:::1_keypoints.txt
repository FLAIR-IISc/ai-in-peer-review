- Combines bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious triangular model (PTM), and stochastic variational inference for scalable latent-space inference.
- Demonstrates scalability to networks with over a million nodes and hundreds of latent roles, with substantial runtime reductions.
- Reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) (prior triangular models) to O(K) via parameter sharing in PTM.
- Achieves time complexity of O(NK) with the proposed stochastic variational inference algorithm.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real networks show competitive accuracy while greatly improving speed, outperforming MMSB/MMTM baselines.
- Builds on prior latent-space and motif-based models (e.g., Ho et al. NIPS 2013) and situates contributions clearly relative to existing work.
- Theoretical grounding is solid, using established probabilistic modeling (Dirichlet priors, variational inference) and clear algorithmic justification.
- Paper is generally well-written and well-organized, with a clear problem statement and solution outline.
- The stochastic variational inference details and approximations (mixtures of deltas) are intricate and may be hard for some readers to follow.
- Hyperparameter selection (α, λ, step sizes) lacks thorough sensitivity analysis and detailed justification.
- Method shows sensitivity to initialization (requires seeding with correctly assigned nodes), raising practical concerns.
- Strong parameter-sharing assumptions in PTM may not hold in all scenarios and need further discussion of limitations.
- Choice of a 10% mini-batch and its impact on convergence and accuracy is not well justified.
- Overall assessment: significant, novel, and practical contribution with strong empirical validation; minor concerns about complexity, initialization, and hyperparameter discussion do not outweigh the paper’s merits.
