- Addresses scalable latent space inference for networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of latent roles.  
- Builds on MMSB, MMTM, and scalable a-MMSB, which do not scale well to very large networks.  
- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter space from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing.  
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm that yields O(NK) complexity.  
- Demonstrates ability to analyze networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of roles in hours on a single machine.  
- Shows orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over prior methods (e.g., MMSB needing days for ~100k nodes).  
- Thorough experimental validation on synthetic and real networks using NMI, link prediction, and runtime metrics.  
- Achieves competitive or improved accuracy while providing substantial scalability gains.  
- Presents a clear generative model and well-justified parameter reduction strategy.  
- Provides theoretical justification and carefully positions the work relative to prior NIPS contributions.  
- The stochastic variational inference uses an effective O(K) approximation for local updates that preserves performance.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and contains sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Effectively addresses three principles for large-scale inference: succinct representation (triangles), parsimonious modeling (PTM), and scalable inference.  
- Comprehensive comparisons to relevant baselines (MMSB, MMTM Gibbs, PTM Gibbs) across network sizes and structures.  
- Strength: novel parameter reduction enabling O(NK) complexity.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation demonstrating both accuracy and scalability.  
- Strength: clear demonstration of practical applicability to real-world networks.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of scenarios where O(K) parameterization is insufficient versus more flexible models.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of information loss from the triangular motif representation.  
- Weakness: scope could be expanded beyond network-analysis-specific tasks to show broader applicability.  
- Novelty: significant technical contribution (O(K^3) → O(K)) and a novel SVI tailored to the model.  
- Significance: high—enables large-scale applications in social, recommendation, and biological networks.  
- Recommendation rationale: technically sound, experimentally validated, and aligned with NIPS interests; some concerns about model flexibility and motif information loss.
