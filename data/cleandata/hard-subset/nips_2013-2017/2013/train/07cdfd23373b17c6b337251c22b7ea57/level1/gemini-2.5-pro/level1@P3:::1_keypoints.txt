- Proposed method: scalable probabilistic latent-space inference using a bag-of-triangles representation (MMTM), a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), and an SVI algorithm with an O(K) local update enabling analysis of ~1.1M nodes and K=100 roles in hours.

- Strength — Significance & Scalability: tackles a hard scaling problem, achieves orders-of-magnitude faster inference (e.g., K=100 on 1.1M nodes in ~4 hours) vs prior methods.

- Strength — Novelty & Technical Quality: PTM is a well-justified parameter reduction and the structured mean-field SVI plus O(K) local update is a creative, non-trivial contribution.

- Strength — Clarity & Organization: paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and well-structured.

- Strength — Empirical Evaluation: comprehensive experiments (synthetic recovery, link prediction, large-scale runtimes) convincingly show speed and competitive or improved accuracy.

- Weakness — Approximate Local Update Justification: claims that the O(K) approximation matches the full update need direct comparisons (lower bound, convergence, task metrics) and sensitivity analysis to random off-diagonal choices.

- Weakness — Triangle Representation Limits: PTM ignores single-edge and empty triples, so it may perform poorly on low-clustering or tree-like networks and for tasks relying on bridge edges.

- Weakness — Comparison with a-MMSB: runtime comparisons are indirect and could be affected by implementation/hardware; a direct empirical comparison and qualitative discussion of edge- vs triangle-based latent structure are needed.

- Weakness — Link Prediction Complexity: prediction appears to require summing over all other nodes for each candidate link (O(N)), so clarify prediction-time complexity and practicality at large N.

- Summary — Quality: High (technically sound with strong empirical support).

- Summary — Clarity: Excellent.

- Summary — Originality: High (novel synthesis and scalable inference innovations).

- Summary — Significance: Very high (practical, scalable advance likely to be widely used).
