- Problem and approach: Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using bag-of-triangles, parameter sharing (Bxxx, Bxx, B0) to reduce triad parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Inference method: Uses structured variational posteriors, SVI with natural gradients, mixture-of-deltas local approximation, and δ-subsampling to achieve O(K) local updates.  
- Empirical results: Strong latent recovery on synthetic data, competitive or better link prediction on real networks, major speedups and scalability to ~1M nodes and K≈100 on a single multi-core machine.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Ho et al. (triangles), MMTM, and borrows a-MMSB parameter-sharing and SVI ideas; main novelty is the specific O(K) triad parameterization and scalable structured SVI.  
- Technical soundness: Model and variational scheme are well-defined and ELBO-valid; complexity claims are plausible; authors note PTM is not a full generative graph model.  
- Empirical evaluation quality: Thorough synthetic experiments and runtime evidence, but lacks direct head-to-head comparisons with a-MMSB on the same datasets and K.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Standard SVI convergence holds, but no theoretical analysis of errors from δ-subsampling or mixture-of-deltas truncation—only empirical justification.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and organized; some derivations deferred and mixture-of-deltas/A sampling would benefit from pseudocode and ablation details.  
- Originality: While motifs and parameter sharing have precedents, the O(K) triad parameterization and tailored structured SVI with O(K) local updates appear novel.  
- Significance: Enables scalable motif-based mixed-membership inference, allowing analysis of much larger networks; expressivity trade-offs (e.g., B0) need discussion.  
- Strengths: Significant parameter reduction (O(K^3→O(K)), efficient structured SVI and engineering, strong empirical scalability and competitive accuracy.  
- Weaknesses — comparisons: No direct experimental comparison to a-MMSB, only indirect timing reference (Brightkite).  
- Weaknesses — model expressivity: Sharing via B0 and Bxx may underfit nuanced distinct-role triad interactions.  
- Weaknesses — approximations: δ-subsampling and mixture-of-deltas lack theoretical error analysis and limited ablation quantification.  
- Weaknesses — initialization & reproducibility: Reliance on 2% seeded initialization in synth; details on link prediction scoring, negative sampling, and memory usage need clarification.  
- Suggestions: Add head-to-head a-MMSB comparisons, ablations (full VI vs mixture-of-deltas, varying A and δ, sensitivity to K/step-size), analyze subsampling bias, report memory/per-iteration cost, and release code.  
- Broader impact: Well-aligned with NIPS themes; methodological and practical contributions likely useful to the community.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel and practical O(K) parameterization, scalable structured SVI, strong empirical scalability and competitive accuracy.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Missing direct scalable-baseline comparisons, potential underfitting due to parameter tying, and limited analysis of approximation errors.  
- Overall recommendation: Leaning toward acceptance, contingent on addressing comparative and ablation/analysis gaps.
