- Summary: Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and a stochastic variational inference algorithm that cuts parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) and runs in O(NK), enabling scalable latent-space inference for large networks.

- Strength — Scalability: Method handles networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of roles, outperforming prior scalability limits.

- Strength — Efficient inference: Stochastic variational algorithm is computationally efficient with low time complexity suitable for large graphs.

- Strength — Competitive accuracy: Achieves comparable or better latent recovery and link prediction than existing methods.

- Strength — Theoretical soundness: Solid theoretical derivation of PTM and the inference algorithm.

- Weakness — Limited evaluation: Experiments are limited to a few synthetic and real datasets; broader testing is needed.

- Weakness — Simplifying assumptions: PTM ignores empty and single-edge motifs, which may not hold for all network types.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter tuning: Paper lacks detailed guidance on tuning critical hyperparameters for inference.

- Weakness — Incomplete comparisons: Needs more comprehensive benchmarking against state-of-the-art methods.

- Originality: Novel combination of a parsimonious motif-based model with scalable stochastic variational inference for large-scale network modeling.

- Significance: Potentially impactful for network analysis tasks (social networks, recommendation, link prediction) at large scale.

- Clarity: Well-written with clear, detailed derivations accessible to knowledgeable readers.

- Quality: High-quality manuscript with strong theoretical foundation and a decent empirical evaluation.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand evaluation and comparisons and address limitations.

- Pro (for acceptance): Novel scalable approach, competitive accuracy, and significant practical implications.

- Con (against acceptance): Limited dataset coverage, structural simplifying assumptions, and missing hyperparameter details.
