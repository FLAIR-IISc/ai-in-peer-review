This paper proposes a scalable probabilistic approach for latent space inference on large networks by (i) representing graphs as a bag of triangular motifs, (ii) introducing a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that shares parameters across role configurations to reduce triangle-generation parameters from O(K3) to O(K), and (iii) developing a structured stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with natural gradients and an O(K) local-update approximation. The method targets million-node networks and hundreds of roles, reporting substantial speedups over MMSB/a-MMSB and MMTM with competitive or improved accuracy in latent role recovery and link prediction. The work builds on and relates to MMSB [Airoldi et al.], a-MMSB [Gopalan et al., NeurIPS], motif-based triangular modeling (MMTM) [Ho et al., NeurIPS], ERGM statistics, and SVI/natural gradients [Hoffman et al.; Amari].

Quality: The modeling choice to group triangle role configurations by equivalence classes is well-motivated and yields a coherent PTM likelihood. The structured mean-field plus stochastic natural gradient is standard and appropriate. The O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” local approximation is pragmatic, though its approximation error is not quantified. Empirical results are extensive, but some comparisons (e.g., link prediction scoring based on triangles vs. MMSB edge probabilities) may not be strictly apples-to-apples. The reliance on seeded initialization for synthetic data could bias recovery comparisons; robustness to unseeded starts is only briefly discussed.

Clarity: The paper is generally clear and well-organized. The description of parameter sharing and induced equivalence classes is helpful; still, a compact figure mapping role configurations to parameters would aid readability. Some algorithmic details are relegated to the appendix; adding a concise pseudo-code block for the local/global updates would help replication.

Originality: The key novelty is bringing O(K) parameterization to triangle-based latent role models and coupling it with scalable SVI. Parameter sharing ideas echo a-MMSB, but the combination with motif-based modeling and the O(K) local approximation is original and practically impactful.

Significance: Strong. Scaling to 1M nodes and 100 roles on a single machine is noteworthy, and the demonstrated speedups over a-MMSB/MMTM are compelling. The approach should be of interest to practitioners working on large-scale graph analysis.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear modeling advance: O(K) parameterization and efficient SVI for triangle-based models.
- Demonstrated scalability with competitive accuracy on synthetic and real graphs.
- Careful engineering (mini-batching, structured mean-field, natural gradients) and parallelism.

Cons against acceptance:
- Limited theoretical analysis of the O(K) local approximation’s bias/variance and identifiability.
- Seeding on synthetic data; limited robustness analysis to initialization and δ-subsampling.
- Missing comparisons to strong non-probabilistic baselines (e.g., DeepWalk/node2vec) for link prediction and to recent scalable GNN embeddings.
- Wall-clock comparisons mix thread counts; per-core-normalized reporting would strengthen claims.

Suggestions: Add ablations for structured vs. naive mean-field and for the mixture-of-deltas size; evaluate unseeded initialization; compare against popular embedding baselines; provide per-core timing and memory profiles; and analyze sensitivity to δ and K.
