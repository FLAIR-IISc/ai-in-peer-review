

This paper proposes a scalable approach for latent space inference of large networks using a "bag of triangular motifs" representation. The authors address three key scalability challenges: (1) succinct network representation, (2) parsimonious statistical modeling, and (3) scalable inference algorithms. They build on the triangular representation from [8] but introduce a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) that reduces the parameter space from O(K^3) to O(K) through clever parameter sharing, and develop a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity.

The paper's main contribution is the PTM's parameter sharing strategy, which enables linear scaling in the number of latent roles K. This is a significant improvement over the MMTM model [8] which has O(K^3) parameters. The stochastic variational inference algorithm implements a structured mean-field approximation that captures dependencies better than naive mean-field, with convergence in 2-5 data passes.

Strengths:
- The paper clearly articulates the scalability challenges in large network inference with compelling examples of existing methods' limitations.
- The theoretical justification for the PTM's parameter reduction is sound and well-explained.
- Experimental results are impressive, demonstrating orders of magnitude speedup over state-of-the-art methods (e.g., 4-hour runtime on 1M-node Youtube network versus days for competitors).
- Complexity analysis is thorough, showing O(NK) inference versus O(N^2K^2) for MMSB [1] and O(Nδ^2K^3) for MMTM [8].
- The structured mean-field approach is well-motivated and performs better than naive mean-field.

Weaknesses:
- The paper lacks strong theoretical justification for why triangular motifs are sufficient for capturing network structure. While they reference network motifs [15], they don't demonstrate how this representation captures key network properties better than edge-based representations.
- The "mixture-of-deltas" approximation for the local update (Section 4) is ad-hoc and lacks theoretical grounding. The paper claims it "works as well as the full parameterization" but provides no evidence of approximation error.
- The comparison with a-MMSB [5] is limited. While they mention a-MMSB's downsampling approach, they don't explain why their triangular representation is better for large networks, especially since a-MMSB also addresses scalability through downsampling.
- The paper doesn't discuss the interpretability of the learned latent roles, which is crucial for network analysis applications.

Originality: The parameter sharing strategy in PTM is novel in the context of triangular motif-based network models, though similar ideas appear in a-MMSB [5] for edge probabilities. The novelty is incremental but valuable for triangular motif models.

Significance: The method enables analysis of networks with 1M+ nodes and hundreds of latent roles in hours, which is a major practical contribution. This could significantly impact applications in social network analysis, recommendation systems, and other domains where network size has been a barrier.

Quality: The technical content is sound with clear theoretical arguments and well-executed experiments. However, the paper would benefit from more ablation studies to understand the impact of the parameter sharing strategy and the local update approximation.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and organized. The technical details are clear, though the motivation for the parameter sharing strategy could be more intuitive.

Arguments for acceptance: Solves a significant real-world scalability problem with a technically sound approach, demonstrated by impressive results. The method is well-motivated, the technical approach is solid, and the results are compelling.

Arguments against acceptance: The theoretical justification for the triangular representation is weak, the local update approximation lacks grounding, and the comparison with a-MMSB could be more comprehensive.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to scalable network analysis. The weaknesses noted are not deal-breakers for a NIPS paper, but addressing them would strengthen the submission. The demonstrated scalability to 1M+ node networks with hundreds of latent roles represents a significant advancement in the field.

Recommendation: Accept
