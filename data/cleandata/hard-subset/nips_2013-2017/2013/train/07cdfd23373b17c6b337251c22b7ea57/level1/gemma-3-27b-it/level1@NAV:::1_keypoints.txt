- Proposes a scalable probabilistic latent space inference method for large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation and parameter sharing.
- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Uses stochastic variational inference for efficient estimation and scalable learning.
- Demonstrates ability to analyze networks with over a million vertices and hundreds of latent roles.
- Experimental results show competitive or improved accuracy while being orders of magnitude faster than prior methods.
- Provides clear explanation of accuracy–scalability trade-offs, ablation studies, and comparisons to existing techniques.
- Includes convergence analysis and runtime benchmarks on real-world networks.
- Technically sound with strong theoretical grounding and thorough evaluation on synthetic and real data.
- Paper lacks a detailed discussion of limitations, potential biases, and information loss from using triangular motifs.
- Initialization relies on seeding with ground-truth roles in experiments, which is unrealistic for many real-world scenarios.
- Appendix contains synthetic data details, but the main text should more clearly explain the network generation process.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality work with strengths outweighing weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
