- Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method using triangular motifs and a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) and uses structured SVI with an O(K) local approximation.  
- Builds on mixed-membership blockmodels, motif-based statistics, and stochastic variational inference, extending Ho et al. (NIPS’12) triangular representation with a simpler parameterization.  
- PTM’s parameter sharing (Bxxx, Bxx, B0) is well-motivated, yields only O(K) Dirichlet parameters, and plausibly improves estimation and stability.  
- Structured variational family couples triangle latent variables to avoid high-rank posterior failures of naive mean-field; natural-gradient SVI and minibatches are appropriate.  
- Exact local updates are O(K^3); the “mixture-of-deltas” approximation reduces this to O(K), but its effect on gradient variance from resampling A is not analyzed.  
- Model is not a full generative model (triangles may be inconsistent; singles/empties omitted); δ-subsampling for high-degree nodes is claimed to preserve triangle distributions but induced bias is not quantified.  
- Synthetic experiments vary N and K well and measure latent recovery appropriately, but all methods are seeded with 2% ground-truth which is a strong supervision signal.  
- Real-data evaluation compares only to MMSB; missing comparisons to a-MMSB and MMTM (at smaller K) and unclear link-prediction metrics (AUC/precision@k not specified).  
- Paper is generally clear and organized, but notation is heavy; a figure for equivalence classes, main-text update forms, and formal link-score definition would improve clarity and reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel application of parameter-sharing to triangle motifs and an SVI scheme that scales in N and K, analogous in spirit to a-MMSB but distinct in motif focus.  
- Significance: scalable inference for 100 roles on million-node graphs is impactful, but PTM’s aggressive sharing limits expressivity for role-specific asymmetries and minority-role heterogeneity.  
- Suggested experiments/analyses: compare to a-MMSB/MMTM, study δ-subsampling bias and variance, report link-prediction metrics and baselines (degree-corrected SBM, spectral methods), analyze identifiability/role collapse, and quantify variance from the O(K) local approximation.  
- Pros: strong scalability story, clear modeling innovation, structured variational approach, and competitive accuracy on synthetic and real data.  
- Cons: limited comparative evaluation, potential underfitting from parameter sharing, unquantified biases/variance from approximations/subsampling, and limited applicability beyond link prediction.  
- Overall: well-motivated and carefully engineered contribution that materially advances scalable probabilistic latent inference; leans toward acceptance pending stronger baselines and further analysis.
