- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) representing networks as a bag of triangular motifs for latent-space inference.  
- PTM reduces parameter count by sharing parameters within groups of latent roles (parsimonious modeling).  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) time complexity.  
- Algorithm scales to networks with over a million vertices and hundreds of roles on a single machine in hours.  
- Approach is related to MMSB/MMTM but distinct by combining triangle-based representation, parameter sharing, and scalable inference.  
- Strength: provides a scalable solution to latent-space inference for very large networks.  
- Strength: PTM is more parameter-efficient than prior models due to role-group sharing.  
- Strength: inference algorithm is computationally efficient and practically scalable.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., a-MMSB).  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation limited to synthetic data and a small set of real networks.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of how PTM results support tasks like community detection and link prediction.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, scalable model and efficient inference with practical large-scale applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical evaluation, missing baseline comparisons, and insufficient interpretability/application details.  
- Overall reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
