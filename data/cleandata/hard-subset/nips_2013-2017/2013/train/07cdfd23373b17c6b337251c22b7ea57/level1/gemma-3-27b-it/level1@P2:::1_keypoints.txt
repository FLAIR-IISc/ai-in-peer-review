- Presents a scalable approach combining a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious statistical model, and stochastic variational inference for large networks.  
- Claims to overcome scalability limits of prior methods (representation, modeling, inference) while maintaining competitive accuracy.  
- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), reducing parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing across latent roles.  
- Employs stochastic variational inference with a structured mean-field approximation and natural gradients to efficiently exploit model structure.  
- Experimental results show orders-of-magnitude speedups on synthetic and real-world networks, including networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of roles.  
- Thorough empirical validation with ablation studies, multiple baselines, and clear reporting of hyperparameters and compute resources.  
- Strength — Scalability: demonstrably handles very large networks and many latent roles.  
- Strength — Novelty: effective combination of triangular representation, parsimonious modeling, and stochastic VI.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: well-motivated design, clear algorithmic details, and use of natural gradients.  
- Strength — Empirical robustness: strong comparisons to state-of-the-art and comprehensive experiments.  
- Strength — Clarity and reproducibility: well-written with sufficient implementation details.  
- Weakness — Triangular representation may discard information; need deeper discussion of its impact on certain network structures or tasks.  
- Weakness — Potential sensitivity to hyperparameters; more analysis and guidance on tuning is needed.  
- Weakness — Limited justification/exploration of the chosen structured mean-field versus alternative structures.  
- Weakness — No comparison to modern Graph Neural Network approaches (e.g., GraphSAGE, GAT).  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant, well-supported contribution with high potential impact despite noted limitations.
