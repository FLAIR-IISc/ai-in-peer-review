- Proposes scalable latent-space inference for large networks using a "bag of triangular motifs" representation.
- Addresses three scalability challenges: succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference.
- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing.
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity and 2–5 data-pass convergence.
- Main technical contribution: PTM’s parameter-sharing strategy enabling linear scaling in latent roles K.
- Uses a structured mean-field approximation that captures dependencies better than naive mean-field.
- Experiments show orders-of-magnitude speedups (e.g., 4 hours on 1M-node YouTube network versus days for competitors).
- Complexity analysis compares favorably to prior work: O(NK) vs O(N^2K^2) for MMSB and O(Nδ^2K^3) for MMTM.
- Strength: Clear articulation of scalability challenges and sound theoretical justification for parameter reduction.
- Weakness: Weak theoretical justification for why triangular motifs alone sufficiently capture network structure.
- Weakness: The "mixture-of-deltas" local update approximation is ad-hoc and lacks analysis of approximation error.
- Weakness: Limited comparison with a-MMSB and insufficient explanation why triangular representation outperforms downsampling approaches.
- Weakness: Paper omits discussion of interpretability of learned latent roles, important for applications.
- Quality: Technical content and experiments are solid, but more ablation studies are needed to assess design choices.
- Originality: Parameter sharing in PTM is novel for triangular motif models but incrementally relates to a-MMSB ideas.
- Significance: Enables practical analysis of 1M+ node networks with hundreds of roles—major applied impact.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized, though the intuition behind parameter sharing could be clearer.
- Recommendation: Accept.
