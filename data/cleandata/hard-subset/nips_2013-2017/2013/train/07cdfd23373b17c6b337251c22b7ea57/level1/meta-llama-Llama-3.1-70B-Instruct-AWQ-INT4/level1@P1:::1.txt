This paper proposes a scalable approach for making inference about latent spaces of large networks. The authors represent networks as a bag of triangular motifs and develop a parsimonious statistical model, called the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which reduces the number of parameters by sharing parameters within certain groups of latent roles. They also develop an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm for performing approximate inference under the PTM. The algorithm has a time complexity of O(NK) and is capable of analyzing real networks with over a million vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine in a matter of hours.

The main ideas of this paper are related to previous work on latent space models for network analysis, such as the Mixed-Membership Stochastic Blockmodel (MMSB) and the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM). However, the authors' approach is distinct in that it combines a succinct representation of networks as a bag of triangular motifs with a parsimonious statistical model and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. This allows their method to scale to much larger networks than previous approaches.

The strengths of this paper include:

* The authors' development of a scalable approach for making inference about latent spaces of large networks, which is a key challenge in network analysis.
* The PTM is a parsimonious statistical model that reduces the number of parameters by sharing parameters within certain groups of latent roles, making it more efficient than previous approaches.
* The stochastic variational inference algorithm is efficient and has a time complexity of O(NK), making it capable of analyzing large networks in a matter of hours.

The weaknesses of this paper include:

* The authors do not provide a thorough comparison of their method with other state-of-the-art approaches, such as the a-MMSB algorithm.
* The experimental evaluation is limited to synthetic data and a few real-world networks, and more extensive experiments are needed to fully evaluate the performance of the method.
* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how the PTM can be used for tasks such as community detection and link prediction, and more details are needed on how to interpret the results of the method.
