- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) for scalable latent-space inference in massive networks.  
- Reduces model complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) by sharing parameters across latent role configurations.  
- Employs a structured mean-field stochastic variational inference with a novel O(K) local update for efficiency.  
- Achieves practical scalability to million-node graphs with hundreds of roles, running in hours on a single machine.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedup over prior methods while maintaining suitability for very large graphs.  
- Empirical evaluation shows competitive or superior latent-space recovery and link-prediction accuracy versus MMSB and MMTM.  
- Originality lies in synthesizing triangular representations, parameter sharing, and stochastic inference into a novel system.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly positions its contributions.  
- Limitation: framework is not strictly generative for networks—collections of triangles may not form a valid graph.  
- Limitation: the O(K) local update approximation is primarily empirically justified and lacks deeper theoretical motivation or sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper with strong practical contribution; scalability benefits outweigh minor limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: large speedup, novel effective combination of techniques, strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: non-generative modeling limitation and limited justification of the O(K) approximation.
