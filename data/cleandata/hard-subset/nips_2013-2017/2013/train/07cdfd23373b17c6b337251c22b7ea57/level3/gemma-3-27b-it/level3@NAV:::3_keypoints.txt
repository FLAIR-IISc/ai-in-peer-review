- Presents a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference method using a bag-of-triangles, a parsimonious triangular model (PTM), and stochastic variational inference.
- Core contribution: enables analysis of networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine.
- Scalability: moves from edge-based to triangle-based representation and uses parameter sharing in PTM to reduce complexity, achieving linear scaling in K.
- Uses stochastic variational inference for fast convergence on large networks.
- Empirical results: strong performance on synthetic and real datasets, faster than a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.) and MMTM Gibbs sampler.
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and tackles an important, challenging problem.
- Terminology: “parsimonious modeling” needs clearer definition or unpacking for readers.
- Misleading claim: assertion that down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy should be revised in light of Gopalan et al.’s findings.
- Conceptual flaw: the LDA analogy is flawed since the triangle model can generate samples that are not valid networks.
- Theoretical grounding: cite Hoffman et al. alongside Amari and Sato for natural gradient methods; Sato’s algorithm does not directly apply with local variables.
- Experimental detail: provide a clearer description of how power-law networks were generated in the latent space.
- Baselines: include direct comparison with Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB algorithm (not just batch MMSB) for a more complete benchmark.
- Manuscript edits: remove the unnecessary last sentence on page 8 and update bibliography to cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference.”
- Arguments for acceptance: significant scalability improvement, strong experimental validation, clear organization, and relevance to network analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: minor terminological/factual inaccuracies, need for stronger theoretical citations, and more comprehensive experimental baselines.
