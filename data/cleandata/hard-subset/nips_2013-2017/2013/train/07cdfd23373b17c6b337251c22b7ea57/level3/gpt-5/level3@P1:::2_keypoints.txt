- Proposal: Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that models graphs as bags of triangles and ties role-triplet configurations into three equivalence classes (Bxxx, Bxx, B0), reducing parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Inference: Develops a structured mean-field SVI with natural gradients and a “mixture-of-deltas” local approximation to achieve O(K) time per triangle instead of O(K^3).
- Empirical results: Reports orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMSB/a-MMSB and MMTM with comparable or better latent role recovery and link prediction, scaling to a 1M-node graph with K=100 in ~4 hours on one machine.
- Relation to prior work: Combines triangle-based modeling (MMTM) with parameter tying ideas (a-MMSB) and SVI, with novelty in the equivalence-class parameterization and efficient O(K) updates.
- Strength: Clear modeling that preserves triangle benefits while removing the O(K^3) bottleneck via principled parameter sharing.
- Strength: Practical, well-engineered SVI algorithm using natural gradients, mini-batching, and the mixture-of-deltas approximation for efficient local updates.
- Strength: Strong scalability (O(NK) global updates, convergence in 2–5 passes) demonstrated on large real datasets; good parallelization and low memory footprint.
- Strength: Empirical performance competitive or superior to baselines, with substantial runtime improvements.
- Weakness: ERGM analogy is overstated; PTM is not a full generative ERGM counterpart and the claim should be softened and clarified.
- Weakness: No discussion of directed, signed, or weighted graphs and whether equivalence-class tying extends to directed 3-node motifs.
- Weakness: Notation clarity issues (∆1, ∆2 not explained); mapping of Bxxx,1/2 and Bxx,1/2/3 to motif events should be explicit.
- Weakness: Subsampling rationale (δ-based node-centric triangle subsampling) is brief; needs concise argument or empirical sensitivity analysis, especially for heavy-tailed degrees.
- Weakness: Interpretability is limited—paper should include qualitative analyses (top nodes per role, metadata correlation, motif composition) to demonstrate what roles capture.
- Weakness: Evaluation choices (threshold θ̂ = 0.125) need sensitivity analysis or calibrated alternatives (AUC, KL divergence); consider sparse priors or feature-based approaches.
- Weakness: Synthetic “power-law latent space” simulator is unspecified; provide precise model or citation for reproducibility.
- Weakness: Complexity confusion—clarify that PTM has O(K) parameters but collapsed Gibbs can be O(K^3) per triangle, whereas proposed SVI achieves O(K) updates.
- Weakness: Robustness to missingness is limited—recommend testing higher holdout rates (50–90%), cold-start nodes, and sparser observation regimes.
- Weakness: Counterintuitive result of PTM beating MMSB on MMSB-generated graphs needs ablation (vary K, α, thresholds) and discussion of plausible causes.
- Quality: Methods (structured mean-field SVI, natural gradients, mixture-of-deltas) are reasonable and empirically supported; stronger theoretical justification for subsampling and approximation quality is needed.
- Empirical support: Extensive size and runtime experiments, but would benefit from additional sensitivity and interpretability analyses.
- Clarity: Overall organized but needs clearer exposition of ∆-notation, equivalence classes, subsampling rationale, and a figure/table mapping classes and computational complexity.
- Originality: Moderate—combines existing ideas (triangles, parameter tying, SVI) but contributes a specific equivalence-class parameterization and efficient O(K) local update.
- Significance: High practical significance for scalable role/community discovery and link prediction on million-node graphs using a single machine.
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong scalability, clear algorithmic innovations, and comprehensive empirical validation that will be useful to practitioners.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited theoretical treatment of subsampling/approximation, missing experiments/extensions (directed/weighted, higher missingness, interpretability), and presentation issues.
- Recommendation: Accept conditioned on revisions to clarify notation and claims, add brief sensitivity analyses (thresholding, δ), and include discussion/experiments on directed extensions and interpretability.
