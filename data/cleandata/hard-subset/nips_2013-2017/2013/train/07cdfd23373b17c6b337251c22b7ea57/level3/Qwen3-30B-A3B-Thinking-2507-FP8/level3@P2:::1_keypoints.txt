- Paper proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing triangle-parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) with a stochastic variational inference algorithm of O(NK) complexity.  
- Demonstrated scalability: analyzes networks with >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine in hours, outperforming prior methods.  
- Builds on triangular motif representation to avoid O(N^2) edges by using O(ND^2) motifs and introduces strategic parameter sharing for triangle generation.  
- Technical approach is sound, theoretically founded, and experiments are well-designed, but several critical investigations are missing.  
- Key missing experiment: no comparison of PTM vs. MMTM performance as a function of K to validate benefits of large-K parameter reduction.  
- Lacks analysis of scenarios where PTM assumptions fail (e.g., true triangle parameters require O(K^3) distinct values).  
- Probabilistic validity concern: bag-of-triangles representation may not correspond to any real network distribution, potentially making likelihood ill-defined.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and reproducible with precise algorithm descriptions.  
- Originality is modest: parameter-sharing scheme is novel but overall represents an incremental advance over prior triangular approaches.  
- Significance is conditional: method is useful if simplifying assumptions hold, but paper does not show simplifications don't degrade practical performance.  
- Title and claimed "orders of magnitude" speedup are too strong given missing sensitivity analyses and validation.  
- Strength: clear presentation and strong experimental demonstration of scalability on real large networks.  
- Strength: efficient inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity and good comparisons to state-of-the-art on synthetic and real data.  
- Weakness: no model-selection procedure described for choosing K on real datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of impact of the δ subsampling threshold on results.  
- Weakness: unjustified claims that triangular representation yields accuracy benefits over edge-based methods.  
- Weakness: lack of exploration of the local-update approximation’s impact as K grows.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, addresses a significant scalability challenge, and shows substantial speed improvements while maintaining competitive accuracy.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing key experiments validating core contribution, unresolved probabilistic validity concerns, and conditional significance weaken the paper.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: technically competent but lacking critical validation and probabilistic clarity — weak reject pending requested experiments and clarifications.
