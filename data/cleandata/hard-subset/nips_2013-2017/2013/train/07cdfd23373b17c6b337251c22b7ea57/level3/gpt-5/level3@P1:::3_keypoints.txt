- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing (B0, Bxx, Bxxx).  
- Introduces structured mean-field SVI with natural gradients that jointly optimizes local three-node role assignments and approximates local updates in O(K).  
- Empirically scales to million-node graphs and ~100 roles on one machine with competitive or improved role recovery and link prediction versus MMSB and MMTM.  
- Builds on Ho et al. (NIPS’12) triangle representation and mixed-membership blockmodels, connecting to ERGMs and SVI literature.  
- Strength: Scalability — O(NK) global updates and practical O(K) local computations; converges in a few passes on large graphs.  
- Strength: Methodological clarity — well-justified equivalence-class partitioning and structured mean-field; natural-gradient SVI appropriate for conjugate-exponential models.  
- Strength: Empirical performance — strong runtime improvements and compelling convergence (2–5 passes).  
- Strength: Practical engineering — triangle subsampling, mini-batching, discarding local states keep memory modest and enable parallelization.  
- Weakness: “Parsimonious” needs explicit definition as symmetry-driven parameter sharing reducing DOF from O(K^3) to O(K) to justify statistical benefits.  
- Weakness: Incorrectly frames zero-downsampling as always harmful; must temper claim and cite a-MMSB (Gopalan et al., NIPS’12) showing careful negative subsampling can preserve accuracy.  
- Weakness: LDA analogy is imperfect because PTM can generate triangle bags that aren’t graphs; must acknowledge and discuss why this doesn’t undermine inference or add constraints.  
- Weakness: Convergence/theory citations incomplete — cite Hoffman et al. (JMLR 2013) for SVI with local variables and reference Robbins–Monro conditions rather than relying on Sato.  
- Weakness: Local update approximation claim (any O(K) support A yields valid lower bound) needs formalization and quantification of bias/variance trade-offs versus full O(K^3) optimum.  
- Weakness: Missing baseline — must compare to fast a-MMSB (Gopalan et al., NIPS’12) with edge subsampling/SVI or explain/approximate its performance if code unavailable.  
- Weakness: Experimental reproducibility — provide exact generator for “power-law latent-space” networks or cite a precise source.  
- Weakness: Link-prediction evaluation fairness — clarify held-out edge treatment in triangle extraction and consider an edge-subsampled a-MMSB baseline to control representation differences.  
- Weakness: Report sensitivity/robustness to δ, mini-batch size, K selection, and initialization; include ablations for structured vs. naive mean-field.  
- Quality: Technically sound overall, but theoretical claims about O(K) approximation and gradient unbiasedness need short formal justification.  
- Clarity: Generally well written; fix terminology (“mixture-membership”→“mixed-membership”), define parsimonious, address non-network generative issue, and move derivations to appendix if needed.  
- Originality: Novel combination of triangle representation, O(K) parameter sharing, and structured SVI that extends MMTM to large K and N.  
- Significance: High practical impact — enables million-scale networks and hundreds of roles with competitive accuracy, likely useful to researchers/practitioners.  
- Minor edits: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership”, remove last sentence on p8, and cite JMLR version of Hoffman et al.  
- Acceptance case: Strong scalability, clear methodological contributions, and competitive empirical results argue for acceptance.  
- Rejection risks: Missing comparison to a-MMSB, gaps in formalization of local support restriction, treatment of non-network samples, and reproducibility details.
