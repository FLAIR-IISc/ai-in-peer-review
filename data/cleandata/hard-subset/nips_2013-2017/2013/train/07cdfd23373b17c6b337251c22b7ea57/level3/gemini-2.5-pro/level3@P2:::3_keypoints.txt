- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) by sharing parameters across latent role configurations.  
- Builds on bag-of-triangles representation and inspiration from a-MMSB to enable parsimonious triangular modeling.  
- Develops a stochastic natural-gradient variational inference algorithm that processes mini-batches of triangles with a structured mean-field approximation and O(K) local updates.  
- Achieves O(NK) time per pass and demonstrates scalability to million-node networks, inferring 100-role latent spaces in hours on a single machine.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over batch MMSB and Gibbs MMTM while maintaining competitive or superior accuracy on latent recovery and link prediction.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-executed, with convincing synthetic experiments confirming accuracy and scalability.  
- Main experimental weakness: lacks a direct comparison to the state-of-the-art stochastic baseline a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.), weakening claims of superiority.  
- Writing and organization are clear; the three principles for scalability provide a strong narrative framework.  
- Recommend defining "parsimonious statistical modeling" earlier in the introduction for reader clarity.  
- Synthetic power-law network generation details should be included in the main text or appendix for reproducibility.  
- The characterization of a-MMSB in the introduction should be rephrased to be more neutral and precise.  
- Originality: novel and effective synthesis of triangle-based representation, parameter sharing, and tailored SVI with O(K) updates advances scalable network modeling.  
- Significance: makes latent-space analysis feasible at scales previously out of reach, likely valuable to researchers and practitioners.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important scaling problem, presents a novel, effective model+inference, and provides impressive empirical validation.  
- Against acceptance: missing direct a-MMSB comparison limits the rigor of empirical claims.  
- Suggested revisions: add a-MMSB experimental comparison, cite Hoffman et al. (SVI, JMLR 2013) in the global update discussion, strengthen discussion of the model’s non-generative/inconsistent-triangle limitations.  
- Minor fixes: correct "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership" and tighten the paper’s final sentence to avoid redundancy.
