This paper proposes PTM, a parsimonious variant of the mixed-membership triangular model (MMTM) that reduces O(K^3) triangle parameters to O(K) via parameter sharing, and develops a structured stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) global updates and an O(K) local approximation. Building on the “bag-of-triangles” representation (Ho et al., NIPS’11), the work aims to bring mixed-membership latent-space inference to million-node networks, contrasting with edge-based MMSB and its scalable variant a-MMSB (Gopalan et al., NIPS’12).

Quality: The computational contribution is clear and thoughtfully engineered (structured variational family, natural gradients, subsampling), and the writing is excellent. However, several empirical and modeling gaps undermine the claims. First, the practical advantage of enabling large K is not established: mixed-membership models often work well with modest K, so the paper should show performance versus K for PTM and MMTM to justify the linear-parameter design. Model selection is not described—how was K chosen on real data? Without a principled procedure (held-out likelihood, cross-validation, or nonparametrics), comparisons risk bias. The simplifying parameter sharing is insufficiently stress-tested: there is no discussion or experiment where the true B tensor has O(K^3) distinct values, in which PTM should fail. Synthetic experiments omit the precise true B used and should include both favorable and adversarial settings for PTM. Several approximation choices lack ablations: the effect of triangle downsampling threshold δ on accuracy vs. speed; justification for why triangle subsampling avoids the accuracy losses reported for downsampling non-edges in a-MMSB; and the impact of the O(K) local-update approximation and different sample sizes (e.g., 2K/4K/100K tuples) as K grows. Finally, the “bag-of-triangles” likelihood is not a generative model of networks and may place mass on non-networks; the probabilistic validity and its implications for evaluation (e.g., link prediction) should be discussed.

Clarity: Very clear and well organized.

Originality: Moderate. The modeling move is incremental but interesting: constraining parameters for scalability in latent-space network models is valuable.

Significance: Conditional. If the assumptions hold, the method could materially affect practice given its speed; absent the missing analyses, the impact is uncertain.

Title: Too broad for a paper centered on scalable PTM; consider narrowing.

Pros:
- Clear exposition; strong scalability engineering.
- Interesting O(K) parameterization and structured SVI.

Cons:
- Missing K-sensitivity, model-selection protocol, and fairness checks.
- Under-analyzed assumptions; incomplete synthetic setups.
- Insufficient ablations on δ and local-update approximation.
- Questionable generative validity of the bag-of-triples likelihood.
