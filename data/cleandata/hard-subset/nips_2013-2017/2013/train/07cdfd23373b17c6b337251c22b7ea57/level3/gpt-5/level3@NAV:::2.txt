This paper proposes PTM, a mixed-membership latent-space model defined on bag-of-triangle statistics, with parameter tying that reduces triangle-configuration parameters from O(K3) (as in MMTM) to O(K). A structured SVI algorithm with global/local updates yields O(NK) complexity, and an intuitive “mixture-of-deltas” approximation brings the local updates from O(K3) to O(K). Empirically, the method scales to million-node networks and hundreds of roles, with competitive link prediction and latent-space recovery. The work connects prior NIPS research on MMSB/aMMSB and triangular representations (MMTM), and leverages SVI for scalability.

Quality: The modeling idea—partitioning triangle configurations and sharing parameters—is sound and improves conditioning. The SVI derivation is standard but appropriate; the local-update approximation is elegant and practical. However, several claims need clarification: (i) the PTM/ERGM analogy is overstated—ERGMs rely on a broader set of sufficient statistics; (ii) why δ-subsampling preserves sufficient information is not theoretically justified here; and (iii) the counterintuitive superiority over MMSB on MMSB-generated graphs suggests initialization/metric effects and merits diagnosis. Robustness to holdout rate and to the 0.125 threshold is not explored.

Clarity: The presentation is generally clear, but bookkeeping around the equivalence classes could be streamlined. The distinction between Δ1 and Δ2 is easy to miss; explicitly relate these to the number of outcome categories. The stochastic local approximation (choice, size, and re-sampling of A) should be consolidated in one place with a small ablation versus the exact O(K3) update.

Originality: The contribution is a judicious combination of known pieces (aMMSB-style parameter tying + MMTM + SVI) applied to triangle counts, with a neat local-update approximation. Novelty is moderate but useful.

Significance: Strong. A practical O(NK) algorithm that works at million-node scale is valuable to the community and likely to be adopted.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear scalability advance via O(K) parameterization and SVI
- Intuitive local-update approximation with strong empirical speedups
- Competitive accuracy and compelling wall-clock results
- Bridges edge- and motif-based latent models

Cons against acceptance:
- ERGM analogy is unclear and potentially misleading
- Limited discussion of interpretability of the inferred roles
- No pathway to directed networks
- δ-subsampling adequacy and thresholding (0.125) not justified or stress-tested
- Experimental gaps: unspecified power-law generator; performance under sparser observation; explanation for outperforming MMSB on MMSB data
- Complexity discussion mixes parameter count with PTM-CGS K3 costs and may confuse readers

Suggestions: clarify Δ1/Δ2, justify δ-subsampling and threshold choices with sensitivity analyses, report exact simulators, analyze the local-update approximation vs. exact on small graphs, discuss directed extensions, and provide qualitative role interpretations.
