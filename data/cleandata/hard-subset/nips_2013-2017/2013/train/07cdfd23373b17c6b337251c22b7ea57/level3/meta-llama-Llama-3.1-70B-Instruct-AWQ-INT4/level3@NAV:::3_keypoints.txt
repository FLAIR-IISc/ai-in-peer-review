- Proposes a novel Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) representing networks as a bag of triangular motifs for scalable latent-space inference.  
- PTM is more compact and lower-dimensional than the mixed-membership triangular model (MMTM).  
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm for PTM with runtime O(NK), aimed at handling millions of nodes and hundreds of roles.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world networks show competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link-prediction accuracy.  
- Paper is well-written, well-executed, and overall acceptable.  
- “Parsimonious modeling” is not clearly defined and needs a brief explanation in the paper.  
- The claim that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy is not supported by Gopalan et al. and should be revised.  
- The analogy to LDA is imperfect/misleading because PTM can generate non-networks while LDA models text.  
- Theoretical background and citations for the stochastic variational inference should include Hoffman et al. in addition to Amari and Sato.  
- Experimental details are incomplete—specifically, how power-law networks were generated in the latent space needs clarification.  
- The manuscript should compare PTM scalability to Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB algorithm to strengthen scalability claims.  
- PTM and its inference algorithm represent a significant contribution with potential for wide adoption.  
- Remaining minor issues (definitions, unsupported claims, citation improvements, experimental detail) should be addressed to further strengthen the paper.
