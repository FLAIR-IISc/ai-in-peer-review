This paper proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), a scalable adaptation of the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM) for latent space inference in large networks. Building on MMTM's "bag-of-triangles" representation, PTM reduces the parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) by introducing simplifying assumptions on latent role interactions. This parsimonious structure is combined with a stochastic variational inference algorithm to achieve scalability. The work is an incremental but interesting step in a line of research that simplifies models like MMSB (e.g., a-MMSB) for large-scale applications.

**Strengths:**
The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents an interesting approach to a challenging problem. Achieving scalability in latent-space network models is a significant goal, and the strategy of constraining the parameter space of a higher-order model is a novel contribution. If the model's underlying assumptions hold in practice, the method could have a substantial impact on the analysis of massive networks.

**Weaknesses:**
Despite its promise, the paper suffers from significant weaknesses in its experimental validation and analysis of its core assumptions, which undermine the main claims.
1.  **Quality and Experimental Rigor:** The central motivation—that PTM enables effective learning with a large number of latent roles (K)—is not substantiated. The authors fail to demonstrate the practical advantage of using a large K and omit the crucial experiment comparing PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K. The simplifying assumptions are under-analyzed, with no discussion of failure modes or synthetic experiments using ground-truth parameters unfavorable to PTM. Furthermore, the impact of key approximation parameters (e.g., threshold δ, local-update sampling size) on the accuracy-scalability trade-off is ignored.
2.  **Fairness and Clarity:** The method for selecting K on real datasets is not explained, raising concerns about the fairness of comparisons to baselines. Claims about downsampling avoiding accuracy loss are made without justification.
3.  **Probabilistic Soundness:** A foundational concern is that the "bag-of-triples" generative assumption may not correspond to a valid network, questioning the model's probabilistic validity.

In summary, while the core idea is valuable, the work feels incomplete. The claims of scalability and improved performance are not sufficiently supported due to missing experiments and a lack of analysis regarding the model's crucial assumptions and approximations. The title is also overly broad for the specific contribution made. Substantial revision is needed to validate the proposed method.
