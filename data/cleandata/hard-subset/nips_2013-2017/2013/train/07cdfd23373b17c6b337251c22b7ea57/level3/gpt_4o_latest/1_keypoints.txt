- Paper contribution: Introduces PTM, reducing MMTM parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) with stochastic variational inference and approximations for scalable latent-space inference on large networks.
- Strength — Scalability & efficiency: O(K) parameterization and SVI enable analysis of networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Competitive accuracy on synthetic and real datasets with orders-of-magnitude speedups versus prior methods.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains model, inference, and experiments clearly.
- Strength — Originality: Parameter-sharing strategy and scalability improvements are novel and promising.
- Strength — Significance: Potentially substantial impact for large-K latent-space network modeling if assumptions hold.
- Weakness — Scalability vs practicality: Lacks experiments comparing PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K to demonstrate practical benefits.
- Weakness — Model selection bias: Real-world K selection method is not well-described, risking biased results.
- Weakness — Simplifying assumptions: Impact of parameter-sharing and other assumptions is not thoroughly analyzed or stress-tested.
- Weakness — Synthetic data setup: Choice of B matrix in synthetic experiments is unclear, limiting interpretability of results.
- Weakness — Approximation analysis: Effects of approximations (e.g., threshold δ, O(K) local updates) are inadequately analyzed.
- Weakness — Probabilistic concerns: PTM may not be a proper generative model, raising questions about its probabilistic foundations.
- Weakness — Experimental fairness: Unclear whether baselines received sufficient tuning or comparable initialization.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing concerns about scalability vs. practicality, model selection, and assumptions.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel scalable approach, strong empirical results with large speedups, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: Unclear model selection and fairness, insufficient analysis of assumptions/approximations, and probabilistic validity concerns.
- Suggestions for improvement: Add experiments varying K vs MMTM, clarify K selection method, analyze simplifying assumptions and approximations, explore failure scenarios, and address probabilistic foundation issues.
