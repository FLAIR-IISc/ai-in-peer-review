This paper proposes a scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks. The authors present a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces the number of parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), allowing for faster inference. They also develop a stochastic variational inference algorithm that is capable of handling large networks.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough explanation of their model and algorithm, and they demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on synthetic and real-world networks.

However, there are several concerns and limitations with the paper. Firstly, the practical advantage of the PTM over existing methods is unclear, and the authors do not provide sufficient evidence to demonstrate the benefit of using a large number of latent roles. Additionally, the paper is missing experiments that compare the performance of the PTM and MMTM as a function of K, which would help to demonstrate the benefit of the PTM's reduced parameterization.

Furthermore, the authors do not explain how they chose the value of K for the real-world datasets, which could lead to biased comparisons with other methods. The paper also does not provide a thorough analysis of the scenarios in which the PTM may fail, such as when the true B has O(K^3) distinct values.

The synthetic experiments are also incomplete, as the authors do not describe the true B choices and do not include cases that are both favorable and unfavorable to the PTM. The impact of the threshold δ on accuracy versus scalability is also not analyzed.

Moreover, the paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the local-update approximation, and the impact of the O(K) approximation and different sampling sizes on the quality of the results is not explored. The fairness of the experimental evaluation is also doubted, as the authors do not provide sufficient evidence to demonstrate that the competitors were evaluated fairly.

Finally, the paper's originality is moderate, as the modeling is incremental, but the constraint on parameters for scalability is an interesting direction. The significance of the paper is conditional, as the method could impact practice if the simplifying assumptions hold, but the scalability of latent-space network models is rare.

Overall, while the paper presents an interesting approach to scalable latent space inference, it has several limitations and concerns that need to be addressed.
