- Proposes a novel MCTS approach for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Introduces a general MCTS template that can be instantiated with various selection methods.  
- Proves that if the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent and meets exploration requirements, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with a clear and concise introduction.  
- Formal analysis is rigorous and the proof and underlying assumptions are clearly explained.  
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates convergence to approximate NE and competitiveness with other MCTS variants.  
- Does not provide a finite-time convergence bound, limiting practical performance prediction.  
- Assumes perfect information, reducing applicability to many real-world games.  
- Fails to give guidance on choosing the critical exploration parameter γ.  
- Lacks comparison with specialized extensive-form game algorithms (e.g., CFR).  
- Original contribution is significant, though applying ε-Hannan consistent algorithms in MCTS builds on prior work.  
- Reviewer conclusion: recommend strong accept, with suggested future work addressing listed limitations.
