- Proposes a novel model fitting triangle count statistics by combining aMMSB with MMTM for efficient between-community parameterization.
- Introduces an intuitive stochastic variational inference approximation for local updates, enabling a more scalable implementation.
- Suggests extending the method to directed networks as a promising future direction that would broaden applicability.
- Claims PTM is a "latent space counterpart" to ERGMs, but this claim and the interpretability of the inferred latent space are unclear.
- Lacks clarity on the distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 statistics and their roles in the model.
- Raises concerns about the adequacy and effect of subsampling triangles on inference quality.
- Questions the robustness of latent space recovery results presented in the paper.
- States the PTM CGS algorithm complexity as O(K^3) despite the model having O(K) parameters, creating a scalability/explanation discrepancy.
- Notes the method’s counterintuitive superior performance over MMSB on synthetic MMSB-generated networks, warranting investigation.
- Expresses concern about method performance when fewer edges are observed and sensitivity to sparsity.
- Acknowledges the paper’s strengths: novelty of approach, efficient between-community parameterization, and scalable SVI implementation.
- Criticizes presentation clarity and requests more justification, explanations, and detailed discussion of implementation choices.
