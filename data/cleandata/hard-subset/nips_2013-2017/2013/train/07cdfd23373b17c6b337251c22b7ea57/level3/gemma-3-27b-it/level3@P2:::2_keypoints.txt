- Proposes scalable probabilistic latent-space inference using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious Triangular Topic Model (PTM), and stochastic variational inference (SVI).  
- Core idea: succinctly represent networks via triangular motifs and model them with far fewer parameters than MMTM/MMSB, achieving O(NK) complexity.  
- Demonstrates competitive accuracy and much-improved scalability, including results on a 1M-node graph in hours.  
- Well-motivated: tackles the crucial scalability bottleneck in network latent-space inference.  
- Builds on prior work (bag-of-triangles, SVI) and effectively combines succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference.  
- Technical contribution: parameter sharing in PTM reduces complexity from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K).  
- Valuable derivation: stochastic variational update rules and an efficient approximation for the local update.  
- Presentation weaknesses: strained/unclear connection to Exponential Random Graph Models (ERGMs).  
- Missing justification: insufficient intuition for triangle subsampling per node (δ) and why it adequately approximates the full model.  
- Explanation unclear: the distinction between Δ^1 and Δ^2 is not well-explained.  
- Interpretability claim unsupported: authors assert latent-space interpretability but fail to demonstrate what insights are obtained.  
- Possible technical inconsistency: PTM claimed O(K) parameters, but Gibbs sampler (PTM CGS) discussion references O(K^3) complexity — needs clarification.  
- Evaluation concern: robustness of latent-space recovery to threshold choice (0.125) is not analyzed; sensitivity study needed.  
- Experimental detail missing: the latent-space model used to generate the power-law networks is not specified.  
- Surprising empirical result: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated networks is counterintuitive and warrants investigation.  
- Heldout-link experiments too easy: only 10% edge removal may not sufficiently stress generalization; higher removal rates recommended.  
- Modeling suggestion: consider a more explicitly binary notion of role membership if goal is recovering discrete role sets.  
- Strengths summary: major scalability (O(NK)), parsimonious parameterization, competitive accuracy, and clear problem motivation.  
- Weaknesses summary: unclear presentation, insufficient justification for design choices, weak interpretability evidence, complexity inconsistency, and limited evaluation robustness.  
- Acceptance arguments: addresses an important scalability problem, technically sound combination of methods, and compelling scalability results.  
- Rejection arguments: convoluted presentation, unmotivated design choices, incomplete/insufficient evaluation, and tenuous ERGM connection.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify presentation, justify design choices, analyze sensitivity, and discuss interpretability and directed-network extensions.
