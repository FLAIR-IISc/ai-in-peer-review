- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) via role-sharing, enabling O(NK) stochastic variational inference.  
- Demonstrates scalability: processes 1M-node networks in hours versus days for prior methods.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation and strong technical execution of model and inference algorithm.  
- Shows significant speedups (orders of magnitude) with competitive accuracy on synthetic and real networks.  
- Unclear practical benefit of scaling to large K; no demonstration that larger K yields tangible improvements.  
- Missing critical experiments comparing PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K.  
- Real-dataset K selection is unexplained, risking biased comparisons with baselines.  
- Synthetic experiments omit true parameter (B) details, preventing assessment of whether setups favor PTM.  
- Parameter-sharing assumption lacks analysis of failure cases (e.g., true B with O(K^3) distinct values).  
- Probabilistic validity of the bag-of-triangles representation is questionable and may invalidate likelihood-based inference.  
- Claims about downsampling advantages are unsupported theoretically and empirically; effect of δ threshold not analyzed.  
- Local-update approximation (sampling O(K) role combinations) lacks study of how sampling size impacts accuracy as K grows.  
- Experimental fairness issues: inconsistent resource settings across methods and unexplained exclusion of baselines on large experiments.  
- Contribution feels incremental; parameter reduction is interesting but not sufficiently novel or broadly justified.  
- Title is overly broad; should be more specific to PTM and its inference technique.  
- Recommendation: rejection due to incomplete experimental validation, unaddressed modeling assumptions, and unclear practical advantages.
