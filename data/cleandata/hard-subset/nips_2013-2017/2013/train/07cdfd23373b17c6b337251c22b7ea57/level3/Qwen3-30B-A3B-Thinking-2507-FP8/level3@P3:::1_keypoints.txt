- Proposes PTM that reduces triangle parameter complexity from O(K³) to O(K) via parameter sharing.
- Uses a bag-of-triangles representation and stochastic variational inference to scale latent-space inference to million-node networks.
- Technical contributions: PTM parameter-sharing, O(K) stochastic variational local updates, and large-scale experiments.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.
- Demonstrates significant computational improvements and scalability (e.g., 1M nodes in hours).
- Novelty is moderate: main idea is incremental parameter sharing for scalability.
- Practical advantage of learning with large K is not demonstrated; mixed-membership models often work with small K.
- Missing critical experiments comparing PTM vs MMTM performance as a function of K to validate large-K claims.
- Model selection (how K was chosen for real datasets) is unclear and may bias comparisons.
- Simplifying assumptions (e.g., parameter sharing) are under-analyzed; need discussion of failure modes when true B has O(K³) distinct values.
- Synthetic experiments lack description of true B and should include both favorable and unfavorable cases for PTM.
- Impact of threshold δ and subsampling on accuracy vs scalability is not analyzed.
- Claims that downsampling/non-edge ignoring does not harm PTM (unlike a-MMSB) are unsubstantiated.
- O(K) local-update approximation effects and different sampling sizes (2K, 4K, 100K) on quality as K grows are unexplored.
- Experimental fairness is questionable due to missing analyses and unclear model selection.
- Probabilistic validity is questioned: bag-of-triples may not correspond to real network generative processes, risking invalid likelihoods.
- Title overclaims by stating a general "scalable approach" when contributions are specific to PTM.
- Overall technically sound but experimental design and analysis gaps weaken the paper’s scientific contribution.
- Recommendation: reject due to critical experimental gaps, unaddressed theoretical limitations, and insufficient validation of large-K benefits.
