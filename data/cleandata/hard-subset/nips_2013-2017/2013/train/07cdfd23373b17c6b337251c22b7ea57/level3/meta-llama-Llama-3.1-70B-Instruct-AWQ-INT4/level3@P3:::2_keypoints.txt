- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), a probabilistic latent-space mixed-membership model based on a bag-of-triangles representation.  
- Introduces parameter reduction by partitioning triangle-configuration space and sharing parameters within groups for a compact, lower-dimensional model.  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm using a structured mean-field approximation with O(NK) time complexity for scalability.  
- Empirically demonstrates comparable or better latent-space recovery than prior work while running substantially faster.  
- Shows scalability to large real-world networks with faster convergence than recent scalable network models.  
- Strength: presents a scalable probabilistic latent-space model tailored to large networks.  
- Strength: provides a tailored SVI algorithm and thorough evaluation on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion on extension to directed networks.  
- Weakness: the analogy claiming PTM as a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is unclear and underexplained.  
- Weakness: does not clearly explain the distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2.  
- Open question: unclear whether the inferred latent space is interpretable and what it reveals about graph similarities/differences.  
- Open question: robustness of latent-space recovery to the chosen threshold (0.125) is not evaluated.  
- Experimental gap: does not specify which latent-space model generates power-law networks despite multiple models being capable.  
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforms MMSB on networks synthetically generated by MMSB, which is not explained.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written but explanations of PTM and the SVI algorithm could be more detailed and easier to follow.  
- Originality: novel combination of mixed-membership modeling with triangular motif representation.  
- Significance: addresses a hard problem in network analysis and provides a scalable solution with useful applications (community detection, link prediction, exploratory analysis).  
- Acceptance arguments: scalable model and SVI, thorough evaluations, and significant contribution to network analysis.  
- Rejection arguments: missing directed-network extension, unclear technical distinctions, interpretability/robustness concerns, unspecified experimental generators, and unexplained counterintuitive results.
