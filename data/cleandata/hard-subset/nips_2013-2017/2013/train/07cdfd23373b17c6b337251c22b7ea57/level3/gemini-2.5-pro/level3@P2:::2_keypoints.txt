- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM): a scalable latent-space model using a bag-of-triangles representation with O(K) parameters and an O(NK) SVI algorithm.

- Shares parameters across triangle role configurations to avoid the O(K^3) parameter explosion of prior triangular models.

- Proposes a "mixture-of-deltas" O(K) approximation for local variational updates, reducing per-triangle computation dramatically.

- Demonstrates scalability to networks with >1M nodes and ~100 roles on a single machine.

- Empirically achieves orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMTM and a-MMSB while maintaining competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction.

- Significant engineering and modeling achievement by combining bag-of-triangles and parameter-sharing/SVI techniques.

- The mixture-of-deltas approximation is an elegant, pragmatic solution that preserves empirical performance while cutting complexity.

- Strong experimental validation: fast convergence (2–5 passes), robust latent recovery across generative models, and competitive link prediction.

- Clarity issues: unclear relationship between model parameter complexity (O(K)) and reported O(K^3) complexity of the PTM Gibbs sampler.

- Presentation problems: confusing notation for parameter spaces (e.g., simplexes Δ¹ vs Δ²) and reliance on supplementary material.

- Overstated analogy: claiming PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is not well justified given ERGMs' broader statistics.

- Methodological gap: no discussion of extending the triangle-based method to directed networks with more complex triangle motifs.

- Sampling justification: δ-subsampling of triangles is under-explained; more intuition or analysis is needed for its adequacy on heterogeneous degree distributions.

- Evaluation choice concern: latent recovery uses an arbitrary 0.125 threshold for binarizing memberships without testing NMI sensitivity or considering explicit sparsity priors.

- Unexpected empirical result: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated data is counterintuitive and needs explanation (initialization vs regularization?).

- Link prediction robustness: holding out only 10% of edges may not stress triangle-based methods; experiments with larger holdouts/sparser settings are needed.

- Missing qualitative analysis: the paper lacks interpretable examples of learned roles in real networks to demonstrate exploratory utility.

- Overall: important and scalable contribution, but clarity, methodological justification, and additional experiments/analysis would substantially strengthen the paper.
