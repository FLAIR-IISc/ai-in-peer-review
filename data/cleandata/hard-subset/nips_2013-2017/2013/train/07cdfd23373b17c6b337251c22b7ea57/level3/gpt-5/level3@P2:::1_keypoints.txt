- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) by grouping role-triplets, enabling scalable latent-role inference.  
- Develops a structured stochastic variational inference algorithm with natural gradients and an O(K)-cost local approximation, plus node-centric δ-subsampling to scale to million-node graphs and hundreds of roles.  
- Builds on prior triangle-based models (MMTM) and SVI techniques, moving from edges to triangles and imposing parameter sharing instead of Gibbs sampling.  
- Manuscript is clear and well organized; technical ideas are easy to follow.  
- Elegant parameter sharing dramatically reduces the core tensor and enables O(NK) global updates.  
- Structured variational treatment of per-triangle latents and natural-gradient SVI are principled and appropriate choices.  
- Demonstrates strong scalability (up to 1M nodes, 100 roles) with multi-threading and fast convergence.  
- Empirical speedups of several orders of magnitude over MMSB variational and MMTM/PTM Gibbs; competitive or better link prediction on large social networks.  
- PTM (like MMTM) models a bag-of-triangles, not a true graph generative model; implications for inference and downstream tasks are not analyzed.  
- Key simplifying assumption (shared B entries) is not stress-tested; no experiments where ground truth requires O(K^3) structure.  
- No sensitivity analysis for the O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” local-update approximation (how accuracy/ELBO vary with candidate set size).  
- Effect of δ-subsampling on accuracy vs runtime is not quantified; no systematic study varying δ.  
- Model selection for K is not described; choosing K could bias comparisons.  
- Fairness concerns: baselines use different threading/compute setups and unclear if data splits/negative sampling are identical.  
- Several crucial experimental and derivation details are missing or deferred (synthetic data generation, full local-update derivations, link-prediction scoring, stopping criteria, hardware).  
- Core novelty (parameter sharing + O(K) approximation) is pragmatic and incremental relative to prior triangle models and SVI.  
- Significance depends on assumptions holding; paper lacks evidence that large-K advantages and robustness hold in practice.  
- Requested experiments: PTM vs MMTM as function of K on tractable datasets (shared vs rich B), reporting NMI/held-out likelihood/link prediction.  
- Requested experiments: sensitivity to δ (e.g., 20,50,100,∞) with runtime vs accuracy plots.  
- Requested experiments: sensitivity to local approximation size/type (1K,2K,4K,10K, full O(K^3)) on small graphs.  
- Requested experiments: transparent model selection for K and fair resource normalization across methods; tuned a-MMSB comparisons.  
- Requested analysis/theory: when ignoring non-edges or subsampling triangles preserves θ recovery; provide bounds or diagnostics.  
- Minor comments: consider narrower title, clarify memory footprint and discarding local variational parameters, discuss identifiability and role permutation/merging issues.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong scalability, elegant parameter sharing, structured SVI, clear exposition, promising empirical speedups.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete validation, missing sensitivity analyses and fairness controls, unanalyzed failure modes under misspecification.  
- Recommendation: Reject (borderline); promising but needs additional analyses and experiments.  
- Reviewer confidence: high on methodological/empirical concerns, moderate on exhaustiveness of related work coverage.
