- Proposes PTM: a parsimonious mixed-membership triangular model reducing O(K^3) triangle parameters to O(K) via parameter sharing and a structured SVI algorithm with O(NK) global and O(K) local updates.
- Strong computational contribution: carefully engineered variational family, natural gradients, and subsampling for scalability.
- Writing and organization are excellent and very clear.
- Key empirical gap: practical advantage of large K is not demonstrated—need performance vs K for PTM and MMTM.
- Model selection is not described—no principled procedure (held-out likelihood, CV, or nonparametrics) for choosing K on real data.
- Parameter-sharing assumption is insufficiently stress-tested; no experiments where true B has O(K^3) distinct values to show PTM’s failure modes.
- Synthetic experiments lack details (precise true B) and should include both favorable and adversarial setups.
- Missing ablations on triangle downsampling threshold δ and its accuracy vs speed tradeoff.
- Missing justification/analysis for why triangle subsampling avoids the accuracy losses seen when downsampling non-edges in a-MMSB.
- Missing ablations on the O(K) local-update approximation and on different tuple sample sizes (e.g., 2K/4K/100K) as K grows.
- Probabilistic concern: the bag-of-triangles likelihood is not a generative network model and may assign mass to non-networks; implications for evaluation (e.g., link prediction) are not discussed.
- Originality is moderate: an incremental but useful scalability constraint in latent-space network modeling.
- Significance is conditional: could materially affect practice if assumptions hold, but impact is uncertain without the missing analyses.
- Title is too broad given the paper is centered on scalable PTM.
- Pros summary: clear exposition, strong scalability engineering, and an interesting O(K) parameterization with structured SVI.
- Cons summary: missing K-sensitivity and model-selection protocol, incomplete/insufficient synthetic tests, lack of ablations (δ and local updates), and questionable generative validity of the bag-of-triangles likelihood.
