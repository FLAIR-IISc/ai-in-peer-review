- Proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference method representing networks as a bag of triangular motifs with a parsimonious statistical model and SVI.  
- Parameterizes triangle configurations to identify K roles, reducing parameters and improving scalability.  
- Develops efficient stochastic variational inference with derived global and local update rules and an intuitive approximation for the local update.  
- Novelty is a straightforward combination of aMMSB and MMTM, but implementation requires tedious bookkeeping and could be presented more clearly.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over existing methods while maintaining competitive or improved latent recovery and link-prediction accuracy.  
- Lacks discussion of extension to directed networks.  
- The claim that PTM is a "latent-space counterpart to ERGMs" is questionable and inadequately justified.  
- Unclear whether the inferred latent space is interpretable or what it reveals about graph similarities/differences.  
- The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.  
- Unclear why subsampling triangles per node (using delta) provides an adequate approximation.  
- If objective is recovering a set of non-zero roles, the model may need a more binary notion of role membership; current thresholding (0.125) seems arbitrary and unevaluated.  
- Experiments do not specify which latent-space model produces power-law networks, leaving generative assumptions unclear.  
- Confusion over complexity: PTM claimed to have O(K) parameters but PTM CGS reported as K^3 complexity.  
- Performance with sparser observations (holding out more than 10% of edges) is not evaluated.  
- Counterintuitively outperforms MMSB on data synthetically generated by MMSB, suggesting it captures aspects MMSB misses.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, but several methodological and presentation issues remain that should be addressed.  
- Overall contribution: enables a more scalable O(NK) mixed-membership blockmodel on triangle counts, but requires clarification and further validation.
