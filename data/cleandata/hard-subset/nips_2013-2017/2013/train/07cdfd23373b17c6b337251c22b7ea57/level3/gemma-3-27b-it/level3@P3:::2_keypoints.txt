- Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method for large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation, PTM model, and stochastic variational inference.  
- Core idea: parameterizing triangle configurations to reduce dimensionality, building on MMSB/a-MMSB and MMTM.  
- Main strength: strong focus on scalability, achieving O(NK) complexity versus prior higher-order costs.  
- Empirical validation shows scalability (100-role latent space on a 1M-node YouTube network) and appropriate use of SVI.  
- Bookkeeping and the novel combination of aMMSB and MMTM are underexplained and feel understated.  
- Description of Delta^1 and Delta^2 is missing, obscuring the triangle subsampling strategy.  
- Theoretical justification for δ-subsampling approximating the triangle distribution is lacking.  
- Role-membership threshold (0.125) appears arbitrary and lacks sensitivity analysis.  
- Claim that PTM is a “latent space counterpart to ERGMs” is questionable and potentially misleading.  
- Interpretability of inferred latent roles in real networks is unclear despite synthetic recovery.  
- Reported complexity has a contradiction: PTM stated as O(K) parameters but PTM CGS reported as K^3 complexity.  
- Concerns about performance on sparse networks; experiments only hold out 10% of edges which may be insufficient.  
- Counterintuitive result—PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated data—raises questions about experiments or assumptions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant scalability advance, competitive empirical performance, and a novel integration of methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity/presentation, insufficient theoretical support, unclear interpretability, and potentially misleading claims.  
- Overall: promising and scalable engineering contribution that requires clearer exposition, stronger theoretical justification, and improved experiments before acceptance.
