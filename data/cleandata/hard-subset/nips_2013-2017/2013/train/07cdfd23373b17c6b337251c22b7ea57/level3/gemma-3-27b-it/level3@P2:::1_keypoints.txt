- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that represents networks as bags of triangular motifs for scalable latent space inference.
- PTM reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) via parameter sharing, enabling efficient stochastic variational inference.
- Demonstrates significant runtime speedups over a-MMSB and MMTM Gibbs sampling on networks up to a million vertices and hundreds of roles.
- Properly situates the work within related literature (ERGM, triangular representations, MMSB and scalable variants).
- Strength — Scalability: convincingly handles much larger networks than prior probabilistic methods.
- Strength — Clear presentation: well-written and technically accessible.
- Strength — Novel parameterization: O(K) reduction is a notable contribution addressing MMTM scalability limits.
- Strength — Empirical validation: synthetic and real-world experiments provide compelling runtime evidence.
- Weakness — Practical advantage of very large K is not clearly justified; benefits beyond small K are unclear.
- Weakness — Missing experiments: lacks systematic evaluation of PTM vs MMTM as a function of K (e.g., link prediction, NMI).
- Weakness — Model selection: no principled explanation for how K was chosen on real datasets, risking biased comparisons.
- Weakness — Simplifying assumptions: parameter sharing is a strong assumption with limited discussion of failure cases requiring O(K^3).
- Weakness — Synthetic experiments incomplete: unclear how true B parameters were chosen and lacking cases unfavorable to PTM.
- Weakness — Downsampling justification: insufficient rationale for ignoring non-edges and why PTM avoids a-MMSB’s accuracy loss from downsampling.
- Weakness — Local update approximation: needs analysis of how O(K) local-update approximation accuracy scales with K and different sampling sizes.
- Argument for acceptance: tackles a major scalability challenge with a novel, effective approach and strong empirical results.
- Argument against acceptance: practical benefits, missing K-sensitivity experiments, unaddressed simplifying-assumption limitations, and model selection concerns weaken the contribution.
- Overall assessment: solid, novel, and scalable work but requires additional experiments and discussion; reviewer recommends weak accept with revisions.
- Suggested revisions: add K-varying experiments, justify K selection, analyze approximation and downsampling effects, and temper the paper title and bag-of-triples probabilistic validity concerns.
