- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameter complexity from O(K³) to O(K) via parameter sharing and uses stochastic variational inference with O(NK) runtime.
- Claims PTM enables latent-space inference on million-node networks with hundreds of roles in hours and outperforms MMSB/MMTM.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized writing and strong presentation quality.
- Strength: Scalability claim supported by experiments on real-world networks.
- Strength: Core idea of parameter sharing is a reasonable, potentially impactful technical contribution.
- Weakness: Practical advantage of large-K inference is unclear—no justification for needing large K in mixed-membership models.
- Weakness: Fails to show performance as a function of K, undermining the central scalability/accuracy tradeoff claim.
- Weakness: Model selection on real datasets is unexplained, risking biased or unfair experimental comparisons.
- Weakness: No analysis of failure modes (e.g., when true interaction tensor B has O(K³) distinct values).
- Weakness: Synthetic experiments lack details on true B and do not include unfavorable cases for PTM.
- Weakness: Approximation analysis is insufficient—impact of threshold δ and local-update sampling sizes (2K, 4K, 100K) on accuracy is unexplored.
- Weakness: Claims about downsampling and ignoring non-edges lack theoretical or empirical justification versus MMSB.
- Weakness: Experimental fairness and selection bias concerns due to missing analyses and validations.
- Weakness: Title is overly broad; bag-of-triples probabilistic assumption is questionable and may place mass on unrealistic "non-networks."
- Overall: Method is promising under specific assumptions but lacks comprehensive analysis and validation, weakening the contribution.
