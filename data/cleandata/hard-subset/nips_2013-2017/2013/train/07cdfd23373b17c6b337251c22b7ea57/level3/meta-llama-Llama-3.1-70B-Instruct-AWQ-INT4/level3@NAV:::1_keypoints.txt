- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), improving scalability.  
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm tailored for large-scale networks with fast convergence.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real networks show competitive or improved latent space recovery and link prediction.  
- Novel and significant contribution to latent space network modeling.  
- PTM is well-motivated and effectively lowers computational complexity.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and uses figures/tables effectively.  
- Experiments are thorough and well-designed overall.  
- Needs clearer explanation of the practical advantages of learning mixed-membership models with large K.  
- Authors should detail how K was chosen for real datasets and how that choice affects comparisons.  
- Simplifying assumptions of PTM are not thoroughly analyzed; missing discussion of scenarios where PTM may fail.  
- Synthetic experiments are incomplete; should include both favorable and unfavorable cases for PTM.  
- Impact of threshold δ on accuracy versus scalability is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Require more justification for why PTM avoids accuracy loss seen in a-MMSB and why ignoring non-edges is acceptable here.
