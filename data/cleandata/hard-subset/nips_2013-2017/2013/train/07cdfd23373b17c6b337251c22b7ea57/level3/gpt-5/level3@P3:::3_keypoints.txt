- Summary: Proposes scalable latent-space inference by representing networks as bags of triangles, introducing the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), and an SVI algorithm with O(K) local updates to scale to million-node graphs.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on triangle-based models (Ho et al. 2012), mixed-membership models (MMSB, a-MMSB), and natural-gradient SVI (Hoffman et al. 2013; Amari 1998).
- Technical soundness: Variational objective, mini-batching, structured mean-field, and natural-gradient updates are appropriate and well motivated.
- Key engineering contribution: The O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” local-update approximation is plausible and effective empirically but is heuristic.
- Requested ablation/diagnostics: Add comparison of full O(K^3) local updates vs O(K) approximation on small graphs to quantify ELBO and accuracy tradeoffs.
- Convergence caveat: State convergence guarantees carefully to reflect optimization under the restricted variational family induced by the approximation.
- Baseline omission: Missing a crucial baseline—fast a-MMSB—which should be included in experiments.
- Experimental scope: Comprehensive on synthetic and real data but needs the a-MMSB baseline and clearer reproducibility details.
- Clarity improvements: Define “parsimonious statistical modeling” at first mention and fix minor wording (“mixture-membership” → “mixed-membership”).
- LDA analogy warning: Remove or temper the LDA analogy since PTM/MMTM samples need not correspond to valid simple graphs.
- Originality: PTM parameter-sharing and the structured-SVI with O(K) approximation are novel and non-trivial extensions.
- Significance: Scaling latent-role inference to million-node networks with hundreds of roles is impactful and practically useful.
- Specific fix — a-MMSB claim: Correct the a-MMSB discussion to acknowledge subsampling non-edges yields unbiased stochastic gradients with little loss in accuracy.
- Specific fix — citations/theory: Cite Hoffman et al. (JMLR 2013) alongside Amari (1998) for convergence theory of stochastic natural-gradient VI with local variables.
- Specific fix — reproducibility: Move power-law generator description to main text and specify latent-space mechanism, degree exponent, role priors, and generation parameters.
- Specific fix — baselines: Include Gopalan et al.’s a-MMSB code with identical threading/hardware and report runtime and accuracy.
- Bibliography fixes: Use the JMLR (Hoffman et al., 2013) citation first in-text and add the a-MMSB NIPS citation.
- Minor edits: Replace “mixture-membership” with “mixed-membership” (p2) and remove the last sentence on p8 for a clearer conclusion.
- Strengths: Clear scalable contribution, strong empirical speedups with competitive accuracy, careful engineering, and useful discussion of triangle representations.
- Weaknesses: Missing a-MMSB baseline, heuristic O(K) approximation lacks ablation, misleading a-MMSB claims, and unsuitable LDA analogy.
- Pros for acceptance: Significant scalability advance, well executed and written, conceptually neat parameter-sharing with practical impact.
- Cons for acceptance: Incomplete baseline comparison, limited analysis of approximation effects, and citation/terminology issues.
- Overall assessment: Accept, contingent on adding a-MMSB comparisons, clarifying the power-law generator, correcting the a-MMSB claim, and tightening citations.
