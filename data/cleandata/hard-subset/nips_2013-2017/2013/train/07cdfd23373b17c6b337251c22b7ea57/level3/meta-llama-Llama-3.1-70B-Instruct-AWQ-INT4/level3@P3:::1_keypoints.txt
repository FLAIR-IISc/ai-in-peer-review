- Proposes PTM, reducing parameters from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) for faster, more scalable inference.  
- PTM leverages a triangular network representation effective for extracting mixed-membership roles.  
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) time complexity, parallelizable across triangles.  
- Algorithm shown efficient and effective for latent-role recovery and link prediction on synthetic and real networks.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains the model and algorithm.  
- Experimental results are impressive, achieving competitive or improved accuracy on large-scale networks.  
- Unclear how the number of latent roles K was chosen for real-world experiments; selection rationale missing.  
- Lacks thorough analysis of simplifying PTM assumptions (e.g., shared triangle-generating parameters) and their effects.  
- No comparison with other state-of-the-art latent-space inference methods (e.g., a-MMSB) to contextualize performance.  
- Insufficient analysis of the impact of approximation tricks in the SVI algorithm (e.g., O(K) local update approximation).  
- Quality: 8/10 — technically sound but with concerns about K choice and model assumptions.  
- Clarity: 9/10 — clear exposition though some technical details may be hard for non-experts.  
- Originality: 7/10 — novel parameter reduction but builds on existing triangular representation and SVI techniques.  
- Significance: 8/10 — meaningful contribution with practical potential, but real-world impact and comparisons are underexplored.  
- Acceptance pros: scalable model, clear presentation, strong experimental results, practical applicability.  
- Acceptance cons: unclear K selection, unexamined simplifying assumptions, missing SOTA comparisons.
