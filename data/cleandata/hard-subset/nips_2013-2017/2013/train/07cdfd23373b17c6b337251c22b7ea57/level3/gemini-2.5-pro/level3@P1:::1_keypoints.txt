- Proposes PTM: a parsimonious triangular model reducing triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) and an O(K) stochastic variational inference enabling O(NK) complexity.
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speed-ups over MMTM and a-MMSB on large networks with competitive or improved accuracy.
- Strength: tackles scalability, enabling inference on million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine.
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly positions PTM relative to MMTM and a-MMSB with detailed algorithm descriptions.
- Strength: PTM parameterization and the "mixture-of-deltas" local update are novel and effective for reducing complexity.
- Weakness: Motivation for scaling to large K is not convincingly demonstrated; no experiments showing performance as a function of K.
- Weakness: Strong PTM assumptions (aggressive parameter sharing) are not analyzed for cases where they fail to capture true O(K^3) interaction structure.
- Weakness: Synthetic data generation is under-specified—unclear how ground-truth B was generated and whether experiments are favorable to PTM.
- Weakness: Critical approximations (δ-triangle subsampling, O(K) mixture-of-deltas update) lack ablation studies on their impact, runtime/accuracy trade-offs, and comparisons to full O(K^3) updates.
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons may be unfair due to unclear model-selection procedures, particularly how K was chosen on real datasets.
- Weakness: Conceptual issue inherited from MMTM—bag-of-triangles generative process doesn’t guarantee assemblable graphs or a valid likelihood over networks.
- Suggestion: Title should be more specific to PTM; provide nuanced discussion about discarding non-edges given sparse networks.
- Suggestion: Add direct experiments (e.g., link prediction or recovery vs. K and ablations over δ and size of set A) to justify the need for large K and evaluate approximation effects.
