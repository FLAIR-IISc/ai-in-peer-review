This paper proposes a novel approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks, which is a crucial task in network analysis. The authors introduce a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) that represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs, reducing the dimensionality of the parameter space and enabling efficient inference algorithms. The PTM is combined with a stochastic variational inference algorithm, which has a time complexity of O(NK), making it scalable to large networks.

The paper makes several key contributions. First, it introduces a new representation of networks as a bag of triangular motifs, which is more compact and efficient than traditional edge-based representations. Second, it proposes a parsimonious statistical model (PTM) that reduces the dimensionality of the parameter space, making it more scalable to large networks. Third, it develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm that has a time complexity of O(NK), making it efficient for large-scale networks.

The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach through experiments on synthetic and real-world networks. The results show that the PTM-based approach achieves competitive or improved accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction compared to state-of-the-art methods, while being orders of magnitude faster.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and efficient approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks.

* The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach through experiments on synthetic and real-world networks.

Weaknesses:

* The paper lacks a clear discussion on how the method would extend to directed networks.
* The claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is questionable, as ERGMs use more varied statistics and the analogy is unclear.
* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2.
* The paper does not evaluate the robustness of latent-space recovery to the chosen threshold (0.125), which seems arbitrary.
* The paper does not specify which latent-space model generates power-law networks, which is a limitation of the experimental results.
