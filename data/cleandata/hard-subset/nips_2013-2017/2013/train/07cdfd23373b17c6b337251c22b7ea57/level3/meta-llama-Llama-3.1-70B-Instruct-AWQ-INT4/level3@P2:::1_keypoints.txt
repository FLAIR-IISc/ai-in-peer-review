- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) for faster latent-space inference.
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm and additional scalability tricks.
- Main idea: represent networks as a bag of triangular motifs rather than edge-based representation.
- PTM achieves time complexity O(NK) versus MMSB O(N^2K^2) and MMTM O(NK^3).
- Evaluated on synthetic and real networks; reports improved latent-role recovery and link-prediction accuracy and scalability.
- Authors provide a detailed analysis of model limitations, failure scenarios, and potential biases.
- Practical advantage unclear: unclear benefit of learning MMTM with large K since mixed-membership may work with small K.
- Experimental setup incomplete: true B choices not described and lacking cases both favorable and unfavorable to PTM.
- Impact of threshold δ on accuracy vs. scalability is not analyzed.
- Local-update approximation is not sufficiently studied.
- Fairness of competitor evaluations is questionable due to missing analyses and selection choices.
- Originality judged moderate: incremental modeling but interesting parameter-constraining direction for scalability.
- Significance conditional: could be impactful if simplifying assumptions hold, since scalable latent-space models are rare.
- Writing is clear and well-organized, but the title is broader than the specific contribution (scalable PTM).
- Probabilistic validity questioned: bag-of-triples assumption may not correspond to real networks, risking an improper generative likelihood.
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions — authors should add analyses/experiments, study δ and local updates, clarify limitations/biases, and revise the title.
