- Introduces PTM: ties triangle-generation parameters to reduce free parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) and presents scalable SVI for large graphs.  
- Inference uses structured mean-field, mini-batch SVI with natural gradients, an O(K) mixture-of-deltas local approximation, and δ-subsampling of triangles.  
- Empirical claims: orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMSB/a-MMSB/MMTM, high NMI on synthetic recovery, competitive link prediction, scales to 1M nodes with K≈100.  

Strengths:
- Principled parameter tying matching motif-equivalence logic, yielding O(K) triangle parameters (B0, {Bxx}, {Bxxx}).  
- Scalable, memory-efficient inference: parallelizable, discards local factors between iterations.  
- Strong empirical scaling and fast convergence (2–5 passes) with convincing wall-clock speedups.  
- Competitive accuracy on synthetic role recovery and real-world link prediction.  
- Clear writing, well-structured derivations and algorithm detail.  

Weaknesses / Quality:
- No analysis quantifying when parameter tying introduces bias vs full O(K^3) tensor; lacks synthetic MMTM-generated experiments to find crossover regimes.  
- Missing ablations of SVI approximations: impact of mixture-of-deltas size, accuracy vs K, and variance from resampling A not evaluated.  
- δ-subsampling sensitivity and robustness across degree heterogeneity not explored; triangle-extraction cost claims rely on prior work.  
- Model selection for K is unclear and appears ad hoc; no held-out ELBO/AUC or consistent selection procedure reported.  
- Potentially unfair comparisons: PTM/MMTM use triangle bags while MMSB uses edges/non-edges; representational mismatch not reconciled.  

Clarity:
- Exposition strong but needs a concrete equivalence-class figure/table inline, explicit per-pass complexity in N,K,δ,batch size, and hyperparameter tuning details.  

Originality:
- Moderate novelty: natural extension of prior tying ideas to triangles; main contribution is the tying scheme plus scalable structured SVI and local approximation.  

Significance:
- Conditional practical value: attractive for large-K models if tying assumption holds; impact depends on demonstrating accuracy vs MMTM as K increases.  

Probabilistic validity:
- Models triangle bags, not full graphs—acceptable for latent inference but raises concerns for tasks needing absent-edge info or higher-order consistency; failure modes should be discussed.  

Suggested additional experiments:
- Vary K on real/synthetic to map PTM vs MMTM accuracy/runtime and find crossover points.  
- Ablate local O(K) approximation size: report accuracy/runtime vs number of off-diagonal triplets and variance from re-sampling A.  
- Sensitivity analysis for δ across degree distributions and report accuracy/speed curves and empirical triangle-statistic preservation.  
- Compare structured vs naive mean-field on ELBO and downstream tasks.  
- Adopt held-out model selection (ELBO or link-prediction) and apply consistently across methods.  
- Ensure fairness: compare methods on matched data representations or report both triangle- and edge-based metrics.  

Minor comments:
- Title may be broader than contribution; suggest narrower wording.  
- Report memory footprint and multi-thread scaling.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong scalability and clear algorithmic contributions, competitive empirical performance, and well-written presentation.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Key assumptions and approximations insufficiently stress-tested, limited analysis of tying harms, and fairness/model-selection concerns.  

Overall:
- Promising, well-executed step toward scalable motif-based latent models; would be a solid contribution after the suggested empirical analyses.
