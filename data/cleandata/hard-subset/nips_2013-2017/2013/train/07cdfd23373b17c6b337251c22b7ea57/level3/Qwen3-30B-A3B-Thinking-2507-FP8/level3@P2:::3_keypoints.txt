- Paper proposes a scalable latent-space inference method combining bag-of-triangles, a parsimonious triangular role model (PTM), and stochastic variational inference, achieving O(Nδ²) motif representation, O(K) parameters, and O(NK) inference.  
- Enables analysis of networks with >1M nodes and K=100 roles on a single machine in hours, far faster than prior methods (e.g., MMTM Gibbs, MMSB batch variational).  

Strengths  
- Quality: Technically sound with rigorous experiments on synthetic and real networks; high accuracy (NMI >0.9) and massive speedups (e.g., 12 minutes vs. 8 days).  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written with clear motivation, modeling, and inference presentation.  
- Originality: Novel integration of triangular motifs, parsimonious role-sharing, and stochastic VI for O(K) parameters.  
- Significance: Enables latent-space analyses previously infeasible (e.g., YouTube with 1M nodes) and outperforms prior scalable methods on prediction and recovery.  

Weaknesses  
- Clarity: "Parsimonious modeling" is not explicitly defined; claim that down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy contradicts Gopalan et al. [5].  
- Originality: LDA analogy is misleading because the triangle model can produce inconsistent edge assignments across triangles; needs clarification.  
- Quality: Missing citation to Hoffman et al. [10] for SVI theory on local variables; power-law network generation lacks technical detail.  
- Significance: Lacks comparison to Gopalan et al.’s fast a‑MMSB [5], a critical baseline for scalability claims.  

Pro/Con summary  
- Pro: Breakthrough scalability, competitive accuracy with large runtime improvements, strong experimental validation, and clear methodology.  
- Con: Minor terminology/citation issues, missing key baseline, and ambiguous down-sampling claim.  

Recommendation  
- Strong accept: Significant, well-validated contribution with minor, fixable issues.  

Specific revision suggestions  
- Define "parsimonious modeling" (e.g., reducing parameter dimension via role-sharing).  
- Correct the down-sampling claim and acknowledge a-MMSB [5] maintains accuracy with zero-downsampling.  
- Replace the LDA analogy with a clear statement that PTM is a triangle-based latent-space model that does not enforce consistent edge assignments across triangles.  
- Add Hoffman et al. [10] for SVI local-variable theory and detail the power-law network generation procedure.  
- Include Gopalan et al.’s a‑MMSB [5] as a baseline in experiments.  
- Fix terminology (e.g., "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership"), remove the final sentence on p.8, and cite the JMLR SVI version in references.
