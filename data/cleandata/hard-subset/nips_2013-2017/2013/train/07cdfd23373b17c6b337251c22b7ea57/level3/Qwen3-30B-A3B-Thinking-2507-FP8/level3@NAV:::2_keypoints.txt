- Proposes scalable latent-space inference by modeling triangle counts as a "bag-of-words" analog (Parsimonious Triangular Model, PTM).  
- PTM reduces parameter count from O(K^3) in MMTM to O(K) via parameter sharing across triangle configurations.  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity, enabling analysis of networks with >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.  
- Technically sound combination of ideas from a-MMSB and MMTM with well-motivated O(K) parameterization.  
- Experiments show competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction accuracy with orders-of-magnitude speed improvements on large networks.  
- Claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is questionable and inadequately justified.  
- Distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.  
- Rationale for subsampling triangles per node (using delta) is unclear.  
- Paper lacks discussion on extending the method to directed networks.  
- Interpretability of the inferred latent space is not addressed.  
- The role-membership threshold of 0.125 is arbitrary and robustness is not tested.  
- Complexity claims are inconsistent: paper asserts O(K) parameters but reports PTM CGS as K^3 complexity.  
- Counterintuitive result that PTM outperforms MMSB on MMSB-generated networks needs stronger explanation.  
- Experiments do not specify which latent-space model generates power-law networks.  
- Performance under sparser observations (only 10% holdout used) is not evaluated.  
- Overall: promising scalable approach with solid technical advances but weakened by methodological gaps and presentation/clarity issues that should be addressed before acceptance.
