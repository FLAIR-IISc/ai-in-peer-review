- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and SVI algorithm for scalable latent-space inference in large networks.  
- PTM reduces triangle-parameter complexity from O(K³) to O(K) via principled parameter sharing.  
- Develops an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm with mini-batches and O(K) local-update approximation, scaling to million-node graphs and hundreds of roles.  
- Builds on the "bag-of-triangles" representation to succinctly summarize higher-order network structure.  
- Demonstrates dramatic runtime speedups (orders of magnitude) over prior methods and practical ability to infer hundreds of roles on million-node networks.  
- Novel synthesis of triangular representation, parsimonious parameterization, and tailored SVI; the O(K) local-update approximation is a clever scalability detail.  
- Technical approach is well-motivated and experiments (synthetic recovery, link prediction, scalability) support accuracy and efficiency claims.  
- Comparison baseline is unfair: stochastic PTM compared to batch MMSB instead of a-MMSB (Gopalan et al. 2012), overstating performance gaps.  
- Intro characterization of a-MMSB is imprecise; the downsampling justification and statistical validity in prior work should be acknowledged more accurately.  
- PTM is not a true generative graph model (sampled triangle bags may not form valid graphs); the LDA/document-model analogy is misleading and should be removed.  
- Citation and exposition issues: cite Hoffman et al. (2013) for SVI with local/global variables, include main-text details of the power-law latent-space generator, and clarify "parsimonious modeling" earlier.  
- Minor wording fixes: use "mixed-membership" (not "mixture-membership"); tighten the repetitive concluding sentence.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant, and original contribution to scalable network modeling; recommend acceptance after addressing baseline, clarity, and citation issues.
