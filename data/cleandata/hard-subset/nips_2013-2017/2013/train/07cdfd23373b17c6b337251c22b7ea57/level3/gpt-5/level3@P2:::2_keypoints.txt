- Summary: Proposes PTM — represent graph as bag of triads, reduce triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), and use SVI with a structured mean-field + mixture-of-deltas to get O(K) local updates and scale to N≈10^6, K≈100 on a single machine.

- Strength: Clear scalability: O(K) parameterization and O(NK) global updates, O(K) local update approximation, fast empirical scaling and convergence.

- Strength: Uses triangle motifs with δ-subsampling to reduce data while preserving signal, aligning with motif-based modeling.

- Strength: Structured variational family captures within-triangle dependencies; mixture-of-deltas is simple, parallelizable, and effective.

- Strength: Extensive experiments on synthetic and real data showing competitive/improved NMI and link prediction versus MMSB/MMTM and much faster runtimes.

- Strength: Solid engineering (mini-batching, natural gradients, memory efficiency).

- Weakness: ERGM analogy overstated — PTM is not a full generative ERGM and should soften/clarify that comparison and cite triad ERGM work.

- Weakness: Undirected-only experiments — needs discussion/extension to directed triad types and computational implications.

- Weakness: Interpretability unclear — require qualitative analyses (top nodes per role, role prevalence in open vs closed triangles, role-role patterns, case studies).

- Weakness: δ-subsampling effects not quantified — need bias/variance, accuracy vs δ curves, sensitivity to degree heterogeneity and hubs.

- Weakness: Role-recovery thresholding arbitrary (θ̂>0.125) — report threshold robustness or threshold-free metrics (PR/AUC for multilabel).

- Weakness: Binary vs continuous roles mismatch — consider sparsity-inducing priors or post-processing for binary memberships.

- Weakness: Synthetic simulator underspecified — must detail the "power-law latent space" generative process and parameters.

- Weakness: Complexity statements inconsistent — clarify O(K) global parameters vs O(K^3) costs in Gibbs due to triplet enumeration and unify complexity accounting.

- Weakness: Counterintuitive result where PTM beats MMSB on MMSB-generated data — provide explanations and controlled ablations (parameter sharing vs SVI effects).

- Weakness: Link prediction evaluation light (10% holdout) — report harder holdouts (e.g., 50%), cold-start, and variation across clustering coefficients.

- Quality: Technically sound application of variational inference and natural gradients; mixture-of-deltas is pragmatic but lacks theoretical bias guarantees and δ-subsampling needs empirical quantification.

- Clarity: Generally well written but needs earlier definition of Δ^1/Δ^2, clearer exposition/figure for parameter sharing/equivalence classes, unified complexity statements, and precise simulator description.

- Originality: Moderate — combines known ideas (triads, parameter sharing, SVI) with the novel parsimonious triangle parameterization and practical local-update approximation.

- Significance: High practical impact — enables previously infeasible scales for latent role inference and aligns with interest in higher-order network structure.

- Questions for authors: How extend to directed/weighted graphs and runtime/parameter implications? How sensitive are results to δ and off-diagonal set size? Can recovery be reported across thresholds or with induced sparsity? Why does PTM outperform MMSB on MMSB data — can ablations isolate causes?

- Arguments for acceptance: Strong scalability and engineering, competitive accuracy, clear practical contribution (O(K) parameterization and updates), relevant to large-scale network inference community.

- Arguments against acceptance: Presentation/method gaps (directed extension, δ justification, interpretability), incremental novelty, and robustness/evaluation choices leave open important questions.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — technically solid and practically significant, but revise for clarity and add empirical analyses on interpretability, δ-subsampling effects, and robustness.
