- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), a bag-of-triangles latent-space model that dramatically reduces parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Introduces a scalable stochastic variational inference algorithm that enables inference on networks with >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.  
- Uses a mixture-of-deltas variational approximation for local updates requiring O(K) work per sampled triangle.  
- Uses stochastic natural gradient ascent for global updates, preserving asymptotic convergence while simplifying updates.  
- Demonstrates competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link prediction on synthetic and real networks.  
- Shows practical scalability: infers a 100-role model on a 1M-node YouTube network in ~4 hours on an 8-thread machine.  
- Novelty: PTM is a new, efficient latent-space model enabling far larger K than prior approaches.  
- Significance: Approach materially advances large-scale network latent inference and is broadly applicable to social networks.  
- Clarity issue: The claimed analogy between PTM and ERGMs is unclear and needs further explanation.  
- Methodological gap: No discussion of how PTM extends to directed networks.  
- Notation/description gap: Distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not clearly explained.  
- Subsampling concern: The subsampling of triangles per node using delta is not clearly justified.  
- Experimental ambiguity: The paper does not specify which latent-space model was used to generate power-law networks.  
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforms MMSB on networks synthetically generated by MMSB without explanation.  
- Robustness concern: Latent-space recovery depends on a chosen threshold (0.125) whose choice and robustness are not evaluated.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on authors addressing methodological clarifications, directed-network extension, delta/subsampling explanations, specification of generative models, and robustness analyses.
