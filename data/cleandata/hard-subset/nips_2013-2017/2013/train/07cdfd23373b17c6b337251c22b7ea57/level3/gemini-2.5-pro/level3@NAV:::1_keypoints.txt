- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), reducing MMTM parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) with simplifying latent-role interaction assumptions and stochastic variational inference.  
- Incremental but interesting contribution in the vein of simplifying higher-order latent network models for scalability (similar to a-MMSB).  
- Strength: Well-written and clearly organized; constraining the parameter space is a promising strategy for analyzing massive networks if assumptions hold.  
- Weakness: Experimental validation is weak—no demonstration that PTM benefits from large K or comparison of PTM vs MMTM as K varies.  
- Weakness: Simplifying assumptions are under-analyzed; lacks synthetic experiments and discussion of failure modes when assumptions do not hold.  
- Weakness: Ignores impact of key approximation hyperparameters (e.g., threshold δ, local-update sampling size) on the accuracy–scalability trade-off.  
- Weakness: Unclear how K is chosen on real datasets, raising fairness concerns in baseline comparisons; claims about downsampling preserving accuracy are unjustified.  
- Weakness: Probabilistic soundness concern—“bag-of-triples” generative assumption may not correspond to a valid network.  
- Overall: Core idea is valuable but incomplete; scalability and performance claims are insufficiently supported and the title is overly broad—substantial revision needed.
