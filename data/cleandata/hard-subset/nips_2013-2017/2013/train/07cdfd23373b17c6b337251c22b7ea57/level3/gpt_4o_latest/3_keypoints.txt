- Paper summary: Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) with parameter sharing and stochastic variational inference to enable scalable approximate inference for triangle-based network models (O(K) parameters, O(NK) inference), achieving competitive accuracy and orders-of-magnitude speedups on million-node networks.

Strengths:
- Well-written and clearly organized manuscript addressing a critical scalability challenge in network analysis.
- Novel and practical model that reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Effective use of stochastic variational inference, with solid experimental validation.
- Comprehensive experiments (synthetic and real-world) demonstrating scalability and strong accuracy, including million-node networks processed in hours.

Weaknesses:
- The term "parsimonious modeling" is nonstandard and should be clarified or replaced.
- Intro claim that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy is misleading given Gopalan et al.'s results; revise for accuracy.
- LDA analogy is inappropriate because LDA generates valid documents but the triangle model does not always generate valid networks.
- Method for generating power-law latent-space networks is vague and needs more detail.
- Missing direct comparison to Gopalan et al.'s algorithm; include for a more complete evaluation.
- Minor edits: change "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership," remove the last sentence on page 8, and cite the JMLR version of Stochastic Variational Inference.

Pro arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles an important scalability problem.
- Introduces a technically sound and novel model/inference approach.
- Empirical results convincingly show scalability and accuracy.
- Clear presentation makes contributions accessible.

Con arguments against acceptance:
- Some introductory claims are misleading or insufficiently supported.
- Inappropriate LDA analogy weakens the argument.
- Notable omission of direct comparison to Gopalan et al.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance conditional on addressing weaknesses: revise the introduction, clarify terminology and modeling details, and include a comparison to Gopalan et al.'s algorithm.
