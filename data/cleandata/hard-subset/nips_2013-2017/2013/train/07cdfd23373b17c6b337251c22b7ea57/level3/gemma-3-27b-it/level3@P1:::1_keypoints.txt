- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using triangular motifs and parameter sharing to reduce parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) with a stochastic variational inference algorithm, showing large speedups on synthetic and real networks.

- Builds on prior latent-space/network models (MMSB, MMTM, ERGMs) and targets their scalability bottlenecks (MMSB O(N^2), MMTM O(K^3)).

- Strength: Clearly identifies scalability problem and provides a practical, theoretically justified parameter-reduction that yields substantial empirical runtime gains.

- Strength: Uses stochastic variational inference with mini-batching and structured mean-field for efficient, reproducible inference.

- Strength: Experiments demonstrate compelling runtime improvements and the ability to handle networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of roles.

- Weakness: Does not convincingly demonstrate practical benefits of scaling to very large K; missing direct PTM vs MMTM comparisons as a function of K.

- Weakness: Real-data choices of K are not justified; no principled method for selecting K, risking biased comparisons.

- Weakness: Parameter-sharing simplifying assumptions are insufficiently analyzed; possible failure cases where full O(K^3) parameterization is needed are not discussed.

- Weakness: Synthetic experiments lack detail on true parameters (B) and do not include scenarios unfavorable to PTM; impact of subsampling threshold (δ) is not explored.

- Weakness: Local-update approximation (reducing to O(K)) may harm inference quality as K grows; need analysis across different sampling sizes.

- Weakness: Experimental fairness concerns due to limited details on baseline parameter tuning and initialization.

- Weakness: Probabilistic validity questioned—bag-of-triples assumption may break structural constraints and place mass on non-networks.

- Arguments for acceptance: significant speedups, novel scalable parameter-sharing strategy, clear writing, and empirical ability to handle very large networks.

- Arguments for rejection: insufficient demonstration of large-K benefits, inadequate analysis of simplifying assumptions, fairness and probabilistic-validity concerns.

- Overall assessment: Valuable, well-presented contribution but requires minor revisions addressing parameter sensitivity, K-comparisons, limitations of assumptions, and a refined title; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
