- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that represents networks as a bag of triangles and reduces triangle-parameterization from O(K³) to O(K) via role-configuration grouping.
- Develops a structured mean-field stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with an O(K) approximation for local updates, enabling O(NK) runtime.
- Empirically demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedup over MMTM and MMSB, scaling to million-node networks with competitive or better latent recovery and link prediction.
- Major strength: achieves significant scalability, allowing inference on million-node, 100-role networks in hours on a single machine.
- Major strength: novel and effective parameterization that both enables scalability and appears to regularize latent-space recovery.
- Major strength: clever O(K) local-update approximation (mixture-of-deltas) and use of SVI with structured mean-field to make inference practical.
- Clarity issue: ambiguous definitions (e.g., probability spaces Δ¹ and Δ²) that are not explicitly explained.
- Clarity issue: apparent contradiction in complexity claims (CGS listed as O(Nδ²K³) vs. model’s O(K) parameterization) that needs explicit explanation.
- Overstated analogy to ERGMs; the connection is tenuous and could mislead readers.
- Methodological gap: δ-subsampling of triangles is used without discussion of its statistical bias or effects on inference.
- Methodological gap: lacks discussion of applicability to directed networks and of qualitative interpretability of inferred roles.
- Experimental concern: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated data is counterintuitive and unexplained (initialization vs. regularization?).
- Experimental concern: arbitrary thresholding (θᵢ at 0.125) for NMI lacks robustness analysis.
- Experimental concern: link prediction evaluated only with 10% edge holdout; performance in sparser graphs with fewer triangles is not studied.
- Originality/significance: novel synthesis of MMTM and a-MMSB ideas; significant practical impact by enabling scalable latent-space inference previously infeasible.
