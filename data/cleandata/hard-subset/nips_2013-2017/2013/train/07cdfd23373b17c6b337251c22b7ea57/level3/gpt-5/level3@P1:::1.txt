This paper introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and a stochastic variational inference (SVI) procedure for latent space inference on large graphs. Building on the triangular “bag-of-motifs” representation of networks [8], the paper ties triangle-generation parameters so that the number of free parameters scales as O(K) rather than O(K^3) as in MMTM [8], with three groups Bxxx, Bxx, and B0 analogous in spirit to a-MMSB’s reduction of O(K^2) edge parameters to O(K) [5]. The inference uses structured mean-field on per-triangle role triplets, mini-batch SVI with natural gradients [10, 2], and an O(K) local-update approximation via a mixture-of-deltas that restricts attention to diagonal and a random subset of off-diagonal role triplets. The result is an algorithm with global O(NK) updates and per-mini-batch local updates linear in K, further sped up by δ-subsampling of triangles [8]. Empirically, the method is orders of magnitude faster than MMSB [1], a-MMSB [5], and MMTM [8], achieves high NMI in synthetic latent role recovery, competitive or improved link prediction, and scales to 1M-node networks with K≈100 on a single multi-core machine.

Strengths
- Clear modeling idea: principled parameter tying that yields O(K) triangle-generation parameters (B0, {Bxx}, {Bxxx}) and matches the motif-equivalence logic.
- Scalable inference: structured SVI with mini-batches and natural gradients; memory-efficient (discard local variational factors between iterations), trivially parallelizable across triangles.
- Strong empirical scaling: convergence in 2–5 passes; convincing wall-clock speedups on large graphs, including a 1M-node YouTube network.
- Competitive accuracy: good latent role recovery on synthetic data, and strong link prediction on larger social networks relative to edge-based MMSB.
- Writing and organization: the paper is well-structured, with derivations and algorithm details clearly laid out.

Weaknesses and detailed comments

Quality
- Limited analysis of when parameter tying helps or hurts. The paper does not quantify the modeling bias introduced when the true triangle-generation tensor is genuinely high-rank with O(K^3) distinct interactions. A synthetic study where data are generated from a full MMTM with varying tensor complexity would test PTM’s robustness and identify the crossover point where MMTM outperforms PTM for small K.
- SVI approximation choices need ablations. The O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” local approximation is crucial for speed; its impact on accuracy as K grows (e.g., 2K vs 3K vs 6K vs 100K off-diagonals), and the variance induced by re-sampling the set A each update, are not evaluated. Similarly, the benefits of structured mean-field over naive mean-field are asserted but not quantified across datasets.
- δ-subsampling sensitivity is underexplored. Since δ trades accuracy for speed, the paper should report accuracy/speed curves versus δ, and robustness across degree distributions (especially high-degree networks). The claim that <1% time is spent on triangle extraction and that δ preserves open/closed triangle distributions rests on [8]; an empirical sensitivity analysis here would strengthen conclusions.
- Model selection for K is unclear. Real-data K choices appear ad hoc; no held-out model selection strategy (e.g., validation ELBO, link prediction AUC, or information criteria) is reported. This can bias comparisons, particularly against baselines for which K might be less forgiving.
- Fairness of comparisons. PTM and MMTM are trained on triangle bags that ignore empties and single edges, whereas MMSB uses edges and non-edges. The paper should clarify whether negative instances are comparably informative across methods and, ideally, compare on matched data representations or report both triangle- and edge-based link prediction metrics.

Clarity
- The exposition is strong overall. Suggested clarifications:
  - Make the equivalence-class argument concrete with a concise figure/table inline (not just appendix).
  - Explicitly state computational complexity per pass as a function of N, K, δ, and mini-batch size, and provide wall-clock breakdowns (local vs global updates).
  - Describe the hyperparameter settings’ impact (α, λ, SVI step sizes) and any tuning effort.

Originality
- Moderate. The parameter-tying idea is natural given [5] and the triangle representation in [8]. The main novelty is the specific tying scheme for triangles plus the scalable structured SVI with an efficient local approximation. While incremental, it is a meaningful contribution to making latent-space motif models practical.

Significance
- Conditional. If practitioners need large-K overlapping role models on very large graphs, PTM’s speed and simplicity are attractive. The impact, however, depends on how often the tied-parameter assumption holds; demonstrating competitive or superior accuracy at large K versus MMTM as K increases would substantiate the main practical advantage.

Probabilistic validity and evaluation mismatch
- The authors are transparent that PTM/MMTM are not generative models of graphs, but of triangle bags. This is acceptable for latent-space inference, but it raises concerns for tasks that depend on absent edges or higher-order consistency across overlapping triangles. The strong link-prediction results suggest the triangle view captures useful signal, yet a discussion of failure modes (e.g., when non-edges are crucial or motifs beyond triangles matter) would be valuable.

Suggested additional experiments/analyses
- Vary K on real and synthetic data; show PTM vs MMTM accuracy and runtime as K grows, with a small-K regime where MMTM may win and the crossover where PTM dominates.
- Ablate the local O(K) approximation size; report accuracy/runtime vs the number of off-diagonal triples in A, and quantify variance from re-sampling A.
- Sensitivity to δ (and to degree heterogeneity); report accuracy/runtime curves and show preservation of triangle statistics in practice.
- Structured vs naive mean-field: show ELBO and downstream task differences.
- Model selection for K: adopt a held-out ELBO or link-prediction-based procedure, and use it consistently for all methods.
- Fairness: compare MMSB on a triangle-augmented feature representation or evaluate PTM on edge-based link prediction constructed from its inferred θ.

Minor comments
- Title is broader than the specific contribution; consider “Scalable Variational Inference for a Parsimonious Triangular-Motif Latent Role Model.”
- Report memory footprint and multi-thread scaling.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong scalability with clear algorithmic innovations.
- Competitive accuracy on both synthetic recovery and real-world link prediction.
- Clean, well-written presentation and practical implementation details.

Cons for acceptance
- Key assumptions and approximations insufficiently stress-tested (K selection, δ, local approximation).
- Limited analysis of when tying harms accuracy relative to full MMTM.
- Some fairness/model-selection concerns in empirical comparisons.

Overall, this is a promising and well-executed step toward scalable latent-space modeling with higher-order motifs. With the suggested empirical analyses, it would make a solid NIPS contribution.
