- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that models networks as bags of triangle motifs instead of full adjacency matrices.  
- Key modeling advance: parameterizes triangle role configurations (identical, majority, all distinct) to reduce parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Inspired by a-MMSB and MMTM; main novelty is combining parameter sharing with triangle-based representation.  
- Uses stochastic variational inference (SVI) with linear scaling in nodes and roles, enabling million-node, 100-role analyses on one machine.  
- Demonstrates large empirical speedups over MMTM Gibbs and MMSB variational methods.  
- Introduces an O(K) "mixture-of-deltas" local update approximation that avoids the O(K^3) bottleneck by restricting posterior mass to a sampled subset.  
- Claims PTM is a latent-space counterpart to ERGMs — reviewer finds this analogy questionable and potentially misleading.  
- Presentation clarity issues: originality and synthesis of prior work not clearly positioned.  
- Technical details underexplained (e.g., distinction and justification for parameter spaces ∆¹ vs. ∆²).  
- Lacks qualitative analysis of inferred latent roles and their interpretability on real networks.  
- δ-subsampling of triangles is insufficiently motivated beyond citing prior work; sampling rationale unclear.  
- Model does not produce sparse/binary role assignments; evaluation thresholds (0.125) for non-zero roles are arbitrary and robustness not tested.  
- No discussion of extensions to directed networks, limiting applicability.  
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforms MMSB on MMSB-generated synthetic data — requires deeper investigation to rule out baseline or overfitting issues.  
- Link prediction experiments may be optimistic: only 10% edges held out and sparse-regime performance untested.  
- Complexity reporting is confusing: PTM has O(K) parameters, but some baselines reported with O(K^3) complexity; should clarify that SVI avoids the O(K^3) cost.  
- Synthetic data generation description is incomplete in the main paper (details relegated to an appendix not provided).  
- Overall: significant engineering and scalability contribution with clever algorithmic tricks, but weakened by presentation, methodological justification, and incomplete experimental validation.
