- Overall: Well-written, technically significant paper with strong experimental support; acceptable for NIPS.
- Main contribution: Scalable probabilistic latent-space inference using bag-of-triangles, a parsimonious model, and stochastic variational inference achieving large speedups with competitive accuracy.
- Importance: Addresses critical scalability limitations of prior latent-space inference methods for very large networks.
- Clarity/terminology: Define “parsimonious modeling” briefly (representing phenomena with minimal parameters) to aid readability.
- Misleading claim: Rephrase the claim that downsampling zeros in a-MMSB does not compromise accuracy — downsampling mitigates but can impact accuracy as a speed/accuracy trade-off.
- Inappropriate LDA analogy: Remove or revise the LDA analogy because PTM samples can produce non-networks, weakening that justification.
- Theory/citation fix: Cite Hoffman et al. (2013) SVI alongside Amari and Sato for stochastic natural gradient theory with local variables.
- Experimental detail: Provide precise details on how power-law networks were generated (exponent, role assignment, edge probability) for reproducibility.
- Baseline comparison: Compare against Gopalan et al.’s a-MMSB (fast MMSB) rather than batch MMSB to better demonstrate scalability gains.
- Minor wording: Change “mixture-membership” to the standard “mixed-membership.”
- Manuscript ending: Remove the redundant final sentence on page 8 and end with the penultimate sentence.
- Bibliography: Include the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference” (Hoffman et al., 2013).
- Argument for acceptance — scalability: Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups for large-scale network analysis.
- Argument for acceptance — accuracy: Achieves competitive or improved accuracy on synthetic and real datasets.
- Argument for acceptance — novelty: Introduces a novel combination of bag-of-triangles, parsimonious triangular model, and stochastic VI.
- Argument for acceptance — evidence: Experimental results strongly support the paper’s claims.
- Argument against acceptance — misleading statement: Initial downsampling claim needs clarification.
- Argument against acceptance — flawed analogy: LDA comparison is inappropriate and should be removed.
