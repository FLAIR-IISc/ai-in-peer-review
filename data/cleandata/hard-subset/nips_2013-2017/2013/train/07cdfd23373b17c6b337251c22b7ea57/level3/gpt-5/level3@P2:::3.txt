Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space approach for large networks that (i) represents the network as a bag of triangular motifs, (ii) introduces a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) that shares parameters across triangle role configurations to reduce the number of triangle-generating parameters from O(K3) to O(K), and (iii) develops a structured stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with natural gradients and an O(K) local-update approximation via a “mixture-of-deltas” variational family. The resulting method scales as O(NK) in global updates and O(K) per sampled triangle locally, enabling inference on networks with over one million nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine. On synthetic data, the method recovers latent roles with high accuracy and converges in 1–2 passes; on real networks, it yields competitive or better link prediction while being orders of magnitude faster than prior probabilistic methods.

The work builds on and contrasts with: MMSB [1] (O(N2K2)), a-MMSB [5] which reduces parameters and uses SVI but still processes edges with negative sampling, and MMTM [8], which uses triangles but requires O(K3) parameters and blocked Gibbs. The proposed PTM inherits the advantages of triangle-based representations (informative higher-order structure with fewer motifs than edges) while avoiding the O(K3) bottleneck. The SVI framework follows the natural-gradient variational literature [2, 10, 20] and extends structured variational ideas to the triangle setting.

Strengths
- Clear modeling idea: principled parameter sharing that collapses O(K3) triangle tensors to O(K) while preserving key role-dependent distinctions (all-same, two-same, all-distinct).
- Scalable inference: structured SVI with mini-batching of triangles, natural gradients, and an O(K) local-update approximation that empirically matches the exact structured update.
- Strong empirical scaling: million-node networks with K≈100 converged in hours on a single machine; consistent convergence in 2–5 passes.
- Competitive accuracy: solid latent role recovery on synthetic data, and strong link prediction on large social networks.
- Practical engineering: triangle δ-subsampling and discarding local variational factors reduce memory and runtime substantially.

Weaknesses and specific comments
- Clarify “parsimonious modeling” (p1): explicitly define it as “parameter sharing across triangle role configurations yielding O(K) triangle-generation parameters (B0, {Bxx}, {Bxxx}) rather than O(K3).”
- Introduction claim about downsampling zeros (p1): the statement that a-MMSB “downsampling zeros is still not fast enough to handle N≥100k” is too categorical. Gopalan et al. [5] demonstrate that negative sampling in a-MMSB scales favorably and does not materially compromise accuracy; please nuance the claim (e.g., “remains computationally heavy for hundred-thousand–node networks in our setting compared to triangle-based subsampling”).
- LDA analogy (p4): saying the model is acceptable by analogy to LDA is not compelling because PTM samples triangles that need not correspond to a valid graph; please acknowledge the mismatch and emphasize that the goal is latent-space inference, not generative network modeling, and discuss any implications for downstream tasks (e.g., link prediction).
- Variational theory and citations (p5): when claiming convergence of SVI with local and global variables, please cite Hoffman et al., “Stochastic Variational Inference” (JMLR 2013) in addition to Amari [2] and Robbins–Monro [18]. Sato [20] does not cover the stochastic optimization with local variables used here.
- Baselines (p6): include a direct comparison to a-MMSB [5] (with the same hardware/threads) in addition to batch MMSB. Even a time-to-target-objective or accuracy-vs-time plot on shared datasets would strengthen the case. The textual comparison to reported runtimes is helpful but weaker than side-by-side experiments.
- Experimental detail on power-law simulator (p6): provide a precise generative description (role priors, degree mechanisms, link probability parameterization, how hubs are induced, parameter values) sufficient for reproduction.
- Initialization fairness: many synthetic runs seed 2% of nodes with ground-truth roles. Please also report unseeded runs or a practical, label-free initializer (the suggested heuristic based on 3-triangle counts) to assess robustness.
- Approximation quality: the “mixture-of-deltas” local variational family is a key approximation. Please quantify its effect (e.g., bound tightness or accuracy vs. exact structured update on smaller K) and discuss failure modes.
- Minor wording (p2): replace “mixture-membership” with “mixed-membership.”
- Manuscript ending (p8): remove the last sentence and end with the second-to-last sentence, which is a stronger, self-contained conclusion.
- Bibliography: cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference” (Hoffman et al., 2013). Ensure all NIPS references are complete.

Evaluation
- Quality: The model and inference are technically sound. The structured variational formulation, natural-gradient updates, and O(K) local approximation are well motivated, with careful engineering for scalability. Claims are generally supported by experiments, though inclusion of a-MMSB baselines and more detail on the power-law simulator would further strengthen the case.
- Clarity: Generally well written and organized. Suggested clarifications above (parsimonious modeling, theory citations, simulator details, and a fairer introduction claim) will improve readability and correctness.
- Originality: Parameter sharing across triangle role configurations and the specific O(K) local variational approximation are novel in this context; combining triangle-based representation with structured SVI is a meaningful contribution beyond prior MMTM and a-MMSB.
- Significance: High practical impact for large-scale network latent-space inference. The method enables million-node analyses with hundreds of roles on a single machine and shows strong accuracy, making it likely to be adopted.

Pros (arguments for acceptance)
- Elegant reduction from O(K3) to O(K) with minimal loss of modeling fidelity.
- Scalable, parallelizable SVI with strong empirical convergence and runtime.
- Competitive or improved accuracy on role recovery and link prediction.
- Addresses an important bottleneck in probabilistic network modeling.

Cons (arguments against acceptance)
- Missing direct experimental comparison to a-MMSB.
- Some theoretical attributions/citations need correction; approximation quality could be characterized more.
- Reliance on seeded initialization in synthetic experiments; robustness not fully explored.
- Triangle-only representation may omit edge-level information; scope of this trade-off could be discussed more.

Questions for authors
- Can you report side-by-side results against a-MMSB with identical compute budgets?
- How sensitive is performance to the choice and size of the off-diagonal set A in the mixture-of-deltas approximation?
- Do unseeded initializations reach comparable solutions on synthetic data?

Recommendation
Accept after minor revisions addressing the clarifications, citation fixes, and, if feasible, adding an a-MMSB baseline or at least a detailed time/accuracy comparison on one or two datasets.
