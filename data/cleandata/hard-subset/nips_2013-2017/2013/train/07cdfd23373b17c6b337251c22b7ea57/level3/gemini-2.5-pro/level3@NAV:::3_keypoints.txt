- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangle Model (PTM), a parameter-sharing subclass of triangle-based networks reducing complexity from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Develops a scalable stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm enabling inference on very large networks.  
- Main contribution: combination of succinct representation, compact model, and scalable inference for large-scale network analysis.  
- Empirical strength: demonstrates impressive scalability—million-node networks processed in hours on a single machine.  
- Strong experimental results on synthetic and real-world data, with competitive latent space recovery and link prediction.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly communicates core ideas.  
- Experimental comparison is unfair: authors compare to slow batch MMSB rather than the fast a-MMSB baseline (Gopalan et al., 2015) they mention.  
- Introduction claim that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy misrepresents Gopalan et al. and should be revised.  
- Theoretical justification for generative limitations is weak; sampling can produce non-networks (p4) and this caveat should be acknowledged.  
- Terminology needs clarification: explicitly define "parsimonious modeling" (p1).  
- Citation issue: natural gradient update should cite Hoffman et al. (2013) since Sato (2001) does not apply to models with local variables (p5).  
- Reproducibility issue: must explain how synthetic power-law networks were generated (p6).  
- Minor fixes: change "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership" and cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper, recommend acceptance contingent on addressing the above issues—especially the experimental baseline comparison.
