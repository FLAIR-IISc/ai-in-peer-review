- Presents PTM: a parsimonious triangular model reducing triangle-parameter tensor from O(K^3) to O(K) to enable scalable latent-role inference.  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with structured mean-field and an O(K) approximation for local updates.  
- Demonstrates significant runtime speedups and ability to infer 100 roles on a million-node network on a single machine.  
- Strength: addresses an important scalability problem in probabilistic network modeling and enables larger K than prior methods.  
- Strength: parameter-sharing scheme is a novel, clever way to enforce parsimony and scale complex probabilistic models.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, clear, and well-structured.  
- Weakness: central parsimony assumption is very strong and under-analyzed; lacks discussion of networks or role interactions where PTM fails.  
- Weakness: inherits the bag-of-triangles representation which may not define a valid graph generative process; probabilistic coherence is not addressed.  
- Weakness: missing the critical experiment directly comparing PTM and MMTM as K increases to show performance trade-offs for scalability.  
- Weakness: lacks explanation of how K was chosen on real data, harming reproducibility and fairness of comparisons.  
- Weakness: synthetic-data generation is insufficiently described—specifically, the true B tensor used is not reported, and robustness to violations of PTM assumptions is not tested.  
- Weakness: impact of the O(K) local-update approximation on inference quality is not studied; no comparison to exact updates or analysis of dependence on sampled triplet size.  
- Weakness: no sensitivity analysis for the δ triangle-subsampling hyperparameter and its accuracy/runtime trade-offs in the PTM context.  
- Weakness: unsupported claim that the approach avoids accuracy loss from downsampling (compared to a-MMSB) without empirical or theoretical evidence.  
- Comment: request qualitative discussion of network structures where PTM’s parameter-sharing is a poor fit.  
- Comment: suggest a more specific title highlighting the Parsimonious Triangular Model.  
- Comment: experimental fairness concerns—comparisons may reflect sampler weaknesses rather than model differences; better MCMC baselines could change conclusions.  
- Comment: ask whether the randomness in selecting off-diagonal role combinations for the O(K) approximation was analyzed for variance and stability.  
- Conclusion: promising and impressive runtime results, but the work needs deeper analysis of modeling assumptions, inference approximations, and more complete experiments to substantiate claims.
