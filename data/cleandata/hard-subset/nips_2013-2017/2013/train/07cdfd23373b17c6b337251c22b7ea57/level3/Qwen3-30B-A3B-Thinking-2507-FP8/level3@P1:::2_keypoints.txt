- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that cuts triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) via role-sharing, enabling O(NK) inference.  
- Uses stochastic variational inference (SVI) with structured mean-field and triangle subsampling for scalable latent inference.  
- Demonstrates large-scale performance: recovers latent space and predicts links on 1M+ node networks in hours on a single machine.  
- Empirically validated scalability: 1M-node YouTube converged in ~4 hours vs. days for prior methods.  
- Achieves high accuracy on synthetic data (NMI > 0.95) and improved link prediction on real networks (Brightkite, Stanford).  
- O(NK) complexity claim is well-motivated and supported by experiments (Section 5.2).  
- SVI algorithm (Algorithm 1) is technically sound with O(K) local-update approximations that avoid O(K^3) costs.  
- Evaluation is thorough, including ablation studies across N, K, and role sparsity, and shows robustness to parameters (e.g., δ = 50).  
- Presentation clarity issues: PTM parameterization (Δ¹ vs. Δ²) is poorly explained, making complexity reduction hard to follow.  
- Misleading ERGM analogy: PTM relies solely on triangles, unlike general ERGM statistics, and the paper fails to clarify the relation.  
- Arbitrary "non-zero roles" threshold (0.125) lacks justification and may distort NMI for continuous memberships.  
- Complexity confusion: paper claims O(K) parameters but reports PTM Gibbs as O(K^3), creating inconsistency and ambiguity about scalability.  
- The O(K) advantage applies to SVI only; unclear statements blur the distinction between SVI and Gibbs complexities.  
- Methodological gaps: limited discussion of interpretability—what inferred roles reveal about network structure.  
- Lacks directed network support, which is important for many real-world applications.  
- Unexpected result (PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated data) is unexplained and needs deeper analysis.  
- Experimental limitations: only 10% edge holdout may not test robustness in sparse networks.  
- Reproducibility issues: power-law network generator is unspecified, and triangle subsampling rationale lacks theoretical justification.  
- Originality: not fundamentally novel but an effective integration of aMMSB scalability and MMTM motif ideas; O(K) parametrization is a pragmatic advance.  
- Significance: high practical impact enabling probabilistic latent analysis on previously intractable large networks.  
- Recommended fixes: clarify parameterization and threshold choices, explicitly distinguish SVI vs Gibbs complexity, and add discussion of directed extensions.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (Weak Accept) — strong technical contribution with substantive scalability gains despite clarity and evaluation gaps.
