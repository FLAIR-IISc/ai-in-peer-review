- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing MMTM parameter complexity from O(K³) to O(K) via parameter sharing and uses stochastic variational inference.
- Demonstrates promising scalability, claiming inference on a 1M-node network with 100 latent roles.
- Clear, well-organized writing that makes the method accessible.
- Paper does not convincingly show why learning with very large K yields practical benefits over smaller K.
- Lacks direct comparisons of PTM and MMTM performance as a function of K to justify increased model size and cost.
- Choice of K for real datasets is unexplained, raising potential bias in comparisons.
- Synthetic experiments omit details on ground-truth B generation and fail to test adversarial cases where true B has O(K³) distinct values.
- Impact of the δ-subsampling threshold on accuracy is insufficiently explored.
- Justification for ignoring non-edges in PTM (contrasted with a-MMSB) is incomplete and potentially flawed.
- Local-update approximation and sensitivity to sampling sizes (2K, 4K, 100K) need further investigation as K grows.
- Claim of avoiding accuracy loss during downsampling is not fully supported by the presented reasoning or evidence.
- The “bag-of-triples” assumption has questionable probabilistic validity and may assign mass to non-network structures.
- Overall contribution is an incremental but useful scalability advance; however, insufficient experimental validation and under-analyzed assumptions weaken impact.
- Title is broader than the specific technical contribution, making it somewhat misleading.
- Acceptance pros: clear scalability path, neat technical reduction, good presentation; cons: unclear practical advantage, experimental fairness concerns, and unresolved modeling issues.
