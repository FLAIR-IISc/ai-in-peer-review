- Proposes PTM: mixed-membership latent-space model on bag-of-triangle statistics with parameter tying reducing triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Introduces structured SVI with global/local updates achieving O(NK) complexity and a “mixture-of-deltas” local-update approximation reducing local cost from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Empirically scales to million-node networks and hundreds of roles with competitive link prediction and latent-space recovery.  
- Connects prior MMSB/aMMSB and triangular representations (MMTM) and uses SVI for scalability.  
- Modeling idea (partitioning triangle configurations and parameter sharing) is sound and improves conditioning.  
- SVI derivation is standard but appropriate; the local-update approximation is elegant and practical.  
- ERGM analogy is overstated—ERGMs use a broader set of sufficient statistics than claimed.  
- No theoretical justification provided for why δ-subsampling preserves sufficient information.  
- Unexpected better performance than MMSB on MMSB-generated graphs may stem from initialization or metric issues and needs diagnosis.  
- Robustness to holdout rate and to the 0.125 threshold is not explored.  
- Presentation is generally clear but bookkeeping around equivalence classes and the Δ1/Δ2 distinction needs streamlining and explicit linkage to outcome categories.  
- Stochastic local approximation details (choice, size, re-sampling of A) should be consolidated and ablated against the exact O(K^3) update.  
- Contribution is a useful combination of known techniques (parameter tying, MMTM, SVI) with moderate novelty.  
- Paper is significant: a practical O(NK) algorithm that works at million-node scale and is likely to be adopted.  
- Pros: clear scalability advance, intuitive local-update approximation with strong speedups, competitive accuracy, and bridging edge- and motif-based latent models.  
- Cons: unclear/misleading ERGM analogy, limited discussion of role interpretability, no directed-network extension, δ-subsampling and 0.125 threshold not justified or stress-tested.  
- Experimental gaps: unspecified power-law generator, unclear performance under sparser observations, and no explanation for outperforming MMSB on MMSB data.  
- Complexity discussion mixes parameter counts with PTM-CGS K^3 costs and may confuse readers.  
- Suggestions: clarify Δ1/Δ2, justify δ-subsampling and threshold via sensitivity analyses, report exact simulators, compare local-update approximation to exact on small graphs, discuss directed extensions, and provide qualitative role interpretations.
