Summary and relation to prior work: This paper introduces a scalable approximate inference framework for latent-space analysis of large networks built on a bag-of-triangles representation. The main technical contributions are (i) the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), a parameter-sharing subclass of the mixed-membership triangular family that reduces triangle-generation parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), and (ii) a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with structured mean-field and an O(K) local-update approximation, enabling analysis of million-node graphs with hundreds of roles. The work connects to and extends prior NeurIPS contributions on triangle-based modeling (Ho et al., NIPS 2012), MMSB and a-MMSB (Airoldi et al.; Gopalan et al., NIPS 2012), and SVI/natural gradients (Hoffman et al., JMLR; Amari). Compared with MMTM Gibbs and batch MMSB, the proposed approach claims orders-of-magnitude speedups with competitive or better accuracy.

Quality: The variational formulation and natural-gradient updates are sound; the structured mean-field is appropriate, and the mixture-of-deltas O(K) local approximation is plausible. Please add quantitative evidence that the O(K) local approximation closely matches the full O(K^3) update (e.g., bound values or accuracy gap on a subset).

Clarity: The paper is well written and organized. Please clarify “parsimonious modeling” (p1): explicitly define it as parameter sharing that collapses role-triplet tensor parameters into role-specific Bxxx, Bxx and a global B0, yielding O(K) free parameters. Ensure complexity statements are consistent (global update O(NK) vs. full-pass cost O(Nδ^2K)).

Originality: The parameter-sharing idea parallels a-MMSB’s O(K) off-block simplification but is novel in the triangle-model setting; the structured SVI with triangle subsampling is a useful combination.

Significance: Strong; demonstrated scalability (1M nodes, 100 roles on a single machine) and competitive link prediction make this likely to impact practitioners.

Specific suggestions and fixes:
- Introduction (p1): The sentence implying that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy is misleading; Gopalan et al. show it preserves accuracy while improving speed. Please revise.
- Theory/citations (p5): In addition to Amari and Sato, cite Hoffman et al. (JMLR “Stochastic Variational Inference”) and rely on it for the local/global-variable SVI theory; Sato’s online VB does not cover this setting.
- LDA analogy (p4): Avoid defending PTM by analogy to LDA; triangle-model samples can yield non-graphs, so the analogy is not appropriate.
- Experiments (p6): Detail how the power-law networks are generated in the latent space (parameters, role-degree mechanism). Include a comparison to the fast a-MMSB of Gopalan et al., not only batch MMSB.
- Minor wording: replace “mixture-membership” with “mixed-membership” (p2). Remove the last sentence on p8 and end with the penultimate sentence.
- Bibliography: Cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference” with full details.

Pros: scalable; principled inference; strong empirical results. Cons: missing a-MMSB baseline; minor theoretical and citation issues.

Overall assessment: Accept.
