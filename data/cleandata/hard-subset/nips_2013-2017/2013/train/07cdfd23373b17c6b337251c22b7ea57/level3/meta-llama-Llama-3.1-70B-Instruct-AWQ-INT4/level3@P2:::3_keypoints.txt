- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) representing networks as a bag of triangular motifs, reducing cost versus edge-based models.
- Introduces an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm and a fast stochastic natural gradient ascent with O(NK) time complexity.
- Main contribution: a scalable approximate inference algorithm for triangle-based network models capable of handling million-node networks with hundreds of roles on one machine.
- Experimental results: competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link prediction compared to state-of-the-art probabilistic methods.
- Demonstrated scalability: can analyze real networks with over a million vertices and many latent roles in hours, beyond reach of many existing methods.
- Paper is well written and well executed with clear explanations and strong empirical/theoretical support.
- Missing clear definition of "parsimonious modeling"; authors should explicitly define the term.
- Incorrect claim that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy; this statement conflicts with Gopalan et al. and should be revised.
- The LDA analogy is unfair/misleading because the triangle model can generate non-networks, unlike LDA’s generative setup.
- Methodological citation gap: should cite Hoffman et al. in addition to Amari and Sato because Sato’s algorithm doesn’t cover local variables and Hoffman provides necessary theory.
- Experimental detail missing: need to explain how power-law networks were generated in the latent space.
- Comparative experiments should include Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB algorithm (or replace batch MMSB) for a stronger baseline.
- Strengths: scalability, efficient inference algorithm, and competitive/improved accuracy on recovery and prediction tasks.
- Weaknesses: terminology clarity, a misleading claim about down-sampling zeros, an unfair LDA analogy, and missing experimental/comparative details.
- Overall assessment: acceptable paper with significant contributions; minor revisions would improve clarity and completeness.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
