- Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method using a bag-of-triangles representation, PTM model, and stochastic variational inference.
- Addresses scalability gap: achieves O(NK) time allowing analysis of million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine.
- PTM drastically reduces parameter dimensionality via parameter sharing (O(K) vs O(K³) in MMTM), enabling scalability.
- Structured mean-field variational inference with stochastic natural gradients converges in 2–5 data passes, improving efficiency.
- Empirical results: higher accuracy on synthetic data as size increases and orders-of-magnitude faster runtime than competitors.
- Real-world results: converges in minutes on Brightkite (58K) and Stanford (1.1M) versus days for prior methods.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and builds logically on Ho et al. (2013) by addressing scalability.
- Experimental setup is thorough with appropriate metrics (NMI, link prediction) and comparisons to multiple baselines.
- Needs explicit definition of “parsimonious modeling” to distinguish it from standard statistical parsimony.
- Claim that down-sampling zeros does not compromise accuracy is misleading and should cite Gopalan et al. (2013) a-MMSB.
- The LDA analogy is problematic because the triangle model can generate non-networks; the comparison should be reconsidered or clarified.
- Theoretical grounding for stochastic variational inference should cite Hoffman et al. (2013) in addition to Amari and Sato.
- Experiments should clarify how power-law latent-space networks were generated (critical for realism).
- Should compare against Gopalan et al.’s a-MMSB (fast MMSB) as a scalability baseline, not just batch MMSB.
- Minor corrections: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership,” remove the final sentence on p.8, and cite the JMLR version of "Stochastic Variational Inference."
- Significance is high: enables latent-space analysis at previously unattainable scales with practical applications in social networks and recommender systems.
- Meets NIPS quality criteria: sound technical approach, thorough experiments, excellent clarity, strong originality, and clear significance.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance after minor revisions addressing the points above.
