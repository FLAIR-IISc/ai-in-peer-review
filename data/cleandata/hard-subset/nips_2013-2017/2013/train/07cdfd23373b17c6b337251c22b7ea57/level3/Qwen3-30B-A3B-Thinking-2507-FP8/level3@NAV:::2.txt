

This paper proposes a scalable approach for latent space inference in large networks by modeling triangle counts as a "bag of words" analog. The key innovation is the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which reduces the parameter space from O(K^3) in the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM) to O(K) through clever parameter sharing across triangle configurations. The authors develop a stochastic variational inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity, enabling analysis of networks with over a million nodes and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine.

The paper makes a technically sound contribution that effectively combines elements of a-MMSB and MMTM. The O(K) parameterization and stochastic variational inference approach are well-motivated and demonstrate clear scalability advantages over prior work. The experimental results show competitive or improved accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction, with orders-of-magnitude speed improvements on large networks.

However, several concerns undermine the paper's quality and clarity. The claim that PTM is a "latent space counterpart to ERGMs" is questionable, as ERGMs employ more varied statistics and the analogy is unclear. The distinction between Delta^1 and Delta^2 is not adequately explained, and the rationale for subsampling triangles per node (using delta) remains unclear. The paper lacks discussion of how the method would extend to directed networks, and the interpretability of the inferred latent space is not addressed. The threshold of 0.125 for role membership is arbitrary and untested for robustness.

The complexity claims are inconsistent: the paper claims O(K) parameters but reports PTM CGS as K^3 complexity. The counterintuitive result that PTM outperforms MMSB on networks generated by MMSB (Figure 1) requires stronger explanation. Additionally, the experiments do not specify which latent-space model generates power-law networks, and the method's performance with fewer observed edges (only 10% holdout) is not evaluated.

Overall, the paper presents a promising approach with solid scalability advantages but suffers from significant methodological and presentation weaknesses that limit its scientific contribution. While the technical advance is valid, the lack of thorough analysis of the model's limitations and unclear explanations of key concepts prevent this from being a strong contribution to the field. The paper would benefit from addressing these concerns before acceptance.
