- Proposes PTM: a parsimonious mixed-membership latent-role model on bags-of-triangles with O(K) parameter tying and structured SVI (δ-subsampling, O(K) local updates, mini-batching).
- Empirically reports orders-of-magnitude speedups over a-MMSB and MMTM and competitive latent-role recovery and link prediction.
- Main novelty is the triangular parameter-tying scheme and the tailored structured SVI with O(K) local updates.
- Strength: clear motivation and writing; well-organized and technically accessible.
- Strength: significant engineering for scalability (mini-batching, natural gradients, discarding local states) enabling million-node graphs.
- Strength: sensible structured variational family over triangle role-triplets avoiding naive mean-field pathologies.
- Strength: strong wall-clock results — convergence in a few passes and much faster than baselines.
- Strength: parameter-tying idea (O(K) vs O(K^3)) aligns with a-MMSB’s spirit and is practically useful.
- Weakness: practical advantage at large K is unclear; missing plots of accuracy vs K comparing PTM to MMTM/a-MMSB.
- Weakness: model selection for K on real datasets is not described, risking biased comparisons.
- Weakness: tying assumptions not stress-tested; lacks analysis of failure modes when true B has many distinct triplets.
- Weakness: synthetic experiments are incomplete and simulator details for “true B” are not fully specified; need PTM-favorable and PTM-unfavorable regimes.
- Weakness: δ-subsampling lacks sensitivity study on accuracy–runtime tradeoffs and comparison to full triangles or matched subsampling for baselines.
- Weakness: O(K) local-update approximation quality is not quantified; need comparisons to exact O(K^3) updates and sweeps over off-diagonal counts.
- Weakness: fairness of baselines is unclear (matching subsampling, stopping criteria, threading, hyperparameter tuning, initializations, same K).
- Weakness: probabilistic validity concern — triangle-only likelihood is a pseudo-likelihood that may not correspond to a network; clarify statistical target and biases (e.g., overlapping triangles, ignoring non-edges).
- Weakness: complexity claims should explicitly state dependence on δ and degree heterogeneity (total work is O(|I|K) with |I| = O(Nδ^2)).
- Quality: variational derivations are sound, but many key claims depend on unquantified heuristics and favorable tying; experiments lack critical ablations.
- Clarity: generally very well written, but needs added details: precise synthetic B generation, K-selection procedure, consolidated complexity including δ, and discussion of non-network generative status.
- Originality: moderate — parameter tying for scalability is not new, but the triangular tying scheme plus structured SVI is a useful extension.
- Significance: conditional — could impact practice if tying assumptions hold and approximations preserve accuracy, especially at large K.
- Requested experiments/questions for authors: detail K selection; show accuracy/time vs K; sensitivity to δ with matched a-MMSB subsampling; evaluate O(K) approximation by varying off-diagonals; describe true B including fully distinct Bxyz; justify triangle-only modeling.
- Pros for acceptance: clear, scalable probabilistic latent-role inference with impressive runtimes and sensible structured VI.
- Cons for acceptance: missing critical analyses (K selection, accuracy vs K, δ and local-update sensitivity), potential baseline bias, limited failure-mode and probabilistic validation.
- Overall recommendation: leaning reject in current form; recommend revision with the requested experiments and clarifications to become strong.
- Title suggestion: narrow to reflect contribution, e.g., “Scalable Parsimonious Triangular Role Modeling via Stochastic Variational Inference.”
