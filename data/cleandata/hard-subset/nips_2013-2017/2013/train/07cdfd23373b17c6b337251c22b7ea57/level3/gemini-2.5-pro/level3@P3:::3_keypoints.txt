- Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method using triangle motifs, parsimonious MMTM parameter sharing (O(K³) → O(K)), and SVI with structured mean-field to achieve O(NK) time.  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability (million-node networks in hours) with competitive latent recovery and link prediction.  
- Major strength: substantial scalability advance enabling hundreds of roles on million-node graphs with orders-of-magnitude speedups.  
- Technically sound design: combines triangle representation, parameter sharing, and structured mean-field SVI that preserves intra-triangle dependencies.  
- Originality: novel synthesis for a triangular model, including a specific parameter-sharing scheme and an efficient O(K) local SVI update.  
- Clarity: generally well-written, but several points require clarification or correction.  
- Weakness 1: Mischaracterizes a-MMSB — downsampling zeros is a principled approximation per Gopalan et al. (2012) and should not be framed as inherently flawed.  
- Weakness 2: Inappropriate LDA analogy to defend non-generative triangle samples; must explicitly state limitation that sampled triangles may not form a valid, edge-consistent network.  
- Weakness 3: Theoretical grounding is incomplete — must cite Hoffman et al. (2013) for justification of integrating out local variational parameters in SVI.  
- Weakness 4: Experimental baselines are insufficient — should include a comparison to the fast a-MMSB (Gopalan et al., 2012).  
- Suggestion: Define "parsimonious modeling" clearly as parameter sharing reducing parameter complexity (O(K³) → O(K)).  
- Minor wording: correct "mixture-membership" → "mixed-membership" (and clarify "mixture-of-deltas" usage).  
- Reproducibility: explicitly describe how synthetic power-law networks were generated (appendix currently missing details).  
- Manuscript ending: remove repetitive final sentence and end with the stronger performance summary sentence.  
- Bibliography: update Hoffman et al. citation to the 2013 JMLR version.  
- Overall: valuable and acceptable for publication if the exposition, theoretical citation, and a-MMSB experimental comparison are addressed.
