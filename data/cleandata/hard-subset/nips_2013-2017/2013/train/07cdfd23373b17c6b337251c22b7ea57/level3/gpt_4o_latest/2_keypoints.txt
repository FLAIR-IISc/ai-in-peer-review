- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) with bag-of-triangles and stochastic variational inference achieving O(NK) runtime for million-node networks.  
- PTM reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) by grouping triangle configurations.  
- Stochastic variational inference algorithm is computationally practical and scales to large networks on a single machine.  
- Empirical results show competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction, converging on a 1M-node network in hours.  
- Introduces an O(K) approximation for local updates that balances efficiency and accuracy versus naive mean-field.  
- Novel unification of a-MMSB and MMTM ideas and argues advantages of triangle-based modeling over edge-based approaches.  
- Presentation clarity needs improvement (e.g., unclear Δ^1 vs Δ^2 distinction and insufficient justification for triangle subsampling δ).  
- Paper lacks discussion on interpretability of the inferred latent space and what it reveals about graph similarities/differences.  
- Method is limited to undirected networks with no presented extension to directed networks.  
- Robustness concerns: arbitrary 0.125 threshold for latent recovery and unknown performance when fewer edges are observed.  
- Counterintuitive experimental result: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated data, raising questions about experimental fairness or generality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses scalability, demonstrates strong empirical runtime/accuracy trade-offs, and presents an innovative combined framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation/subsampling details, missing directed-network extension and robustness analysis, and unexplained counterintuitive results.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, justify key design choices, and discuss extensions to directed networks.
