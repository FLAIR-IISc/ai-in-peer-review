- Proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference method (PTM + stochastic variational inference) that handles networks with >1M nodes.  
- Core contribution is a parsimonious triangular model (parameter-sharing triangle model) coupled with a stochastic variational inference algorithm.  
- Uses a bag-of-triangles representation and parameter sharing to reduce model complexity.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and provides clear mathematical formulation.  
- Empirical results demonstrate strong accuracy and a major scalability improvement over prior methods (e.g., 1M-node YouTube network with 100 roles processed in hours).  
- Includes extensive ablation studies and careful algorithmic tuning (mini-batch size, adaptive step size).  
- The phrase “parsimonious modeling” needs a clearer, concrete definition early in the paper.  
- The claim that Gopalan et al.’s downsampling compromises accuracy is misleading and should be revised.  
- The analogy to LDA is potentially confusing/unfair because triangle-model samples can produce invalid networks; clarify inference vs. generative simulation distinction.  
- Theoretical framing should cite Hoffman et al. (JMLR) on stochastic variational inference/natural gradients in addition to Amari.  
- Synthetic experiments need more detail on how power-law networks and latent vectors were generated to induce degree distributions.  
- Evaluation should include Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB (scalable variant) for a more direct comparison.  
- Minor edits: use “mixed-membership” consistently and remove the redundant final sentence on page 8.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, well-executed contribution with high impact; address the minor clarifications and citation/benchmarking issues.
