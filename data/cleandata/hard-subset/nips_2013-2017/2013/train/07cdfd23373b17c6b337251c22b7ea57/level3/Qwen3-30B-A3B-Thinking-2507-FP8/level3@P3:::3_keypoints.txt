- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) representing networks as bags of triangles with parameter-sharing that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) and SVI that scales O(NK).
- Demonstrates scalability to 1M+ nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine in hours, outperforming MMSB and MMTM Gibbs with much faster convergence.
- Clear exposition and strong experimental validation showing advantages in speed and accuracy on synthetic and real networks.
- Strength: clear articulation of the three principles for scalable inference (succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, scalable algorithms).
- Strength: effective parameter-sharing mechanism reducing model complexity to O(K).
- Strength: well-executed stochastic variational inference with explicit complexity analysis.
- Strength: strong empirical results demonstrating scalability to very large networks.
- Strength: generally good baseline comparisons, though missing the most relevant fast MMSB baseline.
- Weakness: “parsimonious modeling” is not precisely defined and needs clarification.
- Weakness: misleading claim about a-MMSB downsampling — should state downsampling preserves accuracy but is insufficient for N ≥ 100,000.
- Weakness: LDA analogy is inappropriate because triangle sampling can yield inconsistent edges; remove the analogy.
- Weakness: citation issue — cite Hoffman et al. [10] in addition to Amari and Sato for natural gradient with local variables.
- Weakness: experimental detail missing on how power-law networks were generated in latent space; add a brief description.
- Weakness: must compare against Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB as a more relevant baseline.
- Minor: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership.”
- Minor: remove the last sentence on p8 and end with the preceding sentence.
- Bibliography: cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference” [10] rather than the conference version.
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution with sound technical content and compelling experiments; suggested revisions would improve precision.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses major scalability challenge, provides clear effective solution, shows orders-of-magnitude speedups, and is well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance (minor): some technical claims need correction, some methodological details require clarification, and the LDA analogy is inappropriate.
- Recommendation: Accept.
