This paper proposes a scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks. The authors present a parsimonious statistical model, called the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. The PTM represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs, which requires much less computational cost compared to edge-based models. The authors also develop a fast stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm for performing variational inference, which has a time complexity of O(NK).

The main contribution of this paper is the development of a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model. The authors demonstrate that their approach can analyze real networks with over a million vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine in a matter of hours, which is out of reach for many existing methods. The experimental results show that the proposed method achieves competitive or improved accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction compared to state-of-the-art probabilistic approaches.

The paper is well written and well executed. However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. First, the term "parsimonious modeling" is not clearly defined. The authors should provide a clear explanation of what they mean by this term. Second, the introduction sentence claims that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy, which is not accurate according to Gopalan et al. This claim should be revised. Third, the LDA analogy is unfair, as the triangle model can produce non-networks, whereas LDA is a generative model. Fourth, the authors should cite Hoffman et al. along with Amari and Sato, as Sato's algorithm does not apply with local variables, and Hoffman provides the needed theory.

In terms of experimental details, the authors should explain how power-law networks were generated in the latent space. Additionally, it would be useful to compare the proposed method to Gopalan et al.'s fast MMSB algorithm in addition to (or instead of) batch MMSB.

The strengths of this paper include its ability to scale to very large networks, its competitive or improved accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction, and its efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. The weaknesses of this paper include its lack of clarity in defining "parsimonious modeling," its misleading claim about down-sampling zeros, and its unfair LDA analogy.

Overall, this paper is acceptable, and its contributions to the field of network analysis are significant. With some minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above, this paper has the potential to make a major impact in the field.
