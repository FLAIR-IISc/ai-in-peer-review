- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing parameters from O(K^3) to O(K) and a stochastic variational inference algorithm for large networks.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with thorough model/algorithm explanations and experiments on synthetic and real data.  
- Practical advantage of PTM over existing methods is unclear; insufficient evidence that large K offers benefit.  
- Missing experiments comparing PTM and MMTM as a function of K to demonstrate benefits of reduced parameterization.  
- Authors do not explain how K was chosen for real-world datasets, risking biased comparisons.  
- No analysis of scenarios where PTM may fail, e.g., when true B has O(K^3) distinct values.  
- Synthetic experiments are incomplete: true B choices are not described and both favorable and unfavorable cases to PTM are missing.  
- Impact of threshold δ on the trade-off between accuracy and scalability is not analyzed.  
- Local-update approximation lacks thorough analysis; effects of O(K) approximation and different sampling sizes on result quality are unexplored.  
- Fairness of experimental evaluation is questionable; insufficient evidence that competitors were evaluated fairly.  
- Originality rated moderate (6/10): modeling incremental but parameter-constraint idea is interesting.  
- Significance rated conditional (7/10): could matter if simplifying assumptions hold, but scalable latent-space models are rare.  
- Clarity high (8/10) and overall quality decent (7/10).  
- Recommendation: authors should add K-dependent comparisons, justify K choices, analyze failure modes, study δ and approximation impacts, and ensure fair competitor evaluation.
