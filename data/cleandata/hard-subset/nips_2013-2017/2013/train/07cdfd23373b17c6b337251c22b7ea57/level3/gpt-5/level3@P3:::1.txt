Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), a mixed-membership latent-role model built on a bag-of-triangles representation of networks. PTM replaces the O(K^3) triangle parameter tensor of the Mixed-Membership Triangular Model (MMTM) with an O(K) parameterization by tying parameters across role-triplet configurations, and develops a structured stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with several scalability heuristics: δ-subsampling of triangles, a “mixture-of-deltas” O(K) local update, and mini-batching with natural gradients. The paper empirically demonstrates large speedups over a-MMSB (Gopalan et al., NIPS’13) and MMTM (Ho et al., NIPS’11) and reports competitive performance on latent-role recovery and link prediction.

The work connects to MMSB (Airoldi et al.), a-MMSB (Gopalan et al.), the triangular representation (Ho et al.), ERGMs and motif statistics, and SVI (Hoffman et al.). The main novelty is the specific parameter-tying scheme for triangle motifs and the structured SVI with tailored O(K) local updates.

Strengths
- Clear motivation and writing; the paper is well organized and technically accessible.
- Significant engineering to scale to million-node graphs; the implementation details (mini-batching, natural gradients, discarding local variational states) are practical and useful.
- Sensible structured variational family over triangle role-triplets; avoids rank-1 pathologies of naive mean-field.
- Strong wall-clock results: orders-of-magnitude faster than MMTM and a-MMSB; convergence in a few passes.
- Interesting parameter-tying idea (O(K) vs O(K^3)) that aligns with the spirit of a-MMSB (O(K) vs O(K^2)).

Weaknesses and missing analyses
- Practical advantage of large-K unclear. Many mixed-membership models work with moderate K. The paper should demonstrate that (i) learning with large K truly improves downstream metrics, and (ii) PTM maintains accuracy as K grows where MMTM/a-MMSB degrade. Absent plots of accuracy vs K for PTM and MMTM, the core modeling claim remains under-supported.
- Model selection for K is not described for real datasets. Without a principled or at least consistent procedure (held-out likelihood, link-prediction AUC, perplexity), comparisons to baselines may be biased by favorable K choices for PTM.
- Simplifying assumptions not stress-tested. PTM ties most of the O(K^3) parameters; the paper should analyze failure modes when the true B has many distinct triplets (e.g., triads whose probability depends on specific role triples). A synthetic study that varies the proportion of distinct Bxyz (from fully tied to fully distinct) would reveal when PTM underfits.
- Synthetic experiments incomplete. The simulator details for “true B” under triangle models are not fully specified; please include both PTM-favorable (tied) and PTM-unfavorable (untied) regimes and report recovery as K grows.
- Approximation and subsampling heuristics insufficiently studied.
  - δ-subsampling: No sensitivity study of δ on accuracy–runtime tradeoffs. Why does δ-subsampling not hurt PTM while downsampling non-edges is said to harm a-MMSB? Provide ablations varying δ and compare to full triangles on manageable graphs.
  - O(K) local-update approximation: Quality vs K is not quantified. Compare exact O(K^3) local updates vs O(K) approximation on small graphs; sweep the number and form of off-diagonals in A (e.g., 2K, 4K, 100K) to show stability as K increases.
- Fairness of baselines. It is not clear that competitors were run under comparable regimes (e.g., same subsampling of data; matched stopping criteria; threaded vs single-threaded; hyperparameters tuned; identical initialization policies; and same K). For a-MMSB, does PTM also downsample to the same extent? Given the strong speed claims, these details are crucial.
- Probabilistic validity. Both PTM and MMTM define a likelihood over bags of triangles that may not correspond to any network and ignore empty/single-edge triples. This “pseudo-likelihood” raises questions about what is being optimized and why ignoring non-edges is justified here but not in MMSB. Please clarify the statistical target, potential biases (e.g., overcounting edges via overlapping triangles), and conditions under which triangle-only modeling leads to consistent latent-role recovery.
- Complexity claims. The paper states O(NK) in places; with δ-subsampling the total work is O(|I|K) with |I| = O(Nδ^2). If δ is treated as a constant this is linear in N, but the dependence on δ (and degree heterogeneity) should be made explicit.

Quality
- Sound variational derivations and a reasonable structured family. However, many key claims rest on unquantified heuristics (δ-subsampling, O(K) local updates) and on favorable parameter-tying assumptions. The experimental section lacks the critical ablations needed to validate these approximations and to show when PTM helps or hurts versus MMTM/a-MMSB.

Clarity
- Very well written. Derivations are clear; algorithmic choices are explained. Please add: (i) a precise description of the synthetic B generation; (ii) the K-selection procedure; (iii) a consolidated complexity statement including δ; and (iv) a clearer discussion of the non-network generative status.

Originality
- Moderate. Parameter tying for scalability is not new (e.g., a-MMSB), but the specific triangular tying scheme plus structured SVI is a useful extension.

Significance
- Conditional. If the tying assumptions hold and the approximations do not degrade accuracy, the method could impact practice because scalable probabilistic latent-role modeling on million-node graphs is rare. The significance hinges on demonstrating benefits at large K and on rigorous studies of the approximations.

Specific questions for the authors
- How was K chosen per real dataset? What metric and search procedure were used?
- Provide accuracy/time vs K for PTM vs MMTM (exact or approximate) to substantiate the “large-K” advantage.
- Sensitivity to δ: show accuracy/AUC and runtime as δ varies; compare to a-MMSB with matched subsampling.
- Evaluate the O(K) local-update approximation by varying the number of off-diagonals (2K, 4K, 100K) and reporting any accuracy gaps to exact updates on small graphs.
- Describe the true B used in synthetic studies and include settings with fully distinct Bxyz to illustrate PTM’s limits.
- Clarify why ignoring non-edges and using triangle-only likelihood is appropriate for link prediction and role recovery; relate to pseudo-likelihood ideas in ERGMs.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear, well-executed scaling of probabilistic latent-role inference; impressive runtimes.
- Sensible structured VI; good engineering practices; competitive performance on several tasks.

Cons for acceptance
- Critical missing analyses (K selection, accuracy vs K, δ and local-update sensitivity).
- Potentially biased comparisons and unclear fairness versus baselines.
- Limited exploration of failure modes and probabilistic validity.

Title
- Consider narrowing to reflect the specific contribution (e.g., “Scalable Parsimonious Triangular Role Modeling via Stochastic Variational Inference”).

Overall recommendation
- Leaning reject in current form due to missing analyses that are central to the claimed advantages. With added experiments addressing K-selection, accuracy vs K, sensitivity to δ and local-update approximation, and clearer fairness/probabilistic justification, this could become a strong paper.
