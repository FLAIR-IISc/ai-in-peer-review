- Proposes a scalable approximate inference framework using a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Introduces a structured SVI algorithm with an O(K) local-update approximation enabling analysis of million-node graphs with hundreds of roles.  
- Builds on and extends prior work on triangle-based modeling (Ho et al. 2012), MMSB/a-MMSB, and SVI/natural gradients.  
- Claims orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMTM Gibbs and batch MMSB with competitive or better accuracy.  
- Variational formulation and natural-gradient updates are sound; structured mean-field and mixture-of-deltas O(K) approximation are plausible.  
- Requests quantitative evidence that the O(K) local approximation closely matches the full O(K^3) update (e.g., bounds or accuracy gap on a subset).  
- Paper is well written; asks to explicitly define “parsimonious modeling” as parameter sharing collapsing role-triplet tensors into role-specific Bxxx, Bxx and global B0 (O(K) params).  
- Requests consistency in complexity statements (clarify global update O(NK) vs. full-pass cost O(Nδ^2K)).  
- Notes parameter-sharing idea parallels a-MMSB’s O(K) off-block simplification but is novel for the triangle model; triangle subsampling SVI is a useful combination.  
- Demonstrated significance: scalable to 1M nodes and 100 roles on a single machine with competitive link prediction, likely impactful to practitioners.  
- Suggest revising the introduction sentence that misleadingly claims down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy, since Gopalan et al. show it can preserve accuracy while improving speed.  
- Ask to cite Hoffman et al. (JMLR “Stochastic Variational Inference”) for local/global-variable SVI theory in addition to Amari and Sato.  
- Advise removing the LDA analogy because triangle-model samples can produce non-graphs, making the analogy inappropriate.  
- Experimental requests: detail how power-law networks are generated in latent space (parameters, role-degree mechanism) and compare against fast a-MMSB, not just batch MMSB.  
- Minor edits: replace “mixture-membership” with “mixed-membership,” remove last sentence on p8, and cite the JMLR version of SVI in the bibliography.  
- Pros: scalable, principled inference, strong empirical results; Cons: missing a-MMSB baseline and minor theoretical/citation issues.  
- Overall assessment: Accept.
