- Introduces a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference method using a bag-of-triangles representation, parsimonious modeling, and stochastic variational inference.
- Core idea: reduce complexity by focusing on triangular motifs and parameter sharing, enabling analysis of networks with >1M vertices and hundreds of roles.
- Demonstrates significant speedups over state-of-the-art probabilistic methods with competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction accuracy.
- Builds on MMSB and a-MMSB literature and addresses their quadratic/cubic scaling limitations.
- Novelty: combines triangle-based representation with a parsimonious statistical model and scalable inference in a way that scales to many latent roles.
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments on synthetic and real networks convincingly show efficiency and effectiveness.
- Writing: paper is generally well-written and clearly organized.
- Terminology: "parsimonious modeling" is used without a clear definition and needs elaboration.
- Misleading claim: statement about a-MMSB downsampling not affecting accuracy is incorrect; Gopalan et al. acknowledge a speed–accuracy trade-off.
- Analogy concern: comparing the triangle model to LDA is problematic because the triangle model can generate invalid network configurations.
- Theoretical foundation: variational inference discussion should cite Hoffman et al. (2013) and clarify applicability of Sato’s algorithm given local variables.
- Experimental details: generation procedure for power-law latent-space networks is insufficiently described for reproducibility.
- Baseline comparison: should include direct comparison to Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB (a-MMSB) rather than only batch MMSB.
- Minor issues: correct "mixture-membership" to "mixed-membership" and remove redundant final sentence on p8.
- Recommendation: strong contribution addressing a critical scalability challenge; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the above points.
