This paper proposes a scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks, introducing the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and a stochastic variational inference algorithm. The PTM reduces the number of parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), allowing for faster inference and improved scalability. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on synthetic and real-world networks, showing competitive or improved accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel and efficient approach to latent space inference, which is a key challenge in network analysis.
* The PTM model is well-motivated and effectively reduces the number of parameters, making it more scalable than existing methods.
* The stochastic variational inference algorithm is well-suited for large-scale networks and demonstrates fast convergence.
* The experiments are thorough and well-designed, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the practical advantages of learning mixed-membership models with large K.
* The authors should provide more details on how K was chosen for real datasets and how this choice affects the comparisons with competitors.
* The simplifying assumptions of the PTM model are not thoroughly analyzed, and the authors should discuss scenarios where the model may fail.
* The synthetic experiments are incomplete, and the authors should include cases both favorable and unfavorable to the PTM.
* The impact of the threshold δ on accuracy vs. scalability is not thoroughly analyzed.
* The authors should provide more details on why the PTM avoids accuracy loss that harms a-MMSB and why ignoring non-edges is justified in this case.

Originality and Significance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to latent space inference, which is a key challenge in network analysis.
* The PTM model and stochastic variational inference algorithm are original contributions that advance the state of the art in latent space network modeling.
* The paper demonstrates the significance of the proposed approach through experiments on large-scale networks, showing competitive or improved accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction.

Quality and Clarity:

* The paper is well-written and clear, with a logical structure and effective use of figures and tables.
* The authors provide a thorough explanation of the PTM model and stochastic variational inference algorithm.
* The experiments are well-designed and thoroughly explained, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of latent space network modeling, introducing a novel and efficient approach to probabilistic latent space inference. While there are some areas for improvement, the paper is well-written and clear, and the experiments are thorough and well-designed.
