- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and stochastic variational inference to scale latent-space network inference to millions of nodes and hundreds of roles.  
- Main technical contribution: parameter reduction from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) via parameter sharing, enabling much faster inference.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized; methodology and experiments are presented logically.  
- Primary concern: paper does not convincingly demonstrate that large-K scalability is necessary or beneficial in practice.  
- Empirical evaluation emphasizes speedup but fails to show tangible advantages in latent-space recovery or downstream tasks due to larger K.  
- Missing critical experiment: direct comparison of PTM vs MMTM performance as a function of K to show accuracy degradation in MMTM and PTM’s robustness.  
- Model selection is unclear: authors do not explain how K was chosen for real datasets, risking biased comparisons.  
- Simplifying assumptions (parameter sharing) are under-analyzed; authors should discuss scenarios where this fails (e.g., B with many distinct values).  
- Synthetic experiments are incomplete and irreproducible: true B choices aren’t described and adversarial cases against PTM (non-sparse B) are absent.  
- The effect of the δ-subsampling threshold on the accuracy–scalability trade-off is not sufficiently studied.  
- Claims that downsampling avoids accuracy loss are unconvincing; justification for ignoring non-edges in PTM vs MMSB is weak.  
- Local-update approximation concerns: impact on quality as K grows is unexplored, and sampling sizes (2K, 4K, 100K) lack justification.  
- Experimental fairness is doubtful due to missing analyses and unclear model-selection procedures for competitors.  
- Originality is moderate: parameter constraints for scalability are interesting but the modeling advances are incremental.  
- Significance is conditional: method could be impactful if simplifying assumptions hold, but title overstates scope of scalability claim.  
- Probabilistic validity is questionable: bag-of-triples assumption may not correspond to real networks, risking invalid generative likelihoods.  
- Overall verdict: technically sound and fast, but lacks compelling empirical evidence and robustness analysis; reviewer recommends rejection pending stronger experiments and analyses.
