- Proposes a scalable method for latent-space inference in very large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious model, and stochastic variational inference.  
- Main contribution: a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model with parameter sharing and SVI for massive networks.  
- Demonstrates scalability by analyzing real networks with over a million vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction versus state-of-the-art probabilistic approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, well-executed, and clearly explains the approach.  
- Request to define and unpack “parsimonious modeling” for clarity.  
- Correct the misleading claim that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy (cites Gopalan et al. showing otherwise).  
- The analogy to LDA is unfair because the triangle model can generate non-networks, unlike LDA’s coherent topic generation.  
- Cite Hoffman et al. in addition to Amari and Sato, since Sato’s algorithm doesn’t cover local variables and Hoffman provides SVI theory for that case.  
- Provide clearer details on how latent-space power-law networks were generated.  
- Compare against Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB algorithm (or include it alongside batch MMSB) in experiments.  
- Suggest minor wording edits (e.g., “mixture-membership” → “mixed-membership”).  
- Recommend removing the manuscript’s final sentence as unnecessary/misleading.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel scalable approach, strong experimental validation, clear writing, wide applicability (community detection, link prediction, exploratory analysis).  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs minor clarifications/corrections, unfair LDA analogy, and requires more detailed comparisons to some state-of-the-art methods.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
