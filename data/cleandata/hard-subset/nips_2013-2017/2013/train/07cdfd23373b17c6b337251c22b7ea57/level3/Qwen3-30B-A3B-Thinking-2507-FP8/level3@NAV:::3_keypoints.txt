- Presents a scalable latent-space inference method using a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) with stochastic variational inference.  
- Key technical contribution: parameter-sharing subclass reduces parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Algorithmic complexity: stochastic variational inference achieves O(NK) time.  
- Empirical results: effective on networks with over a million vertices and hundreds of latent roles, far faster and more scalable than prior methods.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and addresses an important network-analysis problem.  
- Experiments show competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction accuracy with orders-of-magnitude speedups.  
- Revision needed: define/unpack the term “parsimonious modeling” (page 1).  
- Revision needed: revise claim that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy—Gopalan et al. (2014) contradicts this (page 1).  
- Revision needed: remove or rework the LDA analogy (page 4) because triangle-model samples can produce non-networks, making the comparison invalid.  
- Theoretical citation fix: cite Hoffman et al. (2013) alongside Amari and Sato for stochastic variational inference with local variables (page 5).  
- Clarify methodology: explain how power-law networks were generated in the latent space (page 6).  
- Baseline comparison: include Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB algorithm in experiments in addition to batch MMSB.  
- Minor edits: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership” (page 2), remove the last sentence on page 8, and cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference” in the bibliography.  
- Overall recommendation: core idea of parameter-sharing in the triangular model is novel and well-executed; with revisions the paper is an excellent fit for NIPS.
