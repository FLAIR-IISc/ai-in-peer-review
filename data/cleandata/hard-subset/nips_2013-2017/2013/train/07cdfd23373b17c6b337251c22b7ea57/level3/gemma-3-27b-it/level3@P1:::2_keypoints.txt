- Paper proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using triangle counts as a compact network representation and SVI for scalable latent-role inference.
- Novelty: combines triangle-based representation, parameter sharing, and scalable inference to enable analysis of very large networks.
- Scalability: Achieves O(NK) inference complexity and demonstrates large speedups on networks up to 1M nodes.
- Parsimonious modeling: Parameter-sharing reduces model complexity from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K), improving scalability and likely generalization.
- Clear algorithm derivation: SVI with stochastic natural gradients and explicit global/local update rules are well-presented.
- Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world data show competitive accuracy with substantial speed improvements.
- Practicality: Rapid convergence (2–5 passes) and memory savings by discarding local variational parameters make the method practical.
- Conceptual clarity issues: Framing connections to ERGMs and distinctions like Delta^1 vs Delta^2 are under-explained.
- Subsampling justification: Delta-subsampling of triangles lacks adequate rationale and deeper analysis of its impact on accuracy.
- Role interpretability: The paper does not sufficiently explain or interpret the semantics of inferred latent roles.
- Model assumptions: Method appears limited to undirected networks and lacks discussion of extensions to directed graphs.
- Experimental limitations: Link prediction holds out only 10% of edges and synthetic power-law data generation is underspecified, weakening reproducibility and robustness claims.
- Inconsistent complexity reporting: Claims of O(K) parameters conflict with mentions of an O(K^3) Gibbs sampler, requiring clarification.
- Counterintuitive result: PTM outperforming MMSB on MMSB-generated data is unexpected and needs further investigation.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a core scalability challenge, offers substantial technical contributions, and provides strong empirical evidence.
- Arguments against acceptance: Presentation clarity, methodological justifications, interpretability, and some experimental details need improvement.
- Overall assessment: Solid, impactful contribution worth acceptance conditional on clarifying conceptual points, subsampling effects, parameter-sharing explanation, and role interpretability.
