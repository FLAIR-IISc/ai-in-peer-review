- Proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference method for large networks using a Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and fast stochastic variational inference.  
- PTM is more compact and lower-dimensional than the mixed-membership triangular model (MMTM), enabling more efficient inference.  
- Main contribution: a scalable approximate inference algorithm for the triangle model, a parameter-sharing subclass, and use of SVI for very large networks.  
- The algorithm achieves latent-space recovery accuracy comparable to or better than prior work while running in a fraction of the time.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and experimentally strong, scaling to millions of nodes and hundreds of roles on a single multi-core machine.  
- Request clearer definition and explanation of the term “parsimonious modeling” in the context of PTM.  
- Revise the claim that down-sampling zeros compromises accuracy because Gopalan et al. do not support that statement.  
- The analogy between PTM and LDA is misleading; authors should clarify differences (PTM’s complexity and ability to produce non-networks).  
- Cite Hoffman et al. in addition to Amari and Sato, since Sato’s algorithm doesn’t handle local variables and Hoffman provides appropriate theory.  
- Provide more details on how power-law networks were generated in the latent space for experiments.  
- Compare the algorithm to Gopalan et al.’s fast MMSB algorithm (in addition to or instead of batch MMSB).  
- Minor wording fix: change “mixture-membership” to “mixed-membership.”  
- Minor text suggestion: remove the last sentence on p8 and end with the previous sentence for better flow.  
- Cite the JMLR version of “Stochastic Variational Inference.”  
- Overall verdict: strong, significant contribution with impressive scalability and results, pending clarifications, additional comparisons, and improved citations.
