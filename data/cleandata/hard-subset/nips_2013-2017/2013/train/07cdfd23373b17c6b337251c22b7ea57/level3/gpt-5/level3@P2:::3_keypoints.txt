- Paper proposes a scalable latent-space model representing networks as bags of triangles with a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) reducing triangle parameters from O(K3) to O(K).  
- Introduces structured SVI with natural gradients and a “mixture-of-deltas” variational family yielding O(NK) global and O(K) local complexity, enabling million-node inference on one machine.  
- Empirically recovers latent roles accurately on synthetic data, converges in few passes, and gives competitive or better link prediction much faster than prior probabilistic methods.  

Strengths:
- Clear modeling: principled parameter sharing collapsing O(K3) triangle tensors to O(K) while preserving role distinctions.  
- Scalable inference: mini-batched structured SVI with natural gradients and O(K) local updates that match exact updates empirically.  
- Strong empirical scaling: million-node, K≈100 experiments converge in hours on a single machine.  
- Competitive accuracy: good role recovery on synthetic data and strong link prediction on large social networks.  
- Practical engineering: triangle δ-subsampling and discarding local factors save memory and runtime.  

Weaknesses / Specific comments:
- Define “parsimonious modeling” explicitly as parameter sharing yielding O(K) triangle-generation parameters.  
- Soften claim that a-MMSB downsampling zeros is categorically too slow; acknowledge Gopalan et al. show favorable scaling for negative sampling.  
- LDA analogy is weak because sampled triangles may not form valid graphs; clarify goal is latent-space inference not full generative fidelity and discuss implications.  
- Add citation to Hoffman et al. (JMLR 2013) for SVI convergence with local/global variables; Sato (2001) is insufficient.  
- Include direct a-MMSB baseline (same hardware/threads) or time-vs-accuracy comparisons rather than only textual runtime comparisons.  
- Provide full reproducible details for the power-law simulator (priors, degree mechanism, link parameterization, hub induction, values).  
- Report unseeded initialization results or a practical label-free initializer to assess robustness beyond 2% seeded runs.  
- Quantify the approximation quality of the mixture-of-deltas family (e.g., error vs exact structured update, bounds, failure modes).  
- Minor wording: replace “mixture-membership” with “mixed-membership.”  
- End manuscript with the penultimate sentence; remove the final sentence.  
- Bibliography: cite JMLR version of Hoffman et al. (2013) and complete NIPS references.  

Evaluation:
- Quality: technically sound modeling and inference with good experimental support, but needs a-MMSB comparison and simulator details.  
- Clarity: generally well written; suggested clarifications and citations will improve correctness.  
- Originality: novel parameter-sharing and O(K) local variational approximation in the triangle context.  
- Significance: high practical impact enabling large-scale latent-space inference on a single machine.  

Pros (for acceptance):
- Elegant O(K3)→O(K) reduction with minimal fidelity loss.  
- Scalable, parallelizable SVI with strong empirical convergence and runtime.  
- Competitive or better accuracy on role recovery and link prediction.  
- Addresses an important scalability bottleneck in probabilistic network modeling.  

Cons (against acceptance):
- Lacks direct experimental comparison to a-MMSB.  
- Some theoretical attributions/citations need correction; approximation quality not fully characterized.  
- Reliance on seeded initializations; robustness underexplored.  
- Triangle-only representation may omit edge-level information; trade-offs need discussion.  

Questions for authors:
- Can you provide side-by-side results against a-MMSB with identical compute budgets?  
- How sensitive are results to the choice and size of the off-diagonal set A in the mixture-of-deltas approximation?  
- Do unseeded initializations reach comparable solutions on synthetic data?  

Recommendation:
- Accept after minor revisions addressing clarifications, citation fixes, and if feasible adding an a-MMSB baseline or detailed time/accuracy comparison.
