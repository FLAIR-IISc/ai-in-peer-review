- Presents a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference method for large networks based on three scalability principles: succinct representation, parsimonious model, efficient inference.  
- Uses the bag-of-triangles representation (MMTM) and introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reducing triangle parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing.  
- Proposes a scalable stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with structured mean-field and an O(K) approximation for local updates, yielding overall O(NK) time.  
- Empirically demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMTM and MMSB and competitive or improved latent recovery and link prediction on synthetic and real networks up to 1.1M nodes.  
- Significance: enables latent-space inference on million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine, pushing practical limits of probabilistic network modeling.  
- Novelty and soundness: PTM’s parameter-sharing and the SVI design (structured mean-field + O(K) updates) are well-motivated, non-trivial, and technically sound.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation via the “three principles” and detailed model/algorithm descriptions.  
- Experimental evaluation: comprehensive—synthetic recovery, runtime/convergence plots, large real-world datasets, and strong link-prediction results supporting claims.  
- Limitation: reliance on bag-of-triangles discards single-edge and broader topology information; may perform poorly on sparse or tree-like networks with few triangles.  
- Link-prediction detail unclear: marginalizing over the third node k appears to require summing over N−2 nodes—authors should clarify how this is computed efficiently.  
- O(K) local-update approximation needs direct validation: recommend a small comparison against the full O(K^3) update (e.g., variational bound or NMI) to justify the approximation.  
- Generative limitation: PTM is not a valid graph generative model, preventing simulation-based goodness-of-fit checks—an acceptable but important caveat.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, significant contribution with minor clarifications requested; strongly recommended for acceptance.
