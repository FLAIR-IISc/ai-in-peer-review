The paper proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference method using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) with O(K) parameters, and an SVI algorithm with structured mean-field and O(K) local updates.

Key engineering contribution: an O(K) “mixture-of-deltas” local-update approximation plus δ-subsampling for high-degree nodes enabling linear-in-N-and-K scalability and trivial parallelism.

Technical novelty: grouping role triplets into multiplicity classes (all same, two same, all distinct) to reduce parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).

Inference design: structured mean-field variational family with stochastic natural gradients and an O(K) local variational update that preserves a valid lower bound.

Scalability claim: careful complexity analysis and memory-efficient implementation allow million-node experiments in hours on a single multi-core machine.

Empirical validation: extensive synthetic and real experiments show orders-of-magnitude speedups over probabilistic baselines with competitive or improved role recovery and link prediction.

Positioning: builds on triangle representation and parameter-sharing ideas from prior work, connecting to known challenges in scalable network inference.

Expressiveness concern: PTM’s symmetry assumptions (single Bxx for two-same patterns, single B0 for all-different) may limit modeling of heterogeneous inter-role triangular patterns.

Comparative baseline gap: direct empirical comparisons to a-MMSB on the same datasets are missing and would strengthen claims.

Variational-objective question: mixture-of-deltas with re-sampled off-diagonals A creates a sequence of lower bounds—clarify whether gradients are unbiased for a fixed objective or provide convergence characterization.

Link prediction ambiguity: practical scoring/ranking procedure and its computational complexity (full sum vs. sampling/restricted sums) are not detailed.

Sensitivity and ablations lacking: limited analysis of δ, minibatch size, step-size schedule, number of off-diagonals in A, and comparisons between full structured mean-field and mixture-of-deltas.

Quality: technically sound with clear modeling assumptions and standard variational derivations, but some theoretical aspects (objective consistency under re-sampling, unbiasedness) need clarification.

Clarity: well-organized and clearly written; equivalence classes, algorithm steps, and complexity are explained cleanly; minor clarifications needed for prediction scoring, triangle extraction cost, and bound stability.

Originality: novel combination of known components with an original and impactful O(K) parameterization and O(K) local update for triangular latent-space models.

Significance: high practical impact for large-scale probabilistic network analysis due to substantial speed improvements and competitive accuracy.

Essential suggestions: include a-MMSB comparisons, clarify theoretical status of stochastic gradients with mixture-of-deltas and A re-sampling, and detail link prediction scoring and runtime.

Nice-to-have suggestions: ablations on δ/minibatch/A size and mean-field variants, discuss relaxations of symmetry assumptions (e.g., low-rank/clustering), report sensitivity to hyperparameters/initialization, and release code/triangle pipeline.

Pros: elegant O(K) parameterization and updates, strong scalability with practical implementation, competitive accuracy and fast convergence.

Cons: limited direct a-MMSB comparisons, strong parameter-sharing assumptions may reduce expressiveness, and some theoretical/practical details (objective unbiasedness, prediction complexity) need clarification.

Overall recommendation: strong contribution likely acceptable with added clarity on variational objective under local approximation and inclusion of a-MMSB in empirical comparisons.
