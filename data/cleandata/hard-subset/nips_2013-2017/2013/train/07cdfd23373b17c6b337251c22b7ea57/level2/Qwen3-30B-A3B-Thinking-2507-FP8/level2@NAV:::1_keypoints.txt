- Presents a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference method for large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious triangular model (PTM), and stochastic variational inference (SVI).
- Main claim: achieves orders-of-magnitude faster inference (hours for million-node networks) while maintaining competitive or improved accuracy in latent recovery and link prediction.
- PTM is parsimonious with O(K) parameters versus O(K³) for MMTM, enabling much lower model complexity.
- SVI inference has O(NK) complexity versus O(Nδ²K³) for Gibbs sampling, giving substantial scalability gains.
- Empirical speedups: Brightkite (1M nodes) converges in ~12 minutes and Stanford in ~4 minutes versus 8 days and 18.5 hours for prior methods.
- Synthetic experiments show robust accuracy (NMI > 0.95) as network size grows while competing methods degrade or fail to converge.
- Strong technical quality: clear theoretical analysis, comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real data, and sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Properly contextualizes against related work (MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM) and highlights three integrated scalability principles missing from prior methods.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- Limitation: triangular representation does not preserve all network information and the paper under-discusses scenarios where edge-based representations are necessary or motifs fail to capture structure.
- Suggestion: include more detailed analysis identifying when PTM/SVI outperforms edge-based methods to strengthen accuracy claims.
- Overall judgment: high-quality, significant contribution to scalable network modeling; recommended for acceptance.
