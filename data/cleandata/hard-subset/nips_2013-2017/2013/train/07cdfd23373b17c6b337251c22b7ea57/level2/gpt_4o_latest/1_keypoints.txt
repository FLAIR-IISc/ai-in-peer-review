- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and a stochastic variational inference algorithm for latent space inference in large networks.
- Claims competitive or superior latent recovery and link prediction accuracy while being orders of magnitude faster than prior methods.
- Introduces a compact PTM parameterization that reduces triangle-generating parameters from O(K^3) to O(K).
- Leverages a bag-of-triangles representation for efficiency.
- Presents an inference algorithm with O(NK) complexity.
- Demonstrates impressive scalability: handles networks with >1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.
- Empirically outperforms baselines in runtime and achieves competitive or better accuracy on synthetic and real data.
- Practical utility for large-scale tasks like community detection and link prediction; likely to be adopted by practitioners.
- Well-organized paper with clear explanations, figures, and tables.
- Does not thoroughly discuss limitations beyond noting PTM is not a generative network model.
- Potential information loss from the bag-of-triangles representation and reliance on δ-subsampling for high-degree nodes not fully explored.
- Method appears sensitive to careful initialization, which may hurt robustness when ground-truth is unavailable.
- Comparisons are limited to triangle-based methods and MMSB; lacks comparisons to other scalable embedding/graph neural methods.
- Reproducibility concern: no public code or detailed pseudocode provided.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles critical scalability challenge, strong empirical validation, and a novel parsimonious parameterization.
- Arguments against acceptance: initialization sensitivity and limited discussion of limitations; missing broader comparisons and public code.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestion to address limitations and improve reproducibility in future revisions.
