- Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious triangular model (PTM), and stochastic variational inference.  
- Reduces triangular model parameterization from O(K³) (MMTM) to O(K) via parameter sharing across role configurations.  
- Achieves linear scaling in number of nodes and latent roles, enabling inference on million-node networks with hundreds of roles on a single machine.  
- Stochastic variational inference further lowers complexity to O(NK), yielding hours-long runtimes instead of days/weeks.  
- Addresses a major scalability bottleneck in probabilistic latent-space/network models.  
- Empirically matches or improves latent-space recovery and link-prediction accuracy versus state-of-the-art while being orders of magnitude faster.  
- Strong experimental validation on synthetic and real networks demonstrating convergence and dramatic speedups over MMSB/MMTM.  
- Technically sound and well-structured with clear generative model, variational inference, and implementation details.  
- Theoretical complexity analysis is thorough and correctly identifies prior bottlenecks.  
- Limitation: the model does not generate valid networks (omits empty and single-edge triples), focusing on inference rather than network simulation.  
- Strength: elegant parameter-sharing strategy captures meaningful structural patterns while substantially reducing dimensionality.  
- Strength: O(K) local update approximation is well-justified, efficiently implemented, and trade-offs are clearly explained.  
- Could be strengthened by more explicitly linking triangular motifs to latent-space semantics and showing a concrete recovered latent-space example on a real network.  
- Well-situated in the literature, clearly differentiating from MMSB and MMTM and citing relevant prior work.  
- Significant practical impact for large-scale network analysis (social networks, recommender systems, etc.) by making latent inference tractable at million-node scale.  
- Meets NIPS evaluation criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance); minor suggestion to discuss extensions to dynamic networks or node attributes.
