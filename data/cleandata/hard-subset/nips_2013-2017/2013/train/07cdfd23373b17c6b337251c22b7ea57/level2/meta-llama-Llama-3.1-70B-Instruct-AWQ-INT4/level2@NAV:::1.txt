This paper proposes a scalable approach for making inference about latent spaces of large networks. The authors introduce a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) that defines the generative process for a bag of triangular motifs, which is a more compact and lower-dimensional representation of networks compared to existing methods. The PTM is combined with a stochastic variational inference algorithm that has a time complexity of O(NK), making it more efficient than previous methods.

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The proposed PTM model is more scalable and efficient than existing methods for latent space inference in large networks.
2. The stochastic variational inference algorithm is effective in recovering latent space roles and achieving competitive or improved accuracy for link prediction.

The support for these claims is based on:

1. Theoretical analysis of the PTM model and the stochastic variational inference algorithm, which shows that they have a lower time complexity than existing methods.
2. Experimental results on synthetic and real-world networks, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in recovering latent space roles and achieving competitive or improved accuracy for link prediction.

The usefulness of the ideas presented in the paper is evident in the fact that they can be applied to large-scale networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of latent roles, which is a challenging task for existing methods. The paper also provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed approach, including a comparison with existing methods and an analysis of the convergence of the stochastic variational algorithm.

The paper reflects common knowledge in the field, and the authors demonstrate a good understanding of relevant literature. The references provided are comprehensive and relevant to the topic.

The novelty of the work lies in the proposal of a new model (PTM) and a stochastic variational inference algorithm that can efficiently handle large-scale networks. The paper also provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed approach, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The completeness of the paper is good, and the authors provide sufficient details for reproducibility. However, some of the technical details, such as the derivation of the stochastic variational inference algorithm, are relegated to the appendix.

The limitations of the paper are acknowledged by the authors, including the fact that the PTM model is not a generative model of networks per se and that the stochastic variational inference algorithm may not always converge to the global optimum.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas. The reviewer's criticisms are minor and focused on the presentation of the paper rather than the technical content.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

1. The paper proposes a novel and efficient approach for latent space inference in large networks.

3. The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed approach, including a comparison with existing methods and an analysis of the convergence of the stochastic variational algorithm.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
