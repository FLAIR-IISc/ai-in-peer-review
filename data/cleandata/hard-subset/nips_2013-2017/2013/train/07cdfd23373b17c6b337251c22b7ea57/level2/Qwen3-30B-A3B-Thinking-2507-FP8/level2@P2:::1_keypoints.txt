- Proposes a scalable latent-space inference method using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious triangle-generating model (PTM), and stochastic variational inference.  
- Emphasizes three scalability principles: succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference algorithms.  
- Introduces a novel parameter-sharing scheme that reduces triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), enabling O(NK) inference complexity.  
- PTM’s parameter sharing is well-motivated via role-triplet equivalence classes that allow sharing across structurally similar configurations.  
- Develops a stochastic variational inference algorithm with a structured mean-field approximation to capture higher-order dependencies missed by naive mean-field.  
- Experiments demonstrate strong scalability, e.g., latent inference on a 1M-node network in ~4 hours on a single machine, while maintaining competitive accuracy.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with thorough experimental design, evaluations for latent recovery and link prediction, and detailed runtime comparisons showing large speedups.  
- Theoretical justification for the parameter-sharing scheme is superficial; lacks rigorous analysis of when sharing will generalize across network structures.  
- Comparison with MMTM Gibbs sampler may be unfair; authors note Gibbs gets stuck in local minima but do not thoroughly compare solution quality, especially for larger K.  
- Claims of “competitive or improved accuracy” need more evidence, particularly on real-world networks and statistical significance of improvements.  
- Related work is well-covered and contributions are clearly distinguished from prior methods, highlighting simultaneous satisfaction of the three scalability principles.  
- Work has clear practical significance for large-scale network analysis (millions of nodes, hundreds of roles) with compelling runtime gains (e.g., Brightkite 12 minutes vs 8 days).  
- Main remaining weakness: insufficient empirical and theoretical substantiation of accuracy claims; needs broader experiments and deeper theoretical analysis.  
- Overall: strong, impactful paper with well-executed technical contributions and scalability results, but requires deeper validation of accuracy and stronger theoretical support for parameter sharing.
