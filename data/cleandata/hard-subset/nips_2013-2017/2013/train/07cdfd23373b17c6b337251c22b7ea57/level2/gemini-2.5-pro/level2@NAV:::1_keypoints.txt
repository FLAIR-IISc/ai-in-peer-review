- Introduces the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) for scalable probabilistic latent-space inference in large networks.
- PTM reduces interaction parameters from O(K^3) (MMTM) to O(K) via parameter-sharing inspired by a-MMSB.
- Presents a tailored stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with a structured mean-field approximation and an O(K) approximate local update.
- Claims orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMTM and a-MMSB while maintaining or improving latent recovery and link-prediction accuracy.
- Significance: enables inference of hundreds of roles on million-node networks in hours on a single machine, with strong practical impact.
- Originality/quality: novel synthesis of triangular representation, parsimonious parameterization, and specialized SVI, backed by comprehensive experiments and runtime comparisons.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly motivates the problem and method.
- Weakness: PTM is not a generative network model and the triangular representation can discard information, potentially problematic for networks where triadic closure is weak.
- Weakness: the O(K) approximate local update needs more robust empirical validation via direct comparison to the full O(K^3) update on smaller datasets.
- Overall: a substantial, high-quality advance likely to be of strong interest to the community.
