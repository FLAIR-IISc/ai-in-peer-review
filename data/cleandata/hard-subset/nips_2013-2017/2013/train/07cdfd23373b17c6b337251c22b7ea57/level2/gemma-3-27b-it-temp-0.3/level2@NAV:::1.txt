## Review of "A Scalable Approach to Probabilistic Latent Space Inference of Large-Scale Networks"

This paper addresses the critical challenge of scaling probabilistic latent space inference to networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of latent roles – a regime inaccessible to many existing methods. The authors propose a novel approach built on three key principles: succinct network representation (bag of triangular motifs), parsimonious statistical modeling, and scalable stochastic variational inference. They introduce the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which reduces parameter dimensionality compared to prior work like the MMTM, and combine it with a fast stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly articulating the limitations of existing methods (MMSB, a-MMSB, MMTM) in terms of scalability. The core contribution – the PTM – is a clever parameter sharing strategy that demonstrably reduces complexity from O(K<sup>3</sup>) to O(K), enabling inference with a larger number of latent roles. The use of stochastic variational inference with a mini-batch approach further enhances scalability. The experimental results are compelling, showing that the proposed method achieves comparable or improved accuracy to state-of-the-art approaches while being orders of magnitude faster, particularly on large networks. The comparison to the a-MMSB and MMTM implementations is particularly strong, highlighting the practical benefits of the PTM.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the triangular motif representation. While acknowledged, a more thorough exploration of the information loss compared to edge-based representations would be valuable. Additionally, the choice of hyperparameters (α, λ, step sizes) seems somewhat arbitrary; a more systematic exploration or justification would strengthen the results. The convergence analysis, while demonstrating fast convergence, could be enhanced with visualizations of the lower bound over time. Finally, while the code is not provided, mentioning its availability (or planned availability) would be a positive addition.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Significant Scalability Improvement:** The PTM demonstrably scales to networks with millions of nodes and hundreds of latent roles, surpassing existing methods.
*   **Novelty:** The parsimonious parameterization and stochastic variational inference approach are novel and effectively address the scalability bottleneck.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper provides compelling evidence of competitive accuracy and significant speedups on both synthetic and real-world networks.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Potential Information Loss:** The reliance on triangular motifs may lead to information loss compared to edge-based representations.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The choice of hyperparameters could impact performance and warrants further investigation.

**Overall:** This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of network analysis. The proposed method addresses a critical scalability challenge and offers a practical solution for analyzing large-scale networks. Despite minor limitations, the novelty, empirical results, and potential impact warrant acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points mentioned above.
