- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) representing networks as bags of triangular motifs, reducing parameter dimensionality from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference algorithm for PTM with time complexity O(NK), suitable for large networks.  
- Technically sound: claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Experimental results show effective recovery of latent roles and improved link prediction on synthetic and real networks.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear figures/tables.  
- Some derivations (especially the stochastic variational inference) may be hard for non-experts to follow.  
- Presents a novel, original approach addressing scalability issues of existing latent-space inference methods.  
- Thorough comparisons with existing methods demonstrate the proposed approach’s advantages.  
- Significant impact: scalable probabilistic latent inference applicable to social, biological, and information networks.  
- Pro: Novel scalable method with strong experimental validation and clear comparisons.  
- Con: Exposition may be challenging for non-experts, lacks implementation/computational-resource details, and needs more experiments on larger networks.  
- Revision recommendations: add implementation and resource details, include larger-scale experiments, and clarify notation/derivations for non-experts.  
- Overall: well-written and significant contribution; ready for publication after minor revisions.
