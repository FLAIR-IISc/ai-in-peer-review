- Proposes a scalable Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) using triangular motifs as a compact latent-space representation of networks.
- Introduces a stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm with O(NK) time complexity for efficient inference.
- Claims PTM is more scalable and efficient than existing latent-space inference methods.
- Claims SVI effectively recovers latent roles and yields competitive or improved link-prediction accuracy.
- Theoretical analysis shows lower time complexity compared to prior methods.
- Experimental results on synthetic and real networks support role recovery and competitive link prediction.
- Applicable to large-scale networks (millions of nodes, hundreds of roles) with thorough empirical evaluation and convergence analysis.
- Literature review is comprehensive and authors demonstrate good understanding of related work.
- Novelty lies in the new PTM model and a scalable SVI algorithm for large networks.
- Paper is largely complete and reproducible, though some technical derivations are deferred to the appendix.
- Limitation: PTM is not a full generative network model and SVI may not always reach the global optimum.
- Criticisms are minor and focus on presentation rather than core technical contributions.
- For acceptance: novel and efficient approach, strong experimental support, and thorough evaluation.
- Against acceptance: needs more detailed limitation analysis and clearer placement of key technical derivations.
- Reviewer rating 8/10, high confidence, recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
