- Presents a scalable probabilistic latent space inference method using a bag-of-triangles representation, PTM, and stochastic variational inference.
- Motivated by limitations of MMSB/MMTM for networks with millions of nodes and many latent roles.
- Clear argument that prior methods addressed only one or two desired principles; this work aims to unify succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, and scalable inference.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, making technical details accessible.
- Related work coverage is thorough, with appropriate citations and discussion of limitations.
- PTM’s parameter sharing reduces dimensionality from O(K^3) to O(K), a significant contribution.
- Stochastic variational inference with mini-batches and natural gradients appears well-suited for scalability.
- Empirical evaluation shows substantial speedups versus MMTM/MMSB and strong convergence on real-world networks (Brightkite, Stanford).
- Demonstrates practical impact by analyzing networks with ~1M nodes and hundreds of roles on a single machine.
- The combination of parsimonious model and stochastic inference effectively addresses computational bottlenecks.
- Limited discussion of trade-offs from the triangular representation; representation fidelity vs. scalability needs more detail.
- The subsampling threshold δ choice seems arbitrary and lacks sensitivity analysis on performance impact.
- Algorithm dependence on initialization is concerning; robustness and mitigation strategies are not analyzed.
- Synthetic power-law network generation is described too briefly and needs more detail for reproducibility.
- Convergence plots (Figure 2) are hard to interpret due to scaling issues.
- Arguments for acceptance: scalability gains, novel combination of ideas, strong empirical validation, clear presentation, and addressing scalability bottleneck.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of triangular trade-offs, arbitrary δ without sensitivity analysis, initialization dependence, and sparse synthetic data details.
- Overall recommendation: paper is a significant contribution with minor weaknesses and is recommended for acceptance.
