This paper proposes a scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks. The authors develop a parsimonious statistical model and an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm that can analyze real networks with over a million vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine in a matter of hours.

The main ideas of this paper are related to previous work on network analysis and latent space models. The authors build upon the idea of representing networks as a bag of triangular motifs, which has been shown to be effective in extracting informative mixed-membership roles from networks. They also draw inspiration from the mixed-membership stochastic blockmodel (MMSB) and the mixed-membership triangular model (MMTM), but improve upon these models by developing a more compact and lower-dimensional parameterization that allows for more efficient inference algorithms.

The strengths of this paper include its ability to scale to large networks, its competitive or improved accuracy for latent space recovery and link prediction, and its efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on synthetic and real networks, showing that their algorithm converges in a fraction of the time reported by recent work on scalable network modeling.

However, there are some limitations to this paper. The authors assume that the number of latent roles is known in advance, which may not always be the case in practice. Additionally, the authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the theoretical properties of their model, such as its consistency and asymptotic behavior.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of network analysis and latent space models. Its scalable approach and efficient inference algorithm make it a valuable tool for analyzing large-scale networks. However, further work is needed to fully understand the theoretical properties of the model and to extend it to more general settings.

Quality: 9/10

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-written description of their approach. The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach. However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the theoretical properties of the model.
