- Presents a scalable approach for probabilistic latent-space inference in large networks.  
- Core idea: combine triangular-motif network representation, a parsimonious statistical model, and stochastic variational inference.  
- Claims these three principles enable scaling to millions of vertices and hundreds of latent roles.  
- Builds on MMSB and MMTM and identifies their quadratic/cubic complexity bottlenecks in number of roles.  
- Leverages triangular motifs (from Ho et al. 2013) as central to complexity reduction.  
- Proposes the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) and a stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm.  
- Achieves linear complexity in number of roles, a major improvement over prior work.  
- Empirical results show significant speedups over state-of-the-art while maintaining competitive accuracy.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on synthetic and real-world networks, including comparisons to MMSB and MMTM.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains technical details and algorithm design.  
- Provides a thoughtful discussion of mean-field limitations and justifies the structured mean-field approach.  
- Includes convergence analysis and a detailed explanation of algorithmic complexity.  
- Needs more detail on δ-subsampling: impact of different δ values on accuracy and runtime.  
- Lacks explicit discussion of how α and λ choices affect expected latent-space properties and parameter-tuning guidance.  
- Convergence analysis could better address step-size parameters (τ0, τ1, κ) and their effects on convergence.  
- Could elaborate strategies to improve MMTM/PTM Gibbs samplers, which are important baselines.  
- Reference to Ho et al. (2013) is vague and should cite the specific paper.  
- Should discuss limitations of triangle-only modeling and how to incorporate edge-based features or other higher-order statistics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant scalability gains, technically sound design, comprehensive experiments, addresses key bottleneck, accessible writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient exploration of parameter impacts (α, λ, δ), deeper convergence-property analysis needed, future directions could be more specific.  
- Overall verdict: strong contribution, recommended for acceptance.
