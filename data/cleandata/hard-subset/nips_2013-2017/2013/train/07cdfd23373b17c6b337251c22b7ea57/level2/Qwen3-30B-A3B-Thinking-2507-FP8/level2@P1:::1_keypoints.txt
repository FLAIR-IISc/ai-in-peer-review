- Proposal: Introduces a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) and stochastic variational inference for scalable latent space inference in large networks.  
- Scalability principles: Replaces O(N²) adjacency matrices with O(Nδ²) motifs, reduces triangle probabilities from O(K³) to O(K), and uses an O(NK) stochastic inference algorithm.  
- Empirical performance: Recovers latent space and predicts links on 1M-node networks in hours on a single machine, outperforming prior methods by orders of magnitude in speed with competitive accuracy.  
- Strength: Novel combination of three scalability principles that prior work only partially addressed.  
- Strength: Parameter sharing (Bxxx, Bxx, B0) cuts MMTM’s O(K³) complexity down to O(K).  
- Strength: O(K) stochastic variational local updates avoid O(K³) exact updates while maintaining accuracy.  
- Strength: Strong experimental evidence—e.g., Brightkite converges in 12 minutes vs. 8 days for a-MMSB; Stanford in 4 minutes vs. 18.5 hours for MMTM.  
- Strength: Ablation study demonstrates superior convergence and accuracy as network size grows, with minimal parallel overhead.  
- Weakness: Choice of neighborhood size δ=50 lacks theoretical justification and discussion of its effect across degree distributions.  
- Weakness: "Mixture-of-deltas" O(K) approximation is well-motivated but needs clearer contextualization and a small illustrative example.  
- Weakness: Does not investigate scalability limits for very large K (e.g., K=500) or discuss upper-bound behavior.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, impactful, technically sound, clearly presented work that meaningfully advances scalable network analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
