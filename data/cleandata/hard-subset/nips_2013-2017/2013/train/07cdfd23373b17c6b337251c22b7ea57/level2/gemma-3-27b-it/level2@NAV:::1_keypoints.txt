- Addresses scalability bottleneck in probabilistic latent space inference for large networks.
- Proposes a novel approach built on triangular motif representation, a parsimonious PTM, and stochastic variational inference.
- PTM reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K) via parameter sharing across latent roles.
- Achieves orders-of-magnitude speedups while maintaining competitive accuracy on synthetic and real datasets.
- Demonstrates scalability to networks with over a million vertices and hundreds of latent roles.
- Thorough experimental evaluation against several baselines, with compelling runtime results on Brightkite and Stanford networks.
- Technical derivations (variational inference and parameter sharing) are clear and well-presented.
- Authors acknowledge limitations: simplified generative model and possible convergence issues in Gibbs sampler baselines.
- Impact of the δ-subsampling parameter on accuracy is mentioned but not thoroughly analyzed.
- Lacks comparison to recent scalable network embedding methods (e.g., GraphSAGE, node2vec) for broader context.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution and well-written; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
