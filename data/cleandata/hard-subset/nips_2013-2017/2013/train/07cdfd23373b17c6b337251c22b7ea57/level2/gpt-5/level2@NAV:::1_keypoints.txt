- Proposal: Scalable probabilistic latent-space inference combining bag-of-triangles, a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) reducing triangle parameters from O(K^3) to O(K), and structured SVI with natural gradients and an O(K) mixture-of-deltas local update.  
- Empirical results: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMTM and MMSB on synthetic and real networks (up to 1M nodes, 100 roles) with competitive or better role recovery and link prediction.  
- Modeling quality: Sound, well-motivated by triadic closure and ERGMs; structured mean-field and mixture-of-deltas are appropriate and clever.  
- Empirical quality: Strong runtime and convergence evidence; accuracy measures (NMI, link prediction) reasonable but need more statistical rigor.  
- Key flaw: SVI step-size inconsistency — Algorithm 1 requires κ ∈ (0.5,1], but Section 5 sets κ = −0.5, violating diminishing-step conditions and likely requiring correction.  
- Reproducibility concerns: Missing repeats/error bars, significance tests for link prediction, and unclear fairness controls for initialization (e.g., seeding with ground-truth roles).  
- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized; some notationally heavy local-update cases and triangle extraction/subsampling would benefit from concise pseudocode and complexity bounds.  
- Originality: Novel and meaningful O(K) triangle parameterization via role-equivalence classes, extending MMTM ideas analogous to a-MMSB for edges.  
- Algorithmic novelty: Structured SVI combined with an O(K) local approximation is new and practically important for scaling.  
- Significance: High — enables single-machine inference at million-node, hundred-role scale and may outperform edge-based probabilistic models on large social graphs.  
- Pros: Principled route to O(NK) inference, substantial empirical speedups, solid probabilistic foundation and practical scalability.  
- Cons: Step-size/exponent inconsistency, lack of robustness/variance analyses, limited baselines on large graphs (no spectral or random-walk embeddings), and limited link-prediction reporting.  
- Suggestions: Correct SVI step-size, report multiple runs with error bars and significance tests, add ablations (δ, size of A, structured vs naive mean-field), compare to strong non-probabilistic baselines, and release code/data splits.
