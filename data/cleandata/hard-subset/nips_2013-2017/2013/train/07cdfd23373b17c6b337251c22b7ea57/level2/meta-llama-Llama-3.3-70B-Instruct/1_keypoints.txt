- Proposes Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) representing networks as a bag of triangular motifs, cutting parameter dimensionality from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Introduces a fast stochastic variational inference algorithm with time complexity O(NK), significantly faster than prior methods.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of model and algorithm.  
- Experimental results show competitive or improved latent-space recovery and link prediction on synthetic and real networks.  
- Empirically achieves orders-of-magnitude speedups over recent scalable latent-space models.  
- Strength: PTM is novel and effective for dimensionality reduction, enabling scaling to large networks.  
- Strength: The stochastic variational inference method is well-designed and converges efficiently.  
- Strength: Experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate superiority over existing approaches.  
- Weakness: Assumes the number of latent roles K is known a priori, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: May not be suitable when K is large, since runtime is still O(NK).  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of limitations and potential extensions.  
- Overall: Significant contribution to scalable network latent-space modeling with strong empirical support, but practical limitations around choosing/scaling K remain.
