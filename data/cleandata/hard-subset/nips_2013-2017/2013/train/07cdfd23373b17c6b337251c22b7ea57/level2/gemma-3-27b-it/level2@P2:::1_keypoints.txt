- Introduces a scalable approach for probabilistic latent space inference on very large networks using a bag-of-triangles representation, a parsimonious model, and stochastic variational inference.  
- Core technical contribution: Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) reduces parameter complexity from O(K^3) to O(K).  
- Develops a stochastic natural gradient ascent algorithm enabling large-scale inference and faster convergence than batch methods.  
- Demonstrates significant runtime speedups and competitive accuracy on synthetic and real networks, scaling to >1M vertices and 100 latent roles.  
- Well-motivated paper with a clear conceptual framework: succinct representation, parsimonious modeling, scalable inference.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and compares against relevant baselines, showing empirical gains in runtime and accuracy.  
- Convergence analysis is convincing, showing faster convergence than batch methods and competitive real-data performance.  
- Sensitivity to δ-subsampling is under-explored; lacks ablation study varying δ and its effect on performance and runtime.  
- Comparison to MMSB is incomplete—MMSB results reported only for small networks; showing performance on somewhat larger networks would strengthen claims.  
- Hyperparameter choices (α, λ, τ0, τ1, κ) lack systematic sensitivity analysis or justification.  
- The structured mean-field approximation is insufficiently explained; the paper does not fully justify why naive mean-field fails and why the chosen structure is superior.  
- The independence assumption within equivalence classes (mixture-of-deltas) needs further justification and assessment of its impact on accuracy.  
- Paper could provide concrete examples of distinct latent roles in studied datasets (e.g., YouTube) to strengthen practical relevance.  
- Although PTM isn’t presented as a generative model, a brief discussion of possible generative extensions would be valuable.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and scalable contribution with solid empirical support; recommended for acceptance despite some methodological and empirical analyses that could be deepened.
