- Presents a three-part scalable framework: bag-of-triangles representation, Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM) with linear-in-K parameters, and an O(NK) stochastic variational inference algorithm.  
- PTM achieves parsimony by sharing parameters across role configurations, breaking the O(K^3) bottleneck of prior triangular models like MMTM.  
- Introduces an O(K) approximation for local updates (mixture-of-deltas) and uses structured mean-field with natural gradients to enable inference on million-node networks with hundreds of roles.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show orders-of-magnitude speedups over MMTM/MMSB while maintaining or improving latent recovery and link prediction.  
- Manuscript is technically sound, well-written, and clearly motivates each component relative to limitations of MMSB/MMTM.  
- PTM generative process and parameter-sharing scheme are clearly explained; variational derivation is detailed and appropriate.  
- Experiments are thorough with relevant baselines, clear setup, and convergence plots illustrating stochastic vs. batch efficiency.  
- Lacks a small comparative experiment validating that the mixture-of-deltas O(K) update matches the full O(K^3) update in practice.  
- Link prediction procedure involves marginalizing over all third nodes; the paper should explain how this was implemented efficiently for reproducibility.  
- Original combination of triangle-based representation and stochastic VI is novel, and PTM adapts a-MMSB ideas to higher-order motifs effectively.  
- Demonstrates significant practical impact (e.g., 100 roles on a million-node graph in hours on a single machine), likely to be influential.  
- PTM’s parameter-sharing relies on strong assumptions about triad role counts; the paper should discuss structural implications and trade-offs between scalability and expressiveness.  
- Should more explicitly analyze limitations of the bag-of-triangles representation (e.g., performance on sparse/low-clustering networks) and sensitivity to δ-subsampling.  
- Needs clearer description and computational cost analysis of the link prediction scoring formula to improve clarity and reproducibility.
