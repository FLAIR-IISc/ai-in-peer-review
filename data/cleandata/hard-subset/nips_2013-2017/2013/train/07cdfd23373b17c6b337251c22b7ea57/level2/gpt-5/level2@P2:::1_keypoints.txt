- Introduces Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM): bag-of-triangles latent model with O(K) triangle parameters via parameter tying (B0, Bxx, Bxxx).  
- Uses structured stochastic variational inference with natural gradients and a mixture-of-deltas local posterior to get O(K) local updates and O(NK) global updates.  
- Empirical results show competitive or better role recovery and link prediction versus MMSB and MMTM/PTM Gibbs at much lower runtime, including a 1M-node K=100 run in 4 hours on 8 threads.  
- Novelty: combining bag-of-triangles motif representation with an O(K) parameterization and scalable SVI is a meaningful and novel contribution.  
- Modeling/inference strengths: parameter-tying is well motivated and reduces complexity; structured variational family and SVI derivation are sound.  
- Computational approximation: mixture-of-deltas support restriction is computationally effective but its monotonicity effects on the ELBO need clarification.  
- Synthetic experiments convincingly demonstrate scalability and robust recovery as N and K grow.  
- Real-data link prediction favors PTM on large social graphs, but comparisons lack simple triangle-based heuristics as baselines.  
- Expressivity concern: sharing Bxx across all minority roles may miss pair-specific interactions and hurt modeling of disassortative or heterogeneous triads.  
- Approximation quality concern: need quantitative comparisons to full O(K^3) parameterization on small graphs to validate the O(K) local update approximation.  
- Sensitivity issue: results may depend on the size/composition of the reduced support A; sensitivity analyses are missing.  
- Evaluation leakage risk: must clarify whether triangles are re-extracted after masking edges to avoid using held-out edges in training motifs.  
- Subsampling (δ) central to scalability: need sensitivity analyses showing δ’s effect on accuracy and runtime and reporting how often high-degree nodes are truncated.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written; explanations of equivalence classes and probabilities are clear.  
- Presentation suggestions: move key local update equations from appendix to main text and provide clearer pseudocode for constructing/resampling A.  
- Memory/implementation: explicitly state memory footprint and how sufficient statistics are accumulated and discarded.  
- Originality assessment: moderate-to-high—builds on known motif modeling but applies a novel, principled O(K) parameterization with scalable SVI.  
- Significance: high—O(NK) inference with million-node scale and hundreds of roles on a single machine is compelling.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong scalability, competitive accuracy, large-scale empirical validation, and clear exposition of practical implementation.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited ablations on local approximation and subsampling, potential expressivity limits, incomplete baselines, and insufficient runtime/reproducibility details.  
- Key author questions: does re-randomizing A guarantee non-decreasing ELBO in expectation; how is K chosen; are triangles re-extracted after masking; sensitivity to α and λ; report memory and wall-clock breakdown for the 1M run.  
- Suggested experiments: ablate exact vs approximate local updates on small graphs, vary size/composition of A, test δ-subsampling sensitivity, and add triangle-based and degree-corrected baselines.  
- Reproducibility suggestion: provide more runtime/environment details, stopping criteria, and release code or detailed pseudocode.  
- Overall verdict: solid, well-executed paper with meaningful algorithmic contributions and strong empirical scalability, pending clarifications and additional ablations.
