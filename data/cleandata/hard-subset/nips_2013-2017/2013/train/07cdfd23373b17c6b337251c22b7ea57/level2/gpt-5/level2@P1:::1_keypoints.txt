- Summary: Proposes scalable latent-role inference by combining bag-of-triangles, a parsimonious triangular latent-role model (PTM) with O(K) parameters, and SVI with structured mean-field and a mixture-of-deltas O(K) local update.
- Key computational claim: Enables O(NK) global updates and O(K) local updates, recovering roles on synthetic data and scaling to ~1M nodes and 100 roles in hours.
- Modeling: PTM sharply reduces O(K3) role-triplet complexity into three parameter families (Bxxx, Bxx, B0) and is adequate for tested datasets, though it models triangles only rather than full networks.
- Inference: Structured mean-field avoids rank-1 pathologies; mini-batch SVI is appropriate; the mixture-of-deltas for O(K) local updates is clever and practically important.
- Theory concern 1: Unclear unbiasedness/convergence of stochastic natural gradients when using the mixture-of-deltas approximation with re-sampled candidate sets A; convergence to the original bound is not established.
- Theory concern 2: Reported step-size exponent κ = −0.5 contradicts Robbins–Monro conditions (appears to be a typo) and must be corrected.
- Empirics: Synthetic recovery experiments are well designed and baselines fairly compared; real-data experiments convincingly demonstrate scalability and fast convergence.
- Empirics limitation: Some experimental details, ablations (exact vs approximate local updates, sensitivity to δ), and initialization details are missing.
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well organized; key update equations are in the Appendix but the step-size inconsistency should be fixed.
- Originality: Novel and effective combination of prior ideas; the PTM design and mixture-of-deltas approximation appear original and impactful.
- Significance: Advances scalability for probabilistic mixed-membership/role models to hundreds of roles and million-node graphs while maintaining competitive accuracy.
- Pro: Strong scalability and orders-of-magnitude speedup over prior probabilistic approaches (a-MMSB, MMTM).
- Pro: Sensible structured variational approximation and memory-efficient implementation that discards local factors.
- Pro: Competitive or improved accuracy on latent role recovery and link prediction across datasets.
- Pro: Clear articulation of modeling choices and computational trade-offs.
- Con: Missing theoretical justification for convergence under inexact local (mixture-of-deltas) updates.
- Con: Potential underfitting from aggressive parameter sharing (B0 and Bxx may miss role-specific triadic asymmetries).
- Con: Limited experimental ablations and sensitivity analyses (δ, mini-batch size, exact vs approximate local updates).
- Con: Possible typo/issue in SVI step-size schedule (sign of κ).
- Request 1: Clarify whether stochastic natural gradient remains unbiased with mixture-of-deltas and re-sampling, or cite inexact SVI convergence results.
- Request 2: Confirm if κ = −0.5 is a typo and report actual step-size schedule satisfying ∑ρt = ∞ and ∑ρt2 < ∞.
- Request 3: Discuss extending PTM to allow role-pair-specific parameters for two-equal-one-different triads or multiple B0 components and provide an ablation on parameter count vs accuracy.
- Request 4: Specify link-prediction scoring details: normalization over k, negative sampling, metric (AUC/PR), splits, and variance reporting.
- Request 5: Report sensitivity to δ and mini-batch size, memory usage, triangle-extraction time, and results with heuristic (no-seed) initialization.
- Request 6: Provide an exact O(K3) vs O(K) local update comparison on a mid-sized dataset to quantify any accuracy gap.
- Minor requests: include exact natural gradient forms in the Appendix, state size of A used (e.g., 6K), clarify per-pass complexity (O(mK)+O(NK) and m vs N for datasets), and release code/triangle-extraction tools.
- Overall recommendation: Strong contribution with practical impact; lean toward acceptance pending clarification of SVI step-size and convergence under the local approximation.
