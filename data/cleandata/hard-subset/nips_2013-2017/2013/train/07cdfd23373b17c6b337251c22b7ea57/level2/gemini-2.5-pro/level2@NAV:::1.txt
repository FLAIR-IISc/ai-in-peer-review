This paper presents a highly scalable method for probabilistic latent space inference in large networks. The core contribution is a novel model, the Parsimonious Triangular Model (PTM), which builds upon the "bag-of-triangles" network representation from prior work (MMTM) but drastically reduces model complexity. Where MMTM required O(K^3) parameters to model interactions between K latent roles, PTM uses a clever parameter-sharing scheme inspired by a-MMSB, reducing this to O(K). This parsimonious model is coupled with a tailored stochastic variational inference (SVI) algorithm, featuring a structured mean-field approximation and an efficient O(K) approximate local update. The authors claim this approach is orders of magnitude faster than existing methods like MMTM and a-MMSB, while maintaining or improving accuracy on tasks like latent space recovery and link prediction.

**Strengths:**

*   **Significance and Impact:** The paper tackles the critical and difficult problem of scaling probabilistic network models. The ability to infer hundreds of latent roles on million-node networks in a few hours on a single machine is a significant practical and scientific achievement. The results convincingly demonstrate a state-of-the-art method that will likely be widely used by researchers and practitioners.
*   **Originality and Quality:** The synthesis of triangular representations, a parsimonious parameterization, and a specialized SVI algorithm is novel and effective. The claims are exceptionally well-supported by a comprehensive set of experiments on both synthetic and large-scale real-world data. The runtime comparisons against strong baselines like MMTM and a-MMSB are direct and highlight the dramatic performance gains. The technical work is sound and the results are impressive.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized. It effectively motivates the problem, logically develops the model and inference algorithm, and presents experimental results in an easy-to-understand manner.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Model Limitations:** The authors correctly acknowledge that PTM is not a generative model for networks and that the triangular representation discards some information. It would strengthen the paper to briefly discuss the potential limitations of this representation—for instance, its suitability for networks where triadic closure is less meaningful.
*   **Approximation Justification:** A key component for scalability is the O(K) approximation to the local variational update. While the authors state it performs as well as the full O(K^3) update, this claim could be more robustly supported with a direct empirical comparison on a smaller dataset where the full update is feasible.

Overall, this is an excellent paper presenting a substantial advance in scalable network analysis. The proposed method is elegant, effective, and thoroughly evaluated. The weaknesses are minor and do not detract from the significance of the contribution. This work is of high quality and will be of great interest to the NIPS community.
