- Proposes a scalable probabilistic latent-space inference approach for large-scale networks.
- Develops a parsimonious statistical model with a compact, lower-dimensional parameterization.
- Builds on representing networks as a bag of triangular motifs and on MMSB/MMTM ideas.
- Introduces an efficient stochastic variational inference algorithm.
- Can analyze networks with over a million vertices and hundreds of latent roles on a single machine in hours.
- Demonstrates competitive or improved accuracy for latent-space recovery and link prediction.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real networks show much faster convergence than recent scalable methods.
- Assumes the number of latent roles is known in advance (practical limitation).
- Lacks thorough theoretical analysis of properties such as consistency and asymptotic behavior.
- Well-written and clear, though some technical details may be hard for non-experts.
- Contribution is significant via compact parameterization and efficiency, but not entirely novel relative to prior work.
- High potential impact across applications (social networks, recommendation systems).
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Acceptance arguments: significant contribution, scalability, efficient inference, strong experiments.
- Rejection arguments: requires known role count, insufficient theoretical analysis, needs deeper comparison to related work.
