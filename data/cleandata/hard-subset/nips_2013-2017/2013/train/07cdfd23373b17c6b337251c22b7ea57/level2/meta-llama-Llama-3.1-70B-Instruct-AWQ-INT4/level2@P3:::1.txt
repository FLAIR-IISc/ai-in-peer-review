**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks. The authors present a parsimonious triangular model (PTM) that represents networks as a bag of triangular motifs, reducing the dimensionality of the parameter space from O(K3) to O(K). They also develop a stochastic variational inference algorithm that has a time complexity of O(NK), making it suitable for large networks.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the PTM and the stochastic variational inference algorithm. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in recovering latent space roles and performing link prediction on synthetic and real networks.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed approach. The notation is consistent throughout the paper, and the figures and tables are well-organized and easy to understand. However, some of the sections, such as the derivation of the stochastic variational inference algorithm, may be challenging for non-experts to follow.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel approach to probabilistic latent space inference of large-scale networks. The PTM and the stochastic variational inference algorithm are original contributions that address the scalability issues of existing methods. The authors also provide a thorough comparison with existing methods, demonstrating the advantages of their approach.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for network analysis, as it provides a scalable approach to probabilistic latent space inference. The proposed approach can be applied to a wide range of networks, including social networks, biological networks, and information networks. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach in recovering latent space roles and performing link prediction, which are important tasks in network analysis.
