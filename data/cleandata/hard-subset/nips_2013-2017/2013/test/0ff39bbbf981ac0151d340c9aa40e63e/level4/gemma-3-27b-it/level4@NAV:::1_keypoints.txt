- Introduces a matrix completion method for cross-domain classification using labeled auxiliary-domain data plus unlabeled parallel data.  
- Two-stage approach: complete a unified incomplete document-term matrix via projected gradient descent with low-rank and sparsity constraints, then apply LSI and train a classifier.  
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and reports promising experimental results.  
- Clarify the motivation for reducing feature dimension (d → k) with LSI: is it addressing sparsity capacity or mainly computational efficiency?  
- Explain why LSI was chosen over other dimensionality reduction methods (e.g., PCA).  
- Separate the paper’s two contributions—leveraging labeled source data vs. incorporating unlabeled parallel data—and present corresponding results independently.  
- Provide a clear, detailed description of the experimental setup (training/test splits, exact counts of source/target/unlabeled data).  
- Confirm whether section 5.3 varies only the number of unlabeled parallel documents and reconcile the performance discrepancies between Figure 1 and Table 1 (e.g., CL-KCCA vs TSL at 2k).  
- Justify the low-rank assumption for a fully observed document-term matrix, since documents are unlikely to be linear combinations of each other.  
- Give intuition for why TSL outperforms CCA and other multiview methods, and explain why matrix completion is appropriate here.  
- Discuss how the low-rank constraint compares to other relevant techniques and alternatives.  
- Report the algorithm’s running time and compare runtime to baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).  
- Minor corrections: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” in the two instances before Eq.(2) and remove “such as” before Eqs.(4) and (5).  
- Overall assessment: simple, effective, and generally well-written approach but needs the clarifications and additions above.
