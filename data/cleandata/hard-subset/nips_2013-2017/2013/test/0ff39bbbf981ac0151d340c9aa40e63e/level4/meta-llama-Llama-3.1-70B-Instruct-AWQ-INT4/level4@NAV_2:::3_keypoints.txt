- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation learning method: matrix completion to build a parallel document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual document embeddings.  
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification using Amazon product reviews.  
- Demonstrates better performance than several existing cross-language methods, especially when parallel bilingual documents are scarce.  
- Paper is well-written and explanations of method components are clear and easy to follow.  
- Experimental setup is described clearly overall, but some details are split across Table 1, making them harder to follow.  
- Method is technically sound and experimentally effective.  
- The approach appears original and not previously proposed in the literature.  
- The work is significant for improving cross-language text classification, an important NLP application.  
- Lacks comparisons to current state-of-the-art methods in the field.  
- Would benefit from additional experiments using other classifiers (e.g., boosted decision trees).
