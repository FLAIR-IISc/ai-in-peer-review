The paper proposes a matrix completion approach to cross-domain classification that leverages labeled data from an auxiliary domain as well as unlabeled parallel data. The method proceeds in two stages. First, it builds a single incomplete matrix spanning all documents and domains, then completes this matrix under joint low-rank and sparsity constraints using projected gradient descent. Second, it applies LSI to reduce the dimensionality of the completed matrix and trains a standard classifier on the resulting representation. The paper also provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and reports favorable empirical results.

Comments and questions:
- After estimating the completed matrix M, you reduce the feature dimension from d to k with LSI because M “lacks sufficient capacity of handling feature sparseness.” Could you elaborate? Is the primary motivation computational convenience, since M* will be relatively dense even with sparsity regularization? If so, why choose LSI over other dimensionality-reduction methods (e.g., PCA)?
- The paper seems to exploit external data in two distinct ways: (1) leveraging labeled source-language data to improve learning in the target language, and (2) leveraging unlabeled parallel data. If this interpretation is correct, it would help to clearly separate these two aspects and report results for each.
- The experimental setup in Section 5.2 is somewhat unclear. Are you training with (a) 4k labeled source-language documents, (b) 100 labeled target-language documents, and (c) 2k unlabeled parallel documents, and then evaluating on the remaining 1,900 labeled target-language documents? In Section 5.3, do you vary only the number of unlabeled parallel documents from 200 to 2k? If so, why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL for EFM and EGM at 2k in Figure 1, but not in Table 1?
- Could you clarify why a fully observed document–term matrix would be low rank? It is sparse, but typically no document is an exact linear combination of others in the vocabulary space, so its rank should be min(d, k).
- Please provide intuition for why TSL generally outperforms other methods, especially CCA. A multiview approach like CCA seems natural for cross-lingual representation learning, while matrix completion is less obviously so. Is the main advantage the low-rank constraint on M*? How does this compare with alternative techniques?
- What is the runtime of the algorithm? Does projected gradient descent take more, less, or comparable time relative to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?

Suggested edits:
- Change (ij) to (i,j) two sentences before Equation (2).
- Remove “such as” in the sentences preceding Equations (4) and (5).

Overall, the paper presents a matrix completion approach to cross-domain classification. Although several technical details need clarification, the method is simple, effective, and generally clearly presented.
