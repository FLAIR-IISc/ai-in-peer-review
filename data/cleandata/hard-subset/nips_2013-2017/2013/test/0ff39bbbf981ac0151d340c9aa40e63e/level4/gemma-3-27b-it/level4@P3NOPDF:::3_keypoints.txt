- Proposes combining matrix completion with CL-LSI for translingual representation learning; an interesting, potentially impactful idea.  
- Concern that observed performance gains may stem from LSI’s scaling properties interacting with a linear SVM, not from the proposed two-step method.  
- Strong recommendation to test with classifiers less sensitive to feature scaling (e.g., boosted decision trees/Weka) to verify robustness.  
- If results hold across classifiers, the method would be a meaningful and principled contribution to translingual representation learning.  
- Novelty: the integration of matrix completion and CL-LSI appears to be a new contribution.  
- Clarity issue: ambiguous description of the “unified document-term matrix” (unclear how named-entity surface forms are represented).  
- Experimental reporting is fragmented (scattered across Table 1); consolidate experiment details into the main text for readability.  
- Overall: promising work but needs experiments with alternative classifiers and clearer methodological/experimental descriptions.
