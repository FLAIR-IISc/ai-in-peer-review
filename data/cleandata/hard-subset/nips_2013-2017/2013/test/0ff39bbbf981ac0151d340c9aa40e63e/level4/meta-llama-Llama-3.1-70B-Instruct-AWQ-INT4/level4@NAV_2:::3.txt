The paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross language representation learning, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then induces a low dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix.

The proposed method is evaluated by conducting a set of experiments with cross language sentiment classification tasks on Amazon product reviews. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed learning method outperforms a number of other cross language representation learning methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its components, including the matrix completion problem and the latent semantic indexing step. The experimental setup is also well-described, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

In terms of quality, the paper presents a solid contribution to the field of cross language representation learning. The proposed method is technically sound, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness. However, it would be interesting to see the results of the proposed method compared to other state-of-the-art methods in the field.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its components, and the experimental setup is well-described. However, there are a few minor issues with clarity. For example, the description of the experimental setup is split across Table 1, which can make it a bit difficult to follow.
