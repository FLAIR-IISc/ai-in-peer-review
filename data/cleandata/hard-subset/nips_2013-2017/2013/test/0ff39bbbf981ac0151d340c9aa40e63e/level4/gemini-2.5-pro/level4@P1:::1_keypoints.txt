- Paper proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion via projected gradient descent on a unified document-term matrix, then LSI to obtain shared low-dimensional representations for classification.  
- Authors provide a convergence proof for the optimization and show strong empirical performance on cross-lingual sentiment classification, outperforming several baselines.  
- Strength: method is simple, effective, well-presented, and novel for this task.  
- Clarify motivation for applying LSI to the completed matrix M*: is it for dimensionality/computational efficiency, noise reduction, or another reason, and why LSI rather than PCA or other techniques?  
- Ablation needed to disentangle contributions of labeled source data vs unlabeled parallel data (e.g., performance using only labeled source data).  
- Experimental setup needs clearer description/confirmation (which docs used for representation learning, training, testing) and resolution of apparent inconsistency: Table 1 vs Figure 1 for CL-KCCA vs TSL on EFM/EGM.  
- Provide stronger intuition/justification for the core low-rank assumption of the fully observed document-term matrix.  
- Explain why global matrix completion and low-rank enforcement outperform CCA-like methods that directly learn correlated projections between languages.  
- Report computational complexity and practical running time, with training-time comparisons to baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).  
- Minor fixes: change "(ij) ∈ Ω" to "(i, j) ∈ Ω"; rephrase the Section 4.1 sentence that incorrectly describes f(M) as a decomposition example.
