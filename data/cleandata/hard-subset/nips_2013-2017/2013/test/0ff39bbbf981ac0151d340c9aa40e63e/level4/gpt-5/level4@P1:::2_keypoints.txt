- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: convex matrix completion on a unified bilingual document–term matrix followed by LSI to obtain shared low-dimensional representations.  
- Formulates completion with trace-norm (low-rank), ℓ1 (sparsity), nonnegativity constraints, and squared-loss penalties for observed and missing entries.  
- Solves the problem with projected gradient descent alternating gradient steps, singular value shrinkage, and projection, and provides convergence guarantees.  
- Evaluated on multilingual Amazon reviews across 18 language/category transfer tasks, showing consistent improvements over monolingual and strong cross-lingual baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).  
- Demonstrates particular effectiveness when only a small number of unlabeled parallel documents are available.  
- Experiments are thorough and credible: repeated trials, averages and standard deviations, fixed hyperparameter settings, and clear optimization details.  
- Paper is well organized and reproducible, though the Introduction could more strongly motivate why matrix completion before LSI yields better cross-lingual co-occurrence recovery.  
- Novel application of matrix completion prior to LSI and a tailored projected gradient formulation; approach is practically significant and likely useful when parallel data are scarce.
