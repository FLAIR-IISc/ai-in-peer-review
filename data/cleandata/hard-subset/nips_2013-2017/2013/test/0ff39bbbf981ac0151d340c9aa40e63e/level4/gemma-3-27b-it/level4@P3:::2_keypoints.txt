- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method combining matrix completion with LSA.  
- Uses projected gradient descent for optimization and convergence analysis.  
- Evaluated on cross-lingual sentiment classification with Amazon reviews.  
- Shows improved performance over strong baselines (BOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).  
- Gains are especially notable when parallel data is limited (low-resource settings).  
- Addresses an important, practical problem in multilingual NLP.  
- Builds on prior cross-lingual embedding, topic modeling, and translation-based adaptation work.  
- Novelty lies in applying matrix completion as a preprocessing/refinement step for cross-lingual representations.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including varying amounts of parallel data and multiple tasks.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, clearly written, and reproducible (good parameter description).  
- Suggests elaborating the matrix completion motivation in the introduction (sparsity and co-occurrence handling).  
- Significance: practical approach for leveraging limited parallel data and potential applicability beyond sentiment tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, novelty, comprehensive evaluation, clear presentation, theoretical justification.  
- Arguments against acceptance: improvements are not always large; theoretical analysis of the matrix completion step could be stronger.
