

Review Summary: This paper introduces a two-step approach for learning cross-lingual topic representations via matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis. A projected gradient descent algorithm optimizes the matrix completion step. Experiments on the parallel Amazon review dataset demonstrate that the proposed method surpasses monolingual baselines and cross-lingual baselines in sentiment analysis, achieving high accuracy with relatively modest parallel data requirements. Learning curves across varying unlabeled data sizes confirm the algorithm’s efficiency in acquiring precise cross-lingual representations using limited parallel resources.  

Quality: Experiments are rigorously conducted to substantiate claims. The proposed two-step algorithm is meticulously compared against two baselines (monolingual bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  

Clarity: The paper is well-structured, with all parameters and their selection criteria clearly documented, enabling straightforward reproducibility. A minor suggestion: elaborate on the motivation for matrix completion in the Introduction (currently detailed in Section 3).  

Originality: While cross-lingual representation learning is established, applying matrix completion to this domain is novel. The method demonstrates superior performance over existing literature.  

Significance: The two-step approach consistently outperforms all baselines, establishing matrix completion as a viable preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification. This formulation shows potential for broader applicability across related tasks. The work demonstrates that the proposed method achieves superior results compared to monolingual baselines, cross-lingual baselines (including a state-of-the-art technique), and remains robust across varying sizes of unlabeled parallel data.
