This paper presents a matrix completion framework for cross-domain classification that leverages labeled data from an auxiliary domain as well as unlabeled parallel data. The method has two stages. First, it builds a single incomplete matrix covering all documents and domains, then completes this matrix via projected gradient descent under low-rank and sparsity constraints. Second, it applies LSI to reduce the dimensionality of the completed matrix and trains a standard classifier on the resulting representation. The paper also provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and reports favorable experimental results.

Here are some comments and questions:
- After estimating the completed matrix M, you reduce the feature dimension from d to k with LSI because M “lacks sufficient capacity of handling feature sparseness.” Could you elaborate? Is the main motivation computational, since M* will be relatively dense even with sparsity regularization? If so, why choose LSI over other dimensionality reduction methods (e.g., PCA)?
- There appear to be two distinct components in how external data are exploited: (1) leveraging labeled source-language data to aid learning in the labeled target language, and (2) leveraging unlabeled parallel data. If this is accurate, it would help to clearly separate these aspects and report results for each.
- The experimental setup in section 5.2 is somewhat unclear. Are you using (a) 4k labeled source-language documents, (b) 100 labeled target-language documents, and (c) 2k unlabeled parallel documents for training, and reporting classification results on the remaining 1,900 labeled target-language documents? Then, in section 5.3, are you varying the number of unlabeled parallel documents from 200 to 2k? If so, why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL for EFM and EGM at 2k in Figure 1, but not in Table 1?
- Could you clarify why a fully observed document–term matrix would be low-rank? It is sparse, but typically no document is a linear combination of other documents in the vocabulary, implying the rank would be min(d, k).
- Please provide intuition for why TSL generally outperforms other methods, especially CCA. A multiview approach like CCA seems natural for cross-language representation learning, whereas matrix completion feels less so. Is the primary advantage the low-rank constraint on M*? How does this compare to other techniques?
- What is the running time of the algorithm? Does the projected gradient descent procedure take more, less, or about the same time as methods like CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?

Here are some suggested changes:
- Change (ij) to (i,j) two sentences before Equation (2).
- Remove “such as” in the sentences before Equations (4) and (5).

Overall, the paper presents a matrix completion approach to cross-domain classification. Although several technical points need clarification, the method is simple, effective, and generally clearly presented.
