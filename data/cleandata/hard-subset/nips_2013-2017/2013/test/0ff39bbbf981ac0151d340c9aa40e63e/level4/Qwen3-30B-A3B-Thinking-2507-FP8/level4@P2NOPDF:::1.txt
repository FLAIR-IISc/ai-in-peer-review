

The paper introduces a matrix completion framework for cross-domain classification, leveraging labeled data from an auxiliary domain and unlabeled parallel data. The approach constructs a single incomplete matrix encompassing all documents and domains, then completes it via projected gradient descent under low-rank and sparsity constraints. Subsequently, it applies LSI to reduce the dimensionality of the completed matrix and trains a standard classifier on the reduced representation. The work provides a convergence guarantee for the optimization algorithm and demonstrates strong empirical results.  

**Major Comments:**  
1. **LSI Justification:** The paper states that LSI reduces dimension from *d* to *k* because the completed matrix *M* "lacks sufficient capacity for handling feature sparseness." Please clarify: (a) Is dimensionality reduction primarily for computational efficiency (given *M* becomes dense despite sparsity enforcement), or does it address a specific modeling limitation? (b) Why LSI over alternatives like PCA, which preserves variance more directly?  

2. **Disentangling External Data Utilization:** The method appears to exploit two distinct data sources: (i) labeled auxiliary-domain data for target-domain learning, and (ii) unlabeled parallel data. The paper does not clearly distinguish these aspects or report performance separately for each. Clarifying this separation would strengthen the analysis.  

3. **Experimental Setup Ambiguity (Section 5.2):**  
   - Training data: 4,000 labeled source documents, 100 labeled target documents, and 2,000 unlabeled parallel documents.  
   - Test data: 1,900 labeled target documents (not used in training).  
   - Section 5.3 varies unlabeled parallel documents (200–2,000).  
   **Question:** Why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL in Figure 1 (EFM/EGM at 2,000 unlabeled documents) but not in Table 1? The experimental configurations appear inconsistent.  

4. **Low-Rank Assumption:** The paper assumes a fully observed document-term matrix is low-rank. However, a standard document-term matrix typically has rank *min(n, d)* (where *n* = documents, *d* = vocabulary) and is not inherently low-rank due to sparsity. Please justify this assumption or clarify the context (e.g., under what conditions does low-rank hold?).  

5. **TSL vs. CCA Performance:** TSL consistently outperforms CCA-based methods (e.g., CL-KCCA). Given that multiview approaches like CCA are intuitive for cross-domain representation learning, why does TSL (a matrix completion method) achieve better results? Is the key innovation the low-rank enforcement on *M*, and how does this compare to other low-rank techniques?  

6. **Computational Efficiency:** What is the algorithm’s runtime? How does the projected gradient descent step compare in speed to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?  

**Minor Suggestions:**  
- Correct "(ij)" to "(i,j)" two sentences before Equation (2).  
- Remove "such as" in the sentences preceding Equations (4) and (5).  

**Overall Assessment:**  
The proposed method is conceptually simple, well-motivated, and empirically effective. The technical exposition is clear, and the convergence guarantee adds rigor. Addressing the above points—particularly the low-rank justification, experimental consistency, and LSI rationale—would significantly strengthen the paper. The work makes a valuable contribution to cross-domain learning through its matrix completion formulation.
