This paper proposes a novel two-step method to learn cross-lingual semantic data representations for cross language text classification by exploiting unlabeled parallel bilingual documents. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to infer unobserved feature values of the concatenated document-term matrix in the space of unified vocabulary set from the source and target languages. Then, it performs latent semantic indexing over the completed low-rank document-term matrix to produce a low-dimensional cross-lingual representation of the documents. Monolingual classifiers are then used to conduct cross language text classification based on the learned document representation.

The proposed method is closely related to previous work on cross language text classification, which has demonstrated successful performance on various cross language text classification problems. However, the proposed approach is distinct in that it only requires a small set of unlabeled parallel documents, whereas previous methods require at least translating all the training documents in one language domain.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors have done a thorough job in comparing their proposed method with several baselines, including a monolingual baseline and state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms. The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms all the comparison methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

The application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is novel, and the proposed algorithm achieves better performance than previous methods in the literature. The results of the two-step approach are consistently higher than other baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

One minor comment is that the motivation for using matrix completion could be explained more in the introduction, although it is described in Section 3. Additionally, it would be interesting to see how the proposed method performs on other cross-lingual tasks, such as machine translation or question answering.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed method is a significant contribution to the field of cross-lingual representation learning.
