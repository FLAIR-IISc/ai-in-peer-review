- Proposes a two-step method: matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis to learn cross-lingual topic representations.  
- Optimizes the matrix completion step with a projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on a parallel Amazon review sentiment task.  
- Learning curves show robustness and effectiveness even with limited parallel data and varying amounts of unlabeled data.  
- Experiments are thorough and compare against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear parameter settings and guidance, aiding reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: better motivate the use of matrix completion in the Introduction.  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution.  
- The two-step approach consistently improves performance and may generalize to other applications.
