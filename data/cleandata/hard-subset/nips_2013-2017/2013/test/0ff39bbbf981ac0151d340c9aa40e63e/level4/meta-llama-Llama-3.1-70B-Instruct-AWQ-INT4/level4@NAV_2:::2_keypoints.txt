- Proposes a novel two-step method: matrix completion to build a complete parallel document-term matrix, then latent semantic indexing to induce low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.
- Demonstrates superior performance over several cross-lingual methods, particularly when few parallel bilingual documents are available.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a thorough review of related work and clear motivation.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and convincing, focusing on cross-language sentiment classification tasks.
- Carefully compared against multiple baselines, including monolingual and several cross-lingual approaches.
- Provides detailed analysis of how the size of unlabeled parallel data affects performance.
- Overall high-quality work with consistent and convincing empirical results.
- Originality stems from applying matrix completion to cross-language representation learning, a novel and effective use.
- Significant contribution with practical insights relevant to real-world cross-lingual applications.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective method, comprehensive experiments, and useful analysis of unlabeled data size.
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.
- Arguments against acceptance: needs more thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art cross-language representation methods.
