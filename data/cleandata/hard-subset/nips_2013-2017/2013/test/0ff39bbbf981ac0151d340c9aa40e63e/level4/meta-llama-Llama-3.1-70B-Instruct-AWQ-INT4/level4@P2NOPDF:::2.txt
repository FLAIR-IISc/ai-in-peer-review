This paper proposes a novel two-step method for learning cross-lingual topic representations by leveraging matrix completion and latent semantic analysis techniques. The authors employ a projected gradient descent algorithm to optimize the matrix completion problem, demonstrating its effectiveness in sentiment analysis tasks on a parallel Amazon review dataset. Experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms both mono-lingual and cross-lingual baselines, achieving highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations even with a relatively small number of parallel data points.

The quality of the experiments is high, with thorough comparisons to two baselines (plain bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms. This careful evaluation supports the authors' claims and strengthens the paper's contributions.

The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow and reproduce the results. The authors provide clear explanations of the parameters and their selection process. One minor suggestion is to further elaborate on the motivation behind using matrix completion in the introduction, as it is primarily discussed in Section 3.

Although cross-lingual representation learning is not a new task, the application of matrix completion to this domain is novel and yields better performance than previous methods. The proposed two-step approach consistently outperforms other baselines, demonstrating the potential of matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks. This formulation may also be effective in other tasks, making this paper a valuable contribution to the field.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to cross-lingual topic representation learning, providing a new perspective on how to approach this task. The experimental results are convincing, and the proposed method shows promise for future applications.
