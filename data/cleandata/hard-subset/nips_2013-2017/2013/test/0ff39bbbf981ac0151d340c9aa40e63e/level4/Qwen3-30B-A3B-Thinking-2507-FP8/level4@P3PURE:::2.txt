

Summary: This paper introduces a two-step method for learning cross-lingual topic representations via matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis (LSA). The approach employs projected gradient descent to optimize the matrix completion component. Experiments on parallel Amazon review data demonstrate that the proposed method consistently outperforms both monolingual baselines and cross-lingual baselines—including a state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction technique—in sentiment analysis. Learning curves across varying unlabeled data sizes confirm the algorithm's ability to achieve highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations with minimal parallel data.  

Quality: The experimental evaluation is thorough and well-justified, providing strong support for the claims. The two-step learning algorithm is rigorously compared against two baselines (monolingual bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  

Clarity: The paper is well-structured and clearly written. All parameters and their selection criteria are explicitly detailed, ensuring reproducibility. A minor suggestion: the motivation for using matrix completion should be expanded in the Introduction (it is currently described in Section 3).  

Originality: While cross-lingual representation learning is not novel, the application of matrix completion to this task is original. The proposed method achieves superior performance relative to existing literature, as clearly demonstrated in the results.  

Significance: The two-step approach consistently yields higher accuracy than all baselines. This work establishes that matrix completion serves as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification, with implications for broader applicability across related tasks.
