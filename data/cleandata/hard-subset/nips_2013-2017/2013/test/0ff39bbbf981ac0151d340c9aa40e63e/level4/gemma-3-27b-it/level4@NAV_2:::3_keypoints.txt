- Proposes cross-lingual representation learning by applying matrix completion to create multilingual document vectors, then performing LSI on those representations.  
- Approach appears promising but robustness of results is not yet established.  
- Performance gains may be due to LSI’s scaling properties aligning well with a linear SVM, not inherent method improvements.  
- Recommends evaluation with a scale-invariant classifier (e.g., boosted decision trees) to confirm results are not classifier-dependent.  
- If robust, the method is significant because matrix completion provides a controlled, non-linear inference approach.  
- Novel combination: using matrix completion as a preprocessing step before LSI is uncommon in the literature.  
- Presentation clarity needs improvement, particularly the ambiguous description of the “unified document-term matrix” construction.  
- Unclear whether terms are represented by a single surface form or labeled per language (e.g., language-specific tokens).  
- Experimental details are fragmented (spread across Table 1), making the setup hard to follow.  
- Overall: an interesting idea but requires additional validation with alternative classifiers to rule out scaling artifacts.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, promising empirical gains, effectiveness with few parallel bilingual documents.  
- Arguments against acceptance: uncertain robustness of results and clarity issues in method and experimental description.
