- Proposes a novel two-step method combining matrix completion and latent semantic analysis for cross-lingual topic representation learning.  
- Uses a projected gradient descent algorithm to solve the matrix completion problem.  
- Shows superior performance over mono-lingual and cross-lingual baselines on sentiment analysis with parallel Amazon review data.  
- Learning curves indicate the method learns accurate cross-lingual topic representations with relatively little parallel data.  
- Experiments are strong and thorough, comparing against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible, with clear parameter descriptions and selection procedures.  
- Minor critique: introduction should give a clearer motivation for using matrix completion (though Section 3 explains it).  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is an innovative contribution.  
- Significance: consistently higher results suggest matrix completion is a promising preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and potentially other tasks.
