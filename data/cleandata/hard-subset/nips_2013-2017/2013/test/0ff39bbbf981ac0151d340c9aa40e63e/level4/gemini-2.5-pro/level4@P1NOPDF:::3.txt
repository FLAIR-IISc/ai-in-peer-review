The paper proposes a method for translingual representation learning that involves two steps: first, using matrix completion to create a multilingual vector representation for each term, and second, performing Cross-Lingual Latent Semantic Indexing (CL-LSI) on the resulting vectors.

**Quality**
While this is a promising approach, I have concerns about the robustness of the results. The positive outcomes reported may not stem from the core contribution but could instead be an artifact of LSI's scaling properties being more compatible with a linear SVM compared to OPCA or CCA.

To isolate the effect of the proposed representation from such artifacts, I strongly encourage the authors to evaluate their method using a classifier that is insensitive to feature scaling. Boosted decision trees, for example (as implemented in Weka), would be a suitable choice. If the proposed two-step system still outperforms the baselines when using such a classifier, it would provide much stronger evidence for the method's effectiveness. Without this validation, it is difficult to determine if the result is robust or merely a scaling artifact.

**Significance**
If the results are shown to be robust, the contribution would be significant. The use of matrix completion is particularly interesting as it is a well-principled, non-linear inference technique.

**Novelty**
The proposed approach appears to be novel.

**Clarity**
The paper could be improved in terms of clarity. For instance, on line 113, the authors mention "construct[ing] a unified document-term matrix for all documents." It is unclear whether this involves mapping different surface forms of the same entity (e.g., "Merkelen," "Merkelde") to a single canonical term, or treating each language-specific surface form as a distinct feature. This point requires clarification.

Additionally, the description of the experimental setup is fragmented, with key details distributed between the main text and Table 1. Consolidating this information would make it easier for the reader to understand the evaluation.

**Conclusion**
In summary, the paper presents a novel and interesting idea. However, my primary concern is that the reported gains may be an artifact of the interaction between the representation's scaling and the linear SVM classifier. Verifying the results with a scaling-invariant classifier is crucial to substantiate the paper's claims.
