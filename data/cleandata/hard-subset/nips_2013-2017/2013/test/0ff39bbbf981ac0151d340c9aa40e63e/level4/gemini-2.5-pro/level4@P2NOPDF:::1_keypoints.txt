- Proposes a novel matrix completion method for cross-domain classification using labeled source data and unlabeled parallel data; two-stage: PGD-based completion (low-rank + sparsity) followed by LSI and classifier, with convergence proof and strong empirical results.  
- Method is simple, effective, and well-presented but requires clarifications to strengthen the paper.  
- Clarify motivation for applying LSI after matrix completion and justify choosing LSI over alternatives (e.g., PCA); is it mainly for dimensionality reduction/computational efficiency or to handle feature sparsity?  
- Provide an ablation study to separate and quantify the contributions of labeled source-domain data versus unlabeled parallel data.  
- Explicitly state training/testing splits (e.g., counts of labeled source, labeled target, unlabeled parallel, and held-out target) and resolve the discrepancy between Table 1 and Figure 1 where CL-KCCA appears to outperform TSL at 2,000 parallel documents.  
- Justify the low-rank assumption for document-term matrices and explain why text data should be expected to be low-rank despite bag-of-words representations often being full-rank.  
- Give deeper intuition for why TSL outperforms multi-view methods like CCA; discuss whether global low-rank structure across domains is the main driver and compare implicit assumptions.  
- Discuss computational complexity and empirical running time of the projected gradient descent algorithm relative to baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).  
- Minor edits: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before Eq. (2) and consider removing “such as” before Eqs. (4) and (5) for conciseness.  
- Conclusion: interesting and effective approach; addressing the above concerns will improve clarity and impact.
