Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation learning method that uses matrix completion followed by latent semantic indexing to bridge disjoint language feature spaces.

Exploits a small set of parallel bilingual documents to induce low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.

Relates to prior cross-language methods (MT, bilingual lexicons, comparable corpora) but requires only a small unlabeled parallel corpus.

Evaluated extensively on multilingual Amazon product reviews for cross-language sentiment classification.

Experimental results show significant improvement over several baselines, especially when parallel bilingual documents are scarce.

Technically sound with a clear formulation of the matrix completion problem and the LSI step.

Recommendation to test robustness using different classifiers (e.g., boosted decision trees) since linear SVM may be sensitive to feature rescaling.

Potentially significant impact for cross-language text classification due to the novel use of matrix completion + LSI.

Novel and original idea compared to existing cross-lingual representation methods.

Clarity is generally good but marred by minor issues (experimental setup split across Table 1, some wordy sentences).

Arguments for acceptance: novelty, thorough experiments, and promising results with practical impact.

Arguments against acceptance: need for robustness analysis, questionable choice of only linear SVM, and some clarity issues to fix.
