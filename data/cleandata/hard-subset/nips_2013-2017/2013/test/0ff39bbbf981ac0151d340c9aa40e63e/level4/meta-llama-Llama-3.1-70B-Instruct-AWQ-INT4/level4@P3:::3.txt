This paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which is a crucial task in natural language processing. The authors' approach involves first formulating a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then inducing a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix.

The proposed method is related to previous work in cross-language text classification, which has employed various techniques such as machine translation tools, bilingual lexicons, and cross-lingual representations. However, the authors' approach is distinct in that it only requires a small set of unlabeled parallel documents, whereas other methods often necessitate translating all the training documents in one language domain.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including the matrix completion problem and the latent semantic indexing method. The experimental setup is also well-described, with a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method on 18 cross-language sentiment classification tasks.

In terms of quality, the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by comparing it to four other methods, including TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA. The results show that the proposed method outperforms all other methods in general, with significant improvements in classification accuracy. However, it would be beneficial to investigate the robustness of the results by using other classifiers, such as boosted decision trees, which are insensitive to rescalings of individual features.

The significance of this work lies in its potential to advance the state of the art in cross-language text classification. The proposed method is novel and well-motivated, and the experimental results demonstrate its efficacy. However, further evaluation is necessary to confirm the robustness of the results.

In terms of clarity, the paper is generally well-written, but there are some minor issues that require clarification. For example, the authors should provide more details on how they construct the unified document-term matrix, specifically whether they use one term for every surface form or label each surface form from each language.

Overall, this paper presents a promising approach to cross-language representation learning, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness through comprehensive experiments. However, further evaluation is necessary to confirm the robustness of the results, and the authors should consider using other classifiers to validate their findings.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The proposed method is novel and well-motivated.
* The experimental results demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed method.
* The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The results may not be robust, and further evaluation is necessary to confirm their validity.
* The authors should consider using other classifiers to validate their findings.
* Minor issues of clarity require further explanation.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
