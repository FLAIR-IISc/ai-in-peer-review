- Proposes a two-step approach: impute missing entries in a unified bilingual document-term matrix via matrix completion, then apply LSI to obtain shared low-dimensional document representations.
- Models imputation as a convex matrix completion problem with trace-norm and sparsity regularization and non-negativity constraints.
- Optimizes the convex relaxation with a projected gradient descent algorithm and provides convergence guarantees.
- Evaluates on multilingual Amazon product reviews (EN, FR, DE, JP) for cross-lingual sentiment transfer across 18 transfer tasks.
- Uses a low-resource target setting (100 labeled target examples), repeated trials, linear SVM classifiers, and transparent hyperparameter selection.
- Compares against reasonable baselines (TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) and achieves consistent accuracy gains.
- Demonstrates robustness to limited parallel data, with strong performance even when parallel documents are scarce.
- Technical quality is high: clear specification of optimization, convex relaxation, and convergence analysis.
- Paper is well organized and reproducible, with clear algorithmic and parameter-selection details.
- Novel contribution: using matrix completion to impute cross-lingual features before LSI distinguishes it from prior direct-applications of LSI/KCCA/OPCA.
- Minor critique: introduction should more explicitly motivate why completing the bilingual matrix before LSI better leverages cross-lingual co-occurrence structure.
- Significance: method yields substantial, generalizable improvements and could extend to other multilingual tasks beyond sentiment classification.
