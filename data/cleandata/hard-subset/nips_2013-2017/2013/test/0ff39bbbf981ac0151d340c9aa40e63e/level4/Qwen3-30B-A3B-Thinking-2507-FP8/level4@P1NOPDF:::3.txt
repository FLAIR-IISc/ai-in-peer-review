

The paper introduces a translingual representation learning method that first employs matrix completion to generate multilingual vectors per row, followed by CL-LSI on these vectors.  

**Quality:** While the approach appears promising, I question the robustness of the reported results. The positive outcomes may merely reflect LSI's superior scaling properties relative to OPCA or CCA when used with a linear SVM, rather than the efficacy of the proposed two-stage framework. To validate this, I strongly recommend evaluating the method with a classifier insensitive to feature rescaling—such as boosted decision trees (e.g., in Weka). If this classifier consistently outperforms alternatives when applied to the multilingual vectors, it would provide compelling evidence for the method's validity.  

**Significance:** If the results hold under rigorous validation, this would be a meaningful contribution, as matrix completion offers a well-controlled non-linear inference mechanism for cross-lingual representation.  

**Novelty:** The core idea appears novel within the context of translingual learning.  

**Clarity:** Several ambiguities require clarification. Line 113 states the paper constructs "a unified document-term matrix for all documents." It is unclear whether this involves treating all surface forms as a single term (e.g., "Merkel" across languages) or treating language-specific surface forms as distinct terms (e.g., "Merkelen" and "Merkelde"). Please specify the intended interpretation. Additionally, the experimental setup description is fragmented across Table 1, making it difficult to reconstruct the methodology efficiently.  

Overall, the approach is promising, but the lack of robustness validation via rescaling-insensitive classifiers casts doubt on the results. The authors should address these concerns to strengthen the paper's contribution.
