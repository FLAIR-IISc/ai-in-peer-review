- Paper proposes a two-stage translingual pipeline: matrix completion to build multilingual vectors, then cross-lingual LSI (CL-LSI) for projection and cross-lingual document classification.  
- Reviewer finds the idea appealing but doubts empirical gains are robust and may be due to feature-scaling effects benefiting linear SVMs + LSI.  
- Key methodological checks requested: evaluate scale-insensitive classifiers (e.g., boosted/tree ensembles) to rule out scaling artifacts.  
- Request to report results with standardized/normalized features (per-feature z-score or L2 normalization) across all methods and note whether normalization was already applied.  
- Ask for ablation experiments: (a) matrix completion only, (b) CL-LSI only, (c) vary latent rank/dimension, (d) apply OPCA/CCA on completed matrix to isolate source of gains.  
- Request statistical significance testing or confidence intervals over multiple runs/splits.  
- If gains hold under these checks, the method would be a meaningful contribution, especially for low-resource or partially aligned settings.  
- Combination of matrix completion with CL-LSI is novel and interesting.  
- Major clarity issues: need to specify unified document–term matrix construction (shared vs. language-tagged tokens), normalization/transliteration/stemming, OOV and morphology handling.  
- Experimental setup is fragmented; reviewer requests a concise unified description of datasets, splits, preprocessing, feature weighting, normalization, and evaluation metrics.  
- Matrix completion details missing: objective, solver, hyperparameters (nuclear norm, rank), stopping criteria, sparsity handling, whether completion is global or per-row, and coupling of language blocks.  
- CL-LSI details missing: weighting scheme (TF/TF-IDF/BM25), centering/whitening, latent dimension choice, and how cross-lingual alignment is enforced; clarify interaction with completed vectors.  
- Baselines need to be fair: match dimensionality, normalization, and training data with OPCA/CCA, and consider including stronger recent cross-lingual methods or bilingual embeddings.  
- Robustness analyses requested: sensitivity to hyperparameters, proportion of missing alignments, and language typological differences.  
- Strengths: conceptually clean pipeline, principled inference step via matrix completion, novelty, and practical relevance for partial alignment scenarios.  
- Weaknesses: possible scaling confound with linear SVMs, unclear unified vocabulary and experimental details, and limited classifier/ablation exploration.  
- Concrete suggestions: add tree-based classifier experiments, consistently normalize features, run thorough ablations and significance tests, clarify full pipeline and matrix/completion details, report runtime/scalability, and release code if possible.  
- Overall assessment: promising and novel but borderline due to possible scaling artifacts and insufficient experimental clarity and robustness checks.
