The paper proposes a method for translingual representation learning by leveraging matrix completion to create multilingual vectors for each row, followed by performing Cross-Linguistic Latent Semantic Indexing (CL-LSI) on the multilingual vectors.

Quality: Although the proposed approach appears promising, I have concerns regarding the robustness of the results. The observed positive outcomes might be attributed to the scaling of LSI being more suitable for a linear Support Vector Machine (SVM) compared to Orthogonal Procrustes Problem (OPCA) or Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA). To address this concern, I recommend employing a machine learning system that is invariant to rescalings of individual features, such as boosted decision trees (available in Weka). If the two-step system with boosted decision trees yields better results than other techniques, I believe the findings would be more convincing.

Significance: If the results are indeed robust, the contribution would be substantial, as matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique.

Novelty: The idea presented in the paper seems novel.

Clarity: There were some clarity issues in the paper. For instance, the explanation on line 113 regarding the construction of a unified document-term matrix for all documents is ambiguous. It is unclear whether the authors used one term for every surface form (e.g., "Merkel") or labeled each surface form from each language (e.g., "Merkelen" and "Merkelde"). I suggest providing clarification on this point. Additionally, the description of the experimental setup is dispersed across Table 1, which made it somewhat difficult to understand. The authors may consider reorganizing this section for improved readability.

Overall, the idea is intriguing, and I wish the authors had explored other classifiers beyond linear SVM to further establish the robustness of their results, which currently seem uncertain due to potential scaling artifacts.
