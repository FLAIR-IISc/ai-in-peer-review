- Paper proposes a matrix completion method for cross-domain classification using labeled auxiliary-domain data and unlabeled parallel data, with projected gradient descent for completion and LSI + classifier for final prediction.
- Request clarification on why LSI is used to reduce dimensionality of the completed matrix M^: is it primarily for computational efficiency given M^ becomes denser, and why choose LSI over alternatives like PCA?
- Suggest explicitly separating and reporting results for the two uses of external data: (1) labeled source-language data helping labeled target data, and (2) unlabeled parallel data.
- Ask for clarification of the experimental setup in Section 5.2: confirm numbers used for training (4k source labeled, 100 target labeled, 2k unlabeled parallel) and whether results are reported on the remaining 1.9k target labeled documents.
- Ask whether Section 5.3 varies unlabeled parallel documents from 200 to 2k, and reconcile why CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 for EFM/EGM at 2k but not in Table 1.
- Question the assumption that a fully observed document-term matrix would be low-rank, noting sparsity does not imply low rank and documents are unlikely exact linear combinations of others.
- Request intuition for why TSL generally outperforms methods like CCA and how the proposed matrix-completion low-rank constraint compares conceptually and practically to multiview approaches.
- Ask for runtime and computational-cost comparisons of the projected gradient descent algorithm versus baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).
- Minor suggested edits: replace “(ij)” with “(i,j)” two sentences before Equation (2), and remove “such as” before Equations (4) and (5).
- Overall assessment: method is straightforward, effective, and well-written but several technical points and experimental details need clearer explanation.
