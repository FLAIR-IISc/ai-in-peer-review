The paper proposes a matrix completion approach to cross-domain classification, leveraging labeled data from an auxiliary domain and unlabeled parallel data. The method involves two stages: first, constructing and completing a single incomplete matrix encompassing documents from all domains, enforcing low-rank and sparsity constraints via projected gradient descent; and second, reducing the feature dimension of the completed matrix using Latent Semantic Indexing (LSI) before training a standard classifier on the resulting representation. The paper also provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and demonstrates promising experimental results.

Here are some comments and questions:

- Following the estimation of the completed matrix M, dimensionality reduction to *k* dimensions is performed using LSI due to M’s limitations in handling feature sparseness. Could you elaborate on this reasoning? Is the primary motivation a reduction in computational complexity, given that M will likely be relatively dense even with sparsity enforcement? If so, is there a specific reason LSI was chosen over alternative dimensionality reduction techniques like PCA?
- The paper appears to utilize external data in two distinct ways: leveraging labeled source language data to improve learning on labeled target language data, and leveraging unlabeled parallel data. If this interpretation is correct, it would strengthen the paper to clearly differentiate these two aspects and present results for each separately.
- Regarding the experimental setup (section 5.2), clarification is needed. Was the training data comprised of (a) 4,000 labeled source language documents, (b) 100 labeled target language documents, and (c) 2,000 unlabeled parallel documents? Are classification results then reported on the remaining 1,900 labeled target language documents? If so, the primary difference in section 5.3 is the variation of the number of unlabeled parallel documents from 200 to 2,000? In that case, why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL in Figure 1 for both EFM and EGM at 2,000 documents, while this is not observed in Table 1?
- Could you briefly explain the intuition behind expecting a low-rank structure in a fully observed document-term matrix? While it is sparse, documents are unlikely to be linear combinations of each other in vocabulary space, suggesting a rank of min(*d*, *k*).
- What is the reasoning behind TSL’s general superior performance compared to other methods, particularly CCA? A multi-view approach like CCA seems intuitively well-suited for cross-language representation learning. In contrast, the matrix completion approach feels less natural. It appears the low-rank constraint on M* is a key driving force; how does this compare to other techniques?
- What is the running time of the proposed algorithm? Does the projected gradient descent algorithm exhibit comparable, faster, or slower performance than other methods such as CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?
