- Proposes a novel translingual representation learning method combining matrix completion with Cross-Language LSI to produce multilingual row vectors.
- Concern about robustness: positive results may be due to LSI scaling being more compatible with linear SVM than OPCA/CCA, not the proposed method.
- Recommendation to evaluate with a classifier insensitive to individual feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka) to rule out scaling artifacts.
- If results hold with robust classifiers, the method would be a significant contribution since matrix completion is a well-controlled nonlinear inference technique.
- The idea is novel and original.
- Clarity issue: ambiguous description of the "unified document-term matrix"—unclear if terms are merged across surface forms or kept language-specific.
- Presentation issue: experimental setup is scattered (notably across Table 1), making it hard to follow.
- Overall: intriguing idea but authors should test additional classifiers beyond linear SVM to confirm result robustness.
