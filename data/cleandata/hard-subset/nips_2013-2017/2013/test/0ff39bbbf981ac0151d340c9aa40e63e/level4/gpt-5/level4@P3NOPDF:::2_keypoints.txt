- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual topic representation method: matrix completion via projected gradient descent followed by LSA.
- Evaluated on parallel Amazon reviews for cross-lingual sentiment classification, outperforming monolingual and cross-lingual baselines.
- Shows consistent gains and robustness across varying amounts of parallel unlabeled data, with strong learning-curve evidence of data efficiency.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares against appropriate strong baselines (bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction).
- Projected gradient descent for matrix completion is effective and empirically validated.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and documents parameter settings and selection for reproducibility.
- Introduction lacks a concise high-level motivation explaining why matrix completion is critical for cross-lingual term-document matrices.
- Novelty lies in applying matrix completion as a preprocessing step before LSA for cross-lingual representation learning.
- Results suggest broader applicability beyond sentiment analysis to other tasks needing cross-lingual low-dimensional representations.
- Strength: principled two-step formulation addressing sparsity/missing correspondences in cross-lingual settings.
- Strength: solid empirical evaluation with reproducible parameter reporting and consistent performance gains.
- Weakness: missing ablation studies to isolate the contribution of the completion step and compare alternative methods or ranks.
- Weakness: insufficient optimization details (convergence criteria, step-size schedule, initialization) and lack of complexity/memory analysis for scalability.
- Weakness: no detailed sensitivity analysis to hyperparameters or to varying quality/quantity of parallel data.
- Weakness: statistical significance of comparisons not clearly reported.
- Weakness: limited evaluation domain (Amazon reviews); generalization to other domains/language pairs is unclear.
- Suggestion: add a concise Intro paragraph motivating matrix completion for cross-lingual sparsity/alignment.
- Suggestion: include ablations (LSA alone, different ranks, alternative regularizers) and convergence/time-to-solution plots plus complexity analysis.
- Suggestion: report statistical significance/confidence intervals and discuss extensions to multilingual and weakly aligned corpora.
- Overall: well-executed, novel, and effective approach with minor clarifications and ablations needed; recommend acceptance.
