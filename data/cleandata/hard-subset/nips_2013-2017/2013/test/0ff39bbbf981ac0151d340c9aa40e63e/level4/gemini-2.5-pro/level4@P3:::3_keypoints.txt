- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation method (matrix completion to fill missing bilingual document-term entries, then LSI for shared semantic space).
- Builds a unified (sparse) document-term matrix across source and target languages and treats disjoint vocabularies as missing data.
- Uses matrix completion to recover a dense, low-rank bilingual matrix capturing cross-lingual co-occurrence patterns.
- Applies LSI on the completed matrix to induce low-dimensional shared representations usable by standard classifiers.
- Novelty: combining matrix completion with LSI for cross-lingual learning is a new, well-motivated approach.
- Potential impact: principled method that is promising especially when only a small set of parallel documents is available.
- Strong empirical results: consistently outperforms TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA across 18 sentiment classification tasks.
- Major weakness: evaluation relies solely on linear SVMs, which are sensitive to feature scaling, so gains may reflect scaling effects rather than superior representations.
- Suggestion: validate results with scale-insensitive classifiers (e.g., XGBoost, LightGBM, Random Forests) to confirm robustness.
- Clarity issue: unclear how identical or shared words across languages (e.g., proper nouns, loanwords) are represented—single feature or duplicated by language.
- Clarity issue: experimental setup details are fragmented across text and table captions and should be consolidated for reproducibility.
- Recommendation: promising and novel but borderline — would be a clear accept if additional, scale-invariant evaluation confirms the findings.
