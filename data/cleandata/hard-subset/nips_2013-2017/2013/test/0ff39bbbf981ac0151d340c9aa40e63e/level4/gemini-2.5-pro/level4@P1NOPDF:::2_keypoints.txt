- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual topic representation learning method combining matrix completion with LSA, optimized via projected gradient descent.  
- Evaluated on a parallel Amazon review sentiment task, the method outperforms a mono-lingual baseline and several cross-lingual baselines.  
- Learning curves show strong performance even with relatively small amounts of parallel data.  
- Experiments are thorough and convincingly support the paper’s claims.  
- Rigorous comparisons against two simple baselines (bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  
- All parameters and selection criteria are clearly described, facilitating reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: introduce the intuition/motivation for using matrix completion earlier in the Introduction.  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution.  
- The two-step approach is consistently superior and stable with limited unlabeled parallel data, and may be useful for other tasks.
