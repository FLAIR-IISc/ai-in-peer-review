- Summary: Proposes a two-stage translingual pipeline—matrix completion to densify term vectors, then CL-LSI to produce final representations; outperforms OPCA/CCA on cross-lingual document classification with a linear SVM.  
- Novelty: The combination of matrix completion and CL-LSI is novel and addresses data sparsity in cross-lingual embeddings.  
- Significance: If robust, the approach offers an elegant way to incorporate controlled non-linear inference into cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Major concern: Experimental validation is limited and may not convincingly demonstrate robustness of the reported gains.  
- Classifier sensitivity: Reliance on a linear SVM (sensitive to feature scaling) raises the possibility that improvements stem from favorable scaling properties of LSI, not superior modeling.  
- Suggested experiment: Re-evaluate with classifiers insensitive to feature scaling (e.g., gradient-boosted decision trees) to confirm true method superiority.  
- Clarity issue 1: Unclear how terms are unified across languages (e.g., whether surface variants or declensions are treated as the same term).  
- Clarity issue 2: Experimental setup details are scattered; datasets, preprocessing, and evaluation protocol should be consolidated for reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: Interesting and potentially valuable idea, but current claims are insufficiently supported without robustness checks and clearer methodological details.
