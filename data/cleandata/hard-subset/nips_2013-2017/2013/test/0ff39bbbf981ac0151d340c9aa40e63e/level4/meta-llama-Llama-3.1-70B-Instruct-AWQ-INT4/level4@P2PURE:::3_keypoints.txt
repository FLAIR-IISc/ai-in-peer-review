- Proposes translingual representation learning via matrix completion to create multilingual row vectors followed by Cross-Linguistic LSI (CL-LSI).  
- Results may not be robust: positive outcomes could stem from LSI scaling being better suited to linear SVM than OPCA or CCA.  
- Recommends testing a feature-rescaling-invariant learner (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka) to verify robustness.  
- If robustness holds, contribution is significant because matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference method.  
- The idea appears novel.  
- Clarity issue: ambiguous description of the unified document-term matrix—unclear whether terms are merged by surface form or labeled per language.  
- Experimental setup description is scattered (partly in Table 1) and should be reorganized for readability.  
- Overall: intriguing idea, but authors should evaluate additional classifiers beyond linear SVM to rule out scaling artifacts.
