## Review of NIPS 2013 Submission: [Paper Title - *Please fill in*]

**Summary:** This paper introduces a novel two-step method for learning cross-lingual topic representations, leveraging matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis (LSA). The authors employ a projected gradient descent algorithm to address the matrix completion problem. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed approach outperforms both monolingual and existing cross-lingual baselines in a sentiment analysis task using a parallel Amazon review dataset. Notably, learning curves illustrate the algorithm's ability to learn accurate cross-lingual topic representations even with a limited amount of parallel data.

**Quality:** The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and convincingly supports the claims made in the paper. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is rigorously compared against a diverse set of baselines, including a simple bag-of-words model, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction techniques. The breadth of comparison strengthens the validity of the observed performance gains. The authors clearly detail the experimental setup and provide sufficient information for replication.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. All relevant parameters, including the strategies used for their selection, are explicitly stated, facilitating reproducibility. A minor suggestion would be to expand on the motivation for utilizing matrix completion within the Introduction. While adequately described in Section 3, briefly outlining the rationale earlier would improve the paper’s flow and accessibility for readers less familiar with the technique. 

**Originality:** While cross-lingual representation learning is a well-established research area, the application of matrix completion to this problem is a novel contribution. The results presented clearly demonstrate that the proposed algorithm achieves superior performance compared to previously published methods. This innovative application of matrix completion distinguishes the work and provides a valuable addition to the literature.

**Significance:** The consistent and substantial performance improvements observed over the established baselines are noteworthy. This work establishes that matrix completion can serve as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and suggests that this formulation may extend to other cross-lingual tasks. The authors convincingly demonstrate the benefit of a two-step approach – specifically, performing matrix completion *before* applying LSA for cross-lingual sentiment classification. The stability of the method across varying amounts of labeled parallel data further highlights its practical utility. 

**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong submission that addresses an important problem with a novel and effective approach. The thorough experimental evaluation and clear presentation of results contribute to the paper’s quality. I recommend acceptance.

**Potential Questions/Suggestions for the Authors:**

*   Could the authors comment on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly the matrix completion step, and its scalability to larger datasets?
*   While the Amazon review dataset is a good starting point, would the authors consider evaluating the method on datasets with different characteristics (e.g., different domains, languages, or degrees of noise) to further assess its robustness?
*   The authors mention the potential for applying this approach to other tasks. Could they speculate on which tasks might benefit most from this formulation and what modifications, if any, would be necessary?
