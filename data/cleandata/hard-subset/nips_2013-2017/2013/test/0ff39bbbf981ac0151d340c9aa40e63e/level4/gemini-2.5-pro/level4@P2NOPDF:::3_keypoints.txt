- Proposes a two-step translingual representation: matrix completion to densify monolingual document-term matrices, followed by Cross-Lingual LSI on completed vectors.
- Approach is intriguing and could be a significant, novel application if results are robust.
- Main evaluation weakness: relies solely on a linear SVM, so gains may be due to feature scaling compatibility rather than better representations.
- Suggests validating with a classifier insensitive to monotonic feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees) to confirm representation quality.
- Novelty: using matrix completion as a precursor to CL-LSI appears novel.
- Clarity issue 1: ambiguous description of "unified document-term matrix"—unclear whether vocabulary is language-agnostic or language-specific.
- Clarity issue 2: experimental setup details are scattered between the text and Table 1 caption and should be consolidated.
- Summary: idea is promising but evaluation is inconclusive; additional scale-insensitive classifier experiments are essential to substantiate claims.
