

Quality: Though conceptually sound, the robustness of the results is questionable. The observed improvements may merely stem from LSI's superior scaling behavior relative to OPCA and CCA when used with a linear SVM. I recommend evaluating the two-step framework with a rescaling-insensitive classifier, such as boosted decision trees (e.g., in Weka). If this classifier outperforms alternatives, it would validate the result's robustness.  
Significance: A robust outcome would be significant, as matrix completion offers a well-controlled non-linear inference mechanism.  
Novelty: The approach appears novel.  
Clarity: The paper contains clarity issues. Line 113 references a "unified document-term matrix for all documents" without clarifying whether this implies a single term per surface form (e.g., "Merkel") or distinct terms per language (e.g., "Merkelen" and "Merkelde"). Additionally, the experimental setup description is fragmented across Table 1, complicating comprehension. Overall, the idea is promising, but the lack of non-linear classifier evaluation leaves uncertainty about whether the results reflect robustness or scaling artifacts.
