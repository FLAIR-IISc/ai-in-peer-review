- Proposes a two-step translingual representation method: matrix completion to generate multilingual row vectors, followed by CL-LSI on those vectors.
- Concern about result robustness: positive outcomes may reflect favorable scaling of LSI versus OPCA/CCA when used with a linear SVM.
- Recommends testing with a feature-rescaling-invariant classifier (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka).
- States that superior performance of boosted trees would increase confidence in the method’s validity.
- Significance: if robust, this is an interesting contribution because matrix completion is a well-established nonlinear inference method.
- Novelty: the proposed idea appears novel.
- Clarity issue: unclear whether the unified document-term matrix uses a single term per surface form or separate language-specific labels (e.g., “Merkel” vs “Merkelen”/“Merkelde”).
- Clarity issue: experimental setup description is fragmented (spread across Table 1), making it hard to follow.
- Overall recommendation: explore classifiers beyond linear SVM to rule out scaling artifacts.
