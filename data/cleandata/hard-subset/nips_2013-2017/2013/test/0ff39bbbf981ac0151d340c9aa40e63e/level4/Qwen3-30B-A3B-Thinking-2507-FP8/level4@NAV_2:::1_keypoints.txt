- Paper proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: construct an incomplete multilingual document-term matrix, complete it via low-rank + sparsity matrix completion using projected gradient descent, then apply LSI to obtain cross-lingual representations; includes convergence guarantees and strong sentiment-classification results.

- Clarify whether LSI is used mainly for computational efficiency (due to densification of M*) or to fix representation issues, and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.

- Explicitly separate and quantify the individual contributions of labeled source-language data vs. unlabeled parallel documents by reporting performance with each component alone.

- Clarify the experimental setup (counts of labeled source, labeled target, unlabeled parallel, and test splits) and explain the apparent discrepancy between Figure 1 (TSL vs CL-KCCA at 2,000 unlabeled docs) and Table 1 (TSL outperforming CL-KCCA across tasks).

- Justify the claim that a fully observed document-term matrix would be low-rank; explain why documents would be linear combinations of others rather than yield rank ≈ min(vocabulary, documents).

- Explain the specific mechanism by which enforcing low-rank structure on M* outperforms CCA-based approaches and compare to other low-rank methods in the literature.

- Report running time and computational complexity of the projected gradient descent algorithm and compare it empirically/analytically to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.

- Editorial fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before equation (2), and remove “such as” preceding equations (4) and (5).

- Overall: simple and effective approach clearly presented, but several technical details need clarification to fully assess novelty and advantages.
