The paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross language representation learning, which is capable of exploiting a set of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents to bridge the feature spaces of different languages. The approach involves two steps: first, it constructs a unified document-term matrix for all documents from the auxiliary source language domain and the target language domain, and then completes this matrix while enforcing low-rank and sparsity conditions using a projected gradient descent algorithm; second, it reduces the feature dimension of this completed matrix using latent semantic indexing (LSI), and then trains a standard classifier on this new representation.

The paper presents a clear and concise introduction to the problem of cross language text classification and the challenges that arise from the language barrier. It also provides a comprehensive review of related work in the field, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches and the advantages of the proposed method. The technical details of the approach are well-explained, including the formulation of the matrix completion problem, the optimization algorithm, and the convergence analysis.

The experimental evaluation of the proposed method is thorough and well-designed, with a clear description of the dataset, experimental setting, and results. The comparison with other cross-lingual representation learning methods, such as CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA, is also informative and helps to demonstrate the efficacy and robustness of the proposed method.

However, there are some technical points that need to be clarified. For instance, the motivation for reducing the feature dimension of the completed matrix using LSI is not entirely clear. Is it mainly for computational convenience, or are there other reasons for choosing LSI over other dimensionality reduction techniques? Additionally, the relationship between the proposed method and other techniques, such as multiview approaches like CCA, could be further explored.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed method is simple, effective, and clearly presented. The experimental results are promising, and the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of cross language representation learning.

Strengths:

* Novel two-step approach for cross language representation learning
* Effective use of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents to bridge feature spaces
* Clear and concise introduction to the problem and related work
* Thorough and well-designed experimental evaluation
* Comparison with other cross-lingual representation learning methods

Weaknesses:

* Some technical points need to be clarified, such as the motivation for reducing feature dimension using LSI
* Relationship between the proposed method and other techniques, such as multiview approaches like CCA, could be further explored
* Running time of the algorithm and comparison with other methods could be provided

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective approach for cross language representation learning
