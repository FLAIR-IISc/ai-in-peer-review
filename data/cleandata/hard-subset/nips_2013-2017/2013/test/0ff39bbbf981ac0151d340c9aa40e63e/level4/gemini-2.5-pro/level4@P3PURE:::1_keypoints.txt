- Paper proposes a two-step matrix completion approach for cross-domain classification using labeled auxiliary data and unlabeled parallel data, with low-rank+sparsity constraints solved by projected gradient descent, LSI for dimensionality reduction, and a convergence guarantee plus experiments.

- Method is described as simple, effective, and generally well-presented, but several technical clarifications are needed.

- Clarify why dimensionality reduction (LSI) is applied after completing M — is it mainly for computational efficiency given M* may be dense despite sparsity, and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.

- Distinguish the contributions of the two external data types (labeled source-domain data vs unlabeled parallel data) and provide an ablation study or experiments isolating each type’s impact.

- Clarify experimental setup in Section 5.2 (confirm counts for labeled source, labeled target, unlabeled parallel, and test split) and whether Section 5.3 only varies the number of parallel documents; resolve the apparent performance discrepancy between Figure 1 and Table 1 for CL-KCCA vs TSL with 2k parallel docs.

- Provide intuition/justification for the low-rank assumption on a fully observed document-term matrix, since documents are unlikely to be exact linear combinations and the matrix could be full-rank.

- Explain why the matrix completion formulation outperforms multi-view methods like CCA, specifically how the low-rank constraint compares to objectives used by other techniques.

- Report computational complexity and empirical runtime: how does projected gradient descent convergence time compare to baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).

- Minor edits: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before equation (2), and remove “such as” in the sentences preceding equations (4) and (5).
