- Contribution: Proposes a two-step cross-language classification method—matrix completion to unify vocabularies, then LSI on the completed matrix—with a convex relaxation solved by projected gradient descent and a convergence guarantee.  
- Empirical result: Shows competitive performance on multilingual Amazon sentiment benchmarks against CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA using a standard SVM.  
- Strength: Clear, simple pipeline that leverages matrix completion to bridge vocabularies before dimensionality reduction.  
- Strength: Uses a scalable projected gradient method with a convergence argument based on non-expansive operators.  
- Strength: Directly leverages observed cross-lingual co-occurrences while regularizing missing entries.  
- Question: Need clearer rationale for the LSI step—why reduce M* to k dimensions and why choose LSI over PCA or other factorization methods?  
- Question: Clarify roles of labeled source data vs. unlabeled parallel data and include ablation experiments to separate their contributions.  
- Question: Experimental setup ambiguity—confirm counts for labeled source (4k), labeled target (100 train / 1900 test), and 2k unlabeled parallel docs; clarify what is varied in Section 5.3.  
- Question: Explain discrepancy where CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 for some settings but not in Table 1.  
- Question: Justify the low-rank assumption—under what conditions is the document–term matrix low-rank (e.g., latent topics), and how the trace norm enforces this.  
- Question: Provide intuition for why matrix completion+LSI outperforms multiview methods like CCA/KCCA—explain advantages in reconstructing cross-lingual features and reducing noise.  
- Question: Provide runtime and scalability comparison (theoretical per-iteration complexity and wall-clock) between projected gradient for the proposed method and CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA.  
- Minor edit: Change “(ij)” to “(i, j)” before Equation (2).  
- Minor edit: Remove “such as” before Equations (4) and (5).  
- Assessment — quality: Optimization is sound and empirical evaluation convincing, but theoretical claims (low-rank justification) need clearer explanation.  
- Assessment — clarity/originality/significance: Generally well organized and novel in combining matrix completion with LSI; practical and effective but needs clearer experimental protocol and motivation for LSI.  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, principled method with convergence guarantee, strong empirical results, and practical relevance.  
- Cons/concerns: Need clearer low-rank/LSI justification, experimental ambiguities, and no runtime/scalability comparisons.
