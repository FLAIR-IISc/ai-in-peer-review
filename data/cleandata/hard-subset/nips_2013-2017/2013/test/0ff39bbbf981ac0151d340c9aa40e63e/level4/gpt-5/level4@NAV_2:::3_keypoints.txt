- Proposes a two-stage cross-lingual representation: matrix completion to fill a bilingual document–term matrix, then cross-lingual LSI on the resulting document vectors.

- Concern that reported gains may not be robust and could stem from LSI’s scaling interacting favorably with a linear SVM rather than improved representation.

- Recommends evaluating with a classifier insensitive to feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka) to test robustness.

- If gains persist under such checks, the result would be significant because matrix completion is a principled nonlinear inference tool.

- The overall formulation appears novel.

- Clarify whether the unified document–term matrix uses a single feature per surface form across languages or separate language-specific features.

- Experimental setup description is fragmented around Table 1 and needs clearer presentation.

- Overall assessment: promising idea but needs classifier diversity beyond linear SVM to rule out scaling artifacts.
