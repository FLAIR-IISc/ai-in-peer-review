Summary: This paper presents a two-step method for learning a cross-lingual topic representation using matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis. It optimizes the matrix completion objective with a projected gradient descent algorithm. Experiments on a parallel Amazon reviews dataset for sentiment analysis show that the proposed method outperforms both monolingual and cross-lingual baselines. Learning curves across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data indicate that the algorithm can learn highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations with relatively little parallel data.

Quality: The experiments are thorough and support the paper’s claims. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is carefully compared against two baselines (plain bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.

Clarity: The paper is well organized. All parameters and selection procedures are specified, making the results easy to reproduce. Minor suggestion: expand on the motivation for using matrix completion in the Introduction (though it is discussed in Section 3).

Originality: Although cross-lingual representation learning is not new, applying matrix completion to this task is novel, and the proposed algorithm clearly achieves better performance than prior methods.

Significance: The two-step approach consistently outperforms other baselines. This work shows that matrix completion can serve as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification, and this formulation may be applicable to other tasks. Specifically, the method performs matrix completion before latent semantic analysis for cross-lingual sentiment classification, outperforms monolingual and other cross-lingual baselines (including a state-of-the-art method), and remains stable across different sizes of unlabeled parallel data.
