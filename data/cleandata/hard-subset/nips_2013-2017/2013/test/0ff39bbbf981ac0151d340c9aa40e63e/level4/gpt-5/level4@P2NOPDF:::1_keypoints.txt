- Proposed method: two-stage matrix-completion for cross-domain classification—complete a single incomplete document matrix under joint low-rank and sparsity constraints via projected gradient descent (with convergence guarantee), then apply LSI and train a classifier; empirical results favorable.  
- Clarify rationale for post-completion LSI: is it primarily computational because M* may be dense despite sparsity regularization, and why choose LSI over PCA or other DR methods?  
- Distinguish contributions of data sources: separate effects of labeled source-language data vs. unlabeled parallel data and include ablation studies isolating each.  
- Experimental setup clarity: confirm train/test split (4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel, test on remaining 1,900 target) and clarify whether only the number of unlabeled parallels is varied in Sec. 5.3.  
- Explain discrepancy in results: why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL at 2k in Figure 1 for EFM/EGM but not in Table 1?  
- Justify the low-rank assumption: explain why a fully observed document–term matrix should be low rank given sparsity alone does not imply low rank.  
- Intuition for TSL’s performance: explain why TSL generally outperforms others (especially CCA) and whether benefits stem from enforcing a low-rank prior versus alternative regularization/representation-learning techniques.  
- Runtime and scaling: report wall-clock times and scaling behavior, and compare projected gradient descent runtime to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.  
- Minor edits: replace “(ij)” with “(i, j)” before Eq. (2) and remove “such as” before Eqs. (4) and (5).  
- Overall assessment: simple, effective, and clearly presented method that is promising though several technical points need clarification.
