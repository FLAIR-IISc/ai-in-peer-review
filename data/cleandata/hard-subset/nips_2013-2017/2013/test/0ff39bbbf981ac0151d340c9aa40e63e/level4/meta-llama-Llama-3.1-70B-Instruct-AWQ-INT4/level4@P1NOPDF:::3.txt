The paper proposes a novel approach to translingual representation learning, combining matrix completion with Cross-Language Latent Semantic Indexing (CL-LSI) to create multilingual vectors for each row. 

Quality: While this idea seems promising, I have concerns about the robustness of the results. The positive outcomes might be attributed to the scaling of LSI being more suitable for linear SVM than OPCA or CCA, rather than the proposed method itself. To alleviate this concern, I recommend using a machine learning system that is insensitive to rescalings of individual features, such as boosted decision trees (available in Weka). If the proposed two-step system, coupled with boosted decision trees, outperforms other techniques, I would be more confident in the results.

Significance: If the results are indeed robust, this would be a significant contribution, as matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique. 

Novelty: The idea appears to be new and original.

Clarity: There were some clarity issues in the paper. For instance, line 113 mentions "construct[ing] a unified document-term matrix for all documents," but it is unclear whether this involves using a single term for every surface form (e.g., "Merkel") or labeling each surface form from each language (e.g., "Merkelen" and "Merkelde"). Please provide clarification on this point. Additionally, the experimental setup description is scattered across Table 1, making it somewhat difficult to understand. 

Overall, the idea seems intriguing, but I would like the authors to explore other classifiers beyond linear SVM to confirm the robustness of the results and rule out the possibility of scaling artifacts.
