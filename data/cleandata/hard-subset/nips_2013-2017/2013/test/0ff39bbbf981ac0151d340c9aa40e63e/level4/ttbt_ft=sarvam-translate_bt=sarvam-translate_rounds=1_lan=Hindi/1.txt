This paper proposes a matrix completion approach for the Cross Domain Classification Task, which is capable of using labeled data in one auxiliary domain and unlabeled parallel data. This approach involves two steps. First, it constructs a single incomplete matrix containing all the documents and domains, and then completes this matrix using the Projected Gradient Descent algorithm, applying the conditions of low rank and sparsity. Second, it reduces the feature dimension of this complete matrix using LSI, and then trains a standard classifier on this new representation. The paper also presents a convergence guarantee and favorable experimental results on the Projected Gradient Descent algorithm.

Here are some comments and questions:

- After estimating the complete matrix M, you reduce the feature dimension with LSI from d to k because M "does not have enough capacity to handle feature sparsity". Can you elaborate on this? Is it basically that you want a smaller dimension for computational convenience, as M* would be relatively dense even with sparsity enforcement? Also, in that case, is there any reason you chose LSI over other dimensionality reduction techniques (e.g., PCA)?
- This paper seems to have two unrelated aspects of using external data: 1. Leveraging labeled source language data to help learn labeled target language data, and 2. Leveraging unlabeled parallel data. If this is correct, it would be helpful to clearly distinguish the two and report the results separately.
- On a related note, the experiment settings are a bit vague (Section 5.2). So, you were using (a) 4k labeled source language documents, (b) 100 labeled target language documents, and (c) 2k unlabeled parallel documents for your training data? And you are reporting the classification result on the remaining 1900 labeled target language documents? So, the only difference in Section 5.3 is that you are changing the number of unlabeled parallel documents from 200 to 2k? In that case, why is the performance of CL-KCCA in Figure 1 for EFM and EGM better than TSL, but not in Table 1?
- Can you briefly explain why the fully observed document-term matrix would be low rank? It is sparse, but perhaps no document is a linear combination of other documents in vocabulary, which means that the rank in that case would be min(d, k).
- Can you give an intuition as to why TSL is generally able to perform better than other methods, especially CCA? A multi-view approach like CCA seems like a natural approach for this cross-language representation learning task. In contrast, the matrix completion approach is not as natural. It seems that the main driving force is to enforce the condition of low rank on M*. How does this compare with other techniques?
- What is the running time of the algorithm? Does the Projected Gradient Descent algorithm take more, less, or about the same amount of time compared to other methods like CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?
