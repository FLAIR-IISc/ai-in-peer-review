- Proposes a two-stage matrix completion framework for cross-domain classification using labeled auxiliary data and unlabeled parallel data, with projected gradient descent under low-rank and sparsity constraints, LSI dimensionality reduction, and convergence guarantees; reports favorable experiments.
- Request clarification on why LSI is applied after estimating M (is it mainly computational because M* is dense despite sparsity regularization?) and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.
- Suggests separating and individually evaluating the two ways external data are used: (1) labeled source-language data and (2) unlabeled parallel data.
- Asks for a clearer description of the experimental setup (training/testing splits and whether unlabeled parallel data are varied from 200 to 2k) and notes an apparent inconsistency where CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 but not in Table 1.
- Questions the claim that a fully observed document–term matrix would be low-rank, noting typical sparsity and lack of linear dependence among documents.
- Requests intuition for why TSL generally outperforms other methods (especially CCA) and whether the low-rank constraint on M* is the primary advantage versus alternative techniques.
- Asks for running-time comparisons: how long the projected gradient descent procedure takes relative to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.
- Minor suggested edits: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before Equation (2) and remove “such as” before Equations (4) and (5).
- Overall judgment: the approach is simple, effective, and clearly presented, but several technical points require clarification.
