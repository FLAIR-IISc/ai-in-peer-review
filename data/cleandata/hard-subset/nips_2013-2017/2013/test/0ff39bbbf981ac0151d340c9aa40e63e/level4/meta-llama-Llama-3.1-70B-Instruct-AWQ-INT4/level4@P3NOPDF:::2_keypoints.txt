- Proposes a novel two-step method for cross-lingual topic representations using matrix completion followed by LSA.  
- Optimizes the matrix completion problem with a projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Demonstrates superior performance over monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on sentiment analysis using a parallel Amazon review dataset.  
- Experiments are high-quality, comparing against two simple baselines and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Comprehensive evaluation provides strong empirical support for the authors' claims.  
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with experimental parameters and selection process clearly stated for reproducibility.  
- Minor recommendation: add more motivation for using matrix completion in the Introduction rather than Section 3.  
- The application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is novel and yields consistently higher results.  
- The method is a promising preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and potentially applicable to other tasks.
