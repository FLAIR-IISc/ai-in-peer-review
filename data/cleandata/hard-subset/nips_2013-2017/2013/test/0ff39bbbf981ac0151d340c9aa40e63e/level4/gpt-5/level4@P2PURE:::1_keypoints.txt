- Paper proposes a two-stage matrix completion method for cross-domain classification: complete a joint document-term matrix under low-rank and sparsity constraints, then apply LSI and train a classifier.  
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent completion algorithm and reports favorable empirical results.  
- Question: clarify why LSI is used to reduce dimensionality of the completed matrix—primary motivation computational convenience because M* is dense, and why LSI over alternatives like PCA.  
- Suggest separating and ablation-testing the two uses of external data: labeled source-language data versus unlabeled parallel data.  
- Experimental-setup clarification requested: exact train/dev/test splits (4k source labels, 100 target labels, 2k unlabeled parallel, 1.9k target test?) and which variables are varied in Section 5.3.  
- Explain discrepancy: why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL for EFM and EGM at 2k in Figure 1 but not in Table 1?  
- Theoretical question: justify why a fully observed document–term matrix should be low-rank given typical document sparsity and lack of exact linear dependence.  
- Provide intuition for TSL’s generally better performance versus CCA and other multiview methods—assess the role of the low-rank constraint and comparison with alternative techniques.  
- Empirical/runtime question: report runtime of projected gradient descent and compare to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.  
- Minor suggested edits: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before Eq. (2) and remove “such as” before Eqs. (4) and (5).  
- Overall assessment: method is simple, effective, and clearly presented but several technical and experimental details require clarification.
