- Proposed method: apply matrix completion to obtain multilingual vectors per row, then perform CL-LSI on those multilingual vectors.

- Quality concern: reported results may not be robust; improvements could stem from LSI’s scaling being more favorable with a linear SVM than OPCA/CCA.

- Recommendation: test with a classifier insensitive to per-feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka).

- Criterion for convincing result: if boosted decision trees on the proposed two-step system outperform other techniques, the result would be convincing.

- Significance: if robust, the effect is noteworthy because matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique.

- Novelty: the idea appears new.

- Clarity issue: ambiguous whether a unified document-term matrix uses a single token per surface form across languages or language-specific tokens.

- Clarity issue: experimental setup description is split across Table 1, making it harder to piece together.

- Overall: neat idea but authors should evaluate classifiers beyond a linear SVM to rule out scaling artifacts.
