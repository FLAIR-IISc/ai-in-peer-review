- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual topic learning method: matrix completion (optimized via projected gradient descent) followed by latent semantic analysis.
- Demonstrates the method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithm, on parallel Amazon review sentiment classification.
- Experiments are thorough and convincingly support claims, comparing against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two SOTA algorithms.
- Learning curves show the algorithm learns accurate cross-lingual topic representations with relatively little parallel/unlabeled data and remains stable across varying data amounts.
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible, including parameter selection details.
- Minor clarity suggestion: motivate the use of matrix completion earlier (in the Introduction) rather than only in Section 3.
- Novelty lies in applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning, yielding clear empirical improvements.
- Significance: matrix completion as a preprocessing step shows promise for cross-lingual sentiment classification and potentially other tasks.
