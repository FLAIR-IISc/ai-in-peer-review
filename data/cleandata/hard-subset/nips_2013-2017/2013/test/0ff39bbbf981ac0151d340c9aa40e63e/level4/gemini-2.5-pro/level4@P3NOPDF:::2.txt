### **Summary**

This paper introduces a novel two-step method for learning cross-lingual topic representations, combining matrix completion with Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA). The approach first applies a projected gradient descent algorithm to complete a bilingual word-context matrix, effectively addressing data sparsity. Subsequently, LSA is performed on the densified matrix to derive the final low-dimensional representations. Experimental results on a parallel Amazon review dataset for sentiment analysis show that the proposed method outperforms both monolingual and established cross-lingual baselines. Furthermore, learning curves demonstrate that the algorithm achieves high accuracy with a relatively small amount of parallel data, highlighting its data efficiency.

### **Quality**

The paper's claims are well-supported by a thorough empirical evaluation. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is rigorously compared against a comprehensive set of baselines, including a standard bag-of-words model, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods. This careful comparison provides convincing evidence of the proposed method's effectiveness on the sentiment classification task. The analysis of the model's performance with varying amounts of unlabeled data further strengthens the conclusion that the method is robust and practical.

### **Clarity**

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide detailed descriptions of all parameters and their selection criteria, which greatly facilitates the reproducibility of their results.

A minor suggestion for improvement would be to more explicitly state the motivation for using matrix completion in the Introduction. While this is well-explained in Section 3, introducing the intuition earlier—framing it as a solution to the data sparsity inherent in cross-lingual contexts—would improve the narrative flow and better position the paper's core contribution from the outset.

### **Originality**

While the broader area of cross-lingual representation learning is well-established, this paper's primary contribution is the novel application of matrix completion to this problem. Specifically, the formulation of matrix completion as a pre-processing step to densify a sparse word-context matrix before applying a latent semantic model is an original and effective approach. The strong empirical results, which surpass those of previous methods, underscore the value of this contribution.

### **Significance**

The significance of this work is twofold. First, it presents a method that consistently and significantly outperforms several strong baselines on a practical cross-lingual task. The algorithm's stability and effectiveness, even with limited parallel data, make it a valuable tool for real-world applications where such data is often scarce.

Second, and more broadly, the paper successfully demonstrates that matrix completion can serve as a powerful pre-processing technique for cross-lingual NLP tasks. This formulation offers a principled way to handle the inherent data sparsity in cross-lingual settings, and this insight could potentially be extended to other related problems, such as cross-lingual information retrieval or document classification. The work effectively establishes a new and promising direction for improving cross-lingual semantic models.
