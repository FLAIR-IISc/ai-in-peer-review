- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation pipeline: unified document-term matrix completion followed by LSI to produce cross-lingual embeddings.  
- Constructs a unified matrix where parallel documents have full entries and non-parallel documents have missing cross-language entries.  
- Solves matrix completion via projected gradient descent and provides a rigorous convergence proof.  
- Applies LSI on the completed matrix to obtain low-dimensional representations enabling monolingual classifiers for cross-lingual tasks.  
- Differs from prior work (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) by completing the matrix before LSI rather than applying projection/LṠI directly on sparse data.  
- Evaluation shows up to 14.61% gains over baselines but uses only linear SVMs, which are sensitive to feature scaling.  
- Raises concern that observed gains may be due to LSI’s implicit feature scaling rather than the matrix completion step.  
- Recommends comparing against scaling-insensitive classifiers (e.g., RBF SVMs, gradient-boosted trees) to validate robustness.  
- Clarity issues: ambiguous vocabulary handling (whether surface forms across languages are distinct or shared) and fragmented experimental setup scattered between table and text.  
- Significance is promising for low-resource settings if robustness is shown, but current evidence is inconclusive due to limited classifier evaluation.  
- Pros: novel two-step approach, solid theoretical analysis, strong empirical results with small parallel data; Cons: limited classifier evaluation, unclear vocabulary treatment, and fragmented experimental description.
