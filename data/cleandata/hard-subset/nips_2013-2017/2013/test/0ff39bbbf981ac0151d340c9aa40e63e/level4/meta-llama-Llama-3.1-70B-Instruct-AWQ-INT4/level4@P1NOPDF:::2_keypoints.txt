- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual topic representation method combining matrix completion and LSA, optimized with projected gradient descent.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to monolingual, cross-lingual baselines and one state-of-the-art method on cross-lingual sentiment classification using the parallel Amazon review dataset.  
- Learning curves show the method learns accurate cross-lingual topics with relatively few parallel examples and is stable across different sizes of unlabeled parallel data.  
- Experiments are thorough, well-designed, and comprehensively compare against two baselines and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible, with clear parameter descriptions and selection procedures.  
- Minor clarity issue: motivation for using matrix completion should be explained earlier (Introduction) rather than only in Section 3.  
- Originality lies in applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning, yielding improved results over prior methods.  
- Significance: shows matrix completion is a promising preprocessing/formulation for cross-lingual sentiment tasks and potentially other tasks.
