- Proposed method: matrix completion for cross-domain classification using labeled source data and unlabeled parallel data; complete an incomplete document-term matrix via projected gradient descent under low-rank and sparsity constraints, then apply LSI to reduce dimension and train a classifier; convergence guarantee and favorable experiments reported.

- Clarification 1: Explain why LSI is used to reduce d→k after completion—whether for computational convenience because M* becomes dense, or for a theoretical reason—and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.

- Clarification 2: Explicitly separate the two ways external data are used—(i) labeled source-domain supervision and (ii) unlabeled parallel data—and report results isolating each effect.

- Clarification 3: Clarify the experimental setup (4,000 labeled source, 100 labeled target, varying unlabeled parallel 200–2,000, test on remaining 1,900 target) and reconcile the reported discrepancy where Figure 1 shows CL-KCCA outperforming TSL at 2,000 unlabeled docs for EFM and EGM but Table 1 shows the opposite.

- Clarification 4: Justify the low-rank assumption for the fully observed document-term matrix—provide intuition or evidence why documents/term co-occurrence should be well-approximated by a low-rank model despite sparsity.

- Clarification 5: Explain why TSL consistently outperforms CCA-based methods (e.g., CL-KCCA); identify the primary mechanism (e.g., low-rank enforcement on M*) and compare this mechanism to what CCA and other baselines do.

- Clarification 6: Provide the computational complexity of the proposed projected gradient descent algorithm and empirical/runtime comparisons to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.

- Minor revisions: fix "(ij)" to "(i,j)" before Equation (2), and remove "such as" from sentences preceding Equations (4) and (5).

- Overall: method is simple, effective, and well-presented; addressing the above clarifications would significantly strengthen the paper.
