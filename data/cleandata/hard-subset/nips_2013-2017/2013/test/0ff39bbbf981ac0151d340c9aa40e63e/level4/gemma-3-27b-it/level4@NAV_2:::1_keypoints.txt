- Proposes a two-step method: matrix completion to fill a document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual representations.
- Leverages labeled source-language data plus unlabeled parallel documents to address disjoint feature spaces.
- Formulates matrix completion and uses projected gradient descent with a stated convergence guarantee.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on cross-language sentiment classification, especially with limited parallel data.
- Positions method within cross-language representation literature, similar to CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA but unique in completing the matrix first.
- Main contribution: novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning with empirical gains.
- Strength: clear problem definition, well-motivated approach, and theoretical justification.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation with thorough comparisons to strong baselines.
- Strength: insightful analysis of the effect of parallel data size.
- Weakness: weak justification for using LSI over other dimensionality reduction methods (e.g., PCA).
- Weakness: experimental setup not clearly explained—interplay of labeled source, labeled target, and unlabeled parallel data is ambiguous.
- Issue: discrepancy between Table 1 and Figure 1 (notably CL-KCCA results) requires investigation.
- Weakness: the low-rank assumption for a fully observed document-term matrix is not well motivated.
- Weakness: no discussion of computational complexity or running time compared to baselines.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong results, convergence proof, and comprehensive experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak LSI justification, unclear experiments, underexplained low-rank intuition, and missing complexity analysis.
- Overall: strong paper with clear contribution; addressing the highlighted issues would further improve it.
