Summary: The paper introduces a two-stage approach for learning cross-lingual document representations by combining matrix completion with latent semantic analysis. The matrix completion problem is optimized via a projected gradient descent procedure. Empirically, the method surpasses both monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on cross-lingual sentiment classification using the multilingual Amazon reviews dataset. Learning curves across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data indicate that the method achieves high accuracy with relatively few parallel pairs.

Quality: The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and supports the central claims. The proposed two-step pipeline is systematically compared against two baselines (a target-language bag-of-words classifier and cross-lingual LSA) as well as two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality-reduction techniques.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well structured. Hyperparameters and selection procedures are fully described, facilitating reproducibility. A minor suggestion is to better motivate the use of matrix completion in the Introduction (the rationale is currently deferred to Section 3).

Originality: While cross-lingual representation learning is a well-studied problem, employing matrix completion for this purpose is a novel contribution. The distinction from prior work is clear, and the empirical results demonstrate improvements over existing methods.

Significance: The two-step approach consistently outperforms competing baselines. A key takeaway is that matrix completion can serve as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification, and the formulation is likely applicable to other tasks. The method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art approach, and remains robust across different quantities of unlabeled parallel data.
