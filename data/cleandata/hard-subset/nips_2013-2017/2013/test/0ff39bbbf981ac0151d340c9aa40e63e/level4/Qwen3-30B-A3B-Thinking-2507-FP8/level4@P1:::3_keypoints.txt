- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion to fill missing entries in a bilingual document-term matrix, followed by LSI to produce low-dimensional cross-lingual embeddings using unlabeled parallel documents (no full translation).

- Novelty and motivation: using matrix completion to recover a low-rank bilingual document-term matrix before LSI is well-motivated and resource-efficient for sparse bilingual feature spaces.

- Theoretical soundness: provides convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent algorithm.

- Experimental breadth: thorough evaluation on Amazon reviews (EN, FR, DE, JP), covering 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks and systematically varying parallel data size (200–2000).

- Empirical gains: TSL outperforms baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) by 8.59–14.61% average accuracy and remains robust with small parallel data.

- Methodological concern 1: evaluation uses only linear SVMs; SVM sensitivity to feature scaling may confound whether gains come from representations or classifier interaction—recommend testing classifiers less sensitive to scaling (e.g., boosted trees).

- Methodological concern 2: unclear unified vocabulary construction—uncertain if vocabulary uses language-specific surface forms or a shared semantic representation, which affects interpretation of matrix completion.

- Presentation concern: experimental setup details are fragmented across table and text; include a concise summary of data partitioning (e.g., labeled target, unlabeled parallel counts) to improve reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: novel, technically solid, and empirically convincing work; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing classifier robustness and vocabulary-clarity concerns.
