- Presents a two-step method (matrix completion then LSA) for cross-lingual topic representation, optimized with projected gradient descent, and evaluated on parallel Amazon reviews.
- Method outperforms monolingual, cross-lingual baselines and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms on sentiment classification.
- Learning curves show high accuracy can be achieved with relatively small amounts of parallel unlabeled data.
- Experiments are thorough and comparisons/supporting results are strong.
- Paper is well organized and reproducible with clear parameter specifications; suggest better motivating matrix completion in the Introduction.
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is novel and achieves superior performance.
- The approach is significant and stable across varying unlabeled data sizes, and may generalize to other tasks.
