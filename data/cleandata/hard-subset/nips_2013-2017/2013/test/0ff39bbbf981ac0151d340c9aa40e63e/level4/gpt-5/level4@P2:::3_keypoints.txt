- Summary: Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method — convex matrix completion on a bilingual document-term matrix followed by LSI to learn shared representations for monolingual classification.
- Novelty: Combining convex matrix completion on a unified bilingual matrix with subsequent LSI is presented as a novel approach relative to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.
- Experiments: Evaluated on 18 English–French/German/Japanese sentiment transfer tasks, showing consistent gains, especially with few parallel documents.
- Strength — formulation: Clear convex relaxation (trace norm + ℓ1 + nonnegativity) with a simple projected gradient scheme and standard convergence argument.
- Strength — empirical coverage: Broad evaluation across languages and domains plus analysis versus number of parallel documents.
- Strength — performance: Consistent improvements over CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA, notable when parallel data is scarce.
- Concern — scaling confound: Improvements may stem from favorable feature rescaling; recommend testing scale-invariant classifiers (e.g., boosted trees, random forests).
- Concern — ablation studies needed: Report results for LSI on the original concatenated matrix, classifier on the completed high-dimensional matrix without LSI, and effects of nonnegativity and the ℓ1 term.
- Concern — hyperparameter sensitivity: Evaluate sensitivity to γ, ρ, and regularization on missing entries.
- Concern — inductive vs transductive: Clarify whether representations use test target documents (transductive); report an inductive variant using only allowed training/parallel data.
- Concern — statistical significance: Add significance tests across runs for per-task comparisons.
- Concern — efficiency: Provide runtime, number of SVDs/iterations, memory footprint, and computational cost comparison to CL-KCCA and CL-OPCA.
- Clarity — unified vocabulary: Clarify whether language-specific surface forms are kept distinct and how identical strings across languages are handled.
- Clarity — experimental setup: Consolidate details on numbers of labeled target examples, which unlabeled documents are used for representation learning, and parameter selection protocol.
- Clarity — algorithm details: Specify stopping criterion, average iterations, adaptive stepsizes/warm-starts, and practical choice of τ.
- Significance: If robust to scale-invariant classifiers and valid in an inductive setting, the method could be practically useful for low-parallel-data cross-lingual transfer.
- Additional suggestion: Include SCL as a baseline, test more domains/language pairs (including non-English↔non-English via pivot), and show qualitative term alignments recovered by completion.
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, well-motivated and likely novel idea with strong empirical gains and principled optimization.
- Cons against acceptance: Possible dependence on scaling and classifier choice, transductive training risk, missing ablations, efficiency analysis, and some clarity issues.
- Overall: Promising and neat idea with encouraging results, but requires classifier-agnostic evaluations, ablations, inductive-setting clarification, and clearer experimental/algorithmic details to confirm robustness.
