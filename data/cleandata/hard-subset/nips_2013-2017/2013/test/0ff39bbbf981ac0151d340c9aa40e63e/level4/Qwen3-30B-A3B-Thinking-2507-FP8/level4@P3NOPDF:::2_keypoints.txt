- Introduces a novel two-step cross-lingual topic representation: matrix completion via projected gradient descent followed by LSA.  
- Method leverages projected gradient descent to solve matrix completion as a preprocessing step.  
- Produces cross-lingual topic representations via LSA after completion.  
- Empirical results on parallel Amazon reviews show consistent superiority over monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including a SOTA dimensionality reduction method.  
- Method remains accurate and robust even with limited parallel (labeled) data—learning curves show strong performance with small labeled sets.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing multiple baselines and including ablation studies on unlabeled data sizes.  
- Results are statistically sound and all parameters/hyperparameter selection are documented, enabling full reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-organized and technically clear; minor suggestion to present the rationale for matrix completion earlier in the Introduction.  
- Demonstrates clear originality by applying matrix completion to cross-lingual sentiment classification rather than incremental improvement.  
- Work is significant and generalizable beyond sentiment analysis, proposing a two-step paradigm applicable to other cross-lingual NLP tasks; reviewer recommends acceptance.
