- Proposes a two-step translingual representation: matrix completion to create multilingual term vectors, then Cross-Lingual LSI on those vectors.  
- Reviewer questions robustness: positive results may be due to LSI’s scaling aligning better with linear SVM than with OPCA or CCA.  
- Recommends testing with a scaling-insensitive classifier (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka) to isolate representation effects from scaling artifacts.  
- If the method still outperforms baselines with such a classifier, the evidence for its effectiveness would be much stronger.  
- Contribution would be significant if results are robust; matrix completion is a well-principled, interesting non-linear inference technique.  
- Approach appears novel.  
- Clarity issues: unclear whether the unified document-term matrix maps language-specific surface forms to canonical terms or treats them as distinct features.  
- Experimental details are fragmented across text and Table 1 and should be consolidated for clarity.  
- Main conclusion: novel and interesting idea, but verification with a scaling-invariant classifier is crucial to substantiate the claims.
