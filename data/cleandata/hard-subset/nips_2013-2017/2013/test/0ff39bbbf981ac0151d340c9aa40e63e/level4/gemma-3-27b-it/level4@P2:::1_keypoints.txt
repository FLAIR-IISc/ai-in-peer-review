- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion of a combined document-term matrix followed by LSI for representation learning.  
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm used in matrix completion.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on Amazon reviews, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Paper is generally well written and clearly explains the method.  
- Novelty lies in completing a combined document-term matrix instead of relying on translation or bilingual lexicons.  
- Builds on low-rank matrix completion literature as theoretical foundation.  
- Motivation for applying LSI after completion (vs. other dimensionality reduction methods) is under-explained.  
- Contribution of labeled source data versus unlabeled parallel corpora is not clearly disentangled or reported separately.  
- Experimental setup details (train/test sizes, roles of labeled/unlabeled data) need clarification for reproducibility.  
- Inconsistency between Table 1 and Figure 1 for CL-KCCA results at 2k parallel documents requires investigation.  
- The low-rank assumption for a fully observed document-term matrix is not sufficiently justified.  
- Lacks deeper intuition for why the method outperforms alternatives like CCA and the specific role of the low-rank constraint.  
- No discussion of computational complexity or running time compared to baseline methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, effective in low-resource settings, and clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear rationale for design choices, imprecise experimental details, missing complexity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing LSI motivation, experiment clarifications, CCA comparison, complexity discussion, and minor typographical fixes.
