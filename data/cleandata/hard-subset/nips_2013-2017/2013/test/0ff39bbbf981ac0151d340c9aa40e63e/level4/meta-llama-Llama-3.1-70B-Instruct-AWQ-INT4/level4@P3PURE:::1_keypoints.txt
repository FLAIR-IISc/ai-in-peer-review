- Proposes a matrix completion method for cross-domain classification that uses labeled auxiliary-domain data and unlabeled parallel data, followed by LSI and standard classification.
- Algorithm: construct a single incomplete document matrix, complete it enforcing low-rank and sparsity via projected gradient descent, then reduce dimension with LSI and train a classifier.
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and reports favorable experimental results.
- Question: Clarify why LSI is used to reduce d to k after completion—whether this is for computational convenience because M* becomes dense despite sparsity enforcement, and why LSI was chosen over PCA or other methods.
- Request: Distinguish and separately report the two ways external data are used—(1) labeled source language data and (2) unlabeled parallel data—to isolate their individual contributions.
- Experimental clarity: Confirm training/test splits—4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel for training, and 1900 labeled target for testing—and clarify the variation in unlabeled size in Section 5.3.
- Point out an apparent inconsistency: why CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 for EFM and EGM at 2k but not in Table 1—please explain.
- Theoretical question: Explain why a fully observed document-term matrix would be low-rank despite sparsity and lack of obvious linear dependence among documents.
- Request intuition: Why does TSL generally outperform methods like CCA; why is matrix completion preferable or competitive—how critical is the low-rank constraint versus other approaches?
- Practical question: Report running time and compare projected gradient descent runtime to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.
- Minor editorial suggestions: change (ij) to (i,j) before equation (2), and remove "such as" before equations (4) and (5).
- Overall assessment: Despite technical clarifications needed, the method is simple, effective, and generally clearly presented.
