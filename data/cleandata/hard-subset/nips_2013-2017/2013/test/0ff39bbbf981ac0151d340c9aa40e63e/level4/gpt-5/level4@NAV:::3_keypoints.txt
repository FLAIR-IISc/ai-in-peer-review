- Paper proposes translingual representation learning: matrix completion to get multilingual row vectors, followed by CL-LSI on those vectors.
- Quality concern: results may not be robust; observed gains might stem from LSI’s scaling fitting a linear SVM better than OPCA/CCA.
- Recommendation: evaluate with a classifier insensitive to per-feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka) to test robustness.
- Significance: if effect holds, matrix completion would be an interesting, well-controlled non-linear inference method for this task.
- Novelty: the idea appears novel.
- Clarity issue: unclear whether the “unified document-term matrix” uses a single term per surface form or separate language-specific variants (e.g., “Merkel” vs “Merkelen”/“Merkelde”).
- Clarity issue: experimental setup description is fragmented around Table 1, making it harder to follow.
- Overall: neat idea, but authors should try classifiers beyond linear SVM to rule out scaling artifacts.
