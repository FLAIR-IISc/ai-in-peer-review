- Proposes a two-stage method combining matrix completion with latent semantic analysis for cross-lingual topic representations and uses projected gradient descent for optimization.  
- Evaluated on parallel Amazon reviews for sentiment analysis, the method outperforms monolingual, cross-lingual baselines, and state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Learning curves show the algorithm learns effective cross-lingual representations even with limited parallel/unlabeled data.  
- Claims are well-supported by comprehensive experiments comparing against standard (bag-of-words, LSA) and SOTA baselines.  
- Paper is well-structured and reproducible with detailed parameter descriptions; minor suggestion to introduce matrix completion motivation earlier.  
- Novel contribution: applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning, with empirical superiority over existing methods.  
- Significant impact: matrix completion as a preprocessing step yields consistently better sentiment classification and may generalize to other cross-domain tasks.
