- Paper proposes a two-stage matrix completion method for cross-domain classification: complete an incomplete document matrix via projected gradient descent with low-rank and sparsity constraints, then apply LSI and train a standard classifier; includes convergence guarantee and promising experiments.  
- Query why LSI is used for dimensionality reduction after completion: is it mainly for computational efficiency since M may be dense, and why not use PCA or other alternatives?  
- Recommend clearly distinguishing and separately reporting the two uses of external data: labeled source-language data versus unlabeled parallel data.  
- Request clarification of experimental setup: confirm whether training uses 4,000 labeled source, 100 labeled target, and 2,000 unlabeled parallel docs, with evaluation on the remaining 1,900 target docs, and whether section 5.3 varies unlabeled data from 200 to 2,000.  
- Point out an apparent inconsistency: why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL in Figure 1 at 2,000 unlabeled docs for EFM and EGM but not in Table 1?  
- Ask for intuition and justification for assuming a low-rank structure in a fully observed document-term matrix given sparsity and the unlikelihood of documents being linear combinations of each other.  
- Question why TSL generally outperforms other methods (especially CCA), and how the proposed matrix completion’s low-rank constraint compares to multi-view approaches for cross-language representation learning.  
- Ask for running-time comparison: how does the projected gradient descent algorithm’s runtime compare to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?  
- Minor suggested edits: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before equation (2), and remove “such as” before equations (4) and (5).  
- Overall assessment: method is simple, effective, and clearly presented, but several technical clarifications are needed.
