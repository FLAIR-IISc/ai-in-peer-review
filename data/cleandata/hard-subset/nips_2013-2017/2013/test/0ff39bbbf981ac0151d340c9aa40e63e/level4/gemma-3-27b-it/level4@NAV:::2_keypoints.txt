- Introduces a novel two-step cross-lingual topic representation method: matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis.
- Matrix completion stage is solved with a projected gradient descent algorithm.
- Method outperforms monolingual and existing cross-lingual baselines on sentiment classification using a parallel Amazon review dataset.
- Learning curves show the approach yields accurate cross-lingual topics even with limited parallel data.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and benchmarks against simple bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction techniques.
- All parameters and selection methods are thoroughly documented, aiding reproducibility.
- Minor clarity suggestion: better motivate the use of matrix completion in the Introduction (details are in Section 3).
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution.
- Consistent, significant performance gains suggest matrix completion is an effective preprocessing step and may generalize to other tasks.
- The two-step method achieves state-of-the-art results and is robust across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data.
