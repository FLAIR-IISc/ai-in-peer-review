**Summary**

This paper presents a two-step method for learning a cross-lingual topic representation that combines matrix completion with latent semantic analysis. The matrix completion problem is optimized using a projected gradient descent algorithm. Experimental results on a parallel Amazon review dataset demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms a mono-lingual baseline and several cross-lingual baselines on a sentiment analysis task. Furthermore, learning curves show that the algorithm can learn a highly accurate cross-lingual representation even with a relatively small amount of parallel data.

**Quality**

The experiments are thorough and convincingly support the paper's claims. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is rigorously compared against two baselines (plain bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-organized. All parameters, including their selection criteria, are clearly described, which should make the results straightforward to reproduce. As a minor suggestion, the motivation for using matrix completion could be explained earlier in the Introduction. While this is detailed in Section 3, introducing the intuition at the beginning would improve the paper's readability.

**Originality**

While cross-lingual representation learning is an established area, the application of matrix completion to this problem is novel. The proposed algorithm also demonstrably outperforms previous methods on the experimental task.

**Significance**

The results show that the two-step approach is consistently superior to the baselines. This work demonstrates that matrix completion can be an effective pre-processing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification, and this formulation may prove useful for other tasks as well. The method not only outperforms other cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art approach, but also proves to be stable even when trained with limited amounts of unlabeled parallel data.
