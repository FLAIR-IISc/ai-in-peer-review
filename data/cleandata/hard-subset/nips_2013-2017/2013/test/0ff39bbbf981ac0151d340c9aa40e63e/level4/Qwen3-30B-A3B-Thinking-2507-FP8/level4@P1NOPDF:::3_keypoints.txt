- Paper proposes a translingual representation method: matrix completion to generate multilingual row vectors, followed by CL-LSI.  
- Major quality concern: reported positive results may reflect LSI's scaling advantage with a linear SVM, not the two-stage framework’s effectiveness.  
- Strong recommendation: validate with a classifier insensitive to feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka).  
- If rescaling-insensitive classifiers still outperform alternatives on the multilingual vectors, that would strongly support the method’s validity.  
- Significance: if results hold under rigorous validation, the approach would be a meaningful contribution because matrix completion provides controlled non-linear cross-lingual inference.  
- Novelty: the core idea appears novel in the translingual learning context.  
- Clarity issue 1: unclear whether the "unified document-term matrix" treats surface forms across languages as the same term or as language-specific tokens—please clarify.  
- Clarity issue 2: experimental setup description is fragmented across Table 1, making the methodology hard to reconstruct and requiring consolidation.
