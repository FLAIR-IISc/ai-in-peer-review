- Proposes a matrix completion approach for cross-domain classification using labeled source data and unlabeled parallel data, completed via projected gradient descent and reduced by LSI before classification, with convergence proof and favorable experiments.  
- Clarify the motivation for reducing dimensionality from d to k after estimating M—why is LSI needed and is it primarily for computational reasons or because M* remains dense despite sparsity enforcement?  
- Justify the choice of LSI over other dimensionality-reduction methods (e.g., PCA).  
- Clearly separate and report the two ways external data are used: (1) labeled source language data and (2) unlabeled parallel data, with results presented independently.  
- Clarify experiment setup (training: 4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel; test: remaining 1900 labeled target) and explain the discrepancy where CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 for EFM/EGM at 2k but not in Table 1.  
- Question the assumption that a fully observed document-term matrix is low-rank—provide justification since documents are unlikely linear combinations of others.  
- Note that TSL generally outperforms other methods and argue that enforcing low-rank on M* may drive performance; compare against multiview/alternative techniques (e.g., CCA) to contextualize strengths.  
- Report running time of the projected gradient descent algorithm and compare computational cost to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before Equation (2) and remove “such as” before Equations (4) and (5).  
- Overall: method is simple and effective but requires clarifications on motivations, assumptions, experimental details, comparisons, and runtime to strengthen the paper.
