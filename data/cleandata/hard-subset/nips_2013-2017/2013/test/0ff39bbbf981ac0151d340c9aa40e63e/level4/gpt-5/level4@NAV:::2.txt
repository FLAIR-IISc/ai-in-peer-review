Review- Summary: The paper proposes a two-stage method for learning cross-lingual topic representations by combining matrix completion with latent semantic analysis. The matrix completion step is optimized using a projected gradient descent algorithm. Experimental results demonstrate that the approach outperforms both monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on a sentiment analysis task using a parallel Amazon reviews dataset. Learning curves across varying amounts of unlabeled data show that the method achieves highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations with relatively small quantities of parallel data.

Quality: The experimental evaluation is thorough and supports the claims. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is carefully compared against two baselines (plain bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.

Clarity: The paper is well organized. All parameters and selection procedures are clearly reported, making the results easy to reproduce. Minor suggestion: elaborate more on the motivation for matrix completion in the Introduction (though it is explained in Section 3).

Originality: Although cross-lingual representation learning is not new, employing matrix completion for this problem is novel, and the proposed algorithm clearly achieves superior performance relative to prior methods in the literature.

Significance: The two-step method consistently outperforms other baselines. This work shows that matrix completion can serve as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and that this formulation may benefit other tasks. Specifically, the paper applies matrix completion before latent semantic analysis for cross-lingual sentiment classification; experiments indicate it surpasses monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art approach, and remains stable across different amounts of unlabeled parallel data.
