- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual topic representation method: matrix completion followed by LSA, solved via projected gradient descent.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to monolingual and several cross-lingual baselines on sentiment analysis using a parallel Amazon review dataset.  
- Shows the method learns accurate cross-lingual topics even with limited parallel data.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, with rigorous comparisons to diverse baselines and clear replication details.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; however, introduce the motivation for matrix completion earlier in the Introduction.  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is an original and valuable contribution.  
- Results indicate substantial and consistent performance gains, suggesting broad practical utility and stability across varying amounts of parallel data.  
- Authors convincingly argue the benefit of performing matrix completion before LSA for cross-lingual sentiment classification.  
- Overall assessment: strong submission with thorough experiments and clear presentation; recommend acceptance.  
- Questions/suggestions: clarify computational complexity and scalability of matrix completion; evaluate on other domains/languages/noisy datasets; discuss other tasks that could benefit and required modifications.
