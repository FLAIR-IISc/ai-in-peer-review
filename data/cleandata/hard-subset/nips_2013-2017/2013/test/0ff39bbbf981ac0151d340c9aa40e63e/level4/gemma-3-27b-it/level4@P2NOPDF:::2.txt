This paper introduces a two-step method for learning cross-lingual topic representations, combining matrix completion with latent semantic analysis (LSA). The approach utilizes a projected gradient descent algorithm to address the matrix completion problem. Experimental evaluation on a parallel Amazon review dataset demonstrates that the proposed method surpasses both monolingual and existing cross-lingual baselines in a sentiment analysis task. Notably, learning curves indicate the algorithm effectively learns accurate cross-lingual topic representations even with limited amounts of parallel data.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and convincingly supports the paper’s claims. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is rigorously compared against two straightforward baselines – a plain bag-of-words model and cross-lingual LSA – as well as two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction techniques. This thorough comparison strengthens the validity of the results. 

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, facilitating reproducibility. All relevant parameters and their selection criteria are explicitly detailed. A minor suggestion would be to elaborate on the motivation for employing matrix completion earlier in the introduction; while the rationale is presented in Section 3, highlighting it upfront would improve the flow and accessibility of the paper.

While cross-lingual representation learning itself is a well-established field, the application of matrix completion to this specific task represents a novel contribution. The demonstrated performance improvements over existing methods clearly establish the value of this approach.

The consistent outperformance of the proposed method compared to the baselines suggests a significant contribution to the field. This work demonstrates the potential of matrix completion as an effective pre-processing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and suggests that this formulation could be applicable to other related tasks. In essence, the paper presents a two-step methodology – matrix completion followed by LSA – for cross-lingual sentiment classification, achieving superior performance and robustness across varying amounts of parallel training data.
