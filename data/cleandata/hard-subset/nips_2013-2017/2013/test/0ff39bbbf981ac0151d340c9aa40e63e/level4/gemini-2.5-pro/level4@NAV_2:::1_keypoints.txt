- Introduces a novel two-step cross-language classification method: unified partially-observed document-term matrix → convex matrix completion (trace norm + L1) → LSI for low-dimensional representation → monolingual classifier.

- Novelty: Formulating cross-lingual representation learning as a matrix completion problem combined with LSI is creative and new.

- Strong empirical performance: Outperforms baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) across 18 tasks, especially robust when parallel data is scarce.

- Theoretical support: Provides convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent solver.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and organized with a clear step-by-step presentation.

- Questioning the low-rank assumption: More justification needed for why a fully observed document-term matrix is “naturally low-rank” despite document-level sparsity.

- Motivation for LSI: Clarify why LSI is needed after completion if M* becomes dense—whether for computational efficiency or added semantic benefit.

- Comparison to CL-KCCA: Provide intuition/analysis for why enforcing low-rank matrix completion outperforms methods like CL-KCCA that use paired data.

- Experimental reproducibility: Clarify the training/evaluation split (are 4000 labeled source, 100 labeled target for SVM, and 2000 unlabeled parallel used as stated, with evaluation on remaining 1900 target docs?).

- Result inconsistency: Resolve discrepancy where Table 1 shows TSL > CL-KCCA on EFM/EGM but Figure 1 at np=2000 shows CL-KCCA equal/better.

- Ablation study needed: Quantify contributions of labeled source vs unlabeled parallel data (e.g., experiment without parallel corpus) to isolate matrix completion’s effect.

- Complexity/runtime: Report computational complexity and empirical running time compared to baselines to assess practical utility.

- Minor editorial fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i, j)” before equation (2).

- Minor phrasing fix: reword the phrase before equation (3) so f(M) is presented as a definition rather than an example.
