- Paper proposes a two-stage matrix-completion approach for cross-domain classification: complete a single incomplete document×feature matrix with low-rank + sparsity via projected gradient descent, then apply LSI and train a classifier; includes a convergence guarantee and favorable experiments.
- Clarify why post-completion dimensionality reduction with LSI is needed—are motivations computational (dense M*) or for generalization, and why LSI specifically versus alternatives like PCA; report sensitivity to this choice.
- Distinguish and ablate the two external data sources (labeled source-language data vs. unlabeled parallel data) to isolate each contribution to performance.
- Clarify the experimental setup (confirm counts: 4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel, 1.9k target test) and explain the apparent performance discrepancy between Figure 1 and Table 1 for CL-KCCA vs TSL.
- Justify the low-rank prior on a document–term matrix: state structural assumptions (e.g., latent topic models) that make low-rank realistic and explain interaction with sparsity.
- Provide intuition for why TSL outperforms CCA-based multiview methods—explain whether the low-rank constraint on M* is the main advantage and why it beats correlation-maximizing projections.
- Report runtime and complexity: time per iteration and iterations to converge for projected gradient descent, compare wall-clock time vs CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA, and give complexity in terms of documents, vocabulary size, and nonzeros plus actual runtimes on the datasets.
- Fix minor edits: replace “(ij)” with “(i, j)” near Equation (2) and remove “such as” before Equations (4) and (5).
- Overall assessment: simple and effective method with promising empirical results, but several technical clarifications required (LSI rationale, experimental details, low-rank justification, runtime).
