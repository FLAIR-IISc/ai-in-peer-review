- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual topic representation method: matrix completion followed by LSA.  
- Solves matrix completion via projected gradient descent.  
- Demonstrates superior performance over monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on Amazon review sentiment classification.  
- Learning curves show high accuracy with minimal amounts of unlabeled parallel data.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and rigorously compare against two monolingual baselines, two cross-lingual baselines, and two SOTA dimensionality-reduction methods.  
- Results consistently show gains across all evaluation metrics.  
- Paper is well-structured, parameters and selection criteria are explicitly described, ensuring reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: expand the motivation for using matrix completion in the Introduction.  
- Original contribution: novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Significance: practical, efficient approach with potential applicability to other cross-lingual NLP tasks; overall strong empirical contribution.
