- Paper proposes a matrix-completion approach (build a single incomplete matrix, complete via projected gradient descent with low-rank and sparsity constraints, apply LSI and train a classifier), provides a convergence guarantee and reports favorable experiments.

- Clarify the justification for using LSI for dimensionality reduction—Is it primarily for computational reasons given the completed matrix is dense despite sparsity constraints, and why LSI instead of PCA or other methods?

- Explicitly distinguish the two types of external data (labeled source-domain data vs. unlabeled parallel data) and report their individual contributions via separate ablation experiments.

- Clarify the experimental setup (confirm training splits: 4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel, and 1900 test target) and explain the apparent discrepancy where CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 but not in Table 1 under similar conditions.

- Provide intuition and justification for assuming low-rank structure in the document-term matrix, since a fully observed sparse text matrix is unlikely to be low-rank.

- Explain conceptually why TSL outperforms alternatives, especially multi-view methods like CCA—how the low-rank constraint drives better performance relative to competing objectives.

- Report computational complexity and empirical running time, and compare projected gradient descent convergence time to training times of baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).

- Minor editorial fixes: change "(ij)" to "(i, j)" before equation (2) and consider removing "such as" before equations (4) and (5) for conciseness.
