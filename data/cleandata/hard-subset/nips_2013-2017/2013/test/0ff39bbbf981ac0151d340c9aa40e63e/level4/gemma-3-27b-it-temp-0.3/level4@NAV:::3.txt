This paper introduces a translingual representation learning method combining matrix completion for generating multilingual vectors with CL-LSI. While the approach is promising, the robustness of the reported results is questionable. The observed improvements may be attributable to the inherent scaling properties of LSI being more favorable for a linear SVM compared to OPCA or CCA. 

To strengthen the findings, I recommend evaluating the proposed system with a machine learning model less sensitive to feature scaling, such as boosted decision trees (readily available in Weka). Demonstrating superior performance with boosted decision trees would provide more convincing evidence of the method’s efficacy.

The potential impact of this work is notable; a robust result would be valuable as it leverages matrix completion – a controlled, non-linear inference technique – within a translingual learning framework. The core idea appears to be novel.

However, the paper suffers from some clarity issues. Specifically, line 113’s description of “construct[ing] a unified document-term matrix” is ambiguous. It is unclear whether this refers to a single term representing all surface forms (e.g., “Merkel”) or separate labels for each language-specific form (e.g., “Merkelen” and “Merkelde”). Clarification is needed. Furthermore, the experimental setup is fragmented across Table 1, hindering comprehension.

Overall, the concept is interesting. However, further investigation with alternative classifiers – beyond the linear SVM – is crucial to determine whether the observed results are genuinely robust or a consequence of scaling artifacts.
