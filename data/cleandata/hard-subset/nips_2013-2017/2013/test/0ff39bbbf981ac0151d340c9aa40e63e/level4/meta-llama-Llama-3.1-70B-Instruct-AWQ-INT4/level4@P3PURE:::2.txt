This paper presents a two-step method for learning a cross-lingual topic representation that leverages matrix completion and latent semantic analysis techniques. The proposed approach utilizes a projected gradient descent algorithm to optimize the matrix completion problem. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms both monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on a sentiment analysis task using a parallel Amazon review dataset. Furthermore, learning curves with varying sizes of unlabeled data illustrate that the proposed algorithm can learn highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations with a relatively small number of parallel data points.

Quality: The experiments conducted in this study are thorough and well-designed to support the authors' claims. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is carefully compared to two baselines (plain bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms, providing a comprehensive evaluation of its performance.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized, and the authors have provided a clear description of all parameters, including the selection process, making it easy to reproduce the results. One minor suggestion is to provide a more detailed explanation of the motivation behind using matrix completion in the introduction, as it is primarily described in Section 3.

Originality: Although the task of cross-lingual representation learning is not new, the application of matrix completion to this task is novel. The results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm achieves better performance than previous methods in the literature, showcasing its innovative contribution.

Significance: The results of the two-step approach consistently outperform other baselines. This work suggests that matrix completion can be effectively used as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks, and this formulation may be applicable to other tasks as well. The proposed two-step method for learning a cross-lingual topic representation, which combines matrix completion with latent semantic analysis, demonstrates promising results on a cross-lingual sentiment classification task. Experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including one state-of-the-art method, and remains stable even when trained on different sizes of unlabeled parallel data.
