- Proposes a novel two-step method: matrix completion on concatenated bilingual document-term matrix, then latent semantic indexing for cross-lingual representation.
- Exploits unlabeled parallel bilingual documents to infer missing features and produce a unified low-dimensional document representation.
- Uses monolingual classifiers on the learned representations for cross-language text classification.
- Thorough evaluation on cross-language sentiment classification using Amazon product reviews.
- Experimental results show significant improvement over four comparison methods, including two baselines and two SOTA dimensionality-reduction algorithms.
- Method requires much fewer parallel documents to achieve strong cross-lingual classification performance.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with reproducible parameter selection details.
- Original contribution is applying matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual representation learning.
- Significance is high: consistent performance gains suggest broader applicability to other tasks.
- Minor weakness: motivation for using matrix completion should be better explained in the introduction.
- Potential drawback: technical details of the matrix completion and LSI steps may be hard for some readers to follow.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, and significant contribution with strong experimental support.
