Two-step method: apply matrix completion to a unified document-term matrix, then use latent semantic indexing to obtain low-dimensional cross-lingual representations.

Optimization detail: formulates matrix completion solved via projected gradient descent on parallel bilingual documents.

Strong empirical results: consistently outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, especially with limited unlabeled parallel data.

Comprehensive evaluation: validated across 18 cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks with careful baseline comparisons (TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).

Clarity and reproducibility: paper is well-structured with detailed theoretical and implementation descriptions and documented parameter selection.

Minor clarity suggestion: motivation for using matrix completion should be stated earlier in the Introduction.

Originality: novel use of matrix completion to bridge language feature spaces before applying dimensionality reduction.

Significance: substantial practical gains (up to 14.61% accuracy improvement on EFB) and applicability to resource-constrained cross-lingual scenarios and other NLP tasks.
