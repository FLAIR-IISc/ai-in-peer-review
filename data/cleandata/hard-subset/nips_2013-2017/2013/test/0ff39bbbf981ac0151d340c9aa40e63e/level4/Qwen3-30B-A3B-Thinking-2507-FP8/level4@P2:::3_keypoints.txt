- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion to fill missing entries in a bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Differs from prior work (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) by explicitly addressing sparsity before dimensionality reduction.  
- Main contributions: (1) framing cross-lingual feature recovery as matrix completion, (2) a projected gradient descent algorithm with convergence guarantees, (3) improved cross-lingual sentiment classification, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Technical approach is sound and supported by rigorous convergence analysis.  
- Major experimental limitation: evaluation uses only linear SVMs, which are sensitive to feature scaling—observed gains may stem from LSI scaling rather than matrix completion.  
- Recommendation to validate with classifiers insensitive to scaling (e.g., boosted trees, random forests) to isolate the matrix completion effect.  
- Clarity issue: ambiguous construction of the unified document-term matrix—unclear if terms are language-shared or language-specific.  
- Clarity issue: experimental setup description is fragmented across sections and tables, hindering reproducibility and comprehension.  
- Originality: the specific two-step pipeline (complete then LSI) appears novel and is well-positioned relative to prior approaches.  
- Significance: potentially high impact if results are robust, as the method works well with minimal parallel data.  
- Strengths: elegant formulation, theoretical guarantees, and compelling robustness to small parallel datasets.  
- Weaknesses: limited classifier evaluation and clarity concerns about matrix construction and experimental reporting.  
- Overall: recommend acceptance contingent on addressing the classifier-dependency concern in the camera-ready version.
