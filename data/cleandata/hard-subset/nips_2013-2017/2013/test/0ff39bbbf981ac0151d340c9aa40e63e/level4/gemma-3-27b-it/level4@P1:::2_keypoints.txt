- Proposes a novel two-step method (matrix completion followed by LSA) for cross-lingual topic representations, optimized via projected gradient descent.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to monolingual baselines and existing cross-lingual methods on a parallel Amazon review sentiment task.  
- Learning curves show the method remains highly accurate even with limited parallel (unlabeled) data.  
- Experiments are thorough, comparing to bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality-reduction methods.  
- Convergence analysis of the optimization algorithm is a strong technical contribution.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with detailed parameter settings that facilitate reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity issues: motivate matrix completion more in the Introduction and simplify occasionally dense mathematical notation.  
- Originality: applying matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual representation learning (combined with LSI) is novel.  
- Significance: consistent performance gains and robustness suggest the approach could benefit other cross-lingual tasks beyond sentiment classification.  
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, robustness with limited parallel data, convergence proofs, and reproducibility.  
- Cons against acceptance: performance gains are not always large; lacks failure-case analysis/ablation studies and evaluation on tasks beyond sentiment.  
- Overall verdict: strong paper with recommendation for acceptance.
