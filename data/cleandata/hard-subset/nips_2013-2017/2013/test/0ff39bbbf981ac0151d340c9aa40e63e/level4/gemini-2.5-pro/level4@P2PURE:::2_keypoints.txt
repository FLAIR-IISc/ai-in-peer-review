- Proposes a two-step method: matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis for cross-lingual topic representations.
- Optimizes the matrix completion step using a projected gradient descent algorithm.
- Demonstrates superior performance to monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on a parallel Amazon review sentiment dataset.
- Learning curves show the method learns accurate cross-lingual topics with relatively small amounts of parallel data.
- Experiments are thorough and benchmark against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality-reduction methods.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and reports parameter choices to aid reproducibility.
- Minor clarity suggestion: motivate the use of matrix completion earlier (in the Introduction) rather than deferring to Section 3.
- Novel contribution: applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is new and effective.
- Key insight/significance: matrix completion as a preprocessing step improves cross-lingual sentiment classification and may generalize to other cross-lingual tasks.
- Robustness: the two-step approach outperforms SOTA and remains effective across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data.
