- Proposes a novel translingual representation pipeline combining matrix completion with Constrained LSI (CL-LSI).
- Evaluation methodology is flawed and raises robustness concerns about reported results.
- Primary experimental issue: use of linear SVMs which are highly sensitive to feature scaling and may bias results.
- Observed performance gains may be due to LSI’s scaling effects rather than the proposed method’s superiority.
- Authors should test with rescaling-invariant classifiers (e.g., gradient-boosted trees like XGBoost/LightGBM) to validate robustness.
- Significance of the contribution depends entirely on resolving the scaling/artifact question.
- The combination of matrix completion and CL-LSI appears novel within the literature.
- Clarity problem: ambiguity in how the unified document-term matrix handles surface forms (normalization vs. language-specific tokens).
- Experimental description is fragmented across the paper and tables, harming reproducibility.
- Recommended revisions: replace linear SVMs with tree-based classifiers, clarify term-construction approach, and consolidate experimental setup.
- As presented, the contribution is interesting but lacks sufficient evidence to justify claimed superiority.
