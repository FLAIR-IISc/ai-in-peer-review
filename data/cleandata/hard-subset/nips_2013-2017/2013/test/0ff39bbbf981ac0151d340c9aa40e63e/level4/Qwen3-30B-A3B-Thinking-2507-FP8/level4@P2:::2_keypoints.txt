- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion on a partially observed document-term matrix using a small set of unlabeled parallel documents, followed by LSI to obtain shared representations.  
- Key insight: a small parallel seed can bridge disjoint feature spaces by filling missing feature entries before LSI, unlike previous methods that translate all data or apply LSI directly to incomplete matrices.  
- Methodological contribution: formulates the task as matrix completion with a projected gradient descent algorithm for a non-smooth objective and provides convergence guarantees.  
- Experimental claim: consistent, substantial improvements over strong baselines (TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) on 18 cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks.  
- Empirical gains: average accuracy improvements of 8.59–14.61% over baselines, even with as few as 200 unlabeled parallel documents.  
- Datasets and settings: evaluated on multilingual Amazon product reviews across English–French, English–German, English–Japanese and categories Books, DVD, Music.  
- Reproducibility: thorough parameter selection and implementation details provided (γ=0.1, ρ=10^-4, k=50).  
- Technical quality: theoretical analysis and convergence proofs are sound and well-presented.  
- Experimental rigor: well-designed comparisons, clear tables/figures, and comprehensive ablations/parameter studies.  
- Originality: novel use of matrix completion as preprocessing for LSI and clear distinction from existing cross-lingual approaches.  
- Practical significance: effective with minimal parallel data, addressing realistic scarcity of large parallel corpora.  
- Writing and organization: exceptionally clear, easy to follow, and technically detailed for experts.  
- Main weakness: lacks discussion of potential limitations or failure cases (e.g., typologically distant languages or vocabulary disparities).  
- Additional weakness: insufficient analysis of computational complexity and scalability of the matrix completion step compared to baselines.
