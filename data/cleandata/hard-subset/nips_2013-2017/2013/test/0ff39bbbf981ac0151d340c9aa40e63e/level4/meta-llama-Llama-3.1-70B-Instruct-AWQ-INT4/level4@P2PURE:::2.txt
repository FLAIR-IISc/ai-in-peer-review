This paper proposes a two-step method for learning a cross-lingual topic representation, leveraging matrix completion and latent semantic analysis techniques. The authors utilize a projected gradient descent algorithm to optimize the matrix completion problem. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms both monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on a sentiment analysis task using the parallel Amazon review dataset. Furthermore, learning curves with varying sizes of unlabeled data illustrate that the proposed algorithm can learn highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations with a relatively small number of parallel data.

Quality: The experiments conducted to support the claims are thorough and well-designed. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is carefully compared to two baselines (plain bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms, providing a comprehensive evaluation of its performance.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized, and the authors have provided detailed descriptions of all parameters, including their selection process, making it easy to reproduce the results. A minor suggestion is to expand on the motivation behind using matrix completion in the Introduction, as it is currently only described in Section 3.

Originality: Although the task of cross-lingual representation learning is not novel, the application of matrix completion to this task is a new contribution. The proposed algorithm's superior performance compared to previous methods in the literature is evident.

Significance: The results of the two-step approach consistently outperform other baselines, indicating that matrix completion can be effectively used as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks. This formulation may also be applicable to other tasks, making this work a valuable contribution to the field.
