- Proposes a two-step method for cross-lingual topic representation combining matrix completion and latent semantic analysis (LSA).  
- Solves the matrix completion problem with a projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Evaluated on parallel Amazon review sentiment data, outperforming monolingual and cross-lingual baselines.  
- Demonstrates superior performance versus two baselines (bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Learning curves show the method learns accurate cross-lingual topics even with relatively small amounts of parallel unlabeled data.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and well-designed to substantiate claims.  
- All parameters and selection criteria are clearly documented, aiding reproducibility.  
- Clarity is strong; paper is well-structured and easy to follow.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: motivate the use of matrix completion earlier in the Introduction.  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is novel.  
- The approach is significant as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and potentially applicable to other tasks.
