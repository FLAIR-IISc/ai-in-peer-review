- Summary: Proposes a two-step cross-lingual topic representation method—matrix completion solved by projected gradient descent followed by LSA—that outperforms baselines on parallel Amazon review sentiment data and is data-efficient.

- Experiments/Quality: Thorough empirical evaluation against strong baselines (bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, two SOTA dimensionality reduction methods) with consistent performance gains.

- Reproducibility: Paper is well organized with parameters and selection procedures clearly specified, making results easy to reproduce.

- Clarity/suggestion: Generally clear, but Introduction should add a brief intuitive motivation for using matrix completion earlier.

- Originality: Novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning, convincingly combined with LSA to beat existing methods.

- Significance: Method is robust across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data, achieves strong performance with small parallel corpora, and is promising for other cross-lingual tasks.
