The paper presents a two-stage approach to cross-lingual representation learning: it first completes a joint bilingual document–term matrix via matrix completion to obtain bilingual document vectors, and then applies cross-lingual LSI to those vectors.

Quality: The idea is sensible, but I am not convinced the reported gains are robust. The improvements might stem from LSI’s scaling interacting favorably with a linear SVM compared to OPCA or CCA. I recommend evaluating with a learner that is comparatively insensitive to individual feature rescalings, such as boosted decision trees (e.g., in Weka). If boosted trees atop the proposed two-step pipeline also outperform the alternatives, that would strengthen the claim substantially.

Significance: If the gains hold under such checks, the result would be interesting, as matrix completion is a principled nonlinear inference tool.

Novelty: The overall formulation appears novel.

Clarity: A few points need clarification. Line 113 mentions “construct[ing] a unified document–term matrix for all documents.” Does this imply a single feature per surface form across languages (e.g., “Merkel”), or separate features per language-specific surface form (e.g., “Merkelen,” “Merkelde”)? Please specify. In addition, the experimental setup description is fragmented around Table 1, which made it slow to reconstruct.

Overall, this is a promising idea. I would have liked to see classifiers beyond a linear SVM; as it stands, it is hard to tell whether the gains are genuinely robust or primarily a scaling artifact.
