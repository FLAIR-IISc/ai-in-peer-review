- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual topic representation method: matrix completion followed by LSA.  
- Solves the matrix completion stage using projected gradient descent.  
- Experiments on parallel Amazon reviews show consistent improvement over monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction method.  
- Learning curves indicate strong cross-lingual representations even with limited parallel data.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, well-executed, and provides robust support for claims.  
- Rigorous comparisons made against monolingual bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with explicit parameter choices enabling reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: expand the motivation for using matrix completion in the Introduction.  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is original, and results outperform existing methods.  
- The two-step formulation consistently improves cross-lingual sentiment classification and may generalize to other cross-lingual tasks.
