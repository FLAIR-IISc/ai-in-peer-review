- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual topic representation: matrix completion (via projected gradient descent) followed by LSA.  
- Outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on parallel Amazon review sentiment classification.  
- Achieves high accuracy with relatively little unlabeled parallel data; learning curves show strong performance as data varies.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, with careful comparisons to bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Paper is well organized and reproducible (parameters and selection procedures documented); suggestion to better motivate matrix completion in the Introduction.  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution.  
- The method consistently outperforms baselines, is robust to varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data, and may generalize to other cross-lingual tasks.
