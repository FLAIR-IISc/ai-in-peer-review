## Review:

**Summary:** This paper introduces a novel two-step approach for learning cross-lingual topic representations, combining matrix completion with latent semantic analysis. The matrix completion stage is optimized using a projected gradient descent algorithm. The proposed method demonstrates superior performance compared to both monolingual and existing cross-lingual baselines in a sentiment analysis task utilizing a parallel Amazon review dataset. Importantly, learning curves demonstrate the algorithm's ability to learn highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations even with limited amounts of parallel data.

**Quality:** The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and effectively supports the paper's claims. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is rigorously benchmarked against two baselines – a simple bag-of-words model and cross-lingual LSA – as well as two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction techniques. 

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly written. All relevant parameters and their selection methods are thoroughly documented, facilitating reproducibility. A minor suggestion would be to further elaborate on the motivation behind employing matrix completion within the Introduction section, although a description is provided in Section 3.

**Originality:** While cross-lingual representation learning is an established field, the application of matrix completion to this problem is a novel contribution. The results clearly demonstrate that the proposed algorithm surpasses the performance of previously published methods.

**Significance:** The consistent and significant performance gains achieved by the two-step approach are noteworthy. This work highlights the potential of matrix completion as an effective pre-processing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification, and suggests that this formulation may be applicable and beneficial to other tasks as well. In essence, the paper presents a two-step method – matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis – for cross-lingual sentiment classification, achieving state-of-the-art results and demonstrating robustness across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data.
