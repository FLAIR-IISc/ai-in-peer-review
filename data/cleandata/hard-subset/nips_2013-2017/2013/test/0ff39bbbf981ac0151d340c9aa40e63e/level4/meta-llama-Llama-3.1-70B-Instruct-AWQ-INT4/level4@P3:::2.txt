This paper presents a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which leverages matrix completion and latent semantic analysis techniques. The proposed approach is carefully evaluated through extensive experiments on a cross-language sentiment classification task using a parallel Amazon review dataset. The results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms both monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method, including the optimization algorithm and the experimental setup. The parameters used in the experiments are also clearly stated, making it easy to reproduce the results. One minor suggestion is to provide more motivation for using matrix completion in the introduction, as it is primarily described in Section 3.

The application of matrix completion to cross-language representation learning is a novel contribution, and the proposed algorithm achieves better performance than previous methods in the literature. The results are consistently higher than other baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of the two-step approach. This work suggests that matrix completion can be used as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks, and this formulation may be effective in other tasks as well.

The strengths of the paper include:

* A novel application of matrix completion to cross-language representation learning
* Thorough experimental evaluation, including comparisons with multiple baselines
* Clear explanation of the proposed method and optimization algorithm
* Easy reproducibility of the results due to clearly stated parameters

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* Limited discussion of the motivation for using matrix completion in the introduction
* No analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm
* No exploration of the robustness of the proposed method to different types of noise or outliers in the data

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of cross-language representation learning, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed two-step approach.
