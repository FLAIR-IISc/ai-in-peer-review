This paper introduces a translingual representation learning approach combining matrix completion for generating multilingual vectors with CL-LSI. While the proposed method is conceptually promising, the reported results lack conclusive robustness. The observed performance gains might be attributable to the inherent scaling properties of LSI being more favorable for a linear SVM compared to OPCA or CCA, rather than a fundamental improvement in representation quality. 

To strengthen the findings, I recommend evaluating the method with machine learning systems less sensitive to feature scaling, such as boosted decision trees (readily available in tools like Weka). Demonstrating superior performance with such classifiers would significantly bolster confidence in the results.

The potential significance of this work lies in leveraging the controlled non-linearity of matrix completion for translingual learning, and a robust positive result would be a valuable contribution. The core idea appears novel.

However, the paper currently suffers from some clarity issues. Specifically, the description of document-term matrix construction on line 113 requires clarification. It’s unclear whether “unified document-term matrix” refers to a single term representing all surface forms (e.g., "Merkel") or distinct labels for each language-specific form (e.g., "Merkelen", "Merkelde"). Furthermore, the fragmented presentation of the experimental setup across Table 1 hindered quick comprehension. 

Overall, the approach is interesting, but further investigation with alternative classifiers is necessary to establish the robustness of the findings and rule out potential artifacts related to feature scaling.
