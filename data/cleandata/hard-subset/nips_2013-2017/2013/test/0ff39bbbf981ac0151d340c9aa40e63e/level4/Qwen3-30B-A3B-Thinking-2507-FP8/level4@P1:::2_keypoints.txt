- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual text classification method: matrix completion on a bilingual document-term matrix followed by LSI to get shared document representations.  
- Novelty: fills missing entries via matrix completion before dimensionality reduction, differing from prior CL-LSI/KCCA/OPCA that operate on incomplete matrices.  
- Evaluated on Amazon reviews across English, French, German, and Japanese, covering 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks.  
- Strong empirical gains: consistently outperforms monolingual TBOW and three state-of-the-art cross-lingual baselines, with +8.59% to +14.61% over TBOW.  
- Robust experimental design: systematic parameter selection (3-fold validation), analysis of unlabeled data size (200–2000 parallel docs), and 10 repetitions reporting standard deviations.  
- Particularly effective with scarce parallel data: maintains strong performance with only 200 parallel documents where competitors degrade.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a convergence proof for the projected gradient descent matrix completion algorithm.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: paper is well-organized, precise, and lists preprocessing, SVM settings, and parameters (γ, ρ, k).  
- Minor clarity issue: needs stronger motivation comparing matrix completion to simpler imputation (e.g., treating missing as zeros).  
- Limited analysis of failure cases: lacks deeper explanation for underperformance on specific tasks (e.g., EJB).  
- Significance: reduces parallel data requirements for cross-lingual learning and has practical relevance for low-resource languages.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, theoretically sound, empirically effective contribution; recommended for acceptance.
