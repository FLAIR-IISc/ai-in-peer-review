- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion of an incomplete document-term matrix followed by LSI for dimensionality reduction.
- Completes the matrix using a low-rank + sparsity-constrained formulation solved by projected gradient descent with a proven convergence guarantee.
- Generates cross-lingual document representations via LSI and uses them for classification.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on Amazon cross-lingual sentiment tasks, especially when parallel data is limited.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly presented, and positions itself relative to prior machine-translation, bilingual-lexicon, and cross-lingual representation work.
- The convergence analysis for the optimization algorithm is a positive theoretical contribution.
- Experimental results consistently improve over several baselines, supporting the method’s effectiveness.
- Clarify why LSI is applied after matrix completion and why LSI is preferred over alternatives like PCA.
- Disentangle and report the separate contributions of labeled source-language data versus unlabeled parallel data.
- Provide exact counts of labeled and unlabeled documents used in training/testing to improve reproducibility.
- Address the discrepancy between Table 1 and Figure 1 for CL-KCCA on EFM/EGM at 2k parallel documents.
- Explain and justify the low-rank assumption for a completed document-term matrix given inherent sparsity.
- Provide intuition for why TSL outperforms projection-based methods like CCA and how low-rank constraints drive gains.
- Include runtime and computational-cost comparisons with competing methods.
- Reviewer’s acceptance arguments: strong empirical results, clear presentation, convergence guarantee, relevance, and novel combination of matrix completion + LSI.
- Reviewer’s rejection concerns: unclear rationale for LSI choice, ambiguous experimental setup and inconsistencies, weak intuition vs CCA, and missing runtime analysis.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarifications and include runtime information.
- Minor edits: replace “(ij)” with “(i,j)” near Eq. (2) and remove “such as” before Eqs. (4) and (5).
