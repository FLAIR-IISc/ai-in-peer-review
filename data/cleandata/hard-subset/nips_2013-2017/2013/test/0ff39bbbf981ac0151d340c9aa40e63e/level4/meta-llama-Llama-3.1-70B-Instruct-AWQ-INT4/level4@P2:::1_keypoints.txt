- Proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning using unlabeled parallel bilingual documents.  
- Step 1: construct concatenated bilingual document-term matrix and complete it with projected gradient descent enforcing low-rank and sparsity.  
- Step 2: apply LSI for dimensionality reduction on the completed matrix and train a standard classifier on the new representation.  
- Clear motivation and discussion of limitations of existing methods that rely on MT or bilingual lexicons.  
- Technical details (matrix completion, projected gradient algorithm, convergence guarantee) are well-explained.  
- Experimental results show the method outperforms other cross-lingual representation methods, especially with few parallel documents.  
- Needs clarification why the completed matrix M* cannot handle feature sparsity sufficiently.  
- Needs justification for choosing LSI over other dimensionality-reduction techniques.  
- Experimental settings require clearer reporting: counts of labeled source, labeled target, and unlabeled parallel documents, and exact test split used.  
- Minor corrections suggested (e.g., fix notation (ij) → (i,j); remove unnecessary phrases before eqs. (4) and (5)).  
- Overall well-written, organized, and easy to follow.  
- Original approach distinct from translation- or lexicon-based methods.  
- Potentially significant contribution to cross-language text classification if clarifications are addressed.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, simple, effective method with strong results; rejection arguments: unresolved technical clarifications and experiment-reporting gaps.
