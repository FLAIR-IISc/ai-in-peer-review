- Proposed a novel two-step method for cross-lingual topic representations using matrix completion followed by LSA, solved with projected gradient descent.  
- Experimental results show the method outperforms mono-lingual and cross-lingual baselines on Amazon review sentiment classification.  
- Learning curves indicate the algorithm learns accurate cross-lingual topics even with relatively few parallel data points.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, comparing against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction baselines, supporting the authors' claims.  
- Paper is well-structured and reproducible with explicit parameter settings; minor suggestion to motivate matrix completion earlier (in the Introduction).  
- Applying matrix completion to this task is an innovative contribution, yielding improved performance over existing methods.  
- The method achieves state-of-the-art cross-lingual sentiment classification and is stable across different amounts of unlabeled parallel data, with potential applicability to other tasks.
