- Proposes a two-stage method: matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis to learn cross-lingual topic representations.
- Matrix completion step is optimized using a projected gradient descent algorithm.
- Evaluated on parallel Amazon reviews for sentiment analysis and outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines.
- Learning curves show high-quality cross-lingual topic representations with relatively small amounts of parallel data.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares the method against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction methods.
- Paper is well organized with clear reporting of parameters and selection procedures, aiding reproducibility.
- Minor clarity suggestion: motivate matrix completion more in the Introduction (explained later in Section 3).
- Original contribution: novel use of matrix completion for cross-lingual representation learning, achieving superior performance.
- Significance: matrix completion as a preprocessing step yields consistent gains and may benefit other cross-lingual tasks, remaining stable across different unlabeled data sizes.
