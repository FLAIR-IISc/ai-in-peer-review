- Paper proposes a matrix completion method for cross-domain classification that completes a single incomplete document-term matrix with low-rank and sparsity constraints via projected gradient descent, then applies LSI and a standard classifier; convergence proof and favorable experiments are reported.
- Please clarify the motivation for reducing M’s dimension with LSI (is it primarily due to computational convenience because M* becomes dense despite sparsity enforcement?) and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.
- Distinguish clearly the two ways external data are used—(1) labeled source-language data and (2) unlabeled parallel data—and report ablation results separately for each contribution.
- Clarify the experimental setup (e.g., whether training used 4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel, and testing on the remaining 1900 target documents) and explain the apparent inconsistency where CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 at 2k but not in Table 1.
- Justify the low-rank assumption for a fully observed document-term matrix, since documents are unlikely to be exact linear combinations of others and the rank could be min(d, n).
- Provide intuition for why TSL typically outperforms other methods (especially CCA) and explain how the matrix-completion approach’s low-rank enforcement compares conceptually and practically to multiview approaches.
- Report running time and computational cost of the projected gradient descent algorithm and compare it empirically to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.
- Minor editorial fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before equation (2) and remove “such as” before equations (4) and (5).
- Overall: method is simple, effective, and generally clearly presented, but several technical clarifications and experimental/explanatory details are needed.
