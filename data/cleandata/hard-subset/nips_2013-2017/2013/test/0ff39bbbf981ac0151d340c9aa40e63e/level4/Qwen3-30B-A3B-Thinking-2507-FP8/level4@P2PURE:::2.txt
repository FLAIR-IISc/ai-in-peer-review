

Summary: This paper introduces a two-step method for learning cross-lingual topic representations via matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis (LSA). The matrix completion problem is optimized using projected gradient descent. Experiments demonstrate that the proposed approach outperforms both monolingual baselines and cross-lingual baselines—including a state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction method—on sentiment analysis using the parallel Amazon review dataset. Learning curves across varying unlabeled data sizes confirm that the algorithm achieves highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations with relatively small amounts of parallel data.  
Quality: The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-executed, providing robust support for the claims. The proposed two-step algorithm is rigorously compared against two baselines (plain bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms.  
Clarity: The paper is well-structured and clearly written. All parameters, including their selection criteria, are explicitly detailed, ensuring reproducibility. A minor suggestion: the motivation for employing matrix completion should be expanded in the Introduction (it is currently described in Section 3).  
Originality: While cross-lingual representation learning is established, the application of matrix completion to this task is novel. The results demonstrate clear performance gains over existing methods in the literature.  
Significance: The two-step approach consistently outperforms all baselines. This work establishes matrix completion as a viable preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification, suggesting broader applicability to other cross-lingual tasks. The method remains robust when trained on varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data.
