**Summary:** This work introduces a two-stage approach for learning cross-lingual topic representations, which combines matrix completion with latent semantic analysis. To address the matrix completion problem, the authors employ a projected gradient descent algorithm for optimization. On a parallel Amazon review dataset, experiments in sentiment analysis demonstrate that the proposed method surpasses both monolingual and cross-lingual baseline models. Furthermore, learning curves generated with varying amounts of unlabeled data indicate that the algorithm can learn highly effective cross-lingual representations even with a limited quantity of parallel data.

**Quality:** The claims made in the paper are well-supported by a comprehensive set of experiments. The proposed two-stage learning algorithm is rigorously evaluated against several relevant models, including two standard baselines (bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art methods for cross-lingual dimensionality reduction.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and easy to follow. The authors have detailed all parameters and their selection criteria, which should make the results straightforward to reproduce. A minor suggestion for improvement would be to introduce the motivation for using matrix completion earlier in the Introduction, rather than waiting until Section 3, to better frame the contribution.

**Originality:** While the field of cross-lingual representation learning is established, the application of matrix completion to this task represents a novel contribution. Moreover, the proposed algorithm demonstrably surpasses the performance of existing methods reported in the literature.

**Significance:** The performance of the proposed two-stage approach consistently exceeds that of all baseline methods. This work effectively demonstrates that matrix completion can serve as a powerful preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification, suggesting that this formulation could also be beneficial for other cross-domain tasks. The paper's core contribution is a method that applies matrix completion prior to latent semantic analysis for sentiment classification. Empirical evaluation confirms its superiority over monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art competitor, while also showing robust performance across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel training data.
