- Proposes a novel two-step method: matrix completion to create a complete parallel document-term matrix, then latent semantic indexing to produce low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Requires only a small set of unlabeled parallel documents, unlike many prior methods that need full translation of training data.  
- Related to prior cross-language approaches (machine translation, bilingual lexicons, cross-lingual representations) but distinct in data requirements.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the matrix completion formulation and LSI application.  
- Experimental setup is comprehensive, evaluating the method on 18 cross-language sentiment classification tasks.  
- Demonstrates outperforming TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA, with significant accuracy improvements.  
- Recommends assessing robustness using additional classifiers (e.g., boosted decision trees) that are insensitive to feature rescaling.  
- Requests clarification on how the unified document-term matrix is constructed (single term per surface form vs. language-labeled surface forms).  
- Significance: method is novel, well-motivated, and can advance cross-language text classification.  
- Main concerns: potential lack of robustness and minor clarity issues that require further evaluation/explanation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
