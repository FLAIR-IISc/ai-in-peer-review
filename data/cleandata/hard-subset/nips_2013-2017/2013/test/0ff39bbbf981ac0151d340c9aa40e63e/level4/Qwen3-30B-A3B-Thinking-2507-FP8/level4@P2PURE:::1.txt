

The paper proposes a matrix completion approach for cross-domain classification, leveraging labeled data from an auxiliary domain and unlabeled parallel data. The method involves two steps: first, constructing an incomplete matrix encompassing all documents and domains, then completing it under low-rank and sparsity constraints via projected gradient descent. Second, dimensionality reduction is applied to the completed matrix using Latent Semantic Indexing (LSI), followed by training a standard classifier on the reduced representation. The paper provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and reports favorable experimental results.  

Key comments and questions:  
- After completing matrix $M$, dimensionality is reduced from $d$ to $k$ using LSI, as $M$ "lacks sufficient capacity to handle feature sparseness." Could you elaborate? Is this primarily for computational efficiency (since $M^*$ becomes dense despite sparsity enforcement), and why LSI over alternatives like PCA?  
- The paper treats two distinct uses of external data: (1) leveraging labeled source-domain data to aid target-domain classification, and (2) leveraging unlabeled parallel data. Please clarify and report results separately for these aspects.  
- The experimental setup (Section 5.2) requires clarification: Training uses (a) 4,000 labeled source documents, (b) 100 labeled target documents, and (c) 2,000 unlabeled parallel documents. Results are reported on 1,900 remaining labeled target documents. Section 5.3 varies unlabeled parallel documents from 200 to 2,000. Why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL in Figure 1 (EFM/EGM at 2,000 unlabeled data) but not in Table 1?  
- Why is a fully observed document-term matrix low-rank? It is sparse, but it is unlikely that any document is a linear combination of others in the vocabulary, suggesting rank $\min(d, k)$.  
- Why does TSL consistently outperform methods like CCA (a natural multiview approach)? The matrix completion approach seems less intuitive than CCA; is the low-rank enforcement on $M^*$ the key advantage over other techniques?  
- What is the algorithm’s runtime? How does projected gradient descent compare to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA in speed?  

Suggested revisions:  
- Correct "(ij)" to "(i,j)" two sentences before Equation (2).  
- Remove "such as" before Equations (4) and (5).  

The method is simple, effective, and clearly presented, though several technical points require clarification.
