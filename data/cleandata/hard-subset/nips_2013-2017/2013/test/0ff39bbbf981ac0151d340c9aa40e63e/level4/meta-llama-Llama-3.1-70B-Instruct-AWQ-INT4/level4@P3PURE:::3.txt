The paper proposes a method for translingual representation learning, which involves two steps: first, using matrix completion to create multilingual vectors for each row, and then performing Cross-Lingual Latent Semantic Indexing (CL-LSI) on the multilingual vectors.

Quality: While this approach seems promising, I have concerns about the robustness of the results. The positive outcomes may be attributed to the scaling of LSI being more suitable for a linear SVM than Orthogonal Projection on Convex Sets (OPCA) or Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA). To address this, I suggest the authors use a machine learning system that is invariant to rescalings of individual features, such as boosted decision trees (available in Weka). If the two-step system with boosted decision trees outperforms other techniques, I believe the results would be more convincing.

Significance: If the results are indeed robust, this would be a significant contribution, as matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique.

Novelty: The idea presented in the paper appears to be novel.

Clarity: There are some issues with clarity in the paper. For instance, Line 113 mentions "construct[ing] a unified document-term matrix for all documents." I would like the authors to clarify whether this involves using one term for every surface form (e.g., "Merkel") or labeling each surface form from each language (e.g., "Merkelen" and "Merkelde"). Additionally, the experimental setup description is scattered across Table 1, making it difficult to follow. I wish the authors had explored other classifiers beyond linear SVM to further establish the robustness of the results, as I am unsure whether the outcome is a genuine improvement or a scaling artifact.
