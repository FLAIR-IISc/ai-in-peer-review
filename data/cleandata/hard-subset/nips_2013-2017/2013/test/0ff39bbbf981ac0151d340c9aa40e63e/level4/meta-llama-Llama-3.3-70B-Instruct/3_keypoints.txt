- Proposes a novel translingual representation: use matrix completion to produce multilingual row vectors, then apply CL-LSI.  
- Promising empirical results, but robustness is questionable.  
- Positive outcomes may be due to LSI’s scaling being more suitable for a linear SVM than OPCA/CCA.  
- Recommend validating with scale-insensitive classifiers (e.g., boosted decision trees available in Weka).  
- If results hold with robust classifiers, the method would be significant because matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique.  
- The approach is novel and not extensively explored.  
- Clarity issues: construction of the unified document-term matrix is unclear (single term per surface form vs. language-labeled surface forms).  
- Experimental setup description is fragmented across Table 1 and needs a clearer, consolidated explanation.
