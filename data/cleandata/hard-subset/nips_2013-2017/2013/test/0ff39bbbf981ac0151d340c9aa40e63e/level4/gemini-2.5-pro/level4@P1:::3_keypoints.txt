- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation: matrix completion to fill a unified document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional semantics.  
- Targets cross-language text classification, evaluated on Amazon sentiment reviews across multiple language pairs.  
- Empirical results show consistent, strong gains over baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA), especially with very limited parallel data.  
- Method is well-motivated and principled: leverages co-occurrence and low-rank structure; convergence analysis provided.  
- Main concern: all evaluation uses a linear SVM, which is sensitive to feature scaling and geometry and may favor the proposed representation.  
- Possible artifact: improvements might reflect better scaling/linear separability for SVM rather than intrinsically superior semantics.  
- Strong suggestion: rerun experiments with scale-invariant classifiers (e.g., boosted trees or random forests) to validate representation quality.  
- Clarity issues: clarify how the unified vocabulary is constructed (language-tagged features or merged identical forms).  
- Presentation suggestion: move critical experimental details currently only in Table 1 caption into Section 5.2 for readability.  
- If robustness holds across classifiers, the approach is significant for low-resource cross-lingual learning and is an original contribution.  
- Recommendation: add the suggested classifier experiment to strengthen claims and increase confidence in results.
