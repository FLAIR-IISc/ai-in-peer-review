- Introduces a two-step cross-lingual topic learning method: matrix completion followed by LSA.  
- Uses projected gradient descent to optimize the matrix completion component.  
- Experiments on parallel Amazon review data show consistent improvements over monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction technique.  
- Learning curves demonstrate high-quality cross-lingual topic representations can be learned with minimal parallel data.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares rigorously against monolingual bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two SOTA cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with explicit parameter settings supporting reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: expand the motivation for using matrix completion in the Introduction rather than only in Section 3.  
- Originality lies in applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning, despite the broader task not being novel.  
- Significance: the two-step approach consistently yields higher accuracy and shows matrix completion is an effective preprocessing step with broader applicability.
