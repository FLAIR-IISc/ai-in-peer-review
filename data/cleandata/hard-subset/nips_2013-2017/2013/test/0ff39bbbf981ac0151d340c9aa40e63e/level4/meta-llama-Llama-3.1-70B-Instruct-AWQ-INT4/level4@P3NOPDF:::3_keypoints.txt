1. Proposes a novel translingual representation method combining matrix completion with CL-LSI to produce multilingual vectors.  
2. Approach is intriguing but robustness of results is uncertain.  
3. Reported performance may be due to LSI's scaling advantages over OPCA/CCA for a linear SVM.  
4. Strong recommendation to test with classifiers invariant to per-feature rescaling, e.g., boosted decision trees (Weka).  
5. If the two-step system plus boosted trees outperforms alternatives, the results would be significantly strengthened.  
6. If robust, the work is significant because matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique.  
7. The method appears novel and is a fresh approach to translingual representation learning.  
8. Clarity issue: ambiguous description of the "unified document-term matrix"—whether terms are unified across surface forms or language-specific.  
9. Experimental setup presentation is fragmented (spread across Table 1) and should be consolidated or explained more clearly.  
10. Conclusion: further experiments with different classifiers are necessary to confirm robustness and rule out scaling artifacts.
