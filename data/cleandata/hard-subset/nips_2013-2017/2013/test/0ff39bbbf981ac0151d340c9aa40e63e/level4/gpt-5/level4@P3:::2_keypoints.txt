- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual text classification: convex matrix completion on a bilingual document-term matrix followed by LSI and monolingual SVM training.
- Completion objective combines trace norm (low-rank), L1 sparsity, squared loss on observed entries, small quadratic penalty on missing entries, and nonnegativity.
- Optimization solved via projected gradient descent with singular value shrinkage and a convergence proof under standard step-size conditions.
- Evaluated on multilingual Amazon reviews (EN, FR, DE, JP; Books/DVD/Music) across 18 transfer tasks.
- Method consistently outperforms target-only BOW and three cross-lingual baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).
- Robust performance even with very small numbers of parallel documents (down to ~200 pairs).
- Hyperparameters selected once and reused; results averaged over multiple draws with reported standard deviations.
- Missing analysis of runtime and scalability despite repeated SVDs in the inner loop.
- Lacks ablation studies (remove L1, remove nonnegativity, skip LSI) to isolate component contributions.
- No statistical significance testing beyond means and standard deviations.
- Clarity: generally well-organized with precise notation and reproducible hyperparameter details.
- Suggest clarifying motivation for completion before LSI and whether parallel documents overlap labeled data.
- Originality: novel application of convex matrix completion before LSI in cross-lingual classification; contrasts with existing CL-LSI/KCCA/OPCA approaches.
- Omits comparison to important baselines like Structural Correspondence Learning (SCL) and some co-training methods.
- Significance: simple, effective, and broadly applicable, but evaluation is English-centric and limited to Amazon sentiment.
- Suggested improvements: add runtime/memory diagnostics, ablations, comparisons to SCL/co-training, tests on non-English pairs and varying target-labeled sizes.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper with practical gains and a lean toward acceptance, pending added scalability and comparative analyses.
