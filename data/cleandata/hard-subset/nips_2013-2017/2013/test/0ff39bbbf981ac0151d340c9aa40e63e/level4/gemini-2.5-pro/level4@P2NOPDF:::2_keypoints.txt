- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual topic representation method: matrix completion on a cross-lingual term-document matrix followed by LSA, optimized with projected gradient descent.
- Demonstrates superior performance over mono-lingual and multiple cross-lingual baselines on a parallel Amazon review sentiment dataset.
- Shows the method learns accurate representations with relatively small amounts of parallel data (learning-curve evidence).
- Experiments are thorough and comparisons to a comprehensive set of baselines (BOW, cross-lingual LSA, two SOTA methods) strongly support the claims.
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible with detailed parameter descriptions; minor clarity suggestion to motivate matrix completion earlier in the Introduction.
- Novel contribution: applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning, validated by improved empirical results.
- Significant and stable performance gains across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data, with potential applicability to other cross-lingual NLP tasks.
