This paper proposes a novel two-step method for learning cross-lingual topic representations by leveraging matrix completion and latent semantic analysis techniques. The authors utilize a projected gradient descent algorithm to optimize the matrix completion problem. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, outperforming both monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on a sentiment analysis task using a parallel Amazon review dataset.

The quality of the experiments is high, with thorough comparisons to two baselines (plain bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms. This comprehensive evaluation provides strong support for the authors' claims.

The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and methodology. The parameters used in the experiments are clearly stated, including the selection process, allowing for straightforward reproduction of the results. One minor suggestion is to provide additional motivation for using matrix completion in the Introduction, as its explanation is currently relegated to Section 3.

Although cross-lingual representation learning is not a new task, the application of matrix completion to this problem is novel and yields better performance than existing methods in the literature. The proposed algorithm's consistently higher results compared to other baselines underscore its significance. This work demonstrates that matrix completion can be effectively used as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks, and this formulation may be applicable to other tasks as well.

Overall, the paper presents a well-motivated and well-executed study that contributes to the field of cross-lingual representation learning. The experimental results are convincing, and the proposed method shows promise for improving performance on cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks.
