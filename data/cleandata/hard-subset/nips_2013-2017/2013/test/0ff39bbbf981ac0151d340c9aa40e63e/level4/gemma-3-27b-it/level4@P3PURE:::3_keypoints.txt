- Proposes translingual representation learning by first performing matrix completion to generate multilingual vectors, then applying Canonical-Latent Semantic Indexing (CL-LSI).  
- Reported improvements may not be robust; gains could be due to LSI’s favorable scaling properties versus OPCA/CCA when used with a linear SVM.  
- Recommends evaluating the method with classifiers less sensitive to feature scaling, e.g., boosted decision trees (available in Weka).  
- If the two-step system still outperforms with boosted trees, confidence in the validity of the results would increase.  
- Significance: a robust demonstration would be valuable because matrix completion provides a well-defined, non-linear inference technique.  
- Novelty: the core idea appears novel.  
- Clarity issue: unclear whether the “unified document-term matrix” uses a single term for all surface forms or retains separate language-specific surface forms (e.g., “Merkel” vs “Merkelen”/“Merkelde”).  
- Clarity issue: the experimental setup is fragmented across Table 1, making it difficult to fully understand.  
- Overall: neat idea, but primary concern is whether observed performance gains are robust or an artifact of LSI’s feature-scaling characteristics; authors should test classifiers beyond linear SVM.
