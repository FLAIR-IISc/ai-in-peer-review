- Method: Proposes translingual representation learning using matrix completion to produce multilingual row vectors, followed by CL-LSI on those vectors.
- Quality concern: Results may not be robust; observed gains could be due to LSI’s scaling properties favoring a linear SVM rather than the proposed method.
- Validation suggestion: Authors should evaluate classifiers insensitive to feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka) to test whether gains are methodological or scaling artifacts.
- Significance: A robust confirmation would be valuable because matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique.
- Novelty: The core idea appears novel.
- Clarity issue 1: Unclear whether the “unified document-term matrix” uses a single term per surface form across languages or language-specific surface-form labels.
- Clarity issue 2: Experimental setup description is fragmented (spread across Table 1), making the experiments hard to follow.
- Overall: Promising idea but conclusions depend on addressing the scaling concern and evaluating alternative classifiers.
