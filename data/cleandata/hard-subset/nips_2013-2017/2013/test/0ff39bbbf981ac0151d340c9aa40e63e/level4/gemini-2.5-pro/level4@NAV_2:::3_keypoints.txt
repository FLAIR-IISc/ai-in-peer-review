- Proposes a novel two-step method: matrix completion to fill cross-lingual missing entries, then LSI to get shared dense representations.  
- Leverages a small set of parallel documents to bridge vocabulary gaps between source and target languages.  
- Constructs a unified (sparse) document-term matrix across both languages with missing cross-language entries.  
- Frames completion as a convex optimization with trace-norm and l1 regularization, solved by projected gradient descent with convergence analysis.  
- Related to CL-LSI in spirit but introduces matrix completion as a principled densification step before dimensionality reduction.  
- Evaluated on cross-lingual sentiment classification (Amazon reviews) and reports superior performance over several baselines.  
- Strength: novel combination of matrix completion and LSI that directly addresses missing cross-lingual information.  
- Strength: potentially significant for low-resource scenarios given effectiveness with few parallel documents.  
- Strength: solid technical formulation and convergence-backed optimization algorithm.  
- Weakness: experimental validation limited—only a linear SVM used as downstream classifier, raising concerns about result robustness.  
- Concern that observed gains may be due to scaling/geometry favorable to linear SVM rather than better representations.  
- Request: run classifier-agnostic experiments (e.g., Random Forests or Gradient Boosted Trees) to confirm representation quality.  
- Request: clarify how the unified vocabulary handles orthographically identical words across languages (merged vs. language-tagged).  
- Request: consolidate and clarify experimental setup (train/test splits, use of parallel data, parameter selection) in one place for reproducibility.
