- Proposes a two-stage translingual representation: matrix completion to build multilingual vectors, followed by CL-LSI on those vectors.
- Promising idea, but results may not be robust; observed gains could reflect LSI’s favorable scaling with a linear SVM rather than better representations.
- Recommends evaluating a classifier insensitive to per-feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees) to rule out scaling artifacts.
- If gains persist under scale-invariant evaluation, the contribution would be significant because matrix completion is a well-understood nonlinear inference tool.
- The approach appears novel.
- Clarity issue: unclear whether the unified document–term matrix uses a shared cross-language vocabulary or language-specific surface forms.
- Clarity issue: experimental setup is fragmented around Table 1; needs a consolidated description of datasets, preprocessing, features, and evaluation protocols.
- Overall: neat idea, but more experiments beyond a linear SVM are needed to confirm genuine representational improvements.
