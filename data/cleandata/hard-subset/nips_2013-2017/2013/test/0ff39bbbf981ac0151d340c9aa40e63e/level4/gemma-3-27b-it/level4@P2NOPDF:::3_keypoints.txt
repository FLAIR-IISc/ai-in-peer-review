- Proposes a translingual representation learning method combining matrix completion with Cross-Lingual LSI to create a shared document embedding space across languages.
- Reported improvements look promising but may not be robust—gains could stem from LSI’s scaling properties favoring a linear SVM versus OPCA/CCA.
- Recommends evaluating with a classifier less sensitive to feature scaling (e.g., boosted decision trees available in Weka) to validate the findings.
- If the two-step system still outperforms alternatives with boosted trees, confidence in the method’s effectiveness would increase substantially.
- The use of matrix completion as a controlled non-linear inference for cross-lingual representations is potentially a significant contribution if validated.
- The core idea appears novel.
- Clarity issues: it’s unclear whether the “unified document-term matrix” collapses all surface forms across languages into single terms or keeps language-specific forms distinct—this must be clarified.
- Experimental setup presentation is fragmented (details scattered, especially across Table 1); a consolidated description would improve readability.
- Overall: concept is appealing but requires additional experiments with alternative classifiers to rule out scaling artifacts and establish robustness.
