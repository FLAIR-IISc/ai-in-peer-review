- Paper proposes a matrix completion method for cross-domain classification using labeled source data and unlabeled parallel data, with projected gradient descent and LSI-based dimensionality reduction.  
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and shows favorable experimental results.  
- Ask authors to elaborate why LSI is used to reduce M from d to k — is this for computational reasons because M* becomes dense despite sparsity enforcement, and why LSI rather than PCA or other methods?  
- Request clear separation and reporting of the two ways external data are exploited: (1) labeled source data transfer and (2) unlabeled parallel data usage.  
- Experimental setup is unclear — confirm training used 4k labeled source docs, 100 labeled target docs, and 2k unlabeled parallel docs, with results reported on the remaining 1900 target docs; clarify experiment variations in Section 5.3.  
- Point out inconsistency: why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL in Figure 1 for EFM and EGM at 2k unlabeled docs but not in Table 1?  
- Question the claim that a fully observed document-term matrix would be low-rank — request clarification/justification for this assumption.  
- Ask for intuition why Two-Stage Learning (TSL) generally outperforms methods like CCA, and how enforcing low-rankness on M* compares to other techniques.  
- Request running time comparison: how does projected gradient descent runtime compare to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?  
- Minor editorial suggestions: fix (ij) to (i,j) near equation (2) and remove "such as" before equations (4) and (5).  
- Overall assessment: method is simple, effective, and generally clearly presented, but several technical points and experimental clarifications are needed.
