- Proposes a matrix completion approach for cross-domain (cross-language) classification using labeled source data and unlabeled parallel data.  
- Two-stage method: (1) build a single incomplete matrix spanning documents and domains and complete it via PGD under low-rank and sparsity constraints; (2) apply LSI to the completed matrix and train a classifier.  
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the PGD algorithm and reports favorable experimental results.  
- Rationale for dimensionality reduction after completion is unclear—ask whether it’s mainly computational because M* becomes dense, and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.  
- Need to disentangle the roles of external data: separately evaluate the contributions of labeled source-language data vs. unlabeled parallel data (ablation studies requested).  
- Experimental-setup clarification requested: confirm training uses 4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel, and testing on 1,900 remaining labeled target documents.  
- Clarify Section 5.3: is the only change varying unlabeled parallel documents from 200 to 2k, and why does CL-KCCA beat TSL in Figure 1 for some datasets at 2k but not in Table 1?  
- Question the low-rank prior: justify why a fully observed document–term matrix should be low-rank given sparsity and lack of linear dependence among documents.  
- Ask for intuition why TSL outperforms CCA-based and other baselines—specifically whether the low-rank constraint on M* is the primary driver and how this differs from what CCA captures.  
- Request running-time and complexity information: wall-clock times and how PGD completion compares cost-wise to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA for the datasets used.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i, j)” and remove “such as” before Equations (4) and (5).  
- Overall assessment: method is simple, effective, and generally clearly presented, but several technical points require clarification.
