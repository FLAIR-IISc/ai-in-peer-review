- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method using matrix completion followed by LSA, solved via projected gradient descent.  
- Empirical evaluation shows the method outperforms monolingual baselines and existing cross-lingual techniques on a parallel Amazon review dataset.  
- Demonstrates robustness: maintains high accuracy even with limited amounts of unlabeled parallel data.  
- Strong technical quality with thorough experiments and comparisons to bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction baselines.  
- Well-organized and clearly written; all relevant parameters and selection methods are documented for reproducibility.  
- Novel contribution: unique application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning despite the task itself not being entirely new.  
- Suggestion: better motivate the use of matrix completion earlier in the Introduction.  
- Weakness: performance gains over some baselines are consistent but not always large.  
- Weakness: theoretical convergence analysis could be expanded to better address the cross-lingual data context.
