- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion followed by LSA to bridge feature gaps between languages.
- Uses a small set of parallel bilingual documents to complete a partially observed document-term matrix, then induces low-dimensional cross-lingual embeddings.
- Solves matrix completion via projected gradient descent and provides convergence guarantees.
- Evaluated on Amazon cross-lingual sentiment classification and outperforms several baselines, especially with limited parallel data.
- Method is technically sound with a clear approach and thorough experimental validation.
- Carefully compares against monolingual bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSI, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction baselines.
- Experiments that vary amount of parallel data demonstrate robustness to scarce parallel supervision.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; experimental setup and parameter selection details improve reproducibility.
- Suggestion to expand the matrix completion motivation in the Introduction for better accessibility.
- Novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual learning, clearly differentiated from related work with appropriate citations.
- Results are significant and suggest potential extension to other cross-lingual tasks and broader impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical gains, robustness with limited data, clarity, and reproducibility.
- Arguments against acceptance: gains are consistent but not always large; needs deeper analysis of why it works and stronger theoretical development.
- Overall assessment: solid, novel, and empirically validated contribution that is worthy of acceptance, though further theoretical work or larger performance gains would strengthen it.
