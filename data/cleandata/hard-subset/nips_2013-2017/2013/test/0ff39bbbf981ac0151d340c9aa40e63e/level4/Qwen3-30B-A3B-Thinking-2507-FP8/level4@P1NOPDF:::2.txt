

Summary: This paper introduces a two-step approach for learning cross-lingual topic representations via matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis (LSA). The method employs projected gradient descent to solve the matrix completion problem. Experiments on parallel Amazon review data demonstrate that the proposed technique consistently outperforms both monolingual baselines and cross-lingual baselines—including a state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction method—on sentiment analysis. Learning curves across varying amounts of unlabeled data reveal that the algorithm achieves highly accurate cross-lingual representations even with limited parallel data.  

Quality: The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-executed, providing robust support for the claims. The proposed two-step algorithm is rigorously compared against two baselines (monolingual bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  

Clarity: The paper is well-structured and clearly written. All parameters and their selection criteria are explicitly detailed, ensuring reproducibility. A minor suggestion: the motivation for employing matrix completion could be expanded in the Introduction (it is currently described in Section 3).  

Originality: While cross-lingual representation learning is not a novel task, the application of matrix completion to this domain is original. The results clearly demonstrate superior performance over existing methods in the literature.  

Significance: The two-step approach delivers consistent improvements over all baselines, establishing that matrix completion effectively preprocesses cross-lingual sentiment classification. This formulation may also generalize to other cross-lingual tasks, offering broader applicability beyond sentiment analysis.
