- Introduces a matrix completion framework for cross-domain classification using labeled auxiliary-domain data plus unlabeled parallel data.  
- Two-stage method: (1) build unified incomplete document-term matrix and complete it via projected gradient descent with low-rank and sparsity constraints; (2) reduce dimensionality with LSI and train a classifier.  
- Provides convergence guarantees for the optimization and reports strong empirical performance.  
- Clarify why LSI is applied after completion: is it for computational efficiency given relative density of M*, or does LSI better address sparsity than alternatives; why LSI over PCA/other methods?  
- Explicitly distinguish and separately evaluate the two uses of external data: (a) labeled auxiliary-domain data for target classification, and (b) unlabeled parallel data.  
- Clarify experimental setup (training: 4k source, 100 target, 2k unlabeled; testing: 1.9k target) and explain why CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 (EFM/EGM at 2k) but not in Table 1.  
- Justify the low-rank assumption for a fully observed document-term matrix given feature sparsity and typical rank bounds (min(d, k)).  
- Provide intuition for why TSL/matrix completion outperforms CCA-based multiview methods and how enforcing low-rankness on M* specifically yields performance gains.  
- Report algorithm runtime and compare projected gradient descent speed against CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.  
- Minor edits: correct "(ij)" to "(i,j)" near Equation (2) and remove "such as" before Equations (4) and (5).  
- Overall: method is simple, effective, and clearly presented, but several technical clarifications and experiments are needed.
