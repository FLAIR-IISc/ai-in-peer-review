The paper proposes a matrix completion approach to the cross-domain classification task, capable of exploiting labeled data in an auxiliary domain and unlabeled parallel data. The approach involves two steps: first, constructing a single incomplete matrix encompassing all documents and domains, and then completing this matrix while enforcing low-rank and sparsity conditions using a projected gradient descent algorithm. Second, it reduces the feature dimension of this completed matrix using LSI, and trains a standard classifier on this new representation. The paper also presents a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm, and reports favorable experimental results.

Here are some comments and questions:

* After estimating the completed matrix M, you reduce the feature dimension from d to k with LSI because M “lacks sufficient capacity of handling feature sparseness.” Could you elaborate on this? Is it primarily for computational convenience, given that M* will be relatively dense even with sparsity enforcement? Also, is there a specific reason you chose LSI over other dimensionality reduction techniques (e.g., PCA)?

* There seem to be two distinct aspects to leveraging external data in this paper: 1) utilizing labeled source language data to aid learning in the labeled target language, and 2) leveraging the unlabeled parallel data. If this is correct, it would be helpful to clearly distinguish these two and present results separately.

* On a related note, the experimental settings are somewhat unclear (section 5.2). You used (a) 4000 labeled source language documents, (b) 100 labeled target language documents, and (c) 2000 unlabeled parallel documents for training? And the classification results are reported on the remaining 1900 labeled target language documents? Furthermore, in section 5.3, the only variation is in the number of unlabeled parallel documents, ranging from 200 to 2000? In that case, why does CL-KCCA exhibit better performance than TSL in Figure 1 for EFM and EGM at 2000 parallel documents, a discrepancy not observed in Table 1?

* Could you briefly clarify why a fully observed document-term matrix would be expected to be low-rank? While sparse, it's not necessarily true that each document is a linear combination of others in the vocabulary, which would imply a rank of min(d, k).

* Could you provide some intuition for why TSL generally outperforms other methods, especially CCA-based approaches? A multi-view approach like CCA feels like a natural fit for cross-lingual representation learning. In contrast, the matrix completion approach is less intuitive. It seems the main driver is enforcing the low-rank condition on M*. How does this compare with other techniques?

* What is the running time of the algorithm? Does the projected gradient descent algorithm take more, less, or about the same time compared to other methods like CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?

Suggested changes:

* Correct “(ij)” to “(i,j)” in the two sentences preceding equation (2).
* Remove "such as" from the sentences before equations (4) and (5).

Overall, the paper proposes a matrix completion approach to the cross-domain classification task. Although some technical points require clarification, the method is relatively simple, effective, and generally well-presented.
