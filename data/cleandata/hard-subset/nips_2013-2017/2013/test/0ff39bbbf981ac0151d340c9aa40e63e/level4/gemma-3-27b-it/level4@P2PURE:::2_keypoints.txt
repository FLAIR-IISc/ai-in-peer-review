- Proposes a two-step method (matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis) for learning cross-lingual topic representations.  
- Uses a projected gradient descent algorithm to solve the matrix completion optimization.  
- Evaluated on parallel Amazon review sentiment data, outperforming monolingual and existing cross-lingual baselines.  
- Compared against a bag-of-words baseline, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Learning curves show the method learns accurate cross-lingual topics even with limited parallel/unlabeled data.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and supports the paper’s claims.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible with clear parameter documentation.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: motivate the use of matrix completion earlier in the Introduction.  
- Novelty: applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution.  
- Significance: consistent gains indicate matrix completion is a useful preprocessing step and the approach may generalize to other tasks.
