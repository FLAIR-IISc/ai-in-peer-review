

Quality: While the proposed approach to translingual representation learning—using matrix completion to generate multilingual row vectors followed by CL-LSI—appears conceptually sound, the robustness of the reported results is questionable. The positive outcomes may merely stem from LSI's scaling properties being more favorable than OPCA or CCA for the linear SVM classifier employed. I recommend evaluating the method with a classifier insensitive to feature rescaling, such as boosted decision trees (e.g., via Weka). If the two-step system achieves superior performance with such a classifier, it would substantiate the claim of robustness.  
Significance: If the results hold under more rigorous evaluation, this would represent a notable contribution, as matrix completion provides a well-controlled non-linear inference mechanism for multilingual representation.  
Novelty: The core idea appears novel.  
Clarity: The paper contains several clarity issues. Line 113 states the method "constructs a unified document-term matrix for all documents," but it is unclear whether this implies using a single term per surface form (e.g., "Merkel" across languages) or labeling each language-specific surface form separately (e.g., "Merkelen" and "Merkelde"). This requires clarification. Additionally, the experimental setup description is fragmented across Table 1 and surrounding text, requiring careful parsing. Overall, the approach is interesting, but the results remain ambiguous without validation using classifiers less susceptible to feature rescaling artifacts.
