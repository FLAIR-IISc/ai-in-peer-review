- Paper proposes a translingual representation learning method combining matrix completion with CL-LSI.  
- Method is conceptually promising but reported results lack conclusive robustness.  
- Observed gains may stem from LSI’s favourable scaling for a linear SVM rather than genuine representation improvements over OPCA/CCA.  
- Recommend testing with classifiers less sensitive to feature scaling (e.g., boosted decision trees available in Weka).  
- Demonstrating superior performance with such classifiers would significantly strengthen the results.  
- Potential significance: using controlled non-linearity of matrix completion for translingual learning could be valuable.  
- Core idea appears novel.  
- Clarity issues: description of the document-term matrix (line 113) is ambiguous—unclear if “unified” means a single term for all surface forms or language-specific labels.  
- Presentation issue: experimental setup is fragmented across Table 1, making comprehension harder.  
- Overall: interesting approach but needs additional experiments with alternative classifiers to rule out scaling artifacts and confirm robustness.
