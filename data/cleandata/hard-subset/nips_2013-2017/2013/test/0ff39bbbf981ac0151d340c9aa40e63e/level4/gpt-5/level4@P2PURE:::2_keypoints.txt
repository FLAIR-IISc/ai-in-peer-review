- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual topic representation: matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis (LSA).  
- Optimizes the matrix completion objective using a projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Experiments on a parallel Amazon reviews sentiment dataset show the method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines.  
- Learning curves indicate the algorithm achieves high-quality cross-lingual topics with relatively little parallel data.  
- Experiments are thorough and compare against plain bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Paper is well organized and reproducible, with all parameters and selection procedures specified.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: expand the Introduction’s motivation for using matrix completion (currently discussed in Section 3).  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution.  
- The two-step formulation is significant: it consistently outperforms baselines, is stable across varying unlabeled parallel-data sizes, and may generalize to other tasks.
