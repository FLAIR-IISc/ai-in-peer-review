- Proposes a novel matrix completion method for cross-domain classification using labeled auxiliary data and unlabeled parallel data, followed by LSI and classifier training.  
- Uses projected gradient descent with low-rank and sparsity constraints and provides a convergence guarantee.  
- Experimental results are promising and the presentation is generally clear.  
- Primary concern: unclear motivation for using LSI for dimensionality reduction and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.  
- Question whether dimensionality reduction is mainly for computational efficiency since the completed matrix may remain dense.  
- The paper mixes two external-data uses (labeled source data vs. unlabeled parallel data); these should be delineated and evaluated separately.  
- Experimental setup is unclear about training data composition (4,000 source labeled, 100 target labeled, 2,000 unlabeled?) and evaluation on remaining 1,900 target documents.  
- Inconsistency between Figure 1 and Table 1 regarding CL-KCCA vs TSL performance at 2,000 unlabeled documents needs resolution.  
- Needs stronger justification for assuming a low-rank structure in document-term matrices, given sparsity and unlikely simple linear document combinations.  
- Requests intuition for why TSL outperforms CCA and whether the low-rank constraint is the primary factor compared to other methods.  
- Missing analysis of computational cost and running time compared to baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).  
- Minor typographical fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before eq. (2) and remove "such as" before eqs. (4) and (5).
