- Proposes a two-step method: matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis, optimized with projected gradient descent.  
- Evaluated on parallel Amazon reviews, the method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines in sentiment classification with modest parallel data.  
- Learning curves show the algorithm efficiently acquires accurate cross-lingual representations using limited parallel resources.  
- Experiments are rigorous and compare against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality-reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-structured and reproducible with parameters and selection criteria clearly documented; recommend moving the matrix-completion motivation to the Introduction.  
- Original contribution: novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning with superior empirical performance.  
- Significance: consistently outperforms baselines and remains robust across different sizes of unlabeled parallel data, suggesting broader applicability.
