Paper proposes matrix-completion for cross-domain classification: build incomplete document matrix, complete it with projected gradient descent enforcing low-rank/sparsity, reduce with LSI, then train classifier; convergence guarantee and good empirical results.

Clarify why LSI is used to reduce d→k after completion — is it mainly for computational convenience because M* becomes relatively dense despite sparsity enforcement, and why LSI rather than PCA or other methods?

Distinguish two separate uses of external data: (1) labeled source data to help target, and (2) unlabeled parallel data — report their effects separately.

Experimental setup is unclear: confirm training/test splits (e.g., 4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel, test on remaining 1900 target) and whether section 5.3 varies unlabeled docs from 200→2k.

Explain discrepancy between Figure 1 and Table 1 where CL-KCCA outperforms TSL at 2k for EFM/EGM in the figure but not in the table.

Question the low-rank assumption: why would a fully observed document-term matrix be low-rank given sparsity and likely full rank up to min(d,n)?

Provide intuition why TSL tends to outperform other methods and why multiview CCA is less effective than the proposed matrix-completion approach — is low-rank enforcement the main factor and how does it compare to alternatives?

Report running time: how does PGD-based completion compare in runtime to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?

Minor suggested fixes: change (ij) to (i,j) two sentences before Eq.(2); remove “such as” before Eqs.(4) and (5).

Overall: method is simple, effective, and generally clearly presented, but several technical points and experimental details need clarification.
