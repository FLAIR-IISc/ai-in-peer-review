- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method: projected gradient matrix completion to densify a bilingual word-context matrix followed by LSA for low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Effectively addresses data sparsity in cross-lingual settings by completing the sparse bilingual matrix before dimensionality reduction.  
- Shows strong empirical results on a parallel Amazon review sentiment dataset, outperforming monolingual and established cross-lingual baselines.  
- Demonstrates data efficiency: achieves high accuracy with relatively small amounts of parallel data.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and compares against a comprehensive set of baselines, supporting the paper’s claims.  
- Analysis of performance across varying amounts of unlabeled data indicates robustness and practical applicability.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides detailed parameter descriptions aiding reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: motivate the use of matrix completion earlier in the Introduction to frame it as a solution to cross-lingual sparsity.  
- Original contribution: novel application of matrix completion as a pre-processing step before latent semantic modeling.  
- Significant implications: method consistently outperforms strong baselines and suggests matrix completion as a promising pre-processing technique for other cross-lingual NLP tasks.
