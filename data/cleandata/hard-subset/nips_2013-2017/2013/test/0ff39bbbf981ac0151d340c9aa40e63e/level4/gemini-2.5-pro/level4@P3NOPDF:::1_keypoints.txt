- Novel approach: matrix completion for cross-domain classification combining labeled source, labeled target, and unlabeled parallel data with projected gradient descent, low-rank/sparsity constraints, LSI, and classifier; convergence guarantee and promising results.

- Strength: elegant, simple unified framework that effectively integrates multiple information sources; well-written paper.

- Major clarification needed: motivation for applying LSI after matrix completion is unclear — is it for handling sparsity, computational efficiency, or another reason; justify LSI over alternatives like PCA.

- Experiments lack ablation study: separate contributions of labeled source data vs. unlabeled parallel data are not disentangled.

- Experimental setup ambiguity: confirm training/evaluation splits (e.g., 4,000 labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2,000 unlabeled parallel, 1,900 labeled target test) for reproducibility.

- Reported inconsistency: Figure 1 (EFM/EGM tasks with 2k parallel docs) shows CL-KCCA outperforming TSL, but Table 1 reports TSL as superior — clarify discrepancy.

- Model assumption concern: low-rank assumption for the full document-term matrix is not well-justified; provide intuition or evidence why documents are well-approximated by a low-rank structure.

- Comparative intuition missing: explain why enforcing low-rank structure outperforms canonical methods like CCA; clarify the conceptual advantage of matrix completion vs. correlation-based projections.

- Computational cost omitted: include running-time analysis and compare projected gradient descent training time with baseline methods (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, etc.).

- Minor editorial fixes: change "(ij)" to "(i, j)" before Eq. (2); consider removing "such as" before Eqs. (4) and (5) for conciseness.

- Overall: promising and effective method but needs clearer motivation for design choices, experimental clarity, justification of assumptions, and runtime discussion.
