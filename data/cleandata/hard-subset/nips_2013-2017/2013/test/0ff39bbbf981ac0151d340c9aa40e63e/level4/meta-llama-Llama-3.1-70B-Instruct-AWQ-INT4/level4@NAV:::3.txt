This paper introduces a novel method for translingual representation learning, combining matrix completion and Cross-Lingual Latent Semantic Indexing (CL-LSI) to generate multilingual vectors. 

Quality: Although this approach appears promising, the reliability of the results is uncertain. The observed positive outcomes may be attributed to the scaling properties of LSI being more suitable for linear SVM than Orthogonal Procrustes Problem (OPCP) or Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA). 
To verify the robustness of the results, I suggest employing a machine learning system that is invariant to individual feature rescalings, such as boosted decision trees (e.g., Weka). If the two-step system outperforms other techniques when paired with boosted decision trees, it would strengthen the findings. 
Significance: If the results are indeed robust, they would be noteworthy, as matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique. 
Novelty: The proposed idea seems to be novel. 
Clarity: Some aspects of the paper require clarification. For instance, line 113 mentions constructing a unified document-term matrix for all documents, but it is unclear whether this involves using a single term for each surface form (e.g., "Merkel") or labeling each surface form from each language (e.g., "Merkelen" and "Merkelde"). Additionally, the experimental setup description is fragmented across Table 1, making it somewhat difficult to understand. 
The paper presents an interesting idea, but I would like the authors to explore other classifiers beyond linear SVM to confirm the robustness of the results and rule out potential scaling artifacts.
