This paper presents a matrix completion approach for cross-domain classification, designed to leverage both labeled data from an auxiliary domain and unlabeled parallel data. The approach consists of two steps. First, a single, incomplete matrix is constructed to encompass all documents and domains. This matrix is then completed using a projected gradient descent algorithm that enforces both low-rank and sparsity constraints. Second, Latent Semantic Indexing (LSI) is used to reduce the dimensionality of the completed matrix, and a standard classifier is trained on the resulting low-dimensional representation. The authors also provide a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and present favorable experimental results.

**Comments and Questions:**

*   The paper states that LSI is used to reduce the dimensionality of the completed matrix M because it "lacks sufficient capacity of handling feature sparseness." Could you elaborate on this justification? Is the primary motivation for dimensionality reduction computational, given that the completed matrix will be dense despite the sparsity constraint? Furthermore, was there a specific reason for choosing LSI over other techniques like PCA?

*   The method appears to leverage two distinct types of external data: 1) labeled source-domain data to aid classification in the target domain, and 2) unlabeled parallel data. If this understanding is correct, the paper would be strengthened by explicitly distinguishing these two contributions and reporting their effects separately in the experiments.

*   On a related note, the experimental setup in Section 5.2 is somewhat unclear. To clarify: was the training data composed of (a) 4k labeled source documents, (b) 100 labeled target documents, and (c) 2k unlabeled parallel documents, with testing performed on the remaining 1900 labeled target documents? If so, and if Section 5.3 only varies the amount of unlabeled parallel data, could you explain the apparent discrepancy in the results? Specifically, in Figure 1, CL-KCCA outperforms TSL for EFM and EGM with 2k parallel documents, whereas TSL outperforms CL-KCCA under what appear to be the same conditions in Table 1.

*   Could you provide more intuition for the low-rank assumption on the document-term matrix? While such a matrix is sparse, it seems unlikely to be low-rank in a fully observed scenario. One would expect documents to be linearly independent, resulting in a full-rank matrix (rank = min(num_docs, num_features)).

*   Could you offer some intuition as to why the proposed method (TSL) generally outperforms alternatives, particularly multi-view approaches like CCA which seem very natural for this task? The driving force behind your method appears to be the low-rank constraint. How does enforcing this constraint compare conceptually to the objective functions of competing methods?

*   What is the computational complexity and empirical running time of the proposed algorithm? How does the convergence time of the projected gradient descent compare to the training times of the baseline methods (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA)?

**Conclusion and Suggested Changes:**

In summary, this paper presents a simple and effective matrix completion approach for cross-domain classification. While the method is generally well-presented, several technical points require clarification as detailed above.

Minor suggestions:
*   In the text preceding equation (2), "(ij)" should be "(i, j)".
*   The phrase "such as" could be removed from the sentences before equations (4) and (5) for conciseness.
