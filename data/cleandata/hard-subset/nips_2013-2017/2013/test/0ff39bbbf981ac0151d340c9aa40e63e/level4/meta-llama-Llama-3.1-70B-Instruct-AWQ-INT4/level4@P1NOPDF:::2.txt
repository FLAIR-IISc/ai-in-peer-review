Summary: This paper proposes a two-step method for learning a cross-lingual topic representation, leveraging matrix completion and latent semantic analysis techniques. The approach utilizes a projected gradient descent algorithm to optimize the matrix completion problem. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms both monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on a sentiment analysis task using the parallel Amazon review dataset. Moreover, learning curves with varying sizes of unlabeled data illustrate that the proposed algorithm can learn highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations with a relatively small number of parallel data points.

Quality: The experiments conducted in this study are thorough and well-designed, providing strong support for the claims made. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is carefully compared to two baselines (plain bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms, demonstrating a comprehensive evaluation of the approach.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear description of all parameters, including their selection process, making it easy to reproduce the results. One minor suggestion is to further elaborate on the motivation behind using matrix completion in the Introduction, as it is currently only described in Section 3.

Originality: Although the task of cross-lingual representation learning is not novel, the application of matrix completion to this task is innovative. The proposed algorithm achieves better performance than previous methods in the literature, demonstrating its value.

Significance: The results of the two-step approach consistently surpass those of other baselines. This work highlights the potential of matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks and suggests that this formulation may be effective in other tasks as well. The proposed two-step method for learning a cross-lingual topic representation, which combines matrix completion with latent semantic analysis, demonstrates promising results on the cross-lingual sentiment classification task. Experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including one state-of-the-art method, and remains stable even when trained on different sizes of unlabeled parallel data.
