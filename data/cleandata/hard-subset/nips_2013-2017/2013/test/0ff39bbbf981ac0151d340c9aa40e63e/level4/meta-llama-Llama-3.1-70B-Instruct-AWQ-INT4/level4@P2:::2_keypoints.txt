- Proposes a novel two-step method: matrix completion on concatenated document-term matrix followed by LSI to learn cross-lingual document representations for classification.
- Uses monolingual classifiers on the learned low-dimensional cross-lingual representations for cross-language text classification.
- Requires only a small set of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents, unlike prior methods that translate all training data.
- Thorough experimental comparison against monolingual and state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction baselines.
- Empirical results show the method outperforms all baselines, especially when parallel bilingual data is scarce.
- Novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning with consistently higher performance.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and experiments are well-presented.
- Minor critique: motivation for using matrix completion should be explained earlier in the introduction.
- Suggestion: evaluate the method on other cross-lingual tasks (e.g., machine translation, question answering).
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: placement/clarity of motivation and limited task evaluation scope.
