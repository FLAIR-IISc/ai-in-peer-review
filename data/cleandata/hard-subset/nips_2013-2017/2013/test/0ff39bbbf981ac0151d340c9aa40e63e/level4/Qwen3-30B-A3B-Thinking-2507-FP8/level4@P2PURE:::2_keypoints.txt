- Introduces a two-step method (matrix completion optimized by projected gradient descent followed by LSA) for learning cross-lingual topic representations.  
- Demonstrates superior performance over monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction method, on the Amazon review sentiment dataset.  
- Learning curves show the method achieves high accuracy with relatively small amounts of parallel/unlabeled data and remains robust across data sizes.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-executed, with rigorous comparisons against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two SOTA dimensionality reduction algorithms.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written; all parameters and selection criteria are explicitly detailed for reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: expand the motivation for using matrix completion in the Introduction (currently in Section 3).  
- Novelty: applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution with clear empirical gains.  
- Significance: establishes matrix completion as a viable preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and potentially other cross-lingual tasks.
