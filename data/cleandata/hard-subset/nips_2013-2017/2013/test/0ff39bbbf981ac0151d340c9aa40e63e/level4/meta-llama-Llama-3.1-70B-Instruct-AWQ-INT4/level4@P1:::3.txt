The paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which addresses the challenge of cross-language text classification by bridging the disjoint feature spaces of different languages. The authors exploit a set of parallel bilingual documents to induce a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by first formulating a matrix completion problem and then applying latent semantic indexing.

The paper relates to previous work on cross-language text classification, including methods that transform training or test data from one language domain into the other language domain using machine translation tools or bilingual lexicons, and methods that construct cross-lingual representations using auxiliary resources such as bilingual word pairs, comparable corpora, and multilingual resources. The proposed approach shares similarities with these methods but requires only a small set of unlabeled parallel documents.

The authors evaluate the proposed method by conducting extensive cross-language sentiment classification experiments on multilingual Amazon product reviews. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method significantly outperforms several other cross-language representation learning methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

Quality: The paper presents a technically sound approach to cross-language representation learning. The authors provide a clear and well-structured description of the proposed method, including the matrix completion problem and the latent semantic indexing step. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are promising. However, it would be beneficial to investigate the robustness of the results by using different classification models, such as boosted decision trees, which are insensitive to rescalings of individual features.

Significance: The proposed method has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of natural language processing, particularly in cross-language text classification tasks. The use of matrix completion and latent semantic indexing provides a novel approach to inducing cross-lingual document representations.

Novelty: The idea of using matrix completion and latent semantic indexing for cross-language representation learning is new and original. The authors provide a clear explanation of how their approach differs from previous methods.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written, but there are some minor issues with clarity. For example, the description of the experimental setup is split across Table 1, which can make it difficult to follow. Additionally, some sentences are wordy and could be rephrased for better clarity.

Overall, the paper presents a promising approach to cross-language representation learning. With some minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and original approach to cross-language representation learning.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are promising.
* The proposed method has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of natural language processing.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the robustness of the results.
* The use of linear SVM as the classification model may not be the most robust choice.
* Some minor issues with clarity need to be addressed.
