- Proposes a matrix completion method for cross-language classification that leverages labeled source-language data and unlabeled parallel data.
- Method: build a single incomplete document-language matrix, complete it enforcing low-rank and sparsity via projected gradient descent, then apply LSI and train a classifier.
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and reports favorable experimental results.
- Question: Why reduce M to k dimensions with LSI—due to capacity/sparsity issues or computational convenience—and why choose LSI over alternatives like PCA?
- Request to clearly separate and report results for the two types of external data usage: (1) labeled source-language transfer and (2) unlabeled parallel data.
- Experiment settings unclear: confirm training used 4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel, and testing on remaining 1900 labeled target documents.
- Clarify experimental variation in Section 5.3 (varying unlabeled parallel documents 200→2k) and reconcile why CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 but not Table 1.
- Theoretical question: why is a fully observed document-term matrix assumed low-rank despite document sparsity and likely full column/row rank?
- Request intuition why TSL generally outperforms other methods (especially CCA) and why the matrix completion approach, driven by low-rank enforcement, is preferable.
- Ask for running-time comparison: how does projected gradient descent runtime compare to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?
- Minor editorial fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” two sentences before eq.(2).
- Minor editorial fixes: remove “such as” before eq.(4) and eq.(5).
- Strength: method is simple, effective, and clearly presented.
- Strength: includes convergence guarantee for the optimization algorithm.
- Weakness: several technical points and assumptions require clarification.
- Weakness: experiment descriptions and settings are ambiguous and need to be made explicit.
- Weakness: inconsistent reporting/interpretation of CL-KCCA vs TSL performance between figure and table.
- Overall: well-written, novel, and promising; with minor clarifications and fixes it could be a strong contribution.
