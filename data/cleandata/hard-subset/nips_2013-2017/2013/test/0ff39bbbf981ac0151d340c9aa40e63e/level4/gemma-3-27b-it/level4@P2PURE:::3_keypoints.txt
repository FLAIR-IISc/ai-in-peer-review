- Paper introduces a translingual representation learning method using matrix completion to generate multilingual row vectors, followed by CL-LSI.
- Proposed approach appears promising but result robustness is questionable.
- Reported improvements may stem from LSI’s scaling properties favoring linear SVM over OPCA/CCA rather than true modeling gains.
- Reviewer recommends testing with a classifier less sensitive to feature scaling (e.g., boosted decision trees, available in Weka).
- If the two-step system still outperforms with boosted trees, the results would be more convincing.
- Significance: a robust, validated result would be valuable because matrix completion offers a well-defined nonlinear inference mechanism.
- Novelty: the idea appears novel.
- Clarity issues: line 113’s “unified document-term matrix for all documents” is ambiguous—unclear whether surface forms are merged or kept language-specific.
- Experimental setup presentation is fragmented across Table 1, hindering understanding.
- Overall: interesting idea; authors should explore classifiers beyond linear SVM to rule out scaling artifacts.
