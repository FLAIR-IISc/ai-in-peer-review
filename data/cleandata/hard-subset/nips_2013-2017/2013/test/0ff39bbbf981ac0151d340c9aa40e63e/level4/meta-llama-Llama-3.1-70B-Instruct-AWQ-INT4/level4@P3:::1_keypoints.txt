- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation learning method using parallel bilingual documents.  
- Step 1: build a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix and recover missing entries via matrix completion solved with projected gradient descent.  
- Step 2: apply latent semantic indexing (LSI) on the recovered matrix to induce low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Method is related to prior cross-language representation approaches but differs by performing matrix completion before LSI.  
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Experimental evaluation on multilingual Amazon sentiment classification shows favorable results.  
- Outperforms baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA), especially when parallel bilingual documents are scarce.  
- Paper is well written and the method is clearly presented.  
- Needs clarification why the completed matrix M lacks capacity to handle feature sparsity.  
- Needs justification for choosing LSI over other dimensionality-reduction techniques.  
- Should more clearly distinguish two uses of external data: leveraging labeled source data vs. leveraging unlabeled parallel data.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” and remove “such as” before equations (4) and (5).  
- Strengths: novel, effective method with strong experimental support.  
- Weaknesses: technical clarifications and clearer framing of external-data usage required.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10; recommend accept with minor revisions.
