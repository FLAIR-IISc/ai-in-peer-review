- Paper proposes a novel matrix completion method for cross-domain classification using labeled auxiliary data and unlabeled parallel data.  
- Method pipeline: build one incomplete matrix across domains, complete it enforcing low-rank and sparsity via projected gradient descent, then apply LSI and train a classifier.  
- Authors provide a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Experimental results are reported as favorable overall.  
- Clarify the claim that the completed matrix M “lacks sufficient capacity” and why LSI (not PCA or other methods) was chosen—whether dimensionality reduction is for capacity or computational reasons.  
- Distinguish clearly between leveraging labeled source data and leveraging unlabeled parallel data as two separate aspects, and report results separately.  
- Experimental setup needs clarification (confirm counts: 4,000 labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2,000 unlabeled parallel; test on remaining 1,900 target) and explain varying unlabeled sizes in Section 5.3.  
- Explain why CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 for EFM/EGM at 2,000 unlabeled but not in Table 1.  
- Justify the claim that a fully observed document-term matrix is low-rank, given sparsity and unlikely linear dependence between documents.  
- Provide insight why TSL generally outperforms CCA and whether enforcing low-rank on M* is the main driver compared to other methods.  
- Report running time and compare projected gradient descent complexity to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.  
- Minor edits: replace “(ij)” with “(i,j)” near equation (2) and remove “such as” before equations (4) and (5).  
- Overall: method is simple, effective, and well-presented, but several technical clarifications are needed.
