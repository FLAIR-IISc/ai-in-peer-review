- Proposes a two-step method: infer missing cross-lingual term features via matrix completion, then apply LSI for shared dense document representations.  
- Formulates matrix completion as a convex optimization solved by projected gradient descent with convergence guarantees.  
- Evaluated on Amazon product reviews across 18 cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks.  
- Outperforms monolingual baseline (TBOW) and cross-lingual methods (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA), especially with few parallel documents.  
- Demonstrates robustness to varying amounts of parallel data and effective performance with limited parallel resources.  
- Technically sound with extensive experiments and rigorous comparisons supporting the claims.  
- Well-written, logically organized, and provides parameter selection details aiding reproducibility.  
- Novel contribution: using matrix completion as a preprocessing step before dimensionality reduction in cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Minor suggestion: introduction should briefly foreshadow the matrix completion idea to better frame the contribution.
