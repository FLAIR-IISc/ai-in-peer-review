- Paper proposes a matrix-completion method for cross-domain classification using labeled source data and unlabeled parallel data, completed via projected gradient descent with low-rank and sparsity constraints, then reduced with LSI and classified.

- The method includes a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient algorithm and reports favorable experimental results.

- Question: Why reduce dimensionality of completed matrix M with LSI — is this due to lack of capacity for sparse features or mainly computational convenience, and why LSI instead of PCA or other methods?

- Suggestion: Clarify the two distinct uses of external data—(1) labeled source data and (2) unlabeled parallel data—and present results isolating each effect.

- Experimental clarity needed: confirm training/test splits (4000 labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2000 unlabeled parallel for training; 1900 target test) and explain varying only the number of unlabeled parallel docs in section 5.3.

- Point out inconsistency: explain why CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 for EFM/EGM at 2000 parallel docs while Table 1 does not show this discrepancy.

- Theoretical question: justify why a fully observed document-term matrix should be low-rank, since documents being linear combinations of others is not obviously valid.

- Request intuition: why does TSL generally outperform CCA-based methods and how exactly does enforcing low-rank on M* compare to other cross-lingual techniques?

- Practical question: report running time of the projected gradient descent algorithm and compare computational cost to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.

- Minor editorial suggestions: correct “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before equation (2) and remove "such as" before equations (4) and (5).

- Overall assessment: method is simple, effective, and well-presented, but several technical points and experimental clarifications are required.
