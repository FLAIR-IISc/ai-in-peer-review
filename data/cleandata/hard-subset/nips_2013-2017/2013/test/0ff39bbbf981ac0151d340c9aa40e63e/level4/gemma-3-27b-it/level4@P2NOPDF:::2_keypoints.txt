- Proposes a two-step method for cross-lingual topic learning: matrix completion followed by LSA.  
- Solves the matrix completion problem using a projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Evaluated on a parallel Amazon review dataset, outperforming monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on sentiment classification.  
- Learning curves show the method learns accurate cross-lingual topics even with limited parallel data.  
- Experimental comparisons are comprehensive, including bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality-reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with all relevant parameters and selection criteria detailed for reproducibility.  
- Minor suggestion: motivate the use of matrix completion earlier in the introduction to improve flow.  
- Novelty: applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning constitutes a new contribution.  
- Significance: method offers a robust preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and may generalize to related tasks.
