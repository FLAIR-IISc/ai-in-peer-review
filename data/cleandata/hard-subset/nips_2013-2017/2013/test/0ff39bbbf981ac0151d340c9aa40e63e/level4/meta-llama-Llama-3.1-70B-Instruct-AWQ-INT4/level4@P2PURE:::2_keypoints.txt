- Proposes a two-step method for cross-lingual topic representation using matrix completion followed by LSA.  
- Uses projected gradient descent to optimize the matrix completion problem.  
- Experimental results show the method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines on Amazon sentiment classification.  
- Learning curves indicate the algorithm achieves high accuracy with relatively small amounts of parallel unlabeled data.  
- Experiments are thorough and well-designed, comparing against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-organized with detailed parameter descriptions and reproducibility; suggests expanding the Introduction motivation for matrix completion.  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution despite the task itself being common.  
- The two-step approach consistently outperforms baselines and could serve as a useful preprocessing formulation for other tasks.
