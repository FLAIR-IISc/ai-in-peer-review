- Paper proposes a two-stage matrix completion method for cross-domain classification: complete a unified document matrix under low-rank and sparsity constraints, then apply LSI and train a classifier.
- Authors provide a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent optimizer and report strong empirical performance.
- Reviewer asks for clarification on why LSI is used for dimensionality reduction (capacity/sparsity reasons) and why LSI was chosen instead of alternatives like PCA.
- Suggests explicitly distinguishing the two ways external data are used (labeled source data vs. unlabeled parallel data) and showing their effects via separate ablation studies.
- Requests clarification of the experimental setup (counts of labeled source, labeled target, unlabeled parallel, and test data) and whether Section 5.3 varies only the number of unlabeled parallel documents.
- Notes an apparent discrepancy between Figure 1 and Table 1 where CL-KCCA sometimes outperforms TSL and asks for explanation.
- Asks for justification of the low-rank assumption on a (sparse) fully observed document-term matrix, since documents may not be linear combinations of others.
- Requests intuition for why TSL outperforms CCA-based multiview methods and how the low-rank constraint conceptually compares to competing objectives.
- Asks for comments on computational complexity and empirical running time, specifically convergence time of the projected gradient method versus baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).
- Minor editorial suggestions: fix "(ij)" to "(i,j)" before Eq. (2) and consider removing "such as" before Eqs. (4) and (5).
- Overall assessment: novel, simple, effective, and clearly presented method, but several technical clarifications are needed.
