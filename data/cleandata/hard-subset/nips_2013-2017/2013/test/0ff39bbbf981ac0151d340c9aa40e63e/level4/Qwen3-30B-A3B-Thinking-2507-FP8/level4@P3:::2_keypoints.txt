- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion to recover missing features, followed by LSI for low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Constructs a unified document-term matrix from parallel bilingual documents, leaving missing entries for non-parallel parts.  
- Solves a low-rank matrix completion problem via projected gradient descent and provides convergence guarantees (Lemmas 1–3).  
- Evaluated on Amazon reviews (English, French, German, Japanese) for sentiment classification.  
- Demonstrates consistent superiority over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA across 18 cross-lingual tasks.  
- Achieves significant gains even with minimal parallel data (e.g., outperforms competitors by up to 14.61% using only 200 parallel documents).  
- Experiments are rigorous: 18 tasks, 10-fold randomization, and systematic parameter selection (γ, ρ, k).  
- Minor weakness: lacks ablation study isolating the contribution of matrix completion versus applying LSI to the incomplete matrix.  
- Minor concern: scalability of the optimization algorithm to much larger datasets is not addressed.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible; Algorithm 1 and parameter choices are clearly specified.  
- Suggests improving Introduction by explicitly contrasting the approach with CL-LSI and justifying ℓ1 regularization for sparsity.  
- Novelty lies in framing missing bilingual document-term entries as a principled matrix completion task, not simply treating them as zeros.  
- Significance: practical for low-resource cross-lingual applications and adaptable to other representation tasks.  
- Recommendation: accept—technically sound, experimentally validated, and practically impactful.
