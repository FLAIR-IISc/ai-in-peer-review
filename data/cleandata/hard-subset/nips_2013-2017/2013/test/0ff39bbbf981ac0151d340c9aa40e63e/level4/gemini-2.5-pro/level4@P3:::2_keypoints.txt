- Proposes a novel two-step method for cross-lingual document representation: matrix completion on a unified doc-term matrix followed by LSI.  
- Treats monolingual documents as having missing features for the other language and formulates recovery as a low-rank matrix completion problem.  
- Optimization uses projected gradient descent with a provided convergence proof.  
- Encourages low-rank and sparse solutions via trace-norm and L1 regularization and handles observed/unobserved entries in the loss.  
- Applies LSI to the completed matrix to obtain a dense shared semantic space.  
- Evaluated on 18 cross-language sentiment classification tasks using Amazon reviews.  
- Significantly outperforms monolingual baseline and strong cross-lingual baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).  
- Particularly strong performance when only a small amount of parallel data is available.  
- Technical approach is principled and the paper is of high technical quality.  
- Comprehensive, convincing experimental evaluation with thorough baseline comparisons.  
- Analysis of performance across varying amounts of parallel data highlights robustness and practical utility.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and describes experimental setup and reproducibility details.  
- Minor presentation suggestion: emphasize the core insight (missing vs zero-filled features) earlier in the introduction.  
- Novelty stems from combining matrix completion with LSI and reframing cross-lingual learning as matrix completion.  
- Results are significant and likely impactful for NLP/ML, especially for low-resource scenarios.  
- No major concerns raised; only minor suggestion on motivating the framing more strongly.
