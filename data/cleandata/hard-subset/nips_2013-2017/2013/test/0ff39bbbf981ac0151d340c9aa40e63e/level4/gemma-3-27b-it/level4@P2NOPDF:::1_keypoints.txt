- Proposes a two-stage matrix completion method for cross-domain classification (complete a unified document matrix with low-rank+sparsity via projected gradient descent, then apply LSI) and provides a convergence guarantee with promising results.  
- Unclear rationale for using LSI to reduce dimensionality—need to state whether this is for computational efficiency due to an expected dense completed matrix and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.  
- Paper seems to use external data in two ways (labeled source data and unlabeled parallel data); these contributions should be clearly delineated and evaluated separately.  
- Clarify the experimental setup (confirm training: 4,000 labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2,000 unlabeled parallel; test on remaining 1,900 target) and explain the discrepancy between Figure 1 and Table 1 regarding CL-KCCA vs TSL at 2,000 unlabeled documents.  
- The low-rank assumption on the (fully observed) document-term matrix needs stronger justification—why should documents be linear combinations leading to low rank?  
- Explain why TSL generally outperforms other methods, especially CCA, and discuss how the low-rank constraint compares to other regularization or dimensionality-reduction approaches in cross-lingual learning.  
- Report computational cost: compare running time of the projected gradient descent algorithm to baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).  
- Minor edits: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before Equation (2) and remove “such as” before Equations (4) and (5).  
- Overall assessment: paper is well-written, simple and effective with promising results; addressing the above clarifications would strengthen the contribution.
