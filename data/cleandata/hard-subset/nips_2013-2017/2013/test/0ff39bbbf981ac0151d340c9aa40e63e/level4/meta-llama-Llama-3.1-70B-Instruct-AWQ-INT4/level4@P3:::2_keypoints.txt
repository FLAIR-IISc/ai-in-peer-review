- Proposes a novel two-step method combining matrix completion and latent semantic analysis for cross-language representation learning.
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification using a parallel Amazon review dataset.
- Method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction algorithms.
- Paper is well-organized with a clear explanation of the method, optimization algorithm, and experimental setup.
- Experimental parameters are clearly stated, enabling easy reproducibility of results.
- Minor suggestion: provide more motivation for using matrix completion in the introduction (currently introduced mainly in Section 3).
- Strength: novel application of matrix completion to cross-language representation learning.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation with multiple baseline comparisons.
- Strength: clear presentation of the optimization algorithm.
- Weakness: lacks analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.
- Weakness: no exploration of robustness to different types of noise or outliers in the data.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
