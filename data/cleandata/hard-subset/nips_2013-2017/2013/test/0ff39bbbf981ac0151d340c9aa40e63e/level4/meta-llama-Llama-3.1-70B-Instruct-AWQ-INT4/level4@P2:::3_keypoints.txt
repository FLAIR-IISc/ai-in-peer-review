- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to build a complete parallel document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on multilingual Amazon sentiment classification, outperforming other methods, especially with few parallel bilingual documents.  
- Method is technically sound with a clear, detailed description of the approach.  
- Calls for more robust evaluation, including sensitivity analyses to different classification models and feature scaling.  
- Criticizes use of linear SVM as potentially fragile due to sensitivity to feature scaling; recommends models insensitive to feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees).  
- Highlights significance: potential to improve cross-language text classification and use of matrix completion as a controlled non-linear inference technique.  
- Rates novelty as high for combining matrix completion with LSI in cross-language representation learning.  
- Notes clarity issues: experimental setup description is fragmented (split across Table 1).  
- Requests clarification of technical terms, e.g., “unified document-term matrix.”  
- Overall judgment: promising and potentially significant but requires revisions to address evaluation and clarity concerns.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong experimental results, and robust matrix-completion-based inference.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient robustness checks, questionable choice of classifier, and unclear terminology/experimental description.
