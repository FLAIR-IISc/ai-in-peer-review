- Proposes matrix completion for cross-domain classification using labeled source data and unlabeled parallel data.
- Two-step method: complete an incomplete document-domain matrix under low-rank and sparsity constraints via projected gradient descent, then apply LSI and train a classifier on reduced features.
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and reports favorable empirical results.
- Clarify why dimensionality reduction (LSI) is needed after completion—explain capacity/sparsity trade-offs, density of M* despite sparsity enforcement, and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.
- Separate and report results for the two distinct uses of external data: (1) labeled source-domain transfer and (2) unlabeled parallel data.
- Experimental setup needs clarification (training: 4,000 source, 100 target, 2,000 unlabeled; test: 1,900 target) and reconciliation of why CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 (at 2,000 unlabeled) but not in Table 1.
- Challenge the low-rank assumption for a fully observed document-term matrix—sparsity does not imply low rank and documents may not be linear combinations of others.
- Explain why TSL consistently outperforms CCA and other multiview methods—does low-rank enforcement on M* provide the key advantage?
- Report algorithm runtime and compare projected gradient descent speed against baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).
- Minor corrections: change "(ij)" to "(i,j)" before Eq. (2) and remove "such as" before Eqs. (4) and (5).
- Overall assessment: method is simple, effective, and clearly presented, but several technical and experimental clarifications are required.
