- Proposes a two-step method that combines matrix completion with LSA to learn cross-lingual topic representations.  
- Uses a projected gradient descent algorithm to solve the matrix completion optimization.  
- Shows the method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art method, on a parallel Amazon review sentiment dataset.  
- Learning curves indicate the algorithm achieves high accuracy with relatively small amounts of parallel/unlabeled data.  
- Experiments are thorough and well-designed, comparing against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality-reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible, with clear description and selection of parameters.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: motivate the use of matrix completion earlier (in the introduction) rather than primarily in Section 3.  
- Novelty lies in applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Significance: method is robust across different unlabeled data sizes and could serve as an effective preprocessing approach for other cross-lingual tasks.
