- Proposes a two-step translingual representation method: matrix completion to generate multilingual vectors, then Cross-Lingual LSI (CL-LSI).
- Core idea is promising; matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference and could be significant if results are robust.
- Major concern about experimental robustness—reported gains may be artifacts of the evaluation, not better representations.
- Specific issue: linear SVM is sensitive to feature scaling; LSI may produce scaling that favors SVM over OPCA/CCA.
- Recommendation: validate with a classifier insensitive to monotonic rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees) to confirm representation quality.
- Novelty: the combination of matrix completion and CL-LSI appears to be novel.
- Clarity issue: ambiguous description of the “unified document-term matrix” — unclear whether terms are canonical across languages or language-specific surface forms.
- Clarity issue: experimental setup description is fragmented, with key details dispersed in text and Table 1.
- Conclusion: central claim is not convincingly supported; re-evaluation with a scaling-insensitive classifier is needed to demonstrate robustness.
