Summary: This paper presents a two-step method for learning a cross-lingual topic representation using matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis. The matrix completion problem is optimized with a projected gradient descent algorithm. On a cross-lingual sentiment analysis task using a parallel Amazon reviews dataset, the proposed method outperforms monolingual and cross-lingual baselines. Learning curves across different amounts of unlabeled data indicate that the approach attains high accuracy with relatively small quantities of parallel data.

Quality: The experiments are thorough and support the paper’s claims. The proposed two-step algorithm is carefully compared against two baselines (plain bag-of-words and cross-lingual LSA) as well as two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms.

Clarity: The paper is well organized. All parameters, including selection procedures, are clearly specified, making the results easy to reproduce. Minor suggestion: better motivate the use of matrix completion in the Introduction (although the rationale is described in Section 3).

Originality: While cross-lingual representation learning is not new, the application of matrix completion to this task is novel, and the proposed algorithm achieves better performance than prior methods reported in the literature.

Significance: The two-step approach consistently outperforms the baselines and remains stable across different sizes of unlabeled parallel data. This work demonstrates that matrix completion can serve as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and suggests that the formulation may generalize to other tasks.
