The paper introduces a translingual representation learning approach that first applies matrix completion to derive multilingual vectors for each row, followed by CL-LSI on these multilingual vectors.

Quality: Although the idea is appealing, I am not convinced the results are robust. The observed gains could simply arise because LSI’s scaling is better suited to a linear SVM than OPCA or CCA. I recommend evaluating with a learning algorithm that is insensitive to per-feature rescaling, such as boosted decision trees (available in Weka). If boosted decision trees on top of the two-step pipeline outperform competing techniques, I would be persuaded by the result.

Significance: If the effect is truly robust, it would be interesting, as matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference method.

Novelty: The idea appears novel.

Clarity: There were some clarity issues. Line 113 mentions “construct[ing] a unified document-term matrix for all documents.” It is unclear whether this entails using a single term for each surface form (e.g., “Merkel”), or labeling each language-specific surface variant separately (e.g., “Merkelen” and “Merkelde”). Please clarify. Additionally, the description of the experimental setup is split around Table 1, which made it harder to follow.

Overall, this seems like a neat idea. I wish the authors had tried classifiers other than a linear SVM—I still cannot tell whether the result is robust or a scaling artifact.
