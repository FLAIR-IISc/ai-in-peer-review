- Paper proposes cross-language text classification via matrix completion on a unified bilingual document–term matrix, followed by LSI and SVM classification.
- Missing cross-language features for non-parallel docs are imputed by a convex optimization combining trace-norm, ℓ1 penalty, nonnegativity, and weighted squared loss.
- Optimization solved by projected gradient descent with singular value shrinkage and nonnegativity projection; convergence is proven.
- Empirical evaluation on multilingual Amazon reviews shows gains over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA, especially with few parallel documents.
- Method differs from prior CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA by performing matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.
- Optimization builds on known trace-norm minimization techniques and adds convergence guarantees.
- Strength: clear, modular two-step pipeline (imputation then compression).
- Strength: simple, scalable algorithm with a convergence guarantee.
- Strength: strong empirical performance across 18 tasks and robustness in low-parallel-data regimes.
- Strength: requires only a relatively small set of unlabeled parallel documents, not large-scale translation.
- Question: unclear motivation for applying LSI after completion—need clarification whether for dimensionality reduction, robustness, or computational efficiency.
- Question: why choose LSI specifically over PCA or other reductions, and does the choice affect empirical results?
- Question: need ablation experiments to disentangle gains from labeled source data versus unlabeled parallel data.
- Question: clarify experimental splits and reconcile apparent discrepancies between Figure 1 and Table 1 results.
- Question: low-rank assumption needs justification or empirical evidence (e.g., singular value decay) for document–term matrices.
- Question: explain advantages of TSL over CCA-based multiview methods like CL-KCCA and provide high-level comparison.
- Question: report runtime, memory usage, scalability with vocab size/documents/parallels, and stopping criteria for projected gradient.
- Question: discuss hyperparameter sensitivity (γ, ρ, τ, k), how τ is chosen, and typical iteration counts.
- Quality: optimization formulation and convergence argument are sound; more experiments would strengthen claims.
- Clarity: generally well written but experimental protocol and LSI rationale need clarification.
- Originality: novel use of matrix completion prior to LSI compared to direct CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA approaches.
- Significance: practical and strong results, especially valuable when parallel data are scarce.
- Pros: simple effective pipeline, convergence guarantee, consistent gains, minimal translation reliance.
- Cons: unclear low-rank/LSI justification and incomplete experimental ablation and runtime analysis.
- Minor suggestions: fix typographical and notation issues, define symbols at first use, and justify nonnegativity and ℓ1-to-linear equivalence.
