- Proposes a novel translingual representation method combining matrix completion and CL-LSI to create multilingual vectors.
- Results look promising but their robustness is uncertain and may be due to LSI’s scaling properties.
- Improvements might reflect suitability of LSI for linear SVM rather than genuine method superiority over OPCA/CCA.
- Recommend testing with classifiers invariant to feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka) to rule out scaling artifacts.
- If robust, the approach is significant because matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique with substantial impact potential.
- The method appears novel and offers a fresh perspective on translingual representation learning.
- Clarity issues: unified document-term matrix construction (line 113) is unclear—whether surface forms are merged or language-labeled is unspecified.
- Experimental setup description is fragmented (spread across Table 1) and should be reorganized for readability.
- Overall: intriguing idea; authors should validate robustness with different classifiers (e.g., boosted trees) and improve clarity.
