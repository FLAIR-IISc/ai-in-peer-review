This paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross language representation learning by exploiting a set of parallel bilingual documents. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then induces a low dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms a number of other cross language representation learning methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain the motivation and contribution of the proposed method. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on cross language sentiment classification tasks.

The quality of the paper is high, with thorough experiments and convincing results. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is carefully compared with several baselines, including a monolingual baseline and several cross-lingual baselines. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the impact of the size of unlabeled parallel data on the performance of the proposed method.

The originality of the paper lies in the application of matrix completion to cross language representation learning. While matrix completion has been used in other applications, its use in this context is novel and effective. The authors demonstrate that the proposed method achieves better performance than previous methods in the literature.

The significance of the paper is high, as it proposes a new and effective method for cross language representation learning. The results of the paper are consistent and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method on a range of cross language sentiment classification tasks. The paper also provides insights into the impact of the size of unlabeled parallel data on the performance of the proposed method, which is an important consideration in real-world applications.
