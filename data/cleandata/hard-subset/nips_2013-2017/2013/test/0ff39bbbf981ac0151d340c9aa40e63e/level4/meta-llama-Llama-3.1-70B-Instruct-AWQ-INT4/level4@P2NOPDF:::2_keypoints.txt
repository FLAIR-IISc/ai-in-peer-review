- Proposes a novel two-step method for learning cross-lingual topic representations using matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis.  
- Optimizes the matrix completion problem with a projected gradient descent algorithm and applies it to sentiment analysis on a parallel Amazon review dataset.  
- Experimental results show the method outperforms mono-lingual and cross-lingual baselines, achieving accurate cross-lingual topic representations with few parallel data points.  
- Evaluation quality is high, with thorough comparisons to two baselines (bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible, with clear parameter explanations; suggestion to better motivate matrix completion in the introduction.  
- Applying matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution and could generalize to other tasks.  
- Overall, the paper makes a significant, convincing contribution with promising results for future applications.
