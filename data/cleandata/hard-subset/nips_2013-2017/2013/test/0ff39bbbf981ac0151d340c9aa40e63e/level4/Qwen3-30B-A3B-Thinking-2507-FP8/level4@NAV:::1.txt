

The paper introduces a matrix completion framework for cross-domain classification, effectively utilizing labeled data from an auxiliary domain alongside unlabeled parallel data. The method comprises two stages: (1) constructing a unified incomplete matrix encompassing all documents and domains, followed by completion under low-rank and sparsity constraints via projected gradient descent; (2) reducing feature dimensionality of the completed matrix using Latent Semantic Indexing (LSI), then training a standard classifier on the resulting representation. The work also establishes convergence guarantees for the optimization algorithm and demonstrates strong empirical results.  

Key comments and questions:  
- After completing matrix M, dimensionality is reduced from d to k using LSI due to M "lacking sufficient capacity for handling feature sparseness." Please clarify: Is this primarily for computational efficiency (given M*’s relative density despite sparsity enforcement), or does LSI specifically address sparseness better than alternatives? Why LSI over PCA or similar techniques?  
- The approach leverages two distinct aspects of external data: (1) labeled auxiliary-domain data for target-domain classification, and (2) unlabeled parallel data. Please explicitly distinguish these in the methodology and report separate results for each.  
- The experimental setup (Section 5.2) requires clarification: Training uses (a) 4k labeled source documents, (b) 100 labeled target documents, and (c) 2k unlabeled parallel documents; testing occurs on 1,900 remaining labeled target documents. Section 5.3 varies unlabeled parallel documents (200–2k). Why does CL-KCCA outperform TSL in Figure 1 (EFM/EGM at 2k) but not in Table 1?  
- Clarify the rationale for assuming a fully observed document-term matrix is low-rank. Given sparsity and the typical rank of min(d, k), why is low-rankness justified?  
- Provide intuition for TSL’s consistent superiority over CCA-based methods (e.g., CL-KCCA). Why is matrix completion more effective than multiview approaches like CCA for this task? How does enforcing low-rankness on M* specifically drive performance gains compared to alternatives?  
- Report the algorithm’s runtime. How does projected gradient descent compare in speed to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?  

Suggested revisions:  
- Correct "(ij)" to "(i,j)" two sentences before Equation (2).  
- Remove "such as" in the sentences preceding Equations (4) and (5).  
The paper presents a simple, effective, and clearly articulated method. While some technical points require clarification, the core contribution is well-motivated.
