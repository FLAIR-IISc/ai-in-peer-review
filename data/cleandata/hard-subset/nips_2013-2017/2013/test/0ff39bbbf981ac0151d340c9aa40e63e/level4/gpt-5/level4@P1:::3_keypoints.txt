- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion on a unified bilingual document–term matrix, then LSI followed by a linear SVM; reports consistent gains versus CL-LSI/OPCA/KCCA, especially with few parallel documents.  
- Novelty: imputing the bilingual document–term matrix before applying LSI appears new compared to prior methods that operate on original concatenated matrices.  
- Optimization is well-founded: trace-norm (low-rank) + ℓ1 (sparsity) + nonnegativity with a projected gradient/shrinkage solver and a convergence guarantee.  
- Main empirical concern: gains may be due to LSI-induced feature scaling interacting with the linear SVM rather than a fundamentally better representation.  
- Suggestion to test robustness with scale-insensitive classifiers (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka) to rule out scaling artifacts.  
- Significance: if improvements hold under scale-insensitive learners, matrix completion could be a impactful way to bridge languages with limited parallel data.  
- Clarity issue: ambiguous construction of the unified document–term matrix — unclear whether columns are shared surface forms or language-tagged variants.  
- Experimental clarity: key experimental details (which docs labeled/unlabeled, number of parallel pairs, train/test splits, hyperparameter selection) are dispersed and should be consolidated.  
- Pros: conceptually simple pipeline, solid optimization theory, strong empirical performance with few parallel documents, and potential broad applicability.  
- Cons: possible scaling confound with linear SVM, only one classifier evaluated, unclear vocabulary/column definition, and scattered experimental description.  
- Concrete recommendations: add boosted-tree experiments, explicitly clarify matrix construction with a small example, and consolidate experimental protocol and hyperparameter selection in one subsection.  
- Overall: promising and neat idea that becomes much stronger if scaling artifacts are ruled out and exposition/experimental details are clarified.
