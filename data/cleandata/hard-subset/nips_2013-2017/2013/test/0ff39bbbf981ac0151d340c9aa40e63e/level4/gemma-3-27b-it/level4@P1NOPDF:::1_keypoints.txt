- Proposes a two-step matrix completion approach for cross-domain classification: complete an incomplete document-domain matrix via projected gradient descent with low-rank and sparsity constraints, then reduce dimensionality with LSI and train a classifier.  
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and reports promising experimental results.  
- Asks for clarification why LSI is used to reduce d to k—whether the motivation is handling feature sparsity or computational efficiency, and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.  
- Points out two distinct uses of external data—labeled source data vs. unlabeled parallel data—and requests these approaches be clearly separated and evaluated independently.  
- Requests clarification of the experimental setup (4,000 labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2,000 unlabeled parallel for training?) and confirmation that results are reported on the remaining 1,900 labeled target documents.  
- Notes a discrepancy: CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 for EFM and EGM at 2,000 unlabeled documents, but this is not reflected in Table 1—asks for explanation.  
- Questions the rationale for expecting a low-rank structure in a fully observed sparse document-term matrix, since documents are unlikely linear combinations of others.  
- Asks for intuition why TSL beats methods like CCA and how the low-rank constraint on M* compares to other techniques as the main performance driver.  
- Requests running-time comparisons: how the projected gradient descent runtime compares to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.  
- Suggests minor edits: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before equation (2) and remove “such as” before equations (4) and (5).  
- Overall judgment: method is straightforward, effective, and well-presented but needs clarification on several technical and experimental points.
