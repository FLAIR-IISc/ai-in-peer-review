- Quality concern: Method conceptually sound but reported results may not be robust.  
- Possible confound: Gains could be due to LSI's favorable scaling for the linear SVM versus OPCA/CCA rather than true method superiority.  
- Recommendation: Evaluate with a classifier insensitive to feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees via Weka).  
- Validation criterion: If the two-step system outperforms with such a classifier, robustness claims would be substantiated.  
- Significance: If validated, the work would be notable because matrix completion offers a well-controlled non-linear inference mechanism for multilingual representation.  
- Novelty: Core idea appears novel.  
- Clarity issues: Ambiguity whether the unified document-term matrix uses shared surface forms across languages or language-specific tokens (needs clarification).  
- Presentation problem: Experimental setup description is fragmented across Table 1 and text, making it hard to parse; results remain ambiguous without the suggested validation.
