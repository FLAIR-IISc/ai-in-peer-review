- Proposes a two-step method combining matrix completion and latent semantic analysis to learn cross-lingual topic representations.  
- Solves the matrix completion problem using a projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Evaluated on a parallel Amazon review sentiment analysis task and outperforms both mono-lingual and cross-lingual baselines.  
- Learning curves show the algorithm achieves high cross-lingual accuracy with relatively small amounts of parallel data.  
- Experiments are thorough and compare against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-organized with detailed parameter descriptions and selection methods, aiding reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity concern: the Introduction could motivate the use of matrix completion more fully.  
- The application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is novel.  
- The approach is significant as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification, showing superior performance and stability across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data and potential applicability to other tasks.
