1. Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion of a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix with missing cross-language entries, followed by LSI for low-dimensional embeddings.  
2. Uses unlabeled parallel bilingual documents to recover missing entries via a low-rank and sparse-constrained matrix completion formulation.  
3. Evaluated on Amazon cross-lingual sentiment classification across 18 tasks, showing significant improvements over baselines, especially with limited parallel data.  
4. Distinguishes itself from prior work (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA) by completing the matrix before dimensionality reduction rather than applying dimensionality reduction to the incomplete matrix.  
5. Strengths: well-motivated problem, clear technical presentation, strong empirical gains, and a convergence proof for the projected gradient descent algorithm.  
6. Experimental design includes ablation studies on unlabeled parallel document size (200–2000), demonstrating robustness when parallel data is scarce.  
7. Requests clarification on why LSI was chosen over other dimensionality-reduction methods (e.g., PCA) and whether the choice is computational or yields specific cross-lingual benefits.  
8. Notes the experimental setup mixes labeled source-data transfer and unlabeled parallel-data representation learning, making it hard to isolate each component’s contribution.  
9. Asks for explicit detailing of training/test splits (composition of labeled source, labeled target, unlabeled parallel, and test sizes) and explanation of discrepancies between Figure 1 and Table 1 results.  
10. Challenges the unjustified claim that a fully observed document-term matrix would be low-rank and requests clarification of the rank assumption.  
11. Asks for intuition on why enforcing low-rank on the completed matrix is preferable to multiview methods like CCA that model two language views directly.  
12. Requests runtime and scalability comparisons with baselines given large matrix sizes in cross-lingual learning.  
13. Minor technical issues to fix: typos (“parellel”→“parallel”), “ij”→“i,j” before Eq. (2), and removal of “such as” before Eqs. (4) and (5).  
14. Overall assessment: valuable, well-presented contribution suitable for NIPS pending the requested clarifications to strengthen reproducibility and motivation.
