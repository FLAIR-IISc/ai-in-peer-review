- Proposal: Two-step method using matrix completion on parallel bilingual documents followed by LSI to produce low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.
- Motivation/Related work: Well-motivated positioning against MT-based, bilingual-resource, and shared-space approaches with comprehensive related work coverage.
- Novelty: Sequential combination of matrix completion and LSI for cross-lingual representation learning appears novel.
- Problem definition: Clearly articulates cross-lingual text classification challenge and disjoint feature-space issue.
- Theoretical support: Provides mathematical formulation and a projected gradient descent algorithm with convergence guarantees.
- Empirical results: Shows improved performance on Amazon product reviews, especially when parallel documents are limited.
- Strength in low-resource settings: Method advantages are significant when parallel data are scarce, a practical benefit.
- Robustness concern: Improvements may stem from LSI’s interaction with linear SVMs rather than genuinely better cross-lingual representations.
- Classifier dependency: Evaluation relies heavily on linear SVMs, limiting generalizability to other classifiers.
- Clarity issues: Description of unified document-term matrix construction and handling of multilingual terms needs clearer explanation.
- Experimental reporting: Experimental setup is detailed but somewhat fragmented across text and table presentation.
- Limited ablation: No ablation study to isolate contributions of matrix completion versus LSI, weakening causal claims.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded approach with positive empirical gains and potential to advance the field.
- Arguments against acceptance: Uncertain robustness, over-reliance on linear SVMs, minor clarity problems, and missing ablation study.
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on additional experiments with non-linear classifiers, clarification of matrix construction, and an ablation study.
