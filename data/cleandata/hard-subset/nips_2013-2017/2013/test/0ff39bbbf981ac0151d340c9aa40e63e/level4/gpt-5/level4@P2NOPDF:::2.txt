Summary: This paper proposes a two-step approach for learning cross-lingual topic representations by first performing matrix completion and then applying latent semantic analysis. The matrix completion step is optimized via projected gradient descent. On a sentiment classification task using parallel Amazon reviews, the method outperforms both monolingual and cross-lingual baselines. Learning curves across varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data indicate that the approach achieves high accuracy with relatively little parallel data.

Quality: The experimental evaluation is thorough and supports the paper’s claims. The proposed two-step method is compared against a plain bag-of-words baseline, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction techniques. The comparisons are careful and comprehensive, and the analysis of performance as a function of unlabeled data size is informative.

Clarity: The paper is well organized and clearly written. All parameters and selection procedures are documented, making the experiments reproducible. A minor suggestion: strengthen the Introduction by more clearly motivating the use of matrix completion (currently discussed primarily in Section 3).

Originality: While cross-lingual representation learning is a well-studied problem, applying matrix completion to this setting is novel. The paper convincingly demonstrates that this formulation improves over existing approaches in the literature.

Significance: The two-step approach consistently outperforms the baselines, indicating that matrix completion is an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification. The formulation appears robust to the amount of available unlabeled parallel data and may extend to other cross-lingual tasks. Overall, the work offers a practical and empirically strong contribution to cross-lingual representation learning.
