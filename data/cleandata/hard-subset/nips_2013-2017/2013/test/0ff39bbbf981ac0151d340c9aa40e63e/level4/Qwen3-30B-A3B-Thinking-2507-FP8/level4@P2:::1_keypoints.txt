- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion on a partially observed document-term matrix followed by LSI.  
- Builds a partially observed doc-term matrix where parallel documents provide full rows and non-parallel documents have missing cross-lingual entries.  
- Solves a matrix completion problem with low-rank and sparsity constraints to impute missing entries.  
- Applies latent semantic indexing to the completed matrix to obtain low-dimensional cross-lingual embeddings.  
- Novelty: contrasts with prior work (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) by completing the matrix first to capture cross-lingual co-occurrence patterns.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: rigorous convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Strong empirical results on Amazon reviews (EN-FR, EN-DE, EN-JP), showing consistent improvements and up to 14.61% accuracy gain over baselines.  
- Demonstrates robustness with very limited parallel data (as few as 200 parallel documents).  
- Clarity: generally well-written with clear math and a logical two-step presentation.  
- Clarification needed: why LSI was chosen after completion instead of alternatives like PCA or random projections.  
- Clarification needed: experiments conflate labeled source data and unlabeled parallel documents; the effect of matrix completion is not isolated.  
- Clarification needed: the paper asserts fully observed doc-term matrices are low-rank but insufficiently justifies this assumption.  
- Experimental discrepancy: Figure 1 vs Table 1 conflict for EFM and EGM at 2,000 parallel documents needs explanation.  
- Main weakness: insufficient explanation of key technical choices and experimental design details.  
- Overall assessment: high quality, high originality, high significance; recommended for acceptance pending minor clarifications.
