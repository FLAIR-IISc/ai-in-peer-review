- Proposes translingual representation learning via matrix completion to create multilingual document vectors, followed by Canonical LSI (CL-LSI).
- Results seem promising but robustness is questionable; improvements may stem from LSI’s favorable feature-scaling with linear SVM rather than true method superiority.
- Recommends evaluating with a scaling-insensitive classifier (e.g., boosted decision trees available in Weka) to verify results aren’t a scaling artifact.
- If robust, the use of matrix completion would be a significant contribution due to its well-defined, non-linear inference capability.
- Core idea appears novel.
- Clarity needs improvement: unclear whether “unified document-term matrix” uses a single term for all surface forms or treats each surface form per language as distinct.
- Experimental setup description is fragmented (spread across Table 1), making it hard to follow.
- Overall promising but would like to see experiments beyond linear SVM to confirm true performance gains.
