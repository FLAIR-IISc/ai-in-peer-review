- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation learning method: unified document-term matrix completion (low-rank + sparsity via projected gradient descent) followed by LSI and classifier training.  
- Leverages unlabeled parallel bilingual documents to bridge feature spaces across languages.  
- Technical formulation, optimization algorithm, and convergence analysis are well-explained.  
- Clear and concise introduction and comprehensive related-work review highlighting limitations of prior methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, well-designed, and compares favorably to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.  
- Method is simple, effective, and experimentally robust, making a valuable contribution to the field.  
- Motivation for using LSI for dimensionality reduction is not fully justified—unclear if it’s for computational convenience or other reasons.  
- Relationship to multiview approaches (e.g., CCA) and other related techniques needs deeper exploration.  
- Running time and computational-cost comparisons with other methods are missing and should be provided.  
- Strengths: novel two-step approach, effective use of unlabeled parallel data, clarity, and strong experiments.  
- Weaknesses: unclear LSI motivation, incomplete comparison to multiview methods, and lacking runtime analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, promising results, clear presentation and technical detail.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved technical clarifications and insufficient comparison to related techniques.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
