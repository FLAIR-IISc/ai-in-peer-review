**Summary**

This paper presents a two-step method for learning cross-lingual topic representations by combining matrix completion and latent semantic analysis. A projected gradient descent algorithm is used to solve the matrix completion problem. Experimental results on a sentiment analysis task using a parallel Amazon review dataset show that the proposed method outperforms both mono-lingual and cross-lingual baselines. Furthermore, learning curves demonstrate that the algorithm can learn highly accurate cross-lingual topic representations from a relatively small amount of parallel data.

**Quality**

The paper's claims are well-supported by thorough experiments. The proposed two-step learning algorithm is carefully compared against several methods, including two standard baselines (bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA) and two state-of-the-art cross-lingual dimensionality reduction algorithms.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-organized. All parameters, including their selection methods, are described in detail, which should make the results easy to reproduce. As a minor suggestion, the motivation for using matrix completion could be explained more fully in the Introduction, although it is well-described in Section 3.

**Originality**

While cross-lingual representation learning is an established research area, the application of matrix completion to this task is novel. The experimental results clearly show that the proposed algorithm achieves better performance than previous methods in the literature.

**Significance**

The proposed two-step approach consistently outperforms the other baselines. This work demonstrates that matrix completion can serve as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification, and this formulation may prove effective in other tasks as well. The experimental results show that the method not only outperforms other cross-lingual baselines, including a state-of-the-art method, but is also stable when trained on varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data.
