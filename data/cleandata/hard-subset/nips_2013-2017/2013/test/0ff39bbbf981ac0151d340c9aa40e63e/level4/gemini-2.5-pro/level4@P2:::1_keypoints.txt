- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method that completes a unified document-term matrix then applies LSI to obtain shared dense representations for classification.  
- Leverages a small set of unlabeled parallel documents to bridge vocabulary gaps between source and target languages.  
- Novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual learning, differing from prior projection-based approaches (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).  
- Strong empirical results: consistently outperforms strong baselines across language pairs and domains, especially robust with few parallel documents.  
- Provides a convergence proof for the projected gradient descent algorithm, adding theoretical soundness.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear in presenting the method.  
- Motivation for applying LSI after matrix completion is unclear—need clarification whether LSI addresses capacity, noise reduction, or computational efficiency and why LSI over alternatives.  
- Core low-rank assumption for a fully observed document-term matrix needs stronger justification or intuition.  
- Lacks intuition explaining why enforcing low-rank structure outperforms multiview methods like CL-KCCA.  
- Experimental setup needs clearer description (clarify training/dev/test splits and confirm np=2000 setting).  
- Discrepancy between Table 1 and Figure 1 results (TSL vs CL-KCCA for np=2000) requires clarification.  
- Recommend ablation to disentangle contributions of labeled source data vs unlabeled parallel data (e.g., remove labeled source to assess impact).  
- Missing discussion of computational complexity and empirical running time; need scalability analysis versus baseline methods.  
- Minor editorial fixes: correct “(ij)” to “(i, j)” in Section 3.1 and remove redundant “such as” in Section 4.1 before equations (3) and (5).
