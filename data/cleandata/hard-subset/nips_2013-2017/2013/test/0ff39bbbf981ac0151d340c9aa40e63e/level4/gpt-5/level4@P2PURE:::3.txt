The paper proposes a method for translingual representation learning that first applies matrix completion to obtain multilingual vectors for each row, and then performs CL-LSI on these multilingual vectors.

Quality: Although the idea is appealing, I am not convinced the results are robust. The positive outcomes might simply arise because LSI’s scaling is more favorable than OPCA or CCA when paired with a linear SVM. I urge the authors to use a learning algorithm that is insensitive to per-feature rescaling—for example, boosted decision trees (available in Weka). If boosted decision trees on top of the proposed two-step system outperform other techniques, I would find the result convincing.

Significance: If the effect is truly robust, it would be noteworthy, as matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique.

Novelty: The idea appears new.

Clarity: There are some clarity issues. Line 113 mentions “construct[ing] a unified document-term matrix for all documents.” It is unclear whether this means using a single term for each surface form (e.g., “Merkel”), or assigning distinct terms to each language-specific surface form (e.g., “Merkelen” and “Merkelde”). Please clarify. Also, the description of the experimental setup is split across Table 1, which made it harder to piece together.

Overall, this seems like a neat idea. I wish the authors had tried classifiers beyond a linear SVM—I cannot tell whether the reported improvements are robust or simply a scaling artifact.
