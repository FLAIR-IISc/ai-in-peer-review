- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion to fill missing bilingual document-term entries, followed by LSI for low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Evaluated on 18 cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks using Amazon reviews across English, French, German, and Japanese.  
- Claims novelty over CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA by emphasizing matrix completion to capture cross-lingual co-occurrence patterns.  

Strengths:
- Technically sound formulation with convex relaxation for rank minimization.  
- Thorough convergence analysis and correct projected gradient descent proofs.  
- Comprehensive experiments across 18 tasks and an informative ablation on number of parallel documents.  
- Clear organization, precise mathematical formulation, and accessible pseudocode.  
- Novel application of matrix completion before LSI and effectiveness with as few as ~200 parallel documents.  
- Demonstrates significant accuracy gains (8.59–14.61% average) and practical value for low-resource settings.

Weaknesses:
- Does not convincingly show matrix completion is necessary versus using parallel documents directly for mapping.  
- Lacks comparison to modern neural cross-lingual models (e.g., multilingual BERT, XLM-R).  
- Hyperparameter selection is ad-hoc (tuned only on EFB task) with limited justification for generalization.  
- Insufficient explanation for the low-rank assumption of the concatenated matrix.  
- Unclear why completing the matrix before LSI outperforms applying LSI to partially observed data.  
- Idea bears similarity to collaborative filtering; differences from such domains are not well highlighted.  
- No detailed analysis of computational complexity or discussion of failure cases (e.g., very dissimilar languages or extremely scarce parallel data).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Well-motivated method with clear technical contribution and rigorous optimization analysis.  
- Strong and consistent empirical improvements, especially with minimal parallel data.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Novelty claim may be overstated given related prior ideas.  
- Missing comparisons to current state-of-the-art neural methods and more ablations on the matrix completion step.  
- Computational cost concerns are not addressed.

Overall recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

Required revisions:
- Compare against recent multilingual transformer baselines (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R).  
- Better justify why matrix completion is necessary and how it uniquely captures cross-lingual co-occurrence patterns.  
- Provide computational complexity analysis versus baselines.  
- Clarify hyperparameter selection procedure and demonstrate its generalizability across tasks.
