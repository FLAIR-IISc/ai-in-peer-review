- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: fill missing entries in a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix via matrix completion, then apply LSI for representation learning.  
- Shows improved cross-lingual sentiment classification on Amazon reviews and claims robustness with limited parallel data.  
- Correctly positions relative to translation-based and cross-lingual representation methods; novelty is performing matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.  
- Novelty: combining matrix completion with LSI to leverage limited parallel corpora.  
- Empirical strength: consistent, statistically significant improvements over strong baselines; useful ablation on parallel data size.  
- Theoretical strength: provides convex relaxation, projected gradient descent optimization, and convergence analysis.  
- Presentation: generally well-written with clear explanations and readable mathematical formulations.  
- Practical significance: method achieves good performance with small amounts of parallel data.  
- Scalability concern: limited details on computational complexity and memory costs of matrix completion on large document-term matrices.  
- Parameter sensitivity: γ, ρ, k selection appears ad-hoc and tuned on a single task; needs systematic analysis or auto-tuning.  
- Limited dataset: evaluation only on Amazon reviews may not demonstrate generalization to other domains or text types.  
- Optimization details lacking: missing specifics about implementation, libraries, and practical optimizations or challenges.  
- Missing comparisons: does not compare against recent neural pre-trained cross-lingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical and theoretical contributions, practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved scalability, parameter sensitivity, generalization concerns, and lack of neural baselines.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with clear contribution; weaknesses are addressable and do not fundamentally undermine the work.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions asking for scalability/runtime details, parameter sensitivity or auto-tuning, comparisons to mBERT/XLM-R, and tests on more diverse datasets.
