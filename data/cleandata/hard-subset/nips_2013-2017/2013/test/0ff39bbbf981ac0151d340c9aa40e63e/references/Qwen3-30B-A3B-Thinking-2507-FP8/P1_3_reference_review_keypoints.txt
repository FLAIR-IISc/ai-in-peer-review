- Summary: Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual method that completes missing entries in a concatenated document-term matrix via matrix completion then applies LSI to learn cross-lingual representations, evaluated on 18 sentiment tasks.

- Strength: Novel Approach — two-step matrix completion then LSI conceptually addresses treating missing entries as zeros and differs from prior CL-LSI/CL-OPCA/CL-KCCA methods.

- Strength: Theoretical Foundation — provides a convex optimization formulation and convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent algorithm (Theorem 1).

- Strength: Comprehensive Evaluation — experiments across 18 tasks, multiple languages and categories, and varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data (200–2000).

- Strength: Strong Results — consistently outperforms baselines (8.59–14.61% improvement over TBOW), especially effective with only 200 parallel documents.

- Weakness: Missing comparisons with recent neural cross-lingual methods (e.g., multilingual BERT / transformer-based approaches).

- Weakness: Limited analysis of why matrix completion outperforms supervised fine-tuning or how it captures cross-lingual relationships.

- Weakness: Parameter Sensitivity — reports optimal parameters but lacks thorough sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness: Ambiguity about how unlabeled parallel documents are obtained and whether translation noise or effort is involved.

- Overall Assessment: Well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically convincing contribution that would be strengthened by comparisons to neural methods and deeper analysis of matrix completion.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Revision Suggestion: Add comparison with at least one recent neural cross-lingual method (e.g., multilingual BERT).

- Revision Suggestion: Clarify how unlabeled parallel documents are obtained and discuss potential noise/collection effort.

- Revision Suggestion: Provide analysis or case study showing how matrix completion captures cross-lingual alignments (example aligned words).

- Revision Suggestion: Discuss computational complexity relative to baselines for large-scale use.

- Arguments for Acceptance: Novel two-step approach, strong theoretical grounding, comprehensive experiments, significant performance gains, and effectiveness in low-resource settings.

- Arguments Against (Minor): Lack of comparison with modern neural methods, insufficient analysis of matrix completion's role, and ambiguity around the nature of "unlabeled parallel documents."
