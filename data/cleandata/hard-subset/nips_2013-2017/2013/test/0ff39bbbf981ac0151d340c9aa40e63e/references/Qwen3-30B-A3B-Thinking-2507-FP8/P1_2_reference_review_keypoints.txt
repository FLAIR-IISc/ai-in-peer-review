- Proposes a two-step cross-language method: fill missing entries in a bilingual document-term matrix via matrix completion, then apply LSI.  
- Claims significant improvements over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA on Amazon cross-language sentiment tasks.  
- Method framed as distinct because it completes the matrix before applying latent-space methods rather than operating on the incomplete matrix.  
- Presents a clear mathematical formulation with non-negativity constraints for matrix completion.  
- Provides a convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent optimization.  
- Experiments are extensive: 18 cross-language sentiment classification tasks across multiple language pairs and categories.  
- Shows consistent empirical improvements and analyzes the effect of varying amounts of unlabeled parallel data.  
- Lacks statistical significance testing to support performance claims.  
- Fails to compare against recent deep learning state-of-the-art cross-lingual models (XLM-R, mBERT, etc.).  
- Does not thoroughly discuss limitations or failure modes, especially when parallel data is scarce.  
- Writing is clear, methodology is logically presented, and paper organization is strong.  
- Reproducibility is partially addressed but missing implementation details for large-scale matrix completion and parameter selection.  
- Novelty is incremental: the distinction from CL-LSI and CL-OPCA is subtle and not convincingly justified.  
- Related work section is comprehensive but does not adequately differentiate the contribution from similar methods.  
- The reported gains (8.59–14.61% average) are meaningful but their significance relative to modern neural methods is unclear.  
- Practical advantage: method can work with as few as ~200 unlabeled parallel documents.  
- Strengths: clear problem statement, solid theory, comprehensive experiments, practical data requirements, and good writing.  
- Weaknesses: missing comparisons to neural baselines, subtle novelty, no significance testing, limited discussion of limitations, and insufficient implementation detail.  
- Recommends weak acceptance contingent on major revisions addressing comparisons to SOTA and deeper analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: compare to XLM/XLM-R/mBERT, add significance tests, expand limitation discussion, clarify why matrix completion helps, and detail implementation.  
- Acceptance pros: sound method, consistent empirical gains, low parallel-data requirement, and convergence proof.  
- Acceptance cons: limited novelty, missing modern baseline comparisons, modest advancement over existing methods, and weak empirical robustness reporting.
