- Two-step method: matrix completion on a bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI (SVD) on the completed matrix to train a linear SVM with source labels + 100 target labels.  
- Step 1 details: complete M0 using nuclear-norm (low-rank), L1 (sparsity), squared-loss on observed entries, L2 toward zero on missing entries, with nonnegativity; optimized by projected gradient + singular-value shrinkage + nonnegativity projection.  
- Step 2 details: apply LSI (truncated SVD) to recovered matrix, derive shared low-dimensional representation, train linear SVM.  
- Empirical result: outperforms TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA on most of 18 EN/FR/DE/JA Amazon review transfer tasks and is robust down to ~200 parallel documents.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on CL-LSI/OPCA/KCCA and trace-norm matrix completion; novelty is “densifying” the bilingual matrix via completion before LSI.  
- Optimization relation: follows fixed-point/shrinkage methods from nuclear-norm minimization.  
- Technical soundness: modeling and updates are plausible, but convergence proof is inadequate because sequential prox steps do not guarantee convergence for the composite nonsmooth objective.  
- Suggested remedy for convergence: need rigorous justification (three-operator splitting, Dykstra, ADMM) or empirical convergence diagnostics.  
- Modeling concern: treating missing-language features as “missing” (not zero) is a strong modeling choice that needs empirical justification and ablations.  
- Empirical evaluation gaps: no runtime/memory/scalability analysis, no statistical significance testing, and missing reproducibility details (stopping criteria, iterations, SVD settings, preprocessing).  
- Baselines gap: important modern baselines are missing (cross-lingual word embeddings, SCL-style pivots, MT-then-monolingual).  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized with consistent notation; minor issues include loss formatting, initialization specifics, hyperparameters, and complexity reporting.  
- Originality: novel combination (matrix completion then LSI) but components are standard and theoretical contribution is limited.  
- Significance: empirically promising and practical if scalable, but broader impact uncertain without stronger theory and additional baselines/ablations.  
- Pros: simple intuitive method, effective with small parallel corpora, consistent empirical gains, clear algorithm, analysis vs amount of parallel data.  
- Cons: insufficient convergence justification, missing ablations on key modeling choices, potential scalability/SVD cost, absent strong baselines and significance tests, sparse reproducibility details.  
- Questions for authors: provide runtime/memory/iteration counts and SVD type; ablate completion/no-L1/no-nonnegativity and vary ρ/γ; empirically justify missing-not-zero assumption; compare to SCL and cross-lingual embeddings; clarify convergence guarantees or adopt a provably convergent solver.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising empirical results but needs stronger theoretical grounding, broader baselines and ablations, and scalability/reproducibility details.
