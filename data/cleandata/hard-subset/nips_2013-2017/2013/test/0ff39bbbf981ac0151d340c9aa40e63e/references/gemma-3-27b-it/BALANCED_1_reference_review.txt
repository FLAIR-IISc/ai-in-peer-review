This paper proposes a two-step method for cross-language text classification, leveraging unlabeled parallel bilingual documents. The core idea is to first perform matrix completion on a concatenated document-term matrix to handle missing values arising from the language barrier, and then apply latent semantic indexing (LSI) to derive cross-lingual representations. The authors provide a detailed description of the method, including the optimization algorithm used for matrix completion, and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on multilingual Amazon product reviews.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and positions the proposed work within the existing literature. The related work section is comprehensive, covering various approaches to cross-language text classification, including machine translation-based methods and cross-lingual representation learning techniques. The technical details of the proposed method are presented clearly, with a well-defined optimization problem and a detailed description of the projected gradient descent algorithm used for solving it. The experimental setup is also well-defined, with clear descriptions of the datasets, baseline methods, and evaluation metrics.

A significant strength of this work is the thoroughness of the experimental evaluation. The authors compare their method with several strong baselines and conduct ablation studies to assess the impact of the size of the unlabeled parallel data. The results consistently demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method, particularly in low-resource scenarios where the amount of parallel data is limited. The convergence analysis of the optimization algorithm is also a positive aspect, although the proof relies on existing literature and doesn’t present novel theoretical contributions.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. First, while the theoretical justification for matrix completion is provided (low-rank assumption), the choice of the specific regularization parameters (γ, ρ) seems somewhat arbitrary, relying on a limited parameter selection process using only one cross-lingual task (EFB). A more systematic parameter tuning strategy, perhaps using cross-validation, would strengthen the results. Second, the practical significance of the performance gains, while statistically significant, could be further discussed. How much does the improvement translate to in a real-world application?  Finally, the presentation of results could be improved. While Table 1 is clear, the text describing the results reiterates much of the table’s content without providing deeper insights. More analysis of specific cases where the proposed method performs particularly well or poorly would be valuable.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of matrix completion and LSI for cross-lingual representation learning is a novel approach.
*   **Empirical Performance:** The proposed method consistently outperforms strong baselines on a standard dataset.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The method is grounded in sound theoretical principles (matrix completion, low-rank approximation).
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Parameter Tuning:** The parameter tuning strategy is limited and could be improved.
*   **Practical Significance:** The practical impact of the performance gains is not fully discussed.
*   **Result Analysis:** The discussion of the experimental results could be more insightful.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that presents a novel and effective method for cross-language text classification. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the work outweigh them. I believe this paper would be a valuable contribution to the NIPS conference.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the parameter tuning strategy and provide a more in-depth analysis of the experimental results. Specifically, I suggest including a cross-validation procedure for parameter selection, discussing the practical implications of the performance gains, and providing more detailed analysis of specific examples.
