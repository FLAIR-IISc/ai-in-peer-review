- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: build a partially observed bilingual document-term matrix, apply matrix completion to fill missing entries, then use LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual representations; shows strong results on Amazon reviews.  
- Distinguishes itself from MT-based and bilingual-pair methods by leveraging matrix completion on parallel unlabeled data; connects to matrix completion and sparse recovery literature.  
- Novelty: combining matrix completion with LSI to explicitly handle missing data in concatenated doc-term matrices.  
- Empirical results: convincing improvements over several baselines across multiple tasks.  
- Robustness: method performs well even with limited parallel data.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and the two-step procedure is clearly described.  
- Theoretical support: optimization algorithm and convergence analysis provide a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Scalability concern: projected gradient descent for matrix completion may not scale to large document-term matrices; computational cost unclear.  
- Parameter sensitivity: multiple tunable parameters (γ, ρ, k) with insufficient analysis of sensitivity or ablation.  
- Limited baselines: lacks comparisons to recent pre-trained multilingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R).  
- Dataset scope: experiments restricted to Amazon product reviews; generalizability to other domains is untested.  
- LSI choice: no strong justification for using LSI over other dimensionality reduction methods.  
- For acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with strong empirical and theoretical support and useful robustness to limited parallel data.  
- Against acceptance: unresolved scalability, missing modern baselines, and limited analysis of parameter sensitivity and dataset diversity.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; suitable for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (with minor revisions).  
- Recommended improvements: add scalability discussion and possible large-scale strategies, compare to state-of-the-art multilingual models, perform parameter ablation studies, evaluate on diverse datasets, and justify or compare the choice of LSI.
