- Proposes TSL: convex matrix completion on a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix with missing entries, followed by LSI (SVD) and linear SVM for cross-language text classification.

- Completion uses trace-norm, L1 sparsity, squared loss on observed entries, a small penalty ρ on unobserved entries, nonnegativity constraints, solved by projected proximal gradient with singular value thresholding.

- Experiments on multilingual Amazon reviews (18 English↔{French,German,Japanese} tasks) show consistent gains over CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA and large improvements over a target-only BOW baseline.

- Method is robust to small parallel corpora, degrading less than competitors when parallel pairs are reduced (e.g., to 200).

- Novelty: imputing missing bilingual blocks via convex low-rank+sparse completion before LSI; conceptually distinct from CL-LSI/OPCA/KCCA.

- Strength: simple, clear convex formulation combining sparsity, low-rank structure, and nonnegativity with a convergence proof for the projected proximal scheme.

- Strength: completion step can propagate cross-lingual co-occurrence from few parallel pairs and LSI further denoises representations.

- Strength: broad empirical coverage across multiple tasks and directions.

- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to modern strong baselines (MT-based translate-then-train, mapping-based cross-lingual embeddings, multilingual PLMs like mBERT/XLM-R).

- Weakness: Limited ablations — no “completion-only” experiment and no isolates for nonnegativity, L1 term, or penalty ρ.

- Weakness: Scalability and efficiency not addressed — SVD shrinkage each iteration, no runtime/memory/iteration counts or use of truncated SVD reported.

- Weakness: Theory is standard and limited to convergence; no identifiability or recovery-condition analysis for completion in this context.

- Weakness: Experimental details missing — no significance tests, minimal hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, μ fixed arbitrarily, no error analysis or per-language vocabulary sizes.

- Weakness: Narrow evaluation scope — only Amazon sentiment classification; unclear generality to other tasks or more divergent language pairs.

- Quality: Formulation and optimization are technically sound, but practical impact is uncertain without modern-baseline comparisons and scalability data.

- Concern: The role and tuning of ρ (penalty on unobserved entries) is not well justified and may substantially affect results.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well written; construction of M0 and handling of parallel vs non-parallel rows are clear and Algorithm 1 is concise.

- Clarity issues: Some incomplete references, typos, and missing implementation details (stopping criteria, SVD variant, preprocessing/tokenization/vocab sizes).

- Originality: The two-step completion-before-LSI pipeline is a novel combination in this setting though components are existing techniques.

- Significance: Results are promising against classical baselines but limited in significance due to missing modern baselines and scalability evidence.

- Key questions for authors: comparisons to MT and multilingual PLMs; runtime/memory/iterations and SVD truncation; ablation studies (completion-only, no L1, no nonnegativity, varying ρ); minimal parallel pairs needed; evaluation on other datasets/tasks.

- Pros summary: Clear convex formulation, consistent empirical gains over classical CL baselines, robustness to small parallel corpora.

- Cons summary: Missing modern baselines, no scalability/runtime analysis, limited ablations and theoretical insight.

- Minor comments: Clarify initialization choice, provide precise stopping criteria/tolerance, and fix citation/typo issues.

- Reproducibility: Dataset and methods are standard but code release and more implementation details (SVD variant, stopping, preprocessing) are needed.

- Recommendation: Lean reject — paper needs modern-baseline comparisons, thorough ablations/sensitivity analyses, and scalability/runtime evidence to meet NeurIPS standards.
