- Summary: Proposes filling missing entries in a bilingual document-term matrix via matrix completion, then applying LSI to obtain cross-lingual representations, showing strong gains on 18 Amazon sentiment tasks.

- Strength: Clear and well-structured presentation with precise mathematical formulation and a clearly described algorithm.

- Strength: Strong empirical results, outperforming baselines by 8.59–14.61% and working with as few as 200 parallel documents.

- Strength: Provides a rigorous convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent optimization.

- Strength: Method is practically relevant due to effectiveness with limited parallel data.

- Weakness: Insufficient differentiation from prior work—need to justify why filling missing entries before LSI is fundamentally better than applying LSI directly to the incomplete matrix.

- Weakness: Limited comparison set—evaluated only against older methods (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) and missing modern neural baselines like mBERT, XLM-R, LASER, and MUSE.

- Weakness: Narrow experimental scope—only Amazon sentiment classification; unclear generalization to other tasks and no analysis of computational complexity for matrix completion.

- Weakness: Inadequate explanation of parameter selection (used EFB task without justification) and lack of robust parameter-selection methodology like cross-validation.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why matrix completion is appropriate here (why document-term matrix is low-rank and how missing-data structure suits completion).

- Methodology concern: Choice of ℓ1 sparsity and trace norm low-rank regularizers is standard but not justified against alternatives like ℓ2 or other regularizers.

- Methodology concern: Impact of the non-negativity constraint on optimization and recovery quality is not discussed.

- Methodology concern: No analysis of how step size τ affects convergence speed or solution quality in Algorithm 1.

- Suggestion: Add comparisons with recent neural cross-lingual methods to provide a more meaningful benchmark.

- Suggestion: Include an ablation study comparing LSI on the incomplete matrix versus LSI on the completed matrix to demonstrate the value of matrix completion.

- Suggestion: Expand evaluations to other tasks (e.g., text classification, NER) and include computational complexity analysis.

- Suggestion: Use more robust parameter-selection procedures and justify the choice of tasks used for tuning.

- Overall assessment: Technically sound with convincing results on the benchmark, but novelty is undermined by insufficient differentiation from prior work and lack of comparisons to modern methods.

- Recommendation: Weak acceptance with major revisions required to strengthen novelty claims and empirical comparisons.

- Final thought: Reasonable and promising incremental contribution that would be significantly improved by updating evaluations to reflect current state-of-the-art methods.
