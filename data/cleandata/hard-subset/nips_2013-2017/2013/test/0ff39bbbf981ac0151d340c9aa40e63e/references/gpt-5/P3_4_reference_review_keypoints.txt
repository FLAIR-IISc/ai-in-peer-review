- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual document representation: convex matrix completion on a concatenated doc–term matrix, then LSI + linear SVM for classification.
- Uses a trace-norm (nuclear norm) penalty, L1 (reduces to linear under nonnegativity), squared loss on observed entries, and a ρ-weighted penalty on missing entries with nonnegativity constraints.
- Optimization uses projected gradient descent alternating a gradient step, singular value thresholding (SVT) for nuclear norm, and projection onto the nonnegative orthant.
- Experiments on 18 English–French/German/Japanese Amazon review sentiment tasks show consistent improvements over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA and robustness to limited parallel data.
- Conceptually related to prior parallel-data CL methods (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) but differs by imputing missing entries via convex matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.
- Also related to SCL-style pivot methods but does not require bilingual lexicons; optimization borrows standard nuclear-norm minimization ideas.
- Strength: clear problem setup and simple pipeline that leverages monolingual classifiers.
- Strength: sensible use of unlabeled parallel data to couple vocabularies while including all monolingual documents.
- Strength: convex surrogate with a straightforward iterative algorithm and a convergence discussion.
- Strength: empirical gains are consistent across many tasks and data-efficient down to ~200 parallel pairs.
- Main technical weakness: proximal gradient handling of the composite nonsmooth term (nuclear norm + nonnegativity indicator) is incorrect—SVT followed by projection is not generally the prox of the sum.
- The provided convergence argument shows non-expansiveness but does not prove that fixed points minimize the original objective with both nonsmooth terms.
- Recommendation: adopt a principled optimization (e.g., ADMM, Dykstra’s algorithm) or prove the sequential prox composition is valid.
- Modeling concern: claim of “naturally low-rank” document–term matrix is debatable; need spectrum plots and discussion of approximate low-rankness and rank k selection.
- Regularization of missing entries toward zero via ρ is critical but under-analyzed; may bias imputations and conflict with cross-lingual coupling—ablation needed.
- Lacks key ablations: CL-LSI on raw concatenated matrix, classification directly on completed matrix without LSI, and variation over k to attribute gains.
- Scalability/practicality concerns: per-iteration SVD cost, no runtime, memory, iteration counts, or truncated SVD method reported.
- Baselines are incomplete: missing MT-based baselines, SCL with bilingual pivots, and modern bilingual embedding approaches.
- Hyperparameter tuning used labeled target data from one task and reused across tasks, risking bias; per-task validation or unsupervised criteria preferred.
- Generality limited: only Amazon reviews and English-centric pairs considered; no other datasets or non-English pairs reported.
- Reproducibility issues: missing implementation details (truncated SVD method, stopping criteria, initialization, number of iterations), no code, no statistical significance testing.
- Quality: empirical study thorough within dataset, but optimization flaw and lack of runtime analysis weaken technical contribution.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written and organized; proofs readable but incomplete for the composite nonsmooth case; minor typos/notation issues.
- Originality: novel application of convex matrix completion to cross-lingual doc-term imputation prior to LSI; optimization strategy is standard.
- Significance: promising if optimization and baselines are strengthened; current methodological flaw and limited experiments reduce confidence.
- Questions for authors: provide correct optimization (or proof), runtime/memory/iteration details, sensitivity/ablation for ρ, γ, k, comparisons to MT and SCL baselines, and results on other datasets/language pairs.
- Pros for acceptance: simple intuitive method, novel use of matrix completion, robust to small parallel data with consistent gains.
- Cons for acceptance: theoretically unsound optimization handling, missing strong baselines and ablations, unclear scalability.
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending fixes to optimization, added ablations/baselines, and runtime/complexity analysis.
