- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: construct concatenated bilingual document-term matrix with missing entries, fill them via matrix completion, then apply LSI to get cross-lingual representations.  
- Claims strong empirical gains over baselines (8.59–14.61% over TBOW) on 18 cross-language sentiment classification tasks (EN→FR/DE/JP across three product categories).  
- Technical soundness: formulates a clear optimization problem, provides a projected gradient descent algorithm (Algorithm 1) and a convergence proof (Theorem 1).  
- Comprehensive experiments: evaluation across 18 tasks shows consistent superiority of the proposed TSL method over listed baselines.  
- Practical utility: method performs well with very small amounts of unlabeled parallel data (as few as 200 documents).  
- Clear presentation: paper is well-organized with lucid mathematical formulation and logical flow.  
- Major omission: no comparison with recent neural cross-lingual models (e.g., multilingual BERT, XLM-R, mT5), undermining claims of significance.  
- Insufficient theoretical justification for the low-rank assumption in the cross-lingual setting; no empirical/theoretical evidence that the concatenated matrix is naturally low-rank.  
- Parameter sensitivity is not addressed—parameters chosen on one task (EFB) with no analysis of sensitivity or guidance for new domains.  
- Scalability concerns: computational complexity of matrix completion on very large unified vocabularies (d) is not discussed.  
- Novelty unclear vs CL-LSI: distinction between applying LSI directly and filling missing entries first is not sharply articulated.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid and empirically promising, but significance is limited without neural baselines; most relevant when neural methods are infeasible.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept with major revisions—add comparisons to recent neural methods and strengthen justification for matrix-completion approach and scalability/parameter analyses.
