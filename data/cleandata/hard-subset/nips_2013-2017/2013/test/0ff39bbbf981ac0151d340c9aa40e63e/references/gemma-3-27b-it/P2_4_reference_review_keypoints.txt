- Summary: Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method using matrix completion on a concatenated document-term matrix followed by LSI to produce cross-lingual representations for monolingual classification, evaluated on Amazon sentiment tasks with improved results.

- Novelty: Sequential combination of matrix completion then LSI for cross-lingual learning is a novel application of established techniques.

- Strong empirical results: Consistently outperforms strong baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA), especially with limited labeled target data.

- Robustness to parallel data size: Method performs well even with few unlabeled parallel documents.

- Clear presentation: Paper is well-structured, algorithm and experimental setup are clearly described.

- Thorough related work: Comprehensive overview positioning the method within existing cross-lingual approaches.

- Theoretical justification lacking: Insufficient explanation for why the two-step approach is superior to direct LSI or other factorization methods.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Parameters tuned on a single task (EFB) and reused, raising concerns about generalizability and potential suboptimality.

- Scalability concerns: Need more discussion on scalability of projected gradient descent, memory requirements, and parallelization for large vocabularies/datasets.

- Limited analysis scope: Evaluation focuses on sentiment classification; broader tasks (e.g., topic categorization) are not tested.

- Quality score 8/10: Solid technical approach and empirical support, but theoretical and tuning issues limit score.

- Clarity score 9/10: Very clear writing and consistent notation.

- Originality score 7/10: Novel combination but uses well-established techniques; originality is in application.

- Significance score 8/10: Significant empirical improvements and practical value for low-resource scenarios.

- Arguments for acceptance: Strong performance, novel technique combination, robustness to parallel data scarcity, and clear presentation.

- Arguments against acceptance: Weak theoretical backing, hyperparameter/tuning concerns, scalability not fully addressed, and limited evaluation breadth.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Suggested revisions: Add theoretical discussion of matrix completion benefits, perform systematic hyperparameter tuning across tasks, discuss scalability/practical implementation details, and add experiments on at least one additional cross-lingual task.
