- Summary: Proposes two-step cross-lingual classification—nuclear-norm matrix completion on concatenated bilingual doc-term matrix, then LSI+SVM—using few parallel documents.
- Method details: Completion objective mixes trace norm (low-rank), ℓ1 sparsity (linear under nonnegativity), squared loss on observed entries, and Tikhonov-like penalty on missing entries; solved by gradient step + singular value thresholding + nonnegativity projection.
- Empirical result: Evaluated on Amazon reviews (EN/FR/DE/JA) across 18 transfer directions and 3 domains; TSL outperforms CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA, TBOW and is robust down to ~200 parallel pairs.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on translation-based transfer, multi-view learning, CL-LSI/OPCA/KCCA and SCL; novelty is nuclear-norm completion before LSI.
- Strength: Clear, interpretable modeling leveraging few parallel pairs to propagate cross-language co-occurrence via convex relaxation.
- Strength: Simple, resource-light approach with consistent empirical gains across multiple transfer settings.
- Strength: Convex formulation (after relaxation) and straightforward algorithm with convergence argument and operator definitions.
- Weakness: Optimization rigor lacking—composition of proximal operators (shrinkage + projection) is not guaranteed to solve the constrained objective; convergence proof is insufficient.
- Weakness: Computational aspects under-specified—no complexity analysis, iteration counts, partial SVD strategy, runtimes, or memory footprint.
- Weakness: Missing ablations—no analysis of individual regularizers (γ, µ, ρ), nonnegativity effect, or necessity of the two-step (completion vs. direct factorization).
- Weakness: Baseline fairness unclear—insufficient detail on how CL-LSI/OPCA/KCCA were configured, normalized, and given access to non-parallel data.
- Weakness: Limited breadth—only sentiment classification on Amazon reviews, no other tasks (e.g., topic classification, CLIR) or datasets and no error analysis.
- Weakness: Statistical testing absent—means and stddev reported but no significance tests.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—incomplete references, typos, and some notational imprecision.
- Quality: Modeling idea is sound and empirically plausible, but theoretical contribution is limited due to the weak convergence claim.
- Clarity: Generally clear and well-structured, but missing implementation details reduce reproducibility.
- Originality: Moderate novelty—novel composition of known tools (nuclear-norm completion + LSI) but conceptually straightforward.
- Significance: Potentially useful in low-parallel-data settings, but impact limited by narrow evaluation and theoretical/experimental gaps.
- Suggested fix (optimization): Provide principled optimization or rigorous proof (e.g., ADMM, Dykstra splitting) that the algorithm solves the constrained problem.
- Suggested fix (scalability): Report runtime, iteration counts, partial SVD/approximation strategies, and memory requirements.
- Suggested fix (ablations): Add ablations for LSI, completion, nonnegativity, and regularizer sensitivities; compare to direct factorization (NMF).
- Suggested fix (baselines & preprocessing): Clarify preprocessing, ensure fair baseline configurations, and expand baselines (SCL, bilingual topic models).
- Suggested fix (evaluation): Add significance tests, error analysis, and experiments on additional tasks/datasets for broader validation.
- Pro acceptance: Resource-efficient, convex, interpretable method with consistent gains and robustness to few parallel pairs.
- Con acceptance: Convergence to stated objective not convincingly established, limited experimental breadth, missing ablations, and moderate novelty.
- Overall recommendation: Weak reject (borderline); acceptability hinges on fixing optimization proof/algorithm and strengthening experimental analysis.
