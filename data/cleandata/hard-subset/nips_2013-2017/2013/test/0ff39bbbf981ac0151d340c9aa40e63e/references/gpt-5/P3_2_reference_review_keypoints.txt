- Summary: Proposes imputing a unified bilingual document-term matrix via convex matrix completion (trace-norm + ℓ1 + non-negativity), then applying LSI for cross-lingual classification; shows improvements on multilingual Amazon reviews.  
- Strength: Well-motivated idea to use parallel documents to bridge vocabularies and denoise with LSI.  
- Strength: Simple, modular pipeline using standard building blocks (nuclear norm minimization + SVD) that is easy to implement.  
- Strength: Empirical gains across 18 English–French/German/Japanese tasks and robustness with few parallel documents.  
- Strength: Practical optimization (singular value thresholding + projection) with a sketched convergence argument.  
- Strength: Useful ablation on number of parallel documents for low-resource settings.  
- Weakness: Convergence/theory is flawed—composition of SVT and non-negativity projection is not generally the proximal operator of the full objective, so claimed guarantees are insufficient.  
- Weakness: Objective design issues—nuclear norm, ℓ1, and ρ-term all bias toward zero and may conflict; no ablation isolates necessity of ℓ1, ρ, or non-negativity.  
- Weakness: Redundant two-step reduction—matrix completion enforces low-rank yet LSI (SVD) is applied again; no “without step 2” ablation.  
- Weakness: Missing strong baselines and fairness concerns—CL-SCL and MT-based translation baselines not evaluated; competitor hyperparameters and SVM C not properly tuned.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns—per-iteration full SVD is expensive; no runtime, memory, or truncated-SVD details reported.  
- Weakness: Experimental details lacking—unclear instantiation of CL-LSI/OPCA with limited parallels, limited reproducibility details, and incomplete references.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis—no qualitative cross-lingual alignment checks, limited sensitivity analysis for k, γ, ρ, and missing variance/significance reporting.  
- Quality: Method is plausible and implemented, but optimization theory is unsound and empirical evaluation lacks stronger baselines and ablations.  
- Clarity: Generally clear motivation and notation, but algorithmic description and proof sketches are too terse and some claims need clarification.  
- Originality: Incremental—matrix completion + LSI idea is reasonable but not conceptually groundbreaking.  
- Significance: Potentially useful for small-parallel settings if stronger baselines and scalability/theoretical issues are addressed; currently moderate.  
- Suggestions: Provide correct convergence proof or reframe algorithm as proximal-splitting; ablate ℓ1, ρ, non-negativity, and the LSI step; add CL-SCL and MT baselines and tune SVM C; report runtime/memory and use truncated SVD; clarify CL-LSI/OPCA setup; add qualitative alignment analyses and per-task variances; fix references.  
- Recommendation: Score 5 (weak reject) — promising idea but theory and experimental comparison need substantial fixes.  
- Confidence: Reviewer is highly confident (expert in cross-lingual representation learning and matrix factorization).
