- Paper proposes a two-step method: build concatenated bilingual document-term matrix with missing entries, apply matrix completion, then LSI to learn cross-lingual representations; claims significant gains on Amazon review sentiment tasks.

- Technical strengths: well-defined matrix completion optimization with convergence proof and a standard, well-executed experimental setup.

- Empirical strengths: consistent, statistically significant improvements over older baselines across 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks (2.7–3.3% over CL-KCCA).

- Data-efficiency strength: method works with very few unlabeled parallel documents (as few as 200).

- Main theoretical weakness: no convincing justification that the document-term matrix is low-rank or that matrix completion is the right model for cross-lingual semantics.

- Baseline weakness: comparisons are limited to older methods (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) and omit modern neural baselines like mBERT/XLM-R.

- Parameter-selection flaw: hyperparameters tuned on a single task (EFB) and applied to all tasks, risking suboptimal performance.

- Scalability concern: paper omits discussion of computational complexity of matrix completion, raising practicality issues for large datasets.

- Missing analysis: lacks qualitative inspection of learned representations (e.g., top words per semantic dimension) and ablation showing the matrix completion step’s impact.

- Clarity: generally well-written and precise, but needs clearer motivation for matrix completion before LSI, a simple illustrative example, and plots showing performance vs. amount of parallel data.

- Originality: idea of applying matrix completion is somewhat novel but distinction from CL-LSI is unclear without ablation/theoretical support.

- Significance: addresses an important low-resource cross-lingual problem and shows robustness, but limited by BOW focus and lack of neural comparisons and scalability discussion.

- Recommendation summary: technically sound and promising but insufficiently novel/justified and missing modern baselines; reviewer recommends weak rejection.

- Suggested improvements: add comparisons to neural methods (mBERT/XLM-R), run ablation (with/without matrix completion), use robust cross-validation for params, analyze complexity/scalability, include qualitative visualizations, and strengthen theoretical justification for low-rank assumption.
