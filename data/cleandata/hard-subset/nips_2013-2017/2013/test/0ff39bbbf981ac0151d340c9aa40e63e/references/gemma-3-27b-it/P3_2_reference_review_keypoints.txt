- Overall: Conceptually sound two-step method (matrix completion then LSI) with strong empirical improvements; suitable for NIPS.  
- Problem & method: Addresses cross-lingual text classification by completing a partially observed document-term matrix from parallel documents, then applying LSI for cross-lingual representations.  
- Positioning: Avoids heavy translation and differs from prior parallel-data methods by performing matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.  
- Empirical strength: Convincing results on multilingual Amazon reviews, consistently outperforming strong baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).  
- Novelty: The combination of matrix completion and LSI for cross-lingual learning is novel and well-motivated.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Presents a projected gradient descent solver with convergence analysis for the matrix completion step.  
- Parallel-data analysis: Thorough exploration of how varying parallel corpus size affects performance, showing robustness with limited parallel data.  
- Presentation: Well-written, clear mathematical formulation and algorithm explanation.  
- Trace-norm choice: Limited discussion/justification for using trace-norm regularization over other matrix completion options.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Lacks sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (γ, ρ, k).  
- Computational cost: No analysis or empirical comparison of computational complexity or runtimes versus baselines.  
- Dataset diversity: Experiments confined to Amazon reviews; broader datasets (news, scientific text) are missing.  
- Failure modes: Missing discussion of scenarios where the method may struggle (e.g., very dissimilar language pairs).  
- Acceptance pros: Strong empirical gains, well-motivated novelty, solid theoretical/algorithmic work, clear presentation.  
- Acceptance cons: Incomplete discussion on matrix completion choices/hyperparams, no complexity analysis, limited dataset diversity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add hyperparameter sensitivity and computational complexity discussion.
