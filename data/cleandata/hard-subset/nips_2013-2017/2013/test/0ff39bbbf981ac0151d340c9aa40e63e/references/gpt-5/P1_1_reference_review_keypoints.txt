- Summary: Proposes a two-step cross-lingual document representation: convex matrix completion on a concatenated bilingual doc-term matrix (trace-norm + l1 + nonnegativity + observed/missing losses) via projected gradient with shrinkage, then LSI+linear SVM; shows gains on Amazon sentiment transfers (EN/FR/DE/JP) and robustness with few parallel pairs.

- Novelty/Relation: Novel to explicitly complete missing cross-lingual entries before LSI; connects to CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA, SCL, and matrix-completion literature.

- Strength: Principled convex objective combining low-rank, sparsity and nonnegativity with clear algorithmic formulation and a correct convergence argument.

- Strength: Simple, understandable projected-gradient-with-shrinkage algorithm and convergence proof using non-expansive operator arguments.

- Strength: Empirical improvements over CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA on 18 transfer tasks and robustness when only ~200 parallel pairs are available.

- Strength: Sensible experimental protocol (single-task hyperparameter tuning reused, means/std over splits).

- Weakness: Questionable low-rank assumption—document-term matrices are typically high-rank; need justification or ablation showing trace-norm utility.

- Weakness: Objective design potentially redundant (nonnegativity + l1 becomes uniform shrinkage; missing-entry penalty ρ||M||F^2 may overlap with µ), requiring µ/ρ ablations.

- Weakness: Scalability concerns—per-iteration SVD for shrinkage is expensive; unclear whether truncated SVD/rank truncation is used and what runtimes/memory are.

- Weakness: Transductive setup uses all (including test) documents for representation learning; must be made explicit and compared to inductive scenarios.

- Weakness: Limited baselines—missing modern embedding-based or translation-based baselines that don’t require parallel data.

- Weakness: Narrow evaluation—only sentiment on reviews; need other tasks or feature variants (binary/TF vs TF-IDF) to show generality.

- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps—no code, seeds, stopping criteria, SVD implementation details, or extensive parameter sensitivity reported.

- Quality: Optimization setup and convergence proof are sound, but no theoretical recovery guarantees and empirical ablations are lacking.

- Clarity: Well-written overall; needs explicit M0 construction, statement of transductive use of test docs, per-iteration complexity/stopping criteria, and wording fixes (e.g., “naturally low-rank”).

- Originality: Conceptually novel combination (matrix completion then LSI) relative to classic CL methods; optimization techniques are standard.

- Significance: Potentially practically valuable for low-parallel-data regimes, but impact limited by missing modern baselines and narrow experiments.

- Pros for acceptance: principled method with convergence proof, consistent empirical gains, robustness with few parallel pairs, and an easily adoptable pipeline.

- Cons for acceptance: limited baselines/domains, unclear scalability, insufficient ablations, and transductive evaluation risks overstating inductive performance.

- Questions for authors: How do results change with µ=0 and/or ρ=0 or without nonnegativity? Do you use truncated SVD and what are ranks/runtimes? What if representation learning excludes target test docs? How sensitive are k, γ, ρ across tasks?

- Recommendation: Borderline accept—interesting and well-executed but needs stronger baselines, ablations, and scalability/generalization evidence to be a clear accept.
