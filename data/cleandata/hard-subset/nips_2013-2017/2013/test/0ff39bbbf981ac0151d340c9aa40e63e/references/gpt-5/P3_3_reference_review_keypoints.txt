- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual text classification: nuclear-norm matrix completion on a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI + linear SVM.
- Uses a small set of unlabeled parallel documents to fully observe parallel rows and treat others as partially missing.
- Formulates completion as convex optimization with nuclear norm, ℓ1 sparsity, squared loss on observed entries, a small penalty on missing entries, and non-negativity constraints.
- Solves the optimization via projected gradient descent with singular value shrinkage and non-negativity projection, and provides a convergence argument.
- Empirically evaluated on Amazon reviews (EN/FR/DE/JA), outperforming CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA, and TBOW, reportedly robust with as few as 200 parallel docs.
- Related to CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA and multilingual topic models; novelty is imputing cross-lingual structure before LSI rather than applying LSI/CCA directly.
- Omits empirical comparison to SCL and discussion of modern cross-lingual representations (bilingual embeddings, multilingual transformers).
- Strength: conceptually simple and intuitive framing of cross-lingual alignment as feature imputation then compression.
- Strength: works with very small parallel corpora and shows consistent empirical gains across 18 tasks.
- Strength: convex relaxation with reasonable regularizers and clear data/loss formulation.
- Major concern: convergence proof is incomplete—composition of proximal operators for nuclear norm plus non-negativity is not generally valid.
- Concern: claim that document-term matrices are low-rank is overstated; nuclear norm likely acts as a denoiser rather than exploiting inherent low rank.
- Concern: missing methodological ablations (effects of µ/ℓ1, non-negativity, ρ, necessity of LSI step, SVM on M* vs Z).
- Concern: computational scalability not addressed—per-iteration SVD cost, lack of truncated/randomized SVD details, runtimes, and convergence diagnostics.
- Concern: evaluation appears transductive (uses all documents for representation learning); need results using only train + parallel unlabeled data.
- Concern: missing strong baselines (SCL, translate-train/test with MT, bilingual embedding projection), and no statistical significance reporting.
- Quality: empirical results promising but theoretical proof and experimental rigor are insufficient.
- Clarity: writing is mostly clear but minor notation/typos and missing algorithmic complexity details should be fixed.
- Originality: interesting combination of known components (matrix completion + LSI) but novelty limited to the specific pipeline.
- Significance: potentially useful for low-resource cross-lingual classification if robustness and efficiency are validated.
- Questions for authors: provide correct convergence guarantee or switch to ADMM/primal-dual, ablations for non-negativity/ℓ1, runtime/SVD details, transductive vs inductive performance, and comparison of SVM on M* vs Z plus missing baselines.
- Recommendation: borderline reject—promising idea but needs stronger optimization theory, ablations, scalability analysis, and baseline comparisons before acceptance.
