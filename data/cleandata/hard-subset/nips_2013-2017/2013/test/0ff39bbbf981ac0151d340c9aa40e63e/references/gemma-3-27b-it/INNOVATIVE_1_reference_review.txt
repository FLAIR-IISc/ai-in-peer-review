This paper proposes a two-step method for cross-lingual representation learning, leveraging unlabeled parallel data via matrix completion and latent semantic indexing (LSI). The core idea – utilizing matrix completion to bridge the vocabulary gap and then applying LSI – is novel and potentially impactful. It addresses a crucial challenge in cross-lingual learning: effectively exploiting limited parallel resources. The paper is well-structured, clearly outlining the problem, related work, proposed approach, and experimental setup. The experimental results on Amazon reviews demonstrate significant improvements over several baselines, suggesting the method’s effectiveness.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of matrix completion and LSI for cross-lingual representation learning is a genuinely fresh approach. While matrix completion and LSI are individually established techniques, their application in this specific two-step framework for cross-lingual transfer is new.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The consistently superior performance across various cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks, especially with limited parallel data, provides strong evidence of the method’s efficacy. The ablation study (impact of parallel data size) is particularly valuable.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The mathematical formulation is reasonably clear, and the algorithm is presented in a digestible manner.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The method addresses a practical problem – reducing the need for large labeled datasets in target languages – which is important for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Optimization Details:** The projected gradient descent algorithm, while described, lacks crucial implementation details. The choice of step size (τ), regularization parameters (γ, ρ, µ), and the convergence criteria aren’t fully justified.  More specifics are needed to facilitate reproducibility.
*   **Scalability Concerns:** The paper mentions scalability as a concern but doesn’t provide extensive empirical validation on very large datasets. While experiments are conducted on a reasonable scale, the runtime performance of the matrix completion step for significantly larger vocabularies and document collections remains unclear.
*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** The theoretical justification for the choice of a low-rank approximation and the effectiveness of matrix completion in this specific cross-lingual context is somewhat limited. A more rigorous analysis of the assumptions underlying the method would strengthen the contribution.
*   **Baseline Comparison:** While the comparisons are generally favorable, some baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) are somewhat dated. Including more recent state-of-the-art cross-lingual methods would provide a more robust benchmark.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The novelty of the approach is significant.  The combination of matrix completion and LSI to address cross-lingual transfer is a compelling idea.
*   The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods, particularly in low-resource settings.
*   The potential impact of reducing the reliance on labeled data for cross-lingual tasks is considerable.
*   The paper is well-written and presents the ideas in a clear and organized manner.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of detailed implementation specifics for the optimization algorithm hinders reproducibility.
*   The theoretical justification for the approach could be strengthened.
*   The scalability of the method to very large datasets remains an open question.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a novel and promising approach to cross-lingual representation learning. While some aspects of the implementation and theoretical analysis could be strengthened, the strong empirical results and the originality of the idea make it a valuable contribution to the field. I believe the potential impact of this work justifies acceptance, despite the minor weaknesses.  I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the reproducibility concerns and perhaps a brief discussion of potential scalability limitations. The reviewers should request authors provide more details regarding the implementation and justification for the parameter choices.  



**Rating:** Accept with minor revisions.
