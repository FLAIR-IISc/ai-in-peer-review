- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated two-step method (matrix completion + LSI) with empirical gains; recommend Accept with Minor Revisions.
- Problem & approach: addresses cross-lingual text classification using unlabeled parallel documents, constructing and completing a concatenated document-term matrix then applying LSI.
- Related work: compares appropriately to CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA but needs positioning against recent multilingual pre-trained models.
- Strength: clear motivation and problem statement emphasizing reduced labeled data in target language.
- Strength: sound methodology—matrix completion addresses sparsity and LSI provides meaningful dimensionality reduction.
- Strength: empirical validation—consistent improvements on Amazon reviews across language pairs and categories.
- Strength: scalable optimization—projected gradient descent algorithm with convergence analysis is practical for large-scale data.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments across multiple language pairs and categories improving generalizability.
- Weakness: incremental novelty—combines established techniques without a paradigm shift or clear differentiation from prior factorization methods.
- Weakness: lack of justification for key design choices and parameter settings (γ, ρ, τ, k) and missing ablation studies.
- Weakness: limited discussion of method limitations and sensitivity to parallel data quality, noise, bias, or low vocabulary overlap.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on the source, quality, and preprocessing of unlabeled parallel data.
- Weakness: presentation verbosity and redundancy; Algorithm 1 feels misplaced and could be moved to the appendix.
- Detailed question: need stronger motivation for using ℓ1 norm for sparsity and whether alternatives were considered.
- Detailed question: provide computational complexity analysis of the projected gradient descent algorithm.
- Detailed question: justify the choice of 100 labeled target documents—was this from sensitivity analysis or pilot experiments?
- Detailed suggestion: report p-values/statistical tests for performance differences shown in Figures 1 & 2.
- Detailed suggestion: expand related work to include pre-trained multilingual models (mBERT, XLM-RoBERTa) and broader cross-lingual transfer literature.
- Pro for acceptance: demonstrable empirical improvements, practical implementation, thorough evaluation, and scalable algorithm.
- Con for acceptance: incremental novelty, weak justification for design choices, and limited discussion of limitations.
- Recommendation actions: strengthen justification of parameters, discuss limitations/future work, detail parallel data, include recent multilingual models in related work, and streamline presentation.
