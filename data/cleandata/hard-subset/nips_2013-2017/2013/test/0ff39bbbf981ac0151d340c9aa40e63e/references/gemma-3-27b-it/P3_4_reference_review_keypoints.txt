- Overall: Conceptually sound two-step method (matrix completion then LSI) with strong results; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Method summary: Fill missing entries in a partially observed cross-lingual document-term matrix via matrix completion, then apply LSI for cross-lingual representations without heavy MT reliance.  
- Relation to prior work: Differentiates from MT-based and direct-dimension-reduction approaches by performing matrix completion before LSI and connects to matrix completion literature.  
- Strength: Sound methodology addressing sparsity and cross-lingual alignment.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance on Amazon reviews, outperforming several baselines (TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).  
- Strength: Robustness to amount of parallel data—maintains good performance even with limited parallel resources.  
- Strength: Clear presentation, well-explained math, Algorithm 1, and convergence analysis.  
- Strength: Thorough comparisons against diverse baselines, strengthening empirical claims.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—combination is effective but individual components are not new.  
- Weakness: Parameter sensitivity—γ, ρ, and k selection rationale is unclear and reported tuning only on one task.  
- Weakness: Computational cost and scalability concerns—matrix completion can be expensive and resource usage is not reported.  
- Weakness: Limited error analysis—paper reports overall accuracy but lacks analysis of specific error types.  
- Detailed comment: Justify choice of ℓ1 norm for sparsity and consider alternatives (e.g., ℓ0 approximations).  
- Detailed comment: Clarify choice and sensitivity of step size τ in the projected gradient descent.  
- Detailed comment: Explain experimental setup and whether same parameters were used across all 18 tasks.  
- Detailed comment: Discuss large error bars in Figures 1 & 2 and potential sources of variability.  
- Detailed comment: Provide computational complexity discussion, particularly for the matrix completion step.  
- For acceptance: Paper shows clear empirical improvement, sound motivation, good writing, and convergence guarantees.  
- Against acceptance: Incremental novelty, opaque parameter selection, and missing scalability discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; request added parameter sensitivity analysis, discussion of computational cost/scalability, and a brief error analysis.
