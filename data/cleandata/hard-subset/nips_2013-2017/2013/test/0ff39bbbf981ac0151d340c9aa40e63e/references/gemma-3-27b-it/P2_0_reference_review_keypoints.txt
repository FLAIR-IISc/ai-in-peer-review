- Paper proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion on concatenated bilingual document-term matrix followed by LSI to induce cross-lingual representations.
- Experiments on Amazon product review sentiment classification show the method outperforms TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA, even with limited parallel data.
- Positioning: contrasts with MT-based and direct bilingual-resource methods, emphasizing efficient use of limited unlabeled parallel data and matrix completion preprocessing.
- Novelty: combining matrix completion with LSI addresses sparsity from limited parallel corpora and is a reasonable new approach.
- Empirical strength: consistent and significant performance gains, notably with only 100 labeled target documents.
- Robustness: method performs well even with as few as 200 unlabeled parallel documents.
- Reproducibility: Algorithm 1 clearly describes the projected gradient descent procedure.
- Related work: literature coverage is thorough and well-positioned.
- Theoretical gap: lacks rigorous analysis explaining why low-rank matrix completion is appropriate for cross-lingual document-term matrices.
- Parameter sensitivity: insufficient detail and systematic exploration of key parameters (γ, ρ, k); sensitivity analysis is missing.
- Dataset limitation: evaluation only on Amazon product reviews raises generalizability concerns across domains and text types.
- Baseline comparison: missing evaluation against more recent strong baselines (e.g., multilingual pre-trained models like mBERT/XLM-R).
- Optimization details: implementation specifics (e.g., stopping criteria for projected gradient descent) are not provided.
- For acceptance: novel, effective approach with strong empirical results and clear presentation addressing a relevant problem.
- Against acceptance: limited theoretical justification, narrow dataset scope, and lack of comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Overall verdict: strong accept with minor revisions recommended.
- Suggested improvements: expand theoretical analysis, provide systematic parameter tuning and implementation details, evaluate on diverse datasets, and compare to stronger recent baselines.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 7/10.
