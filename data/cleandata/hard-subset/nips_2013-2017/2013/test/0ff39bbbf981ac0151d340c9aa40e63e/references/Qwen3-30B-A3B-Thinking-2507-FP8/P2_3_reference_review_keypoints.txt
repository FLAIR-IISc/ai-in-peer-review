- Proposal: Two-step cross-lingual method—matrix completion to fill missing language vocabularies followed by LSI—evaluated on 18 sentiment tasks across EN/FR/DE/JP; outperforms baselines by 8.59–14.61% and works with only 200 parallel docs.
- Quality: Strong technical rigor with a well-motivated matrix completion formulation, convergence proof for the optimizer, and thorough experimental design and metrics.
- Originality: Novel use of matrix completion prior to LSI, differentiating it from prior CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA approaches.
- Significance: Practically important—substantially reduces required parallel data and yields large accuracy gains, useful for low-resource languages.
- Clarity: Well-organized, clearly written, and experimentally reproducible, though matrix construction could be slightly clearer.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why matrix completion improves over direct LSI on partially observed matrices.
- Weakness: Omits comparisons to recent neural cross-lingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), limiting context of relative performance.
- Weakness: No discussion of computational complexity or scalability of the matrix completion optimization.
- Weakness: Missing language-pair-specific analysis to explain which languages benefit most and why.
- Overall assessment: High-quality, novel, empirically strong paper with practical value; recommended for acceptance subject to addressing theoretical, comparative, and efficiency analyses.
