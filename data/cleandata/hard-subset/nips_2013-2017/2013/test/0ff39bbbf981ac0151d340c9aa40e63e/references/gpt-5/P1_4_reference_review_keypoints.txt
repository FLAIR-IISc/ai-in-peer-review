- Summary: Two-step method—convex matrix completion on a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix to impute missing cross-lingual entries, then apply LSI (SVD) to learned matrix and train a linear SVM.
- Optimization: Uses projected gradient descent with singular value shrinkage and nonnegativity projection; convergence argued via non-expansive operator composition.
- Empirics: Evaluated on multilingual Amazon reviews (EN/FR/DE/JA), 18 transfer tasks with 100 labeled target examples, outperforming TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA and robust to few parallel pairs.
- Relation to prior work: Connects to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA, bilingual SCL and trace-norm minimization literature; novelty is explicit matrix completion before LSI.
- Originality: Novel combination of trace-norm completion on bilingual doc-term matrices followed by spectral reduction, though related methods (NMF tri-factorization, collective MF, SCL) are close.
- Technical soundness: Formulation is clear and convex, algorithm resembles proximal gradient, but the convergence proof for the composite nonsmooth objective is incomplete.
- Convergence concern: Applying prox of nuclear norm then nonnegativity projection does not generally guarantee minimization of the composite objective; stronger proof or alternative scheme (e.g., ADMM) is needed.
- Modeling choices: Use of nuclear norm + l1 (linear under nonnegativity) + l2 shrinkage on unobserved entries is unconventional and requires justification.
- Ablation gap: No ablation studies isolating effects of matrix completion, l1 term, or ρ shrinkage; these are needed to justify modeling choices.
- Baseline gap: Missing comparisons to SCL (pivot features), NMF tri-factorization, and translation-based baselines.
- Scalability gap: No runtime, memory, or SVD implementation details; unclear applicability to larger vocabularies/datasets.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written but missing stopping criteria, SVD details (full vs truncated/randomized), per-iteration complexity, and exact numbers in tables/figures.
- Theoretical clarity: Need to clarify treatment of l1 term under nonnegativity and its implications for Lipschitz/step-size assumptions.
- Intuition gap: Could provide examples of recovered cross-lingual co-occurrence patterns to illustrate what completion learns.
- Significance: Practical contribution if convergence and ablations are addressed—reduces reliance on large parallel corpora and shows consistent gains on a standard benchmark.
- Pros: Simple, modular approach; convex formulation with reasonable inductive biases; strong empirical gains; fixed hyperparameter protocol across tasks.
- Cons: Incomplete convergence guarantees; missing ablations and broader baselines; unclear scalability; evaluation limited to sentiment domain.
- Questions for authors: Provide rigorous convergence guarantee or switch to a proven proximal scheme; report sensitivity to ρ and µ; give runtime/memory and SVD method; quantify gain from completion vs CL-LSI; compare to SCL and NMF tri-factorization.
- Minor issues: Typos/formatting errors, missing norm squares, and lack of precise data splits and stopping criteria for reproducibility.
- Recommendation: Weak accept (6/10) contingent on tightening convergence analysis and adding at least an ablation isolating the contribution of the completion step.
