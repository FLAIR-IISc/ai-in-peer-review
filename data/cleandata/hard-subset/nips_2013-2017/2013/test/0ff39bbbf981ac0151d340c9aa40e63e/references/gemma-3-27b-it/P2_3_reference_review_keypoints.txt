- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion on concatenated bilingual document-term matrix followed by LSI to derive cross-lingual representations.  
- Demonstrates improved cross-lingual sentiment classification on Amazon reviews, outperforming TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA.  
- Key finding: method remains robust even with few parallel documents.  
- Positions work relative to machine translation, bilingual lexicon, and shared-representation approaches, highlighting matrix completion as the novel pre-LSI step.  
- Novelty: combining matrix completion with LSI to handle sparsity/missing data across languages is a fresh contribution.  
- Empirical strength: thorough evaluation and ablation study on parallel-data size showing consistent improvements.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear method description, mathematical formulation, and helpful Algorithm 1.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a brief convergence analysis for the optimization algorithm.  
- Practical impact: addressing robustness to small amounts of parallel data tackles a key limitation of many cross-lingual methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for the low-rank assumption in matrix completion specific to cross-lingual similarity.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed parameter sensitivity analysis for γ, ρ, and k.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to a single dataset (Amazon reviews), reducing generalizability claims.  
- Weakness: omits discussion of computational complexity and scalability of Algorithm 1.  
- Weakness: does not compare against modern pre-trained multilingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), missing stronger baselines.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with compelling empirical results, clear presentation, and added theoretical rigor.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need stronger matrix-completion justification, parameter sensitivity analysis, broader evaluation, and comparisons to state-of-the-art multilingual models.  
- Recommendation: accept (borderline) with minor revisions to (1) justify low-rank assumption, (2) add parameter sensitivity analysis, (3) discuss computational complexity, and (4) include comparisons to recent multilingual models where possible.
