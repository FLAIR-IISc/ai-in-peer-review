- Proposed two-step method: build a unified document-term matrix from unlabeled parallel bilingual docs, complete it via matrix completion, then apply LSI to obtain cross-lingual representations.  
- Empirical claim: outperforms CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA on Amazon product review sentiment tasks, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Related work: clearly positioned against MT, domain adaptation, and other bilingual representation methods; novelty is doing matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.  
- Strength — Novelty: two-step matrix completion then LSI is a principled way to fill sparse cross-lingual data and derive robust representations.  
- Strength — Empirical: strong, consistent gains across several cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks.  
- Strength — Experiments: comprehensive evaluation including ablation studies on amount of parallel data.  
- Strength — Theory/optimization: clear problem formulation and a projected gradient descent algorithm with convergence analysis.  
- Strength — Writing: generally well-written and organized, making technical details accessible.  
- Weakness — Optimization details: Algorithm 1 lacks crucial implementation specifics (step size τ, shrinkage operator), raising reproducibility concerns.  
- Weakness — Computational cost: scalability and runtime are not addressed; matrix completion may be expensive on large vocabularies/doc collections.  
- Weakness — Low-rank assumption: claim that the full document-term matrix is “naturally low-rank and sparse” needs stronger justification for cross-lingual data.  
- Weakness — Limited baselines: missing comparisons to recent multilingual pre-trained models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R) which represent current SOTA.  
- Weakness — Loss choice: using least-squares loss for matrix completion may be sub-optimal; more robust loss functions were not explored.  
- Quality rating: 8/10 — technically sound with strong empirical support but missing implementation and scalability discussion.  
- Clarity rating: 7/10 — generally clear but mathematically dense and lacking some optimization/parameter explanations.  
- Originality rating: 8/10 — novel application and sequencing of matrix completion and LSI for cross-lingual rep learning.  
- Significance rating: 8/10 — addresses an important problem and is useful when target-language labels are scarce and parallel corpora exist.  
- Pros for acceptance: demonstrable performance gains, novel and motivated approach, thorough experiments, theoretical convergence support.  
- Cons against acceptance: reproducibility issues, unaddressed computational scalability, and absence of comparisons to modern multilingual baselines.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add implementation details, discuss scalability/runtime, and include comparisons to recent multilingual pre-trained models.
