- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion on a concatenated document-term matrix with missing entries, followed by LSI to produce cross-lingual representations.  
- Evaluated on Amazon cross-lingual sentiment tasks, showing significant improvements over several baselines and robustness with limited parallel data.  
- Positions work relative to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA; key novelty is performing matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.  
- Related work section is well-structured and appropriately cites relevant prior methods.  
- Novel combination of matrix completion and LSI is a reasonable and potentially effective approach for incomplete cross-lingual matrices.  
- Empirical results are strong and consistent, with useful ablation on parallel data size.  
- Provides theoretical grounding: convex relaxation, projected gradient descent algorithm, and convergence analysis.  
- Paper is clearly written and the two-step process is explained concisely.  
- Method is practical, performing well with relatively small amounts of parallel data.  
- Limited theoretical depth: insufficient explanation why low-rank matrix completion is a valid assumption for this problem.  
- Algorithm complexity and scalability are under-discussed; lacks concrete computational cost, memory, and large-scale runtime evidence.  
- Convergence analysis lacks empirical demonstration of actual convergence speed in practice.  
- Parameter sensitivity (γ, ρ, k) is not thoroughly analyzed; robustness to hyperparameters is unclear.  
- Comparisons omit stronger modern baselines like pre-trained multilingual models (mBERT, XLM-R).  
- No detailed error analysis to characterize types of classification mistakes.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical gains, novel well-motivated approach, clear presentation, and theoretical analysis add credibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical justification of core assumption, missing scalability details, limited hyperparameter analysis, and lack of comparison to modern baselines.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: expand theoretical rationale for matrix completion, detail computational complexity/scalability, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, and include comparisons to pre-trained multilingual models.
