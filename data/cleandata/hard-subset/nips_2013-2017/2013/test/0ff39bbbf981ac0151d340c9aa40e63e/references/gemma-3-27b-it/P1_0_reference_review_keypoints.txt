- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion on a concatenated document-term matrix followed by LSI to obtain cross-lingual representations.
- Leverages a small set of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents to bridge vocabulary gaps rather than translating full datasets.
- Positions work relative to translation, domain adaptation, multi-view learning, and bilingual-resource methods, emphasizing matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.
- Novelty: first to combine matrix completion with LSI for cross-lingual representation learning.
- Empirical performance: shows significant and consistent improvements on cross-lingual sentiment classification across language pairs and categories.
- Practical advantage: effective even with very limited parallel data (e.g., ~200 parallel documents).
- Optimization: includes a detailed projected gradient descent algorithm with convergence analysis.
- Weak rationale: insufficient justification for assuming low-rank structure of the document-term matrix.
- Parameter sensitivity: lacks comprehensive analysis of how γ, ρ, and k affect performance and tuning procedures.
- Scalability: limited discussion and evidence on computational complexity and memory requirements for large-scale datasets.
- Baselines: comparisons omit some recent state-of-the-art cross-lingual methods, weakening empirical claims.
- Presentation: figures could be more informative (e.g., add error bars on plots); tables are clear.
- Quality: technically sound formulation and empirical support, but needs more analysis on sensitivity and scalability.
- Clarity: generally well-written and organized, though some optimization sections are dense and could be simplified.
- Originality: clear novel contribution by focusing on unlabeled parallel data and the two-step pipeline.
- Significance: potentially valuable for real-world tasks by reducing reliance on costly annotations and extensive parallel corpora.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, strong empirical results, practical relevance, and solid theoretical analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak justification of low-rank assumption, insufficient parameter/scalability analysis, and missing recent baselines.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to justify matrix completion, analyze parameter sensitivity, and address scalability.
