- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual method: concatenate document-term matrices with missing entries, perform matrix completion, then apply LSI for representations.  
- Positions itself relative to prior cross-lingual methods (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) and emphasizes completing the matrix before dimensionality reduction.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements on Amazon cross-lingual sentiment classification and robustness to limited parallel data.  
- Ablation study on parallel-data size is valuable and shows method effectiveness with scarce parallel resources.  
- Authors provide a brief theoretical framing of matrix completion as an optimization problem and a concise convergence analysis.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized, making the approach easy to follow.  
- Theoretical discussion is shallow; more analysis is needed on why matrix completion specifically improves LSI representations.  
- Parameter selection is insufficiently detailed; unclear how settings generalize beyond the evaluated dataset.  
- Lacks comparison to recent strong baselines using pre-trained multilingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM‑R), which are current state-of-the-art.  
- Computational cost and scalability of the matrix completion step are not addressed, raising practical concerns for large datasets.  
- No error analysis or examination of failure modes to explain when/why the method succeeds or fails.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, solid empirical gains, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: superficial theory, incomplete baseline comparisons, missing scalability discussion, and limited error analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on addressing stronger baselines, scalability discussion, and deeper theoretical justification.
