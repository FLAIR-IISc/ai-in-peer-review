- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: build concatenated document-term matrix with missing entries, perform matrix completion to impute, then apply LSI; evaluated on Amazon sentiment data with limited parallel data.

- Overall assessment: sensible and well-executed with consistent gains, but limited theoretical novelty and modest empirical improvements.

- Quality (7/10): solid use of established techniques and detailed optimization/convergence analysis, but weak theoretical justification for the specific matrix completion/regularization and opaque parameter selection.

- Clarity (7/10): generally clear and organized; Section 4 (Optimization) is dense and some design choices (e.g., ℓ1 norm) need clearer motivation; experimental setup could be more concise.

- Originality (6/10): incremental contribution by combining known methods; insufficient discussion of relations to prior techniques like CCA.

- Significance (7/10): tackles an important problem and shows consistent improvements (notably with limited parallel data), but gains are not large and practical advantages over simpler methods are unclear.

- Strengths: clear motivation, solid experimental validation, superior performance to baselines, detailed optimization/convergence analysis, robustness with limited parallel data.

- Weaknesses: limited theoretical novelty, reliance on existing techniques, arbitrary-seeming regularization choices, unclear parameter selection process, modest performance gains, limited discussion of computational complexity/scalability.

- Pro arguments: relevant NLP problem, technically sound, empirically validated improvements, convergence analysis strengthens credibility.

- Con arguments: incremental for a top venue, non-transformative gains, potential computational cost limits scalability.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on better justification of matrix completion choice, clearer parameter-selection details, and discussion of computational complexity/scalability.
