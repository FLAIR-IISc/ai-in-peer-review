- Two-step method: convex matrix completion on concatenated bilingual doc-term matrix (trace norm + ℓ1 + nonnegativity + weighted squared loss) followed by LSI (SVD) and linear SVM.  
- Optimization: solved with projected gradient descent alternating gradient, singular value shrinkage, and nonnegativity projection; a convergence argument is given.  
- Empirical results: outperforms TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA across 18 Amazon review transfer tasks and is robust with few parallel pairs.  
- Novelty: imputation of the unified doc-term matrix via matrix completion before LSI is a novel architectural twist versus applying LSI/KCCA/OPCA directly.  
- Modeling strengths: objective is interpretable—nuclear norm for low-rank co-occurrence, ℓ1+nonnegativity for sparsity, principled use of parallel pairs.  
- Major optimization concern: sequential prox (shrinkage) then projection is not generally the prox of the sum; the proof does not show fixed points equal minimizers of the composite constrained objective.  
- Recommended fix: clarify theoretical guarantee or adopt a splitting method (e.g., Douglas–Rachford/ADMM) that properly handles two nonsmooth terms.  
- Notation/theory issues: ambiguous loss c(x,y) (likely 1/2‖x−y‖^2), unclear norms, and no stated convergence rate (e.g., O(1/k)) for the smooth+nonsmooth case.  
- Empirical gaps: missing direct ablation “LSI on raw vs LSI on completed M,” no runtime/scalability analysis despite repeated SVDs, and transductive training (representations learned including test docs).  
- Baseline gaps: lacks common baselines such as translate-train/test using MT and modern bilingual embeddings.  
- Clarity: generally clear but needs fixes—clarify c(x,y), norms, initialization/stopping, choice of τ,γ,ρ, sensitivity plots, and exact instantiation of CL-LSI/CL-OPCA baselines.  
- Originality: moderate—novel combination in this setting, but uses well-known components (nuclear-norm completion, LSI).  
- Significance: empirically meaningful improvements, but impact would be stronger with tighter theoretical guarantees, stronger/modern baselines, and runtime evidence.  
- Pros: simple, reusable pipeline; consistent gains across many tasks; leverages weak supervision (unlabeled parallel pairs).  
- Cons: incomplete convergence treatment, transductive evaluation only, missing ablations and stronger baselines, no computational cost analysis, and some notational imprecision.  
- Questions for authors: provide correct convergence guarantee or use splitting; ablate matrix completion vs LSI; report wall-clock/memory vs CL-LSI/CL-KCCA; sensitivity of γ,ρ,μ,k and principled ρ; can method be made inductive?  
- Overall assessment: promising and conceptually appealing but borderline—likely acceptable if theoretical/experimental gaps (optimization proof, ablations, baselines, runtime) are addressed, otherwise weak reject.
