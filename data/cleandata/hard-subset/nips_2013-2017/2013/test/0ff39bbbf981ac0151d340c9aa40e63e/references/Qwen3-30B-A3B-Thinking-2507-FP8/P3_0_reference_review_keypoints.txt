- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion to fill missing entries in a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI for cross-lingual representations.  
- Claims strong empirical gains, especially with very limited parallel data (as few as 200 documents).  
- Provides a thorough convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent optimization.  
- Experiments are extensive: 18 cross-language sentiment classification tasks on Amazon reviews across four languages and sensitivity analyses.  
- Core contribution (matrix completion + LSI) is not clearly differentiated from prior work like CL-LSI that uses a similar concatenated matrix.  
- No ablation study to isolate the effect of the matrix completion step versus applying LSI directly on the incomplete matrix.  
- Fails to compare against modern neural cross-lingual methods (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), a major omission given their state-of-the-art status.  
- Theoretical justification for the low-rank assumption of the document-term matrix is weak and unsupported in the cross-lingual context.  
- Computational complexity and scalability are not discussed, raising concerns for real-world large-scale use.  
- Motivation for using matrix completion over simpler missing-data strategies (mean/nearest-neighbor imputation) is inadequately explained.  
- The distinction between the proposed method and CL-LSI remains unclear in presentation and justification.  
- The choice and justification for ℓ1 norm and trace norm regularization terms are not well argued.  
- Parameter selection is based on a single task and sensitivity across language pairs is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- The combination of known techniques is somewhat incremental and not highly novel.  
- Reported improvements (8.59%–14.61% over baseline) are meaningful but not transformative for the field.  
- Significance is limited by the prevalence and superiority of neural approaches for cross-lingual tasks.  
- Paper would be stronger with comparisons to neural baselines, ablation studies, stronger theoretical motivation, and discussion of limitations.  
- Specific suggested improvements: add neural baselines (e.g., XLM-R), perform ablations comparing with LSI on incomplete and simply imputed matrices, justify matrix completion theoretically, and discuss failure cases and scalability.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and well-executed experiments but incremental, missing critical comparisons and analyses; reviewer recommendation: Weak Reject.
