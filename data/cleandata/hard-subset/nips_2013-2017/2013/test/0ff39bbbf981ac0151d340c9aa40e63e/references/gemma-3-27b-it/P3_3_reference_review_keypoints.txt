- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; solid engineering contribution with clear practical benefits.  
- Proposed method: two-step approach—matrix completion on concatenated document-term matrix followed by LSI for cross-lingual representation.  
- Motivation: addresses scarce labeled data in target language by leveraging unlabeled parallel documents to “fill in” missing information.  
- Relation to prior work: positions against translation, bilingual lexicons, and other cross-lingual dimensionality reduction methods, and connects to matrix completion literature.  
- Empirical results: strong improvements on Amazon product review dataset and consistent gains across multiple language pairs.  
- Ablation study: analyses impact of parallel data size, showing robustness to varying amounts of parallel data.  
- Presentation: well-written, clear notation, and straightforward algorithm description.  
- Scalability: acknowledges challenges and proposes a projected gradient descent algorithm (Algorithm 1) for practical implementation.  
- Robustness: consistent performance gains suggest method generalizes across language pairs.  
- Theoretical weakness: lacks deeper theoretical justification for using matrix completion and for assuming low-rank structure of the document-term matrix.  
- Parameter sensitivity: parameter selection appears ad-hoc with limited discussion of variability across language pairs or datasets.  
- Algorithmic detail: convergence analysis cites lemmas without fully outlining them and omits practical convergence criteria used in implementation.  
- Baseline comparison: limited—missing comparisons to recent transformer-based cross-lingual models (e.g., multilingual BERT).  
- Recommendations: expand theoretical discussion, run parameter sensitivity analysis, summarize key lemmas and practical convergence criteria, clarify nonnegative projection in Algorithm 1, and consider stronger baselines.  
- Acceptance pros: strong empirical results, well-motivated approach, good writing, and scalability considerations.  
- Acceptance cons: insufficient theoretical justification, ad-hoc parameter tuning, and limited comparison to state-of-the-art transformer approaches.
