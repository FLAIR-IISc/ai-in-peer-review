- Summary: Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method—matrix completion on a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix with missing entries, followed by LSI—and evaluates on 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks using Amazon reviews.

- Strength: Clear problem statement articulating the cross-language classification challenge and motivation.

- Strength: Technically sound matrix completion formulation with convergence analysis and suitable projected gradient descent for large-scale problems.

- Strength: Practical contribution showing strong performance with very small amounts of parallel data (e.g., 200 documents).

- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation across 18 language pairs and analysis of parallel data quantity effects.

- Strength: Related work coverage is comprehensive and positions the method relative to prior approaches.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to state-of-the-art—missing transformer-based baselines like mBERT and XLM-R, so performance claims lack modern context.

- Weakness: Insufficient theoretical justification for the chosen regularization (γ‖M‖* + µ‖M‖1) and the least-squares loss choice.

- Weakness: Parameter selection is questionable—parameters tuned on one task (EFB) and reused for all tasks without rigorous validation.

- Weakness: Ambiguous vocabulary construction—paper does not explain how the unified vocabulary is formed or how parallel documents connect vocabularies.

- Weakness: No ablation studies to isolate contributions of matrix completion versus direct LSI application.

- Quality assessment: Technically sound and empirically presented, but limited by lack of modern comparisons and deeper theoretical justification.

- Clarity assessment: Generally well-written and organized, but transitions to matrix completion, loss rationale, and unified vocabulary construction need clearer explanation.

- Originality assessment: Two-step idea is reasonable but incremental; novelty not strongly established against recent methods.

- Significance assessment: Practical value in low parallel-data settings, but significance limited by missing comparisons to current state-of-the-art and unclear broader impact.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—promising and technically solid but needs stronger comparisons and theoretical/empirical support.

- Specific recommendation: Add comparisons to recent transformer-based cross-lingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R).

- Specific recommendation: Explain in detail how the unified vocabulary is constructed and how parallel docs bridge languages.

- Specific recommendation: Conduct ablation studies to quantify the benefit of matrix completion versus direct LSI.

- Specific recommendation: Justify or compare different regularization strategies (γ‖M‖* + µ‖M‖1) theoretically or empirically.

- Specific recommendation: Use a more rigorous parameter selection procedure (e.g., cross-validation across tasks) rather than tuning on a single task.
