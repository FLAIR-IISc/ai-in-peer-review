- Proposes a novel two-step method: matrix completion to build a complete parallel document-term matrix, then LSI to get low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification using multilingual Amazon product reviews.  
- Strength: simple and effective, outperforming several other cross-language representation methods.  
- Strength: can operate with a small number of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents.  
- Strength: provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent solver.  
- Weakness: unclear why LSI dimensionality reduction is necessary after matrix estimation and why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA.  
- Weakness: experimental setup is unclear—needs clarification on training and testing data/splits.  
- Weakness: lacks running-time and computational-efficiency comparisons of the projected gradient descent algorithm with other methods.  
- Weakness: minor editorial issues (notation fixes, removal of unnecessary phrases).  
- Originality/significance: novel and significant approach distinct from machine translation or bilingual-lexicon-based methods.  
- Quality/clarity: generally well-written and easy to follow, with some minor editorial improvements needed.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel effective method, handles small parallel corpora, and includes convergence proof.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: insufficient justification for LSI use, unclear experiments, and missing runtime comparisons.  
- Overall: valuable and original contribution that requires minor clarifications and editorial fixes.
