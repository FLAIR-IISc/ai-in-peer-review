**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which aims to bridge the feature spaces of different languages by exploiting a set of parallel bilingual documents. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then induces a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix. The proposed method is evaluated by conducting a set of experiments with cross-language sentiment classification tasks on multilingual Amazon product reviews.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The proposed method is simple and effective, and outperforms several other cross-language representation learning methods in the experiments.
* The method can handle a small number of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents, which is a common scenario in real-world applications.
* The paper provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm used to solve the matrix completion problem.

Weaknesses:

* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of why reducing feature dimension with LSI after estimating the matrix is necessary, and why LSI is chosen instead of other alternatives like PCA.
* The experimental setup is unclear, and the reviewer requests clarification on the training and testing data used in the experiments.
* The paper does not provide a running-time comparison of the projected gradient descent algorithm with other methods, which would be useful for understanding the computational efficiency of the proposed method.
* There are some minor editorial suggestions, such as fixing the notation and removing unnecessary phrases.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which is different from previous approaches that rely on machine translation or bilingual lexicons. The method is original and significant, as it provides a new way to bridge the feature spaces of different languages using parallel bilingual documents.
