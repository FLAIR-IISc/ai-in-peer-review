- Proposes a two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to fill a parallel document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification of Amazon reviews, outperforming several baselines, especially with few parallel bilingual documents.  
- Strength: Simple, effective approach that can be applied across NLP tasks.  
- Strength: Leverages well-established matrix completion techniques.  
- Strength: Empirical results show strong performance when parallel data is scarce.  
- Weakness: Unclear why LSI is applied after estimating M and why LSI is preferred over alternatives like PCA.  
- Weakness: Experimental setup lacks clarity—training and testing data details need to be specified.  
- Weakness: Reported results contain inconsistencies (CL-KCCA vs TSL discrepancy between Figure 1 and Table 1).  
- Weakness: Theoretical justification for the low-rank assumption of the document-term matrix is not provided.  
- Weakness: No clear explanation why TSL often outperforms other methods, notably CCA.  
- Weakness: No running-time or computational-cost comparison with other methods.  
- Originality: Novel combination of matrix completion and LSI for cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Significance: Addresses an important NLP problem and is applicable to tasks like cross-language classification and sentiment analysis.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written with clear notation, but some experimental details are missing.  
- Pro acceptance: Novel and effective method with supportive experimental results.  
- Con acceptance: Lacks technical justifications, experimental detail, and runtime comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—clarify low-rank assumption, experimental setup, and provide runtime comparisons.
