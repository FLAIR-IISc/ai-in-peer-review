- Proposes TSL: a novel two-step method (matrix completion then LSI) for cross-lingual text classification using a unified document-term matrix.
- Addresses disjoint vocabularies by formulating matrix completion to fill missing language-specific features, assuming low-rank and sparse complete matrix.
- Frames completion as convex optimization with trace-norm and L1 regularization and solves it via projected gradient descent.
- Provides a formal convergence proof for the optimization algorithm.
- Differs from prior CL-LSI work by explicitly completing the feature matrix before dimensionality reduction.
- Empirical evaluation on 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks shows TSL consistently and significantly outperforms monolingual and three strong cross-lingual baselines.
- Demonstrates strong robustness to limited parallel data, maintaining high accuracy with as few as ~200 parallel documents.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and experimentally reproducible with detailed parameter and setup descriptions.
- Introduction could better motivate the low-rank assumption and the rationale for using matrix completion upfront.
- Lacks a discussion and complexity analysis of scalability (SVD per iteration is expensive); suggests addressing large-scale approximations (e.g., randomized SVD).
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant, and practically impactful contribution with minor presentation/scalability issues; strong recommendation for acceptance.
