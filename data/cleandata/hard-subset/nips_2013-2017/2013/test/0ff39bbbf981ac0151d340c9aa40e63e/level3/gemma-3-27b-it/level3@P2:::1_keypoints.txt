- Paper proposes a two-step cross-language method: concatenate bilingual document-term matrices, complete missing entries via matrix completion (PGD with convergence guarantee), then apply LSI for cross-lingual representation.  
- Evaluated on Amazon cross-lingual sentiment tasks, showing superior performance over baselines, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Problem is well-motivated and placed appropriately within prior work (MT, bilingual lexicons, comparable corpora).  

Strengths:
- Technical soundness: matrix completion formulation with trace-norm and sparsity regularization is appropriate.  
- Convergence analysis: projected gradient descent with provided convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical performance: consistently outperforms CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA in experiments, notably in low-resource settings.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and the two-step approach is clearly described.  

Weaknesses:
- Motivation for applying LSI after matrix completion is unclear; alternatives like PCA are not justified.  
- Roles of labeled source data vs. unlabeled parallel data are not clearly distinguished or separately analyzed.  
- Experimental details need clarification (confirming train/test splits and that unlabeled data varied 200–2k across all experiments); inconsistencies between Figure 1 and Table 1 should be resolved.  
- Low-rank assumption for document-term matrices is questionable and needs justification.  
- Lacks deeper intuition and comparison explaining why TSL outperforms CCA-based methods.  
- Computational cost and runtime comparison with baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) are missing.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, simple, and technically sound approach to cross-language representation learning.  
- Strong empirical gains, especially in low-resource scenarios.  
- Theoretical contribution via convergence guarantees.  
- Clear presentation and organization.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Several technical and experimental clarifications are required before publication.  
- Key design choices (notably LSI) are not fully justified.  
- Missing discussion of computational complexity and runtimes.  

Overall assessment:
- Promising and effective method; recommend acceptance with minor revisions contingent on addressing experimental clarifications, LSI justification, and computational analysis.  

Minor editorial suggestions:
- Replace “(ij)” with “(i,j)” before Eq. (2).  
- Remove “such as” before Eqs. (4) and (5).
