- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion followed by latent semantic indexing.  
- Formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix from bilingual documents.  
- Induces low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations via LSI on the completed matrix.  
- Method is technically sound with supporting theoretical analysis.  
- Provides clear explanation of the matrix completion formulation and the projected gradient descent solver.  
- Includes convergence guarantees for the optimization algorithm.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides sufficient background and related work.  
- Experimental setup and results are clearly presented and reproducible (parameters and selection procedures reported).  
- Method is original and outperforms other cross-language approaches, especially with few parallel documents.  
- Learning curves show strong, stable performance even with limited parallel/unlabeled data.  
- Experiments are thorough, comparing to bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Minor suggestion: better motivate the use of matrix completion in the Introduction.  
- Minor concern: technical details of matrix completion and projected gradient descent may be hard for some readers.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 9/10.
