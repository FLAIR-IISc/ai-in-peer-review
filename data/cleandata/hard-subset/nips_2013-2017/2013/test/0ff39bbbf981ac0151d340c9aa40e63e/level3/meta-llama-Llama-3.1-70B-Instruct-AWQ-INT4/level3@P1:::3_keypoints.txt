- Two-step method: matrix completion on parallel bilingual documents to produce a complete document-term matrix, then apply CL-LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification using Amazon product reviews.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly outlines challenges and limitations of existing cross-language text classification methods.  
- Novelty: using matrix completion to create multilingual row vectors followed by CL-LSI appears to be a new approach.  
- Authors provide a thorough related-work analysis and clearly differentiate their method from prior work.  
- Main concern: observed improvements may be due to LSI scaling rather than the matrix completion step.  
- Recommended experiment: evaluate with classifiers insensitive to per-feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees) to rule out scaling artifacts.  
- Clarity issue: ambiguous phrasing around the “unified document-term matrix” (unclear if terms are language-agnostic or language-specific).  
- Clarity issue: experimental setup description is fragmented and difficult to follow (information scattered, e.g., across Table 1).  
- Significance: matrix completion is a controlled non-linear inference technique; if results are robust, the approach could substantially improve cross-language classification.  
- Actionable revisions suggested: address clarity problems, consolidate experimental description, and add robustness experiments and alternative classifiers.  
- Overall: neat and promising idea with potential for significant contribution pending additional experiments and clearer presentation.
