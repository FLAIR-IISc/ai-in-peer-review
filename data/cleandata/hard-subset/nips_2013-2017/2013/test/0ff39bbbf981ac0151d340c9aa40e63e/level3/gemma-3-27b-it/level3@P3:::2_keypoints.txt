- Proposal: Introduces a two-step cross-lingual representation method using matrix completion to impute a document-term matrix followed by LSI for low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Novelty: First application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning, distinguishing from prior CL-LSI-style methods.  
- Empirical performance: Outperforms several baselines including state-of-the-art (CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) on cross-lingual sentiment classification.  
- Low-resource strength: Particularly effective when parallel bilingual data is limited.  
- Experimental scope: Thorough evaluation on 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks with ablation on parallel-data amount.  
- Reproducibility: Paper reports parameters, selection procedure, and uses standard SVM/libsvm, aiding reproducibility.  
- Theoretical grounding: Provides convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent algorithm used in matrix completion.  
- Clarity: Well-written and clearly explained method and experimental setup.  
- Motivation weakness: Initial rationale for choosing matrix completion could be better justified beyond assuming low-rank structure.  
- Computational concern: Scalability and computational cost of matrix completion for large datasets are not fully addressed.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Lacks deeper analysis of how sensitive results are to parameter choices.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, effective, and well-supported contribution to cross-lingual NLP.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel technique, significant empirical gains, reproducible, theoretically supported, and broadly impactful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Modest issues around motivation clarity and computational scalability.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
