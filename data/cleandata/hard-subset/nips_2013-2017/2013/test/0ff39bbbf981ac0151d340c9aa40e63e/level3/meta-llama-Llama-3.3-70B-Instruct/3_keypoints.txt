- Proposes a novel translingual representation learning method using matrix completion and CL-LSI to create multilingual vectors.  
- Method grounded in unbiased estimators of loss for empirical risk minimization under class-conditional random label noise.  
- Presents two ways to modify surrogate losses: unbiased estimators and a reduction to classification with weighted 0-1 loss, yielding similar risk bounds.  
- Claims theoretical risk bounds and a well-controlled non-linear inference technique via matrix completion.  
- Paper appears novel and potentially significant for robust multilingual representation learning.  
- Provides thorough analysis and detailed discussion of related work and proposed methods.  
- Experimental result quality is questionable and may be confounded by LSI scaling advantages with a linear SVM.  
- Recommends verifying robustness using scale-insensitive learners (e.g., boosted decision trees).  
- Clarity issues exist in parts of the paper, notably the construction of the unified document–term matrix.  
- Strengths: novel approach, thorough analysis, and potential for robust results.  
- Weaknesses: questionable empirical results, insufficient clarity in sections, and need for further verification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, solid analysis, and potential contribution to NLP/ML.  
- Arguments against acceptance: empirical concerns, clarity problems, and required additional validation.  
- Overall: promising contribution but requires further clarification and stronger empirical verification.
