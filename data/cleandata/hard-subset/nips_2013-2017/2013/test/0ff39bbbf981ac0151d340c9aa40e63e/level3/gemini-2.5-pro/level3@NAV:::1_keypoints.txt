- Proposes a two-step learning (TSL) method: construct an incomplete cross-language document-term matrix, complete it via projected gradient descent with low-rank and sparsity constraints, then apply LSI and train a classifier.  
- Novelty: explicitly performs matrix completion before dimensionality reduction, unlike CL-LSI/CL-KCCA which operate on sparse concatenated matrices.  
- Strength: simple, elegant method with strong empirical performance, especially with limited unlabeled parallel data.  
- Strength: provides a convergence guarantee for the optimization algorithm.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Needs justification for the central low-rank assumption on document-term matrices and explanation why documents are linear combinations of few basis documents.  
- Clarify why LSI is applied after matrix completion (e.g., is M* dense and LSI a computational convenience?) and justify choosing LSI over alternatives like PCA.  
- Experimental setup is ambiguous—confirm training/test splits (4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel, 1.9k target test) and explicitly report separate effects of labeled source vs. unlabeled parallel data.  
- Resolve apparent inconsistency where CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 (EFM/EGM) but not in Table 1.  
- Include a running-time comparison against baselines.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change (ij) to (i,j) before Eq. (2) and remove "such as" before Eqs. (4) and (5).
