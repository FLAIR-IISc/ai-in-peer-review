- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion to fill missing entries in a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix, followed by LSI for low-dimensional representations.
- Differs from prior methods (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) by recovering missing values before dimensionality reduction instead of applying reduction directly to the incomplete matrix.
- Presents a well-justified projected gradient descent algorithm for matrix completion with a rigorous convergence proof, indicating strong technical quality.
- Empirical evaluation on Amazon product reviews (EN–FR, EN–DE, EN–JA) compares against TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA and shows consistent 8.59–14.61% classification accuracy improvements.
- Demonstrates robustness with very little parallel data (as few as 200 documents), addressing a critical practical challenge in cross-lingual learning.
- Paper is clear and reproducible, with complete parameter descriptions, transparent methodology, and well-structured experiments.
- Motivation for using matrix completion could be strengthened in the introduction to better position the approach against existing work (minor issue).
- Shows significant originality by applying matrix completion as a preprocessing step in cross-lingual representation learning, with substantial practical significance for sentiment classification and potential extension to other tasks.
- Main limitation: evaluation is restricted to sentiment analysis; broader NLP task applicability remains to be shown.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, empirically strong, and well-suited for NIPS.
