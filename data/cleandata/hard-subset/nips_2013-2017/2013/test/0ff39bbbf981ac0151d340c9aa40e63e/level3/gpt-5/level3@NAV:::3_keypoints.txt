- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual pipeline: convex trace-norm + ℓ1 nonnegative matrix completion via projected gradient descent, then cross-lingual LSI (SVD) to learn shared low-dimensional document features.  
- Positioned against CL-LSI, CL-CCA/CL-KCCA, OPCA, and SCL; builds on matrix-completion theory with a proximal PGD convergence argument.  
- Optimization is technically sound, appropriate, and scalable; convergence argument is provided.  
- Empirically outperforms CL-LSI/OPCA/KCCA on Amazon sentiment with limited parallel supervision.  
- Main empirical confound: linear SVMs are sensitive to feature rescaling, and LSI’s singular-value scaling may advantage SVMs over OPCA/CCA, so gains may stem from scaling not matrix completion.  
- Recommended robustness checks: (i) evaluate with scale-insensitive classifiers (e.g., boosted trees in Weka), (ii) include ablations: MC-only and LSI-only on the original concatenated matrix, (iii) standardize or ℓ2-normalize features post-projection across methods, (iv) report sensitivity to embedding dimension k and to using singular-value scaling vs. not.  
- Two reproducibility ambiguities: (1) “unified document-term matrix” is underspecified—are columns concatenated language-specific surface forms or language-agnostic terms, and how are parallel-document pairs fused (concatenate, sum, etc.)? Formalize with indexing and an example.  
- (2) Experimental setup details are fragmented; consolidate data splits, preprocessing (TF-IDF, normalization), hyperparameter selection, and classifier settings in one subsection.  
- Originality: novel combination of matrix completion with CL-LSI and a non-linear inference angle distinct from OPCA/CCA/LSI-alone baselines.  
- Significance: potentially impactful if improvements hold under scale-invariant evaluation—matrix completion gives a principled non-linear way to propagate cross-lingual co-occurrence with few parallel pairs.  
- Pros: simple, novel pipeline with solid optimization and promising low-parallel accuracy.  
- Cons: possible scaling artifact with linear SVM and unclear data/experimental reporting.  
- Overall recommendation: neat idea but needs additional scale-robust evaluations and clearer exposition before acceptance.
