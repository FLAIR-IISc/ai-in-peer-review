- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion to fill missing multilingual features, then LSI to produce a low-dimensional shared semantic space.  
- Extends prior work (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA) by introducing matrix completion as an explicit, non-linear inference step to bridge lexical gaps.  
- Original and elegant idea: applying principled matrix completion to generate dense cross-lingual document vectors is a meaningful contribution.  
- Empirical results are promising, especially strong performance with limited parallel data.  
- Major experimental weakness: exclusive use of a linear SVM may conflate representation quality with feature scaling effects from LSI.  
- LSI’s intrinsic feature rescaling could make the representation artificially favorable to linear SVM compared to baselines (OPCA/CCA).  
- Recommendation: evaluate with scale-invariant classifiers (e.g., boosted trees or random forests) to decouple representation quality from classifier sensitivity.  
- Clarity issues: the term “unified document-term matrix” is ambiguous—unclear if terms are language-agnostic or concatenated surface forms.  
- Experimental setup description is fragmented (text vs. Table 1), hindering reproducibility.  
- Overall: promising and original, but additional scale-invariant experiments and clearer exposition are needed to substantiate claims.
