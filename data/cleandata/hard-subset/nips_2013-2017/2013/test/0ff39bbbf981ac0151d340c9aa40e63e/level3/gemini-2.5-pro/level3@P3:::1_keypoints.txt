- Proposal: two-step learning (TSL) for cross-language text classification — matrix completion to fill missing cross-lingual terms, then LSI + SVM on the completed matrix.

Strengths:
- Conceptually simple and intuitive: fills missing language representations instead of projecting into a shared space.
- Strong empirical performance: outperforms four other methods across 18 tasks.
- Effective with limited parallel data (e.g., 200 parallel documents).
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent optimizer.

Weaknesses / Questions:
- Motivation for LSI step unclear: why apply LSI after completion and why not use PCA or other alternatives?
- Experimental setup unclear: confirm exact data splits used for training, representation learning, and testing.
- External data conflation: need experiments separating contributions of labeled source data vs unlabeled parallel data (and a source-only baseline).
- Clarify Section 5.3: do experiments vary unlabeled parallel documents from 200 to 2000?
- Inconsistent results: Figure 1 vs Table 1 discrepancy for EFM/EGM with 2000 unlabeled docs needs explanation.
- Low-rank assumption requires justification: why is the fully observed document-term matrix inherently low-rank?
- Intuition for superior performance: explain what structural properties the global low-rank constraint captures that CCA-based methods miss.
- Computational cost missing: provide runtime comparison with CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.

Minor editorial:
- Notation and phrasing fixes: "(ij)∈Ω" -> "(i,j)∈Ω" and remove informal "such as" before Eqs. (4) and (5).

Overall assessment:
- Promising, novel, and effective approach but needs clarifications on motivation, experimental details, theoretical justification, and runtime to fully validate contribution.
