- Proposal: two-step method (TSL) — matrix completion to fill bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual representations, unlike prior work that applies dimensionality reduction directly on incomplete matrices.  
- Empirical claim: TSL outperforms CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA on 18 Amazon sentiment tasks, especially with scarce parallel data.  
- Novelty: leverages matrix completion to bridge languages via co-occurrence patterns instead of translation or explicit bilingual resources.  
- Theory: provides a convergence-proofed projected gradient descent algorithm for the convex optimization problem.  
- Experimental breadth: tests multiple language pairs (EN-FR, EN-DE, EN-JP) and varying amounts of parallel data.  
- Empirical strength: reports statistically significant improvements (e.g., +14.61% on EFB) and robustness with as few as 200 parallel documents.  
- Main methodological concern: improvements may be due to LSI’s inherent scaling effect benefiting linear SVMs, not the matrix completion step.  
- Missing controls: no experiments with scale-insensitive classifiers (e.g., boosted trees, random forests) to rule out the scaling confound.  
- Comparative ambiguity: CL-LSI and CL-KCCA also use LSI, so TSL’s advantage might come from applying LSI after imputation rather than from imputation itself.  
- Clarity issue: “unified vocabulary” is ambiguous — unclear whether entries are language-specific surface forms or interlingual concepts, which affects interpretation of matrix completion.  
- Reproducibility issue: experimental setup (parameter selection, dataset splits) is fragmented and key details are buried, hindering replication.  
- Significance caveat: method is potentially impactful for low-resource settings but its claimed efficacy is contingent on resolving the classifier/LSI confound.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising idea but evaluation lacks rigor; authors should (re-)run scale-insensitive classifiers, clarify the unified vocabulary, and consolidate experimental details.
