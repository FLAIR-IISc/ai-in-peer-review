**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which aims to bridge the feature spaces of different languages using a set of parallel bilingual documents. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then induces a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing (LSI) on the obtained matrix. The proposed method is evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification tasks on Amazon product reviews, and the experimental results demonstrate that it outperforms several other cross-language representation learning methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

**Strengths**

* The paper proposes a simple yet effective method for cross-language representation learning, which can be applied to various natural language processing tasks.
* The method is based on a well-established technique, matrix completion, which has been widely used in various applications.
* The experimental results demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed method, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of why reducing feature dimension with LSI after estimating M is necessary. Is it for computational convenience because M* becomes relatively dense despite sparsity enforcement? Why LSI instead of alternatives like PCA?
* The experimental setup is unclear, and the reviewer requests clarification on the training and testing data.
* There is an inconsistency in the results, where CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 for EFM and EGM at 2k unlabeled but not in Table 1.
* The theoretical justification for the low-rank assumption of the document-term matrix is unclear.
* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of why TSL often outperforms other methods, especially CCA.
* The running-time comparison between the proposed method and other methods is not provided.

**Originality**

* The paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which combines matrix completion and LSI.
* The method is based on a well-established technique, matrix completion, which has been widely used in various applications.

**Significance**

* The proposed method can be applied to various natural language processing tasks, such as cross-language text classification, sentiment analysis, and machine translation.
* The experimental results demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed method, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

**Clarity**
