- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to fill a parallel document-term matrix, then latent semantic indexing (LSI) for low-dimensional representations.  
- Method only requires a small set of unlabeled parallel documents, unlike approaches needing full translation of training data.  
- Technique is well-motivated and technically sound; authors provide clear presentation and a convergence proof for the optimization algorithm.  
- Use of matrix completion to create multilingual row vectors is an interesting, potentially widely applicable idea.  
- Results significance is questioned: improvements may stem from LSI’s scaling properties rather than the proposed method.  
- Reviewer recommends evaluating with classifiers insensitive to per-feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees) to rule out scaling artifacts.  
- Paper is original and has high potential impact for cross-language representation learning.  
- Paper is generally well-written and concise.  
- Clarity issue: phrase “unified document-term matrix” is ambiguous—unclear if terms are language-agnostic or language-specific surface forms.  
- Experimental setup description is fragmented and unclear, currently spread across Table 1.  
- Recommendation: clarify the unified matrix wording and provide detailed experimental setup (preprocessing, parameter settings).  
- Overall: neat idea that could make a significant contribution after addressing clarity and evaluation concerns (use non-rescaling-sensitive classifiers).
