- Proposes a novel approach to learning from noisy labels by modifying surrogate losses for class-conditional random label noise.
- Develops two methods: one using unbiased estimators of the loss.
- Develops a second method using a weighted loss with label-dependent costs.
- Provides theoretical guarantees showing strong performance even at high noise rates.
- Handles non-uniform (class-dependent) noise rates.
- Competitive with state-of-the-art methods on benchmark datasets.
- Clear, well-organized, and accessible presentation.
- Assumes knowledge of noise rates, which may not always be available in practice.
- Methods are robust to misspecification of noise rates, partially mitigating the assumption.
- May not be suitable for very high noise rates or datasets with many classes.
- Empirical evaluation could be more extensive (more comparisons and sensitivity analyses).
- Overall significant and well-supported contribution to learning with noisy labels.
