- Proposes a two-step cross-language method: fill missing entries in a unified document-term matrix via matrix completion, then apply CL-LSI to the completed matrix.  
- Unified matrix setup: parallel documents yield fully observed rows; non-parallel documents have missing values for the other language’s vocabulary.  
- Key difference from prior work (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA): they apply dimensionality reduction to the incomplete matrix, whereas this paper completes the matrix first.  
- Novel methodological contribution.  
- Solid theoretical development: projected gradient descent algorithm with convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical results show significant improvements over baselines, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Matrix completion step framed as a well-controlled non-linear inference useful for cross-lingual learning.  
- Major concern: use of linear SVM (sensitive to feature scaling) may mean gains come from scaling effects rather than the matrix completion method.  
- Suggested remedy: evaluate with classifiers insensitive to per-feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka) to rule out scaling artifacts.  
- Clarity issues: description of the unified document-term matrix is ambiguous about term treatment (language-agnostic vs language-specific).  
- Experimental setup presentation is fragmented (spread across Table 1), making results hard to follow.  
- Overall judgment: interesting and technically sound but evaluation is insufficiently robust; recommend rejection pending additional experiments.
