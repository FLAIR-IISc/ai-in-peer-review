- Proposal: two-step cross-lingual method — convex matrix completion on a unified bilingual doc-term matrix, followed by LSI (SVD) and linear SVM.  
- Matrix formulation: parallel pairs fully observed, monolingual rows partially observed; relaxation uses trace-norm + L1 + nonnegativity with squared loss and soft regularization of missing entries.  
- Optimization: projected gradient descent with shrinkage and nonnegativity projection; convergence argued via non-expansiveness but composition of prox operators needs clarification.  
- Empirical setup: careful parameter selection, consistent k, multiple runs and learning curves; shows consistent gains over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA, especially with few parallel documents.  
- Ablation gap: missing experiments — LSI on unified matrix with zeros (no completion) and completion-only (no LSI) to isolate contributions.  
- Theoretical gap: need rigorous justification or citation that composing shrinkage and nonnegativity projection converges to minimizer of the full objective.  
- Scalability gap: no runtime/complexity, iteration counts, or truncated SVD / wall-clock time reported despite per-iteration SVD cost.  
- Clarity: paper is readable with algorithmic details and lemmas; suggest stronger motivation for low-rank assumption and clearer guidance on ρ and µ plus vocab/rank sizes.  
- Originality: novel use of convex matrix completion to bridge disjoint cross-lingual vocabularies and leverage limited parallel data.  
- Significance: consistent improvements across 18 sentiment tasks; especially valuable for low-resource settings and likely generalizable beyond sentiment.  
- Pros: principled two-step formulation, sound convex relaxation, simple optimization, strong empirical gains, robust with few parallels, reproducible setup.  
- Cons: missing theoretical justification for proximal composition, absent ablations and some baselines (SCL, multilingual topic models), limited dataset scope, and no scalability analysis.  
- Suggestions: add targeted ablations, report computational/time/memory details, clarify/prove convergence or reformulate proximal operator, expand evaluations and baselines, and better motivate low-rank assumption and hyperparameter choices.  
- Overall: solid, novel, well-executed work that merits acceptance after minor theoretical clarification and additional experiments.
