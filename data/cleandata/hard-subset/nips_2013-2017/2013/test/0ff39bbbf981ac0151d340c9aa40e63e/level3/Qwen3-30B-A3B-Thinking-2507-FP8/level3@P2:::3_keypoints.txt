- Proposed method: two-step cross-lingual learning — matrix completion to fill missing entries in a bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI to produce low-dimensional representations; evaluated on Amazon reviews (EN/FR/DE/JP) with claimed large gains, especially with limited parallel data.

- Novelty/significance: Filling missing entries before LSI is a novel idea for cross-lingual transfer and could be important for low-resource settings if validated.

- Technical quality: Optimization (low-rank + sparsity matrix completion) and projected gradient descent are well-formulated and include a convergence proof.

- Critical experimental flaw: Reported improvements may be an artifact of LSI-induced feature scaling interacting with linear SVMs, not the matrix completion step.

- Missing robustness check: Authors did not evaluate with classifiers insensitive to feature scaling (e.g., boosted decision trees/AdaBoost); such tests are necessary to rule out the scaling artifact.

- Clarity issue — vocabulary: The term "unified document-term matrix" is ambiguous — unclear whether vocabulary is language-agnostic (translated) or language-specific, affecting reproducibility.

- Clarity issue — experiments: Experimental details and parameter selections are fragmented across text and table; need a consolidated experimental setup and parameters for all tasks.

- Strengths: Conceptually clean framework, theoretical grounding, convergence guarantee, and consistent empirical wins on 16/18 tasks (subject to artifact checks).

- Weaknesses: Unverified empirical claim due to SVM-scaling confound; lack of discussion on computational scalability for large vocabularies.

- Recommendation: Weak reject; paper would be acceptable only after rerunning experiments with a non-SVM classifier to confirm robustness and after clarifying vocabulary construction and consolidating experimental details.
