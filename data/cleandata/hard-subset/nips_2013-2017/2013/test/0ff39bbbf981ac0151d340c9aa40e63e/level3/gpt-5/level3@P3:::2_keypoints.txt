- Summary: Proposes a two-step cross-lingual pipeline — convex matrix completion (nuclear norm + L1 + nonnegativity) to impute a bilingual doc–term matrix, then LSI/SVD on the completed matrix for shared embeddings.  
- Novelty/Relation: Novel use of principled matrix completion as a pre-processing step before LSI, differing from prior CL-LSI/KCCA/OPCA/SCL methods that treat missing entries as zeros.  
- Optimization: Uses projected gradient descent with singular value shrinkage and nonnegativity projection, and provides a convergence proof and practical parameter constraints.  
- Empirical results: Strong, stable gains over mono- and cross-lingual baselines on 17/18 tasks across EN/FR/DE/JP Amazon reviews; robust with ~200 parallel pairs.  
- Reproducibility: Hyperparameters, selection procedures, and implementation details are reported for replication.  
- Strength — Method: Conceptually simple, well-motivated pipeline addressing the drawback of treating missing entries as zeros.  
- Strength — Optimization: Principled algorithm with convergence guarantees and clear practical guidance.  
- Strength — Empirics: Consistent improvements and thorough multi-language/domain evaluations.  
- Weakness — Motivation: Introduction could better motivate why matrix completion is the right preprocessing, with an intuitive example or schematic.  
- Weakness — Transductive setting: Representation learning uses unlabeled target/test data while some baselines (TBOW) do not; need target-only LSI baseline, an inductive variant, and evaluation vs bilingual topic models.  
- Weakness — Ablations: Missing ablations: CL-LSI on incomplete matrix, matrix completion without LSI, full pipeline comparison, and sensitivity analyses for γ, ρ, and k.  
- Weakness — Computational details: Repeated SVDs raise scalability concerns; need wall-clock times, memory, and whether partial/randomized SVDs are used.  
- Weakness — Loss on missing entries: Quadratic penalty on imputed entries (weight ρ) may bias toward small values; justify ρ range and impact on recall.  
- Weakness — Generalization: Clarify how to embed new unseen documents without re-running matrix completion (fixed projection / mapping).  
- Quality: Technically sound, well-executed, and empirically strong; additional ablations and runtime/sensitivity analyses would strengthen the work.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized; strengthen the Introduction’s motivation and add an intuitive example.  
- Originality: Novel application of nuclear-norm + sparsity + nonnegativity matrix completion before cross-lingual LSA.  
- Significance: Demonstrates a generally applicable preprocessing idea with practical impact for cross-lingual tasks, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong empirical gains, simple general method with convergence guarantee, thorough experiments and reporting.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Possible transductive advantage inflating gains, missing ablations, and limited discussion of scalability and comparisons to multilingual topic models.  
- Questions for authors: How does inductive training-only representation perform? Provide wall-clock/memory and SVD details. Effect of removing LSI? How to embed new documents at test time?  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
