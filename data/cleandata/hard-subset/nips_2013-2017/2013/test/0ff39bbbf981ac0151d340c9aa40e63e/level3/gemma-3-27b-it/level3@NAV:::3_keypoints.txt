- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion on a partially observed document-term matrix from parallel bilingual documents, then LSI for low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Positions method relative to prior cross-lingual approaches (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) and differentiates by applying matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.  
- Novel application of matrix completion to bridge disjoint cross-lingual feature spaces.  
- Algorithm is clearly presented and includes a convergence analysis for the optimization procedure.  
- Experiments compare against several baselines on the Amazon product review dataset, showing reported improvements.  
- Major quality concern: reported gains may stem from LSI-induced scaling that benefits a linear SVM, not from improved representations.  
- Experimental confound: authors only use a linear SVM, which is sensitive to per-feature scaling.  
- Required fix: evaluate with a classifier insensitive to feature scaling (e.g., boosted decision trees) to validate robustness.  
- Clarity issue: unclear whether the unified document-term matrix uses language-agnostic terms or language-specific surface forms—this must be explicitly stated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of approach, clear algorithm with guarantees, and empirical improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential artifact of scaling, lack of evaluation with non-scaling-sensitive classifiers, and ambiguity about the term representation.  
- Recommendation: Reject with request for major revisions—perform suggested non-scaling-sensitive experiments and clarify the unified term representation; if addressed, the paper could become a strong contribution.
