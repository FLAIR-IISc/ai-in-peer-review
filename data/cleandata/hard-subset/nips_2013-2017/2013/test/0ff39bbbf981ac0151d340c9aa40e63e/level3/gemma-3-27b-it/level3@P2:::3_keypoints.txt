- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion to fill a concatenated document-term matrix from parallel documents, followed by LSI for low-dimensional representation used for sentiment classification.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains on Amazon reviews, especially with limited parallel data, outperforming several baselines.  
- Novelty: combining matrix completion prior to LSI is a new contribution for cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Provides convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent algorithm used in matrix completion, giving theoretical justification.  
- Builds on and properly situates the work within existing cross-language methods (MT, bilingual lexicons, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).  
- Strength: clear problem statement and mathematical formulation.  
- Weakness: possible confounding effect — improvements may stem from LSI scaling on a denser matrix rather than the representation itself.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses only a linear SVM, which may be sensitive to feature scaling and limits generalizability.  
- Weakness: ambiguity in the definition of the “unified document-term matrix” vocabulary (language-agnostic concepts vs. surface forms).  
- Weakness: experimental details are fragmented across sections (not consolidated), hindering reproducibility and clarity.  
- Quality: technically sound but robustness concerns remain due to classifier choice.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but needs clarification on vocabulary and a consolidated experimental setup.  
- Originality: method is original and clearly differentiated from prior work.  
- Significance: potentially important for low-resource cross-lingual learning if gains are robust beyond LSI scaling effects.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — evaluate with a classifier less sensitive to feature scaling (e.g., boosted decision trees), clarify vocabulary, and consolidate experimental setup description.
