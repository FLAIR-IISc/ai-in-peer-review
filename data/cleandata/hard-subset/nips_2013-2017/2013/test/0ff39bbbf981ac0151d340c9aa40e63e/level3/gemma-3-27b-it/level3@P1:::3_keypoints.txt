- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion to fill missing word features, then LSI for low-dimensional document representations.  
- Uses parallel bilingual documents to complete a unified document-term matrix.  
- Demonstrates improved cross-lingual sentiment classification on Amazon reviews, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Novelty lies in applying matrix completion as a preprocessing step before LSI.  
- Properly positions work among MT, bilingual lexicons, bilingual word-pair, comparable-corpora, and prior CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA methods.  
- Method is conceptually sound and mathematically principled.  
- Includes convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent optimization with theoretical guarantees.  
- Experimental results consistently outperform several established baselines.  
- Major concern: gains might be due to LSI-induced feature scaling benefiting linear SVMs rather than matrix completion itself.  
- Suggests validating results with classifiers less sensitive to feature scaling (e.g., boosted decision trees).  
- Clarity issue: unclear whether the unified document-term matrix uses language-agnostic concepts or raw surface forms.  
- Experimental setup is fragmented across text and Table 1 and should be consolidated.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions if classifier-scaling concerns and clarity issues are addressed.  
- Notes potential significance if matrix completion is shown to be a robust non-linear inference technique for cross-lingual learning.
