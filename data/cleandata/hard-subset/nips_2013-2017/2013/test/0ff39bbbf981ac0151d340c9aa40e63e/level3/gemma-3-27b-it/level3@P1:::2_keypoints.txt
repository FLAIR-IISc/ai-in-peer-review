- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion on parallel bilingual documents followed by LSI, optimized via projected gradient descent with convergence guarantees.  
- Evaluated on Amazon cross-lingual sentiment classification and outperforms several baselines, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Novelty: requires only a small amount of unlabeled parallel documents and uses matrix completion as preprocessing, distinguishing it from CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA.  
- Technical soundness: clear methodology and theoretical convergence guarantees for the optimization algorithm.  
- Strong empirical results: consistent superiority over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA across language pairs and categories.  
- Robustness to data scarcity: learning curves show good performance even with few parallel documents.  
- Thorough and reproducible experimental setup with clear dataset, metrics, and parameter selection.  
- Original contribution: applying matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, with clear notation and algorithm explanation.  
- Weakness: motivation for why matrix completion bridges the language gap is not sufficiently explained.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of sensitivity to parameter variations.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability of matrix completion with large vocabularies are not thoroughly addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, empirically strong, robust to scarce data, well-written and reproducible.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear motivation for matrix completion and inadequate discussion of computational cost.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify motivation and discuss scalability.
