- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language method: matrix completion to fill a parallel document-term matrix, then LSI to induce low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification of Amazon reviews and outperforms several baselines, especially with few parallel bilingual documents.  
- Requires only a small set of unlabeled parallel documents, unlike translation- or lexicon-based approaches that need extensive translated training data.  
- Uses matrix completion to learn a low-rank document-term matrix and applies projected gradient descent with a provided convergence guarantee.  
- Demonstrates robustness to the size of unlabeled parallel data, achieving strong performance with as few as 200 parallel documents.  
- Question why LSI is applied after estimating M: is it for computational convenience because M* becomes dense despite sparsity enforcement?  
- Asks why LSI was chosen over alternatives like PCA and requests justification of this design choice.  
- Requests explicit distinction and separate reporting of two external-data uses: labeled source-language data vs. unlabeled parallel data.  
- Experimental setup is unclear; asks to confirm training/test splits (e.g., 4k labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2k unlabeled parallel, 1.9k test target).  
- Asks to confirm whether Section 5.3 varies unlabeled parallel docs from 200 to 2k.  
- Notes an inconsistency: CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 (EFM/EGM at 2k) but not in Table 1 — requests clarification.  
- Theoretical concern: unclear why a fully observed document-term matrix should be low-rank given sparsity and documents not obviously linear combinations.  
- Requests intuition for why TSL often outperforms other methods, particularly CCA, and how matrix completion’s low-rank enforcement compares to alternatives.  
- Suggests including a runtime comparison of projected gradient descent against CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.  
- Overall assessment: method is simple, effective, and clearly presented but needs clarifications on technical points and experimental details.  
- Strengths: simplicity, effectiveness, robustness to parallel-data size, and strong empirical performance.  
- Weaknesses: unclear experimental setup, result inconsistencies, unclear low-rank theoretical justification, and limited comparison with CCA.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address technical and experimental clarifications; rating 8/10.
