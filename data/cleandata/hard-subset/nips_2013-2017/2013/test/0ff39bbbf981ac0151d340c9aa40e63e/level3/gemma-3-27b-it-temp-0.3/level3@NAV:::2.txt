## Review of “A Novel Two-Step Method for Cross Language Representation Learning”

This paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-lingual representation learning, combining matrix completion with latent semantic analysis (LSA). The core idea is to address the disjoint feature space problem in cross-lingual text classification by first completing a document-term matrix built from parallel corpora, and then applying LSA to induce a shared semantic space. The method is evaluated on cross-lingual sentiment classification using Amazon reviews, demonstrating strong performance compared to several baselines, including state-of-the-art techniques like CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA. 

The work builds upon existing approaches to cross-lingual learning, such as machine translation-based methods and those leveraging bilingual resources (as discussed in the Related Work). However, it distinguishes itself by focusing on matrix completion as a preprocessing step to bridge the language gap, a relatively unexplored avenue in this context. This is a clever application of a technique commonly used in recommender systems to the problem of cross-lingual representation. The authors also provide a thorough experimental evaluation, including ablation studies examining the impact of the amount of parallel data. The inclusion of learning curves and detailed parameter settings enhances reproducibility, a significant strength. The projected gradient descent algorithm for matrix completion is well-described, and the convergence analysis provides theoretical grounding.

A minor weakness is the initial motivation for using matrix completion in the Introduction. While the problem statement is clear, a more explicit explanation of *why* matrix completion is well-suited to address the cross-lingual feature space issue would be beneficial.  The paper is generally well-written and organized, though the mathematical notation could benefit from slightly more explanation for readers less familiar with matrix factorization techniques. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning is a novel contribution.
*   **Significance:** The method demonstrably outperforms existing approaches, particularly in low-resource scenarios, suggesting practical value.
*   **Thoroughness:** The experimental setup is comprehensive, with comparisons to strong baselines and analysis of parameter sensitivity.
*   **Reproducibility:** The authors provide sufficient detail for others to replicate their results.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The initial motivation for matrix completion could be strengthened.
*   Mathematical notation could be more accessible.

**Overall:** This is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field. The proposed method is well-motivated, thoroughly evaluated, and demonstrates promising results. I recommend acceptance.
