- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion to build a full parallel document-term matrix, followed by latent semantic indexing for low-dimensional representations.  
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification using Amazon reviews and outperforms several existing cross-lingual methods.  
- Particularly effective when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small, showing strong and stable learning curves with limited data.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with easy-to-follow methodology and reasoning.  
- Related work section is comprehensive, covering machine translation, domain adaptation, and multilingual representation learning.  
- Technical presentation is sound, with clear explanations of matrix completion and LSI steps.  
- Original contribution: novel application of matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Significance: demonstrates matrix completion’s potential to improve cross-lingual sentiment classification and possibly other tasks.  
- Minor weakness: introduction could better motivate why matrix completion is particularly useful for cross-language text classification.  
- Experimental weakness: could be strengthened by adding more baselines or comparisons to additional state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, original, and significant work that is a strong candidate for acceptance.
