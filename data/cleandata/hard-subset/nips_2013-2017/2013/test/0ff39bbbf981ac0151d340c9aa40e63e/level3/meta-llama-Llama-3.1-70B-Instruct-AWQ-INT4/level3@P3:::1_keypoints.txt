- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: concatenate bilingual document-term matrix, perform matrix completion, then apply LSI.

- Uses projected gradient descent for matrix completion and provides a convergence guarantee.

- Method is simple, scalable, effective, and outperforms several baselines, especially with few parallel bilingual documents.

- Demonstrates robustness: achieves good performance with as few as ~200 unlabeled parallel documents.

- Paper is well-written and the method and experiments are generally clearly presented.

- Unclear rationale for reducing feature dimension with LSI after estimating the low-rank matrix; “computational convenience” needs more justification.

- Authors should explicitly distinguish and separately report the two uses of external data: labeled source-language data vs. unlabeled parallel data.

- Experimental setup needs clarification: exact numbers of labeled source, labeled target, and unlabeled parallel documents; confirm whether Sec. 5.3 varies unlabeled docs from 200 to 2000.

- Inconsistency between Figure 1 and Table 1: CL-KCCA vs. TSL performance (EFM and EGM at 2k) needs clarification.

- Theoretical concern: why a fully observed document-term matrix should be low-rank given sparsity and non-linear document composition; provide more intuition.

- Comparative question: explain why TSL often outperforms other methods (e.g., CCA); offer analysis or insight.

- Request running-time comparison between projected gradient descent and other methods (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA).

- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant and original contribution, but requires minor revisions to address technical and experimental clarifications.

- Recommendation and scores: Accept with minor revisions; Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
