- Problem: Tackles cross-language text classification where source-language labels must transfer to a label-scarce target language with disjoint vocabularies.  
- Method: Proposes a two-step learning (TSL) that first completes a sparse, unified document-term matrix via convex matrix completion (trace norm + L1, non-negativity) and then applies LSI for shared low-dimensional representations.  
- Optimization: Solves the matrix completion with a projected gradient descent algorithm and provides a convergence proof.  
- Novelty: Novel application of matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual representation learning, distinct from prior CL-LSI/CL-KCCA approaches.  
- Experimental evaluation: Thorough comparison against strong baselines on a standard benchmark across 18 language/domain pairs, showing consistent superior performance.  
- Low-resource strength: Shows robust performance with very few parallel documents (e.g., 200), outperforming competing methods in low-resource settings.  
- Technical quality: Formulation and convex relaxation are sound; inclusion of regularizers and constraints is appropriate.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-written, organized, and describes experimental setup and parameter selection in enough detail for reproduction.  
- Significance: Results are impactful and likely to inspire similar matrix-completion-based transfer approaches across modalities/domains.  
- Minor weakness: Introduction could better motivate framing disjoint vocabularies as a missing-data problem to justify matrix completion earlier.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — novel, technically rigorous, empirically convincing, and practically relevant.
