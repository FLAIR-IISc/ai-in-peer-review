- Proposes a two-step approach: complete a partially observed document-term matrix via low-rank+sparse matrix completion with projected gradient descent, then apply LSI for cross-lingual representations.  
- Evaluated on Amazon product reviews across multiple language pairs.  
- Technically sound combination of matrix completion and LSI, with a notable convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Experimental results show consistent improvements over baselines (TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA), especially when parallel data is limited.  
- Clarification needed on why LSI is applied after matrix completion despite enforced sparsity—are computational reasons (M* becoming dense) the only justification, and why LSI instead of PCA or other methods?  
- Experimental setup description is inconsistent: reported "4k source, 100 target, 2k unlabeled parallel" conflicts with Section 5.3 where unlabeled parallel data varies from 200 to 2k.  
- Inconsistency between Figure 1 (CL-KCCA outperforms TSL at 2k for EFM/EGM) and Table 1 (TSL consistently outperforms CL-KCCA) requires clarification.  
- Theoretical justification for the low-rank assumption on document-term matrices is weak given document sparsity and potential non-linear relationships.  
- Needs intuition for why TSL outperforms multiview methods like CL-KCCA, which seem more natural for cross-lingual tasks.  
- Should include running-time and computational comparisons against other methods.  
- Writing is generally clear and the method is simple and effective, showing promise for limited parallel-data settings.  
- Overall meets quality standards but requires technical and experimental clarifications and improved presentation before acceptance.
