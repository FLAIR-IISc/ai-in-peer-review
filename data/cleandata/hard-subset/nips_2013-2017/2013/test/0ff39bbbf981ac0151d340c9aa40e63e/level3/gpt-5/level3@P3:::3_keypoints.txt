- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual document representation: convex matrix completion on a bilingual unified document–term matrix, then cross-lingual LSI (SVD) and monolingual classifier training.
- Optimization: convex objective combining trace norm, L1, nonnegativity, and squared-loss with tunable ρ for missing entries; solved by projected gradient descent with singular-value shrinkage and convergence guarantees.
- Experiments: evaluated on 18 EN–FR/DE/JP Amazon sentiment transfer tasks, comparing to TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA, showing consistent gains, especially with few parallel documents.
- Novelty: the use of convex matrix completion to impute cross-lingual co-occurrence before CL-LSI appears new and well-motivated for TF–IDF data.
- Methodological soundness: convex objective and proximal/shrinkage algorithm are well-founded and referenced.
- Empirical scope: broad set of language pairs and parallel-data regimes, highlighting low-parallel benefits.
- Potential significance: could generalize to multilingual representation learning if gains are robust.
- Main confound: linear SVM sensitivity to feature scaling may explain improvements (LSI’s scaling/whitening differs from OPCA/CCA), so gains might be due to LSI–SVM interaction rather than completion.
- Attribution concern: insufficient controls to show TSL’s improvements over CL-LSI persist under scale-normalized evaluation or scale-insensitive classifiers.
- Clarity issue 1: “unified document–term matrix” is ambiguous—need to state that columns are language-specific surface forms (no lexical unification) and clarify IDF computation.
- Clarity issue 2: experimental normalization choices (row normalization before SVD, whether singular values are used in projections, per-document length normalization, SVM input normalization) are not clearly specified.
- Missing robustness checks: no experiments with scale-insensitive classifiers (e.g., boosted trees, Random Forests) or explicit per-feature standardization; limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
- Computational/scalability gap: no wall-clock, memory, or SVD computation details reported for larger corpora.
- Quality assessment: optimization is solid, but empirical claims need ablations and scale-robust evaluations to isolate matrix completion’s contribution.
- Requested ablations: compare LSI on original vs completed matrix with matched normalization; TSL with ρ=0 and with/without nonnegativity; sensitivity to k and SVD tolerances.
- Clarity requests for reproducibility: report tokenization/stopwording/stemming per language, whether IDF is joint or per-language, row centering/length-normalization, and exact SVD variant/tolerances.
- Suggested experiments: evaluate with classifiers insensitive to feature scaling, apply per-dimension z-scoring and L2-normalization, and include more hyperparameter sweeps.
- Pros for acceptance: novel principled idea, solid optimization, promising low-parallel results, potential broad use.
- Cons for acceptance: possible scaling artifacts with linear SVM, unclear attribution of gains, missing robustness tests, and some experimental clarity gaps.
- Overall: interesting and potentially significant work but needs scale-robust evaluations, ablations, and clearer experimental details before the claimed improvements can be fully trusted.
