- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method: build a partially observed bilingual document-term matrix, perform matrix completion to fill missing entries, then apply LSI for low-dimensional representations.
- Formulates matrix completion as a convex optimization with trace-norm and ℓ1 regularization.
- Solves the optimization via projected gradient descent and provides a rigorous convergence proof (Theorem 1, supported by Lemmas 1–3).
- Evaluates on cross-lingual sentiment classification using Amazon product reviews.
- Compares against four baselines (TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) across 18 tasks.
- Consistently outperforms baselines by 8.59–14.61% accuracy, with particularly strong gains when parallel data is scarce (e.g., 200 documents).
- Parameter selection is transparent and experiments report standard deviations over 10 runs.
- Paper is well-organized, reproducible, and includes precise algorithmic descriptions and thorough experimental setup.
- Novelty: first to use matrix completion as a preprocessing step before LSI to exploit cross-lingual co-occurrence patterns.
- Significance: enables effective cross-lingual learning with minimal parallel data, valuable for low-resource languages and potentially generalizable to other tasks.
- Minor weaknesses: limited discussion of computational complexity/scalability compared to other methods.
- Minor clarity suggestion: strengthen Introduction motivation by explicitly explaining why matrix completion is preferable to direct LSI on sparse matrices.
- Overall judgment: technically robust, original, and practically significant — recommendation: Accept.
