- Two-step method: convex matrix completion of a bilingual document–term matrix followed by LSI (truncated SVD); solved by projected gradient with singular-value shrinkage and a convergence proof.  
- Empirical result: TSL generally outperforms CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA on Amazon EN–FR/DE/JA sentiment tasks, especially with few parallel documents.  
- Relation to prior work: similar in spirit to CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA and SCL, but novel in imputing a bilingual bag-of-words matrix via convex matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.  
- Strength: Conceptually simple, modular, and easy to reproduce and plug into existing classifiers.  
- Strength: Solid optimization setup (convex surrogate), clear algorithm, and a clean convergence guarantee.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance in low-parallel-data regimes with comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Weakness: Low-rank assumption on document–term matrices is not well justified; need explanation when low-rank holds and how violations affect completion.  
- Weakness: Unclear rationale for applying LSI after completion — clarify whether for computation, denoising, or both, and why not alternatives like PCA, NMF, or OPCA.  
- Weakness: Data usage is unclear—mixing labeled source, labeled target, and unlabeled parallel signals; require ablations to separate their contributions.  
- Weakness: Experimental protocol needs clarity/confirmation (e.g., exact train/test splits and whether unlabeled parallel docs vary as claimed).  
- Weakness: Inconsistency between Figure 1 and Table 1 (CL-KCCA sometimes outperforming TSL) needs reconciliation (splits/hyperparams/averaging?).  
- Weakness: Runtime and scalability concerns due to per-iteration SVDs; request wall-clock times, per-iteration costs, SVD ranks (full vs partial), and complexity analysis in terms of t, d, k.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to the missing-entry penalty ρ is not analyzed; need intuition and sensitivity experiments.  
- Minor issues: Editorial/notation fixes required (e.g., (i,j) vs (ij), remove “such as” before equations, typos like “documentterm”).  
- Quality: Optimization component is technically sound; main open technical issue is the low-rank assumption’s validity and impact; experiments are thorough but lack runtime detail.  
- Clarity: Writing is generally clear; adding a schematic of the pipeline, clearer data-split descriptions, and LSI rationale would improve reproducibility.  
- Originality: Components are known but their combination for imputing a bilingual bag-of-words matrix is novel; the convergence proof is a contribution.  
- Significance: Important and practical for cross-lingual sentiment classification, especially under scarce parallel-data conditions.  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, effective method with strong results, convergence guarantees, and robustness when parallel data is scarce.  
- Cons for acceptance: Questionable low-rank assumption, missing runtime/scalability analysis, and experimental ambiguities/inconsistencies.  
- Camera-ready suggestions: Justify low-rank assumption, provide ρ sensitivity, add runtime comparisons vs baselines, clarify LSI choice and data contributions, reconcile experimental inconsistencies, and fix editorial issues.  
- Overall assessment: Well-executed and promising paper that, with clarifications on modeling assumptions, experiments, and runtime, would make a solid NIPS contribution.
