- Proposes a two-step method: matrix completion on a concatenated document-term matrix using projected gradient descent, then LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.
- Provides a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent optimization.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on cross-language sentiment classification, especially when parallel data is limited.
- Novel combination of matrix completion and LSI that leverages unlabeled parallel data rather than heavy translation or bilingual dictionaries.
- Method is theoretically sound with a well-motivated regularized optimization formulation.
- Experimental results consistently outperform several baselines in low-resource parallel-data regimes.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized; Algorithm 1 is helpful.
- Choice of LSI after completion is under-motivated; alternatives like PCA are not discussed.
- Paper should better separate and report the effects of (1) labeled source-to-target transfer and (2) unlabeled parallel data.
- Experimental setup needs clarification (confirm counts: 4000 labeled source, 100 labeled target, 2000 unlabeled parallel, 1900 test; Section 5.3 varying unlabeled parallel from 200 to 2000).
- Inconsistency between Table 1 and Figure 1: CL-KCCA appears to outperform TSL in Figure 1 for some tasks but not in Table 1.
- Claim that fully observed document-term matrices are “naturally low-rank” requires stronger justification given document sparsity.
- Lacks deeper intuition for why TSL often beats CCA-based methods; need explanation of how low-rank enforcement yields gains.
- No discussion of computational complexity or running-time comparison with CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with strong low-resource empirical results and convergence proof.
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved technical/experimental clarifications, weak motivation for LSI choice, and missing computational-cost analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong, simple, and effective paper recommended for acceptance contingent on addressing listed weaknesses.
- Minor editorial fixes: replace “(ij)” with “(i,j)” before Eq. (2) and remove “such as” before Eqs. (4) and (5).
