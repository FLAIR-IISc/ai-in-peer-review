- Proposes a novel two-step method: matrix completion via projected gradient descent followed by LSI for cross-language representation learning.  
- Core idea of using matrix completion to bridge cross-lingual feature spaces is interesting and potentially valuable in low-resource settings.  
- Paper is well-situated in related work and clearly written; theoretical convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent is a major strength.  
- Experimental results on Amazon reviews are competitive, showing consistent outperformance of several baselines.  
- Motivation for applying LSI after matrix completion is underexplained; need explicit rationale (e.g., computational efficiency) and why LSI over alternatives like PCA.  
- Experimental setup details are unclear: need confirmation of training/test splits (4k source labeled, 100 target labeled, 2k unlabeled parallel, 1.9k test) and whether unlabeled parallel docs were varied from 200 to 2k.  
- Inconsistency between Figure 1 and Table 1 for CL-KCCA’s performance at 2k unlabeled needs resolution.  
- The low-rank assumption for a fully observed document-term matrix is not sufficiently justified and requires more explanation.  
- Need discussion on why TSL often outperforms CCA-like multi-view methods and how low-rank enforcement compares to other techniques.  
- Provide running-time comparisons of the projected gradient descent algorithm against CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA for practical assessment.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before Eq. (2) and remove “such as” before Eqs. (4) and (5).  
- Overall recommendation: promising, simple, effective method; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing technical and experimental clarifications.
