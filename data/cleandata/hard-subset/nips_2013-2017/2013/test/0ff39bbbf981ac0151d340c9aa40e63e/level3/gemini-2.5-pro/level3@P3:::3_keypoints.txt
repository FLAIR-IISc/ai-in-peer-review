- Proposes a two-step method: build a unified document-term matrix with concatenated vocabularies, use matrix completion to infer missing cross-lingual features, then apply CL-LSI for low-dimensional shared representations.  
- Main contribution is introducing matrix completion to densify multilingual document vectors before dimensionality reduction.  
- Technical formulation and projected gradient descent optimization are sound.  
- Experimental results show significant improvements on cross-lingual sentiment classification versus several baselines.  
- Major concern: improvements may be an artifact of LSI’s feature scaling interacting favorably with a linear SVM, not the matrix completion step.  
- Evaluation uses only linear SVM and LSI, while baselines (CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) may produce differently scaled representations, creating a confound.  
- Clarity issue: “unified document-term matrix” is ambiguous—unclear if vocabulary columns are language-specific surface forms or mapped to a language-agnostic space.  
- Clarity issue: experimental setup details (datasets, parameters, splits, protocol) are fragmented and should be consolidated for reproducibility.  
- Originality: the combination of matrix completion with LSI for cross-lingual document densification appears novel and well-positioned relative to prior work.  
- Significance: approach could be valuable for low-resource cross-lingual learning if results are shown to be robust.  
- Suggested fix: re-evaluate using classifiers insensitive to per-feature scaling (e.g., Random Forests, AdaBoost) to verify that gains stem from matrix completion.  
- Recommendation: promising and novel but current evaluation is insufficient; recommend rejection now with encouragement to resubmit after addressing experimental concerns.
