- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to build a complete parallel document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional representations.
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification using multilingual Amazon product reviews.
- Shows better performance than several baselines, especially when few parallel bilingual documents are available.
- Method is technically sound with clear explanation of components.
- Optimization algorithm and convergence analysis for the matrix completion step are well-explained and thorough.
- Experimental results are well-presented with clear comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Concern: uses a linear SVM which may be an unsuitable classifier for this task.
- Concern: performance gains might be due to LSI scaling rather than the proposed matrix completion method.
- Concern: positive results could be an artifact of LSI outperforming OPCA/CCA specifically with a linear SVM.
- Recommended fix: evaluate with a classifier insensitive to per-feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees in Weka).
- Significance: approach could advance cross-language text classification if results are robust; matrix completion is an interesting non-linear inference technique.
- Novelty: using matrix completion to bridge language feature spaces is new and well-motivated.
- Clarity issue: phrasing about a "unified document-term matrix" is ambiguous regarding language-agnostic vs. language-specific terms.
- Clarity issue: experimental setup description is fragmented and partially buried in Table 1, making reproduction and understanding harder.
- Overall: well-written and promising, but needs additional evaluation and clarity revisions to address robustness and presentation concerns.
