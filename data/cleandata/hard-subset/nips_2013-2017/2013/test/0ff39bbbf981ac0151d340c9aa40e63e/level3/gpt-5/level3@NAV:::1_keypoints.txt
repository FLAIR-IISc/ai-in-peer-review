- Proposed method: two-step pipeline—matrix completion via PGD with trace-norm and L1 regularization, then LSI on completed matrix for classification.
- The algorithm is technically sound with a clear convergence argument via non-expansive operator composition.
- Empirical results are strong, especially in low parallel-data regimes.
- Novelty: the completion-then-LSI decomposition is an appealing twist relative to prior cross-lingual projection methods.
- Major methodological concern: low-rank assumption for document–term matrices is not justified—sparsity does not imply low rank and documents may not be linear mixtures.
- Request empirical or theoretical support for low-rank premise (e.g., spectral decay/empirical rank plots or translation-induced co-occurrence theory).
- Questioning the need for an extra LSI step after estimating M*: clarify motivation (computational/density reasons) and why LSI over PCA/OPCA or a direct nuclear-norm-constrained classifier.
- Request ablation study comparing completion-only vs LSI-only vs both to isolate contributions.
- Clarity fixes requested: correct (ij) notation, remove “such as” before Eqs. (4)/(5), and state stopping criteria, iteration counts, and typical ranks selected.
- Scalability/runtime concerns: report wall-clock times, scalability, and a running-time comparison of PGD vs CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.
- Experimental details to clarify: explicitly separate effects of labeled source vs unlabeled parallel data; confirm train/test protocol and parallel-data ranges used.
- Reconcile reported discrepancies: why CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 but not Table 1, and provide intuition for why TSL often beats CCA methods.
- Pros summarized: simple, principled, effective; convergence guarantee; strong performance with little parallel data.
- Cons summarized: low-rank assumption not fully justified; missing runtime/scalability info and key ablations; some experimental and presentation details unclear.
