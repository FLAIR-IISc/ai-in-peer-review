- Proposes a novel two-step method: matrix completion on a partially observed bilingual document-term matrix, followed by LSA to produce low-dimensional cross-lingual representations that require only a small set of parallel documents.  
- Extends prior cross-lingual methods (e.g., CL-LSI, CL-KCCA) by introducing matrix completion as a preprocessing step to fill missing entries before dimensionality reduction.  
- Formulates a convex optimization problem incorporating rank minimization, sparsity, and nonnegativity, and provides a convergence proof for the projected gradient descent algorithm despite non-smoothness.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on 18 Amazon cross-lingual sentiment tasks against multiple baselines (TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) showing consistent outperformance and large gains (8.59–14.61% average accuracy) with only 100 labeled target documents.  
- Clear and reproducible presentation: well-structured math and algorithms, full reporting of key parameters (γ, ρ, k) and selection procedures, plus learning curves demonstrating stability with as few as 200 parallel documents.  
- Original contribution: novel application of matrix completion to capture cross-lingual co-occurrence patterns before dimensionality reduction, distinct from translation-based approaches.  
- Significant practical impact: effectively bridges disjoint feature spaces with minimal parallel data and is likely extendable beyond sentiment analysis to other cross-lingual tasks.  
- Minor weaknesses: introduction could better motivate matrix completion and cite prior NLP matrix-completion work; discussion of computational scalability for larger datasets is limited.  
- Overall verdict: technical and experimental strengths far outweigh minor issues; strong theoretical and practical contribution with a recommendation for acceptance.
