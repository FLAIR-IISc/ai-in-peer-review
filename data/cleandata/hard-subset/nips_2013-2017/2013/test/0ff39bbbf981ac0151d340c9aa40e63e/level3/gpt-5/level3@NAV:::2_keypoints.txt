- Two-step method: convex matrix completion (trace-norm + sparsity) optimized by projected gradient descent with convergence guarantees, followed by LSI to obtain shared semantic space.  
- Key novelty: complete missing cross-lingual features before LSI, unlike CL-LSI/OPCA/KCCA and multilingual topic models.  
- Empirical gains: outperforms bag-of-words, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA on Amazon Reviews (EN/FR/DE/JA) across 18 transfer tasks.  
- Robustness: strong performance even with as few as 200 parallel documents.  
- Theoretical/algorithmic quality: sound convex formulation with a clear proximal-style algorithm and convergence proof.  
- Experimental rigor: careful parameter selection, appropriate monolingual SVM, and thorough baseline comparisons.  
- Suggestion: report runtime and memory profiles (SVD cost per iteration) to demonstrate scalability.  
- Suggestion: add an ablation isolating the effect of matrix completion (e.g., LSI on zero-filled or naïve imputations).  
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters γ, ρ, and k beyond single-task tuning.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized and reproducible with algorithmic details and selection procedures reported.  
- Minor clarity improvement: better motivate matrix completion in the Introduction with intuitive examples and explain nonnegativity and ℓ1 regularization choices.  
- Originality: novel application of convex matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Significance: meaningful improvements over strong baselines and portability to other tasks, but would be strengthened by experiments beyond sentiment/Amazon.  
- Pros summarized: principled formulation with guarantees, consistent sizable gains, robustness with scarce parallel data, clear and reproducible presentation.  
- Cons summarized: limited task/domain scope, lack of runtime/scale analysis, missing ablation isolating completion vs. LSI, and weaker introduction motivation.
