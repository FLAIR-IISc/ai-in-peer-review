- Paper proposes a two-step cross-language classification: convex matrix completion on a unified doc–term matrix, then LSI (SVD) on the completed matrix for SVM training.  
- Completion objective: trace-norm (low-rank) + ℓ1 sparsity + squared loss on observed entries + weak quadratic regularizer on missing entries + nonnegativity constraints.  
- Optimization: projected gradient descent with singular value thresholding and nonnegativity projection; convergence proof via non-expansive operators.  
- Experiments: 18 Amazon review transfer tasks (EN↔{FR,DE,JP} across domains) show consistent gains over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA, especially when parallel data is scarce.  
- Relation to prior work: contrasts with translation pipelines and direct representation methods (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA); novelty is imputing missing cross-lingual features via convex completion before LSI.  
- Strength: principled convex formulation with convergence guarantee and simple, coherent method.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance across many tasks and robustness at low parallel-data regimes (np=200).  
- Strength: method leverages both monolingual co-occurrence and limited parallel data effectively.  
- Strength: clear presentation of optimization algorithm and convergence argument.  
- Weakness: low-rank assumption is asserted but not justified; need to articulate the modeling assumption (e.g., topic/LSA view) and when it holds cross-lingually.  
- Weakness: unclear necessity of second-stage LSI after completion; need ablations comparing (a) LSI on incomplete matrix, (b) completion only, (c) completion+PCA/NMF, or direct training on M*.  
- Weakness: experimental data usage/protocol ambiguous — clarify labeled/unlabeled counts per task and whether only np is varied in Sec.5.3.  
- Weakness: reconcile Figure/Table inconsistency where CL-KCCA appears better in some plots but not in the table (explain variance, k, averaging).  
- Weakness: runtime and scalability concerns due to repeated SVDs per iteration; report wall-clock times, complexity comparisons, and whether truncated/approx SVDs were used.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity and optimization details are missing — report sensitivity to γ, ρ, k, τ, stopping criteria, iterations, and ablations for nonnegativity/ℓ1 terms.  
- Weakness: need to disentangle contributions of labeled source data vs. unlabeled parallel data (ablation reporting).  
- Weakness: add conceptual intuition relative to CCA and, if possible, a toy example showing when completion+LSI outperforms CCA.  
- Quality: technically sound convex relaxation and correct convergence argument, but stronger modeling justification and ablations required.  
- Clarity: generally well written; clarify protocol, reconcile figures/tables, and add suggested ablations.  
- Originality: novel combination of convex completion on a unified cross-lingual matrix followed by LSI; simple but original twist on prior pipelines.  
- Significance: practical utility shown by gains and robustness; broader impact would follow from other domains and runtime/ablation analyses.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled method with proof, consistent accuracy gains (esp. with few parallel docs), leverages monolingual+bilingual signal, simple to implement.  
- Cons against acceptance: inadequate justification for low-rank assumption and post-completion LSI, missing ablations/runtime, experimental ambiguity and figure/table inconsistency.  
- Minor edits: fix notation “(ij)”→“(i,j)”, remove “such as” before Eqs.(4),(5), define symbols consistently (Y, Ŷ, E), and specify settings (np, k) in captions.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clarifying protocol, reconciling figures/tables, and adding brief ablations and runtime reporting.
