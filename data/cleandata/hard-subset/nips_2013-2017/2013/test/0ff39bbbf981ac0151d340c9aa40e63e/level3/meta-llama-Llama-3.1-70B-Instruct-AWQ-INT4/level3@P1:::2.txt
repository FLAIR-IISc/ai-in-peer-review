The paper "A Novel Two-Step Method for Cross Language Representation Learning" proposes a new approach to cross language text classification by leveraging a set of parallel bilingual documents. The method consists of two steps: first, a matrix completion problem is formulated to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then latent semantic indexing is applied to the obtained matrix to induce a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation. The proposed method is evaluated on cross language sentiment classification tasks on Amazon product reviews, and the experimental results demonstrate that it outperforms several other cross language representation learning methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and methodology. The introduction provides a good background on cross language text classification and the challenges associated with it. The related work section is comprehensive, covering various approaches to cross language text classification, including machine translation, domain adaptation, and multilingual representation learning. The authors also clearly explain their proposed method, including the matrix completion and latent semantic indexing steps.

The experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing the proposed method to several baselines, including mono-lingual and cross-lingual methods. The results show that the proposed method outperforms the baselines, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small. The learning curves also demonstrate strong and stable performance even with relatively small amounts of parallel/unlabeled data.

The paper is original in its application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning. The use of matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification is a novel contribution, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness.

The significance of the paper lies in its demonstration that matrix completion can be an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and potentially other tasks. The proposed method has the potential to improve the performance of cross language text classification systems, especially in scenarios where parallel bilingual documents are limited.

However, there is a minor suggestion for improvement in clarity. The introduction could better motivate the use of matrix completion. While the authors mention that matrix completion can capture important and robust low-rank information based on word co-occurrence patterns, they could provide more context on why this is particularly useful for cross language text classification.

In terms of the NIPS conference reviewing guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance. The paper is technically sound, with a clear and well-organized presentation of the methodology and experimental results. The writing is clear and concise, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning. The paper is original in its application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning, and the experimental results demonstrate its significance.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to cross language text classification using matrix completion and latent semantic indexing.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing the proposed method to several baselines and demonstrating its effectiveness.
* The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and methodology.
* The use of matrix completion as a preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification is a novel contribution with potential applications to other tasks.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a clearer motivation for the use of matrix completion in the introduction.
* The experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including more baselines or comparing the proposed method to other state-of-the-art methods.

Overall, the paper is well-written, original, and significant, making it a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
