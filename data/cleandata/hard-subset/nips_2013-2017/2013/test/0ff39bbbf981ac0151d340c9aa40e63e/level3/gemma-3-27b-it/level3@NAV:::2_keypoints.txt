- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual method: use matrix completion to fill a sparse bilingual document-term matrix, then apply LSA for dimensionality reduction.  
- Leverages parallel bilingual documents to bridge disjoint feature spaces between languages.  
- Novel application of matrix completion (from recommender systems) to cross-lingual NLP.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on Amazon cross-lingual sentiment classification, outperforming several baselines.  
- Particularly robust and effective when only limited parallel data are available.  
- Positions work relative to prior approaches (machine translation, CL-LSI, CL-KCCA) and highlights advantages of the two-step process.  
- Technically sound: clear mathematical formulation, projected gradient descent optimization, and convergence analysis.  
- Thorough experimental setup with comprehensive baselines and informative learning curves.  
- Excellent reproducibility: detailed reporting of parameters and selection procedures.  
- Weakness: introduction could better motivate matrix completion by explicitly connecting low-rank structure of document-term matrices to its benefits.  
- Recommendation: Strongly Accept (meets criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance).
