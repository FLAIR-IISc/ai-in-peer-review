- Proposes Two-Step Learning (TSL): matrix completion to fill missing cross-lingual term entries, then LSI to learn low-dimensional cross-lingual representations.  
- Formulates matrix completion as recovering a low-rank, non-negative, sparse matrix via convex optimization.  
- Optimization solved with projected gradient descent and a proven convergence guarantee.  
- Novelty: explicitly imputes missing cross-lingual term co-occurrences before dimensionality reduction, unlike prior CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA approaches.  
- Empirical evaluation is extensive and rigorous across 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks, showing consistent superior performance to strong baselines.  
- Demonstrates robustness in low-resource settings, achieving strong results with very few parallel documents (e.g., ~200).  
- Well-written and organized with transparent experimental details and reproducible parameter reporting.  
- Minor clarity issue: motivation for matrix completion should be introduced earlier in the Introduction.  
- Scalability concern: SVD at each optimization iteration could be computationally expensive for very large matrices and needs discussion.  
- Significance: reframing cross-lingual learning as imputation-plus-projection is insightful and likely impactful for low-resource cross-lingual/cross-domain NLP.
