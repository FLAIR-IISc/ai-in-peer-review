- Proposes two approaches for risk minimization under random classification noise: unbiased loss estimators and reduction to weighted 0-1 loss.
- Provides theoretical guarantees for both approaches and similar risk bounds for the two modifications of surrogate losses.
- Demonstrates effectiveness via experiments on synthetic and benchmark datasets, with strong performance even at high noise rates.
- Delivers general results for convex surrogate losses without assumptions on the true distribution.
- Resolves a theoretical gap by showing practical methods (biased SVM, weighted logistic regression) are provably noise-tolerant.
- Methods are straightforward to implement, making them appealing for practitioners.
- Notation and organization are unclear in places; some sections and equations are hard to follow.
- Lacks intuition on why the proposed methods outperform alternatives like CCA.
- Does not discuss algorithm running time or compare computational cost to methods such as CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.
- Experimental evaluation could be expanded to show robustness across more noise types and datasets.
- Minor fixes needed: resolve notation inconsistencies and remove unnecessary phrases.
- Overall, a significant contribution with both theoretical and empirical merits, but clarity and additional experiments/runtime comparisons would strengthen the paper.
