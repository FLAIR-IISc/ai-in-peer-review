- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion on a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix, followed by LSI for low-dimensional representations.
- Differs from prior work (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) by filling missing entries before dimensionality reduction rather than applying LSI to the incomplete matrix.
- Provides a convergence proof for the projected gradient descent algorithm used for matrix completion.
- Evaluated on 18 cross-lingual sentiment classification tasks using Amazon reviews.
- Outperforms four strong baselines (TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA), with statistically significant gains (e.g., +14.61% over TBOW on EFB).
- Experimental setup is rigorous: 10 repeated runs per task, reported standard deviations, and appropriate statistical testing.
- Demonstrates robustness and practical effectiveness with very small parallel datasets (as few as 200 documents).
- Uses a theoretically sound matrix completion formulation with ℓ₁ and trace norm regularization.
- Experimental design and writing are clear and highly reproducible; all parameters and procedures are documented.
- Novel application of matrix completion to bridge cross-lingual feature spaces and clearly distinguished from related approaches.
- Significant practical impact for low-resource cross-lingual tasks, delivering substantial accuracy improvements.
- Weakness: introduction could better motivate why matrix completion is necessary before presenting the method.
- Weakness: lacks an ablation study quantifying the impact of individual components (e.g., ℓ₁ vs. trace norm).
- Verdict: strong, well-executed contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
