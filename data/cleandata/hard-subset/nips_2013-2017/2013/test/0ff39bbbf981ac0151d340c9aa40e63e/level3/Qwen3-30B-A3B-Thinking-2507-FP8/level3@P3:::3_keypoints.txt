- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion to fill a bilingual document-term matrix, then CL-LSI for low-dimensional representations.  
- Novelty: first use matrix completion to bridge vocabulary gaps between languages before dimensionality reduction.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for the projected gradient descent optimization.  
- Empirical results on Amazon reviews show improvements over several baselines, especially with few parallel bilingual documents.  
- Strength: matrix completion offers a principled way to handle missing data in bilingual document-term matrices.  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis of the optimization algorithm.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments (18 tasks) and ablation on varying amounts of parallel data.  
- Primary weakness: reported gains may be a scaling artifact due to linear SVM sensitivity and LSI scaling, not the completion step.  
- Questionable comparison to CL-LSI since both use LSI; need to isolate the effect of matrix completion versus LSI.  
- Methodological fix suggested: evaluate with classifiers insensitive to feature scaling (e.g., boosted trees or random forests).  
- Clarity issue: ambiguous definition of the “unified document-term matrix” (language-agnostic vs language-specific terms).  
- Clarity issue: experimental setup is fragmented across text and Table 1, making replication/understanding difficult.  
- Recommendations: run scale-insensitive classifiers, clarify unified vocabulary, consolidate experimental setup, and add ablation studies isolating completion vs LSI.  
- Significance: approach is potentially important and novel, but its impact depends on validating robustness to the scaling artifact.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and interesting but undermined by possible scaling artifact; recommendation = Weak Accept pending additional experiments.
