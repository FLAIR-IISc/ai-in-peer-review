- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to form a unified document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual representations.  
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification of Amazon reviews and shows significant improvement over prior methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes a thorough related work review.  
- Experimental setup is described, results are clear and analyses are detailed.  
- Method is technically sound with thorough analysis of the matrix completion optimization algorithm.  
- Originality is high: novel combination of matrix completion and LSI and clear distinction from prior work.  
- Significance is high: potential to improve cross-language text classification on real-world data.  
- Concern: uses a linear SVM for monolingual classification, which may not be the best choice—robustness with other classifiers should be tested.  
- Concern: insufficient detail on experimental preprocessing and how the document-term matrix was constructed.  
- Clarity issue: ambiguous description of the “unified document-term matrix” (language-agnostic vs. language-specific terms unclear).  
- Clarity/organization issue: experimental setup details are fragmented (spread across Table 1) and hard to follow.  
- Recommendations: evaluate with additional classifiers (e.g., boosted decision trees), provide preprocessing and matrix construction details, and clarify/reorganize ambiguous experimental descriptions.
