- Proposes a two-step cross-domain classification: matrix completion via projected gradient descent with low-rank/sparsity constraints, then LSI for dimensionality reduction and classification.
- Core idea is sound and addresses bridging disjoint feature spaces in cross-lingual learning.
- Combining matrix completion and LSI is interesting for capturing latent semantic relationships across languages.
- Theoretical convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent method is a strong positive.
- Empirical results show favorable performance on cross-lingual sentiment classification.
- Unclear motivation for applying LSI after estimating the completed matrix M; need stronger justification for choosing LSI over alternatives like PCA.
- External data usage (labeled source vs. unlabeled parallel data) should be reported separately to isolate their individual contributions.
- Experimental setup lacks detail and confirmation of dataset sizes (4k source, 100 target labeled, 2k unlabeled parallel, 1.9k test); clarify whether unlabeled-doc sweeps in Sec. 5.3 apply to all tasks.
- Discrepancy between Figure 1 and Table 1 (CL-KCCA vs. TSL performance at 2k unlabeled docs) must be resolved.
- The assumption that a fully observed document-term matrix is low-rank needs further justification and explicit assumptions.
- Need intuition/explanation for why TSL often outperforms methods like CCA despite CCA’s suitability for multiview tasks.
- Provide a running-time comparison between projected gradient descent and baselines (CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA) to assess practicality.
- Minor editorial fixes: change “(ij)” to “(i,j)” before Eq. (2) and remove “such as” before Eqs. (4) and (5).
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound with convergence proof, strong empirical performance, addresses an important problem, and well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient justification for LSI, incomplete experimental details, inconsistent reported results, weak low-rank assumption explanation, and missing runtime comparison.
- Reviewer recommendation: weak accept pending substantial revisions addressing the above concerns.
