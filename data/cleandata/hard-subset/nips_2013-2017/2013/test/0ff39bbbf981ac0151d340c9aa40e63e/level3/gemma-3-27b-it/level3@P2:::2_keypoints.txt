- Proposed method: two-step approach — complete a partially observed document-term matrix from parallel bilingual documents via matrix completion, then apply LSI to obtain low-dimensional cross-lingual representations.  
- Optimization: matrix completion solved with a projected gradient descent algorithm that has convergence guarantees.  
- Evaluation: tested on cross-lingual sentiment classification (Amazon reviews), outperforming several baselines, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Positioning: situates work among MT-based methods, bilingual-pair approaches, and comparable-corpora techniques; relates to prior CL-LSI work.  
- Novelty: novel use of matrix completion combined with LSI to bridge disjoint cross-lingual feature spaces.  
- Empirical strength: consistently outperforms strong baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA), with significant gains in low-resource scenarios.  
- Thorough evaluation: multiple sentiment tasks and learning curves showing impact of varying amounts of parallel data.  
- Technical soundness: well-motivated algorithmic design with detailed optimization and parameter selection.  
- Clarity & reproducibility: paper is well-written, organized, and reports key parameters and procedures for replication.  
- Weakness — motivation: introduction could better explain intuitively why matrix completion is appropriate for bridging cross-lingual gaps.  
- Weakness — parameter sensitivity: lacks analysis of robustness to variations in γ, ρ, and k.  
- Weakness — scope: experiments limited to Amazon sentiment classification; generalizability to other tasks is untested.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid, strong empirical results, well-documented and relevant contribution.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient parameter-sensitivity analysis and narrow experimental domain may limit perceived robustness and impact.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper with strengths outweighing minor weaknesses; strong accept recommendation (score 8/10).
