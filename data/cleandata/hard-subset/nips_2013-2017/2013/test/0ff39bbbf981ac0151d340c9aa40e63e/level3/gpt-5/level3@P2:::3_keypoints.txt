- Proposes a two-step cross-language representation method: convex matrix completion (trace norm + L1 + nonnegativity) followed by LSI (SVD) on the completed matrix for low-dimensional shared representations.  
- Optimization solved by a projected gradient–shrinkage (proximal-style) algorithm with a convergence argument.  
- Experiments on multilingual Amazon reviews show consistent gains over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA, especially with few parallel documents.  
- Novelty: first to apply matrix completion to impute cross-lingual features prior to dimensionality reduction in this domain.  
- Technical soundness is reasonable, but the proof should clarify when composition of shrinkage and projection yields optimality (proximal composition concerns).  
- Empirical gains are encouraging but may be confounded by LSI post-processing (whitening/rescaling) rather than the completion step itself.  
- Baselines are not perfectly matched; missing ablation studies prevent isolating the contribution of matrix completion.  
- Missing ablations/controls recommended: LSI on the unified matrix without completion, classification directly on the completed matrix without LSI, and simple imputation/zero-fill comparisons.  
- Also recommend consistent feature normalization across methods to remove scaling artifacts.  
- Suggest testing classifiers less sensitive to feature rescaling (e.g., boosted trees, random forests, k-NN with cosine) to rule out SVM/LSI advantages.  
- Request statistical significance testing across the 10 runs to validate empirical differences.  
- Efficiency concerns: report runtime, memory usage, and number of SVDs per run for scalability assessment.  
- Clarity issues: define precisely the “unified document–term matrix” (concatenated language-specific vocabularies vs. shared translated columns).  
- Experimental setup is dispersed; consolidate preprocessing, construction of Ω/Ω̂, parallel/non-parallel row formation, splits, hyperparameter grids, and model selection protocol.  
- Minor clarity fixes: define all symbols at first use and ensure consistent notation for indicators Y, Ŷ.  
- Originality: the combination is new and nontrivial though the formulation and algorithm are straightforward.  
- Significance: would be strong if gains persist after suggested controls; currently impact is weakened without ablations.  
- Pros: principled formulation, convergence-guaranteed algorithm, strong empirical performance across 18 tasks, robustness with few parallel docs, and novel angle vs. CL-LSI/KCCA/OPCA.  
- Cons: potential LSI/scale confound, missing ablations, unclear unified matrix and experimental protocol, limited computational cost analysis, and convergence proof needs stronger justification.  
- Suggestions: run proposed ablations, evaluate scale-robust classifiers, report time/memory and sensitivity to γ/ρ/µ/k, clarify vocabulary/Ω construction, and consider releasing code.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending additional analyses; likely acceptable if suggested experiments and clarifications are provided.
