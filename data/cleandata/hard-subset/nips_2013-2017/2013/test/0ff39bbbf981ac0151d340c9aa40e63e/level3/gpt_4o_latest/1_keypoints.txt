- Proposes a novel two-step method: matrix completion on a sparse document-term matrix followed by LSI for cross-language text classification using labeled source and unlabeled parallel bilingual documents.
- Uses projected gradient descent for matrix completion, then LSI to obtain low-dimensional representations for training a monolingual classifier.
- Well-motivated solution to disjoint feature spaces with strong empirical results on multilingual Amazon reviews.
- Novelty and simplicity: effective combination of matrix completion and LSI; inferring missing entries is a novel contribution.
- Provides a theoretical convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm.
- Empirical performance: outperforms CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA across 18 tasks and is robust with limited parallel data.
- Presentation is clear with detailed explanations of the optimization algorithm and experiments.
- Choice of LSI is not adequately justified over alternatives like PCA or CCA; the role of LSI in mitigating sparsity needs clarification.
- The low-rank assumption for a fully observed document-term matrix is questionable and requires discussion or empirical validation.
- Experimental setup is unclear (training data composition); reconcile discrepancies between Figure 1 and Table 1.
- Computational efficiency and running time are not reported; should be compared to baseline methods.
- Results should separately quantify the contributions of labeled source data and unlabeled parallel data.
- Minor issues: fix notation inconsistencies and redundant phrases.
- Provide more intuition on why TSL outperforms others, particularly regarding the low-rank condition and its interaction with matrix completion.
- Recommendation: accept pending revisions to address LSI justification, low-rank assumption, and experimental clarity.
