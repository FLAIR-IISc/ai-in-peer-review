- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language method: matrix completion to build a complete parallel document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional representations.
- Method requires only a small set of unlabeled parallel documents, avoiding full translation of corpora.
- Related to prior cross-lingual learning work but more efficient and scalable than translation-based approaches.
- Thorough literature review covering translation, bilingual lexicons, bilingual word pairs, and parallel-corpus methods.
- Evaluated on multilingual Amazon sentiment classification tasks.
- Experimental results show significant outperformance of several state-of-the-art cross-language methods.
- Method maintains good performance even with very few unlabeled parallel bilingual documents.
- Paper clearly explains the matrix completion formulation, LSI step, and the optimization algorithm used.
- Originality: novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning.
- Significance: demonstrates matrix completion as an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual tasks with limited parallel data.
- Technical quality is sound and experiments are thoroughly presented (learning curves and comparisons).
- Clarity is generally high, but the introduction should better motivate matrix completion and provide more background on its applications.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution, well-written and organized.
- Acceptance arguments: novel, effective, scalable, strong empirical results, clear presentation.
- Rejection concerns: need stronger motivation and more background on matrix completion.
- Reviewer rating: 9/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
