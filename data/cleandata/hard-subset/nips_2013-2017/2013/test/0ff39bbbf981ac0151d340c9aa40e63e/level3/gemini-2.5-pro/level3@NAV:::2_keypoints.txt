- Proposes a two-step method: matrix completion to impute missing cross-lingual term entries, followed by LSA to obtain low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Builds a unified document-term matrix where non-parallel documents have missing entries for the other language’s vocabulary.  
- Frames imputation as a matrix completion problem solved with projected gradient descent to recover a low-rank matrix.  
- Applies standard LSA on the completed matrix to derive dense cross-lingual embeddings.  
- Novelty lies in explicitly treating missing cross-lingual information as a matrix completion preprocessing step, distinguishing it from prior CL-LSA and projection-based methods.  
- Technically sound and reproducible.  
- Extensive, thorough experiments on a standard cross-lingual sentiment dataset, comparing to monolingual and strong cross-lingual baselines (e.g., CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).  
- Learning-curve analysis shows robustness even with small amounts of parallel data.  
- Consistently outperforms strong baselines and advances the state-of-the-art, with implications for other cross-modal learning tasks.  
- Well-written and organized; minor suggestion to strengthen the Introduction’s motivation for matrix completion.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
