- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual classification method: matrix completion on a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI (SVD) on the completed matrix followed by a linear SVM.  
- Novelty: first imputes missing cross-language blocks via nuclear-norm + l1 + nonnegativity regularized completion, then reduces dimensionality—distinct from CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA.  
- Optimization: uses a scalable projected gradient method with shrinkage and nonnegativity projection; convergence is established.  
- Empirical claim: outperforms TBOW and generally beats CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA on Amazon multilingual sentiment tasks, especially with few parallel documents.  
- Strength: conceptually simple and practical—unified incomplete matrix captures within- and cross-language co-occurrence before completion.  
- Strength: clear, scalable algorithm with convergence guarantees and described projections/shrinkage.  
- Strength: strong empirical results across 18 adaptation directions, robust when parallel data are scarce.  
- Strength: paper well written and situates itself relative to CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA literature.  
- Question: unclear rationale for the LSI step—computational, statistical (denoising), or both—and why LSI vs PCA/NMF or enforcing rank during completion.  
- Suggested ablation: compare using M* directly, LSI on incomplete M0 (missing=0), and LSI on only fully observed parallel rows to isolate completion vs LSI benefits.  
- Modeling concern: low-rank claim for BoW is not obvious—need to clarify that nuclear norm enforces approximate low-rank and provide intuition (shared topics/sentiments).  
- Missingness issue: structured block missingness (whole language per doc) violates random missingness assumptions; discuss identifiability and whether the ρ-term is regularizer or prior.  
- CCA comparison: need explanation why TSL outperforms CL-KCCA—hypothesis that completion leverages non-parallel monolingual data; recommend analyses (alignment quality, topic coherence).  
- Experimental clarity: request explicit protocol details on which documents are used for representation learning, labeled training, unlabeled parallel pairs, and test splits.  
- Inconsistency: Fig.1 vs Table1 discrepancy where CL-KCCA beats TSL on some settings—reconcile averaging/variance/parameters.  
- Scalability: provide runtime and memory comparisons (including SVD) vs CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA as functions of vocab size, docs, and parallel pairs; report iterations and wall-clock times.  
- Parameter sensitivity: include sensitivity plots for γ, ρ, and k since parameters tuned on one dataset are transferred to others.  
- Statistical significance: add significance tests for small differences (beyond means and SDs).  
- Quality assessment: optimization and convergence arguments are sound; theory would be strengthened by discussing identifiability under structured missingness.  
- Clarity assessment: generally clear, but experimental protocol and data roles should be spelled out and minor editorial fixes applied.  
- Originality: combination of bilingual matrix completion followed by LSI appears novel relative to prior cross-lingual and multi-view methods.  
- Significance: empirical gains in low-parallel-data regimes are practically meaningful if runtime is competitive.  
- Minor edits: notation fixes (e.g., (i,j) before Eq.2), remove “such as” before Eqs.4–5, define symbols on first use, and state τ selection/backtracking.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple general method with convergence guarantee, robust empirical performance, and effective use of parallel and non-parallel data.  
- Cons for acceptance: need clearer justification of modeling assumptions, resolve experimental ambiguities, and add runtime and ablation analyses.  
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on clarifying experimental protocol, reconciling CL-KCCA discrepancy, and adding runtime/comparative ablations and justifications for LSI and low-rank assumptions.
