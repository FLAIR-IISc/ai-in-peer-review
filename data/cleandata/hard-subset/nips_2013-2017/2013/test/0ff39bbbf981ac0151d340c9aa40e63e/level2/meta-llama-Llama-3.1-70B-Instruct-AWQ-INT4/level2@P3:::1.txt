**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which aims to bridge the feature spaces of different languages by exploiting a set of parallel bilingual documents. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then induces a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix. The proposed method is evaluated by conducting a set of experiments with cross-language sentiment classification tasks on Amazon product reviews.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a new approach to cross-language representation learning, which is different from existing methods that rely on machine translation or bilingual lexicons.
2. **Effective use of parallel data**: The method makes effective use of parallel bilingual documents to learn cross-lingual representations, which is a valuable resource for cross-language text classification.
3. **Good experimental results**: The paper presents extensive experimental results on cross-language sentiment classification tasks, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity of the method**: The proposed method involves two steps, matrix completion and latent semantic indexing, which may be computationally expensive and require careful tuning of hyperparameters.
2. **Limited analysis of the method**: The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its theoretical guarantees and limitations.
3. **Comparison to other methods**: While the paper compares the proposed method to other cross-language representation learning methods, the comparison is limited to a few methods, and more comprehensive comparisons would be useful.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the proposed method is well-motivated and clearly explained. The experimental results are extensive and well-presented, and the paper provides a good discussion of the results. However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the method and more comprehensive comparisons to other methods.

**Clarity**

**Originality**
