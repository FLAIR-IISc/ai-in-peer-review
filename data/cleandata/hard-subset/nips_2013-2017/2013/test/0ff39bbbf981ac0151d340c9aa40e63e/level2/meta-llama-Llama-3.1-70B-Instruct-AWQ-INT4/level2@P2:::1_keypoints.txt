- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to build a complete parallel document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional representations.  
- Method targets bridging feature spaces of different languages using a small set of parallel bilingual documents.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: large-scale dataset and multiple baselines.  
- Method is technically sound with a well-motivated matrix completion formulation.  
- Uses projected gradient descent to solve the optimization, which is well-justified.  
- Experimental results show significant improvements over baselines and robustness to the size of unlabeled parallel data.  
- Strength: effective use of a small number of unlabeled parallel documents to bridge languages.  
- Limitation: assumes availability of parallel bilingual documents, which may be unrealistic for low-resource languages.  
- Limitation: uses a simple linear SVM for monolingual classification; other models (e.g., deep networks) were not explored.  
- Originality: combination of matrix completion and LSI for cross-language representation is innovative despite components being known.  
- Significance: contributes to cross-language text classification and more robust cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, technically sound, significant contribution; rating 8/10 with confidence 8/10.
