This paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which is a crucial task in natural language processing. The authors address the challenge of cross-language text classification by exploiting a set of parallel bilingual documents. The proposed method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages and then induces a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, which outperforms other cross-language representation learning methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.
