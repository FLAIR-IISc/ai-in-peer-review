- Proposes TSL: a two-step method that uses matrix completion on a unified cross-lingual document-term matrix followed by LSI to produce a shared semantic space.  
- Treats missing cross-lingual vocabulary entries as missing (inferred) rather than zeros, improving over CL-LSI-style approaches.  
- Novel and intuitive application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning, with a solid technical contribution and convergence proof.  
- Strong empirical support: outperforms CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA on 18 cross-language sentiment tasks.  
- Demonstrates practical utility in low-resource settings, performing well with as few as 200 parallel documents.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, methodology, and experimental setup.  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity and scalability of the matrix completion step for large document-term matrices.  
- Needs a more thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (γ, ρ, µ, k) to assess robustness across tasks.  
- Should acknowledge and briefly compare to recent neural/multilingual pre-trained model approaches to situate the work within the current state of the art.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with novel contribution and thorough validation; weaknesses are minor and addressable.
