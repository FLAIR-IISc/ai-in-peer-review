- Proposes a two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion on concatenated document-term matrix followed by LSI for cross-lingual embeddings.
- Uses projected gradient descent for matrix completion and provides convergence analysis for the optimization algorithm.
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over several baselines on Amazon product review datasets.
- Approach is well-motivated and conceptually sound, leveraging parallel data to bridge language gaps.
- Theoretical analysis and convergence guarantees are a clear technical strength.
- Ablation study on the amount of parallel data is valuable and supports claims.
- Experimental setup lacks detail (e.g., SVM kernel, parameter tuning, validation procedure), harming reproducibility.
- No thorough parameter sensitivity analysis for matrix completion hyperparameters (γ, ρ, τ) or their assumptions.
- Related work discussion is superficial; distinctions from multilingual topic models and links to collaborative filtering are underexplored.
- Overstated claim about needing only a small parallel set ignores unsupervised/semi-supervised translation methods.
- Practical significance of the performance gains is not well discussed (e.g., annotation effort saved).
- Assumes high-quality document-level parallel data and does not evaluate robustness to noisy or misaligned translations.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically rigorous approach with promising empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental detail, limited related-work depth, and unclear practical impact.
- Overall assessment: weak accept contingent on addressing experimental detail, parameter analysis, and related-work/robustness concerns.
