- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to build a parallel document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional representations.
- Targets cross-language text classification using parallel bilingual documents.
- Shows strong empirical performance, especially when parallel bilingual data are scarce.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains motivation and method clearly.
- Provides a thorough related-work review and a clear description of the optimization algorithm.
- Uses a simple linear SVM for monolingual classification in experiments.
- Assumes availability of parallel bilingual documents, which may be unrealistic in many real-world scenarios.
- Does not evaluate performance when parallel documents are absent or severely limited.
- Lacks experiments with more complex classifiers (e.g., deep neural networks) to test generality.
- Does not present a detailed computational-complexity analysis of the proposed method.
- Originality: significant contribution via the novel two-step approach.
- Significance: addresses an important NLP task with potential impact on cross-language classification.
- Recommendation: accept for publication.
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.
