- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual pipeline: convex matrix completion on a concatenated bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI (SVD) and linear SVMs for classification.  
- Completion objective uses nuclear-norm, ℓ1 (reduces to linear under nonnegativity), squared loss on observed entries, squared shrinkage on unobserved entries weighted by ρ, and nonnegativity.  
- Optimization via projected gradient descent with singular-value shrinkage and nonnegative projection; convergence argument based on operator non-expansiveness.  
- Empirical evaluation on Amazon multilingual reviews (EN, FR, DE, JA) shows consistent gains over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA, especially when parallel data are scarce.  
- Novelty: explicitly imputes missing cross-lingual features via convex matrix completion before performing LSI, differing from zero-filled or direct CL-LSI/OPCA/KCCA approaches.  
- Strength: simple, clear idea bridging disjoint vocabularies without heavy reliance on large parallel corpora or full translation.  
- Strength: convex formulation with a scalable proximal/projection algorithm and a stated convergence argument under specified step size.  
- Strength: thorough experimental protocol across many language/category pairs and informative analysis of performance vs. number of parallel pairs.  
- Strength: sensible hyperparameter selection and analysis showing larger gains with fewer parallel pairs.  
- Weakness: the shrinkage penalty ρ biases missing entries toward zero and may suppress cross-lingual signals; sensitivity to ρ needs deeper analysis.  
- Weakness: convergence proof lacks practical stopping criteria, convergence rates, per-iteration complexity, and details on SVD routine (full vs. truncated/randomized).  
- Weakness: missing runtime, memory footprint, and average rank reports to assess practical scalability on large vocabularies/corpora.  
- Weakness: lacking key ablations — “no completion” (γ=0,ρ=0) and “completion only” (skip LSI) — to isolate contributions of each step.  
- Weakness: additional baselines like multilingual topic models (bilingual LDA) or probabilistic/weighted matrix factorization with missing data are missing.  
- Weakness: statistical significance testing across runs is not reported despite means and standard deviations.  
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps — unspecified TF-IDF computation (per-language vs. joint), feature normalization, preprocessing, initialization details, and stopping tolerances.  
- Clarity issue: phrase “complete parallel document-term matrix” is misleading; clarify that only paired documents are fully observed.  
- Clarity issue: need better intuition why a second SVD is needed after nuclear-norm minimization since both enforce low-rank structure.  
- Clarity issue: describe how parallel bilingual documents were constructed (human vs. machine translation, alignment quality, domain match).  
- Originality: combining convex nonnegative matrix completion with LSI for bilingual document-term matrices appears novel relative to prior weighted factorization approaches.  
- Significance: empirical gains in low-parallel-data regimes are promising and the method could generalize beyond sentiment classification; adding other domains would strengthen claims.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity plots for γ, ρ, τ; term reweighting/rescaling across languages; learned ranks prior to LSI; and details on constructing pseudo dual-language documents for baselines.  
- Suggestions: add ablations and runtime/memory reports, provide qualitative analyses (cross-lingual neighbors or topics), compare integrated proximal/nonnegative formulations or factorized models, and discuss multi-language extensions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptually simple, convex with convergence guarantee, consistent empirical improvements especially with few parallels, and relevant to NIPS audience.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited baselines/ablations, missing runtime/scalability evidence, and evaluation limited to a single dataset/domain.  
- Overall: solid and promising contribution that would be stronger with additional ablations and computational analysis; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
