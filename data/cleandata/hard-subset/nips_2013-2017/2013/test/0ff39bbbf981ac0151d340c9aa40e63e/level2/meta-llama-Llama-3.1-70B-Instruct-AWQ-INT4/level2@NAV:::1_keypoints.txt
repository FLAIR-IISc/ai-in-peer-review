- Summary: Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation method using matrix completion to form a parallel document-term matrix followed by LSI to obtain low-dimensional representations, evaluated on Amazon reviews.
- Main claim 1: The method effectively learns cross-lingual representations for text classification.
- Main claim 2: The method outperforms existing cross-lingual representation methods, especially with few parallel documents.
- Experimental support: Evaluated on 18 cross-language sentiment tasks, compared to TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA, showing consistent gains (avg +8.59% vs TBOW).
- Data-size robustness: Outperforms other methods across various sizes of unlabeled parallel data for 16/18 tasks.
- Usefulness: Practical application for reducing target-language annotation in cross-language text classification.
- Field knowledge: Demonstrates strong understanding and comprehensive related work review.
- Novelty: Novel combination of matrix completion and LSI for cross-language representation learning.
- Reproducibility/completeness: Provides sufficient experimental setup, dataset, and implementation details for replication.
- Limitation: Requires a (possibly small) set of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents, which may not always be available.
- Constructive criticism: Should more extensively study the effect of parallel data size and provide insights into interpretability of learned representations.
- Pro-acceptance arguments: Novel, effective approach with strong empirical gains and reproducibility.
- Con-acceptance arguments: Dependence on parallel documents and insufficient analysis of data-size effects.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance for publication.
