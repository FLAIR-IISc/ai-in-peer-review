- Proposes a two-step cross-language representation method: complete a partially observed document-term matrix from parallel bilingual documents, then apply LSI to the completed matrix.

- Addresses the challenge of disjoint feature spaces in cross-language text classification with a sensible bridging approach.

- Demonstrates effectiveness on Amazon product reviews, especially when parallel data is limited.

- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and logically motivated.

- Related work section is comprehensive and situates the contribution well.

- Optimization algorithm is detailed and includes convergence analysis and theoretical justification.

- Focuses on the practical scenario of limited parallel data, showing superior performance in that setting.

- Uses a projected gradient descent algorithm with convergence guarantees and presents an informative ablation study on parallel data amount.

- Assumes the document-term matrix is low-rank but fails to justify this assumption or provide empirical rank analysis.

- Selection of the LSI dimensionality k appears arbitrary (values {20,50,100,200,500}) without systematic rationale like cross-validation.

- Experiments are limited to sentiment classification on a single dataset (Amazon reviews), reducing evidence of generality across tasks/datasets.

- Algorithm 1 lacks sufficient implementation details for reproducibility, notably the initialization of M via “nonnegative projection of the rank-1 approximation of M0.”

- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong empirical results with limited parallel data, theoretical convergence, and clear presentation.

- Arguments against acceptance: unjustified low-rank assumption, arbitrary k selection, limited experimental scope, and missing reproducibility details.

- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution that would become stronger with revisions addressing justification of low-rankness, systematic k selection, broader experiments, and clearer algorithmic details.
