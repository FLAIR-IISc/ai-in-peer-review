- Thorough experimental evaluation: 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks (FR/DE/JP) on Amazon reviews.  
- Strong empirical gains: TSL outperforms baselines (TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA) by 8.59–14.61% accuracy.  
- Robustness with scarce data: Ablation shows TSL remains effective with 200–2000 parallel documents.  
- Sound optimization theory: Convergence proof for the projected gradient descent algorithm is technically solid.  
- Clear implementation details: Optimization procedure is well-documented.

- Critical mathematical error: Claim that ℓ1 norm becomes a smooth linear function under nonnegativity is incorrect and undermines the optimization foundation.  
- Weak novelty argument: Using matrix completion before LSI is insufficiently differentiated from prior matrix-completion methods (e.g., cited [11]).  
- Ambiguous baseline usage: Paper fails to specify how baselines exploit unlabeled parallel data, making comparisons unclear/unfair.  
- Missing significance testing: Reported means and SDs lack statistical tests to confirm performance differences.  
- Scalability concerns: No discussion of runtime or memory costs of matrix completion for large vocabularies.

- Fix ℓ1 mischaracterization and clarify its sparsity role (or adopt a smooth alternative if intended).  
- Compare and contrast explicitly with matrix-completion-based prior work to justify novelty.  
- Specify exactly how each baseline uses unlabeled parallel documents to ensure fair comparisons.  
- Add statistical significance tests for reported accuracy gains.  
- Address computational scalability (runtime/memory) of the matrix completion step.

- Overall: Promising approach with solid results but critical technical error, insufficient novelty justification, and experimental ambiguities weaken the contribution.  
- Recommendation: Reject — major revisions required to correct mathematical errors and clarify novelty/experiments before reconsideration.
