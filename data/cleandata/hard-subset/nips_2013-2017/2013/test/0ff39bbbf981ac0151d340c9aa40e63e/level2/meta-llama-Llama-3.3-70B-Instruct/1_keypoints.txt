- Proposes a two-step representation learning method using parallel bilingual documents to bridge feature spaces across languages.  
- Main claim: method outperforms other cross-language representation learning approaches, especially with limited parallel documents.  
- Formulates a matrix completion problem to create a complete parallel document-term matrix, then applies latent semantic indexing for low-dimensional cross-lingual representations.  
- Evaluated on Amazon cross-language sentiment classification tasks and shows significant improvement over baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and includes a thorough review of related work.  
- Provides theoretical analysis and convergence guarantees for the proposed method.  
- Experimental results are comprehensive and well-analyzed across different settings.  
- Strength: novel two-step approach that is theoretically grounded and empirically effective.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments and clear explanations make the contribution accessible and convincing.  
- Weakness: relies on availability of parallel bilingual documents, which may not always exist.  
- Weakness: performance may degrade when the number of parallel documents is very small.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis on robustness to noise and outliers.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novel, effective, broadly applicable method with strong empirical and theoretical support.  
- Con-accept arguments: dependence on parallel data, sensitivity to small parallel sets, and insufficient robustness analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, with suggested additional robustness analysis.
