- Proposes a two-step cross-language method: convex matrix completion with nonnegativity and sparsity, then LSI on the completed matrix for shared representations.  
- Solved by projected gradient descent with shrinkage and includes a convergence proof based on non-expansive operators.  
- Evaluated on 18 English–French/German/Japanese Amazon sentiment tasks and compared to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA.  
- Empirical results show consistent gains, especially when parallel data is scarce.  
- Statistical significance of results is not reported.  
- Computational efficiency and scalability (per-iteration SVD cost, memory, wall-clock time, hardware) are not discussed.  
- Regularizing missing entries toward zero (ρ term) could bias imputations; no ablation study on ρ/µ.  
- Reproducibility is fair with hyperparameter ranges, but implementation details (runtime, stopping criteria, TF-IDF handling) are sparse.  
- Novelty lies in explicitly imputing cross-lingual co-occurrences via convex matrix completion before dimensionality reduction.  
- Method is simple, principled, compatible with standard classifiers, and useful in low-parallel-data regimes.  
- Limited dataset diversity (only Amazon reviews, sentiment) and no evaluation beyond document-level sentiment.  
- No comparison to word- or sentence-level bilingual embeddings or recent cross-lingual methods.  
- Missing ablations isolating the effect of matrix completion versus LSI (e.g., “LSI-only” vs. full pipeline).  
- Suggested improvements: add significance tests, ablations on ρ/µ and LSI-only baseline, runtime/complexity results, comparisons to bilingual embeddings, and analyses of learned cross-lingual structure.
