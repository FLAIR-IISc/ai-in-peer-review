**Review of "A Novel Two-Step Method for Cross Language Representation Learning"**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross language representation learning, which bridges the feature spaces of different languages by exploiting a set of parallel bilingual documents. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then induces a low dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix. The proposed method is evaluated by conducting a set of experiments with cross language sentiment classification tasks on Amazon product reviews.

**Claims**

The main claims of this paper are:

1. The proposed two-step method can effectively learn cross-lingual representations for cross language text classification.
2. The method outperforms other cross language representation learning methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

**Support for Claims**

The paper provides extensive experimental results to support its claims. The authors conducted 18 cross language sentiment classification tasks on multilingual Amazon product reviews and compared the proposed method with four other methods: TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA. The results show that the proposed method significantly outperforms the other four methods in general, with an average test accuracy increase of at least 8.59% over the baseline TBOW method. The authors also investigated the impact of the size of unlabeled parallel data on the performance of the proposed method and found that it outperforms the other three methods across the range of different np values for 16 out of the 18 cross language sentiment classification tasks.

**Usefulness**

The proposed method has practical applications in cross language text classification, which is an important task in natural language processing. The method can be used to reduce the manual annotation effort in the target language for learning good classification models.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the field of cross language text classification and the challenges associated with it. The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work and clearly explain the limitations of existing methods.

**Novelty**

The proposed method presents a significant improvement over existing approaches by exploiting a set of parallel bilingual documents to learn cross-lingual representations. The use of matrix completion and latent semantic indexing is a novel combination that has not been explored before in the context of cross language text classification.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including the experimental setup, dataset, and implementation details.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge that the proposed method requires a small set of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents, which may not always be available. However, they demonstrate that the method can still produce good performance even with a very small number of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents.

**Constructive Criticism**

One potential criticism of the paper is that the authors could have explored more extensively the impact of the size of unlabeled parallel data on the performance of the proposed method. Additionally, the authors could have provided more insights into the interpretability of the learned cross-lingual representations.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Arguments Pro Acceptance:

* The proposed method presents a novel and effective approach to cross language text classification.
* The method outperforms other state-of-the-art methods in the field.
* The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the field and provides sufficient details for reproducibility.

Arguments Con Acceptance:

* The method requires a small set of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents, which may not always be available.
* The authors could have explored more extensively the impact of the size of unlabeled parallel data on the performance of the proposed method.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper for publication. The proposed method presents a significant improvement over existing approaches, and the paper demonstrates a good understanding of the field and provides sufficient details for reproducibility.
