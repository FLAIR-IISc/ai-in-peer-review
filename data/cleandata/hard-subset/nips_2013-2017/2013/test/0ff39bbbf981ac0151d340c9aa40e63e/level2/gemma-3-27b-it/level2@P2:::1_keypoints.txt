- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion of the document-term matrix followed by LSI for low-dimensional embeddings.  
- Provides a projected gradient descent algorithm for matrix completion with convergence guarantees.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on cross-lingual sentiment classification (Amazon reviews), especially when parallel data is scarce.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates the work effectively within related literature.  
- Related work section covers translation-based and bilingual-resource methods and positions the contribution appropriately.  
- Technical presentation and optimization details (including convergence analysis) are clear and comprehensive.  
- Experimental setup is thorough with clear dataset, baselines, and evaluation metrics; results and discussion are well-presented.  
- Ablation study on parallel data size supports the method’s robustness in low-parallel-data regimes.  
- Matrix completion formulation introduces several hyperparameters (γ, ρ, τ) that require tuning and lack a detailed sensitivity analysis.  
- Unclear justification for why the two-step approach outperforms directly applying LSI to the partially observed matrix or other dimensionality-reduction methods.  
- Comparisons omit recent pre-trained multilingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), limiting evaluation against current state-of-the-art.  
- No assessment of representation transferability to other target languages or classification tasks.  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity and scalability, particularly for the matrix completion step.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add complexity discussion, parameter tuning details/sensitivity analysis, and comparisons to multilingual model-based approaches.
