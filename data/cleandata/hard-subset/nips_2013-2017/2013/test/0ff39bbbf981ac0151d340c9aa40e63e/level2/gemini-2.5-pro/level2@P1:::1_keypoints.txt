- Tackles cross-language text classification by leveraging unlabeled parallel documents to bridge disjoint vocabularies.  
- Novel formulation: treats cross-lingual feature inference as a matrix completion problem rather than treating missing features as zeros.  
- Convex relaxation: replaces rank with trace norm and adds L1 term, yielding a principled convex optimization objective.  
- Optimization: solved via a projected gradient descent algorithm with a provided convergence proof.  
- Two-step pipeline: matrix completion to fill missing entries, then LSI to learn low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Empirical strength: evaluated on 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks and consistently outperforms four strong baselines.  
- Very robust in low-resource settings: maintains strong performance with very few parallel documents (e.g., 200).  
- Clear and well-organized presentation, with logical motivation and experimental reporting.  
- Questionable necessity of the LSI step—an ablation (classifier on completed matrix M*) is needed to justify it.  
- Scalability concerns: per-iteration SVD on t×d matrix may be computationally prohibitive; no complexity or runtime comparisons provided.  
- Parameter sensitivity: hyperparameters tuned on one task only; a sensitivity analysis for γ, ρ, and k is missing.  
- Lack of qualitative analysis: no inspection of filled-in cross-lingual terms to show if completions are semantically reasonable or interpretable.
