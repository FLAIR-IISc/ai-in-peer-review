- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to build a parallel document-term matrix, then LSI to induce low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.

- Effectively leverages parallel bilingual documents rather than relying on machine translation or bilingual lexicons.

- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on cross-language sentiment classification with extensive experiments on Amazon reviews.

- Method involves potentially high computational cost and requires careful hyperparameter tuning.

- Lacks thorough analysis of theoretical guarantees and limitations of the proposed approach.

- Comparisons are limited to a few baselines; more comprehensive benchmarking against other cross-language methods is needed.

- Technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly explained with well-presented experimental results.

- Well-written and easy to follow, with consistent notation and a clear problem introduction.

- Original combination of matrix completion and LSI for cross-language text classification not previously explored.

- Addresses an important NLP problem and shows potential to improve state-of-the-art cross-language classification.

- For acceptance: novel approach, effective empirical results, and tackles a significant NLP task.

- Against acceptance: insufficient theoretical analysis, limited baseline comparisons, and underdeveloped discussion of implications and future work.

- Recommend providing a more thorough theoretical and empirical analysis of the method.

- Recommend conducting broader comparisons to a wider range of cross-language representation techniques.

- Recommend expanding the discussion on implications, potential applications, and future extensions.
