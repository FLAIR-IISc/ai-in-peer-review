- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion to fill bilingual document-term matrices, then LSI on the completed matrix.  
- Core claim: matrix completion followed by LSI yields superior cross-lingual representations, especially with minimal parallel data.  
- Positions method against CL-LSI and CL-KCCA, emphasizing two-step process is distinct from applying LSI/KCCA directly to incomplete matrices.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: convergence proofs for the projected gradient descent algorithm (Lemmas 1–3).  
- Empirical evaluation on 18 Amazon sentiment tasks shows TSL outperforms TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA by 8.59–14.61% average accuracy.  
- Results are statistically significant and robust (10 repetitions per task).  
- Demonstrates practical advantage: strong performance with as few as 200 unlabeled parallel documents.  
- Baselines and literature context are appropriate and well positioned relative to seminal NIPS work.  
- Presentation and methodological detail are clear and sufficient for reproducibility within conference scope.  
- Weakness: lacks failure-mode analysis for highly dissimilar language pairs (e.g., English–Japanese).  
- Weakness: missing ablation study directly comparing matrix completion vs. applying LSI on the same incomplete matrix.  
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to sentiment classification; broader NLP tasks (e.g., NER) would increase significance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address failure analysis and include suggested ablations.
