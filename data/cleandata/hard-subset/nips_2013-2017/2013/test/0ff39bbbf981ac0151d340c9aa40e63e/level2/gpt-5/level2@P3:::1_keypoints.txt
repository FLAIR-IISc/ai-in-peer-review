- Summary: Proposes a two-step cross-lingual pipeline—convex matrix completion to fill a bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI + linear SVM—and shows consistent gains on 18 Amazon sentiment tasks, especially with few parallel documents.

- Relation to prior work: Novel in performing matrix completion before representation learning, propagating bilingual signal to non-parallel documents, distinct from CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA and bilingual topic models.

- Method quality: Objective (nuclear norm + ℓ1 + observed/unobserved losses + nonnegativity) is reasonable and algorithmically plausible, but the convergence analysis is flawed because proximal composition with projection is not justified.

- Experiments quality: Experimental setup is comprehensive with sensible protocols and error bars, but using all unlabeled documents (including test) is transductive and should be noted when comparing to baselines like TBOW that do not use unlabeled data.

- Missing baselines: Does not compare to strong baselines such as Structural Correspondence Learning (SCL) or representative bilingual topic models, and lacks a machine-translation-based baseline.

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear about construction and algorithm, but omits key implementation details (per-iteration SVD method, stopping criteria, runtime/memory, sizes of t and d) and overstates the “naturally low-rank” claim.

- Originality: Combining matrix completion with cross-lingual representation learning appears novel and the specific convex formulation is a reasonable contribution.

- Significance: Empirically valuable for low-parallel-data scenarios and potentially useful in practice if the optimization/convergence issues are resolved.

- Pros: Simple and clean two-step pipeline, robust performance with few parallel documents, and broad comparisons to classical cross-lingual methods.

- Cons: Incorrect or incomplete convergence proof for the stated objective, missing important baselines, limited discussion of computational cost and scalability, and unaddressed transductive vs inductive settings.

- Essential suggestions: Correct the optimization section with a valid solver/proof (e.g., proper proximal splitting with Dykstra/Douglas–Rachford or ADMM/PDHG) and demonstrate empirical parity; add SCL and (if possible) bilingual topic-model baselines; report computational complexity, SVD approach, stopping tolerances, and runtimes.

- Optional suggestions: Provide ablations (direct classification on completed matrix, remove ℓ1/nonnegativity, sensitivity to hyperparameters), evaluate an inductive setup, and analyze how many parallel pairs are needed to propagate cross-lingual structure.

- Overall assessment: Promising and empirically effective paper but currently borderline due to the methodological/convergence gap and missing baselines; would be a solid contribution after fixing the solver and strengthening comparisons.
