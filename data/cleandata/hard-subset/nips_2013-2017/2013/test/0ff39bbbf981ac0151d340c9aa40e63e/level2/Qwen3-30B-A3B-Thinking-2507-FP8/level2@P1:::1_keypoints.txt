- Proposes a novel two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion on a partially observed bilingual document-term matrix followed by LSI.
- Uses convex relaxation of rank minimization with ℓ₁ sparsity and solves it via a scalable projected gradient descent with provable convergence (Theorem 1, Algorithm 1).
- Evaluated on Amazon product reviews (English + three other languages) for cross-lingual sentiment classification.
- Shows consistent empirical gains: TSL outperforms baselines by 8.59–14.61% accuracy across 18 tasks, especially robust with limited parallel data (e.g., 200 documents).
- Distinguishes itself from prior work by completing the matrix before applying LSI, avoiding full document translation.
- Experimental setup (dataset, parameter tuning, SVM classifier) is detailed and reproducible.
- Evaluation is limited to a single domain (Amazon reviews), leaving generalizability to other domains or distant-language pairs untested.
- Omits comparison to some more recent cross-lingual representation methods, weakening the baseline scope.
- Lacks an ablation study isolating the impact of matrix completion versus applying LSI directly to incomplete data.
- Does not analyze sensitivity of performance to hyperparameters (γ, ρ, k) across language pairs, affecting practical deployment guidance.
- Overall assessment: technically solid, original, and significant for low-resource cross-lingual learning with convincing results.
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions suggested to include ablation and broader baseline comparisons.
