- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual representation method: matrix completion of a bilingual document-term matrix followed by LSI to obtain shared representations.  
- Motivates approach by aiming to capture cross-lingual co-occurrence patterns and reduce reliance on parallel data.  
- Provides clear mathematical formulation and uses projected gradient descent for optimization.  
- Positions work relative to CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA, claiming lower parallel-data requirements.  
- Experimental setup uses the Amazon product reviews dataset with reasonable baseline comparisons.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms baselines, especially when parallel data is scarce.  
- Includes an ablation study demonstrating the impact of parallel data size.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis or empirical runtime assessment of the projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Parameter selection (gamma, rho, k) is only briefly described; sensitivity and tuning procedure are unclear.  
- Presentation is primarily tabular and would benefit from visualizations (e.g., t-SNE) of learned representations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational complexity, parameter sensitivity, and add visualization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong empirical performance, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient complexity discussion, sparse parameter-selection details, and missing visual intuition.
