- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual text classification: convex matrix completion on a unified bilingual document-term matrix, then LSI + linear SVM on the completed matrix.
- Optimization minimizes trace norm + ℓ1 norm + squared error on observed entries + ridge penalty on missing entries, subject to nonnegativity.
- Solver: projected/proximal gradient with nuclear-norm shrinkage and nonnegativity projection; convergence argued via non-expansiveness.
- Empirical evaluation on Amazon multilingual reviews (EN, FR, DE, JA) across 18 transfer tasks shows consistent gains over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA.
- Method performs particularly well when only a small number of parallel documents are available.
- Conceptually related to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA, and multi-view/co-training, but novel in using convex matrix completion before LSI.
- Strength: clear, simple pipeline with interpretable objective terms (low-rank, sparsity, missing-entry penalty).
- Strength: empirical protocol is careful (fixed parameters across tasks, 10 random splits) and demonstrates robustness to few parallel pairs.
- Strength: optimization procedure is straightforward to implement using proximal gradient and singular value thresholding.
- Theoretical concern: composition of prox operators (trace-norm shrinkage then projection) does not generally equal prox of the sum, so optimality of fixed point is not proven.
- Theorem 1’s convergence claim is incomplete; need rigorous proof (e.g., Dykstra, ADMM, or appropriate splitting) or algorithm reformulation.
- The “low-rank” claim for fully observed document-term matrices is misleading; low-rank should be presented as an inductive bias/approximation (LSI), not a natural property.
- Ablation analysis is insufficient: need experiments isolating completion vs. LSI and effects of ℓ1, nonnegativity, and missing-entry regularization (µ, ρ, γ).
- Missing important baselines: translation-based methods (translate-train/test) and CL-SCL; SVM C should be tuned for fairness.
- Scalability is unaddressed: no runtime, memory, or iteration counts despite repeated SVDs; randomized/approximate SVD and complexity analysis recommended.
- Experimental clarity issue: unclear how non-parallel documents are used by baselines under reduced parallel data; fairness needs clarification.
- Empirical section is strong, but missing theoretical guarantees and ablations weaken causal claims about modeling choices.
- Paper is generally well written; minor notation/typo fixes and clearer links between algorithm and problem formulation needed.
- Originality: novel combination of nuclear-norm completion on a bilingual document-term matrix plus LSI for cross-lingual representation.
- Significance: promising practical gains for low-parallel settings if theoretical issues and scalability are addressed.
- Key suggestions: provide correct convergence/optimality proof or change algorithm, add ablations, include translation/CL-SCL baselines, tune hyperparameters, report computational costs, and release code.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, intuitive method with strong low-parallel empirical results and novel use of matrix completion.
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete/possibly incorrect convergence argument, missing strong baselines and ablations, and no scalability analysis.
