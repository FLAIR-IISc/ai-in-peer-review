- Proposes a two-step cross-language representation learning method using matrix completion on parallel bilingual documents followed by latent semantic indexing (LSI).
- Matrix completion fills a complete parallel document-term matrix for both languages; LSI induces low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification using Amazon product reviews.
- Shows significant improvements over existing cross-language representation methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains the method, related work, and experiments.
- Provides a thorough review of prior work and clearly distinguishes the proposed approach from existing methods.
- Strength: novel approach exploiting parallel bilingual documents to bridge feature spaces.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental results demonstrating effectiveness.
- Weakness: assumes availability of parallel bilingual documents, which may be unrealistic in some applications.
- Weakness: requires careful hyperparameter tuning, which is time-consuming and may not always yield optimal results.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale applications.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, important problem addressed, thorough literature review, and strong experimental validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on parallel data, hyperparameter sensitivity, and missing computational complexity analysis.
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10; overall positive contribution with suggested improvements.
