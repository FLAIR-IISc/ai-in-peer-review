- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method using a small set of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents to bridge disjoint feature spaces.
- Step 1: Matrix completion on a concatenated document-term matrix using projected gradient descent with convergence guarantees.
- Step 2: Apply LSI on the completed matrix to obtain low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.
- Evaluated on 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks (multilingual Amazon reviews) and outperforms baselines, especially with limited parallel data.
- Technical soundness: well-defined optimization problem and detailed convergence analysis.
- LSI on the completed matrix is a logical means to reduce dimensionality and noise.
- Practical relevance: reduces dependence on labeled data in low-resource languages.
- Clear presentation, thorough methodology, and parameter sensitivity/performance trend analyses.
- Does not compare against recent multilingual transformer-based embeddings (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), limiting SOTA contextualization.
- Potential scalability concerns due to growth of document-term matrix with vocabulary size; unclear applicability to very large corpora.
- Evaluation scope limited to sentiment classification; generalizability to other tasks (e.g., topic classification, QA) not shown.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically rigorous, strong empirical gains in low-resource settings, well-motivated.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing comparisons with modern multilingual models and unresolved scalability issues.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address scalability and include comparisons with recent multilingual models.
