**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which bridges the feature spaces of different languages by exploiting a set of parallel bilingual documents. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages and then induces a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix. The authors use a projected gradient descent algorithm to solve the formulated matrix completion problem with convergence guarantees. The proposed method is evaluated by conducting a set of experiments with cross-language sentiment classification tasks on Amazon product reviews.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper relates to previous work on cross-language text classification, which has been addressed by various methods, including transforming training or test data from one language domain into the other language domain using machine translation tools or bilingual lexicons, and constructing cross-lingual representations by exploiting readily available auxiliary resources such as bilingual word pairs, parallel corpora, and other multilingual resources. The authors' approach shares similarities with these approaches in exploiting parallel data, but only requires a small set of unlabeled parallel documents.

**Strengths**

* The proposed method is novel and effective in bridging the feature spaces of different languages.
* The use of matrix completion and latent semantic indexing allows for the capture of important and robust low-rank information based on word co-occurrence patterns.
* The method is scalable and can be applied to large datasets.
* The experimental results demonstrate the efficacy and robustness of the proposed method, outperforming other cross-language representation learning methods.

**Weaknesses**

* The method requires a set of parallel bilingual documents, which may not always be available.
* The choice of hyperparameters (e.g., γ, ρ, k) may require careful tuning.
* The method may not perform well on tasks with limited labeled data.
