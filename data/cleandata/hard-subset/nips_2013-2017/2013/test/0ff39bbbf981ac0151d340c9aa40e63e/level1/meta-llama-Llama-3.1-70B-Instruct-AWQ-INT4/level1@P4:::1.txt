**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which aims to bridge the feature spaces of different languages using a set of parallel bilingual documents. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then induces a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix. The proposed method is evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification tasks on Amazon product reviews, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness.

**Related Work**

The paper discusses several related works on cross-language text classification, including methods that use machine translation tools or bilingual lexicons to transform training or test data from one language domain into the other language domain. It also mentions methods that construct cross-lingual representations by exploiting bilingual word pairs, parallel corpora, and other multilingual resources. The paper highlights the differences between the proposed method and existing methods, such as the use of matrix completion to fill missing entries in the document-term matrix.

**Technical Soundness**

The paper also provides a thorough analysis of the optimization algorithm used to solve the matrix completion problem, including a proof of convergence. The experimental evaluation is thorough and well-designed, with a clear presentation of the results.
