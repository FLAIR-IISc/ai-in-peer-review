- Paper proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: matrix completion on a concatenated document-term matrix with missing values, followed by LSI for low-dimensional representations.  
- Provides convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on cross-lingual sentiment classification (Amazon reviews), especially with limited parallel data.  
- Novelty: first to explicitly address missing-data via matrix completion before dimensionality reduction in this setting.  
- Theoretical justification: includes rigorous convergence analysis, adding methodological rigor.  
- Empirical strength: consistently outperforms CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, and CL-KCCA baselines, particularly in low-resource scenarios.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written, algorithm clearly presented, and experimental setup well-defined.  
- Evaluation: thorough experiments varying amounts of parallel data to test robustness.  
- Weakness: assumption that the document-term matrix is “naturally low-rank” is under-justified and needs empirical/theoretical support.  
- Weakness: parameter selection was done on a single task (EFB); sensitivity analysis across tasks for γ, ρ, k is lacking.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability of the matrix completion step are not analyzed or compared in runtime.  
- Weakness: limited baselines — missing comparisons to modern pre-trained multilingual models (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R).  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of negative results or scenarios (language pairs/datasets) where the method fails.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically strong, clear writing — merits acceptance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need stronger justification for matrix completion, deeper parameter and complexity analysis, and comparisons to state-of-the-art multilingual models.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address matrix completion justification, parameter sensitivity, computational cost, and add at least a brief comparison to a multilingual pre-trained baseline.
