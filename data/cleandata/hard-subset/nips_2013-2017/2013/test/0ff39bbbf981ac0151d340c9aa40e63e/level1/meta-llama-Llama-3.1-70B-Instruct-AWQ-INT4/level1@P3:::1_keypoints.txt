- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to create a complete parallel document-term matrix, then latent semantic indexing (LSI) for low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Formulates the problem as matrix completion and derives an optimization algorithm to recover missing cross-language features.  
- Evaluated on Amazon cross-language sentiment classification and outperforms other methods, particularly with few parallel bilingual documents.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with thorough related-work coverage.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and results are presented clearly.  
- Strength: novel approach addressing an important cross-language classification challenge.  
- Strength: thorough literature review situating the contribution.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with matrix completion and LSI; needs more background for non-experts.  
- Weakness: optimization algorithm is complex and lacks detailed convergence analysis.  
- Weakness: experiments should include more baselines and additional datasets.  
- Originality: innovative use of matrix completion + LSI to bridge feature spaces across languages.  
- Significance: useful when parallel bilingual documents are scarce and potentially applicable to MT and IR.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add background on matrix completion/LSI, detail optimization convergence, and expand experimental baselines/datasets.
