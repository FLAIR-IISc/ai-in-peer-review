- Proposed method: two-step approach — matrix completion to fill document-term matrix, then LSI for dimensionality reduction to enable cross-language text classification; evaluated on Amazon reviews with improvements, especially when parallel data is limited.

- Technical soundness: methods are standard and reasonable; convergence guarantee for projected gradient descent adds rigor.

- Parameter choice concern: selection of loss function and regularization parameters (µ, ρ, γ) appears arbitrary and needs stronger ablation or theoretical justification.

- Limited analysis: experiments lack deeper investigation of why the method works (e.g., inspecting completed matrix, impact of vocabulary size).

- Dataset limitation: evaluation uses only 100 labeled target examples, which may not reflect real-world scenarios.

- Reproducibility issue: unclear whether parameters were tuned via cross-validation or a separate validation set.

- Clarity strengths: paper is well-written and organized; the two-step process and Algorithm 1 are easy to follow.

- Clarity weaknesses: notation inconsistent at times; related work is dense; convergence analysis could be more accessible.

- Originality: combining matrix completion before LSI is a novel application though both components are known in prior work.

- Practical relevance: focus on low-parallel-data scenarios is valuable and aligns with real-world needs.

- Significance: method can reduce reliance on large parallel corpora and shows consistent improvements over strong baselines.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel preprocessing idea, empirical gains on a standard benchmark, practical focus, convergence guarantee, clear presentation.

- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient ablation/sensitivity studies, small labeled dataset, weak parameter justification, and limited conceptual novelty.

- Overall assessment: reviewer recommends acceptance (strong accept) but urges more experimental analysis and better parameter justification.
