- Proposes a two-step cross-language representation method: convex matrix completion on a unified bilingual document–term matrix, then LSI on the completed matrix.
- Optimization uses nuclear-norm, L1 (reduces to linear under nonnegativity), and squared losses with nonnegativity constraints, solved by projected gradient with singular-value shrinkage; convergence proof provided.
- Experiments on Amazon reviews across 18 transfer directions show consistent gains over CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, CL-OPCA, and target-only baselines, especially with few parallel documents.
- Novelty is moderate: neat, novel combination of convex matrix completion before LSI compared to prior CL-LSI/KCCA/OPCA and SCL work.
- Technical quality is strong: sound derivations and appropriate convergence argument.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and includes sensitivity analysis to number of parallel pairs.
- Missing runtime/complexity analysis for repeated SVDs and no stated stopping criteria.
- No ablation study to separate the contributions of matrix completion versus LSI.
- Claim of solution sparsity is not fully justified given the global-sum L1 term under nonnegativity.
- Clarity is generally good, but details on computational cost, memory footprint, parameter selection rationale, and statistical significance reporting are lacking.
- Significance: improvements in low-parallel regimes are compelling and likely transferable to other cross-lingual tasks.
- Pros: principled two-step formulation with convergence guarantees; strong, consistent empirical gains; clear relation to and improvement over baselines.
- Cons: limited scalability analysis (costly repeated SVDs), missing ablations and significance tests, and comparisons omit modern bilingual embeddings and are English-centric.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
