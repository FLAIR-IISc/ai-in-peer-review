- Proposed method: two-step approach using matrix completion to fill cross-language document-term matrices, then LSI to obtain cross-lingual representations for classification.  
- Evaluation: tested on Amazon product reviews for sentiment classification with comparisons to several baselines and analysis of parallel data size.  

Strengths:
- Novel combination: applying matrix completion followed by LSI to handle disjoint feature spaces is a novel application.  
- Empirical improvements: consistent performance gains over baselines, especially with limited parallel data.  
- Theoretical rigor: includes convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent optimization.  
- Practical relevance: addresses an important cross-lingual NLP problem and is useful in low-resource parallel-data scenarios.  

Weaknesses:
- Optimization clarity: Section 4 and Algorithm 1 are dense and lack intuitive explanation for choosing projected gradient descent.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: tuning limited to a single task; broader/automatic selection and sensitivity analysis are missing.  
- Baseline coverage: lacks comparison to recent transformer-based cross-lingual methods (e.g., multilingual BERT).  
- Scalability: no empirical results on computational cost or behavior with very large datasets.  
- Method assumptions: limited discussion on the low-rank assumption and robustness to noisy document-term matrices.  

Originality:
- Novel application of known techniques; combination appears original for cross-lingual representation learning.  

Significance:
- Results are significant for low-parallel-data settings, but impact would be greater with comparisons to transformer-based approaches.  

Quality:
- Technically sound derivations and well-defined experiments, though some explanations could be more accessible.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Well-motivated, novel approach with strong empirical and theoretical support and practical relevance.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Dense optimization exposition, missing recent baselines, limited hyperparameter/scalability analysis, and unexamined matrix-completion assumptions.  

Overall assessment:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve optimization clarity, add transformer-based comparisons, and address hyperparameter and scalability concerns.
