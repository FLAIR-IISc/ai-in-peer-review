- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to build a complete parallel document-term matrix, then LSI to induce low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Targets the problem of disjoint feature spaces across languages.  
- Requires only a small set of unlabeled parallel documents, unlike prior methods needing translations of all training documents.  
- Evaluated on multilingual Amazon product reviews for cross-language sentiment classification.  
- Experimental results show the method significantly outperforms several baselines, especially when parallel bilingual documents are scarce.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with clear mathematical notation and explanations.  
- Provides a detailed explanation of the projected gradient descent algorithm used for matrix completion.  
- Strength: captures robust low-rank word co-occurrence patterns within and across languages.  
- Strength: achieves good performance even with very few unlabeled parallel documents.  
- Limitation: assumes availability of parallel documents, which may not hold in many real-world scenarios.  
- Limitation: solving the matrix completion problem can be computationally expensive for large-scale datasets.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and significant (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, effective method with thorough evaluation and strong empirical gains.  
- Rejection concerns: reliance on parallel data, high computational cost, and need for a deeper discussion of limitations and future work.
