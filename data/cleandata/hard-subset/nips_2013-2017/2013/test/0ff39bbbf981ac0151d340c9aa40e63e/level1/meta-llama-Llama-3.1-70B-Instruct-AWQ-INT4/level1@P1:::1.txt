This paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which addresses the challenge of disjoint feature spaces in different languages. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then induces a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix.

The proposed method is related to previous work in cross-language text classification, which has demonstrated successful performance using various methods such as machine translation tools, bilingual lexicons, and cross-lingual representations. However, the proposed method only requires a small set of unlabeled parallel documents, whereas previous approaches require at least translating all the training documents in one language domain.

The authors evaluate the proposed method by conducting extensive cross-language sentiment classification experiments on multilingual Amazon product reviews. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method significantly outperforms a number of other cross-language representation learning methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and methodology. The use of mathematical notation and equations is clear and concise, and the authors provide a detailed explanation of the projected gradient descent algorithm used to solve the matrix completion problem.

The proposed method has several strengths, including its ability to capture important and robust low-rank information based on word co-occurrence patterns expressed both within each language and across languages. Additionally, the method can produce good performance even with a very small number of unlabeled parallel bilingual documents.

However, there are some limitations to the proposed method. For example, the method assumes that the parallel documents are available, which may not always be the case in real-world applications. Additionally, the method requires a large amount of computational resources to solve the matrix completion problem, which may be a limitation for large-scale datasets.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective method for cross-language representation learning, which has the potential to improve the performance of cross-language text classification tasks. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of the proposed method and demonstrate its advantages over other existing methods.
