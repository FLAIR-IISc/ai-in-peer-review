- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual document representation: matrix completion to fill missing cross-lingual term frequencies, then LSI (SVD) to obtain shared low-dimensional representations.  
- Frames missing entries as a convex matrix completion problem minimizing trace norm (low rank), L1 norm (sparsity), and reconstruction error, solved by projected gradient descent with convergence proof.  
- Novelty: explicitly inferring missing cross-lingual term frequencies before dimensionality reduction, unlike prior methods that implicitly zero-fill.  
- Empirically evaluated on 18 cross-lingual sentiment tasks across multiple language pairs and domains.  
- Shows consistent and significant performance gains over baselines (CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA).  
- Demonstrates robustness in low-resource settings, performing better when few parallel documents are available.  
- Technical soundness: standard regularizers, appropriate optimization algorithm, and formal convergence analysis.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation with precise mathematical formulations.  
- Main computational concern: each iteration requires a full SVD for shrinkage, making the matrix completion step potentially very expensive and not discussed in runtime comparisons.  
- Questionable redundancy: performing LSI after recovering a low-rank matrix may be unnecessary since completion’s SVD factors could serve as representations.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: multiple tunable hyperparameters (γ, µ, ρ, τ, k) were fixed after tuning on one task, with limited analysis of sensitivity across tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with solid theory and strong empirical results, especially in low-parallel-data scenarios.  
- Arguments for rejection: scalability concerns due to computational cost, unclear justification for the two-step pipeline, and insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
