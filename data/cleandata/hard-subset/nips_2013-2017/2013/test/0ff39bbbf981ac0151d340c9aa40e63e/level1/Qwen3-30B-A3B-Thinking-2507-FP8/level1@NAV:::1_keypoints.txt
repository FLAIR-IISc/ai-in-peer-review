- Proposes a two-step cross-language representation method: fill missing entries in a bilingual document-term matrix, then apply latent semantic indexing for low-dimensional representations.
- Uses matrix completion solved by projected gradient descent, with provided convergence guarantees.
- Evaluated on Amazon product reviews for cross-language sentiment classification.
- Demonstrates superior performance to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA, especially with limited parallel data.
- Empirical results are strong and thorough: comparisons across 18 language pairs and categories.
- Reports substantial accuracy gains (8.59–14.61%) over baselines.
- Technically sound contribution: novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual learning distinct from prior methods.
- Theoretical analysis is solid, offering optimization convergence guarantees.
- Method is practically relevant for low-resource language scenarios.
- Paper is well-organized and cites key related work appropriately.
- Weakness: limited discussion of method limitations (e.g., performance on languages with very different grammatical structures).
- Weakness: lacks comparison with recent neural cross-lingual representation methods.
- Weakness: matrix completion itself is not entirely novel and has prior use in other domains like collaborative filtering.
- Recommendation: acceptance based on novelty in this context, strong empirical gains, and solid theory.
