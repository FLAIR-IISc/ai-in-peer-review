- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation method: matrix completion to fill a parallel document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations.  
- Evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification (Amazon reviews) and shows effective results.  
- Contrasts with MT/lexicon-based and bilingual-pair/parallel-corpus methods; novelty lies in using matrix completion to fill missing document-term entries.  
- Technical approach is clear and well-organized, with a novel application of matrix completion and an optimization algorithm with convergence proof.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, well-designed, and results are clearly presented.  
- Paper is well-written, with consistent notation and a concise abstract.  
- Originality: using matrix completion for cross-language text classification is novel.  
- Significance: addresses an important NLP problem and can help when labeled target-language data are scarce.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and effective approach, strong technical presentation, thorough experiments, tackles an important problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs more detailed comparison with existing methods, more baselines/metrics in experiments, and relies on availability of parallel bilingual documents.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10, recommending acceptance but suggesting stronger comparisons and a more comprehensive evaluation.
