This paper proposes a novel two-step method for cross-language representation learning, which addresses the challenge of cross-language text classification by bridging the feature spaces of different languages. The authors formulate a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages and then induce a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix. The proposed method is evaluated on cross-language sentiment classification tasks on Amazon product reviews and demonstrates superior performance compared to other cross-language representation learning methods, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, which helps to situate their contribution within the broader context of cross-language text classification. The proposed method is described in detail, and the optimization algorithm used to solve the matrix completion problem is thoroughly explained. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.
