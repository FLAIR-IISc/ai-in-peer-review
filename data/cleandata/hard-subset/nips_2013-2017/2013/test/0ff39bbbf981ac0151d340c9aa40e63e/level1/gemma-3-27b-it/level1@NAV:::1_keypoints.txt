- Proposes a two-step cross-language method: complete a partially observed document-term matrix via matrix completion, then apply LSI for cross-lingual representations.  
- Develops a projected gradient descent algorithm for matrix completion with convergence guarantees.  
- Demonstrates superior performance on cross-lingual sentiment classification (Amazon reviews), especially with limited parallel data.  
- Positions the work well within prior cross-lingual methods and cites relevant literature (MT, bilingual lexicons, CL-LSI/CL-KCCA/CL-OPCA).  
- Connects the matrix completion approach to collaborative filtering and signal-processing ideas.  
- Strength: clear technical formulation and convergence analysis of the algorithm.  
- Strength: consistent empirical improvements over baselines and good reproducibility from detailed algorithm/experiment descriptions.  
- Strength: notable finding that the method is effective with scarce parallel data.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of computational complexity and scalability for large document-term matrices.  
- Weakness: practical parameter tuning (γ, ρ, τ) is not thoroughly explored.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to sentiment classification and a single dataset, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness: impact of the initial matrix approximation on results is not discussed.  
- Weakness: quality of parallel data is not evaluated or discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated technical contribution with empirical gains and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved scalability concerns and narrow experimental evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability and broaden evaluation.
