- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language method: matrix completion to create a complete parallel document-term matrix, then LSI for low-dimensional cross-lingual document representations, solved via projected gradient descent with convergence guarantees and evaluated on Amazon sentiment tasks.  
- Builds on prior cross-language work by exploiting parallel data but only requires a small set of unlabeled parallel documents.  
- Strength: Novel and effective at bridging feature spaces across languages.  
- Strength: Matrix completion + LSI captures robust low-rank word co-occurrence information.  
- Strength: Scalable to large datasets.  
- Strength: Empirical results outperform existing cross-language representation methods.  
- Weakness: Requires parallel bilingual documents, which may not always be available.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameters (γ, ρ, k) may need careful tuning.  
- Weakness: May struggle on tasks with limited labeled data.  
- Quality: Technically sound with theoretical analysis and supporting experiments.  
- Clarity: Clearly written, well organized, and sufficiently motivated with related work.  
- Originality: Novel combination of matrix completion and LSI in this context.  
- Significance: Relevant for cross-language text classification and potentially other multilingual tasks (e.g., MT, QA).  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance.
