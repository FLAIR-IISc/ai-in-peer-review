The paper "A Novel Two-Step Method for Cross Language Representation Learning" proposes a two-step representation learning method to bridge the feature spaces of different languages by exploiting a set of parallel bilingual documents. The method first formulates a matrix completion problem to produce a complete parallel document-term matrix for all documents in two languages, and then induces a low-dimensional cross-lingual document representation by applying latent semantic indexing on the obtained matrix.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear overview of the challenges in cross-language text classification. The proposed method is novel and has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in cross-language text classification. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the method, including a convergence analysis of the projected gradient descent algorithm used to solve the matrix completion problem.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, with a comparison to four other methods on 18 cross-language sentiment classification tasks. The results show that the proposed method outperforms the other methods in general, especially when the number of parallel bilingual documents is small.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which is an important consideration for large-scale text classification tasks. Additionally, the authors do not provide a comparison to other state-of-the-art methods in cross-language text classification, such as those based on deep learning architectures.
