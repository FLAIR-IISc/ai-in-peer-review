- Proposes a two-step learning (TSL) method for cross-language text classification: matrix completion to fill missing language features, then LSI for shared low-dimensional representation.  
- Addresses disjoint feature spaces by treating absent-language features as missing and recovering a low-rank unified document-term matrix.  
- Novelty: explicitly formulates cross-lingual representation learning as a matrix completion problem before dimensionality reduction.  
- Technique: well-defined optimization with a projected gradient descent algorithm and a formal convergence proof.  
- Empirical significance: strong results on Amazon product reviews, outperforming baselines and especially robust with few parallel documents.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and methodologically clear from motivation to classification.  
- Evaluation weakness: hyperparameter tuning seems minimal and not rigorously cross-validated across tasks.  
- Comparison weakness: baselines are mostly older methods; lacks comparisons to recent neural cross-lingual embedding approaches.  
- Clarification needed: using TF-IDF with a matrix completion model that assumes non-negativity needs further explanation.
