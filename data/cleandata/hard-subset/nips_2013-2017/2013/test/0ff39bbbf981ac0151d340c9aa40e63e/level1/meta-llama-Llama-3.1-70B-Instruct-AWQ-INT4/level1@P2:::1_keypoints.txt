- Proposes a novel two-step cross-language representation learning method: matrix completion to build a parallel document-term matrix, then latent semantic indexing for low-dimensional cross-lingual representations.
- Well-motivated with a clear overview of cross-language text classification challenges.
- Provides convergence analysis for the projected gradient descent algorithm used in matrix completion.
- Experimental evaluation compares against four methods on 18 cross-language sentiment classification tasks.
- Empirical results show the method generally outperforms baselines, especially when parallel bilingual documents are scarce.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow (Clarity: 9/10).
- Quality rated 8/10; originality rated 8/10; significance rated 8/10.
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale text classification.
- Does not compare against other state-of-the-art methods, notably deep learning architectures.
- Missing discussion of real-world applications and practical deployment considerations.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical performance, comprehensive experiments, clear presentation.
- Arguments for rejection: absent complexity analysis, missing SOTA comparisons, and limited application discussion.
