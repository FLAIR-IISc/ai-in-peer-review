- Proposes a two-step cross-lingual method: convex matrix completion of a concatenated bilingual document–term matrix, then LSI (SVD) on the completed matrix for classification.
- Completion objective: trace norm (low-rank) + ℓ1 (sparsity) + nonnegativity, squared loss on observed entries and weighted penalty ρ on missing entries.
- Optimization via projected gradient descent with singular value shrinkage and nonnegativity projection; convergence argued via non-expansiveness.
- Novelty: imputing cross-lingual features via convex matrix completion before learning low-dimensional representations, differing from direct LSI/CCA/OPCA and tri-factorization/co-regularization methods.
- Experiments on Amazon multilingual sentiment (EN/FR/DE/JA across Books/DVD/Music) show improvements over TBOW, CL-LSI, CL-OPCA, CL-KCCA across 18 transfer settings.
- Stronger gains reported when the number of parallel documents is small; practical setup requires modest unlabeled parallel pairs and few labeled target examples.
- Strength: clear convex formulation with a simple, scalable iterative solver and a standard convergence guarantee.
- Strength: conceptually appealing use of completion to exploit within- and cross-language co-occurrences followed by robust LSI.
- Strength: thorough evaluation across 18 tasks and systematic study of parallel data size effects.
- Weakness: missing key baselines such as CL-SCL and translate-train/translate-test with MT, weakening empirical claims.
- Weakness: limited ablation/diagnostics—no comparisons to weighted low-rank LSI with missing data, trace-norm-only, ℓ1-only, or skipping Step 1.
- Weakness: sparse scalability details—no runtime/memory numbers, rank/truncation strategy, or discussion of costly per-iteration SVDs.
- Weakness: some modeling choices under-justified—squared loss on TF-IDF, role/sensitivity of ρ, and ℓ1 equivalence under nonnegativity.
- Weakness: clarity issues around parallel-row construction (concatenation vs two rows), stopping criteria, step-size selection, and missing statistical tests.
- Quality assessment: technically sound convex program and algorithm; empirical gains are promising but incomplete without stronger baselines and scalability evidence.
- Clarity assessment: generally well organized, but ambiguous implementation/modeling details and small inconsistencies remain.
- Originality assessment: the two-step completion-then-LSI pipeline is novel for cross-lingual classification; optimization approach is standard.
- Significance assessment: potentially useful for low-parallel-data scenarios, but impact is moderate until stronger baselines and larger-scale validation are provided.
- Questions for authors: clarify parallel-pair representation (single concatenated row or two rows) and how token counts are handled.
- Questions for authors: report SVD routine (full vs partial), rank truncation, per-iteration time/memory, and convergence/stopping criteria.
- Questions for authors: provide sensitivity analysis for ρ, γ, μ and ablation results when μ=0 or ℓ1 is removed.
- Questions for authors: explain why not compare to weighted low-rank approximation (LSI with missing entries).
- Questions for authors: provide comparisons to CL-SCL and translation-based baselines.
- Suggestions: add CL-SCL and MT baselines, run ablations (no completion; trace-norm-only; ℓ1-only; no nonnegativity), and report statistical tests.
- Suggestions: include runtime/scaling plots, implementation details (truncated SVD, stopping rules), and clarify parallel-row construction and TF-IDF loss rationale.
- Reasons to accept: simple, convex, well-motivated method with convergence guarantees and consistent empirical gains, especially with few parallel pairs.
- Reasons to reject: missing strongest baselines, lack of ablations and complexity analysis, unclear scalability and some clarity issues.
- Overall: promising idea with solid optimization and encouraging results, but empirical section needs stronger baselines, diagnostics, and scalability evidence; reviewer leans borderline accept if addressed, otherwise borderline reject.
