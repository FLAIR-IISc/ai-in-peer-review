- Paper proposes a two-step matrix completion approach for cross-domain classification: build a single incomplete matrix across documents/domains, complete it with projected gradient descent enforcing low-rank and sparsity, then reduce dimension with LSI and train a classifier.  
- Authors provide a convergence guarantee for the projected gradient descent algorithm and report favorable experimental results.  
- Clarification requested: why reduce feature dimension with LSI after estimating M — is this for computational convenience because M* becomes relatively dense despite sparsity enforcement, and why LSI instead of alternatives like PCA?  
- Suggest explicitly distinguishing and separately reporting the two ways external data are used: (1) labeled source-language data to help labeled target data, and (2) unlabeled parallel data.  
- Experimental setup is unclear — reviewer asks to confirm training: 4k labeled source docs, 100 labeled target docs, 2k unlabeled parallel docs, and testing on remaining 1.9k target docs; and whether Section 5.3 varies unlabeled parallel docs from 200 to 2k.  
- Point out inconsistency/question: why CL-KCCA outperforms TSL in Figure 1 for EFM and EGM at 2k unlabeled but not in Table 1.  
- Theoretical question: why would a fully observed document-term matrix be low-rank, given sparsity and that documents are not obvious linear combinations of other documents?  
- Request intuition on why TSL often outperforms other methods (especially CCA); CCA feels natural for multiview cross-language tasks, whereas matrix completion’s main driver appears to be enforcing low-rank — how does this compare to other techniques?  
- Ask for running-time comparison: how does projected gradient descent time compare to CL-LSI, CL-KCCA, and CL-OPCA?  
- Minor editorial suggestions: fix (ij) to (i,j) before Eq. (2); remove "such as" before Eqs. (4) and (5).  
- Overall assessment: method is simple, effective, and generally clearly presented, but several technical points and experiment details need clarification.
