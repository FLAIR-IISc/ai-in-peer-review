- Proposal: method uses matrix completion to create multilingual row vectors, then applies CL-LSI on those multilingual vectors.  
- Quality concern: results may not be robust; improvements might be due to LSI scaling rather than the proposed method.  
- Specific confound: positive results could be an artifact of LSI scaling outperforming OPCA/CCA for a linear SVM.  
- Recommendation: evaluate with a classifier insensitive to per-feature rescaling (e.g., boosted decision trees available in Weka).  
- Significance: if robust, the approach is interesting because matrix completion is a well-controlled non-linear inference technique.  
- Novelty: the idea appears to be new.  
- Clarity issue 1: ambiguous phrasing about a “unified document-term matrix” — unclear whether terms are language-agnostic or language-specific surface forms.  
- Clarity issue 2: experimental setup description is fragmented (spread across Table 1), making it hard to follow.  
- Overall: neat idea but authors should try classifiers beyond linear SVM to rule out scaling artifacts.
