- Proposes a two-step method: matrix completion followed by latent semantic analysis to learn cross-lingual topic representations.  
- Optimizes the matrix completion step with a projected gradient descent algorithm.  
- Outperforms mono-lingual and cross-lingual baselines (including state-of-the-art methods) on sentiment analysis of parallel Amazon reviews.  
- Learning curves show strong, stable performance even with relatively small amounts of parallel/unlabeled data.  
- Experiments are thorough and compare against bag-of-words, cross-lingual LSA, and two state-of-the-art dimensionality reduction methods.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible; all parameters and selection procedures are reported.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: better motivate the use of matrix completion in the Introduction.  
- Originality: novel application of matrix completion to cross-lingual representation learning.  
- Significance: shows matrix completion can be an effective preprocessing step for cross-lingual sentiment classification and potentially other tasks.
