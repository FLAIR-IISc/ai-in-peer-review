- Proposes an iterative unsupervised method that jointly learns structure and parameters of Stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAGs).  
- Introduces "And-Or fragments" to unify composition and reconfiguration learning within a single framework.  
- Optimizes the posterior probability of the grammar using derived sufficient statistics for efficient computation.  
- Demonstrates applicability across data types (events and images), showing competitive or superior empirical performance.  
- Connects to prior work on stochastic grammars, sum-product networks, and structure learning in NLP and vision.  
- Advances beyond prior methods that required manual grammar specification or separated composition and reconfiguration learning.  
- Strength: unified, principled approach addressing a key limitation of earlier methods.  
- Strength: theoretical grounding via posterior optimization and efficient sufficient-statistic computations.  
- Strength: general framework agnostic to data modality, with clear formalization.  
- Strength: positive empirical results, notably on a challenging synthetic event grammar dataset.  
- Weakness: algorithmic details (likelihood gain, context matrices, prior vs. likelihood interplay) are under-explained and rely on supplemental material.  
- Weakness: experimental scope is limited—image experiments are synthetic/compare to few baselines, lacking broader state-of-the-art comparisons.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., α) not thoroughly analyzed; impact on grammar size/complexity unclear.  
- Weakness: scalability claims lack detailed analysis of runtime or behavior with larger datasets/complex grammars.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of failure modes and scenarios where the method may struggle (e.g., highly ambiguous data).  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound contribution with promising results and broad applicability.  
- Against acceptance: challenging-to-follow algorithmic exposition, limited experimental breadth, and unresolved scalability concerns.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution worthy of acceptance after minor revisions to clarify methods, broaden experiments, analyze hyperparameters and scalability, and discuss limitations.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions, with specific requests to clarify likelihood computations, expand experiments, analyze hyperparameter effects, provide scalability analysis, and discuss failure cases.
