- Summary: Proposes an iterative unsupervised algorithm that jointly learns SAG structure and parameters by inserting "And-Or fragments," optimizing grammar posterior and demonstrating strong results on event and image datasets.

- Novelty: Joint learning of structure and parameters with And-Or fragments is a significant and original contribution.

- Theoretical foundation: Clear probabilistic formulation optimizing posterior probability with sufficient statistics for efficient computation.

- Generality: Generalized SAG formalization applicable to multiple data modalities (text, images, events).

- Empirical validation: Competitive or superior performance on event and image grammars, especially on the complex event dataset.

- Clarity: Generally well-written with helpful figures, though some algorithmic details are under-explained.

- Implementation complexity: Practical implementation appears complex, involving many components (n-gram tensors, context matrices, etc.) and greedy/beam searches that may be suboptimal.

- Scalability concerns: Computational cost and scalability to larger datasets/complex structures are not thoroughly analyzed; running-time claims lack detail.

- Limited comparison: Experimental comparisons are narrow (only two prior methods; one baseline is old); lacks comparison to recent state-of-the-art.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance seems sensitive to hyperparameter α, but no sensitivity analysis is provided.

- Overclaiming: Some statements (e.g., "more efficient and robust") are not sufficiently quantified.

- Quality assessment: Technically sound with well-justified posterior treatment and sufficient statistics, though more empirical analysis would strengthen claims.

- Clarity assessment: Generally clear but needs expanded explanations of likelihood gain computation and implementation for reproducibility.

- Originality assessment: Novel core idea and valuable generalized SAG formulation.

- Significance assessment: Promising results with broad applicability, contingent on improvements in scalability and usability.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically solid approach with efficient posterior gain computation and strong empirical results across modalities.

- Arguments against acceptance: Complex implementation, scalability questions, limited baselines, and missing hyperparameter analysis.

- Overall assessment: Solid contribution worthy of acceptance pending minor revisions addressing scalability, comparisons, and hyperparameter sensitivity.

- Rating: Accept with Minor Revisions.
