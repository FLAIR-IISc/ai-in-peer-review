- Proposes an unsupervised method that jointly learns both structure and parameters of Stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAGs) by iteratively inserting And-Or fragments.
- Claims novelty in unifying learning of composition and reconfiguration, improving robustness in data-scarce settings and search efficiency.
- Optimizes a posterior probability using sufficient statistics, providing a principled (non-heuristic) optimization foundation.
- Demonstrates applicability to multiple domains (event and image grammars), showing comparable or superior empirical performance to prior methods.
- Positions the work relative to probabilistic grammar learning, SPNs, and recent image/event grammar methods, noting connections to biclustering/tensor coherence.
- Core strength: simultaneous learning of structure and parameters addresses limitations of fixed-structure approaches.
- Core strength: And-Or fragments effectively capture compositions and reconfigurations in a compact, learnable way.
- Core strength: Theoretical grounding via posterior optimization and derived sufficient statistics distinguishes it from heuristic approaches.
- Core strength: General, data-agnostic formalism validated on both event and image tasks.
- Core strength: Strong empirical results, notably outperforming prior work on image grammar tasks and handling complex synthesized event subtypes.
- Strength: Well-written and clearly organized paper with helpful figures illustrating concepts and algorithms.
- Weakness: Computational complexity of searching potential And-Or fragments remains a concern; scaling analysis is insufficient.
- Weakness: Sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., α) and search parameters is underexplored and may affect results.
- Weakness: Comparisons are limited; lacking evaluation against more recent or supervised grammar-learning methods and no justification for omitted baselines.
- Weakness: Event experiments use controlled datasets; robustness on noisy, real-world event data is untested.
- Weakness: Choice of sparsity prior (α‖G‖) is not well-justified and alternatives are not discussed.
- Recommendation: Overall positive — novel, well-motivated, and empirically strong; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: provide detailed scalability/complexity analysis, hyperparameter sensitivity studies, broader comparisons to related work, and discussion of alternative priors.
