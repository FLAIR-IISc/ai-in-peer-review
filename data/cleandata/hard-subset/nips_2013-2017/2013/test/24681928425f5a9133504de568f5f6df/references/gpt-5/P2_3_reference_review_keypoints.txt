- Proposes an unsupervised method that grows stochastic And-Or Grammars (AOGs) by iteratively inserting joint And-Or fragments across modalities (text, images, events).
- Search optimizes an explicit posterior combining a sparsity prior on grammar size with a Viterbi approximation to the likelihood.
- Main technical contribution: efficient posterior-gain estimates via sufficient statistics — an n-gram tensor and a context matrix — yielding a factorized coherence score under unambiguity.
- Empirical results show improvements over prior AOG structure-learning methods on synthetic image grammars and real image/event datasets.
- Builds on prior AOG and unsupervised grammar induction work; acknowledges links to SPN structure learning but could discuss that literature more deeply.
- Derivation relies critically on the Viterbi likelihood and an unambiguity assumption; the practical impact of unambiguity is not empirically analyzed.
- The claim that any context-free AOG can be constructed from And-Or fragments is stated but not proved; needs formal justification.
- The encoding prior uses per-symbol code lengths (sa, so) without discussion of choice or sensitivity to these constants.
- Image experiments include a good synthetic evaluation and lower perplexity on a real dataset, but event experiments compare only to narrow baselines.
- Missing comparisons to general-purpose structure learners (e.g., PCFG induction, SPN structure learning) and no ablation isolating joint fragment benefits.
- No sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (alpha, beam width), no error analysis, and no runtime/scaling results on larger grammars or datasets.
- Reproducibility concerns: key derivations, pseudocode, and implementation choices (relation types, smoothing, context definitions) are deferred or underspecified.
- Presentation issues: several figure captions/labels are garbled and need clearer, self-contained diagrams of fragments, tensors, and context matrices.
- Needs precise definition of “context” across modalities and explanation of how And-rule energy terms are parameterized and converted to probabilities.
- The unambiguity assumption should be stated up front and discussed with respect to practical violations and consequences.
- Originality: joint And-Or fragment operation and the coherence-based posterior-gain computation are novel and address a key limitation of prior AOG learning.
- Significance: approach could influence structure learning for compositional models, but limited datasets and missing analyses reduce confidence in generality and scalability.
- Pros: principled objective, efficient sufficient-statistics search criterion, unified And-Or fragment concept, cross-domain demonstrations, and solid synthetic evaluation.
- Cons: dependence on untested assumptions (unambiguity, Viterbi), limited baselines, unclear implementation/details, and missing ablation/sensitivity/runtime evidence.
- Suggested improvements: provide a proof or sketch for fragment completeness, sensitivity/runtime analyses, And-only/Or-only ablations, test impact of unambiguity violations, and add PCFG/SPN baselines and standard benchmarks.
- Recommendation: borderline accept — promising and principled work but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer exposition to solidify the contribution.
