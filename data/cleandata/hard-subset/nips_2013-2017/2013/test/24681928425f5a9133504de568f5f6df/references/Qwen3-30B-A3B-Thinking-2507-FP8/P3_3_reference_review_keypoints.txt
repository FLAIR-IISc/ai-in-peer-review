- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of SAOGs using unified "And-Or fragments" applicable across data types.

- Theoretical contribution: formalizes SAOGs agnostically and introduces And-Or fragments to jointly learn compositions and reconfigurations, addressing prior separation.

- Algorithmic innovation: derives efficient posterior gain computation using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors, context matrices) and links likelihood gain to tensor coherence.

- Empirical validation: demonstrates consistent improvements over prior methods on event and image grammar tasks, including challenging synthetic datasets and multiple metrics.

- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, with clear formal definitions and illustrative figures.

- Weakness — scalability: limited discussion of how the method scales to very large datasets/complex grammars and computational cost for large n-gram tensors.

- Weakness — theoretical gaps: lacks formal proof or rigorous conditions showing why unified And-Or fragment learning outperforms separate learning.

- Weakness — comparisons: does not sufficiently compare to recent neural or differentiable grammar-learning approaches.

- Weakness — robustness: omits discussion of handling ambiguous or noisy training data (e.g., mislabeled/missing atomic actions).

- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with strong theoretical and empirical support.

- Recommendation: Accept (NIPS), with suggested attention to scalability and stronger theoretical/robustness analysis.
