- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that learns both structure and parameters of Stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAGs).
- Introduces iterative insertion of And-Or fragments to jointly learn composition and reconfiguration.
- Optimizes grammar structure and parameters via posterior maximization using sufficient statistics for efficiency.
- Demonstrates competitive or superior performance on event and image grammar learning tasks.
- Tackles a challenging problem by removing the need for manual grammar structure specification.
- And-Or fragment idea is novel and effective, especially in data-scarce scenarios.
- Principled optimization of posterior probability is a strong methodological contribution.
- Use of sufficient statistics improves computational efficiency and scalability in practice.
- Generalizes SAGs to be data-agnostic, applicable to text, images, and events.
- Empirical validation is solid, with particularly strong results on a complex event grammar dataset.
- Paper is well-written, mathematically clear, and organized.
- Algorithm appears complex; more discussion on computational cost and scalability is needed.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., α sparsity prior) is not thoroughly analyzed.
- Comparisons to more recent or state-of-the-art methods are limited.
- Lacks an ablation study to quantify contributions of individual components.
- Image grammar experiments rely heavily on synthetic data rather than realistic datasets.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and significant contribution with promising results despite some gaps.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, principled optimization, empirical support, potential impact, and clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: algorithmic complexity/scalability, limited SOTA comparison, and synthetic-data reliance.
- Qualitative ratings: high quality, good clarity, high originality, high significance, but some technical details could be clearer.
- Recommendation: Accept (NIPS 2024), with suggested improvements on complexity analysis and broader comparisons in future revisions.
