- Tackles unsupervised learning of stochastic And-Or grammars, learning both structure and parameters from unannotated data.  
- Introduces “And-Or fragments” to unify learning of compositional structures and reconfigurations.  
- Explicitly optimizes posterior probability of structure and parameters, using sufficient statistics for efficiency.  
- Generalizes the SAG framework to be data-agnostic (images, events, text).  
- Strong empirical validation on event and image grammars with synthetic and real datasets showing competitive or superior performance.  
- Formalization and likelihood-gain derivation are theoretically sound and well-presented.  
- Clear motivation: addresses limitations of fixed-structure grammar learning.  
- Presentation/readability can be dense in places (notably Section 3) and some figures are cluttered.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., α) is insufficiently discussed and lacks guidance for selection.  
- Scalability is unclear: experiments are limited in scale and complexity, and computational complexity discussion is brief.  
- Limited comparison to more recent or stronger unsupervised baselines, including deep-learning approaches.  
- Reliance on Viterbi likelihood may bias toward unambiguous parses; potential impact not fully explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with significant theoretical contributions and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation issues, scalability concerns, limited baselines, and Viterbi-related bias.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept.
