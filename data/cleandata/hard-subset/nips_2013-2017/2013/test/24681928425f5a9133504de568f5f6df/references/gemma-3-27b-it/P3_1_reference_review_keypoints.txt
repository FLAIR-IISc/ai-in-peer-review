- Presents a novel unified framework that jointly learns both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs).  
- Introduces And-Or fragments to simultaneously model composition and reconfiguration, improving robustness to data scarcity and ambiguity.  
- Optimizes a posterior probability using derived sufficient statistics, with theoretical grounding linked to biclustering coherence measures.  
- Positions work relative to prior grammar and image/event learning literature, distinguishing itself from methods that fix grammar structure.  
- Empirically validated on event and image grammars, showing strong performance especially on challenging event datasets with action subtypes.  
- Abstracts the SAG formalism to be data-type agnostic, increasing potential applicability.  
- Paper is generally well-written and well-organized, with helpful examples and figures.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability; concrete runtimes and larger-scale analysis are missing.  
- Choice of sparsity prior α = 0.5 appears arbitrary and lacks sensitivity analysis or justification.  
- Limited treatment of how to learn and represent different relation types within And-rules beyond spatial/temporal relations.  
- Comparisons to related methods are somewhat limited; needs broader baselines, particularly methods that learn from atomic actions.  
- Experiments use relatively small datasets; larger, more realistic datasets would strengthen validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient scalability analysis, arbitrary hyperparameter choice, limited comparisons, small datasets.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with important ideas (And-Or fragments, unified learning); recommended for acceptance with minor revisions addressing scalability, α sensitivity, and comparative evaluation.
