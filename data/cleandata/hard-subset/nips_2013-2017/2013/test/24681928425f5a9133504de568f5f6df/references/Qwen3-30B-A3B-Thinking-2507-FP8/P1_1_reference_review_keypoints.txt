- Proposes unsupervised learning of stochastic And-Or grammars via unified "And-Or fragments" that learn compositions (And) and reconfigurations (Or) simultaneously.  
- Optimizes grammar posterior using sufficient statistics with efficient likelihood-gain computation via context matrices and n-gram tensors.  
- Evaluated on event and image grammars, showing competitive or superior performance to prior work.  
- Originality: novel unified use of And-Or fragments to avoid separate, redundant fragment learning.  
- Quality: clear formalization, data-type agnostic theory, and sound derivation with solid empirical design.  
- Significance: addresses manual grammar specification bottleneck and improves applicability across domains.  
- Clarity: well-organized paper with helpful figures and sufficient algorithmic detail; supplementary derivations provided.  
- Weakness — computational complexity: claims of reasonable runtime lack quantitative comparison to prior methods.  
- Weakness — grammar unambiguity: reliance on Viterbi likelihood assumes unambiguous grammars and lacks analysis for violations.  
- Weakness — event-grammar modification: converting top-level And-nodes to Or-nodes for multiple events is under-justified and unclear in generality.  
- Weakness — fragment interpretation: limited analysis of why certain And-Or fragments are preferred beyond posterior gain.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong theoretical and empirical contribution with a compelling unified approach.  
- Suggestion: add computational complexity comparison with prior methods.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion and possible extensions for ambiguous grammars.  
- Suggestion: better justify and generalize the event-grammar modification.  
- Suggestion: include a small case study showing learned And-Or fragments and reasons for their selection.  
- Overall: high-quality, broadly applicable advance in unsupervised grammar learning that needs minor clarifications but is suitable for publication.
