- Proposes an unsupervised method to jointly learn structure and parameters of context-free stochastic And-Or Grammars by iteratively adding And-Or fragments that couple composition (And) and reconfiguration (Or).  
- Optimizes a posterior combining a sparsity (MDL-like) prior over grammar size and a Viterbi likelihood, with fragment search via greedy/beam search and random restarts.  
- Main technical contribution: efficient posterior-gain estimate using sufficient statistics—an n-gram tensor of fragment configurations and a context matrix—factorizing into two coherence terms.  
- Demonstrates cross-domain applicability with experiments on video event parsing and image grammar induction (synthetic and real animal-face), showing competitive or improved results over prior AOG learners.  
- Builds on prior AOG, unsupervised grammar induction, and SPN literature, with links to PCFG split-merge and structure learning in SPNs (some related works not cited).  
- Strength: clear algorithmic idea—And-Or fragments as principled search units that couple And/Or learning.  
- Strength: elegant and computationally attractive sufficient-statistics formulation for posterior gain.  
- Strength: sensible posterior combining rule-cost and savings; demonstrated improvements across domains.  
- Weakness: likelihood-gain derivation assumes grammar unambiguity, which is strong for AOGs and not fully clarified or analyzed for violations.  
- Weakness: And-rule parameterization and learning are underspecified—relation energy definitions, estimation, and regularization are unclear and affect reproducibility.  
- Weakness: grammar-size encoding costs (sa, so) and α are treated as fixed constants with no sensitivity analysis, risking hyperparameter dependence.  
- Weakness: potential scalability issues—constructing/scanning n-gram tensors may be expensive; no complexity analysis or thorough runtime scaling experiments.  
- Weakness: event evaluation uses small, pre-segmented datasets with limited baselines and missing statistical reporting; Table 2 referenced but numeric details and significance are lacking.  
- Weakness: image evaluation lacks detail on KL/precision–recall estimation and uses only perplexity on real data without smoothing or variance reporting; qualitative parses and learned structures are not shown.  
- Clarity issue: presentation mixes context-free assumptions with relation-dependent energies that may induce context sensitivity; conditions for remaining context-free are not precise.  
- Clarity issue: likelihood-gain formula uses nonstandard notation and lacks a compact derivation in the main text; a proof sketch is needed.  
- Clarity issue: claim that any context-free AOG can be built by successive And-Or fragments needs a proof sketch.  
- Originality: unified And-Or fragment search and the posterior-gain estimator via coherence are novel and conceptually advance AOG/SPN structure learning.  
- Significance: method could be practical across modalities if ambiguity and parameterization issues are addressed; current empirical claims need broader baselines and ablations.  
- Pros summary: principled And-Or fragments, elegant sufficient-statistics scoring, joint structure/parameter learning, cross-domain demos with strong perplexity gains.  
- Cons summary: unclear And-rule parameters and ambiguity handling, hyperparameter sensitivity not explored, limited baselines and missing quantitative details, and scalability not analyzed.  
- Key questions for authors: how are And-rule energy parameters defined/learned; what exactly is the unambiguity assumption and behavior under ties; sensitivity to α, sa, so, beam width, and max n; runtimes/memory vs vocabulary and samples; ablation of And-only vs Or-only vs And-Or; justification for the constructive claim about AOGs.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept—promising and methodologically interesting but needs clearer treatment of And-rule parameterization, ambiguity assumptions, and more thorough evaluation/ablations.
