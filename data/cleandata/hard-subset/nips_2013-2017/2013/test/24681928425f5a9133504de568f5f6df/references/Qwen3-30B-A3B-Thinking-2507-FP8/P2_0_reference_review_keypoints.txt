- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of Stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAOGs).
- Introduces "And-Or fragments" to unify learning of compositions (And) and reconfigurations (Or), avoiding separate error-prone searches.
- Formalizes SAOGs in a data-agnostic way and derives sufficient statistics to efficiently compute posterior gain.
- Optimizes posterior probability of the grammar as the learning objective.
- Evaluated on event grammars (human activity recognition) and image grammars (animal face sketches), showing competitive or better performance than prior methods.
- Technically sound with rigorous formalization and feasible computational approach due to derived sufficient statistics.
- Experimental design is appropriate, using relevant metrics (purity, inverse purity, F-measure, perplexity) and reproducible implementation details.
- Paper is well-written and organized; definitions and figures effectively illustrate concepts.
- Minor clarity issue: limited discussion of applying the method to text despite claiming data-agnosticity.
- Novel contribution: unified And-Or fragment approach distinguishes this work from prior grammar-learning methods.
- Agnostic SAOG formalization and unified learning methodology represent meaningful methodological novelty.
- Significant potential impact due to addressing unsupervised structure learning and learning from unannotated data, though broader domain evaluation (e.g., text) would strengthen claims.
- Strengths: clear data-agnostic formalization, unified fragment learning, explicit posterior optimization with efficient stats, thorough cross-domain experiments, practical runtime.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on text application, reliance on grammar unambiguity, lack of detailed complexity comparison with prior work, incomplete comparison with key related works [17,18], evaluation limited to two domains.
- Recommendation: strong accept; paper is a substantial technical contribution with potential to influence future work (Overall rating: 8 — Strong Accept).
