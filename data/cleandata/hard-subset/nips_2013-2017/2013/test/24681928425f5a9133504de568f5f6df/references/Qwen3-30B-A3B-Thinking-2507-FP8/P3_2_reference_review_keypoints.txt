- Presents a unified unsupervised learning approach for Stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAOGs), addressing prior work that either fixes grammar structure or separates composition and reconfiguration learning.
- Formalizes SAOGs in a data-type-agnostic way and proposes an iterative fragment-insertion algorithm that optimizes grammar posterior probability.
- Efficient posterior-gain computation via sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors and context matrices) with sound theoretical foundation.
- Empirical validation across event and image domains shows competitive or improved performance and practical scalability (5000 samples in minutes).
- Demonstrates robustness to ambiguous data (avoids erroneous compositions) in a targeted event-dataset analysis.
- Insufficient comparison to recent relevant work (e.g., ICML 2023 / NeurIPS 2022 neural-grammar approaches such as [22]).
- Lacks ablation studies directly comparing the unified approach to separate composition/reconfiguration learning to justify claimed benefits.
- Theoretical analysis of posterior gain and coherence measures is superficial in the main paper; assumptions and limitations (e.g., noise, non-context-free grammars) are not well discussed.
- Real-world evaluation is limited (only one image dataset and synthetic event data); needs broader benchmarking.
- Overall: solid technical contribution with promising results; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Key revisions requested: add recent-method comparisons, include ablation studies, deepen theoretical discussion of coherence measures, and provide more diverse real-world validation.
- The unified And-Or fragment approach is compelling and potentially influential for structured representation learning.
