- Presents a grammar-agnostic framework with sufficient statistics that enable efficient posterior gain computation without full re-parsing.
- Technically rigorous: provides a clear derivation addressing a key computational bottleneck in grammar learning.
- Strong experimental design: careful baselines on synthetic and real-world datasets with statistically meaningful improvements.
- Clear, well-organized writing with helpful figures and supplementary implementation details for reproducibility.
- Novelty: first to unify composition and reconfiguration learning via And-Or fragments, overcoming limitations of separate searches.
- Demonstrates data-agnostic applicability on images and events and claims potential extension to text.
- Significant contribution: advances unsupervised structured representation learning with practical cross-domain implications.
- Strengths: unification idea, solid theoretical foundation, cross-domain empirical validation, clarity, and good contextualization.
- Weakness: lacks experiments on text to fully support the data-agnostic claim.
- Weakness: relies on a grammar unambiguity assumption that may not hold in many real-world scenarios.

- Weakness: limited computational complexity and scalability analysis beyond a prototype runtime statement.
