- Summary: Proposes an unsupervised method that jointly learns structure and parameters of Stochastic And-Or Grammars by iteratively inserting And-Or fragments and optimizing a posterior balancing model complexity and data likelihood, using sufficient statistics for efficient computation; evaluated on event and image tasks with competitive results.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on stochastic grammar and unsupervised induction literature, distinguishes itself by joint structure+parameter learning with And-Or fragments, relates to Sum-Product Networks and improves over cited structure-learning baselines.

- Strength — Unified framework: Elegantly unifies composition and reconfiguration via And-Or fragments, addressing limitations of prior separated approaches.

- Strength — Principled optimization: Maximizes a well-defined posterior with a sparsity prior and efficient posterior-gain computation using sufficient statistics.

- Strength — Generality: Framework is data-type agnostic (text, images, events) through a formalization of SAGs.

- Strength — Empirical validation: Demonstrates practical effectiveness and improvements, especially on challenging event grammar learning.

- Strength — Presentation: Well-written, organized, and illustrated, aiding comprehension of methods and results.

- Weakness — Relation complexity: Implementation and parameterization of different relation types in And-rules are not thoroughly specified.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Impact of the sparsity hyperparameter α is not extensively analyzed.

- Weakness — Limited comparisons: Evaluation could be broader, e.g., including Bayesian nonparametrics or hierarchical clustering baselines.

- Weakness — Scalability: Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability to very large or high-dimensional datasets.

- Weakness — Incomplete implementation details: More explanation needed on the Viterbi likelihood approximation and smoothing used for perplexity.

- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound approach with a significant unified contribution, strong empirical results, and clear writing.

- Against acceptance: Outstanding concerns about relation learning detail, hyperparameter robustness, scalability, and breadth of comparisons.

- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution whose strengths outweigh weaknesses; suitable for acceptance pending minor revisions on relation details and hyperparameter analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).
