- Proposes a unified unsupervised method for learning Stochastic And-Or Grammars using "And-Or fragments" to learn compositions and reconfigurations simultaneously.  
- Optimizes posterior probability with derived likelihood and prior gains to enable efficient greedy search.  
- Evaluated on video event grammars and image scene modeling, showing significant improvements over baselines.  
- Technically sound with clear derivations and a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Prototype runs efficiently (minutes on 5000 samples) and experiments are thorough (synthetic and real datasets).  
- Consistently outperforms prior methods (e.g., SZ [17] for image grammars, previous event-grammar methods).  
- Minor weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis.  
- Minor weakness: does not fully analyze how the Viterbi approximation affects theoretical guarantees.  
- Well-organized and clearly written with effective figures and precise formal definitions of SAGs.  
- Some derivations (sufficient statistics) may be hard for readers without strong probabilistic modeling background.  
- Suggests adding an intuitive explanation and a small example (e.g., for the context matrix) to improve accessibility.  
- Novelty: unified And-Or fragment approach advances prior work that treated composition and reconfiguration separately.  
- Broad applicability: formalizes SAGs agnostic to data type (text, images, events).  
- Could strengthen discussion by more explicitly connecting to recent sum-product network work.  
- Recommendation: accept — technically strong, clear, and empirically significant contribution to unsupervised grammar learning.
