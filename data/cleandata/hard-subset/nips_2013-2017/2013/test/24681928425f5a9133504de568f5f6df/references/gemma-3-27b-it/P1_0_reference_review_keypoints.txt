- Novel method for unsupervised learning of Stochastic And-Or Grammars that jointly learns grammar structure and parameters from unannotated data.  
- Iterative insertion of And-Or fragments optimizes a posterior balancing model complexity and likelihood.  
- Uses sufficient statistics to make posterior gain computation efficient and scalable.  
- Demonstrated on event and image grammar tasks, achieving competitive or superior performance to prior methods.  
- Properly positions work relative to prior grammar learning, distinguishing from fixed-structure and separate composition/reconfiguration methods.  
- Connects to related models such as sum-product networks and hierarchical Dirichlet processes with comprehensive references.  
- Unified learning of And- (composition) and Or- (reconfiguration) rules is a key strength improving robustness, especially with limited data.  
- Principled optimization of posterior probability with a sparsity prior helps prevent overfitting.  
- Theoretical derivation of sufficient statistics is a substantial contribution for scalable algorithms.  
- Empirical validation is convincing, particularly on the complex event grammar dataset.  
- Approach is data-agnostic and generalizable across data types.  
- Paper is well-written and uses helpful diagrams to illustrate concepts.  
- Algorithm appears complex and the paper lacks full detail on implementation choices, tuning, and computational costs.  
- Potential sensitivity to the initial grammar is not sufficiently discussed or evaluated.  
- Baseline comparisons are somewhat limited, especially in image experiments, missing newer image parsing methods.  
- Missing qualitative visualizations of learned grammars that would aid interpretability and intuition.  
- Learning still relies on greedy/beam-search heuristics, leaving risk of local optima and unclear search-space pruning.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, unified framework with promising results and good fit to venue.  
- Against acceptance: practical implementation complexity, limited comparisons, and heuristic search concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing implementation details, expanding comparisons, and adding qualitative examples.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
