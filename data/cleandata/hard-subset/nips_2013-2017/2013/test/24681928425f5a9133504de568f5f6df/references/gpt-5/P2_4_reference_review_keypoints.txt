- Proposal: unsupervised method to learn structure and parameters of context-free Stochastic And-Or Grammars by iteratively inserting And-Or fragments that introduce an And-node and child Or-nodes.
- Objective: posterior combining an MDL-style sparsity prior over grammar size and a Viterbi (best-parse) likelihood to favor compact, less ambiguous grammars.
- Efficient search: derive sufficient statistics (n-gram tensor and context matrix) so likelihood gain under an unambiguity assumption factorizes into fast coherence scores for greedy/beam search.
- Empirical claim: improvements over prior AOG learners on event parsing and image grammar learning, including synthetic and real datasets.
- Relation to prior work: extends classic grammar induction (Bayesian model merging, PCFG induction) and recent AOG image/event learning; conceptually related to SPN structure learning.
- Strength: clear algorithmic contribution—jointly searching And- and Or-fragments addresses data scarcity and redundancy from separate searches.
- Strength: principled objective—MDL-like prior + Viterbi likelihood is well-motivated and leverages known Viterbi/unambiguity behaviors.
- Strength: efficient scoring—n-gram tensor and context matrix reduce re-parsing costs, enabling practical greedy/beam search.
- Strength: cross-domain applicability—same framework applied to temporal (events) and spatial (images) relations.
- Strength: empirical comparisons show competitive or better performance vs baselines on multiple datasets.
- Weakness: relies on unambiguity assumption for the posterior gain derivation; real-world grammars are often ambiguous and impact is not quantified.
- Weakness: prior specification lacks justification and sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (α, sa, so) that strongly affect structure selection.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on And-rule parameterization and estimation of energy terms across modalities, hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: scalability concerns—constructing n-gram tensors across relation types can be expensive and there is no complexity analysis or large-scale experiments.
- Weakness: framework requires domain-specific design of relation sets and contexts; context definition and relation selection are under-specified.
- Weakness: evaluation gaps—small event splits, ad hoc post-hoc grammar modifications, insufficient details on smoothing/perplexity, no confidence intervals.
- Weakness: missing ablations—no direct comparison of And-only, Or-only, and And-Or fragment learning, and limited diagnostics on ambiguity, compactness vs accuracy, and sensitivity to beam width/α.
- Weakness: clarity issues—some derivations deferred to supplement, dense figures, and under-specified definitions (context, encoding cost).
- Assessment (Quality): technically sound at a high level with supporting experiments, but key assumptions and hyperparameter choices need deeper examination; reproducibility concerns remain.
- Assessment (Clarity): generally organized and motivated, but several definitions and derivations are under-specified and figures need improvement.
- Assessment (Originality): novel unified And-Or fragment search and sufficient-statistics-based posterior gain, nontrivial extension over prior work.
- Assessment (Significance): advances AOG structure learning across modalities, though broader adoption requires stronger scalability and ablation evidence.
- Suggestions: quantify ambiguity effects and report ambiguity metrics; provide ablations for And-only/Or-only/And-Or and sensitivity analyses for α, sa, so, beam width, n.
- Suggestions: clarify implementation details—context definitions, relation encodings, And-rule energy estimation, smoothing for perplexity, and runtime/memory profiles.
- Suggestions: include complexity analysis, larger-scale experiments, and pruning heuristics for scalability; improve figures and add parsing examples.
- Verdict: solid and timely contribution with promising results; lean toward acceptance if authors address theoretical and empirical gaps (unambiguity, ablations, sensitivity, scalability) in the camera-ready.
