- Proposes a modality-agnostic, context-free AOG formalization and a bottom-up algorithm that jointly induces And-Or fragments (And-node + Or-nodes + rules).  
- Introduces a posterior objective combining a sparsity prior over grammar size and a Viterbi likelihood (with unambiguity bias).  
- Provides an efficient candidate-fragment scoring method using sufficient statistics: an n-gram tensor over configurations and a context matrix yielding closed-form likelihood-gain approximations and simple MLEs for Or-rule probabilities.  
- Search uses greedy/beam strategies with random restarts and a prior term that penalizes fragment size while rewarding reductions.  
- Connects to prior grammar induction, Bayesian model merging, ADIOS, AOGs for images/events, and decomposable SPNs; main novelty is joint induction of And-Or fragments and explicit posterior scoring with sufficient statistics.  
- Joint And-Or fragment induction elegantly addresses data sparsity and redundancy and makes search practical via sufficient-statistics scoring.  
- Bayesian objective with sparsity prior and Viterbi/unambiguity bias is clear and supported by prior NLP work.  
- Formalism and algorithm are general across modalities and applied to both event and image domains.  
- Posterior-gain formula avoids repeated re-parsing, yielding efficient runtimes (minutes on nontrivial datasets).  
- Empirically outperforms prior AOG structure learners on event and image tasks, with gains under increased ambiguity and on synthetic/real image metrics.  
- Likelihood-gain derivation assumes grammar unambiguity, a strong assumption—sensitivity to ambiguity and possible corrections/bounds are not analyzed.  
- The Viterbi likelihood and duplicate-sample aggregation factor is unusual and needs formal justification to rule out dataset-composition artifacts.  
- No theoretical guarantees on search quality or proofs that accepted fragments monotonically increase posterior under the approximations; consistency/identifiability results are missing.  
- The claim that any context-free AOG can be constructed by fragment additions is plausible but unproven; a sketch or citation is requested.  
- And-rule “energy” parameters appear specified by relations but their estimation, learning, or regularization (e.g., for spatial Gaussians) is unclear or not addressed.  
- Prior coding lengths (sa, so) and hyperparameter α lack discussion on selection and sensitivity; ablations/sensitivity analyses are missing.  
- Key derivations (tensor/context coherence, RD/CM construction) are deferred to the supplement; the main paper lacks concise theorems and complexity claims.  
- Search implementation details (candidate generation, n values, relation types, pruning, beam width, restarts, stopping criteria) are under-specified and need pseudocode.  
- Image parsing/inference procedure is not clearly described; the paper does not explain whether analogous tricks to CYK are used for spatial relations.  
- Perplexity smoothing for real images is unspecified (method and parameters).  
- Baselines are limited; comparisons to adaptor grammars/HDP-PCFGs, SPN structure learners, recent structured sequence models, and other compositional image models are missing.  
- Dataset scale is modest (61 videos, 320 images); robustness to larger, noisier datasets and detector noise is not evaluated.  
- Missing ablations include And-only vs Or-only vs And-Or fragments, the effect of the context-matrix term, co-occurrence-first vs joint methods, and effects of violating unambiguity.  
- The joint fragment induction and sufficient-statistics scoring are novel and potentially impactful, bridging grammar induction and SPN-like architectures.  
- Paper organization is generally good, but several technical definitions are too terse for reproduction and some figure captions are garbled.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel joint fragment learning, clear Bayesian objective, efficient scoring, cross-domain applicability, and empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on unambiguity and Viterbi approximations without analysis, unclear And-rule parameter learning and inference, limited baselines and datasets, and crucial derivations in the supplement.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on clarifying the likelihood-gain derivation, handling of ambiguity, And-rule parameterization, and adding ablations and sensitivity analyses.
