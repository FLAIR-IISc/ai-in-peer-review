- Overall: Solid contribution on unsupervised structure learning for SAGs, recommending acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem: Learns both structure and parameters of Stochastic And-Or Grammars from unannotated data.  
- Novelty: Introduces And-Or fragments to jointly learn compositions and reconfigurations, improving over prior separate approaches.  
- Method: Principled posterior-maximization framework with a sparsity prior to avoid overfitting.  
- Theory: Strong theoretical grounding, including posterior gain derivation and connections to tensor/matrix coherence.  
- Generality: Unified SAG definition applicable across modalities (language, images, events).  
- Algorithm: Iterative bottom-up learning algorithm is well-motivated and clearly explained with helpful diagrams.  
- Empirics: Experiments on event and image grammars show competitive or superior performance versus state-of-the-art.  
- Notation: Mathematical notation is dense and can be hard to follow; more intuitive presentation would help.  
- Hyperparameters: Sensitivity to the sparsity hyperparameter α is not thoroughly explored.  
- Compute: Computational cost and runtime behavior, especially on large datasets, are not sufficiently analyzed.  
- Failure modes: Limited discussion of scenarios where the method struggles (e.g., highly ambiguous or complex data).  
- Algorithm details: Clarify practical implementation of greedy/beam search and stopping criteria.  
- Fragment restrictions: Explain and justify the specific restrictions on allowable And-Or fragments.  
- Experimental details: Provide rationale for the "co-occurring" relation, more on image quantization preprocessing, and clearer tables/figures with error bars.  
- Writing: Paper is sometimes verbose and could be more concise; some repetition should be removed.
