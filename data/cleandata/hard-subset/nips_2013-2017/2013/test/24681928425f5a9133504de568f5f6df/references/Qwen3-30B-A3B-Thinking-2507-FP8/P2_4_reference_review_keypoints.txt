- Proposes a novel unsupervised method to learn stochastic And-Or grammars by iteratively adding unified "And-Or fragments" that model composition and reconfiguration.  
- Formalizes a unified And-Or grammar framework agnostic to data type (text, images, events).  
- Learning algorithm optimizes posterior probability of the grammar rather than relying on heuristics.  
- Provides rigorous derivations for likelihood and prior gain when adding fragments.  
- Derives sufficient statistics to compute posterior gains efficiently, making learning computationally feasible.  
- Evaluated on event (human activity videos) and image (synthetic and real animal faces) domains with competitive or improved performance over prior methods.  
- Handles practical issues like multiple events per video via minor algorithmic adjustments.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear definitions and effective visualizations; algorithmic details are presented rigorously but accessibly.  
- Does not explicitly report computational complexity or scalability limits, and full pseudocode is only in supplementary material.  
- Lacks statistical significance tests for small performance differences reported in results.  
- Original contribution: And-Or fragments jointly learn And and Or rules, addressing inefficiencies of prior separate-learning approaches.  
- Unified formalization across data types increases potential applicability, but broader impact and limitations for wider adoption are under-discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept (Final score: 8) due to novelty, technical rigor, and solid empirical results despite minor concerns.
