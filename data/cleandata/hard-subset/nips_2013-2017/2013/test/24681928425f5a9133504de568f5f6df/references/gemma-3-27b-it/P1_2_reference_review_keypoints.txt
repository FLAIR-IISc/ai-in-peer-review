- Proposes a novel unsupervised algorithm that jointly learns SAG structure and parameters by iteratively inserting And-Or fragments and optimizing a posterior objective.  
- Introduces And-Or fragments to unify composition and reconfiguration, improving robustness especially with limited data.  
- Uses sufficient statistics for efficient optimization to avoid re-parsing the full training set each iteration.  
- Presents a data-agnostic grammar formalization applicable to text, images, and events.  
- Empirically validated on event and image grammars, showing competitive or superior performance to existing methods.  
- Situates work well in prior literature, differentiating from fixed-structure and separate-composition approaches and relating to SPNs and prior grammar learning.  
- Paper is generally well-written with helpful examples and figures (notably Figures 1 & 2).  
- Algorithm complexity is high with multiple hyperparameters and search strategies; sensitivity analysis is lacking.  
- Experimental scope is limited; synthetic image dataset lacks real-world complexity (e.g., ImageNet-scale tests).  
- Lacks comparison to stronger generative baselines (e.g., VAEs, GANs) that could learn similar compositional structures.  
- Explanation and implementation details of the context matrix and its scaling are vague.  
- Assumption of grammar unambiguity may restrict expressiveness and its impact isn’t discussed.  
- Overall: strong, novel, and promising contribution supported by theory and experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—address hyperparameter sensitivity, expand experiments/ablations and stronger baseline comparisons, and clarify implementation details (context matrix).
