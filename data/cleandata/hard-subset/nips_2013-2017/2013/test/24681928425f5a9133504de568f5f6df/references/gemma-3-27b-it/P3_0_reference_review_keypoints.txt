- Paper tackles unsupervised learning of Stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAGs) by jointly learning grammar structure and parameters.  
- Core contribution: iterative insertion of "And-Or fragments" guided by posterior probability maximization.  
- Formalization is data-agnostic and leverages sufficient statistics for more efficient learning.  
- Empirical validation shows encouraging results on event and image grammars, including synthetic images.  
- Positions itself well relative to prior work, improving on methods that fix structure and building on language and image/event grammar literature.  
- Connects appropriately to sum-product/decomposable networks and biclustering literature.  
- Strength: unified framework that learns both structure and parameters simultaneously.  
- Strength: fragment-based approach balances compositionality and reconfiguration effectively.  
- Strength: posterior maximization provides a principled alternative to heuristics.  
- Strength: use of sufficient statistics improves computational efficiency and applicability.  
- Strength: general, data-agnostic formulation broadens potential applications.  
- Strength: clear writing and helpful figures aid understanding.  
- Weakness: search over And-Or fragments may be computationally expensive; no detailed complexity analysis provided.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially the sparsity prior alpha.  
- Weakness: scalability to high-dimensional images/videos is unclear; experiments use low-resolution data.  
- Weakness: risk of overfitting with flexible grammars; needs more rigorous generalization analysis.  
- Weakness: no comparison to contemporary deep learning baselines for related tasks.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address complexity analysis, alpha sensitivity, and inclusion of brief deep-learning comparisons.  
- Confidence of reviewer: 8/10.
