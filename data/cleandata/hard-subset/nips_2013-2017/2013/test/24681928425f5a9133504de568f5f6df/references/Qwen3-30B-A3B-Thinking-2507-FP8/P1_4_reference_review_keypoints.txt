- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars from unannotated data.  
- Formalizes stochastic And-Or grammars in a data-agnostic way and iteratively adds "And-Or fragments" to unify composition (And) and reconfiguration (Or) learning.  
- Optimizes posterior probability of the grammar and uses sufficient statistics for efficient likelihood and prior gain computation.  
- Evaluated on event grammars (activity recognition) and image grammars (scene parsing) with competitive or better performance than prior methods.  
- Strength: technically sound with clear theoretical foundations and an efficient sufficient-statistics approach.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation with appropriate baselines, multiple datasets, metrics, and ablation studies.  
- Strength: original unified framework reduces redundancy, handles data scarcity, and is broadly applicable due to data-agnostic formalization.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on how "relations" in And-rules are specified; lacks concrete examples or definition mechanisms.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons could be stronger with more baselines; runtime claim lacks hardware/dataset context.  
- Weakness: scalability to very large datasets is not addressed, and integration with deep learning methods is not discussed.  
- Recommendation: accept — novel, well-justified, and empirically validated contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: unified framework, solid theoretical basis, efficient computation, strong empirical results, broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing relation-specification details, limited scalability discussion, needs more baseline comparisons.  
- Suggestions: clarify relations with concrete examples, provide implementation details for sufficient statistics, discuss scalability limits, and compare briefly with deep-learning grammar approaches.
