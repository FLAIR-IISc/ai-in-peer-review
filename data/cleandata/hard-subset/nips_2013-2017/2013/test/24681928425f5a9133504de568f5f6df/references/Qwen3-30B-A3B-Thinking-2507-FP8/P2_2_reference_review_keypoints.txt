- Presents an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars by introducing "And-Or fragments" and optimizing the posterior.  
- Provides a data-type-agnostic formalization and derives efficient sufficient statistics for posterior gain computation.  
- And-Or fragments unify learning of compositions (And-rules) and reconfigurations (Or-rules), addressing prior separation/heuristic issues.  
- Technical advance: factorization of likelihood gain into coherence measures of n-gram tensors and context matrices for efficient computation.  
- Empirical validation across event and image domains shows competitive or superior performance and substantial perplexity improvement in image experiments, with practical runtime.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with helpful examples, pseudocode, and visualizations enabling reproducibility.  
- Limited evaluation scope: few baselines, small real-image dataset (320 samples), and no comparisons to recent neural grammar or modern structure-learning methods.  
- Incomplete discussion of limitations: unclear behavior on very complex grammars, noisy/incomplete data, and the simplifying single-pattern-per-sample assumption.  
- Some technical sections (likelihood gain, context matrix/n-gram tensor) lack intuitive explanations for non-expert readers.  
- Overall: novel, technically sound, significant contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
