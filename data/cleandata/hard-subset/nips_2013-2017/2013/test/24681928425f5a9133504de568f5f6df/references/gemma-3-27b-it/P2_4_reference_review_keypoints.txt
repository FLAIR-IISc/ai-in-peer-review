- Proposes an iterative, unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of Stochastic And-Or Grammars by adding “And-Or fragments.”
- Optimizes the posterior probability of the grammar using sufficient statistics for efficiency.
- Unified learning of composition and reconfiguration addresses data scarcity and ambiguity.
- Demonstrated applicability to both event and image data, claiming data-type agnosticism.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets are encouraging, with strong perplexity on real images.
- Clear motivation and positioning relative to prior work.
- Efficient implementation leveraging sufficient statistics and a likelihood-gain formulation.
- Mathematical formulation (especially likelihood gain) is dense and hard to follow; needs more intuitive explanation and visuals.
- Impact of the sparsity prior parameter α is underexplored; sensitivity analysis is lacking.
- Comparisons to existing methods are sometimes insufficiently detailed; stronger benchmarking needed.
- Scalability to much larger datasets or more complex grammars is unclear; fragment addition/evaluation may be a bottleneck.
- The role and practical interpretation of the “context matrix” are not well explained.
- Technically sound and complete work with claims generally supported by experiments (Quality).
- Writing is generally good but mathematical density reduces clarity; some concepts need clearer exposition (Clarity).
- The approach appears novel in combining posterior optimization with And-Or fragments and a data-type agnostic formulation (Originality).
- Learning both structure and parameters unsupervised is significant with broad potential applications (Significance).
- Pros for acceptance: novel, principled, empirically promising, broadly applicable.
- Cons for acceptance: accessibility issues due to complexity, limited parameter-sensitivity and scalability discussion, and incomplete baseline comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, analyze parameter sensitivity and scalability, and strengthen benchmarks.
