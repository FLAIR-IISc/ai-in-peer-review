- Proposes a novel joint method to learn both structure and parameters of Stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAGs) by iteratively inserting “And-Or fragments” and optimizing posterior probability.
- Formalizes SAGs in a data-agnostic way (images, events, text) and derives an efficient posterior-gain computation using sufficient statistics.
- Joint learning of structure and parameters via And-Or fragments is original and addresses scalability/assumption limits of prior work.
- The posterior-gain derivation and use of sufficient statistics is a strong technical contribution improving efficiency.
- Paper is generally clear, well-written, and uses helpful illustrations and consistent notation.
- Empirical results on event and image grammar tasks are encouraging and competitive, with a useful ablation study.
- The approach is significant for unsupervised learning of compositional structures in vision and language.
- Implementation likely requires substantial engineering; paper lacks detail on search-space handling and large context matrices.
- Experiments are limited to synthetic or small datasets; scalability and impact of pre-quantization on real images are unclear.
- Sensitivity to the sparsity hyperparameter α is not analyzed; fixed α may strongly affect outcomes.
- The paper should discuss limitations more thoroughly (computational cost, overfitting risk, sensitivity to initial grammar).
- For acceptance: the work is novel, technically sound, and empirically promising.
- Against acceptance: high implementation complexity, limited-scale experiments, and insufficient hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Revision 1: Expand implementation details on managing And-Or fragment search and efficient context-matrix computation.
- Revision 2: Add experiments on larger, more complex datasets to demonstrate scalability and robustness.
- Revision 3: Include a sensitivity analysis or adaptive approach for the sparsity parameter α.
- Revision 4: Expand the discussion of limitations and challenges in implementation and scaling.
