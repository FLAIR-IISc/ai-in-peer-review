- Proposes joint induction of AOG structure and parameters by iteratively inserting And-Or fragments to maximize a posterior with a sparsity/length prior and a Viterbi-likelihood approximation.  
- Scores candidate fragments efficiently using sufficient statistics: an n-gram tensor over configurations and a context matrix over configuration-context co-occurrences to avoid full re-parsing.  
- Assumes (approximate) unambiguity to factorize the likelihood gain into two coherence measures and derive optimal Or-rule probabilities; key derivations are deferred to the supplement.  
- Novel contributions: unified And-Or fragment search (vs separate And/Or searches), explicit posterior-based scoring with sparsity prior, and use of simple sufficient statistics for fragment scoring.  
- Strong synthetic-image results (improved F1 and KL over prior AOG method [17]) and real-image perplexity gains; event parsing is competitive or better than baselines with the grounded variant performing best.  
- Heavy reliance on unambiguity and Viterbi approximation without quantification of their practical validity or failure modes.  
- Missing ablations and analyses (And-only vs Or-only vs And-Or, fragment arity/size, beam width, Viterbi vs full marginal likelihood).  
- Clarity and reproducibility issues: hard-to-parse equations/notation glitches, underspecified encoding lengths and relation parameterization, and essential pseudocode relegated to the supplement.  
- Limited baselines: no comparisons to SPN structure learning (e.g., LearnSPN and successors) and more recent grammar-induction methods.  
- Search heuristics (greedy/beam with restarts) lack formal guarantees; no complexity or thorough runtime analysis provided.  
- Event experiments are small-scale and rely on presegmented/clean terminal symbols; robustness to noisy terminals not evaluated.  
- Suggestions: quantify ambiguity effects, add ablations, detail encoding/smoothing choices and parameterization, compare to SPN methods, report variances/statistical significance, and release code/pseudocode.  
- Overall: promising, novel, and cross-modal approach with solid initial results, but theoretical assumptions, clarity, baseline breadth, and reproducibility need strengthening; reviewer leans weak accept conditional on addressing these issues.
