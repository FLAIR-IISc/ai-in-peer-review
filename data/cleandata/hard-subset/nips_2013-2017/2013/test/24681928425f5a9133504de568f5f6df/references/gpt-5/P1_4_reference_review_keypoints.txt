- Tackles unsupervised structure learning for stochastic And-Or grammars (AOGs) by iteratively inserting And-Or fragments into a trivial grammar using a MAP objective with sparsity prior and Viterbi likelihood.

- Main technical idea: efficient posterior-gain computation via sufficient statistics—an n-gram tensor and a context matrix—yielding closed-form Or-rule probabilities under an unambiguity assumption.

- Search procedure: greedy/beam search with random restarts; experiments on event and image grammars show improved F-measures and perplexities over prior AOG learners and a quantized-scene model.

- Strength: clear, principled MAP objective with explicit sparsity prior and practical Viterbi likelihood aligned with grammar induction practice.

- Strength: joint induction of composition and reconfiguration via And-Or fragments avoids pitfalls of alternating And/Or-only searches.

- Strength: sufficient-statistics view reduces re-parsing and enables efficient gain computation and parameter updates.

- Strength: framework applies to sequences and 2D structures, demonstrating cross-modal empirical gains.

- Strength: sensible search heuristics with runtime claims and a modification to handle multiple events per video.

- Weakness: central likelihood-gain derivation depends on a strong unambiguity assumption and is deferred to the supplement, with no analysis of violations.

- Weakness: limited empirical scope—small datasets (61 videos; 320 images), few baselines, and no natural language evaluation despite claimed generality.

- Weakness: important design choices under-specified (relation parameterization/encoding, context definitions, discretization of continuous relations, selection of sa/so and α); lacks sensitivity analyses.

- Weakness: missing ablation comparing And-Or fragments to And-only or Or-only searches to validate claimed robustness and compactness.

- Weakness: no theoretical guarantees (consistency, convergence) and no formal proof that any context-free AOG can be constructed from And-Or fragments.

- Weakness: some evaluation metrics and parsing assumptions (e.g., CYK for events) may not generalize to richer AOGs; perplexity uses smoothing and is indirect.

- Quality: algorithmically plausible and mostly sound, but key assumptions and deferred derivations weaken theoretical rigor; empirical evidence useful but limited.

- Clarity: overall reasonable organization but many critical details are in the supplement; definitions of “context,” relation schemas, and preprocessing need clearer exposition; some figures/pseudocode problematic.

- Originality: unified And-Or fragment search and sufficient-statistics factorization are novel relative to prior AOG, PCFG, and SPN structure learning.

- Significance: moderate—important problem and useful approach, but limited scale and breadth of evaluation restrict immediate impact.

- Key reviewer requests: include full likelihood-gain derivation in main text and discuss ambiguity effects; clarify context definition and enumeration; specify relation representations and discretization; justify and analyze α, sa, so; provide complexity and memory analysis for tensors/search.

- Additional reviewer requests: add ablations (And-only vs Or-only vs And-Or; with/without context matrix; Viterbi vs full likelihood), expand baselines (PCFG/SPN and modern grammar induction), report variability across restarts and smoothing details, and sketch proof that And-Or fragments can construct any context-free AOG.

- Pros for acceptance: conceptually neat unification of composition/reconfiguration, efficient sufficient-statistics formulation, and positive cross-modal empirical results.

- Cons for acceptance: reliance on strong assumptions, limited theoretical backing and proofs, small-scale evaluation, missing ablations and sensitivity analyses, and clarity/reproducibility gaps.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept, confidence 0.7.
