- Tackles unsupervised structure and parameter learning for stochastic And-Or Grammars (AOGs) by growing grammars with greedy/beam search over And-Or fragments.
- Key method: introduce And-nodes and child Or-nodes as fragments and optimize a posterior with a sparsity prior and Viterbi likelihood.
- Efficient posterior-gain computation via sufficient statistics: an n-gram tensor (covered configurations) and a context matrix (configuration-context co-occurrences).
- Under an unambiguity assumption, likelihood gain factorizes into coherence terms of these statistics; prior penalizes fragment size and rewards reductions.
- Derives closed-form Or-rule probabilities and avoids repeated reparsing through sufficient-statistics-based scoring.
- Evaluated on event datasets (activity sequences) and image datasets (synthetic animals, real animal faces) with improvements over prior AOG methods.
- Conceptual novelty: unifies composition and reconfiguration by searching And-Or fragments, addressing sparsity and redundancy of And-only/Or-only searches.
- Practical algorithm: greedy/beam search with random restarts, claimed to run in minutes on moderate datasets.
- Major concern: reliance on an unambiguity assumption combined with Viterbi training introduces bias; paper lacks analysis/diagnostics for ambiguity violations.
- Evaluation limitations: event experiments use gold atomic actions and tiny test sets; image experiments use one real dataset and limited baselines, missing comparisons to SPNs and other hierarchical models.
- Missing ablation and sensitivity studies (fragment type ablations, beam width, α, coherence components, noise in detections).
- Clarity issues: some formulas/figures poorly presented, key derivations deferred to supplement, and hyperparameter choices (sa, so, α) not fully specified.
- Theoretical gaps: no guarantees for greedy/beam search, no complexity bounds for sub-tensor search, scalability characterization is limited.
- Generality claim overstated relative to experiments; adding a small NLP/PCFG induction experiment would strengthen modality-agnostic claims.
- Technical soundness is plausible but hinges on unambiguity assumption and supplement derivations; empirical section needs more thoroughness.
- Suggestions: measure ambiguity sensitivity, run ablations, report hyperparameter settings and sensitivity, provide runtime/memory scaling, expand baselines and test-set sizes, clarify smoothing for perplexity.
- Originality: And-Or fragment search and posterior-gain via n-gram/context coherence are novel contributions.
- Significance: Potentially valuable for compact, interpretable compositional models in vision and events, but broader validation required.
- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept to weak accept; paper is promising but needs stronger empirical validation, clearer assumptions, and improved presentation.
