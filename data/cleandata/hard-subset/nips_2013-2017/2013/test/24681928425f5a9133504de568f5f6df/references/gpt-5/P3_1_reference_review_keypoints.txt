- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (AOGs) from unannotated data via iterative bottom-up insertion of And-Or fragments.
- Presents a unified, context-free AOG formulation claimed to be data-type agnostic (text, images, events).
- Optimizes a posterior with an MDL-like sparsity prior exp(−α‖G‖) and approximates likelihood with Viterbi (unambiguity-biased) scores.
- Main technical contribution: closed-form posterior-gain approximation using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensor and context co-occurrence matrix) to avoid full reparsing.
- Empirical results show improvements over prior AOG learners on event parsing and large perplexity gains on an image dataset.
- Builds on prior AOG, PCFG induction, Bayesian model merging/MDL, and decomposable SPN work; novelty lies in joint And/Or fragment search and efficient gain estimation.
- Strength: elegant joint discovery of And and Or structure via fragments that reduce redundancy and mitigate sparsity.
- Strength: clear objective-driven approach with a computable gain proxy using counts to avoid repeated global parsing.
- Strength: unified formalization with demonstrations across temporal event and spatial image domains.
- Strength: empirical gains over baselines and prior AOG learners.
- Weakness: relies on strong unambiguity and Viterbi assumptions, unclear robustness to inherent ambiguity in real AOGs.
- Weakness: And-rule parameterization and how “energy” terms (P(A→…)) are estimated is underspecified.
- Weakness: “Agnostic to data type” claim depends on domain-specific relation/context choices that are not precisely defined.
- Weakness: potential combinatorial explosion in searching n-gram tensors across relation types; lacks complexity analysis, n/beam settings, and scalability metrics.
- Weakness: expressivity claim that any context-free AOG can be built by fragments is asserted without proof or constructive argument.
- Weakness: limited evaluation—small event dataset, narrow baselines, no ablation studies, no statistical significance or runtime/memory reports.
- Weakness: ad hoc handling of multiple events per video (flipping top-level And to Or) may distort learned structure.
- Weakness: coherence factorization idea is intriguing but not fully developed or clearly defined.
- Quality: generally sound but requires clarification on likelihood-gain under ambiguity, And-rule energies, context definitions, and expressivity proof.
- Clarity: high-level exposition and figures are good, but key implementation details and some equations are missing or hard to parse.
- Originality: And-Or fragment concept and posterior-gain computation via sufficient statistics are novel in AOG learning.
- Significance: potentially impactful if technical clarifications and broader evaluations are provided.
- Key questions for authors: how are And-rule energies parameterized/learned; what is a “context” column per domain; how are reductions computed under ambiguity; practical n/relations/beam/runtime scaling; sensitivity to α/sa/so; possible extensions to handle ambiguity; proof of fragment expressivity.
- Suggested improvements: add detail on energy models and context instantiations, run ablations (And-Or vs And-only/Or-only, Viterbi vs EM, α, fragment size, beam), provide runtime/memory scaling, expand evaluations and baselines, and move derivations/proofs to supplement.
- Pros: elegant joint learning, efficient gain estimation, cross-domain demonstration, promising empirical gains.
- Cons: missing critical modeling/algorithmic details, strong Viterbi/unambiguity assumptions, limited evaluation and scalability analysis, unproven theoretical claims.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on clarifying parameter learning and adding ablations/runtime analyses.
