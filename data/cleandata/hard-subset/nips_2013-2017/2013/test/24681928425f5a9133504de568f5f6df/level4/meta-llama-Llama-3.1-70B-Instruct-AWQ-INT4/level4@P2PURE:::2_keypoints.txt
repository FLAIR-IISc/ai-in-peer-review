- Proposes a method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars for recursive phenomena (language, images).  
- Introduces a novel unsupervised structure-learning scheme that incrementally adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates likelihood and prior gains.  
- Claims a tractable approach to modeling grammars with AND and OR rules.  
- Empirically evaluated on two tasks: event grammars and image grammars, achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Reviewer criticizes lack of scalability analysis; unclear if method handles large datasets (thousands of datapoints) efficiently.  
- Reviewer notes absence of experiments on natural language sentences, a primary application area for AND-OR grammars.  
- Suggests testing on Penn Treebank and comparing to prior work (e.g., Klein and Manning) to strengthen claims.  
- Overall promising results but additional experiments—especially on natural language and scalability—are needed to bolster contributions.
