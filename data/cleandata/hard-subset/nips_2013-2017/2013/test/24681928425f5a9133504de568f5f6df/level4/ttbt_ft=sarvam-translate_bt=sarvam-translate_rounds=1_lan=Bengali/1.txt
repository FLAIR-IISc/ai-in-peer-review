This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic and-or grammar. This paper is considered to "generalize" previous work on structure learning, but it is actually a special case, which simplifies the problem. It is a reasonable topic in itself, and the paper makes a nice contribution, so I will not try to argue that the problem is more general than the problem of structure learning encountered in NLP. The basic algorithm is understandable and successful compared to other methods for introducing grammar.

Experimental evaluation can be improved by adding more comparisons with other methods. In particular, the methods of Stolke and Omhoff (reference 14) can be applied to both tasks, and this would be a valuable comparison. It is a reliable paper presenting a method for learning the structure of a special class of grammar.
