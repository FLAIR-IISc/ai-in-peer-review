This paper introduces a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. While the authors position this work as a generalization of prior structure learning approaches, it is more accurately described as a specific, and crucially, tractable case. This clarification, however, does not diminish the paper’s contribution; the resulting simplification allows for a practical algorithm. Indeed, framing the work as tackling a particular class of structure learning problems, rather than claiming broader generality, is a reasonable and defensible position. 

The core algorithm presented is sound and demonstrably effective, achieving competitive results when benchmarked against existing grammar induction techniques. The experimental evaluation, while solid, could be strengthened with a more comprehensive comparison to related work. Specifically, the method proposed by Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears applicable to the tasks considered in this paper and would provide a valuable baseline for assessment. 

Overall, this is a well-executed paper that makes a clear contribution to the field of grammar learning by providing a practical method for inducing the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars.
