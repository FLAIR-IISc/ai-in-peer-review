This paper presents a method for learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, which can represent various recursive phenomena, including natural language and image grammars, using a combination of AND and OR rules. The authors propose a novel approach for unsupervised structure learning by introducing new AND-OR fragments incrementally and evaluating the likelihood and prior gains of their model.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments on two tasks: learning event grammars and learning image grammars. The results are competitive with the state-of-the-art, showcasing the potential of their approach.

Overall, I found the paper to be a promising contribution to the field, as it provides a tractable way of learning stochastic grammars that can be modeled using AND and OR rules. However, I have several criticisms that I believe can strengthen the paper.

Firstly, the authors do not provide any information on the scalability of their learning algorithm. It is unclear whether it can handle thousands of datapoints efficiently. Evaluating the algorithm's performance on larger datasets would provide valuable insights into its practical applicability.

Furthermore, I would have liked to see experiments on natural language sentences, as this is arguably the most obvious application of stochastic AND-OR grammars. It would be particularly interesting to investigate whether the proposed method can be applied to the Penn Treebank dataset, which has been a focus of previous work (e.g., Klein and Manning). This would help to establish the method's effectiveness in a more challenging and realistic setting.

In conclusion, while the paper presents a compelling approach to estimating the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars and reports promising results on two tasks, I believe that additional experiments, particularly on natural language data, would significantly enhance the paper's contribution and impact.
