- Proposes a novel unsupervised method to induce unified AND-OR fragments rather than searching AND/OR operations separately.  
- Computes the value of a new fragment efficiently using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on parsing events and visual object tasks.  
- Overall judged strong, well-motivated, and technically sound (reviewer could not verify supplementary derivations).  
- Needs clarification whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is an exact quantity or an approximation.  
- Needs clarification whether the method can learn fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to a CNF-like form.  
- Experimental section is hard to follow regarding exact procedures, data representations, and the nature of learned structures.  
- Assumes familiarity with recent visual-grammar work (notably S.C. Zhu’s group), reducing accessibility for broader readers.  
- Authors urged to use supplementary material to provide more detail and concrete experimental illustrations.  
- Evaluation is purely quantitative and should include qualitative examples demonstrating the meaningful structures learned and comparisons to competing methods.
