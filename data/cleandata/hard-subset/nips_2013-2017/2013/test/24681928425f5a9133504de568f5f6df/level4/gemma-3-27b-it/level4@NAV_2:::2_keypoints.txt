- Presents an unsupervised method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars by iteratively introducing AND-OR fragments.
- Optimizes posterior probability by evaluating both likelihood and prior gains for proposed fragments.
- Well-motivated and situated within related work on stochastic grammars and sum-product networks.
- Builds on prior structure learning for language and images/events and offers a unified approach to compositions and reconfigurations.
- Key contributions: data-agnostic formalization, posterior-probability optimization, joint learning of AND/OR fragments, and empirical validation.
- Strength: principled structure-learning framework with an elegant likelihood-gain computation based on sufficient statistics and tensor/matrix coherence.
- Strength: convincing argument and empirical evidence for the benefits of simultaneously learning AND and OR fragments.
- Empirical results on event and image grammars are competitive and demonstrate practical utility.
- Connects to Bayesian model merging literature and acknowledges potential scalability concerns.
- Weakness: limited discussion and analysis of computational complexity and scalability to larger datasets or more complex grammars.
- Weakness: no experiments on standard natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank) or comparisons to established parsing methods, limiting claims of generalizability.
- Weakness: presentation could include more detailed explanations of some technical aspects.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability analysis and natural language evaluation.
