

This paper proposes a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. While the authors claim their approach "generalizes" prior work on structure learning, it in fact constitutes a special case that enables tractable solutions—making the problem solvable rather than more general. This is a valid and valuable contribution, and I do not dispute that the problem addressed is distinct from those in NLP structure learning. The core algorithm is well-motivated and empirically effective, outperforming competing methods for grammar induction in the experiments presented.  

The experimental evaluation would be strengthened by including additional comparisons to relevant baselines. Specifically, the method by Stolcke and Omohondro (2000, reference 14), which is applicable to both tasks considered here, would provide a meaningful benchmark. Such a comparison would better contextualize the proposed approach's advantages and limitations.  

Overall, this is a strong contribution presenting a clear and effective method for structure learning within a specific class of grammars. The technical approach is sound, and the results are convincing within the paper's scope.
