- Presents an unsupervised method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (AOGs) via searching And-Or fragments that introduce an And-node and child Or-nodes together.
- Optimizes a posterior objective combining a sparsity prior (penalizing grammar size) with Viterbi likelihood to score candidate fragments.
- Derives sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors and context matrices) to enable fast posterior-gain computation under an unambiguity assumption, with rule probabilities estimated from reduction counts.
- Evaluated on event and image parsing, showing competitive event performance and substantial perplexity reductions on images.
- Builds on prior grammar induction, image AOG learning, event grammar learning, and is conceptually related to SPNs and Viterbi/unambiguity biases.
- Strength: Clear contribution — unified fragment learning addresses data sparsity and redundancy from separate And/Or induction.
- Strength: Principled objective — explicit posterior optimization and coherent likelihood/prior accounting via sufficient statistics.
- Strength: Generality — AOG formalization is data-agnostic and demonstrated across temporal (events) and spatial (images) domains.
- Strength: Empirical gains — competitive on events and notably better image perplexity vs prior work.
- Weakness/question: Unclear whether the reported likelihood gain computed via tensor/matrix coherence is exact under stated assumptions or an approximation.
- Weakness/question: Unclear support and efficiency for fragments with arity > 2; possible practical restriction to binary rules affects expressivity.
- Weakness/question: Reliance on an unambiguity assumption — sensitivity to violations and lack of diagnostics/safeguards is not addressed.
- Weakness: Experimental clarity — event experiments lack concrete mappings from raw data to terminals, relation sets, fragment search constraints, and reduction update details.
- Weakness: Lack of qualitative analysis — no visualized learned fragments, parse trees, or structural comparisons to baselines to substantiate structural claims.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns — key implementation details, hyperparameters, search settings, and preprocessing are deferred to the supplement.
- Suggestions: Clarify exactness vs approximation of likelihood gain and characterize any approximation error.
- Suggestions: State support for n-ary fragments and discuss search scaling with arity or justify binary restriction.
- Suggestions: Provide qualitative examples and visualizations of learned fragments and parse trees, plus extended supplement with derivations, pseudocode, parameters, preprocessing, and ablations.
- Pros for acceptance: Novel unified fragment-based AOG learning, principled posterior objective, efficient sufficient-statistics computation, and cross-domain quantitative improvements.
- Cons for acceptance: Hard-to-follow experiments without background, lack of qualitative learned-structure analysis, and open questions on likelihood exactness and higher-arity support.
- Overall: Promising and technically sound work with good quantitative results, but needs clearer experimental exposition, qualitative examples, and supplemental details to address open questions.
