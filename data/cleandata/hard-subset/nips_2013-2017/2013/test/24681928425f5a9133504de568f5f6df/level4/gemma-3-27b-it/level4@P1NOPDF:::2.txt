This paper presents a method for learning both the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. These grammars, which incorporate both AND and OR rules, are well-suited to represent various recursive phenomena, including natural language and formal grammars. The authors introduce a compelling approach to unsupervised structure learning by iteratively adding new AND-OR fragments and evaluating the likelihood and prior gains of the model.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments on two tasks: learning event grammars and learning image grammars. In both cases, they achieve results competitive with existing approaches. 

Overall, I found this to be a strong paper, as it proposes a tractable approach to learning stochastic grammars modeled using AND and OR rules. However, I have a few criticisms:

1) The authors do not discuss the computational scalability of their learning algorithm. Specifically, it would be valuable to know if the method can scale to datasets containing thousands of data points.

2) Given that natural language is a particularly obvious application for AND-OR grammars, I would have liked to see experiments on natural language sentences. It’s unclear whether the presented methods could be successfully applied to a standard dataset like the Penn Treebank, where previous work (e.g., Klein and Manning) has been focused. While the paper presents a promising method for estimating the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, and achieves encouraging results on two tasks, further experimentation – particularly with natural language data – would strengthen the work.
