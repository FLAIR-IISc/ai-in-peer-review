This paper proposes a method for jointly learning both the structure and the parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars. These grammars consist of And- and Or-rules and can capture recursive structure across domains, including natural language and event grammars. The approach performs unsupervised structure induction by iteratively adding And-Or fragments and evaluating their contribution via gains in likelihood and prior, thereby optimizing the posterior.

The authors evaluate on two settings: learning event grammars and learning image grammars. In both cases, the method attains results that are competitive with prior work.

I found the paper appealing overall, as it offers a plausible and practical route to learning stochastic grammars defined with And/Or rules. My concerns are as follows:
1) The paper does not clearly address the computational tractability of the learning algorithm. How well does it scale to datasets with thousands of examples?
2) I would have liked to see experiments on natural language, which is a primary application area for such grammars. For instance, could the proposed method learn on the Penn Treebank, where prior work (e.g., Klein and Manning) has focused? While the paper demonstrates a framework for estimating both structure and parameters and shows solid results on two domains, additional experiments—especially on natural language data—would strengthen the contribution.
