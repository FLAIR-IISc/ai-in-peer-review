This paper introduces a novel method for unsupervised learning of AND-OR grammar structure, differing from prior work by inducing unified AND-OR fragments instead of separately learning AND and OR components. The proposed approach efficiently computes the value of these fragments using sufficient statistics. The method is evaluated on tasks involving parsing both events and visual objects.

Overall, this is a strong paper addressing an important problem with a well-motivated and technically sound approach. While I was unable to fully verify the supplemental derivations, the algorithm appears robust. I would suggest the authors clarify a couple of points: First, the precision of the “surrogate measure” for likelihood gain – whether it is an approximation or an exact calculation – could be explicitly stated. Second, the paper would benefit from discussing the scalability of the approach to fragments with arity greater than 2, and whether it is limited to learning grammars in Chomsky normal form.

My primary concerns relate to the experimental evaluation. Understanding the specifics of the experiments, the data representation, and the learning process proved challenging. The presentation assumes a high degree of familiarity with recent visual-grammar work from S. C. Zhu’s group, potentially limiting accessibility for those outside this research area. While acknowledging the space constraints of the NIPS format, I strongly encourage the authors to significantly expand the experimental details and include concrete examples in the supplementary material should the paper be accepted.

Specifically, I would be very interested to see examples of the structure learned by the proposed method. While the quantitative results are reported as satisfactory, the paper would be more impactful if it also demonstrated the discovery of interesting, and comparatively better, structural patterns. In conclusion, this is a promising contribution to unsupervised AND-OR grammar learning. However, the experiments and results are difficult to interpret without significant prior knowledge of related work. Supplementing the paper with more detailed explanations of the experimental setup and illustrative examples of the learned structure would greatly enhance its clarity and impact.
