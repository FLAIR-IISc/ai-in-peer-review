- Introduces a unified framework for unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars applicable to images and event sequences.  
- Formalizes stochastic And-Or grammars in a data-type-agnostic way.  
- Proposes an iterative algorithm that learns grammar structure and parameters by optimizing the posterior probability.  
- Key innovation: "And-Or fragments" that jointly search for new compositions (And-rules) and reconfigurations (Or-rules).  
- Starts from a trivial initial grammar and iteratively inserts fragments to optimize posterior, improving robustness and efficiency.  
- Uses sufficient statistics for computational efficiency in computing posterior gains.  
- Theoretical derivation of likelihood and prior gains is thorough and well-motivated.  
- Empirical evaluation on event and image grammars shows competitive or superior performance, especially on complex datasets.  
- Paper is generally very clear with precise formal definitions and helpful algorithmic examples.  
- Section 3.3’s theoretical analysis could use more intuitive explanations for non-expert readers.  
- Original contribution: unifies composition and reconfiguration learning, yielding more compact grammars and cross-domain applicability.  
- Significant impact: enables learning grammar structure from unannotated data across diverse domains, useful for vision and activity recognition.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Main weakness: limited experimental comparisons to recent baselines (e.g., Stolcke & Omohundro); add such comparisons to strengthen validation.
