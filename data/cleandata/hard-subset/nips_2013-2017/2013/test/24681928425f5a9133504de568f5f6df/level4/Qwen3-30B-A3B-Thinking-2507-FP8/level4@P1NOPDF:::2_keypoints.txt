- Introduces a method for jointly learning structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars to model recursive phenomena.  
- Proposes a tractable unsupervised structure-learning procedure that incrementally adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates likelihood and prior gains.  
- Evaluated on event grammars and image grammars, obtaining competitive results relative to prior work.  
- Core idea is compelling and provides a practical framework for modeling AND/OR grammar structure.  
- Major limitation: no analysis or experiments on computational scalability—unclear if the method handles datasets with thousands of instances or its time/space complexity.  
- Major limitation: no experiments on natural language data (e.g., Penn Treebank), so applicability to syntactic learning is untested.  
- Authors should either extend experiments to natural language benchmarks or explicitly discuss challenges and feasibility for sentence-level grammar learning.  
- Overall assessment: well-motivated and technically sound, but missing scalability and natural-language evidence limits the paper’s impact.
