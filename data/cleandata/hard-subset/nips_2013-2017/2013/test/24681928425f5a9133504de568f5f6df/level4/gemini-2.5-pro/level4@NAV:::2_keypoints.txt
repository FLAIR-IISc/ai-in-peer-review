- Introduces a method for jointly inferring structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars capable of modeling recursive phenomena.
- Proposes unsupervised, incremental structure learning by adding AND-OR fragments guided by likelihood and a prior.
- Validated on two tasks—event grammar induction and image grammar learning.
- Reported performance is on par with established prior methods in the evaluated tasks.
- Paper is well-presented and the approach appears computationally feasible.
- Major criticism: scalability is not addressed—no analysis of computational complexity or handling of large datasets (e.g., thousands of examples).
- Major criticism: experimental validation is limited and lacks application to natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank), leaving applicability to NLP unclear.
- Suggestion: broader experiments, especially on linguistic data, would increase the paper’s impact.
