- Proposes a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Authors frame the approach as a generalization of prior work; reviewer argues it’s better seen as a special case that makes the problem tractable.  
- That framing distinction is reasonable and the work is a valuable contribution.  
- The basic algorithm is well-conceived.  
- Method shows empirical success relative to other grammar induction methods.  
- Experimental evaluation could be strengthened with more comparative baselines.  
- Recommends adding a comparison to Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14), which appears applicable to both tasks.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper that makes a worthwhile contribution to learning the structure of a specific class of grammars.
