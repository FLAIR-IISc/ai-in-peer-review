- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for AND-OR grammar induction that unifies AND and OR fragments and computes fragment values using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on parsing tasks for events and visual objects.  
- Overall a strong, well-motivated, and technically sound contribution, though supplemental derivations were not fully verified.  
- Clarify whether the “surrogate measure” of likelihood gain is an exact calculation or an approximation.  
- Clarify if the method supports fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental setup, data representation, and learning process are insufficiently explained and hard to follow.  
- Presentation assumes heavy familiarity with prior visual-grammar work (S. C. Zhu’s group), limiting accessibility.  
- Supplementary materials should be expanded with detailed experiment explanations and concrete illustrations.  
- Results are presented only quantitatively and lack qualitative insight into the learned structures.  
- Provide examples showing the discovered grammar structures and comparisons demonstrating that the method learns more interesting structure than competitors.
