This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. While the authors suggest their work "generalizes" previous structure learning methods, it is more accurately described as a special case, a restriction that is key to the problem's tractability. This focus on a tractable subproblem is a valuable contribution on its own, and the authors need not frame the work as more general than the structure learning problems typically faced in NLP. The proposed algorithm is sensible and demonstrates strong performance when compared against the other grammar induction methods in the evaluation.

The experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including additional comparisons. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro [14] seems applicable to the tasks presented and would provide a valuable point of comparison.

In summary, this is a solid paper presenting an effective method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars.
