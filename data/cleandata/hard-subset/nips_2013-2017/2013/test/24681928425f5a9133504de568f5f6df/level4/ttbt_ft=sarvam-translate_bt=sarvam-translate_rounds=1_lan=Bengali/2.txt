This paper presents a method for learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. Such grammars contain AND rules and OR rules, and these can be used to represent several recurrent phenomena in natural language and even in grammar. The authors have presented an excellent method for unsupervised learning of these grammars by introducing new AND-OR fragments in successive steps and measuring the likelihood and prior gain of their models.

The authors have presented experiments on two tasks: event grammar learning and image grammar learning. In both cases, they have achieved results that are competitive with prior art.

I liked my overall paper because it seems like an easy way to learn stochastic grammars that can be modeled using AND and OR rules. My reasons for criticism are as follows:

1) The authors did not mention how simple the learning algorithm is. Will it scale to thousands of data points?
2) I wanted to see experiments on natural language sentences because natural language is the most obvious application of such grammars. For example, would it be possible to learn using the methods presented in the Penn Tribbank dataset, where previous works (e.g., Klein and Manning) focused? This paper presents a way to estimate the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars and presents beautiful results on two tasks; I would have liked to see more experiments, especially on natural language data.
