This paper presents a method for learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, which can be used to represent various recursive phenomena, including natural language and images. The authors propose a novel approach to unsupervised structure learning by introducing new AND-OR fragments incrementally and evaluating the likelihood and prior gains of their model.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments on two tasks: learning event grammars and learning image grammars, achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art methods. Overall, the paper presents a tractable approach to learning stochastic grammars that can be modeled using AND and OR rules.

However, I have some criticisms and suggestions for improvement. Firstly, the authors do not provide an analysis of the scalability of their learning algorithm. It is unclear whether the algorithm can efficiently handle large datasets with thousands of datapoints. Secondly, I would have liked to see experiments on natural language sentences, as this is a primary application domain for stochastic AND-OR grammars. Specifically, it would be interesting to investigate whether the proposed method can be applied to the Penn Treebank dataset, which has been a focus of previous work (e.g., Klein and Manning). While the paper presents promising results on two tasks, additional experiments, particularly on natural language data, would further strengthen the contributions of this work.
