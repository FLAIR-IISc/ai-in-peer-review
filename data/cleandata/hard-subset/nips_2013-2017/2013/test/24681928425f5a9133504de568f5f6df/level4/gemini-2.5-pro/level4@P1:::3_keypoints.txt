- Proposes unsupervised learning of And-Or grammar structures via unified And-Or fragments rather than separate And/Or rule searches.  
- Key technical innovation: efficient computation of posterior gain for adding fragments using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on parsing events in videos and objects in images.  
- Strength: tackles an important, fundamental problem in unsupervised grammatical structure induction.  
- Strength: Novel and elegant unified-fragment approach that may be more robust and efficient, especially with limited data.  
- Strength: Algorithm appears technically sound, though supplementary derivations were not independently verified.  
- Weakness: Experimental setup is unclear—insufficient detail on data representations, procedures, and learned grammars; assumes domain-specific familiarity.  
- Suggestion: Provide a more detailed, self-contained experimental description in the appendix/supplement for clarity and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Results are purely quantitative with no qualitative examples of learned structures.  
- Suggestion: Include visualizations and comparative examples of discovered fragments to illustrate what the model captures versus baselines.  
- Question: Is the “surrogate measure” an approximation or an exact reformulation under the grammar unambiguity assumption?  
- Question: Is the method limited to binary (arity-2) compositions or can it tractably learn higher-arity rules?  
- Overall: Novel and technically interesting contribution, but impact and accessibility are limited by opaque experiments and lack of qualitative evidence.
