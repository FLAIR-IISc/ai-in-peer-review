- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments instead of separately searching for AND and OR operations.  
- Computes the value of new AND-OR fragments efficiently using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on tasks including parsing events and visual objects.  
- Tackles an important, well-motivated problem in grammar learning.  
- Algorithm appears technically sound, though supplemental derivations were not verified by the reviewer.  
- Unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Uncertain if the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental section is hard to understand; details about example representations and learned structures are unclear.  
- Paper seems targeted to readers familiar with S. C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work, limiting broader accessibility.  
- Recommends adding supplementary materials with detailed experiment descriptions and concrete illustrations if accepted.  
- Lacks examples of interesting discovered structures and comparisons to competing approaches.  
- Quantitative results are satisfactory but not particularly compelling; overall impact is reduced by clarity and illustration gaps.
