- Proposes an unsupervised method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars using unified And-Or fragments.  
- Formalizes stochastic And-Or grammars in a data-type-agnostic way and optimizes grammar posterior using sufficient statistics from training data.  
- Avoids separate optimization of And (composition) and Or (reconfiguration) components by unifying them in a single framework.  
- Evaluated on event and image grammars, showing competitive or superior performance to prior work [1,3,7,17,18].  
- Originality: unified framework addresses prior data-scarcity issues and advances structure learning approaches.  
- Technical quality: rigorous derivations using n-gram tensors and context matrices; empirical results show consistent improvements and efficient scaling.  
- Significance: method is data-type agnostic, robust to sparse data, and broadly applicable to pattern recognition tasks.  
- Clarity weakness: experimental section lacks detail for non-specialists (data representation, "co-occurring" relations, effects of grammar changes) and needs concrete examples/visualizations.  
- Technical ambiguity: unclear whether the surrogate likelihood gain (Eq.1) is exact or approximate and whether method supports fragments with arity >2.  
- Significance gap: paper misses qualitative analysis showing why learned structures matter (e.g., capturing domain-specific relations like temporal vs. co-occurrence).  
- Pro acceptance points: novel unified framework, strong technical foundation, competitive empirical results, fits NIPS focus.  
- Con acceptance points: insufficient exposition for outsiders, unresolved arity questions, and missed opportunity to showcase learned structures; minor revisions recommended.
