- Introduces a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- The paper’s claim of generalizing prior structure learning is overstated; it addresses a specific, tractable case.  
- Focusing on this restricted case yields a practical, tractable algorithm and does not diminish the contribution.  
- Framing the work as solving a particular class of structure learning problems is reasonable and defensible.  
- The core algorithm is sound and achieves competitive results against existing grammar induction techniques.  
- Experimental evaluation is solid but should include more comprehensive comparisons, notably Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14) as a baseline.  
- Overall, a well-executed paper that clearly contributes a practical method for inducing stochastic And-Or grammars.
