- Proposes an unsupervised, incremental method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars by adding fragments based on data likelihood and a model prior.
- Demonstrates the approach on event and image grammar tasks with results competitive with prior work.
- Main contribution is a seemingly tractable, data-driven approach to the challenging problem of grammar induction.
- Structure-learning procedure is well-motivated and incremental.
- Paper lacks discussion of algorithmic tractability and scalability; unclear if it scales to datasets with thousands of examples.
- Needs computational-complexity analysis or empirical runtime measurements to support scalability claims.
- Omits evaluation on natural language; applying to benchmarks like the Penn Treebank would enable direct comparison to prior grammar-induction work (e.g., Klein and Manning).
- Current experiments show potential but require more thorough validation, especially on natural language data, to fully assess contributions.
