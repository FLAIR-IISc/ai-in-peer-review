- Introduces a novel method for unsupervised learning of unified AND-OR grammar fragments, computable via sufficient statistics; evaluated on event parsing and visual objects.
- Paper is generally strong, novel, well-motivated, and technically sound, though supplemental derivations were not independently verified.
- Needs clarification whether the surrogate measure for likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.
- Needs clarification whether the method supports fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to Chomsky Normal Form.
- Experimental details are insufficiently explained: methodology, input representations, and learned structures are unclear.
- Assumes familiarity with S. C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work, making results hard to assess for outsiders.
- Strong recommendation to provide extensive supplementary material with concrete examples and visualizations of the experimental setup and outcomes.
- Major shortcoming: results are purely quantitative and do not illustrate the meaningful grammatical structures learned.
- Reviewer asks for (1) examples of specific, interesting structures discovered and (2) evidence these structures beat competing approaches.
- Summary: compelling theoretical contribution but experiments require substantial clarification and illustrative content; addressing these would make the paper valuable.
