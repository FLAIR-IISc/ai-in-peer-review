- Proposes a bottom-up, unsupervised algorithm for stochastic And-Or grammars that starts from a data-memorizing grammar and iteratively adds unified And-Or fragments to improve posterior probability.  
- Core contribution: jointly induces compositional (And) and reconfigurable (Or) structures via unified And-Or fragments, avoiding separate error-prone steps.  
- Technical advance: derives an efficient closed-form posterior-gain computation using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensor + context matrix), enabling feasible greedy fragment search.  
- Claimed generality: framework is domain-agnostic and evaluated on both temporal event and spatial image data, showing comparable or superior quantitative performance.  
- Strength: original and well-motivated idea—unifying And/Or induction addresses a known challenge and can avoid local optima and produce more compact grammars.  
- Strength: solid Bayesian grounding and significant technical contribution in the efficient posterior-gain derivation and connections to tensor/matrix coherence.  
- Strength: demonstrated generality and strong quantitative results across multiple datasets and domains.  
- Weakness: experimental section lacks detail (data representations, quantization, atomic patterns, temporal relations), hindering interpretability and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: missing qualitative analysis and visualizations of learned grammars (e.g., meaningful parts in animal faces, qualitative comparison to separate And/Or methods).  
- Weakness: minor technical clarifications needed—clarify whether posterior gain is exact under the unambiguity/Viterbi assumption or a surrogate, and discuss scalability for n-ary rules with n>2–3.  
- Recommendation: accept, but require expanded experimental details and qualitative examples in supplementary material to improve clarity and impact.
