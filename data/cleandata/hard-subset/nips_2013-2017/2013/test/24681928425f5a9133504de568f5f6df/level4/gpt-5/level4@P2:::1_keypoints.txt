- Presents an unsupervised method to learn structure and parameters of context-free stochastic And-Or grammars (AOGs) with a data-agnostic unified formalization.
- Starts from a trivial grammar and iteratively introduces And-Or fragments (And-rule + Or-rule sets) to capture compositions and alternatives jointly.
- Optimizes a posterior objective with a sparsity prior, using Viterbi likelihood and sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors and context matrices) under an unambiguity assumption.
- Applied to event parsing and image parsing, showing competitive or improved performance compared to prior AOG methods.
- Builds on prior AOG and grammar induction work, but offers a unified cross-modality formalization and joint induction of And/Or structures.
- The contribution is more a principled specialization to context-free AOGs with Viterbi/unambiguity bias than a generalization of all prior structure learning.
- Posterior maximization and derivations for likelihood/prior gains are clear and well motivated.
- The Viterbi/unambiguity approximations are standard but may introduce bias in learning.
- Experiments show consistent gains over selected baselines but the comparison set is limited.
- Missing comparisons to Bayesian model merging and additional NLP-style induction baselines weaken empirical claims.
- Ablation studies (fragment types, α, beam width) and details on beam/restart strategies are absent and needed to explain gains.
- Reproducibility would improve with more implementation details (beam size, encoding costs sa/so, runtime on larger datasets).
- Paper is generally well written; unified AOG definition and figures are helpful but some figure labels are hard to read.
- Recommend defining sa and so earlier, moving key derivation parts from supplement to main text, and including concise pseudocode.
- The And-Or fragment idea and efficient posterior-gain computation via sufficient statistics are original and practically useful.
- The method is significant for practitioners across vision and event understanding but not a theoretical breakthrough.
- Pros: clear cross-domain AOG formulation, joint And-Or induction, principled sparsity objective, efficient search, solid event/image results.
- Cons: limited baselines, reliance on Viterbi/unambiguity assumptions, missing ablations/implementation details, scalability not fully demonstrated, possible overstatement of generalization.
- Suggested actions: add comparisons to Stolcke & Omohundro and other induction baselines, run ablations, clarify coding costs and pseudocode, and discuss runtime/memory scaling.
- Overall assessment: solid, practical, and novel AOG structure-learning paper with sensible methods and good empirical results; leans toward acceptance.
