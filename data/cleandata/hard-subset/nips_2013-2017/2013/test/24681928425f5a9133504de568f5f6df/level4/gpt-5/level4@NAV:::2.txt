This paper proposes a method to learn both the structure and the parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. These grammars comprise AND and OR rules and can model various recursive phenomena, including natural language and grammars themselves. The authors present a solid unsupervised structure-learning approach that incrementally introduces new AND-OR fragments at successive steps, assessing the gains in model likelihood and prior.

The paper evaluates the method on two tasks: learning event grammars and learning image grammars. In both cases, the results are competitive with prior work.

I liked the paper overall, as it appears to provide a tractable way to learn stochastic grammars expressible with AND and OR rules. My criticisms are as follows:
1) The paper does not address the computational tractability of the learning algorithm. Will it scale to thousands of data points?
2) I would have liked to see experiments on natural language sentences, as natural language is the most obvious use case for such grammars. Is it feasible to learn with the proposed methods on the Penn Treebank, for example, which prior work (e.g., Klein and Manning) has targeted? The paper offers a framework for estimating both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars and shows good results on two tasks; additional experiments, especially on natural language data, would strengthen it.
