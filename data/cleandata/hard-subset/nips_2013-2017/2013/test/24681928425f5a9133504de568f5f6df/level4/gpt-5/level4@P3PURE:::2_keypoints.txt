- Proposes joint learning of structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars that model recursive phenomena.
- Introduces an unsupervised structure-learning method that iteratively adds AND-OR fragments evaluated by likelihood and prior gains.
- Evaluated on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks with competitive results against prior work.
- Reviewer finds the approach compelling and the empirical results encouraging.
- Major concern: missing analysis of computational tractability and scalability (complexity, runtime, memory) and no empirical scaling experiments.
- Major concern: lacks experiments on natural language (e.g., Penn Treebank) or discussion of feasibility/limitations for sentence-level grammar learning.
- Recommendation: strengthen the paper with scalability analysis and include or substantively discuss natural language experiments to better establish impact.
