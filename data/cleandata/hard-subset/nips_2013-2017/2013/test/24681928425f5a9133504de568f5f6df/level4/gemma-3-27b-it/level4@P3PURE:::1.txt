This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors argue this work “generalizes” previous structure learning approaches; however, it is more accurately described as a special case, a restriction that facilitates tractability. This is a reasonable justification in its own right, and the paper makes a worthwhile contribution – the authors should not claim broader generality than exists relative to structure learning problems in natural language processing. The core algorithm is sensible and demonstrates success when benchmarked against existing grammar induction methods. 

The experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including comparisons to a wider range of approaches. Specifically, the method proposed by Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears applicable to both tasks considered and would serve as a valuable comparative baseline. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting a method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars.
