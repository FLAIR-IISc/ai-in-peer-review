This paper proposes a novel approach to learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars, presenting it as a generalization of prior work in structure learning. However, it is more accurate to consider this as a specialized case that renders the problem more tractable. Notwithstanding this, the paper offers a valuable contribution in its own right, and it is unnecessary to debate its generality in relation to the structure learning challenges encountered in NLP. The fundamental algorithm is sound and has demonstrated success in comparison to other grammar induction methods.

To further enhance the experimental evaluation, it would be beneficial to incorporate additional comparisons with alternative methods. Specifically, the method developed by Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears to be applicable to both tasks and would provide a valuable point of comparison. Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a method for learning the structure of a specific class of grammars.
