- Presents a novel method for unsupervised induction of unified AND-OR fragments rather than separate AND/OR searches.  
- Key technical feature: efficient computation of a fragment's value using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on parsing tasks for events and visual objects.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated contribution with seemingly solid technical algorithmic aspects.  
- Reviewer could not verify derivations in the supplementary material.  
- Request to clarify whether the "surrogate measure" for likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Ask whether the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is effectively limited to Chomsky-normal-form (binary) grammars.  
- Major critique: experimental section is hard to follow—unclear methodology, data representation, and nature of learned outputs.  
- Experiments assume deep familiarity with S. C. Zhu’s work, reducing accessibility for outside readers.  
- Strong suggestion to include comprehensive supplementary material detailing experimental procedures and concrete illustrations.  
- Desire for qualitative examples of discovered structures—current quantitative results are satisfactory but not sufficiently illuminating.  
- Conclusion: valuable contribution but impact is tempered by unclear experimental presentation; supplementary clarifications and qualitative examples would substantially improve the paper.
