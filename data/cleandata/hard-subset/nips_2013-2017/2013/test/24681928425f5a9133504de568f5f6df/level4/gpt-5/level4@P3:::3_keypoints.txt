- Presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an unsupervised method to jointly learn And-Or fragments (compositionality and reconfigurability) from unannotated data.
- Optimizes a posterior with a sparsity prior, approximates likelihood via Viterbi, and derives sufficient statistics (n-gram tensor and context matrix) for efficient fragment gain evaluation under an unambiguity assumption.
- Experiments on event grammars (video-derived action streams) and image grammars (synthetic and real) show performance comparable to or better than recent baselines.
- Relates to prior work in probabilistic grammar induction (NLP), And-Or templates in vision, and decomposable sum-product networks; novelty is unified fragment search with explicit posterior objective and sufficient statistics.
- Technical soundness: objective and search are clearly defined, but derivation relies on supplementary material and the unambiguity assumption; need clarity on where approximations enter.
- Scope of fragments: fragments appear to allow arbitrary arity n; authors should explicitly state supported arities and practical tractability for n>2.
- Relations and parameterization: method is relation-agnostic, but performance depends on relation encoding/scoring—more detail needed for reproducibility.
- Search and complexity: uses greedy/beam search with random restarts; requires a complexity analysis, pruning criteria, and runtime/memory stats to assess scalability and robustness.
- Experiments: quantitative results are promising, but preprocessing, featureization, terminal inventories, relation sets, smoothing, and parsing settings are under-specified.
- Clarity: need to clarify “surrogate measure” vs exact likelihood gain and conditions (e.g., unambiguity) for validity.
- Clarity: provide concrete definitions and small examples for the n-gram tensor and context matrix in both event and image domains.
- Clarity: summarize search procedure steps (candidate generation, scoring, acceptance) and termination criteria in the main text.
- Clarity: clearly describe event experiment variants (IDs vs action vectors, order constraints, grounding) and provide diagrams of learned structures and parses.
- Clarity: show qualitative parses/templates for image experiments and at least one compare-and-contrast against competing methods.
- Originality: joint learning of And and Or via fragments and posterior-based fragment selection with sufficient statistics is novel and synthesizes ideas across fields.
- Significance: method can induce compact, interpretable structured models across modalities and shows empirical improvements, but broader impact is limited by clarity and reproducibility issues.
- Specific question: Is the likelihood gain expression exact under grammar unambiguity and Viterbi training, or still approximate, and what is the empirical impact when unambiguity is violated?
- Specific question: Which arities n are explored, and how do runtime and accuracy scale with n and candidate pool size?
- Specific question: How are And-rule relations parameterized and scored across domains, and are relation sets fixed or learned?
- Specific question: What smoothing is used for computing image perplexity?
- Specific question: How sensitive are results to sparsity hyperparameter α and encoding costs sa, so?
- Specific question: Please show qualitative examples of learned event hierarchies, image part decompositions, and parse trees.
- Pros: principled sparsity-regularized objective, efficient sufficient-statistics for search, unified fragment learning coupling composition and reconfiguration, domain-agnostic demonstrations, and interpretable models.
- Cons: reliance on unambiguity and Viterbi approximations without sufficient justification or ablation, experimental exposition is hard to follow, search heuristics and complexity under-documented, and dependence on manual relation sets may limit portability.
- Suggestions: provide clear posterior gain derivation and ablation on unambiguity, include qualitative visualizations, detail preprocessing/terminals/relations/smoothing/evaluation, report runtime/beam/restarts and sensitivity to α, and release code or detailed pseudocode for reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: technically interesting and well-motivated contribution with solid quantitative results; recommend acceptance conditional on improved clarity and richer qualitative/supplementary materials.
