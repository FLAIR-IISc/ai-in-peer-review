This paper presents a method for learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. Such grammars contain AND rules and OR rules and can be used to represent multiple recurrent events, including natural language and even grammar. The authors have presented a good method for unsupervised learning of these grammars by introducing new AND-OR clauses in sequential steps and measuring their model likelihood and prior probabilities.

The authors present experiments on two tasks: event grammar learning and image grammar learning. In both, they obtain results that are competitive with prior art.

I liked this paper overall because it seems like an easy way to learn stochastic grammars that can be modeled using AND and OR rules. My criticism of this paper stems from the following comments:

1) The authors have not mentioned how easy the learning algorithm is. Will it scale to thousands of data points?
2) I would have liked to see experiments on natural language sentences because natural language is the most obvious application of such grammars. Would it also be possible to learn on pen-treebank datasets using the presented methods, for example, which focused on previous work (e.g., Klein and Manning)? This paper presents a method for estimating the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars and presents good results on two tasks; I would have liked to see experiments, especially on natural language data.
