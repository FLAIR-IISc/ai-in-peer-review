- Introduces a method for jointly learning structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, capturing recursive AND/OR compositions.  
- Proposes an unsupervised incremental structure-learning algorithm that adds AND-OR fragments and scores them by likelihood and prior gains.  
- Evaluated on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks and shows competitive performance with existing approaches.  
- Overall assessment: strong, promising, and potentially practical approach to learning stochastic grammars with AND/OR rules.  
- Lacks discussion of computational scalability; unclear how the method performs on much larger datasets (thousands of examples).  
- Missing experiments on natural language data, despite AND-OR grammars being well-suited to NLP.  
- Recommends evaluation on standard NLP benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank) and comparisons to prior work (e.g., Klein & Manning) to strengthen claims.
