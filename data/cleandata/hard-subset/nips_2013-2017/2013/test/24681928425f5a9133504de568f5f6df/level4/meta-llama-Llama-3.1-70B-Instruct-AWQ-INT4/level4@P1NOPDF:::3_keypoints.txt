- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for learning AND-OR grammars by inducing unified AND-OR fragments rather than separate AND/OR searches.  
- Shows the value of a proposed AND-OR fragment can be computed efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on tasks including parsing events and visual objects.  
- Algorithm is well-motivated and appears technically sound (reviewer did not verify supplemental derivations thoroughly).  
- Unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Unclear whether the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental section is hard to follow: specifics of procedures, example representations, and learned outputs are not clear.  
- Paper assumes familiarity with S. C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work, reducing accessibility for readers unfamiliar with that literature.  
- Reviewer requests more experimental details and concrete illustrations in supplementary material if accepted.  
- Wants examples of interesting structure learned and evidence that this approach uncovers more/better structure than competing methods.  
- Overall: interesting and important contribution but experiments need clearer presentation and demonstration of qualitative structure learning.
