- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- AND-OR grammars use AND and OR production rules to represent recursive phenomena like language and compositional visual scenes.  
- Introduces an iterative structure-learning approach that proposes new AND-OR fragments and evaluates them by increase in data likelihood and model prior.  
- Evaluated on two tasks: learning event grammars from video and learning image grammars.  
- Reported results are competitive with prior work on both tasks.  
- Primary criticism: scalability is not addressed—no computational complexity analysis or tests on larger datasets.  
- Primary criticism: lacks experiments on natural language and benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank), limiting assessment of broader applicability.  
- Conclusion: promising and tractable approach, but would be strengthened by scalability analysis and empirical evaluation on natural language data.
