- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments instead of searching for AND and OR separately.
- Value of a proposed AND-OR fragment can be computed efficiently using a set of sufficient statistics.
- Method is evaluated on tasks including parsing events and visual object learning.
- Paper is well-written, addresses an important problem, and the algorithm appears technically sound.
- Reviewer did not fully verify supplemental derivations due to limited time.
- Unclear whether the reported "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is an exact or approximate notion.
- Unclear if the approach extends to learning fragments with arity >2 or is limited to grammars in Chomsky normal form.
- Major concerns about the experimental section: methodology, representation of examples, and presentation of learned results were hard to understand.
- Paper seems targeted to readers familiar with S. C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work, reducing accessibility for others.
- Recommendation to include more experimental details and concrete illustrations in the supplementary material if accepted.
- Reviewer would like to see examples of interesting structures learned, since quantitative results alone were not very illuminating.
- Overall: interesting contribution but experiments are hard to follow and need clearer presentation and illustrative results.
