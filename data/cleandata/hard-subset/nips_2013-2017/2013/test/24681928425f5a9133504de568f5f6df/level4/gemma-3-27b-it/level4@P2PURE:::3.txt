This paper introduces a novel approach to unsupervised learning of AND-OR grammar structure. Unlike prior work that independently searches for AND and OR operations, this method induces unified AND-OR fragments, and efficiently computes their value using sufficient statistics. The approach is evaluated on tasks involving parsing events and visual objects.

Overall, this is a strong paper addressing an important problem with a novel and well-motivated technique. The algorithm appears technically sound, though a complete verification of the supplemental derivations was beyond the scope of my review. I would encourage the authors to clarify two points: first, whether the “surrogate measure” of likelihood gain is an approximation or an exact calculation; and second, whether the method extends to fragments with arity greater than 2, or is limited to grammars in Chomsky normal form.

My primary concerns relate to the experimental evaluation. I found it difficult to fully understand the experimental setup, data representation, and learning process. The presentation strongly assumes familiarity with recent visual-grammar work from S. C. Zhu’s group – ideally, a reader who has thoroughly studied and potentially reimplemented this prior research. While the NIPS format is concise, the supplementary materials offer an opportunity to provide significantly more detail and concrete illustrations of the experiments. 

Specifically, I would be interested to see examples of the structure discovered in the learned grammars. The paper effectively establishes the importance of grammar and structure learning, but the results are presented solely quantitatively, which is somewhat disappointing. While these quantitative results are satisfactory, they lack compelling insight. Demonstrating that the approach learns interesting structure, and learns *more* interesting structure compared to competing methods, would significantly strengthen the paper.

In conclusion, this is an interesting contribution to unsupervised learning of AND-OR grammars. Although I found the experiments and their interpretation challenging due to reliance on specialized prior work, I believe the paper has merit. If accepted, I recommend the authors substantially expand the supplementary materials with detailed explanations of the experiments and illustrative examples of the learned structures.
