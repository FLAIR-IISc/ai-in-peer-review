- Presents an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars via iterative bottom-up growth from a trivial memorizing grammar.  
- Introduces "And-Or fragments" to jointly learn compositional (And) and alternative (Or) rules, guided by maximizing a posterior combining Viterbi likelihood and a sparsity prior.  
- Formalizes stochastic And-Or grammars in a data-agnostic way, enabling application across modalities (images, events, potentially language or time-series).  

Strengths:
- Principled optimization-based approach that greedily maximizes a well-defined posterior instead of relying on heuristics.  
- Unified And-Or fragment concept elegantly integrates learning of composition and reconfiguration, reducing risk of poor local optima.  
- Technical soundness: derivation of posterior gain using n-gram tensors and context matrices makes greedy search efficient and tractable.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive or state-of-the-art performance on event grammar and image grammar tasks across synthetic and real datasets.  

Weaknesses / Areas for improvement:
- Lacks a formal scalability and complexity analysis; unclear how runtime scales with sample size, sample length, vocabulary size, and n-gram dimensionality.  
- No experiments on natural language benchmarks, leaving generality and ability to capture recursive linguistic structure untested.  
- Insufficient detail on the search procedure: how candidate sub-tensors are generated, the range of n considered, and specific greedy/beam search heuristics.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, principled method for unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars with a unified And-Or fragment approach and general formulation.  
- Demonstrated empirical effectiveness on two distinct, challenging domains.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Missing complexity analysis and absence of canonical natural language experiments limit assessment of scalability and broader impact.  

Overall:
- High-quality technical contribution with impressive domain results; main reservation is the lack of NLP evaluation to fully demonstrate generality.
