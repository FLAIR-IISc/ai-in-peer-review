

This paper introduces a method for learning both the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, which incorporate AND rules and OR rules to model recursive phenomena such as natural language and image structures. The authors propose an unsupervised structure learning approach that incrementally introduces new AND-OR fragments while evaluating likelihood and prior gains to guide model construction.  

Experiments are conducted on two tasks: learning event grammars and image grammars, where the method achieves competitive results relative to prior work. The approach presents a computationally tractable framework for learning stochastic grammars defined by AND and OR rules, which is a promising contribution. However, two key concerns remain:  
1) The computational tractability of the learning algorithm is not addressed. It is unclear whether the method scales effectively to datasets containing thousands of data points.  
2) The authors omit experiments on natural language data, despite natural language being the most prominent application domain for such grammars. It is uncertain whether the method can be applied to standard benchmarks like the Penn Treebank (e.g., as used in Klein and Manning's work), which previous approaches have targeted. The paper would benefit from additional experiments, particularly on natural language datasets, to demonstrate broader applicability.
