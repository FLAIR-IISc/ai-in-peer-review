- Paper presents a method for learning stochastic And-Or grammar structure but the “generalizes” claim is misleading — it’s a tractable special case.  
- Offers a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to data type.  
- Proposes an iterative bottom-up algorithm that starts from a trivial grammar and adds intermediate nonterminals to raise posterior probability.  
- Basic algorithm is sensible and performs well compared to other grammar induction methods.  
- Experimental results: competitive on event grammars and outperform prior methods on image grammars.  
- Experimental evaluation needs more comparisons to other methods, notably Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14).  
- Strength: unified formalization applicable across data types.  
- Strength: sensible, successful iterative bottom-up learning process.  
- Strength: particularly strong empirical results on image grammar learning.  
- Weakness: insufficient empirical comparisons to alternative methods.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of limitations and directions for future work.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound, well-motivated, and well-executed, though evaluation breadth could improve.  
- Clarity: well-written with clear notation and helpful figures.  
- Originality: approach is novel within structure learning despite the broader problem being established.  
- Significance: makes a meaningful contribution with potential applications across NLP, vision, and robotics.  
- For acceptance: novel, well-motivated method with promising experimental performance.  
- Against acceptance: experimental comparisons are limited and discussion of limitations/future work is insufficient.
