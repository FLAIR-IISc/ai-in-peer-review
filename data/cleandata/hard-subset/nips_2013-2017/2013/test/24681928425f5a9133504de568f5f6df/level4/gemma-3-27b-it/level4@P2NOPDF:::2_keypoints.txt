- Introduces a method for jointly learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- AND-OR grammars capture recursive phenomena useful for natural language and compositional image generation.  
- Proposes an unsupervised structure-learning approach that iteratively adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates them by likelihood gains and priors.  
- Evaluated on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks, showing competitive results with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong, promising, and potentially tractable method for learning stochastic grammars with AND and OR rules.  
- Major concern: scalability—no discussion of computational complexity or handling of large datasets (e.g., thousands of datapoints).  
- Experimental gap: no evaluations on natural language data, a key application domain for AND-OR grammars.  
- Suggestion: include experiments on standard NLP benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank) and comparisons to prior work like Klein and Manning.  
- Recommendation: expanding experiments to natural language would significantly strengthen the paper’s impact and demonstration of potential.
