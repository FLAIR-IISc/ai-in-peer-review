

This paper presents a method for learning both the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. Such grammars, which incorporate AND rules and OR rules, can model various recursive phenomena, including natural language. The authors propose a tractable approach for unsupervised structure learning by incrementally introducing new AND-OR fragments and measuring the associated likelihood and prior gains.  

The authors evaluate their method on two tasks: learning event grammars and image grammars, achieving results competitive with prior work. The paper presents a compelling approach for learning stochastic grammars using AND and OR rules, with promising empirical results. However, I have two primary concerns:  
1) The paper does not address the computational tractability of the learning algorithm. Specifically, it remains unclear whether the method scales to datasets containing thousands of data points.  
2) Natural language represents the most obvious application domain for such grammars. The authors should demonstrate results on natural language data, such as the Penn Treebank dataset (a standard benchmark used in prior work like Klein and Manning), to validate the approach's applicability to this key domain. While the current experiments are well-executed, additional evaluation on natural language sentences would significantly strengthen the paper's contribution.
