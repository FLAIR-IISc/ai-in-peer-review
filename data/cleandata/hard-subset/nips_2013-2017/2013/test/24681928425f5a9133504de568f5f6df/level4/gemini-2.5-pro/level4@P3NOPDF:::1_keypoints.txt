- Introduces an algorithm for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars with a sensible, well-motivated approach and promising results.
- Focuses on a particular subclass of grammars to make the learning problem tractable; this pragmatic restriction is a clear, well-justified contribution.
- The paper is solid and presents a valuable method for grammar induction.
- The framing as a "generalization" of previous structure learning methods is misleading; the method targets a specialized subclass rather than a general solution.
- Recommend revising claims to emphasize effectiveness for a specific, well-defined class of grammars rather than broad generality.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and would be strengthened by including additional baselines.
- Specifically, include comparison to the state-merging method of Stolcke and Omohondro (1994) as a relevant, classic baseline to better assess performance.
