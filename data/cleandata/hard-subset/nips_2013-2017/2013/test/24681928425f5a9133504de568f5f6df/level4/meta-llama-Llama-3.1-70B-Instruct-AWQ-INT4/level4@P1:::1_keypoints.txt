- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars from a trivial initial grammar via iterative induction of compositions and reconfigurations.  
- Introduces a unified, data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars enabling application across data types.  
- Defines "And-Or fragments" as a mechanism to search for and add high-posterior grammar fragments.  
- Evaluated on event and image grammar learning, achieving comparable or better performance than prior approaches.  
- Experimental evaluation is solid with task-specific comparisons, but lacks broader baseline comparisons.  
- Specifically recommends comparing to Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14), which appears applicable to both tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear algorithm description and implementation details.  
- Provides an analysis of time complexity but could benefit from a more detailed complexity study.  
- Main strengths: novel unified formalization, general learning approach, and solid empirical results.  
- Main weaknesses: limited experimental comparisons and potentially narrow audience due to focus on a specific grammar class.  
- Recommendation: accept, with a request to add more baseline comparisons and expand time-complexity analysis.
