- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for learning stochastic AND-OR grammars by inducing unified AND-OR fragments instead of separate AND/OR searches.  
- Core technical idea: compute fragment value efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Empirically evaluated on event and image parsing tasks.  
- Overall assessment: strong, important, novel, well-motivated, and technically sound.  
- Builds on and extends prior grammar structure learning work and improves recent AND-OR grammar methods for images and events.  
- Technical presentation appears rigorous, though some supplemental derivations were not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Needs clarification on the “surrogate measure” for likelihood gain (is it approximate or exact?).  
- Unclear whether fragment arity is limited (can it learn beyond binary branching or is it restricted to CNF?).  
- Primary concern: experimental presentation is opaque—insufficient detail on setup, data representation, and learning process.  
- Experiments require substantial background knowledge, particularly of prior visual grammar work, to understand.  
- Paper lacks qualitative analysis and examples of the learned grammar structure, weakening the impact despite solid quantitative results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, efficient algorithm, competitive performance, tackles a significant problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear experimental details, limited qualitative insights, and high required background knowledge.
