This paper presents a method for learning both the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. These grammars, incorporating both AND and OR rules, are capable of representing a variety of recursive phenomena, including natural language and compositional grammars. The authors introduce a compelling method for unsupervised structure learning, iteratively adding new AND-OR fragments and evaluating their inclusion based on likelihood and prior gains.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on two tasks: learning event grammars and learning image grammars. In both cases, the results are competitive with existing methods. 

Overall, this is a strong paper that proposes a tractable approach to learning stochastic grammars modeled using AND and OR rules. However, I have the following criticisms:

1) The paper does not discuss the computational scalability of the learning algorithm. Specifically, it is unclear whether the method will scale effectively to datasets containing thousands of data points.
2) Given that natural language is a particularly obvious application for AND-OR grammars, I would have appreciated experiments on natural language sentences. It remains an open question whether learning is feasible using the presented methods on established datasets such as the Penn Treebank, on which previous work (e.g., Klein and Manning) has focused. 

This paper successfully estimates the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars and presents promising results on two tasks; however, further evaluation, particularly with natural language data, would strengthen the work.
