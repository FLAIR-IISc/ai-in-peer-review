This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars, which the authors claim generalizes previous work on structure learning. However, it can be argued that this approach is actually a special case that makes the problem tractable. While this may limit the generality of the method, it is a reasonable point that contributes to the field, and it would be unfair to criticize the paper for not tackling the more general structure learning problem faced in NLP. The basic algorithm proposed is sensible and demonstrates success when compared to other grammar induction methods.

To further strengthen the experimental evaluation, additional comparisons with other methods would be beneficial. Notably, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears to be applicable to both tasks and would provide a valuable point of comparison. Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars.
