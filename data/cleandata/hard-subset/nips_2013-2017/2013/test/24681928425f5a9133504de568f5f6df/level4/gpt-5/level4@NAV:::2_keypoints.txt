- Proposes a method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- Notes AND-OR grammars can model recursive phenomena including natural language and grammars themselves.  
- Introduces a solid unsupervised, incremental structure-learning approach that adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates likelihood + prior gains.  
- Evaluated on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks, producing results competitive with prior work.  
- Overall positive: provides a tractable way to learn stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- Criticism: Does not address computational tractability or scalability to thousands of data points.  
- Criticism: Lacks experiments on natural language (e.g., Penn Treebank) to demonstrate feasibility and strengthen claims.
