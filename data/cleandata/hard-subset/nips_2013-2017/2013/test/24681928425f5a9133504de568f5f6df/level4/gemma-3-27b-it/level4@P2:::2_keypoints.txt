- Introduces a novel unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs).  
- SAGs provide compact representations for compositional and reconfigurable data across domains like language, images, and events.  
- Proposes an iterative algorithm that incrementally adds And-Or fragments and optimizes a posterior balancing model complexity and data likelihood.  
- Builds on prior structure-learning work for grammars but generalizes it to a broader, data-type-agnostic framework.  
- Unifies learning of compositional (AND) and alternative (OR) structures instead of treating them separately.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness with experiments on event and image grammar learning, achieving competitive or superior performance.  
- Theoretical framework and optimization procedure are well-defined and justified.  
- Empirical results are encouraging and show practical utility.  
- Main weakness: computational complexity and scalability are not thoroughly analyzed or empirically tested on large datasets.  
- Missing evaluation on standard NLP benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank), limiting comparison to established NLP methods like Klein & Manning.  
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance, contingent on addressing scalability concerns and adding discussion/experiments for broader applicability.  
- References to relevant prior work include Klein & Manning (2004) and Stolcke & Omohundro (1994).
