- Proposes a novel method for joint structure and parameter learning of stochastic AND-OR grammars that capture recursive phenomena.  
- Introduces unsupervised structure learning by iteratively adding AND-OR fragments and measuring likelihood and prior gains.  
- Evaluated on event grammars and image grammars, achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Claims a promising, potentially tractable framework for learning stochastic grammars.  
- Scalability concern: the algorithm’s feasibility on large datasets (thousands of data points) is not addressed.  
- Missing natural language experiments: no sentence-level evaluation; recommends testing on benchmarks like the Penn Treebank and comparing to prior work (e.g., Klein & Manning).  
- Overall: favorable initial results, but additional experiments—particularly on natural language—are needed to strengthen the paper.
