This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars, which is a specific class of grammars. Although the authors claim that their approach generalizes previous work on structure learning, it is actually a special case that makes the problem more tractable. This is a reasonable point on its own, and the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field.

The proposed algorithm is sensible and has shown success in inducing grammars when compared to other methods. The experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach, but could be improved by including more comparisons to other relevant methods. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears to be applicable to both tasks and would provide a valuable comparison point. Incorporating this comparison would strengthen the evaluation and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's performance.

Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars. The authors provide a clear and well-structured presentation of their approach, and the experimental evaluation is well-executed. With some additional comparisons to other methods, the paper would be even stronger.
