- Proposes a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Claiming to "generalize" prior work is misleading; the method is actually a special case that enables tractable solutions.  
- The contribution is valid, valuable, and distinct from NLP structure-learning problems.  
- The core algorithm is well-motivated and empirically effective, outperforming competing methods in the presented experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation should include additional baselines—specifically Stolcke & Omohondro (2000)—which apply to both tasks considered.  
- Adding that comparison would better contextualize the proposed approach's advantages and limitations.  
- Overall, a strong contribution: the technical approach is sound and the results are convincing within the paper’s scope.
