- Proposes a novel method for unsupervised structure and parameter learning of stochastic AND-OR grammars by iteratively adding AND-OR fragments and measuring likelihood/prior gains.  
- Provides a clear, unified formalization of stochastic AND-OR grammars that is data-type agnostic.  
- Introduces an efficient learning algorithm using greedy/beam search and sufficient statistics to compute posterior gains.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical results on event-grammar and image-grammar learning from unannotated data.  
- Paper is well-written with a detailed, easy-to-follow description of the algorithm and its components.  
- Lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis and unclear scalability to much larger datasets.  
- Does not include experiments on natural language sentences (e.g., Penn Treebank), a key application area.  
- Could provide more discussion on how the method relates to and extends prior work on stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- The approach is original in its specific method for structure learning and has broad potential applications (NLP, vision, robotics).  
- Experimental evidence supports effectiveness, but broader benchmarks and scalability tests are needed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add complexity analysis and consider natural language experiments.
