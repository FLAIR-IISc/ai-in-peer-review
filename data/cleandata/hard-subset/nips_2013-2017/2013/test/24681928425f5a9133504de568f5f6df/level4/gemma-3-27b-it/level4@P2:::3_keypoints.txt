- Introduces unified AND-OR fragment induction instead of separately learning AND and OR operations.  
- Key contribution: efficient computation of fragment value using sufficient statistics.  
- Method evaluated on event and image parsing tasks.  
- Paper is novel, well-motivated, and appears technically sound (supplemental derivations not fully verified by reviewer).  
- Unclear whether the proposed “surrogate measure” for likelihood gain is an approximation or an exact calculation—needs clarification.  
- Unclear if method generalizes to fragments with arity >2 or is implicitly restricted to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental section lacks sufficient detail on representations, learning process, and outcomes, making replication and understanding difficult.  
- Paper assumes strong familiarity with Zhu’s group literature, reducing accessibility for broader readership.  
- Lacks qualitative examples of learned structures; exclusive focus on quantitative results limits intuitive insight.  
- Reviewer requests more comprehensive experimental details and illustrative examples in supplementary materials for acceptance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, theoretical justification, computational efficiency, promising experimental results, tackles an important problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental clarity, missing qualitative structure demonstrations, and unresolved method details (arity and surrogate measure).
