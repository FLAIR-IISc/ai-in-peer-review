This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. While the authors suggest their approach generalizes previous work on structure learning, it is more accurately a special case. This specialization is precisely what makes the problem tractable and thus represents a valuable contribution in its own right. The paper would be strengthened by framing its contribution as a tractable special case of the structure learning problem in NLP, rather than as a generalization.

The core algorithm is sensible and demonstrates success when compared to the other grammar induction methods tested. However, the experimental evaluation could be improved by including more comprehensive comparisons. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro [14] appears applicable to the presented tasks and would serve as a valuable point of comparison.

Overall, this is a solid paper presenting an effective method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars.
