- Introduces a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments rather than separately optimizing AND and OR components.  
- Efficient computation of fragment value via sufficient statistics; framework is technically sound.  
- Problem is well-motivated and the core idea advances prior work.  
- Ambiguity: unclear whether the "surrogate measure" for likelihood gain is an approximation or exact, affecting theoretical guarantees.  
- Ambiguity: unclear if the method generalizes beyond binary (arity-2) fragments or is effectively restricted to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental descriptions assume strong familiarity with S. C. Zhu’s prior work, making the setup opaque to general readers.  
- Missing details on preprocessing, what structural elements were learned, and how proposed fragments differ from prior grammars.  
- Results rely solely on quantitative metrics (e.g., parsing accuracy) without showing the actual learned grammatical structures.  
- Lack of concrete, interpretable examples (e.g., parse trees, hierarchical object decompositions) reduces impact and novelty perception.  
- Recommend expanding supplemental materials with step-by-step induction examples, visualizations of learned structures, structural comparisons to baselines, and a glossary of domain terms.  
- Overall: technically solid and novel, but needs clearer technical exposition and accessible experimental/visual evidence; acceptance recommended only if authors address these issues in the final version.
