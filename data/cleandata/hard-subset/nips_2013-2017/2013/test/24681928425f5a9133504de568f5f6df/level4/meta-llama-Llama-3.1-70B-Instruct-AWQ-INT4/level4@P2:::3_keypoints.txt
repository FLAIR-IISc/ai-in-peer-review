Paper proposes a novel unified approach to inducing AND-OR fragments for unsupervised grammar learning.  
Efficient computation of a proposed fragment's value via sufficient statistics.  
Evaluated on multiple tasks, including event parsing and visual object parsing.  
Approach is well-motivated and technically sound; reviewer could not verify supplemental derivations due to time constraints.  
Unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is approximate or exact—needs explicit statement.  
Unclear if the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form—needs clarification.  
Experimental section is hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with prior visual-grammar work; methodology, example representation, and learned structures are not clearly described.  
Reviewer requests more supplementary details, concrete illustrations, and examples of interesting discovered structures to improve accessibility.  
Quantitative results are somewhat disappointing; paper should demonstrate it learns more interesting structures than competing methods.  
Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, and potential applicability across tasks.  
Arguments for rejection: inaccessible experimental presentation, limited illustration of learned structures, and unclear extensibility to higher-arity fragments.
