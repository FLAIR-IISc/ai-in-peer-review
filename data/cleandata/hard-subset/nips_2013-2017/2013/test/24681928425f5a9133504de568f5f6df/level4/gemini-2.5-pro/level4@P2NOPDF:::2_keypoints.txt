- Proposes an unsupervised method to jointly learn the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars by iteratively adding grammar fragments guided by data likelihood and a prior.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on two tasks (event and image grammar learning), achieving competitive results with prior work.
- Strength: jointly learning structure and parameters uncovers compositional and recursive patterns in data.
- Strength: incremental fragment addition is a principled, potentially tractable way to explore the grammar search space.
- Major weakness: lacks analysis or empirical evaluation of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.
- Major weakness: no experiments on natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank), so comparison to established grammar induction methods (e.g., Klein & Manning) is missing.
- Overall: promising and elegant approach, but would be strengthened by scalability analysis and NLP benchmark evaluation.
