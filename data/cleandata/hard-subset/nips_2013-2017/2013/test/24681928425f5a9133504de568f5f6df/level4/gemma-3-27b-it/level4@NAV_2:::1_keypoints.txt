- Presents an unsupervised method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars with a unified formalization for multiple data types.  
- Builds on prior structure-learning work (NLP and image grammars) and extends them to jointly learn compositions and reconfigurations.  
- Core idea: iterative induction of And-Or fragments to represent compositions and reconfigurations together.  
- Novelty: introduction of And-Or fragments as a new representation to improve efficiency and robustness.  
- Principled objective: explicitly optimizes posterior probability of the grammar.  
- The likelihood-gain formulation is connected to tensor/matrix coherence measures.  
- Empirical results show competitive or superior performance on event and image grammar tasks.  
- Presentation is generally clear with sufficient algorithmic detail.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparison to some relevant prior methods (e.g., Stolcke and Omohondro).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to event and image grammars, weakening the claim of broad generality (e.g., no NLP experiment).  
- Mathematical discussion of the likelihood gain could be made more accessible and intuitively explained.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant work that, with broader comparisons and expanded experiments, supports acceptance.
