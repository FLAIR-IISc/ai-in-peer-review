- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for learning AND-OR grammar structure by inducing unified AND-OR fragments rather than separate AND/OR searches.
- Computes fragment value efficiently using a set of sufficient statistics.
- Evaluated on parsing tasks for events and visual objects.
- Overall assessment: solid paper addressing an important problem with a novel, well-motivated, and seemingly sound algorithm.
- Could not verify supplemental derivations due to limited review time.
- Unclear whether the “surrogate measure” of likelihood gain is an approximation or an exact quantity.
- Unclear if the method generalizes to fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to Chomsky normal form for tractability.
- Experimental setup was hard to understand: representations, procedures, and what was learned were not clearly explained.
- The paper appears targeted to readers already familiar with prior visual-grammar work (especially S. C. Zhu’s group), making it less accessible to outsiders.
- Strong recommendation to include detailed supplementary material with concrete illustrations, data representations, and step-by-step experimental procedures if accepted.
- Requests explicit examples of interesting structure discovered; quantitative results alone are not sufficiently illuminating.
- Conclusion: interesting and likable paper, but needs clearer experimental presentation and illustrative supplements to be fully interpretable.
