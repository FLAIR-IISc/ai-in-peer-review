This paper introduces a method for jointly learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. These grammars, incorporating both AND and OR rules, offer a powerful representation for recursive phenomena found in areas like natural language and compositional image generation. The authors propose a compelling approach to unsupervised structure learning, iteratively adding AND-OR fragments and evaluating the benefit of each addition based on likelihood gains and prior considerations.

The method is evaluated on two tasks: learning grammars of events and learning grammars of images. In both cases, the results are competitive with existing state-of-the-art approaches. 

Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a promising and potentially tractable method for learning stochastic grammars with AND and OR rules. However, several points warrant further consideration.

My primary concern is the scalability of the learning algorithm. The paper does not discuss its computational complexity or its ability to handle large datasets. Specifically, it remains unclear whether the method can scale effectively to datasets containing thousands of datapoints, a common requirement for many real-world applications.

Furthermore, while the results on event and image grammars are encouraging, the absence of experiments on natural language data is a notable omission. Natural language is arguably the most intuitive and prominent application for AND-OR grammars, and a demonstration of performance in this domain would significantly strengthen the paper's impact. It would be particularly valuable to see results on established natural language benchmarks, such as the Penn Treebank, and a comparison to relevant prior work – for example, the approaches of Klein and Manning. 

The paper effectively demonstrates a method for estimating both the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars and provides strong results on the chosen tasks. Expanding the experimental evaluation to include natural language data would substantially enhance the work and better illustrate the proposed method’s potential.
