- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments instead of separate AND/OR operations.  
- Computes fragment value efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on event parsing and image modeling tasks.  
- Problem is important, approach is novel and well-motivated.  
- Algorithm appears technically sound, though supplemental derivations were not verified by the reviewer.  
- Unclear whether the “surrogate measure” of likelihood gain is an exact computation or an approximation.  
- Unclear whether the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental details are hard to follow (what was done, how examples were represented, and what was learned).  
- Experiments assume familiarity with prior visual-grammar work (Zhu’s group), reducing accessibility.  
- Paper lacks qualitative illustrations of the learned grammar structure—results are purely quantitative.  
- Would be more convincing if it demonstrated learning more interesting structure than competing methods.  
- Recommendation to expand supplementary material with detailed experiment explanations and concrete structure illustrations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: joint structure+parameter learning, efficient fragment value computation, competitive empirical performance, and theoretical soundness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: opaque experiments without prior-field knowledge, missing qualitative results, and needed clarification on likelihood exactness and arity limitations.
