This paper introduces a method for jointly learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, a formalism capable of representing recursive structures found in domains like natural language and compositional grammars due to its inclusion of both AND and OR rules. The authors propose an effective unsupervised structure learning approach that incrementally adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates them based on likelihood and prior gains.

The method is evaluated on two tasks—learning event grammars and image grammars—and demonstrates performance competitive with existing approaches. 

Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a promising and potentially practical approach to learning stochastic grammars with AND/OR rule capabilities. However, the paper would benefit from a discussion of the computational scalability of the learning algorithm. Specifically, it remains unclear how well the method will perform with larger datasets containing thousands of examples. 

Furthermore, given the natural suitability of AND-OR grammars for natural language processing, I would have appreciated experiments on a natural language dataset. Assessing performance on a standard benchmark like the Penn Treebank, and comparing results to previous work in this area (e.g., Klein & Manning), would significantly strengthen the paper's contribution. While the results presented on the two chosen tasks are encouraging, extending the experimental evaluation to include natural language data would provide a more complete picture of the method’s capabilities.
