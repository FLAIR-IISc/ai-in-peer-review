This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors claim this work “generalizes” previous work on structure learning, but it is more accurately described as a special case – a restriction that makes the problem tractable. This is a valid point in itself, and the paper represents a worthwhile contribution; I wouldn’t argue that the problem addressed is more general than the structure learning problems commonly encountered in natural language processing. The core algorithm is sensible and performs well when compared to existing methods for grammar induction.

The experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including more comprehensive comparisons. In particular, the Stolcke and Omohondro method (reference 14) appears applicable to both tasks and would serve as a valuable benchmark. Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a method for learning the structure of a specific class of grammars.
