- Proposes a method for learning structure of stochastic And-Or grammars; algorithm is well-motivated and shows strong performance against selected baselines.
- The paper frames the method as a generalization of prior work but is more accurately a focus on a specific subclass of grammars.
- This specialization is a major strength because it renders an otherwise intractable grammar-induction problem tractable.
- The authors should reframe their contribution to emphasize the tractable special case rather than claiming broad generality.
- Primary weakness is limited experimental evaluation.
- The paper needs comparisons to other established grammar-induction methods, notably Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14), which seems directly applicable.
- Adding that and other relevant baselines would make the empirical claims more robust and better contextualized.
- Overall: solid and sound contribution, but would be more compelling with broader experimental validation.
