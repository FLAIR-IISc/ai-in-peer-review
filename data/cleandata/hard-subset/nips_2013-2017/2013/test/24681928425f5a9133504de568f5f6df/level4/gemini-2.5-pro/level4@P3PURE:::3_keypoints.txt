- Proposes a new method for unsupervised learning of unified AND-OR grammar fragments rather than searching for AND and OR productions separately.  
- Computes candidate fragment values efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on parsing events and visual objects.  
- Well-motivated and novel core idea; algorithm appears technically sound.  
- Reviewer could not verify derivations in the supplementary material.  
- Clarification needed: is the "surrogate measure" an approximation or an exact likelihood gain calculation?  
- Clarification needed: can the method learn fragments with arity >2, or is it limited to a Chomsky-normal-form–like structure?  
- Main concern: experimental setup is hard to understand—example representation and learned structures are unclear.  
- Presentation assumes familiarity with the authors’ prior visual grammar work, making it opaque to outsiders.  
- Quantitative results are satisfactory but not very illuminating relative to the paper’s motivation of learning meaningful structure.  
- Strong recommendation to provide much more detailed experimental descriptions and concrete illustrations in the supplementary material if accepted.  
- Crucial request to include qualitative examples showing the interesting/meaningful structures learned and comparisons to competing approaches.
