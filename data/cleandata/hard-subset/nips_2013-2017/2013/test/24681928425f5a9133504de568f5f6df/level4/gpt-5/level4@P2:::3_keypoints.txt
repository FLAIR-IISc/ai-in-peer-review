- Presents an unsupervised method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars by inducing And-Or fragments.  
- Joint fragment induction models composition and reconfiguration in a single step, unlike prior separate And-/Or-rule searches.  
- Candidate fragments are scored using sufficient-statistics-based efficient computation and a Viterbi likelihood objective with a sparsity prior.  
- Evaluated on event parsing and image grammar learning, showing competitive or superior quantitative results to prior work.  
- Approach is well-motivated and appears technically sound, though supplemental derivations were not fully checked.  
- Clarification needed: state whether the “surrogate measure” for likelihood gain is exact under assumptions or an approximation, and list those assumptions.  
- Clarification needed: whether the search supports fragments with arity >2 or is effectively restricted to binary (Chomsky-normal) productions; discuss computational trade-offs.  
- Experimental section is hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with prior AOG literature and lacks a self-contained description of preprocessing and representations.  
- Paper should more clearly specify how examples are represented, which relations are allowed in And-rules, and what structures were learned.  
- Recommend adding qualitative illustrations of learned fragments and parse trees to demonstrate interpretable structure.  
- Originality: joint induction of And-Or fragments with efficient sufficient-statistics scoring is a notable contribution over separate induction methods.  
- Potential significance: could enable practical induction of compact, reusable grammars across modalities if broadly applicable.  
- Impact would be clearer with qualitative evidence showing differences from structures learned by competitors.  
- Suggested additions: clarify likelihood gain exactness/assumptions and sensitivity if violated.  
- Suggested additions: specify supported arities, discuss binarization, and report effects on speed and quality.  
- Suggested additions: provide a self-contained experimental protocol (terminals, relations, contexts, smoothing for perplexity).  
- Suggested additions: report search details (candidate generation, beam size, restarts), stopping criteria, and runtime.  
- Suggested additions: include qualitative examples and side-by-side comparisons with baselines, plus ablations (And-only, Or-only, separate induction).  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important unsupervised grammar learning problem, introduces a novel unified induction with efficient scoring, and shows strong quantitative results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experiments are hard to reproduce/interpret without prior familiarity, lack qualitative analysis, and key technical points are unclear.  
- Overall: solid and promising work but needs a more self-contained supplement and clearer experimental/technical exposition for broader accessibility.
