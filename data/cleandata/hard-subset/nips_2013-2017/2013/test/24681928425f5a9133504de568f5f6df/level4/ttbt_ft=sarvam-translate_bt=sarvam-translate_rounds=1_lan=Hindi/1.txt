This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic end-or grammars. The paper suggests that this structure " generalizes" previous work on learning, but it is actually a special case that simplifies the problem. This is a valid point in itself, and the paper makes a good contribution, so I will not try to argue that the problem is more general than the problem of learning the structure encountered in NLP. The basic algorithm is more insightful and successful than other methods for motivating grammars.

Experimental evaluation can be improved by comparing it to other methods. This is a solid paper that presents a method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars.
