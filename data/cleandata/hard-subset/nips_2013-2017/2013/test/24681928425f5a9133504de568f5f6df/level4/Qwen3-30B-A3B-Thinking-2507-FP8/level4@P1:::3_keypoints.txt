- Introduces an unsupervised method that jointly learns structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars by inserting unified And-Or fragments.  
- Core contribution: optimize grammar posterior via iterative insertion of unified fragments that model composition and reconfiguration together.  
- Derives efficient sufficient statistics to compute posterior gain of candidate fragments, enabling scalable learning.  
- Empirically validated on event and image grammars, showing competitive or superior quantitative performance (F-measure, perplexity).  
- Strength: fills a gap by unifying structure and parameter induction within one formal, data-agnostic framework applicable across domains.  
- Strength: posterior optimization avoids heuristics and the sufficient-statistics derivation is a notable technical advance.  
- Strength: experiments cover two domains (events, images) with synthetic and real data and include an informative ablation study.  
- Weakness: unclear whether the surrogate measure for likelihood gain (via n-gram tensor/context matrix factorization) is exact or an approximation.  
- Weakness: unclear scalability to fragments with arity >2 and whether higher-arity compositions are supported efficiently.  
- Weakness: experimental presentation lacks concrete learned-grammar examples (event fragments, relation patterns) and precise definitions of image relation types.  
- Suggestion: include visual examples of learned grammars and concrete relation instances in supplementary material.  
- Suggestion: explicitly clarify mathematical exactness of the likelihood-gain factorization.  
- Suggestion: discuss feasibility and limitations for higher-arity fragment learning.  
- Overall: strong, novel technical contribution with solid empirical results; recommend accept pending minor revisions to improve experimental clarity.
