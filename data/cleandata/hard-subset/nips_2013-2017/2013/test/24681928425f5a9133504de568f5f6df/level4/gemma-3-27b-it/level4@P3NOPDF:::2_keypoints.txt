- Introduces a method for joint structure and parameter learning of stochastic AND-OR grammars for hierarchical/recursive domains.  
- Proposes a novel unsupervised structure-learning procedure that iteratively adds AND-OR fragments judged by likelihood gains and priors.  
- Evaluated on event and image grammar tasks, showing results competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Claims a promising, potentially tractable approach that explicitly models conjunctive and disjunctive relationships.  
- Major concern: scalability not addressed—no computational complexity analysis or empirical testing on large datasets.  
- Important omission: no experiments on natural language benchmarks, despite NLP being a prime application area.  
- Recommends evaluation on standard datasets (e.g., Penn Treebank) and comparison to related work (e.g., Klein & Manning).  
- Overall: solid empirical results for chosen tasks, but broader evaluation and scalability analysis are needed to strengthen the contribution.
