This paper introduces a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. While the authors position this work as a generalization of prior structure learning approaches, it is more accurately described as a special case – a restriction that, crucially, renders the problem tractable. This is a valid and worthwhile observation, and the paper represents a solid contribution; it would be sufficient to frame the work as addressing a specific instance of the broader structure learning problem common in natural language processing. The core algorithm is well-reasoned and demonstrates successful performance when benchmarked against existing grammar induction methods. 

The experimental evaluation would benefit from additional comparisons, specifically including the method proposed by Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14). Given its applicability to both tasks, this would serve as a valuable baseline. Overall, this is a sound paper that presents a method for learning the structure of a defined class of grammars.
