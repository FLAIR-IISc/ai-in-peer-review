

This paper introduces a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is data-type agnostic and proposes an unsupervised learning approach for both grammar structure and parameters. The authors develop an iterative method that starts from a trivial grammar and progressively incorporates And-Or fragments to optimize the posterior probability, thereby unifying the learning of compositions and reconfigurations in a single framework. The method is evaluated on event grammars and image grammars, achieving competitive or superior performance compared to prior approaches.

The paper's strengths include a clear formalization that generalizes across different data types (text, images, events), a principled approach that explicitly optimizes the posterior probability of the grammar rather than relying on heuristics, and a unified treatment of composition and reconfiguration that enhances both efficiency and robustness. The experimental results demonstrate practical effectiveness across two distinct domains, with careful evaluation metrics that support the claims.

The main weaknesses of the paper are related to the lack of discussion regarding computational scalability (the paper does not address whether the approach scales to thousands of data points or large datasets) and the absence of experiments on natural language data, which represents the most prominent application domain for stochastic grammars. While the paper demonstrates success on event and image grammars, it would significantly strengthen its contribution to the field if it included results on standard natural language datasets such as Penn Treebank, where previous grammar learning work (e.g., Klein and Manning) has primarily focused. The authors would benefit from addressing these two limitations to better establish the broader applicability and practical utility of their method.
