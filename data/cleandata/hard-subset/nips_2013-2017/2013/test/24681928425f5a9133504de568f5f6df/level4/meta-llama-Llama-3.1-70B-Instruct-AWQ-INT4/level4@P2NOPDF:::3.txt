This paper proposes a novel approach to unsupervised learning of the structure of AND-OR grammars, departing from previous methods by inducing unified AND-OR fragments rather than searching for AND and OR operations separately. The value of a proposed new AND-OR fragment can be computed efficiently using a set of sufficient statistics. The approach is evaluated on several tasks, including parsing events and visual objects.

The paper tackles an important problem and presents a well-motivated approach. The algorithm appears technically sound, although I was unable to verify the supplemental derivations due to time constraints. However, there are a few points that require clarification. Firstly, it is unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of the likelihood gain is an approximate or exact notion. Secondly, it is uncertain whether the approach can be extended to learning fragments with an arity greater than 2, or if it is limited to learning grammars in "Chomsky normal form".

My primary concerns lie with the experimental section, which I found challenging to understand. The details of the experiments, including the representation of examples and the learned structures, were not clearly explained. The paper appears to be geared towards readers familiar with recent visual-grammar papers from S. C. Zhu's group, which may limit its accessibility to a broader audience. While the NIPS format has its limitations, the inclusion of supplementary materials can help address these concerns. If the paper is accepted, I strongly recommend that the authors provide additional details and concrete illustrations of their experiments in the supplement.

I would have liked to see examples of interesting structures discovered in the learned grammars, which would have strengthened the paper's narrative on the importance of grammars and structure learning. Although the quantitative results are satisfactory, they are not particularly exciting or illuminating. A more compelling presentation would have demonstrated the learning of interesting structures and compared them to those obtained by competing approaches. Overall, this is an interesting paper on unsupervised learning of AND-OR grammars, but its impact is diminished by the lack of clarity in the experimental section and the absence of illustrative examples.
