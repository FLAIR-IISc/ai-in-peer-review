This paper introduces a method for jointly learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. These grammars, incorporating both AND and OR rules, are well-suited for representing recursive structures found in domains like natural language and formal grammars. The authors propose a compelling approach to unsupervised structure learning, iteratively adding AND-OR fragments and evaluating them based on likelihood gains and prior contributions.

The method is demonstrated through experiments on event grammar learning and image grammar learning, achieving results competitive with existing approaches in both cases. 

Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a potentially tractable approach to learning stochastic grammars with AND and OR rules. However, the paper lacks a discussion of the algorithm's scalability. Specifically, it is unclear how well the learning process will perform as the number of datapoints increases – will it scale to datasets containing thousands of examples? 

Furthermore, given the natural applicability of AND-OR grammars to natural language, I would have appreciated experiments on a natural language task. It remains an open question whether the presented methods could successfully learn from datasets like the Penn Treebank, a common benchmark for this type of work (e.g., as used by Klein and Manning). While the paper effectively demonstrates structure and parameter estimation for stochastic AND-OR grammars with promising results on two tasks, additional experiments, particularly those involving natural language data, would significantly strengthen the contribution.
