This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The paper suggests that this "generalizes" previous work on structure learning, but it's actually a special case, which makes the problem tractable. This is a reasonable point on its own, and the paper makes a nice contribution, so I wouldn't try to argue that the problem is more general than the structure learning problem faced in NLP. The basic algorithm is sensible and successful when compared against other methods for inducing grammars.

The authors present a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of the data being modeled. This formalization allows for a unified approach to learning the structure and parameters of such grammars. The proposed approach is based on an iterative bottom-up process that starts from a trivial initial grammar and gradually adds new intermediate nonterminal nodes to generalize the grammar and increase its posterior probability.

The experimental evaluation shows that the proposed approach is competitive with previous approaches on the task of learning event grammars and outperforms them on the task of learning image grammars. The results are promising, but the experimental evaluation could be improved by adding more comparisons to other methods. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) seems like it could be applied to both tasks and would be a valuable comparison point.

* The paper presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of the data being modeled.

Weaknesses:
