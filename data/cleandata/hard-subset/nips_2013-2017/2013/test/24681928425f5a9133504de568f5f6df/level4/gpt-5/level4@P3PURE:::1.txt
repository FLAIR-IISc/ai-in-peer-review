This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. While the paper suggests that this approach “generalizes” previous work on structure learning, it is in fact a special case that makes the problem more tractable. This is a reasonable and useful framing on its own, and the contribution is worthwhile; however, the paper should avoid claiming greater generality than the structure learning problems typically addressed in NLP.

The basic algorithm is sensible and performs well compared with other grammar induction methods. The experimental evaluation, however, would benefit from additional baselines. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears applicable to both of the evaluated tasks and would provide a valuable point of comparison.

Overall, this is a solid paper that presents an effective method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars.
