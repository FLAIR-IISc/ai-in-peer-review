- Introduces an unsupervised, iterative bottom-up method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or Grammars by progressively adding "And-Or fragments."  
- Proposes a unified approach that jointly induces compositional (And) and alternative (Or) structures to improve robustness and search efficiency.  
- Frames learning in a Bayesian objective maximizing grammar posterior using a Viterbi likelihood plus a sparsity prior.  
- Derives an efficient posterior-gain calculation based on sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors and context matrices) to make greedy search practical.  
- Presents a data-agnostic, general formalization of AOGs applicable across modalities rather than tied to a specific domain.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on two domains (video activity event grammars and image object grammars), outperforming or matching prior specialized methods.  
- Lacks experiments on natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank), limiting evaluation against the core grammar induction literature.  
- Omits a formal scalability/runtime complexity analysis and detailed discussion of how runtime scales with sample size, sequence length, vocabulary size, and grammar complexity.  
- Does not sufficiently discuss implications of using the Viterbi likelihood approximation (e.g., potential unambiguity bias) versus full marginal likelihood.  
- Overall: novel, principled, and empirically strong work with limited experimental scope for broader grammar-induction relevance.
