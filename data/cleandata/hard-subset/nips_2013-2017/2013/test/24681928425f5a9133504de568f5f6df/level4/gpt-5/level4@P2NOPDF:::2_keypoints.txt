- Proposes a method for jointly learning structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- AND-OR grammars can represent recursive phenomena (e.g., natural language) using both AND and OR production rules.  
- Presents an unsupervised, incremental structure-learning algorithm that adds AND-OR fragments based on likelihood and prior gains.  
- Evaluated on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks, achieving results competitive with prior work.  
- Paper is clear, approach is appealing, and empirical results are encouraging.  
- Major concern: no analysis of computational complexity or reporting of runtime/memory; unclear scalability to large datasets.  
- Major concern: lacks natural language experiments on standard corpora (e.g., Penn Treebank) or discussion of practical barriers to such evaluation.  
- Overall: well-motivated contribution that would be stronger with scalability analysis and thorough natural-language evaluation.
