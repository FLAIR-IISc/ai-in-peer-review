This paper proposes a method for learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, which can represent various recursive phenomena, including natural language and image grammars. The authors introduce a novel approach to unsupervised structure learning by iteratively adding new AND-OR fragments and evaluating their model's likelihood and prior gains.

The experimental results on learning event grammars and image grammars are competitive with existing state-of-the-art methods. Overall, the paper presents a tractable approach to learning stochastic grammars using AND and OR rules, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

However, there are a few aspects that warrant further consideration. Firstly, the authors do not provide an analysis of the learning algorithm's scalability. It is unclear whether the method can efficiently handle large datasets with thousands of data points. Evaluating the algorithm's tractability would strengthen the paper's claims and provide a clearer understanding of its limitations.

Furthermore, the absence of experiments on natural language sentences is notable, given that natural language is a primary application domain for stochastic AND-OR grammars. It would be beneficial to investigate the method's performance on standard natural language datasets, such as the Penn Treebank dataset, which has been used in previous work (e.g., Klein and Manning). Additional experiments, particularly on natural language data, would enhance the paper's impact and demonstrate the method's broader applicability.

While the paper presents promising results on two tasks, a more comprehensive evaluation would further establish the method's effectiveness and potential for real-world applications.
