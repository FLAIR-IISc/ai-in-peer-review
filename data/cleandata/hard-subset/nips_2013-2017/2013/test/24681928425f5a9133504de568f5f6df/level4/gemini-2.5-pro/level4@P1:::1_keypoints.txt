- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn stochastic And-Or grammars by starting from trivial per-sample grammars and iteratively introducing And-Or fragments bottom-up.  
- Search is guided by maximizing the posterior probability, balancing data likelihood with a complexity prior favoring smaller grammars.  
- Framework is data-agnostic and demonstrated on two domains: event grammars from video and image grammars from visual data.  
- Key contribution: introduces "And-Or fragments" that unify discovery of compositional (And) and alternative (Or) structures in grammar induction.  
- Algorithm is well-motivated by a Bayesian objective; posterior gain is derived and can be computed efficiently from sufficient statistics.  
- Empirical results are strong: comparable or superior to prior specialized methods, with consistent outperformance on image grammar tasks.  
- Positioning could be improved: claims of generality should be tempered in favor of framing the method as a powerful specialization that makes structure learning tractable.  
- Experimental evaluation should include more comparisons to relevant baselines from grammar induction literature.  
- Specifically recommend adding a comparison to Bayesian model merging (Stolcke & Omohundro) for the discrete sequence/event experiments.  
- Overall: solid, original, technically sound paper with convincing results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
