- Proposes joint learning of structure and parameters for stochastic AND-OR grammars via iterative fragment induction scored by likelihood gain minus a complexity prior.

- Strength: Clear, appealing framework for unsupervised induction that captures compositional (AND) and alternative (OR) structures.

- Strength: Intuitive fragment-induction strategy guided by a principled likelihood-plus-prior objective.

- Strength: Empirical evidence on both event and image domains showing competitive, cross-domain results.

- Strength: Addresses the challenging joint problem of structure and parameter learning in rich grammar formalisms.

- Weakness: Scalability and tractability are not quantified—no complexity analysis, runtimes, or memory profiles are reported.

- Weakness: No experiments on natural language corpora; missed opportunity to compare with established grammar induction baselines (e.g., PCFG induction).

- Weakness: Search procedure details are unclear—fragment generation, pruning, heuristics, and convergence criteria are not specified.

- Weakness: Sensitivity to initialization, hyperparameters, and prior choice is not analyzed; no ablations exploring these factors.

- Weakness: Evaluation lacks diagnostic analyses such as learning curves, error breakdowns, and qualitative examples of learned structures.

- Clarification requested: Provide theoretical or empirical complexity analysis per iteration and how candidate counts scale with data and grammar depth.

- Clarification requested: Report training/inference times, memory usage, and scalability experiments across dataset sizes.

- Clarification requested: Detail the fragment proposal mechanism and pruning (e.g., beam search, MDL penalties, caching) used to keep search tractable.

- Clarification requested: Discuss feasibility on natural language, including obstacles like vocabulary size, nonterminal explosion, sparsity, and potential fixes.

- Clarification requested: Explain how the prior is chosen (data-driven or tuned) and include sensitivity analysis to prior strength.

- Suggestion: Add at least one NLP experiment (e.g., subset of Penn Treebank or synthetic language) to demonstrate applicability to language.

- Suggestion: Include scalability studies with wall-clock and memory profiling, or show partial strategies (minibatching, parallelism, stochastic search).

- Suggestion: Provide ablation studies on fragment induction (depth/width limits, prior strength, fixed-structure baselines) to isolate gains.

- Suggestion: Offer qualitative visualizations or case studies of learned fragments to demonstrate interpretability and compositionality.

- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility by detailing hyperparameters, initialization, stopping criteria, and other experimental settings.

- Overall: Strong, timely contribution with promising cross-domain results and a well-motivated approach, but needs thorough scalability analysis and at least targeted NLP validation to solidify impact.
