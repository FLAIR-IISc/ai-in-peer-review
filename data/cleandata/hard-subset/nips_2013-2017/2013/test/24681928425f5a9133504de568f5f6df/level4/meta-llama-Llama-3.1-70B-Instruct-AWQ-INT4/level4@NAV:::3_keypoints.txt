- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments rather than separately searching for AND and OR operations.  
- Introduces an efficient way to compute the value of a proposed fragment using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Demonstrates the approach on parsing events and visual objects.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and technically sound.  
- Supplemental derivations were hard to verify; the supplementary material needs clearer presentation.  
- The nature of the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain (approximate vs. exact) is not explained clearly.  
- Unclear whether the method can learn fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to Chomsky normal form grammars.  
- Experimental section lacks methodological detail, making it hard to understand how experiments were run.  
- Missing detail on data representation and the specifics of learned outcomes in experiments.  
- Assumes familiarity with S. C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work; would benefit from more background for broader readers.  
- Needs concrete examples of interesting learned grammar structures and comparisons to competing methods to increase impact.
