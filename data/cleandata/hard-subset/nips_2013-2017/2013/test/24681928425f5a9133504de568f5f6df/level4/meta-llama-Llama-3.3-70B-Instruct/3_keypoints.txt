- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments instead of separately identifying AND and OR operations.  
- New fragments’ values can be computed using sufficient statistics.  
- Method is demonstrated on parsing events and visual objects.  
- Paper addresses an important problem with a well-motivated, technically sound approach.  
- Unclear whether the proposed "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Scalability questions: can the method learn fragments with arity > 2 or is it limited to Chomsky normal form grammars?  
- Experimental methodology, example representation, and the nature of learned outcomes are insufficiently clear.  
- Presentation appears tailored to readers familiar with S. C. Zhu’s group, reducing accessibility for broader audiences.  
- Recommends supplementary materials with detailed explanations and concrete experiment illustrations.  
- Lacks qualitative examples of interesting structures discovered in learned grammars, which would make results more compelling.  
- Quantitative results are satisfactory but lack the insight and excitement that qualitative comparisons with competing approaches would provide.
