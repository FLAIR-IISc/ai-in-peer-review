- Paper proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments (instead of separate AND/OR searches).  
- Fragment value is claimed to be efficiently computable using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on event parsing and visual object tasks.  
- Strengths: important problem, novel and well-motivated approach, algorithm appears technically sound.  
- Reviewer could not verify supplemental derivations due to limited time.  
- Clarification needed: is the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain exact or an approximation?  
- Clarification needed: does the method handle fragments with arity >2 or is it restricted to Chomsky normal form?  
- Major concern: experimental methodology is hard to follow (example representation and specific learned structures unclear).  
- Paper seems targeted to experts in S. C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work and lacks accessible details for outsiders.  
- Supplementary materials should include more experimental details and concrete illustrations.  
- Reviewer wants examples of interesting structures discovered; current quantitative-only results lack insight and excitement.  
- Recommendation: experimental section requires significant improvement; if accepted, provide supplements detailing procedures and key structural discoveries.
