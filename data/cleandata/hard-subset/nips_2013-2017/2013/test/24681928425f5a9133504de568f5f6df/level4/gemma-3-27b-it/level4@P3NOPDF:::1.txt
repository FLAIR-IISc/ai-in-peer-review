This paper introduces a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAGs). The authors position their work as a generalization of previous structure learning approaches; however, it is more accurately described as a tractable special case. While this reduction in generality is not a weakness – in fact, it’s a reasonable and well-motivated simplification that enables a practical algorithm – the framing as a generalization could be misleading. The paper nonetheless makes a valuable contribution by successfully adapting structure learning techniques to this specific, and useful, grammar class, and I do not believe the authors need to claim broader generality than is warranted. 

The proposed algorithm is sensible and appears to perform well. The empirical evaluation demonstrates its effectiveness when compared to existing methods for inducing grammars. The results presented are encouraging and support the claim that the approach is a viable solution for SAG structure learning.

However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened with additional comparative analyses. Specifically, the method proposed by Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) seems particularly relevant, as it is applicable to the tasks addressed in this paper and would provide a valuable benchmark. Including a comparison against this established approach would further contextualize the performance gains achieved by the proposed method and enhance the impact of the results. Furthermore, a discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed method, especially in relation to the size of the grammar and the training data, would be beneficial. Providing such analysis would allow readers to better understand the scalability of the approach.

Overall, this is a solid and well-presented paper that proposes a method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars. The approach is well-motivated, the algorithm is sensible, and the experimental results are promising. Addressing the suggestions regarding the inclusion of the Stolcke and Omohondro method and a discussion of computational complexity would further strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance.
