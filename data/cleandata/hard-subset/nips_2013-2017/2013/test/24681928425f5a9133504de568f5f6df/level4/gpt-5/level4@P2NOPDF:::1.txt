This paper proposes a method for learning the structure of stochastic And–Or grammars. While the authors present it as a generalization of prior structure-learning work, it is more accurately a specialized instance that ensures tractability. This specialization is reasonable and yields a meaningful contribution; however, the paper should avoid suggesting that the problem is more general than the structure-learning challenges typically addressed in NLP.

The core algorithm is well-motivated, sensible, and shows competitive performance compared with existing grammar induction approaches. That said, the empirical evaluation could be strengthened with broader comparisons. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohundro (reference 14) appears applicable to both tasks considered and would provide a valuable baseline.

Overall, this is a solid paper that advances structure learning for a specific class of grammars.
