This paper proposes a method for jointly learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. These grammars contain AND and OR rules and can represent several recursive phenomena, including natural language and even grammars themselves. The authors introduce a clear approach to unsupervised structure learning by iteratively adding new AND-OR fragments and evaluating gains in likelihood and prior.

The paper reports experiments on two tasks—learning event grammars and image grammars—achieving results competitive with prior work.

Overall, I found the approach appealing, as it offers a seemingly tractable way to learn stochastic grammars expressible with AND and OR rules. My main concerns are:

1) The paper does not discuss the tractability of the learning algorithm. Will it scale to thousands of datapoints?

2) I would have liked to see experiments on natural language sentences, since natural language is the most obvious application for such grammars. Is it feasible to learn with the proposed method on datasets like the Penn Treebank, which prior work (e.g., Klein and Manning) has targeted? While the paper provides a method for estimating both structure and parameters and shows solid results on two domains, additional experiments—especially on natural language data—would strengthen the contribution.
