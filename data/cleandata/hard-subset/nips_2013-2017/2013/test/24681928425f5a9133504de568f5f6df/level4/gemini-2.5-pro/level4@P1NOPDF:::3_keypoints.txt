- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for AND-OR grammar structure learning that induces unified AND-OR fragments instead of separate AND/OR searches.  
- Key technical contribution: an efficient method to compute fragment value using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on event and visual object parsing tasks.  
- Paper is strong, novel, well-motivated, and appears technically sound.  
- Reviewer could not verify derivations in the supplementary material.  
- Clarification requested: is the "surrogate measure" for likelihood gain an approximation or an exact calculation?  
- Clarification requested: can the method learn fragments with arity >2 or is it limited to grammars in Chomsky normal form?  
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation is opaque—methodology, data representation, and learned grammars are not clearly described.  
- Evaluation assumes familiarity with prior work (notably S. C. Zhu), limiting accessibility to a broader audience.  
- Recommendation: include a more detailed supplementary document with experimental details if accepted.  
- Recommendation: add qualitative results and examples of learned grammatical rules and comparisons to show interpretability and meaningful structures.
