This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors argue that their approach generalizes previous work on structure learning, but clarify that it addresses a specific, tractable case. This is a reasonable approach, and the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed algorithm is sensible and demonstrates successful performance when compared to existing methods for grammar induction.

The experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including more comparisons to related work. In particular, the approach by Stolcke and Omohondro [14] appears applicable to both tasks presented and would serve as a useful benchmark. While the paper currently demonstrates competitive results, incorporating this comparison would further solidify its contribution. 

Overall, this is a solid paper presenting a method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars. The approach is well-motivated, clearly explained, and empirically validated. The authors effectively address a challenging problem in unsupervised learning and provide a valuable contribution to the literature.
