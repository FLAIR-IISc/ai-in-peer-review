This work proposes a technique for inducing the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. Although the authors describe it as a “generalization” of prior structure-learning efforts, it effectively focuses on a restricted case, which is what makes the problem tractable. That positioning is reasonable and the contribution is meaningful; I would not assert broader generality than the structure-learning problems typically addressed in NLP. The core algorithm is sensible and shows competitive performance relative to other grammar induction approaches.

The empirical evaluation would be stronger with additional baselines. In particular, the Bayesian model merging method of Stolcke and Omohundro (reference 14) seems applicable to both tasks and would provide a valuable point of comparison. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting an effective method for structure learning within a specific class of grammars.
