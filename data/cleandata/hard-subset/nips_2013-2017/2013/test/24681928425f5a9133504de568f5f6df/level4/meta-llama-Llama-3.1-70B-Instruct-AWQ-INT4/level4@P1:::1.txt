This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars, which is a specific class of grammars that compactly represent both compositionality and reconfigurability. The authors propose an unsupervised approach to learning both the structure and parameters of such grammars, starting from a trivial initial grammar and iteratively inducing compositions and reconfigurations in a unified manner. The approach is evaluated on learning event grammars and image grammars, achieving comparable or better performance than previous approaches.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled. This allows the authors to propose a general approach to learning the structure and parameters of such grammars, which can be applied to different types of data. The authors also introduce the concept of And-Or fragments, which are used to search for new grammar fragments that can be added to the grammar to increase its posterior probability.

The experimental evaluation of the paper is solid, with comparisons to previous approaches on two different tasks: learning event grammars and image grammars. However, the evaluation could be improved by adding more comparisons to other methods. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) seems like it could be applied to both tasks and would be a valuable comparison point.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed approach and its components. The authors provide a detailed description of the algorithm and its implementation, as well as an analysis of the time complexity of the algorithm.

Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars. The approach is well-motivated and well-evaluated, and the paper provides a valuable contribution to the field of grammar learning.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled.
* The authors propose a general approach to learning the structure and parameters of such grammars, which can be applied to different types of data.
* The experimental evaluation is solid, with comparisons to previous approaches on two different tasks.

Weaknesses:

* The experimental evaluation could be improved by adding more comparisons to other methods.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the time complexity of the algorithm.
