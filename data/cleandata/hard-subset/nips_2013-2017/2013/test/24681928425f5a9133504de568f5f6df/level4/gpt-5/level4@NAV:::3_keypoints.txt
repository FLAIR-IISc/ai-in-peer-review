- Introduces a new unsupervised method for learning AND-OR grammar structure that induces unified AND-OR fragments rather than separate AND/OR searches.
- Candidate fragment values can be computed efficiently using sufficient statistics.
- Evaluated on tasks including event parsing and visual object parsing.
- Overall assessment: strong paper—important problem, novel and well-motivated approach, and technically sound algorithm.
- Supplementary derivations were not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Unclear whether the “surrogate measure” of likelihood gain is an exact quantity or an approximation.
- Unclear if the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is effectively tied to Chomsky normal form.
- Experimental section is hard to follow: unclear representations, experimental procedure, and what was actually learned.
- Presentation seems targeted to readers already familiar with prior visual-grammar work (especially Zhu’s group), limiting accessibility to outsiders.
- Reviewer requests more concrete illustrations and detailed experimental descriptions in supplementary material if accepted.
- Would like examples of interesting structures discovered; current focus on quantitative results is underwhelming without qualitative structures.
- Recommendation: include more detailed explanations, examples, and comparisons to show that the method discovers more compelling structure than competing approaches.
