- Proposes learning structure of stochastic And-Or grammars; essentially a specialized instance of grammar induction rather than a broad generalization.  
- Specialization to stochastic And-Or grammars makes the problem tractable and is a meaningful contribution within that targeted setting.  
- Algorithm is well-motivated, technically sound, and described with enough detail to follow.  
- Empirically competitive against existing grammar induction methods in the reported experiments.  
- Strength: clear and principled formulation that yields an efficient, useful search space for the targeted domains.  
- Strength: sensible, coherent algorithmic design.  
- Strength: demonstrated empirical effectiveness within the evaluated tasks.  
- Weakness: overstates generality; should frame as a targeted, tractable solution rather than a broad generalization.  
- Weakness: limited experimental comparisons; missing key baselines such as Stolcke & Omohundro’s Bayesian model merging.  
- Weakness: lacks ablation studies to identify which components drive performance gains.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational complexity, runtime, and scalability.  
- Weakness: no sensitivity/robustness analysis for hyperparameters, initialization, or varying data conditions.  
- Suggestion: reframe claims to emphasize tractable specialization to stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Suggestion: add comparisons to Bayesian model merging and other standard grammar-induction baselines, reporting accuracy and computational cost.  
- Suggestion: include ablations, error analyses, and runtime/scalability and sensitivity experiments.  
- Overall: solid, credible paper with promising results; would be more compelling with broader empirical evaluation and tempered positioning.
