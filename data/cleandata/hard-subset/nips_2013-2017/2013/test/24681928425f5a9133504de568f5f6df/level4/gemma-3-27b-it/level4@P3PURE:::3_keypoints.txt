- Proposes a novel method for unsupervised learning of unified AND-OR grammar fragments, with fragment value computed efficiently via sufficient statistics and evaluated on event and visual object parsing.  
- Paper addresses an important problem with a well-motivated, seemingly technically sound algorithm.  
- Reviewer could not fully verify supplemental derivations.  
- Unclear whether the “surrogate measure” of likelihood gain is an approximation or an exact calculation.  
- Unclear whether the method generalizes to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to grammars in Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental section lacks clarity on what was done, how examples were represented, and what was ultimately learned.  
- Presentation appears targeted to readers already familiar with Zhu’s visual-grammar work, making it less accessible to outsiders.  
- Reviewer requests expanded supplementary material with detailed experimental procedures and concrete illustrations if accepted.  
- Reviewer wants examples of interesting structural patterns discovered by the learned grammars, noting current results are largely quantitative and not illuminating about structure.  
- Overall: promising and interesting work, but experiments and presentation need clearer exposition and supplementary detail.
