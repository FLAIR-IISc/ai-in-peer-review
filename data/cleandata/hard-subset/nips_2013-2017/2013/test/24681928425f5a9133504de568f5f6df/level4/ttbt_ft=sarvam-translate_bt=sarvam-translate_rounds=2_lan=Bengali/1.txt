This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic and/or grammar. This paper is considered a "generalization" of previous work on learning the structure, but it is actually a specific case that simplifies the problem. It is a reasonable topic in itself, and the paper makes a nice contribution, so I will not try to argue that this problem is more general than the problem of learning the structure encountered in NLP. The basic algorithm is understandable and successful compared to other methods of introducing grammar.

Experimental evaluation can be improved by adding more comparisons with other methods. In particular, the methods of Stolke and Omohoff (reference 14) can be applied to both tasks, and this would be a valuable comparison. It is a reliable paper presenting a method for learning the structure of a specific class of grammar.
