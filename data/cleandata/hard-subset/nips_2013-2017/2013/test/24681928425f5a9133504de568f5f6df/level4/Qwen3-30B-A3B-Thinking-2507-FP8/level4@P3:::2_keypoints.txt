- Presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is domain-agnostic (images, events, etc.).  
- Proposes an unsupervised algorithm that learns both grammar structure (rules/fragments) and parameters.  
- Introduces "And-Or fragments" to jointly handle compositions (And-rules) and reconfigurations (Or-rules).  
- Learning procedure starts from a trivial grammar and iteratively adds fragments to optimize posterior probability.  
- Uses sufficient statistics for efficient likelihood and prior gain computation with solid theoretical derivations.  
- Prototype implementation is efficient on the reported scale (minutes on 5000 samples) and includes practical implementation details.  
- Empirical evaluation shows competitive or superior performance on event grammars (activity recognition) and image grammars (scene modeling).  
- Well-organized, clearly written, with helpful examples (Table 1) and effective illustrations (Figures 1–3).  
- Significant originality in unifying structure and parameter learning compared to prior work that separates or hand-specifies structure.  
- Insufficient analysis of computational complexity and scalability to larger datasets or more complex grammars.  
- Lacks experiments on natural language data (e.g., Penn Treebank), limiting demonstration of broader applicability.  
- Limited and insufficiently explicit comparison/discussion of connections to sum-product networks despite conceptual similarities.  
- Some technical algorithmic details (context matrix, n-gram tensor) need clearer explanation or examples.  
- Overall judgment: technically strong and valuable contribution with recommendation for acceptance, tempered by noted omissions.
