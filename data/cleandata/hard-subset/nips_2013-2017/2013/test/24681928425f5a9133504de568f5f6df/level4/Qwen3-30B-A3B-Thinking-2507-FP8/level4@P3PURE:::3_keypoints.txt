- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments instead of separately searching AND/OR operations, with fragment value computed via sufficient statistics and evaluated on event parsing and visual object tasks.

- Well-motivated and technically sound algorithm; derivations appear rigorous though supplemental derivations were not independently verified.

- Clarification needed on whether the "surrogate measure" for likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.

- Clarification needed on whether the method supports fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to Chomsky normal form grammars.

- Experimental section is hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with prior visual-grammar work and assumes that background.

- Key experimental details are missing, including input representations, experimental procedures, and the actual learned structures.

- Results are presented only quantitatively without examples or visualizations of discovered grammars or explicit comparisons of structural quality across approaches.

- Recommendation to add extensive supplementary material (protocols, input representations, visualizations) to improve accessibility and justify the claimed structural learning contribution; overall promising but needs greater experimental transparency.
