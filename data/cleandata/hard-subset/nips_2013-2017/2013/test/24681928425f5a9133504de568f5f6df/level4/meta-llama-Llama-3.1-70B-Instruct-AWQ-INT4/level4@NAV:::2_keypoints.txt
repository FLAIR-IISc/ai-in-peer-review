- Proposes a novel approach to jointly learning structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars using AND and OR rules.  
- Introduces an unsupervised structure-learning algorithm that iteratively adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates likelihood and prior gains.  
- Evaluated on event grammar learning and image grammar learning tasks, showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall presents a promising framework for modeling recursive phenomena in language and images.  
- Major concern 1: No assessment of the algorithm’s scalability—unclear if it handles large datasets with thousands of examples efficiently.  
- Major concern 2: Missing experiments on natural language data; evaluation on datasets like the Penn Treebank (used in prior work) is recommended.  
- Conclusion: Effectiveness demonstrated on two tasks, but further experimentation and scalability analysis are necessary to fully validate the approach.
