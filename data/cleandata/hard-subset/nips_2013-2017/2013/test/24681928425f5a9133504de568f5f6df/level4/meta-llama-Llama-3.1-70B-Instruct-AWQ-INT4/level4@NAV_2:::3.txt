This paper proposes a novel approach to unsupervised learning of the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. Unlike previous methods, the authors introduce a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled, and propose an unsupervised approach to learning both the structure and parameters of such grammars. The approach optimizes the posterior probability of the grammar and induces compositions and reconfigurations in a unified manner.

The authors provide a clear and concise summary of the main ideas and relate them to previous work in the field. The paper is well-motivated, and the algorithm appears to be technically sound. However, some aspects of the paper could be improved for better clarity and understanding.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to learn both the structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars in an unsupervised manner. The authors propose an efficient algorithm for learning And-Or fragments, which can be used to construct any context-free And-Or grammar. The use of sufficient statistics to compute the posterior gain of adding an And-Or fragment is also a notable contribution.
