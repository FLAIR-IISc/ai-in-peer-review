This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. While the authors claim that their approach generalizes previous work on structure learning, it is more accurate to say that they have identified a special case that renders the problem tractable. This is a reasonable contribution in its own right, and the paper makes a valuable addition to the field. I do not take issue with the authors' focus on this specific problem, as it is distinct from the broader structure learning challenges encountered in natural language processing (NLP). 

The proposed algorithm is sensible and demonstrates success in inducing grammars when compared to other methods. However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened by incorporating additional comparisons to other approaches. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears to be applicable to both tasks and would provide a valuable point of comparison. Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars.
