This paper proposes a novel approach to unsupervised learning of the structure of AND-OR grammars. Unlike previous methods, the authors introduce a unified approach to inducing AND-OR fragments, rather than searching separately for AND and OR operations. The value of a proposed new AND-OR fragment can be computed efficiently using a set of sufficient statistics. The approach is evaluated on several tasks, including parsing events and visual objects.

The problem addressed in the paper is important, and the approach is well-motivated and technically sound. However, I was unable to verify the supplemental derivations due to time constraints. A few aspects of the paper were unclear, which may be due to my limited familiarity with the topic. To improve clarity, the authors could explicitly state whether the "surrogate measure" of the likelihood gain is an approximate or exact notion. Additionally, it would be helpful to clarify whether the approach can be extended to learning fragments with arity greater than 2 or if it is limited to learning grammars in "Chomsky normal form".

My primary concerns with the paper pertain to the experimental section. I found it challenging to understand the methodology, representation of examples, and the learned structures. The experimental section appears to be tailored to an audience familiar with recent visual-grammar papers from S. C. Zhu's group. To improve the paper's accessibility, the authors could include additional details and concrete illustrations in the supplementary materials. This would enable readers without prior knowledge of the specific research area to better comprehend the experiments and results.

I would like to see examples of interesting structures discovered in the learned grammars. While the paper effectively conveys the importance of grammars and structure learning, the quantitative results are somewhat disappointing. To make the paper more compelling, the authors could demonstrate that their approach learns more interesting structures compared to competing methods. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the approach's capabilities and its potential applications.

Overall, this is an interesting paper on unsupervised learning of AND-OR grammars. However, I had difficulty following the experiments and interpreting the results due to my limited familiarity with prior work in the field. If accepted, the authors should include supplementary material with more details and illustrations to facilitate a deeper understanding of the experiments and the learned structures.

Strengths:
- Novel approach to unsupervised learning of AND-OR grammars
- Efficient computation of the value of proposed new AND-OR fragments using sufficient statistics
- Evaluation on multiple tasks, including parsing events and visual objects

Weaknesses:
- Experimental section is not easily accessible to readers without prior knowledge of the specific research area
- Lack of clarity on whether the approach can be extended to learning fragments with arity greater than 2
- Limited illustration of interesting structures discovered in the learned grammars

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel approach to unsupervised learning of AND-OR grammars
- Strong technical soundness
- Potential for the approach to be applied to various tasks

Arguments for rejection:
- Experimental section is not easily accessible to readers without prior knowledge of the specific research area
- Limited illustration of interesting structures discovered in the learned grammars
- Unclear whether the approach can be extended to learning fragments with arity greater than 2
