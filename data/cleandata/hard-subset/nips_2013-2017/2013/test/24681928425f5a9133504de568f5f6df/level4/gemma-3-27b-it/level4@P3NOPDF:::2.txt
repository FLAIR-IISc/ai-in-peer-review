This paper introduces a method for jointly learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. These grammars, which incorporate both AND and OR rules, are well-suited to represent hierarchical and recursive structures found in domains such as natural language and compositional image generation. The authors propose a novel approach to unsupervised structure learning, iteratively introducing new AND-OR fragments and evaluating their contribution based on likelihood gains and prior considerations.

The method is evaluated on two tasks: learning grammars of events and learning grammars for images. In both experimental settings, the presented results are competitive with existing state-of-the-art approaches. Overall, this is a promising paper, presenting a potentially tractable method for learning stochastic grammars that explicitly model both conjunctive and disjunctive relationships.

However, several points warrant further consideration. My primary concern relates to the scalability of the proposed learning algorithm. The paper does not discuss the computational complexity or empirical performance with respect to dataset size. Specifically, it is unclear whether the method will scale effectively to larger datasets containing thousands of examples – a crucial factor for practical applicability.

Furthermore, while the results on event and image grammars are encouraging, the most natural and compelling application of AND-OR grammars lies in the domain of natural language processing. The absence of experiments on standard natural language datasets is a significant omission. It would be valuable to assess the method’s performance on a benchmark like the Penn Treebank, particularly in comparison to related work such as that of Klein and Manning. Addressing this would significantly strengthen the paper and demonstrate the broader applicability of the proposed approach.

The paper clearly articulates a method for estimating both the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, and the empirical results presented are solid for the chosen tasks. However, expanding the experimental evaluation – particularly to include natural language data – would substantially enhance the contribution and impact of this work. Addressing the question of scalability is also crucial for demonstrating the practical viability of the proposed method.
