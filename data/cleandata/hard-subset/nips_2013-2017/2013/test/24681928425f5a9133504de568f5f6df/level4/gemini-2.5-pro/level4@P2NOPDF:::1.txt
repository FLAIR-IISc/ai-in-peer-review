This paper introduces a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The proposed algorithm is well-motivated and demonstrates strong empirical performance against the selected baseline methods for grammar induction.

While the authors frame their contribution as a generalization of previous work on structure learning, it could be more accurately described as focusing on a specific subclass of grammars. This specialization is a key strength, as it renders the otherwise intractable problem of general grammar induction manageable. By defining a tractable special case, the paper makes a valuable contribution, and it would be beneficial for the authors to frame their work in this light rather than claiming a level of generality that might overstate the scope.

The primary area for improvement lies in the experimental evaluation. The paper would be significantly strengthened by a more comprehensive comparison against other established methods. In particular, the grammar induction technique of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears to be directly applicable to the tasks presented and would serve as a highly relevant and valuable point of comparison. Including this or other similar baselines would provide a clearer context for the performance of the proposed algorithm and make the paper's claims more robust.

In summary, this is a solid paper that presents an effective method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars. The core idea is sound, and the approach is a clear contribution to the field. The work would be more compelling if the experimental validation were expanded to include a broader range of relevant baselines.
