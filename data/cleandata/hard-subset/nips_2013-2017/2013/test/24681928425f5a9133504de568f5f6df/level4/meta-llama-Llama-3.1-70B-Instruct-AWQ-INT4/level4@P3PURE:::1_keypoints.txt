- Proposes a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars.
- Authors' claim of generalizing prior structure learning is overstated; the method is a special-case that makes the problem tractable.
- The special-case formulation is reasonable and represents a valuable contribution.
- The method is not more general than the structure learning problems typically seen in NLP.
- The proposed algorithm is sensible and shows empirical success against some existing methods.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and should include more comparisons to alternative approaches.
- Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14) appears applicable to the tasks and should be included as a comparison.
- Overall assessment: a solid paper presenting a method for learning a particular class of grammars.
