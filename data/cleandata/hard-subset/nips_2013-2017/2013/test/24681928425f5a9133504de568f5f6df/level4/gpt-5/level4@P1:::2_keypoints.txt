- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (AOGs) with a unified, data-agnostic definition.  
- Introduces a bottom-up iterative learning procedure that starts from a trivial grammar and inserts And-Or fragments (an And-node plus child Or-nodes).  
- Optimizes posterior P(G|X) with a sparsity prior on grammar size and uses Viterbi likelihood to approximate data likelihood.  
- Computes posterior gain for fragment additions using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors and context matrices) to avoid full reparsing.  
- Uses greedy or beam search with random restarts to explore fragment insertions efficiently.  
- Unifies discovery of composition (And) and reconfiguration (Or) by searching for combined And-Or fragments rather than separately.  
- Connects to prior PCFG/AOG structure learning work but extends them by jointly learning structure and parameters across modalities.  
- Evaluated on event grammars with three variants; joint learning of relations performs best and is competitive with or superior to prior work.  
- Evaluated on image grammars: higher F1 and lower KL on synthetic sketches and substantially better cross-validated perplexity on a real animal-face dataset.  
- Prototype runtime reported: minutes on a desktop for 5,000 samples with >10 atomic patterns each.  
- Strength: principled posterior objective and sensible use of Viterbi likelihood for unsupervised induction.  
- Strength: elegant And-Or fragment search that improves robustness under data sparsity and reduces redundancy.  
- Strength: efficient fragment scoring via sufficient statistics and flexible incorporation of relation types.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of scalability and computational complexity with dataset size, grammar size, fragment arity, beam width, and relation complexity.  
- Weakness: absence of natural language experiments (e.g., Penn Treebank) despite clear relevance; needs discussion or small-scale trials.  
- Quality: technically sound with solid derivations; would benefit from complexity analysis and ablation studies.  
- Clarity: generally well-organized but some notation- and figure-heavy parts need more intuition alongside equations.  
- Originality: novel And-Or fragment search and unified posterior optimization across modalities.  
- Significance: addresses unsupervised structure learning for models used across vision and events; broader impact would increase with NLP or larger-scale demos.  
- Pros for acceptance: unified principled method, empirical gains, data-sparse robustness, and practical efficiency.  
- Cons for acceptance: lacking scalability analysis and natural language benchmark experiments.  
- Recommendation: overall positive/lean accept, contingent on clarifying scalability and discussing or adding feasibility experiments on natural language data.
