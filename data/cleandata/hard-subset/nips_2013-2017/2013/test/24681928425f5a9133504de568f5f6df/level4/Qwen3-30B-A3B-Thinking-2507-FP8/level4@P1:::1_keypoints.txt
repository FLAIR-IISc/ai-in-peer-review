- Introduces a data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars applicable to images, events, etc.
- Proposes an unsupervised learning algorithm that jointly learns grammar structure and parameters from a trivial initial grammar.
- Unifies composition and reconfiguration learning via And-Or fragments, avoiding separate procedures that fail with scarce data.
- Optimizes posterior probability of grammar structure and parameters with clear likelihood and prior gain derivations.
- Uses sufficient statistics to make the optimization computationally feasible.
- Experimental results show competitive or superior performance on event grammars, especially with subtyped actions.
- Image grammar experiments substantially outperform Si et al. (2013) on synthetic and real datasets (F-measure and perplexity).
- Provides clear comparisons to relevant prior work and a well-motivated experimental setup per domain.
- Technical quality and clarity are high; formal definitions and algorithm descriptions support reproducibility.
- And-Or fragment concept is well-presented and effectively illustrated (e.g., Figure 2).
- Claim of data-type agnosticism is plausible but under-supported by experiments limited to events and images.
- Experimental comparisons could be more comprehensive, e.g., include Stolcke and Omohundro's approach.
- Work is significant for unsupervised grammar learning and fits NIPS scope, advancing robustness and efficiency in structure learning.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated, technically sound paper with minor experimental-scope and comparison limitations; recommend acceptance.
