- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for learning unified AND-OR grammar fragments (vs. prior separate induction of AND and OR fragments).  
- Uses sufficient statistics to enable efficient computation of fragment values.  
- Evaluated on event parsing and visual object recognition tasks.  
- Technically sound and well-motivated overall, with convincing algorithmic correctness.  
- Unclear whether the “surrogate measure” for likelihood gain is an exact computation or an approximation—needs explicit statement.  
- Unclear if the method generalizes to fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to Chomsky Normal Form.  
- Experimental methodology, data representation, and the precise nature of learned structures are difficult to grasp from the main paper.  
- Evaluation assumes substantial familiarity with prior visual grammar work (notably S.C. Zhu’s group), making results hard to interpret for general readers.  
- Strongly recommends expanding the supplementary material with detailed experimental setup, data representation, and implementation details.  
- Calls for illustrative examples of learned grammar structures to provide intuitive appeal and show superiority over competing methods.  
- Quantitative results are satisfactory but lack intuitive, qualitative demonstrations of the learned structure.  
- Conclusion: interesting and promising contribution, but accessibility and impact would improve with clearer explanations and richer supplemental illustrations.
