

This paper introduces a novel approach for jointly learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, which incorporate both AND and OR rules to model recursive phenomena such as natural language. The authors propose an unsupervised structure learning method that incrementally introduces AND-OR fragments while evaluating model likelihood and prior gains.  
The method is evaluated on two tasks—event grammar learning and image grammar learning—where it achieves competitive results relative to prior work. The paper presents a computationally feasible framework for learning stochastic grammars defined by AND and OR rules, which is a significant contribution. However, two key limitations require attention:  
1) The tractability and scalability of the learning algorithm are not addressed. It remains unclear whether the method can handle datasets comprising thousands of data points.  
2) The absence of experiments on natural language data is notable, given that natural language is a primary application domain for such grammars. Specifically, it would be valuable to demonstrate feasibility on standard benchmarks like the Penn Treebank dataset, as utilized in prior work (e.g., Klein and Manning). While the results on event and image grammars are promising, the inclusion of natural language experiments would substantially strengthen the paper's impact.
