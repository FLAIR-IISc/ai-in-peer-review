- Summary: Proposes unsupervised induction of unified AND-OR fragments with efficient evaluation via sufficient statistics, tested on event and visual object parsing.

- Strength: Addresses an important problem—unsupervised learning of structured representations with broad impact.

- Strength: Novel idea to induce unified AND-OR fragments instead of separate AND/OR searches; conceptually well-motivated.

- Strength: Algorithm appears technically sound; using sufficient statistics to evaluate candidates is elegant and suggests scalability.

- Strength: Empirical scope spans both event parsing and visual object parsing, indicating cross-domain applicability.

- Clarification needed: State whether the surrogate likelihood gain is an exact objective (tight bound/reformulation) or an approximation, and implications for guarantees.

- Clarification needed: Specify fragment arity support—can fragments have arity >2 or are they restricted to binary (Chomsky-like) compositions—and consequences for capacity and search.

- Clarification needed: Summarize key supplemental derivations, lemmas, and assumptions in the main paper so readers can verify technical claims without the supplement.

- Experimental concern: Experimental setup is under-specified—data representation, initial grammar/primitives, constraints, and search details are hard to reconstruct.

- Experimental concern: Results are mostly quantitative; paper lacks qualitative illustrations showing what structures were actually learned and their interpretability.

- Experimental concern: Baseline descriptions are insufficiently detailed—need precise differences in search space, scoring, and representations to attribute gains to the method.

- Suggestion: Provide concrete qualitative examples—visualizations of learned fragments, annotated parses, and side-by-side comparisons with baselines.

- Suggestion: Make experiments self-contained—detail datasets, preprocessing, features, initialization, search/stopping criteria, hyperparameters, and compute costs.

- Suggestion: Clarify the surrogate objective—state exact/approximate status, discuss bias/variance trade-offs, and report any empirical effects on search quality.

- Suggestion: Explicitly state arity capabilities and, if limited to binary, justify and discuss binarization or other workarounds and their impact.

- Suggestion: Improve readability for non-specialists by recap'ing the AND-OR grammar formalism and concisely contrasting prior separate AND/OR search approaches.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated contribution with a plausible efficient algorithm, but needs clearer, self-contained experiments and rich qualitative evidence to be fully convincing.

- Recommendation: If accepted, require supplementary material with qualitative visualizations, full experimental details for replication, and clarifications on the surrogate objective and fragment arity.
