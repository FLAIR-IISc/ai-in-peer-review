This work introduces an approach for jointly inferring both the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. These grammars, which utilize both AND and OR productions, are capable of modeling various recursive phenomena, including natural language. The paper proposes an effective method for unsupervised structure learning by incrementally introducing new AND-OR fragments, guided by an evaluation of gains in model likelihood and the prior.

The authors validate their method on two distinct tasks: the induction of event grammars and image grammars. In both experimental settings, the reported results are shown to be on par with previously established methods.

Overall, the paper is well-presented and offers what appears to be a computationally feasible approach for learning stochastic grammars based on AND-OR rules. My criticism of the paper is based on the following points:

1) The scalability of the learning algorithm is not addressed. There is no discussion of its computational complexity or its ability to handle larger datasets, for instance, on the order of thousands of data points.

2) The experimental validation could be significantly strengthened by application to natural language sentences, which represents a primary domain for such grammars. It remains unclear if the proposed technique could be effectively applied to standard benchmarks like the Penn Treebank, a dataset that has been a focus of related work (e.g., Klein and Manning). Although this paper successfully presents a method for estimating the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars with commendable results on two tasks, its impact would be greater with a broader set of experiments, particularly on linguistic data.
