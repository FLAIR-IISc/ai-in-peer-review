- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments rather than separately searching AND and OR operations.  
- Computes the value of a proposed AND-OR fragment efficiently using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on tasks including event parsing and visual object grammar learning.  
- Paper is well-written, addresses an important problem, and the approach is novel and well-motivated.  
- Algorithm appears technically sound, though supplemental derivations were not verified by the reviewer.  
- Unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation — needs clarification.  
- Unclear if the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to grammars in Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental section is hard to follow: representation of examples and exactly what was learned are not clearly explained.  
- Paper seems targeted to readers familiar with S. C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work, making it less accessible to others.  
- Recommends including supplementary materials with more experimental details and concrete illustrations if accepted.  
- Would like to see examples of interesting structures discovered; current results are mostly quantitative and lack qualitative insight.  
- Quantitative results are satisfactory but do not convincingly show that the method learns more interesting structures or outperforms competitors.
