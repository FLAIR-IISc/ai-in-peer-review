- Introduces an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars for recursive domains like language and vision.  
- Grammars combine compositional (AND) and alternative (OR) rules, yielding high expressivity.  
- Proposes an incremental, bottom-up framework that proposes new AND-OR fragments and keeps them if they improve a likelihood+prior scoring function.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on event grammars from video and image grammars from scenes, achieving results competitive with prior work.  
- Strength: principled, seemingly tractable approach to grammar induction with an intuitive incremental fragment proposal/evaluation strategy.  
- Empirical results are compelling and show utility on non-trivial tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of computational complexity and scalability—unclear cost of proposing/evaluating fragments as grammar and dataset size grow.  
- Weakness: no experiments on natural language (e.g., Penn Treebank), so applicability to canonical grammar-induction tasks and comparisons to established baselines are missing.  
- Overall: valuable and well-motivated contribution, but impact limited by missing scalability analysis and absence of NLP evaluations.
