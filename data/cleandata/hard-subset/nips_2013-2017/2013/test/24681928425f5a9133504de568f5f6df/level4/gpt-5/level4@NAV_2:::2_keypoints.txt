- Proposes a method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars via unsupervised induction.  
- Method iteratively adds And-Or fragments and evaluates their contribution using likelihood and prior to optimize the posterior.  
- Applicable to domains with recursive structure, including natural language and event grammars.  
- Evaluated on event-grammar and image-grammar settings.  
- Achieves results competitive with prior work in both evaluated domains.  
- Strength: offers a plausible and practical route to learning stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Concern: paper does not address computational tractability or scalability to thousands of examples.  
- Concern: lacks experiments on natural language (e.g., Penn Treebank) where such grammars are a primary application.
