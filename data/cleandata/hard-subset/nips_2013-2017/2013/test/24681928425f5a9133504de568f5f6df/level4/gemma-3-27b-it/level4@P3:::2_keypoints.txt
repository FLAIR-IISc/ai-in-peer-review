- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars by iteratively introducing AND-OR fragments and optimizing a posterior.  
- Unified framework elegantly combines compositional (AND) and reconfigurable (OR) aspects in a single learning step, unlike prior separate treatments.  
- Demonstrates competitive results on event and image grammar learning tasks, showing effectiveness across data types.  
- Clear problem statement, contributions, and strong connection to related work (stochastic grammars, SPNs, structure learning).  
- Novelty lies in simultaneous structure and parameter learning for a powerful formalism representing compositional/reconfigurable patterns.  
- Lacks analysis of computational complexity and scalability, leaving tractability on large datasets unclear.  
- Unclear how the method scales to thousands of data points; experimental evaluation on larger datasets is needed.  
- Underexplored applicability to NLP; recommends experiments on standard corpora (e.g., Penn Treebank) and comparison to Klein & Manning.  
- Paper mentions algorithmic iteration but needs more detailed runtime and memory usage analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel unified approach, demonstrated cross-domain effectiveness, clear presentation, tackles a challenging unsupervised problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient scalability discussion, no natural language experiments, limited complexity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability concerns and include NLP experiments or stronger complexity/runtime analysis.
