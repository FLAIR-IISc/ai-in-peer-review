- Proposes a new unsupervised method for learning AND-OR grammar structure by inducing unified AND-OR fragments instead of separate AND/OR searches.
- Claims efficient computation of a fragment’s value using a set of sufficient statistics.
- Evaluated on multiple tasks, including event parsing and visual object parsing.
- Overall assessment: strong paper addressing an important problem with a novel, well-motivated, and seemingly sound algorithm.
- Could not verify supplemental derivations; technical details in the supplement were not checked by the reviewer.
- Unclear whether the “surrogate measure” of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation—needs explicit clarification.
- Unclear whether the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to grammars effectively in Chomsky normal form.
- Experimental section is hard to follow: unclear what was done, how examples were represented, and what was actually learned.
- Presentation assumes close familiarity with prior visual-grammar work, making the experiments inaccessible to outsiders.
- Requests: if accepted, include much more supplementary material with detailed experimental setup, data representations, and concrete illustrations.
- Notes the lack of qualitative examples showing interesting learned structures—results are quantitative but not very illuminating or compelling.
