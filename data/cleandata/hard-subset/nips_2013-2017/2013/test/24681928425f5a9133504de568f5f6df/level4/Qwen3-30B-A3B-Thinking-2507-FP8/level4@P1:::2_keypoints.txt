1. Proposes a unified framework for unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars modeling compositionality and reconfigurability.  
2. Introduces an iterative algorithm that jointly learns grammar structure and parameters by optimizing grammar posterior from a trivial initialization.  
3. Key innovation: unified And-Or fragments that learn And- (composition) and Or- (reconfiguration) rules simultaneously, avoiding separate-handling limitations.  
4. Method is formally grounded in posterior optimization and uses sufficient statistics for computational efficiency.  
5. Demonstrates competitive or superior performance on event (human activity videos) and image (synthetic and real animal faces) grammar learning, especially in complex scenarios.  
6. Provides a clean, data-type-agnostic formalization enabling cross-domain application beyond domain-specific prior work.  
7. Unified learning approach is theoretically sound and reduces reliance on ad-hoc heuristics common in prior structure learning methods.  
8. Empirical evaluation is well-executed; algorithm description is clear and analysis explains why unified fragments mitigate data scarcity and redundancy.  
9. Lacks rigorous analysis of computational scalability—no explicit complexity bounds or benchmarks on large-scale datasets to substantiate "reasonable efficiency."  
10. Prototype runtime claim ("few minutes on a desktop with 5000 samples") is insufficient for real-world, larger-scale applications.  
11. Omits experiments on natural language data (e.g., Penn Treebank) and comparisons to established language grammar induction methods, weakening generality claims.  
12. Overall: technically strong and novel; recommend acceptance provided authors address scalability analysis and natural language experiments in the final version.
