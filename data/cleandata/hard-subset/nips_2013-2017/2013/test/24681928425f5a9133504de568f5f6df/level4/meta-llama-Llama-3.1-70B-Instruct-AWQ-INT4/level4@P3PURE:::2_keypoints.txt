- Proposes a method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars for recursive phenomena (e.g., language, images).  
- Introduces an unsupervised structure-learning procedure that adds AND-OR fragments iteratively and evaluates likelihood and prior gains.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on two tasks (event grammars and image grammars) with competitive results versus prior work.  
- Claims a tractable approach for estimating AND-OR grammar structure and parameters.  
- Criticism: no discussion of scalability or efficiency on large datasets (e.g., thousands of data points).  
- Criticism: lacks experiments on natural language sentences; recommends evaluation on Penn Treebank and comparison to prior NLP grammar work.  
- Overall: promising contribution but would be strengthened by additional experiments, especially on natural language data.
