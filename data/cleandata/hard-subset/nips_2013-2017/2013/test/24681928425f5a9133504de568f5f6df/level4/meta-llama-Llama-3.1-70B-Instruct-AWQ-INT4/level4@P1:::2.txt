This paper presents a method for learning the structure as well as the parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, which can be used to represent various recursive phenomena, including natural language and event grammars. The authors propose a unified formalization of stochastic AND-OR grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled and develop an unsupervised approach to learning both the structure and parameters of such grammars. The method iteratively introduces new AND-OR fragments to optimize the posterior probability of the grammar, making it a tractable way to learn stochastic grammars that can be modeled using AND and OR rules.

The authors provide a clear and concise overview of stochastic AND-OR grammars, including their definition, properties, and applications. They also present a detailed description of their learning algorithm, which is based on a greedy search or beam search to find the AND-OR fragments that lead to the highest gain in the posterior probability of the grammar. The algorithm's efficiency is improved by deriving and utilizing a set of sufficient statistics from the training data.

The paper presents experiments on two tasks: learning event grammars and learning image grammars. In both cases, the authors achieve results that are competitive with prior art. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in learning stochastic AND-OR grammars from different types of data.

However, there are a few aspects that could be improved. Firstly, the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their learning algorithm. While they mention that their prototype implementation can finish running within a few minutes on a desktop with 5000 training samples, it is unclear how the algorithm will scale to larger datasets. Providing a more detailed analysis of the algorithm's complexity and its scalability would be beneficial.

Secondly, the authors do not present experiments on natural language sentences, which is the most obvious application of stochastic AND-OR grammars. It would be interesting to see how the proposed approach performs on natural language data, such as the Penn Treebank dataset, which has been widely used in previous work on natural language processing.

Overall, the paper presents a nice method for unsupervised structure learning of stochastic AND-OR grammars, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness. However, providing more experiments, especially on natural language data, and a more detailed analysis of the algorithm's complexity and scalability would strengthen the paper.

Arguments For Acceptance:
- The paper presents a novel and tractable method for learning stochastic AND-OR grammars.
- The authors provide a clear and concise overview of stochastic AND-OR grammars and their applications.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in learning stochastic AND-OR grammars from different types of data.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
- The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their learning algorithm.
- The paper lacks experiments on natural language sentences, which is the most obvious application of stochastic AND-OR grammars.
- The authors could provide more experiments to demonstrate the robustness and scalability of their approach.
