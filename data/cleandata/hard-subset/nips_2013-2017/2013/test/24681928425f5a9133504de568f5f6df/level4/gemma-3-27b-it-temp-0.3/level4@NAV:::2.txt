This paper introduces a novel approach to simultaneously learn both the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars. These grammars, leveraging both AND and OR rules, offer a powerful representation for recursive structures found in domains like natural language and formal grammars. The authors propose an effective unsupervised learning method, iteratively adding AND-OR fragments and evaluating them based on likelihood and prior gains.

The method is evaluated on two tasks – event grammar learning and image grammar learning – demonstrating performance competitive with existing techniques. 

Overall, this is a strong paper presenting a promising, practical approach to learning stochastic grammars with AND/OR rule capabilities. However, the manuscript would benefit from a discussion of the algorithm’s computational scalability. Specifically, it is unclear how well the learning process will perform with larger datasets containing thousands of data points. 

Furthermore, given the natural suitability of AND-OR grammars for natural language processing, I would have appreciated experiments on a natural language corpus. Investigating the method’s applicability to standard datasets like the Penn Treebank, and comparing results to established work in the field (e.g., Klein & Manning), would significantly strengthen the paper. While the presented results are encouraging, additional experiments, particularly those involving natural language, would broaden the scope and impact of this work.
