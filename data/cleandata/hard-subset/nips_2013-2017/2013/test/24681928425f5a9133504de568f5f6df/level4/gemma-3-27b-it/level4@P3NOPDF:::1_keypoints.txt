- Introduces a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAGs).  
- Claims to generalize prior structure learning approaches but is more accurately a tractable special case.  
- The reduction in generality is reasonable and well-motivated because it enables a practical algorithm.  
- Authors should avoid overstating broader generality beyond the specific grammar class.  
- Proposed algorithm is sensible and empirically performs well compared to existing grammar induction methods.  
- Experimental results are encouraging and support the method as a viable solution for SAG structure learning.  
- Experimental evaluation should include a comparison to Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14) as a relevant benchmark.  
- Paper should include a discussion/analysis of computational complexity with respect to grammar size and training data to clarify scalability.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-presented work; recommend acceptance.
