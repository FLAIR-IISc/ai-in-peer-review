- Introduces a method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- AND-OR grammars can represent recursive phenomena (e.g., natural language, other grammars).  
- Proposes an unsupervised structure-learning approach that iteratively adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates them by likelihood and prior gains.  
- Experiments on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks.  
- Results are competitive with existing approaches.  
- Reviewers view the work as a promising, feasible contribution.  
- Major concern: scalability of the learning algorithm is not addressed (performance on datasets with thousands of samples).  
- Major concern: lacks experiments on natural language sentences (e.g., Penn Treebank) to demonstrate applicability to the most intuitive use case.
