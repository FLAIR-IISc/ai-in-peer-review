- Introduces a method for learning both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars for recursive phenomena (natural language, images).
- Proposes an unsupervised, incremental structure-learning algorithm that adds AND-OR fragments guided by likelihood and prior gains.
- Evaluated on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks, achieving competitive results compared to prior work.
- Claims a computationally tractable framework for learning stochastic grammars with AND and OR rules.
- Concern: Computational tractability/scalability is not demonstrated—unclear if the method scales to datasets with thousands of examples.
- Concern: No experiments on natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank), so applicability to standard NLP tasks is uncertain and additional experiments are needed.
