- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments rather than separate AND and OR operations.  
- Value of a proposed AND-OR fragment can be computed efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on parsing events and visual object tasks.  
- Problem is important; approach is novel and well-motivated.  
- Algorithm appears technically sound, though supplemental derivations were not verified by the reviewer.  
- Unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or approximate.  
- Unclear if method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental section is hard to understand: unclear what was done, how examples were represented, and what was learned.  
- Paper seems targeted to readers already familiar with recent visual-grammar work, limiting accessibility for outsiders.  
- Reviewer urges inclusion of more experimental details and concrete illustrations in the supplement if accepted.  
- Reviewer requests examples of interesting structure discovered and comparisons showing more structure learned than competing methods.  
- Quality: technically sound and novel, but supplemental verification lacking.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but experiments need clearer exposition and additional illustrations.  
- Originality: approach is novel and addresses an important problem.  
- Significance: potential to contribute significantly, but more evidence is needed that it learns richer structure and outperforms alternatives.
