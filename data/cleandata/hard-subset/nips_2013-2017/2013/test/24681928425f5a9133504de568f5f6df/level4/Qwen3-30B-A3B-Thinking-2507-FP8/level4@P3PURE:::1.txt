

This paper proposes a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors claim this approach "generalizes" prior work on structure learning, but it in fact constitutes a special case that renders the problem tractable. This is a reasonable contribution in its own right, and I do not dispute that the problem addressed is more specific than the structure learning challenges encountered in NLP. The core algorithm is sensible and demonstrates competitive performance against existing methods for grammar induction. While the experimental evaluation is generally sound, it could be strengthened by including additional comparisons to relevant baselines. Specifically, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears applicable to both tasks and would provide a valuable benchmark for comparison. Overall, this is a solid contribution presenting an effective method for learning the structure of a specific class of grammars.
