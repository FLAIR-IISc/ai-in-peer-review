- Presents a unified, data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars applicable to images, events, and language.
- Proposes an unsupervised algorithm that learns both grammar structure and parameters from unannotated data.
- Introduces "And-Or fragments" to jointly search for composition and reconfiguration rules in a single learning step.
- Starts from a trivial grammar and iteratively adds fragments that optimize the posterior probability of the grammar.
- Leverages sufficient statistics for efficient computation and implementation of the posterior optimization.
- Extends prior domain-specific And-Or grammar work (Zhu et al. 2011; Si et al. 2011) into a general cross-domain framework.
- Explicitly optimizes posterior probability rather than relying on heuristics, improving principled structure learning.
- Unified learning yields more compact grammars that are more robust to data scarcity than separate approaches.
- Empirical evaluation on event and image grammars shows competitive or superior performance; one grounding-based experiment is especially promising.
- Strong theoretical justification and clear derivation of sufficient statistics enable efficient implementation.
- Paper is generally clear and well-structured, with helpful examples and figures; some supplementary technical details could be moved into the main text.
- Original contribution: And-Or fragment concept novel and addresses limitations of prior separate composition/reconfiguration learning.
- Related work is adequately cited but could better distinguish differences from prior approaches.
- Main concern: insufficient analysis of algorithmic scalability and performance on larger datasets or complex grammars.
- Main concern: absence of experiments on natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank) limits assessment of applicability to NLP.
- Main concern: need clearer theoretical or empirical justification for why And-Or fragments outperform separate learning of components.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution, recommended for acceptance pending more scalability and NLP experiments.
