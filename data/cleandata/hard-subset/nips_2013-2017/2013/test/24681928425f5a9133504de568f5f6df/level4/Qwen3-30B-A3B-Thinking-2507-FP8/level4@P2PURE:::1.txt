

This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The claim that this "generalizes" prior work on structure learning is inaccurate; it is actually a special case that renders the problem tractable. This observation is reasonable and constitutes a valuable contribution, so I do not dispute that the problem is more general than the structure learning problem addressed in NLP. The core algorithm is sensible and demonstrates successful performance relative to other methods for inducing grammars. The experimental evaluation would be strengthened by including additional comparisons, particularly with the approach by Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14), which could be applicable to both tasks and would provide a meaningful benchmark. Overall, this is a solid paper presenting a method for learning the structure of a specific class of grammars.
