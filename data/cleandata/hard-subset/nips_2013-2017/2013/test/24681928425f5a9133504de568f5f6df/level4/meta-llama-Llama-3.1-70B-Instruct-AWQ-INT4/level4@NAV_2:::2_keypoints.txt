- Presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and a novel unsupervised learning method that iteratively adds And-Or fragments to optimize posterior (likelihood vs prior).
- Method unifies induction of compositions and reconfigurations, improving efficiency and robustness to data scarcity and ambiguity compared to prior work.
- Evaluated on event grammars and image grammars, achieving competitive or better performance than previous approaches.
- Clear, concise presentation with detailed method description that is easy to follow.
- Thorough evaluation across two different tasks demonstrates generality.
- Does not discuss scalability; unclear if the algorithm handles large datasets (thousands of datapoints).
- No evaluation on natural language data (e.g., Penn Treebank), an obvious and important application for stochastic And-Or grammars.
- Lacks deeper insights or visualizations of the learned grammars and their relation to underlying data structure.
- Quality: 8/10 — well-motivated and clear but needs scalability discussion and additional experiments.
- Clarity: 9/10 — well-written but would benefit from more visualizations and examples.
- Originality: 8/10 — novel approach though building on previous work.
- Significance: 8/10 — strong results on two tasks but limited by missing NL evaluation and scalability analysis.
- Pro-acceptance: novel, well-motivated method; thorough multi-task evaluation; accessible writing.
- Con-acceptance: missing scalability analysis; no natural language experiments; insufficient analysis/visualization of learned grammars.
