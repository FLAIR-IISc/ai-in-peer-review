- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars via iterative addition of AND-OR fragments optimizing grammar posterior.  
- Presents a unified, data-agnostic formalization of stochastic AND-OR grammars applicable to recursive phenomena (e.g., language, events, images).  
- Details a learning algorithm using greedy/beam search and sufficient statistics to improve efficiency.  
- Demonstrates competitive results on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks, showing effectiveness across data types.  
- Lacks a detailed computational-complexity analysis and clear discussion of scalability to larger datasets beyond a prototype runtime claim.  
- Omits experiments on natural language sentences (e.g., Penn Treebank), an obvious and important application.  
- Needs more experiments to establish robustness and scalability of the approach.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, tractable method; clear exposition; effective empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing complexity/scalability analysis; no NLP experiments; insufficient empirical breadth.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
