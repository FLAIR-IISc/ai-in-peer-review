- Proposes a unified framework for unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars that handles diverse data types (images, events).  
- Introduces iterative insertion of unified And-Or fragments to jointly learn grammar structure and parameters, avoiding separate handling of And- and Or-rules.  
- Optimizes the grammar posterior with efficient likelihood-gain computation using sufficient statistics from training data.  
- Evaluates on event grammars (human activity videos) and image grammars (synthetic and real), showing competitive or superior performance to prior methods.  
- Contribution 1: data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Contribution 2: unified learning framework that explicitly optimizes both structure and parameters.  
- Contribution 3: empirical validation across multiple domains demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Strength: Important, well-motivated problem connecting to prior stochastic grammar work for images and events.  
- Strength: Technically novel and sound; unified fragment method addresses limitations of separate And/Or learning, especially in data-scarce regimes.  
- Strength: Efficient computation of likelihood gains via sufficient statistics is a notable technical advance.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation with well-justified comparisons to previous methods.  
- Weakness: Lacks concrete illustrative examples of the learned grammars to demonstrate interpretability and distinctiveness.  
- Weakness: Ambiguity about the "surrogate measure" for likelihood gain and whether fragments with arity >2 are supported.  
- Weakness: Experimental section assumes familiarity with Zhu group’s prior work; needs clearer explanations of data representations and learning procedures for broader accessibility.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, original work recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to add illustrative grammar examples and clarify technical details (surrogate measure, arity, data/learning descriptions).
