This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors suggest their approach generalizes previous work on structure learning; however, it is perhaps more accurate to view it as focusing on a specific, tractable special case. This focus is not a weakness—on the contrary, it is what makes the problem solvable and represents a valuable contribution. Nevertheless, the authors should be careful to position their work accurately and avoid overstating its generality relative to broader structure learning problems, such as those in natural language processing. The proposed algorithm is sound and performs well against the presented baselines.

The experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including additional comparisons. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro [14] seems applicable to the tasks in this paper and would provide a valuable point of comparison.

Overall, this is a solid paper presenting an effective method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars.
