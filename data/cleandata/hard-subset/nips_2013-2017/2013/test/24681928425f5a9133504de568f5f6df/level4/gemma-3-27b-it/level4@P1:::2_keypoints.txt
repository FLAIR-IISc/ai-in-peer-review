- Introduces a method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.
- Uses an unsupervised iterative procedure that adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates changes in likelihood and prior.
- Evaluated on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks with competitive results against existing methods.
- Employs a sound theoretical foundation optimizing posterior probability and uses sufficient statistics for efficiency.
- Presentation is clear and well organized.
- Does not discuss computational scalability or how performance degrades with increasing data size.
- Unclear if the method can scale to datasets with thousands of samples.
- Lacks experiments on standard natural language benchmarks despite grammar origins in NLP.
- Recommends adding evaluation on datasets like the Penn Treebank for useful comparisons to prior work.
- Strengths for acceptance: novelty in joint structure+parameter learning, solid theory, competitive empirical performance, clear writing.
- Weaknesses against acceptance: missing scalability analysis and absence of natural language experiments.
