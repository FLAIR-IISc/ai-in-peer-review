- Proposes a novel unified method for unsupervised induction of AND-OR fragments instead of separate AND/OR searches.  
- Computes the value of proposed AND-OR fragments efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on tasks including event parsing and visual object learning.  
- Problem is important, approach is well-motivated and novel.  
- Algorithm appears technically sound, though supplemental derivations were not verified by the reviewer.  
- Unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Unclear if method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental setup is hard to understand: representation of examples, learning process, and results are unclear.  
- Paper seems targeted to readers familiar with S. C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work, limiting broader accessibility.  
- Results are presented only quantitatively; lack of qualitative examples weakens insight into learned structure.  
- Reviewer suggests adding detailed examples of discovered structures and comparisons to competing methods in the supplement.  
- Overall potential to contribute to the field but needs clarifications and better experimental presentation.  
- Quality: 8/10.  
- Clarity: 7/10.  
- Originality: 9/10.  
- Significance: 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated, technically sound, addresses important problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experiments presented only quantitatively, limited accessibility, and unresolved methodological clarifications.
