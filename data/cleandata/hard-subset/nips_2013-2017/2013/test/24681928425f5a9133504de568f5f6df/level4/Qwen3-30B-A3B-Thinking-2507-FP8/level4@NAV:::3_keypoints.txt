- Proposes a novel unsupervised method to learn AND-OR grammar structure by inducing unified AND-OR fragments.  
- Computes fragment value efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on parsing events and visual objects.  
- Strong, well-motivated, and seemingly technically sound work (supplemental derivations not verified).  
- Clarify whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Clarify whether the method supports fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental methodology is unclear: how examples are represented, what exactly is learned, and the precise procedure.  
- Paper assumes familiarity with prior visual-grammar work; supplement should include detailed explanations and concrete illustrations.  
- Major shortcoming: no illustrative examples of discovered structures—results are purely quantitative and lack qualitative/structural analysis.  
- Including discovered-structure examples and comparative structural quality analysis would significantly strengthen the paper.
