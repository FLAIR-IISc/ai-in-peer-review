- Proposes a method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars using incremental addition of AND-OR fragments and evaluation of likelihood and prior gains.  
- Novel unsupervised structure-learning approach combining AND and OR rules to model recursive phenomena (language, images).  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks with competitive, state-of-the-art results.  
- Overall promising and tractable contribution to learning stochastic grammars.  
- Major concern: no information on scalability—unclear if algorithm handles thousands of datapoints efficiently; needs larger-scale evaluation.  
- Major concern: lacks experiments on natural language data; recommend testing on datasets like the Penn Treebank to compare with prior work (e.g., Klein & Manning).  
- Recommendation: additional experiments, especially on natural language, would significantly strengthen the paper’s contribution and impact.
