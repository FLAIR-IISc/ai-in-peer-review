- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments instead of searching for AND and OR separately.  
- Shows fragment value can be computed efficiently using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on multiple tasks, including event parsing and visual object parsing.  
- Addresses an important problem, is well-motivated, and appears technically sound.  
- Reviewer could not verify supplemental derivations due to time constraints.  
- Unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Unclear if the method generalizes to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky-normal-form grammars.  
- Experimental methodology, data representation, and learned outcomes are hard to follow.  
- Paper seems targeted to readers familiar with S. C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work and may be inaccessible to outsiders.  
- Reviewer requests supplementary materials with detailed experimental illustrations and examples of interesting learned structures; current results are mainly quantitative and lack qualitative insight and comparisons.
