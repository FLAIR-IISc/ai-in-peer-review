This paper proposes a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars, presenting it as a generalization of previous work on structure learning. However, it is more accurate to view this approach as a special case that renders the problem tractable. This distinction is reasonable in its own right, and the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field. The basic algorithm is well-conceived and demonstrates success in comparison to other methods for inducing grammars.

To further strengthen the experimental evaluation, the inclusion of additional comparisons to other methods would be beneficial. Specifically, the approach of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) appears to be applicable to both tasks and would provide a valuable point of comparison. Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a method for learning the structure of a particular class of grammars, making a worthwhile contribution to the field.
