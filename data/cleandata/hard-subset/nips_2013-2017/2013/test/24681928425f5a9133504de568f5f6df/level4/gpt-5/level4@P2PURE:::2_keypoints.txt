- Proposes a method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars using iterative unsupervised addition of fragments with likelihood+prior gain evaluation.  
- AND-OR grammars can express recursive phenomena including natural language and grammars themselves.  
- Experiments on event grammars and image grammars show results competitive with prior work.  
- Approach is appealing and appears to offer a tractable way to learn stochastic grammars with AND/OR rules.  
- Major concern: paper lacks discussion of algorithmic tractability and scalability—unclear if it scales to thousands of datapoints.  
- Major concern: no experiments on natural language (e.g., Penn Treebank); feasibility on such datasets relative to prior work (Klein & Manning) is not demonstrated.
