- Proposes a method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.
- Notes AND-OR grammars are well-suited for representing recursive phenomena (e.g., natural language, formal grammars).
- Introduces an unsupervised structure-learning approach that iteratively adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates likelihood/prior gains.
- Evaluates the method on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks.
- Reports results competitive with existing approaches.
- Overall assessment: a strong, tractable approach to learning stochastic AND-OR grammars.
- Major criticism: no discussion of computational scalability to large datasets (e.g., thousands of data points).
- Major criticism: lacks experiments on natural language corpora (e.g., Penn Treebank) to demonstrate applicability and comparison to prior work like Klein & Manning.
