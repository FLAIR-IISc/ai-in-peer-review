This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors propose a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled and develop an unsupervised approach to learning both the structure and parameters of such grammars. The method optimizes the posterior probability of the grammar and induces compositions and reconfigurations in a unified manner. 

The paper relates to previous work on structure learning of stochastic grammars in natural language processing and image and event modeling. The authors extend the previous work by proposing a more general and unified framework for learning stochastic And-Or grammars. The method is evaluated on two tasks: learning event grammars from human activity data and learning image grammars from synthetic and real datasets. The results show that the proposed method is competitive with previous approaches on the first task and outperforms them on the second task.

The strengths of the paper include the development of a unified framework for learning stochastic And-Or grammars, the proposal of an efficient algorithm for learning the structure and parameters of the grammar, and the evaluation of the method on two different tasks. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. The experimental evaluation could be improved by adding more comparisons to other methods. In particular, the method of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14) seems like it could be applied to both tasks and would be a valuable comparison point. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results and a discussion of the limitations of the proposed method.
