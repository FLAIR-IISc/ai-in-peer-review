- Proposes joint learning of structure and parameters for stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- Uses an unsupervised, incremental structure-learning method that adds AND-OR fragments evaluated by likelihood and prior gains.  
- Target grammars can represent recursive phenomena like natural language, event grammars, and image grammars.  
- Experiments on event and image grammar induction show results competitive with prior work.  
- Reviewer views the approach as promising and seemingly tractable.  
- Major concern: paper lacks sufficient discussion/analysis of computational complexity and scalability (runtime/memory, scaling to thousands of datapoints).  
- Major concern: missing experiments on natural language—feasibility on benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank) or smaller-scale/ablation studies is unclear.  
- Recommendation: strengthen the paper by addressing scalability and adding or thoroughly discussing natural language experiments.
