- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that induces unified AND-OR fragments instead of searching AND and OR operations separately.  
- Main technical contribution: an efficient procedure to compute the value of a proposed fragment using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on parsing tasks for events and visual objects.  
- Paper is well-written, tackles an important problem, is novel and well-motivated, and appears technically sound (though reviewer could not verify supplemental derivations).  
- Needs clarification on whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation and the implications for theoretical guarantees.  
- Needs clarification on whether the approach extends to fragments of arity >2 or is limited to binary/Chomsky-normal-form-like grammars, affecting expressiveness.  
- Primary concern: experimental details are unclear—procedures, example representations, and precisely what was learned are not well-specified.  
- Presentation assumes substantial prior knowledge of the authors’ prior visual-grammar work, making the experiments hard to follow for outsiders.  
- Strong recommendation to add extensive supplementary material with input examples, feature representations, and a clear experimental pipeline.  
- Missed opportunity: no qualitative results showing the discovered grammatical structures; evaluation is purely quantitative.  
- Suggests visualizing portions of the learned grammars and comparing them to competing methods to demonstrate what was learned and validate claims.  
- Overall: interesting and novel work, but limited impact and accessibility due to opaque experiments and lack of qualitative evidence; authors should expand experiments and appendices.
