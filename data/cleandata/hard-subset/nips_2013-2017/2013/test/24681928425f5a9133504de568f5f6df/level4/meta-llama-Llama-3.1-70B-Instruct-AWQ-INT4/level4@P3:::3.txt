This paper proposes a novel approach to unsupervised learning of the structure of AND-OR grammars. Unlike previous methods, the authors introduce a unified approach to inducing AND-OR fragments, rather than searching separately for AND and OR operations. The value of a proposed new AND-OR fragment can be computed efficiently using a set of sufficient statistics. The approach is evaluated on several tasks, including parsing events and visual objects.

The problem addressed in this paper is important, and the approach is well-motivated and novel. The algorithm appears technically sound, although I was unable to verify the supplemental derivations. However, there are a few aspects that require clarification. Firstly, it is unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of the likelihood gain is an approximate or exact notion. Secondly, it is unclear whether the approach can be extended to learning fragments with arity more than 2, or whether it can only tractably learn grammars in "Chomsky normal form".

My primary concerns with the paper are related to the experiments. I had difficulty understanding most aspects of the experimental setup, including the representation of examples, the learning process, and the results. The paper appears to be targeted towards readers who are familiar with recent visual-grammar papers from S. C. Zhu's group. While the experimental results are satisfactory, they are presented purely quantitatively, which is somewhat disappointing given the emphasis on the importance of grammars and structure learning.

To improve the paper, I would suggest including more details and concrete illustrations of the experiments in the supplementary materials. Specifically, it would be helpful to see examples of interesting structures discovered in the learned grammars. This would provide more insight into the capabilities of the proposed approach and make the paper more compelling. Additionally, including more information on how the approach compares to competing methods in terms of the learned structure would be beneficial.

Overall, this is an interesting paper on unsupervised learning of AND-OR grammars. While I had some difficulties following the experiments and interpreting the results, the paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field. With some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, the paper could be even more effective in conveying its message.
