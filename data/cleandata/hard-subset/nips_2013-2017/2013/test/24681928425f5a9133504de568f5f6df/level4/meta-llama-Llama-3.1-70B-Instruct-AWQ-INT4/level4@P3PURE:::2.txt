This paper presents a method for learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, which are capable of representing various recursive phenomena, including natural language and image grammars. The authors propose a novel approach to unsupervised structure learning by introducing new AND-OR fragments at consecutive steps and evaluating the likelihood and prior gains of their model.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on two tasks: learning event grammars and learning image grammars. In both cases, they achieve competitive results compared to prior art.

I found the overall paper to be a promising contribution, as it provides a tractable method for learning stochastic grammars that can be modeled using AND and OR rules. However, I have several criticisms that stem from the following observations:

1. The authors do not provide any discussion on the scalability of their learning algorithm. It is unclear whether the algorithm can efficiently handle large datasets, such as those with thousands of data points.

2. I would have liked to see experiments on natural language sentences, as natural language is a primary application area for stochastic AND-OR grammars. It would be particularly interesting to evaluate the proposed method on the Penn Treebank dataset, which has been a focus of previous work (e.g., Klein and Manning). This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's capabilities and limitations.

Overall, the paper presents a novel approach to estimating the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars and demonstrates promising results on two tasks. Nevertheless, I believe that additional experiments, particularly on natural language data, would further strengthen the paper's contributions.
