- Introduces a novel incremental method for unsupervised structure and parameter learning in stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- Constructs grammars by adding fragments stepwise and evaluates both likelihood and prior terms to guide learning.  
- The approach is theoretically grounded and maintains a tractable framework for joint structure and parameter estimation.  
- Avoids exhaustive search over grammar space, achieving a practical solution that outperforms prior methods on evaluated tasks.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on event grammar learning and image grammar parsing.  
- Algorithmic description is clear and the experiments use solid baselines with statistical validation.  
- Major gap: computational tractability and scalability are not addressed—no runtime complexity, memory analysis, or empirical scaling experiments.  
- Major gap: lack of natural language experiments; no evaluation on standard NLP datasets (e.g., Penn Treebank) to show generalization to syntax.  
- Unanswered NLP questions: can it learn syntactic structures from raw sentences, how it compares to PCFG parsers, and limits on long/complex sentences.  
- Suggested fixes: add runtime/scalability studies on varying dataset sizes (e.g., 100–10,000 examples) and a small-scale Penn Treebank experiment (e.g., ~500 sentences).  
- Overall assessment: promising and technically solid contribution, but requires major revisions on scalability and NLP applicability before publication.
