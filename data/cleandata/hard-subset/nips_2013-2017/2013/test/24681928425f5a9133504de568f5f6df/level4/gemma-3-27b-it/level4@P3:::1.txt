This paper presents a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors argue that their approach offers a unified formalization applicable to various data types, and propose an unsupervised learning algorithm that iteratively induces grammar structures while optimizing posterior probability. This builds upon prior work in both natural language processing and computer vision, adapting techniques for structure learning to the specific context of And-Or grammars.

The core idea – learning both structure and parameters simultaneously, and unifying the search for compositions and reconfigurations – is a sensible one. The authors correctly identify a key limitation of previous approaches that rely on manually specified or fixed grammar structures, which can become intractable for complex data. The proposed And-Or fragment learning strategy appears to address this, and the results demonstrate competitive or superior performance compared to existing methods on event and image grammars.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of unsupervised structure learning for stochastic And-Or grammars and motivates its importance.
*   **Novel Approach:** The unified learning of structure and parameters, alongside the novel And-Or fragment strategy, represents a meaningful contribution.
*   **Sound Methodology:** The optimization of posterior probability provides a principled framework for learning. The efficient computation of posterior gain, leveraging sufficient statistics, is a valuable optimization.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on event and image grammars demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   **Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the complex concepts accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Comparison:** While the authors compare their approach to existing methods, the comparison could be more comprehensive. Specifically, the method proposed by Stolcke and Omohondro (1994) – iterative biclustering for probabilistic grammars – appears relevant and would provide a valuable benchmark. Evaluating against this approach, which addresses a similar problem in a different domain, would strengthen the paper's claims.
*   **Scope of Generalization:** The claim of generalization might be overstated. While the formalization *allows* for application to different data types, the experiments focus solely on events and images. Further exploration with other data modalities would be beneficial to truly demonstrate the generality of the approach.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The paper does not thoroughly discuss the sensitivity of the algorithm to the hyperparameter α. An analysis of how α impacts performance would be helpful.
*   **Clarification on Initial Grammar:** The process of generating the initial grammar, while explained, could benefit from a more intuitive explanation. The direct mapping from data samples to initial Or-rules might not be immediately clear to all readers.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem.
*   The experimental results are promising and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.
*   The focus on unifying structure and parameter learning is a valuable contribution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The comparison to existing methods is not exhaustive.
*   The claim of broad generalization could be stronger.
*   The analysis of parameter sensitivity is lacking.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of unsupervised structure learning. While the comparison to existing methods could be more comprehensive and the claim of generalization refined, the novelty of the approach, the technical soundness of the methodology, and the promising experimental results warrant acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions, specifically addressing the need for a more thorough comparison to related work (e.g., Stolcke & Omohondro, 1994) and providing further discussion regarding the sensitivity of the algorithm to its parameters.
