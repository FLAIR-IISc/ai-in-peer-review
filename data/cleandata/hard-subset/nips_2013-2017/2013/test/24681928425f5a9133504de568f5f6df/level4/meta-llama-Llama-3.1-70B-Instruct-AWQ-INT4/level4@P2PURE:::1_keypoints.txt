1. Proposes a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars, claimed to generalize prior structure learning work.  
2. The approach may actually be a special-case that makes the problem tractable, limiting generality.  
3. Despite that limitation, the contribution is reasonable and it’s unfair to fault the paper for not solving the fully general NLP structure learning problem.  
4. The basic algorithm is sensible and shows success compared to other grammar induction methods.  
5. Experimental evaluation should be strengthened with additional method comparisons.  
6. Specifically recommend comparing to Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14), which appears applicable to both tasks.  
7. Overall judgment: a solid paper presenting a method for learning structure of a particular class of grammars.
