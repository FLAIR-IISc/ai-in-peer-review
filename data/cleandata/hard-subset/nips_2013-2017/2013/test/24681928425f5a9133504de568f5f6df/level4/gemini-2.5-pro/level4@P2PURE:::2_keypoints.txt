- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- AND-OR grammars use AND and OR rules to represent various recursive phenomena.  
- Learning algorithm iteratively introduces new AND-OR fragments, accepting them based on improved data likelihood and a model prior.  
- Demonstrated on event and image grammar tasks, achieving results competitive with prior work.  
- Main strength: presents a seemingly tractable approach for learning expressive grammars.  
- Concern 1: computational tractability and scalability are not discussed—unclear if it scales to thousands of data points.  
- Concern 2: omits experiments on natural language (e.g., Penn Treebank); testing on language benchmarks is needed to establish general utility and compare to prior work.
