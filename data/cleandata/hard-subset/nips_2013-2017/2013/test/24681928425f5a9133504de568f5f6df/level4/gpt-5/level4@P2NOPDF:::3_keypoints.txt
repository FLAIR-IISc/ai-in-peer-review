- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn unified AND-OR grammar fragments, evaluated on event and visual object parsing.  
- Novel, well-motivated, and appears technically sound, though supplemental derivations weren’t verified.  
- Unclear whether the “surrogate measure” for likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Unclear whether the method supports fragment arity >2 or is effectively limited to binary (Chomsky-normal-form) fragments for tractability.  
- Experimental description is hard to follow: unclear data representations, which model components were learned, and the exact evaluation protocol.  
- Presentation assumes familiarity with prior visual-grammar work (S. C. Zhu’s group), reducing accessibility for other readers.  
- Strong recommendation to provide a comprehensive supplement detailing datasets, preprocessing, and feature representations.  
- Request for precise definitions of learned fragments and grammar components, plus full search procedure, objectives, and hyperparameters.  
- Request for detailed decoding/inference procedures, evaluation metrics, and ablation studies.  
- Request for concrete visualizations and qualitative examples of learned fragments, parse trees, and example parses to demonstrate interpretability.  
- Overall: interesting and solid contribution, but acceptance should require clarifications on the surrogate’s exactness, arity capability, and a detailed, illustrated supplementary document for the experiments.
