- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn structure of stochastic And-Or grammars via iterative posterior optimization.  
- Core idea: jointly learn grammar structure and parameters, unifying search for compositions and reconfigurations.  
- Introduces an And-Or fragment learning strategy to avoid reliance on fixed, manually specified grammars.  
- Demonstrates competitive or superior performance on event and image grammar tasks.  

Strengths:
- Clearly defines and motivates the problem of unsupervised structure learning for And-Or grammars.  
- Novel unified approach and And-Or fragment strategy represent a meaningful contribution.  
- Uses principled posterior optimization and efficient posterior-gain computation via sufficient statistics.  
- Empirical validation on multiple domains (events, images) supports effectiveness.  
- Well-written and organized presentation.  

Weaknesses:
- Comparison set is limited; lacks benchmarking against relevant methods like Stolcke & Omohundro (1994).  
- Claim of broad generalization is weak because experiments are restricted to events and images.  
- No thorough analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameter α.  
- Initial grammar generation description could be more intuitive; mapping from data to initial Or-rules is unclear.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, technically sound approach to a challenging problem.  
- Promising experimental results and clear exposition.  
- Unifying structure and parameter learning is a valuable contribution.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Incomplete comparison to existing related work.  
- Overstated generality without broader empirical support.  
- Lacks parameter sensitivity analysis.  

Overall assessment:
- Solid paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen comparisons (e.g., Stolcke & Omohundro) and analyze parameter sensitivity.
