- Introduces a novel unsupervised method for AND-OR grammar learning that induces unified AND-OR fragments rather than searching AND/OR independently.  
- Computes the value of new AND-OR fragments efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on tasks involving parsing events and visual objects.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper — important problem, novel, well-motivated, and technically sound (supplemental derivations not independently verified).  
- Clarity issue: unclear whether the “surrogate measure” of likelihood gain is an approximation or an exact calculation.  
- Scope question: unclear if the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to learning CNF-style grammars.  
- Experimental concerns: descriptions of what was done, how examples were represented, and what was learned are difficult to follow.  
- Paper appears targeted to readers familiar with S.C. Zhu’s visual-grammar work, making it less accessible to others.  
- Recommendation: significantly expand supplementary material with detailed experimental procedures and concrete illustrations.  
- Results presentation: only quantitative metrics are given; reviewer wants examples showing interesting learned grammar structure and comparisons demonstrating more interesting structure than baselines.  
- Conclusion: interesting and appealing work but needs more supplementary details and visualizations of learned structure if accepted.
