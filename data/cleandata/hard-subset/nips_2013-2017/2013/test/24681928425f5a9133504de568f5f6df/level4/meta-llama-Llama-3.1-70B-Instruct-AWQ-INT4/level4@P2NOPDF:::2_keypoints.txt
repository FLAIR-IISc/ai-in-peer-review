- Proposes a method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars for recursive phenomena (e.g., language, images).  
- Introduces a novel unsupervised structure-learning approach that iteratively adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates likelihood and prior gains.  
- Experimental results on event and image grammars are competitive with existing state-of-the-art methods.  
- Presents a tractable approach to learning stochastic grammars, making a valuable contribution.  
- Lacks analysis of the learning algorithm’s scalability; unclear if it handles large datasets efficiently.  
- Omits experiments on natural language sentences, despite NL being a primary application domain.  
- Recommends evaluation on standard NLP datasets (e.g., Penn Treebank/Klein & Manning) to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Needs a more comprehensive evaluation to establish effectiveness and suitability for real-world applications.
