- Proposes a method for learning structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars able to represent recursive phenomena (e.g., language, images).
- Introduces a novel unsupervised structure-learning approach that iteratively adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates likelihood and prior gains.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on two tasks (event grammars and image grammars) with results competitive with state-of-the-art.
- Presents a tractable approach to estimating stochastic grammars using AND and OR rules.
- Does not assess scalability of the learning algorithm to large datasets (e.g., thousands of data points).
- Omits experiments on natural language sentences, a primary application area for these grammars.
- Recommends evaluation on established NLP datasets (e.g., Penn Treebank) to strengthen contributions and show broader applicability.
