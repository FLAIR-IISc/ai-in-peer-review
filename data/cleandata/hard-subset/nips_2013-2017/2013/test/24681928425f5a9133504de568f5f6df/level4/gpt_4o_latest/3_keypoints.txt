- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that discovers unified AND-OR fragments instead of separately searching for AND and OR operations.  
- Introduces an efficient computation of a new fragment’s value using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on multiple tasks (event parsing and visual object parsing).  
- Overall assessment: solid, significant problem, innovative and well-motivated approach.  
- Algorithm appears technically sound, but supplemental derivations were not verified by the reviewer.  
- Clarification needed: whether the “surrogate measure” of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Clarification needed: whether the method supports fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky Normal Form.  
- Experimental section is unclear about procedures, example representations, and what structures were actually learned.  
- Paper assumes familiarity with prior visual-grammar work (esp. S. C. Zhu’s group), making experiments hard to follow for others.  
- Recommendation: include supplementary material with detailed experimental procedures and concrete illustrations or examples of learned structures.
