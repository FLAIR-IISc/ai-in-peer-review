- Proposes an unsupervised method that induces unified And-Or fragments for stochastic And-Or grammars and evaluates them efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Framework is instantiated on multiple domains (event parsing, image/object parsing).  
- Contribution is important, original, and technically reasonable overall.  
- Supplementary derivations were not verifiable by the reviewer.  
- Needs clarification whether the “surrogate” objective for likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Unclear if the method supports learning higher-arity fragments in practice or is effectively limited to CNF-like binary splits for tractability.  
- Experimental section is hard to follow: unclear input representations, how fragments map to data, and what was concretely learned.  
- Presentation assumes deep familiarity with prior visual-grammar work and is not accessible to a broader NeurIPS audience.  
- Paper lacks qualitative illustrations of non-trivial induced structure; results are mostly quantitative and not very illuminating about learned structure.  
- If accepted, paper should include a comprehensive supplement with step-by-step experiment descriptions, concrete examples, and visualizations of learned grammars.
