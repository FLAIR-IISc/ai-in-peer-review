

This paper introduces a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. While the authors claim this approach "generalizes" prior work on structure learning, it in fact constitutes a special case that enables tractable learning. This represents a valid contribution, and I do not contest the paper's framing of the problem scope relative to NLP structure learning challenges. The core algorithm is well-motivated, logically sound, and demonstrates competitive performance against existing grammar induction methods.  
The experimental evaluation would be strengthened by including additional comparisons, particularly with the method of Stolcke and Omohondro (reference 14), which appears applicable to both tasks and would provide a meaningful benchmark. Overall, this is a well-constructed paper presenting a notable contribution to structure learning within a specific grammar class.
