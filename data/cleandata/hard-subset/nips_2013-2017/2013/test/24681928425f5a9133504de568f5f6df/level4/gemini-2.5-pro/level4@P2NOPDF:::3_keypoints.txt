- Introduces a novel method for unsupervised learning of unified AND-OR grammar fragments, unlike prior separate AND/OR searches.  
- Key algorithmic contribution: efficient computation of new fragment values using a set of sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on parsing tasks for events and visual objects.  
- Paper is well-written, addresses an important problem, and appears technically sound (supplementary derivations not verified).  
- Unclear whether the introduced "surrogate measure" for likelihood gain is exact or an approximation—needs clarification.  
- Ambiguity about grammar arity: unclear if method supports fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky-normal-form-style binary rules.  
- Experimental section assumes strong familiarity with prior work, hindering accessibility for a broader audience.  
- Experimental descriptions (setup, data representation, learned structure descriptions) are too condensed and need expansion.  
- Major missing element: lack of qualitative results demonstrating the meaningfulness of learned structures.  
- Quantitative metrics alone are insufficient; the paper should show examples of discovered grammars and parsed outputs.  
- Suggests qualitative comparisons to competing methods to demonstrate more plausible or insightful learned structures.  
- Recommends using supplementary materials to provide detailed, accessible explanations of experiments, data representations, and visualizations.  
- Overall assessment: promising and novel contribution, but experimental clarity and qualitative evidence are the primary weaknesses to address.
