

This paper introduces a method for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors claim their approach "generalizes" prior work on grammar structure learning, though it is in fact a special case that renders the problem tractable—a reasonable and valuable contribution in its own right. The core algorithm is well-motivated, and empirical results demonstrate competitive performance against existing grammar induction methods, supporting the paper's technical claims.  

The experimental evaluation, however, would benefit from more comprehensive comparisons. Specifically, the method proposed by Stolcke and Omohondro (Reference 14), which is directly applicable to the tasks addressed in this work, should be included as a benchmark. This comparison would provide stronger context for assessing the proposed method's advantages over established techniques in the field. While the current results are promising, omitting this comparison weakens the paper's relative performance analysis.  

The paper makes a clear contribution to the problem of grammar structure learning within a constrained class of models. The tractability achieved through the authors' formulation is a meaningful step forward, particularly for applications where computational efficiency is critical. The writing is generally clear, and the technical exposition is sound.  

To strengthen the submission, the authors should address the missing comparison with Stolcke and Omohondro’s approach. This would not only align the evaluation with standard practice but also clarify the method’s practical significance. Beyond this, the paper’s core ideas are solid and well-executed, with results that validate the proposed methodology.  

Overall, this is a strong, focused contribution to the literature on grammar learning. The method is novel within its specific framework, the experiments are largely convincing, and the results are well-presented. The minor gap in the evaluation does not undermine the paper’s core merits, but addressing it would elevate the work to a more definitive level. The authors have made a valuable addition to the field, and the paper is suitable for publication with minor revisions.
