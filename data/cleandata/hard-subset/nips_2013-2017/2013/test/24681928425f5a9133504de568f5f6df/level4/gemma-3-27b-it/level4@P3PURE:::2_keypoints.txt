- Presents a method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.
- AND-OR grammars can represent recursive phenomena like natural language and compositional grammars.
- Introduces an unsupervised, iterative structure-learning algorithm that adds AND-OR fragments and evaluates them by likelihood and prior gains.
- Experimental validation on two tasks (event grammars and image grammars) shows competitive results with existing methods.
- Overall assessment: strong, tractable approach to learning stochastic AND-OR grammars.
- Criticism: paper does not address computational scalability or whether the method scales to datasets with thousands of examples.
- Criticism: lacks experiments on natural language (e.g., Penn Treebank), so feasibility on established NLP benchmarks is unclear.
- Recommendation: further evaluation, particularly on natural language data, would strengthen the work.
