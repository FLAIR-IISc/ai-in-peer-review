- Introduces a method for jointly learning structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars.  
- AND-OR grammars are well-suited for representing recursive structures in domains like natural language and formal grammars.  
- Proposes an unsupervised structure-learning approach that iteratively adds AND-OR fragments evaluated by likelihood gains and prior contributions.  
- Demonstrates the method on event grammar and image grammar learning tasks.  
- Achieves results competitive with existing approaches on the two demonstrated tasks.  
- Presents a potentially tractable approach to learning stochastic grammars with AND and OR rules.  
- Lacks discussion of algorithm scalability and unclear performance as dataset size (e.g., thousands of examples) increases.  
- Missing experiments on natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank), leaving applicability to NLP an open question.  
- Recommends additional natural language experiments to strengthen the paper’s contribution.
