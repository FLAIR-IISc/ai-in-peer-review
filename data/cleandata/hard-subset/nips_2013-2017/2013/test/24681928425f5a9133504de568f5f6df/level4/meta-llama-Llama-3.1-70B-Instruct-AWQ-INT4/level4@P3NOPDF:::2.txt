This paper proposes a method for learning the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, which are capable of representing various recursive phenomena, including natural language and image grammars. The authors introduce a novel approach for unsupervised structure learning by iteratively adding new AND-OR fragments and evaluating the likelihood and prior gains of their model.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments on two tasks: learning event grammars and learning image grammars. The results are competitive with state-of-the-art approaches, showcasing the potential of their method.

Overall, the paper presents a tractable approach to learning stochastic grammars that can be modeled using AND and OR rules. However, there are a few aspects that warrant further consideration. Firstly, the authors do not provide an assessment of the scalability of their learning algorithm. It is unclear whether the algorithm can efficiently handle large datasets, such as those containing thousands of data points.

Furthermore, the absence of experiments on natural language sentences is notable, given that natural language is a primary application domain for stochastic AND-OR grammars. It would be valuable to investigate the performance of the proposed method on established natural language datasets, such as the Penn Treebank dataset, which has been a focus of previous research (e.g., Klein and Manning). Additional experiments on natural language data would strengthen the paper's contributions and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's capabilities.

In summary, the paper presents a promising approach to estimating the structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars, with encouraging results on two tasks. Nevertheless, further experimentation, particularly on natural language data, would enhance the paper's impact and provide a more thorough understanding of the method's limitations and potential applications.
