- Proposes a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an unsupervised greedy procedure that adds And-Or fragments to maximize a sparsity-regularized posterior (Viterbi likelihood).  
- Core idea: search fragments that introduce compositions (And) and alternatives (Or) simultaneously, computing posterior gain via sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors, context matrices) under an unambiguity assumption.  
- Evaluated on event parsing and image modeling, showing comparable or improved performance over prior AOG learners and sensible behavior on a synthetic grammar-identification task.

Strengths:
- Clear, principled objective with a compactness prior; Viterbi training is well-motivated.  
- And-Or fragments unify composition and alternative search, reducing sparsity and redundancy.  
- Efficient posterior-gain computation via sufficient statistics is elegant and practical.  
- Cross-domain evaluation supports modality-agnostic formalization and shows non-trivial improvements.

Weaknesses:
- Relies on a strong unambiguity assumption; impact on correctness and bias is unquantified.  
- Comparisons are narrow (mostly to two prior works); lacks recent/stronger baselines and end-task metrics.  
- Limited sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (α, fragment arity n, encoding costs) and no statistical significance testing.  
- Reproducibility details are thin (beam/greedy settings, initialization, runtime/memory, feature quantization).  
- Performance on images still depends heavily on discretization/quantization choices.

Quality:
- Derivations and optimal parameter solutions are sound under assumptions; algorithmic framework coherent; experiments supportive but limited in scale.

Clarity:
- Generally well-organized, but some notation (context matrices) is dense and figures could be clearer.

Originality:
- The unified And-Or fragment search and posterior optimization offer a meaningful advance over heuristic structure learners.

Significance:
- Moderate: broadly applicable with measurable gains, but needs stronger evidence on scalability and ambiguous settings.

Pros/Cons for acceptance:
- Pros: principled, novel fragment search; cross-domain applicability; improved results.  
- Cons: strong assumptions; limited baselines and analysis.

Key suggestions:
- Perform ablations (And-only vs Or-only vs And-Or; vary n and beam width).  
- Evaluate robustness to ambiguity (e.g., entropy regularization, annealed EM).  
- Add more baselines, end-task metrics, and statistical significance tests.  
- Report runtime/memory and full reproducibility details (initialization, beam settings, quantization).  
- Analyze sensitivity to discretization/quantization choices.
