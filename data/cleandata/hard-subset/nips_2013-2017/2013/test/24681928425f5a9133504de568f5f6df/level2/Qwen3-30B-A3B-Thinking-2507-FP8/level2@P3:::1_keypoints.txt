- Presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an unsupervised method to jointly infer grammar structure and parameters.
- Introduces "And-Or fragments" to systematically unify learning of compositional (And) and alternative (Or) structures, enabling efficient posterior optimization.
- Formalization is data-agnostic and clearly illustrated across domains (natural language, events, images).
- Algorithm iteratively adds And-Or fragments to optimize the grammar posterior, with technical rigor in the approach.
- Derives efficient sufficient statistics for likelihood and prior gains, avoiding the need to re-parse all data at each step.
- Empirical validation on event and image grammars shows consistent improvements over prior work [17, 18], especially on complex datasets where prior methods fail.
- Ablation study demonstrates the benefit of grounding grammars to atomic actions and learning relations jointly.
- Original and significant: resolves prior limitations (separate composition/reconfiguration learning, data scarcity) and suffices to construct any context-free And-Or grammar.
- Appropriately contextualizes relation to sum-product networks [10] without overclaiming novelty.
- Minor weakness: scalability discussion is brief and lacks analysis for much larger datasets (e.g., 100k+ samples).
- Minor weakness: handling of videos with multiple unrelated events via post-hoc top-level Or-node conversion feels ad-hoc and could be more principled.
- Minor weakness: comparison to SPNs could be expanded to better clarify differences in modeling compositional hierarchies.
- Well-written and reproducible, with theoretical claims supported by derivations and supplementary material.
- Significant contribution to principled probabilistic modeling and structured representation learning; minor issues do not undermine the core contribution.
