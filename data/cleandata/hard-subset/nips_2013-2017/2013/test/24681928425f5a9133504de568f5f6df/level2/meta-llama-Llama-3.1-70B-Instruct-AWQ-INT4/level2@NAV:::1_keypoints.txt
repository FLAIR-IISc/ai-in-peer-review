- Unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars applicable to multiple data types (images, events).
- Unsupervised method to learn grammar structures and parameters by optimizing the posterior.
- Algorithm is efficient, scalable, and robust to data scarcity and ambiguity.
- Empirical results on event and image grammars are competitive or better than prior work.
- Evaluation is limited to two datasets, questioning generality across applications.
- Method relies on assumptions (e.g., grammar unambiguity) that may not hold in practice.
- Paper lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis for large-scale use.
- Original contribution: combines And-Or fragments with posterior optimization for grammar learning.
- Significant potential impact for image/video analysis, NLP, and HCI by modeling complex structures.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with sufficient methodological detail.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS despite noted limitations.
