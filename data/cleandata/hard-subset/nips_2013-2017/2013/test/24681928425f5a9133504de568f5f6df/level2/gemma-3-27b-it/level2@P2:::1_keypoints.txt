- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs) from unannotated data.  
- Presents a unified formalization of SAGs applicable to multiple data types (images, events), consolidating prior work.  
- Introduces an iterative method that induces compositions and reconfigurations by optimizing the grammar posterior.  
- Core innovation: learns And-Or fragments jointly rather than treating composition and reconfiguration separately.  
- Joint structure and parameter learning is done by directly optimizing posterior probability, a principled approach.  
- Theoretical grounding: likelihood gain linked to tensor/matrix coherence and biclustering, with efficient computation via sufficient statistics.  
- Empirical results on event and image grammars show competitive or superior performance versus existing methods.  
- Experiments include varying dataset sizes and comparisons to baselines.  
- Algorithm description is dense; formulation of likelihood gain and context matrices may be hard to follow without more examples or pseudocode.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., α=0.5) is not analyzed or justified.  
- Comparisons exclude some recent baselines; some referenced baselines are relatively old.  
- Computational cost and scaling behavior are only briefly mentioned; more detailed complexity analysis is needed.  
- Use of synthetic image data is limited; more extensive evaluation on diverse real-world image datasets would strengthen claims.  
- Overall assessment: novel, theoretically sound, empirically validated contribution that outweighs identified weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated novel approach, strong theoretical basis, competitive empirical performance, clear problem presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: complex exposition, limited hyperparameter and cost analysis, and restricted baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
