- Proposes a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an unsupervised algorithm to learn both structure and parameters from unannotated data.  
- Starts from a trivial grammar and iteratively induces And-Or fragments (composition and reconfiguration) to optimize the grammar posterior.  
- Derives sufficient statistics that allow efficient computation of posterior gain when adding new fragments, providing a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Joint handling of composition (And) and reconfiguration (Or) avoids separate, error-prone procedures used in prior work.  
- Demonstrates the method on event (activity videos) and image datasets, showing comparable or better performance, especially on complex datasets.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive with synthetic and real datasets and meaningful baselines.  
- Addresses the practical limitation of manual grammar specification by learning compact, generalizable grammars.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized, with helpful examples and illustrations.  
- Limitation: assumes a single pattern per sample; the treatment for multi-event videos is only briefly modified and not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Missing analysis of computational complexity scaling; runtime claims (minutes on desktop for 5000 samples) need more detail.  
- Connection to sum-product networks (SPNs) is mentioned but not sufficiently compared or related to SPN learning techniques.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel unified approach with strong theoretical and empirical support.  
- Recommendation: accept; paper is well-suited for NIPS and of broad interest to the ML community.
