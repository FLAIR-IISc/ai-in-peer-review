- Unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to data modality.  
- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Introduces "And-Or fragments" that can generate multiple configurations of existing nodes.  
- Derives sufficient statistics to efficiently compute the posterior gain of adding an And-Or fragment.  
- Extends prior grammar structure learning work and improves on recent And-Or grammar methods for images and events.  
- Thorough, well-designed experimental evaluation on event and image grammars with comparable or better performance than prior approaches.  
- Clear writing, organization, and a comprehensive review of related work.  
- Assumes training data are i.i.d., which may be unrealistic in many settings.  
- Lacks a theoretical analysis of convergence properties.  
- Computational complexity is not thoroughly analyzed and lacks detailed efficiency comparisons with other methods.  
- Novel contributions include the And-Or fragments and the sufficient-statistics-based posterior gain computation.  
- Potentially significant applicability across NLP, computer vision, and robotics.  
- Recommendation: accept, but require authors to address convergence analysis and provide detailed computational comparisons.
