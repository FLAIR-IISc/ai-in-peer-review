- Presents an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs) with a unified formalization across data types.
- Core contribution: iterative induction of compositions and reconfigurations using “And-Or fragments” that optimize the grammar posterior.
- Evaluated on event and image grammars, showing comparable or better performance than existing grammar-based methods.
- Novelty: jointly learns grammar structure and parameters rather than relying on manually specified structures or fixed grammars.
- Theoretical grounding: posterior gain formulation using sufficient statistics and links to tensor coherence measures.
- Strong literature connections to prior work in natural language processing and computer vision grammar learning.
- Missing detail: rationale for specific relation choices in And-rules (e.g., spatial vs. temporal) is under-explained.
- Empirical limitation: comparisons are limited to grammar-induction baselines and lack broader unsupervised learning baselines.
- Hyperparameter concern: complexity-control parameter α is fixed and lacks ablation or sensitivity analysis.
- Evaluation gap: discussion of limitations and failure cases (e.g., highly complex or noisy data) is brief and needs expansion.
- Overall recommendation: significant and theoretically sound contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the above issues.
