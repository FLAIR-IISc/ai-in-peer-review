- Proposes a novel, unsupervised method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or Grammars (AOGs).
- Introduces "And-Or fragments" that unify learning of compositional (And) and alternative (Or) rules.
- Formalizes AOGs in a unified, data-agnostic framework applicable across domains.
- Presents an iterative, bottom-up greedy algorithm that starts from a trivial grammar and adds fragments to maximize a posterior.
- Optimizes a posterior combining a Viterbi likelihood term with a sparsity prior to encourage compact models.
- Derives posterior gain using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors and context matrices) to make fragment search computationally feasible.
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly written with effective figures and organization.
- Empirically validated on event grammars from video and image grammars (synthetic and real), outperforming or matching state of the art.
- Strength: approach is robust to data scarcity and ambiguity due to the unified fragment formulation.
- Weakness: greedy search may be prone to local optima; analysis of random restarts or alternatives is lacking.
- Weakness: reliance on Viterbi likelihood could introduce an unambiguity bias; theoretical discussion is limited.
- Overall recommendation: strong, significant contribution with minor weaknesses; strongly recommended for acceptance.
