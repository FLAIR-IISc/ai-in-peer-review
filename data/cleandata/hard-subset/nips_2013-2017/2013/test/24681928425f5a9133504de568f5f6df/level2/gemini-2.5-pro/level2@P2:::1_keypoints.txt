- Proposes an unsupervised framework to learn stochastic And-Or grammars by starting from a trivial grammar and iteratively adding fragments.
- Main contribution: the "And-Or fragment" that jointly induces compositional (And) and alternative (Or) structures within a Bayesian posterior objective combining Viterbi likelihood and a sparsity prior.
- Provides an efficient method to compute posterior gain for candidate fragments using sufficient statistics from data.
- Connects likelihood gain to coherence of an n-gram tensor and a context matrix, giving intuition for the optimization target.
- Demonstrates generality by applying the same algorithm to temporal event grammars (video) and spatial object grammars (images).
- Strength: Novel joint learning of compositional and alternative structure, addressing limitations of methods that learn them separately.
- Strength: Data-agnostic formalization that transfers across modalities, supported by empirical results.
- Strength: Solid technical contribution deriving posterior gain and offering computationally efficient search criteria.
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation against recent baselines, achieving strong performance and useful ablation studies.
- Weakness: Relies on Viterbi likelihood approximation, which may mislead learning in highly ambiguous grammars and needs discussion.
- Weakness: Greedy/beam search strategy risks local optima; sensitivity to search parameters and effectiveness of random restarts need more analysis.
- Weakness: Key derivation (likelihood gain) is relegated to supplementary material and needs clearer intuition in the main text.
- Weakness: Complexity prior hyperparameter α is under-discussed; lacks justification or sensitivity analysis for practical use.
