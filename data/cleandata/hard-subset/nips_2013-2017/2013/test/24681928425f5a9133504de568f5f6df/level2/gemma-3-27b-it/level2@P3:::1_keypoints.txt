- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs) with a unified formalization for diverse data types.
- Introduces an iterative learning algorithm that jointly induces compositions and reconfigurations via And-Or fragments optimizing posterior probability.
- Key contribution: unification of composition and reconfiguration learning through And-Or fragments, improving performance in data-scarce settings.
- Presents a clear, well-structured paper with concise formalization of SAGs and a logically explained algorithmic framework.
- Strong theoretical grounding: derives posterior gain using sufficient statistics and employs coherence measures for fragment selection.
- Experimental evaluation on event and image grammars shows competitive or superior performance compared to existing methods.
- Good design choice: grounding grammar learning to atomic actions in event grammar improves results.
- Limited discussion and detail on the prior P(G) and hyperparameter α selection/tuning; sparsity prior specifics are unclear.
- Experimental section scope is limited; synthetic image gains are significant but dataset simplicity and limitations are not discussed.
- Real image experiments report only perplexity; additional metrics (e.g., segmentation accuracy) and qualitative examples of learned grammars are missing.
- Comparisons to prior work are pertinent but not comprehensive; more baselines and comparisons to recent state-of-the-art are needed.
- Convergence properties and potential issues with local optima are not discussed despite the termination criterion based on posterior gain.
- Theoretical justification for choosing And-Or fragments over other fragment types is underdeveloped; current support is primarily empirical.
- Overall assessment: solid, novel contribution with strong theory and initial empirical support; recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing experiments, prior/hyperparameter details, and convergence discussion.
