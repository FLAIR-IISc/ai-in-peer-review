- Unified formalization: Provides a general SAG framework applicable to diverse data types.
- Joint structure and parameter learning: Simultaneously learns grammar structure and parameters, avoiding previous limitations.
- And-Or fragment learning: Introduces And-Or fragments to unify composition/reconfiguration search and improve robustness in low-data regimes.
- Theoretical justification: Optimizes posterior probability with derived sufficient statistics and links to tensor/matrix coherence.
- Empirical evaluation: Shows promising results on event and image grammars, outperforming or matching existing methods.

- Algorithm complexity: Method is complex with several hyperparameters (e.g., α) and limited detail on greedy/beam search implementation.
- Limited comparison: Evaluation omits many recent baselines, especially modern deep learning approaches for events and images.
- Synthetic image data: Reliance on synthetic images raises concerns about real-world generalization despite some real-data experiments.
- Scalability concerns: Unclear how the method scales to larger datasets or more complex grammars; computational complexity/bottlenecks not discussed.
- Clarity issues: Explanations of reductions, the context matrix, and the link between theory and implementation are sometimes unclear.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel, theoretically motivated, and empirically promising work with some complexity and clarity caveats.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach to a hard problem; solid theory; promising empirical results; potential impact across multiple domains.
- Arguments against acceptance: Algorithm complexity and scalability issues; limited baseline comparisons; unclear presentation of some algorithmic details.
- Rating: Strong Accept (6/7).
