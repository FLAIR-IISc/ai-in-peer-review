- Proposes an unsupervised, data-type-agnostic formalization and learning algorithm for stochastic And-Or grammars that unifies composition and reconfiguration via And-Or fragments.  
- Provides a formal, domain-agnostic definition of stochastic And-Or grammars applicable to text, images, and events.  
- Introduces a novel learning algorithm that iteratively induces grammar fragments by optimizing the posterior probability rather than relying on heuristics.  
- Efficiently computes likelihood and prior gains using sufficient statistics, with mathematically rigorous derivations.  
- Distinguishes And-rules and Or-rules clearly and presents the algorithm in sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Uses And-Or fragments to theoretically unify learning of compositions and reconfigurations, addressing prior approaches' limitations.  
- Experiments are well-designed with appropriate baselines on synthetic and real datasets for event and image grammars.  
- Empirical results show clear improvements over prior methods, especially on challenging synthetic datasets with action subtypes.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful examples and figures clarifying the method.  
- Related work is appropriately cited and the paper positions itself well relative to NLP, image, and event grammars.  
- Claims of data-type agnosticism are weakened by evaluation on only two domains (events and images) and no text experiments.  
- Lacks details on the modification used to handle videos with multiple unrelated events and its effect on generality.  
- Does not thoroughly discuss trade-offs of using Viterbi likelihood versus standard likelihood despite citing supporting work.  
- Computational complexity and scaling behavior are not fully addressed; reported runtimes lack analysis for larger datasets or complex grammars.  
- Choice of greedy search over beam search is not justified, nor is the impact of search strategy on grammar quality discussed.  
- The superiority of the third event-learning method and why ordering matters are not fully explained or theoretically justified.  
- Overall significance is strong with clear advances in unsupervised structure learning; reviewer recommends acceptance.
