## Review of “Unsupervised Structure Learning of Stochastic And-Or Grammars”

This paper presents an unsupervised approach to learning both the structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs), aiming for a unified formalization applicable to various data types. The core contribution lies in iteratively inducing compositions and reconfigurations using “And-Or fragments,” optimizing the posterior probability of the grammar. The approach is evaluated on event and image grammars, achieving comparable or better performance than existing methods.

The paper effectively addresses the challenge of automatic grammar induction, a significant bottleneck in utilizing SAGs. The proposed method moves beyond manually specifying structures or learning parameters from fixed grammars. The introduction of And-Or fragments, aiming to jointly learn compositions and reconfigurations, is a novel and potentially impactful idea. The authors clearly connect their work to existing literature in both natural language processing and computer vision, referencing key papers in grammar learning (e.g., [8, 10, 17, 18]). The mathematical formulation of the posterior gain, leveraging sufficient statistics and drawing parallels to tensor coherence measures [22], demonstrates a solid theoretical grounding.

However, the paper could benefit from further elaboration on several aspects. The rationale behind the specific choice of relations within And-rules (e.g., spatial relations for images, temporal for events) isn’t fully detailed. While the empirical results are promising, the experimental setup lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art unsupervised learning methods beyond those focused on grammar induction.  The parameter α, controlling grammar complexity, is fixed; an ablation study exploring its impact would strengthen the findings. The discussion of limitations is somewhat brief; a more in-depth analysis of potential failure cases (e.g., highly complex or noisy data) would be valuable. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to jointly learning structure and parameters of SAGs.
*   Theoretical justification through posterior probability optimization and connection to tensor coherence.
*   Demonstrated performance on diverse data types (events and images).
*   Clear connection to related work.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited comparison against broader range of unsupervised learning techniques.
*   Lack of sensitivity analysis of key parameters (e.g., α).
*   Brief discussion of limitations and potential failure cases.



**Overall:** This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of unsupervised grammar learning. Despite some minor weaknesses, the novelty of the approach, theoretical soundness, and promising experimental results warrant acceptance, with encouragement to the authors to address the mentioned points in a revised version. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
