- Proposes a unified formalization of stochastic context-free And-Or grammars and an unsupervised structure-learning algorithm that induces And and Or jointly via And-Or fragments.
- Learning objective maximizes a sparsity-regularized posterior: Viterbi likelihood with a prior penalizing grammar size.
- Iteratively proposes candidate And-Or fragments, evaluates posterior gain using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensor and context matrix), and greedily/beam-search adds best fragments with parse reductions.
- Instantiated for event grammars (temporal relations) and image grammars (spatial relations); reports comparable or better accuracy than prior work on two event and two image datasets.
- Builds on prior stochastic grammar and unsupervised induction work, and relates to SPNs; novelty lies in joint And-Or fragment induction and sufficient-statistic-based gain computation.
- Strength: unified fragment type couples composition and reconfiguration, addressing data scarcity and redundancy from separate And/Or learning.
- Strength: posterior-based criterion with explicit size prior and efficient sufficient statistics; coherence analysis linking likelihood gain to tensor/matrix measures is insightful.
- Strength: empirical evidence across modalities including synthetic ground-truth experiments shows consistent gains; practical greedy/beam search with reasonable runtimes.
- Weakness: derivation assumes unambiguous parses and Viterbi likelihood, potentially biasing toward unambiguous grammars and understating uncertainty.
- Weakness/question: under-specified parameterization and learning of relation energies for And-rules and how they enter gain computation and sufficient statistics.
- Weakness/question: prior coding costs (sa, so) and α are not justified; sensitivity to these hyperparameters is unexplored.
- Weakness/question: candidate enumeration and n-gram tensor construction may be expensive; search/pruning, time and memory complexity details are missing.
- Weakness: claim of modality-agnosticism is weakened because instantiations require relation vocabularies; guidance for porting to new domains is limited.
- Weakness: baselines are narrow (event baselines limited and image baselines omit models like SPNs); potential unfairness where some baselines lack access to richer information.
- Weakness: evaluation details are incomplete (smoothing for perplexity, KL estimation, confidence intervals, and statistical significance).
- Suggestion: clarify relation energy parameterization and P(A→...) computation, add sensitivity analyses for α, sa, so, and study robustness to parse ambiguity.
- Suggestion: add ablations (And-only, Or-only, remove context coherence), broader baselines (e.g., SPN structure learning), and detail candidate generation/pruning and complexity.
- Suggestion: consider a small NLP experiment to support modality-agnostic claim and provide a proof/sketch that And-Or fragments can represent any context-free AOG.
- Pros: elegant unified fragment-based learning, efficient sufficient-statistic gain computation, cross-domain empirical gains, conceptual links to SPNs and MDL.
- Cons: strong unambiguity/Viterbi assumptions, missing details on relation energy learning and prior coding, narrow baselines, limited ablations, and clarity/reproducibility gaps.
- Overall assessment: promising and solid contribution to unsupervised AOG structure learning leaning toward acceptance if relation parameter learning, experiments (ablations/baselines), and complexity/sensitivity analyses are clarified.
- Reviewer confidence: moderate.
