The paper presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and proposes an unsupervised approach to learning the structures and parameters of such grammars. The authors' main contributions are: (1) a formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the types of atomic patterns and their compositions, (2) an unsupervised learning approach that explicitly optimizes the posterior probability of both the structure and the parameters of the grammar, and (3) a unified manner of inducing And-Or fragments of the grammar, which avoids or alleviates the problems associated with previous approaches.

The paper relates to previous work on stochastic grammars, including stochastic context-free grammars and decomposable sum-product networks. The authors also draw connections to natural language processing, image parsing, and event recognition. The proposed approach is distinct from previous work in that it learns both the structure and parameters of the grammar in an unsupervised manner, without relying on manual specification of a fixed grammar structure or heuristics for structure learning.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors provide a clear and concise formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The proposed learning approach is well-motivated and grounded in a solid theoretical framework.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach in learning event grammars and image grammars, with competitive or better performance compared to previous approaches.

However, there are also some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationships between stochastic And-Or grammars and other grammatical models, such as probabilistic context-free grammars and hierarchical Bayesian models.
* The authors could provide more insights into the choice of the sparsity prior and its impact on the learning results.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to learning stochastic And-Or grammars, which is distinct from previous work in the field. The authors' use of And-Or fragments and the formulation of the posterior gain are new and innovative.
