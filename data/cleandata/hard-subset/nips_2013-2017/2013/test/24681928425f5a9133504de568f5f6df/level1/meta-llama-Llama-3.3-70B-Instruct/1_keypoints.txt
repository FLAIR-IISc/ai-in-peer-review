- Presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an unsupervised method to learn their structure and parameters.  
- Proposes a novel algorithm that iteratively induces compositions and reconfigurations to optimize the grammar posterior.  
- Evaluated on event and image grammars, achieving comparable or better performance than prior methods.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and thorough algorithm explanation.  
- Strength: unified formalization applicable to different data types.  
- Strength: efficient and robust algorithm for joint structure and parameter learning.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Weakness: algorithm complexity and scalability may be problematic for large datasets or complex grammars.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter choices (e.g., sparsity prior) may require careful, potentially time-consuming tuning.  
- Weakness: relies on a context-free grammar assumption that may not suit all applications.  
- Pro acceptance: novel, significant contribution with thorough evaluation and clear presentation.  
- Con acceptance: concerns about complexity/scalability, limited applicability for more complex grammars, and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Recommendation: accept, but encourage authors to address scalability and hyperparameter concerns.
