- Proposes a unified framework for unsupervised learning of stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs) that jointly learns structure and parameters.  
- Introduces iterative induction of compositions and reconfigurations via “And-Or fragments” optimized by posterior probability.  
- Addresses prior limitation of relying on pre-defined grammar structures by learning them automatically.  
- Well-written, well-organized paper with accessible math and helpful explanation of And-Or fragments.  
- Algorithmic efficiency improved by leveraging sufficient statistics and an effective posterior-gain formulation.  
- Experiments on event and image data show competitive or superior performance, especially on complex event data.  
- Supplementary material provides additional supporting details and acknowledges limitations.  
- Missing discussion connecting to probabilistic programming and modern deep generative models (e.g., VAEs, normalizing flows).  
- Lacks detailed ablation studies, such as quantifying joint vs. separate learning of And-Or fragments.  
- Insufficient analysis of algorithm scalability with respect to data complexity and grammar size.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel joint structure/parameter learning, principled And-Or fragments, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited broader related-work discussion, missing ablations, and unclear scalability analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept.
