Summary and relation to prior work
The paper presents a unified, context-free formulation of stochastic And-Or Grammars (AOGs) and an unsupervised algorithm that jointly learns grammar structure and parameters from unannotated data. Key ideas:
- Start from a trivial grammar that exactly memorizes the training samples.
- Iteratively induce And-Or fragments: each fragment introduces one new And-node A with an And-rule A→O1…On and sibling Or-nodes Oi→aij that map to existing nodes. This unifies composition (And) and reconfiguration (Or) induction.
- Optimize a sparsity-regularized posterior using Viterbi likelihood. The marginal likelihood gain of adding a fragment is computed from sufficient statistics under an unambiguity assumption via an n-gram tensor (configurations) and a context matrix (surrounding patterns), with a simple prior based on grammar size.
- Experiments on event grammars (temporal relations: followed-by/co-occurs) and image grammars (spatial relations: relative positions) show comparable or better performance than prior work on event AOGs [18] and image AOGs [17].

This work extends grammar induction ideas from NLP (e.g., EM, Viterbi training, model merging, and biclustering: [11–16, 20, 21]) to AOGs used in computer vision and activity understanding [1,3,7,17,18]. It is related to SPNs [10] and to structure learning for deep probabilistic models; a comparison to structure learning in SPNs (e.g., Gens & Domingos, ICML 2013) would further contextualize the contributions.

Strengths
- Clear, unified AOG formalization agnostic to modality; the same learning framework applies to images and events.
- Joint induction of composition and reconfiguration via And-Or fragments is elegant and addresses common failure modes when learning And and Or parts separately (data sparsity, redundancy).
- Principled objective: maximize a posterior that balances fit (Viterbi likelihood) and parsimony (size prior), with closed-form Or-rule probabilities within a fragment.
- Efficient evaluation of fragment gain using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensor and context matrix); the factorization into coherence measures provides intuition about context-freeness support in data.
- Solid empirical evidence: improvements over prior AOG structure learners [17,18] on both synthetic and real datasets; cross-validation and multiple random restarts are used.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Dependence on unambiguity: The derivation of the posterior gain assumes grammar unambiguity. While Viterbi training and the final top-level Or conversion push toward unambiguous parses, real AOGs (especially for images) are often ambiguous. Please assess sensitivity to ambiguity: report how often Viterbi parses change across iterations, or provide an ablation using full likelihood (even on small subsets).
- Prior specification: The size prior uses α and coding costs sa, so, but the coding scheme is not rigorously justified. An MDL-style derivation (e.g., explicit description lengths for node identities, relations, and parameters) or sensitivity analyses over α, sa, so would strengthen the claim that the posterior balances fit and complexity.
- Relation learning vs. specification: Although the framework is modality-agnostic, experiments fix relation types (temporal “followed-by”/“co-occurs”, spatial relative positions). It remains unclear how relation families and their parameterizations are chosen and whether they too can be learned (e.g., multiple spatial relation templates). Clarify how relation parameters (energy terms) are estimated, and whether mixtures of relation types within the same And-node are supported.
- Search details and complexity: The method relies on greedy/beam search over a large space of candidate fragments, yet the candidate generation and pruning are only sketched. Please include:
  - Beam width, restart strategy, and stopping criteria.
  - Complexity analysis in terms of vocabulary size, n, and number of relation types.
  - Empirical runtime vs. dataset size and n; how large can n be in practice?
- Evaluation breadth:
  - Events: The test set is small (6 videos); provide confidence intervals. Compare against more recent event parsing methods and report detection precision/recall per class. Since you modify the top-level structure (convert And to Or) for multi-event videos, discuss any mismatch between training and testing objectives.
  - Images: Perplexity is useful but depends on smoothing; specify the smoothing scheme. Consider adding qualitative parse visualizations and, if possible, part discovery metrics or parsing accuracy on synthetic data where ground truth parse trees exist. Comparing to SPN structure learning baselines would be informative given the conceptual links to AOGs/SPNs.
- The claim that And-Or fragments alone can construct any context-free AOG is interesting; a proof sketch or intuition would help (or move this to the supplement with a pointer in the main text).
- Notation and clarity: The likelihood gain formula is dense; a worked toy example would aid understanding. Define “context” more precisely for images/events. Figures 1–3 contain helpful schematics but are hard to read; consider simplifying or enlarging.

Quality
The approach is technically sound overall, with a principled objective and efficient statistics for greedy search. However, key assumptions (unambiguity, fixed relation families) and the simplicity of the prior merit deeper discussion and sensitivity studies. Experimental methodology is generally appropriate but could be broadened and better documented.

Clarity
The paper is well organized, with clear motivation and a coherent algorithmic narrative. Some derivations and implementation details are deferred; pointers to the supplement should be more explicit. Figures could be clearer.

Originality
Combining And and Or induction into a single fragment-based move with analytically computable posterior gain is novel and addresses practical weaknesses of prior separate searches. The unified, modality-agnostic formalization is also a useful contribution.

Significance
Learning compact, compositional, and reconfigurable grammars from raw data is important for vision and activity analysis. The reported gains over prior AOG learners and the cross-domain applicability indicate potential impact. Broader comparisons and analysis would further solidify significance.

Pros for acceptance
- Unified AOG formulation and a practical, general-purpose unsupervised structure learner.
- Novel And-Or fragment idea with efficient gain computation from sufficient statistics.
- Empirical improvements over relevant baselines on events and images.
- Conceptual link between likelihood gain and tensor/matrix coherence adds insight.

Cons for acceptance
- Reliance on an unambiguity assumption; limited analysis of its validity and impact.
- Prior design and hyperparameters not rigorously justified; limited sensitivity analysis.
- Limited baselines and relatively small-scale evaluations; missing comparisons to SPN structure learning and other modern structure learners.
- Some implementation and relation-learning details are under-specified.

Overall assessment
A solid and promising contribution that advances unsupervised structure learning for AOGs with a clean, general framework and encouraging results. With stronger analysis of assumptions, clearer specification of the prior and search, and broader empirical comparisons, this paper would be a strong NIPS contribution. I lean toward acceptance, contingent on addressing the noted issues.
