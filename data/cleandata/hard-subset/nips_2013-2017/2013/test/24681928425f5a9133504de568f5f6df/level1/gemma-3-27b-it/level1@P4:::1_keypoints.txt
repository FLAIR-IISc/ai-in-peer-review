- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of Stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAGs) via iterative And-Or fragments optimizing posterior probability.  
- Offers a unified formalization of SAGs applicable to diverse data modalities (events and images).  
- Introduces And-Or fragments to jointly learn compositions and reconfigurations, with theoretical grounding (tensor coherence measures).  
- Connects the approach to existing probabilistic models (e.g., sum-product networks) and prior stochastic grammar work.  
- Demonstrates competitive or superior empirical performance on synthetic and real event/image datasets.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presents methodology and experiments.  
- Detailed algorithm description—particularly likelihood gain computation—is complex and hard to follow without supplementary material.  
- Performance shows sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., α); more thorough sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Baseline comparisons are limited because some prior methods cannot use the same atomic-action features; more comparable baselines are needed.  
- Scalability to much larger datasets and more complex grammars is not sufficiently discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled approach, demonstrated effectiveness, clear presentation, tackles a real challenge in SAGs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reproducibility concerns from complex description, hyperparameter and baseline limitations, unclear scalability.  
- Overall assessment: significant, theoretically sound contribution with promising results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify algorithm, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, and discuss scalability and baselines.
