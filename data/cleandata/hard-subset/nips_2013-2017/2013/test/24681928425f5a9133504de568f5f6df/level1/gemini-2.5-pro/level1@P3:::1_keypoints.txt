- Proposes a general, unsupervised method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars, applicable across data types (events, images).
- Uses an iterative bottom-up algorithm that starts from a trivial memorizing grammar and progressively adds "And-Or fragments" capturing compositional And-rules and alternative Or-rules.
- Fragment search is guided by maximizing the grammar posterior P(G|X), combining a Viterbi likelihood term for data fit and a prior that penalizes grammar complexity.
- Key technical contribution: efficient computation of posterior gain for adding a fragment using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors and context matrices).
- Builds on prior stochastic And-Or grammar work and differs from methods that only learn parameters for a fixed structure.
- Claims improved robustness to data scarcity and greater efficiency by jointly learning compositions and reconfigurations via And-Or fragments versus separate/heuristic approaches.
- Strength — principled Bayesian framework: objective naturally balances model complexity and data fidelity; And-Or fragments are a novel, unifying atomic unit.
- Strength — generality: same algorithm successfully applied to both temporal human-activity grammars and spatial image grammars.
- Strength — technical soundness and efficiency: derivation of posterior gain and use of sufficient statistics enable practical greedy/beam search over many candidates.
- Strength — strong empirical validation: state-of-the-art or superior performance on event and image datasets, including reduced perplexity and good results on synthetic event data.
- Weakness — local search: reliance on greedy/beam search (with unspecified random restarts) risks getting stuck in local optima; search heuristics/details are underexplored.
- Weakness — approximations/assumptions: use of Viterbi likelihood and assumed grammar unambiguity are significant approximations that may not hold for ambiguous data.
- Weakness — clarity: core technical derivations (Section 3.3) are dense and deferred to supplementary material; connections to tensor/matrix coherence are under-explained.
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: key prior hyperparameter α and encoding costs (sa, so) are fixed in experiments without analysis of selection or sensitivity.
- Overall evaluation: high quality, good clarity with dense parts, high originality and significance; strong recommendation for acceptance.
