- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs) by iteratively inducing compositions and reconfigurations from a trivial grammar.
- Optimizes posterior probability to jointly learn grammar structure and parameters, addressing a key challenge in grammar learning.
- Introduces "And-Or fragment" learning that unifies composition and reconfiguration as the core technical innovation.
- Provides theoretical justification using sufficient statistics and claims efficiency gains over separate approaches.
- Presents a general formalization of SAGs applicable to diverse data types (images, events).
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets shows competitive or superior performance to existing methods.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear explanation of concepts and algorithms.
- Algorithm description is complex (likelihood gain calculation, n-gram tensors), making practical scalability unclear.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (sparsity prior α) is insufficiently analyzed and its impact on results is not explored.
- Comparisons to prior work, especially image grammar methods, are limited and lack statistical significance reporting.
- Experimental setup differences (feature extraction, quantization) versus baselines are not fully detailed.
- Lacks qualitative analysis or visualizations of learned grammars to provide intuition about discovered structures.
- Mentions connection to sum-product networks (SPNs) but does not fully explore potential benefits or techniques from SPN literature.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a hard unsupervised structure-learning problem with a novel, principled approach and promising empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about algorithm complexity/scalability, insufficient hyperparameter analysis, limited comparisons, and underdeveloped SPN connection.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions addressing hyperparameter discussion, more detailed comparisons, and added qualitative visualizations.
