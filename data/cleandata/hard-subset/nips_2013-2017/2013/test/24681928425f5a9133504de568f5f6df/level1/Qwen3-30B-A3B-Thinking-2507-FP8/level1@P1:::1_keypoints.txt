- Unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to data type and learns structure and parameters unsupervised.  
- Introduces "And-Or fragments" to jointly learn compositions (And-rules) and reconfigurations (Or-rules), improving efficiency and robustness.  
- Extends prior image/event grammar and NLP structure-learning work, formalizing AOGs as a context-free extension of SCFGs.  
- Technical contribution: derives efficient sufficient statistics for computing likelihood and prior gains during grammar learning.  
- Empirical results: competitive or superior performance on event (human activity videos) and image (synthetic and real animal faces) domains.  
- Ablation study demonstrates the importance of grounding grammars to atomic patterns and compares three application methods.  
- Minor quality concern: assumes one pattern per training sample, which complicates videos with multiple unrelated events (authors propose a modification).  
- Clarity: well-written and organized with clear formalization and helpful figures; some detailed derivations are relegated to supplementary material.  
- Originality: novel unification of composition and reconfiguration learning via And-Or fragments and a data-agnostic theoretical formulation.  
- Significance: advances unsupervised structure learning for grammars with broad applicability and practical utility.  
- Strengths: unified formalization, novel fragments, efficient computations, thorough evaluation, strong baseline comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: unclear scalability to very large datasets/complex grammars; limited discussion of single-pattern assumption; limited comparison to sum-product networks; greedy search local-optima handling underexplained.  
- Recommendation: strong accept; meets criteria for high-quality NIPS research.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss scaling properties, expand comparison to sum-product networks, detail how greedy search avoids local optima, and address single-pattern limitation in the main text.
