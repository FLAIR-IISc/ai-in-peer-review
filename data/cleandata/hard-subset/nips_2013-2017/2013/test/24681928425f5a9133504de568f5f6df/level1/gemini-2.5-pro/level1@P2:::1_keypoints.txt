- Proposes an unsupervised, data-agnostic algorithm to learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or Grammars (AOGs) from unannotated data.  
- Introduces a unified formalization of context-free AOGs emphasizing compositionality (And) and reconfigurability (Or).  
- Core contribution: bottom-up learning that iteratively adds "And-Or fragments" unifying And-rule and Or-rule discovery into a single step.  
- Uses a greedy search to select fragments that maximize grammar posterior defined by a Viterbi likelihood term and a complexity prior.  
- Develops an efficient posterior-gain computation using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors and context matrices) to avoid full re-parsing.  
- Demonstrates empirical validation on event grammars from videos and image grammars for animal faces, showing comparable or superior performance to prior methods.  
- Technical quality: method is well-founded in a Bayesian framework and the Viterbi approximation is reasonably justified.  
- Key technical contribution (posterior-gain computation) is well-explained in the paper, with full derivation in the supplement.  
- Insightful connection between likelihood gain and tensor/matrix coherence measures.  
- Experiments are thorough, use multiple domains and datasets, and show substantial improvements over recent baselines.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and accessible with helpful figures and algorithm descriptions.  
- Novelty: the "And-Or fragment" concept is a significant and original unification of compositional and alternative structure learning.  
- Additional originality in presenting a domain-agnostic AOG learning framework beyond prior domain-specific methods.  
- Significance: advances the practicality of AOGs by addressing structure learning, with potential applicability to other domains (NLP, biology).  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, significant idea with strong empirical results, principled Bayesian formulation, generality, and clear presentation.  
- Argument against: reliance on greedy/beam search with no global optimality guarantee and limited discussion of local minima risk.  
- Argument against: crucial posterior-gain derivation is relegated to the supplement, hindering full verification from the main paper.  
- Argument against: an ad-hoc post-hoc modification in event experiments (converting top-level And-nodes to Or-nodes) feels disconnected from the core framework.  
- Overall verdict: reviewer strongly recommends acceptance, citing overwhelming strengths despite minor weaknesses.
