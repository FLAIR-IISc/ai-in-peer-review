**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes an unsupervised approach to learning stochastic And-Or grammars, which are a type of probabilistic grammar that can model both compositionality and reconfigurability. The authors present a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled. The approach iteratively induces compositions and reconfigurations in a unified manner and optimizes the posterior probability of the grammar. The authors also propose a new type of grammar fragment, called And-Or fragments, which can generate multiple configurations of existing nodes. The approach is evaluated on learning event grammars and image grammars, and the results show that it achieves comparable or better performance than previous approaches.
