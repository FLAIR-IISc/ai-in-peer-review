- Paper formalizes stochastic And-Or grammars in a way agnostic to atomic pattern types and compositions.  
- Proposes an unsupervised learning approach that jointly optimizes grammar structure and parameters via posterior probability.  
- Introduces a unified method for inducing And-Or fragments that avoids issues of prior approaches.  
- Connects to prior work on stochastic grammars, sum-product networks, NLP, image parsing, and event recognition.  
- Distinct contribution: learns both structure and parameters in an unsupervised manner without manual grammar specification or heuristics.  
- Strength: clear and concise formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Strength: learning approach is well-motivated and grounded in solid theoretical framework.  
- Strength: experiments show effective learning of event and image grammars with competitive or superior performance.  
- Weakness: needs more detailed discussion of relationships to other grammatical models (e.g., PCFGs, hierarchical Bayesian models).  
- Weakness: lacks insight into choice and impact of the sparsity prior on learning outcomes.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive with more datasets and state-of-the-art comparisons.  
- Originality: presents a novel unsupervised approach using And-Or fragments and posterior gain formulation.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and has potential impact across vision, NLP, and robotics.
