- Proposes a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an unsupervised method to learn their structure and parameters.
- Approach is an iterative bottom-up algorithm that starts from a trivial grammar and adds intermediate nonterminals to raise posterior probability.
- Claims to generalize previous structure learning work, but in practice appears to be a restrictive special case that makes the problem tractable.
- The basic algorithm is sensible and empirically performs well compared to other grammar induction methods.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including event grammars and image grammars, and tests on a real animal-face dataset.
- Results: competitive with prior methods on the first dataset and superior on the more complex second dataset.
- Paper is technically sound, with theoretical analysis and experiments that support the claims.
- Well-written, well-organized, and contains sufficient detail for an expert to reproduce results.
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques, clearly distinguished from prior work with adequate citations.
- Significance: advances the state of the art and is likely to be useful to practitioners and researchers.
- Limitations: needs more comparisons to other methods (notably Stolcke and Omohundro, ref. 14) to provide a stronger baseline.
- Limitation: lacks deeper analysis of trade-offs between using And-Or fragments versus pure And- or Or-fragments.
- Overall recommendation: accept — solid contribution that addresses a difficult problem effectively.
