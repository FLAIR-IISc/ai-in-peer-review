- Unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that integrates structure and parameter learning and is agnostic to data type.
- Algorithm uses And-Or fragments to jointly learn compositions and reconfigurations, addressing data scarcity and ambiguity.
- Strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world event and image datasets, often outperforming prior approaches.
- Paper is well-written and organized, with helpful figures and examples.

- Claim of generalizing prior work is overstated and does not fully subsume methods like Stolcke and Omohondro's.
- Experimental comparisons are limited, notably missing Stolcke and Omohondro's grammar induction as a baseline.
- Scalability to larger datasets or more complex grammars is not thoroughly discussed or demonstrated.

- Novel and valuable contribution likely to inspire further research in grammar induction.
- Method is technically sound and shows strong empirical results.
- Addresses a challenging problem and advances applicability of AOGs.

- Lack of key comparisons and insufficiently substantiated generalization claims weaken the submission.

- Recommendation: accept, but temper generalization claims and include additional comparisons in future versions.
