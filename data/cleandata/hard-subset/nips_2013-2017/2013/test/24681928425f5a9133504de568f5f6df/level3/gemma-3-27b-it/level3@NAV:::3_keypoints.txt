- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for learning stochastic And-Or grammars by inducing unified AND-OR fragments.
- Core idea: compute fragment values efficiently using sufficient statistics, building on existing grammar induction work.
- Method is technically sound, well-motivated, and extends prior natural-language grammar work to event and visual parsing.
- Demonstrates versatility by applying the framework to both event parsing and visual object parsing.
- Promises improved robustness in data-scarce settings via unified AND/OR fragment induction.
- Significant contribution: efficient computation using sufficient statistics.
- Experimental presentation is severely lacking and hard to follow.
- Paper assumes familiarity with S.C. Zhu’s group work, making it inaccessible to outsiders.
- Missing crucial experimental details: data representation, parameter settings, and full experimental setup.
- Results are mainly quantitative with no qualitative visualizations of learned structures.
- Unclear whether the “surrogate measure” for likelihood gain is exact or approximate.
- Ambiguity about arity limitations and whether the method is restricted to Chomsky Normal Form.
- Fails to convincingly show that it learns more interesting structure than competing methods; comparable performance alone is insufficient.
- Recommendation: reject unless revised with expanded supplementary material, concrete examples of learned structures, and stronger comparative experiments.
