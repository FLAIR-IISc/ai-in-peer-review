- Proposes optimistic concurrency control (OCC) as a middle ground between strict concurrency and coordination-free parallelism for distributed ML.  
- Demonstrates OCC on clustering, feature learning, and online facility location.  
- Paper is technically sound with a clear, well-structured presentation.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated paradigm for distributed machine learning.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of correctness and scalability.  
- Strength: well-organized with sufficient background and related work references.  
- Results are significant, showing potential for high parallelism without sacrificing correctness.  
- Weakness: experimental section is unclear and hard to understand for readers unfamiliar with related visual-grammar work.  
- Weakness: results presented only quantitatively; lacks qualitative demonstrations of learned structure.  
- Suggestion: provide more intuition for why OCC suits ML algorithms.  
- Suggestion: explain plans to extend OCC to more complex models and tasks.  
- Suggestion: give more implementation and experimental setup details and concrete illustrations.  
- Suggestion: address potential limitations such as hyperparameter tuning and other challenges.  
- Overall: strong contribution that would benefit from revisions to clarify experiments and show qualitative results.
