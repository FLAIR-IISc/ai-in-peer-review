- Proposes a unified, data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Introduces an unsupervised algorithm that jointly learns structure and parameters via iterative And-Or fragment introduction.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on event grammar and image grammar tasks with competitive results.  
- Key innovation: use of And-Or fragments to efficiently search for promising grammar rules.  
- Able to learn recursive stochastic grammars in a tractable way, modeling complex patterns.  
- Technical presentation is clear, well-organized, and notation/definitions are consistent.  
- Experimental results are well-presented with thorough discussion.  
- Original contribution with potential applications to NLP, vision, and robotics.  
- Major limitation: does not address algorithmic tractability or scalability to large datasets (thousands of datapoints).  
- Lacks experiments on natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank) and comparisons to prior work like Klein & Manning.  
- Reviewer requests more experiments overall, especially on natural language data.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing scalability concerns and adding NLP experiments.
