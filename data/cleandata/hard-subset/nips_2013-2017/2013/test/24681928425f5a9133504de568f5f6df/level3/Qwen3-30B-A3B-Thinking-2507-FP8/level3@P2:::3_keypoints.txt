- Presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters across data types (images, events).  
- Key innovation: learns combined "And-Or fragments" to capture compositions and reconfigurations, avoiding separate searches for And- and Or-fragments.  
- Method is technically sound and data-type-agnostic, unifying prior language, image, and event grammar work.  
- Efficiently computes posterior gain of adding fragments using sufficient statistics, making learning computationally feasible.  
- Empirical results show competitive or superior performance on event and image grammar learning tasks versus prior approaches.  
- The likelihood-gain derivation (Section 3.3) is unclear—uncertain whether the "surrogate measure" is exact or an approximation.  
- Unclear whether the method generalizes to fragments with arity >2 or is constrained to Chomsky normal form, limiting clarity about generality.  
- Experimental sections lack clarity: event data representation (vector sequences, context matrix, ID sequence differences) is hard to follow without prior-work familiarity.  
- Image experiments provide no concrete examples or visualizations of learned grammars, hindering assessment of meaningfulness.  
- Originality: novel unified approach to jointly learn compositions and reconfigurations, addressing limitations of prior separate-search methods.  
- Significance: important for unsupervised compositional modeling across domains; unified framework is practically useful.  
- Strengths: well-motivated unified approach, solid technical contributions, efficient fragment evaluation, demonstrated across two domains.  
- Weaknesses: opaque experimental presentation, lack of qualitative examples, and unclear theoretical derivations and method limitations.  
- Pro: important problem, novel and sound approach with promising empirical results.  
- Con: clarity issues, missing qualitative illustrations, and unresolved theoretical/arity limitations reduce accessibility and impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions—authors should clarify derivations, state arity limitations, expand experimental details, and include qualitative/visual examples and supplemental material.
