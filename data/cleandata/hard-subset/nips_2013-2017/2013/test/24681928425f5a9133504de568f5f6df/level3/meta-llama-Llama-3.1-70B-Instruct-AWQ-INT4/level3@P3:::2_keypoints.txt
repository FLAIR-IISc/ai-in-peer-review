- Proposes a unified, type-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Introduces an optimization procedure that jointly maximizes the posterior over grammar structure and parameters.  
- Presents a unified method for learning both compositions and reconfigurations via And-Or fragments.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides thorough background and problem framing.  
- Algorithm framework and And-Or fragment design are described in detail.  
- Experiments show competitive or superior performance on event-grammar and image-grammar tasks.  
- Analysis discusses advantages and limitations of the approach.  
- Does not address algorithmic tractability or scalability, a major practical concern.  
- Lacks experiments on natural language data, an important application domain for stochastic grammars.  
- Relies on heuristics (e.g., greedy and beam search) that may yield suboptimal solutions.  
- Insufficient detail on implementation specifics and computational resource requirements.  
- Some sections (e.g., And-Or fragment descriptions) would benefit from more diagrams or illustrations.  
- Novel contribution: use of And-Or fragments and joint structure+parameter learning is original and significant.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggested fixes to scalability discussion, implementation details, and additional NLP experiments.
