- Presents an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars from unannotated data.  
- Proposes an iterative algorithm that starts from a trivial grammar and incrementally adds fragments by optimizing the posterior probability.  
- Introduces “And-Or fragments” to unify learning of compositions and reconfigurations, improving efficiency and robustness over separate learning.  
- Evaluated on event and image grammar tasks, showing competitive or superior performance to existing methods.  
- Positions the work within prior stochastic grammar literature and connects to sum-product networks and unsupervised induction approaches.  
- Claims to generalize previous structure-learning methods but is more accurately a specialized, effective application for And-Or grammars.  
- Strength: principled posterior-probability optimization framework for structure learning.  
- Strength: technically sound algorithm with clear presentation and sufficient detail for replication.  
- Strength: And-Or fragments are a novel idea that help when data are scarce.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons are limited and omit a key baseline (Stolcke & Omohondro’s Bayesian model merging).  
- Weakness: unclear scalability to more complex datasets or grammars with large branching factors.  
- Weakness: assumptions behind approximating likelihood with the Viterbi likelihood are not fully justified.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include the missing baseline and add discussion of limitations and scalability.
