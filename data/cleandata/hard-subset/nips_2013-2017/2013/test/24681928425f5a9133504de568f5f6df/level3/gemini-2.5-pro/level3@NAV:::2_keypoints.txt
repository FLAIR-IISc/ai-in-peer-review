- Introduces a novel "And-Or fragment" for jointly learning compositional (AND) and reconfigurable (OR) grammar structure.
- Greedy iterative algorithm that optimizes the grammar posterior, balancing data likelihood with a sparsity-inducing prior.
- Builds on Bayesian model merging and extends And-Or grammar formalisms with a clear technical formulation.
- Empirically strong results on event and image grammar learning; paper is well-written and framework is clearly explained.
- Claims of tractability and scalability are not supported by formal complexity analysis or empirical scaling experiments.
- Experimental scope is limited: no experiments on natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank), preventing comparison to major prior NLP work.
- Conclusion: promising and technically sound approach, but contributions are weakened by insufficient evaluation.
- For acceptance: novel unified fragment learning and compelling vision-task results.
- Against acceptance: unsubstantiated tractability claims and missing NLP experiments; recommend adding complexity analysis and standard NLP benchmarks.
