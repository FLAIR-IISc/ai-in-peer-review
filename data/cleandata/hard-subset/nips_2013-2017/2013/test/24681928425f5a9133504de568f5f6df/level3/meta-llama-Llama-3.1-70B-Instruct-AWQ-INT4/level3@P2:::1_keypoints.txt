- Presents a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters.  
- Starts from a trivial grammar and iteratively induces compositions and reconfigurations by optimizing grammar posterior.  
- Extends prior structure-learning work for language and improves recent And-Or grammar structure learning for images/events.  
- Thorough related-work review and clear explanation of differences from existing methods.  
- Clear, concise presentation and well-organized writing.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation showing comparable or better results on event and image grammars.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness and reproducibility for expert readers.  
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis supporting the claims.  
- Original contribution: novel formulation and posterior-based optimization, though And-Or fragments idea itself is not new.  
- Significant potential impact for computer vision and NLP.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation scope is limited and lacks comprehensive comparisons to other grammar induction methods.  
- Specific missing baseline: comparison to Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14) recommended.  
- Weakness: claim of "generalizing" prior work is misleading; method is a tractable special case and should be stated as such.  
- Paper lacks clear discussion of limitations and potential extensions.  
- Overall: solid contribution, well-executed, and deserving of acceptance despite noted weaknesses.
