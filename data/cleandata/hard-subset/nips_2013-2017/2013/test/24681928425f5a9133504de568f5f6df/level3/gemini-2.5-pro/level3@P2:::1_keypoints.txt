- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars, agnostic to data modality.  
- Uses an iterative bottom-up greedy algorithm that starts from trivial grammar and adds "And-Or fragments" to introduce compositional rules and alternatives.  
- Learning objective maximizes posterior probability combining Viterbi likelihood with a sparsity prior penalizing grammar size.  
- Key technical contribution: efficient posterior-gain computation using sufficient statistics (n-gram tensors and context matrices).  
- Builds on prior grammar induction work (e.g., Stolcke, Omohundro) and And-Or grammar methods in vision, with the main novelty being the "And-Or fragment" as the atomic learning unit.  
- Strength: principled Bayesian framework with clean derivation of posterior gains guiding greedy search.  
- Strength: the "And-Or fragment" effectively and jointly learns compositional structure and alternatives, improving robustness with sparse/ambiguous data.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on event and image grammars, matching or outperforming recent domain-specific methods.  
- Strength: general formulation and successful application to different modalities (temporal events and spatial image patches).  
- Weakness: claim of "generalization" over prior work is overstated—method is a practical specialization that makes the problem tractable.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons should include foundational grammar-induction baselines (e.g., Stolcke & Omohundro) to better contextualize improvements.  
- Weakness: high-level description of the greedy/beam search over sub-tensors lacks concrete details needed for reproducibility.  
- Overall: technically sound, novel atomic learning primitive, practically significant, but needs clearer positioning, broader baselines, and more search-procedure details.
