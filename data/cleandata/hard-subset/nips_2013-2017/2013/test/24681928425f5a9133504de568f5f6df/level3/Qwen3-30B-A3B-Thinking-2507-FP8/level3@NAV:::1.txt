

This paper presents an unsupervised approach for learning the structure of stochastic And-Or grammars, with a unified formalization applicable across different data types (images, events). The authors develop an iterative algorithm starting from a trivial grammar, progressively adding And-Or fragments to optimize the grammar's posterior probability. Unlike previous approaches that learn compositions and reconfigurations separately, their method unifies these aspects, claiming improved efficiency and robustness.

The paper makes several strong contributions. The formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars is well-motivated and agnostic to data types, providing a solid theoretical foundation. The algorithm is technically sound, with careful derivation of sufficient statistics for efficient computation of likelihood gains. The empirical evaluation on event and image grammars demonstrates competitive results, particularly on more complex datasets where the approach outperforms previous methods.

However, two significant concerns must be addressed. First, the claim of "generalizing" previous structure learning work is misleading—the approach is actually a special case of existing methods, as the restriction to And-Or fragments makes the problem tractable but limits the scope of the contribution. Second, the experimental evaluation lacks sufficient comparison to relevant baselines, particularly Stolcke and Omohundro (ref. 14), which would provide a valuable point of reference for the grammar induction community.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and technically rigorous. The quality is strong, with proper theoretical justification and empirical validation. While the significance is limited to a specific class of grammars, the contribution is valuable for this particular domain. The approach represents a sensible improvement over previous methods for learning stochastic And-Or grammar structures.

Overall, this is a solid contribution to the specific problem of learning stochastic And-Or grammar structures. The technical quality is strong, but the paper would be significantly improved by tempering the claims of generalization and including comparisons to Stolcke and Omohundro. With these minor revisions, I recommend acceptance.
