- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that iteratively builds stochastic And-Or grammars by adding unified "And-Or fragments" instead of learning compositions and alternatives separately.  
- Frames learning as posterior maximization combining a Viterbi likelihood term with a sparsity-inducing prior on grammar size.  
- Technical contribution: an efficient method to compute posterior gain from candidate fragments using sufficient statistics and tensor/matrix coherence measures, enabling feasible greedy search.  
- General, data-agnostic formalization evaluated on two domains: event grammars from video and image grammars from synthetic and real images.  
- Builds on prior grammar induction and And-Or grammars work (e.g., Stolcke & Omohundro 1994; Si & Zhu 2013).  
- Strength: addresses fundamental unsupervised structure learning problem with a well-motivated, potentially impactful approach.  
- Strength: technically sound framework with solid theoretical grounding in posterior optimization.  
- Major clarity concern: derivation of likelihood gain is under-explained—unclear whether formula is exact under unambiguity or a surrogate approximation.  
- Major clarity concern: unclear whether the method is limited to fragments of specific arity (e.g., binary rules) or supports general And-rules.  
- Experimental section is hard to follow for outsiders; data preprocessing and conversion to terminal sequences are not sufficiently described.  
- For image experiments, relying on prior paper [17] for atomization makes the setup non–self-contained.  
- Lack of qualitative results and visualizations of learned grammar fragments prevents assessment of whether discovered structures are meaningful.  
- Presentation seems tailored to a niche audience and is not accessible to a general ML readership.  
- Recommendation: promising but not ready for publication; lean toward rejection unless major clarifications and additions are made.  
- Suggested improvements: make experiments self-contained, include concrete qualitative examples/visualizations, clarify likelihood derivation and fragment scope, and provide extensive supplementary material for reproducibility.
