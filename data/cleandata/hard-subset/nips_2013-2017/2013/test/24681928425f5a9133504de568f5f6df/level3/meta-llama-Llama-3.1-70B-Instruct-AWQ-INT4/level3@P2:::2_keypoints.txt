- Proposes a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an unsupervised method to learn their structure and parameters.
- Builds on prior stochastic-grammar work for images, events, and natural language structure learning.
- Main contribution: joint unsupervised algorithm that iteratively introduces And-Or fragments and measures likelihood and prior gains to optimize posterior grammar.
- Algorithm induces compositions and reconfigurations in a unified framework for structure and parameter learning.
- Experiments on event and image grammars show competitiveness on events and improved performance on images versus prior methods.
- Strength: unified formalism and effective unsupervised learning approach demonstrated on two tasks.
- Weakness: paper does not address algorithmic tractability or scalability to large datasets (e.g., thousands of datapoints).
- Weakness: no experiments on natural language data despite claims about modeling recursive phenomena.
- Recommendation: include experiments on NLP datasets (e.g., Penn Treebank) and comparisons to prior work like Klein & Manning.
- Clarity: well-written and easy to follow, but key technical derivations (e.g., likelihood gain) are relegated to supplementary material.
- Originality: novel use of And-Or fragments and likelihood/prior gain measurements for structure learning.
- Significance: potential impact for ML/NLP/image recognition/event detection if scaled and validated further.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, effective experimental results, and potential broad impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of scalability analysis, missing natural-language experiments, and insufficient technical details/experiments.
