- Proposes unsupervised learning of both structure and parameters for Stochastic And-Or Grammars (SAGs).
- Provides a unified formalization of SAGs and an iterative learning method that induces AND-OR fragments to optimize a posterior.
- Core contribution is simultaneous structure and parameter learning, using AND-OR fragments to improve efficiency and robustness.
- Introduces a likelihood-gain formulation based on sufficient statistics to make learning more tractable.
- Shows competitive or superior results on event and image grammar learning tasks.
- Builds on prior work in NLP grammar induction and recent SAG applications to visual data.
- Major limitation: no analysis or discussion of algorithmic tractability and scalability as dataset size increases.
- Experiments omit comparisons on standard NLP benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank), undermining state-of-the-art claims.
- Lacks comprehensive ablation studies (e.g., effect of sparsity prior α) and detailed analysis of the learned grammars.
- Needs more experiments on larger and more complex datasets to demonstrate generalizability and scalability.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel joint learning approach, promising AND-OR fragment induction, competitive empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, missing standard-benchmark comparisons, and limited ablation/analysis.
- Overall recommendation: reject as submitted, with encouragement to address scalability and include standard NLP experiments in a revision.
