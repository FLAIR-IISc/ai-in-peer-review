- Proposes an unsupervised method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars by iteratively adding AND-OR fragments optimized for posterior probability with a sparsity prior.  
- Evaluated on event and image grammars, showing competitive or improved performance over existing methods.  
- Builds on prior stochastic grammar and image modeling work, unifying search for compositions and reconfigurations.  
- Technically sound formulation with clear explanation of posterior optimization and AND-OR fragments.  
- Uses sufficient statistics to improve computational efficiency, a valuable implementation contribution.  
- Lacks a thorough analysis of algorithmic complexity and scalability, making tractability claims weak.  
- Generally well-written with consistent notation and helpful figures, but the likelihood gain computation is under-explained.  
- The connection among the n-gram tensor, context matrix, and posterior gain needs clearer exposition and illustrative examples.  
- Novel contributions include unifying AND/OR rule search via AND-OR fragments and a data-agnostic formalization of stochastic AOGs.  
- Efficient posterior gain computation using sufficient statistics is a noteworthy original aspect.  
- Addresses an important unsupervised learning problem and can model compositional/reconfigurable structures, but broader applicability is not demonstrated.  
- Experiments are limited in scope and omit natural language benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank) and more challenging tasks exploring recursion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel unified approach, competitive empirical results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing scalability/tractability analysis, absence of NLP experiments, limited experimental breadth, and insufficient detail on likelihood gain.  
- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage revision to include complexity analysis, experiments on standard NLP datasets, and expanded experimental validation.
