This paper proposes a novel unsupervised method for learning stochastic And-Or grammars, which are powerful models for representing patterns that combine compositionality and reconfigurability. The approach is technically sound and well-motivated, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness on tasks such as event parsing and visual object parsing.

The key contribution of this paper is the introduction of And-Or fragments, which unify the search for new compositions and reconfigurations in the grammar. This allows for more efficient and robust learning of the grammar, and the authors demonstrate that their approach can learn more interesting structures than competing methods.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details of the approach. The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including comparisons to previous approaches and an analysis of the results.

However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved. For example, the supplemental derivations are not provided, and the reviewer could not verify them. Additionally, it is unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation. Furthermore, the method's ability to extend to fragments with arity >2 or its limitation to Chomsky normal form is not clearly explained.

The experimental presentation is also somewhat unclear, and it assumes familiarity with prior work by S. C. Zhu's group. The reviewer had difficulty understanding what was done, how examples were represented, and what was learned. The results are largely quantitative and not very illuminating, and the reviewer would like to see more qualitative examples of interesting structures discovered by the approach.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of unsupervised structure learning. With some revisions to address the areas mentioned above, it has the potential to be an even stronger paper.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel and technically sound approach to learning stochastic And-Or grammars.
* The approach is well-motivated and has the potential to be widely applicable.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on several tasks.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details of the approach.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* Include more qualitative examples of interesting structures discovered by the approach.
