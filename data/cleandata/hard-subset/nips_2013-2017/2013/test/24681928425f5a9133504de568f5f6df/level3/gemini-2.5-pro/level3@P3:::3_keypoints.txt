- Proposes a novel unsupervised bottom-up method that learns unified "And-Or fragments" for stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Starts from an overfitting trivial grammar and iteratively adds fragments to maximize a posterior combining Viterbi likelihood and a sparsity prior.  
- Introduces an efficient technique using pre-computed sufficient statistics to compute posterior gain of adding fragments.  
- Demonstrates domain-agnostic applicability on human activity (temporal) and animal face (spatial) datasets.  
- Original and significant idea addressing data scarcity and search complexity in grammar induction.  
- Principled formulation as greedy search to optimize grammar posterior; technically sound derivation of gain computation.  
- Broad applicability shown by successful results across modalities.  
- Major weakness: experimental descriptions lack critical details (data representation, relation definitions, fragment proposal process) hindering reproducibility.  
- Insufficient qualitative results or visualizations to demonstrate interpretability and meaningfulness of learned structures.  
- Ambiguity about the "surrogate" likelihood gain measure—unclear if exact or an approximation and what guarantees hold.  
- Unclear whether method supports arbitrary arity And-rules or is effectively limited (e.g., CNF-like arity 2–3).  
- Reviewer requests clearer, step-by-step derivations for the likelihood gain in the supplement.  
- Reviewer requests qualitative examples/visualizations of high-scoring fragments (e.g., grouped terminals and composed And-nodes).  
- If accepted, demands extensive supplementary materials: pseudo-code, precise data/relations, hyperparameters, and full experimental setup for reproducibility.  
- Recommendation leans toward acceptance contingent on addressing clarity, reproducibility, and qualitative evaluation concerns in the final version.
