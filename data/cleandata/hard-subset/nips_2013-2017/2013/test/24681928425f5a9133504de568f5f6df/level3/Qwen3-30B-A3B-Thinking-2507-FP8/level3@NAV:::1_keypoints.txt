- Presents an unsupervised method to learn stochastic And-Or grammar structure with a unified formalization across data types (images, events).
- Proposes an iterative algorithm that starts from a trivial grammar and adds And-Or fragments to maximize the grammar posterior.
- Unifies learning of compositions and reconfigurations instead of treating them separately.
- Formalization is well-motivated, data-agnostic, and provides a solid theoretical foundation.
- Algorithm is technically sound with careful derivation of sufficient statistics for efficient likelihood gain computation.
- Empirical results are competitive and show improved performance on more complex datasets.
- The claim of “generalizing” prior structure learning is misleading; the method is a special case due to restriction to And-Or fragments.
- Experimental evaluation lacks key baseline comparisons, notably Stolcke and Omohundro (ref. 14).
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and technically rigorous.
- Contribution is significant within the specific class of grammars but limited in broader generality.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to temper generalization claims and add comparisons to relevant baselines.
