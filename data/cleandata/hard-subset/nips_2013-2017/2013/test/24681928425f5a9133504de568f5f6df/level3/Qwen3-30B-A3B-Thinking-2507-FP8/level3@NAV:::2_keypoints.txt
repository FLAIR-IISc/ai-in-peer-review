- Paper presents a unified formalization of stochastic AND-OR grammars and an unsupervised method that jointly learns grammar structure and parameters.
- Introduces AND-OR fragments to model compositions (AND-rules) and reconfigurations (OR-rules) in a single framework.
- Learning begins from a trivial grammar and iteratively adds fragments to maximize posterior probability using efficient sufficient statistics for likelihood and prior gains.
- Technically sound approach with clear theoretical foundation and well-organized presentation.
- Empirical results on event and image grammars are convincing and competitive.
- Unified treatment of AND-OR fragments is a meaningful advancement over prior work that treated composition and reconfiguration separately.
- Formalization is data-type agnostic, suggesting potential applicability beyond evaluated domains.
- Major weakness: no natural language experiments, despite claims about modeling recursive phenomena.
- Missing comparisons to established NLP benchmarks (e.g., Penn Treebank) and prior NLP work (Klein & Manning).
- Scalability and algorithmic tractability are not adequately addressed; 5,000-sample experiments are insufficient to demonstrate real-world scalability.
- Because natural language—a primary application for stochastic grammars—is unaddressed and scalability is unclear, the paper's significance is limited.
- Recommendation: reject due to critical omission of NLP experiments and insufficient scalability analysis.
