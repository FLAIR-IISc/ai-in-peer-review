- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for stochastic And-Or grammar induction by unifying And-Or fragments and optimizing grammar posterior probability.
- Jointly learns grammar structure and parameters, addressing composition and reconfiguration in a unified framework.
- Evaluated on event parsing and image-grammar tasks, showing competitive or superior performance (improved F-measure and perplexity).
- Achieves efficiency by using sufficient statistics to compute posterior gains for new fragments.

- Strength: Tackles an important problem with a clear improvement over prior separate treatments of structure and parameter learning.
- Strength: Technically sound algorithm with a well-defined posterior objective and efficient sufficient-statistics computation.
- Strength: Empirically validated on synthetic and real datasets with quantitative gains over competing methods.

- Weakness: Key technical details (e.g., the surrogate likelihood-gain measure and higher-arity fragment applicability) are under-explained.
- Weakness: Experimental section assumes prior field knowledge; datasets, metrics, and results lack accessible explanation and qualitative examples.
- Weakness: Paper provides few qualitative illustrations of learned grammars, limiting interpretability and insight.
- Weakness: Needs supplementary materials (detailed derivations, pseudocode, visualizations) to improve reproducibility.

- For acceptance: Novel, technically sound approach that yields efficiency and performance improvements; unified fragment learning is a meaningful contribution.
- Against acceptance: Clarity and experimental accessibility issues plus lack of qualitative examples and supplementary materials limit impact.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, add qualitative insights, and provide supplementary materials.
