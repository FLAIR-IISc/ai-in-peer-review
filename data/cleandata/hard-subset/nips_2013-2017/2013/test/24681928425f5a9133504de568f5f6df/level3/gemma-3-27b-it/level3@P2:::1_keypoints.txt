- Presents a novel iterative algorithm for unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs) that jointly learns grammar structure and parameters by inducing And-Or fragments.  
- Formalizes SAGs clearly and unifies composition and reconfiguration learning via And-Or fragments.  
- Uses posterior probability maximization and sufficient statistics to efficiently compute posterior gain, improving tractability on larger datasets.  
- Demonstrates competitive or superior performance on event and image grammar learning tasks.  
- Well-situated in prior work, citing relevant literature from probabilistic grammars, sum-product networks, and prior image/event grammar learning.  
- Main contribution is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly presented.  
- Claim of “generalization” of previous structure learning work is overstated; method specializes to And-Or grammars and is not fully general.  
- Constraints from focusing on And-Or fragments limit generality, which should be acknowledged more clearly.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited and should include Stolcke and Omohondro’s Bayesian model merging [14] as a strong baseline.  
- Lacks a detailed ablation study to isolate the impact of components (e.g., And-Or fragments vs. separate composition/reconfiguration learning).  
- Needs deeper analysis explaining why the method outperforms others on complex event datasets with subtypes.  
- Some technical sections (likelihood gain computation, connection between context matrix and n-gram tensor) are dense and would benefit from more intuition and simpler examples.  
- More detailed pseudocode and implementation details would improve reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to temper generalization claims and expand experimental comparisons.
