- Novel method for joint unsupervised learning of SAG structure and parameters using iterative introduction of And-Or fragments that unify composition and reconfiguration.  
- Principled optimization framework that maximizes the posterior probability of both grammar structure and parameters.  
- Data-type agnostic formalization of SAGs and derivation of sufficient statistics for efficient computation.  
- Promising empirical results on event and image grammars, with comparable or superior performance to existing methods.  
- Clear mathematical formulation and a well-explained algorithm.  
- No analysis of scalability or computational complexity; unclear behavior on much larger datasets.  
- Missing evaluation on natural language data and lack of empirical comparison to established NLP baselines (e.g., Penn Treebank).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope; needs more datasets, parameter studies, and broader baseline comparisons.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing scalability concerns and ideally adding at least a small NLP evaluation.
