This paper presents a novel approach to unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors propose a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled, and develop an algorithm to learn the structure and parameters of such grammars. The approach is based on inducing unified And-Or fragments, which is a departure from previous methods that search for separate And-rules and Or-rules.

The reviewer finds the problem important, the approach novel and well-motivated, and the algorithm technically sound. However, there are several concerns and suggestions for improvement. Firstly, the reviewer was unable to verify the supplemental derivations and requests clearer derivations and clarifications. Specifically, it is unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation. Additionally, it is unclear whether the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form.

The experimental presentation is also a major concern. The reviewer found it difficult to understand what was done, how examples were represented, and what was learned. The presentation assumes familiarity with prior work from S. C. Zhu's group, making it inaccessible to outsiders. If accepted, the paper should include extensive supplementary material with more experimental details and concrete illustrations.

Furthermore, the reviewer would like to see qualitative examples of interesting structure discovered by the approach. The current results are largely quantitative and not very illuminating. It would be helpful to demonstrate that this approach learns more interesting structure than competing methods.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-motivated approach to unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars. However, the clarity of the paper could be improved, particularly in the experimental presentation. The originality of the paper is high, as it presents a novel approach to learning stochastic And-Or grammars. The significance of the paper is also high, as it addresses an important problem in the field of machine learning and artificial intelligence.

Overall, the reviewer recommends acceptance of the paper, but with the condition that the authors address the concerns and suggestions mentioned above.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars.
* The algorithm is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-motivated approach to learning stochastic And-Or grammars.
