- Proposes a novel unsupervised algorithm that jointly learns structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars via "And-Or fragments."
- Unifies composition and reconfiguration learning, addressing limitations of prior separate approaches.
- Presents an efficient method to evaluate posterior gain of adding fragments using sufficient statistics.
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance on event parsing and image grammar learning tasks, outperforming prior work in some cases.
- Technically well-motivated and sound, with a clear algorithmic formulation.
- Experimental descriptions lack clarity—insufficient detail on event representations (beyond "ID sequences") and image quantization/atomic patterns.
- Supplementary derivations are hard to verify; need clearer, more detailed proofs and explanation of the "surrogate measure" for likelihood gain (exact vs. approximate).
- Scalability to fragments with arity >2 is unclear and may limit expressiveness; potential implicit reliance on Chomsky normal form should be discussed.
- Lacks qualitative analysis and visualizations of learned grammar structures to show what the model actually learns.
- Significant potential impact if clarified, as unsupervised SAG learning would reduce manual grammar specification and broaden applicability.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, efficient computation, and competitive results.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear experiments, insufficient supplementary detail, limited qualitative insight, and unclear scalability.
- Overall: promising contribution but requires major revisions for clarity, supplementary derivations, qualitative examples, and discussion of limitations.
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions.
