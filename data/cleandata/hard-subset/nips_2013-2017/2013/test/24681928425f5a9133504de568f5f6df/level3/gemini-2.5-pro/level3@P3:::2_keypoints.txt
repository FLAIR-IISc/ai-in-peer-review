- Presents an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars by iteratively refining a memorizing grammar with "And-Or fragments."  
- And-Or fragments unify learning of compositional (And) and configurational (Or) structure, allowing joint discovery of composition and alternatives.  
- Fragment selection is guided by maximizing grammar posterior: a Viterbi likelihood term plus a complexity-penalizing prior.  
- The posterior gain is expressed via sufficient statistics (n-gram tensor and context matrix), enabling an efficient greedy search procedure.  
- Method is formalized in a data-agnostic way and tested on event grammars from video and image grammars from synthetic and real images.  
- Empirically competitive or superior results reported on both video event and image grammar tasks.  
- Novelty: unified search for composition and reconfiguration contrasts with prior split approaches (e.g., model merging/splitting, And-Or grammar work).  
- Strength: technically sound, well-motivated objective balancing fit and complexity, and clear presentation with effective illustrations.  
- Weakness: no formal analysis of computational complexity or scalability; search for best fragment is presented without rigorous runtime characterization.  
- Weakness: no experiments on natural language data despite motivation from NLP and claims about learning recursive phenomena.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope—datasets are small/specific; larger and more complex evaluations are needed to demonstrate generality.
