- Proposes a novel iterative algorithm that jointly learns structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Introduces And-Or fragments and optimizes posterior probability using a tractable formulation based on sufficient statistics.  
- Provides a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars applicable across different data types.  
- Extends prior work from NLP and sum-product networks to more general compositional and reconfigurable data.  
- Empirical results on event and image grammars show competitive performance compared to existing methods.  
- Theoretical strengths include posterior optimization, efficient computation of likelihood gains, and a general hierarchical learning framework.  
- Well-articulated connection to existing NLP concepts like unambiguous grammars.  
- Major weakness: insufficient discussion of algorithmic tractability and scalability to large datasets.  
- Unclear how the method performs with thousands of training examples or highly complex learned grammars.  
- Major omission: no experiments on standard NLP datasets (e.g., Penn Treebank) or comparisons to established unsupervised parsing methods like Klein & Manning.  
- Limited experimental scope and potentially insufficient evaluation metrics to fully assess grammar learning quality.  
- Presentation issues: occasional gap between mathematical formulation and intuitive explanation; Figure 1 needs clearer labeling/explanations.  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on addressing scalability concerns and adding a brief NLP experiment/comparison.  
- Suggests authors clarify computational complexity and potential optimizations in a revision.
