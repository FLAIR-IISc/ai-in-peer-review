- Proposes an unsupervised structure-learning algorithm for stochastic And-Or grammars that induces unified And-Or fragments across modalities (events and images).  
- Optimizes a posterior combining Viterbi likelihood and a sparsity prior, using greedy/beam search.  
- Key technical contribution: evaluate posterior gain of inserting a fragment efficiently via sufficient statistics (n-gram tensor and context co-occurrence matrix).  
- Claims avoid repeated re-parsing by using the sufficient-statistics-based gain computation.  
- Experiments on event parsing and visual object parsing show competitive or improved quantitative performance over prior work.  
- Builds on unsupervised grammar induction and prior AOG structure-learning, improving by unifying And/Or induction into fragments.  
- Connects to decomposable sum-product networks and stochastic context-free grammars; cross-domain formalization of context-free AOGs is useful.  
- Novelty: unified fragment induction and sufficient-statistics fragment valuation are interesting advances.  
- Problem is important and the high-level approach is technically sound and grounded in prior evidence.  
- Derivation of the likelihood-gain factorization and unambiguity assumptions are deferred to supplement and hard to verify from the main text.  
- Unclear whether the surrogate measure is exact or an approximation; counting conventions and when duplicates are merged need clarification.  
- Complexity and scalability claims lack explicit bounds for tensor/context construction and candidate enumeration as functions of alphabet size, n, and beam width.  
- Method section is generally motivated but derivations and experiments are hard to follow without deep prior AOG knowledge.  
- It is unclear exactly what is learned (rules, parameters, relations) and how examples are represented in each domain.  
- Paper would benefit from a concrete running example of fragment induction and qualitative illustrations of learned structures/parses.  
- Unclear implementation details on arity: whether n>2 is supported, if CNF/binary restriction is used, and how Or siblings are preserved.  
- Experimental details are insufficient: frame-to-action-vector conversion, ID merging, relation instantiation, CYK parsing specifics, and image quantization are under-specified.  
- Missing hyperparameters, sensitivity analysis, and runtime/memory stats for tensor/context construction.  
- Qualitative results are missing; need example learned fragments, counts/probabilities, and held-out parses, plus side-by-side comparisons to baselines.  
- Clarify scope of “unsupervised”: events use annotated atomic actions, so supervision at primitive level should be stated.  
- Missing baselines/ablations: compare to separate And-only/Or-only searches, model merging, and re-parsing-based gain to validate the surrogate.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel principled approach, elegant sufficient-statistics idea, cross-domain applicability, and promising quantitative results.  
- Cons for acceptance: unclear derivations/assumptions, hard-to-follow experimental presentation, limited reproducibility and qualitative evidence.  
- Suggested revisions: full derivations and assumptions, algorithm pseudocode, complexity analysis, complete experimental details, sensitivity studies, qualitative examples, and code/data release.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept—novel and promising but needs clearer derivations, experimental clarity, and qualitative validation to be convincing.
