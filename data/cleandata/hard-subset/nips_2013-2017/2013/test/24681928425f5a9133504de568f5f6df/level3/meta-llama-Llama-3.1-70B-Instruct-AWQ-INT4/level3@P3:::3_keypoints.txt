- Proposes a novel unsupervised method that learns stochastic And-Or grammars by inducing unified And-Or fragments instead of separate And/OR searches.  
- Presents a unified, data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars applicable to text, images, and events.  
- Key contribution: use of And-Or fragments enabling unified search for compositions and reconfigurations, improving efficiency and robustness.  
- Provides a clear, concise definition of stochastic And-Or grammars and an efficient algorithm for learning structure and parameters.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-motivated.  
- Experimental results are competitive on event parsing and visual object parsing tasks.  
- Experimental presentation is weak: assumes familiarity with prior work (S. C. Zhu’s group) and is inaccessible to outsiders.  
- Experiments are hard to understand—unclear what was done, how examples were represented, and what was learned.  
- Lacks qualitative examples illustrating interesting structures discovered; results are mostly quantitative and not illuminating.  
- Clarity could be improved with more intuitive explanations and more descriptive headings/subheadings.  
- Originality and significance are high; approach has broad applicability across NLP, vision, and event parsing.  
- Needs more discussion of related work and clearer differentiation from previous contributions.  
- Recommendation: add detailed experimental setup explanations, more qualitative examples, comparisons showing more interesting structures than competitors, better headings, and more intuitive technical explanations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient approach; technically sound; satisfactory experimental performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor experimental presentation, lack of qualitative examples, and unclear experimental details.
