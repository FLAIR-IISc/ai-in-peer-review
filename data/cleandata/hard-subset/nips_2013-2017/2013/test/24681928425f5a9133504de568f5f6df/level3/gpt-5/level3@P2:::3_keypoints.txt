- Paper proposes a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or Grammars and an unsupervised algorithm that searches over And-Or fragments to learn structure and parameters from unannotated data.
- Learning objective is posterior P(G|X) with a sparsity prior and Viterbi-likelihood approximation; efficient posterior-gain computation uses an n-gram tensor and context matrix.
- Under an unambiguity assumption the likelihood gain factorizes into two coherence terms; prior gain trades off fragment size vs data explained.
- Empirical results on event parsing and image grammar induction show competitive or superior performance, including large perplexity improvements on real images.
- Situates work at intersection of PCFG/AOG induction and sum-product network structure learning, extending prior AOG methods by unified And-Or fragment search.
- Novelty: unified And-Or fragment search and sufficient-statistics-based posterior gain computation are original and compelling.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated fragment formulation that addresses data scarcity and redundancy from separate And/Or induction.
- Strength: posterior-driven search with explicit sparsity prior and efficient sufficient-statistics makes greedy/beam search practical.
- Strength: framework is data-type-agnostic and demonstrated on both event and image domains.
- Strength: empirical gains over prior AOG structure-learning methods.
- Weakness: experimental section lacks clarity—representations, relations, and learned outputs are hard to reconstruct without prior AOG familiarity.
- Weakness: unclear whether the surrogate/factorized likelihood gain is exact under unambiguity or an approximation; derivation missing from main text.
- Weakness: unambiguity assumptions and robustness to ambiguity/noise are under-explained.
- Weakness: fragment scope ambiguous—arity limits and permissible relation types per And-rule not fully specified; CNF requirements unclear.
- Weakness: limited qualitative analysis and few concrete examples of learned fragments/parses make interpretability hard to judge.
- Weakness: evaluation lacks ablations (And-only vs Or-only vs And-Or, with/without unambiguity) and additional baselines; some datasets are small and event setup uses synthetic variants.
- Quality: technically sound overall, but key derivations are deferred and experimental documentation insufficient for full reproducibility.
- Clarity: core ideas clear but paper assumes heavy prior knowledge; notation and figure explanations need streamlining and earlier definitions.
- Originality: high—the fragment search and gain derivation are novel and the cross-domain unification is appealing.
- Significance: potentially significant if made accessible and reproducible; empirical results support impact but broader comparisons and qualitative evidence needed.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, novel approach; unifies composition/reconfiguration; demonstrated improvements across domains.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental detail and qualitative illustrations; missing main-text derivations and assumptions; limited ablations and small test sets.
- Key questions for authors: is the likelihood-gain formula exact under unambiguity or approximate? How is “unambiguity” defined and handled in noisy/ambiguous data?
- Key questions for authors: can fragments have arbitrary arity and mixed relation types; is CNF binarization required? How are relation types specified/scored—learned or discretized?
- Key questions for authors: how are sa and so chosen and how sensitive are results to α, beam width, and encoding? What is computational complexity per iteration and total runtime?
- Suggested improvements: add detailed experimental appendix (preprocessing, feature definitions, relation parameterization, parsing, hyperparams, runtime, code).
- Suggested improvements: provide multiple qualitative examples of learned fragments and parses, and side-by-side baseline comparisons.
- Suggested improvements: include ablations and sensitivity analyses; move essential derivations from supplement to main text and state assumptions explicitly.
- Suggested improvements: improve figure readability and define notation (RD, CM, sa, so) upon first use.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept, contingent on substantially improved clarity and experimental documentation.
- Reviewer confidence: medium.
