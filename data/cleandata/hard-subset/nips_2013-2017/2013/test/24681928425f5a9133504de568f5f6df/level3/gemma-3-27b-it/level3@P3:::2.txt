This paper proposes a method for unsupervised structure learning of stochastic AND-OR grammars, aiming to jointly learn both the grammar structure and its parameters. The core idea is to iteratively introduce AND-OR fragments into an initially trivial grammar, optimizing for posterior probability based on likelihood and a sparsity prior. The approach is evaluated on learning event grammars and image grammars, demonstrating competitive performance. The work builds upon previous research in stochastic grammars, particularly in natural language processing (e.g., Klein & Manning, 2004) and image modeling (e.g., Zhu & Mumford, 2006), aiming to address the challenges of manually specifying complex grammar structures. It also relates to recent work on structure learning of AND-OR grammars (Si & Zhu, 2013; Si et al., 2011), improving upon those approaches by unifying the search for compositions and reconfigurations.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach to a challenging problem. The formulation of the posterior probability optimization and the introduction of AND-OR fragments are clearly explained. The use of sufficient statistics to improve computational efficiency is a valuable contribution. The experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach on the chosen tasks, achieving comparable or better performance than existing methods. However, the paper lacks a thorough discussion of the computational complexity of the algorithm. While the authors mention efficiency gains from using sufficient statistics, a more detailed analysis of scalability, especially concerning the number of data points, would significantly strengthen the work. The claim of tractability feels unsupported without such analysis. The authors are careful to acknowledge the limitations of their approach, but the lack of rigorous complexity analysis detracts from the overall assessment of technical soundness.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The notation is consistent, and the concepts are explained in a relatively accessible manner. The inclusion of Figure 1, illustrating the learning process, is helpful. However, the description of the likelihood gain computation could be more detailed. The connection between the n-gram tensor, context matrix, and the posterior gain formula isn't fully elucidated and requires deeper explanation for readers unfamiliar with these concepts. More illustrative examples, particularly relating to the computation of likelihood gain, would enhance clarity.

**Originality:** The work presents a novel approach to structure learning in stochastic AND-OR grammars by unifying the search for compositions and reconfigurations. The introduction of AND-OR fragments, rather than treating AND and OR rules separately, is a significant contribution. While the paper builds upon existing work in stochastic grammars, it offers a unique perspective and a practical solution to the problem of unsupervised structure learning.  The formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars agnostic to data type is also a noteworthy contribution. The efficient computation of posterior gains using sufficient statistics is also a novel aspect.

**Significance:** The proposed method addresses a fundamental problem in unsupervised learning – discovering underlying structure in complex data. The ability to learn both structure and parameters simultaneously is crucial for adapting to new data and domains. While the experiments on event and image grammars are promising, the real significance of the work would be greatly enhanced by demonstrating its applicability to more challenging tasks, such as natural language processing. The potential to model recursive phenomena, as highlighted by the authors, is particularly exciting, but remains largely unexplored in the current work.  The proposed method could be useful for various applications where compositional and reconfigurable structures are present, but its impact is limited without broader validation.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to unsupervised structure learning in stochastic AND-OR grammars.
*   Unification of composition and reconfiguration learning with AND-OR fragments.
*   Competitive performance on event and image grammar learning tasks.
*   Clear and generally well-written presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of detailed analysis of algorithmic tractability and scalability.
*   Absence of experiments on natural language data (e.g., Penn Treebank).
*   Limited scope of experimental evaluation.
*   Insufficient detail in explaining the likelihood gain computation.

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of unsupervised structure learning. The proposed method is technically sound and demonstrates promising results. However, the lack of scalability analysis and the absence of experiments on natural language data are significant weaknesses.  I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to address these concerns in a revised version. Specifically, the authors should provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their algorithm, conduct experiments on standard natural language datasets (e.g., Penn Treebank), and expand the experimental evaluation to include a wider range of tasks and datasets. Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its potential impact.
