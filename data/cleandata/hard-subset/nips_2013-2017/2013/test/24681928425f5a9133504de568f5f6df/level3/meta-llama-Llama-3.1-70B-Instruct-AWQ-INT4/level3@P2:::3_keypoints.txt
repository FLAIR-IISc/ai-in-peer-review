- Proposes a novel unsupervised method to learn stochastic And-Or grammars by inducing unified And-Or fragments instead of separate AND/OR searches.  
- Provides a unified, data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and an algorithmic framework to learn both structure and parameters.  
- Main contribution: And-Or fragments that unify search for new compositions and reconfigurations in the grammar.  
- Shows that adding only And-Or fragments suffices to construct any context-free And-Or grammar.  
- Introduces an efficient method to compute posterior gain for adding fragments using sufficient statistics of the training data.  
- Evaluated on event parsing and visual object parsing; competitive on event parsing and outperforms prior methods on visual object parsing.  
- Reviewer: problem important, approach novel and well-motivated, algorithm technically sound.  
- Concern: supplemental derivations could not be verified; clearer derivations/clarifications are needed.  
- Concern: unclear whether the proposed "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Concern: unclear if method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is restricted to Chomsky normal form.  
- Major concern: experiments are hard to understand—representation of examples, what was done, and what was learned are unclear.  
- Experimental presentation assumes familiarity with prior work and needs extensive supplementary material, concrete illustrations, and qualitative examples of discovered structure.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on clarifying derivations, experimental details, and providing more illustrative supplementary material.
