- Contribution: Unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars agnostic to data type and an unsupervised method to learn structure and parameters.  
- Contribution: Explicit optimization of the posterior of grammar structure and parameters instead of heuristic rules.  
- Contribution: Unified learning of compositions and reconfigurations via "And-Or fragments."  
- Contribution: Empirical validation showing competitive or superior results on event and image grammars.  
- Strength: Technically sound algorithm with efficient computation of sufficient statistics.  
- Strength: Elegant unified approach addressing prior separation of And- and Or-fragments.  
- Strength: Convincing experimental results on complex datasets.  
- Weakness: Claim of generalization is overstated—method is a tractable special case of a broader grammar class.  
- Weakness: Insufficient experimental comparison to key baselines (notably Stolcke and Omohundro [14]) and other relevant methods.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion and formal analysis of computational complexity versus prior approaches.  
- Quality: Strong technical content and well-motivated approach, but experiments could be more comprehensive.  
- Clarity: Well-written, well-organized, and aided by helpful figures illustrating the learning process.  
- Originality: Novel unified use of And-Or fragments, but generalization claims are misleading.  
- Significance: Meaningful contribution for learning grammars from unannotated data with potential cross-domain applications.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand comparisons and clarify relation to prior work.
