- Proposes an unsupervised method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (SAGs) via iterative addition of “And-Or fragments.”
- Optimizes a posterior probability over grammars to guide fragment addition and parameter estimation.
- Claims a unified framework handling both compositionality and reconfigurability through And-Or fragments.
- Builds on prior grammar induction and SAG learning work from NLP and vision/event literature.
- The claimed generalization of prior work is overstated—method focuses on a specific fragment-addition strategy.
- Core algorithm is sensibly designed and tractable for the demonstrated tasks.
- Experimental results are competitive and often outperform prior methods, especially on a complex event dataset.
- Experimental evaluation lacks stronger baselines; should compare to Stolcke and Omohondro (ref. 14).
- Would benefit from additional comparisons to recent deep learning-based scene/event understanding methods.
- Potential concerns about algorithmic efficiency and scalability to very large or complex datasets.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions to add suggested baselines and temper generalization claims.
