- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for learning stochastic And–Or grammars.  
- Introduces And–Or fragments to unify search for new compositions and reconfigurations.  
- Method is technically sound, well-motivated, and effective on event parsing and visual object parsing.  
- Claims more efficient and robust grammar learning that finds more interesting structures than competitors.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains technical details.  
- Provides a thorough evaluation with comparisons to prior approaches and result analysis.  
- Supplemental derivations are missing, preventing verification.  
- Unclear whether the reported "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or approximate.  
- Not clearly explained whether the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental presentation assumes familiarity with S. C. Zhu’s prior work and is hard to follow for outsiders.  
- Results are largely quantitative and not very illuminating; more qualitative examples of discovered structures are needed.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution that would be improved by addressing the above clarity and supplementary-material issues.
