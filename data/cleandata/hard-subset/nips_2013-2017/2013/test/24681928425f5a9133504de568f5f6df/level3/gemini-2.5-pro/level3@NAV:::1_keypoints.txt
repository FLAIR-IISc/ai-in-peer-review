- Proposes a novel unsupervised method to learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars.
- Core idea: start from a trivial grammar that fits the data and iteratively introduce more general structures.
- Induces "And-Or fragments" bottom-up in a greedy procedure that optimizes the grammar posterior.
- Frames work as an extension of prior grammar induction in NLP and vision, unifying compositional (And) and reconfigurable (Or) learning.
- Main contribution: a unified, elegant approach that discovers compositionality and alternation simultaneously.
- Method is sensible and pragmatic: restricted special-case formulation makes the hard problem tractable and yields a robust algorithm.
- Empirical results show strong performance on event and image grammar learning tasks compared to recent related methods.
- Primary weakness: limited experimental scope and insufficient range of baseline comparisons.
- Recommends adding established baselines, notably Bayesian model merging (Stolcke & Omohondro 1994), to strengthen claims.
- Suggests tempering the claim of "generalizing" prior work and instead position the method as a powerful technique for a specific grammar class.
- Overall: solid, principled contribution with sound methodology and promising results, but needs improved positioning and broader evaluation.
