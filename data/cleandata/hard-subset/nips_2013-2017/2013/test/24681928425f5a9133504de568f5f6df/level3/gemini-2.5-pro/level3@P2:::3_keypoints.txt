- Novel unsupervised method that iteratively builds stochastic And-Or grammars by adding fragments that maximize posterior probability.
- Unifies learning of compositional (And) and alternative (Or) structures, addressing limitations of prior separate approaches.
- Principled Bayesian formulation that optimizes the grammar posterior is a significant contribution.
- Efficient candidate scoring derived via sufficient statistics (n-gram tensor and context matrix) makes greedy/beam search feasible.
- General, data-agnostic formalization positions the method as a broad structure-learning algorithm.
- Unclear whether the likelihood-gain formula is an exact Viterbi gain under the unambiguity assumption or an approximation — needs clarification.
- Paper does not specify arity constraints for And-rules (e.g., binary/CNF), leaving expressive power and search scope ambiguous.
- Experimental descriptions lack detail on data preprocessing and representations, impairing reproducibility.
- Evaluation is primarily quantitative (F-measure, perplexity) with few or no qualitative examples illustrating learned structures.
- Presentation relies heavily on prior work and is not self-contained, reducing accessibility for broader readers.
- Recommended fixes: clarify derivations, state rule constraints, expand experimental methodology, and add qualitative visualizations/parse examples.
