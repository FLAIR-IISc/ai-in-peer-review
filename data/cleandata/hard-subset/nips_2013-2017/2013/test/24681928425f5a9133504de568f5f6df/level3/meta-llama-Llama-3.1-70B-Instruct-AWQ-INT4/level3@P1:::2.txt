This paper proposes a novel approach to unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars, which are a powerful tool for modeling complex patterns in various types of data, including images and events. The authors present a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled, and develop an algorithm that jointly learns the structure and parameters of such grammars.

The paper's main contributions are threefold. First, it presents a formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is applicable to different types of data. Second, it proposes an unsupervised approach to learning the structure and parameters of these grammars, which is based on iteratively introducing And-Or fragments and measuring their likelihood and prior gains. Third, it demonstrates the effectiveness of this approach on two tasks: learning event grammars and image grammars.

The paper's strengths lie in its ability to learn stochastic grammars that model recursive phenomena, such as natural language, in a tractable way. The authors' use of And-Or fragments is a key innovation that allows them to efficiently search for good grammar rules. The paper's experimental results are also competitive with previous approaches on the tasks of learning event grammars and image grammars.

However, the paper has some limitations. One major critique is that it does not address algorithmic tractability or scalability, particularly to thousands of datapoints. The authors' algorithm may not be efficient for large datasets, and it is unclear how it would perform on such datasets. Another critique is that the paper lacks experiments on natural language data, such as the Penn Treebank, and comparisons to prior work like Klein & Manning. The reviewer would like to see more experiments overall, especially on natural language datasets.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-written description of their approach. The paper's organization is logical, and the authors provide sufficient background information on stochastic And-Or grammars. The experimental results are well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of their findings.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide clear explanations of their approach and results. The paper's notation is consistent, and the authors provide sufficient definitions for all technical terms. The paper's figures and tables are also well-presented and help to illustrate the authors' points.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars. The authors' use of And-Or fragments is a key innovation that allows them to efficiently search for good grammar rules. The paper's experimental results are also original and contribute to the state of the art in the field.

In terms of significance, the paper's contributions are significant, particularly in the context of natural language processing. The authors' approach has the potential to be applied to a wide range of tasks, including language modeling, parsing, and machine translation. The paper's results are also relevant to other areas, such as computer vision and robotics.
