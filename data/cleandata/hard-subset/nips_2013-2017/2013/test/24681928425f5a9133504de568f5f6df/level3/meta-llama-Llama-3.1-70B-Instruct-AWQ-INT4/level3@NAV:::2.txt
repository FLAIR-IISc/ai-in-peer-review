This paper proposes a method for jointly learning the structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars (AND and OR rules) in an unsupervised manner. The authors present a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled. The learning approach starts with a trivial initial grammar and iteratively introduces new fragments into the grammar to optimize its posterior probability. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two tasks: learning event grammars and image grammars.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed method is able to learn both the structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars in an unsupervised manner, which is a challenging task.
* The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, including the formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars and the learning algorithm.
* The experimental results show that the proposed method is competitive with previous approaches on the task of learning event grammars and outperforms them on the task of learning image grammars.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* The authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of their approach, which is an important consideration for scalability.
* The paper lacks experiments on natural language data, which is a significant omission given the relevance of stochastic grammars to natural language processing.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with previous work, such as the approach of Klein and Manning, which is a notable omission.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective approach to learning stochastic And-Or grammars in an unsupervised manner. However, there are some significant weaknesses that need to be addressed in future work.

Quality: 8/10

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach. However, the lack of analysis on computational complexity and the omission of experiments on natural language data are significant weaknesses.

Clarity: 9/10

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. However, some of the technical details may be challenging for non-experts to understand.

Originality: 8/10

Significance: 8/10

The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of stochastic grammars, but the lack of experiments on natural language data limits its impact.
