- Presents a novel, well-motivated unified formalization for unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars.  
- Proposes an algorithm that induces unified And-Or fragments, differing from prior separate And/Or rule search.  
- Addresses an important problem with potentially high significance for ML/AI.  
- Algorithm is technically sound according to the reviewer.  
- Reviewer could not verify supplemental derivations and requests clearer, more detailed derivations.  
- Unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Unclear if the method supports fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form.  
- Experimental presentation is difficult to follow and assumes familiarity with prior work from S. C. Zhu’s group.  
- Paper needs extensive supplementary experimental details, concrete illustrations, and clearer example representations.  
- Results are largely quantitative; reviewer requests qualitative examples showing interesting learned structure.  
- Reviewer wants evidence that the approach learns more interesting structure than competing methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on addressing clarity, derivation, experimental, and generalization concerns.
