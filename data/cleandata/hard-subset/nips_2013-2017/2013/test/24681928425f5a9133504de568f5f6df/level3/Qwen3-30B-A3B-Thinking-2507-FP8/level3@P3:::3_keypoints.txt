- Presents a unified, data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars with an unsupervised method to learn both structure and parameters.  
- Key innovation: learn unified AND-OR fragments (instead of separate AND and OR searches) to represent compositions and configurations within a single fragment.  
- Uses sufficient statistics to compute fragment value efficiently, avoiding re-parsing of all training samples.  
- Paper is well-motivated, technically sound, with clear problem definition, formalization, and learning framework.  
- Empirical results on event and image parsing are competitive or better than prior approaches, demonstrating practical value.  
- Strength: novel unified AND-OR fragment approach that is well-justified.  
- Strength: data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars useful for future work.  
- Strength: significant technical contribution in efficient fragment-value computation.  
- Strength: cross-domain experiments provide evidence of effectiveness.  
- Weakness: experimental section is unclear—representations, procedures, and learned outputs are not well explained and assume prior work familiarity.  
- Weakness: theoretical derivations (surrogate likelihood gain, suff. statistics) are insufficiently clear or verifiable.  
- Weakness: unclear whether method supports fragment arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form, raising expressive-power concerns.  
- Weakness: lack of qualitative examples or visualizations of interesting learned structures to illustrate advantages.  
- Requested fixes: add detailed experimental descriptions and visual grammar examples, clarify theoretical derivations and likelihood-gain measure, discuss arity limitations, and include qualitative results.  
- Overall: promising and technically solid but needs substantial clarification and supplementary material; reviewer would support acceptance if concerns are addressed.
