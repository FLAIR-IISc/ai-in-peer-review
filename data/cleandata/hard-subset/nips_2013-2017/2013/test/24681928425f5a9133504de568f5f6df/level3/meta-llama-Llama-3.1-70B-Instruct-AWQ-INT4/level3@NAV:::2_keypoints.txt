- Proposes an unsupervised method to jointly learn structure and parameters of stochastic And-Or grammars.
- Presents a unified, data-agnostic formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars.
- Learning starts from a trivial grammar and iteratively introduces fragments to maximize posterior probability.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on event-grammar and image-grammar learning tasks.
- Strength: learns both grammar structure and parameters unsupervised, addressing a challenging problem.
- Strength: clear, concise exposition of the formalism and learning algorithm.
- Strength: competitive results on event grammars and superior performance on image grammars versus prior methods.
- Weakness: no thorough analysis of computational complexity or scalability.
- Weakness: lacks experiments on natural language data, limiting relevance to NLP.
- Weakness: insufficient detailed comparison to prior work (e.g., Klein and Manning).
- Overall assessment: novel and effective approach but significant omissions to address in future work.
