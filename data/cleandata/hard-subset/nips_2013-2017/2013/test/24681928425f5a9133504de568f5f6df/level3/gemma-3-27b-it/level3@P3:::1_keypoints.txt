- Proposes unsupervised structure learning of stochastic And-Or grammars applicable to images and events.  
- Uses an iterative algorithm that starts from a trivial grammar and incrementally adds And-Or fragments by maximizing posterior probability.  
- Unifies composition and reconfiguration learning within And-Or fragments to reduce error-prone separate learning.  
- Derives an efficient posterior-gain computation using sufficient statistics to make fragment addition tractable and scalable.  
- Empirical results show competitive or superior performance on tested event and image grammar tasks.  
- Strength: Technically sound derivation and tractable approach; And-Or fragments balance compositional structure and alternatives.  
- Strength: Sensible iterative refinement with solid theoretical grounding via posterior maximization.  
- Strength: Presents a unified stochastic And-Or grammar formalization applicable across data types.  
- Weakness: Claim of “generalizing” prior work is overstated; method is essentially a special case of Bayesian grammar induction.  
- Weakness: Restricting to And-Or fragments limits expressiveness compared to allowing arbitrary rule additions.  
- Weakness: Experimental baselines are limited and missing a crucial comparison to Stolcke & Omohondro (1994).  
- Weakness: Datasets are relatively constrained; needs evaluation on more diverse and complex datasets.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized, but some mathematical derivations need clearer explanations and examples.  
- Originality: Novel combination of And-Or fragment learning with posterior maximization and efficient posterior-gain computation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—tone down generalization claims and include comparison to Stolcke & Omohondro (1994).
