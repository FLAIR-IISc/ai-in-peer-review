- Proposes a method for jointly learning structure and parameters of stochastic AND-OR grammars (AND and OR rules).
- Uses unsupervised structure learning by iteratively introducing AND-OR fragments and measuring likelihood and prior gains.
- Shows competitive results on two tasks: learning event grammars and image grammars.
- Claims a tractable way to learn stochastic grammars that model recursive phenomena (e.g., natural language).
- Main critique: paper does not address algorithmic tractability or scalability (e.g., to thousands of datapoints).
- Main critique: lacks experiments on natural language data—e.g., Penn Treebank and comparisons to prior work like Klein & Manning.
- Reviewer would like to see more experiments overall, especially on natural language datasets.
