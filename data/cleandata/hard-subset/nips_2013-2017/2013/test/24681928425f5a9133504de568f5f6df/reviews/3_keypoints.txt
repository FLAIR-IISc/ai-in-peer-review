- Proposes a novel unsupervised method to learn AND-OR grammars by inducing unified AND-OR fragments rather than separate AND/OR searches.  
- Value of a proposed AND-OR fragment can be computed efficiently using sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluated on tasks including event parsing and visual object parsing.  
- Reviewer finds the problem important, the approach novel and well-motivated, and the algorithm technically sound.  
- Reviewer could not verify supplemental derivations and requests clearer derivations/clarifications.  
- Unclear whether the "surrogate measure" of likelihood gain is exact or an approximation.  
- Unclear whether the method extends to fragments with arity >2 or is limited to Chomsky normal form.  
- Major concern: experiments are hard to understand—what was done, how examples were represented, and what was learned are not clear.  
- Experimental presentation assumes familiarity with prior work (S. C. Zhu’s group), making it inaccessible to outsiders.  
- If accepted, the paper should include extensive supplementary material with more experimental details and concrete illustrations.  
- Would like to see qualitative examples of interesting structure discovered; current results are largely quantitative and not very illuminating.  
- Suggestion to demonstrate that this approach learns more interesting structure than competing methods.
