- Praises the idea that older data benefits from more layers and that deep RNNs are reasonable for improving short-term processing.  
- Suggests adding extra nonlinear layers between timesteps and asks whether this was tried.  
- Asks how important the gradient normalizations are and how they “avoid the problem of bifurcations.”  
- Points to a relevant ICML 2013 U. of Montreal paper on gradient truncation/optimization that should be considered.  
- Questions whether normalizing the gradient could disrupt SGD’s averaging behavior.  
- Recommends comparing results to Mikolov et al. on Wikipedia compression tasks.  
- Finds the text-generation experiment (Table 2) most persuasive but warns that layer “surgery” interpretations are confounded by complex interdependencies.  
- Identifies a key missing experiment: test whether higher layers genuinely gain from upstream processing versus simply relocating units to the first layer.  
- Raises concern about an unfair comparison due to parameter-count differences; asks for baselines with the same number of parameters or units.  
- Suggests plotting performance for various DRNN depths trained from scratch (including wider layers to match parameters/units) instead of the current Figure 2.  
- Minor: define DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in the text (not only in Figure 1).  
- Overall: paper is worthwhile, readable, and largely convincing but needs a few additional experiments and comparisons.
