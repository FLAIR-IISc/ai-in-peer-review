- Reviewer mostly agrees with the "one task" criticism in the rebuttal.
- Suggests trying truncated BPTT with ~30 steps; may miss parentheses but could match entropies at much lower cost.
- Paper studies usefulness of deep recurrent neural networks.
- Main result: deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs when number of parameters is held constant on character-level language modeling.
- Finding: deeper layers encode more long-range information.
- Finding: plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior belief that HF was necessary.
- Criticism: paper improperly claims to have introduced the deep RNN architecture; prior work (e.g., Graves 2013) already used similar models.
- Acceptable to focus on analysis rather than proposing a new architecture.
- Concern: results may be task-specific; analysis should be replicated on additional tasks (e.g., speech) to strengthen claims.
- HF experiments may be unnecessarily expensive; truncated BPTT (as used by Mikolov) likely sufficient and should be tested to confirm.
