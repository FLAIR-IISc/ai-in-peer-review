- Proposes a new deep architecture that combines hierarchical deep learning features with time-series modeling (HMMs/RNNs).  
- Introduces a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training procedure using a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Trains very large networks (≈10 days) and reports improved performance on a Hinton et al. Wikipedia dataset versus prior work.  
- Provides detailed analysis of network behavior: higher layers capture long-term dependencies and parentheses matching appears as a pseudo-stable attractor-like state.  
- Paper quality is high: well-written, experiments and figures are clear and supportive.  
- Main experimental limitation: evaluation on a single, not widely used dataset and comparisons restricted to RNN-related models, excluding hierarchical HMMs; other approaches listed are on different corpora and thus not comparable.  
- Overall contribution is stronger in analysis of how the model operates than in demonstrating conclusive performance gains; results are somewhat inconclusive relative to the paper’s stated goals.
