- Proposes a novel hierarchical DRNN architecture where each layer is an RNN and higher layers receive previous-layer hidden states to capture temporal hierarchies.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the architecture and benefits.
- Experimental results show state-of-the-art performance for RNNs on Wikipedia character-level language modeling.
- Analysis demonstrates emergent time scales, indicating the DRNN can learn long-term dependencies and temporal hierarchies.

Strengths:
- Novel architecture enabling hierarchical processing of time series.
- Comprehensive experiments validating effectiveness on character-level modeling.
- Insightful analysis of emergent time scales and temporal representation.

Weaknesses:
- Limited analysis of the proposed incremental layer-wise training method.
- Insufficient comparisons to other recurrent architectures (e.g., LSTM, GRU).
- Lack of a detailed discussion of the DRNN's limitations.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Significant architectural contribution to recurrent networks.
- Strong experimental evidence of performance gains.
- Empirical and analytical evidence of modeling long-term temporal structure.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Unclear effectiveness and analysis of the training procedure.
- Missing direct comparisons to competitive architectures.
- Inadequate discussion of failure modes and limitations.

Recommendations:
- Provide a thorough analysis and ablation of the incremental layer-wise training.
- Compare performance and behaviors to LSTMs, GRUs, and other baselines.
- Add a detailed discussion of the DRNN's limitations, scalability, and failure cases.

Overall:
- A well-written, promising contribution with strong results, but needs deeper training analysis, broader comparisons, and clearer limitations.
