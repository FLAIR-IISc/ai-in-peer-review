This paper investigates the properties of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the layer below as its input. The central hypothesis is that this deep structure allows the network to naturally form a temporal hierarchy, enabling it to process time series information at multiple time scales. The authors test this hypothesis on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia dataset. The proposed DRNNs, trained with a simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD) procedure, achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained with more complex models and sophisticated second-order optimization methods. The paper provides a detailed analysis of the trained models, offering evidence that the layers learn distinct time scales and that the network can capture very long-term dependencies.

This work builds upon the broader success of deep architectures in machine learning and connects to previous, more limited explorations of hierarchical RNNs. While similar stacked RNN architectures have been used before, for example in speech recognition (Graves et al., 2013), this paper distinguishes itself through its specific application to large-scale character-level language modeling and, more importantly, through its in-depth analysis of the model's emergent properties.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on a difficult and widely used benchmark. Achieving this with a conceptually simple model and a standard SGD training algorithm is a significant result. It suggests that architectural choices can be as important as, or even more important than, complex optimization algorithms for tackling challenging sequential tasks.

2.  **Excellent Analysis and Insight:** The main contribution and greatest strength of this paper is the thorough analysis of the trained models. The authors do not simply report performance scores but provide several well-designed experiments to understand the model's behavior:
    *   The investigation of different output connection schemes (DRNN-1O vs. DRNN-AO) and the subsequent analysis of individual layer contributions provide a clear picture of how different parts of the network specialize.
    *   The perturbation experiments (introducing a typo or switching context) compellingly illustrate that a temporal hierarchy emerges through training, with higher layers exhibiting longer memory and slower dynamics than lower layers.
    *   The analysis of parenthesis-closing is a concrete and intuitive demonstration of the model's ability to learn and maintain state over hundreds of time steps, showcasing its capacity for capturing long-range dependencies.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and easy to follow. The motivation is well-established, the model is described precisely, and the experimental setup and results are presented in a logical and accessible manner. The figures effectively support and clarify the main points of the analysis.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**

1.  **Limited Architectural Novelty:** The core idea of stacking RNN layers is not entirely new. The paper would benefit from a slightly more detailed discussion of how its architecture and findings relate to prior work, such as the bidirectional LSTM stacks used by Graves et al., to better delineate its specific contributions beyond the novel analysis.

2.  **Limited Number of Experiments:** The conclusions are drawn from a single training run for each of the three main network configurations. Given the high variance often associated with training large neural networks, reporting results from multiple runs with different random initializations would significantly strengthen the paper's claims. While the authors' note about the long training times is understood, acknowledging this as a limitation and framing the results accordingly would be appropriate. The assertion in footnote 3 that performance differences are minimal for large models is a strong claim that is not substantiated with evidence.

3.  **Heuristic Training Procedure:** The layer-wise training method for the DRNN-1O model, while effective, appears somewhat ad-hoc. A clearer justification for the specific training schedule (e.g., why the number of training iterations increases with each new layer) would be beneficial for reproducibility and for understanding the principles behind this successful strategy.

**Evaluation:**

*   **Quality:** The work is of high technical quality. The experiments are well-designed to support the paper's central claims, and the results are significant.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exemplary in its clarity. It is well-structured and written in a way that makes complex ideas accessible.
*   **Originality:** While the model architecture itself has precedents, the application to this specific task combined with the rigorous and insightful analysis of the model's internal dynamics constitutes a strong and original contribution.
*   **Significance:** The paper is highly significant. It provides strong evidence for the power of deep recurrent architectures and offers valuable insights into how they function. The demonstration that simple training techniques can yield state-of-the-art results on these models is an important practical contribution that makes these powerful techniques more accessible to the wider community. The analysis of emergent time scales has likely influenced subsequent research in understanding and designing deep sequential models.

In summary, this is a very strong paper that combines impressive empirical results with insightful analysis. It provides a compelling case for the use of deep recurrent networks and sheds light on the mechanisms that make them effective. Despite the minor weakness regarding the limited number of experimental runs, the quality of the analysis and the significance of the findings make it a valuable contribution to the field.
