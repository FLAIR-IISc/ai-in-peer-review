- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): hierarchical RNN layers that take previous-layer hidden states as input, claimed to better capture temporal hierarchies and achieve SOTA char-level language modeling with simple SGD.  
- Key contributions: (1) DRNN architecture with two output configs (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO), (2) empirical BPC results on character-level LM, (3) analysis of emergent time scales via perturbations, (4) tests on modeling long-term dependencies (e.g., parentheses).  
- Paper quality: well-written, clearly organized, with appropriate equations and illustrative figures.  
- Experimental rigor: thorough experimental setup, careful hyperparameter tuning, solid performance metrics.  
- Time-scale analysis: insightful typo-perturbation experiments showing layer-specific temporal processing.  
- Evidence of long-term modeling: text generation and parentheses-closing tests support DRNNs’ ability to capture long dependencies.  
- Practical contribution: shows simple SGD can match complex optimization (Hessian-free) for recurrent nets, which is practically significant.  
- Ablation analysis: layer-specific contribution tests give useful insight into internal processing.  
- Weakness — prior work comparison: insufficient contextualization and unclear differentiation from stacked bi-directional LSTM work ([8]).  
- Weakness — SOTA claim: marginal BPC improvement over MRNN [19] (1.46 vs 1.45) without clear explanation of significance.  
- Weakness — DRNN-AO vs DRNN-1O: paper fails to explain why output-from-all-layers underperforms top-only output, contradicting rationale.  
- Weakness — training procedure: layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is reported as necessary but not justified; standard BPTT failure and implications are unexplained.  
- Weakness — theory connection: time-scale findings aren’t clearly linked to theoretical advantages of hierarchical temporal processing.  
- Suggested improvements: (1) better differentiate from prior stacked-RNN work, (2) explain DRNN-AO vs 1O performance gap, (3) justify necessity of layer-wise training and BPTT limitations, (4) explicitly tie time-scale analysis to hierarchical-processing theory.  
- Overall verdict: promising architectural extension with strong experiments and useful insights, but claims of novelty and significance need stronger substantiation and clearer comparisons to prior work.
