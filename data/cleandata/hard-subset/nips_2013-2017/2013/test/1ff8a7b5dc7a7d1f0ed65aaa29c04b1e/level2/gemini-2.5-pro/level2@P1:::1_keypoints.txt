- Paper studies Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNNs) and hypothesizes they form a temporal hierarchy processing multiple time scales.
- Evaluated on large-scale character-level language modeling (Wikipedia), achieving state-of-the-art performance.
- Achieves strong results using simple SGD rather than complex optimizers, highlighting the importance of architecture.
- Provides extensive analysis showing different layers learn distinct time scales and longer-term dependencies.
- Compares output connection schemes (DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO) and analyzes individual layer contributions.
- Perturbation experiments (typos/context switches) demonstrate higher layers have slower dynamics and longer memory.
- Parenthesis-closing analysis illustrates the model can maintain state over hundreds of time steps.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and well-organized with effective figures.
- Architectural novelty is limited since stacked RNNs have been used before; more discussion relating to prior work (e.g., Graves et al.) is needed.
- Experiments are limited to a single training run per configuration, which weakens claims given typical training variance.
- The authors’ claim that performance differences are minimal for large models (footnote) is unsubstantiated.
- The layer-wise training schedule for DRNN-1O is heuristic and needs clearer justification for reproducibility.
- Overall judged high quality, significant, and original in its analysis and application, despite the noted experimental and novelty caveats.
- Conclusion: strong empirical and analytical contribution that increases the accessibility and understanding of deep recurrent architectures.
