- Studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNNs) with two output topologies (DRNN-1O with incremental layer-wise training, DRNN-AO with all-layer outputs), reporting strong BPC using SGD.
- Shows higher layers encode longer temporal context via perturbation experiments, context-switch tests, layer-output ablations, and a parentheses long-range case study.
- Differs from prior work by applying simple stacked tanh-RNNs trained with plain SGD at scale, systematically comparing output strategies, and analyzing emergent time scales.
- Strength: clear, simple, and easily reproducible architecture bridging deep feedforward hierarchies and temporal modeling.
- Strength: competitive bits-per-character on a large Wikipedia corpus using first-order optimization, comparable to HF-trained MRNNs under similar parameter budgets.
- Strength: careful analyses (per-layer ablations, perturbation-based time-scale characterization, long-range behavior) provide insights beyond leaderboard metrics.
- Strength: incremental supervised layer-wise training schedule is effective and well documented.
- Weakness: DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO comparison confounded by different training schedules and total data passes (≈10 vs 7), so topology vs compute/pretraining effects are unclear.
- Weakness: BPC comparisons use a different character set (95+1 vs 86), risking unfair SOTA claims without matched setups or normalization.
- Weakness: lacks a stacked LSTM/biLSTM baseline on the same corpus and budget to contextualize results and the hierarchical-time-scale claim.
- Weakness: limited ablation study and apparently single random initializations; absence of variance estimates hinders robustness assessment.
- Weakness: time-scale inferences rely on Euclidean hidden-state distances (scale-dependent); needs complementary, output-tied measures (e.g., per-layer cross-entropy changes).
- Quality: technically sound with explicit training details (BPTT length, batch construction, gradient normalization, learning-rate schedule); minor typo in Eq. (3) likely in learning-rate sign.
- Clarity: well written and organized; suggests clarifying gradient normalization threshold/clipping vs normalization, random seed handling, and exact Wikipedia preprocessing.
- Originality: stacking RNNs is established, but applying simple tanh DRNNs with SGD at scale plus time-scale analyses and incremental supervised training is a useful, timely contribution.
- Significance: practically important demonstration that stacked simple RNNs with SGD can match HF MRNNs and provide insights into temporal hierarchies that may influence model design.
- Essential fix: equalize training budget or report compute-normalized results to disentangle topology from schedule/pretraining effects.
- Essential fix: provide matched evaluation against a stacked LSTM with comparable parameters/training or qualify SOTA claims to tanh-RNNs only.
- Essential fix: correct Eq. (3) and detail gradient normalization implementation (threshold, global vs per-group).
- Essential fix: resolve alphabet comparability by evaluating on the 86-char setup or showing conversion effects are negligible.
- Nice-to-have: report variability across multiple random seeds (mean±std) for at least one configuration.
- Nice-to-have: study depth vs width trade-offs under a fixed parameter budget to support the depth→time-scale claim.
- Nice-to-have: complement hidden-state distance measures with output-centric metrics (per-layer contribution to cross-entropy under perturbations).
- Nice-to-have: release code and trained models to aid reproducibility.
- Pro-acceptance: strong empirical results with a simple approach, insightful analyses on temporal hierarchies, and clear training guidance.
- Contra-acceptance: incomplete baseline coverage, unmatched comparisons and variability reporting, and missing stacked LSTM comparison weaken the paper’s claims.
- Overall: solid empirical and analytical contribution that would be stronger with tighter, compute-matched comparisons and clarifications; suitable for acceptance after revisions.
