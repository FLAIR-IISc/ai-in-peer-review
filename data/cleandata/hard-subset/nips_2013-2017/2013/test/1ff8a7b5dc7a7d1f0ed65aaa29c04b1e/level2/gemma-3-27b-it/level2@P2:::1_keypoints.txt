- Investigates deep recurrent neural networks (stacked RNNs) for character-level language modeling, arguing stacking produces a hierarchy of timescales.
- Shows state-of-the-art results on a Wikipedia corpus using simple SGD, outperforming Hessian-free optimization baselines.
- Provides analysis of emergent timescales with experiments like typo perturbation, context switching, and parenthesis closing to probe internal dynamics.
- Motivates hierarchical processing in RNNs and situates work relative to prior hierarchical RNN and deep learning literature.
- Novel idea: explicitly exploiting multiple timescales via stacking is a principled contribution with empirical support.
- Strong empirical contribution: competitive performance achieved with a simple optimizer highlights architectural importance.
- Thorough analysis: goes beyond performance to examine internal dynamics and layer-wise contributions.
- Clear writing and organization with adequate experimental details (figures referenced but missing).
- Training considerations addressed: layer-wise incremental training (DRNN-1O) to mitigate vanishing gradients and gradient normalization to prevent explosions.
- Limited experimental scope: evaluations confined to text prediction, unclear generalization to other time-series domains (speech, sensors).
- Network size and architecture choices (≈4.9M parameters, 5 layers) are not fully justified and may affect generalizability and training cost.
- Lack of systematic ablation studies (varying layers/units) to establish optimal DRNN configurations.
- Missing referenced figures (1–4) hinder full evaluation of results.
- Some acknowledged limitations (e.g., difficulty with nested parentheses) warrant deeper discussion and future work directions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, strong empirical results, insightful analysis, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited scope, insufficient justification of size/layers, missing figures, and absent ablation study.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to include figures and expand experimental justification.
