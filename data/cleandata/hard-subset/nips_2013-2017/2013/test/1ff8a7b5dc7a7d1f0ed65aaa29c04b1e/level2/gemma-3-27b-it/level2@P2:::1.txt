This paper investigates deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), which are essentially stacked RNNs, for time series processing, specifically character-level language modeling. The core idea is that stacking RNNs naturally creates a hierarchy of timescales, enabling the network to capture temporal dependencies at different granularities. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus using simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD), surpassing results achieved with more sophisticated algorithms like Hessian-free optimization. They also provide an analysis of the emergent timescales within the network.

The paper builds upon prior work in deep learning and recurrent neural networks. The motivation for hierarchical processing in RNNs is clearly articulated, contrasting with the memory-focused functionality of standard RNNs. The authors appropriately reference previous attempts at hierarchical RNN architectures [3, 5, 6, 8], acknowledging their limitations and positioning their work as a more thorough investigation, particularly with an emphasis on analysis. They also connect their work to the broader context of deep learning, drawing parallels with deep neural networks (DNNs) and their hierarchical processing capabilities. The reference to [19] and the comparison to Hessian-free optimization is important for establishing the significance of their SGD-based results.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Insightful Idea:** The concept of explicitly exploiting a hierarchy of timescales by stacking RNNs is a valuable contribution. It provides a principled way to approach temporal modeling, and the analysis confirms the emergence of such hierarchies.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance with a relatively simple training algorithm (SGD) is a significant achievement. It suggests that the architectural choice is crucial, potentially outweighing the benefits of more complex optimization methods.
* **Thorough Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply demonstrating performance and attempts to analyze the internal dynamics of the DRNNs. The experiments investigating time scales (typo perturbation and context switching) are well-designed and provide valuable insights into how the network processes information. The parenthesis closing experiment is clever and further supports the claim of long-term dependency modeling.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The motivation is clearly presented, and the experimental setup is described in sufficient detail.  The inclusion of illustrative figures (though referred to but not provided in the submission) would further enhance clarity.
* **Careful Consideration of Training:** The authors address potential issues like vanishing gradients with the DRNN-1O training strategy (layer-wise incremental training) and gradient explosions with gradient normalization.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the text prediction task is a good starting point, the paper would benefit from exploring other time series domains.  The analysis is largely focused on text, and it's unclear how well the DRNN architecture would generalize to other types of data (e.g., speech, sensor data).
* **Network Size and Generalizability:** The choice of network size (approximately 4.9 million parameters) is motivated by comparison to other work [19]. However, the authors acknowledge that larger networks could further improve performance. It would be beneficial to discuss the trade-offs between network size, training time, and performance. The number of layers (5) also seems somewhat arbitrarily chosen.
* **Lack of Ablation Studies:**  While the authors investigate the contribution of individual layers in the DRNN-AO, a more systematic ablation study (e.g., varying the number of layers, the number of units per layer) could provide further insights into the optimal configuration of the DRNN architecture.
* **Figure References Without Figures:** The text refers to Figures 1, 2, 3, and 4, but these figures are not included in the submission. This makes it difficult to fully evaluate the experimental results.
* **Discussion of Limitations:** The paper acknowledges some limitations (e.g., difficulty with nested parentheses). A more comprehensive discussion of potential weaknesses and future research directions would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and insightful idea for hierarchical time series modeling.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate the effectiveness of the DRNN architecture.
* The analysis provides valuable insights into the internal dynamics of the network.
* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The scope of experiments is limited.
* The network size and number of layers are not thoroughly justified.
* The missing figures hinder evaluation.
* A more thorough ablation study would strengthen the conclusions.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a strong paper with a novel contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The core idea of exploiting a hierarchy of timescales by stacking RNNs is compelling, and the empirical results are convincing. While there are some weaknesses, particularly the limited scope of experiments and the missing figures, I believe the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, primarily addressing the missing figures and potentially expanding the experimental evaluation. Specifically, the authors should include the referenced figures in the revised submission.
