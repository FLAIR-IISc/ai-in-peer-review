**Summary of the Paper**

The authors propose a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture that combines the benefits of deep neural networks (DNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The DRNN consists of multiple layers, each of which is a recurrent neural network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This architecture allows for hierarchical processing of time series data and can capture temporal hierarchies more effectively than traditional RNNs. The authors evaluate the performance of the DRNN on a character-level language modeling task and demonstrate that it achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent neural networks on a Wikipedia text corpus.

**Claims and Support**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The DRNN architecture is more effective than traditional RNNs in capturing temporal hierarchies in time series data.
2. The DRNN achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent neural networks on a character-level language modeling task.

The authors provide support for these claims through experimental results on a Wikipedia text corpus, which show that the DRNN outperforms traditional RNNs in terms of bits-per-character (BPC) and can capture long-term dependencies in the data.
