- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): hierarchical RNN layers where each layer receives the previous layer's hidden state to model temporal hierarchies.
- Claims standard RNNs do not explicitly accommodate temporal hierarchies; DRNNs aim to capture multiple time scales.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance among recurrent networks on character-level language modeling on a Wikipedia corpus.
- Builds on prior deep and hierarchical RNN work but contributes new analysis of emergent time scales in stacked recurrent layers.
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing that makes the architecture and analysis easy to follow.
- Strength: thorough analysis showing DRNNs capture temporal hierarchies and long-term dependencies.
- Strength: experimental results are well-presented and empirically support DRNN effectiveness on the chosen task.
- Weakness: lacks direct, detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art recurrent architectures, notably LSTMs.
- Weakness: does not provide a detailed breakdown of computational cost or training resources despite long training times.
- Weakness: would benefit from additional experiments on tasks beyond character-level language modeling to demonstrate generality.
- Originality: presents a novel architecture and a new analysis of emergent time scales—meaningful contribution.
- Significance: potentially impactful for NLP tasks requiring long-term dependency modeling (e.g., language modeling, machine translation).
- Pro-accept: novel architecture, thorough analysis, and strong experimental results support acceptance.
- Con-accept: missing LSTM comparisons, computational cost details, and broader experimental validation argue for more work before acceptance.
- Overall: reviewer recommends acceptance as a strong contribution but requests additional comparisons and experiments.
