- Proposes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) combining DNN and RNN by stacking recurrent layers that take previous layer hidden states for hierarchical temporal processing.
- Claims DRNN better captures temporal hierarchies than traditional RNNs.
- Claims state-of-the-art performance for RNNs on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia corpus).
- Experimental results show improved bits-per-character (BPC) and ability to capture long-term dependencies.
- DRNN is applicable to tasks with time-series data (speech recognition, language modeling, machine translation).
- Provides a detailed analysis of the architecture and its performance, serving as a useful reference.
- Emphasizes the importance of hierarchical processing in time-series data and offers a novel solution.
- Demonstrates strong understanding of related literature and challenges in training deep networks.
- Architecture is novel and a significant improvement over traditional RNNs.
- Paper is thorough and complete in literature review, model description, and evaluation on the chosen task.
- Limitations include high computational training cost and challenging hyperparameter optimization.
- Constructive criticism: evaluate DRNN on a wider range of tasks and datasets (e.g., speech, MT).
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, comprehensive evaluation, and solid field knowledge.
- Arguments against acceptance: high computational cost and limited evaluation breadth.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
