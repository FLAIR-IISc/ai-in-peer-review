- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (stacked RNN layers) hypothesis: hierarchy processes different time scales.  
- Two variants: DRNN-1O (only top layer outputs) and DRNN-AO (all layers output).  
- Evaluated on character-level language modeling on large Wikipedia dataset.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance using simple SGD with gradient clipping, matching multiplicative RNNs and Hessian-Free methods.  
- Experiments (typo perturbation, parentheses handling) show higher layers retain longer-term memory and capture long dependencies.  
- Generative examples and ablation studies illustrate distinct functional roles of layers.  
- Strength: significant finding that simple architectural depth can outperform complex optimization approaches.  
- Strength: high-quality, well-designed quantitative and qualitative analyses supporting the temporal hierarchy claim.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing, diagrams, and experimental details.  
- Weakness: stacking RNNs is not novel; main contribution is large-scale validation and analysis rather than a new architecture.  
- Weakness: incremental/layer-wise training for DRNN-1O appears ad-hoc and needs deeper justification or analysis.  
- Weakness: unexplained result that DRNN-1O outperforms DRNN-AO; paper should discuss possible causes.  
- Weakness: experiments use only tanh units; should justify choice and comment on potential gains from LSTMs.
