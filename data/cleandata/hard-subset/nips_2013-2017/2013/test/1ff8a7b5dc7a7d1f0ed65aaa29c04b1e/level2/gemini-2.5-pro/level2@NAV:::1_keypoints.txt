- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN): stacked RNN layers where each layer consumes the hidden state sequence of the layer below.  
- Central claim 1: DRNNs naturally induce a temporal hierarchy with higher layers operating on longer time scales.  
- Central claim 2: DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using simple SGD.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN ideas but emphasizes a thorough analysis of emergent properties rather than just performance.  
- Primary strength: comprehensive, insightful analysis providing strong evidence for the temporal-hierarchy claim.  
- Empirical result: DRNNs match a Multiplicative RNN trained with Hessian-free optimization while using only SGD, highlighting architectural benefits.  
- Experimental design includes clever visualizations of learned time scales (e.g., measuring decay of a perturbation/typo through layers).  
- Qualitative evidence: DRNNs capture long-term dependencies (e.g., correctly closing parentheses across hundreds of characters).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and details its experimental setup.  
- Weakness: validation is limited to a single dataset/task (character modeling); additional domains like speech or MT would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: analysis focuses more on the DRNN-AO variant, offering less insight into the better-performing DRNN-1O.  
- Recommendation: strong accept—significant, original contribution with well-supported claims; main counterargument is limited empirical breadth.
