- Paper studies stacked hierarchical RNNs (DRNN) for character-level language modeling to capture multi-scale temporal dependencies.  
- Core idea: each RNN layer receives previous layer hidden state, enabling hierarchical processing.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results using simple SGD and analyzes emergent time scales.  
- Well-motivated connection between deep architectures and recurrent hierarchical processing.  
- Good overview of related work, but novelty emphasized via combined performance and analysis.  
- DRNN architecture is straightforward and mathematically well-defined.  
- Strong empirical evaluation: SOTA performance, typo perturbation and context-switching analyses, and ablation studies.  
- Compelling visualizations (e.g., layer contributions in parenthesis matching) showing long-range dependency learning.  
- Related work discussion could be more nuanced, especially regarding prior multi-scale/subsampling approaches ([5]).  
- Insufficient justification and exploration of chosen network size (~4.9M parameters).  
- Training setup section is dense with implementation details; motivations for learning rates and initializations are unclear.  
- Need clearer comparison of computational cost and scalability versus Hessian-free optimization.  
- Parenthesis matching results are interesting but limited; broader tasks probing long-range dependencies are needed.  
- Paper is generally clear but would benefit from more concise organization and moving implementation specifics to an appendix.  
- Arguments for acceptance: SOTA performance, novel deep recurrent application, in-depth emergent time-scale analysis, strong visualizations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weaker related-work treatment, unclear network-size rationale, overly complex training details, limited experimental scope.  
- Essential recommendation: justify network size and study varying layers/neurons; simplify training description and relocate details to appendix.  
- Important recommendation: provide a detailed comparison to existing hierarchical RNN architectures.  
- Optional recommendation: expand experiments to other long-range dependency tasks and analyze computational cost/scalability relative to Hessian-free methods.
