- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) that combine deep hierarchical processing with recurrent temporal feedback.  
- Claims DRNNs capture temporal hierarchies and excel at character-level language modeling.  
- Reports state-of-the-art recurrent-network performance on a Wikipedia text corpus using SGD.  
- Analyzes emergent time scales in DRNNs, showing layers model short- and long-term dependencies.  
- Novel and significant extension of RNNs that advances character-level sequence modeling.  
- Comprehensive experiments and comparisons show competitive performance with less compute than Hessian-free methods.  
- Practical utility demonstrated in text generation, preserving grammatical structure and long-range dependencies.  
- Experiments are limited to character-level language modeling, reducing demonstrated generality.  
- DRNN-1O requires incremental layer-wise training, which may hinder scalability and usability.  
- Lacks comparisons to other advanced sequence models (e.g., Transformers).  
- Reproducibility concerns due to high computational cost and no public code release.  
- Suggests evaluating DRNN on diverse time series tasks to show versatility.  
- Recommends exploring alternative training methods (e.g., second-order optimizers) to avoid layer-wise training issues.  
- Advises comparing to Transformer-based models and releasing code to improve context and reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing task diversity and reproducibility in future work.
