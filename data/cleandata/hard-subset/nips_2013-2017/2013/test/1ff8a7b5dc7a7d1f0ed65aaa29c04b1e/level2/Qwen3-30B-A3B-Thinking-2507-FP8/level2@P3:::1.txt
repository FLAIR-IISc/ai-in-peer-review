

The paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture where each layer is a recurrent network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, creating a temporal hierarchy. This structure allows different layers to process information at different time scales, addressing a limitation of standard RNNs that do not explicitly accommodate temporal hierarchies. The authors demonstrate DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling using simple stochastic gradient descent, without requiring more complex training methods like Hessian-free optimization. They provide detailed analysis of emergent time scales through perturbation experiments, ablation studies, and examination of long-term dependencies.

The work makes a significant contribution by extending the deep learning paradigm to recurrent networks in a way that explicitly creates temporal hierarchies. The paper effectively contrasts with previous hierarchical RNN approaches [3,5,6,8] by focusing on the temporal hierarchy aspect rather than just performance gains. The analysis of time scales through the typo-perturbation experiment is particularly compelling, showing clear differences in how information is processed across layers. The generative text examples and parentheses-closing experiments provide concrete evidence of the model's ability to handle long-term dependencies.

The quality of the work is strong, with thorough experimental validation across multiple metrics. The ablation studies on layer contributions and time scale analysis provide valuable insights into how the architecture functions. The training method using SGD is well-justified and effective, demonstrating that simpler training approaches can achieve competitive results. The paper adequately addresses the strengths and weaknesses of the approach, noting that DRNN-AO (with output connections at all layers) performs slightly worse than DRNN-1O (with output connections only at the top layer) but provides more detailed insights into layer contributions.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with appropriate figures and explanations of the architecture. The experimental setup is well-described, though additional implementation details (e.g., exact network parameter configurations beyond layer counts and neuron numbers) would improve reproducibility. The comparison to related work, particularly the stacked LSTM approach [8], is thoughtful and well-justified.

The main area for improvement is the theoretical foundation. While the experimental results are compelling, the paper lacks a deeper theoretical analysis explaining why DRNNs create effective temporal hierarchies and how this relates to the network's performance. A more rigorous theoretical treatment of the time scale hierarchy would strengthen the paper significantly. The paper could also more explicitly contrast its temporal hierarchy approach with previous hierarchical RNN works, particularly regarding the explicit modeling of time scales rather than using subsampling.

The significance of the work is clear: DRNNs offer a promising framework for modeling temporal hierarchies in time series data, achieving state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks with simpler training methods. The analysis of time scales provides valuable insights into how hierarchical processing can be effectively applied to temporal data. The paper opens several important future research directions, including supervised pre-training for deep architectures and applying second-order optimization techniques to DRNNs.

The paper advances the state of the art in recurrent network architectures by demonstrating how explicit temporal hierarchies can be built into the network structure. The results on character-level language modeling are particularly convincing, showing that DRNNs can model long-term dependencies effectively. The work has potential applications beyond language modeling to any temporal data with hierarchical structure.

Overall, the paper makes a strong contribution to the field, with solid experimental validation and insightful analysis. With minor improvements to the theoretical grounding and implementation details, it would be an even stronger contribution. The work is well-suited for NIPS as it addresses a fundamental question about recurrent network architecture with clear experimental validation and significant implications for future research.
