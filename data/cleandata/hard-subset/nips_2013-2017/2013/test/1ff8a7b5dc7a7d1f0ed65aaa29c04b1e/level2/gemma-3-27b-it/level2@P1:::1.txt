This paper investigates the effect of stacking recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to create a hierarchy, termed a Deep RNN (DRNN). The motivation stems from the observation that time series often exhibit hierarchical temporal structure, which standard RNNs don't explicitly model. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art character-level language modeling performance using a DRNN trained with simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD), and offer an analysis of the emergent time scales within the network.

The paper is well-motivated and addresses an interesting question. The idea of explicitly modeling hierarchical temporal structure with stacked RNNs is intuitive, and the results demonstrating competitive performance with more complex training algorithms (Hessian-free) is a significant contribution. The analysis of emergent time scales, while not entirely novel (see related work below), is valuable and provides some insight into how the DRNN architecture learns to process time series.  The connection made between DNNs and RNNs through this hierarchical approach is also a noteworthy perspective.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong empirical results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling with a relatively simple training method (SGD) is a clear strength.
*   **Clear motivation and explanation:** The paper clearly articulates the motivation behind the DRNN architecture and the potential benefits of modeling hierarchical temporal structure.
*   **Analysis of time scales:** The attempt to analyze the emergent time scales within the DRNN is a valuable addition and provides some insights into the network's behavior.
*   **Well-written and organized:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with a logical flow of ideas.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited novelty in architecture:** The core idea of stacking RNNs isn’t entirely novel (e.g., [5, 8] are mentioned, but the contributions of those works are somewhat downplayed). While the authors acknowledge related work, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of how their approach differs and builds upon existing hierarchical RNN architectures.  Specifically, the connection to hierarchical temporal memory models would be useful.
*   **Limited discussion of hyperparameters:** The paper provides some detail on the network setup, but lacks a comprehensive discussion of the hyperparameter tuning process. How sensitive are the results to the choice of learning rate, number of layers, and number of units per layer?
*   **Analysis could be more in-depth:** While the analysis of time scales is a good start, it could be expanded. For example, the authors could explore how different layers respond to different frequencies of input signals.  The analysis primarily focuses on perturbing the input; analyzing the learned representations within each layer would be beneficial.
* **Output Connection Strategies:** The rationale for DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO isn’t entirely convincing. The explanation regarding pathological curvature feels slightly hand-wavy.  More rigorous justification or ablation studies would be helpful.
* **Comparison to LSTMs:** The paper compares to a standard RNN but not to LSTMs or GRUs directly. Given that those are the dominant RNN variants, a direct comparison would be valuable, even if the authors anticipate DRNNs not necessarily outperforming them on tasks specifically designed for long-term memory.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel perspective on combining DNNs and RNNs for processing time series.
*   The empirical results are strong and demonstrate the effectiveness of the DRNN architecture.
*   The analysis of emergent time scales provides some insights into the network’s behavior.
*   The paper is well-written and organized.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The core idea of stacking RNNs is not entirely novel.
*   The analysis of time scales could be more in-depth.
*   The hyperparameter tuning process is not fully discussed.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a solid contribution to the field, and I lean towards acceptance. The empirical results are compelling, and the paper addresses an important question about modeling hierarchical temporal structure.  However, the authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by providing a more thorough discussion of related work, the hyperparameter tuning process, and a more in-depth analysis of the learned representations.  Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its impact.

**Rating:** 7/10 (Accept with minor revisions)
