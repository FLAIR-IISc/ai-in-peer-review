- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) where each layer is a recurrent network receiving the previous layer’s hidden state, forming a temporal hierarchy.  
- Enables different layers to operate at different time scales, addressing standard RNNs’ lack of explicit temporal hierarchies.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on character-level language modeling using simple SGD, avoiding complex training like Hessian-free optimization.  
- Provides detailed analysis of emergent time scales via perturbation experiments, ablations, and long-term dependency examinations.  
- Extends deep learning to recurrent networks by explicitly constructing temporal hierarchies, a significant conceptual contribution.  
- Differentiates from prior hierarchical RNN work by focusing on temporal hierarchy rather than only performance gains.  
- The typo-perturbation experiment compellingly demonstrates clear differences in information processing across layers.  
- Generative text samples and parentheses-closing tasks offer concrete evidence of effective long-term dependency modeling.  
- Strong experimental validation across multiple metrics supports the model’s effectiveness.  
- Ablation studies on layer contributions and time scales yield valuable insights into how the architecture functions.  
- Demonstrates that simple SGD is sufficient and well-justified for training DRNNs, achieving competitive results.  
- Notes DRNN-AO (outputs at all layers) performs slightly worse than DRNN-1O (top-layer outputs only) but aids layer contribution analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes appropriate figures and architectural explanations.  
- Experimental setup is well-described, though lacking exact implementation details (network parameter configurations) for full reproducibility.  
- Comparison to related work, especially stacked LSTM, is thoughtful and well-justified.  
- Main weakness is limited theoretical grounding explaining why DRNNs produce effective temporal hierarchies and how this relates to performance.  
- Recommends a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the time-scale hierarchy and clearer contrasts with subsampling-based hierarchical RNNs.  
- Highlights significance: DRNNs are a promising framework for temporal hierarchies with broad applicability beyond language modeling.  
- Identifies future directions: supervised pre-training for deep architectures and applying second-order optimization methods to DRNNs.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with solid experiments and insightful analysis; minor theoretical and implementation details would further strengthen the paper and make it suitable for NIPS.
