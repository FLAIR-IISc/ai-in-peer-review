This paper proposes and analyzes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture for modeling time series. The architecture consists of multiple stacked RNN layers, where each layer receives the sequence of hidden states from the layer below it as its input. The central hypothesis is that this hierarchical structure allows the model to process information at different time scales, which is a natural fit for complex sequential data like language. The authors apply this model to the task of character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia dataset. They present two variants: one where only the top layer produces an output (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The key findings are that these DRNNs, trained with simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and gradient clipping, achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained with more complex models (Multiplicative RNNs) and advanced second-order optimization methods (Hessian-Free). Furthermore, the paper provides an insightful analysis demonstrating that the layers of the DRNN indeed learn to operate on different time scales and can capture very long-term dependencies.

This work builds upon the general trend of deep learning, which posits that hierarchical representations are powerful. It relates to prior work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio, 1996) and, more recently, stacked LSTMs for speech recognition (Graves et al., 2013). However, where prior work was often focused on performance or applied to simpler tasks, this paper's main contribution is a detailed analysis of the emergent properties of the deep recurrent architecture on a very challenging, large-scale benchmark, providing strong evidence for the temporal hierarchy hypothesis.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Significance and Impact:** The paper's most significant contribution is demonstrating that a relatively simple architectural modification—stacking standard RNNs—can achieve state-of-the-art performance using a first-order training method (SGD). At a time when significant effort was being invested in complex second-order optimizers to train RNNs, this result strongly suggests that architecture design is a crucial and perhaps more straightforward path to improving performance on difficult sequential tasks. This is a very important finding for the community.

2.  **Quality of Analysis:** The paper goes well beyond simply reporting a new state-of-the-art BPC score. The authors conduct a series of well-designed experiments to analyze the model's internal behavior, which substantially strengthens the paper.
    *   The "typo perturbation" experiment (Figure 3, left) provides direct evidence that higher layers maintain memory of perturbations for longer, quantitatively supporting the claim of a learned temporal hierarchy.
    *   The analysis of how the model handles parentheses (Figure 4) is an excellent, concrete example of the model's ability to learn and maintain state over hundreds of time steps, demonstrating a grasp of non-trivial syntactic structure.
    *   The generative examples in Table 2, particularly the ablation studies where top or bottom layer contributions are removed, offer compelling qualitative insights into the functional roles of the different layers in the hierarchy.

3.  **Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The DRNN architecture is explained concisely with helpful diagrams. The motivation for the two variants (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) is well-articulated, and the experimental setup is described in sufficient detail. The results are presented effectively, making the paper easy to follow and the conclusions convincing.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**

1.  **Originality of the Architecture:** While the analysis is novel, the core architectural idea of stacking recurrent layers is not entirely new, as the authors rightly acknowledge by citing prior work. The main novelty is in the demonstration that this structure, when scaled up and properly analyzed on a difficult task, exhibits these properties and achieves such strong performance. The paper could be strengthened by positioning its primary contribution more explicitly as the analysis and large-scale validation of this architectural principle, rather than the invention of the DRNN itself.

2.  **Training Method for DRNN-1O:** The incremental, layer-wise training scheme used for the DRNN-1O is described as "necessary to reach good performance." This is an interesting methodological point, but it feels somewhat ad-hoc without further justification. A deeper discussion on *why* this is necessary would be very valuable. Is it a way to mitigate the vanishing/exploding gradient problem through a very deep (in time and space) network? Providing more intuition or analysis here could elevate this from a training "trick" to a more principled contribution for training deep sequential models.

3.  **Performance of DRNN-AO vs. DRNN-1O:** It is interesting that the DRNN-1O performs slightly better than the DRNN-AO, even though the DRNN-AO was partly motivated by providing more direct error signals to the lower layers. The paper does not speculate on why this might be. One hypothesis could be that forcing intermediate layers to make predictions constrains their ability to learn more abstract representations solely for the benefit of the layers above them. A brief discussion of this counter-intuitive result would be a welcome addition.

4.  **Choice of Tanh Units:** The experiments exclusively use simple tanh RNN units, which are known to be difficult to train on tasks requiring long memory. While the results are impressive, it would be beneficial to include a brief justification for this choice over more sophisticated units like LSTMs. Was this done to isolate the effect of depth? A comment on whether the authors expect that combining the deep architecture with LSTMs would yield further improvements would be helpful for situating the work.

In summary, this is a strong paper that presents an important result supported by a thorough and insightful analysis. It successfully demonstrates that architectural depth is a powerful mechanism for learning temporal hierarchies in sequential data. The weaknesses are minor and mostly relate to opportunities for deeper discussion and clarification. The work is a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning and recurrent neural networks.
