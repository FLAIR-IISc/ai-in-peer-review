- Proposes deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture stacking RNN layers hierarchically to model multiple time scales.  
- Evaluated on character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus.  
- Main contributions: matches state-of-the-art with simple SGD (vs. complex second-order methods) and provides detailed analysis showing a temporal hierarchy emerges.  
- High technical quality: clear model definitions, sound experimental setup, and fair comparison to strong baselines.  
- Analytical probes convincingly show different layers learn different time scales and capture long-range dependencies (e.g., parentheses).  
- Very well-written and organized with clear motivation; model equations, training details, and informative figures (notably Fig. 3).  
- Originality: architecture builds on prior work but is novel in its deep, systematic analysis of why stacking RNNs works and demonstrating SGD efficacy.  
- Significance: highlights that architectural depth can rival optimization choice, introduces diagnostic techniques useful to others, and likely influenced wider adoption of multi-layer LSTMs/GRUs.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion on why incremental, layer-wise training for DRNN-1O was necessary and related optimization challenges (e.g., vanishing gradients).  
- Suggestion: clarify the exact gradient normalization/clipping mechanism and the values used for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: consider (future) comparisons to wider flat RNNs to further isolate the benefit of depth despite computational cost.  
- Overall: excellent paper with strong contributions and only minor clarifications needed.
