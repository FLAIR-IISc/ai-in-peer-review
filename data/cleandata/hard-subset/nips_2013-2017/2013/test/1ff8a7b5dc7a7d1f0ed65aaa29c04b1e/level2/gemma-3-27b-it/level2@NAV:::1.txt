## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper investigates the effect of stacking recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to create a deep recurrent architecture (DRNN) capable of processing time series with inherent hierarchical structures. The core idea – building a hierarchy of RNNs where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous one – is a natural extension of deep learning principles to the recurrent domain. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling using a relatively simple training procedure (SGD), and provide initial analysis of the emergent time scales within the DRNN.

The paper builds upon prior work in deep learning and RNNs, correctly identifying the limitations of standard RNNs in explicitly capturing temporal hierarchies. It acknowledges related work on hierarchical RNNs ([3, 5, 6, 8]), but argues their analysis is lacking. The DRNN architecture studied is clearly defined, and the mathematical formulation of hidden state evolution is presented concisely. The experimental setup, focused on character-level language modeling with a large Wikipedia corpus, is appropriate for validating the hypothesis that DRNNs can effectively capture both short- and long-term dependencies. 

A key strength of this work lies in its empirical results. Achieving state-of-the-art performance with only SGD is noteworthy, especially given previous reliance on more complex algorithms like Hessian-free optimization [19]. The analysis of emergent time scales, through perturbation experiments and observation of parenthesis handling, provides initial insights into how the network organizes information across layers. The visualizations are helpful in illustrating these dynamics.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. While the results are promising, the ablation studies (removing layer contributions) are somewhat limited. A more systematic analysis of the impact of different network configurations (e.g., varying the number of layers, neurons per layer) would strengthen the conclusions.  Furthermore, the training details, while adequate, could benefit from a more thorough discussion of hyperparameter tuning. The comparison to other models (Table 1) includes results from different datasets/criteria, making direct comparison difficult. The discussion regarding the advantages of DRNNs over LSTMs for tasks focusing solely on memory depth is somewhat dismissive. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:** The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture for processing sequential data, achieves strong empirical results, and offers preliminary analysis of internal network dynamics. The simplicity of the training approach is particularly appealing.

**Arguments against Acceptance:** The analysis of the emergent time scales is somewhat limited, and a more thorough ablation study would be beneficial. The comparison to prior work could be strengthened.

**Overall:** This is a solid contribution with interesting findings. While not groundbreaking, it represents a worthwhile addition to the literature on deep recurrent networks. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points mentioned above. The authors should consider expanding the ablation studies and providing a more consistent comparison with existing models.
