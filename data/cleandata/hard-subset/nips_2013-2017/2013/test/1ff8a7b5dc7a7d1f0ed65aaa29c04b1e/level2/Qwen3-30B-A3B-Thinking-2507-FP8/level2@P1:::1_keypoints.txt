- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacking RNN layers to create an explicit temporal hierarchy.  
- Demonstrates DRNNs learn distinct time scales per layer (bottom layers short-term, top layers long-term) with empirical evidence.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using plain SGD (BPC=1.15), challenging the need for complex optimization.  
- Rigorous experimental analysis: typo-perturbation test, parentheses-closing task, ablations, and comparisons to standard RNNs and DRNN variants.  
- Paper is well-organized and clear, with effective figures and concise notation.  
- DRNN-1O training procedure lacks key details (how layers were added sequentially, learning rate choices), harming reproducibility.  
- The reason DRNN-1O outperforms DRNN-AO is not explained; trade-offs of output connections need discussion.  
- Scope is limited to character-level language modeling; generalization to other domains (speech, video, word-level tasks) is untested.  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing hyperparameters (batch size, exact sequence lengths, epochs), single-GPU statement, and only three network instantiations.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, novel contribution with addressable weaknesses.  
- Pro-accept summary: interpretable temporal hierarchy, SOTA with simple SGD, thorough empirical analysis.  
- Con-accept summary: limited task scope, methodological ambiguities, and insufficient evidence of generalization.
