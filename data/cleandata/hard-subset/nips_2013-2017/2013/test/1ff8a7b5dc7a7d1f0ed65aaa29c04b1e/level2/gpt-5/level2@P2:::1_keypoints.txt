- Paper studies stacked vanilla RNNs with two output schemes (top-only DRNN-1O and all-layer summed DRNN-AO), showing competitive BPC on a large Wikipedia char corpus using SGD with gradient normalization and incremental deepening.  
- Main novelty is empirical: simple stacked tanh RNNs trained with SGD can match HF-trained multiplicative RNNs, plus a careful analysis of emergent temporal hierarchies and deep supervision effects.  
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated architecture and simple, effective incremental layer-wise training.  
- Strength: Competitive performance on a large dataset using plain SGD, narrowing gap with Hessian-free MRNNs at similar parameter counts.  
- Strength: Thoughtful empirical analyses probing layer time scales (typo/context experiments) and long-range behavior (parentheses), with interpretable findings that higher layers capture longer contexts.  
- Strength: Useful ablation by removing layer outputs in DRNN-AO to assess direct predictive contribution across depths.  
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental—stacked/hierarchical RNNs and deep supervision are established in prior work.  
- Weakness: Missing crucial baselines—no LSTM or stacked LSTM matched for data and hyperparameters, making claims about long-term modeling weaker.  
- Weakness: BPC comparability is muddied by differences in character set and preprocessing/splits (95 vs 86 chars); the impact is asserted but not quantified.  
- Weakness: Limited runs (only three) and no variance estimates, reducing confidence in fine-grained comparisons (e.g., DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO).  
- Weakness: Layer-output ablation (zeroing) may be confounded by inter-layer synergies; more principled probes are needed.  
- Weakness: Observed time-scale hierarchy appears training-dependent (e.g., due to incremental deepening) rather than purely architectural, requiring deeper analysis.  
- Weakness: Some training choices need justification (zero-initialized outputs, fixed BPTT=200, single depth/width trade-off), and claims of >500-char memory lack clear gradient support.  
- Quality: Technically sound and experiments are reasonable, but evidence for “state-of-the-art” is not fully controlled due to missing baselines and dataset mismatch.  
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written and reproducible in many respects, but some references and preprocessing/initialization details are incomplete.  
- Originality: Moderate—main contributions are empirical demonstration and analysis toolkit rather than novel architecture.  
- Significance: Moderate—useful to practitioners wanting strong char-level models with SGD and to researchers studying temporal representations, but impact limited by missing baselines/benchmarks.  
- Key suggestions: add LSTM/stacked LSTM baselines trained comparably; evaluate on standardized datasets (e.g., enwik8) or match char set; report mean±std over multiple runs.  
- Key suggestions: explore depth/width trade-offs, use linear probes on frozen layer states instead of zeroing ablations, and analyze layer dynamics more directly (e.g., Jacobians/spectral norms).  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — insightful empirical analysis and useful training recipe, but paper needs stronger baselines and controlled comparisons to fully justify claims.
