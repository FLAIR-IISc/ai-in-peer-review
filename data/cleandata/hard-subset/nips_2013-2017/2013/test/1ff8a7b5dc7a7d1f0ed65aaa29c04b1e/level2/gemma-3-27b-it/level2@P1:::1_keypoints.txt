- Paper proposes Deep RNN (DRNN): stacked RNNs to model hierarchical temporal structure and analyzes emergent time scales.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using DRNN trained with simple SGD.
- Motivation is clear: time series often have hierarchical temporal structure that standard RNNs don’t explicitly model.
- Strong empirical results showing competitive performance versus more complex training methods (e.g., Hessian-free).
- Clear explanation and connection between DNNs and RNNs via hierarchical stacking.
- Analysis of emergent time scales provides useful insights into DRNN behavior.
- Paper is well-written and organized, with a logical flow.
- Limited novelty: stacking RNNs is not entirely new and related work (e.g., prior hierarchical RNNs) is somewhat downplayed.
- Needs a more thorough discussion of how this approach differs from and builds on existing hierarchical temporal models.
- Hyperparameter tuning is under-discussed; sensitivity to learning rate, layer count, and units per layer is unclear.
- Time-scale analysis could be deeper—e.g., layer responses to different input frequencies and analysis of learned internal representations.
- Rationale for output connection strategies (DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO) is not fully convincing; requires more rigorous justification or ablation studies.
- Lacks direct comparison to LSTM/GRU baselines, which are the dominant RNN variants for long-term dependencies.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel perspective combining DNN and RNN ideas, strong empirical results, and insightful time-scale analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, insufficiently deep analysis, and incomplete hyperparameter/reporting details.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution, leans toward acceptance pending revisions to address related work, hyperparameter discussion, and deeper analysis.
- Reviewer rating: 7/10 (Accept with minor revisions).
