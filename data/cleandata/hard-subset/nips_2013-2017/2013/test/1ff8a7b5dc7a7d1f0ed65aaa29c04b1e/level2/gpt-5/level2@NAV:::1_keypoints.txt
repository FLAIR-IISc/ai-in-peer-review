- Proposes two DRNN variants (single-output DRNN-1O and all-layers-to-output DRNN-AO) for character-level language modeling and matches prior SOTA for comparable RNN size on a large Wikipedia corpus.
- Uses simple SGD with gradient-norm normalization and an incremental layer-wise training schedule to train deep recurrence effectively.
- Introduces analyses (perturbation experiments, context-switch tests, bracket-closing case study) showing emergent temporal hierarchies across layers.
- Connects to prior hierarchical and second-order RNN work but emphasizes architectural design and interpretability over new optimization methods.
- Methodology is generally sound with clear model definitions, training details, and useful ablations (layer contribution tests).
- Analyses of time scales and long-range dependencies are insightful and novel, especially the perturbation-based probes and parenthesis case.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to one dataset and only three model instantiations, with no variance across seeds or statistical testing.
- Missing important baselines, notably stacked LSTM/GRU and HF-trained stacked vanilla RNNs, weakening comparative claims.
- Reporting could be clearer on exact training passes, gradient normalization vs clipping, and wall-clock/compute budgets.
- Originality is incremental (stacking not new) but contributions like all-layers output, incremental training protocol, and temporal analysis are valuable.
- Significance is promising as a simple alternative to second-order methods and as an interpretability tool, but impact is limited by narrow evaluation.
- Pros: strong performance with simple SGD, careful multi-scale analyses, and practical training insights (incremental schedule, AO output).
- Cons: narrow empirical scope, lack of statistical rigor (few runs, no error bars), and reproducibility concerns due to long training and no released code.
- Constructive suggestions: add strong gated-RNN baselines and multiple seeds, report compute/gradient-handling details, evaluate on more corpora, provide per-layer parameter/compute trade-offs, release code, and quantify per-layer effective context.
- Overall: promising and well-analyzed work that would be a clear accept with stronger baselines and broader, more rigorous experiments.
