- Proposal: Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) where each layer is a recurrent network processing the previous layer’s hidden state to enable hierarchical temporal processing.  
- Claims: DRNNs naturally capture multi-scale temporal hierarchies in time series.  
- Claims: DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art recurrent-network performance on character-level language modeling using only SGD.  
- Claims: Layer-wise time scales emerge during training, verified via perturbation analysis and parenthesis-closing tasks.  
- Experimental support: Strong evidence for claims (1) and (3) — time-scale analysis and parenthesis task convincingly show layer-specific memory and long-term dependency modeling.  
- Time-scale analysis: Fig. 3 shows distinct bottom-layer short-term and top-layer long-term processing, especially for DRNN-AO.  
- Parenthesis task: DRNN-AO outperforms standard RNNs (12% failure vs. 28%), validating modeling of long-term dependencies.  
- Language modeling results: DRNN-1O achieves 1.37 BPC vs RNN 1.51 BPC, statistically significant and competitive with Hessian-free baselines.  
- Compute caveat: Paper understates computational comparisons — Hessian-free baselines used 8 GPUs while DRNNs trained with single-GPU SGD.  
- Strengths: Clear theoretical motivation for temporal hierarchies, solid ablation studies (e.g., layer removal), and insightful emergent time-scale analysis.  
- Limitations: Significance claim (SOTA) weakened by no comparison to modern architectures (e.g., Transformers or Transformer-XL).  
- Experimental scope concerns: Only three network instantiations, no hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and no scalability analysis beyond 5 layers.  
- Methodological concern: Layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is presented as necessary without theoretical justification, raising reproducibility questions.  
- Related work: Correctly cites foundational RNN/LSTM work but misses recent hierarchical temporal models and somewhat overstates architecture novelty given prior stacked-LSTM uses.  
- Suggested revisions: Compare to Transformer-based baselines, test on explicitly hierarchical tasks (e.g., multi-resolution video), and explain or justify layer-wise training.  
- Overall verdict: Core insight about emergent time scales is valuable, but narrow experimental scope and missing comparisons limit impact; address critiques and it could be a strong temporal-modeling contribution.
