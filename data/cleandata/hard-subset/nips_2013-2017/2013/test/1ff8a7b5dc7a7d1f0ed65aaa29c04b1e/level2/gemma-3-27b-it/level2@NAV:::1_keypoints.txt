- Studies stacking RNNs into a deep recurrent architecture (DRNN) to capture hierarchical temporal structure; core idea is a natural deep extension.  
- DRNN architecture is clearly defined with concise mathematical formulation of hidden state evolution.  
- Experiments on character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus are appropriate to validate the approach.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art character-level performance using simple SGD, notable compared to prior reliance on Hessian-free optimization.  
- Provides initial analysis of emergent time scales via perturbation experiments and parenthesis-handling observations; visualizations are helpful.  
- Situates work relative to prior hierarchical RNNs, arguing earlier analyses were insufficient.  
- Ablation studies are limited; more systematic exploration of layer counts and neurons per layer is needed.  
- Training and hyperparameter tuning discussion is insufficiently detailed.  
- Table 1 comparisons mix different datasets/criteria, hindering direct comparison to other models.  
- Discussion downplays LSTM advantages for memory-depth tasks in a somewhat dismissive way.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand ablation studies and provide more consistent comparisons with existing models.
