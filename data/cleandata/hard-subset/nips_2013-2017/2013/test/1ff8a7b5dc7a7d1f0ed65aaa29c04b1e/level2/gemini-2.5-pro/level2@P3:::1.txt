This paper proposes and analyzes a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture, where multiple RNN layers are stacked hierarchically. The central idea is that such an architecture is better suited for modeling time series with complex temporal structures, as each layer can process information at a different time scale. The work evaluates this architecture on the task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. The main contributions are twofold: first, it is shown that a DRNN trained with simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD) can achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained with more complex second-order optimization methods on different recurrent architectures. Second, the paper provides a detailed analysis of the trained DRNNs, presenting compelling evidence that a temporal hierarchy emerges, with different layers learning to operate on different time scales. This analytical component is a significant contribution, moving beyond pure performance metrics to offer insights into the model's internal workings. The work builds on prior research into hierarchical and deep recurrent models, such as the work by El Hihi & Bengio (1996) and more recently Graves et al. (2013), but distinguishes itself by its in-depth analysis of the learned representations and temporal dynamics on a challenging natural language task.

**Quality**
The paper is of high technical quality. The proposed models are clearly defined, and the experimental setup is sound. The claims are well-supported by a thorough set of experiments, including not only performance benchmarks but also a series of insightful analytical probes. The investigation into the learned time scales, the roles of different layers, and the ability to capture long-range dependencies (e.g., parentheses) is particularly well-executed and provides strong evidence for the paper's central hypotheses. The comparison against a strong baseline from previous work (Sutskever et al., 2011) is fair and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Clarity**
The paper is very well-written and organized. The motivation for DRNNs is clearly articulated by contrasting them with both standard deep feed-forward networks and standard "flat" RNNs. The model descriptions, equations, and training procedures are presented with sufficient detail. The figures and tables are informative and effectively support the main points of the analysis. Figure 3, in particular, provides a clear and convincing visualization of the different time scales emerging in the network layers.

**Originality**
While the concept of stacked or hierarchical RNNs is not entirely new (e.g., Graves et al., 2013), the originality of this work lies in its deep and systematic analysis of *why* such an architecture is effective. It moves beyond simply reporting a new state-of-the-art result to providing a qualitative and quantitative understanding of the model's behavior. The application of this architecture to character-level language modeling and the detailed analysis of the learned temporal hierarchy are novel contributions. Furthermore, demonstrating that this architecture can match the performance of more complex multiplicative RNNs trained with Hessian-free optimization using only SGD is a significant and original finding.

**Significance**
The results presented are highly significant. The work provides strong evidence that architectural choices, specifically depth in RNNs, can be as important as the choice of optimization algorithm for achieving high performance on complex sequence modeling tasks. This finding has broad implications for practitioners and researchers. The analytical techniques used in the paper (e.g., the "typo test," layer-contribution analysis) provide a valuable template for understanding and diagnosing other deep learning models. The success of the DRNN architecture likely encouraged further research and the now-widespread use of multi-layer LSTMs and GRUs in a vast range of applications.

**Suggestions for Improvement**
The paper is very strong, but could be further improved with a few minor clarifications:
1.  The incremental, layer-wise training procedure for the DRNN-1O model is noted as being necessary to achieve good performance. It would be beneficial to expand on why this was the case. For instance, was training the full deep stack from scratch prone to vanishing gradients? A brief discussion of the optimization challenges encountered would provide valuable insight for others working on similar deep sequential models.
2.  The gradient normalization procedure is described as a "trick of normalizing the gradient before using it for weight updates." For reproducibility, it would be helpful to specify the exact mechanism, such as stating whether this corresponds to clipping the gradient norm to a fixed value and, if so, what value was used.
3.  The comparison is made between a deep, narrow DRNN and a flat, wide RNN with the same parameter count. To further strengthen the argument for depth, it might have been interesting to also compare against a wider standard RNN, though this is understood to be a significant computational expense and could be considered future work.

In summary, this is an excellent paper that presents a powerful model, achieves state-of-the-art results, and, most importantly, provides a deep and insightful analysis of its inner workings. The strengths far outweigh the minor weaknesses, and the work represents a significant contribution to the understanding and application of recurrent neural networks.
