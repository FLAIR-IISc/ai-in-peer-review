## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper investigates the impact of hierarchical recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) on processing time series data, specifically character-level language modeling. The core idea – stacking RNNs to create a hierarchy capable of capturing multi-scale temporal dependencies – builds upon prior work in both deep neural networks and recurrent architectures, notably referencing the limitations of standard RNNs in explicitly modeling hierarchical structure (as highlighted by comparisons to DNNs) and acknowledging related hierarchical RNN approaches [3, 5, 6, 8]. The authors propose a DRNN where each layer is an RNN receiving the hidden state of the previous layer, and demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus using simple stochastic gradient descent.

The paper’s strength lies in its clear articulation of the motivation for hierarchical RNNs and the empirical validation through competitive results. Achieving state-of-the-art performance with SGD, surpassing previous results requiring more complex optimization techniques like Hessian-free training [19], is a significant achievement. The analysis of emergent time scales, evidenced through perturbation experiments (Figure 3) and parenthesis-closing tests (Figure 4), provides valuable insight into the network’s internal dynamics and supports the claim of hierarchical processing. The detailed training setup and ablation studies (examining layer contributions) further strengthen the analysis.

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses. While the results are compelling, the limited number of network instantiations (three) raises concerns about the robustness of the findings. The discussion of the DRNN-1O training strategy (incremental layer-wise) feels somewhat ad-hoc and lacks a deeper justification. Furthermore, the comparison to other models is occasionally hampered by differing datasets or evaluation criteria. The analysis of time scales, while insightful, could be expanded with more quantitative metrics beyond Euclidean distance. The writing, while generally clear, occasionally becomes dense and repetitive.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Achieves state-of-the-art results with a relatively simple training method.
*   Provides empirical evidence for the emergence of hierarchical time scales.
*   Clear motivation and connection to existing literature.
*   Detailed experimental setup and ablation studies.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited number of network instantiations.
*   Ad-hoc training strategy for DRNN-1O.
*   Inconsistent comparison metrics across different models.



**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep recurrent neural networks. While some aspects could be strengthened, the compelling results and insightful analysis warrant acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more thorough discussion of the training strategy and a more robust evaluation with a larger number of network instantiations.
