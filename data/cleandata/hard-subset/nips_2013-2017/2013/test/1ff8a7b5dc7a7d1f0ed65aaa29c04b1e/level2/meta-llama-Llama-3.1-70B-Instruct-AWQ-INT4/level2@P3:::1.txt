This paper presents a novel approach to training and analyzing deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs). The authors propose a hierarchical architecture where each layer is a recurrent neural network, and each subsequent layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This architecture allows for hierarchical processing of time series data and captures the temporal hierarchy present in the input signal.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed architecture and its benefits. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the DRNN in character-level language modeling, achieving state-of-the-art performance for recurrent neural networks on a Wikipedia text corpus. The authors also provide an analysis of the emergent time scales in the DRNN, showing that the network can learn to model long-term dependencies and capture temporal hierarchies.

The paper's strengths include:

1. Novel architecture: The proposed DRNN architecture is a significant contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The hierarchical structure allows for more efficient processing of time series data and captures the temporal hierarchy present in the input signal.
2. Experimental results: The authors provide comprehensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the DRNN in character-level language modeling. The results show that the DRNN achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent neural networks on a Wikipedia text corpus.
3. Analysis of emergent time scales: The authors provide an analysis of the emergent time scales in the DRNN, showing that the network can learn to model long-term dependencies and capture temporal hierarchies.

The paper's weaknesses include:

1. Limited analysis of the training method: The authors propose a training method that involves incremental layer-wise training, but they do not provide a detailed analysis of the effectiveness of this method. A more comprehensive analysis of the training method would strengthen the paper.
2. Limited comparison to other architectures: The authors compare the DRNN to a common RNN, but they do not provide a comparison to other architectures, such as LSTMs or GRUs. A more comprehensive comparison to other architectures would provide a better understanding of the DRNN's strengths and weaknesses.
3. Limited discussion of the limitations of the DRNN: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion of the limitations of the DRNN. A more comprehensive discussion of the limitations would provide a better understanding of the DRNN's strengths and weaknesses.
