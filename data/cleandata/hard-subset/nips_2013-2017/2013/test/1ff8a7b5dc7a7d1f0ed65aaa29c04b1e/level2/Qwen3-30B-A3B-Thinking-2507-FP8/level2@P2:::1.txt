

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a hierarchical architecture where each layer is a recurrent network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue this architecture better captures temporal hierarchies in time series data compared to standard RNNs, which implicitly embed temporal hierarchies in network dynamics rather than explicitly modeling them. The paper claims DRNNs reach state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks in character-level language modeling using simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD), previously requiring more advanced Hessian-free training.

The paper makes several key contributions: (1) proposing DRNN architecture with two output configurations (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO), (2) demonstrating their effectiveness in character-level language modeling with BPC as metric, (3) analyzing emergent time scales through perturbation experiments, and (4) showing DRNNs' ability to model long-term dependencies like parentheses closing.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with appropriate equations and figures explaining the architecture. The experimental setup is thorough, with careful attention to hyperparameters, training details, and performance metrics. The analysis of time scales through typo-perturbation experiments is particularly insightful, revealing how different layers develop distinct temporal processing capabilities. The text generation experiments and parentheses closing test provide compelling evidence of DRNNs' ability to model long-term dependencies.

The paper's main strength lies in its experimental validation of the DRNN architecture's theoretical advantages. The authors convincingly demonstrate that DRNNs can achieve comparable performance to more complex training methods using simple SGD, which is a significant practical contribution. The analysis of layer-specific contributions to the output (through ablation tests) provides valuable insight into the network's internal processing.

However, the paper has several notable weaknesses. First, the comparison to prior work is insufficiently contextualized. While the authors cite [8] as using stacked bi-directional LSTMs, they fail to adequately differentiate their work from this prior contribution. The paper claims "more in-depth analysis" of the architecture, but doesn't clearly articulate how their analysis differs from [8] or what specific insights their analysis provides that weren't previously known.

Second, the paper's claim of "state-of-the-art performance" lacks sufficient context. While Table 1 shows DRNN-1O outperforms the common RNN, the difference between DRNN-1O and the Hessian-free trained MRNN [19] is marginal (1.46 vs 1.45 BPC). The paper should more clearly explain why this small improvement is significant, especially given the paper's emphasis on using simple SGD instead of advanced optimization.

Third, the paper doesn't adequately address why DRNN-AO (with output connections at all layers) underperforms DRNN-1O (with output only at top layer), which seems counterintuitive given the authors' stated rationale for DRNN-AO. This is a critical gap in the analysis that weakens the paper's main claims.

Fourth, the training procedure for DRNN-1O is described but not justified. The paper states layer-wise training "was necessary to reach good performance," but doesn't explain why standard BPTT fails for this architecture or how this limitation affects the broader applicability of DRNNs.

The paper's analysis of time scales is interesting but could be strengthened by connecting these findings more explicitly to the theoretical advantages of hierarchical processing. The paper shows DRNNs develop different time scales at different layers, but doesn't clearly articulate how this hierarchical structure specifically addresses the limitations of standard RNNs in modeling temporal hierarchies.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding RNN architectures and their temporal processing capabilities, particularly with the insightful analysis of emergent time scales. The demonstration that simple SGD can achieve SOTA performance for recurrent networks is significant and practical. However, the paper's claims about the architecture's advantages are not fully substantiated, and the comparison to prior work is insufficiently contextualized.

For improvement, the authors should: (1) more clearly differentiate their analysis from prior work on stacked RNNs, (2) provide a more detailed explanation for the DRNN-AO vs DRNN-1O performance difference, (3) better justify the necessity of layer-wise training, and (4) more explicitly connect the time scale analysis to the theoretical advantages of hierarchical processing.

Overall, the paper presents a promising architectural extension to RNNs with interesting empirical findings. While the technical contributions are sound, the paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing the identified weaknesses and providing more precise claims about the novelty and significance of the work. The quality of the experiments is high, the clarity is good, but the originality and significance claims need more careful substantiation.
