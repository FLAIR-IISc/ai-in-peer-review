This paper introduces the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture where multiple RNN layers are stacked, with each layer taking the hidden state sequence of the layer below as input. The central claims are twofold: first, that this architecture naturally encourages a temporal hierarchy where higher layers learn to operate on longer time scales; and second, that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance on challenging character-level language modeling tasks using only standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The work builds upon prior ideas of hierarchical RNNs but distinguishes itself by providing a thorough analysis of the model's emergent properties, in contrast to previous works focused primarily on performance.

The paper's primary strength lies in its comprehensive and insightful analysis, which provides strong evidence for its claims. The empirical results are significant: the proposed DRNNs match the performance of the more complex Multiplicative RNN trained with a second-order Hessian-free optimizer, doing so with simple SGD. This highlights the power of the architectural design itself. The quality of the analysis is excellent. The authors design clever experiments to visualize the learned time scales, such as measuring the decay of a perturbation (a typo) through the layers. Furthermore, the analysis of long-term dependencies, demonstrated through the model's ability to correctly close parentheses over hundreds of characters, provides compelling qualitative evidence that the model is learning meaningful, non-trivial structure. The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and the experimental setup is detailed.

The work has some minor weaknesses. The claims about the general utility of DRNNs are supported by experiments on a single, albeit difficult, dataset and task. While character modeling is an excellent testbed, demonstrating the benefits on other sequential domains like speech or machine translation would further strengthen the conclusions. Additionally, the analysis of the different emergent time scales focuses heavily on the DRNN-AO model, with less insight provided into the differently-trained DRNN-1O, despite its slightly better performance.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution. It not only presents a strong result on a benchmark task but, more importantly, provides valuable insights into *why* the deep architecture is effective. The analysis of the learned temporal hierarchy is a key takeaway that will likely influence future research in recurrent models. The strengths heavily outweigh the minor limitations.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   Achieves state-of-the-art results with a conceptually simple model and training procedure.
*   Provides excellent, well-designed experiments and analysis supporting the central claim of a learned temporal hierarchy.
*   The work is significant, original, and clearly presented.

**Arguments for rejection:**
*   Empirical validation is confined to a single task.

I strongly recommend acceptance.
