- Paper studies stacked deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) with top-layer-only (DRNN-1O) and all-layers-to-output (DRNN-AO) variants, trained with SGD and gradient normalization.  
- Introduces incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O and evaluates on large-scale character-level language modeling (~1.4B chars, ~4.9M params).  
- Both DRNN variants match or approach prior state-of-the-art RNN results (MRNN+HF) on BPC with comparable parameter count.  
- Provides analyses of emergent time scales via perturbation experiments, layer ablations, and a targeted long-range parentheses task, claiming stacked RNNs form a temporal hierarchy.  
- Situates work relative to prior stacked/hierarchical RNNs, MRNN/HF, and deep supervision ideas; emphasizes simplicity and analysis breadth over architectural novelty.  
- Methodology is straightforward and well-defined; uses truncated BPTT (unroll 250, backprop 200), gradient normalization, and linear LR decay.  
- Experiments use a large dataset and matched-parameter models; reported BPC is competitive and analyses are informative.  
- Limitations include only three model instantiations, no variance estimates, and missing key baselines (single/multi-layer LSTM, MRNN with SGD).  
- Character set differs from prior work, potentially affecting BPC comparability; data split and validation protocol under-specified.  
- Truncated BPTT with likely hidden-state resets conflicts with claims about modeling dependencies longer than the backprop horizon; needs explicit treatment.  
- Paper is generally clear and organized; architecture, training, and analyses are concisely described.  
- Clarification needed on hidden-state resets, exact character vocabulary mapping, use of a validation set, and rationale for zero-initializing output layers.  
- Originality is incremental: stacking is not new, but the training strategy, all-layers-to-output design, and breadth of temporal analyses add value.  
- Significance: practical interest in showing simple stacked tanh-RNN+SGD can match HF-trained MRNNs; provides useful insights and a strong baseline.  
- Strength: simple competitive architecture with first-order optimization and careful, large-scale, parameter-matched comparisons.  
- Strength: insightful analyses (per-layer ablations, time-scale perturbations, context switch sensitivity, parentheses study).  
- Strength: effective and practically useful incremental layer-wise training strategy for DRNN-1O.  
- Weakness: missing LSTM/gated baselines and MRNN+SGD comparisons limit claims about relative merit.  
- Weakness: limited experimental breadth (three configurations, no variance, limited hyperparameter tuning details).  
- Weakness: comparability issue from different character set not quantified.  
- Weakness: TBPTT and probable hidden-state resets undermine claims about modeling very long dependencies without analysis.  
- Weakness: optimization and interpretability effects of all-layers-to-output not fully explored (e.g., learned vs fixed layer weightings).  
- Questions for authors: Are hidden states reset between sampled sequences; did you try TBPTT with state carryover?  
- Questions: What is the dev/validation protocol and were hyperparameters tuned on a held-out set?  
- Questions: Can you quantify the BPC impact of using 95 vs 86 characters (e.g., via mapped alphabet)?  
- Questions: How sensitive are results to gradient normalization and the linear LR schedule?  
- Questions: Can you report 1–2 layer LSTM results with similar parameter counts to assess depth vs gating?  
- Pros for acceptance: strong large-scale results with a simple method, thorough temporal analyses, and practical training insights.  
- Cons for acceptance: incremental architectural novelty, missing standard baselines, and remaining experimental/comparability gaps.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clarifying sequence handling, adding LSTM baseline, and quantifying vocabulary effects.
