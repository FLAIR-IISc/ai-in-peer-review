- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked RNN layers that induce temporal hierarchies across layers.  
- Claims DRNNs create different time scales per layer, capturing temporal hierarchies better than common RNNs.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art recurrent network performance on character-level Wikipedia language modeling using simple SGD.  
- Provides clear theoretical formulation and well-defined equations for DRNNs (Section 2).  
- Experiments are well-designed with appropriate baselines (common RNN, Hessian-free MRNN) and metrics (BPC).  
- Rigorous analysis of layer time scales via typo perturbation, context switching, and parentheses-closing experiments.  
- Exceptionally clear organization, explanations, and illustrative figures showing layer contributions.  
- Novel contribution: systematic theoretical and empirical analysis of temporal hierarchy in stacked RNNs.  
- Significant implications: DRNNs can model long-term dependencies and offer deeper understanding of sequence processing.  
- Limited scope: evaluation restricted to character-level language modeling, leaving generalizability to other tasks untested.  
- Training complexity: DRNN-1O needs layer-wise training, adding complexity not fully discussed or justified.  
- Missing comparisons to contemporary RNN variants (e.g., LSTMs, GRUs), focusing mainly on Hessian-free baselines.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clear, and significant work that merits acceptance despite minor scope and comparison limitations.
