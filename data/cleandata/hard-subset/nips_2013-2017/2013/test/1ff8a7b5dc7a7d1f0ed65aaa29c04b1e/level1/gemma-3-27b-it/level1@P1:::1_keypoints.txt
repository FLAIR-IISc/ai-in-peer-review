- Investigates stacking simple RNNs (DRNNs) to create hierarchical time-scale processing for character-level language modeling.
- Achieves state-of-the-art on a Wikipedia corpus using SGD, outperforming prior RNNs trained with more complex optimizers.
- Analyzes emergent time scales in layers and uses typo perturbations and parenthesis matching to demonstrate hierarchical representations.
- Positions work relative to prior hierarchical RNN and LSTM research, emphasizing more focused architectural analysis.
- Novelty: systematic exploration of stacked RNNs forming a deep recurrent architecture with inherent time-scale hierarchy.
- Performance: strong empirical gains suggest the architecture itself drives improvements.
- Analysis: provides valuable insights into internal dynamics and learned time scales.
- Clarity: well-written with helpful figures describing architecture and experiments.
- Comparison: limited direct comparisons to other modern architectures (e.g., transformers).
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: learning rate sensitivity (notably for top layer) needs deeper investigation.
- Computational cost: training is expensive (≈10 days per network), limiting scalability and exploration.
- Interpretability: further probing (hidden-state visualization, activation analysis) required for deeper understanding.
- Output connections: rationale for using outputs from all layers (DRNN-AO) is not rigorously justified; needs ablation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical and analytic contributions.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons, high computational cost, and interpretability gaps.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions; recommendation for acceptance.
- Rating: 7/10 (Strong Accept).
