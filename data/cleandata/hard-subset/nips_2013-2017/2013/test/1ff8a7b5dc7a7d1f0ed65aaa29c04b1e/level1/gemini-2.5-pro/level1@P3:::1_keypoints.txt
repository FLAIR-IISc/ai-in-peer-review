- Proposes a stacked Deep RNN (DRNN) for hierarchical temporal processing with two variants (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO) and evaluates on large-scale character-level language modeling.  
- Trains DRNNs with simple SGD + gradient clipping and achieves state-of-the-art recurrent network performance, matching more complex models/optimizers.  
- Provides in-depth analysis showing higher layers operate on longer timescales and the model captures long-range dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses over hundreds of chars).  
- Strength: Demonstrates strong empirical results using a simple architecture and basic optimization, challenging the need for complex second-order methods.  
- Strength: Offers insightful, well-designed analyses (temporal hierarchy experiments, parenthesis task) that explain why deep RNNs work.  
- Strength: Paper is clear, well-organized, and explains model variants and training procedures effectively.  
- Strength: Significant impact—supports depth as crucial for sequence models and makes powerful models more accessible.  
- Weakness: Results are from single training runs per model, so performance variance and statistical significance are uncertain.  
- Weakness: Layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is heuristic and not rigorously compared to end-to-end training, weakening motivation for the procedure.  
- Weakness: Ablation-based analysis of layer contributions in DRNN-AO has limited interpretability due to inter-layer interactions.  
- Comment: Comparison to prior work should clarify dataset character set differences and support the claim that extra rare characters don’t affect cross-entropy.  
- Comment: Parenthesis experiment reveals nuanced, context-dependent forgetting (e.g., after full stops), suggesting learned sentence-structure heuristics.  
- Comment: Future work should compare DRNNs with second-order optimization to see if architectural and optimization advantages combine.  
- Conclusion: An excellent, original, and impactful paper with minor weaknesses; strongly recommended for acceptance.
