

# Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), where multiple RNN layers are stacked such that each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that common RNNs do not explicitly accommodate temporal hierarchies (where information exists across multiple time scales), and DRNNs naturally create different time scales at different layers. The paper studies DRNNs for character-level language modeling on a Wikipedia corpus, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD (previously requiring complex Hessian-free training). The authors also analyze how different layers develop distinct time scales through various experiments.

The paper builds upon previous hierarchical RNN work (e.g., [3, 5, 6, 8]) but makes a significant contribution by providing a systematic analysis of the temporal hierarchy inherent in DRNNs. While [8] used stacked bi-directional LSTMs for speech recognition, this paper focuses on character-level language modeling and provides deeper theoretical and empirical analysis of the time-scale hierarchy.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper provides a clear theoretical formulation of DRNNs with well-defined equations (Section 2). The experiments are well-designed, with appropriate baselines (common RNN, Hessian-free trained networks) and metrics (BPC). The analysis of time scales through typo perturbation, context switching, and parentheses closing is methodologically rigorous and provides valuable insights.

2. **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written. The DRNN architecture is well-explained with equations and a schematic figure. The experimental setup is detailed, and results are presented with appropriate statistical measures. The analysis of different layers' contributions (Figures 2-4) is particularly clear and insightful.

3. **Originality:** While hierarchical RNNs have been explored before ([5, 8]), this paper provides a novel theoretical and empirical analysis of the temporal hierarchy that emerges in DRNNs. The demonstration that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance with simple SGD (rather than complex Hessian-free training) is a significant contribution, as it challenges the prevailing view that more complex training is necessary for good performance.

4. **Significance:** The paper demonstrates that DRNNs can effectively capture temporal hierarchies in time series data, which has implications for many sequence modeling tasks. The analysis of time scales across layers provides a deeper understanding of how these architectures process information. The finding that DRNNs can model long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses after long phrases) suggests practical advantages over standard RNNs.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Scope of Analysis:** While the paper focuses on character-level language modeling, it doesn't explore whether DRNNs provide similar advantages for other temporal tasks (e.g., speech recognition, time series forecasting). The generalizability of the findings could be strengthened with additional experiments.

2. **Training Complexity:** The DRNN-1O architecture requires a layer-wise training approach, which adds complexity compared to standard RNN training. The paper doesn't thoroughly discuss the trade-offs between this complexity and the performance gains.

3. **Comparison to Recent Work:** The paper doesn't compare against more recent RNN variants (e.g., LSTMs, GRUs) that might have achieved similar performance with different architectures. The state-of-the-art comparison focuses primarily on the Hessian-free MRNN from [19], but doesn't address whether DRNNs outperform other modern RNN variants.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Provides a clear theoretical and empirical analysis of temporal hierarchy in RNNs
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using simple SGD
- Offers novel insights into how different layers process information at different time scales
- Well-written with strong experimental design and analysis

*Con:*
- Limited scope of analysis beyond character-level language modeling
- Doesn't compare against recent RNN variants beyond Hessian-free methods
- Requires complex layer-wise training for some architectures

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding the properties of hierarchical RNN architectures. The demonstration that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using simple SGD is significant, as it challenges the assumption that complex training is necessary for good performance. The analysis of time scales across layers provides important theoretical insights that advance our understanding of how recurrent architectures process temporal information.

The paper is technically sound, well-written, and makes a clear contribution to the field. While the scope is limited to character-level language modeling, the insights about temporal hierarchy are broadly applicable to sequence modeling tasks. The work provides a solid foundation for future research on hierarchical RNNs and their training.

The paper meets all four criteria for quality: it's technically sound with well-supported claims; it's exceptionally clear; it makes a novel contribution to understanding RNN architectures; and it has significant implications for sequence modeling. The minor weaknesses noted do not detract from the paper's overall contribution.

I recommend acceptance to NIPS.
