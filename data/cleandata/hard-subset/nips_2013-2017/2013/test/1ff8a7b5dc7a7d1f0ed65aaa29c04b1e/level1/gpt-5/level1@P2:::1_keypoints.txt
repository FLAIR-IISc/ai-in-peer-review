- Paper studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling with outputs from top-only (DRNN-1O) or all layers summed (DRNN-AO).

- Training uses SGD with gradient normalization, BPTT over 250 steps, and incremental layer-wise pretraining for DRNN-1O.

- On a 1.4B-character Wikipedia corpus, 5-layer DRNNs (~4.9M params) match prior MRNN+HF results using only SGD.

- Distinctive contributions: simple stacked tanh-RNN architecture, deep-supervision variant, and empirical analysis of layer-wise time scales and long-term behavior.

- Model definition, training protocol, and analyses (perturbation, ablation, parentheses test) are technically sound and well-explained.

- Empirical results show DRNNs outperform single-layer RNNs and demonstrate hierarchical time scales, especially for AO variant.

- Parentheses experiments provide targeted evidence of DRNNs' ability to capture long-range dependencies.

- Comparisons are limited: no LSTM/GRU or MRNN reimplementation and only a single dataset/task used.

- Only three model instantiations were tried, with no variance across random seeds or reported standard deviations.

- Character-set differences (95 vs. 86) complicate cross-paper BPC comparisons and state-of-the-art claims.

- The impact of the unusual optimization heuristic (gradient normalization) is not ablated or formally explained.

- DRNN-1O time-scale findings may be confounded by its staged incremental training procedure.

- Paper clarity is good, with precise architecture/training descriptions, but some protocol details are missing.

- Suggested clarity improvements: report validation protocol, consolidated BPC tables with parameter/char-set details and stddevs, formalize gradient normalization, and include training curves and per-layer norms.

- Originality is moderate: stacked RNNs and deep supervision are not new, but combining them with analysis on a large corpus is novel.

- Significance: shows depth + SGD can close gap with HF-trained models and provides insights into why depth helps, but lack of gated-RNN baselines limits impact.

- Strengths: simple reproducible architecture, competitive BPC with SGD, multifaceted temporal analyses, careful ablations, and discussion of training strategies.

- Weaknesses: limited baselines and datasets, few runs/no uncertainty estimates, unablated training heuristics, confounded time-scale results, and no regularization/validation details.

- Suggested improvements: add LSTM/MRNN baselines with matched params, use held-out validation and multiple seeds, ablate gradient normalization and deep supervision, evaluate on additional corpora, probe other long-range patterns, and release code.

- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical result on a large benchmark, insightful analyses, and a practical training recipe likely to be adopted.

- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete baselines and experimental breadth, incremental architectural novelty, and unisolated methodological choices.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — solid empirical and analytical contribution despite limited baselines and scope.
