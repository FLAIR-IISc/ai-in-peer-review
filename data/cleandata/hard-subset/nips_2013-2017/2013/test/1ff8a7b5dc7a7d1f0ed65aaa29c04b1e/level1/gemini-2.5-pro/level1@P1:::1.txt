### Paper Review

**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper investigates the properties of a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The motivation is that standard RNNs, while deep in time, are architecturally "shallow" at each time step, which may limit their ability to perform the hierarchical processing needed for complex time series like natural language. In the proposed DRNN, the hidden state of each layer serves as the input sequence for the layer above it. This structure is hypothesized to naturally capture hierarchical temporal structures and create different timescales at different levels of the network.

The authors evaluate DRNNs on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. They propose and compare two variants: one where the output is generated only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). Using only standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with gradient normalization, they demonstrate that a 5-layer DRNN achieves state-of-the-art performance, comparable to that of a more complex multiplicative RNN trained with the sophisticated Hessian-free optimization technique [19].

Beyond reporting performance, a significant portion of the paper is dedicated to analyzing the trained models. The authors provide compelling evidence that the DRNNs learn a temporal hierarchy, with higher layers exhibiting longer memory and slower dynamics than lower layers. Through ablation studies and analysis of text generation, they show how different layers contribute to modeling structure at different scales (e.g., sub-word vs. longer-term). Finally, they demonstrate the model's ability to capture very long-range dependencies, such as correctly closing parentheses hundreds of characters after they are opened. This work positions architectural depth as a critical and effective alternative to more complex optimization methods for improving RNN performance.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The architectural proposal is simple, well-defined, and strongly motivated. The experimental setup is rigorous, using a large-scale, established benchmark that allows for a direct and impactful comparison with prior state-of-the-art results. The claims are well-supported by the empirical results.

The key strength of this paper is its extensive analysis. Rather than simply presenting a new model and a new top score, the authors conduct a series of insightful experiments to understand *why* the model works. The analysis of emergent timescales (Fig. 3), the investigation of individual layer contributions (Fig. 2), and the specific test on long-term dependencies (parentheses, Fig. 4) provide convincing evidence for the central hypothesis that DRNNs learn a temporal hierarchy. This level of analysis is commendable and adds significant value beyond the performance numbers.

A minor weakness is that some aspects of the training are heuristic. The layer-wise training scheme for the DRNN-1O, while effective, feels somewhat ad-hoc. A more thorough justification or ablation study on this specific training strategy would have been beneficial. Similarly, the use of gradient normalization is a practical trick, though its effectiveness is not in doubt. The claim that performance variation between initializations is low for such large models is plausible, but testing only a single run for each architecture is a limitation.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The introduction clearly motivates the need for hierarchical processing in sequence models and contrasts the proposed DRNN with standard RNNs. The model description is concise and accompanied by a helpful diagram (Fig. 1). The experimental results and analysis are presented with clear figures and tables, and the authors' interpretations are well-argued and easy to understand. The paper provides sufficient detail for an expert to grasp the methodology.

**Originality:** The idea of stacking recurrent layers is a natural extension of deep feedforward networks and had been explored in some prior work, which the authors appropriately cite (e.g., [5], [8]). However, this paper's contribution is highly original in its context and focus. It is one of the first to systematically demonstrate the power of deep recurrent architectures on a large-scale, real-world task. Critically, the originality lies in framing the architectural depth not just as a means to more parameters, but as a mechanism for learning a temporal hierarchy. The detailed analysis of this emergent property is a novel contribution that provides significant insight into the functioning of these models. Furthermore, demonstrating that this architectural change can rival gains from advanced second-order optimization methods [19] was a novel and important finding at the time.

**Significance:** The significance of this work is very high. It compellingly argues for the importance of architectural depth in recurrent models, a principle that has since become standard practice in state-of-the-art sequence modeling (e.g., stacked LSTMs are now ubiquitous). The finding that a simple architecture trained with SGD can match more complex models/optimizers makes high-performance sequence modeling more accessible. The paper's analytical approach set a high standard for future work, encouraging researchers not only to chase metrics but also to understand the models they build. The insights into how DRNNs learn multi-scale temporal representations have likely influenced subsequent research into hierarchical sequence modeling. This paper addresses a fundamental problem in a better way than previous research and has had a clear and lasting impact on the field.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**
*   Presents a simple, effective, and well-motivated architectural improvement for RNNs.
*   Achieves state-of-the-art results on a difficult benchmark using a simple first-order training method, demonstrating the power of architecture over complex optimization.
*   Provides an outstanding and insightful analysis of the model's learned behavior, offering strong evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy.
*   The paper is extremely well-written, clear, and places itself well within the context of existing literature.
*   High potential for significant and lasting impact on the field of sequence modeling.

**Con:**
*   The core architectural idea is an incremental, albeit powerful, extension of existing concepts.
*   The training procedure for one of the models (DRNN-1O) is somewhat heuristic.
*   The empirical validation is confined to a single (though important) task.

**Conclusion:**

This is an excellent paper that makes a strong and significant contribution. The weaknesses are minor compared to the strengths. The combination of state-of-the-art performance with a deep, insightful analysis makes this a standout piece of work. It not only demonstrates a better model but also provides a deeper understanding of why it is better. I strongly recommend acceptance.
