- Introduces DRNN: stacked RNN layers where each layer receives the previous layer's hidden state to capture temporal hierarchies.  
- Claims DRNNs better model multiple time scales in time series than standard RNNs.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on Wikipedia using simple SGD instead of Hessian-free optimization.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of hidden-state evolution and why stacking induces different time scales.  
- Empirical analysis includes the "typo-perturbation" experiment showing layers maintain different temporal contexts.  
- Experimental design is strong, reproducible, and includes appropriate ablation studies (e.g., removing layer outputs).  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and clearly written with useful figures and equations.  
- Main clarity issue: inconsistent/late definition of DRNN variants (DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO) could confuse readers.  
- Original contribution: explicit analysis of temporal hierarchies in stacked recurrent architectures, distinct from Graves et al. (2013).  
- Significance: practical impact from SOTA results with simple training and theoretical insights into long-term dependency modeling.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to modern RNN variants like LSTMs and GRUs and unclear differentiation from prior stacked LSTM work.  
- Weakness: comparison to Hessian-free methods unclear due to unspecified total training data for fair benchmarking.  
- Weakness: broader applicability beyond language modeling is not thoroughly explored.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept based on novelty, analysis, experiments, and convincing results.
