- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (stacked RNNs) hypothesis: different layers learn different temporal scales, capturing temporal hierarchies.  
- Evaluated on character-level language modeling (large Wikipedia) and achieves BPC 1.479 with SGD, comparable to MRNN+HF (1.48 BPC).  
- Contribution is more analytical than architectural: detailed investigation of properties and internal behavior of DRNNs.  
- Demonstrates significance: simple architectural change can match complex models and second-order optimizers, highlighting architecture vs. optimizer trade-offs.  
- Strong analysis: experiments show higher layers operate on longer time scales via perturbation decay measurements.  
- Layer-contribution analysis suggests division of labor: lower/upper layers tied to outputs, middle layers transform features.  
- Case study (closing parentheses) shows learning of long-range dependencies and generalizable rules over hundreds of timesteps.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and provides reproducible training/experimental details and illustrative diagrams.  
- Architecture originality is limited—stacking RNNs builds on prior work (El Hihi & Bengio 1996; Graves et al. 2013).  
- Best model (DRNN-1O) required ad-hoc layer-wise incremental training, suggesting unresolved optimization/initialization issues.  
- Empirical validation is narrow, focused on a single task; broader benchmarks or synthetic long-memory tasks are missing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: state-of-the-art results with simple model+SGD, novel and insightful analysis, high-quality execution likely to influence RNN research.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental architectural novelty, reliance on heuristic training, and limited empirical scope.  
- Overall: despite incremental architecture, the paper’s rigorous analysis and strong results make a compelling case for acceptance.
